## Left Page

### Sun. June 9, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delicious day, brilliant, breezy, neither cold or hot — Had my run down early &
bkfp. at 9 — It was after 11 before Ralph
\+ I started out — we walked up to the
Park & sauntered about there until
1.30 — Then home to dinner — Afternoon
wrote notes & later, napped — Eve'f
wrote a letter to Breadner objecting
to tearing out all of the pipe between
house & Annex to put in a larger
return piece to secure a circulation
of hot water & suggesting a middle
course. — Took it to G. C. Station
before 11 & mailed it —

## Right Page

### Mon. June 10, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

a very pleasant day — warmer — after
bkfp. wrote to Scott Brown about draw-
ing deed for a swap of gores of land with
Wm. Nielson at Keene Heights — & a note to
J. Weitenkampf. Then to Dr. Gerster's — he
says my neck is healing finely. Then to
studio & until 7 o'clk. clearing up things
there — awful confusion — Lunch — At 2.30 —
Mary, Ralph & I went to Wanamaker's
& spent the afternoon there selecting beds
& furniture for Keene. R — & I stopped at
Goodyears & got him rubber boots & hat —
Then we walked home to dinner. Geo.
dined with us. Eve'f. Charl came in.
I got a letter from Jim — eyes troubling him
— wants some advice about treatment — I
wrote in reply & took it to G. C. Station
at 10.30
To day Bessie went to Summit to
visit E. Holt.
