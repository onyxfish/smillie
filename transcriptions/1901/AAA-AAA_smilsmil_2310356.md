## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 28, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thanksgiving Day.

A very fine day — in spite of prognostics of snow by the clerk of the weather — Up about 8.30 \+ immediately after it started the boys for Keith's for a morning of amusement — then I went to studio \+ worked on Borden plate till 2 — then home to dinner — Charl dined with us — we had a table full — Mary, the girls, Kate, Charl \+ my boys — we had a good dinner \+ it was enjoyed — it was so late when we got thro' that I did not go back to studio — Some of the family were hungry enough to eat again at 7 o'clk — at 7.45 Jim, Ralph \+ I went to G.C.S — \+ Jim took the 8 o'clk train for New Haven \+ Ralph \+ I loafed about till 8.35 when he went off with a lot of Hackley boys for school. Then I went to Charl's — Mary, Kate \+ the girls there — C. played some of the dear old Beethoven Symphonies for us — Home at 10.30 \+ our Thanksgiving was done.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Yesterdays snow got in behind time to-day — the day was leaden \+ gloomy \+ for a short time the snow flew quite lively — but it did it amt. to much. I got to studio at 9.30 — put on all draughts — got a roaring fire — then the water in pipes began to boil \+ things got very lively — steam \+ boiling water over the roof — neighbors thought it was a fire. worked on Borden Plate — Home to lunch — back — worked till 4 — cleaned [illegible] — walked the streets for 3/4 of an hour. Home at 6 — dinner — Evef, newspapers — then a little writing —
