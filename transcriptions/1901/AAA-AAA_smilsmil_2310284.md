## Left Page

### Fri. July 5, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very muggy, thunderous, sticky day with many showers (some of them very heavy) all about. Up at about 6.30 — went downtown before bkfs. \+ back just in time to escape the first shower — After 9, Clarey, from Pitman's, came \+ we went to the house \+ took a look at the work to be done — He tells me that owing to funerals he cannot commence the work before Monday. He soon left \+ sent a couple of darkies who carried boxes from the attic to the barn till 12 o'clk. I worked at selecting \+ putting aside many things in Studio's closet and etching room. Dinner after 1 — part of the afternoon at work at the house — To Sayres after 4 — a furious thunder storm before 6. Tea at 6.30 \+ a stupefied evef. — To bed at 10.

The family at Mary Sayre's consists of Mr. \+ Mrs. Pennypacker, 3 young daughters \+ niece of Phila. \+ Miss Jewel.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 6, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day — hot at midday but breezy. After bkfs. I went to P.O. \+ got a letter from Mary — but no word from Jim yet — Wrote to Mary from Cooley's Office — went to shoemaker \+ harness maker \+ then to mill \+ made arrangements for lumber for boxing — then to Pope's and engaged a team to get Spruce lumber from my place \+ take it to the mill. — It was then after noon \+ I went to Sayre's. — Dinner at 1 — \+ after that to Kent's where the Spruce planks \+ ends of timbers were being loaded — Then to mill where the Spruce was delivered — this is lumber that was left over from building of water tanks years ago. The mill buys it \+ I get hemlock boards to make packing boxes. After this I went to Sayres \+ had a rest — then out \+ again to the Kents — Tea at 6.30 — Went again to P.O. — but got nothing. Then back to Sayres \+ sat till near 10 on the lawn with the Pennypacker's then to bed.
