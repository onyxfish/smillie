## Left Page

### Sat. June 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fearfully hot — no let up - modified by a pleasant breeze that soon became too hot for comfort. The. in my bedroom up to 91°. \+ not below 83°. It has not fallen below 83° in my room any night for a week or more - After bkfst. I went up to Dr. Allen's in 47th St. He says my teeth do not need attention. Then to Fried-rich's \+ bought Water Colors etc - Home \+ spent rest \+ m't. packing big box for Keene - got that done before lunch. After lunch I spent the afternoon in gathering things together in my room - I am slow at this work \+ find that now I become easily confused - but I made progress \+ felt very well - Dinner at 6.30 - Charl spent the ev't. with us - after 9 I began to feel uncomfortable about the stomach - squeamish - with a little tenderness — went to bed rather unhappy \+ spent an uncomfortable night.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 30, 1901.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim rel\[?] from Pomfret.

This seems to have been the most insufferably hot day yet altho' the Ther. does not so record it - getting little, if any, higher than yesterday - Jim got to the house before 8 - having come on the night boat from New London where his class had their dinner at the Pequot House. He looks well but, as usual, is uncommunicative - He answers questions briefly but volunteers nothing. Bkfst. about 9 - after a night that brought little refreshment I feel very much like work but in the course of the day managed to do a good deal in the way of forwarding my trunk packing \+ getting things in order for leaving. Dinner at 2 - by shifts of rest \+ work I got thro' the day. About 10 A.M. I went to G. C. Station \+ got R.R. tickets for Mary \+ Party - \+ about 4 P.M. I went to Freese's drug store \+ got my summer stock of drugs. Charl spent the ev't. with us.
