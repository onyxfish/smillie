## Left Page

### Sun. July 21, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably hot day. Up at 7 after a night of sleeplessness — I had a bath + blkfst at 8 o'clk. The m'f was spent mostly lolling about indoors — Maud, with her daughter Eleanore, came up + stayed until after 12 — I had a good nap from 1 to 2 — then dinner — more loafing about — May very interesting in her talks about army life, Manila, China + Japan — Ralph + I went to Newton Creek where I used to make studies — many of the old butternut trees have disappeared — another rest \+ tea at 6.30 — Jack Driven came in for a few minutes — we sat out under the trees until driven in by mosquitoes — Then Maud came + we chatted till after 9 — Then we all walked home with her — Then back — to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Mon. July 22, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot, disagreeable day. The m'f was spent very quietly indoors — most of the time in library talking with May of army life in the east + of work to be done — at 12 I had a restful lunch at 1. After that we said our good bye to Ella + with May rode to town where she had business — she took us to D.L.+W. station where I expected to take a train at 2.55 to connect at Binghampton with D.&H. at 5.25 — I had misread time table — it was an A.M. train that left at 2.55 — The P.M. train left at 3.55 + got to B. 20 m. too late for D.&H. — I was much disgusted but there was no help for it — May bid us good bye + we sat on shady platform + read till the train came — late — hot, dirty ride — got to B. at 6. + went to Arlington Hotel — took a room + had dinner. After that sat in front of hotel — cooler — Then to D.&H. station, bought mileage ticket + arranged to take 1.55 A.M. train to Albany — then back to hotel + to our room soon after 9 o'clk — it was very hot + close, but in spite of it we got some sleep — up at 1 o'clk. — went to station — train late — did not get started till 2.40 — soon in our beds + had more sleep —
