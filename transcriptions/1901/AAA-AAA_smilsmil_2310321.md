## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 19, 1901

**Weather:** Cloudy & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy & cold. Webb came. he saw Cook at Keene Valley yesterday & C. told him that yesterday he had delivered the bulk of the Prescott shipment at St. Hubert's & had told Walter Estes to inform me. This was pure fabrication. he delivered it at my house this afternoon — no one saw him. He met Jno. Otis on the road to the Forks yesterday & turned him back, telling him that he (C.) had all of the stuff. Webb worked all day fitting window blinds. I worked until after 11 on Borden 12" × 18", preparing it for painting. Then I went to hotel & to Scott Browns. Then Mary, Bessie & Charlie came along & we walked in the Reserve for a mile or more. Then back to hotel where I was introduced to Arthur Ryle. To dinner at 1.30. Mollie Adams & Ralph went up Noon Mark in the m.f. After dinner I set palette and commenced painting on Borden & soon got into a mess. I stuck to it thoroughly tired — then I lay down & took a nap & and after that I painted as long as I could see. Bessie brought up Road Roller from Westport. Tea at 6.30. Eve'g with newspapers & marking towels & sheets for Jim.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 20, 1901

**Weather:** Bright m.f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m.f. but soon clouded over the day being gloomy — very cold last night. Ther. down to 33° outside & below 56° in my bedroom. Webb came & put in north window in my bedroom extension. I worked in studio painting on Borden subject. After 12 I went to hotel & then back to dinner — afternoon painted but was much interrupted by having to give Webb instructions & in efforts to solve some perspective problems that are bothering me. It was about 5 when I went down the hill to Harry Budie's about some spruce "slabs" he has for me. Then a short walk & home to tea. Mollie & Ralph went off for a tramp over Gothic Trail, 17 miles. Soon after 5 h.f. to get back about 4+ p.m. reporting that they had been almost frozen on the mt. Eve'g. Reading newspapers.
