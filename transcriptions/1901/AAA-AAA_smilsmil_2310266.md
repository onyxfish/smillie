## Left Page

### Tues. May 28, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day with some mild sunshine thro' the middle portion of it — waked at 5 A.m. with racking headache — was not able to get up until after 11 — I sunned myself on the north side of the street for a little while — Dr. Yale \+ Berg came before 1 — Dr. B — dressed my throat \+ said that its condition was satisfactory — but they would not let me see it — Lunch at 1.30 — After lunch I went to studio — saw Maynard \+ he paid me $400 on acct. of rent — much to the gratification of both of us — I bought for myself a bottle of wine \+ a bottle of Phillips Tonic — I did not get into shape to do any work or write any letters — Dinner at 6.30 — Roelf, Kate, Ralph \+ I went to Charl's \+ stayed there till 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate went to Po'keepsie.

Another most dismal day — rain during the night \+ almost continuously during the day about 2 o'clk. a thunderstorm with an exceedingly heavy fall of rain for a few minutes. I did not go outside during the day — I wrote a letter to Mr. Kent — Dr. Berg came about 1 o'clk \+ dressed my neck — says that the healing is progressing very rapidly \+ satisfactorily. Lunch — more writing — Kate got away for Po'keepsie on the 4 o'clk. train — Ralph \+ I had a quiet dinner at 6.30 — followed by a quiet even'g. reading.
