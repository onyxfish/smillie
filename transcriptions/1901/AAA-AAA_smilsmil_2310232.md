## Left Page

### Thur. March 21, 1901

**Weather:** Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the night \+ very heavy in the early m't. — ceased before noon — clearing in the afternoon — fine night. To studio at 9.30 — worked awhile on diplp't. head. Then wet down some paper before I went home to lunch — after lunch went to Durand Ruel's to see exhibit by "the ten". Very little to interest me — a remarkable picture by Benson — two children out in sunshine — sea beyond — beautiful in color \+ tone values — a veritable bit of sunlight — met Maurice Sterne there — then to studio \+ took points from diplp't. head — just the face alone — Home before 5 \+ had a rest — at 6 dressed for dinner — having been invited to Walker's to meet the Rev. Mr. Smith \+ wife — parents of the young man Catharine Cook is to marry — Just before 7 — Edw'd Cook came to say that the dinner was off — his mother \+ Sallie at Atlantic City \+ his father had forgotten to inform me — but w'd. be pleased if I w'd. dine with him — I went, but was satisfied that I was not expected — home at 8.30 \+ worked on accts.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 22, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — When I went to studio I found it so cold — not yet 60° — owing to the carelessness of Jennings, that I could not work so I went to deposit vault. 2'. Nat. Bk \+ get cet. of d.o.m. stock that I am going to sell — then again to studio \+ worked on diplp't. head — much discouraged — waste of time — took couple of points of face alone — Home to lunch at 1 — after that, a short nap \+ to studio at 3 — wrote letter to Jim — Home at 6 — Mable Cooly came to visit the girls — Dinner at 6.30. Evel. Ralph \+ I went to The Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw "A Temperance Town" — not a very edifying performance — There was much in it that was ridiculous but very little that was witty or good — its moral, if it could be said to have one, was very questionable.
