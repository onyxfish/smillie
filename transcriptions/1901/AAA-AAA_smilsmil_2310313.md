## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 3, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant mild day - thunderstorm after 2 o'clk. passed south of us - a fine low rainbow on the face of the Giant. Webb worked all day - Otis made out what he called 3/4 of a day. finished spreading earth on north terrace - Jim + Ralph made an all day tramp up Hopkins - I loafed about a good deal - doing a little here + there + getting quite tired before dinner - I had a rest in the afternoon + about the eve + skimming thro' a book: "The Crime of M.Y. Vane" - It is very frivolous and flimsy after Ely. Phelps's fine ideal of a woman in "Dr. Zay" - K. + E. + Jim at the Hardenburghs - all to bed before 10 + the house was left open for them.

About 1 the hotel porter came over to say that someone at Ausable Forks wanted me on the Telephone - he went back for further information (as I was alone in the house + was not strong enough to go to the hotel) + I heard nothing further.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 4, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably warm day - showers to north of us - a very light one passed over us in the afternoon - fine thunderstorm on east side of Giant. I got up with headache that bothered me all day - I followed Webb + Otis most of the time - about 11 went to hotel. The telephone yesterday was from Prescott + Son at Keeseville - have no yellow pine - can get none in Plattsburgh or Burlington. I went to 'phone + talked with them - they will send to Albany for it. Loafed awhile on hotel piazza - charming effects - then down the road to the new part + went along that for a ways - then home + rested till dinner time - Planned a rough printing table + got Webb at that - from 4 to 5 had a rest - tea at 6.30 - Eve'g reading a fool book + walking on piazza.
