## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 27, 1901

**Weather:** clear, cold + windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

clear, cold + windy. To studio after having gone to Bayerdorfer with my watch. I painted all the m't on "After Showers" changing foreground left hand corner. Reducing forms + correcting + simplifying color — I think the picture is improv'd + I shall risk rejection in sending it to the Soc'ty of Am. Painters next month. Sterne called me up on telephone from N. A. telling me that the Etching class was at work + it w'd not be necessary for me to make the journey there — Home to lunch at 1. It was after 3 o'clk when I got to studio until near 6 o'clk. I was engaged house cleaning + pulling away studies and things that have been accumulating about — Then walked for half an hour on Park Ave. + home to dinner — Read paper until near 9 o'clk. Then Ralph + I went to Charles — He + Mr. Meyer were going to the Water Color exhibit but they gave that up. R + I stayed until 10 — then home.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 28, 1901

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day. To studio + worked for a while on "the Rise of the River" — then spent the rest of the m't in a final clearing up of studio — Home to lunch at 1 + back at 2 — Chas. Collins came having some questions concerning the heavy use of steel by engravers — he remained until Hattie + Bessie came — followed by Mary and later by Kate, all of whom came by invitation to see my latest work — we had a chatty time with compliments all around, of course. After they left Emma + Mary Ireland came + we had more chatting + compliments. About 5 I went to Bayerdorfer's about my watch — he showed me a second-hand chronometer which he recommended strongly — I shall think of it. Then to Sterne's + McCreary's in 23d St. looking for a good writing lamp without success. Home to dinner at 6.30. Evg. loafing at home.
