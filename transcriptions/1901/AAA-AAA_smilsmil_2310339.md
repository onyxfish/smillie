## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 25, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - an incursion of mechanics in the m't. before I had finished my bkf't. Allen \+ his helper came \+ got to work on the dining room chimney flue - they made a great mess \+ finally broke into the flue from the second story \+ took out a lot of fallen brick. Then Douglas's man came \+ commenced to take down cove moulding in my room - \+ then Klees' man came with new cove - I got to studio at about 10 \+ etched all day on Borden plate with exception of lunch time. A little before 5 Yale came to studio \+ made a purely social visit - He did not say a word about my note, his reply - on my visit to Dr. Cilley - and I followed his example - at 5.20 I rode down to Wanamaker's \+ bought a \#12? moirain rug for Jim - then home - supper - We't wrote a letter to Jim \+ mailed it. my room being in confusion I slept in room of the parlor.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - I worked all day on the Borden plates making fair progress. On my way to studio I stopped at Dr. Cilley's \+ saw him for a few moments - read Yale's letter (written to me) to him \+ told him that as Y. seemed to take my suggestion of occasionally consulting Dr. C - so seriously for the present I would not press it but that sooner or later I was satisfied that I must consult some one else than Dr. Y. Being a half work-day the mechanics made little progress in my room. I Rec'd from dead-letter office (P.O.) my letter of Oct. 9th to Frank Webb with check for \$75 ? It seems that there is no Hulls Falls P.O. as Webb stated - I forwarded the letter to him at once. Eve \+ Charl came in for a little while.
