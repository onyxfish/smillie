## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - Immediately after bkfst. Robt. Neilson came to look at my horse lawn mower with a view to buying for Hotel Co - Then I rode with him down to the new Road + there I met W.G. Neilson + with him went over the blazed lines that divides us on N. + E. I fancy he has it in his mind to make some sort of a bargain, if he can to be rid of the worthless triangle of his that lies n, or me + to the lines of the new Road, with a caving sand bank 300 + 400 feet long that will Require constant attention. When I got home it was after 10 - Robt. N - came + took away my mowing machine - By this time I was feeling very uncomfortable + went to my room + lay down upon my bed - feeling feverish I tested it with Ther. It recorded 100° + I decided that I should call a doctor. Jim went for D. Tillinghast - I ate a little dinner + then again on my bed till the D. came about 4 o'clk. He thinks my Kidneys are not taking any active part in the present disturbance - it is Catarr of Stomach + Bowels + he advised a clearing out with calomel to be followed in the mng by Phosphate of Soda - I did as advised -
Eve+ Vaudeville at Hotel Casino maid of The Keene, Haefn's library - all hands went but of course I did not go - they got before 11 o'clk. having had a good time + no sight of many short comings - The auction sale of posters was a very tame affair - Mr. White bought my poster for $10.00 my letter explaining significance of color was mislaid, so Mr. Howell said, so my attempt to be amusing went for nought.

Webb worked all day + Jno. + Will Otis, with teams, worked all the afternoon hauling sod + top dirt from the new road where it crosses meadow at foot of hill - This to to top-dress about the house + lay the foundation for a bit of lawn.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 27, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - after a night of tolerable discomfort I got up with Temp. a trifle above normal but feeling weak + useless. Webb (as agreed last evt.) did not come - Jn°. + Will with team hauled top earth all the afternoon. I hung about but was not able to accomplish anything - loafing on the piazzas - + trying to feel happy upon a diet of sweet, fresh air + lovely mt. views - but an access of fever in the afternoon put me on my back on my bed again - Ther. getting up to 100.7/10 again - D. Tillinghast came after 4 P.m. + said he thought I was getting along all right - but that was not the way I felt. Eve+ the Hardenburghes + Vanthorns + Maghees were all on hand to enjoy the "d'licious moonlight"
