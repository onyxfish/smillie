## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 14, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fairly pleasant during early part of day but changing later to raw, east wind. I was awake before 5 a.m. with a headache that made me more or less uncomfortable all day. Ralph came home a little after 9 A.M. I went to studio \+ spent most of the m't copying a new Will from rough notes that I made some days ago - about 1 o'clk went to Clausen's \+ ordered a frame for the little Agate painting that I am going to give to K.A. of D. Then to Knoeler's \+ fifth Ave. Art Galleries to see paintings - at the latter gallery I met Dolph who was making himself interesting \+ agreeable by claiming to have painted more than thirty years ago a picture in the collection on exhib \+ signed with the name of Jas. Smillie - He says he did it before he began to paint animals \+ it was first sold in one of his sales - It was unsigned when he sold it. To lunch at 1.30. To studio at 2.30 - painted on an old study painted at Beedes in 1868 - looking south thro' Ausable pass - It is a good study but too hot \+ I took some of the snuffy brown out of it - I intend to work in this way upon the best of my studies so that they may be in better shape to leave behind me - The poorer ones, under instructions in my Will, are to be destroyed. At 5 went to Clausen's gilding shop in W. 37 St. to give instructions about mending a frame that fell from an easel \+ was injured - home after a short walk - dinner at 6.30 - a part of every given to wrestling with Ralph's arithmetic. To bed early -

Rec'd a note from Hedwig Horkler stating that the Cambridge Encyclo. Pub. Co. had written to her to know when I was going to write the final chap. to the new Ed. of Horklers "Etching"

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 15, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night clearing to mild, pleasant day. Wrote letter to Miss Horkler in answer to her note in which she sent me copy of note from Cam. Enc. Pub. Co. enquiring when I wd. finish concluding chap. to Horkler's book on Etching. In my reply I told her of my doubts about the ability of the Co. to re-pub. the work in proper manner. To studio \+ during the m't painted over portions of a study that I painted at Beedes in '69. It had good parts \+ I tried to bring the rest of it up to them - home to lunch - afternoon painted on a study in Ausable gorge at Beede's - 1870. It makes me sick to see that I did good work thirty years ago \+ have not improved since as I should. At 5 o'clk. went to meeting of Directors of Ireland Real Estate Co. at Ed. Cook's house - Mary, Ra., Walker, Ed. \+ I present - Home to dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g, after reading newspapers I went to Charles who has some new Pianola music - Bessie \+ Ralph there - home at 10.30 -

The invalids are getting along slowly
