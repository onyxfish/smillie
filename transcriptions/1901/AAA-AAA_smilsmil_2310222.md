## Left Page

### Fri. March 1, 1901

**Weather:** Dark day threatening rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark day threatening rain — to studio \+ spent the w'f painting on "Passing Showers". Taking much of the coal bk. out of the foreground. Home to lunch at 1 o'clk — back at 2 — wrote a letter to Jim — also notes to Mrs. Ge. Steam \+ to Mr. Adolphe Apenhym, sending them cards for Century exb'n. Home at about 4.30 \+ had a rest before dinner. Ev'y Ralph \+ I went to the Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw "Secret Service", a play that is very popular but is absurdly impossible in fact. That should not trouble me in a play, I know, but it is a defect of my organization. Ralph was amused but did not lose his critical attitude — out at 11.20 \+ to bed tired.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 2, 1901

**Weather:** Rainy m'g, clearing afternoon — fine night — mild weather  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rainy m'g., clearing afternoon — fine night — mild weather. After bkf. did some writing \+ at 10.30 went to 2°. Nat. Bank — made deposit \+ got a loan of $700 so that I may pay Pomfret School bill, Cutler \+ school bill — Cooly (montrose) bill \+ some other arrears. It was about 11.30 when I got to studio \+ I fussed about there until 1 o'clk. Then home to lunch — after that went to U.A.D. etching class — took with me A. T. Agate painting "Coconut Grove \+ Temple — Fiji Islands" \+ wrote note of presentation to be delivered to Council at meeting next Monday ev'g. Found class busy at work, m plates, after model of young girl in costume. I got home at 6 \+ rested until dinner at 6.30. Ev'y to Century at about 8 — My "Rise of the River" was well hung \+ had many paintings \+ comments. Charles \+ Dr. Augustus R. Thayer were there. I remained until near midnight — then walked home with Chas. Collins.
