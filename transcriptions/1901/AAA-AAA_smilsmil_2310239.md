## Left Page

### Thur. April 4, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. Bauck dined with us.

A nasty wet day - I spent the day at the studio worrying over printing. I took pp. from etched plate of father at $15 [illegible], also from Campino - also from the zinc soft ground "Fish Market at Nice." - I worked on the plates \+ I also did some experimental work on small plates - I was tired \+ disheartened when I went home at 5 o'clk. - I lay down on my bed until 6 - then dressed for dinner - Mr. Bauck, with whom Mary \+ the girls lived in Dresden, came to dinner - She is a very bright \+ entertaining woman \+ we had a pleasant eve't notwithstanding drawbacks. Mary had to leave the table, owing to faintness but was able to come to the parlor later in the eve't. Charl dined with us. Mr. B's son, Svim, came later - \+ so did Geo. \+ Nellie.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 5, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, quiet \+ mild - I got to studio about 10 \+ worked all day on soft ground work. I made a sketch on a x plate after my oil study painted on Oct. afternoon on the fair ground at Montrose - this, with a small, experimental plate, I bit in - they were not successes I were improvements upon what I have been doing within the past few days. I gave up work at 4 o'clk \+ went to several of the picture shops on 5th Ave. - until 6 o'clk - then home \+ rested until dinner at 6.30 - Eve't wrote to Jim.
