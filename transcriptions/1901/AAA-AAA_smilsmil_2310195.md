## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 6, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A charming day in the morning growing grey + overcast in the afternoon. It was 10 o'clk. before I got down to bkfs. after that. news paper for a time \- others wrote draft of a letter to Mrs. Kent in reply to her note of Dec. 29 agreeing to take the Montrose property at $7000. About 1 went to Charls for a few minutes, then walked up + down Park Ave. on the sunny side of the way for some time \- then to studio, got dirty brushes + home to dinner at 2 o'clk. About 3.30 I gave myself up to "resting" + continued at that labor till 6 o'clk. Tea at 6.30. Evg wrote letter to Mrs. Kent + at 10 o'clk. Took it to G. Cent. Station + mailed it. Then wrote to H4 W. Sprague, Erie Co. Savings Bank B[illegible], Buffalo, N.Y. + put the ten deeds to The Montrose property m[illegible] \- to mail to him to-morrow m[illegible].

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 7, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim went to New-Haven.

A bleak, overcast m[illegible] \- clearing until at night the weather was fine. A little before 10 I went to R.R. Station with Jim who started for New Haven on the 10.02 Train to visit Katie Elkin for a day + then go on to Pomfret. Then I went to P.O. Station + mailed Montrose deeds to H4 W. Sprague \- then to Murray Hill Theatre + bought two tickets for friday evg. Then to studio + painted on 33×44 \- but I did not work with comfort \- feel unwilling to exert myself mentally or physically. Home to lunch \- wrote a note to Tribune concerning the Hudson River School of landscape painters. In the Sunday issue Cortesy says that the H.R. School was formed on Dusseldorf teaching. I continue that there was no imported virus \- the School \- if it can be so called \- was indigenous \- a proper outgrowth of the time + the soil. To studio at 3 \- worked until near 5 \- then went to Arnold + Constables + bought underclothes. Home to dinner at 6.30. Evening loafed over some reading.
