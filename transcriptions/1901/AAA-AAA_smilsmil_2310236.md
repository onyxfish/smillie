## Left Page

### Fri. March 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, windy + cool — To studio early + to work on two soft ground etchings — one, a repetition of the little pencil sketch of father at the pig farm — 1842 — the other — 7×10 — after a water color sketch — a chapel at Campiano — Lake Maggiore — made in '92. I have the fever on me to do something in the etching line, stimulated by Sterne + the boys in my NA dp. Etching class. I think the figure of father will better represent the original sketch if done on sof gr'd. than the line etching done the other day. I worked very closely all day — made tracings on the tissue paper — laid grounds + made the drawings on the plates — the drawing of father was not a success — so I made another tracing on tissue for a new drawing — At 4 o'clk I went to Rogers + Est. 32d S. + Bdevay about some clothes for Ralph — back to studio at 5 + worked until 6 varnishing plate for biting to-morrow. Then home to dinner — Evett wrote letter to Jim \+ mailed it at Grand Central Station.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 30, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

mabel Cooley ret? home.

A very fine day but high wind — got up with a wretched head ache + thought that I would not be able to get thro' the day — went to studio before 10 + worked up strength to try to bite in the soft ground drawing of the little roadside chapel near Davano — it was a failure — the ground is too hard + had not clung to the tissue paper as it should — I was much disappointed + home about 1 o'clk — Had a nap for about an hour + felt so much better that at 3 o'clk I went to NA dp. — Found Sterne there — the boys had been printing + then left — I found that they had ruined the blankets so that they were cut through in a number of places. I was very angry + told Davis not to let any of them have the key to my printing closet — At 4.30 I started for home, walking thro' the Park to Mad. Ave. + 72d S. — Took car + rode home — Dinner at 6.30 — Evett felt quite recovered from my headache.
