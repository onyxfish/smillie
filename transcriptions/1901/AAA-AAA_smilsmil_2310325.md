## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 27, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day - Webb did not come to work \+ I put in a full day on the Border painting making slow progress. Active work going on through the house, preparing for the change next week. After 4 p.m. I went to Scott Brown's to see that there was no mistake about Ralph \+ self getting refuge there for a few days after the family leaves for N.Y. Scott \+ wife went to Lake Placid this m't to be home again on Sunday or Monday. I saw Miss Pond - then I walked over to W. Openburnis \+ saw there Mr. Adler - also Mr. Felix Adler. Then I walked up the wood road that goes over a shoulder of the mt. \+ out by the Reserve gate - enjoying it very much. Home after 6 - supper — a wonderful moonlight night — singularly brilliant - spent most of the even't in studio reading.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 28, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A really warm day. Webb came to work \+ did a lot of odd jobs about the house. After bkft. Ralph \+ I got a lot of ice from the icehouse into the cooler — then I made preparations to pack cases with bed linen \+ other like things from Montrose to go to N.Y. but I was tempted again to set my palette \+ I worked closely all the m't on Border. Dinner at 1.3 — then I worked at getting things from attic down to north piazza - boxes etc. preparatory to packing - About 3.30 Ralph, Webb \+ I went down to Harry Beede's mill \+ saw the spruce slabs he has - then we went to the McCarthy cottage \+ took from its sides 9 or 10 slabs, much worm eaten under the bark \+ very fine in color. I expect to use them somewhere inside the house for decorative purposes - there to feed Beede's \+ arrange with him to take up new slabs to take the place of those I had taken off \+ bring the old ones to me - Webb to feet up the new ones in [illegible]. On our return met Mr. \+ Mrs. Maghee down in their garden \+ walked up the hill with them - when I got home I felt so used up that I lay down on my bed \+ did not get up until after 4. Mr. \+ Mrs. Maghee - Mr. Strodenburg \+ her grand-daughter Katie came to tea but I was not able to see them.
A change in the weather. over cast \+ strong southerly wind.
