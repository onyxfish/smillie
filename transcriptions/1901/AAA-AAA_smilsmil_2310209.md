## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 3, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant, still mft. clouding up later — snow at night. Bkft. at 9 — after my usual grooming — I read \+ then wrote up acct. and checks for Janf. bills — about noon I went over to Charl's \+ found him with his Trainer going thro' his exercises — then to Century \+ saw pictures by daylight of a poor sort — then a walk \+ home at 1.30 — dinner at 2 — from 3.30 to 6.30 I had a good long sleep \+ made up fully for what I lost last night — then supper — Evet., some more reading — then wrote to Miss Koehler about the interview I had with S.P. Avery last night as to possibility of her getting a place in Print Dept. Pub. Library also wrote to Del Mar — to G.C. Station at 10.30 \+ mailed letter — more writing \+ to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 4, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal day — snow \+ sleet nearly all day — After bkft. I went to Meyrowitz's, 42d. S. near Bdway. to have a broken leg to my spectacles replaced — I bought a pair of eye-glasses \+ it was about 11 when I got to studio. Painted on left hand, foreground co. of "The Rise of the River." I'm afraid that I am "chewing it up" too fine. I would like to be able to treat the whole subject in mass \+ in detail, in a large way — that would be masterly — unfortunately, I am not a master. Home to lunch as usual \+ worked in the afternoon, in spite of darkness until 5 o'clk. A short walk \+ home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Evt. Reading — Mrs. Seton-Thompson's "A woman tenderfoot" which I found to be exceedingly feminine over amusing — I puzzled for a time, also, over acct. of Prescott \+ Son of Keeseville — Rec'd. some days ago. \+ blocked out form of letter to send to them
