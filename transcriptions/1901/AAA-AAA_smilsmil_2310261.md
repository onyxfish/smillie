## Left Page

### Sat. May 18, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the night \+ pretty much all day — sometimes very heavy — Up soon after 6 \+ wrote a number of notes \+ short letters — Bkfst. at 8. Painter came then \+ got studio Keep. — It was about 10 when I got to studio — Westenkampf there all the m'g \+ I assisting him in getting dates for prints. note from Beckwith asking me to go with him to Jacob Schiff's This p.m. Home about 12.30 — lunch — at 1.15 Ralph \+ I went to Mt. Sinai Hospital — Dr. Gerster \+ his assistant were ahead of time — We went to the operating room — little time was lost — R. went under the influence of gas without trouble \+ the adhering prepuce was to be freed back without bloodshed — R. came promptly from under the influence of the gas \+ Dr. Gerster went back to his cigar which he had not yet gone out — we were in the hospital about half an hour — R. \+ I rode home — There he seemed so bright \+ well that I left him \+ went to Beckwith's Studio at 2.30 — J. Q. Brown soon came in \+ we went to Fifth Ave — took a cab \+ rode to Mr. Jacob Schiff's — No. — He met us very cordially \+ after a little conversation consulted to waive his condition \+ make his gift of $5000 to the N.A.D. at once — He then showed us his pictures, some of which are valuable — We left at about 3.40. I then went to Baycodorfer's about my watch \+ then to studio where I found Westenkampf at work — I was with him until 6.30 — then home to dinner — every at home — Rather stupied with headache \+ aching eyes —

## Right Page

### Sun. May 19, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very Raw, dark day — no rain but lots of threats — Sometimes almost enough to wet the walks. I had bkfst — at 9 — newspaper \+ some work on acct until near 12 — Then Ralph \+ I went out \+ roamed about the streets until 1 — then to Charl's — There for about half an hour — then home to dinner after going to Third Ave. and buying three pints of Ice Cream \+ taking it with us — After dinner, some more newspaper — then feeling heavy I lay down upon my bed \+ slept until 6.15 — it seems to me that I'm doing a lot of sleeping lately — Geo \+ Charl came in — tea at 6.30 — Tues'f. at 8 o'clk. went to Emma Ireland's to bid her good-bye thinking she was to sail for Bremen on Tues — She is not to sail until the 28th — the Tues. following — but I had a very pleasant visit instead of a parting. Home at 10.15
