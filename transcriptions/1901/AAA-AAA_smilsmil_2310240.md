## Left Page

### Sat. April 6, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beastly day of high wind \+ very heavy rain \- I had intended to take an entire holiday but the weather prevented. I went to studio before to and spent the m'g experimenting in soft-ground work without much success. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- at 3 I went to Mendelssohn Hall. 40th St. near 6th Ave. \- Farewell Recital Mr. \+ Mrs. Henschel. \- Senna Ireland, to whom I had given a ticket some times ago, was in her seat. It is just about twenty years since Annie got tickets \+ took me with her to a Henschel Recital in Steinway Hall in 14th St. \- shortly after we were engaged. \- I remember distinctly her pleasure then \- some of the pieces sung then were on the programme to-day \- I can hardly know whether pain or pleasure was uppermost. Their voices, some, seem to have suffered little \- Henschel's voice was never wholly agreeable to me, but both have an immense amount of "go" \- \+ much fine art. Henschel sang with great dramatic power. The Erkling by Loewe \- I could not control the tears \- it was the same when first H. sang so sweetly the Schubert "the Bird" \- Heine's words. "I stood before her picture" to \- "And ah I cannot believe it, Not indeed thou art lost to me." After the concert, altho' the wind was driving \+ the rain was pelting, we walked to 42nd St. \+ did some shopping \- then to Mad. Ave. where I got into a car. Dinner at 6.30 \- Every quietly at home \- worrying over accts. until 11 o'clk \- This is Cautting night.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 7, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Continued disagreeable weather. \- light rain in the m'g \- better in the middle of the day \+ an attempt at clearing at night. \- Bills at 9.30 \- then my newspaper \- at noon went to Charles for a little while \- then to the Century \+ saw the pictures \- Two by H.R. Butler interested me more than the others. \- Then a short walk. \+ home to dinner at 2 \- After that worked at my accts. until 4 \- then gave up to a feeling of heaviness \+ slept until 6.30 \- then tea \- We\+ at 8 went to 139 Mad. Ave. \- \+ made a call on Miss Kent of Buffalo who is there for a few days with her younger sister who is here for treatment of her eyes \- At 9 o'clk. I went to Charles where Katharine, Mary \+ Ralph were \- also the Sheldon \+ Val \- later Kate \+ Bessie came in, making a family party \- Home before 10.30 \- wrote a note to Mr. McLean (W.P.) 330 E. 15th St. concerning some life modes of whom she has written to me.
