## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 21, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katharine \+ Elie[illegible] went to New Haven \- \} Emma Bourne left.

A very fine day \- To studio at 9.30 \+ spent another day painting on Borden Library \- At 12.30 went to Allen's \- the mason, Cor. 46' S'. \+ 7' Ave. to hurry him up \- to dinner at 1 \- \+ to studio at 2.30 \+ painted till 4.30 then to Wanamaker's \+ looked at Rugs \- I selected two to be sent to my house on trial \- I want to see how they will look in my bed Room \- Home at 6.30 \- Mary \+ I sat down to tea alone \- a lonesome house \- at 8 we went to Charl's, back at 9.30

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 22, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate came from Po'k \- \+ returned \-

Mild pleasant day \- Allen, the mason, came before I had finished breakfast \- \+ by that I got to studio rather earlier than usual \- Painted all day on Borden Library \- Had a half hours walk before lunch at 1 \- To studio soon after 2 \- Could not see to paint after 4.15 This work is a very severe trial to my eyes \- also to my nerves \- The uninteresting exactness of it wearies me exceedingly \- a short walk before supper \- After that wrote a letter to Ralph \- At 10 went to Charl's for a few minutes \-

Kate came from Po'k this m'g \- lunched with Mary \+ me \+ ret to Pok by afternoon train.
