## Left Page

### Thur. June 27, 1901

**Weather:** Hotter yet  **Temp:** got up into the 90°s

I got to studio about 10 after writing to Miss Kent. I worked all the way at studio laying out the many things I am to take with me \+ putting other things away \+ by 12.30 had things in an orderly shape. Lunch — \+ a rest — again to studio \+ worked till 5 o'clk. getting almost through with selecting \+ packing. Then home — at dinner felt the effects of heat \+ tiring very much, so that I lay on my bed until 9 o'clk. Josephine B. Smillie called in the evef. but I did not see her. Charl spent most of the evef. at the house. I wrote a letter to Jim after 9 o'clk. \+ sent him a Postal order for $20 — mailed it at the G. C. Station.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 28, 1901

**Weather:** The hottest day yet  **Temp:** getting up to 96°

In my bedroom it got to 92°. \+ last night, in spite of the pleasant breeze it did not fall below 83°. After bkf. went to Dr. Celley's with a note — then to Wanamakers \+ saw W. West Mr. Post \+ other dept. men \+ was promised that my goods should be shippped all together on Monday. Then to studio \+ finished my gathering together \+ packing. Lunch at 1. Afternoon at home packing the big box that is to be sent to Keene — About 5 went to Owen's \+ then to studio \+ carried home the prep. to be packed in big box. Dinner at 6.30 — We'f. Charl came in \+ I loafed until 10 o'clk.
