## Left Page

### Fri. April 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katharine went to Hospital.

At last a fine day after almost three weeks of unpleasant weather. I went up to Mr. Borden's immediately after breakfast \+ saw him \- showed him sketches but he made no comments \- but said \- go ahead \- Price is no object \- when I said that I would ask $300 he said Price was a secondary matter \- what he wanted was the very best I could do \- I hope that I shall be able to please him \- I worked until near noon \+ made two sketches \- one in library one in gallery \- then to studio \+ worked over them with pen \+ ink until 1.30 \- a young woman called to see if I wd give her some lessons in water color work \- from Margaret Parker White \- sent her to Geo. Then home to lunch. To studio after that \+ worked on sketches until after 4 \- then home \+ wrote letter to Jim \-

Bessie went with Katharine to Hannemann Hospital soon after 6 o'clk. K\- is to have have for an operation this afternoon \- Her fellow thumb has not healed properly \+ it is to be cut again \+ sewed to get it into proper shape \- Bessie left about 6 \+ reported that the operation had been successfully performed \+ she had left K\- there to stay over night in charge of nurses to recover from the effects of the chloroform \-

Dinner at 6.30\- Evey Ralph \+ I went to the Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw The White Horse Tavern \- altho' there was much to be desired, in the acting, we found it amusing.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 27, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ beautiful day. I got to studio at 9.30 \+ spent the m'f working on sketches made in W. Borden's library \- N.Y. Lewis called to inquire abt Camp Choconet \- she thinks of sending her boy there this summer \- Home to lunch at 1 \- found Katharine there \- just in from her day at the Hannemann Hospital bringing her sore thumb with her. She has had a very interesting experience \+ is in good spirits \- After lunch I went to N.A. to Etching class \- A number of things combined to make it depressing to me \- I am discouraged by the sloppy \- shiftless\- unthinking way in which much of the work is done by the boys. I arranged with them to commence on their competitive work next week. Ralph met me at the school at 5 o'clk. \+ we walked down to Riverside drive \+ south to about 80th St. there we took 3rd Ave. cars \+ got home to dinner a little late \- Evey I was stupid \+ headachy \+ loafed over my newspapers.
