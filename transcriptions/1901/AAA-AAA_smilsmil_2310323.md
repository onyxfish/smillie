## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 23, 1901

**Weather:** Warm \+ cloudless with strong S.W. wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pic nic party of the entire family got away soon after 9 under the personal direction of Messrs Maghee \+ Warren Hardenburgh for a days picnic at Mr. Hardenburgh's cottage down the valley \- I remained at home to look after Webb \+ Adams, who made his appearance to-day. Bessie \+ Ralph went on their wheels \- the others went in W. Maghee's vehicles. I had hoped to be able to paint on the Borden, but with starting Adams \+ getting Webb going on alcove finish \+ writing to Jim, \+ James \+ Douglas, I had a very busy day \+ never set my palette. I had my dinner solus \+ then went to the hotel with my letters \- The pic nickers returned before 6 \+ reported having had a glorious time \- Tea at 6.30 \- Eve'f on south piazzas enjoying a dreamy moonlight night \+ to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 24, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Peculiar weather \- strange clouds \+ wind from N.W. in the m'f, at times looking very portentous \- but gradually the sky cleared \+ the afternoon \+ night were very beautiful but rather cold. Both Webb \+ Adams at work to-day \+ much of my time given to them \- I got a boy from the attic \+ packed into it blankets, bed clothes, towels etc for Jim at New-Haven. I wrote him a letter in answer to the one rec- yesterday \+ sent him a check for $50 \- towards furnishing his room in Pierson Hall. My time was so entirely taken up by these various matters that I got no chance to paint at all. I went to haul aunt Charl after dinner \- Eve'f Maghee \+ Warren Hardenburgh came in for awhiles bringing Katharine \+ Elisabeth.
