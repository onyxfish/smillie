## Left Page

### Fri. June 21, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dr. Cilley came in the mtf. \+ found that I had passed a quiet night from midnight till 5 o'clk. with succeeding naps — also, a goodly quantity of urine of satisfactory appearance. at which he said he was gratified. He ordered a continuance of gruel diet with lots to drink. I spent the day rolling in bed. I spent the day thrashing my bed with occasional relief of the bowels —
Dr. came again in the evening \+ I had a very pleasant hour or more with him, talking of education (Jim) \+ many other things. He impresses me very agreeably. After that he had a visit with other members of the family — developing several lines of mutual acquaintance — Charl has known his father very pleasantly at the Union League for some years.

for treatment to-day I had Citrate of Mag. in the mtf \+ again in the afternoon. The nurse was kept fairly busy.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 22, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day. Doctor Cilley came in the mtf \+ expressed himself as well pleased with the report of the nurse \+ after giving me some general directions together with permission to get up in the afternoon \+ be dressed like a Christian he dismissed himself \+ the nurse — dismissal of the latter to take effect this afternoon. After my mid-day gruel I got up \+ was dressed \+ went into Mary's room \+ about the middle of the afternoon Miss Elisabeth Stewart took her departure \+ again I was a free man. I had my every meal in Mary's room \+ sat there with the family till bed time.
