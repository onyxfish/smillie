## Left Page

### Tues. July 23, 1901

*Pine Knoll — Keane Heights.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to Albany on time at 6.30 — had a very hurried bkfst. in R.R. Station Restaurant \+ got away for Westport on 7 o'clk train. The weather was delightful — bright \+ breezy \+ the run to Westport was very pleasant — Got there a little behind time — about 11.20 — after some delay we were started in the stage for Elizabethtown \+ got there soon after 1 o'clk. \+ had dinner. At a little after 2 we started for Keene Heights — There were but three others, ladies, a mother \+ two daughters. We had a very pleasant \+ comfortable ride — The ladies stopped at the Tahawas House — I was much surprised on getting to the foot of our hill to find that a veritable cloud burst on Wednesday of last week had so torn up the road on the hill as to make it impassable — gullies were cut in some places ten feet deep — It was completely wrecked — we walked up the hill the stage went around — got to the house about 6 — expected only in a general way. We found all well \+ were very glad to get home. We spent a quiet evening \+ got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 24, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at about 7 after a grand night's rest — the mts was lovely — fresh \+ breezy — but clouds came up later with strong cold wind \+ before eve'g me rain came — I found much of general character to do about the place — Mr. \+ Mrs. Openhym. Alex Schilling, Mr. Maghee \+ the Misses Bor called \+ the mt got away from me before I knew it. After dinner there was unpacking of trunk \+ the big box that I sent from 38th St. in N.Y. — Eve'g I spent with newspapers \+ to bed at 10.
