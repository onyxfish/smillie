## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph went to visit the cuttings at Oakdale. L.I.

Left at 6 o'clk. — as dismal a m\+ as could be well imagined — dark \+ rain falling steadily — Ralph got up \+ was ready for his b\+cp\+ \+ a start by 7 — we got away from the house \+ to the foot of 34th St. a little before 8 \+ there met Mr. Cutting's housemaid who at once took Ralph in charge — I got to studio a little after 8 \+ for a time was arranging Prints \+ studies in drawers \+ on shelves — Then I spent the rest of the m\+ writing a revised will from notes of the chances that I have decided to make from my will of five years ago — Home to lunch at 1 — The storm had cleared away \+ it was a breezy, cloudy day — Went to N.A. \+ met etching class. Plates that had been ordered had not come \+ the big ground Roller that one of the students had brought at Seeler's did not work satisfactorily — I laid a number of grounds on half plates \+ left before 4 o'clk — Went to Beckwith's found him painting a portrait of W. Wilkinson of Pbk — he made appointment to call at my House to-morrow ev'g — Then to H.R. Bullen's not in — Then Home — Dressed \+ at 7 went to Edw Cook's — her friends, invited, could not come — but we had Clarence Smith, Catharine Cook's fiancée — Tom Cook \+ his friend Thurston — Mary Cook read me along letter from Carleton who is now in the South of France — Home soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 13, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day altho' rather cold \+ windy — I got up at 8 — \+ got down to my d\+p\+ before 10 — while at the table Dielman called with two copies of a letter to Carnegie signed by himself, J.G. Brown, J.C. Nicoll \+ J. Carroll Beckwith — it been again recast from my recast of H.R. Butler's note \+ is now in excellent shape I think — I signed it without hesitation. I wrote to H\+ Sprague promising to send to Recorder of Deeds at Montrose for abstract of my title — After 12 I went out for a short walk — went in to Charl's — Then to Century \+ saw pictures there — Last night was Annual Meeting which (to my disgust now) I forgot while I was spending the ev'g at Edw Cook's — Dinner at 2 — Afternoon G\+o. \+ Charl came in — I wrote to W.H. Warner at Montrose — at 6 Beckwith came \+ we had a few minutes talk concerning H.A. matters and the new attack on Carnegie — He told me some things that were very reassuring to me. Tea at 6.30 — a poky even alone —

Mary \+ Kate still in their beds —
