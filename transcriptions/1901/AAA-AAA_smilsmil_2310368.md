## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 24, 1901

**Weather:** Bright, pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, pleasant day — The day at the studio putting the last touches on the Borden plate. I think I could spend a good deal more time putting on finishing touches — At 4 o'clk I went to Z. C. Stedman's — 115 E. 16' S'. \+ found him in an excited state, as it seemed to me — perhaps not quite recovered from the New-England Socy. dinner of last night. we talked a great deal till after 5.30 — a very little of it directly concerned the business in hand — The etching of a portrait of ex-gov. Morton after a painting by Bonnat. for a book to be publ. by the Stock Exchange Historical Socy. Stedman being editor in chief. I am to see him again next monday at 12 — downtown — walked home dinner at 6.30 — Every went up to D. Gester's 34 E. 75' S'. \+ left painting — Study painted at Seaof-mass. — more than 30 years ago — Stony clouds \+ sunset — then walked down to Yale's \+ left there proof of etching — Father. Po'keepsie. 1842 — Men home, having walked a little more than four miles since 4.30 —

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 25, 1901

**Weather:** Disagreeable day — overcast, damp  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — overcast, damp \+ raw — rain at night — owing to Castor oil trouble my sleep was much disturbed last night \+ I did not get up to my h'lp. till after 9 o'clk. By this arrangement I was spared seeing the dealing of Xmas present h'lp. which always, nowadays, affects me unpleasantly — After my h'lp. I went to studio \+ worked on Borden plate till almost 2 o'clk — then hurried to Charl's who has a family dinner party to-day. Ac°, wife \+ three boys were there — Mary \+ her two girls — Kate — Walter Pierce, wife \+ Boy \+ Josephine (Will's wife.) my two boys \+ my own self were there — It was a very elaborate function \+ seemed much enjoyed by all — we were at table about three hours — after that Charl played piano \+ organ for us — It was after 8 before we got home — I wrote a short letter to Scott Brown enclosing check for taxes, 1901, \+ mailed it — then to bed —
