## Left Page

### Thur. May 16, 1901

Mary, Kathanie & Bessie went to Rockmore.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — Mary + her girls got away to Rockmore — a place in Ulster Co — for a two weeks vacation, I wish I could go too — Got a note from J.H. Maghee, just home from Europe, asking me to lunch with him at the Century on Sat. at 1 — I replied that my engagement at Mt. Sinai Hospital w— prevent — Then to studio — Carpenter there — I left him before 11 + went to County Clerk's office in City Hall park + had a Certificate attached to Deed for Mr. Kent — Then I got some hardware for studio + to studio at 12.20 — Home to lunch at 1.30 — It was 4 before again I got to studio — there till almost 6 — Then strolling awhile on Park Ave. before dinner — a quiet table — Kate, Ralph + myself — We f went to Charl's for a little while — Wrote to Lattraps + Titsworth, sending them Fire Ins. policies to be transferred to Mr. Kent.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 17, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — after bkfst. wrote letter to Mr. Kent + then mailed it to her with the deed for the Montrose property — Weitenkampf came just as I finished — Then to studio where he joined me + spent the m— writing cat. of Father's proofs. The carpenter finished his work in the dark room — I then went to Douglas, the Painter — Home to lunch at 1.30 — Rec'd. from Strong, Sturgis & Co — brokers, acct. of recent sales + purchases — 118 shares, pref. Am. Smelters — 100 shares U.S. Steel + check for $261 ? After lunch went to bank — deposited check + put shares in safe deposit — Then to studio Painter came from Douglas + stained wall in dark-room a deep brown. Weitenkampf came later — Painter + W— worked until 5 — I left them + went home + wrote letter to Jim — Had a half hour rest before dinner — We f went with Ralph to Murray Hill Theatre + saw an amusing farce, "A Tejan Steer". Parts were bright + parts were funny — Allen did well as Maverick Brander + was really the life of the play by her vivacious acting — she is pretty in spite of an ugly shaped mouth — Altogether, I thought it more amusing than the more select + pretentious presentation of Capt. Jinks by Ethel Barrymore.
A heavy dash of rain just after we got in from the Theatre
