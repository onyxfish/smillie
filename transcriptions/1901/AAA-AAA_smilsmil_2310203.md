## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 22, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Queen Victoria died at 6.30 P.m.

A very fine day - bright \+ mild - to studio before 10 \+ got to work on "Passing Showers. -Evening". I made up my mind that a radical change was necessary - so - altho' it took some courage, I pitched into it \+ made radical changes in color and drawing of the clouds. I worked very hard all day \+ was very much discouraged. I went to Friedrichs for paints at noon \+ then to lunch - back at 2.30 \+ painted as long as I could see - about 5 o'clk. Ralph came \+ I went with him to Alexander's \+ got a pair of dress shoes for him - Home to dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g. I wrote notes to W. N. Barnes, Montrose \+ to Mrs W. P. McBean who wrote an article in Sunday's Tribune illustrated by the picturesque head of an old jew beggar - I wrote to the Mr. about it as I want to get hold of him for a model - my note was sent to Mrs. McB. - \+ she wrote me that she is trying to find him.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 23, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - cooler than yesterday \+ more windy. After bkft. I went to N.A. of D. \+ spent the m'g with etching class - printing. walked across the Park to Mad. Ave. \+ 102d. St. \+ took car - out at 48th St., went to Medrich's \+ got a lot of oil colors - To lunch at 1.45 - To studio at 2.30 and painted on Passing Showers, repainting solidly the right hand end hills \+ water - after 5. I went to 135 W. 47th St. \+ called on H. B. Butler who has been at home, sick, for the past nine days - had a talk with him about Carnegie \+ N.A. of D. He knows nothing of what Dielmann has done recently \+ could tell nothing of C's attitude - Home to dinner at 6.30 - eve'g. feeling heavy with sleep \+ tired. I lay down on my bed at 8.30 \+ slept until 10 - then I got up \+ wrote along letter to Miss Koehler - to bed at 12.
