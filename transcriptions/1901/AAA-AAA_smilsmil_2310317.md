## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 11, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A showery day. I took advantage of it + spent most of the day working on the terrace north of the house — raking it over — seeding it to grass + then tamping it with a board. Two men (Jacquith + assistant) commenced work on the culvert that runs under my Road as it enters the public Road — this is to be entirely relaid + regraded to conform to the new Public Road + make a good turn at the corner. The rain interfered with their work. Webb continued his work on the attic store Room. Miss Ogilvie got up at 11 A.M. — cooked her own bkf. (as previously arranged) + started with O.in Beede for Port Henry at 3 o'clk. The family were not up at that time. Evelf. I sat in her bed room + Read —

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very wet day, but rain did not begin to fall until about 10 a.m. — from that time till late in the afternoon it fell almost continuously — about 5 there were signs of clearing + later many stars were to be seen but the temperature continued mild. I made fire in studio stove to dry the atmos. phere rather than to warm it. I wrote a long letter to Mary Cook. There were little chores etc that I did about the house. Webb did not come + if the men did any work on the culvert at entrance to my road it must have been very little. Before 6 my stomach gave me trouble \+ I went without my supper. Evelf. about 8 I went down to main Room + was with the family until about 10 — gent. chatting + telling of stories.
