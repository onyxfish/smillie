## Left Page

### Wed. April 10, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very little sunshine to-day — some high wind. After bkfs. went to N.A. f.s. — Etching class disappointed by its Model, an Indian girl — still hunting a model when I got there — got one of the students from the Antique class. As it was not necessary for me to stay longer I left soon after 11 — Stopped at Pan. Inn. Hit Deb., Sherwood Bel. — then to studio before 12 — worked on plate bitten yesterday — Home to lunch at 1 — then to studio and worked until near 5 finishing face with light dry-point — then walked down to Union Sq. \+ paid Fussell his bill — then up Fifth Ave. Met Mr. Sprague, Mary Cook \+ Payot Coffin at 38 St. — walked home with Mr. Sprague to Ed. Cook's — then home — dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g., after my newspaper, a little writing [illegible — ~3 words]

## Right Page

### Thur. April 11, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ breezy — to studio about 9 o'clk \+ worked until 12.30 on portrait of Father, 1825 — with graver \+ dry-point until 12.30 — then, feeling stale, I went out for a breezing to 38 St. \+ East River \+ along the docks to 42 St. taking note of things — then home to lunch — Again to studio \+ worked until 5 — got ready for printing to-morrow — Walked down Lex. Ave. — met Walter Cook — turned \+ walked with him — Congratulated him on engagement of Sallie Cook to [illegible] — also spoke to him of my idea of selling the holdings of the Ireland Real Estate Co. \+ reinvesting in lots in the annexed district \+ putting up first class apartment houses. Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g. with Ralph — went to Charl's "dark-room" \+ developed a roll of R's films — Home at 10.30.
