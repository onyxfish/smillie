## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 8, 1901

**Weather:** Pleasant day \- growing colder toward eve'g \+ wind changing to east  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 after I had written a note to Mrs. J. Frank Brownell whose husband died yesterday \- I painted on 33×44 until 12.30, then went to 2° Nat'l Bank \+ home to lunch at 1 45 after stopping at various places to see pictures \+ prints. It was near 3 when I again got to studio \+ painted until near 5 o'clk. getting in a good slice of foreground in R.h. corner \- It takes time for me to go over the canvas as I study out the detail very carefully as I go along \- After my work walked down to the picture camen, Illeck, \+ paid him bill of $2.00 Then to Alexander's \+ ordered a pair of shoes \- Home at 6 \- dinner \- Eve'g Charl came in \+ There was "gabbin" until near 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 9, 1901

**Weather:** Overcast, with a raw mildness in the air.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Immediately after bkfst. I went to the Na of D. \+ was with my Etching class until 12.30 teaching them how to print \- I worked at the press most of the time \- then gave each one a chance to take a print of his plate \- then I walked across the Park to 105 St. \+ took Mad. ave. car home getting to lunch at 1 45 \- To studio at 2.30 \+ painted on foreground 33×44 until 4.30 \- at 5 went down to Arnold \+ Cox \- Fable's about my underclothes \- Home to dinner \- Eve'g wrote to Hy Sprague in Kelsey to his request for abstract of title to Montrose property \- Kate is in bed today with "grip."
