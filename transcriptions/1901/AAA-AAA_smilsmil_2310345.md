## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 6, 1901

**Weather:** Fine day. clear & quite cool.  **Temp:**

To studio before 9.30 & to work biting Borden plate
\- at 1 went to bank & deposited
Steel div. check - on my way home had
a peculiar & quite indescribable mental
disturbance or confusion, thinking of my
work & getting it mixed up with auto-
mobiles passing in the street. Naturally
it made me feel very uncomfortable
\+ I did not get straightened out all day
I could not help wondering if my kidneys
were at the bottom of the trouble - They
have been unusually active for the last
two days - To studio after lunch but
I could not concentrate my mind on
my work as is now especially necessary
so I gave it up soon after 3 o'clk. & walked
up to Central Park - about there, & back -
doing more than three miles without fatigue
but feeling cold all the time - home at 5.30
\+ lay on my bed until dinner at 6.30 -
Eve'g Took Mary to Murray Hill Theatre
\- Hay to "The Soldier" — nothing that interested
me in that I could enjoy - one of Hayto's
earlier works, I believe. Not up to such
level as he afterwards established.
To bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 7, 1901

**Weather:** A fine day.  **Temp:**

at 9 went to Dr. Alley's
\+ took specimen of my urine. he asks
for another to-morrow m'g. Then to
studio & continued the biting of Borden
plate till 12.30 - Fred Stahr's brother
brought me three of Fred's plates for use
in Pencil & Brush publication. Went
to 2° Nat. Bk & home to lunch at
1.30 men at work in my bedroom -
coloring ceiling cove & fixing floor.
To studio at 2.30 & worked till 4.30
I am getting along with the biting
very slowly. Then went to Central
Iron Works - 420 E. 48th St. about a
grating to cesspool, Sniffler Court.
Then walked the streets till near 6.30
Home to dinner - Many dined with
Charl - Eve'g with newspaper &
making checks, etc.
