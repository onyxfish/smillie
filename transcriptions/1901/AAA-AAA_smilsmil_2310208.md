## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 1, 1901

**Weather:** Cold day — partly cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold day — partly cloudy — Spent most at the studio doing little else than get my two paintings "Passing Showers" & "After Showers" ready for Century carmen who came \+ got them while I was home at lunch. Back to studio at 2.30 \+ looking at "The Rise of the River" I saw what could be done much to the betterment of the trailing mists and cloud effects, so I set palette at once \+ painted all the afternoon \+ I think I did better it very much. At 4.30 I went to Century \+ got some Visitors cards for Galler \+ sent one to Emma Ireland — home at 5.30 \+ rested until 6.30 — dinner — Evelf Charl came in for a while — he left after 9 — Then I finished my letter to Jim \+ at 10.30 took it to G\. Central Station — to-day rec\. pleasant letter from Fanny Wright Davis \+ photo\. of her five children — one girl \+ four boys — from 8½ to 18½ years — all healthy, handsome looking children — She is living at Colorado Springs.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 2, 1901

**Weather:** A fine winter day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine winter day. Spent the most at studio painting "the Rise of the River". I painted in a group of Arbor vitae leaning over \+ covering a portion of the near cliff on the right hand side — bettering the composition I think — also painted off the top of pine tree crowning foreground of left hand side and decided upon other Radical changes for same group — Ralph came in about 12 having been to the scene of the first Ave. fire with his camera \+ had aid from the firemen there but at last the policeman drove him off — To lunch at [illegible] — then to N.A. \+ had Ch-[illegible] Stitching class — Soft ground work from Mediane long feather — Awaited a lot of last summers work — Home at 5.15 \+ rested until dinner — Evelf went to Century — very anxious to see how my two shower pictures looked among other pictures — I was pleased to get the congratulations of several of the painters but did not fail to note that the warmest came from the del men — G\. Hall was especially enthusiastic over "After Showers" \+ T.W. Wood came up \+ shook my hand \+ was warm in his commendation — Gifford \+ Tiffany were commendatory \+ several laymen were equally kind — The paintings showed me very plainly some of their shortcomings, some of which I hope to correct — I got into confab with Chas Collins \+ was kept so late that it was near 1 o'clk. where I got home — Collins \+ I walked together.
