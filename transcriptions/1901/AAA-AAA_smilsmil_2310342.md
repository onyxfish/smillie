## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 31, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wonderful weather still continues — foggy m'f clearing mild. To studio at 9.30. worked on Borden etching till 12.30 — then sauntered on Park Ave till 1 — then lunch — to studio's soon after 2 \+ etched till 4.30 — then to Scribner's \+ saw Mr. H[illegible]. Smith \+ reported progress on Borden plate — then to Wunderlich's \+ saw some Rembrandt etchings \+ to Clausen's — Home about 6. dinner. Even with newspaper \+ fixing up my bank acct. Went to Charlie's for a few minutes.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 1, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At last, a change in the weather — dull m'f — a little rain after 1 o'clk. barely enough to wet the pavements — then clearing again — back to good weather. clear, starlight night. Steene came while I was at b[illegible]. with p[illegible]. from some plates that he etched last summer — to studio \+ worked on Borden Etching — I had hoped to finish the laying in of lines to-day — but there was not light enough. To lunch at 1. The new man, taking Jennings' place, came on to-day — When I could no longer see to etch I made some progress in clearing up my acid room preparatory to the biting of Borden plate — Sauntered about the streets for about an hour — home at 6 — dinner. Evey wrote notes to Louis Tiffany — Mrs. Beckwith \+ parents of J. Stahn about his etched plates.
