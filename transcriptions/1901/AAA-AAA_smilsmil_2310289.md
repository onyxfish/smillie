## Left Page

### Wed. July 17, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm, stickily unpleasant m'f. - soon after 1 p.m. heavy thunder showers passed over us \+ the p.m. continued overcast but there was no change in humidity which seemed to be very near the point of saturation. I went to K's soon after 9 \+ worked over boxes insides till near 1 - then to dinner - back about 2 taking Mr. Pennypacker with me \+ showed him portions of house \+ grounds. Then worked with Radeker \+ ass't until 6 o'clk. but could not get quite finished. Had a very short rest before tea time. Evelf. some reading \+ to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 18, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hazy most of the day \+ hot - pleasant breeze. I went to work at the K's after I had been down town \+ arranged with Pope for carting my goods to R.R. Station - \+ to station where I arranged to have them at once transferred to a car to run through to Westport - Got to K's at about 10 - Radeker came about 11 - \+ by noon I had everything packed \+ nailed up except one box of fragile things. - Then I went to Sayres \+ rested until dinner. At 2 - again to K's - Pope's team there loading up - Two' a mistake - Two teams had been there at 1. but got nothing - The mistake was mine \+ by it only two loads were taken to station - the third being left till to-morrow. I waited at K's until 6 - then to Su Evelf went down town \+ settled some small bills. Later I read Kipling until 10 - then to bed.
