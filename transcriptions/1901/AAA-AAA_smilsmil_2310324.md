## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 25, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sister Kate \+ Mollie Adams left.

Superb day, brilliantly clear \+ almost cold. I lighted my bedroom fire about 6 o'clk. \+ before bkfst. wrote a short note to Smma Ireland who is due to arrive to-day on the Crown-Prince William. Bkfst. was a little hurried. Ferd. Beede came at 7. 30- by mistake an hour ahead of time. Kate had her bkfst. - then drove over to the VanHorns where all W. Vautt - \+ young Mrs Vautt - joined her - we all went down to the gate \+ bid them good bye as they started for Westport. Then back to the house. Webb finished the alcove in main Room its appearance disappoints me - I cannot determine just where the defect lies - I set my palette \+ worked on the Borden quite persistently but progress is very slow, there is so much exacting detail. Shurtliff, his wife \+ her sister called in the afternoon. Mollie Adams started for Westport at 5 p.m. to take the night train for N.Y. Tea at 6.30. Ev'y all hands went over to Mathees to a camp fire, leaving Ralph \+ me to Keep house - I read newspaper and dozed in my chair.

Adams, the painters, did not come to-day.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day after a very cold night. A very lame stomach all day kept me from active exercise but I made up for it by sticking closely to my work on Borden painting - which, in spite of my efforts, progresses very slowly. Webb did not come to-day - nor did Adams, so I had a quiet time.

Ev'y, after reading newspaper got to bed early.
