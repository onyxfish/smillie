## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 7, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another hot day. Webb on hand \+ I had trouble to keep him going on odd jobs. He made a small box for me \+ I packed my poor, broken watch in it. \+ to keep it company I put in, at Webb's request, a big turnip of a time keeper that he said was ailing. I went to hotel and started the box on its way to Bayerdorfer, 410, 4th ave. N.Y. by Exp. Then to blacksmith's \+ ordered irons to hold up laundry sashes. I met at the hotel, Mr. Fisher \+ daughters, ladies whose acquaintance Charl had made on the train coming here. He took a great fancy to them \+ took Mary over there to introduce her \+ he then introduced me. I went to Scott Brown's \+ saw him after I had seen Otis \+ talked with him about building up stone or timber protection to keep the said bank on new road from caving down. Dinner at 1.30 — afternoon from 3 to 6 worked with Otis setting out tent Cedars about the house. Evey. K. \+ E. \+ Jim went to the Meagher camp — a lean-to shed built in the woods near M's house \+ there with Miss Van Horn they spent the night sleeping out of doors \+ thinking they were having a good time. A strong wind from the north arose \+ the Ther fell to near 50°. I read until near 10.

Much excitement over news that McKinley has been shot.

I feel very well this m'g \+ my bowels behaved in a more natural way than for a month past but in the afternoon my stomach went on a sort of a strike \+ feel very uncomfortable.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 8, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliantly clear, almost cold, day. I got up still feeling much of the discomfort of yesterday. The high wind of last night had disturbed me considerably. Bkfs. at 9. I had a comfortable fire in my bedroom \+ much of my m'g was spent there \+ altho' there were several letters that I should write I did not write them but lay on my bed. The campers got home before 7 o'clk. having been refreshed by hot gin \+ insisted, of course, that they had passed a comfortable night. Dinner at 2 — I loafed about the house during the afternoon \+ did a little reading \+ a little writing. Soon after 5 I strolled over the northern, wooded side of "Windy Brow" with Charl \+ Ralph. Tea at 6.30. Charl dined at the hotel with Mr. Fisher \+ her daughters. My head ached \+ I lounged in big chair before a fine fire until about 9 o'clk — then to my room.
