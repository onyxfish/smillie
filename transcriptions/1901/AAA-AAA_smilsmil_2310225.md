## Left Page

### Thur. March 7, 1901

**Weather:** Cold \+ windy \- growing milder \+ quieter at evef.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 \- Saml. Colman made a pleasant call about 11 o'clk. With exception of that time I spent the m'f preparing for experimental soft ground etching \- "Fish Market, Nice". I made Tracing \+ re-Tracing in Red on tissue paper \- then cut cardboard to fit plate \+ stretched the tissue on that \- then spent some time cleaning zinc plates \+ at last, laid soft ground on plate \- It vexed me that so much time seemed to be wasted in preparation \- such a thing should be done off hand \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- gave a turtle there, Katharine \+ Bessie at medoators \- K. had her thumb lanced for a felon \- To Studio at 2.30 \- Commenced to draw subject on tissue but soon discovered that the ground was too thick, so I cleared it off \+ laid a thinner one \+ then Re-commenced the drawing \- Interrupted by young man from Mail \+ [illegible] wanting information about my Etching class at the [illegible] \- after half an hour I got at my drawing again \+ finished it \- I find that as the plate gets colder the grd. does not come up so easily \- If I could keeps it quietly warmed the tissue w'd. Take up the ground more evenly \- Then I set to work getting my acid room in order \+ found that the wooden bottom of tray or sink had spet \+ leaked badly \- I worked until near 6 trying to fix that \- I had a half hours walk on Park Ave. then home to dinner \- Evef. ## Right Page

### Fri. March 8, 1901

**Weather:** Overcast \+ milder \- To studio at 9.30 \- had to get leaky sink in acid room fixed before I could begin work biting soft ground plate \- Fish Market \- Nice \-  **Temp:** [illegible]

The work went well until I carelessly let my acid get too hot \- Result was closing up of smaller openings thro' the ground \- so that only the larger ones bit, spoiling the character of the grain \- I cleaned off ground \+ at 1 went out \+ walked on Park Ave. for half an hours \- then to lunch \- back to studio at a little after 2 \- then laid thin ground over plate \+ with tissue paper on face of plate, ran it thro' the press, the grain of the tissue being transferred to plate as a general tint \- The squeeze was too tight on one side \- so the transfer was not a complete success \- nevertheless I went on with the biting of tints until 5 o'clk \- then I had to give up, leaving it to finish to-morrow \- Then walked up 5 Ave. to 54th S\. Stopping at Friedrichs to order a little panel-back stretcher. Home to dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. wrote a letter to Jim \+ mailed it to him soon after 10.
