## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 1, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced very early in the m'f continuing until well on in the m'f. \+ then raining at intervals thro' the rest of the day \+ eve'f. Had to get up before 5 a.m. \+ had a very uncomfortable time with both stomach \+ bowels. The discomfort in bowels seemed to be all down at the very base - very little passed, of a strange consistency \+ color covered with whitish mucus - no blood. There was so much pain in the discomfort that I took 3 gr. Codine as advised by D'. 1— Then I lighted my lamp \+ wrote a letter to M'r. Kent. Bkf at 8.30 — about 11 when I got up — Spent the day in Main Room. W. G. Neilson called about 1 o'clk. I read Eliz. Stuart Phelps' "D'. Zay". The Van Hornes \+ all came, in spite of showers, after 4 o'clk. \+ in spite of noisy gabble from every body I got a nap between that \+ 6.30 — Eve'f I continued reading D'. Zay \+ passed a tolerably comfortable day notwithstanding a number of spasms. I had something to eat about 9 o'clk — \+ not long after, to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 2, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ar°. left us, for M.Y.

Rain during the night — dull m'f clearing very slowly during the day — clear night. Up at about 5 — bowels moved — almost normal — feeling much better. Ar°. left for M.Y. city at about 9.30 to drive over to Keene Centre to meet E. town stage for Lake Placid — to take eve'f train for M.l. B. Tillinghast called after 10 — had a very satisfactory talk with him after which he dismissed himself, saying that I no longer required a physician. I spent the day loafing about the house — went to dinner \+ supper — Webb did not come to work — W. Otis came at 1 o'clk — \+ spent afternoon spreading earth on north terrace. I wrote a note to Alice Ireland asking if she could harbor Jim for two or three days before he went to New Haven on the 23? Rec'd. a pleasant \+ sympathetic note from H. L. Smith, from Paris — Aug. 20°. Eve'f Katharine, Bessie \+ Jim went to hotel — to entertainment by the Borrams \+ friends.
