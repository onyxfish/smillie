## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 2, 1901

**Weather:** Cloudy but pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy but pleasant. Webb came \+ commenced work this m'g. I followed him closely all day, most of the work being done in my studio which I am trying to clear up \+ get into working order. Will Otis was most of the m'g cutting grass, weeds \+ young trees growing on the hill side, south \- P.M. he cleared away the remnants of crates \+ packing boxes on front porch. At noon I went to hotel \+ got mail \- afternoon in studio until 4.30 \- Then to Scott Brown under the impression that a meeting was to be at the Reserve office with reference to new Road, or Repairs \+ did one on hill. The meeting is called for to-morrow P. M. on my way over I was recognized by Mr. Louis Tiffany \- on the piazza of her Cottage \- much to my surprise \- I did not know that the family was here \- on my return I again stopped at the cottage \- one after another came, after I had met members of the family, a daughter of Louis' \+ a Miss de Forest. Charming looking girls \- Mr. \+ Mrs. Sidney Smith Mr. \+ Mrs. Stewartson \- Miss Wilson \+ others \- Tea was served on the piazza \- a pleasant \+ unexpected junction \- I left at 6 \-
The us was ever \-, Reading news \- Paper \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 3, 1901

**Weather:** Rain last night \+ in the early m'g, clearing warm \+ pleasant in the afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night \+ in the early m'g, clearing warm \+ pleasant in the afternoon. Webb came \+ after bkfs. he built up scaffolding in studio so that I could tack burlaps over the exposed chimney. I found it a very troublesome job \+, with many other things to do, could not finish it before dinner. Wm. Otis helped Webb for awhile \- At 3 o'clk. I went to office of Adirondack Reserve \- meeting of Keene Town Commissioners \- Two Estates \- Lamb \- Walton, on one side, \- Rob't. Neilson Maghee \- White \+ Scott Brown, Representing Reserve, on the other. There was a long discussion \- The Com'r. were not in a position to promise anything \- town was already deeply in debt \+ they feared opposition from Tax payers "down the Valley" \- they agreed to build the new Road unless the interests at the Reserve \+ Hotel end of the Valley agreed to pay half of the cost. Finally all adjourned, to the end of the proposed new Road \+ I designated the large trees that must not be injured in the making of the Road \- only upon such condition would I grant "Right of way." The proposition finally agreed to was that the town is to expend $200 in grading new road and a like sum is to be expended by Residents of the Heights.
Cocy. as usual \-
