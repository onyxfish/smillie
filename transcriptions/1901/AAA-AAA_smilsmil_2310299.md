## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 6, 1901

**Weather:** Clouded  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded day with threats of rain which came about 6 p. m. Soon after help. Mr. & Mrs. Lownes called. \+ then miss Wilson \+ her friend Miss Eaton(?) I gave some time to Webb who got started on the finishing of alcove, main room. Otis did not come in the m'f but came in the afternoon with his br. Rob who had the towns road machine which was worked for a couple of hours on my road without producing much effect. I made discouraging little progress in getting nearer to my work on the Borden plate Evelf. reading aloud \- The Crisis.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 7, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain last night, continuing until the middle of the m'f \- clearing, showery afternoon. Webb did not come. Otis worked during the afternoon. M'f I wrote to M' H'd. Smith of Scribners \- telling him I had been ill \+ feared that I could not get The Borden plate finished by Sep'. 1' \- I worked until noon tacking birdlaths in alcove main room. Then I walked on Lake Road into the Reserve. Afternoon made some headway in getting the many things, for which I have no places put away \- clearing my studio for work which I am impatient to get at. Evelf. again read aloud from The Crisis to Mary, Kate \+ the boys.
