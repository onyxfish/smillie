## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. To studio at usual time I had no work laid out for the m'f \+ did not feel in working trim so I fussed about for a while \+ then went to 2\. Nat'l Bank \+ attended to business there \- To lunch at 1 \- \+ then to N.A. f.d. \- Etching Class \- I was engaged until nearly 5 o'clk preparing soft ground plates for the class to work on at the next session \- Then walked across Park to Mad. ave. at 102 St. \+ home by car \- dinner at 6.30 Then dressed in Eve'g Regimentals \+ at 8.30 went with Bessie to McWaldorf Astoria to swelle concert by University Glee Club \- a very enjoyable affair \- met there Chase with Miss Howell, an old friend of his. It was late when we got home \+ after 12 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 27, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Sixth Anniversary of Annie's death.

A brilliant day, rather cold \+ windy \- Bkf. about 10 after a good rubdown \- Then I sat for about an hour with my newspaper \- About noon Ralph \+ I went out, went to Charl's for a few minutes \- then to 5th Ave. \+ met D. Gerster \+ walked with him a short distance \- Then thro' some of the cross streets to see some new buildings \+ home at 1.30 \- Katie Etkin had been waiting for some time to see me but had gone. Bessie Parke was there \+ stayed to dinner \- From 4 to 6.30 I had a quiet sleep, making up for the time lost last night. In that way I passed the dreadful hour when, six years ago, I kneeled by the side of the same bed upon which lay my dying Annie. The agony of that time comes to me now very clearly in memory but with a merciful mitigation of the keen anguish that like to fell me then. but the lonesomeness \- the emptiness \- is ever present \+ depressing \- I sigh many times \+ wish that the end of the tragedy had been reached \-

Geo. \+ Charl came to the house in the afternoon Tea at 6.30 \- Eve'g I looked over Koehler's Outlines of Etching preparatory to beginning to write the Chapter desired by the publishers for the Second Ed'n
