## Left Page

### Mon. March 25, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mean, overcast, raw day with rain in the late afternoon \+ at night. I got to studio as usual \+ feeling that I must have a change of work I made gelatine tracing of the full length pencil sketch of Father at Po'keepsie, Aug. 1842 — laid ground, got excellent transfer \+ drew it all in on the plate before 1 o'clk — then home to lunch — p.m. varnished gelati[ne] preparations to biting — then got out the little oil sketch of Father painted by Roft W. Weir about 1830 \+ spent the afternoon making a crayon portrait based upon the Weir sketch — These are a part of the preparations to put Fathers "Pilgrimage" — (autobiographic sketch) into book form for private circulation — Home at 6.30 dinner — Evef. after newspaper I worked on accts until 11 —

## Right Page

### Tues. March 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Weather worse than ever — wet all day — at times raining heavily. To studio before 10. Commenced biting sketch of full length figure of Father. Po'aug. '42. but spent most of the time working with crayon on the copy of Weir portrait — At 12.30 went to 2d. Nat'l bank — home to lunch — back to studio — finished the biting successfully \+ got the crayon finished all but the hands which I shall have to study from life — I worked on plate with dry point until 6 — then home — dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g with newspaper — writing notes \+ accounts.
