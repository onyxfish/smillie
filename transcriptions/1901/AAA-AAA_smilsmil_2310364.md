## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 16, 1901

**Weather:** Cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

After blef: went to B. Cilley's to get report of his exam. of urine — He says that the amt. of urea passed is much larger than at the exam. before — but is still somewhat under the amt. he would like — Then to studio — a very pleasant note from Wm Bailey & a nomination Com. — Casting — offering me nomination on Com. on Admissions — I accepted — Then young Keppel came with message from his father asking if I would etch a small portrait — well see to. This p.m. — Then to work on Borden plate with burnisher — at 12.30 went to Bank \+ borrowed $1000 to meet bills — This is very disheartening. To lunch \+ then again to studio — B. plate till 4 — There to H. Irving P. Gas Stove Co. about gas Range at home — Then to Keppel's — Saw him — the portrait is of Edw. P. Morton by Bonnat, E.C. Stedman's writing, a history of N.Y. Stock Exch. to be pub. \+ the plate is wanted for that. Price $125 — to this I agreed — I to have the printing. Home to dinner at 6.30 — Weth wrote to Jim \+ to G? Cook — G? says he thinks I wrote to him some time ago that the books he wishes to sell would be worth to me $300 I have no Recollection of such a letter — He says that Ireland's figures are! to $170 — \+ he will accept any sum that I will give him between those two sums — again I wrote at length explaining that the smallest sum was, beyond by means telling him that I had this day made a loan of the Bank I said I would let him share the loan with me \+ enclosed in my letter my check to his order for $50 —

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 17, 1901

**Weather:** Some bright weather  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some bright weather in the m'f — then snow flurries in the afternoon \+ eve'f a regular old fashioned snow storm that looked as though it would not stop short of two feet. Got to studio late \+ put in a broken day passing over Borden plate preparing to try to do some printing from it to-morrow. I has been avisee a job to arrange the Zinc plate to register the book-plate in the title page \+ have the etched plate print come right on opposite page. I went to Clausen's — saw my Water Color "on the Road to Ravello from Amalfi" in its new frame — I thought it looked so well that I would make another effort to sell it — so, Told C. to put it in his windows — Weth at home — Mary \+ Bessie went with Charl to hear Capt. Slocum of "the Stray" lecture about his voyage.
