## Left Page

### Tues. June 11, 1901

**Weather:** Overcast m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m't. \+ muggy \- threatening rain After bkfs. went to market \- then down town to Sanford's \- 24-6 Vandewater St. \+ ordered a plate for Borden Etching \- then up town to Rommel's \+ got photo reduction of outline of Borden library \- Then to Pennypln Slate Co. \+ ordered a three part slate laundry tub \- then to Jenning's \- 5 W. 29t St. \+ saw the original sketches by Cole for his "Voyage of Life" \- form in one frame \- each sketch about 10×14". Manhood \+ Youth materially changed in final compositions \- very interesting \- then to Walter's. \+ saw sketch for Keene Bay window \- then home \+ had lunch \- after that to studio \+ commenced tracing Borden outline on gelatine \- worked till 5 o'clk \- Then home \+ rested until dinner \- Charl came in \- much excited over Jennings' outbreak \- J. declares that he is overworked \+ imposed upon \+ gave notice that he is going to quit\- Charl thinks he is demented \- Eve'g I went to Manhattan Hotel by appointment \+ saw Mrs. Barhydt \- I had pls. of work by Etching class \+ she looked them over carefully, commenting freely \- She was much pleased with the showing \+ if her compliments had been dollars I would have been well paid \- Home at 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sister Kate arr. from Po'k.

A beastly hot day \- after bkfs. Sweat to 42°.5 \- bought that \- then to Lewis \+ Conger's then to studio \+ to work on gelatine tracing after working at it for an hour I was convinced that I could not finish it \+ get a transfer on plate \+ away from this city next week \- so I at once gave it up \- I shall not trust to getting my Montrose Etching \+ Printing estab't. to Keene where I shall complete the tracing \- lay ground make transfer \+ go on with the Etching \- I worked \+ putting things away in studio until 12.30 \- Lunch at 1 \- Took 2°5 train to Yonkers \+ got to Miss Jones' before 3 \- saw Miss J \- \+ her married sister Mrs \- Had a pleasant, chatty time \+ looked over many pls. of Alfred Jones' work \- was surprised to find how few they had \+ how little care had been taken of them \- His reputation (justly a high one) could not be maintained by the pls. he left behind him \- Left at 5.30 got 5.54 train, home at 6.30 \- dinner \- Eve'g wrote some notes \-
