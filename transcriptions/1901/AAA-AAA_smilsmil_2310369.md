## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day developing into rain in the afternoon that continued growing heavier into the night. To studio \+ then to Kimmel \+ Voigt with the title pages of Borden Cat. upon which my aug? plate is to be printed — also the zinc plate that I have prepared to secu[re] proper Registering of my plate \+ the book plate that is to go in the centre of letter press. Got pfs from plates by members of my etching class prepared to send to Mr. Marlon Editor of Brush \+ Pencil — Chicago. Back to studio \+ spent some time arranging proofs to send to Mr. M —. Home to lunch \+ back at 2.30 — did little, as the afternoon was very dark, but arrange to take some india pfs from Borden plate to-morrow. Home at 4.30 — feeling out of sorts. \+ tried to get a rest before dinner — miss Ida Ogilvie, daughter of Clinton Ogilvie \+ Katchen Cook, who is visiting with her, dined with us \+ spent a pleasant evef. — at least, they said so —

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 27, 1901

**Weather:** Bright \+ pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasant — worked all the m[orning] getting printing things ready — cutting india etc \+ taking three pfs from Borden plate — two small paper, one large — the work last done on the plate now tells and these pfs are the finished pfs. Home to lunch — afternoon took two more large paper, finished pfs. Three in all — all that I am allowed to take — not enough to let me get the best possible pf — so much depends upon proper inking. \+ my printing is, of course, that of an amateur — and Voigt is not quite sympathetic in treating work that is full of refined gradations — He wants something strong \+ brilliant \+ makes my work look violent \+ coarse.
Dinner at 6.30 — Weff Jim \+ Ralph went to theatre by invitation of Emma \+ Mary Ireland. — I foaled away the evef alone.
