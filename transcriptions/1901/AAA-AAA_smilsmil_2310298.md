## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 4, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day. Bkft. about 9 - after my usual Sunday mt. exercises - After bkft. I paced north piazza awhile in full enjoyment of the wonderful beauty of sky + mountains. Then I wrote a letter to Emma Breland + at about noon I got to work stretching burlaps over chimney in studio - a very tedious job - finished it \+ then found that inspite of my care, it had bulled crooked on one corner - Dinner at 2 - after that took tacks out of burlaps so that I may straighten it - Later, Ralph + I walked out to Chappee Pond to learn the extent of the wreck of Mr. Loring's camp, upon which there was a land slide last winter from the rocks above it - back to the house in time for supper at 6.30 - Evef. Reading aloud to the family of "The Crisis" by Winston Churchill - Katharine + I doing the Reading -

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 5, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant, almost cloudless day, + quite cool. I spent most of the mt. correcting the burlaps that I hung out of plumb on the studio's chimney on Sat. I also gave some time to Sanders who put in what he called a days work - + I wrote to Mr. Kent and Radeker about a little bailer (etching room) that seems to have been left at Montrose. After dinner nearly all of the p.m. was taken to set up big stone in studio + to put tacks through burlaps on the wall at regular intervals. I had little chance to enjoy the delights of out of doors. Evef, more "Crisis" read aloud - To bed at 10.30.
