## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 19, 1901

**Weather:** Bright & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & pleasant — got up feeling tolerably well — after 4&½'. worked awhile upon accts relating to Studio self in 36 St., getting it into order to show exact cost — expense of running & income — about 11 went to bank — made deposit — drew some money — then to Wruederichs, Kranshars — Tooth's — Knordle's & Avery's — met W. T. Richards & Miss Bridges at A's — we are all growing old. Home to lunch late — worked on Studio accts until near 5 — then my head was aching — eyes hurting — & felt much out of sorts generally — Rested & gave my eyes wet bandages till dinner time — could eat but little & passed a wretched eve'g.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 20, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

not a pleasant day — windy & cloudy — After a fairly restful night I got up feeling much better than I did last night — after bkft. I worked for awhile on Studio self. accts, then went out to Claussin's where I met Monks — looked at pictures there — then to Ostime's & saw the Madrago portraits — then to Durand Ruel's & saw the Zorns & a lot of modern French vulgarity. Then home & worked on accts. until lunch at 1.30 — wrote a letter to Mrs. Frances Davis (Fanny Wright of Germantown) thanking her for the photos. of her five children sent to me two or three weeks ago. again on accts. & have got a correct statement of the Studio self as an investment. Respite of Maynard's arrears it has for the past five years paid 5% net on cost — about $170. per an. for my Studio rent — If Maynard should pay up to date it w'd reduce my rent nearly $100 per an. for the past five years. I could not get such a Studio for less than $600 per an. so I have done well. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. about 8.30 Mary, Kate, Ralph & I went to Chas's — He & Mr. Meyer were out — so we amused ourselves for an hour looking over illustrated papers — then home.
