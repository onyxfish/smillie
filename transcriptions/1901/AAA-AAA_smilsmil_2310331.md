## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 9, 1901

Uncle Willie \+ Aunt Agnes lunched with us —

**Weather:** Pleasant \+ milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant \+ milder — Little matters kept me till 10 o'clk. when I got to studio — worked on Borden outline on gelatine till 12.20 — then to bank — then home to lunch — Uncle Willie \+ Aunt Agnes there — on their way to P'ok from Newark. Wrote to Jim \+ to Katie Elkin arranging to go to New Haven on Sunday next — Preparations for Ralph's going to Hackley progressed — but he did not get ready to go to-day as I had planned — To studio at 3 \+ worked till 5.15. Then walked till after 6 — Tea at 6.30 — Ev'g. Charl came in — I packed Ralph's bed clothes for Hackley Hall — \+ wrote checks to pay some Keene bills —

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 10, 1901

went with Ralph to Hackley Hall.

**Weather:** Dubious weather in the m'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dubious weather in the m'g. — thick \+ foggy — but later it cleared very warm. After bk'ft. I went to Jackson's S'p. \+ to G.C. station \+ bought tickets to Tarrytown — then back \+ helped Ralph — then to studio \+ worked on Borden gelatine tracing till 12.30 — then home — lunch — \+ Ralph \+ I took 2.05 train \+ got to Tarrytown at 2.50 we took a hack at once to Hackley Hall \+ Mrs. Williams met us at the door — after greetings we were put in charge of Miss Williams the Matron \+ were taken up to Ralph's room on the third floor, which, owing to want of room he is to share with a boy, Kyle. They will be much crowded — then we went out — met Val. Smillie \+ together we went over to athletic field where a member of the boys were playing foot ball exercises — the air was warm \+ sunny \+ the landscape was very lovely — the fact that I am leaving Ralph in such delightful surroundings \+ such advantages for education \+ development greatly mitigates the pain of separation — but it is a bad day for me — I do not know how I am to get along without him, he has for me so much of his mother — we went to the buildings in course of erection \+ all over them — the situation seems to me to be ideal — about 5 we went back to the house \+ on the front porch I bid my boy good bye \+ left him — he seemed to be in very good spirits. I walked down to the station \+ took 5.37 train \+ got home at 6.30 — tea — Ev'g. The headache that has been bothering me all day took possession of me — Mary \+ the girls went with Charl to the Murray Hill Theatre \+ I was left alone.
