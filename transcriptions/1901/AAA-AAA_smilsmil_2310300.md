## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 8, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thunder storms about 9 am. soon clearing to a misty, humid day — again in the afternoon from before 6 till after 7 a very heavy thundershower clearing soon. Webb \+ Otis at work. W. finished work in bay window — main Room — I worked there most of the day. The girls all worked to get things in order as Miss Lane of Po'k was expected but did not come because of the storms. Lamb, the surveyor, \+ Estes, the contractor for the new road up the hill north of house commenced work to-day. A line was run through \+ the choppers commenced to take down the trees. Afternoon Mm \+ the girls went to Mrs. Openhymis tea give for Mrs. Stewartson —
Ever more Crisis aloud.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 9, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

miss Alice Lane arr.

Brilliantly beautiful m'g. clear, breezy \+ fresh — afternoon clouded \+ light rain before?). I had Webb engaged nearly all day in the attic fixing up rude closets \+ shelves where I could stow the many things I have. After 11 I went to hotel then to Scott Brown's. Dinner at home at 1 — I went down to see how the men were getting along chopping out the new road. I am standing guard over certain trees — I went again at 6 o'clk. Teams \+ men have made a slash through almost to the old road at my gate. I got much of cumbering stuff in my studio in place in the attic by working hard all the afternoon. Schilling about m'g on piazza visiting the girls. Miss Alice Lane of Po'k was expected by stage from Westport about 6.30 but stage was belated until near 8 — I spent the evening in my Room by a pleasant little fire reading.
