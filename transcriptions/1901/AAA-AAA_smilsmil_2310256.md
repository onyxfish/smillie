## Left Page

### Wed. May 8, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annual meeting \- N.A. f D \-
4 Trimble in Wall St \-

A dull day, but no rain \- Soon after bkft went to N.A. f D. to fix up the rts. of class work for the awards Com. \- Dickman, Blum \+ Church \- Voigt promised to have the prints there before 11 but it was 12 before they were delivered \- Sterne helped me get them up \- Dickman \+ Blum were on hand \- Church was not \- The display was creditable \- the awards were not quite what I expected \- V got first 30 Mally got second \+ Hon\. mention \- Then lunch \- after that the business meeting \- Rather small attendance \- nothing disturbed the harmony \+ the meeting was over \+ done 6 \- then adjourned to dinner at 7 \- loafed about during the recess \- The dinner was fairly good \+ developed into a love feast \- there was some fairly good informal speaking \+ more singing than usual \- Young mind was as bad spectacle as usual, he was drunk \+ told smutty stories \+ sang smutty songs \- His only son was drowned a few weeks ago \- the father should have had sense enough to remain home \- I got home about 10.30 \- found a note from Charl saying that he had sold \$4000 bonds \+ nine at a high figure \+ had bought U.S. Steel pref. at a high figure (98) \- This was entirely unexpected \- our understanding was that he was to consult Sturgis \- but he has taken precipitate action \- He says I must see him in the mf.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 9, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain all day \- damp \+ foggy at night \- Before 9 o'clk. went to Charl's \+ he explained that yesterday m'f on advice of Sturgis \- of Strong, Sturgis \+ Co. \- he had not only sold on my acct. five $1000 C \+ O bonds \+ four $1000 Cent. Pac. bonds, but he had bought 110 sh. pref. stock U.S. Steel at a little below par \- This surprised me because I had expected him to consult me after his consultation with Sturgis \- I did not expect him to buy for me, as we have talked of selling to take advantage of high prices \+ then holding to get the benefit of the break that everybody knew was coming sooner or later \- the sale was a good one but I am skeptical as to the purchase \- At 10 I went to 2 J Nat. \- got the bonds \+ then to Wall St. \+ left them at C's office with Sadie Zabriskie \- then to see Thea \- Nassau S\. \- not in \- then to Produce \[illegible\] \- Tremendous excitement in Wall St. the break of yesterday has continued today \+ many have been wrecked \- At the Exchange there was the usual turmoil \- but it seemed to me, not more than usual \- then to R.R. home \- lunch at 1.30 \- then to studio \- most of the afternoon with old portfolios \+ their contents \- Prints, drawings \+ sketches by father \- Mrs. McLean called bringing a little Russian Jewish child \- extremely poor \- Thinking \+ he might get some aid for her by an engagement to pose \- I gave Mrs. McL \- notes to J.Q. Brown \+ Henry Davies \- home to dinner at 6.30 \- See if soon after 8 went to Charl's \- he dined at Union League \- I waited for him until 9.15 \- then home.
