## Left Page

### Thur. May 2, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, raw day - east wind + occassional spitting of rain - but after 7 p.m. the rain fell heavily until after midnight - At 9 am. met Pach's photograhes at W. Bordens - showed him what I wanted + arranged to meet him there with his cameras, etc - to-morrow at noon if the weather is bright - then I went down town to Com. f jurrs on summons - I pleaded infirmities - the let me off - then to Dep. of Wales supplies + paid for the year in advance. Then to dee She's - Whit 1. dee not in - left my old will + draft for new one - then to Geo. & Read's office - 60 Cedar St - saw R. & Van Pelt. Had a talk with Van - about Real estate matters - Touching my general proposition to sell the holdings of the Orrland R.E. Co + re-invest in lots in annexed district + build upon them inf. tenement or apartment houses - He promised to make a report to me. Then to Cullen the photof in 4th St - then to Charl's office - not in - then to Produce Ey. where the stock brokers are now temporarily + watched the row there for awhile - then to the Battery + Aquarium for half an hour then to studio + home to lunch at 1.30 - After that I went to N.A. where the etching class were at work drawing their model Mandara, on the plates - I remained with them some time criticising their work + giving suggestions and instructions about the biting to be done to-morrow then home + had a rest - Ivert Charl came before 8 + with him, Mary + Katharine I went to the Private Concert of the University Glee Club at the Waldorf Astoria - a brilliant audience + good Concert - Mosen thal's setting of Hiawatha was very well given - Concert did not begin until 9 + it was near 11 when it was over - very heavy showers with thunder + lightning - Charl got a carriage + so we reached home at 11.30

## Right Page

### Fri. May 3, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day - somewhat cloudy in the m'f and breezy - clearing cloudless late in the afternoon - after 6½p. went to Kimmel & Voigt's in Canal St. Taking about 25 lbs. of zinc plates - etchings by various members of N.A. + S. etching class - I arranged with H. V. - to take two prints from each so that I can have them on exht. at the annual meeting next We. It was nearly 11 when I got to studio - worked there until 11 40 - then went to W. Borden's + waited until 12.35 before Meyer, Pach's operator came with assistant + cameras etc - Mr. Borden came also - we worked until 1.20 before Meyer was ready to make his first exposure - as that was to be for an hour I went home - had lunch - + Sat to Mr. Borden's where Meyer had just completed his first exposure - one hour + 10 m - then he took a second view, covering the library, music room, + his breakfast room - Then, after much preparation he took one view in the picture gallery - It was after 4 o'clk when at last he got through + I rode right down to D. Colners + saw Mr. Smith who said that he had had a talk with Mr. Borden about getting up a Cat. of his paintings with etchings on a larger scale than has yet been thought of - Mr. B. hinted this to me + for that reason I went to see Mr. Smith - then home + had a rest before dinner - Very Charl came in - I wrote my weekly letter to Jim + mailed it at Grand Central station before 11.
