## Left Page

### Sat. June 1, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant sunny m'f. - growing thick in the afternoon - rain at night. I went to studio after bkfs., did some writing \+ then to D. Yoster's. 34 E. 75th S. - at 11, where he with D. Berg dressed my neck - putting on strips of plaster to draw the wound together. Then I went to Lenox Library on 5th Ave. \+ 71st - saw Weitenkampf - got Noble's life of Thos. Cole \+ read it until noon or a little later, then to Central Park - a great number of children there playing over the grass - Sauntered down to 5th ave entrance \+ then home to lunch. - Afternoon - Resting \+ writing - Charl came in - \+ while we were at dinner Bessie came. Just arr. by train from Po'Keepsie - Ev'f. at 8 I went to S. P. Avery's to ask him if he knew where the Cole paintings - "Voyage of Life" are - he does not know - I walked with him to Century but did not go in because my appearance - swathed neck - was not ornamental - walked home in a heavy shower.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 2, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Slightly overcast - easterly wind. Rather fine thunderstorm about 4 P.m. - Had bkfs. Rather late - it was after 12 when Ralph \+ I went to studio - then to foot of 34th S. E. River - crossed to Green Point - then took cars down to tower of new East R. Bridge - took a look about there \+ then by ferry nearby to foot of 23d. St. thence by cars home, 10 m. - late for dinner. - Afternoon - some reading - some writing \+ then some napping until tea time at 6.30 - Ev'f. I wrote to Kate \+ then to Jim, as I failed to write to him at the usual time last Friday ev'f. Took letters to G. C. station after 10.
