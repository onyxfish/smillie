## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 15, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy Rain during most of the day \+ continuing after I went to bed at night — There was an appearance of clearing in the afternoon for a brief period — I got some exercise on the piazzas — settled my accts. with Jim \+ did some work on Borden drawing. The eve'g was so mild that most of the family sat out on the north piazza — Jim got his trunk packed \+ is ready to start for New-York to-morrow m'g to begin his collegiate course at Yale —

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 16, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant m'g — heavy showers fell between 1 \+ 2 o'clk — then weather cleared fine again — I awoke soon after 5 \+ waked Jim — Before 6 I took his trunk on a barrow down to the public road \+ then I went to hotel to make sure that it would be taken by the baggage man — The sun was just rising — its light on the tops of the western mountains \+ beautiful colored clouds — it was a glorious sight \+ I accepted it as a good omen for Jim — Then back to the house \+ K. \+ I had b'kf't with Jim \+ then we three went to the hotel — \+ at 7 o'clk. promptly — Jim started for N.Y. to begin his college life. Then home — Natchen, Mollie \+ Bessie were up \+ having b'f'st \+ before 8 they started for the top of Giant, Rastle going with them — Webb came \+ worked all day — Jacquith \+ his helper came \+ worked on culvert — They must have been interfered with by the rain — I worked on Borden drawing — Soon after 2 the Giant party ret. pretty wet — but the showers there had passed — late in the afternoon I walked into the reserve \+ some distance up the River Trail — Then out \+ down to Harry Beede's — met him on the road — \+ bargained with him for spruce slabs — Every read news papers \+ to bed early.
