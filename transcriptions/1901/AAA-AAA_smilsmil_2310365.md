## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 18, 1901

*Tarrytown —*
*Katie Elkin came —*

**Weather:** Fine cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine cold day — Got a letter from Jo. Cook proposing to take my $50 cts. as payment. m acct. \+ $100 for books — he to have privilege of redeeming them within two years from date — I wrote accepting, wrote a note to Sallie Cook, who is to be named the p.m. apologising for not having wedding present and then to Claremont Anw W.C. (a road near Tarville) [illegible] to new frame — so pleased with it that I told him to try to sell it at $125 to $75. Then to studio — late of course — Worked on Border plate \+ got two prints — one with Book plate a little [illegible], Reasoning complete — then home to lunch — Katie Elkin there. After lunch I dressed — then back to studio \+ cleaned up \+ home at 3 — Bessie \+ I went to Tarrytown by 3.35 train — J. town at 4.20 — We rode to Hackley Hall in same vehicle with Mr. Hackley — She seemed to me to be an unamiable, unmanly old woman — Ralph greeted us at the hall — a large number of people went up on the train, \+ the Hall was soon festive with Mr. \+ Mrs. Williams \+ the masters \+ wives greeting the arrivals — about 5 we were led down to the gymnasium which had been fitted up as a theatre — up to some delay the first play — a Conan Doyle story "The Stripped Bandé" arranged for stage by one of the actors was given — its boys work, it was creditable — then the H.H. Quartette (string) made some doleful attempts at music \+ after that came the farce.
It was very amusing \+ well given, all of the boys doing very well, especially V.L. Smillie [illegible] the star as Taraxicums Twitters — The play was over about 6.30 \+ then all went up to the main floor and supper was served as we stood — oysters, salad, rolls — chocolate — Coffee — Ice cream \+ cake — Ralph Bessie wandered over the building until 8 o'clk — then the dancing commenced \+ Bessie \+ I decided to take the first H.R.R.R. train that we could get — not time to catch 8.29 — my time table gave 9.05 as the next — pleasant moonlight walk — when we were within 100 yds. of station — train came — the 8.29 belated — in 4p. to off. We could not catch it — then found my time table out of date — no 9.35 train — next at 9.31 more than 45 m. to wait — very vexatious — but at last train came — stopped at every station \+ got in at 10.30 — Home \+ to bed as promptly as possible.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 19, 1901

*Jim came from New Haven —*
*Katie Elkin ret'd. to New Haven —*

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — I sat for some time after off. to chat with Katie Elkin — then to studio — I spent the wh. experimenting in printing — I have ink that H4 Vacet used \+ look strong looking up. with but I cannot get the same effect — the trouble is in vike \+ rag — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — Jim had arr'd. for his Xmas holiday — Karfunkee came about etching — I persisted in my printing experiments getting some better results.

Dinner at 6.30 — Comp. pretty much used up by a headache.
