## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 20, 1901

**Weather:** Very cold & clear — not so windy as yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

up at 7 \+ had my bath — bkfs. at 9 — Spent
the m't in reading news paper \+ writing
letters — wrote to Barnes. Deputy Recorder at Mon-
trose — to H\. Sprague — to Alex DelMar — to Herbert
Smith \+ to Delancy bro's. To J.C. Beak with
\+ to the Tribune about an old few beggar
whose portrait was in to-days paper. I
went to Charl's bet. 12\+1 — \+ had a short
walk before dinner — Dinner at 2 —
more writing in the afternoon — Geo. —
came in — \+ Kate was up \+ in the parlor
but Mary did not venture out of her
room — Charl came in about tea time
Eve'g I read — got my bank acct.
straightened out \+ wrote a note to D.
Chas. Allen \+ sent him check f\# 25
in Paymt. of his bill.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 21, 1901

**Weather:** much milder — cloudy — Rain at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio \+ painted all day on 33 × 44 —
The Rise of the River — \+ at last got the canvas
all covered — I hope that I have got the fore-
ground laid in in such color that I can build
its superstructure unaffected, true \+ not altogether
conventional — but it is so easy to be conventional
\+ so very hard to be unconventional without affec-
tation — home to lunch as usual — At 5o'clk
went to Alexander's on 6th Ave. \+ got shoes that
have been made for me — Home to dinner
at 6.30 — Eve'g. Reading until after 9 —
to bed early — The invalids progressing
slowly — I saw Clausen about painting
Island of Capri, from near naples — that he
now has — told him to sell it, frame \+ all,
for \$100 if he could.
