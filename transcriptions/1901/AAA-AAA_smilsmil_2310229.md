## Left Page

### Fri. March 15, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — cold \+ raw and trying to snow. To studio at usual time \+ worked all the m'g on ditto plate — grudgingly — but I've got to see it through. Home to lunch at 1.30 — afternoon — wrote note to Cline Wilson \+ mail \+ Express — also wrote a letter to Jim \+ worked awhile on plate. At 5 o'clk. went to Walter Cook's Office 3 W. 29' St. \+ there met Ed'w Cook by appointment — Go. Cook did not come. Ed'd Read correspondence relative to dock at Cleveland — an offer to rent it for 3 or 5 years for $2000 \+ taxes per annum — was advised to accept. Home to dinner at 6.30. At 12 o'clk. This m'g I went to Grolier Club \+ spent an hour studying some very interesting etching pfs. that once belonged to Seymour Haden. They were by Frenchmen — Bracquemond perhaps the most interesting. ## Right Page

### Sat. March 16, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast — not so raw as yesterday, \+ clearing in the afternoon. W'f to studio \+ worked faithfully on ditto plate — got it ready for a proof \+ made preliminary preparations for taking it. Home to lunch at 1 — \+ at 1.30 went to N.A. \+ spent afternoon with Etching class — work was from a model — I worked a little on some of the plates after the students had their drawing in. I also made over some soft ground. At 4.30 I left N.A. \+ walked down to The Paulico — N. 97 Central Park W. \+ called on the Colmans — Mr. C. was alone but S.C. came in later — She seemed to be surprisingly well. Talked with energy \+ much animation \+ told of her visit to the new house of a friend \+ walking about; walked home getting in a little after 6.30 — dinner — Sueff. with newspapers \+ sleepy — To bed early.
