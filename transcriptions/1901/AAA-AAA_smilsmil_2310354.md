## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 24, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, wet day \- B\[illegible\] at 10 o'clk. At 12 I went to studio + worked till 42 o'clk with india ink on one of the three Jap. pfs. that Voigt took from the Borden plate yesterday P.M. Then home to dinner + back to Studio at 3 + finished the work up on the one \- I think I got it to look very well, but of course I could only add color \- I could not take any away + in many places there was far too much \- Then I redampsed the three pfs + put them in the press under a heavy pinch \- Home at 5 \- Charl. came in \- Stayed till 6.30 \- Tea \- [illegible] Ge? came in. I wrote note to Mr. Strange \- also to Mr. Chas. Osgood. Division Officer. faculty of the Freshman Class \- Yale \- acknowledging receipt of "warning" \- also to International News Co.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 25, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day \- moist + spattery \- To studio about 9.30 + worked till 11 on pf. of Borden plate touching it up with india ink \- Then to Scribner's with it + saw Mr. H.f. Smith \- the pf. pleased him but he told me that he had persuaded Mr. B\- to give up his idea of having three copies by Xmas + to wait two or three weeks for me to finish the plate properly. This was joyful news to me \- I ret\[urned\] to studio + commenced to work on the plate \- Home to lunch at 1 \- to Studio at 2 \- worked till 4.30 \- then to 23° S. + bought some mosquito net for printing \- then home. Dinner a 6.30 \- Walt wrote a letter to him urging him not to grind at his studies at night.
