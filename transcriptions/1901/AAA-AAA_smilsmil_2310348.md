## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, raw, wet day. worked all day on Borden etching with graver but owing to poor light had a short day. \+ owing to the rain I had no exercise my bedroom is now out of the hands of the mechanics so I had my bed made up there \+ once more feel at home in it. Eve'g wrote a note to Mr. Chester the salesman at Bradley \+ Currier from whom I got the mantel piece \+ tiles in my bed room. I complained of the manner in which the tiles are set. Then \- as it had cleared cold and windy, I went to Charle's for a few minutes \+ there met Mr. Striven, of Singapore, who was dining with him

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 13, 1901

**Weather:** cloudy \- windy \+ cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 \+ worked with graver till 12.30 on Borden Plate \- then to 320 E. 45th St. Central Iron works \- \+ left order for grating for culvert a cor. fury studio bldg. then home to lunch \- To studio soon after 2 \+ worked until after 4 when I could not see to work longer. Young Vondrous brought his last year's prize etching (plates) for "Brush \+ Pencil". At 5 I walked to Irving Pl. \+ 15th St. To Gas Range Place \- then home \- dinner \- eve'g wrote a note to Mr. A.B. Strange (Wm. T. Ryle's sister) who has my pen study of Piæto \- Venice, \+ has written me that she is willing to lend it to me to etch.
