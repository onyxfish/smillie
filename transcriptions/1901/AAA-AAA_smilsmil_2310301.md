## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 10, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another Regulation Dogday. Rain last night + again in the early on't. Close, muggy + warm. clouds hanging formless + low with now + then some veiled sunshine. Webb did not come. Will this was at his work all day + John hauled some gravel + two cedar posts for clothes lines. I spent some time at the new road contending with Road Supervisor Estes as to which trees should be sacrificed + which ones spared. I think that he means to be reasonable even from my point of view altho' he cannot comprehend my regard for a tree + to meet my demands will require trouble + added expense. I continued the work of clearing my studio for action. In the afternoon Estes came to me in trouble declaring that he could not save a large finely shaped Yellow birch that I had said I would not consent to have cut down. I went with him + again looked over the ground. his contention is not unreasonable. at last I very reluctantly gave my consent to its destruction. The girls had tea in the afternoon + almost the entire Vanthorn Maghee family turned out. I had got my studio in order + for a time they rallied there. Mary got a letter from Geo. to our great relief. He was so sick in Cal't that he had to come home. We knew that he was on his way back + were worried about him. He is now at Charles + because of his anxiety to get to Nellie + Val. (who is out of sorts) he declines my invitation to come here to recuperate. Every with newspaper. Young people at the Hotel.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 11, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A misty, clouded day with now + then a little light, mizzling rain. I wrote a letter to Geo. in the morning + then Ralph + I walked in the Reserve to Gill brook flume. We went into one of the wind slashes near there, made in the great gale of last Nov. + the wreck + destruction of forest was sad to see. We walked home in a light rain getting in a little late for dinner. In the afternoon I was surprised by a visit from Denis Tiffany, his wife + a younger daughter. He came over from Lake Placid this m't. They were very pleasant. I arranged with L. to take a tramp with him to-morrow p.m. Eve't. The young folks went to the hotel where the Hampson darkies gave a Sunday entertainment. I kindled a wood fire in my big Studio stove + wrote a letter to Charl.
