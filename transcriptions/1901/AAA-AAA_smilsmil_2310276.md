## Left Page

### Mon. June 17, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - feel rather better to-day - To studio soon after 9. Douglas came \+ we went over the building \+ I gave directions for some painting - all exterior work - worked for a while getting things put away - then to fred-reich's - 5th Ave. \+ got colors \+ brushes. Lunch at 1 - To studio - worked till 4 - then very tired - home \+ on my bed till 6\. Dinner - Eve't. went to Charl's hoping to induce him to go with me to Pomfret for an outing but has business that he cannot leave. Then home \+ wrote a note to Jim telling him that I was much under the weather \+ might not feel able to take the journey to Pomfret to be present at the school exercises on Prize Day - Then to G. C. Station \+ mailed it.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 18, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day. When I got up in the m'g. I was disgusted to find that in addition to vertigo, which still held me, I had headache. At first I thought I would go to Pomfret in spite of all - but was obliged to give it up. About 10 I went to G. C. S. \+ telegraphed to Jim - then to Douglas - to Miller - to studio \+ to Wanamaker's, where I continued my Keene purchases. Left there at 12.30 - To Penrhyn Slate Co. in 17th St \+ learned that my tubs had not yet been started for Keene - Then to Limes \+ Allmend, 3d Ave. - then home to lunch. After that, rested for an hour - then with Ralph went to Rogers, Put \+ Co. \+ Smith, Grey \+ Co. \+ got clothes for him - then to Wanamaker's \+ nearly wound up my list of purchases \+ at 6 walked home. Dinner. Eve't. - wrote to Jim - also to J. T. Breadner - Port Henry - then to Charl's for a few minutes - Mr. Aug't Meyer called at the house. Charl came in later \+ left with him.
