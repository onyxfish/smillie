## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 8, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, pleasant day — owing to nat-ural disturbances following a dose of castor oil taken on going to bed I got up late \+ had my bkfst after 9 o'clk. It was after 10 when I went to Dr Silly with the more complete specimens of urine he has asked for \+ it was quite 11 before I got to studio to resume my "biting" — to lunch at 1 as usual \+ after 2 when I got back to studio my day having been shortened I did not make much progress with work on plate, all the time being spent on some intricate "stopping out". Mr. M. Sean of 330 E. 15th S: a contributor to the N.Y. Tribune called to get hints to aid her in writing concerning Am. Painters \+ illustraters in relation to the Picturesque in Am. life — I had a walk about the streets of about three quarters of an hour before dinner. Evely wrote to Jim — \+ notes to Scott Brown \+ D. A. Adams.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 9, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Maghees lunched with us.

Fine day — continued \+ finished biting Borden plate — worked all this mf at it closely. At home a little before 1. Mr. \+ Mrs. J. H. Maghee — Mrs. Hardenburgh \+ Miss Van Home lunched with us. It was 3 o'clk. before I could get back to studio \+ when it got to be so dark that I could no longer see to work I was not yet ready to clean off my plate — but I had to do it — I think I have a very success-ful "bite" — took some time to clear up — then to Allen — the Mason — 46 S. \+ Broadway — Walked till nearly 6.30 — then home — high tea at 6.30 — Evelf. at 9 went to Century — there till 10.30 — then home \+ to bed.
