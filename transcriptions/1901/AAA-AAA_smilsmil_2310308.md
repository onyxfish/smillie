## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 24, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain during last night \+ a cloudy day, with wind changing from south to north \- neither Webb or O'is it work. Before I got out of bed in the m'g. I wrote a letter to W. Howell, intended to be amusng \- concerning the Poster I made for library benefit to be read, if he approves, before the sale of the Poster at auction \- After 11 I went to Scott Brown's \+ there met W. J. Neilson who has just arr'd from Phila \- made app't to meet him \+ Brown to go over new Road at 2.30 this p.m. \- Then went to Mr. Robinson's Cottage \- found him seated on his porch \- last year, when here, he was a splendid specimen of abundant health \+ energy \- a slight trouble on his right foot developed blood poisoning \+ for eight months he suffered terribly \+ early in July his right foot was amputated \- Then he came home, wasted, weak \+ crippled to sit on his porch \+ look over the golf links where for years he has played \- It was to me exceedingly pathetic \- but he seems to be wonderfully philosophic \- Dinner at 1 \- at 2.30 went to new road \+ met Rob't Neilson \+ Scott Brown \- later W. J. Neilson \+ his daughter Grace came \+ together we went over the line of the new \+\+ of the old road \+ with a simple case he took grades \- the length of the new road is 1500 feet \+ the rise is 122 feet \- the heaviest grade will be about 12% \- We got through about 5 o'clk. Then home \- tea \- every I read part of a story by Paul Ford \- "The "The great K.\+A. Train Robbery \-

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 25, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A charming day, altho' some signs of weather sulks still remain \- bits of clouds still form and cling to the tops of the highest mts. Brilliant moon at night \+ heavy dew \- I got up at about 6. lit a fire in hearth \+ then had my bath \- then to bed again \+ finished Ford's story; finished it after 8 \- then up \+ had bkf't at 9 \- Very soon after that I got to work in studio on poster for entertainment that Mr. Sidney Smith is getting up for benefit of Spring Farm fresh air charity \- I worked all the m'g on it \+ had a short walk before dinner at 2. Mr. Smith came with her Priest about 3.30 \- I walked with her over to Mr. Downes' \+ had a pleasant visit there \- then wandered about hunting for W. Howell then over to call on the Openhyms, but near there met miss Millie Goldmark \+ her mother \- They told me that the O's were in camp \+ that Schilling left to-day \- Then to Scott Brown's \- there met Mr. \+ miss Howell \- went with them to their cottage \+ read my note describing \+ explaining the significance of color scheme of Poster for library \- They said they were pleased. Then home \- met Mr \+ Mrs. W. J. Neilson with several members of my family on Windy row. Tea at 6.30. Sue, J., Mr. \+ Mr. Mathee, Mr. Kaufman \+ Mr. Vanshaumont came over to enjoy our moon light \- I sat on piazza until I was very chilly. To bed at 10.30 \- [illegible — ~10 words]
