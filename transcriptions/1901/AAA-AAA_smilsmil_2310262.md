## Left Page

### Mon. May 20, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

All of father's pfp taken to Lenox Lib.

A dull raw day. To studio after 9 — found Weitenkampf \+ a young man there with portfolios paper \+ string waiting to pack up the pfp. from father's plates to take them to the Lenox Library. This consumed considerable time. Then W\- \+ D went over the other pfp of the work of Hinshelwood \+ other engravers contemporaneous — simply counting them. I find that the total number of pieces is nearly 900. It was after 12 when we finished — The painter got through with the straining of the dark room. Home before I \+ made a little change in my dress \+ then to the Century where I met Mr. J\- \+ H. Maghee by appointment \+ we had lunch. M\- was full of chat, as usual, on all subjects \+ time passed rapidly until near 4 o'clk. Then we left the Club House \+ walked down Fifth Ave together — I left him at 38th S\- — went home \+ with Ralph went to studio where we put away many things in the dark room — Home at 5.30 \+ feeling heavy I lay down \+ slept soundly for half an hour — Dinner at 6.30 — Svef, news papers \+ wrote some notes that S went out \+ mailed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 21, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded up, gradually clearing to pleasant. To studio soon after 9 o'clk — Express man came \+ took large prints to Lenox Library — Got from Weitenkampf a statement of the No. of prints that I have given to the public Library. — Pfp of father's work 428 subjects — 624 pieces — other pfp Am. eng\- etc — Total 919 pieces. Then I made a slight scale drawing of alcove in main Room at Keene \+ went to Walter Cook's in 29. St. to get his assistance — he was in Buffalo — then to Bayerdorfer's \+ got my watch — then to studio \+ cleared up my Table \+ laid out my Borden work. Home to lunch at 1.30 — after that to Friedrichs \+ got Academy board — 12×18 \+ then to studio \+ laid it out in squares to transfer sketch of Borden's Library. I shall have to rely upon my freehand sketch rather than upon the flash photos!
