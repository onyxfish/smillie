## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mild, lowery day. Webb came \+ Otis put in a day. At last I got to work on the Borden order \+ outlined on gelatine for transfer to etching ground. Dinner at 1 \+ as soon as possible thereafter went to Louis Tiffany's cottage. A letter from Ge. was rec. this m'f in which he asks if Gordon may come with him if he decides to accept my invitation. My letter to him had been sent to P.O. I went there \+ got it \+ added a few words telling him to bring Gordon along — then I went to Louis' \+ we took a tramp together to top of Roaring Brook falls \+ a short distance further up the stream. We were much surprised at the growth of trees making passage up the stream almost impossible — Louis seemed to enjoy the tramp very much \+ to me it was very pleasant — Shelf in my bed some reading.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 13, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant beautiful day. Had a great variety of occupations in the m'f that prevented me getting to work until 11 o'clk — among other things I was so much interested in a sky to the north, that I could not resist the temptation to make a little pastel hint of it in oil — I worked less than a couple of hours in outlining Borden subject on gelatine — Then dinner — Katharine, Bessie \+ Miss Jane away with Mr. Maghee climbing "the Nibble". They left at 5.30 — my afternoon was almost wholly taken by Webb in his work putting locks on inner vestibule doors — main room. Every thing connected with those doors seems to have gone wrong and first from the beginning the front vest. doors are fitted right handed — Webb put on the knob balls on left hand door \+ then found that the lock was a left hand lock — This proves to be very awkward in various ways \+ puts me out of patience — I signed for Jamb, a "dedication" of a strip of my land to the town of Keene, for a road, 11 rods long \+ 3 rods wide — I had a delightful tramp on the Windy Brow hill for 3/4 of an hour before sunset — There supped — Evening in my room reading newspapers.

Otis at work all day.
