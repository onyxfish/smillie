## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 10, 1901

**Weather:** Clear, cold \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold \+ windy — Bkfs. at 9.30 After 11 I went to studio for a few minutes — then rode up to 106th St \+ went to Central Park Greenhouses \+ saw the Chrysanthemum Show which is interesting — then walked down thro' the Park to met. Mrs. of Art \+ a hurried walk thru the galleries — met David Johnston there Home to dinner at 2.15 — a little late. Read till 4 — then had a nap — George \+ Charl came in. Tea at 6.30 — Evelf wrote to Arthur Ryle — to D. A. Adams — \+ to n.a. Students (Vondrous \+ O'Malley) asking for their prize plate for "Brush \+ Pencil".

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 11, 1901

**Weather:** Overcast with light rain late in the afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast with light rain late in the afternoon — went to Dr. Gilley's \+ got Report from a laboratory — D.H.T. Brooks — on specimen of urin Nov. 8 — the most important item seems to be the low amt. of Urea. 1.1 % — which is nearly 1/7 less than what should be my normal output. Then to studio \+ to work with drypoint \+ graver on Borden etching. I'm afraid that I have been much too timid in the biting At 12.30 went to Bayerdorfer's with kitchen clock — then home to lunch \+ to studio at 2 \+ worked until 4.30 when I could see no longer — feel discouraged. Home thro' the rain — at 5 — Sat before the fire \+ dozed till dinner at 6.30 — Evelf letters — good ones — from Jim \+ Ralph — I wrote a note to Mr. Chester, Salesman at Bradley \+ Currier — complaining of the manner in which the tile work had been done about mantel piece in my bedroom.
