## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 24, 1901

**Weather:** over cast  **Temp:** [illegible]

over cast all day - To studio about 10
\+ spent all day painting on Passing
Showers. put in all of landscapes nth
left hand side of the picture. I think
it is now in a much healthier condition
than it was in the last painting - it
is better in color - much stronger \+ has
more of the ever\[?\] sentiment \+ a feeling
of movement in the clouds - when I
put it in the frame, late in the afternoon
I was disappointed to find that the
frame was very unbecoming. I went
to Clausen's \+ then to his gilding shop
to see if I could not have it bronzed to
a lower tone. Home at 6 \+ Rested till
dinner at 6.30 - Roger Adams \+ his
wife called at the house for a few
minutes before 8 o'clk while on their way
to the train. Ge\[?\] came in for a little
while. Ralph lunched to-day at
the Cutting's with Bayard.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 25, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the night - overcast m\[?\] -
snow flying - cleared about mid-day -
To studio at 10 \+ to work "in Passing Showers"
letting a little more light into the gloom
of the foreground \+ going over portions of
the sky "drawing things together". A short
walk before lunch at 1.30 - To studio again
\+ painted until after 4 - Went to Abrahams
in 6th Ave. near 29th St. \+ got more bronze pow
ders to experiment on large frame. Then
home \+ rested an hour before dinner -
Both Mary \+ Kate were at the dinner table
for the first time in more than a fortnight.
Every I wrote my regular weekly
letter to Jim \+ mailed it at 10.30. Then
to bed.
