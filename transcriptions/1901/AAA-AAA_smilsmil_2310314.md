## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 5, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another uncomfortably warm day — very few clouds — got up with a headache that had bothered me all night but it wore off \+ I felt better \+ stronger \+ got through with considerable work. Webb worked all day on a printing table for me. This was all day getting out a number of stumps of small pine trees near the house that were cut down last summer. In the afternoon H. \+ E. had an afternoon "hen tea" — a sort of reception or tea exclusively for girls. During my afternoon siesta, from 4 to 5 o'clk. the peculiar cackling noise as I heard it in my room was at first interesting but soon became tiresome. Later I went on a stroll looking for young arborvitaes to transplant. Tea at 6.30 — a quiet eve'g — very mild — on piazza until near 9 o'clk. an hour of reading before going to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 6, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day. I got up early \+ for some time was engaged in attempts to face some jute stuff that I bought to cover chimney breast in my bed room. It is not quite harmonious with surroundings by wetting \+ bleaching in the sunshine I have modified a mustardy yellow to its betterment. Webb was here all day — finished printing table in the m'f'g \+ at odd jobs all the rest of the day. This on hand all day. I went with him before 11 \+ got a group of arborvitaes growing in a bunch. It was taken up with much earth \+ little disturbance \+ the roots removed on a barrow \+ set on south side of steps to North Piazza. The O went to his dinner. Charl came soon after 1 o'clk — from S. Town having left N.Y. city yesterday. Then we had dinner — About 3 — O \+ I went to same place at W.Q. Nelson's hillside among young trees \+ got two more bunch growing flat cedars — one was placed on north side of steps, the other at N.E. cor. of North Piazza. Then I had a bath \+ change of clothes — my pine watch — that has gained only 44 seconds since the 23d of June last, was in the fob poset of my trowsers without guard — I threw the trowsers over the piazza rail \+ the watch peppared to the soft earth, 20 feet below. I am afraid it is ruined \+ cannot forgive myself for my blundering stupidity. Tea at 6.30 — eve'f I moped on piazza disconsolate.
