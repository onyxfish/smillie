## Left Page

### Wed. July 3, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Montrose. St Mary Sayres.

Ups soon after 6 + cleared most of the things out of my room into front room. Then went down to library + completed the packing of my trunk - at about 8. The boys + I went to Okfi. at Grand Union - Then to Millers + left Key of house - then to Station J. + left summer address - Then to Ryan's - left final instructions + got chk for July rent. Then home + was kept busy attending to the many little last things - About 10.30 - Express came for trunks + boxes - It was after 11 when Mr. Bradley got through with her work and Jennings had finished - Then I bid Jim good-bye - he is to take the night boat at 6 o'clk. I will be the last one to leave the house. Ralph + I went to foot of Christopher street - evidences of the extreme heat were to be seen in dead horses + fainting women. The R.R. Station was fearfully hot + inside the car was unendurable - a thunderstorm came a few minutes after we left + a fresh breeze thro' the car made it really pleasant. We had a succession of showers - none of them severe - until about 4 - on Mt. Pocono it was almost cool - We had dinner in the dining room car - + supper at 6.30 - good + reasonable in charge. We got to Alford 3/4 of an hour late - + to Montrose 1/2 hour late. Went at once to Mary Sayres - I wrote to Jim + to Charl + mailed the letters -
Some idiatic young fellows kept beating drums + firing cannon till after 1 A.m., much to my discomfort

## Right Page

### Thur. July 4, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot day - but not unbearable - a fine thunder storm between 9 + 10 p.m. I got up at about 7 - bkft. about 8 - It was about 9 when we went to R.R. Station - our trunks came in on the mf. train - Then to P.O. - Saw many people that I knew - It was after 11 when I got to Mr. Kent's - pleasantly Rec'd. There for more than an hour - Then to Mary Sayre's + had dinner at 1 - Afternoon loafing about with news paper - went to Tetman's house + saw him about helping with packing to-morrow - Then to my room + had a long nap. Tea at 6.30 - After that, Mrs. Kent, daughter + nieces, came down the Ave. + I Rel'd with them to the house - There until after 8 - Then Tom. Sayres just in time to escape a heavy thunder storm.
