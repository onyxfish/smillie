## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 1, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A great racket last night over the advent of the 20° Century, which upon precise calculation came in about 17 hours ahead of our tardy celebration. Dubious weather in the m't, but the sun shone out before noon — The air was raw \+ chilly. To studio at 9.30 and put in a full days work on 33 × 44 painting in waterfall in foreground \+ Rocks \+ Trees up on right hand side. I am still in a hopeful mood concerning my work on this painting thinking that possibly, even at this late day, I may do something creditable. Home to lunch \+ back as usual — About 6 p.m. went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ bought tickets for Friday night for Jim, Ralph \+ Self. Eve'g. The family went to Charl's but as I felt tired \+ stupid I stayed \+ toasted my shins before my own fire.
