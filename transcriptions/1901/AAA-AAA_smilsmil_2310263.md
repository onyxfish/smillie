## Left Page

### Wed. May 22, 1901

**Weather:** Dull day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day. \+ so was my head - I worked all the m'g outlining Borden library on Academy board 12"×18"— going over a pencil outline with pen \+ black ink, but making little progress. It is an irritating, uninteresting piece of work - Home to lunch \+ back to studio - After 4 I gave up work \+ went to N.A. of D. \+ got a lot of the K. \+ P. prints from work by the class \+ got home in time for dinner — Stupids ev'f. I went to Yale to have him look at my neck - there is a swelling on my "swallow", just left of Adam's apple that threatens like a boil \+ that is what he says it is. On his advice I went home - made a poultice \+ passed the night with it on my throat.

Mary Cook invited Ralph \+ me to dine with her to-morrow ev'g.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 23, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

a rather muggy day with veiled sunshine - after an uncomfortable night I got up - to studio about 10 after leaving a note at Mary Cook's I tried hard to work on the Borden outline \+ made but little head way - Home to lunch early intending to go to Greenwood in the afternoon - but the weather changed - I did not feel equal to it — \+ I had rec'd from Mrs. Kent a check making payments for unexpired fire Ins. \+ for the connecting with barn tanks of Public water — so I went to bank instead \+ then home \+ tried to get some rest itself my neck troubled me so much that Ralph went for Yale - he came \+ lanced it; altho' it was not ready but he thought it might accelerate the development of the trouble -
