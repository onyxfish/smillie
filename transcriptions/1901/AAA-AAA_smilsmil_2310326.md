## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain in the early mng. clearing weather later in the day \+ late in the afternoon heavy showers again. Thunder \+ lightning bet. 7 \+ 8 p.m. \+ then high wind from S.W. I got up about 7 o'clk. \+ had my scrub \- bkf. at 9 \- my stomach continues very weak \+ discomforting which prevented my doing the packing work as I expected but I attended to many little things \+ wrote to H\[illegible\] \+ Smith, Douglas, Rev. M. Williams, Katey Elkin \+ others \- Dinner at 2. some cleaning up in studio \-
A little reading in the eve'g. Blashfields' "Italian Cities".

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 30, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary, Chas, \+ the girls left to day \- At Scott Browns.

A bleak mng that cleared somewhat about the middle of the day. Bleak again later \+ then a clear, cold night. A busy day \- Webb was not on hand but Adams was. The work of bearing down \+ stowing away was continuous \- I was far from well \- much dis comfort in stomach \+ bowels that interfered with all my movements. Dinner at 1 \- at 2 Munroe Halts teams came \+ Mary \+ her girls \+ Charl got away for E-town with all their luggage. The servants are to go down to-morrow mng. meeting the others at Westport at 12.19 \+ all go through to N.Y. together except Charl who is to go to Buffalo \- Ralph \+ I remained behind \+ had tea at home \+ at about 7 we went to Scott Browns \- a pleasant coey till 10 \- then to bed.
