## Left Page

### Sun. May 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Mt. Sinai \+ had throat cut.

Dismal m\+ after a dismal night \- rain commenced to fall again about 11 and continued without abatement thro' the rest of the day. Things on my neck have evidently changed for the worse. I got my b\+fp\- after g \+ then went to Yale's. He looked at my throat \- said it should be cut again at once \+ prepared to do it \- I told him that was what I had expected \+ I wished Dr. Gerster to do the cutting at Mt. Sinai Hospital \- Y. assented at once \- telephoned to G's house \- found that he was at the hospital \- phoned to the hospital \+ asked G. to wait there until we came \- then we went to the hospital \- after considerable delay \- G. was ready to attend to me \+ we went to the same room where Ralph had been operated on a week before \- I was stretched \- put under influence of nitrous oxide gas \+ the cutting was done very thoroughly \- a chute artery had to be tied \- [illegible — ~3 words] operations \- heard clearly \+ understood all that was said but felt no pain \- heard G. ask for the ligature \+ wondered why he wanted it but did not feel tying the artery \- physical senses seem to be deadened before the mental senses are \- no unpleasant sensation followed the use of the gas \- no sense of shock or weakness. G. said that I had a nasty carbuncle \- I went home alone greatly relieved \- had a light dinner at 2 \- got some sleep in the afternoon \- wrote a note to Emma \+ read \+ sent it to her by messenger at 8 \- Tea \- [illegible] some more writing and reading newspaper \- wrote to Hy. L. Smith \- Kate dined with Charl \+ Ralph went for her about 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 27, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal rain all day long. I got down to [illegible]. Late after a fairly restful night. No fever \- greatest discomfort was in swallowing \- I had a headache more or less all day. I did considerable writing of letters in answer to accumulations of the past few days. About 1 o'clk. Young D. Berg Dr. Gerster's assistant, came to see how I was getting along. It was a dreary business getting rid of the day. In the afternoon about to Dr. Yale came \- he had proofs Ill\[ustrated\] for his article that is to appear in Scribner's next month \- "Uncle ". also an old camera box that he thought possible to fix over \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Suet\[?] Ralph \+ I tinkered over the camera box for some time.
