## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 9, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very smoky day - almost cloudless \+ milder than yesterday. I did not feel well \+ decided to let things slide \+ take a "day off" — Webb worked during the m'f at packing up the coal bin. Otis assisted. In the afternoon W. commenced work on the new store room in the S.E. cor. of the attic. Otis fussed about the annex - cutting kindlings. I told him that I would not want him after to-day. I went to hotel in the m'f to have my hair cut but failed to get the barber. Afternoon I did some fixing up in studio \+ spent most of the time on my bed. Eve'g. a little reading \+ then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 10, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mollie \+ Katchen with Miss Ogilvie arr.?

Still smoky \+ rather cool. The m'f was given to writing \+ general work. Went to hotel at about 11 \+ waited there until noon. Again the barber eluded me. Afternoon, fixing burlaps on studio wall. Later took a ramble down the valley with Jim \+ Ralph. Webb continued his work on attic store room. Tea at 6.30. Just as we had finished, Mollie \+ Adams, Katchen, Cook \+ Miss Ida Ogilvie made a sudden incursion upon us. They were in roughest tramp guise. We have been expecting Mollie \+ Katchen for some days but Miss O. was a complete surprise. In explanation M. \+ K. said they had written last Wed. to inform us that Miss O. was to come with them to stay over night. We vacated the dining room \+ they took their supper \+ then went to call on the Goldmarks getting back a little before 10. The three were then sent to the annex to sleep in two single beds — the best we could do for them. Miss O. is to leave at 5 o'clk to-morrow m'g. Maghee — Miss VanHorn \+ Katie VanHorn spent the eve'g with us.
