## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 7, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A much milder \+ very pleasant day. Had a sad time trying to light a soft coal fire in dining room grate A cold, damp chimney resented it so furiously, puffing out great clouds of soot, so that I was obliged to pour water on the fire \+ put it out. Hugh Douglas came in \+ I upbraided him. he promises to correct errors regardless of cost. It was after 10 when I went to Miller's \+ to studio \+ then with Ralph to Rogers \+ Peet \+ got a good part of his outfit for the winter — Home to dinner at 1 — after that I rested till 3 — then again out with R — we went to Scribner's I saw Mr. Smith — He was more amiable than might have been expected under the disappointment of not getting the etching as I promised, by Sep'. 30" — He gave me three weeks (to the 28.) to produce something acceptable \+ I have undertaken to do it — rather rashly I'm afraid — I am completely unstrung \+ feel as though I can never recover my strength or nerve — We went to Alexanders \+ I got two pairs of shoes for R — then to Bradley \+ Counters \+ had a book [illegible] the samples from which I ordered matting last June — I find that the hearth border of tiles has not been put in — then to studios — the home — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g in my disordered room.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 8, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very fine day — The m'f was nearly all spent in attending to various little matters — Taking my studio belongings brought from home to studio — unpacking and placing them \+ preparing to again attack the Borden work — I find that I shall have to abandon the partly made outline on gelatine made during the humid weather last summer — now that it is dry it has shrunk in length fully 3/16" making it impossible to fit in the unfinished tracing yet to be done — Home to lunch at 1 — Miller called about the titles \+ other work. It was after 3 where I got to Studio — worked on gelatine tracing about two hours \+ then out \+ walked until after 6 — went to Bayerdorfer's — then to Lewis \+ Conger's \+ got alcohol lamp. Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. R. \+ I went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw "A Trip to Chinatown" — one of Hoyt's farces — It was full of romping fun ridiculous situations with, of course, some modified vulgarity — It was a sort of vaudeville with singing — dancing — acrobatics — mimicry — Out at 10.45 — Home \+ to bed
