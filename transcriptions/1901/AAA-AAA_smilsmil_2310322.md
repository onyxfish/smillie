## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 21, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day - clear & cool, with some clouds. Webb worked on floor of extension, my bed room. I painted steadily during the m't on Borden & am getting on to the trick of brushes, as I find that this work, with which I am unfamiliar Requires Tools that I have not ordinarily used. Dinner at 1 sat 2.30 Charles, Ralph & self walked into the Reserve to go by Gothic trail across to Beaver Meadow fall & then back by the River Trail. Soon after entering the Reserve Ferd Beede overtook us with his team & buckboard & he invited us to ride, which we gladly did, thereby saving ourselves about two miles of walk along the road. Then we walked over to the River C. had his camera with him & he took two photos of the river & looking over to the Giant. The wreckage through the forests caused by the gale of last Nov. was saddening to see — many times trees were down by twos & threes & sometimes they were down in great slashes. We got home after 6 — after a walk that was to me very enjoyable & not at all fatiguing.
Evening, loafing in big chair & to bed at 10. At 11.30 I was awakened by very severe cramps in both legs — so severe that I could hardly get out of bed & then could not stand up — I was several minutes before the muscular contractions subsided & I suffered exceedingly. My legs continued sore most of the night, disturbing my sleep.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 22, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very mild, pleasant day with a little smoke in the air — Up early & had my bath & b'kf't at 8 o'clk. This was so that Katharine & Bessie could go with W. Maghee on a tramp to the top of Hopkius — At 10 o'clk. Kate, Chas., Ralph & I strolled to the top of Roaring Brook fall where we spent a very pleasant hour. We came down soon after noon & then I went to Kate Hillard's cottage & paid her a visit & home at 1.30 — Dinner at 2. After that I did a little writing & then feeling tired I had a nap. Shortly after tea I did some loafing & then some writing. In the course of the ev'g all of the Van Horn's, Hardenburgh's & Maghees came over & spent some time on the piazzas.
