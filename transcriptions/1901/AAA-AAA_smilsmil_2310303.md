## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 14, 1901

**Weather:** Cloudless mʼg.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless mʼg. followed by a warm day. Webb & Otis at work. O. at the wall for terrace north of house. Webb fooling with locks on vestibule doors. They are complicated & expensive locks & he seems to have bewitched them, getting into all sorts of messes. I am disgusted. The doors are spoiled. I wrote a letter to Katie Elkin & Mary Ree — a letter from Geo. saying that he was on his way here — did some work on Borden order. Tried to get an effect with india-ink on one of the photo-reductions of outline — bad paper & many interruptions. I did not get far.
All of the Vaulton's & Maghee's at the house in the eveg. to see the big bonfire made by the boys who for the past three days have been industriously gathering all sorts of combustible waste as a farewell entertainment for Miss Davis. It was a fine show for a few minutes. Chinese lanterns were strung on the piazzas & the effect was very fine — Ice cream & cake was served.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 15, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

George arrived.

Misty mʼg. growing thicker till after 5 p.m. when rain commenced, falling very heavily until about 8. Webb & Otis at work. W. on various jobs & O. on the terrace north of house. I worked upon India ink drawing — Borden library — Saw hands at hotel at noon to greet Geo. on his arrival by stage. Stage passed hotel without stopping. I saw it from a distance & met Geo. near the house — he looks thin & tired but is improving. Dinner at 1. Busy during the p.m. with many things — I again worked on Borden drawing. Eveg.
