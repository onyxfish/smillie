## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 28, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright for a little while in the m'g then overcast — raine at night. At studio for a short time — then I went to Kimmel & Voigt's — Taking the Borden place & his ex-lebns place & the Zinc place that I made to secure correct registering of the two eng. — there I saw Mf V. & arranged to be at his place as early as I could manage it on Monday next — then to Scribners & to the bank — then up 5th Ave, stopping at picture shaks & home to lunch at 1. At 2, Jim, Ralph & I went to Mad. Sq. Garden Theatre & saw the play of Alice of old Vincennes with Virginia starred in the leading part — I did not think much of her or the play — there was too much of buncombe and hysterics — Margaret Gordon, taking a secondary part was the most interesting person to me, pretty in face, graceful in action, refined yet vivacious in manner. We walked home about 5 o'clk dinner at 6.30 — Evey I read the book by Dreyfus descriptive of his five years imprisonment & persecution — a monotonously dismal book that might be quite as effective if compressed into a dozen pages —

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain almost without cessation all day & into the night — much of it very heavy. After my bath I had bkfs. at about 10 — then with news paper till about 11.30 — then worrying over accts till near 1 — then to Charles and home to dinner at 2. From 4 till 6 I had some sleep. At 8.30 I went to Ed. Cook's Mary arr. from Buffalo yesterday. Edna able to sit up for a few minutes at a time. Saw Catharine — she is to be married on the 14th Jany. Home at 10. Raining heavily.
