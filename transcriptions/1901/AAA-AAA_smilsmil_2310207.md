## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 30, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow commenced falling about 10 a.m. \+ continued thro' the day. It was very fine \+ less than two in. fell. I went up to Dr. Vale's immediately after bkfst. to get advice about a bunion on my left foot that is troubling me very much — advised to use a compound of Salicylic acid \+ flexible Collodian. To studio at 10 \+ worked all day on "The Rise of the River". Painting over much of the distant mt. \+ sky with what seemed to me, encouraging results. Home to lunch as usual. After my afternoon's work I went home at 5 p.m. \+ had a rest before dinner. Eveg I wrote to H. Sprague \+ to Herbert Smith.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 31, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, but windy \+ cold. Wrote to Mr. Ainey of Montrose at request of H. Sprague concerning title abstract for Mr. Kent. To studio at 10 \+ spent the mt. working on "Passing Showers" — at 1 o'clk. I went to Clausen's to make sure that my large frame wd. be sent to studio this p.m. as promised — then home to lunch. To Studio before 2.30 worked for a while on "After Showers" — then took it off the old stretcher \+ tacked it on panel-back stretcher. The frame for "Passing Showers" was brought, much improved by the change of bronze \+ a glaze over the gold. I got that picture into frame also "After Showers" \+ looked upon the two with some complacency — \+ have almost determined to send both to the Century w-morrow. At 5.30 I went to Clausen's \+ ordered another frame. A little walk — then home to dinner. Eveg soon after 8, attention was drawn to the light of a conflagration — Ralph \+ I went out, \+ found an immense fire on First Ave. from 32d to 31st St. It was an exciting spectacle — vast crowds \+ a great number of engines, trucks, etc — we got home after 9. Charl \+ Mr. Aug. Meyer spent the eveg at the house.
