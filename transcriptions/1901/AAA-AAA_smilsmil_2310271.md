## Left Page

### Fri. June 7, 1901

**Weather:** overcast & muggy m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast & muggy m'f. Heavy shower soon after 10'clk — Rain continued till over then cleared cool. Up early & wrote before bkfp. To studio about 9.30 — Finished outline of Borden Library & before 12 o'clk. took it to Tournele. Photo. 12 E, 15th St. He is to make photo. Reduction to size of etching 3½" × 8¼". Then m'f. Then home to lunch. Afternoon making up list of things I have to get for Keene. Jno. Maghee called \+ I had a half hours chat with him. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'f. I wrote to Jim — At 9.30 went to Charles — Geo. there — Then to G. C. Station & mailed letter to Jim — walked awhile in Park Ave. enjoying cool eve'f air — then home

## Right Page

### Sat. June 8, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightfully cool & pleasant — After breakfast I went to 42d St. & bought a summer hat — then to Lewis & Conger and bought Nickel plated towel rack & other like furniture for 2d. Story bath-room — Then to Walter Cook's in 29th St. He showed me the sketch for alcove in Main Room at Keene. I am afraid that in spite of my protestations he is planning something far too elaborate — Then to Brooks Bros. & bot a suit of clothes. Home at 12 & wrote a note to Jennie Whiting about cottages at Keene Heights. Lunch at 1. Then to studio & worked all the afternoon clearing up \+ getting the prints that have been lying about ever since the others were sent to the library together. Walked about the streets for half an hour or more before dinner at 6.30 — Much of the eve'f was spent putting up the nickel plated fittings in bath room.
