## Left Page

### Wed. March 27, 1901

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - To studio at 2 o'clk in the afternoon as I spent the m'g with my etching class at N.A. The soft grd. etchings of last Sat. were bitten - Some one in the school has been meddling with acids, plates etc - so I gave Davis a list of the Etching class students \+ told him to keep that room locked \+ to allow no one, not a student, to have the Key. I left at 12 \+ walked down thro' the Park as far as 72d. S. Then rode home to lunch at 1.30. The stone mason finished the work of replacing the defective stones in house front with new ones. To Studio at 2.30 - did a little work with dry-point on etching of Father \- Po'K \- 1842 - \+ then took four prints - While it does not turn out as well as I had hoped, it is to me, on the whole, encouraging. It wo. have been better if I had bitten it with Nitric acid instead of Dutch Mordant. At 5.30 I went to Lime \+ Amanda's \+ got Quinine, Bismuth \+ Salicin. Men home - dinner - eve'g. bothered with a headache so that I fooled away the time over newspaper.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 28, 1901

**Weather:** Cloudy day \+ rather windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy day \+ rather windy - After b'kf'. went to Clausen's gilding shop in 40. 37th S. \+ gave directions about finishing a frame that is now underway for me there - Men to Studio \+ at 10 Mary \+ Mabel Cooley came \+ spent half an hour. Men to Fine Arts Galleries in W. 57th S. Varnishing Day, Soct'y of Am. Artists - my "After Showers" is hung in the little east gallery on the line - the gallery is small \+ only half lighted but I have nothing to complain of - the collection seems to me to be an average one, a few very bad \+ big things - nothing stunningly good - a fair lot representing the fashion of the day in art. Home to lunch at 1.30 - then to studio Maurice Steme came in \+ spent some time \- I made preparations to do some work in Etching \+ did not leave Studio's until 6. a short walk \+ home to dinner at 6.30. Eve'g wrote a letter to Miss Koehler \+ a note to Mr. Alfred Nietchell.
