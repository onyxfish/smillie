## Left Page

### Tues. May 14, 1901

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day. Another day among my prints — at last I have got Father's work arranged in chronological order, a difficult task because many times the dates on the several prints conflicted — \+ I weeded out the duplicates, selecting the best for the N.Y. Public Library. I took a short walk before lunch at 1 30 — \+ after that back to studio where I worked until 5.30 — then I went to Madison Sq) — \+ loitered there until 6.30 — Then dinner — After 8.30 I went to Charl's — He is still a good deal under the weather — Geo. came in \+ we stayed until 10 — Then home — \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 15, 1901

**Weather:** weather pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

weather pleasant — at 5tép. rec'd. by post from Mr. Kent a form of deed of my Montrose property requiring my signature, also two drafts on N.Y. City bank, amounting to $7000 — Then to studio \+ worked there clearing out the acid room so that the carpenter who is to fit it up as a photo-dark Room could get to work when he came I went to 2d. Nat'l. Bank — deposited the drafts \+ paid off all my loans there amounting with int. to date, to over $6700.00 — Then back to studio \+ home to lunch at 1 — Kate so used up by a head ache that she gave me her ticket to the Garrick Theatre to see Ethel Barrymore as Mlle. Trenton in Cap't. Jinks of the Horse Marines — an inoffensively good — somewhat silly play with a ballet (!) able parts — parts of it amused me — the parts of the old ballet master, Belliants, \+ the policeman were well taken — the others were a very poor lot. Ethel B. has a pretty face — a pleasing manner — a good piano touch \+ is a very light \+ graceful dancer but she has an unfortunately disagreeable voice — at 4 30 got back to studio — the carpenter there at work — he told me that Mr. Ferris Lockwood Sup't. of Public Library had been there about 3 \+ waited awhile for me — I worked among the prints until after 6 — then a week to Mad. Sq/- \+ home to dinner — Every wrote to Mr. Kent — to Jas. Morris Jun'. To Albert Winch \+ others — I went out after 11 \+ mailed Them — it was after midnight when I got to bed.
