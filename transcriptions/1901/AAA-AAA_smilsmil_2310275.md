## Left Page

### Sat. June 15, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold, rainy day. After 6 kp. a vertigo came on that increased as the day passed until I was very uncomfortable. I worked about the house until near 11 o'clk — then Ralph went with me to D. Gerster's — he dressed my neck, Touching a spot with caustic — then we went to Studio \+ there R. helped me get my large kp. into boards \+ portfolios. Lunch at 1 — Afternoon I worked with difficulty \+ discouragement being very drunk \+ swimming — Eve'g I tried to find Yale — but he had left town some days ago — So I prescribed for myself 1 gr. of calomel

## Right Page

### Sun. June 16, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant, beautiful day. Dosed myself with Citrate of Magnesia. Vertigo still very troublesome — went up to Yale's \+ found that he had left the address of D. Cilley — 130 E. 36th St., I went there \+ saw him. He thinks the vertigo results from indigestion — advised a dose of castor oil \+ to go to bed. I followed advice — Spent the p.m. on my bed — The oil got through in about four hours \+ made very thorough work but the vertigo remained obstinate — Took a light meal at 6.30
