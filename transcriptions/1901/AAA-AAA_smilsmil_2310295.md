## Left Page

### Mon. July 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A sulky day, with some light showers \+ heavy rain at night. Jim, Ralph \+ I had a job infilling the ice box of the col room with ice from the house. Then I put handles on inside of doors \+ meat hooks on the walls. It was noon when I got to the hotel. I saw head porter. The promised to come to the house with three men \+ carry printing press \+ stove up stairs at 3.30 \- He came as promised \- The press \+ stove were carried up in short order but bad marks were made on floors \+ door jambs \+ some outside castings of the stove were broken. I worked all the remainder of afternoon fixing stoves and clearing up. Evett, reading.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 30, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain nearly all last night \- clouds in early m'f. clearing to a charming day. A fine moonlight night. writing notes \+ interviewing applicants for position of chore boy. Engaged a son of LeGrand Hale. Afternoon went to Otis's \+ saw his son Rob., engaged Will. him to do some job work about the place. Then home to dinner. Afternoon fooling about \+ trying to write a satisfactory letter to Wanamaker about his bill and some items that were not sent with the other goods, altho' chg'd on the bill. Jim \+ Ralph went to the top of Noon mark in the afternoon \- about 6 I went up the old trail a ways to meet them on their return \- at 6.30 I turned back getting back to tea. They did not come in till after 7. Evett on piazza enjoying the moonlight.
