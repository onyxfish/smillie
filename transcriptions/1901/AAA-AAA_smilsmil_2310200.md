## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 16, 1901

**Weather:** Overcast m\+  **Temp:** [illegible]

my 68th birthday.

Overcast m\+ again, clearing mild. After bkft. I went to N.A. Etching class while the students drew from model I made up some soft ground \- It was after 12 when session ended. I walked across the park \+ out to mad. Ave. \+ home to lunch at 1.40. Then to studio \+ painted upon little sketch "We\+., Raquette", repainting a portion of the foreground water \- worked awhile on the gorge picture upon which I worked for awhile yesterday. At 5. went out for a walk. went to Durand-Ruel's \+ was filled with disquet over the faundle \+ mad stuff there shown as art \+ picture painting \- I cannot comprehend how it is that any sane person can be duped into thinking that there is reason \+ beauty in such productions. To me it is the work of egotistical fools who have lost the last remnant of mental balance. Then I walked up to the Century \+ there met D. off Van Dyke \+ others \- again walked \+ then home to dinner \- Eve\+. Geo. Millie \+ Sheldon came in \- many \+ Kate still keeps their beds \- Kate is very low down in spirits.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 17, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, disagreeable day closing with drizzling rain \- At studio I painted on "Cabin from near Naples" \+ now I think I have done on it all that I know how. until after I have seen it in exhibit\+. There, no doubt, I shall be ready either to repaint the whole thing or paint a new picture on top of it. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- Rec'd a Registered packet of Cambridge Enc\+. Co. letters from Miss H. \+ Korhler addressed to her \+ concerning the new edition to be pub\+. of her father's work on Etching \- the letters tend to deepen my distrust of Alex. Del Mar who so far as I know, is all that there is of the Cam. Enc\+. Co. I wrote to Barnes at Montrose, the Deputy Recorder, asking him for an abstract of title to my Montrose property \- It was too dark to see to paint, so I did little. Eve\+. I rec\+. a note from Del Mar stating that it was his plan to pub. the Korhler book ab\+. Dec\+. of next April \- I wrote in reply that I c\+. try to be ready altho' severely pressed by many engagements.
