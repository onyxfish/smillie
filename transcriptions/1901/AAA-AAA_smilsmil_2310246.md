## Left Page

### Thur. April 18, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull & disagreeable day. Got up early feeling better & went down to breakfast. I spent the day in-doors & worked closely in posting last years accts. from cash book to ledger. I did not move about much because there is still much soreness & stiffness in many muscles. Last I read Lossing & Pollard to get material concerning the battle of Gettysburg \+ wrote notes to help Ralph who has to write an off hand composition to-morrow describing the struggle \+ as usual, he left it to the last moment before making preparation or saying anything to me about it. Of course he knows nothing about Gettysburg & says there is no description of the fight in his school history.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 19, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Kipling sisters lunched with us.

A dull day but not disagreeable. I spent [illegible] all of the morning in-doors at work on accounts. At 1.30 we had lunch & the Kipling sisters Hattie & Lily were with us by invitation. Katharine & Bessie have met them at times & like them very much. I have not seen them since we lunched at their house in Paris in 1892 or 3. They are bright & interesting girls. In the afternoon I went around to the studio for a little while & then had a short walk. Evening I did some more writing.
