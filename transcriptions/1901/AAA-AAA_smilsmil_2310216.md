## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 17, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day, bright \+ crisp. Up before 8 \+ had a good scrub \+ change - then a short rest in bed before getting up to bkfst. at 9. Read newspaper for awhile - then out \+ walked up \+ down Park Ave. \+ met Kate \+ walked with her until she tired. - It was after 12 that I went to Walter Cook's \+ found him in bed - Resting. I talked to him about building matters to get information to use in discussing N.A. \+ D matters - another short walk \+ home about 2 oc. Edw. \+ Mary Cook came - Edw. asked me to go with him to Tax Com. concerning Tax assessment on dock property in Brooklyn. Dinner at 2 - after that a little nap on dining room Sofa - worked awhile on posting accts. in ledger - a small blood vessel in cornea of right eye broke \+ discomforts as well as disfigures me. Geo. came, \+ Nellie, in the afternoon - later Charl came - It was a relief to me when he told me that he had sold half of his Smelters. con. \+ that I could get out if I wanted to - I gladly did so. Tea at 6.30. Lying about with wet cloths on my eyes - this has been a very depressing day to me.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 18, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow last night - perhaps an inch or more - but it had all disappeared before night fell. There was sunshine in the middle of the day but the air, tho' raw, was not cold. I came down to my shpt. but felt quite useless all day - The condition not describable farther than that I could do nothing - head \+ eyes distressed me \+ legs failed me. I went out after 11 o'clk in spite of the snow - went to Locke's in day. Ave. \+ selected covering for a chair that he is fixing - Then to druggist's \+ got bottle of Tonic. Then home. I had done some work during the mf posting last years accts. in ledger. Lunch at 1.30 - most of the afternoon was loafed away - part of the time with wet applications to my eyes. Charl came at 6. \+ bot' me check - $172. Profit in Smelters - If I had sold two or three weeks ago it w. have been $1200. but I'm glad I'm out — Dinner at 6.30 - a weary evelf. How much worse it will be probably - next winter if I live - Jim in New-Haven Ralph at Hackley - Charl \+ the rest, abroad. That is their plan.
