## Left Page

### Thur. July 25, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Coal - quite a thick, smoky atmosphere coming down from the north. I soon got to work in the w'f at unpacking the Wanamaker shipment that has been left for me to attend to. I wrote to Ryan about plumbing in Kitchen $110 E. 38th St. \+ sent check to Charl in payment of Vantine bill. Jerd Beede sent his boy Orrin for me \+ he took me to St Hubert's where I saw Jerd who told me that my Montrose plunder, weighing 4200 lbs was at Westport. I told him to bring it up as soon as possible. Saw Strauss, whom I met here last year - says he wants one of my paintings. Afternoon, went to see Maghe — not at home — then down to Harry Beedes, hoping to be able to get him to do some carpentering for me — wife says he is engaged until Sep. 1st. Ralph \+ I put in a lot of work unpacking. Ev'g — fine very pleasant — Read newspaper \+ to bed early —

## Right Page

### Fri. July 26, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day - cool \+ invigorating - the Ther. outside, fell to 54° last night — in my room it was 58°. The smoke of yesterday gradually cleared away \+ at night it was a brilliant cloudless moonlight. The last of the unpacking of the Wanamaker goods — I find that a few things are nun est — \+, more strange, a few things that I have are not charged in my bill. Jim \+ Ralph worked like good fellows all the m'f clearing out the two spare rooms on the main floor where most of the unpacking has been done. About noon I went to hotel, mailed notes, got mail \+ some fish for dinner. Afternoon after 3. I felt heavy \+ tired \+ had a long nap — then I went to Vanthorn's \+ Maghee's — had pleasant call \+ talked of the proposed new road up the hill from the valley — to take the place of the one washed out. Ev'g, Maghee came to us in his Tunisian costume — a very stunning looking fellow. The boys had a big fire made of packing waste \+ dry dead wood. It was very effective with the moonlight.
