## Left Page

### Sat. July 27, 1901

**Weather:** Pleasant m't — then overcast & rain after 7 p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day was spent, mostly, in cleaning up & transferring unpacked furniture to the annex bed rooms & putting the spare rooms in main bldg in order — every thing was nicely cleaned up late in the afternoon.
About 10 I went to the hotel effect-ing to attend an informal meeting of hotel people & property owners interested in the proposed new road up the hill upon which my house is perched — but there was no meeting. Afternoon I went down & saw Albert Winch & Ferd Beede — found Mrs. Maghee in her garden painting a study of holly hocks — Eve'g just as rain was beginning a load of goods from Montrose arr.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 28, 1901

**Weather:** Very heavy rain nearly all day — some of the heaviest was about 2 o'clk — accompanied by thunder.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkft. at 9 — after that Jim & I unpacked two bbls. from Montrose containing crockery, bottles & photographic & etching chemicals — took the crate from the store that I bought of Cooley. Then I felt very heavy & I had a nap before noon. Dinner at 2 — after that another nap. I think that at this rate I shall get rested soon. Eve'g, Mr. Maghee & Miss VanHorn came in & chatted till after 9. I got to bed soon after 10 having accomplished very little through the day. I had intended to write some letters but failed to do so.
