## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 18, 1901

**Weather:** Very cold, clear & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold, clear & windy. At studio all the m't but did not paint — wrote to Kimmel & Voigt — a long letter to Miss Koehler & a longer one to Jim. At 12.30 I went to 2°. Nat'l. to get Nor. Pac. Trust & ref. Cert. to be exchanged for regular stock cert's. Home to lunch — late in getting to studio. wrote on copy of new will — Home at about 6. Dinner at 6.30 — I read newspaper awhile — then wrote a short note to Miss Koehler acknowledging receipt of biographical sketches of her father Minn. Pierce came in for a little while — I went with her to Charles — then to R.R. station & mailed letters to Jim & Miss K. — then home & worried for some time over the balancing of my bank acct.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 19, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bitter cold day with high wind — morning was overcast & about 10.30 a sudden snow squall drove over the city, clearing away in less than half an hour — afternoon & night, cloudless. After [illegible]. for some time I worked over my bank acct — just balanced — have made two or three mistakes that have tangled acct? Men to studio & wrote on copy of new Will that I am preparing — Clausen sent frame for small "Oct. study — Montrose fair g?" It disappoints me & I shall have to get a new one. I sent to him, for sale, my "Island of Capri from near Naples". Home to lunch at 1 & then to N.A. for. & spent P.M. with Etching Class — I took to them examples of soft ground work, aqua-tint (wet & dry) & mezzotint — I laid soft grounds & made experiments — class much interested — Steme brought in a young girl from one of the other classes, made a drawing on soft gr. on the back of an old copper plate — he is very enthusiastic — left at 5 o'clk. & tried to walk down 8th ave — but it was so cold & windy that I gave it up — rode to Mr Paulico — stopped there & stopped there to see the Calmans — found Mrs C. serving tea to Emma Ireland and Dr. Carrol Durhams — the rooms are [illegible] were & the chatting was lively & entertaining — I was there for about half an hour — then walked, or trotted, home — dinner at 6.30 —, well with newspaper — The invalids are improving slowly — Kate sat in the parlor for an hour —
