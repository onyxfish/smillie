## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 1, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully brilliant \+ beautiful day. Bkfs. before 8 \+ at about 8.30 went over to the house. The servants had gone — Mr. Ducharme was washing up the last of the bed clothes — Adams was painting \+ Webb was doing odd jobs. I spent a good part of the m't getting all the lamps wicked \+ ready for use next summer. Then arranging things for final packing — big boxes \+ trunks. The trouble below the belt continued to distress me so that I was much impeded in my work — To Scott B's to dinner at 12.30 — back at 1.30 — Adams finished his work about 5 — Mr. Ducharme, before that — \+ Webb left to return to-morrow for his tools. Magree came in for a little time late in the afternoon. To tea at 6.30 — Evef very quiet \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 2, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m't. Threatening rain which came about 1.30 \+ fell very heavily until about 6 P.m. Bkfs. at 7.30 \+ got to the house about 8.30 — Webb got there soon after — having put the new slabs on the Mc Carthy house to replace the worn decorated ones that I took off. He pottered about till noon \+ then I paid him $42.°° on acct. of $117.00 for 39 days @ $3.°° \+ he took his departure. I spent the day in getting things together in their proper places \+ arranging for packing the three big boxes \+ my trunk — To dinner at 12.30. Beautiful effects of trailing rain clouds over the mts. \+ veiled sunlight. Rain commenced as soon as we reached the house on our return — We left the house at 6 when the rain ceased \+ went to Brown's — had tea — Evef. Read until 10 — then to bed.
