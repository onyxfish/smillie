## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 6, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold, clear day — Having some notes to write \+ other matters requiring immediate attention I did not get to studio until after 10 — \+ very soon after Katie Elkin came \+ we talked for more than an hour about Jim. She realizes now as she did not at first that Jim presents a difficult problem. I worked on Bordeu plate about noon then home to lunch, after going to Second Nat'l. Bnk, — then to studio at 2 o'clk \+ worked till 4 — laid ground \+ etched in parts rather than get color by dry-point or graver — then to Tonnelle's \+ got photo prints from my pen drawing of Rialto from Flower Market — Dinner at 6. Sue\+ wrote to Jim.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 7, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day \+ much milder — To studio at 9.30 \+ spent the m'f drawing in new work on the Bordeu plate — varnished the plate making it ready for the acid — then home to lunch — back — but the work \+ worked with dry-point as long as I could see — 4.30 — then home \+ had a rest before dinner at 6.30. McghEe came — dined with us \+ then took Mary \+ her girls to the Empire Theatre to see John Drew \+ others in "The Second in Command" — About 9 I went to Century Monthly meeting \+ had an agreeable time for a couple of hours — had a little chat with Edwin Abbey who is here to put up the last of his series of Holy Grail paintings in the Boston Library.
