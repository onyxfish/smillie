## Left Page

### Wed. March 13, 1901

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f., bright + crisp — overcast afternoon with some rain at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'f., bright + crisp — overcast afternoon with some rain at night. I got up with a headache which bothered me all day + at 4 P. M. I was obliged to yield to it + go to bed. After 6hfs!. I went to N.A. f.s. + spent the m'f. till 12 with my class, working with graver + dry point on some of their plates while they watched me. + then I took imps from the work done. At a little after 12 I started to walk home but loitered by the way so that at 5q' St. + Mad. Ave. I had to take a car + then got home late to lunch — At 2.30 to Studio — Clausen sent the Waltner etching of "Portrait of Rembrandt" (not the Gildes, as I have written) + I studied it with great satisfaction + set to work on [illegible] head hatching over most of the face with graver. At 4 o'clk. I had to give up + go home — I lay on my bed till 12 o' clk. — Then undressed + went to bed like a Christian.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 14, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Weather looks like April + feels like March — Sunny by fits — almost mild — but in the afternoon wind shifted, race, to east + at 5.30 it began to rain. I got up feeling somewhat the worse for last night's experience. went to studio + spent the day working on [illegible] plate. Home to lunch as usual, stopped work in the afternoon before 5 + then walked up 5th Ave. to Friedrichs + [illegible] on small stretcher. Then on to Plaza Hotel \+ enquired for Mrs. Beach but she had left to Peekskill. The rain came + I had to ride home. Eve'g. wrote note to Ella Beach, apologising.
