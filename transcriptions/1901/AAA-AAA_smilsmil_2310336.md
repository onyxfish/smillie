## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 19, 1901

**Weather:** overcast — then cleared bright — a thunder shower about 2 — breezy P.M. — then clear.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Spent the day at studio painting on Borden library — I have got etching to such stage that I feel the need of the color study + I have not yet painted over all of the surface of my 12 × 18 study. The architectural detail is a great strain on my hand + eye. Went to Douglas + Miller before lunch at one — About 3 when I got to studio + could not see to paint after 4.30 — from 5 to 6.30 walking the streets — air mild. — Emma Bourne came back to us to stay over Sunday — Eve'g, I went to Charl's with the rest of them after writing a note to Wm Fuller about his article on the habits of Whistler + Sargent at Buffalo, Pub'd. in we'y Post Oct. 5th.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 20, 1901

**Weather:** Charming day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

bkfs't at 9 but one thing after another kept me until near 12 before I started out for my walk. In Central Park I met Wm Fuller + we walked together + I went over the matter of which I wrote him last wk — that is, the adulation of Whistler + Sargent by their followers here, to which these idols appeared to be wholly indifferent. Then I went on to Columbia College. The scenery in Central + Morningside Parks was simply delicious. The improvement in the upper part of the city is very rapid. Home to dinner at 2 — had my usual Sunday P.M. rest after that — At 6 went to the Ireland's — 331 E. 17th S. — found Mary only at home — I chatted with her for the best part of an hour then walked home. Read awhile — then wrote a note to Kate — then to G.C. Station — home + to bed.
