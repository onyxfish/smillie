## Left Page

### Wed. June 19, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightful day - very swimmy when I got up but I got thro' the day fairly well altho' pretty well used up at night. After bkft. I went to Walter's in 29th S. \+ got addresses for wooden mantle sellers then to Alexandres - then to Vautines \+ bought rugs \+ bamboo shades - then to Penrhyn Slate Co. \+ to studio at 12 - packing till lunch at 1 - after that, Miller came - then to Wanamaker's \+ finished my buying - then to Tournele's then to 23d. St. - 127 E. \+ then 119 W. - two large establishments where I looked at wooden mantles to find one for my bed room - At Bradley \+ Currier's I found one that pleased me - I hope to be able to get it to fit my chimney breast by slight modifications - home after 6 - dinner - Well! Charl came in for a few minutes. Being tired I did little \+ got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 20, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - Got up with the usual amt. of vertigo, which quite satisfied me that the digestives are not the seat of trouble. I went to Dr. Cilley's office, 130 E. 36th St. soon after 9 o'clk. - he was out - I went to studio \+ again to his office - still out - again to studio \+ worked at gathering things together for packing until 12¼5. There to Dr. C's \+ found him in - he at once began to give me serious attention - He gave me the half of a small tablet, saying that it should have some effect in about 15 min. Before 5 m. had gone a great wave of vertigo passed over me - my ears were filled with ringing - everything was dark \+ I passed into a swooning when I came to I was on my back, Dr. Cilley and Currier each side of me pumping my arms and thumping my chest - I was extremely weak and dazed - after some minutes I was lifted onto an operating table \+ lay there for 20 m. - then a cab came \+ Dr. C. took me home \+ he put me to bed. By this time my head was aching badly \+ I continued to grow worse until eve'g. when I thought I should go wild - in the mean time I was taking 1/10 gr. doses of calomel until I had taken 16/10. Dr. the every P. M. Third visit \+ proposed to have Dr. Packard consult with him - D. F. came later \+ agreed with D. C. - as to cause of trouble \+ proper treatment - A nurse was then sent for \+ at about 11 Miss Stewart came - my headache was then passing away \+ I was making more freely - . It was midnight when Dr. C. I left me - Miss S. then gave me a quieting enema and I was soon asleep -
