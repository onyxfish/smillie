## Left Page

### Sun. March 17, 1901

**Weather:** Beautiful m'f., sunny & mild - overcast afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

I did not wake until near 9 o'clk - consequently did not get to my o'kfst. until 10.30 - at 12 went to Chas - met Ralph there with his camera - we walked up to Central Park \+ R. got a number of snap-shots - Home to dinner at 2. - After that wrote a letter to W'm Warner of Montrose - At 4.30 went to studio \+ wet down paper to print to-morrow - then walked to Emma Ireland's 2.17th St - nobody in - Then walked home - Mary \+ Kate dined at Charles - I wrote a note to Emma I. - sending Henschel ticket to her - at 8.30 R. \+ I went to Charles Ae.? There - home at 10 - Katharine's felon thumb has behaved better to-day [illegible — ~12 words]

## Right Page

### Mon. March 18, 1901

**Weather:** Bright \+ pleasant but, at times, very windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio as usual - finished washing out some old printing rags \+ then to work printing working inks. from the Si\[illegible\]. head. As I had to grind ink \+ use new rags, etc - etc - it is no wonder that I had bad luck,. After taking four inks, I was much discouraged because I believed that the plate was in worse condition than when I went to work on it three days ago. \+ am more than ever assured that I made a mistake when I commenced it - it should never have been begun - then I took some prints from some plates by Stone. - Home to lunch at 1.45 gave Atwater there - after lunch went to the barber's \+ had my hair cut - after 3 when I got to studio - worked on Si\[illegible\]. head preparing to get K. \+ V. pfs. It was near 6 when I went to Gay \+ dooper's to get the chronometer that I have thought of buying - I am to carry it for a while to see how it runs - Home to dinner at 6.30 - Evg. Ralph \+ I had the parlor to ourselves - Kate has been in bed all day with neuralgia in her back - Katharine's room is less peconious - I wrote to Rev. Tho. C Williams - Head Master - Hackley School, making application for a place for Ralph next. Oct.
