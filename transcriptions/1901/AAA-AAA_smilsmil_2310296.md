## Left Page

### Wed. July 31, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day — Will Otis came over — it was Rob that I saw yesterday — Will commenced work at 1 o'clk. cutting grass \+ young growth. Beede came with a load of cases \+ crates. The ones opened, containing books, drawing \+ printing paper had been exposed to rain \+ moisture had penetrated — many of the books \+ some of the paper was badly mildewed \+ every thing smelt musty — of course Beede insists that the cases had not been wet while in his care — it will be impossible to fix the blame. Jim went with the Afternymes to Methodist Calvin. Taking most of the day — from 9 A.m. to 4.30 p.m. — had lunch with him —

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 1, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ cool with a few light showers about in the afternoon. A hard days work unpacking large cases \+ getting things into the house — all sorts of things scattered everywhere. Beede brought his last load. \+ the front Piazza looked as though a cyclone had struck it. Ralph \+ I worked hard \+ we wrecked the last of the crates \+ cases \+ strewed the contents about. Hanging blankets \+ spreads out to air \+ piling all sorts of furniture into the large room. Schilling came up with the girls about noon. Miss Vantine dined with us. Otis helped carry the heavier furniture up to studio. Webb came with his tool chest \+ promises to get to work to-morrow. Evett, Jim \+ Katharine went to a party given by by the Bornum girls. Bessie at home with a headache.
