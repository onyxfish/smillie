## Left Page

### Mon. March 11, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain during the early mfg. To studio about 9.30 — the young man from Maid & Hpp. came + got the paper that I wrote yesterday — Then I experimented with soft ground — but feeling out of sorts I did not accomplish anything — after 12 I went to Clausen's taking my little framed sketch (oil) "Wet. Raquette". Left it for sale at $35.00 W. Mr. Elderkin bought my "Capri from near Naples" — $75. Picture + frame. Home to lunch at 1 o'clk — at 2.30 — at Carnegie Hall — Josef Hofmann Piano Recital. — Bessie was in her seat when I got there. — It was a wonderful performance. — Prelude + Fugue. D. My Back — Rondo G. Maj. Beethoven — Soiree de Vienne Schubert-Liszt. — Ballade G. min. Grieg — Impromtu Etude + Sonata, C minor, by Chopin — Nenia by Sgambatti — Rutencelles, Moszkowski — Bar-carelle. F. Min. Rubinstein, + Tannhäuser Overture Wagner-Liszt — The last was a tremendous culmination + left me very much wrought up + I feel that I could not ask for any more — but the Savages insisted she played with wonderful power the Isolde death song + still the savages cried for more — any other mortal man would have been worn out but he played a daintily affective arrangement of the Straus aradie — The Beautiful Blue Danube — Then man carried the piano away — Men went to Sherwood Studio bldg to Pan-Am. Office + saw Coffin — Curray + Neill — I went to learn if there was to be any show of Etchings — I did not learn anything encouraging — Then I went up to Beckwith's room + talked of N.A. affairs with him. Saw Mr. B. for a few moments — Home to dinner at 6.30 Evening spent very Stupidly —

## Right Page

### Tues. March 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, clear + cold — To studio at usual time. Got out the Lippincott W.C. Sketch of Girl's Head + my etched plate after it + commenced work upon it again — I hope that this will be the last bout. I see much to be done + I can see to that I made a great mistake in ever beginning the work — The original is not sufficiently studied previous to justify the amt. of work that must be expended upon the etching. — I made the mistake at the beginning of thinking that I could make an etching as free + sketchy as the original water-color sketch — Another one might have done so but it was impossible for me + now, with the same study and labor I might have etched a plate after Rembrandt. at 1 o'clk. went to Clausen's + he promised to send the Wattier pl. of "the Gilder" to the studio this P.M. — but he did not. I want to study it to help me in finishing this plate + "the Goldsmith's Daughter." Home to lunch at 1.30 — To studio at 2.30 + worked on etching until near 5 — Then went to Schubert's — Union Sq. + bought two tickets to the Henschel concert — after 6. Dinner at 6.30 — every wrote a note to Emma Ireland asking her to accept one of the Henschel tickets.
