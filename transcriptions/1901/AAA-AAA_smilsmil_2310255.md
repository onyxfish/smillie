## Left Page

### Mon. May 6, 1901

**Weather:** Dubious m'f., clearing later — warm in the sunshine — a chilly tang in the Easterly wind.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After 6½ f' — to studio + then to Pach's — the Borden Photo-prints not ready. Then to Scribner's + saw Mr. H. H. Smith + left with him a copy of Wilson's Coll. Cat. + of the Etching Club Cat. of 1891 — for his information in considering the matter of changing size of the Borden Cat's. Then to Calverly's to see about getting my bust finished — I want the neck tie + coat collar changed — that is all. Then to Dr. H. D. Allen, dentist in 45th St. — one of my teeth has been troubling me — he thinks ulceration is in progress — then to 2d Nat'l Bank — then home. Found one of my Etching class waiting — brought two of his plates — but he is too late for the competition. After lunch went to studio + spent the afternoon picking up things — my headache got the better of me about 4 o'clk + I went home + was in my bed till 6.30 — then dinner — Eve'g — Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 7, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

20th Anniversary of my wedding day —

Very pleasant day — after 6½ f' I went to Charl's to arrange about sale of my Northern Pacific and Union Pacific stocks + conversion into Smelters Pref. Then to studio + to Pach's — got photos. of Borden library + then to Scribner's + saw Mr. Smith — left the photos with him — then to Safe Deposit + got Cert. of Stocks + telephoned to Charl about them — Then to studio — met Maurice Sterne + he went to studio with me + worked awhile on one of his plates that I had there. At 1 went to Miller's + left order for a man to come to the studio in the afternoon. Then home to lunch — To studio at 2.30 — Miller's man came + I gave directions for the fitting up of my acid room as a photo dark room. I accomplished very little during the afternoon beyond some cleaning up. At 5 I went out + sauntered about until after 6 — then home to dinner — Eve'g, after 8 I went to Charl's — a wild plump in Nor. Pacific pref. stock to day — price ran from 102 to 113 — my block of stock was sold — at the lowest figure of course — Charl telephoned down to his brokers at the opening of the market to sell — if he had waited until two hours later it would have been $500 in my pocket — but that is spilled milk. — American Smelters pref. was bought with proceeds of sale.
