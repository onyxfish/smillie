## Left Page

### Tues. March 19, 1901

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — got up with a headache I struggled along until noon — then home and bed where I stayed until 6.30 p.m. — After bkfs. I went to studio \+ tried to write the beginning of Chapter that I have promised for new edition of S. R. Koehler's Etching but was obliged to give it up owing to headache — got up to my dinner \+ felt better — after that went to Chas. for awhile — saw him \+ W. Meyer — then home — read newspapers — did a little writing \+ then to bed — very much disappointed because I could not go to Louis Tiffany's entertainment.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 20, 1901

**Weather:** A nasty day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day — drizzling wet, with fog \+ raw east wind — after bkfs. went to N.A. p.s. met etching class — biting the plates that were drawn in last Sat. I was with them till noon — then rode down to 59th St. \+ called at Pan-Am. rooms for a moment — saw Coffin, Snell \+ Curran — then home to lunch — after that to Kimmee \+ Naigts with Hoffs. plate. My first took three very good p.p. proue \+ I was somewhat encouraged — it didn't look quite so bad as it seemed to yesterday — To studio before 5 \+ tacked my pfc. down to dry. At 5.45 Maurice Stone came in bringing with him two framed pfc from recent plates by him — one, line — the other, soft-ground — both very clever. He stayed until 6.30 — then home to dinner — Twets \+ Chas. came in.
