## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still remarkably fine weather - Etched on Borden plate m'g \+ afternoon. At noon went to Second Nat'l. Bank - then home to lunch. Afternoon, at 4 o'clk. I felt very tired so I stopped work - rode to 72d. St. then into the Park \+ walked down \+ out at 59th St. Home soon after 5 \+ then rested until 6.30 then dressed \+ with Mary went to Charls' - had dinner - \+ then to Mad. Sq. Theatre \+ saw a frivolous, foolish play called "Liberty Bells" - a squaling lot of girls that sang rather badly \+ did some general dancing - It is astonishing how little it takes in some ways to please the public. To bed at 12.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 30, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still unbroken good weather - an other day on the Borden etching. I hope to have it all drawn in on friday Maurice Sterne came in answer to my note. He is willing to let Brush \+ Pencil of Chicago have ph. of his statues for Rebroduction. At noon I went to Klees Bros. to find such a small moulding as I wish, to finish one in my bed Room - then to Miller's - then home to lunch - after that again to Klees Bros. then to studio \+ continued etching as long as I could see. - Home before 6 - dinner - \+ some reading \+ writing to [illegible] -
