## Left Page

### Fri. May 24, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very close \+ warm. Thunderstorms in the early evnt. After a very restless \+ uncomfortable night I got up \+ had my bkfs. about 9 \- no change for the better, that I could discover, in my condition a little more pain \- a little more swelling \- a little more fever. I hoped to be able to go to the studio in the mf. but my head was too heavy \+ my neck was too stiff. I got a little sleep but not enough to make amends for what I did not get last night. Immediately after dinner Kate went to Charles to see how he was getting along. I asked her to call Hale up on the Phone \+ ask him some questions about treatment for my neck. He was in very bad humor at being disturbed \+ was rude to her, closing the Phone \- when he got to the house we were both mad \+ I took no trouble to conceal it. He soon grew mild \+ asked me not to call him off the night again. I told him that I had not called him. Kate was asking for instructions that he was too much out of temper to give. I wrote a letter to Jim \+ Ralph \+ mailed it before 10.

Emma Ireland made her P.P.C. call \- she is to sail on Tues. next to join Virginia Swan somewhere in Southern Europe.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 25, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night \- a brief dry spell in the mf. Then rain commenced again \+ fell constantly, \+ sometimes heavily, until well into the evnt. I had another weary night of unrest \- at 6 o'clk, nevertheless, my temp. was normal. I got up \+ had bkfs. soon after 9 \- after that Ralph \+ I went to Studios \+ got my Bowen work \+ took it home \+ I was able to do a very little in the course of the day \- not more than a couple of hours, but it helped. Lunch. Then the afternoon passed slowly. working \- pacing the parlor \+ stretched out on my bed. Dinner at 6.30. Evening, loafed over the "Post" \+ then to bed. Temp. on going to bed, a little over 100°. I have kept the wounds in my neck open \+ under poultice all the time but there has been little discharge.
