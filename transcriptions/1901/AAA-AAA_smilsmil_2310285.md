## Left Page

### Sun. July 7, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day, barring two heavy thunder showers - the first, before 1 o'clk - the second about the middle of the afternoon. I got up at about 7 \+ had a short walk before bkfst. After that, wrote to Jim \+ sent money orders to Dr. Overlock \+ Rogers. Peet \+ Co - Then Ralph \+ I sauntered out by fail Road to the back entrance to the Kent Place \+ then we explored the grounds, getting back to the Sayres at about noon. Dinner at 1.30 - Afternoon I loafed around - got a copy of Sunday Tribune - had a nap \+ after I went to Keuls - invited to play to dinner - To Sayres about 8 - It was then uncomfortably cool - put on a heavier coat \+ took a walk to warm up. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 8, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cool, pleasant day - Ther. 56°. outside at 6 Am. Bkfst. at 8 - then went to house \+ waited for Radeker \+ the others until near 10 o'clk. Then I went down town very much out of patience to seek for them - I got them to the work after a time - Radeker \+ assistant came at about 11. Clarey \+ Smith did not get to work until about 3. All of the work was done in the barn - \+ when all four were at it, things seemed, at last, to move. I gave up at 5 p.m. \+ went to my room \+ rested until dinner (tea) at 6.30 - at the close of the meal we were surprised by the bringing in of a cake with 14 lighted candles which was set down in front of Ralph, it being his birthday. Eve'g. I went down to P.O. at 8 - got a note from Mary - also from W.A. Coffin. I saw Wm Harrington \+ got from him $9.00 for hay that he bought of me about two years ago.
