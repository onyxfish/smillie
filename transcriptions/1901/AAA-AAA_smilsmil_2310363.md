## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mild hazy day. It was after 9.30 when I got to Dr. Culley's — had to wait awhile for him + then spent some time with him. I left with him specimens of urine. It was nearly 11 when I got to studio + then until 1 I was preparing pf. of Borden plate + cutting a mount for it, getting it into presentable shape to submit to W. B. Home to lunch — after that I went to Scribner's + left B. pl. with Mr. H. L. Smith. Then to Wanamaker's + saw salesman in rug dept. + left bills to be corrected. Then to Edw. Cook's + saw Louise \+ got address of Mary (the mother) + mailed the letter that I wrote to her last night. Then home + rested till dinner. Eve'g. Kate \+ I went to Murray Hill Theatre + saw The Lottery of Love — arranged from the French by Aug. Daly. A very amusing piece and perfectly clean. Of course it was vulgarised by the poor acting of the Co. — but the fun of it could not be entirely destroyed. I had a good hearty laugh + that did me good. Out at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 13, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day — muggy, foggy + wet. At studio I did a little preliminary work to secure proper registration in the printing of Borden plates + this book-plate on Cat. title page — all having to be done at one time. Then to Scribner's + saw Mr. Hatch — after proved. Then to Sanford's in Vandewaler St. + got zinc plate — his forman commenced cutting a square hole in it but was so bungling about it that I stopped him + took the plate to studio. Lunch — again to studio \+ all the afternoon was spent in cutting the hole + fitting the Borden book plate idea at. Dinner — Chas. D'Arme came in — then Chas. It was midnight when I got to bed.
