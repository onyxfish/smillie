## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 21, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold, wintry, windy day — an admixture of Feb. \+ March. After bkft. I wrote for some time to then Ralph (who is having Lincoln \+ Washington Holidays together) \+ I went to The Water Color Show at The old Academy bldg. — The galleries looked very natural but The pictures did not — very few of them interested me — I have tried conscientiously to understand \+ like it but it is very disagreeable to me into appeals either to emotional sense or pure reason. we got home to lunch. Some reading. Then I rested for an hour — then more writing. Charl came in for a little while about 6 o'clk. — Dinner at 6.30 — Self wrote part of a letter To Mr. J. N. Maghee.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 22, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rather disagreeable day — raw, with snow flurries. After bkft. I finished letter to Mr. J.N. Maghee — then mailed it. To studio \+ set palette \+ to work at about 11 o'clk. on "The Rise of the River". worked over middle distance. Home at 1 — back to studio soon after 2 \+ worked until 4. Then washed up \+ rested in my big chair for an hour or more — Then out for a walk but did not get far before I was driven home by a snow squall — Dinner at 6.30 — Sself Geo. \+ Val. came in and stayed until near 10.
