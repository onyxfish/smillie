## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 28, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Continued lovely weather - light showers about 9 p.m. I got up in the m'g. after an uncomfortable night, with severe headache. I loafed about the piazzas while Katharine \+ Bessie - Maghee Van Santvoord got away for their all day picnic climb to top of [illegible]. Will Otis spent the m't spreading earth at south part of house preparatory to seeding with grass - \+ in the afternoon he \+ John, with team, hauled dirt from new road. Webb did not come. In the early afternoon my fever came on \+ went to 100⅘°. with much discomfort in bowels - desire to evacuate - \+ passage of mucus with little streaks of blood. Mary went for the doctor \+ he came about 4 \+ at once said that I must go to bed \+ stay there until my bowels were healed - so, I gave up \+ went to bed much depressed \+ discouraged. Ev'g. the girls went to Mr. Sidney Smith's fresh Air Concert at Hotel \+ Ralph read aloud to me from one of Bullen's works.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 29, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day from beginning to end. I awoke about 6 when my bowels routed me out \+ I had an uncomfortable time - as I had had at about 1 A.M. when discomfort made me get up — But I was free of headache \+ free of fever — I got back to bed \+ soon quieted \+ had no return of the discomfort until about 4 P.M. when I had a bad quarter of an hour or so — The doctor then came \+ gave me codia to take if there sh'd be recurrence of the pain. I kept very quiet, either on my bed or in my big chair all day. I sat by open window \+ piazza door - I wrote to E. P. Palge - Montrose - to start my road raiser on its way to me. Ev'g. Ralph read Bullen to me for a while; thro' the day I read Geo. Meuruger's "The Garden's Story" with pleasure.
Webb came \+ worked all day - Will Otis came at 8 A.M. \+ worked a short m't \+ for about two hours in the afternoon - Jno. hauled dest in the afternoon.

I was routed out at midnight. \+ me pain in bowels \+ a movement that seemed to have less mucus than usual during the past few days.
