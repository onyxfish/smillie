## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 30, 1901

**Weather:** Over cast until late in the afternoon rain before noon with a little distant thunder. **Temp:** air close + heavy.

Awake at 6 - not feeling as well as yesterday m't. - a discomfort in bowels + a weakness that was distressing. I got up at 10.30 + dressed but kept very quiet all day. At 1 o'clk I had a movement of bowels that rather scared me, the mucus being in much larger quantities than ever before + much of it streaked with blood. I wrote a letter to W. H'd. Smith of Scribners - giving a brief history of the past summer in explanation of my failure to have the Borden Plates finished by the 1st of Sept. as promised. Webb was here - worked on shelving in annex bedrooms. Otis came late in m'g + left at 4 on acct. of rain - back before 2. - I don't know how late he worked, but he didn't put in a full day. — Young folks went to a circus in the Valley [illegible] in the afternoon —. Ev'g Ralph Read aloud to me for awhile. — light shower about 9 p.m.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 31, 1901

**Weather:** Rain in the early m't. continuing to the middle of the forenoon. **Temp:** the rest of the day dull + muggy - fog at night.

I had a fairly comfortable night's rest but had an uncomfortable time at about 4.30 A.M. with acidity of stomach - so that I had to get up for bi-carb soda. Later in the m't had stupid headache. Dr. Tillinghast came soon after 10 + said he that that I was getting along all right. but my stomach gives me much trouble + I have a general discomfort + weakness that is distressing + depressing. I got up soon after the Dr. left + went down stairs + spent the rest of the day + ev'g there, finishing Russell's "Three Stranded Yarn" which did not impress me very favorably. Later in the day was much incommoded by wind in lower bowels + acid stomach. Ev'g. I sat by fireside with the family + Read.
