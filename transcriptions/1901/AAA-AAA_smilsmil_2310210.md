## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 5, 1901

**Weather:** Very cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold & windy - awake at 4 a.m. with sore throat & headache - could not get to sleep - so I lighted candle at head of my bed & wrote for nearly an hour a part of what I intend to be a preface to Father's autobiography, "A Pilgrimage" - I have already written other parts of it. Bkfst. at 9. Painted all day on 33 x 44 - going a second time over the middle distance & think it is improved. At 10'clk went to Bayerdorfer's with my watch - then home to lunch. Sellesk delivered my "Normandy Barnyard" from N.A. Lyden. Stene came with a proof of a portrait etching that he has just finished - a very creditable work. Home at 5 feeling much out of sorts. Slept until dinner at 6.30. Eve'g I wrote to Wm. Spense & sent him a check.
