## Left Page

### Thur. May 30, 1901

A veiled may day, sickled o'er with a pale cast of sunshine. It was not a bad day, the past rainy ones being remembered. I did some writing at home — then out for a walk — D. Berg came about 12.30 \+ dressed my throat \+ told me that it was progressing very satisfactorily. After lunch I went to the Lenox library \+ spent some time looking over a portion of the Morgan prints on 45th, then out into the park \+ wandered there until near 6 o'clk — It was "hoodlums" day there — crowded with all sorts enjoying themselves in much rough play. Then home \+ had a little nap before dinner — R. \+ I alone — Welf He \+ I read aloud to one another until Mary \+ Katharine arr?, about 9 o'clk., from Woodland, having stopped at Po'k on their way down \+ left Bessie there — We chatted until after 10 \+ then to bed.

About 10 AM. went to Ed W? Cook's \+ saw both Ed W? \+ Mary.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 31, 1901

A dull m'f. after lunch a light shower after which the weather cleared pleasantly. Lovely moonlight night. After H'lp — Ralph went with me to Studio \+ from there to Scribner's where I saw M. H f. Smith for a few moments — then to Sherer's Photo. gallery in 23d St. to find that Jim got his photos. at the Belway gallery — Then R. \+ I parted \+ I went to 2d Nat'l. Bk\* It was about 12. when I got home — D. Berg came before \+ dressed my neck \+ arranged that I was to go to D. Gerster's at about 11 to-morrow m'f. Lunch at 1.30 — Then R. \+ I went out again — To Knorder's where I bought an etching print to help me in my Borden work — Then to Sharer's in Beway. 1162. ordered 1 doz. Prints from Jim's negatives \+ R. — had two sittings. Then to 5th Ave \+ saw Police Annual Parade — a remarkable showing — especially the mounted police — Home about 5. dinner at 6.30 — Welf got a note from W. H. d. He — has my will ready — went to University Club \+ there we went over the will together \+ two of his lawyer friends signed it as witnesses — Got to Charl's at 10 — Mary, Katharine \+ Ralph there — Home before 11 —

\* Stopped at Walter Cook's — 29th St. with plan of alcove in main Room at Keene That I wish to have columns drawn for — had a half-hour talk with Walter.
