## Left Page

### Tues. April 2, 1901

**Weather:** overcast - quiet + milder  **Temp:**

To studio and set to work carefully to solve the problem that has come up Recently of soft ground - for some reason that I cannot yet discover the acid acts unequally upon what seems to be exposed metal - some particles very slowly while other parts go furiously - biting hard in the midst of work that is hardly biting at all - I made a drawing on a 7×9 plate of Chapel, Campino, taking extraordinary precautions - I had the plate on a thin board on a warm soapstone - to be sure that the ground was not too hard - when I removed the tissue paper the take up from the ground seemed to be all that could be desired \+ I was much pleased. I worked also on the little full length figure of Father. — Home to lunch at 1.30 - Ralph complaining of headach etc - found that he had a temp. of 102°. - To studio at 2.30 \+ bit in Campino — the same trouble - acid very slow all over except in about half a dozen small spots where it bit savagely — home very tired \+ discouraged — Ralph more under the weather - after dinner I went to Charles \+ telephoned to Chas - he advised Calomel. Home \+ followed directions \+ got R- to bed.

I wrote to Mrs. P. H. Badhydt, giving her a report to date of what has been done in the Etching class this season.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 3, 1901

**Weather:** Very heavy rain all the m'f. partial clearing in the afternoon  **Temp:**

I did not go to my Etching class but to studio. Laid another soft gr. on Campino \+ drew again over some of the parts that were much too weak. \+ this I bit with the same results as first biting - portions biting very rapidly while the greater portion bit very slowly - I made some other experimental work \+ at the end of the day felt very tired \+ discouraged - Eve'g, loafed around with newspaper -

Ralph responded to the calomel, given to him last night, at 3 A.m. He had little fever at 8 A.m. \+ I gave him citrate of Magnesia which completed the clearing out. He was kept quietly in-doors all day.
