## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 18, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another overcast day but not so raw as yesterday. At noon I got letters from Jim \+ Ralph \- Jim's letter is in reply to mine upon the subject of the warning rec'd. from the faculty. he indulges in some boyish hysterics \+ twists the purport of my letter with vexations ingenuity. I got to studio at 9.30 \- laid ground over Borden plate \+ commenced etching the ceiling \+ other parts left open on the first ground \- To lunch as usual \+ back at 2 and worked till a little after 4, when I could see no longer \- Then to Union Sq. \- ordered window shades \- Then to [illegible] \+ spent some time with Allison looking over lithographic prints \- ran up by A. Cassell's work \- Then home to dinner \- After that, at 8 o'clk. I went to Edw. Cooks \- he Ret'd. from Buffalo yesterday \- I enquired about Edna \- he says she is better \- not out of danger \- They are hopeful. \- There to Charles \+ left my watch \- Then home \+ wrote a short note to Jim about Edna \- \+ a note to W. Morton Editor of Brush \+ Pencil. \- Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 19, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright for awhile in the a.m. \- Then clouded \- a very light flurry of snow at noon. Continued etching Borden plate \- Mr. H.F. Smith sent 30 title pages for the Borden Catalogue \- They are on mis. jabs \- the title on one half of the sheet \- the pl. printing place to be on the other half. At 4.30 I went to Scribner's to see him about it as I think dampening the title page, as w'd. be necessary in printing my plate, w'd. destroy the gloss on it. W. S. \- tells me to experiment \- Then to Brooks Bros. \+ ordered a suit of clothes (Ready made) \- Then home to dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g. wrote draft of letter to Catharine Elkin concerning Jim.
