## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 10, 1901

**Weather:** Bright, breezy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, breezy \+ of course, dusty \- there was heavy rain \+ some wind last night. To studio at 9.30 \+ spent much time in getting my press in good running order \- then ground ink \+ took imps. of Borden plate \- the black that I have is very poor \- giving a weak, grey effect \- Home to lunch at 1 \- back at 2 \+ took two pfs. on jasp. paper \- Altho' very unsatisfactory as pfs \- they were enough to show me that the plate is practically finished \- cleaned up \+ then went to Kimmel \+ Voigt \+ arranged to go there to-morrow mf. \+ have a final pf. I took also the plates by Prize winners in Etching class \+ left them \- Then walked up to Scribners \- 5 Am. \+ 22 \+ saw W. Hy L. Smith \- told him that the plate was finished \+ read his congratulation. Then to Clausen's \- my Water Color sketch \- "On the Road from Amalfi to Ravello" was there \+ I arranged to have a new frame made for it \- well then gave it to Sally Cook as a wedding present! Then home to dinner. Evef. looking over back (summer) numbers of Punch.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 11, 1901

**Weather:** A very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day \- After bkfs. to studio \+ got Borden plate \+ went to Kimmel \+ Voigt's on Canal St. getting there about 10. Henry took four pfs. from the plate \- one on Etching paper \- three on mlp. jasp. I was surprised \+ not well pleased to see that the one on Etching paper came out better than the others. To studio about 11.30 \+ backed down pfs. \+ studied over them \- I think I can do all that I can see to be done in a day or a little more \- To lunch at 1 \- after that to barber's \+ had hair cut \+ head shampoo'd \- to studio \+ on after 3 until 4.30 I was clearing up things \- my studio is in a sad state of dirt \+ confusion. Then home \+ had a rest before dinner \- Evef. at 8.30 went to Walter Cook's. A meeting of the Delaware Real Estate Co. \- had been called for this evef. \- but instead Walter showed me a brief statement prepared by Ed. Cook showing that there was but a very small bal. to Co. \- not more than enough to meet expenses \- one item showed Geo. Cook to be indebted to the Co. for sums collected, for about \$200 \- this will probably have to be chg'd. up to Profit \+ loss act. I saw Salley \+ her elect [illegible] \+ Louise \- Mr. \+ Mrs. Geo. Walton Green came in \- all much engrossed in the coming wedding \- Set for next Wed. \- I was de trop \+ got out \- went home \+ wrote a note of sympathy \+ congratulation to Mary Cook at Buffalo. having just learned that Edeed. had been moved, without ill effects, from Athol Springs to Buffalo.
