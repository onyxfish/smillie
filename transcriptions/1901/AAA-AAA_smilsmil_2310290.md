## Left Page

### Fri. July 19, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a night of almost sleepless, I got up at 7 - feeling very slim. Immediately after bkft. I went to Keuts just in time to see last load of my things leave for station. Bia Miss Mussy & Mr. Potter goodbye - they go to Buffalo this noon - then to RR station - then to Cooley's & selected 2nd hand stove for Keene - Ran around paying debts & closed my acct at Bank - To Sayres s on after noon & rested until dinner - wrote to Mary - Checks to Brooks - also Olmsted - Telegram to Divens - After dinner again to station - arranged about freight with Bast, Stationmaster - then to Cooley & other places - at H, at Village Hall - at 4.30 Warner & Edgar Tunnell came & we spent three quarters of an hours inspecting the interior - then to Sayres & had a rest before 6.30 - Priscilla Poor & her daughter invited to tea - had a short visit with her - Then down town & made further settlements & at 8.30 went to Mr. Keuts & made my parting call - left after 9 - To bed after 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 20, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Willow Brook - Elmira - N.Y.

Delightfully cool nts, waxing into a very warm day - 64°. at 6 a.m. in my bed room. Up at 6 & packed my trunk before bkft. which I had at 8.30 - after that I said my goodbyes & went to R.R. Station, Mr. Penny Parker carrying my bag for me - Got shipping bill from Bast for my freight & at 9.50 left Montrose, probably for the last time - made close connection at Alford & after a hot, dusty ride got to Elmira at 1.05 - Ella Diven was waiting for us at the station & we got to Willow Brook promptly by trolley car - Ella gave us a light lunch & we spent much of the afternoon with her & May Liscum - Dinner at 6.30 & later Ella, Ralph & I, with driver, went out riding - The air was very soft & mild. We stopped & saw Geo. Diven & his son Alick & his young wife - then to Capt. Reed's & saw him & his wife (Tut) then to young Eugene's & saw him & wife - then out to the Country Club & saw Mr. Eugene (the elder) Mr. Jack Diven & lots of others. Then back through town & on our return stopped at Maud Silsbee's where we made a short visit - then took May with us and drove home - To bed soon after 10.
