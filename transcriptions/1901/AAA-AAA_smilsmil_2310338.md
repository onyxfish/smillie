## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 23, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very mild, pleasant day. I got up feeling weak + uncomfortable in the head + determined to get thro' the day with little work. It was after 10 when I got to studio + I painted on Borden Library till 12 - then I went to Dr. Cilley but he would not give me professional advice till he had seen Dr. Yale - but he advised me to take a dose of Castor Oil at once, so I went to druggists + did so - then home to lunch - Painted for an hour + a half - The castor oil interfered with other work - Home soon after 5. Supper at 6.30 - but I was in little humor for it - Eve'g wrote a letter to Yale asking the privilege of straying now + then.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 24, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy + colder - fine moonlight night. got to studio about 9.30 - Etched all day on Borden plate - Tormented all the time by the thought that I am doing it too mechanically correct — that it should be "free hand" + painter-like - But I cannot see how such a subject can be treated in a free hand way without taking greater liberties with Mr. Borden's beautifully exact + highly polished wood work than he would approve of - so I go on + on under a constant protest. Home to lunch + back at 2 - worked till near 5 - then walked till after 5. met Elg. Howe + walked a little way with her - met W. H[illegible]. Smith + he stopped to urge me to hurry on with the Borden plate. Supper at 6.30 - Got a note from Yale - he does not approve of divided Responsibility with physicians - I must choose between Cilley or him - I wrote + mailed a short letter to Ralph.
