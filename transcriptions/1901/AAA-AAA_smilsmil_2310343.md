## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 2, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The same monotonously beautifuls weather \- not a cloud \- a niels without bring warm \- The country people are now crying for rain \- The tile men came to fix the border of hearth in my room \- worked awhile \+ then went away because they had not tile enough \- The plaster man has not been here since Wed \- his work on the cove is unfinished \+ the room in horrible condition. To studio at 10 \+ in spite of headache I finished the drawing in of Borden Etching at 4\. The most elaborate \+ mechanically dexterous piece of work that I have yet done. I am now very anxious about the biting which will necessarily be an intricate process \- much will depend upon the success of it as to whether the work turns out cold \+ mechanical or brilliantly effective \- I walked for nearly an hour before I went home at 6 \- my headache was then so bad that I went to my bed \+ could take no dinner \- At 8 I got up \+ went to druggists \+ took a dose of castor oil \- This kept me busy during a part of the night.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 3, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Continuous good weather, owing to disturbances last night I got up rather later than usual \- Bkft. before 10 \- After reading newspaper awhile I went to studio \- opened windows to give it an airing \+ varnished the back of my plate \- Then I went to Central Park 106" St. to The Green houses \+ saw the Chrysanthemum show \- The atmosphere, the color \+ the views in the Park were especially fine \- I had a short walk before going home to dinner at 2 o'clk. Stopped at Charles for a moment \- There met Mary \- I had a rest in the afternoon \+ at 6 o'clk. went to Emma Ireland's to Tea \- Mary \+ Grace there \- After a very pleasant evg. home at 10.30.
