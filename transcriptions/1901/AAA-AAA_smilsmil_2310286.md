## Left Page

### Tues. July 9, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - 61° at 7 a.m. 78° at the highest \+ 64° at 10 P.m. Very soon after bkf. I went to the Kents \+ worked there until after 12 \+ then to Sayres \+ rested till dinner - then again to K's \+ worked till 5 - Radeker, Clarey \+ Smith at work - nearly all boxing \+ coating material used up - more promised from the mill but no attention paid to promise - work delayed - I packed a part of the tack-downs etc \+ superintended the packing of Kates crockery - a barrel of bottles - dark room \+ acids - \+ the coating [illegible] printing press \+ other things - a hard day's work - had a half hour rest before [illegible] meal - Wrote to Mary \+ sent to her some mail matter &c? from [illegible]. — Got checks from Smelter Co. \+ Howe Mft Co. \+ sent them to 2? Nat. Bank - went to [illegible] after 9 - to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 10, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - warm, but with a pleasant [illegible] breeze. When I came out from bkf. found Radeker waiting to tell me that Stevens, the mill man, had gone away for a week without filling the order for boxing lumber that he had promised for yesterday - It was a peculiarly aggravating case \+ I was very angry. I went down town with Radeker \+ after a good deal of enquiring about found Ayres, Partner of Stevens, \+ induced him to go to the mill \+ run out the lumber as wanted - It was near noon before it was delivered at the Kents barn. I found while downtown that Clarey was not going to work for me because Pittman had gone away for two days - I told him he must find some one to take his place \+ he sent with me to the shop of Dennis \+ his son promised to work for me this afternoon. The carpenter who worked with Radeker was not on hand and Dozier was found to take his place - So the whole outfit went, with lots of work \+ nothing done - I was completely discouraged - Dined at 1 - Spent the afternoon with the men and got a lot done - coating, standing press \+ printing table - light Ratan chairs - crockery \+ clumps in same - bust of Annie \+ many other things - a day of such work now \+ I shall begin to see daylight ahead - At 5 o'clk. I gave up - dead tired - went to Saynes \+ had an hour on my bed before the evening meal — after that I loafed about until 10 - Then to bed.
