## Left Page

### Sun. April 14, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day growing duller, windier \+ more Raw \- there was some sunshine during the early part of the day \- I got up feeling very seedy \+ after bkf. went to D. Yale. I stethosed \+ he summered punched \+ pouched my bowels but did not discover anything to explain my complaining \- \+ he advised Warburg mixture \- then home \- at 12.30 I went to Walter Cook's \+ met Ed. W. \+ Ge? There by appointment \- The matter of Retaining the services of a lawyer in connection with application for reduction of Faxes on Brookleyn dock was discussed \- Home to dinner at 2 after a short call at Charl's. \- Then I slept until 4.30 \- then to Beckwith's Studio \+ met J. G. Brown \- Ways \+ Means Com. We discussed the work of the Com. to the present time \+ the propriecy of asking for a discharge at the Annual Meeting N.A. of 3 \- May 8th \- Home at 6.30 \- my head hurting me very much \+ making me useless during the every. To bed early.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 15, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Unpleasant w.f. develofking into a heavy rain face, with lots of wind, before night \- I feel completely used up but I went to Studio \+ worked in a perfunctory way on a portion of the coat of 1825 portrait of Father, finishing it up with graver \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \+ then gave up to my feelings \+ spent the afternoon on my bed \- I got a telephone message from Beckwith to meet him \+ J.G. Brown at the Ceuning at 8.30 to make a call on W. E. Dodge \&c/. concerning NA \+ fd Ways \+ means business \- at 8 o'clk. I sent a note to Beckwith at Century stating that I was too unwell to keep the engagement \- Jim went to Dr. Charl's at 9 o'clk who telephoned to D. Yale the came to see me very soon after \- It is Rheumatic neuralgia with some fever \+ complicated by the strain \+ burst that I gave myself when printing last Friday \- A small dose of morphine kept me quiet until about 3 am. \- then I had a bad time.
