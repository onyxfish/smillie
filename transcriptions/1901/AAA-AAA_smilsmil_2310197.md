## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 10, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day — wet & Raw — So dark that I did not go to studio until after 10 — Then I felt so out of sorts that I did not attempt to work — I could not shake off a depression of spirits that followed a dream of Annie that I had last night — Since her death I have dreamed of her frequently — She has always been restored, as pathetic or indifferent — never like her real self — This has increased till last night I dreamed that she was contemptuous \+ ignored me altogether — it was so distressing to me that I awoke \+ for some time was afraid to go to sleep again — It has made me unhappy all day — It is unaccountable to me that while in memory she is to me my ideal of a strong yet sweet \+ loving woman — always buoyantly cheerful \+ considerably kind in my dreams she should be cold, undy[illegible]pathetic \+ at last unloving — It is dreadful to think of \+ I cannot put it out of my mind.

I spent the day assorting contents of drawers \+ cleaning up some neglected places. Dinner at 6.30 — Ev[illegible] Charl came in with another new camera —

Miss Justine Cutting sent a messenger with a note inviting Ralph to join Bronson in a trip to Oakdale to-morrow to spend Sunday. As R. \+ I. have tickets for Theatre to-morrow eve. I replied that if agreeable R. would go down on Sat. my \+ join them at Oakdale.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 11, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, nasty day — like yesterday only more so — I went to studio \+ continued the work of yesterday — with an intermission at lunch time — \+ again through the afternoon — Pretty tiresome work.

Home early \+ wrote a short letter to Jim before dinner.

Ev[illegible] Ralph \+ I went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw Bronson Howard's "Aristocracy" — a trashy piece of work, I think shallow sentiment, sham virtue, bombastic heroics \+ bad morals made up the play. Home after 11.
