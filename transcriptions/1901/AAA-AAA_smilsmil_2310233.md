## Left Page

### Sat. March 23, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright fine day — but the weather in my head continues very cloudy. I went to studio + worked with discouragement on ditto plate wishing that I had courage enough to destroy it. Home to lunch at 1 + then to N.A. of D. where I was with the etching class until near 5 — They worked from model on soft gro. and I worked with graver + dry point on some of the etched plates to show how errors could be corrected — walked down thro. the Park to 90th St. + Mad. Ave. + took car home. Dinner at 6.30. Evef at 8.30 went to Century + heard an exceedingly wrestling address from W. Jas. Barnes, the war correspondent in Africa for the London Mail. He gave graphic descriptions of a modern battle with imitations of the sounds made by rifle balls + shells flying thro. the air — upon a screen he showed a great many photos — + one of them scenes upon the field during action that gave me a clearer idea of the reality than I had before. He told a few stories in a very clever way + evidently pleased the large audience of Centurions. I went home at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 24, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day — + my head was equally dismal. Rain most of the day. I had painful dreams of Annie last night — as in all of them of late, she was perfectly indifferent to me + was planning to effect an understanding + reconciliation — these dreams are very depressing to me. I was able to get up + have my bath + then ckfst. at 9.30. It was 1 o'clk. when I went to Charl's + set my watch by his infallible clock. Dinner at 2 — about 4.30 I gave up + was on my bed until about 8 o'clk — then I got up + spent some time clearing up my writing desk.
