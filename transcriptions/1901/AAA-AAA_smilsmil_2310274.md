## Left Page

### Thur. June 13, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day altho' warm — without the sense of suffocation of yesterday. Miller came after bkft. \+ we spent an hour or more going over the house \+ deciding upon the work to be done during the coming summer. Then I went to market. Then to 2d. Nat'l. Bank. then to Douglas. Dey. Ave. near 42d. St. \+ there spent some time looking at Wall coverings \+ planning changes in my bed room. Then to lunch — after that, to Kimmel \+ Voigt's \+ got bill agt. N.A. of D. then to Sandford's \+ got Copper Plate. Then to Wanamaker's \+ bought a number of things for the Keene house. Then home at 5. Dinner at 6 30 — Ge. dined with us — Eve'g. I wrote to Breadner — mr. Whiting — mr. Waters \+ miss Jones.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 14, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very muggy, hot day — after a clouded m'g. Douglas came after bkft. and we went over parts of the house \+ I gave directions about the finishing of my bed room and other parts. Then to studio with Ralph and Jennings \+ by tackle \+ fall got my framed painting of a Normandy Barillard from the hall way up into studio. Then to Grant's — 42d. W. \+ 7th Ave. \+ looked at Wooden mantle for my bed room. To lunch at 1 — after 3 R. \+ I went to Alexandre's \+ got shoes. Then to Vantine's \+ looked at Bamboo screens for Keene piazza. Then to Wanamaker's \+ continued purchases — Lamps — wooden ware, etc. Home after 6 — dinner. Eve'g. wrote letter to Jim \+ a note to Mr. Olmsted Headmaster Pomfret School, enclosing a check for $20. in answer to his solicitation for Electric light fund.
