## Left Page

### Tues. March 5, 1901

**Weather:** Dull day - growing colder  **Temp:** [illegible]

walked to 3d Ave. \+ 18th St. \+ bought 7 lb. bottle of Acid for etching class - \+ took it to studio - Davis sent for it - then spent the m't working on old pictures for Chas. Up hill work - I don't enjoy working over old failures. I shall now put it aside to dry - Home to lunch at 1.30 - Dr. Gersler came to the house for me - I ret'd. to studio with him at 2. To take a look at a big old painting of Mr. Sinia, belonging to Maynard, that he wishes to give to Mt. Sinia Hospital. - It is in bad shape \+ will need much restoration before it will be presentable. - After Dr. G. left I painted for a little while on the original sketch for "Passing Showers", painted at Leroy 32 years ago - Dr. G. wishes to buy it. About 4.30 I went to Wanamaker's \+ bought a small German student's lamp - then to Bayer \+ others \+ agreed conditionally to buy a second hand chronometer watch that he recommends strongly - Home to dinner at 6.30 - every George came in \+ stayed until near 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 6, 1901

**Weather:** Very cold \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfst. went to N. A. of D. \+ met my class - all turned out with Sterne in charge - biting their plates. All have improved \+ some of the work is very encouraging - I was with them until 12 o'clk - then home - lunch at 1 - Kate so under the weather owing to neuralgia in the face that she could not go to Daly's to see \+ hear "Saw Toy" - a comic play, so I bought her ticket \+ Ralph went with Mary, Katharine \+ Bessie - I went to Durand-Ruel's \+ saw Twachtman's ghosts \+ shreds of paintings - then to Clausen's - Mr. Eldokin has made a low bid for my painting "Capri, from near Naples" - I said I cd. sell it for $50 \+ the price of the frame, ($25). If he buys it I will buy of Co. a signed pf. of "Rembrandt to Gilder" by Waltner. Then to 2d Nat'l Buk, then to studio \+ made a little charcoal sketch of Fish Market at Nice preparatory to an experimental soft ground etching. To Clausen's again at 5 - then home \+ rested until dinner at 6.30 - Every wrote a note to Alex Del Mar enquiring if he still planned to bring out a very small ed. of Koehler's "Etching" in April.
