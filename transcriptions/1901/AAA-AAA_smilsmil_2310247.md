## Left Page

### Sat. April 20, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull m'g. Rain commenced about 10 o'clk \+ increased, the wind rising until it was almost a gale \+ the rain a deluge. My head troubled me all day but I worked persistently at my accounts \+ made good progress. If ever I "catch up" I shall feel like a free man.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 21, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal day - raining steadily except for a while in the afternoon - after which the rain came down as persistently as ever. my head, after troubling me all night kept me pretty miserable thro' the day. Bkf. at 10 - before I had finished Miss Koehler came. Talked of her affairs for about an hour \+ then left to go to Mr. Avery's. I worked for nearly two hours over Annie's accts running from 1881 until her death to find how much money had been spent at Montrose. In 13 years about $35000 was expended upon the place \+ living there - I am sure that not less than $22500 was spent upon the grounds the buildings \+ the furnishings - which (of course) makes me very sad. Dinner at 2. From 4 to 5 I walked up \+ down Park Ave. Then for nearly an hour I sat at Charl's looking over illustrated papers. Then home 9fe \+ Charl there - tea at 6.30 - We \+ for a time I worked on Petty Cash Accts.
