## Left Page

### Sat. July 13, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant, clouded day — Last night from 12 to 1 o'clk. I had a very bad nervous turn which scared me somewhat — caused no doubt by over-work \+ worry. I controlled my self with difficulty, but at last quieted down \+ got to sleep — felt very much exhausted when I got up \+ adhered to my determination formed last night to give myself wholly to rest — The day was spent on the porch or on my bed until late in the afternoon when I went to Mrs Kent to explain to her \+ apologise for leaving such confusion in the studio — A considerable accession to the Rosemont family — Mr. Pennypacker, his son Ramsey \+ a nephew — also Mr. \+ Mrs. Chambers of Mt. Vernon, N.Y. came. Eve'g I went down town \+ did a little shopping \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 14, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant summer day — cloudy part of time — \+ cool air tempering heat — up at 7.30 after a quiet, restful night. That gives me renewed confidence in myself. Bkfst. later — sat on front porch with Mr. Isaac Pennypacker discussing his work on Gen'l Meade recently pub'd. by the Appletons. It was after 11 when Ralph \+ I went up to Mr. K's barn \+ there I packed fragile things for an hour or so — back to Sayres for dinner at 1.30 — again along confabulation with Pennypacker — It was after 5 when I went to the K's \+ did some packing in the studio — back to Sayres \+ had a light meal at 6.30 — finished letter to Mary, written this mo'g \+ wrote checks etc \+ went to P.O. with them — Stopped at Judge Jessup's \+ had a pleasant visit with Mr. J — \+ her daughter, Mrs. Dissesning — The rest of the eve'g I spent on my bed.
