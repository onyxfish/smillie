## Left Page

### Fri. April 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day - worked all day on portrait of father, 1825 - Took first print in the mf. & then spent the rest of the day working on the plate & taking prints - as usual, I am not doing what I had intended to do - as usual, instead of being free \+ sketchy - as I intended that I should be, it is pussed over & worked over until that is made evident in every part. Home to lunch at 1.30 - wrote a letter to Mr. Dashydt, acknowledging her letter & receipt of check. Then to studio & resumed work & printing. Miss Abbott called late - Home after 6. Dinner. Eve'g got a card from Miss Ellen Kent asking me to call on her at the Buckingham - I sent her a note & went with Jim & Ralphe to the Murray Hill Theatre & saw the farce: A Contented Woman by Chas. Hoyt - a shallow, clumsy piece of absurdity - of course I had to laugh at the antics of Donnelly as old uncle Todie but there was no real wit in the play & I was ashamed of my self. Every time I go to this theatre & take my bag I feel that I am misguiding them.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 13, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant but growing gray & coming in rain - Got up with a headache that troubled me all day - To studio & worked until one o'clk. on plate - Father - 1825 - going over the coat with graver closing up the lines - Then home to lunch - Then to N. A. f.D. where I worked with the Etching class until near 5 o'clk. - Then walked part way home - about 2 miles. Dinner - Eve'g - with Ralph went to Bro. Charles' Dark room & developed some of R's latest film exposures - home about 10 -

Had a hard time during the night - had a recurrence of the pain (that seems to be neuralgic) over the bowels - with headache & bad dreams the misery was nearly complete.
