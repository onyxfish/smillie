## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 11, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A repetition of yesterday's weather - but moreso - a little wet in the m'f - then murky - then sunny \+ warm - After bkfs. to Jackson's 84p - then to 640 6th Ave. \+ Registered. delay - did not get to studio till 10.45 - worked on gelatine outline - Borden, till 1 o'clk - then home to lunch. back soon after 2 \+ finished outlines except Right hand end - commenced to scrape it. Geo. came in for awhile - At 5 I went to 2297 Broadway (83d St.) to get metal part of Pasteur filter - ($6.00) to take the place of the one stolen from Butlers pantry by some one during the past summer - walked down thro' the Park - lovely early ev'g effects - dinner at 6.30 - Ev'g with newspaper and fooling about.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 12, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull nearly all day - after bkfs. Mr Martin my next door neighbor came in to ask permission to fasten an iron Rod to my fence as a stay for clothes lines - To studio about 10 and finished gelatine tracing - Borden. At 12 went to barber's \+ had my hair cut. Home to dinner at 1. Chas. Collins called at the studio for a few minutes. P.M. scraped gelatine tracing \+ prepared it for transfer - then prepared surface of copper plate \+ laid a ground on it. Left studio at 4.30 went to 3d av. Cook's - \+ saw Catharine - to enquire about Edna who is at Athol Springs down with Typhoid. She is very sick but Catharine did not seem to be alarmed. Then to Geo.'s studio - Mad. Ave. \+ 60th St. \+ saw his work - Home to supper at 6.30 - Ev'g after my newspaper I opened the cases just arr. from [illegible]. It took to till near 11 o'clk.
