## Left Page

### Wed. June 5, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot day — at home writing until 10.30 then to Dr. Gerster's — Berg dressed my throat — & he says it is in excellent condition I am to dress it my self after this — then to the Penrhyn Slate Co — in E. 17th st. \+ received about slate laundry tubs for Keene — then to 2? nat. Bk — then to some printshops \+ home to lunch at 1. About 3 to studio \+ spent afternoon working on Borden library outline Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'th Ge\. \+ Charl came in — I wrote birth-day letter to Jim \+ at 10.30 went to Grand Cent. Station and mailed it together with others from Mary, her girls \+ Ralph.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 6, 1901

*Jim's Birthday*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day — spent the m'f at home writing — at last I got off my formal note to the Trustees of the Public Library offering my collection of prints from Father's work — also from other Am. Engravers — wrote a letter of instruction to J.T. Breadner, the plumber at Port Henry, about setting laundry tubs \+ changing hot water pipes at Keene — Mary \+ the girls away at lunch — Ralph \+ I alone — after lunch to studio \+ worked on Borden outline until near 6 — then walked for a while on Park Ave — Ge\. dined with us. Ev'th loafed about — too hot for comfort. Charl came in for awhile.
