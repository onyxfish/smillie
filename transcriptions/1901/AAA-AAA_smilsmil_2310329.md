## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 5, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day. I got to house about 9 o'c \+ was kept very busy all the m'f between work \+ callers. Otis \+ his son John came up \- the first, to get contract for filling ice house the second, to present his bill \- then Winch came to run the water out of the tank \+ [illegible] dry out traps \+ fill the latter with Kerosene \- then Maghee \+ Lamb called \- L. with "dedication of strip of land to Keene town for highway that I signed some time ago \- This he consented that I should amend by interlineation specifying the conditions upon which I gave right of way \- i.e. the sparing of certain trees bordering closely upon the Road as surveyed. Then Lord Breede came for boxes, bicycles \+ trunks \- he had to wait until I finished packing my trunk \- but his son Orrin got away with his load at 10.30. Until 12 I was busy about the house \- then to Brown's. The color, as seen when walking over the [illegible] links was something wonderful \- The woods on the base of noon mark were like flame \- it was near 1 o'clk when we had dinner \- after that I walked down to Maghee's \+ bid him \+ Mrs. M. \+ Adelaide, good bye \+ then met Fred Beede's team at the gate with Mr. M., Brown \+ Ralph \- we had a comfortable ride to E. town where we left the Browns \- then into West Port, getting there about 5.30 \- for the last half hour I was cold \- went to hotel near station \- R. \+ I had a brisk walk \- then to hotel \+ tea \- we had weary waiting about hotel \+ station until 10.55 when the train came \- we got into our section without delay \+ I had a fairly good night's rest \-

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 6, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Awake before 6 \+ found that we were passing through the Highlands. I did not get to sleep again \- got into G.C. Station on time \- 7.20 \- \+ went at once home, got in by my key \- Kate Seward was up \- but Katharine \+ Eliz.y did not put in an appearance till 10 o'clk \- Mary in Po'k. \- we soon had bkft. \+ then looked about \- the house is in a frightful state of confusion \+ dirt \- my room, for which I had hoped so much was a dreadful disappointment to me \- The buckram on the wall is streaked at every joint \- The cove is a bungling failure \+ the woodwork is too yellow. About noon I went to Charls \- he got home last night \- he has little to say that is favorable to the Pan American Show \- News to studio \- more dirt \- dinner \+ 2 o'clk \- \+ a good nap after it \- Mary came down from Po'k. late in the afternoon \- Tried to distribute the contents of my trunk with little success \-
