## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 3, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, cool mt. giving promise of a pleasant day — but the clouds soon came after 12 a light shower \+ after 1 quite a heavy one — then again the weather cleared \+ there were beautiful cloud \+ color effects down the valley — my cold has quite got the best of me \+ I have been much depressed — This was added to by a letter from W. H\[illegible\] Smith of Scribner's &c, this afternoon — my failure to get the plate done has placed him in an awkward position with Mr. Borden — \+ he has good reason to be impatient, but he writes kindly \+ considerately — I wrote at once that I was preparing to return to the city — that I was discouraged \+ ready to give up \+ count my work so far as nothing. I said I hoped to see him early next week \+ would any course he would advise — I got a letter from Jim enclosing, for my signature, a bond or promise to pay all bills (College) of Jim's incurring under the rules during his course of studies at Yale — I filled it out in the P.O. \+ Mr. Maghee signed it as a witness — I got along with my work at the house fairly well — got two big cases packed \+ closed — at think the partly packed \+ most of the things to go into my trunk ready, alongside, for packing — If I am no worse to-morrow than I have been to-day I think I can get through all right — To Brown's at about 6 — he is completely used up by a cold — a quiet eve with illustrated books \+ newspapers \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 4, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** Ther. fell to 28°. last night.

Clear mt\[illegible\]. cold — soon clouded over — flurries of snow in mt. tops that would dust them over white — later the clouds passed \+ the evening was very quiet \+ cold — after 4\[illegible\]. I went to the P.O. \+ got from Mr. Robertson my watch — then to the house \+ worked there all the mt. — back to Brown's after 12.30 — dinner — to house again about 2 — Webb there — got his tool chest — I gave him his watch &c. with mine from Bayerdorfer — Maghee came in \+ visited awhile while I packed my trunk. By 5 o'clk I had got things almost finished up \+ Ralph \+ I went to Reed, Beedie's to make sure that all matters for conveyance to-morrow \+ bay \+ baggage were fully understood — There we walked to Brown's getting there about 6 — Tea — eve \+ news paper \+ chatting — my cold has now got into my head \+ I fear that I am in for a regular influenza attack.
