## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 14, 1901

**Weather:** Cold, windy & cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, windy & cloudy — It was 10 before I got to studio & worked all day with graver & dry-point. At 12.30 went to Miller's & to Douglas's & then home to lunch — to studio at [illegible] & worked till 4.30 — Rec. came in for a little while — After 5 I went to Scribner's & saw Mr. H. L. Smith. He tells me that The Borden Cat. has had a set back in the printer's hands which will give me a week or ten days more time. Then home — dinner at usual time — Suett. Charl came in & I could get no more than a start on my letter to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 15, 1901

**Weather:** Pleasant, altho' at times cloudy and gusty  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant, altho' at times cloudy and gusty. Got official warning from Yale Faculty that Jim is below minimum average allowed in recitations & that unless he improves he will be dropped at Xmas — with it came a very short note from Jim asking for more money that he may go to the theatre & dismissing the matter of low grade in very few words "am very sorry — will try to do better" — This is a humiliating shock to me. Worked all day on Borden Plates. Sterue and O'mally came with etched plates for "Brush & Pencil" — At 3 o'clk. had a three mile walk with comfort. Dinner at 6.30 — Westf. wrote to Ralph & to Jim — to the latter I tried to write in a way that would not irritate him & at the same time firmly & seriously. I told him that he had reached a crisis & that his whole future possibly depended upon the course he chose to adopt now. To bed at midnight.
