## Left Page

### Sun. June 23, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An excessively hot day — Got up feeling fairly well + went down to my bkf. about 9 o'clk. The day was spent in careful loafing + the imbibation of fluids of one kind + another — By permission of the doctor I had a fairly good dinner at 2 o'clk — In the way of work I wrote some notes. Evett,, Chase came in — About 10 P.m — Thunderstorm with some heavy rain.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 24, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another exceedingly hot day — After a night of fairly good rest, up to bkf. at 8 — Very soon after it Weitenkampf came + I spent half an hour with him + electing 4 or 5 die plts to ill. the article in "the Bookbuyer" of which he wrote to me some days ago — Then Douglas + his nephew + a painter. I gave an hour or so to the mixing of a satisfactory tint for wood work in my bed room — Then wrote + read until 12.30 — Then to D. Gilley's + got from him the record of analysis of my urine last Thurs. I showed the analysis by D. Biggs, made more than eleven years ago + after looking over it he said that it represented much the same condition as I am now in — Then home to lunch. After that I had a nap — Then Edna + Tom Cook called. Edna just home from her 10 months visit to Spain — Then I did a little more work towards getting out of town — With Ralph I went to studio + got things there + brought them to the house — Dinner at 6.30 — Ralph + I again went to studio + brought home things. Chase came in + spent most of the evening I worked at getting back numbers of magazines together — nedo 11 where I got to bed
