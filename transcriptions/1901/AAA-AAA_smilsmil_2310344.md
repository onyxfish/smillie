## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 4, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katharine & Elizabeth ret. from New Haven

Still beautiful weather. Tile men came \+ finished the laying of border around hearth in my bed room \+ the plaster men came \+ finished putting up the little moulding between cove \+ ceiling. I got to studio at 10. John, the new man, had let me find out it was cold. I finished cleaning up acid Room \+ got every thing ready to begin biting on Borden plates. Mr. \+ Mrs. Stahr — parents of Fred C. Stahr — my Etching pupil came to see me for half an hour or more sang the praises of their boy who, for a year or more past, has been painting in Madrid — they are to send some of his plates to me that I may get $5.— for "est Pencil" — Then I went to Kieser \+ Amend \+ got muriatic Acid \+ returning to studio made up a lot of fresh Dutch Mordant — Home to lunch at 1 — Katharine \+ Elizabeth had Returned from their new haven visit — to studio again \+ got my plate into the acid — every thing went beautifully but, of course, slowly — I did not make much advance \+ at 4.30 had to give up for want of light — went to Wanamaker's to learn why Franco-Persian Rugs had not been sent \+ countermanded order — Then home, stopping on my way for a few minutes, at Kelpie's — Dinner at 6.30.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 5, 1901

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Election day.

overcast \+ raw — light rain in the m'f. clearing at night. I got up with a severe headache that interfered with my comfort \+ work all day. After bkfst. went to Charl's \+ saw him for a few minutes. Then to studio \+ got things in train to begin "biting" Borden Place — got plate in acid before going home to lunch — After lunch went to polling place — 640 6th Ave. \+ voted a straight Citizen's Union ticket hoping that our plundered city may be rescued. Then to studio \+ continued biting until near 5" — then walked the streets till after 6 — dinner at 6.30 — evg. I continued \+ finished letter to Ralph
