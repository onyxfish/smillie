## Left Page

### Wed. June 8, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After some gentle rain last night the m'f the muggy, sullen character of yesterday, but later it cleared charmingly, bright \+ reasonably cool — about 5.30 there was a sudden change — wind shifting east \+ blowing in a sea-fog — about 8 p.m. showers \+ thunder. I awoke about 4 A.M. with distressing head ache — drugged myself — \+ again, an hour or two later — the pain very severe — but at 7 I was able to get up, staggering about — ate a little bkft. \+ went to Bornemann's \+ got some photo. material — went to Charles — then my m'f. chores \+ to studio — my head clearing with the weather — unusual activity of kidneys — spent the rest of the m'f. developing the half-dozen film exposures that I made last Sunday — none of them good for various reasons — Home to lunch at 1 — went down town to Charles at 2. — together we went to No. German Lloyd's \+ finally took Able Room 266 Princess Irene — from Genoa — Sep. 22? I am to go to Hamburg — Am. Line — Fake Return passage — get 10% dis — then return to No. Ger. Lloyd \+ transfer to Princess Irene — went to H.A. office — but there was a rush on \+ we left — I ref. to studio at 4 — at 5 I was at H. D. Allen's the fussed for awhile at my teeth — then I walked home — 74th St. \+ Bdway \+ across Park — in time for dinner at 6.30 — Ev'f. wrote some notes —

## Right Page

### Thur. June 9, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 59°

A dismal day — cold N.E. wind \+ heavy showers. Ther. below 60° — three days ago, in the streets it was 90°. After bkft. I spent some time writing to miss Stokes — when I got to studio I found Zogbaum preparing to get his big Dewey picture \+ frame out of my large window — as it cannot be got down the stairs. I objected \+ had it run out thro' the slit in my west wall. I was obliged to clear away lots of things \+ there was trouble in rigging tackle to brace on wall. It was noon before this was all over — then I washed the brushes that had hardened while I have been at home sick — Home to lunch at 1 — after lunch Herbert Smith came about insurance on Studio bldg. To studio at 3 \+ till 5.30 — I was clearing \+ cleaning \+ preparing to paint on Nes ruins to-morrow. Then home \+ lay on lounge reading Ev'f just till dinner at 6.30 — Ev'f. wrote to Smith — to Kramer, the photo-gravure man — \+ to Klemmel \+ Voigt.
