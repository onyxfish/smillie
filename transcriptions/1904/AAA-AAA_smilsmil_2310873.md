## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 30, 1904

**Weather:** mf. overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

mf. overcast \- a damp mist pervaded \-
later it cleared beautifully with strong
breeze \- This continued rising as the day ad-
vanced \+ something of a sea rose \- The damp-
ness increased \+ again the sky was over-
cast \+ the weather could hardly be described
as pleasant. Up at 7 \- had a breezing on
deck before bkft. at 8 \- Saw Capt. \+ asked
him if he could show me a chart of the
Azores \- Told me to come "to the bridge" at 10 \-
I did so \+ he not only showed me chart \+
let me make notes but he loaned me a copy
of the N°. Atlantic Directory \- a large book with
much very interesting information as to winds,
tides, currents \- steamer routes with sailing
directions \+ general descriptions. The day
passed, reading it \+ the usual amt. of loafing,
eating \+ sleeping \- on deck for awhile at 10.30
everything wet \- starlight overhead \- a wet looking
moon rising from a vaporous bank of clouds.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 1, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A squally day after a squally night \- strong S.W.
wind \- a moderate sea \+ squalls of driving rain
from time to time \- between times the sun
co? shine out \- had a long spell of it in the
afternoon \- seemed as tho' it had cleared
for good but rain came again in the
ev'g. The day was passed as usual \-
buffeted by wind on deck \- reading in
state-Room \- sleeping \+ eating \- Temp.
very mild \-
