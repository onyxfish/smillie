## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 11, 1904

**Weather:** Cloudless \+ very cold  **Temp:** Ther. fell to 9°

during the night \+ was at 21° when I got up at 7 o'clk. I had my scrub \+ at 8.30 went to studio \+ fixed furnace fire so that it c°. heat the bldg. Then back to hkfp°. At 10.30 Ret°. to studio \+ painted Till after 1 o'clk. changing the clouds very much \- adding more light. Then I had a short promenade on Park Ave. \+ just before 2 o'clk. went to see Charl \- Home to dinner at 2 \+ at 3.30 again went to Charl's \+ saw the very interesting photos \+ specifications of an electric mule designed to haul boats on a canal \- C. is to send them to Germany to interest parties there. Then to studio, cleaned palette. Home at 6, after calling at Ed°. Cook's \+ seeing Edna Bo[illegible] Murphy \+ arranging with them for a studio call next Wed. Found Lizzie \+ Jessie Smillie at the house — they stayed to tea \+ left at 8.30

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 12, 1904

**Weather:** more snow \- continued thro' most  **Temp:** [illegible]

of the day. After bkfp°. I wrote to Jim \+ Ralph \+ a note to Mr. Sidney Smith. It was about 11 when I got to studio. I saw a few spots of on River's Source that I thought should be subdued \+ some changes in detail \- These I bettered I think by 1 o'clk \- \+ then I concluded that it was as good as I knew how to make it (\+ that was no praise) \+ I would work no more on it before I sent it to the Academy 246°. when it comes back no doubt I shall then see lots more to do on it \- Home to lunch afternoon I gave to developing films \- The Kodak machine does not yet work satisfactorily \- there are dark bands across some of the films that I cannot account for. It was 6.30 before I got home to dinner Evely. I should have written my Xmas letter to Kate but I felt too strapped \+ so, fooled the time away
