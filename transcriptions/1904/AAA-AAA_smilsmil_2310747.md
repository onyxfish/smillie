## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 20, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day, dark, raw + cold. Some very light pellets of frozen snow fell in the aft., about 2 o'clk. Some sleet free that froze into a thin film of ice making traffic on the asphalt pavements where the snow had been removed almost impossible. Rec'd. letter from Mr. Williams in violent contrast to his first one to me in the Ralph controversy, being extremely courtious + cordial — instatement a complete back-down from statements + expressions made in letter No. 1 — To studio at 10 — Transposed canvas of "Cumulus, after sunset" to new panel — back stretcher + then glazed portions + otherwise worked over it. At the last moment I determined to paint out cloud forms that come upon the top field of blue, leaving the cumulus to rise supreme — then washed up + home to lunch + at 1.45 started for h.a. — lots of trouble on trolley cars — abandoned them + went up on elevated with etching class till nearly 5 — Stahr did some very good printing. Rec'd. home by elevated — Dinner — Evef went to Emmie d's with W's letter — She approved of the brief reply which I drafted + supplied the stationery upon which I wrote the fair copy that I mailed on my way home.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 21, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The nastiest sort of a day — not so cold as yesterday, by raw + dark + wet. The streets are in a filty condition. The roadways are hummpy + bumpy with ice — the sidewalks that yesterday were slippery were strewn with sand, ashes, dirt + sawdust + to-day they are indescribable muck — the street cleaners have been throwing ashes + even garbage into the street + covering them with snow — when a thaw comes it will be something awful. To studio at 10 — carried out my determination of yesterday + painted out the cloud forms on upper sky — much improved — about noon Will Parke came in — he says he wishes me to finish Vesuvius from near Sorrento (33×44) for him — Right away — I told him I that it worth $500. He says Bessie's husband, Percy Ballantine has an income of from $6000 to $70000 a year. + that she will come in + buy some of my paintings — home to lunch — back + attacked another part of Cumulus — painted a group of upright evergreens in the centre of the composition — washed up + at 5 went to Alexanders for my shoe — walked home — Dinner — Evef wrote part of letter to Ralph.
