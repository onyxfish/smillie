## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 6, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some rain during the night — clouded up, but clearing about the middle of the day — Ufo rather late + had bkft. at about 9 — Then reading paper till Geo. came in — he sat with me till nearly 11 o'clk — then I worked repacking rice after 12 — then had a scrub + shampoo that occupied me till 2 o'clk — then had lunch — After that, fussing about till near 4 o'clk — then I had a little nap — after my lunch I went to butcher's + got a steak — stopped at studio for a few minutes — Mary, in the absence of any girl, cooked a very simple + acceptable dinner which we ate at 6.30 — Well, in my easy chair, by student lamp, with feet to fire, I read my paper — I have promptly fallen into the old, well worn rut.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 7, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cool, crisp day — such an one as should make a fellow feel snapper, but I do not respond — I feel dull + listless with a discomfort in my head that does not amt. to an actual headache — part of the head trouble is catahrral, I think — I awoke at 4 a.m. + did not get to sleep again — Bkft. soon after 8 — Colored woman, engaged by Mary, came soon after to begin the cleaning of studio — I went there about 10 + Jennings followed with her — I started the work + left them — went to grocer's + butcher's met Mary Cook — then to bank + arranged about delivery of Nesquehoning stock — then to Scribner's + saw Mr. H. L. Smith for a moment. then home to lunch — after that I lay on my bed for some time but did not sleep. Later I got up + wrote letter to Mary + Kate + mailed it for to-morrow's steamer — most of evg. with newspaper, as of old —
