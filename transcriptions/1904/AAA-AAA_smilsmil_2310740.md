## Left Page

### Monday Jan. 4, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bitter cold day - bright sunshine, but not the slightest indication of thaw at midday in full sunshine - Got to studio at about 10 primed for the work that I had planned on "Passing Showers" - found the Ther. in my studio registering 45° \+ but a small fire in furnace - Jennings not in evidence - I piled in coal - oksened the draughts \+ then went home - did some photo printing \+ ret'd to studio a little before 12 - Ther. then 62°. I painted till 1 on 14×18 - Cumulus Clouds, Wely. \+ then went home to lunch - back at 2.15 \+ painted for another hour - then cleaned up \+ home before 5. after going to Miller's in 40° S. Dinner at 6.30. Harold Milbank dined with us on Jim's invitation \+ later they went to Weber \+ Field's Theatre - a quiet even'g going over my accts. till a few min after 10 - while writing this an engine came struggling thro the snow \+ attached to hose to the hydrant across the street in front of our house - Mr. Knowlton's house was on fire - Ralph \+ went out - the first story seemed to be filled with flame \+ smoke \+ promised a conflagration - but in less than an hour it was subdued. Ther. outside at this time 3°.

## Right Page

### Tuesday Jan. 5, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another brilliant \+ excessively cold day - Ther. stood at 3° above zero till noon. I went to studio about 10 \+ finding it too cold for work I went to grocers, butchers \+ then to the bank \+ got back to studio at 11.15 - then to work on Passing Showers, repainting water - Home to lunch at 1 - back soon after 2 \+ worked till 3.30 - then washed up \+ home at 4.30 - Before 5 went with the boys to the G.C. station. Ralph to take the 5.05 train to Tarrytown. Found great crowd \+ much confusion - all in-coming trains "indefinitely late" (as per bulletin) - out going trains not ready \+ employees running wildly about - Waited till 5.30 - a big crowd \+ so signs of 5.05 on the track I told R. that I thought it unsafe to travel at such time \+ under such conditions - so I had him return to the house with me - Jim went to Harold Milbank's to dinner \+ later came home \+ he too stayed overnight - Dinner at 6.30 - Every. by the fire - some reading \+ some writing.
