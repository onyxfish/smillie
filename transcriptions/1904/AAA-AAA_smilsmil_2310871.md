## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 26, 1904

339 m. arrived at Gibralter.
980 m. from Genoa.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day - after a night thro' which I did a fair amt. of sleeping I awoke in as good shape as could be expected - I got up at about 7. very groggy \+ weak - I had an exceedingly light bkft. at 8 \+ then waited for Gibralter - It was nearly 11 when we came to anchor there - after some delay a comfortable tender came \+ took all who cared to go at 50 cts. to wharf \+ back - a Black board was put up "the boat will Return at 2 \- Gibraltar time" - that gave us just two hours on shore - A Cook man was on board selling carriage tickets for a tour of the sights of the town - I bought one for $2. - Promptly on arriving ashore we were put into a conveyance \+ driven [illegible] - intended able to speak some English - \+ willing - \+ with a large party to Galleries - entered names on board - \+ with a large party were put in chg of a good-looking \+ civil soldier - had an uphill pull for some distance thro' a sunken way before we entered the Rock - The galleries are all on an upgrade of perhaps 20 to 25 in 100 - they are not so large as I expected - The Rock is evidently soft \+ crumbly - Port holes small \+ opportunity for handling large guns nil - but we were allowed to see but one small portion \+ in that there were a few old guns - our guide pointed to higher guns saying that these was the important portion of the work one gallery having room for 400 men - The place was pretty tough for me in my condition but I got thro' all right - Then drove to the Alameda, or Parada - Then to the dry-dock in course of construction - then to Europa Point - then back thro' town - bought P.O. cards \+ sent one to many, one to Kate - then to Market \+ bought some pine grapes - \+ time being nearly up we went on board The Andiro at 2.30 were again aboard the Irene - It was 3 when I very cautiously ate a little lunch \+ after taking such siesta as the ascending shore of Europe \+ Africa I rested in my bunk for an hour. There have been up on deck \+ sat in my chair till dinner at 7 - there have been several changes of persons at our table - it seems to be settled now - there is a lady, her daughter (of perhaps 17) \+ a cub of a boy - two young fellows - came aboard at Gibraltar - have been doing Tangier \+ most of Spain in a fortnight - \+ a gay party of three - so far we are spoken to be harmonious - after dinner I was in State Room with Ralph till near 10 - then up on deck \+ sat in moonlight for half an hour - then turned in -

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 27, 1904

from Gibraltar 324 m. \+ noon. To N.Y. 2906.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully beautiful day - brilliantly clear - beautiful clouds - warm enough - cool enough \+ breezy or quiet as you chose to go on one side or the other of the vessel - clouds of unpromising kind gathered after sunset \+ the moon had a look that was wet. all day the swell had been increasing \+ by every. the Irene pitched a good deal but rolled not at all - I started the day at 7 \+ was up \+ down - awake \+ asleep - till I turned in after 10 p.m. I have no walking legs yet \+ most of my time is spent loafing - I read \+ sleeps, eat a little \+ think about nothing.
