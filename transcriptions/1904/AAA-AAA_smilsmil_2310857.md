## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 29, 1904

Meran - Delpeudence Hotel Graf von Meran Rooms 2 & 3. Pension Steinhauser -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb m't after 2 or 3 dubious early hours. I was up + had b'kfst. soon after 7 - the boys got up later - After consultation with head Porter + the Barometer I decided to make the Trafoi Trip + leave here at 3 P.M. for Meran - So trunks were packed to be sent to Zurich + all preparations made + I we strolled out + about till 1 when we ret'd to hotel + had tabled' hote lunch in the great new dining room built very recently - immense plate glass windows looking out to mts + garden - a big thing - Troubles commenced soon after in trying to get the trunks sent on ahead to Zurich while we took the Round by (Bozen, Meran + Trafoi to Landeck - The t. train arr. not start till 7 P.M. + there was a crush of baggage ahead for the 3 P.M. Train - I required three Porters + about $1.50 in bribes + tips to accomplish it, but not till all other baggage was out of the way - then I had to run my train after exhausting excitement - The Trip once the Brenner + down to Bozen recalled many memories of the time 12 years ago when Annie + I, with the boys, came over it from Innsblock to Innsbruck - The day was a glorious contrast to our dismal experience of yesterday - we got to Bozen at about 7.30 + waited there 35 mins. before we started for Meran, to which place we got at 9 o'clk. + were at once beset by a horde of hackmen + drivers offering us all sorts of inducements to hire their outfits for Trafoi - we worked loose from them + got into a bus for the Graf von Meran where we arrived after a time to be followed by a persistent driver who professed to have a Return carriage for Trafoi that he would let us have at less than Diligence Ratio - I doubted the honesty of both the porter + the driver + decided to take the Diligence Seats - At 9.30 the boys had a hearty supper - I had a glass of beer, a Roll + a Raw egg - then to our Room in an old house, back in a dark little garden a short distance from the hotel. Our Rooms were decent but poor.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 30, 1904

Hotel Trafoi - Rooms 83 - 84 c

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day - very mild - Up early - had b'kfst. before 8 o'clk - then off for Trafoi at 8.30 - The promise made by the hotel man that I sh'd. have three front seats on the Diligence was not kept - I had seat next driver - he + I the only ones on it - one seat on the front coupé + one on the back one - both end seats + good but not together as promised - We were able to see how beautifully Meran is situated - high mts on three sides + open to the South - we started with four horses, but a few miles out two more were added while we climbed a long grade - After that thro' a long, wide valley, with a great number of vineyards - much of the way the wind was with us - then the dust from the macadam road was discomforting - The glare from the white road was trying - The mountain tops were bare of snow except the very highest + at times the long ride became monotonous - my seat with the driver having no back, tired me - do I change ed to the one behind it, more comfortable + equally good for seeing - one of the boys taking my seat - we made two or three stops to bait the horses + at about 12.30 got to a village, Schlanders, where we had a filling dinner - a novel menu - served in a large alpine room on second story - The towns we went thro' were picturesque, stone, stucco faced + white washed buildings strung along the road, crowding it so closely that many times there was barely time for two teams to pass - houses in all cases a combination of dwelling, barn + stable - We went on after a 45 minute dinner, with now + then a fine gorge with high peaks beyond on our left (South) the mts on the north side were many times bare of trees or snow - without cultivation + unlovely - passed a number of castles - some of them fine - About 3 o'clk we came to where we turned from the Saudeck Road. - There we stopped for 20 m. + had beer or coffee - Then on for a mile or two, straight across a flat valley - + then at once entered the mountains on the celebrated Stelvio Pass Road over into Italy - for 3 hours we climbed slowly a splendid Road up an unbroken grade - Soon we began to see the fine, snow covered peaks of the great winter groups ahead of us - + looking back could see fine snow peaks in the distance - The effects grew constantly in impressiveness - as the eve't drew it was cooler + I walked a good part of the way - we got to the hotel at about 7 - very dirty, but got some of the dust off before we went to table d'hote dinner at 7.30 self. Rather dreary - hotel full of people. To bed early.
