## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 28, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An overcast day - threatening snow - to studio after 10 \+ got to work again on Vesuvius - all day on sky - which I get into better shape, I think. To lunch as usual \+ back - Afternoon Maurice Sterne came in - I had thought he had gone abroad under his scholarship but he told a sad story of the long illm. \+ finally, the death, of his brother, who was buried a few days ago - \+ the serious illness of his mother - its movements will now depend upon her condition. - I got home soon after 5 - feeling very tired \+ rested till dinner - Evett Charl came in \+ spent the evef with us.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 29, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

About 3" of snow fell last night - was much more thro' the day - fine, light dry snow - it covers the dirty ice that clings to sidewalks \+ causeways - hiding it for the time, at least. I got to studio at usual time \+ painted all day on Vesuvius, getting in the mts \+ the sea - not as I would like to but in a way that suggests better things in the next painting. Mollie Adams came in for a few minutes to say that she will not stay with us to-night - but on Sunday night - To lunch as usual. Painted till after 4 - then gave up very tired - home at 5 \+ had a rest before dinner. Evett - wrote to Jim \+ Ralph. out at 10 \+ mailed letters - Still snowing.
