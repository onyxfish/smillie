## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still fine weather - so mild that during the middle of the day I went about without my light overcoat - I had bkfs. soon after 8 - Sadie still serving us. Mary H. started at once upon a hunt for servants - I was engaged till nearly looking over letters etc that were waiting + then I went to laundry + from there rode down to Wanamakers + got prices on crockery + kitchen ware destined at the Ark - from there to Second Natl. Bank - got bonds left with bank as security for letter of Cr. + rel. them to my safe box. Home to lunch at 1 - after that at work upon bank acct. + Stokes accts two problems to be worked out - then, at 3, went to studio for a little while - there home + for the rest of the afternoon wrote letter to Mary + Katie - at 6 went to Charls + learned that he was to arrive from Boston at 9.45 - then home to dinner - then finished letter + at 9.30 went to station to meet Charl - the train came in but he was not on it - then to his house + found that he had ret'd on 8 o'clk. train - we talked till after 11 - then home + to bed shortly before midnight -

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine, mild day - Bkfs. at usual time - then some writing until mid-morning - then to grocer's + butchers - then to Alexander's + ordered a pair of shoes to take the place of the pair that I paid for last June but did not get - then to Brooks Bros. + tried on some clothes - Home to lunch at 1 - afternoon worked out my financial matters - letter of Cr. European Expenses - Bank balance etc. successfully - also the Stokes acct. About 5 went again to Brooks Bros. + got a suit - home to dinner at 6.30 - Sadie still doing the work in the kitchen. Mary has engaged a cook to come next Monday - also a chambermaid - Evel., bro. George \+ Geo. Cook + Emily came in.
