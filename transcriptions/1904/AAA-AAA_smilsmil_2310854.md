## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 21, 1904

Salzburg. Nelböck Hotel. Room 69. annex.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely mf. at 5 a light spatter of rain — later, clear & mild — then very warm to hot with indications of electric disturbance — clouded over at 5 & at 7 a heavy thunderstorm at Salzburg. Up at 6.30. Bkft. at 8 — then walked a pleasant half mile along lake shore to Bahnhof for Rack & Pinion Road to top of Shafberg. Train started at 8 — most of the way 25% grade — a very interesting ride — very near an hour in getting to the top — a few rods of walk after leaving train — then reached a fine, 5900 ft — a superb view — mts on one side — plains on the other. a few snow mts — a one very irregular shapes, can be seen in distance — the Attersee, Mondsee, St. Wolfgang & one or two other sheets of water at our feet — an inspection satisfied me that nothing was to be gained by going over St. Wolfgang & Mondsee by boat — we did get just as fine views from the train & it skirt ed these waters — stayed more than an hour on the top — atmosphere getting more hazy — at 11.20 started down — taking as long to go down as it did to climb to the top — walked to hotel & had dinner on the big veranda overlooking lake — sunny but in spite of awnings & the boat to come — lake for train to Salzburg — did not start till 3.20 so we went up to our room — the boys had a note \+ wrote another section of letter to Charl — after some confusion about the several boats we got started on the right one — Train on time & for a good part of the way we enjoyed 1st class section — the two friends & women with the music and luggage got in — they were 1st class & we crowded in but the guard allowed them to remain as we made no serious fuss. — sky clouded over — coming thunder storm about 5 o'clk — a part of the ride after leaving Mondsee was of common place sort but the approach to Salzburg in spite of clouded sky was interesting — many glimpses of distant mts. being fine — the entrance to Salzburg is not picturesque — past brickyards — clay heaps — R.R. shops &c. with much smoke & dirt. — We got in at our 69 — at once walked to Hotel del' Europäer — a handsome bldg near Station, in fine park — no rooms — our tracer piloted us farther to the Nelböck — also in fine park. Almost no rooms here — but an extra bed was put us & the three of us were crowded into one small room in a dependence across the road, with promise that something better may be done to-morrow — wrote closing section in letter to Charl — then had our supper to the accompaniment of thunder, Rain & wind — later, went to our room & having nothing better to do went to bed at 9.30 — horrible bed — dome shaped — insufficient bed covering unless the ridiculous feather beds, square, — long enough only to cover ones legs or shoulders — were used.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 22, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The mf. was cloudy & cool — Rain commenced falling about 7 o'clk. — let up for awhile about 3–4 then came again heavily & continuously for the rest of the day. Bkft. about 9 & soon after we started for sight seeing — thro' Stadt park — over Franz-Karl bridge \+ along the foot of the Monchsberg to the tunnel — thro' it — visited the little Grotto where a stalactite & stalagmite formation is going on — back thro' tunnel — past Holy stables & marble "Rose trough" — thro' the burial ground of St. Peter — into chapels of St. Maximus & St. Gertrude & on to the Cable Railway by which we were hoisted into the Hohen-Salzburg or citadel, now a barracks — made the round of the castle under guidance of a picturesque old woman & were greatly interested in the deep, curious full of hideous traditions — thro' some old Rooms "Restored" in glaring colors & to terraces & Tower tops where we had wonderful views of plains & distant mts. We returned to street level by a long, rapidly descending road, thro' many courts & under archways — very interesting & at the last stage came upon a little terrace with one seat under some trees from which we had a most delicious view over the plain to the mountains — the Castle & its views one of the most picturesque & interesting combinations that I know of — on reaching the Rose trough — thro' the Dom Platz & Residenz Platz, crossed the Stadt bridge & then hurried back to hotel as the Rain was beginning to fall — after a rest, for we got quite warm in our running — we had dinner — & had our room changed — going from the little room with its three beds up a flight higher to No. 70 & 71 — larger, more convenient — as it rained & we could not go out we loafed away the time till after 4 when, as the Rain had ceased, we again went out, walked thro' some of the streets & bought a number of photos — then the Rain came on so violently that we had to get a hack & ride back to hotel — until 7 o'clk — read Hall Cain's The Shadow of a Crime — then our evening meal — the boys had a stupid evening — and I finished my novel — if it is a good example of Caine, I cannot his popularity — to bed early.
