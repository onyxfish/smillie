## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 30, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - breezy \+ cool but pleasant on the sunny side of the street. Up at 7 - had a good scrub \+ at 9. Had my 6×[illegible] - after that photo-printing till 12 - Then rode down the Bowery to Delancy St. \+ from there walked to the [illegible] bury bridge \+ half way across it - I made four exposures - using up a film that had some [illegible] exposures on it - It was very cold \+ uncomfortably windy up on the bridge. Home to dinner at 2 - Later in the afternoon I had a nap - Tea about 7 - [illegible] Charl \+ George came in \+ we looked over the European photos. that I brought back with me -

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 31, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still wonderfully fine weather - To studio about 10 - Douglass' men washed the ceiling of stairway \+ put another coat of Kalsoming on it - I worked, experimenting upon the 1" photo-grav. plate from Kramer - Home to lunch at 1 - at 2 went to 2?. Nat[illegible] - Then to studio \+ continued my experiments till 5 - Then Geo? \+ I walked down to D. B. Butler's \+ planned a new frame for my 33×44 painting "The River's Source". We then walked up to 42?. S[illegible] \+ I. home. Dinner at 6.30 - At about 8 curiosity led me to the Mad. Sq. Garden where a great Parker Mass meeting is under way - I found no end of policemen guarding all approaches to the building - very small crowd out side \+ no excitement - home at 9 - read newspapers \+ to bed.
