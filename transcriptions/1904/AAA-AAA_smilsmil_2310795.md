## Left Page

### Mon. April 25, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain sometime during the night - dubious m't - clearing close \+ warm - To studio before 10 \+ painted on Vesuvius - a very trying time - nothing went right - gave up before 12.30 - went to barber's \+ had hair cut - home at 1.30 Mr. Walter James waiting for me with a sketch \+ design for glass mosaic to be put up in Scranton Church by Will Parke as a memorial to his father - After lunch, on my way to studio I met Chas. Collins - he came back to the house to look at framed etchings - then I went with him to his house \+ took print to studio to be mounted - I painted till near 5 o'clk \+ then gave up - very tired \+ much disheartened - I went home \+ had a short rest before dinner - After that I read my Post \+ then wrote notes to Kuntzy \+ Barr -

## Right Page

### Tues. April 26, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some rain during the night. Clear \+ warm for a time in the m'f - then overcast \+ rain at night. To studio before 10 \+ painted on Vesuvius - coming down on the right hand side from distance to foreground \+, I think, improving it - home to lunch as usual - back at 2.30, painted till 4.30 - washed up \+ home at 5.30 - dinner - Eve'g Rec'd note from Miss Mitchell from Aitten - S.C. - enclosing a check for $300 from Miss Caroline Phelps Stokes - I went to Walter's to enquire of Louise concerning common usage in the matter of cutlery \+ like ware in renting summer houses furnished - The Misses Stokes are asking me to furnish "Cutlery" - Back home at 9.30 - Eleg. Coffin was delivered of a daughter last night - 7½ lbs.
