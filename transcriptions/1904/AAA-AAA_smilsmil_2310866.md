## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 16, 1904

A fine day but a high wind blew pretty steadily from the north. I got up early \+ wrote a letter to Uncle Willie, congratulating him on his 93 birth-day (Sep. 23°) \+ one to Charl. \- At 10.30 we took a rowboat \+ went out for a Random scoot on the lake \- but the old fellow who rowed us was so impervious to our french that we had hard work \- tho' he was willing enough, to get where we wished we went to where we had a view of the Fiume dalla gorge but the old man seemed to be averse to our going nearer \- saying it was unimportant \- Trop see... so we drifted along near to the Vareuna terraces \+ got a conflee or more photo, then back to hotel at 12 \- lunch at 12.30 \+ soon after 2 o'clk we started on steamboat for Colico \- Wind so strong as to nearly blow the hair off our heads \- I found the upper end of lake very interesting \- if there had been snow, as usual, on the mt. tops, portions we have been grand. It was after 4 when we got to Colico \+ nearly 6.30 when we got back to Bellagio\- we had dinner at 7 \- Eve'g. I was tired \+ sat reading Room for a time with news papers. A man \+ woman had advertised to appear in the eve'g. to entertain us with necromantic tricks \+ music \- but nobody cared for it \- all were out on the terraces to watch a poor attempt at illumination in honor of the birth of a son to the Italian King yesterday or this m'g. The necromantic gentleman, in black silk stockings \+ pumps \+ his musical consort, packed, in evident disgust, the kit that had been spread out \+ took themselves off.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 17, 1904

Milan \- Hotel de Ville \- Rooms 201\- 202\.

Charming day \- Up before 6 \+ packed trunk \- off. at 8 \- settled bills, paid tips \+ went to Steamboat landing at 9 \- It was 20 m. after that when the boat came \+ we started for Como\- The sail was pleasant but the scenery was uninteresting when compared with the upper part of the lake or even Lecco \- We reached Como at about 11.30 \+ to me the place appeared so uninviting as seen from the boat that I decided to go right on to Milan \+ not to wait for a later train as at first I planned \- so we piled into a non-descript sort of conveyance at the dock \+ with little delay got to the R.R. station \+ were off for Milan at about 12\. our R.R. car was the shabbiest we have yet seen \- our train was a slow one. Taking 1 hour \+ 20 m. to go 29 miles \- our fellow travelers were four men \+ one woman, natives \- Two of the men were very talkative \+ amused us greatly by their vehemence \+ gesticulations \- We got to Milan at 1.20 \+ found great confusion there \- a strike was on that tied up local transit of every kind \- every body had to walk \- So, under direction of a kindly porter we left our hand luggage in the parcels dep't. \+ walked to the Hotel de Ville \- a good long walk \- \+ there we got good rooms \+ a good lunch \- our hand luggage was got by hotel porters in an hour or two \- but we did not get our trunks. After lunch we walked out \+ found the streets crowded with shouting people but otherwise saw no excitement \- a Reg't of Soldiers, in camp rig't without music, marched by us, without commotion. Two or three times crowds were hustled \+ dispersed by police without evidence of ill will. The boys dined at Table d' hote \- but I took nothing.
