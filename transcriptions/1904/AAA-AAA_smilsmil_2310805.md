## Left Page

### Sun. May 15, 1904

**Weather:** Heavy rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain in the m'f. clearing in the afternoon - a shower between 6 & 7 P.m. I got up before 7 \+ from that till 9 I was busy with scrubbing \+ shampooing. After bkfst. newspaper \+ podging about getting ready for travel to-morrow. at 1 o'clk I lay down on my bed \+ had a very good nap before dinner at 2 - After dinner I went to studio and cleared up there - Zogbaums was in \+ at his request I went up \+ looked at his painting of Dewey entering the harbor of Manilla - Then I prowled the streets for awhile \+ home - Chase came in bet. 5 \+ 6 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 16, 1904

**Weather:** Fine  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine m'f. - cold rain latter part of day - Up at 6 \+ before bkfst. wrote a letter of condolence to Louis Tiffany whose wife died a few days ago - Bkfst. at 7.45 \+ at 8.45 Mary H. \+ I started for St. Hubert's on the fast mail - weather was pleasant till we passed the Catskill mts - rain began before we got to Albany a little before noon - no dining car till Whitehall. at 2.30 - Too late for us, so I got sandwiches at the Albany restaurant - a succession of showers till we neared Westport - due there at 4.12 - it was 4.35 when we got there - Team from Kelloggs met us \+ we drove over to E-town comfortably in spite of mud \+ cold - cordially greeted by Kelloggs - father, son \+ daughter - had a front supper by an open wood fire - Spent the eve'g till 9 o'clk chatting with Miss Kellogg - in front of big fire in the new big fire place, of stone, in the office.
