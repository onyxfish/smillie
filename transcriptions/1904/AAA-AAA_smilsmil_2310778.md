## Left Page

### Tues. March 22, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, muzzling day with fog - All day at work at studio "Taking account of stock" of B.P. of etchings by myself + getting some examples into frames for the St. Louis Exh'n. It wearied me greatly + discouraged me. - Home to lunch at 1.30 - back at 3. + continued at my work till near 6, interrupted at times by young etchers anxious about their exhibits + by having to telephone in different directions to learn how the work of getting exhibits ready is coming on - Home at 6.15 completely tired out - dinner at 6.30 - well a sort of loaf - + to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 23, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast but not unpleasant. After a wakeful night I got up with a headache that unfitted me for work all day - I was able to go to studio in the m'f. + got my St. Louis exhibit in finished condition. I shall send eight examples. 3 dry-points - 1 point etching - one soft-ground - one aquatint - 1 mezzotint + a little celluloid plate with drypoint not inked ready for proof. Nicoll is to send one of my reproductive etchings. At 1 o'clk. I went to barber's + had hair cut. Then phoned to etching class - No go - that I would not be able to meet them - Then home went to bed + was there till 6.30 when I got up + ate a little - At 8.30 at Century - Com- on admissions - these till Recess at 10 - then I slipped out + went home.
