## Left Page

### Wed. July 20, 1904

**Weather:** Brilliant cool day  **Temp:** [illegible]

we left the hotel at 8.30 a.m. \+ got back at 10.30 p.m. after 14 hours of constant work - went first to Victory mon. - The boys climbed to top - I had been up on Mond - then we went to Reichstag \+ with a great company were escorted about the building. The guide delivering his lecture in German, very rapidly - always winding up with a joke - very amusing to the crowd but lost upon us - the building is very imposing \+ the interior is very ornate in parts - The great hall for the Bundesrat is sumptuous \+ so is the Reading Room for the Bund - The Eastern entrance - the stairway - the bronze statues of early emperors, very fine - glass in windows, bad - The Vorsal for the Reichstag was very odd in its ceiling decoration \+ not pleasing in color to me but very effective in photo - went in into Tribune \+ to the Kings box - then in an hour \+ some to Natl gallery - I went thro' that thoroughly - a noble bldg - but as a practical arrangement of galleries \+ light for showing pictures I do not think it a complete success - as a collection of pictures it was very disappointing to me - many bearing celebrated names were not representative - the Portrait of Mommsen by Prof. Knaus impressed me most of all the works - Lenbachs portraits I cannot admire. The staircase I think much more effective, architecturally \+ decoratively, than the one in the old Museum - The top on third floor, I think fine - color \+ form - The large paintings by Mallart \+ Piloty very decorative \+ harmonious - then to Bourse - quiet compared to N.Y. - with garden \+ Restaurant court evidently they fake business easier - then to a cheap Rest. Kaiser Wm. Str. Luther Pl. \+ had a good lunch - potato soup, veal \+ beer - then car to Rathaus \+ climbed the tower - fine view - then with guide thro' the bldg - nothing remarkable - frescoes at top of staircase ptd by Mühlenbruch I liked very much - then cars to Potsdamer Platz \+ there took another line out to St. Hubertus \+ at a Restaurant there, by the side of a little lake of greenish water had a cup of coffee - then on by same line of cars to Hundeforte [illegible] by a short walk reached a popular Restaurant - beside a larger lake - loafed about there awhile - then back to cars \+ went on to Halleusee - at about 6 - went to new \+ very popular Resort - Halleusee Terrace - one of the most astonishing \+ unique places of the kind that I have ever seen - looked like a glorified straw Babylonish architecture - four great Pagodas or stages of Tables - thousands of them - found place in centre - at 6.30 we had an excellent dinner with wine \- $2.50 for the three - regular programme of music - sat at Table near 8 - charming evening effects from our terrace over the water \+ woods - then as every bell rang we promenaded about over the terraces from roof to ground \+ out upon the lawn to the water - abortly illumination of colored glass Pagodas \+ towers \+ electric color - illumination of four tiers - ruddy glow at horizon - indigo blue in East - moon 14 old - illumination of towers \+ terraces - music \+ vast throngs - Home at 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 21, 1904

**Weather:** A pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

we were all pretty well tired out by our work yesterday \+ got up late. Bkfst about 9 - then we sat in Reading Room for some time \+ read papers - then to my room where I wrote diary of yesterday - still feeling best we loafed about till after 12 when I took camera \+ we started for the Dom \+ that neighborhood to get some snap shots - clouded over with promise of continuance - ret? to hotel - got lunch - then to Potsdamer Platz \+ took electric cars to Zoo - elevated \+ under ground - 3d. rail - 2 cars in train, 2.03 class only - very small - everything small - train every 5 m. - very heavy grades when going underground or coming up - at the Zoo we changed to the Ringbahn - elevated, steam, R.R. \+ went to the Leicester St. - then out \+ to the Austelleing Park where the annual Summer Exhn of the Grosse Berliner Kunst Austelling is under way - the Exhibition is in a great shabby, barn-like bldg - with some of the exterior appearance of a defunct Crystal Palace - there are 43 enclosures or galleries - \+ one with much showy decoration
