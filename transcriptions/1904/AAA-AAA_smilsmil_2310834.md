## Left Page

### Tues. July 12, 1904

**Weather:** clear overhead \- hazy to foggy distance  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up before 7 \- clear overhead \- hazy to foggy distance vast number of gulls about \- point of land far away, projecting from fog bank \- Dkfp. at 8 \- passed point Lizard before 8.30 \- Took a shot at it \- Clearing beautifully \- after passing the point we were soon at sea again losing sight of land \- about noon it was very w\dom, a silvery mist hanging over the water \- Prof Boon had his class on the after deck, the band playing for the movements \- I took several snap shots at the class. I did not take any lunch, the memory of the Captain's dinner last night being sufficient \- but instead I took a nap \& after that finished letters to Surma S., Charl \- Katie Elkins \+ Will Parke \+ gave them to the Purser \- It is expected that the Dover passengers will be landed at about 5 o'clk. to\-morrow m'f.. so that we will get to Cuxhaven \+ be landed there at about 5 o'clk. Thurs. m'f.. \+ by train to Hamburg before 11 \- The rest of the afternoon was dawdled away till dinner at 7 \- a very quiet affair as compared with its predecessors \- There are many to land at Dover \+ there was much to achieve \+ preliminary leave takings \- the boys are quite distressed at the prospect of breaking up \- Jim expressed his readiness to continue the voyage indefinitely \- There was some music, singing by Coffid \+ Johnson after q in the saloon \- and the band played on the after deck where there was dancing \- I turned in soon after 10 \-

## Right Page

### Wed. July 13, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Splendid m'f.. \- cloudless \+ mild \- up at 4.30 \- Reached Dover soon after 5 \- \+ made fast to Pier at about 6 \- great hubbub \- passengers going \+ coming \- some for special train for London \- others for Calais \- steamers on to Paris \- a number got on for Hamburg. It was about 7.30 when two tugs pulled us around from the Pier \+ we got under way again \- We had little more sight of land again during the day. The weather was very warm \- a light haze hung up on the water which was smoother than any we have passed over since leaving N.Y. The day was decidedly monotonous \- The engineer told me that he w\ld let me take the boys down through the engine \+ into the stoke-hole at 3 o'clk. So at that hour we were at hand \- but it was after 3.30 before we got hold of him \- then we went down \+ found three or four Germans there \- we were told that there were tanks for 1000 tons water ballast \- that 450 tons of drinking water was carried \- They have a plant for distilling water but it is too expensive \- I packed at old \+ filled during the day \+ got nearly all of it done before dinner. During the afternoon I went among the steerage passengers \+ got two good shots I think \- Even the Germans had a good time in the saloon \- singing \+ chaffing one another \- Birnbaum played some of his best pieces on his violin \- it was all very amusing to me \+ was kept up till 11 o'clk. when the lights were turned off. Got to bed about 11.30
