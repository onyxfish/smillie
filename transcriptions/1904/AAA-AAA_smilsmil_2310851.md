### Mon. Aug. 15, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

furiously hot, in my room, northern exposure, it was above 80°. \+ it was a Church holiday - so between the heat \+ the holiday my plans for the day were much interfered with - the boys got up at cir 8 \+ at about 9 we went to a Café not far from the hotel \+ had our Coffee \+ Rolls like ordinary vienese - Then went past Karlsplatz Stadt bahn Station - to the Karla Kirche - a showy ch. commemorating a cholera visit - found a service in progress - then thro' Kartnerstrasse market - bought peaches - to the Roosemaiden fountain - to the Royal Stables but could get no permit - holiday - then I went to the Nat. Hist. Mus. - open because it is a holiday \+ the boys went to Palace \+ got permit to visit the Imperial Treasury \+ Ret'd to me at the museum - we went thro' the Saales on the "ground floor" - Mineralogical - Prehistoric \+ Ethnographic - a great work - a wonderful collection in a superb setting \- The museum was closed at 1 - Then we went to our favorite Restaurant \+ got mid-day meal - After that it was so hot that I told the boys I did not think it safe to go about in the streets - owing to holiday no stores were open - we Ret'd to hotel \+ remained there till after 4 - Then to Opera House - holiday - could not get entrance - we went to St Stephens - delightfully cool \+ sombre - for 1/4 of an hour before the great bell tolled - its reverberations thro' the bldg. very fine - at 5 there was a service of some sort - music - organ \+ choir - then intoning by the Priest \+ responses by the people - singular effect - the composite voice of the gathering of worshippers at the intersection of nave \+ transept - its rhythmic repetition - they were far from me as I sat at extreme end - the responses came like a small, recurring wave breaking upon a distant rock - I was deeply impressed - the interior is very noble - the light is beautifully subdued - Then we walked out to The Frahen Josephs quai \+ to some delightful park-terraces \+ there we sat under the trees \+ watched the people \+ children for nearly an hour - then the Sem leaving, gone down we took car down the Schotten Ring to Volksgartin at about 7.30 \+ then had our beer \+ bread \+ listened to the band of

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 16, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day - warm in sunshine but tempered by a refreshing breeze - Rec'd letter from Charl. Aug 5th enclosing two pitiful letters to me from Farrington. B\&fo. at 9 - At 10 we went to The Imp. \+ Royal Palace \+ in the Treasury saw the crowns, sceptres, Royal jewels, etc. etc - a dazzling show - some of the Relics had a historical interest aside from what they professed to be. From Then went to office \+ Opera Ho. nearby - where there are elaborate arrangements for the sale of tickets to Opera \+ Burg Theatre - were directed to Opera Ho. to get tickets to see inside of it - went - automaton gave us tickets at 60 k. each - guide took us thro' the bldg. onto the stage - up to the bridges \+ hoisting tackle - into dressing rooms - property rooms - to the loft where thousands of costumes for the various operas were arranged in closets - then to the foyer \+ great stairways - to the jet loge \+ to the Kaisor loge \+ to the very handsome apartments where he receives his guests - also his private entrance - the stage was lighted \+ filled with workmen setting up scenery for the premiere to be given Thurs. ev'g. Soon after we went to our favorite restaurant, Volksgartin \+ had dinner - From there to the Hofburg Theatre where we get admittance as at the Opera House - we were not taken to stage or dressing rooms but over the house - I never saw so beautiful a foyer or more palatial entrances, Forthere are two noble stairways, one on either side of the bell. The daylight in foyer \+ entrances was beautiful. The frescoes \+ statuary - the draperies, etc. superb. Here too the Kaiser has spacious apartments \+ reception of his friends \+ a fine private entrance - at 3 o'clk. we went to The Kunst Histor. Mus. \+ stayed till it closed at 4 - then again to the office of the Opera ho. \+ after some study bought for 24 K. loge No. 12 - 2 galerie links - for Thurs. ev'g. It was near 5 when I got to hotel for a rest. The boys went off somewhere - it was after 7 when we went out \+ had our supper in a Restaurant near of Hartner Str. \+ Wallfischgasse - fine situation - sat at window \+ watched passers-by, lighted streets - the Opera ho - etc - To Bathans at about 9 \+ went thro' the wine cellars there being personally conducted by a waiter who took us into the private cellar of the burgomasters - brilliantly lighted - fine portraits of head burgomasters - tables set - very inviting - no burgomaster had yet arr'd. The whole show very interesting - back to hotel at 9.30 - wrote letter to Mary - mailed it \+ to bed at 11.
