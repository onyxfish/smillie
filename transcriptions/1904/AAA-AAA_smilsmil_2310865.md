## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 14, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy, steady rain all last night & till afternoon to-day — cleared rapidly after 3 o'clk & from 4 to near 5 there was bright sunshine & some fine effects of rising clouds — later, clouds closed over again. I got up & had bkfs. after 8 — the boys slept on — I spent the m'g. reading news papers & writing to H & J. Smith & to Kate from whom I rec'd. a letter. As I did not feel like taking the regular Table d' hote lunch I waited till after the boys had theirs & then got a cup of tea, bread & butter — It was after 3 when the boys came to my Room & then I went out with them & walked south & turned east, up a flight of steps that brought us to about level stretch of lawn leading to (I think) the Villa Giulia — we walked to the Villa & there found a good carriage leading south — by this time the sun was shining brightly & I was regretting that I had not brought my camera — going south & ascending a hill we came to a place where we had a fine view of the lake, Como & Lecco side, looking north. Here Jim offered to go back to the hotel 1½ m. to get my camera & this he did in 40 m. — but by that time sunshine had faded & the pictures I took must be failures. we then Ret'd. to hotel about 6 & I rested till dinner at 7 — Evett a conjurer amused us in the central hall way — He performed some clever tricks in a facile way, doing a great deal of talking in what seemed to me to be a mixture of French & Italian —

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 15, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very heavy thunderstorm with torrents of rain passed over us between 5 & 7 A.M. after which the sky cleared brilliantly & a small gale of wind blew raising considerable surf at which I took two or three snap shots before bkfs'. It was considerably after 10 o'clk before the boys & I started out for a tramp — we went south past the Villa Giulia & up the mt. Road, past the little town of Vignola & at last, leaving the Road, climbed up a very steep back, among big Hazel trees, till we got an unimpeded out look to the north — over the Bellagio Promontory getting a fine view of Como & Lecco arms of the lake & away up towards Calico. It was after 12 when we turned to retrace our steps & after 1 when we got back to hotel — too late for Table d' hote lunch — we waited for awhile till that was out of the way & at about 2 had lunch a la carte. we loafed about on the terrace for a time — then got canes & went up on the Serbelloni hill where we did more loafing about the villa terrace & gardens & then thro' grottos & little wood ways. It was near 6 when we got to our rooms & I had a little nap before dinner — In the eve'g. I had to admit that I was tired — so I went to bed early.
