## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 29, 1904

**Weather:** fine clear, cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine clear, cold day \- to studio at 10 with Ralph \+ we spent the m'f together in making solutions \+ developing by machine two of his rolls of films exposed abroad. Home to lunch at 1 \- back at 2. met Weitenkampf at door \- he stayed for some time \+ after he left I worked as long as I could see on Borden photogravure plate \- Jim \+ Ralph came to Studio about 5 \+ we went home together \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Jim went to Pomfret alumni dinner at Hotel Astor \- Ralph \+ I printed from his negatives \- Charl came \+ spent the ev'f.

Jane H \- improves very slowly \+ is retarded no doubt by her fretting about it.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 30, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

'Mary went to Po'keepsie

Dull m'f \- very unpropitious looking but the weather cleared to mildly pleasant. Jane H \- was determined to go to Dewisburg (or some such name) a place a few miles from Ridgefield, where Mary Burling is living \- She \+ Mary H \- are to stay there some time \- but I would not permit it. She is not well enough yet. I went to Fine Arts Bldg. W.57 S' \- Varnishing Day for \[illegible\] \- My "Source of River" painting hangs in a prominent place in the large gallery. It looks fairly well but is lacking sadly in simplicity \+ breadth \- The foreground is overcrowded with small forms. Home to lunch \- Afternoon, busy at Studio. Home at 5.30 \+ had a rest before dinner \+ at 5 o'c Ralph \+ I went to Madg. Garden Theatre \- met there Emma D. \+ Frank Waller's daughter \+ Jim \+ Louise Cook \- a theatre party of Summa's \- The play was Geo. Ade's "Pictorial Comedy, the College Widow" a satirical skit that is having a run of popularity now \- The overshadowing importance of foot ball in a college education being the theme \- It was an amusing piece of nonsense played with a good deal of spirit. It was over at 11 \+ as peremptorily directed by Summa, we all went to a livery stable in Fourth ave. where Summa has an acct., where she \+ the two girls got into a cab \+ she took them home \+ then Jim, Ralph \+ I walked to our home.
