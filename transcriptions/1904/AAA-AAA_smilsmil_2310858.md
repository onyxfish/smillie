## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 31, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The early mntf gave us some hope for a pleasant day - the clouds covered the highest peaks but now & then an opening w. let sunshine thro' for a little time. - I had bkfst. before 8 - The boys were later - we went out soon after their bkfst & had quite a climb about the foot of the cliffs above which two of the glaciers hung - I had my camera & made two exposures under difficulties. At last we were driven in by rain - Everything & very where very cold & cheerless - I bought a Baedeker for Switzerland & spent some time studying that - making myself nearly wild in trying to follow proposed line of travel over the several maps - Had a poor lunch at 12.30 - wrote to Mary sending her the R.R. tickets from Innsbruck to send back - I went out for a walk but soon the Rain came & I was driven in again - Rain fell steadily for the rest of the p.m. 7.30 - poor Table d' hote dinner - Cooking is bad - Service is bad & I had a stupid time. Kept puzzled again over Baedeker maps.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 1, 1904

Post Hotel - Landeck -
for müller  Rooms 30 - 31 -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

When I got up at 5 am, an old moon was looking down from a perfectly clear sky & the snow-tops about cut clear & cold agt. the blue. It almost tempted me to stay over another day - the clouds came down before & after 12. It rained steadily & heavily - we were up early - bkfst. at 7 & at 7.30 our diligence started for Landeck - I was glad that I had put on extra under clothing it was so raw & cold - we had some fine views of mt. tops, before us & behind us as we went down - got to valley in 1½ hour - half the time it took to go up - & then turned to the Landeck road - The valley was not very interesting or picturesque - much like the latter part of road from Meran - clouds thickening all the time & threatening - passed thro a number of little towns - strings of irregularly placed buildings, half dwelling, half stable & barn - crowding upon the road way, so that in some places there was barely room for teams to pass - manure heaps & stagnant pools of drainage always in evidence - all the people living in evident defiance of fundamental sanitary laws - we got to St. Valentines at about 12 & stopped for 3/4 of an hour for dinner - a poor meal of boiled beef & rice - The Rain began here and when going on over the bleak divide the wind blew in our faces carrying Rain & clouds & the driver's smoke that smelled more of some kind of wood than of tobacco - we protected ourselves by pulling a horse blanket up to our chins - we had also the leather apron - as we descended on the other side we were less incommoded but the Rain fell heavily as we entered the [illegible] The, a wild, narrow gorge thro' which one of the branches of the Inn flows - it was harrowing to have to ride thro this chasm & be able to see only the bottom of it - & that very imperfectly - Baedeker stars & points & notes the views of towering snow covered peaks from time to time - we stopped at a house perched in the wildest place - I think, Stock - Finstermüns, from Reading Baedeker - & as we were chilled we had hot coffee & cogniac — From here on, for 20 miles or more, the road was cut into the cliffs - sometimes tunneled - with outlooks cut through - the Inn always boiling & roaring below us, sometimes hundreds of feet - it was immense - even if nothing more than just what we could see - it was now growing dark the gloom adding to the grandeur of the place - at about 8 we drove up to the Post Hotel at Landeck - & were cheerly Rec'd. by the manager - we soon had something to eat & were assigned comfortable Rooms & then got to bed - was very tired - it was a hard days work for me, I added to the travel that had gone before - 12½ hours - more than 60 miles - cold & wet on a hard seat - I was glad when it was done - no women passengers - all jolly Germans or Austrians - one, a Newcomer too much to an inside pose & speaking English - Kindly offered to exchange seats - so that I might be protected - poor, feeble old man.
