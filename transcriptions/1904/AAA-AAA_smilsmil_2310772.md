## Left Page

### Thur. March 10, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day. Some sunshine in the m'f — overcast afternoon with promise of rain — after b'fst. went to Nellie's and other regular "chores" — Then to studio at usual time — Spent the day "biting" etching f self — find myself very timid \+ uncertain in the use of acid — Result of my knowing so well how much depends upon good judgment in its use — Home to lunch \+ back — finished biting before 4 — cleaned up. Scholl came with a plate that needed some hammering up" — I did it for him — home at 6.15 — dinner — We'f., with Jane \+ Mary went to Charl's expecting to see a new lot of lantern slides — but Cullen, the photogr. had disappointed he — so we had some music instead —

## Right Page

### Fri. March 11, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beastly weather again — Very dark — sleet \+ ice \+ at night a light fall of snow. To studio rather late after writing a number of St. Louis Exposition notes concerning etchings — Then to work on study of an old man's head — with Graves — I had hoped that I could make an etching pure \+ simple but I do not have the gift, that is plain to me now — I am hopelessly mechanical — Home to lunch \+ back — Connolly called with a number of his plates but no prints. I gave him a note to Davis passing him in to Etching room \+ giving him privilege of using the press, he to furnish blankets, ink \+ paper — It was 5 when I got home — a short rest — then wrote letters to Jim \+ Ralph — dinner — Evg. meeting of Etching Com. at Rooms of Nielats 135 E. 15 S. Full Com — Nicoll, Schilling Chapman \+ Nielats — also Watrous as Chairman of Ex. Com. \+ Ney \+ Kurtz as Genl. Sec'y. We transacted business till about 10.30 — then adj — I left them there \+ walked up home.
