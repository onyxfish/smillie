## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 27, 1904

**Weather:** fair int.  **Temp:**

Up at 6. fair int. with promise of a good day — which promise was broken — rain coming on again in the afternoon \+ falling heavily at night we had bkf. soon after 7 — but it was not till 7.45 that we got away in our landeau. we were an hour getting across the plain to the mountains the air was very chilly \+ I was anxious about the boys who were insufficiently dressed — it was not long after entering the mts. before we passed the Austrian border \+ were inspected by a customs officer. Almost all of the way we rode by a rushing, clay colored stream — there was little that was wild or very picturesque. The view of the Watchman as we entered the Berchtesgaden Valley was fine \+ I took some snap shots at it — we passed thro 13 — about 10 — pleasingly situated — a collection of hotels, pensions \+ villas — then on to Moneau, got there soon after 11 — a great crowd had already arrived \+ taking advantage of promise of fine day after six days of rain — we rushed to secure about [illegible] got No 14 \+ were told that we wd have to wait 3/4 of an hour before it came in — we waited 1 1/4 hours — crowds going off in the big Rundalot boats, stowed like cattle — we had bread \+ meat \+ built a fire at the Restaurant \+ waited — clouds gathering crowds thickening — off in our boat 12.15 — in many ways the lake recalled memories of lower Ausable — I do not think it equals to the photo — I did not see it under most favorable conditions — got to upper end in 1 1/4 hours \+ at once footed over the half mile to Obersee — a mere pond with one fine view — but not so good as the photo — light rain began to fall before we left to boat — repeated showers wet us before we got back now \+ then there were aggravating gleams of sunshine. We found our coachman \+ got away as soon as possible \+ started on our return — reached the salt mine about 4.30 Bought our tickets \+ in Robing Room dressed ourselves in grey uniform — were given lanterns with candles \+ then took our places in a party of eight, two of whom were women — We entered the mine from the level of the road thro masonry arch way \+ walked for a long distance up \+ down before we reached any evidence of salt — part of the way was masonry \+ part timbered —

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 28, 1904

**Weather:** cloudy \+ threatening  **Temp:**

Up at 5 o'clk. cloudy \+ threatening — I packed my trunk \+ at 6 o'clk. waked the boys — at 6.30 waked the boys \+ at 6.30 had bkf. settled bills \+ got away on the 7.40 train for Innsbruck. The first part of the ride was very interesting — especially the Pass Lueg. The defile of the Salzach. The weather grew worse as we went on until after passing Zell am See the rain \+ clouds came down shutting out all mt. forms about us. This was a great disappointment to me as Baedeker described the positions of peaks \+ mts. about. There was no improvement till after we had passed Jenbach when we ran out of the storm — the clouds were higher \+ the mt. tops uncovered — the change was quite sudden — got to Innsbruck on time \+ went at once to Tirolerhof \+ were given Rooms 2 \+ 3 on first floor. We then had lunch \+ after that went to the Hof Kirke — then across the river \+ climbed a hill to the Schloss Weiherburg \+ from a terrace there we had a charming view over the valley \+ city to distant mts. From this terrace we ret? by another road \+ by a long walk got back to our hotel — as I did not feel in eating humor the boys had dinner at table d' hote \+ I wrote a letter to Mary — Coart, after some more writing I got to bed early.
