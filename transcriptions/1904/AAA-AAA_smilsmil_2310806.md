## Left Page

### Tues. May 17, 1904

*The Ark \- St. Hubert's.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day \- up at 6.30 after a fairly restful night \- Before bkfst. at 8 went over to Deer's Head Inn \+ saw Mr. \+ Mrs. Tracy \- had a short walk, then back to Windsor \- After bkfst. \- a short loaf \- \+ about 9 started on our side over to St. Hubert's \- same team, rig \+ driver (Hays) as yesterday \- We had a very comfortable ride \+ enjoyed it all \- Stopped in town \+ bought some provender \- \+ got to Wucchi about 12.30 \- he told me that water was not yet turned on at the house \- some impediment in the pipe \- Otis now working at it \- Got up to the Ark at about 1 \- Sadie Otis there cleaning up \- as well as she could without water \- I sent Mookey to find Otis \+ see if he could assist to hurry the coming of the water \- he set to, saying the cause of the trouble had been found \- The pipe was frozen under the Highland Cottage \- Mary \+ \& I had a meagre lunch at about 2 o'clk in the studio \- Then we worked as best we could to further the work of putting the house in order \- It was not till late in the afternoon that water was got thro' the pipes into the house \- At 6 o'clk. we went to Mr. Wardenburgh's on invitation to tea \+ had a fellow out from with her, Mrs. Magfree \+ Adelaide \- Then home \+ some more work was done \- Mookey came up about 9 o'clk \+ slept in Jim's Room \- I got to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant for awhile in the m'fg., but there were evidences of the coming rain that got here at about 5 p.m. It was steady \+ quite heavy thro' the eve'g. Up at about 6 \+ went down to waken Moody but he had flown \- set to my Room \- dressed \+ at about 7 went to the Kitchen affecting to find fire in Range \- none there \- I soon kindled one \- Mary came to my aid \+ by 8'clk we sat down to a good bkfst. of coffee \- brown bread \- \+ butter \- Rolled wheat \+ eggs \- Then Sadie Otis Moody appeared \- After bkfst. \- I wrote to H. D. Allen asking him to make an app't ointment for me next Monday if possible \+ Then Mary \+ I went to windy inn \+ then across to the P.O. at the Hotel Annex \- Thence home at 10.30 \+ the rest of the m'fg was devoted to general work \- I gave out room after 12 \+ Rested till 1 when Mary \+ I went over to Mr. Wardenburgh's \+ had a good, comforting Roast beef dinner \- There till 2 o'clk \- then again I went to the hotel \+ found a letter from Ralph \- Then home \+ worked all the afternoon \- always just on the verge of giving out \- Mary worked like a trojan putting up curtains all over \- I got the Main Room into complete order except for the leak over the piano which has assumed serious proportions \- I sent down to the Village for Breadener but of course he did not come \+ the leak went on \- About 6 o'clk \- I got a little nap just before supper \- Mary got up a very good one of tea, bread \+ butter, canned cherries \+ ginger snaps. Then the dishes were washed \+ I fixed the fire for the night \+ we Retired to the main house \- The big Room was lighted up \+ looked very cosy \- but Mary was tired (no wonder) \+ at 8.30 the lights were turned out \- I went to my Room \+ wrote for awhile \-
