## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 10, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant but cloudy all day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant but cloudy all day — a shower at 8.30 P.M. Up at 6 + wrote a letter to Charl before bkft. + at 8.40 we started on boat + went to Ponte Tresa, taking about 1½ hour. Then by little R.R. (narrow) to Luino on Maggiore — a pleasant ride, almost all the way by the side of a stream, but not especially picturesque or interesting — reached Luino in about an hour + had to wait there 1½ an hour for boat — then on making landings at various places. Nearly all of it was quite familiar to me especially where we got into the Pallanza bay + where we got to Baveno. I saw the well remembered hotel in its garden by the lake side where Annie + I stayed twelve years ago — + the beach where she + the boys bathed with Éminant, Margot + Ping. We left the boat with a great crowd of tourists at Isola Bella at about 2.30 after having had a shocking bad + expensive lunch on the boat. On landing we made directly for the gate of entrance to the gardens; up a mean little street — I had forgotten that there was such a group of buildings on the island — thought it was wholly occupied by palace + garden — This collection of poor houses, Restaurants + church are like a thorn in the side — Many booths lined the way from the landing + were beset by women young + old, offering all sorts of wares, postal cards \&c — we were admitted to the gardens in groups — all as I remembered very well + I found it as interesting as ever — some parts fine — some, rather — after the gardens we went to the palace + after a wait were shown thro' that. When Annie + I were here it was closed to visitors as the duke was at home — I was surprised to find it so extensive — some of the apartments are really fine — the bed occupied by Napoleon before the battle of Marengo was interesting — also Queen Caroline of Sweden's bed — we got thro' at about 4 + had a tiresome wait at the landing for the return boat — Rate to Luino over same route — thence — train in waiting — thence to Tresa + there had another tedious wait for boat which came at last — the return trip on Lugano could not be described as interesting — night came down rapidly, clouds covered the sky — for a time we watched the peculiar effect of searchlight at bow of boat — + the shooting in the dark, thro' the narrow span of R.R. bridge that crosses the end of the lake — It began to rain before we got to Lugano. The boat did not stop at Wm. Tell standing out carried us on to the central one — whence we had to walk to the hotel, but the trees along the promenade were protection — about 8.30 when we got in — had a light supper + to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 11, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy clouds + thunderous atmosphere — some bit sunshine as we came out to Lake Como — later, again heavy clouds + thunderstorm — Rain till after dark — Up early + completed packing for start. Bkft. at 8 — paid bills + tips + left the Beaurivage at 10.30 — boat at 10.45 for Porlezza at N.E. end of lake — The clouds gave fine effect to some of the wilder hills — but bad for photos — nevertheless I snapped at a number of fine bits — I was particularly impressed by San Mamette, near upper end of lake — The Val Solda — the jagged mt. peaks + the old town of Castelo, high up on the mt. side — we got thro' the annoyance of custom Ho. inspection with less trouble than we feared after watching the process as administered to some others — one poor old man a native, carried a basket in which he had about ¼ of a pound of tobacco on which, to his manifest sorrow, he had to pay a duty + the trunk of a French-Swiss at a tiny boy of tobacco was confiscated + he had to pay 4fr. when it came our turn, after our assurance that we were not smokers, but one trunk + one bag was opened for inspection + after the usual rummage nothing found to the bottom — nothing found. We had along out at Porlezza before train started — The ride over to Conolake is very interesting — some of the mt. views are very fine + the panorama of the Como lake as we descend to Menaggio is beautiful — The difference between the road from Luino on Maggiore to Tresa on Lugano + this from Porlezza over to Menaggio is pretty in favor of the latter — we had been told by someone wait for the boat, the R.R. ride having taken about an hour. The run across the lake to Bellagio was a short one + we reached the Grand at about 2 o'clk + were at once shown to Rooms — The boys have two, connecting — mine, No. 86 — is but a short distance from them. We washed up + then had a light meal + after that went out on terrace walked under the trees to a seat where we sat for some time + watched the approach of the thunder storm. at about 6 I gave up to a headache — the result, I am sure, of eating some chocolates yesterday, poisoned with vanilla — I was on my bed for about two hours — The boys at dinner at Table d'hotel — later I got up, wrote a note to Miss Dougan concerning the closing of Keene House + a reply to one from her — also a post-script to a long, interesting letter from Charl (rec. here) which I forwarded to Mary — I had there a little from Flo. Then I went out + found the boys in a Café near by, listening to a strolling band costumed Italians — a singer with 4 or 5 instruments accompanying — then a stroll — back to hotel about 10.30 — everybody seemed to have gone to bed — so we followed suit.
