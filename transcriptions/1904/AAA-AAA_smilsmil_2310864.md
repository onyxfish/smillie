## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 12, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb m'f. \- brilliant \+ exhilarating. Clouds came in the afternoon \+ hung threateningly over the mts. in the N.W. After our b'f'st. we got tickets for the Villa Cerbelloni \+ climbed to the top of the promontory \- It is a fascinating place, with its beautiful trees \+ its many little walks, its grottos, its terraces \+ balconies. We spent the m'f' there wandering over the various paths' in constant delight. Back to hotel in time for lunch \+ at 1.45 were at the landing place to take the steamboat for Cadenabbia. There was some delay \+ confusion because the Como boat came along side of the Lecco boat \+ the passengers for the two places were all mixed at once \- The Como pass'r passing over the Lecco boat we could not get on to this at first \- we could not understand our time table, further complicated because the hours ran up to 24. It required some figuring after finding that the boat we reach a certain landing at 15.00 to learn just what o'clk that was. However, matters straightened out \- the Como boat went on to Como \+ we followed as far as the next landing, San Giovanni \- Bellagio. Then turned, crossed the lake to Cadenabbia \+ back to Bellagio \+ did not finally leave for Cadenabbia until after 5. \- Then we stopped at various towns along the way \- the most interesting to me was Menaggio \- rather picturesque in itself, backed by a wild basin of mts. The views were interesting in many ways \- the mt. forms \+ construction seeming to be different from that of the Como arm of the lake. \- We got to Cadenabbia at about 4 \+ stopped there 40 m. during which time we took a hurried run thro' town \+ went up to the new big church \- on our way back the lake at noon light, especially when looking to the south, was very beautiful \- south, the sky was extraordinarily free from clouds \- east \+ west north they gathered upon the mt. tops \- fine effects. \- It was not long after 6 when we got back to Bellagio \- a short rest then dinner at 7 \- Every, the strollers who played \+ sang in the Restaurant last night, held forth on the terrace in front of hotel \+ discoursed pretty badly till about 10 p.m. \- but they helped get rid of time.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 13, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded \+ threatening \- light showers at times \- heavy rain at upper end of lake all day. \- came to us in the eve'g. Up at 6.30 \+ wrote the most of a letter to Charl. before b'f'st. \- After that went out to the long walk under trees at lake side \- north \- pleasant effect \- light \+ low, but steady \- air very still \- set camera on tripod \+ took photo. of ave. of trees \- 6 min. \- smallest diaphragm \- hope I have got something \- some showers \- I finished C's letter at 11. \- weather a little more promising \- with the boys walked down the lake almost to the Landing S. Giovanni Bellagio \+ then back before lunch at 12.30 \- After lunch, by 1.47 o'clk. boat we went across to Cadenabbia \+ walked down to the Villa Carlotta \+ under guidance went with a large party thro' the vestibule of Residence (closed to visitors now) where we saw the Canovas, Thorwaldsen's sculptures which did not move me greatly, \+ then into the wonderful gardens, or perhaps, more properly, the arboretum \- There were few flowers \- but the most astonishing \+ luxuriant growth of trees from all parts of the world \- Himalaya \+ Lebanon Cedars \+ noble specimens of the tree growth of China, Japan, North \+ South America \+ the Tropics \- an immense Magnolia tree, trunk at base 18 in, a grand tree \- great Rhododendrons, Azaleas \+ Laurels must make outburst of flowers in the spring \- the walk thro' the gardens was a great treat \- we were in the grounds about 3/4 of an hour \- Then we walked down the road to Tremezzo \+ beyond that for some distance \- then retraced our steps \+ walked for a considerable way north of Cadenabbia \- then, as it began to look very black north of us, we went back to C. \+ had a boatman row us across to Bellagio \- the water was quite rough under a high wind \+ heavy rain made a curtain across the upper end of the lake \- the row over was quite pleasantly exciting \- got to hotel at 5.30 \+ I got a nap before dinner at 7 o'clk \- Every, heavy rain so that we had to amuse ourselves indoors \- the boys read in their Rooms \+ I wrote in mine.
