## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Went to Hackley with Jim -
Ralph came home with us
Dull day - light fall of snow - went to N. Yale's after b'kfs. to try to get some comfort from him - got little - it was about 11 when I got to studio. I commenced a tracing from my Venice sketch of the Church tower of San Trovaso - I purpose painting it upon a panel I have 11"×14". I went home soon after 12 - lunch at 1 - at 2°° Jim & I took train to Tarrytown getting there exactly on time - Rode at once to Hackley. We took a turn thro' the house & then it was time to take our seats to see the play - The Rivals - an ambitious effort of under the circumstances, thought a successful one - Of course, stage accomodations & settings were very inadequate but that was unimportant - the one out of door scene - the Morth Parade - was painted by one of the boys & - answered the purpose - the part of Sir Anthony Absolute was well taken by Chas. f. Bush Jan. '05. Cap. Jack - Caulder Cobb '04 - was the only "stick" in the play - he recited his lines without giving the slightest evidence of appreciation - Faulkland - Hayden Channing '04 went thro' his small part in an unobjectionable way - Bob Acres - Kenneth G. Carpentes '04 - a sprightly little fellow went thro' his part in an amusing way - Sir Lucins O'Trigger, Nelson Dougherly - '05 - was capital - He is a very good looking boy - fine form - easy manner - engaging, merry face & a delightful brogue - he won my heart as a fine type of a bright & lovable boy - tag. Natl. S. Smith Jun. '07 - was very subservient. David proved to be Sampson (Thompson 5 -'05) Mr. Malaprop, Sam. A. Matthewson '06 - a really young fellow was well made up & might have passed for a female if he did not have to talk - his bass voice was an absurd contradiction - Lydia Languish - Wm. E. Reis Jun. '06 - was almost as badly equipped in the matter of voice - the dialogues between the two women were extremely funny - the languishing love passages between Lydia & Cap. Jack were Red with shouts of laughter by the boys - Lucy - Chas. L. Seeger Jun. '04 - was the star of the event - she was such a "peach" of a girl - face, voice & manner were all charmingly feminine - the little kissing passage between him & Sir Lucius was a hit - The Mandolin Club gave two selections cleverly - the whole entertainment - as a bit of boy school

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 20, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very bright & sunny but I was in such condition that I did not go out of the house - Laryngitis, Bronchitis & some other disorders of like character made me very unhappy. The boys were off together all day - shopping in the m'f \+ to the theatre in the afternoon - Jim went to Edw. Cook's in the eve'f. Charles came in & spent the eve'f with us.

like was deeply interesting to me. I looked back over my early experiences sadly - & for my boys beside me I was very glad - About 6.30 we had a stand-up feed of oysters - salads - creams & coffee - After that, dancing was on the programme but I called a carriage at 7.45 & took the 8.23 train & got to N.Y. at 8.45, the boys with me - I got to bed promptly -
