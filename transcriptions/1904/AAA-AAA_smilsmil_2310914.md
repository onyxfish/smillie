## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 21, 1904

*Raelph came home.*

**Weather:** Bright & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & cold. To studio late, being detained at home writing some notes. Until noon I was engaged varnishing Emma J's portrait of her grandfather Geo. Ireland, painted by Waldo & [illegible] \- a responsible job \- I got excited over it before I got thro' \- Home to lunch. after dinner I fixed my camera for odd work & made two exposures before the Ireland portrait \- then developed them. Eve'g. Velox printing again went to studio at 9 p.m. got plates of J. portrait; took them home & got some prints \- not very successful \- the hot, red background coming up as black as the coat \-

In the afternoon I went to

Maynard returned.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 22, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not a pleasant day \- my work at the studio all day was photographic \- very tiresome because there was so little success in it \- I developed, by machine a roll of films exposed in President the day we went to the Bastei \- very interesting material, but all the views were more or less marked by unequal development resulting in bands of light & dark running crosswise of the post. I cannot explain the trouble but think it must be some fault in the machine work. After working at cutting masks for printing at about 4 o'clk., I went to Cullen's & got another Velox printing machine in place of the one I got two or three days ago. Dinner \- Eve'g. very tired \- With the boys went to Char[les'] for awhile \-
