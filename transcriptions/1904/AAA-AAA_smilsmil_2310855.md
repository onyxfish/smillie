## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 25, 1904

**Weather:** Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain poured down incessantly all day long — I heard it at 1 A.m. \+ it is falling now — I hear it at near 10 p.m. We have had a cheerless trying day — I lay in my bed \+ read for near an hour before I got up at a little before 8 — breakfasted alone letting the boys sleep away the time we did not go out during the m'f except to cross the road to our meals. I wrote part of a letter to Mary \+ part of one to Emma I — In the afternoon we got desperate \+ at about 4 o'clk started out on a Random scott \+ did not get back until 6.30 — pretty wet but feeling better — we went away up to the Franz Joseph Park \+ back thro' the very narrow \+ interesting street that winds at the foot of the Kapuzinerberg — we bought more photos \+ some books — Supper after 7 — Reading \+ loafing hopelessly in our rooms during the eve'g \+ to bed early —

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 26, 1904

**Weather:** Clouded  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded m'f. cold \+ unpromising — we got up late \+ after bkft. we kept our rooms for some time — I wrote to Tyrolerhof at Innsbruck — finished letter to Mary \+ wrote for some time on letter to Emma — It was near noon when we started out — walked to river which is much swollen \+ watched men fishing at small drift wood — then on to the house where Mozart was born — N\. 7 Getreidegasse \+ mounted to the third floor which is now a sort of Mozart museum — a very simple diet of rooms but two, the parlor \+ bed room being open to the public — little changed since his birth — same floor — unusually wide boards — were shown the corner in which his cradle stood — his spinet \+ piano \+ [illegible] his skull \+ violin — also some of his letters \+ M.S.S. music — we ret'd to hotel thro' slight shower \+ had dinner after I — I finished letter to Emma — The boys went off for a walk — \+ after 5, as they had not ret'd I went alone — crossed the river to the Monchberg passed the stables — went up the stair east of Riding school \+ by a new path (to me) leading to Katze Rest nt I got to the top — delightfully shaded with blicks of the city \+ its towers lighted up by setting sun — I went over the top to where I could get the view to the north. It was very beautiful in the eve'g light — I saw more of the high m'ts than at any other time — clouds higher the last rays of sunlight on castle not to be forgotten. Back at 7.15 — boys getting anxious — They had supper — I took none — Read sketches in Jacobs' Sea Urchins \+ to bed early.
