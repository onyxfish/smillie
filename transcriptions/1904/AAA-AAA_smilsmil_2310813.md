## Left Page

### Tues. May 31, 1904

**Weather:** Overcast + quite chilly  **Temp:** [illegible]

Over cast + quite chilly - got up with severe headache + thinking that I needed a cathartic I Took Cit. Magnesia at 7.30 — After bkft. my head very light + giddy + Recalling my experience two years ago when my Kidneys got the cr- of misbehaving I went to Dr. Yale — He told me to keep my urine for the next 24 hours - then take a specimen to Chemists in 42? S. for analyses — It was near 11 when I got to studio — I rubbed a strong glaze over the sea on 33×44, Vesuvius + then home to lunch — my cathartic dose began to get into work in the afternoon in a too awful way + I was quite useless. I loafed about the Studio for awhile — then home — Rain came on later + I fell thro' the night. I was able to eat some dinner — but I got to bed early + passed a very bad night being routed out a number of times with unusual suffering.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 1, 1904

Uncle Willie + aunt Agnes arr.
Jane Banks + Catharine Bradley wait.

**Weather:** A disagreeable day — showery + chilly  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day — showery + chilly — I got up — dressed + went down to bkft. but soon found that I could not stay up — my bowels continued turbulent + I was in almost continuous discomfort — Jane Banks came to the dining room to bid me good bye + to say that if I did not have a woman to take her place she would stay over for a few days — for which I thanked her — I did not see Miss Bradley. I spent the rest in the lounge — 2d. story front — did some reading. By noon time, as my condition seemed to be getting worse I tried, without success to get a Dr. Miss [illegible] to take anatie to Yale — no answer to Repeated Rings — About 4 o'clk — I gave up — undressed + went to bed. It it wd. now be very awkward to have Uncle W. + Aunt A. come at this time — no servants — I sick in bed + arrangements to have the boys here next Monday night. I wrote a telegram for Mary to send to Uncle W. asking him to postpone his visit but before she got to telegraph office Uncle W. + Aunt A. came in having come from 1 o'k. on the noon boat. Mary + Jane scrabbled about + made them as comfortable as they could — it is a severe test for them — During the day I wrote a letter to Jim concerning his coming of age + the transfer to him of his Mother's legacy of $2000 — Early eve. Mary went to Charles + he 'phoned to Yale, who thought it unnecessary for him to visit me + advised Paregoric + Bismuth.
