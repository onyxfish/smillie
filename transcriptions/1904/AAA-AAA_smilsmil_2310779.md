## Left Page

### Thur. March 24, 1904

**Weather:** A fine, seasonable day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine, seasonable day - To studio at usual time + spent the on[?] clearing up the mess that has resulted from my getting out all sorts of prints + mounts + framing materials + having them about as I used them. - I worked till after 1 o'clk Then, feeling stale + tired I sauntered up Lex. Ave. to 45th + then across to Mad. Ave. + home at 1.30 Just as I sat down to lunch Mr. Dexter Thurber came - has not yet been able to find a studio to finish her portrait - must have one for 2, or 3, or 4 weeks - I Fold her I might be able to let her have mine if it wd answer her purpose - + we made a[n] appointment - she to be at my studio at about 5 - I finished my lunch - Returned to studio + resumed my clearing up - Mr. Vaughan Trowbridge came. - Mrs Thurber came at 5 + after looking about concluded that she could get along nicely - I agreed to let her have the use of the Room at $50 per month. I am to try to get it ready for her by Sat. mg. - I was near 6.30 when I got home - dinner - eve'g loafing + Reading.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 25, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant - All day putting away etching pls. making a partial list of same + cleaning house preparatory to the arrival of Mrs. Thurber. Jennings came in the m'g. took the rugs away beat + swept them. - I had lots to do in getting things stowed away out of sight. - Home to lunch + back in the afternoon Jennings came again cleaned up the floor + lots of other cleaning + then helped me arrange the furniture. It was after 6 before I got home. - I had a very few minutes of rest before dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g wrote to Jim + Ralph + a couple [illegible] - To Edith Holt + Agnes Donaldson in the interest of the Trowbridge girls.
