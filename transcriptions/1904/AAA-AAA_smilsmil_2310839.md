## Left Page

### Fri. July 22, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant altho' at times cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant altho' at times cloudy. Bkp'. at g. cab to Arsenal at 10 - wandered thro' it at will - The boys much interested in the evolution of fire arms, especially cannon - The Hall of Fame on upper story, impressive the half tone light - low toned color - The bronze statues & the large paintings of historical events were very effective - After this we went over to the Royal Palace & were led around thro' the usual rooms of state on north front - one view out over the dust garden to the old museum & the Nat. gallery was like a bit of ancient Athens - The Rooms were fine, but none of them were really impressive to me - there was a large party, 50 or more, shuffeling in big felt slippers over the marquetrie floors - in the crowd met Renshaw & Coffin of Patricia - We did not get in to the Chapel - out at 12.45 - guard mount - then over to dust garden - heard band play - then to a poor Restaurant in Dorotheans - & had lunch - then cars out the Charlottenburg - thro' Tiergarten & Berlinérstrasse to Royal Palace & Mausoleum - The mausoleum was to me unexpectedly small - interesting of course but meagre architecturally - then we wandered & loafed in the Park for some time - then went thro' the part of the palace open to the public under guidance - small rooms - rather tawdry & mean in decoration, but showy - Rooms all small - on ground floor opening out on the park - Reminding me in many ways of the Trianon & in a very small way of the large palace at Versailles - We had intended to go to Kroll's to get dinner & spend the eve. there, but all being tired we went to the Kaiser hof - had dinner & went to bed early - I think we all have a touch of malaria - The boys are bunged up with catarrhal colds & I have Rheumatism in shoulders - all took quinine before turning in for the night. I had a very unsuccessful night - I had a suggestion of a chill & possibly a little fever during the night.

### Sat. July 23, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

had a chill.

Fine day. Up early & wrote a letter to Mary telling her that I planned to be in Dresden not later than Wed. - The boys did not get up till 9 - bkp'. at 9.30 & after 10 when we started out, I taking my camera - we walked first to the Deutsche Bank where I drew £25 = 509 k. 35 pf - then to Schiller Platz & onto the great Dom square when I took a number of shots at the Dom - Statue of LW.1 - Nat. museum & other places. we went thro' a part of the old museum, that I might take another look at the old picture - The Rembrandt gallery & all the Dutch section in the west end of the belf. was closed. much to my regret - The old Italian & Spanish paintings disappointed me - I think we have better examples of those early masters in N.Y. - Then I went to the Print dept. in the new museum & had a pleasant interview with an official who spoke English - he explained to me how application to see prints must be made - less Restrictions than in the British Mus. - we sat in the dust Garden & heard band play - & then, at about 2 Rel? to hotel; all hands being out of sorts - we had lunch - Then I tried to get a nap on my counge - but as time went on I grew more & more uneasy. felt at about 5 I was in great distress - cold sweat & not knowing whether [illegible] or [illegible], or both, w. insist upon precipitate action - The boys came in & fixed & cab. Soda for me - then followed a chill - not very severe & an hour or more later a fever that developed 101°46 - I got to bed about 7 - took 15 gr. of Quinine - very severe pain in left shoulder. Prevented free breathing - The fever subsided about midnight but did not leave me - & I was able to get some rest before m'g.
