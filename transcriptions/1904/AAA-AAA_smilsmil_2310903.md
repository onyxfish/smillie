## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 29, 1904

**Weather:** Dull & threatening — Raw & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

growing milder as the day advanced. —
To studio earlier than usual \+ to work
on 33"×44" going at foreground \+ painting
more light into it — much to its advantage
I think — home to lunch \+ back as usual.
Went to 2°. Nat'l. Bank at 2 o'clk — on my
way stopped at Butler's \+ saw my frame
for 33×44 under way — Painted as long as I
could see but did not get done all that
I wished to — Fred'k. W. Morton ([illegible] \+ [illegible])
came in — he has a scheme to get him out of his
present financial troubles \+ will also enable him
to come to N.Y. \+ establish his [illegible]. That is, ten
artists are to advance $50 each — taking his note
to be repaid from first earnings of [illegible]. N.E. to be
[illegible] in N.Y. City — He had written promises from
H.W. Ranger — Partridge \+ Vonnah — I have no faith
in his plan — he is very sanguine — I had to decline —
Home at 5 — Rested till dinner — even with
news papers \+ writing —

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 30, 1904

**Weather:** Dull, threatening  **Temp:** [illegible]

weather in the m'g but it
managed to clear about the middle of the
day — milder — After [illegible] I went to Yale's —
my back has been bothering me for some days.
Itching between over shoulder blades where I have
difficulty in seeing or reaching \+ — Q — said he
could see nothing to explain it, but he kept me
till near 11 o'clk showing me his photos — he has
done some remarkable work — Then to studio
worked till 1 — lunch at home — Then to bank —
to studio before 3 — a little more painting
on 33×44 while daylight lasted — Then home
\+ finished letter to Mary before dinner — I
shall send it to Dover, expecting it to be sent
by S.S. agent to Purser of Patricia when she lands
at Dover — At 8 went to Charl's — he is
out of sorts \+ D.' has been treating
him electrically — home at 9 —
