## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 24, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — little else than that to record — I loafed about the decks feeling completely unstrung — I could not eat — \+ after a vain attempt at dinner \+ sure that my temp. was above normal I called in Dr. Mandowsky who ordered me at once to go to bed \+ to stay there certainly all to-morrow then he gave me 10 gr. quinine — I obeyed \+ passed a miserable night of fever \+ headache.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 25, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, breezy \+ cool — so I was told. I was in bed all day, by order of the Doctor \+ by my own will — fever lasted thro' the night \+ went high — how high I don't know — did not measure it — but high enough to make me exceedingly uncomfortable — it broke towards m'g. \+ I lay in my with thro'out the day with little discomfort, Reading the short stories in W. W. Jacob's "Light Freights" — I had no appetite \+ ate very little.
