## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 1, 1904

**Weather:** Unsettled  **Temp:** [illegible]

Unsettled weather - bright m'f. - then overcast \+ bright again in the afternoon - To studio before 10 - all the m'f. waiting for frame 33×44 promised by Dutter - doing dark-room \+ other work while waiting - Telephoned to him at 11 o'clk. \+ he replied that the frame had left his shop \+ I w'd get it in a few minutes - At 1 it had not yet come \+ I teleph'd to him again - He could not explain further than to say it had been sent out with other things two hours before - I went home to lunch \+ found the big frame in my library - the fool had sent it to the house instead of to the studio - after lunch I got Jennings \+ his helper to take it to studio - There I found that it murdered my painting \+ I was greatly disturbed - I put it in my old frame \+ it looked well in that - I put Vesuvius in the new frame \+ I think that with a little painting I can bring it \+ the new frame into harmony - I painted as long as I could see on the Sources.
Then I went to Pennant's - 24 E. 13th St. \+ got some photo materials - walked home - in at 6 - dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g. fussing around.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 2, 1904

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast most of the day - but not unpleasant. To studio before 10 - I painted over a portion of 12×16 - Evening in the Woods - I gave some time to the preparation of some photo. solutions - Home to lunch \+ back as usual, most of the afternoon was spent worrying over photo. solutions that are puzzling me because I have developer, fixing \+ toning formulas from various trustworthy sources that vary so widely in the proportions of some chemicals that, not being prepared to experiment, I am at a loss to know what to do. The carmen for Century Pictures came very late \+ got the Source \+ Eve'g. Home after 6 - dinner - Eve'g. wrote to Jim \+ Ralph.
