## Left Page

### Wed. July 6, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Slight variations of weather. m't., some clouds that hung low \+ a cool, damp feeling in the air — then, much warmer, almost oppressively so, with an oily looking sea that reflected the clouds — later, a better, cooler air \+ more movement of the water — Ralph did not get up to his bhp. — ate nothing, Temp 99 2/5°. but still has dis comfort in his head — he got up later \+ we spent some time on boat deck \+ above bridge. There were two amusing specimens of english posted as notices to Passengers — one signed "Captain I have made copies" "On the Request of ladies — invalids etc. the passengers (specially the singers, Tradders (?) noisemakers behind the room windows of others) are kindly requested to cease after 11 o'clk. With all such amusements which could prevent other passengers from their well deserved sleep." Captain." The other was put up beside the ladder that led to boat deck — "No admittance after sunset. No admittance for boys or big girls (except where guided by chaperones) — The p.m. was passed with Reading, sleeping \+ wandering about the vessel. In the eve'g. Ralph seemed quite recovered, greatly to my relief — after dinner the band played \+ there was a lovely promenade — later there was an informal concert in the Saloon — some clever violin playing by Mr. Aley — Birnbaum — After that, out on bow watching the display of phosphorescence in the foaming water. Then, with some young people inspected the bakery rooms where from 6 to 10 bbs of flour are used each day — in full operation at midnight. While in gangway saw stewardess guiding a miserable looking woman steerage passenger aft to hospital, I was told — As she was big with child it was suspected — I turned in at about midnight.

At 3 p.m. I had a very satisfactory bath \+ after it a solid nap.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 7, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasanter day than yesterday — fresher air — some movement in it — also over the surface of the water. I slept well till the rising bugle — had a little deck walk before bhp. The m't was spent loitering about — much time with Ralph on the upper bridge — a delightful cyme. The woman (a Roumanian) that I saw on her way to hospital last night, was delivered of a male child at 3 o'cl. this m't. The stone doctor attending her. After lunch I lay down on my bed \+ slept soundly for an hour or more — wandering \+ reading after that till dinner — after dinner I was called in my rugs \+ had several naps while the promenaders pranced past me — after 9 a short informal concert \+ recitations by the Californian.
