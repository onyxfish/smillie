## Left Page

### Fri. July 8, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A change in the weather - a silvery damp mist in the m'f early with lively breeze \+ later, sunshine - then a general clouding over. Toward eve't again clear \+ much cooler with a bracing wind. I kept about most of the m'f. on deck before bkf't. \+ after it sat in chair rolled in rugs \+ read - then wandered about noon W. H. J. Boos - Physical instructor - N.Y. Uni't had a Calisthenics class on the after deck - of which I got two or three snap shots - then lunch - \+ after that I continued the letter to Charl of which I had written a part in the m'f. much fun in watching a german game, a sort of tag on after deck - had a nap before dinner - a surprise at dinner - when I went to dinner I found an elderly couple sitting in the places of Jim \+ Ralph \+ soon explained that they were. Mr. \+ Mrs. Stevens who had given up their seats at table across the Saloon to make place for Jim \+ Ralph - a birth-day dinner being given by several young people as a surprise to Ralph - After my dinner I went over to that table \+ witnessed the opening of presents to R -- There was considerable fun \+ a number gathered about to see it. Cap't. Raising came over \+ shook hands with R - \+ congratulated him - Ralph was very much pleased \+ carried himself very well - after the exercises I had a pleasant chat with Dr. \+ Mrs. Radcliffe of Lafayette College - Easton, Pa.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 9, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully beautiful day - cloudless \+ delightfully cool without being cold - it was simply gorgeous - fine sunset. I had a little breezing on the after deck before bkf't. at 8 - the rest of the m'f was divided between writing to Charl \+ Emma \+ loafing about the deck watching the games played - lunch at 1 \+ the afternoon went like the m'f. much time on the upper bridge - I had a short nap just before dinner at 7 - Eve'g., a little more writing in my state room \+ wandering - there was music by the band \+ dancing on the after deck - at 9:15, by the captain's order, a Holmes light was thrown overboard. a sort of Acetaline light that floats \+ burns for nearly half an hour - we could still see it as it rose upon the top of a wave far away twenty minutes or more after it was thrown overboard.
