## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 26, 1904

**Weather:** Fine day — after bkfs.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Regular mtt. chores, then to Kimmel & Voigt. 242 Canal St. where Henry S. pulled a couple of pfs. from photo-gravure plate after my Borden drawing, showing a surprising amt of color — I'm afraid that there is much trouble ahead for one on this plate — then to 2d Natl. Bk. \+ deposited Miss Stokes check — then home to lunch. — To studio soon after 2 — worked a little while on plate — then wrote letter to Mary — about 5 had a short walk with Geo — went into 42d. St. Subway Station — To be opened to the public to-morr. Dinner before 7 — a new waitress has given warning that she is not going to stay — says she did not like the house when she came into it. Evef. Century Com. on Admissions, a pleasant, unimportant \+ brief session. Home before 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 27, 1904

**Weather:** Fine day — clear, windy \+ rather cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 \+ got started on experimental work for the Borden photo-gravure plate when W. Kramer came, bringing with him the original drawing \+ a new plate that he had made during the past summer because he was not satisfied with the first one made. He made a long call — advised mr. [illegible] to the best way to work on the plates \+ left the first plate with me to experiment upon — then I experimented with megatint Rocker till 1 o'clk — then home to lunch — To studio at 2.s \+ continued experiments till near 4 — then went down town with half a dozen rolls of films to Cullen's in Cor. St. \+ left them with him to develop. Home at 6 — dinner before 7 — Evef. wrote to Ralph \+ sent to Ed. Cook a check for $500 — a loan for a few days to Ireland Real Estate Co — to pay taxes before 1st Nov.

Waitress left this mtt — work is too hard for her.
