## Left Page

### Mon. July 4, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Weather more beautiful if possible — sea very smooth — without white caps — \+ sky almost cloudless — very little air stirring but it was delightfully fresh — In the dining Room no motion of the boat was perceptible — singularly quiet \+ steady. Again the boys had early baths \+ I had none, but was up for blk's — soon after 8 — The exercises for the day we celebrate commenced soon after — three or four of the forwardest young men bossed the job \+ Capt. Reising kindly gave them the freedom of the vessel — all passengers on the Promenade deck were called upon to "fall in" and march — the circuit of the promenade was made two or three times, headed by the band — Then, down to \+ out upon the open deck aft, given to 2d class — There the band took to place on a big raised hatchway — An address was made by a California lawyer — Rather rambling \+ with a moderate amt. of humour. The eagle screamed in a mild-mannered way — a showy young woman, wrapped in the flag, personated the U.S — The flag was raised \+ saluted — "The Star Spangled Banner" \+ "America" were sung very effectively to the accompaniment of the band — It was a moving moment when from that crowd, amid ocean the sublime words \+ music of America were borne up on the still air of this wonderfully beautiful day — Every one had words in printed sheets — Lunch \+ the afternoon passed without special incident — The dinner was a great affair — the dining saloon was crowded — on the program of music were several popular airs — "my old Kentucky Home" — "Dixie" \+ others — as the band played the diners sang them lustily \+ with exhilarating effect. After dinner came the "Grand Ball" on the Promenade deck — which had been enclosed — all chairs moved — decorations \+ colored electric lights hung about — This was on one side only — port — quite at the extremum of along room — The other side was left open \+ dark for cooling off \+ dozing — There was some wild \+ amusing dancing — a party of Southerners travelling together on a 98 day excursion were conspicuous — the actions of some of them not always within the lines of good taste — one, a good-looking, well-formed girl, with a figure second to match, in her exuberance of joy made a fine type of graceful abandon in a wild, gesticulating dance, regardless of conventional form or dance figure — in little sweep she reminded me of Mary Anderson — A big, grizzled German (50 years old he told me) made lots of fun by his boyish enjoyment of the affair — The Virginia Reel was a great romp. "The closing waltz, Strauss' "Blue Danube" brought to mind \+ strongly the first time I heard it, many, many years ago, with Jan Mulford at all A. of D. Annual Cyfed Reception — After 11 when I turned in.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 5, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

No abatement in the good character of the weather. The quiet of the sea \+ the steadiness of the boat is remarkable — In the dining saloon it is impossible to detect any evidences of motion — no tremor or vibration — no swaying or rocking — no sound — It is only when I go farther aft, \+ lower down, to my stateroom \+ lie down in my berth that I can feel the vibration of the machinery or the pulsations of the propellers — often but slightly. The hose reel, having been oiled, made very little noise when used at about 2 a.m. I got up at 7 \+ had a short walk on the deck outside our stateroom before bkfs. I kept about all day taking little rest — The children of the Second Cabin have made a play ground of the deck under our window \+ their noisy chatter could easily make me think that I was in some little apartment opening upon a W. Street. On the Second Cabin Promenade a party in the every sang German songs — among them Musse Dein — \+ harmony did its work — Ralph was out of sorts again — headache \+ swimming head \+ in the afternoon a temperature of 101.2. I got the sick's doctor who gave him a sedility powder \+ anti pyrin — no incident in the evnt. I was kept after deck till 11 — then to bed —
