### Sat. July 16, 1904

Hotel Kaiserhof - Room 201 - Berlin -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A furiously hot day - must be near 90°. Bkfst. at about 9 - after that the boys went out to look about \+ do some shopping \+ I repacked the two trunks that by this time were thoroughly tumbled over - This work took till nearly o'clk - Then settled bill - about $4m. for two days less re bunch) \+ including telegrams, moving baggage etc. - $3.21 - or $7 each. a very good hotel - Reasonable Rates - The boys were off again \+ I waited about till their return \+ at 11.45 we went to Dammtor Station \+ had an interesting time in getting our baggage checked \+ ourselves on our train in due time - There was a great crush for a few minutes but by watching closely \+ gesticulating vigorously I all came out Right \+ we got away on time. 12.38 The Ride was fearfully hot \+ exceedingly dirty - country as flat as a pancake \+ about as interesting - Harvest in progress - wide fields of grain - wheat (most) barley \+ oats - not a machine to be seen at tho' some of the wheat fields must have covered 100 acres - The average was, of course, much less - The work all done by men \+ women - old fashioned scythes \+ cradles - we had our dinner in the Speisewagon - a diner car divided into two compartments, smoker \+ non-smoker - 18 seats in each - Tremendous jostling \+ little space \+ poor service - but the meal was good - a la carte \+ cost 2m. 50pf each. Got to Berlin 4.10 - 10m. late - more gesticulating before the baggage riddle was solved - drove at once to Kaiserhof on William Platz - a large \+ showy hotel - Rooms on top floor 201-2-3 - well furnished - comfortable looking - we worried a long time before Ralph \+ I got a bath - Jim had to Take a sponge bath in his Room - It was after 7 before we went down to dinner - The court of the hotel a lofty \+ imposing dining Room - we had a very satisfactory dinner - in an adjoining hall or dining room - opening into our court, there was a large company having a function with speeches, the singing of college songs \+ much acclamation - After dinner we loafed out to Unter des linden - Hired seats \+ sat there till after 9 - Then to hotel \+ to bed soon after 10 -

## Right Page

### Sun. July 17, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - warm, but not hot, with a pleasant breeze - It was near 9 when we had bkfst. \+ at near 10 we went in a cab to the Kaiser W- memorial Ch - out by the Zoological Gardens - got there at 10.15 - after service had begun - heard the singing - long hymns - no choral - drowned by powerful organ - we sat thro' 25 m. sermon - edified - at a pause in service went to gallery \+ heard closing hymns - The building is large \+ showy but did not interest me at all - The glass in windows I think barbarous - Went to Zoo after getting out of church at 11 - spent 2 hours there - I was not favorably impressed - nothing remarkable in collection - style \+ decoration of some bldgs - amusingly bad - climbed to top of tower - enjoyed views \+ breeze - a great spread of tables under trees, by side of pool (Stagnant) at Restaurant - music there at times - had a very good lunch - Then wandered into Tiergarten \+ thro' it all the way back to hotel - along walks - in after 3 - all of us lay in our lounges in our Rooms \+ slept - At 5.30 took cab by [illegible] \+ Rode thro' principal streets till 7 o'clk under den linden - to the Palaces \+ public buildings \+ back \+ the Round of Tiergarten \+ the sights to be seen There - The Reichstag Bldg - monuments, statues along the Spree - \+ by the line of Restaurants - open air - orchestras - thousands of tables \+ tens of thousands of pleasure seekers, eating - drinking \+ listening to music - Dinner - We \+ I did not go out - sat in Reading room reading English papers till 10.30 when the boys came in -
