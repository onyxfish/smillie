## Left Page

### Tues. April 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day - overcast - light sprinkles of rain - a few timid outbreaks of sunshine \+ late in the afternoon clearing with cold N.W. wind. - To studio at 10 putting in a good days work on Borden outline \+ finishing it. Home to lunch at 1 o'clk. very dark about 2 - To studio at 2.30 finished outline before 5 - then worked down to Wanamaker's \+ bought a sit of good shirt buttons $5.50 \+ a pair of silver cuff buttons $1.00 - home at 6 - dinner at 6.30 - newspaper - later gave my head a thorough shampoo — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 20, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold winter day with considerable snow flying in the m'f. Ther. 28°. at 8 A.M. \+ 32°. at noon. To studio at 10 - some time in getting my cup. Fable ready for work - then set about cleaning up the seven Confecterine etched plates of N. A. of D. Class. They all need a little brushing up before prints are taken by Mr. Vogt. Home to lunch at 1 - I got a letter from Kate at Neuheim baths \+ Geo. got one from Mary at Dresden I took them to Charle in the afternoon. I worked till 5 \+ then out - went to Clausen's \+ then to Nat. Arts Club in 34th St. to see Chas. McKurtz - Home soon after 6 - dinner - Eve'g wrote to Kurtz about my Etchings for S. Louis.
