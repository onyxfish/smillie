## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 25, 1904

**Weather:** Very cold & windy  **Temp:** highest to-day 15°, abt. 45° yesterday

My head has been very troublesome all day. Perhaps it is neuralgia — I went to market in the m't. — first time in several days — & to studio about 10.30 — Tried to make soft-ground — but owing to my stupid condition, spoiled it — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — Tried soft-ground again — laid trial ground on a plate \+ it seemed to work properly — an acid test is necessary to prove it. Ritchie sent me photo. of Bancroft head asking me to give estimate of prices for etching — I Replied that I was in such condition that I did not feel that I could promise to get work done at any given time — I understand that the plate would be wanted in a short time — Home before 5 \+ Rested before dinner — Well did not feel able to take the long, cold journey to the N.A.f.D. St. Louis full Advisory Comm. So I loafed at home, very unhappy, in front of fire.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 26, 1904

**Weather:** A fine winter day  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ I feeling somewhat better in the m't. but still far from being in working trim — the ability to think or to put myself at any work requiring thinking seems to have passed from me — I went to the studio \+ spent the m't. in biting some experimental work done yesterday upon the new soft ground \+ in satisfying myself that there is too much tallow in it — so I remade the ground adding more hard ground to the mixture. Home to lunch \+ back at 2.30 — continued my fussing with the soft ground \+ laid a sample of it on a copper plate but did not get time to try the acid as Mielatz came in \+ took an hour of my time for St. Louis Etg'n. He \+ Chakman were the only members of sub-com. on etchings at The N.A. p. meeting last night. In spite of my informal Resignation I am kept as chairman — he is declining my [illegible] circular is approved \+ it is to be printed out at once — To my mind the attempt now to get together material for an etching exhibit is hopeless — Home at 5 \+ had a Rest dinner — eve'g. I wrote to the boys under great difficulties — not only my headache but Charl came in with a lot of photo lantern slides of Florida subjects. In spite of all impediments I managed to get two short letters written \+ mailed before 10.30 \+ then to bed — Neuralgia in head very troublesome.
