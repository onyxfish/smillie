## Left Page

### Wed. March 2, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still disagreeable weather — a little sunshine for a short time in the m'f. Then grey, raw sky \+ atmosphere. After b'fs. Nicole came \+ we talked of St. Louis Exp'n. business till almost 11 o'clk. Then I went to studio \+ made another effort to get the idea of "ev'f under the pines" in composition lines \+ masses approximating to the idea vaguely in my mind — without success. Home to lunch at 1 — after that to N.A. f.d. — gave some time to Etching class — little to encourage me — Telephoned to Chapsman \+ arranged with him for a meeting of sub-com. on Etching (St. Louis) at his studio on Friday ev'f next — wrote notes to Schilling — Niclatz \+ Kurtz \+ Teleph'd. to Nicole — Home at 5.30 — a rest before dinner — Ev'f. Charl came in for a few minutes. I pasted newspaper scraps that I have cut from time to time into a letter that I am preparing for Bessie

## Right Page

### Thur. March 3, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Foggy, wet, dark \+ muggy — To studio at the usual time \+ made another attempt to get my idea of ev'f under the Pines into more satisfactory shape — made a new charcoal sketch on Academy board 9" × 15". Home to lunch \+ back at 2.30 \+ altho' it was too dark to see properly I painted in monotone on the sketch made in the m'f till after 4 — Home at about 5 \+ had a little rest. Ev'f. with Jane \+ Mary H. — went to Charl's where he entertained us by an exp'n. of his lantern \+ slides from Photos. taken by him all over north Am. \+ Europe
