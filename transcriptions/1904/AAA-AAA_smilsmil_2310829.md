## Left Page

### Sat. July 2, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

On S.S. Patricia - State room 45 A.

Superb day - Up at a little before 5 o'clk. \+ soon had everything ready for a start - wrote to Emma J - Charl \+ Alexander, the shoe man, who has served me so badly - breakfast at about 6 - carriage came at 6.45 - with the luggage we got to ferry at 7.15 \+ to steamer at about 7.30 - got all our traps promptly into state room - saw Chief steward \+ got our places at Table without trouble - Then deck steward had chairs placed - not quite so fortunate - Then bath steward - found letter from Kate Elkin \+ one from Eli - Through for Jim - I wrote a note for Charl \+ a card for Emma \+ sent them back by the pilot who left us at 10.12 m - We got away from the dock at 8.15 - Everything was monotonously pleasant - Have not yet been long enough aboard to get a good idea of the general character of our fellow passengers - Women are in the large majority \+ there are many young ones - Germans seem to predominate \+ there are some Jews \+ a few pronounced or unmistakeable Americans of rather noisy type - There are 184 first class \+ 178 Second Cl. passengers \+ a very considerable No of emigrants - I have seen not less than 250 or 300 on the deck at one time. - At dinner Ralph, who has been troubled all day with headache \+ dizziness gave out \+ kept quiet in his chair till after 9 P.M. - when he felt better - There was some desultory singing \+ piano playing in the dining saloon in the even. The boys \+ I turned in at about 10.30

## Right Page

### Sun. July 3, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

323 m.

The wonderful weather continues - almost cloudless - with delightfully refreshing breeze - Had a good nights rest except for a few minutes at about 2 A.M. when the reel of hose attached to outer wall of our State room was unwound for deck-flushing - The noise for a few minutes screeching \+ discordant - The boys had their early bath, I did not but was up \+ had a breezing on the 2d cabin deck before 6.45. at 8 - This deck is open to sunshine \+ air \+, as it is little used, is preferable to the best of the 1st Cabin promenades which is under cover - much shut in \+ crowded with chairs \+ occupants - There was service in the dining room at 10.30 but I slept thro' it in my State room - lunch at 1 - I find the menu is rather trying to me \+ consequently had some discomfort - but kept wandering about much of the time - Ralph quite recovered from his headache of yesterday - He \+ I wandered over the forward deck, crowded by Steerage passengers of all nationalities (our steward tells me there are between 12 \+ 1400 on board) to the bow where we watched the rush of water - Dinner at 7 - I lost a part of it by falling asleep again after being awakened by the bugle call - Gust spent some time rolled in my blankets in chair - Saw the moon rise at about 10.40 \+ to bed soon after -
Citrate of Mag- routed me out at about 1. A.M. no objection -
