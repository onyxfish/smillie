## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 72-73

Ischl. Hotel Goldenes Kreuz — Rooms 72-73.

Superb day — clear, cool \+ breezy. Awoke about 5 but did not get up for nearly an hour — then to work packing — Ralph had packed one trunk last night, so I had but the one to finish — it proved to be considerable work as I took every thing out \+ repacked from the bottom. Roused the boys at 7.30 — settled bills. Paid tko, had bkfst at 8.30 \+ left in hotel omnibus amid salutes from all hands at 9.20 — rode to West baulign — got luggage receipts without trouble — good seats \+ off at 10 — the country we passed thro was varied \+ interesting — at first a hilly rolling, agricultural country with many farm houses — then hilly \+ wooded \+ about 1 o'clk in sight of mts which we reached before 3 — the ride along the shore of Trauen or Gmunden see very interesting but not always exactly picturesque — after passing the lake the road ran up a fine stream thro a deep valley — Ischl at 4. Surprised to find it a place of such resort — a long line of Hotel Busses at the station — the bus man of the Vornals Bauer declined to take us because we had not written or telegraphed for rooms — the man of the Goldenes Kreuz was less particular \+ we went there — an odd old jumble of a place \+ at once got decent rooms — we washed \+ then went out on a balcony overlooking a rapid running stream \+ had a nice beer — then we walked to the Kursal \+ thro a Park-like place \+ then on to the Esplanade along the bank of the river which is here walled on each side — four rows of trees for a long, pleasant promenade. Midway was the band stand — music \+ throngs of people seated or promenading — nearby an open air restaurant or cafe — we walked up \+ down for a while \+ returned to the hotel — the boys had supper — I went without. Ev'g. feeling tired I remained in \+ wrote a letter to Mary — the boys prowled in the young moon light.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 20, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Drossel zum Weissen Ross — Room 31. St. Wolfgang.

Fine day — Up before 7 — had bkfst at 8 \+ by 9 o'clk started for Hallstedt \+ got there in a little more than half an hour — crossed the lake by steamboat at once pro R.R. station to Hallstadt, a curious jumble of buildings stuck in a most inconvenient way upon a narrow, steep side hill — we walked out to overlook \+ part way up the valley that runs back of the town but not finding anything very picturesque \+ interesting we Ret. \+ climbed a steep pathway running thro woods \+ above the town — leading up to Rudolf's Thurm — a tower high above the town, sculptured by manager of the salt mines higher up — after a time we came to another path that descended \+ on reaching the other end of town we continued our walk to the end \+ then returned to the Hotel Seauer at the landing \+ after a rest had dinner at about 1 — after that, some loafing — then hired about a man who rowed us about for an hour getting some fine effects of cloud, shadow \+ misty light — the best views on the lake are from opposite Hallssiad — in fact there is little else about the lake that is really picturesque. The rock formation with its well defined folding strata is not suitable for picture purposes — our boatman left us near the R.R. station where we waited till the 4 o'clk train came — by it to Ischl — there we found our luggage in charge of porter at the station — got into car on narrow gauge R.R. \+ in a few minutes were off for St. Wolfgang. The afternoon light was beautiful — the ride is short, less than half an hour, was very interesting — thro a cultivated valley, steep rocky mts. on either side — Strobel is at the extreme end of the lake of St. Wolfgang — from R.R. to boat is nearly half a mile — man took our luggage on hand cart \+ we went to boat — after sail of half an hour we reached St. Wolfgang — this hotel is at the landing — we were at once shown to this Room on upper floor, overlooking the lake — curious place — three beds in the Room — low ceiling — roughly carved rafters \+ door casings — huge lock — wrought iron hinges \+ handle of fanciful patterns — low doorway thro' thick wall — etc etc. we had our evening meal on a wide veranda overlooking the lake — after a short sauntter — Ev'g. I tried to do some writing — all got to bed early —
