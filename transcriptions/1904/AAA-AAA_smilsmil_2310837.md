## Left Page

### Mon. July 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

light rain in the early mg - weather soon cleared to brilliant sunshine \+ strong cool wind - Ther. 60 at 8 Am. did not get above 70° at any time during the day - We started out rather late \+ walked down Unter den linden to the old Palace of Wilhelm I \+ went thro' it with a great crowd \+ a german speaking guide - the old Emporers study was very interesting - a sort of museum crowded with many odd bits of furniture \+ decoration - The domed ball Room was interesting because, among other things, of a Remarkable echo repeated 26 times very clearly - Then out \+ walked about. Sat awhile in the Lust graten - walked about the Dom - \+ the Royal Palace \+ Then thro' the Royal Stables where we saw 2 or 300 idle horses \+ almost as many carriages \+ vehicles of all descriptions from the gilded State carriage to the kitchen wagon Then we looked in vain for an inviting looking Restaurant \+ took cab back to Kaiser hof \+ had lunch there - I Rested \+ again the boys went off - after a nap I went out to Tiergarten \+ to Victory tower - climbed to top - 200 feet - beautiful view of city \+ Tiergarten - charming light among the trees of the garden \+ over the lawns \+ flower beds - Then I walked to entrance of Kroll's garden \+ looked in - Then, at 7 - Cab to Kaiser hof - boys there - all of us pretty well used up. Jim could not eat his dinner - Ralph \+ I had tea \+ Roles - Every Jim was at his lowest. Ralph wrote to Charl \+ I wrote to Mary.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A chilly, cloudy day - Ther. 60° \+ did not get up above 65° during the day - Bkf. at about 9 - delays in bkf. avieg for days work prevented our getting away till 11. I wrote a note to Mary before bkf - Replying to one Rec from her before I got up. We took Cab for the Lust garten \+ then walked thro' the old \+ the new museums - it was really a look through The great Staircase in the new museum disappointed me - it was not well proportioned \+ impressive - The Kaulbach frescos are much cracked in places \+ I am sure must have grown dark in color after such a cursory view I think the collections in the two museums not so fine as the British museum - The linings of many cases where small objects are exposed are often much faded \+ many times discolored - spots showed where articles Removed had been - giving an unkempt appearance to all. Explanatory cards are not as frequent or so full as in the British or Met. Mus. The Nat. Mus. was not open to-day - We went into the Pergamum Mus. \+ were much interested there - Then out \+ as it was 1.30 we wandered seeking a Restaurant \+ found one in Dorotheenstr - which proved to be very good there we had lunch - Then out to Unter den Linden where we sat awhile - Then I left the boys \+ Returned to the hotel, where I slept soundly for an hour or more. Then wrote to several Bros. at Geneva about forwarding Rece. of steamer Rugs. After 6 the boys came in \+ I went out for a short walk with Jim on Leipsiger Str. \+ Friedrich Str - bought some poor print \+ back to hotel - none of us in very good shape - the sudden change in weather has upset us - I have worn winter clothing to-day - two days ago - Ther above 40° We had a very light meal - tea \+ Rolls - Later the boys went out again - After a rest I wrote on letter to Charl till 10.30 - Then the boys came - had been bumming around - cheap shows etc -
