## Left Page

### Thur. May 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rather a sulky day so far as the weather was concerned — some heavy rain last night — some bits of sunshine during the day \+ a heavy thunder storm between 8 \+ 9 P.M. Up early — \+ after b'kf't. I spent much of the m'f is getting telephone \+ bell service into commission — one battery short in butler's pantry owing to loss of insulator for zinc pencil — weak action from that end — some error in the arrangement of wires connecting front door bell with kitchen causes annoy. bell to ring continuously when the switch at the house is on — Mary continued the work vigorously \+ Sadie Otis cleaning generally — At 1 I went to Mr. Hardenburg's \+ had dinner — Mary excused herself \+ got her own mid-day meal. After dinner wrote to Ralph \+ went to P.O. Then home \+ to work clearing out rooms of Jim \+ Ralph — a great time — I got boxes \+ put the contents of their bureaus drawers into them \+ put boxes in attic — I worked till 6.30 \+ then thinking it was 5.30. (misread my watch) I told Mary that was the time \+ I lay down on my bed for a rest — At 7.30 I was aware that something was wrong — I went to the kitchen \+ found Mary scurrying around — fire almost out — darkness falling — no lamps lighted — we had our tea very comfortably after 8 — had to go up to the house thro' heavy \+ unexpected rain — every wrote inventory of first floor main house \+ to bed at 11. Moody Rec'd a note from Miss O. Stokes directing him to have the house aired \+ fires made preparatory to arrival of her party to-morrow afternoon —

## Right Page

### Fri. May 20, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

E. Town — The Windsor — Room 46.

A disagreeable day — for a little while in the m'f it looked like clearing, but later it passed into mizzling, light rain \+ showers — Got up at 6, had b'kf't. at 7 \+ right to work — Mary H. stuck to it all day persistently all tho' she was very tired — The clearing out of the linen closet was a hard bit of work \+ by noon I had (except the linen closet) completed the inventory of the main house. Scott Brown got a telephone message from The Stokes' at Cascadeville saying that they were on their way over from Placid — I went to Mr. Hardenburg's \+ telephoned to C.ville but learned that the S's had stopped there only to telephone — At 1 we went to Mr. H's \+ had dinner. I then 'phoned to Hotel at Keene Centre but The S's had not stopped there — Then I 'phoned Egleson at K. Valley without success — then to Kellogg's telling him that I expected to get to the Windsor at about 8 \+ then to look Reede for a lame at 4 — Men to work on inventory that is all that remained to be done — At 3 o'clk. The S. party arrived in two conveyances — I don't know how many women there were — the Ark has now a new set of animals, but they are not in pairs — Miss O. S. goes on crutches owing to some trouble with a knee — both sisters were very civil but Miss O. showed more vivacity — There was much confusion \+ I had so much difficulty in going on with inventory of annex, now in possession of servants, that at last I begged Miss Mitchell to complete the work for me \+ then lend the work to me with a list of articles that she thinks I should supply — Mrs Reede \+ Ham had been waiting for me for nearly an hour — so at about 5.30 we started leaving the Stokes party in possession — we stopped in the valley to pay bills \+ as the whistle for 6 o'clk blew we started for E. Town \+ after some furious driving thro' mud \+ water \+ Rain we got to Kellogg's at 8 o'clk — 16 miles, under such conditions, in two hours, I thing good time — we had a light supper \+ to bed early —
