## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 17, 1904

**Weather:** Fine, clear, cold winter day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine, clear, cold winter day. To studio at 10 \+ continued painting on The Source \- the work going along as well as could be expected \- Home to lunch \- soon after 1 o'clk. \+ back in an hour \- Painted till near 4 \- cleaned up \- Rested \- went to Clausen's \- not in \- a short walk \- home at 6 \- Read news paper till dinner. At 8.30 went to Charl's \- called Ralph up on the phone \+ arranged with him for our trip to New Haven on Sat. to see the Foot ball game \- home at 9.30 \- wrote a note to Jim \+ at 10 took it to G.C. St. Then home \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 18, 1904

**Weather:** A fine day but pretty cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day but pretty cold \- to studio at usual time after routine of usual duties \- I Tackled the problem in the lower left hand corner of The River's Source \+ courageously slapped into the too-dark mass of greens some grey rocks \+ I painted solidly over the wall of rocks that drop out of the picture, making them lighter. Altogether the picture is bettered but still unsatisfactory to me \- it is labored \- studied \- not large \+ spontaneous \- in short, it lacks the inspiration of genius \+ is a fair example of what may be accomplished by drudgery. I have now finished the painting for this stage. When its new frame comes I shall take it up again. Home to lunch \+ back \- washed brushes \- cleaned up \- Awrote a letter to Kate, mailed it at 5 \- a short walk \- home \- a rest \- dinner \- Eve'g. Charl came in \- after he left I prepared things for my bag \- start of Pilgrimage to New Haven to-morrow to be present at the great Yale\-Harvard game of foot\-ball.
