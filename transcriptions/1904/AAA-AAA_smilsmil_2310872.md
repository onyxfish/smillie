## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 28, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A perfect specimen of a day - almost cloudless - delicious color of sea & sky & a temperature that left nothing to be desired I got up at usual time - about 7 - & had a brief sunning & breezing on deck before 8\. at 8 - after that the day passed in loafing about - dozing in the sunshine - sitting on to deck - among boats & ventilators, - with Miss Brown \+ Talking about the Butlers & Sedgwicks of Stockbridge - the Irelands & others with whom she is acquainted or has met — Reading "the Skipper's Wooing" by Jacobs - sleeping & eating - a day of rest cure surely - part of evef. in State room & part in watching moonlight over the water. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 29, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still wonderfully beautiful weather - Up at about 7 on deck & found that we were just passing a large island to south of us that Cap. Dannemann told me was San Miguel - another to the N.O. was just coming in sight - At 8 when I had bf. we were approaching it - At each place at the bf. Tables there was a printed tract giving a good acct. of the Azores, statistical, geographical & political - The Cap. told me that we were within 7 miles of Terceira - we could with a glass easily see the houses & cultivated fields, we were sometime passing & I got a snap shot at the general view. We continue is rather interesting - after passing it we drew rapidly to the north, Graciosa - then we cut across to the eastward of San Jorge, a long, narrow island - we came very close to the southern shore of it - The Cap. said within ¼ of a mile - the shore was precipitous & the uplands were terraced & under high cultivation - several water falls came down to the sea - one in particular of considerable volume made an unbroken jump of several hundred feet - I could not guess how much. There being no known measurement near for a standard - I got several snap shots - after going about half the length of San Jorge we turned away towards Pico on our left (or south) & Fayal right ahead - while crossing this strait diagonally, I had my lunch - The peak of Pico = 7613 f. high, had been for some hours an interesting point in the cloud (or sea) scape. from the eastside I got two or three distant views - clouds making a bar across it - as we drew nearer, under these clouds, we lost sight of the summit which was revealed again later, when we got to the west of it when I took my last snap at the Azores. As we passed the Westerly end of Pico we had to deflect our course in more southerly direction to pass between it & Fayal which are here quite close together - the shore (north) of Pico is a singular wall of black rock - even, & perhaps 30 to 50 f. high against this the surf was breaking - The channel bet. Pico & S. Jorge is from 10 to 12½ m. wide - Puffs of hot vapor still rise from Pico. Snow lies on upper part 8 mos. in the year. Two great rocks (Magdalena Rocks) only 271 f. high are separated from shore and reduce width of channel a bit. Pico today at 2⅝ m. - We passed very close to Horta, leaping from all, & could clearly see all the better details - we gave the town with its salute - The harbor is flanked by two high points, Guia Head & W. & Espalamaca Point the E. - I got snap shots of both - after to pass we Fayal I got my last look at Pico from the west, partly covered by clouds - then we entered upon our long stretch of Atlantic - Newfoundland, 1680 m. west, is nearest land - After my activities among the Azores I had a nap after 5 o'clk. Eve. 4 - some moonlight on deck & some reading in cabin & Fate Room where I have had a movable electric light conveniently placed.
