## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 13, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain all day — Blcfs. at 9.30 after a very thorough scrul — When I went into Jim's room to see if he had come in last night I was surprised to find two sleepers in his bed — A onel had by some strange mischance missed his train \+ so, he shared Jim's bed with him.
The weather was so persistently bad that I did not go out of the house which gave me a chance to talk over matters with Jim — He \+ G— came down to a private blcfs. at 11 o'clk.
Dinner at 2. Jim Ret? to New Haven on 8 P.M. train — Charl came in the eve't — \+ so did Gordon Smillie.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 14, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Blustering, cloudy \+ cold after the storm of yesterday which proved to be wide spread \+ very violent. The barometer fell lower than at any time for some years past. I got to studio at usual time \+ worked all day on Vesuvius foreground getting some advance — not much. I worked till 4 o'clk. Then cleaned palette \+ went down town to Cullen's photo. establishment \+ had a talk with one of the bro: C — about the disappointing results in some of my films exposed while in Europe last summer. I bought some photo. material — walked up Nassau St — bought 95 Diary — took out way train at City Hall — got to 42nd St. in 10 m. Found Mary Cook at the house making a call. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve't, tired — fooled time away over newspapers.
