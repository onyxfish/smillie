## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 5, 1904

**Weather:** Dull, Raw day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, Raw day \- to studio after 10 to spent the day working on pen\- sketch \- the dome of Frauenk\- from Terrace, Dresden \- I am taking too much care in the preparation of this sketch \- the result will be absence of freedom \+ the abandon essential to etched work. Home at 1 \- back at 2.30 \+ worked till 4.30 \- could not see to work any longer \- had a short walk \- then rested for three quarters of an hour \- dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g Rain \- went to Century \- I had a fairly pleasant eve'g till near 11 \- then home \- Rain continued \- I fussed over photo prints, sent by Cullen, till after 12.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 6, 1904

**Weather:** Rain in the m'g \- weather unpleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the m'g \- weather unpleasant till noon \- then it cleared brilliantly \- I had blkp. at about 9 \+ then with newspapers \+ the Cullen photos, trying to get the six dog. in the order in which they were taken \- my notes made at the time of photographing are not always sufficiently full \+ clear. It was about noon when I went to studio \- there I mounted the pen sketch made yesterday of the Frauenkirk \- Dresden \- then had a short walk \+ home to dinner at 2 \- Jim not yet am\- altho' he had written that he co'. be home Sat. night \+ Sunday m'g. About 4 o'clk. I went to Charl's \- taking my photo. prints \- while looking at them I managed, clumsily, to drop two pkg\. of negatives (1 doz. in each) into C's slop jar \- \+ in an instant they got wet enough to stick together \+ for an hour I worked them under water before I could separate them \- At 6 o'clk. took them home in a pail of water \+ then tacked them up to dry. Eve'g \- Reading \+ fussing around \+
