## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 14, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — to studio before 10 \+ to work on Source — by the end of the m\. I awake to the fact that I was treating my foreground without proper consideration of its relation to the whole work the sky is really the motive of the picture. I have succeeded in part in treating it simply \+ with largeness \+ the foreground is now bristling with detail, showing knowledge \+ some dexterity but detracting from the sky, in stead of being subordinate to it — For some time I could not make up my mind to paint out what I have been so carefully painting in — but, after a time I went at it with a big brush \+ heroic determination. I made a change but am not sure that it is for the better. Home to lunch at 2 — P 3 went to Mr. Sidney Smith's musical tea, across the way — Grace Arnold, sang delightfully \+ Miss Lawrence played the piano charmingly — Miss W. J. Stillman (Marie Spartelli) was the lioness of the occasion. Mr. T.C. Williams came in late — he came to me very cordially with face wreathed with the smiles that wont come off — but I would have none of it — I told him frankly that I adhered to the correctness of my detraining Ralph over night \+ considered his note to me unfair in its assumptions — He said of course that he had no intention to be discourteous \+ hoped that I wd come often to see Ralph — He has not heard the last of it from me. Walked up Madison Ave to 40 St. with Duncan I — [illegible] J.H. MacPhee told me that his wife continues to be very miserable man to studio — cleaned up — Dinner at 6.30 — Chas sent his ticket to Boston Symph. Concert. I went — [illegible] in other seat — Ferruccio Busoni played the piano part of Henselts Concert in F minor most admirably — Loeffer's Symphonic Poem — The death of Tintagiles, I did not understand \+ the Beethoven 8th Symphony did not move me — walked home.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 15, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, clear cold day. After Help. went to Chas's — then to studio \+ worked all the m\. on lower R.h. cor. of "The Source" — I don't like it in form, color or handling — home to lunch \+ after it to 2d. Nat. Bk. \+ to studio at 2:15 Pitched into lower L.h. corner \+ slung fresh paint all over it careless of what was underneath — Soon after 4 I gave up, very tired, I went home \+ stretched on my bed till 6 — Dinner. Even\. finished letter to Bessie written Wed. Even\. \+ mailed it at 8 o'clk. Wrote to Jim \+ Ralph — mailed them at 10 o'clk.
