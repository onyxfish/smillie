## Left Page

### Tues. June 14, 1904

**Weather:** Cool \+ pleasant day  **Temp:**

Cool \+ pleasant day \- I got up with head ache which did not leave me till the afternoon. I got to studio at 10.30 \+ painted on Vesuvius. Home to lunch at 1 \- after that gave way to my head ache \+ slept for an hour \- to studio at 3.30 \+ worked till near 6 \- then a short walk \+ home to dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g at about 8 I went to studio \+ developed a roll of half-dozen exposures made last Sunday \- This is by way of experiment \+ to make myself familiar with my new camera \+ to working before I go abroad \- Home soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 15, 1904

**Weather:** Delightful day  **Temp:**

Delightful day \- Painted on Vesuvius. Wrote to Parke that I expected to get it done before I left the Country \- An engraver, Cade. by name, made a long call seeking information on a number of technical points \- \+ then Vandrous came bringing paper \+ ink, desiring to do some printing on my press \- Home to lunch \- painted for a little while in the afternoon \- then set the painting aside to dry before giving the final treatment. Went to H. D. Allee's at 5 \- \+ by a little work he relieved some discomfort in my mouth \- I walked across the Park to Lex. Ave. \+ 57th S't \+ there took car home \- After dinner I went to Charl's \+ together we revised the itinerary of my summer trip \- arranging it so that I may meet Mary at Hanover \- Home at 10.30

Appalling disaster on East River \- the burning of Steamboat Gen't. Slocum \- with S. School Excursion \+ loss of 500 or more lives.
