## Left Page

### Sun. June 26, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Intensely hot m'f\- blazing sunshine \- a smart shower with thunder after 2 P. M. a steaming, clouded afternoon \- clearing eve'g. Got my v'clp's\- at about 10 after an elaborate bath \- after that I went up to the park with my camera \+ made half a dozen exposures \- then to studio \+ developed them \- did it learn much \- in spite of ice in my trays the heat injured them all more or less. Home to dinner at 2 \- a rest after that \- then some writing \+ sorting of things \- some tea at 7 \+ then I went to Emma Ireland's \- Cha'. Collins came in later \- \+ together, at about 10, we came up town to our homes.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 27, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still hot, but much modified by clouds \+ breezes \- Up early \+ wrote to Miss Stokes sending her a Money order for $2.22 for freight \+ "cartage" on some coffee cups \+ frying pans \- also to Chas Moody \- Soon after Miller came \+ we went to Regan's \+ discussed partition with him \- then to Grocer \+ Butcher \- to studio \- got films \- home \- photo\. printing \+ assorting under clothing etc till 1 \- lunch \- at 2 o'clk\. Jim, Ralph \+ I went to Hoboken \+ spent an hour or more wandering over the Patricia \- then by ferry to Barclay St\- \+ went to J. A. Matt's \- 88 Beekman St\- for Franklin Stove \- none there but got card to Jackson's, No. 50 Beekman \- found one there for $27.00 Miss Stokes agrees to pay half the expense \- I bought it \- then home at 6 o'clk \- dinner at 6.30 \- then wrote a short letter to Mary \- Chaol came in \-
