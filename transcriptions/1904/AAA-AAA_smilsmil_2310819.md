## Left Page

### Sun. June 12, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very beautiful day — bright \+ pleasantly cool — I got up at 7 \+ was busy till 9 with rubbing \+ scrubbing — after bkfst. I read newspaper for awhile — then out with my new camera — To fifth Ave — to Bdway — To 59th St — to 5th Ave — \+ then to Mad. Ave. \+ took cars home, having made six 24 posures — full no. on roll. Dinner at 2 — I had my Sunday afternoon nap — then wrote letter to Kate — after 8. I went to Charl's \+ met Mr. Schultz — a Swiss Canton merchant — just in from crossing the Pacific \+ this continent \+ now on his way to Switzerland — Home soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 13, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A portion of the day was overcast — chilly \+ rather raw, so that a light top-coat was desir-able. To studio at 10.30 \+ tackled Resinvins \+ painted over Trees in foreground — not successfully but leaving them in such shape that it may not be difficult to finish them into passable shape in another bout. Clausen sent for \+ got 33×44 frame to Repair it. Home soon after 1 — lunch — back \+ painted till about 5 — then washed up — Vondrous came to arrange for the privilege of doing some printing on my press. Home at about 6 — dinner at 6.30 — evef. finished letter to Mary — mailed it \+ the one written to Kate — then, a little after 9 — I went to Charl's — Ge. \+ Sheldon there. Charl had just explained to them the Proposition from Jno. Hays Hammond — that Sheldon go, with son of Hammond, West where Mr. Beebe, his right hand man, will set them to work, without salary, under conditions that will soon show what they are good for — then they will get their reward. Ch. showed us a lot of slides on screen from Lantern. Home after 11.

Mr. Kramer called this mng. after bkfst. about the Borden Photo-gravure — went to studio with me \+ took drawing promising to let me see Photo-Reduction in a few days —
