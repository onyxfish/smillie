## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 27, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine winter day — but, as seems to be usual, I got up with a headache, \+ as is too frequently the case, it stuck to me all day growing in intensity till at about 4.30 I came home \+ went to bed. I got to studio after 10 but could do no work Bessie Parks Ballantine with her husband \+ his sister Mrs. Young came in for a few minutes — the visit was very unsatisfactory to me as the studio was in great confusion \+ I felt very wretched. — I had a short rest after lunch — then to 2d. Nat'l Bank — then to Pomeroy's \+ had truss recovered \+ new pads — then to Keppel's \+ was much interested in a collection on view of contemporaneous german Etchings \+ Lithographs — some of them puzzled me to know how they were done. — Then I walked up to Winderlich's \+ saw the collection of etchings by Mielatz — a very good one — then home \+ have up to my headache that now had become very severe. — I curled up on my bed. — Pain \+ discomfort increasing every minutes till it was almost unbearable — nausea so great that I was in a cold sweat from head to heels. Mary H. put a mustard plaster on the back of my neck easing the pain there while the pain in my eyes \+ temples seemed to grow greater — sometime in the mf. I was able to get up \+ take my clothes off \+ after that I got a little sleep.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 28, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet day — sometimes the rain fell very heavily — but at night it cleared with some gleams of moonlight. — I got up at 9 o'clk. feeling unexpectedly well after a night of such pain \+ discomfort — I went down to my bkfst., read newspaper — wrote some notes — Rested from 1 to 2 \+ then had dinner — again some writing — \+ again rest from 4 till 6 — tea at 6.30 — Evef. some more writing — Charl came in \+ spent part of the evef. with us.
