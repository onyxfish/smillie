## Left Page

### Fri. May 13, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day \- milder \- to studio at usual time \+ worked all day on Bordeu, but with little energy because of headache. "The Source" came back from Scutury \- to lunch \+ back at 2 30 \+ worked till 5 when I had to give up because of the pain in my eyes \+ the back of my head. \- I roamed about the streets till after 6 \- then home \- dinner \- Charl came in while we were at table \+ stayed for a few minutes. He got home at 2 A.M. Eve'g. I tried to write got letter to Ralph finished \+ just as I commenced one to fine, Charl came in again \- he stayed till 10.30 \- we talked of his trip just finished \+ I talked of my projected trip across the Atlantic. He gives very unflattering description of St. Louis \+ her Exposition \- to bed with a splitting headache.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 14, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'g \- overcast \- east wind \+ very chilly towards night. \- up for bkf. after an unrestful night \- got thro' my m'g chores \+ to studio for a few minutes \- then to bank \+ home at about noon \- Then, before lunch, made up for some of the sleep lost last night \- lunch at 1 \- Afternoon I went to studio \- packed prints from five of the Etching class competition plates \+ four or five Roth p.p. \+ sent them by mail to Mr. Fred. Morton of Brush \+ Pencil. Chicago \- I also wrote him a letter \+ took it, with the pkg.; to Station H. Then to Van Glahn's hardware store, 42° S. \+ got a clock to take to Keene \- I bought my R. R. tickets to Albany in the m'g. Home soon after 6 \- dinner. Eve'g with newspapers till Charl came in \- he stayed till after 10 \-
