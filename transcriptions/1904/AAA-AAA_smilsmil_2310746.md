## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An extremely cold day. Official Ther. at noon 14°. Strong N.W. wind. It was after 10 when I got to studio - feeling tired \+ discouraged - after a look at my "Source" I decided to put it away for a time. I brought out the monolone portrait of Father on his 76" birthday, painted a year ago, \+ I worked on it all day \+, I think improved it in every way. I went home to lunch \+ Ret. to studio as usual \+ worked till nearey 4.30 Then to Alexander's with a shoe that needed a little attention - Then to Ditson \+ bought ticket to Grossmith's entertainment To-morro p.m. - Then to Keppel's - made selection of four Swan-Durand's Reproductions of Rembrandt etchings for the N.A. school. There till 6 - Then home to dinner. Charl came in for a few minutes - I wrote to Williams, to Emma \+ to Keppel.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. down to 1° below zero - Some sunshine - some wind. Got to studio at about 10 \+ found it comfortably warm - Worked till after 1 on "Passing Showers" \+ think it is now finished. Washed up brushes etc. - Then home to lunch late. After that by fine till near 3 o'clk - Then to Mendelssohn Hall - 40th St. W. of 6th Ave. \+ heard Geo. Grossmith in Humorous Recital - He gave "Things you must have noticed" with mock German Song, "I ch Liebe Dich" - "Oh dear, what can the motor be?" \+ a musical illustration of "Haunted by Sally Gray." Then he gave us a lecture on "How to succeed" Song "Going a Bridging Sir, she said" \+ illustrated how he once appeared on the Cinematograph - This was exceedingly funny - showed us first how he posed, going quietly thro' the movements \+ then how he appeared in the Cinematograph - jigging \+ jerking thro' the movement already rehearsed. He wound up with some instructions how to write a modern musical Comedy - parodying in most ridiculous manner the absurdities that characterize the modern musical comedy - altogether, he was very funny \+ I had a good laugh. Emma \+ Grace - with Lilian Cherry not far from me - I met them \+ had a pleasant little chat. Then to Frank Millet's Studio, 6 E. 23. at 5.30 \+ met the 10 or a dozen Artist Patrons of the Champeny Sale to come off on Thurs. T Friday evengs. The matter of making the sale a lively one was discussed but nothing very definite was agreed upon, none of us, except Geo. Innes jr. being in position to assume financial risks - no one was willing to spend $20 - Home at 6.30 \+ had dinner - We ½ quietly before a fine fire did not have courage to leave it \+ go again to Mendelssohn Hall to hear Rob. Lansing address Am. Geographical Socy on "Questions settled by the Award of the Alaskan Boundary Tribunal — with Stereopticon views.
