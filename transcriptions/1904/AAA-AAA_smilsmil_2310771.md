## Left Page

### Tues. March 8, 1904

**Weather:** Clouded - with a pleasant spring-like feeling in the air  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at usual time \+ made a successful transfer from gelatine tracing of old man's head \+ then to work drawing it in - At 12.30 I walked down to 2°. Nal°. Bk. then home to lunch \+ at 2.30 to studio \+ at 3.30 had head all drawn in - now it will have to be carefully revised \+ the face worked over to develop \+ finish the drawing - Then varnished the plate. Then unpacked box from Gill containing painting - "Cumulus after Sunset." Then I walked down to Keppel's in 16th St. \+ spent an hour with him \+ Allison in selecting a number of Parrish Etchings for the St. Louis Exp°. Keppel wrote at once to Mr. P. at London for permission - Parrish is now somewhere in Italy - Home at 6.30 - dinner - Every wrote to Nicoll. C. A. Platt \+ Wm. Saffau - Charl came in for a little while about 9 - We went out together I left Laff. a note at his door - \+ C. \+ I then walked to 42°. S. - \+ home by Park Ave.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 9, 1904

**Weather:** Clear with high, cold wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio a little earlier than usual - spent a little time in revising the work on face that I laid in yesterday - Then I prepared my two paintings. Passing Showers \+ Cumulus, after Sunset. for sending to Socy of Am. Artists Exbn. Home to lunch \+ at 2 went to N.A. for Etching class - very languid interest there - little work doing - left soon after 3 - got to Keppel's at a little before 5 - Nicoll met me there \+ soon after \+ we looked over such work as K. had by Parish. Platt, Bacher, Church, Washburn \+ others \+ made selections for St. Louis subject to events to follow - Home at 6 - Mr. Mc. Williams Presb°. pastor at Blessiming, a friend of Fanny's dined with us - a very pleasant fellow - at 8.30 I went to Yale's \+ spent an hour with him selecting from his etchings - took 8 or 9 away with me to show to Com.
