## Left Page

### Tues. May 3, 1904

**Weather:** Very pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — got to studio a little earlier than usual — to work on "The Source" — cleaned some of the coal black out of the lower l.h. cor. of foreground \+ then tackled the tree that has caused me so much trouble yesterday — I painted it in \+ out a number of times during the m'g \+ just before I went to my lunch I scraped it all out — back at 2.30 — took a big brush \+ again painted it in \+ left it altho' not at all what I want — After 4 Mr. Dodd \+ her niece Miss Dodd came \+ soon after them Kitty Riley \+ her friend Miss Sutherland came — after visiting me they went up to George's studio. It was near 6 when they left — Home soon after 6 — dinner — Eve'g I went to the Chelsea — W. 23\. S\. called on Mrs Thurber \+ saw Mr. T — \+ the eldest boy — left at 9.45 — walked home.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 4, 1904

**Weather:** A very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — got to studio at about 9.30 \+ put things in order to begin work again on the Borden drawing — cleared away the paints etc. \+ got out sables \+ india ink \+ worked till near 1 — then home to lunch — After that rode down to Canal St. \+ got plates \+ p.p. — Etching Class, from K. \+ V. \+ back to studio at 3 — worked on Borden — s'uprised by a visit from Belle \+ Mary Tweedy — had a very pleasant chat — continued my work till near 6 — then home after a short stroll — dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g at 8.30 went to Fred Ireland's \+ conferred with him \+ Alice about Jim's schedule of studies for next year at college — coming to a decision to ask Jim to come home next Sunday \+ then, all together, we will revise the schedule that he has already constructed — Home at 10.30.
