## Left Page

### Sat. April 23, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant, but with a trang in the east wind that was foreboding  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 \+ promptly got to work on Vesuvius repainting sky \+ distant mts — did not work as freely as yesterday — there was no atmosphere on my palette that I could mix into my pigments. Home to lunch \+ back soon after 2 - worked till near 5. Then walked to 22d. St. \+ Fourth Ave. to pay Charity \+ Outlook dues — both offices closed at 12 o'clk. Walked back to studio — washed brushes \+ home soon after 6 — dinner — Sueff. for awhile with newspaper too tired for anything — lay down on my bed \+ had a nap. Then got up \+ worried over my accts \+ wrote some notes — To bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 24, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast much of the day — some hours of bright sunshine about the middle of it. Strong cool easterly \+ southerly winds — Up early \+ had a good scrub — bkfst. at 9 — newspaper \+ writing after that till near noon — wrote to Miss Mitchell that I planned to go to leave to get the house ready for the Misses Stokes on May 16th. Then I went to studio \+ opened it up for ventilation — then wandered about \+ down to the East River front looking for picturesque material for sketching. Home at 1.30 — dinner at 2 — an afternoon nap. a call from Mrs. Giles who came to tell me what an invalid her daughter is \+ to show me process prints from some illustrations she has made recently. Chase came in to bid me good bye — off with Steven to-morrow on a business trip east, west \+ south — may be gone two or three weeks. Wrote to Scott Brown \+ to Chas. Moody — At 8 o'clk. went to Edwd. Cook's \+ spent the evg there. Saw Edwd., Mary — Mrs. Sprague — Carlton \+ Miriam
