## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 6, 1904

Luzernerhof - Rooms 87. 90.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

When I got up in the m'g. nothing could be seen out over the lake - a veil of mist covered everything - by 11 o'clc. the sun shone brightly - but nothing could be seen of the mts. At last Pilatus emerged faintly \+ the Rigi \+ later in the afternoon some more distant mts. with Brunig could be barely seen - a few clouds were in the sky - the veil over all was like autumn haze at home - we had bkfo. at about 8.30 \+ after that I settled with the Madam \+ we left saying that we c'd telephone instructions about the baggage - Then we walked down the hill \+ about thro' the town, over the two old bridges \+ Then we took a look at the hotels - went into the National, one of the largest \+ newest on the lake but were told there was not a vacant bed - Then to Luzernerhof - which is under the same management as the great Schweizerhof - just west of it, \+ there we got fortunately two very pleasant rooms on 4th floor - the double bedded room has a balcony overlooking the lake - we are much pleased - we had table "d'hote" lunch at 12.30 - after that wandered about the town \+ along the Promenade till near 4 - then I turned in \+ the boys went out in a row boat - I had a rest \+ at 6 went out \+ got some photos - at 7 I had a light supper \+ at 7.30 the boys had table d'hote dinner - I loafed in Reading room with news papers nearly \+ we went to Schweitzer hof \+ heard music for awhile - Then to the National \+ saw a very good display of fireworks - Then to Kursaal, expecting to get tail end of a concert but it was a play - Ret'd to Schweitzerhof \+ heard end of programme there — there to our Rooms.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 7, 1904

Hotel Beau séjour - or, Hotel du Park \+ Ceres in - Rooms 12 - 13 -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Day opened fine but as we progressed southward it grew more overcast \+ after we got thro' the St. Gotth. and Tunnel we found the clouds more rainy looking. when I got up at 6 \+ looked out the m'f was beautifully clear - all the mts visible as I had not seen them before I was undecided as to whether to stay longer \+ go up Pilatus \+ go on - bkfo. at 8 - clouds seemed to be banking up at the north - Porter told me that train left at 8.98 so we settled at Hotel (which we have found to be most satisfactory) \+ were transferred by bus to Station on arrival in train we found it 1st class only - by paying 8 f. each we could take first class seats - so, we let that train go \+ went to the Place over mts. near by \+ later took 10.50 train - very long \+ crowded we got seats in section with elderly man \+ two daughters from Pittsburgh going to Naples to Return to U. S. by Princess Irene - The R. R. ride was the most remarkable, in one ways, that I have ever taken - as a piece of engineering it is simply gigantic - one must go over the road to understand fully the daring methods employed in overcoming steep grades at short distances - mountain torrents \+ the mts. themselves - The culmination of this work is, of course, the tunnel 9 1/4 mi. long - but there was nothing dramatic or exciting in sitting for 18 mins. in a dark, or dimly lighted stuffy, gaseous compartment with a roaring in your ears. It is more evident to the senses when you go into a hole in the ground \+ after 5 or more m., emerge a couple of hundred feet above or below the level at which you entered - by slight effort you can realize that you have made a spiral, effort loops thro' the solid Rock - this was done more than half a dozen times - we went Thro' 80 tunnels \+ over 324 bridges of more than 32 ft span - The views looking down into valleys, \+ up to snow tops were very fine - at about 7.30 we got to Faido, at the mouth of the tunnel - stopped 25 min. \+ had lunch in the table d'hote - 3 fr. - it was a great lunch on plate \+ good meats \+ good wine - at 5.30 we got to Lugano \+ found much pleasure in to find the Real Hotel du Parc - Took us of Beau séjour Hotel du Parc - got two very pleasant rooms - washed up \+ Then out for a stroll so that I might if possible, get my bearings. The old Hotel du Parc - to which Annie \+ I came 12 years ago has been torn down \+ a great hotel has been built in its place. Right past the old church - the place no longer recognizable the hotel where we are is under the old Drapner for is in what the porter describes as "an old castle built hundreds of years ago". We dined at table d'hote at 7 - men the boys went out for a promenade \+ I wrote for awhile - \+ to bed early -
