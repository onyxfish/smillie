## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 25, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Patricia arr. bringing Mary \+ her girls.

A cold winter day - light snow early in the day increasing till night. I got up at 7 \+ just about 9. Charl sent word that the Patricia would reach her dock at 10.30 - so we scrabbled \+ gobbled our bkft out of 45 Jim, Ralph \+ I started for Hoboken \+ got to the Steamm dock just as the gangway was being hoisted into place - Charl already there - Mary \+ her girls soon joined us - all in good shape after a rough \+ stormy voyage but without mishap of any kind. They had no such reports to make as the newspapers have published concerning the big fast Steamers that have arr. within the past fortnight. There were 13 trunks, boxes, bags, bundles \+ suit cases - but there was no trouble with Customs officials \+ after an hour the boys \+ I left for home - Charl \+ "the arrivals" following soon after - We had a festive lunch at about 1. It was after that that I sent a Cable message to Kate. George came in later. Dinner at 6.30, Janet - so unwell, severe cold, that she was in bed. After dinner, an informal exchange of Xmas presents. I worked till 11 taking velox prints.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 26, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold, raw, dark day. Bkft. at about 9 o'clk - all late - Jane H. - so unwell that she was unable to get up. It was nearly 11 when I went to grocers \+ butchers \+ then to studio - there for a few minutes - then to Jno. Cook's \+ saw Edna \+ told her that the boys could not come to lunch at 1.30 as bro. Charles is to have his dinner at 2. The boys went there for about half an hour or more \+ helped dress the tree - I went home \+ cut mats for making white margins in printing velox photos. I wrote a letter to Scott Brown \+ sent check for [illegible] $6.49 - taxes \+ Xmas present for her $3 - At 2 o'clk. Mary \+ her girls, the boys \+ I went to Charl's \+ had a swell dinner - at table for about two hours - After that we went thro' his house to admire the order \+ cleanliness. At about 6 I went home \+ until 8.30 was at work making velox prints - Then I cleaned up \+ just before 9 went to Charl's again - a very nasty night - nuzzling rain \+ freezing making walking difficult \+ dangerous! I fancy got a little from Kate - very bright \+ satisfactory - I think it will benefit her greatly to be left alone to rely upon herself - All home before 10 - The girls sleeping at Charl's. Mrs. H. somewhat better at night.
