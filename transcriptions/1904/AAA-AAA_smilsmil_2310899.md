## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 21, 1904

**Weather:** Cloudy m'g. clearing to beautiful day over f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'g. clearing to beautiful day over f. I was late at home after bkfst \+ was caught by Fred't. W. Morton editor of Brush & Pencil of Chicago — He held me in conversation for some time, then went with me to studio — After a long time I found that he was here seeking financial relief the B. & P. being "held up" for want of $. — He has a plan to get hold of Carnegie — He had an appointment to meet Mr. Caldwell at the Century at 12.30 \+ asked me to join him there, which I did, losing my entire m'g — When I got there M — \+ C — were there — H.R. Butler had been asked, over phone, to come \+ promised to be along in a few minutes — Then C — excused himself. Butler came in a few minutes, then I excused my self — home to lunch — to studio at 2 — The afternoon was divided between superintending some plumbing work done in "Dark Room" \+ writing a letter to Mary to meet her as she sails on the "Patricia," Dec. 3 — Home at about 5 — dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g at 8 o'clk went to Charl's — home before 9 \+ started a dress to go to a Musical entertainment given by Louise (Mr. Walter) Cook — but felt too much more like going to bed that I gave it up — wrote notes to Ware \+ Herbert Smith \+ Louise \+ then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 22, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant altho' hazy \+ overcast much of the day. It was after 10 when I got to studio. I commenced a second day's work on 12 × 16 "The day's end" — \+ by 3 o'clk had gone over it getting it all together \+ with as much finish as I think it should have — in fact I think I had better leave it as it is. Home to lunch \+ back as usual — When I had cleaned my palette I telephoned to W.C. Cullen enquiring if he w'd. be in for an hour or so — till I could get to his store \+ was answered that he w'd. be in — Then went to Butler's to see why my 33 × 44 frame had not yet got to the gilder's hands — excuses unsatisfactory — Then I went to Cullen's — he was out — the fool-boy at the phone had got my name wrong \+ C — did not know, altho' he tried, who to communicate with — The other bro. could not give me the information I wished to get from U. C — \+ I was much vexed. I bought a plate back for my camera \+ holder \+ a dozen plates — Home at 6.15 by subway — I don't like the smell of it — dinner at 6.30 — Evening read newspaper \+ fussed over photos.
