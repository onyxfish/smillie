## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 8, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant but clouded  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant but clouded — I got up \+ had bkfo. about 9 — but feeling very seedy — I went to studio, to grocers \+ butchers \+ then to bank where I got from Labst a partial statement, showing that all drafts from abroad, except the last $10 were in \+ that still I had bal. enough to make it safe for me to draw $100 to meet expenses of E. Herbert's trip. Then home to lunch — got note from Eleanor Cook announcing her engagement to D. Frederick Robertson Jones, instructor of economics at Bryn Mawr — To be married next June. Jane Hinshelwood spent the day with Mary. I was so used up that I had to go to bed. Temp. 99 2/5 — \+ severe pain in front of head \+ eyes — I think it may be nasal catarrh —

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 9, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

I don't know what the weather was as I was in bed all day — I know that it rained a little during the night \+ that a very little sunshine fell upon my window shade — my night's rest was disturbed by my fever which disappeared during the night but an uncomfortable headache did not leave me till later. In the afternoon I was able to do some reading. Plate. Geo. came in \+ sat with us for awhile
