## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 9, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant, cool \+ breezy day \- Bkfs. bet. 8 \+ 9 \- the day was spent in coakfay sort of work, winding up matters generally \- The boys went off m.f. \+ afternoon with Kath. \+ Eliz\^t\. \- we had our dinner at the Hotel du Nord after which I was glad to take a Rest feeling rather tired. It was after 4 when I went to Mary's \+ with her took a parting walk ufs the new Bismark Str. to the American Ch. \+ then out to the new Lutheran Ch. \- which stands at the edge of a wheat field \- The growth of the city in this direction is singular \- There are no suburbs \- streets are graded \+ paved. \+ fine houses built right out into farm lands with no intermediate zone between city \+ country \- back to hotel at 6 \- Rested till 7 \- Then boys came \+ Mary, Kate, Kath \+ Eliz\^d \- \+ we went to the Bouhl Terrace Belvedere \- There the boys \+ I had bread \+ butter \+ beer \- The others, later, had drinks of one kind \+ another. \+ we listened to the Willy Olsen orchestra till 10 \-\. To hotel \+ to bed before 11 \-

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 10, 1904

Prague, Hotel U. černého Koné or, the Black Horse \-
Rooms 32 \+ 33.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day \- Up at 6 \+ after two hours work I had written to Deutsche Bank in Berlin \- Kaiser \+ Pr. Berlin \- To the Hotel Bristol Vienna \+ to Mr. Colt of the Anglo Am. Club \- Had almost completed my packing of the two trunks \- Then I had Bkfs \- a short Rest \+ completed my work \- Then to bank \+ had 200 m\- cl\. \+ 23 Hr. Austrian money \- Then to Anglo\-Am. club left note for Mr. Colt \+ looked over last Am. papers \- then to Mary's for a few min. \- Before 11 I had settled everything at hotel \+ with porter \+ luggage started for Station, attended by boys \+ the T. party \- Had to pay 3 m. in trunks \- up sat my finances \- all in Krona \- Train from Berlin on time \- 11\^d \- Great Rush \- no seats \- car added \- got good seats. Bessie bossed the job \- train left on time, 11 28. \- Followed the windings of the Elbe for more than 50 m \- After passing a picturesque castle Schrockenstein a great plain was crossed \- At Kralups came to Moldau River \- again rocky borders, very barren \+ unpicturesque \- Reached Prague at 3½ on time \- At Bodubach, 33 M. from Dresden, we reached the line \+ passed a custom Ho. \- 4 p.m \- Trunks were all hauled into a room opened \+ poked into \- hand baggage was looked at in the car \- a vexatious formality \- detention about 30 m \- Leaving at 7 o'clk \- we went into the dining car, small, inconvenient \+ very dirty (especially the kitchen part) \+ had a poor dinner at a cost of about 87 cts each. \- We had no trouble at Prague in getting trunks and driving at once to the Black Horse Hotel where we were given rooms, front, 2\^d floor (or 3\^d as we w\^d count) 32 \+ 33 \- In a few min. \- we started out in open wagon \+ rode for two hours \- 4 to 6 o'clk. We drove over the Habsburgbrucke stopping to look at the outside of many churches, schools \+ museums \+ up the hill to the Palace \+ Cathedral, we drove thro' several courts \+ went into the Cathedral where an old verger took us about \+ between his German (trans. by boys) \+ Baedeker we got a fair description of its many historical objects \- on descending we recrossed some bridge \+ went thro' old Jewish quarter now being demolished to make way for fine modern improvements \- we went back \+ forth thro' many streets \- The University \+ schools \+ other interesting bldgs \- being pointed out to us as we passed. At 7 we had our ever. meal in the picturesque court, or garden, of the hotel \- after that, a short walk \- Then back to hotels.
