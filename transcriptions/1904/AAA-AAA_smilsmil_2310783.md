## Left Page

### Fri. April 1, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came in afternoon

Persistent Rain all day long. In the m'f. at b'kf. got a letter from Miss Virginia Lewis Mitchell written from Keene Heights stating that she had been looking for a summer home, near St. Huberts, at the request of friends — Scott Brown had shown her over the Ark — She w'd. be in N.Y. City to-day only \+ asked me to call her up on the phone, giving No. as she wished to ask some questions — at 9.30 I went to Charles's phone \+ soon got my lady — She was as shrewd \+ business-like in her conversation as she had been in her letter — I asked her if she was acting in the way of a friend or a business agent — She said that for six years she had been the private sec'y of the Misses Stokes — sisters of Anson Phelps \+ the others — They are now somewhere south \+ she is going to join them — She asked me many questions — expressed herself as well pleased \+ asked me to write a description of the place, as I had given it to her, \+ mail it to Charlestown, Jefferson Co, W. Va — where she would for a few days \+ then she w'd. go to the Stokes sisters \+ submit it to them. I remained in-doors all day but made little progress on the Border outline — Lunch at 1 — Ralph had not yet arr'd \+ Jim was away — lunching with alfalfa fellow — Ralph came along later —. I made some notes of what I w'd. write to Miss Mitchell. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'f. at 8 — Jane \+ Mary H — The boys \+ I, went to Charles's \+ had along else [illegible] there he giving us an exhibition of Lantern slides, made from negatives by him, taken within the past twenty years, at home \+ abroad — He has a good little Lantern with electric light \+ made an interesting show — Home before 11 — cloudless, but very moist, moonlight.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 2, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m'f. — quite mild — later the sky was clouded \+ threatening \+ the wind blew up cold — clear, cold moonlight night. After b'kf. I went to Treese's telephone, called up Nicoll \+ had a talk with him — then home again — Spent most of the m'f. writing description of Keene Ht. for Miss Mitchell \+ take to the Misses Stokes \+ rough plans \+ sketches of exterior. Rent $900 for season. Lunch at usual hour — after that went to Etching Class at N. A. of D. — found 7 students drawing from young Tony Mondara — all working upon their plates \+ asking for one more session, which I granted — They are all trying very hard, evidently, but not so intelligently as I could wish — I did not stay long with them — I felt too much out of sorts — Rose down to Park Ave. \+ 59 St. \+ went to 466 Park — where Geo. boards — Found him on lounge — has been out of sorts for several days — Trouble with his bowels — I then walked home, getting there about 5.30 — on my red till dinner at 6.30 — Did not go to Century in the eve'f. — too unwell — but went with the others to Charl's where we saw another lot of Photo. Lantern slides from those shown last eve'f. Home before 11 with an aching head.

Before lunch went to studio \+ then to Davies Bros. Coal yard, foot of E. 37th St. \+ ordered a ton of coal for studio.
