## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 7, 1904

**Weather:** overcast with a pleasant, cool wind. Threatening Rain — one short dash at about 11  **Temp:** [illegible]

We got up late \+ I had a Rub-down at about 10 with The boys went to Mary's. I gave them money \+ they went to the opera \+ bought tickets for "Queen of Sheba" to-night. I went to the Grosser Kunst Austellung, spent the Rest of the m'g there. An indescribable collection of pictures in a labyrinth of small galleries, me supposing exterior let me to effect carefully planned galleries — the only large gallery is the one under the dome, given to sculpture — the others are all made with white muslin ceilings — the day was dark \+ cloudy \+ the light good — Sunny days there may be too much — the side walls are decorated in all sorts of ways — covered with burlaps, Kalsomined in varying shades of grey, generally greenish in tone \+ splotched — the paintings are all kinds from fine specimens of last century work to incomprehensible caricature — from large, carefully studied work down to insignificance — the galleries were generally given to the work of one man — one town or one set of ideas — much interested in preparatory studies by Neuvel — also a lot of pencil sketches by Rodin — Puvis — I had no time to look at anything carefully — it was 12.45 when I completed my walk thro' — About 9.30 Am I went into the Rooms of Anglo-American Club, same bldg. as Hotel du Nord — to make enquiries — it was in charge of servants who could not speak English — I left my card saying I w. Return at 1 — I did so \+ was met cordially by Mr. Colt — said he had just put my name down for courtesies of Club for one week \+ asked me to make myself at home — I was much surprised but gathered from what he said that he knew I was a member of the Century boys \+ had that was my endorsement — Went to hotel, met \+ had a pleasant chat — Rain came on in the afternoon \+ considerable fell before it cleared at about 8 — I went into A.A. Club about 5 o'clk \+ spent some time looking over newspapers — Mr. Huntington, a non-Resident Centurion, introduced himself \+ we had a pleasant chat — Went to Mary's before 7 for a little while — Took no supper — The boys went to the Opera \+ had a very good time — I went to hotel \+ wrote part of a letter to Charl \+ to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 8, 1904

King of Saxony's birth-day.

**Weather:** A cool, breezy day — cloudy with bits of bright sunshine  **Temp:** [illegible]

I took bkfst. at usual time \+ after that we wandered down Prager Str. — I bought several bits of things \+ got from Kolm the amt. of bill for R.R. tickets to Genoa — it was after 10 when we got to Mary's — Kath. Els. Jim, Ralph \+ I walked to the Opera House where S't-bat- tickets for to-night — it being the King's birthday some of the principal streets were decorated with flags \+ banners — very gay — all colors except blue — crowds were gathering in Opera Square \+ on the Brühl Terrace — so, as it appeared to be in preparation we waited for the function — soldiers marched into the sq. \+ took position — the police cleared the sq. — one got place on front line \+ waited for one hour \+ twenty minutes — at last a Party on foot came from the palace — walked around the inside of the sq. of soldiers — the band played — the soldiers buzzed three times \+ that was all we got for our long waiting — The great bell, rung at 12 o'clk was well worth hearing — Kath. \+ Els. left us \+ we went to the Belvidere \+ had dinner — then to hotel — got letter of a—s to bank just before 3 — had to wait till 3 — bank hours 9-1 from 3 to 6 — got money — went to Kohu's — got R.R. ticket \+ paid him 4.00 m — then to Mary's for awhile — then Elsie \+ had a rest till 6 — a light supper with the boys \+ at 7 we joined the other members of the family \+ went to the Opera — Bessie had got three tickets for us in a box on the second row — middle — at 5 m. 50 pf. each — the others went to upper seats — the house is of medium size — handsome foyer \+ stairway — boxes small — 7 chairs in a box — very close \+ uncomfortable to sit on — the orchestra 43 — \+ at times drowned the singers — stage setting good — not remarkable. Opera "Mignon." The part of Mignon was taken by Wedekind — (Philine) by Traul, Schlenker — Wm Meister by Jager — Lothario by Blaschke etc. — etc. — The singing of Wedekind was the leading attraction
