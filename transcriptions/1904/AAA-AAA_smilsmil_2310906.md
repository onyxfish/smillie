## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 7, 1904

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day, raw, threatening snow. To studio at usual time - Spent m'f working with my new Kodak developing machine - Developed one spool of 12 films with but one hitch - the apron caught on mid-division on going over from one compartment to the other, stopping it for an instant before I could release it, making a developer mark upon one film. Home to lunch \+ after that went to Ed.'s Cook's office, 150 Nassau St., thinking there was to be a meeting of Ireland Real Estate Co - learned that the time meeting was third Wed. in Dec. We talked awhile about Co's affairs - then I Ret. to studio and used machine in developing another spool of films - worse luck than in morning - the apron caught \+ was more obstinate in letting go \+ several of the films were badly marked. Home at 6 - dressed hurriedly \+ went to Mr. Sidney Smith's reception, across the street - there for a few minutes \+ home to dinner at 6.45. So: I got to experimenting with my Velox print developer \+ did not stop till 12 o'clk, when I had used up all my Velox paper.

These thin paper pages make trouble for me when I am so careless as to turn two leaves, instead of one, without noticing the mistake at the time.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 6, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - Spent the m'f at studio some work on Boden photo-gravure \+ some fussing at toning photo-prints. This photo business is taking much time \+ considerable money with but small results so far. Home to lunch at 1 o'cl then to 2d. Nat'. Bank - then down-town to W. C. Cullen's - Char came in while I was there - I found a Velox printing machine being demonstrated by an expert \+ was so much interested that I bought one \+ a film developing machine. I took these home with me \+ played with them till dinner. Self - went to Water Color Soc'y meeting. Sherwood Studio Bldg Special, called to act on Report of Com. that has been looking for some place for next Exhib'n - The old Durand-Ruel Galleries \+ the Rooms in N.A. of D. bl'd, 109th S. were considered. There was much diversity of opinion \+ no one but Chairman, the Sec'y. seemed to have any definite opinion - C. strongly urged the old D-R galleries which to my mind are impossible - I think the real trouble is not so much gallery as it is the want of good, interesting stuff out of which to make an ex'b'n - after 10, as there seemed to be little likelihood of any decision being reached \+ the air being close \+ foul with tobacco smoke, I left and walked home.
