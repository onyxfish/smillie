## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 1, 1904

**Weather:** A  **Temp:** [illegible]

A blazing hot day - Me boys went to the T's before 8 \+ with them to Grosses Garten \+ there had bkfst. out of doors. I had bkfst. at hotel \+ at 10 went to Mary's - from there I went to the picture gallery at the Zwinger \+ spent the mf. there till 1 o'clk. - It is a noble coll. of paintings, ancient \+ modern - the galleries are impressive but the lighting is not ideal. The sun blazed down thro' the sky lights in the large galleries \+ the small, side-lighted galleries more tide had sun shining in directly \+ the lighting of the large galleries would be imp'd. I think, by adding a hanging canopy under sky-light. I paid my devotions to the Sistine Madonna first \+ was, I think, duly impressed - I must be confessed that I have seen paintings that come more easily within the range of my comprehension than the works of Raphael - Van Dyk, for instance or Rembrandt. I worked diligently till 1 o'clk - then cab to hotel where the boys were waiting for me - dinner - after that a rest - then I went to Kohn's with my bundle of steamer rugs \+ heavy coats \+ he has promised to send them to Genoa \+ have them in our Stateroom when she sails Sept. 22d, - all for 7 m. - This is a load off my mind - then with the boys to a shop where I spent 12 m. - for photos of Dresden - at 6.30 we went to Mary's \+ all hands went out by car to the Ausstellungs Halle, Grosser Garten where in the Restaurant attached we had food \+ drink - the boys \+ I had our every meal of bread \+ butter \+ beer while we listened to the band of one of the Regiments stationed here - the even being very warm I felt no discomfort in sitting out under the trees. Great crowds were present \+ it was a lively scene. At 9.40 after wandering about Mary, Kate, Ralph \+ I left, Katharine \+ Jim remained - K. \+ I left M. \+ K. at the door of their Pension \+ then to hotel \+ bed - noisy neighbors \+ noisy streets made me restless for a couple of hours - then quiet \+ sleep.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 2, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast all day - now \+ then some sickly sunshine - muggy hot - Up late after a night disturbed by all kinds of noises - bkfst. at 9 - it was not till after 10 that I got to the museum of pictures - went into Print Dept. - but could not find any cat. in English to aid me - saw some good Water-colors \+ specimens of Reproductive processes of various kinds - everything was under glass - Tables for students - but all that I saw were looking at photos, from prints \+ pictures - I did not see any originals in portfolios - Then up to paintings \+ spent best of mf. among modern paintings where I found much that was interesting. At 1 went back to hotel \+ had dinner with the boys. After that they went off to a suburban Resort where there is just now as a sort of Shooting match - Crossbows at a big image of Bird on a pole. I rested awhile \+ then I sauntered thro' some of the streets. Tea near 6 - then Kate came, the forerunner of the others, the boys coming in from their outing at the same time \+ at about 7 we all went to the Belvedere on the River Terrace - Here the boys \+ I had our every meal of bread \+ beer while we listened to the music of Willy Olsen's orchestra which seemed to me even more noisy than it was on Sunday even — We walked back at about 10.30 leaving the T's at their Pension - a few drops of rain - again so much disturbed by street noises that I could not sleep till about 1 o'clk when I got up - closed my windows \+ shut myself up in a hat box - choosing what seemed to be the lesser of two evils.
