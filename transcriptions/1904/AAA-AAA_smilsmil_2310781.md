## Left Page

### Mon. March 28, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

not an agreeable day - overcast, with a chilly rawness in the air - a few gleams of sunshine & many flying flakes of snow at times. After bkfs. I went to grocer & butcher & stopped at studio & saw Mr. Thurber - then home & tried to get to work upon drawing of Borden library - but felt miserably uncomfortable. I stretched a sheet of common paper, laid out a scale for the drawing & determined angles of perspective. Lunch at 1 - I persisted in my work but my discomfort increased - muscles of arms & legs felt sore - head was heavy but did not ache & every effort, mental or physical was pain or discomfort. I went out & walked for more than an hour & came in feeling worn out, chilly & very unhappy - I could eat no dinner & suffered till 10 o'clk. when I went to bed with temp. at 100/5°. It seems to me that I am out of sorts now all the time - with few exceptions of feeling well - never really well to the point of enjoyment.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 29, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless day - rather cool - rather breezy. After a trying night I awoke about 6 o'clk with normal [illegible] temperature & a dismal headache - I had bkfs. in bed & got up at about 11 - Then out & sauntered on the sunny side of Park. Ave. for a considerable length of time - met Alice Dreland there & she walked with me awhile. Lunch at 1. I was unable to do any work & after 3 I went out again & prowled about. I went to studio about 4 but Mr. Thurber had gone. It was near 5 when I came in - on my bed till 6.30 then I ate a very little & after that passed an uncomfortable evening.
