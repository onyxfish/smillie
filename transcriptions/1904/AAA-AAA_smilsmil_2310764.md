## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 23, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fickle day of clouds + sunshine. The Ther. Ranging between 40° + 50°. I got up in the m'g + went down to my S'dp'. but did not think that I would be safe for me to go out-of-doors. The soreness in throat + chest is much less — I had not energy enough to do anything. I slept in the m'g + again in the afternoon. In the eve'g. I wrote notes to Nicoll + to Chapman advising them to elect a new Chairman for their Sub-com. on Etching + Engr'. St. Louis S + P.m. To-day I Rec'd circular calling meeting of full Advisory Com. at N.A.f.D. Mus. eve'g. + stating that Budworth's rooms had been engaged for exhibits March 15-17". This means that we cannot possibly get up an ex'b'n of etchings + engravings by that time. It is a repetition of the Buffalo fiasco.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 24, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another day - comparatively mild. Ther. almost 50° — some sunshine — more shadow. I remained at home till about 11 - Then the rain has ceased + the sidewalks had dried — I went out + walked on the sunny side of Park Ave — then to 34th St. + Fifth Ave - went in to see the Illustrators Show — I found my two contributions — the Morton etching up to the ceiling + the Pazzi Chapel Pen sketch down to the floor with a broken glass. "The lite doesn't count for much when Trombone + kettle drum" have the floor". Home — lunch at 1 — a rest + at 3 went to Studio — made a list of Etcher Exhibitors at Club ex'b'ns from 1882 to 1892 — from Catalogues — then home — Nicoll came in at about 5 o'clk. + we had a talk about prospects of making an Etching show at St. Louis — Dinner at 6.30 — after that, wrote a note to Mr. Taylor, Sec'y of Com. on Admissions at Century excusing myself for this eve'g + enclosing a dollar — Sent by messenger — Eve'g wrote out form of proposed circular inviting etching exhibits + sent it to Nicoll care of Davis at the N.A.f.D. — Then to bed.
