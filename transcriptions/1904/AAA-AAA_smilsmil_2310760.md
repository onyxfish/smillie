## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 15, 1904

**Weather:** Fine clear, cold day  **Temp:**

Got a letter from Wm. Parke enclosing check for $50 - payment for frame on Vesuvius from near Sorrento. To studio about 10 \+ to work on Vesuvius. — at the close of the day have worked generally of over sky, distance \+ water. Home to lunch as usual - back at 2.30 after having been to bank to make deposit - worked till near 5 \+ then washed up - Then to 23d. St. \+ got some colors - home at 6.15 - dinner soon after — to Charles' at 7.30 to make enquiries about European mails Then home \+ finished long letter to Kate

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 16, 1904

**Weather:** The coldest day of this date on record.  **Temp:**

A very cold day - reaching it about Zero in the early mng. The Ther. fell rapidly until at night the wind \+ the cold were bitter. To studio at usual time \+ worked on Vesuvius - disheartening work - I cannot get "the hang" of it. Home to lunch \+ back soon after 2 \+ painted till 4 - then cleaned up \+ got to Carlton Chapman's - Sherwood Bld. - about 4.30 - Com. on Etching \+ Eng.": St. Louis Exp.". Chapman, Schilling, Mcoll \+ self present - Nichols absent owing to sudden illness. Chas. Kurtz present, representing Art Dep. Chapman proposed that I should be Chairman - no objections were made - we three discussed matters informally as to probable extent of Etching exhibit - the list of those to be invited \+ the circular of invitation to be sent out. We separated a little before 6 - Mr. Kurtz to communicate with us as soon as he is able to secure a room for reception of offered exhibits. Schilling \+ I walked down 5th Ave. to cor. \+ 38th St. - I got home just before 6.30 - dinner - "Avey" wrote to Mr. Thurber \+ W.A. Parke.
