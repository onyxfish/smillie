## Left Page

### Tuesday April 5, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Alice Ireland. dined with us.

A delightful day - cloudless - breezy - balmy. After bkfs: I was for some time arranging my acct - was late in getting to grocer &c - went to studio + saw Gee who had dragged himself down there - sick - but anxious to get away from the misery of his boarding house. Then I went to bank - on the way called to see Stetshen Painsh - not in - on my way home - met Emma Ireland - turned + walked with her - took two or three turns with her in Mad. Sq. Grand then home at 1 - The boys went to Brooklyn this m'g with Alice Ireland to call on Frank Adams + wife - they got back in time for lunch at 1 - after that I did some writing + went to station + with letters - It was nearly 4 when I got to work - upon Borden outline - stuck to it till near 6 - then a rest for half an hour - It was near 7 when Alice Ireland came to dinner - Part of the ev'g she was at the Piano with Jim despitefully using college songs -

## Right Page

### Mon. April 4, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Splendid day - cloudless - brilliant + pleasantly cool - After bkfs - went to Davies Coal yard, foot of E. 37th St - on my way there, went all over a building approaching completion in E. 38th St. Public Bathing house erected by Mr. A. A. Anderson - an excellent, philanthropic work. Saw some picturesque "stuff" at the docks - then to grocer - butcher + studio + home - a little work on Borden - Then to Bank - to Scribner's + home to lunch. Worked till 3.30 - Then head ached so that again I went out for a sunning - went in to Durand-Ruel's + saw the Van D. Perrine painting the latest art freak - Such exhibitions - such "criticisms" anger + depress me - I cannot believe it sincere. Then home + worked till near 6. Alice Ireland called - I went to studio + then home to dinner at 6.30 - The boys had gone down the bay somewhere + did not get home till 7.30 - Ev'g Lizzie Smillie came in + spent the ev'g with us.
