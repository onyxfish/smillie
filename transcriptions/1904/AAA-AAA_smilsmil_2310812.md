## Left Page

### Sun. May 29, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - up at 7 \+ until 9 given over to ablutions etc. - then breakfast - then newspapers - \+ before 12 went to Edw. \+ then to Walter Cook's - neither in - then home \+ Read "Letters of a Chinese Official" till dinner. At 3.15 I started for Greenwood \+ got there in less than an hour - walked out to our plot \+ loitered there for a time - then back - got to Third Ave. \+ 18th S. at just about 6 - went over to Emma Irelands - 331 E. 17th S. \+ visited there till 9 o'clk - Dr. Emerson came in a little before that - I walked home getting in at about 9.30.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 30, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ muggy all day - soon after 6 P.M. a heavy thunderstorm - to studio at about 10.30 again at work on Vesuvius - not feeling very fit but I fetched into the sky \+ changed it radically painting out all the clouds in the lower sky \+ down to horizon so that now the mountain is not entangled with other forms. Home to lunch as usual \+ back at 2.30 \+ painted till 4.30 when, having got over sky \+ distant mts. I stopped - cleaned up \+ after a short street loaf home soon after \+ had a rest on my bed - dinner at 6.30 Eveg. - some reading \+ some "clearing up" - to bed early.
