## Left Page

### Tues. July 26, 1904

**Weather:** Dull, cool day \- light rain in the m'g until about 11 at intervals.  **Temp:**

I got up at 7 feeling very much out of sorts \+ with severe muscular pain in right side \- The weather \+ my condition obliged me, very reluctantly, to give up expedition to Potsdam to-day. The boys did not seem unhappy about it, both of them somewhat out of sorts. I wrote to Mary \+ spent most of the m'g lying on my lounge \+ reading Monte Cristo. Lunch at 1.30 \- The boys went off together in the afternoon \- At 3.30 the weather \+ the pain in my side being better I went to a photo. place \- at 9 Steck balm \+ got a number of photos. Then wandered back to Hotel by Schloss platz. Tiergarten \+ Wilhelm Str. doing a little shopping on the way. Dinner at 7. Well \- boys out \- I read more Monte Cristo \- He seems to be the original of Sherlock Holmes.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 27, 1904

At Potsdam.

**Weather:** A pleasant day altho' much of the time cloudy.  **Temp:**

We left hotel soon after 8. Cab to Potsdam Platz \- train left in a few min. 7m 20pf \- got to 1\- 17½ m. in about ½ hour \- The new [illegible] was not open till 10 \- so we loitered in dust garden \- went to Art Market \- under full headway \- took a look at outside of Ch. of S. Nicholas There to Wilhelm Platz \+ back in time to enter Town palace with first party \- Some of the Rooms, historically interesting but the imp. of the whole was that it was a mean, somewhat tawdry place. Then to garrison ch. \- Visited the vault under pulpit \+ stood by coffin of Fred. The great, his father \+ looked at the interesting coffin of [illegible] from Waterloo \+ Sedan each side of pulpit. Then we walked thro' to the Brandenburg gate \+ entered the Park \+ walked to the Friedens-Kirche \- did not go into the church but did go into the mausoleum of Emp. Fred. III. \- impressive, much more so than the Charlottenburg mausoleum \- The sculpture figure of Fred. fine \- Then on thro' the park to the terraces of Sanssouci \- The broad walk \- The fountains \+ flower beds were exceedingly beautiful \- The terraces I did not think much of \+ the palace at the top is a poor affair \- The statue, last moments of Fred. the great, placed at the window where he died was, to me, pathetic \- Then on to the orangery \- beautiful terrace Chinese astronomical bronzes, looted from Pekin 1901 \- nothing of interest in interior but wide \+ charming views from tops of towers \- The terraced garden here was very beautiful. We had Rolls \+ Beer at a Cafe at the foot, after passing the old mill. After a rest of half an hour we started on broad walk to New Palace \- The interior of which, altho' a summer residence of the Kaiser, hardly more interesting than the other buildings \- Bad. Painting \+ imitation of marble everywhere \- poor stuff. The caricature portrait of Voltaire by Freak. the only object that amused me \- Then thro' park, past Charlottenburg \+ out up on public Road \- very tired \- Train cab to Brandenburg gate. There took cab \- Rode to [illegible] \- pictures que old [illegible]. Climbed to top of tower \+ got the finest views we have yet seen about here \- a remarkable expanse of forest \+ water \- Ret. thro' Russian colony \- passed marble palace \- did not go in \- on to Babelsburg \- town cab waited while we went into the Park \- had a survey of palaces \- did not do this one \- Ret. to Cab \- Rode to Glienicke Bridge \+ there took boat for Wannsee \- at about 6 \- got to [illegible] soon after 7 \- waited for train \+ got to Potsdammer Platz at 7.75 \- Cab to Hotel \+ had dinner at 8 \- after a very hard but interesting day \- to bed early.
