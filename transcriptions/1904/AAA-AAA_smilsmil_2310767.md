## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 29, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day, dark \+ raw ending in sleet. I got to studio at about 10 \+ continued experiments with soft ground getting satisfactory results with my last mixture. Then I commenced work upon a charcoal sketch 9"×15"- upright - pine under pine trees looking out over a quiet water to trees beyond \+ an eve \+ sky. By the way, before I went to studio I took letter just recd. from Kate to Charl \+ read it to him. Home to lunch. back before 3 \+ finished sketch — not what I intended it to be. I had in mind a different effect as well as treatment. At about 4.30 I went to 34th S\. 15th Ave. \+ got my two contributions to the Illustrators Club. The glass on Pazzi Chapel broken — \+ no redress. Took them to studio — then home — a short rest before dinner — Eve\+ after newspaper, feeling unfit to write, altho' there are a number of notes \+ letters that I should write, I commenced reading Crawford's "Heart of Rome" — read till after 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 1, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beastly day — dark \+ raw — ice, sleet, rain slush till night — then a clearing. I got up as usual but had a depressing discomfort in my head that was enough to prevent my doing any real work. I got to studio after 10 \+ found a card from Nicoll — he had missed me while I was attending to my chores at Butcher's \+ Grocer's. After a little more experimental work on soft ground with acid I went thro' slush \+ rain to W. 10th S\. \+ called on J. G. Brown \+ J. C. Nicoll — was with them till 12.30 — then walked home stopping at 2d Nat. Bank \+ home to lunch at 1.30. It was about 3 when I got to studio \+ for a couple of hours worked at clearing up the confusion that has been gathering there for some weeks. Then home \+ had a rest before dinner. Eve\+ I wrote a note to Meta Girot (Mr. Chas. P. Adams) \+ part of a letter to Bessie. To bed before 11.
