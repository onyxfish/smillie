## Left Page

### Sunday April 3, 1904

**Weather:** Bright, cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold & windy - Up soon after 7 \+ went Thro' my scrubbing - bkfst. at 9. Before 11 Jim, Ralph \+ I went out for an investigation of the excavating work now being done by the N.Y. Central R.R. from 44th St. up to near 57th S. It is something immense - we went up day - \+ Park Ave. across to Mad. Ave. \+ home at about 12 - I had a nap before dinner at 2 — After dinner I did some writing - Mary Cook \+ her mother, Mrs. Sprague, made a pleasant call on me in the afternoon - Tea at 6.30 Eve'g - wrote to Scott Brown - W.G. Neilson \+ W.G. Parke - also some reading

## Right Page

### Wed. April 6, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant m'g - wind from east - sky gradually overcast - Raw, chilly afternoon. The usual m'g chores after bkfst. - Went to studio but did not see Geo. Worked all the m'g on Borden outline - To studio again to see Geo. - just missed him. Lunch at 1 - After that to NA of D. Etching Class - Found Stahr taking mfrs. from the Competition plates - eight of them - all below what I had hoped - the subject given them too trying perhaps - Home at 5 - a rest before dinner - Jim \+ Ralph \+ The H's at Nat. Hist. Mus. Dinner at 6.30 Eve'g I wrote part of letter to Mary - Rec'd letter from Fannie this m'g.
