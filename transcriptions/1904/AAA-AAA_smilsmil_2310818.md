## Left Page

### Fri. June 10, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very cool, pleasant day - after 6½p. went to Charl's - Then my chores - Then to studio & once more got at Vesuvius & put in what I now think is a satisfactory day's work - I commenced upon the sky going lightly our lower part - & over the mts down to the sea where I commenced solid painting - I covered all of it before I went home to lunch - about 2 Ret. to studio just in time to see the police & fire dep. come up in answer to alarm sent in by Ryan - a man in his employ had heedlessly tossed a burning match into a barrel where there was loose oakum - The smoke was immediate & dense smoke - the little fire was soon put out & the excitement was over - later I saw Ryan & found him in ugly mood because I would not let him have all the cellar room - Talked threateningly of going to another place - as soon as the smoke cleared I resumed painting - Soon after 3 I went to Scribner's & saw Mr. Smith & told him I thought of sending in bill for $200 for Borden drawing - he would not make comment but told me to send in bill - Then I went to Hoboken & spent an hour going over The Princess drove upon which I have dec. cured a stateroom for Return from Genoa Sept. 22. To studio at about 6 - Then home & had a little Rest before dinner - After that wrote a short letter to Jim - Then Clark came in - I got to bed soon after 10 - pretty tired.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 11, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hackley Commencement

A most glorious day - Up early - wrote note - sent check to Hamburg Am. line - b½p. + then to G.C. Station soon after 9 - Emma & Mary Dreland soon came along & I got tickets for all & at 9¹⁵ we took seats in Hackley Special car - Helen Butler soon joined us & from T. station we all rode up to the school - the three were enthusiastic - never having been there before - we were set down at Goodhue Hall where the Graduating exercises were to be & Ruffle put us in good seats - There was a long delay for some unknown reason - but at last there was a start - Rev. Mr. Slicer making introductory address in place of Carroll D. Wright Pres. of Bd. of trustees, who was absent. Minot J. Savage followed in an earnest & impressive address that to my mind was upon a high plane & thought & ideal with regard to education & formation of character - He was followed by an extremely tiresome old man ( ) who meandered along in a dreadful way, failing dismally to be funny - Prof. Putnam spoke well for a few minutes - Then the diplomas were given - The graduating class presented a bible to the school - & to the junior class, the class cup - a latin hymn, written by Mr. Williams, was sung very effectively - The school singing is always very impressive to me - It was after 1 before the exercises closed - Then we went to house - thro' the dormitories & then lunch - after that to chapel to witness planting of class ivy - Then - Alice Dreland & Mary & Helen D. - left us - Emma & I went to the athletic field & got pleasant seats on the high ground under trees where we could overlook the field - it was all ideal - we sat patiently thro' interminable waits & saw nearly all of the games, some of which were interesting but not widely so - We left at about 5.30 took livery conveyance at Gate - Rode to Station - took 6 ox train - got to G.C.A. at 7 & there parted - I went home - dinner waiting - very grateful as my lunch had not much staying quality - Later in the Eve if I went to Charl's & there met Mr. Schuetz - a Swiss - just am from China by way of Vancouver - Home early & to bed.
