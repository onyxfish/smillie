## Left Page

### Sat. July 30, 1904

*Rathen + the Bastei.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm day, but with delightfully cool breeze to temper it — as agreed we went to the T's before 10 o'clock Mary, Kath. + Eliz". walked to Central Station + took 10 Train for Rathen which we reached in about half an hour, following the Elbe closely nearly all of the way — toward the end of our journey the rocks on the other side of the river took the Raufpart or Bastei, form not unlike, in a small way, the Palisades on the Hudson — they are quarried extensively to the destruction of picturesqueness. Where weathered the stone is dark but where fresh cut it is a light buff not unlike Caen stone + apparently as soft. At Rathen we crossed the River in a primitive ferry-boat attached to an anchor a couple of hundred feet up mid-stream which runs rapidly — aided by a rudder the boat swings from one side of the stream to the other like a pendulum. By an easy trail we climbed 615 feet to the top of the rocks which are much weathered + eroded + fantastic in shape, taking the form of pinnacles + detached towers — near the summit a fine stone bridge has been thrown across a chasm — very effective. There are numerous "blocks" on the way up + the view from the tops is wide + interesting, showing a great sweep of the Elbe + the butte-like forms of rocks rising above the surrounding country. At the tops is a fine Restaurant — we sat under the trees on the flat Rock top — with outlook on three sides of us — for in one than an hour + were served with a good dinner for — m. apiece. About 3 we started on our way down. Returning by a different path — down thro' a remarkable defile in the rocks — very wild — historical interest — the story being that an army penetrated the supposedly impenetrable pass + captured their opponents on the other side — the path brought us out at our place of starting + at the River side — at a Gasthaus, on a terrace under fine trees + with a pleasant view up + down the River — we had coffee + beer, each according to his or her taste — After a half hour rest we crossed the River to R.R. Station — 15 m. ahead of train time + then had another 15 m. of uncomfortable waiting. The train being that much behind time — a hot ride in to Dresden getting in after 7 — we went to Hotel + after a short rest had a light meal at 8. Went in our Rooms reading + resting after a very pleasant day excursion —

## Right Page

### Sun. July 31, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright + very warm day — I got up at about 7 + had a sponge bath + had b'kft. alone at 9 — the boys breakfasting later — It was about 11 when we started out + loitered down to the riverside + took a look at palaces + churches + then back to hotel a few min. before 1 — Men dinner — Table d'hote — after that I went to my room + had a nap — then wrote a letter to the Tweedys at Thun — + at 4.30 went to Rácknitzer St. — the boys there — + remained till about 6 — then we walked to the Königliches Belvedere — a terrace on the River bank — a fine Establishment where there is a concert room — many tables under the trees + many more people — 50 pf. entrance. 10 tickets for 3 m. I got ten tickets — we had bread + butter + beer for our supper + enjoyed a good programme of music well played by an excellent orchestra — Willy Olsen, Kapellmeister — we kept our seats until 9.45 — then out on terrace + watched the crowds of people — the lights shimmering over the River etc etc — Cab to Hotel + to bed at 11.
