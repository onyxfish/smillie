## Left Page

### Wea. SAT. JUNE 6, 1904 Ther.

Jim + Ralph came home.
Mond. 6th

Jun's 21st Birthday —

Are uncomfortably hot day in spite of strong breeze — Ther. getting nearly up to 90°. in the streets Gov't. Report at 2.30 85°. Eve'g — it fell below 70°. I had an early b'f'st. + got to Allan's at 9 o'clk — + he spent half an hour fitting lower set — Then home + spent some time marking with indelible ink all sorts of things for the laundry — lunch at 1 in much confusion — Uncle W. + Aunt A. had gone, intending to spend the day making divers excursions to various parts of the city before taking boat to Bo'n — This arrangement greatly disturbed J.H. who are sure that the aged couple will be killed in the streets some way — Mary took their bag to Divings while many went hunting for a servant — Jane came in distressed to find a woman from an intelligence office offering herself for place — Mary did not come in till after 1.30 having found no one — The girl had waited her coming + was engaged to come to-morrow — At 2 I went to Yales + spent a large part of P.M. with him — explained to me the meaning of hard, or contracted kidney — thinks I need not worry about my chances of such trouble while I am abroad — I am in fair condition, my age considered — went to Douglass — 4th. Ave. + paid bill — Then to Laundry + home — Jim + Ralph arr'd at about the same time — 6 o'clk — afy we went to Charley's + he gave us a very good dinner. After that we went over our projected summer trips upon a map + then looked at photos taken along same route — Geo. came in for a little while — home at 10.30 —

This is Monday — June 6 th
"

## Right Page

### Wea. SUN. JUNE 5, 1904 Ther.

A very uncomfortably hot day — up at 7 after a fairly comfortable night — Had a scout + b'f'st. at 9 — It was near 11 when I went out with my new camera, loaded with a 6 film spool, to make some test exposures — I got a view of the monstrous pile of steel ribs, or skeleton, of the Hotel Belmont, then of the steel frame work of the Armory — cor. 34th S. + Park Ave — Then of excavation — Lex. Ave. from 45th to 50th S — Then a view on Fifth Ave. — + then a view of the times flat iron bl'f — from Dorey acre Sq. — Then home, stopping to rest in Bryant Park — Dinner at 1.30 — Meff's had cooked a Roast + beef very nicely — I got ice-cream + the dinner was good — Again I had a long afternoon nap — Then to 23 2. 46th S. + saw Geo. + Emily Cook — Then to B. Yale's + saw him for a few minutes — + then turned home at 7 to escape a shower that did not come — Eve'g. Mabel Hues Kelewood, a daughter of Robert, spent part of the eve'g with us — just from Chester, S.C. where, under the Pres'n B'd of Education, she has been teaching colored children — very enthusiastic — Chane came in — also Val. Smillie — + there was general chat till 10 —
