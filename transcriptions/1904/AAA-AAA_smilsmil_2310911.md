## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 15, 1904

**Weather:** Dull day - not so cold as yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

After [illegible]. I went to Charls - was detained there till near 11 - then to studio. Developed the two plates I made from "River's Source" - yesterday. Home to lunch - back at 2, Emma Ireland came in for a few minutes - then Niclatz came \+ stayed for some time - we talked of his work at the N.A. [illegible]. Stelnis class. He left with me a copy of Report [illegible] by judges judges in [illegible] of Ill. ustrating \+ etching. Then I developed a [illegible] of Salzburg exposures, the majority of them proving unsuccessful - then home - a rest of half an hour before dinner at 6.30 at 7.40 went to studio - from there to Carnegie Hall, getting there too late to hear first no on Programme. The old Church music of 16th \+ 17th Centuries did not appeal to me. I do not know enough to enjoy them but I did greatly enjoy the modern work - the three quartet songs by Johannes Brahms, with piano accompaniments \+ the three three-part songs by Ed. Elgar, with small orchestra - "The Snow" \+ "Fly Singing Bird" for female voices, \+ especially "Spanish Serenade" for four mixed voices - that was very charming to me - After concert [illegible] Emma \+ Mary in car \+ I walked home.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 16, 1904

**Weather:** Bright day - at studio at usual time  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ worked three or four hours on Borden photo-gravure plate - as has been too frequently the case recently. I wasted good time over photo prints from european negatives. Home to lunch \+ back at usual time. After four I walked down to 31st St. \+ 8th Ave. to see the excavations being made there for the Penn'a. R.R. station - under the snow \+ mud. The wreckage \+ disorder, \+ the tunneled streets, the four blocks are a dismal show. Home soon after 5 \+ met dinner at 6.30 I worked at Velox printing. After dinner wrote to Ralph - at 9 went to Charls - home at 10 - more Velox work. To bed before 12.
