## Left Page

### Wednesday JAN. 6, 1904

*Jim \+ Ralph left home.*

**Weather:** milder, but yet very cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

milder, but yet very cold — Pleasant sunshine, growing overcast \+ threatening snow later in the day — to studio soon after 10 \+ after painting for an hour or more concluded that I cannot do more at present on "Passing Showers". I forgot to write that immediately after breakfast I went to G\. Cen\. Station with Jim \+ Ralph — Jim got away for New Haven on aq o'clk. Train — the 8.50 train for Tarrytown did not get away till nearly an hour later. At 1 o'clk. I got telegram from R. — telling me of his safe arrival — I left studio soon after 12 \+ went home — Stahr came at about 1 \+ I gave him key to closet in Etching Studio at N.Y. as I shall not go up this p.m. — After lunch I gave up to a feeling of sleepiness \+ slept for more than an hour — then to studio \+ cleaned up — home soon after 5 — a telegram from Morton of Brush \+ Pencil about N.A. of D. Exhbn. Cat. cuts — I wrote Reply \+ took it to Station H — Then home to dinner — Evelf. wrote on same subject to Siis. Chare came in for awhile.

## Right Page

### Thursday JAN. 7, 1904

**Weather:** Very much milder — a haze in the air — mild sunshine \+ sloppy walking  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very much milder — a haze in the air — mild sunshine \+ sloppy walking. I got to studio at usual time — Got out 33 × 44 "the Source of the River" — washed its face \+ commenced repainting making radical changes in color, tone \+ sky forms — I am very hopeful that I may be able to pull something fairly good out of it. At 12.30 I went to bank \+ home to lunch soon after 1 — to studio soon after 2 \+ painted as long as I could see — home at 5 \+ had a nap before dinner — my days work had tired me — dinner at 6.30 — Evelf. wrote a letter to Mary.
