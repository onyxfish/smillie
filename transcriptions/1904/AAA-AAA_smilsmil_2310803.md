## Left Page

### Wed. May 11, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annual meeting - N.A. of D -

Very pleasant in spite of showery threats - \+ went to studio as usual \+ worked awhile on "Borden" - then to Am. Art Ass'n. where the Water-Color Socy. is open - my first visit - hurried thro' it - then to 2d. Nat'. Bank \+ made loan - then to elevated RR - \+ to 110th St. - \+ to N.A. of D. - a few min. after 1. - Ample time for lunch - sat with deForest, Nicoll \+ others. Meeting called to order at 2.30 \+ went through without a hitch \+ with singular unanimity - all old officers re-elected. It was after 5 when the meeting adjourned. After 6 I went out alone for a walk - went up to Columbia University to see French's Alma Mater. I am disappointed - I dislike the gilding very much - met Geo. There \+ we walked back to the N.A. - Dinner was announced soon after 7 - we sat down as we pleased - I was near the end of a table - Bolton Jones on my left - Will Low \+ Keny. on Cox on my right - Watrous opposite \+ Frank Jones. Millet \+ C.Y. Turner - we got along together very well - the dinner fairly good - the after-dinner talking was commenced by old Sellstedt in a child-like rambling talk about a book of Recollections he has just written - Keny. on Cox made, in answer to a call - a simple, dignified \+ short address - in time Dr. Chas. Miller got off his usual buffoon display - but this was followed by some serious talk about prospects of all working together to secure for Art a great building where the federated societies could have a home. Beckwith announced a plan being prepared - the Lenox Library block being the site. The matter was discussed in a manner that was gratifying to me, showing as I this - that the leaven of Butler's plan of years ago was still working - \+ that possibly its failure then but prepared the way for a greater success in the future - home at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 12, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day - brilliant \+ cool, growing warmer - up as usual, feeling little the worse for yesterday's exercises. After bkft. - to Q.C. Station \+ bought Parlor Car seats for Mary H. \+ self. to West Port for next Monday - then to studio worked awhile on Manite panel for Keene - At 12 o'clk. feeling heavy from loss of sleep last night I went home \+ slept till 1 - then lunch - Rec'd. letter from A. Winch, St. Huberts - saying that Maghee w'd. not allow him to take any one in to lodge - this is, I think, a subterfuge - it upsets my plans completely - I wrote notes to Winch - Moody - Brown, Otis \+ his daughter, \+ took them to the Sub-station H - then to studio's at 3 \+ worked on Borden drawing till 5.30 - then sauntered in the streets till about dinner time - home - dinner - Eve'g par from lively -
