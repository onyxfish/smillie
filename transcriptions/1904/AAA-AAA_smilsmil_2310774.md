## Left Page

### Mon. March 14, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

more weather misery - dull morning soon followed by snow - I got to studio a little before 10 \+ got to work Printing study of old mans head - I had expected a dissappointment when I saw print, but it was a greater one than I was prepared for. As a whole it is much overbitten - I made various changes on the plates before I went home to lunch - After lunch Mr. Trowbridge called with a number of his color printed etchings to protest agt. the action of the Etching Com. last friday evg meeting agt. admitting prints in color - After our attended conference he said he wd. send to my house the prints in frames as he wished to exhibit them at St. Louis, on friday evg next - so that the Com. may see them. It was after 3 when I got to studio \+ resumed printing - Yale came in \+ bossed the job for some time - After making a number of changes I got a [illegible] that I thought was in good condition to leave for study after a day or two of rest - It was 6 o'clk. when I got home - had a few minutes rest before dinner - Eveg. I got Ready to go to meeting of Advisory Chairmen \+ Executive Com. at Vaudaro's studio but gave it up at the last moment - Stayed at home \+ finished paste pot letter to Bessie.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 15, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

more disgusting weather - Raw - gusty with a few flying snow flakes \+ now \+ then a ghostly gleam of famished sunshine - After bkft. I went to Station H. Taking Bessie's muslin dress to be forwarded to her by parcels post - To studio soon after 10 - Got my jap. pfs. Taken yesterday damped between blatters in press - Men laid soft-ground on 9' x 12' zinc plate - Then made a slight sketch, same size, that I propose to draw on the plate - Then made a pulp-board frame upon which to stretch the india paper upon which to make the drawing — Then home to lunch - after that finished my letter to Bessie by writing a P.S - Then to studio stretched india at frame \+ commenced to make tracing of sketch - Then Vondrous came in with some new plates - \+ a little later Connolly came with some very poor prints that he had made from some of his etched plates - before they left it was after 4 \+ I had to rush to keep my appointment with W. J. Baer at 90 Grove St. I got there at about 4:35 \+ looked over a lot of prints in all conditions - states \+ stages from plates by Blum - There were fewer than I had expected - very few in good condition, but Baer has most of the plates \+ says that he can have prints Taken from them if he wishes - I did not find what I had hoped to for St. Louis - I left at 5.30 \+ walked most of the way home getting in at 6.10 - dinner - Eveg. Reading newspaper \+ writing.
