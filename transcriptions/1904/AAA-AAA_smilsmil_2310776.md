## Left Page

### Fri. March 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

About two inches of light snow in the m'f. - then rain - then, in the afternoon, clearing. After bkfs'. I wrote part of letter to Raefen - got to studio after 10 - all the m'f. looking over stacks of my pfs. and making selections to show to the com. to-night. Mr. Vaughan Trowbridge called - He had been to the house \+ left a big pkg of framed pfs. colored etchings - Yondrons also called with pfs - Home to lunch - back \+ worked till 4.30 - then Home \+ Rested an hour - wrote letter to Jim - dinner at 6.30 Eve'g at 8 Charl came \+ took J. \+ M. to his house to show them some new Eastern china. The com. on Etchings for St. Louis Expo'. began to come at 8.45 \+ it was considerably after 9 before the last one came - Nicoll, Chapman - Schilling, Watrous, Hurty - Muелatz is in Florida - The Trowbridge colored etchings were seen \+ declined because of color - Prints by N.A. \+ S. students were considered - Then I showed a number of mine \+ was pleasantly complimented - adjourned at 11.30 to meet at Budworth's next Monday p.m.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day - to studio soon after 10 \+ spent the m'f. making a list of pfs. that I have from my own etchings \+ making selections for St. Louis Expo'. - 1 o'clk. when I got home to lunch - after that to N.A. of D - Taking students pfs. submitted to the com. last eve'g. I got home before 6 o'clk \+ had a brief rest before dinner - Eve'g at 8 I went to Mr. Barkyd's at her request, to see some prints she has - She is imposed upon by dealers in France \+ Germany \+ has brought home a number of things of little or no value - It was placed at my disposal for the class \+ I selected to fairly good prints - a Ribera \+ a Van Dyck - Got to Century about 10 - met Nicoll \+ a lot of other fellows - Talked etchings for St. Louis with little effect. Met Winslow Homer \+ Tho. Eakin - Homer is looking very old \+ bald \+ Eakin is very fat - They were picturesque couple as they sat together. I had an interesting (to me) Talk with Henry Oyton Taylor about desirable books to get for him to read in line with his (T's) Ancient Ideals something in the way of Comparative History of Religions - as I hope to induce my boys to think seriously, reverently, on such subjects - Home before midnight.
