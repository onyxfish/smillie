## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 16, 1904

The Windsor \- E\-Town. Room 15

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day \- cloudless, quiet & warm. Bkf: at about 8 & to work immediately afterwards completed the dismounting, put away the last of curtains & things & emptied out the batteries. Satie Otis came & washed up things in the kitchen \- Charlie came & helped Pond's boy get a barrel of water in the tank then fed Reeds came & I gave him instructions about manures, where & how to spread it. Then Winch came The & Moody cleaned out the traps & filled them with kerosene \- & put up shutters on windows \- At 12.30 we had a very good dinner \- & at 2.15 we started for E\-town, stopping by the way to pay bills to Harry Reeds, Geo. Kelley \- Sanders & Olds \- We had an exceedingly comfortable ride \- Wm Reeds drove \- & got to the Windsor at 5 o'clk we were welcomed by the Kelloggs & W. & Mrs Tracy \- We had supper soon after 6 \- In The evng. Mek's (most of them) & the Ts went to Church & I was so sleepy that I wanted to go to bed at 8.30 but when the party rel\- from church I got to "gabbin'" & it was 10.30 before I went to bed \- clear moonlight & a very cold night.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 17, 1904

At home \- 110 E. 38th S.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day again \- almost cloudless \- brilliant sunshine \- invigorating air \- Read in my bed for some time, then up & went out for a short walk before bkf\. \- Bkf\- soon after 8 \- then lounged about till after 10 & at 10.45 we left for West post \- after a very comfortable ride we got there at 11.40 & the train did not come along till about 12.30, being more than ten minutes late \- Soon after 1 we had a lunch in what is called a Café car \- a poor substitute for a Regular Dining car \- we had a wait at Albany of nearly half an hour where the train from the West came along to which our car was then attached \- I had spent most of the time walking of on station platform \- we left A\- at 5 & went thro' to N.Y. without a stop \- There was a lovely sunset which gave us much joy, as did the Catskills, which were passed in the twilight \- We got into N.Y. at 8, in time. Jennings at Station, carried our luggage home \- found there Geo. Smillie & Geo. Cook & Emily \- also Sadie, a serving woman, two Alice Ireland \- She had a good meal ready for us \- While we were at it Will Parks came in & he stayed till 10.30 then to bed \-
