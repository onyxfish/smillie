## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 11, 1904

**Weather:** Bright + cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

After 10 when I got to Studio — wrote a note to Clarence Smith about "Tax on $20000 Personal" threatened by Tax Com'r. To work again on Vesuvius — started on sky — Trying to repaint that but made poor work of it — I'm out of sorts in some way — I do not exactly know in what way — but can't do any thing rightly. Home to lunch — After that I got a couple of things ready for the Illustrators show — a pf. of my etched portrait of L.P. Morton — + one of my Florence pen sketches. Painted on Vesuvius till after 4 — Then took my exhibits to the Ill. show. Cor. 34th S. & 5th Ave. Then into see the F.H. Smith's drawings — Then to Knoedls. To see some Parsifal Color Symphonies — Then to Durand-Ruels to see some Jongers portraits — Then in to see a great raft of canvases by the late [illegible] auction sales ones — Then to Studio + cleaned up + home at 5.45 dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g feeling very much used up — to bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 12, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day — another of those head aches of which I have had three within the past eleven days — too much for comfort. I did not get up till 8.30 P.M. Then I managed to write two short notes to the boys + just before 10 went out and mailed them — to bed again before midnight.
