## Left Page

### Sun. March 6, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

So wet \+ raw that I did not go out of the house all day \- After my usual scrub I had bkfi. at 9.30. The rest of the m'f. was divided between newspaper, The Heart of Rome \+ walking, like a bear in a cage, in the 2°. story front room \- Dinner at 2 \- afternoon finished Heart of Rome \+ had a little nap \- Tea \- Eve'f I did some pasting on Bessie's letter \-

## Right Page

### Mon. March 7, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pouring rain \+ high wind \- I got up with a headache \+ did not get rid of it all day \- I got to studio about 10 \+ at a little before 11 I made up my mind that it was time for me to face the Tax Commissioner's demand for Personal property tax at $20000 \- I went to the office Bsway \+ Chambers St. \- a long line ahead of me \- but their was great celerity \+ I got through without difficulty in 20 min \- Then back to studio at 12 \- Jas. S. King, Portrait Painter \+ Etcher, called to see me about sending some of his work to St. Louis Sep\? Home to lunch \+ back at 2.30 \- I made a gelatine tracing from the enlarged study of old man's head \- Maurice Steme called to bid me good bye \- He has made his arrangements to sail for Europe at the end of the week taking his mother with him \- She will stay with another son over there while Maurice follows his studies under the conditions of his Scholarship \- I got home a little before 6 \- was not able to eat any dinner \- Chad came in at 9 \+ stayed for an hour after I had gone to my bed room \-
