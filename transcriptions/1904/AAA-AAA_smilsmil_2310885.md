## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 24, 1904

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - went to studio to begin work there - spent some time with George - Home to lunch at 1 - Back to studio at 2, found myself in one of those discouraging conditions, now so frequent, when, with no specific complaint - no headache or other defined pain or discomfort - I am unfit for work - the energy that makes the machine go is lacking - with all before me that I wish to do before the end comes, this is disheartening - I cannot forget what there is to do - yet, I am not able to do it. Poor father, I can remember his trials, years ago, along lines much the same - After podging about till about 4 o'clk. I went to Docke's about Studio curtains - then to Butlers \+ looked at frame patterns for 33×44 - Source of the River - then to Keppel's - there with Kapple \+ Jo. Fennell till 6 - then home - Dinner at 6.30 - new Cook \+ waitress came to-day - Every Charl came in for a short time - my time went stupidly \+ for nothing.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 25, 1904

**Weather:** Very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - It was after 10 when I got to studio - spent most of the m't getting my engraving table cleared for work \+ covering anew the blued - hanging curtains etc. - Home to lunch at 1 \+ back at 2.30 - General work till 4.30 - then to Docke's with old curtains (thin) from big window - to have new ones made - Then to St. Cook's 154 E. 37. S'. \+ met Geo. \+ Walter \+ Clarence Smith to consider the Problem of assessment of Taxes on W'sburg dock property - discussed till after 6 \+ agreed that legal contest shall be continued - then home to dinner - Felt especially stupid - slept in my chair most of the time till 10 o'clk.
