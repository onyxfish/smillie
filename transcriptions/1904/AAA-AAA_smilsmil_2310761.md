## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 17, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Exceedingly cold day. clear, with a biting west wind - awoke at 5 a.m. with severe headache which disabled me for the rest of the day altho' I was able to keep up \+ about - It was 10.30 when I got to studio - I podged about. Trimmed enlarged photo. portrait of self \+ mounted it \+ did other little things about the studio. Home at lunch time - had a little nap \+ back to studio soon after 3 - Retouching photos \+ such like work. planning ahead. Home at 5.30 — ate a very light dinner at 6.30 \+ loafed thro' the evef. going to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came home.

Still very cold \+ bright - got up feeling fairly well - \+ got to studio soon after 10 \+ foainted on the r.h. foreground of Vesuvius, but without enthusiasm. Mt. Gel. E. Senseney of 45 Wash'n Sq. So. called \+ introduced himself, enquiring if I knew any one who laid mezzotint grounds. I gave him such information as I could. Before noon I began to feel uncomfortable generally \+ especially about the head. When I got home to lunch I could eat little \+ after it went to my bed where I lay till 4.30 - about that time Jim came in from New Haven, starting his Washington's Birthday holiday early so that he may go to Hackley to-morrow to the Dramatic Entertainment. I went to studio - cleaned up \+ then home again - could eat but little dinner. Was not able to go with Chas. to Boston Symphony Concert as arranged - Jim went in my place - I spent the evef. nodding by parlor fire.
