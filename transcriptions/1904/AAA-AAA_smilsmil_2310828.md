## Left Page

### Thur. June 30, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy showers about 6 a.m. continuing till middle of forenoon - then cleared muggy - Thunderstorm about 7 p.m. - breezy & cooler in the eve'g, but very humid - Up early & before bkfs' wrote part of letter to Mrs. Kent. Bkfs' - at 8 - then met a succession of unexpected demands so that I did little in accordance with programme - Kramer came with photo. proofs of Borden interior - after a long talk I sent him with note to Mr. H.I. Smith. Vondrous came - I went to studio - to grocers & to butchers. Then Schuff's man came with valises - took them back - Ralph & I went to Schuff's & I got a large case for him & ordered a 26 bellows top valise for self - then I went to Alexanders to enquire about my lost shoes - then to Scribners & saw Mr. Smith - then to 2d. Nat'l. Bank & wound up my business there - then home to lunch at 1.30 - a rest after that - finished letter to Mrs. Kent & wrote to Miss Olivia Stokes - & packed the half of my valise - Dinner at 6.30 - worked at all sorts of things till about 9 - then Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 1, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fearfully close, muggy, overcast morning - Up at 6 & did some general work before bkfs' - at 8 - at a little after 9 I went to Gales - he had the analysis of urine & said there was nothing revealed that need give me any uneasiness. - Then home - Schuff brought Ralph's coat case & my valise - then to butchers & grocers - gave final orders & settled bills - home again, packed valise & began on steamer trunk but took a rest before lunch. - Alice Ireland came in & bid us good bye - I had great tribulation when it came to the end of the packing - I had not, room enough & realized that I had made a serious mistake in buying my $18 valise that fails to answer my purpose in several ways - a young man from Alexanders came saying he was tracing my missing shoes & promising very confidently that before eve'g he w'd bring my shoes & w'd refund the money - I got a note - about 5.30 I gave up very tired & much disgusted - I lay down on my bed & got quiet & a little nap - Dinner before 7 - every Charl came in early - I told him of my dilemma in packing - a valise too heavy to handle & yet too small to hold all that I wished to carry - & far too handsome to be subjected to the rigors of Travel - He at once said that he had an old Steamer Trunk that had been back & forth several times - I was welcome to that - Ralph & I went with him to his house on P. & I carried the trunk back to 110 & I immediately transferred contents of valise & many other things besides, to it greatly to my satisfaction - it was an immense Relief to my mind - Charl bossed the job - also the Roll-up of the Rugs & overcoats - it was 11 3⁄4 when I got to bed & after midnight before I got to sleep - but till 5 to-morrow m'g I had an unbroken nights rest.
