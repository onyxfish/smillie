## Left Page

### Thur. July 14, 1904

Hotel" Vier Jahreszeiten. Hamburg.
Rooms 49-50

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very mild, pleasant day, clouding over late in the afternoon with two or three light showers - Bkft. at 8 - after that completed our packing + got every thing in Readiness for landing - it was not till afterq that we could see the low shores as the air was hazy - at 10 tf we got up to our dock but it was 10.30 before we could go ashore - a short walk took us to the Custom Ho. - asked + we were there an hour before our luggage was inspected - my trunk was badly rummaged by a surly inspector - the other trunk was but a formal insp'n. We waited in the Train Room till 12.? + had to fight for three cups of coffee + three Rolls - When we were let out we got three good seats + had a compartment to ourselves - the country from Cuyhaven to H- is almost a dead level + uninteresting - we passed no place of importance till we came to Harburg - There the engine changed ends + we ran north a few miles + entered Hamburg - at about 3 - there was a great crowd + confusion at the station but we managed to disentangle ourselves + got to our hotel between 3.30 + 4 o'clk. We were given two pleasant Rooms on the 4th fl. - Nine has a loggia overlooking the Binnen Alster - a wide + very interesting view - as I had but but 5 pf. left after paying the Cabman - I borrowed 20 m. + the porter + we went out + bought a Baedeker - A light Rain then fell - + I went in - we had our dinner before 6 + after that wandered about another light shower - the boys, nothing daunted kept on + I Rested - they came in before 10 + we all got to our beds early.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 15, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day, altho' a little too warm for comfort during the middle of it - my first night ashore was not altogether a comfortable one - The Germans do not know how to make a good bed - was awake for some time after 4 put at last got to sleep + did not wake till 7.30 - bkft. late - after it went to Cook's + drew 500 m. + bought tickets for Berlin - tomorrow - 12.32 - Then to hotel + paid porter the 20 m. - I borrowed of him - then to landing at head of Binnen Alster + took the Winterhude boat + went the length of the Aussen Alster - saw many charming homes on the banks of these waters, little to be commended in architecture but with pine trees, plants + lawns kept with wonderful neatness + care - I never saw anything finer - for a time in the early m'g the atmosphere was so hazy that I could not get a proper light for a photo. from my loggia - but I think I got some good ones on this boat trip - we rode by the boat we went to our starting place - then to the Rathaus + went through the main part of it - imposing + rather showy but in my judgment a show of money expenditure rather than of good taste - after a little shopping we got back to our hotel after 1 o'clk. + then got our mid-day meal - at 3 we got good seats in one of Käse's Bound Trip wagons - four horses, going around the Alster basin + then transferred to a launch twice + made the Round of the harbor + back to St. Pauli landing bricke - + from there by Tram Car back to the Rathaus - + walked to our hotel. The Ride was exceedingly pleasant - the Residence streets we went thro' were delightfully shaded by fine trees - The houses were all detached, the grounds about ample with extraordinary fine vegetation beautiful flowers - vines + lawns - it was quite a paradise of homes - architecture generally bad - many brick paths at one side of paved street - asphalt greatly inferior to ours - The trip thro' harbor immensely interesting - I am sure that none of our sea ports can make such a display of vessels of war + commerce - the Rig was small - we were in the bow - the water in places was rough + on shipping a sea the boys got a ducking - we got to hotel at about 6 - dinner at 7 - then a lounge in our loggia enjoying beautiful twilight effect over Binnen Alster till after 9 - Then to Rathaus - Alster - nothing doing - back to Alster Terrace - + took boat to Fährthaus - a great beer garden - crowded with people - a sight to be seen - a garden at the waters edge - a great number of Tables - thousands of electric lights - large buildings for [illegible] in inclement weather - band stand + large military band - overture to Tannhäuser - etc - all Tables occupied - every body eating + drinking - hundreds promenading about - the passage - ways - alively scene - we left at near 11 + got to bed at 11.30
