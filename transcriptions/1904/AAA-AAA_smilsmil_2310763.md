## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 21, 1904

**Weather:** Bright m[illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m[illegible] -, overcast + saw afternoon with rain in the evef. I had much heat + irritation in throat + chest about 3 o 4 o'clk. a.m. with slight rise of temp was so uncomfortable at 9 a.m. that I telephoned to Dr. Yale + he came before 11 - He advised me to keep my bed + prescribed Heroin - of the Morphia family. I had a dreary day in bed - Neither of the boys went to a Ritualistic ch. + heard some music that interested them - Chard came in + made a short visit - Jim ret.- to New Haven in 8 p.m. train - Ralph read aloud to me in the evef.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 22, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. above 45° on north side of house - after a night of rain the day seemed muggy + damp but before night the weather had changed to drier + brighter. At 5.30 A.M. my throat + chest gave me much trouble + my temp. was almost 100°. At 9 o'clk. I was so uncomfortable that I asked Ralph to telephone for Dr. Yale who came at about 11 - he thinks that my illness is a form of influenza + that patience will prove to be the best treatment - He stayed with me in very social mood till 12.30 - I remained in bed till 3.30 - then, the weather being brighter + I feeling better, I got up, dressed + went to the parlor - Ralph left to take the 4.58 train to Tarrytown. I sat at table at dinner at 6.30 + ate very sparingly - Dr. called in the m[illegible] + again in the afternoon. I loafed the evef away in big chair before the fire.
