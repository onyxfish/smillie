## Left Page

### Mon. April 11, 1904

**Weather:** more sunshine & shade following a damp early m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After my chores I went to Borden's & was there till 11.30 then walked home stopping at the Cemetery to see some modern jobs. Water-Color paintings on silk — I could not appreciate them. Home some afternoon. Sue Whiting came in & took lunch with us. I fussed a little with some photo-printing — old Montrose negatives & worked on Borden outline till nearly 5 — then to Birdgeman's — E. 19c S. & ordered garden seeds for Keene. Then walked up to studio — found a note from Mr. Thurber saying she was done & gone but did not find my studio keys anywhere. Then home to dinner. Eve'g — wrote to Chas. Mooley telling him I had sent the garden seeds on to him.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 12, 1904

**Weather:** Rain last night & early this m'f — cloudy & windy till afternoon — then bright & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up with a headache that unfitted me for work all the m'f. Went to studio. To grocer's & butchers — then to Knoedler's — saw the Blum collection there — much interested. Then to bank — then to Buskird's & got paper. Then to Simingein's price-cream & home after 12 lunch at 1 — at 2 to Studio — Knoedler's men on time. Miss Judge's portrait was passed out of the window & by 2.30 I was home. Put in about three hours on Borden outline — then \+ went with Ralph to Station — he took 4.58 train to Tarrytown — then home & had a little rest before dinner. Phabe Whiting dined with us \+ Jim acted as her escort to a theatre. Eve'g I wrote to Mr. Thurber — to Kelly & to Otis of Keene Heights.
