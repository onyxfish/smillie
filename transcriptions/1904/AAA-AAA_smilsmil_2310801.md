## Left Page

### Sat. May 7, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Anniversary of my marriage.

A very fine day - got a good start in the mt, \+ put in a good days work on Borden drawing - To lunch at home \+ back as usual worked till near 5 - had a little stroll then home \+ had a rest - Jim brought with him, to dinner, a young college chum — good of Yonkers, a son of Delia Black an acquaintance of many years ago - He was given a very nice dinner - left Table at 8 \+ then the boys went to a theatre - Chas. Collins came for me \+ together we went to the Century monthly meeting - I did not enjoy myself - my painting "The Source" distressed me - so, soon after 10 I started for home -

## Right Page

### Sun. May 8, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim dep. to New-Haven.

Another very fine day - up at 7 until 9 at work scrubbing \+ shampooing - then beds. Very soon after that I went to the N.A. \+ D. Schools - meeting of Council and Instructors - To decide awards for school work - I did not wait for them to finish but as soon as they got thro' with Etching Class - just before noon - I left \+ walked down Riverside Drive to 86th St. thence through to Broadway \+ took car home - in at 1.30 - dinner at 2 — after that I had a rest till 4- Fred Ireland came at 4.30 \+ for about an hour we discussed with Jim his schedule for Senior year - making two changes - dropping Study of Christianity \+ taking up the broader one of History of Religion - dropping Music \+ adding a French course - I had a little walk - at 7.40 I went with Jim to Station \+ he took the 8 o'clk train to N.H. - Later I fooled with newspaper \+ a little writing.
