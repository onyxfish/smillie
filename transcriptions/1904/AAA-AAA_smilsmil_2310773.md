## Left Page

### Sat. March 12, 1904

**Weather:** Bright, windy & rather cold  **Temp:**

Bright, windy & rather cold. Wrote some notes after Bkft. & got to studio a little later than usual. Worked on head of old man with graver & dry-point. Home to lunch. Then to N.A. of D. Found that the class had drawn from model in the m/f & but two or three were present. So I got away as soon as possible. Home at 4.30. I felt tired & out of sorts. So I lay down on my bed & had a nap before dinner — after dinner I felt yet more uncomfortable — a dull, rheumatic sort of headache unfitting me for anything. I had intended to go to Century. I tried to read & at last gave up in disgust & went to bed again.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 13, 1904

**Weather:** A very bright & pleasant day  **Temp:**

A very bright & pleasant day. My night's rest ended at about 3 a.m. altho' I got some light naps between that time & 5. At 6.30 I got up & had any scrub in water almost cold — but I was, probably, none the worse for that. Bkft. at 9 — a little time over newspaper. Then to studio. There till 12.30 looking over p.p. of my work & laying out some of them to show to Com. at its meeting next Friday eve'g. Then for about an hour or half I strolled about the streets looking at new buildings & any thing else that I could find interesting. Home to dinner at 2 — wrote somewhat — went to studio — closed windows & wet paper for printing to-morrow. Then a short walk on Park Ave. Home at 6 — tea soon after — Then some reading — then went to Yale's with his Etching p.p. for St. doris. Home at 9.15 — more writing & then to bed.
