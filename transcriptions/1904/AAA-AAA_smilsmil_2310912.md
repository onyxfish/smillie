## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 17, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day - snow falling afternoon + evell - more than 4". To studio as usual - Practically the whole day was given over to photography + a good part of the night. After bkf. I wrote reply to F. Vivanti 995 Madison Ave. who enquires, for a friend, if the Art at St. Hubert's is for sale. I said that I was not seeking a purchaser - The place was not in market, but if I was offered not less than $10000 I would consider it. I wrote to fin the letter I should have written last night - Home to lunch - Afternoon I developed one of my Zurich spools + had bad luck - There was a bad wrinkle in the film that caused about half of the film to tear away from its backing + roll up so tightly upon itself that the developer could not get to the emulsion - I cut off the half that had develop'd + then made up more developer - tore the roll open - it stuck like shoemaker's way - I developed that - I got something but, of course, all the pictures were more or less marr'd - But for the accident some of them w'd have been beauties. Home to dinner at 7 o'clk - Even I worked at Velox printing till late + got to bed at 12.30

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day overhead - bright + mild - consequently, sloppy underfoot. After my regular Sunday m'g scrub + change had bkf. at 9 - at about 11 went to studio - there for an hour - then I walked on the sunny side of Park Ave. till after 1 - then to Charl's - He is still complaining - He had a short walk on Friday - the first time he has been out for a fortnight or more. Dr. Cilley has been treating him. - Home to dinner at 2 - Soon after 3 o'clk. I lay down + slept soundly till 5.30 — Then some writing. Then dinner - no, supper, but I did not take any - At 8 o'clk I went to D'Yale's to get some hints as to paper (Velox) print developing - I was there till after 10 - we developed some prints from one or two of my negatives, but I learned nothing new - Mrs. Yale came in for awhile.
