### Fri. Aug. 5, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Prebischtor Excursion —

A fearfully hot day - had a good nights rest last night - I fancy that my trouble in sleeping has been caused by drinking beer - I shall experiment - The boys were up at 6 \+ went before 7, with Katharine to Schandau a place some miles further up the Elbe than Rathen, the place we went to the other day, \+ from there they walked to the Grosse Winterberg, a height some miles away - the arrangement was that Mary \+ I were to take a later train go on to Sciona - some miles beyond Schandau there cross the River \+ take carriage - met them at Winterberg - have dinner \+ return by the Prebischtor to Sciona \+ then back by RR to Dresden. Mary \+ I got to Sciona at 11 - crossed to Herrn-Kretischen \+ there arranged with proprietor of hotel who spoke English to take us as far as we could ride on the way to top of Prebischtor - she had forgotten the name of the place where Katharine had promised for us to meet \+ have dinner together - she could think only of a place that the hotel man told us was 6 hours away - as we rode she thought of Winterberg \+ tried to make the driver understand - he could not speak a word of English - Mary could speak no German \+ we were utterly stranded - we got to the foot walk up the Prebischtor \+ there fortune favored us - we met Mr. Louis Hoffmann \+ sister of Cleveland, Ohio, who came up \+ speaking German pleasantly explained that there was only a foot path to Winterberg - so we discharged each man \+ with M'r \& Miss H. climbed to the top of Prebischtor. The heat \+ climb nearly using many up - at the top we got a very poor dinner on a hot balcony with no outlook \+ after the dinner finished the ascent, many remaining at the Restaurant - the rock formation is the same as at Medastel - not picturesque but singular - a natural bridge of considerable size - the view from top wide over hills covered with cultivated stones - not so interesting as view from Bastel - the River not seen. While on top the boys came up - just from Winterberg, Katharine with her mother - we soon went down \+ joined M \& K - after a bit walked down to where the wag[on] was waited \+ a picturesque walk - met old women, bare legged, toiling up carrying supplies to Restaurant - work should be done by donkeys - not a wagon \+ rode back to Herrnkretischen - had some drink - then ferry to other side of River \+ took 5 o'clk train to Dresden - great crowd - infleing heat \+ other discomforts - Dresden about 6.15 - to hotel - washed up \+ had our evening meal - on lounge reading till 9 - then to bed \+ had a good nights rest.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 6, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot \+ thunderous - clouds banked up in a very threatening way in the afternoon but nothing came of it - We had a late bl[ea]k[fast]. After that went to Mary's - Katharine went with us from there to the shop where my sketching table was mended \+ got it - Then Ralph \+ I went to the Brühl Terrace \+ for an hour or more I worked making a few sketches of the dome of the Frauen Kirche as seen over the picturesque gables of old houses - looking up thro' a narrow, curving street - after 12 I ret'd to Mary's - met the boys \+ with them went to the Belvedere Terrace \+ had dinner - then the boys went back, met Katharine \+ went on an excursion to an old castle Weesenstein.
I returned to my sketching place \+ worked till 5 o'clk. Then to hotel with my traps - then to Kohns to spend a good part of an hour with him - he is to ticket me thro' all my trips to [illegible] which will save me much annoyance in many ways - About 7 the boys ret'd \+ we had our early [illegible] meal at the hotel - later we went to Mary's - to hotel soon after 10.
