## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 22, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — most of the m'g was spent with Jim in efforts to explain to him the financial situation so far as it concerns him. He now, for the first, enters practically upon the management of his own affairs. Before he came of age he had an allowance of $35 per month from which he paid for his clothes & pleasures — all other bills I paid. After June 8\. when it on his $20000 legacy began & while we were abroad I made quite cash advances for pocket money — all other expenses I met — being a gift to the two boys — Now I have paid over to Jim accrued interest & he has a considerable sum of money in his hands — more than he has ever had at one time before. I am anxious that he shall be careful in the management of it. Went to grocer & butcher. Lunch at 1 — Jim hurried away & went to West Point to see foot Ball game between Cadets & [illegible] — he got home at about 7 & spent the even at Alice Ireland's — I did some writing & shopping in the afternoon — Dinner before 7. Evey, all — went to Century — where I saw Joe, Pennell & Mr. Vandyke & had an interesting talk with them about projected History of Etching & Eng. in Am. That I am is to write. It was after 11 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 23, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim rec'd to New-Haven.

Fine day — Bkf't about 9 — until 1 o'clk. was taken up with newspapers & sorting old letters from Jim — then I went out & walked till 2 — went thro' considerable portion of the new Hotel Astor — then dinner — Jim dined with his friend Harry Gourd & in the afternoon went to Brooklyn to call on Miss Stoughton. a girl we met on the Patricia — He rec'd to M. on evg train — I wrote to Ralph. Char came in late in the afternoon & I showed him the Bromide prints that I brought with me from Europe — At 6 I went to Emma Ireland's — & took tea — Catharine, Emma, Mary & Grace there — after tea, with Cath. & Emma, the summer travel was discussed till 10 o'clk — then home.
