## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 25, 1904

**Weather:** Very variable, clouded cold & raw in the m'g  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim Ret'd to New-Haven.
Bundy " " Hackley.

\- a slight snow flurry - some sunshine & improved weather later. - Late b'k'p'. - it was after 10 when I got to studio. Until 12 o'clk I was engaged with dark-room work - camera etc — Mr. Cashman of Phelps & lawyers called to arrange to have a trunk belonging to Maynard's sisters removed from his studio. At 12.30 I went to 2d Nat'l. Bank. there home to lunch. Harvey Bundy left for Hackley immediately after. I Ret'd to studio soon after 2 & wrote letter to Kate - then again at work in dark room - man came & changed faucets put in the other day. it was about 6 when I got home - dinner at 6.30 - Jim left for New-Haven at 8 - Ralph & I went to Studio where he developed a spool of his photo films exposed about Dresden. Chark came to house with letter from Katharine Troops telling that sailing date of Patricia had been changed from Dec 3d. to Dec. 10th — After 11 when we got home - to bed at 12.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 26, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not a pleasant day because of cold, wind & cloudiness. - After b'k'p'. I went to Dorman's & got some Photo-supplies & then to studio where I spent the m'g making solutions & getting everything ready for developing some of the many spools of films that I have exposed during the past summer. Home to lunch. back at 2. & developed one spool with moderate success - then Ralph came & developed a spool - & after him I took a turn again. It was 6.30 before I got to a stopping place. then I hurried home to dinner. - Eve'g Chark came in to see if I was not going to the Century to hear Cap't. A.E. Piorkowski speak on the Military aspects of the Russo-Japanese War. we went, taking Ralph - & we had a very stupid time - Cap't. P. talked for one hour & a half in a rambling way without telling us anything that we had not already seen in the newspapers. Home before 11 - very tired.
When we went to Century we left Lizzie Smillie visiting the Hinshelwoods.
