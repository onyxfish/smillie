## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 28, 1904

**Weather:** fine day \- milder & hazy  **Temp:** [illegible]

after 10 o'clk when I got to studio \- worked for an hour or more on Borden Photo-gravure, experimenting. Then to 2d. Nat'.. & paid $3000 on acct. of loans made during the past three or four years to meet deficits \- expenses exceeding income that amt. Home to lunch \- for an hour or more after that I was printing from some of the films that I got from Bormann in the m'f. Soon after 3 o'clk \- I Rode to 110th St. \- my first experience on subway \- I think I should prefer the elevated R.R. except when the Pulp. Trains will serve my purpose \- at 110's. I went to N.A. & D. & Saw Mr. Jius for a few moments \- Then around by Womans Hospital & the new wing of St. Luke's Hospital to Columbia University \- Reception \- a sort of celebration of 150th Anniversary of founding of King's College. Had a gracious hand shake from Mrs. Butler at entrance to Library after which I made a round of the buildings \- all of which were open to visitors \- I did not have time to make even a hurried visit as tho' I rushed it quite two hours \- I went thro' the Library, which I had already gone thro' on previous occasions \- Fayerweather, Schermerhorn. Havemeyer. Engineering. University. Law. Stores & Barnard College. I was greatly impressed by what is \- by what is in progress \- as a centre for learning it refreshed & filled me with reverent admiration \- So I went thro' the various Laboratories. I could not tell which interested me most \- I wished to be a young man, with health, strength & opportunity to get all that I could out of it \& was happy to think that all of that was before Ralph. Barnard College, with its cosy, pleasant rooms \- an air of femininity hanging over all \- elegance, tact even, whose, made me wonder as to what the effects upon home life would be after a return from four years of such surroundings \+ few girls who find a home already in such government \- was the great be happy fitted for the prosaic life \- perhaps a Fair & Trial, before her? Home at 6.30. I suffered very much from Hyper-acidity of stomach \- could eat very little dinner & was useless till after 9 P.m. \- Then wrote a letter to Jim \- mailed it & to bed at 12.30

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 29, 1904

**Weather:** A very pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- it was nearly noon when I got to studio \- Practically the whole of the day was wasted in printing from films that Bormann developed for me \- If they had been good photos. the use of the time w'd not have been so wasteful \- I am much disappointed at the outcome of these two spools \- there is but one really satisfactory film \- The others have been misjudged as to their exposure \- or worse yet, for some reason that I do not know. I came home to lunch & was surprised to find Ralph \- he is down on adv't. business for the Hackley paper \- he was off all the afternoon. I did not go to studio till near 4 o'clk \- & home again at about 5 \- Ralph came in soon after & I went to J.C. Stratton with him \- he set \- to Hackley on 5.40 \- Dinner before 7 \- Lucy went to Charley & stayed with him till 10.30 \- He showed me the films that he exposed during his trips abroad \- developed by Cullen \- all successful \- some Remarkably so. It makes me more disgusted than ever with the Results of my work in that way, so far as developed
