## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 16, 1904

**Weather:** Overcast m\+f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

my 71st Birthday.

Overcast m\+f. Rain \+ sleet began to fall at about 2 p.m. but ceased before night Bkfs. at usual time \+ got thro' my chores \+ to studio about 10. Worked all the m\+f on l.h. lower corner of "Source" getting it into better shape than when it was left last ev\+. I shall now lay it aside to dry \+ get it out of my mind for a while \- Home to lunch \+ before 2 o'clk started for N.A\+D. met my class then took up the 21 etching &c that W. Avery gave me for the class \+ showed them to the class with critical discourse thereon Home at 5.30 \- dinner at 6.30 \- Ev\+f loafing before the fire with my newspaper \- \+ so passed my 71st birthday.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 17, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very cold day \- got up with headache that made me very uncomfortable all day. my bath etc. kept me till 10 o'clk \- then Bkfs. after that I looked over newspaper \+ wrote draft of protest to Rev. T.C.W \- then to studio then to Charles \- then home to dinner at 2 o'clk In afternoon I sought relief from headache in sleep \- it was nearly 6 when I woke I went at once to Emma D's \+ read to her the letter as I proposed to send it to T.C.W. I suggested nist that he suspend sentence on Ralph \- she advised to strike that out \+ confine myself to protest alone \- the reasons she gave convinced me that she was right. I walked up home at 9.30.
