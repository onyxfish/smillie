## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 7, 1904

**Weather:** Dull threatening  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull threatening m't - clearing before noon as yesterday did - To studio soon after 10 \+ finished pen sketch of Frauen Kirche, Dresden - It is a clean, workman-like piece of work very inferior to the rough sketch that I made on the spot last Aug. It is without spontaneity \+ Real artistic feeling. Home to lunch at 1 - after that went to Bank - then to studio - mounted original Frauen K - sketch \+ worked a little on that. Home at 5.30 — Dinner at 6.30. Ever with newspapers - Reading political speeches - one by Σ. M. Shepard interested me as being above the average in tone \+ argument - but it did not convince me - his ideal Democracy is not the real. Tammany truig -

Rec'd - a letter from Mary this m't. Took it to Charl after b'kf't. \+ Read it to him.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 8, 1904

**Weather:** Superb day - brilliant \+ clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came home to vote.

Superb day - brilliant \+ clear - To studio about 10 \+ worked till after 12 on the three pen sketches - two Frauen Kirche \+ one old church building near Lugano. Then I got my camera \+ took a half dozen snaps shots \+ the new Times bldg \+ other places about for experimentation in film developing - Home to lunch at 1 - Jim was expected at 2 - at 2.30 he had not arr. Then I went to station - Train had come in on time - I waited for two Boston trains 3.10 \+ 3.15 but did not see him - so I went to Pole \+ voted alone, greatly disappointed that I did not have my boy with me - again went to station to await arrival of 4.20 Boston train - disappointed again - then went home wondering what would be the explanation of this non-appearance - At the door he met me - just back from the pole where he had cast his vote - He had come on the Boston 3.15 - getting in 10 m- late - he was not on time, as written on the black board - I had mistaken an Albany train that came in 3.15 - so Jim came 5 m - after I left the station. I had a rest before dinner - Jim brought in his friend Bordeaux - later they went to a theatre - after dinner I went to Charl's - his friend, Will. Delaney, was there - Stayed till after 9 - then went to 42d St \+ along to the Times Bld't at B'dway - The crowd was immense - everybody seemed in good humor - there was no excitement but a great deal of senseless noise - horns, bells \+ all sorts of racket - The election returns were all overwhelmingly one way but seemed to take little attention \+ caused no particular demonstrations - I walked down B'way to 34th \+ so - then to Mad. Sq - The vast throng of people \+ women crowding the street from curb to curb - The brilliant display of electric lights of all colors \+ the incessant din combined to make a memorable experience - It was an outpouring, an irruption of humanity, moved by no definite purpose - all were good natured in their crowding \+ pushing \+ silly in their noise making - It was not a merry-making or rejoicing - just a surging tide, back \+ forth of floating men \+ women - all \+ young - tired of it at 11 o'clk - Home \+ to bed at 12.
