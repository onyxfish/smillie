## Left Page

### Sun. July 10, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cool, breezy mtf., partially overcast — I had quite a tramp on after deck after my bkfs. \+ before service in the dining saloon at 9.30. This was conducted by Archbishop Mr. J. Keane of some western diocese. It was very interesting to me — a forcible address. The text "Be ye perfect as your Father in Heaven is also perfect". He was in Canonical robes but there was nothing said or done (aside from the robes) of a dogmatic or denominational character — It was a strong appeal to all to strive for elevation — intellect, conscience \+ free will are the traits that characterise man — The intellect tells us what it is best to do — conscience tees us what is right \+ free will reserves to us the power of selection — Rock of Ages — nearer my God to thee — \+ Lead Kindly Light — were the hymns sung — Denomination clues seem to be growing faint — Service was over before 12 — I was made uncomfortable by my bkfs — so ate no lunch — the afternoon passed pacing the after deck \+ writing or letters to Ch. \+ Emma — while at dinner a short, sharp thunderstorm came out of the misty, windy surroundings — quite as uforial — Well — Concert for benefit of Ham. A. m. Pension fund. Birnbaum played on violin a Rondo Cap. Saint-Saiens. Aria. Bach — Nocturns. Chopin — \+ several encores — B — \+ Mattfeld played a duo on piano, Tannhauser march. Frank Coffin sang. Save me your aid — Queen of Sheba. Gounod — \+ encores — Mr. Mattfeld sang Aria from Gounod's Faust \+ encore — Id. Johnson sang Cecilia, Strauss — Four leaf Clover. Brownell — \+ several encores — Mrs. Carrie Coutran sang Sogno. Tosti, \+ encore — T. Hushays (bass) sang Prologo, Pagliacci, Leoncavello — Aria from Rinaldo, Handel, several encores \+ the audience sang America — Archbishop Keane offered exercises commending the charity — After the concert Lasker, chess champion played blindfold, two games with Meyer \+ Boss — 10 sec. for a move \+ check-mated each in less than 5 min — 11.30 when entertainment was over. Went to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 11, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A raw, damp, overcast day — so foggy about midday that the whistle was blown at short intervals for some time. Clearer in the afternoon but damp \+ chilly at night. Bkfs. at 8 \+ wt loafed away doing a little reading — The Purser changed my $17.50 into 70 m. 80 pfs. Lunch at 1 — more deck walking — Reading, writing \+ sleeping. The Captain's dinner at 7 — Far away the best dinner we have had on board. Saloon decked with flags — Tables all dressed — Confection center pieces \+ other decorations — a very demonstrative company of diners \+ some vociferous chorus singing putting the orchestra quite in the shade — After dinner, dancing on the Promenade deck — one side enclosed with awnings \+ dressed with flags \+ many colored electric lights — great jollity \+ general good feeling.
