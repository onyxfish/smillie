## Left Page

### Mon. June 20, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably hot day — light breeze from south but no mitigating clouds. Up early \+ bkf. at 7.30 so that Ralph might get to his Preliminary Exams. at Columbia at 9 — I went to Grocers butcher \+ to studio \+ got negatives that I developed yesterday — then home, printed from them — wrote a letter to Miss Alice Douque, a letter of Miss Stokes concerning the Franklin stove which they seem determined to have — Then I worked at trying to get some order out of the chaos in \+ about my bedroom — lunch at — Ralph back from his ordeal in good spirits but not over confident — all the afternoon I podged about \+ before dinner felt quite used up — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef. at 8.30 Ralph \+ I went to Charl's. found him a little used up — severe influenza — digestive disturbance — toothache \+ some other discomforts — Home before 10 \+ to bed — Big thunder-storm passed south of us at about 10 \+ a mere sprinkle reached us.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 21, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Just before 2 o'clk. this mf. there was a very violent thunderstorm with heavy rain — the day has been steaming hot in spite of brisk southerly breeze — Bkf. at 7.30 so that Ralph may get to his Columbia Exams. by 9 o'clk. After bkf. I went to Charl's to see how he was getting along — \+ found him improving — then to studio \+ to butcher \+ grocer — home — then to Wanamaker's \+ bought shirts \+ socks — then to Bayerdorfer with Ralph's watch. Then home — lunch — Ralph did not come home to lunch — I spent much of afternoon copying Keene House inventory — duplicate for Miss Stokes. Had a saunter with R — before dinner — Evef. Uncle Charl came in — \+ after him, Gordon —
