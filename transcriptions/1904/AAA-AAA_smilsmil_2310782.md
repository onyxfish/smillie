## Left Page

### Wed. March 30, 1904

*Jim came home.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day but I was unable to get out of my bed - I was in a fever all night \+ such sleep as I got was oblivious. I dreamed that I was awake \+ trying all the tricks \+ devices I knew of to induce sleep - during such sleep I had such hard work that on waking I would keep myself awake for the sake of the rest - at 2 a.m. my temp. was 101/5° at 7 it had fallen one degree - I sent for Yale \+ he came at about 11 \+ made a long visit - does not think it grip or influenza - a nervous disturbance perhaps that will be relieved by a day or two in bed - Rec. note from C. M. Kurtz with printed Rules \+ Regulations - St. Louis Art Dept. - showing that etchings printed in colors were permissable \+ invited \$o. I wrote at once, sitting up in bed, to Trowbridge telling him that I thought he had written to the Herald complaining \+ advising him to see Kurtz as under the Rules issued a year or more ago his colored prints were admissable - Then I wrote to Nicoll \+ to Kurtz on the subject. Jim came home before lunch - I was very uncomfortable all day owing to persistent headache but temp. seemed to be normal - Late in the afternoon, a reporter from the Herald, Chas. Haight Piffard, called \+ came to my bed room - He surprised me by telling me that Scusany wrote the complaining letter to the Herald - not Trowbridge, as I had thought - I told him there had been a misunderstanding \+ that color printed etchings are to be admitted - Then I wrote to Trowbridge \+ told him of my mistake in thinking he had written also to Scusany \+ to Nicoll \+ to Kurtz - making quite a day's work for me \+ my aching head - Charle came in \+ spent the evg. with us - most of the time in my bedroom -

## Right Page

### Thur. March 31, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark day \+ steady rain - strong contrast to yesterday - I awoke at about 7 much refreshed after my sleep - I got up \+ took my b'kfs'. with the family - I got thro' the day with a fair amt. of comfort - I spent some time in the m'f with Jim reading to him bits from Taylor's Ancient Ideals - A light lunch at the usual time. Afternoon I did some work on the Borden drawing - working out the problem of perspective as that of the photo. is too violent to be followed - At 6.30 had a light meal - Eve A. Aronson came to tell me that the Etching Class competition had begun - Young Mondara, as a pottery decorator - two afternoons already spent on drawing - class asks for another - The drawing on the plate to be done on Sat. p. m. I agreed - Evg. with newspapers \+ magazines - Jim went to Ed'w Coale's for awhiles.
