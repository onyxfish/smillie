## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 20, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day \- clear \+ remarkably cool \- did not get up till late \- arranged our "Washi" before we went down to bkfs'. after 9\. After bkfs'. walked along the Via Balba to Piazza Princese from whence we were taken by cable tramway up to the Castellaccio or Rigé \-1516 yds. at a grade of 35\-100. 1155 ft. above sea level \- the view was superb \- city at our feet \- the sea stretching boundless \- back of us the mts., those in near foreground treeless, sterile \+ barren \- great fortifications crowned the summit \- we wandered about till noon. The wind blowing so cool that I was glad I had my overcoat \- It was 1 o'clk. when we got to hotel \- the boys had lunch \- I had tea \+ rolls \+ after that, feeling tired \+ out of sorts I asked the boys to go off alone while I remained \+ rested \- I slept on the lounge in my Room till almost 5 o'clk \- Then I got up \+ went out alone \- wandering about without a special purpose \- it being the Anniversary of Garibaldi's entrance into Rome, a holiday, many of the stores were closed \- the streets were quiet but many soldiers were about \- I went into some churches \- the Annunziata is very ornate with its inlaid marble columns \+ its gilding \+ color overhead \- a service of some kind going on \- a priest intoning \- worshippers, mostly women \- repeating his responses \- an organ playing harmonious chords \- crude but effective \- I then wandered to other streets \- passed the Teatro Carlo felice \+ the Hotel where Annee \+ I stayed when we first came to Genoa in the Spring of 1892 \- went into the Church of S. Lorenzo \- then wandered thro' some of the narrow little alleys, almost dark at their bottoms before 7 o'clk \- at last I hurried my steps to get out into more open places \+ where I might have a chance to run \+ get back to Hotel before 7 \- when time came for dinner I did not feel like eating so again I went without \+ got to bed early \-

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept 21, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very fine day \- up early \+ wrote letters to Mary before I woke the boys \- bkfs'. soon after 8 \- then for a time, newspapers \- Then out to the docks \+ I wandered about there looking for photo. subjects \- got a view of Princess drive loading \+ two other harbor bits \- we had strolled about a good deal \- it was near noon \- I felt very weary \- asked the boys to excuse me \- then went back to hotel \+ rested on lounge till 1 \- Then boys came \+ we had a simple lunch of chops \+ potatoes \- \+ soon after that we went out again to hunt for more subjects \+ visit two or three of the most prominent churches \- I must confess to evidences of being completely satiated \+ tired \- I have no longer desire or ambition to see things \- I have not gone into any one of the interesting palaces here \- I want to go home \- but have a good rest to come again \- we walked out to the new street of 20 September \- commemorating Garibaldi's entrance into Rome \- it promises to be, parts of it already are, very noble \- large arcades over broad, tesselated pavements \- handsome show windows \- everything on a large \+ elegant scale \- This work is going on, taking the place of extensive demolitions where high ground is levelled \+ very old, insanitary buildings of all kinds are torn down \- it is interesting to see how ruthlessly the new rides down \+ obliterates all trace of the old \- we found some interest in places \- Terraces, Parks, Gardens \+ old churches \- we went into S. an [illegible] \+ an official took us thro' the streets, pointing out Paganini's seat where he had sat as a musician, etc. etc. \- back to hotel before 6 \- the boys almost as much dragged out as I am \- it is time we had a little Rest Cure \- Rested till 7 \- then dinner \- long but not heavy \- left table at 8.30 for a few minutes out on the Acqua square, before Chris. Columbus Monument \+ R.R. station \- cloudless moonlight \- then returned in preparation for the morrow.
