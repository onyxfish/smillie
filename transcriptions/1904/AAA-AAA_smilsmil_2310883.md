## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 20, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - overcast early m'f. \+ late afternoon. Bkfs. at 8.30 - after writing for sometime, while sitting in bed - Transcribing father's autobiography - I hope to be able to carry that work Right through now. Went to grocer \+ butcher soon after 10 - Men to Republican County Com. Mad. Ave \+ 23d S. about certificate that Jim has sent me - under which voters away from home can have special R.R. Rates for Registration \+ voting - Then I rode to Tax Office, Chambers S. \+ Bdway \+ paid Taxes. The valuation has not been Raised but the Rate has - so that the amt. this year is $832.37 with a Rebate of $5.67 - then home - lunch at 1 - afternoon I worked out Jim's acct. - somewhat complicated as I had to pick out the little advances of pocket money made from time to time last Summer - \+ I wrote him a letter of explanation \+ advice - \+ I wrote a letter to Ralph also. - At about 4.30 I went to Station H with them \+ I had a little walk - in before 6 - Woolsey Carmalt favored me with a somewhat lugubrious call extending over a half or three quarters of an hour. He left at 8 -

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 21, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came home.

Pleasant afternoon after a gale of wind and rain in the m'f. I spent all the m'f. in doors finishing accts. \+ writing letter to Miss Alice Donean, Sec'y to the Misses Stokes. also notes \+ bits of accts. together with a considerable amt. of Resultless podging - At noon went to grocer etc - lunch at 1 - afternoon more writing \+ podging, wasteful of time - the days go \+ at the close I have to write that I have done nothing - an infirmity of age I suppose - Jim came in from New Haven before dinner which was late after 7 - Every. we went to Charl's \+ at Jim \+ I went to 6th Ave. near 37 S. \+ Registered preparatory to Presidential election - Jim's first vote - Men we Ret. to Charl's - Geo. \+ Gordon There - home at about 11.
