## Left Page

### Fri. March 4, 1904

**Weather:** Bright, windy & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, windy & cold \- After bkfst \- wrote a short letter to Mary \- It was about 11 when I got to studio \- worked on 9×15 monotone \- home to lunch at 1 \- then back & finished the monotone then wrote a letter to Jim \- home at 5 \- had a rest before dinner \- We & went to Carleton Chapman's studio, after I had written letters to Ralph, getting there (58 W. 57 St.) at 8.45 \- later all the members of the Com. \- Nicholz (Secy.) Nicoll, Schilling \& Kurtz came in \- we worked over the list of etchers, selecting a few names as "Exempt" from Jury \+ portioning other names among members of Com. to look after their work. It was after 11 before we closed our evenings work \- I walked home \- getting in just before midnight.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 5, 1904

**Weather:** A very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day \- I made a few sketch during the [illegible] from photo. enlargement of the little one that Ralph took June last fall as a study for an old man's head \- my sketch is preliminary to a possible etching. Home to lunch \+ then to the N.A. of D. \- Took four plates by Vondrous with me \+ got Stahr to take prints from them \- I think them promising. I left at about 4 \+ walked over to the Third Ave. Elevated Station at 106th St. \- Then to studio \- put the V. prints in press \+ then home \- had a short rest before dinner. At 8.30 Chas. Collins called for me \+ I went with him to the Century \- home at 11 \+ read for about an hour Crawford's Heart of Rome.
