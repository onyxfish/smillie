## Left Page

### Sun. March 20, 1904

**Weather:** Bright & pleasant - somewhat breezy  **Temp:**

Up soon after 7 \+ had a good scrub \+ bkfst at 9 - after that sat awhile with newspapers - then to studio \+ opened windows - then wandered about the streets till 1 - home - napped - [illegible] till dinner at 2 - after that wrote some notes connected with Etching \+ Exhibit at St. Louis - then to studio - closed up \+ again strolled in the streets - Tea at 6.30 - Evef. wrote more notes \+ read some fool stories in the magazines - Charl came in at about 9 \+ went with me to Fresa's \+ got P.O. Stamps for my notes - Home soon after 10 - a little more Reading - then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 21, 1904

**Weather:** Dull day till about the middle of the afternoon  **Temp:**

when a sleety fall of Rain began increasing with small hail, Till after 6 when a crunching mist covered everything. To studio about 10 \+ spent the mf. getting old frames out, Taking prints from them \+ replacing others for the St. Louis Expt. Recd. notice from Soct. of Am. Artists that both of the paintings I sent there had been Rejected. That "Passing Showers" should be Refused did not surprise me - but I did expect "Cumulus after Sunset" to pass. I do not know what good art is according to present standards. Home to lunch at 1 \+ then to Birdworths 424 W.52d. St. \+ met the Com. on Etchings for St. Louis. We had a most discouraging lot of stuff to look over - The best men were not Represented at all - \+ the judgments of the other members of the Com. passed my understanding - I do not believe that there will be even a very good show of etchings - We got through with our work at about 4 \+ then I Returned to the studio \+ continued the work of framing till 5.30 - then home - dinner - Evef. wrote to Nicoll \+ to W.G. Parke.
