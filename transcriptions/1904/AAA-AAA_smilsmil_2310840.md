## Left Page

### Sun. July 24, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very close, warm day — hazy till late in the afternoon when a thunder shower passed followed by great improvement in the atmosphere. My fever last night abated somewhat so that I got some sleep before m'f. I still had some fever at 7 a.m. but was normal at noon. My head felt like a ballon full of buzzing bees \+ my hearing was much affected by quinine. I took bkf't. with the boys about 9.30 \+ I had a little stroll to Schiller platz some time during the m'f. The boys went off on an independent stroll. We had lunch about 2. \- after that they went to the Ethnographic museum. I slept — wrote to Mary \+ then went out for a stroll — out to Leipsiger St. — to d. platz — thro Bellevue St. to the Tiergarden. The air was very close \+ still — clouds thickening indications of thunderstorm near. I got back to hotel at about 6 — a mild little thunderstorm soon after \+ then pleasant, cool air. Dinner at 7 — Jim complaining — did not take any dinner — says he is completely used up.
Evelf in my room — to bed early.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 25, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'f. about the middle of the afternoon a thunderstorm, with considerable rain lasted, with slight intervals till about 6. Bkf't. at about 9 — after 10 when we started out — went to Schloss platz. \+ took many snap shots there \+ in that neighborhood. Went thro' some streets in older part of the city, watched boats moving thro' locks in canal \+ spent some time selecting photos. Back to hotel after 1 \+ had lunch — after 3 o'clk. Jim being in a "mood" declined to go with R. \+ me to "Berliner Secession" Exb'n where we saw, as I expected, a strange assortment of paintings — very few of which I could understand. The building in a restaurant garden, is shabby \+ small \+ the number of pictures small. The thunderstorm came on just before we got to the place — we were really prisoners there for more than an hour. The pictures looking lightness in the bad light. During a let up in storm we went out \+ by cab to Cornelius St. No109 \+ saw Dr. Happe in his very fine apartments on the 4th floor — He rec'd. us very cordially \+ we had a pleasant visit, regretting very much the absence of Mr. Fanny Happe \- Moser who was out of town for the day. It was near 6 when we took cab \+ drove to Kaiser hof — found that Jim had returned. Dinner about 7. Evelf in my room reading. R. in his room reading — Jim went off alone giving us no hint of where he was going. Back at 11. I had a quiet talk with him.
