## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 12, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull raw day that before night de-relapsed into wet snow \- I awoke before 6 \+ finding, after a time, that I was not going to sleep again, I lighted a candle at the head of my bed \+ read "Dorothea" by Maarten Maartens till 7.30 when I got up \- bkfs.\- at 8 \- after that I wrote to Prof. [illegible] C. Van Dyke in answer to his invitation to dine with him at the Century next Sat. ever.\- to meet for Counsel to confer with them about a work that [illegible] \- has undertaken a history of Etching \+ Engraving in America \- We started for the Ark after minutes before 10 \- well bundled up in protection ag't cold \- the ride could hardly with truth be called pleasant \- we were comfortably warm \+ in the grim greyness of the day \+ its nipping air found something at times picturesque \+ interesting \- The higher mts. west of Keene Valley were powdered with snow \- Stopped at Estes' \+ got groceries \- at Sanders' got a steak at Kelly's \+ got butter \+ eggs, also at Winch's \+ got milk \- Got to the Ark at about 1 o'clk \- Ch'ds Moody \+ Sadie Otis there \- I was exasperated on finding that the water had been cut off some days ago \+ none was in Tank \- no fire in kitchen or dining room \+ none in Range because of fear of water back \- as the boiler was full. I had fire made at once \+ some lunch prepared \- after lunch I went to Scott Brown's \+ said that by hook or by crook I must have water \- Ben Pond drove over to Otis's \- who said it would be impossible to turn water on to the section that serves me \- He said that my tank in attic held two barrels of water \- if they would fill that for me each day of my stay here it w'd be very well \- that was promised \- Then I let it go \+ soon Pond drove up with two bbs. of water \+ an assistant \- with Moody's help the water was soon transferred to Tank \+ my troubles on that score were ended \- I arranged with Moody that he is to sleep in the house at night \- Then I began to check off the articles in inventory \- Mary, assisted by Sadie, got up a very acceptable dinner at 6.30 \- after that writing \+ fussing about \- The large Room very comfortable \+ pleasant \- snow falling outside. I kept the fire in my bed room more or less actively alive all night \- up at 1 o'clk \- \+ again at 3 to attend to it,

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 13, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

When I looked out at 6 o'clk. a silvery mist filled the air \+ about 2 in. of wet snow clung to the trees \+ everything \- it was a very pretty sight \+ lasted but for a short time \- the snow soon melted \+ dripped away \- the air, while not cold, was raw \+ disagreeable \+ so continued all day \- I lay in bed very comfortably for an hour \+ a half \- until 7.30 \+ read Dorothea by the light of my student's lamp \- then up \+ had bkfs. about 8.15 \- During the m'g Moody went to the village for supplies \+ while he was away Pond came with a couple of bbs. of water that was transferred to the tank in attic \- Mary \+ [illegible] worked about all the m'g \- dinner soon after 1. \- I worked till about 4 when feeling very tired I lay down \+ had a good nap \- up again after 5 \+ more work till dinner before 7 \- Sue F. Moody brought in the laundry that the Stokes' had sent to Mrs. Sanders \- sheets, pillow cases, towels etc. \- I had to make a list of it \- \+ I worked for some time over the inventory checking, that had taken a good part of the day \- Moody came in at about 9 o'clk \+ soon after we all turned in for the night.
