## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 27, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day - mizzling rain that at night came down quite heavily - much fog. To studio after 10. Again spent much time over photos for boys albums - went to Dr. Cilly's at 1 o'clk. \+ asked him to call at house to see Jane - Then home to lunch - The afternoon at studio writing a letter to Kate which I mailed at 5 p.m. Dinner at 6.30 - while we were at Fable Jane got up, went into bath room fell in a faint - struck her head on gas stove, cutting it - Revived \+ getting to be rang - Mary went to her - excitement. At 8 Ralph \+ I went to the New Amsterdam Theatre - 42d St. west of 7th Ave. \+ saw the spectacle Humpty Dumpty - It was a nonsensical \+ purely spectacular affair - there was some good singing a great deal of dancing \+ some good acrobatic dance work - the Acrobatics Aerialists were very graceful in their flights, suspended by wires that were invisible - The dance of Nellie Daly \+ John Mc. Veigh was one of the maddest things I ever saw. There was a dazzling display of feminine legs, The most of them shapely \+ appended to good looking but much paint bedaubed girls - Two of the Chinese were quite gorgeous in ensemble - We got out soon after 11.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 28, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little bright weather in the mf growing colder \+ rapidly drying - but clouds soon came. - Another day wasted over my photography - a good part of the time being given to making up some new photographic solutions - for Velox development - Acid fixing bath, etc - Home to lunch at 1 - back at 2 \+ at 4.30 went to Cullen's with 8 spools of films - the last of last summer's exposures - I have given up attempting to develop the films because of non-success. Home at 5.30 - dinner at 6.30. Eve'g, loafed away -
