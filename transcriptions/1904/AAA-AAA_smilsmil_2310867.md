## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day - Bkft. shortly after 8 - Then Read newspapers for awhile + from the Paris Herald learned something of the Strike trouble here - in a Labor Riot down in Sicily a few days ago the soldiers killed a striker - this was the basis of a call issued by the Syndicats (I don't know what sort of an organization it is) + placarded all over the walls in the city calling upon laborers of all kinds to cease work for 24 hours - They have ceased generally now for more than twice that long - an immense meeting was held in the Arena here + there was a show of Red flags + much noise - The strike was called as a great demonstration agt. the government - the fear is that it may get beyond the limit of quiet - The anarchists + socialists are doing what they can to foment more trouble. we walked to the cathedral + tried to get to the Roof but were denied because of the disturbance - we went to the Castello - but it was closed against us for the same reason - Ret. to Cathedral + heard High Mass sat thro' a sermon - not so interesting to me as the one at St. Stephens - Vienna - choir of men + boys led by an old fellow who beat time energetically - the organ was too much for the voices which may have been good but the acoustic effect under the dome was bad - confusing reverberations - as a musical effect it was unsatisfactory - the crowd - the function - the place were spectacular but not greatly moving to me as at Vienna - To Hotel + had lunch - very good. After that a long walk to the Brera to find it closed for fear of trouble - Then another long walk to the Church of St. Maria delle Grazie hoping agt. hopes to be allowed to see da Vinci's "Last Supper" but we could not convince the attendant that we were not dangerous - so we had to give it up + after another long + uninteresting walk got back to hotel. I hear that some Cavalry has been brot. into the city. Squads of Police + soldiers patrol the streets while are thronged by people - an attempt has been made to stop telegraph + R. R. service - I have decided to take no chances + shall get on to Genoa as soon as possible. After dinner the boys went out to town about - have not been long gone when there was a great commotion on the street - I found the large front door closed - only a small postern unguarded - the street was full of running crowd - shouting - going towards a hill - another shouting crowd came over - it was scanning indeed a demonstration agt. the little half closed door - it was scanning unit + I heard the bolt - I stood in the crowd - unpleasant moment - soon passed - I was very uneasy till the return of the boys half an hour later -

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 19, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Genoa. Hotel Savoie - Rooms 45-46.

A fine day - Up early + made ready for a start - Sounds of traffic in the streets were reassuring - we had bkft. before 8. + left at about 8.30 - The Hotel del - is a superior + expensive hotel - but we dared not stay - At R.R. Station we met the R.R. porter of our hotel who told us that telegrams had been Rec'd from a place about half way bet. Milan + Genoa stating that a portion of the Road had been destroyed by the strikers + possibly we might not be able to get thro' - I had a few uncomfortable moments until a later telegram came with better news - The appearance of the station was warlike - soldiers crowded about + after we left could be seen along the track - we left at 9.10 in an excellent car in striking contrast to the one that brot. us from Como - Long + heavy - a Raw smoother over a good Road bed - Country just + uninteresting for two hours or more - then we entered barren dim hillsides - tunnels - up a steep grade - thro' a lot of tunnels - one, very long - then down thro' a deep valley to sea level - We were detained for half an hour or more outside of Genoa - only explanation to us was in the number of soldiers all along the track - we learned afterwards that demonstrations agt. the R.R. had been made at several points - no trains came in from Turin yesterday. In Genoa two men were killed + 22 wounded - When at last we got into station - great confusion - Train mobbed by people - to get out before we could get off - After a struggle we extricated ourselves + got out of station soon after 1 o'clk. + our pachin carried luggage across sq. to Hotel where we got Rooms promptly + had some lunch - Then out to count first to office of M. Garrman + Lloyd + had a pleasant interview with Mr. Leopeld after presenting Chas's letter of introduction - he promises me a letter of introduction to the Capt. of Vr. Arena - Then to Thos. Cook + drew £10 - found the "Caslin" in his compartment with iron shutts closed - one candle only lighted - said that yesterday they were barricaded fearing attack - Then we walked thro' some portions of the city where there had been trouble + saw smashed windows - Then along the docks till we came to where we could see Princess Irene + that people were going to + from her in small boats - hailed one + were taken to her + got aboard - interviewed Chief Steward - secured seats at table - Then deck steward + arranged for chairs + places - Then bath Room stw. - at last our Room steward - so feeling that we had done a good afternoons work we Ret. to shore - walked to hotel + at had dinner - very meagre - Room pleasant - not large but high - very few at dinner - the hotel is pretentious in some ways - pretty good - our Rooms are very pleasant, overlooking R.R. Station Plaza Columbus M. - out to sea - on III [illegible] - but have to go up two stories to get to I [illegible] - did not running for want of water - we got to our beds early, tired + hoping that our troubles are now over -
