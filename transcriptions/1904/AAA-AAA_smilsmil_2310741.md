## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 8, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow all day — to studio before 10 \+ was able to work on "The River's Source" till about 11.30 after which it was too dark for me to see — Carmen took "Passing Shower" \+ "Cumulus" to Century \+ I went home shortly after 12 — lunch — from 3 to 4 I was on my bed — then I went to studio — cleaned up — home soon after 5 — Finished letter to Mary \+ mailed it — then dinner — Quel wrote to the boys — at 10.15 went to G\. Cent\. Station — thru home \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 9, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. At bkft. time got a letter from Rev. The. C. Williams with Reference to my detention of Ralph over Tues. night. It was uncivil \+ unjust \+ hurt me deeply — I gave some time to writing a rejoinder \+ then took it to P.O. Station 11. Lex. Ave \+ 44th S\! Then to studio \+ painted on "Source" till 1 o'clk — then home to lunch — then a rest — then dressed \+ after 4 o'clk. went to the Ballard Reception. 61 W. 8th S\. — there I met all the B's \+ more two — also Mr. Jeremiah — \+ learned that Mary Pelherums is not expected to recover from Congestion of lungs — then by a hard ride on three lines of cars I got to Mary Cook's. 174 E. 37th S\. — where she had a demonstration in free sewing — a check system for hats \+ coats \+ a delectroman announcer — met lots of people there — then to studio — cleaned up palette etc \+ home to dinner late — Soef went to Century annual meeting — I had there "Passing Showers" \+ "Cumulus after Sunset" — They were well spoken of by several artists \+ friends whereat I was much pleased of course — Home at midnight.
