## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 11, 1904

Vienna - Hotel Bristol - Rooms 326-327.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, but very hazy - had our bkfp at usual time - Then started out to do the town on foot - went up the Vaclavské náměstí - a broad ave - up grade - to the Bohemian Museum, at the top of the hill - commanding position - the Pantheon is an imposing hall in size, but bare - a few statues - I rushed thro' the galleries - purely museum - very carefully arranged - everything spick & span - Took a look at the Stradt Park - some time buying views - could not find good photos - walked to K. Franz bridge & over it to other side of River - Then along River side thro' odd little ways to the Karl's brucke - crossed it - Then along the new terrace by River - The Malrazi Koraninho Prince Rudolfa - Then into the Rudolf museum, a new, large & handsome bldg. Great central hall - square & empty - will be fine when walls are decorated & statuary placed. galleries very large - light, not good - like all other galleries here recently, too sunny middle of day - a few good pictures - most of them modern - spent very little time looking at them - Thine to the old few quarter now being demolished - fine large shops being erected along new wide streets - back to hotel soon after 1 & had our dinner in the court. It was a little before 3 when we went to R.R. Station getting good seats on train that left at 3:15. A long uninteresting ride to Vienna - 7 hours - for a long time we were passing over a level plain - stretches of wheat fields - crops now being gathered - few small towns - remarkably few farm houses - after a time, in a wooded country - rolling, with small streams of water - before dark we passed a very hilly country forest covered for the most part - a ka [illegible]. Running stream, crossed many times & many tunnels cutting thro' the hills - at 10.15 we entered the Nord Bahnhof & promptly got a vehicle & rode to the Bristol with all our luggage - we were delayed at the station for a few minutes to go thro' the form of a Customs' examination - at the hotel we were shown rooms on what is known as the III floor - Really the fifth - I selected two large rooms at the back of the house, north side - we were too dirty to go to bed without a wash - got hot water - a wash & to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 12, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Light rain about 8 am. Sprinklings of rain later - cloudy till middle of afternoon when it cleared pleasantly, with cool breeze.

Up at 7.30 after a good night - best bed since I left home. Boys up after 8 & bkfp. at 9 - all of us somewhat tired - lounged awhile after bkfp - got our wash ready for laundry - I wrote short letter to Mary - at about 11 out with the boys - walked past the Hof garden - going up Kartner Ring, Opera Ring \+ Burg Ring to the Kunst Histor museum - superb stairways & rotunda - too much ornament - great galleries - not well lighted - do not like the slate-green color of the walls - we hurried thro' - a few fine old paintings took my attention - upper floor - vast number of passable water-color drawings - poorly lighted rooms - did not go into basement - ground floor rooms which are museums - back to hotel - well tired - rested awhile & then had our [illegible] which was really a dinner - after that the boys went out \+ I had a nap - They got at 5 - then we went out - took open carriage & rode for two hours going out to the Prater, entering by way of the Haupt Allee, in a sort of "midway" jumble of amusements of all kinds - also sort of Coney Island - Then along the Allee, straight as an arrow - lined with Horse Chestnut trees, as far as the Lusthaus - Then our driver turned to left & drove over to the River Danube which is here a great canal with stone-paved banks. Returning again to the Prater he drove a long distance over good roads - well shaded where we could see fields & many grown people & children wandering about - much of the Prater is in a wild state - none of it is laid out like a park - the garden or Central Park - it is an open ground for the people - the one long, straight driveway being the only place for display. Early - I had no supper but wrote a letter to Mary - the boys went out & had their supper at a Kurbalon in the Stradt park & then they wandered about seeing lots of things till near 10 - then back to Hotel.

The driver who took us about, showing the sights this afternoon drove to a street where he showed us a building that he told us was the highest in Vienna - it was eight stories high.
