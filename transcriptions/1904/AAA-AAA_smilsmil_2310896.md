## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 15, 1904

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. To studio at usual time. made a start at repainting the River's Sour. \- Tackled the very dark left hand lower cor. \+ worked a lot of black out of it \- \+ changed a line in the composition. Ed. Scholl came in for half an hour about noon. Home to lunch at 1.20 \- back at 2.30 \+ painted till 4. Then I went to Alexander's the shoe maker \- on my way home stopped at Wunderlich's \+ saw an ex'tn of Whistler's etchings \- Home soon after 6. dinner \- Eve'g, wrote to Katie Selkins \- Jim \+ Ralph with reference to foot ball game on hat. went to G. C. S. \+ mailed them before 11.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 16, 1904

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day \- partially clouded \- To studio at 10 \+ continued painting on The River's source. I am getting some fun out of it altho' I am fully aware of the fact that my work is along hopelessly antiquated lines \- but I keep at it because it seems to me to represent the facts (with some measure of success) that stirred me with pleasure when I saw them before me \- Home to lunch \+ back soon after 2 \- worked till 3.30 \- Then cleaned up \+ went to Barkers \- then to Claussens ordered 12"×16" frame \- Then home \+ read paper till dinner \- After that, at 8 o'clk I went to Charles \+ was with him for an hour \- then we walked down to the Waldorf where I left him \+ ret'd home. To bed early.
