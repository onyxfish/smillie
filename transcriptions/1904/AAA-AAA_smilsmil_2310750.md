## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 26, 1904

**Weather:** Vile day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Vile day - snow commenced falling after 10 am. This, after an inch or two was on the ground turned to freezing rain + soon everything was covered with slush. The storm held up at night with indications of freezing. It was 10.30 when I got to studio - too dark to see to work. Alice Ireland came + stayed till after 12. A part of my m'f was given to overhauling electric batteries at home + at studio. Trouble with the bells. To lunch at 1 - back at 2.30 + as long as I could see I worked with crayon on enlargement from photo. of Noah II. Home at 5. Men from Con. Gas had been at house making efforts to clear service pipe - instead had choked it altogether so that we had no gas at all + had to depend upon lamps + candles. Dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g read newspaper + wrote a couple of notes.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 27, 1904

ninth anniversary of Annie's death.

**Weather:** Bright freezing m'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright freezing m'g. + as anticipated the streets are in a horrible condition from frozen slush - slightly overcast + milder later in the day. I got to studio rather late + spent the m'g. in working with crayon on Noah II + upon a place of young Stahr, a member of the etching class, + other preparation for class in the afternoon. Home to lunch at 1 + then to Na.f.D. where I worked with class till after 4 - then home + was able to get a little rest before dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g. I went to Century Com. on Admissions + was there till 11.15. Howard Mansfield was elected chairman + Taylor, Sec'y. m. - seems to be a business like, efficient chairman + the humor of T. promises to enliven work that is often dreary.
