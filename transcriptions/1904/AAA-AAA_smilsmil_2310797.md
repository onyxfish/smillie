## Left Page

### Fri. April 29, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant, clearing up very slowly + mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up with a headache that incapaci-
tated me all day. I was at studio for a
portion of the m'f. I planned some changes
in "the Source" — went to Outlook Co. + Charities
\+ paid bills — then to 2° Nat'. I arranged
business there — then home to lunch — to
studio soon after 2 — wrote a letter to
him concerning the schedule of studies
that he has prepared for his senior year
at Yale — It is not at all satisfactory to
me + I know that I am not competent,
alone, to give best advice — I must take
counsel — a loaf in the streets till 6 —
then home — Eve'f., worrying over bank
accts + writing letter to Ralph. Alice
Dreland came in for awhile. Sue
Whiting called at the house in the after-
noon.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 30, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f., about 1.30 a shower, with a little thunder, came up + for a time it rained smartly — showery p.m. — fine moonlight night very mild.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I started the day at about
4.30 with a headache so severe that I thought
I would not be able to get up for b'kf't — but,
abnormally under the influence of acetanelid
\+ caffiene + a trace of magnesia I pulled thro'
\+ did a satisfactory day's work. I got to studio
a little earlier than usual + painted on "The
Source" remodelling entirely the middle dis-
tance + painting in groups of cedars in lower
a.h. cor. all greatly to the advantage of the
picture, I think. I worked till 5 o'clk. stop-
ping only when I went home to lunch. I
had a few minutes saunter in the street
before 6 — then home + got 15 m. rest before
dinner — at 6.30 — Eve'f. at 8 o'clk. went to
Chat. Collins' — 133 E. 36th St. + gave him my
idea as to framing his murillo print — at about
9 we went to The Century + I had an agree-
able time till 10.30 — then home.
