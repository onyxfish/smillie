## Left Page

### Thur. April 21, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Much pleasanter weather than yesterday but still unusually cool — After bkfs. wrote to Fred. W. Morton Ed. Brush \+ Pencil — got to studio late — went to Scribners — saw M. H. Sneath \+ Reported progress on Bordeu drawing — then to 2°. Nat. to get some bond quotations — then to Grolier Club \+ spent an hour looking at a very full exhibit of Whistler's early work — then to studio at 12. \+ worked there till 1 \+ home to lunch. After lunch, feeling very heavy \+ tired \+ gave way to it \+ had a half hours nap — then to studio — got naf d. etching class plates \+ took them to Kemmel \+ Vogets — in Canal st — arranged with H. Voigt to take pts. met Schneider there — he was having two large dry-point plates proved — I walked up 5th S. to Astor Pl. \+ took car — getting home at 6:30 — dinner Eve. f. with Mary \+ Jane H — went to Charles \+ saw the last lot of Lantern slides — over 400 — altogether — Home at 10:30.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 22, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine spring like day — up early — after bkfs. I finished writing a long letter to Mary. it was after 11 before I left the house — At the studio I made preparations to begin the repainting of Vesuvius from near Sorrento for W. Q. Parke — Home to lunch at 1 — back soon after 2 — \+ Painted till near 5. went over much of foreground in l.h. cor. I think that I was successful in laying in a good foundation \+ am hopeful that I may yet get a good picture out of it — walked the streets for awhile — home at 5.30 — a rest before dinner — Eve. f. wrote to the boys — also to W. J. Baer \+ to Chas. M. Kurtz about the Blum etchings for St. Louis —
