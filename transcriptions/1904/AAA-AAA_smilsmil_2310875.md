## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 4, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Decided change of weather - wind went round to North & West & it was quite cold - Clouds hung heavy, but well formed, all the m'f. - clearing very considerably in the afternoon - The sea went down + the Princess was as steady as a ferry boat - I walked more than on any other day during the voyage - + had a solid nap in the afternoon - The Cap't° dinner in the eve'g - was almost hilarious + recalled some of the jolly times on the Patricia - The salon was decorated with peas & festoons of oak leaves in paper - The dinner itself was elaborate + very poor - The tables fare has been very trying to me - elaborate menus from which it was impossible to select a simple solid meal - After the dinner the tables were quickly cleared + there was an "entertainment" under the direction of Fran d'isa Calppiani - She had announced at the dinner table some days ago her intention of getting up something for the benefit of the fund for widows + orphans of sailors but she had great trouble in finding material out of which she could develop attractions - She was determined that we should not escape her - a majority of the passengers answered her invitation to be present + she opened the exercises with a harangue. She is a very large, portly woman with voice to match. Dressed in white she took her place at the Cap't° seat + with much energy & gesticulation delivered her address - her English, added to her dramatic manner was very amusing, especially in the serious parts, where she attempted rhetorical flights - The laughter was continuous + on one or two occasions where it was altogether inappropriate, she was evidently annoyed - But, the sketch ended she introduced a lady who played on the piano - then the Representative of the Italian Govt. aboard ship who looks after the immigrants - he said two or three things - this is nose, then a young woman who sang - at last Fran'd Calppiani announced that she w'd. close the entertainment by playing something herself.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 5, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliantly beautiful day - Up early + on deck - the coast of Long Island clearly in sight - After b'kf't it was constant excitement as we drew nearer + nearer to the entrance to the harbor - Passengers looked strange in their land gear - Packings were completed - cabin luggage was brought out + piled up on deck + hoisting rigging was made ready to get things out of the hold - It was after 11 when we got to the dock - a pilot was taken on not long before that - we stopped at quarantine for a short time for the Doctor to come aboard - Then the Customs & Emigrant officers - The Customs officers took their places in the large saloon + passengers had to pass them in turn + make declaration as to whether or not they have dutiable goods in their trunks - That took considerable time + we had almost reached the city before we got through with it - We had an early lunch before the officers came aboard - it was nearly 1 o'clk when we reached our dock + were promptly warped alongside - about 1.30 when we got ashore - I was greatly disappointed that no one was there to meet us - we soon got our baggage together + then an inspector who managed to make himself disagreeable on very slight provocation - Then I got a cab + without trouble + got away from the dock in about 3/4 of an hour after landing - we rode home at once getting there at 3 o'ck - very prompt + all very factory work. Mary Hutchinson met us at the door - Jennings was shorn on hand - the house was clean but Mary has not yet succeeded in getting servants - there is an Irish girl in the kitchen to do the cooking but she is to leave to-morrow - I learned that Geo. is in Ridgef'd + Charlie is away west somewhere on business - The boys at once set to work unpacking + repacking - I felt very much relaxed + lay on my bed for a couple of hours - We had a very good dinner at 6.30 - I went out + got a lot of beer & ice cream so that the boys might have their fill after the poor table fare in the steamer - Ralph hurried away for his train to Tarrytown - 7.30 + Jim went to New Haven on the 8 o'clk. train - JS had a quiet eve'g looking over a lot of mail matter that had collected in my absence to bed at about 10 - Right glad to be home again + already finding it hard to realize that for the past three months I have been scurrying over thousands of miles of land + water.
