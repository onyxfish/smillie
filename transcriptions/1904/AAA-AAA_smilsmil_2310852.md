## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 17, 1904

**Weather:** Pleasant day \- too warm for comfort in sunshine  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- too warm for comfort in sunshine \- bkfs. at 9 at café \- back to hotel \- got camera \- then to Nat. history museum \- there till after 1 \- then to Minorie Ch. to see Mosaic \+ found front of ch. \- much of it in course of reconstruction \- so we did not see mosaic \- back to Volks garten took some snap shots \- went in to Restaurant \+ had dinner. Taking plenty of time to it \- some more photographing \+ then to Stables \+ found that we had wasted time \- just 3 o'clk. when we got there \- the hour of closing. then to the Ring \+ spent some time selecting photos. to hold after 4 \- The boys went off somewhere \+ I had a rest \- then had my hair cut \- boys back at 7 \- then out to café \+ had supper \- then wandered up Kartner Str. to the Graben looking in to the store windows \+ at the people \- went into a photo store \+ bought more photos \- to hotel after 9 \- laying out plans for closing up the Venice episode \+ launching into the Tyrolese Mts.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 18, 1904

*Emperor's birthday*
*Opera*

**Weather:** A very hot, sunny mft.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot, sunny mft. \- heavy thunder storm about 4 P.M. \- Rain continued till well on in the evg. \- Bkfs. at about 9 at Café on Kartner Str. Ret. to hotel, got camera \- \+ walked over to the Belvedere, looking for characteristic subjects for snap shots \- at Belvedere got two or three \- then on to the Arsenal thro' streets \+ parks just being laid out \- a hot \+ dusty walk \- no cabs \- no cars that we could see \- at the Arsenal I had hoped to see the Ruhmeshalle, or Hall of fame \- but \- a holiday \- it was closed. This was a great disappointment after our hot, dirty walk \- no bells \- we walked to the Sud bauhof, not far off, got a cab \+ rode to hotel \- brushed ourselves up a bit, then to the Weiner Bäckerei \+ got another fr. 25 \- Then to the Graben \+ bought a lot of photo films \- then by pace, to Volks garten \+ had our dinner at about 2 o'clk \- The thunderstorm was then threatening \- we got to hotel \- The rain fell heavily \+ there was heavy thunder \- we did not go out till about 6 \- then to café \- Supper \- \+ at 7 o'clk to Opera. Surprised to find no ticket takers at the entrance \- we passed without challenge to our box \- after the entrance \- we passed an official came \+ got my documents showing title to the box \- little ante room \- such a long mirror \- electric light \- hooks for wraps \- curtain to box proper \- four chairs, a lounge for two \- 2d Rank \- 2d box to left from front \- our box \- fine view of stage \+ a good one of both sides of the house \- our loge was No. 12. I paid 24 Krone (fr. 4.80) for it the six seats \- we three had the entire box \- as I was uncomfortably warm in my black coat I took it off \+ sat in my shirt sleeves on the lounge at the back of the box where I had a perfect view of the stage but could not be seen from the house \- the stage setting was exceedingly good \- most satisfying \- The pageants were very effective \+ the costuming of the throngs was artistic \- Lohengrin was \- Hr Schnedes \+ Elsa was Fr. Weidt, Otrude was Fr. Raulich \+ Friedrich was Hr. Weidemann \- The orchestra was very full, numbering about 80 \- I was much impressed by the Swan, it was so intelligent, turning its head and nodding knowingly to Lohengrin. Act II was especially effective \- fine architecture \- terraces \- broad flights of steps \- crowds of revellers \- varied costumes \- The scene at entrance of church when Otrude disputes Elsa's Right of precedence was one of much energetic confusion \- it was sung \+ acted with much spirit. The scene in the Bridal Chamber was exceedingly pretty \- the setting \- the lighting \+ the costumes all combined to make a lovely picture \- The music was tender \+ charming as Elsa pleads with \- \+ he tries to pacify her. The Telmakund passage was given very dramatically \- The coming of the Swan ford \- \+ his address to it \+ Elsa's unspeakable grief was all quite thrilling \- all over at about 11 o'clk.
