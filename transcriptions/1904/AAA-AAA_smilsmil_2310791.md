## Left Page

### Sun. April 17, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. but rather cool for the season \- up at 7 after a fairey good nights rest \- \+ after my usual Sunday morning performance had bkfp. at 9.30 \- After 11 I went to studio \+ opened it up for airing \- found Ge°. in his room getting his W. C's ready for to-morrow \- out \+ loafed on the sunny side of streets till 1.30 \- then home \- Dinner at 2 \- Charl came in for a few minutes while we were at Table \- At 4.30 I went to studio \+ closed up windows \- a short walk \+ then home \+ had a rest before supper. Eve'g walked down to 331 E. 17th St. \+ had a visit with Summa. Mary Ireland tumbled down stairs last Wed. \+ bruised herself severely \- bumping her forhead \+ scraping her nose so seriously that a sanguinary \+ scary time followed for a few minutes. \- walked home, getting in at 10.45

## Right Page

### Mon. April 18, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An open \+ shut day, as the country man w'd say \- sunshine \- then cloud \- air chilly \- I got to studio \+ put in a fair days work on the Borden outline bringing it near to completion \- dreadful work \- Home to lunch at 1 after having gone to Noe's gallery to see the works there of "The younger American Artists" \- dreadful \- at most of it \- Back at 2.30 \+ worked till after 5. Atonson came bringing the Competition Etching class plates \- I bought neckties \+ went to the Century for a few minutes before going home to dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g Charl came in \+ we talked over the possibilities of my going to Europe with the boys next summer \- after considering many figures I came to the conclusion that it will be a good thing for me to do. I shall study the matter more carefully
