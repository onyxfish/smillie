## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 5, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fairly pleasant day, to studio as usual the m'f divided between work experimenting with dry resin grounds on zinc plates for Etching class \+ writing to Katharine throo a description of my Passifa experience \- To lunch at 1 \- Back at 2.30 \- Continued experiments \- Vondrous came \+ I showed him how I laid the aquatint ground \+ a bit the tint on his plate for him \+ was detained till near 6 at Studio \- At home I found that Mallie's friend, Miss Davies, had invited herself to dine with us \- she spent the eve'g \+ left at 10.15 I got my letter to Katharine off \+ two apologies for letters to the boys \- Emma \- came in the late afternoon \+ left theatre ticket for me \- Candida \- about 8 o'elk. Frank Adams \+ his bride came in \- she is as pretty pleasant little thing \+ was brimming over full of their recent journeyings in Texas \+ Colorado \- had a number of photos \- it was late before I got to bed \+ S was very, very tired.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 6, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

mallie sailed \-

Disagreeable damp day. The sun came near getting at us thro' the clouds two or three times \- then gave it up. I got up before 7 \- bkf. at 7.30 so that Mallie could get to her steamer in Hobaken before 9 \- Alice Ireland \+ Miss Davies came to the house to see her off \- miss D. went in carriage with her to the steamer \- I got to studio before 10 \+ spent the m'f working on two plates one by Vondrous, the other by Aronson \- working with scraper \+ burnisher on the aquatint bitten over them \- Home to lunch at 1 \+ at 2.30 was at Carnegie Lyceum to see Bernard Shaw's play of Candida \- met Emma there, we sat together, ticket came from que at water \- The play was amusing to most of the audience. To me it was rather tragic \- no doubt, I took it more seriously than was intended \- Rev. Jas. M Morrell (Dodson Mitchell) is the husband of Candida (Dorothy Donnelly) \+ they are very much in love \- they have picked up a youth, Eugene Marchbanks (Arnold Daly) an impossible fool poet \- who has a radium Rayway of looking at things \- he finds conventionalities everywhere \+ taking the place of simple truth \- naturally lots of clever cynicism is evolved \- He finds that the love between Morrell \+ his wife is a sham in the light of absolute truth \- He loves Candida \- assails Morrell with plausible arguments to prove his unworthiness \+ claim Candida as rightfully his \- He succeeds in making Morell doubt himself \- Marchbanks is left alone with Candida \+ is found on his knees before her \- There is a trying scene \+ then Candida's feminine wisdom \+ her true love for her husband brings light out of darkness \- These scenes of married life \+ love are very trying to me as they bring before me pictures of my loss \- I went to the Barnard Club with Emma for a few minutes \- then home \- \+ then gave up to the headache that had been threatening me all day \- I was not able to go to the monthly meeting \- Century Club.
