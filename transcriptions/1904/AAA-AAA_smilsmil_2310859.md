## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 2, 1904

Hotel Bauer au Lac - Zurich
Room 147-148

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at about 8 - after a fairly good night + glad to find that I was not entirely bunged up - I got the boys up at about 9 - no use to get them up earlier as it was still raining + continued to rain most of the day - we could not go about but could see that Landeck is a hole in the ground steep, high mts standing closely around - We left the hotel soon after 10 by 'bus - to Station a mile or more away - large crowd of tourists - Train at 11.93 - already almost full - we had to get into compartment where there were but two vacant seats - occupants - two german + two English women - two latter were not pleased at our intrusion - unpleasant - the german women got off Bludenz - then the English women intro duced two men from another compartment, one more than there was room for - ignored us - opened windows + played cards - they were very superior people - too much for us - Talked about "Those Americans" in a compartment forward - at Buchs - where customs exams. were made the train was Remade + we got another compartment more to our liking - Up to this point, from Landeck, the scenery had been spectacular - a wonderful diversity of gorges - the R.R. running most of the way along the edge of cliffs - thro tunnels + Rock cliftings with wildest scenery above + below - we could catch glimpses only of the wildness above when now + then the cloud lifted, we could see snow - but never the tops - in places waterfalls seemed to come out of the clouds - the little strips of pasture in the narrow valleys were wonderfully green + the evergreen trees on the mt. sides dark, solomn - The Arlberg tunnel - 6½ m. long - was a monotonous ride of about 16 m - I pumped for the impressive, but did not get it - The scenery changed entirely after entering the Rhine valley - At Buchs the Customs officer came to our compt. I said something to which we said No + he went his way + our luggage was not unlocked - In the Rhine valley the foot hills, clothed with hard wood, marked a change from the evergreen covered steeps of the Tyrol - Lake Wallenesse showed some fine mts on the northern side, but Lake Zurich was a great disappointment - as I saw it, it was commonplace - low banks - cultivated + quite thickly strewn with villas - ordinary habitations along its shores - We got to Hotel Bauer au Lac at about 6 - (by the way, we had a very poor dinner on the train) + were assigned Room at once - found a goodly batch of letters awaiting us - one was a great surprise - from Charl written on the S.S. Deutschland - he is now in London on important business very suddenly developed - to be in London + Antwerp three weeks - then home - Dinner in Restaurant - Evef. wrote letter to Charl.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 3, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another wet mf. but it cleared sunny + misty after 3 pm. Had bkft. after 9 - after that went to an Exch'e. office + got Swiss money for Austrian - Then, in obedience to the demands of a Seidlitz powder taken at 5 A.M. + because it had begun to Rain again, I act'd to hotel + busied myself there about till Table d' hate lunch at 1 - After that, with the boys, I wandered about Town - along the Promenade on the West side, which is delightful + on to the Haven - took a look at the Lion monument - then thro' Belvoir Park which pleased me very much - some novel effects in tree effects + gardening - by running over around + cruising the tree trunks of spruce - Then walked back to Hotel - had a Rest + at 7 had a Table d' hote dinner - very good - We sat in Public Room - glass covered Roof in court - music by small orchestra - Promenimus Printed + on tables - great central table with piles of news papers - small tables - coffee + cigars - wrote letter to many while I listened to music - to bed after 10.
