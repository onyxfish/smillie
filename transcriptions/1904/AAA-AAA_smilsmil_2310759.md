## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 13, 1904

**Weather:** Fine winter day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine winter day. After bkfs. went to Dr. Gale's to see if he had any professional suggestions to make — says he thinks that my chief trouble now is intestinal indigestion — should it wonder — he advises a disinfection of the tract by calomel \+ then for some time treatment with Kreosote. Got to studio before 11 — feeling shabby \+ unlike work, so I passed with a crayon drawing, enlargement of small photo of self, Noah [?]. Home to lunch \+ after that, feeling heavy with sleep I gave up a good hour to it — got up feeling better — then to studio \+ till near 5 continued fussing with portrait of self, preparing to etch it. Then went to Frasers. 5 Ave. Near 29. S; \+ got Kreosote tabloids prescribed by Gale — then home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — at 8 I went to Chas. Collins' 133 \+ 36 S. \+ with him went to Century. Sat with a ring of "fellows" \+ drank, as my share, a bottle of "White Rock water" — Ellsworth, of Chicago, made himself agreeable by paying for the water \+ telling stories. Home at 11 \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 14, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fall of fine snow during most of the day \+ far into the night. Bkfs. at 10 o'clk after a good scrub. After my bkfs. \+ newspaper I wrote till after one o'clk — much of the time on a letter to Kate. Then I went, after a short walk, to Charl's \+ got correct time — then home to dinner at 2 o'clk. For more than an hour in the afternoon I wrote on letter to Kate. Then I had a long nap before supper at 6.30. Well at 8 I walked down to Emma J's — E. 17 \+ 5. \+ had a very pleasant chat with her, before a cheering fire, till 10 — then walked home — still snowing.
