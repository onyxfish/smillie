## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 22, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nastier day even than yesterday — a thin coating of glass-like ice over everything in the m't. sleet falling \+ freezing — walking almost impossible — everybody scattering sand, ashes, dirt, sawdust, anything, over the sidewalks. dark — Rain came later, falling freely — warmer — then fog in the air \+ filth in the streets. I got to studio \+ put Vesuvius on the easel — for an hour or more I was scraping it down — sand papering \+ scrubbing it — then I felt too tired \+ the day was too dark \+ dreary to begin the work of repainting it — so I made a gelatine Tracing of the photo. of Self-Taken at Keene last Sep't. that I have titled Noah II. \+ by squares enlarged it to 9×12" — Home to lunch — back at 2.30 — worked on crayon head till after 4 — then home — dinner — Evey wrote to Jim \+ Raelph.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 23, 1904

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very disagreeable day — fog \+ Rain in the m'g. dark, damp afternoon \+ eve'g. To studio at about 10.30 \+ worked for awhile on crayon head after photo. Noah II. Gill the art dealer of Springfield mass. came in with Geo. saying that he had been advised that he should get something from me for his 246[illegible]. I showed him "Cumulus" \+ he said if I w'd let him have that he w'd. ret. it to me, if not sold, on Nch 8. so that I could send it to Socy. of Am. Artists — I set palette \+ at once repainted portion of foreground trees as I had planned — Home to lunch at 1 — then to N.A. Etching class. Worked on plate till 5 o'clk then, down — went to Clausen's \+ home at 6 — dinner — Evey, Charl came in with enlargements from some of his small photos Taken while abroad last summer.
