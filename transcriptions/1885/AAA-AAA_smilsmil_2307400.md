## Left Page

### October, Friday 23, 1885

Mild & pleasant — at times in
the afternoon threatening showers.
Rained out twice during the night
& feel pretty slim to-day. Pettus
came up with a load of Tarbell man-
ure — but he dawdles so that I put
curry with him & the result was the
before 6 o'clk he had drawn 12 or 15
excellent loads. I went about
very little but did some packing
work in doors & finally drove the
stakes for 23 trees on the newly
graded lot. Mr. Dau. Sayre
called in the afternoon. In
the eve'g Annie & I had a
game of cards & then to bed
about 9.30.

In the m'f wrote notes to Dr.
Bellows — H+f Farmer — R.S. Gifford
& Mr. Coleman, enclosing $2.20
each — the last P.&E. dividend.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 24, 1885

A very mild, pleasant day, altho' at
times overcast. Got up at 8 after a
quiet night, but still feeling unsettled
below. Set Coyle & Rhinevander at
digging holes for trees. & Pettus & curry,
at two loads of manure, at hauling
light earth to mix for top dressing on grass.
About 10.30 went down town — to different
places, paying & getting bills. About
3 o'clk I set Coyle & Rhinevander
at taking up two trees to transplant
but they could not get them up by
I & had to leave them. Zufus came
turned water pipes away from the
house. Ben Mulford & wife called.
About 6 I went down town and
gathered some bills — then home
to tea — brilliant, cloudless
moonlight night. Ther. went
down to 36°. To bed at 10.30
