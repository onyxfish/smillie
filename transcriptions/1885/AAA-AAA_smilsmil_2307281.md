## Left Page

### February, Thursday 19, 1885

Clear cold day — nearly 10.30 when I got to Studio — Tried to work on a small cliff subject — painting in the Promontory — did not feel at all like work & got on very slowly — Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — worked until 4.30 — Then washed up & went to McKeeve's in 42°. S— & saw 21×31 frame in progress. Home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — House very cold — Stoked all of the fires, from the furnace to the nursery — & was successful — gas meter suffering from cold — lights peeping — Permit that froze yesterday & was taken away brought back to-day — Charl came in at 8.30 for a moment — Annie & I — in the absence of good light played cards for awhile — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 20, 1885

Clear & cold . Ther. below 20°. Got up with a heavy head — discomforted me all day — Did not get to Studio until 10.30 — then to work painting in immediate foreground on 18×28 — Cliffs near Etretat" — Home at 1.30 back before 3 — Painted for about an hour then washed up — At 5 went to J. & C. Johnston's (after having stopped in at Miss Abbatt's — entered her work & met Miss Humphreys then) & did a little shopping — then to J— S— & called on the [illegible] — [illegible] about the proposed visit to N.Y. of Father, Mother & Kate — Home a few minutes after 6 dinner — Wrote a note to de Schwienitz in answer to one of his — Annie & I played part of a game of Bezique — To bed soon after 10.
