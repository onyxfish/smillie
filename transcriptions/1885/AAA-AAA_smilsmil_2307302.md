## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 8, 1885.

overcast Wt. — warm + close — heavy showers between 3 & 4 P.M. — clearing
& colder to night — To studio at 10.30 +
worked awhile, very unwillingly, on
charcoal sketch "Normandy Barnyard" —
Soon after 12 went to N.A. & learned that
Geo. T. Seuey bought my "Fallow Field" — Then
at 12.45 to 37th St. + saw Father + Mother for
awhile — Home to lunch at 1.30 — after
& fixed cord on parlor curtains & got
to studio soon after 3 — W[m]. Park of Montrose
made a long call — I did a little work
on charcoal sketch. Home soon after 6 —
Found Mrs. (W[m]. Justice) of Germantown) + her
daughter Mary there as expected. Dinner
at 6.30 — Even[ing] in parlor until 9.30 —
To bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 9, 1885.

Brilliant day — after 10 when I got to
studio — worked awhile on charcoal
sketch of Barnyard — mounted it — +
home to lunch at 1.30 after having
made a short call in 37th St. Back to
studio before 3 — did a little work +
before 5 went to 37th St. + made a
second short call on Father + Mother —
Home at 5.30 — went out + got flowers — in
different ways was delayed so that I
was not dressed until 6.40 — W. + Mrs.
Brownell & niece from New Bedford, Miss Cummings
Miss Sedgwick of Syracuse (alone) Mr. Ordway +
W. Lippincott arr[ived] on time — dinner before
7. Things seemed to go off fairly well —
altho' the sauce for the fish was forgotten
until too late — the young ladies were
charming + the young men seemed to be
properly impressed — The older members
were becomingly sedate — After dinner
Walter + wife + Ed[w]d. Cook came in —
Our guests left soon after 10 & we went
at once to bed.
