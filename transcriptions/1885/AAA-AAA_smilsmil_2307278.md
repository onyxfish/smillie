## Left Page

### February, Friday 13, 1885

Overcast mtf. — Snow in the afternoon & at night. Got up with a heavy head. Did not get to studio until 10.30 — could not work — sat for a time in George's Room — Home at 1 — at 2 a Vinil rehearsal which I enjoyed — Snowing heavily on coming out. Went to W.C. Club & had a talk with Ed W. Brown — met Geo. Theis & Mr. Roux. Then to studio — Home at 6 — dinner — at 7.30 took Annie to 110 Mad. Ave. & left her with Jack Downe to go to see some private theatricals — then I went on to 51 W. 10th S. Meeting of Etching Club in Nicoll's Room — Home at 10.30.

Letter Rec'd. from Father saying that now he sleeps in his easy chair, cannot breathe comfortably lying down. Note from S. R. Koehler saying that Cassell & Co. buy my plate "A Fallow Field." Mr. C. A. A. During called in the morning — to day the boy's arm was freed from its confinement — 14 weeks since accident.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 14, 1885

Overcast mtf., driving cold snowstorm afternoon & night. To studio at 10 & to work repainting sea on big picture — soon got into a mess — lost some elements of color in first painting that I was anxious to retain — in other respects, believed it — Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — Afternoon, Mr. Frank Parsons called & I gave him water color bot F. Scrap Book — Carpenter put up sash in closet sky-light — Storm made it so dark that I had trouble in seeing to work. Home at 6 — dinner — Ev'g. with Annie went to Phil — a very delightful concert — but my attention was divided between thinking of Father or my big picture — Home at 10. 30 & right to bed.
