## Left Page

### February, Thursday 5, 1885.

Clear, windy & cold — to studio about 10 & got to work repainting "the Edge of the Wood" — went over it pretty thoroughly, taking from every part of it the hot, foxy color to be found in sky and greens of the landscapes — & painting in cool tones & greys very greatly to the advantage of the picture — Home to lunch as usual at 1 & back at 2.30 — About 4 Mr. Downs & her son Jack called — an elder son Conn was to have called with them but had to return to Boston by 3 o'clk train — Dinner at 6 — Evef with A — went to Daly's & saw "Love on Crutches" — a comical piece — did not think it was very well played — out at 11 — met Mr. & Mrs. Foster Higgins & Mr. & Mrs. Wm Dart — to bed before 12.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 6, 1885.

Clear, cold day — to studio at 10 — and soon got started on second paint ing of my big picture "Cliffs of Normandy" — scraped the sky and painted with fear & trembling being anxious not to lose any of the energy of the first painting — Home to lunch at 1.30 lunch — wrote letter to Cottrell, sending check — back a little before 3 & worked as long as could see over distance — then washed up brushes — rested awhile — home at 6 — dressed — & with A— went to Ed. W. Cook's to dinner — Mr. Jennie Downs & her son Jack at dinner — Mother Cook & Valentine later in the evef — Home soon after 10 — To bed soon after 11.
