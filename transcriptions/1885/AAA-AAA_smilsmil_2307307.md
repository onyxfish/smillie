## Left Page

### April, Saturday 18, 1885

Beautiful spring day — Father complaining but he had a good morning in the m[orning] and a short walk in the afternoon. I got to studio shortly after 10 — feeling, as usual now, very unlike working, but by a little coaxing & a little driving I nearly finished the retracing of "a Normandy Barn Yard" — Home at 1.30 & back at 3 — a little more work — Dr. Yale came in — he wants to make arrangements for a consultation with Dr. Janeway — then Annie came in — just from the Private View of Prize Fund 34th. About 6 she went home & I walked down to 9th S. & 5th Ave. & left some etching P.P. for Ehninger — 7 o'clk. where I got home. — dinner alone — Evelf. Geo. came in & we spent it in dining room. Father appearing quite bright — he went to bed a little before 10 — when A. & I retreated to our rooms & had a game of Basique — to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 19, 1885

Very bright & warm — Bkf: late — Father got up after 10 & sat from 12 to 2 in the sun shine in the open bay window, seeming quite bright. I did not go out. Well, Jos. [illegible] Walter & Charl came in. Charl took Mother up to Elder's to church. Dinner at 2. Chale came at 3 & half an hour later Dr. Janeway came & they made a thorough & careful examination of Father & held a consultation. It was thought best to take Father to draw off some of the effusion. About 4 I walked out with Father to the cor. of Park Ave — down to 37th S., back, & again half way to 37th S. — then home. Of course, we walked very slowly — when we got in Father panted a good deal but said he was not tired — then I went to Mother Cook's where Annie was with the boy — Home about 6 o'clk — then walked up to Central Park. Took a short turn in & back, & at 6.20, stopped at Yale's & arranged with him to come tomorrow m'f. to take Father — Home at 7 & had tea — Evelf. all (but A., who was with the boy) sat in dining room. Charl came in — Father to bed at 10 — I wrote until 11 — to bed soon after.
