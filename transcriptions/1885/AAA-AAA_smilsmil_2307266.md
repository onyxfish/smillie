## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 20, 1885

Up very late — the boy behaved so badly
that Annie did not get her allowance
of sleep during the night — at 6 a.m —
called Bella to take her place — & then
she came to bed with me & we snoozed
until 9 — near 10 when we finished bkf-
& near 11 when I got to studio. Charming
winter day — Worked among my etchings
& W.C. sketches. Young man came from
K's & got 20 pf. from Ley Co. neighbor etc" — &
gave me $20. Home to lunch at 1.30 — after
it, dressed & about 3 went with A. to Met
museum, to see the Watts pictures — Can't
like them in any way — either as allegories
or works of art — in no sense do they appeal
to me — false in color — bad in drawing &
weak in purpose. Nearly 5 when we got to
Mr. Dows, cor 5th Ave & 69th S— Rec'd [illegible] P.m. — to call
on Mr. Rich. Hoe Jnr. Pleasant call — met Mr. Sharpless
there — a rode home, I walked to studio, then home
at 6.20 — Many there from Po'k — to stay over night —
Dinner — Ev'g. chatting until 10.30 — To
bed soon after 11 — Rec. a note from Mr.
Whitney saying that Mr. CO was ill.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 21, 1885

Clear & cold — windy & dirty — To studio
at 10 and started big water color drawing
washing in sky & sea. The same old
disappointments — cannot get simplicity
& breadth — quality or color — the work
soon becomes jagged & petty instead of
having the boldness & freeness of color that
is so fascinating to me. Home to lunch &
back as usual & worked as long as I could
see — Then to Knoedler's & got some colors
home at 6 — Dinner — & at 7.30 went
with Annie to Mother Cook's where I
left her. Geo. C. is ill, at home, & this
ev'g. Dr. [illegible] says his trouble is Dypthéna.
Then I went to W.C. meeting which
was over soon after 9 — and I went home.
Many left us after bkfs. to return to Po'k
on the 3.30 P.m. Train. To bed about
11.
