## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 11, 1885.

Clear — Extremely cold with wind —
To studio late -10.30 — Found Mr.
S. R. Koehler there — wants an etched
plate for a proposed work — Did
not get to work until nearly 12.
Painting on foreground rocks
in big picture — Home at 1 — back
soon after 2 & worked until 4.
Had to stay until 6 while a
man brought up ½ a ton of coal.
Then home to dinner — Every
Read aloud for awhile from
Carnegie's "An Four in ttand
in Birthday" — Then a game of
Bazique & to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 12, 1885.

Milder — fine winter day — Got
to studio shortly after 10 and to
work on big painting, finishing
the second painting of foreground
rocks & going back over the
grass bank etc — Home to lunch
as usual — At 4.30 went over to Y —
M. C. A. & saw Ferguson's new frame
on large picture — then to Gradey's
& at last gave order for my big
frame — At 6.30 went into Dunn's
to see if they could give Father
Mother & Kate rooms if they came
to the city — Home to dinner at 7.
About 9 got to Union League Club.
Monthly meeting — very fine collect-
ion of pictures — my "Edge of the Wood"
hung alongside of a Constable & it
made me very sick — Home at 11
& right to bed — Koehler at the
studio in the m'g. To see if he could
get an etched plate from me for
a new publication.
