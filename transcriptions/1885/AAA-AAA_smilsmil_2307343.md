## Left Page

### June, Monday 29. 1885.

Cold, Raw & Cheerless — a heavy mist, almost like rain — Ther.
down to near 50°. A man came up from Payne & I set him to work cleaning
up the roadway. I was full of work all day — Got Cottrell to come in the
afternoon & started him on the Piazza stairs — Saw Thos. Warner & made an
arrangement with him to let me have Herrick — Dinner about 1.30 — The
missing trunk was delivered at the house, by 4.30, before we were up
in the nt. — After podging about all the p.m. went down town about
6 & got mail — Then home & had tea — Had a good wood fire in main room — but distressed by
the bad behavior of the chimney which has taken to smoking. Sweetf. had
a game of Bazique & to bed soon after 10. Ther. lowest, 48°.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 30. 1885.

Brilliant cold wt. — Ther. below 50°. work all over — had to work hard to keep warm — Fire again, all day —
Two freight cases came after dinner while I was hunting my boiler cleaning the attic Fank. I opened them
& found that the Tiffany glass had come without injury — Spent some time with Mr. Woodice (who has bought the Cooper
place) & a friend of his — They went over part of the house with me & I found them very agreeable. Sweetf. very
tired, but I went down town at 6 & got mail — Tea after 7 — In the Nf.
paper we were much shocked to find the published notice of the death of Julie
Haring on Monday last — a sad loss —
By our smoky fire until near 11 & then to bed —
