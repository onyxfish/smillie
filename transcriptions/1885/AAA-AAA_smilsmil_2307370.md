## Left Page

### August, Saturday 22, 1885

A muggy, overcast specimen of a dog day — neither clouded nor sunshine — intended to go out sketching but nothing favored. Went to Father immediately after help. Found that he had passed another very wretched night &
all were much depressed. I went down town before noon. Dinner about 1, 30 — again, much of the afternoon with Father — I am much concerned about him & wonder if he will have strength to get home. About 6 — Annie, Miss Abbatt & I went down town — we called at Kate Riley's — also on Priscilla Pore — did not see either of them — Home to tea about 7 — Eve'g, chatting & a game of cards. Paid a short visit to Father about 9 — It was near 11 when we got to bed. Brilliantly cloudless moonlight.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 23, 1885

Overcast most of the day with now and then light sprinkles of rain. Got down to see Father before help — found that he had had some rest during the night (Mary sat up with him) but had a spasm of prostration in the w.f. — Home & had help — bet. 8 & 9 — again to Father's soon after 10 — he seemed quite bright & while the others were at church I read to him. Soon after noon he had another very bad turn of distress in his stomach — I stayed with him until after 1 — then home to dinner — Annie & Miss Abbatt went to Episcopal Ch. about 3, we started for Colored Camp's meeting, but it began to rain & I went to bed & had a good nap. Before 6 again went to Father — home to tea at 7 — Eve'g — Annie read aloud to us until about 9.15 — then I went to Father & with Katie's help got him nicely into bed with hopes of a quiet night of rest — then home at 10.
