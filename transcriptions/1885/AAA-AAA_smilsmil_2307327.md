## Left Page

### May, Thursday 28, 1885.

Cool, pleasant day. B'kfst about 8 o'elk
after that reading newspapers for an hour
then out riding, Annie & I with Mr. &
Mrs. Justice — went first to Germantown
& telegraphed to Douglass to send floor finish
to Dana Austin — then rode down the
Wissahickon road & back, getting in at
about 12.30 & soon after lunch. After
in wandering about the place with
Jim. Sat on piazza & had long confab
with, Wm Justice concerning Religious &
Ethical matters — about 5 went out alone
& had a short walk — Mr. & Mrs. Tho.
Justice came in to dinner at 6.30 — Mr.
Wm J., Celia & Miss Dreer went off to
a fête. We spent the evef'in general
chatting until 10. Then Mr. & Mrs. Tho.
went home & we went to bed —
a very cool night.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 29. 1885.

A cool mrg. — wood fire in the
library — about 10 Annie, the boy & I
went to Mr. Tho. Justice's & made a
call — delightful old place — 5 acres of
ground — big, old trees, boxwood, arbor-
vitae hedges, etc. There alone I went
to Wayne Junction & telegraphed home
to have dinner ready for us at 7 — then
to Wm Justice's — Headached & I lay down
on lounge & got a nap — Lunch before 2.
Threats of rain & occasional sprinkles.
Sat about until near 4 — then to
Queen Lane Station — Took 4.3 Train
to Germantown Junction — & there 4
o'elk Train from Broad St., getting in
to N.Y. at about 6.30 — Carriage up
home, delayed waiting to get our luggage
to house at 7.15 & had dinner, having
got through very comfortably — about
8 — Mother Cook & Geo. came in —
To bed soon after 10.
