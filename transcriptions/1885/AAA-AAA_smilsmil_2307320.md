## Left Page

### May, Thursday 14, 1885

Nasty day — cold with rain in gusty showers — bfb — about 8 — abg went out &
got a cab to take Father & Kate to the
N.A. where Geo. met them & Father
spent an hour looking at the pictures.
I got to Met. museum soon after 10 &
finished Arnold & drew hills — then
home at 1 & had lunch. Ed. came
in while at lunch & arranged to meet
Father & Kate at 4 train. I went to depot
& got Drawing R.C. seats — back — [illegible] at
the house — At near 3 I went back to
museum & worked until 6. drawing
Argyle & a second head of Swinburne —
then home — weather pleasanter. Father
& Kate got off all right under George's
care — Dinner — at 8.30 to Union League
Water Color Show — home in an hour —
Collines at the house — after he left
we had a game that kept us until
11, then to bed.

During the day, met at Met. Mus.
Miss Hobart; Elisha & Dr. Mulford and
Sellstedt.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 15, 1885

Clear & hot; to studio soon after 10 — to
Am. Art Galleries at 11 & saw Kurtz — gave him
five process drawings from the Watts heads —
met Misses Annie Putnam & Mary Burnham
there & spent about an hour with
them; then to studio & painted on the
centre, middle-ground, group of trees in
"View near Crowninshield's Point, near
Marblehead". Home to lunch at
1.30 — back at 2.45 — again fussed
with the painting until 4.30 — then
packed things preparatory to a
trip to Julia Sprues's at Harvey, Mo.
Home at 6.30 — dinner. Evelft
wrote letters to Col. Renties Ingraham —
Father — Cottrell, Dana Austin, and
Frank Herrick about finishing Montrose
floors — posted them before 10 — then
played half a game of Basque with
Annie & to bed before 11.
