## Left Page

### February, Friday 27, 1885

Snow flying or floating all day with nothing to show for it at night. Got up with such a heavy head that I determined not to go out, so I spent the day in doors — The Adams girls came in just after we had had lunch & Annie went with them to the W. C. Rptr. — Late in the afternoon I felt very wretched — a condition that seemed to culminate before 9 o'clk. After that, A & I had a game of Bagatelle where I had an extraordinary run of luck — wish I could have the same luck in getting well. To bed about 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 28, 1885

In bed all day with a horrible headache & a general feeling of cussedness. Annie sent for [illegible] & he came between 10 & 11 — I stayed in bed until about 10 o'clk at night, then up, washed & dressed & gave my bed an airing — then to lounge in Annie's bed room until 4 A.m. — then to my own bed again & got some sleep before 9 o'clk.

Geo. Hearn & wife celebrated their Silver wedding.
