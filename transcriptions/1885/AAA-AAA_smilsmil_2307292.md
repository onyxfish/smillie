## Left Page

### March, Thursday 19. 1885.

Cold & wintry — flying snow most of the m'f. & pale sunlight — a heavy snow-flurry lasting an hour or more, after 2 o'k, then clear & cold. Annie used up by a bad headache. To studio soon after 10. All the m'f. spent working on W.C. "In an Orchard — Normandy". Washed out a place for a foreground tree trunk on the R.h. end & put that in — also some general work, greatly to the advantage of the picture, I think. Home at 1.15 — after lunch, wrote notes to Mr. Watrous & Mother Cook concerning Annie's tickets to the Diet Kitchen Parlor concert this ev'g. To studio after 3 & spent the p.m. working on "Bluff on Marblehead Neck." W.C. Kurtz came in & I promised to work for him to-morrow that. At 6 went up & saw Father for a few minutes. Think that he is slowly improving — Home & took dinner alone a letter before 7 — Annie had her dinner while in her bed. Spent most of the ev'g writing — Finished letter to Mr. Whitney. To bed after 11.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 20. 1885.

Very cold & windy. To studio soon after 10 — about 10.30 went over to N.A. and commenced work for Kurtz on his ill. cat. made sketch after Annie — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked until after 5 — made sketches after Bryant & Murphy. Found my Cliffs in the N.W. corner of the South Room — in some respects a very good position — during a portion of the m'f. the sunlight falls directly upon it. The frame casting a shadow on the sky. It disappoints me — it is not crisp, fresh or strong — there is something common place in the color that I cannot define. I could not find my small painting "A fallow field". To studio at 6 — then home — dinner at 6.30 — At 8 went to see Father & found him still improving — though slowly. Clark came in while I was there. Home soon after 9 — had a game of Bazique with Annie & to be soon after 11.
