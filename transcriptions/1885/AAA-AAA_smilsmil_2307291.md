## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 17. 1885.

Very cold & clear. Ther. did not get above 18° all day & at times was much lower. To studio at usual time & to work on pen sketches for N.A. & Am. Art Ass. Ill. Cats. Finished one commenced some time ago of "Cliffs of Normandy" & worked some time on "A Stranger in a Strange Land" — 1.30 when I got home to lunch & I when I got back to studio — then destroyed the Stranger sketch & the [illegible] & made another, much smaller & better — At 4.30 gave up & went to Grady's with sketch for tablet for Stranger. Then to Father's — getting there about 5.30 — Altho' he complains very much of a vile taste in his mouth & no appetite I think he is decidedly better — He sleeps a great deal — very soundly & with much comfort. Stayed about an hour then home to dinner about 8. Took A. to Miss Rodgers, 55 W. 22nd St. where she spent the eve of piano playing — I went to studio, & tried to write a letter to Mr. Geo. Whitney — did not finish it — Soon after to went for Annie — home about 11 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 18. 1885.

A very pleasant day, altho' cold — Got to studio about 10 & to work on W.C. "in an orchard — Normandy" — don't like the composition — made tracing for & then, upon tracing, made changes — introducing foreground trees upon the left & right hand sides — then carefully washed off the color where I wished to paint the tree trunk, getting the paper quite clean — Putting the l.h. one very successfully I think & greatly to the advantage of composition — home at 2 & had lunch — then to work in a general way helping to get things ready for the reception that A. gives to our friends this afternoon — from about 4.30 to 7 the house was thronged — A. with Mary Sprague Cook &c. in the parlor. Fanny H. & Mary J. did the honors of the table in the Reception Room — everything seemed to go off very nicely — the ladies looked well — the boy behaved well — the fires & the house generally looked well — the crowds of friends seemed to enjoy it — at 7.30 I sat down alone to cold beef & baked potatoes & at 8.10 I went to annual meeting of the Water Color Society — full meeting — as usual, Hopkinson Smith was lively & urged the society to go into the advertising business — it was nearly 11 before adjournment — then went right home & to bed — I was elected member of the Board of Control for the ensuing year.
