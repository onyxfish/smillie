## Left Page

### July, Saturday 25, 1885

*at Beede's — Keene Valley*
*Room 16*

Overcast, promising to be cooler — B'kf.
about 7.30 — at 8 we got away in a
very comfortable vehicle — for the
greater part of the way the temperature
was delightful but when we got over
into Keene Valley the sun shone out &
it was blistering hot — Got to Beede's at
12 much oppressed by the heat —
Dinner at 1 — then met Kate Hilliard
In spite of the great heat we went
to our 4 acre lot & spent an hour
there under the pines — At 5 went
to Kate H's & had Russian tea — To
the hotel at 6 & had American tea —
then met Miss Eliza Torry — Every
we again went to Kate H's where
we met Misses Cornell & Mr. Chapin
Mr. White Col. Loring & wife, Messrs
Smith & Martin & Dr. Bull — The
mail came about 10 when we
got a Postal card from home —
Then to bed —

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 26, 1885

Decidedly cooler — much of the
day overcast — Spent the m'g on
Miss Torry's piazza writing letters,
she leaving us in possession — At 10.30
the Sunday Tribune was delivered —
at noon I took a nap & at 1 had
dinner — After, about 3.30 Annie
& I went to Mr. White's & made a
short call — then again to our lot,
thence over Windy Brow — down to the
brook & along to the foot of Roaring Brook
fall — we climbed thence to the top
& went across through the woods &
out on Beede Brook back of the old
house — Back to tea at 6.30 — after
that a rest — then in dining room
wrote letter to George — To bed after
a sharp walk on the piazza to get
ourselves warm — The night really
quite cold.
