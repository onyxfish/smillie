## Left Page

### April, Thursday 16, 1885

Bright day, but rather cool — [illegible]
a little later than usual. It was
10.30 before I got to 37th S. Found Kate
scurrying about, packing to leave.
After 11 when I got to studio — I
fussed about, doing nothing in particu-
lar — went to Bank & deposited $40.
Rec. from Kurtz — then again to 37th S. and
advised Kate to take Father up to Central
Park for a ride on their way over to our
house, then home to lunch & at 3
back to studio — a long call from Dr.
Yale relative to Father's case — Home
about 6 — Found Father, Mother &
Kate there — after a very pleasant ride
in the Park — Father particularly bright —
Evef Kate & I went to Chorus Society Club
Concert at Steinway Hall. Heard Mackenzie's
Dramatic Oratorio "The Rose of Sharon" — found
it rather monotonous for a stretch of 3 hours —
Portions of it pleased me very much — I would
have been much better pleased if only the 3d & 4th parts
had been given — Kate sat beside Mrs. Kimball &
I sat behind her & beside Mr. Chas. Stewart Smith
Home at 11.30 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 17, 1885

Brilliant day & very nice — got to studio
soon after 10 — there for a few minutes — then
went over to Am. Art Ass'n, Varnishing Day
for "Prize Exhib." Four $2500 prizes — Taken by
Gifford, Alex. Harrison Hamilton. Mosler & Boggs —
met many friends & spent the rest of
the mf in chatting & looking at the
pictures — a very good collection —
Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 3.15
— wrote a letter to Cottree & sent him
a check — entertained a couple of
callers & at 6 went home, Geo. walking
up with me & making a call on Father &
mother. Dinner at 6.30 — Evef Kate
went with Charl to Daly's Theatre &
I chatted with father until near 10
when he went to bed.
