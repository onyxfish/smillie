## Left Page

### July, Friday 31, 1885

*Delevan House. Albany. Room 88.*

Up at 6.30 — glorious mts. Dep. at 7.15 & at 7.50 off for
Port Henry on the new road. Pleasant good-byes from a
number of our friends. The ride for 6 miles through the
woods, simply delicious — air cool & fresh — Road good
& glimpses of Rock — forest depth — Stream & pond too
much for description — then we came upon the old
Road & a burnt district extending 9 miles — second
never saw before such a display of fire-weed in bloom,
growth only a few years old — a dismal side — the
sunshine very hot — 6 miles from Port Henry we came
to Mineville or Moriah where we had a superb view
of Champlain & the Green Mts. beyond. A pleasant
breeze tempered the heat & we enjoyed the view
down to the very depot. 25 minutes ahead of the
— blistering hot at depot.
Train due at 12, having driven the 20 m. in 3 h. 45 m.
Got to 12. at 12.35 having been chased most of the way
by a thunderstorm — 3/4 of an hour for dinner, served on
the steamboat "Cornwall." Storm caught us & made the
rest of the ride delightfully cool & dustless — Got to
Saratoga at 3.35 — Dit Howe met us at the depot
where we waited about 15 m. Had quite a visit —
Got to Albany at 5.10 & went at once to the
Delevan Hts., asked for a quick room — then
we went out for a little shopping — Annie bought
a book & I bought a pair of glasses & some awning cord
about 7 we had tea & about 8.30 we went to bed.
Before 10 a hotel employee insisted upon coming
in to smell if our gas was escaping — the night
was one of pandemonium — every possible
noise seemed to be actively engaged & we got
very little sleep in our "quiet" room.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 1, 1885

*At home again.*

Got up about 6.45 & had our bkfst.
about 7.30 — then, by 8.25 train started for
Binghampton — For the first 2 hours or
so, it was very pleasant, the air cool
& but very little dust, after that it
got rapidly hotter & dirtier until 2.20
when we got to Binghampton quite
used up. At Oneonta, at 12.5 we stopped
10 m. for lunch. At Bing., at 12.50 a D. L. & W.
train passed eastward — I interviewed the
conductor to get him to stop with us at New
Milford, but without success — so we had to
wait until 6.20 for another train — we took
a ride on horse cars across the Chenango &
back — then we waited in depot for some
time & at 5 we went to the
— waited until 5.45 & then had supper
— then to depot — a picnic of children on
their way home to "the bend" — Got to New
Milford soon after 7 — Mr. Somers came to us
to point out her baby — Prall waiting for us
— a pleasant drive home — getting in at 8.30
all hands waiting — Jim just — very much pleased —
After a little I went down & saw father & folks —
then home & had a good scrub & got to
bed about 10.30 — Trip cost $109.58.
