## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 20, 1885

According to Ther. which recorded above 80°, a very warm day — but so tempered by breezes that I was not incommoded. After 10 I got to work on the Saratoga pen work — on the Arnold Head Quarter House. Had to take a nap before dinner after 2 — At about ¼ before 4 Annie & Julie went out in a buggy & I accompanied them riding a mare that had a little & very easy trot. We went to Trappe & then around by the mills, a ride fro or 12 miles — some interesting bits, altho the country is very flat & poorly cultivated. I had rather more of a ride than I wished — but got in in very good condition at 6.40 — then an entire change of clothing & a good scrub down — Tea soon after 7. &rest, rested awhile on bed, then to parlor. — flossed an intruding bat — To bed at 10.30.
Glorious moonlight night.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 21, 1885

Overcast & cooler — Brkf. at 7.30 — Read for awhile after it — then wrote to Geo. Austin & finally got to my work. A little nap before my dinner at 2.30. At work again in the afternoon tracing & retracing the Saratoga Monument. It was after 5 when I went out in with Annie & little row boat — we pulled up an arm of the bay to North & East of the house. Found some good bits of tree groups. Sky overcast & color sober — 7.20 when we got back to the house & found that the family had had tea — &rest in parlor. Miss Lizzie Platt (Charley P.'s sister) came from N.Y. on the 10 P.M. Train — After her arrival at 10.30 we went to bed — a very cool night.
