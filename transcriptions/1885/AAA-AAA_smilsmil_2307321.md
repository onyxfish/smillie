## Left Page

### May, Saturday 16. 1885.

Beautiful day. Bkft. at usual time. Went to studio about the usual time 10 o'clk. + put things in order for leaving — bid Geo. good bye + back home at 11 — then packed + at 12 went out + ordered carriage + &c. Lunch soon after 1 — Carriage came at 3 — got to foot of Desbrosses St. at 3.30 — intended to take 4 o'clk train but almost without knowing it we took the 3.40 train. At Germantown Junction had to wait for train to Queen Lane, so that we got there no sooner than if we had come by 4 o'clk train — To Queen Lane at 6 — Edith there + we were at once driven to Hazelbrook — Miss Barrington, visiting Edith + Geo. W. Ames there — Mr. Ames took tea with us — Annie had to leave the table, being completely used up by a violent sick headache from which she suffered very much during the evefg. The boy also got excited + unmanageable in his strange quarters — so I had my hands full — but things got settled + soon after 10 I got to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 17. 1885.

Very pleasant day — awakened very early by the birds — but got to sleep again. Up + had bkft. at 8.30 — then out + spent some time going about with Annie — the boy and Edith (Jas. Wright + Marion in N.Y.) The boy wild with delight. Spent a part of the aftf. looking over Boughton + Abbey's Holland — Mos. book which much amused me. — My head began to ache + I had a nap before dinner at 2. — At 3.30 we went out to ride, Annie, Miss Barrington, Jas., the driver + I. — along the Wissihickon Road + back over Chestnut Hill — back before 6. — I was so used up by my headache that I went at once to my room + threw myself on the lounge — The pain became intense + I suffered as I had not for a long time until 10 when it began to abate — then Annie came to bed but I was not able to follow her until 1 o'clk in the morning.

Jas. Wright came home in the evefg. The Wrights took dinner with us.
