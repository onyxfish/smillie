## Left Page

### September, Sunday 27, 1885

A very hot day. The Ther. in
doors, going up to 78°. Last
night it did not go below 54°.
Up late — bkfst. at 9 — All the
mf with Annie. Part of the
time writing letters — Eliz —
Fanny & Belle ? — went to ch.
Dinner after 2 — Afternoon
hammocks & cool places in
demand — I did some more
writing — Put about 20 pails
of water on new sodding after
sunset — tea about 7 —
Evef. finished my letters
and at 10 o'clk went with them
to the P.O. — Then home & to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 28, 1885

Again cloudless & very warm — Ther.
up to 76°. Last night it did not go
below 58°. Up soon after 7 — The Hawleys
came to begin laying the wall on line
between our lot & Fruk. & Eldred. Some
trouble about determining exact line
& I went down town to get Cha. Ruau
who surveyed the lot to come & establish
corners but he had gone off on a Road View.
So, told the men to go ahead on old line.
After 9 when I got my bkfst. — then many
things to attend to — it was 11 before
got to my work on Study on Fair Ground
Rocks — Stuck to it until almost 2. —
Then home to dinner — a little work
on "Mc Esmerelda" — & overlooking men
at work — felt pretty well eased up.
Tea at 6.30 — Then dressed — at 7.30
went to see Eldred about getting a
slice from his lot — At 8 went with Eliz by
Fanny & Belle, by invitation to Parks &
met the de Schweinity s — Mr. & Mrs. Bur Richards
Mr. & Mrs. Hy Riley — & Mrs. Gast — played whist.
Home at 11 & to bed at midnight.
A very encouraging letter from Kate
concerning Father.
