## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 23, 1885

Stormy m'f — wind & driving rain until after 10 — then changed to high
wind from N.W. & growing very cold —
Payne, not at work — Coyle after 11 —
the others, full day. I kindled fire
in studio furnace & on the hearth
— delightfully comfortable — Tried to
get to work on A.B.N. Co's drawings
— made little progress with v'g't. of
war vessel "Esmerelda". Dinner
at usual time — again at work
when Bella came to tell me that
Annie was not well — found her in
bed — alarmed by some evidences
pointing to a miscarriage — Tel. to
Yale — got letter from Mary — met
Collins down town & we walked
home — Tea — Ev'eff. Part of time
with Annie, having made a fire in
the bed room — wrote notes to Father
& Dr. Yale. Walked with Collins
to P.O. at 10 — brilliant, cold
night — just touched 32°.
To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 24, 1885

Pleasant day, altho' unsettled —
growing warmer — windy — Mr.
Collins left us by 9 o'clk. Stage.
I spent the m'f in manifold
duties until 10.30 o'clk — then to
my studio & worked on war str
"Esmerelda" for a while & for a
pleasant change, on charcoal
sketch on canvas — Up a tree for
awhile before dinner cutting rt
dead wood — Afternoon, again
in studio — about 5 went out to
Warner's woods to study on a sub-
ject there — after 6 when I went
to P.O. got Yale's letter giving
a prescription for Annie — Near
when I got back — Tea — Ev'eff
we sat in bed room reading —
I wrote note to H.P. Bellows —
Annie has kept quiet on her
back all day. Has some symp-
toms that give her increased
uneasiness —
To bed at 11.
