## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 1, 1885.

Pleasant day — breezy & bright —
blkp — after 8 & at 9.30 to hotel.
Father extremely weak — Char
& Will came in — Before 11 I
went to Newman's — then to Noony's
& sent a fowl to Mother Cook to
make broth — then to studio and
bank — to Sweet Clover farm office
& got a battle of milk & took it
home to Father — Took lunch
with Mother & Kate — Sat with
Father until near 4, then out
& strolled to new building at
G.C. Depot — back in about an
hour — Char. & Will & E² came
in — At 6.30 went to 39th S. & had
dinner about 7. Back to S.C.
soon after 8 — Father very dull
during the evef. Swollen feet
cracking & giving him trouble.
Back to 39th S. about 10 & to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 2, 1885.

*At home. Montrose.*

Up before 6 o'clk — a clear, cold
mf. Slipped out of 104 E. 39th without
seeing any one & got over to St. Cloud
before 7 o'clk. No change in Father.
At 7.30 went down to blkp — with Mother
& Kate. Sat with Father for awhile
after that, but he seemed very drowsy
& disinclined to talk. At 8.15 I bid
him good bye — a sad trial to me, as
I fear that I will never see him
again alive — Rode down in 7th Ave. car
— mdo Weldon — crossed Christopher St.
ferry & took 9 o'clk train — got to M.
depot at 3. Buggy & overcoat in
waiting — very acceptable — home
at 4.30 — All out — at a Bap tist
fair — Annie — Miss Abbatt — Marion
Wright & Misses Hagbaum & Clark —
soon came in — Tea at 6.30 —
Uncle W. & Aunt A. came in when
we were at tea to get latest news
from Father — Evef. wrote a
letter to Father & took it to P.O.
before 9 — To bed soon after 10.
