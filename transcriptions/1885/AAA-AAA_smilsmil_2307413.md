## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 18, 1885

Pleasant, altho' cloudy. After hlf. went
to Mother Cook's & got a check for A—
to make good the 16000 dollar dollars
that she is to pay Sears, mortgage on 10
E. 38th. It was after 10 when I got to
studio — Finished sketch for new draw-
ing for West Shore — Young man came for
it — then to work with graver on Fattur-
ion die — worked tirelessly — re-cutting
& giving force in portions. Went to
Bank & Goupil's after going in to see a
big Pastel by Chambrey — Home to lunch
at 1.45 — back at 3 — worked on die until
4.15 — then to Water Color Socy & Bd. of Control
Wood's Studio. 51 W. 10 — Home at 6 — dinner
at 8 at N.A. of D. Water Color Socy meeting —
Home about 10.15 — To bed soon after.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 19, 1885

Mild, pleasant u.t. — then cloudy grow-
ing colder — bet. 5 & 6 P.m. a driving rain
from N.E. — later, clear moonlight — Aunt
Ollie died at Plainfield at 2 o'clk. This
m.t. after a lingering & very painful ill-
ness, supposed to be cancer of the stomach.
To studio at 9.30 — worked on Fatteirion
die until 12.30 — then down town to A.B.N. Co —
Took lunch with Will — got pf. of die and
arranged about new West Shore drawing — then
back to studio at 3 — Fixed die pfs — then
J.C. Nicoll came in — Polished up two
framed pfs. of "A corner at home" & "The delayed
traveler" & took them to Mr. Dewis 112 E. 16 St.
for a Dietkitchen Fair to be held there to-
morrow — Saw Mr. L. & her sister — Raining
hard — home at 5.30 — dinner at 6 —
Evg. Geo. C. came in with cancelled
mortgage on Annie's house — later
Wm. Parks came in — stayed until 10
— then I got to bed — Annie
continues, apparently, the same.
