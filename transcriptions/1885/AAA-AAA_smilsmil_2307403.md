## Left Page

### October, Thursday 29, 1885

*at home — 110 E. 38th St.*

overcast, with a strong east
wind — B&p before 8 — Pitched
in to lots of things — Herrick at
hand putting up shutters — some of
the Smiths working on Main
Room floor. I finished packing
my trunk & got to packing cases
away before 10 — Dinner at 12 ½ —
Pettus took away trunks — abt. 1 ½
carriage came & abt. 2 we left.
Raw wind & a few drops of rain —
got to New Milford in good time
& took 3:21 train — uneventful
journey — Annie feeling very well
& the boy behaving properly — Rain
set in about 5 o'clk & abt 9.15 we
got to Hoboken in a heavy storm —
Poole carriage waiting for us —
Got home before 10 — Mother,
Cook & Valentine there, waiting
for us — a cheerful fire — It
was about 11 o'clk, when we
went to bed in our new bed
room, off the parlor —

## Right Page

### October, Friday 30, 1885

Dull day — n.w. wind & at night
rain & snow — Up late & did not
have b&p until 10 — Started out
about 11 — went to Gurney's 5/p — then
to Simpson & Macaulay's — ordered a
lot of groceries — then to Nooney's
— ordered meat — then to studio
& saw Geo. & Nellie — then to A.B.N.G.
— lunched with Will. got $25 for
Esmeralda drawing — went to
Equitable & paid prem. on life
ins. then to 2d. Nat. Bk. & got
A.B.N. Co's check cashed — then to
studio & hung curtains — then to
barber's & had hair cut — & home
about 5 — Chas came in about
6 & took dinner with us. Before
he left Geo. came in — I wrote
a short letter to Father — took
it to G.C.D. at 9.30 — then home &
to bed, very tired — Dr. Yale called
at the house during the day —
Annie seems to continue per-
fectly well, showing no ill
effects from her journey.
