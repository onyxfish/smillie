## Left Page

### September, Friday 11, 1885

Another raw, dull day — up at 7 &
went down town & to butcher's & got
something for bkfst — which we had
soon after 8 — Man came from Foundry
& put up piece of band iron on Studio
fireplace to keep tiles in place — I
etched on Jones die for awhile,
then, after 12, up a tree & sawed
out dead wood until dinner, soon
after 1. Again etched & finished
Jones die & varnished it — then
out with Coyle, directing work on
walks & putting up iron supports
for vines. Down town soon after 6
& back to tea at 7 — Good reports
still continue from Father — Every
read newspaper. To bed at
usual time.

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 12, 1885

Still overcast, but milder, wind
from the south, with sunshine at
times — all day fighting with a
headache but I kept busily at
work — most of the time in the chim-
ney & fireplace of main room, try-
ing to make sure against possibility
of fire getting through the single layer
of bricks back of mantelpiece — also
setting some of the tiles which have
slipped from their places. Dinner
soon after 1 — All the afternoon out
among the beech trees with my
hatchet clearing away dead
bark & wood & clearing out the
moist places, full of snails and
creeping things — Tea about 6.30
Every read paper for awhile
& went to bed about 9.30.
