## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 24, 1885

And yet another beastly day —
and no change apparent in
Annie's condition. To studio before
10 & to work on second drawing
of West Point — Sending for West Shore
Bonds. Home to lunch at 1 — back
at 2.30 — much of my short after
noon taken by a call from Jewell
who wants to know more about
etching. Berrian came in &
sent a man who fixed the lock
of my door — Home at 5. dinner
at 6 — Evef. put up some
of the portieres — looked over back
Nos. of Ill. Lond. News & got to
bed before 11.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 25, 1885

*Geo. Cook married —*

Gloomy day. To studio before 10 &
to work on 2d. West Shore drawing
until 2 o'clk. This is a whitewashed
thing — then home to lunch — left
the house at 3 30 & went to 250 E.
61st St. Mr. Sandmairs — bro.-in-law of
Miss Smiley Hunt; Geo. Cook's fiancée
The wedding was set for 4 sharp,
but it was 4.20 before the couple
marched in — no bridesmaids or
groomsman. Robt. Collyer performed
the ceremony — With very few ex-
ceptions, only members of the families
were present — an excellent sup-
per from 5 to 6 — Then I walked
down home — About 6.45 we were
surprised by a call from Geo. C. & Mrs.
Geo. C. on their way to the train — they
called on Annie (who continues without
change) Evef. looking over back
Nos of Ill. Lond. News. Wrote a note
to Nate & at 10 took it to G.C. Depot.
