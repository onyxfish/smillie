## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 26, 1885

Most of the day clear & hot. Ther.
got to 84° on the cool side of the
house. B'kf't at us usual time — then
for some time walked on piazza —
to work about 9.30 in garden party
& stuck to it pretty closely all the
m't. A little recreation under
the trees before dinner at 2.15.
After dinner went out to see some
of the sheep that I had seen shorn
in the m't. got a dip in a barrel of
solution of Tobacco & sulphur — Worked
until near 5 there out with Postm
Julie & Annie — Rode over to Lloyd's
Spring — a very Picturesque Spring
of iron water in a dell in a wood —
Home at 7 & tea — Eve't a recitation
from Macbeth by Margery & [illegible] —
then on piazza in beautiful moon-
light until 11 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 27, 1885

*at Mr. Com Justice's Georgetown —*

Close & muggy m't, but not unpleasant. B'kf't before 8 — then packed up and
got ready for our departure — at 10 I was
ready & went to my work on Garden
party & after 2 hours work finished it —
Dinner at 1 — & at ¼ before 2 we left
the house with immediate prospect of
a shower, which did not come however,
got to station & found Mr. & Mrs. Recker
there, also on their way to N.Y. Train on
time — 2.23 — & we were off — Detained
at Clayton 35 m. — Due at Phil'x at 6.50 —
got in at 7.15 — after passing through considerable rain — had tea in Station Res't at 8. Took Train to Truesdane getting
there at 8.19 — Mr. J. carriage met us at the
station — driven at once to the house and
warmly welcomed — the boy pretty well
tired out was got right to bed — we
chatted for an hour or so & at 10 o'clk.
we too went to bed.
