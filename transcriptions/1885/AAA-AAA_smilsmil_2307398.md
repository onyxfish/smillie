## Left Page

### October, Monday 19. 1885.

Still threatening, but no storm. All day out, most of the time smearing the trees & myself with tar — Pettus hauling manure — 8 loads — also 1 load from Cum. Afternoon, cleaned out & filled hollow in the top of a beech near the house, with tar & small stones — After 6 when I went down town — Evg. — after a little reading, spent some time in fire place in main room, working over again with the asbestos cement. About 10.30 when I got to bed — very mild night with strong Southerly wind. Ther. did not go below 55°.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 20. 1885.

Another day of high wind from South East — a shower in the mg. — another in the evg. — again spent the mg. in the trees — again smeared myself with tar — Pettus hauling leaf mould from Cooley's place. & at last Payne's boys have got the stone off the graded lot. Down to P.O. about 6, stopping at Aunt H's, she expects to start for Po'k. to-morrow — at P.O. got a letter from Dan. Brewster, lawyer, asking if he shall sue me for the $150 that Coon claims — still very discouraging news from Father — it distracts & depresses me — Annie improves, but it will not be safe for her to move yet.

Evg., we had a game of Bazique — then I spent some time again in main room fire place. After 11 when I got to bed.
