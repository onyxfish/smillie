## Left Page

### December, Saturday 26, 1885

Very cold & blustering — near 10 when I got to studio — at once I tried & succeeded in getting a good aqua-tint granulation over a part of my plate — bit it & there succeeded in getting granulation over the rest of my plate except the sky — Home to lunch & back as usual — bit second granulation — then spent the rest of the day trying to get a granulation over the little bit of bare sky but could not do it — Puzzled almost to madness to know where the trouble lies — Home at 6, very much disgusted — dinner — wife read aloud for awhile, & as usual soon succeeded in putting Annie to sleep — then wrote awhile — then had a game of cards.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 27, 1885

Brilliant and cold — It was not until near noon that I went out — then I walked down to the studio, possessed with the idea that at last I knew why I could not get an aqua-tint granulation to suit me — but an hour of experimenting proved that I was as far as ever from the solution of my perplexities — Home & had dinner at 2 — the afternoon passed in dozing & reading — Tea about 7 — Wrote wrote my letter home to mother & just before 10 took it to G. Debat — then home & to bed very uncomfortably sick during the night —
