## Left Page

### March, Thursday 5, 1885

Another pleasant day — got to studio about 10 + to work on big picture, putting in gulls + other little work, waiting for my frame which did not come until after I had gone home for lunch. back at 2.30 — got picture into frame which is painfully brilliant + new. Cleared up things about the studio + after H, Annie, her Mother — Mary (Ned) + Marie Cook came to see the "Cliffs" — I walked up with Annie + together we went + made a short call on Father + Mother — they were at our house in the n'f. Yale made a careful examination of Father in the afternoon — second visit — Home to dinner at 6.15 — Eve'g reading until 9 — I gave my poor feet a bathing + to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March. Friday 6. 1885

Pleasant day, but with wind from the east + other indications of change. To studio soon after 10 — the outside oak member for my big frame was sent to studio + proved to be too small by 5/8 of an inch — after considerable fusing + loss of time it was sent back. I worked for awhile in spite of confusion, on pen + ink reduction of big picture — Home at 1.30 — back at 3 — Carmen came + took big picture to Century. Rec'd note from Howland of Toronto, asking me to send etching p'f's including "Stiel on Copper" to [illegible] there. Wrote to him + Koehler; finished pen sketch. Home at 6.15 — dinner — At 7.30, with Annie went to 3rd St. — saw Father + Mother for a few minutes. Then took Kate with us to the Casino + saw "Patience" revived — Enjoyed it altho' it was not given with much spirit — saw Digby Bell for the first time — pleasant voice — no actor — Ripley seemed quite used up. Home at 11 + to bed at once.
