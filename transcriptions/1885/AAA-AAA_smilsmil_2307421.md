## Left Page

### December, Friday 4, 1885

*Father died —*

Pleasant m't. Got to studio about
9.30 — a few minutes after 10, a boy
came with tel. message from Kate
"Father worse — come immediately"
I rushed for the 10.30 train & just
caught it — met Will & Charl there,
Geo. came on 2.30 train — We got to
the house ¼ before [illegible] o'clk. Father called
& recognized us — patting our hands
& my head, saying with great difficulty;
"good boys — good boys" — a little later
he said "I see me" — these were the
last words he said — About 1.20 we
went to the dining room for something
to eat & back in 20 m — Above I
saw that the end had come. Father
tried to raise himself. Will & prang
to his right side & took his hand —
many & I to the left side — many took
his hand while I supported his head.
Kate — Ed & Miss Halton were at the foot
of the bed — Mother & Charl were still
down stairs — The struggle was very
brief & hardly perceptible — He opened
his eyes wide for a minutes & then closed
them — at last I removed the pillows
that had propped him up & laid
him back — the brows contracted, then
a placid expression followed and
my dear father was asleep in Jesus.
What a sad happiness that I had
been allowed to hold his dear head
in that moment — Mother bore the
shock wonderfully — I went at
once for Post, the undertaker — Will
& I went out for a little air —

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 5, 1885

Heavy wind & rain storm in the
m't. lifting in the afternoon. Got
to studio before 10 & spent the m't
compiling from Father's autobiographical sketch: "A Pilgrimage, a slight
sketch — T. A. Richards came in
about noon for particulars, in act
of A. A. of D — Home to lunch at 1.30
then to Trib. office with sketch &
saw Hitchcock — got to studio
about 4 — home at 5 — dinner.
Evng. Dr. Yale came & stayed
until after 9 — got to bed
about 10.30.
