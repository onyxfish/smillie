## Left Page

### June, Saturday 27, 1885.

Up before 6 — Bkf. about 7 — at 7.30
Hts. man came for trunks — Mother
Cook came in — a rued flurry to get
everything in shape to leave — & a few
minutes after 8 we locked the doors &
by 4th Ave. cars & Christopher St. line we got
to the ferry — only 5 of our 6 trunks there —
train (9 ock) off on time — drawing room
car full — an exceedingly hot, dirty
ride without incident — Dinner at
Scranton & got to M. depot at 3 — found
four, 2 horse teams there waiting for us —
the result of a mistake — Annie, Ruby,
Bella, Kate & I got into one — the trunks
& Jim Goodwin in another & the other
two went empty — got to town at 4, 45 —
& soon after a heavy shower, lasting
nearly an hour, made a pleasant change —
found Cottrell's house opened — fire in
stove — ice in Refrigerator — waiting for us —
At 6.30 I went down town to do a little
shopping — Called on the Parks to thank them
for an invitation to dinner — Home & had
dinner about 7.30 — & soon after 10 got
to bed thoroughly tired.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 28, 1885.

Up late & bkf. about 9 — a hot day —
wandered over the place & did some
unpacking in doors. About noon got
the boy to sleep in a hammock on the
piazza where he slumbered placidly
until near 2 when a heavy rain storm
came up which continued at intervals
for more than two hours. Ther. fell very
rapidly — continued cloudy until eve'g.
I wrote a letter to Father — a note to [illegible]
— also to Gurney about the missing 6th
trunk — About 6 Annie & I went out —
A. to Aunt H's & I down town to mail
letters — then on to Payne to get him to
do some work for me — It was after 7
when I got back to Aunt H's for A. — then
home & had tea — to bed about 10
