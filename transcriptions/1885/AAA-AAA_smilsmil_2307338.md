## Left Page

### June, Friday 19, 1885

*Walter Cook's · at Bellport.*

Very warm day — got up with so bad a headache that I feared that I would not be able to go to Bellport. After bkfs. I went to Studio — then in to Tiffany's & saw Louis about getting a little more glass — Home at 11 & lying down, I got one or two naps — Dr. Adams & Nelie came in — Lunch soon after 1 — I wrote a note to Col. Ingraham. At 2.45 I went to G.C.D. & sent cab for Annie & Bella & I took 4th Ave. car to foot of 34th St. — where we all met & crossed the ferry — on the train met Walter — 3 excursion tickets to Bellport $9 00 Drawing Room seats $1.50 extra. A warm dusty ride. 3.20 from N.Y. side — got to B. port at 5.30 — W's carriage there & we rode at once to his place — on piazza until dinner soon after 7 — . Eve'g lounging & chatting until after 10 when I got to bed, having kept up in spite of headache.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 20, 1885

Beautiful day, bright & cool — Bkfs'. after 8 — After a time spent on the piazza, Walter, Annie & I went down to the beach & wandered about for some time, back about noon — then I took a nap. Lunch at 1 — Soon after 2, Walter Annie & I, (with Ed W.) were driven to the dock at Town where a 5½ ton sloops was in waiting — we went on in her & spent the afternoon sailing, going east — landed at one point; crossed the sand dunes & went over to the surf — It was very inspiriting — I was glad to have my light overcoat — It was 6.30 when we got back — Nelson was waiting for us & we were driven right home — Dinner after 7 — then for some time on piazza — then in-doors & had a game of Bazique — near 11 when we went to bed.
