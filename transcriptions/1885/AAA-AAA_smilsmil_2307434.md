## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 30. 1885.

Mild, hazy day — like Sep. Got to studio about 10 — worked awhile with burnisher on Casini plates & at 10 30 went to K. & V's & got [illegible] — back abt 1 30 & worked until 3 30 — then home to lunch at 2 — after that I had a short nap — when came Hertzel came in — saw her for a moment; then to T.W. Wood's studio — W. C. Board meeting at 3 — there until 4 — then down to Fulton St & bought a diary — back to studio — did not get home to dinner until after 6 — while at the table Bella [illegible] who had been vomiting — Jim, she was terribly alarmed — he was so pale as a sheet & went into a swoon & stupor from which we could not rouse him — I was thoroughly frightened & ringing for a messenger boy sent him for Yale & then I went to Dr. Lewis's in Park Ave. & brought him in — he reassured us — Yale came in & in about half an hour Jim revived after a mustard plaster had been put on his stomach — he was then put to bed & in time went into a natural & quiet sleep — Mrs. Park called — Yale stayed with us until near 9 — soon after, Charle came in & stayed until 10.30 — it was 11 when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 31. 1885.

A dismal, rainy pouring rain. Down after 7. The water back in the kitchen cause burst & the lower floor was flooded — as no help could be got we went with Jim (who seems to be quite himself again) to Worthier Cook's & got our help. There — it was 10 15 when I got to studio — Spent the rest of the mtf. burnishing on Casini plates — Home soon after 1 — Mrs. Satchell & her daughter there to lunch — back to studio about 2 30 until 4.30 Rebiting — then cleaned up & home, exceedingly tired & discouraged — Dinner — Evtf. Annie Read aloud for awhile — & then we had a game of cards — To bed before 11 — quite demoralized — and so ends the year.
