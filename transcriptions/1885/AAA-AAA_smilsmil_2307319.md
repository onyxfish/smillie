## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 12. 1885.

Pleasant day but breezy & growing cooler.
Got to Met. museum at about 10.30 & to
work on Swinburne but found that I
had only Ross paper No 4 to work on & that
preparation I cannot manage as well
as No. 5 — di Cesuela, Marquand & Wr. Loring
Andrews came in & chatted for awhile —
I tried Tennyson's head & then gave it
up, determined to do no more on No. 4.
At 1 o'clk. started for home, getting there at
1.30 — lunch at 2.30 — went to Bank for
Arrive then to Am. Art Ass'n. & saw Kurtz —
delivered 11 drawings which were at
once sent to the A.B.N. Co to be photogr-
eng'd — then to Brooks Bros. & bought a
suit of clothes — then to Studio — near
5 — Charl came in — Ed. had been there
— Father & Kate expecting to come to the
city to-morrow for a day or two — Abt
went to [illegible] — 3d. ave & 54th St. Home
at 6.15 — dinner. Early part of evef.
writing — then a game & to bed
before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 13. 1885.

An overcast day with poor promise
for a pleasant spell of weather for
Father's visit. Got to Met. museum
about 20 m. after 10 & to work — drew
Wm Morris & part of Arnold — then
home to lunch at 1 — Father & Kate
came in before 1.30 — Ed. came down
with them — Father appears to be
remarkably improved since last
I saw him. About 2.30 went to the
annual meeting. N.A. & I spent the
rest of the p.m. in a not very enlivening manner — At first ballot for N.A. —
no result — great indignation — Brandt
moved a reconsideration — which resulted
in election of Millelt — then Maynard,
then Nicoll — & then, F.S. Church — among
associates, a reconsideration elected Witt.
Home before 6.30 — dinner — Ever'g.
Charl came in & we had a little
family party until 10 — then to
bed.
