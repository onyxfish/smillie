## Left Page

### December, Sunday 6, 1885

A pleasant day — bkfst. at 9 —
the wtf. — Spent — with Annie
who now has to keep very
quietly upon her back — Dr. G —
called in the wtf & again at
evtf. It was near 1 o'clk.
before I started out for a walk.
Mary Willard & Mother Cook
having called in — I went up
to Central Park & back in
time for dinner at 2 — wrote
a short note to Hitchcock &
Tribune. Tea before 7 —
Gale made quite a call after
that — then read — in the
afternoon I read a part of
Father's autobiographical
Sketch; "A Pilgrimage" aloud to
Annie — to bed soon after
10.

## Right Page

### December, Monday 7, 1885

*Father Buried.*

Brilliant, cold, windy day — Up
soon after 6 — bkfst. at 7 — Took 8 o'clk.
Train to Po'k. — Met Fred & Will at depot —
Old Mr. Menger was there but I advised
him not to undertake the journey —
Got to Po'k. at 10.30 after a very cold
ride — to the house at 10.45 — Found
Mother & Father very well — Rev. Nosse
& Hendrick were there — Father in
the back parlor — centre of Room —
Prayers & short addresses by each
of the clergymen — About 11.30 the body
was removed & at 11.45 we followed
Geo. Will — Fred & I taking the 12
10 train — Chas. Remaining with
Mother — We got to N.Y. at 2.12
Found Rev. Mr. Elder & Mr. Main
waiting & before 2.30 we got started
west to South Ferry & out to Greenwood, getting to the gate 5 minutes
before 4 — We got to the plot on
Ocean Hill & the dear body was laid
away at 4.25 just as the sun was setting.
Mr. Elder made a short prayer — the wind
was bitter cold — but the sky & far-stretching
ocean were beautiful & very impressive — we
were driven rapidly back to So. Ferry —
dismissed the carriage soon after 5 &
then home by Elevated R.R. I got home
about 5.30 — Tea abt 6 — every I read
aloud awhile Father's Pilgrimage — Dr.
Gale came in & made quite a visit —
To bed about 10.30 —
