## Left Page

### June, Sunday 21, 1885

Delicious day — very breezy & quite cool. Bkfn before 9 — For an hour or two wandered with Walter over the place, planning new approach to the house & selecting site for the barn. About 12 I retired to my couch & got a nap. Lunch at 1.30 — after that wrote letter to Father — About 3.30 we went out to ride — first going to the west, when I mailed my letter — then returning & going east to the "Club House" & over about [illegible] Wood Road Back at about 6.30 — in an hour had dinner — a wood fire was kindled in the hall way fire place which was comforting & enjoyable. While I sat by it, Walter, Mamie & Annie went over to call on the Hoyts — they got back before 10 & we went to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 22, 1885

Strong wind all night — Up at 7 — Spiteful, spitting showers — Bkfn. before 8. Walter went into the city by 8.30 train — weather cleared beautifully about 9.30 — I wrote to Lambdin & Cottrell. Then out with Annie hunting for 17 year locusts — Too hot for her — so she ret. to the house & I continued until noon. — Lunch soon after 1.30 — & at 3 we bid our good byes & were driven to the depot to take 3.26 train — met there Miss Coshine [illegible]. A hot, dusty ride — got in at 5.30 & home about 6 — finding it comfortably cool in town — Dinner at 7 — after it made haste & carefully wrapped up lot of back nos. of Ill. London News. Charl came in for a few minutes with pt. from instantaneous photos taken at Po'k. from the boy & other members of the family, but they were experimental plates & not successful — To bed before 11.
