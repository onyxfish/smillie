## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 9, 1885.

Clear & very cool day — a drop of 20°
from yesterday — Got to studio at 10,
& by working steadily until 1, finished
Hilton's House — Home to lunch at 1.30 —
After that to A.B.N. Co — getting there
at about 3.20 — after a little delay,
Will Foak me over the establishment —
an exceedingly interesting visit —
nearly an hour & a half going from cellar
to garret — At 5 went by Bridge to Brook-
lyn & by elevated road out to Lexington
& Nostrand Ave. Then a short walk to No.
213 Quincy St, where I found John
Gadsby Chapman & got three auto-
graphs upon pts from Father's engr. after
his paintings — a jolly, little old man
(77 years) dull of hearing but sharp of
sight & memory — Saw his son also — got
away soon after 6 & home at 7.5 & had
dinner at once — Sneff. read until
9 — then a game with Annie &
to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 10, 1885.

Pleasant day, but growing warmer —
To studio at 10 & to work at once on
double sketch "Clarendon Piazza & Winds or
Hotel" — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 3 —
Col. Ingraham called & we agreed
upon all details for settlement. By
working until after 6 I got my double
sketch done — then I did some things
about the studio preparatory to going
away for the summer. I got about
½ dz. of used up paint tubes & mailed
them to Will Cook — About 7 stopped
at Geo's. in 28th St, then home to dinner.
After that wrote a note to Kate telling
her that we would make our start to
Po'k on Friday P.M. At 8.30 took Annie
to her Mattie's & I went to Yale's — Saw
him & tried to arrange with him
to go with us to Po'k — could not —
then to 39th St., Marie & Walther there
— we got home before 11 & right
to bed.
