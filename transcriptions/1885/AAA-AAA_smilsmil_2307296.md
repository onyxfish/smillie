## Left Page

### March, Friday 27, 1885

Very nice & pleasant — very light rain before
6 — soft rain at night. Got to studio at
a little after 10 — gathered painting traps
& went to N.A., & spent the m'f there
painting on my cliffs — think that
I improved it — gave snap to the
quills & cleared the color on parts of the
foreground rocks — to studio again
soon after 2 & home to lunch at 1.30
— feeling very tired I lay down on
my bed after lunch & had a nap —
after 4 when I got up. Then went
to 57.5; & spent an hour with Father
who is complaining of his head to-day.
Mother & Kate took dinner with Mrs. Mf.
Norton. Home before 6 & dressed.
Dinner at 7.15 — Mr. & Mrs. Carleton & Prager
Chas. Collins. Mary Willard — Ed'o & Mary
Cook — much enthusiasm over Mf proving
the leading topic of conversation. Up
from table at 8.40 — chatting until
10.30 when our guests left —
thine, clear moonlight —
to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 28, 1885

Clean — overcast after 4 P. m. —
with a little rain about 6 and
snow later — To studio after 10 &
gave myself up to general fuss-
ing — a call from the German who,
years ago, was with Snedecor, & sold
prints. I gave him a few etching
p's that he might try to sell for me.
With palette knifes spread color
over sky & sea on "Bit near Marble-
head" — being so [illegible] ridged that
I could do nothing with it — Ferguson
came in & I worked awhile on his
drawing for Am. Art Ass'n. Cat. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — back at 3 — Annie
came in from doing Matinee at 5.15
& we went up to Blashfield's Rec'p'n.
Home at 6.40 & had dinner —
Rec'd. note from Jos. (Will's wife) de-
clining invitation to dinner to-
morrow — left for or with her last Thur.
by Annie — Every[one] — Read awhile
Vernon Lee's "Counters of Albany" —
had a game of Beziques and
at 11 to bed.
