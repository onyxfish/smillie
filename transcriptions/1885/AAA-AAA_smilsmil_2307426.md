## Left Page

### December, Monday 14, 1885.

Dark day with rain — Rec'd a letter from the Examiner, declining to print my protest ag't. the Compressed Oxygen adv't. & I wrote a short reply. To studio before 10 — To work on drawing of Mill at Bennington V.t. — Home at [illegible] & back at 2.30 — Could not see to work after 4 — Home soon after 5. Dinner — Evelf. Annie, [illegible] setting up for a little while — we had a game of cards — I wrote a note to Dr. Elder thanking him for going to Greenwood with us — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 15, 1885.

Bright cold winter day — To studio at usual time & worked steadily all the w't on Bennington Mill drawing, getting it finished by 1 o'clk. — Then home to lunch, meeting Marie Cook at the door as she went in to call on Annie. Back to studio at 2.30 & to work on etching after Pasini — W'm. Park & his br. called — & later, just as I lost light, Chas. Parsons came in & we had a chat. About 5 I went to Am. Art As'n & saw the Whitney collection. Home & had dinner at 6 — Evelf. wrote notes of protest to Starkey & Palem of Phila. & Dr. Turner of 148 5th ave. ag't. further publication of the advertisement of Compressed Oxygen "Among the Bank Notes". To bed soon after 10.
