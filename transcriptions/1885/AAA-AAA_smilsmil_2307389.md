## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 29, 1885.

Another hot day — lowest Ther. last night 60°. Highest to-day, 76°. Again 11 o'clk before I got to my work on Fair Ground. Mr. Eldred came up before 8 o'clk. He agreed to sell me 1 Rod off the cor. of his lot running to a point at 7 rods, for $25.00. I worked at my study until 2 — then home to dinner — Will sent a telegram to hurry up drawing of Esmerelda. I worked on it but was bothered by having to look after the men. Tea at 6.30 — then put up Chinese lanterns on piazza — very pleasant effect — then down town — Still good news from Father — back at 8.30 — Brought Annie down to large room, & she took a hand at Bazique — She is encouraged to think she is better to-day. It was after 11 when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 30, 1885.

Another very warm day — tried to finish "Esmerelda" in the mtf. But had too many other things to look after — The Halley's — (Stone wall men) complained of the work they had to do in moving stone — I would not yield & at noon two of them stopped work — Dinner between 1 & 2 — Finished my drawing & mailed it with a letter to Will at 4 o'clk. Then got a vehicle & horses & about 5 o'clk went out for a short ride with Belle, Fanny, Eleg[?] & Jim Jnr. (he slept all the time) — Back at 6 — Tea about 7 — Again Annie was brought down stairs — She has not been as well to-day — Again it was 11 before we went up to our beds.
