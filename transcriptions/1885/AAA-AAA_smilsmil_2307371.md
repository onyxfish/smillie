## Left Page

### August, Monday 24, 1885

Another dog-day — hot, windy &
muggy until near 5 when a
furious thunder storm came up
with floods of rain, passing into
a steady rain at night. Went to
Father's before bkfs. & found him ex-
tremely wretched, having passed
a very bad night — home to bkfs. &
back about 10 & passed the m[orning] with
him — in great & constant distress
which depressed me exceedingly
— it seemed that he could not long
survive — went down town and
telegraphed to Kate to come up — I
fear that he is not in the city —
Home to dinner at 1.30 — back to
Father's at 3 & was delighted to find
him wonderfully better — with him
until the storm threatened then I
hurried home — a grand sight —
about 6 again to Father & then
down town — Rain — tea about
7 — Eve[ning], Read aloud a couple
of sketches by James — after 9
went to Father & saw him in bed
with promise of a quiet night
— home before 10 — Rain —
to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 25, 1885

Persistent rain, like a help-
day — Spent a little time with
Father who seems a little easier
then worked for awhile on the
Bank Note Etching — Dinner as
usual — again worked etching
until 4 — then to bed & had
nearly a couple of hours trying
to get a nap — about 6 went
down town — then tea — then
read aloud awhile & about
9.30 went to Father's & sat up
with him all night — He was
very restless & did not at any
one time get five minutes of sleep
— it seems only a question of en-
durance — no mortal could
live many days without rest —
About 4.30 I called Kate & at
5 went home, by dawn, & to
bed — It had been a brilliant
moonlight — Uncle Willie &
Aunt Agnes arr[ived] at 6 p.m.
& stayed at the Turville House.
