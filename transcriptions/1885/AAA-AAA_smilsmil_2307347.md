## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 7, 1885.

Overcast, wind from S. E. with promise of a bad day — about noon heavy showers came up — Later, thunderstorm with a change of wind & clearing about 2. — most of the a.f spent working with putty, after Austin, on windows in large room. Nearly 2 when we had dinner — After that went up to the fair ground to see the inflation of a hot air balloon — very slow & not very interesting — waited about an hour, then back to the house & worked on the glass in front door — saw the balloon at 5 o'clk as it floated, without the oars, for a short distance —
Tea at 7 — did not go down town —
Swinging in hammock — Dan Sayre &
wife called — then read newspaper
— then a game of cards & to bed before
11.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 8, 1885.

Delicious m'g, & a hot day with showers in the afternoon — felt very strongly dis-inclined to work and did much loafing, watching the carpenters at their work — nothing of any interest transpired in the family history of the day —
Part of the early ev'g spent in hammock — then reading & newspaper & to bed about 10.30.
