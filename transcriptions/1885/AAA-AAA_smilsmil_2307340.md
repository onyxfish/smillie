## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 23. 1885.

A delightful day, with pleasant
cool breeze — Bkfst. at 8.30 — After 10
when I got to studio — worked hard
all the mf — at getting things into shape
to leave — At 12.30 went to Nat. Exp.
with pkg of photos for Col. Ingraham &
then to Bank & deposited his check for
$157.50. then home to lunch — Sl. called
& told me that A.P.I. had spent most of
yesterday in conference with Mary &
uncle Willie. To studio at 2.30 and
worked until after 6 — then home,
very tired — Dinner at 7 — query.
Charl came in for a few minutes on
the A.P.I. business — Mr. Howard White
called — also Chas. Collins, but I did
not see them — my evef engaged
planning for summer ex[hibition] &
writing letters. Went to G.C.D.
at 10.30 with Ingraham's letter —
to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 24. 1885.

Pleasant day, altho' warm — Got up
with a headache, but a double dose
of Citrate of Magnesia & Caffine seemed
to save me. After bkfst I went to Grady's
to arrange bills & order a big packing box,
then rode down town to Boyle's in Fulton S.
to see about awnings & stuff for covering the
piazza floor — then to Lenon Bach's & ordered
a mirror for the Montrose hall — then to Bank
& drew $50 — then to barber's & had hair cut &
a refreshing shampoo — then home to lunch
at 1.30 — got to Tiffany's before 3 & until 5
was engaged selecting glass for front door
& transom — then to studio for a few moments
then to O'neele's & bought pair of shoes —
home at 6.30 — dinner at 7 — Evef until
9, carpentering, correcting the blunders
made in Grady packing box — & when
finished, then packing rugs & glass. To
bed before 11.
