## Left Page

### April, Monday 20. 1885.

Clear & very warm — Dr. Yale came about 10.30
with his pump — Father had suffered during the
night from short breath & very soon yielded to the
proposal to tap him — He submitted to the operation
without flinching & nearly 3 pints of healthy liquid
was drawn from the left pleural cavity — The oper-
ation did not seem to inconvenience him in
any way — Nearly 12 when it was all over and
Annie & I walked down to studio — we called on
miss Abbatt — then I telegraphed to Nashville saying
that I would take $60 — for "a Chemung Co, Pasture" —
then to Price Express & saw Sutton, Kirby & Kurtz —
Home at 1.20 — lunch at near 2 — Sat with
father until near 3 when he got up. Then
to studio — Van Cleef came in & spent about
an hour, then Hitchcock & Tribune who is
after Etching material for all White, Stokes &
Allen book — He stayed with me until 6.30
when we walked up together — Dinner —
father up & quite frisky — Charl came in
& stayed until 9 — then Geo. came — father &
mother to bed before 9 — Annie & I had a
quiet game of Basique & to bed soon after
11.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 21. 1885.

Clear & hot — ther. at 3 P.m., on shady side of the
street, 82°. Got to studio about 10.30 & spent
some time putting things in order — found a
female sparrow flying about, having come in
at the open window — It stayed all day, not
seeming to know enough to go out at it came
in — I did some work on the outline of
"Dillomandy Farm" — Home at 1.15 — lunch —
Father seemed much overcome by the heat.
Out at 3 o'clk. went out to ride in the
Park with Mother, Annie & Kate. I got
back to studio at 3.15 — again a little
work on outline — about 4.30 Hitchcock
came & we talked Etchings until near
6.30 — then he walked up home with
me — Dinner about 6.45 — Eve'g. Mr.
& Mrs Wimans called — & then Charl came
in — then Mr. Ordway — then W. & Mrs Oliver
day — all away soon after 9 — To bed
about 10.30 — Bella again commenced
to take charge of the boy through the
night & Annie returned to me.
