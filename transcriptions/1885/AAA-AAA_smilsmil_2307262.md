## Left Page

### January, Monday 12, 1885.

warm, spring-like day with showers in the mf — clearing brisk and cool at night — 10 o'clk when I got to McKee's shop in 42d S — Spent some time in arranging about a new frame for "Coming Shower" Got to studio at 11 — found note from Boston Art Club announcing that all three of my paintings had been rejected — a mortal blow — I could not work after it — Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — Fussed about — At 5 went with Geo. to McKee's — Home to dinner at 6 — Evg passed in playing Bazique with Annie before the parlor fire — Wrote notes to F.S. Church Bishop Putnam & Mrs. M. Nimmo Moran. To bed at about 11.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 13, 1885.

Charming clear day — To studio before 10 — then to work for a while on the left-hand end of my first & unsuccessful attempt at "a Coming Shower" — having cut it in two, I changed the form of the group of trees & added a man hoeing — & will call it "a showery day" — Then I set to work on the right-hand end — washed out the cow — put in a figure carrying water & will call it "a bit on a Normandy farm" — The two are not bad sketches, judged by my average. Home to lunch as usual — After working as long as I could see I made paste & mounted the Normandy farm sketch — Home to dinner at 6 — very tired — Evg in the dining room — Spent a little time with A. looking over her accounts — then we had a game of Bazique — To bed soon after 10 —
