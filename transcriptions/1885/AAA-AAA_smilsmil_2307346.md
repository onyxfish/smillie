## Left Page

### July, Sunday 5, 1885

A steaming hot day — after breakfast all sat about on the piazza or swung in the hammock for some time & before 11 we all strolled to the Warner lot on the top of the hill, taking the boy with us. We went into the Fair Ground & sat there for awhile on the rocks — then back fell & through the woods, picking dried straw berries — home before 1 — Dinner at 2 at which time a very heavy thunderstorm came up — one of the big maples by studio was struck with, apparently, very slight damage — the shock was quite startling — other showers followed later in the afternoon — I took a nap — & then wrote letters to Father & Charl — did not get them to the P.O. owing to showers. Tea about 7 and evg. in-doors, as it blew up freshly, chatting until 10.30 — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 6, 1885

An overcast wlf. & a muggy day, the Ther. getting up to 80° — but after 6 a smart shower circled over us coming from south & cast a clear, breezy evg. B'kf. about 8 & carriage came & a few minutes later Miss Hunt, Geo. & Valentine got away for Montrose depot. I busied myself with Cottrill, Heinck & Austin — the latter putting some of the glass in sliding sashes in main room. After 11 I went down town & attended to various things. Opened an acc. at the bank by depositing a $250.00 check of Irvine's — Dinner at 1 — most of the afternoon I worked on the north piazza putting in some of my new glass — in the wlf. I made drawings for wardrobe in D.W. bedroom — Tea before 7 — after it went down town — back in about an hour — read my newspaper for awhile — did a little writing — The boy very wakeful, did not go to sleep until near 10 — almost 11 when we went to bed.
