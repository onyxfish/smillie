## Left Page

### June, Monday 15, 1885

*at home.*

Clear & very hot. Ther. got up to 87°. Up at 7 & had a short walk before bkfst. At 9 got to my work on Saratoga Sketches. "Broadway in Winter" & worked until about 10 when Chase took 3 instantaneous photos of the boy by the front door steps. Then worked until 10.30 when Rose, the lawyer came & he, with Mary, Chase & myself spent an hour in going over the Throop case. Then again to my work until 1 when we had dinner. From som after 2 to near 3.30 I worked & finished sketch then packed up. A little before 4, a carriage came — I bid my good byes and Mary, Chase & I went to Rose's office & Mary swore to her complaint which is to be served on the D. this evef as he starts for N.Y. on the boat. Chase & I then went to the Depot, met A. B. & the boy & took 4.50 train getting into N.Y. at 7. Home at once & had tea. — Evef. Thunder showers. News came to us of the serious illness of Julie Spring. To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 16, 1885

[and] hot — Ther. got up to 95°. After bkfst. I went to studio. While there had one or two callers. C. Mt. Wdg came & paid me $75.00 for the Watts drawings — then rode down to 10th St. — got a dozen chimneys from Donnell's — then to Tommy's & while I waited half an hour, had trees re-covered — it was about 12 when I got home — worked awhile on "U.S. Hotel in Winter" — the last of the Saratoga pen sketches — lunch about 1 — afternoon worked on Hotel until near 6 — then again to studio, but it was so hot that I could do nothing. Home at 7 — dinner — Evef. Chas. Collins called — then Walter Cook — they stayed until after 9 — then I read awhile — To bed about 11. Soon after, thunder storms came up which continued some time cooling the air delightfully.
