## Left Page

### April, Thursday 2, 1885

Overcast & raw. After 10 when I got to
studio — not in the humor for any work —
Engaged for a while on the background
of the flower picture that Miss Abbatt
gave to me yesterday — I took it out of
the frame & quieted the washes of the
background, by so doing giving more
light & prominence to the far & flowers —
Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 &
did nothing industriously all the after-
noon — much of the time superintending
a man mend my chairs — Before 5
went to McKeever's with mat from the
frame on Miss A's drawing to have it re-
matted — then to 37. St. & spent about
an hour with Father & Mother. 82, there.
Read a letter from Uncle Willie describing
the operation for removal of ovarian tumor
just performed on Aunt Agnes, who
seems to have got along wonderfully well so far.
Home to dinner at 6.30 — Sister Mary & her
two children there — Charl came in from
8 to 9.30 — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 3, 1885

Very mild & pleasant day — After
[illegible] — went over to Daly's Theatre and
bought tickets for to-night to see the
farce: "A night off" — then to 2°. Nat.
Bk. & made deposit for Annie.
then to Studio — got paints & brushes and
went to N.A. — Varnishing Day — Worked
a little on "A fallow field." — Did not
get home to lunch until 1.45 — back
to studio at 3 — looking about for some-
thing to do I found an old W.C. drawing
made in 1876, still on a drawing board
— a sort of a Scrub Race subject —
washed out horses & riders & washed
in trees & a river & eventually a new
fore ground, so that nothing but original
sky remains — will christen it "A California
River." About 6 called for a few minutes on
Father & Mother. Met Mary there & we R[ode?] home
to dinner at 6.30 — At 8 to Daly's where we
had a good laugh — out at 11 — To bed
before 12.
