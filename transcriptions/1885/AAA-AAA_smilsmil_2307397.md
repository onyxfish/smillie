## Left Page

### October, Saturday 17, 1885

A delightful day — out with the men & attending to trees all day —
The final raking of the lot almost completed — I also worked in Main
Room chimney putting on another coat of Asbestos cement. Mr. & Mrs. [illegible]
came up on horseback. Annie out on piazza sunning herself — after being
quite well for five days she is much cast down by a reappearance of the trouble — Smith at work painting
2nd coat on shutters. F. B. Corn came up with his bill for sod & anistein
of $1.50 for ½ a days work — man & team
2 years ago — a matter between him & Payne — he went away very mad &
would not take pay for his sod, because I would not buy the [illegible]
[illegible] — Went downtown about 6 —
then tea & everg. Reading
paper & Nov. Harper's — Better
news from Father this eveg.
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 18, 1885

Pleasant mild day — partially overcast — most of the day
pottering about the house doing things that have to be done
preparatory to leaving. It was after 2 when I had dinner —
then wrote & read awhile —
then again about, some of the time out of doors — more
writing in the eveg & bed —
went 9 & 10 went down to P.O.
with letters — then home & to
bed.
