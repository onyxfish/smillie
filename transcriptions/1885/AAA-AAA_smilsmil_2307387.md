## Left Page

### September, Friday 25, 1885

A very warm day — in the mrf. worked in studio on charcoal sketch on canvas of subject in Fair Ground preparatory to painting upon it out of doors — Dinner at 12.30 & at 1.15 Eliz. started for D.L.&W. depot for Fanny & Belle ? — I took oil colors & went into woods on Warner's lot & sketched in a subject that has much attracted me for a number of years got through before 6 & on my return to the house I met Fanny & Belle. Tea about 6.30 — Down town soon after 8 — Home at 9 — Rest of everg. we all sat in Annie's Room — She keeps quietly on her rack. we hope for the best but there is nothing upon which we can rest — we hope & fear

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 26, 1885

Cloudless & very warm — In the mrf. I had many duties that kept me busy until 11 — after that I took canvas & colors up to Fair Ground & got so engrossed in my work that it was nearly 2 before I thought of dinner — then home & hurried through the meal — after that more duties, so that it was almost 5 when I got out to my work with charcoal on the subject that I sketched in in color yes'd p.m. — Home at 6 — Paid off the men — Tea before 7 — Evegs after 8 — went down town — by mail heard of disappearance of A. F. Oakey — Sat in our bed room, all together, chatting & reading until after 10 — just about 11 when I got to bed.

No change in Annie's case.
