## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 6, 1885

Nasty day — mixed & wet. To studio at usual time & to work on "coming storm" Work all day — Dinner at 6. Ed Parker called at the house in the evg. to say that Mother & Kate may be down some day this week. Avery Brown called in the evg & at 8.30 I left him & walked down to the Century where Twelfth Night was celebrated — a large crowd — everybody had to wear some badge of a Tom fool — Paper caps & light muslin togas were supplied but a number of the members wore fine costumes — The character of the evg was Col. Carter of Cartersville. S.C. (F. Hopkinson Smith) who was trying to "float" a Rail-Road Scheme. — He was capitally made up & carried his part with great spirit & ready wit. A champagne & game supper was served at about 10.30 — At 11 I left — the best of the fun being after that time. I was afterwards tired — but I wanted to be in trim for work To-morrow — so, walked up home & got to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 7, 1885

Cooler, overcast & windy. Got to studio about 10 & to work on "coming shower" — Can't get effects that I wish — in spite of me, instead of freshness & brightness I get gloom & mud. Home to lunch at 1.30 & back at 2.30 & worked as long as I could see. Took a nap in my big chair & home to dinner at 6. Frames for Water Colors came to-day — don't improve my work as I hoped they would — Champney came in to talk of last night's amusement — a fire, that for a few moments threatened to be serious was started among the decorations after 3 A.M. at the Century. At 8.30, with Annie to Art Students League, 38 W. 14th St. Presentation of Scholarships (Hallgarten & Harper) to W. Ernest S. Major — addresses by Ely Adler, Frank Miller, Stetman etc — very interesting & enjoyed addresses in spite of heat & foul air — walked home — found that Will, Joe, Nienn & Walter had called while we were out — To bed at 11.
