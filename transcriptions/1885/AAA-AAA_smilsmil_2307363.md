## Left Page

### August, Saturday 8, 1885

A charming day — Spent the m[orning]
as usual, with carpenters — Coyle
& Lerfus the tin man. Great preparations in town for the Grant obsequies
this afternoon. Dinner at 1.30 —
With Henick & Lerfus most of
the afternoon supervising during
the putting up of Ventilating shaft
& some other fixings about the
Earth Closet. Then with H. &
the boy to see Father & Mother
— down town — Tea about 7 —
Fannie Trvoels with us — Mary
& Katie went to depot to meet
Kate & Charl — from N.Y. I took
Fannie back about 8 & waited
for half an hour — the party
from depot did not get in
until 9.25 — I went home
before 9 — had a game &
to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 9, 1885

Charming day in spite of an east
wind & prognostications — Belpn-
latis — About 10 Annie, the boy &
I went to Father's — & later, all,
except the boy, went to the Baptist
Church & heard Mr. Husted preach
from the text "Ephraim is a cake
not turned". After evening awhile
in Tunnel piazza after our return
went home — Dinner at 1.45 —
After dinner, a little reading —
then Charl came — & later,
Mary's children — At the home
most of the afternoon — a little
time in the hammock — a little
time writing. Late in the after
noon we went to see the folks
for awhile — Home & tea at 7.
Eveng. Reading & wrote a letter
to W.G. Nielson. After 10.30
when we got to bed. Light
rain.
