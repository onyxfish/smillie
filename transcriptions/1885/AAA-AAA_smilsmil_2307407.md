## Left Page

### November, Friday 6, 1885.

*went to Po'k.*

Very nice bright wt. After H[illegible] went up to see Male, but missed him — then to studio — unpacked big case from Montrose — there for a time worked over pf. of Father's die "Lions" re Fonching it — About 12.30 rode up to Male's — saw him & he arranged to go to Po'k. with me if he could — Then I went home to lunch — Sat & read until after 3 — then to depot to 4 to Po'k by 3.30 train — missed Male through a misunderstanding & he did not go with me — Got to the Terrace at 5.45 — Found Father sitting up in bed — peaceful — even cheerful & much interested in the proofs of the die that I brought him — but no sigh of impatience escaped him because he can not finish it — After taking tea & taking many home I sat and chatted with him until 8 when I started for depot — Rained before I got there — got home at 11.10 — Right to bed —

I found Father very weak and surely sinking — He is now much troubled by the swelling of his legs which extends up to his body —

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 7. 1885.

Overcast — very warm — 70° out of doors — with showers about 1 o'clk — Nearly when I got to studio — Spent the wt[illegible] in further arranging of the mass of stuff that fills my studio — Detained by a shower, it was 1.30 when I got home to lunch. — In the afternoon, Rose came down to studio & washed up the floor — at 5 I went to Am. Art Ass'n & saw Kirby about etching & etchers for Cat. of the Morgan Sale — then a look through the Galleries — Home to dinner at 6 — Evg in parlor — did not go to "Century" the evg was so muggy & enervating — we read — played cards & went to bed soon after 10.
