## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 28, 1885.

Rain in the m'g. freezing as it fell — then snow at night —
brilliant moonlight + exceedingly cold. To studio at 10 + to work
on Meadow Pond, struggling with the middle distance. — Home
at 1 + back at 2.15 — worked for half an hour, painting in't
edges not solid to square of a little wood study that I painted
Aug 25, '65. Then at Meadow Pond as long as I could see. —
Dinner as usual — Every one dining room — I spent time analyzing Mulligan's
(carpenter) bill + writing him a note on the subject. —
To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 29, 1885.

A brilliant day + very cold — To studio at 10 + to work worrying
over the hill side in Meadow Pond. — Fussed all the morning
+ could not get what I wanted. — home to lunch at 1 — back at
2.30 + found Miss Spring waiting for me. after a visit of medium
duration she purchased a [illegible] of my neighbor's Hurl and for $9.00
McKeever called — I think that there is trouble brewing between
him + Gradey — I worked as long as I could see. — then home — dinner
+ at 8 o'clc at Fifth Ave Theatre +
saw Booth in Hamlet — B. is growing old + made a sad impression on
me — wrecking the beautiful image in my memory of him as Hamlet
twenty years ago. His Elocution is still very fine. — superior to Irving's
but he has not the electrifying energy of Irving. — As a whole the
performance seemed tame — It was
cut up on the stage in the old conventional way — out at 11.20 — to bed at
midnight —
