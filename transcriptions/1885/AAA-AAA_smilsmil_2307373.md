## Left Page

### August, Friday 28, 1885

Milder - a charming day -
Bkfst. after 8 - It was after 9 when
Dr. Y. & I got to Father who was in a
state of great prostration - Brandy
was freely given & he soon rallied.
It was about 11 when the Dr. &
I went down Town - back - at
Father's for awhile - Then home
& with Miss Abbott, we went to
the Fair ground & around - Dinner
at 2 - At 3.30 went out for a
ride - Uncle W. & Aunt A. - Miss
A. - Dr. Y. - Annie - boy & self -
out until about 6 - Then to Father
Yale. Talked him & took from him
1½ pt. - Trouble in getting needle
in - Father felt relief - Home
to tea at 8 - At 9.30 again we
went to Father - Saw him propped
up in bed - Then home at 10
about 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 29, 1885

*Went to N.Y. with Father*
*St. Cloud Hotel - Room 72 -*

Up at about 6. Brilliant wtf. - Bkfst.
at 7.30 after having been down Town
to attend to some messages - It was
8.45 when the barouche came to Aunt
A's & some time was spent in leave taking.
Father seemed more dead than alive &
many tears were shed as he was carried
out & placed in the carriage. In Brighton
ed as we got to the depot - Train in time
11 10 - Had large compartment in Parlor
car - Father, Mother, Kate, Yale & I. The
R.R. ride very exhausting to Father. Got
to Hoboken at 5.45 - 30 m. late - Char[les]
there with carriage. Yale carried F.
as he would a baby - Charl & I went by
elevated R.R. & got to St. Cloud ahead
of carriage - Father at once carried to
Room 65 - large, pleasant & comfortable.
Yale stayed with us until after 8 - Charl
brought in Dr. Rabe, who had a talk with
Yale. - After 10 C. brought in Miss Holton
a trained nurse - Father proved
rather restive & seemed excited -
I got to bed about 11 - heavy rain
during the night which disturbed
me.
