## Left Page

### May, Sunday 10, 1885

Pleasant day — Bkf. before 9 —
wrote a letter to father — Read. for
awhile — then on lounge & took a nap.
At 1, Self walked down to studio —
back & dinner at 2 — another nap.
At 4.30 went to Mother Cook's for
Annie — Saw Mr. Sprague there. At
4.45 we rode up to Mad. Ave &
72°.5′ walked across the park &
went to Will's 343 w73°.8″. Found
Geo. Hearn there & one of his daughters.
Minn, Walter & the baby came in
soon after — we stayed until 6.30
then we walked down to 6' Ave & 59'
5″. & Rode down to 47′ — Got to M. Gibbon's
at 7.10 — found, besides the family —
M. & Mrs. O.[?] Day & Miss Fidelia Bridges.
Tea at 7.30 — In the evng Prof. [illegible]
Monti came in — Home a little
before 11 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 11, 1885

A very pleasant day. Got to museum
at 10.30 & to work on Love & Life, making
it with lithographic crayon & much larger
than the other drawings. Then "Death on
the Pale Horse." At 1.30 went outside
& sat with the obelisk & ate my lunch —
back & finished off Death — & then polished
off Cardinal Manning. Found it very
much pleasanter, being a fair day, than
Saturday — comparatively few people —
About 5.30 I gave up work — rode down
to studio, then home — Dinner at usual
time — Evnf. Charl came in, just
down from P.o'k. — Reports favorably —
A.P. Moods has paid many a short
visit. He brought me a finished [illegible] of
the photo he took of me in my studio —
I read & wrote a note or two &
got to bed at 11.
