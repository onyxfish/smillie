## Left Page

### September, Saturday 19, 1885

Very heavy mist or fog in
the m'f, clearing into a
charming day. After
spending some time with
the men I took plate &
went to Fairgrounds and
continued the etching of
No. 33 until after 6 o'clk —
without quite finishing it
then home to dinner —
About 3.30 carriage came &
we all went out for a ride
— down Snake Creek Road —
back on to Somerville & then
across to New Milford, Hart
Lake Road & then home at
6 o'clk — then to P.O — Katie's
letter says that Father is again
suffering much distress in
breathing — Every after tea
we had some card playing
& then walking on piazza
until near 11 o'clk — glorious
moonlight.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 20, 1885

A heavenly day — cloudless
with warm sunshine & cool
air — nearly 9 when we had
[illegible] — nearly 11 when Annie,
Eliz", Chat. Collins, the boy & I
started out for a saunter. we
went around the top of the
hill, lingering & loitering and
back at 1.30 — Dinner about
2 — then I had a short nap
in hammock on piazza — then
wrote a letter to Father. —
took it to mail about 6 —
then home to tea — Every
wrote notes to Dr. Bellows, Fam
[illegible]. Passenger agent of D. L. & W.
R.R. About 10, Collins & I went
to P.O. — glorious moonlight —
after 11 when we went to bed.
