## Left Page

### April, Sunday 12, 1885

Bright sunshine with showers —
About 11, Annie & I went to 37' S. —
spent an hour there — then to 39' S.
to see Mother Cook & Nellie, who is
quite sick with a cholera-morbutic
attack — Home to dinner at 2 —
After 4 again went to 37' S. with
an invitation to Father, Mother and
Kate, from Annie, to spend a week
with us, beginning next Thur.
Accepted gratefully — About 6 left
them & took a walk — Tea at
home at 7 o'clk. Ruelf a little
Reading & to bed at 10 — so as to be
prepared for a circus by Jas. Junt.
who is to be deprived of his little
potation of midnight milk — He
raised something of a row — but
nothing like as bad as he might
or as was expected.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 13, 1885

Bright Alpine like day — sunshine & shadow.
Up late — b&p. at 9 — 10.30 when I got to
studio & then to work on W.C. drawing
"Coming Shower — near Tretat" — I added
much force & color through the centre
making the whole thing broader and
stronger. W.H. Lippincott made a call
before noon — It was 1.45 when I got
home to lunch & 3 when I got back
to studio — Found Hitchcock of the Tribune
waiting for me in Geo's Studio — he wants
to get some points concerning Etching in
this country — It was after 4 when I got
to work again & I stuck to it until 6 —
Then to 37' S. & saw Father & Mother for a few
minutes — thus home & had dinner at
7 o'clk. Ruelf — a game of Basique and
letter writing until after 10 o'clk.
