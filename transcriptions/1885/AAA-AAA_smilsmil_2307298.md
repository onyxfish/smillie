## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 31. 1885.

Very mied & pleasant day. To studio at usual time, but feeling headachy. I tried for awhile to work & then gave it up & spent some time reading "much Ado about Nothing" in preparation for To-night — Home to lunch at 1.30 — Fanny K. there — lunched with us — About 3 I went to bed & had a nap, getting up about 4.30 feeling somewhat better — Went over to 37 S. & made a call on Father & Mother — home & had dinner at 6.5 — At 8 with a. at the Star Theatre — very good balcony seats — Mr. Carleton Sprague — Mr. & Mrs. Alex F. Takey sat behind us — met Mr. & Mrs. W. Shrunger in the lobby — Enjoyed the play — Ellen Terry exceedingly graceful & to quant — Irving wanting all of the elements, physical & mentals, of Benedick — nevertheless he was interesting — out before 11 & walked up. A shower had fallen just before & for a time it rained heavily after we got home. Sat & read until after midnight — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 1. 1885.

Delicious, mied storing day — Got up feeling rather dragged — To studio after 10 & tried to paint awhile on 20 × 24 "Gothic Mts" but made poor work of it — Soon after 12 went up to 37 S. & made a short call on Father & Mother — Then home to lunch about 1.30 — To studio soon after 3, having gone to 2d. Nat. Bank, to make a deposit for Annie — Got out a canvas 20 × 30 & made a charcoal sketch upon it of "The Maine Port" — near "[illegible]." Miss Abbatt came in with a fine, framed Water Color painting "Poppies in a vase" which she gave to me as a long deferred wedding present — at which I was much surprised & pleased — Home soon after 6 — Kate there & Belle Tweedy — Before 7, Pel & Jakey came & then we had dinner — a very pleasant eve'g followed. Before 10 Mr. Benedict called for Belle — I took Kate home — Saw Father & Mother for a moment — met Charl — went into Union League Club for a moment, then home to bed at 11.
