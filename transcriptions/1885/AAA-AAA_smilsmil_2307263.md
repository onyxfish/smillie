## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 14. 1885.

Lovely day — crisp + cool, without a cloud —
to studio at 10 after making a call at the
N.A. of D + getting W.C. dish — Frame came
from Grady's for "Coming Shower" — proceeded to
put it in — fixed up other drawings —
cutting a final touches + pasting them
into their frames — Home to lunch at
1 — Emma + J there — walked down
Lex. Ave. to the Raths with her — then to
studio — found that I would have to
mount Coming Shower decidly; it buckled in
the frame so badly — made paste + mounted
it + put it in press — then put etching
by Fortuny into frame — went to N.A.D —
there home at 5.30 — hung in dining room
etchings by Fortuny + Ulcher — then dressed — At
6.30 Mr Yale + wife — Prof. Morse + wife + Mr. Haven
Putnam + wife came + we had dinner —
about 9 — Chas. Collins + Eliz. Howe, Walter
+ his wife — Emma + Mary J. Geo. Cook + Jno.
Valentine came — things seemed to go
very pleasantly — all left about 11 —
nearly 12 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 15. 1885.

Snow in the nlt., heavy rain before
noon — nlt. mild + muggy — Got to the
studio soon after 10 — Spent some time in
putting a "Coming Shower" into its frame,
having to cut an opening in trunk board
back + carefully paste the mounted draw-
ing into its place. Rossele came for the
pictures soon after 12. Rec'd. by Exp. from
Mr. Koehler, a ph. from the de Scheunnis plate that
is an out + out crib from my "Old Cedars" — Took
it over to Farrer + he agrees to its exhib. in
the etching show together with a ph. of my plate.
Then to Bank + made deposit for Aurnie —
then home at 1.30 to lunch. Feeling very
tired, did not go to studio but took a
long nap. Wrote note to Charl, accompanying
note of introduction to Mr. Geo. Whitney — Dinner
at 6 — wrote letter to Koehler — Took it to G.C.D —
left Charl's note at Murray Hill Hotel — Home
at 8.30 + with A. went to Mad. Hope Kirk's piano
recital at Steinway Hall — there at 9 — out at 9.45
fairly good pianist — home soon after 10.
To bed about 11.30.
