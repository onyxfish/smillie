## Left Page

### April, Saturday 4, 1885

Heavy, driving rain in the morning,
ceasing about noon & clearing at night.
Got to studio about 10 + finished the
furbishing up of an old W.C. drawing
Home at 1 — Annie had spent part of
the mt. with Mary at the U. A. [illegible] —
afternoon, she went alone to the last
Irving Matinée to see Louis XI. I
ret. to studio at 2.30 + worked until
6.30 — making pastes, mounting draw-
ings, etc — fixing Miss Albatt's [illegible]
into its frame — etc — etc — Dinner
about 6.45 — Quite surprised
by calls from W. + Mrs. Ehninger
\+ Mr. + Mrs. Saml. Coleman, who
stayed until near 10 o'clk — so
I did not go to Private View or
Century — but to bed at 11 —
my little picture H. fallowfield
sold at U. A. to-day.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 5, 1885

Charming day — Soon after 10 went to the
Murray Hill Hotel — saw Coleman + asked
him + Mrs. C — to dinner to-day — but they
had other engagements — I then went to
39th St — At 10.30 brought Mother + Mary to the
Mad. Ave. cars at 38th St — to ride up to Elder's
church — then, after stopping a few minutes
at home, I went to U.A. [illegible]. where again
I met Coleman + we looked through the
[illegible] — Soon after 1 I went to the Century
when I met W. + Mr. Ehninger + Miss Shepherd,
of Saratoga. A headache was rapidly getting
the better of me — home at 2 — was able to
attend the carving + then went to bed
where I stayed until after 7 — then up +
had a light supper — about 9 J. B. Parker
came in + stayed for an hour — after
that I read + wrote + to bed soon
after midnight. Smart showers.
