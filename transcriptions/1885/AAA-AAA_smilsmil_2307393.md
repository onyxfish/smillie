## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 7, 1885.

Rather cold & breezy - with fine
clouds & promise of settled clearing
. Again routed out early in the m'f
- beginning to get discouraged -
B.k.p. in our bed room - after that
got to work etching on 34 - About
noon went down town - went to
St. Richardson & got some advice
for myself, then home & had dinner
Fireman's Parade - a great turn
out in town - my men could not
work in the afternoon. I spent
a couple of hours inside of dining
Room chimney, giving a portion of
a coat of Asbestos Plastic Stone lining as
a security against fire — Evening,
I wrote notes to Father & Dr. Yale —
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 8, 1885.

A bleak, disagreeable day, opening
with a snow storm. I spent the m'f
in bed room etching on 34 & went
down town before 12 with letters. Got
a lot of blocks at Crandall's for Jim.
Dinner with Annie at about 1.30.
At 3.30, while I was out with the
men a telegram came from Pt. D. L.
Adams, saying that he, Nellie & Master
Bob were at M— depot waiting for a
carriage. I went down town, telegraphed
him & sent a carriage - then home
& spent the rest of the p.m. getting
the house ready, lighting fires, etc.
At 6 went down town. About ½ the
party arr'd, had sent a telegram in
the m'f that did not get to us - We
had tea about 7.30 — Ev'f spent
in visiting & chatting. To bed ab
