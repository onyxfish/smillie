## Left Page

### August, Monday 10, 1885

cloudy & hot. Rain with thund between 6 & 7 A.m. Showers
during the day — Cottrell finished
his piazza railing & Herrick worked
on Earth Closet & floor of Bay window.
Smith, the painter, with son & son
inlaw put in an appearance
in the afternoon & set to work
puttying nail holes in Vestibule
& lower hall. Chard spent some
time with us — dined with the
Parks & started back for N.Y. at
6 — to take a night train —
Father seems very well to-day.
Evef. after reading until
after 9 had a game of cards
& to bed ab 10.30 —

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 11, 1885

Very hot & muggy. threatening
& at last Raining heavily bet.
6 & 7 P.m. light rain continuing
later. Spent the mf, or most
of it, in getting my printing press
ready for Father — he, in the mean
time, in a hammock — went back
with him at noon — Cottrell took
away his bench this mf. Dinn
at 1.30 — Spent the P.m. with
Smith, staining bay window in
studio & puttying the nail holes —
Tea at 7 — Evef, about 8, went
down town — Then home — Read
news papers until 9. To bed
ab 9.30.
