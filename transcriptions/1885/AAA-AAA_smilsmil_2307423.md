## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 8, 1885

Brilliant, cold wet, overcast in the afternoon — At 10.30 — Will
gel & I went again to Po'k, when we met Charl — dinner at the
Terrace soon after 1 — Many came in after 1 & between 2 & 3 — as we
were gathered in the front room from which Charl had had the
bed removed. I [illegible] & read Father's Will together in an informal, accompanying paper in which he made distribution
of his more valuable books &
pictures — It was a trying ordeal
Perfect harmony existed among us
as it was evident that the arrangement had been made with a most
earnest desire to be just & impartial — At 4 Charl & I went to lawyer Ross'
office & took the first steps to file the will
for probate — At 4.40 Geo. Will & I were at
the depot — Train 45 m. late — an hour late when
we got into N.Y. at 8 — home & had dinner
Then from 9 to 11 sat looking over "In
memoriam" — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 9, 1885

Dismal day with some very heavy
rain — got to studio about 10 — found
Resolution of Condolence passed by
the Council of N.A. of D. Spent some
time writing copies to send to the Pres.
Also got started on the biting [illegible] small
etching for Etching Club Cat. It
was 1.30 before I got home to lunch.
Summa called — again I wrote
copies of N.A. Resolutions & it was
after 3 when I got to studio — Then
finished biting small plates —
about 5 went to Up town offices
of Herald, Tribune, World & Times to
see if notices had been inserted
as agreed — no notice in Trib. or World.
$1.25 Refunded — Home at 6.15
Dinner — wet. Reading papers
until 9 —
