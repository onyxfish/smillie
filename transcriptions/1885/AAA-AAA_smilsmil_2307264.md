## Left Page

### January, Friday 16, 1885

Nasty day of wind + rain. To studio at usual time + spent the day in general work — daubing + pressing some Bridgman Etching plts — finishing pen sketch for U.C. Cat. — stowing away things not needed for the present and putting things about the studio in more orderly shape. When at 1, I got home to lunch, I found Annie with the boy asleep in her arms — an hour or more before, he had fetched out of the rocking chair, over the arm, + had evidently hurt himself — we feared had fractured his left collar bone — I sent for Jake — To studio about 3 — back at 5.30 + found that Jake had been to the house + said that there was a clean, vertical fracture of the collar bone close to the shoulder — the boy's bare arm was strapped by bands of adhesive plaster to his body — the elbow back + fore arm across his chest so that it could not be moved in any way — It is astonishing how amiably the little fellow submits to the bondage + how ingeniously he tries to make one hand do the work of two — we prepared for a bad night, but to our joy he got through it very well — most of the evening Annie + I spent in the dining room, a game of Barique — after she had gone to bed Geo. Cook came with a note from Miss [illegible] his fiancée, saying that she was too ill to meet Annie family at lunch to-morrow, as already arranged to take place at our house — and so ended my fifty-second birthday.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 17, 1885

Clear day with high wind + pretty cold. To studio at 10. Worked about generally — fussing with Etching plts — trying to reduce the irreducible disorder that ever reigns in my studio's. Home to lunch at 1 + back at 2.30. France came from Grady + I mounted + put into it the two plts. One from my plate of Old Cedars, the other, de Schenius' "irl" that Koehler sent me. Later, I took it to N.A.D. About 5 a young man called, a canvasser, who is preparing for a Southern + western trip + wants to take some of my etching plts, but he is too much a John for my business. Home before 6 + dinner soon after — Shortly before 9 Ed[ward] + Mary Cook called — after they left — Annie + I had a game of Barique — It was after 11 when I got to bed.

The boy continues in very good spirits — times down right jolly. in spite of his being bandaged up like a half finished little mummy.
