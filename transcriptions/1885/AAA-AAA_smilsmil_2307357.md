## Left Page

### July, Monday 27, 1885.

A very pleasant day - at times slightly overcast — Bkfs. about 8 —
an hour later went to Widow Beede's &
saw her & Mrs. Stetson — met there Misses
Morris, Cummings & Payne — They walked
down with us to the bridge across to Wyants
where we met Minor & a pulpit, Miss
Gardener — Annie, Miss Morris & I went
on to Wyants where we spent some time
with W. his wife & wife's sister — on our way
we met Mr. Comans — on our way back
Minor met us & took us to his Bungalow.
From there we returned by way of the old
Beede House & Sandy Gats Road, stopping for
a little while at Mr. Neilson's — then at 5 dinner —
after that for a time on piazza, then a long help-
up in time to go with Annie to Miss Cornell's
to 5 o'clk. tea where we met Rev. W. Sattevlee
& wife & Mr. & Mrs. Grinnell — Soon after 6 we
went to Neilson's to tea — her Sister, Miss
Champion & Mr. Ley there — on piazza
until after moonrise — then ret. to hotel
with Mr. Ley — very cool night — a party
(said to be 40) at the house for a dance
from the Valley — all away about 10 & we got
to bed soon after.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 28. 1885.

*on the Ausable Ponds —*

Glorious day — Up soon after 6 — Bkfs. at 7 —
at 7.50 we got away on our days excursion — Annie
& I on horseback, Misses Cornell & Irlson walking &
Grand Hale for guide & Jesse in charge of horses.
The ride through the woods was as delightful
as ever — we got to the lower lake at 9.20 & went
at once to Rainbow fell — 40 minutes — then rowed to
upper end of lake — 45 min. — water much lower than
used to be — margin of lake much overgrown — less dead
wood — walked the two mile carry in about 3/4
of an hour & got to Silver Birch camp at 12. At once
to work preparing for dinner which was served a
little after 1 — immensely enjoyed — after lounging
a little As I rowed up to the inlet & a short way into it —
it never looked lovelier — beautiful sky — beautiful
mountains — beautiful reflections — back to camp about
3.30 — then Hale took Misses C. & W. out for an hour — we
left camp at 5 & went right through to foot of lower
lake & there had tea & at 7 E. started out — we
got to the Beede Hse. at 8.30 just at moonrise —
the gloom in the woods wonderfully fine —
in the twilight heard (I am sure) the Hermit Thrush —
Miss Hilliard had a Bonfire party & for a
while we sat with them — Mets H. in bandit
hat & cloak, with guitar, sang some Italian songs —
we got to bed before 10 — Horrible Racket for an hour
