## Left Page

### July, Sunday 19, 1885

A very hot day, but with a cool, tempering breeze that made it delightful on the shady side of the house. Up late. Hd p. about 9 — After 10 went down & spent a while with Father — He is evidently feeble — found him sitting in the garden under the apple trees. Near noon when I ret'd home — wrote to Yale & to Koehler — Dinner at 2 — after that, on the grass in the shadow of the maples with Annie & the boy — later, wrote a note to Preston Spring & after E went again to see Father, having had tea — Borrowed a little trunk of Kate & took it up to the house on a wheel barrow — Packed & got to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 20, 1885

*Saratoga Springs —*

A very hot day. Up at 6.30. Hd p. at 7 — At 8, down to [illegible] & from there left, on top of the Valley Ho coach — a pleasant ride altho' we felt the heat before getting to New Milford — 10.4 train got to Binghampton before 11 — transferred trunk & got tickets to Saratoga; then, with a took horse cars & went into town & shopped for hardware for wardrobe — could not find what we wanted — both of us very headachy — Took lunch at [illegible] at the Elite Dining Rooms — There to Station & took 1.10 train — Exceedingly hot & dirty — Parlor car on Erie end of train — got to Quaker Street at 5.55 & then got into car at the end of a long coal train & went [illegible] 13 miles to So. Schenectady, a junction — nothing else, where, from 6.55 to 8.10 we sat on our trunk — fortunately we had lunch with us & we ate it as we sat — at 8.10 we took West Shore train & got in to Saratoga at 9.10 when we were met at the depot by Jno. W. Ehninger, just from Mr. Trask's dinner table & radient in clean linen & a dress suit — he rode at once to his house — He ref. for Mrs. E — leaving us with the maid who got up a fine supper for us with Champagne — Then we went to bed without waiting for the E's, but not until we had had a good wash — we were immensely dirty & tired.
