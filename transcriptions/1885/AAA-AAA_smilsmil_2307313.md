## Left Page

### April, Thursday 30, 1885.

Clear, quite cool m'g, growing rapidly warmer — soon after 10 when I got to studio
& spent the m'd on big charcoal 35×60.
Home soon after 1 — back before 3 &
feeling under the weather from a fit
of dyspepsia I did no work. Home
at 6 — dinner & at 8 Annie & I
went to Chickering Hall. Last concert
of the season of the Amateur Choral
Club, which proved to be a very en-
joyable affair — out at about 11 — very
warm — walked home — & right to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 1, 1885.

Heavy rain & driving wind nearly all
day. To studio soon after 10 — set charcoal
drawing on 35×60 "Normandy Barnyard"
& commenced rubbing in a frotte of
transparent color with turpentine oil
& siccatif. Home at 1.30 — heavy rain —
back at 2.30 & worked until after 5
getting my tinting over the canvas; —
as I find that I can with a turp-
rag wash off easily any tinting that
I have put on I will spend a day
or two working over it, enriching it &
more carefully adjusting harmonies &
values. Already I feel encouraged to
hope for a success equal to the cliffs, if
not greater. Home at 6 — dinner —
shelf. Read awhile — then went
to Annie & I amused ourselves with
cards, celebrating her birth day —
news from home still continues good.
To bed at 11.
