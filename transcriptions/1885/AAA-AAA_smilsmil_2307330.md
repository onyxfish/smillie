## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 3. 1885.

Another very pleasant day - After
10 when I got to studio - To work
on "Summit of Mt. McGregor & finished
it - Mr Hitchcock of Tribune called
& asked me to look over the proof-
sheets of his etching book, which
he will send to me at Montrose -
Home at 1.30 - back at 2.45 and
worked until 5 on Congress Park -
Then, for an hour or more fixing
up file of Lond. Ill. news - wrapping
& pasting the Vols. separately.
C.M. Kurtz called - Home at
6.30 - dinner - Eve'g. working
on file of back vols of Lond. News
at home - Chas came in with
two photos from "Normandy Cliffs"
- not a success - To bed about
10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 4. 1885.

Overcast, close & muggy - a very little
rain fell between 1 & 2 o'clk. Got to
studio about 10 - Finished view in
Congress Park - about 12.30 went with
Champney to Bank - then to Knoedler's -
then to Sarony's to see his Eastman
Johnston "Corn Husking" - then home to
lunch at 1.30 - back at 3.00 - Spent
most of the p.m. in revision work -
about 5 commenced packing back
vols. of Ill. Lond. News. Home at 6.30
- had dinner - wrote a letter to Koehler
on the subject of etching Johnson's
Corn Husking - Annie read aloud for
a little while & at 9 we went to the
Annual Prize entertainment of Woman's
Institute of Technology - very hot, but
we had ice cream & met Lib Howe,
& some of her friends - At 10.30
we walked up home - To bed at
11.
