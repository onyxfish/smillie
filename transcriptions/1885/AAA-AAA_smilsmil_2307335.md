## Left Page

### June, Saturday 13. 1885.

Up at 7 — clear & promising to be a hot day — a short walk before bkft — at 8.15 — some time spent in talking & lounging before I got to my work — Finished "Grand Union Hotel" and commenced on "Broadway (Saratoga) in Winter" — Dinner at 1 — Telephoned up to Bedell for horses & carriages to take a ride at 4.30 & worked until that time. Then Mary came to the house — was invited to join us but declined on acct. of her sore throat. Annie, Kate Father & I drove out beyond Varsha's when suddenly the nigh horse went very lame & I turned about & drove to the stable & got another horse in her place there drove out to Hill Side & back at 6.30 found Charl — up by 3.30 train — Tea — Self Annie, Charl & I drove out to Judge Homer Nelson's to see him, but he was not at home — Saw Mrs. N. & made appointment with her for him to-morrow at 2.30 — On our return home Annie & I went up to Mary's & spent the rest of the evef with her & Aunt Huldah — Home & to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 14. 1885.

An exceedingly hot day — none of us felt like venturing out, especially as it was known that A.P.T. was about in a state of "exaltation" as he terms it. I went to Mary's about 12 & found that he had breakfasted there had forced himself upon Mary to tell her that he would not leave the house & had taken the children to church with him — I waited until he brought them home & went away, then, at 1 I went home to dinner — At 2 Charl & I drove out to Judge Homer Nelson's (by advice of J.G. Shearman) & consulted with him for more than an hour — He wrote a letter & advised us to see a young lawyer named Rose — He would draw all papers & he (Nelson) would superintend any trial — After 4 I returned to Mary's — A.P.T. was there — I saw him & asked him if he would acquiesce in an amicable action for separation — He became highly excited & declared "never" & threatened wildly — The interview was very short — He left the house and about 5 I returned home — Tea soon after 6 — Ed there — about 8 Annie, Charl & I went up to Uncle Willie's & left Annie there while we went to 86 Academy St. I saw Mr. Rose & made an appointment for him to come to the Terrace at 10.30 to-morrow P.M. Then I ret'd to Uncle W's & visited there with Annie until 9.30 — The first time I have seen Aunt Agnes since her surgical operation — Emma was in bed & Dave Smillie adorned the front porch. Home before 10 — & to bed soon after.
