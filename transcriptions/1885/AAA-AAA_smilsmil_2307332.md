## Left Page

### June, Sunday 7, 1885

warm — rather close — part of the day, overcast — R[illegible]. at 9.
After that, some reading. Mary
Willard came in — then Chap
& Mother Cook. I wrote quite
a long letter to Koehler — also
one to Will Cook — just before
2 took letters to G. C. Depot —
then dinner — After that more
reading & a nap — very short —
The boy very fretful, but other-
wise does not seem unwell.
Wrote letter to Father — at 6 —
went to G.C.D. & mailed it &
then sauntered about for an
hour — Tea about 7 — Evely
wrote a note to J. Sinton Chap-
man. To bed before 11 —
Thunderstorms during the night.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 8, 1885

Very hot — 85° in the shade — muggy,
close & cloudy — a short dash of rain
about 7 p.m. — Evely, blew up cool.
Got to studio at 10 — finished
Saratoga Spring with drinkers — and
commenced sketch of Hilton's House.
Syracuse pictures arr[ived] — a loose
punched through sky of Adirondack
sketch — Boston pictures arr[ived]. Bl[ack]
& white Lake Geo[rge] arr[ived] from Providence
with glass broken. Got some ice-
cream & took it home, to lunch at
1.30 — back at 3 — Will sent up some
[illegible] of Photo-eng. for Saratoga work — I
rec[eived] documents from Pullman's Car
Co. office in Phila & sent safely — then
worked on Hilton's house until 6 —
then home — dinner about 7 —
Evely, wrote letters to Secretaries of
R.I. School of Design & Syracuse
Art Club — just before 10 went to
G.C. Depot with them — Runn'ing, had
a short game with Annie & to bed
before 11.
