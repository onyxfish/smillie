## Left Page

### October, Saturday 31. 1885.

*(header note: Po'keepsie.)*

Brilliant day — cold wind from the
north. B&b. after 8 — then hurried to
G. C. Depot to catch the 10.30 train —
found it did not stop at West Point —
Took 11 train & got to W. P. before —
Spent an hour or more getting
a sketch from above R.R. tunnel
looking So. Very good material —
fortunately, the place was sunny
& sheltered from the wind — at noon
lunch — then for an hour walk
about the place & returned to the
ferry at 3.15 — Took 4.25 train to
Po'k & got to Terrace soon after 5.
Found Father very feeble — not able
to stand alone — but mentally clear
& aware that he is not far from his
end. Dr. Clark, dentist, came with
some teeth that he has been finishing
for him. — I talked with Father
as much as I dared — then, felt
very uncomfortable — I went to
bed before 9.30.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 1. 1885.

*(header note: At home. N.Y.)*

Clear, bright mtf, & very cold —
B&b. about 8 — Sat with Father
most of the mtf. He talked a
good deal, altho' with difficulty,
most of the time of approaching
death & giving many instructions.
He told me how he wishes me
to finish his die of Diens, after Rosa
Bonheur. About noon I took a
short walk to the Vassar Hospital.
Dinner at 1 — near 3, when for a
few minutes I went to Mamp's — then
back to Father who was very drowsy
all the p.m. Bid him good bye at
4.20 & when I got to depot found that
the 4.50 train does not run on Sundays
— back home — found Father up & much
brighter — tea at 6 — & again left there
at 6.40 — to train by hack & reach
N.pp. on 7.05 getting in at 9.15 — 15
m. late — heavy rain — Bishops
Doane, of Albany, next seat to me —
very nearly used up with a head-
ache — Found Geo. Cook & his fiancée
Miss Hunt, at the house. To bed at
about 10.30
