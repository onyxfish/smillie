## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 28. 1885.

In bed all day with a headache.
Got up about 5 P. m. Dinner
soon after 6. Quite a thunder
storm came up about 4 o'clk &
from that it settled into a storm
of wind & rain which continued
most of the night. I was disap-
pointed that I was not able to
go to the Amateur Photographer's
rooms, to see Geo. B. Wood's "Pres-
entation of his work" — to bed
at about 11.30 & to sleep some-
time in the n't.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 29. 1885.

Cold & driving rain with some snow in
the n't — clearing to a march-like day
before noon — to studio at 10.30 — Pre-
pared a small scale drawing of my
"projected" "Normandy Barnyard" and
experimented upon changes in the
composition of the Central group of
trees — home at 1.15 — back at 2.30 —
worked on large canvas with charcoal —
a visit from Van Cleef of the Herald —
Home at 6.30 — dinner — dressed at
8 — by 8.30 Rosalie & Helen Butler
came then Emma & Mary J — Mary [illegible]
Mother Cook, Jn° Valentine — Walter &
Mary. Charles Collins — Ge°. Cook &
miss Hunt followed — Ice cream
& coffee at 10.30 — all gone soon
after 11 — then to bed.
