## Left Page

### March, Saturday 21. 1885.

Very cold — windy & dirty — After going to Studio to N.A. about 10.30 — & until 12.30 worked on a sketch after Eastman Johnson's portrait — flt. H. Warts — Then home — lunch & back at 2.30 — until 5 on sketch after Porter's portrait of a boy — Then to studio — washed some brushes — Rested awhile — There, at 6.30 to Fanny K's — Kate there — Annie soon followed — Miss Willard also then & the K's cousin, Miss Louisa Storm — Tea about 7 — In the eveng we were taught a new game of cards called "Stoho", which was described as belonging to the family of crazy patch work. It was very amusing until 10 when we started for home, leaving Kate at 21 W. 37th S. on our way — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 22. 1885.

Still cold & raw — overcast — Belle's about 9 — at 9.30 Rec'd a note from Kate asking me to come at once as Mother had a sudden colic & she had more than she could attend to — I got there before 10 & found Weather suffering a good deal of pain — St. Clair Smith had been to see her & before I left he came again — the pain ceased about noon, when I went home — Father appeared to be improving but had not yet got up. At 2 Miss Abbott came & we had dinner — There in parlor until 4 — after Miss A. left I prepared to go again to see how Father & Mother were getting on — Walter & Fanny came in so that it was 5.30 when I got to 37th E. Mother still in bed — very sore generally — Father up and evidently much better — Charl there — stayed until near 7, when we left — home to tea — Belle's p.m. out — 8.30 before Jas. Jnit. went to sleep — Then I wrote for a little while — Read awhile Harper's mag. [illegible] — bathed my feet & to bed shortly after 11.
