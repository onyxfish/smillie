## Left Page

### January, Sunday 18, 1885

Cold & rather raw day. Du[illegible] came about 11 o'clk. Took off some of the old adhesive plasters on [illegible] arm & readjusted them to the expressed discomfort of the boy. Mary Willard called a little earlier —
At 20 m. before 1 Annie & I started out for a walk — went up Mad. Ave to 72d St, to & through Central Park — out at 59t St. & 5th Ave. Dinner at 2.
Mary Ireland came in about 3 — feeling very heavy I took a nap —
wrote my letter to Father — Tea at 7. Eve'g, sat with Annie in the dining room — wrote a long letter to Geo. Whitney — after 11 when I went out to mail it & up to G.C.D. with Father's letter, then home & to bed at 12 o'clk.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 19, 1885

Cold, raw day. To studio at usual time. & after some house work commenced retracing large w.c. on to the drawing paper about 20×29 — Etretat Beach from the last end cliffs — Did not feel like work & made slow progress. Home to lunch & back as usual — Worked for awhile, then made up a bundle of my etching pf. & went to Keppel's in 16th St — he said he could do nothing with my smaller plates but was interested in "my scored neighbor's Henl[illegible]yard." — I had only two on hand — on fine Jap. paper taken by Kimmel & Voigt — these (20 of them) I agreed to sell to him without signature, to be sold by him out of town — at $1. each — & he took them — Stopped at Knoedler's —
Home soon after 6 & had dinner — Eve'g feeling very stupid — I stayed at home to make things pleasant for Annie. Wrote awhile in my mem. books — [illegible] of my work — To bed about 11 —
