## Left Page

### January, Friday 30, 1885

Dull, overcast & mieder. After
10 when I got to studio. Then
to work, after scraping off some
of yesterdays work — on central
a group of trees in Meadow Pond
& before lunch hines had them
rubbed in — then home &
after lunch, feeling very tired
knowing that I had a nights
work before me I deliberatley
fixed myself for a long nap —
Did not get up until 5 — then to
Studio & fixed up things there —
Dinner at 6. At 8 took A. & Miss Wms
to Met. Opera "Die Walküre" — left
them — bought ticket of speculator for
50 cents — then went to Private View
f W.C. Exhib at N.A. f.D — Before 10
was thoroughly smoked — there, I
returned to Opera — Got in before
end of 2nd act — good standing place
in Parquette — then up stairs — my
seat not a good one — but got a good
one near Annie — Did not affect
the opera to end before 1.15 but by
cutting we got out at 12.30 — I had
more for my money than I could
digest — of course I could neither,
understand or enjoy it — Took Miss
Williams home — after 1 when I
got to bed — Mr. Jim had a circus
for about two hours — from 3 to 5 —

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 31, 1885

Up late — waited until after
10 for Dr. Yale — bandages removed
from Master Jim — much music —
Dr. says the bone has knit properly
but bandages had better be kept on
2 or 3 days longer — Dr. walked with
me to Studio & went in with me.
I did little work before I went
home to lunch at 1.30 — near 3 when
I returned — worked as long as I
could see — Dinner at 6. Eve'g.
feeling rather played out — I
begged off from going to the Morse
entertainment — Annie gave up
good naturedly & read aloud
for awhile from Julian Hawthorne's
biography of his father & mother —
Bathed my poor feet & to bed
at 10.30.

Burpee's Day — W.C. Exhib at N.A.
my "Wayfarers" — & sketch near
that — cliffs — were sold.
