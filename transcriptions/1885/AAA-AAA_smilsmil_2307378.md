## Left Page

### September, Monday 7, 1885

Dubious sort of day — Raw in the mtt — growing milder — got a cheering telegram from Kate about Father before 10 A.m. Went down town after 10 — was engaged there until near noon. The girls off on a pic-nic to Hart Lake — I pruned trees & kindles fire in Etching Room preparatory to going to work there — Dinner at 1.30 — then down town & telegraphed to did Howe. In the mtt I saw Kasson & Co. "Post" about the proposed road through my lane — again up in the trees sawing out dead wood — Down town again at 6.30 — Tea at 7. Evett reading Balpen & then a game at Bazeque all joining — near 11 when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 8, 1885

Overcast mtt, steady rain in the afternoon & heavy at night — About 9 a.m. Amice & the girls (Marion on horse back) started out for a ride — joined by Mr. & Mrs. Park — they went to creamery at So. Montrose & did not get back until near 1 — I worked most of the mtt in Etching Room, laying grounds — Down town about noon — Dinner at 1.30 — afternoon etched awhile on die (Jones) for A.B.N. Co. Soon after 5 went down town — got a most encouraging letter from Mary telling of Father's improvement — Tea soon after 7 — Before 8, carriage came to take the girls to Parks' — Amice & I backed out — we enjoyed our own fireside. Soon after 11 the girls returned & we got to bed.
