## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 21. 1885.

Another extremely hot day —
I was routed out about 5 A.m. &
had a thorough clearing out. A few
drops of rain fell — Help. at 9 —
after that W. & Mrs. E. went to the
consecration of a Children's hospital
a beneficence of Mr. Trask's. While I
wrote a letter to Kate & then with
A. went to Washington Hall & saw
Lib Howe — I went down from back
to a swell lunch at 1. Bishop
Doane of Albany & Mr. Trask the
guests — Champagne etc — Mr. Bishop
in uniform & amusing — benignant
& modeled closely after the English
Tradition. They left at 3 & then
we napped. Tea at 6. At 6.30
we went out riding for about
two hours — then A. & I went
down to the hotels & Park and
sampled the Bands — very few
people indeed — Extremely
warm night — back at 10.30
& to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 22. 1885.

Another very hot day — Help. at 8.
Annie & I went for Lib Howe & at 9
we went to Mr. McGregor Station &
had a delightful ride to the top of
the Mt., getting there at 9.45 — The
views of the Greene Mts. were very
beautiful & the air delightful. We
of course saw Genl. Grant's cottage, but
he was too ill to be out to-day — In 20
m. the train ret'd & we got back to
Saratoga in about 40 m. Annie
went with "Lib". & I ret'd to the E's.
Lunch at 1 — after that, lounging &
napping & reading — for a time we
all sat upon the balcony until
dinner at 6. Mr. Thompson called.
Then we called on Mrs. Wayland —
about 8.30. Mr. & Mrs. E., A. & I started
for the U.S. Hotel to hear the music.
Took Lib Howe along with us. Sat
on U.S. piazza until after 10 —
to bed about 11, after packing our
trunk.
