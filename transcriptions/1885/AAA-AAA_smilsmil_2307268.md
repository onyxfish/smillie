## Left Page

### January, Saturday 24. 1885.

Nasty day — snow in the mg — then heavy rain that made slush
on a bed of ice. Cleared at night. To studio at 10 & to work on big
W.C. over which I am getting very hopeless — do not believe that I
can pull it through. Home to lunch & back at usual time & worked as
long as I could see. — Dinner at 6. At 7.30 took Annie to Ed. G's where
she met Mary C. & Alex Oakey & wife & [illegible] theirs went to see a little French play
at the old Union League Club Theatre — I Ret'd home & spent the eveng making
up my accts for the year 1884 & made clear to myself the pleasant fact that
by the most favorable showing I Ran behind for that year more than $500.
Annie came in soon after 11. It was 11.30 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 25. 1885.

Due day — Spent the day loafing about the house. About 1 went with
Annie to her mother's in 39th St.
George Cook is getting along slowly. Took a 15 minute walk before dinner
at 2. The afternoon was spent as was the morning. Dr. Jaee called
in the mg & readjusted the boy's bandages. Tea at 7. The boy did
not go to sleep until about 9 o'clk & after he had had a row — then I
read for awhile & after 10 wrote my letter home — near 11 when I
took it to G.C.D. — then home to bed about 11.30.
