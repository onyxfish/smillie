## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 4, 1875

Extremely stiff & sore. D.
Vail came — examined
my knee & concluded that
no serious damage had
occurred but advised
absolute quiet for some
time. Great pains in the
back from head & neck
so that I could only move
my head a few inches to
one side or the other — back
very stiff — Rec'd a long letter
from Oscar & Emma D. Had
a number of calls so that
the first day in bed did
not prove very tedious.
Geo. has to wait on me as they
I were a baby — The Tweedy's
Dalcott's Taylors & D. Riley's
am'd this P.M. — Every
Geo. Read news papers &
me turned in about 10.
[illegible — ~3 words]

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 5, 1875

Improvement quite rapid to-
day — had a large number of
callers. Isaac Riley, D. & Elisha
Mulford. Mr. Tweedy. Mr. Cheshire.
Priscilla P— Mollie Sayre, how
[illegible], & others in the eveg.
Aunt H. & uncle Mr. & Mrs. Mulford
Mrs. M. & Dutton Wells. When
not engaged with visitors I
spent my time reading
a book lent me by Mr. Tyler,
"Barriers Burned Away" by Rev.
E. P. Roe. Took my meals
regularly, had very little
fever & feel tolerably well —
everything considered — lay
on my bed & read until 10.30,
then put out the light & tried
to sleep — Got along pretty
well.
