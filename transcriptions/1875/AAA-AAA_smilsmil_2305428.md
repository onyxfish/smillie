## Left Page

### May, Friday 28, 1875

Wild — cloudless — delicious — Up & [illegible] at 7.30 — then W. W. arrived in a carriage & we drove along the beach to the inlet where he hired a boat & we set out for a out of sailing or fishing — horribly pestered with gnats — where we first stopped to fish — then sailed up back of Brigantine Island — first rate sailing breeze — came to anchor in a new place & fished for nearly an hour — W. W. caught a shark about 3 feet long but was unable to land him — I caught a sea spider — that was all our sport — sailed again — then ashore at 11.30 & rode to hotel — I sketched in cedar — Dinner at 1.30 — another sketch on the beach until 3 — at 4.15 all off for [illegible] where we arrived at 7 — carriage waiting — tea at 8 — all sunburned — tired — happy — Every spent chatting & telling Mrs. W. of our trip — Young Gratz called for a few minutes — [illegible] bed at 10 —

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 29, 1875

Charming day, getting to be overcast during the evening. [illegible] at 8 o'clk. At 9.45 I started out & went to new bldg of Penna. Academy of Fine Arts — then to new City Hall — then took Walnut St. car & rode out to West Phila — 3811 Chestnut St. & called on Mr. Wm. Main — Found the old gentleman very feeble & a perfect mental wreck — not gone. Thence took cars back & at 12 called on Miss Gratz — then to the Bogg's — there until 2.40 — then to W. W.'s at 3. Dinner — After that, as I felt decidedly unwell — I lay down & dutifully out before tea at 6.30 — about [illegible] — all of us met up in the carriage to Mr. Lewis's — a very pleasant little company there, but I was unfit to enjoy it — back at 10.45 & right to bed — splitting headache and pulse 110 —
