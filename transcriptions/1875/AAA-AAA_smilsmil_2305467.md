## Left Page

### August, Monday 16, 1875

Pleasant day — that is, there was no rain. Got to studio at 8.30 & to work soon after g. on "Hudson R. from Sunny Brook farm". Mr. Tweedy with Mr. Tiffany Mr. & Miss Sackett came to studio with Geo. To dinner at 1, back at 2. D. Mulford came, then Mr. Isaac Riley. I worked until 6 but did not finish what I had hoped to. To tea at 6, but delayed ½ an hour waiting for Mr. Dud & Miss Vic. D. who came at 6.30 — About 7 Rt? to studio & washed up — then to house and dressed — then down town and at 8.30 at Jan M's — a little company — left at 10.30 — got to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 17, 1875

Pleasant but very warm day — no rain, altho' showers passed near us. All day under the influence of a headache so that I could not work. I fussed at [illegible] doors, drawing stakes for walks & fences etc. — Dinner at 1.30 but ate very little. Got to studio at 2 — found a cow in just the position I wanted, set palette in a hurry & "menu" for her — but only got sketch. Rubbed in when she picked herself up & cleared out. Then, for the rest of P.M., I devoted myself to a maple tree trunk. — Painted until 6 — then to tea — back to studio & washed up — headache returning, I lay upon bed until after 8 — then dressed & went to Betty S — there until near 11 — walked up the hill with Miss Butler. To bed about 11.30.
