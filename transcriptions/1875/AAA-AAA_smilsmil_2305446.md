## Left Page

### July, Saturday 3, 1875

Charming day, warm, but
tempered by a strong breeze.
Bkfst. at 7. The mrf. spent
wandering about, part of the
time alone. Sat under the
Kittredge pine trees & listened
to what it had to say. Dinn.
abt. Mr. & Mrs. B. went to NY.
at 1½ o'clk. but Deacon Freeland
was left in their place. At
same time Ella, Gel. & I went
to Lake Mohegan, hired a boat
& rowed, later, Emma & Dea. T.
joined us. Lucy K. & Violet B.
all in the same boat, gathered
pond lilies, listened to birds
& rowed until 7. Then home to
tea. Met J.C. Nicole on our way
out. Evely spent chatting
— to bed at 10 and slept until

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 4, 1875

Threatening & overcast with strong
wind from S.E. Herbert Beecher,
youngest son of H.W.B. — took meals
with us. The mrf. with Emma
[illegible] until 11 o'clk. then off
with Miss Sallie K. until 1.
wandering on the hill tops and
loafing under the pine. To
dinner abt. 1.15. At 3 o'clk.
Ella, Herbert B., Geo. & I drove
out to Nicoll's. but he was not
in — Saw Mrs. N. Back at 6 —
went all over the Beecher house.
Tea at 6.30 — Evely Read
aloud a B. sermon. "The
after life in Relation to his."
To bed at 10.45. [illegible — ~2 lines]
