## Left Page

### June, Monday 7, 1875

At last the rains and plenty of it — Bkfst. before
8 — then in to town — the
Rain did not begin until
after 10 — Wrote letters to
Claghorn on Centennial
business — Dinner about
1 — Uncle W. called in the
m'f — Saw my "On the Saco"
& accepted it — Slight, but
steady Rain all the afternoon
wrote a letter to Anna Cook
Held up (Rain) about 6, so
that again I went into
town — Tea at 6.30 — Every
got away in general gab
and I to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 8, 1875

Glorious day. Bkfst. at
7.45 — then in to town and
much of the m'f was spent
with [illegible] about his mill —
Back home at 11 after
a charming walk. Dinner
at 1 — Read until near
3 — then started out with
Water Colors and made
a sketch on Livingston
St. but did not get it
quite finished — Father
came & I walked with
him until 6.30 — then in
to tea — Rob't Palmer
& wife called until 9 o'clk
— more family gossip
& to bed soon after 10.
