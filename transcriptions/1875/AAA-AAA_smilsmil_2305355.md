## Left Page

### January, Saturday 2, 1875

Snow & sleet in the mrg., clearing in the afternoon & a tornado of wind at night. Got to work on my drawing about 9 o'clk. & stuck to it until dinner time, 1 o'clk. At 2, at it again & did not give up until about 4 when I went out to walk. Went down Levingston St. to the River & saw many "bits" that I would like to have in my "bag". In at about 5, as it took more strength than I had to stumble & trudge over the rough roads and through the snow. Tea at 6.15. Emy did some reading & writing & got to bed at 10.45.
Geo. went down to N.Y. in the 9.12 train.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 3, 1875

Glorious day, mild & bright. After a good scrubbing, got my dcps. at 9 o'clk — wrote a letter to W. Whitney then went in to town to ch— Heard a very tame & sermon from Jas. Beecher — "Our duty as christians to paupers" — Dinner at 1 o'clk — again writing until almost 4 — then went to Mattis and with Ed. went to Gronlp's & out to College Hill — In at 5.30. Father tumbled over a chair back as he lighted the gas, no bones broken. Tea at 6 — Emy wrote to W. T. Richards. E. J. Magoon & J. W. Lewis. [illegible — ~4 lines]
