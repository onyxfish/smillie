## Left Page

### July, Sunday 11, 1875

Warm & muggy morning. Bkfst. at 8.30 — devouring flies that persist in sticking much closer than brothers torment more than musquitors. W+ went to D.R. Ch. Cor. of Mill & Catherine & heard Mr. Elmendorf — Luke 19th Ch. 41-2° V— A very good sermon I have no doubt, but I was too intent upon other things. After calling at Matts to dinner at 1 — P.M. note to S.C.A. & Josie Bentty & then as it had cleared most charmingly I walked out by Judge Minott's & down to Cemetery back to tea at 6.45 — Every trying to write out a plan of a will for Father.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 12, 1875

Still delightfully cool. Up & the m+, worked on "Baby Bacchus" — Dinner at 1 o'clk Uncle W., Aunt A., & Emma B. made a flying call — Dunly p.m. Finished drawing and packed up. Mrs. Thorne called for a few moments on her way to Saratoga. Tea at 6 o'clk. Bid good bye and at 7.23 took Saratoga Exp. & joined to 42nd St. depot at 9.30 right to Düring's — found them waiting for me & after chatting & unpacking trunk I got to bed at 10.45.
