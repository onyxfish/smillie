## Left Page

### September, Saturday 25. 1875.

Another glorious day — R[illegible] at 8. Then down town — arranged to have Horace Allen bring up Mitchell's "Jack" in the m[orning] + Sear's "Calp[?]. Jack" in the[?] m. Ab[ou]t 10 got to work out of doors + kept at it until 12 — Then to dinner — Gratz went home. Back to studio at 1.30 — at 2 Allen came with "Calp[?]. Jack" — Stationed him outside of my big window + I worked from him in-doors — an arrangement that gives me great comfort. Several calls — Kitty B. + Mr. Schafer among others — to tea at 6.30 at 7 back to studio + cleaned up. Rain commenced about 8 o'clk. Down to P.O. — then to Fain's went with her to D. Smith's for Ben. Gans at the house — which + then a romp until 11 — to bed at 11.30 — Heavy Rain all night.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 26. 1875.

Breezy, cloudy day — R[illegible] at nearly — Then up to studio until church time — Then to Baptist Ch. Cheshire has ret[urned] from his vacation and preached from John. 3. Ch[ap]. 30 v. "He must increase but I must decrease." Christ or Christianity must increase. Judaism + all opposed must decrease. A very long sermon. Walked up with Flora S. + Bessie C— Dinner at 1.30 — to Studio at 3 — notes to S.C.F. Father + Dining — Had two or three callers — Tea at 6 — After tea called on Dr. Vails — Then to studio, fixed fire + got letters. Then to Fain's. Gil came in — sat there until 10 — then, at 10.30, to bed.
