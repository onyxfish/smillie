## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 24, 1875

Very pleasant day. Got up feeling somewhat out of sorts no doubt the result of last night's meeting. Got to studio about 9.30 & to work on 2 large cartoon of Horse Race — trying to locate it on the plains somewhere. Worked until near 2 without accomplishing much — at to dinner at 2.15 — at 3.30 dressed & at 4.15 was at Uncle Al's — 800 Fifth ave. Junie was married to Ch[arles] Gunther at 4.45 — quite a gay crowd of 50 or so — at 5, a supper — at 7.30 I rode down with Sister Mary. Heard that Tyler has thrown down a part of my fence in Montrose — at 8.30 at the Kimball's — at 10.30 at the studio and at 11.30 to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 25, 1875

Thanksgiving — Up late & got to studio at 9.45. Glorious day — under my door found a kind note from Mr. Enoch Lewis enclosing check for $100.00 — so that makes thanksgiving for me — then to work on large Cartoon — Charl. Miles R. & Rogers came in about noon — at 1, walked up with them to 41 st. — Turkey dinner — at 2.45 back to studio and worked as long as I could see — then made letters to Messrs Enoch Lewis & John Sarbaie — to tea at 7 — At 8, walked up to Will's — there until 9.45 — then walked down to 41 [st.] — to bed at 10.30.
