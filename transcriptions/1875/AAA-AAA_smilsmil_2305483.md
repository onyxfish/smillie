## Left Page

### September, Friday 17, 1875

Bleak, cold, miserable day — could not get ther. in my studio up to 60°. The wind going through the place as through a sieve. Got to pluck at [illegible] but it was sometime before I got to work on 23×44. Painted in the ground — Dinner at 1.30 — back at 2.15. Painted until 5. Tremendous wind & growing colder all the time. Ther. in studio, 55°, with as big a fire as I can blow up. Wrote letters to Whitredge asking about [illegible] terminal art matters. Tea at 6.20. Then dressed — went to P.O. cold driving rain — Then at 7.30 to Fain's — waited at 8. Fan, Ben [illegible] & I went to Alice Stroud's — a pleasant company of young folks — left at 12.15. Beautiful moonlight night. To bed at [illegible].

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 18, 1875

Cold, cloudy, blustering m'f. Got to studio a little before 9 & blew up the fire. Wrote letters to Charl. Dining & [illegible] and about noon went down to J. Sear's to bid Josie good-bye — found that she is not to leave until rarey next week — invited to tea to-night. Dinner at 1.15 — then to studio & to work on 23×44. Fan — Mr. Ch. [illegible] & Miss Shemard called — worked until 6.10, when at my canvas covered. & I got to Sear's a little [illegible] 7 — Sear's son after. At [illegible] o'clk. we went up to Fan's to bed about 10.30.
