## Left Page

### August, Friday 20, 1875

After a night of very little sleep,
up at 11 o'clk. At 4.30 down the
hill to Mrs. Sears's — glorious moon-
light, East, all bright. Swallowed
some bread & tea — there with Nate McC-
& Ge. got to depot at 5 a.m. Swickhammer
at 7 — Bkfp — (very poor) & at 8.46 train
to Elmira where we arr'd at 12.30 — Ge.
Diveen met us with carriage & drove
at once to the general's where we were
expected. Maud & Ella met us at the
door & at once we went into Jack's room,
washed our faces & hands & then down &
into the presence of the dead — Mrs.
D— was but little changed & lying
with her face turned to her right —
seemed to sleep almost as if asleep —
Lunch was served to us, when S.
walked with Gen. D. about his grounds
& newbuds to the tower. At 3 the
funeral service commenced — then
was singing by a quartette with
solemn effect — after an immense
cortege set out for the cemetery —
at 5 we got to the general's — had
dinner — the girls would not listen
to our leaving, so we stayed all
night — the evng being passed
walking outside with Maud & Jack
& in the library with Maud & Ella
until 10 — when we went to bed.
The day had been glorious, and
in most striking contrast to the
sadness of the occasion —

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 21, 1875

Up about 8 o'clk. Delightful mfg.
Bkfp at 8.30 — the rest of the mfg
was spent out of doors about the
house, under the trees and in the
grape house. It was really a
relief to notice the quiet demeanor
of the family — A lunch was
prepared for us at 12 — at 12.25
we were driven to the depot — we
started at 12.55 — got to Swickhammer
at 3.56 — transferred to the M.R.R.
& reached Montrose at 6 — very
tired & very dirty, the trip
having cost $6.78 — walked up
to town — tea at Mrs. S's at 6.45 —
Seth walked down to P.O. —
met Fanny M. & Hetty S. — went
home with Hetty — then to Fan's —
the & Mrs. Post & Sue H. came in —
then Ge. — there until after 10 —
then up & to bed at 11.
