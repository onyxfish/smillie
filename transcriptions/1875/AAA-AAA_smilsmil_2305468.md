## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 18. 1875.

Very heavy rain until after 10 — Did not get up until after 9 — then got a strong cup of tea + went down the hill. Newton to fan's — had a game of whist + bid Mr. Mam[illegible] good bye. Up to Studio at 12 — Dinner abt 1 — again to Shop, but I accomplished but little besides making some sketches for studio furniture. The P.M. close + muggy — Tea at 6 — Evg. Jet + I called on Priscilla at Ge B's — then on Mr. Hunt J — then on Lillie J — then to see Mrs. Julea[illegible]'s night blooming Cereus — a beautiful sight. Met Miss Sackett — walked up the hill with [illegible] + got to bed about 11 —
I worked on a Chalk sketch of the woman of Samaria after mill for M. Cheshire on S.S. Blackboard from 4.30 until 6 P.M.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 19. 1875.

Very pleasant mf. and yet so cool that I made up fire in Studio. Went to work on "Bridal Veil Fall" trying to get it finally finished up. Hetty B. + Miller, her fiancee, called to join B— About noon there came a telegram from Maud D. saying that her mother would be buried at 3 P.M. to-morrow — It was a great shock — I went down + told this news to Fan. + to Kate. Mr. C. me arranged to start for E. to-morrow mf., then I went to my dinner abt. 1.15. then to Studio + continued work on "B. V. Fall" — Fan + Priscilla called — I worked until 6 — then to Tea — After, Kit + I cleared up — then down town to P.O. — Got a letter from Snyder who says he has suitable brushes that I have not [illegible] — wrote him — mailed it — to bed at 9.45 — but not to sleep.
