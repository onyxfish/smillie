## Left Page

### June, Thursday 3, 1875

Quite cool + overcast — B[illegible] —
at 7.30 — after that, a walk with
Father — In before 9 — My head
still very uncomfortable — I
spent a good part of the
m[orning] reading newspapers
\+ did a little writing on the
correspondence that has now
got away ahead of me. —
Dinner at 12.30 — After
that a little nap — Then
wrote to the Tweedys and
to Fau M. — A short walk
with Father before tea at
6.30 — In the evey, some
more writing and to bed a
few minutes after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 4, 1875

No Rain yet — near 8 when
I took [illegible]. Most of the m[orning]
was spent wandering in the
old Livingston woods, after
some writing. The singing of
the birds was very enjoyable —
Back to dinner about 1 o'clk —
again writing, finishing letters
to Mr. Geo. Whitney — Kurtz — D[r.]
Noyes — During — Miss Sawtelle
and others. — About 5 went out
with the horse + sprinkled
and watered front and rear
as well as the road. — Tea
between 6.30 + 7 — Then Father
amused himself until quite
8 o'clk. with the new hose — Then
I went into town just ab[ou]t with
letters — called on Matt — there
until 10 — Then home — nearly
11 when I got to bed.
