## Left Page

### June, Saturday 5. 1875.

Close hot day with southerly
wind but none of the rain we
pray for. B[illegible] at 7.30. My
mad feeling cleaner & better —
About 9 walked into town and
did some shopping — then back
& spent a couple of hours writing —
I have already written just a
dozen letters since I came up
here — At 12 o'clk, again into town
& right back — dinner a little
before 1 — while at the Table, the
Dr. and. from N.Y. . . I stretched
paper for drawing on large and
small boards & feeling overcome
took a little nap. Then wrote a
note — then into town — At 5.30 went
out with hose & wet everything I
could reach until tea time at 6.30 —
from 7 to 8, music in the Park, very
agreeable — a short call on Mrs.
Thorne after 8 — Ed. & Matt called —
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 6. 1875.

Overcast, but only a little sprinkle
between 12 and 1 o'clk — Wife —
walked into town with Father,
Mother & Geo. Then went to the
Dutch Ch[urch] — a long, formal
service. Read from book — in con-
nection with the Communion —
Walked up Mill St. & met Uncle
W. & family — then to Matt's and
at 1. home in a slight shower —
Dinner at 1.30 — After that
reading newspapers until
near 4 o'clk. Then took a
walk before tea time — In
the even[ing], after dark, got
out the hose and gave the
thirsty grass a drink — To
bed soon after 10 —
