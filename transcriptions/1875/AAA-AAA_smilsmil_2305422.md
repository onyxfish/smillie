## Left Page

### May, Sunday 16, 1875

Clear, cool & breezy. Up at 8.30 — Wt. went to S. Hastings
"the choir sang" "I would not live alway" & "The Last Rose of Summer" & I left.
Went to studio & wrote a letter to Mr. Geo. Whitney & at 2.15 started for Jas. Hart's 94 First Place. Brooklyn. Boats & cars connected nicely so that I got to his house in just one hour. Dinner at 1.30. Chatted until near 4, then he & I walked to the Heights & about. He left me at Fulton ferry & at 5.40 I went to the Beach's — 96 Columbia S—
There to tea & until 9 o'clk. Very pleasant, informal call. Crossed Fulton ferry at 9.15 — glorious moonlight — walked up to 23d St. In at 10.30 & Right to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 17, 1875

Beautiful day. At 9.15 at Dr. Noyes. He squirted some preparation of iron up my nose & back into my throat that made me miserable all day. Got to studio about 9.45 & to work in "Bridal Veil Fall". To dinner at 1 — back at 2.15 & worked until 5.20. getting over all the forego'd & some of the trees. At 4.30 Fau M. came & at 5.30 I went up to the hotel with her. Then started for 41st St. where I met Fanny & Carrie K — went home with them — took tea there & at 8.20 back to Stuyvesant Ho. & took Fau to N.A. of D —
To 41st St. about o'clk & Right to bed. [illegible — entire passage]
