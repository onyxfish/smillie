## Left Page

### December, Sunday 12, 1875

Charming day — Up at 8. had a good bath & [illegible] — then
to D. Hastings's & heard a very
good sermon from him on
Will Power & the exercise of
it — Rom. 7° ch. 18° v. After
service went to studio, wrote
a letter to father & at 3.30
went to C. S. Welles. 31 W. 39°
St where I was invited to dine —
Dinner soon after 2 — left
at 4.15. walked up to Uncle
A's — Tea there at 6 — Mr. & Mrs. Ch.
Gunther at home. Arndt & Farts
for the South to-morrow — At 7.30
walked down to Holsworths Ch.
He preached of veils over the heart
from 2° Cor. 3° ch. 16° v. Out at 9.30 —
glorious moonlight — walked
down to studio — wrote a letter
to Santaine there to H'l S.S. &
to bed at about 11.

## Right Page

### December, Monday 13, 1875

A real March day, with
sunshine wind & snow squalls.
Gab's studio at 9 o'clk. At
10.30 Mr. Delleuw came & I
worked from him until 12 o'ck.
Then to work outlining on big
drawing — To dinner at 12.45 —
back at 2 — Chas. Parsons
came in & we made arrange-
ments to have my big subject
eng'd. & published in Harper's
Weekly — Worked at outline
as long as I could see — To
Renner's & 6 & gave final
order for frames for big seep.
To tea at 7. At 8.10 at Miss
Burdelle's — 42° St. At 9. Studio —
wrote a letter to W. J. Jones on
Artists of N.Y. & Centennial Gifts
which I did not send. At
10.30 to H'l S.S. & all to bed.
During a heavy snow storm.
