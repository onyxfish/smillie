## Left Page

### February, Saturday 27. 1875.

overcast, raw and disagreeable.
Heavy snow fell from 2 to 5 o'clk.
Then it cleared brisk & cold.
Got to studio at 9 o'clk. and
to work on Saco. No 2. Got
it very much better so
that now I think it as good
a drawing as I have made.
To dinner at 1.15 after being
detained by Mrs. Pommning
& Miss Burdell. Back at 2 ¼ —
then snow. Miss Abbatt &
sister called. Yale came &
worked at my desk — to tea
at 7 — at 8.30 at studio and
at 9.30 at N.A. Great crowd.
Did not close until 10.30 —
waited until I saw darkness
fall upon the 8th annual [illegible]
of the A.S. of P. in N.C. — then
to 41st St. & to bed at 11.40

## Right Page

### February, Sunday 28. 1875.

Very cold and windy. In
the m'f. went to D. Hastings
& heard him preach from
Luke 18th Ch. 1st v. "men
ought always to pray and
not faint". After service
walked to Mad. Ave. cars with
Josie Jessup & then [illegible] — then
walked alone up to their house
& dined there. At 4.30 walked
down the ave to Uncle A's —
then wrote a letter home —
then over to Will's — Charle
there — took tea & at 8 me
walked to 7th ave cars — I walked
to 41st; wrote awhile &
got to bed about 10.30
