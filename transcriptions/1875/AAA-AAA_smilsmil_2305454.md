## Left Page

### July, Monday 19, 1875

Glorious, clear, cool day —
R[ose?] at 7.30 — Then down
town — called on Mr. [illegible]
about board on behalf of the
Jacketts. Then harnessed
H. & Tyle's horse to wagon and
spent the rest of the a.m.
transferring my things from
W. J. it's house to studio —
Dinner about 1. Spent
the afternoon getting every
thing [unpacked?] & distribut[ed].
Tea at 6. [illegible] saw [illegible]
but can get no information
out of him as to cost of moving
my building — Trimmed lamp
of S.E.T. & wrote to Miss Sackett
& to Miss [Abbatt?]. Then went
down to [bed?] in my room —
to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 20, 1875

Very pleasant day, but overcast
with threats of change. Spent
the [morning?] in studio making
closets out of packing boxes.
Found that I got lots of
room in a very economical
way. Dinner at 1.15 —
Then spent the P.M. until
5 o'clk arranging things —
napping, resting & finally
on the green seeing the
old men of Montrose play
base ball. At 5.30 dressed
& at 6 at Fanny Jessup's to
tea — met a large company
with many reminders of
old times. Passed a very
pleasant evng. In at 11 —
Read "[Trib?]" & to bed at 12.
