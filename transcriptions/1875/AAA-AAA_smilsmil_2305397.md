## Left Page

### March, Saturday 27, 1875

Delightful day. Shining like a mild. Went over to Hayakis — did not get to Studio until after 10 — wrote notes to N.C. [illegible], Smints, Kendall, &c and at 11.30 to 41 [illegible]. Took a bath and dressed — dinner at 12.45 — at 2 started for down town. Got to foot of Desbrosses St. at 2.40 — had to wait until 3 before all of the party came along. Gel + Annie Cook + Sm[illegible] + "Li'l" Home. Got to Germantown junction at 5.45. Met there by Jos. Wright's carriages & drove right to Hazelbrook. Haven't [illegible] all ready there. Tea at about 7 — Every, a very pleasant little company with chatting & mu[sic] until after 11. Met for the first time Mrs[illegible] Justice. Mansfield (Momotjoy) + others. To bed a little after 12 — Gel C. Haven[s] + I roommates — H. P— mu[illegible] ted fellows.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 28, 1875

*Hazelbrook. Germantown.*

Up early. Still delightful weather. [illegible] at 8 o'clk. and at about 10 all of us walked to W. Ames's Church where a grand moral baby show opened the service at 11.30. Ames began his sermon; And I saw a "New Jerusalem" &c. Sermon over at 12.30. Delay, chatting + introductions at the church door. Rode home. Dinner about 2. M. thus, Ames there. Rode in the Park. Very muddy, towards sundown, overcast. In between 6 + 7 and a half an hour after. Every spent in general chatting until 10.30 — to bed soon after 11.
