## Left Page

### June, Sunday 27, 1875

The hottest day yet. Ther. over 90°. Mrs f. went to ch.
stopping first at Episcopal ch. in Market St. and heard
the singing — then to the Congregational ch. on Mill [illegible].
Heard a young man Read a composition upon "smoking
hay". Saw Uncle W. & made sure that he got out home
last night safely. Home to dinner abt. The P.M.
spent seeking cool corners striving to sleep. Tea at 6.
Evef. wrote notes to Ferg. Enyma Beach & Anna [illegible].
Took them to P.O. abt 11 o'clk then home & to bed.
Father feels better, but not able to go out.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 28, 1875

Very hot m[illegible], but mitigated by being slightly overcast. I
waited about until 10.45 when Uncle Willie came for me — then
after a number of delays, out to Hillside — nothing done before
dinner abt. Soon after 2 got to work out of doors and
by 6 o'clk. had got all done that I can do for the present.
I am much dissatisfied with the result. Tea at 6.30 and
at 7.30, David drove in town & I with him — a great change
in the weather — all the P.M. a strong, cool breeze, making
a difference of nearly 20°. Very much surprised on
getting home to find that Father had gone down to
[illegible]. To bed about 10.30
