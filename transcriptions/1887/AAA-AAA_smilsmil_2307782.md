## Left Page

### November, Sunday 20, 1887

Overcast m[orning] — clear afternoon —
growing much colder — B[reakfast] late — Between 10 & 11 Annie
Jim & I went down to Aunt Annie's
(Ireland) in E. 17th St where we had
a pleasant visit until 1 — then home
Dinner at 2 — After that, read
awhile, then wrote a letter to
Mary & Kate at P.O.K. & at 5 went
out & mailed it at the G.C. Depot
then walked on up to 57th St and
back home by way of 5th Ave. —
Eve'g had something to eat at 7
Charl came in for a while —
I wrote a letter to Kaehler &
before 10 took it to the G.C.D. to
mail — then to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 21, 1887

Charming day — went to studio
in the m[orning] to meet paper hangers
as agreed with young McCormack on
Friday — but no paper hangers — At
10.30 went to 9 W. 17th St saw Beman
& complained to him — then to O'Neil
photographer — Cor 16th St & Union Sq —
left with him small photo. f[rom] Mrs.
Geo. L. Rives & daguerreotype of Annie's
father to be photo'd enlarged — Then
to A.B.N. Co — saw Will — presented bill
& was paid $120 — Then to 2d Nat. Bk
& deposited it together with Mr. Rives
check of $150 — payment for etched por-
trait of A.B. Durand — then to studio
found that the men had been there &
left a bar paper to put on partition. I
left a note saying I would have only
a good stout paper. Then home. Lunch
[at] — afternoon, worked on Ruppert
made many drawing, making changes.
At 5 went to studio — met Gel & went with
him to the Century — home at 6.15 — Dinner —
Every went along to Bible lecture by Rev
Mr Calthrups — after that went to see Otto
Wolf's painting "Christ & the Adulteress"
then home & to bed at 11.
