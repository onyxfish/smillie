## Left Page

### April, Thursday 28, 1887

A day of showers — most of the time wet. To studio about 10 and to work on A.B.N. Co. drawing — a view of the Po'k bridge — made little progress the work is so very distasteful to me. To bank, then home to lunch — took fine to Tinder gartin in quite a shower — then to studio — worked on bridge until 5 — then to Flint & Co's — in 14th St. about furniture — Home soon after 6 — again heavy rain. Dinner — Kate, the cook, gone to Brooklyn yesterday, sick — Margaret officieating in her place. At 8 Annie & I went to Daly's Theatre where we saw Mr. Tamine [illegible] in Shrew beautifully put upon the stage — a succession of beautiful Tibleaux vivants. I enjoyed it very much — after 11 when we got home to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 29, 1887

A very pleasant day with a sharp shower about 4 o'clk. At 9.30 went to Union League Club & saw Chase for a few minutes — then to studio at 10 o'clk. Went in & saw Calverly — then started on large size charcoal sketch of a piece of modeling that I think of doing for a Montrose fireplace. At 12 o'clk went to A.H. Ass'n & saw Sutton for a moment about etching after Eastman Johnson's "Stage Coach" painting — He has seen Klackner the print publisher & he says it would make a popular print. So that is "off" — Went to Wakefield Rattan Co & bought two chairs for Montrose. Home to lunch at 1.30 — After that, with Annie to 3rd Prize-fund zybit at A.A.A. met Mr. & Mrs. Vinton — Miss Hallowell & others there — out at 4.30 — to studio & worked until 6 on fireplace sketch — Chase came in — walked up home with him & [illegible]. Dinner at a little before 7. [illegible] some reading & writing and to bed soon after 10.
