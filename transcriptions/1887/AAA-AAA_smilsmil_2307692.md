## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 24, 1887.

Overcast part of the time — a hot day with damp, cool breeze blowing. To studio at 10 — men at work in Wallers with shellac — cleaning etc. Will came about Po'k bridge drawing — with die [illegible] forms to touch — I worked faithfully on that work — Home at — back at 2.30 — Man came at 4 for my drawings — Kept him until 4.30 — then he took them — at 5 went to N.A. of D. Exhibition of much Paintings for Educational Purposes — very little that was interesting to me — Private view. Home at 6.30 — Suel f. George Cook came & delivered to Annie all of her possessions in shape of Bonds etc. Later Clark came.
It was 11.30 before I got to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 25, 1887.

Hot day with threats of showers in the afternoon, which amounted to nothing. To studio before 10 — To work in a general way preparing to move & getting ready to go to Montrose. The floor of No. 13 was shellaced. I made a list of Annie's securities & took them to the C°. Nat. Bank Safe deposit vault & put them in our box there. Hale came in for a while — Home before 1.30 — lunch — then a rest. It was after 3 when I returned — moved a number of my things down into my new studio — & packed my etching plates for Montrose. 6.30 when I got home, dinner — Suef — read newspaper — wrote a note to Cesnola thanking him for his note announcing to me my election as Patron of Met. Museum of Fine Arts.
