## Left Page

### March, Friday 11. 1887.

Bright & cold — To studio at
10 — Got out 20×30 canvas "Evening
the Pond" — glazed over it & then
to work painting — Abt 4 o'clk went
to Knoedler's — saw Delme — accepted
his offer of $225 for W.C. "Stretch—"
& got check for the suit. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — to studio at 3
& painted as long as I could
see — home soon after 6 — dinner
— even'g writing notes & reading
— To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 12. 1887.

Bright & pleasant — To studio
at 10. made changes in A.B.N.
Co's drawing of Train with sleeping
cars. About 1 went to Bank — &
having sent W.C. drawing "the Narrows,
[illegible], from Blk. Mt. Trail". to Richards
I stopped there to see him, but
he was not in — then home to lunch.
At 3.30 met Charl at G.C. Depot
& to P'k by that train getting to the
terrace before 6. Found Mother
in much the same conditions as
when I last saw her — very weak
& looking exceedingly frail — After
tea we spent some time chatting
with her — later, a wood fire was
kindled in front room & there,
Charl, Mary & I chatted until
after 11 when we went to bed —
Ed Parker there a part of the
time.
