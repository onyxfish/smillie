## Left Page

### March, Saturday 5, 1887

Bright m'g., overcast afternoon & snow at night. To studio about 10 — Spent most of the m'g working on W. C. "The Narrows. Lake George, from Trail on Black Mt." — Put in a foreground figure. — Home ab't 30 — back before 3 — Set palette & worked on 11×24 "Study at Marblehead Neck." — painting bit of rock in extreme right — etc — etc — Sent W.C. "Étretat" to Knoedler's hoping through [illegible] to sell it — went there at 5 — Roos & Knoedler abuse my frame & say I must discard it & let my big drawing go into a portfolio — very discouraging. There to N'l Art. Ass'n — Saw Sutton & Kirby — Got $15 for Repairs on damaged frame — Left p'f. of Johnson etching at Mr. Stedman's 44 E. 26th S — then home — dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g. did not go to Century (monthly meeting) but sat at home while Annie read aloud to me — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 6, 1887

A beastly day — drizzling rain freezing & clinging to everything. B'k'f't after 9 — read my paper for awhile — then wrote my letter to mother — then attended to several small chores about the house & looked after Jim while Annie went to see Mr. Ireland who has had a slight paralysis. Dinner about 2 — at nearly 4 I walked down to the "Century" & took a look at the pictures — then walked up to 44th S't to G.C.D. & mailed letter to mother — found the walk to be a pretty long pull — When I got in I had a scare, thinking that I was about to have another spasm of stomach pain but it passed away after had rested awhile — Tea between 7 & 8 — Ev'g. Annie read aloud from "Tom Brown at Rugby" until after 10 — To bed at 11.
