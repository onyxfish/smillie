## Left Page

### January, Friday 28, 1887

An overcast & rather raw day. Got to studio soon after 1 & spent the m'f painting in sheets on 11/2 × 2-1/2 — Marblehead Neck Rocks. Home abt 3.0 & back before 3 hrs. the afternoon painted "a sky & sea on little painting 'Near Connieshield's Point'" done first about 3 yrs. ago. Then walked down to Cassels & got my imperfectly bound B'ys Art exchanged for a perfect copy — Home soon after 6 — dinner. Abt about 8.30 got to Water Color Private view — where I suffered heat, smoke & a mass of uninteresting pictures until about 11 o'clk. Then home & got to bed before midnight.

Rec'd Telegram from the Ehingers announcing death of Mr. Wayland. May Lewis's engagement announced.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 29, 1887

Very heavy rain commencing at about 11 a.m. Got to studio about 10 & after a few minutes went over to N.A. of D. — Buyers Day — There for an hour or more, as Member of B'd of Control — but being from home rec'd to studio after a pleasant chat with Mr. & Mrs. Taylor (Soffie Munroe) — Home to lunch. Before 2 — Annie & Jim had gone to Orange, N.J. to spend the day with Margaret Parker White — After lunch I felt so tired out that I spent the afternoon until 5 o'clk. on lounge — then to studio. There until 6.15 — then home. Annie & Jim just in — dinner — Eve'g. I read aloud from Harper's Mag. To bed abt 11.
