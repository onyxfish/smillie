## Left Page

### October, Monday 3, 1887

Heavy showers during the m'f so that the brick layer & Judson were able to work only an hour — but the afternoon was delightful & again the chimney progressed. The Smiths painted the blinds & Herrick worked on the Cherry mantel — I got the studio cleaned up & started on arms drawing for Uncle Willie's Canadian Co — Went down town about 6 P.m. Ev'f, Annie read aloud for a while — some cribbage and pears & grapes — To bed at the usual time now — 11 o'clk — Some slight showers in the a.m.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 4, 1887

Bright for a little while in the m'f, then overcast and rain — Trall sent up horses for us to go out riding but we sent them back. The brick man persisted at his work in the m'f & finished the big chimney by noon — I worked on Hamileton Arms drawing for Uncle Willie — Herrick was sick with cholera morbus. The Smiths painted on window blinds. The afternoon was bleak & raw with some quite heavy rains — At 5 o'clk. I went down town — got horse & buggy & drove to depot of Montrose R.R. & there met Genl. A.S. Diven who has come from Elmira to deliver an address here at the Fair. Tea about 7 — Ev'f in general chatting until 10 — To bed at 11.
