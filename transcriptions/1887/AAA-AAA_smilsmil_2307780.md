## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 16. 1887.

A beautiful day — clear & fresh. Worked until noon on "Petropolis
Palais Imperial" drawing — finished it —
then out to market & to Philharmonic
office to see if we could dispose of our
seats for the season — no success — Home
to lunch at 1. Will sent for H.B.N.
Co- drawings about 2 & sent to me the
material for a bill head drawing for
Ruppert's Brewery — on which I worked
until 4.30 — Then I went to studio
helping to meet McCormick & arrange
for the speedy finishing up of the room.
Home to dinner — Charle came in &
dined with us — At 8 Annies & I went
to the second Bible lecture by Rev. W.
Cal Thrulp — home at 9.30 — I wrote a
note to W. G. S. Rives in answer to one
from him — on the subject of an
etching — posted it — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 17. 1887.

A beautiful day, but I was quite
useless with a headache — After
1 o'clock I went to studio — then to
Tyson's, at 5th Ave. Hotel — to Pond's, on
Union Sq. & to Schubert's — trying to
dispose of our Phil. tickets without
success. Then to N.A.D. — it
being varnishing day for the Fall
Exhib. & there I spent some time.
It was noon when I got home —
At 1 o'clk. I had lunch & after
that I went to bed & there spent
the rest of the afternoon — Up
at 6 & had a light dinner — at
8.30 I went to Mr. Geo. L. Rives, No.
15 W. 29th St. & talked with him of
making an etched portrait of his
deceased wife — I was disappointed
to find that he has no good photo. of her.
At 9 o'clk. I went to Ed.n Cook's — 154
E. 37th S. — & there met Rev. Mr. Cal Thrulp
& spent a very interesting two hours
with him, finding him exceedingly
enthusiastic — After 11 when I
got to bed.
