## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 8, 1887

Another very pleasant day —
Spent some time fixing things about
the house — wrote a letter to Koehler.
Got to studio about 11 o'clk. called
on Miss Abbatt — then tried to
do something in studio in the way
of getting a little order out of the
chaos — not much progress. Home
to lunch at 1 — Mary had come
down from [illegible] on a mtf. train.
In the afternoon again went to
studio & did a very little — it is
hopeless work. Charl came in
about 5 o'clk. At 5.30 I went to
Mr. Dav. Huntington's but he was
not in — Home at 6 — dinner
at 6.30 — Evelf. Geo. & Charl
came in — to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 9, 1887

*Mother Cook died last night*

Very pleasant day — Mary Sprague Cook
came before 8 o'clk. & saw me, to announce
that Mother Cook died about midnight, very
suddenly & without a struggle. I got up with
a wretched headache, dragged about for an
hour or two, then back to bed where I
stayed until after 12 — then to 2° Nat.
Bk. — then to A.B.N. Co. where I lunched
with Will & spent some time looking
over S.A. photo material from which I
selected a number of subjects for drawings.
After 3 when I rode up town with Jas.
F. Sutton — Got eyeglasses of Weyrowit[illegible] —
stopped at Studio, then home before 5 —
Sister Mary left in the mtf. & went
to Mr. Geo. Hertzel's — Dinner at 6.30 —
Evelf., freight boxes arr. from Montrose.
Spent an hour or more unpacking —
Then Charl came in — he stayed
until near 11 — then I went to bed.
