## Left Page

### May, Friday 20, 1887

Very hot — worse than mid summer. Got up with a headache which bothered me more or less all day. To studio at 10 — went to N.A. Got "Meadow Pond" over to Rose & Co's where I ordered a new frame for it to suit Mr. P. Spicer, its purchaser — sent Silleck to Mr. Huntington's — he got "The Goldsmith's Daughter" but not Durand's Portrait — worked awhile on Liberty. Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.30 — went into Tiffany's & got a little cathedral glass. Worked on Liberty until 4.30 — at 5 went to Kimmel & Voigt's — on my way back stopped at Mr. Huntington's — nothen there to studio — Home at 7 — dinner — Eve'g. wrote to Mr. P. Spicer & to Wm. g. Parks. Then with Annie to Mother Cole's. About 9 Charl came there for us — Home before 10 — Charl stayed until 10.30 — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 21, 1887

Very hot. To studio about 10. Spent rest of the mng. working on Liberty drawing — A woman at work in the old Waller Studio trying to clean it out. Mr. Waller called & delivered a second key. It was 1.45 when I got home to lunch & 3 when I got back to studio. About 4 Charl came in & the rest of the p.m. was spent photographing the two Huntington paintings — Portrait of A.B. Durand & "The Goldsmith's Daughter". I called at H's this mng & then sent Silleck who brought to me portrait of Durand. Home soon after 6 — dinner at 7. Eve'g wrote to Farrer, Ticknor & Hale. Out at 10 & mailed them — To bed about 10.30.
