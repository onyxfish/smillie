## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 4, 1887.

A fine winter day — After 10 when I got to studio — for a little time fussing with frames & passepartouts for drawings. Then to N.A. & saw the Halstead collection & the Artist Fund Annual Exh'n — a sorry lot — Then to Am. Art Ass'n — & saw Sutton about the reported lease & [illegible] rest of their galleries — I am looking for a new studio — Then to Bank — then to Schaus's & saw the remarkable Rembrandt "The Jester" — Then home to lunch 1.45 — to studio before 3 — worked on one of the cows that has given me much trouble in Oct Study — Yewell came & left his plate etc — I pasted Old Mill Creéy — into its frame — Home at 6 — Chave in for a moment — complaining — dinner — wrote a note to Yale — Jim does not seem to improve. Wet. Annie read aloud Lowell's lectures on Democracy — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 5, 1887.

Dismal day — light snow in the mf., heavy rain after 5 p.m. & through the night — Up late, Jim having been wakeful & very troublesome during the night, so that we got little sleep. To studio at 10 — some time spent over Yewell's ground roller & plate. Finished black cow in "Oct Study" after much fussing — pasted it into passepartout. Home to lunch soon after 1 — The Adams girls there — back at 2.30 — Started to put in figures in small landscape sketch that I am going to re-christen "Cowboy country". At 4 — to Wood's studio — Board meeting — mainly to consider the matter of hanging Etching Club exhibit in Corridor — Home at 6 — got soaking wet — dinner — Wet. Reading & to bed before 10 —

Dr. Yale came in the mf. and said that Jim was all right.
