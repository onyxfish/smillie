## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 21. 1887.

A very smoky day, at times over-
cast — wind from W. & S.W. Battles
with Thomas' team & Payne & team
at work — also Arch. P— & the boys
Wood & Cerny — Mr. Hotchkiss finished
the little chimney at Herrick's shop &
went home before noon. Reynolds
came with Judson as helper. Herrick
built a scaffold at the Studio chimney
& before noon it was retained. It—
[illegible] — built a scaffold at the main
chimney in the afternoon. Coyle set
out a lot of berry bushes got from
G. Fordham. The Smiths painted
Evett. I wrote notes to Littlefield
concerning a coke oven [illegible]. To W.M.S.
& to the Stewards of Century telling
him of shipment of Huntington's portrait
of H.B. Durand. Jim, while
not well, seems to be better.
To bed at 10.30 —

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 22. 1887.

Rain until after 10 — for a time, signs
of clearing — in the afternoon more
gentle rain. After b'kf'st I spent
some time seeding & raking over
some places about the house — then,
with Herrick helping me, I put
up the remaining tiles in main
room fireplace. In the after-
noon I finished the corners on the
ceiling of the cherry reception room.
Box with Durand's portrait was
sent by Adams Exp. Through care-
lessness it was sent off without pre-
payment or insurance. I went
down town at 6 o'clk. & had some trouble
in having the matter arranged which
was finally done. I insured & prepaid.
Evett — most of the time with news-
papers. To bed soon after 10 —

The Smiths painted under the
piazza.
