## Left Page

### October, Saturday 15, 1887

Brilliant + cloudless day — Ther
went down to 25° last night and
was still below freezing when I
got up. My dark room water pipe
burst over the etching room windows
\+ flooded out + in. The day was so
fine that I spent most of the time
out of doors, climbing trees to see about
cutting out dead wood etc. etc. The
painters got to work on outside work
painting on the studio. All of the
afternoon I worked with pick,
spade + wheelbarrow transplanting
Virginia creepers + manuring + mulching
plants — Went down town at 6 —
Dwell, felt tired + stupid and did
little else than read newspapers
— had a game of Basique with Annie
before going to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 16, 1887

Very pleasant day + milder — Ther.
last night went to 29°. Wind from
the S.E. — I spent the mng again
with spade + wheelbarrow fixing the
flower beds about the house + preparing
for winter by mulching etc. — I also
did some transplanting. — Dinner
at 2. — After that, a nap. Then I
did some pruning — then wrote
short notes to Gen. Diven (who sent
a lot of Berkeny seeds last Friday)
and to Judge Shunger who is to sail
on the 29 inst. with his wife for an
extended travel. — Evell, a little
writing — Annie reading aloud from
time to time from Gen. Grant's auto bio-
graphy.
