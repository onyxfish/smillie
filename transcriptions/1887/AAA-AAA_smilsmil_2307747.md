## Left Page

### September, Sunday 11, 1887

A dull, overcast, fall like day with rain at night — I got up
soon after 7 completely tired out from having lain awake all night.
I found my head very light and my legs shaky. I did little else
all day than lie about, going out now & then for a few minutes to
get some but of door air — I got a very short nap before dinner
abt. 30 — Evef. I wrote a note to Bro. Will. To bed abt. 0.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 12, 1887

Dismal day — strong easterly wind with rain most of the m't. Cloudy
& raw all day. I got up soon after
7 & while dressing a severe pain in the back suddenly caught me — I
presume it was lumbago — for the rest of the day I could move only
with much pain & difficulty. I tried to do some work but could
not. I managed to write a letter to Charl. Kimmel & Voigt sent to
me some imps. from the Durand etched
portraits. In the evef. I Ret'd. one
with my approval & wrote to them
a letter with some instructions for
the printer — also a note to Kate.
Annie read aloud a part of Rider
Haggard's Story of "She".

The Smiths painted under piazza —
Herrick worked — none of the other
men did.
