## Left Page

### January, Thursday 20, 1887

Still milder & overcast — again late to studio — 10.30 — don't feel like work — Continued on the Montrose picture giving a light dragging of color over the foreground — but in spite of my care I soon "tightened" the whole thing, destroying the play both of color & brush. Home to lunch & back — worked as long as I could see & ended my days work much discouraged.. Stephen Shaunessey called as I was cleaning up. Home to dinner at 6 — Lucy the Adams girls went with Geo. & Valentine up to the Polo grounds to "Toboggan" — I wrote some letters & read a little. Then a game of cards. The girls came in at 10.30 — to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 21, 1887

mild, pleasant day. To studio at 10 & to work on Montrose painting going over it generally & heightening the key. Home to lunch at 1. Then to bank — met Champney returning with him & stopping awhile in his studio. Painted until near 4.30 getting picture into better shape, but as George remarks — "it looks too deliberate" — It wants spontaneity — what else can be expected, working in the sickly, spinsterly, half hearted way that I do. Home at 6, Mrs. Helen Atwater took dinner with us & left at 9 o'clk. Then we had a game of cards & to bed about 11 —

Annie & the Adams girls went through the A.B.N. Co. establishment this p.m. under Willis' guidance.
