## Left Page

### October, Sunday 9, 1887

Mild, overcast m'g. — Rain com-
menced about 2 o'clk. by eve'g it
fell quite continuously & at times heavily.
It was near noon when Annie, Jim,
a stray kitten & I started north from the
house & went across lots & through the
woods over into the hollow where Sayres'
farm is — we skirted along the edge
of the woods finding much that was
very enjoyable; climbed the hill and
came out on the road some distance
beyond Beebe's — There our stray kitten
switched off — again we climbed [few]
& got home, very warm, at 1.40 — Dinn-
at 2 — Late in the afternoon I gave up
& took a nap. Eve'g. finished letter
to Mary, but it rained so hard
that I did not take it to P. O.
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 10, 1887

Very warm & very moist, but no
rain until after 6 P.M. when it
fell heavily & continuously. — In the
m'g got started on an autumn
subject (in oil) that attracted me
yesterday m'g while we were out
walking — had to go down town &
was interrupted until after 11.
Then painted until dinner; at 1.
Again at my painting about 2.30
but was repeatedly interrupted —
Jerfuss came with gal. iron
tops for the chimnies, but I stuck
to my work as long as I could see.
Then went down town — Heavy
shower before I got back to tea
at 7 — Eve'g. looking over Sayres'
bills & counting up the cost of
labor & other operations in this place
— a game of cards & to bed soon
after 10.
