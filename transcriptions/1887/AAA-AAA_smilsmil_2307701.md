## Left Page

### June, Saturday 11, 1887

A wonderfully beautiful day — felt pretty stiff after yesterday's work. — my fingers ends all pretty badly burned by the cement. Nelson Warner came in the m'g at 9 o'clk. & painted 2nd coat on ceilings of Studio & Cherry Room — I was out & about all day attending to the men at work — Annie went down, shortly after dinner, & saw Mrs. Parke, her baby & her Nurse who is engaged to attend Annie. The box of trees & shrubs came from Mettle, too late to be delivered — also Studio stove & case — Evelf Reading "Art Reviews" no. 1 (with my etching) mit "A Woodland Pool") sent by W't Kelly —
To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 12, 1887

A wonderfully beautiful day — a rare day in June — brilliantly clear & warm & cool. Bkf— about 9 — then for some time in hammock on Piazza — then wrote a letter to mother — Had dinner about 2 — afternoon loitering about. Wrote to Yale, to Charl — Mary Ireland & Sue Cook — Tea about 7 — Took letters to P.O. at about 8 — Then loafed — To bed soon after 10.
