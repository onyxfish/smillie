## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 15, 1887

Rain & fog in the m'g., clearing in the afternoon. To studio late & did little, feeling heavy & unlike working. Tried to do something on Bank Note drawings & made some progress on a large locomotive subject. Delivered the Potosi drawing. Berman, Gorlet's agent, came in & I agreed to take my studies for another year. Home to lunch & back. More work on locomotive drawing. W. Greer, the etching club salesman came in just as I discovered four forgotten parchment [illegible] & "Voice from Cliff". As the demand for [illegible] still continues I promised to let him have them. Home & dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. Ge°. Cook came in & Charl. talked of Cattle Ranching until after 10 when they left — then Annie read aloud a couple of chapters from Rusty's Fallen Idol. To bed a little before 12 —

Jim getting along very nicely — the news from Mother not encouraging to me.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 16, 1887

Pleasant day — Up with a headache & got to studio at 10.15 — I tried to work on A.B.N. Co's drawings but made no progress. Home at 4 & went to bed & stayed there until 6 when I got up & had some dinner — Katie & Molly Adams with us — they spent the eve'g at Mother Cook's. Charl. came in at 9 — at 9.30 we went to his dark room where he developed the 8 plates that I exposed the other day, getting some very good views of street cars, of which I am to make drawings for A.B.N. Co. Home at 10.45. To bed before 12.
