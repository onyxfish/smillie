## Left Page

### July, Friday 29, 1887

Overcast & muggy morning, but bright, hot & humid as ever in the afternoon. Glorious moonlight night — Finished my Durand etching in the morning and spent the afternoon in preparation for biting & further experimenting with the Stanley photographic plates — Made up new Pyro-stock solution & while I got better results I could not get a good negative — which ended in my being very much disgusted & tired — Evef. part of the time with Annie & part of the time on piazza enjoying the delicious night. To bed at about 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 30, 1887

Hot, hot, hot. Did not get started on my biting until after 10 — then in my etching room all day, getting through about 6 P.M., with what appears to be a very successful bite. Nearly 8 in the evef. when I went down town — Most of the evef. after my return spent on piazza — Superb night a hazy, silvery moonlight full of mystery — with pleasant singing by a band of young people at a distance, having a good time. To bed before 11.
