## Left Page

### July, Friday 15, 1887

A cool day — much of the time overcast — in the afternoon a few, feeble attempts at showers. Nelie announced her decision to return home to-day so at 10 o'clk I went down town & arranged with [illegible] to take her to depot — at 11.30 she started. I spent some time, as usual now — in the new pasture, pulling up mulliens & other noxious weeds — also in directing Herrick in studio — Dinner at 1.30 — after that with N. Warner who put the first coat of paint on studio walls. Eve'g, a part of the time with Annie — then down stairs reading newspaper until 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 16, 1887

An exceedingly hot, uncomfortable day — I spent considerable of the m'g, as usual, weeding & working with the vines about the house. I tried to get started on another drawing for A. B. N. Co, but got no farther than getting material together — in the afternoon I made some paste & papered the ceiling of my photo-dark room — Down town about 9 o'clk p.m. — after that sat about the house trying to find a cool place until 10 o'clk — then to my warm bed but did not get much sleep.
