## Left Page

### March, Sunday 27, 1887

A pleasant, though hazy morning,
— a raw east wind. Breakfast
at 9. Between 10 & 11, started out
with Annie — Miss Faucon & the three
boys — after half an hour A. returned
while Miss F— & I continued on
through South Orange & by a broad
road went over the mountain, on
the other side of which, near the top
we found a pleasant water fall — then
back, getting in at 1.30 — dinner at 2
afternoon there was chatting & talking
— I took another, a shorter walk —
tea about 7 — Eve'g Mr. White
read aloud to us, very effectively,
some of Lowell's poems — it's first
wife was her mother's sister. It
was about 10.30 when we got
to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 28, 1887

Heavy rain during the night — fog
& rain in the early m'g. Bkft. at 8 —
cue set? to N.Y. by the 9.04 train — I
got to studio soon after 10. I
spent all the day getting a number
of my etching proofs into shape to
give away to the Boston Museum — to
Yale, Farrer, Nicole & others — Home
to lunch & back as usual. In
the afternoon, heavy showers of
rain — worked until after 6 — then
home to dinner — Eve'g at 8.30
went to Photo. Exhib'n at Ortgies'
gallery — met there Geo. G. Rockwood
& Geo. Cox, members with me (I am
Chairman) of the jury of Awards. We
looked over the exhib'n until 9 — then
there was a display of lantern slides.
Also went to Century & there saw
Perry, another member of the jury —
Home at 11 & right to bed.
