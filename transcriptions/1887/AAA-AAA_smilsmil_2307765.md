## Left Page

### October, Monday 17. 1887.

My. opened with fog & easterly
wind — Ther. last night 39°. Before
noon the weather cleared charmingly
giving a hazy, almost cloudless and
mild afternoon. The Smiths painted
on the outside of the studio. the Paynes
picked up stone on the orchard patch.
Herrick made horses & put up the
scaffolding in oak bed room to finish
that ceiling. I packed Hamilton
Arms drawing & at 11 went down town
& sent it by U.S. Exp. to G. P. T. Smillie —
28 John S. Mlf. city as directed by telegram
rec'd Sat. evef. Went to G. Fordham's
about sketching stool seat — Dinner at 1.
It was nearly 2.30 when Annie, Jim & I
got away in a buggy for a ride. I took
my camera & made a number of shots —
back at 4.30 — Rec'd telegram from Winter
& Smillie saying that Charle w'd sail on Men.
for home — Eve'f. until after 9 developing
the photos. of the afternoon — appear to
have secured two or three good ones & to
have made some stupid blunders.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 18. 1887.

Much of the day cloudy — on the whole
it might be called pleasant — I
spent the m'f. with Herrick put-
ting up newly painted window blinds
& laying out the ceiling of oak bed room
— also, some time with the Painters —
In the afternoon the Paynes came
with a load of leaf mould — then
two loads of manure that was drawn
into the garden plot. I spent a
part of the afternoon pasting heavy
paper on the inside of my "dark room"
to make it perfectly light tight. Went
down town with Jim before 6 — then
tea, after which I again went down
town & bought a spring trap & a box of
"rough on rats" & then spent some time
getting my trap & poison fixed — A cat,
or some other animal has been burrowing
through the flower beds threatening des-
truction to the honeysuckle vine roots —
It was about 11 when I got to
bed.
