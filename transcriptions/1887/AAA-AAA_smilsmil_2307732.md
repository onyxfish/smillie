## Left Page

### August, Friday 12, 1887

Brilliant, breezy & cool day — O'Brien
& team, with Blair for helper, worked
at hauling small stone. Piled in pasture
lot & filling in foundation of workshop.
Hot duties as well — I worked all the
mf. Printing pfs. of Durand plate
took 6 thin paper, Jap., pfs. the best
I have yet taken — Dinner at 1.15 —
Afternoon spent loafing & enjoying
the coolness & purity of the air —
wandered over the new pasture with
Jim, weeding & watching the men.
In the afternoon, late, Minnie Mulford
& Will Mulford's wife called — also, Mr.
de Schweinity & her sister in law — Tea —
Before I went down town — 9 when
I got back home. To bed soon
after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 13, 1887

Superb day — commenced my
days work by jambing the nail
of middle finger of right hand, so
that it immediately turned blue.
This was in handling a back log
for the morning fire. In spite
of the soreness I managed to
print eight thin Jap. paper pfs.
from Durand etching. I got
through before 1 o'clk. The
afternoon was spent in going
about among the men at work.
Did not go down town until
after tea — back about 9 —
Read news paper & to bed
at usual time.
