## Left Page

### October, Friday 21, 1887

A very disagreeable day — after a rainy
night a mild wth. west wind with
indications of clearing — but the wind
got around to the north — it grew rapidly
colder — spiteful, gusty storms of wind
& rain followed each other in rapid
succession & at night it snowed.
Painters tried to work outside but gave
it up — Herrick went down town and
got irons from Hewston to brace cistern — then
got heavy trunk & with chains & jack screws
the bulged corner of studio building was
pulled in — The rest of the day he worked
at putting up the irons. I made a fine
in etching some & worked there — finished
pasting lining paper on walls of "dark room".
The canvas for bed room ceiling came late
in the afternoon from Boyle. Went down town
before 6 — Then tea — Eve'g with newspaper
& a game of cards with Annie. Furious
wind & snow flurry about 10 — Then
to bed. A wild night of howling
wind & very cold but as I did not
put Ther. out — do not know how cold
it was.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 22, 1887

A cold winter day — heavily overcast
with bleak, strong wind from W. & N.W. —
Herfuss came & with Herrick got the
studio cistern again into good shape —
This time I hope it will stay. The
Smith's finished painting blinds &
commenced on the painting of the board
winter shutters — I Rec'd bill from Little
stating that my fruit trees had been
started by freight two days ago. Went
for Battles to get him to dig holes for them
as the Paynes continue to disappoint
me. I staked out places for
the various trees & bushes — went
down town before 6. As the wind
had moderated I took Jim out on
the piazza for a run before tea time.
Eve'g with news & after until near
9. 11 where I got to bed.
