## Left Page

### April, Saturday 30. 1887.

Cool + windy — too much like march. To studio about 10 + spent the m'f. on Po'k Bridge drawing — at noon went to A.A.A's. Saw Sutton + talked with him about etching "The Old Stage Coach" — He gives me permission, reserving no rights for himself — then to Bank — then home to lunch at 1.30 — back to studio at 2.45 — worked on Bridge until after 5 — Jack Downs called — then Frank Waller, who gave up key to his studio — then I went to Sargent mf't Co. w. Bdway below 12th S'. + looked at some chairs — then in to "Century" — near 7 when I got home to dinner. Ev'f. read awhile — then a game of cards with Annie — then wrote a couple of notes + to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 1. 1887.

Bright, mild + spring-like — Bkf'. at 9 — Reading papers for an hour or so, then took Jim with me to the Academy + painted a little while on the Marble Head Neck Study bought by Mr. Young of Po'k — He objected (with reason) to the lengths of the neck of one of the sheep in the foreground. In a few minutes I was able to correct it. Met there Winslow Homer — Jat. Hart + his daughter — Home at about 1 — at 2 we went to Mother Cook's where we had dinner — Home again at 4 + at 4.30 Jim, Annie + I celebrated Al's birth day by riding up to 90th S'. where we entered the Park + wandered thro' + out at 96th S. gate — sunlight, air + budding things, very beautiful — Home at a little before 7 — then supper. Ev'f. read awhile — then wrote a note to Chard about Ins., and a letter to Mother — To &c. Depot at 10. To bed about 10.30.
