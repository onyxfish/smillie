## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 9, 1887

Pleasant w'f. — soon overcast rain at night — About 10 when I got to studio — I worked very faithfully on the Meadow Pond Gifford coming in & giving me some general hints — I painted out some of the little objects & made it broader in light & color. Home at 1.30 — back about 2.40 & worked untill near 4 when I determined to call it done. About 4.30, Charl's friend, Mr White, came in & made a pleasant little call — Soon after he left Charls came in & after to we walked up town together — Even'f, at 8.30 I went to N.A. of D. business meeting — an innovation — all the new & young men out — a quorum 32 — but only 30 were present — an informal meeting organized — Huntington presiding. Frank Mullet presented Resolutions covering much ground — Referred to a com. at his request — adjourned at 10.30 — home & to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 10, 1887

Rain intermittent — clearing before 11. To a bright, breezy day — Got to studio at 10.30 — Will came in about 11 — stayed for an hour — then I went to 17th S. & saw Mr. Reft. Gorlet about studio — then to Bank for Annies — Home before 1 — Jim Re-commenced his Kindergarten — I had hair cut & got to studio at 3 — Soon saw something I wanted to do on the Meadow Pond & worked away at it for an hour or more when Gilbert Gaul came in — Home soon after 6 — Even'f. Annie finished reading aloud "Tom Brown at Rugby" about 9 went with A — to her mothers, then I went to Union League Club. & stayed there until nearly 1 — Othune (of M. Knordler & Co) offered me $225. for my Water Color, Stretsh — I asked him to let me sleep over it — Home — & to bed at 11.30.
