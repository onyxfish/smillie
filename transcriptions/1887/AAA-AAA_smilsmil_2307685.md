## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 10, 1887.

mild, milky & pleasant. To studio
at 10 & spent the mtg making a
new study from Statuette of Liberty.
Home to lunch at 1.30 — To N.A. of
D. at 2.30 expecting to aid in the
auditing of Alfred Jones' accounts.
but, unaccountably, he was not
ready — then to studio & met
S. R. Koehler on his way out — He
turned back & visited for more
than an hour. I worked on sketch
of Liberty until 5 — [illegible] Parker came
in on his way to train — his account
of Mother is not encouraging. Chap.
got a trained nurse who was to
go up to-day — At 5 went over to
Holbrooke's & paid him $20 — for
wrought iron fire irons complete
for studio in Montrose — Then to Am.
Art Ass'n for a few minutes — Home
a little after 6 — dinner — wrote
anote to Ticknor — Took letter to
depot & posted it — To bed soon
after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 11, 1887.

A very fine day — Ther. getting up to 87°.
About 9, Annie, Jim & I went to Battery
by Elevated R.R. had to wait half an hour
for 10 o'clk boat for Bedloe's Island — Statue
of Liberty — a very pleasant sail — 20 minutes
— then climbed to top of pedestal — from
which I took sketch looking N. to city
up the rivers — Returned on 1.30 boat
& got home at 2.30 — lunch at 1 — Adams
gives there — To N.A. of D. at 2. Annual
meeting. A very full meeting — 52
present. With [illegible] Rogers I was app-
tiller which proved to be a busy office
a harmonious meeting without
any excitement — Dinner served
soon after 6 — by Pinard — a very good
one — my seat between J. G. Brown and
Mrs. Hicks — before it was over moved
myself — after dinner an attempt
at speeches — Chas. Miller aired him-
self much to his satisfaction, and
[illegible] Beard drivelled amusingly.
— broke up soon after 10 — home &
to bed at 11.
