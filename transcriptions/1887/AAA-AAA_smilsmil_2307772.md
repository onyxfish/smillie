## Left Page

### October, Monday 31. 1887.

Ther. last night 12°.

Brilliant mf. Everything frozen hard, with beautiful effects of frost work on windows & elsewhere. Kate (the cook) got away for N.Y. at 6.30 A.M. preceding us two or three days. Mr Payne worked until 5 p.m. — mulching trees & plants & filling in the hole at S.W. cor. of house where Zerfass finished putting in the new trap & drain pipe. F.B. Smith came to work at 10 o'clk. Albert S— came at 1 & they painted on piazza posts & railings. Herrick jabbed about, & I had my hands free in directing them all. Nelson Warner came in the mf. & under my direction painted the first coat on the oak bed room ceiling. I went down town with Jim at 1.30. Evtf. being very tired, I rested.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 1. 1887.

Ther. last night 30°.

Another brilliant day with brisk, cold N.W. wind — up at 7. The Smiths got to work painting about the piazza. Ambrose mulching plants & setting rugs until after 10, when I went with him over to the old Warner farm & before 12 we got out a big maple & had it in place at the turn of the road from the lane. After dinner I went with him again and got a smaller one — about 4 o'clk. Battles came along & he helped us get a third tree & set it. When we got through with this I was so very tired that I was glad to stop — and rest. Evtf. I developed 5 photos some of them taken this mf. — nothing very interesting — again I rested.
