## Left Page

### September, Friday 9, 1887

The day opened pleasantly but the wind soon changed to S.E. & sky became overcast. O'Briece came with his team & worked all day with the boys currying & wood. Am Payne worked with pick & shovel — Battles came with Thomas's team at 11 & commenced ploughing the new road laid out yesterday, to the proposed barn. In the afternoon he came with dirt scraper & his boy. Hotchkiss finished the garden wall — then the foundation for barn was commenced. Herrick came to work — The Smiths painted — Zerfuss fixed some headers. I was very much used up by cold (apparently) settling in stomach & was hardly able to get about — Coyle worked — At 5 P.M. Annies went to a "lunche party" at Mr. W. D. Susk's — She ret'd before 8 — Eve'g with news — walked & to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 10, 1887

In bed all day — Dis comfort in the [illegible] that increased with the day — winding up in a wretched sort of headache with supplemental aches all over. Had a small [illegible] in bed & some amusement about 10 o'clk at night — did not get up at all — awake all night —

Battles came with Thomas's team at 9.30 — his boy with him — they worked on the new road. O'Briece came with his team in the afternoon & ploughed up a portion of the to-be orchard and strawberry bed. Ambrose Payne worked all day — & so did Willie Curry. Hotchkiss worked on foundation wall of barn — Coyle helped Battles — Herrick worked around & the Smiths painted.
