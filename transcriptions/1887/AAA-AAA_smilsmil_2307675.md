## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 20, 1887

Charming day — To studio about 10 & to work on ideas — ink. "Blowing Dandelion Seeds" — Hale came in & we talked over the possibilities of his being in Montrose to help A— through her trial in July. Then Gifford came in — poor fellow — very much crushed by the loss of his two boys in seven weeks — Before he left, Will came in about a Po'k bridge drawing. Home to lunch at 1.30 — took Jim to Kindergarten — then to studio — then to White, Stokes & Allen with my drawing — to my great surprise Allen told me that I was too late — merely a fib — he has probably got a cheaper plate there to N.A. then to studio & worked again on "Blowers" — Before 6 walked up 5th Ave to 50th S— then home to dinner. Evg. wrote to Herrick — Payne & Hasson & Gilbert — all of Montrose. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 21, 1887

Mild & pleasant, altho' much of the day was overcast. Got to studio about 10 — worked for some time over plans of studio & cherry room ceilings at Montrose — arranging for putting up the cameras. After 11 — young man came from A.B. N. Co. — I made sketch of Po'k Bridge which he took away with him — At 12.45 I went to Bank — then to Newman's & to Jackson's — stopped to see some paintings & got home to lunch at 1.45 — took Jim to Kindergarten & then I got to studio about 3. Worked on Rough Charcoal Sketch 18×24 — "Studying from Nature" until 4.15 — then to Nicoll's studio — met A.H. Baldwine there & we audited the Etching Club accts. Home at 6.15 — Mr. & Mrs. Chas. Sprague & Edw'd & Mary Cooks dined with us soon after 7. At 9 I went up & saw Charl about a possible chance to sell the H.S. house. Home at 10 — our company left at 10.30 — To bed soon after 11.
