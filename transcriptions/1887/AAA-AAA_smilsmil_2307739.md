## Left Page

### August, Friday 26, 1887

A cold, raw day — much of the time cloudy & windy — at night cloudless
the Ther. went down last night to 46°.
O'Brien & Thomas at work with their teams — also Payne, Battles, Judson and
his friends. Hotchkiss at the wall —
Herrick dividing his time between
shingling roof on new building and
putting small ornaments on studio
mantle piece. Coyle cutting grass
& the two Smieths painting — The
problem of colors on the house grows
more difficult. I wasted much
time & patience on it to-day — I
was also with the men at work in
the field — I went down town about
noon & again at 6 with Dr. Muelford
who called — Eve'g, newspaper
a note to Higgins' son enquiring
for some contrivance to improve the
draught of our main chimney and
to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 27, 1887

Very bright w'f but before 11 it
clouded over & at times threatened
showers. The men all at work —
Thomas & O'Brien with their teams
Battles, Payne, Judson, Keator and
Hotchkiss — the two Smieths painted
Herrick worked on the roof of his
studio & Coyle cut grass. I
took my camera into the field
& took a number of shots at the men
& horses in action — there to dark
room & developed. Some of them getting
good results. Dinner at 1, soon
after 2, carriage came & Kate, Annie,
Jim & I went out riding — out by
Jones' pond — over the hill, by the Poor
house on to the Weshoppen valley road
& home by Brewsters at 4.30 — after that
I paid off all hands & discharged
some — Then tea — Eve'g, read
awhile — To bed at 10.
