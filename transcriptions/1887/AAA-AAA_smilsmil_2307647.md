## Left Page

### February, Monday 21, 1887

A nasty day — my hope to get up disappointed — Annie sent for Hale who came before noon. Seemed to think my trouble only an ordinary stomach but it seems to me to be singularly persistent. I kept very quiet in bed but did not escape many pains, discomforts & some unwilling exercises — Eve†. Annie read aloud, finishing Anstey's "Fallen Idol" —

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 22, 1887

Rather an unpleasant day, starting with about an inch of snow that fell late yesterday — my below stairs energies were quiescent during the day after giving me the usual trouble during the night — I kept on my bed all day — In the m†h wrote a letter to Herrick — In the afternoon I was left with Jim — by night time I felt thoroughly used up. Charle came in in the afternoon & told me that Ed. Parker had been obliged to make an assignment of his mill business — which was no surprise to me. I am very sorry for P—. Eve†. Annie after reading aloud a little while went to bed. I lay in my bed and read "Tom Brown" until 11 o'clk.

Geo. Cook & wife visited us in the afternoon.
