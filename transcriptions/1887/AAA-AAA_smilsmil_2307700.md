## Left Page

### June, Thursday 9, 1887

Delightful day — warm and cloudy in the m'f with slight showers — not enough to wet —
Eve'g, much cooler. Many
Ireland went home, leaving
the house at 11.15 in the m'f.
I was very busy looking after
the men at work putting earth
on the road banks, in cleaning
the old bed room tiles and in
spading up & working over the
flowerbed at the West end of
the house. Went down town
after tea — got Copper plates
from Sharp — Eve'g. Reading
paper — wrote check for Sharp
& letter to H. P. Bellows —
To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 10, 1887

A very pleasant day — the m'f
spent in looking after the men
at work making the bank at the
turn of the road & getting flower
beds in front of the house. Annie
sitting on a chair while Jim & I
put in the seed — In the afternoon
I laid the tile hearths in our
bed room & had a tremendously
hard job of it — my back was nearly
broken, my legs infected [illegible] &
my stomach was entirely used up —
But I got the hearths down, after a
fashion, before 7 o'clk. Kate, the
cook, went to the P.O. for me — got
a letter from Mary describing Mother's
condition as extremely low — Spent
the eve'g in big chair & to bed
at 10 o'clk.
A heavy shower in the afternoon.
