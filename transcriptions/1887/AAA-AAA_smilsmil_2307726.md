## Left Page

### July, Sunday 31, 1887

A hot & breezy day, overcast in the m'g, with quite a shower between 10 & 11 o'clk. Before dinner I wrote a letter to Charl — Dinner som after 2 — in the afternoon wrote to Uncle Willie, sending him a check for $115— first money payment on Greenwood lot — and a letter to Anthony enquiring if I could exchange my Stanly for Cramer plates. Evening loafed about until after 10 enjoying the piazzas & the night — then to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 1, 1887

A hot day — Got my printing arrangements in order & took proofs of my Durand etching — just from the acid — eight of them — to me the plate is in a promising condition. Herrick came back to work — in the afternoon he helped me in putting up the vines for the vines to run upon on the south front of the house. Thunderstorms passed about us — a part of one went over us about 5 p.m. I went down town before 8 — on return sat with Annie until 9.30 — when I retired to give Mr. Hagmer a chance as she has a severe attack of diarreah — I sat on piazza until after 10 — then to bed with a lame shoulder (right) — rheumatic, I suppose & a headache
