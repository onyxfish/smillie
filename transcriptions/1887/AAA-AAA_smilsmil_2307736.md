## Left Page

### August, Saturday 20, 1887

Charming day, altho' quite cool
in the wind — Hotchkiss at work —
& Thomas & team — Blair & Judson —
Battles 9 hours — Couple free day. The two
Smith's painted all day upon the house
making more plain to me that I have
a very serious problem in the way of [illegible]
harmious & agreeable colors. My
day was spent with the men,
most of it with the painters — In
the afternoon Annie went off riding
with the Parkes & Mrs. Scranton — back
after 6. Early eve'g, before Annie
had finished her tea, Mr. & Mrs. Woodin
called — about 8.30 I escorted them
with my lantern & then went down
town for mail & messages — home soon
after 9, & to bed soon after 9 —

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 21, 1887

Pleasant day — Took my breakfast
in bed, having been disturbed by
bowel troubles during the night —
After I got up I spent a couple
of hours in a hammock before dinner
& most of the afternoon in the same
place — Eve'g Annie read aloud
to us some of the delightful "Old
Salem Sketches" by Eleanor Putnam"
& Mrs. Arlo Bates — To bed soon after
10 —
