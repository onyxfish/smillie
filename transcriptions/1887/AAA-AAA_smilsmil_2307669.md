## Left Page

### April, Friday 8, 1887

Delightful day — Sue, Annie &
I took 9.30 car & went into town to
Holy Trinity to hear Phillips Brooks. We
were 20 m. early & got good gallery seats
the service was a great disappointment
to me — there was with difficulty that I could
understand him — he read his discourse
which to me, was far from thrilling. Out
at 12.45 — Sue & Annie Ret. to C — I
went to Boston Art Club. Press & Profession
View — Water Colors etc — There was one
other man there — a rough looking
young fellow — At 2 went to Providence
Depot & had dinner — at 3, to St. Botolphs
— wrote a note to Geo at 4, across Public
Gardens — Pretty pictures, boys sailing
their boats. Went down Beacon St. looking
for Ed. Miles' studio — then back across the
Common to Hamilton R. to find that he had
moved to Boylston St. a couple of doors from
St. Botolphs — the point from which I started.
At 5 o'clk — car to Cambridge —     Met f.
Annie & I visited W. & Mr. Toppan where
we met Mr. T's sister Miss Cushing — Home
before 10 — to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 9, 1887

Delightful day. At 9.30 Robert Toppan
came for me & we spent the morning
together wandering about the college
grounds & going into such buildings as
were open to us — At 12.30 we got back
to his new house, now building, & spent
half an hour going over it — then I Ret.
to Sue's — Dinner at 2 — at 3 Annie
& I went into town to Victor's Studio —
his reception afternoon — met his wife
& Mr. Sewell, a Boston painter — passed
a very pleasant hour — then out & wander-
ed up Tremont St., went into Doll & Richards
& saw paintings by W. & Mrs. J. Appleton Brown.
Then on to Bowdoin Sq. & after some waiting
got into an Auburn car — stuffy & horribly
crowded — had to stand all the way out.
To Sue's at 6.30 — Quietly. Reading
until 10 — then to bed.
