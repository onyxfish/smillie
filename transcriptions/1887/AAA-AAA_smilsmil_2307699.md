## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 7, 1887

Thick, foggy & warmer. Smith with his team came & worked with Coyle under piazza at N.N. cor. of house digging down the bank to give more room for rebuilding the cistern, the bottom of which was burst out by ice last winter. The earth was carried down to the lane & spread upon the broken stones there. I got started upon fixing tiles & spent some time in cleaning the old tiles taken up from bed room hearth. Down town twice — Evety. fixed one of the broken lamps which took some time — then chatting. Annie read aloud for a little while — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 8, 1887

Thick mist but warm — cleared about 2 o'clk very charmingly. The furniture, all but lounge, am°. from Flint's — for cheney room. Smith & team, Jackson & Coyle at work on lane spreading ashes over the broken stones — Hewitt's team also hauled 14 loads. Afternoon, men worked on a bank of earth on Fink's lot and got some good earth to spread over slope from carriage road. I got to work with cement in the afternoon & layed first bed of grouting for Cheney room fireplace — also cleaned a lot more of old oak room tiles. Zerfass fixed drain on 2nd story piaz. Evety. very warm — out on piazza until 9 o'clk. Then wrote a note to Gurney's offs. Rec'd a letter from Geo with very depressing news from Mother. To bed soon after 10.
