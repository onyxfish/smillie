## Left Page

### October, Friday 7, 1887

Delightful day — clear & exceedingly mild — The [illegible] left us at 9 Am
to return to Elmira — I went down
town about 10 — got tintypes of Hamilton
Arms & sent it to Fred — back home &
again down town with Camera & phot[ographed]
a bay Stallion — Home at 12.30 — After-
noon, after making up some new
solutions I spent some time in the
dark Room developing negatives —
Then out & about looking after the
painters who have worked all day.
Down town at 6 — Pwelf. a short
time in dark Room — then some
writing & to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 8, 1887

Very warm & cloudless. Spent a
part of the m'g in Studio working
on the gelatine tracing of Huntington's
Goldsmith's Daughter — Went down
town about 11.50 — Three back & Took
a number of photos. of Annie, Jim &
the baby on the piazza — Dinner
at 1 & at about 2.20 Annie, Jim
& I went off for a ride — At times
it was almost too warm but the
dreamy haze & warm Oct. color
was delicious — back at 5 —
tea at 6.30 — Pwelf. for an hour
more in the dark Room, develop-
ing my negatives of the m'g. Not
enough light on them to clear the
shadows —
