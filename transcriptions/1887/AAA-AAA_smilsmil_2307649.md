## Left Page

### February, Friday 25, 1887

Clear day. Rather windy & cold.
Spent the ev'g in bed, most of the
time sleeping. D[illegible] came but
could reach no satisfactory diagno
sis of my attack yesterday. Annie
went to studio & got [illegible] of etchings
& letters. Learned from [illegible] that Shel
don is full blown with measles.
I got up at 1.30 — washed & dressed
& had lunch. Spent the afternoon
in his chair in nursery — reading
& writing — wrote a note to Kate
Ev[illegible]. Read "Tom Brown at Rugby"
& got to bed at 11 but not to
sleep until long, long after.

Collins called in the ev'g. A —
saw him, I did not. He sails
on a pleasure trip to the West
Indies to-morrow. Charl came
in & spent some time with me.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 26, 1887

Heavy snow storm all the m't.
about the middle of the afternoon
it turned to heavy rain, making
a dismal day. I had my [illegible]
in bed — then up — washed & dressed
wrote notes to Koehler — also to Galt
& Greer. Then a long call from
Mr. Ernest Flagg with plans for
the enlargement & alteration of
a building in 42d St — giving me
a chance to buy a studio in it.
Dinner at 1.30 — P.m. spent
podging about — went down to
my tea at 6.30 — Ev'g Annie
read aloud from "Tom Brown"
until 9.30. To bed soon after
10 & again lay awake for three
or more hours before going to sleep.
