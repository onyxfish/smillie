## Left Page

### February, Sunday 13, 1887

Cold, bright day — after which
we had B&B. Annie & Jim
seem to be tolerably comfortable
after a broken night — wrote letters
home & read Salpen. Yale came
after 12 — considers Jim's condition
satisfactory — I walked up to 4[?]
5'. with lf — then on to 72° thro'
the Park & home at 2 — Feeling
out of sorts. I ate no dinner —
afternoon, wrote a number of
letters & notes — After 5 gave Annie
a chance to rest — tea at 7.30 —
Eveff. some reading — wrote a
note to Swain Gifford whose eldest
boy died last Thurs.

## Right Page

### February, Monday 14, 1887

Pleasant day — milder — at times
overcast. Late again on getting
to studio — worked all day on
A.B.N.Co. Potosi drawing and
did not get it finished until
5 o'clk. Home to lunch as
usual & dinner at 6.30 —
Eveff. Clinton Ogilvie &
wife called — Annie excused
herself & I did the honors.
They invited us to dine with
them next Tues. After
they left Annie read aloud
from Anstay's "Fallen Idol" —
To bed about 11.
