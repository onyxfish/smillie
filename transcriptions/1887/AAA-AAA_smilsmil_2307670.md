## Left Page

### April, Sunday 10, 1887

A very warm & enervating day —
Ther. got up to 80° not a cloud
or a leaf to break the force of the
sunbeams on the hot, dusty road.
Matthew & I walked to the Parish
Church — A. & Sue rode down — I
sat with them & heard Mr. Hall
preach a good Easter Sermon —
I was in good mood & much interested
& at times moved by the services —
after it the children had a service
of song — Jim & the other little ones joined
us. Home soon after — dinner at 2.
Afternoon, wrote letters to Mother,
Mary, Geo. & D. V. Gardner in answer to
Mr. & Mrs. Wm. Downs drove out in the
afternoon to see us — Mr. Warner, a
lawyer, friend of Sue's called later
& in the eve'g her uncle, Mr. [illegible] & his
daughter & her husband Mr. & Mrs.
[illegible] spent an hour with us —
To bed about 10, 30.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 11, 1887

A pleasant day, opening with a
shower in the morning — Rec'd my
weekly letter from Herrick — much ex-
ercised lest I should hold a grudge
against him because I felt obliged
to raise his wages. I wrote in reply.
Took it & other letters in to Boston with
me on 11 o'clk car. Got to Museum a
few minutes before it opened at 12 — then
with K— & the Rembrandt Prints until
2 — then we went to Prov. depot & had
dinner — after 3 when we parted — then
I went to Boston & Albany Depot, bot. tickets
& secured seats for Elf, Wr'd mvf. There
at 4 o'clk. to Ed. Niles' studio — spent half
an hour with him — then for some time
trying to find some prints suitable
for Clara, Sue's nurse, to copy — Home
at 6 o'clk. Eve'g, Annie & I went
to Mr. Jue Read's & made a call —
Reading until after 10 — then to bed.
