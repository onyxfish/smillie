## Left Page

### May, Sunday 8, 1887

*Po'Keepsie —*

Dull & damp m'f. Hd[?] at 8 —
& ab[ou]t took train for Po'k. Geo.
joined me on the train — got
to P— ab[ou]t 1.45 & walked to the house
— a surprise to all — Found Mother
evidently feebler & thinner than
when I saw her last; For a
short time she was much affected
by our coming — In the afternoon
about 3 o'clk I went out for a walk.
close & muggy — went to Cemetery
& to the high Rock south of it from
whence there is a fine view up & down
the River. Home again about 4.30 —
Uncle W. came in — Talked with Mother
& Kate about having a trained nurse
They assented — At 5.45 Geo & I said
our good byes — Took 6.15 Train —
got to city at 8.15 Annie had waited
tea for me — Later Charl came in —
I wrote a note to Will — also
letter to Herrick — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 9, 1887

Warm, pleasant day, altho' wind
from East. To studio about 10 — I
tried to work on "Statue of [illegible]"
for B. N. Co — but felt so out of sort
that I could do nothing — At noon
I went to Bridgman's in 19th S[t]. and
got a lot of garden vegetable seeds
to send up to Montrose — then to
Century & took a look at pictures
there. Then home to lunch at 1.30
Emma & J— there — back to studio
at 2.45 — To work on Statue & did
very well until 5 o'clk — then to
Holbrook's — 424, 6th Ave. & ordered
a set of wrought iron fire irons for
$20 — then to Hunter's Hd[?]optician —
Home soon after 6 — dinner —
Eve'g. wrote a letter of instructions
for Curry about the seeds — also
another for Herrick — Posted
them & to bed about 10.
