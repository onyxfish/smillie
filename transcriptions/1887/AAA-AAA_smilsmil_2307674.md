## Left Page

### April, Monday 18. 1887.

The beastliest storm of the season — Snow & sleet all day — falling to the depth of 2 in. or more. To studio at 10. Found tickets from Mad. Schiller for two Piano Recitals. Wrote a note of thanks — also several other notes — To work on tracings from photos, to compose a sketch in blk & white for etching of two youngsters (Jim & Teddy White) blowing Dandelion seeds — To bank and — then home to lunch — back at 2.30 — again at work on sketch of the w'f. Home at 6.30. Dinner Eve'g. Geo visited with Mary, who did not return to Po'k as at first intended — I wrote letter to Frank Herrick — mailed it — went to Walter Cook's with check — not in — then home & shovelled the walk — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 19. 1887.

Brilliant & pleasant day — Mary & the children got away for Po'k on the 10.30 A.m. Train. I got to studio about 10 & to work on India ink sketch 7×9" children blowing Dandelion seeds" — all the w'f at it — Annie & Jim came in about 12.30. We made a short call on Miss Abbatt — Then J— I walked up home to lunch — Annie rode, back at 2.30 & worked until nearly 6 on drawing — then walked down to 14th S't — & up 5th Ave. Home at 6.45 — Dinner — Eve'g. Reading — wrote notes to Preston Stevenson & to Charl — also to Sir. Wm Drake London, England — sending in my resignation as an Original Fellow of the Society of Painter-Etchers. To bed before 11.
