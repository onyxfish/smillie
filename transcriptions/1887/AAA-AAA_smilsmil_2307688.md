## Left Page

### May, Monday 16, 1887.

Now we are beginning to complain of the fine weather — it is too dry. To studio at 10 — attention much divided between Liberty drawing & carpenter putting up closets for me in Waller's old Studio. Home at 1.30 — back at 2.45 + worked on Liberty. Will sent up Brooklyn Bridge drawing for slight change & a die pl. to be touched. At 5 I went to Mr Huntington's & made final arrangements with him to stick for Century Club. His portrait of A. B. Durand. Painted 30 years ago. Home at 6.30. dinner. Ev'ef, wrote a long letter to Herrick — also, a note to Mr. Jul. D. Spicer of Troy, about frame on "My Meadow Pond" — Posted them at 10 — It was just about 11 when I got to bed.
Ed Parker was in for a few moments at lunch time.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 17, 1887.

Still fair, warm & dry — 10 o'clk. when I got to studio — Again on Liberty & with carpenter. Home to lunch as usual. — After that wrote some notes — Ed Parker came in & took away a jar of calves foot jelly just made for mother. He came down for that alone. At 3 I went to Valkening 2.30 E. 44th S— & looked at some marble slabs — want one for Studio in Montrose — at 4. to D'Alleni's in 47th S— Putting my new piece for lower jaw with back teeth. Then again to Valkening's & selected a slab of Knoxville grey. Then to Studio at about 6 — Rested for awhile then home — dinner — ev'f, a little reading & writing & to bed.
