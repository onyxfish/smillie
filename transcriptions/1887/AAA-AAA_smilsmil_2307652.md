## Left Page

### March, Thursday 3, 1887

overcast & raw — 10.30 when I
got to studio — Spent a little
time fixing up some etching pfs.
Mr. Greer came in — Abt 1.30 went
to W. 9 W. 17th St & after waiting for
some time saw Mr. Robt. Garrett
about my project for remodelling
S.E. cor. of Lex. Ave. & 27th St. into a
small studio building — He seemed
to be well pleased with it & promised
to give it attention at once — It was
just about 4 when I left — Stopped
at Knordle's — home to lunch at 1.30
to studio at 2.45 — worked on Car
drawing until 5 — Then mounted
some etching pfs on my way home
left pf. of Johnson etching at Dr. W. Def
Days — 56 E. 34th St — Dinner at 6.30.
Eve'g Annie read aloud from
"Century" — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 4, 1887

Raw & overcast, clearing in the
afternoon — brilliant moonlight
tonight. After 10 when I got
to studio — went to N.A. for a few
minutes, taking my last pfs to Mr.
Greer — Beman called — so did
Mr. Ernest Flagg — & later Mr. Greer.
I got some work done on Sleeping
car drawing — Home to lunch at
1.45, having been detained by
Geo. B. Wood who came in with
a bag full of his photos — Back
to studio at 3 & by 5 had finished
Car drawing — Charlie came in —
brought up with a cold & bringing
very discouraging news of Mother's
condition — St. Clair Smith went up
to Po'k. on Wed — says mother's trouble
is chronic dysentery — that she may
recover — but more likely, will not.
Dinner at 6.30 — About 8 Annie &
I went around to Mother Cook's —
[illegible] & his wife also there. — Home
at 10.15 & to bed about 11.
