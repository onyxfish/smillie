## Left Page

### October, Thursday 13, 1887

Ther. went down to 30° last night — A very fickle day — Brilliant sunrise — at 10 heavily overcast & threatening — at noon, all smiling again — at 6 p.m. a light shower. I got up soon after 6 & went through the house attending to fires — then back to bed for half an hour. After hlf p. took measurements in oak bed room & commenced making drawings for the finishing of ceiling & other details. At noon went down town with Annie & Jim — to Bank — to Payne's — left h. at Wm. de Schweinitz's — I went to St. Richardson's — then home to dinner. Feeling headachy I worked out of doors in the afternoon at transplanting & such work. D.R. came & vaccinated the baby — [illegible] drove up for a moment with Gen. Beaver — the Gov. of Pennsylvania — The Smiths painted on the outside of the house most of the day. I went down town at 6 — tea before 7 — evef. I loafed.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 14, 1887

Ther. went to 31° last night. Brilliant mt. but soon overcast with strong, cold wind from N.W. Later in the day it cleared again — brilliant star-light quiet night. I spent much of the day working on ceiling plans for the oak bed room & other details — with Jim went down town at 11.30. The Smiths painted outside in the mt., but gave up on acct. of the wind & cold & painted blinds in the shop in the afternoon. I worked in studio until 5 — went down town at 6 — Evefg. looking over letters & papers that have accumulated during the summer and weeding & burning — To bed at about 10.30. (At least, that was what I thought when I went up stairs to bed — but found fire out in [illegible]'s room — it had to be lighted — Ralph Ireland Smillie was troublesome & it was 11.30 when I got to bed.)
