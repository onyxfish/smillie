## Left Page

### November, Monday 14, 1887.

Pleasant & mild, growing overcast towards night. Light rain before 10.
B[illegible] — early & before 8 went to Y.C. Dep't with Sue & got her started for Boston.
Then home & worked on A.B. N. Co's drawing "Botafoco" until 12.30 — Then out with Jim & attended to some messages — nearly 1.30 when I got back to lunch. Afternoon worked on & finished Botafoco —
Before 5 walked down to Mr. Sam Huntington's, part of the way with Louis Tiffany, recently Ret'd. from Europe with his new wife, who 7 weeks ago presented him with a pair of daughters.
Found W. H. at home. He urged me to press on with the etching after his painting of The Goldsmith's Daughter & vis'd my bill against Century Club for the Durand portrait etching — then home to Dinner. Eve'g took Annie to 39th St. & I Ret'd & finished getting books into bookcase — went for A — at 9.30 — home & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 15, 1887.

Rain last night — overcast day —
worked at home until 12 on A.B. N. Co. drawing "Petropolis — Palais Imperial."
Then walked over to Cherry's — 6th Ave. & 35th St. & left my umbrella to be covered — then to Lewis & Conger's & got a wooden bread platter — Home to lunch at 1 —
Afternoon, Annie took Jim to Kindergarten at Mrs. Jones's — W. 37th St. While she was away Mr. Phillips Lockwood called & I saw her. I worked on drawing until after 4 o'clk. Then as I could see no longer, at 5 o'clk. I walked to studio — then to Einer & Arend's — cor. 58th Ave. & 16th St. — then home. Dinner at 6.30 — at 8, Annie & I went to the lecture room of All Souls — 4th Ave. near 20th St. and heard the first of Rev. W. Carthroke's Bible lectures after which we walked up home — part of the way with Ed'w Cook & Mary & her Mother — To bed soon after 10.

D. Yale called & spent some time with me in the m'f.
