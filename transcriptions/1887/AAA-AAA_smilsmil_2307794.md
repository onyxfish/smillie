## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 14, 1887

Very mild day — To studio about 10 — worked on tracing of enlarged
photo of Mr. G.S. Rives — At noon
went to Washington Wilson's with a
"China pf" copy of Poeto & Urchins, which
he paid me $10 for — Home at 1
taking silk dress pattern from Pypl
to Annie — my Xmas present to her —
Kate, the cook, so sick with a bad
sore throat thatfare was sent for —
he quarantines her — says it may be
serious — Back to studio at 2.30 —
finished tracing & retracing Mr. Rives.
Then for an hour cleaning old studies
& trying to get closets in order — at 6.
took "Goldsmith's Daughter" to Mr.
Huntington at 49 E. 20th St — Then home
to dinner — Evef with A — to 39th
St. Nelie there — home at 10 — at 10.30
Edith Wright arr'd having come from a
concert — Stayed all night — To bed
soon after 11.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 15, 1887

Very wet & very dark — on my way
to studio stopped at barber's and
had my hair cut & my head
thoroughly shampooed — Then to
H.W. Rangers — 23 E. 25th St — & There
spent an hour or more looking
over his recent work — especially
to see one of which Mr. B. Lauckner
has written to me, wishing me to
make an etching after it. I saw
it & liked it very much — To studio
at 12 — worked awhile outlining on
portrait of Mr. Rives — Then home to
lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked
until near 4 on same thing —
then until near 6 assorting my
studies & getting them packed
away in closet — Then home —
Edith W. started for Phila at 6.20
Dinner — Evef. wrote to Lauckner
Peter Mc Cornick & sent check to
Mr. R. Raynesford. To bed soon
after 10.
