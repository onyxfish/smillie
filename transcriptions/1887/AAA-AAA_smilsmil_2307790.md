## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 6, 1887

A beautiful, clear day — Got to studio about 9.30. Mrs. Callavan there — she finished her floor scrubbing at 11 + I soon had the room sketched into shape — but yet a dismal mass of confusion behind the scenes — in the closets — the drawers + such places. Home at 1 — after lunch. Mr. Jos. Ireland of Bridgeport, came in. It was 3.30 when I got to studio — then Mr. Knudsen came + he did not go until dark. Then I went to Newman's + gave orders for step ladder, door mat + floor brush — then to Grady's + gave orders for picture mouldings for studio. Then home — Evely wrote a letter to Herrick —

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 7, 1887

Very bright, mild wtf — overcast afternoon. To studio after 9 — the wtf passed without my accomplishing anything of importance owing to the number of callers who wished to see how I was getting along with the settlement of my new studios. Nearly 1.30 when I got home to lunch — Miss Ireland of Orange, there — lunched with us. About 2.30 Annie + I went to Mr. Schaus's 30 E. 38th St. — + again saw his Rembrandt "Le Doreur" + other paintings — also a piece of statuary. "Acis". Met Mr. Daul, Huntington + others there — then to studio + used the remaining daylight in putting away some of the many things for which no place had yet been found — Home to dinner — 6.30 — Evely some reading — To bed early — about 10.
