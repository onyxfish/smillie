## Left Page

### November, Thursday 10, 1887

Dull day until about noon, then rain began to fall & continued through the afternoon — at times, quite heavily — Starlet evef. I had a number of duties that kept me until near 10 — then to butcher's & grocer's & on to the studio where I met Reman the agent — It was about 11.30 when I got back to the house & began to get ready to work on A.B.N. Co's drawings — lunch abt — worked for awhile on a drawing "Casa Grande. Arizona" — about dark. Sue. Cook arr'd from Cambridge — she is to stay with us until after the funeral — Family Trimble called about the same time — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef. Ed'd & Mary Cook called — I worked at getting my books into book case — about 10.30 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 11, 1887

*[top margin note: Mother Cook buried —]*

A cold & very bleak day — did not get up until after 10 o'clk. owing to a headache — then went out to the butcher's & grocer's with Jim — back just in time to escape a snow squall — lunch abt — Before 2 Sue. Annie. Walter Wright & I went to the funeral of Mother Cook — Rob't. Colleger conducted the services — It was after 3 before we left the house for Greenwood — & we got to the cemetery gate at 4.35 — the service at the grave was very brief and we were nearly perished — I rode over with Jas. Wright & we ret'd by Hamilton Ave. ferry & elevated R.R. I got home a little before 6 — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef. the Cook family assembled to hear the reading of the Will — an equal division among the children — no bequests — the three sons & two sons-in-law (Dr Adams & Jas. Wright) appointed executors — the remaining son-in-law, of no account, & else not to be trusted. At 9.30 I went to Charls's — there met Mary & Geo. Will had gone home. Home at 10.30 — & to bed at 11.
