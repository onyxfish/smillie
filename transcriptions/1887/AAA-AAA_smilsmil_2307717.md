## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 13, 1887

An intensely hot day — a strong
breeze blowing, but little refreshment
in it. In the evef. the wind shifted
to the north which brought decided
relief. I did but little during
the day but wrote three or four
notes & direct Herrick in the
coloring of the oak wood work in
the studio. In the evef. I worked
as long as I could see at the vines
about the house. No lights were
lighted & we sat about enjoying
the coolness of the evef. after the
heat of the day — I got to bed
soon after 10.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 14, 1887

A very delightful day — pleasantly
cool — most of the m't was spent in
weeding in the new pasture and in
fixing up press in etching room — I
now begin to feel that I had better
settle myself for my summers work
after dinner, again in etching room
putting things to rights & making up
photographic solutions — later, I
worked about the vines that run over
the piazza trying to get them into
shape — about 8 oclk. Annie had a
slight nervous chill, the result, no
doubt, of over excitement & tiring.
Her bed was changed, she being
moved into another one, & the room
was very thoroughly cleaned and ven-
tilated — I went downtown after 8
— sat & read newspaper on my
return until 10 — To bed at 10.30.
