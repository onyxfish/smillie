## Left Page

### June, Friday 17, 1887

Warm & overcast with now & then
a little sprinkle of rain — also,
a few streaks of sunlight — about
7 p.m. there was a pleasant shower.
Herrick at work on the Cherry room
ceiling — & with Zerfass. For a time,
when he put up marble slab & basin
in studio — I was with them — I
set out some vine roots & was
with the other men at work about
the place — went down town
at 6.30 — Eveth. on Piazza
until near 9 — then reading
paper. Wrote a letter to
Emily Wells of Binghampton
& then to bed at about 10.30.

Shower before 7 p.m.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 18, 1887

Very warm & pleasant — at
times overcast with appearance
of coming rain. I was about
all day with the men — Herrick
at work on ceiling of Cherry room
Zerfass tinkering studio cistern
— Jackson digging a bit back
of cow shed & Smith & team
& Coyle on lawn & new ground
at entrance — had the bank
south of the drive way seeded
in the [illegible]. Emily (Mrs. Geo
J Cook.) arrived at 4.30 in
the afternoon — Early eveth
Mr. Dawl. Sayre called —
Eveth chatting until about
10 — then to bed.
