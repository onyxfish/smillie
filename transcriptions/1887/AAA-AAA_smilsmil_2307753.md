## Left Page

### September, Friday 23, 1887

Cold & bleak — some sunshine about the middle of the day, but the temperature fell steadily — I went down town soon after 10 — dinner a little before 1 — at 1.45 Eliz². started for the depot & Pet². about 4.30 bringing Mr. Parker back, being very thoroughly chilled through — The blazing fires were very grateful to them — Fire was made in the cherry bed room — there was also, a big one in studio where I put things a little more in order. We⁴ at 8 o'clk. went to Jn°. Cortright's with Annie & Eliz², they riding while I walked, Mr. Parker stayed at home. At C's there were 15 or 18 people — we played whist until 10 — then carriage came — home about 10.45 — to bed at 11.

Afternoon, Payne & team & Willie wood worked — The Smith's painted Coyle did not work —

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 24, 1887

Very cold, windy, overcast & uncomfortable. Ther. fell to 31°. last night. I was out among the men a good deal. Battle's with Thomas's team worked about 6 hours — O'Brien worked with his team about 8 hours, going away before 6 o'clk. his mare having cut one of her feet badly on a sharp stone. Payne & team worked all day — Ambrose Payne — Judson — Willie — Amy worked all day. The Smith's painted in the m.f. but complained so of the paint "crawling" that they did not work in the afternoon. Coyle did not work — I had a fine fire in the studio & commenced work, carving one of the corners for the cherry mantle piece in Reception Room. Jim & I went down town at 6 o'clk.

Coyle did not work
Eve⁴. Annie read aloud from Harper's for Oct; after which we regaled ourselves with popped chestnuts & grapes.
