## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 16, 1887

A light rain last night — again overcast mtf., clearing afterwards.
I spent the day out of doors —
went down town about 11 —
With Mr Hotchkiss I went over
the line that is disputed by
Henry Tyler & very carefully
ran a straight line & staked
it. As O'Brien had again come
with his team I started old
Mr Hotchkiss again at work
upon the wall. I worked for
awhile training the vines at
the west front of the house — in
the evtf., before 6 o'clk. went
down town — Later, newspapers
& chatting — To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 17, 1887

Pleasant mtf — growing gradually
more overcast & thicker until night.
Thomas & his team, Battles & Judson came
on to work in the mtf. O'Brien & team
came at 9.30 — Coyle also came to work.
Mother Cook made up her mind to
accept Wm Parke's offer of an escort to N.Y.
So she left at 11.20. Herrick took her to
the depot. Parke will meet her at Scranton.
Tyler came over in the mtf early & saw
Mr Hotchkiss & admitted that he was
in error when he disputed the line on
which I was having the line wall built.
The stakes convinced him that the wall
was really considerably in upon my side.
Rec'd a letter from Mr. Huntington express-
ing satisfaction with my Durand etching
& suggesting a few simple modifications.
Spent the afternoon with the men —
Evtf. went down town — then wrote to
Mr. Huntington — the Putnams & to Simp-
son & Macauley — Annie's new nurse girl
came this mtf. To bed at 11.
