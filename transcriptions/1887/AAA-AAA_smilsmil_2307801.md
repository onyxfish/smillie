## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 28, 1887

Dismal, dark day with wind and rain. About 2 o'clk., for a few minutes, a deluge. About 4 it cleared suddenly. The decorators came at 9 o'clk & to work on dining room ceiling — am not satisfied with the way the muslin is put up. To studio at q. 45 worked on "Feeding by the Wayside" but was much delayed by the weather. The carpenter got floor in big closet done & I spent some time getting my old frames & canvases up & out of the way — Home at 1.30 — back before 3 — Home again at 4.30 & took Jim up to Mr. H.F. Villard's — a xmas tree & about 20 youngsters — a very pretty sight. Anna, Tiffany, Mitchell & their two daughters there. — Annie joined us before 6. After refreshments we left and got home at 7 — dinner — Evef. Read & drew upon plans that I will send to Herrick — To bed about 10.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 29, 1887

Clear — high wind & very cold. To studio a little before 10, & worked for some time on W. C. drawing "Feeding etc." — & then put it away — again tried to make the likeness of Mr. Rives more satisfactory in my india ink drawing — then gave it up. Home to lunch, after going to the Bank soon after — back to studio at 2.30 and commenced on a small W.C. sketch — a view on Raquette — made a lot of Mr. Rives' photos, & the india ink drawing — wrote him a letter & at 5 went out & started them to Washington for him — then went down town & got an Excelsior Diary — Home soon after 6 — The decorator has finished the ceiling in dining room but has made a mess of one part of it. Dinner — Evef. worked on some drawings for Herrick — Charl came in about 9 & stayed until near 11 — then I went to bed.
