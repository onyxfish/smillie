## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 29, 1887

Bitter cold — high wind — dirt and
snow flurries — After bkfs — wrote
to Herrick & sent checks — also, to
Colman — then to G. C. Depot and
bought tickets for Boston & Drawing
Room seats — It was after 11 when
I got to studio — again at work
over my etching pfs — Home to
lunch at 1.30 — after that, to studio
for a few minutes — then to Brooks
Bro's — bought a pair of Pantaloons —
there to 10th St. Studios — Saw Nicoll and
gave him some Etching pfs — There to the
Photo. Exbn at Botsie's Gallery — There
until near 6 — Studying the exbn.
Home to dinner — back to Photo. exbn at 6.30
There met Ge. C. Cox & E. Wood Perry — spent
until 10 o'clk. going over the exbn together — then to
Century Club where I wrote the body of the
report while Cox & Perry arranged the order
of awards — after 11 when we got through
with our work — then I walked up home
to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 30, 1887

Still bright, cold & windy — To studio at
about 10 — the whole day was spent in
finishing up proofs — assorting them &
putting the studio in order for my absence —
Home to lunch at 1 after having called
on Sutton at N.A.A. galleries & talked with
him about etching large plate of Eastman
Johnson's Stage Coach — The Adams girls
at lunch — took Jine to Kindergarten & back
to studio at 2.30 — a friend of
Rodney Kimball's called to learn something
about etching processes. At 6 went to Dr.
Yale's with etching pfs to give him — not
in town. Home to dinner at 6.30 — Evelf
wrote notes to Kate, to Yale & to Koehler —
Chard came in at 9 — I packed my
valise & got to bed at 11.
