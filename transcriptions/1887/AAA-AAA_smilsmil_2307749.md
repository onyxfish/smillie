## Left Page

### September, Thursday 15, 1887

Bright mf — Men hazy and at times looking again like rain — clearing in the afternoon & at night a shower. Payne & Team & Battles with Thomas's team at work all day. Ambrose Payne & three boys — Cerny, Wood & Battles at work with the small stone heaped in pasture. Hotchkiss commenced making stone gutter by road way. Coyle seeded the new earth under trees north of the house. The Smiths painted — in the afternoon Herrick helped me & I worked with cement in getting tiles about the fire places in main room & oak bed room — Rec. a letter from Gel enclosing a long letter from Chas. Aug 28th Amsterdam. Mr. Courtright & Flora Lathrop called Eve'g. I wrote a short letter to Hde to accompany Chas's letter — I was quite used up with influenza. To bed at 10 —

## Right Page

### September, Friday 16, 1887

Breezy, bright & cool — Got up after a comfortable night, but felt so used up all day, discomfort in head & throat, that I kept in doors all day, only going out for a few minutes at a time & taking precautions — Payne & Team and Battles with Thomas's team at work — also Ambrose Payne, Willie Curry & Corrie Wood — much of the work being on the bit of road from the front door up to the barn site. Hotchkiss worked on drains — the Smiths painted — Herrick worked about his sheds — I only saw Coyle in the afternoon. Rev. Sam. Lines & wife spent a little time on our piazza — Elis. Howe came over by Lehigh Valley R.R. Annie & Jim walked over to little depot to meet her, but the train was delayed so that she did not get to the house until after 7.30 — Annie went to Sam. Sayre's to tea, while I remained at home and rec. Elis. We chatted until Annie's arrival about 8.30 — then continued the exercise until 10 — then to bed.
