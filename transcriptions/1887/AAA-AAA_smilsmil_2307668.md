## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 6, 1887

Bright, clear, windy & quite cool — after
b.b. wrote letter to Herrick with checks —
At 10.15 Annie, Jim & I took car into Boston
we parted at Providence R.R. Station. A
going to see Helen Atwater. I went to the
club where I spent the rest of the m[?]
Reading N.Y. papers — writing notes to Ge-
to Saml. Colman & chatting with W. Julius
Eichberg — At 2 had my dinner at the
Prov. Station & after that called at 118 Boylston
St but failed to see him — then on to the
museum where I spent some time
with the Rembrandt etchings, visited
Koehler but for a moment — then took
a general look through the galleries &
about 4.30 left the bldg. with Mr. Greenleaf
the Librarian etc. I walked all the way
out to Sue's — about 5 miles — a rest
before tea — Robt. chatting in parlor —
Miss Clarkson in an enquiring frame of
mind — To bed at 10 — Brilliant
& quiet moonlight night.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 7, 1887

Bright, cold & windy — went into town
by 9.30 car — directly to Club — Read
newspapers — met several acquaintances
— a long chat with Anthony — introduced
to Fawcet — an unpromising looking
man — heavy, ignoble face. About noon
went to Art Museum — there with K.
among the Rembrandt etchings &
Dürer plates until 2 — then we
went to Dining Room — Providence. Dept
& had dinner — out at 3 — went to
Enneking's Studio — filled with
pictures — broad & slap-dash — then
to Gaugengige's — one picture — small
& highly finished — like a Fischel.
It was near 5 when we came out
— parted — I went to Bowdoin Sq
& took car to Cambridge. In
the evg. Mr. Hall, Pastor of [illegible]
Society in Cambridge, took tea & spent
the evg. with us until 9.30 —
To bed soon after 10.
