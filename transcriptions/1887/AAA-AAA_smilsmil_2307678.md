## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 26, 1887

overcast m'f. clearing very pleasantly before noon. To studio about 10 - very heavy with a headache, so that I was unfit for work. Met Sam. Colmous + he went up stairs with me. Will then came in on his way to Frank Ballard's funeral. Geo. also came in. At 1.40 I went home to lunch - our new waitress, Mary, came - At 3 Annie & I went to Chickering Hall to Madame Schiellie's second piano recital - At 5 we went to Flint's furniture store & selected some furniture for Montrose - It was 6.45 before I got home to dinner — Eve'g. Chas. Collins called at the house. Chatted until 10.30 — To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 27, 1887

*Poughkeepsie.*

Very charming day - After b'kf's- wrote a letter to D. Wm. Chandler, So. Orange asking him if he was likely to be in Montrose early in July as Annie expects to be confined there. At 10.30 took train to Po'k. getting there at 2.35 - walked to the house, getting there before 1. Found Mother having one of her good days, but very, very frail. In a few minutes I had dinner - then sat & chatted with Mother until she seemed tired - then out for a walk - on my way back went to Uncle W's & saw him & Aunt H. Home about 4.30 - another visit with Mother - At 5.30 had a cup of tea - prepared some sandwiches - bid my good byes & at 5.50 started for the depot - the 6.15 was on time - got into W'f. at 8.15 - Home & had a cup of tea - then to Mother Cook's where Annie had taken dinner — Home at 10 - and right to bed.

Bessie Throop came home from school in the afternoon out of sorts with symptoms of measles.
