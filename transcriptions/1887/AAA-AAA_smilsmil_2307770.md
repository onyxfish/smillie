## Left Page

### October, Thursday 27. 1887.

Ther. last night 29°. another nasty
day — heavily clouded m[illegible] which at
last settled into a warm, foggy mizzle.
The Smiths tried to paint for awhile
on piazza railings but gave it up. May
painted the interior of my dark room
black — Battles & Am. Payne commenced
digging for trap in drains at S.W. cor. of house.
Soon after B. was called off to look after
his burglarious son — back in less than
2 hours — the rest of the m[illegible] setting
currant bushes & evergreens — at
noon I sized more canvas — then
dinner — after that again setting
out trees. Changed small ash from
place on sodded bank & put Montana
pine in its place. B. left before 4 —
[illegible] stayed until Jan 5 — Herrick worked
tacking up canvas & making wooden
connection for drain — Tea at 6. 30 —
m[illegible] wrote to Sittie — Clark & Charl.
Sized more canvas in studio —
So to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 28. 1887.

Ther. last night 41°. A mixed day.
Heavily clouded & foggy in the m[illegible] —
clearing & at times sunny in the after
noon — clear moonlight — then heavy fog.
Spent much of the m[illegible] getting [illegible]
Diven's Barberry seeds into the ground
for a hedge. Battles & A. Payne at work — a
number of holes dug for locusts — also,
sometime spent moving big cistern in
the S.W. cor. — Herrick worked at raising
the cor. without success — then tacked
up canvas on ceiling of oak bedroom.
afternoon, the Smith's came — did not
think it safe to paint — so they put up
the remaining blinds — Went off with
B.&P. & got a couple of soft maples &
set them out at turn of road into lane
— also changed places of large spruce
& a Basswood. I worked continuously
out of doors & at night was very tired —
went down town before 6 — Rec. a
telegram from Charl. at 5 announcing
his safe arrival home — Eve[illegible]
all settled about until 10. Then
to bed.
