## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 5, 1887

Bad weather still persistent overcast all day with light
rains, while at blkp. A Gen. [illegible]
& Roger Searle called on Gen. Diven
Herrick came to work & helped
the brick layer build a scaffold
about the north chimney — a short
m't. Judson came & made a
short afternoon — I worked on
Hamilton Arms & finished them
The Gen. amused himself
down town. I went down at
12 & again at 7.30. Evetg
met & saw — again, a general
chatting until 10 —

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 6, 1887

And still bad weather — some signs
of clearing — about midday quite pleasant but again it clouded over, with
light showers. Elig't. Howe & Chas. Collins
left us at 9 a.m. for N.Y. by Lehigh Valley R.R.
about 11 o'clk. Annie, Jim & I went to
the Fair & spent an hour looking over
the animals & the vegetables — Gen.
Diven delivered a short address there
in the afternoon but I did not hear
him. The brick layer got through
with his work by 2 o'clk. Judson kept
on until 6, cleaning up the mess —
The Smiths did not paint — In the
afternoon I felt so used up that I
took a nap. Ev'tg. I went down
town with the Gen. & after I attended
to my chores went into the Court House
to the Farmers Institute & heard the
General's address — We got back home
about 9.30 & sat awhile chatting —
To bed at 10.30.
