## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 22. 1887.

Charming day — got to studio about 10 o'clk. Paperhanger there — after consultation, decided to have muslin stretched over partition & arranged to meet him again at 2. — Then home & finished the Trade mark drawing of Ruppert — Back to studio at 2 and worked with the man until 4.30 when I could no longer see — Had much trouble with the man as a he knew nothing of putting up the sized canvas — I wish Herrick could be here. Home at 5. Dinner at 6.30. Rueff, as Annie did not feel like going out to Calthrufs's lecture so I finished plan for Montrose Etching room ceiling & about 9 walked down to the Yandel Gallery — 5th Ave. & 19th S. & saw Piloty's "painting" "the Wise & foolish Virgins" — very large & very poor. Home at 10.30 — & then to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 23. 1887.

Overcast day, but otherwise, pleasant Bkf. before 8 — got to studio soon after. Paperhanger there struggling with the difficulties of stretching the sized canvas upon the closet partition. I worked with him until 11 o'clk. when I paid him $3.00 & sent him off while I stayed & worked until I getting it finished — then I went home to lunch — after that, finished a letter to Herrick, with instructions & also a list of bills of Montrose tradesmen with cheques in payment & plan for etching room ceiling — Posted it on my way to the Studio where I experimented upon some of the puckered canvas by wetting it — Home soon after 5 — Dinner at 6.30 — Rueff. Read Harper for Dec. Charl came in after 9 & with him I went to his room. cor. 40th S. & Mad. Ave. Home & to bed at 11.
