## Left Page

### August, Sunday 14, 1887

Delightful weather still continues — Up late — and a day of loafing — Spent two hours or more of the morning in the hammock. Dinner at 2.30 — Went wrote to Huntington & Koehler, at 10 o'clk. took them down to the P.O. then to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 15, 1887

Overcast & threatening in the m'g but the weather cleared pleasantly. Got up with a wretched headache which was much aggravated by an exasperating letter from H & C. Tyler who complains that the wall that I am now building is away over on his land. I went down town & took counsel of Dav. Searle. Back in time to see Mrs. Hagnier (Annie's nurse) Off in the stage. Then I went to bed & stayed there until near 7 when I got up & had something to eat. I wandered about the place while there was daylight — then sat about in doors until 10.30 — then to bed.
