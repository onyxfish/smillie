## Left Page

### September, Thursday 29, 1887

Very heavy fog in the m'f. with some slight attempts at rain but later the weather cleared & it was comparatively pleasant. Reynolds did not appear to work on the chimney. Payne & Ambrose came with more manure. I spent all the time that I could get at work on mantel carving. The Smiths painted on blinds in the shop. In the afternoon about 5 o'clk. I went down town with Chat, Calkins & Jim. I found a young brick layer from Waverley & engaged him & his helper to come to work on the chimney to-morrow. Eve'g. Annies read aloud for a while — after & walking on the piazza for a short time by the light of a foggy moon. To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 30, 1887

Between 3 & 4 A.M. deluges of rain & a heavy thunderstorm that passed a little south of us. I was uneasy lest the scaffolding with bricks upon on the roof should be blown down also, lest the lightning rod which was attached to the large chimney its down, should prove a destructive agent — a very mild day, heavy clouds, fog, drizzle & some struggling sunshine in the m'f. The Waverley brick layer came in the m'f. but did not commence work. Laying up the chimney that has been taken down until the afternoon. Derrick was his helper until 4:30 when Judson came along. Am. Payne hauled a load of brick & mortar in the m'f. The Smiths painted under Studio piazza. Mr. Parker left us for N.Y. at 11:15 A.M. I worked on the Cherry mantel. Went down town in the m'f. & again after tea. Eve'g. we had reading, cards & conversation — to bed at 11.
