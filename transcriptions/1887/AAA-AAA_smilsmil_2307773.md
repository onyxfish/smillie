## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 2, 1887

Ther. 21° last night.

A glorious day — mild & cloudless. After a very still, clear cold night. I spent most of the day in doors getting things in order for packing. At 10.30 I went down town and attended to various little matters — back soon after 12 — some time with A. Payne who dug post holes to set boxes in which to set posts for clothes lines — then he & Battles, who came about 10) spent the rest of the day setting out a number of small soft maples, to form a sort of hedge to hide the high stone walls of the Fair ground — 70 are to be set out. The Smiths painted about the piazza — Herrick jobbed about as directed — I well wrote a short note to J. T. A. Ward in answer to one from him. Then, with after 10 packing big home case with eng. books etc etc. Toiled a little before 11, very tired.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 3, 1887

Ther. last night. 39°.

Delicious Indian Summer day — very mild & hazy — Set to work in earnest in studio packing studio's case — went down town about 10.30 — soon back again at work, getting case nearly packed by dinner time — Then a short rest, but soon had to look after the men — the Smiths painting about the piazza — Battles & Mr. Payne came in the afternoon & set out a lot more of small soft maples along the line of the Fair ground wall. — Herrick was kept busy making packing boxes etc — & doing something in the way of packing — I finished up about the studio & outside matters & had a little rest before tea time — After that, finished the home case & got things together for my trunk — by the time I had all my little domestic cares attended to it was 10 o'clk — being very tired, I went to bed.
