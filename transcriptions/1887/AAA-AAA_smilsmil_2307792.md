## Left Page

### December, Saturday 10, 1887

Nasty wet day — To studio before 10
& worked all the m'th on A. B. N.
Co's drawing Water-Cart — Home
at 1 — back before 2.30 & worked as
long as I could see, which was
until about 4 o'clk., when I
finished Water-cart drawing —
Just as I was about leaving for
the day Koehler's friend, W. J. D.
Lauge of 42 E. 63d. S. with his friend
Jean T. Harfin came in to look at
my painting "The Edge of the Wood" —
I had told K— that I would sell it
for half price, or $75.00, frame & all —
on those terms W. Lauge said he would
take it — I advised him to see it by day
light, so he is to come in on Tues. m'g. at 11.
Home at 6. Rueth went to Phil. concert.
Mr. Lincoln, a friend of Geo's occupied
Annie's seat. I walked part-way home
with Charl & he told me Winter's proposition
which I advised him to accept.
Home at 10.40 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 11, 1887

Mild, moist & foggy — In
bed all day with a severe
headache — did not get up
until after 9 o'clk. P. m — To
bed again about 1 A. m.

Miss Abbatt dined with
Annie — also her bro. Edw'd.
Cook & Carleton — Charles
called & saw me at about
6 p. m.
