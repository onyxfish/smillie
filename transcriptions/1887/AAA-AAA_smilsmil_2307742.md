## Left Page

### September, Thursday 1, 1887

Another delightful day, but warmer. O'Brien & team & Payne & team at work all day. also Ambrose Payne & boy, Battles & Willie Curry — picnic frames — Hotchkiss at the wall. — Hemick at work on window, [illegible] the Smith's painting & Coyle weeding in the pasture lot. After being with the men for some time I began to think of getting out my painting things, but Mr. & Mrs. Parke & Mr. John Scranton called. I went down town at 1:30 — & made a few photo. prints at Doolittle's. The afternoon was spent with the men. Went down town before 8 — got letter from Mima B — with some of Miss Clara Howard's drawings & application for admission to N.A. of D. Schools. Wrote notes to Mima & to N. A. Richards. To bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 2, 1887

Rain commenced about midnight & continued steadily through most of the m'g — & again heavily during part of the afternoon. The Smith's painted under the piazza. Hemick worked in his shop — none of the other men were able to work. I spent all of the day with the painters — In the afternoon Annie went by invitation to the Parke's to lunch — at 4:30 a party of a dozen — a muddle — Mr. P. intended to invite me — I did not understand it so — The carriage sent back for me but I wrote a note begging off. Kate went down to Kitty Benuty Schaeffer's to tea — Jim & I took tea alone. Annie ret'd before 7 — before 8 I started for Kate but met her with Mr. S — half way home. — Eve'g. I read news papers & wrote a couple of business notes for Annie. — To bed about 10.
