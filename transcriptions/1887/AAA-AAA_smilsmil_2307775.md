## Left Page

### November, Sunday 6, 1887

A delightful day — after 8 when
I had b[illegible]. Soon after that
Yale came in — saw the youngster
have his bath & spent some time
with us. Learned from him that
Mother Cook had been alarmingly
ill last night — vomiting & pain —
easier this m[orning]. It was near
1 o'clk when I went down to the
Studio Century to see the paintings.
LaFarge's large encaustic painting
to decorate Whitelaw Reed's house was
there — Met LaFarge & had a very
pleasant half hours chat — Dinner
at 2 — Wrote to P.S.K — also to Lyns
& Ticket agent D.L. & W.R.R. at Binghampton
about my seat trouble on Friday — Went out
at 5.30 — mailed letters & went for Annie
who had been with her Mother for some
time — tea at 6.30 — Eve[ning]. Geo. & Millie
made a long call — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 7, 1887

A very pleasant day — after
attending to a number of things
about the house I went down
town, getting to the A.B.M. Co.
at 11 — waited an hour & a half
for Will — there gave [illegible] — Went
to Boyle's in Fulton St. & got
some cotton stuff to cover wooden
partition in Studio here — Sent
it to Herrick to have it sized.
Home & lunched at 1.30. Annie
with her Mother who is very
weak. Before 3 went to Safe
Deposit Co. Got gas coupons &
deposited them in Bank. New
to Knoedler's — met Belle & Mary
Tweedy — then to A.A.A. & saw
the Beecher pictures, books, etc
Home before 5 — dinner at
6.30 — Eve[ning]. quite used up
feeling miserably weak & sore all
over. Charle came in & spent
some time — at 10 I went with
him to his new rooms, Mad. Ave.
& 40th St. Stopped to enquire after
Mother Cook — no better — To bed at 11.
