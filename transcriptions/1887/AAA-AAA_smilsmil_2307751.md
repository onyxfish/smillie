## Left Page

### September, Monday 19, 1887

Very warm & pleasant — about all day with the men except from 11 to 12 when I went down town & took some photo. [illegible] at Doolittle's. Battles worked with Thomas's team until 4 p.m. — Payne & his team. & Ambrose & Willie wood worked until 5 — O'Brien & his team came on at 9.30 — Willie Currie worked in the [illegible]. Coyle worked getting a place ready for blackberry bushes. Hotchkiss almost finished the road gutter — The Smiths finished the first painting of the house. Herrick worked on chimney room mantel. Nayler came with daughter & cleaned & set up the stoves. Minnie's muleford & Miss Cook called in the afternoon. Evelf, placidly uneventful — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 20, 1887

Up at 4 o'clk — Jim had a little spell of croupy coughing — Up for all day soon after 7. A very hazy, smoky day, warm in the sunlight but with a chill in the air that was not at all pleasant. Battles worked with Thomas's team, but not a full day. Payne & team at work, also Ambrose Payne & the boys Willie, Amy & Willie Wood. One of Battles' boys worked for a time — Coyle poked over the ground where berry bushes are to be put. Hotchkiss, after fixing a bit of walk in front of the house, laid up the sheeting in the wall between [illegible] Tyler's son land — Then he started the chimney in Herrick's shop. Herrick assisted. The Smiths commenced the painting of the second coat on the house. Went downtown bet. 11 & 12 & again at 6 — Evelf with newspaper — Jim quite restless but showing no fever.
