## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 27, 1887

Overcast mng., clearing warmer
at the middle of the day. Payne &
his team & Willie Curny worked until
11 & again in the afternoon when Ard.
joined them. I went down town
between 11 & 12 — & then worked for
a time on carving for mantle piece.
Dinner at 1. — At about 2.30 we all
went out riding — I took pick and
shovel with me & near Hart Lake
dug a couple of laurel roots & on
our return home had them set
out — we got back at 5 — then
down towns with the team —
Evelf. wrote a letter to Charl.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 28, 1887

Overcast & dismal — fine mizzle
of rain commenced about 4 P.m.
Ambrose Payne spent most of the
day hauling manure upon the new
sloughed ground that is to be an or-
chard. In the afternoon Reynolds.
the brick layer came with Judson &
with Herrick to help them they got
the main chimney down to the shoulder
by 5 o'clk. I spent much of the
day in the studio carving at the cherry
mantel corners. At 4.30 Chas. Collins
arrived to make us a visit — I went
down town about 6 — Evelf. spent
in chatting with some exhibitions
of games with cards. To bed at
11.
