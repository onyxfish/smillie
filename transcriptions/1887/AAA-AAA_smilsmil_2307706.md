## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 21, 1887

Very hot & dog-dayish. Much of the time overcast with strong south wind — Evelf. before 8, a strong wind storm with thunder & lightning came up from the south & after a time settled into a steady rain — Herrick at work on little closet in etching room & about wash basin in studio — Jackson picking stones in pastures — N. Smith & team until noon or so working over new seeded ground in lane. Whereupon about I was at work upon one of the 3 S.A. drawings for A.B. N. Co. Evelf. During the storm until 9 o'clk. sitting with Annie & Emily & reminiscing. Then a little writing & to bed at our usual time — 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 22, 1887

Very heavy rain all the mtf. many leakages about the studio cistern, the worst one at the faucet & basin — water running over the studio floor — Soon after 9 went for Zerfass — he came & stopped the leak — I got to work at last on one of the three drawings for A. B. N. Co. "An Entrance to Suite" Before dinner I took a short walk & after dinner worked until 6 on drawing. Down town before 7 — then back to tea — got a letter from Dr. Wells of Binghamton practically saying that he cannot take charge of Annie's case. Evelf. worked awhile on Suite drawing then packed it to Expp. To-morrow mtf., while A— read aloud the newspaper accts. of Victoria Jubilee —
