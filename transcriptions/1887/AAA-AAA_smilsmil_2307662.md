## Left Page

### March, Friday 25, 1887.

Very pleasant day — To studio at 10 —
To work on Brooklyn Bridge drawing.
Soon after 2 went to Washington Wilson's
on Union Square — bought some collars &
had quite a long visit with him, so
that it was near 2 when I got home
to lunch — After it Eliz. Howe came
in — I took Jim to Kindergarten &
to studio at 3 — Finished the bridge
drawing soon after 5 — Mr. Beach of
the Amateur Photo. Soc'y called. wishes
me to be chairman of Jury of Awards.
The Exhibit to open to-morrow at the
Ortgies Gallery — I wrote notes to the
other members of Jury, C.Y. Turner —
E.W. Perry, Geo. G. Rockwood & Geo. C. Cox.
Home to dinner at 6.30. Eve'g wrote
a letter to Kate — then a game of
cards with Annie — At 10 went to
G.C.D. with litter — home & to bed

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 26, 1887.

Still bright & pleasant — Got to
Studio about 10 — The m'f. sketch —
in general work about the studio.
Home to lunch at 1 — after that
dressed, packed my bag and a
little before 3 Annie, Jim & I start-
ed for the foot of Christopher St. where
we took the 3.35 train for Mountain
Station where we arr'd about 4.30 —
The Parker-White house is very near to
the Station — We found that Howard
White had been suddenly & unexpec-
tedly called west — but we were greeted
by Mr. W— & her friend, Miss Faucon
of Melton, near Boston — I soon went
out with Jim & the two boys & as they
got along very well I sauntered off for
an hours stroll — when I ret'd Miss
Noland & her sister Mr. Cornell were there
calling — Dinner between 6 & 7 —
Eve'g chatting until after 10 —
then to bed.
