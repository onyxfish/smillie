## Left Page

### January, Saturday 22, 1887

Overcast & raw, altho' not cold.
Annie gave me a scare by not
being able to get up in the m'g.
It may be that her A.B.N. Co. ex-
pedition was too much for her
yesterday — She kept her bed all day.
Got to studio at 0.30 & worked faith-
fully all day on Montrose picture
getting the second painting finished.
Home to lunch dot — then to Bank
& got to studio about 2.30 — Katu &
Mollie Adams ret'd to Ridgefield
on 4 P.M. train — About 4 o'clk
two ladies called to see pictures —
one of them from Indianapolis —
Home at 6 o'clk. Even'g reading
& dozing in big chair until 10.
Annie asleep in her bed —
to bed about 11.

Geo. & Charl. went to Pk. to
spend Sunday.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 23, 1887

A bright & very nice day —
Ther. on N. side of house, above 60°.
Bkf't about 9 — Annie better, but
she did not get up until noon.
After reading paper I took Jim
at 1.30 & went up to Central Park
with him — Uncomfortably warm
in the sunshine — mud & water
everywhere — Home soon after 1
& dinner at 2.15. Afternoon spent
in reading — Emma & Mary D.
came in & made a call at
twilight — Tea at 6.30 — then
the Hibernians went off to Mad.
Sq. gardens to give Michael Davitt
a farewelling — I wrote letter
home & one or two other notes —
waited until 11 before my
ladies came in — then to
bed.
