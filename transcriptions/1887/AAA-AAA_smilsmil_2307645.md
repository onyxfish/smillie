## Left Page

### February, Thursday 17, 1887

Charming day. To studio at 10 &
worked awhile on sketches for car view
for A.B.N. Co. Then to Bank & deposited
$18,000 for Annie, from sale of Jackson
Iron Co. stock. Then to 9 W. 17th St. — office
of Rob't. Gorlet & saw him — talked for
half an hour about plans for a studio
building — I promised to make some for
him for a proposed bldg. cor. 28th St. & 4th
Ave. Then to Grand Central Depot to see
Mr. Webb of Sleeping Car Co — about A.B.N. Co.
drawing — not in town — then home
& had lunch. To studio soon after 2.
Found invitation from W. Beach to act as one
of the judges at Amateur Photographers
Ex'n to be held next month — all the
p.m. at work mounting etching pts. Home
at 6.30 — dinner — At 8.30 went to
Walter's & until 10 o'clk. Talking over
plans for Montrose House — the interior
finish of studios & cherry room. Home
& to bed soon after 11.
Charl went up to P'k — called at the
house on his return at 8.30 & said that
he left mother very weak.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 18, 1887

Nasty day — dark m'f. Very heavy
rain in the afternoon & thunderstorms
during the eve'g. To studio about 10 —
Took 3 parchment pts of "Voice from Cliff"
to Greer at U.A. [illegible] — then back & worked
on large locomotive drawing for A.B.N.
Co. until 12.45 — then home to lunch —
Before 2 went to Phil. Rehearsal
with the Adams girls — Annie following
soon after — Did not enjoy the music
immensely — At 4.30 back to studio
in a drenching shower. Berniau
came in & I signed studio's lease for
another year — worked until 6 at
mounting etching pts. Home at 6.30
caught in a thunder shower —
Dinner — Eve'g Annie read
aloud tons from The Broken or Fallen
Idol until 10 o'clk — To bed at 10.30
