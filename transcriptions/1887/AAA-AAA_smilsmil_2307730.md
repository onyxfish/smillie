## Left Page

### August, Monday 8, 1887

Very cool & clear. Last night the Ther. fell to 47° — stood at 56° when
I got up at 7.30. O'Brien came with his team & commenced ploughing the
lower part of the pasture, but, breaking his plough point he gave up work about
3 P.m. Herrick did not come to work.
I worked on my Durand plate and took the first pft. on thin Jap. paper
upon which it seemed to me to look very well. Edgar Luvele called in the
afternoon with ff. of Jewell's plain for me. About 5 Jim & I went down town.
I hired a couple of men to work at digging up stones on the pasture lot.
Evth. Annie was with us in the large room — we read newspapers
& played Bazique. I Rec'd India paper from Kimmel & Voigt, so I wet
down paper for printing to-morrow —
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 9, 1887

Delightful day. O'Brien came
with his team & hired man & worked until 10, then he left, leaving team
& man, who worked the rest of the day — White (a white man) & Jordan
Blair (a darkie) came in the with & worked all day, continuing the wall
between H. C. Tyler's lot & mine. Mr. Hotchkiss came in the afternoon &
commenced laying foundation for the shop. I worked pretty closely
all day taking pfts of Durand plate — some India — some thin Jap — and
some on Inkennial Jap. Thinking that the plate was finished — but at the
end of this day's work concluded that I would do some more finishing on it.
Evth. went down town before 8 o'clk — read newspaper & loafed.
To bed at 10.
