## Left Page

### November, Thursday 24, 1887.

*Thanksgiving.*

A raw, overcast, windy day. To studio before 10 & spent the mf, wetting & stretching the canvases over the closet partitions where it was wrinkled. Home before 1 & had a little lunch — then, as my head ached badly I went to bed & stayed there until near 5 o'clk — Then up & with A. & Jim went to Ed W. Cook's where we had a quiet dinner — many's nephew & niece — & Williams being there. After 7 I went home with Jim & put him to bed — then back — Before 10, home with Annie — Sat & read for an hour, then to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 25, 1887.

Some rain in the mf. — very little — overcast, foggy, raw — milder in the afternoon & some moonlight at night. To studio before 10 — Young McCormick came & I arranged with him to have a carpenter come in the mf. to put up some mouldings — Then to back & spent some time in the safe deposit — making a careful inventory of contents of box there. Then to D'Neil's & got photo copies of Mr. Rives's photo. Told Mr. Cook's daf. — both unexpectedly good. Home before 1 — lunch — Afternoon, tracing photo, S.A. scene — water cart — About 5 went to studio & other places — found that Mr. C. had sent some of the mouldings to studio — from the saw — without planing — Dinner at 6.30 — After 8 in the evef. went to Mr. G. L. Rives' 15 E. 29th S. — with enlarged photo copy portrait of Mr. R. — he was much pleased — Home soon after 9 — looked awhile at back numbers of London Punch.
