## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 15, 1887

Overcast in the m'g with some
prospects of rain — but it burned
away before noon & became very
hot. Had Jackson dig a lot of
holes in the afternoon which helped
me set out the evergreens —
Zerfass & Hemick finished the
repairing of the broken cistern at
the N.W. cor. of the house — By
afternoon mail I got letters from
Kate & Charl. telling that Mother
is now extremely low — Mrs.
H.f. Tyler called at 8 o'clk. while
we were at tea — After 9 o'clk.
I wrote a letter to Mother & after
10 took it to the P.O. — About
11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 16, 1887

A hot day — about 4 o'clk some
promise of a shower, but nothing
came of it until 8 o'clk. when we
got the edge of a shower that passed
south of us — Hemick commenced
work on the ceiling of the cherry
room — Jackson working about the
new plants & digging the last doz-
d 105 holes — Smith & team and
Coyle working on lane road and
ploughing at the turn of the lane —
Eve'g on piazza until after 9 —
then read news paper & got
to bed about 10.30.
