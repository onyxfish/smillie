## Left Page

### April, Saturday 16. 1887.

Dull day — I got up with a heavy head which made me quite useless during the day. It was 11 o'clk when I got to studio. I busied myself with my etching plt. getting them into order. Went to the Bank — & to K. Noadlers before going home to lunch at 1. Mary went with the children to Barnum's circus in the afternoon — I did little work & home at 6.30 — Dinner. Charl with us — Evetf. he took us, Mary, Annie & me, to Mr. S. Martin where we saw Denman Thompson as Joshua Whitcombe in "The Old Homestead" — an amusing trifling thing — Thompson, a natural impersonation of a simple countryman — To bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 17. 1887.

A raw day with struggling attempts at sunlight — or sunshine. Bkf. about 9 — Many went to hear Mr. Armytage but I did not get out until noon when I took Jim out. He was so much interested in the bridges just north of Grand Central depot that I had hard work to get him home in time for dinner at 2 — Afternoon I took a nap, while Annie, Jim, Mary & the girls went to George's — about 5 o'clk, Joe & little Walter came. They waited until the family returned. Tea about 7. Evetf. Charles came in. I wrote letter to Mother & one to Koehler — It was about 11 when I got to bed.
