## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 7, 1887

The day opened with a heavy thunder shower at about 6 o'clk. The
rest the mty was cloudy but ground
very warm & muggy. Only Mr. Hotchkiss
at work — The Painters at work under
blazes — In the afternoon O'Brien
& Payne & teams came — also the boys
Cuny & Wood. About 3.30 a very
heavy storm of thunder & lightening
wind & hail came up — the hail was
furious, much of it being as big as hickory
nuts — Every body was driven off but
the Painters who were under cover.
a succession of heavy showers fell
until near 6 o'clk. when it cleared
windy, sparkling & almost cold —
Evef. I went to G. Fordham's & spent
more than an hour talking over the
matter of selection of fruit to put into
our little orchard garden — here
home — Katie's last eve'f with us —
chatting until after 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 8, 1887

Up at 6 — Ther. fell last night to 46°.
mty, breezy & very cool. — Latter part
of day delightfully clear & breezy —
O&fp. at 6.30 & at 7 I drove with Kate
in a buggy to Montrose Station. getting
there, in spite of muddy roads, in less
than one hour. Train on time at 8 21. &
Kate left for N.Y. & O.K. after having been
weighed & found to have gained 7 lbs.
in five weeks. Got home at 10. after
a bleak & chilly ride — Spent the rest
of the mty with O'Brien (the only man
at work in the field) & the Painters —
O'B. finished ploughing garden & got
started on the ploughing of the orchard
garden — Sarbell came over & spent some
time in wandering about with me —
Ambrose Payne came at 4 o'clk. and
commenced digging ditch to be filled
with stone on the west side of drive
way —
