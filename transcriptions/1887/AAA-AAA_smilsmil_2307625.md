## Left Page

### January, Saturday 8, 1887

Very cold & disagreeable — To studio at the usual time & got started upon an upright sketch — "A bit of cliff near Etretat" — on a board that I bought at Windsor & Newton's in London — The board is beautiful in appearance but it proved a snare & a delusion — I could not make a wash upon it & the color washed right off it — still I persevered, thinking it good discipline — Home to lunch & back — worked as long as I could see, then took a nap in my chair — Home at 6 — dinner — After 8 took Annie around to her mother's — then I walked down to the Century — Annual Meeting — Some interest in the voting — Opposition ticket swept everything — my Water-Color friends — left early — Home at 11 & to bed at once.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 9, 1887

Dull day with flying snow all day, at times falling quite heavily — Up late — after bkfst — Reading paper awhile — About 12.30 Annie & I went to Century to see the pictures — Rode up in 3rd. Ave. cars, stopping awhile at Ed[ward] Cook's. Then home & dinner at 2.30 — wrote a little in the afternoon — Charl came in before 5 o'clk — we went out together about 6 — I mailed letter to Po't — then to Yale's & got some colonel for Jim — Arl's worked for some time touching upon a [illegible] for him — Near & when I got home & had a cup of tea — Then a little reading & to bed before 11 —

Young Stedman called with ph[oto]s of some eng[raved] portraits by Hollyer for me to look at.
