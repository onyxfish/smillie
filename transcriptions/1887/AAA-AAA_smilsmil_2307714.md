## Left Page

### July, Thursday 7, 1887

A good, honest hot day with delightful
breeze blowing. Spent the mtf until
11 o'clk. weeding the flower beds in
front of the house — then with Henick
directing the finishing of studio ceiling
tree downtown — then a little work
on Julio's theatre drawing — Dinner
after one. Afternoon finished the
tracing & retracing of drawing —
wrote a note to Gee. Every
week downtown before I — went to
Dr. Richardson's to see that he was
on hand ready to respond at a
moment's call. Then home — we
sat on Piazza until after 9, then
read paper for awhile before going
to bed at about 10, 30.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 8, 1887

*Our second boy was born at 9·10 P.m*

An exceedingly hot day — with
showers south & north pus, but nothing
came over us. Old Coyce cutting
the tall grass to make hay for "Jessie."
I worked at intervals upon Julio's
Theatre drawing & also with Henick
clearing out the studio preparatory to
my getting into it. Went downtown at
7.20 p.m. on my return Annie told me
that I had better send for Mrs. Hagmeyer
the nurse, now at Mr. Parke's. I sent Kate
down with a note — in 10 m. Nelie told me
that I had better run for the Dr., which I did
instanter, going across lots — got back with the
Dr. as soon as I could — heard a child's cry as
I got over the wall at Eldred's barn — Rushed
in & found the boy born. Nelie & Mr. Curry with
Annie — Mr. C. acting as midwife — the child was
born with a caul, which Mr. C. removed — the chord
was twice around the neck — Dr. R. removed
that & severed & tied the chord. The after birth
soon followed with little hemmorage — in an
hour she was washed & bandaged, bed changed
& by 11 we were all ready for our respective
beds — but I did not get much sleep — I
had been wound up too tight.
