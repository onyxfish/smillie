## Left Page

### May, Monday 2, 1887.

Warm, clear day — To studio at 10 — spent some time writing letter to Herrick — then to work on Poll Br. drawing — Home soon after and back before 3 — some time spent planning closets in the Waller Room into which I expect to move. Then to work on Bridge drawing which I finished shortly before 6 — Packed it up — Took it to Adams Exps. & sent it to W. Cone at Housatonic, Mass, at Will's request — sent him a letter also. Walked up to 155 W. 47' S. hoping to see Dr. Allen — not in — Nearly 7 when I got home to dinner — Spent reading awhile — then selecting vegetables & flower seeds from catalogues. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 3, 1887.

Another warm spring day — Abg. 30 went to U.S. Club to see Charl about Mr. — found him quite out of sorts — then went down 6th Ave to Holbrook's, near 24th S. to see some wrought iron andirons that I looked at yesterday p.m — just sold, but he could order a new pair — then to Conover's in 25th S. & looked over their stock. To studio at 10.30 — found that the men had started to patch ceiling in Waller's old Studio. I worked on large locomotive drawing for N.B.N. Co. until 11.45 — then went to 9 W. 17th S. Gorlet's office — was there told that I should have a new ceiling & closets — on my way back stopped in to the new Vandel Gallery to see the exhibition of Socy of Am. Artists — met Wm. Chase — Home to lunch at 1.40 — To studio at 2.30 & worked on drawing until 4.50 then to Met. Museum of Art. Spring Opening. A remarkable show of new pictures recently presented to the museum — lovely walk thro' the Park — Home at 7 — dinner. a card from Charl — sick — at 8.30 went to see him — seems to have a severe cold. Home at 9.30 — wrote a note to Dr. H.D. Allen & to bed before 11.
