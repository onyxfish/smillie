## Left Page

### December, Friday 30, 1887

Very bright & cold & less windy than yesterday. Went to Douglas' shop
in 40' S. & saw him about the flaw in the decoration of the dining room
ceiling. The decorator came in the mf & covered it up with a spray
of wild Rose. To studio before 10 & to work on three water-color sketches —
two bits on Maryland east shore and the third at Rowsley, England — working
alternately upon them while washes were drying. Had most trouble &
made most progress upon a gray effect Maryland. Home to lunch at 1. Wm
Douglas called — back to studio at 2.30 —
worked as long as I could see — then to Fre. Stokes & showed him sketch
for "Competitive Blowers" — from Etching.
Men to Grady & McKeever's & ordered a small water-color frames. Home
to dinner — Evef. Chat. Collins came in & stayed until after 10 —
Gretia being out we had Ralph on a sofa in the parlor. To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 31, 1887

Dull day. Bleak & raw with snow commencing in the afternoon —
Got to studio a few min. after 10 — disappointed at the appearance of my work. It looked worse
than I had thought. To work on the long Harleigh one & worked all the rest of the day, leaving
it without much satisfaction —
Frame for Wayside Feeders came — the appearance of that drawing
is a disappointment to me.
This is so hot, smudgy & cottony —
About 1.15 went to Bank — 1.45 when I got home to lunch — back before
3\. Home to dinner at about 6
Evef. Read awhile & got to bed about 10. So ends the year 1887. Very prosaic.
