## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 23, 1887

Pleasant day, though much of the time overcast — after being up at 2 & 8 A.M. I felt discouraged & sent for the Dr. again — He came soon after 9 & made up a new prescription for me. I spent most of the day on the bed feeling very weak & stupid — got up in the afternoon & sat in my big chair — Annie went out and mother Cook played in the [illegible]. Evelf. after some reading, Annie & I had a game of cards & to bed at 10 o'clk. hoping that at last my bowel troubles is about over.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 24, 1887

Cold, windy & march-like — I got up & went down to [illegible] — feeling better but very weak — Sat in dining room — Dr. Yale came in about 11 o'clk. Very soon after pains came upon me & from 12 to 2 I was in an agony of tor-ture — such pains as I experienced the winter before I was married & for which no satisfactory explanations could be given by the doctors — From 2 to 4 I lay upon my bed in a sort of drowsy stupor — then got up, finding myself extremely weak — Evelf. & Mr. Koehler came to dinner — I was able to sit at the table but ate nothing — Then, until 9.30 — going over Father's early work & history — At that time I was so used up that I had to excuse myself & went to bed in a fever & passed a restless and uncomfortable night.
