## Left Page

### April, Saturday 2, 1887.

Very heavy snow storm, continuous
all day — About noon I walked down
to the square & back getting pretty
well wet — The rest of the day
was obliged to keep in doors —
Annie wrote a note to Mr. Toppan
excusing ourselves from going to tea
there as invited. I read and
slept & ate & walked in doors,
so getting rid of the day — In
the evef we amused ourselves
trying "Vitesse" a new game
of cards to us — To bed before
11.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 3, 1887.

Brilliant, cloudless day — the snow
running off in rivers of water — Jim
misbehaved outrageously after 4 Am.
& I got up feeling very seedy. Skip-
ping — did some snow shoveling
on piazza — at noon, some writing.
Dinner at 2 o'clk. Afternoon a little
more shovelling of snow — Reading —
writing & walking on piazza — Tea
about 7 — Evef. Two brothers Read,
Chas. & Good[illegible], I think, called —
they left at about 10 — then to bed.
