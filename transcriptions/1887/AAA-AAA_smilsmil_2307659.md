## Left Page

### March, Saturday 19, 1887

Still overcast but somewhat milder — after 10 when I got
to studio & to work on Arms vigt.
Uncle Willie came in — I worked
until about — then home to
lunch — back & finished Arms
about 4 — then spent the rest
of the afternoon at work among
my etching plt. Home to dinner
at 6.30 — Evel't went to Special
Century meeting — the comm'. on selection
of new site for bldg asked to have the
limit of $150,000. Removed — animated
discussion — some advocating en-
largement of club — increase of dues
& initiation fees — & a $400,000 club house,
others more moderate & yet others
begging to be left as they are. Mr. Prentiss
made a point that there was a consti-
tutional limit of $300,000 fixed for the
club's holdings & that if there was to be
such wide extension the constitution
would have to be changed — so his amend-
ment was adopted, practically binding
the com. as before. Adjourned at 10.30 —
walked briskly home & to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 20, 1887

A pleasant day — I took cold
last night & have a voice like
a double basso — Spent much
of the m'g, Reading — About noon
Annie & I took Jim & went to the
A. A. A. & saw the Stewart Collection
being of the privileged few who
get in on Sunday — a pretentious,
but very poor lot of pictures — Home
to dinner at 2 — Annie went
to see The Ireland's in the after-
noon — I took Jim to Mother Cook's
& then ret'd home to write — at
6 again to Mother C's & brought Jim
& Annie home — Tea — Evel't wrote
my letter home & at 10.30 went to
G. C. D. with it — after 11 when
I got to bed.
