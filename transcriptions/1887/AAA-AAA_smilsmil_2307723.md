## Left Page

### July, Monday 25, 1887

Cloudy mtf. sun coming out very hot before noon. Got a late start at my studio work — At a little before noon went with Jim to the Foundry — then to P.O. & got a letter from Charl with a Waiver of Citation in case of mother's will — and deed of Uncle Willie's Greenwood lot transferred to me. Dinner after — a tremendously heavy thunder storm and flood of rain between 3 & 4 o'clk — at 4.30 I went down town & had a Notary (Amos Nichols) attest my signature to the Waiver — then I posted it — Another steady Rain between 7 & 8. Every I wrote to Will & to Uncle Willi

Heavy thunder storm about 1 o'clk. A.M. after which Rain fell pretty steadily for the rest of the night.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 26, 1887

Very heavy rain until about 10 o'clk, then it cleared muggy & very hot. I worked about out doors with weeds & vines until nearly 12. — then to my etching room & before dinner at 1.30 I had laid etching grounds on the Durand plate & the large Huntington plate — after dinner I made a gelatine tracing from the photo. of Durand's Portrait, scraped it & got it ready for a transfer. Down town before 6. Then tea — Every sat with Annie & Read — the early part of the evef. enjoyed the moonlight. To bed at 10.30.
