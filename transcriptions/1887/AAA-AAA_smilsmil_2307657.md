## Left Page

### March, Sunday 13, 1887

Brilliant mtf., overcast after 4.
I got up about 8 & had a walk
to the top of the trust hills before
breakfast — mother seeming to be
refreshed — the mtf. was divided
between short chats with her &
retirings to the front room. Dr. Otis
came about noon & seemed to be
more hopeful that mother might
rally & get about again — I took
a short walk before dinner — in
abt — again the time was passed
as in the mtf. Before 4 I went
out & walked for more than an hour.
Emma Bourne came to the house.
Tea at 5.30 — our good byes to Mother
& at 5.50 started for depot — Train on
time — got into G.C. Depot about
8.20 — home — wrote a short note
to mother & took it to depot and
mailed it — home & to bed
about 11.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 14, 1887

Bright day but windy — overcast in
the evef. — raw & marchy — To studio
at 10 — before I got to work Uncle
Willie & Aunt Agnes came in and
stayed until 11.30 — I then got to work
making tracings of parts of Walter
Cook's architectural drawings for
Montrose. Then took drawings to
Adams &pp. & sent them to Herrick.
Home to lunch at 1.30 — back before
3 — Stopped at Miss Abbatt's & made
a call — then to work on a drawing
of Iron Mills for A.B.N. Co. — Wallen
came in — then Fred Smillie who
wants me to make a drawing for
the Canadian Co. a long talk with
him on matters of faith — Home at
6.30 — dinner — Evef. wrote letter
to Herrick & posted it before 9.
Then Annie & I had a game of
cards & to bed at 10.30.
