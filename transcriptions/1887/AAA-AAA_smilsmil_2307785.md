## Left Page

### November, Saturday 26, 1887

Very warm & pleasant day after a dull
& foggy early mf. Got to studio soon after
9 — carpenter there — not ready to go to
work — only wanted to know what I wanted
— I was exceeding wroth — I told him
that I had told his boss, Mc.Cornick, what
I wanted him to do — & more than anything
else I wanted him to get to work — then
he went away for his tools — I went to O'Neil &
ordered two more [illegible] from enlarged photo.
of Mr. [illegible] Rives — again to studio — waited until
nearly for carpenter — then to Schans's & bought
a doz. fine Bristol Boards — back & found that
the carpenter had been there & again gone away —
this time for nails — so I gave it up & went home at
11.45 — worked until 1 on Tracing of Water Cart.
S.A. drawing — lunch — To Studio at 2.15. Carpenter
there & at work — I stayed with him for more
than an hour — home — worked as long as I could
see on tracing — then gave up to a headache and
took a nap — Dinner at 6.30 — Well, Geo., Emily
& Valentine came in for awhile — then I marked
a lot of linen for Annie —

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 27, 1887

A very warm day — did not go out
at all — spent the whole morning
arranging books & fixing the book case
in Hi's story front room — Dinner at
2 — after that spent some time in
reading — then with the children —
Clare came in about 6 & stayed
awhile — something to eat at 7 —
Eve.[illegible] wrote notes to S. W. Marvin
Mr. Cramer concerning Otto Wolf's
painting — to Uncle Willie & to [illegible] —
after 10 went out & posted them —
about 11 when I got to bed.
