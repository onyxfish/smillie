## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 5, 1887.

Overcast day with occasional light showers from the South & East —
About 8 o'clk. quite a heavy shower
the rain falling for some time. I
was more or less used up all day
by a headache — Spent most of
the afternoon on the lounge — I
helped Herrick at times during
the day in putting up the long
mouldings on Studio ceiling.
Between 7 & 8 o'clk. went down town
— Evelf, read newspaper, and
to bed at usual time.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 6, 1887.

A very hot day with thunder
storms all about us but only one slight
shower fell on us in the evelf. I was
much under the influence of a
headache all day — with Herrick
much of the time directing him in
the finishing of Studio ceiling, on
parts not fully explained in the
drawings prepared by Walter Cook.
Dinner as usual — afternoon,
some fine cloud effects — put two
plates into camera & made my
first photo-exposures for the season.
Evelf, after 8, as we were sitting
on piazza — a thunder storm at some
distance to the N.W. [illegible] there was a
blinding flash & terrific crash of
thunder that nearly upset us, but there
was no rain & no storm followed. — The
rest of the evelf indoors reading — To
bed before 11.
