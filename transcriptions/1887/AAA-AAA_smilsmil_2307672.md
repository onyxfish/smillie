## Left Page

### April, Thursday 14, 1887

Bright m'f. but overcast later in the day. 11 o'clk. when I got to the studio. Spent what remained of the m'f. attending to letters &c that required attention — then went to N.A. & spent an hour looking at the Spring ex'btn. which to me is very interesting — It was near 2 when I got home to lunch & 3 when again I got to the studio where I mounted some etching plt. that I have orders for. Eve'f. it was almost 7 where Mary & her two children got in from O'k — Three dinner — After that I wrote a short letter to mother — Took it to G.C.D. on the way stopping at Murray Hill Hotel to enquire for Sam'l. Coleman — not in — Followed the Columbia boys in their night gowns up 5th Ave. to 50th S. — a parade celebrating Columbia's 100th anniversary — then to Union League club — Water-Color show — there from 9 to 10 — then home and chatted for half an hour with Mary & Annie — then to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 15, 1887

Bright m'f becoming a gradually fainter until after 2 when it began to rain — a heavy shower at 5 — To studio at 10 — again at work with etching plt. — Mary came in — At 12.45 went to Bank — then to White, Stokes & Allen — then to Knoedler's & selected some prints to send to Sue's child's nurse, Clara. Left etching plt. with Mr. Stevenson — 102 24. & another with Rt. Day. 56 E. 34 S. — home to lunch at 1.45 — to studio at 3 — wrote some notes & soon after 4 went to J. W. Wood's studio. 57 W. 10th S. — Water-Color Board meeting — back to studio before 6 — Home to dinner at 6.30 — chant with us. Eve'f. wrote a letter to Farrer about Poets & Etchers' business. To bed at 11.
