## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 29, 1887

Weather getting warmer — almost cloudless day. I felt pretty well but tired — Had many things to look after — Warner came at 10.30 & repainted a part of dining room ceiling and studio ceiling — At about 11 30 I went out riding in a buggy with Emily & Jim — It was so warm that we sauntered wherever we found shade — went out to Hart Lake & around by Summerville Road, getting home at 7. Tea — Revel on piazza in the moonlight until after 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 30, 1887

Weather much warmer — having little energy for work. Rode down town in the aft. with Parke & attended to messages. Hired a team of Prall & then had Herrick drive down to the D.L.& W. depot with Emily to catch the 12 52 train — after which he waited there until 7.31 P.m. when Nellie arr'd from NY, having left Ridgefield this mft. She got to the house a little before 9 P.m. in very good spirits spite of a very sore eye caused by the lodge[ment] of a cinder — after she had had some supper she sat on the piazza with us until after 10 —

Warner finished painting the dining room ceiling —
