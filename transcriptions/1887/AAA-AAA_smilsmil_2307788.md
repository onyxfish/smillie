## Left Page

### December, Friday 2, 1887.

Overcast with something enough like rain to wet the pavements about night-fal— To studio soon after 10 — carpenter & one colorman still there & did not get through until noon. I hung about, cleared out closets & did what I could to get things ready for Mrs. Callanau, the cleaning woman. Home to lunch abt — back at 2.30 by which time Mr. C. had got the closet floors cleaned — All the rest of the afternoon hard at work upon the mountain of dirt & confusion before me. Home at 5.30 — Dinner at 6.30 — Evetf. Read aloud from Dec. "Century" Lincoln's Inaugural address & its history — Spent a little time over Montrose Bank book that has just been sent to me from the Bk. To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 3. 1887.

Overcast with threats of rain — clear moonlight at night. To studio at 9.30 & spent the m'f trying to reduce the disorder & dirt — Mr. Callanau, with soap & water gave me little aid — I went home at 1 o'clk. very tired & had lunch — back at 2.30 — the rest of the afternoon I finished putting up big book case & dusted most of the books & got many of them into place — Took a rest in my big chair after 5 o'clk. Home at 6 — Dinner — Evetf. too tired to go down to Century meeting — instead, read aloud from Dec. Century Magazine & to bed before 11 —

Jim has quite an upset this m'f, scattering the contents of his stomach with lavish disregard of himself or surroundings, after which he evidently felt better.
