## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 19. 1887.

Dubious looking m'f that cleared into a delicious day with wonderfully fine sunset & young moon. — The Smiths painted balustrades the Paynes hauled manure to the new garden plot & Herrick and Kerlass hauled out the studio cistern. In the afternoon Herrick climbed one of the big maples east of the studio & cut out most of the top which is decayed & dying. I again worked in dark room pasting some heavy paper over the sides. Mrs. Azur [illegible] called & I escorted her to her door as I went down town before 6 o'clk. Eve'g, after reading news papers I drew a plans for etching room ceiling — To bed at the usual time.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 20. 1887.

Disagreeable day — cold, raw wind from S.E. — cloudy, threatening sky, but no rain — Hotchkiss came in the m'f & worked for about three hours with Herrick letting down a portion of piazza floor on North side of the house that was an inch or two too high. Coyle worked part of the m'f getting earth for closet — etc. — I was with the painters superintending the mixing of some colors — Dinner at the usual time. Battles came with two loads of excellent manure from Thomas which he spread upon the lawn in front of house. I was about with Herrick attending to little jobs — Went down town at 5.30 Went to Payne's to see why he did not come to-day to work. — Eve'g drew some plans & read aloud for a little while from Grant's Personal Memoirs. Rain at night.
