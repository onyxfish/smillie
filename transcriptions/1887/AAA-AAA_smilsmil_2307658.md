## Left Page

### March, Thursday 17, 1887

More raw, windy, dirty weather — To studio at 10 — worked on Iron Hill drawing until noon — then to White, Stokes & Allen & saw one of the partners who desired me to etch a plate for a new book but as they offered only $75 — it was very little of an inducement — Back to studio & finished mill drawing — Home to lunch at 1.45 — at 3 to Ortega's Gallery — Bdway near 14th St — & saw the Mrs. Bliss Baker pictures — To studio at 4 & worked on an Arms drawing for Uncle Willie's until 6 — then home — dinner — evening quietly by a comfortable fire.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 18, 1887

Partially overcast. Still cold and windy — worked all the morning on Arms drawing for Lucell [illegible] — Home to lunch at 1 & at 2 — with Annie went to Met. Opera House — Phil. rehearsal. The Mendelssohn Symphony very charming — but Rubinstein hung heavily — out at 4.20 — went to Gradie's & arranged to have some frames altered. Then several little messages that kept me till near 6 — To studio & then home. Eleg. Howe to dinner with us — Charl. came in for a short time — I wrote a letter home & took it to G.C. Depot — also one from Annie to Sue — telling her that if agreeable to her we would start for Cambridge next Sat. Then back & took Eleg. to her quarters — 108 Mad. Ave — To bed about 11.
