## Left Page

### May, Saturday 14, 1887

Still charming weather — Got to studio's about 10 — traced sketch of Liberty Statue & in the afternoon retraced on to drawing paper. McCormick put a carpenter under my instructions to put up closets in New Studio — Spent some time with him — went to Grand Hotel to see Aunt Huldah, just am. from the South & on her way to P'ok — Did not see her, went to Putnams & got size of page of Durand mem'. Home at 1.30 — back at 2.45. Charl came much depressed by news from Mother — he is suffering from overwork. At 5 went to N.A. of D. Saw Miss Hallowell there. Then to Huntington's not in but made three small sketches from Durand portraits — to give idea of proportions — then again to N.A. & saw Mr Huntington there — met Prof. Comfort — Home at 6.30 — dinner — every Charl came in — still nervous & desponded — wants me to go to P'ok with him to-morrow — He left us at 10.30 — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 15, 1887

Yet another delightful day — B'kf't about 9 — At 11 Annie, Jim & I went to Aunt Anna's — saw her, Emma & Mary J — there until 12. Then to N.A. of D. & took a last look at the paintings — the [illegible] closed last night — Home at 1.30 — At 2 Eleg't Home came & we had dinner — after that Bella came & took Jim away to the Park with her. I wrote my letter to Mother. Before 5 I escorted Eleg't to 108 Mad. Ave, then to Mother Cook's & met Annie. Home about 6 — At 8 went to G.C. Depot to meet Charl — waited until 8.30 — then home — There found Geo. Had some supper — At 9.30 Charl came in, his train having been belated — He left about 10 and I finished letter to Mother & took it to G.C.D. Charl reports Ben as weaker & suffering from fits of nervous ness, glad to see Aunt H. & satisfied with Miss Jordan as a nurse. To bed after 11.
