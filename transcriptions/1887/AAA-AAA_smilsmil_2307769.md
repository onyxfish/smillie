## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 25, 1887

Ther. last night 29°

A sullen, disagreeable day with a threat in the air as of snow — Payne &
son finished digging holes in the m'f, then
commenced to transplant trees under my
direction — while at work Ambrose accidentally
struck his father who fell to cursing him most
violently. As he did not soon tire I told him
that as the blow was accidental he had cursed
enough — then he was insolent to me & I told
him to leave the place — He did not come in
the afternoon but Ambrose did & I had Battles
help him — B— had got 15 blk berry roots from
G. Fordham & set them out — with A. he transplanted a large Spruce & an Ash. McSmith
painted some board blinds & some sashes —
Herrick worked on Wash room ceiling & on
boxes to set in the ground for clothes line posts
to set in. Besides overseeing the men I
re-sized the canvas stretched yesterday —
Went down town at 5 — Ev'f. newspaper.
chores — & a game of cards with Annie —
No. 2's vaccination has at last taken — an
eruption has broken out from head to foot.
Brilliant moonlight as I went
to bed shortly before 11.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 26, 1887

Ther. last night 20°

Brilliant m'f — very cold — 29° when
I got up. Clouded at midday and
again clear at ev'f. Battles & Ambrose
Payne at work — Transplanted 3 more of
the five large Spruces — then the case
of fruit trees from Little came & we
hurried in planting them — Herkes came
with iron railing for well stair curb & stone
& man drilled holes to set it — At 12
I went down town with Jim & to the
Darkey valley trying to find some one
to dig for me. Home at 1 — dinner —
after that sized two more stretchers of
canvas for oak bed room ceiling — then
out & worked with the men until near
6\. Got out 11 of the fruit trees & 3 doz-
eny bushes — Herrick finished the
chestnut sockets for clothes line posts —
Down town soon after 6 — then tea —
Rec'd a long letter of Charl's from Po'k —
sent to Mary — written from Melrose —
Durham & London — Ev'f. Reading —
To bed about 10.30 — very tired —
Quite a fine in town last night — or
rather, at 2 o'clk. this m'f.
