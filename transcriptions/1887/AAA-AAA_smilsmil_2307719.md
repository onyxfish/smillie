## Left Page

### July, Sunday 17, 1887

A close hot day — a heavy shower of more than an hours duration fell commencing about 9 A.m. another, heavier & shorter fell soon after 2. I did not get up until late — B.P. about 10 — After the rain ceased I spent some time weeding. After 1.30 I took a short nap & dinner at 2.30 — In the afternoon I wrote a letter to Ehninger Eve'g about 8 o'clk. a heavy thunderstorm came up with quite a gale of wind — the rain continued until 9.30. I wrote notes to Mr. Saml. Colman & to Miss A.B. Abbatt. Storms continued about until 11 o'clk. when I went to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 18, 1887

A very heavy thunderstorm at 4 A.m. with much wind & rain. The day was cloudy, windy & much cooler, altho' muggy. I spent much time about the place. fixing, running vines, etc. Warner painted studio walls, second coat. At 6 P.m. I went downtown — got mail — then to Parke's & saw Mr. P. to get address of Scranton greengrocer — currants for jelly — After when I got home — sat with Annie until after 10 — then to bed. Got a note from Charl. announcing that he expects to sail for Europe on the Umbria — Aug. 6th.
