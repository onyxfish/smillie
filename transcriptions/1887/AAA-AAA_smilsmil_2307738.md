## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 24. 1887.

Again more rain during the night & several heavy showers during the day. Binninger, the lightening-rod man came with his assistant and by working hard got up his rod over house & studio before night, when he got his $200 pay. Hotchkiss worked all day — so did Judson — O'Brien came at 9.30 & stayed the rest of the day. Thomas came at 9 but left in the afternoon — Payne worked in the afternoon & Battles worked after 2 o'clk. The two Smith's painted all day. Coyle pottered about all day. Herrick was sick & came to milk me & evef. I wandered about all day watching the men — in the afternoon Mr. Woodin came & wandered about with me for some time — Evef. I wrote a note to Emma Bourne in answer to hers of 15th inst, & read newspapers. Annie read aloud an article in Sep. Harper — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 25. 1887.

Breezy & cold — very cloudy in the mtf — gradually clearing until evef when it was cloudless. Both Thomas & O'Brien & teams at work — also, as helpers, Payne, Judson, his friend (a new hand) & Battles — Ploughing commenced in earnest. Hotchkiss commenced wall around garden plot. Herrick at work on the foundation at S.W. cor. Piazza — sagged down — found that the cistern has a bearing upon the sill. Coyle at work all day — also the two Smiths — painters. I spent the mtf with the men — went down town about noon — Dinner at 1 — at 3.30 went out with Kate, Annie & Jim riding — down South Road, past D. Norris's & home by the back road — The atmosphere was perfection — the distance very lovely & the ride much enjoyed — Back at 6 — tea before 7. Evef. with newspapers & a little writing in Cherry Reception Room which proves to be a very cosy & comfortable one.
