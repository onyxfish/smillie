## Left Page

### July, Saturday 9, 1887

Payne came up & took in our hay, just
in time, for a thunder shower came
up before noon which brought a change
of weather — it remained overcast &
other showers followed — much needed
but not enough. Went down town in
the m'f, & telegraphed the news to
various members of the families —
Annie did not sleep well last night
but nevertheless is in very good con-
dition. Herrick got all the lumber
out of studio's & under cover out by
fairground wall — He got to work
upon the side walls of studio's, stretching
the canvas. I wrote notes to
several different ones. Even'g,
after sitting awhile with Annie
I got to bed at about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 10, 1887

overcast with strong west wind
with some light, flying showers and
growing quite cool — the Ther. falling
below 60° — had a fire in Annie's room
& one in large room in the even'g. Up
late — after when we had bk'fs. A
good part of the day I spent in
writing letters to different members
of the two families & some friends
whose names Annie had given
to me. About 1 o'clk. went down
town — for a walk — called on the
Parke's — Dinner at 2. more
writing — Tea at 7 — finished
a letter to Fanny Kimball & took
it to P.O. about 9 o'clk — To bed
soon after 10.
