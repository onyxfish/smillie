## Left Page

### October, Saturday 1, 1887

overcast with with some showers about 11 after which the weather cleared. The afternoon being very charming — brilliant moonlight night. Work progressed on the big chimney & it was got up to within about a dozen courses of the top. The Smiths painted & Herrick podged about. I spent some time carving the letters on Chamy Mantit but did not finish it — much of my time in the afternoon was spent on the chimney top — Annie, Eliz't & Chat Collins went off for a walk. Went down town before 6 — Eve'g reading & cards — also walking on the piazza.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 2, 1887

A very wet day — mild & April-like — a series of showers with struggling sunshine between — at night a very humid moonlight. A late slip — in the m't wrote a letter to Charl — dinner at 2 — some reading & some exercise on piazza — wrote to Little, the Rochester nursery man, & ordered fruit trees for our orchard. Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. Reading and chatting & piazza strolling.
