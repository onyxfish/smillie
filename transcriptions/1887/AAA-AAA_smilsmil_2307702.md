## Left Page

### June, Monday 13, 1887

Fine day, altho' at times overcast
& threatening a change of weather.
Went down town about 10 A.m. &
attended to some small matters.
For the rest of the day hard at
work setting out the shrubs
rec'd from Little of Rochester —
Had Coyle, Jackson & Henrick at
work digging holes, 105 plants &
small trees being in the box.
By 6 o'clk. about 50 holes were
dug & 50 plants were set out — I
was exceedingly tired. While
at tea Father a telegram came
from Will about But. Noll' drawings —
For a moment I was alarmed, think-
ing it was from Po'K. Before 8 I
went down town — on my return
read news papers & to bed
soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 14, 1887

Another delightful day with beautiful
clouds — Got to work at transplant-
ing soon after 8&b' & stuck to it
until 12 — then down town —
dinner abt. — Again at work with
shrubs, Henrick & Jackson helping
& did not give up until 7 o'clk
when I got nearly all but the ever-
greens into the ground — Tea
at 7.30 — To P.O. at 8. Suet —
Read news papers until 10 — then
to bed.
