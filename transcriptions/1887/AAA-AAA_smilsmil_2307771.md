## Left Page

### October, Saturday 29, 1887

Ther. last night 32°

Heavy fog — everything dripping wet all
the m't. At times the sun made an
attempt to shine — in the afternoon at
times he succeeded, but it was not a
pleasant day — The briethes could not work
or any good work outside — They mixed up
a lot of odds & ends of old paint & painted
Heinck's shop — then some work about the house.
Mr. Layne worked until 11 mulching plants
& trees & cleaning up. Battles brought two
loads of manure & 1 of mulching — at
11 we went out & got a fine large soft
maple & set it at the N. E. cor. of the
house — After dinner we got a hard maple
& set it in the lane — then another on the
vault — & it was 5 o'clk. Did not go
down town to-day at all — Well
with newspaper — & then a game
of cards with Annie — To bed
about 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 30, 1887

Ther. last night 24°

Dismal, cold & bleak — a sleet storm
last night covered everything with ice.
All day the sky was leaden with a
look & feeling in the air of snow — At
night it cleared quiet & cloudless. I
got fires in studio furnace & in the
etching room & spent the day in
putting things in order & preparing
to leaving for the winter. Dinner at
2 — a short visit — there again at
it as long as I could see — Well
took things easy — wrote a letter
in the m't to Charle — Packed my
acids etc. in etching room after 9
P.m — Superb moonlight nights.
To bed soon after 10.
