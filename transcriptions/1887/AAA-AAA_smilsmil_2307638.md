## Left Page

### February, Thursday 3, 1887

Very dark & dismal with fog
& rain — To studio soon after
10 — Got out canvas x that
I painted while in Stretrat —
"The Maaunporte, at low tide"
& tried repainting it — Home
to lunch at 1.45 — after it,
so dark that I did not return to studio but instead,
deliberately took a nap, it
being too dark to see to work.
At 5 I rid to studio & cleaned
up things — Home to dinner at 6.
At 8 Annie & I went to Met.
Opera Ho. & heard "Fidelio" given
very well by a German Company —
After it a ballet, called "Selections
from Vienna Waltzes" — a poor thing
to my mind — The house was
crowded — out at 11.30 & walked
home by pale moonlight thro'
snow & slush — To bed at 12.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 4, 1887

Bright cold day — To studio at
10.15 & spent the [illegible] painting
over portions of sky & sea on 11½
x 24½ — "Study — Marblehead Neck"
and sky & sea on "Near Crownin-
shield's Point" — Home at 1.30
back at 3 & painted along as I
could sea on "Maaunporte" — At
5 went to [illegible] Art Ass'n then to
N.A.D. then to studio — cleaned
palette & home at 6.20 — dinner
every. Reading. Could not
get up spunk enough to go to the
Academy of Music to hear & see the
Careyco School Indians — To bed
about 10.30.

x Tickets given by Champney.
