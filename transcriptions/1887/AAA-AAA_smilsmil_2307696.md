## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 1. 1887.

Heavy Rain until about 9 A.M. —
after which it gradually cleared.
Also, to Studio's — Gurney's men there —
most of the men used in moving
stove. Heavy case, books & book case.
I went to bank. To Adams & [illegible] —
& Johnston's — then to Station 7 —
P.O., on my way home had my
hair cut. To lunch at 1.45 —
in the afternoon got things to-
gether & packed bag case —
Dinner before 7 — then wrote
a letter to mother. packed
my trunk — Chase came in
about 10 o'clk we walked up
to depot & mailed letter —
home, finished packing &
to bed about 11.30.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 2. 1887.

*To Montrose*

A very thick, muggy day — up at
about 6 — blkp. at 7 — got everything
nicely ready for our start — at 8 the
carriage came — but no Express man
— Drove down to Gurney's — said he
didn't know what train I wanted
to catch — didn't think he could catch
the 9 o'clk train — said he must — and
he did — but I got a good deal worked
up — got away from the house at 8.15 —
leaving Mother Cook & Jno. Valentine
to lock up — to Chins. S: ferry at 8.45 —
& after all our fuming got away nicely
Mary Ireland with us — the muggy
weather changed as we went on — heavy
showers beyond Water Gap — clearing
beautifully before we got to Scranton
— Reached M. depot at 2.52 — Will Parke
there with his carriage & took us up
to town very comfortably — in spite
of very muddy roads — got to house at
5 — met by Herrick — found everything
looking very lovely — tea about 7
enjoyed a wood fire until near 10 —
then to bed — all being pretty
well tired.
