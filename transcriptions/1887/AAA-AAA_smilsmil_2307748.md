## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 13, 1887

A dismal day of rain & fog and
east wind — my lumbago gradually
improved, but I could get around
only with difficulty — I commenced
to outline on gelatine to transfer,
Huntington's head of "The Goldsmith's
Daughter". The Smiths painted
under cover of the piazzas, but
no one else, except Herrick, worked.
Eve'g, after reading newspapers,
Annie & I chatted until nearly
10.30 — then to bed — Dense fog
through which seemed to play
sheet lightening —

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 14, 1887

The day opened dismally with fog
but before noon it cleared muggy &
very warm — my lumbago had
limbered a little more so that I
got about without much discomfort.
In the m'f I worked awhile on
gelatine tracing of "The Goldsmith's
Daughter" & spent some time with
Smith in mixing colors — The two
Smiths painted all day — And "
Payne worked at digging in the
afternoon. O'Brien came up in the
afternoon & hauled small stone for
little, indirectly working for my benefit
altho' he got no pay from me.
Eve'g with news paper —
to bed soon after 10.
