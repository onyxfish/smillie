## Left Page

### May, Thursday 12, 1887

Another hot day — To Dr Allen's at 10 & had a little fitting done. Then at 10.45, to 2° Nat. Bnk. Hired a boy in Safe Deposit vault — To studio soon after 11. W. Geo. Kelley — Editor etc. of New Am Art Review called — bought out of door Etching "A Woodland Pool" & paid me $50.00 for it — Home to lunch at 1.45 — back at 2.45 & worked on sketch. Liberty Statue — for H.B.N.Co. Miss Ticknor of Boston called — Home at 6.30 — Ed Cook & his two eldest dined with us, the mother & other children now visiting in Ridgefield — Swett Clark came in — no good news from Mother — I wrote to Herrick accepting his terms $420 for the year — To bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 13, 1887

Delightful day — at 9 o'clk. at Dr. Allen's — 9.15 m. to have some fitting done — Then to studio — made paste & mounted or stretched drawing paper. W. Kelley came with some p.p. by Shave from my plate "A Woodland Land" — I worked on Liberty Statue sketch — An offer was sent over from M.A.f.S. — of $325 for my 7 400 "Meadow Pond" — I accepted it — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.45 & finished Liberty Sketch — about 5 went to W. Huntington's & saw centering portrait of Durand by W.H. — painted in '57 & to be etched as an illustration at Huntington's monograph on Durand. Then in to Dr Blackwell's & talked with him about his confinement & my want of faith in Montrose Doctors — Then to Arnold's & Constable's & bought Curtain Rods for Montrose — Home at 6.30 — Edwd — Catherine & Carlton again to dinner — also Juliette Ireland from Bridgeport who stayed with us over night — Aunt Huldah reached the city to-day on her way to Po'k from Georgia — swell. Reading — & to bed early.
