## Left Page

### November, Friday 4, 1887.

*Went to New York*

Ther. 42°. last night.

Up at 6.30 - bkfst - about 7.30 Kate
mf[?]. I packed my trunk - soon after
8 Battles came with Thomas's team and
our trunks & cases were piled upon
the wagon - 6 trunks & 6 cases - They
started for the depot soon after 9 -
I then went down town - settled
some matters & back at 10.30 - The
barouche came for us at 11 - in ten
minutes we were away - There had
been a sudden change in the weather
- the sky was overcast & a strong wind
blew from the S.W. - but we got to depot
very comfortably & nearly half an hour
before the Train - no seats for us in
the parlor car altho' I had telegraphed
the day before - seats were plenty after
leaving Scranton - we got in to Hoboken
on time - 6.47 - found Poole's carriage
waiting - home before 8 - Kate Ready
for us - soon had tea - Evelf. Geo. Coats[?]
came in - Then Charles Smillie - he stayed
until 9.20. It was near 11 when I got
to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 5, 1887.

Bkfst about 8 - a very pleasant
day - after unpacking at about
10 o'clk. I went to studio - Spent
some time with Geo. - & Calverey - &
in my own studio, which is a most
disheartening looking pile of dirt &
disorder. Home to dinner at —
after that, felt so entirely used up
that I went to bed & had a rest
& some sleep. Evelf. I did
not feel energetic enough to
go to Century meeting, so, stayed
at home & tried to arrange things
somewhat in my bed room - To bed
about 10.
