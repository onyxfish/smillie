## Left Page

### October, Sunday 23, 1887

Ther. went down to 25°. Last night.
A bleak & novemberish day — heavily
overcast all the mtf., breaking away
in the afternoon — fine sunset — after which
a roaring wind — Spent the morning
writing letters — between 1 & 2 out on
the piazza for a sun with Jim —
Dinner at 2 — after that, Read
until 4 — Then went out & let
the cow graze on the fine grass
on the new ground near the lane
while I acted for an hour or more,
as cowherd. until Herrick came
to milk — Evg. Annie Read
aloud — as usual it was about
11 o'clk. before I got to bed —
High wind —

## Right Page

### October, Monday 24, 1887

Last night was comparatively mild
the ther. going to 39°. The mtf. promised
a fine day but before 9 o'clk. the wind
suddenly shifted to N. f W. blowing hard
& cold, while a bank of clouds came up
making the day very disagreeable. The
painters painted on the window sashes a
part of the mtf. but at last beat a retreat
& painted wooden shutters in the shop.
The Paynes dug holes for fruit trees
in the mtf. & hauled a load of leaf-
mould in the afternoon & also hauled
away the stone dug out in making holes
for trees — Herrick stretched canvas
for oak bed room ceiling & I made
sizing & sized it — I went down town
with Jim about 11 — & worked about
all the afternoon — down town between
5 & 6. Evg. newspaper & attending
to my cares about the house — To bed
at 10.30.
