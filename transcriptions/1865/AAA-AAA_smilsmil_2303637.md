## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 23, 1865

Cold, windy & disagreeable day —
A bit of Nov. weather astray —
Spent the morning doing labor
out of doors — with Pick & Crowbar —
P.M. Painted on "Cascade, a
Recollection of the Catskills" 6½×9.

Ther. at 7 o'clk. P.M. 56°.

### Thursday 24.

Glorious day, but too cool
to work out of doors. Painted
on "Cascade", making but
little progress, as my light
& accommodations generally
are of a very inferior order.
Mary & Matt spent the day &
night with us — In the evg. we
all went up to Mr. Beachie's
& had a pleasant time — and
two young ladies from Brooklyn.
Mr. B. is laid up with chills
& fever —

## Right Page

### August, Friday 25, 1865

Another great day. Myself finished
Study No. 6 — Think it the best
of my studies so far — It shows
less of labor, more of knowledge
it is I think, generally truer than
the other five — The girls walked
to town this mg — P.M. worked
on study No. 7 — painting in the
east of west bank of river, & the
water — Wind interfered.

### Saturday 26.

Still another fine day — Myself
finished Study No. 2, "Redeeming
it" I think in great measure. I
think from its terrible greenness
& hardness — But it is still very
green & very hard — But for the little
glimpse of distance, which pleases
me — I would not have had courage
to work on it so long. P.M. —
worked on No. 7. Finished nothing —
foreground & painted again on the
water — The wind interfered seriously.
Later, worked on No. 8. "Maple & Beach" —
