## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 20, 1865

went back again to work on
"From Under Sunset Rock". Painted
a little on sky then took hold
of "Fall on Westbrook" (9th day).
Jas. Hart worked a little upon
it, with great advantage, adding
more color + broader light —
Snedecor took my two 6×10's "Near
Montrose" + "Twilight" — allowing me $10
on my bill.

### Wednesday 21.

Again at work upon "From Under
Sunset Rock" (5th day). These hot
days now but little work is done.
My Physical energy is all gone
+ my moral courage is nearly
used up — Did but little —
dragging about on middle distance
+ foreground — Spent the Evng.
at Martens (111 W. 21st) + at Annie
Ballard's where Sister Mary is
now stopping.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 22, 1865

Fearfully hot in the mrg.
Heavy thunder Storm + cool in
the P.M. Worked on "From Under
[Sunset Rock]" (6th day). Painted the foreground
all over — I think it looks tolerably
well, but it is very sketchy.
[Illegible] called on the Kimballs —
Saw Mr. + Mrs. K — yng folks out.

### Friday 23.

Some more hot. Very little work done,
that was on "Westbrook" (10th day).
Fussing over it generally — Kate
Fessenden + friend called — Many
[illegible] to [illegible] keep an eye on the afternoon
work. Sister Kate came down in the
mrg. Dr. Dunning called at the
Studio to see his painting "From Sunset Rock".
It is disheartening to me to know
that he is not favorably impressed
with it. During the night, had
a tremendous Colic. Dr. Throop was
with me from 1.30 until 3 o'clk A.M.
