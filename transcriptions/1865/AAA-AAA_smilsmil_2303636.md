## Left Page

### August, Saturday 19, 1865

Fog until nearly 10 o'clk —
Fighting all the mrng with head-
ache — Accomplished nothing.
Warm & Cloudless p.m. Worked
on Study No. 7 — painting in the
opposite side of the River —
Success very moderate, but the
work fascinating. Evng,
heavy thunderstorms all about,
which we got a slight touch.

### Sunday 20.

Very hot mrng, walked into town then
to the Methodist Ch. & heard Alfred [illegible]
Cookman of Phila. was hardly paid.
Afternoon writing —
Evng, Thunder showers.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 21, 1865

Took breakfast with John
Roosevelt — Quite a pleasant
bachelor affair — Mrng very warm,
worked on Study No. 6. At noon
& started off with Parker to meet
Mary, Matt & Miss Parker to visit West
Point & see evening parade — P.M.
Could n't keep up umbrella etc. on
account of wind, to work on No. 7 — So
continued on No. 6 — a quieter place.

### Tuesday 22.

Cool mrng, threatening Rain, which
came down steadily in the afternoon.
Painted over picture, (10½ × 18 in)
for Mr. Tracy Harris. Think
that I have done pretty well
on color — covered the entire
canvas — Paid Uncle N. to-
day $32.00 Board up to last
Sat. night — Also $22.57
interest money due him.
