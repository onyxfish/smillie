## Left Page

### Nov. Friday 3, 1865

overcast, but mild. At the studio until 11.30 — Mr. Mulvaney scrubbing &
washing. Went down town, got Henry
Volkening's contract to buy 4 lots in 55th st.
all signed & $300.00 to bind — paid $175.00
back interest — gave $75.00 to Father &
kept the Remaining $50.00. Saw American
Bank Note Co folks & Continentals, both
wishing me to draw or engrave for them.
Promised nothing definitely — Eve'g
at home.

### Saturday 4.

Heavy Rain all day — went
to Phil. Rehearsal in the mg.
Meeting — Miss Dunning — the Irelands
etc. etc. Sister Mary went with me.
P.M. most of it, spent at the Studio.
Eve'g went with Mary to the Phil.
Concert.

## Right Page

### Nov. Sunday 5, 1865

Cold, windy day. Very much
troubled with my left ear, which
appears to be quite closed with
wax. Worked away at it, making
it no better, — think that I have
managed to spread some of the wax
upon the drum of the ear — Did
not go to church — Late in the
afternoon went to see Dr. Bartlett.
He advises an original application
of sweet oil & cotton.

### Monday 6.

Clear, cold, windy day — All day
watching the man color the
Studio ceiling, & clearing up
his muss after him.
Spent the evening pleasantly
at Mr. Ireland's, No. 50 W. 25th —
with the "parents" Mr. P. Spring,
Miss Durand & others, Fred. Miss
Emma I — her bro. Oscar & Miss
Dunning who is visiting there for
a week.
