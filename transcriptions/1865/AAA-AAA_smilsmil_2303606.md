## Left Page

### April, Friday 21, 1865

Another miserable, rainy &
cold day — Finished (10½ days)
"From the top of Buttermilk Ravine"
14×20 in — I think that I have
a pretty good general tone — that
the foreground is painted with more
understanding than anything else
that I have done — but the middle
distance is overworked — Commenced
"Charcoaling" from Sunset Rock"

### Saturday 22.

Disagreeable day — Have spent
the whole of it trying to make
something of a little "Curfew Bell"
sketch — painted 3 or 4 yrs. ago.
— more of a task than I had
expected — don't like to work
over old things.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 23, 1865

Cold & blustery day —
went to Dr. Jos. P. Thompson's
in the m[orning] & heard him preach
a discourse that he had prepared
for last Sunday m[orning] on the com-
pletion of the 20th Year of his pastorate
of the Bdway Tabernacle —
In eve[ning] stayed at home.

### Monday 24.

Painted all day (5½ days) &
I hope have finished "on the
Hudson, above Poughkeepsie"
10½ × 14½ in. Have painted
quite over all the foreground objects,
what it needs most now, I think
is a little glazing here & there
to bring it more together and some
touching on high lights. Evening
mounted two of Breevoort's oil
sketches for him — Hy Foster
& several others were at the
house.
