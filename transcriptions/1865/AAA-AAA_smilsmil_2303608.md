## Left Page

### April, Saturday 29, 1865

Showery and gale of wind in the afternoon — Attended
Phil. Rehearsal in the m'g.
afternoon, worked a little
on Ferry drawing for A.B.N. Co.
In the evening it rained like
fun, with some thunder & lightening —
went to the last Phil. Concert of the
Season — Mr. & Mrs. Wykoff came
to the house to take up their abode
for the coming year.

### Sunday 30.

Glorious morning — evening cloudy.
In the ev'g heard J. P. Thompson
on "The Life of Abr. Lincoln, and
its Lessons for our future Policy."
The building was crowded to
overflowing — Dr. T. spoke until
10 o'elk & kept his congregation.
This m'g Thos. Gillelan Sr. died
he has been sick five or six
weeks, suffering from a variety
of troubles not properly understood
by his physicians.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 1, 1865

Miserable Rainy day —
Drew on Ferry drawing for
A.B.N. Co. Spent the [illegible]
at home — This is our first
day without a home, knocking
about loose — We have picked
up our meals — but the commence-
ment is not encouraging, I must
admit —

### Tuesday 2.

Delightful day — Finished
drawing Ferry (2½ days)
Our feeding arrangements
slightly improved upon yesterday.
Got a tolerable dinner for
75 cents — In the evening,
went to the Ireland's to bid
Mr. & Mrs. Preston B. Storing
good-bye — They sail on
the "Persia" to-morrow, for a
3 months trip on the "other
side."
