## Left Page

### August, TUESDAY 15, 1865

Glorious day. all the m'g on
Study No. 6. Finish painting.
Effect very gray — no shadow
& little light in which to paint.
P.M. worked on Study No. 5
but got only the Evergreen
trees on left hand painted in.
At night at about 2 o'clk to-
morrow m'g Uncle W. & Dave
arrived direct from Canada.

### WEDNESDAY 16.

Slight clouding — M'g worked
on Study No. 6 getting it all
in but one Tree trunk. Have
had less trouble with it so far
than any other study, & I think
it far the best. P.M. worked
on Study No. 7 (12×18) painting
in sky — Splendid Cumuli
— Thunder Storms passed
over between 6 and 8 o'clk

## Right Page

### August, THURSDAY 17, 1865

Cloudy, cold & windy. In the m'g
rowed Mr. Beach's boat up to his
place & visited Mr. B. & daughter,
then Geo. & I sauntered over the grounds
until noon. P.M. worked with
the men hauling gravel over the
walks. Sverg drove Dave, Uncle
W. & Father into town. Uncle W. &
D. went down to N.Y. We called on
many — Matt — there met Geo. who had
walked in. Ret. bet. 8 & 9 o'clk.

### FRIDAY 18.

Still too cool & breezy to paint
out of doors. So G. & I took our
pencils & paper & started up
the Hyde Park Road. Got an
outline view from the old Clew
place looking south & finished
a little pencil study commenced
some days ago. P.M. Painted
over the grays on Study No. 3.
Question whether his attended,
to paint grass, under strong sun
light — I find, in one way, at
least, no light undertaking.
