## Left Page

### February, Monday 20, 1865

Down town with Continental Bank Note Co. finishing [illegible]'s die Locomotive (small). Rumors of the fall of Charlestown. S.C.
In Ev'g went to see Edwin Booth as Hamlet. Was pleased, as far perhaps as I can be pleased by a Theatrical Representation. Booth was artistic, so was the Scenery — the Rest — bad.
Got my money from Leeds. $100.

### Tuesday 21.

Great rejoicing over the fall of Charleston. A glorious bright day. Down town with Father all day "biting in" his "Rocky Mts" — great success. Commenced at 8.30. Stopped at 6 P.m. Acid on 10 or 12 times. 5 parts Acetic to 1 Nitric. In Ev'g Read at Mr. Dunning's — 1st 3 acts of Hamlet. Pleasant Ev'g.
Got money from Thurston $259.94.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 22, 1865

Painted (10th day) on Haines. 3d sky — better — A Mr. Kinney promises me $250 — for it (Frame & all) if I finish it to please him. Another fine day. Every body jubilant, + Washington's Birth day celebrated almost like an old fashioned 4th of July.

Meyer took his 12th lesson.

### Thursday 23.

Warm + Rainy. Painted (11th day) on Haines. Muddling all day on overhanging branches (f/g) + middle distance Trees.
Cannot get the color that is neutral + yet full of color — Tendency either to get color too positive all over, or else run to the other extreme of monotone greys.
Miss Dunning called.
