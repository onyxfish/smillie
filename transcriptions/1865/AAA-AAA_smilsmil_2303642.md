## Left Page

### Sept. Tuesday 12, 1865

Has taken all day to clear
off — Myf outlined a subject
6¾×9. (At Hyde Park, on the Hudson)
also primed an old canvas
12×18 for a new painting. P.M.
drove Aunts A, M, & J. into town &
out to Vassar College. Called
upon Mother, Matt & Kate.
Evng beautifully clear.

### Wednesday 13.

Myf cloudy &muggy — P.M.
Runny & hot. Did little or
nothing but fight with a head-
ache. Pat 4 o'clk Geo. walked in
to Po. to start by boat for N.Y. tonight.
At about 5. Will. & wife, Clare & wall.
Brained arrived from Po. took tea.
At 7 started to Return. — Horses
fractious — women & children frightened.
Martin drove Will's team & I drove
Uncle W's, taking in Aunts Mary & Jessie
& Geo's trunk. Met him at boat —
Uncle W. arrd from N.Y. My head-
ache was so violent, I stayed at Mrs. Parker's
all night.

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday 14, 1865

Delightful day — breezy & June-like
at about 10 o'clk A.M. Will, Clare
& I drove out to Uncle W's —
P.m. made pencil sketch of
apple trees on trellis walk.

### Friday 15.

Glorious day — Myf pencilled
under large drooping apple tree.
P.m. painted on Study No 7.
Charl. arrd from N.Y. this myf.
Came up to Po. on Mary Powell last
night. George probably started
for Montrose this myf. Got
word that Snedecor has sold my
Buttermilk Ravine, frame & all for
$180.00. A Mrs. Earle, aunt
of Aunt Agnes' arrd this p.m.
