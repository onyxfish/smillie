## Left Page

### August, Thursday 3, 1865

Mnf intensely hot — worked on
Study No. 2 "over the River" — on
Evergreens &c on left hand side —
P.M. Rain, with thunder, worked
indoors, a little on No. 2, & painted
a sky on No. 3. — Even'g
Earle arr'd     Rec'd a letter
from Mr. Mayer. Enclosing a
check for $75.00 for teaching
his son Lloyd — Letter very
kind.

### Friday 4

Hot & cloudless — My worked on
2d Study — very slowly but with tolerable
satisfaction — p.m. on 5th Study —
foreground, vines etc — At about
7 o'clk P.M. started with the horses
the Bournes & Martha Earle to visit Vassar
College. Thunder Storm threatened us —
— when within ½ mile of College, turned
about — got horses nicely under shed and
girls safely into Mr. Parkers, when down
came the Rain — waited until 9.45
Reached home 10.20 — all safe and
dry —

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 5, 1865

Very moist stifling sort of a day — My
watching plows — cows & things — P.M.
drawing on block "Ploughman" —
Rained from 4 to 6 o'clk — At tea
time (5) Father was taken sick
and for an hour or more had a
very severe time — Bilious.

### Sunday 6

Drove into town with Ella & Emma B.
Martha Earle — Met Mary Matt in
mile St. We went to the Baptist Cha
heard Dr. Babcock. on Coming out of
church, caught in the Rain, waited for
half an hour at the Northern House — all
serene — another heavy storm in the
p.m. — another in the even'g — Father
better, but still complaining.
