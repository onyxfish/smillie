## Left Page

### June, SUNDAY 4, 1865

Excessively warm. Ther.
85° in my room. Morning hard
Thompson — P.M. wrote to Father
& to Mary — Eve'g went to D.T.
(Barway Tabernacle) & heard discussion
between Dr. Vinton & Bermilyea
on the possibilities & advantages
of an organized Christian Union.
Dr Vinton was extremely logical &
concise & Dr. T. followed splendidly,
both opposing any effort at such organ-
ization.

### MONDAY 5.

Hotter & more hot — Ther. 88°
in the studio — Day almost
wasted — incapable of work,
what I did was upon die
of Ferry Boat. (4 ii day)
Rain & wind in the afternoon
& tolerably cool evening —
Spent the eve'g at home
clearing up house.

## Right Page

### June, TUESDAY 6, 1865

Great change in weather,
Ther. 72° in Studio — 16° less than
yesterday — Relit on Ferry boat
worked with graver — (5 ii day)
It is an intolerable bore for
me to do such work, & I wonder
at myself for consenting —
Mother arrived from Po'keepsie
on a visit for a few days.

### WEDNESDAY 7.

Got up with a miserable
headache — Went to Studio —
set my palette & fought along
until noon, when I gave up
the ship — Returned home
& went to bed —
