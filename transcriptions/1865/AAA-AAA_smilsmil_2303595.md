## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 8, 1865

Blue day, after Reception. Felt very tired. So little work was done. Charcoaled &
rubbed in "View from top of Buttermilk Ravine - Catskill Clove" 14×20 — Commenced Raining about 5 o'clk P.M. George Meyer took his 14th lesson —
In evng. Dr. Weston & wife to tea. Mr & Mrs Nykoff. & Dorman called.

### Thursday 9.

Dark & rainy all day — Still feel tired. Painted in sky & distance middle distance of Buttermilk Ravine. Tolerably successful only — Mr Nykoff called at the house to-day & agreed to take it for one year at $1700 — Geo. & self to Retain two Rooms. to lodge only.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 10, 1865

Dull in the mrg, cleared up in the p.m. Painted (3rd day) on Buttermilk. Got it all painted in except branches that come down over left hand corner of sky. Think it a pretty good start.
Vedder called at Studio.

### Saturday 11.

In the mrg went to Phil. Rehearsal — Painted in the afternoon on Buttermilk finishing the laying in. 3½ days. In the Evening went to Philharmonic Concert, taking J.C. F —
But little interested.

George Meyer took his 15th lesson
