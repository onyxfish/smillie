## Left Page

### August, THURSDAY 31, 1865

Cloudless — hazy & very windy — After rain attempts to
keep my umbrella in the ground — gave it up — spent
Rest of m'g painting up sky.
P.M. on Study No. 3.
It is something almost wonderful to me, the subtle character of
color in nature, compounding coldness, warmth, clearness &
indefiniteness.

### September, FRIDAY 1

Oppressively warm & quite windy but worked all m'g on No. 9 —
The Sep't. haze makes distant hills look milky & my work to look
decidedly skim-milky. P.M.
on No. 7. As the afternoon had clouded over, I worked without sun-
light — Consequently to no great advantage. Over'g G's & I spent
the Ev'g at Mr. Beach's — Quite a number (8 or 10) y'g folks there —
Left at 11.30 o'clk in a good Rain.

## Right Page

### September, SATURDAY 2, 1865

Soaking Rain all the m'g.
Walked into town & back, doing a little private shopping —
P.M. it cleared off very close
warm. Very Mother —
Kate came home, after visiting among Catskill Mt.
Relations for six weeks.

### SUNDAY 3.

In bed all day with a headache — also quite an unaccountable trouble in one of my toes on the left foot — So inflamed &
swollen as regularly to cripple me.
