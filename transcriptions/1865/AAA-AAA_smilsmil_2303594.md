## Left Page

### March, Saturday 4, 1865

Painted (1½ day) on Haines' —
on left hand Birch trunks
& foreground Rocks. Day
so dark & rainy that many times
could not see — did little
work. It cleared up about
3 o'clk. but too late for the
Grand Procession in honor
of our victories.

### Sunday 5.

Clear, bracing & windy —
Heard Dr. Thompson in
the m'g — walked in the
Park in the afternoon,
and spent the ev'g at the
Sents' where D. C. F. is visiting.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 6, 1865

Glorious day — Clear & cool.
Little or no work was done.
The great Victory celebration
was a success. The procession
passed our Studios. Mary, Matt & Kate,
Mr. & Mrs. Baker & daughter & S. C. F. were at
the Studio to see — In ev'g
went to see fireworks at Union Sq.
Visited the Paysons, pleasantly
gave them tickets our Reception.

### Tuesday 7.

Painted a little on Haines' Fall.
But most of the day was spent
in sweeping & cleaning up, for
our Reception in the Ev'g.
Georges' Kaaterskill Fall & my
Hainds' although both unfinished
were hung up in our Studio, we
were much praised. Altogether
quite a success. I had
S. C. F. under my charge.
