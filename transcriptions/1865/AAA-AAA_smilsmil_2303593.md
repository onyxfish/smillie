## Left Page

### February, TUESDAY 28, 1865

Painted (15th day) on Haines —
painted out large light
Rock & much small Tree
painting on Right hand Side
at Recommendation of Mr.
J.M. Hart. A great improvement.
In eveng. met with others
at Mr. Dunnings & Read
3d & 4th acts of Hamlet.

### March, WEDNESDAY 1.

Painted on Haines (16th day) on
the middle part of the Picture
& glazing all the foreground Rocks.
The frame came to-day — Think
it looks very well. Oscar Whe[i]l
called at the studio, home
for 2 months with badly wounded
Right hand. Looks well. full
of pluck & energy and is very
interesting.
Meyer took his 13th lesson.
Spent eve[ning] at Mr. Tracy Harris's. 46. m. 46[illegible].

## Right Page

### March, THURSDAY 2, 1865

Painted on Haines (17th day) on
foliage of foreground Trees —
especially the extremities coming
down upon the Sky. Commenced
to Rain in the afternoon. In
evening after drawing an hour or
two on "Arms of Mississippi," went
around to the Lents & finished
the evening — Sarah F. is
visiting there.

### FRIDAY 3.

Painted on Haines (17½ day)
foreground Trees, & spent
½ day finishing drawing of
Arms for Continental Bank
note Co. Dr. Bartlette
called at studio. — Offers me
$200.00 for my Haines Fall.
I want $300.00 with the
frame — Miss Dunning
called in the afternoon — So
did Mr. Francis Copscutt.
