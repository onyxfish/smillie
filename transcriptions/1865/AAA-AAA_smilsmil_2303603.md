## Left Page

### April, Sunday 9, 1865

In the M[orn]g heard J. P.Y. —
Discourse called out no
doubt by Unitarian Convention
of last week — Mr. Well had
a grand crowd to hear J.
P.Y. — till now he would treat
the Southern Conspirators. —
He preaches the broadest
Christian leniency — The
services were very impressive.

### Monday 10.

Rain all day — but N.Y.
has another wild start in the
news. Rec[eive]d here at about
11 o'clk last night of the
surrender of Lee this army.
Every body is wild. —
Painted all day (9½ days)
on "Buttermilk." Scumbled sky
with Simon yellow & white & rubbed
a very thin glaze of Madder lake
over distance. Painted on trees
in foreground. — Am quite
pleased. At Parsons' in eve[nin]g.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 11, 1865

Sick headache all day.
Did little else than lie
about the Studio.
Set my palette & painted
a little on Buttermilk
but gave it up.
Went home & went to
bed at 7 o'clk.
Uncle Wm T. has been quite
sick since Sat. Bilious.

### Wednesday 12.

Still Rainy — Painted all
day on the sky of "On the
River, above Po'keepsie"
10½ × 14½ — (24½ days)
Miss Dunning called —
Miss Lila Martin & 22°
of her, Father & Mother
spent the eve[nin]g with us.
Uncle W— too unwell
to see them —
