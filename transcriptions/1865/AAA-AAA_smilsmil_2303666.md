## Left Page

### Dec. Sunday 17, 1865

Cloudy & cold, with slight fall of snow last night. Mr[s?]
heard Jo. P. Thompson on the true elements of Puritan Ideas
& the cause of their success —
A collection was taken up (about
$4000 or) for the purpose of evangelizing
the South. P.M. walked in the Park.
Eve[ning] again heard Thompson on
the Apostolic Creed.

### Monday 18.

Overcast. All day at work on
Locomotive, dry pointing & biting.
After dinner waited in
Brevoort's Room until 8.30. o[clock]
Mr. & Mrs. Jas. Hart called in
before I left. Rest of evening
at home writing to Miss Dunni[ng].
Mary commenced her visit
to Mr. Nyckoff this eve[ning].

## Right Page

### Dec. Tuesday 19, 1865

Miserable rainy day —
Worked on Locomotive die, burnish-
ing. I have worked now, five
good days on the die, and
only hope that it is that much
better. Eve[ning] at home
writing.

### Wednesday 20

Dismal sort of a day. At
11 o'clk. P.M. went down to Continental
Bank Note Co with "Locomotive"
die. Got p[roofs]. Pres[ident]. says that it
is much improved — delivered it.
Walked up town stopping at the
various picture galleries, and at
Dr. Dunnings. Got to the Studio
after 3 o'clk.
Eve[ning] at home. Snow in
the Eve[ning], Rain later.
