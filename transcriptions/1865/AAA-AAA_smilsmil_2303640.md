## Left Page

### Sept. Monday 4, 1865

Warm, muggy and misty —
To-day an out + out cripple
with poor prospect ahead —
foot very much swollen + inflamed.
Painted all day on 10½ × 18 —
intended for Mr. Tracy Harris —
worked second time over the
sky + painted middle distance.
Find it hard work painting
out of the studio — especially in
a room papered a delicate blue.

### Tuesday 5.

Cloudy, muggy m[orning]. P.M. Clear
\+ delightful — Still kept about
the house by my foot.
Painted on 10½ × 18. worked
over the large groups of
trees — Tendency of color to
blackness — At about
5 o'clk. P.M. Mary, Matt, and
Mr. W. Parker, came up by row
boat from P—. Took tea and
at 8.30 — glorious moonlight
drove into town.

## Right Page

### Sept. Wednesday 6, 1865

Cloudy, warm with a dash
of Rain — Another glorious
P.M. + [illegible]. Used up all
day with a headache —
Two of these visitors a week
is getting rather more frequent
than is agreeable —
Aunts Jessie + Mary arrived from N.Y.
Mary went down on the barge
to-night, to start on the D.L. + W.
R.R. for Montrose to-morrow m[orning].

### Thursday 7.

Fine day — got a bit of carpet +
made a rig for my foot + then
went to my Study No. 9. Finished
painting the opposite shore of River.
Feel now extremely anxious to
get the two Studies 7 + 9 finished.
Afternoon worked on Study
No. 9, painting in some of the
foreground trees —
