## Left Page

### April, Thursday 13, 1865

Unpleasant morning —
fine P.M. Have fought
all day with a headache,
but pulled through in pure
spite — Painted "Twilight"
6×10 — headache & no
headache — the best piece
of color I think that I have
yet done.

### Friday 14.

A glorious Spring day.
Painted 1st day on "a Recoll.n
of Hillyer's Ravine, Catskill Clove"
6½×9. Feeling tired and
"springy" did not succeed
so well as yesterday.

*[pasted note:]*
President Lincoln
was murdered this
Evening.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 15, 1865

A dark day for our nation.
We were stunned, sickened, this
m'g. by the news of the assassination
of Pres. Lincoln & the attempted murder
of Sect. Seward at Washington, last night.
It rained afternoon & wolf. I
could do no work. In the morg.
it being "varnishing day" — went to the
N.A.D. — in their new bl'g cor. of
23d St & 4th Ave.

### Sunday 16.

Everybody seems to flock to
church — It seems instinctive,
& I am glad to see it — D. Thompson's
Church is draped, & when I heard
the "solemn voiced" organ I could
not restrain tears — but there was com-
fort in the D.'s discourse — In the eve'g
there was not standing room to hear
D.s Thompson — Duryee & Prof.
Hitchcock — The services
tended to lift us up to the great
work that the Almighty has put before us.
