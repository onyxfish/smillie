## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 31, 1865

Hotter. Worked all day (6¾ days) on "Fall on West Brook" — Painted on Middle Rocks, etc.
Lloyd Mayer took his 28th lesson. At 5 o'clk P.M. took train for Plainfield N.J. to visit Uncle Geo. In the west we were greeted by a very pleasant surprise party of some 14 or 16 persons — Kept it up until 1 o'clk.

### June, Thursday 1.

Hotter yet — Got up with a headache — An early cup of coffee made it better — General day of prayer etc. So we had a fine pic nic to "Washington's Rock" Miss Kerr — Mrs Vander Veer & Mr Safford, with Uncle Geo. & Aunt C. — Fred, Geo. & self made the party — Started at about 10 — Ret'd. at about 5 o'clk P.M. All in all a very pleasant day — Went to bed soon after 9 o'clk — well tired out —

## Right Page

### June, Friday 2, 1865

Up bright & early & in to N.Y. by the 7.25 Train — Reached the studio at 10.30 o'clk — Glazed & worked over "West Brook" until noon. Spent the afternoon finishing on "view near Montrose" — 6×10 — (3½ day) I must say I rather like it — I will try to sell it with "Twilight" as a mate — Calling them "Morning & Evening" — Eve'g spent at home.

### Saturday 3.

Some more hot — Painted (3¾ days) on "Fall on West Brook". Managed however, between disinclination & calls, to do but little —
Afternoon took a drive with Mr Wykoff through the Park — Everything looks wonderfully charming.
Evening at home.
