## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday 20, 1865

Cold & foggy, cleared off by about 9 o'clk — Worked all the mng.
on Study No. 9 — Still on middle distance
P.M. on No. 7 — To-day, although
clear & tolerably warm has been
an uncomfortable one for
sketching — being quite windy
& the air with a touch of
rawness in it.

### Thursday 21.

Glorious mng. Finished middle
distance on No. 9 — P.m. clouded
over about 4 o'clk — but worked
until about 5 m No. 7 — Progress
for the day quite satisfactory.
About 5 o'clk tried my hand
for the first time in Mr. Roos-
evelt's outrigger shell boat.
A risky, teasing sort of a
thing, but pleasant excitement.

## Right Page

### Sept. Friday 22, 1865

Warm & pleasant — Mng. Somewhat
cloudy — Mng. painted on No. 9 —
put in the near hill, grass covered
& two or three of left hand foreground
trees — Afternoon on No. 7 — which
at last I call done, if not finished.
At 5.30 took horse & saddle & rode
up the East Road & around to Union
Corners, then to Hyde Park & home —
Called 10 miles — time 1 hour & 5 m.
Splendid evng.

### Saturday 23.

Clouded until 10 o'clk — Until
that time at work on pencil outlines
on the "East Road" — at 10 — commenced
again on No. 9, painting in middle fore-
ground trees — Afternoon Cloudy
& windy — worked a little while
out of doors upon No. 1 —
Evng Father & Uncle W. arrived
from N.Y.
