## Left Page

### June, Thursday 8, 1865

Very hot day, but worked through it with commendable vigor,
on "Fall on West Brook" (8th day)
I feel pretty well pleased — that rather alarms me —
I think that I should push right out to sweet-tempered
nature, to see how I have been abusing her, & so come back
to proper humility.

### Friday 9.

Excessively hot — with heavy thunder showers — In the m'g
got a couple of drapery sketches from Joe. Then made a drawing
upon wood for Hooper. Ill lines by Millis — dawn — Tolerably
successful I think —
Evening, went to Trinity Chapel — 25th St — to hear a public rehearsal
of Church music — Amused for the first half hour — but there
was but one kind of food, & all from one dish — Mr. H. Walter —

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 10, 1865

A sticky, thunder-showery day —
very heavy storms passed over us last night — & 3 or 4 to-day —
much of the time it has been too dark to work. Painted on sky
& distance of "From Sunset Rock" 14×20
(5th day) also put a glaze of Bitumen
Madder Lake & Cadmium over all the
middle ground — to try a warm glow
to work upon. Even'g in at Will's

### Sunday 11.

Glorious cool, clear, breezy day — In the m'g went to hear
[illegible] T — "Let y'r Moderation be known
to all men" — P.m. in the Park,
Evening went to Thompsons again
