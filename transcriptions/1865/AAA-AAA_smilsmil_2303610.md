## Left Page

### May, Sunday 7, 1865

Most glorious day — went to the Madison Ave. Baptist Ch., heard Dr. Weston.
Took a very satisfactory dinner at the Broadway House, Cor. 42[illegible].
Eve'g went to B'dway Tabernacle, heard J. P. Thompson. Moonlight beyond compare — So walked up to the Central Park with Metr. Gavit — Returning at 10.30 o'clk.

### Monday 8.

Threatening rain — Worked all day on, & finished "On the Hudson above Po'keepsie" 10½ × 14½ —
6th days. Like most of my paintings it is hard & metallic. The sky does not recede, & the foliage is speckled up, without dash or brilliancy. Color tends to blackness. Eve'g caught in the rain, stopped at Miss Dunning's until 8.30 o'clk — Then borrowed an umbrella & by 9 o'clk got our dinner.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 9, 1865

Heavy rain all day — worked all day, first day painting, on the sky of "Sunset Rock" 14 × 20.
Cannot get it all to suit me. It seems weak & colorless, I want to see it full & juicy — Miss Dunning called to bid us good bye. Tomorrow she starts for Europe, her new country home, to be gone some months. Today Mrs. [illegible] Polhemus was buried — She died Sunday, of apoplexy.

### Wednesday 10.

Lloyd Mayer took 24th lesson.
Clear day — Painted first day (2nd with outline) on "Falls on West Brook" 12 × 18 — Worked feeling miserably unlike it. Result of course unsatisfactory.
This afternoon was elected an associate of the N.A. of D — C.C. Coleman, J.O.B. Inman & myself were elected out of a list of 13 — My large painting hangs on the Academy walls unsold — I haven't a dollar to my name & am out of sorts generally. I have commissions — but no money.
