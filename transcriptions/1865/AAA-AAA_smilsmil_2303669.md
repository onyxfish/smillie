## Left Page

### Dec. Friday 29, 1865

Delightful day — clear and breezy but not cold. All day on Graham's Steel die, after my drawing for Con. B.N. Co. Ch[illegible] a little with graver, laid a Re-biting ground + rebit [illegible]. At 3 o'clk. P.M. took Mr. Myck[illegible] horse with Saddle, Bridle + Rod[illegible] in the Park.
• Evey at home.

### Saturday 30.

Heavy Snow storm all day. Burnished an hour or more on Steel die. Charcoaled-in roughly, "On the Hudson, near by de Park" 25×48. P.M. went to Phil. Rehearsal. Met Miss Dunning. She told me that a week ago, sh[e] promised to marry the young Meth-odist Clergyman, at Lenox.
I know no friend that I would be more lothe to lose, + none for whom I have a more sincere Regard + Respect. Katy G — took 4[th] les[son —illegible]

## Right Page

### Dec. Sunday 31, 1865

Dull, disagreeable day — Threatening snow, + part of the time doing it — Went to D[r]. Thompson's M[orning] + Eve[ning] — Afternoon made a call upon Miss Dunning.
