## Left Page

### April, Monday 17, 1865

Under the weather, altho' the weather is delightful —
Painted (3½ days) on the
"Hudson above Poughkeepsie" —
Scumbled the sky, worked
over a part of the blue and
painted in distance. In
the Eve'g Mr. & Mrs. Harris
at the house — also Cy. Bradley.

### Tuesday 18.

Painted in (4½ days) the
Evergreen & Remainder of
"Hudson above Poughkeepsie" —
It is now strong — but the
Evergreen tends too much
to black — Fond of using
transparent colors — I some
times run into this error
before I know it — What
looks bright & clear on the light
palette, appears very different
when on the Canvas as a body color.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 19, 1865

A glorious Spring day, very
warm — As this is the day of Pres.
Lincoln's funeral at Washington, no
business is done & service is held
in most of the churches. The city
presents a doleful spectacle —
Painted all day of "Twilight" (6×10)
2d day — Scumbled & repainted
sky — getting much more light —
glazed & painted on Landscape —
Reading at Miss Dunning's —

### Thursday 20.

Strong wind all morning — heavy
rain in afternoon — To-day was first
set apart as a Holiday by Gov. Fenton
on acct. of recent victories — but after
the murder of Pres. L. — it was changed
to one of mourning — & so it has been
observed — Miss Dunning drew
at Studio — Finished little twilight
— I feel better pleased with it
than anything that I have painted
so far — Miss Guthrie of Ohio
& Miss Seymour of Buffalo were
introduced at the Studio yesterday
by Kittell —
