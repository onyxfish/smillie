## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 29, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely day \- B&fo'. at 9 \- then some time given to newspapers \- then I entered on a new phase of photo. work \- that of assorting the prints for the albums from those for my own collection \+ putting aside the negatives to be intensified or reprinted \- It was after 12 when I went out for my walk Riverside \+ then I regretted that I had not started an hour earlier. It was very delightful \- Back at 2 \- David Harris with Jim to dinner. After 4 o'clk I had a long nap \- no supper \- Eve'g writing various notes.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 30, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary \+ her "girls" arrived \-

A dull m'f. \- rather threatening \- but gradually clearing to fairly pleasant \+ mild \- About 9 A.M. Chase called me up on 'phone to say that it was reported that the Moltke w'd dock at about 12.30 \- I got to studio \+ painted on Warner lot sketch, a useless waste of time as I make no progress in bettering it \- At 11.30 I 'phoned to Hoboken docks \+ was told that the Moltke co'd. get to her dock at about 1 \- so, soon after 12 I cleaned up \- went to Clausen's \- saw frame for [illegible] painting \- gave directions as to its repair \- then walked to 5th Ave. \- 2.3rd St. \- car to Ferry \- our boat crossed the bow of the Moltke \+ I got to the dock just as she was warping in \- Chase was there \- It was about 1.30 when they landed \- it was about an hour before their six trunks \+ two cases were brought together \+ examiner secured \- then I left them with Chase \+ went home \- getting there at about 3 o'clk \- had some lunch \- loafing \+ a nap \- Dinner with Jim \- Eve'g we went to Chase's \+ had a visit with the new arrivals \- they were tired but seemed to be happy \- Men home \+ to bed.
