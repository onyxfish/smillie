## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 29, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Remarkably fine winter day — brilliant, fairly cold, 19°-25° \+ fairly quiet — probably a weather breeder — On my way to studio, stopped at D. Appleton's \+ got my old etched plate — "Cedars by Moonlight" — To studio soon after 10. Worked awhile on "Pont Marie" — laid ground for new etched work on it — Varnished Mr. McKlessoris High Sierra painting — At studio I found notes from W. Fitz Roy Carrington \+ Kelpsel \+ Co. asking if I wd. be willing to give optical demonstration of the technical methods of the mezzotint, aquatint, drypoint, etching \+ graver work processes to Mr. Ju\. d. Cadwleader \+ some friends — on my way home, about 1 o'clk — I stopped at Kelpkels — Saw Mr. Carrington (also K.) \+ had a pleasant chat — Told Mr. C. that I would be much pleased to meet Mr. Cadwleader \+ his friends at the studio \+ explain to them \+ exhibit before their eyes the various styles of engraving — also printing — But I did not wish to make any "demonstrations" before frivolous girls who were following a fad — Home \+ lunch at 2 — Some Reading — then a pleasant three mile walk on Riverside at sundown. Dinner before 7. with Jim — Eve\. he went to armory to shoot — I read —

## Right Page

### Sun. March 1, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast mt. Raw \+ quiet, snow in the afternoon \+ later, rain. I got up at about 6 \+ had a scrub, then back to bed — a nap \+ up at about 8 \+ bkfst. — The Sunday papers took too much of my time, then I got to work upon a revision of my edited arrangement of Father's autobiography. I am now going over it for the last time, cutting \+ condensing — at about noon I went out to Riverside Park \+ walked up nearly to Grant's Tomb \+ back — quite four miles — with very little sense of fatigue — Dinner with Jim at 2 — Reading \+ a nap — Supper at 6.30 — Eve\. continued the revision work till 10 —
