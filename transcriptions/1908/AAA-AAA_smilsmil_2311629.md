## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 5, 1908

**Weather:** An overcast, cold wintry day  **Temp:** [illegible]

An overcast, cold wintry day — after a very uncomfortable night, calomel \+ Citraci of Mag. brought matters to a focus. — did not get up to my help till near 10 o'clk \+ then felt very peak \+ weeble — I did not go out during the m'f but busied myself about little matters at home — lunch late went out for a little while but the cold \+ wind discouraged me. — My occupation during the afternoon was no more important than that of the m'f. — At 6.30 I went to Charles' had dinner with him \+ in the ev'g we are to the Boston Symphony Orchestra concert at Carnegie Hall — the first of the season — Max Fiedler, the new conductor leading — he is too jerkily intense in movement for my nerves — but the orchestra is a wonder of perfect training. — We had Leonora, No. 3 — Then a Brahms Symphony No. 1, C. Minor — I was able to enjoy a little of it — The "Flieh? S Franz" did not understand at all — Love Scene" I euros not — but the Tannhäuser was given heroically \+ roused me as of old. — Home after concert \+ to bed feeling better after my outing.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 6, 1908

**Weather:** Raw day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw day — a suggestion of snow in the air — To studio at 10.30 — a little more than one lb. of steam on — radiators all shut off — Ther. 76° — what am I going to do about it — where before I had difficulty in getting heat, now I have trouble in restraining it. I spent much of the m'f. in making title pages for the three or four copies of my Yosemite Article (pub. in Picturesque America — printed on one side of paper only) that I intend to have bound — Home to lunch at 2 — Afternoon passed with negatives getting them in shape to print for the boys' albums. — Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g wrote to Mr. Davis, Colorado Springs \+ to Ralph.
