## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 31, 1908

**Weather:** Bright mtf — then clouds & wind & clear evef.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio after 10 & worked on etched plate "Yosemite" — pines with Sentinel Rock in vista — only a little graver work on the foreground rocks closing up some of the straggling lines — Then commenced to make changes on "Moonlight & Cedars" — Stoning down sky about the moon where the lines are over bitten & very wirey — By the way — on my way to studio stopped at Miller's — The carpenter. He sent his foreman, Fred, & together we went to cellar & laid out work to be done there in mending coal bin & enlarging my corner compartment under the stairs. Home — lunched with Rachel at 2 — Afternoon we had a walk on Riverside — I had dinner alone — the boys had gone off with others to dine at Moquin's — 6 Ave. near 25th St. & to see the old year out — Evef I worried over balancing my bank acct. to agree with the Bank Statement. Then to be waked at midnight by the ceaseless din of steam whistles, bells, horns & all sorts of discordant noises that has become the fashion of the hour — a hideous barbarity worthy of the Heathen Chinee.
