## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 7, 1908

**Weather:** Clear, cold \+ windy. Ther. down about 20° \- yesterdays thaw frozen hard.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio at 10 \+ worked with dry point on face of Goldsmith's Daughter till 12.30 \- then to Bank \- from there home \+ lunch at 2 \- Afternoon arranging photos. in geographical order \- Dinner alone at 6.30 \- Jim came at 8¹⁵. He is discouraged at the outlook \- while the company is driving the employees to over-work it is only preparatory to laying off a considerable number \- He thinks that his turn may come soon \- Evett wrote to Ralph

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 8, 1908

**Weather:** Very clear, cold \+ windy \- I found it a little more keen \+ bitter than yesterday.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio at 10 \+ worked till near 1 o'clk on Goldsmith's D\- with dry point \- trying to deepen the tone of all the light portions of the face, hoping thereby to give it a sense of color quality, the lack of which has been a matter of worriment to me ever since I gave the plate up as finished more than twenty years ago \- I rec'd. note from 2. Nat'l stating that $24.30 had been rec'd. from the Schenker Co \- of Munich, settlement of an over-charge of £5. on a draft last Aug. Home at 2 \- lunch \- Afternoon continued my work of arranging collection of photos \- took a short walk for air \+ exercise about 4 o'clk. Dinner with Jim at 6.30 \- Evett. he went out \+ I bummed around reading etc. etc till 11 \- then to bed.
