## Left Page

### Tues. March 10, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - To studio before 10 \+ got started again on "The Goldsmith's Daughter" - got painting \+ pf. in good light - studied awhile \+ then started with dry-point to lay in the light side cheek that I had scraped out. I spent the m't over it - Home to lunch about 2 - at 3.30 I went to the "Congested N.Y. city exhibit" at Mus. of Nat. History - at 4 I went to Lenox Library \+ saw Westenkampf - It was after 5 when I left there \+ walked back home. Dinner with Jim - Eue'f he went to call on Mr. Holdalple. I read -

## Right Page

### Wed. March 11, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright pleasant day - aetho' rather breezy at times - To studio at 10 - worked till after 1 o'clk - on face \+ neck of Goldsmith's Daughter" - Preparing to print to-morrow — wetting paper, rags etc - Home soon after 2 — at 3 went to Met. Mus. \+ saw the T. Gaudeus collection of statuary - home at 6 - very tired, as I walked both ways \+ was on my feet all the time - I should not have done it as I have been out of sorts for a week or more \+ that should have been a resting. Jim dined with the Hasbrouchs \+ I dine alone at 7 after trying to get a rest. Evening I read - on going to bed at 10 I had a temp. 100½° - enough to prevent me going to N. A. of D. meeting.
