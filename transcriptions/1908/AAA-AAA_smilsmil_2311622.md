## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 22, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day \- pure, clear air \+ sunshine. Jim started in work again with the Western Electric Co. After bkft. I went to Dr. Little's. my left eye, inside of lower lid, is much inflamed \+ painful \- I think automobiling \+ the long ride on the rear platform of D\&.W. Train on Tues. was probably a matter of too much dirt \+ irritation. Dr. advises continuance of Boric Acid treatment. Then I went to 2\. Nat\. Bank \- Then home after 11 \- Lunch at 1 \- Before 2 o'clk I went to studio \+ scolded [illegible] (Ryan's Man) because so much time is being lost in finishing up the heating outfit with asbestos jacketing etc. I got my Turner Gallery \+ took it to Stikeman the book binder \+ left it with him to bind. Then I wandered to the Penn\. R.R. Co's new building enterprise, 31-33 St.\+7'. 6\+ Ave\. \- a wonderful work. It was after 6 when I got home \- dinner 6.30 \- Eve\. unpacking some cut glass ware from Wanamaker's \- Reading newspaper \- loafing \+ writing.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 23, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant, mild m\., but clouded afternoon with a little very light rain. I wrote to Ralph in the m\. \+ worked at home getting many things into more orderly shape. About noon I went out on Riverside \+ in the Park \+ had a walk \- Then in to lunch \- Afternoon continued the work of the m\. \+ also did some sleeping. Dinner with Jim at 6.30 \- Eve\. I wrote to Mrs. Paul Victor Baur (Sue whiting) in response to a gushing note of thanks for the etching [illegible]. I sent her as Wedding present.
Before dinner I went to Wanamaker's \+ bought a down bed \+ bread for my bed.
