## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 3, 1908

Election Day \-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raelph ret'd to New-Haven at 11 p.m.

Superb day \- Up before 8 \- from my window I could see the polling place in Alt's Tailor store on opposite side of street \- At that time there was a queue out onto the sidewalk of 30 or 40 waiting voters \- when I went out just before 10 there were not less than 120 \- they extended to the corner & down Broadway \- I went to Charles & spent some time with him looking at mounted p'fo. of his last summers photo work \- then to studio for a few minutes & home at 12½ \- At that time there were about 40 in line \- Jim, Ralph & I went out, took our places & waited on the sidewalk 55 minutes before we got in to vote \- If the weather had been unpleasant I could not have borne the exposure \- As it was, it was a very tiresome wait \- When we got in we found matters moving along in a very deliberate way \- The average length of time required for each vote was about 1¼ m. That each voter should write his name is a good plan but the polling districts should be smaller \- he says there were 548 Registered & only 400 minutes for them to vote \- The last one voted at about 7.30 \- Poll closed at 5 \- We went home & had lunch after 1 o'clk \- The boys went off & I devoted the rest of the day to my photos \- After 5 G.E. Finley Cook came in & made a short call \- we had dinner at 6.3 Before 8 David Sturvis came for them & they went off to get election returns & see the sights \- Raelph took his suit case & ret'd to New Haven by 11 o'clk train \- at about 10 I went to 42d & from there walked with immense crowds to Herald Square \- There were crowds of vehicles, mostly automobiles, as well as trolley cars \- all well managed by many police, mounted & on foot \- It was a constant & howling discord of all sorts of diabolical noises made by peoples of whom better things would be expected; bad manners & good humor ruled the crowd \- At about 11 I went to the Century Club where election returns were being read \- I sat there awhile till assured that Taft & Hughes were safe \- Then home & to bed at 12.30

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 4, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable weather \- Raw & cold, with some spitting rain that did it out. to anything. To studio soon after 10 \- Met Ryan, very mad, some one has turned water on the boiler & then left it on & for the third time the cellar & the asbestos jacket has been flooded & soaked. & the radiators all thro' the bldg. filled with water. No one, besides Ryan & self, has keys to the cellar but Winton & he swears positively that he is not guilty \- It is hard to believe him \- The room was overheated \- 76° \& I had an uncomfortable time till noon when I took to Stickeman another lot of books to be bound \- Went to 2d Nat'l. & made a deposit \- Then home at 1 & had lunch \- Afternoon I gathered "Punch" Nos. for the past three or four years \- & other periodicals, to be bound \- Chas. Collins came in at about 4.30 & we had a cheerful chat for about an hour \- Dinner 6.30 \- Eve'g., newspapers & some writing.
