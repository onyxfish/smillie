## Left Page

### Sat. June 20, 1908

To New Haven - 477 Prospect St.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The hottest & most uncomfortable day of this season - Ther. above 90°. & Humidity 82°. Bkfs. before 8 - a number of things took my time till after 10. Then I took a mounted pf of the Heber Bishop Jade plate to the Met. Mus. of Art & in the library saw Mr. Clifford who is acting Librarian - He had a copy of the Jade book brought & on comparing my pf. with the print in the Vol. I found to my surprise & disgust that the print was not from the original plate but from a reduced, process plate. The color - the result was completist failure, to which my name attached is most shamefully distinct. It made me helplessly furious - I left my pf. with Mr. C. for the museum with a few words written on the margin under the mount. From Mus. I went to studio & worked there till near noon - then home - had lunch with the boys at 1.30 & at 3.20 we started for R.R. station & there took 4 o'clk. train for New Haven - big train - crowded - hot & dirty - got to N.H. without incident - & by cab, leaving the boys, I rode to 477 Prospect St. getting there at 6. at 6.30 Raleph came & we had dinner - Buck we sat upon piazza - & later looked over an album of Remarkable photos - Taken by Mollie Adams in the Canadian Rocky Mt. Region -

## Right Page

### Sun. June 21, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another exceedingly hot day - Ther. above 90°. I got up at about 7 - & loafed on piazza till bkfs. at about 8.30 — At 10.15 I was at Woodsey Hall, without a ticket, to hear the Baccalaureate sermon by Prof. Hadley - I had to wait in lobby till 10.35 before non-ticket holders were admitted. Fortune favored & I got an excellent seat - I had difficulty in hearing all that Pres. H. said owing to his faulty delivery - He defined four kinds of Philosophy - In summing up I understood him to say that a combination of Stoic & Christian Philosophy made a desirable blend. The singing was very effective as usual - After service I went to Ralph's Room & waited for him there till 12.35 but he did not come in - Jim came in for a few minutes - Then I ret. to Prospect St. - Ralph followed soon after. We had dinner at 1.30 — The afternoon was spent loafing - I slept from 3 to 4 o'clk. R. came to tea at 6.30 & left soon after - Wm went down town & Katie & I sat on piazza till 10 o'clk. From Tribune Report - "The Christian philosophy element added - keep as much of the Stoic view of life as there is in you - we have far too little of it. × × × But the knut choice lies between Stoic reason & Christianity. These are the two philosophies which are today contending with one another in close & not unequal strife × × × The fate of the American people - the fate of the whole civilized world, is bound up with the possibility of maintaining amid all difficulties an essentially Christian philosophy of life".
