## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 17, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rather cold \+ windy \- but dry \- the heavy rain has been of immense advantage to the snow \+ ice covered streets \- to studio before 10 \- worked again on G. D. Have made up my mind that it is a waste of time to try any longer to make the work over a considerable portion of the face come up to requirements \- so, I shall scrape it out \+ begin again from the bottom. It was after 2 o'clk. when I got home \+ nearly 3 when I finished lunch, too late to go to Annual meeting of Met. Mus. of Art \- I went to the Grolier Club \+ read David McN. Stauffer's work on The Engravers of America \- He has gathered some notes \+ a surprising number of names \- everybody of every grade, even to letter engravers, of whom I have ever known anything, has a place in his collection \- It was after 6 where I got home, having stopped at Tiffany's \+ got my watch left there for repair \- I had dinner alone, Jim dined at the Dielmans. I spent the eve'g writing titles \+ dates in one of the 1907 Photo. Albums for the boys.

I called at D. Appleton's \- 436 5th Ave \- tasked to see Mr. Brennen \- Head of Art Dep't to whom I wrote some days ago about plate "Cedar by Moonlight" \- I wish to buy it if I can find it \- he sent out word by boy that he was too busy to see me \- had forgotten to answer my letter \- did not know any thing about the plate \- 8 or 10 years ago the firm sold or destroyed a lot of such material \- uncivil lobster \- Eve'g I wrote to Mr. W. Appleton \-

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 18, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day \- delightful for exercise \- to studio at 10 \- again worked on G. D. scraped out all of light side of her face \- now I must send for the original \- I hope to be able to put in the new part with some freshness \- Home at 2 \+ had lunch \- afternoon, writing titles \+ dates in photo albums \- about 5 o'clk. out \+ walked for an hour on Riverside \- a bit of excitement when an engine on the H.R.R.R. pushed a burning freight car up to the foot of 92 St \- where a street comes thro' to the River \- almost at once a fire engine came rushing down \+ in a very few minutes water was on \+ the fire was out \- great crowd seemed to spring out of the ground \- There were two Hook \+ Ladder outfits \- three engines \- a fire patrol \+ an automobile with District Chief \- In at 6 \- dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g Jim went out to a card party \+ I continued Album work \-
