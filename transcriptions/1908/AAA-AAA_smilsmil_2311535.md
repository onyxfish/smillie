## Left Page

### Wed. April 29, 1908

**Weather:** Grey day \- Sunny day \- very pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

awoke without a headache \- encouraging sign \- Blkp. before 8 \- to oen after 9 bec went to studio \- then began to feel distressingly uncomfortable \- could it tell exactly how. I drove myself to work that did not require any brain fitness \- the cleaning of my plates preparatory to aqua-tint work \- depression increased till at 12 o'clk it was difficult for me to keep up \- I got home \- had a glass of sour milk \- then found my temp 101½°. I at once went to bed \+ after some sleep felt better. at 6 o'clk. Temp 100° \- at 10 p.m. 99°. I give these figures as a record of my decline \- This sudden accession of fever I suppose means a plugging for awhile of the uretea \+ prompt absorption of pus. For two days there has been a dull pain which I filled my lungs, in my back, low down under the ribs \- that has passed away \- These attacks are depressing \- they interfere so with the work that I am trying to finish \+ make me think that my proposed trip to Europe in the summer is risky \+ unwise. I hac to-day from Bessie in which she tells of the severe attack of rheumatism from which Mazie is suffering, in addition to her other troubles \- this fills me with grave fears for her.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 30, 1908

**Weather:** Sunshine \+ showers  **Temp:** [illegible]

at 6 o'clk this mng my temp. was normal. I got up \+ had my Blkp. with Jim, feeling very slim. Before 10 I was at F. Tuttle's \- found him in the midst of moving from 48 to 49 W. 49 S. \- just across the way \- I described to him my experiences of the past two or three days \- he tried to comfort me by saying that in a chronic case like mine such perturbations were less likely to result seriously than in fresher \+ more active cases. From there I walked down to the studio, stopping at Miller's to order a board upon which to mount D. T's Miquat painting. I did some more scouring of zinc plates \+ made some preparations for printing in the near future. Lunch at home before Two \- a rest, some reading \+ some writing \+ making of May Day checks. Dinner alone at 6.30 \- Jim at office \- he got home to his dinner after 8 \- some exceedingly heavy showers in the early eveg. Charles had sent me two tickets to University Glee club concert but between ailing \+ the weather I did not go.
