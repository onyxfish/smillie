## Left Page

### Mon. May 11, 1908

**Weather:** A clear + very warm day with mild south wind.  **Temp:** about 72° outside + 62° inside — a chilly atmosphere to work in.

Much time wasted trying to touch up broken lines in ff. taken on Sat. I made a beginning at mounting the "Miguet" that belongs to D. Tuttle — the panel from Miller is not exactly square — I took it there to be made true — Home to lunch before 2 — After that, as I lost considerable sleep last night I more than made it up by a long nap — Then went to 313 W. 46' S. in answer to card rec'd. from Lydia B. Harrington with whom I used to play many years ago at Montrose when she was a guest of Fanny Millards — met Jim Cannuet there \+ we had an agreeable chatting time — Lydia is a great talker \+ I was kept so long that I was half an hour late for my dinner — Eve. \+ quietly at home.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 12, 1908

**Weather:** Oppressively warm day.  **Temp:** got up to 80°

I spent practically all the m'g at the Studio mounting D. Tuttle's Miguet painting on a panel — \+ I think that I made a very good job of it — Home to lunch at 1.30 Afternoon I went down town to Wm C. Cullen's taking my camera to be overhauled \+ a lot of films to be printed from — Then to Mc.German \+ Lloyd \+ got passes to visit S.S. Kaiser Wm der Grosse — Then home — Dinner alone — Jim came \+ in \+ had his dinner after 8 having had over time work at office — Eve. \+ with newspapers \+ loan.
