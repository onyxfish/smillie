## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 11, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary, her girls \+ Charl, to dinner.

Unpleasant day \- cloudy, threatening m\+f. Rain from noon on thro' the night — Al tho' I felt far from well I went down to Wanamakers \+ got two or three things to complete arrangements for dinner to-night — then to Scribner's, saw W.H.L. Smith \+ ordered some books — then to Second Nat'l. Bank — then to the studio — by this time the rain began to fall. On my way to sub way met Edw. Gay \+ his wife — we rode together to 79th S — I had some lunch at home — the p.m. was dark \+ miserable \+ I gave up to coddling myself. Eve'g at 7 o'clk. Chat., Mary, Katharine \+ Elizabeth arr'd and shortly we were settled at the dinner table. Old Kate had spread herself \+ in so doing spread an excellent but over abundant dinner — All hands suffered — After that much of the eve'g was spent looking over photos of some of our recent trips.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 12, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright sunshine toast of the day — I kept my bed till after 1 — then up, dressed \+ hung about for the rest of the day. At 5 a.m. my head \+ throat were in such condition of irritation that I feared I was in for serious trouble — but that passed very gradually — but a depressing head ache continued — I was able to do some reading — but altogether, as I could not go to the Gordon Ireland reception — to the Phil. concert in the eve'g (to which Emma J. had given me tickets) or to the Bishop Potter mem'l meeting at the Century I felt rather dismal. Eve'g I sat at dinner. Goold dined with Jim \+ later they went to some athletic games at 7th Reg't. armory.
