## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 11, 1908

**Weather:** Wea.  **Temp:** Ther.

Disagreeable day — dark mf. — Rain about noon, heavy fog in the afternoon — To studio soon after 10 — met Fred. Keppel on my way — he told me that Pennell w'd. be at his store in the mf. At the studio it was so dark that I could do but little — I mounted a pl. of Yosemite etching on a sheet of paper \+ prepared it as title page to one of the ten sets, printed on one side of the paper, of my Yosemite article for Appleton's Picturesque America given to me by W. Williams Appleton at the time of publication 1873 — Then I went to Keppel's \+ by good fortune found Jos. Pennell \+ his wife there — they were writing their autographs in copies of the Life of Jas Whistler — Two big volumes — English prints with Sifferath's imprint — I secured a set \+ Pennell \+ his wife kindly humored me by writing their autographs in my set. Then I went to Mourowith's with father's old engraving glasses — to have them repolished \+ put in new frames — there I was advised to go to Bausch \+ Lomb — Home \+ had lunch before 2. The afternoon was spent browsing thro' the Whistler P's — seems to have omitted nothing, altho' Jos R. told me that they had avoided discussing some well known controversies where W. was undoubtedly wrong. What I gathered from so much as I read confirmed my opinion that W. was a colossal \+ unresoning egotist, the embodiment of an unlovely form of genius —
Dinner — \+ most of the eveg again with Whistler.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 12, 1908

**Weather:** Wea.  **Temp:** Ther.

Bright day — clear, cold \+ windy — on my way to studio stopped at Keppel's \+ paid him for the Pennell Whistler — \$10 — Then to studio \+ made a start on the restoration of the Miguet painting belonging to Dr. Tuttle — sanded \+ papered the portions that I had filled with paint last spring \+ brought the surface down even — it is now in good shape to begin painting on — Soon after 12 went to the Grolier Club \+ spent some time looking at a conological collection of Pennell's etched work. The effect upon me was depressing — I had to realize my short comings as an etcher — I have command of the technicalities — I can do any thing that requires skill, intelligence \+ deliberation — none of which are essential in the composition of a good etcher — in fact, deliberation w'd. be destructive — Temperament is the first necessity — and that I have not — but I can recognize it — \+ that leads me to think sadly that possibly it might in early years have lain dormant in my composition \+ been smothered beyond resuscitation by years of repressive \+ misdirected education as an engraver — Had these been in this city at that time any art atmosphere in which I could have found aid \+ development I might have made a better — a very different record — As it was, under the severely mechanical demands of Bank note die engraving, every art impulse was crushed out of me \+ I was left groping in darkness until too late to undo the evil already done.
Afternoon I went down town to Ringler's in Barclay St. where I left three copper plates to have steel facing removed \+ to Callen's where I left the negatives from which prints are to be taken for the boys Albums.
