## Left Page

### Tues. May 5, 1908

Wu Ting fang -

A lovely spring day - mild, bright & quiet. After bkfst. I went to see D. Tuttle, quite as much on Jim's acct. as my own - Jim is now complaining so constantly of his eyes & his head that I am really concerned as to what is best to do for him - I asked Dr. T. to dine with us to-morrow eve't. Then he will have a chance to give Jim a thorough exam. Then I went to Water Color Show & got a general view of it - then to Riverside & loafed there in the sun till lunchtime, then home - Afternoon, did considerable writing - part of it as I sat in Riverside Park - Dinner with Jim at usual time - eve't we went to Carnegie Hall - last meeting of season of Cave Forum & heard minister Wu. speak on the "Awakening of China." The address was carefully prepared & written - As a speaker he is not as successful as in composition - He has the Chinese peculiarity of keeping some part of a word rolling about in his mouth a while, as a general thing his pronunciation was Remarkable, but sometimes stumbled unnecessarily over long & intricate words. Kaleidoscopic, for instance - He is an enthusiastic Chinaman & of course - He told wonderful stories of advance & to him the future was full of rosy visions. Genl. Stewart & Woodford introduced him & then Wu fumbled under his silk skirt & pulled his M.S. speech from out his stocking - He was somewhat hampered by having to read it but he was energetic in delivery & used some snappish gestures to give force or point. After his address questions were sent up from the audience - Some were silly - some were impertinent, but he answered all good naturedly - one question was "Are investments in Gov't., R.R. & other Chinese Bonds safe?" To which he answered promptly & energetically "Sure!" a perfect rendering of the vernacular that pleased the house immensely -

## Right Page

### Wed. May 6, 1908

Grey day - some light rain in the afternoon - growing colder - The bulk of the m'f was spent in a visit to Hoboken to see the Ham. Am. line S.S. Amerika & the Stateroom 407 reserved for our return from Hamburg next Sep'r. The result was satisfactory - altho' on the Main deck, which seems to be the bowels of the vessel it is roomy & well lighted from the outside - I ret'd by way of the tunnel under the river - a commonplace trip if your imagination is not too lively. Up town by elevated - lunch at 1.30 - Afternoon wrote & mailed a letter to Katharine P. T. At 7 o'clk D. Tuttle came & dined with us - Kate gave us a very good dinner - In the eve't the Dr. questioned Jim closely concerning his ailments without eliciting anything very definite - He gave some general suggestions - I had asked for results more active & encouraging.
