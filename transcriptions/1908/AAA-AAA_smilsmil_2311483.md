## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 16, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

my birthday \- 75.

A nasty day, snow + sleet in the m'g \- clearing in the afternoon \- owing to the weather I decided to remain at home \- bkf't before 8 \- I rec'd. a congratulatory note from Emma Bourne + at once acknowledged receipt of it \- By mistake a small jardinere of flowering plants was left at the door \- Kate, being sure they were for me was greatly delighted + was heart broken when I showed her the card directed to another family in this settlement. The most of the m'g was given to the man who hung the curtains in the dining room \- a light lunch at 1 \- After 2, went to Wanamakers, bought cut glass + a number of less important articles. Got home at 5. Rested till dinner \- Hate + Katie spread themselves on the feast \- the best china, glass + silver were out \- Kate came to me in the m'g anxious to have me invite someone to dine with us \- but I begged off \- we had oysters, jellied broth \- quail, sweet breads \- Ice cream \- Macaroons + coffee \+ to cap all a wide spreading cake with 75 pink candles was brought in \- the figures 75, being made by small green candles surrounded by the pink ones \- The [illegible] was short lived \- the heat from the many burning was so great that they were all melting into pink \+ green pools of wax over the cake frosting \- It was an excellent dinner + a kindly [illegible] from the two women \- Jim hurried away immediately after it to drill + I amused myself alone in various ways till bed time.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 17, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day \- brilliant \- Got to Studio soon after 10 + worked on Q\-D\- interrupted by calls from Carpenter \- Dumb-waiter man, Janitor etc. It was after 2 o'clk. where I got home \- Lunch \- after 3 I went to the Globe-Wernicke Store + bought a case that I can use to keep my photos. in in a systematic + orderly manner \- Then I walked down to Brooklyen Bridge + had a look at the immense work commenced for the new Terminal \- then by subway to 33d St \- walked across to Macy's \- went up to the glass cutting dept. \- Saw W. Mc-Donough + Told him what I wanted in the way of a butter dish in cut glass \- gave tentative order + he is to have one cut. Then up town by elevated R.R. \- home after 5 \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Jim went to a Morse-School Dinner \- Eve'g alone \- wrote to Ralph \+ to Emma S\-
