## Left Page

### Tues. July 28, 1908

*Windsor*

**Weather:** Cloudless mt.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless mt. I got up soon after 7 \+ had bkfst. at 8 - the boys following soon after but the Athertons came so late that we could not get the 10.16 train a Paddington - but we did get the 11.15 - \+ reached Windsor at 12.32 We left the hotel in two cabs - The sky was over cast \+ at times the weather looked quite threatening - The country over which we passed was flat \+ uninteresting - one bright spot was the wonderful display of flowers on the grounds of Fetch the 'Readsman' - from the Windsor Station we went directly to the castle. our party - Mr. \+ Mrs. Atherton - Eleanor - D - Chas. Parke - Jim, Ralph osself kept together as we went thro' St. Geo. \+ Albert chapels - \+ then thro' the State Apartments - in these there has been a great change since my visit in Victorian times King Edward has put his house in order \+ now it is one of the finest palaces in this or any other country - I was surprised to find so many fine specimens of painting - Then we all went to the top of the Round Tower - by this time the clouds had broken \+ we had soft, misty sunshine \+ slow moving cloud shadows - we had a lovely time up there \+ I took a number of snap shots - It was almost 3 o'clk, when we got to the White Hart for lunch - By hurrying after lunch we caught a train that carried us to Slough \+ there we hired a couple of vehicles \+ drove two miles to Stoke-Pogie - part of the way thro' delightful lanes lined by old trees - we walked about the monument to Gray in Stoke Park \+ then to the church - went into it \+ thro' the church yard \+ paid homage at the tomb of the Poet - then by hurrying we got to Slough Station in time to catch a train that got us into London after 6 - then a Ride by the old "Black", under ground R.R. to Gower St. \+ a walk to our hotel I had a Rest \+ then dinner alone in Restaurant after 8 - I had a long chat with an old fellow from Chicago who Reminded me of Jules.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 29, 1908

**Weather:** Charming day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - At about 10, started out with the boys for some shopping - we went to Swan \+ Edgar's \+ I bought belt \+ gloves - then to \+ bought a two guinea Steamer Rug - \+ a small holder. It was soon over \+ we got back to hotel before 1 - had lunch there \+ then went to the Franco-British 24p. I had my camera \+ by payment of a shilling got a permit to take it in \+ take out of door photos. - none allowed in the buildings - The light was very brilliant - some snaps were diaphrained down to 32 - others to 16 - I took 6 or 8. Took a hurried look thro' British Art section - then to the French Cinematograph \+ saw the Marathon race again in sections - then we went to the Canadian Toboggan \+ had a slide - then to hotel before 7 - I had a short rest - then Table d'hote dinner with my boys \+ afterwards regretted it - I think very little of the Table d'hote here - Evelf. sat for awhile with the Athertons.
