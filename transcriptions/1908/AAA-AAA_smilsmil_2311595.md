## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 29, 1908

Amsterdam — Amstel Hotel, Room 70.

A pleasant day in spite of threatening showers that hung about the horizon. As our train did not leave till after 10 I had a little time to spare after bkfp. \+ loitered about the canals, or watched the movements of a large party of Americans personally conducted — we got away in hotel bus a little before 10 then the Paris to Amsterdam train had a compartment to ourselves — Train nearly on time — run by time table 55 min. — we were a little more than an hour passing thro Delft — Dordrecht \+ Harlem — The country is very flat \+ given over almost wholly to pasturage — a large number of Holstein cattle always dotting the landscape — There was sunshine \+ beautiful cloud \+ sky effects. At Amsterdam there was little delay in getting to our hotel \+ were given pleasant rooms at the second floor overlooking the Amstel canal — we were soon in place but loitered about till 1 p.m. — then lunch. While at table I saw Katie \+ W\[m\] Elkin sitting near by — they had just come from Scotland where they had been visiting — They were on the look out for us, they said as they were sure we must be somewhere in the neighborhood — After lunch, a rest — then all out together — we wandered about getting some snap shots — then to the Zoo — there the E's went into the Restaurant \+ the boys \+ I devoted ourselves to the Garden \+ Aquarium till 6 o'clk. The Zoo, 28 acres, has some fine trees but I found no fine animals \+ their buildings were much older \+ inferior to Antwerp. On entering a broad avenue at the gate we were greeted by the harsh, discordant screaming of Cockatoos, Macaws, hanging on perches on both sides of the road — their unmusical \+ continuous cries could be heard over the entire grounds — we loitered about till we got to the Aquarium where we were much interested — It is not a large one — about the same number of tanks as at Naples, perhaps — \+ many less than at the N. Y. Aquarium — I could not compare the value of exhibits —
We got to hotel before 7 \+ had dinner — I, W\[m\] \+ S sat with the E's till 10 — then wrote to Mary \+, to bed

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 30, 1908

Excursion to Marken —

A fine day, with high wind — cold on the water — we left the hotel at 10, Katie \+ W\[m\], the boys \+ self — took small steam boat at the DeRuyter Quai at 10.30 — the first part of the trip was thro a canal — slow \+ not very interesting except as showing how flat the country is — almost entirely devoted to cattle pasturage — in parts as much as 12 or 15 below the canal we were on — some beautiful sky scapes over the flat lands — our first stop was at a little village — Broek, where our boat load of passengers trooped ashore, going into an establishment, a combination of dwelling, stable \+ cheese factory where the process of making cheese was shown to us — nothing picturesque about Broek — then on to Monnikendam — a very conservative old town — old church, taking boat at another landing at a lock — the street a long winding one, totrees, buildings mostly brick — out of the canal into the open water we crossed to the island of Marken — of wide extent, rising a few inches above the water, with a few mounds a little higher up on which little clusters of houses were built, leaving at a little distance the appearance of islands — we wandered about here for an hour finding much that was picturesque but the people \+ their boats — all the boats \+ the men were in it being Sunday. The boats bedecked in the little haven \+ the men dressed in their funny best, loitering about — The women \+ children were in gay costume — all men, women \+ children looking comically clumsy — I caught a few snap shots — The dwellings were not examples of neatness \+ thrift — back yards, hew \[illegible] coops \+ green water pools \+ little canals were far from inviting — I got as many or two at them. Then we went across to V [illegible] on the main land — the largest of the four places visited to-day — the costumes a little different. We poked about — many of the visitors entering the houses — post cards \+ souvenirs were offered for sale in many places \+ girls \+ children asked to be photographed — for a consideration, of course. There is a little colony of artists there — we saw two or three working in water-colors. I expended all of my small change upon soliciting models, but doubted if I got the worth of my money — conscious posers are not what I want. The man from the boat who was, in a general way, guiding us about, led us to the open space in front of the church \+ we lined up to see the congregation come out — a proceeding that did not seem to disturb the natives — The number of native worshippers was surprising — the great majority of them women, all in Sunday gear — one old fellow in a lamb's wool cap — red coat \+ dark blue velvet baggy trousers was a picture — we had lunch on the boat at 1 o'clk, before getting to Marken — we left Volendam after 4 \+ sailed on the open sea to Amsterdam — the entrance thro the great locks to the city canals, some two \+ three feet lower than the sea, was very interesting — the locks are so large that a number of boats, large \+ small, enter at one time — large steamers \+ small sail boats \+ motors — we got to the hotel at about 6 o'clk — dinner at 7 — evening, loafing —
