## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 17, 1908

**Weather:** Opera  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - After bkfst. we went to Cooks to buy tickets for country, as planned, to Antwerp but C. has no such coupons, so we bought only to Amiens - Then we went to the Bibliotèque Nationale - and saw many interesting things - we had a peep in to the Reading Room \+ I had a peep into the Print Room where years ago I did some studying - Then to the Gardens of the Palais Royal - the light was fine for photography \+ I saw a picture that made me sigh for my Camera. We then went to the Restaurant Marguery on the Boulevard near Port S\. Davis - strongly recommended by Charr - leaving a reservation - the boys had a full meal, I contented myself with a plain omelette - Then by underground to Port Maillot where I was under the impression we could get tram to Sèvres, but no one there could give us any information, but we got a lot of misdirection - finally we took tram to St. Cloud \+ enjoyed the ride along the outside of the Bois de Bologne crossing the River we left tram \+ took boat down to Sèvres - a pleasant sail - at Sèvres at 4 o'clk - The S. Museum \+ factory very near the wharf - we were admitted to the factories where one man was at work - he amused us for awhile playing with a lump of soft clay on his wheel - then we were led past some kilns not in use \+ that was all we saw of the works - then thro' the Museum \+ saw some very wonderful \+ beautiful things - Out at 5 - Tram to P. dela Concord - cab to Hotel before - I dressed for the Opera at once - then had a half hours rest - then light meal in Restaurant \+ at 7.15 went to Opera - house opens at 7.30 - we were the first ones to get to our Logg. N\? 33 - Troisième - I secured front seats - soon followed by Lester \+ two old American women, possibly from some town in Conn - the usher asked us to give up our seats to the two ladies - we did so, got no Thanks, much to my disgust - The Opera was Romeo \+ Juliette by Gounod - Juliette by Mlle Vuillaums (Debut) - Romeo by M\. Gautier - The opera is tuneful \+ pleasant \+ beautifully staged - 3rd Scene especially fine - fine orchestra, but the singing was not, I think, Remarkable - The ballet in the 4th act was an exhibition of springing jumps, twisting \+ shaking the legs - nothing graceful or elegant to my mind - interior decoration overdone - general effect very fine - shabby drop curtain - I don't like the gigantic nude females that fly over proscenium boxes - They might have a drape - between acts we went down to the foyer - a Noble Apart\[ment\] \+ out upon the balcony overlooking the street - animated scene - it was 12 when we got to Hotel - to bed without delay.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 18, 1908

**Weather:** Versailles  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day - quite cool in the shade - 60°. at my window when I got up at 7.30 - bkfp. after 8 - Then we drove to Gare des Invalides \+ took the 10.23 train going out to Versailles by the Rive Gauche \+ getting to V. in about 35 m. - 11 miles - the views were in places very charming - beautifully wooded hills \+ valleys with villas - also many ugly places. We drove at once to the palace \+ worked faithfully "doing" the interior, following the plan to make sure that no corner open to the public escaped us - I was much interested in the pictures that impressed me so powerfully 46 years ago - Venice - "La prise de la Smala" \+ [from Malakoff] - paintings - I had to smile sadly - at was 1.30 when we completed our Tour - Then we went to the Hotel des Reservoirs, a very swell place \+ had a swell lunch - it was after 2.30 when we entered the Park thro' the court of the hotel, a portion of which was once a residence of Madame de Pompadour - we wandered in the delightful woods till near 4 o'clk - There realizing that we had little time left we hurried over to the Grand Trianon - went first thro' the show of state carriages - then to the palace \+ passed thro' the Rooms in a sort of jam of a crowd - very unsatisfactory - The Napoleon I. furniture was, I think, been reupholstered since I saw it - I remember it as very shabby - now it looks very new - Then we went to the Petit Trianon - we waited awhile for a party to form - it was slow - so we left \+ went into the Park to the "Hamlet" - There was a good light on only one House - I took two or three snap shots - Then we hurried away to catch the 4.53 train, Rive Droit - The Rive d'station was a long one \+ we just missed the train - had to wait till 5.38 for the next one - the views from the Road on portions are fine - the late afternoon light over the woods \+ the distant view of Paris were charming - after 6 when we got to Gare S\. Lazare - \+6.30 when we got to hotel - I rested till near 8 \+ then had chops in the Restaurant — every wrote a short note to Chase at Agenstein — Lloyd \+ Horace \+ Apple called \+ the boys went out with him -
