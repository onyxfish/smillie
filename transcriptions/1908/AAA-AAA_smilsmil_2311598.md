## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 4, 1908

Hanover - Royal Hotel - Room 204.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

About as uncomfortable day, owing to storm of Rain + wind as I ever remember to have traveled in - It was unmitigated + without let up - Our Train started at 10 - R.R. Time - There is a difference of 20 m. bet. R.R. + local time - We had a compartment to ourselves all the way to Hanover - cold + Raw - The country that we traveled over for two or three hours after we left A- seemed to be just emerging from the sea - for miles + miles there were dunes of wind swept sand + vast stretches of a stunted purple heather in bloom - very few trees were to be seen - the monotony of the thing was broken by a very poor lunch-pork, tongue, cabbage + such like - I was reviled by the boys for ordering a soft boiled egg + bread + butter - This was served soon after 12 in a jolting, jiggling Spiers waggon - The monotony was again broken at about 2 when every thing was turned out a Brewtheim - It was merely a form - During the ride I got a very little sleep but not enough to give me rest - As we got to land where crops were grown I was surprised to see men + women working in the fields in the Rain - the men ploughing, the women spreading manure - There was much grain out, standing in shocks - At last, before 6, we got to Hanover - Rain falling lightly - our trunks were carried across the square to the hotel by porters - I had a fire made in my room at once + soon was feeling the comfort of warmth - At 7 we had a fairly good dinner - In its day this had been, no doubt, a fine hotel - but now, paris is marked every where - Wrote to Charl + to bed -

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 5, 1908

**Weather:** Ther. fell to 51° in my room at night.

Bright mng - I was out before the boys came down - After they had breakfasted we started out going first to the Hanover Bank where I drew £30 - Then onto the Masch park where I got some snaps at the new Rathaus, yet incomplete + at the Provinzial Mus. which we entered + hastily went thro' - no pictures of any great importance - 3 by Sir Tho. Lawrence, a Piatto, + a lot of modern work of which I thought but little - we walked back to hotel + had lunch soon after 1 o'clk - The mng. tho' rather too cool had been pleasant + but for the interference of clouds, was good photo. weather -
Before 3 - we started on a drive to see the Town - the matter of communicating my wishes to the driver was difficult - at times impossible - it was cold - twice it Rained - one very heavily for 8 or 10 minutes - many parts of the drive were long + uninteresting - the driver taking us past lyceums, museums, schools + hospitals + to crown all I had considerable rain in my intervals - consequently memories of the ride are not altogether rosy - The driver did as well as he could, I suppose - under the instructions he was able to understand - We drove out to the Eilenriede going up the Hohenzollern Str. on one side, then crossing it to the Hargarten side - The wood is very extensive and belongs to the town - there are many restaurants in it - it is a popular resort - On either side H. + T-, we saw many fine Residences in gardens more or less expensive - Entering once again we were driven thro' principal streets to the Masch park where we had been in the mng - then around past the Waterloo (a parade are.) into the old town - Here the heavy Rain caught us + we had to pull up the hood and afo soon leaving us little chance to see out till the shower passed - we kept dry but the driver did not - We went thro' the old part up to the Herrenhausen Allee + up that for some distance - As conditions for walking in the garden were not good we Reth. thro' the Allée to hotel at 4.30 - In a short time the sun shone out brightly + as I wished to get a photo shot at the water tower we walked down there - then made a long detour getting into the Masch park at last - The boys left me for a further prowl + I Ret'. to hotel - Rested - + had dinner at 7 - Soll. - The boys went to a variety show - I did some writing - Then to bed -
