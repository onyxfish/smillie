## Left Page

### Tues. June 30, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Onboard S.S. Kaiser Wm der grosse Cap. C. Polack. State Room 329 —

Red hot day — Up at 6 \+ finished up every-thing before ship? at 7 — Chas called me on phone to say that it was too hot for him to think of going to the ship to bid us good bye — it would be cooler to bid us good bye over the 'phone — At 8½° we got away from the house — drove to 23? 5. a long wait \+ got to Hoboken Pier at 9:15 — an immense crowd — hot, excited, perspiring — we soon had our possessions in our Stateroom where Ther. recorded 84°. We left the dock at 8:2° I wrote notes to Chas — Katie Elkin \+ Emma Ireland to be rel by pilot — The movement of the vessel over the hazy, steaming water — a light air with us, gave little relief from the heat — We had luncheon at 1 o'clk — I met few people that I know — Prof. Felix Sales, with Mrs A — two daughters \+ son Lawrence, are on board on their way to Berlin where the Prof one of the Exchange professorships — I had a chat with Lawyer Phelps — We had dinner at 7 — a very good dinner, a la carte — I like the little Tables — at our Table there are five seats, two men one from St. Louis, the other, an Americanized German are at Table with us — Swell? I got to bed early, feeling the need of sleep — Extremes of Temp. 84° — 77° in our Room.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 1, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

504 m. at noon —

A quiet sea \+ hot misty m/f — the little air stirring seemed to be going with us — At noon there was a pleasant change, a cool wind blew from N.E. \+ cleared a cloudless atmosphere — but our room continued close \+ warm — at about 76° — I loafed about \+ got photos onboard ship from two points, on the bridge looking forward \+ from 2? Cabin promenade away aft, looking forward — lunch at 1 — a long nap — Then walking \+ sunning myself on the after deck — Dinner at 7 — most of the avelf in steamer chair

Only 3° degrees of change in Stateroom during the 24 hours — 75° — 78°.
