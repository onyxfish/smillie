## Left Page

### Sun. March 8, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A rather mild m't. with occasional gleams of sunshine \+ some very ineffectual attempts at snow flurries or showers till the afternoon when at about 5 o'clk. it began to rain. About 8 it rained steadily. I did not have bk'f. till 8.30. I spent much of the morning working among my photos \+ wrote two or three notes. an hours walk before dinner at 2. At 4 o'clk. I went to 42 W. 77 \+ 2 — the residence of W. Ewald Fleitman to see a number of portraits painted by Hans Kounat [illegible] an itinerant I suppose, here looking for work. Dr. Gerster \+ his wife among the sufferers. I don't think much of Mr. K's work. The portrait of Dr. G. was perhaps the best one there. The Rain came on just as I got home. Supper at 6.30. Self Jim \+ I went to 6th Civic Forum Lecture at Carnegie Hall by Fred. Van Eeden a Sociologist from Holland — "Practical Communion — Work \+ Bread." I had hoped for some sort of a clear \+ practical statement from a practical man \+ thinker. His English was fairly good \+ he did not, at any time, seem at a loss for words to express himself but it was hard work to listen \+ catch all that he said. There was little that was practical to my mind — all you have to do is, like the bees, make a community of sober, sensible people \+ there you are. No calculations seems to be made for what seems to be inherently base in human nature. The selfish \+ the lazy \+ the avaricious \+ the dishonest. The whole race must be purified \+ lifted to higher levels of Ethics \+ morals before the plans of these dreamers can bear good fruit. Van Eeden is preaching a doctrine of altruism such as has been preached with slow growing effect thro' ages. The Race is improving. Social problems are being handled with greater energy \+ intelligence than ever before but it will be ages before we reach the heights dreamed of by Van Eeden \+ others. Nevertheless, such belief should not discourage us from making constant effort

## Right Page

### Mon. March 9, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A rainy m't. — continuing unpleasant until later — in the late afternoon it cleared very cold. I did not go to studio but stayed at home \+ wrote to Weitenkaumpf, Fanny Wright Dairs — W. Twr, Atheston \+ others — at 1 o'clk. I had lunch \+ during afternoon I fussed about accomplishing little. I walked for about an hour before 6. Dinner with Jim at 6.30. Evening, reading.
