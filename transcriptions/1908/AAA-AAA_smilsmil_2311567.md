## Left Page

### Thur. July 2, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** Ther. in Room 76°–70°

Delicious m'f — delightfully cool \+ brilliant in light \+ color — sea very quiet \+ wonderfully blue — I had my bkft. at about 8 \+ after that till 10'clk I was about, much of the time sunning myself on 2° after deck — I wrote part of a letter to Mary — D. Felix Adler came to me \+ introduced Mr. E. M. Bassett a member of the Public Service Com. of N.Y. City — \+ we had a pleasant little chat — After lunch, when it was getting decidedly cooler, I went to my bunk \+ had a long nap — Then on deck — sky all gray — sea very smooth \+ a damp chill in the air — I sat Rolled in my rug for some time — At 7 o'clk. The boys went to dinner but I had no appetite \+ other evidences that I would be better without food — so, I passed — Eve'f I went up on deck — A great change — we had passed from under the grey sky — all was beautifully clear but a dark bank of cloud lay along the west. ern horizon \+ above was the young moon The water was without ripple \+ the moon was reflected in a waving, broken ribbon of silver light. It was enchanting I walked the deck for an hour or two — About 10 p.m. a large steamer passed quite near going west.
Uncle Willie died —

## Right Page

### Fri. July 3, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day — blue, blue, blue, overhead \+ underneath — not a cloud till afternoon, only the wind made by our own motion \+ a quiet sea — I had bkft. alone at about 9 — \+ spent the m'f on deck basking in the cool air \+ warm sunshine — Mr. Bassett introduced me to his sister in law, Miss Preston \+ we had a pleasant chat — \+ Mr. B. conferred with me concerning that part of his itinerary between Vienna, Munich \+ Zurich Lunch soon after 1 — Then for some time I studied maps \+ wrote out an itinerary — Then more walking \+ lounging in the sunshine — a good nap after 3.30 — \+ more on after deck — Dinner at 7 — a dance in the eve'g bands placed between first \+ second class that both, being on same deck, lighted \+ decorated with flags, had their dance — there was little dress \+ less formality — I walked again on upper deck — moonlight \+ sea not so interesting as last night.
