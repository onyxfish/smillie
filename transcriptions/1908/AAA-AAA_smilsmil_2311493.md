## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 5, 1908

**Weather:** Very cold m't  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold m't - in the early hours the Ther. fell to 1° above 0. Official - growing warmer in the afternoon - Snow before eve't I got to studio before 10 \+ spent most of the m't grinding up a lot of old ink in preparation for printing. I wet down paper for use to-morrow. It was nearly 2 o'cl. when I got home - lunch - The afternoon was lost - I found it easier to sleep than do anything else - Dinner alone at 6.30 - Jim came in at about 8 \+ had his dinner - Eve't reading \+ writing titles on European photos. Snow falling when I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 6, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

About 8" of snow fell last night. Thawing in the m't - everything sloppy - I got to the studio at 10 \+ see the m't worked at printing the face of the Goldsmith's Daughter to see the effect of the work I put on it some days ago - It was impossible to get a really good print - everything was too fresh \+ I out of practice - The proof that I have, taken 20 years ago is the best of all the 30 that I took from the plate - it is very beautiful - I had to take half a dozen this m't. before I got one that was good enough to work from. Home \+ had lunch at 2 - Afternoon I finished writing titles on photos. Dinner alone at 6.30 Jim worked over time \+ then went to drill. I commenced the work of arranging my collection of photos - I have several hundred Now that I have a Waonicke holder for them I am making a regular file of them.
