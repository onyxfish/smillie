## Left Page

### Thur. July 16, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A miserable day, rain falling heavily much of the time. I had bkfs. alone — the boys, soon after. It was after 10 when we started out — Tube to High St., Kensington, then' bus. To Holland House Park a few rods from Leighton's house — to-day there was no bar to our entrance — the house & its contents form an exceedingly interesting exhibit. The grounds back of the house, with award & noble trees — in the midst of a great city, is surprising. The interior is a combination of ordinary construction & extraordinary decoration — the oriental apartment, marble paved with fountain in centre, is lined with precious old Persian tiles — the upper floor is mostly studios & alcoves — hung with hundreds of sketches & studies illustrating the development of important works, represented by photo-gravures. Altogether it is an excellent showing of the mental & artistic calibre of the man — it was after 1 o'clk when we left there — Raining as usual — bus to tube, High St. to Victoria Station — auto down Vauxhall Road to Tate Gallery — there we had lunch before "doing" the galleries — the boys were very docile, following me & listening to my lectures with apparent interest — we worked hard till nearly 5 o'clk — then it was raining hard & we were lucky enough to secure a cab & drove directly to the hotel — & had a nap & at 7 had dinner — we spent the eve'g with concert Room — I rec'd a letter from Geo. describing the funeral of Uncle Willie — I wrote to K.P.P. enclosing Geo's letter.

Arranged to-day at hotel for pension rates — 10/ per day for Room — bkfs's dinner & service —

## Right Page

### Fri. July 17, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another day of spots — some sunshine, more rain — We got away at about 10.30 & went to the Wallace Collection. Hertford Ho. Manchester Sq. — a bewildering collection of paintings — bric-a-brac — Armor, carvings & majolica — I was surprised to find so many rare paintings, representing especially the French school of the early part of last century — also some very fine English & Dutch — By 1 o'clk. I was tired out — we had made the round of the galleries in an unsatisfactory way to me, — I wished to look more carefully at many of the paintings — I bid the boys good day & ret'd. to the hotel — had a light lunch & then a nap — After 4 o'clk. we took cab & drove to The Boltons. No. 17 — Mr. Tennant's, where Sarah & Eleanor Atherton are now visiting — There we met Mr. T. & two of her daughters — also Sarah & E — The T's are people of means & live in a very pleasant house facing a park with ground enough to give them a Tennis Court with fine trees — delightful in the heart of a city like London — we were very cordially rec'd — Tea was served — a couple of young ladies came in between — a couple of young people went into the garden — it was after 6 when we left — To hotel & dinner at 7 — eve'g the boys went to theatre & I lounged about.
