## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 27, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty stormy m'g. following a windy night. The Rain continued in showers till near noon — then it cleared suddenly \+ for a time the sun shone brightly — later in the afternoon the sky was hazed over. I was up at 7 — b'kf't at 8 — About 9.30 started out with the boys in the gusty rain \+ went to Schenler \+ fils — bankers \+ drew £30 in Guilders — Then we went to the Mauritshuis Gallery \+ saw the pictures, but few of them impressed me greatly — Rembrandt's "Lesson in Anatomy" is a Remarkable picture in many Respects \+ is in excellent condition — The heads are splendidly painted but I dont like the corpse — I think the drawing is faulty \+ the color bad. I was especially pleased with two Van Dyck portraits. Sir \+ \+ Shepfield \+ his Wife, Anna Wake — Paul Potter's Bull is better than I thought from copies that I have seen — from the Mauritshuis we passed to the Binnenhof where we saw the Chamber of the States of Holland (like our Senate \+ then to the Second Chamber (like our Ho. of Representatives) then to the Gevangenspoort, an old tower \+ gateway, formerly used as a prison — gruesome stories were told by our guide \+ gruesome exhibits of instruments of torture were shown. Then we R't to the hotel \+ had lunch at 1 — shortly after 2 o'clk. we started out \+ walked a mile \+ a half thro' the Haagsche Bosch — a forest with many grand trees — some of the finest beeches I ever saw — The undergrowth was generously dense — These was an open bit of water \+ beyond it "The Huis ten Bosch", a Royal Villa of about 1650. It is now a museum — The International Peace Conference of 1899 met in the large Octagonal Room, covered with Paintings — not impressive as a Room — Here was also a Room once occupied by J. Lothrop Motley, while at work writing the Rise of the Dutch Republic — a portrait of Bismarck upon one of the Walls — Then by tram we R't to Hotel \+ I went hunting for photos — got five — Then I had a Rest before dinner at 7 — W'd't I read newspapers \+ wrote — The boys went off on a prowl.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 28, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some sunshine in the m'g — but rain came before noon — heavy showers \+ gusty wind all the afternoon. I got up before 7 \+ roused the boys before 8 — Before 10 we walked to the Hotel des Indias \+ got the letter that Mary sent there forme — Then we walked thro' parks \+ by canals till we got to the Jaan van Meedervoort \+ the Museum Mesdag — I took a number of snap shots on the way — The collection of pictures in the Mesdag house is very interesting but not enjoyable to me — The first of Mr. \+ Mrs M. seemed to run to unfinished paintings — the first rubbing in or "rotté" — he had a surprising number of Rousseaus — some very large — \+ other of the Barbizon men — from mere outlines up \+ the canvas to half painted pictures, interesting to me only as showing the manner of the men — The gem of the collection, an unusually large canvas for Millet to paint, Hagar \+ Ishmael, is disagreeable to me \+ entirely beyond my comprehension — There were, in fact, but few pictures that I could understand — When we came out it was raining \+ we had to run some distance to get a tram car. That carried us down to the Gemeente or municipal museum. There was, up on the ground floor a very general \+ mixed collection of curios \+ a few pictures on the first floor that interested me till I got into the Saloon of the ancient paintings — here three paintings by Jan van Ravesty \+ held me — He was, I was told, a pupil of Rembrandt — if so he came so near to his master that I can not see the difference — "Officers of the Guild of S't Sebastian descending a stair case and (or is) admirable in every way — The picture was cleaned six years ago, without injury as it seems to me, \+ is now as fresh \+ vigorous in color as when it was painted — The other, "A Banquet" with 23 figures is to my mind, from all points, a better painting than Rembrandt's School of Anatomy — Such a judgement is of course Rank heresy \+ ignorance combined — nevertheless I put it down in black \+ white — The two paintings impressed me profoundly — quite as much as any Rembrandt — we got to Hotel between showers \+ had lunch — Before 3 o'clk I persuaded the boys that, in spite of the weather, we had better take our chances at Scheveningen, \+ we started by R.R. that passes in front of the hotel. Before we reached S — (20 minutes) a fierce gust of wind \+ rain made me almost give up the attempt, but as we got to the station the Rain passed \+ we had only the wind to contend with — we went first to one of the Terraces, then along the beach — quite a heavy sea running \+ a low scud of clouds — it was surprising to me — all of the beach chairs, tents \+ bath wagons were bundled far up the beach near to the wall — an air of desolation \+ desertion was everywhere — I found it as interesting as the ordinary fair weather crowds. There are but few houses at the beach — not more than three or four large ones. The Kursaal or Casino facing the sea. We R't to Hotel by Train No 8 The pine branches. Trees \+ by beautiful Residences — Surely there was an interesting demonstration in front of our hotel \+ at the R.R. Station back of it — The people turned out to welcome the Burgomeister on his Return from a vacation — we had a fine view of it from windows of boys' Room \+ arc light illuminating it, while they created a sudden gust of wind \+ heavy rain came in — The Transformation caused by the prompt placing \+ thousands of umbrellas — glistening wet under the arc light — was very funny —
