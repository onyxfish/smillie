## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 30, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another lovely day — I spent the m't, first part in the Macbeth Gallery 450 fifth Ave. in answer to Mr. M's request for information concerning the paintings of Cole's "Voyage of Life" — the latter part in my studio + I got out p'fs. of etching of the Frauen Kirch, Dresden + selected one as wedding present for Sue Whiting who is to be married next week — Home to lunch at 1 — Afternoon went to Butler's + ordered frames for etching — then to Lenox library + for some time was with Wittenkampf — After 5 went to Prestn. Hospital + spent nearly an hour with Jessie then walked home getting into dinner late — I felt too stupid to read my news paper — a discouraging condition — why does it fall upon me so promptly after my returns. — It was not thusly while I was on my travels.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 1, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thin mist or high fog all day — late afternoon signs of clearing. After a very restless night I slept late — B'kf't at 9 — It was 11 before I was ready to start for studio — Trying to find some one to do some house cleaning — without success so far — I worked with chalk + crayon on some p'fs. taken last June on dark toned paper, of the Frauen K— Dresden — 1.30 when I got home to lunch — after that again to studio + fussed there till after 5 — home at 6 — dinner alone — Jim dining with Cromwell. — I felt I loafed about feeling stupid + accomplishing but little.
