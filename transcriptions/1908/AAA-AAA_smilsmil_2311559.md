## Left Page

### Tues. June 16, 1908

*Ralph came home. Ball game.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day - brilliantly clear + cool with out being cold - To studio - Took to Clausom some of the mounting bds upon which I had marked lines for cutting - on return to studio met Nicoll + had another long Talk with him - answering many questions about the Ark + the facilities for housekeeping there - after which he decided to accept my proposition. Home to lunch at 1.30 + found Ralph there - we had lunch +, as he had provided tickets, we went to the Polo grounds + saw the final game (ball) between Princeton + Yale - It was a stubborn + exciting game standing 2+2 until the last half of the 11th innings when, at the last moment Princeton scored two runs on one play - Then of course P. went wild - It was a great game + a great day - 6 o'clk. when over - home at 7 - dinner - Eve'g at 8.30 I went to Charl's to discuss steamer rugs etc - home at 11.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 17, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - To studio before 10 - Then finished my mounts + went to Clausens with them - + while he was cutting back boards for the mounts I went to Altmans + looked at his steamer rugs - did not find what I wished - Then to Clausens + then to studio - home + lunch. Afternoon I went to Ham. Am. Line + paid the bal. for return on S. S. Amerika - Sch. 17 - $337.50 or $412.50 Total for 3 passages - Then to Cullens + got photo films - Then to Equitable Bldg to see W. Wm Alexander but was too late - Then home - Dinner at 6.30 - The boys went out in the eve'g + I wrote to Scott Brown + to his wife + other notes - Got check of $50 from Nicoll.
