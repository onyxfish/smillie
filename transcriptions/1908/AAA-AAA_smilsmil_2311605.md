## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 18, 1908

Stopping at Southampton \+ Cherbourg.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely m'f. mild, quiet, sunny — I had my chair moved to the boat steamer deck \+ saturated myself with sunshine \+ pure air — About noon we ran under clouds \+ by the time we got up to our anchorage below Southampton it was raining smartly — the embarkation \+ disbarking of passengers \+ their hetrogenous belongings was a tedious process tho' for a time interesting — The tender bringing the passengers was very clumsily handled; it was 20 m. moving back \+ forth, before it could be brought into place for the gangway — meantime the passengers huddling under a scant awning \+ umbrellas — it was not a cheerful spectacle — The whole business took an hour — we had lunch at 12.30 to release stewards for the work of handling hand luggage — we are at 2 o'clk. Hamburg time \+ left at 2 o'clk. Greenwich time — The weather improved there was no more rain — we got to Cherbourg in the late twilight — after 7 — Water perfectly still — like glass — a Royal Mail Steamer — all aglow with lights reflected in the water was a fine spectacle. The embarkation of passengers, baggage, mail \+ stores from three tenders, all handled very cleverly — The big one with passengers came alongside without a hitch \+ her immense load of passengers, light luggage \+ mail was put aboard \+ she was away in 23 mins. — 3 more than it took the English boat to get alongside — our dinner was delayed till 8 o'clk. \+ as we had had our lunch at 12.30 there were a lot of hungry people — I spent the ev'g in our state room reading \+ went to bed at about 10 —

I had 20 m. this m'f in Gym — wrote to Mary \+ posted it at Cherbourg.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 19, 1908

15,6/miles — (naut') p'hour. Run 233 from Cherbourg more.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day — for last night so that for several hours the engines slowed down to 7 knots so the Cap't. told us. Fortunately for us the fog whistle can hardly be heard away down in our state room — I drew from the library Kippling's Stalky \+ Co. \+ gave considerable time to it in the very pleasant \+ altogether unsteamerlike Reading room — It was no sort of a day for loafing on deck — but I got some very acceptable exercise in the gym. Dinner at 7 — Ev'g. divided between reading \+ loafing in the fore smoking room listening to the auctioning off of pool cards on to-morrows run.
