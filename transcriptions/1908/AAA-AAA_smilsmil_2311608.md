## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 24, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

more disagreeable weather \- fog in the early mf. \- mild temp., no wind no sea \- it grew lighter till after lunch when, for a time the sun shone bright \+ warm \+ the sea lay still \- After bkfs. at 8 I went to Reading Room \+ sat there till I had finished Margaret Deland's book \- an admirable work in many respects but disappointing to me in some ways. Some of the characters, as Sam's Sam, are so overdrawn as to be quite impossible \- The story of Helena is a sad one \+ Dr. Lavender's ethics are sound \+ his Religion is good food \- I spent much time on deck till about 3 o'clk or after when the fog came up again \- Chart. \+ I had a chat with 1st Officer Royce \+ I asked him about the crazy boy that he was called to quell yesterday. He is an Austrian steerage passenger. "There is method in his madness" \- It was his fourth appearance when I saw him \- he evades watchers \+ gets among the 1st class passengers \+ when an attempt is made to remove him he "throws a fit" so to speak, howls \+ fights the seamen who attempt to subdue him, excites the women \+ then begs for money \- he has friends on board who have been warned that he may be deported when he gets to N.Y. Evg. we had our farewell "dinner" \- Dress was not de rigeur \- Some of the women were very much dressed \- Some of the men \- dinner wants but many appeared in their usual garb \- There was considerable hilarity \+ the chief's play of the illuminated ice cream procession was duly enacted with some costumed characters \- Germany \- Uncle Sam \- an Artist, etc., were added \- After dinner there was a dance.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 25, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A singularly mild quiet day \- I had my bkfs. at 8 o'clk \- Then took my rugs to the boat deck, fixed my chair in a desirable position \+ loafed there all the mf. \- Chart. \+ the boys with me most of the time \- The sky was cloudless \- the sea smooth, a slight luminous haze hanging over the surface of the water, which on to-days log is reported at 74° \- 11° warmer than the air \- no breeze but that made by the movement of the vessel \- I tried to work, but it was pleasanter to loaf in my chair \- I did not go down to lunch. After noon I read awhile \- then had my regular nap \- Then packed my trunk \- By 6 o'clk a fog came down upon us \+ for some time we had a solo on the fog whistle to which, for some time there was answer from a slower steamer going in our direction on the starbord side \- It was a wierd duett. Dinner at 7 \- Evg. in saloon reading, reading \- To bed early.
