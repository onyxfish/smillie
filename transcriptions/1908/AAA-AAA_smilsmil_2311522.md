## Left Page

### Fri. April 3, 1908

**Weather:** Cold & windy but bright  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold & windy but bright — B'kfs. at 8- left house soon after 9 & went to Pt. Tuttle's as he said he wd. like to see me — but my luck was the same as yesterday — I waited for half an hour for the patient ahead of me to come out. There I left & walked to studio — about a mile & a half — Then I worked on Port Marie & Marblehead neck getting ready for printing to-morrow. — Chas. Huntington came in & interfered with my work — I got home to lunch at 2 — Then went to Montross Gallery & saw the work of Ten Am. Painters — The work of Benson of Boston moves me greatly I think he is a rare artist. Then to studio & finished the work of the m/f & wet down paper for printing — Home just before 6 — Dinner alone — Jim dined with Croswell Evef. I wrote to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 4, 1908

Jim went to Ridgefield
Ralph came from New-Haven.

**Weather:** Brilliant day, unusually cold & high wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day, unusually cold & high wind — To studio before 10 & to work at once printing — Took infs from two small dungren plates for Waitents m/sf — poor plates — Then printed from Marblehead neck & Port Marie — The condition of M'kd. Nk. is unsatisfactory — I shall have to take out a portion of sky & re-etch it before I can get what I want — The Port Marie is better but will be greatly imp'd, I think, by an aqua-tint Tone over the whole of it. At 11 ock W. Tolfree called & I had a conference with him & Ryan upon a proposition to install a new heating plant & furnish steam heat to Tolfree, my neighbor — Ryan is to investigate & furnish figures — At 1 ock I had not finished my printing work — I went home & had lunch before 2 ock — Surprised to find Ralph there — came down to help a cold by change — his throat has been bothering him for some time — At 3 I ret'd to studio & completed my printing — cleaned up & got home a little before 6 — Rested till dinner — Ralph & I at table — Jim went with Bradford Boardman to Ridgefield to stay over Sunday with him — Century meeting but I stayed at home & spent the eveg. with Ralph.
