## Left Page

### Sun. March 22, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Veiled sunlight in the m'g - overcast after-
noon - I got up at 9 - sat with newspapers
till noon - then out to Riverside \+ wandered
with little comfort in the open spaces of the
Park - a sparing dinner at 2 - with Jim - I
made notes for a Revision of my Will \+ wrote to
Clarence B. Smith about it - I replied also to a note
rec'd a few days ago from Howard Thayer Kingsbury
asking me to second him as candidate for member-
ship in Century - a cup of malted milk, then with
Jim went to Carnegie Hall - Civic forum - \+ heard
Dr. Edwin A. Alderman, Pres't of University of Virginia
lecture on "The Growing South" - very carefully prepared.
He is optimistic \+ believes the South has wisdom enough
to meet the problems before her \+ gave many statistics
to prove how well she is doing - of course, the negro
looms large \+ in 40 years has shown wonderful
aptitude - nevertheless he is hopelessly inferior \+ in
time by stress of circumstances will be driven out. He
says the the number of blacks is already decreasing in
the south - He approved the disfranchising of the
negro \+ said that in time we must all come to the
practice of a limited suffrage - in this I agree with
him - we have here at the North too much suffrage
\+ it was wicked \+ my ist to extend it to the slaves -
Suffrage should be based up on Education \+
property holding - The audience was not a large
one \+ I thought it not very responsive - I thought
the Southerners with would turn out in force to wel-
come him \+ his statements \+ doctrines - She was
so loyal to the sentimental south they should have
done so - The discussion after the lecture was
spiritless -

## Right Page

### Mon. March 23, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wet \+ disagreeable - Up at usual time
\+ had bkfst with Jim - about 9 Dr. Tuttle
called me on 'phone \+ said he would like some
recent specimens of my urine, so, not with-
standing the weather I went to him with two
specimens which he said were encouraging as
showed a much fuller amt. of pus than the ones
before - I am greatly depressed by my con-
dition which is a new one to me \+ seems to pre-
sage a general breaking up - stomach, liver,
bowels \+ heart as well as kidney - of course he
tried to cheer me - that is his business - I ret'd
home \+ spent the rest of the day there - I
revised carefully some portions of Father's
Auto biography - but was not fit for any-
thing - my temp. is slightly above normal
all the time - does not go above 100/2 - I
have no desire to eat \+ have much dis-
comfort in my chest.
