## Left Page

### Wed. May 27, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Suffocatingly hot — Ther. 86°. \+ humidity 62°. I spent the m't at studio scraping and burnishing — trying to reduce the effect of the overbiting of aqua-tint. Home to lunch at 1.30 — after that I went to Wanamaker's \+ selected some plated ware as wedding presents from Jim \+ me to Belle Tanner (Jim's nurse) who is going to Ireland to be married. I got to Studio about 3.30 \+ made preparations to take a print from my plate to-morrow. Home at 6 after a short walk in Riverside Park. I found mounted photo's from Cullen — Now I shall complete the 1904 trip Albums for the boys. Dinner — In the ev'g we went to Colonial Theatre — 62? St. Broadway. Vaudeville — the first that I have seen in this city. I am amazed at the stuff that seems to amuse \+ satisfy the mass of people — The Theatre is large \+ crowded. A few of the programme items were amusing for one reason or another — some were execrable. Fred Karno's "Early Birds" presented. A night in the London Slums — a tattered \+ torn dirty looking mob of maybe a dozen who jumped about like cats, maauling \+ tumbling over one another, all in Pantomime — to me it was idiotic — but the audience laughed \+ seemed to be amused — Wm Rock \+ Maud Fulton did some amazing dancing — it was frantic — atmospheric conditions considered, I don't see how they could do it — at the end they were evidently distressed — Then we left \+ did not wait to see the rest of the bills — we walked home — To bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 28, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** Ther. fell to 62° at night. 56° at 11 o'clk P.M.

A great improvement in temperature — It continued very warm, tempered by pleasant breeze, Till noon — Then above 80° after which, in four hours it fell 12°. I got to studio before 10 \+ got to work at once printing. I took one imp. it was enough — The effect was disheartening — the tint prints very much heavier than I expected. So, I set to work at once with charcoal, scraper \+ burnisher to take out the sky \+ reduce the other parts as much as possible — Home to lunch at 1.30 — after it lay down on my lounge \+ slept soundly till 2.45. To studio at 3.30 \+ continued my scraping work Till 5 — A walk in Riverside \+ home at 6. Dinner with Jim at usual time. Isabel Tanner came in to say good bye \+ was quite overcome by her presents — she quite broke down — I hope she is making no mistake — She goes back to Ireland to marry an old bachelor.
Ev'g. Jim went out with the Dielmans — I arranged the new prints in the 1904 Albums.
