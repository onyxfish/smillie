## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 5, 1908

**Weather:** Overcast m\+ but clearing to a fine clear, breezy cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio before 10 \+ worked till near 2 o'clk. on Normandy Farm Yard. \- As I stand at my work \+ do not take any resting spells I am very tired by 1 o'clk \- it is after 2 when I get home \+ after 3 when I have had my lunch \- There is then but the small end of the daylight of a winter day \+ tired as I am I am able to make but little use of it \- the habit of taking a long nap is growing on me \- it may be economy in the long run but it seems, in view of all that I have laid out to do before I "peg out" an extravagant use of precious time \- I slept from 4 to 5 \- then some writing till dinner \- Jim dined out \- I dined solus \- At 8.30 I went to Century \- monthly meeting \- Old Jno. Bigelow (up in his nineties) presided \- In app\[ointing\] the nominating Com \- he stated that in one or another official capacity he had served the Century 40 years \+ now, with the weight of years upon him he begged for release \- After adjournment, in view of the Milton Tercentenary that will come in a few days, Prof. [illegible] of Columbia was called upon for an "appreciation" \- His appreciation was [illegible] in the extreme \- He placed Milton above Shakespeare \+ alongside of Homer \- Of course much "the most of it was" over my head \- he dwelt [illegible] upon technical matters of style \+ form in his poetry \+ prose that I could not understand \- he was evidently under the spell \+ blind to everything else \- I met many that I knew \+ had pleasant chats with most of them \- There was a full collection of pictures \- of rather low average I thought \- My prints of Smillie's Key \- Cal. seemed to hold its own modest[ly] among the others \- Home \+ to bed after midnight \- Gorgeous moonlight.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 6, 1908

**Weather:** Fair, but not pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- some sunshine in the m\+ \- then clouds \+ a nipping chill in the air that told of snow not far off \- I got my full quota of sleep by lying in bed till near 9 o'clk \- then a scour \+ [illegible] \- at about 10 \- after that with newspapers till after 12 \- Jim went to Church with Hugh Crowell \- I went out \+ had a tramp on Riverside \- in at 2 \- \+ had dinner \- The bulk of what remained of the afternoon was given to more sleep \- Up at 5 \- some writing. Eve\+ reading \+ work with photos.
