## Left Page

### Tues. May 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Jim went to Seagirt.*

Pleasant till evening \- alight sprinkle of rain at 6 p.m. I awoke at about 4 am much distressed in head & throat \- at 4.30 I called Jim who got up & left the house before 5 o'clk \- to go with his Co. to Seagirt to shoot at the Range there. \- It was 8 o'clk before I felt able to get up. The Trouble seems to be a form or mixture of catarrh in the head, some facial neuralgia & laryngitis. I have temp. a degree or two above normal \+ am unfit for anything \- I loafed away the day reading in a desultory sort away & doing a little writing. \- Jim came in at about 9 o'clk \- Reported a long, hard day with lots of work & little that he cared to eat \- He had a warm bath & soon got to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 20, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain all day. Again awake at 4 a.m. \- head & throat worse than yesterday m'f I had my b'kfst. in bed \- after 9 called up D. Tuttle to know if he could suggest any treatment of relief \- he advised small doses (1 gr. each) of Quinine, four times a day. Remembering constantly my three plates at the studio, ready for the acid, this condition is exasperating. Like a caged bear I got rid of the day \- I wrote a short letter to Bessie Throop \- went thro' a form of eating dinner with Jim &, surviving a stupid eve'g, got to bed early.
