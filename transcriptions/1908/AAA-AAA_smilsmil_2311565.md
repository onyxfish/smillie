## Left Page

### Sun. June 28, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day with refreshing breeze — very hot in the sunshine. I got up at about 7 \+ had my bath — a rest in bed \+ bkfs. at 9 — I found plenty to do in general gathering up \+ putting away \+ in folding garments selected \+ placing them beside my trunk — I have felt so much better to day that I am much encouraged altho' the mystery that surrounds Ralph's movements worry me — he sends no word \+ I don't know where he is — I had a short walk before dinner — \+ got an hours rest in the afternoon — Charl came at about 4 \+ at 6 we went to Walter Pierce's \+ saw him \+ Ninn — Even if I did not accomplish much \+ felt very tired with a return of unpleasant symptoms in throat \+ head —

## Right Page

### Mon. June 29, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph returned.

Hot day with little refreshment from any breeze — I felt very shaky on getting up \+ had my bkfs. before 8 — It was about 9.30 when Jim \+ I left the house — we went by Elevated to Christopher St. thence by tunnel to Hoboken \+ then to S.S. Kaiser Wm. where we interviewed the Obersteward \+ the desk steward \+ arranged satisfactorily for table \+ deck chair sets — Jim went down town \+ I ret'd home by way of 23d St. \+ to bank — I drew $100 \+ had it changed to english 20£. 6–6d. Then home at 12 \+ feeling much out of sorts I rested on my lounge till lunch at 1.30 — just before that Ralph came in, rather surprised that I should have worried because he was so tardy \+ gave no information concerning his movements — The afternoon \+ packing work, was a busy time for the boys — I too packed in a leisurely way — dinner at 6.30 — again the boys were hard at it — I got to bed early.
