## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 6, 1908

Braunschweig. The Deutschohof — Room 19 —

**Weather:** leaden sky + cold wind all day  **Temp:** [illegible]

leaden sky + cold wind all day — Up early + fixed every thing for our start at 12 — Then with the boys we left the hotel — took a Tram + in 20 m. Rode out to the Schloss Herrenhausen — went first into the Berggarten + wandered there for awhile — saw the great foelm house + the Mausoleum but did not enter either — Then, across the Road to the Schloss Garten — 120 acres — few people out + in the cold, grey light — every thing looked very dismal — we walked about getting some idea of extent + character — great fountain not playing — Then out + took Tram — an interesting ride alongside the great avenue of trees of the Eilenriede — to hotel at 11.30 — paid bill, paid every body in sight — went over to R.R. Station + started for Braunschweig at 12.10 — compartment to ourselves — uninteresting ride over a very flat country — got to Bs. soon after 1 o'clk + to the Deutsches Haus at 1.30 — shown to our Rooms at once — Then dinner — here they are so primitive as to have dinner in the middle of the day — at a set hour — diners sitting at long tables served regular Table d'hote style — Afternoon we went out + walked for a couple of hours thro' the old part of town which is very quaint + in parts picturesque — very many very old buildings — whole streets of them — there was no light for photographing but I secured a number of subjects to snap at if the light is good to-morrow — we went carefully thro' the Cathedral, all newly painted + decorated but it failed to move me — + down into the Crypt where Royalties lie, all with labels — I had a Rest before supper — eve'g I wrote to Charl.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 7, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal leaden grey m'g — but, at last, the afternoon brought us bright sunshine — letter from Charl + cards from K + E at our bed room doors when we got up — I had b'kft. alone — Then Replied to Charl — Then, when the boys were Ready, started out — the weather seemed so hopeless that I took a number of snaps with deadl't. at 4 or wide open. We carefully compared our work with Baedeker's directions + found, when we reached the hotel, that we had done almost every thing — in some things more — a short rest + at 1.30 a dinner that was very unsatisfactory to me — The sun shining, Ralph + I took another turn with the Camera, duplicating some of the best subjects of the m'g — Back to hotel + at 3.30 we started on a ride about town — we drove out the Steinweg to the Hof Theatre then out Kaiser Wilhelms St. passing many beautiful + modern Residences + out beyond the limits of our map into what seemed to be an embryo park — the Roads were good — the trees small — large areas were nearly free nurseries — the air was delightful + the sky cloudless which in small measure reconciled me to the lack of interest in the drive — circling about to the east of the city we drove a long way out over the Wolfenbuttler Str. a bumpety stone paved Road, without any interest whatever to us; to Richmond which seemed to be a Restaurant Pleasure Resort, I, so far as I could understand, we caught sight of the chateau of some titled person — Returning over the same Road we got to the Bürger Park, passing more embryo park — we drove thro' the Park — very pretty in Parts, thro' Lisen bahn took on to Brucktor from — past Sieges Pl. — around the August Thor from to the Wend mühlen bg. which we climbed + had a ble. at the city — Then to Monument Pl. + around by the Palace + to hotel at 5 — the sunshine was so bright that I took another snap — then bought some photos. Then I gave up — dinner disagreed so with me that I ate no evening meal — the boys went to a Rathskeller for theirs — Eve'g in my Room — Jim Read aloud for awhile —
