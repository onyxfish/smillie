## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 28, 1908

**Weather:** mixed weather - still mild - a little rain \+ threats of more  **Temp:** [illegible]

After help. I had the satisfaction of untangling my bank acct. \+ made the bank's figures \+ my check stub agree, as usual there was no error, no real difficulty. The trouble came of my unfamiliarity with figures \+ accts. Got my bill from Ryan for heating apparatus which after a month of impatient waiting is barely finished. we had a meeting that was not cordial. I told him that I wished he had been as prompt in getting his job done as he was in sending me his bill. I fussed among old unbound books that many years ago were pub. serially. Home to lunch after 1 o'clk. Afternoon selecting photo prints from last summer's work to make souvenir albums for the boys. Evef a friend of Jim's came in — Bissell - an old Morse school fellow. He interested us telling of his travels in Ceylon, India, China \+ other countries of the far East.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 29, 1908

**Weather:** Disagreeable day - cloudy m/f - Rain falling before noon \+ continued through the night  **Temp:** [illegible]

At times heavily. To studio at 10 - took my check for $638.99 to Ryan, payment for new heater ($600) \+ other repairs. R. has treated me shabbily in this transaction but it will not better matters to quarrel with him. I fussed away what remained of the m/f in arranging Roberts' Holy Land, China, Belgium \+ other works, that I have in numbers, for the binder. Home at 2. lunch. a good part of afternoon writing letter to Mary \+ Katharine jointly. Dinner. Evef went to 127 E. 69 - Wm Woodin's \+ called on Fanny Mulford Connel. I have not seen her for many years - she looked very well for 66 yrs. \+ evidently is very happy - proud of her boys of whom she talked much. Mr. W. was ill. I did not see her. Mr. W. was out. I had a drabbly time going \+ coming - owing to discontinuance of transpers I had to take three cars, going \+ coming - six changes \+ had to wait in rain for each one.
