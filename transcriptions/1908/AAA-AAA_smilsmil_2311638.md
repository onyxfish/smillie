## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 25, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day - mixed weather, rain, fog & slime. I got up before 7 after a restless night owing to discomfort in the region of right kidney, so that I could find no easy position, sitting or lying. I consulted Dr. Tuttle over the phone. I got to studio at about 10 o'clk & painted till 1 on the Mignot restoration, getting all the broken parts painted over - by their painting I greatly modified the red hot tone that pervaded the whole painting. I think it is now better than it ever was before & if Mr. Mignot is where he can take cognisance of what I have done I feel quite confident of his approval. On my way home to lunch I went to Clausen's \+ gave orders to send to Dr. Tuttle's for frames to repair & regild. Afternoon was mostly given to assorting \+ trimming photo prints - Ralph came before 5 o'clk — we had dinner at the usual time - Eve'g the boys went to the Half ale Club \+ I continued my work with the photos.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 26, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Continued mild, damp, foggy weather - I got up \+ had bkfst. before 9 - The boys did not get up till near noon - I spent the ev'g writing up a number of arrears - With the boys I had a short walk before dinner. Chas. was to have dined with us to-day but when I called him up on phone I found he was in bed with one of his characteristically violent attacks of influenza. Old Kate had made especial efforts to score a success with her dinner \+ was greatly disappointed because of the absence of C - The dinner was a very ample \+ good one but the boys in their attacks upon it made but little impression on its bulk - I had to take a nap after it. Ralph ret'd to New-Haven on the 8 p.m. train \+ after that Jim went to Charl's \+ saw him — he was in sad plight but recovering - I spent the eve'g alone quietly.
