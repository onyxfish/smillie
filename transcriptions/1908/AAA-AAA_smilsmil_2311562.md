## Left Page

### Mon. June 22, 1908

**Weather:** Hot, but somewhat modified after yesterday  **Temp:**

\- Up before 7 & had a little walk before bkfs'. after 8 - The mg was spent lounging on piazza after a short walk north on the road from the house to see the process of tarring the road bed - lunch about 1 o'clk. At 4 Katie & I went down Town & spent some time looking at the Niemeyer paintings - there we met Miss Walker & her friend Mr. White - we left the blf at 6 & wandered thro' the campus where we met Jim in visited Ralph in his room
his outrageous trennial rig - Then we went on to Buyer Hall, Taking Mr. White with us to some sort of a reception - While there a commotion was made by the entrance of Wm Taft - he was rec. with college cheers & a little ovation - we had a chance to hear his jolly laugh - it was nearly 7 when we got home & found Roger Adams & his daughter Ann just arv. - also Ralph - we had dinner at once & soon after Ralph & I went to the Glee Club concert which I enjoyed greatly - Every number was encored & some of the greatest hits were among the pieces given on encores - The grotesque & absurd College & darkie songs were the favorites - T.C. Fowler distinguished himself by acting as well as singing & C. A. Lehmann exhibited a very fine bass voice - it was almost 11 when the concert was over - I walked up home & got in at 11.20 -

## Right Page

### Tues. June 23, 1908

**Weather:** A red hot day - Ther. above 90°  **Temp:**

a crashing thunder storm from 2.30 to 1.30 - then clearing to a suffocating afternoon - At 10.30 I went to general meeting of Yale Alumni in Alumni Hall - Pres. Hadley's address was interesting - The Presiding officer was Hart Lyman of N.Y., Class of '73 - a lawyer - witty & amusing. There was an ovation & applause given to the retiring Dean, Prof. My Farles Wright. Rev. Wm S. Hubbell, Class of 58, made a remarkable address - charming in its easy run, full of interesting reminiscences & anecdote - he spoke for the members of his class, 25 or 30 of whom were on the platform with him - Then Gardener Richards on, Representing Jim's class, spoke - He was badly scared - Then a son of Genl. Tecumseh Sherman spoke - Then Horace Taft, a brother of "Big Bill" argued that the work in Prep. Schools was not broad enough - that American boys were two or three years behind English, German & French boys - I Ret. to Katie's soon after 12 - A tremendous Thunder storm broke over us while we were at lunch - the lightning & thunder were unusually severe - the rain came down in a deluge & for a time it seemed as tho' the ball game must be put over to another day - The sun shone out before 2 o'clk. & we started for the ballground - the usual procession of cars - The great crowds - hetrogeous absurd crowds of grotesque uniforms on the returning classes - We got to the ground at about 3 - The roads & grounds were in surprisingly good condition - The march of classes around the field was unusually animated - Wm Taft marched twice around the field with his class & created great excitement - at last the game started - Yale scored one on the first inning - after that it was a long array of 0's till the 7 "inning when Yale scored two runs - on the 8 "neither side scored & Harvard failed to score on the 9" - Then Yale broke loose - it was a wild time - Immediately after the game there was a mock Automobile race - Small toy machines, into which a small man could perch were pushed about an improvised ring - a motley howling crowd following - now & then a machine & rider would be spilled - I got back to the Elkins soon after 6, or about 6. & had a good rest before dinner at 7 - Ralph dined with us but left soon after - To bed soon after 9 -

In answer to my question I was surprised when Wm Elkin said that he had done nothing to get Commencement ticket for me.
