## Left Page

### Fri. March 20, 1908

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - Bkft. with Jim + at 9 o'clk went to D. Tuttle's - he had me strip + feet carefully over abdomen, spleen, liver + kidney - tenderness pretty much every where - part from clogging of gall bladder + kidney - also from lodgement of gas in various pockets - I did not get much encouragement from him - I did not expect it I know as well as he does that it is an "interesting case". From his office I walked to my studio + put in some new work on the neck of the Goldsmith's Daughter in a way that was profoundly discouraging. After 12 I started for home walking a considerable part of the way - lunch at 2 - reading, loafing napping thro' the afternoon - was interviewed by Karsten's (grocer) clerk begging that I wd. renew my trade there - later got a note from K. begging - but I refused - Dinner with Jim at 6.30 - Evef together - I wrote to Raphael + to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 21, 1908

**Weather:** Delightful day - cloudless - quiet + crisp  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day - cloudless - quiet + crisp. Last night was a trying one owing in part to a degree or more above normal temp, + partly to the discomfort in right Kidney, preventing me from lying easily in certain positions - At 5 am. temp 100 2°. - I got up + had bkft. with Jim. Soon after 9 went to D. Tuttle's taking samples of urine. He is anxious that the partial blockade of right kidney shd. be raised + spoke significantly of a possible extirpation operation - I told him that I knew the risk wd. be great but I felt willing to take the risks - I then went to Safe Deposit got my Will + then back home after 11 - I slept soundly for more than an hour before lunch at 1.30 After lunch I revised my will made before Jim was of age - too many complicated provisions - now, as R. will come of age, July 8th I wish to annihilate that event - pay him his Mother's legacy + make Jim + him executors + putting them purely in possession of the estate immediately after my death.
Then I went out + loafed in the sunshine in Riverside Park - still feeling very seedy + with temp. of 100°
