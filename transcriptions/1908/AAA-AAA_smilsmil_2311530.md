## Left Page

### Sun. April 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A poor Sunday for Easter bonnets \+ other finery — a little rain \+ much threatening — I got to studio at about 10.30 \+ had a good time completing the washing of a lot of printing cloths \+ grinding ink for to-morrow. Home in time for dinner with Jim \+ Ralph at 2 o'clk. — I had awafs before Charle came in from 5 to 6.30 when he left — then supper — Eve\[ning\] Ralph \+ I went to Peoples Institute Meeting at Coopers Union. Fr. Cha\[rles\] P. Fagnanie held forth on God \+ Mammon — He is a fluent speaker but strains his voice unpleasantly. He evidently held the attention of his audience and seemed to please them but his address was a disappointment to me — he is a "labor socialist" \+ I think failed completely in his logic — He defined care fully the terms God \+ Mammon \+ then proceeded to show that under our present bakelie social system we are all, rich \+ poor alike, worshippers of Mammon — Every one is trying to get ahead of his neighbor instead of loving \+ serving him. The cure, it would seem, must lie in everyone being so it that he does not in any way interfere with his neighbors' interests — \+ such a condition w\[ould\] alter materially the struggle for life — it would no longer be the survival of the fittest but an altruistic struggle to help the other fellow before helping yourself — a millennium of good fellowship — a thing in our "social system" beyond possibility of description. I do not see how such theorizing can help the deplorable condition of things — it is at variance with the fundamental conditions of our existence — a number of questions were put by persons in the audience — some of them shrewdly puzzling — but F. downed them all in short order.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 20, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, threatening mg., but clearing fine in the afternoon. — I got to studio at about 9.35 \+ set to work immediately printing from "Chapel near Lugano" \+ "Frauenkirche, Dresden" — Every thing worked well \+ I got good proofs — four. from Chapel — \+ two from Frauenkirche — The paper I used was some fine old French charcoal \+ crayon paper brought from Paris years ago by A.H. Baldwin — it is beautiful in texture \+ color — I shall work upon these pfs. with white chalk \+ then produce the effect by aqua-tint gr? I had my lunch in studio before 2 o'clk — cleaned up \+ left at 4.30 — Home at 5 \+ had a rest till 6 — dinner at 6.30 Eve\[ning\], Jim \+ Ralph went to theatre — I was too tired to go with them \+ spent the eve\[ning\] at home.
