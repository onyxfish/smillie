## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 27, 1908

**Weather:** Pleasant day growing cold & windy at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 — made along morning-time divided between preparing asphaltine varnish — varnishing tray or sink in acid room looking for p[illegible] of plates that I am going to work upon \+ working upon plate "Pont Marie, Paris — etched three years ago. I propose to put an aqua-tint over it — It was almost 2 o'clk. when I left studio \+ consequently very late when I had lunch. After that wrote a letter to Roth, Florence. About 4.30 I went out \+ had a two mile walk on Riverside — on Return I found a note from W. McKesson making an appointment to meet me at studio at 3 o'clk. this afternoon — the note was mailed this m\[orning\]. Dinner with Jim at 7 — then he went to drill — I spent the evening reading \+ writing —

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 28, 1908

**Weather:** Clear, bright day — very cold & windy — After [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Had a talk with W. McKesson over the 'phone — He called at studio yesterday \+ saw George who explained to him the results that w\[oul\]d follow taking the coat of varnish from off his picture — This m\[ornin\]g he told me to go ahead — Rec\[eive\]d a note from D. Appleton \+ Co — saying they w\[oul\]d sell etched plate, Cedars by moonlight for $10 — I sent check — Rec\[eive\]d note from Clarence Smith informing me that tax assessors had raised assessment on my studio Bldg — to $20000 — This is about $4000 more than it cost me — It is an imposition that makes me sick — I wrote to Smith instructing him to fight it — It was 10.30 when I got to studio — I poured alcohol over face of the [illegible] — Painting \+ the varnish rolled off like molasses — I was amazed — It cleared up very successfully — There was no glazing or thin painting to be disturbed. Then I got to work on plate "Pont Marie" \+ was engaged on that till 1.30 — Home at 2.15 lunch — wrote letter to Ernest Roth — Out for a walk at 5 — Too cold \+ windy for comfort — in [illegible] an hour — Dinner with Jim at 7 — Eve\[nin\]g he went to see [illegible] Writing after miserable with a cold — I wrote to Ralph \+ a second note to Clarence Smith.
