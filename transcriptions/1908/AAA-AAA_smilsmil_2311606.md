## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 20, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely mtf., singularly clear - southerly wind with a refreshing coolness in the air — but all of this passed away in the early afternoon, after which we had mist \+ rain. During the mtf. I loafed about on the Boat Deck, sunning myself \+ reading Stalky \&Co. Kipling's story of three preternaturally cunning, knowing boys who, led by Stalky, devoted their energies to harassing their masters in some sort of a prep. school \+ bedevilling the others in their form — ultimate success always following every event — at the end all attain eminence or distinction by the same tactics — disRespect of authority — Stalky being the shining light \+ a model martial Briton — It reads to me in parts very like an autobiography — it is full of school slang \+ jargon — the class of school — a prep. for the military service, is one that I do not know of — The book is mildly interesting only to a foreigner \+ in moral effect, not above criticism — got some snaps shots from the deck above the bridge of Charl \+ the boys on the boat deck — much of the afternoon was given to sleeping \+ a little to gym. Dinner at 7 — we't in the Social Room (Reading) on the upper deck — a charming Room — ceiling unusually high — 9 or 10 feet or more — with tall, curtained windows \+ a sky light — many easy chairs \+ lounges — nothing suggestive of a steamship about it — For passengers, in spite of the great number carried this is the most commodious boat I have ever traveled on — the spaces for general use, outside \+ in, are large \+ amply supplied with comforts — there is, however, a tremor that is felt through out the boat — I read \+ slept in my chair till 10 o'clk — then chatted awhile with Charl before going to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 21, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mixed weather all day — some sunshine — some fog — some rain \+ some sea — just enough to give a gentle pleasant swing to this big boat that some, unfortunately for them, found discomforting. I passed the day in a loafing way — I finished W.W. Jacobs' Dialane Story — Do not like it so well as some of his shorter stories that I have read — Tried to get another book to my liking but had, for want of better, to put up with "Shorty McCabe" — I was twice in the gym. \+ I had a nap mtf. \+ afternoon — at night I went to bed at 10 o'clk. —
