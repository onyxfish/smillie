## Left Page

### The Marathon Race — Fri. July 24, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A remarkably fine day- unusually warm \+ sunny with little movement of the air - I felt shaky when I got up but nothing of that kind could shake my determination to but in The Olympic Stadium at the close of the Race - with the boys went by tube \+ got to Franco-British exbt about 10.30 - we at once went to the booking booths to get tickets for the afternoon to avoid the crowding but were told that no p.m. tickets wd. be sold before 1.30 thus insuring a crush - We went to the picture Galleries \+ loafed there till 12.30 - then to a Restaurant \+ after much trouble got something to eat - At 2.0 we went to the booths \+ took place on an immense queue - the Englishmen saying it was rotten management - at 1.30 sale began \+ in time we got our 2/6 seats - then the crowd went rushing back \+ forth, under contrary directions from policemen \+ employees, looking for the proper gate to enter - stopping at last in a jam where we waited till the gate was opened - then a suffocating crush \+ as much rushing as wd. be found at home - once in under the Stadium seats we were held for some time at another bar but at last we were projected up a stairway \+ out into the great Stadium \+ then another rush for seats - we were able to get excellent ones. It was then near 2.30 - time for opening exercises - but the surrounding crowd was intense enough - then came swimming Races - high diving - Pole vaulting, hurdle \+ Relay Racing - all going on at the same time - by 4 o'clk. The vast arena was packed with humans - The greatest spectacle of its kind that I ever saw - impossible to describe adequately - a beautiful sight under the sunshine - gay colored parasols - light dresses \+ straw hats - 5 m. before 5 o'clk - order by megaphone to clear the track for the end of the Marathon Race - at intervals announcements had been made of the progress of the 57 Racers - the No's of the three leaders being given - Up to within 3 or 4 miles no No's of Americans had appeared - then came the No. of Hayes. U.S.A. 3rd. The suspense \+ excitement was positively painful - at last, two guns to signal that the leader, Dorando, an Italian, was within a mile of the Stadium gate - Hefferon, So. Africa 2d Hayes U.S.A. 3rd then a Rocket signalled that they were entering the outside grounds. The crowd was hushed - then a rush of officials at the entrance on the side opposite to us \+ the finish - then a little tottering figure appeared \+ a roar went up - it was Dorando - he reeled in onto the track, at once it was evident that he was on the verge of collapse - a few yards \+ he fell - he could not get up his but he was helped up - a little farther he fell again - four times he fell - once near us - attendants rubbed his chest, his arms \+ his legs - to set him on his feet - his eyes were closed - he could nt stand alone - it was a heart rending sight - within a few feet of the Tape he fell for the last time, unable to move - then Hayhes entered - at a good gait - when he got to where the Shield d on his breast could be recognized a cry of consternation went up - "Its an American!" - the English were sure it was Hefferon of So. Africa - they could submit to his beating, he, a Colonist, but not an American - they already had more than their share - on he came - Dorando was picked up and pushed over the Tape then put on a stretcher \+ carried out. Soon after, Hefferon came in - The Italian flag was run up \+ under it the U.S. flag - all about were protests that it was not a fair win as Dorando had been carried over -

## Right Page

### Sat. July 25, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast mf. but bright afternoon - we got up rather later than usual \+ spent some time reading the various accts of yesterday's race. Then, needing more money we went to a Cook's Office 22 High Holborn, \+ drew £50 - then I went to Brit. Mus. to get information \+ unexpectedly met Mr. Colvin - He left two gentlemen, came to me \+ spent several minutes with me explaining some of the plans to keep prints \+ drawings protected agst dust - he was much interested in my acct of yesterdays Race - then he put me in charge of one of the attaches who proved to be one of the young men who had written up ruins in 1892-3, \+ to whom I had presented a pen knife on parting - He gave me fullest description of the several ways in which the Museum prints are kept - I took notes - Rel'd to Hotel at 1.30 \+ had lunch with the boys - They then went away \+ I had a nap - then a long time writing - Eve'g I had a light dinner in Restaurant - The boys at dinner Table d'hote - Hayes, the Marathon winner dined here with a number of friends - His trophy, a bronze figure, recumbent, of the original Marathon Runner - Eveñg loafing in Palm Room.
