## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 26, 1908

**Weather:** Bright m'f, sunny & mild. Disagreeable afternoon — clouded. Raw, East wind wet night —  **Temp:** [illegible]

I had little sleep before 3 A.m. after that I slept till 9.15 then up & had bkfs. At 12 Jim & I went out & walked till near dinner at 2. Afternoon Charl came in after 4 \+ stayed till 6 — at 6.30, supper — Eve'g Reading — greatly disappointed because I could not go to Cooper Union \+ hear Rev. Tho. Slicer's closing lecture. I did not think it safe for me to go out in such weather.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 27, 1908

13th anniversary of Annie's death.

**Weather:** Day at times clouded — a portion of the time bright sunshine, cold. Raw & windy when overcast.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up feeling seedy but drove my self out, determined not to give up yet. Went to studio — a horrible mess there made first by carpenters & then by plasterers — I worked about accomplishing but little, till near 1 o'clk — Then to Miller's & to Douglass & home at 1.30 feeling that I had been thro' a hard struggle — ate a light lunch — Rec'd Warrant from City Marshal threatening to levy for arrearage of Personal Tax amounting to $7.97 — I sent check. In about 3/4 of an hour after lunch I had severe pains in stomach that completely used me up for the rest of the day & eve'g. Eve'g Ernest & Phœbe Whiting came to dinner as invited — I could not see them — Jim did the honors — Kate got up a fine dinner.
