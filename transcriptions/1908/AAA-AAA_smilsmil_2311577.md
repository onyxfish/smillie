## Left Page

### Wed. July 22, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day — after a much disturbed \+ very uncomfortable night I was satisfied in the m'f that the wisest course for me would be to remain in bed. nausea with a feeling of tenderness, almost pain in the bowels led me to think that I was probably in a catarrhal condition — after 8 I rang my bell \+ asked the chamber maid to call Ralph — he soon came, went out \+ altho' it was only 8.30 he had to walk nearly to Holborn before he found a store open where he could get Castor oil \+ Horlick's Milk — I took the oil \+ was thoroughly purged — about 1 I had tea \+ toast with Horlick served to me in bed — about 8 I had some more Horlick \+ toast — a lady-like young woman — an attaché of the Hotel made a sympathetic call \+ said she w'd be glad if I would allow her to do any possible thing for my comfort — the chamber maids too were sympathetic — from 9 to 10 I was in Jim's Room while my room was being aired \+ my bed remade — Then to bed for the night. The boys went to the Zoo in Regent's Park in the m'f \+ to the Stadium in the afternoon —
Rec'd letters from Chas. \+ Mary —

## Right Page

### Thur. July 23, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another pleasant day — after a quiet restful night I got up at 8.30 but soon found that I was not yet out of the woods — occasional spasms of pain passed thro' my stomach \+ the bowels were unexpectedly active — I determined to keep as quiet as possible thro' the day. I ate a very light bkfs't \+ then spent much of the m'f on my back in my room — at 1.30 I had something to eat — wrote to Charl \+ then 2½ hours on my back — I have arranged at the office that hereafter I shall have my dinners a la carte in the Restaurant; the boys having their dinner at table d'hote — I had my meal at 6.30
