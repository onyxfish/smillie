## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 20, 1908

**Weather:** Bright day, windy + rather cold, growing milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright day, windy + rather cold, growing milder - Got a note from Raefsn explaining requirements in the case of a Student Apprentice seaman in the Conn. Naval Volunteers - He says he has made careful enquiries + assures me there need be no fear of entanglements - So, I signed the papers + sent them to him - It was rather late when I got to studio + I spent the rest of the m'f working on my printing press to get it in condition for printing - It was near 2 when I got home to lunch - after it I went to Am. Art Ass'n where a collection of paintings, property of the late Mr. Hunt is on expon prior to sale - I was told that a painting by me was among others - found it to be a High Sierra view painted about 35 years ago - not a bad thing for me - Then I went to Vantine's + other places + found at O'Neill a Minn. waiting for me - they stayed till near 6 - then I had a short rest - Dinner alone - Jim did not come home - there was a Regimental drill in honor of Maj. Hoff's 50 anniversary in the Regt - I got there rather late but as luck would have it I got in upon the floor by Jim who was doing guard duty - I stood by him for some time watching the drill - then up stairs + took a look thro' the Co. Rooms - then, after the parade was over I went thro' the fine large rooms on the main floor \+ at 10.30 started for home getting there about 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 21, 1908

**Weather:** Pleasant + very mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Funeral of E. C. Stedman.

Pleasant + very mild - Started for studio about 10 - stopped at Miller's to learn why work had not been started on faking out dumb waiter - then to Kobbleson's to get a boy to come to studio to clean rust off from my printing press - he did it come as promised - I worked at sorting pamphlets \+ papers to pack away in closet. The elevator men came + lost no time in getting the dumb waiter out - I got home to lunch before 2 o'clk - + back to studio at 3 - carpenters had started work in Maynard's Room laying floor in the dumb waiter shaft - at 4 with Geo. went to the Church of the Messiah - Park Ave. + 34th St. to funeral of E. C. Stedman - Centurions gathered in the lecture Room then entered the church in procession + had seats in middle aisle - the bldg. was crowded - the service was simple + impressive - Dr. Collyer spoke + then Hy Van Dyke - both of these were evidently deeply moved - After service I went home - 5.30 o'clk - I had dinner alone as Jim's work keeps him late so that he does not get home till about 8 - At 7.30 I went to Am. Art Ass'n thinking that the Hunt sale was to take place there - a mistake - the sale of the paintings takes place to-morrow eve'g. I got home at about 9 - Read newspapers \+ to bed.
