## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 21, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very fine day \- It was after 10.30 when I got to studio \- I got to work on Normandy Farm-Yard \+ worked till near 1: At glazing heavily \+ bringing things together in preparation for another painting. The central group of trees is too much chewed up in handling \- It must be simplified \+ treated more broadly \- \+ there are parts out of key in color \- I am primed to get at it again \- About 1 oclk. went to Clausen saw H. Tuttle's "Louis Micjust" nits frame \+ gave some final directions \- Met there \- Dawson \- an English Painter \+ Stchres. Home to lunch with Ralph \- Afternoon \- with Kate \+ Katie engaged in a bug hunt \- we have been much Troubled lately \+ from all that I can learn the house is infested \- we found an unpleasant lot of game not only in crevices \+ joints of the bedstead but on the wall back of it \+ up, even to the ceiling \- It makes me anxious to leave the house, but my lease till next Oct. binds me. Dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. boys went to theatre \- I read Weitzenhamfel \+ wrote till bed-time

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 22, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, raw day. Threatening snow \- To studio at 10 \+ worked on Normandy Farm Yard till 2 o'clk \- Now I am wondering, after having reduced many crudely strong \+ blackish parts if I have not emasculated the work by bringing the parts more into harmony \+ color \- A clearly defined purpose \+ direct work is my ideal in painting \- This is impossible without knowledge aided by experience \- Knowledge that comes by study directed by genius \- \+ experience gained by years of patient work \- These conditions not being present \- I fail \+ am fully conscious of it \- and yet, I persist \- which is unreasonable \- Home at 2.30 \+ had lunch with Ralph \- Read till after 4 \- then a nap \- Dinner \- Evef. the boys went to the Yale Club \+ I continued my Reading \-
