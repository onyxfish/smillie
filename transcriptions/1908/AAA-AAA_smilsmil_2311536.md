## Left Page

### Fri. May 1, 1908

Annie's Birthday.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold wintry day with bleak, high wind Ther. down to 40°. After bkfs. I went to the bank, attended to business there, then walked to studio getting there at about 11. The studio was very cold — but I busied my self with some active work till 12.30 then home walking a good part of the way altho' suffering discomfort in my right side. but feeling that I must have exercise \+ out-door air — lunch at 2 o'clk — Soon after that I went down to Rector St., office of Kneeland \+ Perry \+ got from Miss Murphy the last of the type written pages of Father's autobiography — Then to Clarence Smith, taking a deed \+ other papers concerning Studio Bldg. — He was in a great hurry to catch a 4 o'clk. Train at Grand Central \+ we rushed in a hurry for Subway \+ together we went up town — he got to 42d. S. with time to spare \+ Soon after he left I found that I had left my type written pages in Smith's office — at 72d. St. I took Return Train got my pkg. \+ again uptown getting home at 5. I had a rest before dinner — Evg. I wrote a letter to Mary.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 2, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not so cold out of doors as yesterday but colder in the studio — After bkfs. I wrote to Ralph, made out checks to tradesman \+ did not get to studio till about 11 — Ther. down to 60°. but I worked for an hour \+ a half fitting at mat to use in printing "Chapel, Lugano" \+ "Frauenkirche" — in filing \+ burnishing plate edges \+ in grinding up some old ink — Then I walked up to 42d S. Subway Exp. to 72 \+ walked the rest of the way home — It is almost painful for me to walk. the discomfort in my right side is so great — a see-saw game seems to be going on between my gall bladder \+ my right kidney — now the kidney seems to be sub-siding \+ the gall bladder is coming up — I fight agt. myself — I will not admit that chronic invalidism is up on me — but how can I dispute the evidence of the past winter when I have rarely felt well enough to enjoy living for more than two or three days at a time — I fight agt. the depression — the wearisomeness of it — but its presence is more than morbid weakness — it is a physical fact present most of the time in discomfort that is often actual pain — lunch before 2 — at about 4 o'clk. I went out to Riverside \+ wandered there till light Rain came on. Dined alone — Jim had his dinner with Gil Kinney — Evg. Reading.
To-night was Century Meeting, but the Rain, that soon passed, offered me an excuse.
