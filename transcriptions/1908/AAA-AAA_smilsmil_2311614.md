## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 6, 1908

**Weather:** A mild, pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mild, pleasant day — I worked at home till about 10 o'clk, numbering \+ arranging my last summer negatives then to studio \+ saw Ryan \+ arranged with him the figures to be submitted to Mr. Tolfree as compensation for furnishing steam for heating his stable (or garage) next adjoining my building — $15 per month for not less than five months each winter — not binding for more than one year — This amt. covers fuel, attendance \+ a small rental. — Then home to lunch at 1.30 — Afternoon continued my work on negatives — a short walk before dinner. Evg. with Jim went to New-Amsterdam Theatre \+ saw the Merry Widow — Amusing but silly — in no way an incentive to better things — Why is it not possible to have some amusement of this kind — plays — that are light, laughter-moving, witty, funny, wholesome? We have to go back to Gilbert \+ Sullivan. — Sonia, the widow, was Georgia Caine \+ the Prince was Chas. Meakins. I had considerable fever when I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 7, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded day with some threats in the n'th but later the clouds broke away — I got up at 5 o'clk \+ took a Castor Oil cock-tail \+ at about 8 I was rewarded — I felt very limb all day — It was near 10 o'clk. when I had bkfs. —
I continued work on negatives — many of them are imperfect \+ I am tempted to fake improvements in one way \+ another — In some cases the films were defective to begin — then many are imperfectly developed — \+ in others I have been at fault in attempting to get impossible subjects or in blundering some way — Lunch at 1.30 — At 4 I went to barbers \+ had my hair cut — Then to studio \+ home at 6.30 dinner — Evg. at home, reading \+ writing.
