## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 14, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

As dismal a day as could well be imagined — what a mockery after the lovely moonlight last night — It has misted, nizzled \+ rained all day long — After bkft. went to Ham-Am. Office \+ got my New-York receipt exchanged for my steamer ticket \+ then got instructions concerning baggage — Inquiring about the Roll of steamer rugs sent from London July 31. to be delivered on board S.S. Americ. at Hamburg Sept. 17 I was advised to go to the Dept. of Baggage, directions being given — went there in a cab \+ could get no trace, on their books in the office or among piles of refuse in the warehouse — I was promised that a telegram w°. be sent to London \+ I was advised to write at once (tho' it seems to me too late) to the London Office — Ret'd to hotel \+ wrote — Lunch at 1.30 — Loafed about, suffering from cold till after 3 — Then out with the boys, bought a couple of little presents for Kate Leonard \+ Katie — Then down by St. Nicholas Vegetable market in the open Square — immense display — also of fruit — I found some good subjects on canals for photos. I left the boys, ret'd to hotel \+ had a nap before dinner at 7.30 — Early I bundled up in my own Room \+ Read —

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 15, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charles arrived.

A dubious mng. — that cleared at last into a beautiful day — Up at 7 — bkfst. alone at 8 — Later the boys came down \+ soon after 10 we went to the Gebec Office of the Ham-Am. — To learn if any trace had been found of the Roll of Steamer rugs — No reply had yet been Rec'd. to Telegram of yesterday — but later in the day I rec'd. Telephone message from the office saying that the Roll w'd. be delivered on board at Southampton — So, for two days I shall be at sea without my Ulster \+ rugs — Then I left the boys \+ prowled off alone with my camera — There was some beautiful sunshine, I found some good bits \+ got some satisfactory shots — Back to hotel soon after 1 o'clk. \+ had lunch — Then a half hour rest, \+ at 3 we went to the Binnen Alster Landing \+ took boat to Winterhude, the farthest environs reached by the boats — went ashore \+ looked about, finding nothing especially interesting we ret'd. by boat the boys stopping at the Restaurant Fährhaus \+ I went on to hotel.
