## Left Page

### Fri. March 6, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day of wet, sloppy snow - 2 or 3 in of it - I did not go out of the house, busied my self trying to arrange my 1904 photos of European Trip - that being my first photo excursion I did not manage it upon a liberal plan - I developed some, Boorman dev. some \+ Cullen did some \+ I attempted to print all - it was a mix up \+ failure - I mounted a number on gray boards, now I am trying to get good prints \+ have them mounted to correspond with my Southern last years' inks - also to get the boys' albums for 1904 complete. Jim did not come home to dinner, so, I was entirely alone all day \+ even. - Jim has a special parade drill to-night.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 7, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clearing day - not altogether pleasant. To studio at 10 \+ spent the in[illegible] till near 1 working on Marblehead Neck. I stoned, scraped \+ charcoaled the distant sea \+ sky giving, I hope, a quality of atmosphere not before in the plate. - Then I went to the bank - attended to some business there - copied portions of studio deed for the use of Clarence Smith - then home to lunch at 2.30 - Before 4 o'clk. I went to the new buildings of the College of the City of N.Y. \+ saw the large mural painting in the great Assembly Hall by Blashfield - met several pleasant people - B- was there. Nielman introduced me to the Pres. of the College Dr. Jno. H. Finley - He said Prof. H. Van Dyke had given him a [illegible] of the book plate I etched for him with autograph verse concerning Mow Bray my self - The boy, the book \+ the fish he did not catch - Had dinner alone - Jim went to a fraternity dinner — Even I went to Century, monthly meeting - after business Hopkinson Smith addressed the members at request of Pres. Bigelow - He pictured the good old times \+ then, in contrast, dwelt upon the dreadful decadence of the present - later I asked him if he was preparing for the ministry.
