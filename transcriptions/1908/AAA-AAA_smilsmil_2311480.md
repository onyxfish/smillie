## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 10, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant cold day, very inspiriting. To studio at about 10 \+ spent the m'f in clearing out one of the closets there that has become completely clogged \+ cluttered up. Home at 1.30 \- lunch. After 3 went down town To Cullen's with the two photo albums. Found that Mr. C. C. is at home with grip. Left albums \+ then went to 21 S Fate S\- \+ saw Clarence Smith to see how garage matters were progressing \- nothing doing \- Home at 5 o'clk. At 6.30 Gil Kinney came \+ we had dinner \- he left at 9 o'clk. I wrote a short letter to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 11, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wild, pleasant day, easterly wind threatening change of weather. To subway. 79 S\- at 10 \- Trains ran no further south than 42d S\- owing to fire at 4th Ave. \+18th St. there was fear that falling wires would crush in roof of subway \- great congestion \+ delay. I was held up about half an hour \- on getting to studio I worked till 1 o'clk. clearing up painting corner \+ closet \- then to 33d S\- subway \- trains had resumed running \- lunch before 2\. The remainder of afternoon spent reading \+ I must admit it \- sleeping \- I am ashamed of the kind of life I am living when there is so much unfinished work that should be done. Dinner at 6.30 \- We \+ [illegible] went to Annual meeting of Century Club \+ had pleasant chats with a number of men \- left at 11\. If I had stayed a few minutes later I might have seen Sec'y W\. Taft who stopped for a short time on his way to midnight train. I had a chat with Dr. Tagnain \- his wife died a few weeks ago.
