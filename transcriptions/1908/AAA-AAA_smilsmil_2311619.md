## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 16, 1908

**Weather:** At W. G. Parkes' - Scranton - Pa.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm hazy day - Bkfs. before 8 - left the house at 9 - ments post of W. 23d St., ferry to Hoboken \+ D.L.T.W. Train at 10.5 for Scranton - got parlor car seats in end car with observation platform at the rear - Some very charming suggestions of autumn light \+ color thro' central New-Jersey - Had a good dinner on the train after passing the Water Gap - Got to Scranton at 2.15 - 15 m. late - Parke met us at station \+ went us to his home in his carriage - Found Helen there entertaining a number of young ladies - Mrs. P. greeted us from the midst of her work - I had a headache \+ lay down on my bed for an hour - Then had a short walk with Jim - Dinner at about 7 - Helen has a friend visiting her - Miss Williams - Slept in "Parkes' Den" with him \+ his wife - to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 17, 1908

**Weather:** Montrose  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very warm \+ hazy - Bkfs. at about 8 - Jim \+ I tried a walk - but it was too warm for comfort - During the m'f I got Vesuvius out of its frame \+ in Mr. P's bath room gave it a good washing - also washed Lake George - one of the thousand islands, preparatory to varnishing - we had lunch at about 12 - the Parkes, Jim \+ I went to station \+ took 1st train for Montrose - Got to Alford at 2.30 \+ then by M. St. to Montrose getting there soon after 3 - on train was introduced to Mr. Wainwright \+ his and, B. W. - head of West Side Hospital in Scranton \+ had pleasant chat with her - at M. went to Cooley's met for amusement for Williams \+ young Cooley - I was looking for Broderick who has charge of the Kent house - not finding him we went up the hill to the house \+ found him there - we went all over the place \+ I visited many of my foster children trees, surprised to find many of them in such good condition - then we went thro' the house \+ there too I was pleased to find the interior in an excellent state proving the wisdom \+ economy of doing things thoroughly \+ well at the beginning - It made me very sad \+ homesick - This was deepened when I went into the beautiful studio where I have happily done so much work - At 5.30 we ret. to train \+ got back to Scranton at 8 - then to the house where Mr. P. served us a belated dinner - Then, till 10 Miss Williams, Park \+ I chatted in his den -
