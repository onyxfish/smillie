## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 10, 1908

**Weather:** Bright day till about 4 p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright day till about 4 p.m. where it clouded over \+ the wind blew gusty \+ cool — after we were ready to go out in the mt. we took cab \+ drove directly to the Alt Museum taking my p[?] with me, went to Print Room \+ saw W. Taro Springer (so I read his autograph) the official who rec'd. me yesterday — I showed him the p[?]. \+ my note to the Director to accompany them — When he saw the p[?]. \+ understood that I was not trying to sell them, he dissolved at once \+ was very gracious — He took me to the dept. of Modern works — where the Room for students is a delightful large room, finely lighted \+ with more than double the area of the British Mus. Print Room — he showed me how prints are kept — gave me a small specimen of mounting board with addresses of manufact. urers \+ in other ways showed his good will — I found that he knew S.R. Koehler \+ the work he had done \+ that Dr. W. Valentiner of the Met. Mus. of N.Y. was a friend of his — Then we went to Nat'l. Museum — closed till the P.M. — Then rec'd. to New Mus. \+ were in the Sculpture \+ Egyptian \+ all there till noon — Then out — some snaps about \+ back to hotel \+ had lunch at 1 — a rest for half an hour — Then out with the boys — went to Carl \+ Giss' place in Dorotthiom Str. \+ brought a binoculars for about \#29.(M120) Then to Frederick S[?] \+ went to several Opticians till I found a 4" mag. glass — M.15 — Then to hotel — had hair cut \+ shave too — Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'y. boys went to theatre \+ I Remained in my Room.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 11, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fair day, quite cool \+ windy, owing probably to the wide disturbance centred over England \+ Scotland where there is high wind with snow \+ sleet. Soon after 10 we went to the Deutsche Bank \+ drew £30 — which I hope will get us home. This makes a total of £310 — \$1550 ago, the [illegible] more than the bal. of \$1700 in Second Nat'l. Bank. From the bank we walked to the Nat'l. Gallery \+ spent the rest of the [illegible] there — I'm getting blasé on pictures — the collection was not particularly interesting to me \+ I wondered why many of the paintings had a place there — Back to hotel \+ had lunch at 1 — after that I had a half hours rest — Then to the Grosse Berliner Kunstausstellung where we wandered among the 2175 works of art till 5 o'clk — Then I felt too tired to go on with the job \+ we retreated to our hotel — I found a good deal that was interesting to me — less freak work than I expected — There were a few atrocities that we saw at Munich last year — The etched \+ printed work interested me more, perhaps, than the paintings or Sculptures. There several galleries full \+ much puzzled me to know by what process it was done — there were some fine aquatints original \+ forceful \+ many that were merely brutal but the influence on me, on the whole, was stimulating — I wanted to go home \+ try to do something worth doing — I had a rest before dinner at 7 — Rec'd. a letter from Mary — Ev'y. The boys went out \+ I wrote a letter to Ernest Roth now in Constantinople —
