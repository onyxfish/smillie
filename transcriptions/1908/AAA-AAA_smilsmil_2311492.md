## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 3, 1908

**Weather:** Bright day \- not so cold as yesterday.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfs\. some time settling accts for past month \- then to Second Nat\. Bank \- from there walked to studio getting there at about 11. Fussed about till 1 o'clk \- Home at 2 \+ had lunch \- Back to studio at 3.30. Clara Williams there washing up the floor \- I loung about till near 5 \- then rel\. home. Taking a walk by the way. Had a rest before dinner alone at 7 \- Jim came in after 8 \- he is again rushed by work at office \- a little writing \+ some lounging \+ to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 4, 1908

**Weather:** Cloudless \- windy \+ bitterly cold.  **Temp:** 7°

Bkfs\. before 8 \- to studio before 10 \- morning spent in sorting \+ putting away a lot of paper of all kinds taken from Montrose to St. Hubert's \+ from there back to NY. It has been lying in my way for a year or two \- There was also considerable work to do in putting things in order after Clara's clean-up yesterday. At 1 o'clk, went to Miller's about the covering that is to take the place of sky-light over dumb-waiter shaft, studio \- Lunch at home before 2 \- Afternoon, wrote to D. Appleton \+ Co. making enquiries after my plate "Cedars by Moonlight" \- They publ\. it in Art Journal Dec. 1880 \- I w\. like to buy it if I could find it \- tinkering with locks \- out for a short walk before 5. Dinner alone at 6.30 \- Evg., went to Carnegie Hall \+ heard Wm. Jennings Bryan lecture before "The Civic Forum": "Thou shalt not steal". Jim joined me there \- It was to me an interesting performance \- Bryan impresses me an ordinary man \- a man of the "people" \- of average brain power, backed by immense physical power \+ unlimited gift of gab. In all his talk about taxes, tariffs, finance, trusts it was rant rather than reason \- After his discourse questions were passed up, written, \+ he answered always ready \+ confident \- a cock sureness that amused his audience if it did not convince them. It was 10.45 before he finished \- he had been on his feet howling for two hours \- The house was full.
