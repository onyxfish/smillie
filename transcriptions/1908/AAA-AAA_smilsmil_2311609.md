## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 26, 1908

**Weather:** Overcast \& foggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

At home. \-

Overcast \& foggy \- very warm \& still \- Up so early \- light fog at that time \- learned from officer that considerable time had been lost last night hunting in the fog for Nantucket light \- It was 11 when we passed Fire Island light Ships that was audible for some time before we could see her \- then on, slowly, till we got to Sandy Hook light Ships at about 12.30 \- All this time we had been loafing on the boat deck \- even there, it was uncomfortably warm \- Then we went down to lunch \- We got to Quarantine about 1.30 \- took on Pilot at light Ships \- at Quarantine we lay for more than half an hour \& were further detained by the U.S. Mail boat that hung alongside \& took off the mail \- It was 4 o'clk as we got up to our wharf \& commenced to work in, the tide running out making it difficult \- Ten tugs were at work, six of them butting her stern around from Port side \& a bow pulling on her starbord end \- There was an immense crowd on the pier \- all Jews \- except Val \& Gordon Smillie who were waiting for Charlie \- We were fortunate in getting our luggage together with little difficulty \- Then an inspector \& appraiser \- no trouble with customs \- then a porter to haul our inspected plunder out of the melee \- Then a hack upon which it was all loaded \& at 5 o'clk we were at the Ferry House \- \& before 6 at the Wallis \- Kate Leonard \& Katie Bradley had every thing in readiness for us \- all lights turned on \& everything looking very bright \& clean \- Dinner before 7 \- too late for Ralph to get to New-Haven \- Rect 7 \- Unpacking \& to bed tired at 10.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 27, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day \- warm \& muggy \- with now \& then a little rain \- I got up early \& had a scrub \& shampoo \- then to bed again \& had a nap before Ship at 9 \- The day was a loafing one for self \& the boys \- unpacking was done \& things were scattered around \- I feel rather un-strung \& indolent \- a natural reaction \- The boys showed reaction too \- Dinner at 2 o'clk \- At 3.30 Ralph went to Grand Central Station, Jim with him, \& went to New-Haven on the 4 o'clk train \- I went thro' an apartment up stairs (6th floor) with Shuter, the janitor, but decided that I was better off in the apartment that we are now in \- Early loafing about \& to bed early.
