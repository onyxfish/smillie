## Left Page

### Sat. April 25, 1908

*Jim went to Yonkers.*

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'f - easterly wind - a very light shower about 4.30 p.m - partial clearing after. I spent the m'f at studio advancing work on Port Marie \+ Frauenkirche preparatory to aqua-tint ground. Home at 1.30 \+ had lunch afternoon, some writing - went to Ansonia Ap? to see D. Grace - not in - then to Riverside \+ sauntered there till near 6 o'clk. Dinner alone at 6.30 - Jim has gone to Yonkers to spend Sunday with the Goulds - Eve'f newspapers, writing \+ arranging Photo prints of 1904 trip to finish the albums started long ago for Jim \+ Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 26, 1908

*Jim ret? from Yonkers late -*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delicious day, except that it has been rather too warm for comfort - a typical cloudless, hazy day with a shimmer of pale gauzy green over shrub \+ tree - it seemed almost that one could see the leaves unfold - I did not get up till about 8 - then a good scrub \+ bkfs. before 10 - It was nearly noon before I went out - then up to Nat'l School where Davis showed me over the bldg - the first time that I have been there since the rebuilding after the fire - I was especially interested to know how the Etching Class is progressing - found one of the members there at work - Report was good - I left at 1 o'clk. \+ walked home by way of Drive \+ Park \+ had dinner at 2 - doafing - some reading - some writing \+ before 6 went to Park \+ sauntered there for half an hour - Tea at 6.30 - Eve'f I went to Cooper Union - People's Institute - \+ heard Prof. Dublin late of Chicago University, on Impersonal Immortality - His treatment of the subject was disappointing to me - He promptly dismissed the consideration of Immortality as generally accepted - that is, as a state of existence here-after - no theory or belief on such subject could be proven - it was a matter infinite in extent \+ beyond our finite comprehension - What he preached was a moral or an ethical immortality, wholly impersonal, the result of our good deeds \+ correct lives, passing down thro' future ages - Scientests taught conservation of force or energy - nothing is lost - he taught conservation of moral \+ ethical life - Nothing ever done, good or bad, is lost upon fellow men, but it keeps on \+ is immortal - as I looked over that audience how satisfying such a doctrine as to be to them. Two or three of the questioners after the discourse got very much excited in propounding their metaphysical, sociological, moral \+ ethical questions -- as a discussion it seemed to me hardly worth while - mental vagaries of widest range were in evidence - I think such people should be treated to hard, common sense talk about practical life as they have to live it - better than abstractions.
