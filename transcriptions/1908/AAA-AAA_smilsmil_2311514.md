## Left Page

### Wed. March 18, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beastly day - cold, raw fog till after 10 - then sleet, hail \+ rain - I got up after a restless night feeling very seedy but inspite of unfitness I went to studio getting there before 10. I took a print from Goldsmith's Daughter head \+ altho it was disappointing it was not hopeless - There I took three good [illegible] from Port Marie - some portions are still overbetten \+ I must stone them down I had a wet time getting home at 2 o'clk - ate a light lunch, read awhile then I lost sense of discomfort by going to sleep - Jim's friend Crowell came along at 6 - Jim soon followed \+ we had dinner at 6.30 - After that they went to the Theatre \+ I tried to do some writing.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Unpleasant remains of yesterday in the m't but in the P.M. there was a high cold wind \+ clear sky. I got up at usual time \+ had bkfst. with Jim \+ then to studio as usual altho' still feeling very much out of sorts. I worked on Goldsmith's Daughter's face hopelessly. I now know that I made a mistake in touching it - not that I did not need a change but the work was beyond my ability now - is beyond my ability - I know just what is wanted but I am not dextrous enough to do it - Home to lunch before 2 - the sunshine coming into my Room so pleasantly I forced myself out to take a walk - but the wind was too high \+ keen for me - I wrote to W. Jno. Cadwalader in answer to his note - At 6.45 I was at Charl's \+ took dinner with him \+ then fifth \+ last Concert of Boston Symphony Orch's season. We heard more of Dr. Karl Muck's novelties - First Converse of Boston - New Pagan Poem, after Virgil by Leffler with Gebhard as Pianist \+ last - MacDowells "Indian" - All of it too arudite for me - I got home at 11 \+ to bed at once.
