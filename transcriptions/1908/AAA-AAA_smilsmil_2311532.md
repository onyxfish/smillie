## Left Page

### Thur. April 23, 1908

**Weather:** An uncomfortably warm day  **Temp:** Ther. getting up to 78°

After bkfo. I went to Civic forum office in 44th St. \+ exchanged my tickets for the lectures by Taft \+ Wu for better ones — paying $2.50 — Then to Century — Then to Tiffanys and bought gold Watch chain \+ left order to send for Parlor clock that has gone on a strike — Then to studio \+ worked there awhile. Home \+ had lunch at 1.30 — after that I went to barber's \+ had hair cut — then to Riverside Park \+ sauntered for awhile there, but it was too hot. Dinner alone at 6.30 — Jim came in after 8 having worked at office overtime — Evel \+ I wrote \+ mailed a letter to Katharine

## Right Page

### Fri. April 24, 1908

**Weather:** A fine day — not so warm as yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent the m'f at studio fussing about pfo. \+ getting things in such order that Clara Williams could come in \+ clear up in the afternoon — Ch. Vondrous came with a number of his recent etchings — he is doing some very good work. Home \+ had lunch before 2 o'clk — P.M. got notification that State Room 329 on Kaiser Wm der Grosse had been Reserved for me for June 30th — I accepted \+ wrote to Ralph. Evel, with Jim, went to The old 14th St. Academy of music \+ saw what is described as a Comic Opera — "Mille Modiste" with Fritzi Scheff as the bright particular star — a popular performance \+ a great crowd. I am discouraged in my attempt to find what it is in such a show that so pleases the mass of pleasure seekers — as a play it is conventional \+ commonplace to the last degree — not a bit of wit or sparkle in it — no mind or imagination in construction — \+ the music is a mass of jingling — The playeres were no better — Fritzie is not good looking, nor is she a good singer — She is not graceful — she is thin \+ paints her face absurdly — but she is full of vim \+ go \+ begins to enjoy her work in a way that takes the fancy of her audiences. She went thro' a rattling bit of bravum work with an energy \+ abandon that brought down the house — The Three Sisters Constantino gave what the Program announced as a "Ballet Advertisement". I would describe it as an Athletic Tornado — I never before saw such wild rushing, swirling, kicking, summersaulting topsy turvying — \+ I cant understand the physical make-up of such women. Home \+ to bed before midnight
