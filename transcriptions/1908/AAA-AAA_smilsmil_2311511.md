## Left Page

### Thur. March 12, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a very Restless night I took my b'kf' in bed \+ lay there till about 1 — then got up \+ dressed — feeling weak \+ miserable I do not know what specific thing is at bottom of my disturbance — it may be the kidney which is now discharging pus freely \+ giving me a little local discomfort — it may be stomach or it may be bowels — my shoulders \+ arms are sore with rheumatism \+ I am stiff all over with fever enough to unfit me for anything — a few days ago I was feeling unusually well, \+ now for no cause or indiscretion that I know of every part of me is crying out. The day was lovely — like spring for nearly two hours I sat at a few windows Had Jim with me at dinner — after it he went to drill. Willie Cook called me up on 'phone to say that work would prevent him from keeping his engagement to dine with us \+ go to theatre to-morrow evening.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 13, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came from N.H.

A charming, spring-like day. I had a fairly restful night but felt very shaky when I got up at 7 o'clk. I took matters slowly \+ easily \+ soon after 10 started for the Studio There I prepared at once to do some printing — by 12 I succeeded in taking two imps. from Marblehead neck plate \+ find that I have reduced the sky in parts so much that it is unsound — There I got home \+ left Bessie's letter at Cha's house — Lunch \+ then I tried to rest — Jim came in at about 4 o'clk — sick — used up by a headache — he took a cathartic \+ went to bed. Ralph came in soon after, as neither Jim or Willie Cook could use the theatre tickets Ralph took the two down to Box office \+ exchanged them for others for next Wed: R. \+ I had our dinner at 6.30 sat \+ then went to the N.Y. Theatre Broadway \+ 45th St \+ saw the Soubriss — a wretched pretense at a play — We went to see it because of the dancer Mlle. Adeline Genee who appears in it. The house was crowded — Genee is a wonderful dancer but to me she is not an interesting person to look at \+ her dresses were not becoming — she is a very light blonde with shiny candy colored hair which she wears in curls at the side of her face English fashion of 1825 — Her agility is amazing \+ she seems to be unimpeded by weight — There was no end of girls dressed \+ undressed — some of them most abominably — some of the play was funny — much of it was vulgar — a few, I de. Skewriski (Barney Bernard) was a remarkable representation of a typical son of Israel \+ greatly pleased the house. A very effective scene represented N.Y. city at night scintillating with electric lights, as seen from the Singer Tower — a background to some moralizing meditations by Lucifer. It was all over by 11 \+ at 12. I was in bed.
