## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 24, 1908

**Weather:** Dull m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m'f. - with a very light scatter of rain — during the afternoon the sun shone out for a little but an attempt at rain followed it. About 11 I went to Stikeman's with more books to be bound - he was not in — I left them - then to Office of Carriere & Hastings - 5th Ave & 26th St- neither of them in - saw a manager & questioned him about progress of finishing work in Print & also Pub. Library Nothing has yet been done — then to Butlers about frames — then to studio - found the work in Steam heater practically done - asbestos jacket on & pipe covered - Got out more books to be bound - Home to lunch before 2 o'clk - more work among books - a short walk before dinner - Jim dined with Harris - I dined alone - Eve'g I wrote a note to Sarah Fessenden Hammond in response to a note to Mary in my care, from her, which I opened — also a note to W. Parke asking for information about Sarah Atherton.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 25, 1908

**Weather:** An overcast, muggy day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

An overcast, muggy day — Up soon after 6 had a bath & then to bed again till after 8 — then up & had bkf't about 9 — with newspapers & arranging books till near noon — then out & sauntered & loafed on Riverside Park seats till after 1 o'clk - then home to dinner at 2 - with Jim who got up in time to join me — After that, as the atmosphere was depressing, more loafing — some writing - Charl came at about 5 & loafed with me for an hour — Supper at 6.30 Eve'g wrote part of a letter to Fanny Wright Davis & had a talk with Jim about possible employments — It was not encouraging as it seems to be impossible to present my views to him in such way that he catches fully my meaning or purpose. — A suggestion that he might find a profitable & congenial employment in some department of the manufacture of books brought out the fact that after his experience at Scribners, books in any form are as Red Rags to him — he will have none of it — He says that but for leaving me, he would be willing to go any where away from home for employment — That puts me in an awkward hole.
