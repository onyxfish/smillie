## Left Page

### Mon. July 20, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A gloomy grey day without break — little rain — we had planned to spend the day at the Franco-British Exptn. & Olympic Stadium — but I was not fit — The weather has got into me, & possibly some of the foreign sauces or flavourings served at table — I was quite used up by a general misery most potent in the head — I got up before 9 — had a light bkfst. alone — then had a fire made in my room & remained there all day giving some time to studying Bardilero [illegible] — The boys went away, following their noses without premeditation —

## Right Page

### Tues. July 21, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At Olympic Games —

A very fine day — at times in the afternoon the sun shone out really hot thro' the misty air —

We got away at about 10 o'clk & went by tube to the Exposition grounds — The first impression on entering was disappointment — there was nothing large, simple or impressive — it was a huddle of glaring white buildings overloaded with Rococco ornamentation & detail — the many little Kiosks & the latticed windows on some of the walls gave an oriental look to parts — on the whole it was to me like a great circus show — we went into the picture part of the show for a few minutes — the galleries were fine — well lighted & the collections — French & English — were exceptionally good — We went to the Olympic Stadium — Entrance from Exptn grounds, an immense structure of steel & concrete — to get an idea of "the lay of the land" we went in at a 6 penny gate — no seats — The Stadium is an immense structure about a grass arena 700 long by 300 wide in which there is a swimming tank 330 long & 50 wide — outside the arena is a cinder path 14' wide, 3 laps to the mile & outside of that is a concrete cycle track 35 wide & 1/3 of a mile long — outside of this rise tier upon tier of concrete rows of standing places & seats — 150000 people can find place within the Stadium, 50000 having seats — We watched running, wrestling & high diving — The pool has a depth of 14' — We returned the Exptn grounds & had a very poor lunch at one of the crowded Restaurants — After that we went again to the Stadium getting very good 2/6 seats — The number of people present was estimated at 12000 — We saw some exciting sprinting — preliminary heats — The Americans had it all their own way — Sheppard was greatly applauded for his running — Americans won 15 out of 39 heats — J. B. Taylor, a darkie from Penn. — did some fine running — It was well on for 6 o'clk when we re-entered the Exptn grounds & then we passed thro' to the Shepherd's Bush Station & had a chance to see how crowds are handled there — it was not much of a crowd, just a good, orderly squeeze where every one was anxious to get a seat — but every one didn't — there was a full number of strap-hangers — everything went smoothly & with celerity — I had a rest before dinner — Ever & with The Athertons — greatly pleased over an unexpected honor received thro' their friends the Tenmants who are connected by marriage with Prime Minister Asquith — They were invited to an informal lunch at 10 Downing St. There met the Bishop of London — Lord Crewe & other big wigs — J. Pierpont Morgan was also there — there were 18 in the party — The A's were delighted with the unaffected urbanity of their host & their guests — & well they might be, for they were brought into direct contact with the governing brains & the very heart of England — the leader of nations —
