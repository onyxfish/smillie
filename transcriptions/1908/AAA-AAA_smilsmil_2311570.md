## Left Page

### Wed. July 8, 1908

Ralph's twenty first birthday.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the night & morning — sometime in heavy showers with wind — I got up & had bkp alone at a little after 8 — as there were some signs of clearing I roused the boys at about 9 — The Athertons got away for Winchester at 10.30. After the boys had had bkp. we went out for a prowl — I took a snapshot at the Poultry Cross — poor light — we were driven in by showers — Later we tried again & went out to the River west of the Cathedral where I saw one or two good pictures but there was no light — Back to Hotel & had lunch at 1 o'clk. After that I had a good restful nap — when I awoke the sky was clearing — I went again to the Poultry Cross & got a snap at it under a fair light — later, after 4 — there was more clearing & I went to the River & got a view of the Cathedral — All this time the boys were tramping elsewhere. At 7 we had dinner — with Champagne etc — also a great lot of Strawberries that we bought in the m'f — This was the birth day feast & celebration — Self we arranged to have an automobile take us out to Stonehenge at 9 o'clk to-morrows m'f — I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 9, 1908

Winchester — "The George" Room 21

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some fine sunshine in the m'f. after some heavy Rain last night — overcast afternoon — Rain about 5.30 — Up at 7 — bkp. at 8 & at 9.° got away for Stonehenge in an automobile that ran a part of the way at 27 miles an hour — the run of 12 miles took a good half hour — the start in a rain flurry was not auspicious — but it soon passed & we had beautiful sunshine — it was a delightful ride thro' typical country — when we got to the stones the Keeper who Farkes told was not there, so we crawled thro' the wire fencing — he came later & got 4 shillings — we wandered about for half an hour — I took half a dozen snap shots. We returned to Salisbury by another road thro' Amesbury — over high, wide stretching ground with few trees — not so interesting as the road by which we went — We stopped at Old Sarum for about half an hour & climbed to the top of the earth work — a wonderful work when the primitive people & the refractory character of the soil is considered — Back to Hotel before 12 — lunch at 1. Left hotel at 2 — train left at 2.13 Romsey first change — detained few minutes only. At Eastleigh we changed at 4 in & got to Winchester at 4 o'clk — Drove to George Hotel — got our luggage into our Rooms — then to Cathedral where we had a considerable wait till service was over — effect of organ & singing was fine — after service we secured the attention of an old verger who took us all about — into the east end where very extensive repairs are under way the place being filled with scaffolding & workmen — down into the crypt where we saw workmen drawing water from a spring that was used by the Romans at the beginning of the Christian era — he made the visit very interesting — when we came out it was raining & we had a run for the Hotel — At 7 we had a table d'hote dinner — the worst & most expensive yet — after dinner a Dowager Duchess in the office, in furs & long-legged white kid gloves, gave us information about trains to London — in my room the Ther. stood at 63°. A fire was kindled in fire place in the boys Room, I spent a comfortably warm evening there.
