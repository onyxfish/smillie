## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 13, 1908

**Weather:** Not very pleasant, generally grey & cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not very pleasant, generally grey & cloudy — I waked at 7.30 after a tolerably quiet night — up only twice in answer to oil which acted mild & efficiently — All had b[k]f. together at about 9.30 — I hung about the house. The boys & Charl went off on a for owl together. I rested from 12 to 1 when I had lunch with the others — we loafed after lunch till about 3.30 when, in two cabs we started out with general directions to the driver to take us thro' the oldest streets in the city. we drove about for nearly two hours on both sides of the river east & the centre of the city — all about the universities — & thro' the student district — We saw much that was interesting but nothing surprising or remarkably picturesque. — I had a rest before dinner at 7 — Suet until 10 with Charl & the boys, then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 14, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day promised to be very fine in the early m'f but after noon the sky was clouded over & the light for photography was unsatisfactory — It was about 10 when we started from the hotel in an auto & in 12 m. got to M'. Parnasse R.R. station getting to Chartres shortly before 12 — a large part of the way was over very level country covered almost wholly by a grain crop just cut & in cocks — there was some rolling & pretty wooded country in the 55 miles — At once we walked up to the cathedral — the first view was disappointing. The spire on north cor. of front is wonderfully beautiful but the short tower & great clumsy spire on the south cor. injures it by contrast — It is impossible to get a complete view of the front except at very close range — I could get nothing on my camera — after a time we went in & noticed the darkness & simplicity of the interior & the effect of the remarkable old glass growing on us till presently we were very enthusiastic — portions of the interior have been marred by "modern" imcrovments — the choir has been plastered over & the columns painted badly to imitate marble — on the wall enclosing the choir on the ambulatory side are very wonderful stone carving, exceedingly delicate tracery, over deep niches in which are groups of small figures — while on the choir side are large panels of [illegible] relief modern work of ordinary character. The place was remarkably quiet — very few people in the cathedral — no verger — no priests — we were allowed to wander without in olestration — an air of neglect was every where — much of the stonework, pillars & walls, had been covered with a thin coating of cement or something of that kind & painted — the bases of the pillars being a dull red — the south nave has been cleaned, the stone exposed & joints pointed up — a great improvement — After 1 o'clk. we went seeking a place to get lunch & following Baedeker's directions went to the Place des Epars, a dreary gravel covered open place — where, from others, we selected the Grand Monarque — we had there a very satisfactory lunch — a plate of good strawberries — then we set out to explore the town & got into the valley where the little stream, the Eure, flows — we found a lot of jashioning subjects old bridge bridges — & distant bits of the cathedral — but [illegible] great & arrow, there was no sunshine — never the less, we took a lot of snaps shots & then passing east of the Cath. climbed a long stair way & came out opposite the north porch — then again into the Cath. & then to train at about 4.30 & got to Paris at about 5.15 or 30 — auto. to hotel — dinner — Suet with Charl who is to leave us in the m'f.
