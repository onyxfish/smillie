## Left Page

### Sun. April 5, 1908

**Weather:** Bright pleasant mrf.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright pleasant mrf. rain came late in the afternoon — I had bkfst. at 9 o'clk. \+ then till near noon amused myself reading — Then, with Ralph, I went out \+ had a walk on Riverside, about 2½ miles — the most comfortable walk I have had in several weeks — Home at 1.30 — dinner at 2 — between 3 \+ 4 I had a rest — at 4 Chasl came \+ with Ralph we Talked over our projected trip to Europe next summer — he had with him maps \+ an itinerary written out — He left after 6 — we had supper at 6.30 Eve'g, as Ralph's throat was troublesome \+ the weather had he decided to stay here overnight — Reading \+ chatting till bed time.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 6, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim Ret? from Ridgefield
Ralf Ret? to New Haven —

Charming day — Ther. got above 60°. Ralph \+ I had bkfst. soon after 8 \+ after that we went to Dr. Tuttle's \+ R's throat was examined — after that we walked to Grand Cent. Station \+ then to studio — then R. ret? home \+ I got to work on Marblehead Neck — Wertenbampf came in for a time — I got home to lunch at 1.40 \+ R. \+ I lunched together — Before 3 I went down town — R. Ret? to New Haven on 4 o'clk train — I went to Ringlers, 21 Barclay St. \+ left plate Cedars by Moonlight to have steel facing removed — Then to Knovals \+ Ferris, 2 Rector St. — saw Miss Anna Murphey \+ left with her the last sheets of Father's auto. bio? \+ my addenda. — Then to Ham-Am. dine — saw W. Meyers \+ he put me in the way of securing some sort of a Room on the Amerika from Hamburg Sept. 17. — Then to Clarence Smith's to have my new Will executed — he was not in, so I ret? home at 5 o'clk \+ had a nap before dinner — Jim came home, having had a good time with Bradford Boardman at Ridgefield — Dinner — Then he went to Dr. Tuttle's \+ had a small swelling on his jaw lanced — he brought back to me from the Dr. an old painting by Louis Mignot, in shocking condition hole punched thro' it \+ loads of dirt — I spent some time washing it — think I can save it — wrote to the Dr. about it.
