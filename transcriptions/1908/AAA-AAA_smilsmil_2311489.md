## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 28, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of mixed sunshine \+ shadow — Kate insisted that I must take my 6\&p. in bed — she had her way \+ I did not get up till 10 o'clk — very sore in the middle region \+ feeling lame \+ weak all over — Very unhappy because it is now forced upon me that I cannot hope to finish, as I have set my heart to do, un-finished work at the studio — paintings \+ etchings — I have not the strength. With the best will to do, I have accomplished nothing this winter — I can be blind to it no longer — I can blame no one but myself — I have been a dawdler when I should have worked — Wrapped in my ulster I sat for half an hour in the sunshine at an open window about 3:clk I was able to eat a little at dinner. Eve\+ Jim went to a dance.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 29, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly disagreeable day — dark \+ raw — a drizzle of snow falling \+ melting as it fell — my stomach \+ liver discomfort less than yesterday but in constant evidence to the effect of depressing me — I am sure that some mischief is going on inside, but how serious I cannot know — I have outlived many aches \+ pains — possibly I may outlive this — Of course I did not go out but applied myself to writing a summary of the more important work that Father did, with dates — to precede the list of pfo. of his works in the Print Dept. of the Public Library — in a work that I plan — Dinner with Jim at 6.30 — Eve\+ I wrote titles on mounted photos. of European subjects, of which I have now a considerable collection.
