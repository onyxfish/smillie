## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 8, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day \+ milder — got to studio at 10.15 much of mf absent soaking photos off an album page to learn the amt. of trouble \+ difficulties in changing the mts. in boys albums so unsatisfactorily done by Cullen who promises to make them satisfactory. Some clearing up done preparatory to getting to work again. — Interviewed Mr. \+ Mrs. Maynard in their sky parlor —
Home to lunch before 2 — Afternoon Josephine Brainerd Smillie called full of woes — I'm very sorry for her. Dinner with Jim at 6.30 after a walk on Broadway of 1½ mile — Evelf wrote to Ins. Hechange proposing changes in Studio bldg. to reduce fire Ins. Rates.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 9, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — very cold — clouded at times. I got to 79th S. Subway Station just about 10 — a man had just jumped in front of the train as it came in \+ we had to wait for some minutes till the body was pulled from under the wheels so that the train could move on — Then it was placed upon a step ladder \+ lifted to the plat form — It was a gruesome sight \+ through I was not aware of a shock at the time it affected me in a nervous way unpleasantly through the day \+ very. To studio \+ mounted the photos — that I soaked \+ made a long call — Home at 1.30 \+ had lunch at 2 — Then out \+ had a walk up Broadway for half a mile \+ back — Soon after getting in Josephine Brainerd Smillie I lay down upon my lounge \+ had a long nap — I went to Charles at 6.45 \+ had dinner — Jim dined at the Club \+ then to drill —. I went with C. to the Boston Symphony Concert at Carnegie Hall — Dr. Karl Mucky gave us a Symphony by Hermann Bischoff. Op. 16 — one hour \+ a quarter — four movements — I could not understand or enjoy it — Then there was a Macdowell Concerto for Piano. D minor No. 2. Mme. Teresa Carreno at Piano — a vigorous dashing performance but I did not enjoy it as I did Paderewski at the last concert. Then a Dvorak Overture — Carneval — Then I walked home — very cold \+ clear.

I stopped at W. S. Miller's \+ Fred came to Studio before 1 \+ took order to take out the elevator \+ lay floors \+ make closets.
