## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 2, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — got to studio early — Mrs. Sullivan came at about 9.30 \+ commenced scrubbing \+ cleaning — I was with her Till 12.30 — then home to lunch — back soon after 3 — Mr. Tolfree waiting for me — with Ryan we had a long conference concerning the matter of letting him have steam from my new plant to heat his old adjoining — I explained my terms — Ryan's to formulate them — In studio till after 4 — then to D. B. Butler's with little frames on Colman sketches — left them to be regilt. Home before 6. Cullen sent up my 35 roles of films that he has developed for me — I spent the eve'g looking over them \+ am much disappointed. The hen dog — that I took with me — seems to have been imperfect — much clouded — not a really high class negative among them — on films bought abroad there is better work — It was midnight before I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 3, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — quite cool in the m'g. Steam was on for awhile in apartment radiators — I was busy with the negatives of last summer's trip till 11 — then to studio \+ worked there till after 1 putting up curtains etc — Mrs. Sullivan came soon afternoon \+ cleaned down the stairs \+ the hall — I left her there to go on with the cleaning of the dark room — The studio is now ready for me to go to work — but with Ther. down to 62° — I cannot begin till Ryan has my new heater finished — I threatened to sue him to-day — Home to lunch at 2 — Put bolts on windows in Katie's bed room — Resumed work upon negatives — many of them very imperfect — I cannot tell whether the fault is in the film or the development — some are much clouded \+ patted. I had an ap before dinner at 6.30 — Well J. had intended to go to the Century, but inertia got the better of me \+ I remained at home.
