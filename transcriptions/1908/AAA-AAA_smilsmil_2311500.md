## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A horrible day of rain + slush — snow all the m't. fell to the depth of several inches — Rain in the afternoon washed much of it away — I did not go out at all but spent the m't finishing my work on the two souvenir 1907 albums — Afternoon I made some progress in the rearrangement of my notes to accompany father's autobiography. Dinner with Jim before 7 + we both spent the ev't loafing.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 20, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m't + clearing to a fine cold day — After bkft. went to the Century, got Huntington's address + wrote asking him if he w'd let me have original painting of Goldsmith's daughter as I shall require it now that I have scraped from the plate a portion of her face — I saw the Howard Russell Butler paintings there — then to McBeth galleries + saw a collection of Ranger + other up to date pictures — then to Montross' + saw Troyon + Dewing paintings — then to studio at about 11 — started the cleaning up of "dark room" which so far in my clearing up has been a catch all — Before I'clk I walked to 42nd St. Subway station — trip to 72nd — thence walked home + had lunch at 2 — afternoon worked on M.S. in connection with father's autobiography — between 4 + 5 o'clk. I had a 2 mile walk — dined with Charl at 7 + then went with him to Boston Symph'y Concert a Carnegie Hall. Didn't understand at all the Reger variations + fugue as a theme. I did enjoy the Beethoven Concerto — D. maj. It was wonderfully given + Fritz Kreisler, whose violin was delightful especially in the larghetto — I would gladly dispensed with the Rondo — The Bach I did not understand. walked home — 1 mile — to bed at 12.
