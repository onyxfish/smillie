## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 8, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Berlin - Kaiserhof - Room 269.

A lovely day - bright, sunny, warm - I have felt all day as though I had escaped from a land of bad dreams - I was up early \+ after bkfs'. we got every thing in readiness for our departure - then left the hotel for a tramp - with my camera I got to the most picturesque bits \+ photo'd. them again in better light than I had yesterday - we tramped to parts of the town north of where we had been before when I got back to the hotel I was quite jubilant feeling sure that I had some very desirable subjects under favorable light - Dinner at 12.45 - so that we might have plenty time to reach the 2.40 Train. There was a party ahead of us with an immense lot of luggage - so that the porter was confused. We were somewhat belated - We went to station by cab, by way of the Monkey fountain of which I got a photo. in our haste - We got away on time, comf't. to ourselves - Paris to Berlin train - good time all the way - for the first half it was uncomfortably hot \+ dirty - Remarkable contrast to cold rust of some time past - Country flat \+ uninteresting - much grain still being gathered - after a couple of hours passed all evidence of recent Rain - Farmers raising a dust as they ploughed their fields - We got to Berlin a little before 6 \+ got to the Kaiserhof with little delay - I was dismayed when I saw that Wilhelm Platz, with its fine trees \+ flower beds had been destroyed in the building of a new underground R.R. We was fixed up - had dinner at 7 - Every the boys went out \+ I wrote to Charles -

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 9, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day - Up at 7 - bkfs'. alone at 8 - when the boys were ready we went to the New Museum stopping on our way to buy some films - At the Museum went directly to the Print Dep't. where I found an austere man presiding - My reception was chilling - said the Director was not in town - I w'd. have to return in three weeks - nobody was in Berlin now - I could take my prints to the Dresden Museum where the representation of contemporary work is fuller than in the Berlin coll'n. After further conversation I was told that I might leave my prints with a note to the Director \+ my address \+ that eventually I might hear from him - then we glanced about - went out to the National gallery - found the Pergamon Mus. closed - then to the Dom \+ did all that was to be done there - The interior disappointed me - seemed ornate - not impressive - Then out \+ along the Space \+ around the Palace - got some snaps at the great Monumental Group \+ of Palace - then back to Hote - lunch at 1 - at 2.30 we went to the Emperor Frederick Museum \+ walked thro' that - both floors - was greatly impressed by the facade of the Palace of M'shatta - a gift to Imp. Wm II from Sultan Abdul Hamid - originally a desert palace of the Bedouins, dating from 4th to 6th cent. A.D. This is a portion of the original facade - a guess, 150' long \+ 20' high - light red porous stone, richly carved, deep cutting, in entirely original design - a small portion only of the building as shown by plans hanging opposite - To me it was very beautiful - The pictures did not interest me very much - It seemed to be a very full chronological exhibit of Dutch \+ Flemish work - I admired greatly the portrait of the merchant Gisze by Holbein the younger. 1532 - The examples of the Early Italian Schools, Botticelli' at al seemed to me unusually important - The Rembrandts, Van Dyks \+ Rubens, I did not think were representative - After coming out I got a couple of snaps at exterior - wandered down to dust fortress then cab to Hote - Rested till dinner at 7 - loafed in Palm Court till 9 - then to my Room -
