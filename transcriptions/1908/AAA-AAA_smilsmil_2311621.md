## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 20, 1908

*Home again.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A great change in the weather over night. The smoke has cleared very much & it is much cooler — I overslept & it was after 8 when I came down to bkft. There was great activity in the Parke family, with flyings about in preparations for coming journeys — Jim & I got Vesuvius into its frame & then loafed about — went out for a short walk & back to lunch at 12 — We rode to R.R. station with Helen for escort & started for home on 12 40 Train — I rode in rear platform of observation car till after we passed the Water-Gap. There was much delightful color & a good deal of dirt — We got to Hoboken at 5. & to the Wellesley before 6 — Kate had a dinner for us — Eve & Charl came up to see the photo prints from my summer negatives that Cullen had sent home — It was near 11 o'clk. when he left.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 21, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded mf with a raw, chilly wind — but before the afternoon it cleared pleasantly & was milder — Bkfp at 8 — to studio at 10 to meet R.M. Docking of Jat. T. White & Co. He asks me to write a more extended biographic sketch of Father for a revised edition of the Nat'l. Cyclopaedia of Am. Biography — Considerable time was taken up in Talk — then I completed my arrangement of Proof Copy of Tronce's Gallery for the binder — then home — lunch at 1.30 — Afternoon till 4 arranging photo prints — then to Wanamaker's & bot. some cut glass finger bowls. Home at 6 — dinner. Eve f. loafing with newspaper —
Jim rec'd. offer from Western Union Co. of his old place at old rates ($18.) temporarily — He went to the office & agreed to go to work To-morrow saying that he did not wish for a permanent engagement as he is looking for something better.
