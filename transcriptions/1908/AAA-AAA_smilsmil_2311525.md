## Left Page

### Thur. April 9, 1908

**Weather:** Bright, windy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, windy day — Bkfs. in bed — Temp. a little below normal but later it rose to 99°. At 11 I got up \+ took a half oz. dose of Castor Oil \+ in consequence spent a wretched afternoon, nauseated \+ generally uncomfortable — again I promised never again to take Castor Oil. I tried to amuse myself by reading Jack London's "Before Adam" — It was a disappointment. — It did not have for me the ferocious, aboriginal \+ animal element that I expected \+ altho' entirely unlike, I found it constantly suggesting Kipling's Jungle Book. — Then I read some of Stevenson's New Arabian Nights — more to my liking.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 10, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fickle weather — a combination of Feb. \+ April — Rain in the afternoon, continuing thro' the night — I got up feeling very shaky but determined to go to studio \+ do some work. I got there before 10 but soon found that it would be very unwise for me to attempt to do any Printing — I had some talk with Ryan about the plans for putting in a steam heating plant — an expert is to call this p.m. to look over the premises \+ tell the changes that must be made in pipes \+ radiators — the size of furnace \+ boilers required etc. etc. I did not feel able to meet him \+ [illegible] home at 11. \+ slept before lunch at 1.30 — afternoon, I had another sleep \+ feel that I am gaining slowly — I had a light dinner at 6.30 — Jim came in after 8 — overwork at office — Kate gave him his dinner — Evng I wrote to Ralph.
The Century carman got "the Goldsmith's Daughter" for [illegible], the Club is to make of Huntington's work.
