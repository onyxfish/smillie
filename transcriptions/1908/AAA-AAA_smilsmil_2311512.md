## Left Page

### Sat. March 14, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very spring-like day - trouble in my right side has developed more fully \+ seems to be a return of the gall bladder obstruction of which I have already had two attacks - at 9 I called up Dr. Tuttle to consult him as to treatment - he said he would like to make an examination - I could not go then bring in the midst of the activities of a compound cathartic - It was about 11 when I got to studio \+ I felt exceeding thin or slim rather. Nevertheless I managed to take two fairly good proofs from Port Marie plate \+ at 1 o'clk. I was at Dr. Tuttle's - he made an exam. \+ said he was satisfied that the trouble was a clogged gall bladder - then I went home \+ had lunch with Ralph - I lay about \+ slept a part of remainder of afternoon - Jim \+ Ralph came just before 7 - a hurried dinner then they went to a theatre \+ I got down to Charl's - he had his lantern set up and showed a lot of slides that he has just had made, selections from his last summers work in middle Germany - I left at 10.30 \+ got home \+ to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### Sun. March 15, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph ret. to New-Haven.

A mixture - a sort of premature Spring - sunshine - shade \+ a brisk thunderstorm in the afternoon - I did not get up till after 9 - the boys got up at about 10 \+ went to church \+ after that a walk \+ did not get in till just dinner time at 2 o'clk - I did not feel well enough to go out, so, loafed about — Afternoon I sat at an open window until the thunderstorm after 4 o'clk - It was short \+ sharp - I took no supper - the boys left at 8 - Jim went with Ralph to the R.R. station - I had an uncomfortable eve'g in consequence of a cathartic that would not work - I had a slight rise of Temp. \+ got to bed early.
