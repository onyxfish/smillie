## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 12, 1908

**Weather:** Brilliant, cloudless day **Temp:** much cooler

up at 7 — bkfst — newspapers — wrote to Will Parke
then to work on negatives — they are a bad lot,
owing partly to defective films \+ partly to care-
less development — Of course, some are defective
because I snapshooted at non-photographable sub-
jects — but I did so knowingly — Charl's summer
work that I saw last night is almost without
exception unaccountably bad — he is furious over it.
At noon I went to bank \+ left book to be balanced
Then to studio — the steam fitter had a wood fire
in furnace \+ steam on, testing — Home to lunch at
1.30 — more negative work — at 4 went down town
to Clarence Smith's \+ signed papers in effort
to get Fax reduced on Studio bldg — Home at 6 — No
word from Jim — he was to be home Sunday night
or Monday m'f. I phoned to Dielman's address —
phone taken out — I went to his apartment — 5 W. 82 —
he had moved \+ left no address — I got home \+
I phoned to Century — Dielman had left a few
minutes before — then I had some dinner — then
I went to Century hoping to get track of D — there but
no one could help me — Talked with Turner about
mezzotinting — he is doing some experimental work
\+ showed me some pffs. Then went to Charl's \+ he
phoned to Ridgefield \+ had a messenger sent to
Dielman's house to say to J. C. S. if he was there, that
his father wanted to know his whereabouts — then
I went home \+ as I got there Charl called me up
to say that Jim had reported \+ was greatly sur-
prised that I should be uneasy — It was three 10 o'clk —
my mind was relieved \+ I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 13, 1908

**Weather:** Pleasant day **Temp:** [illegible]

\- I spent the m'f assorting my
negatives, sorting them \+ faking skies on the
defective films with a stump — as I made
400 exposures during the summer \+ many of the
films were defective, the job of getting them
in order \+ ready for printing has been a very
tiresome \+ difficult one — Before lunch I
had a long walk in Riverside Park — Jim
returned from Ridgefield at 2 o'clk. Of course
he cannot understand why I wanted to know
where he was Monday eve'f. After he had told
me on going away that he would come home
Sunday eve'f. or Monday m'f.     I went to Studio
in the afternoon to see how matters were
progressing in the new heating work — I went
to Wanamaker's \+ bought a piece of cut glass
\+ some silver fruit knives — Dinner at home
eve'f loafed with newspapers \+ magazines.
