## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 8, 1908

**Weather:** overcast mft. clearing to be unusually warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

I had bkft. before 8 — went early to studio — got pf. of etching for Sue Whiting — took it to Dultens \+ then home — Dressed for wedding trip — had a light lunch at 12 — then with Jim to R.R. station \+ by 1°2 Train, to New-Haven — Special car — not so many mit as I expected — sat for awhile with Mary Cook — too early to go to the Whiting house so Jim, Miss Dielman \+ I went to Ralph's Room, Fayorweather — the Swaps let us in — we were there for less than half an hour, then went to the old Downs' House, Whitney ave. — Guests arriving \+ soon there was a jam that it was very difficult to thro' — the ceremony was fairly prompt when Sue \+ Paul Victor Christolpher Baur were made man \+ wife — finding it impossible in the crowd \+ confusion, to find any social pleasure I left soon after \+ left, with Jim, \+ took the 5°1 train getting to W\[.\] at 7.15 Jim went to Doill \+ Co. meeting \+ I went home \+ had dinner at 8 — Evtf. alone, reading.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 9, 1908

**Weather:** Mildly pleasant day — overcast a good part of the time, \+ somewhat misty — a typical fall day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim got away from the house at about 8. to take train for Ridgefield where he is to spend Sunday — I spent the mtf. over my negatives I went out for a walk in Riverside Park for about an hour before lunch at 1.30 — Afternoon resumed work on negatives \+ got them all properly numbered \+ titled at last — there is still considerable work to be done — spotting \+ otherwise correcting or modifying defects before I can order printing — This season's work has not been well treated by Cullen's operator. — It has more unnecessary defects than any lot that he has yet done for me — Ralph came at about 6 \+ we had dinner together. Most of the evtf. was spent showing him the negatives of last summer's work.
