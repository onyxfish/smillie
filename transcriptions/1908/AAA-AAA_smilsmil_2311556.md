## Left Page

### Wed. June 10, 1908

**Weather:** Very hot day \- Ther. above 80°  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very hot day \- Ther. above 80° I got to studio before 9.30 + spent the mng there in sorting + packing away things. C.Y. Turner came to ask questions about mezzotinting \- laying grounds \- preparing \- penciling in color etc. He thinks of making an attempt at it \- Home to lunch before 2 \- most of the afternoon was given to writing a letter to Katharine P. T. Some work of preparation for flitting was done at home. Willie Cook, who had been invited to dine with us but had not responded, appeared unexpectedly after we were at table \- Eve'f he + Jim wandered away \- I wrote to Chas. R. Huntington about returning to him the Goldsmith's Daughter painting.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 11, 1908

**Weather:** Mild, overcast m'g \- a light fall of rain about 11 o'clk \- then gradually clearing \- delightful eve'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild, overcast m'g \- a light fall of rain about 11 o'clk \- then gradually clearing \- delightful eve'g. I got to Clarence Smith's office at about 10 o'clk + had to wait for him awhile \- then I signed some papers + got others for Jim + Ralph to sign, winding up the Trusteeship of Annie's Estate \- then to No. German Lloyd Office + paid bal. due for three passages to London \- then as the rain had commenced I went home getting there at about 11.30 + busied myself till lunch packing my wardrobe in tar paper \- To studio soon after 3 + turned out some pf. from my plates to make selections to take to British Museum Print Room + to the Berlin Print Room \- Soon after 5 called at 154 E. 37 S\- to see Mary Cook \- no one at home \- then home \- Eve'g further work with wardrobe \- wrote to Geo. Cook + to Scott Brown \- then I went out + walked for 3/4 of an hour on Riverside Drive + in the Park \- very lovely \- The views off over the river \- the long reflections of lights \- the dreamy haze \- the moonlight \- the snuggling young couples all combined to make it interesting.
