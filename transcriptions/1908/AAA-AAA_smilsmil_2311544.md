## Left Page

### Sun. May 17, 1908

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast during earlier part of day — clearing \+ very warm in the afternoon. I did not get up till near noon — then Jim \+ I went to the Riverside Park \+ sat sunning ourselves for more than an hour — home \+ had our dinner at 2. After 3 we rode down to 36th St. Jim called at Miss Boutellier \+ I called at Miss Mary Collins with a bunch of carnations to congratulate her on her 80th birthday. I think the little attention was very agreeable to her — Home after 5 — Chas. came in \+ stayed till 6.30. Eve'g Jim went out \+ I loafed, feeling rather weak \+ disordered.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 18, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** above 75°

Another very warm day — Ther. above 75°. After a fairly good nights rest I got up to find that my legs were groggy under me \+ that generally I felt far from fit — but I went to studio at usual time \+ spent a couple of hours "stopping out" on the grounds \+ let preparations to first dip in the acid — Home at about 1.30 \+ had lunch — I spent practically the whole afternoon loafing in Riverside Park in company of nurses, babies \+ youngsters of all ages — but did not feel refreshed by it — Dinner with Jim — He went to bed early as a preparation for a 4.30 am start in a 7th Regt. shooting expedition — my eve'g was little better than more loafing.
