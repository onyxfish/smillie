## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 14, 1908

**Weather:** Pleasant day \- bright windy \& cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

after an early bkft. I finished letter to Ernest Roth \- To sub station P.O. \- \& got Money order for $25.00 (128.75\+) \& mailed it to Roth in letter \- then to bank \& then to studio at 11 \& worked on G. D. Till 1 o'clk \- with dryfpoint \- quieting sky \- Lunch at home before 2 \- After that went to Wanamaker's \& bought Table-cloths, Napkins etc. \- also silver fish forks \& plated dessert dish \- Home at 5 \- a rest before dinner \- Eveg with Jim went to Waldorf-Astoria \- To Annual meeting of M.L. Zoo \- The Secy Madison Grant described in an uninteresting way the present Game conditions in Alaska \- Ernest Thompson Seton described with much spirit some of his adventures in the great Barren Grounds of Canada \- he showed many photos on screen, some of them good \- Then Arthur Robinson showed a number of moving pictures of wild animals in New Brunswick \& New-foundland \- Remarkable \& interesting \- some where he followed swimming Moose till they got to shallow water and rushed, splashing, ashore, were the best \- Instruments for this work are now made in very portable shape, carrying 200' of film, worked by a crank \- The operator was in a boat where he followed The swimming Moose.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 15, 1908

**Weather:** A mild, pleasant day somewhat misty  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkft. at 8 \- To studio at 10.30 \- interview with Ryan about alteration in cellar to meet the requirements of Fire Ins. worked on "Goldsmith's Daughter" Till 1.30 \- dry-point over most of the flesh work to give more tone \- Home \& lunch at 2 \- After 3 went down Town to Cullen's \& after a talk with him \& looking over the two albums he promised that they should be made entirely satisfactory. Home at 5 \& read anat before dinner at 6.45 Eveg newspapers \& a little writing \-
