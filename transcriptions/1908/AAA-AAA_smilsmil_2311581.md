## Left Page

### Thur. July 30, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day - a little warmer - 74° by govt Report - After 9 got away with the boys for Covent Garden to see the flower market - disappointed when we got there - closed at 9 - we went thro' the fruit \+ vegetable markets - From there we walked to the office of the Ham. Am. Line \+ arranged to send steamer Rugs etc onto Hamburg to await us there - Then to Whitehall \+ into the United Service Museum \+ the Great Banqueting Hall, so rich in stomp - Then to St. Paul's where we sat for some time - The interior was wonderfully fine in the light in which we saw it - Then to the Criminal Courts Bldg - great crowd waiting for a chance to see Sevier, a sporting man on trial for Blackmailing a man named Joel - Then on to High Holborn then to the extremely interesting old ch. S. Bartholomew founded 1123 - Then to hotel at 2.30 - lunch - Then I gave up the Remainder of the day to Resteur. Every went with the boys to the Savoy \+ saw the Mikado - It was something of a disappointment to me - It was handsomely put upon the stage but none of the performers rose above mediocrity - some did not reach that - H.Y. Lypton was the Mikado - C.H. Workman was Ko-Ko \+ Rutland Barrington was Pooh-bah - His popularity seemed to be the result of his absurd facial contortions - There was a general tendency to clownish lines that marr'd gilbert's delightful absurdities - Ko-Ko was, on the whole, the best filled part \+ at the close he warmed up to considerable agility — but there was none of the graceful dancing that Moulan \+ Gertrude Quinlan gave us, years ago, in N.Y. It was after midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 31, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine weather still continues - Up rather later than usual, owing to dissipation last night - We got our steamer blankets, overcoat etc into hold - all - Took cab \+ went to Ham. Am. Office \+ started it on its way to Hamburg to be delivered to us on board S.S. Amerika - Sofo. 17. Then down the Strand - bought a dozen film spools - in another store bought some photos - Then back to Hotel at 2 o'clk. \+ I had a light lunch - after that I rested, the boys going off somewhere - I wrote a letter to Harry Anderson - Then out for a short walk. While out I felt a tenderness \+ weight in the region of the right kidney that disturbed me - I had dinner alone in the Restaurant - Every the boys went to a vaudeville show - I sat with Mr. Atherton - The discomfort in right loin increasing - so that when I went to bed at 10 o'clk - I was very apprehensive of trouble -
