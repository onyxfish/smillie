## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 15, 1908

*[Written above header: "Charles leaves Paris —"]*

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — Up at 7 +, with the boys, took bkfs. at 7.45 with Chase + later saw him started in an automobile for the R.R. Station. He expects to stop over at Strasbourg + one or two other places on his way to Axenstein where Mary + her girls are now waiting for him. — We hardly knew what to do with ourselves as the hour was too early for any of the in-door sights — we went to the Arc de Stoile, climbed to top + spent some time up there. Then to hotel + studied plans until our time of departure, set for next Thursday — lunch at ? — at 2.45 we started in Auto for St. Denis — 4½ miles — The ride was uninteresting — long, poor streets till well past the fortifications — Then by the river — no scenery — bad roads + much bumping when arriving at 2.5 miles the hour — got to Cathedral in less than half an hour — a crowd of common people passing each half hour in herds from nave, around the choir, into the Treasury + crypt + then out — we had to wait 20 m. + it took as much longer to get around. We went into the Treasury + down into the Crypt — The whole thing was a great disappointment to me — The exterior is not imposing. The interior is not impressive — much of the glass is atrocious + The effigies from tombs are arranged in rows + labeled as tho' in the headers — absolutely without sentimental interest because we cannot forget that the Tombs have been desecrated + broken up + the Human remains thrown together in a trench + destroyed by quick lime — what we see is a patch work of Remnants + Restorations. — The only part that did interest me was the small bit of old Norman masonry in the Crypt. It was all an exceeding great disappointment — could not get even a good light on the exterior — the sun shone went off it just as soon as we came out + a big cloud hung over the sun till we left. — we were in the bldg. for nearly an hour — The Run back to hotel was Rapid — auto bill 20 f. — + our driver objected to so mean a pour boire as 2 f. a lesson for me. To Hotel at 5 — a rest — dinner alone is Restaurant — a short walk with the boys on the boulevard at 10 p.m. Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 16, 1908

**Weather:** A very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — up late — after bkfs. at 10.30 we went to the Opera Ho. — I bought three seats in loge en face — No. 33 Troisième — at 10 f. each — [illegible] monday Then to Cookes to buy R.R. tickets to Antwerp, with stops, but finding that they had no such coupons, bough three to Amiens. Then to Bibliothèque + spent? From the Opera House we went to the Madelaine + heard High Mass — we left before sermon. After a little wandering we ret. to Hotel + had lunch. After that, a cab + went to the École des Beaux Arts to find that it was closed for Repairs — then to Hotel de Ville + went thro' that — fine apartments — not what I remember in older times — Then to Arts + Metiers + spent time in going thro' long galleries — I think it far behind the South Kensington Museum. Then a look at the old Ch. of St. Nicholas — Then to Hotel autofs of 'bus — Dinner — after a quiet evef. at about 10 p.m. we had a long stroll. Then to bed.
