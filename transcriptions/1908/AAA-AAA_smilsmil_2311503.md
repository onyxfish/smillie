## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 25, 1908

*Scarlet Fever in the "Wellesley".*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A march-like day, with winds + clouds - snow at 10 p.m. To studio before 10 - the m'f. was occupied with work upon sink in the acid or dark room - a split in the wooden bottom has been a trouble for some time + I have tried in various ways to mend it - To-day I tried caulking - I got oakum from Ryan + caulked it with asphaltum + oakum. After 12 I went to 2° Nat. Bank - Then home + had lunch after 2 - then a rest - Then dressed + went to Clarence Bishop Smith's - 150 E. 63° where a Reception was given to Tom Cook's fiancee, Miss Wilcox - It was a crowded house + all branches of the family + many friends were there - Miss W. is a good looking slim girl + is already a great favorite with the immediate family - Tom is said to have made a great catch - the girl has an income of $30,000 + is heiress to millions - I got away at 6 + had a long walk home, getting in at 6.45 - The lower hall decorated by a Health Dept. Poster announcing that there is a case of Scarlet Fever in the House - the 7th floor is quarantined - Dinner with Jim + at 8 we went to Carnegie Hall - Fifth Meeting - Civic Forum - Jos. W. Folk - Gov. of Miss. "The Bar of Conscience" - It was a disappointment to me - he does not have the advantage of good manner, voice or appearance - What he said was well enough - it was a good sermon without marked individuality enough - he has no felicity in phrasing - compared with J. P. Thompson or Thos. Hastings he would be nowhere - the audience was large but there were many vacant seats - The discussion following the address was languid - it was near 11 before it was over -
David Bispham sang three songs with mighty voice.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 26, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day, steady rain till late afternoon when it cleared - I spent the day at home - in the aft wrote some letters, balanced acct° in my check book etc - etc - Lunch - then more writing, resting, reading till after 5 - then I went out + walked on Riverside - charming Twilight effects over the River + far bank. Dinner at 7 - alone - Jim with Gored - Eve'g went to Republican Club Rooms, Broadway + 81st St. Saw + heard illustrated Lecture, Puerto Rico + its People" by Orrel A. Parker - a lawyer of this city - has lived there three years - took a large number of photos, some of them very good + interesting - a series illustrating the execution of five men by garrote which he insists is the most expeditious, painless + humane form, better than electricity, being simpler. Home soon after 10 -
