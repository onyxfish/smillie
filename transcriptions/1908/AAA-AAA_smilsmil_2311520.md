## Left Page

### Mon. March 30, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful, brilliant day \- Bkfs. at 8 \- got to studio soon after 10 \+ spent the rest of the mf scraping \+ stoning out a portion of group of trees on far side of River on Port Maria plate \- I dont like the form, too lumpy \- I propose to put in something lighter in structure. Then I wet down paper \+ prepared for printing to-morrow \- Home \+ had lunch at 2 \- After 3 o'clk. I went to Clarence Smillie's \+ gave instructions concerning new will \+ executed complaint concerning Tax assessment on Studio bldg. Then I went to Ham. Am. Line \+ saw Ernest Schmalck \+ arranged for state room 28 on the Graf Waldersee to sail July 11th. Then home \- dinner with Jim \- 6.30 \- Well I went to Mr Frank Dodd's, 333 W. 77. S. \+ spent a pleasant hour \+ a half with Katie Parker Riley, her sister Mattie (Mrs Dodd) \+ Mr. D \- Home at 10 \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 31, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal day of rain \- Bkfs. at 8 \+ to studio at 9.30 \+ to work at once printing \- by noon I had pulled four pfs. from Marble head Neck, working on the plate between pfs. The last pf. seemed to me to be about as near right as I can get the plate \- So, greatly relieved in my mind I went home to lunch at 2 o'clk. During the afternoon I made up a lot of the sleep that I lost last night. Jim dined at Miss Boutellier's \+ I dined alone \+ fooled away the ave f. I had a talk with Chase over the 'phone about my projected trip to Europe next summer \- he urged me to put in an application for possible place on the Amerika to sail from Hamburg \- Sept. 17 \- he has a room on her \- so, I wrote to Schmalck about it \- the ship is now booked full but there is a chance that a room may be given up, \+ I stand in line for that.
