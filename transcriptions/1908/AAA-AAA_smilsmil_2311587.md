## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 11, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day, quite cool - we did not get started early - I was too glad to do nothing owing to internal discomforts. The boys went to the Louvre to add to their stock of knowledge, \+ I rode with Charl to the Palais de Justice Notre Dame \+ then back to hotel where we all had lunch together. It was near 4 o'clk when we started out in motor cab \+ went to the Trocadero - There we went carefully thro' the architectural wing until closing time at 5 o'clk - Then we took Metropolitan under ground R.R. \+ by changes reached the Opera Station near our hotel. This was Charl's introduction to the Paris under-ground. We had our dinner at - I \+ as my digestives are making themselves disagreeable I spent the evey quietly in-doors with Charl.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 12, 1908

Paris Sewers - D. Paul Muller -
11 Rue Royale -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunshine \+ cloud - weather getting unsettled - So am I - was encouraged when I got up, by absence of discomfort to believe that I had, for the time, got the best of my digestive trouble - but the uneasy feeling in stomach \+ bowels began again before noon. It was about 11 when we drove, all four of us in motor cab - to the Chief Engineer of Water, etc, Place de l'Hotel de Ville No 9 for tickets to go thro' the Sewers - the clerk told us that they were all issued for to-day - no more for two weeks - Then we drove to the Palais Pantheon \+ with a crowd went thro' the Crypt \+ saw the tombs of the various celebrities there - then looked at the wall decorations in the Pantheon above - a man came to us with tickets admitting to the sewer this p.m. These tickets are issued gratuitously. The man asked 3 f each for them - I bought three, Charl having seen the sewers did not care to go again - Then we went to the little church of St. Étienne du Mont where C- was greatly pleased - then back to hotel at 1 \+ had lunch - Before 2 o'clk started with the boys for the Rue d. Étieves S. Martin opposite the Church S. Nicolas des Champs where the opening to the sewer is - There we found a crowd of 50 or more ticket holders waiting. - Promptly at 2.15 the chain was dropped \+ we crowded down. The steps into a vaulted passage well lighted \+ dry \+ low. After a hundred number of yards we entered a larger passage where a number of short open cars, coupled together were waiting for us in a sort of rough - abunding platform alongside. This portion was lighted by electricity \+ the men were driven by [illegible] power - Globe lamps were on the cars. There was some delay in getting the party seated - Then we ran 10 or 11 minutes thro' a vaulted passage with numerous pipes overhead - in size from 2 feet or more to 3 or 4 in - The largest pipes were at the sided supported on crutches; the others were suspended - all were labeled - River water - Spring water - Compressed Air - Telegraph \+ Telephone - The names of the streets overhead were shown \+ when there was connection with a house its No was given - Side sewer \+ sewer entrances were marked - Then we came to a place where we all got out on a wide platform - I side windows looking to the River - a delay for about party ahead to get out of the way - then we got into boats - long canal boats - side by side. The thing was clean \+ ship shape - a white globe lamp at bow \+ stern - a pilot one \+ steered on ledges on each side. I found us all being a little faster than the current - The prevailing odor was that of the woman along side of me. - Here the passage way was much larger \+ our boat ride was ten minutes - we had been underground for about half an hour - we came out on the quai at the east end of the Louvre - Charl met us at the opening - we went to the Louvre that he might have a look at the Venus de Milo \+ the Nikke - Then as I was ailing we drove to hotel where Charl got D. Paul Muller, 11 Rue Royale - who feeling my abdomen said bowels were clogged - prescribed castor [illegible] - I took it \+ had two calls during the night -
