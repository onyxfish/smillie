## Left Page

### Tues. April 21, 1908

**Weather:** Overcast but not unpleasant — clearing finely later in the day  **Temp:**

At 10.30 I went to 21 State St. with Clarence Smith went to Tax Com. office in Records Bldg at 11 — we had to wait for more than half an hour before we were ushered into the presence of Com. Purdy his Board of Assessors — Smith made his plea but was choked off — Mr. P. told him pleasantly that he did not agree with his proposition that the assessed value of the court should be put upon the rear stables who had the use of it — but added that he thought some relief might be given — I had no chance to say any thing. Then I left Smith + went on to the Brooklyn Bridge for an airing + to see the new work in progress there. Home, + had lunch with Ralph — at 2.30 went to studio + with chalk + crayon worked on pls. of figures, chapels + frozen kirches to determine just what I shall try to do with aquatint. Home before 5 o'clk. Rested till 6 — then R. + I had dinner — Jim did not come home — At 7.30 we were at Cooper Union + got good seats on the platform — Bryan came in nearly a quarter of an hour late + was, of course, vociferously welcomed by the immense crowd — his text was Brotherhood + helps. he talked about a great many things he did not forget to refer, now + then to Brotherhoods — my opinion of his calibre is in no way changed — He's not a deep thinker + he has a vigorous way of saying vacular commonplaces with oratorical flourish + gesture — He enlivened things now + then by a bit of humor + had his audience with him boisterously to the end — He managed to make a fine display of the Am. Eagle at full abroad + remind his audience that to members led the world in all essential matters — There was some fun over the questions + answers that followed the address but no light, that I could see, was shed upon anything — His speech was 1 h. 40 m. long + questions + answers took another 1/2 h.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 22, 1908

Ralph returned to New-Haven.

**Weather:** Bright, windy + quite cold  **Temp:**

Ralph + I went to office of N. German Lloyd — 5 Bdway + learned that it wd. be possible to get a fairly good stateroom on the Kaiser Wilhelm der Grosse, sailing June 30 — then we went to Charl's office + after waiting till he got thro' with his m[orning] mail he gave us considerable time in considering steamers + their sailing dates — He phoned to Mr. Englebert of N. German Lloyd + arranged that I sh'd. call on him — then R. + I ret'd up Town — lunch at 1 + at 2.30 we went to N.G.L. — Saw Mr. Englebert + after consulting the list of bookings he thought we might have 329 on main desk, but he could not be sure for a day or two — then we went home — Dinner at 6 + Ralph left soon after to take 7.45 to New-Haven — Eve'g for some unaccountable reason, I went to pieces + was fit for nothing — could not read or write — + so, loafed uncomfortably till bed time.
