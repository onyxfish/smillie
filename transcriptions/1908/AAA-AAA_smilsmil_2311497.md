## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 13, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable wet day - but in spite of it I went to studio + put in another m\+ on Miss Goldsmith's face. I am in doubt as to whether I am really improving the plate. I am trying to improve the figure as well as to lower the tone of the flesh. but the work I am doing is, I feel, mechanical - rather than artistic. Vondrous came in with several new plates - I think he wanted to get proofs from them but I could not do it - Home to lunch at 2 - Spent the afternoon revising M.S. in connection with Father's autobiography - at 6.30 I went to Charls to dinner. Even. I read to him the most interesting portions of the autobiography \+ my prefatory note \+ my notes concerning Father's work to precede the printed list of prints that I gave to the Print Dept. of library - He was very much interested - he surprised me by saying that while he knew that I had the M.S. of the autobiography he had never seen it or heard it read - I find that he has a prejudice agst. autobio's. He says they are too intimate for public inspection - I tell him that I think he is an exception - the public, as a general thing, is only too glad to get a chance to butt in behind the scenes. It was after 11 when I left.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 14, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild - foggy m\+ - sunshine in the middle of the day - fog again in the afternoon - To studio before 10 - still fussing over flesh work on Goldsmith's D\- Insurance Smith came to see changes that I have made in fire risks on studio bldg.
Home to lunch at 2 o'clk - after 3 I went to Wanamakers \+ did some shopping - I got home soon after 5 - Dinner alone at 6.30 - Jim dining out under emergency call with a young woman. I spent the even\+ writing notes - one to W.M. Kasson telling him that I w\. not dare take off the heavy coat of varnish on his little painting without first consulting with him.
