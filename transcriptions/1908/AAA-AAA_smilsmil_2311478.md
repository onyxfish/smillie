## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 6, 1908

**Weather:** not so cold as yesterday — clear fine  **Temp:** [illegible]

day — I didnt get away from the house till after 10 — then to 2d. Nat. Bk. \+ from there walked to studio. Started Winton with painting of three cows To Emily Cook's — E. 46th S. Some fussing about the studio \+ home at 1 o'clk \+ had lunch with Ralph — at 3 went to the Ansonia \+ saw Dr. Thos. Grace about repairs to my upper set of teeth — not reassuring — I am afraid it is not in his line of work altho' he seems anxious to undertake it Rel. home — Chatted with Ralph till near 4.30 — then rested till dinner — Eve. the boys went to an East Sides German place, very democratic, to see the Merry Widow in German — I fussed till near 11 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 7, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Ralph Rel. to New Haven —*

Overcast till noon then a sleety rain commenced that rapidly increased in violence — strong N.E. wind \+ torrents of water — Bldp. at 8 — at 10 Herbert Smith came about fine Ins. of Studio Bldg. The rate has been greatly increased because Ryan keeps gasalene \+ torches in the cellar. He talked for an hour or more — After he left I went to studio getting there a little before 12 — Saw Maynard for a few minutes — first time since he was married — Home at 1.5 — pretty well wet — Had lunch with Ralph \+ spent most of the afternoon with him — Rec'd letter from Emily Cook effusively thanking me for painting of three cows — and telling me what I had entirely forgotten, that I had given her an Adirondack landscape a year ago — a surprising joke on me — Dinner soon after 6 — Ralph left at 7 to take 7.45 train to New Haven — Jim left at 9.30 to go to a dance — I podged about as usual.
