## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 11, 1908

**Weather:** Very mild & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild & pleasant — To studio at 10 o'clk. took the other eye out of the Goldsmith's D. & worked till 1.30 on the plate. Home & had lunch at 2.30 — Aretta (Sullivan) Morgan called at the studio this m'f at with two of her children — After 3 o'clk. I went to the Grolier Club & introduced myself to Mr. Morton, the Custodian — Told him that I wished to see a recent work on American Engravers Recently pub'd. by the Club. He was very kind & took me to the library & gave me in charge to Miss [illegible] The lady who has charge there — She too was very amiable & I was given a seat at a table by a South window & the two Vol. work to look over — I made no notes & can not now give the name of the compiler but hope to take another turn at it later — The fad of collecting the works of Am. engravers is growing as I wrote yesterday. It was after 5 when I left — then to Tiffany's & left my watch to have case spring rebound — then to 42d St. subway station & home before 6. Dinner with Jim at 7. Eve'g. spent going over the photo albums that Cullen has sent home. They are not yet exactly as I would have liked to see them but a great improvement over the first ones.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 12, 1908

**Weather:** Very mild m'f & pleasant  **Temp:** 40°

Very mild m'f & pleasant — Ther. at 40° after 4 o'clk. wind changed — sky overcast, air raw — sloppy underfoot — To studio at 10 & until 1 o'clk. worked on Goldsmith's D — It seems to me that I am improving it — my work is confined altogether to the face & neck where I am elaborating the work & lowering the tone — Home before 2. & had lunch with Jim, he having holiday — Lincoln's Birthday. After lunch I wrote a note to Art Editor of Tribune criticising term "dry-point etching" used in a pub'd. notice of the Grolier Club exh'n. From 4.30 I walked on Riverside for about an hour — little pleasure in it — Dinner at 6.30 — Kate Leonard went to Springfield this m'f — a sister there is very ill — not expected to recover — Katie Bradley has charge of us — Eve'g. I wrote to Ralph.
