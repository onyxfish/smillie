## Left Page

### Sun. July 12, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day — wet much of the time — got up late had a bath & bkfs' alone at 9 — The boys got up later I loafed about — wrote a letter to Clarence Smiths to go with documents relating to Ralph's coming of age — these were signed by R — & witnessed by Mr. Atherton later & mailed at night. We went out for a walk & were driven in by the rain — lounged about & read news papers — The boys had lunch — I went without — At 3 o'clk we went to the Atherton's Room & I showed to them the pps. that I have with me — we were there till after 5 o'clk — After that I went to my room & slept till 7 — Then to dinner with the boys — Evening there was a concert, vocal & instrumental from 9 to 11 — we were with the Athertons a part of the time —

## Right Page

### Mon. July 13, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 59°

Heavy rain all the mf. did not have bkfs. till after 9 — It was after 10 before we decided what we wd do & about 11 when, by tube, we got to the Nat. History Museum — we went conscientiously thro every gallery & of course found a vast amount to interest us — at 1 we went to Refreshment Room & had lunch — At 3 we went over to the So. Kensington Mus. & were there till 5 o'clk — Then we were all too tired to do more & ret'd to hotel — I lay down on my bed & slept till 7 — Then up & had dinner — Jim went off to meet Holsabfee — Ralph & I lounged about & discussed matters of taste, humor & manners — I rec'd a letter from Charl telling me that Uncle Willie died July 2d.
The afternoon was comparatively dry — The opening of the Stadium for Olympic Games by King Edward & the Queen is reported to have been a great success.
