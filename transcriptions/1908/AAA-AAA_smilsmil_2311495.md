## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 9, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very cold but a wonderfully beautiful winter day — There was more wind than was pleasant in the m't but in the afternoon it was serene — not a cloud all day — weather breeder, no doubt — Bkbs. at 9 — got a very pleasant note from Mr. J. McKesson to which I wrote a reply. also a letter to M. Alfred Sidlitz, manager of the Schenker & Co. bureau in Munich. About 1 o'clk, went out with Jim \+ had a very comfortable two mile walk — Dinner at 2 — after that some time with news papers — about 4 I went out \+ had a walk on Riverside — the river is almost entirely covered with ice — an interesting sight — I had a light supper alone at 6.30 — Evt'g worked on a device for keeping my mounted photos. under a sort of restraint — they warp so badly if allowed too much space.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 10, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Milder weather — mild sunshine — To studio at 10 \+ till 12.30 worked on Goldsmith's D — The foils of the eyes are much over bitten — I tackled an unpleasant job when I commenced to scrape down \+ hammer up the lady's optics — I demolished but one this m'f. About 1 o'clk. I went to the Grolier Club \+ saw an exbtn of examples of the work of early Am. engravers — coming down to 1850 — The collecting of such prints is a growing fad — It was an interesting but very incomplete showing — few of the prints were really good examples — or the best work of the several engravers. Lunch at home after 2 o'clk — After 3 o'clk. I went to Wanamaker's \+ made a number of purchases. Then to Idew's in University Pl. \+ got a gas fixture for studio lower hall — Then to Scribner's and made a short call on Mr. H\. L. Smith. Then Home at 5 — Dinner with Jim at 7 — Evt'g wasted over newspapers \+ cook book.
