## Left Page

### Wed. June 24, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Commencement \- Home again \-

Still very hot \+ humid \- After bkft W\. said that if I Really wished to go to Commencement, he w\. phone to Sect's office to learn if he could get a ticket \- This, after my waiting ever since my arrival that had no ticket \- I could not get one, nor could Jim or Ralph \- He 'phoned \+ was told that a Reserved Seat ticket w\. be held for him. I at once went to the office \- got ticket \- went to Ralph's room \- found him in bed \- Then to Woolsey Hall \- on going to my seat, found it very undesirable \- waited about till the procession of dignitaries \+ grads. came in \- Then all unoccasioned seats are free \- I secured a first rate seat. The exercises differed from former years only that they had been shortened \- Perhaps the most interesting part to me was the conferring of Honorary Degrees \- There were 14 honors \- Prof. Perin presenting the candidates with much laudation \+ sometimes humor \- J. Pierpont Morgan was the last one \+ rec\. more applause than any other one \- Exercises were over at 12.30 \- I went outside \+ met W\. \+ Elkin by appointment \- Boys were about with Extras \- death of Grover Cleveland \- At 1 o'clk. we went into the Great Hall with the class of '77 \+ had good seats \- the meal was a good one \+ was even over \- Then the speaking began \- a platform \+ great sounding board had been erected at the west end \- Speaking was led off by Pres. Hadley \- He gave a brief history of Yale's power \+ during the past year \- Then he called upon Rev. Hugh Black, a young Scotchman who has come to this country to do evangelical work among young men \- He was followed by S. J. Elder \- who was amusing \- \+ he was followed by old Cy. Prest. Dwight \- \+ at last came Taft \- There was a wild time \- everybody on their feet shouting \+ waving their napkins \- a Yale song was started \+ sung with vim \- The singing was to my mind exceedingly good \- The combination of male voices from high tenor to double bass was exceedingly rich \- There was considerable singing during the session \- Taft was in very happy frame \+ had everybody with him \- All was over at about 5 o'ck \- I went right home \- The headache that had been bothering me all day was then so severe that I gave up to it \+ was on a lounge in my bed room till dinner at 6.30 \- Then a Thunder storm came up \- R \- 'phoned that he was detained by the storm \+ asked me to stop for him on my way to R.R. station \- I left the house a\. after 7 \- drove to R.'s Room \+ together we went to station where I took 7.50 train to N.Y. getting home at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 25, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day but with a delightful mitigation of yesterdays heat \- I had bkft \- soon after 8 \- Found some interesting Ausable Club letters that I spent a little time in replying to \- After 10 \- to 2\. Nat'l. Bank \- Got my letter of Co \- Sent my Cert of Howe Mf\. Co \- Stock to Chas. Atwater with Power of Atty to transfer it to new purchaser \+ send proceeds to me \- Then to Brooks Bros \+ bought Cravenette coat \- Then to Wanamakers \+ bought Collars etc \- \+ home about 12 \- lunch at 1.30 \- Katie got my Cheviot shirts into condition that I could take them to Wanamakers \+ have collar bands put on them This was at 4 o'clk \- From W's I went to Stewart's Carpet cleaning place in W. 57'S \- \+ arranged to have my rugs at home \+ at studio called for, cleaned \+ stored till my return in fall \- Then home \- dinner alone at 6.30 \- While at dessert Will Parke came in for a few minutes. Evef writing.
