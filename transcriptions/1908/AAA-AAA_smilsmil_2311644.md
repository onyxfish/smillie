## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 7, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain during last night, continuing in less quantity during the m[orning] \+ finally clearing pleasantly in the late afternoon. To studio at 10 \+ in spite of darkness I painted until about 3 o'clk on Normandy Farm Yard. I prepared a lunch in the studio at about 2 o'clk \+ ate it while I worked — after 3 I cleaned up \+ then for half an hour rested in my big chair. Then I went to Wanamaker's \+ bought shirts \+ socks for myself — Home before 6 — Jim [showed] that he c[oul]d not be home to dinner \+ I sat alone in solitary state — Eve[nin]g reading and loafing —

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 8, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant day — to studio at 10.30 after stopping at Louis Tiffany's (Mad. Ave. \+ 45th St.) saw him \+ a lot of work in Water Colors that he did last winter in Egypt. Some of his old work in oils is also on ex[hibition]. Then to studio \+ painted till near 2 o'clk — I have now about finished the first stage of repainting the Normandy Farm Yard — shall set it aside \+ when it has dried hard shall then bring it out to glaze \+ paint into tone \+ color harmony as well as I am able. It was 2.30 when I got home \+ before I had my lunch — Then I worked for some time on photo negatives — then the nap that is getting to be a habit — evidence of growing years \+ failing strength — Dinner alone — Jim dined out \+ then to a dance with the young lady — Eve[nin]g reading \+ to bed early.
