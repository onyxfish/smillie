## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 22, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still beautiful weather - a little cooler than yesterday. Got photo. prints from Cullen damaged in taking them from scrap book - two of them, from Ralph's films, I sent to him. Got to studio soon after 10 - Carpenters there had commenced taking out wood work in my dumb-waiter closet - I went to Hofeles an b'g't. boy who worked at my printing press taking off the rust with emery cloth till 12 - I could do no work under conditions \+ so pussed about doing little things - home to lunch at 1 - back to studio at 3 - there till carpenter left at 4.30 - home after 5 - dinner alone at 6.30 - Jim Kept at office by over-work till late - at 8 I went to Am. Art. Qes. to sale of Mrs. A. G. Hunt's picture intending to buy my little High Sierra painting if it did not go above $60 - it sold for $60 - a very fine Bryant, 7" [illegible] each way, the nine, 16½×12, brought $720 - \+ it was worth it. I left as the Bierstadt paintings came on - they sold for low prices - The first $s before I left - home at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 23, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \+ raw - snow at night - To studio at 10 - Carpenter there at work finishing closet in dumb-waiter shaft - Boy from Hofeles's came at 10 \+ worked cleaning press till 12. I podged about unable to do anything in the way of proper work - At 1 I went to Douglas's \+ arranged to have a man come in the mtf to fix the walls. Home through at 2. Afternoon was fooled away - went over to Colman's, 265 Central Park West \+ enquired about him at door, as I had heard a rumor that he was dead - Dinner at 6.30 alone - Jim at a company shooting match. Eveff busy about a number of small things requiring attention.
