## Left Page

### Sat. May 9, 1908

**Weather:** Clouds + sunshine - threatening & then smiling  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim went to New Haven.

Clouds + sunshine - threatening & then smiling - I got started at my Printing work at studio shortly after 10 - taking ph. of Frauenkirche - Dresden. Yondrous came + watched me work for an hour or more - At 1.30 home + lunched with Jim who went to New Haven at 3. P.M. on invitation of the Whitings - at 3 I set? - to studio + continued printing - not very successful - my ink is too old + filled with minute bits of skin which too often clogged the lines - I washed up + left studio at 6 - not as tired in feeling as when I began in the m'g. These perturbations or vagaries of feeling are incomprehensible to me - a short rest before dinner - Eve'g alone - some writing + some reading.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 10, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim returned late to-night.

High northwesterly wind all day, not cold but too boisterous for comfort - delightfully pure some brilliant sunshine - at times, heavy clouds - a very fine day barring the wind - I got up, had scrub + bath before 7 - then to bed, had a nap - B'kf't at 9 - with newspapers till near noon - then out to Riverside + wrestled with the wind till after 1 o'clk - dinner alone at 2 - soon after that Katie + William Elkin called + we had a very pleasant visit together for some time - after 4 o'clk. I went to 547 Riverside, away up about 123° or 4° St - to call on Mrs Gavit (Mr. Chas. P. Adams) but she was not at home. Home at 6 - a rest - something to eat at 7 - then to Charl's. We spent some time arranging an itinerary for the proposed Trip next summer - after getting thro' with that we discussed the autobiography of Father that I have edited + written a Preface for — all legibly Type written with a view to having a number printed for print collections + libraries - Charl has read it over carefully with the question before him of the propriety of such use of the M.S. - He opposes it — objects to making public any details of Father's early struggles —
