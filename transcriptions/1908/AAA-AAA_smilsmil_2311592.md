## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 21, 1908

Antwerfo - St. Antoine Hotel Room 212 -

A very pleasant day, altho' clouds were rather some to photo work. we got a fairly early start after bkft. I first went to a point opposite the Palais de Justice hoping to get a good picture but found that inside \+ a portion of the front was covered by scaffolding, so that I could not get the view I desired - Then we followed the Dyse River, getting some River views - Then crossed a new bridge \+ went toward the centre where a junctions give group of old structures - St. Nicholas Ch. - the Belfry, the Hotel de Ville \+ the Cathedral stand - went into St. N. - was greatly interested in interior - founded in 11 cent., rebuilt in early Gothic style 13 cent. \+ modernized later - the modernization bring extensive building over old piers \+ columns changing to very common place the architecture effects - clerestory windows bricked up \+ covered into a flat wall with stucco have columns enlarged - Rien at intersection of Transept \+ have greatly enlarged \+ form of arch entirely changed this was done probably because of needed strength - to Remove this work \+ Restore completely we avalon this edifice - portions of this later work have been Removed with admirable wisdom, enough to show the underwork \+ make the alterations evident - the church may never have been a good example of architecture - but changes made it hideous - we then went to the Hotel de Ville \+ were escorted thro' various interesting halls - then to the Cathedral of St. Bavon - where we found quite to interest us, the double star being the alter piece "Adoration of the Immaculate Lamb" by Jan \+ Hubert Van Eyck - the verger had more than he could do in personally directing two or three groups of tourists thro' chapels crypt \+ other places - to keep things going in order. we with a few others were locked in the "Van Eyck chapel" for nearly half an hour - Baedeker gives a lengthy acct of the Painting - we studied it carefully - on getting out spent some time looking unsuccessfully for photos - only old silver prints - had to be content with postal cards - I fell to Hotel \+ had lunch - the boys lunched at the Rathskeller - after lunch I went alone to the Chatteau des Comtes - a strange old stronghold said to have been founded in the 9th cent. for a time a factory, now owned by the city \+ partly restored - good example of a medieval fortress - I went from dungeons to roof with our fine guide - thro' the city - then Returned to hotel - boys there - bus to station \+ left on 4.48 train - uninteresting ride - got to Antwerp at about 6.20 - drove at once to St. Antoine - met Mr. Howell the proprietor - very gracious - house full - passengers to sail on S.S. Fatherland to-morrow - we give us rooms for the night temporarily - soon after 7 we had dinner - changes since I was here 46 yrs ago amazing - it is now a great modern city - the R.R. station very fine - noble Boulevards \+ streets from Station to hotel which is well situated on the Place Verte - unpacked portion of suitcase - to bed early -

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 22, 1908

Changed to Room 132 -

Fine day till after 3 o'clk. where weather changed. Rain began after 4 \+ a number of heavy showers till after 9 p.m. - I had a disagreeable time about 1.30 this mt., awakened by cramps in legs which I coud not reduce for some time - the first attack of this trouble since I have been over here - Up before 8 \+ had bkft. about 9 - It was 10 before the boys were ready to go out with me - a letter from S.N.I. - got here a day or two ahead of me - we went first to the Cathedral \+ found some sort of function under way - the figure of the Virgin Mary in Rich Robes in central aisle, was uncovered \+ gorgeous draperies were set against the columns - the great organ \+ full choir were in full blast, for some time we sat \+ listened - then out to the Promenade that runs over the wharfs, a terrace, by the Riverside - then to Steen Str. - where I found, I think, the hotel where Father \+ I stopped 46 years ago - then more wandering - sat about 12 back to hotel where we got into our new Rooms - No. 132-3-4 - ensuite - on first floor - west side, overlooking the court. very comfortable, convenient and pleasant - I read aloud to the boys from Baedeker till lunch at 1 - after that we went to Cathedral which is closed from 12 to 4 - admission 1 fr. - we took our time in going about with Baedeker in hand \+ saw the Rubens' Paintings \+ other objects of note - then we went to the Steen museum - an old castle where the Inquisition at one time held its sittings - went thro' the dungeons under it. The roofed for some time on the north Promenade \+ at last were driven in by the rain - I had a long nap - Dinner soon after 7 - Jim quite busted by a severe cold. I read \+ wrote till 10.30 - then to bed -
