## Left Page

### Tues. March 24, 1908

**Weather:** A bright, pleasant day but it has brought me no comfort  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up, had bkfs. with Jim + got to studio a little after 10. I prepared paper for printing to-morrow + then worked till 1 o'clk. on Marblehead Neck \- then to Lewis + Conger's in 42° 5°, bot. Coffee pot + took it home \- had lunch at 2 \- at 3.30 I went downtown to Clarence B. Smith's taking my Will with me. I spent about an hour with him explaining the changes that I wish \- then home, feeling feverish + exhausted. Dinner with Jim at usual time \- Eve'g trying to read + to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 25, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a very uncomfortable night I concluded in the m'g that it w'd. be hopeless for me to attempt to go to studio + do any printing. I had too much fever + too many other discomforts. I remained in bed and 'phoned to D'. Tuttle who came in the course of the m'g \- he says there is a blocking up of pus in bladder of right Kidney + some consequent absorbtion that causes the fever + general distress \- says it has been coming on for some time + that I have fought ag't. it too long \- it w'd. have been better if I had given up sooner. In the afternoon I was up for awhile to give my bed + room an airing. I wrote a note to Geo. to open up dampened paper so that it might dry \- + to have sent a letter just Rec'd. from Mary.
