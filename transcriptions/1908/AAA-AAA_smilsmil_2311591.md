## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Amiens - Hotel de France & Angleterre - Room No 3

A very lovely day - up at 7 - bkfp'. at 8 - Then I packed Trunk & suit case - At 11 o'clk - every thing was ready for a start - At 11.30 went with the boys to the Palais Royal Gardens, where I took a couple of snap shots - The boys made some purchases & then we Ret'd. to hotel - I bought some photos - Lunch before 1 o'clk. I settled bills, paid Tips all around & at 1.45 we started for the Gare du Nord - had no trouble with baggage - Train left at 2.30 - as we started a common fellow, apparently a waiter & not quite sober got into our compartment - at a station Ralph got out & got an official to ask the man for his ticket - it was III class - he was bounced - generally, if a man has nerve enough, he can ride 1st cl. with little fear of detection & I am confident that it is generally frequently done - The country over which we passed is pleasing & largely covered with grain now cut & in sheaves dotting many miles - At 4.45 we got to Amiens & by Hotel'bus drove at once to this place - We started out promptly & I got a couple of shots at west front of Cathedral - Then to a store & got some photos - Wrote a few words to many & inclined to her three Postales - Dinner soon after at 7 - provincial - The boys went out for a stroll - This Hotel is the only one starred in Baedeker altho' it is the 3d or 4th on the list. its only advantage over the others is that it is nearer to the Cathedral - there is a fine, new looking Terminal Hotel at the R.R. station & we saw two others on the most important street, both far more promising than this Hotel de F. & A - which is old & dirty.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 20, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ghent - Hotel Grand Victoria - Room 27.

An overcast day - with a light shower at 7 o'clk. & a thunder storm at 9 p.m. Up before 7 & hurried to the north side of the cathedral to the River Somme where on Pl. Parmentier an open air market was in full swing - all kinds of vegetable & fruit food - strange vehicles - picturesque people - odd boats, bridges, reflections in the water, the cathedral towering in the back ground - it was a bully picture, but there was no light - nevertheless I opened diaphragm full wide & took a snap - I hope agt. hope that I may have caught some thing - then back to hotel - bkfp'. at 8 - then to station and away on the 9.16 train to Lille - could not get tickets farther. At the next station we had to change to another train - our compartment invaded by two objectionable people - one, with a lot of bundles, unclean & smelly, cooked like a waiter - too much for Ralph - he got out a station, brought official to the door & had him ask the man for his ticket which proved to be Third class - he was bounced. Our Trunks did not go on our train - because our train was as fast one & there was not be time to Transfer it where we changed Trains - they will follow two hours later - we got to Lille at 11 & after strolling about for a short time took a cab & drove about for nearly an hour thro' all sorts of streets - none of them interesting to us, Many schools, institutes etc - good substantial & extensive bldgs. but none of them really imposing or architecturally fine - There were a few open spaces, some statues - some parks & broad boulevards, well shaded with well to do looking Residences - but the imps. made by all was that we were in a provincial, manufacturing city - we went to the Hotel de L'Europe & had lunch - When we came out it was raining lightly & drove to Station - got Trunks & away for Ghent - at a station on the frontier - Haquin, I think, we were all run thro' the Customs mill - perfunctory work & then transferred to another R.R. Finally at about 6 got to Ghent - no cabs at station - a porter took our suit cases on a barrow to this hotel & here we got rooms - The Hotel de la Post was closed last may - we went out & I had a short stroll - then to hotel & rested till 7 - Then a light meal, after which I wrote to Charl & to Mr. Nowsalck of the St. Augustine Hotel at Antwerp.
