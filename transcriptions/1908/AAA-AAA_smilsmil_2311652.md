## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 23, 1908

**Weather:** Bright, pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, pleasant day — about three or four inches of snow fell last night — I did not get to studio till about 11 — then painted till after 2 o'clk — on Farm Yard, in a hopeless way — I cannot be blind to the fact that I am not bettering the work done 20 years ago. Then walked to 2d. Nat'l. Bank — cor. of Fifth ave. \+ 28th St — got money enough. I hope to get me over Xmas — Then home \+ lunch after 3 o'clk — Some writing — Then, being tired I rested — Dinner with Ralfah — Jim dined out — Eve'g at 8.30 R. \+ I went to Mary Cook's — 154 E. 37th St. — a Xmas tree view for old folks. A pleasant gathering. We got there before 9 o'clk — at 10 the tree was lighted \+ trifles were distributed to this amusement of all — Then supper \+ soon after 11 R. \+ I started for home.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 24, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day — To studio soon after 10 — to work on "Normandy Farm Yard." I painted till near 2 o'clk — when I got to the end of this repainting stage — I am greatly disappointed by the result — I have passed into the bog that I feared from the first — the general effect is weakened — the prevailing tone of the foliage green is bad \+ the brush work is worse — My trick of painting with the flat side of a large flexible brush, dragging it lightly over the uneven painted surface results in a sandy, indeterminate, chewed-up, niggled appearance that is distressing — So, I went home to my lunch at 2.30 with a poor appetite — afternoon I had notes to write \+ bills to pay that kept me busy till after 4 — Then out \+ walked on Riverside for an hour — Then rested till dinner — Eve'g Jim \+ Raiteh went to Clark's — I read \+ wrote — Sands, the man who rents Tolfree's bldg. next to my studio sent me a check for $15 — as payment for subsidy of steam heat from my plant for one month in advance — the steam to be turned on when he asks for it. He expects to pay by the month \+ for as little time as possible — may be only two or three months — that is not according to agreement made with Tolfree — the season was to be not less than four months — There is no written agreement — this is probably the beginning of trouble.
