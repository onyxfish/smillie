## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 21, 1908

Harvard \- Yale football Game.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day \- I had a short, delightful walk before bkfst. at 8.30 \- The men passed rapidly in chat + gossip \- At 12 we had a light lunch. I had prepared myself ago. the possibility of the cold. I carried with me a steamer rug \- by car to Fayerweather Hall at 12.45 \- met the boys \- They put me on a car, as they had not yet lunched. I went out to the field alone \- getting to my seat at 1.40 \- top row, west side centre of field \- a wide, unobstructed view \- I enjoyed seeing the constant flow of the crowd coming in \- the river at the entrances ending in trickles as they found their seats \- the boys came about before 2 o'clk. The game was called promptly \- the trains were busy at once \- it was a determined battle from the start, with a good deal of line rushing \- Coy was Yale's bright particular star in punting, but in rushing he did not get the prowards as he did thro' the Princeton players last Sat \- most of the fighting was in mid field but toward the end of the first half Harvard got the ball to where it could, + did, kick a goal from the field, scoring 4 at which all Harvard (largely represented) went frantic \- During the intermission it was a wonderful sight, the dense mass of 35,000 humans packed on all four sides of the field \- from the high place I could look over it to the lovely brown hills beyond \- all washed in soft sunlight \- It was so warm on my under clothing was so heavy that I did not put on my overcoat till the end of the game \- a singular effect was produced by the smokers \- by the angle of our aline from where I sat, a little curl of smoke as it went up was defined prominently \+ the smokers were so many in number that the whole mass seemed to be puffing \- the second half was desperate \+ the Yale men maddening \- twice it seemed certain that Yale w'd score \- once a Harvard player jumped \+ hit back on a forward pass \+ it rolled out of bounds + was secured by a Harvard man \- it was less than a yard from Harvard goal \+ had to be kicked from behind the line \- Yale got the ball + made a beautiful forward pass + again Yale practically sure of a score \- but Yale was penalized 15 ds. because the ball had not been carried 5 yds. before the pass was made \- it was on trial but Yale took it bravely \- Harvard dancing a wild time dancing over the field \- it was the first time in 9 years that H had scored against Y \- It was very hard work getting off the field \- we rode into town \- there I left boys + went up to the Elkins' \- getting there at about 5.30 \- Dinner an hour later \- Ever + I felt tired + was glad to get to bed at 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 22, 1908

Ret'd. to N.Y.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day \- clear, cloudless \- Up at 7.30 \+ had a half hours walk before bkfst. The rime of frost over everything \+ the violet light over the distance was enchanting \- After bkfst \- I chatted with Katie Till after 10 \- then I sauntered down the hill to Fayerweather Hall \+ up to Ralph's Room \- door open, nobody in \- plenty of sunshine \+ Boston \+ N.Y. newspapers with full accts. of yesterday's game \- I sat there \+ read till Bronson \+ young Bangs came in at about noon \- Ralph soon followed \+ then Jim came \- we sat there till about 1 o'clk \- Then Jim, Ralph \+ I went up the hill to the Elkins' \+ had dinner at 1.30 \- At about 3 \- feeling sleepy I retired \+ had a good nap \- when I woke at 4.30 found the house deserted \- note on hat rack \- set out walking \- met the Whitings \- 5 o'clk. Tea \- I got there at 5 \- Mr. Keeler, whom we met at Graustein, summer of '07. were there \- general chatting till 6 \- the boys left for supper \+ Katie \+ I went back to Prospect St \+ had supper. Locke came at 7.15 \- good byes \+ to station \- met the boys there \- Train late \- 15 m. leaving at 8.05 \- Jim \+ I had a snoozy ride \+ got in 15 m. late \+ home at a little after 10 \- \+ then to bed.
