## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 2, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** Ther. from 60° to 64° all the time

Castor oil reported at midnight — I got up at 9 — promise of a fine day — but the promise was kept in shreds & patches with showers between — bkfs. at about 10 — I felt pretty shaky & spent part of the m'g on my bed — a light lunch at 1.30 & later I wandered out with the boys & by watching the clouds managed to catch a few rays of sunshine with my camera. My condition & the weather combined have come near to making this part of our Trip dismal — it is cold as well as wet — I Ret'd to the hotel at about 4 & wrote to Charl — the sun shone out at 5 & then I went out again with my camera — a not before dinner at 7. Even'g finished my letter to Charl & after I had posted it found a letter from C — in the boy's box. Then I wrote a postscript & mailed that.

Thunderstorms at night — Mr. Zimr., the manager says that we are now having Nov. weather — it is cold for thunderstorms to be about.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 3, 1908

**Weather:** A bright pleasant m'g  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright pleasant m'g. with some good photograpbing light before noon, of which I took fullest advantage — but the afternoon was overcast with some very light showers — Up at 7 — bkfs. at 8 & then right out with my camera & went to Sophie Plein & got a shot at the old clock tower by morning light — after a few more exposures Ret'd to hotel — boys had had their bkfs. Then we went to the Six house but Baedeker misled us — our cards were not sufficient — we must have the card of our Am. Consul — as the hours were from 10 to 11.30 it was too late to get that, so, next we went to Coster's Diamond grinding factory — 7 Wavenburgerval, using the card that Mr. Zimr. had given us — we were shown diamonds in the rough — the manner of splitting them up to eliminate imperfections — the crushing of the residuum into diamond dust to be mixed with oil for polishing purposes — then to the grinding rooms where great steel discs are run at high revolving velocity — the diamond to be cut set in soft solder on the end of a stick, set firmly in a fixed arm at proper angle pressing upon the revolving disc upon which is diamond dust & oil. After leaving the factory we wandered seeking interesting bits for photography. Feel I had apposed the end of my Roll — then, as it was near 1 o'clk & we were near the station we took No 11 Tram & Ret'd to the hotel & had lunch — afternoon the boys went off on a short steam boat trip — I had a rest — then I went out & hunted for good Bromide prints but could find nothing but old silver prints, Post cards & colored prints. I was obliged to be satisfied with post cards — wrote to Mr. Sekin sending him the note I Rec'd from the Deutsche Bank in London — Dinner after 7 — I wrote to Mr. Scott Brown — The boys out prowling. Bought tickets for Hanover & made preparations for a start to-morrow m'g.
