## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 7, 1908

**Weather:** Mixed weather - cloudy & threatening &  **Temp:** [illegible]

almost unpleasantly cool - After 1 o'clk. by cab we went to the Luxembourg & went thro' the galleries of painting open to the public - not up to what I Remember - Then we went into the gardens & sauntered for some time - then To the Pantheon & looked carefully at the wall paintings that have been completed since I was last there - to me the details is the least effective of any of them - Harmonust was a surprise - the Chavanne's fit out with architectural effects - at 12.30 back to Hotel by cab & had lunch - after that, cab again & went to the tomb of Napoleon - Then thro' to the Church of St. Louis - the Military Mus. was closed - we then walked across the [illegible] across the Alexander III Bridge - around the Art Bldgs down the Champs Elysees to Place de la Concorde - there took a cab & back to Hotel - I rested. a dinner at [illegible]. Every the boys went out - I wrote until after 9 - then I sauntered on the Boulevard till after 10 - then in & to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 8, 1908

**Weather:** Dull day but no rain - up at 7 or before  **Temp:** [illegible]

wrote to Bessie in reply to letter just rec'd - It was near 9 before we started out - took cab to Hotel Seville - learned that it would not be open till 2 o'clk - Careless of me - then we walked to the Island of St. Louis, where I stopped at some points where I made sketches years ago - then across the Pont St. Louis - Took a look at the outside of the Morque - then to the Palais de Justice & tried to get entrance to the old Prison, but failed because we had not provided ourselves with tickets - then we crossed the Pont St. Michel & went up the Boulevard to the Cluny museum where we remained till 12.30 - we had to be content with a very brief view - The old Roman bath in the heart of Paris was extremely interesting to me - we hurried away leaving my umbrella - To Hotel & had lunch - After lunch, by cab, back to Cluny - got umbrella & then on to the Jardin des Plantes where we spent the rest of the afternoon - the Menagerie part of it is, to my mind, not at all creditable - the "ferocious animals" are housed in an old evil smelling, badly ventilated building - The doors to their open air cages were closed & all the animals were gnawing at bones - the out of door collection is of little value & interest - nothing about the place will bear comparison with Bronx Park Zoo - we were greatly interested in the Nat. History galleries where there is a wonderful collection - the building is, comparatively new - completed 1889 - comparative anatomy has an up to date showing - but it is greatly crowded & our own Nat. Hist. Mus. is I think in the matter of Skeletons & bones, fuller & better housed. One department of fœtal monstrosities was horrible beyond anything that I had ever seen - we crossed the Austerlitz Bridge & by underground R.R. went to Pl. d. l. Bastille - there changed - No - it was the Pl. d. l. Republique - others changed & went to the Opera Station, near our hotel - the underground R.R, so far as we have yet seen it, is not up to the London Tubes - & they are not up to the N.Y. subway - To Hotel - a Rest - dinner at 7 - again the boys went out in the evening \+ I remained in my room. I was much out of sorts & slept poorly - the boys did not come in till 2 a.m. - I had the fidgets badly.
