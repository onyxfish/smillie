## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 16, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal m'f. - fog \+ smart showers till about 11 - from that time the weather cleared to be charming - I had bkfs. with Charl after 8.30 \+ at 10 we went to Ham-Am. Office - were detained there for some time - we each got letters from Mary - from 11.30 till 1 we prowled about - then to hotel \+ had lunch there a rest - at 3 I went out with camera \+ took 2 or 3 snaps - joined Charl \+ boys at Cook's where I drew £5 - \+ changed it into U.S. currency \- $24 - then all off on another prowl I got a number of Hamburg photos - \+ at 5 o'clk left the others \+ ret'd. to hotel \+ rested till dinner at 7 - Eve'g I wrote to Mary - Wm. Elkin - Deutsche Bank London \+ to Ham-Am. London office expressing my dissatisfaction at the blunder by which I have to do without ulster and rugs till we get to Southampton -

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 17, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

On board S.S. Amerika - Room 407.

Dull day - raw \+ chilly but not really cold - Up at 6 o'clk bkfs. at 7. \+ away from hotel, everybody satisfied, at 7.40 No trouble at R.R. station as we had a compartment engaged - train left at 8 - \+ after an uninteresting run got to Cuxhaven at 10 - Debarked promptly - stewards all ready to grab our hand luggage - a march thro' the long customs exam. sheds \+ out to the pier where, after slight delay we got upon tender - another delay while the baggage was being transferred - \+ at last we got upon the steamer about 11.30 - Got our suite cases into 407 in short time - then interviewed Ober-deck \+ bath stewards - they had rec'd. my notes \+ I got nearly what I asked for - It was a not altogether pleasant surprise to meet Saml. Katz (the butcher) \+ his wife among the 1st Class passengers, \+ later, to find their steamer chairs just in front of ours on Prom-deck - The steamer got away at about 12. \+ then we had lunch - a general survey of our fellow passengers at that time was not inspiring - the Hebrew element, in unlovely form, prevailing - Our vis a vis at table, intensely assessing - the Ober, on enquiry, told us they w'd. leave at Cherbourg - Afternoon I unpacked a portion of my belongings \+ had a nap -
