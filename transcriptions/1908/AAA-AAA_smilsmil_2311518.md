## Left Page

### Thur. March 26, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Less fever than the night before. Pus passing freely — was not able to be up — had help in bed — looked over mt'f papers, then had the room darkened + slept till 2 o'clk. a very unusual occurrence for me. after a lunch I got up + sat at an open window for about two hours. Geo. came in to see me. I wrote a note to Wm Shinder, a boy at Hyde Park, returning to him eight execrable drawings sent as evidences of his Talent — advised him without mincing words, to work on a farm if he could make any sort of a living by it. I wrote a short note to Kate Riley who is now visiting her sister, Mrs. Dodd — in 77: St.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 27, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably warm day. Ther. at The weather bureau away off — as it promised a storm for to-day. I awoke at 6 after a fair rest — no fever but felt very weak — about 9 Dr. T. called by phone + I sent back word that altho' in some respects better I felt so weak that I thot it wd. be better for me to keep my bed — he answered that he wd. come + then I "took a brace" \+ got up — He came at about 11. o Talked assuringly I had a light lunch at 1 — + after that, altho' feeling very weak, I went out to Riverside Drive + sat on a bench there for more than an hour. With some weariness I worried thro' the rest of the day + evening feeling that I had made little, if any, physical progress.
