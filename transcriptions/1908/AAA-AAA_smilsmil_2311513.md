## Left Page

### Mon. March 16, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fickle day - some sunshine - some clouds \+ threats of showers - At 5 A.M. after an uncomfortable night, I got up \+ took a dose of castor oil - It got to work promptly \+ all the m[illegible]. I was busy. This is not the place - if it were I could not describe the revelations - Our insides \+ their way of doing business are passing strange -. I was useless all day \+ very wretched - I sat at open window a long time in the afternoon - Later I began to feel better - Jim came at 6 20 Gil. Kenny at 6 45 \+ we had dinner - Later they went out - at 8.20 Willie Cook came \+ visited with me till after 9 - I got to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Tues. March 17, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Unpleasant - wet about the middle of the day \+ did not clear. I got up feeling encouraged after a restful night but my discomforts, kidney \+ gall bladder soon came on \+ again I am disheartened. I got to studio soon after 10 \+ spent the rest of the m[illegible] working on Port Marie plate - Then I went down paper for printing to-morrow I got home at about 2 o'clk. had a light lunch - I felt too uncomfortable to accomplish anything but managed to write a note to Clarence B. Smith about Tax assessment on studio. Eve[illegible]. Jim had invited an old time Morse-school friend, Wadsworth, to dinner - He did not come till 7 o'clk Then we had dinner - W- stayed till 10 - Then we went to bed.
