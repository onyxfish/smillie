## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 30, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold \+ brilliantly cloudless. Ther. 6° at 8 a.m. \+15° at 2.30 p.m. I got up \+ had my bkft. with Jim before 8 - I continued my work of yesterday although feeling in no mood for it - physical conditions too insistently assertive - still, I am slowly improving - I went out at noon \+ walked for some time on the sunny side of the Ave. but it was hard work - After lunch I sat at an open window, in the sunshine, for some time, well wrapped up - I find these lonesome days very long \+ wish that I could get out among people - I ate my dinner alone at 6.30. Eve'g. more writing of titles on photos.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 31, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came from New Haven.

Clear day - very still \+ cold in early m'g. Ther. at 8 a.m. 4° growing warmer thro' the day - overcast at night. To studio before 10. Clara Williams there cleaning up - Carpenters \+ painters have made much dirt - I labored with her till 12 - At 1 o'clk I went to N. Tuttle's - saw him - he is sure that his diagnosis is correct - in addition to my kidney I now have my gall bladder to reckon with. The latter, he says, has been the cause of my trouble for the past few days, the stomach \+ digestion generally being involved - Home - lunch at 2 - afternoon worked awhile upon the summary of Father's work that I am preparing in connection with his autobiographic sketch - Ralph came in before 5 o'clk - We had dinner at 6.30 \+ at 8 J. R. \+ I went to the Euterpine Theatre \+ saw Maude Adams in a foolish play, in rhymed verse, called "The Jester." One thing only I could say - \+ that is much now-a-days - it was entirely clean \+ decent - Maude is an engaging personality with a quick, nervous vivacity that went far to redeem the poverty of the thing - It was over before 11 - then home \+ to bed at 12.
