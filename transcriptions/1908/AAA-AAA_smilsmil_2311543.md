## Left Page

### Fri. May 15, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Weather moderately pleasant, windy and cooler — To studio before 10 \+ Resumed my work of laying aquatint grounds — I got a good ground on "Frauenkirche" — Home to lunch \+ back to studio — cleaned Pont Marie plate \+ then varnished the three ready for the acid \+ went home very tired \+ feeling sick — I had dinner alone, Jim dining at the Dielmans — I managed to write a short letter to Ralph \+ then to bed with Temp. considerably above normal.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 16, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day, after a wretched night, with headache, sore throat \+ fever, from 101½° down to 99°. I suppose the trouble is a "cold" the result of the warm weather — when the weather out of doors was 60°, in my studio it would be 62° or 64° \+ I would be uncomfortable — At about 4 P.m. I got up, wrapped in blankets \+ sat in big chair while my bed \+ Room was airing.
