## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 7, 1908

**Weather:** Clouded but not unpleasant — milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio before 10 \+ spent most of the m'g bleaching old prints with Javelle water made up from a receipt that I have. The process worked well with some stains but was not always successful — I had hoped to be able to bleach an old copy that I have of Rogers' "Pleasures of Hope" Ile. by Turner \+ Stothard but the straining on that did not yield — I am sorry — my copy (from Father) of Pleasures of Memory — a uniform ed'n is in good condition, barring one strain that goes thro' a portion of the book \+ I looked to get Hope into equally good condition \+ have it bound to match "memory" — Home to lunch before 2 and the afternoon was wasted pooling and fussing over a lot of last summers negatives that in one way or another are defective \+ I was trying to take them, mostly by working with a stump so that I may give them place in the boys albums —     Dinner at 6.30 — Evg. anum unbound periodicals — getting them into shape for the binder — wrote several notes —

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 8, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mistily overcast day — the sun being visible all the time — veiled — Up at 6.30 had a bath — then back to bed — a nap — \+ bkfst. at about 9 — After that, till 11.30 wrote to Charlotte Kent \+ read papers — then out, had a long walk on Riverside — then to Columbia \+ home to dinner at 2 — loafed during remainder of afternoon — Charl came in at about 5 — I had supper at 6.30 — \+ at 7.30 went to the Irelands, 331 E. 17th St — \+ spent the evg with Emma, Mary, Grace \+ Eliz. Howe.
