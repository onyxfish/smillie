## Left Page

### Fri. June 26, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Jim ret'd late.*

Altho' warm, a delightful freshness in the air I had bkfs'. before 8' \- at 9.30 I saw Charl while he was at bkfs'. at Union league Club. Gave him letter from Mary to read \- To studio at 10 \- worked till 12 \+ got things stuck away \+ cleared up. Then to Ottendorfer's \+ had my hair cut \- then home \+ had lunch \- A card from Jim saying that he w'd. get home at about 6 \- About 4 a Telegram from him \- "Harris missing wire if any word may arrive this evef" \- As it was without punctuation it was difficult to know how to read it \- no word from Ralph \- All of which is very disturbing to me \- I did a good deal of picking up \+ packing up. Stewart sent for the rugs. I had a lonely dinner at 6.30 \- Evef wrote some notes \- had a walk in Riverside \- My sore throat \+ catarrh in head that commenced while at New Haven still bothers me a good deal, interfering with my comfort \+ my work. Jim came in after 10, much disturbed because he could get no trace of Harris.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 27, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant sunny day with cool refreshing breeze. I got up much out of sorts The trouble in head \+ throat making me feel very wretched \- after bkfs'. I went to D'. Tuttle for advice \- I got it \- Castor Oil \- I took it.

The day was spent in general work about the house \- lunch with Jim \- He went to the Yale\-Harvard Ball game \- A letter from David Harris came from which we learned that he had not been lost at all. Dinner \- Jim went to Yale Club \+ I spent the evef writing.
