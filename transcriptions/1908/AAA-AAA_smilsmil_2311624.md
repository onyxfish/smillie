## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 26, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain that did'nt amt. to much in the m'f heavy clouds \+ a gleam or two of light in the afternoon \- M'f. I went to Stikeman's with copy of the Wilson Cat. \+ gave instructions about binding it \+ the other books that I left there on Sat. Then to studio \+ spent a couple of hours in arranging the Nos. of Roberts' Holyland for the binder \- It was 2 o'clk. when I got home to lunch \- then I went out to see the Fire Dep't parade \+ review on Riverside Drive. \- There was a fine show dash down the hill from 86th St. made by four immense engines \+ tenders \+ two hook \+ ladders. Then I got a place on a rock knowle \+ saw a display on the river by four fire boats that moved about; gigantic fountains, each boat hid from view by the water fall of which it was the centre. \- Dinner at 6.30 \- by invitation Jim's friends David Harris \+ Rob't. Merritt came \+ dined with us \- Kate gave us a very good dinner \+ I think the young men enjoyed it. [illegible — ~4 lines]

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 27, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Peculiar weather conditions to-day. Very dull m'f growing darker till 11 o'clk. when the gloom was like that of a London fog that hangs high \- then a flash of lightning \+ peal of thunder \- a very little rain \- more at about 3 \- then cessation \- I wrote to Ralpth \+ sent him a check \- at 10 I called at Chas. Collins' \+ saw him \+ his sister \- then to studio \- when it got to be so very dark I went home \- finished my letter to Mr. M'f Davis (Fanny Wright) \+ had lunch \- after that I got together photos of Fanny Wright \+ her children, running back many years, made a proper pkg. \+ mailed them to Mrs. D \- Then for the rest of the afternoon I wrestled with the balancing of my bank acct. A short walk before dinner \- Eve'f with Nov. magazines.

Got a note from Melanie Atherton telling that her sister Sarah was now getting along nicely after her operation for appendicitis.
