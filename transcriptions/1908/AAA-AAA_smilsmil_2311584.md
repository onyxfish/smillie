## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 5, 1908

**Weather:** Dull mf  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull mf \- light showers about noon \- increasing heavy showers afternoon \+ eve'g. It was after 10 when we started out for a walk \- Took a look at the Opera House \- went to Cook's \+ had English gold changed for French \- then along the Boulevards to the Madelaine, then to Pl. de la Concorde \+ the gardens of the Tuileries when it commenced to rain \- we had to beat a retreat \+ get to hotel in a cab. Letters from Charl \+ Nicol \- had lunch \- After that as the rain had ceased cue went to the Louvre \+ spent the p.m there in the maze of endless galleries \- When we came out at 5 it was raining heavily \- between showers we rode to hotel in cab. I had a rest before dinner at 7\- I'm afraid that I'm going to have more trouble here finding suitable food than in London Ever. for some time in the reading room before going to my bedroom \-

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 6, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of clouds \+ sunshine with threats of rain \+ light sprinkling at times \- I got up at 7 \+ wrote a letter to Charl, addressed to him on the Kronprinzessin Cecilie at Plymouth. I had bkft. at 8. Called the boys at 9 \- bkft. together \- Then out \+ walked to the Champs Elysees \- to the grand Palace of Art \- which we entered to find a general exposition of an industrial character in preparation \- in the galleries a lot of indifferent paintings were hung. I am sorry to have missed the Regular Salon Exbt. of paintings \- we wandered thro' the bld'g. then across the street to the Petite Gallery of Art. These two bld's form an imposing group, together with the Alexander III bridge \+ surrounding gardens \+ parks. They are admirably adapted for exbt. purposes. The Grand Gallery is where the Salon (I believe) exhibits its Annual show \- The Petite is where paintings \+ sculpture belonging to the nation's seen \- It seemed to be a temporary system the walls being scantily covered by paintings, with few exceptions, of no especial merit, some, so far as I could understand, of no merit at all \- There was a very large de Neuville \- war subject \- an Hermitte, a large market scene \- a Laurens \+ a Maignan that attracted me. we walked back to the Place de la Concord \+ then by cab to Hotel \- after lunch \- we walked to Notre Dame \+ then to the Palais de Justice \- to Ste Chapelle \- Thro' the Galarie Marchands to the great Salle des pas Perdus \- \+ then found that it was 4 o'clk \- \+ just too late to get into the Conciergerie or old prison \- Then I left the boys \+ Ret'd. to hotel \- I had my dinner alone in Restaurant \- as simple as possible \- Parisian cooking does not suit my machinery \- Eve'g. the boys went out \+ I stayed in \- For half an hour we were without light, owing indirectly to the general strike that is being imperfectly carried out.
