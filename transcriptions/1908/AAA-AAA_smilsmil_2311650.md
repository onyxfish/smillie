## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine winter day, brilliantly clear, not too cold + reasonably quiet. Bkfs. at 8 - then with photos, till 11 - then to Bausch + Lombs - 5 Ave. + 27 St. + got Engraving lenses left there some time ago for polishing + new frames - then to the Am. Art Ass. galleries + saw a collection of paintings by a new light. H. O. Tanner Scriptural subjects - treated in the rude, slovenly manner that is the fad of the day - as compositions embodying emotional thought they made no impression on me unemotional clay - then to studio - Took Mignot painting to Clausen's + left it there for the frame - Home to lunch at 1.30 Photos - 2.30 to 4.30 - then a Rest - Dinner 6.30 - Eve'g. boys went to theatre I spent most of the eve'g reading Weitenkampf's "How to appreciate prints" - + after getting thro two chapters had to give it up for want of appreciation - a shallow, pedantic waste - a gathering of scraps from much reading with a jumble of many names - as it impresses me. I shall try to think better of it.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 20, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day - clear + quiet - Ther. above 40°. up at 8 + had a good scrub - Bkfs. at 9 - then with newspapers till after 11 - then a three mile walk on Riverside - I worked awhile on photos before dinner at 2 - Jim + Ralph were up by that time - afternoon, before 4 o'clk I went to Geo. Haven Putnam's taking with me the two books on "Books + Their Makers" to have him inscribe his autograph in them - not in - then a walk + on my return found him at home - I had a charming visit with him + his wife - he wrote his autograph as desired - Eve'g. I went to meeting of People's Institute in the large hall of Cooper Union - a memorial to Bishop Potter. There was an "immense crowd + I had difficulty in getting even standing room on the platform where there was an orchestra of 40 + a chorus of 50. After Tannhauser Prof. Chas. Sprague Smith made the opening address - then, by chorus, orchestra + audience "O God our Help in Ages Past" - then Rev. Percy + Dickinson Grant gave us a prosy address concerning Bishop Potter "the Man" - followed by Chorus + orchestra under direction of Rich'd. H. Warren - then Rabbi Joseph Silverman - on the liberalism of Bish'p. Potter - I had never heard the Rabbi before - a rather small, slight man with resonant voice - he was animated, swinging his arms wide in energetic gesture - he is a good speaker - holding his audience well + bore earnest personal testimony to the liberality of Potter in religious creeds - more music - Jubilate Amen - + then John Mitchell on B'd of Organiz'd Labor - The audience received him with great favor - he is a fairly good speaker - a man of natural talent I was much interested in watching + listening to him - then Dvorak's "At the Hero's Grave" - as I stood between the kettle, snare + bass drums the effect was impressive to me - then Seth Low - "B'p. P. + the Publica" - a smooth + by comparison, polished address - The effort of a trained man - Richard Watson Gilder recited a poem he had written — then the Hallelujah Chorus - Then, Booker T. Washington - the audience gave him a tumultuous reception, far exceeding that given to any other speaker - B.T.W. is a big burly negro - is a well marked negro tho not very dark in color, his face is heavy his mouth is large - while speaking he has a somewhat twitching nervous grimace that does not add to his beauty - he waited, bowing stiffly - a mere nod of the head, while the crowd applauded - then launched directly into his subject - B.P. + "The Negro" - He has a good strong voice - is a ready speaker + has little of negro or southern characteristics in his pronunciation - His language was simple - his manner earnest + entirely unaffected - It was very evident that he had his audience with him - then a Chorale - then the last movement of Beethoven Fifth Symphony - after 11 when service was over.
