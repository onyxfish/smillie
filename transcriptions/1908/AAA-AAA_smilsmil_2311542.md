## Left Page

### Wed. May 13, 1908

N.A.D. meeting \+ dinner.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hotter yet — Ther. getting up to 84°. To studio at 10 — took Mignot's panel out of press \+ found that every part of mounting of the old canvas \+ the backing with duck had gone into place without flaw. Spent some time trimming to a cut line the lining where it came from the back over onto the face \+ then replaced it in press. Worked on p.p. till 12.30 — then to N.A. \+ had lunch with the members — a large number out — more than a third of them I did not know — younger men that have come in since my active days — The business meeting was without special interest. Sat for a time with Saml. Colman \+ was amazed to learn that he has a son three months old — Colman is in his 76th year — a remarkably virile old fellow in spite of years \+ years of invalidism — meeting adjourned at 6 o'clk. after electing two painters — two architects \+ two wood engravers. I then took a turn thro' the school rooms \+ had a look at the exhibits of school work — it seemed to me uniformly bad — the students do not seem to be taught the elements of art — drawing almost uniformly bad — \+ the technique coarse \+ vulgar. I took a short saunter with J.C. Nicoll in search of air — upon Morrison side terrace we came upon half a dozen N.A. on same search — we got to N.A. at 7 \+ at 7.30 sat down to dinner — very good for such a dinner — the associates dined with the N.As \+ as a general thing the behavior was good — Ben. Fraser, Curran \+ others made a noisy — B.F. surprised me. If he had imbibed less his manners would have been better — after dinner, things dragged — there were no good speakers — no spirit — Dialman called upon several who, one after another, made excuses \+ then, to my consternation, called upon me — it is a great mortification to me that I cannot "speak in public" — a paralysis falls upon me at once \+ I can only blunder thro' my excuses in a disgraceful manner.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 14, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Threatening weather all day but rain did not come till night — I got to studio about 10 \+ spent the m'f in getting my aqua-tint box cleaned up \+ in position for work — quite a job. I had the copper plate, "Port Marie, Paris" prepared, that is, with lines filled with asphaltum varnish, \+ surface perfectly clean — I laid an experimental ground but was not successful — I got home to lunch at 1.30 — a short rest \+ back at about 3. Guided by results of morning work I prepared "Zinc Plate" Roadside Chapel near Lugano" \+ was successful ~~never successful~~ — at least, so it appears — The final test will be when the acid is put upon it — Then I can see clearly the texture of the ground as I cannot because of the sheen of the polished plate thro' the transparent mastic. More experience will, of course, enable me to judge before the acid test — It was about 5.30 when I left the studio — Dinner with Jim — Eve'g Isaac Woodbridge Riley — son of "Ike" of old Montrose days, called — He is to be Prof. of Philosophy at Vassar \+ would like to know something about Po'keepsie as a place to live in — I advised that he limit his expectations severely.
