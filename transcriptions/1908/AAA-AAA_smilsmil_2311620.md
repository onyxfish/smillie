## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 18, 1908

**Weather:** Very mild & smoky  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild & smoky - about 10 am. Jim & I took car for down Town - then by Laurel line (or cannon ball) Electric R.R. - we went in 40 min. to Wilkesbarre - then on Lehigh Valley R.R. platform met Meline Atherton & we took 11.40 Train to Glen Summit where the Athertons have their Summer home - another 40 min. ride - at the station met by colored coachman \+ team \+ drove at once to the house \+ met very cordially by Mr. & Mrs. A - learned then that Sarah was suffering from what seemed to be a severe bilious attack - both Mr. & Mrs. A. were evidently anxious - a Dr. came up from Wilkes Barre with us, summoned by 'phone - he gave medicine & advice & left immediately after dinner. Mrs. A. took Meline & Jim & me out for a ride - the team was a good one \+ the road admirable for hard smoothness & the air, having a considerable percentage of smoke, delightful. The views might have been fine if we could have seen them - there was a sense of high upland all the time & over wide stretches of low scrubby growth, following far, it was barren - in a very few places only was there any thing like a forest growth - altogether, horses, air \+ road combined with good company made a very restful & refreshing ride - I had a nap before the evening meal - then the misses Hollenback \+ the sister, misses [illegible], nieces of Mr. A - came in - I had a pleasant chat with Miss Stites who is head of a school in Wilkes Barre, on educational matters & influences that more or moved the juvenile mind \+ character.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very smoky day - dull altho' the sun was visible all day like a copper ball - a chill in the air that was rather unpleasant - Up soon after 6. Mr. & Mrs. A. had had a trying night with Sarah who now is showing symptoms of appendicitis - consequently we got ourselves out of the way as promptly as possible - had [illegible] at 7. \+ with the twins (who go to school in Wilkes Barre) left on 8 o'clk Train getting to W.B. at 8.40 - left there on Laurel line (Electric) at 8.50 \+ got to Scranton at 9.40 \+ then by Green Ridge line & street cars to the house at 10 - then I varnished "Vesuvius" \+ the Lake George (paintings) - then to my room & had a nap - lunch at 1. \+ immediately after, taken by Parke, Jim & I started on an automobile ride - we had a great ride out on the Boulevard & around some Reservoirs - the road wonderfully good, no scenery because of the smoke but we could see enough to be sure that some parts were wide \+ interesting & often really charming altho' most of the country was bleak, high & fire burned - the road has a great many short twists & turns - heavy grades up \+ down hill - it seemed to me that our chauffeur took the turns \+ grades madly but was told that we did not exceed 40 miles an hour - at about 3 o'clk - we drew up at Mr. Jas. Shepherd's house - he has a rather remarkable collection of paintings - largely Mauves \+ Moris, with Israels, Corot \+ others \+ mins - very quiet, midday, Tinyon. Ryden, Blakelock \+ other Americans - He is an enthusiast \+ pumped full by Ros-Macbeth & is interesting to me in his person as all such collectors are. At 4 Park \+ I left, went to his office, there Helen \+ Jim joined us \+ I, with H.Y. went home leaving Parke down Town. Dinner at 7 - Priscilla Beatty Poor with us - we talked much of old Montrose days - later her son \+ his wife came in.

Sarah Atherton was operated upon for Appendicitis this afternoon - Well we heard by 'phone that she was coming from under the influence of chloroform \+ that the operation had been successful.
