## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 18, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - Up before 8 - Rec'd. from Ralph a note that upset me - says he has enlisted as apprentice seaman Conn. Nat. Guard Naval Battalion for three years + sends me enlistment papers to sign approval. I at once wrote asking for further information. Then I went to the Globe Wernicke Co - + got to studio at about 11 - Some work on G.D. plate + home to lunch at 2 o'clk - At 3 o'clk - I went to the dentist, Dr. Grace - Ansonia - 74° 5'. He had the retained upperest [illegible] teeth - not altogether satisfactory - After that a walk on Riverside - Home at about 5 + had a rest - File cabinet from Globe-Wernicke was delivered - Dinner at 6.30 - Qus'y Jim + I went to Concert of the University Glee Club at Waldorf-Astoria - A very dressy audience - went early + got choice seats - Lochimvar, by W.A. Hammond was given with much spirit + so pleased the audience that an encore was insisted upon + it was given, altho' a long piece. The composer played the piano accompaniment \+ Reinald Werrenbroth sang an incidental solo very acceptably - Altogether it was an agreeable entertainment - We got home after 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, cold + windy - Up + out down + Up. at about 9 - The rest of the morning till after 11 was given to getting my collection of photos into the file-cabinet that I bought of the Globe-Wernicke Co - Before noon George Cook + Emily came + stayed till near 1 - Then I went out + walked on Riverside Drive till near 2 - Then in + had dinner with Jim - Before 3.30 [illegible] came in + stayed till about 5 - Then I rested till after 6 - had a light meal + at 7.30 Jim + I went to Cooper Institute - People's Institute - we had platform seats. Rev. Thos. R. Slicer spoke - Second lecture of series of three "Three ways of doing good - This one treated of doing good to save trouble - self-defense - The audience was interesting to me - largely men - young + middle-aged - very few old men - few young women - all evidently of middle or lower class - The lecture interested them - Slicer's way of putting things pleased them - After the lecture questions were asked by different ones in the audience + S - answered with great readiness + spirit - It surprised me that from such an audience there did not come more troublesome questions because no doubt there were among them Representatives of all sorts of social unrest - I think these exercises must be productive of good - The conduct of all was orderly + quiet. There was some singing.
