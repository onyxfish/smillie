## Left Page

### Sat. July 4, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Portions of the very fine — m't mild — p.m.
breeze freshened + much cooler — I spent the
m't wandering about somewhat dis comforted
by the cathartics that I have been obliged to take
I passed lunch as unnecessary — a large
part of the afternoon I spent on my bed sleep-
ing — dinner at 7 — Eve't — for a time in
the smoking room watching the auctioning
of pools on the ship's run for to-morrow — the
pool amounted to between 50 + 100 dollars —
Another dance in the eve't.

Sunday — 5th
The man who bought the "lower field" at
the auction for $50 offered to sell it this
m't for $5.00 when he saw the weather
was so fine, but found no taker — The lower
limit was 524 miles — It was a great sur
prise to every one when 519 m, the lowest
run yet made, was posted — As there
was $510 in the pool he made $460

## Right Page

### Sun. July 5, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day with some wonderfully beautiful spots
in it. There were two or three hazy spells, pleasant,
but the intervals were brilliant — a cool breeze
from N. W. probably about 60° our room, which
has kept at about 70° fell to 65°. Awakened at
7 o'clk by the band playing a fine selection, not
familiar to me — I miss the familiar bugle call
in the m't. There has been no religious service
to-day that I know of — I got up + had bkf't soon
after — the day passed like the other days —
I had no lunch — I wrote, walked + slept —
Dinner at 7 — a little more ornate — confusion among
passengers who are to land at Plymouth + Cherbourg —
Eve't a concert, but we could not get into it — I
paid stewards as far as possible — The eve't was
wet outside + disagreeable — not rain but heavy
mist — There was a concert + Prof. Adler made
a short address but we did not get in — we
packed the steamer trunks + cleaned up as
far as possible.
