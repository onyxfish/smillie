## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 3, 1908

**Weather:** Rouen  **Temp:**

Grand Hotel d'Angleterre - Room 3.

Another superb day - up before 7 - bkfst at 8 - away from hotel at 9 - got to Victoria Station in 25 m - luggage weighed - Trunks registered to Paris - Then to Train - a great crowd - found seats in 1st cl. with difficulty - two fellows who had taken window seats had 2d cl. tickets - we got away promptly at 10 - The run to New Haven of no special interest - got to the dock at 11.30 \+ considering the crowd \+ amt. of luggage, a quick transfer was made to the steamboat Dieppe - a fair sized boat, poorly planned for the comfort of passengers of either class - 2ds were huddled aft - got no chair after much difficulty, p'd for it - Got away at 12 - sea perfectly smooth air delightful - at 1 we had, after patient waiting, an atrocious lunch, poor in every respect - 9/6 for the three of us - Got to Dieppe at about 3 - by handling our own luggage we got ashore \+ into the Custom house quickly - little delay in going thro' the form of examination - no trouble in getting an apartment, 1st cl. to ourselves - At about 3.30 the train started - a very long one - The ride was pleasant - much of it thro' rolling country - farmers harvesting grain - Got to Rouen at 5.25 - drove to hotel - got rooms on the Premier Stage\* - then started out - The Hotel is on the river front where things are not picturesque - We went up to the Cathedral - Then into it - I like the facade very much - seen to disadvantage now - so much of it is covered by scaffolding for restoration work. I don't care for the inside. Then we went to the Palais de Justice - the big clock \+ back to hotel where I had a roast \+ then, after 7 - dinner - after that we sauntered out to a nearby bridge \+ watched the river for awhile - I ret'd to the hotel \+ the boys continued their wandering. I got to bed early -

\* on the mezzanine - not the premiere.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 4, 1908

**Weather:**   **Temp:**

Paris - Hotel Chatham - Room 179.

A hot, misty day - with some signs upon which I based predictions of Rain - Up soon after 7 - Got the boys up at about 8 \+ then had bkfst - after which we started out to "do" the Town - we went to the Cathedral \+ thro' it - as I see it again I can see more that interests me - Ralph wanted to climb to the top of the Spire, 320 steps but there was no time - Then to St. Maclou - Then to St. Ouen - then back to the Palais de Justice \+ went into the large halls but did not get entrance to the Courts - then to the clock, getting a good many snapshots on the way \+ buying a number of photo. views - It was after 1 when we got to hotel - had lunch in Restaurant. Then we went by electric Train up a steep hillside to Bonsecours, about two miles where there is a new \+ much decorated church \+ monument to Jeane d'Arc - From a terrace there is a view up \+ down the Seine that is very popular - It is a fine view but I am sure that the one from a height nearer the city must be finer in some ways - I have a memory of it when father \+ I climbed to it 46 years ago - we spent a little time on the terrace \+ ret'd to the hotel \+ paid our bill amt'g to 75 f or about $15 - for one day for the three of us - an extravagant price - It is a plainly good hotel but badly placed - I saw two others, De la Poste \+ Du Nord, that looked more inviting - We rode to P.R. to take train on which we arr'd yesterday - the boat train to Paris. On the advice of the Porter who told us that it wd. be very difficult to carry out suit cases into our carriage, because as he said "tout le monde" wd. be on the train, we consented to have it registered - The boat train was an hour late but another train took its place \+ we went on at 5 o'clk - part of the ride by the side of the Seine was interesting but not very - The country is generally flat \+ the harvest of grain is being gathered - It looked to be very full - We got to Paris at a little before 7 - A Cook's agent helped us greatly in getting our trunks from the Custom Ho - The examination was a farce - our suit cases that should have been on the train with us could not be found \+ we had to go to hotel without them - being told that they were probably on next train - We drove to Chatham - got our Rooms - had dinner, then the boys ret'd to station \+ got cases without trouble - I went to the Hotel Athénée, not far away, to see the Athertons - They were out. I ret'd to hotel \+ unpacked.
