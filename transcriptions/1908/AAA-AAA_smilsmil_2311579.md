## Left Page

### Sun. July 26, 1908

**Weather:** Richmond & Kew.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A remarkably fine summer day - after my bath, the m't. being so fine I decided that it was too good a day for an out-of-town trip to be lost - I communicated with the boys + it was agreed to go to Richmond - It was about 10 when we got away, by tube to Hammersmith, thence by overhead Trolley Tram to Kew Bridge - we passed long stretches of exceedingly pretty little 2 story homes with front gardens brilliant with flowers + very well kept - Trees lined the way in parts - Linden + Plane - as we rode on top of the Tram we had good views - crossed Kew Bridge on foot, then took horse tram, again on top, + rode to Kew - There we had a long walk to Star & Garter - we should have ridden - The walk along the Terrace, under the old elms, before we reached the Star was lovely + we lingered - We got to Star's + had a 3/6 lunch under an awning + overlooking the garden terrace, the River below + the far-stretching country afar - partly forest covered - Then out on terrace for awhile - then down to River, hired a boat, boys rowing, J. cox wain, + up the River abt. the tide - the River was crowded with all sorts of craft - large steamboats, motors, row boats, punts, canoes, sail boats. The current ran strong - movements of other boats erratic + I had a hard time - we had two or three bumps - nothing serious - back to landing at 4.30 - bus to Richmond Bridge - Taxicab to Kew Gardens - Spent an hour there - then bus at gate - Rode on top to Notting Hill - might have gone on to Oxford Circus - at H. H. tube to Russell Sq. getting to hotel at 7 - I rested for half an hour + then had a light dinner in the Restaurant - Evg. Concert in Palm Room - Sat with W. + Mrs. Chas. Parks - arrived yesterday - called by the Athertons to attend Sarah A. who was taken ill while visiting the Tennants - After 9 I wrote a letter to Kate Leonard + to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 27, 1908

**Weather:** Hampton Court.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beautiful day - seeing it so fine in the early m't. I planned to go to Windsor but before we were all ready I found that the earliest R.R. Train that we could get w'd be from Waterloo at 12 - So, we went by Tube to Hammersmith, thence by electric Tram to Hampton - I w'd not care to repeat that experience but do not regret it as it gave us a chance to see some London suburbs - pretty in parts but uninteresting in the main - acres of hot houses covering tomato vines + acres more of open truck gardens - We got to Hampton after 12 + wandered about till 1 o'clk - then went to "The Castle" hotel where we had a poor lunch on a terrace overlooking the River - Then we went to the Palace + I was pleased to find that we were permitted to take a camera into the gardens - It was a wonderful display of the gardener's art - Every thing worked together to heighten the beauties of the flowers - The Turf + the walks - mass - The sunshine + the air were intoxicating - of its kind nowhere - I never have I seen such flowers + such masses of them. I took a lot of snap shots + hope that I have some good pictures - We strolled thro' all the gardens - saw the old grapevine + the Maze - then we went thro' the greeneries + into the covered Tennis Court + saw some strange Tennis play, the side walls being used to deflect the balls - After 4 o'clk. we were thro' + started for the Station - We saw a small steamboat ready to start - It was going to Richmond + we said "on to Richmond" + got aboard - It took us 1¼ hour to get to Richmond - Some parts of the River - its banks + the residences are beautiful, but the greater part is common place - At R- we walked thro' town to the District Railway Station + by train to Hammersmith - There we changed to the Piccadilly Tube + got to our hotel at 7.30 - I was tired, went to my room + slept for an hour - at 8.40 I had dinner alone + after that sat for awhile with W. + Mrs. Atherton who have rel'd. to the hotel with Sarah + Eleanor - To bed at 11.
