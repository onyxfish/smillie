## Left Page

### Mon. April 27, 1908

**Weather:** overcast with spells of sunshine — mild but tempered by a rather raw east wind.  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast with spells of sunshine — mild but tempered by a rather raw east wind. To studio at 10.30 \+ spent rest of m'g cleaning zinc plates. Frauen Kirche — laying ground and adding more work preparatory to aquatint. Home \+ had lunch at 2 — about 3 o'clk. I gave up to a feeling of heaviness owing to almost sleepless nights for a couple of nights past \+ had a nap. About 4 went to Fr. Grace. Ausonia — about repairs to a set of teeth — then sauntered on Riverside. Dinner with Jim — Eve'g with magazines \+ writing.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 28, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Alternations of grey day \+ sunshine — mild \+ pleasant — To studio about 10 — added some etched work to Chapel near Lugano \+ bit it \+ the work on Frauen Kirche — It was 1.30 when I left studio — home \+ lunch after 2 o'clk — Afternoon I wrote a number of notes and sauntered till after 6 in Riverside Park — Dinner alone at 6.30 as Jim had to remain at the office — At 8 I went to Carnegie Hall — Jim joined me there a little later — \+ we heard "Big Bill" Taft. on Delays \+ Defects in the Enforcement of the law in this Country — substituted at the last moment for the one announced "Influence of American Institutions on Popular Government in the world".
