## Left Page

### Mon. May 25, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day, notwithstanding a somewhat chilly east wind \- P.k.p. after 8 \- To studio at about 10.30 after stopping at Century \+ leaving there my letter to the Com. on Admissions, seconding the name of Mr. H. T. Kingsbury with accompanying note for W. Geo. Mr. Knox Sec'y of Com. Spent some time at studio in getting everything ready for the aquatint biting on "Chapel near Lugano" \- It was near noon before I put the plate into the acid \- Lunch at home at 2 \- at 3 went again to studio \- completed biting \- cleaned plate \+ worked on it with burnisher \+ scraper \- I cannot tell yet what the effect will be \- it looks rather sandy. I left the studios at 5 o'clk \- walked awhile under the trees on Riverside Drive \- luminous atmosphere over the River \- Home after 6 \- Dinner with Jim at 7 \- Quiet eveng. Reading.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 26, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool, overcast mtf., east wind \- feeling like rain but before noon it cleared muggy hot, Ther. going to 80° \- very uncomfortable \- P.k.p. with Jim as usual \- To studio after 10 \+ on getting to work on "Chapel near Lugano" found that by over biting on the second immersion the plate is in bad shape \- all of the upper sky is much too dark \+ some "stopping out" of cloud forms shows very harsh \- It is a great disappointment \- I worked till 1.30 \- home to lunch \- back at 3 \+ worked till 5.30 trying to correct the error by scraping out, burnishing \+ rubbing down all upper part \+ cannot yet determine what will be the outcome \- Home after 6 \- dinner alone before 7 \- Jim off with Gould for Coney Island or somewhere thereabouts. Evng wrote a letter to H. H. Aronson \- Heavy thundershower.
