## Left Page

### Mon. July 6, 1908

The weather in the early m+ was unpromising - misty damp + cold wind - afternoon was delightful - Up at 6 o'clk + finished the last of the packing + ready to go ashore - We were running along the coast, low + uninteresting - we had our bkfp. at 8 - then some weary waiting - The pilot came aboard at 10 + it was after, perhaps, 20 m. that we dropped anchor - Two landing boats came - one took mail - the other passengers + luggage - they were alongside nearly an hour - then aim to the dock 15 or 20 minutes - at 11.40 we set foot on English soil - some impatient waiting while luggage was being brought into customs shed - when we were let in the form of search was quickly gone thro' - I had my London tickets exchanged - for Bristol £ 1.10. in cash - our luggage was piled up on a cab - drove to North Road R.R. Station - caught by close shave train (12.32) to Bristol. It had dining car + we had a fairly good meal - got to Bristol at 4 o'clk - 12 m. beyond Yatton, the junction of Road to Wells - as 5½p. did not stop - at B. we had to wait till 5.25 for train to Wells - direct, without change at Y. - We took open carriage + drove for an hour thro streets - all alive in preparation for festivities on the 9th when King Edward + the Queen will come to "open" a new dock - B. is 4 miles from the sea - B. is a swarming, bustling, dingy town with persistently crooked streets + much uphill - Tawdry decorations of all sorts were being put up - paper festoons - banners + flag staffs - temporary stands along line of march - seats 5/ - + 7/6 - Back to station + took 5.25 train, getting to Wells at 7. The country thro which we passed from Portsmouth to Bristol + to Wells is agricultural, pleasant but rather monotonous. We rode at once to The Swan Inn - a comfortable looking old house - exceptionally well placed in relation to the Cathedral - The afternoon was still bright + we went out + walked out about the Cath. - + dined us + had a very good dinner at 8 - As we were all tired we went to bed early -

## Right Page

### Tues. July 7, 1908

Beautiful m+ - after 3 o'clk - sky clouded - some Rain at 4.30 which lasted for about half an hour - Up, soon after 7 o'clk - had bkfp. with the boys at us 8 - Then out - went at once to Cathedral + spent the morning outside + in it - Clouds made the light fickle at times but I think I got a number of good pictures - Then I went to a photo shop + spent 11 shillings for prints - While the boys climbed the tower I begged my way in to the Bishop's Garden + got a snap shot at the pool of the well with reflection of Cathedral Tower - We went to hotel at about 1 o'clk + had the usual English Cold lunch - a deadly arrangement - I had boiled eggs - At 2 o'clk - we went by bus to a station - I showed Day scene, that as English provincial inns go, I think the Swan a fair specimen - the Rooms were large + pleasant - the fare good + clean - but it is not cheap - We were there an entire day + the bill averaged $4.50 for each - Wells is a hard place to get at or away from - our little Train left at 2.25 + ran to there we changed to another road + ran a few miles further to Westbury where we again changed + at last, at 4.30 got to Salisbury - It was then raining smartly - owing to Races there were no cabs, busses or other vehicles at station - a Town porter loaded our luggage on to his truck - then, by great good luck a Reddison Hotel bus came up with passengers + the driver took us to The White Hart + the porter followed - It soon stopped raining + then we went out for a prowl - went to Cathedral - met a very quietly mannerly member of the Cath. Staff who showed us all about - On our return to hotel I went to my room + had a little nap. Before dinner - Then I was surprised by a call from Mr. Atherton who with his family - that is, Mrs. A. + two daughters, are now in this hotel - he had arranged that we should all dine together at 7 o'clk - the dinner was a very poor one - altho' because of the Races + unusual number of guests, extra effort was made + an extra shilling chef - After dinner the young people went out for a walk - + I went to my room + dozed away the evng on my bed as I felt tired - About 11 I went to bed for the night -
