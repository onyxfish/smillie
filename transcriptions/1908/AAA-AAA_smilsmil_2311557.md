## Left Page

### Fri. June 12, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another bright day. Ther. got up to 80°. I got to studio before 10 \+ the m'f was absent most of the time in making selections of proofs of my etched \+ eng'd. work to take to Print Dep't. of British museum \+ to the Berlin Print Dep't. Home to lunch — back to studio \+ measured pls. carefully for mounts — It was after 5.30 when I got home — Dinner with Jim who went to a Theatre party later. I wrote to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 13, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — above 80° with plenty of breeze. I got to studio soon after 9 to meet J. C. Nicoll. I offered him the use of the "Ark" for a portion of the summer if he w'd. pay water tax \+ cost of putting the house in order — we had a long pleasant chat with some reminiscing — he will answer my proposition after consulting with his daughter. After I had completed measurements for mounts I went to Clausen's to give the order but it was so near noon, the closing hour that I could not place it — then to Tiffany's \+ got Jim's watch — Then home. Lunch — a busy afternoon \+ eve'g in selecting, putting away or packing things. A walk in the Park before going to bed.
