## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 14, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely mild, hazy fall day — Bkfs. before 8 o'clk. — I spent the mf till 11 o'clk. "spotting" my negatives \+ working over them with stump \+ pencil some of them need a good deal of work because of carelessness at one stage or another — because I did not frequently dust my lens there are many spots that will print black unless stopped out. As I made more than 35 doz. exposures there is much work to be done. At 11 I went to studio — a coal fire had been built in new furnace \+ under 1½ lb. of steam the studio was made uncomfortably hot. Home to lunch at 1.30 Afternoon again at work on negatives — Had a twilight walk on Riverside — In after 6. Dinner — Rest. — Reading.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 15, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm cloudless, hazy day — Ther. getting up to 76° in the afternoon. I finished work on my negatives \+ at 11 o'clk. took them down to Cullen \+ left them to be printed — home to lunch at 1.30 — afternoon went to studio \+ was there till after 5 o'clk. arranging proofs \+ letter press sheets of "Turner Gallery" for binding — then to Palette Co. in 23d St. — bought varnish for Farkes' painting — then to Fiker's \+ home by 6. Ave. "L" at 6.15 Dinner — Rest. — wrote to Ralph.
