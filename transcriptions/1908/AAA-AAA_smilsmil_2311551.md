## Left Page

### Sun. May 31, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful contrast to the weather of yesterday altho' rather too balmy. I had my scrub \+ bath at 6.30 - then back to bed - a nap - then up \+ bkfst. before 9 - then a couple of hours with newspapers \+ reading. Then out for a stroll in Riverside Park. - Wind southerly \+ humid. Exercise not inspiriting - Home at about 10clk - dinner at 2 - alone - Jim went away early this mft. for an outing with Hugh Crowell - At 3.13 I met Chas. at 79th. subway station \+ together we went to the College of the City of N.Y. - each had our cameras - \+ both made several snap-shots - about 4 I left him - he went into the Great Hall to the organ Recital - I went on to George's - up by the New-York University. Sat awhile with him \+ Nellie on porch - then Geo., Gordon \+ I went off for a prowl - finally I left them \+ crossed over to the Subway \+ took train getting in before 7 - I had a nap instead of Supper - then completed the writing of titles \+ dates in one of the 2 vol sets of 1904 Photo Albums - to bed late.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 1, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An April-like day - sunshine \+ showers, with a marked improvement in the quality of the atmosphere which was refreshingly cooler. To studio at about 10 after writing a number of checks at home. I wet down paper \+ took one imp. from Chapeel near [illegible], showing a satisfactory improvement - Home - lunch at 1.30 - at 2.30 went to Hoboken \+ visited the S.S. Kaiser Wilhelm der Grosse \+ saw Stateroom 329, engaged for our passage - away down in the hold. It is a good sized room but it is an inside one \+ insufficiently lighted - on same deck as dining room - which is not a very promising or imposing apartment - we will have to go up several flights of stairs before we can get out upon the promenade deck - I can not think that there is any comparison, on score of comfort, between these fast passenger boats \+ the big freighters - I am glad to know that the voyage will be a short one. Returned by tunnel to N.Y. \+ went downtown to Cullen's by 9th Ave. Elevated, taking two Rolls of films to be developed - then home - had a short walk - then a rest - Dinner with Jim - he went to Willie Cook's in the evefg. \+ I wrote titles under photos. in one of the 1904 albums.
