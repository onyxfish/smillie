## Left Page

### Wed. March 4, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - To studio at 10 - finished the biting on Port Marie \+ worked with graver on Marblehead Neck. Tite near 2 o'clk - Then home \+ had lunch before 3 - During remainder of afternoon I wrote note to W. J.L. Wyckoff of Holyake - Read \+ rested - I did not go out for afternoon walk because of headache - Jim came in just before 7 \+ we had dinner - Suet. Reading \+ to bed early -

## Right Page

### Thur. March 5, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - To studio at 10. worked all the m'f on Port Mare \+ have it ready for proving - at 1.30 as I was preparing to go home to lunch Chas. Huntington came bringing with him the original painting of The Goldsmithie's Daughter - It was after 2 before I got away from studio \+ nearly 3 when I got my lunch - after that I read awhile \+ then went out \+ had a good four mile walk in Riverside Park without feeling fatigued - in at about 6 - Dinner with Jim at 6.30 - Then he went to drill - I tried to arrange 1904 European Trip photo prints \+ negatives with a view to completing the albums that I began for the boys - a stupid headache interfered \+ I went to bed early.
