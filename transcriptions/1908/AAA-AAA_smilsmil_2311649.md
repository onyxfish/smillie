## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 17, 1908

**Weather:** Dull m[illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m[illegible] \- bad weather commenced about noon \- but snow did not fall to lie till evening \- My head & throat seemed to be healing to-day \- I did not dare risk anything, so, remained in-doors \- Ralph helped me loaf \- we read \+ discussed some matters \- I wrote & worked over some photo films & prints \- Jim, Ralph \+ I dined at usual hour \- Jim went to Co. drill in the ev[illegible]. Stikeman the binder sent home a lot of the books he has been binding for me \- They make a brava show.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 18, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beast of a day \- the snow that fell last night covered to the depth of about two inches \+ soon became rain, making things about as dismal as could be in a weather way. It was after 8 before I got my Wife \- Of course, it was impossible for me to go out in such weather, so, I was prisoner again for another day \- I gave the time to reading, writing & photo's \- I made some experimental prints upon old Aristo paper they were failures \- Ev[illegible] Jim went out to a dance \+ Jim Ralph met Mary Cook \+ Jack at a Hockey match \- \+ I worried thro' the ev[illegible] alone.
