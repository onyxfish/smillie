## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 31, 1908

Queens Wilhelmina's Birth Day.
The Elkins left Amsterdam.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright mf. promising a good day — not fully Realized — Katie + Wm left at 10 on their way to Dresden — In the mf. we went to the Ryks museum + devoted ourselves to the pictures there — The exterior of the bldg. is not impressive — a street or public passage way runs thro the center of it — we entered at each side — the passage I ascended at once to the first floor. The vestibule is imposing in size + wall decoration — with a few pieces of sculpture, the vista down the vaulted gallery of Honor with four Cabinets on each side filled with paintings, closed by the fine, well lighted Rembrandt Room is very fine — The Room is no longer devoted to Rembrandt — an extension has been erected + in it The Night Watch has a shrine to itself — a picture that by reproductions of one kind + another I have been familiar with — the painting itself is a great disappointment to me — aside from any merit or demerit, it is in bad condition — a bloom is over it so that the background appears only as a dull grey — the lights are all very yellow — a shade of yellow to be seen on many of Rembrand paintings — I think it must be the vehicle that he used that has changed — Here, at the shrine of this artist, God, I found it impossible to worship — We went Rapidly + carefully thro' the many galleries but before I o'clk — Streets swarming with people — every body decorated with orange — a scarf or a button — we did not know till we got to the hotel the reason — Wilhelmena's birthday — went to hotel — lunch — then out again — I went along the thro' fare, Kulver Straat, narrow + crooked, it was crowded from building to building — all stores closed — in many places the large plate glass in windows had barricades of boards, a precautionary protection as if surging crowds — There were many men in absurd make up, most of them having some means of making a pretence at music of some kind — a barrel organ, a fiddle or guitar — a party of two or three were got up like Negro minstrels + all were soliciting money — I did not see that they were very successful — with difficulty I got to a Kodak supply store but it was closed — At 7 o'clk every body, it seemed, in our part of town was sitting in strong tide down the Buiten Amstel to where there was to be a public display of fireworks — we went to the crowd — a considerable percentage of it rather boisterous but always good natured — singing + shoving in football wedges — uncomfortably in the range of danger — The wedges were made up of frequently of young women + men, equally divided — in many ways the crowd was like a Broadway crowd on Election night — at last we got down to a place + were fortunate in getting an excellent place on a step above where we had unobstructed view of the fireworks, the reflection in the water + the illumination of the crowd by bursting Rockets. That stirred me more than the fireworks — the effect of the set pieces was much marred by the smoke driven toward us by the breeze — the last piece had as a centre a double profile — queen + consort — it was greeted with tremendous enthusiasm — band playing national air — crowd singing + cheering — bells ringing + steam whistles blowing — a perfect babel of noise + hilarity — all over at 10 — then mob dispersed like other city mobs.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 1, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another nasty day — a promise of better things in the early mf + then disappointments. I got up feeling very "rocky" — uncomfortable in many different ways — but I ate my bkfs — then started out with the boys + went to the Palace + went thro' that under guidance — There are some fine apartments there, notably the Great Hall, but it can hardly hold its own against the English or French Royal Palaces — I leave out the German because I think little of them — + the Austrian because we saw only the outside of them — Then I went to a store + bought some films + illustrated Post cards — Then some attempts to get photo. snakes but the light would not serve — back to hotel + there gave up — ate no lunch — took a dose of Castor oil + went to bed — Temp. 101/2°.
