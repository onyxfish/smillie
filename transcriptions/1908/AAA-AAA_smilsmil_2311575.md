## Left Page

### Sat. July 18, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal mf. after heavy rain during the night. The day was a succession of showers - cloudy between times - once in the afternoon the sun shone quite clear for about 20 min. About 10, with the boys I went to Royal Academy exhib \+ we spent the mf. there. The impression of the exhib. as a whole, upon me, is more favorable than the one of last year - As a whole English art is not to my liking ( what a pity!) It lacks color, tone \& technique - for instance, Leaden is a leading land-scapist - his drawing is elaborately careful \+ correct, but his color is anemic, his handling, or brush work is without breadth - everything is chewed up - This criticism applies to most of the landscape work - The figure painting, much of it, is conventional, labored \+ hard in technique - Of course the influence of Sargent \+ Shannon is largely seen but too often it is only slap-dash brush work - one painting stands out, in my mind, far above all the others, Hubert Herkomer's remarkable painting, The Council of the Royal Academy. It is a painting 18 or 20 feet long, at a guess - figures life size - arranged sitting in a row, all looking at some painting not in the picture - a back ground of dull red - light from above. The picture is remarkable in every respect - it is intensely Realistic - seen from the furthest point available the figures stand away from the canvas \+ mingle with the people in front - composition is wonderful - not one figure is posing - it might have been a moment caught by an instantaneous photo. Intense individuality is given in each face - On near approach the color is seen to be strong \+ crude \+ the brush work is brutal - At the proper distance it is a living harmonious unit - one of the most satisfying \+ remarkable paintings that I have ever seen. After making the rounds of the galleries we went to the upper galleries \+ saw the interesting collection of Diploma paintings - Then the boys went their ways - I rel. to Hotel - lunch - Then to Print Dep - saw Mr. Colvin's Assistant - offered to put mounts on pp. that I gave to the Dep. 15 years ago - He said he we attend to that - Then in the Print Gallery, where a fine collec. of Mezzotints is on exhibition - Then to Hotel - a rest - dinner before 7 - Then to the Palace Theatre of Varieties - where we had good front seats in Balcony - \+Bumpati - Bumpati was one of the most absurd, laughter provoking exhib. of clownish tumbling that I ever saw. Radford \+ Winchester performed some wonderful Burlesque Juggling - one of them catching apples upon a fork held in his mouth, that were thrown at him from all parts of the house. Maud Allan gave her Classical Dances clad in garments so diaphenous that her naked body could be seen thro' them. Her Vision of Salome was certainly a novelty - parts picturesque - parts rather shocking - a snake-like grace not altogether pleasing - To hotel \+ to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 19, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull mf. - with rain in showers - the afternoon gave some indications of clearing up - so that we were hopeful for the morrow. I did not get up till late - bath - \+ bkf. before 10 - The Athertons ret. from the north last night - I lounged about all of the mf. - the boys went off somewhere \+ got back after lunch - At about 3. we went by Tube to Charing Cross Thence to Nat. Portrait Gallery \+ traced thro' that - then in to the Nat. Gallery \+ walked thro' those galleries. I pointed out the paintings of most interest as we walked. We got back to hotel at about 6 o'clk. I rested till 7 - then dinner - Eve'g. Concert in the great hall - sat with Mr. \+ Mrs. Atherton till 10.30 - then to bed.
