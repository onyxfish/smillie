## Left Page

### Tues. April 7, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm, cloudless day — Ther. got above 70. I got up at usual time \+ had hkfst. but feet unusually weak \+ uncomfortable — some fever but did not test it — wrote a note to Howard Kingsbury about seconding him a candidate for membership in Century — Then to studio at 10 — all the mtg was spent over the pfs. Taken last week \+ electing, cleaning and wetting down paper for printing to-morrow — Mr. Sands, a neighbor \+ [illegible] who is treating with Tolfree for stable adjoining studio \+ who wants me to run heating pipes into that stable, kept me till 1.30 in discussion — Then home \+ had lunch after 2 o'clk — At 3.30 went down town to \+ got plate — Moonlight — from which steel facing had been removed — Then to Hain — Am. Line — \+ was told that I could have stateroom 407 on the Franklin Deck, down in the bowels of the S.S. Amerika — to sail from Hamburg. Sepr. 17 — Then to 21 State St. saw Clarence Smith — executed my Last Will \+ Testament — Then home — [illegible] I went to bed with Temp. of 101° \+ discomfort about Right Kidney —

## Right Page

### Wed. April 8, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — dark \+ Rainy — muy Temp. last night Rose till about midnight Then fell so that I had a little Restful sleep before 8 A.M. when I had hkfst. — in bed — I got up at about 10 feeling much better — at noon, Temp. normal — at 3 o'clk it was 101° \+ at 6 it had passed 102°. Kidneys very active passing much heavy pus — I hung about doing very little \+ grieving all day about the paper that I wet down yesterday for printing to-day — by a desperate effort I finished a letter to Mary that has been lying, half written, on my table for the past two weeks — Then about 9 Kate Leonard gave me a hot foot bath with mustard \+ soda — \+ I got to bed feeling fairly comfortable barring Temp°. Jim was out — Regimental inspection I think.
