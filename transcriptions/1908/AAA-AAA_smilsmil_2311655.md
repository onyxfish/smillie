## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 29, 1908

**Weather:** Pleasant day - mild (comparatively) \+ quiet  **Temp:** [illegible]

Went to Dr. Tuttle's before 10 To Talk with him about the hanging of the "Restored" Mignot painting. Walked from there down Fifth Ave. \+ to studio Had a surprise call from Geo. W. Maynard who handed me a check for $300- for rent of studio — I did not do any work — just fussed about to no purpose, wasting precious time — Home at 2 - lunch — Some work on those everlasting negatives — a walk on Riverside with Ralph — Dinner with Jim at 6.30 — Ralph dined with Keibflee \+ went to Theatre with him — After that, at about midnight, he joined Jim at a Diet Kitchen Dance at The St Regis in which Edna Cook Murphy has been deeply and industriously interested. They got home at about 3 A.M. — "Going it" while they're young. I worked over accts - wrote note to Mr. Geo. W. Maynard — \+ sent her, by request of M. statement of Rent acct. \+ Receipt for $300 rec. this m'f.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 30, 1908

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day — Rain beginning at about noon \+ continuing into the night — Bkfo. before 8 - To Second Nat'l. Bank soon after 10 — walked from there to studio stopping at Lock's — the upholsterer — At the studio I was engaged till 1.30 about various matters of little importance — mainly housekeeping. Then home before 2 \+ had lunch with Ralph — Afternoon. Free after 4 o'clk. with Ralph — Then I gave way to heaviness \+ had a nap. Dinner at 6.30. Ralph's classmate Graham, from Brooklyn, dining with us — Eve'f Jim, Ralph \+ Graham went to Theatre together \+ I passed the eve'f in a desultory way —
