## Left Page

### June, Thursday 17, 1886.

Day opened with a heavy fog —
then showers & after 4 o'clk. a
beautiful clearing — Misty,
moisty, steaming hot — a pure
dog-day. Herrick came and
commenced work. — Coyle also
put in about two hours in the
m'f. I worked awhile on
Tracing on Gelatine of "Eastman
Johnson's." & glass with balsam —
Downtown in the m'f. Dinner
&t — agains on my Tracing in
the afternoons — Downtown at
6 — Ev'f, S.D. Payne & his
son came up about work.
Made out a long list of articles
wanted to send to Simpson &
Macaulay, Grocers, N'f. To bed
soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 18, 1886.

Bright, breezy & cool. — B'k'ft about
7.30 — Herrick came bringing a
workbench & load of lumber. He
got to work clearing the woodwork
from under hearth in main room,
working from the kitchen ceiling —
much of my day was spent
with him. — Coyle worked all
day on privy path. & Truman Payne
worked on the lawn in the afternoon.
I finished Tracing of Johnson —
Scraped it & filled with vermilion
& after some hard rubbing with mery
cloth, got printing press cleaned
up — Downtown, m'f & afternoon.
Tea about 7 — After that went to
Aunt H's — found that she arr-
ived last ev'f. Ev'f. wrote a
letter to Koehler in answer to
his of 17th. To bed about 10.30.
