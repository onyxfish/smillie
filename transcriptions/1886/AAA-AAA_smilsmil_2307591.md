## Left Page

### November, Thursday 4, 1886.

Brilliantly, windy & cold. Ther
as low as 38°. All the mtf. very
busy, about with the men and
waxing my press & other metal
work & preparing for our flitting —
About noon discovered that we
were short of brick — Dinner late
— nearly 3 when I went down town
to find brick — got a lot at Catholic
church & hunted for an hour for
a teamster — Hired Abel Tumell's
horse & W. Ben. Baker (colored gentle-
man) & soon had about 5 oo at the
house — Herrick cutting tree tops —
I got very cold & at 5.30 came in-
doors & warmed myself until tea-
time — then more work with bills —
to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 5, 1886.

Still bright & cloudless — less
windy & warmer — Ther. fell last
night to 35°. A busy day — In the
mtf, besides directing the men I
finished packing studio care &
got things together for trunk etc.
I packed 2 things in Studio to remain
over the winter — after dinner I went
down town to bank & other places
paying bills etc — etc — It was
after 4 o'clk. when I got home &
until 5.30 settling with the men
& finishing up about the house in-
doors — then packed house care.
Tea — after that read paper awhile
— rested. then packed trunk &
got to bed about 11.
