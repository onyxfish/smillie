## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 2, 1886

Ther. did not fall below 45°. last night.
A mild, delightful day — the chimney grew slowly — Hamlin & Hotchkiss. may be good & conscientious workers but they are thimbly slow — Down town in the m[orning] got a bbl. of putty (lime) from Bestwick — settled with Doolittle — had my hair cut then went with Dav. Sayre & swore in my vote without difficulty — Home at noon. All the afternoon hazing about with the men — finished up with Scott & team & paid him off. Evelf made scale drawings of tops to ventilator pipes for Herrick — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 3, 1886

Mild & very pleasant day — Ther lowest last night. 49°. Very busy all day with the men — with Herrick. got the boy joints into old drain pipe & thoroughly tarred — Jerf as came in the afternoon & finished his part of the work — then filled the holes. Pettis finished his work in the with & I paid him $120.00, glad to be rid of him — At noon went down Town — Hamlin got on with chimney — while Herrick was stripping off siding Hamlin took out & rebuilt a part of the back of main room fire place. just about dusk Mr. & Mrs. Parke called to say good bye — Evelf spent in getting the bills for the season in shape to bed near 11.
