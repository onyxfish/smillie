## Left Page

### October, Sunday 31, 1886

Still bleak & disheartening weather.
A few gleams of sunshine — then
squads of drizzle & at times, rain.
B&B. at 9 — about 10 or 11 J. Fred
a fire in the reconstructed fire
place in main room & to my
disgust found that it smoked
worse than ever — I stained
the ash door in cherry bed room
& some panels in main room.
— dinner at 2 — then amusing
p.p. & pants preparatory to packing —
wrote letter to Kate & at 5 Annie
Jim & I went to P.O. & a little
further — a gleam of sunshine &
a bright streak in the west.
On the eve. J. read
aloud for awhile & I took
a turn at it — to bed soon
after 10.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 1, 1886

Brilliant, mild day — work re-
sumed along the whole line.
Coyle came with a son who dug
all day — Herrick boxed the piano
in the mill — Taft a team came
& it was taken away — Hamlin
on the chimney — Scott & Pettus
in the field S — hauling stone & C —
ploughing where surface dirt has
been dug away — Annie. Jim & I
went down town about noon — I
spent the whole day out of doors,
most of the time with Hamlin —
went downtown about 5 —
Eve. most of it spent over the
Sunday & Monday N.Y. Tribune.
To bed soon after 10.
