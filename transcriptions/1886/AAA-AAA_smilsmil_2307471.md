## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 9, 1886

Unsettled weather — sunshine & shadow —
to studio at 9.30 — at 10.30 Wm. Pate came
& in the course of an hour he gave me
a commission to make an etching for
him 16½ × 29 — the subject, one of his char-
coal sketches that I have just finished
based upon studies made at [illegible] Diven
Before P. left, W. Williams, the art dealer
of Boston & his daughter came in — I did not
know who they were until afterwards —
Home at 1 — Annie at Cooking class — lunch —
After that, when I was ready to return to
studio I felt so dragged that I lay down
upon the lounge & slept until H's return
after 3 — About 4, A., Kate & I went to 55th
& [illegible] to Mendelssohn Studio Bldg. Reception
in Shurtleff's — met W. & Mrs. Bronson Howard —
After leaving studios walked down to my own
studio — home at 6.30 — dinner — Smelf. Charl
came in for a little while — I worked
on some lettering for Kate — To bed about
10.30 .

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 10, 1886

Light snow during the night — over-
cast until, clearing to a very pleasant
day — To Studio after 9.30 — Kate Rec'd to
Po'k at 10.30 — Will came to studio about
11 — I worked on "Summer Sea etc" &
made it cooler & grayer — Finished
it about 11.30 — then commenced to
take experimental p[ro]fs from "Rough
Sport" — trying new combinations of
Blk & oil — Home at 1.30 — back at 2.30
& printed until after 5 — getting, I
think, some of the effects in printing
that I have been after — Dr. Medford
came in — Got a letter from Coleman —
also, one from Winslow Homer's brother.
Washed & cleaned up & home at 6.30
dinner — Nelly, Annie & I called
on our neighbors, W. & Mrs Shepherd
about 8.30 — there for an hour — then
home — a little reading to bed
soon after 10.30 .
