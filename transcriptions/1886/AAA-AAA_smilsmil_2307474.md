## Left Page

### March, Monday 15, 1886.

Pleasant day, altho' at time overcast. To studio at 10 and spent the mft. in fruitless experimenting in proving "Rough Sport". Col. Turner came in to enquire about walling wax — Home at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — went in to see Patt about getting some etch-ing up by his bro. Charley — then worked an hour or two on Rough Sport & again tried some proving — Cha. McKurtz came in about 6 — wants me to do something for his Academy notes — Home at 6.45 very tired & disgusted — Dinner — Suet. wrote note to E. C. Stedman about the Homer Etching — also one to Julien Scott.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 16, 1886.

Brilliant spring day — Got to studio about 10 & spent the day at work upon Rough Sport plate — most of the time with graver running close light lines as a tint over strong bitten ones & finishing about blk horse & rider — To Bank bt — home at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — worked on plate until after 5. Then cutting & wetting paper until 6.30 — then home — Dinner — Suet. Read awhile — wrote another note to E. C. Stedman having Rec'd a telegram to-day from Winslow Homer giving me permission to etch his "Voice from the Cliff" — Wrote also to Koehler.
