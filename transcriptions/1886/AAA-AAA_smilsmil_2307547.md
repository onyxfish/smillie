## Left Page

### August, Sunday 8, 1886.

Pleasant day — the mtf. of send
foggy & for some time continued
overcast — Recovering from
my hard work of yesterday
I did little but loaf about.
In the mtf. wrote a letter to
Koehler. Dinner at 2 —
wrote my letter to Kate in
the afternoon — Eve'g. Annie
& the girls went to Presb'n ch.
I excused myself feeling
tired & headachy — I spent
the eve'g in hammock on the
piazza until their return
with Parke about 9 — Beautiful
moonlight night — to bed
before 10.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 9. 1886.

Glorious day — at 10 o'clk. Annie
& the girls left with W't Mr. Parke
for a pic-nic to Silver Lake — I
could not go as I had the Johnson
plate to finish — At 10.30 I went
down town & for an hour ran
about attending to little matters —
Sent p'f. of Johnson plate to Koehler —
Then home & to work — Stuck to it
stopping only for my dinner, until
5 & if I am not much mistaken
have added much to the finish
of the plate, working over it very
generally with dry-point — At 5
out & about the place — in soon after
6 & took Jim in charge — As the
pic-nic party did not return
I gave him his supper at 7 and
half an hour later I put him to bed
At 8.10 I took my own tea — at
9 I began to worry & got to be
quite excited before their arrival
at 9.40. They had had a very
good time & did not think that
I would worry — I went down town
engaged seats in mt'g Stage —
to be at 10.30.
