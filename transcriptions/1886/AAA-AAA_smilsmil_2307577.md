## Left Page

### October, Thursday 7, 1886

Another delightful day —
wrote a number of notes in the
m.f. — Took them to P.O. between
10 & 11 — Saw Mr. Tyler & had a very
unpleasant interview with him in
his office. We walked up the hill
together & after looking over the
ground at the end of the lane we
agreed upon the boundaries of a small
plot, worth about $120, for which I am
to pay him $75 — H.f. Turnell has kindly
consented to sell $5 worth of land at
the end of the lane for $50. With the
men after dinner — old W. Hotchkiss
worked on foundation of chimney — At
3.30 went down town — got carriage
& came to house — Took Emma, Eliza. &
Jack out for an hours ride — then got
Annie & Jim & drove over to Little R.R. depot
& met Mr. & Mrs. Ehninger — got to house about
6 — High tea about 7 — Ev'g. chatting
a short promenade on piazza —
To bed about 10.30.

Coyle worked.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 8, 1886

Glorious day — Eliza. & Jack Howess
left us at 9.30 this m'f. Jo[illegible].
After 10 Ehninger & I went down
town — settled my troubles with
Shapley & Wells, the brick men, also
paid Mr. Tyler $75.00 for the new bit
of ground — After dinner, Parke's man
Geo. came with his horses & took
Mr. & Mrs. Ehninger & Annie out for
a ride — W. Hotchkiss finished
foundation of new chimney & I
set him to work with Pettis at
breaking ground & starting the new
line wall between me & Mr. Tyler.
I went with Emma up to the fair
ground & painted my first out
of door sketch this summer —
Ev'g. went with Ehninger down
town — back before 9 — chatting
To bed about 10.30 —

Coyle worked — pretty drunk I think.
