## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 17, 1886

Charming day — wrote a note to Kate — then to studio — 9.45 — got printing things in order & then to work experimentally with different inks & papers — tried an india proof — Home at 1²⁰ —
back at 2.30 — worked all the afternoon without being able to learn anything apparently —
Did not go to N.A. meeting at 4.30 to vote for Ratification of recent Real Estate Purchase —
Home at 6.30 — exceedingly tired — dinner — & at 8.30 at N.A. annual Water Color body, mostly nothing of especial interest or importance — J.H. Smitts, as usual, made an exhibition of himself, followed, as usual, by Silva as a small side show — Home & to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 18, 1886

Mild, delightful w'f. — overcast p.m. — to studio at 9.30 & spent some time getting yesterdays p'f. in presentable shape — then took a couple of them to Kelpke hoping to make some arrangement with him — He offered me one quarter as my share for all proofs that he would take fine — when asked to put a value on the plate he named $125⁰⁰ & I left, very much discouraged — went in to Wunderlich's & spent some time looking at etchings — then to studio & wet paper etc — home — lunch — Fanny K. there — To studio at 2.30 & spent p.m. taking p'f. — 1 "Rough Sport" — home at 6.30 — very tired — dinner — W. t'f. some reading — To bed soon after 10.
