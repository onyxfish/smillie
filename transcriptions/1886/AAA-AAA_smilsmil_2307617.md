## Left Page

### December, Thursday 30. 1886.

A cold driving N.E. wind all day — the air full of fine driving snow — To studio at 10. Will came with 3 ladies — & afternoon, Fer. guson — At last I got to my work on my big Strekat & put in my foreground figures with some success. Three home to lunch — back before 3 & reworked over sky & sea with clean water making it all more atmospheric & tenderer — About 5 went to 12th St. & B'way — Tibbal's Toy Store & made a change of some of Jim's toys — home before 6 — dinner. Evef. a little reading — then a game of cards & H — went to bed at 9. I wrote a second note to Schaff. To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### December, Friday 31. 1886.

The year has gone out, behaving as badly as possible. The day commenced with a sleety rain which continued with more or less violence until night, everything being covered with a coating of ice — Got to studio soon after 10 — worked for some time on little sketch of "A bluff on Marble-Head neck." adding more color to some sheets in the foreground — Dip[illegible] made a long call. Home to lunch at 1.30 — Jim continues under the weather, coughing & giving other evidences of cold. Back to studio at 2.45 & worked on Sketch "Cowboy Country" & also made some changes in Crécy Mill — Put more etching plates in press — home at 6 — dinner — evef. — Reading — To bed before 11.
