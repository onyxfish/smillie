## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 6, 1886.

A fierce storm of wind & rain — about 10 when I got to studio — laid grounds on 34 & 36 and did a little work on 36 — Soon after 12 went to Dr. Emely Blackwells in 20th St. to see if she was ready to go on with her operation upon Annie. Home at 1 — got everything ready for the afternoon's work that I could do & at 1.30 had lunch. At 2 Dr. Blackwell, Cochins, Kellam & a trained nurse arrived in detachments — Annie's Nurse, Mrs. Hays, was also on hand. It was nearly 4 when it was announced that the operation had been successfully performed. Two ruptures being sewed up — 8 stitches — As A. was a long time coming from under the ether influence, I went to studio — back at 6 — Dinner at 6.30 — Every It. much prostrated — sick & headache — I went to Mother C's — for a few minutes — wrote notes to E.N.D., M.F.K. & home — also to Nicoll & Baldwin — To bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 7, 1886.

Clear wth., but overcast before noon with considerable rain in the afternoon & evng. Annie had a fairly good night & seemed in good spirits — To studios about 10 — Spent the day working over plate 36 — dragging lines afpen, putting in new work & crossing — Wr. Pate came with contract for big etching & I signed it. To bank at 3, then home to lunch — back at 2.30 and worked until after 5 — but did not get home until after 6 — had dinner at 6.30 — Invited to dine at Edwd. C's to meet W. & Mrs. Selstedt (who called at the studio in the mfg.) & Mr. & Mrs. Morse. — I went there at A's request at 9.30 & stayed until near 11 — not far from 12 o'clk. when I got to bed.
