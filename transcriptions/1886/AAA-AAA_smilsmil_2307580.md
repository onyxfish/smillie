## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 13, 1886

Cloudy m'f. threats of showers
but in the afternoon it cleared as
dry & beautiful as usual. Got
up after an uncomfortable night
feeling very stupid & sore. After
bkfs. met H'f. Tyler — got the Turrell
deed from him & paid to him the
$50.00 Part of m'f with men in the
field & part with Smith the painter.
Down town in the m'f. Rec'd from
Charl a pkr. with a lot of his photos.
Afternoon — again down town — no
word yet of the bbl. of pressed brick.
Eve'f. spent sorting my negatives
& looking over Charl's photos —
To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 14, 1886

At last the storm that I have
been dreading — strong S.E. wind, but
little rain — The arch of pressed
brick arr'd from Trenton to-day making
everything now ready — but the weather
has interfered with the brick layers &
they cannot come before Monday next
or later. I spent some time with
Hotchkiss & the others at work on the
stone wall. Then with Smith fin-
ishing the painting in S.W. bed room.
Down town at noon — arranging
arch of pressed brick after dinner —
wrote letter to Charl — went to P.O.
at 5 — saw Gill & brick layer — Then
went to Payne's — saw him in his
bed — a very sick man. His son
Truman died last night — Great
Republican demonstration in Town
to-day & at night in spite of storm.
Eve'f. wrote to Howard White — worked
awhile on plans for N.E. Room, main floor.
To bed at 10.30.
