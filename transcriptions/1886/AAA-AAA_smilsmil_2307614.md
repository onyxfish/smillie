## Left Page

### December, Friday 24, 1886

Very mild day — foggy — sun shine
struggling thro' occasionally. I got
up & went down stairs to bkfst —
feeling groggy about the legs and
foggy about the head. I spent
much of the m't. finishing the
reading of one of Stephensons Buccaneer
Blood & Thunder Romances for Boys.
Geo. came in in the m't & made a
short call — so did Walter C —
I lounged about, in-doors, all day,
feeling discouraged because I did
not grow stronger — Took a nap in
the afternoon. For some time in the
early ev'g. arranging Jim's X mas
presents — To bed about 10.30.
To-day Dr. Y — did not come at
all.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 25, 1886

A pleasant X mas — Soon after
10\. Jim having made acquaintance
with his new toys, I took him
out with me for a walk — the first
time I had been out of doors since
a week ago yesterday p.m — As
Annie complained of feeling very
much out of sorts I determined to go
down to Dr. Blackwell's — 2.20 S — Jim
& I walked to §§. S. & there took a 4th Ave
car, riding to 20th S — Dr. B — not in. From
there Jim & I walked home, getting in
soon after 12 — I felt pretty tired. At 1 —
had a light lunch alone — Annie & Jim
going to Walters where the grand-young-
sters were to meet for lunch — I followed
abt. 45 — The Spragues & others there making
a pleasantly mixed company of old, middle
aged & young — a very extensive lunch —
after 4 where we ret — a nap — before
6 Mary Willard called. Dinner — after
that, read aloud finishing "2 yrs before the
mast." To bed abt 10.
