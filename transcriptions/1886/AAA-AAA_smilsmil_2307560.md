## Left Page

### September, Friday 3, 1886

Another delightful day — Did
not get up until late — I
stile feeling very weak. Dr.
Vail & a young woman from
Buffalo called — Rec'd letter
from Annie & a telegram saying
that she was on her way home.
Parke insisted upon sending to
the depot for her — So, Char
lunched at P's & with Mr. P.
drove over to the 3 o'clk. Train
& brought Annie home at 4.30 —
I spent most of the day in
the hammock — hardly daring
to hope that I felt better —
Annie brought latest news —
mother Coat suffers from shock
— Sue bearing up wonderfully
well — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 4, 1886

Still charming weather — Got
up feeling better & with Char
took two or three interior photo-
views in the house — Mr. Arthur Jeed
of Binghamton introduced him-
self as a struggling artist — After
that more rest in the hammock.
Soon after I was able to eat a fair
dinner — again more hammock.
Then in dark room & developed
the pictures of the mt — Char went
to Darkie Valley & took photos of
darkies — Eve'g, he dined at the
Parke's — Annie & I played a
game of Bezique — then I signed
a few Bridgeman p[illegible] that arr'd in
pkg to-day from N.Y.S. To bed
soon after 10.
