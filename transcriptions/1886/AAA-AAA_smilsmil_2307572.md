## Left Page

### September, Monday 27, 1886

Another warm dull day with showers. Spent the m'f. down town
attending to many little matters —
the most important being with
H.f. Tyler who at last consented
to sell a small piece of ground at
the turn of the lane. Very tired, I
took a short rest before dinner —
The humming in my right ear
ceased during the m'f. Herrick
came to work in the afternoon —
so did Payne's to with teams — but
no Cogle. About 5 I went down town
& saw H.f. Turrell — tried to buy 2 Rods
at end of lane — he is a maddening
idiotic mule — an imbecile — I could
make nothing out of him — Eve'g
put up [illegible] with Breton [illegible] for M[illegible]
Koehler — & wrote him a letter — some
time in dark room filling plate holders
then Annie read aloud for awhile.
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 28, 1886

Another muggy, cloudy day with
showers — a thunder shower passed
south fus between 8 & 9 p.m.
Spent the day podging about.
Down town m'f. & afternoon.
Herrick fixed my big studio
window so that I can open &
shut it & commenced tearing
out on north side of house for
the new chimney. Eve'g
I wrote notes & sent 70 cents
each to Colman, Gifford,
Bellows & Farrer — copyright
in "Ports & Kitchens." To bed at
10.30.
