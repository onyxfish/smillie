## Left Page

### October, Thursday 21, 1886

Bleak windy m'f. Passing into a wonderfully clear m'f. As usual spent my time with the men out of doors — the grading progresses satisfactorily — had Peters plough up walk up the hill & a darkie began digging it out preparatory to filling him with stones regularly arranged. Down town about 11 — some time at the photographer's — after dinner went out with Annie & Jim & got a couple of photos of the house from down on the pike road — then home & developed them — down town at 5 & saw Geo. Youngs about starting work on chimney to-morr. He promises to be up at the house about 8 A.m. — Eve'g. Put my last 12 plates in the holders & assorted my negatives.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 22, 1886

Brilliant, beautiful day — after waiting for the brick layers until after 9 o'clk. I went down town & found them all three, at work on the Catholic church chimneys — there was no word of apology or explanation from Youngs for his shameless lying to me — after a good deal of exasperating dickering with Gill I was at last able to secure Coley, a native artist, who is to commence work on the chimney this afternoon. I then went to Doolittle's. Coley came at 2 o'clk. I spent some time with him as he started the fireplace. Old Mr Hotchkiss finished the wall & I got him started putting in stone on the hill walk — a call from Ambrose Payne. To day we have 9 men & 4 horses at work. I spent some time in trees cutting out wood & tarring them.
