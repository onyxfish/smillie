## Left Page

### September, Thursday 9, 1886.

A hot clear day — All day I and [illegible]
at work with Herrick & Smith
putting down the heavy canvas
on main deck west of the house.
An unexpectedly large amt. of
lead was used & the canvas was
so heavy & stubborn that it seemed
almost impossible to make it
stick to the floor — Evening.
I spent a little while in the
dark room filling my plate holders.
About 9, Arrive & I went up to the
fair ground & took a short look
at the darkie camp meeting in
progress there — then home & sat
for some time on the piazza en-
joying the moonlight. To bed at
10.30 —

## Right Page

### September, Friday 10, 1886.

A very hot, clear day — thunderstorm
between 8 & 9 p.m. Smith & Herrick
worked all day putting down the
canvas on the south front upper deck —
then the bit east of Butler's pantry.
It was 6.30 before they finished work
in the nt'y. I took an imp. of
the Breton plate after writing a note
to Mr. Marin. The sky much too
dark. About 11 went to the green
with camera to get a snap at some
of the school children at play —
Did not get anything. After noon
worked on etching, burnishing the
sky & using the dry point — Tea
at 6.30 — About 7 went down town
— between 8 & 9, developed my four
experimental pictures — none of them
came out successfully — not time enough.
To bed soon after 10.
