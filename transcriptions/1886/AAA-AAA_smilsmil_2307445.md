## Left Page

### January, Saturday 16. 1886.

*I am 53 years old to-day.*

overcast & mild — to studio before 9.30 — met Skinner by appointment & touched up a proof of his die — after my West Shore West Point drawing — then, at about 11, to N. & N. — took a proof of big plate — they were so driven with work that they could not be induced to take p.p. so I had to return to studio & prepare to take p.p. myself — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — & until 4 at work taking p.p. which came out about as well as I expected. Then to N.a. — just too late to see Wood — back to studio — cleaned up & home to dinner at 6 — Mr. Satchell went to Bridgeport — her daughter with her. After 7 went to Hotel Newdome — 41st S. + B'dway — & saw Wood for a few minutes — Home at 8.30 — Reading etc —

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 17. 1886.

Bright w.f., overcast p.m. and clear again at night — Up late & had b.k.p. after 9 — Sat about reading until towards noon — then to studio & put water-color Rough Sport into its frame — then out & walked until 1.45 when I got home. Dinner soon after 2 — about 4 Avery Brown came & took Emma Satchell home with him — then I took a nap — Tea at 6.30 — Evef. writing — at 9.30 to G. C. Delpat — with letter for Mother — Geo. & Charl in P.k. to-day.

Mrs. Mary Bourne Nesbitt was buried to-day — 77 years & 3 days.
