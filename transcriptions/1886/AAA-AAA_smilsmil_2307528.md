## Left Page

### July, Thursday 1, 1886

Charming day. Clear and not too
warm — Herrick & Smith papering
hall in attic — old Wilbur making
hay, nearly a ton of which was
hauled by Payne to Herrick's
barn. I made drawing and
specifications for cow shed &
by sticking to my work got my
Johnson etching better in by
6.30 — Until after 8 watering
plants & working about the
place — Then had a rest —
looked over news paper &
got to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 2, 1886

Hazy almost overcast, warm and
threatening a "dry spell." Found on
looking at my Johnston plate that
my acid (or something) had served
me an inexplicable trick that came
near being serious — on many parts
of the Saure's figure the acid acted
very irregularly, seeming to have
ceased action after about 5 minutes
a slight disposition only to do the
same thing was shown elsewhere.
All my spare time during the day
was spent rerunning a graver thro'
these broken lines — I spent most
of the afternoon with Herrick trying
to get some ceiling panels up successfully but failed — Work was
commenced on the cow shed.
Ev. H. until after 8 watering plants
& training up vines — Then lounged
until after 9 when I lighted up.
To bed at 10.
