## Left Page

### August, Thursday 26, 1886.

Warm & misty — in the mf.
Annie, Mr. & Mrs. White & children
rode over to So. Montrose creamery &
then in — not getting home until after
noon. Mr. Dau. Sayre — Priscilla
Poor. Jessie Comstock. Mr. Berlin
& Miss Dyer called — I finished
my Breton Etching & also fussed
some with photos etching — Dinner
about 1 — Afternoon commenced
biting my etching — After 6 when
I went down town — Tea about
7 — Evnt. developed half a
dozen plates without getting any-
very good — on Piazza for
some time & to bed about
10.30 —
Rec'd. plt. of Johnson Etching
from k. & V.

couple did not work.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 27, 1886.

*great fire in Montrose.*

Very hot day. After bkfs. took two
photos & developed them — Mr. & Mrs.
trusted & Dr. & Mrs. Brown called — At
last I got to work biting Breton
etching — Dinner soon after 1 — then
a short walk — then two more photos
& developments. Worked at my
biting until 6 o'clk. then from
Piazza, accidentally, I saw flames
issuing from top of Crandall's factory.
Chare. Mr. White & I went down town
& turning in with the crowd did what
we could. The wildest confusion —
demoralization — no leader — no system
in fighting the fire which was very
soon beyond all control. About 7.30
I went home & got something to eat.
After 8 again went down town — fire
still spreading — again home & with
Annie & Mrs. W. went down & about
until 10 o'clk. then home — the fire
swallowing its last victim — Bostwick
& Corcoran — then sat & waited for
Mr. W. & Charl. They came in
at about 11.30, after which I soon
got to bed.
