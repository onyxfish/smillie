## Left Page

### April, Monday 26. 1886.

Quite cool + overcast until the afternoon — Got to studio at 9.45 — Laid very successfully a rebiting ground on faces and arms of figures on Homer plate — Gifford came in to consult about the etching of his plate — Home at 1.30 — About 2.30 Mary + the children arr. from Po'keepsie. Back to studios before 5 — Rebit flesh work + then worked with graver + dry-point until after 6 — nearly 7 when we had dinner — Eveʼg. Geo + Charles came in. Mr. + Mrs. Shepard, our neighbors came in to call — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 27. 1886.

Rain in the mʼg + again at night. To studio at usual time + to work on Homer plate — all day with graver on flesh + draperies of figures — Home to lunch + back as usual — About 5 went to Bridgeman's. Seedsman in 19ᵗʰ St. + ordered a bushel + a half of lawn grass seed for Montrose — met Glenger + went in to his place in 30ᵗʰ Stˢ. Home at 6.30 — dinner. Eveʼg. wrote a letter to J. D. Payne — after g went to Charles's dark room — met Yale there + spent half an hour — walked up to his home with him — then home at co.30 + to bed.
