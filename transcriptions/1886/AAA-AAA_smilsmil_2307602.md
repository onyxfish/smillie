## Left Page

### November, Monday 22. 1886.

Very mild & pleasant — To studio
about 9.30 & to work on Mexico
well scene. Got it done by
1.30 — then home to lunch —
It was near 3 when I ret'd —
Started on a single figure
subject — man gathering pulque
juice or something of that kind.
Interrupted by calls from a young
man, sent up by Will, wishing
to enter some art school — & by
young Comstock & a western lady —
Left studio about 5 & walked down
to Union Sq. Home at 6 — dinner.
Wrote notes to Cate — Marvin and
Avery — the latter sent me two copies
of the celebrated Wilson Cat. this p.m. —
one of which I am to buy.

To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 23. 1886.

Dismal dark day with rain —
About 9.30 when I got to Studio
Started enlarging, by squares,
the figure of the Pulque gatherer,
for A.B.N. Co. — Got it outlined
& ready for washes — then went
down town to A.B.N. Co. with drawing
of City of Mexico, well & water carriers —
Took lunch with Will — It was
after 3 before I could get away.
On my way to studio stopped at
2nd Nat. Bk. & deposited the $75.
that I had just got from A.B.N. Co.
Too dark to see to work, so I
just "dodged" about until 5 — then
to N.A.D., to see the Autumn exhibition —
a dismal affair — there for half
an hour — then home to dinner —
Every wrote to Herrick & Read
until near 10 — then to bed —
A very mild night.
