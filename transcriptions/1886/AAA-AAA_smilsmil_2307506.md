## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 18, 1886.

overcast, but not unpleasant. Annie had a great time with girls who came in answer to her adv. no less than 31 called — To studio before 10. & spent the day among my etchings &c. — Home to lunch as usual & back —
I got two a.p. [illegible] packed with [illegible] for Koehler & [illegible] for Mr. Stewart of Plainfield. It was 5 o'clk. when I took them to Adams' [illegible]. Home to dinner at 6.30. Even'g wrote a letter to Koehler & at 8.30 took it to G. C. Delbot & there on, also to G. H. Curtis' — 16 W. 5[illegible]; saw him & showed to him & to Mr. Co. proof of Homer plate & made a final arrangement by which I am to have the Eastman Johnson all summer — on my way home stopped to see Charl — not in.
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 19, 1886.

A wet m'g, clearing before noon. To studio at usual time. Some time mounting etchings &c. At noon went to A.A.A. & had a boy bring to the studio the Bridgman painting "A Hot Bargain." Home to lunch soon after — back at 2.30. Charl came in after 3 & as it had cleared nicely he went to his room, got his camera (& lenses) — [illegible] & took 3 negatives of the Bridgman Painting. At 5.30 I went to K. & V. & arranged to go there to-morrow to have some [illegible] taken from my Homer plate.
Home to dinner at 6.30 —
Even'g very tired — some time looking over old letters left in an old portfolio by Father.
To bed at 10.30.
