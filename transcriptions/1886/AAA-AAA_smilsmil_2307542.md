## Left Page

### July, Thursday 29, 1886.

A very warm, hazy day with light showers — a tremendous heavy thunder-storm with hail about 8 P. M. Got to my work on Johnson place about 10 o'cl. after writing a letter to Johnson & one to Marvin at Scribner's — worked until 12.30 with graver. Then with Herrick who is getting along nicely with Cherry room ceiling — Ed. W. Smith is also at work. Again at my place until 4. Then out riding until 6.30 with the girls, Annie and Jim — went around by Stark lake & thro' woods to Summerville road. A good part of even't spent writing autograph on Rp. of "A Voice from the Cliff." To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 30, 1886.

A very close, muggy day — most of the time overcast. A letter from W. Sprague saying that he & "Mrs. S." would arrive here to-morrow. — hurrying up work on Cherry-room ceiling, but Smith made his "joints" so badly that I had to fake him off & put him at smoothing up. I worked on my Johnson place with dry point & graver in the afternoon & took animals. Preparations to rebiting — after tea for some time at work putting up wires for vines — then repairing [p.p.] of Winslow Homer plate. To bed about 10.
