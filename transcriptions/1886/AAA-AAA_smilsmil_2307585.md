## Left Page

### October, Saturday 23, 1886

Brilliant day — all day with
the men — 11 of them at work —
one carpenter — 1 brick layer — 1 stone
mason — 2 painters — 3 diggers — 2
teamsters — 4 horses & 1 plumber.
Work advanced satisfactorily
along the whole line — Coal,
after some trouble, got the brick and
over the fire place fairly well —
perhaps fixed studio cistern & pipes
also, overflow & drain pipes from
U.W. cistern — I got a couple of
beech trees trimmed out & trained
& the young elms topped by
the new Waters pruning knife —
By night time I was very tired
& had hard work to keep
awake while Annie read a
short story from Nov. Harper's
aloud — To bed at 9.30.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 24, 1886

A pleasant day but with
threatenings of change — After
b[reakfast] — spent a little time
going about over the place —
then wrote letters — met about
— Sent check for brick to Wells
& Bingham — & check for 7 months
studio rent to Wm. Beman agt.
Then with Annie & Jim — wan-
dered over the hill & fair ground
until 2 o'clk — then dinner —
wrote letter to Kate — took down
bamboo screens on piazza &
at 5 went down & called on
Payne who is no well enough
to sit up — then to Jas. Cooley —
& saw him about continuing
work on the chimney to-morrow —
Home at 6.30 — We[nt]. Annie
& I took turns at reading aloud —
To bed about 10.30
