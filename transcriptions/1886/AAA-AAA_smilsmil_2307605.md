## Left Page

### December, Friday 10, 1886

Another very mild, bright day opening with a fog. Bkelp°. Last did not get to studio until 10.30. Sam. Colman came in soon after & made a long call. — I did little before I went home to lunch. Besides Mr. & Mrs. Parke, mother, Cook & Mr. Ge°. C. were there to lunch. I got back to studio at 2.30 — Parke walking down with me. Made pen sketches, slight portraits of Ge°. & self for Champlin's Cyclopaedia of Painters & Paintings. At 5 went to Am. Art Galleries & saw Sutton & the [illegible]. Home at 6. Dinner — Evelf, chatting until 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 11, 1886

Raw, cold day. Got up, feeling pretty well used up as Jim got up at 2. A.M. & was brought to our bed, after which I got but little sleep — after 10 when I got to studio — Colman came in for a few moments — & Mr. Benj. Ellis's martin with his copy of Dobson's poems into which he wishes me to "jot a little art" — a number of the fellows have made sketches upon the margins — so I made one "the pebbly Rillet" — I also worked for some time over the pen portraits for Champlin which I took down to Scribner's at 5 P.M & saw Champlin & Marium. — I spent nearly an hour looking over Koehler's Am. Art, for which I stroked the Homer, Johnson & Bridgman — more than ever mortified that I was not given a place in it as an artist — Home to dinner. Evelf. Mr. & Mrs. P. went to theatre with Charl. — To bed soon after 10.
