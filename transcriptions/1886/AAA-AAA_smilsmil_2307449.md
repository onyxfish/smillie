## Left Page

### January, Sunday 24, 1886

Very cold and windy — a light fall
of snow continuing most of the day —
about noon I walked up to Central
Park & went as far as the obelisk,
finding it rather disagreeable on the
whole. Got back at 2 — just in
time for dinner — after that lounged
about, took a nap — then wrote part
of my letter to Mother & Kate — tea
before 7 — then finished my letter —
then Annie read aloud until 9.30
from Adam Bede — then I took my letter
to G.C. Depot — on my return sat and
read Rochles's book on "Etching" until
after 11 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 25, 1886

Raw & disagreeable — near 10 when
I got to studio & spent the morning
working on big plate with dry-point —
Home to lunch at usual time after
laying a ground over the whole
plate. On my return I etched
in a tint under & back of the
group of horses & showed some
of the lines for rebiting, but I
could not get ready to bite it into
the acid so, at 5 I walked up to
Central Park & 5th Ave & then home
to dinner — about 8 I went to
Avery's & spent an hour or more in
looking over some of his pf. of etchings.
When I got home, a little before 10
I found Chas. Collins there — It
was about 11 when I got to bed.
Rec'd today a notice of enrollment
on the list of jurors.
