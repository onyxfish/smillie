## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 4, 1886.

A delightful day — got at my re-
biting about 9 o'clk & finished it
about the middle of the n'f., & then
worked with burnisher — got Hemick
started on my "dark room" — Rec. a
letter from Charl & also 2 with half
a dozen with p.p. of Bridgman from
K. & V. Wrote a note to Charl &
sent him a check. (Amids) in
payment for photo — outfits — camera
& chemicals. ($112.53) — worked
until 4 o'clk., then we all went
out for a ride — past S°. Montrose —
back over Butterfield's hill — the
light was beautiful & the air was
delicious — all enjoyed the ride
very much — home at 7 — tea —
Ev'g., very tired & sleepy — Mr.
S— took a noisy nap on the lounge
— all went to bed by 9.30 & we
followed soon after.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 5, 1886.

Charming n'f., overcast before
noon & steady rain after 4 o'clk.
Mr. & Mrs. S. left us at 9.30 — Katherine
Cook & Nellie Adams went with them
to the depot. I worked on Johnson
etching with burnisher & graver.
Had a call from the Jessups, Cook
& Miss Stotesbury who spent some
time in the studio — my work in
the afternoon much interrupted by
the putting in of Studio cistern by
Hemick & Zerfus. At 6 o'clk. I
went down town — messages — then
home to tea — Ev'g. Reading
newspaper — wrote a letter to
Koehler & to bed soon after 10.
