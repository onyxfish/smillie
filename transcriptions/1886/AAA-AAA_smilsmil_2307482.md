## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 31. 1886.

Nasty weather still continues —
very mild at mid-day & the
sun made an attempt to
shine — bleak again at night —
about 9 P.M. a thunder storm —
Got to studio about 10 & to work
on Cliff Farm. Trying to paint in
trunks & branches of central group
of trees, but my head was heavy
& I made little progress. Home
to lunch as usual & back —
Scraped off the work of the m'g
& commenced anew & did but
little better. Gave it up at 4.30
Home at 6.30 after having taken
a look at Barnum's preparations
for a parade this ev'g. Dinner
Ev'g. Annie & I both felt
"Springy" — we played a game
of Bazique & got to bed about
10.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 1. 1886.

Bright, breezy day — About 10
when I got to studio — Spent
the m'g. making a camera-lucida
reduction of the [illegible] tower
drawing — Home at 1 — Mother took
Mary C & two of her children, Mrs.
Downs & her daughter, Mrs. Atwater
& her child to lunch — I got
away about 2.15 & back to the
studio where I worked until
after 6. Etching on view of studio
at Montrose — Then home to
dinner — Ev'g. made a pen
& ink sketch for Seal or Arms
for Illinois League Club & sent
it to [illegible] — To bed about
11.
