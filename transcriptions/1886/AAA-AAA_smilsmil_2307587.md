## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 27, 1886

Ther. last night 40°

A dark day of mizzle & rain — I
got up feeling out of sorts & did
little during the day in the way
of accomplishing anything — no men
at work in the [illegible] except Heinck
& Zerfass who worked on the drain
pipes — Old Coyle fooled about for a
time quite tipsy — I kindled fire
in studio furnace — Abl. Pettus
came & went to ploughing — Old
Wittekiss to work on the walk & old
Coyle tried to cut wood but he was
so helplessly drunk that I sent
him home — more rain in the
evef. I wrote a note to Aunt
Waller about Phil. Seats in answer
to a note from her.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 28, 1886

A dismal day — Coyle came in the
mf. — sobered up — set him to work
digging for drain — at it until 11 —
then he left — Zerfass worked all the
mf. at the pipes — & part of afternoon.
Had to give up on acct. of rain — In
the afternoon went down town &
saw Hamlin & Coaley — H. will come
to work to-morrow — then went to
Payne's — saw him — sitting up. I
took a mould of Bavarian cream
to him — Evef. heavy rain
again — a little reading & a
little card playing & to bed
ab. 10.
