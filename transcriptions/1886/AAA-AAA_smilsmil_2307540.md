## Left Page

### July, Sunday 25, 1886

The day opened with a thunder
storm at about 6 A.M. continuing
more or less overcast all day with
a strong breeze from the S.E. I
spent most of the m'f at work
about the vines & fixing wires
for them to run upon — then
I wrote to Charl & Koehler —
Dinner at 2 — then an hour
in the hammock — then wrote
letter to Kate — then out with
Annie & walked to the lake —
Home at 7 & had tea — Eve'f
wrote to Ehninger & Walter
Cook. To bed about 10.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 26, 1886

Heavy, down pouring rain — very
dark & muggy — Worked on plate
(Johnson) stopping out preparatory
to biting the second or supplemental
etching — commenced the biting
of it — Afternoon, finished biting
& cleaned off plate — Smith
painted first coat on dining room
ceiling — Eve'f. quiet, muggy
& tending to fog. After reading
news paper Annie & I had a
game of cards & to bed soon after
10.
