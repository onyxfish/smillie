## Left Page

### June, Monday 21, 1886

Another pleasant day — B&f:
at 7.30 — Then to work. Herrick
selected ash & oak lumber at
the mill — About 10 Mr. Gill, the
builder, came & we had a con-
sultation concerning the beam
under main room fire place.
He set to work with it — soon after
to dig all the wood out — The
men came with the fence &
commenced putting it up. Great
work heating tar — got on fine —
I worked on Bridgman etching
(Second ground) & made up a
pailful of sizing & sized some
duck for the finish of upper
hall way. Down Town about 6.
Tea at 7 — Mr. Ball sent us samples
of his gilt edged butter. Eve'g
for some time reading news-
papers.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 22, 1886

Overcast, Rain at noon — light
showers in the afternoon — B&b. at
7.30 — Gill came after & progress was
made in getting the "header" in front of
main room fire place into position — at
noon I went looking for Hamlin, the
brick layer — found Cooley, his son-in-law.
Dinner soon after 1 — managed to do
considerable work on my Bridgman
plate — I do not know how far it can
be good, having so many interruptions.
Corey came with mortar & sand, but
did not go to work, carpenters not being
ready for him — Do Herrick put up some
of the sized muslin or duck in bed room
hall — I went down town at 6 — Tea
before 7 — Bor from Simpson & Macaulay
am. in the afternoon. Eve'g, reading
newspaper & to bed at a little
before 10.
