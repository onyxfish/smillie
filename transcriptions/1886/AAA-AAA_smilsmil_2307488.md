## Left Page

### April, Monday 12, 1886

Overcast mtf — then pleasant —
a sprinkle of rain abt. 2 & 3 —
fine moonlight night. Got
to studio about 10 & commenced
etching on the Winslow Homer
plate. Had a long call from
J. D. Seymch — consulting me
about building a studio bldg
cor. 44th St. & Broadway — Home
to lunch at 1.30 — back at 3 —
worked on etching until
near 5 — then for a time
putting things in order — went
to 44th St. & Bdway — then home
to dinner at 6.45 — Most
of the evng spent on Diet
Kitchen business — To bed
about 11.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 13, 1886

Heavy rain early in the mtf
then cleared warm — thunderstorm
in the afternoon — To studio about
10 & etched on Homer Steadley
until 1.30 — then to Bank for
Annie — near 2 when I got
home — dinner — Emma came
in to see Annie. At 3.30 I took
train for Po'k. Charl to go up by
6 o'clk train, we to spend to-morrow
in assorting things at the Terrace.
Rev. W. Loomis of Po'k went up in
same train with me. Got to
Terrace on time — after passing
thro' a very heavy thunderstorm
at Peekskill — Tea at home
& visiting with the family —
Charl & Ed. came in soon after
8. Mr Brown came in at 9 to
see about a drain from cellars
of the row. It was after 11
when we got to bed.
