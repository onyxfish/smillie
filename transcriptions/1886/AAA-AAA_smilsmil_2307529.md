## Left Page

### July, Saturday 3, 1886.

A very hot day - No celebration of the glorious 4th in Montrose -
Herrick & Littnan worked all day on the cow shed & nearly
completed it. I worked on the Johnson plate with a graver cutting
up all the imperfectly bitten lines
Every. after watering my vines
Annie fixed me in hammock
so comfortably that I stayed
there until near 10 o'clk. -
Did not light up at all.
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 4, 1886.

A very hot, cloudless day - Ther. got
up to 84°. Kept very quiet all day -
spent part of the m'f in hammock.
Had dinner after 2 & then wrote
letters to Kate, Will - Shunger &
Koehler. At 5.30 went out as usual
with Herrick to superintend the milking of "Jessie" - Tea after 7 - then
to P.O. with letters, stopping & truck
its on the way. On my return
watered vines & then for an hour
lounged with Annie on piazza.
To bed at about 10.
