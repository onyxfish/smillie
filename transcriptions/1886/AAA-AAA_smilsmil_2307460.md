## Left Page

### February, Monday 15, 1886

mild + bright — a shower between 6 & 7 P.M. — then brilliant moonlight —
To studio before 10 & to work on arms for Will — home at 1.30 — back at 2.30
& by 3.30 had finished ref. — Then spent some time stretching p/p.
of Rough Spot. After 4.30 went to Am. Art Ass'n. — Saw Sutton and together we looked at Eastman
Johnson's "Stage Coach" with a view to a large etching. I am very much pleased with it —
To-day rec'd notes from S.E. Stedman, W.H. Hamilton & Eastman Johnson
giving consent to my etching certain pictures — Then went to N.A. of D. & found that my W.C.
"Rough Spot" had been sold —
Home at 6.15 dinner — at 7.30 went up to 21 E. 73rd St. & Saw Mr.
Hamilton & the [illegible] Homer —
walked home getting it before 9 — Read awhile — Tobed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 16, 1886

Pleasant day — To studio at 10. Spent some time fussing about & with
my p/p. Then set palettes & to work on "Chemung Co. Pasture" that
I have promised to send to Evarts Cueller — New Haven. Painted upon
the cows — home to lunch at 1.30 back before 3 & then Repainted
the sky solidly, getting myself into more work than I had
intended — very tired — sat down in big chair & had a nap — Home to
dinner at 6.30 — After that, dressed & at 8.30 Annie & I went up to
Mr. [illegible] [illegible]'s — Strathmore — cor. Bdway & 53rd St. — to meet Mrs. Ale
Bull & a [illegible] violinist, Barmen by name — neither of them there —
but a pleasant company — we stayed until after 11 — then
home & to bed at midnight —
