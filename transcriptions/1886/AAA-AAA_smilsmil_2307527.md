## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 29, 1886

Delightful day. warm sunshine
cool breezes. Smith, the painter came
with Herrick's help. Put up the
paper on ceilings of N.E. & S.W. bed
rooms & hall — also, did some
painting. Old Wilbur cut grass &
old Cogle worked on the roads — I
worked on Johns on plate and
finished the drawing in before
dinner — went down town at noon
Dinner after. — Afternoon, biting
second etching on Bridgman
plate. Went down town after
tea three home & near 9 o'clk.
when I finished watering the
plants.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 30, 1886

Still delightful weather. The
Parks came up in the aft &
took Annie out riding — I
got started on the biting of
the Johnson etching but was
much interrupted by having
to look after Smith & Herrick.
Got some more of the Japanese
bamboo shades up on the piazza
also Chinese lanterns —
about 6 o'clk. M. & Mr. Park &
daughter & Miss Wyckham
came — we had tea before
7 — later in the eve the Park's
carriage was sent for Jas. Carnalt
& his son Churchill who came
& made us a pleasant call.
The piazza, screened & lighted,
looked very prettily. To bed
abt 11.
