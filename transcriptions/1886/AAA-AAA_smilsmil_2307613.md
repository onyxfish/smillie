## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 22, 1886

Still improving - no recurrence of fever but my head quite swimmy - Got up at about 11
a.m. & made myself comfortable rolled in blankets in front of parlor fire — I read news-papers & Annie, as she has
done ever before, read aloud from Davis's "Two years before the mast".

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 23, 1886

Sunny m[or]'g but clouded over before 11 - About that time I got up - washed, dressed & put on a linen shirt, feeling quite a new man — Some head ache - otherwise feeling pretty well.
Dr. Yale called in the afternoon
I read a good deal — further became interested in a Buccaneer Story for Boys and stuck to it until 11 o'clk. — then to bed & passed a fair night.

Got letters off to Herrick & to Payne — sending a check to sq-accounts.
