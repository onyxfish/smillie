## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 12, 1886

Still overcast & dismal — Got up feeling somewhat better but not like going down to the city to attend the N.A. annual meeting — So I gave that up & arranged to stay until tomorrow — After bkfst wrote letter to Koehler & finished my Sunday letter to mother. Very hard work, for my head was exceeding heavy. Dinner about 1 — In the afternoon Annie, Jim & I started out for a walk. Katie & Mollie in carriage overtook us — A. & Jim got in & went on — I walked to Titicns & beyond & then over the hills & back by back roads having had a very pleasant walk. Tea at 6 — Eve'g .. some of the family amused themselves with the letter game. The weather suddenly cleared & we had a brilliant moonlight night — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 13, 1886

*Home again —*

Again, a dull, foggy morning — at about 10 o'clk. Annie, Jim & I started out for a walk — by the time we reached the end of "the street", Katie & Mollie, in a phaeton, overtook us — Mollie got out & walked with me & A. & Jim got in & rode — we went on to Flagg's new house — took a look around & then on for nearly a mile further & then returned by a valley road, much of the way through woods. It was a little after 2 when Mollie & I got in — I took a half hour nap before dinner at 1. It began to rain as we started out to take the 3.50 train home — where we are — at 6 — Heavy rain — Tea at 7 — Bella announced that she had found a place with Mrs. Havemeyer & Margaret said that she must go to her new place on Monday — close it — a pleasant welcome — Geo. & Valentine in for a few minutes in the eve'g. Found a copy of "Rural Cemeteries" — a present from Mrs. Smillie.
