## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 13, 1886

Still delightfully cool —
Spent some time looking for
Payne, at work in the lane &
Coyle, making flower beds near
the house. I stopped out on
Bridgeman Plate & Relief — &
then worked with graver &
dry point — Went down town
between 6 & 7 P.M. — thru home
& had tea — Early in the eve.
Mr. & Mrs. Dr. Price of W.Y. Mr.
Talleman & Mr. Chas Reed
called — after they left, read
newspaper. 10.30 when we
went to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 14, 1886

A day of heavy rain — thunder
& wind — Performances commenced
about 9 A.M. I put in a pretty
good days work with graver and
burnisher on the Bridgeman plate
Heinck, getting to the end of his
Rake forework, owing to want of duck.
I commenced operations looking
to cistern in Studio & "dark room" in
Etching Room. Zerfus came with
cal. iron breast plate for fire place
in main room but could not finish
it as there was fire in the fire place
afterlea I went down town & got
letters from Mother Cook & Mary Sprague
C — from Saml. Colman. Koehler.
Shninger — Heavy rain continued
— To bed soon after 10.
