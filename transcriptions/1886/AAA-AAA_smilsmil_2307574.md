## Left Page

### October, Friday 1, 1886

A bleak, cloudy, windy cold day — after
bkfp. for some time with Hotchkiss —
Burchard, Pettus, Coyle & Herrick —
Then completed drawings for irons
for timbers under chimney — Then
down town & with Jill telephoned to
Binghampton about the brick but with-
out coming to any conclusion of the
muddle — Then to the foundry & arranged
to have irons forged in the afternoon —
home at noon & developed photo. res
from studio window — Dinner — at 2.30
to foundry — with Washington, the black-
smith until 5 — again with the men
(Payne's bro. in law added) until dark —
Tea — Evelf. Read aloud awhile
— then cards & to bed about 11 —
Note from Ehninger saying he &
Mrs. E. would like to visit us next
week.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 2, 1886

Still cold, cloudy & windy — went
down town after bkfp. — With Jill, tele-
phoned to Shapley & Wells, the brick
merchants of Binghampton — could not
get a satisfactory reply — Wrote a letter
to them telling them that their 3000 brick
were here subject to their order — Then
to foundry — my iron work not yet done.
Home soon after 12. Then with Heimck
& other men — Dinner soon after —
Wrote a letter to Mr. Howard White,
explanatory of my plans & drawings
now ready to send to him upon
which he is to base a new system
of trapping & ventilation for our drain-
age pipes — again some time with
the men — Tea — Evelf. Some
reading — then some cards — then
sitting by the fire chatting until
nearly 11.30 — then to bed.
