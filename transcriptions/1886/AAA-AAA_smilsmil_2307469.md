## Left Page

### March, Friday 5, 1886

Bright, beautiful day — got to studio at 9.45 & spent the m't. on charcoal study of lane & stream near Elmira N.Y. to be submitted to W.m Pate. Home at 1 — lunch — Annie & Kate went to Symphony Matinee — back to studio at 2.30 and finished study — Home at 6.30 — dinner — Eve'g, Reading Hitchcock's little book "Etching in America" of which he sent me a copy — Geo. came in for awhile — To bed about the usual time.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 6, 1886

Pleasant day — but miserable with a headache — got to studio at 9.30 & spent some time finishing old charcoal study "Butternut Bend on Diven's Creek, near Elmira". Then I got to work on another old study for all. C. drawing "The Truants" — or "under the Butternuts" — these I am getting into shape to show to W.m Pate who wants me to make for him a large etching. Home at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked on the "Truants" — At 4, met Annie & Kate at Keppel's & took a look at the men on etchings — then to 5th Ave. & saw the Endolithic marbles — there A. & I went up to Louis Tiffany's Reception 72 5th & Mad Ave — Home at 6.30 — dinner — At 8.30 went to Century — Talked business with Eastman Johnson & E. C. Stedman — Home at 11.15 & right to bed.
