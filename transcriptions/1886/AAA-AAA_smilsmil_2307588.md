## Left Page

### October, Friday 29, 1886.

more weather of the same sort — heavy fog with occasional heavy showers — Slaughter came &
worked from 8.30 to 11 — digging a trench by the side of the road —
Hamlin came & commenced work on fire place in large Room
Knocking out the back dirt to be replaced by fire brick — I worked
with him all the wt. After
dinner I got into the fire place
— it was too large — & laid up the
front that had been knocked out
to a height above my head — Before
it was but one brick thick — now it is
two — did not finish until after 5 —
an extremely difficult & tiresome
job — the etching room & studio being
comfortably heated we spent the
day & evef. in that part of the house
using the etching room for a dining room
— heavy showers at night. Annie
read aloud awhile from Dana's
"Two Years Before the Mast" — To
bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 30. 1886.

A continuance of dismal weather —
Some sunshine in the early wf.
then heavy clouds & wind & at
4.30 rain — Pettus & Scott worked
part of the day — so did Hotchkiss
& Coyle & Hamlin & two helpers.
Got all of the brick under cover —
Hamlin finished dining Room
fire place & then worked on the
north chimney — After 11 I went
down town — Some progress in
the digging of trenches & gathering
stone — Ben Nuelford called
to tell me about fruit trees —
then Mr. Asger Jarthrop called — I
worked with the men in the
rain until after 6 getting the
brick in — Evef. — a little
more of Dana. To bed about 10.
