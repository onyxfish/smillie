## Left Page

### May, Saturday 22. 1886.

A hot day. To studio & commenced
work on the Bridgman plate by
starting the outline on a plate
from an untoned photo. pf. brought
to me by Charl. last eve't. As
it darkened I had to work
rapidly. About 11 Champney &
Nicoll came & we went over to
the M.C. to Durantley's funeral
that was over in about half an hour
& I got to studio with N. J. Tait
who gave me his autograph on a
pf. Later, Eastman Johnson came
in to see his painting. Home at
1.30 — back before 3. Jnyon called
— I sent his "Voice from the Cliff"
home. — Charl. came in. — Home
at 6.30 — Heavy thunder shower
at 7 — Eveg. until 10 writing
a sketch of father's life for H+
J. Morgan. Editor of "The Dominion
Annual Register & Review". Ottawa
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 23. 1886.

Another hot day — Up before 9.
Finished sketch of father's life
begun last night — Annie fried
a trip on the 42d St. Weehawken
Ferry with Jim but found no air
stirring — Charl. came at 1.30 & we
went to studio — took a photograph
shot at Eastman Johnson's painting —
Home to dinner at 2. — About 4
Annie, Jim & I went up to Central
Park — there until 6 — great crowds
— then home & had tea — Eveg.
wrote a long letter to mother —
to-morrow will be her 77th Birth-
day. — At 9.45 took it to G.S.D.
To bed soon after 10.
