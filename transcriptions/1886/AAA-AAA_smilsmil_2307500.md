## Left Page

### May, Thursday 6, 1886.

Overcast m'f, clearing soon after 10 — To studio about 9.30.
Laid Rebiting ground on parts of Homer plates. varnished &
"stopped" about — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — during the
biting I met with an accident by which a portion of the varnish
was removed from the forehead of middle girl & before I discovered
it serious injury had been done by the acid, so that I had
to scrape the entire forehead out — I was in despair — at
that time James Shearman came in & made a call of 1½ hours —
After that I worked up on my plate (putting in new work) until
6.30 — then home to dinner —
Ever'f. Read awhile & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 7, 1886.

*Our wooden wedding day.*

Overcast m'f, with rain in the afternoon & at night. To studio at
9.30 & to work taking p.p. from the Homer plates & to my great joy
found it in better condition than I had hoped — in fact I consider it
now practically finished. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — back about 2.30
Agent from Minneapolis Exposition
called. I pulled another proof
or two — cleaned up — then home soon
after 5 & had a half hours nap. then
up & dressed. Dinner at 7. Mr. & Mrs.
Howard White — Geo. Cook & wife — Emma
Ireland & Jul. Valentine dined with
us — In the eve'f, Chas. Collins — Will
K. & Fanny — Mary & Grace J— Mary
Willard — Fred. J— Ed'w Cook came.
The left at about 11 & before mid-
night we got to bed.
