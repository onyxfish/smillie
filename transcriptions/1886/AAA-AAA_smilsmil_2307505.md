## Left Page

### May, Sunday 16, 1886

Charming day — B[illegible]. at 8.30
& spent the mf writing letters
at 12.30 went to 47 S. — saw Charl
& he accepted invitation to dinner
& to accompany us in the afternoon
on my way home met Maee &
wife & walked a short distance
with them — Dinner at 1.30 —
at 2.45 — Annie — Charl & I went
by 3d Ave. Elevated to 30. Ferry by
Hamielton Ave. to Greenwood —
1½ hour. The display of dog-wood
blossoms wonderfully beautiful
went first to where Annie's father
lies — then to Ocean Hill where
father lies — home it was sadly
& very solemnly beautiful —
At 5.30 we started for home &
got to our door in 1 hour & 5 min.
Tea at 7 — Suel wrote letter
to Mother — took it to G. C. D —
& to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 17, 1886

Brilliantly beautiful day — Got to
studio about 9.30 & spent the
mf. clearing up things & unpacking
the case from P.O.K. Pictures sent
away to Detroit & New Haven. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — Margaret. the
waitress, left early this mf. for her
summer place & Bella, with an
abundance of tears bid her good
byes in the afternoon. I got to
studio at 2.30 & had a number of
callers — Mr. Stewart & daughter, of
Plainfield bought some etchings
Chas. Kurtz, Young Stedman & Sam
Marvin called. I undertook to
get the Breton etching finished by
Aug. 20th — Home at 6.40 after going
to newspaper offices with ads. for
waitress — Suel. Charl came
in for a short time — at 8.30 I
went to Geo. Curtis's — 16 W. 53 — Saw
Mr. C — about etching "A Glass with
the Squire" — Home before 10.
To bed at 10.30.
