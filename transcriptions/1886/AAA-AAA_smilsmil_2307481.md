## Left Page

### March, Monday 29, 1886

Beastly day — mizzle, drizzle
& heavy rain. To studio at 10
& to work painting on "Cliff
Farm" — building on extreme
left — Some time given to Jewell
laying a ground for him — just
as I was starting for lunch,
Shringer came in — stayed a
few minutes — Home abt 1.40 —
back at 2.30 — Painted until
4.30 — About 5 went to A.A.A. —
& spent some time with Sutton
talking about etching Eastman
Johnson's "Stage Coach". He
wants me to do it & promises
all the receipts until I am
paid — Home soon after 6 —
dinner — Evatt read aloud
"Mikado" libretto — wrote a
note to T. Moran & to Inter-
national News Co. & to bed
at 10. 30.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 30, 1886

Still nasty weather, raining most
of the day — Got to studio at 10 &
to work painting buildings on the
right-hand end of Cliff Farm.
Annie lunched with Elg[illegible]. Howe &
I lunched alone. Back to studio
before 3 — made deposit in Bk.
Worked until 5 — then took an
uneasy nap in big chair — Home
soon after 6 — Dinner at 6.30
at 7.30 Annie & I walked to
the Fifth Ave. Theatre. Broadway
& 28th St. — & saw (& heard) Gilbert
& Sullivan's "Mikado". It was
amusing but Thorne (as Ko Ko)
was more clown than anything
else & there was a general
disposition, apparently, to make
a broad farce of it. Out at 11.
Thick fog — walked home &
right to bed.
