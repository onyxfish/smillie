## Left Page

### May, Saturday 8, 1886

Ridgefield —

Very heavy rain with wind in the m't. lighter showers in the afternoon. Went to R. & V. with my Homer plate & got imp't. on plain paper & one p.f. on Jap. paper — Then to studio & tacked them on boards. Home to lunch at 1.30 & at 3.30 went with Annie & Jim to Mother Cook's & with her to G.C. depot & by 4 o'clk. train to Ridgefield where we arr'd about 6 o'clk. & at once to Annie's Sisters — Mr. D. L. Adams. We had tea about 6.30 — Jim was homesick on going to bed but soon quieted & was good all night — After an even't of chatting we got to bed about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 9, 1886

A brilliant, beautiful day — the wind blew strongly in the m't but went down in the afternoon & the temperature was very nice'd. B'k'f't about 8 — I got up with a headache which made me more or less useless all day. I managed to hold up until noon — then lay down & slept until dinner at 2, where I ate a few mouthfuls — After dinner went out for a drive — Everything very enjoyable — on our return the off whiffletree bolt broke & let it down on the mare's heels — fortunately she did not become restive & we escaped what might have been a very serious accident. Being near a house we were strapped up so that we got home safely — Again went to bed & again had a nap. Tea — & soon after 8 I gave up entirely & went to bed & passed a miserable night.
