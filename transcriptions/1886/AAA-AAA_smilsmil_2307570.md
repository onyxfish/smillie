## Left Page

### September, Thursday 23, 1886

Heavy Rain during the night. Found
a large red squirrel in the overflow
pipe of my studio cistern, causing
a tremendous overflow onto the stair
way. Overcast most of the m't.
bright afternoon. Saw Gill & sent
order to Binghamton for 3000 bricks
to build chimney on north side of
house. Took 10 p't. from Breton
plates. Dinner. At about 3, Annie,
Elig., Jim & I went to the fair. A
great crowd. I took my camera &
got 10 pictures. Home & developed
them before tea time. Eve'g.
Annie read aloud a couple
of Harker articles. It was
near 11 when we got to bed.

I got a letter from Marvin,
complaining that the sky on my
Breton etching was too flat
& asking if I could not improve
it.

coyle did not work

## Right Page

### September, Friday 24, 1886

Bright, pleasant & warm day.
Mr. & Mrs. Parke came up soon after
help. & took Elig.*, Annie & Jim
off on a long ride. I went down
town, spent some time at Doolittle's
getting prints from negatives that
I took at the fair ground yesterday.
Then home & worked for a time on
Breton plate making some changes
in the sky to please Mr. Marvin.
Took an imp. before dinner. After
that took a couple of p't. and
at 4 sent away the plate. Then
lounged & rested. Eve'g. Annie
read aloud. I did up, in stout
m't. the signed Bridgman p't.
& mounted a Breton for Koehler.
About 9 P.M. a great humming
noise commenced in my right
ear disturbing my night's rest
very much.

coyle did not work.
