## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 5, 1886

Overcast & at times threatening rain.
Spent the m'f. about the place —
grading on lawn — filling holes back
of the house & getting in irons on the
sill where the chimney is to be built.
Down town about 11 — back at noon —
Dinner a little before 1 — ab't 1.30 Els.
& Annie went to depot to meet Emma
Ireland — I worked about the place
until dusk. Herrick got the irons
on the sill — Emma came before
5 — Rest. Chatting until 9 — then
cards until 10 — To bed about
10.30

Coyle worked.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 6, 1886

Overcast but soon cleared to be
a brilliantly warm day — All the
m'f. with the men — about 11 went
down town, wrote a letter to Shapley
& Wells telling them that if I did
not hear from them by Friday I
would buy bricks elsewhere — Home
about 12.30 — again with the men
after dinner until 3 — then took
camera & went to lot back of
the butcher's & got some photos
of sheep — Home at 5 — with men
until 6 — Tea — Felt unwell at
tea table & had to lie down for
an hour — then developed photo
taken this p.m. — Glorious moon-
light night — to bed soon after
10 —

Coyle worked.
