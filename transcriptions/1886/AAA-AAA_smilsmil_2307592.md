## Left Page

### November, Saturday 6, 1886.

Bright m'f, then clouded, with showers.
At night a slight thunder storm and
continued rain. Up at 6.30 — had
bkfp. about 7.30 — Payne came with team
& took away trunks & packing cases —
George, Parke's darkie, came with horse
& three seat wagon about 9. I left Hamlin
at work on the chimney, just getting up thro'
the roof. We left at 9.15 & had some
quite heavy showers before getting to depot.
Train on time at 11.12 — a comfortable
journey, Jim behaving remarkably well.
Annie weighs 135 lbs — Jim, 35 lbs. Got
into Hoboken at 5.20 & up to 38° S: a few
m. after 6. Mother Cook had provided
a hot roast chicken for us & a woman
was in the kitchen — we had a good
meal. Found the house terribly
dirty, partly owing to dust & muss
made in putting in new furnace.
Walter & Geo. Cook & Jno. Valentine
came in for a few minutes — To bed
about 9.30.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 7. 1886.

Bright, breezy, cold day. Bkfp. about
9 o'clk. Between 10 & 11 Geo. Smillie
came in soon followed by his youngest
[illegible] — When Annie took the child
Jim behaved very singularly, evidently
jealous. After 12 I walked with Geo.
down to studio & went in with him.
Home to dinner at 2. Wrote my
Sunday letter to Kate & at 5.30 with
Annie & Jim to Mother C's — Jim & I
went on to G. C. Depot & posted K's letter.
Took tea at Mother C's — Marion Wright
& the Adams girls (they called at our house
during the day) were there. Marion was
very ready to talk of her engagement
to Mr. Jelke. Jim was uproarious in
his enjoyment of the company & amused
them very much by telling stories — Home
before 8 o'clk. Eve'g, most of the time
spent in writing a letter of instructions
to Herrick which was left unfinished
when I went to bed at 10.30.
