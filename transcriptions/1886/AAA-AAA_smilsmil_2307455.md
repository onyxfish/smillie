## Left Page

### February, Friday 5, 1886.

Brilliant & exceedingly cold —
To studio at 9.30 & to work on Sketch
for A.B.N. Co. of Lincoln Mill — Stuck to
it closely & finished it shortly
before 1 — then home to lunch —
then down town — got to Clark's
at 2.30 — no-one — then to A.B.N.
Co. — there for some time — got course
printing rag & some sheets of black-
ed india — then up town — to Am.
Art Ass'n to see the Johnson picture
"The Stage Coach" — but did not —
then to W.C. 24th — met Hitchcock
of The Tribune there — 5 o'clk. when
I got to studio — 6 o'clk. when I
got home — dinner — Eve'g.
wrote notes to Farrer, Gifford &
sister Kate — Annie read aloud
from Adam Bede until 10 —
then I went to J. C. D. with
Kate's note — home & to bed
before 11.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 6, 1886.

Bright m'g — milder — overcast
afternoon & snow at night — Got a
note from Kate while at bhfs — say-
ing that it was so cold at the Terrace
that they would excuse us — so, at 9.30
I telegraphed her that our visit was
postponed to next Sunday — Then
to K. & V. canal S. — with big folate
& several kinds of paper to be wet
down for proving on Monday — back
then to studio before 11 — Set palette
& painted upon a study painted at
Elenora in '76 — Got a letter from Wm.
Pate offering me $1000 for an etched plate
18×30 in. Home to lunch at 1.30. Annie
at Mary S. Cook's — back at 2.30 & painted till
dark — Dinner at 6. At 8.30 went to
Century — Saw Stedman & got a note
to admit me to see his Winslow Homer
"A Voice from the Cliffs" — but he is not
at once ready to give his permission
for me to etch it. About 11 — I
walked up through a snow storm
with Dr. Yale. To bed about
midnight.
