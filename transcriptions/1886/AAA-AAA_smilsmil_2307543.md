## Left Page

### July, Saturday 31. 1886.

Breezy, warm & bright — after a dubious m'g. — I got a very fine Rebiting ground over the ewhore of the Johnson plate & varnished it — much time spent with Herrick & Smith over Chemy Room ceiling —
I got the acid on my plate & worked until after 3.30 when the carriage came & we all went off for a drive over Butterfield's hills. We had expected Mr. & Mrs. Sprague this p.m. but they telegraphed us that they w? come on Monday. Ev'g. by invitation Mr. & Mrs. de Schweinitz & two Miss de Joinville & May & Will Jessup — Syprian Geo. & Harry J — three Miss Cooks & two Miss Stotesburys came & passed a pleasant ev'g — a little dancing & singing of college songs. — The piazza looked very prettily with drawn screens & Chinese lanterns — To bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 1. 1886.

A breezy, warm m'g. Wind from the East — overcast afternoon — light showers with thunderstorms within hearing distance — I spent an hour or more in Chemy Room engaged oiling a part of the ceiling left by Smith — very anxious to get it ready for the Spragues to-morrow. — Then for an hour in the hammock. Then wrote to Chas. & Enoch Lewis. Dinner shortly after 2. Feeling very heavy later in the afternoon I took a nap & felt heavier — Promenading piazza for some time before tea after I had written to Kate — Ev'g. a little writing — a little more promenading and to bed at 10 o'clk.
