## Left Page

### August, Saturday 28. 1886.

Before we were out of bed in the m'g, Annie rec. a telegram from G.
Cook announcing the death of her b-o W'm at Cape Cod, by accidental
shooting. The day was very hot.
I finished the biting of Boston plate
& about 11 went down town with Chase
& took a number of views of the hunt
destrick — Parke brought us up home
about 12.30 — In the afternoon I
took 3 miles from the Boston plate.
Chase & I went down town after
tea — the ev'g was spent in
easy chairs & hammocks on the
piazza until after 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 29. 1886.

A red hot day made bearable
by a pleasant breeze. We all
spent the day in loitering
about the house wherever we
found coolest corners or the
most breeze. I felt very
much demoralized & only
wrote one letter — to Mary —
Ev'g, Mr. White wrote out
specifications for improvements
in our drain pipes here —
To bed about 10 o'clk.
