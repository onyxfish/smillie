## Left Page

### August, Friday 20, 1886

Another charming day — up late —
11 o'clk. before I got to work on my
Breton Etching, giving my time to
Herrick & the "dark Room" & to Coyle
& the lane. Tetched mint dinner
ble o'clk — again until 4.30 —
then for a time finishing about
the dark Room & at 6 went down
town. Evey. Mr White &
Annie, by turns, read aloud.
Fiske's "Destiny of Man" until
near 10. my usual walk on
piazza & to bed before 10.30.
Jim, not yet recovered from
his bowel troubles, but better —

Coyle at work in lane.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 21, 1886

Slightly overcast wtf. clearing
to a charming, breezy day — Wind
S.& E — Herrick finished the "dark
Room" under my supervision —
I felt wholly unlike doing any
work on my Breton Etching as my
head was very heavy — nearly
all the afternoon was spent
in making up solutions and
getting my dark room in shape
for work — took out camera at
about 5 p.m. & made two ex-
posures — Mr. White & Annie
went off with Parke. Riding —
Evey. I developed my two
negatives. Not a brilliant success
— after 9 o'clk. Read newspapers
& got to bed at 10.30 — Jim
still continues out of sorts —

Evey. Rec'd telegram from
Charl — dated Chicago. Saying
that he was on the way to
Montrose.
