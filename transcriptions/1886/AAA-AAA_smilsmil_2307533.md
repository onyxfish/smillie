## Left Page

### July, Sunday 11, 1886.

Beautiful clear, cool day —
date 68°— Spent most of the
m'f. working about the vines
about the house. Then wrote
letters to Winslow Homer — S.
R. Koehler & Jas. Bannister —
Dinner about 2 — Then a
nap. Again writing — about
5— Geo. Lathrop came on
the grounds & work him D'r.
Price, associated with Fred
Durand. We went over the
house — Tea after 7 — wrote
a letter to Boyle, ordering
duck & my letter to Mother
went down to P.O. at 8.30 —
then walked piazza with
Annie — wrote to Geo. & [illegible]
to Wm. Gardner — To bed
about 11.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 12, 1886.

Delightful cool day — Ther.
went down to 49°. last night. In
the m'f. Spent some time out of
doors. Payne again drawing earth
to the lane. Mr. Keeler's boy came
to work on Mac. I laid relisting
ground on Bridgman plate and
etching ground on the Johnson
plate — After dinner the piano
hired by Annie arr. & after a
struggle was got into the house.
Smith came in the afternoon
& painted canvas on piazza &
duck in bed room hall on attic
stair — Aunt H. came up — I
went down town at 6.30 — then
tea — Enjoyed moonlight on
piazza until after 8 — Then
read newspapers & to bed
about 10.

Geo's second boy — Chas. V. S.
Smillie born at Piticus —
conn — at 5 P.m. to-day.
