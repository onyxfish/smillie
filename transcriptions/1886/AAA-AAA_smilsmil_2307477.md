## Left Page

### March, Sunday 21, 1886

Dull, disagreeable day — no rain until night time. Spent the mng. reading — writing to Schringer — Patt & Sutton & a short walk before dinner at 2 — After dinner tried to work upon the rewriting of my paper on Etching to be read before Art Student's League next Sat. eveg. About 5 went around to Mother Cook's where Annie was with Jim — Home before 6 — Tea at 6.30 — Well again worked on Etching Paper — wrote my home letter and took it to depot after 10.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 22, 1886

Raw & windy — to studio at 9.30 & to work on first painting on 35 × 60 — "A Cliff Farm — Normandy". Got a good start & enjoyed myself immensely — About noon, Grady's man came to say that Miss Giles refused to deliver the window Homer. When I got home at 1, found young Stedman there — had been up to see Miss G — not in — to go again. Lunch — to studio at 2.30 — met Ed Parker on the way — Painted until near 5 — then to Grady's about Mr. G. G. Whitnie's frame — then back to Gurney's Efp — met young Stedman — Miss G. placated — I may send again to-morrow. Home at 6.15 — dinner — Well at 8 Annie & I went to Ogilvie's — 55 W. 55 St. and made an hours visit — our first call — then rode down to 29th St. & to 253 Fifth Ave — Mr. Emory's (Sarah Leggett) wedding Reception — there until near 10.30 — then home & to bed soon after 11.
