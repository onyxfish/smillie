## Left Page

### April, Saturday 10, 1886.

Charming, mild day — went to Murray Hill Hotel with a note for the Park's — then to studio at 10.30. Relict big tree on Mt. 37 — got a transfer of Homer tracing onto my ground & varnished the plate — Ready for action —
Home to lunch at 1.30 — It was 3 when I got to Newman's for that tile file & then found that I would have to go to Patterson Bros. in Park Lane for what I wanted — It was 4.30 when I got to studio — Worked until 6 trying to get things set put away & in order that I could start on Monday on Homer etching but could not — Home at 6.30. Then dinner — most of evng. spent with newspapers — Read aloud Gladstone's Home Rule Speech — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 11, 1886.

Pleasant m'f., but overcast and chilly afternoon — Sat in bedroom bay window & wrote notes to T.D. Payne — Herrick, Clarence Sterling, Kortler & mother — after 1 o'clk went out & walked west 2 — then dinner — after that sat some time with Annie, then took a nap — about 6 Charl came in & we arranged to spend W'k 2 in P.O.K. Tea at 7. After sitting with Annie engaged assorting Tatter's p.p. & mine until near 11 — then to bed.
