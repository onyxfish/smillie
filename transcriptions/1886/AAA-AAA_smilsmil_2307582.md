## Left Page

### October, Sunday 17, 1886

Brilliant surf & very cold. Ther.
went down to 26° last night. The
iron pipe from studio cistern to dark
room froze & burst, letting out all the
water. About 11 it clouded over & soon
after there was quite a snow storm
lasting for nearly an hour. Before that
I spent some time up a beech tree
sawing out dead wood. Then in and
read with great pleasure parts of Joy.
fellow's life & letters. Dinner about 2 —
after that more reading & a little
photographing. Tea soon after 6.
Then wrote to Kate — Charl. & Walter
cook. At 8 took letters to P.O. on
my return, wrote to G.H. Curtis about
the return of his Eastman Johnson
painting & to Belle Tweedy about Phil.
tickets. — To bed at 10 —

## Right Page

### October, Monday 18, 1886

Dull & overcast, growing milder
throughout the day, much time
again with the men. Hinck at new
bit of fence, end of lane — Coyle, with
him & on the lane — Hotchkiss putting
up short bit of wall at corner by fair
ground gate. Pettus hauling stone to
him & Scott & his man hauling dirt
& spreading it in the hollows north
of the house. About 1 went down town
— afternoon wrote several letters
to Scribner & Co. acknowledging receipt
of $200 — to W. Howard White — to bro. Will.
acknowledging receipt of two checks & to
Case, Cash. 2d. Nat. Bk. of W.I. city, sending
the checks to him — then out into tree
top sawing out dead wood until dark.
Evg. wrote to Bryan, Taylor & Co. declining
the offer of a couple of plates to etch —
