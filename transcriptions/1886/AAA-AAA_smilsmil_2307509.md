## Left Page

### May, Monday 24, 1886.

Rain in showers most of the day. To studio before 10 — Copper plate from Shark — 3 — laid grounds on them — Charl sent up boy with finished print from his photo of Bridgman's painting — & I worked on the tracing. Home to lunch & back before 3 — Experimented on the taking of gelatine transfers. Mr. Smith, agent for Minneapolis [illegible] came — Annie came in between 4 & 5 & we went to the N.A. to Private View of the "Impressionists" there — They grow more hateful to me. Home to dinner at 6.30 at 8.30 went with Charl. to his dark Room where he tried to develop two "exposures" that I made in his camera this mf. during the Rain — not enough light or time & both plates "light struck" — Failure — Home at 9 —
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 25, 1886.

Raw & wet in the mf., clearing in the afternoon — To studio before 10 & spent the mf. finishing gelatine tracing after Bridgman & transferring it to plate with fair success — At 12.45 I went to A.B.N.Co & lunched with Will & with him at 2 o'clk. went to Tom Shearman's where we met Geo. & Charl & all signed a paper releasing the Executors from the necessity of filing a formal statement of the assets of the Estate of James Smillie. Then I went to 209 E. 18th St — saw old Welleir & got his autograph on some pt. There to Jas. M. Hart & got his autograph on pl. of Leon Lake. Then to Brinderlich's — Then to Studio — then out to horse fountain in Madison Sq. with Charl's camera & made two exposures. Home at 6.30 — Dinner — Evef. at work again with plt. of Father's work reassorting them. After 9.30 Charles Collins came in for a few minutes —
To bid at 10.30.
