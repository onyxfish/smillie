## Left Page

### August, Monday 2, 1886

A glorious breezy cool day. Smith
came to work at 5. A. M. Herrick
did not come at all — I got to work
with Smith & put up some of the
mouldings on the Cherry Room ceiling
myself. I was all the m[?] with
him & at last cleared out the Room
in the mean time acid for rebiting
on Johnson plate was warming &
when I at last got to it I found
it actually boiling — I did not re-
bite — Dinner at 12.45 & at 1.45
with the two Katherines I drove over
to New-Milford for W. & Mrs. Sprague —
They did not come & we returned
wondering. Saw Warner & Coy about
filling cistern at entrance to lane
with earth & wrote a letter for Town
Council — Eve'g. a shower or two —
Signed some more Homer [illegible] —
About 9.30 the Spraques came by
Stage — the 3 [?] Francis would not stop
at New Milford — They were in good
humor — had some tea & to bed
at about 11.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 3, 1886

Great change in the weather — very cold
overcast & blustering — Ther. went down
to 48°. A blazing big fire to keep
us warm all day long — Herrick at
work putting up partition in Studio cellar
— Mr. + Mrs. S. amused themselves
in doors — I was much divided
between rebiting on the Johnson
plate & looking after things generally.
I did not get my Rebiting quite
finished. Eve'g. Mr. S. + Annie
played whist [illegible] — Mr. Sprague & me.
To bed at 10.30 — I went down
town with letters about 8.30
