## Left Page

### February, Thursday 25, 1886

Horrible day of heavy rain
& high winds, ending some time
during the night with a light
fall of snow. Got to studio at
9.30 & to work on 11×15 sketch
that I made before I painted
the large "Normandy Cliffs".
Home to lunch at 1.30 & back
before 3 — Painted as long as
I could see — Home at 6 —
entirely used up. After
dinner lay on sofa in parlor
after 9 o'clk. Annie read loud
for an hour from Howells' "Indian
Summer" — then to bed.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 26, 1886

A horrible day. Ther. 42°. & a gale
of wind — To studio before 10 — for a
while worked on small Normandy
cliffs — then to work on 15×24 study
on Lake Geo — Harbor Islands — Gifford
came in for a little while — Home
to lunch & back at usual time
worked on Lake Geo until after
5 — home to dinner at 6.20 —
Eve'g, got message from Louis
Tiffany urging me to go to his
hearth warming to-night — the in-
vitation sent some days ago having
miscarried — did not feel well
enough to go. Wrote note to Louis
& one to Koehler — out at 9.30 & mailed
them — then had a game of cards
with Annie & to bed about 11.

Got a telegram in the e'vg. from
Charle announcing his arrival
at Cheyenne.

The gale of wind 84 miles an hour
the highest recorded by the Signal
service here.
