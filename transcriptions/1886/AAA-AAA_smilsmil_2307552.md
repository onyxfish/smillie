## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 18. 1886.

Glorious day. Finished tracing of Breton Photo. We't at "Finis-
tère" & succeeded in getting a fairly good transfer. Then
rec'd — Ph't from Eastman Johnson with photo. Letter suggesting
a slight change in the countryman's face. After dinner I made the
change & then packed the plate & sent it with a pl. to K. & V,
wrote a note to E. J. Gone to
Koehler & between 5 & 6 went
down to E'ffo. & P.O. After that
took a walk over the lost hill
& then home to tea. — We't
M. & Mrs. de Scheveinitz called
& spent most of the eve'g. Jim
was up two or three times in
the course of the night, trouble
in the bowels. To bed at
10.30.

Coyle worked on lane —

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 19. 1886.

Another charming day — cool
enough to have a fire in the
early m'f. I got to work on
my Breton Etching — after going
down town in the m't about 9.30.
I also looked after the work going
on in the lane — Ploughing — also
a little **Battle**dore on the Piazza —
Afternoon, some more etching &
some time with Herrick directing
work in the dark Room — At 4.30
we went out Riding, going over the
Butterfield Hill & back by Creek Road
In at 6.30 — Tea at 7 — We't, spent
a little time in dark Room, putting
plates in holders. Chatting until near
10 — Jim, not well, but better
to-day —

Coyle on lane.
