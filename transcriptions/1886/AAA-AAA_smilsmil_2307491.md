## Left Page

### April, Sunday 18. 1886.

Brilliant day — Bkfst. before
9 — some time arranging pts.
for Fred — a short walk before
dinner at 2. About 3 I took
Jim & by Boulevard cars, rode
up to Will's in 73d. St — there for
nearly an hour — then, with David
Belden, took Elevated R.R. — out at
42d. St. & 6th Ave, & crossed to Mother
Cook's — left there at near 6 — &
then home — Many Willard there.
Tea at 7 — Ruth Geo. C. came
in with some Arbutus — a party
off to-day — Long Island. I wrote
a note to Fred Smillie & my usual
letter to Mother — Out at about
10 o'clk. & walked there —
near 11 when I got to bed —

## Right Page

### April, Monday 19. 1886.

A hot day — Ther. on shady side
of the Ave, at 3 P.M. stood at 81°.
Many duties kept me until 10.20
when I got to studio & at once
resumed the biting of Huttoner
etching. At 1 o'clk. went to the
Bank — then to Bierstadt's & got
his autograph on a [illegible] for Rocky
Mts. It was nearly 1.45 when
I got home to lunch & 2.45 when
I got back to studio. Gave
my etching some long bitings
which gave me a chance to
do some other things in the studio.
Home at 6.30 — Most of the
evng. spent over Dick Kitchen
accts. To bed at 10.30.
