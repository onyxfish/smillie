## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 28, 1886

Clear, bright winter day — to Studio
about 10 & got to work on × W.C.
sketch from Nature — the original
for "A Fallow Field" — I will dub
this "An October Study — got some
tones that I think are pretty good
for me. — Messenger from Will came
to see if the Liberty Statue drawing
was finished — home at 1.30 — back
at 2.30 — worked awhile on a
drawing for Miss A — finished An
Oct Study — then commenced to
wash out on another Montrose
Study from Nature — At 4.30 went
downtown — bought Diary — went to
Walter's to look over plans — Home
at 6.30 — dinner — Wrote to Herrick
asking for some measurements.
Collins came in at about 9 —
To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 29, 1886

Raw & overcast. To studio at 10 &
at once to work on the big Street
washed out a lot of foliage &
continued the town through the
large group of trees — was surprised
at the promptness with which
things came together — so that at
4 o'clk. the drawing really
looked to be quite finished &
I was pleased. — And yet — this
"conventional" — in color — in treat-
ment — in composition — and I can't
help it — I can't get the wheels of
my cart out of the ruts. — Schaff
sent me a note & [illegible] of his plans
after Vinton & Sprague Pearce —
Well I wrote a letter to him
& one to Koehler — also to Mr. Peeble
in Montrose — Chase came in
for half an hour — To bed about
10.30. After getting to bed
Jim developed a croupy cough
which pretty effectually des-
troyed our night's rest.
