## Left Page

### December, Saturday 4, 1886

Still very cold windy & Raw with overcast threatenings — To
studio at usual time — made a slight charcoal sketch of
Statue of Liberty drawing — To lunch at usual time & back
before 3 — stretched a piece of drawing paper — then Yewell
came in with his plates — the room in which Gen. Grant died and
I spent the rest of the day light in laying a rebiting ground upon
it for him — Home at 6 — Swell about 9 o'clk. went to Century where
I have quite a show on one of the screens of my eight reproductions
etchings — many very pleasant things were said to me about them —
H[enry] Stanley, the African Traveller, was there — a little, ordinary looking man —
his appearance disappointed me. Walked into town with Ge[o]. To
bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 5, 1886

An old fashioned snow storm.
About 11.30 I went to F.H. Smith's —
350 E. 34th St. by appointment & spent
two hours with him looking over
his recent "publications" — "Well worn
Roads" — "The Tile Club" — etc — etc —
Home to dinner at 2 — most of
the afternoon spent sleeping —
& the rest loafing — until near
9 o'clk — then wrote letters
until 11 — then to bed.
