## Left Page

### June, Friday 11. 1886.

Charming day, altho very warm
in the sun. B.k.p. at 7.30 - worked
at packing until near 10 - Then
went to Inkler's - Ave. B. &17's. & bot.
two old brass door knockers - Then
to 2°. Nat. Bnk. Then to studio and
for more than an hour worked getting
paintings & etchings ready for the
fall exhibits. Then to Moreup's. P.O.
Station 7. Sinks & Macaulay's -
Clark's, Bolane's, Beyerdorfer's -
Gurney's & home at near 2. had
lunch & there, after a rest, to
work packing - made good pro-
gress & had dinner at 6.30 -
Evg. Walter & Marie & later,
Geo., Smiley & Jul. Valentine
came in. I finished packing
case & trunk before 10 o'clk &
soon after that, to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 12. 1886.

Beautiful day - Up before 6 -
B.k.p. at 6.45 - Exp. men came at 7 -
Gas & water man at 7.30 - Miss
Wickham came at 8 - so did Emily
Cook, to whom we gave our last
loaf of bread & the keys of the house.
We got to foot of Christoplerl - at
about 8.35 & had to wait for our
trunks - We got over to our train
about ahead of time very comfortably
Jim behaved admirably -
As usual, Annie felt the excess
of r.r. riding after leaving Scranton
where we met Wm. Parke. At
Montrose depot, about 3, Pralle
was waiting for us with two vehicles.
We got to the house about 4.30 and
there found Herrick who had every
thing in readiness for us - We
had a comfortable meal after
6 & got to bed before 10.
