## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 20, 1886

Another very pleasant day, but not quite so warm as yesterday. Got to studio about 9.30 — to work drawing for A.B.M. Co. — Arms of Dominicana — Home at 1.30 — About 2 St Blackwell & assis Faut came & removed the stitches, pronouncing his parts in excellent condition — Mr. Hayp. the nurse, then left. I got back to studio at 2.30 — Miss Straight of Bridgeport & a friend came with a painting of a cat — gave her some general advice — finished my drawing at 6 — after a hard days work — wet down paper for printing to-morrow — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Every house moving preparatory to resuming my old quarters — wrote a note to Sister Mary & took it to G. [illegible] — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 21, 1886

A very pleasant day — not quite so warm as yesterday — To studio about 9.30 & got things ready for proving. Spent the m'f over No. 37 getting only three proofs from it before I went home to lunch at 1.30 — It was 3 when I got back & I took four pp. (& they were very bad ones) from the Winslow Homer plate — I got home at 6.45 exceedingly tired — after dinner, in the ev'g. I read my paper for awhile, then tried to write some notes but I was too tired & at 9.30 I went to bed.
