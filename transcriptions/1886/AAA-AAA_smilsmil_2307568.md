## Left Page

### September, Sunday 19, 1886

The day began & ended with a thunderstorm & did not quite get over it between times. The day was not a pleasant one. After bkfst., while it was still raining I went on a fruitless hunt for Hewitt the butcher, as I had forgotten to order Sunday's dinner. Later, he sent a boy & we did not go hungry. Before dinner I wrote letters to Kate & Will — after dinner I took a nap — the eve'g thunderstorm came about 6 o'clk. After sunset, some beautiful cloud effects — at 7.30 I went down town & called on Ben Mulford & wife, expecting to find Fanny Connel there, but she left on Friday. Home at 8.30 — wet paper for printing to-morrow — Read until about 10 — then to bed — breezy night.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 20, 1886

Breezy, cool day — Got up with my ears buzzing — Probably a form of cold in the head — much discomfort all day. nevertheless I worked closely on the proving of Breton plate and hardly went out of doors. In the eve'g Elizabeth, Annie & I had a game of cards that kept us up until after 10. It was near 11 when we went to bed.
