## Left Page

### February, Sunday 7, 1886

Dull, gloomy day — Heavy head.
Bkfst about 9 — lounging as
usual until afternoon — then out
& took a 30 minute walk —
Dinner soon after 2. Bet.
3 & 5 had a nap — About 6.
Charl came in & told me
that he rec'd a telegram yester-
day from Jacksonville, Fla. stating
that Aunt H. is there, dangerous-
ly ill with Pneumonia. Mrs.
Powers & Mrs. Chamberlin are with
her. By careless plumber a frozen
drip pipe was left open yesterday
& thawed about 6 P.M. & as Margaret
passed with a lighted candle & took
fire with a slight explosion — no
damage — but there might have
been — Wrote a long letter to
mother. Took [illegible] to G.C.D. at
10 & to bed about 11 — passed
a very uncomfortable night
with headache.

## Right Page

### February, Monday 8, 1886

Brilliant day — milder weather.
Got up with headache which
passed off. At 9.30 went to K. &
R. & spent the morning there with
Proven, trying the effect of pfs.
on different papers — left at
12 & went to Studio — Home
at 1.30 — back at 2.30 & spent
the afternoon with charcoal
trying to work out a composition
sketch 18×30 for Pate & [illegible].
At 5.15 went to Barber's & had
my hair cut — Home at 6 —
dinner — Eve'g. word from
Charl — telegram rec'd — a change
in Aunt H — for better — Annie
read aloud from Adam Bede.
Then we had a game of
cards & to bed soon after 11.

Miss Avery sent us 3 concert
tickets but we could not go.
We sent them to the Adams
girls — & they did not go.
