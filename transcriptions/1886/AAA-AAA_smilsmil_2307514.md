## Left Page

### June, Thursday 3, 1886

Annie & Jim went to Southampton.

A dismal rain in the m'f clearing charmingly in the afternoon. Went to Sakochi's at 9.30 & made an appointment with him for to-morrow m'f. Got to studio's before 11 — Saml. Coleman came in — I worked at getting things in the studio's put away for the summer & preparing for my flitting to Montrose — a serious piece of work for me — Home about 5.30 — afterlunch helped Annie & then went with her & Jim across 34th S. Ferry & started them off on the 3.20 (from M'f. side) Train for Southampton where many Sprague cook is now spending the summer. To studio's at 4 — Home & had dinner at near 7 — Ev'f. Charl came in. wrote a letter to Emma Bourne. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 4, 1886

Delightful day — Up early. Help- at 7.30 at Dr. Sakoch's — W. 54th S. at 8.30 — his partner, Tho's. Fletcher took impressions f my mouth — Got to studio's about 10 & spent the m'f in putting things away & into order — Home to lunch at 1 — Mother & Mate lunched with Mother Cook — back to studio's at 2.15 — Dr. Martin there for his book — I worked at packing etc until 5 — then to Donelly's — 12th S. & B'way — Lamps — then to Dunlap's & Knoy's trying to get a hat but found nothing that pleased me — Home to dinner at 6.30. Ev'f. Charl came in — at 9.30 I went with him to his dark room & he fitted me out with his camera & 22 plates in holders & box — It was 11 when I got home — Right to bed.
