## Left Page

### August, Monday 16. 1886.

Overcast w'f. Heavy rain in the afternoon, ceasing at night —
Mrs. White so much under the weather that she did not leave her room all day. I spent the w'f in writing a letter to Koehler & in preparing pls. of the Johnson plate to send to him. — Afternoon I went to Crandall's Shop & got some felt to pack the windows in my dark-room. Much of the afternoon I spent with Herrick supervising the dark-room construction & tacking felt on to the window sashes. At 7.30 it ceased to rain & I went to H[?]. with pkl. for Koehler & pks. designed Homer pls. for Cassell & Co. Eve'g a little reading of newspapers & a little writing. To bed at 10.30.

Coyle ½ day —

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 17. 1886.

Cloudy & cool all day — After getting Johnson [illegible]. Ready to send to K.&V. I at last got started on the Breton Tracing. Some time in the lane where the Payne boys were at work. In the afternoon, nearly finished tracing — some time with Herrick at his work on dark room. Before 6 o'clk. went down town to P.O. with Annie — back to tea at 6.30 — Eve'g, until 9 o'clk we all sat chatting by the fire light. Then lighted lamp. To bed before 11.

Coye did not work.
