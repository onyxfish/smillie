## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 10. 1886.

Overcast with, with some rain — milder — until near 11 o'clk. Unpacking trunk, putting away things & writing letters — then to studio. Home to lunch & back at 2.30 — Mr. Seddelmeyer, owner of Minnecatship "Christ before Pilate" came in with Geo. S Champney — At 5 I went to Mr. Keever's shop to see about getting some small boards to mount my photos. upon. Home to dinner at 6 — Spent most of the eve'g, with B— going over acts— trying to find just where the excess of money spent this last summer had gone to — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 11. 1886.

Mild & pleasant. Hazing about the studio all day waiting for cases from Montrose — Got Postal card in the m'f. from Farini's announcing their arrival & that I must present bills of lading — Having none I went to Gurney's & wrote a note that his driver might take down — on calling again in the afternoon was told that the driver did not get them because he had not money enough to pay the freight — 2.32 — I cleared up some parts of studio & studied over some things in the picture way that I hope to do — Home before 6 — Every thing there in terrible confusion — carpets up & floors wet. About 7 — after Annie got June to bed we went to Mother Cook's to tea — there until 9 — then home. To bed soon after 10.
Ed Parker called at studio for a few minutes between 5 & 6 o'clk.
