## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 7. 1886.

Again very hot — heavy thunder storms all about us in the afternoon —
very little rain falling upon us.
I went to look after Coyle in the
mf & found him cutting briars —
under a bush, his coat, & under
that, a bottle half full of the worst
kind of whiskey — I told him that
I would take it up to the house with
me where he could come & drink as
often as he wished, but he did not
come. Herrick worked on the bed
room ceilings but we made
little progress. In spite of interruptions I put in a good days
work with dry point on Bridgman
plate — Went down town after tea.
Showers — very muggy & still —
on piazza some time — then
read news papers & to bed
at the usual time.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 8. 1886.

Delightful day — Ther. 65° at
8 A.M. I worked diligently on
Bridgman plate all the morning
with dry-point & graver, finishing
about the figures & horses — all
the afternoon I was with Herrick
& Smith putting down a heavy
piece of duck & white lead upon
a piece of piazza flooring at the
east window of main room. At
8 o'clk. P.M. Parke sent for
us & we went there & spent
an eve'g playing whist. Mr.
& Mrs. Woodin & others there —
Annie & I played with Mr.
& Mrs. Hossa — Home at 11
& to bed.
