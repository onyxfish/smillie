## Left Page

### April, Friday 2, 1886

Beautiful day — Annie went with her mother out to So. Orange.
To studio at usual time & for a time worked on Etching No. 37
"the Shady Side of the house —
Breeze Hill". About 11, went to
N.A. & D. for a few minutes — it
being varnishing day — Home
to lunch & back as usual &
finished etching — Annie
came in about 5 & we went
together to Hayes — Burge & [illegible] —
to look at some clocks — then to
Sloan's to look at some rugs —
for wedding present for [illegible] &
Emily Cook. Home at 6.30 — dinner
— Walt I wrote a long letter
to Saml. Colman — To bed
about 11.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 3, 1886

Cold, Raw & overcast m[orning], clearing
in the afternoon — fine night — To
studio 10, walked down with
Abbott Thayer who was in very
talkative humor. Commenced
biting etching No. 37, biting & adding
more work alternately — Home at
1 — back at 2.30 & worked faithfully
until 6.20 — cleaned ground from
plate & worked for some time
with dry-point — Home soon after
6.30 — Emma & Mary J — to
dinner — I had intended to
go to Century Club in the evening
but felt out of sorts & stayed at
home — The girls left about 10
& we got to bed soon after 11.
