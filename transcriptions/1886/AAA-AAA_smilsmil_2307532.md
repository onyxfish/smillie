## Left Page

### July, Friday 9, 1886

Another delightful day — I did but little work on the Bridgman plate — laying ground etching & biting small marquise bit of sky. I made another effort to transfer Jules Breton Tracing, but spoiled tracing & ground — all the afternoon I spent drawing design for a bed room ceiling & superintending painting [of] wall canvas on attic stair — the cow house was also painted — At 6.30 I went down town — tea soon after 7 — then a walk on piazza with Annie — beautiful moonlight — then reading from an hour & to bed about 10. Thunderstorm during the night.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 10, 1886

Very heavy rain early in the m'f. Cloudy & cool until near 1, then cleared to be a charming day — I got to my printing soon after 1.15 — it was all the m'f. busily engaged at it. Was only fairly successful & got two clean proofs each from the Bridgman & the Johnson. The B. discourages me as it still requires so much work — At 4.30 Annie, Jim & I went out riding over Butterfield's hill & had a very enjoyable time. Back at 6.30 — Glorious moonlight nights — Read until 10 — then to bed.
