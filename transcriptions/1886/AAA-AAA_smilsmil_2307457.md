## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 9, 1886.

Cloudless & quite warm — got to studio before 10 — found that Van Elten had been there to see me. I spent the mtf. on Charcoal sku "on the Saco. Maine" 18 × 30 — Mr. M. S. Beach called — wanted me to fake Mr. Bertie Beecher as a foundel — turned her over to Geo. H.W. Beecher came with her in the afternoon. Home at 1.30 — Annie at Miss Parloa's. Back at 2.30 — Mr. McKnight called with Mr. Scully of Pittsburgh, Pa. — I worked awhile on Charcoal — then got started on a drawing of arms for Uncle Willie's Canada Co. At 6 home to dinner — Edith Wright came in about 7 & with Mollie Adams went to Thomas Concert — Annie read for half an hour Adam Bede, finishing it. Then I wrote notes to Van Elten, J.S. Courtright & D. S. Shawman. At 10 Edith came in & I took Mollie home. Wrote a note to Miss Avery & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 10, 1886.

Dull & threatening — To studio at 9.30 — Part of mtf. spent on Saco Sketch — then to work on Uncle W's vigt. Annie & Edith came in for a while — at 12 went to 71 W. 54th St. Stedman's house — & saw Homer's Water Color — "A Voice from the cliff" — It disappointed me — my memory had idealized it — then to Mr. Billings — 279 Madison Ave — & saw Bridgman's "Tents of the Nomads." I don't think I can make a satisfactory etching of it — then home to lunch — to studio at 2.15, worked on Uncle W's vigt & stretched some R. S. Proofs — Home at 6.30 — Chase there to have me sign the 2d Carghill lease — It was after 7 when I got around to Mother Cook's to dinner — Wef Edwd. & Walter & wives came in making quite a family party. Home about 10.30 & right to bed.
