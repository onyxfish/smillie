## Left Page

### March, Friday 19, 1886

Overcast — a clap of thunder — a flash of lightening, some rain &
hail, between 4 & 5 o'clk. p.m. —
at night, a wet mizzle. To Studio
at 9.30 — Some time fussing with
pf. Falcon yesterday — then set pal-
ette & commenced painting on Mr.
Geo. Whitney's painting, 20×24 —
Also went to Bank — met Gifford
& walked up to 38th S. with him
— lunch — back at 2.30 — painted
Annie went to Brooklyn and
made a call on Mr. Coneway —
Home to dinner at 6.30 —
Sw'f. went to 71 W 54th S. Saw
Miss Giles & arranged to send
for the Homer picture on Monday.
Home at 9 — wrote notes to Stedman
& S. Martin & Grady & McKeever.
To bed soon after
Will called at Studio in the
mf. wants me to design
a seal or arms for Union League
Club of this city.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 20, 1886

Overcast, but no rain until the
eve'g. Got to studio at 9.30 and
to work painting on Mr. Whitney's
20×24 — at which I continued
all day getting it into such con-
dition that I will put it aside
for a drying spell — I got down the
frame that Mr. Whitney sent to me
years ago — this a shocking affair
& I will have to get it made
over before I can consent to let
my painting be seen in it.
Sw'f. home at about 6 —
Geo. Cook & his wife & Ed. & his
wife to dinner — the evening
passed pleasantly until they
left (in a down pouring rain)
a little before 11 o'clk — we got
to bed about 11.30.
