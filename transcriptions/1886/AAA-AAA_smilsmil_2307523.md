## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 23, 1886.

A raw, wet day- wind from N.E.
Cooly came & commenced the brick
work under the main room hearth
& after dinner got it safely shored
up & finished, much to my relief.
I finished the second etching on
the Birdqueau & put in the
countryman's figure on the Johnson
plate — I also helped Herrick
tack up some sized duck in the
2° story hall — About 6 went down
town — tea at 7 — I was off my
feed & went without — Duff
wrote notes to Dr. LaRoche and
C.P. Ball. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 24, 1886.

Damp & overcast — at times in the
afternoon quite bright. Carey came
with the door step & sill early in the
m'f. A used man came & chopped
down two beech trees back of the
house — Herrick worked stretching
muslin for ceilings & other
jobs — in the afternoon Cooly, the
mason, worked awhile a setting
the door sill under studio's bay
window — I put in a good day's
work on the Johnson plate, draw-
ing in the figure of the squire &
some of the background. Neff
after a walk on piazza with
Annie, I wrote notes to Winslow
Homer & Grady & McKeever.
To bed at about 10.
