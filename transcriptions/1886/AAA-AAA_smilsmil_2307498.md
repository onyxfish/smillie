## Left Page

### May, Sunday 2, 1886

Beautiful day — nearly noon before I started out for a walk —
went up to Central Park & saun-
tered about there until near 2
o'clk — then home to dinner —
from 4 to 6 I took a nap —
then went out with Jim for a
walk — went to Bryant Park at
42? S. & 6th Ave — then to Morton
Cook's & home at 6.30 & had tea.
Eve'g. Ed W. Cook came in
& spent the eve'g with us until
near 10 — I wrote to J. D. Payne
& to Hiram Sibley & Co. Rochester —
ordering some hardy vines — then
after 11, wrote my letter to Morton
& took it to G. C. D. — To bed just
at midnight.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 3, 1886

Pleasant day — 10 o'clk. before I got
to studio — feeling very much used
up. I did very little work — R.
Swain Gifford called & got [illegible] of
my Passim Etching. Before I went
to Buk. & got some money for [illegible]
then to Station T. in 3rd Ave. with
a money order from Plainfield for
$10 — payment for [illegible] of "Rough Start."
Got postal note of $4 — for H. Sibley &
Co — then home to lunch — back to
studio before 3 — at 4.30 walked
down to 8th S. — Bible House — to Mr.
Prague — architect — & saw plans of a
studio bldg to be put up at B'way
& 44th S. — Home at 6.30 feeling very
much used up — dinner — Eve'g
lounged about until near 10 —
then to bed.
