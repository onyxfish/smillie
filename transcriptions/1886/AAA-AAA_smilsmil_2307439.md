## Left Page

### January, Monday 4, 1886.

Dark & dismal day. To studio
before 10 & soon got to work at
proving Pas'ini etching — To
lunch, & back, as usual &
at 4.30 I had taken 16 jap.
paper pfs. but very few of them
were satisfactory as I cannot
fathom the deceptions & duplici-
ties of oil & lamp black — The
depressing part of it is that
after a day of very hard work
I am not sure that I have
learned anything — At 5 went
to A.H.A. to see Kirby — not in —
Heavy wind & rain — home —
Mary Ireland there — but she
left before 6 — there dinner —
Jim seems to be very well,
but not yet in all respects
quite himself.    Every. A —
& I read until 9.30 — then
our game & to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 5, 1886.

Clear mixed mf., clouding later in
the day — cool, clear night — Got up
still feeling the effects of a severe
cold or influenza — To studio before
10 & spent the mf. over my etching
pfs. — touching some of the defective ones
with india ink & mounting half a
dozen — Mr. de Meza called — at 1
went to Bank & made a deposit
for Jimmie — home at 1.30 — lunch.
At 2.30 went to Safe Deposit in 42d St.
hoping to see Mr. Kirby — not there — then
to studio at 3 — Spent the rest of the
afternoon — preparing pfs. from other
plates for mounting — at 5 went to
A.A. Ass'n. Saw Kirby & Sutton — both
much pleased with my plate —
gave me $125 — $25 more than I asked
on the price agreed upon — Delivered the
original painting — Home at 6.15. Mrs.
Satchell & daughter there — dinner —
at 8 — took them to Mother Cook's — They
left at 9.30 — Wm. Park & br. called
to enquire about Jim — Eve'g. spent
in reading & to bed about 10.
Jas. Gill of Springfield called in the afternoon.
