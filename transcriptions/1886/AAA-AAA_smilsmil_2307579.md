## Left Page

### October, Monday 11, 1886

The mild beautiful weather continues — Annie drove over to
D.S. & W. R.R. depot with Emma
leaving the house soon after —
I spent the a.m. with the men
getting stone for the new walls &
succeeded in getting a little more
work out of Pattus. Annie returned
about 1 & we had dinner. Again
out with the men until dusk —
Then down town trying to hurry
up new chimney matters — The
3000 brick were delivered to-day
by Raynesford. Eve'g writing
letters — Rec'd from Cassell & Co.
check for etchings after Bridgman
& Johnson together with expenses
$424.35 — To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 12, 1886

Beautiful weather until afternoon
when it clouded over & before 10 o'clk
a few drops of rain fell. About
with the men much of the day —
with Smith the painter in S.W. bed
room — Got draft from Bank and
sent it to Sibley & Co. for seeds —
Cut out top of a beech tree near
the house — dinner — again with
Smith the painter — then cut out
the tops of a yellow birch & farmed
in & the beech. Eve'g after
some reading & writing, Annie
& I had some card playing.
To bed very much stuffed up
with a cold.
