## Left Page

### March, Sunday 7, 1886

Very pleasant day — got up with a miserable head ache — I managed to keep up until after dinner, then gave up & spent the afternoon on the lounge — Geo., Jakey & Sheldon spent part of the afternoon with Annie, Kate & Jim. — I took some tea at 6.30 — Charl came in about 8 — just back from Cheyenne where he has established the Sauce Creek Cattle Co. — He stayed until near 10 — There I wrote a letter home — one to Mr. S. Stoner — to S. R. Koehler, & a note to Wm. Pate — Took letters to G. C. D. to mail at about 11.30 — then home & to bed about midnight [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### March, Monday 8, 1886

Disagreeable day — with some snow & rain in the afternoon — clear at night. Nearly 10 when I got to studio — Spent some time getting pho. of Father's Lion die into a couple of frames — one for Mother. Then to work & finished charcoal sketch of "Under the Butternuts." Home at 1 — lunch — At 2.30, to A.A.A. — Saw Sutton about the Bridgman picture to etch — To studio about 3 — worked on plate Rough Sport, rubbing down shadow under horses with stone — at 5 went to A.A.A. again — saw Sutton — great excitement over sale of Peach Blow Vase for $18,000 — then to Kneedler's — Home soon after 6 — dinner — Eve'g. at 8, after writing short note to Koehler, went with Annie and Kate up to Charl's, where Geo. & Jakey came later — we left at 10.15 — home & to bed at 11.30.
