## Left Page

### April, Thursday 8, 1886.

Raw & windy — part of the time bright. It was after 10 when I got to studio — Spent most of the day adding new work to 37' & biting it in — Home to lunch as usual & back at about 3 — Worked until 6 — After 6.30 when I got home to dinner — It was 8 o'clk. when I started for J. C. Nicoll's — 51 W. 10' S' — when as Chairman of the Auditing com— tonly member present I gave the accts a hasty looking over — It was 10.30 when I got home & 11 when I got to bed —
Annie seems to progress as favorably as possible —

## Right Page

### April, Friday 9, 1886.

Brilliant, beautiful day —
Got to studio about 10 — laid rebiting ground on 37' & "stopped out" — also laid a new ground on plate for Winslow Homer etching after some time spent getting my ground rollers in order — Got a ground that on trial did not cling — Just as I was starting home for lunch, Ehninger came in — lunch was hurried but I got to Phil. Rehearsal in time — After that went to the A.A.A. Private View of the French Impressionists — To me an idiotic display — incomprehensibly bad from every point of view that I can command. Home to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g I spent some time for Annie on Diet Kitchen affairs — Wm Park & wife of Montrose came in for a little while — I wrote a note to Mary & also took it to J. C. D. — To bed about 11.
Annie's progress continues favorable.
