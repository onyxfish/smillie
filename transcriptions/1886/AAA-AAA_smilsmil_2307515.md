## Left Page

### June, Saturday 5, 1886

*Southampton. L. I.*
*At Mrs. Moultons*

Up early again — Bkfst. at 7.30. Glorious day — Went to 3d Ave. and bought a hat — then to Station 7. for Annie — then to Butcher's — then a call on "Lib" — Home at 10 Mad Ave — then to Studio soon after 10 — worked awhile packing — then to Brooks Bros. & bought a pair of Pantaloons — again to studio until near 12 — then to D'da Rochi's in 54th S: Had to go over to 6th Ave. Elevated — another street car strike — a "general tie-up" — in effect pretty developed this morning. From St. Fletcher got my plates for lower jaw, set up with rubber. Home at 1.15 & had lunch. Willie came in about 2. I left Mother & Kate with him as they go to Polk on 3.30 Train — I went to 34th S: ferry & took 3.20 Train to Southampton getting there at 6.40 — At once to Mr. Moulton's when I found Annie & Jim burned red brown by the sun & winds — tea — ever, with Edwd Cook & family —
So bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 6, 1886

Brilliant, beautiful day — Bkfst. at 8 o'clk. After that went out among the hens & took a number of photos. Then to the old mill & a beauty near — Back to the house & with Annie & Jim went to the beach, a walk of more than a mile — had camera & tried shots of surf & two or three groups. Back & had dinner at — Soon after 2 Mary, Miss Williams, Mr. Moulton, Annie, Jim, Tom, Edna & I started out for a ride. Edwd C. driving — we had a delightful drive over the Shinnecock Hills to Canoe Place & then back by another road, getting in at 5 — Tea at 6 — While Annie was putting Jim to sleep I took a short walk — Then, with the others, in the parlor until 10 o'clk. Then to bed
