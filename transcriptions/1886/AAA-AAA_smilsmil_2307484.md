## Left Page

### April, Sunday 4, 1886

A nasty day — quite a snowfall during the night — cold & raw
m'f., snow again in the afternoon. Up late — B'kf. at 9.30 —
It was near 12 when I walked down to the Century — Annie followed
by car & joined me — Met Collins who walked up with us to cor. of
23° S. He went on, we went in to N.A. f's — Home to dinner at
2 — After that I wrote letters to Mother & a note to Whitely,
the copper plate man. About 6 went to studio & wet paper for
printing tomorrow — There to G. C. D. & posted Mother's letter —
Then home to tea before 7 —
Well f. Jine seemed quite out of sorts. Spent some time over
Father's portfolio, selecting proofs for bed. Geo. C, Miley & Mr. V —
came in bringing 3 or 4 rugs for inspection & advice — To bed
about 11.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 5, 1886

Cold, raw, nasty rain — Got to studio a few minutes after 10 & spent the m'f.
taking blp. & working on Etching
No. 37. It was 1.45 before I got home to lunch — back at 2.40 —
Took two p'f's of N°. 29 — Then cleaned up & retouched a p'f. of N°. 37 and
at 5.30 went down to Whiteley's with my new copper plates to have
the bevel refinished — Home at 6.30 — dinner — Well f. Annie &
I went around to Mother Cook's where we spent the evening —
Home ab't 0.30 — Still raining —
to bed.
