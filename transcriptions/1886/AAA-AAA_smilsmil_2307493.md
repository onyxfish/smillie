## Left Page

### April, Thursday 22, 1886

Another hot day - 83° - in the shade at 3 p.m. To studio before 10 & laid rebiting grounds on No. 37 & also on the Homer plate. Had some trouble with the latter — By the time I got the plates varnished it was time to go home to lunch — & 3 o'clk. when I got back — I worked until 5 "stopping out" & dragging some bits on the Homer where the ground was defective — Left at 5.15 — went to Newman's — then to Grady's & saw the Park etching & frame — Home at 6.15 dinner — eve'g wrote notes — To bed at 10.30 —

## Right Page

### April, Friday 23, 1886

A couple of degrees hotter than yesterday. Got to studio about 9.30 — Rebit on 37 & spent the rest etching new work upon a rebiting ground on the Homer plate. Home to lunch soon after 1 — back at 2.30 & until 5.30 biting & rebiting on the Homer — then to Newman's factory in 29th St. near 7th ave. to see some bell pulls — from there to Murray's — 6th ave. near 38th St. — on the same errand — Home at 6.40 — dinner — Eve'g Annie left her bed for the lounge in parlor — we were surprised by appearance of a strange cat — some time before we could get it out of the house. About 10 I went out for a glass of Vichy — To bed at 10.30.
