## Left Page

### Sept. Thursday, 29 1870.

Still another good day, clear, warm + delightful for work out of doors —
got to my place in Smith Beede's
potato patch at 9.15 — set palette
\+ found that I had not white enough
so I had to Return to the house for
it — 10 o'clk before I got to work — Then
Repainted Sky + went over everything —
of course I could get none of the delicious
\+ wonderful variety of color — it changed
too quick — there has been a great
change since day before yesterday
when I painted this in — Went to
Widow Hale's — got hot water + lunched
abt 30 — worked until 3.30 + called my
sketch finished — Home soon after 4 —
a walk down the road — back + washed
brushes — late dinner — Evening as
usual — I feeling heavy from my
dinner — to bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### Sept. Friday, 30 1870.

Mild, laden gray mtf, threatening
to rain at any moment — a little
before 9 o'clk — went out with pencils
\+ paper + got sketches of maple
trees, near the Sugar House + out on
Smith Beede's potato field. I
worked until near 12 o'clk — when
began to rain, slightly + softly —
I gave it up — on my way home,
cut some long blackberry shoots —
Dinner soon after 1 — after dinner
some time spent shelling peanuts
for Miss Newman to make candy —
then worked in the pencil at Black-
berry stalks until 5 o'clk — then
put on big boots + Rubber coat walked
for more than an hour in heavy
rain — then Tea — Evely steady
heavy rain — the usual chat —
Miss N. incensed because I would not
admit that her pie baking, etc, was five
times harder than my painting —
To bed soon after 10.
