## Left Page

### August Sunday, 28 1870.

Up early and out on the lake — Fox rising slowly — back to help cook bkfst. which was late, about 8.30 — while at bkfst. Smith Reed arr. from the valley with provisions \+ our mail — Rec'd with great rejoung Papers to last Thursday. \+ letters from Father, Kate, Matt, Charl, &c. — Mr. Hunt \+ Harmer \+ Mr. Gen'l. Cram who apologises handsomely. Wrote some during the mng., again during the P.M. and evening but did quite as much loafing as the day was miled and evidently preparing for Rain — just before t[illegible] Rowed up the lake \+ into the River with Belle, Mary, \+ Charlie [illegible] a service \+ Beecher sermon was Read in the tent at 11 a.m. Singing hymns in the eveng. Drizzled of Rain just before Rolling in at about 11.30 —

## Right Page

### August Monday, 29 1870.

Up early, waked by the Rain coming into my face — Early mng. Heavy Rain — bkfst. late — Paddled on the lake enjoying cloud effects. at 10, went with Smith \+ Orlando to the lower lake to start our shanty. Orlando went on for provisions. Smith \+ a hired man worked getting out logs — at 12.10 started for Upper lake — Effects on lower lake very grand — and on the camp — Got to camp at 1.30 — lunched \+ set to work on oil study of camp — Clouds broken \+ light variable — worked as long as I could see but could not finish it. After tea, in a superb, cloudy twilight, Rowed with Jennie, Belle, Mary \+ Florence. about 9 o'clk. a Rattling thunder storm which settled into a steady Rain — Rolled in about 11 o'clk — after Reading my news papers.
