## Left Page

### February Thursday, 17 1870.

Bright spring-like day, clouding up late in the P.M. with slight Rain about 8. P.M. Got to Studio at 9.15 — H.R. Newman called soon after — then Walter Shirlaw. It was after 10 o'clk before I could get fairly impressed in my Sky on "Twilight" — 33 × 44 — (11th day) Repainted it again, getting it much nearer right. Home to lunch at 1.40 back at 2.25 — worked until 5 o'clk — at 5.45 went to Snedicor's. Home at 7 o'clk — Rev. W. Cox of Lexington took Tea & stayed all night with us. At 8.30 went to Munroe's in 38th St — not in — then to Diven's — Maud sick — then to Ward's. 49th St — Miss Thomas out — Home at 9 — Sat and chatted with Father & W. C. & got to bed after 11.

## Right Page

### February Friday, 18 1870.

Warm drowing mud & Rain all day — Got to Studio at 9 o'clk — Before I got to work old Mr. Dunham called — then Mr. King Smieth. I painted on trees against the sky of "Twilight" 33 × 44 (12th day) getting in the tops of the large ones — by the Repainting of the sky I had obliterated my drawing and so have lost much of the spunk & energy of the form — at 12.30 France the Chromo man called — at 1.30 home to lunch — at 2.30 at Phil Rehearsal — about 4.30 back to studio — worked a little — Home to Tea at 6.30. Ever very heavy Rain & lightning — I worked a little with the pencil & got to bed about 10 o'clk.
