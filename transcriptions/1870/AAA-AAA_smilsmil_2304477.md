## Left Page

### April Friday, 8 1870.

Superb spring day — got to studio at 9 o'clk and soon commenced Repainting sky on "Adirondack Lake" 14×24 in. — (3d. day) for Pliny Smith — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back to Studio's at 2.15 — About 3 o'clk. the darkie procession celebrating the 15th amendment came along — an orderly, grotesque + to me, very interesting affair — I heard the glad shouts of a poor, down-trodden people who had just found their life — I worked on painting until near 5 — getting over sky + distant mts., tolerably well. — At 6, went to U.M.C.A. — to pay my dues, but could not — at 7 o'clk. — home — at 8 called on Annie Tiffany + Miss Goddard — + spent a very pleasant evening — making little drawings on pin cushions for them — There until 11 o'clk — then home and to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### April Saturday, 9 1870.

Another spring day — got to Studio at 9.10 — at about 9.40 sat for Angers as model for a nice man of the east, all right — got through about 11. when I set to work on "Adirondack Lake" 14×24. (4th day) painting new water + distant trees — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.40 — + painted until about 4 — then Kittell brought me a Carnation pink + until 6 I tried to paint it. Miss Covell + a friend called — also young Wells + Rogers + 3 friends — Home to tea at 7 — at 8.30 called on Mr. Hasken — 4th ave She sent word by her servant that she was entertaining friends at tea — I must excuse her — which I will do for all time — Then went to Fanny Kimball's + had a very pleasant call until 10.40. then home, washed up my brushes + got to bed at midnight.
