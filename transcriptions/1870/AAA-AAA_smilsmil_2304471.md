## Left Page

### March Sunday, 27 1870.

a Regular hurricane —
feeling under the weather
I did not go out of the house
but spent the day lolling
in my big chair —
Welf Charl Read aloud
an excellent sermon by H.
W. Beecher concerning a man
coming to himself — About
9 o'clk I commenced writing
again on letter for Emma
Ireland — My head is as
thick as a pudding and I
worked away abt until
1 o'clk when I went to bed

## Right Page

### March Monday, 28 1870.

Showery, Raining or threatening
all day — got to Studio's at 9.25
Kindled fire \+ got to work on
"Jake Mahkeenac" (6½ days)
worked thinly over all, especially
sky \+ distance — Under advice of
Jas. Hart worked on "Twilight"
Home to lunch abt 1.15 — back abt
2.20 — Did not do much during
the afternoon — Gen. Ward Nichols
\+ his br. in law called bet. 4 \+ 5 o'clk
after they left I set to work sweep-
ing — got through \+ home about
7.15 — Welf finished my
letter to Emma — If nothing
else it is long — To bed
about 10.15
