## Left Page

### Sept. Monday, 5 1870.

Cold, blustering, cloudy day —
but the air fresh and bracing —
Too cold to work out of doors — Fixed
our room with shawls for studio &
worked over sky & distance of study
from top of Rainbow Fall — About noon
Mr. Hunt & Mr. Harmer came from
E-town bringing us a present of
Claret, Peaches & grapes — They
dined with us, after dinner, with
Belle & Mary J — we all walked on
the hill back of the house — at about
4 o'clk. the Mrs's H. went home &
I worked awhile on my study.
Then, with some of the girls walked
down the Road to Potters — I came
about 7 — glorious moonlight
night — Several [illegible] again
walked down near to Potters —
To bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 6 1870.

Cold clear day — about 10, gave
up hopes of working out of doors,
so with Mary, Florence & Jenny &
[illegible] started for up Ausable River
just as we started met Mr. & Mrs.
Norman White & 2 boys of N.Y., going
to the lakes to camp — walked
a mile or so up the Road with
them, then turned in to the River
which we followed up with con-
siderable difficulty. The girls
did admirably — stopped for lunch
at 1.30 — disposed of a bottle of claret —
at 2.30 on again — & soon into the
gorge — the forms & effects very grand —
from Rock to Rock we kept on until
at 4.40 we reached the upper end
then took to the woods & soon found
the Road & out to the house at 6.40 —
Dinner late — Eve'g Fred Durand
came in — "Grab" played until
near 10 o'clk. — Got to bed
about 11 —
