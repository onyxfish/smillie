## Left Page

### July Friday, 29 1870

overcast and threatening. The Yarrow party got off for E-town about 8 \+ nearly geo. \+ I started on a tramp and climb. We climbed the ledge on Mt. spur east of our house, South of Smith Beede's. going right over the top of it and down the other sides, out upon the brook that comes out back of Smith B's. We saw nothing new, a fine view nt through the Ausable Gorge, a near view of the Giant Slide \+ nearer view of lots of gooseberries \+ Blueberries \+ two hard showers. Home at 12.30 — very wet. Bathed \+ changed. Dinner abt. 3.0 P.M. Worked a little on 2° Locomotive drawing for A.B.N.C. \+ took a nap. Well a walk down the Road with Geo. \+ chatting with Miss Walters until 10.30 then to bed.

## Right Page

### July Saturday, 30 1870

Still disagreeable — clouds low. Showers and air quite chilly. took a walk down the Road with Geo. back bet. 10 \+ 11 a.m. got interested in a book "the Countiss Gisela" from the German. Read until dinner time. After dinner, tried to work on my Locomotive drawing but made little progress. After tea, at 8 o'clk — Geo. \+ I started down the valley to Dibble's, our P.O. — said to be about 3 miles — got a letter from Continental B.N. Note Co. asking me to make an mo[ney] for them. Geo. got letter from Charl \+ got bundle of papers. Our walk back very charming — got in at 10 o'clk. and to bed at 11.
