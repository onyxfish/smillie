## Left Page

### February Monday, 7 1870

Splendid, cloudless day. Got to Studio at 9.15 — but with tinkering at a broken stove-grate \+ Receiving the calls of Mr. Yang of Cal. \+ others it was after 11 before I got my fire underway. For a while continued on Mr. Geo. Whitney's subject, at 12 went to the barbers \+ had hair cut \+ had shampood. — Home to lunch at 1.45 — back to Studio at 2.45 after meeting Mary Butter and walking 3 or 4 blocks with her. Out of all sorts for work, but pegged at continuing — about 4.30 Fanny Kimball her Cousin — and Rosalie Goodeman \+ Mr. Cheeny called — at 5 went to Na. of D. at 5.20 up to Gro's — then to my own Room \+ at 6.25 home to Tea — Well making alteration in drawing for Father \+ writing letters and to bed at about 10.30

## Right Page

### February Tuesday, 8 1870

Blinding, driving, sleet \+ snow. Got to Studio at 9.30. Room full of gas, ashes \+ soot, blown down the chimney \+ through the stove. Could not manage it so that the Room would be habitable, so about 11 got to work in Fittell's Room on 10½×20 Mr. Lafayette from the north." The crest of the mt. is covered with snow \+ the time of day is late afternoon. By working like a good fellow \+ stopping but a short time for my lunch. I got all painted in by 4.30 P.M. — Home to Tea at 5.30 — Niatt up at Uncle Aaron's. Eve — wrote a note to Pate \+ Read a little getting to bed soon after 10 —
