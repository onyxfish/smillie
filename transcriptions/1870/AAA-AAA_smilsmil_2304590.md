## Left Page

### December Tuesday, 6 1870

Cloudy \+ Raw \- got to Studio at 9.20 \+ to work with graver on die \- (11/2 days) worked until 1.15 o'clk \- then home to lunch \- feeling very much under the weather with a cold \+ a small boil on lift cheek \- Back at 2.30 \+ worked until 4 \- Stretched myself on the lounge, but Fairman came in \+ stayed until 5 \- then again I lay down until 6 \- then home to tea \- Went to N.A. of D. at 7 o'clk to hear Dr. Rimmer's first lecture for the Season \- Room half full \- the lecture on the skull, especially facial development \- Tracing it from the monkey up \- with Constant illustrating on the board \- out to 9 \- Home at 9.30 and to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### December Wednesday, 7 1870

My mild \- at noon cloudless \- P.M. dull grey \- at Q P.M. Rain \- Got to studio about 10.30 \- after having been down-town to A.B.N. Co's, where Greey gave me a pf. of my die \+ Wm laid a Rebiting ground in it for me \- The pf. altho' not a good one, looks better than I had expected \- Spent remainder of mtf., stopping out etc, etc \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.15 \- afternoon Rebiting \- about dusk Emma Ireland \+ Fanny Kimball came in \+ made a short call \- Home to tea at 6 \- Well, suffering from dyspepsia \+ boil on my face \- so did not feel able to work on drawing of Alabama Arms \- Read Gray's Anatomy and wrote to Uncle Millie instead \+ got to bed about eleven o'clk
