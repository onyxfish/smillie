## Left Page

### January, Friday 14, 1870

Disagreeable. Raw & overcast. Got to studio at 8.30. Father with me to see my drawing. I washed off my Mont Mt. picture "Sifting of the Clouds" 38×44 in. & set to work upon it, scumbling & glazing over the whole of it. I worked as hard as I could until near 2 o'clk at the sky & distant Mts. Then home to lunch & back at 2.30 worked as long as I could. Meta Adams came in just at dusk & stayed until 6. Then home to tea — at 9 went to Reception given to Artists, Authors etc., by Mr. W. T. Blodgett, No. 182 Fifth Ave. — a very brilliant affair & exceedingly pleasant — miserable, sleety night — Home at 2 and to bed —

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 15, 1870

The worst sort of a day. The streets covered with a thin shade of ice, & rain falling & freezing. Got to Studio at 9 — dark, but pushed on with "Mont Mt" working over the foreground, getting out black spots, reducing crude lights & securing more harmony, breadth & color. I think it 100 per cent. better. Lunched in Studio at 2.30. Bayard Taylor made me a short call and towards dusk Dr. F.W. Lewis surprised me, on his way to Boston. His friend Mr. Perkins was with him part of the time. Home to tea at 6.30. Evening feeling tired, read a little & got to bed about 10 o'clk.
