## Left Page

### March Monday, 21 1870

Muggy, threatening m'f. clearing
during the P.M. — Got to Studio at
9.15 — Kindled fire & set to work
on "Twilight" (22d day) glazed
& scumbled about sky until 11.30
when I went to A.B.N. Co. by app't.
Lunch at Parker's at 12.30. Called
at Menges's to see my old painting —
"Haines' fall" — Elaborately bad — then
on Harley, wood engraver & at 2 o'clk
back to Studio — Painted until
near 5 o'clk, going over distance
& Rh. Summoned on the jury —
at 6.30, called on Many & saw Tom
Shearman. — Home to tea at 7. —
Every feeling used up, did
little else than lie on the sofa
and to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### March Tuesday, 22 1870

A spring-like sort of day. Rather mixed.
clear & cloudy by turns, but nothing
long. Walked to Studio with father,
got there at 8.50 — He sat for Augers
to re-touch & finish his portrait. I
touched p.f. of his die & worked on
"Afternoon — Adirondacks" 7×12.
Scumbling & glazing. — Mr. & Mrs.
Colton of Orange, called — also Mr.
S.M. Rutherford, with Album sketches.
Home to lunch at 2 — back at 2.50,
worked most of P.M. in Album sketches
that I gave to Mr. S. a year or two
ago — made them better. — Home
to tea at 7 — after tea at 8.15
with Gel. went to Emma Ireland's.
Had a pleasant visit until
11 o'clk — Home & to bed at
12.15
