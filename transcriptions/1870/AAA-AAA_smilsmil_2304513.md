## Left Page

### June Thursday, 23 1870.

Warmer than yesterday but still charming. Myself wrote to Uncle Willie — + left drawing at Mr. Hinshelwoods — at 9.30 got to studio — made slight sketch for locomotive drawing — then went to Selleck's — G. P. Putnam's + Geo's — to Mr. Buckhout Harlem R.R. office — then to shops — 4th ave + 43d St. to see Mr. Strong, Master mechanic — saw the foreman + got information I wanted. Home to lunch at 2.15 o'clk — at 3.30 went to Snedicor's with a painting of Ferguson's — then to A.B.W. Co. to arrange plans for Rail Road drawing — Drew bal. of my acct $65.00 — Home to tea at 6 — Eveng. very warm with pleasant breeze — Had not courage to work so sat in big chair until near 10 o'clk — got to bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### June Friday, 24 1870.

Exceedingly hot day — Myself went to Harlem R.R. machine shops + got a sketch of Engine smoke stack — then to Bogardus's + left photo of Engine to be dagtyped, then to studio about 10.30 — Varnished + glazed + worked a little over "Lifting of the Clouds" — "Indian Pass" and "Lake Placid" — Home abt 6 o'clk — lunch — then a wash — a nap in my chair — lounging and at 4. P.M. Gilbert Burling called for me + we drove to his home in Spuyten Duyvol — getting there about 6 — dinner soon after — then with his sisters, croquet. Some music on piano + out on piazza until after 11 o'clk — The Burling family all very pleasant — To bed about 11.30 — night very warm and sleep only middling —
