## Left Page

### Sept. Saturday, 17 1870.

Dismal morning — so I worked in doors on small water color that
I worked upon yesterday, making
only a poor, elaborate, weak thing
out of it, but still, having faith enough
in the subject to think of making
a large water color of it for our W.C.
exhibition next fault. Afternoon went out
for a walk & was caught in a sprinkle.
After dinner, set palette & worked
at the front door on study looking
up the valley to the north — painted
over the middle ground trees getting
some of the black & brown out of them
but leaving them far enough from
Right — weather still very mixed —
Between 5 & 7 another walk —
Evelf letters from father, Mary &
Matt — Reading newspapers
until 11 o'clk — an owl came
into the house, made prisoner —
then to bed —

## Right Page

### Sept. Sunday, 18 1870.

Very heavy unpromising morning
after & before we had breakfast —
mind soon got around to the
north and by 10 o'clk. it began
to break away and by noon it
was quite charming — Spent
most of the mf writing to Uncle
Millie and a long letter to
S.C.F. About 12.30 geo. and I
took a leg-stretcher down the
road & back to lunch at 1.30
Afternoon, again writing — finished
letter to S.C.F. and at 4 o'clk. with
gpo. walked down to Pettis's — then
to Dibble's — on way back stopped
at Washburns & saw Myant, back
to Dinner at 6.30 — Evelf wrote
to father — Fred D. called — all
arrangements made to go into
camp to-morrow mf. To bed
at 10.30
