## Left Page

### March Friday, 11 1870

Delightful day \- Got to Studio at 9.30, after a talk in confidence with Fergus on, got to work about 10.30 on "Lake Mahkeenac" (6th day) worked over foreground until 12.30 \- then went to G. P. Putnams \- with him, taking my Bryant Etchings some time \- he then took me to the Union League club \+ through the building \- Home to lunch at 2 \- back to Studio at 2.50 \+painted until 5.40. Had a pleasant call from Emma Ireland at 4.30 \- Home to Tea at 7.45 after calling for about ½ an hour on Mrs. D. R. Brevoort \- Every doing a little of nothing and got to bed at but 11.15 after suffering considerably with Neuralgia from the tooth that Fred Durand filled a few days ago.

## Right Page

### March Saturday, 12 1870

miserable cold day ending at night in the worst sort of a sleety storm \- Got to Studio at 9.20 \- Room filled with gas from a stove so, worked under great difficulties. During m'f, painted for Mrs Beers on the base of a cross that she is painting the words "nothing but leaves \- got started on a little drawing for the Am Bk Note Co. \- Som great western R. R. Depot. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.30 and worked at my drawing as long as I could see, getting it finished after a fashion \- W. Walter Brown called \- Home to Tea a few minutes after 6. Every suffering from nervous headache Read newspapers until near 10 o'clk \- To bed about 10.30
