## Left Page

### August Thursday, 18 1870.

Beautiful day, breezy & warm.
About 9 o'clk. went up to study
of Hemlock & Beech — my head so
uncomfortable that after getting
ready to go to work I stretched
myself on a log and slept until
after 10 o'clk — then worked until
near 1 o'clk — then to dinner & found
W. A. B. Durand, Miss Caine & Fred —
They stayed to dinner & then went
to Smith Beedes to stay for a week —
Back to old Hemlocks at 2.30 —
Again went to sleep and slept
until near 4 o'clk — hope that I
have got to my sleep level again —
worked until 5.30 — home to tea
at 6 — every about nothing in
particular and to bed at
10.30

## Right Page

### August Friday, 19 1870.

Very warm clear day — All got off for
the Giant at about 8.30 — I got to work
on my study of 2° fall at 10 o'clk & 
worked faithfully until 1 when I
lunched. Did not get all the canvas
covered. At about 2.30 or 3 o'clk, we
all started through the woods at
the top of the fall for the cliff over-
looking Chappel Pond — After
great tearing and toiling, the
ladies suffering particularly from
heat and thirst, we got to the cliff
at about 4 o'clk — views up & down,
superb & all seemed delighted. Near
5, back through the woods in double-
quick time all mad for water — When
we got to the brook, wet hands & heads &
cooled off generally — At 5.45, for
home & in at near 7 — a wash up
& late dinner — every body jolly —
Dr. Bellows & daughter, from Keene, while
we were away — wanted to stay all night
but no room — Superb aurora —
to bed at 10.30
