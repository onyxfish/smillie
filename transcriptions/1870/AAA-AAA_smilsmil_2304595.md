## Left Page

### December Sunday, 18 1870

Cold, windy tearing squalls during the mg. — Enjoyed my bath \+ rub-down with hair mittens — then bkfst. — Went to St Hastings \+ heard Dr. Asa D. Smith — Pres. of Dartmouth College — His text no. 14th v of 92d. Psalm. "They shall still bring forth fruit in their old age". \+ the object of his discourse to point out such a way of Christian living as would give a fresh, ripe \+ fruitful old age — After service went to Uncle A's to dinner — The P.M. very windy — but clear \+ glorious — Out walking about after dinner — Home to tea at 6 — Eve. chatting \+ reading until after 9 — To bed about ten o'clk. —

## Right Page

### December Monday, 19 1870

milder \+ hazy, threatening snow. Got to studio at 9.25 — Kindled fire — Recd note from Mrs. Mr. Mulford asking me to get her a Rogers group \+ [illegible] matte to her — worked on drawing of Locomotive No. 2. for A.B. N. C. — at 1 went to H.A. of D. to leave pkg for Miss Richardson — then home to lunch back at 2.30 — Dr Kingsley \+ a Mr. Cartwright of London called — worked on drawing as long as I could see — at 4 o'clk — then to Coffee's — Brooks, \+ to Magrath's Studio — found him \+ interested in his Water-colors. Back to Studio about 5.15 — fixed fire \+ home to tea at 6 — at 8 — Charl \+ I went to 550 Fifth Ave. \+ called on Mr. \+ Mrs. A.B. Marks — there until 10 o'clk — Home in a miserable slushy storm of slety Rain \+ to bed at 11. —
