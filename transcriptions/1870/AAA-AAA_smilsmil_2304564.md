## Left Page

### October Wednesday, 5 1870

Miserable, overcast day, at times trying to rain but succeeding in
nothing positively, but being dis-
agreeable. Soon after bkfst. went
over into the Ravine to get Georges
traps for him. He complains of
dyspepsia. Much interested in
watching water forms & color. Ret'd
to the house, got pencils & pafs &
+ went to cliff overlooking the fall
+ worked until near 4 o'clk, trying
to get an idea of the tumbling water —
Found it cold dreary work. At din'r
had some exquisite broiled partrid[ge]
P.M. very stupid + did nothing but
sit around or lie on the bed — Just
chilly + unpleasant from after [noon]
this a.m. — Well better. Read until
near 9 o'clk — then worked on pen
+ ink study until near 11 — To b[ed]
soon after —

## Right Page

### October Thursday, 6 1870

Another miserable day — clouds, mist
+ mizzle down to the very ground during
the m'g. Towards noon it began to lift
and by 10 P.M. it was struggling moonlight.
After bkfst. (more Partridge) I went to the saw
mill + about there for a while, then back +
spent the rest of a.m. making detail
study of a common weed hereabouts known
as whitetop — before dinner a short run
down the road, three more Partridge yet —
Alma, Maria + Oscar, got away between
10 & 11 o'clk. Mr. O. going home — afternoon
spent getting detail, water color study
of a lavender colored daisy that grows
everywhere about here. Before tea,
chopped a little wood for exercise —
Evening spent working with pen
and ink over some of my sketches —
got to bed about 10.15
