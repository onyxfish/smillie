## Left Page

### December Sunday, 4 1870

Still wonderfulley beautiful weather \- Mrs went to Dr Hastings + heard him preach from Galatians 4th Ch, 19th V. After service, home, saw Mary + her son for the first time since her Confinement \- Then, with Chard, went to Uncle G's to dinner, Bob Hall there also \- After dinner Susie, Chard + I amused ourselves out of doors firing heaps of dead leaves, etc \- The p.m. color, charming \- At 5. started home with Bob Hall, in at 6 \- Tea \- Swell went to Thompson's Heard an old Missionary. Coam, from Sandwich Islands \- been there 35 years \- In spite of the richness of his subject and material, he did not prove very interesting \- Home at 9.15 \= Read until near 11 \- Then to bed \-

## Right Page

### December Monday, 5 1870

Mild, grey day, threatening change \- got to studio at 9 \- Kindled fire \+ to work on die with graver (10 1/2 days) Home to lunch at 1.15 \- found there aunt Jessie + Miss Parker \- Back to studio at 2 \- worked until 4.15 \- then Mary + Belle Ineedy came in \- Chatted with them until 5 \- then walked down home with them, stopping in at Knoedler's \- Home to tea at 6 o'clk \- Dressed and at 8 o'clk. went by invitation to S. Pavery's where Miss Mary Whitney is visiting \- a few young people \- very agreeable evening until 10.45 when I begged off \- Home at 11.10 \+ to bed at 11.40
