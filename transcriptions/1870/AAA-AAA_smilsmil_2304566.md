## Left Page

### October Sunday, 9 1870

Up about 3 o'clk. a.m. — to to the bidding of my base internals — Up for all day about 6.30 — Find myself quite the victim of my abominable or, abdominal viscera which seems to be in wonderful running order — The day very glorious, but sat quietly about the house — About 11 o'clk our mail was brought in to us, letters from Father, Matt & Mary Tweedy to go and papers to Thursday — Reading them until dinner time, when I was n— meditating in the little summer house near the pig pen — After dinner — how so delicious out of doors that taking my blanket & a chair, I sat & enjoyed the view up the valley until the sun moved behind the hill — The nights glorious to match. We's wrote a letter to Ferguson & did some Reading. Got to bed at nearly —

## Right Page

### October Monday, 10 1870

A warming day after all, but with many signs of change — Still I am a good deal used up with bowel trouble — About 9 o'clk went to Smith Beede's potato patch, beyond widow Hales & there worked all this [illegible] with pencil getting outlines of some of the tree forms — My last work here for the season — Back to dinner about 1 o'clk — The P.M. spent reading & quietly getting things together to pack up — Tea about 6 — until 9.30 packing — then, as I felt far from well gave up, altho not finished. To bed about 10.30.
