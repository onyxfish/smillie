## Left Page

### March Saturday, 19 1870

Delightful, spring-like day — Got
to studio at 9.10 — Hanging pictures
& fixing about until 10.30, then to
work on "afternoon among the Adi-
rondacks" 7×12 — (2° day) — Got
over sky & distance by 10 o'clk —
Meuger called & Dr. Yale. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.15
During the P.M. several strangers
called — I worked until about
4.30 o'clk — getting over my painting
my large frame & picture from
Utica", "The Lifting of the Clouds"
came up about 5 o'clk — Set
to work — got my "Twilight" into it —
& it up on legs — Find my Twilight
very low in tone — am uneasy
now for fear that I will not get
it properly finished for the [illegible] —
Home at 7.10 — Tea — washed & mother
& spent the evef in talk — at
home — about 10.30 got writing for Emma
Ireland & kept at it until 12.30 —
Got to bed about 1 —

## Right Page

### March Sunday, 20 1870

Mild day — pleasant — evef threatening
rain — Mrs went to Dr. Hastings and
might have heard him preach a very
excellent sermon, but I was very sleepy
His text was "Work out yr own Salvation
with fear & trembling: for it is God which
worketh in you both to will & to do of his
good pleasure." Phillipians II. d 12-13 v —
After service went to Uncle Ca's to
dinner — After dinner a little con-
versation with Aunt H — not of tenderest
description — Started for home about
4.30 — walked most of the way, getting
in about 6.15 —
Evef, part of it finishing letter
to Emma Ireland — for her sea
voyage — Mother under the weather
today —
To bed about 11.30
