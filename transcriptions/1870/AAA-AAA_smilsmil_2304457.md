## Left Page

### February Sunday, 27 1870.

Raw, overcast day \- Mrs went to Dr. Hastings. He preached from John 17 Ch. 3 v., I think \- I do not exactly Remember the text "That they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ" \- At any Rate, knowing God, was the subject of his discourse \- After service Charl \+ I went up to Uncle a's \- Spittings of Rain \+ snow during the P.M. \- left there soon after 4 \- heavy Rain just as we Reached home \- Evelf. Malt, Kate, Charl \+ I went to Dr. Hastings \- Cold, Sleety Rain, walks covered with ice \- Young Men's ass'n of Church \- addresses by P. Harwood Vernon \- Dr Hastings and the celebrated Dr Jno. Hall, on Christian Work \- a thin house \- exercises interesting but Rather languid \- Home at 9.30 and to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### February Monday, 28 1870.

morning started with a show of a snow storm, but got to be very sloppy \+ drizzly before night \- Got to studio at 9.20 \- Kindled fire \+ went to George's Room on a message for him, as he does not go out to-day. Called on Fisk \+ on Louis Tiffany \- Got some French Canvas from Louis \+ paid him. Then to Knoedler's \+ at 11 back to studio getting to work on "Twilight" (18th day) about 11.30 \- Worked on bushes and rocks in middle \+ to R.N. of foreground. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.15 \+ worked until 5 o'clk \- At 6 went to Laura Hearn's \- 110 W. 12th \+ left my tin case \+ painting \- Home to Tea about 7 o'clk \- After Tea had the fun of clearing snow off sidewalk \- spending the Rest of the evg between Reading \+ Chatting \- getting to bed about 11 [illegible — ~3 words]
