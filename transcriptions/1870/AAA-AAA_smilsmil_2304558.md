## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 23 1870

mrf, overcast \+ threatening — the mrf, gul too much indisposed to appear at bkft. Som after 9 Apl. \+ I went to top of "Giants Leap" — worked on study of the 2d. fall — but about noon it began a small drizzle — took cold lunch at 1. \+ then packed \+ home — Mrs. Guit. had Recovered sufficiently to appear immediately after we left. \+ the par had gone on to Placid — W. \+ Mr. Jonu \+ Miss Walters left for Champlain at about 2 — At 3 I had a warm dinner of broiled venison — Soft Rain, but not much — Read N[illegible] "Tribunes" until 5.30. then walk[illegible] on the Road until 6 — Tea abo[illegible] 6.30 — Eveng. working on pencil sketches that I have Taken m[illegible] the past few days and Then to bed about 10.45

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 24 1870

Heavily overcast and Rain, but not much — Day badly misspent. thought to work on Locomotive drawing but spent most of the mrf. Reading back numbers of Tribunes trying to get up mth the Franco-Prussian and Italian war news. Venison for bkft. and more for dinner — After dinner did no better in the way of work than during the mrf, but, chatted with Miss Joney — Read \+ worked a little with pencil over some sketches. About 4 o'clk, Rigged myself for went out into the wet woods enjoying immensely effects of col[illegible] — around by Smith Beede's — Saw the Durands — Home to tea about 6.30 — Eveng. chatting with Miss J. until after 9 o'clk, when the mail was brought in, a letter for me from Matt — one for[illegible] from Ferguson at Valparaiso, S.A. \+ lots of papers — pb to bed about 11 —
