## Left Page

### March Tuesday, 15 1870

Clear warm day — got to Studio at 8.50 and soon at work on "Mt. Lafayette — from near Bethlehem" 10½×18 — (2nd day) worked until 12.15 when I went to see Fred. Durand about my sore tooth — then home to lunch & at 2.55 back to Studio — worked until 5.30 getting over all but foreground but feeling depressed by an assurance that all that I am doing is very bad — questioning myself as to whether I have any real talent for painting and whether I was wise to abandon engraving — Home to tea at 7 — Mother went to Uncle A's to stay until Sat. This P.M. — Well. Reading until 10.30 to bed at about 11 —

## Right Page

### March Wednesday, 16 1870

Almost a hurricane during the night with much rain — got to studio at 8.55 — almost afraid to stay — wind like to take the roof off — gas and ashes driven out of the stove — but about 10.30 it abated & before noon it had cleared off beautifully — worked until 1 o'clk on 10×18 "Mt. Lafayette" (2½ days) fixed lunch — eat it & then commenced to repaint "Edmonds' Pond" 8×13 — that has been all mutter at Bogardus. Went over it all improving it very much — about 4 o'clk — Allie D[illegible] Annie Lowther & Josie Jessup called after them — Belle, Mary & Jenny [illegible] with two little nieces — they left at 5.20 Home to tea at 7 — Well. at 8.45 went to 17 E. 17th St. met there Gio. called on Jenny I. — Mr. & Mrs. Sam I. — Mr. D. Tiffany — Suffered from a headache — left at 10.20 — home and to bed at 11.15
