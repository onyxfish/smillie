## Left Page

### July, Saturday 23, 1870

Very warm day — worked pretty
steadily all day on locomotive
drawing. (9th day). Was awakened
bet. 5½ a.m. mth thunder storm —
gentle Rain until bet. 9 & 10. Then
cleared into a perfectly charming
day. Smith Biede came over to talk
camping matters mth me, worked
until after 12 — a little stretch —
Dinner from after 1 — a short nap.
Then again to work and kept at
it until about 5 — then went over
into the Ravine & back hunting for
Father's spectacles. No success.
Back at 6.30 and to tea — after
Tea, a pain — passing away in
an hour or so — a long talk
with Miss Walters — Mainly about
Mr. Gel[illegible] — to bed at 10.30
A letter Rec'd. from Mary &
a paper from Will.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 24, 1870

A very hot day — light breezes, but
dry & unrefreshing — Changed under
garments, blacked my boots, put
on a necktie, shaved — and Sunday
was observed. Spent most of mth
with Father under the shadow of
a great rock beside the River.
Back about noon — Lunch at
1.30 and the afternoon spent in
trying to do nothing and keep cool.
Spent a good part of the time on
the floor in the east door — very
breezy for a time. Bet. 4 & 5. p.m.
clouded over, and at night
blew quite hard — Got to
bed about 10 o'clk.
