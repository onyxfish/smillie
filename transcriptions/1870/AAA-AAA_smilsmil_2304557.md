## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 21 1870.

after sleeping soundly, wakened at 5 a.m. by Orlando fixing the fire, then asleep again until near 7 when I got up — bkfst. & off to my work at top & fell a little before 9 o'clk. Started small sketch in oil & some Arbor Vitae trunks — Gel & Miss Walters came about 10, & the rest, Orlando, boys & all about noon — Had a tip top lunch at 1 — I worked on sketch until about 3 — then done — all day fighting at headache — then pencil sketches — about 5 back to camp — all gone — Gel & I got Farge the wood for our fire — washed brushes — got our simple meal — gazed at the fire for some time — went to see Shurtliffe & my aunt — back at 8.30 — built a Rousing fire, by which I see to write this and — at 10 o'clk. Rolled in for the night — Up at 6 o'clk — cloudless night — wind high & fire almost out — built up another Roarer and into my blankets again & slept soundly until 6 o'clk am, — when

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 22 1870.

my aunt woke me with an invitation to breakfast with him & Shurtliffe. Up — enjoyed my wash in the cool brook — & went to bkfst. — venison — flap-jacks etc. — "ye gods & little fishes!" Packed & ready to start a little before 9, when Alfred Jones suddenly appeared. He & wife arrived at Beede's yesterday — I soon started down the road — met Genl. Crane & daughter — then the Mr. Gent on the buckboard closely veiled. Pencil sketch from old Hemlock — at 10 got to work in oil sketch in the woods which I finished about 2.30 — packed again went on until I found water — then stopped, built a fire & at 3 had lunch & again & got pencil sketch of small Hemlock. Near 5 o'clk. the Genl. and daughter again overtook me — Home about 5.30 — Shaved, bathed & made careful toilette expecting to meet Mr. Genl. — my seat was next to hers at table — but she did not appear "prostrated" probably — went Fred & Sister called — all chatty & agreeable — the Crane party all in the Kitchen — except Mrs. Gent who kept her room. To bed at 10.30
