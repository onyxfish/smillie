## Left Page

### January, Sunday 30, 1870

Delightful day — Up + had some [illegible] — at 10.30 but so used up with a headache that I spent the Rest of the m[or]n until near 2 o'clk in my big chair, when I went to bed + stayed there until 6 — then up — Tea — in big chair again until bet[ween] 1 + 2 o'clk A.M. My headache today is of the peculiar class that I know as "Hopeful headaches" — Altho' painful — My mind is crowded with plans for the future, building houses or Studios or planning big pictures — I do not suffer the depression that marks other of my headaches, when to think on any subject is pain and I feel myself to be a more or less mortal — This peculiarity is nothing new — I have noticed it for years — Honeysuckle leaves coming —

## Right Page

### January, Monday 31, 1870

Dark, miserable, drizzly day — Got to studio at 9.5 — Kindled fire + got to work again upon the last or 3rd R.R. Bond drawing for the A.B.N. Co — making some changes upon it that gave — requires + intensifying the effect. Mr. Hart made a call — Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.40 after having called upon Burling. P.M. outlined awhile on 20×24 Mr. Geo. Whitneis picture + at 4 or soon after went to Knoedler's, then to see the Thompson collection of pictures at Seed's — on way back stopped to see the fat cattle in 18th St. — back to studio about 5.30. Home to Tea at 6.45 — Evening making up Water Color accts + compiling some statistics concerning our 3 exhibitions, to be used tomorrow night — got to bed about 12.
