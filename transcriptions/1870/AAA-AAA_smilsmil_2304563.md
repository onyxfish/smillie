## Left Page

### October Monday, 3 1870.

To-day, all that yesterday was not —
Rain commenced about 7 a.m —
Kept it up in Real equinoctial
style all day — Took our last [illegible]
with misses Torrey & Newman — After
that, Rigged for the weather I went
out & walked for an hour, bringing
in some bright Moose berry leaves
to make a water-color sketch, from
miss N, &go. on the Cram subject —
She desires me to burn Mr. C's last
letter — I Refused — at 11 o'clk — thus
got off — I worked on leaves until
lunch about 1 o'clk — after lunch
tried to work — Sleepy — So again I
went out, to the Saw Mill & away
into the woods — home, very wet but
feeling better — Read my papers
until tea time, bet. 6 & 7 — & so
every in same manner —
Rain falling when I went to bed
at a quarter before ten o'clk —

## Right Page

### October Tuesday, 4 1870.

Early m[illegible], very clear, but soon clouded
over after 8 o'clk — about 8.30 went
down the Road, below Potter's, & got
two sketches of Elm Trees in pencil —
in or three slight niggling showers —
Home to dinner about 2.30 — at 1.30
with pencils & paper went to meadow
beyond Smith Reed's and got a
sketch of large Elm Trees — about
2.30 quite a heavy shower followed
at short intervals by several — thus
I worked as well as I could between
the showers & went home about 4 —
worked a little in doors and at
dusk took a walk — back to tea
at 6 — Smilf, working in the pen
over one of the sketches I got this
m[illegible] — ab[illegible] — mail came — Read
my papers until 10 o'clk & got
to bed about 10.30 — G[illegible] — quite
dyspeptic — He went to bed soon
after 8 o'clk —
