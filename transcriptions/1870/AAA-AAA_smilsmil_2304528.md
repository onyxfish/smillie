## Left Page

### July Monday, 25 1870.

MrS clouded + for a time, early,
looked like Rain. Cleared to be
a delightful day. Breezy and
cool in the shade. During the
m't worked on Locomotive, now
the 10th day. Father at the River with
gel. Dinner just before 1. Worked
on drawing until 3. Called it done
\+ then went out with Father. We
crossed the hill back of us, came
out by Smith Reede's + climbed by
the brook + through the woods to the
top of Giant's Leap — Sunlight and
Cloud Shadows, glorious; — Climbed
down along side of the fall, coming out
at the foot. Father doing well and
delighted — Says he is amply paid
for coming to the mts. Back at 6.30.
Fyed Father off with some beef Tea and
whiskey — He seems quite frisky — [illegible]
fyed his things to be ready to get off
early to-morrow m't for home, via
Plattsburgh — To bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### July Tuesday, 26 1870.

Up at 4 o'clk. Cooked hominy, toasted
crackers + poached eggs for Father's
[illegible]. At 5.15 AM. Father, gel + I with
Charley to drive, went down to Dibble's.
Exquisite m't and Father very much
delighted. Dibble got away at 6+ we
[illegible] to B's. Between 8 + 9 got
traps in order + commenced
a watercolor study at the
mouth of the Ravine near the
house. gel + Miss Walters
there also. Very sleepy — took
a nap about noon. Dinner at
1 o'clk. + back again, completing
outline of drawing + tinting the
paper. Late afternoon, quite
overcast + looked very like
Rain. Swept, tired and
lonesome; got to bed just before
10 o'clk.
