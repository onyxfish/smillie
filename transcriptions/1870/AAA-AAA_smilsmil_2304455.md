## Left Page

### February Wednesday, 23 1870

Delightful day, being very much milder than yesterday — Got
to Studio at 9.25 — After some tidying, dusting, etc., to work
on "Twilight" (16th day) going over all the middle distance, which
is still wet & the trunks of the large trees — It certainly is
very low toned, but, I think, not black. I am striving to get it
strong & low, yet full of color.
Home to lunch at 1 — back
at 2 and worked until 5 o'clk
then cleaned up — fixed
myself in two chairs and
took a nap — Home at 7 —
Evef. Reading Miller's "Scie[nce]
of Language" — got to bed
about 10.30.

## Right Page

### February Thursday, 24 1870

Clear, but breezy & dirty — Mr.
Frank Scott called at the house
at 8.50 & we walked to the Studio
where he explained the changes
he desires me to make in a drawing
on wood. At 10 commenced painting
on little sketch 4×8 in., on tin for
Laura Holpsock-Hearn's tin wedding.
Got it done by 12.30 — I think it
will be pleasing — Home to lunch
abt — back at 2 — Painted bowl
of wooden spoon for Belle Tready
then made alterations on Scott's
block — Home to tea at 6.30 —
Evef. pottering with tin easel
for tin picture — made note to
T. Frank Reed — & Read a little
of Max Müller's Science of
Language — To bed soon
after 10.
