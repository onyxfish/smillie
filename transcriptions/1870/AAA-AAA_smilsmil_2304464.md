## Left Page

### March, Sunday 13, 1870

Great storm of wind & sleet all night long and pretty much all day long hardly less violent. Did not get up until about 10 o'clk and as my face is still troubles me did not go out of the house all day — Dinner between 1 & 2 o'clk. After that, a nap in my chair until near 4 — Tea at 6 — and during the eveng wrote part of a letter that I intend for Sinina Ireland's amusements on board ships — She expects to sail about the 10th of April with Annie Cost, for Germany. Got to bed about 11.30

## Right Page

### March, Monday 14, 1870

Mild Running day — Got to Studio at 9.25 — Kindled fire and at work on 7×12 in "a dream of the Adirondacks" (2d day) worked until 1.15. then home to lunch — back at 2.20 — at it until about 5.30 when I got it so near finished, after a fashion, that I hope to get through with it after an hour or two more of work — Home to tea at 6.45 — Well Talking with father until 9.30 — then figuring up accounts until 11.30 — To bed about midnight —
