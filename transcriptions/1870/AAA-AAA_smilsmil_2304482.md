## Left Page

### April, Monday, 18, 1870.

Tremendous Rain in gusts
\+ hurricanes \- went to Sloan's
to see about carpet. Stopped
in at Knoedlers \+ got to studio
at 10.30 \- Had a great time
Kindling my fire, Then
commenced Painting 9×12
Chappel Pond \- at 2.30
called at 3rd Regt Armory
Fair for Sheltering arms to
see Robbins \+ the pictures.
Then home to lunch \- back
to studio at 3 \- P.M. so dark
that I could do no work \-
Home to tea at 7 \- Avery
Reading Dickens' "Hard Times"
To bed at 11 \-

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday, 19, 1870.

Up with a miserable headache.
Day changeful \+ April\-like \-
got to studio at 9.10 and sat
sometime unable to go to work.
Fanny Kimball brought me a
bunch of Arbutus and at 11
I got to work on 9×12 that I
started yesterday \- Home to
lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.25
\+ worked until about 4, when
I finished the sketch \- Miss
Eliot called \- Soon after 4
went to N.A. of D, met here
Mr. Canfield, Dr. Bartlett and
Will Kimball. Back to studio
at 6 \+ washed up \- Home at
7 \- Evening did a little
Reading \+ writing but more
of nothing in particular
\+ got to bed about 10.30
