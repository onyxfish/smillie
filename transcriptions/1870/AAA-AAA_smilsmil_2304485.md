## Left Page

### April, Sunday, 24, 1870.

Beautiful day, must went to Rev. Thos. Hastings and heard him preach a very interesting discourse upon the text — 1st Cor. VI., \+19th. " Know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost?" He urged respect for the body, to keep it clean of defilement and make it a worthy temple. After service Charl \+ I went up to Uncle Aaron's — Mary the Dr. \+ girls all there to dinner. Spent the P.M. wandering about until after sundown \+ soon after 7 — started for home. The other fellows having gone ahead \+ stopped in to Engine House \+ had fire telegraph explained. Eve'f wrote notes to S.R.\+[illegible] and Jan M. and got to bed about 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### April, Monday, 25, 1870.

Bleak, disagreeable morning with Rain between 10 and 11 \+ about 2 it cleared charmingly. Got to studio at 8.50 — Kindled fire \+ set to work touching up an old die pf. on bristol board for A.B.N. Co — at 11.30 went down town — Saw Frothingham \+ the world. Mr. \+ Pliny Smith — took lunch with Father — then to Snedicor's — brought a lot of brushes then to Studio at 2.30 — worked on die pf. \+ touching up a pf. of my Ausable Lake plate until after 6 o'clk. — Home to tea at 7 — Fred Smillie at the house — at 8.30 with Geo. went up to the Diven's — made the call in back kitchen as they are all upside down with painters &c. — Home at 10 o'clk and to bed at 10.45.
