## Left Page

### February Wednesday, 9 1870

Superb day overhead, miserable of course, underfoot. Late P.M.
cloudy. Got to Studio at 9.15 and
as everything there was dust, soot
& ashes, I set to work house cleaning
at which I was engaged until
12 oclk. Then set palette &
commenced Repainting the
distance of "Twilight" (33 × 44
10th day). Home to lunch
at 1.15 back at 2.20 & worked
until 4.30 getting over distance
more cleaning up & home to
tea at 6 — Eve: George and I
went to Kimball's at 8.15 oclk
& had a very pleasant call
upon the family in general
& Miss Rosalie Goodman, their
guest. Left them about 10.30
walked home & to bed at
11.30

## Right Page

### February Thursday, 10 1870

A marchy sort of day. Blue sky,
flying clouds, wind & snow squalls.
Got to Studio at 9. 5. Soon after
Mr. Wm Pate called & I set to work
Repainting his picture 9 × 12 in.
"The Upper Ausable." Worked until
1 oclk. then home to lunch, after
leaving N.A. of D. card at Fanny
Kimball's — back from lunch at
2.25 & at painting until 4.30
when I got over it, after improving
it very much; though yet far from
what I wish — Went to N.A. of D.
to see Richards — not there — then
over to Y.M.C.A. Blde called
on Geo. Tiffany, Gifford & Mr. Hart.
Back to Studio — fixed up & home
at 6.45 — at 8.15 went with George
& called on Quincy Ward at his
new house No. 9 W. 49th St. Spent the
eve'g very pleasantly with his
niece Miss Thomas, & him — Saw
his Studio — did not see Mrs. W—
Home at 10.20 to bed abt 11 —
