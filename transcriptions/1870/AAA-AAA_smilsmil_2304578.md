## Left Page

### November Sunday, 6 1870.

Glorious day — B[illegible]. about 9 — went to Dr. Hastings — heard him discourse from 1st Peter 1st ch — 17th v. "Sojourning in fear" — enlarging upon the varieties of fear + app[ar]ent conflict in Bible References, injunctions or command — He dwelled upon our Relations to the Father, the perils + duties of this life and from a Realizing sense of these things, the inevitable Result, a filial, loving fear — After service went up to Uncle C's to dinner. Still suffering from cold in the head — wandered about until about 4.30 — then home to tea. We[illegible] at home alone + quiet — doing nothing, after a little Homoeopathic Reading in one of the Dr's books. To bed at 10 45

## Right Page

### November Monday, 7 1870.

Dull day — got to studio at 9.15 — lighted fire + got to work, working over little, single sitting studies that I did during the Summer. Varnished + Retouched four of them, sticking to it without stopping until 8.30 P.M. — when I finished my day's work \+ fixed + eat my lunch — Then made two or three calls in the building + entertained Wenzler until dusk — Home to tea at 6 o'clk — Evening worked until 10 o'clk, upon a Second Church outline by Charl — Then washed brushes and to bed at 10.30
