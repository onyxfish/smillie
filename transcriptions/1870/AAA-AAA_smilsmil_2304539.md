## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 16, 1870

Up at 5 o'clk. to bid Mr. Smeedy
good bye — he goes to Ausable Forks —
to bed again for about an hour, then
up. Nelp b? and about 8 o'clk. the
whole party started for the top of
"Giant's Leap", getting there som after
9 — Set to work on study of 2° fall,
from above, great trouble with the
light & position — worked until noon
then cooked a lunch for the party.
At about 3 o'clk. with Belle, Mary,
Florence, Kate & Charl, went away
up the stream, nearly a mile. I
wished to make a color study of
a bit of broken bank & rock that
I have a pencil sketch of, but the
past season has destroyed it — All
home at 6.30 — then dinner —
Evng in general. Talk and
to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 17, 1870

Warm day, but charming — Rkd?:
about 7.30, then feeling somewhat
tired from yesterday's tramp, I
determined to work near the house
— Abg o'clk took pack & went to
the Ravine Ridge & commenced
a study of tree trunks and ground
litter in the woods. My position
very uncomfortable & light bad, so
I worked under disadvantages.
Taking my traps a little farther
on to Hemlocks, left them there
& home to dinner at 1 — Mr. Tom
Smeedy — M. Chat. Smeedy & Doctor
Schuyler arr? from N.Y.. Miss
Walters's sisters & children up
on a visit — Back to my work at
2.30 — my head distressing me, so
that I took a nap on the ground.
Worked indifferently until 5.30
— then home to tea at 6 — Tea at
7 — Evng. Reading newspaper.
To bed at 10.30 — thrown off my
sleep & did not get it until after
midnight —
