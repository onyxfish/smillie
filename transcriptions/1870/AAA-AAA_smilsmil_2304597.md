## Left Page

### December, Thursday 22, 1870

Cold mntry day \- got to studio at 9.15 and spent an hour more blocking out mth char coal subject for large water color drawing \- then to work on locomotive drawing (3½ days) until 1 o'clk. \- Then home to dinner \- did not get back to my work until about 3 o'clk. \+ worked until 4.15 \- then went to Coffees to hear another of his lies \- He is a wonderful liar \- Then to Snedecors \- then to Sneedys \- then until 6.10 then home at 6.40 \- Feeling under the weather mth a cold, spent the even't at home Reading \+ working on problems in perspective until 11 o'clk \- Then put my feet in hot water \+ got to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 23, 1870

Snow during the mf., but not more than enough to make a mntry looking spread \- got to studio soon after 9 \- then to Coffees \- then to Snedecors, having Rich'd [illegible] mth check from Mrs. Mr. Mulford \- then to Astor Library, searching for costumes of Flemish Troopers 17th cent'y. Made the acquaintance of Mr. Fred. Saunders very pleasantly \- then to historical Library \- same errand \- no success \- then again to Coffees \+ carried casts of horses legs to studio \- home to lunch at 1.20 \- back at 2.30 \+ worked charcoaling subject for water color as long as I could see \- then to Bogardus's \- then home to tea at 6 \- Down to Sneedys at 7 \- went mth Mary \+ Belle to Steinway \+ heard R. W. Emmerson lecture before New England Soct'y. 250th ann'y Never heard him before \- a clumsy reader, but, of course, very interesting meetin mth the girls \- home at 11 \- and to bed at 11.30
