## Left Page

### January Monday, 24 1870

Still mild, overcast, threatening a storm — at night, Rain — got to Studio at 9.30 — Kindled fire then sweeping & clearing up until 11.30 when I sat down to Scott's block — worked until 1 — then home to lunch — back at 2.15 + worked as long as I could see (1½ day on block.) Home to Tea at 6 — Sueff Read until 8 o'clk — Then writing dunning letters to Water Color members until near 11 — got to bed about 11.30

## Right Page

### January Tuesday, 25 1870

Horrible day, down pouring Rain + very dark — got to Studio at 9.30 + again at work on wood drawing, over which I used up most of the day — About noon a model, giving her name as Henrietta Hine, came in and showed herself off — I lunched in the Studio — Rain pouring. Finished my drawing. It looks only so so — Charcoaled on canvas 14×24 — Sak Mahkena on Stockbridge Bowl — Home to Tea at 6 — Sueff writing letters for Water Colors (dunning also a letter to W. Frank Scott + got to bed about 11.20
