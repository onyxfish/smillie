## Left Page

### October Friday, 7 1870

Brilliant day — superb sky — and considering the storm just past, wonderful color. A little before A.M. started out for a days sketching with the pencil — first worked at some drift wood on Beada brook — then on some exposed roots on a bank — then to the top of Giant's [illegible] (11 oclk) to get outlines about 2° far. as I have not been able to complete my oil study — at 1 o'clk. built fire \+ cooked lunch — worked until 3.30 completing one careful outline study \+ several of boundaries — a little before 4. down \+ to Chappel Pond Brook \+ where I got a very nasty sketch — Home about 5.30 — Tea soon after 6 — glorious moonlight nights — I worked until nearly, going over my days work — To bed about 11.15

## Right Page

### October Saturday, 8 1870

a superb day — M.F., a heavy white frost, like snow, lay upon the ground, not a cloud — worked on pencil sketches \+ set drawings etc until 9.30, as I was afraid to set out in the woods before that time — then to top of cliff overlooking the fall in Ravine — with Hewlets, etc, was very comfortable, finished my studies \+ painted a small sketch 7×10 in, by 1.30 oclk — then home — a hurried lunch \+ down to mouth of Ravine \+ by 4.30 oclk had finished hastily a study of Running water that I commenced last [illegible]. cold as a cellar \+ I got very much chilled — Home at 5 \+ chopped wood until very warm — G.F. is used up with dyspepsia can do nothing this very anxious to get home — Tea about 6 — Eve. F I worked pencil sketches that I took yesterday until 9.30 — washed brushes \+ to bed in our new Room up stairs at 10.45
