## Left Page

### July, Sunday 3, 1870

Rain from 8 to 10 o'clk —
Then stopped, but did not
clear — Got Sonus + I greut
with miss J — to the Pres ptn
Ch — + heard Rv. W. Meingins
He gave us a characteristic
Rambling + interesting Sermon
on Martha's troubling about
many cares — Luke 10th 40 —
walked to Episcopal Ch —
miss P. joined us — then home —
Dinner about 2 — To church
again at 4 — W. M. preached
a better sermon from 2° Cor.
5th Ch. 9th v — telling very effectively
a story of a poor girl. Home
near 6 — all walked about the
garden, to the River, etc, until
after 7 — Tea, near 8 — Eve'g
sitting by a wood fire or
walking the piazzas to keep
warm — got to bed about
11 —

## Right Page

### July, Monday 4, 1870

*[written above header: Irvington]*

Up at about 7 — W't misty, with promise
of a clear warm day, which it was — The
weather has been delicious beyond everything.
Bkft. pet, 8+9 — + as soon as the grass was
dry enough, some croquet — then found the
W.C. a hot place to spend an hour waiting
under a feminine blockade. Some pistol
firing at a target, more croquet + sitting on
the piazza watching effects, passing boats with
flags etc — Dinner about 3.30 — After that
until after sundown — a glorious time —
a grand combination game of croquet,
3 sides, 2 on a side — obstinate struggle —
miss Anniet and I the winners — Tea about
8 — after tea some fireworks near 9 o'clk — all
went by invitation to next neighbors, Mr.
Mattieson, their friends + our party, 15 or 20 —
Some superb effects of color on lawn
among the trees in front of house. Bengal
lights, Roman candles + Rockets. It was the
real thing, no mock theatrical spectacle —
Refreshments after and a walk home
about 11 o'clk — moon just on the
horizon — To bed soon after getting in —
