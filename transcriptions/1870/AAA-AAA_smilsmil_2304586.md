## Left Page

### November Saturday, 26 1870

Raining until bet. 9 & 10 a.m. -
Charl. started off for Mary's
nurse at 5 o'clk. a.m. - a day of
anxious waiting, but at 1.30 P.M.
when I write this the little stranger
has not arr'd. - Got to studio at 9.30 -
Too dark to see to etch until 10 o'clk
- then to work. - It cleared off
charmingly. - Home to lunch at
1.30 - back at 2.30 - Met Dr. Yale &
he went to studio - Etched as
long as I could see - 4.30 - but did
not get all done that I had expected.
Home to tea at 5.30, at 7.30 went
to Phil. Concert with Matt, Kate
& Charl. - George not being able
to go -. Hardly in a humor to
enjoy it - Walked home with
Belle Tweedy - then home at
11.20 & to bed at 12. -

## Right Page

### November Sunday, 27 1870

Wonderfully beautiful day -
At 3° a.m. Mary gave birth to a
son after a severe labor. - Doctor
came to my bed at 7.30 and gave
me the information with details. -
Up - had bkfst & at 9.30 went
down to Will & Joe's with the news
stopping at the Tweedys to leave a
note - Home at 10.30 - tired &
nervous - at 11.30 went to Central
Park - Crossed to 8th ave & 79th St.
took cars & went to Uncle A's -
Enjoyed very much a Ducal
dinner - After dinner, took a
ride with him to High Bridge -
the afternoon, delicious - He left me
at 125th St & 8th ave, & I took cars home -
in at 5.30 - did not go out -
Aunt Margaret made a long
call - wrote note to the Duttons
& Rolled into my blankets on the
Sofa at 11.15 o'clk -
