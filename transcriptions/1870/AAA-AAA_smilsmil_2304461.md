## Left Page

### March Monday, 7 1870.

Snow more or less all day long —
got to Studio at 9.20 — Kindled
fire & set to work on "Mahtceunc
Lake" (3° day) working over the sky —
2° painting — but could not get
it any where near right — I
know what I want, but like
struggling in a nightmare
Cannot possibly get near it —
I fear that in spite of all my
efforts I can never rise above
Commonplace — Smudhed
at the Studio — worked until
near 5 — painted grey on plaster
skeleton of horse — washed brushes
& home to tea at 7 o'clk — Evely
spent part of it in Will's Room
with Geo. Hearn & his wife and
wrote a note to Post Dodge
Concerning Tableaux — 70
bed about 11 —

## Right Page

### March Tuesday, 8 1870.

Cleared if during the mf brilliantly
Squally p.m. — got to Studio at
9.5 got to sweeping & dusting
so that it was after 10 before I
got to work on "Lake Mahtceenac"
(4th day) Allie Diven came with
invitation that Gel. & I go to their
house to-night — about noon was
surprised by a visit from Mrs.
Lewis A. Hall of Boston — Spent
a very pleasant hour with her.
Home to lunch at 1.30 — Troubled
with neuralgic feeling in face —
Rode down to Fred Durand's to
see about it — Stopped at Medici's
& got to Studio at 3.15 — Painted
over distance & Lake — dissatisfied
with it — Home at 6.30 — at 8.30
with Gel. went to Diven's — an
informal party of some of the neigh-
bors. Annie Sowden & Josie Jessup's
there — left at near 11 — home
and to bed about 11.45
