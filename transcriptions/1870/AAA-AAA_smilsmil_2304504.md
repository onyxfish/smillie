## Left Page

### June Friday, 3 1870.

Mizzling, muggy day. Got to Studio at 9.30 \+ worked all the mf on car drawing getting it finished about noon \- at 1.15 home to dinner \- back at 2.20\_ Waited for my model \- she did not come \- So set to work on charcoal sketch "The lightening bolt", illustrating a passage in "among the Trees"\- worked until 7 o'clk \- Home to tea at 7.30 \- at 8.30 called on Miss Corlies, 146 W. 43° S. Had a very pleasant call \- Home at 10 \- mounted car drawing \- To bed about 11.30

## Right Page

### June Saturday, 4 1870.

Very warm \+ sticky, trying now to Rain and to Clear. Got to Studio about 9 \+ worked all the mf on charcoal drawing that I commenced yesterday P.M. \- Finished it, but it is not a success. Home to lunch at 1.10 \- back at 2.20 \- My model, Zimmerman, waiting for me \- I worked until 5 o'clk, getting two crayon sketches from him \- don't amount to much \- at 5 \- Allie Diven, Josie Jessup, Fannie Sowther called \- \+ worked about until near 7 \- then home to tea \- Evef \- Read "Lothair" and to bed at 11 \-
