## Left Page

### Sept. Thursday, 15 1870.

Beautiful m'g, but soon clouds up, not enough however to make it an unpleasant day — Got to my work on the cliff som after 9 o'clk — \+ worked until 1 oclk on the water, in study — then to the house to lunch — Found two misses Schuyler and their bro. from Irvington — Back to same study at 2, 15 \+ worked on foreground rocks — finishing the thing by 4 o'clk — then went farther along the cliff and got pencil sketch of Arbor Vitae \+ Hemlock trees — Home at 6 o'clk — Wer'g in Famely pleasant conversation with the Schuylers — about 10 d clk slight Rain — To bed soon after 10 —

## Right Page

### Sept. Friday, 16 1870.

Heavy Rain during early m'g — clouds very low \+ wet during day, but little Rain — The Schuylers got away for Lake Placid from after 8 oclk A.M. — I set to work on water color sketch that I laid in from Rock on N.W. side of Upper Lake looking towards the E — worked quite steadily all day until near 5 P.M. when Gl. and I walked around to Smith Beede's \+ called on the Durands — Saw some of the old gentlemans studies — Then walked down the Road to the second bridge \+ home to tea at 6.30 — Early ev'g, pleasant chatting with Miss Torrey whom I am getting to admire very much. Later when left alone, wrote a long letter to Sister Kate and got to bed at 11 o'clk —
