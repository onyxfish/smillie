## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 12, 1870

Beautiful day. Got to studio at 8.50. Set to work on "Adirondack Lake" 14×24. (5th day) \+ painted on trees in R.h. corner — until I went home to lunch at 1 o'clk — at 2 — and M.C.a — to attend the funeral of Kuntze which was to me very impressive D. Bellows delivering an excellent discourse. Met the Tweedy girls in George's Room after service. Ret'd to Studio at 3.50 and worked until 6 o'clk — then went to Avery's — not in — then home to tea shortly after 7. Evening Reading and to bed about 11.30.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 13, 1870

Beautiful day. Got to Studio abg \+ commenced work on "Adirondack Lake" 14×24 in but could not get under way for general excitement through the building owing to its being "varnishing day" at the Academy about 11 o'clk went over \+ there until near 1.30 when I went home to lunch — back to studio at 2.45 \+ got to work on 14×24 (5½ days) finishing the painting of foreground. Between 5\+6 o'clk Rhinehart came in \+ stayed until near 7 — Home to tea at 7.15 — Well Reading — I am very much under the weather — weak \+ dyspeptic — got to bed about 10.30
