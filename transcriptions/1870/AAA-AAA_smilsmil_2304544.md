## Left Page

### August Friday, 26 1870.

Up just before sunrise. Took 3 or 4 of the gives out on the lake — wind very high and cold. At btfst. lots to eat — broiled chicken hominy, etc, etc. Abg. with Gel. + the ladies, I took Orlando and went to cliff on opposite side of lake — Orlando chopped me a splendid view + I took a sketch. Back at 1 o'clk and lunched. At 3 o'clk, with Belle, Mary, Florence, Cha's Truedy — B.S. Gel. + I. climbed "The Slide" — all enthusiastic. The sky cloudless, but smoky again. Back at 6.15 — Found Mr. Charles Benedict in camp — a great surpris. \+ satisfaction. Jolly dinner. boiled chicken — broiled lamb — green corn — potatoes, toast, currant-jelly and lots of little etceteras. Well cold, after 11 when I rolled in for the night.

## Right Page

### August Saturday, 27 1870.

Mng. foggy and chilly — all up late — went out on the lake and saw some beautiful effects of rising fog. The atmosphere soon thickened with smoke so that I gave up the idea of going to the lower lake to work — and after a paddle in the dug-out, \+ enjoying some effects on the water, reflections + lily pads, I set to work on an oil color study of the camp. worked steadily until near sun set, then took a bath in the lake. after that, during twilight, paddled about the lake with Gel. + Misses Belle \+ Florence. until signaled for by Dr. Schugler, who with Mrs. T. Miss Jennie \+ Mr. Chas. Benedict had been up the slide — the evng. sky, the mts. + water, lovely beyond description. Evng. as usual quietly about the camp fire and rolled up for the night — soon after 11 — Very cold towards mrng.
