## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 9 1870.

Threatenings of a change in weather — By 9 oclk got to my
study of foreground stuff in the woods where I worked until
1 oclk — then home to dinner — back at 2.15 and to work on
two old hemlocks — worked until 5.15 when the sun went
down — then Belle, Mary & Jo came for me — packed traps
and back with them — Miss Caroline Durand took tea
with us — Fred came for her early —
Rain — soft & quiet — Spent most of the eveg. Reading up
back news papers — got to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 10 1870.

Rain during early mtg, then cleared into a cloudy blue,
octoberish day — about 9 oclk set up my easel at the front down &
set to work on study looking up the valley, commenced some time
ago — worked over what was already painted, but could not attempt
any of the superb, & at times, almost startling effects of light & color that
were constantly passing over the valley — two or three times, spatters
of Rain & very cold — Afternoon superb — worked until 4 oclk —
then about 4.30, with Mary I went on an exploration up
the Beede Brook — went away up to the gorge which I found
very fine — back about 6.30
after a fine freezing, thoroughly warmed — Suef Fred, D & sister
with us — muffins — apples and popped corn — got to bed
about midnight —
