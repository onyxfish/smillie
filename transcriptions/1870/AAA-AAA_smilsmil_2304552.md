## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 11 1870.

Superb day — went through a regular scrubbing shaving etc.
\+ presented myself at 8kfs½ in linen shirt \+ elk. suit — After 8kfs½ about 10 o'clk with Belle \+ Miss Walters went up — Bude took joined by Fred D \+ Sister — went up to gorge — Misses D \+ W. R[illegible] Fred Belle \+ I went on until 1.30. Then eat a meagre lunch of crackers \+ apples — \+ again on until 2.30. Then left the brook. \+ through the woods over the mt. (Giant) course S.S. on the crest, climbed a Spruce Fr. \+ changed course to E. — at 4.30 came out on slide — Magnificent day \+ view immense — Started down at 4.40 — got to top of fall at 6.55 quite dark — then into the woods. Heard Smith Bude calling — came nt in Bude brook above where me usually go in — while trying to get down over an ugly Rock — B. came with a lantern \+ about 7.30 got to his house — old Mr. D. very cross \+ alarmed — then home about 8 — Nobody scared — very hungry — dinner — Sitting about until 10 o'clk — then to bed —

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 12 1870.

Delightful day, but cold. Mr. R — Mrs. Tom S — \+ Jenny I — went home very early this mf — I did not get up to see them off — About 9 — went to my study \+ Hemlock \+ Beech trunk worked all the mf — very cold — near 1 o'clk — Young Clark — just from town, on his way in to the Lakes called on me — Home to lunch at 1.45 — a Roll Race down the hill with Mary J — then to work at 2.15 on Hemlock Trunks until 4.40 when the sunset — left the woods at 5 walked down to Patters where I met the J's \+ gel walked home — Dinner — beautiful night — the last night for the J's — Fred \+ Sister spent the eveng with us — got to bed at 11.30
