## Left Page

### August Saturday, 6 1870

Early m'g, cloudless, neutral work on water color study about 8.30 &
at 9.30 driven off by a shower, it soon cleared muggy hot, my
headache so that I lay down for an hour, then went over into Ravine
& up the River getting back to
dinner at 1.30 — Thunder storm
several during the P.M. About
4 o'clk. The Ineedys came from
E-town, all shouting and met —
I had expected them by way of
the forks & not before 8 or 9 o'clk. P.M.
lots of talk & fun over detentions
by the way & adventures of travel.
They left Chave & Kate at Lake George.
Tea about 6 & after a walk up
on the hill, back and around —
Evening in general chat and
all to bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### August Sunday, 7 1870

a very hot day. Bkfst — between
8 and 9 o'clk. After bkfst many
Ineedys, Gel & I walked down
to "Potter's" to get our mail, but
it had not been brought up, so
I got P.'s horse & wagon & many &
I drove down to Dibble's & got
letters & papers — among them a
long letter from Father. Back
to P.'s — met Gel & sauntered
home by the Road, not getting
there until after 12 — Dinner
bet. 1 & 2 — After that wrote a
letter to Father & on East down
steps, with blanket & pillow
enjoying shade & cool until
tea, about 6.30 — After tea
all walked down to Ravine —
then around to Smith Beede's —
glorious moonlight — back
about 9 — to bed at 10.30
