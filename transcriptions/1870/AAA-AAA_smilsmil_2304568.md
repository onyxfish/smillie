## Left Page

### October Thursday, 13 1870.

Routed out at 5 o'clk a.m after a good
nights rest. H[illegible] at 5.30 — my bed & board
very fair — $1.00 — Trains started at 6.10, fine
Ride along the Susquehanna River, to the
Bend, charming — a few spattering drops of
rain, then beautifully tinted clouds, the
coming sun, reflected in the River. Got
to New Milford at 7.10 — Sun nicely up,
clouds all gone — Had a glorious Ride
outside of stage — Got to Uncle Mis[illegible] at
9 o'clk — S.C.+F. left at 5 o'clk to take [illegible]
at Montrose Station the train I had
just left — Took a little more H[illegible] then
unpacked my trunk & enjoyed
a thorough wash & change — took clean
linen to [illegible] hew woman & then walked
over the hill past Leonard Searls farm —
Enchanting day — a few sketchy notes — home
to dinner at — At 2 went out to fair
ground with pencils & paints, getting
pretty well tired out, but had clouds,
her holiday & the wind blew cold so
I could do nothing & back at 3.30
— Read, back news papers — Tea at 6
o'clk & 6 o'clk. Went down to Fair [illegible]
Rawled myself sick at Ben[illegible] & [illegible]
then home & to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### October Friday, 14 1870.

Atoims day — Up about 7 o'clk — H[illegible] near
8 o'clk & at nearly 9 o'clk. I started out
with horse & buggy, driving over to near
New Milford & returning by way of Hart
Lake & Jessop's hill, getting several
slight pencil sketches of hill lines.
Back to dinner at one o'clk, and
immediately after went up to the
Fair ground and spent most of the
afternoon making a couple of
sketches looking to the west —
then wandered off back of the Sayre
farm, returning across lots and
through the woods getting in at
6 pretty well tired — Tea at 6.30
At 8.30 went over to Dan Sayre's
& called on the bride — then until
9.45 then home & read papers
until 10.30 — then to bed. In
the early even[ing] a beautiful,
colored Aurora —
