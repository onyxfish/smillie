## Left Page

### November Tuesday, 22 1870

Violent storm of wind and rain —
got to studio soon after 9 — and
commenced etching on die (3d day)
my hand is "out" just enough to make
the work extremely irks me to
me, so that impatient as I am
to get it done, I progress slowly.
The weather so bad that I stayed
& lunched on Graham crackers
& beef tea at the studio — P.M.
surprised by a visit from Chas.
Baker, who married Dr. Vanderspell's
eldest daughter. He is a strapping big
fellow & I did not know him — He
wants me to paint a little picture
for him. So dark that I could
not see to etch after 3 o'clk —
Home to tea at 6. Numa Bourne
at the house all night — very stormy —
worked on Kansas arms drawing
and to bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### November Wednesday, 23 1870

Cloudy & windy, but not cold. Got
to studio at 9 o'clk. and to work
on etching (4th day) Much pleased
to find that my hand is less "out"
than I thought, some of my work being
quite flat enough — The day so dark
that I had trouble to see. Home
to lunch at 1 — back at 2.15 —
etched until 4 — then I could
see no longer & went to Knoedler's
to see the pictures — then to A. of D —
then to Geo's Room — then Coffee's —
then Snedecor's — then studio — then
home to tea at 6 — Westf., a home
entertainment to celebrate Father's
birth day etc. — but I had another
engagement — At 8, at Jmeedys —
met with Belle & Mary to call
on Corinne Coleman Smith at 128
E. 16th — there until 10 — left the J's
at 10.40 — home at 11.15 and to bed
at near 12.
