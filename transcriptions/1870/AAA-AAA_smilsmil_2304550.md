## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 7 1870.

Glorious day — cold in the m[orning] but mild & cloudless P.M. — [illegible] letters from Charl & Father — Instructed to make drawing for A.B.N. Co. at once — Stretched paper and at it — great excitement over war news from France — MacMahon defeated — Napoleon a prisoner, etc, etc — Mr. Oliver I[illegible] arr[ived] near noon — Three wagon loads from E-town arrived at noon — Avery family — the Bishop, Sauvie & others, on their way to the Lakes — at 2 o'clk — I went to Cliff overlooking Ravine & finished the first painting of the top of fall — about 4.30 Mary & Belle I came to where I was — I worked until after sun down — then all home together — The night superb — walked out beyond Widow Hale's with Mary — I — between 9.30 & 11 o'clk — Got to bed very soon after 11 —

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 8 1870.

A wonderfully beautiful day — but I am almost used up with a headache — So not fit to work on any study out of doors, I determined to fight it out in the drawing for A.B.N. Co — the Burlington party, all but Miss Abbett, got off this m[orning] — I wrote a note to Father, & exhibit by them — Worked all day closely on drawing — by the eve[ning], finished it — Then took a short walk on the hill — After tea made a birch bark roll & fixed drawing to go by mail — Soon after 9 o'clk — the moonlight being glorious, Belle, Mary, Florence, Ge[o.] & I — went up to the woods and old Uncle David's mill — The effects over Giant-Carnel's Humps — in the woods, & on the River, were very fine — Home & to bed at 11.15 o'clk —
