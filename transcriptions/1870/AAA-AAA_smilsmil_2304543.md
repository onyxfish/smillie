## Left Page

### August Wednesday, 24 1870.

Up early, after only a passable
night's Rest, Rather chilly sleeping.
Felt better after washing. After
bkfst. Geo. & I took Jenny, Belle and
Mary & Rowed away up the River.
Air still so thick that the mts.
could hardly be seen, but the
River was superb — Back at
12.30, after a jolly mrng. Dr. S.
came in with a few fine fish —
they were cooked — The P.M.
spent fixing up things about
camp and making a pencil
sketch — also getting brush together
for a brush fire — A good mess
of Trout for Supper, which I broiled.
Well wrote letter to Mr. Cram & gave
it to young Nicholson to carry down to-
morrow, at 9 o'clk. I lighted my brush
fire — the night was very dark and the
fire was a perfect success — the flames
shot up 50 or 60 feet — dense smoke circled
in hurrying masses & the trees stood out
spectral ghards to the solemn forests
whose depths seemed inky black behind
them — We Rowed on the lake before the
fire died out & enjoyed some wonderful
Dantesque effects — Rolled into our
blankets at about 10.30, for the night.

## Right Page

### August Thursday, 25 1870.

Up at 6 after a tip-top nights rest,
at 6.30 quite a heavy shower, then
threatened to clear, but left us with
the same miserable mist through
which not a single mt. is visable.
I cooked a pail full of hominy —
a success — myf. spent trying to get
to work at a sketch of camp, but could
not get up the spirit. Enjoyed fussing
about camp until dinner time — then
a shower — later a heavy thunder
storm — ½ inch of water in ½ an hour — I
went out on the lake during its heaviest,
— immense — Sudden clearing — more
Rain during eveg. Orlando came in
from the Valley with provisions, very
wet. Eveg., in the tent, usual
chat — to bed at last at 11.15
wind blew up strong — Aurora —
During the night, high wind and
cold, but slept very well —
