## Left Page

### May, Wednesday, 4, 1870

Very warm day. Got to studio at 9.30. Ther. there at noon 79°. Painted foreground on 12×22. (5° day). Soon after 11, Fan Mulford came in. At 11.35 put her into a stage. At Rushton's took a bottle of Citrate of Magnesia, which Rushed me for the Rest of the day. Dr. Norman Kingsley called about 1 o'clk. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 and painted until near 6 when I finished 2° painting over in an unsatisfactory manner, as usual. Wilcoy of "The Telegram" called — Home tea at 7 — We're in talk with Father \+ [illegible] — wrote a long letter to W. W. Warren at Annapolis, concerning getting up an etching club \+ got to bed shortly before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday, 5, 1870

overcast during m'f, and much cooler. Got to Studio a few minutes after 9 — Commenced 2° painting on "Indian Pass \+ McIntyre" 12×22. (3°. day.) Getting over sky \+ mountains a Mr. Dinsmore \+ Mrs. Childs spent good part of m'f with Ferguson in my Room — Jennie Treddy called — Home to lunch at 1.30 at 2.15 called on J. P. Putnam \+ got order for pictures at Union League Club — Stopped there, then to studios at near 3 o'clk. Near 4 o'clk, Messrs Whitney, Sewis and W. T. Richards called for a few minutes. At 5.30 went to Albemarle with Gp, dined with them. Home at 7.20 — At 8.30 went to see Fan M. at Mr. Gampubanis at 2\. 17 2\. 39 St. there until 10.15 then home \+ to bed at 11
