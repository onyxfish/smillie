## Left Page

### May, Monday 30, 1870

Cloudy \+ threatening \- again headache so that I am unable to work. "Commemoration day" \- as I saw veterans \+ flowers I almost sighed that had not had strength enough to die usefully \+ Respectably for my country \- Went to Sauthier's with Montclair drawings to be pase- -partonted \- then to Snedicors \+ Schaum's \+ back to Studio at 12 \- [illegible] Warren called at noon \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.45 \- P.M. called for first time on young Gaylord. Spent P.M. hanging uselessly about \- home at 6 o'clk \- Evening equally useless \- To bed at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 31, 1870

Muggy day, striving to be clear \- Wrote a note or two after 8 o'clk \- then went to Bogardus's \+ sat again for photo then to Studio \- then to Hudson R.R.R. \- To get sketches of cars for a drawing for the A.B.N. Co. \- Got sketches \+ photos \+ back to studio at 12 \- Home to lunch at 1 \- back at 3 \- Found Henrietta Stoltz waiting \+ as Ferguson was away for the P.M. \+ I wanted the study, she sat for me to draw \- I worked hard from 3 until 5 o'clk getting a poor but encouraging drawing \- This is my first experience of being shut up with a nude female and I am happy to record that wrong was neither done or thought. My mind was wholly \+ honestly on my work \- Finishing at study until after 6 \- Home to tea at 7 \- at 8.30 at Will's \- 16 St. Luke's Place \- not in \- Called at Mrs. Geo. Hearn 12th St. not well \- left card \- Called on S. P. Avery \- nothing \- Met Fanning \+ Will Kimball \- walked down 5th ave. with them \- then to Studio and at 10 o'clk, home \- To bed at 10.30
