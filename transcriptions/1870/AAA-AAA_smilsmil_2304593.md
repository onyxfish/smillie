## Left Page

### December Monday, 12 1870

Horrible blowing rainy day, very dark — Got down town about 10
o'clk — Greeley took pfs. of my due
(16th day) and I worked at Father's
desk, he being home, until noon —
Greely then again took pfs. for
me + at 2 o'clk I went to Farley's
to lunch — At 2.30 went to Pate.
\+ had 3 pfs. of die — then to Legate's
bookstore in Beekman St. There
½ an hour or more — then at 4 go
at Studio — went to Schmitt's + got
boards to pack drawing + die +
at 5.15 home — In at 6 — packed
drawings + die + wrote letter to
Uncle Willie's + at 8.30 at Avery's
to look over some of his etchings.
Part of the ev'g with Mr. at daughter's.
Later with E. D. Palmer — who is
visiting at Avery's — left at
10.25 — walked home — weather
mild + foggy — To bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### December Tuesday, 13 1870

Bright mild m'g, but soon clouded
up — Got to Studio's about 10.15 and
found Neuukow, the cyf. Russian
waiting for me — He stayed an hour
or more — then I kindled fire, about
noon — Jas. Hart came in + stayed
until 1 o'clk — talking over the new
case of the Mr. Hart–Taurman difficulty
caused by the publication by f —
of a literal copy of Hart's lectures. Then
home to lunch — Back at 2.30 — for
a time worked, getting things fixed
up when, Colman — Calverly — Yale.
Misses Outerbridge + Slade came
in one after the other — then Miss
Covell + Mrs. Rev. Boardman + Mrs.
Judson — they stayed until dark —
home to tea at 5.30 — At 7.30
at N.A. of D. — Rimmer's 2d. lecture.
At 9.30, went to Rondel's Studio — a
meeting of Water Color Society —
took active part in discussions
until 10.30 — Home at 11 and
to bed at 11.30
