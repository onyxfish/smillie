## Left Page

### June, Thursday, 9, 1870

Overcast, muggy & threatening Rain.
Got to studio at 9.15 — Set to work
Sweeping & clearing up — then sat
for Augero, study for head, for half
an hour & at last got to work
about 11 o'clk. on drawing of
Niagara for Uncle Willie — Mr.
Heade, the artist, called — Home
to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — Young
Verplank called & about 3 o'clk
Haw W. & Mrs. Hannybhan called.
I went with them to Arnold's to
select carpets — back at 5 o'clk
& worked until 6.30 — at 6.45
went into Madison Sq. & listened
to Music by the band until 7.30.
Home at 8 — Father & Mother back
from Plainfield — Joe & Will
called — also Aunt Mary aret —
got to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### June, Friday, 10, 1870

Rain at last & enough of it. It began
to fall about 10 o'clk — & the Rest of the day
Rode down town with Father at 8.30 —
stopped at A.B.N.Co— at 10 went to
Battery — to go on board "Miantonomah"
monitor. Mr. Chas. Breaker on leave of
absence, but shown over the ship by
a young fellow, Nicholas Anderson. Was
very much interested — Ret'd about
3 o'clk. Heavy Rain — went to World
Life Ass. Saw Mr. Frothingham — does
not altogether like my design —
long Talk — then to Continental Ins.
Co — Ring Smith — short Talk — then
to A.B.N.Co. — & to lunch with Father.
Back to studio at 2.20 — head
ache — on sofa until 5 — then up
& made another sketch for World
Ins. Co. Home to tea at 7 —
[illegible] at 8.45 called on Smeedys —
left at 10.45 — home soon after —
11 & to bed at 11.45
