## Left Page

### August Saturday, 20 1870

Rain during the night and m[ost] all in doors \- Reading Mark Twain's "Innocents abroad" while I worked on Locomotive drawing but my head distressed me so that I did precious little \- and so during the afternoon. With headaches \+ nervous derangement I do not feel any thing as well as I did last summer. The afternoon cleared very thick and smoky \- Bet. 4 \+ 5 o'clk. we all went on a walk down the valley \- at Potter's we got a letter from Mr. Genl. Crain at E.town \- it was foolish and bad tempered, Someone has told her that Charl has been circulating stories concerning her at the Valley House \- E.town \- Evening all very hilarious \- games \+ noise \- about 11 when I got to bed \-

## Right Page

### August Sunday, 21 1870

Fresh day, Rather cool \- Spent most of spare time during the m[orn]g, writing to Mr. Crain, notes for myself and Charl \- Had lunch abt and about 2 o'clk. all, except Florence, started for Chappel Pond \- Stopped at Smith Beede's \- Joined by old W. Durand Miss Carrie \+ Fred \- We enjoyed a delightful walk through the woods to the Pond after leaving Mr. Durand on the brook \- At the pond we fought \+ cut a way round to the S.W. Cor. and had a first rate view of the pond \+ cliffs on "Giant" \- Back about 7 \- Evening Really cold and I felt miserable \- some singing \- I read the Crain correspondence, much amusement \- Midnight before we got to bed \-
