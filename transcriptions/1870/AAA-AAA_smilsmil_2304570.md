## Left Page

### October Monday, 17 1870

a half clouded, warm, dreamy sort of day — Up at 7 — at fp — at 8
\+ at 9 I went to the foundery
where I spent the Rest of the m[illegible]
getting a new pike for my sketching
umbrella \+ getting corner fixings
for my Trunk — Home at noon —
Dinner at 1 — at 1.30 went to st[illegible]
\+ at 2 started out on a drive with
fan — By a mistake on Butterfield
Hill, I took the wrong Road that had
a poor horse I could not make it
to get to the top of Fire Hill as I had
intended — the late P.M. through[illegible]
clouded in the west — got in at 5.30
Eve[ning] at 7.30 called on H\. J[illegible]
\+ Millie at 8.45 went to fan M[illegible]
met there Hettie Searle \+ Joe M[illegible]
Whist — left at 10.30 — Home \+ Read
newspaper until 11 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### October Tuesday, 18 1870

Up at 6.30 — wind, a half gale, with Rain squalls.
Arranged for packing until at fp. at 8 o'clk.
After, ran around, to foundery, carpenters,
etc, little messages — Ret'd. at 9.30. Fixed
corners on Trunk — At 10 commenced to pack
— at 10.30, finished — Bid Fan good bye — and the
[illegible] — At 11 — got off in the Stage — Wind
very high \+ cold — Splendid sky full of clouds —
Depot at 12.40 — Train at 1.20 — Cha[rle]s Groesbe[ck]
my companion — Between 4 \+ 5 o'k. P.M.
got to top of Pokono Mt. — view superb —
very few clouds — air keen \+ clear and
the color delicious — through the gaps
when half the Mt. in shadow — Top, very
very charming — a cup of Tea at Delaware
about 7 — got to Washington Station and
changed cars to Morris \+ Essex R.R. got into
Hoboken at 9 — Home by R[ailway] cars at 10 —
Saw Father, Mother \+ Gil — Got to bed
at 12 — much surprised to
learn that Aunt Mary Gillelgau
was buried last Sunday — She
died
