## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 25 1870

Heavy & threatening — Rain, but more threats than water — Read my papers until near 11 o'clk — then commenced writing to Bell Smeedy & at 1.30 finished a long letter — Dinner about 2, menu Venison — Afternoon Reading until near 5 o'clk — then started out to walk, the weather having cleared delightfully, but was caught by an misbehavior of my abdominal viscera — Had to Return from my walk and go to bed where I stayed until near 10, then up for a few minutes and again to bed, feeling very slim [illegible — ~3 words]

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 26 1870

Weather still quite mild and beautifully clouded — My head feels rather clouded too — After [illegible] not feeling like going out to work, wrote a letter to sister Mary — At 11 went out with pencil & paper & sketched a maple tree — about 1 called at Smith B's & bid the Durands good bye — There met Mrs. Williams & daughters and Miss Otto just from E. Town for a walk — Home to dinner near 2 o'clk — At 3 went to work on a little water color sketch, spoiled an hour and a piece of paper, then home — The Mrs ff for E. town leaving Misses Otto & Newman — On my bed until near tea time — Eve'g spent in general chat, mainly upon the subject of horses — To bed about 10.30
