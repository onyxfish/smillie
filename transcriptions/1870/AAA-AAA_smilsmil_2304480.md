## Left Page

### April, Thursday, 14, 1870.

Very hot wilting day — with Matt
+ Geo. at 8.30 went to Sloan's, to
look at a parlor carpet, selected
yesterday — walked back, stopping
at Snedicor's + got to Studio at
10.20. Very much used up by
some sort of dyspeptic trouble
did not fairly get to work
until near 12 o'clk — then touching
up here + there + working over sky
on 14×24 "Near Po'keepsie" — also
putting in some cattle — Home
to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.15
afternoon working on 14×24 —
"Lake Mahkeenack" — putting in
cattle, etc — Walter Brown bought
my "Edmond's Pond" framed all,
for $55.00 — or $37.00 for the painting
Home to tea at 7 — at 8.30 went
to Union League, Monthly meeting
then to N.A. of D. Private View —
very close + crowded — Home
at 11.20 + to bed about
midnight.

## Right Page

### April, Friday, 15, 1870.

Very hot until late P.M. when it blew
up quite chilly. Got to Studio at
9.15 + set to work painting in a
view at North Conway, on "Artist's
Brook", about 10×14. (1st day) at
1 o'clk, home to lunch — back at
2.15 — painted until after 5 o'clk
when I get about half or my
picture painted in — and in
a manner, considering the weather
+ my feelings, tolerably satisfactory.
Home to tea at 7 — at 8 went
to Miss Tiffany's + worked on
wood pincushions, making little
water color sketches until 11 o'clk.
Sons had some young fellows
to play billiards with him
+ a little supper — Home
about 11.15 — to bed about 11.45.
