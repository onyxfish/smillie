## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 12, 1870

Mild & somewhat cloudy —
got to studio at 9.20 and
at once mounted drawing
and then set to work on
the sketch drawing that I
sent to Phila 26th last — Spring
"On the Saco" — I worked over
the sky & generally, finishing
Miss Sterling came in about
noon & was enthusiastic over
my drawing — I like it better
myself now that it's finished up
& portions gummed out. Home
to lunch at 1.20 back at 2.20
& working on the two drawings until
about 5 when, lighting the gas,
I commenced a sketch for the
A.B.N. Co. for a R.R. Bond drawing.
Home to tea at 6.45. Evening
until 9.30 at work on sketch.
To bed about 10.45

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 13, 1870

Very mild, enervating day, quite
a rain during the mng, after-
noon clear. Got to studio at 9.30
& spent most of the mng getting
the "place" to "rights" & cleared
up — worked on "Saco River" draw.
& fixed up the frame for it —
Home to lunch at 1.45 back
at 2.40 — worked over the water
on my large drawing — taking
the blue out of it, & adding
red — think it better — De-
Sale came in just at dusk —
Home ab. 6.30 — at 7.45 called
on Mr. H.G. Norton — all out, then
on Mr. Knowlton — there for ½
an hour & at 8.40, on Mr. Rob.
Dodge at 7th — 7th ave. where I
stayed until 10.30, when I
walked home — got in abt 11.
& to bed about 11.45
