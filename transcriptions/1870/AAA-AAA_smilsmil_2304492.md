## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 10, 1870

Cold, raw m[illegible], threatening Rain. Got to studio at 9.20 & kindled fire. M[illegible] spent between sitting for Augers to paint head in one of his pictures & painting figures in "Adirondack Lake" 4×24 for Rimy Smith. Home to lunch at 1.35 after stopping at Knoedler's & Fanny Kimball's. Back at 2.30. Rain — at 4 o'clk heavy thunderstorm so that I could not keep engagement to go to N.A. of D. with Fanny K. — Stephen Marsett called, did not see him — Painted Rock cor. in "Indian Pass" 12×22. Home to Tea at 7 — moved books up stairs — at 8.30 called on Mrs. Preston Spring — she sails for Europe to-morrow — at 9.15 called on Fanny K. there until 11.15 then home & to bed at 12.15

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 11, 1870

Rain, unintelligated — Got to Studio at 9.20 & painted over a little sketch about 7×10 that I painted in 2 or 3 years ago at Montrose — "Oct. in Northern Penn." — at 1 o'clk went to N.A. of D. & met Father — we looked at the pictures of the candidates for Academic honors. Home to lunch at 2 — back to studio at 2.45. Worked until 5 getting a little picture that I hope will please & sell. Fan M. came in at 4.30 — at 5 me went to N.A. of D. & at 6 I took her to 217 E. 39th — then home to Tea — Father got in from Annual N.A. of D. meeting soon after 7 — I have again been a candidate for N.A. & have narrowly missed it. I am as little chagrined at the failure as I would have been pleased had I been successful. Out at 8.30 with Gil went to the Tweedey's — there until 10.40 — then home — washed brushes — stretched paper etc. & got to bed soon after 12 o'clk
