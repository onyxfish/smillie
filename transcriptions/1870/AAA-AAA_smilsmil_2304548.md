## Left Page

### Sept. Saturday, 3 1870.

miserable m[illegible], so did not get up until near 7 o'clk — Then had another tip-top chicken bkfst. The Rain falling as though in for a full days work — Packed things \+ at 9.20 with Beede started for Miders B's — all of us carrying some luggage — gentle Rain — got in a little before 11 \+ enjoyed a thorough scrub and change. House running over full — Some went to Crawford's to vacate our Room — Dinner at 1.30 — P.M. spent[?] idly about the house — Rain continuing — Beede went back to our shanty with horse \+ brought down our remaining traps — Tea very late — every evening game of grab — got to bed very tired about 10.30 —

## Right Page

### Sept. Sunday, 4 1870.

overcast and showery, but cleared somewhat during the middle of the day, so that with Mr. Tweedey, Belle \+ Many, I walked down to Potter's \+ back abt 1 o'clk — M[illegible] spent morn mail, letters from Father and Kate — Papers very interesting — Lunch abt 1.30 \+ afternoon reading newspapers, napping \+ writing note to Mr. Cram — to 1 a.m. Hark \+ long letter to Sister Matt — Dinner about 7 and eveg singing \+ Reading — Did not get to bed until about 11.30 — Rain during late eveg \+ P.M. — about 11, strong wind \+ cold —
