## Left Page

### June Tuesday, 7 1870.

Miserable, muggy, Rainy day. got to studio at 9.30 \+ at work on crayon sketch for The World Ins. Co. Worked until near 1 o'clk. Then home to lunch back at 2.15 and worked until 6.30, getting a middling careful drawing done \- While I am very much interested, even encouraged in the drawing of figures, the more I do, only shows me how hopeless at my time of life \+ under my peculiar difficulties must be my ambitious dream of doing anything in that direction from half past 6 until 7, dozing in lounge \- then home to tea and at 8.20 at Griswold's Room, 51 W. 10th St to meet Warren \+ others to form an etching club. A fizzle, only Wm. Hart Warren \+ Griswold there. \- Home at 10.30. To bed at 11.15 \- Father and mother went to Plainfield this morning.

## Right Page

### June Wednesday, 8 1870.

Very hot day. Uncle Willie arrived this mng \- got to studio at 9.15. Worked until near noon on the crayon sketch I did yesterday. Interrupted by heat, callers and the Masonic procession. \- D. Mulford called \- went with him to see a stupid crayon sketch, just finished, purporting to have been done by the spirit of Raphael. called on Dr. Otis \- home to lunch at 1.15 \- back to studio at 2.15 \- P.M. working over drawing of "Golden Gate" \- W. Hy Buckley of Manchester, England, called. \- Home at 6.30 \- Tea \+ at 7.30 called on Fan W. \- 39th St. She wanted to go to Central Park Garden but she had to go to Mrs. Marianne 45th St., so I went with her \+ the Campubans \- left at 10.30. Home at 11.15 \- to bed at 11.30
