## Left Page

### April Monday, 4 1870.

Slush unmitigated. A most horrible day, weatherly speaking. Got to Studio at 9.5 and until 10.30 — cleaning out stove pipes + getting fire kindled. Then set palette + commenced Re-painting an old 9×12 sunset. Home to lunch at 1.45. back to studio at 2.45 — afternoon until after 5 painting, when I finished — It is still very bad in composition — in fact no composition at all — The color, I'm afraid is not much better. Home to tea at 6.15 At 7.15 went with Matt + Kate to Concert at Madison Ave Baptist Ch — which proved a very enjoyable affair — Heard Miss Gibbs for the first time, + liked her — Home at 10.45 — To bed at 11.45

## Right Page

### April Tuesday, 5 1870.

Showery. Rain at night, trying to be sleet. Got to Studio at 9.10. Found my fire out. Re-kindled it + got to work on 14×24 — "On the Hudson near Pok[ee]psie, (5th day) for Ruiny Smith. Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.15 and worked as long as I could see — Bettering the painting I think — but it is still very bad — I never before worked with so keen a sense of the very poor quality of the work I do. — Did not get home to tea until near 7.30 — Feel "springy" and used up — Went. Read paper + hung about until about 11 when I got to bed — Between 10 + 11 P.M. Uncle Willie arrived from Ottawa —
