## Left Page

### October Saturday, 15 1870

Yet another glorious day — Immediately after bkft. got my oil color traps in readiness and went up to the Fair ground — I spent the mornng on a sketch, 7×10 in, looking to the [illegible] my object being to get something of color & atmospheric effects, looking under the sun & over the hills — At 1.15 back to dinner & at 2 back to work — Started a new sketch 8×14 looking from the ledge, S.W. The wind very high & troublesome, weighted everything with stone — temperatures very mild & by 4.30 or 5 o'clk finished both sketches that I think will be of great service to me — Tea at 6.30 at 7.30 called on Fanny Jessup — not in, at her bro. Willis's — Saw the old Lady & Huntting & his wife — At 8.30 went to Wm Jessup's — Found there, besides the family — Fanny J. & Fan M., Miss Tiger, Mr. & Mrs. Hajdlin — Joe Williams & young Koon — Played a mixed game on croquet board — at 10 went home with Fan M. — Taking a little moonlight walk by the [illegible] — Then home & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### October Sunday, 16 1870

Yet another glorious day, cloudless, being almost too warm — Up late & bkfst. — ditto — After bkfst. sauntered about the barn & garden with uncle Wm. — went to the Baptist Ch. and heard Mr. Bowen of Factoryville beg for the Keystone Academy there, with intensely disagreeable voice and manner. After service went with Uncle W. & Aunt H. & called on old Mrs. Turrell — Then, alone, walked to the south of town & thereabouts. Returning, called for a few moments on Fan — Then home to dinner at [illegible]. At 4 went for Fan & we walked up to the Fairground & there enjoyed the effects for ½ an hour — Then in to Jakarmalt's, getting there just at dusk — Back at 8 o'clk — went in with Fan & made a note to Gel., posted it & got home at 9.30 — To bed at 10.
