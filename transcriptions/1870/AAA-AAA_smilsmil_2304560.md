## Left Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 27 1870.

weather singularly uncertain
morning bright, but soon threatening
clouds settled on the tops of the
mts. Immediately after bkf[ast] —
Misses Torney, Otto & Newman go
off for Edmonds' Ponds — I did
not feel well & it was 9 o'clk nearly
before I went off to woods beyond
widow Hale's with my water colors,
worked for an hour to no purpose —
then hurried home, got out trakes
& right back — Met Mr & Mrs Hunk, Mr
Mallory & Halberton on their way out to
the lake — worked like a good fellow
until 3 o'clk — getting my subject coming
in — Then walked in the woods until
I met the Hunk party Returning — back
with them to house at 5 o'clk — Took
a private tea alone — Then Misses T. &
N. Ret'd from Edmonds Ponds — Miss Otto
had gone on to E-town — Evef quite an
animated discussion concerning
calculating and Romantic [people?]
until about 9 o'clk — Then Read
newspapers until 10 & to bed soon
after —

## Right Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 28 1870.

Glorious day — mild & dreamy, a delicious
haze over every thing — Misses T. & Newman
went to top of Rainbow Fall & spent the day.
About 8.30 I went to meadow beyond
Smith Beede's & set to work on a bit
Autumny Color, m'oil, on paper 7×10 —
Very difficult to arrange my light so
that I could see — Got it Roughly finished
by 11 o'clk — then to work on another,
same size — more of a picture in subject
& more carefully painted — At 1 o'clk
went to the brook — kindled fire and
cooked lunch — enjoying it & surroundings
exceedingly — worked until 4.30
when the sun set — packed traps &
got home soon after 5 — the party
from the fall in about 6 & we had dinner
about 6.30 — Evef. in usual chatting
after the ladies Retired I Read
& got to bed about 10.45 —
