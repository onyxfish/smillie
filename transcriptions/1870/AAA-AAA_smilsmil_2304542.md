## Left Page

### August Monday, 22 1870

a miserable, thick smoky day, mountains hardly visible and the effect on me very depressing. Up between 4 & 5 a.m. — to see Kate \+ Charl[ie] off for home, then back to bed \+ up again at 7. Soon after 9 — with Mr. Tweedy Dr. Schuyler went to my stud[y] on top of cliff overlooking fall — commenced laying in color — worked badly all the m[or]n[in]g \+ my head hurts me — back to dinner at 1 — to my work again at 3 but by 5 o'clk. had accomplished very little — then home \+ stretched on the bed — Every. Twitchel \+ wife called on the ladies — after 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### August Tuesday, 23 1870

*Camp on Upper Ausable.*

Woke up with a miserable headache — no idea of going into camp, but the rest of the party ready — So, at 8.30, in desperation, got up, a jolly break — packed traps, took a strong cup of tea \+ at 10 o'clk, our party set off — I rode about a third of the way, walked the rest — my umbrella mocked by getting between the wheel \+ the axle — got to Lower Lake at 12.15 — lunched at Shurtliffe's camp and then on — In the thick, smoky atmosphere, the cliffs seemed to loom up with a new element of grandeur — the party jubilant — got to Upper Lake, all heavily loaded about 3.30 — soon established Mr. Tom Tweedy, Missis Jenny, Belle, Mary, Florence \+ Mr. Cha[rle]s Tweedy, Dr. Mr. Schuyler[,] [illegible] \+ I, the party. Smith \+ Orlando Bede guides — a hearty meal, at 6. Every[one] a noble fire with usual camp chat. All rolled in blankets for the night soon after 10 o'clk —
