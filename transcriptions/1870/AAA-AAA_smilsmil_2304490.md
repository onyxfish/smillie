## Left Page

### May, Friday 6, 1870

Muggy — M[illegible] overcast, struggling to clear about noon — spitting at 2 o'clk — Rains at 5 — and steady during evef. At 9 o'clk called at Albemarle & bid Messrs Lewis Whitney & Richards good bye — at Studio at 9.30 & to work on 12×22 — "Indian Pass" (3½ days) painting over middle distance at 4 o'clk washed up — home to lunch at 1.30 — at 2.30 at Phil. Rehearsal — Saw & spoke with S.C.T. after Rehearsal walked home with the Tweedys — went in, until 6 — Home to tea at 7.10 — Evef until 10.30 hard at work getting my closet trapes moved to second story back — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 7, 1870

Chilly & overcast — noon, cleared. P.M. clouded again. Got to Studio at 9.30 — feeling very unlike work — tried to paint on foreground of "Indian Pass" 12×22 — Mr. Morse (Lydia Smith) of Boston called with her daughter & a friend — The daughter is 16 yrs old & looks just as I remember her mother 18 yrs ago, when she was 18 yrs old — I feel like an old fogy. Nicoll called with a lady & her daughter — Home to lunch at 1.30 back at 2.30 — did as little during P.M. as A.M — Home at 5.30 — At 7 at Phil. Concert — After concert walked home with Isabelle Tweedy — Home at 11.20 — To bed soon after 12 —
