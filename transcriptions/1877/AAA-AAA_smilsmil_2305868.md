## Left Page

### September, Sunday 23, 1877

Up soon after 6. Another glorious day — milder than yesterday — Bkfp — about 8 — at 9, in a 3 seat wagon drawn by mules, Dud, Ben & Walter & I — gel & I started for long Pond — 10 m — exceedingly rough through wild woods — got to Ricketts Hotel at 11.30 — walked through the woods about ½ a mile to an outlook where we had a very fine view of mt gorge & wilderness — back to home & dinner at 12.30 — at 1 drove on about 2½ miles to another view over an immense stretch of country — not unlike view from Catskill Mts &c — the other view, very like looking down Catskill Mt. Clove. Back to Ricketts at 3 & there Right on home where we arr — at 5.30 very tired & bruised — my consciences somewhat pained by the fact that in this wilderness they have no church of any description. Tea soon after 6. For awhile in parlor then wrote a letter to father & to bed soon after 9.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 24, 1877

Cloudless & very warm in the sunshine. Bkfp — a little before 8 — the train from Towanda late — did not start to return until near 10. Mrs Post went there — I went to the Breaker & spent the mng there sketching — Dinner about 1 — It was after 2 when Gel, Dud & I went in to the mine and spent an hour examining the workings — at 3.30, with three of the young ladies, Dud & Ben went riding — made a circuit passing through Green Settlement & Dushore — Back at 6.30 — then Tea — Evef in the Co's office looking over maps of the workings — then to the house — chatting & reading until 10 o'clk — Tobed at 10.30
