## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 12. 1877.

Charming day - nearly 10 when I got to studio -
Set palette & to work on 12×
18 in - "By Still waters". Painted
until near 1 - then went over
to N.A. & took a hurried look
at the Olyphant collection.
Back to studio, lunched
at 2 o'clk. on Venison sent
by Mr. Jacobs. Afternoon
painted until near 4.30
then could see no longer.
While writing A.P. Wood
came in & stayed until
near 6 - took a short walk
& got to Hall's at 6.30 - just
as Annie Cook did - Miss Junis
also there - all very jolly over the
Springfield wedding - they having
R.C. at 4.30 this P.M. At 8.
Geo. & I took Annie & Fanny to
their club - then we called on
Louis Tiffany - out - then on Mr.
Walter Brown & daughter - then
until 9.30 - to studio - cleaned
palette & at 10 went for the girls -
the Cook also there - walked
home with them & got to 41 [illegible].
Soon after 11 & Right to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 13. 1877.

Another mild, spring-like
day - Got to studio at 9.45.
Painted all day on 12×18.
"By still waters" - going over
the foliage that I painted
yesterday so crude & green.
A short walk at 1 - lunch
near 2 - Painted as long as
I could see - At 6 went out
for a walk; stopped at Atwells
& got to looking over etchings
with Roos - 7 o'clk. before I
left. R[e]d to studio & had tea.
Gusty eve'g with a spatter of
rain, then all clear. Spent
the evening in studio, writing
& arranging drawings and
etchings. At 10.30 went
to Union League Monthly
meeting - there until 11 -
on way to 41 [illegible]. caught in a
rain squall - To bed at
11.30.
