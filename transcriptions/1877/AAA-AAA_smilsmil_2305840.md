## Left Page

### July, Sunday 29, 1877

Up about 6 o'clk. got bkfst
on board the boat about
4 - Then took a hack and
Rode up Town to Grand Central
depot, stopping for a moment
at the Studios — took 9 o'clk
train & Reached Po' at 12.5.
close, overcast weather.
Drove right home. Found
all were there — Father, par-
ticularly sensitive — Before
dinner he Rehearsed to me
every agonizing detail
of Willie's death, seeming to
find a morbid Relief by so
doing — Dinner at 1.30 — Then
I lay down + read a nap
& about 4 o'clk went with
Father to Mary's — Saw her and
the Dr. — all in a very quiet mo[od] —
Father + I stayed with them to
tea — Mr. + Mrs. Jno Park'r called
in the even't. Father + I went
home at about 10 — I got to bed
about 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 30, 1877

Up soon after 6. If possible, the
weather seemed hotter & closer
than ever. Bkfst at 7. — at 7.30 at
Mary's — Aunt Jessie & Emma there also
Matt + El + Dr. Kendrick. D.K. Read
selections from Scripture, added a few
remarks & prayed. A few minutes
after 8 the body was removed to the hearse
& Father the Dr. started for Greenwood.
Many did not see the body at all and
did not know when it left the house.
To my great Relief she was exceedingly
calm. Matt, Kate & I spent the day
with her. We undertook to put away
out of sight all of Willie's things — it
was very sad work. Turning out the
contents of the pockets of his Sunday
suit we found a number of Scraps
of paper upon which he had collected,
in Roman characters, bible verses. The
first one we read was: "I will praise
thee with a harp, O thou Holy One of Israel" —
it came like a message from beyond the
grave. I wrote a letter to Emma S —
The weather cleared very warm with
light Thunder showers about. Dinner before
5 — then Kate Ret'd home — it was near 5
before she Ret'd to Mary's with Mother
& Katie — Father & the Dr. Ret'd from
up at 6 o'clk — at 6.30 we all had tea.
El came later — very passed very
quietly. We left in detachments, the
last of us going at near 10 — I got to
bed about 10.30.
