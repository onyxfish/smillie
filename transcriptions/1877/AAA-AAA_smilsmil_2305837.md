## Left Page

### July, Monday 23, 1877

More muggy close weather with fog in the river. Bkfs. at 8.
then to the Bunsby (cook's yacht) with Mr. C. +Geo. J. Little prospect of thus getting off to Greenport to day. Met a Runaway horse, big wagon + child in it — tried to stop it but failed + received too much street the o nerve to rush at the flying brute the wagon belinded [illegible] + all the chances in favor of my being knocked down + run over. At the critical moment I could not resist the involuntary recoil + the opportunity for doing a brave clever thing was lost. Fortunately the horse came to a stop in the [illegible] dock + nobody was hurt. Ab. 0. went with most of the family by bus to the Easton Beach some of them in + into the surf — back abt. 1. After 12. went with Alician + a telegram for cook to the wharf — the Bunsby [illegible] round + [illegible] — back abt. 3.30 — [illegible] awhile. Dinner at near 2.30 — at 3.30 Emma, Geo + I went to the Bunsby + sailed down the harbor. Speed wind + tide against us made it slow work — at 9 — almost a calm with swell — put about + got in to anchorage at Sunset — had dinner on board + sat on deck — beautiful evening. Bright color in the west — many vessels + their lights — reflections — until near 9 — thence ashore — at the house met Rev. Chas. J. Brooks and daughter, Mrs. Stevens. To bed abt. 10:30.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 24, 1877

Charming day. Bkfs. about 8. After that an hour of chatting + soon after 10 started off with Preston S. Prings ponies with Emma for the beach. There left Emma + drove up to Mr. Mrs. J. Richards + met George. Saw Mrs. R. Mr. R. out. Took Geo. + drove about for 3/4 of an hour — back to beach. Took Emma + drove over to Paradise [illegible]. Color + air simply wonderful. Charming views from high ground over the sea with long rounds of grain yellow + almost ripe or new [illegible] whose fragrance seemed densely distilled in the pure salt air. Got home soon after 12. Dinner about 2. Soon after 4. Mrs. J. Grace, Emma, Geo. + I took bus + rode to the end of Bellevue Ave — then walked along the cliff to Bath Road — got going out part of the way. Very little surf but grand light + air — got to the house about 7.30 — Tea above — Several callers in the evening. Among them Mr. Bigelow of N.Y. After 9. Emma + I went out + had a moonlight walk when I floundered in sentimental mine as usual — a little pleasant sotto voce singing on piazza and to bed at 10.30.
