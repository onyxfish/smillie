## Left Page

### April, Friday 20, 1877

Storm still continues — Rain fell heavily until afternoon when, at even't, it cleared with brisk wind from the west. Got to studio at 9.30 & worked under the disadvantage of a headache up on Colgate Ho. N. Side. W. French of Chicago &c. called just at lunch time, so that it was about 1.45 when I got to lunch. 41°S; back about 3 & painted until near 6 — At 6.40 went to K's to tea & took Fanny to her club. Then to N.A. to attend an Academy meeting to amend Con. The trouble growing out of dissatisfaction with Hanging com. The proceedings were very stupid & unmansly — the old fogies led & the majority followed like sheep. Part of what was done will certainly have to be undone — At 10.30 walked up with J.G. Brown & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 21, 1877

Overcast with heavy clouds — a fine clearing day after the storm. Got to studio before 9 and to work painting on Colgate No. N. Side — Haven Putnam came in & for a time we talked of a series of etchings to ill. Bryants "flood of years" — Left S. to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.30 — again Putnam came in with his boy Bayard & talked the subject of the mf. W. Phelps of Burlington [illegible] called & gave me an order to paint a horse picture like "the Gaucho" with a. [illegible] — Finished painting in & to tea at 9.30 — then at 8.30 to Renners — then at 9 to N.A. Talked "biz" with Miss Durfee and at 10 took her home — Then at 11 to studio & at 11.30 to 41°S and right to bed. .
