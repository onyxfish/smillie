## Left Page

### April, Thursday 26, 1877

Beautiful day. Bl-f.-
at 8 went to Crandal's shop
& at 9.45 Bonie Rev. R. H. Bohru
came with his old white man
& we went up on the hills &
the Rest of the with was spent
gathering wood mould and
carrying to holes in my lot —
To dinner at 1 oclk — After
2 Ben hitched up his horse
& we drove down to Scott's woods
Bohree, Wheatley & Johnston
there — dug out four small elms
& hauled them up to my lot —
set them out — very tired.
To tea at 6.30. Elisha & Carmalts
expected — did not come — Ja.
Carmalt there — [illegible]
which curtails 10. To bed
before 11.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 27, 1877

Lovely [illegible], overcast p.m.
Bl-f.- about 8.15. Took things
easy after that until about 10
when Ben drove me down
to Scott's where Wheatley &
Johnston were digging elms —
then to Youngs & engaged
him to "team" my elms up to
town — then back — stopped
at D. Smith's — he examined
my teeth & pronounced them sound
Dinner at 1 — went on the hill
and about 3.30 a load of 10 elms
and until 4.30 with men
setting them out — air chilly &
threatening rain — then to Mollie
Sayre's to tea — big boots & ale —
there until near 10 — then home
with fan — To bed at 11
oclk — exceedingly tired.
