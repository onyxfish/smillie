## Left Page

### March, Saturday 31. 1877.

Beautiful mnt. up early —
got to studio at 9. Miss Colgate
& Wash. Wilson called — It was
11 o'clk. before I got over to N. A.
Varnishing Day. only an hour &
look about — general impression —
hodge podge — not like an American
exhibit. the young men of the Munich
school come especially to the front —
the hanging generally violates tradition
& precedent. At 12.0 went out & got a
lunch — then to foot of W. 30th St. met
Mr. Abner Colgate & took 1 o'clk. train
to Riverdale — then to his father's place
and I spent an hour or so sketching
about the place — then took a walk
and enjoyed the signs of coming
Spring innumerable altho' the day had
become overcast & the air was rather
raw. Ret'd only, by 4.45 train — got in
at 42d St. at 5.15 — went into W.C. [illegible]
house — 53 E. 34th St. met Mr. C. & had a very
pleasant call — then to Studios — to tea at
41st St. at 7 — At 8.15 called on Annie Colt —
out, but saw her mother for a few minutes —
then to Mr. J.T. Williams — 18 W. 36. There
until 10.30 — then to 41st St. & to bed at
11.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 1. 1877.

Raw, bleak east wind — overcast
day — B[illegible] at 9.30 — then went to
Dr. Hastings's & heard him preach
from [illegible — ~5 words]
After service walked down to Studio
then to Manjo to dinner — Rain
came in just in time to pray —
Alpine pose with lots of Easter
bonnets & fiprn's. Between 3
& 4 o'clk p. m. walked up to Willi
& congratulated him upon his
safe return from New Orleans —
At 6.30 called on the Rogers's
23 W. 48th St. Tea about 7 or later
— visited until 9.30 — then to
41st St. & to bed soon after 10.
