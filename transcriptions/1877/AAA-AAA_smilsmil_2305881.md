## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 17, 1877

Still charming weather. I got up with a headache &
after bkfst walked until
almost 10.30, enjoying
immensely the delicious
effects of dreamy light &
color — Got transfer & tracing
& to work on etching "Cattle"
Have great trouble in seeing
& getting my point in such
condition as to work freely —
At 12 went out & walked for
½ an hour — Then in to dinner
again at etching & kept at
it until 5 — then out & walked
until 6 — then tea — at 8
with Jee called on Prof. Ritter
— not in — then on Jerry Might —
found them all on their way
out, then on Mr. Weeks, all out
but him, & &brs chatted about
his dyspepsia for ½ an hour —
then called on Mrs. Hulin —
no one in — left our cards in
the door & home at 9. Washed
brushes, Read paper and
to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 18, 1877

First half of the day exceedingly
pleasant; later, overcast & mied.
After bkfst. wrote a letter to Will and
took it to the P.O. To work at 10
finished etching — At 11.30 commun-
ced biting — Dinner at 12.45 —
Finished biting about 3.30 —
then wet paper, fixed the
press & took proofs with an
ink that I ground composed
of Asphaltum, Burnt Umber
& a trifle of Venetian Red in
addition to the lamp Black &
strong oil. The ink seemed
a good color — the etching
proved provokingly near &
yet so far from what I want.
Took at 5 mi. walk before tea
at 6 — Eveny at home —
wrote letters to Anna Cook &
Mr Yale — at 9 o'clk took them
to P.O. then to Baptis & Ch. —
walked home with Father
& Kate — To bed at 10.30
