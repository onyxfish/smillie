## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 5, 1877

Overcast half, clearing late
but not so hot as yesterday.
Got to studio's at 9 o'clk
& laid more grounds for
wood — then Diiving came
& it was noon before I
got started on painting
of "Western Herder". Replica
of "The Gaucho" for W. Phillips
of Burlington, Vt. To lunch
at 1.30 back some after 2 —
Miss Colgate called, also
several others, but I
managed to get "in" the
sky & ground. To tea
at 7.30 feeling jagged &
nervous — having taken cold.
At 9 went to Mamy's —
There until 10.30 — Then
to 41st St. & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 6, 1877

A Requlean met blanket day,
cold easterly wind with fog
hovering steeple-high from the
ground. Heavy Rain in the
early morning — Got to studio
some after 9 & to work on "Herder"
painting in the horse — S.W.
Wood came & got his plates.
To lunch at 1.15 — back at
2.30 — & painted pretty well
during the p.m. — getting my
horse in, in some respects
better than the A.B. No. one.
Mr. Colley called between
5 & 6 p.m. Heavy Rain
about 2.30. To tea at
7.20 — Went on Sofa, very
much under the weather,
nervous & out of sorts generally —
To bed at 10.
