## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 6, 1877

Clear & cold with tremendous winds — didn't think of going to dépôt to meet walking party, but at 9.30 went to studio. Douglas was there at work on frieze. Mr. Clark of [illegible] Bureau of Education Washington, spent much of the mng with me talking of statistics he has gathered concerning the Art Education of the country. At 1.20 went to Emma J's to lunch but Oscar & Miss Gordon had [illegible] to Springfield last night — Emma had gone with the Cook party, walking — back at 2m to studio & spent p.m. overlooking Douglas at work on my Dado — Evely went to tea at Hotel Royal at 8 [illegible] & so went to the Cooks — They had walked from Glenwood to beyond Sunnyside & claimed to have had a delightful day — They were evidently very tired & sleepy — so we left at 9 o'clk — we got to bed before 10. —

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 7, 1877

Charming day — as I was dressing, missed my keys — at once thought that I must have left them last night in Colvans door so I rushed down to studio but got no trace of keys — back to hotel at 8.15 & after a search found them in George's pocket. Then had b[reakfast] & got to studio at 9.30 — commenced clearing up furniture [&] moving [things] into studio — at 1.30 went out to lunch — & all of the p.m. worked very steadily getting bookcases up & cleared [things] out — most of [the] things in — at 6.30 Geo. & I went out & got tea & after that down to Century Club — there until 8.15 — then again to studio — Miss Durfee called about Phila water color exbn. [illegible] — took her home soon after 9. & I worked at putting books in case until after 10 — then wrote awhile & at 11 went to Hotel Royal — got to bed about 11.30.
