## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 11, 1877

Dull, overcast & threatening. Got to Studio's at 8.30
about 9 went out & got a
pencil sketch of a Beech
tob, then, feeling the need
of exercise I took axe &
saw & worked until near
12 — Then Rested — Dinner
at 1 — Again took pencil
& paper & tried sketching
cows in the meadow until
5 o'clk — Then climbed to
tob of Beech & cut out the
dead wood — Tea at 6.30
Every, had a talk with
Wm Cooken about building
a summer hotel. Wrote
part of letter of Emma J.
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 12, 1877

Wind S. & S.W. — mild & overcast.
So was my head for I suffered
all day with a heavy headache.
Got to studio at 9 — After 10
I went down town & the rest
was spent running around
doing up little "chores" — Dinner
at 1 — At 2.30 again at Studio.
Young Johnston chopping wood
for me. I loitered about,
read a little while & then
went on a tramp among the
woods looking for ash or
Bass wood trees to transplant.
Tea at 6.30 — Every went to
P.O. Then to Cottrell's & carried
a ladder up the hill — Then
made to farther, J.D. Lynch
& miss Leggett — Down the
hill again to the P.O. at
9.45 — To bed at 11 after a
chat with the Sayré's and
Hollingsworth's.
