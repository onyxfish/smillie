## Left Page

### January, Sunday 28, 1877

wonderful day - wet under
foot but a marvel over-head
Mrs. went to Baptist Ch.
Dinner at home about 1 -
Afternoon - in-doors - as I was
troubled with bowels - afraid
of my eyes from snow - dare &
did not wish to wet my feet
with snow-water but at 4 I
could resist no longer, so
went down to River & crossed
over on the ice — In to tea at
6 - Mr. & Mrs. Reading "The
Summer" until near 11 o'cl.
Enjoyed very much its sparkling
feminine humor. There to
bed.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 29, 1877

Still mild & wonderfully beautiful
weather. Bliss at 8. Charle
went to N.Y. in 8.35 Train — I did
not go out but finished "The Summer"
Then commenced free hand
Etching "The Farm Lane" and
stuck to it until dinner at
12.30 — Then again at it until
near 5 when I gave up and
went out for a walk — enjoyed
the effects of color very much
with the draw-back that I could
not keep my feet dry and my
throat felt very uncomfortable
Had to come in before I wanted
to. Tea at 6 - Chatting until
8 — Then up to Father's Room —
fixed light & worked on etching
until 9 — Then wrote until
10 — Most wonderful moonlight
night. To bed about 10.30.
