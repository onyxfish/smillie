## Left Page

### May, Monday 14, 1877

Very warm day — Got to studio at 9 fondly hoping to get rid of plasterers & carpenters during the w[ee]f as there remains but very little to do — but I could not get the men or if I got them they slipped away so that I could only fret & scold without making any progress. Misses Abbatt, Cumming & Jacobs spent most of the w[ee]f with us — I went to lunch at 12:45 & back at 2 — The P.M. passed as did the w[ee]f — to tea at 7 — at 9 o'clk. at Aunse Cook's 14 W. 38 St. to bid her & her mother good bye, as they start for Europe tomorrow — Quite a gathering of friends — there until 10.30 then to 41 S[t]. & to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 15, 1877

Very warm day — Got to studio about 9 and spent the morning trying to get men to do the little odds & ends of fixing plastering & carpenter work — very little to be done but almost impossible to get the men away from the other work. — On way to lunch saw the Carnival Trades Procession — a great fraud — to lunch at about 2 — on way back — in at 11a. — to studio after 3. Finally got things so far finished in my Room that I can go to work as soon as scrubbed out — to tea at 7 — Great rabble at Ross mon[?] the preparatory to Carnival Procession. At 8 went out & down 42 St. to 5th Ave & thence to Brick Church where I found a good place on the railing & so saw the procession as it passed — a very stupid, badly managed affair — immense, good-natured crowd who laughed at the whole thing — a little before 10 [illegible] to 41 S[t]. Read Post & to bed at 11.
