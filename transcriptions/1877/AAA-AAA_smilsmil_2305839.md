## Left Page

### July, Friday 27, 1877

Overcast & much cooler. After bkfst went with Emma to the Rev. Chas. L. Brooks — then to a hardware store where I bought a corkscrew which led to grave desputations & an unusual lengthening of the road home I being obliged to run over two sides of a squ[are] to head Emma off, who, in her mal[e] mind[?] ness was determined to return to the hardware store to acquire the pri[ce] of the corkscrew just bought. Argu[ment] & [illegible] was was tied so, unt[il] I leaned against a lamp post and swore that I was an unconscionable liar she would not return [illegible] with me. At the dinner Table this matter was rehearsed by Emma with much spirit and many amusing notes & comments. During p.m. made pen sketches of sitting Room to enclose in E's letter to Anna Cook. Afternoon foggy & thick — late tea. [illegible] Emma again made sport for us by telling which had occurred at the meeting of the Town & Country club where she had been — At [4?] Gel & I had gone to Mr Schrumd[?] [illegible] to call on Mrs. Tiffany — there I met Mr. James Jr who invited me to call on his daughter Miss Alice. Then Gel & I went to Ocean Ho & called on Robbin[s] sat on piazza awhile — then home. X X X To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 28, 1877

*Steamer "Bristol" Stateroom 61.*

Foggy Raw mrng. Bkfp: at 8. At this bkfp: Table Rec'd P.O. card from Father saying that Willie's symptoms were better but an hour afterwards while sitting with the family was startled by receiving a telegram saying: "Willie died at three this morning". I went at once to R.R. and Steamboat offices to enquire how I could most quickly reach home. By starting at & travelling by R.R. until 8, I could get to N.Y. then by Hudson R. R.R. at 8.30 I could get to Po. by 11 P.M. determined to go by boat as being least fatiguing — Telegraphed to Mrs. Sprues — [illegible] notes to Mrs. Tiffany — Mrs. Richards & Williams. Dinner at 2.30. After dinner Geo Emma & I walked out to the [Tur]ner House & back — Tea soon after — a little pleasant general chat — at 6.30 went to wharf "Bristol" came along about 9 — lights [illegible] got away soon along conceito[?] until after 10 — then Gel & I walked on deck & listened to the fog horns — Turned in soon after 11 o'clk.
