## Left Page

### August, Thursday 2, 1877

Beautiful fresh morning. Up early & had bkfst. at 9 —
after that took a walk with father & went to Mary's —
Back at 9.20 — wrote a letter to Maud Diven and got
my oil traps ready to go out this P.M. Dinner at 1 —
the afternoon clouded over but I went to Fox's place
& charcoaled a study of old cedar & glimpse of
the River — Ret'd. to tea at about 6 — Between 7 & 8 Ge.
& I went to Mary's — there until after 10 — then home
& to bed at 10.45.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 3, 1877

Very fine day — after bkfst. went to Mary's with Father. Spent
some time making drawings for Grub, the carpenter, for closets.
Home between 10 & 11 — worked on sketches in small sketch book
until dinner at 12.30 — About 1.30, Gilbert Pettit came and
stayed for an hour — At 3 I went to my study place and
painted in oil until after 6 — my work very unsatisfactory
because I was in no condition to do anything, my head being
unsettled — Tea at 9 — Rwell
feeling tired & depressed, did not go out — all went to Mary's
but Mother. I stayed, watered the flowers & Rec'd. W. Kendrick
his nephew, W. Wheatley. To bed at 10 o'clk. & passed a miserably
nervous, sleepless night.
