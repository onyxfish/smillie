## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 30, 1877

Yet more beautiful weather.
After bkfo. went into town, called
on Matt & bought pair of Rubbers.
Ret'd. home just in time to go
out sleigh riding with Kate &
Uncle Willie's man. Went out
to Vassar College — back at 11 &
then to work getting etching
Ready to "bite in". Dinner at
12.30 — All the P.m. until near
5 o'clk. over the acid — got
etching bitten — then out for a
walk — in to tea at 6 — Every
almost used up with head-
ache — but dressed & went up
to Mr. Williams's — not in — saw Mr.
Halley & Mr. Hull — left at 8.45 and
until 10 wandered about in the
most wonderful moonlight
ever saw — The effects over the
snow, simply magical — got
to bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 31, 1877

The myth seems to have taken
hold where the night left off —
A haze fills the air that has en-
crusted every thing & most delicate
form with a white frost that
makes the trees at a little distance
to appear like frosty plumes
out of doors is fairy-land — the
sky is violet shading into a pale
dreamy haze that shuts out all
distant objects and makes even
the near ones seem unsubstantial
& dream-like — I never saw any
thing like such atmospheric
effects — or such light & color. I
wandered about until 10 o'clk —
Then in & spent Rest of morning
proving etching — Dinner at
12.30 — Afterward — on new ground
more etching on plate — got it
Ready for the acid & at 4.45
went out with father & paddled
around until near 6 — then in
to tea — Evnf. Mr. Williams
& Jenny Wright called — 8 to 9 —
Then I did some reading
"Mr. J. A. Adamsforeache from
Newba and got to bed about
10.30.
