## Left Page

### June, Saturday 23, 1877

Clear, delightfully cool day. Got to Studio at 9.15 & to work on A.B.N. Co. drawing "Foot of the Shaft — Coal mine". Washing in effect. W. Shurtliff of Springfield made a very pleasant little call. At 1.15 went to lunch — back at 2.30 — very much bothered with my head, but managed to get my drawing about done. Miss Leggett called with a Poem by Whittier that she wishes me to make some illustrations for. To tea about 7.30 — it was raining when I called on Mr. J.S. Williams — 18 W. 36 S. Long chat — until 10.30 — gave orders for stuff for Studio floor — To 41 S. & to bed at 11. [illegible — ~3 words] bed [illegible]

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 24, 1877

Very pleasant day — growing warmer. — Blcp. abg and about 10.30 — after calling at Mamp's, went to Studio — there all the m'f. To Mamp's to dinner at 1 and again to Studio about 3, fussing & getting things together preparing to packing — at 6 went in to Emma J.'s. Kittep there — also Messrs Collins, Howe & —, all at table — sat down & took tea on verandah until 8 — then to Studio & wrote some notes — at 10.30 went to 41 S. & Right to bed.
