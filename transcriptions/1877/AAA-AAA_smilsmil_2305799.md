## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 2, 1877

Fine day, but windy & quite cold. Got to studio's at 9 o'clk & to work on Colgate pictures painting odds & ends about the buildings that had just been done before — To lunch at 1.30 — afr that. Rode down to Deweis — Boway near 4 — & ordered Spring overcoat. Got to studio's near 5 & found Mr. Geo. Whitney & Miss Mary there, worked until 5 — then cleared up studio's — did not finish until near 7 — then to tea & back at 8.5 & found several waiting for me — the first men for the etching club — 17 men invited — 16 came — Gifford etched a little plate, bit in in Yale proved clr and then we organized a club — all hands Falcing hard with enthusiasm — I acted as temporary chairmman — constitution was adopted — then I ab pointed — Perry Wood & Robbins com. on nominations — They Reported L.M. Yale for Pres. J.D.S. for Ind. Secy & Wro. Gifford & Baldwin for Council — Unanimously elected and at 11.15 adjourned — Told S. & to bed at 11.40

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 3, 1877

Still windy & cool, wonderful blue overhead — Got to Studio's before 9 — Miss Hobart & brother called & during the rest of with continuous calls — W. Whitney came & invited me to lunch with him & Miss Mary at the Albemarle at 1 — went there — back at 2 — Mr. Vail came (11) Mr. C. — came at 5 — too late for a lesson — I painted on Chili Arms for A.B.N. Co's — At 6.30 went to tea to K's — at 8. took Fannie & Anna to Chickering Hall, then to Kurtz Gallery & saw Walter Brown's pictures — then to H.a. & at 9 to Studio — wrote until 10 (a letter to Ferguson) then told a for a moment got to Chickering Hall again at 10.15 and for 3⁄4 of an hour listened to Rehearsal of pieces for to-morrow evef. — Then took Fanny & Annie to their Respective homes & to 41. 1 S. & to bed at 11.40 [illegible — ~2 lines]
