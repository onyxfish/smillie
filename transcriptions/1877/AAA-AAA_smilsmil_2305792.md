## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 18. 1877.

Rain commenced during the forenoon & continued heavily with little intermission all day & night. Got to studio about 9 & to work finishing the "painting in" of Colgate's ho. S. Side. W. French of Chicago &c. called. — At 1.30 went out & got some oysters, then to Kurz gallery & saw Claghorn pictures back at 3 (after a short call at Robbins Studio) & painted until 6 — To tea at 7 Every part of it spent on sofa reading — the Remainder trying to think out something of a plan of illustrations for Bryant's Flood of years — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 19. 1877.

Heavy Rain storm continued all day & night. Got to studio at 4.30 & commenced painting on Colgate ho. N. Side, but had several interruptions — worked until nearly 1.30 before I went to 41. S. to lunch — Back at 2.30 & found both Colgate & Rail waiting for me — I gave them lessons. C. (16) + V. (10) while I did some work on my painting. Got to tea shortly after 4 & back to studio at 8.45 — worked on a charcoal ill. for "the Road of Years" (The Eagle eyed artist) until 10.30 — After 11 when I got to 41 o. S. & right to bed.
