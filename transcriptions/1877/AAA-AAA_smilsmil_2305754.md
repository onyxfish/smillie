## Left Page

### January, Monday 8, 1877

Overcast & tried — Streets in a horrible condition — a spatter of rain at 6 — later, cold west winds. Got to Studio at 9.30 — myself spent working over all of my W.C. more or less & getting them finally fixed in their frames. Hef Harner & then — Huffington called — to dinner at 1.30 — back at 2.30 & continued work of the m'f. Clarence Sterling & several others called — got to tea at 6.30 very tired & then learned that mother came to the city this P.M. at 8 went to Mary's & saw her — to Studio at 9.20 — there until 10.15 — then to 41 ÷ 8. ## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 9, 1877

Splendid winter day. Got to Studio at 9.30 — very cold. Stove choked with dirt — lighted fire in the grate — Sterling came & my self until after 11 was lost. Finally got to work on "The Truants" To lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.20 & worked as long as I could see — Mother & get came in — also 9 p° Haven Putnam & others. At 4.45 went down to Avery's — Palmer, the sculptor there — to tea until 1.8. at 6.30 — At 8 called on Chas. Smith, 25 m'f — not in — then to Mary's — saw mother — then to Studio at 8.45 — there until 10.15 — then to 41.° S. & to bed at 10½ [illegible — ~4 words]
