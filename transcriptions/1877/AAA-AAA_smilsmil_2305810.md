## Left Page

### May, Saturday 26. 1877.

Aprie like weather — cool &
crisp with beautiful clouds &
sudden showers — Got to studio
at 9 o'clk. To work on Colgate
picture, painting over the house.
Very much interfered with by
callers & carpenters — To lunch
at 1 — back at 2.30 & at 4 went
to Madison Sq. to see Coaching
club Procession — great crowd
& very gay in spite of showers —
went to Kurtz's & saw the street
from his windows — Then in & saw
Moran's big picture "The Nations
of the Earth Paying homage to Liberty"
Back to studio at 5 — found W. Colby
waiting for me — wanting to see the
Colgate pictures — seemed very
much pleased. — To tea at 7 —
at 8.20 at Emma J's — then for a
few minutes at H.A. — then to studio
& wrote until after 10 — then to 41st
& to bed at 1. Father was in town
for a few minutes this mng — did not see
him — Geo. went to Pa. at 3 p.m.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 27. 1877.

Splendid day — Up at 8.30. Breakfast
at 9.30 & at 10.30 went to 42d St. &
9th Ave. & took at 10.40 Elevated
R.R. to the Battery — got there at
11.5 — Too late for North Shore boat —
so, was just able to catch Quarantine boat — got to Quarantine at
11.30 & walked over to New Brighton
enjoying the walk exceedingly —
got to G.W. Curtis's church a few
minutes after 12 — just in time for
the last hymn — walked home
with the Jessups & wandered about
looking at the new place. Chatted
& shown while at dinner
until dinner, about 1.30 — After that
all went out & sauntered until
near 5 o'clk. At 6, walked with
W.J. down to N.B. landing & took 6.15
boat to the city — then R.R. to 42d St.
at 7.30 was in 41st St. begging for
something to eat — about 9 went
to Mary's — there until almost
11 — then to 41st St. & to bed.
