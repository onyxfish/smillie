## Left Page

### March, Friday 9, 1877

Regular mixture of March winds & Mo. Sunshine and showers — feeling altogether used up did not get to studio until 10 o'clk — first going to N.A. to see the Evans' coll. of pictures — Did some pencil work & rec'd callers until 1 — then to lunch & at 2.30 at Phil. Rehearsal — then to Stewart's — then to Studio — Nicole came — Talked Society matters until 6.15 — at 6.30 to Kimball's to tea — at 8 took Anna & Fanny to their club. Thence to Studio to write. At 10.15 to 41 S. & to bed before 11 — Clear, frosty night. [illegible — ~4 words] [illegible — ~3 words] [illegible — ~5 words]

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 10, 1877

Clear, cold and windy — Got to Studio at 9.30 after going to Manip — seeing Charl about "biz" — then walked down with Geo. The m'f was spent making sketches in an album for D'. Yale and in making changes on an old W.C. drawing "On the Saco" — Having scrubbed out about half of it, I am putting in new groups of trees and cows. To Anna Cook's (14 W. 38) to lunch at 1.30 — There until 2.15 Miss Watson of Buffalo lunched with us. P.m. worked on "Saco" until near 6. Mr. S. Coleman & two ladies called — then to 41 S. to tea. Went at 8 at Steinway Hall — Thomas' Symphony concert: Enjoyed "Mr. Raede", Raff — very much — Walked up home with Geo. & Anna Cook — To bed at 11.30 —

Charl went to Po. this p.m.
