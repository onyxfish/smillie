## Left Page

### September, Thursday 13, 1877

Foggy morning — then overcast —
clearing about noon. Johnston
boys spent the m'f chopping
wood & digging about for me
while I dug up & transplanted
a lot of horse chestnut plants
that I planted last fall —
Dinner at 1 — the Comstocks
left for home this m'f. After
dinner Mr Sam. Sayre & I
drove over to his farm to look
for trees to transplant — found
very fine Ash, Basswood & Maples
Back to Studio at 3.30 — Rested
awhile & at 4.30 went out &
until 6.30 transplanting Horse
Chestnuts etc — then [illegible] —
Went, washed & dressed & went
down to Faris — who got me
this m'f. with the Dr. Then
until after 10. Got to bed
a letter before 11.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 14, 1877

Beautiful day, but very warm.
To studio at 8.40 — Blacksmith wait-
ing for me & until 12 o'clk. with
him superintending putting
up wires on South side of building
for vines to run upon — Dinner
at 1. At Studio at 2.30 — Sore and
tired & feeling so altogether un-
like work that I could not pluck
up pluck enough to get about —
so did little things about until
after 4 — then again transplanting
young Horse Chestnuts & watering
[illegible] [illegible] beautiful twilight —
trees until 7 when I went to
tea completely tired out. After
tea Rested awhile, then a sponge
bath. Showed my painting for Faris
to the Sayres — then to Faris — Elisha,
Mr. Dan's wife & [illegible] there all
directing cards until after 10 —
then Elisha, Dr. Peen & I had
a game of whist — To bed at
11.30 — the Hollingsworth left
for home this noon.
