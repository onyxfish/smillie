## Left Page

### November, Friday 2, 1877

Exceeding dismal day — Tornado of wind with torrents of Rain. Got to studio about 9.30. Tried to concentrate my attention upon charcoal sketching of motives for pictures but thoughts much divided by man who was at work up on my floor. Mr. Coleman very kindly invited us to lunch with him which we did, it saved us from going out in the storm. To work again about 3. Miss Abbatt made her first regular appearance for the winter. About 5.30 — Rain still pouring — Ge. & I went to the Kimball's & invited ourselves to tea — Spent a very pleasant eve. Anna Cook & Walter & his wife came in & made a visit — We left soon after 10 — To bed at 10.45 — High wind & clear sky.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 3, 1877

Cold & clear with much wind — like a March day. Got to studio soon after 9 & to work on charcoal sketch of a subject for Walter Cook drawing. Went out with Ge. for lunch about 1 o'clk and returning afternoon again at work on charcoal sketch. Left, about 5 — went in to the Ireland's & took tea. At 7.30 went with Emma to Thomas's first Symphony Concert at Steinway's — Splendid concert — enjoyed it very much — at 10.30 walked home with Emma — the Cooks — Mr. Walter Brown & Miss Warren B. accompanying to cor. of 25 & 8 — then at 11, to Century Club — monthly meeting — there until after midnight — quite a brilliant gathering of guests — then walked up to Hotel Royal & got to bed before 1.
