## Left Page

### March, Thursday 15, 1877

Bright, blustering, cold day —
At 9 called on Thos. Hardy — No.
52 W 38 S; & criticised a crayon
head he has done. Got to studio
at 9.30 and spent most of the
remainder of the w[ee]k. fussing
with the sky of Insbrant "Bruges"
drawing belonging to Gurnee.
To lunch at 1.15 — back at 2, just
in time for Miss Boyd who took
her first lesson of me. Soon
after W. Nail came & he took
his first W.C. lesson of me and
Mr. Colgate took his M[?] in pencil.
To tea at 7 — back to studio at
9. Cleared up things &
at 10.20 to 11. S— Got
to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 16, 1877

Unpleasant mg. Got to
studio soon after 9. Made sketch
from T. W. Smith's Academy picture
for ill. cat, will lithog ralphic
crayon — then made at mall
[illegible] from my "Study" — also for cat.
Snow began falling soon after
noon. To lunch at 1.15 — back at
2.30 — gave Phil. Rehearsal Ticket
to Geo. & I fussed over figure in
Study — About 4.30 Nicoll,
J. G. Brown, Bellows, Robbins, Smits
& Coleman came & we had a
B[oard] of Control Meeting. To tea at
6.45 or later — The meanest
possible storm of wet snow &
wind — Made up my mind
not to go to Miss Arnold's Choral
Club concert in lecture Room of
Church of Messiah, but stayed
in & wrote part of Report for
W. C. Soc[iet]y. To bed about 10.30
