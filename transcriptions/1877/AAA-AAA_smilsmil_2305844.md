## Left Page

### August, Monday 6, 1877

Dr. Hasbrouck —
Stateroom 46 —

Still charming weather but warmer than yesterday — at 8 went down to St. Hasbrouck & secured Stateroom — then to Matts & then to Mamy's — Dr. went to N.Y. this a.m. to be back this p.m. Home at 10 & did something at packing my trunk — Uncle Willie, Aunt Jessie & Emma called between 12 & 1 — Dinner about 1.30 — then finished packing trunk.
went to Mamy's at about 4, Came Hertzel there — Stayed until after 5 — then home — Tea at 6 — at 6.25 Geo. & I bid the folks good bye — walked down to the propeller Hasbrouck — Enjoyed the evening on deck until after leaving Newby — about 10.30 — then turned in — Too hot in our stateroom to sleep —

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 7, 1877

Got up before 6 — Bkft. at 6.30 & soon after started for foot of Barclay St. — Wf thick & muggy — Had to wait an hour before time of starting of D.L. & W. Train — 8.20 — W. Gilman, his wife, nurse & 3 children came along — W.G. left his family in our charge. Got to Scranton soon after 2 — no signs of disturbance beyond some soldiers in a train of cars on a siding & a great number of locomotives & coal cars on the great R.R. Plaza — The weather had cleared but [illegible] very hot — got to Montrose Station at 3.30 & to village at 5.30 — went at once to Mr. Sayre's — washed up — had tea soon after 6 — then went on the hill to look at my potato patch & trees. Called on Flora S., Uncle W. & aunt H., then down to Fran W's — quite a little party of friends there — note tofattist way — took Priscilla P. home, then on the hill & to bed soon after 10.
