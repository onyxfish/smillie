## Left Page

### Tues. June 19, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Brownell's left for Elmira.

Much cooler. Showers continued thro' the m't. Afternoon, much clearer, with light showers about \- I worked awhile in studio putting up curtains etc \- then downtown \-back before noon\- The stage came for the B's at noon\- I went over to station \+ saw them off \- Dinner after 1 \- much of my time taken in planning, with the plumber for the work in the house to commence to-morrow\. Down-town at about 5 \- Tea before 7 \- a quiet eve't [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### Wed. June 20, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of showers, \+ much cooler. Some of the showers were heavy. Plumbers got started on the in-door work \- an Italian got to work digging a tunnel under the house for Water pipe\. I wrote out a copy of my letter concerning necessity of a sewer system in Montrose \+ took it to Crouser at the "Democrat" office\. After 12 when I got home \- Afternoon with the plumbers for some time \- Wrote another Sewer letter \+ took it to Taylor of the "Independent Republican"\. He \+ Crouser seem to think that I am trying to work up a scare \- Then to Newcomes after my new awnings for Studio Bay Window\. Tea before 7 \- Eve't reading newspaper.
