## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 14, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ratification by N.A's of sale of N.A. of D. to Met. Life Ins. Co.

Dismal m'g. Rain until about 11, then slowly clearing — Splendid, cloudless moonlight night — After bkfp. Took R. to school \+ attended to home matters — then to N.A. \+ arranged things there for the meeting to-night. Then down-town — Saw Mr. VanPelt with reference to leasing the N.A. bldg. from Met. Life Ins. Co. for a year or two — then to Lee \+ Lee's — \+ spent an hour or more there while Mr. Lee drew up the Resolution to be passed by the N.A.s, Ratifying the informal sale, transacted by me last Sept. also, some other resolutions — then home at a little after 2 — It was after 3 when I had my lunch — Then I took a good rest \+ nap \+ got up feeling very fresh after 5 o'clk — Dinner — \+ at 8 went to N.A. — a very full meeting — Read to them the Report that I sent to the Council — It was very well Rec'd \+ promptly approved — Then I presented the Resolution of Ratification — Whittredge made a long, rambling speech in opposition \+ Mrs. Moran endorsed his opposition, but when it came to a vote, every man, except W. — voted to Ratify — After that there was some discussion concerning a lease from the Met. Life — \+ very generally about sites — After that a vote of thanks was given to me very heartily, in recognition for the work I had done — After adjournment I Rec'd many congratulations — It was 11.30 when I got home \+ just midnight when I got to bed —

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 15, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — got up feeling very well but rather tired — After bkfp. went to school with R —, to butcher's \+ to grocer's — then wrote notes to Lee — VanPelt \+ to Katie Throops. Made diagram \+ measurements of dining room floor — then with Annie to a Wood carpeting place in 5th Ave — \+ gave an order — Then we walked down to 23d. St. \+ I called upon W. Kirby of Am. Art Ass'n. to talk with him about a possible arrangement with the A.A.A. by which the N.A. of D. might hold a Spring Exh'n in their galleries. Then to N.A. \+ home at 1.45 — had lunch — at 2.45 went with Annie to W. 120th St. to attend a public opening of the Horace Mann School \+ Teachers College — we were crowded out of the hall where the addresses were made \+ heard very little but we made a complete tour of the buildings and were very much pleased \+ interested — It was getting dusky when we left — home at about 6 — Dinner before 7 — Evng. — both A. \+ self quite tired — so, we got to bed early —
