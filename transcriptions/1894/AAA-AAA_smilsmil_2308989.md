## Left Page

### Fri. June 1, 1894

**Weather:** Clouded m'g, \+ cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

cleaner afternoon showers in the eve'g. Attended to home duties after bkfs. Then ret'd to the house \+ spent the m'g clearing up in Nursery packing my things, some for Montrose the others to remain in the house. Lunch at 1 \- at about 4, with R \- I went to Geo. Schard's in Wooster St. near Spring, taking etched plates, "Rough Sport", "My Col? Neighbor's Hen Roost" \+ "Marblehead Neck Causeway" to be ground off. Then to Fifth Ave. Hotel \+ to Park Ave. Hotel \+ made enquiries of the gas men concerning governors such as Mr. Bucklin \+ Con. Gas. Co \- offers to put in the metres of the N.A.f.D. Testimony of both very satisfactory \- Then home. Dinner at 6.30 \- Cous't Charl came \+ took Kate out \- I wrote letter to Herrick \+ after that read some time.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 2, 1894

**Weather:** Pleasant m'g, clouding over \+ becoming  **Temp:** [illegible]

showery, going into steady rain at night. After bkfs. to butcher \+ grocer \- Then to N.A. \+ then to 2nd Nat. Bk. where I arranged A's business for the summer \- deposited $1200 ch. for her \+ paid a bank loan $500 made last Jan'y. Home to lunch at 1 \- After that A \- Nellie \+ Katie Adams went to see a spectacle "Cinderella" \- \+ at 3 Kate left for Po'k. I podged over my things, especially in getting clear in my mind, the details of the pole-rocker castings that I got of W. James in England \- I now am going to have them finished. Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g at 9 went to Century Club monthly meeting \- not many out \+ I was not in condition to enjoy it, so, after reading awhile I left for home at 10.30.
