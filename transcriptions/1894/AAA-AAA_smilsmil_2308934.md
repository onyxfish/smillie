## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 11, 1894

**Weather:** Bright, breezy \+ cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, breezy \+ cold. It was after 11 that I started out for my walk. went to Century Club \- met Geo. W. Maynard \+ had a chat with him. then on up to Central Park \+ walked up to Met. Mus. \+ took a hasty look through. Home to dinner at 2. after dinner Annie \+ I went to N.A. of D. \+ as long as there was daylight we looked at the W. C.'s \+ Etchings. then home \+ I had a short rest before tea. Geo. was waiting for us when we came in. Evely Cha. came in. I went to Ed. Cook's \+ saw him for a few minutes on the subject of Kansas Farm Mtgs., Central Trust Co. of S. Louis, Mo., having rec'd. yesterday from H. Sprague, giving the latest information concerning the Mtgs that Annie holds. The information, though not encouraging is not altogether hopeless.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 12, 1894

**Weather:** A cold, raw [illegible] with an old fashioned snow storm before night  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold, raw [illegible] with an old fashioned snow storm before night. went with R. to school \- then to grocer's etc. 3rd Ave. \+ then down town to Carter Ledyard's \- 54 Wall. \+ had an interview concerning the loan on Hasting's estate to U.S. of D. Lawyer insists upon having his form of authority for loan from the U.S. \- then home \- got ready for work again on Morgan Head \- went for R. \- lunch at 1 \- afternoon worked on head until 5 \- then had a nap before dinner \- Before 8, dressed for costume \+ walked to N.A. Council meeting. I spent some time in explaining lawyer's position \+ that we must yield \+ have another resolution passed at the next Quarterly meeting. I left meeting before adjournment \+ walked home.

Morganhead, 3 h. [illegible]
