## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 14, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull in the early m't. weather soon cleared — hazy, mild, quiet day. Awoke with severe headache — did not get up until 11 o'clk. Took no breakfast. Annie went to Church & heard old Dr. Furness of Phila. 92 y's old - new sermon prepared for this occasion — she was very much pleased — About 12.30 Annie, Kate, Eugene Atwater, Katherine Cook, Jim, Ralph & I went to the Century where we met Mr Chamboney's & others — then home in time for dinner at 2 o'clk. Gene A. stayed to dinner with us. I loafed about during the afternoon, had improving & got a little nap. Ge. came in for a few minutes just before 6. Tea at 6.30. Eve't Charl came in & took Kate to his rooms to look at photos.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 15, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day — damp & raw, with rain in the afternoon & eve't. Took R. to school. Then I went to N.A. of D. & from there to 2° Nat. Bk. where I went through the preliminaries of arranging an N.A. of D. loan. By the way, just went to J. P. Morgan's & had a brief & satisfactory interview with him — Then to W. Fred't. Sturges. 36 Park Ave, not in — After getting thro' at the bank I went to Avery's gallery & saw the T. H. Smith Venetian Sketches — wonderfully clever — while there Annie & Kate came in — then home — lunch at 3.30 Kate Ret'd to Po'K — I fussed about until 5 — then had as hot rest & at 6 went to Mr. Sturges'. — Pleasantly rec'd by him — we had quite a chat about old Mr. Durand & he showed me some very interesting early art works — Dinner at 6.30 — Eve't Annie went with Ed. & Mary to a reception at Robt. Collyer's house to old Dr. Furness — I went to Council meeting where financial matters mainly were discussed. I got away about 10.30 — home to bed soon after 11.
