## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 22, 1894

**Weather:** Very pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day. while at bkf't — the new stuff for dining room floor [illegible] came — The work of laying the floor was commenced at once & I was delayed by watching them, so that I got to studio late. Until after 12 I worked at cleaning out drawers & replaceing their contents. Then to 2°. Nat. Bk. On my way home met Dr. Carroll Dunham & he gave me a letter (or card) introducing me to his bro Dr. Ed[w]d. after lunch, again to 2°. Nat. Bk. then to Lord & Taylors — & to studio where I continued the work of the m/f as long as I could see. Home at 6. found Katie Herods there, just down from Po'k. Ev'g. with the Dec. Harper & the Nation.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 23, 1894

**Weather:** A nasty, raw day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, raw day. After getting thro' with my m/f entries I went to the Carnegie laboratory — S. 26 St. near Bellevue Hospital, & saw Dr. Ed[w]d. Dunham to learn from him what I should read to inform myself on certain matters that must be discussed in contending for sewers in Montrose — then to studio. Home to lunch — back to studio at 2.30. A woman came soon after & spent most of [illegible] p.m. (that is, 2 hours.) blocking up my cases of drawers, etc, so as to stand even — the floor being much inclined from true level. Annie & Katie T. came in for a few minutes. Ev'g. Charl came to the house
