## Left Page

### Thur. May 24, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At Sue Cook's \- 71 Appleton S\-
Cambridge, mass.

A very nasty, raw, wet day \- Bkfp \- at usual time. I spent the rest writing a number of notes \+ picking up things. Yale came in \+ spent some time with me. Lunch at a little before 1. At 1.30 went to G.C. Station \+ off on 2 o'clk Train for Boston \- changed cars at New Haven, waiting 20 m. for the Washington Syp \- to which I changed \- very heavy rain. The new Train, a very comfortable me, new cars \+ vestibuted. Heavy rain \+ fog all of the way \- got in to Providence Depot, Boston at 8.30 \- just missed a Trolley car to Cambridge \+ had to wait 10 m. for another me \- At Harvard Sy. had to wait 15 m \- for a car out to Appleton S\- \+ at last, just before 10, I got to Sue's, pretty cold \+ cross \- Annie waiting for me \+ soon recovered my good temper before a fine as and wood fire where for some time I Toasted my Toes \- then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 25, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, sulky day with a little rain at night \- Bkfp \- at 7.30 \- I felt the bad effects somewhat of my exposures and discomforts yesterday but soon after 9. I got off \+ at 10 I was at the Museum. There I found Köhler \+ his daughter. I spent the rest with him looking over a number of new acquisitions of prints just secured by him for the Gray \+ museum collections. I met Vinton on my way to the Museum. After 1 o'clk. K. \+ I went out to lunch \+ after that we went into the Public Library. Beef \+ all that \- I found it very interesting, altho' still in a very chaotic state. Then to Museum \+ there we spent some time in looking at the exhibit of the Art Students, none of which, I think, comes up to the work of the N.A. of D. students \- we sat in the galleries until nearly \- Then I returned to Cambridge \+ got in just in time for tea \- a very hearty meal \- Evening, just very tired \+ satisfied \+ got to bed before 10.
