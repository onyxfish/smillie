## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day \- routed out of bed in the mrg, by a disturbance some distance below the belt which left me so rattled that I took my sleep. in bed about 10 I got up but then developed such a headache that I was useless all day \- By careful nursing I was in condition to go to Council meeting in the eveg. a full meeting \+ interesting in preparation for the Special meeting of N.A.'s for the Ratification of sale to-morrow eveg. I got home a little after 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 13, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Storm predicted, but it did not come \- mrg clouded \+ raw, but the p.m. was sunny \+ bright. I went to school with Ralph, who is out of sorts \- furred tongue, swollen tonsils \+ temp. above normal. Then back \+ wrote notes \+ letters until 11 \- then to grocers \+ butchers \+ to 2° Nat. Bk. where I met Annie \- we then went to Lord \+ Taylor's \+ selected a new carpet for dining room \- then to Wood carpet place, but made no selections \- Home, much before 2 \- Much time in what remained of p.m. given to the study of the 100 sites offered by brokers to the N.A. \- Annie dined at Elw[illegible] Cook's \+ with them went to 19° Cent Y. Clb. meeting. I spent much of the eveg with R. who is heavy \+ feverish \- Jack Downs came in for a little while \- to sail to-morrow for study in Paris. I worked again on N.A. sites \-
