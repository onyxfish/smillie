## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 26, 1894

**Weather:** Clear m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear m'f. soon clouded over, wind from E. raw & cold — snowstorm at night — It was about 11 when I got to studio — Real Estate broker came in to straighten a broker's dispute over the S. Luke's Hospital property on 5th Ave. I told him that the dispute did not interest me as it was altogether improbable that he'd, would think of the property as a building site. Then I spent what was left of the m'f. in cutting a mount & getting the Coney drawing in it. To lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — more Real Estate brokers — two of them — I managed to complete mount on Coney drawing & did a little towards assorting & putting in place my cat. files and pamphlets. It was nearly 6 when I got home — Katie Adams at he house — come down from New Haven for a Theatre party. Eleanor Cook transferred herself to Walter Cook's in the afternoon — So Katie A. takes her room. Robt. Cook came to dinner. K's friend came & took her out — I worked at pasting Broker's letters & diagrams of sites in a scrap book that I have for this purpose.

Storm continued during the night. High wind & rain instead of snow before the m'f.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 27, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty m'f. very dark & raining — 4 or 5 inches of snow fell during the night. The weather cleared suddenly in the afternoon, turning very cold & continuing clean & cold during the night. I did not go out during the m'f. but spent the most of it writing a long letter to Mr. Koehler — I worked upon Site con. Scrap Book most of the afternoon arranging the letters & diagrams & pasting them in their order in the book. About 4.30 I walked up 5th Ave. to Central Park & back. Katie Adams Ret. to New Haven. Dinner at 6.30. Suet. we had tho't of going to the Private View of the Inness Paintings — 57th St. galleries — Park Godwin to make an address — Music — but it was too cold — I worked again upon diagram scrap book. Chas. Collins came in & spent the ev'g with us.
