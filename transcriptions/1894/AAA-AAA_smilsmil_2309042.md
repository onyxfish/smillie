## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 15, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate left us to go to Buffalo.

Thick mist in the early m'g. clearing to a very hot, humid day \+ a brilliant, pleasant moonlight night. I was engaged for some time after bkfst. by my duties, \+ then Miss Vastee, of Brooklyn, Pa., came with a lot of her (paintings) for me to criticise \- a hopeless case. Then by m'g mail I rec'd. letter from Lauryell M'r Lee, who sent me advice as to form of letter to M'. Geo. Read \- then I went to look after Vecchami who was digging drains in swamps \+ White, who, with a team was hauling stone to Chapel Hall \- a kicking mare \- could not be controlled \- could not get the work done \- Then, at 12 o'clk. we all went over to Station with Kate \+ started her off on her visit to Miss Brunk at Buffalo. Then I went to see Burgess \- not in \- then dinner. P. M. I wrote, under Mr. Lee's advice to Geo. Read, suggesting that he make an offer for the n.a. as he had refused mine \- \+ further suggesting that $65000 would be accepted. Wrote short note to Nicole \+ to Robbins \- went downtown after 4 \- Then worked in garden, transplanting lettuce until 6.30 \- went to Burgess \+ arranged to get some top earth from him \- Then Tea at 7 \- Eve. reading news paper until 8.30 \- then walking piazza \- \+ dozing while Rolled in a big blanket, until 10\ \-

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 16, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy thunderstorm at 12.30 last night and showers in the early m't. \- cloudy day with occasional gleams of scalding hot sunshine \- about 4 P. m. heavy thunderstorms following one another \- tremendous fall of water \- rain until nearg o'clk. Bkfst. after 9 \- the rest of the m'g. transplanting lettuce \+ other garden work \- Gretta \+ Katie (waitress \+ cook) went off to spend the day at Gretta's home, about 14 miles away. Mrs. Cleany was called in to prepare dinner for us. The storms in the afternoon were so furious that the new croquet ground, so beautifully graded \+ just seeded, were washed \+ gullied so that much of the work will have to be done over again. Between 6 \+ 7 P. m. while Annie was preparing our evening meal, \+ while it was still raining very hard, Gretta \+ Katie came home, none the worse for the storm. Tea at 7 \- Eve. Annie read to the children until their bed time. \- Then I tried to read, but after a while I gave it up \- too tired \- \+ went to bed.
