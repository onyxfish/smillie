## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal, dark day & nothing was done but mope about the house with now & then a look at the window at the constantly falling rain. Bl.fo. late — after 9. My bath & grooming pretty well used me up in the m'g. but I got around all right later in the day. Robt. Cook dined with us.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 3, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright at last, much to my joy — after marketing I went to W. Jno. Bigelow's 21 Gramercy Park — found him at home & had along &, to me, satisfactory inter-view with him concerning the attitude the Tilden Library Trust towards [illegible] field's scheme of a site on the ground now occupied by the old Reservoir at Bryant's Park for the Tilden Library & hall &c. He encourages me to think that the scheme is not only practicable but that it may be an accomplished fact with-in a reasonably short time if we all pull together harmoniously & wisely. Then I went to the studio — there until 12.30 then home — back at 2.30 & found that S. R. Koehler had been there during my absence. At the studio until nearly 5 — Then home and had a rest before dinner. — at 8 I went to N.A.D. Council meeting & took considerable time explaining to the other members what I had heard & learned from Mr. Bigelow in the m'g. The meeting was a full one & every member was evidently much interested. After getting thro' with my part I left for home a little before 10.
