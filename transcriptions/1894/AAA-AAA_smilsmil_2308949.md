## Left Page

### Tues. March 13, 1894

**Weather:** Bright during the mtg, but afternoon clouded over \+ at night there was rain.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Potged about during the mtg. doing little of importance — In the afternoon I went to Kurty's with W. C. drawing "Cathedral Tower, Ravello" to get photo — then to Knoedler's \+ saw him — then to N.A. but Farrington had to go out \+ could not give to me the time I wanted — Then to Linner \+ Munns \+ bot. pestle \+ Mortar \+ some Benzole — then home. Self, Emma J. — Geo. and Emily Cook came to dinner at 7 — a quiet, altogether informal, family dinner — I was uncomfortable \+ stupid under the effects of my sore throat \+ general cold.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 14, 1894

**Weather:** Bright \+ crisp, but with much wind \+ dirt.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got up with a very sore throat \+ head — pretty sore all over and disgusted because above all things I wished to be in good working trim for the meeting of N.A.'s at the Academy to-night. Went with R. to school — then to J. Pierport Morgan's \+ saw him — has but one criticism — unimportant \+ easily acceded to — says the etching is a good one \+ wants to know if I can't have a proof taken for him in a sort of greenish ink — Then I went to N.A. \+ got some statistics and figures from the books of acct — then home — lunch — From that time until near 5 I wrote very steadily upon a paper considering the financial condition of the N.A. — its prospects \+ its needs — urging a policy that shall find life \+ strength in its schools \+ the abandonment of the present building when we can get a large price for it \+ the erection of more commodious, satisfactory \+ cheaper building — Jale came in at the close of the afternoon \+ said that he did not think I would be running any great risk in going to the Academy Meeting — upon which I felt strengthened — Dinner at 6.30 \+ as soon as I could after that, I got away — An unusually full \+ spirited meeting — In arranging for the Good Room — it was voted to raise $8000. Then it was agreed to make the annual dinner a subscription one — Then I read my paper — which R-c- exceedingly respectful attention \+ at the end was roundly applauded — My Resolution at the close, asking the appointment by the Pres. of a "Com on Site" was unanimously passed — After adjournment I was very warmly congratulated by old fellows \+ young fellows \+ by Conservatives \+ Radicals, until I began to feel quite proud. This is the beginning of a long \+ very trying campaign — I hope that I may have strength enough to get it fairly started, then others can carry it on.
