## Left Page

### Mon. May 14, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up before 6. Bright mrg. but soon overcast, chilly wind. Bkfp. before 7. Annie Ralph \+ Edna started for Phila. over the D.L.\+W. R.R. at 7.45 — I went to station with them — then down-town — then back to the house where Gill and an assistant were at work setting up the frame of tank at S. end of new barn. I scurried around getting everything in shape to leave \+ at 12 o'clk. I got away for N.Y. — 40 m. wait at Belford — then a milk train that took 2 h. 10 m. to run to Scranton, 35 miles — then at 3.50 took [illegible] getting to foot of Christopher St. at 8 o'clk. Car to 4th Ave. — stopped at N.A. of D. Council meeting — there until near 11 — then home — door on the latch for me — to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 15, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, superb day — found Kate was housekeeper — bkfst. at 8.30. I felt tired \+ took things easily in the mrg. — went to butcher's \+ grocer's at 10.30 \+ then to N.A. — just too late to meet Nicoll. Spent some time with Walcott \+ Meth[illegible] in the etching room. Home to lunch at 1 — then spent some time at getting things cleared up in my working room. At 4.30 I walked up to 57th S. Students L. — did not see Fogarty, — left a note for him — but I saw Mr. Janvier there. There in the galleries to see the groups \+ for — especially to see Eastman Johnson's 12th Night portrait of himself. That I have so seriously thought of etching for the fun of it. Haven't seen it since it was painted two years ago — \+ now I am rather disappointed — Then had a charming walk in the Park \+ home to dinner. Eveg. Rec'd a letter from Annie — went to Mary Cook's to read it to her, then to Walter's. Louise does not rally from her operation \+ W. is very blue. Spent the rest of the eveg. reading.
