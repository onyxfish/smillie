## Left Page

### Sun. July 29, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day with a good deal of wind, + some portentous clouds that in the afternoon gave us a very little rain. A little rain fell last night. I got up a little earlier than usual + after my bath + before bkfst. I went out to the garden — Greta's sister Bessie came to take her place while she (G.) goes home for a vacation. I meant to get to work writing letters, but in some way managed to let most of the m[orning] get away from me — However, by evening I had finished all my N.A. notes to go with the notices + got them into the P.O. with the exception of my letter to T. W. Wood — After tea I went out to garden + for an hour was very busy transplanting lettuce — while at work lost my jet pen out of my pocket + altho' I searched for it with a lantern I could not find it, much to my disgust. Men down town with letters. [illegible — ~4 lines]

## Right Page

### Mon. July 30, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very hot day. I got up at 6.30, went to the lettuce patch + at once found my fountain pen — Then I weeded + worked about until bkfst. at 8 — The plumbers came + again I had to give them directions + spend some time with them — Herrick also, who is finishing woodwork in lower bath-room. I gave a good part of the m[orning] to writing letters with reference to Academy work — The responses to my circular are beginning to come in + it is astonishing how many questions can be asked about things that are fully explained in the circular — I wrote a long letter to T. W. Wood besides shorter ones to others. I sent remark to Rev. Mr. Benton's for his horse + outfit that he volunteered to lend to me during his two weeks vacation — Soon after 12 I drove to Jones' lake + there got Annie + the boys who had been spending the w[eek] there — We then drove around the lake + got home to dinner at 1 — I don't think much of the Parson's horse as a driver — more writing + more directing of men in the afternoon — After tea Annie + I called upon the Jessups at Judge Lent's house. Found a tolerably large contingent there, among them Mr. + Mrs. M[r.] Jessup of Syria + his daughter Anna. Home at 9 — an quiet hour on piazza + then to bed.
