## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 20, 1894

**Weather:** Cloudy m't, with a little spatter of rain about 9 o'clk - after which it cleared to be a bright, breezy & cool day - Clear, cold moonlight.

All of the day was given up to preparations for the dress-rehearsal to-night & the plays to-morrow night. Herrick was also kept at work upon carpentering in connection with stage business. Evening, there was a dress-rehearsal of but one of the plays - "Second Thoughts" as Jat. McCausland, who has just set from camps at Gettysburg, is not yet ready with his part. Jessie Edgar & Mary, Kate & Katie, together with our domestics, were the audience - The rehearsal went off very nicely - After it Jessie Edgar sang a number of pieces for us very charmingly - It was after 11 when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 21, 1894

First Private Theatrical Entertainment for Village Improvement Socy

**Weather:** A breezy day growing cooler until night, when it was positively cold.

All day we were all very busily engaged in preparing for the entertainment at night. A slight framework was put up & curtains hung - foot lights were arranged - all sorts of stuffs were tacked up on west piazza to break the force of the wind & the girls brought in great masses of vines, ferns & flowers that were festooned every where, indoors and out. The studio was made a dining room for the time - It was nearly 5 o'clk before I got a chance to get a little rest- Consequently, there was something of a rush to get ready to meet the first comers who began to come in soon after 7. They began to crowd in by 7.30 and before 8 every seat was taken - It was so close that the windows had to be kept closed. The plays went off with great eclat & were warmly applauded - a program will be found in one of the pockets of this diary. The plays were over before 10 - Then every body was invited to make themselves at home - The studio was brightly lighted & a large wood fire was much enjoyed. The piazzas were, however, too breezy & cold. The attempts at dancing were not very interesting as the music (volunteer, piano) was not good. However, it was 11 o'clk before the last ones got away & there were many assurances that the affair was "a success". To bed at midnight.
