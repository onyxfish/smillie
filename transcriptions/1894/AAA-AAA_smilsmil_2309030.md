## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 22, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

2°. night of Entertainment.

A very much milder day, growing warmer, until the ev'g., which was very still & mild. Little was done thro' the day. We rested after the fatigues of yesterday & last night in preparation for the work of to-night. I found Stevens & arranged with him that he & his wife should furnish the dance music to-night. When ev'g came we were ready for the first comers who (proved to be the Parkes) who came before we were thro' with our supper. The weather of the ev'g was perfect — all the doors and windows were open without discomfort to any one — There were fewer in audience than the night before but all the chairs were filled & some stood at the open windows on the piazza — The play went off with entire smoothness. Judge Messup moved a vote of thanks at the close & called upon me for a few words in answer to which I gave a very few — saying that we believed in acting rather than in talking & then invited all present into the studio where cake & lemonade were bountifully supplied. It was after 10 when Stevens & his wife came — they gave very good dance music — Piano & Violin. The boys had waxed the floor & the young people had a very good time dancing until after 11 o'clk. The Piazzas were much enjoyed by all — the lanterns & decorations (flowers, ferns & ash berries) making them very pretty — Two of the lanterns conflagrated leaving burn marks but no serious damage — To bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 23, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day — another of the cloudless & pitilessly dry weather. Annie had accepted the invitation of Mrs. Elisha Mulford to lunch at her house to-day, the party to include the Adamses, Carleton. Annie & self — I had hired of Watrous, two days ago a team to take us over, but, there being a big Irish Roman Catholic funeral this m'g, he had let the team go, saying that it would be back in plenty time — not another team was to be had in town. The funeral did not return until noon — too late — I tried to telephone to Mrs. M — wire down, of course — so I had to content myself with writing an explanation & mailing it to her. Then we all gave ourselves over to loafing & resting — and I think we needed it. In the ev'g many of the girls came up, & the young people had a good time — and then to bed a little earlier than for two nights past.
