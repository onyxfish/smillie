### Wed. Jan. 10, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very nasty day — mostly snow —
I awoke with a bad headache &
had bkfst. in bed — then I got up &
was about until noon when I was
obliged to give up, an old fashioned
sick headache having taken possession of me. Annie sent for Yale
he came at about 4 & left some
medicine for me. I lay upon a
lounge perfectly helpless until
9.30 — I managed to get to bed
at about 10 — Annie had gone
with Ed. & Mary to a musical
entertainment at W. & Mr. Peck's.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 11, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw, disagreeable day, with some snow
& rain — most of the night much discomforted
by headache — up at 4 A.M. & took medicine —
Bkfst. in bed — Got up at 10.30. Read news
paper — wrote two or three notes and at
soon commenced to work upon Ravello
Cathedral Tower. Lunch at 1 — worked
as long as I could see & it seems to me
that I have made good progress — Then
at 5 o'clk. I lay down & had a nap —
Dinner at 6.30 — Char with us — he
took Kate to Symphony concert. All
my frames came up from Butler's, so
I worked until near 11 o'clk getting
sketches into them. Rec'd. a
note from J. Pierpont Morgan saying
that he was willing that I should
show etching pf. of J. S. M. — at Century
club on Sat. night.

Ralph still very much bunged up
with his cold.
