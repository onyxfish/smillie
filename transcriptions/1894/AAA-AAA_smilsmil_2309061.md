## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 23, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little wet early in the m'f. Still mild — a sort of leaden fog lay over the hills. I could not work out of doors so I had a chance to accomplish something in my Etching room by experimenting upon different problems connected with dry-ground aqua-tint. Before noon I went down town to P. O. — etc. — After dinner I spent about a couple of hours with Herrick, again at work upon the tree tops — After that there was still time for me to do a little more work in the studio — then, in the twilight, I went out \+ did some pruning — After tea R. \+ I went to P. O. Eve'g wrote a letter to Farrington \+ read newspapers.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 24, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain fall, commencing bet. 6 \+ 7 a.m. \+ continuing almost without cessation until after 5 p. m. I had an unbroken day of work in Etching room — continued experiments with dry-aqua-tint — bit in outline (soft gr.?) Cathedral Tower, Ravello — then laid dry aqua-tint gr. \+ made a start upon that with acid — went to P. O. at noon \+ again at about 5 o'clk. At 7.30 I went to Station \+ met Annie as she ret'd from N.Y. Evening, of course we talked over happenings since our separation about 56 hours ago.
