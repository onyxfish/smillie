## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 14, 1894

**Weather:** Cloudy, windy & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

after my regular m'f duties I went to offices - Paid my bill there, got my prints & had a chat with Geo. Hearne. Then to 2d. Nat. Bk, Safe Deposit Co. & spent some time over m'f. in N.A. f.S. box - then to N.A., then to studio for a few moments & then home to lunch. Back at 2.30 & such time as Real Estate brokers left to me I used in making paste & getting a good mount of my Crécy drawing. Home at 6.15 Eve'g Geo. Cook & Minnie, Roger & Mollie Adams dined with us. Geo. & Nellie Smillie came in for a few minutes during the Eve'g. I wrote to Herrick and got letter with checks off to him.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 15, 1894

**Weather:** Bright & pleasant  **Temp:** 11

before I got to Studio, spent 1 planning new water-color work - came home to lunch - back, taking Ralph with me - he spent the rest of p.m. in my seat with my water-color box & brushes. I was stretching paper, etc. - Soon after we started for home - I tried to get a little rest before dinner with little success. Eve'g. Roger Adams took Katie to one of Mr. Lincoln's series of "Un-cut Leaves" - Readings, by the authors, of unpublished works. I went to the Century. Gibson gave us his lecture on cross fertilization of flowers, with mechanical diagrams. After it talked awhile with Maynard, Son & Dielman concerning N.A. matters. During the lecture I sat alongside of Lake Godwin who chatted very pleasantly of his work & his tastes. It was midnight when I got home.
