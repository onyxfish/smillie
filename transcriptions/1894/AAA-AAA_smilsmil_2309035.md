## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 1, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day, except for the smoke & the horrible dryness. White, the driver of dilly's team, got to the house before I was up with a load of earth from under the Chapel Hall — had driven dirt all day & got 16 loads distributed over parts of the grass east of the house — a man spreading it, and Giorgio Vacchari digging at the Chapel. I spent some time in superintending — then to the foundery & for an hour was engaged, grinding down my mezzotint rockers, at the grindstone moved by steam power. Much time in the afternoon given to the earth grading, etc. —
Evef. Reading newspapers and "Century" for Sebt.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 2 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very, very thick from smoke, but no clouds — no rain — very close & warm during the afternoon & evening. I spent the outf until after 12 in the different gardens, looking after things & gathering vegetables for dinner — afternoon, I did a little writing — from 4 o'clk. until 7 I was transplanting lettuce. Then tea. Evef., a part of it, on piazza, the first evef. for some time when it has been mild enough to sit out of doors. [illegible — entire passage]
