## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 18, 1894

**Weather:** Fine morning — overcast afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

late b'kft. . About 11 I started out for a walk with Jim & Ralph - we went up to the Park - took a look at the animals & then on up to the lake & ramble - on the Mall we met Mary Cook with every one of her chickens except Carlton — we went to the Met. Museum & had just time to walk through the new part, then home to dinner at 2. Robt. Cook, by invitation, dined with us. After dinner, the headache that had been bothering me all day got the better of me & I went to bed & slept until 6.30 - then tea - Evely., wandered around & to bed early.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 19, 1894

**Weather:** Dull, raw m'f., clearing in the afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After taking Ralph to school and doing my regular shopping, I wrote some notes at home, then went to C.M.C.A. &c & decided to take Studio N°. 36. It was near 1 o'clk. when I got home. After lunch, my head was so uncomfortable that for a couple of hours I lay upon the bed - up at 4.30 & at 5 went with Annie to Walter's where Mr. & Mrs. Robt. Underwood Johnston had come to hear Mrs. Cornwell, a Spanish lady give an example of her "Musical Fragments with Words" - It is a sort of Recitation - intoning — chanting — Recitative - with piano accompaniment - She speaks very correctly & prettily, but I found her Recitation difficult to understand always - the portion of Tenhauser that she gave quite took me off my feet - The piece de Resistance was Mr. Johnson's "Paraphrase from the Servian" — "The Norse Liquors" - A weird, uncanny story — He was evidently much interested - we stayed to dinner, & so did Mrs. Cornwell. — had had English Pheasant & quail — at 9.30 I left the table to go to N.A. Council meeting — a full one — I got home soon after 11.
