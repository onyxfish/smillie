## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 8, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day — dark & raining steadily. — After a night of discomfort I got up late & spent the rest of the day lying about, doing nothing of importance. — The day, among other days, is a black.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 9, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull & Raw — the walks dried for a while in the afternoon but were wet again at night. I kept indoors during the a.m. At 12.30 went out for a short walk & brought Ralph home from his school — Mr. Walton's class & Mr. Gilbert Jones', 222 Madison Ave, near 36th St. After lunch Mr. Brownell came & invited us to dine with them next Friday. In the mng, Emma J — came to invite us to take tea & celebrate her 50th birthday next Sunday. After 3 o'clk. Annie & I went to the Women's Portrait exhibition at N.A. of D. very swell and quite crowded. Some good & some interesting pictures there but it does not impress me as a remarkable exhibition. Spent some time with Nicole talking of N.A. matters. Jim joined us & A. went with him on a shopping expedition at 5 o'clk. When I left I walked up to Charl's rooms & left a note for him — then home. Eve'g: Charl came in — talking until 10.30.
