## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 7, 1894

**Weather:** wonderfully brilliant until near sunset \- a threatening sky in the evening  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfo. at 9 \- after my m'f. duties I got to work upon Report of Sale of N.A. \+ continued to write until after 1 o'clk. when I got to the end of it. Then I had a very short outing before dinner \- After dinner I read my report to Annie \- Requires 25 min \- \+ then A \- Eliz. Home, Ralph \+ I went off for a walk \- we went to the top of the Dr. Dunton hill (old Beale farm) \+ thence around thro' darkie Town \+ home \- Eve'g. I revised Report \+ took it to P. O. before 10 \-

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 8, 1894

**Weather:** cloudy \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

It was 10 o'clk. before I was ready to go down town; then R\- \+ I went to the P. O. \+ then with Brue, the jeweller, \+ Percy Read, to the Presbt Ch. where we were joined by Warner the painter, \+ then we had a session, discussing the matter of the painting of the Church \- the colors, or tints, to be used, and the distribution of them. Then home \+ spent more time with Ge. B. Smith, superintending the mixing of color to paint the Herrick ro. Before dinner I took a short walk with Annie, after having been with Stone, directing his work. Afternoon tried to do a little work in studio but had to give Smith more time \- at last I sat down to work but found that my head ached so badly that I went to Stable, saddled Fay \+ took a short horseback ride. Then it was dusk \- Tea at 6.30 \- At 8.30 I was so sleepy that I took a short nap\- then fixed my fires \+ other chores.
Heavy rain after 9 P. m. for a couple of hours.
