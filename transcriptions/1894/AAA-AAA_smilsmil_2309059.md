## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 19, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day - very mild - a perfect specimen of an Oct. day. I was very busy with the men & my own work in the studio. The grading about the barns went on - Hart brought a couple of loads of clay \+ Macwhinny tamped it down at east end of large barn to keep out water from melting snow. I finished biting the soft gr. outline. Tower at Constance. \+ then laid a dry aqua-tint gr. up on it. I also traced the Pavells Cath. Tower sketch \+ transferred it to tissue paper to use in making a soft gr. upon the copper. After dinner I took a short drive with H. \+ B. Then Annie took Kate Parker Riley, who is now here quite an invalid, out for a short ride. I spent the rest of the p.m. in trees cutting off tops and turning the sawed ends. Henrick helping me. Eveng. I studied up on my aqua-tint notes made while in London.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 20, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day \+ actually uncomfortably warm for work or exercise out of doors. I spent most of the day with the men \+ up in the trees sawing out the tops. In the m'g. after giving directions for the grading of the barn yard, which was finished in the afternoon, I got to work in studio \+ gave a couple of hours or more to experimental work upon dry aqua-tint plates. After 12 I went down town. Then dinner. Rev. R.L. Benton borrowed his horse \+ buggy for the afternoon. Henrick worked with me at work upon the trees on the lawn near it. We cut out a number of tops, \+ turned over the stumps. The cutting was done, partly because the trees were spindling up too high, \+ partly because of the barking \+ girdling done by red squirrels. I was very tired \+ rested for ½ an hour before tea. A quiet eveg. \+ to bed early.
