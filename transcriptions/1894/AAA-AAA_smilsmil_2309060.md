## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 21, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless, hazy + very warm — The Ther. stood about 74° in the shade out of doors — A day of wonderful color. Bkfst as usual, afwt — After that, Annie, Ralph + I wandered over the place taking note of the recent improvements — Then I got to work, with chisels and mallet, upon the trunk of an old beech tree north of the house, cutting out decaying + dead wood — Dinner at 1.30 — after dinner resumed my work + after the cutting was completed, I heated coal tar + painted all of the denuded part with that. I worked out of doors until 5 o'clk — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g, read for awhile Horatio Brown's "Life in the Lagoons". Before 9 P.M. Rec'd telegram from Walter announcing the death, last night, of his daughter Mary — This has been expected for some time —

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 22, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annie went to N.Y.

Dull + dismal day — heavy, leaden clouds threatening rain all the mo'g. better in the afternoon. Rather mild. My duties as Supt., manager and upper servant kept me busy until 11. Then I went down town — Teleg. to Edw'd Cook. At noon I drove to M. + H. Station with Annie + started her for N.Y. — She goes down to attend the funeral of Walter's little Mary, which is to take place to-morrow mo'g. Then R. + I ret'd + had our dinner — all the P.M. until 5 I spent with Herrick cutting the tops of trees to the west of the house — The sugar maples were all more or less injured by red squirrels who gnaw off the young bark of the smaller limbs. Eve'g, at 8 o'clk, I attended a special meeting of the Town Council, called to hear what I had to say concerning the first steps necessary to be taken, to get from some good sanitary engineer a plan for a system of sewering the Town of Montrose thoroughly — The meeting was well attended + entirely informal — I explained my views — was listened to respectfully — + asked them to take some preliminary action at their next regular meeting. The meeting broke up soon after 9 — then I went home + to bed.
