## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 27, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ret. to Montrose.

Disagreeable day \- raw & windy. After blep. went to our house taking with me Mr. Morrow to show her how to manage the furnace which I wish her to keep burning for a month, if necessary, to dry out the wetness of the house. I dawdled about until 11.30 & then went to Train starting from Hoboken at 1.15. The journey was entirely uneventful. I was welcomed by the youngsters who came out to meet me with lanterns. I found Fanny Kimball at the house, visiting \- she arrived last Monday eve'g. We chatted away the remainder of the eve'g & then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 28, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day in spite of some indications of a change in weather approaching. My day was spent in going about & directing work. Stone at work on the new strawberry beds & Geo. Walker digging drain in swamp in pasture lot. Derrick ret. at about noon from somewhere \- he has been off for a day or more. I don't know where. I wrote a couple of letters in the afternoon & got a little nap. Evening, Fanny, Annie & I looked over some of our European Photos. In yesterday's Herald & in to-day's Tribune are published accts of the sale of N.A. blep. to Met. Ins. Co.
