## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 5, 1894

**Weather:** Rain last night — cloudy day growing colder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night — cloudy day growing colder — showers in the afternoon \+ eve\+. Some time given to the work about the place \+ at last I got to work in studio \+ made a little progress in starting some small sketches for aqua-tint experiments — worked for a time with Herrick getting set sashes to cover cold-frames where I have set a number of lettuce plants —
Dinner at usual time — awhile in studio — Eve\+. Rec\^d. letter from Robbins that was not quite agreeable to me — wrote a reply to him — and a note to Nicoll — to P. O. at 10 P.m.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 6, 1894

**Weather:** Overcast, som\+ bleak m\+\+. but before  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, som\+ bleak m\+\+. but before noon it cleared to be a brilliant \+ beautiful specimen of an Oct. day — Started fire in the studio furnace \+ after getting through my m\+\+ duties I got to work upon Report of Sale of N.A. to be presented to the Council at its first meeting for the season next Monday eve\+. Wrote until after 12. Then had a short walk with Annie \+ Lleg\[illegible\] before dinner. Afternoon, again wrote on Report — Daud. Jarpe came to see me about plans for chimnies for Chapel Hall \+ the painting of the Pres\+ \+ Church Spire — At 5 I went out for a walk — glorious twilight — walked to the west of the town \+ enjoyed immensely the luminous gloom \+ sentiment of the eve\+. Home to tea, all in a glow — Eve\+. brilliant, cold \+ cloudless moonlight — my time was taken up with looking after fires \+ such like duties, reading newspapers etc. — etc.
