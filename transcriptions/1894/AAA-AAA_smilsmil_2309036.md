## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 3, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

commenced regrading croquet lawn \-

As thick as ever with smoke in spite of a strong easterly breeze. The town clock could not be seen from our piazza \- Sylvester Hart came on with his team at 7 o'clk. \+ commenced ploughing up the croquet ground preparatory to regrading all of that part of the grounds. White continued to haul earth from under Chapel Hall. The green was soon made a horrible confusion of earth, heaps, piles of stones \+ torn sods. I spent the whole day in directing the men at their work. Sue H. Louire, Kate \+ I went to Charles Sayre's \+ heard Jessie Edgar sing \- her Mr. Carleton sang with her at times which was injudicious, to say the least, on his part. We left for home at 10. Jessie sang delightfully \- after her singing, Bessie Throop played several pieces on the piano.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 4, 1894

**Weather:** Fair. Ground.  **Temp:** [illegible]

First day of Veteran Encampment on

Smoke still thick \+ the air close so that, inspite of a cool breeze, a little movement would make me very warm. Hart \+ his team at work on lawn with scraper tearing things up generally \- a great many stones which White hauled down to the Chapel in return for his load of earth. He hauled 14 loads of stone. Again I gave, practically, my whole day to superintending the work. Sue H., by 8 o'clk. mail I rec'd. from Mr. Lee at Bar Harbor, a letter in answer to mine \+ giving me a form of letter to Mr. Geo. Read. He sums things up very much as I see them \+ I shall adopt his form of attack upon R \-. A quiet evening indoors.
