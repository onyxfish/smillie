## Left Page

### Sun. April 8, 1894

**Weather:** Cloudy - cold & raw - spatting rain in the w.f. - clearing at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

date bkfst. as usual. About noon I went out for a walk but there was enough rain to prevent it. I went into the Century for a few minutes \+ found some amusement in a one-wall display by J. Alden Weir. Dinner at 2. Afternoon, took along walk up to 82d. S. \+ through the Park, getting home after 6, having met Clinton \+ Anthony in 43d. S. \+ walked a short distance with them. Tea at 7. Eve'g. I went to Union League Club to see, if possible, R.W. Van Boskirk who called at the house this p.m. to get some W.C. paintings for the Club Exbn on Thur. He was not at the club but I saw Chase \+ Dr. Purdy. Chase walked part way home with us - on his way to see Tom Gillelan, now at the Grd. Union Hotel, on a flying visit from Ottawa.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 9, 1894

**Weather:** Brilliant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

After taking R. to school etc - I got to work upon human Reed portrait - I forget for the moment, that after leaving R. - I went to W. J. Lockport Morgan's, had, as usual, a very brief interview, but it was by far the most satisfactory one yet. He finally accepts the etching as entirely satisfactory, gave orders to have some help that he could take to England with him on the 18th inst. \+ Told me to send my bill to him. Went forth at 12 30. Lunch. worked on Reed plate until 5 — then out for a walk - got some distressing eyes that incommoded me very much. Dinner. Eve'g. Council Meeting. Great time, all hands counting tickets for past week, not much business transacted - my eye very painful. At about 11 I walked up home with Blashfield.
