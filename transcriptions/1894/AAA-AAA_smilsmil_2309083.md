## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 6, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another charming day - to studio after 10 & got to work with water-color on x "At Crécy en Brie, France - a view of the Church, some bits of buildings & a turn in the small river, from a slight sketch that I took when there with Selppevcatt in '84 - I am trying to make a light & luminous transparent color sketch. The paper, a piece that has been stretched for years, is against me - Before I went to 2d. Nat. Bul. & attended to Annie's matters home to lunch at 1.30 - back at 2.30 - worked until 4.30 - Then Annie came. I find the light in my new room exceedingly trying - a double & conflicting light, further complicated at times, by reflections - I gyrated round & round the room trying to find a spot where I could see -
\+ & I went to Mitchell & Vance Co. & bought a gas standard with flexible tube for moving light in parlor - Then I went to Kahlers & got a pair of shoes - Then home at 6.15 - Dinner late as Charl did not come in until 7 - after dinner he took Katie to a Symphony concert - I went out & looked at the illumination of Union League Club - Reception to the new mayor, Strong — worked on N.A.D. sites until after 11 - then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 7, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day - about 10.30 when I got to studio & to work on Crécy drawing - S. R. Koehler came in for a while on his way to train for Boston. Home at 1 - Ralph & I had our lunch alone, Annie & Katie not coming home to lunch. Back at 2.30 - interrupted by calls - Jel came in about dusk & we walked up together - I went to barber's & had my hair cut - Dinner at 7 - Robt Cook came & after it, Annie, Katie & he went to Daly's Theatre to see Twelfth Night. I stayed at home & wrote letters to Agur & Lathrop & Herrick.
