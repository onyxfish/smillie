## Left Page

### Thur. March 1, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild, hazy day — everything afloat with melting snow. Took R. to school To work on Morganhead going very generally over it increasing broad tones — Mr. Reynolds at work with ralson, the sewing machine alongside me — R. playing with his building blocks + engine under my table. a studio at home is not unmixed enjoyment. Went forth. Wrote to W. Heber Bishops — also to Pennell to make sure that he understands my position He wishes very much to see the pf. at 75 dols — for he gets half the commission — worked on head until after 5 — then as Rest — Dinner at 6.30 — Katie T — Dining at Annie Ballards —
Busy looking over Etching proofs — Rec'd a letter from Dr. Mac Alister of the Drexel Ins — Phil 2, ordering a set of pfs. from the Pratt ms — Platts —

Joe + Minnie called during the afternoon — A. was out, but I saw them

Morganhead — 5 h —

## Right Page

### Fri. March 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild day, for the most part, sunny — mf. went to H. + K's + got four proofs from Morganhead — Experimental — back on a different kind of paper — I was much encouraged by the appearance of the plate + think that I can finish it now in a few days — Home at 1 — Annie at Harlem. Unitarian league. lunch at 1 — after it, Millats came in to urge me to accept the offer of $75 for jade pf. says the man has already p'd the money + it is in bank — Told him that I was awaiting Mr. Bishop's reply to my note. Then I backed down + sketched 3 of my 4 pf — after 4 went out with Raffe for a little while — left him at home + then I went up to see Geo. who is in trouble — Val is down with Scarlet Fever — Home to dinner at 6.30. Stupid evef and to bed at 10 —

Morganhead — 5 h.
