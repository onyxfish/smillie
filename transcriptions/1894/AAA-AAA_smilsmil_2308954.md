## Left Page

### Fri. March 23, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very dark, rainy m'g, beautiful afternoon I worked on Morgan plate - adding a very little color here \+ there \+ in other places Taking it away. I shaved my nursery studio accomodations with miss Larkin the dressmaker. Lunch at 1, may Kate \+ Bessie were expected from Po'k to-day but telegraphed that they were detained by the storm. I had a very bad headache \+ rested for about an hour before going to the Art Students League to announce a class for etching there. At 4 o'clk. met W. Joe Evans this Pri\- \+ Mr. Thos. Fogarty - a number of sketches were looked over \+ names of advanced students were recommended. Arrangements were made giving me the use of an excellent studio \+ the league is to supply most of the material required. The names of those invited to join the class are Thos. J. Fogarty - A. V. Tack, Fred C. Yohn. \- Tucker \+ the Misses J. A. \+ W. Walker. May Morgan \- M. Fernier \+ B. H. Jonet. I was taken through the classes \+ Rooms \+ then went down to the Gallenipass when the Socy of Am. Artists exhibition is now open. There until 6. Then walked home. Dinner. Cosy\+. Charle come in \+ stayed until 11.

morgan head 2 h.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 24, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - clear, breezy \+ consequently dirty \+ cool. Immediately after breakfast I went to A. T. V.'s - \+ had two India pp. from Morgan head - one in Terra-cotta red to please N. M. - his part of the business is very discouraging to me. Then young Vaict took 6 prints from the 3 plates etched by my N.A. class - \+ they gave me more gratification than the Morgan prints. Home to lunch at 1 o'clk - After that went out \+ attended to marketing etc. Taking R. with me - when I got at about 3 o'clk. found Mary, Kate \+ Bessie, just in from Po'k. At the same time, a strange young woman came, presenting her card - Toni Robloffs - speaking with a french accent. She is one of Kenyon Cox's class at the league \+ is anxious to join my Etching Class. She showed some promising sketches \+ I told her to be at the league next Sat. p.m. I worried proarwhiles over my pp. - then took a walk up to Central Park \+ back. Dinner at 6.30 - a table full as Tom \+ Louise Cook had been sent around, Mary having a dinner party of her own.

morgan head 2 h.
