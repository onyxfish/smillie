## Left Page

### Sat. April 28, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant m'f. — pleasant again at night, but wet (not very wet) during most of the afternoon. After attending to butcher & grocer I got to work upon Read head, hoping that I was adding the last, finishing touches - worked until lunch at 1 — then a light rain began to fall, to A's distress, because the Diet Kitchen entertainment takes place at the "Tiffany Chapel" after this p.m. — for this enterprise to make some money for the D.K. — she has been working very hard & hofsefully for some time. I worked on my [illegible] until 3 o'clk. I have promised to send it to Durand on Wed.'s steamer, if possible. Then I went to Student's league & met my class at 4 o'clk. \+ worked with them until after 6 o'clk — biting plates. It was 7 when I got home — dinner. Every I felt too tired to go to the Century to hear Parke Godwin on Louis Kossuth. — . Charle came in \+ I sat \+ talked with him until 10.30
