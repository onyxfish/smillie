## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 22, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another mild \+ very pleasant day \- my night was not as bad as the night before \+ at tho' at 8.30 I felt very seedy I was able to get up \+ go down to bkfst. I was not able to accomplish anything \+ simply loafed about the house. Katie \+ Bessie T. went to Po'k. on 3.30 train. Roger lunched with us \+ a party went to an Opera Matinée after it. After 3 o'clk. Mr. H\. Marquand called in the interest of a friend of his, a real estate broker, to urge the claims of a portion of the S. Duke's Hotel grounds \- 5th Ave. \#54" \+ 55" S. \- as an ideal No. of D. site. \- Eve\. Carleton Cook took Annies to a Concert at Carnegie Hall. Sue got to bed early \+ I wasted time \+ patience skimming thro' "The missing Chord" by Miss Dillingham \- I was mad at myself. A. did not get in until after 11 \- then to be[d]

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 23, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold day \- bkfst. after 9 \- after that, read mf paper \+ at about noon ventured out for a little fresh air \+ exercise. Found the air so keen \+ my membranes so sensitive that I did not dare stay out long. I went to the Century \- there for only a moment, then home. Dinner at 2. Robt. Cook came \+ dined with us. After dinner I loafed \- Eve\. Sue, Annie, Katchen \+ Eleanor went to Walter's to tea \- Robt. \+ Louise Cook took tea with the boys \+ me \- at 8, all hands cleared out or went to bed \+ I was left alone. I wrote to Maynard, [illegible] Lathrop, Kimmuel \+ Vaigt, Sayre \+ Shafer \+ Brooks Bros. The party ret'd from Walter's at 10.15 \- To bed very soon after.
