## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 7, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — some sunshine \+ some shadow — I don't feel equal to work but after taking R. to school I set about making some drawings for Herrick — new bath room at Montrose. Went for R. at 12.30 — lunch — and during the afternoon I worked again on plans. I took R. \+ we walked down to 23d. S. \+ back — stopping at the Plumbers, the Assoc\[iatio\]n Artists \+ at the U.A. —

Eve\[ning\] Annie \+ the two Adams girls went to German Theatre. I had to write another letter to Carter \+ Ledyard about the Phillips N.A. Mortgage — also went to Tel. office \+ sent reply to Herald, giving permission to pub. a reproduction from W.C." A street in Tunis". To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 8, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mixed \+ pleasant with more sunshine than shadow — I got up with a my bad head — was able to take R. to school, but after that passed most of the m\[orning\] nursing my back — went for R. at 12.30 — took no lunch — walked up \+ down the sunny side of Park Ave. a few times in the afternoon. Had a call from Blaney, the etcher, from Boston, very happy \+ enthusiastic — on his way to Spain for a 4 months sketching trip. Katie \+ Mollie A. — rel\[atives\] to New Haven this afternoon — I again spent some time on my back. Dinner at 6.45 Charl expected — came about 7.30 \+ took Katie T. to Boston Symphony Concert — I read awhile \+ got to bed early.
