## Left Page

### Mon. June 11, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hotter, if possible, than yesterday. Before [illegible]: at 8 - went to carpenters \+ to grocers \+ butchers - Carpenter came at 9 - was with him until 12. packing big box \+ the crate holding cots \+ mattrasses - went to Storage Warehouse \+ to Syp. while we were at lunch Jacksons men came \+ took away the freight, the warehouse men took away the silver \+ the U.S. Syp. sent for box so that everything was cleared out. Then I devoted myself, as energetically as the weather would permit, to my personal affairs. Farrington came \+ I had a last Talk with him concerning N.A. affairs - By 6 o'clk I had my trunk down in library \+ all my things alongside it ready to be put in. I posted a Fountain Pen to Sig. Vozzi, Cappuccini. Amalfi. Eve Chase came in for a short time, bringing negatives developed by Cullen, my exposure at the Mad. Sq. Fountain a few days ago - I gave him to take charge of them while we were away, the Nor. Pac. Bonds ($9000) \+ a A.R.R. cert. \+ stock for 5 shares - Then I packed my trunk almost to a finish - took a bath \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another exceedingly hot \+ humid day. But a veil was over the sun \+ the heat was not so fierce. Up at 5 with severe headache - Bromo-Caffe - \+ then in bed until 7 - Then up - finished packing \+ got Strawberries - breakfast. Syp. man came for Trunks at 9.30 - After that, podging about - no hurry. We got away from the house before 12 leaving it in charge of Mary Mooney. The journey in the cars was rather trying owing to the heat - it was altogether uneventful - a hasty, but good, dinner at Scranton at 6 - Got to Montrose at 7.40 - At once to the house - As it was not yet dark I prowled about a long as I could see, looking at the new Tanks \+ other improvements.
