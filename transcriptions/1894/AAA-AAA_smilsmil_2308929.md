## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 1, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day over head — It was pleasant to see the sunshine come in at my window. Fever nearly gone — 101° — but I feel as I suppose a collapsed tube must feel. Yale came about 10 o'clk — \+ talked for some time. I got a little sleep in the afternoon \+ got up \+ dressed about 6 o'clk — In the eve'g I went down to the parlor. Geo. came in for awhile — His Private View is "on" to-day at Avery's — He has lugubrious accts to give of his sale — only 3 of the 12 paintings being sold — \+ those sold for only enough to pay for the 3 frames \+ the comm. chgs. on the other paintings.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold day — feeling much better — after help. in bed got up — \+ at noon went for Ralph — afternoon I went to N.A. of D. for a few minutes, but found that I felt that I had done too much, to my great regret for I find at night that I could not go to the Sky Party, Water-Color Socy opening. Charl came in for a few minutes about 10 o'clk while I was quite alone. Annie \+ Katie \+ Mollie Adams had gone to the Opera \+ Katie T. had gone to a little dance at Lucy Morees. I passed a very unhappy eve'g. I think that the recovery from the grip is about as distressing as any other phase of the disorder. I wrote to Carter \+ Ledyard, lawyers, 54 Wall St. in answer to note rec'd. from them that there must be a meeting of the N.A's \+ of the Council to authorize loan of $5000 on mortgage from Mr. Phillips — I informed them that such meetings had been held Jan'y 10th \+ 15th —
