## Left Page

### Tues. April 10, 1894

**Weather:** overcast, raw + very disagreeable  **Temp:** [illegible]

rain commencing after 4. P.M. \- Took R. to school, then went up to Dr. Yale's \- found him in + he removed a from my eye a bit of cinder to my great relief. To work on Reed portrait until lunch time. After that, again on the etching, until 4 o'clk. then went with Annie to Socy of Am. Artists Exbn, 215 W. 57th S. While there met Howard R. Butler + had a short chat with him. A. \+ I labored over the show until 6 o'clk. Then home in the rain. Dinner. Every, some reading \- then wrote part of a letter to Mr. Doce, Chicago.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 11, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

great storm \- High wind, snow and rain all day long. Neither of the boys went to school. I put in a long days work on the Reed portrait. \+ did not go out of the house until 5 o'clk. when I was forced to brave the storm + go to market for some dinner. Dinner at 6.30. Every Chase came in \- then George. They stayed until after 10.
