## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 9, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very bright in the early mfg. before 10 it clouded over + about noon a few drops of rain fell — but the afternoon cleared bright + warm + the moonlight was charming — my mfg. until 1.15 was spent in the garden looking after the condition of things there. Dinner soon after 2 — in the afternoon I wrote to Mr. Wm. Lee sending to him a draft of a note that I propose to send to Mr. Read. Early evef. I wrote notes to Miss Eva Lee, of Brooklyn, Pa. who wishes to submit some of her sketches for criticism + to Mr. Gilson Willets, who asks for some "items of interest" concerning me + my work, for pub. in the Art Interchange. Took letters to P.O. Then, sat on piazza until near 11 — glorious moonlight —

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 10, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A short + heavy shower at 6.30 A.m. Then it grew close + muggy, sunshine striving for the mastery — about 1.30 another shower came up — + before 3 a very heavy rain storm, with thunder, came + continued until about 7 — after which the wind rose + it grew cooler — cloudless moonlight, but with fog + smoke again.
After bkft. I got Stone to work on the Croquet grd. grading, — a part where the rain had not yet made it muddy. He left at 11 — back before 1 — but was driven off at 3 — I worked at my dry aqua-tint experiments + some sketches — also, a small pen sketch of the house, made on an Album card at Annie's request. I went downtown at 6 + got somewhat wet — Evef., newspapers — + later, some walking on piazza.
