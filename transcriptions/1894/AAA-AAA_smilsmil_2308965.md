## Left Page

### Sat. April 14, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright + pleasant day. After my morning duties I got to work upon Reed portrait + spent the rest of the m'f. with scraber + stone taking a strip from top + bottom of the flat tint that I had made as a background. Lunch — then worked on Reed until 3. 30. Then to Art Students League Etching class where I found my old enemy, "the young woman", not in force — nine young women + two young men. It makes me tired. However, I was as patient as I knew how to be + did as well as I knew how — It was after 6 o'clk. when I left + 6.50 when I got home to dinner — Roger Adames there — Destroyed with us over night; going to the theatre. I went to Elew Cooks + for an hour visited with Mr. + Mrs. Sprague, just back from Bermuda — At 9 I went to Century + heard Dr. Wallace Wood lecture on "the Ideal man in art" — a very tame affair — As it seems to me he holds some nebulous + very unimportant theories that in his mind are magnified into vast importance — The walls of gallery were covered by photos + sketches — audience filled gallery but interest was very languid. Home soon after 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 15, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful spring day — Bkfst. at 9.30 — At it Jim + Roger went up to Riverside Park — at 12 Ralph + I rode up to 91st St. + entered the Park there + walked around the large reservoir (where we met W. James to — moore) + out at 82d. St. gate, getting home at 2.05 — then dinner. After dinner Dr. Churchill Carmalt called, she, Annie + I had quite an extended conversation over a variety of topics extending from Bacteria to Higher Education — After that Jim + I had a short walk — when I came in I had a short rest before tea — Roger took tea with us + left soon after. Little Louise Cook also took tea with us — Eve'g. made a ground dabber for the League Etching class —
