## Left Page

### Fri. April 6, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Combination of March & April. Bright &
windy & raw & dirty & at times threatening
showers. Took R. to school, etc — spent
most of the m'f, laying grounds and
varnishing plates for League Etching Cl.
went forth — Lunch at 1 — A — in Brook-
lyn, attending Unitarian league — I
wrote letter of Instruction to Herrick
At 3.30 went to N.A. to see Farrington
then to K. T. V. Canal St & got f. of f.
of Morgan head & home at 6 —
Nearly 7 when we had dinner,
owing to excitement over getting Jim
ready for a Charade party at Moses'
(his school) at 8 I went to N.A. & met
Mr. W. Sanders Davies, Accountant, &
had a preliminary look at the books.
Home at 9.30 — wrote a note to Jno
Durand telling him that I would
be behind time with my Reed
Portrait — After 10.30 when Jim
came in.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 7, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, raw day with mizzle, drizzle
& snow — Spent most of the m'f laying
grounds & varnishing backs of plates
for League Etching Class — also, for a
while, arranging my informal "Talk"
on Etching before students of N.A. of D.
Lunch at 1. Then, until 3.30, mount-
ing & packing three finished Morgan
p. that later I sent to N's house by
David — At 3.30 I went up to League
& met my class. There was some delay,
owing to a change in classes so that we did
not get into our room until 4.30 — There
were 9 or 10 young women & but two men —
this is not to my liking. A young Indian was
the model & Mr. Jacarte & the other young man
made spirited sketches, both of which I bit in —
none of the girls succeeded in doing any thing
that encouraged me. Home to dinner at 6.30
& then I went with Katie T. to the N.A. where
the students organization had a meeting at
which, between recitations & dancing, I was
to talk to them about etching — I found
a very good attendance & at about 9 o'clk.
they were ready for me. I fancy that I
was able to amuse them — at any rates,
there was a goodly share of laughter at
my stories & jokes & so I hope that some
of them learned something — at 10.30,
after Katie had enjoyed a little dancing
I walked her home & did not go to the
Century Club meeting as I had intended
