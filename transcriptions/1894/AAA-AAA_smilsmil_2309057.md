## Left Page

### Wea. ~~Mon.~~ Oct. 17, 1894

Eliz. Howe left us.

bright day, but with a howling wind. Up early — bkfs. at 7 — Eliz. left on 7.50 train. Ralph \+ I walked over to the R.R. station \+ saw her off. Then I put in a good day's work. looking after Hart \+ his team who are ploughing barnyard \+ grading \+ Jos. Maehinney who is banking up the north end of the Herrick house. The painters finished the painting of the Herrick house, at about noon. I worked in studio a good part of the day — mounted W.C. sketches, laid soft grounds — made a tracing \+ got everything ready to put the Constance Tower on the plate. About 5 o'clk went down town with Annie \+ had a walk.
A large part of the evng. was spent in counting up the cost of the Water improvements — The total amounts to just about $1300 —

This page is an acct. of Wed. Oct. 17 a mistake was made in turning the pages as I wrote.

## Right Page

### Wea. Thurs. ~~Thrs.~~ Oct. 18, 1894

Brilliant, beautiful day, altho' still somewhat windy — Again, much of the time out of doors, after the men, \+ doing some pruning — I also did some studio work, making an outline, on soft ground, upon a 5×7" plate, of Tower at Constance — varnishing the plate \+ preparing it for the acid — I wrote a letter to Wm. Douglas, the house painter, giving him directions for work to be done in 38th S. house before our return. At 2.30 I went out riding with Annie \+ Ralph — back before 5 — I gave Tower plate a nip in the acid after my return. Then with the men until 6. Evng. evng. after reading newspaper \+ attending to Stoker duties, I wrote a note to Farmington, Sub. N.Y. [illegible].

This page is an acct. of Thurs. Oct. 18 — a mistakes having been made in turning two pages, instead of one —
