## Left Page

### Wed. May 30, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Steamer "Puritan". Stateroom 208.

Brilliant mg, overcast afternoon \- Bkfp. at 8 \- at about 10 Annie \+ I started out, down the Bath road to the cliff walk \+ along the cliff to Ochre Point, stopping on the way to enjoy the sunshine \+ the sweet air \- then by a public road to Bellevue Ave. \+ by that to Bath Road again. A. \+ ? to the C's \+ I went down town \+ got our boat tickets \+ paid for them \- Telegraph. to Kate \+ then to C's before 1 o'clk. Dinner at 1.30 about 4 o'clk the carriage was at the door \+ Mr. C. A. \+ I went out for a drive, taking around about by way of Polo grounds, then to the Point \+ home by Bellevue Ave \+ other roads passing some of the show places. \- In soon after 5 \+ then I had a nap \- Dinner at 7. at Table until after 8 \- at 8.45 carriage came \- goodbyes \- got to wharf at 9 just as the steamer came up \- it was 1695 where she got away \- 720 bbls of "Scup" or Porgies put on board \- A. \+ I went over the vessel \- remounting eating \- immense affair \- one of the largest steam engines, 7500 horse power \- cab. It was about 11 when I turned in.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 31, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Home again.

Heavy rain during the night \- a little mizzle at times during the mg. \+ more rain at night. \- Up before 6 \+ found that we had entered the East River \- altho' it was raw \+ chilly I kept on deck until we made fast to the dock at 7 \- foot of Murray St. \- walked over to City Hall Park \+ by 3d. Ave. cars (cable) got home at 7.50 Bkfp. soon after \- later, I went to market etc \- then to Butler's \+ got out the paintings that have been stored there since before we went to Europe. \- Then to N. A. \+ spent some time there with Farrington getting things into shape \+ place. Then to 2d. Nat. Bk. \+ attended to A's business \- Home to lunch at 1 \- had a long nap \- after 3 went to Butler's \+ washed \+ oiled out four of my paintings to be sent to the Century where they will hang during the summer \- then to Century \+ arranged to have pictures sent for \- Home after 6. Found Nellie \+ Katie Adams there \- Dinner before 7 \- wet. Charl came in \- general chatteration until 10 \- then shut ups shop. . .
