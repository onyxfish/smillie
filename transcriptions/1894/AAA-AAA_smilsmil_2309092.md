## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 24, 1894

**Weather:** Bright m'f, cloudy afternoon, rainy even'g  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent most of the m'f. with Mr. Rich'd. M. Montgomery, a friend of Charles' who was urging the claims of a site for N.A. D. cor. B'dway & 56th S'., & writing letters to other brokers who had submitted other sites. About 12 I went to Academy of Medecine 17. W. 43d. S.: & spent a little time looking over some works on Hygiene etc. — preparatory to enlightening the Montrosians. — Lunch at 1.30 — Afternoon went to 23d. S. & thereabouts to do a little Xmas shopping, etc. — a bleak, dismal afternoon — did not get home until dusk. Dinner about 7 — Ralph at a Xmas party at Mr. Babcock's even'g. Geo. Cook came in for a few minutes — there was a great array of stockings, with chairs to hold the overflow, preparatory to to-morrow. Sue, Katchen & Eleanor, each having stocking & chair, in addition to our own circle —

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 25, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Before I got thro' with my m'f. to open Dr. Yale came in & we discussed the physical situation — Before noon Kim, Ralph & I went up to Centrale Park for a walk. Charming m'f. — bright & mild — some cloudiness in the afternoon — clear, cold night — We got home to lunch at 1.30
By the way — a couple of hours was spent after b'kf't — opening presents in the parlor. Sue, Katchen & Eleanor having stockings hung up for Santa Claus with the other members of the family. Geo. Smillie came in for awhile in the afternoon. At 6.30 we all went to Ed'wd. Cook's & were served with some eatables — I managed to pick out for myself a small meal. Then the youngsters had charades, absurdly grotesque caricatures of ancient classical, mythological, scriptural & historical subjects. Ralph appeared to advantage as a ham in Abr'r & Isdaa — as Josefle in his coat of many colors & as Jonah when cast overboard to the whale — The series was very amusing — The party broke up at 10 —
