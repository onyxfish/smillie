## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 16, 1894

**Weather:** Bright, balmy \+ spring-like.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After blfp. I spent some time about the house as an assistant \- Then, my regular mf shopping \- Then to N.A. \+ for a little time was with Farrington \- Nud to N.C.A. Bldg to begin my search for a studio. Found there a little upper end room, where I can put some of my things \+ do a little work. I then went to other bldgs \+ saw other rooms, but none of them were satisfactory. I got the refusal of the room until Monday. Then home to lunch \- As I was troubled with a headache the most of the afternoon and loafed away. At 6.30 Annie \+ I went to Mr. \+ Mrs. Brownell's \- No 8. 15th S\- where we met Mr. \+ Mrs. Jas. H. Morse \- Miss Putnam \+ Chas. Collins \- \+ had a very social \+ pleasant dinner. Satur. Mr. Cutter (Principal of Cutter's School for boys) came in with Mr. Cutter \+ added much to the pleasantness of the evef. It was quite 10.30 when all left \- Mr. \+ Mrs. Morse, Mr \+ Mr. Cutter Collins, Annie \+ I Riding uptown together in a 4th Ave. car.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 17, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day \- rain, more or less steady, all day. Up late. After blfp. I wrote several notes \+ fussed about assisting in various ways in the work of bringing order out of the home chaos that has reigned since our arrival. From after lunch until past 4 o'clk I worked at hanging pictures in the dining room \+ distributing much matter that had collected in the library \+ hall. Then I dressed \+ before I went with Annie to the opening of the Harvard Club rooms in their new house, 27 W. 44th S. Bright \+ very simple. Crowded, smart girls forming the larger part. Annie went with Mary Willard to the Barnard Club \+ I went home. A very quiet evef \- some more writing \+ early to bed.
