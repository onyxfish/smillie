## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 30, 1894

**Weather:** not so cold — somewhat overcast \+ very raw.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up late — it was 10 before I had my bkfst. At 12 I walked up to the Park, across to Manhatttan Sq. and took a look at the site offered to the M. on 77° St., then across the Park \+ out at 80° St. \+ down 5° Ave. Dinner at 2 — the two Throps, Roger \+ Robert being our guests. Ralph developed an indisposition after bkfst — \+ at 2 o'clk, his temperature was 104 3/5°. After dinner I went to Dr. Yale's \+ saw him — on my return gave R. a dose of Rhubarb \+ Soda. Geo. \+ Chas came in for a little while in the afternoon. Tea soon after 6, after which Roger left to take his train on the D. \+ W. to Buffalo. Ralph cleared his stomach at about 6.30 — undigested food that had lain there since my G. — fever continued thro' the night. I wrote a letter to Kohler explaining the character of the information I desired him to gather for the benefit of Bell A. while he is abroad. Took it to station \+ mailed it.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 31, 1894

**Weather:** mixed — sunshine \+ grey — pleasant, on the whole.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio at usual time \+ worked on outline "Rivr. Venice". Home to lunch — Ralph's fever in the mf. had abated considerably, but at 1 o'clk. it had gone up again — back to studio at 2.30 \+ home at about 6 — Ralph's temp. had got up to 104°. Immediately after dinner I went up to Yale's and waited there until 8.10 before he came in — getting his advice I immediately left — home — 9 — came in at about 10. R. had then gone into a quiet sleep \+ he was not disturbed. I read some \+ worked awhile upon my Nat. Site Scrap Book.
