## Left Page

### Tues. April 24, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day in spite of some clouds \+ one or two sprinklings of rain. Took K\- to school, &c. Then to work on Reed portrait \+ put in about a couple of hours of work. I wasted more than that time over two poor plates by two of the N.A. students. Went for R\- at 12.30 \- Mrs. Conklin brought him home with her boy, Kennedy, in a cab \- much to his delight. After lunch I resumed my work on students plates \+ at it until near 4 \- then went to R.\+V's \+ made appointment for to-morrow \- then, from Canal St., walked up home, getting in at 6.30. Sam. Coleman, just back from Mexico, called to see me about 5 o'clk. Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'f Charl took Katie to the Star Theatre to see Jos. Jefferson in Rip Vanwinkle \- as I was tired \+ sleepy I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 25, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful Spring day \- fresh \+ invigorating. Mr. Fogarty called just after bhfs. concerning Etching class at the League. Then I went down to K. \+V's \+ had pfs. from Reed portrait \- also from two plates by pupils \- and got home at 1 o'clk. Lunch. After that I went to Sherwood Bldg to A. H. Baldwin's Studio (he died last week) \+ there met his sister \- Mrs. Barlute. I spent a couple of hours with her selecting such things as belonged to Etching \+ printing \- These she presents to the N.A. of D. for the use of the Etching class. A valuable acquisition, I think. Home at 5. Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'f Annie \+ Katie T. went to a concert at Chickering Hall. Later Chas. Collins came \+ spent the eve'f with me until 10 o'clk.

Mrs. Curtis's Orchestra. Burnham Industrial Farm. The. John, Cond'r.
