## Left Page

### Wed. June 13, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot, muggy, misty day. I spent much of the m'f going about looking at the work under way. The proportions of this tank enterprise astonishes me. One item will give an idea of it — the lead for lining weighs nearly two tons. Our freight boxes etc, arrived during the m'f — also something by xpp. Some time was spent in getting the boxes about \+ contents out. Ev'f has a long talk with Cooley \+ Gill — the latter places all the blame for delay in putting up the smaller tank upon Herrick who told him to await until the frame for the larger one was complete. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 14, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very hazy, but cooler. Herrick went off this m'f \+ was gone all day. He said nothing to me about it before going. I shall have something to say to him. I spent much of the m'f with Cooley \+ his plumber laying out a plan for the plumbing in the house. Afternoon I cleaned out my trunk \+ distributed its contents to their various proper places. I went downtown twice during the day \+ spent most of the Ev'f over my news paper.
