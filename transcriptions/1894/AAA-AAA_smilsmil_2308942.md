## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 27, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day overhead — anything but brilliant under foot. After bkfs. I went down to R. & R. & got two pfs. from the Morgan plate — much disappointed — upper part of the head does not suit me it all — so I hurried home to do what I could to rectify it, stopping at Butler's & getting some mounting board — Went for R — Lunch at 1. Afternoon I worked on the head as long as I can see. Then went out & took a walk — Dinner a little earlier than usual — after it dressed & at 8 o'clk. Annie, Katie & I went to the Manhattam Club House where Mr. de Pongeon lectured upon Yucatan with Stereopticon illustrations — She had not reached the middle of her lecture when we had to leave — the subject was extremely interesting to me but it was not handled in an interesting manner — Very we went to S.E. Cor. Mad. Ave. & 37th St the palatial home of Jason Phelps Stokes where a very swell concert I was given in aid of a Charity & tickets $5.0 each — given to Annie Herbert — singing by Miss Marguerite Hall and Plunkett Green — I of course — the atmosphere in the crowded Room was suffocating — we left at about 11.30 & to bed after midnight.

morgan head. 5 h.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 28, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Grey day — everything afloat — mild and thawing — Took R — to school — Spent an hour & a half at Mr. Morgan's touching a proof, trying to bring it to the painting. my trust in the photo. has ruined the plate technically & I fear, artistically. It is without the tone or color sense that is quite possible in simple black & white. I could have got it I'm sure, if I had worked direct from the painting — but, working for such a length of time, depending wholly upon the photo — I get work upon the plate of a character that now I am very sorry is there — my change of base from photo to painting, has necessitated much anxious re working & fussing — The plate has lost me coverably, its spontaneity — Lunch at 1. I worked on the Morgan head until about 3.30 hn., washed up & walked to Central Park & back, on my Return found Mr. Punnell, sales man of Etching Club type — dandy young Jonas — he has sold two of my pen studies & has an offer of $75. for the proof of the jade plate — I am not a call for much to his surprise, for two reasons — first, I must get Mr. Stoke's permission — second, I am sure the offer is made under some misapprehension — the proof is not worth so much, if I can get B's permission & I shall be very glad to sell it for $25 — Reo. Mr. Ames came in with Anna & stayed to dinner with us — cold roast mutton — very much, understood at that, but he did not complain — He delivered a lecture in town this afternoon & there a — got held & nine — He left bef. 9 — Then Charles came in & stayed until 10 — & we got to bed at 10.

morgan head. 5 h.
