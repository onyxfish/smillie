## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 17, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very beautiful day \- As usual, most of the m'f was spent in directing men \+ things about the place \- Before dinner I wrote a portion on my letter to Mr. Wm Lee \- After dinner I completed it \+ then went down town to P.O. I completed my letter to Charl \+ enclosed A's Cert. of 5 shares, U.S.C. \+ H.R.R.R. Stock \+ sent it by Registered Post. Went to look after the Burgess earth \+ Hart to draw it \- Then had a talk with True concerning local politics \+ Sewers as an issue \- Then to the swamp \+ pasture where George is digging a trench \- less than a foot of sour, blk. soil on top, then dense clay. Then set out lettuce plants until tea time \- Eve'g. newspapers and walking on piazza \+ Road way until nearly \- then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 18, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ threatening \- Rain began to fall very gently about 4 P.m. \+ continued at intervals during the ev'g. By m'g mail I rec'd. a note from W. Geo. Read offering $600000 cash for the f'd. property \+ requesting an immediate answer by telegraph \- Teleg. to him to see Mr. Lee, who had instruct ions \- \+ to Mr. Lee, telling him of the offer, \+ saying that I would sign a contract to sell for $600 000, the purchaser to pay all costs of sale. In the afternoon I rec'd \- Reply from W. Lee saying that probably I would have to go to N.Y. on Thurs. \- I did much work out of doors, transplanting lettuce, etc., and directing the repair of damage to grading done by Sunday's storm \- Hart hauled some earth from Burgess's place down town \- In the afternoon I made glue paste \+ covered 12" × 14" aqua-tint box with muslin \- After tea I went down town \- Rain back before 9 \- Read newspapers after for an hour, then to bed.
