## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 31, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a night of howling east winds, rain was added in the early mf. It came in driving torrents until about 11 — then a lull — in the afternoon the wind swept around to the west \+ blew as fiercely from that quarter as it had from the east, \+ with it came lots more of rain. Annie went down town at 9 A.M. to keep an appt. with Smith the dentist. I worked in studio, biting in "Tower at Constance." Kept at it pretty closely, hoping to finish the biting to-day — but my Mordant was old and spent \+ the dry-ground in unfamiliar tome, so, I got along slowly, feeling my way at every step. For exercise, had to content myself with a walk on the piazza from 5 to 6 P.M. Eveff. I wrote titles on a number of mounted Surafsome photos.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 1, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ blustering \+ cold mf. with threats, as it seemed, of snow. The afternoon, however, was very pleasant, quiet, and moderate in temperature. I was engaged much of the mf. with various duties \+ explaining work to be done with Herrick \+ Stone. Then I got at my biting of "Tower at Constance" and completed it, the result interesting me very much. At about 5 P.M. I went down town with Annie. We sent a telegram to Dr. Danl. Adams at New Haven, congratulating him upon having reached his 80th Birthday. We had Rec'd a late invitation to a little celebration, to take place in their new house.

Eveff; I was quite busted by an attack of sneezing \+ cold in the head.
