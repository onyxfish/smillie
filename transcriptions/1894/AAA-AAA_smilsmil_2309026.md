## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 14, 1894

*Pic-nic to Silver Lake.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — partially overcast. Glorious moonlight — I went down town after blkf. & got Perego team — It was about 10.45 when our entire party, Mary & her two girls & Kate started for Salt Spring — Delong driving his greys before our Platform wagon. We got to Salt Spring at about noon & found a great raft of country people there, so we left and drove over to Silver-lake, getting there at 1.15 — Mary & I went in & made a call on Mr. & Mrs. Ed. W. Rose & were very well Reed. They gave us permission to drive in, together with the freedom of the grounds & the lake. They were very kind — got a dinner for Delong & made coffee for us. We had the usual scout by time in eating our meal at nearly 2 o'clk — Standing about or sitting in the dirt eating cold victuals. — Mr coffee came went the meal was over — Then the young Jessups came down from the house & carried [illegible] our young people & they had lawn tennis & after that the young men had a bath in the lake — The older & the younger ones went out in boats. Mary & I called upon Mrs. Wm. Main & her mother, Mrs. Phelibrown — at 6.20 I got away with A — Mary, Kate, Miss Friesduff, Edna, Louise, Jim & Ralph & got home at 8.10 — A great party came up from the Sawyers to admire the moonlight & our Piazzas — at 10.30 the remainder of the party arr — & at 10.45 we were all in bed

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 15, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — very hot & close in the mf. a short, sharp thunder storm at. 2 & 3 P.m. after which it cleared off very cool & breezy — cold moonlight night — In the mf I wrote a letter to Moran who had sent me maps of sites for N.A. I also attended to printing business for Annie's projected entertainment for Village Imp. Soct. After dinner I wrote a letter to Fanny — and then again down town to Printers' etc. Every I read newspapers & got to bed pretty early. The young people are kept pretty busy rehearsing for their theatrical entertainment next week.
