## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 28, 1894

**Weather:** Clear, cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold & windy - Still feeling out of sorts - Stomach disturbance & headache - I did not get up until 10.30 - At noon I went to grocers & butchers - Lunch at 1 - I was able to do a little reading on Typhoid Bacteria, preparatory to a letter to the Montrose newspapers. I rec'd. note from Jno. C. Taylor informing me that a Resolution had passed the Town Council requesting me to make enquiries here as to the probable expense of having a competent Sanitary engineer prepare a plan for the sewering of Montrose. Later, a large map of the Borough came by mail. Every I wrote & mailed a letter to S.R. Koehler -

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 29, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thanksgiving Day -

A bright, wintry day with a cold wind. So much out of sorts that I went out of the house but once during the day - to go to market & grocers in the mfg. I did some work about the house - most it being the gathering together of the things to be taken from the house to my studio - I loafed much of the p.m. - dressed & at 6 went with Annie, the boys & Katie T. to Ed W. Cook's to eat our Thanksgiving dinner - It was as my wont for me. Roast Pig - Turkey - mince pie \- Apple pie - Squash pie - Plumb Pudding \+ Ice cream - all of which I saw but none which was made a part of me. I carved the Turkey & had ample opportunity to wonder at the juveniles appetite & stomach capacity - After dinner came Charades \- like games in which Robt. Cook showed to advantage - His Lion's Roar was a piece of realism to be remembered - It was well after 11 o'clk - before I started for home with Ralph - the others soon followed - & it was midnight when I got to bed -
Walter & Lousie came in after dinner.
