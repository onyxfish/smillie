## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 8, 1894

**Weather:** Dull & dismal  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced fully before 4 p.m. \- [illegible] poured down later in the eve'g. I got to studio late and to work on Crésy drawing \- Home to lunch & back & worked as long as I c'd see \- Home at 4.30 & had a good rest before dinner, at which Eliz'th Hone joined us. \- She & Annie went to a Symphony Concert at Carnegie Hall while I went to an entertainment at the Century given under the auspices of the Art Com. "A Picture Play" written (& read) by Otley. Black with accompanying photo\-stereopticon pictures in illustration, groupings of character figures & out of door scenes, taken under direction of Mr. B\-. The story was rambling but full of clever bits \- on the whole, suggestive of having been written to fit the photographs. Some of the portraits \- were exceedingly good as photo\- & as pictorial arrangement of form, figure & effect \- others were pretty bad \- an amusing novelty & evidently gave some general satisfaction \- Time, 1¾ hours \- also pretty long & unnecessarily stretched for eyes & ears. After it I spent some time "gabbin it" \- Dr. Garster walked home with me \- to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 9, 1894

**Weather:** overcast & raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a late b'kf't. I spent some time arranging the many diagrams & building sites offered by makers to the S'te Com. & the N.A. at 1 o'clk. I walked up to Central Park & back in time for dinner at 2. A very dark afternoon which was spent loafing in-doors. Met at 9.30 Annie & I went to Mr. & Mrs. Gilbert Jones' to a musical entertainment \- quite a smart affair. The house has just been remodeled \- the music room is charming \- There were a number of small lions present literary, artistic & musical \- F. Sinzig played on the piano. Victor de Gomzeski sang in robust tenor Schubert, Rubenstein, etc. Miss Jeanne Faure pleased with a cultivated agreeable contralto voice \- the piece de résistance was a Ballad of Despair, the words by Munger, music arranged by N. Bemberg, who created his patron \- Miss Faure sang her part as the spirit of death, & was ecstatically welcomed by the weary & misanthropic des pair ing one, who followed Readily asking but one favor, that his dog might be allowed to survive him as he was sure that he would sorrow for him when he was gone \- There was a handsome supper \- hot & cold & champagne \-
we left at about 11.30
