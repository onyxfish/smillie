## Left Page

### Wed. June 27, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Much cooler \+ cleaver - most of my day was spent out of doors, much of it with Stone's son, finishing up on the west front where the cellar had been dug under the house. I blistered my hands in using rake \+ shovel myself. I wrote a letter to Col. Ge C. B. Waring upon the subject of Sewers for Montrose, asking advice \+ information. In the afternoon Mr. de Schweinitz \+ Florence Lathrop called while I was at work with a rake \+ without a coat. I went down town after tea - Eve'f. read my paper - wrote a note to Bogle for awning stuff and to C. S. Farrington - took them to P. O. after 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 28, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day - after a very light rain during the night. Smith \+ another man painting on Tank extensions on the barns. Henick at work laying floor of lower bath room - Stone among flower-beds \+ setting \+ patting Strawberry plants. I spent a part of the aft. transplanting lettuce \+ making Pansy bed on West front of house. I also wrote to Sam. Colman. I did some work in studio in the way of re- ducing the confusion — getting ready for serious work - It was nearly 5 when I went down town. back at 6 Eve'f., on Piazza until after 9. Nen spent some time over her garden note book.
