## Left Page

### Tues. July 3, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm day but the air delightfully fresh & clear with breeze constantly blow-
ing. Nearly all day spent directing the men — especially Herrick in his attempts to get straight the vent pipes and to casing running up the west front of the house. The painters, after finishing the barn roofs, painted over portions of the S. front of house on 2d. Story piazza. The Plumbers finished putting in sink in the Kitchen. I went down town in the m/f & gave Newcome a call, found him at work for some body else, & I got mad. — Went down again at 5.30 & did not get back to the until 7. — & at 8, again downtown. ## Right Page

### Wed. July 4, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very cool, pleasant day — Heavy showers passed a little south of us late in the afternoon — Well, almost too cool for comfort. I was out of doors nearly all day attending to things about the place until dinner at 1 o'clk. The boys had a grand time with their fire crackers & Annie & I helped them for a time. I went downtown at 10 — & again at 3 & again at 8 — On my return the last time I set off the fireworks for the boys — Roman Candles — Firewheels — Flower pots, mines & Rockets — Mr. & Mrs. T. Daniel Hughes & two or three young ladies came up to help look on. I wrote a letter to the Md. Republic in answer to D. V. Gardiner & F. J. Lott on the sewer question — It was 9 o'clk. when fireworks were over — Then I read paper for awhile.
