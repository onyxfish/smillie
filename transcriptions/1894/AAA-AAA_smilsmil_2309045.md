## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 21, 1894

**Weather:** cloudless day \- hazy \+ very warm.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I had many things to look after about the place. I wrote to Mr. Lee \+ another note to Ward \- and got to P.O. before 12 \- I also telegraphed to Mr. Lee \- more work in the afternoon on my aqua-tint boxes.
I went down town about 5 o'clk. Hart drew 5 loads of earth for grading \- Geo. Walker worked half a day ditching in the swamps \- Annie \+ Bessie drove over to Elk Lake. Eve'g. Mr. \+ Mrs. Daut. Sayre came in \+ with them young Grorsheck, a nephew of Mr. S's.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 22, 1894

*Bessie Throop left for home.*

**Weather:** A pleasant day, but very hazy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 6 \- blkfs. \+ then, with the children, escorted Bessie Throop to the station \+ saw her started on her homeward way \- then to P.O. \- \+ home. I fussed about over many things \- Hart had two teams drawing earth from Burgess's excavation down town \- bought up all that there was \- 22 loads \- \+ distributed it in various places about \- Stone did the grading \- Henick at work on bath-room door, \+, as usual, has blundered \- Vaccani at work on swamps drain. I got a long letter from Mr. Lee describing the progress of the negotiations with Mr. Read for sale of the l. of D. If the sale is ever consummated, that is, if there is [illegible] Twist auls \+ lefs, the A. will get a very high price for its property. \-
I am very well pleased as things stand \- and I fussed in the studio over my aqua-tint boxes \+ I wrote some notes. Eve'g. I went down to Judge Jessup's office to attend a Village Hub. Socy. meeting \- Only D. Sayre \+ wife \- Dean Tumee, [illegible] Jessup present \- nothing accomplished \- the incorporation of the Socy. was talked of \+ law books were consulted \- Home soon after 9.
