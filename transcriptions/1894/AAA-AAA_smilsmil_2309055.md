## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 11, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Blustering, windy day — with some brilliant sunshine — two or three light, driving showers at about sunset.
I got at my work in studio as soon as I could \+ finished another small sketch in Water-color, one of the four, "Ravello, Cathedral Tower" — \+ worked for awhile on "Towers, Carisbrooke Castle" — gave some time to the painters at Herrick's house — also to H — and his carpenters work — At sunset took R — \+ went down town — we had a walk — \+ in before tea time. Eve'g newspaper — \+ then, writing titles \+ memos of English \+ European photos.

Brilliant moonlight night — cloudless \+ still — Ther. fell to 33°.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ very beautiful day — clouding over at about 5 P.m. Sue f. cloudy \+ mild — It was 11 o'clk. before I got thro' my mult duties — Herrick at work upon his house — Smith \+ his two ass'ts painting it — Georgio at etching in the swamps \+ Stone in the garden — I then set to work on little Water-color, "Entrance Towers to Carisbrooke Castle" \+ before 3 o'clk. finished it — but not to my liking — Annie, Sleg. \+ Ralph went riding, out I amused myself cutting off the tops of a couple of elms that are beginning to shut out our southerly view from the Piazza. Farrington sent telegram about a new tenant offering for 5 \[illegible\] 2, 23d. St., I wrote him a short note in reply \+ mailed it before 6. Sue f. \- newspapers, shores, etc — etc. much troubled by giddiness —
