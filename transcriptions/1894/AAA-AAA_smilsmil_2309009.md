## Left Page

### Wed. July 11, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - a very light shower early in the afternoon - out & about all day, or nearly so. Spent some time in studio - getting all of my materials in order - going over contents of drawers \+ rearranging them for work - and I spent some time in finishing a small box, partly made a day or two ago, for dry ground aqua-tinting. I also worked in the garden, transplanting lettuce until after 8 o'clk. Then I puzzled for some time over a problem in figures - the history of hell, A - Snydams fund investments sent to me by Farrington.

Smith shot 8 sea squirrels about the house, in a very few min, this p.m.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some heavy rain during the night \+ continuous showers until after 10 A.M., then the rain ceased - at times the sun shone out \+ the air became hot \+ heavy. After the rain ceased I got to work transplanting lettuce \+ continued at it until after 12 - Set Henick to work making a slat arrangement to put over growing lettuce when the sun shine is very hot - Annie Rec'd a letter from Walter asking if he \+ Louise may come here next Tues. Afternoon. I worked on some plan drawings in studio - The eve'g, until near 10 o'clk., was spent upon the piazza - mild, southerly breeze \- a veiled, wet looking moon.
