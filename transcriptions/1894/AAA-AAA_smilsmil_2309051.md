## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 3, 1894

**Weather:** Bright \+ pleasant for a little while in the m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasant for a little while in the m'f. \- wind from east \- overcast at times \- ev'f. at 7 o'clk. a thunderstorm with heavy rain \- rain continuing thro' the ev'f. After b'f't I finished my letter to the Republican concerning sewers \+ with Ralph I went to the office and left M.S. there \- then to P.O. etc. \- \+ on our return, after putting the two white rats \+ their two little ones into a basket, we went to the fair taking them with us. There was a great crowd \+ some efforts even made to find purchasers for the Rats. At last we went into a "side show" where a prestidigitating young woman saw the animals took a fancy to them \+ bought them for 35 cents \- Then we got home to dinner at 1.30. Immediately after that Gretta \+ Katie went to the fair taking Ralph with them \- I wrote to Fan Pelt \+ to Miss Abbott \+ again went to P.O. \- Back at 4.30 \+ went up to fair ground for Ralph \-. After tea I read aloud to Elme until his bed time. Later n'l had a severe attack of nausea \+ was glad to be able to get to bed without tumbling to pieces.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 4, 1894

**Weather:** Cloudy, but mild \- clearing in the ev'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy, but mild \- clearing in the ev'f with thunderstorms north \+ south of us \- Go. Smith came in the m'f \+ I started him at painting the Herrick H'o \- I worked with Stone for some time transplanting lettuce into a cold frame. Then, until dinner time, I painted aqua-tint ground boxes \- After dinner I wrote a note to Nicoll and then Ralph \+ I started upon a round of inspection, etc \- We spent some time with Deans \+ his daughter, discussing tax valuation \- then to Rep'n Printing Office where I read pf. of my last sewer letter \- Then to Jeffeis mill where we saw some modern milling contrivances \+ talked horses \- Ev'f, after tea R. \+ I went to R.R. Station \- Annie \+ Eliz. came in on the train \- we walked home \+ the rest of the ev'f was spent chatting \+ with newspapers.
