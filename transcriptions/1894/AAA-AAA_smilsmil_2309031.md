## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 24, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot, dry day — no clouds, but a dim over the blue that seemed an addition to the closeness \+ heat. Rec'd. important letters on Academy business. one from W. Read, saying that he can't keep his clients waiting indefinitely — one from T. W. Wood, from President, declining to sign form of assent to sale of N.A. — one from F. A. Bridgman, signing assent, \+ one from Farrington. I wrote a reply to Read, pacificatory, \+ to W. Co[?]. Lee at Bar Harbor, asking for advice as to next move on my part \+ giving him a summary of what I have learned concerning prices paid for lots in the vicinity of the N.A.

Ev'g, many other girls \+ Kate were at the house — all hands at on the piazza until 10 o'clk — the young people talking nonsense by the light of two mellow Chinese lanterns —

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 25, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katie, Mollie \+ Roger Adams left for home — Carleton Cook left for Buffalo —

A close, muggy m'g. Then hazy \+ over cast — about 5 p.m. a thunderstorm passed north, very near to us — Ev'g, much cooler \+ clear — Up early — a 7 o'clk. b'k'f't — for the Adams's who got away at 7.30, expecting to get to New Haven this ev'g. — Then we had our b'k'f't — at 8 — Then I went down town \+ among other things made arrangements to get the fire loans from under the Universalist Ch. in its new position — Carleton Cook got away for Buffalo on the 12.30 train. I wrote to Farrington, and to Mr. George. A very quiet evening with newspapers \+ Harpers for d'ss't.
