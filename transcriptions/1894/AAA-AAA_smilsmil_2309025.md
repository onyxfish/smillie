## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced after 9 o'clk. \+ for a time it fell briskly but, so far as rain was concerned it was all over by noon - chilly, windy, raw \+ cloudy afternoon. Spent some time in garden getting vegetables \+ setting out lettuce plants - Then wrote a letter to Nicole - Dinner before 2 - Then wrote to Robbins - in both cases asking advice about getting appraisal of N.A. Real Estate by experts - Then I took an hours walk - Evef read Aug. "Cautery."

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 13, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

an overcast, misty m'f, gradually lifting - About 3 p.m. a heavy thunder shower passed to the south of us and after that, of which we got a very light sprinkle, the sky cleared beautifully until at night it was a still \+ cloudless moonlight. I got to work after bkf. \+ by diligent writing I got my letter to lawyer Wm. H.A. Lee written so that I got it off by the noon mail - Dinner at 1.30 - Afternoon I wrote a long letter to Whittredge - Then I went down town - The children went down to Wyalusing Creek \+ later Annie went after them with the Benton horse \+ buggy - getting home just in time for tea - Evef I went down town again \+ spent some time making preparations for pic-nic that is planned to Salt Spring to-morrow - Mary, Kate \+ the girls spent the evef with us - a full house.
