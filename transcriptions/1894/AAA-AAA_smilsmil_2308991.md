## Left Page

### Tues. June 5, 1894

Mollie \+ Frank Adams am: from Germantown.

**Weather:** Raw, overcast \+ disagreeable m\+. a few gleams of discouraged sunshine later in the day. Most of the day I spent in preparing our European photos, for mounting. Annie helping me a part of the time. In the afternoon Mollie \+ Frank Adams came unexpectedly from Germantown where they had been visiting. Jas. Wright was taken suddenly \+ seriously ill — a stroke of apoplexy, probably, from which he is not expected to recover. They (M. \+ F.) stayed to dinner — M. stayed over night \+ F. went to Fred Ireland's. Annie \+ I went to 165 W. 58th S. to the apartments of the Outram Sistrs, where about twenty friends were gathered to listen to Mrs. Dr. Jacobs read the paper in favor of woman's suffrage that she read before the committee of the Constitutional convention at Albany a few days ago. The reading took more than an hours time. It was rather diffuse but seemed to leave no ground uncovered \+ had many very clever \+ telling points. Then we had ice cream, cake \+ punch — a little chatting with one another, after which we walked home getting in at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 6, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day. dull \+ cold until about the middle of the m\+ when rain began to fall, continuing until about 6 o'clk. Mollie \+ Frank left for home in the m\+. After b\+p — Wm. Gee came \+ made us a visit. I went over to W. 29th to Newman's \+ saw my pole rocker under way. Then to Schard's in Wooster St. \+ got copper plates that he has cleaned for me. Went to K. \+ V.'s to sign Morgan p\+p but so much time had already been lost that I was too late to accomplish anything — home to lunch — nearly all of the afternoon was given to making up a new set of etching ground. Dinner at 6.30 — Rec'd a letter from Mr. Durand enclosing a check for \$100 (promised to pay 150) saying that that was all he was willing to lose — that I might have the plate etc, etc. It is the most astounding piece of business to me. I hardly know whether to take it seriously or not — It seems to me, at times, as I try to grasp it, like a ghastly joke. Charl came in later in the ev\+.
