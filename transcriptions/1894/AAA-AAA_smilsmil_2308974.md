## Left Page

### Wed. May 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 83°

A very uncomfortably hot day. Mr. Fogarty called after bkfs- \+ spent some time working upon pf- from his etching "Man \+ umbrella" — then I went to R. \+ V's \+ got 4 pk. of Reed portrait — much improved — 2 of them on Toronoco paper — beautiful pf. — Dr. Ferster came in before I left — Home before 1- Annie \+ Katie not at lunch — I fussed over my pfs- stretched the Toronoco ones. etc — etc — at 5 o'clk. I went to school for R- Dinner at 6.30. Evef made out list of garden seeds for Montrose.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 3, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast much of the time — cooler than yesterday but more muggy — sharp breeze from the east at night. Domestic business after bkfs — then to W. Frank Jones' — 5th Ave. and 15th S." Quarterly Ill'r." A long interview with him concerning paper on etching \+ the club \+ other matters — met Chas. Collins \+ went to his desk with him — then to Keppel's \+ looked at things there — then to Wunderlich's — then to Vantine's and got some silk pó dabbers" — then to Bridge. man's \+ got seeds for Montrose — then to 2° Nat. Bank — then to D. B. Butler's \+ then home — lunch — took R. to school — then back \+ packed Durand's plate \+ a proof — Duran Reed's Portrait. at 4.30 I went to Art Students League \+ met five of the class — with them until 6.40 — most of the time "biting". Home at 7 — dinner — then, at once, Annie, Katie \+ I went to "Sherry's" Cor. Fifth Ave. \+ 37th S: to all woman's Suffrage meeting — a tremendous jam — managed to get inside \+ heard a little but the discomfort was too great \+ soon after 9 o'clk. we extricated ourselves \+ got out a back way \+ then home where we spent an hour or more reading before going to bed.
