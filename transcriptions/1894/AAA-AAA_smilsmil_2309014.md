## Left Page

### Sat. July 21, 1894

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast all day, but dry. A few drops of rain fell bet. 5 \+ 6 P. M. from the outskirts of a thunderstorm S. E. from us. Some time was given to our visitors in the mf, then I wrote a couple of letters. Dinner \+ after that, Louise, Annie, Walter \+ the boys, with fish poles \+ bait walked over to Jones's lake, while I forked up the old Pansy bed, forked it over \+ liberally manured it \+ then set out 48 heads of lettuce. About 5.30 threats of rain — sent Hemck with umbrellas to meet the fishing party but so little rain fell that they got but a slight dampoing. Evef at 8 I went to P.O — then to Judge Jessup's office where Judge W[illegible], Jessup, Agardathro[illegible], Saul, Sayre Edgar Turrell \+ I discussed the matter of getting the newly purchased Universalist ch. building properly deeded to the Village tmfs. Socy — also to get bids for moving the S[illegible] back from the street \+ further away from the new School bldg — After g when I got home, then Louise \+ I played a game of Halma abt Walter \+ he beat us by one move.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 22, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A sulky day — but no rain — close clouds all day until a little before sunset, when, for a few minutes, there was a rift \+ some sunshine. Late blft — I had a stupid headache but managed to keep about — dinner at 2 — It was after 5 when Annie, the boys \+ I went out for a tramp, — we went to the lake \+ skirted its shores all the way around getting back after 7 — then supper — Evef general chatterration, part of the time upon the pleasant subject of sewerage.
