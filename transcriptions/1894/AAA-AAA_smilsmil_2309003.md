## Left Page

### Fri. June 29, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot day, with veiled sun much of the time. Painters at work on East end of house covering the patchwork of new \+ old with a coat of one color. I found a great deal to do out of doors \+ was very little in the studio. At 6.30 Annie \+ I went to Agar Tathraps where we met Mr. \+ Mrs. Christman, Mrs. de Schminity. Mr. Walter Tathrap \+ Jno. Courtright \- all had tea at 7 \+ after it "Duplicate Whist", a new form of the game to me \- an arrangement \+ by which each side replays the game with the "hands" or cards dealt at first \- we played a series of eight games in that way \+ found it interesting. Annie \+ Mrs. Christman played against Mr. C \— \+ me. During the evening we had a comforting thunder shower. Home soon after 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 30, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day \- very warm but wonderfully clear with refreshing breeze \- out all of the m'f. working much of the time in the lettuce bed \+ patting white strawberry plants. At dinner time had home-made ice cream. \+ in the afternoon Jim churned in the little 4 qt. churn that I contrived this m'f. \+ got nearly a pound of butter. I made working drawing for out room door \- Box came from Cullen with European photos mounted. After tea, watering places, \+ such work on piazza until after 9 \- then lighted lamp \+ enjoyed night bugs \+ reading for an hour or so.
