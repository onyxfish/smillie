## Left Page

### Fri. May 4, 1894

**Weather:** Some sunshine, but mostly overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ at times a raw, east wind blowing. After hlp., regular duties — then to Wall S. No. 52 — Office of M.J.S.&W. R.R. with A's Income Bonds \+ got int. on them. Then to N.A. f.D. where I met Mr. Davies, the Accountant, \+ his assistant, Mr. Gardener \+ was with them going over, through \+ under the acts, until about 12.30 or near 2 o'clk. — Then Mr. D. \+ I went to the 2d Nat. \+ Safe Deposit \+ made search there — It was about 2 o'clk. when we parted — then I went to Wells, Jones \& Co. off. with pkg for Jno. Durand — Etched plate \+ proof — Portrait of Luman Reed. \+ after paying $3.70 for yp. \+ my started for home \+ got my lunch at 2.30 — Then prepared a 7×9 plate \+ laid ground for Julius to etch upon. At 5 I went for R— light Rain — Then to N.A. until 6.30 — dinner at 7. Wed., wrote a letter to Jno. Durand. Annie spent most of the day at Yonkers — last meeting for her \+ as m of Unitarian League.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 5, 1894

**Weather:** weather much mixed. Overcast \+ threat.  **Temp:** [illegible]

ening in the m'f. clearing to be close and muggy before noon — overcast again \+ heavy rain at night. After usual duties after hlp. I settled myself, trying to write a short paper on etching for Harry Jones of the Quarterly Illustration — but my head ached \+ I felt so stupid that, before ll, I gave it up. Then I went to Brooks Bros. \+ ordered a suit of clothes — Then to 2d Nat. Safe Dep't. Co. \+ put A's M.J.S.\&W. Bonds in her box — then home to lunch — after that I went to K.p. \+ spent some time with Farrington \+ soon after 3 I went to League \+ gave class a lesson in laying grounds — Home before 7 \+ had dinner. About 9 went to Century — monthly meeting — buzzed with a number of the N.A's. about candidates for Annual Meeting on W'd. There are threatenings in the air but I could not get onto any organized plan of campaign. After 11 o'clk I had to run home through a heavy rain without an umbrella. — got pretty wet.
Charle took Katie to Theatre to see "1492".
