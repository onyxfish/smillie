## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After unsuccessful night I awake with a very severe headache \+ all the indications of influenza. I struggled thro' the day, which was bright and mild, accomplishing very little but suffering much. Much depressed because it seems impossible to get away at the time set, next Monday mt. Sent Rec. Mr. Benton's horse \+ outfit back to him with check for $22.00 Evelf. hung about until 9.15 then to bed, at which time my temp. was 100°.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 3, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Violent wind all night, swinging around from east to west, bringing in the early mt heavy showers of rain. Cleared before 10 o'clk — strong wind \+ heavy clouds all day — growing colder — I got up with normal temperature but with very severe headache, but, altho' it continued with little abatement I managed to get a good deal of work done in the course of the day. I did not go out of doors at all, Annie \+ Herrick doing that work for me. Evelf. finished writing titles on mounts of European Photo. views. Temp. again above normal, but less than last night.
