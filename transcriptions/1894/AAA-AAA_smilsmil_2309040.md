## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 11, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, breezy day, quite cool, so that we had fires in the house \+ studio. \+ I went with Dant. Sayre down to the pasture to see the stone heaps there \+ find a way, if possible, that it may be hauled to fill in in front of Chapel Hall \- Men, for a time, I directed Stone in his grading \- at last, I got to work in the studio \- set my palette \+ commenced a rough oil sketch "View of the Island of Capri, from Naples". Hart came up after dinner bringing some wood ashes \- then he, his man \+ his team worked a part of the p.m. raking up stone \+ taking a couple of loads down to Chapel Hall. I got my sketch laid in at 5.30 \+ then went out \+ walked from hour \- Annie \+ Katie rose over to South Pond. Evef. Mary, Katie \+ Bessie to tea \- They left before 9 o'clk. as Mary \+ Katie return to P.k in the mt early \- Bessie is to visit with us for about a fortnight. Brilliant, still moonlight.
To day I rec'd a letter from Mr. Ge? Read the tone of which makes me hopeful that we may reach the end of this Academy business before long.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary \+ Katie ret. to P.o.k \-
Russia came to us \-

A very pleasant day. A glorious moonlight night. Men with two teams began hauling the pile of stones from the pasture \+ filling in in front of Chapel Hall. I was there for some time \- then I wrote a draft of a letter to send to Mr. Read. At 12 I went to Start's to look at some earth that he thought was good for lawn top-dressing \- but it is nt \- After dinner I spent most of the afternoon with Herrick in the shop superintending the making of a large box (12"×18"×36") for dry-aqua-tint ground laying \- then to Stone, who is still at work grading the Croquet gr'd \- \- then to the pasture \- to Chapel Hall \- to P.O. etc \+ then home. Evef. spent some time walking on the Piazza \- later, Annie read aloud \+ I slept.
