## Left Page

### Mon. June 25, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thick, foggy m'f, with breeze - quite cool, but later in the day it came off very close & muggy. Thunderstorms near us in the late afternoon & early even'g — a gentle rain from 7.30 until near 10 - I had a number of things to look after, the men at their work, etc. etc, until 11. when I went downtown - Jim & Ralph with me. Among my letters at P.O. was one from Heber Bishop, very offensive in tone, repeating his demand for my p's from his jade plate. It seems that he has but just got my letter written to him last march - It quite upset me and made me feel actually sick. At Cooley's I have order for a Sanitas [illegible] syphon water closet - Dinner aff't. Afternoon I worked in studio, trying to paint some Poconias, but I made sad work of it, sorely wearied & worried myself - Before & after tea I had a walk in piazza - Very much time spent over Sunday Tribune.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another foggy, muggy m'f. Some very light sprinkling showers fell before noon - Afternoon a fresh breeze sprang up improving the atmosphere very much. I spent much time during the m'f, bossing the mason who is laying a bit of wall under the West sill of the house, a part of the new water system. I tried for a while to get something in color or black & white, of the beauty & charm of the Poconias, but was at last obliged to give it up as a bad job — very depressing to me. I also did some digging & got very warm before tea — after tea I went downtown - Even'g, after reading newspaper, Annie read awhile, aloud, part of a historical sketch of Venice.
