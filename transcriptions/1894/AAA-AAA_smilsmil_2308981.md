## Left Page

### Wed. May 16, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day but mucky & overcast. After a late bkp. I went out & attended to my errands — Then back & wrote a letter to Mr. Jas. Dole of Chicago — \+ fussed about among the confusions of pkgs & such truck that has accumul. to in my work room (the nursery) at home. Lunch at 1 — Then, for Katie, I took a note to U. L. club for Charl — Then to Donnell's with portions of Montrose lamps — then to Avery Sortgies Galleries — then on to U. A. — Then to Butters' — then home in time for dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g, young Clark called to see Katie T — Charl came in later. At 11 I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 17, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annie \+ Ralph ret'd from Phila.

Pleasant mng. The day growing more overcast & the wind more easterly and chilly until night time. Spent most of the mng at NA with Farrington \+ Nicole. Had a long talk with Nicole about Academy matters — Home before 1 o'clk. On my way to NA stopped at Con. Ga. Co's office \+ had a conference with an official there concerning NA's bills with a view to economising — Just as we were about to sit down to lunch, Annie Katie \+ Ralph arr'd from Germantown direct, all well \+ having had a good time — I wrote a note to W. H. G. Marquand asking an appointment that I might get his advice in Academy matters. Then I went to Art Students' League \+ met there Fogarty \+ Miss Toni Roslofo — with them until after 6. Home at 7 — dinner. Eve'g Charl came in \+ the eve'g was spent in chatter.
