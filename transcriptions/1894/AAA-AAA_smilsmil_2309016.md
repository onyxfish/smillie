## Left Page

### Wed. July 25, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably hot day, with a milky skim in the atmosphere. Thunder storms all about us the afternoon + ev'g but nothing came. In the garden until 11, then to P.O. Got letter from Wm. H. & lee, with form of circular to N.A's + form of assent to sale of N.A., naming me as agent. I went to Rep. Printing Office + gave orders to print then wrote to Wm. H. & lee, to Robbins + Nicoll. Much time has to be given to plumbers who were finishing the setting of Water-closet. They worked until near 9 o'clk P.M. Then left it as finished but when Walter + I tested it we found the action imperfect for some reason, that we could not discover. They will have to come back + reset small fault. Water was turned on about 7 P.M. Ev'g Walter, Louise + Annie sat on Piazza \- 3 chinese lanterns furnishing a dim light.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another milky hot day. The boys were up early to go on a fishing excursion. Bkfs. promptly at 8. at 9, W. A. + the boys got away, riding over to Ben Muelford's farm where they spent the m'g fishing his pond + catching nothing. I spent some time writing to Farrington. Then down town. Read pf. Then to Mr. Benton's, the Prestn minister + talked horse with him. he praises very highly his 9 yr old horse as just the best for Annie. While at dinner E. Goodwin drove up to show me the track that he thinks I should buy. afternoon I wrote short notes to Geo. to Nicoll + to Robbins + to Farrington. went to printers + got circulars and mailed them with notes I had written. It was 5 o'clk. when I got a team at livery stable + soon after, Louise, Annie, Walter + I went off for a ride down Meshoppen Valley. Back at 7.30 Tea at 8. Mr. Gilbert. his daughter + her husband, Withant, called. Ev'g we looked over a lot of our european photos.
