## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 25, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A grey day, not so cold, nor so windy as yesterday \- wind changing to easterly \+ snow at night. Blp: very late \- about 10 \- wrote some notes etc \+ soon after 12 went out \+ walked up to Central Park and back getting home to dinner at 2 \- again in the afternoon I wrot \- quite a long letter to Kashler \- about 5 I again walked up to Central Park, getting back just in time for tea \- Eve\. f. I went to Walter Coat's but both he \+ Louise were out \- sucy at home \- Charl came in \+ stayed until 10.30.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The snowstorm that commenced last night grew in severity until this m\[or\]f it was a howling gale with sleet and oceans of mushy slush \- later in the day it got down to work as a serious, old fashioned snowstorm \+ it kept it up until into the night \- R\. did not go to school \+ I did not go out \- got to work on Morgan head \+ stuck to it faithfully until near 6 o'clk\. \- then I went to the butcher's \+ got some chops \- dinner at 6.30. We f\. did not go to Council meeting but after reading newspaper I got to bed early.

Morgan head \- 6 h.
