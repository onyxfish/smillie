afternoon about 5, clouds, threatening a thunder storm, which did not come, mitigated the heat. An exceedingly quiet clouded evef. ## Right Page

### Sat. July 14, 1894

**Weather:** Cloudy mf. with some thunder \& light spatters of rain but before 11 o'clk. the clouds had disappeared \& the sun blazed out almost as hot as ever \-  **Temp:** [illegible]

With Stone for some time setting out lettuce. Then with Herrick directing about the finish of the two bath rooms \- While at dinner Jim saw a young woodchuck coming across the east lawn. He was chased to the fairground wall, which was too much for him \+ he took refuge under some ferns \- I came up \+ knocked him over with a stone \- Jim carried him to the house, where he revived and I had to beat him over the head with a stick in a murderous manner \- Afternoon, I spent some time in Etching Room \- mounting photos. and covering blinds \+ doing other preparatory work \- Then I had a ladder set up to balcony on the west, from Cherry Pit Room \+ trained Virginia Creeper on balcony \- Evef. at 8 went to P.O. \- train late \- did not get home until 9 o'clk \- Found that Ralph had been crying \+ could not get to sleep because he could not drive from him the image of the murdered wood chuck. [illegible] newspapers until after 10.
