## Left Page

### Thur. July 5, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. below 60° at 8 A.M. The day singularly cool — delicious — We all slept until 8.30 — late bliss. of course. As usual most of my time was spent out of doors attending to the many matters constantly calling for my care in all directions. Before noon I commenced to make a box 12" × 16" × 21" for my dry aqua-tint experiments. After dinner Annie + I went out to the Currant bushes + for an hour we devoted our attention to the gathering of currant worms — while at that work Mrs. Christman, with Joe Williams drove up. Annie + I went with them to Dr. Norris's, 5 or 6 miles South. Mr. N. is very ill. We left our cards — got home at 6.15. Tea — then again went down town.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 6, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy much of the day with threats of rain — a few drops fell but about 9 P.M. there was a good rain — The night was quite cool — As usual, all of my day was consumed by matters outside of my studio work — I wrote some letters + attended to a little business. I superintended Herrick who at last finished casing the ventilating pipe up the west front of house. I directed the painters in mixing color + who did a little work, but left, fearing rain — also the tinman who fixed the roof — + I went down town two or three times. Every I spent some time reading Col. Geo. R. Waring's book. "Modern Methods of Sewage Disposal".
