## Left Page

### Sun. May 6, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some heavy showers in the early mg. Then cleared muggy \+ hot. Late bkfs. — then wrote for a little while on Harry Jones' "Etching" paper then out for a walk — went to the Century Club \+ there met Maynard \+ had a talk with him about N. A. matters. Home to dinner at 2 — then a little more writing — then went to Hotel Waldorf, message for A. — then by Elevated R.R. to 9th Ave. \+ 62d S. Went in to Mus. Nat. His. for a few minutes — then to Park, across \+ \+ but at 65th S. \+ home by Mad. Ave. car. Tea at 7 — Evg. Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 7, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 76°

Pleasant but very warm. I spent most of the mg. writing a short article on "Etching \+ Painter-Etching" for the Quarterly Illustrator. After lunch went to N.A. to meet Mr. Davies, the Accountant — he had his report \+ a statement of N.A. financial affairs prepared but my inquisitiveness upon one or two points made a deal of trouble, especially in the matter of the Suydam fund, so that I was kept until after 6 o'clk. while Davies \+ Farrington were at work unravelling the history of purchase \+ sale of securities for more than 20 years past. Dinner at 6.30 — Evg. Council meeting — nothing exciting — Home at 11.30 \+ to bed at midnight.
