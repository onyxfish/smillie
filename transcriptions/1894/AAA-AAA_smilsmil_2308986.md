## Left Page

### Sat. May 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little sunshine but still sulky & chilly. After 4½p. walked to Harvard Sq. + thence by trolly car to Mus. Fine Arts + there I spent the rest of the day with Koehler until near 5 o'clk. We looked over much work + together studied many old Aqua-tint + mezzotint plates puzzling ourselves over technical questions that have proved unsolvable riddles to other men more able than either of us. At 5 o'clk. we went out + had lunch + after that we went to Victor's Studio to call on him, but he was out, then we ret'd. to the museum + continued our studies. I walked all the way home crossing the new bridge (Harvard) - Saw the start of an informal boat-race — four four-oared shells — I got in after 6 o'clk. Pretty well tired. Tea before 7 — Eveny. Eleanor showed me her art work — I & I read news papers. I got to bed after 10.30.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 27, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At last, some pleasant weather. Early mist, thick + raw, clearing later to sunny + warm. I had a half hours walk before 4½p. - at q. - after 4½p. - I loafed about. Annie went to church + heard young Saml. Elliot, son of Pres. Elliot, preach. Dinner at 2. I took a long stroll down to the College buildings + grounds + got back to the house rather tired + feeling so unlike eating that I took no tea but for an hour or two lay upon the lounge in our bed room. In the Evey. Mr. W. Copeland called. He is a nephew of Prof. Dunbar. The two we met, + made their acquaintance, on a crowded train from Cologne to Ostend a year ago last Septr.
