## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 7, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

miss frueauff ret. to her home.

Very pleasant day \- Immediately after bkft. Miss frueauff, Annie \+ the children walked over to the Montrose R.R. station \- miss f. left on the 9.30 train on her way to Bethlehem \- By the ½ mail I rec. a safely from Mr. Geo. Read to my note of the 5th saying that it will be useless to talk of anything more than $6000.00 for the R.R. I wrote an evasive safely to him \+ then wrote to Mr. Lee at Bar Harbor giving him information to date \+ asking his advice \- The work on Croquet ground grading progressed favorably, Hart \+ his man hauling on the earth from under Chafee Hall \+ Stone spreading \+ raking it \- At 6 P.M. nearly half of the ground was covered \- Evef., Some time to newspaper, as usual, \+ some time to walking on the piazza.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 8, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, foggy mf., rain commenced before 10 A.M. \+ fell quite heavily for three or four hours \- There was some thunder \- After it ceased raining, it sulked \+ it was very close \+ muggy \- Hart came with his team \+ man before 9 but the rain drove him off before 10, obliging him to leave the job of grading the croquet lawn within half a day of finish \- The rain, while sadly needed, comes very inconveniently a few hours too soon \- I got to work at last upon my dry aqua-tint experiments and made great progress with my dusting box and my powdered gums \- I pulverised Mastic Dragons Blood, Burgundy Pitch \+ Resin \+ laid grounds \+ bit in portions \+ each of them \- Evef. given up to newspaper \+ watching some games played by the others.
