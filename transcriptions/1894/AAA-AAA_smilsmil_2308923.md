## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 20, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beautiful day — Got up with a nasty headache which interfered much with work & comfort in the mf. Before 1 o'clk. I was obliged to go to bed where I stayed until near 6 sleeping much of the time — Then I got up feeling better & walked up to 70th St. & 5th Ave. & back before dinner. Evelf Annie went with Mary — to Abbey's Theatre — Bdway & 38th St — to see Coquelin & Jane Hading in a French play — Having had sleep enough in the afternoon I wrote quite a long letter to Wf Russell Wray — critical & historical, at his request, touching his little book — A Review of Etching in the U.S. To bed after midnight.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 21, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day, not cold, but with a raw & disagreeable feeling in the air — up very late — bkft. at 10 o'clk. It was after 12 when Ralph & I went up to Central Park for a walk — we got home a few minutes after 2 — dinner — I loafed away what remained of the afternoon — Elig. Home took tea with us & in the evelf — a nasty, wet one — the two of them went off to a concert at the Met. Opera Ho.
