## Left Page

### Wed. March 21, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day - with fog + showers. my head responsive to the weather - Took R - to school - then I spent most of the m'f making silk dabber, laying grounds + otherwise preparing to meet my Etching class at the M. A. for the first time. Saw Wm Douglass + gave to him Percy Read's order for floor varnish. Went for R - + on our way home were caught in a very heavy shower — I ate little lunch + was then so used up that I went to bed + stayed there until 3.30 - then up + walked down to N. A. with Katie T — after some business with Mr. Farrington I met my class. H.M. Walcott - M. Bigelow - Trausel. Mettkessel. Miss Levick - Miss Stove did not come. I found them very much interested + more than half of them at once impressed me as being promising material. In spite of my headache I was able to talk to them — I had taken down three plates with grounds up on them — Miss Levick. Posed + Walcott, Trausel + Mettkessel made slight sketches upon the copper. It was after 6 when I got away — Dinner at 6.30 — Surely Mary Cook came for Annie + together they went to a Reception given at the house of Mr. (Rev.) Williams to Mr. Fenelosa — I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 22, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, pleasant m'f — cloudy - then heavy rain in the afternoon + eve'f. I took R - to school - then went to W. Morgan's with green + red [illegible] of Etched portrait — had to wait some time for him + when at last he came down he gave me scant time grudgingly — He made one or two more petty corrections + wants to see an imp. - in a terra cotta color — He is leading me in a nagging sauce — I hope that he will get thro' with it by + by — Then I went to market + from there to Einier + Amend's + bought some Nitric Acid — then home + prepared the three plates, etched at the class yesterday afternoon, for biting, lunch — the the afternoon was spent biting in the three plates — between showers I had a short walk before dinner —
Evely Chark came in — other Mr. James Gifford of Hudson; who, with his wife, we found to be such pleasant traveling companions while abroad. Unfortunately, Annie was in bed with a headache + did not see him.
