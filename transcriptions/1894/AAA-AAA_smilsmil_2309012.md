## Left Page

### Tues. July 17, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day but with pleasant air stirring. Another day of pretty accomplishings — I wrote a letter to Ge° — now at Bar Harbor — \+ a note to John Dartain asking him to define the term "a turn" in the laying of mezzo-tint grounds — \+ to the "Sanitas" W.C. people asking how much longer I shall have to wait for the W.C. — ordered about a month ago. The two bath tubs arr— at last to-day. I spent some time with the painters — \+ Hamich finished the last of the little things to be done in up-stairs bath-room. Watering plants until 8.30 — then, until 10 on piazza — Then, lighted up in-doors \+ gave a little time to news paper.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 18, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

C.S.J. \+ W.J. Van Pelt came [from N.Y.]

A very hot day — brazen, cloudless sky — a suggestion of thunder in the air but no storm came. Much of my day was given to directing Warner who was at work in basement bath-room hanging, or rather, pasting to the wall the "Decorator's Burlaps" that I sent for. After Tea I went to R.R. station \+ awaited arrival of train upon which S.C. Farrington \+ W.J. Van Pelt came — went direct to Montrose H° with Mr. VanP., left him there to wash up \+ then, home with C.S.J. who will stay with us over night. About 8.30 Mr. VanP. came \+ for an hour \+ a half we talked over his business, which is an overture to purchase the N.A. for $600000 — He is backed, I am very sure, by the Met° Life Ins° Co. of N.Y. altho' he will not tell. I agreed to work for the accomplishment of such result. He presses for an early settlement \+ a meeting of N.A.'s will probably be called for Aug. 8t — About 10 C.S.J. \+ I walked with VanP. to the Montrose H°. Then home.
