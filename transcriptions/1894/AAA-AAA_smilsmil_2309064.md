## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 29, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another brilliant, cloudless & mild day. All day out of doors superintending Herrick who was at work in the trees cutting out tops & dead wood. The Beeches near the house to the north, took out attention all the m'f. \+ in the afternoon he completed the topping of the maples on the lane. I am much pleased that so much of this work is accomplished. Hart, his team, his man, \+ Joe Mawhinny worked on the road in the lane, hauling hard pan from the ditches in the pasture swamps \+ spreading it over the road. It is an experiment. I am afraid that it is clayey \+ may give trouble in wet weather. I shall have only a portion of the road covered with it. Annie went riding, taking Mr. Courtright with her. I went to P.O. before 6 — At noon I went down, by request, to look at the colors that are now being put on Presb'n. Ch. Spire. Ev'f. wrote to Farrington \+ mailed it — then wrote a few more titles on European photos.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 30, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunshine for a little while in the m'f. Then overcast, growing thicker until ev'f when a high, howling wind prevailed. I spent the entire day out of doors at work among trees \+ plants — most of my time was given to a beech tree from the trunk of which I removed a great lot of dead \+ decaying wood \+ then coated the exposed portions heavily with hot Tar. I had a tree transplanted to the fair ground lane, on the north of the Herrick house. Hart, his team \+ a man hauled hard-pan from the pasture lot swamps to the road in the lane, but as the earth is not to my liking I told him to stop at the end of the day. Mawhinny worked with him. went down town before 6 — Evening wrote a short letter to Charl.
