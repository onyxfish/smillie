## Left Page

### Sun. June 3, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day - very warm in the sunshine but tempered by refreshing breeze. Bklp. at 9 - It was about 11 when Nelie, Katie A., Annie, Ralph \+ I started out - by Elevated R.R. to 8' Am \+ 16" S- thence west, across Morning Side Park, north of Asylum grounds to Riverside Drive \+ Grant's Tomb - Down the Drive to 108' S- \+ thro' to Boulevard Cars \+ home at 2 for dinner - late in the afternoon I had another walk - a short one - Tea at 7 - Evef. gee cook came in - later Alice Ireland, and then, Fred -

## Right Page

### Mon. June 4, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nelie \+ Katie Ret'd. to New Haven.

Heavy thunder storm last night - Cloudy \+ muggy most of the day - at times, sunny \+ my hot. Nelie \+ Katie Adams left for their home immediately after bklf'. After my regular calls I went to shoemakers - then to Newmans W. 29." S- with my Sidney James brass castings \+ a pole rocker - went to Freeman of brass finishing room \+ described to him what I wished. Then to 23' S. \+ saw W. Edwards of Photogravure Co. \+ he took me through a portion of his establsh ment \+ showed me the arrangement for laying dry aqua-tint grounds - Then to 2' Nat. Bank - to Knordler's - to Lord \+ Taylor's - underclothes to N A of D- \+ home to lunch - found Fanny Kimball there - she lunched with us - the most of the afternoon I spent in getting our sun-burn photos ready for mounting - Dinner at 6.30 - Evef, Mr. \+ Mrs. V. Stilwell, auold Phil's playmate of Annie's called - To bed early -
