## Left Page

### Tues. July 31, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very close \+ insufferably hot \- a skim over the cloudless sky giving it a milky, opalescent hue. Up before 7 \- got saddle on the Benton horse \+ rode him probnl half an hour before bkfp. Jogged thro' my work as easily as possible during the day as my head troubles me \+ I feel tired. I am now receiving returns from my circulars \+ several of the M.A.'s have written asking questions that I must answer, altho' replies to them are in the circular sent, so, I wrote about half a dozen notes \- Com. Beard is the only one so far who has declined to assent. He was so unwise as to give his Reasons \+ of course, I "went" for him, proving that his Reasons had no foundation. I got a letter from Van Pelt offering me 8 lots on the So. side of Manhattan Sq/\- At noon I drove to Wykensing creek for the boys \+ brought them home to dinner \- at 5 A. Ralph \+ I went out for a ride, getting in before 7. Then tea \- then to P.O. \+ got more replies \- home at 9 \- wrote awhiles, then to bed

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 1, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary, Kate, Katharine \+ Bessie arr. at Ch., Sayre's.

Very hot \+ baking dry \- much southerly wind \- sky somewhat overcast \- about 5 heavy clouds came up from S.W. \- two\[?\] storms all about us but none came very near \- Another day given pretty closely to writing \- Academy matters \- to N.A.'s who need to be converted \- a dreary work as it is repetition after repetition \- Annie \+ the boys went to Hart Lake to Episcopal Pic nic. at 12.30. I had my dinner alone. They did not return until 8 P. m \- when they came on the same train with Mary, Kate \+ the girls, all apparently in good condition \- Ever, pretty high wind, so that we sat in doors
