## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 5, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

sent letter to W. Geo. Read. naming $675000 as a price for the N.a. —

In addition to he smoke, clouds overcast the sky most of the day — an enervating South wind blew — Hart, his team, + his man continued work on Croquet ground, most of the time harrowing up stone + hauling them down to Chapel Hall — I superintending much of the time — I wrote letter to W. Geo. R. Read naming $675000 as the price for the N.a. of D. + mailed in at 6 o'clk. The first step is taken — At 3 o'clk. went to Fair Ground with Annie, Miss Frueauff + Ralph + Louise, to see the drill + sham battle — I saw a man, Hardy of Lenox, blown away from the mouth of a cannon by a premature discharge. The cannon plew through the trees, fortunately doing no damage, but the man was horribly burned about the face, upper part of his body + arms — I saw him as he struggled up on his knees, all blackened + his shirt on fire — it was a terrible sight — The extent of his injuries are not known to-night — Dr. Gardner thinks it very probable that he will lose his eyes — one of his hands is lacerated, but not seriously —
Evnt. with. news paper until 9 — then for an hour in big chair on piazza before going to bed. Heavy rain from 10.30 until after 11 — another shower later.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 6, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, damp + foggy m'f. — some steaming weather, as sunshine broke thro' the clouds before noon — pleasant p.m. — Hart, his team + his man came on in the m'f + worked all day upon the Croquet ground grading. I drove 15 or 20 stakes at regular intervals over the ground + with spirit-level established absolute levels — from these I arranged a very gentle grade sufficient to run water off the surface, + then had the top-earth that had been scraped off after the first ploughing, hauled back again. I fell to + worked myself for some time. Spent some time in the evn'f, looking over our European photo".
