## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 4, 1894

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day. At 10 o'clk. Silleck \+ [illegible] Russell came to the house with a team \+ took my big easel, mirror, a lot of oil \+ Water color pictures \+ other traps \+ carried them to my studio, getting all things into place at 11 15. Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — E.M. Ward came in \+ urged me to send a couple of Water Colors to a small club reception up town — I agreed to \+ for the rest of the p.m. worked on "Campanile Lugano" \+ "Old Tower, Rav[ello]" making some changes that I thought an improvement. At 5 went to Lord \+ Taylor's \+ ordered my window shade for studio put up to-morrow — Home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Svet'f. Louise came for Annie \+ they went to Choral Club Rehearsal. Katie \+ I had a quiet even'g at home.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 5, 1894

**Weather:** Beautiful  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day. To studio at 10 — wrote a letter to B.H. Northrup, an answer, concerning Village Imp'mt in Montrose. Then prepared to make a start on some Water-Color work. Man came from Lord \+ Taylor's \+ put up dark shade at window. Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — worked as long as I could see on a view on the river Brie in the little village of Crécy, getting it ready for color. Then to H[illegible] \+ there a walk to 50th S. and home. dinner at 6.30 — Svet'f. Eliz'th. Sally \+ Gogo Cook \+ Sally Akres came in to see Katy for a few minutes — at 9 I went out for a walk \+ wandered to [illegible]'s gallery in 5th Ave. near 34th S. where there is a miscellaneous coll'n of engravings to be sold next week. Chatted with O. — \+ took a hasty look around — then home.
