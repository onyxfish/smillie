## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 18, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Unpleasant day - snow flurries, but hardly enough to wet the sidewalks. Took R- to school - then butcher & grocer then home & commenced to fix my table to get to work upon the Morgan head. I made a blind & sorted my proofs etc, etc & at 12.15 went for[th], lunch at 1 - cleaned wax from the Morgan plate and spent some time studying over it & the proofs - At 5 I went to D.B. Butler's with glass - & from there to N.A. & D. - Home soon after 6 - dinner at 6.30. Rec'd from R.C. Penfield the Phila. publisher, a copy of H.H. Russell Wrays: "Review of Etching in the U.S." & to compare and thro' it - a small & not particularly luminous book - then for some time I helped Annie getting out cards for her "Tea" (and Eleg't Howe's) Friday, Jany. 26th.

Recommenced work on Morgan head, 2 h. Did not touch it in 1893.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 19, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, beautiful day - Took R- to school - etc - then got to work upon the Morgan portrait, reducing color on top of head & adding it in lower part of face. Soon after 11.30 went to barber's & had my hair cut. Then for R- lunch at 1 - again worked on Morgan portrait - about 5 went to N.A. - had a talk with P.W. Wood - Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g Annie read to me the paper she has just written to read at the next meeting of the Wed. P.M. club - on the uses of waste - considering what chemistry has accomplished with- in the past few years in making it both possible & profitable to recover many desirable products from what, before, valueless waste - The products of coal tar & petroleum were considered at most length - an admirable paper. Wrote a note to Leila Martin.
