## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 20, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful, bright, nice day — at 11 I went to grocers — then to Columbia College to see Prof. VanAmringe. I left a note for him — then home som after 12 — lunch at 1 — After that I had a call from Saml. Coleman — Mary Pet? to Po'k. by 3 o'clk train then I went to Mr. Danl. Huntington \+ had a half hour chat with him try ing to induce him to accept a place on the Na. com. on 8 its — then home Sue Cook, Katchien \+ Eleanor arr late in the afternoon — Robt. Cook came to dinner — Eve'g a number of the young cousins from 37\. S. came in for a while — I wrote a note to Delacour.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 21, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another bright \+ pleasant day. I had a very uncomfortable night — woke in the m't with some fever \+ much pain in my head — Of course, I could not get up — A dose of Phenacetine seemed to reduce the fever \+ allay the pain but I passed a wretched day in bed. At night Annie had her party of young people \+ the house was full — Bessie came down from Po'k. I heard the piano \+ felt the dance vibration thro' the house. Near 11. Jim and Ralph came, undressed, \+ crawled into bed alongside of me \+ at once were asleep — After 12. Annie came up, they were transferred to their proper beds \+ we got started on our night's rest.
