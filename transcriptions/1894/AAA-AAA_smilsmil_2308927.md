## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 28, 1894

Pleasant day — partially overcast late. blfs — after reading newspapers, spent a little time shovelling snow at the back of the house — at 1 o'clk I went out for a walk — to Central Park \+ back at 2 — dinner — after that completed my letter to Jno. Durand — \+ feeling sleepy, about sundown I took a nap — tea at 6.30 — Evef. Charl came in \+ Annie read to us her W.C.A. paper, "The uses of Waste" — when she had got about half through it, Geo. \+ Nellis came in \+ did not leave until 10.30 — then A. resumed and finished her reading — she has extended \+ improved the paper since she read it to me — It was nearly midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 29, 1894

worked on Morganhead 5 h.

The nastiest sort of a nasty day — rain, sleet, snow \+ slush — I took R. to school — then to my work on Morgan plate until 12.20 when I went for R. — Heavy rain, freezing as it fell — wind blowing great guns — I carried R. all the way home on my back. lunch — Afternoon, worked as long as I could see on Morgan — going over lower part of face with dry-point adding the color tone that I found in the painting on comparing my proof with it — then, feeling out of sorts, I lay down \+ had a short nap — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef — storm still very severe — Annie \+ the Adamss girls went to see, \+ hear, Coquelin \+ Mlle Hading — I went to Council Meeting \+ gave a first airing to my idea of making a True Academy of helld(?) — not merely an exhibition machine, with some schools incidentally attached — Sell the present bldg. as soon a tofs price can be got — buy 6 lots — longest measurement to the north \+ put up a cheap, but good \+ artistic building as nearly adapted for schools — Russet brick \+ terra-cotta tile proof — 3 stories for school purposes — the galleries on the 4th story to be reached by elevators — I wonder of the lots on the S.W. cor. of 24th St — ave. could be got — I do not think now that a better location could be found — walked home at 10.30.
