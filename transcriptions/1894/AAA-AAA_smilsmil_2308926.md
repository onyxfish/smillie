## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annie & Eliz'. Home gave their Afternoon Tea.

Bright cold m'f. overcast about noon with a snowstorm commencing about 3 p. m. & continuing thro' the night. I got away after b'kfs'. as soon as possible & went to H. & K.'s & spent the rest of the m'f. Taking 9 p'f'. from my celluloid dry-point, "Gateway at Roquebrune" — then I filled the plate & wiped it as for a proof — and in this condition I shall exhibit it with a proof — D'Goster came in with as mall dry-point portrait of himself by himself. He had it proved & then we rode up town together — Lunch at 1 — great confusion in getting ready for the functions of the afternoon — Everything had to be cleared out of the bedroom on parlor floor — The callers began to arrive promptly at 4 — Eliz. H. came with her two nieces — Howe & field — Annie had her three H. & M. Adams & H. Throop — Mary (Ed.) Cook & Emily C. — came early & officiated — the house was made to look very pretty & friends crowded it full — Had the weather been fine, it would have been an intolerable jam. I puttered over my work until 5 — then I went into the parlor — About 6.30, 14 of us sat down to the table & had a solid meal of Hot Bouillon — Cold Turkey & Ice cream & coffee & cakes — all seemed to have a very good time — The bedstead was set up again in the back room & H. & M. filled it after 10 p. m. — And the ball was over.

Katie Adams came to us from Brooklyn in the m'f.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 27, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow during the early m'f. about the middle of the day it cleared sunny & warm & the 3 or 4 in. of snow became slush — at night it froze. I got my Hieroglyphic plate (Gateway at Roquebrune) & the proof all cleaned in their mounts — Took them to Butler's — put them in their frames & then took them to the U.A. Spent some time there with [illegible] looking over the Etching ex'm— after attending to other matters it was lunch time when I got home. After that I got my table ready to begin work again upon the Morgan head. Then I went with Annie to Ortgies' Gallery to see the call'm of 144 paintings — 12 artists, 12 each; Ge°. being one of the 12 — we were not favorably impressed — I am afraid that Ge°. is not holding his own ag't. bad mannerisms. There we went up to Avery's gallery — had a last look at 7. H. Smith's Venetian views — I went home at 5 & had a nap before dinner — Every — very quiet. I wrote a letter to Jno. Durand.
