## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 4, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day of varying moods, mild, sometimes sunny, sometimes shady. I managed, altho' under considerable discomfort, to work pretty steadily thro' the day. Got the box of photos, \+ some other things packed — finished up studio matters then transferred my work to the house, packing the large case with rugs, etc — \+ then my trunk. Mr. \+ Mrs. Christmace called to bid us good bye — then Mr. \+ Mrs. Daul Sayre came — Tea at 6.30 \+ while, after tea, Annie was reading to Ralph, I had the refreshment of a little nap.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 5, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

All hands left for New-York.

A nasty, cold, wet day — wakened before 6 — dark \+ dismal — wind howling \+ pouring rain — made up our minds that we would scurry \+ if possible get off by the 7.50 train — \+ we did it, but we left things topsy turvy, trusting to Semele to straighten out things. There was no Drawing Room Car on the train and much of the time it was uncomfortably cold \+ draughty. Edgar Turrell tried to keep me warm by talking "Practical Politics" to me from a Tammany point of view, much to Annie's disgust — We got to Hoboken at 3 — on time, \+ to 110, before 4, where Kate Leonard was waiting for us, having made the house as comfortable as possible. We had a frugal meal in the laundry at 6.30 — At 7.30 I went to Council Meeting at the NA. There was a quorum in spite of the storm — I got away at 9.30 — on getting home found Charl there \+ we chatted until about 10.30 then to bed very tired.
