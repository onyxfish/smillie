## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 25, 1894

At Walters \- 135 E. 37th S\-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 7 \- off at 8 \- Men walked out with N\- going up to Manhattan Sq. (77th S.) \+ looking at a possible site for N.A. Men I left N. and went to Walter's, leaving my bag there \- Then to N.A. \+ from there to Mr. Lee's office, getting there about 11, 30 \- we went over contract of sale together \+ were satisfied \- At 1 o'clk Mr. VanPeet came in, Representing Mr. Ge: R. Read \- He had his copy of contract, bearing seal of Met. Life Ins. Co. but as he had to go to 23d S\- Mr. Lee \+ I went to the "Down Town Club" \+ had lunch \- After that I went to N.A. to see if I could get the seal of the N.A. to affix it to the contract. Waited there until 4 but Farrington did not come in \+ Fell to Lee's office having gained nothing \- It was near 5 when I left \[illegible\] \+ went up town \- went to the house \- 110 E. 38 \- \+ found that a small flower pot had got into the opening to the "leader" or water pipe on extension Roof \+ had so dammed up the water that for all summer it has been running down thro' the walls, floors \+ ceilings of the house making a horrible mess \+ destruction \- The dining Room ceiling is a complete wreck \- would stood over the whole carpet \- It would be impossible in the space here at at command \- It quite demoralised me \- I went to Walters \+ had time to get a little rest before dinner at 6.30 \- Rob: Cook came from Cambridge \- dined \+ stayed over night \- Got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Breezy \+ cool \- wind from the east \- overcast during part of the day \- Went, after off., to Hankinson's and arranged with him to send to the house at 3 to take up the wet carpets \- Got N.A. at 9:45 \+ met Nicole who attached the Corporate seal to the Contract of Sale. Then I took it to Lee's office \- Sent for Van P\- exchanged copies of contract \+ got from him a check for $35000 \- Then we bid Mr. Lee goodbye \- our work with him being fin\- ished \+ went to Mr. Ge: R. Read's office where I was introduced to him \+ had a short conversation on the subject of Sites for the New Academy \- I went out, Prowlike \+ saw, in a store in Nassau St. on foot: a Kinetoscope giving a Representation of a Prize fight \- a wonderful continuance \- Then back to Mr. Read's office \+ Mr. VanPeet \+ I went to the N.A. \+ there I gave him a check for $6100 \- Com. of 1% on sale \- Then, with \+ \- I went to 2d Nat. Bk \+ deposited the $35000 check \- Then I walked up to Century \- met Nicole there \+ had lunch \- It was 3 o'clk when I got to our house \- Clark's man was sitting on the front steps \+ Hankinson's men, with horse \+ wagon were waiting \- In a few minutes H.'s men had the Spare Room \+ dining Room carpets up \+ C.'s man turned on Water \+ Gas \- I then kindled a big fire in the furnace \- Before 6 I went to Walters \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Rob: Cook there \- About 8.30 I went to Century \- wrote a note to Com\- on admissions in behalf of Ripley Hitchcock \- on my way back to Walters at 10 o'clk \- I met Chas. Collins in the street \- \+ we had a walk together \-
