## Left Page

### Tues. May 8, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very uncomfortably warm day. After my usual visit to butcher + grocer I went directly to 5th Ave + 15th S. + saw Mr. Harry Jones + came to him my little paper. He expressed himself at once as very much pleased + asked me to promise him other contributions. Then to Brook's Bros. - to the Bank - to the N.A. + then to McRae - Real Estate dealer, Fifth Ave. + 44th S. Spent some time looking over possible sites for N.A. - Then home to lunch at 1. Then took F. to school. Annie went to Yonkers to lunch with Mrs. Waller + Katie went to [illegible] to stay overnight. At 4 o'clk. I went to O'Brien's - Printer, to see about Treas. Report. Then to N.A. - Council just finishing the judging of awards to pupils. At N.A. until 6 - then geo. went home to dinner with me, leaving soon after. I wrote in my report as Treas. until 10.30 - then to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 9, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

N.A.D. Annual Meeting + Dinner

Delightful day - I went to butcher's + grocer's after bkfs. + then waited until 10 for proof of the Accountant's Report from printer. Then I went to the printer 4th Ave. + 28th S. + he promised to send proof to N.A. I went there + got a quiet + comfortable place in Council Room + sat there + worked upon my report until after 1 o'clk. Then went into large class room where lunch was being served. I could find nothing to eat that suited me so I went home, got lunch + then back to N.A. before the business meeting began. About 45 N.A.s present. My report was very well rec'd. It was brief - no attempt made to read all of the small confusing items. A close + warm contest for office of Pres. - bet. Wood + Dielman. After 3 ballots Wood went in by one vote - 24 to 23 - more trouble over Vice Pres. - Maynard elected - would not serve - then Dielman - he would not serve - 3rd ballot - no one elected - Robbins gaming - 4th D. was elected. At 5.30 I went to [illegible] for half an hour + walked in mad. Sq. At the bal. for Rec. Secy Geo. got every vote but his own. Ballot for Treas. - I also got every vote but my own - every [illegible] out surprise - much excitement - our ballots for Ass. received [illegible] decorations. ringing in [illegible]. N.O. Walker + Cecilia Beaux. It was near 7 when we went in to dinner - subscription $5 each - about 30 at the tables. The dinner was fairly good - at the close I was ineffectually called upon to air my scheme of a new site, new building + large school accommodations. I managed to say something. Perry was amusing in calling out Wh. Beard to read a poem that he had been for some 4 yrs preparing. Beard was more than funny - much of it was witty + some of it was [illegible] serious - while some of it in a drawling, aggravating way, made as [illegible] that would have sunk well in print. Dielman did well - + so did Vinton. Chas. H. Miller as usual made an ass of himself - was immeasurably [illegible], but did not find that Rob. Blum the land of Japanese Art + set about to the company woke up, after what seemed to me to have been a pleasant eve. I much regret that Dielman was not elected P. with Maynard V.P.
