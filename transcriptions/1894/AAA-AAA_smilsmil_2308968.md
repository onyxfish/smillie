## Left Page

### Fri. April 20, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, raw day, with several sickly attempts at sunshine — a little Rain — a nasty, wet night. Took R.; etc, etc here to work on Reed portrait until lunch. Annie lunched at Mr. Thompson's in Mad. Ave. Katie took her lunch to school with her \+ Jim went with Rob. Putnam to stay until to-morrow night with him at his home at Rye. So R. \+ I lunched alone — afternoon I continued my work on Reed plate Dinner at 6.45 — wrote a short letter to Jno. Durand — Ev'f Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 21, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull \+ threatening m'f., but sunshine struggled through before noon \+ the afternoon was a charming specimen of an April day. After bkfp. I went to K. \+ V.'s \+ had p'f? taken from my Reed portrait etching \+ found it in a satisfactory state — also p'p. from plates by Pulsels — Misses Hunt \+ Heinck \+ Mr. Young. Home before 1 — lunch. At 2.30 Annie \+ I went to Art Students League — 57° 5´— Auction sale of pictures \+ muls[?] of Socy of Am. Taken. broad burlesque \+ farce thoroughly enjoyed by the youngsters. It was very crowded there \+ Annie soon left — I followed shortly after. went in to see the designs for decoration of municipal Court Room in the Vanderbilt gallery in 58° 5´— met a number of people there whom I knew — walked home getting in at 5 o'clk. Dinner — Ev'f. went to Century \+ saw an ¾ of a large number of lantern slides, presided over by Chamberney. After that spent an hour or more chatting with various ones. Home before 12.
