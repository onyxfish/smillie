## Left Page

### Fri. July 27, 1894

*Louise \+ Walter ret\. to N.Y.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

anexceedingly hot day \- some hopes for thunder showers in afternoon \& evening but none passed over us \- I got up at 6.30 \- went down town, got W's baggage over to station \& checked for N.Y. Then back to blep \- At about 9.45 Louise \+ Walter started for New\-Milford to take the 11.35 train to N.Y. I got to my work on the N.a.f.s business \+ got off, by P.M. mail, bet. 30 \& 40 of the circulars to N.a.,s Then I discovered that no time has been named as a limit for the return of the assents \-
Every, after watering many plants Annie \+ I loafed in the mild breezes on piazza until after 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 28, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Again, anexceedingly hot day. \+ again disappointed hopes for thunder showers but they passed all about us \- There was a little gentle rain during the night \- I rec\. from Farrington a complete set of addressed envelopes for N.a. At the printing office I had a little note, stating that assents to sale of N.a. should be signed \+ mailed so as to reach me not later than Aug 10\. \-, printed in red at the top of the form of assent \- Then I got out by P.M. mail all circular except a half dozen or so, with which I intend to send a few written words \- The plumbers came, took down tank \+ reset closet getting things to work satisfactorily at about 5 P.M. Ralph inaugurated the W.C. on measurement I find that we have now about 2250 gals. of water in the barn tanks \-
Another evelf., until after 10, on the piazza.
Rec\. a long letter from Dr. Carole Dunham on the subject of Sewerage. Evef. A. \+ I put down, in salt, in a keg, a lot of very fine, yellow wax beans.
