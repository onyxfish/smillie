## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 1, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little, light rain last night. Charming day \- very luminous \- much of my time was spent out of doors, superintending Stone who was at work upon new strawberry bed near fair ground gate, and Henick who got started upon putting in a new sill under a portion of the house he occupies \- For a little while I was with the Italian, Geo. Walker, who is digging drain ditches in the swamp \- I wrote some letters \- to Nicoll \- to Wys, Lee \- Chas. MacRae & Wm. Bemm Annie took Fanny out for a ride in the Mf \+ again in the afternoon Evef. I was very sleepy, but Annie \+ Fanny spent some time over our European photos.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** N.Y.

Annie, Fanny K \-, Jim, Edna \+ Louise go to

Still very beautiful weather. Grand sun-set. Up at 6 o'clk \- \+ all went to R.R. St. Train away on time \+ Ralph \+ I left alone. Poor little fellow he clung very closely to me all day. In the afternoon he said to me, "Papa, it seems to me as though I were not alive \- as tho' I lived in no-man's land \- \+ as though pigs \+ cats \+ persons were not real" \- and so he went on for some time amplifying the idea. He was evidently very lonesome \+ the loss of his companions affected him as he described as I could understand. \- I was about all them\+ until after 11 \- Stone transplanting the gaudy bed of strawberries \+ Henick at work upon the sill of his house. At last I settled in studio \+ wrote a letter to the Republican upon the subject of sewers. Dinner at 1 \- Most of the P.M. I worked with a spade in transferring a number of lettuce plants to a cold frame to protect them from the coming frosts \- After 4 Ralph \+ I went down to the swamp to see how the ditching had progressed \- then to P.O. etc \+ home soon after 5 \- After tea I read to Ralph until I sent him to bed at 8 \- Then I did a little reading and writing \-
