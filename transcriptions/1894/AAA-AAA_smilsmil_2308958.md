## Left Page

### Sat. March 31, 1894

*[header note: First Etching session Art Students League]*

**Weather:** Brilliant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant mf. growing hazy + overcast later in the day. - mf. went down town + had to do considerable shopping before I could find hand vises, emery paper, etc, such as I wanted for my classes. - Then to K. & V. in Canal St. + got the three Jap. pf. Takee Wl. mf. then home in time for lunch. - Many Kate + Bessie sat. to o'k. at 3.30 P. M. On looking at my three Morgan pf. I find them too heavy in tone, so that I shall have to have others taken - of course I am disgusted. - at 3.30 I went to the League, 215 W. 57th S. - met my class or a portion of it, for the first time. - There were but two young men + four or five young women. - They were already at work upon pen sketches from a model. He was dismissed at 5 + from that time until 6, I "discoursed" + gave examples of groundlaying. - I was followed with closest attention. - Then walked down home, washed my hands + went to J.d.w. Cook's - Annie already there - Fred. + Alice J. came soon after + at 7 we had dinner - home soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 1, 1894

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - The usual late bfst. - [illegible] for sometime with newspapers. About 11.30 R. + I went down to Station T. (Sub. Post) + got some information for Annie. - Then to G.C. Central Station + got correct time. - Then to Dr. Yale's + asked him to come to see Jim who seems to need treatment. - Then home to dinner at 2. - After dinner I wrote a note or two and in the evefg I wrote to Herrick. - we learned from Louise Cook that Mr. Kent, of Buffalo, died this mf. about 8 o'clk. - Cancer of the stomach. Evefg. Charl came in + stayed until near 11 o'clk.
