## Left Page

### Sat. March 17, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. Got out the Morgan plate for a last few touches (preparatory to some final pt. on Monday in green, etc, etc, to please M. M. I also spent some time, reworking my batch of etching ground — Annie lunched with Mrs. Geo. Haven Putnam \- R. lunched with Louise. It was 4.30 when I stopped work \+ cleaned up \+ went to Knoedler's \+ some other places, shopping. Dinner at 6.30 Evef. I went to Century Club \+ heard an informal discourse by T. Munson Coan on Sandwich Islands \+ the Islanders, with some reference to underlying causes of the present imbroglio \- some amusing stories but not much light. Met Geo. \+ Charl there. I got home at about 11.

Morganhead, 2 h.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 18, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild, bright day. Bkf: at 9. It was nearly when with the two boys, Jim \+ Ralph I went up to Central Park \+ loitered about there until 1.30 — then home. Dinner at 2 — Apo. Sheldon \+ Gordon dined with us — Val. \+ Nellie being still rigidly quarantined. It was nearly 6 when I went out for a walk — went down to Madison Sq. \+ 5th Ave. \+ around up Mad. Ave. Tea at 6.30 — Evef. \+ Charl came in \+ had early.
