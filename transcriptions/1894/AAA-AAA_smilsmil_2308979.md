## Left Page

### Sat. May 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely day, most of it spent out of doors, the m'f with Apollos Stone - the afternoon with Herrick. I went down town in the elf. Dinner after 1 - I saw Macaulay \+ came near making a bargain with him to grade a portion of the Herrick lot, but H. would not accept the responsibility of bassing the job - Tea at 6.30 -
Went down town for mail at 8 - \+ then to a scout meeting of Village Imp'. Soc'y. concerning purchase of Universalist Ch. Bldg. out before 9 o'clk. I sent to Farrington a large bundle of checks this m'f that he had sent up to me for my signature.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 13, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day, growing much cooler towards ev'g with N.W. wind - much of the m'f was spent wandering over the place with A. \+ children, especially over the new ground, The Tyler lot, speculating on the difficulties to be met in draining the swamps \+ in quarrying the stones from the other portions. It was near 2 when we had dinner - I worked upon a cherry tree cutting out a lot of big worms \+ decayed wood \+ bark \+ then covered the wound with hot tar - Then Gill came \+ I talked over the farm matters with him giving directions, \+ then with Herrick, showing him where I wanted ploughing done in the Tyler lot. W. \+ Mrs. Daw'. Sayer called. Tea at 7 - wrote a short note to Kate and took it to P.O. On return looked over galley ref. of "Etches \+ Painter-Etchers" for Mrs. Harry Jones, condensing it. Then took it to P.O. \+ on returning, went to bed.
