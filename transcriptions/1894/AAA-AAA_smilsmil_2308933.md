## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 9, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day of wind & rain — very dark much of the time. Went with R. to school — A. brought him home at noon. I spent the day writing notes & letters, most of the time being given to a long letter to Herrick with many instructions concerning his work. I had a little rest before dinner. Evef., a little reading & to bed about 10. [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 10, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very disagreeable in the m'f — dark, damp \+ raw. Sunshine came about 2 o'clk. the rest of the day, very pleasant. I spent the m'f. getting some plans ready for Herrick \+ went out for a short walk a little before 1 o'clk. Soon after 1, lunch — two friends of Katie T — with us — Miss Buck of B'k. \+ Misses Wickham \+ Sally Akers. They seemed to have a good time — after lunch Sally Akers sang very pleasantly for us — all cleared out at about 3 — I, feeling unequal to anything, went down to h.a. \+ spent some time looking at Water Colors. Two fumine have been sold \+ there are encour[illegible] inquiries for my pen sketches. Then I went to the library \+ Council Rooms where the Students of the schools were having a very pretty Reception. Home about 6 — Edna \+ Louisa Coats are staying with us — Jack having developed the measles. Dinner at 6.30. Katie T. dined at Mame Swift's. Evef. reading. [illegible — ~3 words]
