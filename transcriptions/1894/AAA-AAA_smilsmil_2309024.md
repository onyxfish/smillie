## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 10, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day followed by a very cool, cloudless moonlight night. My work was of the usual kind, after the m'f duties I got to my writing \+ at noon went to the S.O. I got a long letter from Mr. Wm. Lee containing some information \+ advice concerning N.E. sale \- I made an arrangement with \- Delong \+ he came with his greys at 3 o'clk \+ hitched on to our open wagon \- I had the Benton horse hitched up \+ then the entire party, 13 in all, including Delong, went off for a ride \- going beyond So. Montrose, around by Butterfield's, out on Wyalusing, by school house \+ over Cobb's hill, entering town by way of Darkie's Valley \- getting in at 6, after a very pleasant drive \- Evening the young folk (Kate, Mary \+ her two girls were here) had blankets, rugs \+ pillows on the open piazza, \+ spent an hour or more upon their backs watching the shooting stars \- they saw a few.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 11, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another pleasant day, showing threats of change at night \- I spent my day as usual \- part of it about the place \+ considerable time given to writing N.A. letters. In the afternoon I got out my old set of quoits and hubs \+ set them under the beeches north of the house. 3 o feet bet. hubs. Roger \+ I had four games, the first I have played in many years \- I was fortunate enough to beat him three games, in one of which he did not count. Well, after tea, I went out to the pastures \+ from a lot of brush \+ dead wood there I built up stuff for a huge bonfire. Then all came out to see it, fire was set \+ nearly an hour was spent in watching it \+ the weird effects about. The youngsters were greatly delighted. Before I went to Azur Dabrook's to hurry up the butter supply.
