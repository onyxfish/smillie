## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 30, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thicker \+ smokier than ever \- a chilly wind blew up in the afternoon, but quieter \+ milder in the evef. Afternf duties at about 11 o'clk. I went down town, stopping at Sayres to give a couple of heads of choice lettuce to Mr. \+ Mrs. Fagnani, with whom I chatted for half an hour or more. When I ret? home I spent some time computing relative values of real estate in vicinity of N.a. \+ S., based upon a Table of recent transfers sent to me by Mr. Farrington. After dinner I wrote a letter to [illegible], \+ then, feeling out of sorts, I lay down for an hour. After \+ I again went down-town \+ again stopped at the Sayres \+ chatted awhile with the F's. Evef. with newspapers \+ magazines. [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 31, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

much clearer, altho' still very smoky, and somewhat cooler. I had considerable work outside that I attended to and then wrote a letter to John Sartain. After dinner I wrote to Signor Vozzi, a letter that she. have been written months ago. When at Amalfi. in May '93. Vozzi took a great fancy for my Fountain Pen and I promised to send him one when I ret? to N.f. I sent him the pen last May \+ now have sent the letter that should have accompanied the pen. Then down town \+ arranged to have dirt drawn to our lawn from under the Universalist Ch. Evef Katey Throop came up alone. For a time the letter game was played. At 9 I took her back to Chas. Sayres.
