## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 7, 1894

Katie, Mollie, Roger, Carleton, Edna & Louise arr.

Very pleasant day — feeling very seedy after yesterdays experience — was able to do but little in the m'f but after dinner I slapped some washes of opaque White & Charcoal grey over the pen sketch, view from Studio bay window, for Harry Jones's Quarterly Illustrator — this sent to him with a sketch of the house. Eve'g, I went to station on arrival of 7.45 train expecting to meet half a dozen of the Adams & Cook young people & was much surprised to find none of them there — Then home, Mary, Kate & Katharine there awaiting the arrival of a party — much conjecture — no conclusion. Shut up the house & were fore-paring for bed when at 10 o'clk. the party drove up, having been left at Alford by the 4. & M. Train — of course, they all thought it a very good lark — altho' most of their luggage was missing. no time was lost in getting them stowed away, altho' it was difficult to supply the demand for night shirts.
