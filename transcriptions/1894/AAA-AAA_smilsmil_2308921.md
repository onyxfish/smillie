## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 16, 1894

*My Birth day — 61 years old — Phew!*

**Weather:** Dull, damp & raw — clearing & colder at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Took R. to school — Then grocer & butcher — then home & spent [illegible] clearing up my corner in the nursery and getting many little matters in order — at 12.30 went for R — lunch at 1 — at 2 went to 2° Nat. Bnk. & arr? with Casw. Case for a loan for the N.A.f.D. of $5000 — also re-arranged some of the Academy papers — went to Kurtz's & arranged with him to send on Monday next to Mr. Fred. Sturges for portrait of [illegible] Reed to photograph — then to McCutcheon's — linen goods — & saw him in reference to Dessauer the Jew peddlar who sold Annie some table cloths & napkins some time ago — then I went to 5th Ave. & 15th St. to see Harry Jones about article on Etching — not in, but saw clerk & left message advising that the matter be postponed — then to Keppel's & saw the Pennell [illegible] of Etching & few studies — then walked home & had a rest before dinner at 6.30 — Evening Annie went with Elo?, Many, Eli? Home to meeting of the 19th century club — at 9 I went to Collyer's Church, where Colton delivered an illustrated lecture on the Chemistry of Electricity before the Sunday School [illegible] Soc'y — Jim & young Walter there — I brought Jim home.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 17, 1894

**Weather:** Brilliant, cloudless & inspiring day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Took R. to school & then went right down town — to Lee & Lee, 20 Nassau St. to get advice concerning the N.A. mortgage acp. Dore & you who is very slow in paying his interest — then to Charl's office & waited there until 11, when I had an appointment to meet W. Phillips at his office, 40 Wall — he holds the $60,000 mtg app. the N.A. — he agrees to increase it to $65,000 — I went with him to his lawyers & the new mtf was put on its way. Then to Kimmel & Voigt's — 2+2 Canal, & made an appointment for next Tues. to take pf. of the Morgan portrait — then to N.A. & signed a lot of checks — then home to lunch — At 3.30 R. & I went to Columbia College, Library Bldg, where we enjoyed a very interesting lecture with ingenious illustrations, on the cross fertilization of flowers — out at 4.30 — At 5 o'clk. I rested. Dinner at 6.30 — Every Annie went to Walters — little Mary is very sick. I went to meeting of the Water Color Soc'y at N.A.f.D. — & spent an hour or more listening to a very badly mixed controversy, led by Tom Moran & Albert Sterner, upon the subject of the Evans prize & suggested modifications — at last, upon the subject of prizes in general, Sterner advocating a medal to be awarded by the society — I left at 10 o'clk — home & to bed.
