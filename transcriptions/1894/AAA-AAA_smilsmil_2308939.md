## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 21, 1894

**Weather:** Pleasant & mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant & mild — Took R. to school etc — then spent some time studying over the Morganhead pfo. — then wrote to H\+S Prague and to Mielatz — went out \+ had a short walk before lunch — After that more writing — It was after 4 when I walked down to Kurtz's \+ made inquiries about asphalttum for varnish — then to Knordler's \+ bought charcoal paper for Katy — then to Chaumpney's — 5th Ave. \+ 15th S; \+ spent some time with him — Home at 6.30 — dinner Eve'g, some newspaper reading \+ then Annie read aloud to me a Harper Mag. story — \+ then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 22, 1894

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — R. did not go to school as it is holiday, so I worked uninterrupted on Morganplate until lunch and afterwards until about 4 o'clk. when I rode down to K. \+V's in Canal St — hoping to find someone there so that I could have paper set down \+ [illegible]. Taken to-morrow \[illegible\] — all locked up — then walked up Bdway \+ home, stopping at [illegible]. Fanny Kimball had [illegible] by invitation, dined \+ stayed all night with us — Eve'g Fanny, Annie, Katie T. \+ I went to Collyer's Church 34th S. \+ Park Ave. \+ heard Mrs. Kate Douglas Wiggin read "The Fore Room Rug" — a "Scene from Seveloke's English Experiences" \+ a "Scene from the Story of Patsy" — all of which we enjoyed very much! Miss Lilian Parglow played some pieces on the violin — After the reading a large portion of the audience went into the lecture room where cakes \+ drinkables were served \+ there was general chattering — It was after 11 before we left \+ midnight when we got to bed.

morgan head. 5 h.
