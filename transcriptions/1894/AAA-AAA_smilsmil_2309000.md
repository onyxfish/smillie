## Left Page

### Sat. June 23, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day \- spent the m'g with the workmen, most of the time in front of the house superintending the laying of the soil/edge in the ditch + then filling it in. This work was completed in the afternoon. After dinner I worked for a while on my dry-point Peonias. After tea I went downtown. Eve'g. It was so warm and quiet that Susie + I sat out on the piazza until after 9 o'clk. Then we lighted the lamps + spent some time reading \- among other things, I read aloud Reinhart's sketch in the July Harper's. I think it very poor in every way \- He has been betrayed into making a discreditable exhibition of literary weakness + 'tis a great pity. To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 24, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An extremely hot m'g. In the afternoon the wind got around to the east + blew quite freshly, while thunderstorms went galumping all about the horizon, some of them getting near enough to give us a wetting. B'kf't. bet. 8 + 9. After that I went to garden + gathered Asparagus + the first picking of peas. Dinner before 2. afternoon I was much of the time in studio much interested in some fine Peonia flowers. Eve'g. Thunder storms kept rumbling + grumbling about us but did not pass over us. We had, however, a very pleasant wetting. I wrote to Charl.
