## Left Page

### Thur. April 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day, but rather warm. Took R. to school, etc. — then I wrote a letter to Herrick giving directions concerning Gill's drawings for tank frame at east end of old barn. Before 12 went to N. A. to arrange for Reception of the Baldwin gift etc. Home at 1 — lunch — At 3 o'clk I went to the Baldwin studios \+ there saw Mr. \+ Mrs. Barbite. I spent some time getting printing press ready to move — then to Photo exhibit in Socy of Fine Arts Bldg — and at 5 I met some of my etching class in the league \+ for an hour laid grounds for them — It was late when I got home — dinner at 7 — at 8.30 Annie \+ I went to Murray Hill Hotel to see the Columns — out — then home — I wrote to Jno. Durand \+ to S. R. Koehler.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 27, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild, pleasant day — have fight all day with a headache. Took R. to school \+ then onto the Sherwood where, in the Baldwin Room I found Mrs. Barhyat \+ later, Mr. B—. I spent more than an hour in superintending the movement of press \+ etching materials to the truck \+ after 11 I went to N.A. of D. where I arranged with Davis for the Reception of the things — then went for R. \+ home — lunch — after that I had a call from W. Austin Brereton who came from the "Ill. American" to interview me — an illustration \+ portrait to appear in the number next week. Then young Johns \+ the league came for some plates upon which I had been laying grounds. Then I went to N.A. \+ there until 6.30 looking over \+ sorting the Baldwin things. Home to a late dinner — Evert Mr. \+ Mrs. James Gifford \+ Hudson called.
