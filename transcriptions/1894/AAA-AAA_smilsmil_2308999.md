## Left Page

### Thur. June 21, 1894

**Weather:** Cloudy m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'f. \+ quite cool, later in the day it cleared delightfully. Had Herrick run a level from bottom of barn tank to the house, Faking every precaution to have it correct, + was surprised to find the level in the house about 4' lower than preceding observations. This obliged me to revise my plan, abandoning all the work that was done yesterday. Passing the pipes up thro the floor of up-stairs bath room instead of thro' ceiling from attic. Went to Cooley with [illegible] from Smith + Anthony, J. L. Matt + a Phil'a firm, makers of "Nautilus" closet. Had a long interview with him without reaching any conclusion. Almost all of my time was taken with bossing the work of the different men in different parts of the house or at the barn. Even. I rec'd a letter from Mrs. Charlotte S. Withersbee informing me that her Aunt Mrs. S. H. W. owned the stable in 36th St. over which I hope to have a studio built \- Rather mixed, for it was to Mrs. S. H. W. that I wrote \- Went down town after tea \- lovely even'g \- after sitting for awhile on piazza went indoors + read newspaper.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 22, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudless hot day, Ther. getting above 80°. I got my oil paints out + started to make a color study of a bunch of Peonias but I soon got into a mess \- Then I started to make a dry-point upon a 7"×9" plate + I stuck to that all day as closely as I could, the plumbers, painters + carpenters calling me off every few minutes \- Late in the afternoon Rev. Mr. Hughes, the Baptist minister, his wife + two young women from Chicago, Misses McIlvane, came up the road + I invited them in + took them over the house. at 7.30 I went down town \- Met E. Goodwin (who bot. Sam't Selim) + had a talk with him about horses \- He has a team that he wishes to sell \- Even'g on piazza + reading newspaper \- a very quiet, warm night.
