## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 12, 1894

**Weather:** Very dark & foggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very dark & foggy with tremendous heavy showers during the day. Evg cleared suddenly — cloudless moonlight. I wrote on my Report until 11 when s. Practically completed it — Then I went down to N.A. & got 7 — to go to Walls: for me. Then to Studio for a minute — in to see McC[illegible] — then to Ortgies — to Delancy Graunis. [illegible] in W. 42 S. — to Riker & Son 661 5t Ave. " " & home to lunch soon after 1 o'clk. Lunch in the afternoon I had a good Rest with the result that I felt remarkably fresh in the eveg. Nicole came at 6.30 & dined with us — Geo. came in expecting to see Many, who did not arrive. Read Report to Nicole, my colleague on the com — & he approved. At 8 he & Ge. went to N.A. I went to Ortgies' & bid in a proof of Rolla's plate — for Benf. West after Lawrence — for $2.50 I already have two working jps — Then to N.A. full meeting — Read my Report — will Rac? — & discussed with much interest — No definite conclusion — none are expected — In the debate I took sides warmly agst. "bunking" up on what the millionaires may do for the N.A. insisting that sound business policy & self Respect urged us to cut our garment according to our cloth. Van Belt waited to see me after the meeting — but he gained nothing by it. about 11 o'clk. The galleries are lighted up & a number of the N.A.'s went up & had a private view — It was just 12 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 13, 1894

**Weather:** Cloudy, cold & raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy, cold & raw — Rheumatic headache so severe in the mg. that after going to grocery & market I ret. home & spent the rest of the mg. — or most of it, nursing my head with a hot water bag. At 1.30 A's lunch party came, Mr. & Miss Beeson, Miss Bolling, Mary Cook & Fanny K. & Ely Howe. I sat down to table with them but left at 2.15 & went to Ortgies' gallery where I sat thro' nearly 2 hours of print sale. Part of the Heuseker Coll. — I bought a fine pf. of Rarbour's portrait of himself, engd. by Am. Walker for $5. — & two or three other unimportant things — Then home — Dinner — at 8.30 I went to Century to meet the artist members to plan for 12th night celebration — 300 or more present; much hard pumping — little spontaneous suggestion of fun — atmosphere very close & before 10 I got out of it and away.
