## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 19, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain fell heavily & persistently all day. I went down town for my mail & found letters from Mr. Lee & from Nicoll. Mr. L. writes that he has engagements that will occupy him most of Friday & part of Sat. so I telegraphed to him that I would go to the city on Monday next & meet him at 4 o'clk. Returning to the house I wrote to both Lee & Nicoll — then I finished covering the Aqua-tint box. More writing in the afternoon & some clearing up in studio — went down town in a pour, at about 5. In the eve'g I interviewed Deaus & Ball about the Raising of the assessment for taxes on our property here. — Eve'g, newspaper, more clearing up in studio.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 20, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A showery day — some work in the shop finishing my aqua-tint boxes — some work also in the studio. I wrote to Charl. also to J. Q. A. Ward — & "Round Robin" birth-day letter was compounded by all hands, from Ralph to me, & sent to little Mary Cook (Walters) now at Buffalo. It was just about 6 when I rec'd a telegram from Mr. Lee, saying that after three days of persistent negotiations with Geo. R. Read, they had reached an agreement; R. for his clients, consenting to pay $610.000 for the N. A. of D. Mr. Lee says that for good reasons immediate action was necessary & he asks me to ratify his agreement. Eve'g I wrote notes to Nicoll & Robbins!

Geo. Walker worked part of the day in ditch in swamps.
