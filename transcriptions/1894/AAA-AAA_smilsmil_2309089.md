## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 18, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still fine weather, but colder. I get up feeblely. but with a sore throat \+ much general misery. I was able to do considerable work in dusting \+ cleaning up some portfolios full of pearly \+ late work of Father's — Ralph, Hinchelwood \+ myself. Lunch at 1. before which I went out \+ took a very short walk, but found the air much too crisp for me. I brought Ralph home from school. After lunch LeGrand Benedict, who has charge to N.A. Cat. advertising, came to get another extension of his note — I continued my work of the m.f. \+ got to a stopping place before 5 o'clk. Dinner at usual time. Evef. Charl came in for a few minutes — I read "The Dolly Dialogues" by Anthony Hope. — witty, heartless, \+ cynical —

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 19, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m'f., but clear afternoon \+ evef. got up feeling, it seemed to me, almost worse than ever, but as the day advanced improved somewhat. I did some work upon my N.A. Sittee material \+ wrote several letters in connection with that business — also interviewed LeGrand Benedict about the extension of his note to the N.A. (no I did not — that was yesterday, as I see that I have already recorded.) \+ The person interviewed in the m'f. was Mr. Thompson, of the W.S. P.M. Club — mine being out at the time. Evef. Charl came \+ took Mary \+ Katharine out to the Theatre — Geo. came in as they went out — he stayed until 9.30 —
