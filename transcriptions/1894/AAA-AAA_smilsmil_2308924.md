## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 22, 1894

**Weather:** Bright & mild — a spring day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kurtz's man came before 10 — we went to Mr. Sturges'. 36 Park Ave. & got the Durand portrait of Dunman Reed — the man took it to K's & I followed & gave all necessary instruction — there to Stearn's & bought some Swiss muslin remnants for painting. Then to N.A. & then home. Charl came in with Katherine Throofs — just down from Po'k. to begin her N.A. Antique School course. Annie has given her a room on the 4th floor. Lunch at 1 — During the afternoon I worked upon a slight sketch — dry-point on a celluloid plate "A gateway, Roquebrune". At five o'clk. I had a little nap — dinner at 6.30 & then to N.A. Council meeting. It was 11 o'clk. when I got home — walked up with George.

Saml. Colman came in about 4 o'clk. to say good-bye — He & his wife start for Mexico to-morrow.

Durand-Read head. 3 h.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 23, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

[illegible], but by noon the weather had cleared to cloudless & mild. After bkfst. went to Kimmel & Voigt's & spent the rest of the mft. there went after 1 — getting pfts. from the Morgan portrait place. I had expected to do some of the work myself but young Voigt took hold so intelligently that he succeeded in taking pfts. that I liked better than Goulding's, to my great happiness. Then I took a couple of [illegible] from a small celluloid plate — dry-point, "a gateway, Roquebrune". It was 2 o'clk when I got home — lunch — after a bit of rest I worked until near 5 o'clk getting the wet pfts. tacked down — Then walked away up 5th Ave. to 65th St. & back. Dinner — At 8. took Annie to Mr. Green's — to go to Choral Club Rehearsal. About 9 o'clk., after Katie had gone to bed, Charl came in — at 9.30 we walked up to Hardman Hall, Cor. 5th Ave. & 45th St. where the C. Club was rehearsing — got A. — & walked back

morgan head 5 h.
