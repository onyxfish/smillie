## Left Page

### Thur. May 10, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at the usual time \+ feeling in very good shape, the excitements of yesterday considered. About 10 I went out. Tele? to Herrick - then to Waldorf Hotel to enquire for Mr. Walter Wright - then to 2d Nat. Bk. \+ to Brooks Bros. Then to N.A. - there for some time signing checks etc - Home a few minutes before 12 - a few min after with A - Edna Cook \+ Rdelah we started for Montrose catching the 1o'clk. D.L.\+W. train \+ securing satisfactory Parlor Car seats. The journey was without incident of interest we got to Montrose in the early twilight 7.45 - Herrick at the station - W. Parker Norman P- also there - Bus to house at once. Mr. Otto, a nurse out of employ\[illegible\] had been eng? by Herrick to care for us. Having had our dinner at Scranton we wanted nothing - we sat for a while before a pleasant wood fire \+ about 10 o'clk. started for bed. Delayed for some time by the refusal of the trunk lock to accede to the demands of the key to another trunk - the key locked it but couldn't unlock it. With a monkey wrench \+ an old dinner-knife at last I got the trunk open \+ three we gathered ourselves into our bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 11, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a night of boughing wind, rain came in the n\+. bet. 9 \+ 10 o'clk. but it did not last long. The p.m. was pleasant followed by a cloudless, moonlight night. Parker Norman came up just as we had 6\+P. both on horseback - Norman on the little Pony mare that P- wishes us to buy. I went down town in the course of the n\+ \+ spent much time in fitting a key to trunk cock. A fairly good dinner at 1.30. I spent the afternoon in going about over the place until about 4. then feeling very sleepy I gave myself up to it \+ had a good sound nap. Then down town and back at 7 — Tea. Evely, a little writing \+ to bed soon after 10.
