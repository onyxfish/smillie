## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 13, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy Rain, commencing some time in the very early m't. \+ continuing until about noon \- Then the wind went around from East to west \+ late in the afternoon \+ ev't. there were heavy showers from that quarter. \- I got up with a swimming head \+ was so much discomforted by it that I could not work. I pottered about the studio doing a number of little jobs \+ at noon went to P.O. with letter to N.A. council concerning an application for a loan \- \+ to Bank. After dinner I again went down \+ spent most of the afternoon in going from place to place, upon one piece of business or another \- I went to Dr. Jardiner's \+ consulted him about my swimming head, the discomfort seeming to increase \- He thinks it a nerve disturbance \+ advised aromatic wine of ammonia. Ev't. \- went down town again, \+ arranged with Corwin to have a special meeting of town Council called for a week from next Monday ev'g. \- for me to urge upon them the necessity of securing the services of a sanitary engineer to lay out a plan of sewerage for the town \- Then home and read about frost trees until bed time.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 14, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Almost a continuous storm from m'g. until ev't. \- high wind from the west drifting clouds of fine snow that changed to wet as soon as it touched an object \- Ev't. after 8 o'clk \- it was dry, but there was a high, cold wind \+ heavy clouds. I got up with a severe headache \+ was entirely useless all day. I spent most of the time, rolled in blankets on big chair in the main Room \- Ev't. feeling better, I wrote a letter to Geo., also, an addendum to my Report to N.A. \+ D. council, giving a list of the N.A.'s who objected to sale \- etc \- \+ at about 10 o'clk. I went to P.O. with letters.
