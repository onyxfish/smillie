## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 8, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A sulky sort of day, overcast, hazy \+
very close \+ muggy, but no rain until
about 10 p.m. when a very little fell —

A busy day for me as I got several
letters on Academy business requiring
my immediate attention \+ after bkp-
found that a large filling had come out
of one of my teeth. I wrote until 11.30
then down town to P.O. \+ to Dr. Wm Smith's
the dentist \+ was fortunate in being able to
make an appointment with him for this
afternoon. After dinner Annie \+ all the fam.
at 4.30, went to Mulford's Pond catfishing
at 2.30 I went to Smith's \+ was in his
chair until 4 o'clk. having an unpleas-
ant time — then having my unfinished letter
with me, I went to Cooley's desk \+ wrote
until 5.20 upon letter to Mr. Wm. dee, the
Academy lawyer — about N.A. Sale —
wants me to go to N.Y. to talk the matter
over with him prior to his going off on
his vacation — I teleg. him that I would
go if actually necessary — The letter I wrote
to him contained my opening proposition
to go. Read — The Academy price is $7000.
I had my tea alone as the fishing party
did not return until 8.20, having one
small catfish \+ about 20 pairs of frogs' legs.
On the piazza until nearly 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 9, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very dark, muggy m'g., with great threats, thunder
\+ wind \+, at last, a very little rain. Wind arose
blowing freshly from N.W. growing cooler until
night when it was positively chilly. I got
to work at my Academy letters \+ wrote to
Farrington \+ Nicoll \+ took them to P.O. before
noon. Almost immediately after din-
ner Perego, livery Stable man with team
came \+ took the three Adams' \+ Carretons
to Silver lake to some entertainments
to come back some time to-night. At
3.15 Annie took horse \+ buggy \+ took Jim
\+ Elma out with her for a short ride \+ at
4 went to M. Railway Station \+ met Miss
Friekhoff who has come to stay for sometime
to look after Jim \+ Ralph in the matter of
studies — I wrote a long letter to La Farge — and
then I took a good walk \+ returned home
at about 6.30 — after tea again I went
down town — Evening, newspapers etc —
Annie \+ Miest. exchanging views upon edu-
cational matters.
