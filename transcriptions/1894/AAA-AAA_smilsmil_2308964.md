## Left Page

### Thur. April 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day. Rain + snow until the evef. After bkfs. I went to K. + V's + spent the mf. there. Mr. V. Taking pf. of the Morgan head for Mr. J. P. M. to take to England with him on the 18th. Under my direction he has succeeded in taking pf. to my entire satisfaction. I took with me 4 plates by N. A. and A. S. league pupils + conveniences being given to me I took 8 pf. on my way home I stopped at N. A. and arranged to meet my class there next monday p. m. Home at 1.30 — Annie at Lucy Morse's at lunch. Katie T. went up to Po'K. on the 3.30 p. m. train to be gone two or three days. I worked on Reed portrait until 4.30 — Then, feeling much under the weather I went to bed until dinner time. — Svef., felt so stupid about the head + uncomfortable generally that I did not go to Water Color show at the league. — Rec'd from J. R. Korbler a copy of Bosse, & "Maniere de graver a l'Eau forte", etc — Paris ed. 1758. Spent most of the evef over it trying to translate portions.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 13, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some sunshine in the mf., clouding later in the day — much warmer. Took R. to school + went for him at 12.30 spent the mf. over various small matters — among other things wrote to Mr. J. Pierport Morgan sending him my bill for etching. Lunch after. Annie lunched with Louise, after a call at the house from Mary Willard. After lunch I went to 2d Nat. Bk. + drew a little money. Met J. W. Tryon there + we had a talk about Academy matters. Then to K. + V's — Found young V. taking some excellent pf. of the N. plates — I backed down four to stretch — Then V. took two prints of the Reed portrait + I went home. Dinner before 7 — at 8.30 I went to Union League — Had just gone — Roland Knoedler passed me up to the galleries to see the Water Colors — Saw Mr. Chas. Whitehead there. Home at 9.30. Some reading + then to bed.
