## Left Page

### Wed. April 4, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, wet day — S with easterly wind. Took R- to school etc — then to R \+ V. \+ selected three Morgan [illegible] of cleaner wipes \+ tacked them down to stretch — Then to Brooks Bros — to 2°. nat. Bul. \+ to Nat. Academy where I arranged to meet my etching class to-morrow p.m. Home at 1 — Mrs. W. g. Parke \+ Bessie, her daughter, there to lunch — They left soon after 2. Annie went to the funeral of Miss Emma Clinch in the [illegible]. I worked on Rese portrait from 2.30 to 5.30 — biting [illegible] over back gr. and additional work on face — Then graver. Swef. Charl came in for awhile — I worked for some time getting material arranged for my talk on Etching to the Nat. Academy students next Sat. evef.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 5, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant mf. — cloudy p.m. At [illegible] received note from J.C. Nicoll stating that Farrington had sent his resignation as Sup. of N.A. \+ D — Rather startling — Took R- to school — then went to N.A. \+ D. met Nicoll there — then I went to Farrington's house — 52 W. 135th St — found him very much broken up — the result of over. work \+ worry — evidently in a morbid condition — Tears ran down his face as he talked to me — I was able to pacify him \+ presently he recovered his composure \+ assured me that he felt so much relieved by my call \+ my consideration that he was able to go to work again — from there I hurried back to 42d street getting to School for R- about 10 m. late — Annie went down to the Irelands to lunch — Mrs. Reynolds at work at the house — lunch at 1 — then to market \+ to my Etching class at the N.A. Spent the rest of the afternoon with them in making preparatory pen sketches, laying grounds, etc — etc — There until 6 o'clk then home — dinner at 6.30 — feeling tired in the evef — I went to bed early.
