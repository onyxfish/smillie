## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 18, 1894

Very warm. I spent the day rather lazily, doing nothing particular in the mrng but dodge about, looking after things. I tried to write some letters but was not in the humor. In the afternoon — before 5 o'clk. I got Ayer Lathrop's team of horses + took our platform wagon with three seats, all full, out — we drove until near 7 o'clk, having a very good time, but it did not make me feel very pleasant when, on starting, I learned that the team was the one that ran away with Bert Lathrop, killing him, some three years ago. Jim + Ralph were off with Apolios Stone fishing for Bullheads at Harpin Pond. They got home soon after 8, in great glee with a basket full of small Bullheads. I spent the rest of the ev'g trying to skin them. I managed to get the hides off from 13 + there I gave it up.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 19, 1894

A hazy mrng., close + hot. The afternoon given up to thunderstorms that circled all about us, coming from W. S. E. and N. At times the rain was very heavy + once there was a little hail.

I was in the garden gathering vegetables until near 12 o'clk. I did not do much of anything in the afternoon, altho' I tried to. The cisterns were reported very low — In the one at N. E. cor. of house I found only 9" — before 6 p.m. there was over 4 feet + the other cisterns were running over full.

Ev'g, we got out our European photographs + all hands spent the ev'g until after 10 o'clk. looking at them.
