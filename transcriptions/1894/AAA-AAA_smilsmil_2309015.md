## Left Page

### Mon. July 23, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another clouded day \- rain at night. I got up feeling very miserable after a bad night with headach. Ruan \+ his helper came in the m'f \+ commenced the work of setting bath-tub \+ W.C. in lower bath room \+ to them I gave considerable time \- During the day owing to my condition I accomplished but little. I went to P.O. before 12 \- \+ in the afternoon Annie, Walter \+ I went down town, walking as far as Dud. Lathrop's \- Every, W \- Read a paper prepared by Louise \+ him (jointly) last winter entitled; "Novels \+ Novel Readers" \- very clever \+ amusing \- Fine rain thro' a good part of the night.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 24, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy clouds \+ some fog in the early m'f. Gradual clearing until afternoon when, for a time the weather was charming, about 6 a heavy rainstorm for a short time. My. wrote a note to Mrs. Witherbee abt building studio over her stable in 86th S. \+ looked after Plumbers \+ Herricks. Went to P.O. before noon. Rec'd letters from Farrington \+ W.H.L. Lee \- The latter, while not positive in statement, gives me hope that the Academy business can be got over without calling a meeting in Aug. Dinner \- then wrote to Farrington \- then to livery stable \+ got team \- to house, got d. \+ W \+ A \- when I tried to turn by Jail Road to the west \- off horse balked \- but I coaxed her to go on \- we went south of town up along side \- past old school house \- over the hills \+ around by Sprout's quarry \- then rain came \+ in the hollow were caught in very heavy shower \- all of us more or less wet \- got home at about [illegible] \- horse balked four times \+ I had to get out \+ walk by his head to start him. Late tea \- Every, Newspaper \- Tired \- Louise \+ Walter played Halma \+ I went to bed early.
