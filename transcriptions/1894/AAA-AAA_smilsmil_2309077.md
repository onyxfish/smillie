## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 24, 1894

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- late where I got to Studio \- continued the work of cleaning out drawers & assorting things \- went to Safe deposit Co. \- before going home to lunch \- back to Studio at 2.30 \- Meta, paint Adaams came in soon after & much time was consumed in a sort of desultory and critical conversation concerning the portraits & the painters of them, in the exhibit. "Portraits of women" now shown at the N.A. of d. \- Then I worked on until near 6 \- Home to dinner \- Annie & Katharine went to a Reading by the Rev. E.Z. Hale at the Ch. of the Messiah \- I went to the Century where there was a Centennial Service celebrating the 100th Birthday of Wm Cullen Bryant \- Parke Godwin made the principal address, following an introduction by Huntington. Then R.H. Stoddard read a short paper. It was an interesting service, but not particularly brilliant or stimulating. I had an interesting talk with Mr. Huntington concerning the future of the Academy & the policy that should guide the selection of a site & the putting up of a building \- Home at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 25, 1894

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- late bkfst. of course \- it was 11.30 before I left the house \- went to Studio with a number of old catalogues from the house \- others, at once to N.A. galleries to have a quiet look at the "Portraits of Women" \- found the place in possession of pupils from the Student's league \- they were turned out soon after my arrival \- but Wm Chase continued "to hold the fort" with his class to which he lectured as they passed thro' the galleries. I stayed until nearly then home to dinner. Gene Atwater there \- Robt. Cook was expected but did not put in an appearance. Afternoon, we had calls from Geo. Smillie, Geo. Cook & wife & Chas Smillie \- Eve'g I did some writing & finished up the big Sunday paper.
