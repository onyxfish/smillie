## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 28, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Smoke thicker than ever — clouds probably added to the greyness of the mtf. but in the afternoon the sun appeared like a copper ball. I was not in a humor for work but in the afternoon I wrote a letter to Mr. Wm. Lee, from whom I rec. a letter in the mtf. on Nat. Academy business. This matter is dragging noo in a manner that worries me. The absence from Town of Mr. Lee, who is now off on his vacation, delays the whole business at a critical time. There was a "Granger Picnic" on the fair grd. in the afternoon \+ for a little while I went up there \+ heard Mrs. Thriving ramble on in a wide-angle, reformatory address that was very well suited to her heaven — could not, I think, possibly do them any harm.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 29, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lunched at Mrs. Elisha Mulford's — [illegible — ~2 words] ville. Kate Smillie came to us from Chas. Sayre's — considerable fog \+ much smoke in the mtf., but in spite of it the air was pleasant. At 11 Annie \+ I got away with the Dominic's horse \+ buggy \+ after a pleasant drive (over an unpleasant country) we got to Mrs. Mulford's at 1 o'clk — we were very pleasantly rec'd — all were at home — Mr. M — her two daughters — the eldest, Mrs. Wynkoop? her husband \+ her little boy — miss Tipo \+ Mr. Roland Mulford \+ Woolsey Cabuist. At about 1.30 we sat down to lunch — had a very pleasant, chatty time — after lunch we sat out upon an open piazza, under an apple tree until 4 o'clk. Then we started upon our return getting back a little after 6. I left Annie at the old Jessup homestead where a Presbn. fair is in progress — our family all there to take tea. I went to the house after putting the horse out had tea alone. A — \+ the others came in before 8 \+ I, feeling tired went to bed soon after 9. Kate left the Sayre's \+ commenced her visit with us.
