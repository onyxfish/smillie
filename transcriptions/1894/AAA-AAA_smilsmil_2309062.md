## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 25, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

All day clearing without, at any time, getting to be very pleasant. I "put in" a long day in etching-room wrestling with dry-aquatint. I was much pleased at what seems to be a solution of one of my difficult problems — that is, an easily soluble paint-like composition, over which I can lay a coat of resin varnish, after which it will wash away promptly, leaving the aquatint ground under it exposed to the acid. Whiting, held together with a weak solution of gum arabic, when applied with a brush, dries quickly — a thin coat of resin varnish is passed over all — when dry the plate is immersed in the acid which at once attacks the whiting thro' the varnish & at once clears it away & gets to work on the copper. This process opens up many very desirable possibilities. I went down town just before dinner — after dinner, I spent some time with Stone & three in the swamp ditches with Ge°. Walker. After 5. I went down town again — met Raefolk & took him with me for a walk.
Ev'g. Reading a book — "The Sign of the four" — a detective story by A. Conan Doyle, who is having something of a vogue now — as I read I am out of patience with myself — I read so little now that when I do read it should be something better.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A sombre & fitful day — some stray sunbeams, much shadow & some showers. I got in a pretty good day's work up on my two aqua-tints, carrying the Ravello subject the furthest — but the interruptions are so many, that at best I get along but slowly. I spent some time up in Beech trees with Herrick. The squirrels seem to have gnawed the tender bark in some of them. I went down town about 5.30 — Ev'g. finished "the Sign of the four" — very poor stuff. Read news paper — wrote [part?] of a letter to George — and attended to my fires.
