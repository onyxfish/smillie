## Left Page

### Thur. March 29, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annie & Katie 7. went to New Haven

A nasty, wet day. Took R. to school atc. Then to work preparing to begin the Durand etching, getting my glass & tools, etc in order. Then took hold of young Metthpessel's second etching, added a little work to it & bit it in. Lunch at 1 — very soon after it A. & Katie went to train for New Haven — to-night a little German play is to be given by an Amateur Co — the Adams girls in it. I went to N.A — there to 2d Nat. Bank. Opened "Prize Fund" acct. for N.A. & D — also attended to A's business then to Geo. Haven Putnam's & saw him for a few moments — then to Knoellers — & to Hunters, & to Newmans — then again to N.A. Spent a short time with the Etching class & then home & until 6 worked on the Metthpessel plate. Dinner at 6.30 — Suet & Mary went with Charl. to Symphony Concert — I read awhile — Jim was much out of order about dinner time & about 8 had an explosive employing of his stomach — Cut him to bed in R's bed & R. slept with me — I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 30, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Tonight A. \+ K. Ret'd from N.H.

Brilliant day. Took R. to school, etc then home & commenced etching on Durand's plate, Portrait of Durand. Reed, & by 12.30 I had it all laid in. Then went for R. — Lunch at 1. After that I pussed awhile on plate & one of my Academy class then I walked down to N.A and stopped at D.B. Ruelter's — on my return to the house found two young ladies, League Students — waiting for me — one of them very desirous of joining my class there. Then Annie & Katie Ret'd from New Haven — then Mr. S.R. Koehler came & for the rest of the afternoon we sat & chatted & looked over my work. Dinner soon after 6 — Suet, Kate, Mary & the two girls went with Charl. to the Theatre — then Mr. & Mrs. Dving came & made a call — Jim out & eats again, so that he did not eat any dinner. I intended to go to Reception at the N.A. & commenced to dress & then gave it up O.K. & I sat & gossiped until 10 o'clk.

Reed head. 3 h.
