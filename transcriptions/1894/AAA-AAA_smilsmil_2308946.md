## Left Page

### Wed. March 7, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another charming, spring-like day. Took R. to school \+ attended to marketing etc — Men wrote a note to Mr. Ryle the would-be purchaser of the Jade proof — (I called at Charl's Room at 9 A.M. to enquire about Ryle) \+ enclosed a copy of Mr. Bischofs's letter refusing permission to sell. Then I went to Knoedalers \+ saw him \+ questioned him to learn if engravers or etchers had any recognized legal rights to proofs from their own work — He says that they have none — so far custom has its foundation only in precedent \+ courtesy — He knows of no cases where there have been judiciable decisions. Then I went for R — \+ got home at 12.45 Fanny K — to lunch \+ after that, she \+ Annie went to he Wed. P.M. club. We\+ two young men, medical students — of the name of Clark — acquaintances of Katie T's called upon her.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 8, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful m\+ t., clouding ufo later — then drops of rain before 1 o'clk. I took R — to school etc — then spent the rest of the m\+ preparing monuts for etchings for Drexel Inst. \+ writing back notes — Lunch at 1 — more writing in the afternoon — at 4 o'clk. I walked down to N\^o. 5 E. 12\^th. S — must Annie at Mrs. Januiens (Tho\^s A.) where we had a very pleasant call, Mr. J — coming in \+ making himself very agreeable. From there we went to 4 Kepple's Print shops in 16\^th S — Annie going on — I asked K. about Etchers rights to proofs \+ he was very discouraging — says that if I can get B — to regard custom as reasonable ness I will be successful — but if I make a fight for a legal right I will lose. — Went to Zinear \+ browsed — 3\^d Ave. \+ 18\^th S — \+ got supplies \+ walked home. Dinner at 6.30 — Stepsid every \+ went to bed early with sore throat —
