## Left Page

### Fri. June 15, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. \+ Mrs. Frank Brownell arr.

Very warm day — hazy with some mitigating clouds — I had an interview with Henick after bkfs. during which he took very submissively all that I had to say, but he gave me no chance, as he has upon other occasions, to put an end to our relations. Has he done so I should have taken him at his word. I was about constantly during the day finding countless things to attend to —
After an early tea I went down town \+ attended to some small matters \+ was a half minutes late at the station where I went to meet the Brownell's, just arr. from N.Y. we rode to the house at once had a little twilight \+ pale moon light on the piazza, together with some cold bottled beer, \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 16, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very much the same sort of merciless hot weather. In the morning a Thunderstorm passed to the north of us but we got none of it except the sound — I had many little matters to attend to, following up the plumbers in the tank etc. The big tanks were finished to-day, the lining with lead being completed, but connection with the leaders was not made. Arranged with Smith to begin painting new shingled roof on Monday — Payed taxes \+ saved $5. — Early eve'g, Mr. B. went down town to P.O. \+ then around with me. Another eve'g on piazza.
