## Left Page

### Mon. July 9, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very cool \- cloudy m'f, clearing &
a cloudless sunset & brilliant moon-
light night. All the m'f., as usual,
was spent out of doors about the place.
Stone & Herrick cut the tall grass on the
place for hay. I went downtown
about 12 with awning for Newcomb to
correct some of his mistakes. Annie
at Agur Lathrop's \+ at 1s \- found her at
Dav. Sayre's \- Then dinner \- In
the afternoon I wrote a letter to D.
Carroll Dunham asking him for
information concerning Sewerage and
Sewering. Before 6, went to P.O. with
the boys. Then tea \- about 7 o'clk. Will
Parke came in \+ spent an hour
with us \- Then read news paper.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 10, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day \- warmer \- out
of doors again all day \- following the
painters & others \- To-day has been
a popular election in town upon the
question: "Shall the town put itself
under bonded obligations to pay for an
Electric light plant" \- There was
some excitement on the part of those
in favor of it, during the afternoon &
a hack was kept bringing voters to the
polls. The vote was light \- 144 for \+
68 against it. I discovered that
the army of red squirrels that have
overrun us this summer have been
stripping the bark from the small branches
of the Sugar maples \+ have injured some
of the very finest trees so much as to threaten their ruin. Evg. Annual Meeting of Village Improvement Socy \- dismal
affair. Dav. Sayre \+ wife, Rev. Benton \+ wife
Mrs. Judge Searls. Mrs. Read \+ Hor. Lathrops \- Abel
Turrell's son \- Annie \+ self \- all that were there.
\- little progress \- little interest, \+ debt were
reported \- Dav. Sayre was elected Prest. \+
I was at my demand, reduced to V.P. All the
old officers were re-elected \+ at 10 o'clk. we
went home.
