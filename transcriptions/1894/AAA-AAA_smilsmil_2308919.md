## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 12, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very cold, windy day. I got up feeling quite well in the m'f —
better than I have for many days
past. I got to work up on my
12 × 18 Ravello drawing & was quite
hopeful over it. Jerry came for
my pictures just before noon but I
had only a small number ready
for him — promised to come for others
early to-morrow m'f. Lunch — worked
as long as I could see, but could not
finish the drawing — Dinner at 6.30
Sue & Annie went with Ed'mo & May
to a musicale at Mr. Peck's — I worked
at getting my sketches & drawings
into their frames until near mid-
night — Then Annie came home.
I got everything ready but the
Ravello Cathedral Tower — To bed
before 1 o'clk. pretty thoroughly
used up.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 13, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*(header note: "my show at the Century")*

Fine day. Got up with a beast of a
headache, giving me a hopeless feeling.
After b'k'f— I cut mats for 12 × 18 drawing,
Cathedral Tower, Ravello, & mounted the
drawing — about 11 I went to Century Club
& found Shirlaw & young Humphrey's Johnston
there — Jerry had not got all of my drawings
to the club, so I hung a neutral front and
returned home & worked on Ravello Tower
drawing until lunch time — I was unable
to cut anything — Annie & Kate lunched
with Eliz. Howe — at 1.30 I went to the club
Shirlaw had just finished — The men had
had no lunch, so I had to wait for them
until nearly 3 — It then took about two
hours to hang my show, covering more
wall than I had expected — Then I
returned home, went to bed & got a little
nap, much to the benefit of my head.
Dinner at 6.30 — I was able to eat a
little — Then dressed & at 8.30 was ready
to go to the club — Charl had come in
but he preferred to stay later with Kate.
To club before 9 — full meeting, was
very pleasantly greeted by many friends
who said that my show was creditable.
I seemed to me hardly important enough —
made up too much of small, colorless
items — The water-color over which I
have struggled so, looked to me cold,
dry & hard — The Morgan etching
was favorably commented upon — but
I did not see Mr. J. Pierpont Morgan there
— I left soon after 11 o'clk & got to bed
just at midnight.
