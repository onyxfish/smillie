## Left Page

### Thur. July 19, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

This, I think, has been the hottest day of the season, so far — a little breeze, but not a cloud to mitigate the blaze of the furnace-like heat of the sun. I was too much excited to sleep well last night \+ I got up feeling rather seedy. Bkfst — before 8 — Immediately after it, I went to Montrose \+ o. \+ had a half hours talk with VanP— He would not be confidential but he intimated to me as clearly as was necessary that he is working not upon any speculative project but for backers whom I was allowed to understand by influence, were the Met. life Ins. Co — About 9.30 we got a team \+ Phatton \+ rode up to the house where after waiting a few minutis Mr. Farrington got in \+ the two were driven to New Milford to catch 11.35 train to N.Y. I then, after spending some time with the Pauueds, set to work upon letters for Robbins — Nicoll \+ others — In the afternoon I took a long nap to balance the imperfect rest of last night — after that I sat under the maples until 6.30 and wrote to Robbins — Down town for mail at 8 — on my return, watering plants until after 9 — then on piazza — an exceedingly close, quiet night — Ther. went we went to bed 78°. \+ it did not fall during the night — It got as high as 92° during the day — on out shaded piazza.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 20, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 96°

Louise \+ Walter arr. from N.Y.

Intensely hot day — about 5 P.M. began to thicken \+ cloud — about 7 the temperature fell \+ there were threats of a storm — After 8 there was a wind storm with a little rain, heavy thunder and vivid lightning south of us. I spent much of the day writing to H.W. Robbins — J.C. Nicoll, C.S. Farrington \+ Lee \+ Lee concerning the N.A. business — posting the letters before 6 P.M. After tea I went to R.R. Station where I had a livery team — train on time altho' it ran over a man \+ team this side of Hart Lake — Walter \+ Louise on board — rode to the house at once — some little time spent in showing up, or off, the novelties of the place — for neither L. or W. have been here before — then, soon after 9 — they got to bed
