## Left Page

### Mon. April 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant but rather cold. Took R. to school, etc. To work on portrait of Dunean Reed — bit in etching with Nitric Acid. A new experience for me — my first etching on copper, I think, with Nitric — very successful it seemed to me. Jim went for R. I took a walk down to N.A. \+ back before lunch. After that, completed biting — then laid ground over all \+ drew in new work on face \+ commenced a series of perpendicular lines forming tint background — out for a short walk just before 6. Then a little rest before dinner — Every Council meeting — nothing either very interesting or important — about 10 o'clk — a com. for students. \+ N.A. asked me to make a short, informal address concerning etching next Sat. every before the ass't of students — and I agreed to do so — about 11 o'clk walked up home, Bolton Jones and Blashfield accompanying me.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 3, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, breezy, \+ quite cold — Took R. to sch. etc. — then home \+ to work upon Reed portrait. Finished the tint background went for R. — just as I was going to lunch a photographer \+ assistant from The Ill'd. American — (I think that is the name of the Weekly publication) came to get a photo of me at my easel. I asked him to come ½ an hour later, \+ he did so \+ then took two photos as I sat at my etching table — Then I went to Bk \+ made deposit to my Ct. Then to Photogravure Co. W. 23d St. \+ saw Mr. Edw'd. about my Aqua-tint grounds — He was very agreeable \+ told me what I wished to know — Then to Brooks Bros \+ bought a suit of clothes — Then home \+ had a nap before dinner \+ after dinner, a stupid eve'g.
