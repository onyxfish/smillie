## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 20, 1894

moved into Studio 36.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold day, growing milder towards evg. After bkf. \+ following duties I went to Manhattan Storage Warehouse \+ until noon was engaged turning my traps out of the room where they have been for the past 30 months \+ making selection — one part to go to my new studio, the other part to be re-stored. Went after Ralph at 12.30 — lunch at 1. \+ soon after 2. to N.A. where Manhattan Van brought my printing Table. I had to put into Antique School Room — then to Y.M.C.A. bldg, where the rest of my studio traps were carried to my Room No. 36 on the upper floor. The men got everything up before 4 o'clk. \+ then I spent some time in pulling things around. Home at 5 \+ rested before dinner — at about 8. Eliz. Howe came with Mr. Wells, of New Haven, I believe. Annie was obliged to hurry off with Mr. Green to a Choral Club meeting. Eliz. \+ Mr. W. left at 8.30 — I wrote a letter to Herrick. Mr. Barhy at (A.H. Baldwin's bro-in-law) called to see me (was out.) Mr. B. has not rec'd. any acknowledgement from the N.A. of her gift of her brother's Etching outfit \+ printing press.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 21, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very disagreeable day — Showers \+ mizzle until about 3 o'clk — then fog. Wood carpet men came to lay flooring in dining room as we were finishing bkf. — Men I found that the quartered oak had not been sent, but, instead a straight sawed kind — stopped the work — took R. to school \+ then went on to the Wood Co — Cor. 32d. S. \+ 5th Ave. \+ arranged to have the right sort of oak sent to-morrow. Went to studio \+ spent the rest of the a.m. getting things put into their places — Home to lunch at 1 — heavy showers — did not start back until 2.30 — Went to grocer's \+ butcher's — then to Pearsall's — bat, floor brushes, dust pans etc — then to studio \+ until 4.30, dusting contents of closets \+ drawers. At 5.30 went to Mr. Wm. Lee's, 32 W. 36th St. \+ had a half hour conference with him concerning a lease of N.A. \[&\] D. bldg to met. Life Ins. Co. Home to dinner at 6.30. At 8 went to N.A., meeting of Water-Color Socy — got thro' the business of the eveg quite early \+ had some pleasant chatting with members. Had an interview with Mr. Wm. A. Coffin — Manager of Woman's Portrait Exhn — \+ thanked him for courtesies — Home soon after 10.
