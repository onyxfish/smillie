## Left Page

### Sun. June 17, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day with breezes \+ clouds. Mer. 68°. to 80°. Annie with the Brownell \+ the boys made a circuit of the hill tops in the mf., but I had enough work in the garden gathering Asparagus \+ at the spring getting Water Cress. Then we all loafed, mainly upon the piazza, where it was very comfortable. Dinner at 2. I managed to do some writing. Tea at 7 \+ rested until near 11 upon piazza. I went to P. O. with letters at about 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 18, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Extremely hot \+ sultry with heavy thunder showers. Engaged about the place until the middle of the mf. Then down Town — met D. Sayre M.W. Benson the Presbt\- clergyman \+ — the Methodist one \+ talked strongly the matter of Sewers for the town. Learned that there is a Board of health — went to True, Sect'y of it \+ talked to him. Was requested to attend a meeting of the Board this p.m. Got home just before a very heavy shower with hail. Other showers followed \+ our new tanks got a good supply. The afternoon was spent working about until 6 when a team \+ carriage came \+ Mr. \+ Mrs. B. — A. \+ I went out for a ride. Clouds ins. \+ w. hid the sun \+ made it very pleasant. Roads bad — went to So. Montrose \+ over Butterfield's hill — were hurried on our return by fear of rain — in at 7.45. Tea at 8 — the rain did not come for half an hour — then it was very heavy with brilliant lightning. Every f in-doors. I attempted to read aloud some of Barrie's scotch sketches.
