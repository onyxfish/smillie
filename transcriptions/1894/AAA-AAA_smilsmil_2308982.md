## Left Page

### Fri. May 18, 1894

**Weather:** overcast n't. raw, chilly east wind — before noon, hot sunshine — heavy thunder showers at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast n't. raw, chilly east wind — before noon, hot sunshine — heavy thunder showers at night. At [illegible] much disturbed by receiving a short \+ disagreeable note from Mr Durand stating that my etching of the Luman Read portrait was so unsatisfactory to him that he would not use it but would have a heliogravure instead — went down to A.B.N. Co. saw Jones \+ showed him [illegible] of Reed portrait which he likes very much — then dickered with Freeland for check book for N.A.[illegible] \+ with Cushing for a Cash Book. While downtown I visited Cox — Exchange. Home to lunch at 1. after that I wrote to Merrick \+ Stone Harry Jones sent up Etching article to be cut very considerably — I did that — went to school with R — \+ after him at 5 — wrote other notes. Charl dined with us \+ in the eveg he took A — Kate \+ Katie to a Sousa concert at Mad. Sq. Garden. I stayed at home \+ wrote notes \+ packed \+ cleared up some of my belongings preparatory to the Montrose flitting.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 19, 1894

**Weather:** overcast, windy \+ raw with considerable rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, windy \+ raw with considerable rain. I spent the m'g clearing up the confusion of my work room \+ packing up \+ tidying up things. In the afternoon Katie Throop left us returning to her home in Po'keepsie. I went up to G.C. Station soon after 3 \+ after that walked up to Mr. H.G. Marquand's 11 E. 68th S. cor. Madison Ave. In answer to my note he made an appointment for me at 4 o'clk. I found him Talkative \+ agreeable \+ spent an hour with him Talking over Academy matters. I got no new ideas from him but he approved my plans as far as I could explain them, which was a gratification to me. I left him at about 5.15 \+ went into the Park and thence to 5th Ave. \+ home in time for dinner. Eveg, Charl came in.
