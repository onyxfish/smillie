## Left Page

### Sun. July 1, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day — Ther. got up to 84° in doors. Dawdled along so that I did not get bkfs. until after 9 — Then, I could not tell where the m'g went, but I know that I spent the largest part of an uncomfortably hot hour in the garden picking peas — Then I had a chance to cool off and read before dinner at 2 — Then I had a nap — Then I wrote a letter to Farrington with reference to Homer dee's projected Society for the Preservation of Engraving \+ Kindred Arts. I am offered 2 V. P — but decline. Tea at 7 — For an hour or more, watering flower beds — Then wrote a note to Chas. — Then to P. O. — Then home \+ to bed feeling very much used up, probably the result of exposure to the fierce heat of the sun this m'g.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very hot day but materially tempered by strong breezes — Got up feeling very seedy \+ did not quite recover myself during the day. I watched the men (plumbers) as they loosed the leaded joint of the very crooked 4" vent pipe running up the west front of the house — They got it tolerably straight \+ then Herrick got to work easing it. I wrote a reply to D. V. Gardiner's letter concerning Electric lights \+ Sewers pub. in Republican \+ the Democrat — Even'g, at 8 o'clk. I went down town to attend a meeting of the Town Council to press the necessity for some action in the matter of sewers, but there was no quorum — Home at 9 — newspapers until 10.
