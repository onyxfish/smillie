## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 16, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day, clear + crisp - like an Oct. day. Spent some time after bkft. writing notices of the coming Village Imp't. Society entertainment at our house, then went to "Democrat" but was too late as Conner was crowding in the last forms - Then to "Republican" with better luck. After dinner I wrote to Mortimer Smith \+ to Wm. Berrian asking for appraisals of value of M.A. f.D. Realty. Again down town, seeing that entertainment posters were put up in store windows, &c. - Ev'g, very cool - I read newspaper \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 17, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - quite cool - milder ev'g. Lovely moonlight - M'f, went to Rev. Mr. Benton's to talk with him about his horse - the buying or hiring of it - no conclusion reached - except that he was to have it this p.m. to drive to So. Montrose. I loafed about out of doors a good deal \+ did only a little ainting. When Mr. Benton ret'd. with his horse we talked again for a long time, he urging the merits \+ virtues of his horse - but I would not make an offer. Ev'g. after tea I went to P.O. On my way home stopped at Agur's atty. As an authorized officer \+ agent of the Sus. Co. Agri. Fair, he asked me to deliver the Annual Address on the Fair Ground in Sep. Many then given - \+ Kate - \+ Will Parke, \+ his Bessie - \+ two misses Wickham in addition to our own family, made quite a merry company -
