## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 2, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \+ threatening all them \+ thunder-storms all about. culminating early in the afternoon when there was a tremendous down pour \- gentler rain fell until after 5 o'clk. when the sky cleared. I had a days work again writing letters to N.A.'s who can't see their way clear to signing assents to sale of N.A. I got a number of assents \+ acknowledgment of conversion from Wm Beard, Eastman Johnson \+ Carl Brandt. Mary \+ Kate were at the house in the m'f and in the eve'f, on my return from P.O. I stopped at the Sayre's, where Annie was, \+ had a little visit with our family there.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 3, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some hours of gentle rain during the night \+ before m'f. The day was overcast, very humid \+ breezy \- slight exertion making one uncomfortably hot while the wind made him chilly.

I put in another day of work writing to N.A.'s upon the business of selling the A\- About 12, or before that, Annie \+ I went out riding after the Denton horse \- A\- got along so well, that I was left \+ Kate was taken in \- A. driving until 1\- In the afternoon, about 5, she again went out for a ride, taking Mary. \+ again she got home in safety \- Eve'f, very cool \+ windy \- most of it given to news-papers.
