## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 26, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A thick, smoky day, moderately cool — late 6hft° — \+ after that, until 1 o'clk. I was at work over my lettuce beds \+ set out 60 Trianon Cos plants. Dinner at 1.30 — \+ I don't know where the afternoon went. After tea, Annie \+ I went to the Sayres \+ bid Capt. \+ Mrs. Cromwell good bye as they leave early to-morrow mf. Evef, reading until 9.30 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 27, 1894

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

thicker \+ smokier. Cool wind from the east. I spent much of the mf trying to find White Gillie's teamster, who was to begin hauling dirt from under the Universalist Ch. missing to spread over portions of our grass. Also, in laying out levels for the croquet ground which I propose to have perfectly level \+ then new-seeded so that it shall be good for both croquet \+ tennis. I also spent a good part of p.m. transplanting lettuce \+ Kohl-Rabi. Evef. I feel quite used up — a sort of indescribable collapse — so I lay down immediately after tea \+ did not get up until I undressed at 10 — my feet hurt me like fury.
