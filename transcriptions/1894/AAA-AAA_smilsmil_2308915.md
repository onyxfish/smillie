## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 4, 1894

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast during most of the day, mild — After bkfst. took R. to school — attended to usual duties — then to work on "Cathedral Tower, Ravello" — worked standing, with drawing b? on the easel before me & slapped in a scheme of color (transparent) on blue grey charcoal paper — I am now beginning to enjoy the water-color work getting into the swing of it. At 12.30 went for R — lunch at 1 — Annie & Kate went to Brooklyn and saw Fanny K — I worked as long as I could see — then out & have a half hours walk — then a little nap & dinner at 6.30 — Edw. & Mary with us. Charle came in in the evening.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 5, 1894

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day with some rain in the m+. sunshine during the latter part of the afternoon. I got up in the m+ quite busted — a sort of in-fluenza, I suppose. I did not attempt to work on my drawing in the m+ but wrote a lot of notes that have been piling up against me. Annie went to Unitarian league meeting & so, was not home to lunch. In the afternoon I got to work on my drawing & later I regretted it. Feeling far from well I think that I did the picture a positive injury. Evely wrote a note to J. Pierpont Morgan concerning my etched portrait of his father.
