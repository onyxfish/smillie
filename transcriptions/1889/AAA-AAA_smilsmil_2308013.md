## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 13. 1889.

A very cold, blustering day with lots of dirt. To studio at 10. most of the m'g putting things away — cleaning up & preparing to print to-morrow — Home to lunch at 1 — after writing a letter to W. McNair of Saratoga, from whom I rec'd a letter this m'g giving particulars of Ehninger's death & asking me if Mr. E. might send to me studio properties to be distributed among young & poor artists. After lunch went to S. P. Avery Jun's for awhile — then in to see the Chs. Miller pictures — Ortgie's gallery — then to Am. Art. Ass. — t saw the Stebbins catalogue — then to N.A. of D. & to studio at about 4 — Completed arrangements for printing to-morrow — Home about 6 — dinner — Eve'g before a fine fire — with a little reading & a little writing for occupation. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 14. 1889.

A cold, windy, dirty day — but bright overhead — To studio at usual time & got to work proving my Dry Point "Poppies" but found that I could do nothing with my old ink and strong oil, so, at 11.30 I went down to Knoedel & Voigt's in Canal St. & got ink & oil. — Back to studio & then home to lunch — The Adams girls had arr'd from New Haven to make a visit of a few days. Back to studio at 2.30 & printed until near 5 getting some pretty good proofs. Home at 6 — dinner — Self, Jes. & Emiley came & took the Adams girls to Daly's Theatre — I took A. to her Choral Club — then home & dressed — then to Union League Club — there for an hour — then to 14th St. for A. — & home ab't 11 — have walked 6 miles today & been on my feet all day at work —

As whooping cough has broken out in Ed'd Cook's family, Mrs. Walton came to see A. — to arrange, if possible, to have her little school meet in our front room — upper floor — A. consents.
