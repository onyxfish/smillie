## Left Page

### January, Thursday 10, 1889

A horrible day of wind & rain with
a touch of a cyclone about 8 P.m.
I was so completely used up after
last night's experience that I spent
the m't in bed & got some more
sleep. Yale was sent for — after
lunch — I took a sleeping powder
& again slept — Evef. Annie
went to her Choral Club — Fretta
went to her church & I spent the
time on the nursery lounge, after
the example of the starving prisoners
in Libby who amused themselves thinking
of the feasts they would like to eat.
I thought of the lots of work —
painting & etching that I would
like to do if I were strong & well.
To bed at about 10.30.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 11, 1889

Had a peaceful night of rest & got
up feeling happier than yesterday
m't but very, very seedy — took bkp.
in bed — then up & washed & dressed.
The m't I spent, as usual, with the
baby — & slept again while he slept,
lunch about 1 — Yale came in some
after & gave me a looking over, then
as the day was fine, he thought that
I might go to the studio for a few
minutes — At 3 I started out, stopped
at Butler's & got mat; then to studio and
put pf. of "Reading Homer" into frame &
got things ready to send to the Century.
saw Geo. for a few minutes — Home again
at 4.20 — tea at 6.30 — Evef. wrote
to Frank Herrick — Barnabas Carey —
Lizzie Smillie & a note to W. Heber R.
Newton —
