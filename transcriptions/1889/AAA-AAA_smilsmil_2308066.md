## Left Page

### May, Thursday 30. 1889.

Rain in the early mg. Got up with a wretched headache & felt that between headache & rain but little progress would be made during the day — but before noon it cleared charmingly, altho' the wind continued fresh from the S.E. Went to market after bkft — then ret'd & spent the mg gathering things for packing in big box, trunk etc. Lunch at 1 — at 2.30 Annie, Jim, Ralph, Fretta & I met Charl & Geo. & family at Geo.'s studio & spent an hour or more having groups photos. Then Annie, Jim & I went to Aunt Annie's in E. 17th St — saw her, Mary & Grace — met there Ms. Chas. Butler & Helen — we left about 5 — I rode up to Dr. Yale's & left for him a pf. of my dry point Poppies — home before 6. Dinner at 6.30 — after that, for an hour or more packed big case — then Charl came in & I went with him to his Dark Room to see the negatives just develaped — some of them, very fine — back at 9.30 — Walter at the house — He left at 10 & we went to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 31. 1889.

Strong wind all day from the S.E. with a brisk shower just before noon — much of the day was overcast — at times the sun shone out very warm — After bkft., marketing etc. & some messages — then down town to the International News Co. & to Pate's where I saw the young man & came to a more satisfactory understanding concerning "the Goldsmith's Daughter" — then up town — to Rumsey's Stp. to Safe Deposit — to Studio, where I made my adieu — then to grocer's — plumber's & home at 1.15 lunch — after that I took things very easily as I had plenty of time — I completed all packing before 6 o'clk — Dinner at 6.30 — Evg. I marked cases & did some other odd jobs — Geo., Nellie & Charl. came — C. brought a long and amusing letter from Kate — To bed before 11 o'clk.
