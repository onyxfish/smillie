## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 1. 1889.

Heavily clouded in the early morning —
from 10 to noon, clearing brightly — About
2 P. m. heavy squalls came up with
thunder & lightening — The temperature
was quite mild — We got up late.
bkfs. about 9 — I feel much better
than I did yesterday — but moved
about cautiously — some time with
Cary as he unloaded three large
coping stones for top of brick wall.
At 1 o'clk Crowly & Halpin came —
they had hardly more than got to
work when the first shower came
& they were driven off — shower suc-
ceeded shower & [illegible] all the p. m.
I made a charcoal sketch on canvas
of a subject that I found a day or two
ago. Eve'g reading "Century" for
Oct. & newspaper.

It blew like mad all night.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 2. 1889.

A hateful day, cold & bleak with high
wind & driving showers bet. 8 & 9 A.m.
Carey & his son came, after 7, & worked
until after 3, without going to dinner,
dressing & laying the large coping stones
over the stone & brick wall. Crowley came
about 8 & worked until about 4 when
he left — his job finished — Halpin had
a tumble from his wagon & hurt his
leg so that he could not work, but he
hauled several loads of manure — I
continued to feel better & spent much
time with the men — I set palette &
tried to paint in a bit that I saw
in the valley just west of us two or three
days ago — but I failed — At 5 I went
to the foundery & then down Town —
Eve'g. I read awhile & then wrote
part of a letter to S. R. Koehler. then
Annie read aloud from Oct. Century
until 11 o'clk — then to bed.
