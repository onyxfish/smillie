## Left Page

### February, Monday 25, 1889

A light face of snow last night. The day cleared into a fine, bright bit
of winter. I got to studio's about
10 — again feeling very much
out of sorts & unlike work. I
spent some time at work over
dry-point [illegible] — then got out painting
"A bit on the Susquehanna" & worked
in it for some time with sand paper
& just as I was about setting palette
Chatkutz came in & stayed until
lunch time — then home — lunch —
then to S. P. Avery's but. Then to Ortgies'
gallery — a collection of pictures to be
sold — met there Mr. Abner Harper & had
a pleasant chat. To studio at 4 — worked
for awhile on plans for Herrick — Montrose
— Home at 6.30 — dinner — Evf
some reading aloud from Harker's
mag. for March.

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 26, 1889

Partially overcast with snow at night.
To studio at 10 — had some letters
to write — then C. Freer from Etchin[g]
club came in & stayed for some time
— then it was near noon — so, as it
was too late to begin to paint, I went
to the Bank — then to Knœdler's & saw
the [illegible] etchings & dry points — then
some time with Olme & the paintings —
Home to lunch at 1.30 — to studio at 2.30
all the afternoon working over [illegible] &
mountings & getting head of Mother Cook
that I worked over into frame — 6.30
when I got home — dinner — Evf
& I went to Ed W. Cook's to bid
Mr. & Mrs. Sprague & Miss Williams good
bye — they sail for Bremen to-morrow —
Home before 9 — Finished letter
to Kate — mailed it at 10.30 —
then to bed.
