## Left Page

### April, Sunday 14, 1889.

A delightful day clear, with a fresh breeze.
After a bath & rub down, Jim & I had
a half hours walk before b'kf't — After
off. we sunned ourselves in the garden
until after 11 — Then Jas. Wright, Marion,
Annie & I rode over to [illegible] at
Chestnut Hill — It was an exhilirating
ride — the atmosphere & the spring color
were enchanting — Edith joined us
at W.N.'s, having come up by rail —
We went over the place bringing away
quantities of Roses from his very fine
Rose houses — Home before 2 when we
had dinner — most of the afternoon
again out of doors — Annie & I, with Jim
& Fanny W.'s three boys rambled by the
brook & in the woods until 5.30 — Tea
at about 7 — Every. general chat
until 10. 30 — To bed at 11 —
Glorious moonlight night.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 15. 1889.

*Home again.*

Weather still fine altho' getting to be
a little thick — About 10 o'clk Edith
Walter's wife, Annie & I, drove over
to look at some new small houses
that Edith has been building — home
at 11. 30 — completed packing — lunch
at 1 — Ralph insisted upon sleeping
so soundly that at 1.45 I had to wake
him that he might get his dinner —
Edith had to harness the horse & drive
to the depot but, after all, we caught
the 2.25 train with a few minutes to
spare — at Germantown junction we
transferred to a New York train & got
to N.Y. at 4.40 — It was about 5.30
when we got to the house — At 6.30
we had supper — The trunks came —
the children were put to bed &
we, after unpacking, got to bed
early.
