## Left Page

### February, Saturday 23, 1889

Very cold day — clear & windy — got to studio about 10 — & spent some time writing notes to J.D. Gill &
Frank Herrick — also, in completing the mounting of some "Poppies" proofs.
After 11 took them (4) over to N. A. f.d. & gave them to W. Greer — met Miss McChesney & had a pleasant chat with her — It was after 12 when I got to studio & then put half a dozen more Dry Point p.f.s in proofs in press. Home to lunch about 1.30 — after that, Annie went to Opera Matinee to meet Margaret ParkerWhite — W & Mrs. Knudsen called & I spent some time with them — after that had to play nurse until 3.30 — then feeling headachy, I took a nap — Up at 4.30 with children until dinner. After that, dressed & before 9 went to pt. H. Morse's — Mad. Ave. near 49th b. where we spent the eveff. very pleasantly until midnight — Some tableaux vivants, illustrating poems by Howells, Stedman, many Makers Dodge & a bit by Frank Stockton — all read (present) & by R.W. Gildon — all read by J.H. Morse — Home about 12.30 & to bed about 1.

## Right Page

### February, Sunday 24, 1889

A very cold, clear day — Blkpt soon after 9 — About 11, Annie, Jim & I went to the Water-Color exhibit at the N.A. f.d. where I substituted a later pf. of my Dry Point "Poppies" for the one in the frame — Among the Water-colors until near 1 — then we separated & I went to Charl's washerwoman at 297 Seventh Ave. & then home. Dinner at 2 — after that Charl came in for a few minutes — then Emma & Margery Spring — It was about 4.30 where A. & I got started to make a call on Jos (bro. Will's widow) at 32 4 W. 46th St. we saw her for a few minutes, finding her in a wretched plight — physical & mental — Home about 6 — Had some tea at 6.30 — Eveff. Annie & I sat in front of the parlor fire & read & dozed until after 9 — to bed before 10.
