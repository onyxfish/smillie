## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 19, 1889

Dull day — to studio soon after
10 — put things in order to begin
at my etching work again — then
got out my plate — Huntington's
"Goldsmith's Daughter" & worked
with dry point & graver until
near 1 — then to Bank — then
home to lunch — To studio before
3 & worked with a heavy head
ache until near 6 — then home —
Dinner at 6.30. Before 8 I took
Annie to extra rehearsal of her
Choral Club — then to Century Club
& finished liles to many & posted
it. Read until almost 10 — then
went for A — it was nearly 11 o'clk
before rehearsal was over — we
walked up 5th Ave. with Fanny
& Will Kimball — home at 11.30 &
to bed. Headache all night.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 20, 1889

A nasty day — rain & sleet — To
studio at 10 — worked on Huntington
plate all day — going home to lunch
at 1 — after I finished my day's
work I set up the Ehninger easel
that Mrs. E. sent to me — Kelley
brought today the Ehninger months
painting that he has lined & mounted
for me. Home at 6.30 — Evelf Will
Kimball came & took A — to the
opera. Taking my place, as I did
not feel equal to it. I fussed
about until near 10 & then to bed.
