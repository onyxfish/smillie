## Left Page

### March, Thursday 7, 1889

Bright & breezy — Ralph seemed to be better in the m'fg — having no fever —
but as the day wore on we found that
he could not retain anything on his
stomach — It was after 10 when I got
to studio — I set to work at once to
paint a Spring picture on top of my
study (11×18) for my large painting of
Cliffs of Normandy. I did not attempt
to make a drawing — but drew the
forms with my brush as I painted —
Home at 1 — back before 3 — & painted
until 5 when I got the little painting
done in a way that encouraged me very
much — Home at 6 — After dinner the
younger Miss Bosé came & I took her &
A. to a 5th Ave. Bus & then I went up to
Yale's & saw him — home at 8.30 & wrote a
note to Bernan concerning Mr. Mc. Potse and
her ceiling — Yale came soon after? — lanced
Ralph's gum — At 9.45 I went down to
14th S. for A. & we walked up home
getting in at 10.45 — Ralph sleeping
very quietly — To bed at once.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 8, 1889

Windy & cloudy with a slight fall of snow
at night. Got to studio at 10 & painted
on a study made at Montrose a year
ago last summer 12½×17 trying to make
a picture of it — "A back lot". Home at
1 — Ralph very fretful & perverse after
his nap; so I stayed with him until
3 o'clk. & prevailed upon him to take a
little peptonized milk — His stomach
has retained all that he has eaten
to-day — To studio & painted until 5
Home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Ralph
seems to be better — Evn'g. at 8
Annie & I went to Chas. Isham's, 123
E. 37th S. & made a call there until 9 —
then to E.A. Henry's — on our block in
38th S. Met Thos. Moran & wife & Gleason
& the Lerion League & his wife there —
stayed until 10.15 — then home &
to bed —
