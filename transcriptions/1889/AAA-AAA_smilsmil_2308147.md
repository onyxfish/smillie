## Left Page

### November, Friday 8, 1889

A nasty, raw day with rain &
drizzle, growing constantly worse day —
Annie read her paper concerning
proportions of time that should be
given to Church, Society & home before
the Woman's Auxillary Unitarian body
at Collyeis Ch., 34th S. & Mad. Ave.
Not being a woman I was not al-
lowed to hear it read but am told
that it was, in every way, a success.
She was not home to lunch. I
worked over the proofs that Patt sent
up to me to sign + in the afternoon
got out working p.p. to send to Nat.
museum at Washington. Home to
dinner at 6. Took A. to Ed. W. C's at
7.30 + from there took her, Mary, [illegible]
C. + the Adamss gives to the German The-
atre in 15th S. — then to Century where
I spent the evef. until 10.45 — then
to the Theatre + found it closed — home
— A. had just got in. — To bed
at 11.30.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 9, 1889

Very heavy down-pour of rain all
day — Did not keep engagement to
take p.p. to Mr. Huntington to sign
as I did not wish to risk getting
them wet. To studio at about 10
& spent rest of p.f. making selection
from among p.p. of Goldsmith's "Daughter"
taken by me to get the size to which I am
entitled. — Being boy from Montrose ar-
rived to 2°. Nat. But. — then home to lunch.
I at 1.30 — dined at Alice J's to lunch —
I spent the afternoon unpacking
& putting things away & hanging
curtains — Home at 6 — Dinner.
Eve'g. after some reading I un-
packed big Montrose boy & got to
bed before 11.
