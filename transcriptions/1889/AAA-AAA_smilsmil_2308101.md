## Left Page

### August, Thursday 8, 1889

A very pleasant day but with indications that another change of weather was coming — I spent the morning printing — in the course of the day took five p[roof]s of the Goldsmith's Daughter then waxed the plate, screwed it into its box & at 4·15 was at the [illegible] Co's office with it — then felt like a free man & I wandered about town with Albert & the team & Ralph & Jim — went to the blacksmith's — he took off Selim's shoe & said that he was lamed by gravel which he was able to remove — then drove up to the old Beauty house & found Annie & saw Mr. Shaffer there home. Mr & Mrs. Jaud. Jessup called while we were away.       Eve[ning]
I wrote awhile in Studio's but being very tired I gave it up and went to bed at 10.

From 7 until after 8 P.M. — Mr. Beach talked to interest me in a scheme to build a branch R.R. from Montrose to some near point on the D.L.& W.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 9, 1889

A dull & threatening day with some rain between 4.30 & 6 o'clk p.m. The m[ornin]g was spent about the place, cutting Raspberry vines, etc. & doing some writing — Parke came to a[id in] studio from 12 to 1 — then dinner — wrote to Pate & Co. & to Parke & Tilford — went to P.O. at 4.30 — then numerous small matters about town — to Warner's — Doolittle's & other places — some time with Barney getting a new seat for my sketching stool — Rec[eive]d a very warm letter of congratulation from S.R. Koehler on receipt of p[roof] of Huntington plate.    Mr. Jos. Carnalt, Mr. Parke & Mrs. Sturgis called before tea time.    Eve[nin]g, having Rec[eive]d a very pleasantly enthusiastic letter from W.S.R. Koehler, I wrote to Pate & Co. giving them portions of it — To P.O. about 9 — then went to Apollo Stone's & cut some Hollyhocks — then home.
