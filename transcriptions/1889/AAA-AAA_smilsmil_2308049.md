## Left Page

### April, Friday 26, 1889

A horrible day of rain & wind — I was to go to Ridgefield with Ge°.
to look at a place there for sale,
to studio about 10 — then to AAA
& took a look at the 5th Prize Fund
[illegible] After 11 went to Safe Deposit
Co. with Annie's $27,000 in Bonds &
spent an hour in writing descriptions
& there — cutting coupons & making a
deposit — a little after 12 went to
Latham's — the tailor — not in — then
to studio — & home to lunch before
1.30 — the storm was so fierce
that I did not go to studio until
after 3 — then finished some of the
[illegible] for [illegible] & did other little
things that have been for some time
requiring attention — Home after 6 —
dinner — Eve'g loafed — I should
have gone to adj. Annual Meeting
& Etching Club, but the weather was
too bad & I did not feel well
enough. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 27, 1889

A heavy & continuous rain all
day — Got to studio about 10 and
to work on my Huntington plate
with dry point — & burnisher — It
was 1.30 when I got home to lunch —
after that, rode down to 17th S. & saw
Wash. Wilson to thank him for the cards
he sent us to see the show on Tues.
Then to Latham's to say that we had
seats — then bought some rubber leg
& head coverings — then to studio at
3.30 & until 5.30 burnished on sky
of my plate while Champney sat
beside me & read aloud from a
work by Emerson (English) to
prove that Photography should rank
with the graphic arts, not so high as
painting perhaps, but in advance of
all the monochromatic branches.
Home at 6 — dinner — Eve'g
reading in a desultory way.
To bed soon after 10.
