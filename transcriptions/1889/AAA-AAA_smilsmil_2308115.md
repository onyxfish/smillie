## Left Page

### September, Thursday 5, 1889

much of the time, overcast with
a cool wind, threatening a change
of weather — my trouble does not
leave me & I hung about the house
all day. The men finished the
excavation about noon & later,
Mr. Hank Williams commenced the
laying of the wall. Gretta went
off for a picnic, not to return
until to-morrow. — Mr. Williams
& Mr. de Schweinitz called in the
afternoon — Ely. for Williams
called. Got to bed soon after 10
Annie slept in the nursery with
Ralph, Gretta being away attending
a picnic at Silver Lake.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 6, 1889

overcast wet — Rain, coming in with
a heavy shower, commencing at a little
after 10. Then the men gave up the
work of laying well for the new milk
room. As my bowel trouble continues,
Annie, when down town, asked Dr.
Richardson to call & see me, which
he did at about 3 o'clk. At 1.30
Albert started for New Milford to meet
Sophie Ireland & her sister — He took
to Exp. Office a pkg of pf. to go to
Mr. Huntington for his signature.
The Ireland's got to the house about
5 o'clk. At 6.30 we left them to take
tea with Jess while Annie, Miss
Toshay & I went to Dan Sayres to tea,
about twenty, to meet Mr. Jessie Com-
stock Edgar. Of course I had a foretry
dismal time as I felt wretchedly
& could eat nothing of the delicacies
offered. Home by brilliant moon-
light at 10.30 & to bed at 11.
