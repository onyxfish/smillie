## Left Page

### September, Thursday 19. 1889.

A nasty day — cold, with high
wind — some sunshine and more
showers — for brief moments we
had some lovely octoberish views
from our piazza — A bad day for
the Susy. Co. Fair — In the m'f Annie
assisted me while I signed 53 pf
of the Goldsmith's Daughter — 7 more
I would not sign as they were below
the average. — Then I went to Har-
rington's mills & selected some timber
for sills — then to foundery & saw Beach
— then to P.O. & home before 1. Crowley
& Halfin worked on brick wall all
day — P.m. I prepared plf of pfp.
Took them to Wp. office at 4.30.
Evef wrote note to Pate.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 20. 1889.

A very disagreeable day — cold and
raw with many showers beginning in
the early m'f. Halfin did not work
but sent a substitute who, with Crowley,
commenced at 9 A.m. & gave up at 4
being at last driven off by persistent
rain — I spent the m'f out of doors
with chisel & gouges working among the
trees, cutting away dead wood & cutting
out borers. I went down town just
before noon. Afternoon I worked
on Study of Palsies — 6×6" — that I
have done for the Nat. museum at Wash-
ington — D. Muelford came in & sat with
me for awhile — we walked down
town together at about 5.30 —
Evef, after looking over news papers
listened to Mastey's letters, Annie
reading — until 10 o'clk — then to bed.
