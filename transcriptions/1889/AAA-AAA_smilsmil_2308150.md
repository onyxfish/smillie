## Left Page

### November, Thursday 14, 1889

Bright, beautiful day — Geo. came
to the house & made arrangements
with me to go to Ridgefield with
him on 11 A.M. train — then I wrote to
Mr. Woodin & to Mrs. W—, went to
market & at 10.50 met Geo. at the
station — Got to R— at 1 o'clk — had our
dinner & then walked out to the Sey
mour farm that he wished me to
look at — we saw Seymour & walked
all over the place — I am very much
pleased & hope that Geo. will be able
to buy it — we then walked back &
took the 3 45 train getting into N.Y.
at 6. Eve 4., at 8 o'clk. Annie & I
went to the R.R. men's Clubhouse Cor.
Mad. Ave & 45th S. & heard Champney's
amusing "Chalk Talk" — got home
before 10 o'clk — & right to bed.

a small bill they have claimed ag-
us — then to Sloane's & met Annie and
ordered carpets & shades — then to Wen-
derlich's & saw the Platt etchings — then
to Jackson's & saw some iron wood boxes
To studio about 12 — home to lunch at
