## Left Page

### June, Saturday 1. 1889.

*off for Montrose.*

Up before 6 — dull wtf, threatening rain which came heavily before 8 o'clk.
We had bkfs. before 7 — Truck came for boxes
& trunks at 7.15 — man to cut off gas and
water soon after — carriage at 7.50 at
a few minutes after 8 we all left the house.
Geo. Cook did not come as we expected, to
get the keys. Our baggage came in time —
we had good drawing room car seats &
everything was pleasant in spite of the
rain. We found that by the new time
table we had to go on to New Milford —
at Boonton I telegraphed Herrick to meet
us there — our train got in at about
3.20 — It was 3.40 before we started on
our ride to town — The rain had ceased
& the weather had cleared deliciously —
altho' the roads were exceedingly bad
we enjoyed the ride & the soft, balmy
air very much — It was almost 5.30
when we got to the house. Mrs. Cuny
was there & things were ready for us —
I had a chance to take a hurried
look about before tea time — & again
after that. Every, being quite tired
after a little unpacking we got to bed
at about 9.30.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 2. 1889.

A very pleasant day — awoke early
enough but it was 8 before I got up
& 9 before we had bkfs — After that I
spent the mtf until 1 o'clk. going
carefully over the place looking at
everything. Then in & had a short
nap before dinner at 2 — After that
Herrick came up & I had a long talk
with him — I then finished the un-
packing of trunk & putting away con-
tents — & then another rest until
tea time — After that had to trim
some of the lamps as they had not
been attended to during the winter as
I had directed — Then wrote short
notes to Geo. & Charl. Between 9 &
10 o'clk. Took them to P.O. — then home
& to bed.
