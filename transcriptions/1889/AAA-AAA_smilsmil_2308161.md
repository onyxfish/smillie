## Left Page

### December, Friday 6, 1889

Bright & pleasant — To studio a
few minutes before 10 & to work on
Double Holly hocks, with scraper
broadening & quieting the lights.
When I had gone over that part
there I reinforced the darkest
parts with dry-point work — To
lunch at 1 — on my return to
studio I took proofs, working on
the plate between each — Sam.
Coleman came in. It was
dark before I got things cleared
up. Then home — dinner —
Eve'g quietly at home, toasting
my shins & reading.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 7, 1889

Bright, pleasant day — got to studio
a few minutes after 10 & very soon got
my Hollyhocks into a condition for
a proof — Having 3 pieces of paper
wet I took three p[ro]fs., the state of
the plate being satisfactory. Sam Coleman
brought in some photos of fine Jap. & Chinese
props à propos of a change that he advises
in my large Poppy drawing that I am going
to dry-point. It was after 1.30 when
I got home to lunch — after that I went
to barber's & had hair cut — then to studio
& put one of my m[e]t. p[ro]f. into a frame. Then
took it to Century — A p[ro]f. of the Goldsmith's D[aughter]
had been sent down in the m[ail]. Then
I did a little shopping & got home before 6.
Eve'g. Walter went with Annie to Phil.
Between 8 & 9 I went to Century where
I had a proof of the Goldsmith's Daughter
also of Double Hollyhocks — mezzotint
& dry-point — I was very much flattered
by the attention paid to my proofs
by brother artists & others — Mr. T.B. Clark
broke the ice between us & congratulated
me very warmly upon my success.
It was 12.30 when at last I left the
Club House with Geo. & Chat & walked
up home — To bed soon after.
