## Left Page

### June, Friday 21, 1889.

A very pleasant day until near
5 p.m. when a series of heavy thun
der showers came up — continuing
until after we went to bed at 10.30
The tinkers got studio cistern finished
in the m'f & Herrick finished the
carpenter work about it in the after-
noon. In the m'f gathered a
number of very large strawberries that
had been partly pecked away by robins.
They constituted our first fruits from
the new strawberry bed — Ralph was
much better during the day. I got
to work on my plate at 11 o'clk & con-
tinued until 1 o'clk then dinner —
from 2.30 until after 5 — got plate
about ready or proof — owing to rain
we did not have our drive — Annie
had invited Mr. & Mrs. Christman to go
with us. Tea before — about 8 I
went down town in a heavy shower — had
to go as far as de Schweinity's — Home before
9 — got a letter from Sauckner asking me
to send him 6 of my large plate as an
opening of negotiations for sale. He does
not know that I have sold it to Patri.
Read news papers until after 10 —
then to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 22. 1889.

*Mary Cook & 3 children arrived*

A blustering, windy very cool day — with
clouds that at times threatened showers
& then much sunshine — After bkf-
went down town with Herrick to arrange
about his meeting some stage driver — then
he took the greys & started for Jt. Bend
where Mary Cook & Tom, Edna & Louise
are expected to arrive at 3.45. I went
to the Dr's & asked him to call again
& see Ralph who continues without
appetite & extremely irritable — then home
& attended to several things about the
house — dinner at 1 — again, hanging
pictures & hooking up curtains & bassing
some work at doors — Herrick drove
up with the party at about 6.30, all in
very good order in spite of the cool, windy
drive after a long R.R. journey — Had tea
soon after 7 — then the children were
put to bed & after some general chat-
ting we got there ourselves soon after
10.

Ther. during the night fell to 45°.
