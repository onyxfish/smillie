## Left Page

### October, Sunday 27, 1889

**Ther.** 47° last night.

Heavy Rain, in showers, all day — Blf
at about 9 — drew for two hours,
looking after fires about the house
& such like elevating duties —
then matters about the studio
preparations to our leaving kept me
busy until dinner time — After
that, a nap — again in studio —
Annie read to me her paper —
prepared to read before Women's
Unitarian League — tea at 6.30
Eveft. writing until 9 o'clk —
then to P.O. To bed soon
after 10 — very mild night.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 28, 1889

**Ther.** 41° last night.

Very disagreeable day — heavily clouded
with much mizzle & drizzle — I spent
the mft in my etching room at
work mounting plt. for Roehler, signing
plts for Pate & Co. & writing letters. Sent
proof plt. to P. & Co. in the afternoon.

After dinner I had one of Hunter's
geese brought into the studios & I painted
from it as long as I could see — I had
clothes spread & then tried it by its
legs to a weight — after a struggle it
gave up & hardly moved again — I
got a fairly good sketch — Went
down town before tea — Eveft. I
added a P.S. to letter to Mary that
Annie had written & took it to the
P.O. at 9 o'clk — It was 10.30,
or later, when we got to bed.
