## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 20, 1889

A very mild day — curious weather —
overcast & thick — then struggling sun-
shine. Bkft. after 8 — After some out
of door work went to studio & wrote
a long letter to S. R. Koehler — then
took some proofs from dry-point
plates of "Poppies" — Dinner at 1 —
After dinner wrote a letter to Pate.
At 4 Annie & I went out riding
taking Ralph & Gretta. Back
before 6 — Annie & I had part
of a game of Croquet before tea
at 6.45. then sat upon piazza
for awhile — Woolsey Carmalt
called — left at 9 — then I went
on with the washing of my brushes —
after that read newspaper and
to bed after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 21, 1889

A hazy, hazy, breezy day — Ther. got
up to 80°. I pottered about all
day, fussing with one thing and
another & accomplishing but little
— I feel played out & am taking
a rest — Finished the mounting
of three Goldsmith's Daughter [illegible]
— 2 of them to be sent to Pate & Co.
did not go riding in the after-
noon, but tried to play croquet
with Annie, making some miserable
work at it — Tea at 6.30 — Went
down town about 7.30 — back in
an hour — a dash of rain at
about 9 — & more a little later —
to bed just before 11.

Parks came up in the [illegible] with
a paper to get me to subscribe
for stock in a proposed R. R. from
here to some point on the D. S. & W.
R.R. near Montrose depot —
I didn't "see it".
