## Left Page

### May, Thursday 16, 1889

lunch with Tweedy's at Plainfield.

A very pleasant day — at 10.30
Annies, Jim & I left the house —
went to 6th ave & 33d St — & by Elev —
RR to Courtlandt St — then to foot
of Liberty & by 11.30 Efts to Plain-
field getting there at 12.15. We
were met at the depot by Florence
— drove at once to the house —
pleasantly greeted by all the
family. Lunch at about 1 o'clk
May Tiffany Murray came later —
we spent the afternoon on the
piazza until about 5.15 — then
rode to depot with Belle and
took 5.39 train in to N.Y. getting
home at 7.05 — Supper — Evet'g
reading for awhile — then wrote
into Annies book a full statement
of recent changes of the RR securities
— sales of some high priced ones
& reinvestment of the proceeds.
To bed at about 11.30.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 17, 1889

A very warm day — to studio at
about 10. Worked all the m'f on
plate — before going home to lunch
prepared baker for p.p. in the after-
noon — Champney came in and
read to me from proof sheets his
Harper mag. article on "A half century
of Photography". Rec'd N.A.D. check for
Academy picture "Spring is coming" ($135)
& took it to bank — then home to lunch.
Back at 2.30 & spent the p.m. taking
two p.p. not very encouraging — my
work of burnishing is too timid — the
progress is very slow. Home soon
after 6 — dinner — Evet'g did not
feel well — wrote into a small
pocket book an abstract of Annie's
possessions — to bed at 10.30.
