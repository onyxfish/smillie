## Left Page

### October, Friday 25, 1889

**Ther.** 28° last night

A bleak day with one brief gleam of
sunlight about 4 o'clk in the after-
noon. It was raw & chilly. I was
out of doors all day following and
helping Coyce, Rafferty & Albert in
taking up & setting out three trees.
At night I was very tired, & took
some naps in the big chair before
going to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 26, 1889

**Ther.** 40° last night

Very much milder — a quiet,
grey, brooding day with rain
just before 5 o'clk. Again
I was all day with the men,
but we got only two trees set
& some beds made for Pansies.
In the aft Albert drove over to
So. Montrose taking Annie & the
children with him, getting back
after 12. In the afternoon Mr.
Schaffer & his bro called. I went
down town after 5. Sweet
after reading my paper I took a
nap & after that wrote to W. Kosler
& attended to some other matters.
To-day recd — a pile of [illegible] from
Pete & Co. for signatures — also
a note from Charle, enclosing very
satisfactory letters from Mary & Katie.
After 11 when I got to bed.
