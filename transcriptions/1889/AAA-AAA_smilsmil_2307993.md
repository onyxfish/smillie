## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 2, 1889.

Dull, dark, & raw. Got to studio at 9.30 & to work on large drawing "The Passing Herd." Breaking up the line of foliage as it comes against the sky — I am sure it is greatly improved. — Home at 1.30 — back at 3 — Butter sent frame for my Etching "Reading Homer" — then I went to Kurtz with drawing of Passing Herd to have him photo-grave it — but he was not in — Home at 5 — dinner at 6.30 — at 8 Took Annie to Chickering Hall — last rehearsal before concert of the Choral Club — then to Century where I spent the evef. Reading & chatting with Shirlaw & Perry — went for Annie at 10.15 — home — to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 3, 1889.

Bright & very pleasant day — Got to studio before 10 — took my W.C. drawing "A Passing Herd" to Kurtz's to have him photo. it & make a plate for the W.C. Cat. Then to Bank — to studio before 11 — wrote a note to Mr. Sheldon of D. Appleton & Co, calling his attention to my W.C. painting at Kurtz's, hoping that he will like it well enough to have a photo-gravure made from it for the large work now publishing by D. A. & Co. — then to work on a small W.C. sketch — a bleating calf — "Where's my ma?."
Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — worked until 4.30 — a rest — then home. dinner at 6.45 — R[illegible] & Emily & Jno. Valentine dined with us — at 8 we went to Chickering Hall — to the private concert of the Choral Club in which Annie sings — A very pleasant concert — Home before 11 & right to bed.
