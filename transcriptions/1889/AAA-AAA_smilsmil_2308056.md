## Left Page

### May, Friday 10, 1889

An exceedingly hot & uncomfortable day with a thunderstorm soon after 5 p.m. Got to studio a few min. after 9 & at once to work preparing for a proof of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" which I pulled at 11 o'clk. — My work with the burnisher has been too timid — after proof — used scraper to reduce some parts — Home soon after lunch — At 2.30 went to W. Harthams in 57° S. to get some frames on mezzo-tint processes. To studio before 4 — worked with scraper & scotch stone until 5.30 — the storm made it too dark then for me to work. Cherl came in — Home soon after 6 — Annie came in with Jim, Ralph & Gretta, having been to Geo's to help celebrate Sheldon's birthday — dinner — Evef. Read for awhile — Jel. & Emily came in for awhile — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 11, 1889

Another very hot day — Ther. at 80° in the shade — Cloudy & close much of the m'g, with a light shower in the afternoon — Got up with a headache & fought through with it all day — To studio at 10 — worked all day on big plate with scotch stone, burnisher & dry point. Home at 1.30 — Annie & Jim had gone to Orange to Margaret Parker White's — To studio at 2.45 and worked until near 6 — then home to dinner — Eve'g — at 8 went to Artists View, Soct. of Am. Artists — A very strong & interesting exb'n — there until 9.30 — then home — wrote a letter to Herrick complaining because he had not answered mine of a week ago — then to bed.
