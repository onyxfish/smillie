## Left Page

### July, Monday 1, 1889

An overcast day with many little
nizzles & heavy rain from 5 to
7 P.M. At work out of doors until
nearly noon when at last I got to
work on my plate — after dinner
I worked very steadily making a
number of improvements & at last
got it ready for the proof that
I wish to send to Huntington &
Fate & Co. for approval. Edw
Cook left at 4 P.M. to go to Syracuse
on a short business visit.
After tea I finished my letter to
Kate & wrote one to Mary — also a
short one to Charl & before 8 went
down to P.O. Back before 9 — Annie
read aloud until after 10 from
Bryce.

A dull, wet day — showers & nizzles
with a little sunshine in the after-
noon — I got to work printing at
about 10.30 & before 5 o'clk. had
taken two India [illegible] on large paper
(20×30) & four [illegible] paper [illegible] to
send to W. Huntington & to Fate &
Co. Dinner at usual time —
washed up at 5 & after tea, tacked
my two large [illegible] on a board to stretch.
I still see some work to be done, but
not much. Could I put it aside
