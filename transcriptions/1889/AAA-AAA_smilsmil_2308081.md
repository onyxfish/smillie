## Left Page

### June, Saturday 29. 1889.

A very hot & humid day — bright
in the mt — hazy afternoon &
showers of rain from after 6 until
after 8 o'clk. Grass was cut on the
place & in the afternoon some that
was cut yesterday was put into
Herrick's Barn. After my chores
I got to my printing & took one
proof before dinner — after dinner
I worked awhile on the plate
there took two more p[roof]s — & at
5 o'clk. washed up. Then out &
picked some strawberries — Tea
at 6.30 — Mr & Mrs. Wm Parke called
after that — I went downtown before
8 & back before 9 — Read news-
paper & to bed soon after 10.

Edwd Cook arr'd from N.Y. at about
10.30 Am.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 30. 1889.

Another very hot day, altho' the Ther. did
not get above 80° — but, owing to humidity,
probably, that temperature was quite
oppressive. — A flying mist, almost fog, in
the early mt — a haze in the afternoon
with thunderstorms N.W. of us. — P[roof]s — at
a little after 8 — The mt spent loafing
about — picking peas, etc — wrote a letter
to Kate — dinner at 1.30 — As Selim
limped when taken out, decided not
to ride — so the afternoon was spent
as the mt was — most of the family
on the piazza with screens down,
reading — Tea after 6 — the eve'g
until near 10, spent on piazza
under the meagre light of a Chinese
lantern.
