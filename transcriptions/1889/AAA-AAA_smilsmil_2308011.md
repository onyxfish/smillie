## Left Page

### February, Saturday 9, 1889

Bright & mild — To studio at 10 —
Repainted much of sky on view
near New Haven — about 12 went
to U.A.f.D. Taking 4 plt. from my Dry
Point plates to W. Colbert-meer — then
down town to Park Place, & left the
plate at Ringler's establishment to
be steel faced — Home to lunch at
1:15 — to studio at 2.30 — Got out old
canvas of "A memory of the Connecticut
River" & remodeled it by some
very careless painting — Nellie came
in to tell me that at our visit on Sunday
last we gave Val. the Chicken Pox —
Home soon after 6 — dinner — a long
letter from Kate — After dinner, went
to grocer's — cor. 28th S. & 4th Ave — later,
Annie again read aloud from Robt.
Elsmere. About 11 when I got to
bed.

## Right Page

### February, Sunday 10, 1889

Bright, beautiful day — after 9 when
we got to blkp? I spent the m'f with
newspapers & children until near 12
when I went out with Jim for a
walk — up Mad. Ave to 52d S — left
him at Edw. Cook's soon after —
Annie brought him & Tom back to
dinner at 2. Afternoon divided
between the children & a mals-
dieu crosses than he old driver —
a cup of tea at 6.30 — Eve'f
wrote my letter to Kate, then A-
finished the Reading of Robt. Elsmere —
can't say whether or no I am any better
for it — certainly am no happier. It
is a gruesome book. Then wrote
notes to E. A. Caswell & J. D. Waring.
To bed before 11.
