## Left Page

### September, Friday 27. 1889.

Ther. went to 86° last night — A brilliant mf — breezy & cloudless —
but soon the clouds began to gather
& by the afternoon it was bleak & very
novembery. I kindled fire in Studio
furnace & got things comfortable
there — then wrote a long letter to
Koshler to accompany a [illegible] & dry-
point "Poppies" 6×6. and backed the
proof for Effs. Dinner at 1 — at 2.30
we went out for a drive bundled
up as if in mid-winter — as we started
Rev. W. Warriner came up. He rode
with us to his house — we were out
for a little more than an hour. At
5 I went to foundery to see about my
press — then to P.O. & got a letter from
Mary. Berlin — Sep. 16th. A quiet
evening reading —

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 28. 1889.

A couple of brilliant hours in the mf
after which the weather was cloudy
until about 3 or 4 o'clk. when again
the sky was bright & beautiful. I
spent much of the mf up in the
young trees that are standing up &
growing too tall, cutting out their tops.
After dinner I made a tracing of
my crayon drawing (11×17) of Poppies
& transferred it to an unsmoked ground
upon the plate — then, at about 4 o'clk,
Minnie & I walked over the hill to the
west, down to the brook — where I got a
slight pencil sketch — It was a very fine
subject — I wish I could get a careful study
of it — then walked home before 6 —
Eve'g — Rec'd letter from Charle saying
that he had sold Terrace He. for $7000 — bet.
8 & 9 o'clk. the Montrose Brass Band in
full uniform came up to our lawn and
persuaded us — they want money as they
are in debt — we contributed & went
to bed before 11 o'clk.
