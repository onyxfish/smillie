## Left Page

### October, Thursday 3, 1889

Ther. fell last night to 36°. Brilliant
m't. but before the middle of the m't
the clouds covered the sky — the wind
blew & another storm was threatened —
the afternoon was raw & unpleasant
with a very little rain between 5 & 6
o'clk. P.M. I spent some time with
Albert & Coyle picking up & cleaning
up the stones, bricks, timbers & what
not left about by the mason & wall buil-
ders — went to foundery & down-town
between 11 & 12. Afternoon tried to
get at my painting but duties &
calls from outside interfered. Eve'g
brilliantly cloudless moonlight, after
reading until 9 o'clk. I walked on
the Piazza for half an hour — To
bed at about 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 4, 1889

Ther. fell last night 42° — A day full
of beautiful effects of color, but at times
when clouds were densest rather cold &
windy — Burgess came after 9 A.M. &
commenced work laying the bit of new
piazza floor over the mich room — Drove
nearly all over with Albert, Coyle & the
horses. I went to the Warner woods by
the Sayre farm & worked until near
noon gathering a load of leaf mould —
For an hour before dinner read my
E.P. Roe book on the cultivation of
berries — After dinner gave some
time to Burgess & wrote to Ella Diven
& to Dr. Yale — Eve'g more reading
of Roe — another brilliant moon-
light night.
