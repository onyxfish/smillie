## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 30. 1889.

*Military Parade.*

Beautiful day — the early mf. slightly overcast & cool — sunshine & shadows during the rest of the day — Annie & I got to Wilsons (Earl & Wilson) overlooking Union Sq — at 10:15 & found then quite a little party — we got very good seats & waited, amusing ourselves watching the vast crowds before & below us — At about noon the head of column appeared after marching 20 m. — there was a halt — then we were served with a very nice lunch — at about 1 — the President's party were driven (from down town) past the waiting military to the Reviewing Stand at 24th St. & 5th ave. — The enthusiasm of the crowd was tremendous — Soon after the column marched — the military of the different States came in the order of the entrance of the States into the union & at their head rode the Gov. of each State — every one receiving an ovation — we watched it until near 4 — then being very tired, we left — Annie went home & I went out to Fifth ave. On a stand at Fifth Ave & 28th St. — I got a first rate position for 25¢ & for an hour watched the pictures up & down the ave. were remarkably fine — about 10000 veterans took up the rear — I watched on the cor. of 38th St until 6:15 & then went home to dinner without seeing the end. — Evely G. & Emily came in & took Annie away to see the illuminations while I stayed at home & finished a long letter to Mary — A. came in at about 10:30 — then I mailed my letter & got to bed at about 11.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 1. 1889.

*Civic Parade.*

Brilliant mf. — afternoon overcast, evef.; again clear. — To-day Annie gave Kate, Mary & Gretta permission to take a holiday while she remained at home to care for the house & Ralph. About 9:30 — Jim, Gretta & I walked down Fifth Ave. from 38th St. to 26th. — The sight of the crowd was very fine — we got to Wilsons at about 10 & at 10:45 the head of the procession came in sight — I watched it for two hours with little interest. It was very monotonous — then I left Gretta & took Jim home where I found Mary (née) Mrs. Oakey (her sister) & Miss Richardson — they soon left & Annie got lunch — then I took a nap & before 4 o'clk went to studio where I burnished on my plate until near 6. — home — dinner — evef. Reading.
