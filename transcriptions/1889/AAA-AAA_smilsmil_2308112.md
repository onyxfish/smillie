## Left Page

### August, Friday 30, 1889

A real August day — cloudless, breezeless & hot. I went down to the garden & spent some time there — there with Warner who finished his work in the kitchen and upon the studio's stair. I then got to my work upon crayon study of Poppies — could not do much — Dozing in a hammock for awhile before dinner at 1 — after that finished "Poppies" — At 4.30 locked up the house, the three girls having gone to a pic-nic, & went out with the family left Annie at Mr. Sam'. Sauries — left Jim at Parke's & with Miss Joshay & Ralph rode about until 6th when I took Annie at Mr. Wm. Muelford's & we drove around the lake, getting in at 7 — tea soon after — what remained of the evening was spent with newspaper & upon piazza, the evening being very warm & still.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 31, 1889

The warmest day yet — with a very pleasant N.W. breeze — I spent much of the m'f tying up blk. raspberry vines. Then some work in the studio. Albert's father came to see me a little before noon & insisted upon my getting into his buggy with him & driving out on the road where he could talk to me about Albert who wishes to go west. His father hopes to keep him east. Afternoon. I spent some time loafing in hammock — It was 4.30 before we went out riding — back at 6.30 — finding it exceedingly dusty on the traveled roads. Even with magazines & newspapers until 10.30 then to bed.

Markle came to the house while we were at bkfst. to say that I might have his big Ton Roller for $50 — I took it.
