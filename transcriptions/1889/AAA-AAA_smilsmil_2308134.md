## Left Page

### October, Sunday 13, 1889

A dull, drizzling, Scotch mist sort of day — up late in the mng. — what was the use of getting up early? Spent part of the mng in studio writing letters. Dinner at about 2 — then on piazza for some time with the boys — then more writing. Evng. chatting until 9 o'clk then I went downtown to P.O. with letters — more chat after my return, so that it was after 11, when we went to bed.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 14, 1889

Cold & bleak — some sunshine but not much. Annie & the T's managed to get a walk. The men did not work in the garden — I worked on my dry-point "Hollyhocks" and proved it as I worked, making considerable changes. I don't think much of it, but it is better now, I am sure, than I had hoped to make it. Dinner abt — about 2 men came from the foundery with my Standing press & soon got it into the studio room. I proved "Hollyhocks" until 5 o'clk — cleaned up & went downtown — Tea — Evng wrote to Mary — to Koehler. To P.O. abt 10. Tremendous high wind — after 11 when I got to bed.
