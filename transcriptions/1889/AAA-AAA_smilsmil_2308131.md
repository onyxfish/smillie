## Left Page

### October, Monday 7, 1889.

**Ther. last night: 35°**

Bleak, disagreeable day, with hardly a ray of sunshine — a flurry of snow flew for awhile at about 8 Am. Halpin — Williams & White came to work & dug ditch for stone gutter on road side up to barn — then H. & W. went to garden & commenced digging drains on East end — White stayed as helper to Hotchkiss who worked on the stone gutter — Such time as I could get for studio's work I put upon large dry-point plate for Poppies, cleaning press, through the ground, then transferred tracing — then I cleaned ground off — Went to foundery at 5.30 thru downtown — went to G. Fordham's but he was not at home. Evef.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 8, 1889.

**Ther. last night: 31°**

Very bleak — cold N.W. wind, cloudy — Afternoon the clouds broke away — clear and bright. Spent some time with the men — Hotchkiss at the stone gutter — Halpin Williams & White in the garden digging drains — After I got to studio I wrote a note to Koehler — then set to work upon a 4½ × 7" copper to make a new dry-point for him — more "brutal"; if I can, than the "Poppies" — Soon after 12 went to foundery — then to Gilbert Fordham's & got some instructions concerning the making of Asparagus beds — Home to dinner at 1 — worked on my 4½ × 7" "Hollyhocks" & then out with the men — At 5 went to foundery — my standing press has been put together and the castings painted — Home at 6.30 — Evef. read newspaper — wrote an order for plants to Little's Nurseries & I drafted letter to-day from Belle Tweedy saying that she & Mary may be expected on Thurs.
