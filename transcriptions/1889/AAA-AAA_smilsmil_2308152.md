## Left Page

### November, Monday 18, 1889

Overcast & mild — some spitting rain
in the afternoon & eve'f. Detained
at home after b'kf'st for a time by plumber — then my various m'f calls made
& after 10 when I got to studio. Then
I put Mr. Woodin's proof in frame — then
pasted tissue over blind for etching
table — then got out my work, the
6×6 plate of Poppies — dry-point. At
12.30 went to 2° Nat. Bk. — then to
Hunter's — the Optician — then to Avery's
gallery — & home to lunch at 1.30.
Studio at 2.45. Worked with scraper
& sharp point on Poppies — Yale came
in with sketch made years ago to traverse
my painting "Dark ages. Days golden Death."
— Champney also came in — At 6 went
to Charl's & got a great bunch of Chrysanthemums — then to dinner — Eve'g
Go. came in — after he left I wrote
part of a letter to Kate — To bed at
10.30 — at 2 A.M., Jim routed
us out by an attack of vomiting.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 19, 1889

Horrible day — wind & rain — It was
after 10 when I got to studio. Worked
on 6×6. Poppies until noon. Chambney
came in with B.P. of three of his photo-
gravures to make an exchange for
a (pl.) of "the Goldsmith's Daughter."
I had but one pl. to give, one damaged
in the taking by the paper crumbling,
but he went off much pleased with
that. Then I came down to Kimmell
& Voigt's & got some ink. (printers') It
was 1.45 where I got home to lunch
& & when I got back to studio,
I was so much out of sorts that I
could do nothing — home at 5.30 —
found Jim worse. If than I was —
While we were at dinner he had an
other violent attack of cascading —
at 8 o'clk. The Bosé girls came and
mine went with them to Choral
Club rehearsals. I stayed at home
& wrote letter to Kate — At 10 went
for A. & brought her & the B. girls home.
To bed just before 12.
