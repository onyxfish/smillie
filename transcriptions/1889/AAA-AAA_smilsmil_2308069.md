## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 5, 1889

Bleak & chilly — some sunshine in the
middle of the day & several showers
later — Kindled fire in big stove in
lower hall. Spent the m't in
etching room & studio where Mr. Cumy
was cleaning — also did some work
out of doors with the vines — went
down town & back at 12 — After
noon much of the time with Hemick
who winds up his work here for
the present. Got big roller for sod
from Tarbell's farm — Tea at 6.30
After that went down town — among
other places, to de Bcheverinty's — saw
Mr & Mrs. del — for a few minutes —
Home soon after 8 — eve't with news
paper — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 6, 1889

Cold, bleak m't. High N.W. wind all
day — by noon it was bright & pleasant
but decidedly cool. I spent all day
out of doors — trimming vines, planting
flower seeds & such work. Soon after
b't the piano hired for the summer
was brought up from the rink. I went
down town just before noon — Dinner
a't — the teams was used to haul
pea brush for the garden & raking
from the carriage road. Preparations
to riding's — so, we did not go
act riding but worked over the flower
beds instead — Tea at 6.30 — eve't
with news paper — & wrote a letter
to W. Danl. Huntington concerning
my plate after his painting and its
probable sale to Pate & Co.
To bed soon after 10.
