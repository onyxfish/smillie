## Left Page

### March, Saturday 23, 1889

Delightful day — Got to studio about
10 & worked until after 12, on "The
Goldsmith's Daughter" plate. — Then
to bank with home to lunch and
back before 3 — worked until
5 having several callers —
among them, Miss Hearn — &
Mr Hayward & his friend Mr. Thompson (I think.) Before & walked
up 5th Ave. & stopped at [illegible]
gallery to see a new lot of pictures to be sold next week —
Dinner at 6.30 —     Eve'g
some reading — Annie read
aloud a couple of short stories
To bed before 10.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 24, 1889

Still charming weather — clear
& very mild — B&fo: about 9. Jim
out of sorts — unable to eat — did
not get up until near noon — I
spent much of the m'f with the
baby — after 12 went to Charl's
lodging & found that he had Rs?
from Canada this m'f. Then rode
up to Central Park — Home at 2
& had dinner —   At near 4 I
went with Annie & Jim to the
foot of E. 34th St. & crossed the
ferry & back — then home —   At
[illegible] had a cup of tea — Eve'g
wrote most of my letter to Nat-
Charl came in before 9 and
stayed 15 or 20 minutes — To bed
before 11.
