## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 23, 1889

**Ther. 24° last night**

Clear, cold day — about an inch of
snow fell last night & did not thaw
during the day except where the sun
shine fell upon it. Rafferty got the
beech down before 10 A.m. The rest of the day
cutting it up & degging — I worked until
1 o'clk. Proving Hollyhocks & then finished.
Went down town about 3 o'clk. Saw
W. Alex Smith concerning the Dozier sale
(Sheriffs) 3 weeks from next Friday — Went
in to Doolittle's — Got mail — messages
etc. — Home before 5 — Got letter from
Wm. Pate telling me that the Goldsmith's
Daughter was a success & that they would
issue only Remarque pfs. & then destroy
the plate — Eve'g. wrote to Pate &
to Mr. Woodien — Took letters to P.O.
at 9.30 — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 24, 1889

**Ther. 23° last night**

Brilliant, dry, cold day — delightful
weather to work out of doors — hate [illegible],
a warm bed in the m'g is so seductive.
After that, out of doors until dinner
at 1 o'clk. Sawing the tops out of the
young trees that are growing so tall
as to obstruct our views to South & S.W.
After dinner, out of doors again —
trimming vines & superintending
Rafferty, Coyle & Albert. W. Hunter
of Montrose brought me a couple
of geese this m'g to paint from &
Albert made a pen for them. Eve'g
after reading newspaper I spent
an hour in dark-room developing
some photos that I took to-day — &
then I dampened & put into press
between boards some pfs. of "Holly-
hocks" dry-point to send to W. Corke.
To bed after 10.
