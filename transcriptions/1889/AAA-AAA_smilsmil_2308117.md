## Left Page

### September, Monday 9, 1889

Heavy fog in the m'f, clearing very pleasantly — I had trouble again last night, or rather, at 4 o'clk this m'f. I am discourag'd at the persistency of the ailing — The men got to work at the wall lay'n this m'f. & I spent some time in overseeing them — I rec'd a very pleasant letter from Kate at Eisnach this m'f., I sent it to Charl with a note. I rec'd from Sharp & Sons a copper plates for my big dry-point of Poppies — At 3.30 went out driving taking Miss Joshay, Sophie & Mary [illegible] & Ralph. Saw Casey about stone for the milk room floor & ceiling. & before 6 he came to the house — a load of brick was brought up in the m'f. Ret'd from ride before 6 — tea at 6.30. After reading awhile — more "stop" — nearly 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 10, 1889

Overcast all day — in the afternoon at times the weather was threatening but no rain came & the sun set very red. About 11 a.m. I went to the foundery & saw W. Beach about having a "standing press" made. He took much of my time talking about a proposed R.R. connection between this town & some point on the D.L.&W. R.R. Saw Peter Coyle who has been sick for some days — & then Lozier — Dinner abt — Lozier worked all the afternoon setting strawberry plants (88) in cold frame — at 4, with Miss J., Mary J., Annie & Ralph, I drove over to Casey & saw Casey about stone for milk room floor. Ev'g, we all went to The Opera Ho. & saw a series of Tableaux from Bern Htur given for the benefit of [illegible] Ch—. The show pleased some folks — Home at 10.30 — to bed at 11.
