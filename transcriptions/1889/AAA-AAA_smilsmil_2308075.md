## Left Page

### June, Monday 17, 1889.

Heavy rain until afternoon —
About 5 o'clk it cleared charmingly.
I had a long day of steady work
upon my place & at about 6 go
went down town to attend to various
messages — Went to Herrick's house
but found no one at home. — Home
at 6.30 — Tea — Ralph quite out
of sorts, probably from cutting a tooth
not finding the remedies wanted
I went down town again — went
to Dr. Richardson & consulted with
him — Home at about 8 — wrote
letter to Kate & before 10 took
it down to P.O.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 18, 1889.

Clouded w.f. — clearing imperfectly.
Strong east wind all day — After [illegible]
man from Boyd & Cooley came up to see
Studio cistern — determined that further
patching would be of no use — so, I ran
the water out & will have a new lining
put in. So much time spent in the
garden & with the lawn mower (horse)
that I did not get to my work until
11 — then worked until I had dinner
about 2. again at my work until 4
then out to ride — Took Ralph who
is still very evidently out of sorts — we
called at de Schweinitz & took Mrs. de
S. & her daughter out with us — back
at 6.15 Tea — Sue & Annie read
aloud for awhile — Ralph awoke
at about 10 o'clk. fevered & fretful.
an injection was given but no result
followed until 1 o'clk. when there was a
movement of the bowels & vomiting — Annie
& I had been up with him until that time.
Then I went to bed & A. remained with him
during the rest of the night — Then James
awoke & I was in trouble with him until
the Robins began their morning song after
which I got a little sleep.
