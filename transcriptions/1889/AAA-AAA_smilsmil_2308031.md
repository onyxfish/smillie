## Left Page

### March, Thursday 21, 1889

An exceedingly disagreeable day —
Rain, snow, sleet & slush all day
long — I got to studio at about 10 &
worked all day on two W. C. drawings —
"Oct. Study" for Boston Art Club exhn &
"Where's my ma?" for Chicago Art
Ins. exhn. much time spent in
cleaning up frames & glass and
getting them properly passed in.
Home at 1.30 — back before 3 — and
home to dinner at 6.30 — after
that, dressed — carriage came at
7.40 — Annie & I rode to 331 E. 17th
& got Emma I — then to Chickering
Hall — Choral Club concert — Emma
& I sat in gallery — with Mitchell's in front
of us — Chambereys behind us & &c. and
Emily Cook alongside — the concert was
a very enjoyable one. — out at 10.45 —
— little trouble in getting carriage —
Took Emma home — then home at
about 11.20 — & to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 22, 1889

A delightful day, cloudless, bright
& crisp — Got up with a headache
that unfitted me for work or pleasure
during the day — Got to studio soon
after 10 & was able to do some work
on Huntington plate — At 12 went
to Iffp. office with hardware for
Herrick — then to Bank & deposited
Etching club check & drew out a small
amt. Home at 1 — lunch — then took
a short nap & got to studio a little
after 3 — worked on plate for a couple
of hours — home at 6 — Event of
Barnum's Parade — I wrote letter
to Herrick —
