## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 22, 1889.

Beautiful day — bright & cool —
to studio soon after 9 — got things
into shape to receive my expected
callers — D. Stimson — to select a picture
as payment for his bill — Mr. Hunting-
ton & W. Sanckuer — but none of
them came — Chas. Platt called with
p/p of two plates recently etched —
to ask if I thought them up to his
average — He is not sure — he has
delivered the plates to Klackner
but if I advise it will recall and
destroy them — I think they are clever
etchings, but not his best work — & so
I told him — Home to lunch at 1.30 —
Annie & Jim had gone to East Orange
to call on Mrs. Ernest Wright —
To studio at 2.30 — writing notes &
getting things ready preparatory
to packing up to go away. Mr.
Huntington came in at about 5
& having another engagement was
obliged to make a hurried call.
He expressed himself as very much
pleased with my place & had very
few suggestions to make — Home
soon after 6 — dinner — spent
some writing of notes & magazine
reading.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 23, 1889.

Bright, breezy & cool. To studio soon
after 9 — set palette & painted most of
the m/p on old painting of cattle "Set.
Afternoon — with Cows". Home to lunch
at 1.30 — After that the children were
dressed & a little before 3 Annie, Jim,
Ralph, Petra & I went to Thomas, the
photographer, & had some more pictures
taken — then we left the children with
J. & went to Starr's to look at Silver Mugs —
then to McGibbens's & bought some remnants
of stuffs for Montrose — then to Gorham — the
Whiting man & to Tiffany's to look at
more mugs — Selected one at the Whiting —
to Lord & Taylor's & bought some light flan-
nel shirts — then to 14th St. & 3d Ave. & by E.R.R.
up to 76th S. — then to Louis Tiffany's — 72nd S.
& Mad. Ave. to see a new Yale memorial
window — a very brilliant affair — many
people there — Home at 6.30 — dinner —
at 7.45 went to F.H. Smith's — engaged — did
not see him — home — at 8.30 went with
Annie to call on Dr. & Mrs. Yale — both out —
then to George & Finley's & spent the even.
until 10 with them. Then home and
to bed.
