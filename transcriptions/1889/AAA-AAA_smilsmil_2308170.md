## Left Page

### December, Thursday 26. 1889.

A dull, wet wtf. with quite a heavy thunder shower about noon after which it cleared immediately very niued at first but growing windy & rapidly colder. Got to studio at 10 — spent wtf. fussing over plt. taken on Tues. And went to Saml. Colman's & got a jap. jug to put in my big dry-point Poppy plate. Home at 1.45 — back to studio at 3 — worked on Poppy drawing as long as I could see. Then put india pl. of Hollyhocks in frame & took it & plate down to Klafeld. It was after 6 when I got home — Katie, Mollie, Frank & Roger Adams there — dinner — at 8 took Annie to her club — I went to Century — wrote a note to W. H. R. Bishop. Chatted with Chas. Collins until 10.20 — rushed for A. & found her sitting with Mrs. Mitchell every one else having gone — the M's having stayed to keep her company — home & to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 27. 1889.

Fine clear winter day — got to studio at 10 & to work on large crayon drawing of Poppies for dry-point — worked over the back ground especially — toning & quieting & trying the effect of a jap. pattern — Mr. Alex. Roux came in about 12.30 — small frame came, into which I put small dry-point, Poppies, for Park's friend — home at 1st lunch — back at 2.30 — worked almost as long as I could see on crayon drawing — then made starch & mounted plt. taken from Mezzotint plate before any work was put upon it — then cut a number of dry-point plts. into press. At 5.30 went to Daly's theatre but found the box office closed — then home. Annie, Jim, Ralph & Gretta not yet home from Mrs. Villard's Xmas tree — they came in before 7 — we sat down to dinner at 6.30 — Wtf. Geo. Smilley & Mrs. Valentine came in — I spent most of the evng. writing to Merrick & A. B. Smith about possible purchase of the Lafier property — mailed letters at 10.30 — to bed about 11.
