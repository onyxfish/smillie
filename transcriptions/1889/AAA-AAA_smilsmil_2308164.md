## Left Page

### December, Thursday 12, 1889

Bright, beautiful day — but there was nothing bright or beautiful in me — Having taken 4 gr of Quinine last night & 6 more this m'f my head was like a hive of bees. It was a day of misery — Yale came in in the morning about noon — I lounged about all day — much of the time on my bed — Sam. Coleman came in for a little while about dusk. Annie went to Studio & got my mail — I wrote to Pate & Co. — Wm. Berriau & Ripley Hitchcock. Berriau sent me a pocket check for $25 — finally settling in my favor an old claim for reimbursement. To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 13, 1889

A very pleasant day out of doors — but another misery for me — Soon after I awoke I was conscious that the enemy had effected a change of base — all day I sat with my eyes & nose running while my head ached dismally — a regular Catharral influenza. Dr. Yale came in before 12 o'clk. & made a change in my medicine, but there was no change in my condition all day. Alice Ireland came in about 4.30 p.m. & sat with me for a little while — Between 8 & 9 in the ev'g Geo came in for a short time — To bed before 11.
