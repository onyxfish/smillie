## Left Page

### June, Sunday 9, 1889

Heavy rain in the early m'g —
clearing to be an exceedingly muggy
day. Ther 78°. Albert McCoy went
home to visit his parents — his chores
being attended to by Conkling, his
uncle. I had a time with the horses
having let them out in the pasture
together. I got them in as soon as I
coud & considered myself fortunate
that no harm had been done. The
day was so enervating that I coud not
do the work that I had laid out for
myself to do. Dinner at 2. In the
afternoon I wrote a letter to Mary —
Tea about 7 — The eve'g was delicious.
air cool & soft & fragrant — Night —
moonlight etc — etc — I wrote a
note to Charl after walking on
the piazza with Annie through
the gloamin — I obed soon after
10.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 10, 1889

Wet m'g. but cleared before noon.
very hot afternoon with pleasant breeze.
After being about with the men (con-
stantine started digging back of the barn)
I got at my work in the studio and
fixed my table etc — then went down
town at 11 & back soon after 12 — Dinner
at — after that, worked on my plate —
"The Goldsmith's Daughter" until near
4 — then out riding until almost 6 —
Tea before 7 — Pretea having gone to
visit her family, a younger sister took
her place & there was some trouble
with Ralph — About 8 W. & Mrs. de
Schevenintz called — after they left I
finished my letter to Mary & after 10
took it to P.O. Glorious moonlight —
To bed at a little before 11.
