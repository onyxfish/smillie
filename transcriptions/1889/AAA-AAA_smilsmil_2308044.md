## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 16. 1889.

Pleasant wf. Busied myself about the house until near 10 — then to studio — then to bank — then by elevated R.R. went down town — to Charles' — waited there until 11.45 before Mr. Garner of Shearman & Stirling was ready for us — then executed the deed of sale to de Graaf of the Terrace Property — then to Kimmel & Voigt's — got a Dry Point Poppy plate — then to studio — near 2 o'clk when I got home — after lunch went to Washington Wilson's to see if there was any chance for me to get seats at his windows to see the procession on Ap. 30 — pleasant chat with him — but small chance of getting the coveted seats — To studio about 4. Every Chard came in but I managed to finish a letter to Mary & mailed it before 11 o'clk — then to bid.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 17. 1889.

Rain in the wf — with some showers during the day — dry at night — Got up with a headache — went to studio & spent the wf trying to clean up some of the dirt — Annie came in at about 1 & we walked up home to lunch — after lunch my headache had so increased that I went to bed & stayed there until near 6 — then I got up feeling not much better — dinner — then I dressed & at 8.30 Annie & I went to the Met. Opera House, to the formal opening of the collection of portraits, relics, etc., etc., connected with Washington & his times — it was very interesting & there was a great crowd — Ex pres't. Cleveland, wife & mother-in-law were much gazed at as they made the rounds of the rooms under charge of H.G. Marquand, W.E. Dodge — Mr. Felder & others — we got home at about 11.
