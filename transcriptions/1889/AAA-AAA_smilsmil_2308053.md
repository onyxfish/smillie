## Left Page

### May, Saturday 4, 1889

*went to Ridgefield with Geo. & Nellie —*

Brilliant & beautiful mtf. Had an early bkfst — & at 8 o'clk. met Geo. & Nellie at the New Haven depot & went to Ridgefield to look at some places there that are offered for sale. We got there at about 10 — got at once got a horse & 2 seat wagon & we drove to a place a short distance south. It proved to be very rocky — 26 acres — with a poor little house — after going over the place we drove to other places & about town generally until about 1 o'clk. when we gave up the horse and went to Mrs. Geo. Smith's & had dinner — By this time I was pretty well used up with a headache — We dined with the New Episcopal clergyman & his wife — After dinner we sauntered out & walked through the street, Geo looking at some places with a view to getting some summer board — We met Mr. P. Lounsbury and had a talk with him — took 4 o'clk train — by this time I was completely used up & had a wretched time getting home — got in at 6 o'clk — in a few minutes was at home and went right to bed —

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 5, 1889

Beautiful day & very warm — Got up feeling pretty seedy after a hard night but as the day went on felt better — At 11.30 Annie, Jim & I took Boulevard cars — Rode up to 104th St. then walked to Riverside Park where we sauntered for some time when we met Ed.wo., Mary & their five children — we all went on up to Grant's Tomb and then across to the Boulevard cars & home at 2 os o'clk — dinner — After 3 o'clk. Chare came in with a lot of photos taken during his trip west last fall — After 5 I went down to the Century Club for a few minutes — on my way up stopped in to Panorama of the Battle of Gettysburg — stopped at Studio — home at 6.30 — a cup of tea — eve'g read newspaper & wrote a letter to Herrick —
