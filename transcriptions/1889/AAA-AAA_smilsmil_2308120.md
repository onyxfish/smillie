## Left Page

### September, Sunday 15, 1889

An April-like day with clouds & threatening showers — but none of the showers fell upon us until bet. 8 & 9 P.M. I spent the day out of doors, mostly looking after the trees, until dinner time at 1.30 — At 3 o'clk Miss Foshay, Mary J— Annie, Ralph, Jim & I drove over to So. Pond, getting home at 6 — Tea before 7 — Eve'g I wrote a note to Genl. Diven to accompany to photos of his son Geo's children that I found a few days ago. For a little while read Motley's letters — then to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 16, 1889

A cloudy, breezy, muggy day, growing very gloomy & threatening in the afternoon — but no rain. I spent the m'g about the place arranging for transplanting shrubs that I have & ordering new ones. Went down town before noon. Dinner at 12.15 — as Miss Foshay was to leave us at 1 o'clk. Albert drove over to New Milford taking her & Sophie & Mary Ireland & Jim — Miss F. took the 2.36 train west for Dansville — Albert ret'd. with the others at 5. I spent the P.M. with saw among the trees, giving most time to the grafted apple trees. Tea at 6.30. Eve'g I wrote a note to Charl & a letter to Mary, from whom, thro' Charl, I had rec'd a letter in the m'g — To P.O. at about 10 o'clk — then to bed.
