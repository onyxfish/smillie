## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 13, 1889

Up at about 6 o'clk. with cataclysm
of the bowels — Trouble continued till
after 10 o'clk — then the quiet of pros-
tration — Annie wrote a note to
Dr. Geo. asking him to attend to
my Academy Pictures for me as
to-day is the last day. I kept
very quiet until after 3 P.M. when
I got up & dressed & the rest of
the afternoon on nursery lounge —
St. Hall came in having been
to see Carleton Cook who, by tumbling
into a area, has broken one of the bones
of one of his arms. Eve'g I felt
very uncomfortably weak — Annie
read aloud some of Cable's Stories —
to bed about 10.

to day has been a lovely day —

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 14, 1889

A very pleasant day — feel much
better — got up to bkf's — but did not
go out until after 11 when I rode
down to studio — walked home before
1 — In the afternoon I went to the
barber's & had my hair cut & head
shampooed — Annie called at the
Victoria & saw Mrs. Juell. Ehninger.
At dinner time Edith Winship
came in but went immediately
to Walter's — Charl came in to tell
us that he intends to start for Montreal
to-morrow in the interest of Uncle Willie
— also that he has completed the ex-
change of Po'k Terrace tt. for one in
Devington Ave. upon what seems to
be advantageous terms — Eve'g A—
