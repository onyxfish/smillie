## Left Page

### October, Saturday 5, 1889

Another most trying day — some
sunshine — some exquisite color — &
much heavy cloud shadows & raw wind.
my day was frittered away looking after
workmen — Burgess worked all day on
piazza extension — Carey & his son came
with large stone — they worked at getting
it into place — drilling holes, etc. until
after 6 o'clk. About noon Annie & I
went down town & then home by way
of the foundery — While at dinner we
enjoyed the glorious sunshine & color —
before we had finished the sky was over-
cast. I sent Albert & Coyle out to a
place on the roadside where they got
a couple of loads of good dirt & I had it
put up on a bit of the bank of our drive
& had it seeded. I rec. from Pate
& Co. a copy of Goldsmith's Daughter —
ordered for Cabbe — I wrote to P. & Co.
About 5 went to foundery & then
down town — Home to tea after 6 —
Even'g, quietly reading.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 6, 1889

Bright pleasant m'f., mild and
breezy — clouds & sunshine alternative
— about the middle of the day glorious
light & color over the hills as seen from
our windows — at 3 o'clk. heavy, leaden
clouds with cold N. W. wind & a spatter
of rain after 5 o'clk. I spent the
m'f. out of doors, going about the place
— Dinner at 2 — After that, Annie,
Jim & I walked out to Tarbell's hill
& around it on the N. & W. side — It
was hardly a comfortable walk, the
wind blew so hard & so cold — back
about 5 — Even'g some reading —
wrote a supplemental note to
Pate & Co. & a short letter to Charl.
Took them to P. O. before 10 — Then
home & to bed.
