## Left Page

### May, Friday 24, 1889.

A charming day — a specimen may day — Annie spent the day at Mr. H+ Villard's with Mr. Prof. Wood.
I got to studio soon after 9 — worked on some plan drawings for Herrick +
attended to other small matters —
Mr. Wm. Pate came in at about 12.30
by invitation to see my plate after
Huntington "The Goldsmith's Daughter"
He was pleased + interested + after
some conversation expressed his
willingness to give $500 cash for the
plate — If the plate proves profitable
from the profits he will pay me an
additional $5000 — The bargain is
not complete until he comes next
Tues. with his son, when they are to
give a final answer — I then went
to 2d Nat. Bnk. + home to lunch at
2 o'clk — After that, went down town +
had another talk with Walter Cook about
proposed changes in hall of 38th St. house.
Then to A.B.N.Co — Saw Freeland — then saw
John Doz + gave orders for a 12×20 copper
plate with mezzotint ground — Then
up town — to Brooks Bros + ordered a suit
of summer clothes — Home at 6.30 —
Dinner — wrote a letter to Herrick.
Nearly went with Annie to Ed'd Cook's
where P. Fale + wife — Mr. Johnston —
\+ Walter had dined — we chatted
until about 10.30 — then home and
to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 25, 1889.

Another delightful day, bright + breezy —
Called on Charl at his room at 9 o'clk —
To studio about 7 — Finished some
drawings of plans for Herrick +
then began the work of hauling
things down for the summer — Home
soon after — lunch — back at 2.30
\+ continued the work of putting
things away + packing until 4 —
Then to Keppel's with my last Dry-
point bls. — then to Bridgeman's +
ordered 2 dorgo tomato plants sent
to Montrose. To studio before 5 —
Found Charl in Geo's studio prepar-
ing for some photographic work to-
morrow — we left soon after 6 — home
— dinner — Eve'g — lounging, with
news papers — To bed soon after 10.
