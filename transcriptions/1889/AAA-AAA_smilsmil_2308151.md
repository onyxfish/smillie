## Left Page

### November, Saturday 16. 1889

Clear, but quite wintry. After my usual domestic duties and shoppings I went to Butler's &
collected a moulding & ordered a frame for "The Goldsmith's Daughter"
then spent some time getting a proof mounted for Mr. Woodin — At
2. I went to Knoedler's & looked at pictures there. — Then to 9 10, 17 5t. and
after waiting for some time saw W. Rob't. Goslet about the $25 that I have
been dunning Denman for for two years.
Was pleasantly rec'd — but got nothing definit.
Home at 1.30 — lunch — to studio at 2.30
Spent more time getting Mr. Woodin's pic. into frame — more time. Spent with
Dr. thus, Yale, who called & Charl. —
Home at 6 — Evat., Annie & I
went to Y. Phil. Concerts at Uck. Opera
House. A having subscribed for the
season, & enjoyed an inspiriting
concert. Our seats are fairly
good. 21 & 23. 1. Parquette.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 17. 1889

A mild & very pleasant day —
Bkft. at about 9 — after that, sat
for awhile with newspaper — after
that Annie, Jim & I went out for
a walk & went over to the bluff
at the foot of 50th St. overlooking the
East River — then home. Dr. Yale
came in soon after — & before he
left hand, Col. Moran & wife came in
& made a short visit, all leaving
together — Dinner at 2 — About
3 Charl. came for me & I went
with him to his room to see
some new rugs & curtains — then
with him to Union League Club
to see the Rembrandt (Dr. Tulp) and
some other old pictures. Had very
pleasant chat with La Farge, Shirlaw
& Beckwith — nearly when I got home.
Eve'g, wrote a letter to Herrick —
posted it at 10.45 — then to bed.
