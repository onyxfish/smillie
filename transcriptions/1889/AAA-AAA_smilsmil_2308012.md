## Left Page

### February, Monday 11. 1889.

Dull day with snow flying until
late afternoon when it became
quite a snow storm — by 9.30 p.m.
the moon was shining — To studio
at 10 + to work repainting "A
Memory of the Connecticut" — a
pot-boiler painted last spring. I
worried over the sky, trying to get
clear, pure greys with plenty of color
in them — direct + sunmussed in
touch. Home at 1. Annie not at
home to lunch — with Eliz. Home — back
to studio at 2.30 + painted until 4.30,
then putting some etching pf's into
frames — took one over to Mr. Greer at Etch-
ing Club Sat'n. Home at 6.30 — dinner —
Eve'g. Reading — at 9.30 left at
Charl's lodgings Kate's letter + maps
of Dresden — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 12. 1889.

Bright wintery day, with a snow
flurry or two in the afternoon — To
studio before 10 + painted on old
painting "a memory of the Connecticut" —
Before 1 went to 2°. Nat. But. for Annie —
on my way home stopped in Mad. Ave
to see Eliz — Home — not in — again to studio
at 2.30 — finished 2°. painting ("Memory
etc") — went to N.A. p.d. met Wm H. Gibson
the Artist, he praised my Dry Point —
"Poppies" very highly — left a framed pf.
of "Reading Homer" at Charl's lodging —
home to dinner at 6.30 — Eliz — Home —
there — then to Gericke's concert (Boston
Symphony Orchestra) at Steinway Hall —
met Fanny K. by appointment at the
door — Will K. + his cousin, Mrs. Curtis, also
there — they sat just in front of us. Annie
gave tickets to Eliz, H. & Fanny K. — The concert
was very enjoyable — After it, walked
up home, leaving Eliz. at her lodging
in Mad. Ave. To bed at about 11.
While at lunch, Rossell called + took
my "Cliffs of Normandy" to Union League Club.
This was a mistake as the club do not
want it.
