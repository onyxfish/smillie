## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 30, 1889

Bright & milder, altho' somewhat
windy — got to studio at 10.20
& to work upon "In the Huddle
woods — Lake George". Painted
upon it all day. Removing much
heaviness — clearing up shadows
— etc — etc. In the ev'g a young
man called saying that he wanted
to buy a Water Color — I showed
him what I had. — He professed to
like two of the drawings & promised
to return with his bro. at 4 p.m —
but — I mourn his absence. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — and
worked until after 4 — then, feeling
very tired, I rested — at 5 went
to O'Neil's & ordered another [that?] copy
of his father Oak portrait. Home at 6
— dinner — Eve'g, some Rob. Elsmere
& to bed about the usual time.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 31, 1889

A very mild hazy day with
light rain at night. To studio at
10 & tackled my big Painting —
"the cliffs of Normandy". Ever
since it was first exhibited at the
N.A. of D. I have determined to
repaint the foreground. At last I
have got started at it & am much
pleased to find that I can carry
it further & make it better. I
painted upon it all day getting more
than half of the lower left hand corner
foreground painted over. Dinner at the
usual time — at 8, with Annie rode down
to 14th S. in 5th Ave. bus. Left A. at her club
rehearsal — I went to Century — there until
9 — then started for Wier & Twatchman show
found it raining — went home for umbrella —
then to 6th [?] — view — Ortgies Gallery — 5th Ave — 35th S. —
Saw a strange lot of "impressions" very few
of which were agreeable or understandable
to me — Walked to 28th S. with [?ee?] then
on to 14th S. for A. Rehearsals continued until
near 11 — then had to walk up home —
it was near 12 when we got to bed.
