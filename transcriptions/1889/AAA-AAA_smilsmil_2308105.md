## Left Page

### August, Friday 16, 1889

Beautiful, bright m'f — cloudy p.
m. with some showers in the S. W.
that came threateningly near. After
my m'f duties about the place, I
gathered some poppies & arranged
them, then commenced, direct
from the flowers, upon a 6"×6" plate,
a dry-point study of them, intending
it for the National Museum at Washington, com. by S. R. Koehler. Dinner at 1 —
Had intended starting with the Parkes
at 2 for Dr. Norris's at Fire Hill, but the
threatened shower detained us until
3 45. I worked on my dry-point until
that time, then we went off on a shorter
ride, getting in at about 6.30 — Tea —
Eve'g Annie & I went downtown
& called on Priscilla Poore & Emma
Smith at the old Geo. Beesley house —
there for an hour — then home — Read
newspaper & chatted until after
10 — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 17, 1889

A brilliant m'f, soon clouding over —
the afternoon overcast — sunset, leaden.
After bkfs. spent an hour in the garden
then to studio & repainted the white
hollyhocks in the study of Mrs. last —
then on 6×6 dry point "Poppies" which
I got all "in" by dinner time at 1 o'clk.
At 2 45 we all started for Fire Hill —
Had a very pleasant drive over — back
by the Ridge road getting to town at
6 o'clk. Met Dr. Norris & wife & had a
few moments conversation with him —
Tea before 7 — for some time reading
newspaper — then cleaned palette
etc. — To bed before 11.
