## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 3. 1889.

Overcast m'f. with mizzling rain at times — cleared before noon — delightful afternoon — very hot &
humid — strong south wind — I spent part of the m'f among the pea vines — then studied over proof of my big plate & made some changes on a proof & notes for more work — Dinner at 1 — Afternoon at 3.30 Annie, Mary & I with Ralph — Tom & Louise went out riding — had a long & very pleasant one — home at 6·15. We had been in but a very few minutes when a sudden & very heavy shower passed over — Ev'g I wrote to Huntington — Pate & Co — J.D. Gee — 2°. Nat. But— & others — Before 10 went down to P.O. with them — then home & to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 4. 1889.

Heavy rain in the m'f at about 8.30 — wind from W. & N.W. — cleared before noon — afternoon clear & cool. Spent the m'f out of doors — among the pea vines & weeding flower beds until dinner at 1 — After that we went out with the children & fire crackers & managed in the course of an hour to blow up ten packs of fire-crackers — then I went to the lower garden & worked among carrots — etc — etc until near tea time — Ev'f. very cool — Annie, Mary & Ed[?] (who ret' from Syracuse this m'f) chatted domestic & family matters.     Superb moonlight night
