## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 11, 1889

Still overcast & threatening, with wind from N.E. I spent most of my energies through the day over the men who finished laying the stone wall soon after 11 — & later with Crowley, the mason, who came to make up mortar bed — lime, sand, boards etc were got & I engaged Halpin as helper. It was almost when I started to drive with Annie, Sophie J. & Miss Joshay — we went to the brick yard first & saw Loomis & engaged 3000 bricks of him — then over Brewster's hill & back to So. Montrose stopping at Casey's many — saw him — home at 6 — Evef. after reading newspaper, played "Stop" until 10 — then a walk on Piazza — beautiful moonlight — & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 12, 1889

The mf opened sunny & pleasant but rain from the N.E. commenced before 9 o'clk. I was up at 7.30 to superintend the unloading of the stones for floor of milk room, brought by B. Casey — After bfst. I bossed Lozier at the Strawberry bed — the darkie who unloaded bricks, Halpin the helper & Crowley the mason. At 11 I went to Coyle's & saw his wife — then to the foundery & saw Beach about standing press — then downtown to several stores & home at 12 — Dinner at 1 — after that it rained so hard & constantly that the men gave up work. I fussed about in the studio but accomplished nothing, my head troubling me. Evef. read newspaper until 9 o'clk — then "Stop" until 10.30 — to bed at 11.

& went downtown in heavy rain at 6 o'clk.
