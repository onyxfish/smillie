## Left Page

### March, Friday 15, 1889

Cold, windy, raw — overcast —
It was after 11 when I got to
studio feeling rather shaky. I
did some work on drawing plans
for Montrose Studio's closet — Home
after lunch — then gave my-
self up to the children — finished
reading libretto of "Rhinegold" and
then took a nap. Helen Butler
sent to Annie two excellent seats
for the Opera to-night, so, A — went
out & exchanged her Balcony seats
for others for next Wed. evef.
Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. A. & I
went to Met. Opera Ho. & saw and
heard "Rhinegold" — over at 10.30 —
the Opera was disappointing to me.
To my untutored ear it lacked interest
— but passages were very beautiful
& parts of the spectacle were fine —
As we passed out we had a good look
at Grover Cleveland & wife — he looks to
me like an old H.O.G. — She looked as
though it disagreed with her to live
with him — Home & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 16, 1889

Milder, but still overcast & threat-
ening. Got to studio at 10.15 and
to work upon working plans for
studio closet at Montrose. worked
until near 12 — then to Kurtz's. 6
S. 23d. St. Got "positive" of "Passing Herd" &
saw negative of "Spring is Coming". Take —
for J.W. Sheldon — then to Bank — drew
some money for A — then up 5th Ave. stopping
at Ortgies gallery & saw the Erwin Davis
collection of pictures — they made me very
unhappy — it is fashionable to praise them
but in most of them I can see nothing — I
could not live happily in their presence.
Home to lunch at 1.40 — to Studio at 3.
Jat. H. Morse & two ladies called. Worked
until 6 on Montrose plans — then
home — Eve'g. Rain — loafed.
To bed at about 10.
