## Left Page

### April, Saturday 20. 1889.

Pleasant day — not so warm as yesterday —
thunder shower about 8.45 p.m. —
to studio at about 10 & worked for
awhile on the Atlantic City Sketch —
Disappointed at finding that Champney
& Geo don't think much of it — was
surprised by a visit from Col. James
Fairman — then Geo came in to tell
me of his visit to Ridgefield yesterday
& it was time for me to go home to
lunch. Back to studio at 2.30 — Painted
on "A Memory of the Connecticut River" —
Chas came in at about 5 — He, Geo.
& I walked up at 6 o'clk — Home —
dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. Read
news papers until near 9. Then
Prof. Compton & wife called — They
left at 10.30 & we got to bed soon
after 11.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 21. 1889.

A bright & very warm day — I was
kept busy all day after bkfst — fixing
shades & other things — caring for the
children & writing letters — after 11 I
walked down to the studio — then to
the Century. There wrote a note to D.
Smyber Smith — Com. of Public Works,
asking for 3 passes to the top of 42d St.
Reservoir to see the parades of Ap. 30th &
May 1st — Home before 2 — then dinner —
besides Nellie & Dr., Geo. & Smiley dined
with us — Later in the afternoon Annie
& I took Jim & Ralph to the Children's
Easter Festival at Rob. Collyer's church.
Ralph had a royal good time, walking
all the way there & back — I wrote
a long letter of instruction to Herrick —
Eve'g. Geo. & Nellie called — It
was 11 when we got to bed.
