## Left Page

### February, Thursday 21, 1889

Delightful m'f. In p.m. overcast.
It was about 9.30 when I got to
the studio, feeling headachy & unlike
work. Much of the m'f was taken
by twice going to N.A.of D., Etching
Club exh'n to compare Dry Point
pf's with the pf — only 4 or 5 there —
About noon S. R. Koehler came in
& made a visit. At 12.45 went to
2° Nat. Bk. & Safe Deposit Co. &
got Annie's Erie Bonds — Home to
lunch at 1.30 — to studio before 3
— painted awhile on "Spring is
Coming" — Home at 6.5. Miss
Kate Hillard took dinner with
us — at 8 I took her & Annie to Fifth
Ave. & put them in a bus & returned
home & read until 10 — then
walked down to 124th St. & brought
home Annie & Miss Bosé — To bed
soon after 11.

Charles took dinner with us also —
leaving at 8 o'clk. Gave him the Erie Bonds.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 22, 1889

Overcast day — Rather raw — some sun-
light about the middle of the day — Got
up with a headache that made me
somewhat useless all day — Got to
studio rather later than usual &
spent the morning in fussing about —
Home to lunch at 1.30 — after that
Annie & I walked down to 23° St.
to call on M. Coomans & his two
daughters who are now occupying
Dora Wheeler's Studio — we had a
pleasant call — after that, we went
for a few minutes into Water Color Exh'n
then to studio & I painted for a little
while on "Spring is Coming" — Home to dinner
Early, feeling unequal to going with A.
to the opera of the Minnesingers she sent
for — Mrs Williams & I took them to the
Opera — In less than 20 m. after getting
home I was surprised by the return of A —
the opera had been changed to Tannhauser
which A. had heard but a short time ago —
so they had their money refunded — I
wrote letters to Frank Herrick and Capt.
Monair, mailed them & got to bed
before 11.
