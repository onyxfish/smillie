## Left Page

### January, Friday 18, 1889

No. 459 Prospect St. New Haven

The weather having cleared bright —
minded Annie determined to start
on her visit to her sister Mrs. Adams, at
New Haven — She, Jim & I got away on
the 10 A.m. train & got to N.H. at 12. By
street cars we got to Nellie's No. about
1 o'clk. & half an hour later had our
dinner — Dr. A — is at present in N.Y.
on a visit — On my way up from
the Whitney Ave. cars, thro. Canner St. —
I found a very charming passage
suggestive of coming Spring — after
dinner I returned to the place with
paper & pencil & made a sketch and
took notes of light & color — later I
took another walk coming round
again, to the same place — Eve.g
chatting with the family — part of
the time Jack Downs was with us —
in front of a pleasant soft coal fire —
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 19, 1889

A delightful day but growing rapid
ly colder — after 1 o'clk. I walked down
town, going first to the Art School where
I called upon Prof. J. F. Weir — my call
there was interrupted by Prof. Saulesbury —
then I spent some time in the Galleries
— an utterly incoherent collection stuff,
a good deal of which should not be [illegible] —
some of it was interesting to the student.
then went to Evarts Cutter's Store & saw
him — then to 433 Temple St. but did
not see Kate Riley — then to the Adams'
& had dinner about 2 o'clk. — after that
napped awhile in front of the fire — then
Prof & Mrs. G. P. Brush called on us — after that
at about 5 — Annie & I took Jine to the
Downs's to take tea with the children — &
we walked on to Kate Riley's & called upon
her — then again to the D's where Nellie
joined us & we had tea after 6 — Later in
the eve.g (Jim having been sent home to
bed) Prof & Mrs. Brush & three daughters
came in & the Adams boys & girls —
we had a chatty, pleasant eve.g until
after 10 — then home & to bed after 11.
