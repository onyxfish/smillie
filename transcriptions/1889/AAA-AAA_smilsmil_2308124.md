## Left Page

### September, Monday 23, 1889

Superb day, brilliant & growing warmer. The Ther. fell last night
to 36° — a frost in the valleys. Up
before 6 Am. The Irelands started
for home, leaving the house at 6 45. Annie
& Jim rode over to the depot with
them. Coyle came to work — also
Crawley & a boy in place of Halpin —
I got ready to do some proving of the
Koehler Popkey dry point. Parke & Mr.
Holden, a Scranton Architect, called
— Took me & Ralph to the foundery — then
home soon after & printed until 1 —
dinner — Afternoon with the men and
climbing trees, cutting out tops —
Unwell, feeling rather used up, as
my bowel trouble threatens me again.
Read for a little while & to bed at
9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 24, 1889

Mild, pleasant day with a fiem
betokening change of weather. Gorgeous
sunset. Coyle came back to work —
I set him at the strawberry bed.
Crawley put in a full day & Halpin
came on in the afternoon, a boy took
his place in the mt. I kept quiet
in the mt — did no work but wrote
some notes. After dinner Carey came
& the stones were put in place for
the trols of the milk room — I wrote
to Elea Diven & to Kate Smillie & at
4.30 went to P.O. Back soon after
& superintended Coyle as he trans-
planted some Berbery bushes.
Eve'g a little reading & then a little
writing. To bed at 10.
