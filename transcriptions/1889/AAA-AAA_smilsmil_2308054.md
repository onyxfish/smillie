## Left Page

### May, Monday 6. 1889.

Uncomfortably warm & cloudless —
to studio soon after 10 — for an hour
engaged about different things — then
went to Bank — then downtown — to
A.B.N. Co to see Freeland — not in —
then to Walter Cook's & saw plans in
progress to improve hall way at home.
Then to Sargent's — 37 Chambers St. — to enquire
about horse lawn mowers — Home at 1.30
to studio at about 3 — worked for two
hours on "The Goldsmith's Daughter".
Then to Wunderlich's & showed him
pf. of "What o'clk is it?" — he did not
want it framed, so I took it to Keppel's
Saw Mr. Jordan — left with Mr. Greer
6 finished pfs. dry-point "Poppies" —
Home at 6.30 — dinner — Eve'g at 8.30
went to Century & saw there a very
good collection of Slides (Photo) shown
by lantern & lime light — at 10.30 had
some beer — then home & to bed
after 11.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 7. 1889.

*Anniversary of our Wedding —
May 7th, 1881.*

Another warm, clear day — To studio at
10 — Put a second batch of 12 doz. pfs. of
dry-point "Poppies" in press for Greer —
then to work with dry-point & burnisher
on "The Goldsmith's Daughter" — Home
to lunch — after that, went to barber's
& spent an hour having hair cut & head
shampoo'd — then to studio & worked
until 6 — then home — dinner —
Eve'g wrote to Kate until about 8.30
when Frank Waller & his wife came
in & made a call — at 9.45 resumed
my letter & at 10.15 finished it —
mailed it & then to bed.
