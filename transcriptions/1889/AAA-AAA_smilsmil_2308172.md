## Left Page

### December, Monday 30, 1889.

A raw, windy & disagreeable day with a short space of sunshine
in the m'g. After b'kp'. I went over to Daly's Theatre & bought tickets
for next Monday night — then to 2°. Nat. Bk. Cut coupons & made
a deposit for Annie. Then to Knoedler's & spent some time with Rose looking
over etchings & Water Colors. Then to Am. Art Galleries & borrowed a vase from
Kirby & took it to studio where I set to work upon a pencil study of it.
Colman came & painted for an hour or so. It was 1.30 when I
got home to lunch & 2.30 when I got back to studio & resumed work on
study of vase. Washington Wilson came in & bought a pf. of Huntington Etching & one of
"Double Hollyhocks" Both to be framed. I put the last of 6×6 Poppky dry-points in press
then to Kirby — notion — then with George to Century — Home at 6 — dinner at 6.30
Kate, Mollie & Roger went to Daly's —
Frank went to bed — About 9 Marion & Earnest Wright came in for a few minutes.
I took Parker's wedding present a framed proof of 6×6 Poppies to Mrs. T.G. Cunningham
109 2. 36° S.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 31, 1889.

Bright, cold, windy & raw — Got to studio at 10 o'clk & got to work upon pencil
study of vase borrowed from Sutton.
Finished it before 1 o'clk & returned it to Sutton — then to bank & made
a deposit for Annie & home to lunch at 1.30. Back at 2.30 — worked for
a time on large study of Double
Poppies for Dry-Point — Signed 19
Goldsmith's Daughter pf. & changed
platters in press where I had some
dry-point pf. & before 5 o'clk. went
to Wm. Kurtz's & had a talk with him
concerning the possibility of getting his
process work for the Water-Color Cat.
Then to Danl. Huntington's with pf. of
pf. to sign — then to Century — then
home soon after 6 — dinner — we've
under great difficulties wrote letter
to Kate — Geo. & Smiley came in — later
Chas's Standard lamp, presented to
Annie, was sent, followed by himself —
Feeling headachy & heavy I finished the
letter & at 10.30 Chas. Took front to
post & at about 11 I got to bed.
Good bye 1889.
