## Left Page

### November, Saturday 2, 1889

*p8 — [illegible] Ther. 41° Last night. Red. to 110 E, 38⅞ S[t]*

Awake before daylight — up at 6.30
Thick, overcast m'g. — a few gleams
of sunlight bet. 8 & 9 o'clk. led us to
hope for a change of weather which did
not come. Poke & Williams' team came &
by 7.30 were away with our trunks and
boxes. Without much haste or fretting
we got away from the house at 9 52. Our
drive over to the depot was not a pleas-
ant one. Very strong & very raw So. easterly
winds. Got to the depot in ample time
to attend to freight & other matters. No.
4 came along on time at 11.53 — our seats
were reserved & our journey on was un-
eventful. Heavy rain from Pocono M. on
to Boonton. Muggy & wet; but no rain
when we got to the city. Reached the
house before 7 o'clk. & found Mary &
Kate in readiness for us. — had our tea
— the trunks came before 9 — Charl
came in — & we got to bed soon
after 10 — Clear moon

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 3, 1889

Dismal, rainy day. — we got up
late & I did little else all day
than loaf & lie about. Geo. came
in in the m'g. Af. I went to Dr
Yale's & saw him for half an hour —
Home — dinner at 2 — Afternoon,
Geo. & Emily came in — Mary Cook
& her children, Charles, Ed[w.] &
more children. After tea I got a
short nap — and [illegible] near 11
o'clk — there to bed — clear
moonlight.
