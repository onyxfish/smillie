## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 26. 1889.

Bright, pleasant day. Got to studio
at 10.15. feeling pretty seedy —
managed to give some time
looking over the Goldsmith's Daughter
ph[o]t[o]s that Pate sent up to me and
touched the faces of a number of
them with crayon to correct imperfections of printing. Home to lunch
back before 3 — finished signing
the ph[o]t[o]s. Annie came in for a few
minutes. After 4 I took the ph[o]t[o]s
to W. Huntington & he signed them
while I waited. I got home before
6. dinner. Before 8 I took Annie
down to her Choral Club & then came
home at once. Finished letter to Ma.
Clark came in with petition of
trustees of Mother's estate — wishing me
to get it signed properly to-morrow —
At 10 I went for Annie — home before
11 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 27. 1889.

*New Haven at H. D. L. Adams'.*

Disagreeable m[i]l[d]. The weather continuing to grow worse until at night
it was a howling storm. I got to
studio before 10.30 — after getting a notary
(Barnes) to witness my signature to a petition
to a Dutchess Co. Court for permission, as one of the
trustees, to sell Eastman Terrace to J. J. Beggs.
I put several dry-point ph[o]t[o]s in press and
attended to some other matters at the
studio & before 12 started for home. Got
everything ready for a start — had lunch
promptly at 1 at 1.40 Annie, Gretta, Jim,
Ralph & I started in the rain for N. C.
Depot & took 2 o'clk train to New Haven
getting there at 4.5  15 m. late. Roger Adams
met us at depot with carriage but it was
nearly 5 when we got to the house. Tea
at 6.30 — an even[ing] of general chatting
& to bed at 10.30. Heavy rain at
intervals all through the night.
