## Left Page

### April, Saturday 6, 1889.

Bad weather clings to us — the
day has been leaden overhead
with an east wind growing strong-
er all day — Bkfst. at about 8.30 —
much of the w[illegible] was spent out
of doors on the strange "board walk"
characteristic of this place — A.
& I called upon Mr. Williams and
daughters at the "Traymore" — dinner
at 1.30 — In the afternoon A and
I took another walk but found
the wind very disagreeable —
Ev[illegible]. at first, for a time we sat
in our bed room but finding it
too cool we went to the parlors &
there shiftless about for an hour &
so finding every place uncomfort-
ably hot & draughty — at last,
to get out of it — we went to bed
before 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 7. 1889.

Bleaker & more leaden than
ever — a high wind & a high sea
all day — I had a very wretched
night, the cold in my head came
on so severely that I could not breathe
through my nose — got up quite
discouraged — Bkfst. — before 9 —
Jas. W. came over to see us & A.
went off with him to call on some
Phila. friends — Gretta went to church
& I stayed with the children — When
Ralph took his mf nap, I lay down
beside him & slept for awhile —
Dinner at 1.30 — Afternoon I read
newspapers & did some writing.
Ev[illegible]. we sat in our room and
I wrote part of a letter to Kate.
To bed at about 10.
