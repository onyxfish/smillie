## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 24, 1889.

Bright w'f. but overcast later in
the day & a little spatter of rain
at night. I got to studio at 10
& at once to work proving mezzo-
tint "Double holly hooks" — Took
two on thin jap. & five on peach
India — Two of the india ones being
very good — the others were fair to
middling. I got an exceedingly pleasant & flattering letter from
Chas. H. Hart congratulating me
on the success of "The Goldsmith's
Daughter" plate — Home to dinner
at 1.15. back at 2.45 & worked at
proving as long as I could see — then
until 6, Tacking proofs to stretches —
Home at 6.30 — very tired — tea — at
8 Took Annie to Edw'd Cooks. I
Returned home & wrote my
letter to Mary — Annie Ret'd. at 10
— I mailed my letter — To bed
at 11.30.

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 25. 1889.

Brilliant; mild day — but not
much of a success at home — both
of the children being sick — Jim
complaining of headache & nausea
— Ralph, feverish & constipated —
at 9.30 gave him a dose of oil —
the two were forlorn & spent
most of the time on the bed — I
went out about 11 — went to Char's
& then had a short walk — At
1.15 Annie went to her br. Edw'd —
I stayed with the children — got
my own lunch, as there was no one
in the house — about 3 A. Returned
& I went to Edw's. In half an hour or
so — A. followed with the children, as
they had Revived — & about 4 we Ret.
At 6 I took a short walk — at 7
we had dinner — Geo. Nellie & Charl
sat down with us — Venison and
champagne — a very good dinner.
Our company left at 10 & we got
to bed soon after — About
9 o'clk. Ralph had a Regular
clearing out — a change for the
better.
