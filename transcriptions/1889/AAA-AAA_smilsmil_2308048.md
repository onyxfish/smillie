## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 24, 1889

State bright, breezy & cool — got to
studio about 10 — Spent the mf
cleaning up, writing delayed notes
making a list for Garden seeds
for Montrose, — etc. etc. — about noon
I went to Bridgeman's — the seedsman
— also to N.A. f. D. for a little while
then home to lunch. In the
afternoon I got my press and
printing table in order for print-
ing & take plf. To-morrow (second
working) from my plate "The Goldsmith's
Daughter!" — also cut paper & wet it
down — Home soon after 6 — dinner
Eve'g at about 8 — Annie & I went to
Park Ave & 40th St. & called on W. & Mrs.
Knoedsee — Home som after 9 — at
10 Chave came in for a while
— to bed after 11.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 25, 1889

Bright mf — weather gradually
overcast until at 6 o'clk. there were
showers. Got to studio before 10 and
to work getting ready to take the
2d working plf. from "The Goldsmith's
Daughter." By 11.30 I had pulled
the first impression & was much re-
joiced to find that it looked much
better than I had expected —
Annie & Jim then came in & at 12 we
went to All Souls Ch. cor. 4th Ave. & 20th St.
& saw Lilian Spring married to Walter
Cheny — then I went to O'neil's, the photo.
& then to Latham, the Tailor, about seats to see
the parade — then home at 1.30 — to studio at
2.30 & took two more plf. All of the p.m.
given up to this & to studying the work
that is before me on the plate — Home
at 6.15 — Chave came in with $4 000 in
bonds — Result of recent sales & purchases for
Annie — he took dinner with us then left —
Eve'g: some reading & some writing. —
to bed about 10.30. Very heavy rain
during early eve'g & night.
