## Left Page

### July, Thursday 25, 1889

A perfect day — cool & brilliant — up
at 7 & spent some time in the garden
before bkfst. Got to work printing
"Goldsmith's Daughter" at about 9.30
but had various delays so that it
was nearly 11 before I pulled the
first one, which was spoiled by a bit
of carelessness. Took two others before
dinner — & two others after dinner
Mr. Curry gave my studio a clean-
ing. At 4.30 went out riding, Emily,
Mollie, Jack & the two childrens. Got
in at 6.30. Albert was in such a
hurry to get away that he forgot to
put on their halters & about 8 o'clk.
we found them quietly grazing under
the trees — got them into their stalls
after some trouble. At tea had our
stewed wood chuck — everybody said
it was pretty good & nobody ate much
of it. Even't. on piazza until 9 —
then in doors

## Right Page

### July, Friday 26, 1889

A very pleasant day in spite of the
wind from the S. E. & some clouds that
at times looked ominous of a change
of weather. I worked all the m'f'd
at my proving — after dinner I gave
myself up to fore preparing for the 4 to
6 tea for which Annie had sent out
cards. Jack & the girls have been deco-
rating the house & it really looks
very beautiful. Some of the neighbors
sent in beautiful flowers — these,
with the sunshine & the mild air
made it very charming. About forty
callers came & I think that the whole
affair passed off very pleasantly. Table
chairs & rugs were on the piazzas — ice
cream was dispensed from a table on
the studio piazza while chocolate, tea
& cakes were to be had indoors — friends
thronged inside & out — all seemed to
have a good time. We had tea about
7 & spent the even'g. reading newspapers
& playing card games — To bed at 10.30
