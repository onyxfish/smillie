## Left Page

### November, Monday 4, 1889.

A bright, cloudless & beautiful day.
Bkfs. at 8.30 — Moran, the carpenter,
came to arrange for laying a new floor
in the nursery. It was ten o'clk. before
I got away from the house — attended
to messages on the way to the studio —
when there looked at the dirt for awhile
then to Union Storage Co & to Bank where
I attended to Annie's nov. business, then
[illegible] nearly an hour at [illegible] Gate, then to Nassau
down town, & collected int. on Milwaukee
R.R. Bonds — then to Clark's for a moment
then home at 2 o'clk. — Many Willard
there — After 3 o'clk I went to Century
to see plans of new bldg & [illegible] A. Hamilton
[illegible] after Hovenden's painting — then again
to studio — some time with Geo. & Nellie. Home
at 5.30 — Sunward there — Dinner at 6.30
Even'g Annie & I went to call on
Geo. & Nellie at their "String flat" cor. [illegible]
ex. Ave. & 33d S. — & had a pleasant
even'g until near 10 o'clk — then
home & to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 5, 1889.

Election Day —

Pleasant early int., cloudy & raw
until about sunset when it cleared —
beautiful moonlight night. — To studio
at 9.30 — Mrs. Duff came along at about
10 & until after 11 was scrubbing &
mussing around pretending to clean
up. Except when I went home
to lunch I was with her as hard
at work as she was. — Dinner
at the usual time. — Even'g I
took Annie to her Choral Club
rehearsal in 14th St., then to Century
where I wrote my regular letter to
Kate — At 10.15 went for A. — walked
up home — beautiful moonlight.
It was about mid night when
I got to bed.
