## Left Page

### May, Sunday 12, 1889

Spent part of the day on the Palisades.

A very warm, close day — M's and breakfasted at usual time — At
10 45 Annie, Jim + I went to G.C.
Depot. There met Ed M's + 4 of his
children — Geo. + Emily + Walter's
3 — Fred + Alice Ireland. — Jno
Valentine + Chas. Collins — we all
went to Yonkers — C.C. went on +
the rest of us crossed the river by
ferry + there, by a zig-zag road
climbed to the top of the Palisades.
At 1 o'clk — under a small clump
of trees we took lunch — At 2.30
are started to return — Crossed at
3.30 to catch 3 51 Train — Adam
waiting for it for some time, found
that on Sunday it left at 4.30 then
was nothing to do but wait — Got
to N.Y. depot at 5 — then home —
dinner at 6.30 — Evef. Annie
read aloud from "Scribner" for May
Yale's fishing paper on the Umanishe,
on land locked salmon — To bed at
10.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 13, 1889

Bright m'f — cloudy afternoon, with
rain for about an hour — 3 to 4 o'clk.
Got to studio about 9.30, after stopping
at Geo's house for a few minutes — and
there to M. Coosman's Studio to see a couple
of new portraits — Chamboney + Geo there.
To studio about 10 + to work at proving
my by plate — Took two p'f. + was much
disappointed to find that my burnish-
ing, scraping + stoning had not yet suf-
ficiently reduced the color in parts —
Mr. Greer came in + then Jalt, to offer me
$150 for "Spring is Coming" without the frame.
I ask $300 for the picture — the frame cost $40
I accepted the offer — Home to lunch at 1 —
Back at 2.45 — worked awhile on plate —
then finished + signed 5 p'f. of "Poppies" — Dry
point + took them to Greer — Took three
what o'clk is it?" to Wunderlich — Got
photo- enlargement of Edw'd Cook's daguer-
reotype from O'Neil — Home at 6.15 —
Dinner — Evef. Charl came in and
stayed until 7.30 — Reports contract to sale
of 44th St. house as signed — + that DeGroal
is willing to call the Terrace + Sey. Ave
Exchange "off"! To bed at about 11.
