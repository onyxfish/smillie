## Left Page

### October, Friday 11, 1889

Ther. 38° last night.
Bella & Mary Tweedy am?

Bright m'g — growing vaporous & hazy
but mild & quiet. I spent some time
with the men. Hotchkiss finished his
job bet. 4 & 5 o'clk. Halpin, Williams
& White worked on drain in garden.
Then I made a fire in etching room &
printed & worked on "Hollyhocks" dry-
point — dinner — then to the garden
while there Mr. Woodie came & it
was after 3 before I was again able
to get at my work — don't think that
I will be able to make anything
worth saving out of "Hollyhocks" — It
was 5 o'clk when Albert drove over
to little R. Ridgepat — I went along taking
the boys — just in time for train — Bella
& Mary Tweedy on board. Drove at
once to the house — Eve'g chatting
to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 12, 1889

Ther. went to 45° last night.

A lovely m'g, mild & hazy — after
some time out of doors with the men
I got to work in studio preparing
to paint one of two out of door studies
in the afternoon in which for some
time past I have been much interes-
ted. I outlined the subject on the
canvas & rubbed a frottée with sug-
gestion of color over it, then fixed all
my traps for action. At dinner time
the sky was heavy with clouds & soon
after a down pour of rain came with
some thunder — the rain continued
steadily all the afternoon — the men
gave up working at 2 o'clk. I
painted one of the studies on paper
and to trusting to my recollection —
After 5 I went down town — tea at 6.30
Eve'g chatting until 11 — then to
bed.
