## Left Page

### October, Thursday 17. 1889

**Ther. 41° last night**

Charming day — but got up with a headache — decided to spend the day out of doors — most of the time up a bass wood tree cutting out the tops & farming it. In the garden Halpin hauled manure. Rafferty helping. Coyle & the boy White at work gathering stone. Our team worked there part of the m'f & in the afternoon Halpin's team hauled stone. After dinner, the Mrs, Annie, Ralph & I drove to Salt Spring. The sky was suddenly clouded but it was not threatening or unpleasant. Home at 5.30 — Eve'g, Annie read aloud & I had a nap. To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 18. 1889

**Ther. 37° last night**

Still glorious weather — I worked out of doors all the m'f. Some of the time with the men in the garden. Halpin ploughing. Rafferty & Coyle digging out the stone after him. Then I worked among the trees — cutting & then farming them. Changed boards in standing press, where I have some of the p'fs of "The Goldsmith's Daughter". Dinner a little late. 2.30 when Belle T., Annie, Ralph & I got off for our ride. Mary did not feel well enough to go. I took along my camera & made 6 exposures down by Josh Pettus's bridge where I had hoped to get some oil studies. Home soon after 5 — Eve'g in dark room, developing until near 10 — To bed at 11.
