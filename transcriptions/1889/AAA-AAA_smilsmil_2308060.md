## Left Page

### May, Saturday 18. 1889.

went to Greenwood —

A very pleasant day — 10 o'clk.
when I got to Studio — As my
head bothered me I did not get
much work done — I burnished
on big plate & portions of the hair
that I could not reduce with
the burnisher I scraped — Home
at 1 — lunch — at 2.35 Annie & I
went to Hamilton Ave. Ferry where
we met Geo. & Charl — crossed & by
horse car went to Greenwood where
we am— at 3 — We took seats in one
of the public conveyances & rode
for about 35 minutes when we reached
the foot of Atlantic or Ocean Hill — We
were then but a few steps from our plot —
we found things in very good order — after
awhile we wandered back to the lot where
Annie's mother lies & then out at about
4.30 — The day was delicious — the dogwood
a wonder & the lawns, trees & flowers a
constant delight — The return was
of course tedious — we got home about 6.15
Charl took dinner with us but
left soon after — I read for awhile
& we got to bed at 9.30.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 19. 1889.

A dull day — smart shower about
2 o'clk. Rain, late p.m. & eve'g.
Blkf— after 8 — Reading for some time
at 11 went to see J. H. Smith but he
was out of Town — then to Studio for
a few minutes — Home at noon — dinner
at 12 — a nagging headache for the
rest of the afternoon & most of the
eve'g — About 3.30 went out and
walked down to the east river and
explored — Taking note of the gashouses
— the abbatoirs — the marine dumps
— the German Jew quarter, swarming
with children & back through a
tenement district that seemed to
me more like an ant hill than any-
thing human. A cup of tea at 6.30.
At 8.30 Annie. & I went to Walter's to
talk with him about plans for the
finish & beautification of our front hall —
There until 9.30 — home in a heavy
shower — to bed at 10.30.
