## Left Page

### August, Friday 2. 1889.

Some showers in the mtf but a very pleasant afternoon. I felt under the weather & unlike work — but after a time I got interested in my 6×6 Holley-hocks & I worked upon it & took some [illegible] dinner — then again work & a proof, which belated me in starting on our drive — Stopped at Mr. Spencer's & saw Mr. S's arrangement for keeping milk. Every then was some reading & a short game of cards before we went to bed at 10.30.
W. & Mrs. Wm. Muelford called at about 6 45.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 3. 1889.

A day of heavy showers, one succeeding another all day long, with bright sunshine intervening. About the middle of the afternoon a furious one came with wind & hail — the results of which were very severe. The vines were riddled & many of the flowering shrubs entirely destroyed. During the day I worked at mounting some of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" proofs. About 5 p.m. I got at my Hollyhock 6×6 & worked until tea time. After tea went down for mail etc. — home at 8 — Reading until 10.30 — to bed at 11.
