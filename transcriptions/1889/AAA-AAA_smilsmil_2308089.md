## Left Page

### July, Monday 15, 1889

Heavy & steady rain during the night & until about 7 o'clk — commenced to clear at about noon — delightful afternoon — Very cool — had fires in large room & studio — spent some time in working over working [illegible] of my large plate — Afternoon I wrote to Ge⁰. sending him Kate's letter Rec⁰. from Charl this m⁰ft finished my letter to Kate & sent with it George's letter to me & a phot. of Jim & Ralph taken by Thomas. & the horses had to be shod — it was 5.30 before we started on our ride. Found it very cool — like a Sept. day — home at 6.40 — Tea — Eve⁴ with news papers — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 16, 1889

A splendid, cool day — more like Sep. than July — Ther. fell last night to 51°. Herrick came to work to-day & spent his time upon the dado at each end of the main floor of the house which has to be removed because of faulty construction — water ran through at every storm — I was engaged about many things until near noon when I went down town — went to Herrick's barn to see the hay that has been put in there — cut on the place — it is a [illegible] — Home at 1 — dinner — Took some photo of Bostwick's cow — at 3.30 we went out riding — went to South Pond. & [illegible] at 6⁰. Mr. [illegible] Jn⁰. called after tea — Eve⁴ with Newspaper
