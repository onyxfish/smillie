## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 8, 1889

*Annual Meeting N.A. [illegible]*

Very pleasant day, altho' warm —
It was about 10 o'clk when I got
to Studio — looking at my plate I
made up my mind to take a p[roof]
at once. — I set Baker — ground up
ink & before noon had two p[roof]s —
Taken & all washed up. Home
to lunch at 1 — at 2 to N.A. of D —
I spent the afternoon there at the
Annual meeting — adjourned at
5.30 — then went home for a few
moments — had a little walk and
change of air — back to N.A. at 6.15
Annual dinner — Words. Thompson
on my left — J.C. Nicoll on my right —
Had a very good time — altho'
quiet — It was about 10.30 when
I got home — a little before Annie
who had dined at her bro.
Edward's.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 9, 1889

Very warm & clear day — to Studio
at about 9 o'clk. feeling none the
worse for my Academy dinner — I
worked all day on big plate with
dry-point & burnisher — Home to
lunch at usual time — worked
until near 6 P.m. Miss
McChesney called — her first
visit to the Studio. Home to dinner
at 6 — Evelff Annie & I went to
the Star-Theatre & saw Bronson Howard's
"Henrietta" given by Crane & Robson —
They are common place actors and
the play, while having some good
dramatic situations, is little
better — It was 11.30 when we got
home — right to bed.
