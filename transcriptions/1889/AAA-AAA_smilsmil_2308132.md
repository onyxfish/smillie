## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 9, 1889

Ther. last night. 33°
The Jessup-Woodin wedding.

Very bright, windy m'g. But soon after 10, the sky was overcast, & by noon rain seemed imminent — Later the weather prospects improved & at night the moonlight was beautiful at times. After my regular morning chores, I went to the garden & worked until 12 with Halprin & Williams in making the new Asparagus bed — Then to studio & worked on new dry point for a few minutes — after dinner wrote letters to Chase & Cate — then took a short nap — at 4 went to P.O. — then had my hair cut & ret'd home — & dressed myself after tea for the Jessup-Woodin wedding. Their tea — At 7.30 Albert had the house at the door & we rode to the Presb. ch. where we were given excellent seats, having Fanny Jessup on one side & Lilly Jessup's rescueing on the other side — The bride & party were only 10 m. late (8½°) & the ceremony was over in little more than 20 m — The church & was attractively & pretty decorated with masses of autumn leaves — the brides maids looked very pretty — after the ceremony we rode to our reception at the house — a jam — but every body in exceeding good humor — an excellent supper was served about 10 o'clk — & a small orchestra from Scranton furnished the music — The young couple started for Binghamton on a little before 11 o'clk — via D.L. & W. R.R. — much rice was thrown — we left soon after — & it was after midnight when we got to bed —

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 10, 1889

Ther. last night. 39°

A splendid day, altho' cool & windy. Burgess came & finished the piazza job. man from Boyd & Cooly's came & set gas-pipe railing on coking near the well. Ben Maylor came & set up stove in Cherry Room — Telegrams from Belle Tweedy stating they would come to-morrow — Halprin & the boy White, in garden — Williams came on in the afternoon — I worked on hollyshock dry-point from 2 to 7 o'clk — Then dinner. After that, again worked until 3 — From 3½ to 5½ the family rode. I went to Horton's & looked at his Rye straw-damaged by exposure to rain after it was cut. Suet'g, Read awhile — wrote notes to Aunt Agnes & Barnett Bros — To bed early.
