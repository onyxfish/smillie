## Left Page

### January, Thursday 24, 1889.

Foggy & mild — at night rain —
Got to studio a little before 10 and
to work finishing the painting "A
Pool" — worked all the m'g & home
to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.30 &
painted until 4 — I have changed
the aspect of the painting — making
the sky cooler & greyer & blotting out
the light on the horizon which gives
more breadth. While it is better in
color — simpler in effect & more finish-
ed — I'm afraid that it has lost some
"spunk" — At 4 went to Kurtz's — but
of course, he did not have my block done.
Then to the, & saw F. A. Richards — Tarrer
& Chas. Platt — to studio again at 5. Then
cleaned up — home at 6. dinner —
eve'g. Annie's Choral Club Rehearsal.
I took her to 14th St. at 8.30 then to
Century & spent the eve'g reading
until 10.30 — then for A — Home at
11 & to bed.

Jim's chicken pox is now well devel-
oped & unmistakable.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 25, 1889.

A very bright, pleasant day but
my head troubled me & I was out
of sorts generally — Got to studio at
10 & to work on little painting
"Head waters" — Repainted it making
some considerable changes — Home
at 1 — Margaret Parker White expected
to lunch, but she did not come — To
N. A. of D. Kurtz sent process block
for Cat. after my Water Color "The Passing
Herd" — It is fairly successful — Then
to studio before 3 & worked until about
4.30 — then gave up & went home —
Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g read my
news paper for a time — then
Annie read aloud Rob't. Elsmere
until after 10 — To bed at 11.
