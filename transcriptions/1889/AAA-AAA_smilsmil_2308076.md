## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 19. 1889.

Overcast day with a slight sprinkle of Rain about 11 A.M. Ralph was hardly asleep at all last night & this m'g still had a good deal of fever with imperfect action of the Rhubarb given to him last night — so I went for D. Richardson who came up a little before noon & on his advice a dose of castor oil was administered with good results in an hour — All of the afternoon until 5 he slept very quietly & then got up, without fever & in very good spirits — He went to sleep for the night before 9 — I was good for nothing during the day — men came in the m'g & took out the studio cistern — I was not with them all the time & they managed to make a horrible mess by upsetting a lot of water over the floor & rugs. I went down town about 11 & back at 12 — in the afternoon I managed to do a little work on my plate. Every Annie read aloud for a little while Ralph was very troublesome during the night & again had fever.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 20. 1889.

Glorious day — clear, crisp & very sweet. Ralph got up evidently quite sick — About 10 o'clk. Mr. & Mrs. Parke & Mrs. Atherton called — I rode down town with them — saw Dr. R — home before 12 — Dr. R came up & saw Ralph who had turned over a new leaf & seemed to be improving — I managed to work on my plate about one hour before dinner — The plumbers came with new Studio's cistern at 1 o'clk & were at work all the afternoon putting it in — At 4 Annie & I went out riding — called for Flora Lathrop — had a headache — for Mrs. Atherton — not in — met Mrs. Husted — Took her — drove to Frankline Forks — back at 6.40 — Tea — after that went down town — before 9 Annie commenced reading aloud & finished Mary Hallock Foote's story "The Last Assembly Ball" — To bed soon after 10.
