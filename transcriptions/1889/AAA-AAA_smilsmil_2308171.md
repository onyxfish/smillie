## Left Page

### December, Saturday 28, 1889

Delightfully bright, pleasant day —
To studio at 10 — worked all the m[orning]
on a close pencil study after a
Jap. jar or vase belonging to [illegible]
I want something to take the place
of the Wedgewood vase in my large
Paisley study for a dry-point.
Home to lunch after 1 — after having
gone to Daly's theatre to see if I
could get some tickets — Office closed.
Back to studio's at 2.30 and by
diligent work I finished the
vase study before dark. walked
down to Century hoping to find a
note from W.H.R. Bishop — then home
— dinner — Evelf. Annnie — the
Adams's & I went to Edw. Cook's —
Louise Oakey there — Walter came
in — home about 10 — & there to
bed.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 29, 1889

A nasty, close, damp overcast day —
Bkfst. late, as usual. — After sitting
with newspaper until after 11 I went
to see Charl who has been down
with the prevailing epidemic — now
out again — then I walked up 5th Ave
to 70th St. — & back — a sprinkling of rain —
Lib Home & Jack Downs joined us
at dinner — our family making quite
a table full. — Later in the after-
noon I went with Annie and the
Adams girls to Aunt Annie's in E.
17th St. — Saw Catherine Ireland there —
Home at 6 — Tea — Evelf. I wrote
a letter to Chas. H.F. Hart in answer
to his pleasant note of congratulation
on my Huntington plate, written
a week ago. I also tried to write
a few lines to R.W. Hitchcock, thanking
him for the notice of the plate in
to-day's Tribune. — To bed after
10.
