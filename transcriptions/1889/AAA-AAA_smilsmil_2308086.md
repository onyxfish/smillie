## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 9, 1889.

Very hot — the N.W. breeze still blowing.
much loafing about with preparation
for Mary's departure at 12.30. Dinner
was served at 12 & at 12.45 Albert
started for M. depot with them — M.
& children — to get to Buffalo at 10.30
to-night. I took some dinner at 1 —
in the afternoon tried to do some clear-
ing up in studio & etching room — A —
got back with team at about 5 — I
went down town & had my hair cut-
ter at 6.30. Lonesome after losing so
large a portion of our family — Eve'g
Annie & I sat upon the piazza en-
joying the wonderful moonlight effects
& the soft eve'g air until about 10.30
It was 11 before we got to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 10, 1889.

A very hot day — accomplished very
little during the day — lounged
about under the trees & now & then
did a little in the way fixing vines
flower beds & garden. — worked in
my dark-room, testing paper over
light leaks and making up some
Hydrocinine solutions. A thunder
storm threatened us at about 2.30
but it rapidly passed to the south.
Eve'g. had the horses harnessed
at 7 & started out at tho' another
thunderstorm was coming up. Annie
Jim & I rode about town for an
hour — then came in just as the
rain began to fall — the body of
the storm passed south — the rain
we had was very gentle & soon over.
Annie & I sat on piazza until
10 — then to bed.
