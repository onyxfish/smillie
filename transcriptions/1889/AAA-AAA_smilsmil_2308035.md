## Left Page

### March, Friday 29, 1889

Brilliant day — cool wind — Uncle Willie came in for a few minutes after bkfst — got from me "In Memoriam" of Father's to take to Montreal to show to some gentlemen there — Then Louis Tiffany came to give me his advice about finishing the home hall way. It was a little after 10 when I got to Studio — Young Grady waiting for me — settled up our affairs to date. Then I went over to K.A. & spent the rest of the mf talking with young men. Home at 1.30 Moran, carpenter, came to see about fixing roof — To studio before 3 — worked on G.D. until 5 — washed up & home at 6 — dinner — Ralph's head much stuffed up — Evng. Annie read aloud for a little while, but we were both too sleepy to enjoy it — Got to bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 30, 1889

About an inch of snow fell last night. The day was clear & very bright with strong cold wind blowing — Got to studio at 10 & to work on G.D. with dry-point on the large sleeve — there is a splendid chance for display of texture & variety of line. I hope that I may be able to get something creditable — Home at 1 — back at 2.45 — Ralph seemed so forlorn with his cold in the head & coming teeth that we sent again for Dr. Yale, who came in the afternoon — I worked on sleeve until after 5 — Mr. Harry Watrous & wife made a call on me after 4 o'clk. Home soon after 6, dinner. Evening, Annie & Mary Sprague went down to the Star Theatre, alone, to hear Coquelin and have a spree by themselves — I stayed at home with the children & wrote a part of my letter to Mary. Annie came in after 11 — I started to take Mary home but met Ed. at the corner coming for her — To bed just before 12.
