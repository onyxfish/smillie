## Left Page

### October, Monday 21, 1889

Ther. 29° last night.

The wonderfully beautiful weather
continues. I spent the m'f, (when
not with the men) in Etching Room
proving dry-point Hollyhocks — and
the afternoon, most of it, in same
place in connection with same
plate + finishing P.P. of Goldsmiths
Daughter. The Rp. from Boston Museum
was Rec'd at noon + I at once put
in in standing Press. At 5 p.m.
with Ralph + Gretta I drove to little
depot to meet Annie + Jim — Train
so late + even so cold that at 5.30 I
brought R. back to the house — then
again drove over + waited until
a when I was so cold that I drove
home + had the horses put up — Spent
some time with Hall in settling his
accts. Then Annie came in by stage
at 8 m's later — Tea — Evng [very]
busy writing letters until 10.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 22, 1889

Ther. 27° last night.

Brilliant m'f. Streaky clouds began
to cover the sky soon after noon + by
7 o'clk. fine snow commenced to fall
very quietly. Rafferty commenced to
cut down the dying beach tree on the
N. side of the house — all day spent
digging under the roots to upset it. I
spent the day proving Hollyhock
dry-point plate. Annie finished
my letter to Kate + mailed it in
the afternoon. Mr. W. + Mr. Sant.
Jessup's called. So did Frozier, to
see if he could get financial help
to stop foreclosure of mortgage. Eve'g
I wrote a letter to Geo.
