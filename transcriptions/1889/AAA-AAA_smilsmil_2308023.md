## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 5, 1889

Still dismal weather — mizzle &
mist until late in the afternoon
when it began to blow for clearing —
Got to studio late — Pictures
came home from the Century &
I worked for a time on "Spring is
Coming" — more blue in distance &
corrected drawing of calf — Home at
1 o'clk — At 2 A walked down to Barns
the Notary's & executed a paper for the
selling of Lehigh Coal & Nav. Co's Bonds —
then to studio & called on Miss Abbott — I
went to 2°. Nat. Buk — then to National
[illegible] — then to Kurtz & to studio after 3 —
Painted on "Huddle Woods" as long as
I could see — Then home — Will R—
& Charle to dinner — very [illegible] —
Charle left immediately after and
W.K— at 8.30 — There I wrote a letter to
Mary — to go with Anines — and one
to Mrs. Ehlinger. Abt. 9.30 went out
& mailed them — going up to G.C.D—
with Mrs. 2's letter — It was after 11
when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 6, 1889

Pleasant — altho' much of the day was
overcast — Ralph was quite unwell in
the early m'f — stomach rejected food
& he had fever — It was nearly 11
when I got to studio — Painted over a
large part of "Headwaters" — At 12.30
to 2°. Nat. Buk & home at 1 — Ralph
is still very languid & disposed to
sleep — Dr. [illegible] came in about 2 &
advised a clearing out — gave castor oil —
It was after 3 when I got to Studio —
Continued my painting on Headwaters
& got out much snuffy & lost brown —
Home before 6. Ralph seems to be
a little better — Eve'g Sam. Colman
came in & sat with us until 10 —
To bed about 10.30.
