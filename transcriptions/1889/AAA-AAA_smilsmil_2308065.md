## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 28, 1889.

A delightful day — To studio about 10 —
got my packing very nearly completed
when H. Jay Smith of Minneapolis came
in — & before he left young Pate came.
We had an hour or more of conversation
about the plate "The Goldsmith's Daughter" —
he was very unwilling to commit him-
self & finally it was agreed that after
consulting with his father a note sh'd
be written to me this ev'g containing
a proposition. Then I went home to
lunch — After that, to Avery's gallery and
arranged to leave "Cliffs of Normandy"
with him until next fall — then to Wash-
Wilson's with [illegible] of "Reading Homer" — missed
him — then to Kepple's to see Greer — missed
him — then to Brooks Bros. & bought a pair
of Trousers — then to Knœdler's & got oil colors
then to "Palette" & got Water colors — then to studio
& screwed up my boxes — Home at 6.30
after leaving Marys letter rec'd this m'g
at U.S. Club for Charl —— Dinner — at
8 o'clk. Annie & I went to Chickering Hall
to see a lantern slide exhibit in aid of the
Washington arch — Chauncey being Spoke-
man — Home a few minutes after 10
& to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 29, 1889.

A delightful day — got started at the usual
time after bkfs. on a hard days work of run-
ning about upon all sorts of business — To Station
7 to Gurney's E[illegible]. To the bank & safe deposit
Co. where I arranged matters for the coming
six months — to Knœdler's — to Gorlet's and to
Ponroy's at Bdway & 10[illegible]8 — From there, home to
lunch — After that, rode down to Charl's — not
in — then to Delann's — [illegible] — then to Beckman
St. & got glazed board & platters — then to Sellers
in Dey St. & got mezztint scrapers — then to Park
Place for Drawing Room R.R. seats — then to 429
Bdway & got mileage book — then to Kimmel
& Voigts & got paper & printing ink & at last,
home, just before 6. Found Fanny Kimball
there — dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g, great ex-
citement among the police who thought
that they saw lights in our East hand neigh-
bors house (Martin) and that our west hand
neighbor's front door (Edwards) was not properly
fastened — they kept the neighborhood in a
ferment all the ev'g. I did some
desultory work in the way of work pre-
liminary to packing — to bed soon after
10.
