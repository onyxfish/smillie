## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 29, 1889

Ther. 35° last night.

Another damp, raw, leaden day.
many things occupied my time
until after 11 o'clk. when I got
the grey goose & commenced a
sketch in oil from that — He too
was a good model after his struggle
to free himself & posed for an hour
almost without a movement.
Herrick at last put in an appearance
& did a couple of hours work
at packing in the afternoon. Coyle
& Rafferty worked at digging. To-day
was Albert McCoy's last day & we
parted with mutual assurances of
regard — I had many little things
to attend to — went down town after
tea — then read my paper. Spent
half an hour in dark room — to
bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 30, 1889

Ther. 34° last night.

weather much the same as yesterday.
Got up with a sore throat that troubled
me all day. Herrick at work doing
what he could to help at packing.
Coyle & Rafferty spent most of the
day transplanting a large hard maple
to take the place of the old beech
cut down from the north side of the
house. I worked generally at every
thing in studio & etching room, putting
things away & preparing to pack —
Kate & Mary got away for N.Y. in
the 6 o'clk A.M. stage — in a cold drizzle
I went down town before tea —
Eve'g spent some time mounting
a number of little photos of Jim
& Ralph "Shooting Partridges." Tobed
at about 11.
