## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 28, 1889

And yet another charming day —
I worked for awhile on crayon
study of Poppies — Parke came in &
spent some time talking to me
& about noon Dr. & Mr. Norris with
two lady friends called & spent
more than an hour going all over
the house & expressing themselves
much pleased — we had dinner
soon after 1 — I felt so much out
of sorts in the afternoon that I spent
some time on the bed — Mr. Warner
& Herrick worked in the kitchen papering the walls & ceiling. After 6
I sent Herrick, with horse & buggy
from Hall's, down to Montrose depot
to meet Miss Joshay who got there
on 7.23 train & to the house at about
9 — all to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 29, 1889

The fine dry weather still continues.
After bkfs. I went down town & found
a man who will undertake bossing
the job of digging out a place for our
milk room that is to be — also saw
Mr. Crowley who is to lay the brick
work — Met Parke, his bro. & Mr. Begg
with team — took me to foundry, then
home where Mr. Begg showed us some
remarkably fine specimens of pearls
& diamonds, in settings & without —
Dinner at 1 — Some work on crayon
Poppies — At 4 Annie, Ralph, Miss
Joshay & I went out riding — in at
6.30 — Tea — Evelf, all reading
one thing & another, most of my
time being given to newspapers.
To bed soon after 10.
