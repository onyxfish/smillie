## Left Page

### February, Thursday 7, 1889

Brilliant day — very cold & strong wind — To studio at about 10 and spent the day finishing about a painting — view near New Haven. I got up to Dr. Yale's at 1 o'clk and waited until he had his lunch — then he kept me more than an hour talking variously — I wanted to get some light concerning the pain in my chest that has betrinkled me for a fortnight past — but of which I have had none to-day. He gave me medicine, but no wisdom — I got home to lunch at 2.20 — got to studio about 3.30 — painted for about an hour — then [illegible] in big chair & home shortly after 6. Evening: The Misses Bose called and took Annie to her Chrome Club Rehearsal. I remained at home & commenced to write a letter to Herrick in answer to his request to be free of his engagement to me during the summer — Charl came in — having sold A.B.U.Co's stock belonging to estate of J.S. He explained it & family signature — Ed. & Mary Cook then came in — at 10 o'clk — I started for 145: & brought home Annie & the two Bose girls who live upon the next block — It was after 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 8, 1889

A dull day, growing milder, with snow at nights. Got up with a headache which bothered me all day — but had none of the chest pain. After having called on Charl at his room. To studio about 10, & to work on "Dull Day on French Coast" — glazing and thin painting over the distant sea — and I think I finished it — About 12 went to U.A. & D. to see Falt & Geer. Home to lunch at 1 — found that Annie had given up & gone to bed with a headache. At 2 went to Phil. Rehearsal — Charl's Ticket — Miss Williams used the other ticket given to Annie — he spite his headache I enjoyed the music. After, went to Gradu's — then to young Avery's new galleries to talk business with him — Home at 5 — went to bed — had a nap — dinner at 6.30 — Evng, finished my letter to Herrick — mailed it — Then Annie read aloud from Rob't. Elsmere until 10.20 — To bed before 11.
