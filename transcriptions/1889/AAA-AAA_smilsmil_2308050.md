## Left Page

### April, Sunday 28, 1889.

A day of sunshine, shadow and
April showers — As usual it was
nearly o'clk. before I got through
with bathing & dressing & had
my bkfs. then with newspapers
& children until after 11 — then
out for a walk — to Fifth Ave. &
43d. St. then down to 23d. St. then
to W. Coomans Studio & saw his
portrait of Mrs. Beekman — then
to N.A.f.D. & spent some time
there — then home — dinner at 2 —
Eliz. Home & Miss Abbatt dined
with us — Annie & Eliz. went to
St. Thomas's Ch. I remained at
home — A. ret. at 5.30 & together we
went to 20 Washington Sq. N. & called
on Mrs. Ehruinger for a few minutes — then
walked up Fifth Ave. looking at the pre-
parations for the centennial celebrations —
home at — & had tea — Eve'g we went
to Walters for a few minutes — I wrote
a part of my letter to Mary — then
to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 29, 1889.

*Naval Parade — Beginning of centennial celebration —
inauguration of Washington as first Pres.*

overcast with light rain — Up
before 7 — went to butcher's & grocers before
bkfs. — About 9 Eliz. Home came & at 9.30
we went down town by 6th Ave. Elevated
R.R. — a tremendous crowd — got to 55
Bdway (Walter Cook's office) at 10.15 — most
of the Cook family got there before noon — all
on the roof — a party of 50 or more there — every
roof from which the bay could be seen was
black with people — the weather cleared beauti-
fully — the sight of the great war vessels lying
inline — decked from end to end — top mast to
deck — the many steaming craft — following
the Dispatch from which Pres. Harrison
reviewed the ships — the manning of the
yards — the salutes from heavy guns as
vessel after vessel was passed — the glitter
of sunshine — etc — etc — all combined made
one tingle with excitement — about 12.30 A &
I left Walters roof & went to Chas' window at
62 Wall St. from which we had a fine view
of the Presidential Procession as it passed up Wall
St. — at 2.30 walked up Wall St. to Bdway — could get
no further — back to William St. — then up to Liberty &
so into Bdway — thence up the middle of the street to the
Brooklyn Bridge — walked over — saw the end of the
name parade on its way back — [illegible] by cars &
then by Elevated R.R. got home at 4.30. Rested —
wrote part of my letter to Mary — Dinner — At —
rec'd telegram from Edith Wright saying that her
father had had a stroke of apoplexy — Eve'g
went to Ed'w Cook's awhile — then to Walters —
then walked up 5th Ave. to see the illumination
of Union League Club — home at 9.30.

Annie got a telegram to day saying
that James Wright had a slight stroke of
apoplexy yesterday —
