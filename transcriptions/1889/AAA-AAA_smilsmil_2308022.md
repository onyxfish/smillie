## Left Page

### March, Sunday 3, 1889

A mild, dull, damp day — [illegible] at 9 — About 11 I went to N.A. of D. got framed pf. of "Reading Homer" took it to studio & pasted a fillet inside of the passe-partout & put it under glass — then again to Academy & met there Annie & Jim — after some time looking at the pictures we went to the Century — my "Spring is Coming" looks better by daylight — Home to dinner at 2 — Charl came in & at 5 we went to look at a house in Lex Ave. near 45th St. — man in charge not there, then home — with the children about 4, a short walk. Walter came in & we talked of proposed changes in finishing the hall — a cup of tea after 7 — Evelf wrote a letter to Mr. Ehninger — to bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 4, 1889

A nasty, rainy, windy day — Got up with a headache — to studio late — after 11 went over to N.A. of D. — saw there Colman — Farrer, Chase & others — then to Bank — home to lunch & after that went to bed — up at about 5 o'clk, feeling but little better — Dinner at 6.30 — Evelf Annie Read aloud from Century — to bed about 10.30 —
