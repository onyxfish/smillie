## Left Page

### October, Saturday 19, 1889

**Ther.** 37° last night.

The Tweedys left for home — Annie & Jim went to Wilkesbarre.

A delicious Oct. day. Mild & hazy — Annie determined this m't that she w'd go without us to visit Mrs. Atherton at Wilkesbarre. I have so many things to look after here that I could not go — we had b'kf't at 8 — About 9 I drove over to Montrose R.R. Depot with the T's — Annie & Jim, left them. Ret'd home. Got Ralph & Gretta & drove to S. Montrose Creamery & got butter — Took over good coops & left it with Hunter, requesting him to send me over a couple of geese w't next week that I may make some color studies — Then home ab't 11 — Changed pf's in boards & worked some time with Coyle setting out Asparagus plants — After 1 when I had my lone dinner — After 2 when Albert drove me over to sketching place near Josh Pettus's — Taking Ralph & Gretta alone — left me & I painted from 3 to 4.30 getting a rough rubbing of my subject — Then walked home — Halfpint, his team & his man worked, but not a full day — Rafferty worked all day — Coyle got out the 60 Asparagus plants — 2 doz. Dewberries — 6 Rhubarb — Baxter worked all day — After 6, went down town — After 7 I had tea — while at tea Herrick came to talk about Returning to work — Read newspapers until 9. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 20, 1889

**Ther.** 50° last night.

Charming m't. Mild, quiet & hazy, with clouds — A real brooding Oct. day when the leaves come floating down like feathers — but the afternoon grew thick with a chilly wind from the N.W. that had a feeling of snow in it. I got up at 7.30 & had b'kf't before 8.30 — for some time about the studio with Ralph. Then went out to the berry bushes and worked for nearly two hours setting out some Black Caps, mulching Dewberries etc. etc — Then to etching Room & got the Remaining G.D. P'f's into standing press — Dinner at 1.30. At 2.30 had the horses & with Gretta & Ralph drove down to the deSchweinitz's & took Mr. & Mrs. deS. out with us for a ride — Rather a chilly one part of the way — back soon after 4 — for awhile in etching room — Then spread wood ashes about a number of Strawberry plants — Tea at 6.30. Eve't. wrote part of a letter to Kate — just as I was going to bed — about 10.30 — an alarm of fire created excitement for a time — put out before any much damage was done — building bet. Sayre's store & Griffith's, on Main S.
