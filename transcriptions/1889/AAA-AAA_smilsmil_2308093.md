## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 23. 1889.

Superb day — clear & very cool —
wind from the N.ll. Spent an
hour in the garden before bkfs —
woodchucks are making havoc
there. Myf. in studio & pre-
paring photo. solutions — Dinn
at 1 — Herrick shot two wood-
chucks on the garden wall —
one weighed 9¼ lbs. I spent
an hour or more dressing it —
then out riding with the A's
& Jack — met Prale bringing Emily
from the depot — (his myf. rec'd. a
telegram from Geo. saying that she
had started — I went down town &
sent R. for her.) Home at 6.
Eve'g. wrote to Pate, sending a
signed copy of agreement as to
sale of plates of "The Goldsmith's Daughter"

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 24. 1889.

Very pleasant day — in the garden
before bkfs. After some delay got
to work upon my plate & made
some additions, following suggestions
of Mr. Huntington — Before noon took
to bkfs. Office bkfs. containing two
prints for Pate & Co. for Copyright use.
After dinner worked until pretty
near 11 when we went out riding —
Ralph sitting on front seat beside
me — Emily on the other side of him
& Katie & Jack on back seat — In
at 6. Eve'g on Piazza — then
with newspaper until 9 — then
in dark room until near 11 de-
veloping a lot of negatives that
have accumulated, using for the
first time Hydrochinon — got some
results that seemed to be very good —
Then to bed.
