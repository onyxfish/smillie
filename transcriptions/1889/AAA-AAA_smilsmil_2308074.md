## Left Page

### June, Saturday 15. 1889.

Overcast wf. with rain before
8 — then clearing — a heavy shower
about 1 o.ck — & a very heavy thunder
shower with wind at 5 P.M. after
which it cleared quiet & warm.
I dodged about, as usual, out of
doors until 10 o'clk. then to work
on my plate — did not stop until
1 — then dinner — again to work
shortly after 2. & went out riding
at 4 — the weather then was very
threatening & we drove about
until the storm was so imminent
that we had to come in. Tea
at 6.30 — Will Parke Rode up to the
house & chatted with us for a few min.
His family just up from Scranton. At
7.30 went down town — soon back —
Evef. wrote some notes — to bed
after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 16. 1889.

A pleasant day until about 5 o'ck
when a heavy thunderstorm passed
over us with light showers following
for some time — very dark, clouded
night. Bkf. between 8 & 9 — much
of the mf. spent in clearing up the
mess of things that have been piled
into studio bay window. After noon
I wrote until dinner time at 2
— after that nailed wire netting
over some spaces on upper piazza
through which there was danger of
the children's climbing — Wrote
again before tea & in the eve'g.
At 10 o'clk. went to P.O. Taking
a dozen letters — I have written
to Geo. & Chas., to Pate & Co., to Daut
Huntington — to Brooks Bros. — to
Tom Shearman, with copy of stump
speech delivered by him in '56. I
found it this P.m.
