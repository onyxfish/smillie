## Left Page

### August, Saturday 24, 1889

The hot dry time, so long expected,
seems to have come at last — Clear
& hot. Got up with a headache which
hung to me all day — in spite of it
I commenced a Poppy Study in crayon
& worked upon it in the afternoon.
Herrick fixed posts & vines on the
berry vines & Smith painted the posts.
I did not go riding — too hot & dusty
& too much headache. I spent some
time tying up vines — went down town
after tea & ret'd about 8 — spent the
evef with new Harper & papers —
to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 25, 1889

A perfect day — warm sunshine &
cool N.W. breeze — we gave ourselves up
to the enjoyment of it & did nothing
but loaf — Albert had permission
to be away for the day & Herrick
took his place — He harnessed the
horses & at 5.30 Annie, Jim & I
rode for an hour — then in and
had tea — most of the evef. I
spent in the dark room — to bed
at 10.30.
