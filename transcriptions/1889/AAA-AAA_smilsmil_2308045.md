## Left Page

### April, Thursday 18. 1889.

Mild, overcast day — Before going to
studio went to Bank for Annie, then
to Com. on Military & Civic Procession, Fifth
Ave. Hotel, to see if it were possible to
get some seats in any of the stands to
see the Procession on 30th ins.; none to be had
made application for 2 seats on stand to be
put up on Union Sq. Then to studio & began
sketch for oil — Atlantic City material —
Home to lunch at 1 — after that went to
Avery's gallery to see my "Cliffs" there —
then to studio & smeared transparent
color with Funks over my sketch — Home
after 6 — D. & Nelie Adams at the house
— dinner — Evn'g went to Havemeyer's
34 E. 36 & visited Colman & his wife for
half an hour — he has been exceedingly ill —
Home at 9 — then Annie, Nelie & D. went to
Walter's — Charles came in & stayed
until after 10. After 11 when I got
to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 19. 1889.

A very warm, clear day. Ther. got
up to 75° in the shade. I got to studio
soon after 10 & started on the painting
in oil of my sketch at Atlantic City.
I stuck closely to it until after 1 —
then home to lunch & back about 2.30
Annie & Jim went to Barnum's Circus.
I painted until after 5 when I got
my sketch done — It seems to me not
half bad — I got home at 6 very
tired — dressed myself & at 6.45
Annie, Nelie, D. A. & I went to Ed.
Cole's & dined there with W. & Mrs.
Sallstedt — He (S.) was in a very
jolly humor & we had a pleasant
time — Home at 10 o'clk & then to bed.
