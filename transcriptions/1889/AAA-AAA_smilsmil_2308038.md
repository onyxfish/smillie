## Left Page

### April, Thursday 4, 1889.

A bright day. Got to studio at a little after 10 — met at the door Lauckner, the plate publisher — &
he soon informed me that his errand was to cancel the agreement that has been between us, that I was to etch a Ranger picture for him — I could not object, as I have far exceeded the limits of the time set &
have not yet commenced the plate. Then a young lady representing the Indianapolis Art Assn called — then Mr. Mills, for the Baltimore Charcoal Club called — it was noon before I got over to 2? Nat. Bk to attend to some of Annie's business — then to Reichard's & had a talk with him about etching Hovenden's "In the hands of the Enemy" — It was 1.40 when I got home to lunch. Mr. Ham & married daughter called — also Mary Sprague & Mr. Ernest Wright — after 3 I went to Brook's Bros & got a suit of clothes — then to Kurtz's & got photo prints of "Spring is coming" — then to Am Art galleries & saw the old Spanish pictures — To studio at late 6 — saw Geo. & Charl — they walked up to 38th St. with me — Dinner at 6.30. Every. Walter came in & stayed until near 10. we talked of plans to improve the hallway — he is to make some plans — to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 5, 1889.

*To Atlantic City.*
*The Dennis. Rooms 114–115.*

Bright mf — got up feeling better. After bkfs. went to Poole's & ordered a carriage for 11.30 — then to Brooks Bros. & got my suit of clothes — then to Fanny N. message from Annie — then to studio — Home at 10.30 — dressed & packed in an hour & then Annie & I, with Gretta & the children, started for this place. Got here at 4.30 — a tiresome ride — at depot, Atlantic City, met Jas. Wright & we all went to the Shelbourne where J.W. is to stay — no room for us — so we came here, just across the street & have two small rooms — 4th floor — looking out over the sea — The afternoon, overcast; bleak & chilly — had supper at 6.30 and then got the children to bed — Later we went over to the Shelbourne & saw Jas. Wright — Mr. Tyson & their sister Miss Davis — spent a pleasant evening until after 9 — then back — to bed about 10.
