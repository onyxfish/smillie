## Left Page

### January, Sunday 20, 1889

A brilliant & severely cold wt. The ther. fell to 16°. The afternoon was lazy growing milder with a promise of snow.
At 10'5 Annie & I walked down to the East Ch., in the green & heard the Rev. T. T. Munger preach upon Moses'
request to Abraham that some one from the dead he sent to preach to & warn the living. It was a disappointment to me being more conventional & strictly orthodox than I had expected — altho' he did say that he did not consider faith in the miracles of Christ as essential.
After service a walk around the College buildings & then home — very cold —
dinner at 2 — in front of a fire until 4.30.
Then Jim & I went out & had a brisk tramp of about an hour — on getting home found that Annie & Katie were at Jennie Young's — as it was getting dark & snowing I went there after them — got back at 6.15 — tea — Eve'g again in front of the fire, reading until near 10 — then to bed —
a wild night of storm much of which I lay awake to listen to & wonder if we were to be storm bound to morrow — at times the wind blew very heavily — toward morning there were torrents of rain — in the mts when I got up everything was a sea of slush.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 21, 1889

*Home again.*

Up a little before 7 — the rain had ceased — dressed as quickly as possible — got key to observatory — went there & telephoned into town, after some trouble getting a hackman to promise to come out — then back to the house & had bkft. At 8'5 the hack came — we left at 8.30 — got to depot at at 8.50 & had to wait until 9.35 for the train which came along on time. Annie & Jim got off at Bridgeport, as sunshine was breaking thro' the clouds, to make a visit between trains — horrible slush — I went on getting into the depot on time. 11.40. &
to the house at 11.45 finding all well.
Lunch at 1 — to studio at 2'5. Found flat proof of "Passing Herd" from Kurtz — touched it with belt. & opaque white & then took it to K — stopped at the N.A. of D. & saw Farrer & Dielwan — then to studio — got out canvas 20×30 & commenced to Charcoe my New Haven sketch "a promise of Spring" — Home soon after 5 — found Annie & Jim there — dinner at 6.30 — Evening read newspapers & looked over English papers. Charl came in for a few min — to bed at about 10.
