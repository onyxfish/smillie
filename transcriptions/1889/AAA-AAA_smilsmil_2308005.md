## Left Page

### January, Monday 28, 1889

A raw, cold & overcast day.
To studio before 10 & painted
on "Headwaters" getting much more
light, color & breadth into it —
To lunch & back as usual —
Worked until near 5 — Then
walked up Fifth Ave. to Central
Park — on my way back stopped
at Dr. Yale's — found him in &
he gave some attention to my heart
where there has been more or less
dull pain for some days past — He
gives me to understand that it is
Rheumatic — It was after 6.30
when I got home to dinner —
Even't. before the fire reading —
Later A. read aloud from Rob-
ilsmene — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 29, 1889

Clear, but cold, windy & dirty —
All the N.Y. Street surface car lines,
except 3rd Ave. & 23rd St. lines "tied up" —
The most extensive strike yet known
here. To studio before 10 & to work
on "A Quiet Day on the French Coast".
Painted in the sky, softening many of
the edges & repainted the sea, making
it very much more delicate, also
broader in effect. Home to lunch &
back as usual — Afternoon, worked
as long as I could see — Then a
walk & home before 6 — dinner
about 8 Annie & I walked down
to 36 E. 22nd St. & made our wedding
call on Mr. & Mrs. H.f. Ferguson — At
9 we left & went to Walter Cook's
& were with him until after 10 —
then home & to bed.
