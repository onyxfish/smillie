## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 3, 1889

Cool, breezy & very dry. The men got to work at digging
& made a horrifying amount of dirt. I kept very quiet all
day, my head troubling me very much so that I could not work
or read or write. The horses were at work upon our road
hauling dirt & stone — we did not go riding — Well I felt
miserably sick & spent most of the time on the bed. — I
gave a little attention to the occultation of Jupiter & the moon
& got to bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 4, 1889

No change in the weather, dust getting thicker & thicker — the day
quite cool. Not much improvement in my condition — I managed
to write some short notes but most of the day was spent hanging about
& watching the men at their work.
Coyle was obliged to give up work in the [illegible] — could get no one to
take his place — Well, spent some time in dark room —
To bed at 10.30.
