## Left Page

### March, Monday 25, 1889

A bright, pleasant m't. with a change to slightly cloudy in the afternoon — After bkf' — waited for some time for Louis Tiffany who promised to come in to look at the hall, with a view to improvement & decoration, but he came not. To studio a little after 10 & commenced work on the face of Goldsmith's Daughter with dry point — home to lunch & back & worked until after 5 — after 6 when I got home — dinner — then dressed & Annie & I went across the way to our neighbor Smith's to attend the meeting of an Adirondack book club who are arranging to put up a small blot at Keene Heights — found a pleasant little company of people, all interested in Keene — There were recitations & scotch poetry by Mr. — some singing & some piano playing — we got home before 11 & then to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 26, 1889

A fickle day — sunshine in the m't. then the wind blew from the east & the sky was laden with sure promise of rain — then the wind changed to the West with clear skies — Got to studio at 10 — worked all day with dry point on G.S.'s face & neck — begin to feel that I have undertaken a more difficult piece of work than I thought — Home to lunch & back as usual — met Mary & Florence Tweedy on my way down — Continued my dry point work until after 5 — then a short walk & home soon after 6 — dinner — Evett Charl came in for a little while — I finished my letter to Kate & then wrote one to Herrick — mailed them at 10 — to bed soon after.
