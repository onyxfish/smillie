## Left Page

### December, Friday 20, 1889

A nasty, dull day with very heavy fog in the afternoon — Got to studio before 10 — worked for awhile finishing drawing of steps by well at Montrose (working plan) Then I mounted two or three small dry-point pfs and home to dinner soon after 1. It was near 3 when I got back to studio — so dark that I could do but little — Home at 5.30 — "Doll dressing". Emma & Mary Ireland. Emily & Mary (ned) Cook — Rosalie & Helen Rutter & Mr. Coleman were there — I spent some time carving cold turkey & beef tongue — then dressed — High tea at 7 — Early evef. I completed my letter to Herrick & mailed it — Re°. Cook & [illegible] Valentine — then Saml. Coleman came. The dressing was kept up until about 10 — It was after 11 when we got to bed — Annie very much used up by a severe cold in her head.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 21, 1889

A bright & pleasant day — got to Studio before 10 — Champney called — It was near 11 before I was able to go to Coleman's rooms at the Warrington to see him about a Jap. jug for my big Dry-points. While there saw Foster Higgins & his wife — After leaving C — went to Keppel's with proofs of my Mezzotint "Hollyhocks" — drypoint "Hollyhocks" + 6×6 dry-point "Poppies". After some dickering I sold him the dry-point pfs & the mezzotint plate for $120.00 together with a choice pf. from the mezzotint for $8.00 additional. Went to Dunlap's & bought a hat — then to 2°. Nat. Bk. & home to dinner abt 1 o'clk — meeting Annie & the boys on Park Ave. Back to studio before 3 — fussed with pfs for Keppel as long as I could see — At the house I signed 15 pfs for Pate & Co. Home at 6 — tea at 6.30 — Evef. I read awhile — Annie read aloud for a little while — I wrote a note or two & got to bed at about 11.
