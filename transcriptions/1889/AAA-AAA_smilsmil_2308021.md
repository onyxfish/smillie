## Left Page

### March, Friday 1, 1889.

A delightful day — Annie said to me
the little paper that she has prep and
to read at the N.Y. League of [illegible]
women this mt'f. I got to studio about
10 o'clk. & spent the m'f making drawing
for a closet for Montrose. Starting Room.
Home to lunch soon after — Annie
did not come home to lunch. — I
played with the children for awhile
— then, in preparation for this ev'f's
trials — I went to bed for an hour —
After that read libretto — Dinner at 6.30
at 8, Annie & I were in our seats at
the Met. Opera House & there we stayed
until 12 seeing & hearing the "Meistersinger"
It was very well given — the latter por-
tion being exceedingly fine to me —
Emma & Mary Dreland & Margery Sprig
sat near us — home at 12.30 — to bed
just before 1 o'clk.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 2. 1889.

Dull, overcast, mild, threatening rain —
got to studio soon after 10 — wrote two or
three notes — then spent some time in
getting new big frame onto easel and
my painting "A Normandy Barnyard"
into it — then studied it & thought of
the sort of painting that I would like
to make of it — Before 12 went to N.A.
& D. with P.f. of "Heading Homer" — then to
Kurtz's — then to 2°. Nat. Bk. then again
to N.A. & D's — then to studio & home to lunch
at 1.30 — after that, feeling used up, I
lay down & had a nap — it was after
4 when I got to studio — wrote a
couple of notes — home at 6 — dinner
ev'f. at 8.30 went to the Century —
my "Spring is Coming" looks like whitewash
by gas light & among other pictures & my
Studdle Woods looks like a coal hole.
However, I had a pleasant ev'f, with
my friends — a few agreeable words with
Ex minister E. J. Phelps & at about 12
E. L. Henry, [illegible] & I walked up
town.
