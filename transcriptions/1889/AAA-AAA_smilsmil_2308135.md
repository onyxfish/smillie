## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 15, 1889

**Ther.** 33° last night

Brilliant, cloudless, breezy, cool
day — After spending some time
out of doors with the men I got
to work in the studio & the rest of
the mf was spent in preparing
"Hollyhock" proof for W. Koehler —
getting it into 8vo. pht for him &
writing letter to accompany it —
Dinner at 1 — At 2.5 Annies. Belle
& Mary I — Ralph & I started for
Fire Hill — Glorious ride & view —
but pretty cold before we got home
at 5.30 — Tea — then evef. part
of which was spent in improving
conversation wound up with a
game of "Sloto." To bed at 11.

Williams, Tom Rafferty & White
worked all day in Garden ditch.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 16, 1889

**Ther.** 31° last night

A finer day than yesterday, being
quite as brilliant & much milder
out with the men, until near 11 —
then worked until 1 oclk. dawbing
pfs of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" and
getting them into my new Standing
press — then dinner & at 2.30
we all got off for a drive over to
Pt. Norris' place where we had
the view under most beautiful
light; the horses having been at
work in the garden I had to get
Halpin & his Team to take their
place — Got home a few m.
after 5 oclk — Tea — I welf wrote
notes to Koehler, S. P. Avery Jun — and
& F. Donnelley & Co. Then to P. O.
