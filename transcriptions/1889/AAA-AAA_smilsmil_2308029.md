## Left Page

### March, Sunday 17, 1889

Overcast day & rather cool —
I spent the day in the laziest
possible way in-doors — Playing
with the children — Reading the
newspaper & sleeping — Dinner
at 2 — It was nearly 5 when
I went to the Victoria & called
upon Mrs. Ehninger — Spent an
hour with her. Found her terribly
broken, but through it all she
shows great nerve & spirit.
Walked up to 50th St. & then home
at 7 — Had a cup of tea —
Gretta's night out — Spent some
time getting Ralph to sleep —
Then wrote a letter to Mary.
It was 11 o'clk when I got to
bed.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 18, 1889

Mild — pleasant m'g. — later, the day
was overcast. Soon after 9 o'clk. walked
down to studio with Annie — she went
to aunt's Rehearsal of her Choral Club.
I finished the drawings for studio's closet
at Montrose — About 11 — Renian, Forlet's
agent came in — It was near 12 when
I went out to buy the hardware
finish for closet — It was 1.30 when
I got home to lunch & 3 when I
got back to studio's — after a little
work I again went out — Took drawings
to Adams Exp. & sent them to Herrick —
then went to Jno. Waring's in 16th St. — got
check from him $19.82 for some etching
p[l]s. It was 6.30 when I got home
to dinner. Evey wrote to Herrick
+ read "Uncle Remus" — To bed
soon after 10.
