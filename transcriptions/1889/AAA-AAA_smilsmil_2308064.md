## Left Page

### May, Sunday 26. 1889.

Rain last night — Chilly overcast day —
Up at 7.30 — After 10 went to studio
— there until about 12.30 getting
things together for packing — on my
way home stopped at F. H. Smith's
150 E. 34th St. & spent more than an
hour with him — I went to make
some enquiries about Geo. Wharton
Edwards — Charl called at the
house about 2 o'clk — Geo. & Emily
Cook dined with us at 2 — All
of the domestics being out we had
a domestic time with the children.
At 5, with Jine, I went up to Dr.
Yale's — the Dr. gave me 3 doz. sheets
of fine Imp. Jap. Paper — Home at
6.30 — a cup of tea. Evy wrote
a short letter to Mary & got to
bed at 10.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 27. 1889.

A cold & very disagreeable day —
rain fell heavily all the morning —
Got to studio soon after 9 — So cold
that I had to make a fire — Took
down all the curtains & had a dirty
time — Home to lunch at 1.15 —
back at 2.30 — & worked until 6
when I got my big box and a
smaller one practically packed
— then home at 6.30 very tired —
dinner — At 8.30 Annie & I went
to Walter's to have a little further
conversation with him concerning
proposed alterations in hallway
of house — I find him so opposed
to some of my ideas that very reluctant
ly I am obliged to give up making
any change — In the main I like
his plans very much but he insists
upon painted pine doors where I wish
for butternut — he will not approve of
paint upon the walls — he wishes to
sheath them with red wood —
To bed at about 10.30.
