## Left Page

### November, Saturday 30. 1889

*Home again.*

Bright, breezy mt[?]. with a light snow flurry about 11 o'clk. It was near 10 when Annie, Frank, Roger, Jim & I started out for a walk. We went directly to the foot of East Road & then by foot path to the top of it. While there the snow flurry passed over us — then we descended by another foot path to the foot bridge & path that brought us out on Whitney Ave. not far from the dam & Whitney lake — back home at 12. I lay down & had a little nap. Dinner at 1.30 — Then we sat & waited for the carriage that came at 3.25 to depot — 3.50 train to W.Y. got in very comfortably at 6 — tea soon after. Eve'g. I walked down to grocery & back — looking over back Nos. of Bond. Punch until near 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 1. 1889

Brilliant & beautiful day. Up at about 9. A little before 11 Annie, Jim & I took 3d Ave. Elevated R.R., went to 125th St. & thence by Cable Car over to 182d St. to the Entrance of the new Washington Bridge. We walked across it & down the other side of the river to High Bridge & across that back to the city side & then on down to 145th St. where we took 6th Ave. Elevated RR down to 42d St. & home at 2 — Dinner. Almost immediately after it I went to bed with a splitting headache. Gel came in but I did not see him — also young Carwalt. I got up at 6 o'clk, but took no tea. I hung about, looking over illustrated newspapers until near 11 o'clk. Then to bed.
