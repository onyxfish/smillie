## Left Page

### February, Friday 15, 1889

Delightful clear, mild day — a weather breeder no doubt — Got to
studio about 10 & to work at proving
Dry Point — Some of the [illegible] are
bright & strong, but with every care
I can not produce uniform prints
Home & back to studio as usual
— worked until after 4 — washed
up — rested — after 6 when I
got home — dinner — Eve'g
Susine, the Adams girls and
I went to the Broadway Theatre
& saw "Little Lord Fauntleroy" a
clever piece & acting by the child
Elsie Lessie — Home soon after
11 & right to bed —

Charl called at the Studio in
the afternoon.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 16, 1889

A dull day — Rain commenced about
noon & before 5 o'clk. in poured down
& continued through the eve'g. Got
to studio before 10 & again to work at
proving Dry Point & again had
much trouble — could not get all of
the elements of brilliancy, tone, depth
& velvet that I am sure could be got
by a good Prover — About noon, went
in & saw Louis Tiffany for a few minutes —
Home about 1 30 & back at 2.30. Printed
until 4 — then cleaned up — after which
I rested until near 6 — then home.
Eve'g with newspaper & chatting
To bed about 10.

A box from Mr. Ehninger, contain-
ing one of E's tassels was delivered
at the house this afternoon.

The desks etc for Mr. Walton's
school were moved in to-day.
