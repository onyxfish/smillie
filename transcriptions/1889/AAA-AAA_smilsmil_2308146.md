## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 6, 1889

Bright, pleasant day — my domestic
duties kept me so late that it was
after 10 when I got to studio. Gave
some fixing up there. I got to work
up on the signing of a lot of plt. of
the Goldsmith's Daughter for Bates &
to this I gave the bulk of the day.
Before I went to lunch I had a
pleasant call from Chas. Parsons &
in the afternoon I had a long call
from Dr. Yale. I got home at about
6. We had dinner at 6.30. Kitche
Cook, Katie & Mollie Cook & Churchill
Carnalt dined with us. At 8 o'cl
we all walked over to the Met. Opera
House, where Carnalt left us, the rest
of us going in to the Testimonial Concert
to Theo. Thomas. Geo. & Emily Cook walked
home with us at 10. 30 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 7, 1889

A delightful day — cloudless & cool. Did
not get to studio until nearly 10 o'clk —
most of the remaining time of the m[or]n
was spent going over the latest batch
of Goldsmith Daughter plt. sent up by
Bates & Co. Home promptly at 1 — lunch
& at 1.45 took Jim up to Dr. Yale's where
I took him to be looked over. We
were there until after 3 — then home.
It was near 4 when I got to the
studio. Home before 6 — A. & I got
to Edw. C's a little after 6 — dinner
was served promptly — the Adams
girls & Katchen Cook there — Evel[?], they
went out with Geo. & Emily Cook while
Edw., Neary, Annie & I went to Jos.
Morse's, where there was a sort of
a Unitarian peoples meeting —
a pleasant social gathering —
there until about 11 — then home
