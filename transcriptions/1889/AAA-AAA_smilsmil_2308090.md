## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 17. 1889.

A breezy, pleasant day — out among the peas
& in the garden until near 11 — then to work
in studio. Annie brought me a letter from
Mr. Huntington — very laudatory of my plate
& very gratifying — I at once commenced a
letter to the Pates — Dinner at 1 — after that
wrote until 3 when the carriage came
to the door — Annie — Ralph — Miss J & I
went — Jim has been ailing all day &
consented to stay at home. We had a
very enjoyable drive — home before 6.
Tea soon after — Eve'g wrote to Mr. Hunt-
ington & to Kimmel & Voigt — took letters
to the P.O. at 10 — then home & to bed
before 11.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 18. 1889.

A pleasant day, altho' with indications
of a change of weather — much of the m'f
spent out of doors — when I got to work I
made a pencil reduction of the flagon
used by the Goldsmith's Daughter — made
a tracing of it, laid a ground, transferred
the sketch — drew it in & varnished
the margin about it — In the after-
noon we went out riding after 4 &
did not get home until 6.20, being
made very agreeable (the ride) by
a delightfully accommodating mass of
cloud that moved so slowly as to
keep us in shadow most of the time
Eve'g. until near ten o'clk. we sat
upon the piazza — It was but a
little before 11 when we got to bed
To-day Jim has seemed to be much
better.
