## Left Page

### June, Sunday 23, 1889

Subperb day — [illegible] at 8.30 — after
that, meet after 12 — out of doors about
the place — all hands enjoying the
delightful weather — Ralph evidently
much better but horribly touchy —
Before dinner wrote a letter to Padtke
& Danckner in answer to theirs of
the 20th telling them that I had sold
my Huntington plate — Dinner at 2
at which we had our first mess of
peas & a large dish full of remarkable
strawberries from our new bed — After
dinner wrote to Sec'y Thatcher Adams
& to Dr. Isaac Leet thanking them for
my election as life member of [illegible] for
Instruction of Deaf & Dumb — Then out with
Annie. Met Bostwick the grocer who came
in & went over the grounds with me —
Tea at 7 — Every Annie, Mary & I
sat before a kindle fire and chatted
of purely domestic matters until 10 —

## Right Page

### June, Monday 24, 1889

A quiet, overcast day. Got up hoping
to take proofs of my big plate — got
my stone hat & paper ready but my
head ached so that I gave up the
attempt & spent the day out of doors
with Herrick, most of the time putting
up the wire netting upon which they
run — Some time among the straw-
berries — In the afternoon Annie,
Mary & the children went over to the
lake to amuse themselves taking
poles, lines etc — At 5 I drove over
with carriage & crowded the whole party
in & had a short ride home & through
town — Eve'g. being unfit for
letter writing Annie wrote for me
to Mary — Rec'd from Charl letter
from Mary & Kate — To bed at 10
& passed a very uncomfortable
night.
