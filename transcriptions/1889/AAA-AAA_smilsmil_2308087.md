## Left Page

### July, Thursday 11, 1889.

Rain about 7 A.m. misty mt
with a number of mizzling
showers that continued until
about noon — then the weather
cleared delightfully. Again
a day in which but little was
accomplished. I "dodged" in the
studio, turning over a lot of very
old cuttings, sketches & writing of
my early years, many with dates
written upon them by Father, also,
some more work on photographic
solutions. I went down town before
4 — on my way back stopped at Mr.
Saul. Sayres & saw Mr. Foster — Mr. Chap.
Sayre & Jessie Comstock Edgar — At 4.30
with Annie & the children drove over
to depot of little R.R. to meet Miss Clara
[illegible] coming from Bethlehem Pa —
the train being late we rode about
until almost 6, when it came. Then
home — tea — Evelf. Mr. de Schwerintz
& Mr. de S's two sisters called — I again
went down town to get some Carb. of
Soda but could not find what I wanted.
Back at 9 — on piazza for a time —
To bed at

## Right Page

### July, Friday 12, 1889.

Hot day with pleasant breeze. I
got up out of sorts — discomfort in
stomach to such extent that I
spent a good part of the morning
in my bed & was generally useless
in the afternoon, lounging about,
unable to do anything that required
mental or physical exertion. Spent
some time looking over the old
cuttings & sketches that interested me
yesterday. About 5 o'clk we went
off riding. Annie — Miss 7 — Jim &
self — back at 6.30 — then I drove
down town & met Mr. & Mrs. B. de Schweirintz & his two sisters, Mr. Bruner & Miss
del. & brought them back to the house
where we had tea after which we
spent the evelf. on piazza until about
9.30 — full moon — glorious night —
It was about 11 when I got to
bed.
