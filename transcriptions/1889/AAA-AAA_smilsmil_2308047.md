## Left Page

### April, Monday 22. 1889.

Still bright but very much cooler.
Soon after 9 I went to the Avery
Galleries with my palette & paints
& for an hour worked on parts of the
foreground of "Cliffs of Normandy". On
my way to studio stopped to see the
Castet [illegible]. Got to studio soon after
11 o'clk — worked on Atlantic city
sketch, which I have christened
"our coast offences", until 1 — then
home to lunch — Nelie & St. went
home on 3 o'clk train — then I
went to studio & painted until
near 6 — Mr Knudsen made a
call — Dinner at 6.30 — Evelf
looked over English newspapers
& then wrote part of my letter to
Kate. George came in for half
an hour or so.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 23. 1889.

Still very clear & cool — Moran &
Higgins' men at work on the roof —
It was 10.30 when I got to the studio
& I set to work on a panel ×
drawing a subject from a pencil
sketch made at Hagelbook — set
palette & commenced to paint — Home
to lunch at 1.30, meeting Eliz. Howe
& walking 2 or 3 blocks with her — back
to studio before 3 & painted until
after 5, finishing my sketch — I do
not yet know whether what I have
done is worth the struggle or not. I
fixed the [illegible] "What o'clk is it?" in frame
wedding present for Silean [illegible] —
Home at 6.30 — Dinner — Evelf. Annie
went up to Randolph Reception in
59 St. with Ed[?] & Mary while I
stayed at home, & finished my letter
to Kate. Annie came in before
11 o'clk & then we went to bed.
