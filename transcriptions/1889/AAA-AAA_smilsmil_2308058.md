## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 14, 1889.

Wet & muggy mt — cleared before
noon — some clear, warm weather
then again cloudy — To studio
before 10 — worked for awhile with
crayon on enlarged photo of Ed.s
Cook from a dag — taken 35 years
ago — Then worked on big plate.
Home to lunch — back — worked
on 2d photo of [illegible] Cook — & then
with burnisher & scraper on big
plate — Home soon after 6 —
dinner — Eve[ning]. Charl came in
very much disgruntled because I
had sold "Spring is Coming" — said he
wanted it — I was surprised to find
him so much in earnest — I
wrote letter to Mary — Mailed
it before 11 & then to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 15, 1889.

Delightful day — To studio about 10. Set
palette & worked on small painting
ret[ouched] from M.A. f[or] [illegible] 24×7½" "Early Spring"
By 12 o'clk. had improved it very much.
Before going to studio went to N.A.
to see Galt to see if I could back out
of sale of "Spring is Coming" — Too late — found
that W.H. Havemeyer of 112 Wall St. is the
purchaser — If he is one of the rich sugar
men I think it is contemptible of him so
to jew a poor devil painter — E[ve]. Mulford
called at studio before 1 — Home very
much used up by a head ache — after
a light lunch went to bed for a couple
of hours. Annie, Gretea & the children
went up to Central Park — I got up &
wrote some letters — After 5. went to Studio
cleaned palette & wrote a note to Charl.
Home to dinner at 6 40 — Eve[ning] A. &
I went to Ed.s Cookes — met him & Mary
in the street — Showed them the enlarged
photo from E's dag — much pleased.
I wrote a letter to Herrick — posted
it — To bed about 11.
