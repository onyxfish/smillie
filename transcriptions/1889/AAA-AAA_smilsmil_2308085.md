## Left Page

### July, Sunday 7, 1889

Hotter weather — cloudless — with
a pleasant little breeze from west.
B[k]p soon after 8 — I spent the
m[f]. about most of the time with
the children — Dinner about
1.30 — accomplished nothing
between that & 4 o'clk. when
Annie, Mary, Tom, Jim & I went
driving — altho' not: we found
it very pleasant, sauntering thro'
the woods, waiting while the
boys gathered rasberries — Got
home at 6.30 — Tea — Evelf J
was engaged with flower beds,
horses etc untils after 8 — then
for an hour or more on piazza
— then wrote notes to Dav. Huntington
& McHale & J.M. Campbell — Took
them to P.O. at 10.45

## Right Page

### July, Monday 8, 1889

And yet hotter, but with a delic-
iously tempering breeze from the N.W.
Ralph's birth-day — celebrated by a
birth-day cake with two candles — sent up
after dessert at dinner time — He enjoyed
it very much — assisted at cutting the cake
& carried the pieces about to the different
ones at the table. I accomplished very
little during the m[f]., that little was
in the direction of getting two [k]p of "The
Goldsmith's Daughter" ready to send to Pate
& Co. I got the [k]p off by 4[f]p. at 4 P.M.
Some lolling under trees north of the house.
All hands assisted — After tea time Ralph
was sick — his stomach was thoroughly cleared.
Then he seemed to feel better — About 7
Annie, Mary, Edna & I went out riding —
enjoyed sunset, twilight & moonlight — In
at 8.30 — then I wrote letter to Mary — also
to Chas., enclosing one rec'd from M — this m[f].
— also to Pate & Co. Took letters to P.O.
before 11 — To bed at about 11.30.
Glorious moonlight.
