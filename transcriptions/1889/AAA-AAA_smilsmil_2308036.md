## Left Page

### March, Sunday 31, 1889

A nasty day of snow, slush &
mizzle — did not go out of the
house until 5 p.m. — The rest
spent with the youngsters & news-
papers — Ralph seems to be much
better to-day — a lower tooth has
come through — Mr. Valentine came
took dinner with us — In
the afternoon, late, the weather
having improved a little, I went
to Geo's & there met Charl — we
walked up town together — at
6.30 — had a cup of tea — then
for a time wrote on Mary's letters.
It was about 11 when I got
to bed —

## Right Page

### April, Monday 1, 1889

A horrible day of wind and
rains, so I was felled, I, being
in bed all day, used up with
headache & general wretchedness
the result of cold, Knew nothing
of the weather — I was not
able to get up when evening
came — Charl came in for
awhile in the even[ing].
