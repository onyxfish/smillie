## Left Page

### December, Monday 16, 1889

Struggling attempt at sunshine all day — just enough to keep the snow melting & the air very raw. I continue to improve very slowly. Saml. Coleman came up at about 10.30 & made a call — Dinner at 1 o'clk. & between 2 & 3 o'clk. I prepared & went out on Park Ave. & walked up & down on the sunny side for awhile — the first time that I have been out for nine days. Then took a nap. Tea at 6.30. Eveft. wrote a letter to Kate.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 17, 1889

A nasty, dark, wet day — Did not dare to go out of doors, so, for another day, I was a prisoner. I lounged about & read — & played with Ralph at about 1 o'clk. Edith Wright and her father arr— from New Haven where they have been on a short visit to D. Adams. Dinner at 1.30 — for a time chatting all together — then I escaped from myself for a short time by taking a nap. Annie & I changed our sleeping quarters to second story back — Tea at 6.30 — Eveft. Annie went to her singing club — I stayed at home & played Euchre with Edith & her father until 10 — then they went off to bed & I waited for A. for whom I had sent a messenger boy. She arr'd at 10.45. Then to bed.
