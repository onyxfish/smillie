## Left Page

### November, Sunday 10, 1889

Dull morning but the weather gradually cleared meet, at night
it was very pleasant. Up late —
until after 11 o'clk. loafing, with
newspaper. Then Annie, Jim &
went to Walter Cook's for a little whi
after that I walked with Jim until
near 6 o'clk. Dinner at 2. Charl
came in after that — then Ge° & two
of his boys — & Katie Adams. I
left them at about 4 o'clk. & walked
up to Central Park — It was near
6 when I returned. Addie Dunnin
Hawes Wilson & Emma I — were there
to tea, by invitation. Will & Fann
K — came in later — pleasant even
until near 9 o'clk. when I took
Addie to R.R. at 6th Ave. & 42nd St. On
my return to the house found Oscar
Ireland. All left at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 11, 1889

Disagreeable day — cloudy & Raw with
some attempts to Rain — About 10 o'clk
I got to W. Huntington's with my special
G.D. plt. He was engaged for a time —
I waited & chatted with his son. W.
H. came & signed the plt. for me & I
got to studio before 12 o'clk. Home
at 1 — after lunch I went to Kimmel
& Voigt's & had a protracted conference
with them concerning printing and
paper for the G.D. plate, without
being able to learn much — then
to Sellers — 17 Dey St. & bought two small
scrapers, one for the Nat. museum at
Washington, the other for myself. Got to
studio before 5 & home at 6. Eve'g at
about 8 ¾ I went to Louis Tiffany's 72nd
St. & Mad Ave. & met a number of the
Artists, invited to help him wind up his
Jap. Chrysanthemum Show — most
of them were young — The entertainment
was a feast of season & then a Champagne
supper — It was about midnight
when I got home.
