## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 27, 1889

Overcast most of the day with raw wind from east — some sunshine in the afternoon. Got to studio soon after 10 & to work with dry point on J. D's face. After 12 W. Halsell of Boston came in & we talked of having a photo-gravure plate made from one of my paintings — Home to lunch at 1.30 — It was 3 when I got back to studio — worked for an hour on plate — then to Grady's to get my bill — saw young Grady who told me that the books were "at the factory" — a very strange story — went to the shop in 42d St. & saw some of the men & learned that the business was to be closed up on May 1st. — then went to Bolton Jones's studio & had a pleasant call there — found Jones out — then home at 6 — met Mary Cook on the stoop — found Ralph very sick — fever & croup like cough — I was very much worried — Annie had sent for Yale — dinner at 6.30 — Every wrote some notes — Treas. of Elley Co — Wy Douglass & Jno. Moran — Dr. Y. came in at about 8.30 & reassured us about Ralph — at 9 I went with him to his house & got some medicines — home before 10 — to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 28, 1889

A disagreeable day — overcast, showers at noon continuing until about 4 o'clk. First varnishing day at N.A. of D. I was detained — so that it was near 11 o'clk. when I got to the Academy — concluded that my paintings would not be bettered by oil or varnish. The largest one, "Spring is Coming", is well hung & looks as well as I had expected — I was much interested in Hovenden's painting "In the Hands of the Enemy" & talked with him about etching it. He went to my studio with me & looked over [illegible] of my work — I got home to lunch at 1.30 & to studio before 3 & worked on "J. D." until 5 — then washed up — & home. Ralph has continued to improve all day, but both he & Jim are bunged up with colds in the head with hoarseness, coughing and sneezing — Dinner at 6.30 — Every took Annie to Dr. Wier's at 8.30 — annual meeting of Choral Club — then went to a picture sale in [illegible] cottages 5th ave. gallery — after a short time home — at 10.30 went for a — 37 W. 33d & after long waiting was dragged into the parlor where, being entirely unprepared, I felt very uncomfortable — It was after 11.30 when we left — and just midnight when we got to bed.
