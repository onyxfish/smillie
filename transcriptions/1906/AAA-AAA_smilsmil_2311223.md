## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 26, 1906

**Weather:** High so. westerly wind all day  **Temp:** [illegible]

High so. westerly wind all day — The sky to the north was clear but a vapory skim was overhead \+ late in the day clouds came down over mt. Tpfs to the southward \+ over the Giant. Up early \+ had a good scrub altho' I did not feel in first rate condition Bkfs. at 9 — then wrote to D. Gerster \+ to Jim — went to P.O. at 1 o'clk — Dinner at 2 — feeling much out of sorts — went to bed soon after \+ remained there till near 6 — Tea at about 7 — then I learned that R. felt out of sorts — had a headache — Eve'g. about 9 he said he felt better \+ then we finished a Jack London book of short stories \+ to bed before 11 —

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 27, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced about 7 \+ fell at intervals all day — sometimes heavily — steaming hot in the m'f. but later a cool wind came up — Another m'f. wasted in unsuccessful photo. work — At noon I went to P.O. a brief break of sunshine — it was then I found it so hot. Dinner at 1 — afternoon, more time wasted — part in sleeping \+ part in more attempts to get satisfactory prints from dense negatives — I wrote a description (with plans) of changes made in The Art Gallery — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. we read a couple of f. f. in tho stories — both rather weak then I did a little writing —
