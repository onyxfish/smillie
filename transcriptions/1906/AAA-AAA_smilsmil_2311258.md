## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 4, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another remarkably fine day — after a quiet & restful night I awoke at about 7 & had a dose of Citrate of Magnesia \+ after 8 had v.hlp. Then bath, rubbing \+ dressing — dress up shirt, collar & suit of clothes \+ at about 11 took seat at open window in sunshine — at 1 o'clk — went to dining room, took my place at table \+ had dinner — walked about a little — wrote note to Dr. Gerster — then feeling tired I lay down on bed — Chant came about 3 \+ brought many of his late photos. with him — very interesting but I was much tired looking over them \+ lay down on bed again before he went away after 4 o'clk — my dinner oppressed me \+ at 7.30 I took a little gruel \+ got into my bed for the night — some reading.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 5, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Yet another remarkable day, brilliantly clear \+ mild — after a comfortable night I awoke before 7 o'bkfp. — at 8 then came bath, alcohol rub \+ dressing — sat at open south windows from about 11 to 1 — then had a quail on toast — after 2 o'clk. I lay down upon my bed \+ had a nap — I cannot see that there is any improvement in my physical condition — slow pulse \+ low Temp. — did not get up to 98°. — have not for two or three days past. After J. D. Simpson came — he had with him an electric battery \+ light to make examination of the nasal cavaties — we went into a dark closet but just as he applied the bulb the fuse blew out \+ he could not continue the examination — Then Sumna Bourne came \+ told me how things were going at Po'keepsie. I got up about 5 \+ loafed about — tea — that is raw oysters \+ beef broth at 6.30 — then a tiresome evef.
