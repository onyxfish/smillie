## Left Page

### Fri. July 13, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine specimen of a July day — hot in the sunshine, with refreshing breeze. Bkp. at 8.30 — after that I went down to Beckington's garden \+ got some vegetables — R. went to the village on his wheel. Then ret. to house \+ from there went to Club ho. \+ P. O. — went to laundry \+ made kick because of unsatisfactory work. At P. O. got two very interesting letters forwarded by Ge°. both from Cortina — one from Mary, the other from Bessie — back home — read letters to R. — then wrote short note to Charl \+ mailed see back to him. — Then dinner. — The afternoon went like many others \+ deserves no further record. — Tea at 7 — we f. R. \+ I sat on north piazza till 9 o'clk. — then in \+ I continued the reading aloud of Father's autobiography till near 11 — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 14, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day — but with the qualifying breeze. Ther. in my bed room got up to 78° \+ fell 10° at night. In doors or up on the north piazza there was no discomfort. I spent the m'f writing to D'. Gerster, to Katie Elkin \+ to Jen. — After 12 I went to the P. O. — dinner after 1 o'clk — Afternoon Ralph \+ I had a good time taking down my printing press — a block \+ Tackle was rigged to the collar beam over head by which the weight of the press was managed — after the plank was run out from between the rollers the press was turned on its side \+ then nuts \+ bolts were removed \+ piece by piece the press was lifted apart by use of Tackle. — The whole job was done in a very workmanlike manner in about half, or possibly three quarters of an hour. Tea at 7 — we f. on piazza till 9 — then in and finished Father's autobiography — \+ after that a very poor story by Bullen.
