## Left Page

### Fri. April 6, 1906

**Weather:** Very Aprilish — a little sunshine \+ much shade that at times seemed very showerish — brilliant moonlight following.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up with a bad headache that finally, in the afternoon, used me up for a time. I got to studio at about 10 o'clk \+ spent remainder of m'f in getting to work on "Road side Chapel, near Segano". I laid etching ground on the plate but got no farther. I had lunch in studio \+ then went to my rooms \+ spread myself on my lounge. Sw'f. Jim dined with a friend \+ I had a cup of tea alone — Later I was able to finish almost, my negative cat.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 7, 1906

**Weather:** A brilliant cool m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I awoke after 5 with severe headache again \+ as my kidneys are behaving in a manner the to me seems to require explanation I determined to see m'r. Tuttle after b'kf'p., which I did. He thinks that the lumbar discomfort of which I complains may be rheumatism — \+ possibly the m't headaches as well, \+ advised Salicin. I took some of my (W.) photos to show him — Then I went to Carnige or Carnagie Hall on invitation of Mr'. Anna R. Giles (daughter of W'n Mains) to see some of her painted work. Then walked to studio, getting there at about 11 o'clk. After a walk of about 2½ m. I did not go to work on my etching but finished packing a box of books. Served my lunch after 1 o'clk — Then got out an old charcoal sketch, made in 1869, illustrating some lines of Bryant — a tornado in a forest, \+ worked upon it with crayon, intensifying the darkest parts — The Art Com. of the Century have asked artist members to give something to decorate in blk. \+ white a small dining room just fitted up. To my rooms at 5 — Jim went to Yonkers to spend Sunday with Harry Gould — I dined alone at 7. As it came r' to shower Chad phoned that he would not go to Century with me — later he came to my rooms \+ we went to Century where I saw J. Idelma \+ dow \+ arranged for a meeting at Charles house next friday eve \+ where he will show them some of his lantern slides — Back to my rooms about midnight.
