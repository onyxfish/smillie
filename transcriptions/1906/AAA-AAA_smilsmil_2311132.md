## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 27, 1906

**Weather:** Havana  **Temp:** 74° to 78°

A very fine day — bath & bkfo. as usual — Then out &
loafed about the deck watching for the Nautilus. Early
in the m'g large numbers had been passed — I saw
very close to the vessel but three — they were beautiful
specimens — the water was wonderfully smooth — hardly
a ripple — the boat moving without perceptible swing —
It all got to be monotonous after a time — I don't like
smooth water at sea — it was after 11 when we passed
the Moro at entrance — then thro' the tortuous entrance &
12 when we made fast to a buoy — Then a Health official
came aboard & every passenger had to be rounded up —
a farcical performance — we had lunch soon after 12
but it was nearly 2 before we got off — I got to land
before the Dr. did — Then we walked from the wharf up
thro' a business st. to the head of the Prado — we walked
till I got tired — I took a number of snaps. Then to the
Hotel Inglaterra where we made a bargain for a vehicle
for a two hours drive — we were taken down to the
water front & along a fine drive along the ocean edge
Then around thro' some of the streets of town & back
to the Prado where Carnival crowds were gathering —
we went from one end to the other — down one & back up
on the other — chairs & benches of all kinds lined the street
\+ boys & men — girls & women sat or stood in crowds, throw-
ing Paper Streamers & confetti — a procession of carriages
passing — many of the riders were in domino & masked —
Some of the equipages were very stylish & so were the oc-
cupants — it was very gay & confusing & reminded me of our
experiences in Nice 4 years ago. After 5 o'clk. we had
confiscated the carriage & under 2 ¢ we drove
to the Machine wharf & by launch out to the Vic — get-
ting there soon after 6 — I went at once to my cabin
& had a half hours rest — at 6.30 I got up & dressed for
dinner — a festive affair celebrating Kaiser Billy's Silver
wedding — D. D. Cup! Bruns wil opened the dinner
with a brief speech — the first sentence or two in German
the rest in English stating the nature of festivities + noting
that he was sure that good Americans would be giving
the admiration that Billy, but for an Pres. would be glad
to drink to Health & Happiness for the Kaiser. He was
an effective figure as he stood at the head of the table
his breast carrying many decorations — the toast was
heartily approved & drunk with proper Hoch's — Then
the dinner went on — Many of the passengers re-
mained in town to dine & celebrate — a number went
after dinner — the last boat will return at 2 a.m.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 28, 1906

**Weather:** left Havana at 1 o'clk.  **Temp:** 76° to 69°

A lovely cool day — cloudy on &, but clearing later with
delightful breeze — no bath — water too long — it was after 9 o'clk
before I was ready to go ashore — Dr. T. preceded me. I
asked a junt. Officer who had the direction of the launches
for a chance to get near to the wreck of the Maine — He
very civilly put me aboard of one of them with instructions to
take me where I wished — when near the wreck we ran up on
something & stuck there for some minutes — it may have been a
part of the wreck — I got a snap from near but the sun was
under a cloud & the position was not quite what I wished —
Then on to landing place & till after 11 I wandered about taking
snap shots, especially in Obispo St. where the awnings from
side to side made some very interesting effects — but I fear that
they are not photo graphable — I bought a number of photos
at Olsons — 32 Obispo St — I looked in store windows for some-
thing suitable to take home to the boys — but could not see
anything that seemed to be the right thing — The last boat
from shore to ship was at 12.30 & there was the usual com-
motion in getting away — & after a heavy sea running
as we went out — a fine surf agt. the Moro at which I
got several snaps — for a while after getting out there
was considerable motion & the Vict. & some of the pass-
engers came to grief. I had a very restful two hour
nap after lunch — then on deck wearing a heavier
suit & there was a general donning of warmer clothing
among the passengers. At night, when the Mer. went
below 70 there was a strong wind, steamer rugs & over-
coats came out — I had quite a long & pleasant chat
with Mr. & Mrs. Noyes & did not get to bed till long
after 11.
