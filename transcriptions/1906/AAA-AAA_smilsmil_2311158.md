## Left Page

### Fri. April 20, 1906

**Weather:** Foggy, muggy, warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

Foggy, muggy, warm — I was in bed all day. The fever left me some time in the morg, but the sore throat annoyed me \+ the headache kept up till late in the afternoon. I got up at about 6 \+ went back to bed at 9. I sent for D. Cutti \+ he came about 11 \+ seemed to think I was not in for Grip or Tonsilities. Ralph at my request a couple of days ago had got two Theatre tickets. He asked Katharine Throop to go with him, \+ she did — Jim went to his class dinner at Yale \+ I had my misery all to myself through the eveg. as I had had it thro' the day. When these attacks grow more frequent \+ severe, as they must, I shall have to find some way to secure feminine attendance. I find it rather late in life to try to get along without it. Annie used to weep because she had no daughter — \+ now my tears follow hers.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 21, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day — Thunder showers passed south of us at night. I got thro' last night in fairly good shape but felt very slim in the mog. I had some hot malted milk \+ some bread \+ butter for my bkfs. \+ went to studio at 10.30 to get my etching pls. in shape to send to W. C. Cot[illegible]. I then found that Clausen had disappointed me \+ had not sent the proines as promised. I was able to fix up my charcoal sketch illustrating some lines of Bryant for the Century Club, but that was not as I had ordered C. to make it. I had my lunch in studio — I could do very little \+ went back to Pouvaine rooms at 3.30. I did not see Ralph till after 5 — we had dinner at 7 — Jim had gone to Nutley, N.J. to spend Sunday with a friend. Eveg. Eliz[illegible] Throop called R. on Phone \+ invited him there. — he went — I spent the eveg alone.
