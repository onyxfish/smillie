## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 18, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded day with efforts at clearing — clear at sunset but rain at 10 p.m. After 10 a.m. I went to Charls — found a young man (Semons) with letter of introduction from Prof Gerard — he is a musician come here to seek his fortune — then to studio — then to Kimmnel & Voigt's, Canal St. & got some old book plates belonging to S. R. Koehler & his daughter — then again to studio & to Touraine & prepared a very light lunch. It was late before I got to studio again & I accomplished but little — altho' the vertigo that distressed me two or three days ago is much less it has not left me altogether — there is a swimming confusion in my head that unfits me for action of any kind — Jim & I dined at the Touraine at 7 & in the evening I went to Charls to talk about investments — he showed more slides.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 19, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant sunshine day — To studio after 10 o'clk \+ for some time tied up bundles of "Round Tables" "St. Nicholas," "Charities" &c — files of back nos some of them running three or five years — these I shall return to the publishers — About noon I went out with my camera & an old Roll of last summer films — I went by subway to 72d St — thence to Riverside Park & walked up to 88th St — exposing the full roll — then ret'd to Touraine & had lunch at 2 — then a rest — then down town to Cullen's — 61 Williams St. with camera \+ left it & Roll with him — then to Hamburg Am. office & arranged for a passport — & ret'd to Touraine at 6 — At 7 I went alone to Murray Hill Hotel & had dinner — Jim went off with Harry Gorud — After dinner I went to Charls \+ had a talk with him about investments — Geo. came in — Sheldon has an offer of position in Mexico City.
