## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 8, 1906

**Weather:** Fine clear winter morning  **Temp:** [illegible]

D. Tuttle dined with me

Fine clear winter morning. Vertigo still very troublesome at 9 o'clk I called D. Tuttle up on 'phone \+ told him of my plight \+ that I could hardly hope to be able to go to Century with him to-night to hear F. H. Smith on "Old Plantation Days" but begged that he w. come to dinner at any rate. About noon I went out \+ walked up Broadway half a mile or more \+ then back \+ was better for the fresh air. lunch at 1.30 — After that I found something to do in fixing up in anticipation of D.T.'s coming — The weather changed \+ the afternoon was overcast \+ threatening — The D. came at 6.30 \+ we had dinner — Jim was with us but went out later in the ev'g — Our dinner was a good one \+ nicely served — I enjoyed it as much as I could in the condition I was in — During the ev'g the D. \+ I discussed fully the plan of a trip to Jamaica, Colombia \+ Costa Rica — \+ he gave his assent \+ enthused in his mild way — He left before 10 \+ I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 9, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very light fall of snow covered roofs \+ street when I got up in the m'g. It lay all day under a gloomy, beclouded sky — I had bkfst. before 10 \+ spent some time with voluminous Sunday newspapers — when Jim got up he set to work with me \+ we pulled down most of the structure of a extension book-case "units" \+ divided them, putting a section in parlor — discarding half a dozen of the 12½" units, not needed, \+ dividing the remainder into two groups in the hall — The result was so great an improvement that I decided to keep them — we got the books nearly all on the shelves before dinner at 2. I loafed about the rest of the afternoon till Clare came in before 5 — He left at about 6.30 \+ Jim came in \+ we had supper —

Free discharge of pus in urine last night.
