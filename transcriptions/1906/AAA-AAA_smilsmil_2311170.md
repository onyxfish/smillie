## Left Page

### Mon. May 14, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day — rain during the m'f. — chilly, damp air — dark \+ cloudy all the day — I had bl'fo'. at 8.30 \+ after that resumed my financial investigations of last night — The facts are stubborn — my income is $7000 \+ to live as I w°. wish to live in that charming ap't. w°. cost $7500. I have escaped from under the harrow in 38°5'. I dread getting under it again — for the past four or five months I have been misled in my financial affairs by a bad that really came from sale of 110 2, 38°5'. but lost its identity in the transactions that followed. If I had kept closer watch I w°. have known this sooner \+ been spared this unpleasant surprise. I got to studio before 11 \+ spent the rest of the day, practically, in the dampoing \+ putting between blotters \+ in press the sheets of two Etching cat. of 1892 — I had my lunch at 2 — I fussed about a good deal trying to reduce the disorder \+ dirt of studio — I am hopeless now it never gets any less disorderly or dirty — I can make no progress \+ all the' mountains of work are before me, in my plans, I can make no progress — it is all helpless, hapless doddering. to Touraine at 6.30 — dinner with Jim at 7.30 Eve'g, writing.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 15, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day — very charming about mid-day — the men got to work repairing studio roof this m'f. I got to studio after 10 \+ for some time was putting pages \+ prints from Etching Club Cat. — 1883 — in dry blotters — then I gathered together plates, pfas \+ other material that I propose to finish up at once — After 12 I went out for a short walk — as I have not yet rec'd. Ralph's Sunday letter I went to Touraine to see if it had arrived there — no letter — back to studio — had my lunch there \+ then worked on two or three plates, preparing them for proving — at 4.30 I went up town to look for apartments or houses — to Broadway \+ 75. S. — no chance there — to S Losson \+ Hobbs — Columbus Ave. 74 S. — I got a list of small houses on the west side, at 75 to 75 S. — at Rents from 15 to 1600 per an. — I went wearily over the list \+ found but one that I would consider — in 75°S. I reached the Touraine at 6.30 completely tired out \+ dispirited — no letter from Ralph — that finished me — dinner with Jim at 7 — then telef. to fl. — asking him to wire me if he was well — at 10.30 message came — "yes — fine" — so I had a quiet night.
