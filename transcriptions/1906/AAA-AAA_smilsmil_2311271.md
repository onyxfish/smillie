## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 30, 1906

**Weather:** Bright, sunny m'f - overcast p.m  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- I got up \+ had bkfs't. about 8.30 - I finished letter to Bessie \+ walked for sometime in Riverside Park - I had lunch at 1 - about 2 o'clk. Jane \+ Mary Hinshelwood called - came to see how I was getting along. They left in about half an hour \+ later I went to the Met. Mus. of Nat. History to see if I could find stuffed specimens of the Thrush family - I want to see the difference between the ordinary wood Thrush \+ the Hermit T.. I found the birds but could not be satisfied by the specimens on view - Home at 5 - dinner alone, at 6.30. Jim rushed by office work - close of the year Ev'g. I wrote to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 1, 1906

**Weather:** Overcast \+ raw \+ so continued thro'  **Temp:** [illegible]

the day - light rain about 5 P.m. - a beautifully clear night - Bkfs't. at 9 o'clk. About 11 I went down town to the Globe-Werincke Co. 380 Broadway, \+ purchased a combination extension book case \+ card catalogue case at a total cost of $134.— Then home \+ had lunch before 2 o'clk - the weather was so disagreeable that I did not go out again till after 4 when I went to butcher's \+ grocer's - Dinner with Jim at 6.30 - Ev'g. we looked over his accts. for the past year \+ found that he had a surplus of a little more than $800 for the year - He then agreed to pay one $8? a week board \+ lodging. That will still leave a possible $400 as savings or investment per an. We hung Chawel's etching after Rousseau in the dining room.
