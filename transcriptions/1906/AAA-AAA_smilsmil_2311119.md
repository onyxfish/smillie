## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 1, 1906

**Weather:** A bright [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright [illegible], filmy overcast later — windy \+ raw. P\&lo\. with Jim at 8 — after that, writing for some time — about 10 a clerk from Nathan \+ Cardoza served a summons with notice in the suit of Southack \+ Co — for com — on the sale of 110 S. 38th S\. — that they claim to have made — I went to Pease \+ Elliman — 5th Ave. but no one of the firm was in — then to studio \+ for some time was cleaning up \+ preparing for use old water color paints — to Tourraine soon after 1 o'clk — I prepared a junket lunch — then to J\. \+ E\. — again \+ saw young Elliman — He said he could not make safely to summons on me — a personal matter \+ also to me — so I went down town to Nathan \+ Cardoza — 128 B[illegible] \+ saw both of those gentlemen — Mr. K\. told me that he had said that I promised not to sell thro' any other broker or something to that effect — I said if it came to court \+ such a claim was made it would be only a question of veracity between us — I should deny it — N\. \+ C\. said they could treat with me only on the basis of a compromise — I said I would not compromise for 5 cents — then I went to Clarence Swifts — he called up J\. \+ E\. \+ they assented to his proposition to send the summons to me to their atty. Then I went to Cullis's — got my camera \+ ordered 25 film rolls — then to Tourraine — Dinner with Jim at 7 — a little before 9 we went to Charl's \+ saw a number of his newly finished lantern slides of Norway scenery. Back at 11.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 2, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bitter cold day — cloudless, high N.W. wind \+ lots of flying dirt. At about 10.30 started out — went to my room, 42nd S., \+ got repaired spectacles — then to studio — completed selection of water-colors \+ made list of missing tubes — then to Lock's — then to Palette Co. 23d S\. \+ bought water colors required — then to 2d Nat\. — then to Tourraine at 1. Prepared a light lunch — then a rest — then wrote letter to Ralph \+ cut with it a photo of self Taken 35 years or more ago — to show him what I looked like at my physical best. Then a lot of general writing. D\. Tuttle came a little before 7 — Jim dined at the Yale Club — D\. T\. \+ I had dinner soon after 7 — it was near 9 when we went to Charl's. He entertained us till after 11, showing us a lot of his lantern slides, picturing his his Norway \+ Spitzbergen Trip last Summer. It was after midnight before I got to bed.
