## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 12, 1906

**Weather:** Raw, disagreeable  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw, disagreeable m't. clearing off cold at night — a comfortable night \+ waking at 7.30 — usual routine \+ out at 10 — I got by mail a lot of Ham. Am. S.S. Line literature about winter excursions \+ spent some time reading [illegible] them. then wrote to the line for a state-room plan of the Blenches. wrote to B. T. about it — got rid of m't by pacing up \+ down hall \+ writing etc — Eppent came from the man. Storage to look at the damaged mirror in the hall — dinner at 1 — a short nap bet. 2.30 \+ 3.30 then more pacing \+ writing — Strength increasing — heaving \+ noises in head, very bad — Supper at 6.30 — Jim came in, dressed \+ went to Edw. Cook's to dinner — I lived thro' the ev't \+ got to bed after 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 13, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Windy \+ cloudy with some sunshine. awake about 7 — b't b't — bath, rub \+ newspaper \+ out at about 10 — a long visit from George — then I commenced a letter to Mary \+ devoted some time to it — after 12 went out with Miss D. \+ walked down to 79th St. \+ took a look at the new bl't going up on block from 78th to 79th St. I had to keep my nose \+ mouth well covered the wind was so strong — then in \+ at 1.30 had dinner — Bet. 3 \+ 4 had a nap. then studied plans of S.S. Bluecher — cabin prices etc \+ concluded that altho' the Ham. Am. advertises that there is no 2d. class on these West Indies trips, there is a 2d. cl. All Staterooms in price less than $375 are together in the after part of the vessel \+ have a dining room of their own — I wrote more on letter to Mary — Supper at 6.30 — Jim did not come home to dinner — Ev't rec'd. note from Mrs. Jas. Simpson asking if I could etch book plate for her — I replied at once —
