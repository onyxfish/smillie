## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 15, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day - Ther. went down to 45° in my bedroom last night - Before I started on my days work Otis came \+ with him I went to Club House \+ from the end of 4" water pipe there, measured to a possible junction with my pipe at Highland Cottage, 550' - Caught Thro. Janeway on Golf course \+ told him I wanted official permission to lay the connecting pipe at once - he said he cd. call a meeting of Ex. Com. this eve't \+ act at once. I prepared a letter for him making formal application - Then home \+ worked generally till 12.30 - Then to P.O. \+ back to dinner. I podged about all the afternoon but seemed to accomplish little. The worry of getting away is now upon me. Three carpenters at work on Icehouse roof finished shingling it. Earl went home to spend Sunday. Tea at 6.30 - Eve't \+ some reading aloud but I was so dead sleepy that much of it was lost on me.

### Sun. Sept. 16, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day, brilliant \+ pleasantly cool. Bkfs. at 9 after a good bath. Worked about till after 11 - Then with Ralph, started down the hill to Albert Winche's - met him on the way - sold to him the last bed frames that I had sent from Montrose for $15.00 - Then on to Deedes - not at home. Then back up the hill \+ on to P.O. - Had a long talk with Theo. Janeway about Club matters - wrote to Plattsburgh for two parlor car seats out, to train from West port on Friday next. Dinner at 2.
