## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 6, 1906

Election Day.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another superb day — there has been a remarkable succession of fine days \+ it gives me renewed hope for the belief that it is equally good all over the state — I greatly regret that I am not strong enough to go out \+ vote agt. that most dangerous of demagogues, Hearst. Jim was away at 5 o'clk. to act as a watcher at the polls — I commenced the day, after a restful night — by taking a Citrate of Mag. drawn at 7 o'clk — Help — after 8 — then the routine of newspaper, bath, and, dressing \+ then attending to demands of Cit. Mag. It was after 11 when I took my seat at the open south window — dinner at 1 — on my bed at 2.30 — a nap \+ up before 4. Geo. about most of the mg. with me — Charl came at about 4 — then D. Gerster came \+ then Geo. Cook \+ Valentine — the two latter made a short call — Gerster \+ Charl stayed with me till 5 o'clk. or after — At 6.30 I had my supper — Jim came in later after a fatiguing \+ unsatisfactory day \+ soon went off to deliver his report \+ after that to meet Tom \+ Louise Cook \+ another girl — I loafed about till after 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 7, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still brilliant weather — I awoke at 6.30 after a comfortable night — but my head was in very bad state \+ I felt greatly depressed — I could not arouse myself from it — I had help at 8 \+ went thro the regular business \+ was dressed \+ up at 10.30 — I spent some time in sun \+ air at open south windows. D. Tuttle came at about noon but I could get little comfort from him — Evidently he is waiting for things in my head to "clear up" — Dinner soon after 1. Soon after 2 I put on overcoat \+ hat \+ with Miss Wabbles went down to the street — but there it was so cold \+ the wind so strong that I beat a retreat at once \+ rect. to my room where, by the open window it was mild \+ balmy — Then I lay down on my bed \+ had a short nap — After 3 D. Simpson came \+ his electric apparatus worked \+ illuminated the various sinuses of my head showing no trace of evil such as he said might exist there — When I questioned him he said my distress was no doubt caused by the closing of the Eustachian (?) tubes — It seems as tho the illuminating business was to amuse the patient — I think there is nothing for the doctors to do, or that they can do, but to play with me — both assure me that the trouble will clear up — but I don't feel assured or happy — By effort I lived thro' afternoon \+ evening —
