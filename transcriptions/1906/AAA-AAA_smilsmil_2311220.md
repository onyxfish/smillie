## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 20, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

D.A. Adams came.

A hot, muggy, misty m'f. The air was full of thunderous promise — the first shower came at about 1 o'clk — later, before 3 o'clk. very heavy showers with wind \+ thunder crashed as tho' it c'd. crush the roof — the rain continued into the eve'g \+ at intervals thro' the night. m'g. I wrote to D. Gerster, urging him \+ Mrs. G to make us a visit. D.A. Adams came \+ I set him to work staining such portions of the wood work as had been exposed by the moving of partitions. At 12.30 I went to P.O. \+ got an interesting letter from Charl — written on Steamer Amerika — caught in rain on my way back to the house — dinner — Afternoon much of the time so dark that I could not see to do any work — so, I slept. Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g — weather not suitable to be out of doors so, we commenced our reading early — had a story by T. Nelson Page — then one by Jack London.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 21, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day much like yesterday — only the rain was not quite so heavy — bright \+ muggy for a time — very thunderous — storms passing south of us — afternoon shadows passed over us — I gave much of my time in the m'g to carrying on experiments in camera enlarging in dark room — failures because the lighting thro' the negative does not come directly from the sky — I went to P.O. at 12.30 \+ met there D. John Hawes who introduced himself. I c'd not have known him — Home for dinner but did not take any because of headache which I think, in part due to electrical conditions — afternoon much of the time was dark \+ wet \+ I was on my bed but by 5 o'clk my headache had nearly disappeared \+ I got up — weather clearing — Adams was here part of the day Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g, alone one for reading — we had a story by T. N. Page \+ two by Jack London — This is a short story dissipation that I don't approve of.
