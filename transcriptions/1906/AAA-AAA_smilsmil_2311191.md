### Sat. June 23, 1906

*Down in the Belmont Tunnels*
*477 Prospect S. - New Haven -*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of many showers, some of them exceptionally heavy - Up at 7 o before bkfs. laid out things to take with me + put some of them in Ralph's larger suit case - Bkfs. with R - at 8.30 - just before 10 Charles came + the R. + I went to the office of Mr. Clark, Chief Engineer in charge of the Two Tunnels that are being driven under the East River from the foot of 42d. St. We were detained awhile in Mr. Co office till he got thro' with some business - then we all went together to the foot of E. 42d. S. + went down the shaft 117 feet + commenced our explorations - we went first back to the west end of the tunnel + saw the work of boring into + blasting out the rock which is here very solid - it was every where very wet, muddy + dark inspite of long rows of electric lamps - the smell was not dypartly sweet owing to blasting + other impurifyings but the burling in of condensed air the noise of the drilling engines filled the dismal hole with infernal hissing throbbing noise - then we went to the air ends, to the air locks - there, as the condensed air was being discharged from one of the locks the noise was fearful - the pressure was but 10 lbs - I would liked to have gone in but Mr. Clark said that it was not considered entirely safe for persons older than 50 - then we ref. to surface + went to 34th S. ferry + saw the fountains caused by escaping air from the fenst tunnels - crossed + went to the other end of Belmont tunnels - went down shaft + had full exhibit of air locks - shield + the iron construction that follows the shield - again on the surface we went thro' the power house then to 34th S. ferry - very heavy shower - back to N.Y. Parted with Mr. Clark - got to Touraine after 1 - Jim there - he took R. + me with him to lunch at Yale Club + then thro' the Club house - I left them at about 3 - went to Touraine - finished packing suit case - porter carried it to Station + I took 4 o'clk Train for New Haven - got there at 5.40 + by Westchester Ave car I got to the Elkius soon after 6 - very heavy shower soon after I got in - Dinner at 6.30 - Sunday after chatting with Katie, Mollie + W. dean all 10 o'clk. ## Right Page

### Sun. June 24, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day - superb in air + sunshine - Up at 8 o'clk - bkfs. at 9 - a little after 10 I walked down the hill to Woolsey Hall + had the good fortune to get a satisfactory seat although I had no ticket + I heard Hadley's sermon - was able to follow it fairly well - his pronunciation his delivery is atrocious - some defect in his mouth seemingly makes it impossible to deliver many words in a form recognizable at any considerable distance - Nevertheless without very much trouble I was able to get the most of his discourse which I thought was practical + admirable. It was before 12 when it was over - the singing by congregation was fine - I walked back to the Elkius - dinner at 1.30 - Afternoon I sauntered about - had a nap + got rid of a rue till the 6 o'cl meal. Ralph came in after that + we had a chatty evening - R. had come over from Tarrytown to Mamaroneck. in the afternoon + thence to N.H. He left for his Room before 10 -
