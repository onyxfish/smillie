## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 2, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very fine day as seen out of my window. I am not conscious of any change for the better in my hearing — my head is sealed up in a box — just as I was ready for my bath Geo. came \+ made a long call. It was about 11 when I was helped to big chair in south room \+ there I had a long session with bills \+ making out checks — had a fine dinner at 1 — too much as it proved later. At 2 o'clk. I went back to bed \+ had some uneasy sleep till about 4.30 — then bolstered up in bed I wrote to Ralph \+ to Jane Hinshelwood — sent her check for $25 — At about 9 had some gruel — went I read Post \+ after 9 p.m. was fixed for the night. I am gaining a little in strength — but no improvement in head or hearing.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 3, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another charming day — after a night that was not very restful I had my bkfst. at about the usual time. Am not conscious of any improvement in my hearing. It was nearly 11 when I got into my big chair in south room at the open window — I sat there till almost 3 o'clk — Dr. T. came at about 12 \+ stayed till after 1 — I then had my dinner — At 3. feeling tired I went back to bed \+ had a nap. Dr. Simpson came at about 4.30 — reported favorably — then I got up — \+ I straightened out the Wanamaker bill muddle \+ ret[?]. it to him with explanatory note — wrote to Case of 2. nat[?]. Afternoon I wrote to Jessie Smilie concerning one of the tweedies said to be in surgical corridor — Presbyterian Hospital — about 9 walked the length of the hall \+ took a look at the mirror that had been hung there \+ also at a new arrangement in dining room.
