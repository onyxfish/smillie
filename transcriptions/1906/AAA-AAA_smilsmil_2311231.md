## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 11, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Plumber came.

A lovely day \- bright\. Too warm for work in sunshine \- more than pleasant in the shade \- Duck came up about 9 o'clk bringing a plumber \- but no carpenter \- I told him to come back afternoon, bringing plumber \+ carpenter which he did \- The two worked till 5 o'clk \- I tried again to get a satisfactory photo. of interior of large room \- developed two negatives that promised well \- bring full of detail in both lights \+ shadows \- but when I took trial prints in the afternoon they proved no better than the others \- if I printed till I got the detail in my lights then the shadows were all black \- If I stopped when I got the detail in my shadows, then, the light parts were pure white. To P.O. at noon \- dinner at 1 \- afternoon I did some work in gathering things that are to be packed in linen closet. also, a little more fussing with photography \- this time posing R. posed for one, I for the other. R. developed them \- Tea at 6.30 \- Evef, reading \-

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 12, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day but humid \+ close \- hot in the sunshine \- Plumber \+ carpenter came \+ put in a day \- The job should have been finished at night but after the plumber had wiped a joint on trap under bath tub he said the trap was defective \+ he w. have to come up in them with another traps. I put in a full day in making preparations for our flitting on the 21., getting out \+ airing mattresses, pillows, tack\-downs, sheets etc\. \- etc. I got down from the attic a lot of things that I am going to send down to N.Y. city by freight \- At noon went to P.O. Dinner at 1 \- Then R. did a little portrait photography \- I continued my work till afts \- then I rested \- Tea at 6.30 Fera Deeds's man brought a small load of manure \+ it was put upon Tennis court. Evef R. \+ I sat out upon N. Piazza till after 9 o'clk \- then in \+ read awhile one of Joel Chandler Harris' stories.

P. L. M. Yale died suddenly to\-day \-
