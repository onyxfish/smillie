## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 15, 1906

**Weather:** Bridgetown \- Barbados.  **Temp:** 83°

Bright sunshine \+ light showers at long intervals \- We got into this port at about 6.30
A.M. Bkfp. served from 7 to 9 \- many went ashore
before 8 o'clk \- I had my bath \- bkfp. \- before 8 \+8\+. I.
\+ I went ashore after 9 \- We took a vehicle \+ started
out for a drive, with a reticent coal black driver, who
spoke english when he wished to \- We gave him general
directions to show us all that was worth seeing, for sure than
on from he drove us thro' common streets \+ outside of town
where I was not at all satisfied \- After 11 we reached
an up to date, steam sugar mill \- as we passed thro' fields
whose cane was being cut \+ carried to mill. I took several
shots \- at mill an english overseer put us in charge of the
engineer, a very intelligent man who took us thro' \+ told
the process from grinding the cane to the centrifugal
product, a fine medium light, granular sugar. I
was exceedingly interesting to me \- I got a number of
shots about the mill \- near 12 when we left \- entered a
town on another side \+ passed thro' some outpart ave.
of Royal Palms \- I have seen a more varied \+ finer group
of tropical trees than elsewhere \- the english influence
is in evidence everywhere without changing local
characteristics \- the places are every where \- I many
are poor looking specimens \- The shacks in which I
they live are all alike a base but poor apologies for human
habitations \- The roads are all coral macadam \- smooth,
hot, white \+ dusty \- we drove about the Governor's house \-
a three story building \- extensive but otherwise unpretentious
There are no fine public buildings \- Three Am. war vessels
are in the harbor \- To my sorrow I missed getting shots at
some amusing donkey carts \+ riders \- passing shadows [illegible]
lined with some fine street views with Royal palms \- sat to
ship after 12, very tired \- lunch at 1 \- after that went to
sleep \+ did not wake till near 3 \- Dried tired, weather
showery \- gave up going ashore for photos. watched
8 or 10 darkies, two of them boys of 9 to 4 years old diving for
coin \- doing some wonderful work \- barely missing the coin \-
Splendid physical specimens \- I tried to get photo shot
but the light \+ my position was bad. At 5 o'clk \- up
anchor \+ started. We passed close to each war vessel \-
The Alabama \- The Iowa \+ The Illinois \- The band playing
Star \+ Spangled Banner, \+ saluting \+ getting salutes back \-
Then we started for the big cruises outside but she ran
away from us. She is watching the three steamers who are
to try to slip out to-night undiscovered \- naval maneuvers \-
A very quiet smooth run all night \- Ther. in state room
got up to 82°

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 16, 1906

**Weather:** Port of Spain. Trinidad.  **Temp:** [illegible]

got to Port of Spain, about 8 o'clk, stopping for an
hour to get permit to stop at Point Brea \- I gave
my Ther. a sun bath \+ in 10 min. it went up to 100° \+
then seemed disinclined to go further \- delightful
breeze blowing \- Ther. in shade at 8 o'clk. 77° I
had my bath at 7 \+ bkfp. \- soon after 8.30 \- at about 9 we got
away for Point Brea, 23 miles away \- The sea was very smooth
\+ showers were all about, one very heavy one passing over us \-
The prospect for the day was bad \- It was about 11 when we
got to La Brea Point \+ were landed as promptly as possible \-
Showers threatened \- but passed ahead of us \- along walk on
a narrow gang way over the water \+ then a good half
mile up a gentle hill, without a particle of shade to
mitigate the heat \- the sun shining \+ blazing \- The Pitch
Lake is very picturesque but astonishing to look at \+
walk over \- I walked part way \+ was returning to get
a shot at a grove of dense, tropical growth dominated
by splendid palms. When a shower came suddenly
up on us \- in my effort to cling to my coat, to hang on to my
camera \+ put up my umbrella the camera slipped \+
fell 12 or 15" to the ground \- I picked up my belongings \+
hurried to the shelter of the sheds \- my camera did not
seem to be injured \- in a few minutes the sun shone
out again \+ I took two snaps at my palms \- it seemed
at the time that the shutter did not act properly, but
when, further on, I tried a shot looking out to sea, the
shutter refused to budge \- I was wild \- I went on to the
landing \+ on to the steamer \+ there I made unsuccessful
efforts to remedy the evil \- Steamer started back for
Port of Spain before 1 \- lunch at 1.30 \- there in a quieter frame
of mind I again worked over my shutter \- by an inspiration
I struck the pneumatic cylinder two or time sharp raps
\+ every thing was right again to my intense relief, but I
have lost at least three fine subjects \- We got to Port
of Spain after 3 \+ I went ashore after 4 \- the steamer has
to lie out [illegible] owing to shallow water \- the launches took 30
m. to reach landing \- I sauntered about till about 6 \-
I was impressed by the superior condition of things \- The
english pressure is felt everywhere \- there was no
harassing beggary from the blacks \- no diving for money
about the ship \- I saw no old, interesting buildings \-
There were on Frederick Str. many thriving looking stores \-
Visited a fire engine house \- was much interested in the
[illegible] given by a [illegible] of intelligent darkie members of the
force \- The mts about are fine in shape \+ heavily
wooded \- Rest to ship at 6.30 \- dinner at 7 \- We loafed
on deck \+ tried to think that I was enjoying the music \-
The weather is delightfully balmy \-
