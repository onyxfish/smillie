### Wed. July 11, 1906

**Weather:** A pleasant, cool day \- hazy & at times &  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant, cool day \- hazy & at times & cloudy. B'kfs' at 8.30 \- Kelly, the milkman, bring late as usual \- after that, moving things about the house to look after till near noon when R. & I went to the P.O. & then to Library & Cottage where I got butter then home for dinner at 1.30 \- during the afternoon more "chores" about the house, later, a call from D. Garvin & Mr. Howell who introduced Mr. Kent, librarian of the Century. Supper at 7 \- then R. & I had a chat before the fire till near 9. Then we read a story, "You Sing", by Bullen \- then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 12, 1906

**Weather:** A fine July day \- hazy & warm but not  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine July day \- hazy & warm but not hot. Ther. in my bed Room last night fell to 57° & to-day did not get above 74°. Kate was late with her b'kfs' \- as usual \- it was after 8.30 before we got it \- then were busy with various things about the house \+ grounds till noon then to P.O. \- got my 4 pcs enlarged prints from Cullen \- a disappointment \- they are imperfect reminders only \- notes made at least expense of time or trouble \- no satisfaction to be found in them as photographic records. Dinner at 1.30 \-
The afternoon got away as time usually does \+ there was nothing left to tell about. The hay was gathered from our lawn. I started the taking down of printing press that I have given to Dr. Gerster from whom I have just rec'd. a letter of directions concerning its shipment to Long Lake. After 6 R. & I loafed on north piazza till supper time, near 7 \- after that the loafing was continued till about 9 then indoors & I read till 10 o'clk aloud to R. from Father's autobiography which I have with me here to continue my editing of it for publication in small, choice edition.
