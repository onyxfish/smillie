## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 2, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hippodrome.

Fine cold winter day - Jim \+ I had bkf. together. Before 10 I went to Dr. Brown - he stripped the adhesive plaster strips off my injured rib taking a little of the skin in one or two places - on examination said there was a small pleurisy spot - when the skin heals he wishes me to come again \+ then he will apply an external counter irritant - he says that the disappearance of pus in my urine may possibly be permanent. He weighed me \+ to the gratification of both of us, found that in the past fortnight I have gained 1¼ lb - good sign - I now weigh, with clothes, 108 lbs. Then to 2. Nak back - then to Halmenfelds, got milk - stopped at studio then to Touraine - Prepared my own lunch after 3 o'clk. Ralph \+ I went to studio again went over the many books presented to him \+ Jim in their youthful days, trying to decide what disposition to make of them - then to our Rooms - at 6 had a short rest - at 7 - Jim, Ralph \+ I went to Manhattan Hotel \+ had dinner - after that, to Hippodrome - 3 9th Ave. \+ 43d St. \+ I saw the Remarkable show there - Claire Heliot \+ the dozen big lions that she whipped about like so many dogs \+ the funny burlesque that followed with make-up lions - the one uncanny, the other absurd - the wonderful acrobatic feats - bicycle riders \+ beyond all, a splendid culmination - the Court of the Golden Fountains wound up a very interesting show - the last Tableau are as fine I think as anything I ever saw on the stage - two others come to mind, both seen in London in 1892 - the Xmas pantomime at Drury Lane \+ Kiralfy's Venice - To bed at midnight

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 3, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day - a very light sort of mizzle in the mft. with a steady rain at night - Jim \+ I bkf. together - I spent a large part of the mft. working over my accts. It was after 11 when I walked down to Diner \+ Amend. Cor. 3d Ave \+ 18th St. \+ then back again, stopping at the studio. It was after 1 when I got to my Rooms \+ 2 o'clk. when I had my junkett lunch - Ralph lunched with Jim - Man from Lock's put up Jim's swiss curtains in his Room - much of my afternoon was spent on my lounge recovering from the dissipation of last night. Lloyd Holsapple was to dine with us at 6.45 but there was a block in the subway \+ he did not get here till 7.15 - consequently our dinner was hurried. Jim \+ Holsapple had to go without dessert - Ralph had to hurry his as he had a theatre engagement with a friend - I finished mine deliberately \+ podged about in my room till after 10 - then to bed.
