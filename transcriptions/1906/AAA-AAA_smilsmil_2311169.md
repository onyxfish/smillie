## Left Page

### Sat. May 12, 1906

**Weather:** Pleasant m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfst. I spent some time in my Room writing notes — then to Century + was there till near 12. Reading + making notes that Charl may use, if he pleases, when he shows my W.J. Lantern slides at the Century to night. — I prepared my lunch at studio + continued writing notes — also, some work with the ill. cats. of Etching Club, + [illegible] Seymour Haden's correspondence in the matter of Preface for 1883 publication. — At 4.30 went to Real Estate agt. 5th Ave + 45th S. to get permit to see small house 1242, 36th S. — Stopped at Charl's + got him to go with me — We found the house in such a dilapidated + unsanitary condition that I would not live in it upon any conditions — Rent $2400. — Then to Touraine — dinner alone — soon after 8 went to Century — Charl already there — Hansen the Lantern man, engaged with him in getting things ready for the show — At 8.30 D: Tuttle came as my guest — Promptly at 9 o'clk. the bell was rung + the members flocked in, an unusually large number + good, representative meeting — no time was lost in beginning — Frank Millet introduced Charl + the show was on — Every thing worked very smoothly, C. having planned + provided thoroughly — His remarks were off hand altho' he had prepared notes in case of necessity, + were sufficiently entertaining + instructive — he had a sympathetic audience + held them till 10.30 having shown 180 slides, 16 of them bring, from my West Indian + Venezuela views — It was in all ways a flattering success + he was freely complimented — I got to my Rooms at 11 — no sign of Jim.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 13, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortable day — strong south wind Ther. got up to 85°. I got up at about 8 o'clk — Jim did not come in last night — do not know where he is — I had a good bath + scrub, + at 10 o'clk. had my bkfst. — it was about noon when I started out. Went up to 304 W. 75th S. + left my Copy of Sellstedt's auto bio'. for his autograph — Then I started on a hunt for a small house or an apartment. My attention was taken by a large apartment bldg. cor. West End Ave. + 81st S. — The Wellesley — I went in + the manager was very civil — said no apts. were now vacant but thought one in the third floor might be had next Oct. By courtesy of tenant I was shown two one suite — It was quite ideal + I was greatly pleased — Rent $2700. I tho't I might, by economy, manage to pay that much — I went out to Riverside Park + sat for some time in the shade of a bush dreaming of possibilities — I ret'd to 4th Cent. Stat'n. by sub way + then went to Charl's + had a talk with him — He is evidently much pleased by his Reception at the Century last night — Then to studio + had a light lunch — Then to Touraine after 4 P.m. + found that at 2 o'clk. Jim had phoned that he was at McJowd's at Yonkers ou'. Return in the eve'g. — I set myself to work to analyze my income + outgo for the past seven months (from Oct.) to find the relation of the one to the other under the new style of living — I was much surprised to find that I am really living at a rate that will show a deficit at the end of the year, Oct. 1st — I computed probable expense of house keeping in a $2700 apt. + find I cannot make both ends meet by $500 — This was a sad blow to me — I had dinner alone at 7.30 — then continued my calculations with no better result — Jim came in before 10 — had not thought that I would worry about him. + was repentant.
