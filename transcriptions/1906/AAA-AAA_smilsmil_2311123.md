## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 9, 1906

**Weather:** Charming  **Temp:** 74

Bath at 7 \- Bkfs. at 8.30 \- Ther. on deck 74º at 8 o'clk \- The waiters out in white duck suits \+ the young bloods, male \+ female, in white flannel \- a number of flying fish were seen making long, skimming jumps over the water. The m'f was passed in loitering, with a nap before lunch \- The afternoon, a repetition of the m'f \- nap \+ all \- There were clouds \+ showers about but no one struck us till about 7 P.M. The sunset was fine in some ways, but the clouds were too suggestive of wind \+ rain to be altogether pleasant \- The moonrise (full) was quite dramatic \- it seemed to pop up out of the sea big red \+ luminous \+ then immediately hid itself behind clouds from which it emerged later \+ gave us some glorious moonlight of the pure silver variety \- The band played from 9 to 10, \+ again I dreamed as I listened to familiar airs \+ looked off over the limitless sea tumbling \+ glittering in the mysterious light.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 10, 1906

**Weather:** Glorious  **Temp:** 78º

my bath, with the double port windows wide open, was a delight. Shoals of flying fish were skimming the water \- They are silvery white \+ seem really to fly, rising but little above the water, going 50 or 60 yds \+ rising \+ falling in conformity with the water surface \- Almost everybody in white \- all fans running \- The one in our state Room we allowed to run all night \- Ther. in stateroom at 7 am, 80º on deck 78º The m'f was spent in loafing about, writing parts of letters to Charl. Jim \+ Ralph \+ arranging with W. Askew about inland trips in Jamaica, Venezuela, etc \+ a nap \- Then lunch \- The afternoon showed showery for a time. Interesting sunset \- Splendid moonlight \- a dance on the after deck at 9 \- Slim attendance \- The approach to St. Thomas was more generally interesting \- It could be dimly seen in the dim twilight when we came up from dinner \- It was 9.30 when we first saw the St. T. light \- \+10'5 when the anchor was dropped outside the harbor \- After that we had a quiet, hot night \- not at all restful to me \-

A little flying fish came aboard to-day \+ was cooked by Capt's order \- I don't know who ate it.
