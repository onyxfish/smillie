## Left Page

### Sun. April 8, 1906

**Weather:** Bright + pleasant m't., less pleasant in the afternoon, rain at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright + pleasant m't., less pleasant in the afternoon, rain at night. I got up at 8 + had a good time at my scrub + bkfst. before 10 — then loafing till after 12 — then to studio + after I had some bread + butter — then to Eastman Johnston's funeral at St. George's, Stuyvesant Sq. + 16th. S! I was surprised to find so few people there. I sat with Sam. Colman. after service went to Emma J's — there for an hour or so — then walked over to Mary's — 44 W. 25. S. + saw her + Bessie — felt very much out of sorts — thought it might be grip that had hold of me — got to my rooms about 6 — + had a cup of tea — fooled away the ev'g as best I could. Jim eel'd from Yonkers after 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 9, 1906

**Weather:** A beastly day — Rain + sleet + wind, but  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beastly day — Rain + sleet + wind, but I was not able to be up to enjoy it — was in my bed till after 6 p.m. getting what comfort I could out of the worst head ache I have had for a long time. A special delivery letter came from Ralph at about 8 a.m. saying the he seemed to be in for an attack of Pink eye + was advised by the doctor there to begin his Easter vacation at once. I telegraphed to him at once to come down — He and at 12.30. His right eye is much affected + on my advice he went at once to Dr. Tuttle. As the day wore on my headache wore off + when Jim came in at 6 he + Ralph arranged matters. They dined together + after that Jim went to the Yale Club + engaged a room which he will keep till it is evident what the trouble with R's eye is — I had a light meal served in my room — I dressed + spent the ev'g in my chair reading to Ralph + helping him in the dressing of his eye.
