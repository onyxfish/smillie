## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 6, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Twelfth night at Century.

Fairly pleasant. The day was spent in considerable part at the studio. I found a great box of photos, that had been packed away years ago by Annie — no end of portraits, some very funny, of members of the family showing development thro' many years — a great number of card views at home \+ abroad — In the afternoon about 5 o'clk. I had a rest \+ Jim had a friend to dine with him \+ Ralph dined at Delmonicos with Mr. Brush — one of his Stackley teachers. So I called up Charl \+ invited him to ask me to dine with him — he did so \+ I did so — After dinner I went to the Century Twelfth Night Circus — I got there before 9 — just in time to fall in with the procession \+ get a good seat. Then was much hilarity — some wit \+ some absurd fun. Mr. Curtis was the leading spirit — there was good music \+ first class singing by David Bispham \+ Troyer — the determination of the Centuronians at large to do chorus singing was quite exhilarating — among the literary efforts no one was so enjoyable to me as Hy Vandyke's poem of the Simple Ass. — Joy all around — it was a star as a Spanish dancer — to me he are simply wonderful — a young fellow — Taylor, as a ballet dancer was immense — I was greatly amused on seeing Prof Perry, Hamilton Mabie, Ben Foster \+ other grave men dancing a fandango together in costume — Bispham sang Danny Deever with immense power — the Revels went according to programme till midnight; then for Choate as King was disposed \+ after that it was go as you please — I stayed till near 1 o'clk — then thinking that it would not be safe for me to stay longer I left \+ went to my rooms \+ my bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 7, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasantly cold — the wind at times, was rather strong — I had my bfst' alone at about 9 o'clk. \+ then spent the remainder of the mng in writing — About 1 o'clk Gordon came with an invitation to the boys \+ me to dine with George's family. The boys being dressed we accepted \+ at once set out for Park Ave. \+ 57th St. We had a good dinner \+ a pleasant time — Jim went his way \+ soon after B, Ralph \+ I walked down to the Century \+ went in — a great crowd to see the dressing of the house \+ the fake pictures — the latter are almost all witless \+ clumsy productions. It was after 5 when we got to our rooms. I had a rest — at 7 the boys went down to the Restaurant for their 2d dinner — I did not wish for any thing to eat and went without — Ralph went to Charl's \+ after 9 o'clk. I followed him. I told C. at some length my experiences at Twelfth Night Century frolic. It was near midnight where I got to bed.
