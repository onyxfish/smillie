## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 23, 1906

Kingston. Jamaica.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Blazing hot mt. \+ very brilliant \- we got to the entrance of the Harbor at about 9 o'clk \- but it was after 10 before the Vic. was conyed thro' the narrow entrance \+ into open bay \+ up to the wharf where we went ashore \- The mts from the sea were very fine \+ improved in appearance by the clouds that covered the highest peaks \- In the Harbor were three French War vessels. I got some snap shots that combined mts, town \+ war vessels \- The highest of the mts 7800 ft. we at once got into a vehicle \+ drove to town \+ where the B. spent some time buying photo material. Then more time was lost waiting for a trolley car \- to take us 6 miles to Constant Spring Hotel \- It took us about 40 min. to get there \- we waited a short time \+ the team came as promised by Askew \- Cook's man \- we at once followed on to catch the procession to Castleton, about 13 miles further on \- or about 2/3 of the way across the island at this point \- For a long distance the Road, macadam, was white, dusty and uninteresting \- Crowds of women \+ donkeys passed us on their way to town. The women all bare footed, carrying heavy loads of yams, fruits, etc on their heads. After a long drive we entered the mts \- \+ gradually the scenery grew wild until when we reached the Wag water it was at times grand \- Here the Rain that had for some time threatened commenced falling, at times heavily \- we were able to protect ourselves with carriage curtains \+ suffered no inconvenience further than the loss of the views where the clouds obscured the mts. tops \+ soon after, as we got to the Castleton Gardens, a noble arboretum under English management since 1862. A lunch had been spread in a charming place under some great bunches of bamboo that had tunnels cut into under them \- This had been hurriedly moved up to a cottage at some distance \+ confusion reigned as we all crowded in, more or less wet \+ hungry. I got very little \- the rain held up for a little while \+ the D \+ I wandered in the garden, but saw only a very small portion of it \- Soon after 3 we started on our way back. Rained heavily till we got out of the mts. at Kingston \+ had on all the way, up hill \+ down, always at a rapid trot. It was after 6 \+ quite dark when we drove up to entrance to dock where we left the Vic in the mts \- She was gone \- driving at some distance I saw the launches going back \+ forth to the King St. wharf \- There was no other way for it \- so, we worked our way out, found a cab \+ horse to the wharf \+ got to the ship just before 7 \- got dinner as soon as possible \+ to bed soon after.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 24, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 82°

A delightful day, very hot in sunshine \+ sheltered from the wind \- but in Riding the Tembly, was very pleasant. Up early, had bath \+ bkft. earlier than usual \+ ashore with the Bogwalk Tourists at about 8:30. Most of us were packed into a tram car at Kingsstreet wharf, when too many came, were put on \+ the surplus tourists were put in cabs \+ we went to the R. R. station \+ by special train to Spanish Town \- The old Capital of the Island \- 13 miles \- passing immense orchards of Bananas \- Property of United Fruit Co. We got to Spanish Town at about 10 o'clk. \+ were quickly transferred to vehicles of one kind \+ another \- we are cured a good team \+ comfortable vehicle \+ drove on \- The end of our course being 10 m. away \- for nearly half that distance the Road is uninteresting, but when we entered the Ravine thro' which the Rio Cobre runs it became interesting \- the mts. rising high as we went on \+ the foliage dense \+ varied \- The views along the River were fine \+ I got a number of snaps shots but had to pass fine subjects because the sun was not out. We Reached the end of our drive before 12, there was to be in a rest of half an hour \- We D \+ I wanted to walk back \+ get what views before our team should overtake us \- but it caught us before we got far on \- we made a number of halts to get, I think, some good views \- It was 1:30 before we got to the Rio Cobre Hotel. \+ there little time was spent over a lunch that I did not think much of \- Before 2 we started again \- got to R.R. our special cats were hitched to a request train \+ the negro women in crowds, all carrying big bundles \+ Riding 3rd class, were a picturesque feature. We got to Kingston before 4 o'clk \+ did a little shopping \- K. does not impress me as favorably as Port of Spain \- it does not look prosperous \- it lacks smartness of appearance \- negroes \+ quadcolor are uppermost very where. I got to the ship at about 5 o'clk. Had a rest \- Dinner at 7. After that I slept in a chair on deck till after 10 \- then I wrote up. The lights were put out in library at 11. Trouble with my Bowels \- near midnight \- before I got settled.
