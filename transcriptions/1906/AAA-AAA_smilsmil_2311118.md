## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 30, 1906

**Weather:** A cloudless, mild \+ spring-like day  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent a large part of the m't. in my room writing in my acct. books record of recent transactions in the investment of the most of the proceeds of sale of 110 E. 38th St. Geo. came in for awhile. It was almost noon when I went down to 2°. Mat°. \+ put Am. Tobacco \+ Central Leather securities in Safe Deposit. Then around by Studio \+ to Touraine where, in the seclusion of my bed rooms, — the other rooms being held by house cleaners — I prepared some junket for myself at 2 o'clk — Again the afternoon got away from me with little done — I forgot even to make use of the Lotos Club ticket that Ge°. Kindly gave me — \+ forgot also, till too late, that this eve'f Mr. Rob't. W-de Forest's Reception to Mr. Edw'l Robinson, the new curator of Paintings — Met. Mus. of Art \+ co take place — Jim \+ I went to the annual meeting of Geographical Socy \+ heard Poultney Bigelow give a wandering talk about Am. Colonization in the Tropics — there were many allusions, more or less veiled, to his collision with the Panama Canal authorities — He attempted nothing in the way of serious argument or justification — Trusting to Ridicule \+ a few very poor lantern slides from Photos he had taken at Colon.
I went shopping at 5 oclk — bought shirts \+ socks — met in 23d. St. — Lillie Cheeny — \+ almost many d —

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 31, 1906

**Weather:** A dull, foggy m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- some sickly sunshine about the middle of the day — clouds — a sprinkle of rain — clear moonlight at night —
I was awakened at 4.15 this m't. by Jim coming in bringing Harry Gored with him — I could not get to sleep again I was so depressed — I got up at the usual time \+ at 8 breakfasted alone — I was sick \+ completely unnerved — I could not regain my equilibrium during the day — In the m't. I went to Safe Deposit to look up the certificates of membership as "patrons" of Met. Museum of Art of Jim \+ Self, coming on presently from Annie — this because I have just rec'd. a note from the Secy. requesting me to become a member — There to studio — met Jake \+ he tried to confort me — while he was there a dealer \+ collector of old prints of N.Y. City came to see if I had any p'ts that I would let him have from the old plates enq'd. in 1830-) for Bourne — To Touraine at 1 \+ had an egg, beaten up — Then had some troubled sleep — At 3.30 took check to Geo. Green who janitors my studio bldg — then to studio — but I could do nothing back to my rooms — Jim came in at 6.30 \+ we had dinner at 7 in m'f. I had a quiet \+ serious talk with him \+ I can only hope that it may have some effect.
