## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 3, 1906

**Weather:** Pleasant day altho' overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

at 7.30 went to Man. Storage — then to Douglass — then to studio — saw George — then to Wanamaker's — got knives — then ret. to home — Had lunch at 1 o'clk — Men Hugh Douglas came \+ we consulted about possible changes of color on dining room \+ parlor walls — About 3 I went to studio again \+ had a man help me get some storage trunks from cellar up to studio. It was 5 o'clk before I started for home — found Storage men taking away empty boxes \+ barrels. Marie Patterson, a colored woman, at work all day cleaning closets \+ windows.
Dinner at 6.30 alone — Jim is with the [illegible] I think to-night — I loafed, feeling too tired to attack the work about me.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 4, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A light drizzle for a short time in the m'f. Some attempts at sunshine in the afternoon — [illegible] alone at about 8 o'clk. It was after 10 when I got to Anderson's sup. in 3d Ave. am? with him to call at studio for trunk having table \+ bed linen bet. 12 \+ 1 o'clk — then I went to Wanamaker's to arrange for return of articles bought for laundry, having found that they will not be needed — Marie Patterson doing the wash to-day — To studio before 12 — Anderson came \+ got trunk before 1 — then home to lunch — Man from Storage came \+ paid me $2- for broken crockery — I spent the afternoon in doors — some trouble in getting old bureau into Katie's bed room on 8th floor — I had a rest — Jim came in before 6 \+ rushed off as soon a dressed, without dinner — an engage. went at 7 Reg. Armory — Reception to [illegible] train of marks men — doing a little work. Sorting contents of trunk [illegible] up for studio —

larger amt. of wine passed than usual \+ some pus.
