## Left Page

### Wed. July 25, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day — brilliant, cool & breezy — After bkfst. I wrote for an hour or so & then R. \+ I went down to Ferd. Beede's to make enquiries about a horse, R. wants to hire one if he can — Beede not at home — Met J. H. Maghe & wandered about his garden with him — I carried a large head of lettuce when we left — Then up the hill — R. went to the house with the lettuce & I went on to P.O. — then home & had dinner — At 3.30 R. & I tramped to Roaring Brook fall, taking middle trail which brought us out at a pool about halfway from top of fall — I took my camera to get some shots at a mass of rock that I want some mem. of to aid me in repainting a part of my 32×44 "Source of the River". We got home at 5.30 & I felt rather tired & rested till near dinner time — 6.30 — Eve'g on piazza till 9 o'clk. — Then in — a small fire & reading till after 10 — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 26, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day in spite of some weather signs to the contrary — rather warm & muggy — a considerable part of the m'g. after bkfst. was given to clearing out a saw-dust block-ade of traps under ice box in cold room — After 11 R. & I went to the Library & read there till 12 — then walked up Reserve Road, across to swimming pool & back by trail on the other side of the river — I stopped at P.O. wrote notes to Jim & Charl & then home to dinner at 1.5 — little accomplished during the after-noon — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g on piazza till 8.30 then in & reading aloud till after 10.
