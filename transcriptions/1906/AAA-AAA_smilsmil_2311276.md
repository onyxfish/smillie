## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 10, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day \- Ice \& sleet made the streets almost impassable in the m\[orning\] \-, moist all day \& some heavy rain at night \- An unusual amt. of pus in the urine passed last night \- more than showed yesterday or any time since I was in hospital more than a year ago. At 9.30 I called D\[octo\]r. T \- up on phone \& told him of it \- He is now inclined to think that the vertigo of two days past may have been caused by renal trouble \& advises me to be careful of taking cold \- To-day there is little vertigo remaining \- After 10 I called up manager of Glas\-Wernicke Co \- \& told him that I had overestimated my book-case requirements \& said that I wished to return say 4 of the 12\-unit sections \- he assured \- I did not go out of doors all day but worked quite steadily at assorting my books which are in a perfect muddle of confusion \- lunch at 1.30 \- work continued thro' the afternoon \- Jim came in \& we had dinner at 6.30 \- evening with books \& newspapers \- Jim tired \& went to bed early \-

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 11, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine winter day, bright, cold \& windy. B\&b\. at 8.30 \& after 10 went down to Rawly's \- the Mirror man who is holding up my hall mirror \- found the glass in his back shop \- poor excuses, promised as usual to send it up this afternoon \- but he did it \- Then I walked from 50th S\- down Bdway to 34 \- went to Buskirks \& ordered stationery \- then walked to studio \& spent some time there overhauling the lot of Hinshelwood P\[hoto\]s that I have \- Home to lunch before 2 \- worked all the afternoon trying to get my books into some orderly arrangement on the shelves \- Had dinner alone at 6.30 \& worked till after 10 among the books \- To bed after 11 completely tired out
