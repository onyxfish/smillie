## Left Page

### Wed. March 21, 1906

**Weather:** Fine day overhead — below freezing  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfs. at 8.30 — to studio at 10 — worked all day at mounting Etching Pp, interrupted by carpenter who put up shelf for filter in my closet \+ plumbers who cut water pipe to insert tap for filter — I prepared my lunch at 1.30 — eggs, Zweiback, Triscuit \+ cherries — worked till near 6 — Went to Fontaine \+ had a rest before dinner with Jim at 7 — Even'g, newspaper \+ cataloguing negatives — I did not feel like going to Water-Color meeting, being tired \+ objecting to the small, ill ventilated room where the meetings are held in Sherwood Studio Bldg. \+ where all the fellows smoke till the air is blue \+ foul.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 22, 1906

**Weather:** Bright, cold \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up soon after 7 — my head swimming — troubled me all day — I got to studio a little after 10 — after writing a note to Clarence Smith with reference to Southack, Pease \+ Elliman business — he sent me copies of his correspondence with P. \+ E. — showing clearly that they have deliberately declined to keep their written promise to me — at studio I found that the plumber had got my Pasteur filter in place \+ had turned on the water — I worked over \+ among my etching Pp — all the m'g, working with crayon on some of the Pp. from unfinished plates — at 10 o'clk. I went to Fontaine \+ had a light lunch — after it my head was so troublesome that I lay on my lounge for an hour — at 3.30 I went to Mary's \+ consulted with her about purchasing sheets for Jim's bed \+ nieve — then to Wannamaker's \+ bought sheets, an umbrella — a small Refrigerator for studio \+ rubbers — walked up to Fontaine at 6 — I had bread \+ tea instead of dinner at 7 — Even'g Jim went to Reg's drill with Kinney — I remained in my rooms \+ did some work on cataloguing negatives —
