## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 26, 1906

**Weather:** Overcast, mild & quiet  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, mild & quiet — nights rest much broken after 4.30 a.m. consequently did not get up to my bkfst. as I had intended — dressed after 9 o'clk. After 10 I went to bank — then to Brooks Bros. Then to Pomroy's in 23d S. & had Truss adjusted — then to Man. Storage Co. then to Lincoln Nat'l. Bank for Jim's pass book, which could not be found — got check book for him — then home & had lunch before 2 o'clk. Some reading — then feeling the want of sleep, lost last night I had a nap. Dinner at 6.30 alone — Jim is now driven by overwork & will have to remain down town for the evenings this week. Eve'g balancing bank-book etc.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 27, 1906

**Weather:** Bright & windy m'f. cloudy p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & windy m'f. — cloudy p.m. — I made a break this m'f. got up & had my bkfst. in dining room. After 10 I went down to Ham-Am. S.S. Line — 35 Bdway. and interviewed Mr. Schmalcke & from him learned that I had arranged a correct itinerary, that no rooms were yet engaged on the Vic. for Jamaica or the Prinz August for Cartagena \+ Port Limon — so I put down my name for 88 on Vic. & 8 on August, to leave N.Y. Feb. 20 — fare round trip $140 — all subject to approval of Dr. Tuttle. Then to Charl's office — not in. Then to Cuelen's — then by subway home at 12. Wrote to Charl & to Tuttle — lunch at 1 — wrote to Bessie — Read — had a nap — had a promenade on West End Ave — Jim was late — dinner at 7 — Eve'g alone.
