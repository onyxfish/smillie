## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 16, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beautiful day - but much warmer. A busy day with the carpenters - I got them to work on chimney finish in large room soon after bkfst. \+ had to follow them so closely in the development of my plan that it was not till after 3 o'clk that I was able to get at the work of re-stretching the burlap - I had a stretching frame made - made paste - got heavy paper - soaked it \+ the burlap - pasted the two together \+ then tacked them on the frame. The progress of the men was slow as the difficulties in managing such material is unusually great. I went to P.O. at 12.30. then dinner. at 5.30 being very tired I lay down \+ had a rest till tea time an hour later. R. \+ I sat on the n. piazza till 9.15.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 17, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Carpenters finished.
Library entertainment.

August weather again - clear - Mer. got up to 84°. indoors - tempered by strong breeze. Up at 6.30 \+ got some work done before bkfst. Carpenters came late, with excuses. They worked till about 4 when, their work being done I gave them their discharge. While I was at dinner they bungled the mitring of finish on mantel-shelf irreparably cutting one end of the worn-eaten slab 1/2" short on the point destroying the symmetry of the ends \+ making it necessary to cut 1/2" from end of shelf. I was greatly vexed. went to P.O. at 12.30 - letter from D. Gester asking me to send press to N.Y. city instead of to Long Lake. Ralph engaged in dark Room, fitting my 5×4 camera to make enlargements. He was unexpectedly successful in getting some fair results on old Bromide paper given to him by Chas. It is 8 or 10 years old. After the carpenters left I cleaned up \+ then I had a rest — Tea at 6.30. after that I dressed \+ at 8 o'clk. R. \+ I went to the Club House where an entertainment was given to aid the library. There was a surprising number of people present \+ the scene in the parlor was really a gay one. Young Alex. White made a very good master of ceremonies \+ was amusing \+ clever in his presentation of the business of the occasion. Performance began at 9 \+ closed about 10.30 the greater part of the time being given to the Reading of Miss Kate Hillard. Mrs. [illegible] Janeway sang four or five little songs — French \+ English \+ Lawrence Atlar played upon the piano twice - we got home at about 11.
