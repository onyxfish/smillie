## Left Page

### Tues. June 19, 1906

*Ralph came from N.H.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day. Got up with a headache that stuck to me all day \+ used me up at night. I got to studio about the usual time \+ inspite of headache worked on plate with graver \- having lunch in studio, till after 4 p.m \- then to Touraine \+ later Ralph came in \- down from New-Haven for two or three days \- I sat with him for some time, then gave up to my headache \+ stretched on my lounge. Jim \+ Ralph had dinner \- I went without \- after a sleep I wake feeling somewhat better \- I got to bed at about 1 A.m.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 20, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunshine at last \- \+ a pretty warm day. Woke at 4.30 \+ commenced with a Castor oil cocktail \- which demanded attention, \+ got it, soon after 7 \- I wrote to Winch \+ to Beede \+ after bkfst \- at 8, to Mr. Kent. It was after 10 when I got to studio. Worked with graver on etched plate, \+ had my lunch in studio after 1 o'clk \- Then went to Stikemans, 6 Ave. \+ 26th St. \+ arranged the Haden Preface correspondence as I wish \+ bound in with the Etching Club. Cats Then back to studio, \+ worked with graver till almost 5.30 \- I am disappointed to find that I have not been able to realize my intention to treat this particular etching in style different from my previous work \- Now that it is bitten I find that it requires (or seems to) the same old kind of graver work to get satisfactory texture \+ color \- this discourages me. Because in spite of what seemed to me clear purpose \+ distinct plan of work I have not been able to get away from myself \- I am no longer plastic \- I'm stereotyped \- I had a short walk before going to my rooms \- Ralph \+ then Jim' showed that overwork as it keeps him down town \- R \+ I went to Manhattan to dine \- there we looked over newly mounted photos \- I got to bed early.
