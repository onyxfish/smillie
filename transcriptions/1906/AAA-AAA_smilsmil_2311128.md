### Mon. Feb. 19, 1906

*Caracas, Venezuela — Grand Hotel Klindт — Room 55*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully fine day — we got to La Guayr ra at about 6 A.M. Rushed both oblg — got ashore at 8.30 already enough of the passengers had got ahead of me to crowd the first section of our special train — I got a good seat in the 2d section that got away at 9 o'clk — I had little chance to look about La Guayra m — It is a small place at the foot of steep mts — at a small dock there was a black steamer that looked like a freight but as she fired a salute from two guns on her deck she was reported to be a Venezuelan war vessel — We commenced an ascent from the start — The distance to Caracas in direct line is 6½ miles — by Rail it is 23 m — The highest point reached is 4700 ft — The valley in which Caracas lies is 3000 ft above sea level — The views from the R.R. were wonderful \+ absolutely unlike anything that I had ever seen — The mts. are great masses of earth seamed by deep ravines down which small streams flowed \+ in which there was heavy tropical growth — The color of the foot hills is generally red — the mts are brown — in rainy season they are green — The views as we rose \+ the expanse of the sea below us grew wider — cannot be adequately described — At the highest point it was an abyss \+ the dis. of mother of pearl" — The mt. sides seemed to stand at an angle of 60° — narrow plains covered with trees extended at the first fringed with a wavering line of [illegible] — The R.R. is narrow gauge — The cars are "American style" \+ the run of 23 miles was done in a little more than 2 hours — Carriages met us at Station P.T.S.) were promptly taken to our hotel \+ we went to our room on upper floor. A tennis court — We had lunch at 12 \+ after that prowled till 2.30 going into the Govt. building — Senate \+ Rep'n Chamber — Great reception Room — etc — at 2.30 we went to hotel — carriages in waiting — P.T.S.) were fortunate in getting one for two — an interpreter rode with the driver — we went to Pantheon — then to Palace which was opened to us, all but the private rooms — we were told that we were privileged to photo. anything — then to the Calvary overlooking the city — \+ The valley beyond — small reservoir there — New view fine — Then to hotel, near 5 — having been out 2½ hours — The streets all narrow — a few paved with asphalt, some stone \+ others without anything — Again we wandered — attracted by the University bldg — a party of students invited us, on allowing us around — little was said that could be understood but their intentions were good \+ their efforts were earnest — what we saw was interesting — Then to hotel \+ had a little rest before dinner at 6.30 which was fairly good to my taste — Clef. The Presidents band gave us a complimentary concert — Their string of opposition in our hotel on Bolivar Sq — It was a large band — perhaps 80 strong \+ good — played our national airs to great applause — arc lights high overhead threw light on a great crowd of very mixed character.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 20, 1906

*Puerto Cabello \+ at sea.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very cool, clouded out. but as the day passed on we got down to lower levels the heat increased till we suffered very much in the cars — Between 1 \+ 3 o'clk I tested my ther. \+ in the sunshine it went to 107° \+ in the shade of the interior of the car it stood at 92° — With dust, jetting, noise \+ fatigue it was a trying time after 1 o'clk. Till after 4 when we were refreshed by a sea breeze — I got up after sleeping un-comfortably cold, at 5.45 \+ had bkfp — at 6.30 — at 7.15 got to Station \+ left at 7.30. I secured a good seat on the best side of the car. for a time we saw thro a very fertile valley with great cane fields \+ sugar mills — Then we climbed the mt. \+ commenced the long descent, when the wonders of yesterday were repeated, with the exception of the view of the ocean — what we saw was an ocean of mts, brown, frizled \+ worn into deep gullies — we wound around innumerable curves, rounding the nose of a promontory \+ bridging the back of a gully — wild views changing with amazing rapidity — I tried in vain to get a good snap shot from the window but my clott is not quick enough \+ the swinging of the car changed the view out of view so rapidly that I failed — it was about 11.30 when we got to La Victoria. There we found tables for lunch abroad near the station — I was fortunate enough to get a seat at the first lunch. Mass was not accommodation for all to be served at once — The food was only fairly good \+ served with wild confusion — all were at mid place an hour \+ a half before we started a special car bringing two passengers who were left at Caracas — one Ried, from here on to Valencia who this great coffee growing districts — The Lake of Valencia is a fine sheet of water — The mt. outlines were picturesque, the sky, color of mts. and color making me think of the Champlain spread out as seen from the west side of Champlain — From Valencia the R.R. passed thro a wild ravine at last reaching the sea shore — Then we turned sharply to the Right \+ for half an hour ran near to the sea passing groves of the giant cactus, looking like dead trees denuded of leaves \+ smaller branches — At last, at about 6 when it was quite dusk we reached Puerto Cabello, too late to see anything of it — Our train ran out upon a pier — The Vic's flotilla of boats was waiting for us \+ to them we quickly transferred ourselves \+ were soon aboard — we were fixed up quickly \+ at 7.30 had dinner \+ to bed at 9.30 The stewards said that by 11 o'clk every body was in bed — we were a tired lot.
