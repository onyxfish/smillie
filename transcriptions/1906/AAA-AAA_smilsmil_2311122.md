## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 7, 1906

**Weather:** Run. 313 m.  **Temp:** 45

A brilliant breezy m'f. with considerable sea which we were crossing at an acute angle, consequently there was a great deal of rolling but very little pitching — this disturbed many passengers \+ there was a thin attendance at bkfst. Two ladies only up to 9 o'clk. Later in the day there was less swell \+ much cloudiness — at night, a few clouds \+ glorious moonlight — Two nights before full — the temp. going higher \+ sea quieter all the time.
I got up at 6.30 after a sleepless but very quiet night. At 7 had a bath \+ ran down \+ bkfst at 8 — I did a good deal of walking but find that owing to lack of strength in my legs I do it in pitifully drunken shape. Before 11 I went to my bunk \+ slept soundly till wakened for lunch at 1. After that, more walking \+ more sound sleeping. Dinner at 7 — about as good as Table'd hote at The Park Ave. hotel, N.Y. We \+ more wandering — a long chat with Dallenbaugh.
In the m'f Capt. H. Brunswig invited me to his cabin, showed me his artist's album with water-color sketches \+ asked me to add one to it. Concert by band on deck at 9 p.m.
Last night our state room was uncomfortably warm (steam being left on) \+ bed clothes heavy. The Thes. got up to 75° at one time.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 8, 1906

**Weather:** Run 333.  **Temp:** 68°

Beautiful m'f. — light showers about 1 o'clk — clouds \+ sunshine later followed by a moonlight night. I was up before 7 after a good night's rest \+ had my bath — breakfast soon after 8 after a brief breezing on deck. A "full house" at bkfst! The Capt appeared in white duck trousers \+ shoes — over coats were generally discarded \+ many spring suits were out. The Ther. on deck stood at 64° when I went out \+ by 3 o'clk. got 4° higher — the white caps disappeared from the waves but a long swell continued to which Victoria rolled in response. It is still rather hard for me to walk the deck. I met Mr. \+ Mrs. Dietrich \+ had a pleasant chat with them. I had a nap before lunch \+ while at lunch there was a light shower \+ others about. Later the horizon was clear of them. I kept on my feet pretty constantly wandering about \+ towards dusk had another nap. Dinner at 7 — most of the electric fans were running \+ there was a full attendance at the tables. Some full dress, but not much. Glorious moonlight — band played on deck from 9 to 10 — now \+ then something old \+ familiar, as "Ah I have sighed to leave thee" from Maritana — Sue Post \+ I tried to sing that as a duett to my accompaniment on the guitar, more than a thousand years ago — as I gazed over the moonlit sea it made me feel — I don't know how — Ther. at 11 p.m. 71°
