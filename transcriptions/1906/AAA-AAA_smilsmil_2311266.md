## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 20, 1906

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day — mild \+ hazy — awake at 7 \+ had my bkfp's served to me in bed — then read my newspaper before I got up — washed \+ dressed after 9 o'clk — I found waiting on myself rather tiresome — went out \+ walked on Riverside drive for some time then in \+ loafed \+ read — Kate served me with a tempting bird at 1 o'clk — again I went out \+ prowled about the streets, but felt very weak in the knees — my hearing seems to be improving very slowly — After I came in Young lock came \+ I was with him till about 4.30 getting out curtains \+ deciding where \+ how it would be best to place them. I was very tired \+ lay upon my bed till after 6 — had my eveg meal at 6.30 — Jim not at home — dull — he came in at about 9, we had some talk together of our financial relations there have been no definite arrangements since he came of age —[illegible — ~3 words]

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 21, 1906

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, sad, quiet day with light rain late in the afternoon — Bkfp. in bed — Kate Leonard is determined to coddle me — before I got up George came in — it was 10.30 before I was up \+ dressed — After some writing I went out \+ had a half hours walk on Riverside Drive. I am much pleased to find that I enjoy so much my surroundings here — I thought, before I left no, that I could never be reconciled to living in any other part of the city, but already I find myself making comparisons favorable to my new environment. Dinner at 1 — some reading \+ writing \+ at 3 o'clk. again out for a half hours walk — the air rather raw — after 4 o'clk. on bed for some time — After a conference with Kate \+ concerning the laundry work that is now unsatisfactorily done by Mrs. Patterson, agreed to raise Kate Bradley's wages from $20 to $25 a month if she would do it — at 7 had dinner, Jim's friend Harold Stowe dining with us \+ staying till 9 — I wearied thro' the eveng — I hardly know how.
