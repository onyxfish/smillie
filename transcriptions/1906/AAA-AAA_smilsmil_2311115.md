## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 24, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day, bright & fresh but not really cold. To Charls before 10 & gave him my check for $350.10, paymt. for Am. Tobacco & Cent. leather securities purchased for me — Then to studio & spent the m'f. there till near noon. Then to Kimmel & Voigt's & got Colman, Gifford & Bellows plates, done for "Poets & Etchers" published by Osgood many years ago. Then to my rooms & had a light lunch — In the afternoon I went on a hunt for the parents of Ernest Roth. I found his mother at 263 2362 First Ave. near 121 st S. & got from her some information as to the form in which they sent money to Ernest. Then I did some exploring as it was a part of the city where I had never been before — I crossed & ret. over the Willis Ave. bridge & wandered to the foot of 121 st S. New R.R. to my rooms by way of 2d. Ave. elevated. Jim & I dined at The Touraine. Later I went to Charls & learned that Am. Tobacco — Am. Can & Cent. leather to the amt. of $2020 had been bought for Jim's acct. I hope that there has been no mistake in investing in these securities — but he must have more income to meet his requirements than the 3 or 4% of safe investments would give him & I do not have enough to make good his deficits — The matter was put before him fully & he was told to deliberate & make his decision entirely upon his own responsibility — The risks that he & I have now assumed weigh heavily on my mind.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 25, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright day & colder — got up with a headache that bothered me all day. Before ten o'clk. went to Charls taking Jim's check for $2020 in paymt. for investments purchased on his acct. yesterday. Then to studio & spent a dirty, strenuous m'f. cleaning out the wash closet. It was nearly 2 o'clk. when I got to my rooms & had a very simple lunch. Then rested for an hour — found enough to busy myself till about 5.30 — then I walked up 5th Ave. to 59th S. & back — 2 miles — without discomfort or sense of weariness. This is very encouraging to me — Ten days ago I was reeling with vertigo & my digestive apparatus seemed to be hopelessly out of order. It seems to be impossible to regulate my machinery so as to run evenly. I am trying to be philosophic under these changes. Dinner at 7 — at 8.30 went to Charls thinking that he might have some of the securities recently purchased for me — but he did not — saw a lot more of his lantern slides.
