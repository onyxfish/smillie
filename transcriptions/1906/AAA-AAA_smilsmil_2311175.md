## Left Page

### Thur. May 24, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot weather again - it grew to be uncomfortably hot as the day advanced - I got up early + before bkft. wrote a note to Seardon the Sup'- under Heymann the son in law of Eugene Rothschild the owner of Wellesley sending a check for Oct. Rent + asking him to put into writing his promise that I should have the privilege of transferring my lease to any other apart. ment when it might be to rent if it pleased me better than the one I've taken - at 10 o'clk I went to Second Nat'l Bank - then to Studio + prepared a lot of photos (foreign) for mounting at 12.30 I went to my rooms at the Touraine + dropped the awnings + pulled down the dark shades to keep it cool - then again to the Studio + had my lunch - Finished the work on Studios + now have 102 Ready to mount. At 3.30 I went down town to Carters, 12 Vandewater St - I bought some medium plate printing oil - then to Cullens with my photos. + spent nearly an hour talking with him - It was nearly 8 when I got to Touraine - at 7 Jim + I had dinner - Later he went to the Dielmans + I spent the cost alone, Reading, writing etc.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 25, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cooler to-day - strong breeze, but still quite warm - Before bkft. I wrote to Mr. Brady, sister of Kate Leonard, about the apartment I have leased - so that she may inform Kate. After bkft. to Studio at about 10 o'clk. - worked at different things till 12 then went to W. C. Cullen's with the portrait of Father that I painted in miniature some time ago intending to etch it, + left it there to have a photo reduction made to size of proposed etching - Then to Chas'. + saw him for a few minutes - then back to Studio at 1.15 - had lunch + then to work on plate "Roadside Chapel near Dijon". I etched in a bit of tree work back of building + spent rest of p.m. scraping down embellien parts - I asked Geo. to what extent he would be incommoded by the withdrawal of my furniture - What furniture, he asked. I told him I wd. want my dining room furniture + some others when I moved to my new apartments - + he answered with surprise - Why! I thought you had given us all that - This is extremely unpleasant to me + what Mary had warned me to be careful about owing to the difficulty of making Nelly understand - I tried + thought I had succeeded in making her understand that only a part of what I let them have was a gift. Jim + I dined together at 7.30 - Evg. I wrote to Ralph.
