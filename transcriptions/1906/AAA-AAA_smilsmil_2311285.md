## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 28, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very disagreeable day - dark, foggy, rain and slimy - Bkfs. at 9 - It was near 11 before I left home - went to store of T. B. Adams - 6th Ave. bet. 40th & 41st. St. to get silk lamp shade - They made only glass ones - Then thro' to 5th Ave. \+ walked to Nos's gallery \+ saw Caryl Coleman's pastels of Vesuvius in eruption, from Capri - interesting but not great - as the subject is - Then to studio - Tackled the problem of getting my stands (Smillie Collection) to work but had to give it up - my contrivance is too weak \+ shaky - I fixed my lunch \+ ate it before 2 - Raelph came in after 3 \+ I left the studio with him - he went to electricians for supplies \+ I went home -- feeling out of sorts - stomach \+ bowels. I lay on my lounge. Dinner at 6.30 - Jim went to bed at once after dinner - Raelph made scale drawing for an enlarging box (photo) - I sat in my chair, uncomfortable, reading till 10 - Then to bed -

Got a note from young Walter Cook announcing his engagement to Miss Margaret C. Roper - Pelkam Manor.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 29, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day - dark, foggy, rain - Bkfs. at 8.30 - just as I was about starting for studio at 10 Raelph got up saying that he thought he had mumps, his neck was swollen on right side - he said a young fellow, having a room in his bldg. had been down with mumps \+ he (R.) had been expecting it - I phoned to Dr. Tuttle \+ he came at about noon \+ after carefully looking him over said he did not think it mumps - some lymphatics were swollen but the salivary glands seemed to be normal - There upon R. took up his bed \+ walked - lunch at near 2 o'clk - after that I went to butcher \+ grocer \+ then home - The rugs came from Wild's \+ greatly improved the appearance of the apartments - Dinner at 6.30 - After that Jim went to Sew? Cook's on invitation from Louise - Raelph Read \+ I experimented with photo. printing.
