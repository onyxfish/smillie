## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 10, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

For a little time the mt was overcast then the day cleared + was the tenth in line, as beautiful as any of its predecessors. I was up + thro' all mt duties by 10 o'clk. I got a note from Geo. saying he was detained at home by a cold. I sat again by the open window. After 12 went out with miss D. + walked on sunny side of Ave for about 25 m. Dinner about 1. After 2 I had a poor nap. Afternoon I wrote a draft of a letter to Ms. J. M. Yale. I waited impatienthy for Dr. Tutille. This is the third day that he has not been here. At 7 o'clk I 'phoned to him about my condition — sub-normal Temp — between 97° + 98°. He says it will go up to normal when I get more strength + can exercise. Jim home in the evg.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 11, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bad weather at last — saw n. easter with rain most of the day. Awoke at 8 after a restless night. It was 11 before I got thro' bkft, bath &c — my head all in a cloud again — but otherwise no worse. Dr. Tutille came soon after I got out into parlor + spent more than an hour with me putting every thing in as easy + favorable a light as possible. Says he will call next Wed. I had my dinner served to me in the parlor. Afe. Kinney came + dined with Jim. At 3 I lay down + had a short nap. While I was yet on the bed Charl came + stayed with me an hour or more. After he left I got up + loafed about till supper time. Jim had Rel?. + we all suppered together in the dining room. Later Kinney ret? + with Jim went out. I fooled the evg away. I am no doubt slowly gaining in strength but the condition of my head + hearing continues very distressing. My hearing yesterday the day before was greatly better than it is to-day probably owing to the weather. My Temps. still continues below 98° down to 97°.
