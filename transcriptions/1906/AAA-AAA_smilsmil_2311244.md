## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 7, 1906

**Weather:** mixed  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mixed weather again — bright sunshine in the mt. then strong wind — gray clouds at noon. Rain — some sunshine in the afternoon — cloudy eve'g. — Up at 7 — had a bath in muddy Croton — bkf's. at 9 — at 10.30 walked up Riverside drive as far as 98th St. hurried home on acct. of Rain. I did some good work in getting an approach to order — Dinner at 2 — I worked most of the p.m. At 6 went out \+ had a short walk — enjoyed the twilight over the river — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g, after some reading, Jim \+ I moved the books \+ piled them in the middle of the floor preparing for Douglas who is to come to-morrow to fix the walls if possible — at 9.30 Leroy'd Holsapple came \+ he \+ Jim went out for a walk.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 8, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day — after bkf's. to grocer's, butcher's, bakers — then to cor. Am." Ave. \+ 82d St. and registered for voting. Then home \+ waited for Douglas, he did not come — then down to Wanamaker's \+ was there till 12.30 shopping — then home to lunch — after 2 went to Lincoln Bank \+ left Jim's book to be balanced — then to Douglas's — then to studio \+ unpacked box that was sent from St. Hubert's. It was 5 o'clk when I got home — a rest — dinner at 6.30 Eve'g — fooling about — little accomplish'd — to bed at 11.
