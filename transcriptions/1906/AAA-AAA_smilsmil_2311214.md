## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 8, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A charming day \- cool & breezy \- of course it was pretty hot in the sunshine but the breeze are always refreshing. After an uncomfortable night a miserable headache got the better of me soon after 4 Am. & between that & 7 oclk. I took caffiene Acetanelied & Bromo Seltzer \- I was able to get up & keep about during the day & that was all. I had no dinner. The carpenters were on hand soon after 7 & stuck to their work closely which progressed favorably. I hung about giving directions. Late in the afternoon Ralph & I had a little ramble over the Neilson hill. Tea at 6.30 \- then on Mr. Piazza till near 9 \- then in & a short story by F. H. Smith & at 9.30 we went to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 9, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very delightful day. breezy & cool \- again my time was largely taken up following up the carpenters who finished the ceiling up & the partitions removed. I gave orders for lumber for board & walk between house & annex \- for more shelving for new linen closet. At noon I went for mail \- while waiting for it Mr. Marquand came & made a proposition. He asked for an option for rental of the Ark next summer. To run till next March. & then if nothing had transpired to prevent his taking he would rent it \- His father in laws health is very precarious & his death might disarrange his plans. I assented to his proposition. Dinner about 1 oclk. I was made uneasy in the afternoon by the peculiar action of my heart. When I awoke in the nt. as I lay with my ear in my pillow I noticed that it was running slow \- I tried it & found that pulsations ran only 65 to the minute in the afternoon my attention was again called to it when I found it running 85 to the minute \- Later I passed a small stone \- at least I suppose I did \- there was sharp pain on urinating, followed by a little blood & prompt swelling of the member. These conditions made me very blue. Tea at 6.30 \- Evey. about 7.30 Mr. Hardenburg called for Ralph \- he went over there \- I dozed on N. piazza till 9 \- then went in \- lighted up & read till 10.30 when R. returned.
