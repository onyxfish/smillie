## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 4, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wet lowering m'g. about 8 there was a slight break but soon after the rain came + fell heavily till after 11 — then till about 3 showers came + went + after that the day cleared deliciously making a lovely late afternoon. R— got up about 4.30, had bkft. + got to the Reserve gate where he met Lawrence Adler at 5.30 + they went up the river as far as Beaver meadow fall + then fished down — getting to the house at 6.15 with 14 fine trout. He would have done better but R. was inexperienced + fed most of the bait to the fishes. He caught 8 — so R. had to wind up his day with a fly which did not serve him. I wrote a note of condolence to Nellie (Geo's wife) whose mother died some days ago — then I again wasted time in the dark room trying to get some results without success — lunch at 1 after a trip to P.O. Afternoon I got hold of some old letters + pkg. that I found here — dating back to 1866 + two or three yrs. later — they fascinated me + I read them all living again in the years ago. After 5 I took my camera + made a couple of experimental pictures of the house front, cloudy brow. At 7.15 we had dinner R— having come in, had his bath, dressed + short rest. we had a course of trout. Every while R— lounged I read to him a couple of H.H. Smith's "Under Dog" stories — they impress me as very attenuated after the wild, savage power of Jack London — To bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 5, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 86° / 72°

A very uncomfortably hot, misty, muggy day. A late bkft. after a Sunday morning grooming. Then, because of the weather we could do little else than lie about in shady places. Earl Moody came about 10 o'clk. He opened his campaign by freezing the ice cream — in the afternoon he sat. + fixed up his room + slept in it at night. Dinner at 2 — after I had been to P.O. + got mail. After 4 o'clk I went to Mr. Marquand's cottage + spent a pleasant hour with them. Mr. Marquand is much interested in the Art + speaks freely of the possibility of their hiring it another season — it was nearly 6 when I went to the Hardenburg's + made a half-hour call there — then home to tea. Evelf. R. + I loafed on the north piazza — the evelf. air was pleasant if not refreshing — it was a pleasant relief after the heat of the day. The Ther. in the large room, with doors + windows wide open, got up to 86° — but in the hall way on second story it did not get above 82°. Showers during the night.
