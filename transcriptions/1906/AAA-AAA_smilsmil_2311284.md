## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 26, 1906

**Weather:** Bright  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ not so cold as yesterday. owing to some indescretion at dinner yesterday even't my night was disturbed \+ unrestful consequently I did not get up till near 9 o'clk \+ had bkfst. at about 10. I kept very quiet till noon — then went to butcher's \+ grocer's — so Deardon about tips to employees as some, of whom I had no knowledge, had tackled Kate to get something from me. I learned from Deardon that it was the practice of the tenants to give Xmas presents to all employees — Engineers — firemen — elevator men, hall boys, scrubwomen \+ others \+ I capitulated on $15 which he is to distribute \+ I gave him a check for himself of $10 \+

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 27, 1906

**Weather:** mild hazy  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild hazy day — a little sunshine early \+ an attempt at snow at night — Bkfst — at 8.30 — at 9.30 I tried to get W. Schmolek of Ham. Am. Line on the 'phone — Waited till 10\. then went down to the Co's office — He was not there \+ I arranged matters concerning state rooms on steamers for Jamaica, Cartegena \+ Port Simon as best I could — then up town \+ went to Brooks Bros. — bought two suits of clothes — then to Scribners' \+ saw W. H\. L. Smith — then to Bank — then to studio getting there at 1 o'clk — ate my lunch, set palette \+ painted on Cliffs of Normandy till after 3 — the work being painting out brown spots \+ lines in distance \+ middle distance — Home at 4 — had a rest — wrote to Mr. Schmolek \+ sent check to Ham. Am. Line for $67.50, being 20% on two fares to Jamaica, Cartegena, Port Simon \+ back to N.Y. Dinner at 6.30 — Lou, Ralph \+ I went to the Hippodrome — Jim went to a dance. We had good seats \+ I was amused — Neptune's Daughter is a showy spectacle with some amusing tank incidents — The immense audience was, in itself, an impressive spectacle — it dispersed without delay or confusion — Performance over at 11\. 05 — Home \+ to bed at 12.
