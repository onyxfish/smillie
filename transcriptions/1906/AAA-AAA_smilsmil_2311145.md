## Left Page

### Sun. March 25, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely spring day \- cloudless, quiet \+ mild inspite of the quantities of snow covering the ground. I got up soon after 7 \+ enjoyed my Sunday m.f. \+ bkfp at about 8.30 — after that, for some time with newspapers \- then out for an airing. By subway I went to 137 St. \+ Broadway \- walked down to Manhattans via-duct \- over that to Grant's Tomb \+ along Riverside drive to 96th St. Thence by subway \- to Century \+ had lunch after 2 o'clk. After that wrote notes to Dielman, Millet \+ Low \- Art Com. concerning Charl's photos \+ possibility of showing them some eve'g at Century \- It was about 4.30 where I got to my Rooms \+ feeling tired I had a good nap \- Jim took tea, by invitation, at Dielman's \- I dined alone at 7.30 \+ at 9 went to Charl's \+ spent an hour \+ a half there. Then to my Room \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 26, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beast of a day \- Snow at start, then rain which continued in a sort of misty mizzle all day. I had some writing to do after my bkfp: at 8 \+ did not get to Studio till after 10. Carpenter there at work \- got closets finished \+ I inaugurated my little refrigerator, filling it with ice \+ getting bread \+ butter etc \- I had a comfortable lunch after 1 o'clk \- I got the last of my etching mounts cut \+ then made paste \+ commenced the work of pasting them together. Collins came in with some small specimen process prints after Purner that had been sent to him from England to see if such goods could be marketed here \- I thought not. got to my Rooms about 5 feeling very limp, probably on acct. of the weather \- I had an uneasy nap \- then dinner after 7 with Jim \- He went to Yale club in the eve'g \+ I read some \+ did other things of no moment.
