## Left Page

### Fri. July 27, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day, bright \+ warm, with refreshing breeze — but I was under the cloud of a headache that got the better of me for a time in the afternoon. After bkfs. I wrote to Jane Hinshelwood — To Tho. dock \+ sent supplemental order to W.C. Cullen — Then I went to library \+ sat there till 12 — met Miss Putnam, sister of G.H. Putnam — She \+ her sister Edith have a cottage here. Then I got my mail \+ until dinner wandered over the links \+ W.G. Nailson's hill — Me home to dinner — Mr. \+ Mrs. Marquand had called \+ Ralph had entertained them. After dinner I had to give up to my headache \+ was on my bed till after 4 — Then up \+ till supper I worked at staining some of the mouldings in my bedroom, that had been left unfinished — Even on piazza till 9 o'clk — Then in \+ reading aloud "The Filagree Ball" by Anna C. Green.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 28, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded in f., opening the day at about 9 o'clk, with a thunder storm which \+ on passed over. About 11 o'clk R. \+ I went down the hill to Albert Winch's where I saw his daughter \+ made enquiries about a number of Hot bed glass sashes that I Told him last fall that he might use. She said she believed he had them. On our way back rain commenced so that we set to the house instead of going on to P.O. The shower continued for an hour \+ Harry got the mail. Dinner at one — During the afternoon I did a little work transcribing from father's autobiography M.S. Another shower \+ again another later in the evef. Again we read aloud as usual.
