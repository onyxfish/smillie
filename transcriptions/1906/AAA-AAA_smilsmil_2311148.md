## Left Page

### Sat. March 31, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came to town.
Dull day - milder, clearing to be a fine night - Had quiet night but felt uneasy below the belt when I got up a little before 8 o'clk - Took a dose of Bismuth \+ Morphia \+ had no further trouble. It was 11 o'clk. when I got to studio - I spent what remained of the morning in arranging some pkgs. pkgs. from my etchings, cleaning up mounts etc. About noon Ralph surprised me by a visit bringing nephls classmates, a son of Jno. Kendrick Bangs with him. They have come down on a "lark" - after theatre will go to Rye, McDangs home, \+ spend Sunday there - I prepared a lunch for myself about 2 o'clk. Then worked till 4 - then to continue - found Ralph there - I had a rest for half an hour - Bangs came at 7 \+ he, Jim, Ralph \+ I had dinner in the restaurant. \+ \+ B. went to the Hippodrome - Jim went to Chars \+ I went to Century \+ heard what Mr. Howard Mansfield had to say about Whistler. I was much disappointed - I had expected to hear the collector's side presented to our advantage - to have an intelligent statement of his admiration \+ an appreciative explanation of the excellencies of the work that drew so many dollars from his pocket. Mansfield is an able lawyer \+ has been a collector of prints. I don't think I was expecting too much - He showed a great lot of lantern slides, enlarging the etchings to 6 or 8 feet - This was a blunder that could not have been committed by a sensible lover of the art - they were monstrous exaggerations \+ his comment was foolish - drivel about "joyousness" \+ line - \+ marvelous detail \+ wise mission - everything was praised alike \+ where an attempt at the identical or the analytic was attempted it was too vague to mean anything or childish - bah - Rot - His imitation of W's drawl was amusing \+ sounded as though true - the only thing that was interesting to me.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 1, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant breezy day. not cold but very dirty - I got up at 7. 30 \+ gave so much time to my bath that it was after 9 before I got my bkfs. - After that newspapers \+ other things took my time till 12 - then I went out \+ by sub-way to King's Bridge. I there went out \+ walked over the bridge \+ back - then by train down to Manhattan (127 \+ 5th) - out there \+ walked up to Grant's Tomb \+ down Riverside Drive \+ Park to 72. \+ by subway back to my rooms at 3.30. I had no lunch but I had a good sound sleep till 6 o'clk. Jim did not come in till a little after 7 o'clk. Then we had dinner - Eve'g in my rooms.
