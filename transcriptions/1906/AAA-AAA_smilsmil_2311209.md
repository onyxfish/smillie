## Left Page

### Sun. July 29, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot muggy day \+ thunderous, much modified by delightful brisk breezes. I got up early \+ had a good grooming but b\&p. was after 9 \- Ford Beede came to discuss the possibility of getting a horse for Ralph's use during the remainder of the summer \- It was near noon when R. \+ I went to Club house \- got mail \- then took a three mile walk on Reserve road, then home \+ had dinner after 2 \- later, perhaps about 4, a thunderstorm came up with furious threatenings \- Its bark was worse than its bite \- There was much wind \+, in time, much water, but it cleared peaceably before 6 \- We had a short reading aloud but I was out of sorts \+ did not feel equal to it \-

## Right Page

### Mon. July 30, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the night \+ showers during the day with brief sunshine between \- Clearing at eve\+. misty, damp moonlight \- B\&p. late. My discomforts of yesterday continue \- I cannot make sure of the seat of my ailing \- The spot of slight soreness over right Kidney is more in evidence than usual but whether the Kidney or the liver or just plain nerves is at the bottom of it all I cannot determine \- The latter, probably, are most to blame \- Through them my blessings are made to disagree with me \- \+ what they need is the pressure of necessity \- obligatory, absorbing work \+ a hard, unsympathetic, repressing hand. I worried away the mng in a variety of useless gatherings \- Between showers I went to Club house \+ got a steak for dinner \- which was late \- After it I opened p\&f. from Cullen \+ got out the photo. supplies that I sent for some days ago \- I did a little transcribing of Father's auto biography \- Supper at 7 \- on north piazza till 9 \- wrapt \& aped in my blankets, on lounging chair, it was very delightful \- The rain had passed \- The wind had gone down \- The wood thrushes in the distant woods sang vesper songs so far away that the disconnected notes came to our ear like the tinkling of silver bells, the faint roar of the river forming a fit background note to the song of the thrush \- I drank it greedily, knowing that it was passing good \- In at 9 o'clc. a little fire on the hearth \- Ralph read aloud a couple of J. H. Smith stories \-
