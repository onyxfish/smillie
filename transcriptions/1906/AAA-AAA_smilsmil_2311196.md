## Left Page

### Tues. July 3, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very close, muggy day — the hot sunshine in the early part of the day veiled by a misty sky — thundershowers about 2 P.M. \+ again about 4 o'clk. Up at 6 \+ put things away till 8 o'clk — then had b'kf't — then to Manhattan Storage — then to G.C. Station \+ got the seat tickets that I secured the other day — then to Stikeman's \+ got the bound Vol. Cat of N.Y. Etching Club. 1882–93 with the Haden letters — then to studio \+ left it there to Finnegan's \+ got a block of ice cream \+ took it to Touraine — Ralph getting up — we ate the ice cream with some zwieback — I had arranged over the 'phone, to go to Dr. Tuttle's at 2 o'clk — but the storm coming up at that time I had a 'phone talk with him instead — he said he w'd call on me about 9 p.m. this evey. I continued putting things away \+ packing my trunk until 4 — Ralph \+ I went to Charl's to dinner — another thunderstorm came up as I was preparing to go to the Touraine to meet Dr. Tuttle — it was 9.15 when I got there \+ Dr. T. had just gone. I resumed packing \+ continued till 10.30. Then, the end being in sight, I went to bed.

Jim went to Bellport this afternoon to spend the 4th with Walter Cook's family.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 4, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 6 — dull, threatening m'f'g — furious rain last night — showers during the day but we were fortunate enough to be under cover when each one came. I got up at about 6 \+ before b'kf't at 8 had finished the packing of my trunk \+ had everything ready for a start. We went to the station at 9.30 \+ there met Katie Leonard, her niece Catharine \+ her nephew Harry Bradley — got baggage checked \+ our places on the train 10 m. before it started — our journey was uneventful — we passed two several heavy showers \+ some fine weather — the Catskill mts. showed serene \+ beautiful to me by reason of the loved reminiscences of years long gone by. After we passed White Hall — there were signs of showers ahead — we got to Westport just after a heavy shower. at 5.10 \+ I was glad to get my overcoat out of my trunk to wear during the ride to S-town — Road very bad — got to the Windsor at 7 — I tried to get Howell or Rollickson on the 'phone \+ after long trying, got H. who — Mr. Ford. Beede — I told H. that I was in a bad hole because pipes had been taken up cutting off water from the Ark — He called Scott Brown to the 'phone \+ he told me that I had been misinformed — that water had been turned on my house two days ago — There is a negro in that wood pile somewhere — Then we had our supper. after that sat before a big fire \+ warmed our toes — had a chat with young Roberto who is here waiting for his father \+ mother — To bed early.
