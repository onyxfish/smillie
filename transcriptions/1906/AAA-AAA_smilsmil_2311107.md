## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 8, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow all day, commencing very light in the m't \+ sifting down very slowly, so that at night not more than an inch had fallen — the boys \+ I breakfasted soon after 8 o'clk — later R. \+ I went to the studio, he unpacking one of his old trunks at studio to exchange it for the one at the Touraine that he brought from New Haven. We Ret. to rooms about 1 — I prepared my lunch as usual — it was after 3 when we ret. to studio \+ near 5 when we left there — At 7, Jim, Ralph \+ I dined at the Murray Hill Hotel — Jim went off for the evef. after it — R. \+ I Ret. to the Touraine \+ spent the evef. quietly reading \+ writing till after 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 9, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph ret. to New-Haven.

A brilliant \+ seasonably cold day — the snow in the sunshine melting \+ making a horrible mess. The boys \+ I had bkfst. soon after 8. — after 10 [illegible] I went to studio — he continued his packing at the Touraine \+ had his trunk at the studio sent to him — At 12.30 I went to Bank — on my way up 5th Ave. I stopped in at the Silo auction rooms to see the Richards \+ Boughton pictures — there is an amazing show of Richards' work — his facility \+ untiring industry were wonderful — some of the paintings of 1880 to 85 I liked very much — his later work became thin \+ vaporous. Got to rooms at 1.45 — prepared my lunch — went to studio at 3.30 — back about 5 — At 6.45 went with Ralph to Murray Hill Hotel \+ had dinner — expected to take 8.52 train to New Haven — but the dinner took too long — we went to station, checked trunk \+ then Ret. to Touraine \+ had a quiet evef. I wrote to Violet Beach \+ to Clarence Smith \+ paid his bill — Jim dined with the Burlingames — came in at 10.30 \+ went with Ralph to catch the 11 o'clk. train.
