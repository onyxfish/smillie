## Left Page

### Thur. March 15, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Almost a blizzard \- snow all day long \- or, more correctly, frozen rain for it came down in little dry pellets, that fell inches deep, like sand \- I got to studio soon after 10 \+ got to work looking over my pfs. \+ making selections of my work to let Frank Weitenkamp curator of Prints, Public Library, have for an exhibit that he is preparing of Am. etchings. At 1.30 I went to Touraine \+ had lunch \+ then back to studio where I worked till after 5, having a hard time getting at some pfs. left in the standing press last Spring \- Evelf Jim went to Boston Symphony Concert on ticket from Charles Young Gould, by arrangement; came in about midnight after a class dinner, \+ shared Jim's room with him \- I worked till late cataloguing negatives taken during my recent trip.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 16, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold \+ clear in the mf., clouded over later. I got up earlier than usual \- bffs. before 8 \- to studio at about 10 \+ spent rest of mf. doing very dirty work in getting ready for a regular house cleaner \- I have not got to the last dregs almost \- hope soon to have my studio in shape for some legitimate work. \- At 12 went to Hannemann Hospital Park. Ave. \+ 67'S. \+ saw Mary \- found her sitting up \+ apparently very well \- she complains of want of attention in the hospital \- her stories are very surprising \- At 1.30 I went to Century \+ had lunch \- near 3 where I got to Touraine \- had a rest \- then to Private View of the Am. Soc'y \+ W. 57'S. Met Emma I. \+ Miss Plum there \- parted with them at 6 \+ walked to Touraine where I had dinner with Jim soon after 7 \- Evelf I wrote to Rackl. \+ Jim acted as escort to Sue Whiting \+ took her to Bowling Club up town about 70th S. \+ then at midnight took her to Hoboken train for the South \- After many delays he got to his bed at 2 Am.
