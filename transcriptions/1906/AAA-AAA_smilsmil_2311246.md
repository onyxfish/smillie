## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 11, 1906

**Weather:** Cold \+ windy with some spatterings of Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

about 3 o'clk. \- Brkfo. before 8 o'clk \- I spoke to Jim about his late hours \+ he went away angry. Two men from Douglass came soon after 8 and worked till 4.30 \- I don't like color on Parlors \- it is too light \+ yellow \- I wanted a little black put into it but the artist objected \- the dining room was sized \- Before 10 I went to Dr. Tuttle's \- He gave me a general looking over \- my laryng. is troubling me \- so is a severe bruise on Ribs, left side \- that a rib might be broken \- Dr. T. says No \- have lumbago (?) on left side \+ my rupture is bothering me \- Then to Alexanders \+ ordered a pair of shoes \- Then to Brooks Bros. \+ ordered a suit of clothes \- Then to 2d. Nat. Bank \- then by Subway downtown to Watermans to get my pen \- then home at a little after 1 \+ had lunch \- In doors all the P.M. At 6.30 had dinner alone \- don't know where Jim is \- he is to enter the 7th Regt. Co. This evg. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 12, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Was unable to get up this m't \+ spent the day in bed \- Yesterdays exposure seems to have brought my troubles to a climax \+ I am generally very miserable all over \- Hugh Douglass \+ the painter who was here yesterday came \+ the work of finishing the Kalso-mining of parlor \+ dining room went on \- I think it was completed \- I continued to grow worse \- I was not able to write my diary \- This is written weeks after when I have a hazy memory only of suffering \-
