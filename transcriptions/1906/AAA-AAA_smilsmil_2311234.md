## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 17, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm pleasant day \- light haze over the mts. D. Janeway came after help. \+ brought letter from Dy. Com. Ad\. Reserve, giving me permission to lay pipe as desired \- then I spent some time developoing the two flash light pictures taken last night \- they are not successes. Then the bro. Langman, from the valley came \+ R\- went with them on his wheel to the lower lake \+ spent the day \- then I went to Club house \+ spent the rest of the m\[g?\] at Telephone \- duck \+ Istes at Keene Valley had no 1 gal. ironpipe \+ nearly an hour of writing bot the information that Breadsur at Port Henry could not get it to me by Wed p.m. So, all my efforts to get to pipe down this season have failed \- Then home \+ had lunch solos. Afternoon was busy in various ways trying to get things in order for our going \- I tarred some trees where surgical work had been done \- R\- came home at about 6 \- disgusted because he was not privileged as a member of the Ausable lake Club to go through to the Upper Lake \- \+ because one of his bicycle tires had given out \- Dinner at 6.30. Eve\[ning\] we finished R\. L\. Stevenson's "Journey with a Donkey" \+ got to bed early.

No carpenters to day \- Trumbull is treating me badly.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 18, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Peculiar day \- Opened very warm \+ hazy \- Between 10 \+ 11 a.m. a strange, dark mist came rolling up the valley from the north \- it looked portentous \+ I expected a storm but it passed in a few minutes with a smart blow \+ light rain \- Later in the day it rained again \- Adams \+ his son came but no one from Trumbull. I set A. Jun\. to work painting shingles on ice house roof \+ A. sen\. staining woodwork in doors \- they gave up at noon \- I put in a busy day \- dismantling the house. Earl being an efficient aid \- I rested for half an hour before dinner at 1 \- Afternoon I worked till nearly 6 \+ then a short rest \- tea at 6.30 Evening Earl \+ Harry had their second brush fire to which Ralpeh contributed a lot of old fire crackers \- I read newspapers \+ got to bed early.
