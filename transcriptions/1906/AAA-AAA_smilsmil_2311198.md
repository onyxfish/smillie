## Left Page

### Sat. July 7, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day tho' rather warm for exercise — up at 7 — bkfs. at about 8. About 10 we went down to the foot of the hill to make enquiries about Earl Moody whom I wish to engage for the summer. Then back \+ went to P.O. met a number of people there — Mr. Openhupm — Mrs. Bossam — Mr \+ Mrs. Rob't. Neilson etc. etc. — Scott Brown at Whiperlake — Got a porterhouse steak from W. Eddy, purveyor to the Club. Back home — wrote to Earl Moody asking if he w'd. come to me for the summer — dinner — Nye's man came over with his mower \+ commenced cutting the grass which stands full \+ strong on my ground — I did not affect him \+, had sent to Kelly asking him to come — Nye's man soon gave up because his cutter was so blunt \+ he went away to have it sharp and promising to come back in monday — Ralph fixed up his wheel \+ went down to Keene Valley — back at tea time — we sat on north piazza till about 9 — then in and lighted up \+ took turns in reading aloud till after 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 8, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph's birthday.

A fine m'f. promising a hot day — later, turned to thunder showers. — I overslept myself — did not get up till 8. \+ it was after 9 when I had bkfs. — I spent a good part of the m'f. writing a letter to Bessie — Ralph \+ I did something towards clearing up and putting things in order. It was after 2 when we had dinner — the table was decorated with fern leaves \+ daisies — after the ice cream the cake was brought on with 19 candles. After dinner, more bringing out of things \+ putting them in their places — bamboo screens were put up on north piazza \+ rugs were spread upon the floors — we went to P.O. about 5 — closed — then to Scott Brown's — saw him for a few minutes — then home. — I did not feel like eating supper till 8.30 when I had a light meal. R. \+ I did some reading aloud.
