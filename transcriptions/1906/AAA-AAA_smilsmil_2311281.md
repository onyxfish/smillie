## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 20, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain all day, besides which I had to fight all day with a head ache — Ralph \+ I had Bkft. together at about 8.30. Neither of us went out of doors all day — Part of the time he worked at making catalogues of books while I wrote — I got notes from Ham. Am. Line stating that all sailing dates for Prinzessen Vic. Louise had been cancelled \+ asking me to name another date — I replied giving Prinz Joachim, Feb 16" — four days earlier than the Vic. date \+ then wrote to No 1 — explaining to him what I had done \+ asking his pleasure — If he approves, the dates will stand, if not I shall cancel it \+ make such date as he will approve — Lunch at 1 — after that I gave up to headache \+ spent much of P.M. on my lounge — Dinner at 6.30 — Jim, Ralph \+ I — later Jim went to dine \+ Ralph \+ I spent our evef. reading —

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 21, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, muggy \+ damp — fogium \+ but the weather cleared sunny in the afternoon — Bkft. at 8.30 \+ after 10 Ralph \+ I went out — he to do some shopping — I, to the studio — most of my time there was given to cleaning up — Ralph came before 1 o'clk. \+ together we ret'd. home \+ had lunch before 2 o'clk — At 3 we went to the private view of the M.A. of P. where we made a round of the galleries with the result that I am more than ever assured that there is a something in the art of to-day, as practiced here, that is entirely beyond my comprehension — We got home before 5 — then I had a rest. Dinner at 6.30 Jim, Ralph \+ I — after dinner the boys went off on a "bum" — I spent the evef. quietly — wrote some notes — read a little —
