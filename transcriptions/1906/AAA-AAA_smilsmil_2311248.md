## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 15, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

From this date to Thurs. Nov. 1st my notes are based upon the Record kept by Miss Anna Drabbles, trained nurse, who commenced her ministrations in the eve'g of this day. I had been in a half conscious condition all day — Dr. Tuttle called three times — I had temp. up almost to 105° with pulse 120 \+ Respirations 24 — that is the beginning of Miss D's Record. The sinuses of my head were much congested so was my throat \+ the Bronchia well down upon my chest. I had much neuralgic pain in my face \+ ears, so constant that I could not sleep

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 16, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A great deal of pain in my head \+ neuralgia in my face. Some fever \+ pulse running as high as 120. Took calomel — Cet. Mg. in the afternoon \+ vomited it. Fed malted milk \+ junket.
