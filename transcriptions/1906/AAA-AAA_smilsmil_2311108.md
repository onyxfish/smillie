## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 10, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

N. Acad. recep'n to Sir C. P. Clarke.

Overcast most of the day — clear moonlight. Jim + I breakfasted at usual time. I got to studio at 10.30 + worked at sorting the large box of photos, packed by Annie + recently found. Back to Touraine at 1.30 + prepared my lunch of junket. It was 4 o'clk. when I ret. to studio + after 5 when I got back to my rooms. Then dressed myself for the N. A. f S. reception of Sir Caspar [i]noden Clarke, the new director of the Met. Mus. of Art — Jim + I had dinner at a little before 7 — Immediately after that I went to the Academy — Amsterdam Ave. + 109 S. There was a very full attendance + Sir Caspar made himself generally agreeable. — I enjoyed myself till about 10.30 — then to my rooms + got to bed at about midnight.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 11, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A grey day + mild — I got to studio at 10.30. The Salvation Army man came + took away the pile of Truck I had gathered for him — This helps the clearing up greatly. — I spent the remainder of the m't in trying up the assorted pkg's of photos, found in the big box packed by Annie. Ret. to Touraine at 1.30 + prepared a bowl of junket — I did not go out again during the afternoon. At 6.45 I went to Chas's — had dinner + after we went to Boston Symphony concert at Carnegie Hall — after it we were joined by Geo. + Val. Chas. preferred to ride home — I walked with Geo. to 5th Ave. there parted with him + walked to the Touraine alone.
