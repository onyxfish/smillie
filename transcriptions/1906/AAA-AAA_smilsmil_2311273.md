## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 4, 1906

**Weather:** Clear, windy \+ bitter cold  **Temp:** Ther. 11° at 8 am

Bkfs. before 9 o'clk — After 10 I went to Rawly Bdway near Hq. S. to see why he had not sent hall mirror home as promised — he assured me that he w. send it up by 3 o'clk — but he did nt. Then I went to Lampe \+ Nafsp — h. said he w. come to me Thurs. m/f to hang pictures — Then I walked down to Buskirk's — 6th Ave. \+ 34th S. Then Rode down to 2d Nat. Bank — Then by subway home soon after 12 — Lunch at 1 — Fussed the afternoon away by various methods till after 4 — then out — to butchers \+ P.O. sub station — a short walk — not so cold \+ quieter than m/f. Dinner alone at 6.30 — Self working on accts. Reading etc.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 5, 1906

**Weather:** Beautiful m/f., bright, mild \+ quiet  **Temp:** [illegible]

The afternoon was still mild \+ quiet but overcast threatening a storm of some kind — Bkfs. before 9 o'clk — It was after 10 when I left the house — went to butcher \+ grocer \+ then to studio — for some time with Geo. — then in my studio \+ did a little picking up. Got drawing I made last spring of devise to be used in pressing up on proof margins: — "J.D. Smillie collection" \+ took it to Millers \+ he is to make it according to plan given him. Then I went to the Century \+ saw the pictures there — the two gallery alcoves filled with Water-color — one alcove by Walter Palmer — the other by F.H. Smith. Some remarkable work by Palmer — Smith was not at his best \+ showed to his disadvantage — Home to lunch soon after 1 o'clk — Took quite a long walk on Riverside Drive in the afternoon — Dinner alone at 6.30 — Jim came in at 9.30 quite used up.
