## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 14, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Remarkably cool brilliant day. Ther. did not get above 74° in my bed room altho' sunshine was pouring in — an inspiriting day the inspiration of which came to me only thro' a veil of ills \+ dis comforts — The carpenters worked on board walk till 4.30 when the Tar for smearing timbers \+ boards gave out \+ they had to stop work. Ralph \+ I worked on the problem of design for the finish of chimney breast in main room. R. drawing large scale plan upon which I am to work details — Mr. \+ Mrs. Maramund \+ Miss Cross (?) Mr. M's sister, called before noon \+ I showed them over the house generally — Mr. M. was very enthusiastic — after 12. I went to P.O. \+ then back to dinner — Before 4 o'clk I went to the Hemlocks to learn whether or no Mr. Maghee had any coal Tar — he was away — I sat awhile with Mr. Hardenburg \+ Mr. Van Horn. Then back \+ found such odd jobs as I could to keep the carpenters going — Tea at usual time \+ before 8 R. \+ I went to the Hemlocks \+ saw Mr. M. \+ all the rest of the family except Mrs. M. — Mr. M. said he would let us know in the mng whether or no he had Tar \+ he would have to look in his barn — we made a short call — Then home \+ read some Bret Harte stories.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 15, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very fine day — I got the carpenters to work on odd jobs about after they had finished the board walk — I went to club ho. about 9 o'clk saw Scott Brown \+ got from him a can of Tar paint — Maghee having megaphoned to me that he had no Tar — then back home — \+ worked over the burlap that is to go on chimney breast. After much toil I got it tacked into place — only to find, after it dried, that it was badly puckered at the battone \+ will have to be done over again — wrote short note to Jim \+ went to P.O. at 12.30 — Afternoon I took a rest because I was tired but I looked after small jobs to keep the carpenters going. Tea at 6.30 Ev'g in front of fire till 9 — then we read a couple of Bret Harte stories before going to bed.
