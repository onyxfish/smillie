## Left Page

### Sun. June 3, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — up soon after 7 \+ before bkfs. at 8.30 had a delightful walk out on Prospect St — To a large new blg in process of erection — After bkfs. we sat about till about 10 then I walked down the hill \+ to Pierson. Found Jim \+ [illegible] on lounge \+ Ralph at Chapel — then I went to Chapel \+ heard close of sermon \+ singing — then ret. to Pierson \+ [illegible] — soon came in — others came in — Jim had been out \+ had bkfst — At 12.30 we started for the Downs' home as invitation of Jennie writing to dinner — Saw old Mrs. Downs for a few moments on Piazza — After 1 o'clk we had dinner — Mrs. W— Sue — Hattie Kipling, Jack \+ Ernest W — Jim, Ralph \+ self. After dinner we sat on Piazza — Mrs. Kipling later came \+ joined us — after her, Prof Niemeyer — \+ we kept up a chatter till near 5 o'clk. Soon after the dinner the boys \+ Hattie Kipling went off for a walk \+ Prof B came \+ took Sue for a walk — so, the chatting party for the most part was Mrs. Whiting, Mrs. Kipling, Prof N— \+ self — I left at about 5 \+ strolled back to the Elkins' — nobody at home so I improved the time by taking a nap — Before 6.30 the boys (J. \+ R.) returned \+ we had supper — the boys left at 7.30, Jim to take train for N.Y. The E's \+ I chatted till 10 — then I went to bed — a lovely moonlight night.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 4, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day, not too warm for comfort. Up soon after 7 \+ had a walk before bkfs. 8.30 when we had bkfs. — \+ about 10 I went down Town \+ in the Yale Art Galleries I met Prof Niemeier (or mayer) by appointment \+ he coached me thro' the school exhibits which I found unexpectedly interesting — then we went to the gallery up stairs \+ spent some time among the early American paintings — I left him after 12, did a little shopping \+ got back to the Elkins home before 1 o'clk \+ had dinner — at about 2, Katie \+ I went to Whitney Ave. \+ met the 2.15 Trolley car upon which, as previously arranged, were Mrs. Kipling \+ Jack Downs. We rode out to Carmel \+ then after a walk for nearly a mile, most of the way up hill, we got to his building, a little slice of a place that has more than one title "Stubilers," descr[illegible] the hybrid character of the old is one — Studio \+ Bungalow — or "Upside downs" which is another — it is upon the crest of a Ridge \+ has a charming valley view from its rustic Piazza, to the west, with rolling hills, much wooded country \+ some water. We sat there for some time enjoying the scene, the breezes \+ the birds \+ had tea \+ beer — Jack being our host \+ servant — Then we went inside \+ were amused as the eccentricities of the building were made known to us — it was all very enjoyable — about 5 o'clk we started on our return going down by another road, past the house of Mr — \+ thro' his garden \+ out to the Trolley about a mile nearer town than where we left it when we came — we had a pleasant ride back \+ got to the E's at 6.10 where we found Mollie Adams just arrived from N.Y. \+ Ralph — we had our dinner \+ an agreeable chatty ev'g — Ralph leaving early —
