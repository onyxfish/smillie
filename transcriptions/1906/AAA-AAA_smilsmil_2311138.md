## Left Page

### Sun. March 11, 1906

**Weather:** Brilliant m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant m'f. I was up & out at about 8.30 I had a brisk walk for about half an hour then in & read news paper till bkfs'. was ready at about 9.30 — chatting till after 11 — then I walked down to Pierson Hall & found R. in his room with a lot of young fellows. after a little while we walked around \+ took a look at the house where he has taken rooms for next year — then by Worcester Ave. car to the Selkins' & there had dinner, or lunch, at 1.30 Before 3 I begged off for a half hour nap \+ at about 4 Katie, Wm., Ralph & I started out.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 12, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

When I got up soon after 7 o'clk. I found a brilliant m'f. \+ the ground covered by an inch or two of snow — bkfs'. at about 8.30 — at 9.45 I left the Selkins' \+ by car went to station \+ by 10.30 train reached N.Y. at 12.25 then to rooms at Touraine — To Century at 2 o'clk \+ had lunch — it was after 3 when I started for studio but seeing Collins in his window I went to him \+ spent an hour there. It was about 4.30 when I got to studio and immediately renewed to develop the experimental snap shots that I had made with my Sypo camera. About 1 o'clk. I walked down 5th Ave to 25th S. \+ up Bdway to 34th St. taking snap shots with this camera which is about as big as an old fashioned silver watch \+ takes pictures rather smaller than a postage stamp — then up 6th ave. \+ to Century where I had lunch as already stated — I did not get thro' with the developing till late \+ to Touraine at 7 — Jim had gone to Yale Club for his dinner — I dined alone \+ spent the evg. with my photos. The weather to-day has been even more fickle than yesterday — the night was cool.
