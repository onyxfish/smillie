## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 27, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the m'f. — ceased before noon but continued cloudy — I got up late \+ did not have bkfp'. Till 9.30 I spent the m'f. writing to D. Perster — Ralph — Adams etc — I had lunch at 1.30 — then to Manh'n Storage \+ saw things taken out of my room there to be sent to The Wellesley to-morrow. I have left 11 boxes of unbound books one barrel of glass jars \+ 1 japanned coal hod. — Ret'd. to Touraine after 3 o'clk — Read for sometime — then in lounge for an hour — W'm Dean, a friend of Jim's came just before 6. Jim arr'd soon after \+ the two went to Yale Club to dine — I dined alone at 7.30 — Read awhile \+ to bed early.

Jim \+ Dean came in at 9.15 a.m. bringing Gould with them

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 28, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful mild day — up soon after 7 — bkfp'. at 8 — then to studio \+ docks — Jim got away to his business at the usual time leaving his company sleeping — Gould slipped out before 10 but Dean slept on till noon \+ then he slipped away quietly. I had my lunch at the Touraine \+ then a busy afternoon — Jim came in at about 6 bringing Dean with him \+ they went off together — I had dinner after 7 — just about 9 — when I had knocked off work \+ was preparing for bed I was called up on the 'phone by dear man of the Wellesley saying that Manhattan Van with four men had arr'd. with my furniture — I told d. not to let them in — the sent them away.
