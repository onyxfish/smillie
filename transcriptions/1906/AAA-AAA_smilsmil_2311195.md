## Left Page

### Sun. July 1, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful cool + breezy day — the atmosphere washed + purified by the storms of last night — Up at 6.30 — had a bath + wrote a short letter to Mary — Before I was dressed I worked for some time putting up + away heavy curtains poles, etc. Bkfs. at 8 — + ret. to work wrote several notes — R. got up about 10 — Jim, later. R. + I had lunch at 1.30 Jim went off — Afternoon I continued work, R. helping me — got a lot of books out of book cases + filed them in closet. After 4 o'clk — R. + I went to Studio — had a short walk — then back — At 7 we went to Manhattan + had dinner — From there we went to the Irelands — E. 17. St. — found Mary + Grace in — visited with them till 9.30 — then we walked up to 39. St. together — + then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 2, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Growing rapidly hotter + very humid. I was up early + doing what I could to expedite matters — after bkfs. I went to the bank + fixed matters there — then down town to Cortlandt St. + bought D.T.N.R.R. mileage ticket — then to Water Dep. Park Row, to learn my financial relations to the Dep. last year I paid the usual rate — then was directed by the Dep. to put in a Water Metre, + did so — Now I am told by the clerk that I have a metre chg. agt me of less than $5.00 the city being still in my debt for the bal. — Hurrah for metres — Then to studio + after 1 o'clk — went to Pouraine + had lunch with Ralph.
