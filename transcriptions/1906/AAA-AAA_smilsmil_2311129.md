## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 21, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 83

A hot humid day, I think the most trying that we have yet had — the Ther. on deck \+ in state room stood at same mark, but the state room seemed to be 10°. The hotter — we got to Curaco at 6 a.m. but were some time getting into the little harbor — the Vic. lay near the wharf \+ had a sort of landing stage rigged out so that we got ashore without aid of launch — The town is small yet very interesting as a combination of Dutch cleanliness \+ Tropic carelessness of sanitation — I got ashore at about 10 \+ was soon caught up a brisk shower — the only really wet one of the day. I took refuge in a horse car \+ had a short ride — after that the clouds bothered us but most of them I got a number of well lighted away \+ not \+ I bought 2 bottles of Curaco cordial for $3. Back to ship \+ had a short rest before lunch at 1 o'clk — about 2 — D. P. \+ I went ashore again \+ I bought a few poor photos but more quafs \+ at 4 \+ 5 when we returned to ship \+ spent the time till sailing watching the antics of fights of darkies ashore \+ the scramble for coins thrown from atop of us. There must have been a couple of hundred of these — half a dozen policeman with swords — one tried to drive the crowd back but could do nothing — the crowd saved him — he fought a big negro \+ chased him to the end of the wharf where he evaded the policeman by jumping overboard to the great delight of the crowd ashore \+ on the steamer — The bridge of boats across the entrance to the harbor swing open at 4 o'clk \+ we steamed out amid cheering \+ band playing ashore \+ aboard ship — we carried away a ship load of Curacao cordials, parrots \+ one forlorn monkey that was given to the Capt. The appearance of the island from the sea is very singular — it is said to be coral formation, long stretches of a few level flats in steps — there are a number of crater like forms, rising perhaps 150 or 200 feet, with faces in shore. At the north end of island is a volcanic looking cone 1200 feet high — The sea was very smooth, the air delicious \+ the sunset lovely — After dinner I was on deck till 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 22, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 84

The hottest day yet as testified by the thermometer. The one on deck stood at 79° at 8 a.m. \+ got back there at 10 p.m. — mine in state room got up to 95° \+ at 10 p.m. had fallen less than 2°. There was little air \+ no sea — we ran along as smoothly as tho' on the Hudson — It was very delightful to lie off in the shade with a soft breeze fanning, but it is not what I enjoy most at sea. I want something more strenuous. I wrote a letter to the boys in the morning, \+ part of my diary that has fallen behind for a couple of days — I rode horse back for awhile before lunch — The afternoon, more loafing \+ more writing — There was a great display at dinner when the memory of Washington was honored — To be in the swim the D. \+ I donned our "glad rags" — After that I slept in a chair on deck till near 10 —
