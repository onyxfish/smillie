## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 22, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beastly day — foggy, dark, wet \+ slimy. I got up at about 7 \+ had bkft. at 8, before Jim had finished his — Ralesh appeared much later — At 10 I went to market, grocers \+ studio — there it was too dark for me to see to do anything — so I went to Miller's in 40th \+ explained to Fred how I wished my proof stamping machine refrained — then home at 11 \+ until lunch at 1.30 I wrote in my proposed preface to Father's autobiography — After lunch rested till Miss Murphy came at about 3 — with her till 4.15, reading to her from Father's M.S. — she taking stenographic notes — After that read till dinner time — after that evef with the boys — some reading aloud.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 23, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, clear \+ windy — Bkfs. after 9 — loosed over newspaper till after 11 — then I went out \+ had a brisk walk finding it hard to keep warm — The boys had eld. after 10 \+ then went to All Angels Church across the way — dinner at 2 o'clk. — Afternoon reading S. a. Poe — \+ Rob't. L. Stevenson — Tea at 6.30 — Evef boys went out to call on Hardenburg — I wrote to Scott Brown protesting against to outrageous rise in Keane Taxes — last year I was Taxed $78.00 on a $2400 val\[illegible], this year the amt. is jumped up to $110 on the same val\[illegible] — It is Robbery — wrote notes to Lizzie \+ Jessie Smillie — more reading.
