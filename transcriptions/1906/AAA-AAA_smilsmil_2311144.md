## Left Page

### Fri. March 23, 1906

**Weather:** Brilliant, cloudless day \- cold \+ windy  **Temp:**

I got up much out of sorts, my head swimming as much as I ever in spite of calomel \+ Citrate of Mag. I got to studio at 10 \- the carpenter came soon after \+ worked all day in the closet \- I spent much of the m'f exploring \+ found a number of old p\[illegible\], of my work which I had forgotten about \- at 12.30 I walked up to Dr. Tuttle's to consult him about my condition \- he thinks it is indigestion \+ advises strychnina \- just what I have prescribed for myself \+ been taking \-\- what are doctors good for any how? He showed me a lot of the photos. he took while on our trip \- some of them very good. It was about 2 when I got to the Century. Had a light lunch \+ after 3 o'clk \- went to Wanamaker's \+ bought some pillow cases. Then walked to studio \- getting there about 4 \+ for an hour cut mounts \- It was near 6 before I got to my rooms \- Jim took a friend with him to the Yale Club to dine \- I dined alone \- Very, news paper and marking the new sheets \+ pillow cases.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 24, 1906

**Weather:** Still fine weather but unsea as onably cold  **Temp:**

After bkfst. I wrote to Ralph \+ two or three short notes to others \- Then to studio a little before 11 \- I worked over etching p\[illegible\] \+ mounts till after 1 o'clk \- Then had a lunch that I prepared for myself. After 3 o'clk \- feeling that I needed change of scene, air \+ occupation I went up to the Met. Mus. of Nat. History \+ amused myself there till time of closing \+ 5 o'clk. Then walked down 8th Ave. to Columbus Circle \+ by subway to 42d St. \+ to my rooms \- Dinner with Jim \+ his friend Bradford Boardman \- They went off to a theatre together \+ I went to the Century to hear Prof Fried\- Hirth of Columbia give an ill. lecture on the Blk. \+ white School in Chinese Art \- To me it was a lamentable exh\[illegible\] of a man crowding himself full of useless \+ unedifying information \- He told us lots about men who lived under dynasties centuries ago \+ showed specimens of their work (lantern slides, photos) blk \+ white outline, that I think was utterly worthless from any artistic point of view \- I could not think that it was historically interesting even \- His audience thinned rapidly before the close of his lecture \-
