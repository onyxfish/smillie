## Left Page

### Thur. June 21, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day in spite of an east wind. Ralph \+ I had bkfst. at about 8.30. It was rather late when we got to studio \+ at 1 o'clk. I got a lunch ready in the studio — about 2 we went to 2°. Natl. Bk \+ on our way back stopped at Bayerdorfer and ordered a watch like Jim's for Ralph's birth day that comes July 8 — Then to studio where I worked for a while on father's portrait — but most of the time till 5 I was engaged in putting things away for the summer. At 5 Ralph came \+ we took surface car \+ rode up Broadway to 78th St. \+ walked to West End Ave. \+ 81st St. and took a look at the Wellesley — Then to Riverside drive \+ Park \+ loitered there till about 6.30 — Then to 86th St. Subway Station \+ back to [illegible] Jim in \+ much used up by work \+ late hours. we had dinner together at 7.30 — after that Jim slept on sofa \+ R. \+ I went to Charl's where we spent the eve'g till after 10.

Arthur Stedman called at studio in the m'g to get information concerning Am. Engravers for Am. Edn. of an English Encyclopedia for which he is doing some editing —

## Right Page

### Fri. June 22, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant warm day — up at 7 — bkfst. with Ralph at 8.15 — after some writing got to studio at 10 — worked till 12 getting things put away for the summer — about noon Geo. came in \+ said that he had had my dining room furniture taken out of storage room — I went to Manhattan Storage \+ directed that it be sent down to Lock's to be renovated \+ recovered — back to studio after 1 — Ralph came at 1.30 we had a full \+ informal lunch. At 3.30 I went to barber's \+ had hair cut — then to Rogers Peet \+ Co. for a hat but did not get one — No Couraine about 5 o'clk \+ rested for an hour. at 7.30 Jim, Ralph \+ I had dinner — Eve'g they went to skating rink — I wrote notes \+ fussed about \+ went to bed early.
