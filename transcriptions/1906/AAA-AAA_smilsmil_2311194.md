## Left Page

### Fri. June 29, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably hot day — A heavy thunderstorm passed over the city about 7 a.m but everything was dry an hour later. Went to studio before 10 — Spent most of the day there — Selleck sent "Cliffs of Normandy" \+ I had it set up in hall way. I cleared up things to leave for the summer \+ selected most of the things that I am to take with me to the mt. I had my lunch in studio a about 1.30 \+ went to my Rooms at Touraine at 5 — Kate Semard came \+ made final arrangements for the trip on Wed. mt. July 4th. Jim came in at 6.30 \+ we had dinner together — later we went to Charl's \+ were there till 10. Earlier in the eve\[ning\] I called up Meta Adams — her daughter-in-law told me she was out of town — called up Jessie Smillie \+ had a talk with her.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 30, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came from N.H.

An intensely hot day — Weather Bureaus says 92°. Hottest June in 5 yrs — Temps. in street 8 or 10° more than official — Up before 7 o'clk. \+ spent some time taking down curtains \+ hangings — To G.C. Station \+ bot. tickets to Albany \+ reserved seats — then to bank then to Loch's \+ looked at chair covering but did not find anything that I wanted. Then to Studio \+ had my lunch bet. 1 \+ 2 o'clk — Continued work there till about 4 — Then to Rooms \+ Rested awhile — then "got busy" till 7 o'clk. No news of Jim — went to my dinner alone but before it was finished Ralph came in — just arr. from New-Haven — he then had his dinner with me — A very heavy thunderstorm came up before 8 o'clk. with a brief deluge — it passed over before 9 — as R. \+ I started out to go to Charl's, we met him coming in — I read to him a letter just Rec'd. from Mary. Venice June 19th \+ 20 — He left before 11.
