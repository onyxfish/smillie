## Left Page

### Tues. June 5, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A filmy day in the a.m. — then sunny \+ overcast in the afternoon with heavy thunderstorms from 8.30 to near 10 p.m. I was up early \+ had a walk before bkft. At about 10 I walked down to Pierson to Ralph \+ together we went to the Art Galleries \+ spent some time looking at the early Am. pictures there, especially the Trumbull's — At noon we parted at Commons \+ I Res? to the Elkins \+ had lunch at 1.30. As the afternoon was overcast I followed Katie's advice \+ from 3 to 5 had a good nap. Then we went to the Marsh place, now apart of the university Forestry School \+ wandered about enjoying especially the rhododendron blooms — Mollie joined us there — all back to dinner at 6.30 — Ralph with us — we had a pleasant evef. with the drawback that Ralph was detained by the storm \+ had, at last, at 9.30, to go away in the rain.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 6, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Yale vs Dartmouth — ball.

Day opened overcast \+ humid \+ pleasantly cool but as the clouds burned away it became hot, the ther. getting up to 86°. Thundershowers about 5 o'clk that passed over in about 3/4 of an hour, leaving it cooler but very humid. I went out a little before 8 \+ had a walk — bkft. at 8.30 — about 10 I went to Pierson \+ saw Ralph who came in after his last, or next to last, recitation before exams. We went out together \+ I shopped for collars trying to find some to match two I had borrowed from D. Elkin. I left Th. about 11.30 as I boarded a Whitney Ave. car at the Green — on the car I met Molly Adams — together we went to the Downs home where I made my dinner call — spent some time with Jack Downs in his studio \+ had a two minute interview with the old lady who is extremely feeble — After 12 I walked up to the Elkins — Dinner at 1.30 — soon after 3 D., Σ — \+ I started for the Yale field to see the game between Dartmouth and Yale. Ralph joined us there — It was after 4 before play began — there was nothing exciting till the showers came \+ drove the players from the field on the 6th inning Yale at the bat when the score stood 1 to Y. 0 to D. The rain was very heavy at times \+ flooded the field — it lasted about 3/4 of an hour — the boys had a good time during the wait, singing \+ whistling College Songs, eating peanuts \+ candy \+ cheering the rainbows that came as the shower passed — there was considerable delay in drying the field — Sawdust was spread in the wettest places \+ it was about 6 before the game was resumed. Ralph had to leave us \+ as there was some excitement having ball played in the mud — D. \+ I stayed on to see the dasnt — Dartmouth made a start \+ made 2 in one inning \+ Yale began to play desperately but we gave it up \+ left at about 6.40 getting home after 7 — the dinner was not spoiled \+ Katie was undispleased — I had intended to return to N.Y. on the 8.02 train but that was given up \+ we had a quiet evef. \+ I got to bed early.
