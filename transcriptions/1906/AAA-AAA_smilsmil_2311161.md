## Left Page

### Thur. April 26, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary, with her girls, sailed to day on the mouths.

A very lovely day — mild, quiet & sunny. I got up & had a light bkfst before 10 o'clk. At 10 Charl called me up on 'phone + said the party were ready to start for the steamer — Mary bid me good bye over the 'phone. I have a strong presentiment that I shall not see her again — they sail for Genoa — if they have plans they have plans they have not told me of them — I do not think that Mary has any — she is led by the girls + when she talks of a time for home coming they tell her that will be settled later. After 11 I went to studio + was there for a little while — then I went to Douglas about my sign — then to Bornemann's + across to Mad. ave. + thence to my rooms — I had a light lunch in the dining room — then a nap — at 4 o'clk I went to studio again — there till near 6 — doing nothing in particular — back to my rooms — Had dinner with Jim before 7 — first he took Miss Dielwan to University Glee Club Concert — I podged about till 10, then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 27, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant + spring-like day. I got up feeling very unwell — some fever, much pain in the head back of eyes, nose + fore head — probably catarrhal — + rheumatic pains in shoulders — I went to Dr. Tuttle to get some comfort from him — he said he thought that after such a pull-down as I had had I was doing about as well as could be expected + in time I'd come out of my misery — I squeezed such comfort as I could out of it — much squeezing + little comfort. I went to studio but stayed there for a few minutes only — the rest of the day I kept quiet in my rooms — Will Parke surprised me with a visit — has come to town with his wife to see her off for Carlsbad to-morrow. He told me of the dreadful time he had in his siege with typhoid fever — I ate a very light dinner with Jim at 7 o'clk — later he went out + I wrote to Raelph.
