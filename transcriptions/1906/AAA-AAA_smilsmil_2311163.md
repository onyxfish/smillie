## Left Page

### Mon. April 30, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A smart thunderstorm about 4 a.m. I was not able to get up till about 10 - had fever - catarrhal head ache, larygeths \+ lots of other pains - I called up D'. Tuttle \+ he said he w'. come \+ see me - he did so, after 11 \+ stayed till 1 - he thinks that I need'nt be alarmed \+ that a heroic dose of patience w'. help me along. After he left I had a light lunch \+ later, when the sun came out \+ it was dry \+ mild I went out - to studio - there for a few minutes \+ back to my rooms. At 7 Jim \+ I had dinner - at 8 I went to Charl's - he got out his lantern \+ showed me some new slides from my W. I. negatives - \+ he talked of details of his proposed show at the Century. I got back to my rooms soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 1, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful spring day - I got up in fairly good shape \+ had bkfs - just before 10 - sat with my paper some time \+ then went to 2? Nat'. Bank. Then to Bayerdofer's about clock - then west to 6th Ave \+ 34th S'. to Buskirk's - then up the ave. to 43'. S'. \+ went to Century \+ had lunch there - then read sometime concerning the West Indies - as I was on my way out I met Collins \+ sat down for a chat with him - it was 4 o'clk when I got away - then to Miller, the carpenter, about studio roof - then to studio - sat with Geo. for awhile - then to my rooms after 5 - Jim had to take his dinner alone - I had an indigestion \+ went without - nothing was accomplished in the eve'g -

Helen Butler sent me a box of Pansies in memory of Annie's birth day.
