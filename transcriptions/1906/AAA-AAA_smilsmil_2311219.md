## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 18, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot day — Ther. got up to 87° in the main room — right in the line of a delightful breeze — Thermometers are very stupid in some ways. The day was spent busily in finishing the mantle piece in large room — that is in staining the new wood parts \+ in doctoring with color portions of the joints of the worm-eaten slabs where the inequalities of the stuff made it impossible to get perfect joints at the mitres — and in making it possible to use my camera in making enlargements in the dark room. Ralph had this work in charge \+ cut an opening in dark room door \+ made a shelf upon which the camera is to rest. I went to P.O. at 12.30 — Pkg. from Wanamaker ar. Eve'g. tea at usual time — then loafing on piazza — before 9 o'clk — some building down the valley burned — it seemed to be on Geo. H. Kelly's place — the effect from picturesque point of view was very beautiful — after watching it till the fire went down we went in, lighted up \+ read aloud a couple of Jack London's short stories — [illegible — ~3 words]

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 19, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day opened hot \+ misty, the Ther. getting above 80°. Clouds came up \+ a storm that came after 3 o'clk. threatened as early as 11 o'clk — at one time the rain was driven furiously from the north \+ the thunder was very heavy — In the eve'g we were in a misty fog. I got up at 6.30 \+ had my scrub. Bkfst. at 9. I wrote to Bridgeman for seed \+ sent check for $5° to Hillen — Ralph got to work \+ found that the opening he had cut in dark room door for camera enlargements was not practical because there was not enough light from studio. He gave up very reluctantly \+ then some work was done with camera turned to outside light. There were some encouraging results but the failures were so thick, owing to the Bromide papers being very old, no one mfg. was good all the way through. At 1 o'clk. I went to P.O. \+ got mail — at 2 we had dinner — the thunder storm used up much of the afternoon. We had tea at 6.30 — Eve'g it was too damp to sit out on piazza — so, we commenced our reading early — we went through with Weir Mitchell's "A Comedy of Conscience" — then read a story by F. H. Smith — \+ to bed.
