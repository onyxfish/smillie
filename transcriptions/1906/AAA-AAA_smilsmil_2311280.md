## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 18, 1906

**Weather:** A clear, cold winter day  **Temp:** Bkb.

at 8 30 \+ got to studio after 10. Finding conditions good I varnished "The Cliffs of Normandy" that is - the day was bright \+ dry - the room was warm - no dust was flying \+ as it is 18 years since the picture was painted the paint was sufficiently dry. I did not varnish the sky but beginning at the horizon line with varnish much thinned with alcohol I strengthened it as I passed down till on the foreground I used the varnish without dilutant. It was a piece of work that took till noon to finish - then I put my little stamp on #husselwood proofs till my machine broke down. It was 2 30 before I got home to lunch - after that I wrote a fair copy, with emendations, of the first part of a "Preface to Father's Auto biography" - after that I had a rest before dinner at 7 with Jim - Immediately after I went to No. Charles' \+ with him went to a meeting of the Am. Geo. Soc. at Mendels Hall. Address delivered by Commander Robt. E. Peary's address on his field work of the Peary Arctic Club 1905-6 - It was, of course, a very interesting lecture \+ some of his pictures were exceedingly good - quite wonderful considering the conditions under which they were taken. It seemed to me that he had lost some of the modesty of speech in telling of his personal work \+ adventures that was so charming to me when I heard him last at the Century two or three years ago. He seemed to me rather cocky, when deprecating, as he did a number of times, "giving to my intimate acquaintance with" - some climatic or ice conditions - \+ some shore line or Esquimo or dog characteristics - Perhaps so much patting on the back has developed it.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 19, 1906

**Weather:** Another fine cold day  **Temp:** Bkb.

soon after 8 \+ to studio at 10 - no coal - fire almost out - 'phoned to Davies \+ got coal as soon as possible - but it was too cold for me to stay \+ take chances. I went to White Wauflahn's in 42d S. \+ got screws to take place of overhauled ones on [illegible] machine. Then to Booman's \+ got Photo. materials. Then home before noon \+ fussed about till near 2, then had lunch. After 3 o'clk. Chas. Collins came \+ we had a pleasant chat for an hour or more - Jim 'phoned that he could not be home to dinner till after 8 o'clk. I waited till 7 for Ralph but he did not come at that hour so I ate my dinner alone in melancholy manner. He came along a little before 8 \+ Jim came soon after \+ they had dinner together. a quiet eve't \+ to bed at 11.
