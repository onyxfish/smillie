## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 13, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 78

A fine day, a pretty stiff breeze \+ sea enough to roll us considerably \+ make many sick. I enjoyed my bath greatly — also my bkfp. as I went out on deck we were passing Santa Cruz island — many flying fish — I roamed about but found walking difficult owing to the rolling about 11 o'clc I went to the gym. \+ Rode horse back (electric) for 15 m., then the camel. I had a nap for lunch, tho' I slept well last night — after lunch more loafing till 3 o'clk then again to gym — more horseback — then vibratory abdominal massage — in less than an hour I had a full movement of the bowels. I wrote part of a letter to Emma S. \+ then had another nap — up at 6.30 \+ dinner at 7. Eve'g, more loafing — The Ther. rose from 76° this m'g to 79° at night. Cap't expects to reach Martinique about day light to-morrow. There is no place for landing at S't Pierre — men will be put off to construct a temporary landing place while we go to Port of France \+ return — Cap't says his movements must be determined by moonlight as this part of the trip is without any of the provisions ordinarily available for mariners.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 14, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 82

Fort of France \+ S't Pierre — Martinique —

Just before daylight we reached S't Pierre \+ the vessel stopped \+ sent ashore an officer with men \+ materials to make a temporary landing place for us this afternoon \+ then we went on to Fort of France \+ got there at about 7.30. The clouds had hung heavily over the mts — but lifted as the day wore on — The top of Pelee was at no time clear but the day was delightful — at times the clouds were troubles me in shutting out the sun shine necessary for photos — In the sun it was tremendously hot but a delicious breeze made it bearable — on deck the Ther. varied from 77° to 82. In stateroom it did not vary from 80 — I had my bath at 6.30 — bkfp — at 7.30 \+ went ashore at 8.30 — Fort of France is a mean little place but having some picturesque material \+ some very fine Rubber \+ other trees — I wandered about till after 11 getting a lot of snap shots \+ then feeling that there was nothing to warrant me tiring my self further I rec'd to the ship \+ had a little rest — The U.S. Cruiser Denver was in port — lunch at 1 o'clk — we started for S'nt Pierre at noon — \+ got there before 2 — I went ashore with the first boats — M't Pelee wore a cap of clouds but his bare mud furrowed sides were in sunshine — On shore the heat was very great when sheltered from the breeze but I got about without difficulty \+ did much tramping — a portion of the city was covered with mud but much of it seems to have been cleared \+ is now partly overgrown with weeds — the buildings were substantial \+ of stone — I walked along the sea promenade which must have been very beautiful \+ got many views — not a habitable house has been left. When at 4.30 I rec'd to landing I went into a refreshment tent put up by S.S. Co — \+ had iced lemonade \+ cooled off in the shade before returning to the ship — Then I had a bath \+ change of clothes — then a rest before dinner — I was pleasantly tired — I'm afraid I have rebroken my rib — it has given me some trouble this afternoon — Evening I loafed about the ship — sitting much of the time at the stern, the air being delightfully cool \+ the Ther. marked 79°.
