## Left Page

### Sat. March 3, 1906

**Weather:** 348 m.  **Temp:** 56° to 70°

A nasty day - cloudy on f. with rain in the afternoon - There was considerable sea that increased until about the middle of the afternoon when it was fine, the wind + sea running with us. I stood at the bow, facing toward the vessel + the wind blew strongly in my face At times we rolled heavily making havoc with women + movable things. Racks were necessary on the tables. I had my m.f bath in the sea water without mitigation - probably not much, if any, above 60°. My horseback rides + other gym. stunts at 10.30 after I had finished the letter to S.N.I. commenced a few days after leaving N.Y. I had my m.f. nap + my afternoon nap + did some preliminary packing. At 6.30 dressed for the Captain's dinner which was a hilarious affair - There was a great snapping of crackers, wearing of colored paper caps + blowing of tiny horns + other instruments of noise - almost equalling the pandemonium of a N.Y. election night - The worst came when the lights were turned out before the entrance of the illuminated cream - the Cap. was not present - a speech was made by one of the passengers who proposed the health of Cap. Bruning wig - Then the Cap. appeared + proposed the toast - Pres. Roosevelt - + disappeared as cheers were given for the Kaiser + the Cap.
Fine moonlight after 10 p.m. but it clouded over again before midnight

## Right Page

### Sun. March 4, 1906

**Weather:** Wea.  **Temp:** 46° at 9 am

The weather was fairly pleasant, tho' not quite clear + unpleasantly windy + cold - The Ther. on deck stood at 46° at 8 a.m. At 7 I had my bath, water unusually cold - the Jersey coast in sight - bfp - at 8.30 - after it I waited about for a while till D.T. finished his packing. Then I got to work + had to stick to it till noon when I got it done. I got very warm in my stale room + never suffered from the cold all the rest of the day - had the last lunch of the trip at 1 o clk. as we were coming up to quarantine - Here there was some delay + all passengers had to file past the Health Officer — Then we went to the dining room + made our "declarations" to the Customs Officers - At last we started up the bay + got to our pier at about 4 - some delay in docking - before 5 when we got ashore - fine + charges on the wharf - nearly half an hour before I could get my trunk - then my belongings were promptly passed + before 6 Jim + S + baggage started for the Touraine which we got to + abt. after 6 - I had everything in my room at once - then had a short rest before dinner at 7.15 Every chant came in + I showed him photos etc - got to bed soon after 11.
I have learned that Mary went thro' an operation at Hahnemann Hospital Two or Three days ago - to repair injuries done at the birth of her Willie many years ago - So far she is doing well.
