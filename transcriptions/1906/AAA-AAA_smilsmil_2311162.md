## Left Page

### Sat. April 28, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day, bright, breezy \+ not too warm — nevertheless I got up with a dismal headache \+ struggling thro' my most limited duties I got down to my bkfst — after q — after resting my head for some time I started out cautiously — went to the studio, found Douglas had left sign — got Miller to go to Studio with me \+ we decided where the sign is to go \+ made marks where the plugs for screws are to be it Then I started out to find some clue to Kate Leonard — went to R.C. Cathedral — 5th Ave. \+ 51st S' — Saw sexton \+ got the address of Bradley, her bro-in-law — 124 \+ 51'S'. went there — saw some young ones \+ left my message with them. Then back to Touraine \+ to bed \+ slept pretty soundly till near 6 o'clk — then up, feeling better, but still carrying a very heavy head. Had dinner with Jim — that is, he had dinner \+ I had a cup of tea — then he went off with Harry Gould, whom I think a bad lot, \+ at 10 m. before 8 I went with Chase to Century where we met the Lantern man, Haussen — His lantern was set up for the lecture to come off at q — Chase had a number of his slides to test the lantern as it is the one he expects to use when he shows his slides a fortnight from now — I too had half a dozen slides \+ they were run thro' the lantern — C. deemed it best to order an asbestos curtain as by that he w'd get more light — We then waited for the lecture by Prof Fowler about Crete \+ Excavations — uninteresting finds these — The lecture was carefully prepared \+ wd. have been more interesting to read than to listen to, being too full of detail — The pictures were reserved to the last \+ were generally very poor, being photos from book illustrations — Towards the last he got to be very prosy —

Prof. Harold N. Fowler — Western Reserve University — Excavations in Crete \+ their results.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 29, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day tho' somewhat overcast part of the time. I passed a trying night — to bed late \+ dozed lightly till 3 a.m. when I heard Jim come in bringing Gould with him — from that time I did not sleep again till daylight \+ my headache came back to me with redoubled force. Thro' worry about Jim's behavior. Between 5.30 \+ 7.30 I got a little sleep \+ then up after 8 \+ had bkfst — after q — my return met Jim going out for the day — I did not see Gould. I rested for a while \+ then started on a hunt for a housekeeping apartment — went to 72d S'. \+ stopped at each N.W. cor. upon which there was an apartment \+ enquired about rooms till I reached 110th S'. — The result was very depressing — if they were not too bad — common — dirty — they were too expensive — from $3000 per an. up. — I walked over to 110th S'. Subway \+ el — to Touraine at 2.30 after walking more than three miles, \+ had lunch — spent rest of day in my room — Jim came in a little before 7.
