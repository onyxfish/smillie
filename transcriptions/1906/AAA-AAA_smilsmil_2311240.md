## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 29, 1906

Slept in our new quarters.
The Wellesley — 440 West 2nd Ave.

**Weather:** Dull, threatening  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, threatening day. a light sprinkle in the early m'f. At 8.30 clock's man + three others came at once we all got to work — Jim went to his business early + the fight was on with me alone — it was after 10 when the first load, in a one horse wagon, started up town — I went ahead to The Wellesley — it was a little after 12 when the last of the load was landed in my apartment — then the men went off for dinner — nothing having arr. from the Manhattan Storage I phoned down to them + was promised that my storage goods sh'd. be delivered bet. 7+8 p.m — I rec'd. a note from Wanamaker thanking me for my order for 440 West 2nd Ave. + asking me if I was on their books from any other address — I phoned at once that I had been on their book for years + wanted to know when they w'd. deliver the order given yesterday not for kitchen utensils — I have food in Ketchiquit nothing to cook it in — was promised that things sh'd. be sent up at once — then to Touraine + had lunch — the men came + by 3.30 the 2nd load started up town — I went ahead + by 5.30 that was deposited in my rooms — then the men left — to be at the Touraine on Monday at 8.30 — I went to grocer, butcher + baker + got a lot of eatables — 6.30 nothing from Wanamaker — Kate Leonard was distracted — I was nearly worn out — Jim did not come in till after 5 — At 7 we went to Touraine + had our dinner there — + then did some packing — while we were at work one of the Laugmann boys called on Jim — I left them + went to Wellesley — men + Storage Van unloaded when I got there — the men had got nearly all of the things up to our rooms — one of the barrels of china was open + Kate took from it two broken plates + some broken glass — a bedstead + mattresses of Mary's was brought with the other things — how they got with my things I do not know — I sent them back — of the six bronze hangers on frame of hall mirror but five were delivered — I was much provoked — + got to bed at 11 completely tired out.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 30, 1906

**Weather:** Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the early m'f. — a deluge of it between 9+10 — it was after that that I had a very frugal b'f't. prepared by Kate — Coffee was made in the tin that goes with my spirit lamp + the eggs I ate raw — then I podged about + read newspaper till near 1 o'clk — then to Touraine where I met Jim + we worked till near 6 — I went to the studio with some small articles before 6 — then back + we had dinner at 6.30 — I wrote a letter of complaint to Wanamaker after dinner we continued our work till near 9 — then to our quarters in Wellesley — At 10 Kate Leonard came bringing Katsey Bradley with her.
