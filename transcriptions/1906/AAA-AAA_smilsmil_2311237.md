## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 23, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day — overcast much of the time. I got up at 8 — had my bath and bkfst. at 9 — the boys did not get up till later — I did not go out of the house all day except to get a news paper before bkfst. Felt "dopey", as the boys say \+ gave up to it thinking to get thoroughly rested. I had a nap before 1 o'clk. when my trunk came — I had no lunch \+ loafed away the afternoon. R\. took 4 o'clk train to New Haven — After that Katie Elkin \+ Alice Ireland came in \+ made me a long call — both, for the time being, are grass widows — W\. Elkin taking a Rest Cure somewhere in Conn. \+ Fred Ireland resting on a sea trips to Glasgow \+ back — Jim came in at 6.30 \+ we had dinner at 7 — Ev'g Jim went out \+ I continued the loafing of the day.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 24, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day — Jim \+ I had bkfst. together a little before 8. I was slow in getting about — it was after 10 when I went to the barber's \+ had hair cut \+ a shampoo — then to Manhattan Storage \+ was dismayed when I was told that the moving pressure upon them was so great that they could not move my things from the Touraine — w'd move only my things in storage — then to Borrmanns \+ got some Solio paper \+ developer — then to Lock's \+ made arrangement with him that he will help me out with the moving — then to Studio — saw Geo — then to Touraine — lunch — then for a couple of hours or more — as long as sunshine came in at my windows, I printed from the Ark negatives of interior \+ got some unexpectedly gratifying results. Then the peaches that I have eaten within the past two days got in their work \+ I was so used up that I could eat no dinner \+ spent the eveng. on my lounge.
