## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 14, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day — an ice storm during the night had covered trees & fences with a thin coating of ice — for some time rain fell— then mizzle — then know n't grew milder in the afternoon there was a partial clearing. I had my blép — soon after 9 — Dr. Tuttle called me on 'phone to say that a case at the hospital demanded his attention, so, our trip to Hobaken to see our stale room in the Prinzessen Vic. Louise is off — I fussed about & read till 1 o'clk — then to studio & brought back to the house a bag full of bric a bac — then I prepared lunch for myself — at 3.30 I lay down on my lounge & slept till 6. At 7 Jim \+ I went to dinner at Park Ave. Hotel — Then I left him \+ went to Emma Ireland's taking a lot of the family connection photos for her to identify — these I found in a large box where Annie had packed them. It was after 11 before we got through with these

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 15, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day — Rain at night — I got to studio soon after 10 \+ completed the packing of a box \+ then did some clearing up — At 11.30 I went down town to Clarence Smith's to have a talk with him about the possibilities of getting some relief in the matter of Personal Taxes — the Assessors charging tax on $25 000. a serious matter for me — but he saw no chance for me. I rel? to the Touraine after 10'clk \+ prepared some lunch — Since yesterday a vertigo has been bothering me — increasing all the time it depresses me — I did not go to the studio in the afternoon but about 5 went to Marks, a Tailor in 34th S. near 3. Ave — I was almost afraid to walk in the street — I took no dinner I'm afraid Table d'hote dining does not agree with me — Dr. Tuttle sent me a check for $300, paymt. for his berth on West Indies trip — I sent it to Hamburg-Am. Line together with the bal. I owe — wrote a note to Dr. Tuttle — Took 1 gr. Calomel before I went to bed.
