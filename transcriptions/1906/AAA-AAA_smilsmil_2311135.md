## Left Page

### Mon. March 5, 1906

**Weather:** Breezy & rather cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Breezy & rather cold — I got up late & feel the reaction that naturally follows the excitement of travel — strange climate strange people & the ships life & the sudden return to the routine of every-day life — Bkfs. at about 9 & most of its remainder of m'f spent in looking over mail accumulations, bills & money matters — went to studio for a little while about noon — Re. had called during the m'f & saw me at the Touraine. Feeling out of sorts in lower regions I had no lunch & spent the afternoon on my bed — strange — have gone through all sorts of works, times & seasons — during the past month & have had no such disturbance — Do I need constant excitement to keep one up? Eve'g had dinner with Jim & spent the eve'g in a desultory way — Charl came in

## Right Page

### Tues. March 6, 1906

**Weather:** A fine day — not so cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — not so cold — I did not get up till near 9 o'clk & had my bkfs. at about 9.30 — then, after newspaper, until near 12 going over my bills & other affairs requiring immediate attention — then to studio & spent a short time with George — then to Jock's & then walked up 5th Ave to Century & after 2 o'clk. had lunch — After it, in the gallery, where the monthly exbn. is now on, I met Jessie Edgar & a young lady friends from Baltimore, & I went about the gallery with them identifying (so far as I knew them) the many photos of Twelfth Nighters in their costumes. It was 3.30 when I got to my rooms & made plot of my exposed films & went down town to W. Cullens with them & arranged with him to develop & make one print from each — Then I went to Charl's office saw him — Then Wuller, who asked many questions about the trip as he has some thought of going out with his daughter. Back to rooms & down after 6 — Dinner with Jim — he went down to W. 25th St to see Kath. & Bessie & at 9 I went to Charls & was there till near 11 —
