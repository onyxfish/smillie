## Left Page

### Sun. July 15, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another comfortably hot day — Ther. getting up to 80° indoors with pleasant breeze constantly fanning thro' — Ther. in my bed room down to 67° last night — On going to bed last night I took 1 gr. calomel \+ at 4.30 this mf. I took 1/2 oz castor oil — it called me up about 6.30 \+ I had a dreadful time — There was very severe pain in the bowels \+ nausea that made me faint — for awhile it was agony — I sat on the W.C. seat holding a small vessel in my hands \+ retched over it raising two or three oz. of yellowish green liquid — no solids in my stomach — at every parox ups in the bowels answered — it was half an hour or more before I could crawl back to my bed again thoroughly exhausted then fell asleep — I was able to get up, dress \+ go to my bkfp. at 9.30 — For the rest of the day I moved about very little \+ had two or three movements of the bowels, passing a quantity of catarrhal mucus — I wrote part of a letter to Jim Shearman — Dinner at 2. Afternoon the sky clouded over with indications of a coming storm — R. went over to the Hardenburgh back to tea at 7 — Later Mr. Maghee \+ Mr. Hardenburg came, saying that they stood at their house 2 males to 11 females \+ they had come to have a Rest — They left at 9 \+ Till 10 we read more of Bullens stories — then voted that we did not want any more of them — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 16, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day, clouding over in the afternoon \+ a little shower about 4 P.m. After bkfp., that was late, as usual, I spent some time cutting out dead wood \+ forming among the Arbor Vitæ's that I set out on the east side of the house — Then some writing \+ at noon R. \+ I went to the P.O. \+ then to the laundry, but Miss Rivers, the head wash-lady was not there to hear my objections to the puckering of holes thro' the fabric of my shirts etc — Then, as I passed Dr. Garvan's cottage, he on the Piazza, I stopped \+ had a half hours chat with him about hyper acidity, catarrh of the bowels \+ accelerated heart action — The old, old story — all of it familiar to me — no new light — but it must be discussed — no new light being evolved — but the same unreasoning sense of satisfaction follows. Home to dinner at 1.30 Afternoon, nothing done — R. amusing himself with the Hardenburgs — Tea before 7. Evef. on Piazza till 9 — Men in \+ Read two more rather flat stories by Bullens — having nothing else on hand in the way of novelty. To bed soon after 10.
