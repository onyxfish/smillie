## Left Page

### Thur. July 19, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day — Ther. ranging in my bedroom from 66° to 74°. Day after day passes with surprisingly little work done — I don't think I do much real loafing but time gets away from me + I make no mark — wrote to Charl + to Arthur Trumbull + part of a letter to Mary. About 11 o'clk. R. + I went to Library + spent an hour there — then to P.O. got more + home — Dinner after 1 — There was considerable loafing done this afternoon, surely. We wandered to Windy brow + loafed there in the presence of lovely atmosphere effects over the valley + the mts. about us — then home to supper — Along Tramps on north piazza with R. — then in + read Jack London stories till 10.30

## Right Page

### Fri. July 20, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, overcast day — warm + overcast — a sort of damp weather day — that is, threatening + doing nothing more. A large part of the m'f was spent writing to Mary — + to Jim — At noon R. + I went to P.O. — got information sheets from Mr. Howell, with prices for some of the rooms — these R. — sent to Mr. Whiting at Hurricane Lodge in answer to a note he rec'd. from her yesterday — She is not satisfied where she is — dinner at usual time — Afternoon, more writing + some loafing — much of the time I am physically uncomfortable owing to the condition of stomach + bowels — the latter in bad way owing to catarrh — No drugs that I have tried help me + it seems impossible to hit upon the proper diet. Even R. + I read aloud — the last of the Jack London stories + commenced on Cooper's Deerslayer.
