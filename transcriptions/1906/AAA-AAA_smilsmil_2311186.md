## Left Page

### Wed. June 13, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine bright, cool m'f., weather thickened up in the afternoon \+ grew warmer. B'kf't. at 8.30 - After it I went to D. Tuttle's 48 W. 49 S', \+ left some photos for him - Then to studio, getting there at 10.30 - traced on gelatine the enlargement that I got from Cullen yesterday, fitting it onto the shoulders traced after my painting - I got a very fine transfer to ground on plate - I had my lunch after 1 o'clk \+ then commenced the etching of father's portrait - I worked steadily till near 5 o'clk - \+ felt encouraged by what I had done - Then I had a loitering walk \+ got to my rooms about 6 - Dined with Jim at 7.15 - Evn'f. he went out - I wrote awhile - then, as my head ached I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 14, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming m'f., but rather too warm in the afternoon. Up at 7 - wrote note to W. Tho' Victor \+ packed a dozen of my W. I. photos, \+ later mailed them to him - B'kf't. at 8.30 To studio at 10 - Cut amount for etching pf - Then to work on etching of father - at 1 o'clk I went out with my 5×p.o camera \+ got a lot of experimental snap shots - Back to studio \+ had my lunch - Then again at etching - I got all drawn in but the head - At 5 I went by subway up to 79th S'. - To learn about how hard, or how easy it is going to be for me to get to The Wellesley, or from The W. To my studio. The heat \+ dead air in the subway is really sickening - I could not travel by that route in warm weather. I sauntered about in Riverside Park \+ found it very pleasant. After 6 o'clk. I started back taking B'way Trolly \+ Trans. poring at 42nd S'. Far pleasanter than subway in such weather as this - At 7.30 Jim \+ I had dinner - he went to Yale Club in the ev'f. I wrote some titles on photos recently mounted by Cullen - Views in Europe - West Indies etc.
