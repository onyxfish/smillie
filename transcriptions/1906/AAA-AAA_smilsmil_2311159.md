## Left Page

### Sun. April 22, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day. I got up feeling rather wretched but managed to get thro' my morning bath \+ had a light bkfst. at about 10 — Ralph got up later \+ at about noon we went out together — first to Studio — then to Charl's — found him \+ Mary at Bkft. We sat with them till after 1 o'clk. Then Ret. to [Louraine?] I did not go out again feeling very unfit. A little before 7 R.S. I had our dinner, \+ before 8 I went with him to the station \+ saw him off for New-Haven on the 8.02 Train — Then I returned to my rooms \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 23, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day after a wretched night. Some fever — a very sore throat \+ a very sore head — With great pain I got up before 9 o'clk. \+ called Dr. Tuttle — \+ then threw the catch on the door so that any one could come in. I was in great pain all day — but nevertheless I had to get up \+ answer bell \+ telephone calls — a dismal, desolate way for one to live who, heretofore, has always had a family about him — perhaps I may, in time, get used to it. Dr. Tuttle came before noon — He made a long call but there was little that he could say or do to comfort me. Towards night I had a little food sent up from the Restaurant.
