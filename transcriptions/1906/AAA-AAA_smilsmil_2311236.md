## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 21, 1906

**Weather:** ElizabethTown. The Windsor — Room 52.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant, warm day — Up before 6 \+ started the packing of my trunk — b'fst at 7 — Ralph helped \+ by 10 o'clk. the thing was done \+ Jerd Reede came \+ took away the Trunks, the box \+ the seven crates in which the printing press for D'. Gerster was packed. Then I set to work with R— to complete the inventory finding a stiff proposition in the new linen closet where all the linen, cushions, curtains, rugs, pillows, spreads, tack downs, towels, napkins \+ lots of other things are now gathered together — Dinner soon after 12 — Then at inventory again — Wagon came at 2 o'clk. \+ I was nearly wild — Winch came \+ turned off water \+ there were many last things to be looked after — At 3 o'clk. I had man drive me over to Club House — could not find Mr. Brown then drove to The Hemlock's. bid W. Mrs. MacRae Good Bye — Mr. \+ Mrs. Yant — not at home — Then to the Art \+ closed up matters there in very hurried \+ unsatisfactory manner \+ at 4 o'clk. started for E. Town getting to the Windsor at 7 — had supper \+ loafed about till 10. News to bed —

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 22, 1906

**Weather:** The Touraine — N.Y. City.  **Temp:** [illegible]

cloudy cool mt\. — light rain at noon which continued at intervals till night. I got up at 7 o'clk. after a very troublous night — I had to get out of my bed half a dozen times because of cramps in my legs — very painful \+ difficult to subdue — cramps first in tendons of both legs at the groin — later, down the leg \+ at last, down at the ankles — caused possibly by being for the past two days constantly on my feet, winding up with a tramp half way up over Spruce Hill. Bk'fst. with R — soon after 8 — Then we strolled up \+ down the road for an hour or more — met Schilling \+ he joined — \+ later we met Mrs. Obenhym \+ chatted awhile with her. At noon we started for Westport in light rain — drove over in an hour — found Trunks \+ Gerster freight \+ got bills of lading \+ checks — Train 25 m. late — left at 1.55 — had a very good Table d'hote dinner at once — our seats were good \+ the ride was uneventful. had supper at 7 o'clk. — Kate, Katie \+ Stam got thro' with all their plunder — bag of alpines — teapots etc — got us to G. C. Station just about 7.30 half an hour late — Julia waiting for us — went at once to Touraine — It was near 11 before I got to bed —
