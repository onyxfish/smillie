## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 2, 1906

**Weather:** Clear + Cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear + Cold \- Bkft. after 9 o'clk \- After 11 I went to studio + at 12.30 went to Charl's \- There till 1 o'clk \- Then home + had dinner with Jim at 1.45 I wrote to Ralph, sending him a check for $200\. After 3 I walked up Broadway to 86 St., Euclid Hall, + called on Walter + Minnie Pierce \- Had a pleasant chat \- saw young Walter also \- home about 5\. \- tea before 7 \- Evef. loafing about.

I am gaining strength, but my hearing is not yet normal, nor is my Temp. I keeps at about 97°.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 3, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A variety looking snow storm in the morning but, melting as it fell, it amounted to nothing \- Afternoon, clearing, cold \- windy + cold at night \- I spent a good part of the mf. writing to Mr. Louis Holman of Needham, mass, who is preparing an article on Etching in America on something of that sort + who has written to me + other etchers for information \- I got out father's autobiografphic sketch for some preparatory study before a stenographer comes to take down my revision before having it printed \- Lunch, or dinner, (Kate gives me too much to eat) at 1.30 o'clk \- After 3 I went out + had quite a long walk on Riverside Drive \- I am getting my strength nicely now within a few days \- since my digestion has been behaving better \- my hearing is improving gradually \- Dinner with Jim at 7 \- In spite of severe cold that is on him he went out in the evef.

I rec'd. a letter from Mary this mf. written in Florence \- Nov. 19th.
