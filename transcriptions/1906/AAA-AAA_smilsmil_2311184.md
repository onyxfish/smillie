## Left Page

### Sat. June 9, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull cool m'f that gradually passed into an exceedingly disagreeable, hot, stuffy, humid day, with thunder showers about us at eve'g, but no rain or relief came. On my way to studio stopped at C's \+ saw him for a few minutes - at studio planned \+ made working drawings for a device to impress my stamp [J.D. Smillie collection] upon the margin of prints that I shall give to the Public Library Print Room. Lunch in Studio. At 3 o'clk. I went to Bayerdorfer's \+ got the watch that he got for me for Jim's birth-day present - it seems to me too small but it is now the fashion for young men to wear small watches, I am told. - B. gave me the good case of a larger watch to show to Jim - Then to studio \+ worked there till after 5 - then to Charl's for a little while - Interesting letter from Bessie \+ photos. Taken by Katharine. When I got to my rooms found note from Jim saying he had gone to Yonkers - I went to Manhattan \+ dined there. - Ever lounging about doing a little reading \+ nothing of importance.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 10, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear \+ uncomfortably warm. A great wind storm, with clouds of dirt, came up shortly after 5 \+ threatened a deluge but the rain passed north \+ south of us, little falling in this immediate neighborhood. - delightful change to cool eveng. - Up at 7.30 \+ had my scrub - but it was near 10 when I had my M'fs. I wrote a long letter to Mary - went to studio at 1 o'clk. \+ finished it there - had my lunch there at 3 o'clk. - then a nap in my big chair. - It was near 5 o'clk when I rode up town on a day. Ave. car intending to cool off in the park - but the wind storm came up so suddenly \+ threateningly that I at once took a return car on Mad. Ave. - There was much excitement on our trip down from 86th St., people from the Park hurrying \+ crowding into the car - a fury of wind \+ dirt almost blinded us - I got off at 42d. St. \+ had a long sprint after my hat - I got in before the rain came. At 7.15 I went to Manhattan Hotel \+ had dinner - After 8. I went to Charl's. He was giving Mr. Rawlins a lantern slide exhibition - I remained till 10 - then to my rooms.
Jim came in after 11 - spent the day at Yonkers. I gave him his watch.
