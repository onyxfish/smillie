## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 6, 1906

**Weather:** Disagreeable day — some heavy rain in the m'f — clearing later — a mild, misty evening.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Curtains u/o — pictures hung.

Before I finished my b'kf'. man from Lock's came with curtains \+ soon after him Daub's came to hang my pictures — I hurried thro' b'kf'. \+ was busy with the two men for the rest of the m'g. Lock's man got the curtains up before 12 — Daub's job was longer — I gave him his lunch \+ he got through with the hanging at 1.30 \+ chg'd me 5 dollars for the four hours work — Something of a strike I thought but I was glad to have it done — \+ it helped a poor sinner who has a hard struggle — Then I had lunch \+ after that tried to get some order in the rooms about 4 o'clk. I went out \+ walked on the Riverside Driveway — a delightful, misty, mild sad Twilight — lovely color in sky \+ down over the broad river — but very damp. Jim came home \+ we had dinner before 7 — in spite of his cold he went to drill — I fooled around alone.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 7, 1906

**Weather:** Brilliant, cold m'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

About 10 o'clk. I went to studio \+ spent the rest of the morning there making a commencement at cleaning up preparatory to getting to work — The amt. of confusion \+ dirt is very discouraging — there are lots \+ lots of things lying about that I have no place for \+ do not know what to do with them. Home at 1.30 \+ had lunch — After that I hung about waiting for the Globe-Wernicke men, as promised, with extension book-cases — it was about 4 o'clk. when they came — they were very slow getting them in place \+ it was about 6 o'clk. before they left — The cases are placed, one upon another in one long line in the hall \+ the effect is very disapointing — so much so that I am planning to return the whole lot. An attack of vertigo came on gradually. I began to feel queer soon after lunch \+ by the time the men came with book cases I was reeling — Jim took dinner with me at 6.30 \+ then went out with Dick Boardman \+ Ted Putnam — I wrote to Ralph \+ got to bed early.
