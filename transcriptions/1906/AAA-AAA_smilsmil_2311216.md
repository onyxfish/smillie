## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 12, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully fine day — Brilliantly clear \+ as cool as a Sept. day. I had a fire on my bedroom hearth \+ a good scrub before bkft. At 9. At about 11 o'clk. I went over to the Hemlocks to bid W. \+ Mrs. Hardenburg \+ Kath. good bye — they leave for the sea shore — then to Clb. Ho. got mail — saw Scott Brown \+ asked him to order a bath tub for me — then home at 2 \+ dinner — About 4 o'clk. I went to call at W. G. Neilsons — all out but Miss Esther — a short call — then to Mrs. Openhym's — she was out but I saw young Tho, who is tutor to the youngest son, \+ his wife — then to Adler's — one daughter only at home — then I went home — Tea at 6.30 — Evef. R. \+ I sat for some time before the fire — at about 9 the lamps was lighted \+ for an hour we read.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 13, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another day like yesterday, only more so — the Ther. in our bed rooms went down to 50° last night. I had an uncomfortable \+ night. I think I must have passed another stone — I did not get enough awake to know just what the trouble was — The carpenters came, as usual, rather late — I got them started on the board walk bet.n house \+ Annex. Got out the tar pail — fire in laundry stove, heated the tar \+ got Earl \+ Harry at work tarring the 2"×6" timbers, foundations, as the carpenters laid them down — then at 11 went to Club Ho. to see Scott Brown — not in — back to house — at 12.30 again to Club Ho. — Saw Brown — countermanded the tub order \+ in its place ordered a $32" tub — got mail \+ back to dinner — Before going to Club Ho. the second time I wrote a short note to Mary resent with \+ George's letter to me describing the death of old Mr. Jacobs — \+ then Mr. \+ Mrs. Balcom Shaw, Miss Johnston of Newark \+ May T., wife from N.Y. called — Afternoon was spent loitering around — looking at the men \+ struggling with my digestives. Evef. had fire on the hearth — \+ reading aloud.
