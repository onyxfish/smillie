## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 17, 1906

**Weather:** Port of Spain  **Temp:** 82

Very fine day with delightful breeze that robbed the heat of any suggestion of the tropics. it was like Aug. heat when tempered by a breeze at home. I declined to take a bath in the o't, remembering that the sewage of 300 people was being discharged into the shallow sea from the vessel \- Bkfy a little earlier than usual at about 9. Dr. T. \+ I went ashore. The Princess lies out not less than 3 miles \+ the launch with three boats free in tow was 30 min. in reaching the landing \- Dr. T. \+ I wandered up to Queen St where I wished to get a shat. In the few moments I was so engaged the Dr. got astray \+ I did not find him till late in the day \- I went to a department store \+ bought some poor photos \+ then wandered on. I got up on a St [illegible] car, a fellow passt. Chat. I knew Haven got on \+ together we went to the end of the route \+ returning transferred to a St. Clair car \- nearly completed the circuit of the Savannah, a flat field perhaps 400 acres, with many cattle, a covey of English stock with the sacred cow of India \- The tally runs within the enclosure, close to the light fence with grass growing between the rails \- cars of often pattern similar to ours \- Electric controller made by General Electric Co. \- motorman \+ conductors very civil, speaking remarkably pure English \- we rode to the Governor's Palace \+ went thro a part of the grounds \- noted trees of great variety \- various kinds of Palms, Mahogany, bread fruit \- bananas, Nutmeg, Cacao etc. etc. \- went to the Botanical Garden but saw little of it \- auspicious to get my lunch \- went to Queens Park Hotel, overlook Savannah \- charming breeze \- making it really cool on the veranda when I found a vacant table but had to struggle to get a waiter \- got at last an unsatisfactory lunch of eggs \+ bread \+ butter \- this is said to be the best hotel in the West Indies \- but the passengers from the Vic. undumped it. Then I rode out to Coolie town \- not Chinese Coolies but natives of India \- the men, strange quiet, unclean \+ cooking creatures. The women wearing rings in ears nose \+ bracelets on wrists \+ ankles \- The arms of some were tattooed \- the shacks looked horribly squalid \- met Dellenbaugh there \- then we went again to Queens St again \+ got more shots \- then I went to the landing \- no boat \- several Railway tickets in my pocket \- must use them up \- got on a St. Clair car \+ rode to end of route near Gov.ts Palace \- met Mr. [illegible] \+ sister \+ another young easy \+ young man \- went into botanical garden then dit. parted \- got onto car \- later they got on same car \+ we went to landing \- met Di. \- there \- to ship at 6.30 \- dinner at 7 \- eve'g. to retd effects of electric lights from town shimmering down over a smooth water \-
At 11 went out to gaze at the "Southern Cross" which is to me a great disappointment \- I know that I have not yet seen it to advantage \-

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 18, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day, hot of course but tempered by a breeze that made sitting in the shade delightful \- up before 6 just as we were passing from the gulf of Paria thro the very narrow Mouth of the Dragon \- Bath at 7. Bkft. at 8 \- The band played on deck Em Forte Boing \- before breakfast I wandered about till after 10 \- then had a vibratory massage of liver \+ abdomen \+ a horseback Ride \- after 12. I went to state room \+ had a nap \- a very light lunch at 1.30 \- fussed with my actinometer till I got it to work \- about 3 or 3.30 we came in sight of some small, lumpy islands \+ soon after we came to Margarita Island as I read the maps hung up for our instruction \- high mts \- 3300 \- no evidence that I could see of habitations altho' in one part I saw what looked like cultivated land \- a great bay seemed to separate the island into two. The land connecting them being so low as not to be seen above our horizon \- at 4 had another horseback Ride \+ more vibratory massage \- saw some fine dolphins playing near the ship \- They leaped higher than any I have ever seen before \- Being told that we might catch a mail to N.Y. to-morrow at La Guayra I wrote a letter to George \+ mailed it in the Ship's Post Office \- Dinner at 7 \- Eve'g. a nap in my chair on deck \- Band played from 9 to 10 \-
