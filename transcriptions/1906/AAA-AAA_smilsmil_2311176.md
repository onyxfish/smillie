## Left Page

### Sat. May 26, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm, rather muggy day with strong breeze. Bkfs. as usual \+ got to Studio after 10 \- I spent a lot of time taking pp. out of press \+ blotters \+ getting my two copies of Etching Club Cats. that were badly out of shape owing to haste when published in putting damp photo portraits in with dry etching pp. \+ letter press \- together after they had been damped \+ kept in press for some days \- Then I did some fussing work on some of the plates that I have on my desk \- Lunch in Studio \- A little wandering in Bryant Park before returning to Tourraine \+ having dinner alone \- Jim had gone to a ball game with the Dielmans \+ did not get in till after 10 P.M. I spent the evg., or most of it, reading "The Spoilers."

## Right Page

### Sun. May 27, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A succession of heavy showers all day. I got up soon after 7 \+ had a bath \+ then went to bed again \- up at 8 \+ had bkfs. at 9. I finished reading "The Spoilers" \+ the Tribune \+ loafed about \- At 1.30 I had lunch in the restaurant \+ after that I spent some time in arranging a file of Tribunes \+ Posts from Apr. 19" to May 9" giving a running story of the San Francisco earthquake \+ incidents that followed \- I think that it will be interesting if not valuable in years to come as a contemporary acct. About 5 Clark called me up \+ asked me to take dinner with him \- Jim had been out all day so I went there, had dinner \+ spent the evg. with him \- Jas. came in later \- I got to my rooms soon after 10 \+ a few minutes later Jim came in bringing Gould with him who is going to crowd into his single bed with him \- an imposition as I view it.
