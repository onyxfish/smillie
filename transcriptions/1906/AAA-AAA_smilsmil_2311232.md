## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 13, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A curious day of south easterly wind with haze \+ low-hanging clouds that at times seemed to burn away letting some pale sunshine through. There was occasional thunder - after 6 p.m. a thunder storm passed over us \+ later there was some heavy rain. I was up early \+ the plumber came \+ spent the m't putting in new traps under bath tub \+ when he left saying the job was finished there was leaking at four joints \- \+ with the carpenter came in the afternoon \+ worked for a time at fitting flooring that had been cut out for plumber but d w. not let him nail it in place so long as there were leaks under it \- Trumbull came for a time \- I had work a plenty in airing mattresses etc \- After 6, Mr. Hardenburg came, followed later by Mr. \+ mrs. Macbee \+ mrs. Vautrain \- they came to see the house but it was too dark for that \+ they had to hurry away to escape the storm. Tea at 6.30 \- Sue f. R. \+ I played letter game \- he beating me, 18 to 14 words.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 14, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some rain during last night \+ for an hour or two very high wind about daylight \- the day cleared breezy \+ cold \- temps. nes machine at noon very pleasant \- Three of Trumbull's men got started at work on roof of ice house \+ butlers pantry \- one man did not come till late in the m't. T\- came up for awhile. At night the new roof boards were on \- the tar paper \- the shingle latts \+ one row of shingles \- During the day I continued my work of gathering contents of new linen closet \- aired folddowns, blankets \+ matters. To library at 11.30 with books \- then to P.O. Dinner at 1 \- Worked, with pain \+ discomfort till after 5\. Mr. Scott Brown came \+ looked over things generally \+ agreed to superintend the opening of the house next spring. Then I had a rest before tea at 6.30 \- Sue f. some reading aloud.
