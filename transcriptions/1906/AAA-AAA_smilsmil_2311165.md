## Left Page

### Fri. May 4, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine spring day - after bkft. I did some letter writing \+ got to studio after 10. I tried to interest myself in some of the unfinished work lying about there but it was useless - I was incapable of doing anything - so, when it came 1 o'clk I prepared my lunch \+ after eating it rode up to the Met. Mus. of Art - 5th Ave \+ 82d. St. There I met the librarian \+ had a pleasant chat with him - he found the two Doughty paintings now owned by the museum - I think my little one has better qualities than either of them - then I walked down to the Lenox library to see the exhibit there of Am. Etchings - I am pleased with the manner in which 11 of mine are displayed - I saw Weitenkampf - then I walked down \+ across the Park to 7th Ave Gate - lovely evening effects - \+ out to water color show in the Fine Arts Bldg. - met there Nicoll, J. H. Smillie \+ others - am pleased with the placing of my etchings - I walked all the way down to the Touraine with Smillie who is full of his project of painting in oil - I shall be interested in the outcome - Dinner with Jim at 7 - Every little doing -

## Right Page

### Sat. May 5, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Muggy \+ overcast - a very heavy thunder shower between 4 \+ 5 o'clk. p.m. Up \+ had bkft. at 9 - it was after 10 when I got to studio feeling strangely out of sorts - I cannot exactly describe the sensation - utterly unfit for any exertion mental or physical - I fear a hypochondriacal break down \+ am struggling to get out of the rut that I am in \+ do work of some kind for diversion - I gathered such material as I could find to begin work on the etching commenced 18 months or more ago, "Study of an old Man's Head" from small photo. taken for me at Keene 1904 - but I could not find enlarged photo, which I must have - About 1.30 I prepared lunch in studio - After 3 I left the studio \+ wandered to Park Ave \+ 34th St. - crowds were assembling for Parade of 7th Regt. celebration of century organization - as thunder storm was threatening I went to my Touraine rooms. After the storm passed over the Regt. passed up 5th Ave. \+ I saw it from my windows - Jim went to Katharine Hardenburgh's to dine. Charl came in at 6.30 \+ I went to his house and dined with him - Altho it was monthly meeting at Century I did not feel equal to going there so I spent the evg. with Charles, most of the time being given to preparation for his show to come off at the Century next Sat. Night -
