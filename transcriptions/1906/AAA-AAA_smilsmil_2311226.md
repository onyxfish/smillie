## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 1, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still windy, clear \+ cool \- delightful weather for out of door work \- again, most of the day was spent. Transplanting evergreens At noon I went to Club ho. \- got mail \+ saw Scott Brown \- he said he had got track of bath tub fixings \+ they may be along to-night. Dinner at 1 \- worked till about 5 \- then a rest \- Tea at 6.30 \- Evelf, some reading of Max O'Rell \- Jonathan \+ his continent \- then I wrote a letter to Will Parke \+ to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 2, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine day \- very cool mf. \- growing milder \- still much wind \- midday overcast \- beautifully clear night \- brilliant full moon \- Up at 7 \- \+ had my scrub \- bkfs. at 9 \- delayed somewhat by Katey going to church \+ Earl \+ Harry starting on their climb to the top of Giant \- After bkfs. I wrote a while \- At noon went to Ferd Beede's \- saw his wife \+ got more promises \- Then home \+ at 1. with Ralph, went to Club ho. \+ at 1.30 dined with W. \+ Mr. Mc Hawes \- introduced to W. \+ Mrs. Delafield who sat at same Table \- Miss Hawes was indisposed \- a very good dinner \+ pleasant time generally \- Mr. \+ Mr. D. very agreeable. We left at about 3 \- I went to Mrs. Hardenburgh's \- saw Mrs. Vaultom \- She tells me that Mr. Maghee is in a serious nervous condition \- walked with her to chapel \+ then I went home \- Tea at 6.30 \- Evelf \- some reading \+ some moonlight.
