## Left Page

### Fri. March 9, 1906

**Weather:** Thinly overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thinly overcast day - B'kfs. before 9 \+ at about 10 went to studio - stopped at the house of Chas. Collins \+ learned from him that his sister, over 70, had been operated upon for appendicitis - had passed a crisis \+ was doing better even than could be expected of one of her age. - Then to studio \+ worked there at clearing up till 12.30 - then to dock to Pasteur filter \+ to bank - To Century at 1.30 \+ had lunch after 2 - It was late then again I got to studio \+ became so much interested in my correspondence with Seymour Haden, 1883, that I found in one of my boxes, that I did no work and got to my rooms at about 6 - There I got a pkg. from Cullens containing all the rolls I left with him a few days ago - all developed \+ prints from them - of course I was greatly interested - also disappointed The effects were unsatisfactory, lacking in atmosphere - the contrasts too strong - after dinner I took the prints to Chas. \+ he expressed himself as greatly pleased, saying that what I objected to, represented to him tropical light. - To bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 10, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

New-Haven-

A fickle day. a mix-up of March \+ April - my head was very much mixed up also \+ caused me more or less discomfort all day. I got to studio at about 10 \+ spent the m'f. trimming many of the photo. prints Rec'd. fm Cullens - It was after 1 o'clk. when I got to my rooms - I lay down for half an hour to relieve my head - had a light lunch at 2 \+ after that went to G't. Cent. Station \+ by 3 o'clk. train to New Haven, getting there a few m. before 5\. Ralph met me at station \+ we took a Winchester Ave. car out to Prospect St., or as near to that as the car went - were very cordially rec'd. by Katie \+ W.M. - Ralph remained \+ took dinner with us - Evening we looked over my photos \+ talked of the recent trip till 10 o'clk - then R. Ret'd. to his quarters \+ I went to bed.

We left the city in a snow flurry \+ passed thro' two or three before getting to N.H. - High wind \+ growing very cold - brilliant moonlight \+ cold as Greenland.
