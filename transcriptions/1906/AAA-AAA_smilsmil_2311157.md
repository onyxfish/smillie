### Wed. April 18, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — Rather too warm for comfort — the effect was physi cally depressin After 10 when I got to studio — I went then to Clawson's with mat for Charcoal drawing to be changed — \+ gave order for smoke from for an etching to be sent to W.C. Egbts. It was after 11 when I returned to studio — worked till after 1 o'clk on printing press, Removing iron Rust with Emery ploth — hard work — then had lunch in my studio. I continued my work in the afternoon till 5 o'clk — Then to my Rooms at Touraine — at 7 Jim. Raelph \+ I had dinner \+ the eveg was spent quietly at home — The afternoon papers were full of incoherent news from San Francisco of a very destructive earth quake at 5 o'clk this A.M., followed by fires which because of broken water mains could not be checked.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 19, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm day, so that even a light top coat was a burden — I had bllp — alone \+ at 9.30 went to Douglas about small sign — then to Millen's \+ got to studio after 10 — I got to work without delay on printing press \+ had hard \+ dirty work cleaning journals \+ bearings that were gummed up with oil \+ dirt. By 1 o'clk I had got most of that work done — Raelph Theis came \+ we had lunch in the studio. we had raw oysters, beer, gluten bread \+ butter \+ biscuit — also ice cream \+ prunes — after lunch I prepared papers for printing \+ at 4 o'clk with R — left to Touraine to get ticket to Met. Mrs. \+ Art, thinking it was an afternoon Recepl — but instead, it is from 9 to 11 this eveg — So, then I rested from my labors till dinner at 7, with Jim \+ Raelph — Eveg Jim went to his "neighborhood" dance — R. \+ I Remained at home — much of the eveg was spent reading the dreadful news from San Francisco where earthquake \+ fire have have done \+ the fire is still doing, such appalling destruction —

Before going to bed — at about 11 o'clk., I "caved in." — headache — sore throat \+ fever — I had a dismal night.
