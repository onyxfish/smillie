## Left Page

### Mon. July 9, 1906

**Weather:** Rain during a part of the m'f., cloudy during the rest of the day, with some light showers + thunder \- a wet sort of pale moonlight at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent a part of the m'f. finishing my letter to Bessie which I sent off by mail at 2.15. I saw Scott Brown for a few minutes about noon time + I saw W. G. Neilson + with both I talked about water supply thro' new pipe \- had dinner after 1 o'clk \- Wes. Otis called + together we went over the line proposed for the new 1" pipe, just grazing the S.E. cor. of W. G. N's Highland Cottage lot \- to this I gave my assent after objecting, without result, to paying for pike on the Reserve ground \- as I paid a considerable sum per an. for water I thought the Reserve should deliver it at its boundary line \- as it is W. G. N. + I will bear the expense \- When supper time came I was unable to eat owing to severe acidity \- I was very much distressed by it. Ralph + I read aloud during the ev'g. from Marion Crawford's

## Right Page

### Tues. July 10, 1906

**Weather:** A disagreeable day \- Rain in the m'f. then a half clearing \- then, about noon \- thunderstorms all about \- Later in the afternoon, a chilly rain \-  **Temp:** [illegible]

We had our help \- about 6.30 \- After 10 R. + I went over to the Club house + to the library, where I subscribed for my family for the season. We sat there + read till mail time, 11.15. Then to P.O. where we had another wait till the mail was distributed. We met there Miss Dillard + a number of others. Then home. R. + Harry filled the cooler with ice \- a hard + disagreeable piece of work \- Nye's man came + completed the machine mowing on man with scythe cut where the machine could not go \- The work is done at a bad time. Dinner at 1.30 \- After that, work in doors till tired + chilly. I lay down on my bed + had a nap, while more rain fell \- Tea at 6.30 \- After that we sat before a fire \+ chatted till near 9 \- then we lighted the lamps + for more than an hour took turns in reading Bullen stories.
