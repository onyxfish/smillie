## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 11, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 78°

Very fine day - Stir aboard began before daylight - anchor up + by 6.30 we were moored alongside of the coaling wharf - I had a bath before 7. + then bkfst - The sight of the bare legged women, + the half naked men, all negroes, carrying immense baskets of coal on their heads was picturesque but not pretty - many of the men were diving in the water for coins thrown to them - They were big + splendidly developed, a black bronze - all with superb teeth - Dr. T. + I went on shore in the launch before 9.30 - we wandered to Blue Beard's castle but found little that was picturesque or photo graphable - The light was fickle owing to clouds - we were beset on all hands by beggars + at last got to the ship at about 11 - The harbor of St. T. is a Remarkable landlocked basin with very narrow entrance - I could not get a satisfactory view of it because I did not have a Panorama Camera - I went ashore at the coal yard + took a number of snap shots at groups of coal carriers - The men shove the coal into the baskets + left it to the heads of the women who carry it. At noon the ship got away + headed for Porto Rico. The Passage among the islands was very interesting + recalling, but very different, our trip thro' the Aegean groups. I was surprised at the height of the mts on Porto Rico - The summits of the highest were covered by clouds - in shape they are heavy + rolling like the Catskills - we came to the port of San Juan at about 6 o'clk + dropped anchor at some distance from shore - at once were visited by Revenue + Health officers - The old Spanish fort was very picturesque + altho' the light was poor I made three snap shots at it as we passed - Dined at 7 - about 8 we went to town in the launch - just as we reached the landing place there was a short sharp shower - fortunately I had my umbrella, but nevertheless got quite damp - we went to the plaza where a good band was playing - the square was crowded with people + well lighted by electricity - in spite of the wetting every thing seemed to be quite gay - we came about 11 along, then returned to landing place + to our steamer - very effective moonlight with storm clouds - Then some writing + to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 12, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 82°

Lincoln's birth day -

A fine day with threatening showers almost constantly about - The showers were so frequent in the early mng that I gave up thought of going ashore but Dr. T. braved the threatening + went alone. About 10 the sky over the town was clear while over the hills to the north clouds + showers still prevailed - I went ashore alone + wandered about on foot shooting off my camera in a Random way - at the market a lady accosted me, asking if I were a stranger - she told me that she was the wife of Rafferty the Surgeon of the port, with quarters at [illegible] old fort on the outskirts of the town + gave me a lot of suggestions + directions - She showed me the heart of the cabbage palm + advised me to buy a portion to have it prepared as a salad with mayonnaise dressing - I took a number of snap shots + then to the wharf + landing place - there is but one Proper wharf in San Juan - + to ship where I had lunch - Then a little nap + at 2.30 went ashore again alone - showers threatening from other side of bay but did not reach the town side - I wandered about without settled plan, taking several shots - I got out to the Molo fort but was denied entrance because I did not have a pass - but silver got it for me - + went in taking two ladies also, without passes - The fort is an immense structure + dates back to the 16th Century - extremely interesting - a small number of native troops under Am. officers - no armament. Then into town again but found myself too tired to walk to San Cristobal - went to two photo shops + bought a lot of photos - ninto landing + got to ship at 6 o'clk - well tired - had a nap before dinner - Lincoln's birth day was handed me a crowd - special menu, all English + good dinner - much comparing of purchases in the eve'g. The principal objects being Panama hats - a great activity in preparation for start - a number of visitors from shore - boats coming + going constantly - last boat 10.30 - one or two very drunk on last boat - Search light put in operation - locating buoys - moon up.
