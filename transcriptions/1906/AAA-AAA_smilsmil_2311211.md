## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 2, 1906

*Charl started for Europe.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - up early & did some work before bkft. After bkft. spent sometime in printing experiments - on the enlarger & Efpo negatives & on some of the 5×4 Rock studies developed yesterday - Results on Bromide Efpo enlargements, not satisfactory - the Rock negatives are so dense & "contrasty" that by the time the lights are printed out the shadows are masses of black - I shall have to get some "Special Velox". Ralph was all the m'f mending my big Rattan bedroom chair - Taking out the broken strands & running in new ones that I had bought. I went to P.O. for mail after 12 - Mr. Marquand introduced me to his bro. in law, Mr. Terry. Home to dinner. Afternoon R- developed the remaining six exposures on my roll of films & then spent the rest of day till eve'f repairing one of the large Rattan couches on N's piazza - except when we took a little ramble over the W.Q. Neilson hill & met two of the N-girls. Eve'f on piazza till after 9 - then in to a little reading of "Miss Presia's filagree Ball" - I think very little of this work - it is not ingenious in plot - nor is it entertainingly written - and it is gruesome - I have disliked it with a strange dread since we first took it up - it seemed to give me a sort of nervous fit - we had not read for some nights & I took it up unwillingly - promptly I felt uncomfortable - a disagreeable feeling in my head - a morbid, nervous feeling that grew till when I went to bed at 10.30 I was in a sort of indefinable nervous panic - I got to sleep in after minutes but my night was full of uneasy dreaming.

Arthur Trumbull made a long call in the afternoon & promises to send a couple of men here at once to commence the work of changing location of linen closet & fitting up bath room on bed room floor.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 3, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'f. but weather changing slowly - clouds banking up till after 6 o'clk. when light rain fell. This troubled Ralph whose fishing permit's for to-morrow. He went to the valley on his wheel & made preparations in various ways for the sport. I spent most of the m'f in & out of dark room, printing a number of Efpo negatives & failing to get satisfactory results - also some of the Rock studies, 5×4 - only proving that the whole gross of Velox paper that I have had in an unopened pkg for nearly a year is spoiled - At noon I went to the Club house & got mail - then dinner - Afternoon I was not in working order & what I did amounted to nothing. Tea at 6.30 - after it R- went to see Lawrence Adler who was to have come to meet R- at 5 to dig bait for their trip to-morrow - Lawrence was uncertain about his going & R- was disgusted. We lounged on piazza till 9 — then in & read a couple of J.H. Smith's stories from "The under Dog" - to bed at 10.30 — R- planning to get up at 4.30 to-morrow.
