## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 3, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy Rain \+ thunder during last night. Rain continued in the m't till about 10 \- then clouds broke away suddenly for a short time Rain came again about noon \+ before 3 o'clk there was a gale, lasting for a few minutes, that made the house tremble \- I feared for a few minutes that the north front would be blown us \- later, weather cleared \- I spent part of m't writing to K. P. T \- at 12 I went to Club Ho \- got mail \- Saw B. Hawes \+ invited him, wife \+ daughter to dine with us to-morrow \- Home to dinner at 1 \- after the gale I gave up to headache \+ was on my bed till after 6 \- Then tea \- Eve'g, Ralph \+ I wrote \- I finished my letter to K. P. T.

Adams did not work.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 4, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. \+ Mrs. Jr. Hawes \+ daughter dined with us.

Early m't cold \+ lowering \- the day, very bright \+ beautiful \- Up early \+ made some preparations in-doors for our dinner party \- About 10 I went to Club Ho \+ got a lake trout. On my return to the house Arthur Trumbull came \+ took measures for new roof for ice house etc \- Miss Haswell called \+ told me of a plan to celebrate Scott Brown's 25th wedding anniversary on friday eve'g \- At 1 o'clk. Mrs. Hawes, daughter Florence \+ With came \+ soon after we had a very good dinner \+ all seemed to pass off pleasantly \- Florence was animated \+ amusing \- After dinner we sat in main room \+ chatted till near 4 o'clk \- Some coal \+ a half load of manure came up from Ford Bedes \- I had [illegible] \+ spread the manure as far as it w'd go over the poorer part of new tennis court \- then spread lightly soil por old garden \+ then sprinkle all with the hose \- the water pressure was so poor that it would not run freely from the hose \- a great disappointment to me \- Tea at 6.30 \- Eve'g. Earl brought from the club ho. the nickle plated bath tub fixtures \- R. \+ I had some reading \-

Adams \+ his son worked to-day.
