## Left Page

### Fri. June 1, 1906

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast with now + then a gleam of sunshine \- growing hotter till at night it was muggy hot. Up at 7 + wrestled with balancing my bank acct. which am accomplished before bkfst. soon after 8. At 10 I went to Second Nat'l. Bnk \- then to studio \- it was near noon before I got to work at printing, using up the dampened paper left over from yesterday \- but ink or paper, or rags were not in proper shape + I did not get satisfactory results \- so I cleaned up + had lunch at 2 o'clk. \- After that till 5 I was making ready to start on the portrait of Father on his 75th birthday. \- Then to Grand Central + bought ticket to New-Haven \- then to Touraine \- Jim + I had dinner there at 7\. \- Eve'g I had writing to do + getting ready for my New-Haven skree to-mor. Charl sent around a very interesting letter from Mary \- from Florence.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 2, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

477 Prospect St. New-Haven.

Very muggy, warm day, clearing pleasantly in the afternoon. Two heavy thunderstorms between 6.30 + 9.30 P.M. Up at 6.30 + prepared for my trip to New-Haven. \- Bkfst. at 8 \- at 9 went Charl's with Mary's letter, saw him \- then to G. C. Station + by 10:02 train to New Haven, getting there on time at 12. Ralph met me at Station + we went to his room \- At 1 o'clk. we went to the Y.M.C.A. dining room + had a poor dinner for 30 cents each \- at 2 o'clk. we took a trolley car for Base Ball field + got there early + had a chance to see the Yale fellows practice \- The Princeton boys came on + the game was called promptly at 3 \- It was a close game \- little brilliant play but very exciting \- the afternoon weather was delicious but at the close of the game \- about 5.30 \- there were ominous clouds in the air west + a smell of coming showers in the air. We got down to Pierson promptly enough but time was lost there + more time was lost by just missing a car, obliging us to wait 12 min. for another \- In consequence, the shower fell upon us furiously so that we could not get out of our car \- we rode to the end of the route + then back to Woolsey Hall. The rain was then falling lightly, we got out + waited in the porch for a returning car + finally got to the Elkin's at 6.45 + found them writing for us \- Dinner was served promptly + after that we had a chatty eve'g till about 9 o'clk when threats of another storm drove the boys away \- They just escaped it \-
