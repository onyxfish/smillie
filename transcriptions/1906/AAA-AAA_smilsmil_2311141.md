## Left Page

### Sat. March 17, 1906

**Weather:** Bright day, rather cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up early \+ had bkfst. at 7.30 - It was about 10 when I got to studio - some cleaning up before I commenced upon the work of preparing a contribution for Pub. Library Print dept. of Am. Etchings. Then Dr. Hale came in \+ was with me for some time \+ shortly after he left Weitenkampf came \+ with him the rest of the mf. went - Soon after 1 I went to the Century \+ had lunch - after 2 I went over to Dr. Hale's but was greatly impeded by St. Patrick's Day crowds \+ procession - Y - \+ I were to go to Brooklyn to see Mf. Voigt but we concluded to give it up for to-day \+ I went to my studios at 3.30 expecting to cut some mounts yet it was so uncomfortably warm in the studio that I accomplished nothing \+ left before 5 - Got to rooms at 6 - Dinner with Jim at 7 - Eve'g he yielded to Dick Boardman \+ went off with him when he should have gone to bed - I passed away the eve'g. doing nothing of any importance -

## Right Page

### Sun. March 18, 1906

**Weather:** A brilliant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up soon after 7 \+ had a long session in bath room, rubbing and scrubbing - Bkfst. after 9 - with newspaper till after 11 - It was near noon when I went out taking my camera in which is my 24th role of West Indian \+ Venezuelan exposures with three snaps yet available, \+ blank - I went to Elevated R.R. platform, 6th Ave. 5 33°. St. \+ took two shots \+ then one at Bway \+ 28th St. Then to Mary's, 44 W. 25th St. \+ spent nearly an hour with her \+ Katharine - Then back to my rooms \+ had lunch at 2, just as Jim came down to his bkfst. After that I wrote \+ catalogued my negatives \+ had a nap - Dinner with Jim at 7 - Then to Emma I's - §§1 E. 17th St. - There met Gordon Ireland - the first time I have seen him - He left early - spent a pleasant eve'g. till 10.30 then walked to the Lorraine \+ to bed -
