## Left Page

### Glee Club Concert. Mon. June 25, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another superb day - up at 7 & had a short walk before bkft. at 8 - after 9 I walked down to Pierson, passing over the campus & amused by the members of the different graduate classes that were out in their uniforms - the class of 1903 in stripes as convicts. I found R- at his room & sat with him some time - Then we went out on a shopping tour & I bought neckties - Then we ret- to the Elkins getting there about noon - R- had bought, as I requested, four seats for the Glee Club concert tonight - as good as could have been expected at so late an application - at the end of left hand gallery, by stage, 2d row. We had lunch at 1.30 - R- & I went out & inspected quite thoroughly two new houses, one extensive, now building north of the Elkins house - Then I had a nap - Dinner at 6.30 - R- had gone to his room for change of dress & etc - as we had dinner. Katie, Mollie, R- & I went to the Glee Club Concert which was set at 8.15 but did not begin till 8.40 - It was rather disappointing to me - The selection had nothing above average - The negro songs by a quartette on programme were not given - No reason given - a whistler, I. S. Mills, did some peculiar whistling - it was not true, nor did it harmonize with the voices - H. Butler gave, on encore, a monologue, but altho' very near, I could not discover fun or excellence - The concert was over at about 10.30 - We were fortunate in getting a car at the door & were home before 11.

## Right Page

### The ball game. Tues. June 26, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another glorious mf - the weather continued beautifully clear till about 4 P.m. when a haze covered the sky & it was cooler - Slept till 8 - then bkft. & after that W. Brown came in with two choice Ball game seats to dispose of - They were so vastly better than the two Raehn had that I took them at once - Katie & W.m giving me the chance - of course they were not afford to them - Then, after 10 I went downtown to look for R- & was fortunate enough to find him in the street. We went to office in Osborn Hall & there R- got money for return of his seat. Then we went out among the crowds & I got a number of snap shots by my Vipo camera among the groups of the students & their many class uniforms - Then we ret- to the Elkins & had a lunch before 1 oclk - Raehn & I went ahead of the others - took car to the green & there [illegible] to car to the field which was reached soon after 2 o'clk - our seats were in 4th row from front a little to the right of the catcher - a very good place to see the erratic curves of the balls sent by pitcher - The crowds were immense - The classes & their bands were kept from entering till near 3 o'cl - there we had a circus parade, some parts of which were very amusing & the whole was immensely effective - It was some time before the game was called - it was not till the 2d or 3d innings that Yale scored one & two innings Harvard scored 2 amid immense commotion - 5 on Yale scored another & so they hung tied till the 9 innings had been played - then there was intense excitement - Harvard came alarmingly near to scoring 1 on The 10th innings & for awhile Yale's 10th looked like another 0, but at the last moment Jackson made a tremendous hits to the left along the ground between the fielders & 3 base came home like a steam engine — a whirlwind followed - The field was instantly covered by a yelling, dancing, frantic mob - all the bands played & pandemonium was supreme - We waited & watched for some time - then took car - Rode to Pres- Hadley's house & saw several of the classes come up & pay their respects to the Pres- - We got to the Elkins at 7.20 - dinner nearly over - ev'y. very quiet - every body tired & sleepy - I went to bed before 10.
