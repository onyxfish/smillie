## Left Page

### Mon. July 23, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day opened over cast \+ extremely muggy. The cloudiness burned away \+ at noon it was oppressively hot \+ humid — about 3 o'clk. a rattling thunder storm passed over causing a prompt change for the better in atmospheric conditions. I suppose it must be atmospheric conditions that have thrown me back for to-day I have been very uncomfortable \+ depressed. I spent most of the mf mending clothes \+ getting soiled clothes prepared for laundry — a poor business for a man (or for any one) but do not see how, under existing conditions I can better it. At noon, W\. the R\- went to P.O. got a letter from Charl of depressing character. He writes that between pressure of business \+ the nimble weather he thinks at times he will not be able to pull through — it made me very unhappy when added to my own complainings. In the same letter he gave his itinerary for his summer trip, starting from N.Y. on the Amerika, Aug. 2. to meet Mary \+ the girls, spend six weeks with them \+ home again Oct. 9. — Home to dinner — wrote a letter to Charl. — Evg on piazza \+ later again tried the "experiment" of Sandwichian Macleans duel witted non conformists \+ their religious disputes with Jack London's godless trappers, border men \+ gold seekers — The combination has nothing to commend it.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 24, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 72° to 55°

A cool day \+ cloudy much of the time — am feeling better to-day. Mf. I wrote to bro. Geo. R\- went down the valley on his wheel \+ I went to P.O. by way of the Ghost walk \+ over the old road thro' Neilson's woods. Back to dinner at 1. After that, small chores \+ loafing about till after 4. Then I walked around the block — that is, down the hill, then around by the Putnam camp, then around, past Weston's \+ home over the links. Supper at 6.30 — then along evg on north piazza rather cool but I was rolled in my blanket \+ it was very enjoyable — a Thrush sang till deep dusk — 8.15 — some time after the bats were out — The song, at considerable distance, seemed to me to be continuous \+ much longer than that of the thrush who is domiciled \+ sings near us. R\- said there were two \+ that their songs were blended. I think he was mistaken. I wished it could have been nearer to me that I might have got the details of it. At that hour, with all other birds quiet it was very impressive. About 9 we went in, lighted a fire \+ read aloud for an hour or more.
