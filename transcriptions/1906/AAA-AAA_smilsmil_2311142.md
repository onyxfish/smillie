## Left Page

### Mon. March 19, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A heavy snow storm all day, beginning at about 10 o'clk — I got to studio at that time \+ was storm bound till nearly 6. I went to Hahnenfeld's at 1 \+ got eggs, biscuits \+ cherries \+ had lunch in my studio — I worked all day cutting mounts for etching pts. for the Lenox Lib. Print Dep't. Show \+ for the Etching side-show of the coming W.C. Soc'y exhn.
To my rooms \+ had dinner with Jim at 7 — Eve'g, wrote to Clarence Smith \+ sent him a check $100. to close the Southack-Pease \+ Elliman squabble in which P. \+ E. have failed to keep their written promise to protect me ag't. any claim by Southack \+ $25. to Smith for professional services —

## Right Page

### Tues. March 20, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m'g, followed by a day of clouds \+ sunshine — Up at 7.30 — Sore all over, head, shoulders, back \+ heels — I got down to b'kf't. at 8.30 — went to Clausen's \+ got some mounting boards for my etchings — to studio about 10 \+ spent rest of m'g working on pen sketch. Some of Pantheon, from the Seine, "Paris" — I had lunch in my studio \+ at 2 went down town — Called upon Am. Alexander, at his office in the Equitable Bld'g — Some time — he explaining to me the history of the new Club — The Ausable Lake \+ Mt. Club" — a wheel within the Adirondack or [illegible] Reserve wheel — a plan to make use of St. Hubert Inn property since it has been taken over by the Reserve. I told Alexander that I w? join — there to Queens — Am. not in — saw Edwin — left my photo-helps with him to be mounted — then up town to studio — then took mounting b'ds, bought a year or more ago, to Clausen's to be framed — then to Fontaine \+ had a nap before dinner at 7 with Jim — Before 8 o'clk went to Charl's \+ with him \+ Bessie went to meeting of Geographical Soc'y — \+ heard a more discourse uninterestingly about the fiords of Norway, illustrating his Talk with some very poor photos — Mr. \+ Bessie stopped at my room for a few minutes on their way home.
