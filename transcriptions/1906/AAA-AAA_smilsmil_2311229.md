## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 7, 1906

Silver wedding anniversary \-
Mr. & Mrs. W. Scott Browns

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant warm day with a smart shower just before 7 P.M. work of various kinds during the day, most of it out of doors \- have not felt well today \- have had some little touches of pain in neighborhood of left Kidney \- the discomfort in Right Kidney is slowly progressing \- this is depressing \- I cannot forget the many things that I want to complete before my end for the benefit of the little professional reputation that I take to leave behind me \- Went to P.O. at noon \- Then dinner \- transplanted some balsam trees to north of house \- had call from Winnie Neilson about cutting out some dead Poplars on his line \- Had a trying time in making out inventory of things in kitchen \- Then a rest \- Tea at 6.30 \- Soon after 8 o'clk R \+ I went to the Du Bois woods where a celebration under direction of Mr. \+ Miss Howell was under way of the 25th wedding anniversary of Mr. \+ Mrs. Scott Brown \- The shower did not dampen the proceedings \- It was an extremely picturesque affair. In an opening in the woods, reached by a wood road there was a great fire that radiated light \+ heat within the circle, outside of which it was utmost blackness \- there were benches \+ about forty or possibly more people \- But wife had been blindfolded \+ brought in a buggy \- a complete surprise \- did not for some time know where they were \- great hilarity \- Mrs. Thro. Janeway sang an explanatory song written for the occasion \- familiar air with chorus \- Mock telegrams were read \+ one of the Bab Ballads was acted by the Croton boys under direction of their mother \- later a Silver cake basket was presented \- Then tissue paper cakes of many colors \+ patterns were donned \+ chocolate \+ cakes were served I left at about 9 \+ I feared the damp night air.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 8, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm pleasant day with a delightful trace of coolness in the air \- I got up feeling very seedy \- uncomfortably warm \+ chilly \- Took a dose of quinine \- Part of on \+ about with Earl transplanting trees to the north of house. Ralph went to P.O. for me \- Dinner at 1 \- After that went to the Hardenburg hill side to get a fine Cedar bunch \- Men up to the Hardenburg house \- met a number of callers there Miss Davis \+ Kate Vaultom going to N.Y. city at 4 o'clk \- Good byes \- Then home \+ had a rest \- Tea at 6.30 \- Eve\[ning\] before 8 R. \+ I went to Club House \- soon after all adjourned to the Library \+ D[illegible] Kno[illegible] of Japan gave us a very entertaining \+ instructive Talk about the Social Life of the Japs. Their language \+ caste, or Rank, distinctions. Every body seemed to be greatly pleased \- contributions were asked for interior decorations for school at foot of the hill \- makes or prints \- Sat awhile on piazza with Mr. \+ Mrs. Delafield \- Mr. Frothingham lent me W.J. Bryan's Reply to "Letters from a Chinese" aff[illegible] \- a senseless blunder, I think, \+ a characteristic one on Bryans part.

Shingles for Icehouse were delivered.
