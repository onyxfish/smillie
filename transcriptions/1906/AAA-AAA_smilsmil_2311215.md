## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 10, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The weather could not make up its mind what to do till evef — then it rained a little gently — I was up early \+ was ready for the carpenters when they came — I was after them much of the time as they finished the new bath-room — at noon I went to P.O. \+ got mail — I wrote to Dr. Gerster — \+ sent an order to Wanamaker for some articles —
Dinner at'r 1 o'clk — Load of lumber came from Fletsher Deeds for board walk between house \+ annex — I had Moody cut down more trees about house \+ annex —
Tea at 6.30 — Ralph went to Hardenburg's \+ took Kath'e H — to Club Ho. He came in at 10.30 — I spent the evef as usual — on piazza till 9 — then in \+ read.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 11, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A muggy, threatening mf. — then some sunshine — strong, pleasant breeze — bet. 12 \+ o'clk heavy thunder showers — they continued for an hour or two — then steaming sunshine — Carpenters arrived late — to work at once putting up shelves in new linen \+ store closet — this they completed at about 3 o'clk. I had a busy time — directing them \+ then with Ralph, getting the worm eaten spruce slabs that I have had in the attic for several seasons, down to n. piazza where we worked upon them \+ some hours clearing away the bark — exposing the strangely sinuous worm borings under the bark, cleaning out their saw-dust \+ then adding some rich color tints before varnishing — Some of the slabs under such treatment proved to be very fine — I intend to use them in finishing chimney breast in main room — about 5 o'clk. I went to Club Ho. \+ got mail — R — took tea at the "Shanty" with Hardenburgs. I had tea alone — was on piazza till 9 — then in \+ read \+ wrote — R — came in at 10.45
