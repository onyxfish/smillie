## Left Page

### Mon. June 11, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day - growing to be very cool in the eve'g. Up at 7 - wrote a letter to MatraAint Adams - Bkfs at 8.30 - more writing + got to studio at 11 - Before 12 I went to Brooks Bro. - then to Scribners with Etching Club Cats. + Haden correspondence in 1883 concerning Preface for that year. To consult Mr. Hf Smith about the binding - he was not in - then to Alexanders ab't shoes - the one I wished to see, out to lunch then to Bayerdorfer's + paid for Jim's watch - then to studio - It was 1.30 when I had my lunch - after that I wrote a long answer to Mary's letter which I mailed to her later - then I went again to Scribners saw Mr. Smith + he advised me to go to Stikeman, Cor. of 6 "Ave. + 26" S., Art bindery I did so - saw S - arranged with him + left the books + letters - then again to Alexanders - then to Touraine + got in at about 6 - Jim came in soon after 7 + we had dinner - After that, as I felt strangely out of sorts I lay down on my lounge + slept till after 10 -

This m'f was surprised by getting a very affectionate letter from my old boy-friend Jim Shearman, now in Florence where Mary saw him + gave him my address,

## Right Page

### Tues. June 12, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day - Sunny + cool - wind from the east. M'f before bkfs'. wrote to Geo. Kelly - Ferd Beede, Earl Moody, + W. Howell concerning house keeping in the Ark next summer. It was 10.30 when I got to studio wrote a letter to Ernest Roth + worked at dampning, pressing + mounting etchings - Had my lunch at about 2 o'clk - wrote to Jane Hinsthelwood - Before 4 I went to Stikeman bindery about Etching Club Cats. Then down Town to Coward's Shoe Store - in Greenwich S'. near Warren - + bought a pair of shoes - then to Cullen's + got enlargement of tracing of Father's head - then to Wall S'. Subway Station + to Touraine - Jim + I had dinner after 7 - Eve'g. writing + preparing photos, that I intend to give to W. Veitor + D'. Tuttle.
