## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 20, 1906

**Weather:** A dull, unpleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, unpleasant day — not actually raining, but everything wet \+ slippery — an attempt at clearing in the afternoon. About 3 30 a.m. I was awake \+ heard Jim come in — he had made a night of it — I dread this disposition that has shown itself once or twice before \+ do not know how to meet it — I may easily make bad worse by injudicious objection or interference — It was long before I could again get to sleep — I woke him at 7.45 \+ he got up promptly \+ I then said a few words of warning to him as quietly \+ dispassionately as I could — he said that he had gone to the Yale Club with Gould \+ that there had been some sort of a dinner or function that had brought out a lot of the fellows, but he did not tell me where he went after the closing of the Club — I went to studio \+ [illegible] me m'f in doing up pkg'g of books that are to be given away in different directions — To Touraine at 2. \+ Prepared a [illegible] and [illegible] lunch — Between bad weather \+ a swimming head I did not go out again but spent the time writing \+ fussing about till 4.30 then I walked up to 59 S. \+ back — 2 miles — I came in feeling much out of sorts \+ with my head aching as well as swimming — Jim went off to dine with Boardman \+ then to the theatre an energetic undertaking after last nights expenditure of energy. I had a cup of tea \+ a roll after 8 o'clk. Chart came in to go with me to the Century to hear Curtis tell about the Indians of the plains — with photo. illustrations — but I was too much out of sorts — he was not inclined to go alone, so he spent the eve'g with me —

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 21, 1906

**Weather:** misty, muggy, mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

misty, muggy, mild — a dull unseasonable m'f — afternoon, warm, misty sunshine — At 2 o'clk. a.m. being awake, I heard Jim come in — I got up at 7.30 \+ had a good grooming \+ bath — bkf's at 9 — At 10 o'clk. there was a teleph. call from one of Jim's friends — he heard it \+ got up — made an appointment with some one — dressed at once \+ went off — I read \+ wrote till about 11. then I walked up 5. Ave. to 59 S. \+ over to Park Ave \+ examined the Central R.R. engineering work going on there — back to Touraine before 1 o'clk. Jim came in with Harold Howe but soon went off again — I had a very light lunch at 2 — After 3 o'clk. I went out \+ promenaded on the sunny sides of Park Ave. for about an hour — my head is clearer than it has been for a week \+ now I hope that again I have started on an up grade — on returning to my rooms read with much amusement Oliver Wendell Holmes address "Mechanism in Thought and Morals". Jim did not come in so, at 7 I had a simple dinner — Eve'g wrote part of a letter to Ernest Roth.
