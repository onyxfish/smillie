## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 19, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temps. up \+ down, from below normal up to 104°. Urine running clear. hot water bags at my Kidneys. Dr. Tuttle came three times. There were two periods during the day of high Temps. Sweating during the falling. Dr. Brown, surgeon, was called in consultation by D. T. \+ he advised removal to Presb\[yterian\] Hos. \+ operation. I assented. any thing for a chance. Pain in right ear \+ deafness.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 20, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temps. 99°-101°. Pain in face \+ ears troubling me greatly. Nurse irrigating them with warm water. glycerine applied to soften wax that D. T. says is lodged there. Throat \+ chest still irritable. I am fed on clam, lamb \+ barley broth \+ toast \+ egg. \+ dosed with whiskey, Strychnia, Codeine, Benzoates of Soda etc. The condition of Kidneys seems to be more favorable. Dr. T. will not consent to an operation of any kind if it can be avoided.
