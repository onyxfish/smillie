## Left Page

### Wed. June 27, 1906

Commencement Day
Alumni Dinner —
Ret. to N.Y.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day altho' warm — a fitting end to my New-Haven Trip. Bkfp. as usual \+ about 9.30 started for Woolsey Hall — got there with my red ticket before 10 — went up to my seat \+ found it at the top of the top gallery — not worth saving — kept my stub \+ went out to the green — got a lot of good snaps — when the procession started I returned to the Hall. My red stub gave me precedence of the white tickets that crowded the lobby — I pushed thro' — went to first gallery — more snaps at dignitaries at head of column from balcony — then in — as procession entered all reserved seats not occupied were forfeited to any claimant holding any kind of a ticket — the middle seat on front row was vacant — I took it — best seat in house. The ceremony was very impressive — when Sir Chenting Liang Chang, the Chinese Ambassador rec'd. his degree there was a rousing tune — when Mr. Francis Bacon of Stanford got his degree an undergraduate sprung up \+ called for a Yale Cheer \+ marked time for it — it was given with immense enthusiasm, instantly caught by audience — a stirring incident — The singing was very impressive as usual. Out at 12.30 — went to Corner \+ met Kattie \+ Molly by appointment — then to Dr. Wm. Nieoforly to lunch — met pleasant people \+ had a delightful time. After that I went to the Alumni Dinner where I was not invited \+ had no privileges — I entered at the close of the dinner, was not challenged — went to a table marked "Reserved for the Committee" found a vacant seat, it was almost directly in front of Pres. Hadley — I took it \+ was immensely interested \+ edified by 4 speeches by Mellen, Pres. New Haven R.R. Dr. Francis Bacon \+ the Chinese Minister. It was after 5 when I got out \+ up to the Elkins. I packed my suit-case \+ then had a nap — Rapp came — we had dinner \+ after 7 — carriage came \+ Mollie, R. \+ N Rode to the station — Mollie \+ I to 7.56 train, 20 m. late \+ got to the city a few minutes before 10 — I escorted her to Fred Orland's \+ then I went to my rooms at the Touraine.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 28, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably warm day — up at 7 — had a good bath \+ change \+ felt much better — Bkfp. about 9 — but it was nearly 10.30 when I got away from the house — went to Riker's — 6th Ave. \+ 23d St. — lotion \+ Perfume — then to Alexander's \+ left shoes to be mended — then by d. Road down to Rector St. — went to Cullen's — Wm. 5th — then to Charl's \+ read letter from K. P. P. — Venice, June 12th — then to studio — about 2 o'clk. when I had lunch in studio — I fooled about some time — not much accomplished — went to T. dock \+ Sondley. Ave. — about the many chairs (about 20) that he has there to repair \+ reupholster for me during the coming summer. — then to Touraine — Jim came in at 7.15 \+ we went to Manhattae \+ had dinner — back to our rooms — I called up Meta Gavit Adams on phone — daughter-in-law, Mrs. Phil. Adams answered, Meta out of town — I wrote diary \+ looked over books.
