## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 31, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temp. 97° at 4 o'clk. got up to 98° but soon fell again. I sat in my big chair for about four hours to-day \+ ate chicken \+ steak \+ potatoes. I have to struggle ago'. my nurse \+ my cook who both insist upon my eating more than I think is good for me. my hearing still troubles me very much \- the noise \+ dis comfort in my head is constant.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 1, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day. awoke after 6 after a rather restless night \- very little headache but great distress in head from noises, deafness \+ a feeling of being entirely closed up in the head. was up \+ taken into chair in So. Room \+ placed in sunshine \- D. T. came about 11 \- again I told him how anxious I am about my hearing \+ suggesting expert consultation. He said he w'd. 'phone to D. Simpson \+ ask him to call. I had dinner \+ \- then to bed \- Nurse went out \+ I went to sleep \- Before 6. again in chair \- then D. Simpson came. after an examination of my ears he tried to clear the eustrachian (?) tubes by blowing them out \- without result as it seemed to me \- but he told me not to be discouraged \- said he w'd. come again on Sat.
