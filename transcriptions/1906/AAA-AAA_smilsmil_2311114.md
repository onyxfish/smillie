## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 22, 1906

**Weather:** Misty, muggy & mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Misty, muggy & mild — a most disagreeable \+ unsanitary combination — from a large part of the country westward come tales of extraordinary heat \+ from beyond that are blizzards \+ temps. to 20°. below zero — In the afternoon there was misty sunshine \+ fog again at night. I continued my letter to Rothr. writing till about 11 — then to 2°. Nat'l. bank to Putnam's \+ Bontillée's — then to studio then to my rooms — Breakfasted my lunch then rested — I have hunted much to-day for the swivel or universal joint, belonging to Ralph, that goes between camera \+ head of Tripod but it has disappeared in a mysterious manner that perplexes me greatly — Jim \+ I dined in the [illegible] — last — he got to bed early to make up lost sleep \+ I went to Charl's \+ discussed the matter of investing the funds now lying in the Standard Trust Co. We settled upon two or three Industrial Stocks — Leather — Tobacco — etc —

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 23, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fog again \+ uncomfortable warmth — A sudden fall in temps. is predicted but it does not come — the afternoon was sunshine \+ somewhat dryer — About 10 I went to Charl's for a few minutes \+ then back to my rooms where I spent the m'f. I had lunch in the restaurant at 2 — then commenced a systematic search for the camera swivel joint — I cannot find it anywhere in the rooms — then to studio — Ther. there 85°. I searched there without success \+ now must give it up. Thorne fitted a key to my steamer trunk — two weeks from to-day is sailing day — I am getting ready to pack — Jim \+ I went to Park Ave. Hotel \+ had a very poor dinner — a heavy shower while we dined — To-day, Thro' Strong, Sturgis \+ Co. Charl bought for me Pref. Stock of Cent. Leather (150 shares) Am. Tobacco — (100 sh.) and Tobacco bonds. 4s (10 m) amounting to $35010 — He also made a proposition to Jim concerning his $20000 which Jim accepted. I spent the eve'g writing.
