## Left Page

### Sat. April 14, 1906

**Weather:** Not altogether unpleasant \- aprilish  **Temp:**

Not altogether unpleasant \- aprilish \- Two or three light sprinklings \- rather a langorous, depressing day. I got to studio at 10 o'clk \+ continued the biting that was not finished yesterday \- I got thro' with it during the m't. Ralph came at about 1 o'clk. \+ we had lunch together in the studio. Raw oysters, brown bread, beer \+ prunes. After that I worked a plate (Road side Chapel near Lugano) till 4.30 \- then R\- \+ I went out for a little walk but the weather was muggy \+ the air was full of dust \+ his eyes troubled him, so, we got to our rooms soon after 5. I rested for a while. At 7, Jim, Ralph \+ I had dinner \- At 8.45 I went to Century Theater Prof. Rufus B. Richardson lecture about "The Excavation of Corinth," with lantern slide illustrations \- The lecture was mildly interesting to me, \+ but I wondered if the results following the work \+ expenses of excavating were adequate \- are we getting any valuable enlightenment from this labor?

To my rooms before 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 15, 1906

**Weather:** Dull m't., later in the day clearing pleasantly  **Temp:**

Dull m't., later in the day clearing pleasantly. Up soon after 7 \+ spent a long time grooming myself. It was 10 o'clk. before I had slept \- then with newspapers \+ other reading till noon \- then Jim \+ Ralph got up \- As the weather was clearing Jim went off with Boardman for a stroll of some kind \- I went to studio for a few minutes \- on my return, after 1, Ralph having finished his lunch, we went off together for a prowl \- we went, by subway, to Kings bridge, the present end of the Broadway subway, crossed the bridge \+ went to high ground on opposite side where we loitered for a time \- then ret. to Dyckman Station \+ climbed the hill to the Fort George conglomerate of shows, drinking \+ dancing places \+ all sorts of shops, booths \+ amusements \- there was a motley crowd, common place in character \- after making the rounds we returned to Dyckman Station \+ hence back to our rooms at 5.30 \- I had a rest for an hour \- then R\- \+ I \- Jim not appearing \- went to the Murray Hill Hotel \+ had dinner \- We got back to our rooms after 8 \+ &k. \+ amused ourselves \- I read aloud to R\- some historical bits about our Civil war, about which he had been questioning me.
