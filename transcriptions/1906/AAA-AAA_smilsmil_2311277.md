## Left Page

### Friday Dec. 14, 1906

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day — mild & quiet — Bkfst. at 8.30 — to studio after 10. \+ but in a good mt. work scrubbing Cliffs of Normandy with dafolio & a nail brush I think I was very successful in removing the yellow skin that had formed over it. Now it comes out quite bright & as it was painted. I worked till after 1.30 — then home & lunch at 2 — then a Rest — wrote to Ralph — Dinner at 6.30 — Jim with me — he went out later — I resumed my work among the books trying to arrange them on the shelves in orderly relation to one another — worked till 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 13, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant mt. growing milder — overcast afternoon — Bkfst. before 9 — after 10 when I went to studio — Took "Cliffs of Normandy" out of frame & commenced cleaning it preparatory to sending it to the Corcoran Gall? expt?. at Washington. I was surprised at the extent of discoloration on parts of sky & sea, owing to thin, oily painting. I scoured it off with alcohol — I'm afraid that I have got myself into a mess of work — It was nearly 2 o'ck. when I got home — lunch — Rawly sent home hall mirror at last, & it was hung. I did some work on arranging books before dinner — Jim went to drill in the evg., & I continued the work till 11 o'ck — then to bed.
