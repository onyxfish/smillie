## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 5, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An unpleasant day - saw a overcast in the n.f. - a wet snow flurry bet. 1 + 3 o'clk growing much colder at night. I got up early + did some work before bkfs. at 8 with Jim - then I wrote three or four notes - then to Studio + to bank. I went to Scribners to see W. H. + L. Smith - not in - then back to my rooms at 12 - I had an egg for lunch + at 1 o'clk, met D. Tuttle at 2 3°. S. - ferry - we crossed to Hoboken + went on board of Prinz essen Vic. loise - saw our state room + steward + the obersteward - bath steward etc. - went over the ships + then back to the city at 2.30 - I got to Louvaine about 3 - found that I had no Bathrobe - went to Rogers, Peet + Co. + bought me. Dinner with Jim at 7 - After 8 Charl came in - I got my packing done by 10 o'clk. Charl left at 10.30 + I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 6, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 4°
In Stateroom 540

Onboard S.S. Prinzessen Victoria luise - Room B.

Superb day, but bitterly cold. I got up before 7 o'clk + picked up the last odds + ends - bkfs. with Jim at 8. Bea came to bid me good bye - Charl bid me good bye on the phone - Coupe came for me at 9 + at 9.45 I got to the steamer. Being early I got trunk + other things into my state room at once - It was half an hour later before D. Tuttle came - he was along time getting his luggage into the room - great crowd + confusion - started at 11.° magnificent views of the River, wharves, craft + city - in spite of the cold I was able to keep about the deck - Pilot put off at 1.5 Sent cards to Jim, Ralph + Charl. Then lunch afternoon, wandering about, out + in - had a sort of a nap - at 7 o'clk D. T. + I had our dinner, occupying the seats we secured yesterday - a good dinner - band very noisy - most of the evef. D. T. \+ I spent in our room chatting + turned in at 10.

I had very little sleep during the night - the boat rolled rigorously - I was not Restless but could not sleep - the Ther. in the room else to 75° during the night.
