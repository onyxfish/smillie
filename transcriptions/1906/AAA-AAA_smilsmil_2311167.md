## Left Page

### Tues. May 8, 1906

**Weather:** Clear, breezy & rather cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, breezy & rather cool - up at 7.30
b'fst - after 8 & after 9 went to Dr. Tuttle's with
specimens of urine - was detained some
time by visitor ahead of me. It was about
11 when I got to studio - a little work on
"Study of an old Man's head" - Then hunting for
proofs & plates to go on with finishing of
other old work. Prepared my lunch it
about 2 after I had been to a Realty Co. at
73. 42d St. to get information about an ap-
artment bld now being finished - 72d St. and
Boulevard - Everything promising except scale
of prices - $3000 and upwards - after lunch some
more fussing about, then I went to apartment
building - S.W. cor. 34th St. & Lexington Ave - The rent
($1700) the most desirable thing about it - Hibson
Real Estate agent, met me, told me of a house
in 31st St. - I went there - depressing - $1300 - then
to Wills, the Roofer, in 27th St. to hurry up work
on Studio Roof - went down to 21st St. - looking
for Tonnele, photos, then to Bayardofer - got pen-
dulum for hall clock - Then to Cousins - at 9
Jim & I had dinner - later he went out and I
loafed. Dr. Tuttle phoned me that he
found nothing unusual, that is, differing materially
from previous analyses, in the urine specimens
left with him this m'g.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 9, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

N.A. of D. Annual Meeting & dinner

Steady rain all the m'g. & cold - Steam on
so, I was able to keep warm. I spent the m'g.
writing & arranging photos - & at 1 o'clk - just as
the rain was holding up I went to the N.A. of D. -
Am. Ave. & 109th St. This being the first meeting since
amalgamation of Societies (N.A. of D. & Soc'y of Am. Artists)
I was anxious to be present - I had a also a strong
lunch - meeting at 2 o'clk, everything ran very
smoothly - all the old officers were re-elected & then
it was announced that as agreed the Council had
elected 35 N.As & 30 painters & 5 sculptors. The
business was over before 5 o'clk. - During the meeting
I had the unusual experience of being obliged
to go out to urinate - there is excitement in that
water works dept. - that gives me much con-
cern, & later, I had to leave the dinner table
twice - after the meeting there was a long wait &
the N.As generally took drinks about till dinner
time - Joe & I met at Columbia & continued our
walk around by Morningside - at last dinner time
came - Watrous had given a few of the old fellows
seats to the right & left of the Pres. The guests, two
lawyers, sat on either side of him - then in his right
sat me, & Weir, Sam. Colman, Self & J.B. Bristol -
The new members from Soc'y of Am. Artists & Associates
were present making an unusually large number
of diners - the dinner was a good one - the speaking
that followed came in mild doses - Samuel
Isham made an admirable little address - quite
a model in its way - witty & amusing with an
air of distinction & polish - What Kenyon Cox
said was good & interesting - Van Boskerck was
very funny - Chas. Miller did not get off his load
but R. W. Sewell, who takes himself very seriously
read his written good advice & [illegible] -
Joe & I left at 10.30 - Prof. Niemeyer of New Haven
rode down with us.
