## Left Page

### Sat. July 21, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy showers commencing before 8 am \+ continuing about two hours — then some misty, muggy sunshine \+ about 5 o'clk again a heavy shower with thunder — I had a long sitting in the m'f to finish my letter to Mary. \+ it was not till 12.30 that I went to P.O. — then home to dinner — I did odd chores about the house, mending locks \+ the like \+ had a job in fixing up the lawn mower, a casting being broken, so that it may be used — when I took it out to the grass for trial I found that the grass, mowed a week ago, was still too high for the little lawn mower — it rolled over it — did not cut it — my physical condition to-day is very discouraging — head ache \+ dizziness so that I reel as I walk — hyper-acidity of stomach to a great degree — a pain under triangular space below sternum that I don't understand — feels neuralgic — \+ catarrhe bowels. Eve Y Ralph \+ I had reading aloud —

## Right Page

### Sun. July 22, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot, muggy, thunderous sort of day — Bkf' after — the m'f was spent on North piazza where I read aloud to R — a sermonlike \+ rather gloomy story "Dileas", by Norman Maclean. Then we went to Reserve \+ walked in on the road as far as the old swimming pool, the dam is now washed away — then back by trail — went to P.O. \+ got mail — then home — Dinner at 2 — I had a nap — feeling much better than yesterday — At a little after 5. R. \+ I went to the Hardenburg's \+, by detatchments, saw all but W. Maghee — Home at 6.30 \+ had supper — a good part of the eve' on North piazza — Then in \+ read a mild story by Maclean from the "Dileas" series — "Dwellers in the Mist" \+ a Wolfiskey wild story by Jack London — an unsatisfactory sandwich —
