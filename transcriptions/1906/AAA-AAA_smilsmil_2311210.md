## Left Page

### Tues. July 31, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day - brilliantly clear - fine air - luminous moonlight at night. I spent a good part of the m't clearing up the dark room \+ preparing for work there. At noon I went to Club house for mael - It was late \+ I sat about chatting with people till it came - It brought a letter from Charl in which he complains that Jim has not called on him since I left town \+ says that some time he may want a favor \+ he may not get it - a pretty sab[illegible] of temper that hurt me sorely. He is tired out \+ nervous I suppose. I got a note from Hillen of Plattsburg offering $75 for Susie's piano - an excellent offer, but for sentimental reasons I hesitate to accept it. Home to dinner - nothing accomplished in the afternoon beyond some bottle washing \+ some digging as I bossed Harry Bradley in some outside work - Tea at 6.30 - Suey I was in my blankets on piazza till 9 o'clk. Then I went over to the Maghee camp fire Ralph went at 8 - Mr. Howell \+ sister there. Home before 11 -

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 1, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day. Very soon after 6&[illegible] R. \+ I went into the dark Room \+ he officiated at the developing tray, my eyes are no longer equal to such work. He dev. six exposures, all made at the middle pool, Roaring Brook fall. The negatives all appear to be good \+ will help me, so far as rock form, texture \+ fracture are concerned, when I come to repainting portions of middle distance on "River's Source". At 12 I went to P. O. Then back to dinner - During the afternoon some dark room work was done - then R. \+ I were engaged in repairing a Rattan easy chair, weaving new rattan into it to take the place of broken strands. After \+ we loafed about over windy brow \+ down into the woods - met two of the Nielson girls. Supper at 6.30 - Then on N. piazza till after 9 - lovely eve'g. Then in \+ did some experimental work in photo. printing but failed.
