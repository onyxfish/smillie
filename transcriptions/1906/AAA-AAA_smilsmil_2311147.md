## Left Page

### Thur. March 29, 1906

**Weather:** Wild — overcast most of the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

at about 8.30 — After that wrote a note of thanks to Mr. Thos. F. Veitor for the photos he sent me — also a note to Weitenkampf. It was about 11 when I got to studio. Cleaned up the mounted pls. \+ made a written list \+ selected the ones that I thought best to show in the coming exbn. I had my lunch in studio — raw oysters etc. Afternoon I mounted the panorama photos \+ sent neby [illegible] — \+ did a lot of fussing about other things — \+ then, very tired, to my rooms at 5.30 — Jim dined with Katharine Hardenburg — I dined alone — Eve\+ — had trouble with my bowels — much pain \+ several movements — no cause for it that I know of.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 30, 1906

**Weather:** Beastly weather again — a foggy mizzle, cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

raw all day — my bowel trouble continued \+ caused me much discomfort — I got to studio at 10 — a young man from Weitenkampf. came \+ to him I gave pls. of the etching pls. that I have been fussing with more or less constantly for some days past. Then I spent some time writing a list of the points \+ a letter of explanation that I mailed to W — . I had my lunch at studio \+ during the afternoon did some cleaning up — This work, at the rate it has progressed during the past winter, will never be done — It was after 5 when I went to my rooms — I rested awhile hoping to be able to go to Nicoll's — W.C. came in the eve\+ but the bowel trouble increased \+ I did not go out, but called him up on the phone — I wrote a letter to Ralph.
