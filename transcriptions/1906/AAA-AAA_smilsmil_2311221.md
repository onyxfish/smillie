## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 22, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still the same weather. sunny, muggy m't. Then big thunder clouds — first shower, a mild one. bet. 12 & 1 o'clk. — between 3 & 6 there were very heavy & continuous rain with some thunder — I was again interested in my photo-enlargements & most of the m'f was spent at that work with some success. The Bromide paper that I have is part of an old lot of Charl's & bears date, Ap't, 1886. I think that now most of the difficulties have been solved & that with good paper I could get good work Trumbull came to consult with me about the roof over ice house & butler's pantry — it is in a very bad state — he advised some sort of Rubbering so strongly that I told him to go ahead with it. later, M'. Maghee came — said he had experience with the stuff. It is good enough he said but is such poor looking roof covering that I w'd. never be satisfied to have it constantly under my eyes as it w'd. be on that roof — As I sent a note to Trumbull by Adams countermanding the order — Adams worked p art of the day staining doors & floors. At 12 p I started for P.O. but was turned back by a shower. Dinner at 7 — more photo work — developing films, for which I have to trust to Ralph as I can not see well enough to do the work. Tea at 6.30 more heavy rain — Ev'g. more reading — I was very tired & went to sleep while Ralph read.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 23, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The spell of weather culminates — Early m'f. hot, with sunshine. Then clouds & a shower at noon — in the afternoon, from 3 to 7 a heavy storm going around & & blowing hard from the N.W. Rapid fall in temp. — wind howled & floods of water fell & an uncomfortably large portion of it came in thro' openings in large north window, studio — I continued experiments in camera enlarging — at 12.30 started for Club Ho. but was turned back by rain — Dinner at 1 — went to P.O. before 3 — met M'r. Marquand. The asked me about my terms for renting Ark & said he w't. like an early date named when he c'm's. M — night call — Pgo over it — I answered his questions & talked to — m'rro 5 m. for the rest. not home as the rain came on. I did some clearing up in anticipation to to-morrow's visit. The afternoon storm was very severe — the rain beat so heavily upon the big studio window that a good deal of water was driven in & for some time I was engaged taking care of it. Tea at 6.30 — Ev'g. Reading aloud — as usual — great fall in Temp.

Adams did not come to-day.
