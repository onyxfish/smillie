## Left Page

### Thur. March 1, 1906

**Weather:** At sea — started on home — stretch.  **Temp:** about 70° all day

Cloudy on f. — clearer in the middle of the day. clouded again with threat of showers — fine moonlight night — The usual sea routine. Had horseback ride in gym. \+ other exercises — while the Ther. on deck showed 70° the breeze was so fresh as to make it feel much cooler — more awnings were removed \+ the ship begins to have a striped look the Ther. in cabin fell to 74°. Some light overcoats \+ wraps made their appearance — At dinner the waiters all wore their dark blue uniforms. Altho' we had a very smooth sea we did not get to Nassau till about 5 p.m. We anchored a mile or more from shore — a heavy surf was running \+ when the tender came out to transfer the 41 passengers who left the ship here, with their baggage, there was a bobbing time — The appearance of the place was uninviting to me — a great barn of a hotel a lot of small buildings huddled about \+ a cow barren looking country — the clouds \+ twilight helped to make it unattractive — It was after 6 when the tender started for shore — as our passengers going were to come on later some young men went to shop with the others \+ all at 7 \+ immediately we started on our home run — all easy sea — a fresh breeze \+ a young moon — it is hoped that we shall get to our [illegible] sometime late Sunday p.m. — so that we may sleep in our own beds that night. When I went to bed I found that our steward had got out the heavy Arctic blanket \+ had tied it up in its sheet case so that I must sleep under it entire as though it were mid-winter or else tear it to pieces — I got the double blanket out of the case but it was so fastened at the corners that I could not spread it out smoothly over me — as the Ther. stood at 72° in the Room I was unable to fix my bed covering so as to be comfortable.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 2, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 68° to 70°

Superb day — bright sunny \+ breezy with an agreeable amt. of motion — The Vic. Takes water easily \+ I got quite a ducking while on the boat deck at the foot of the forward smokestack from a sea that jumped aboard. Later in the day the sea abated very much — The moonlight at night was very fine. \+ altho' the Ther. on deck marked 68°. The breeze was so fresh that I had to wear my light overcoat. The day was spent loafing about \+ sleeping. I had a nap in the m.f. \+ another before dinner. I had a horseback ride \+ other work in the gyms. in the m.f.
