## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 30, 1906

**Weather:** Dark \+ dismal \- Raining for a good part of the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark \+ dismal \- Raining for a good part of the day \- after an unrestful night I got up about 8 o'clk. \+ had a bath \- unusual amount of pus in urine \- Bkfst. at 9 \- Ralph got up at 10 \+ showed such mumpy symptoms that I phoned for Dr. T. who came at noon \+ said that now the parotid glands were affected \+ R. had the Mumps. While the Dr. was here young Edw. Cook called me on phone \+ startled me by saying that Edw. M. Cook died last night from heart failure \- Dr. T. said that Edw. has been failing very rapidly for some weeks past \+ he had warned Dr. Cook that the end was likely to come suddenly \- later in the afternoon I called Edw. up \+ asked him to tell Mary that I was not well enough to go out but hoped to call to-morrow \- Dinner before 2 \- Clark came in the afternoon \+ left at 6 \- Tea at 6.30 \- Jim not at home. Ralph \+ I experimented with the 50 candle electric light that he got the other day to use in an enlarging box \- we cut an old paste board box into shape to answer the purpose \+ got a bright, sharp image that seemed to be ample in power for photographic purposes.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 31, 1906

**Weather:** Rain all day \- dismal \+ dark  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain all day \- dismal \+ dark \- Bkfst. before 9 \- Ralph got up later \- neither of us went out of the house all day \- I got a very nice letter from? Schmolck. Ham. Am. Line asking if I w'd not prefer to go to Jamaica on the Kronprinzessin Cecelia \- a much larger vessel than Prinz Joachim \+ just last on the line \- I phoned Dr. Tuttle \+ we agreed that we w'd rather go two days earlier on the smaller boat. Then I wrote to Mr Schmolck \- R. \+ I had lunch at 1.30 after I had written a letter to Mary Cook. After noon I wrote a letter to Dr. Alfred J. Brown at Rome. I did a lot of reading also \- Dinner at 6.30 Evef. Jim went to theatre on a ticket that R. had got for himself \- R's mumps don't seem to trouble him much \- the swelling is confined to the right side \+ is not large.
