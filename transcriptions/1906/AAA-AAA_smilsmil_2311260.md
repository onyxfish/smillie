## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 8, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another of the long suite of remarkably fine days. I awoke before 7 after a night of some discomfort in my right ear, at times amounting to a real ache \- have no theory as to what caused it \- Felt little of the depression that was so distressing to me yesterday, but otherwise it was hard to find any improvement. Bkfst. about 8 \+ got thro' with routine soon after 10. The m\[orn\]g spent at open window. no callers \- Dinner after 1 \+ after 2 went out with Miss Drabbles \+ walked three times up \+ down bet. 81st \+ 82d. St. Then in \- Miss D. went off for an outing \+ I went to bed \+ had a troubled nap \- Got up unrefreshed after it \- \+ began again the weariness of living \- how long is this going to last \- how long can it last? This question is insistent \- Temp. continues very even bet. 97° \+ 98°. Digestion restive but circumstances considered, something to be thankful for that it is as near good as it is. my head has been rather clearer to-day than yesterday \-

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 9, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The ninth successive day of pleasant weather awake at usual time \- Hfp's news paper, battle dressing \+ out at 10.30 \- sat for some time at open window. wrote to Scott Brown \+ Jessie Smillie \- About 11.45 Will Parke surprised me by a visit \- at about 1 Miss D. \+ I went out \+ walked for 15 m. on the sunny side of the Ave. Then in \+ had my dinner \- from 2.30 to 4.30 had some uneasy napping \- then up \- wrote to Ralph \+ later to Manhtn. Storage Co \- about injury to large mirror \- Supper at 6.30 \- Sve\[??\] Jim was in \- no doctor \- my hearing to-day may have been a trifle better \- it varies greatly \- has been better as well as worse \- my legs are very weak \+ my head uncomfortably light as well as hollow \- a distressing feeling \- Temp. still continues below 98°. It is all that I can do to keep up my courage \- I do not know what I shall do when I am left without a nurse.
