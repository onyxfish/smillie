## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 28, 1906

Water pipe laid to Tennis Court.

Very fine day \- Ferd Beede delivered the 3/4" water pipe yesterday so we tackled the laying of it To-day. I went over to the Hemlock \+ borrowed of W. M. a pipe-wrench \- then back \+ on laying out the pipe found that we must have a pipe cutter \+ thread die so again to M's, found both in his absence \+ borrowed them — then the work began \- to get at cut off under Highland Cottage I went to W. G. Neilson's for key \- was told it was at D. DuBois' \- went there \- met Miss Howell \+ we had a pleasant visit with D. \+ Mrs. DuBois \- got key \- water was cut off \- pipe uncovered \- union opened \- pipe cut off \- thread cut \- I inserted \- union closed \+ pipe laid to Tennis court \- then the ditch digging began \+ Moody worked well \- At 12.30 went to P.O. met W.G.N \- there \- he was effusive \- Told not to come to him requesting the privilege of taking small evergreens for transplanting but to go get what I wanted. Dinner at 1 \- the afternoon was spent in digging trench for pipe about 150' long \+ 1½ deep, getting the pipe into it \+ the turf replaced. Moody \+ Harry worked with us well. About 3 o'clk. I went over to Club Ho. \+ saw Nye \+ Mr. Otis about manure \- a very difficult thing to find here \- I had a short rest before tea at 6.30 \- Evg. \- glorious moonlight. I spent a good part of the time on the piazza wrapped in my blankets \- R. went to the Club house seeking company.

Adams did not work.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 29, 1906

Still very fine weather. I set Moody to work on long gore on east side of road up to front piazza where I had some small evergreens set out as shrubs \- I am going to reset it, so I had him dig out the sandy earth \+ replace it with some from the old garden \- then I went to the Hardenbergs \+ with Mr. Vauttons went down the hill trail as far as the "nursery" where I saw the lot of spruces and cedars from which to select for transplanting \- then to Ferd Beede's \- about coal, manure etc \- no one at home but one of the daughters \- Then home \- To P.O. about 12.30 meeting Mr. [illegible] Hawes \+ daughter on the way. Back to dinner at 1 \- Afternoon, with Moody \+ Harry went to "nursery" \+ got a barrow load of small spruces. Hard work getting the trees up the hill to the house \- set them in place \+ had a rest before tea time \- Evg. \- about 8 o'clk R. \+ I went to Club Ho. \- I visited for a time, in the parlor, with Mr. \+ Mrs. Jno. Hawes \+ some of their friends. Mr. \+ Mrs. H. \+ daughter, were at the house in the afternoon \+ spent some time on piazzas \+ going through the house \- I went to Club affecting to see Mr. Marquand but learned that he had left for N.J. in the m'g.

Adams \+ boy put in some time to-day.
