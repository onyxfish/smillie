## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 1, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — bright \+ pleasantly cool. Up early \+ Jim \+ I had bkfs. at about 7.30 very simply served as we had no dishes for cooking in or eating from — I hurried down to The Touraine to meet the movers at 8.30 as agreed — they came at 9.30 — At 9 I went to Manh. Storage \+ made complaint at 9.30 the work of transport began again \+ I had a trying time — back to Wellesley at about 12 — at 12.30 I had my lunch while the men were bringing in the things. By appointment I went to Touraine again to meet them at 2 — they got ahead of me \+ were at work when I got there — Got everything away \+ said my good byes at 3 o'clk — \+ ret. to W — Man from Wanamaker came \+ brought kitchen things — men got in last of furniture etc before 5 — Man from Storage came \+ unpacked bbls. of china \+ glass — We had a good cooked dinner before 7 — Well at work unpacking —

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 2, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant weather — bkfs — at 7.30 — at work unpacking four cases of books till after 11 o'clk. About noon I went to studio — there for a few minutes — then back \+ had lunch at 1.30 — after that a rest — then to Wanamakers — there "shopping" for all sorts of things for Kitchen \+ laundry Till 5.30 then home quite tired out, found that Kate had done a lot of shopping for me at a 10 ct. store — we had dinner before 7 — I had cleaned the gas grate in parlor \+ found that it burned very satisfactorily — so I sat and warmed my feet there \+ read my newspaper — Gil Kinny called on Jim \+ practically nothing was done in the evening —
