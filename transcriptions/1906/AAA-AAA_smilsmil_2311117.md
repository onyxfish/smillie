## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 28, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some rain last night — a clouded \+ sunless day — My Citrate of Mag. roused me at about 4 o'clk. \+ again at 7 — I got up \+ had bkfp. — at 8.30 — Bowels sore \+ uncomfortable all day — at 12.30. went to Charl's for a few minutes — then to D. Tuttle's 48 W. 49t S. — he was not in — back to Rooms — at 2 had a talk over phone with D. Gerster to whom I offered my printing press at St. Huberts as a gift which he accepted with thanks — I had a junkit for my lunch at 3 o'clk — \+ then rested for the sake of my uneasy bowels — Did not go out again — a light supper at 7. Eve'g. Reading — packing things away \+ looking over photo postal cards — Eurafric 1904.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 29, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine, clear cold day — I went to the Am. Art Ass'n. Galleries in the morning to see the Gifford paintings on exbt'n there to be sold in a few days — In the same exbt'n there was a larger number by a man who died two or three years ago — Truesdell, I think is the name — G's pictures were a disappointment to me — the collection did not show him at his best — I met William Sartain \+ we had a long talk — he was interesting \+ amusing as usual — Then to bank \+ arranged affairs — Stopped at Wunderlich's to look at some Whistler etchings \+ got to my rooms after 1 o'clk — Still feeling out of \+ ons in the matter of digestion I beat up a raw egg — then rested \+ read for a while — then out on a small shopping tour, got paper at Buskircks \+ Safety pins (for protection during sea voyage) at Macy's — met there Meta Gaot (Mrs. Chas. F. Adams) \+ had a long old fashioned talk with her — then went to several men's furnishing stores, unsuccessful search, for a light cap to wear at sea in the tropics — Then back to Touraine Dinner with Jim — at 8.30 went to Charl's \+ got my leather \+ Tobacco accessories — Then at [illegible] to Edw. Cook's \+ spent the eve'g till 10.30 with him \+ Mary.
