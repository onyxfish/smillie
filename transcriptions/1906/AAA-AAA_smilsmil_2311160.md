## Left Page

### Tues. April 24, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very stormy day - wind & rain beat upon my windows, front & rear, in a most vicious manner. It gave me a small degree of selfish comfort as I lay in my bed & listened to it - The doctor came & advised Frommer's Malt Ext. with Yerba Santa combs. as an expectorant - my throat is very sore & the pain in my head, back of eyes & nose, is very distressing - Later in the day I was able to sit propped up in my bed & read newspaper - Evelf Jime went to call on his cousins, the Throcks, & bid them good bye. It has been a hard, lonesome day for me.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 25, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day with some bright sunshine & strong wind - I had my Hlfs' in my room at about 10 - I feel so wretched when I got up that I thought he should see me again & so I called him - he came & joined me & made a long visit looking over my West Indian photos. He advised me to go out but the wind blew so strong, there was so much dirt in the street & my eyes & nasal membranes were so sensitive that I thought it best to stay indoors. I opened my windows & got some sunshine & air in that way. I went down with Jim to my dinner at 7 - Evelf after 8.30 Charl came with Mary, Kath[illegible] & Elizabeth & Gordon & stayed till 10 o clk. Then Mary & her girls bid me good bye, as they sail for Genoa tomorrow, & I got to bed tired out.
