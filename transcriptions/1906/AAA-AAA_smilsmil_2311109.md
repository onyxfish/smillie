## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 12, 1906

**Weather:** Overcast, with some weak efforts at sunshine  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, with some weak efforts at sunshine — I got to studio after 10 \+ to work unpacking a barrel that was packed before I left town last summer, \+ that proved to be too large to go thro' my cellar door. I made an inventory of its contents \+ commenced repacking them into a box. About 1 I went to Touraine \+ prepared a lunch for myself — I did not go to studio again till rather late in the afternoon \+ stayed there for a short time only — At 7 Jim \+ I went to Murray Hill Hotel \+ got dinner. There, on Charl's invitation we went to his house \+ he entertained us for nearly two hours by showing a lot of Norway fiord slides, by lantern — He has a remarkable lot of slides, surprisingly fine negatives, especially those taken in the far north \+ within the Arctic Circle — It was after midnight when we got to our beds.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 13, 1906

**Weather:** A dull, disagreeable day. cold \+ raw —  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, disagreeable day. cold \+ raw — rain at night. I went to D. Brown's at 10 o'clk \+ gave him the two University glee club tickets given us by Charl last night. There to studio \+ packed to a finish the box that I worked on yesterday \+ repacked Jim's box \+ put a lot of my things in it but did not quite finish the work. To Touraine at 12.30 — made a bowl of junket \+ soon after 2 went to W. 23°. St. ferry \+ took 3 o'clk boat to Hoboken — There I met Charl \+ Capt. Broun [illegible] \+ together we went to the Hamburg Am. docks \+ on board the Prinzessen Vic. Luise where we spent a pleasant hour with the capt. who was very jolly — entertained us in his cabin \+ took us over the boat — Stateroom 88 that Fis. Little \+ I expect to occupy on our trip in Feby. is a very pleasant room — but I wish it were not so near the water — I fear that we will not have the port hole open as much as will be necessary for ventilation — We left the boat at 4.30 \+ Charl \+ I went up town together. I got to Touraine at 5 — Jim dined out \+ I had dinner \+ also \+ at 8.30 went to Century — Annual meeting — Secy. Cary's necrological report for the year was interesting as usual — his sketch of R. S. Swain Gifford was sympathetic \+ just, but the extended sketch of ex. Secy. McHay was admirable \+ won immediate applause — I left early \+ got back to my rooms soon after 11 o'clk —
