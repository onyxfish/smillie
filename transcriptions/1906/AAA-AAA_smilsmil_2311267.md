## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 22, 1906

**Weather:** Rain in the very early m't — then fog — then clearing to a brilliant breezy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the very early m't — then fog — then clearing to a brilliant breezy day — I passed a very restless night \+ an unusual amount of urine — I had bkfs' in bed \+ when I got up felt like a Rag — but Temp. Touched normal for the first time in two weeks. When the sky cleared \+ the sun shone brightly I went out \+ for nearly an hour walked in Riverside Pk. or on the Drive or at on some sheltered bench. Dinner at 1 — Soon after 2 I walked up 81st street to Columbus Ave. \+ by 6th Ave. Elevated went to Alexander's shoe store — got shoes \+ Rest' as I came, getting in after 3 — Then I had a Rest on my bed — Supper at 6.30. Jim dined with me \+ went to drill af[ter]w[ards]. I wrote to Ralph — Ev'g Mau. Storage man came to say that Miller's men took rack off from Hall mirror when they hung it \+ they are responsible for injury to it — It was taken away to be re silvered this m't. At the same time telephone man came \+ he put a supplemental bell from phone to the kitchen.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 23, 1906

*father's birthday*

**Weather:** Cold \+ windy, with some clouds  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold \+ windy, with some clouds — Bkfs' in bed, as usual, \+ after it, got up so as to call Wm S. Miller up by phone to get some information concerning the hanging of Hall mirror — got Fred, the foreman, who promised to call me at 12 — I went out after 10 \+ walked awhile in Riverside Park — but the wind was too strong \+ cold to be pleasant \+ after half an hour of it I came in — At 12 Di Tuttle came — I explained to him my plan for trip to Jamaica, Carthaginia \+ Port Simon in Feb'y \+ March — he was interested — advised me to go ahead \+ get more details direct from the Ham. Am. office — Dinner at 1 — At 2.30 I rode down to Rawly's — the Glazier. B'dway \+\+4y's. Miller called me up at noon \+ told me that his men had not taken a back board from mirror — that being so the board must have been taken off when it was hung in 102 2.38? years ago — the back of mirror was not properly protected \+ had no [illegible] for silvering \+ so I told Rawley that I w'd pay bill — then I rode up to Presb't \+ Hosp'tal — Jessie, not in, but I had pleasant visit with Bella, Mary \+ Florence — twas'y — From Hospital I walked across the Park to B'dway \+ 72d S — a long walk for me — was surprised to find how little it fatigued me — car to B'd S — Men. at 5, to bed for an hour — Jim dined out — I ate alone — Ev'g — wrote a [illegible] of notes — on to Chas. Collins who called at 4 while I was out — \+ read —
