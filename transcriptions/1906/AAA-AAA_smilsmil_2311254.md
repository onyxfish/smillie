## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 27, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temp. 98°-99°. condition of ears + hearing continues about the same. My eyes have been so extremely sensitive to light since the beginning of this sickness that I have hardly had them open at all but to-day I sat propped up in my bed + for a little while read a newspaper. Still taking codein + Acetanilid. Had Egg-toast, Squab, rice, peas + grapes. Feeling somewhat hopeful but still worrying about my deafness.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 28, 1906

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temp. normal all day - I was up to-day in big chair for a short time but soon tired - Dr. Gerster + bro. Chare visited me - hearing somewhat better - No more irrigating fears of or evidence of discharge in left one.
