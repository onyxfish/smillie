## Left Page

### Sun. May 20, 1906

**Weather:** Brilliantly beautiful clear, cool day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I was out of sorts in the early mng. & did not get up till about 8 o'clk. hkp's at q. — then read news papers — Jim got up a little later & after 11 we started out together & went by subway to Kings Bridge — thence by next train to 137th S't. & there went to some apartment houses in 136th S'. — then over the viaduct to Grants Tomb & down Riverside Drive to 96th S'. — thence by subway to 42nd. — When I left Jim & went to studio & had a light lunch then to Duvaine & wrote a long letter to Mary. Jim came in at about 7 & we had dinner. Every I did more writing.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 21, 1906

**Weather:** Still bright & clear & much cooler.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still bright & clear & much cooler — I got up earlier than usual & got to studio before 10 o'clk. after having gone to Miller's to get a man to put up my little sign on studio sidewalk forbidding the putting of refuse of any kind there. — I got to work on the Quebec portrait of father — laid ground & put in new work, raising the shoulders and broadening the figure — I consider this departure from the original justified, as in the particulars named it is almost a caricature — I then bit in the new work — about noon the colored girl who cleans Maynard's room every Monday came in & was about an hour cleaning up my closet where the water is — Then the man came & put up the sign — It was late when I was able to get my lunch then I finished biting & cleaned my plate. about 4 o'clk I thought I would go uptown & look at some new houses in 80th S't. I found train inco way satisfactory & then I wandered down to the Welesley — cor 81st S't. & West End ave — the house where I could such pleasant rooms a fortnight ago, but they were too expensive $2700 — I went in, saw the same man & asked if he had cheaper rooms — he show-ed me a suite — parlor facing south, the only sunny room — for $1050 — with a room up stairs for extra maid, $60 more. Then he showed another, opening on street & court, same rent, but much more desirable — the occupant can't make up his mind to leave — the occupant? The first is going to leave Oct. 1 but wishes to sublet till then — the manager is, I think, using me — I want the suite opening on the court — that will force that tenant to make up his mind — if I can't get that one I'll take the other for fear the tenant sublets it & go get a tenant in ahead of me — Still — either of them is better than anything I have seen so far — I got to Duvaine at 6 — Jim remained at his place & I had dinner alone. — After 8 Charl called me on phone saying he had just Rec'd interesting letter from Mary & asking me to go to his house — I did so & was there till 10.30
