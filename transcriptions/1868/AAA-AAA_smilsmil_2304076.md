## Left Page

### February Sunday, 9 1868

Snow until 9 a.m. then steady
Rain for the rest of the day —
Mng went to Dr. Hastings and
was much pleased with his
discourse from the text "For
no one liveth to himself alone".
He enlarged upon the influence
that every one inevitably exerted
upon those about him, & consequent-
ly, our Responsibilities. Dinner
as usual, + being a dark + miserable
afternoon, spent part of it napping.
Every did not go out and
got to bed shortly after 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### February Monday, 10 1868

Beautiful day overhead. Clear +
crisp. Got to Studio just before 9 —
got fire started + palette set when
Augero came — Soon at work for
him on Study for Drop Curtain for
Pike's Opera House — As he went out
for the day, I painted in his room.
The subject, "Landing of Columbus"
is on a canvas about 40 in. long and
46 in. high. I am to paint in sky,
sea, trees + foreground — In fact, all
but the Curtains + the figures. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.15, + by
4 had got sky + sea in, much to the
satisfaction of Augero. Waited until
5 o'clk for Maud Diven who had promised
to come take me out sleigh Riding —
Emma Ireland called for a few minutes —
went home with her + at 5.30 to Gym-
nasium — Worked well for an hour —
Home to tea at 7 — Eve'g, pleasantly
tired — In my big chair, Reading
until 9 o'clk — To bed at 9.15
