## Left Page

### July Saturday, 4 1868.

The "glorious 4th" & that as fireworks.
Up at 7 AM. & had bkfst. at 8.30. About
9 o'clk. Will drove over to 8th Ave. for Father
& Mother — they got to the house at about
9.30 the mng was spent — Rather the
whole day, in the most idle manner
possible, under the trees on the
shady verandahs with an occasional
display of vigor at the Billiard table
Dinner at 2.30 — Ther. 96° in the shade
The flesh was severely tried — Tea at
6 or 6.30. Delicious twilight, moon Rose
unclouded — we were all out on the
hay cocks until 8.30 when Father,
Mother, Geo. & Matt went home —
Charl. came up at about 5 o'clk &
he stayed all night. We were up on
the Roof until 10 o'clk — Delightful
breeze, moonlight, fireworks &c. &c.
— then sitting on verandah until 11 —
then on the floor at open windows
overlooking the River until Midnight —
then to bed, comparatively
cool after the hot day.

## Right Page

### July Sunday, 5 1868.

Up at 7 & had to wait two hours for my
bkfst. — The breeze of the night did not
die away until after 9 o'clk — then
it seemed even hotter than yesterday.
Spent the mng in completest undress on a bench under the trees
trying to read, but with no success.
Dinner at 2.30 with much marveling
as to why people should have to eat
such weather — Took a spread out,
sans culottes, after dinner, until
about 3.30 when a thunder storm
began to threaten from the north —
it promised well for a time, but by
4.30 it had given us the go by & but
little more than a sprinkle.
Another storm passed east of us, between 7 & 8 o'clk., giving us a splendid
exhibition of thunder & lightning —
Sat on piazza until 10 o'clk — then
in window of bedroom until after
11 o'clk — enjoying the fresh air
and the odor of white clover —
then to bed.
