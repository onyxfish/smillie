## Left Page

### February Saturday, 1 1868.

Glorious day. At Studio's at 9.10 & took
at once cleaning up for Reception —
Gel helping most of the time. Had a hard
siege — Done at 12. — Had a wash & clean
linen & lunch at Studio's at 1. off
Doors open to visitors at 1.15. There was
soon a crowd & the Run kept up
until 5.30 — I saw many new faces
& many old ones & as I felt very well
enjoyed the excitement. Home at
6 o'clk — everything late & everything in
a hurry — at 6.30 got off with the Girls
& Gel to Philharmonic Concert, & after
much Running & fretting got there just as the
doors were opened & secured the bay
we wished — Great crowd to hear the
Beethoven Choral Symphony. Chorus by
Harmonic Society — The orchestral parts
of the Symphony were delightful, the Chorus
not much. The Basses pretty good, but the
other Soloists, wretched. Enjoyed 8 parts of
"Jessonda". & Schumann's Concerto for Piano,
p. St. Mills playing — Home at 10.30 but sat
in my big chair Resting until 11.45. Then to bed.

## Right Page

### February Sunday, 2 1868.

After my hard work & irregularities
of yesterday, feel rather "played out."
Did not get up until 9.30 — had my
kept quite late of course, & did not
get to church in the morning, but
lounged in my chair with an
uncomfortable headache. After
dinner took a nap & at 4.30
walked up to the Park & around
the upper Reservoir. The sun-set
& twilight were glorious — I never saw
more charming color & tone. The effects
over the pure snow & ice were as
lovely in Color as any autumn. I
was quite & [illegible], trotting along by
myself. It was fair moonlight before
I got out of the Park & 6.45 before I reached
home — after tea, finding myself
tired I did not go to church. Spent
the even'g Reading & writing & to bed
shortly after 10 o'clk.
