## Left Page

### October Friday, 2 1868.

Mild, with clouds hanging low
& shutting out the mts. Much
demoralized by the weather and
determined to get off to Montrose
next Monday if possible. Spent
the m'g getting things together,
preparalong to packing trunk,
assorting things — some to be sent
home, others to take with us —
After dinner for a few moments
it broke away as though coming
off clear. Started off with traps
for Buck Hill — false promise —
it came down as thick as ever, but
I persisted & painted a low-
toned sketch about 7½×15 in.
of the mts looking to the west —
worked, as long as I could see,
until 6 o'clk — Then home to
tea, tired out, got off my note to
the Treedeys & a letter to Charles.
Even'g chatting & got to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### October Saturday, 3 1868.

Cloudless, crisp & breezy day —
Determined to make make what
I could in one days work on Wood
Hill study — having it already outlined
on a canvas 14×26 in — Immediatley
after o'clk, got off, pretty well loaded
but in exactly one hour was on top
of Wood Hill — & spent the m'g blocking
in the color — Spent much time
painting in plain blue sky, owing
to trouble with the light & difficulty
in seeing — At 1 o'clk lighted a fire,
boiled two eggs, made some beef
soup & a cup of tea & enjoyed my
lunch & the day amazingly. Worked
until 5.30 getting a very unsatisfactory
result, of course, but feeling that I
had made a strong effort in the
right direction & that I had learned
something. Jee came up just before
sunset & we walked home together —
Every got a letter from Father.
Spent some time getting a packing box
to send studies & some other things home
in — Fussing around until near
11.30 when I got to bed.
