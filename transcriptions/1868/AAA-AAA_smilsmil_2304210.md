## Left Page

### November Wednesday, 11 1868.

Rain in the m'f [illegible] all day — Had to fight with a headache but stood up to my work all day, nevertheless. Painted over the groups of [illegible] + ground, so that now I have but the open to finish. I have been as successful so far, as perhaps I could have expected — but not to the extent I had hoped. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 P.M. Mr. Beans called with her young ladies — also J. C. Gravins — Home to [illegible] at 6 — every, read for an hour Dana's Geology — washed out my brushes + at 9.30 went to bed, leaving the back parlor in possession of the girls, miss Callie Clark [illegible] her — young man —

## Right Page

### November Thursday, 12 1868.

Fine clear cold day — got to Studio at 9 o'clk. + was soon under way painting on my Cattle. Borrowed at Hart's copy of one of Troyon's cattle pieces + after a little study got to work on the left hand + of the two — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — Aunt Jessie there — At 4 o'clk had my [illegible] finished after a fashion — about as well as I had expected — then, with Coleman, went to Burling[ton] Room, there to meet C. P. Cranch and T. A. Richards from the Board or Council of N.A. of D. to arrange details for Water Color Exhibition next Jany. At 5.30 back to Studio + charcoaled Bank Note subject until 6 — Home to tea — Spent most of the eveg. with Will, arranging card of invitation for our Studio Receptions this Season — To bed at 10.30.
