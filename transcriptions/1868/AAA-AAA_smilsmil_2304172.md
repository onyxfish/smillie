## Left Page

### August Sunday, 23 1868

Another warm day — cloudless
+ somewhat hazy — Up early, but
did not get off [illegible] until 9 o'clk —
Went to Church with the Misses
Sneedy [Presbt.] + heard a sermon
from the pastor who seems to be
an amiable, unpretentious smooth
bore man. Lunch at 1. much
time lost sitting about waiting
for an influx of energy — A nap
and some writing before dinner
at 4.30 — more sitting about. Just
before sunset, a walk by the River.
Ev'g writing to Matt until near
10 o'clk — then for a time in the
parlor + from 10.30 until near
midnight, engaged in heavy
conversation in our hall way
with the Misses Sneedy + Lane +
Messrs Clark + Neilson — Crackers
Raisins + Prunes were also
discussed in the shape of Refresh-
ments — Got to bed about 12.

## Right Page

### August Monday, 24 1868

Holiday for this country. Cloudless
almost during the m'f, hazing up
during the afternoon — Mt worked
on my study. Painting over the tree
trunks, until one o'clk — Then dinner
after that, for some time mending
one of the Ribs of my sketching um-
brella that Wood broke for me —
Then down again to study of
the m'f, painting in some of
the foreground sticks + stuff
that being under the shadow
of the trees has no particular light
on it. Worked until 5 o'clk —
then home — washed up brushes
etc, finished + mailed letter
to Matt + took tea — Ev'g
passed in general chatting —
got to bed about 11 —
Ferguson + Blackman went
on to Keene this m'f.
