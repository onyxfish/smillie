## Left Page

### January, Sunday, 12, 1868.

Still clear & very cold. M.Y.
went to Hastings Ch. & heard
him preach a sermon from
73d Psalm. 28th Verse. "But it is
good for me to draw near to God"
It was, as is usual with him,
a plain, straightforward state
ment & appeal. Dinner at 1.
Father under the weather with
a cold & headache. Until
4.30 P.M. Reading, napping and
writing. Then went up to Central
Park walking up to & around the
large Reservoir. It was extremely
cold & the wind very cutting, but
I got into a fine glow by exercise.
Eve M.Y. went to hear J. P. Thompson.
His text was the story of the ten lepers
as given in Luke XVII. 11.19. He
enlarged upon our aptness to for-
get the Real giver of all our blessings.
After Returning home Read
& wrote until 10.30. Got to bed
about 11.

## Right Page

### January, Monday, 13, 1868.

*[top margin note:]* Sent letters to Haseltine of Phila & A. Jones,

Bright, clear & cold. Got to Studio
at 9. & after getting fire under way,
set to work upon Beckwith's die-
Finished dry-pointing & graver work
& being ready to bite, tried to lay a
biting ground. Couldn't do it, the
ground persistently "crawling". Took
lunch at Studio & at 2.20, with George,
went over to Mary Butler's. She was
not ready. We went by 10th Ave. [illegible] to foot
of Barclay St. there met sister Mary. Took
3.40 train to Orange. Reached Fessen-
den's at about 5 o'clk. Until tea time
saw only Sarah. Fanny F. is away visiting
her mother. Tea at 6.30. Then saw old
Mr. F. now almost blind. Was much
impressed by her quiet, uncomplaín-
ing. Truly pleasant Resignation. Spent
most of the Eve M.Y. with her. S. looked
to me miserable — pale & thin. Left the
house at 9. Train due at 9.14. 10 m. late. In
to Hoboken just too late for the 10 o'clk boat
had to wait until 10.40. Got 11 o'clk. 8th Ave car.
Got to Butler's at 11.40. writing for 3/4 m. & got
home at 12. To bed at 12.25.
