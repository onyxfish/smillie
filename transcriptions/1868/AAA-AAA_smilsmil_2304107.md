## Left Page

### April, Saturday, 11, 1868

Charming day, but quite fresh &
cool — walked up to Mr. Pardee's — 47 [illegible]
St. Left Private View Cards N.A. of D. for
Miss P. Then down to N.A. of D. to see Richards
then to Studio at 10 o'clk. Spent most
of Remainder of m[y] taking French
lesson. Home to lunch at 1 — After lunch
Rode down to Brook's Bro. then to
Sarony's with photo of H.F. Fessenden to
be copied — then to Uncle George's, then
to Schmitt's & pd. him $115 in acct,
then to Studio, then home at 5.30
Tea at 6.30 — After 8 o'clk. Fanny and
Will Kimball, Emma Ireland, George
Hawes, Addie, Ned Dunning came
by invitation to condole with me that
I have to abandon my Room at home —
to be occupied by bro. Will — Their
condolence was quite jolly. My
books, prints, & things generally were
pulled over. They left at 11.30 — I set
to work, got everything cleared up & got
to bed at 1.30 a.m. Charl improves
slowly but suffers much Rheumatic pain.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday, 12, 1868

Slept right through until 12.15
o'clk — much surprised at this
unusual feat. Up in time for
dinner, but my [illegible], a total loss.
Quite a snow flurry during the P.M.
but I know little of it, as I slept
through all — but up again in time
for Tea. Headache — So, managed
to sleep again in the eveng. This
sleeping almost alarms me — Got
my feet into hot water, & sat soaked up
from 10 until near midnight — Another
snow storm of at least 2 in. At 3 a.m.
clear moonlight & pure white snow —
to all appearance, the dead of winter.
As I sat in my Room, I could hear
Charl, & attended to his wants, Father
was also on hand — About 3.30
went off to bed & was soon asleep.
