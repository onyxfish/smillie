## Left Page

### March Wednesday, 18 1868.

Muggy mg, but cleared up very warm. Up at 7. So engaged with French exercises that I did not get to studio until 9.30 a.m. Set to work on Moat Mt. (25th day) & painted over the light Rock & trickling water in the middle foreground, bringing along in a manner quite satisfactory to me — Home to lunch at 12.30 stopping to see Brevoort on my way — Back at 2.15. Found young Bell at work drawing — he took a lesson. At 4 o'clk I took French lesson — at 5 continued my painting as long as I could see — Bell paid me $20.00. Uncle A.V.V. p'd me bal. for little picture — $30.00 Every Mr. & Mrs. [illegible] Dr. Weston took tea with us — pleasant evening until 10 o'clk. Got to bed a little before 11.

## Right Page

### March Thursday, 19 1868.

Glorious day — clear, fresh & cool. got to studio about 9 & set to work (26th day) on Moat Mt — glazed & painted over foreground trees & bushes & dragged very cool, high greys over foreground Rocks very much improving the foreground. Also, changed materially the drawing of pine trees in P.h. Corner, adding some small ones — Home to lunch at attack at 2. T. Frank Reed of Boston "Chocoroo" memory called at Studio — he is going to be a New Yorker. Francis Mr. Morse called also Snedicor — At 5 o'clk p.m. took train to Harlem & took tea with the Lents — 2d ave. bet. 124 & 5th Sts. Quite a little company during the evng. At 11.30 bid them good night & walked to 3d ave. took cars & got home at 12.35 — Got to bed just before 1 o'clk.
