## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 2 1868

Up about 5 oclk. Sky perfectly clear & lake placid. took the boat & Rowed about [illegible] for sketching points. Selected two or three. Returning took Miss Jenny Treedy over to the Tnitchell; Bushnell Camp. She is to ascend Marcy with them to day. Sun Rise superb. Bkfs: about 7. Messrs Treedy & Dunham started immediately after to Return to [illegible] at g Colman, Tiffany, &c.? & I were Rowed to our sketching place. worked faithfully until 1. Sky again clouded over, wind blew & me got very cold. Dinner about 2. Almon Bedu caught the largest Brook Trout that I ever saw. It measured 20 in long. It was supposed to weigh [illegible] about 3 oclk, being too smoky to work. C & I made along the River toward the lower Lake & climbed a slide on the East Mts. The most magnificent specimen of wild wreck that I ever saw. took 3 or 4 sketches. one looking off over the upper Lake & one looking up the great slide. we got back to camp a little after 6 oclk. Smith Bedee had Rice with extra provisions. Tea before 7, after that, again about the fire until near 9 — then I took a lone Row on the lake. The moon just rising over the Mts. It was superb. Returning, took Mr. Mrs. C. Miss Jenny Treedy & the same places — a clouded sky with full moon, grand Mt forms — mirror like water reflections all — mist breathing on its surface and perfect silence of all combined to give some of the grandest impressions that I ever enjoyed. went to Roost at 10 & slept soundly until [illegible] woke up with cold feet. Rectified that & got to sleep and did not wake until 5.30

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 3 1868

Up about 6. weather threatening — determined to make camp after dinner. Bkfs: of broiled chicken, boiled corn & potatoes with many fixens. C. T. & I were Rowed to our sketching place at 8.30. About 9 the clouds began to come down, soon covering the mts. About 10 went through the woods to camp & commenced a color sketch in my book of the camp and mts. worked all the Remainder of the mf out at 1. dinner. broiled Rib of Lamb, and the big Trout of yesterday. It was great. Party got off immediately after dinner. Excitement over the supposed killing of a deer, by a party hunting on the lake. The 2d half of our party (which I was one) left camp at 2. I got over the carry in about an hour. had to wait on the lower pond for the boat for near an hour. Spent the time making a very general sketch of the lower pond. Rowed the length of the lake in ½ an hour. got off on our ¾ mile walk to Thireas Bedee's. got there about [illegible] having been a little over 2 hours doing it. we were all tired, but declared that we were not — all swore that we were delighted and I am sure we were — all hungry and no mistake. Tea at once and a little after 8 I lay down to Rest, fell asleep. It was waked about 10, got to bed soon asleep again & slept until 5.30 next Mf.
