## Left Page

### August Monday, 31 1868.

Pleasant w'y blp'. at 7 at 8.30
with G°. Coleman & Tiffany got off to the
Roaring Brook Cataract — after a hard
pull got to the top at 9.30. Found
some fire still smouldering — Got
to work very soon on subject look
ing up the stream, one of the finest
subjects that I ever sat down before.
I cannot begin to get the splendid
suggestions that make up every part —
worked faithfully until 10'clk — Made
a little oven, built a fire, boiled water
& made Beef soup from Borden's Beef.
After that, made a cup of tea, enj'd
it very much & at 2 got to work again
& worked until near 6 when I finished
my water color sketch — hurried down
the hill to meet the misses T— & Charl
who has just arr'd from E. town —
All walked home together — after
tea, again out of doors — weather
threatening Rain. very tired —
got to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### In Camp — Upper Ausable Pond —

### Sept. Tuesday, 1 1868.

Up at 7 after a night of unrest. Sky heavily overcast
& threatening Rain. I so tired as to hope that we
would not go into camp to-day. Quite a shower
at 7.30 & then it began to clear and we to pack.
At 10 o'clk the Coleman party came along. Mr —
& Mrs. P. C. Miss C. Mr. Dunham & Louis Tiffany, with
Smith Bedee for guide — we followed with Abmon
Bedee & old Uncle Hale, with horse & wheel arrange-
ment to carry our traps. We were Mr. Tweedie —
Misses Belle, Mary & Jenny T— G°. Charl & self —
We enjoyed the walk through the 4 miles of wood
immensely & reached lower pond at 1 o'clk. Ate &
took lunch on shore, high wind & cold — but one boat
had went on. Mr. & Mrs. C. Miss C. Tiffany, J°. & I waited
& went to Rainbow fall & sketched until 4.30 when
the boat ret'd & we went on — Altho' cold &
breezy — Enjoyed the med lake very much — got
to the carry in a few minutes the gently loaded
walked the long mile through to the upper pond
in definight — getting to camp about ½ of a mile
into the lake at 6.30 — Little delay in getting our
tea as we were all hungry & it was almost dark —
Tea by candle light — With Mr. & Mrs. C. his sister
& I went out on the lake — no moonlight — sky being
very cloudy, but every thing immensely suggestive —
the ladies sang some of Mendelssohn's two part
songs which were delightful — the rest of the even
spent about the fire frolicking & singing. Before
10 o'clk we had all turned in — about as close
packed as we could be — I got through the night
with Remarkable Comfort.
