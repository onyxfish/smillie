## Left Page

### August Thursday, 27 1868.

A pleasant day again. Quite a
number left the house, jaunting
in different directions, so that we
look quite mournful in numbers.
Got to my work after 9 & painted
over the foliage on the oak tree
trying to make something of it
in color & form. It is very dry and
speckled. Took dinner at the table
with the Treeday girls, & in the after-
noon, with them, Geo. & Clark, went to
little tales — got no sketches until on
our way home, from a small hill, got
two good outline sketches of "Giant
of the Valley" — "Harrisburg" — Got in
after sunset. Eve'g, all very
much entertained by a work of a
called Williams, an old be-rigged
batchelor, apparently all fall,
about as old as the drift period —
tickled to death with the attentions
of the girls who were almost in hysterics
of laughter — Wood & I got a number of
quite satisfactory sketches of him
& a letter from Charl. — he may be here
Sat. night — Got to bed abt 11:30

## Right Page

### August Friday, 28 1868.

Beautiful m'g, but hazy & the wind
blowing furiously from the South, so
that it was impossible to keep an um-
brella up. Williams came to the house
again & made a fool of himself for the
amusement of the girls most of the m'g.
I understand that he is over 70 — I stood
it until 10 o'clk when I retired — attended
to a number of odds & ends & loafed until
dinner time — after dinner, played
croquet until 4 o'clk with the Misses
Williams (no Relation to the old fool) and
Daisy Sherman — then set to work sorting
& packing what I am to leave & what I am
to take, until tea time — after tea another hour
at it & then from 9, until 10:30 with the Girls,
Lambdin told Mrs. The girls having much
sport again at the expense of ancient
admirers — Got to bed abt 11. very
tired & sleepy — Early part of the
ev'g, glorious moonlight, later,
threatening the Rain, that one way
and another has been promised
for some days.
