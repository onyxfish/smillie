## Left Page

### March, Monday 2, 1868

Blowing a winter hurricane, with snow & hail — streets again block-
aded, & everything like Greenland.
Got to Studio at 9.30 & started
fire, but did not get warmed up
until near noon. Rec'd cards from
Kate Fessenden to Musical Soirée at
Vassar College, next Friday. George
went down town to answer invitation to Jury duty — Excused by Judge
Brady & back at 1. I had my lunch
at Studio & worked all day on the
large Pine trees in R.h. corner of
Moat Mt. (18th day). Eve'g at
home writing to the press, notices
of our Reception next Sat. and
to Geo. Howes of the West. Express,
a sort of biographical note at his
request and to Kate Fessenden
a note acknowledging her compliment & enclosing a Reception
card — To bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 3, 1868

A very clear, cold day. Ther. 6° at
9 a.m. I got up with threatenings of
a headache & got to Studio at
9.30. Did not feel well enough to
work on Moat Mt. So finished 7×9
for Snedicor — "On the Saco" — large
trees on l.h. side, hanging away over,
little glimpse of water. Worked
until 1 o'clk. Home to lunch —
Emma Bourne there — I went to
bed for an hour, then got up and
went to Studio at 3 o'clk; painted
until 5 & then got the thing finished, but not to my satisfaction —
with my throat & head, I have
passed a Dore day. Evening
unable to attend monthly meeting
of Water-Color Society. Stretched
myself out in my big chair & with
the addition of pillows, tried to
make myself comparatively comfortable. Went to bed at
11.30
