## Left Page

### April, Monday, 13, 1868.

Up late and did not get to Studio until near 10 o'clk — After getting fire started, set to work upon drawing of Sheep for A.B.N. Co —
Had a shiviling, over polite and disagreeable passage with Wangler concerning the hanging of certain pictures at N.A. of D. Home to lunch at 1 — glorious day — back at 2.15 then went over to N.A. of D. with Gel. Varnishing Day — Met Ogilvie at the door —
He Refused my offered hand and applied a coarse epithet — If he intended a joke, it was a vulgar one —
One of the pleasures of being on the Hanging Committee. Back to studios by 3 — Again worked on drawing — Did little after 4 —
At 5.30 went to Fanny Kimball's & gave her cards for Private View to-morrow eveg, then home to tea. Early part of eveg, with help of Matt & Kate, got all of my books down to back parlor — Will & Joe — Dave, Aunt Margaret & Dan Sayre called. Many up at Uncle Aaron's — Got to bed at 11.15

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday, 14, 1868.

Bright, clear mg, got to studio before 9 o'clk and after a little clearing up set to work on Sheep drawing for A.B.N. Co — commencing the "washes" — Home to lunch at 12.45 — Aunt Jessie & Emma at lunch.
Back at 2 — weather changed very disagreeably — short shower of rain just before 6 o'clk. Worked as late as I could see on my drawing, but progressed slowly — Home at 5.30 —
the girls Refused to go to N.A. Private View, so Gil & I went alone. Miserable the Rain & wind. Got to Academy just before 9 o'clk — there until 11. Suppose that I enjoyed myself — am sure that I was tired. At home at 11.30 — Redressed myself in an old suit of clothes & stretched myself on the bed beside Charl for the Rest of the night.
He was very Restless and I got no sleep until 4.30 or 5 o'clk A.M. then quite worn out, I slept until 7 a.m. when I went to my own bed & continued it until 9.30
