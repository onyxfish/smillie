## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 8, 1868

The hottest, closest, stickiest sort of a day
m.f., painted sketching board —
touched on p.f. of Bierstadt's Rocky
Mts. (unfinished) for Uncle Mr. J. T. —
Got to studio at 11.30. Commenced
2d sheet of sketches. Ill. Alice Cary's
lines for Mrs. Rutherford's Album.
Home to lunch at 1. Mr. Park —
of Poughkeepsie, at the house — got
sketches from Joe & Minn to
use in my album sketches. Back
to Studio at 3 o'clk. + worked until
6.30, making three sketches in
India ink wash + pen, illustrating "Pictures of Memory" —
Every at home. Mr. [illegible] E. Garb
called + spent an hour or
more with me — I am to do
some etching for him —
To bed at about 10.30

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 9, 1868

Very heavy thunder storm between
3 + 4 o'clk this m.f. Exceedingly
close sticky day. At 8.30 went
down town. Called first at Ritchie's
but as his plate was in process of
biting, could not do anything at it.
Then to A.B.N. Co. + waited until near
12 to see Mr. Gavit — in the mean time
went with Will to A. Brainerd's, 9
Maiden Lane + bought studs and
sleeve buttons — After getting drawing
instructions from [illegible] E. G. — went to
Bogardus's + had daf — then home
to lunch at 1.30 — back to studio at
[illegible] + by working like a good fellow
got daf outlined + ready for a
transfer by 7 o'clk — Charley Adams
called abt. 5 + 6 o'clk — Home to
tea at 7.30. Every spent chatting.
Kate's throat trouble culminated
this p.m. by breaking + discharging
profusely. Got to bed about
10.30.
