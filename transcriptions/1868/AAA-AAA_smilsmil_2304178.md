## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 4 1868.

Up at 7 o'clk — feeling much rested but still tired — My, cold & clouds low down & threatening — getting things together somewhat — cleaned up until 10 o'clk — when I commenced working in water color over some of the sketches in my small sketch book — worked up one of the Rainbow Fall sketches — at lunch time Mr. & Miss Colman came in for a few minutes —
At 4 o'clk — I climbed through a rocky pass on the Ausable River near the house — Found two or 3 splendid subjects of their kind but got no sketches — out until about 6 — then in to dinner — a boy [illegible] girts Rec'd from Polhemus to day. Every Raining steadily — time passed chatting with the Tweedy girls, who, disappointed by the [illegible] in going to Placid to-day, expect to go to E-town to-morrow —
Near 11 o'clk when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 5 1868.

Awake at 6 and up about 7 — Clear almost cloudless My. Got bkfst and off to work at 8.30 after bidding good bye to the Tweedys who Ret'd to E-town at 10 o'clk. I got to work on an oil sketch of "Giant" 11×19 in — at 9 o'clk & worked as for dear life until 1.30. when having got the sky & distant range in, I went to dinner — at 2, started for a point beyond where I worked in the My & commenced a sketch looking through the Ausable pass, worked until 6 o'clk — Charmed with the effects of light & color — I am much gratified at what I have accomplished to-day. These grand subjects make me feel that I am working pure metal and I am growing. Ev'g Rec'd a birch bark note from the Tweedy girls — Sat out of doors until near 9 — in-doors until [illegible] and then to bed —
