## Left Page

### March Friday, 6 1868

Over cast day & mild but raw.
Up at 7.30, but after getting my
breakfast felt quite slim again
& so weak that I did not start
for the Studio until after 10 o'clk.
Spent the m'f putting things
away, preparatory to a clean up.
Took lunch at the Studio — at
2.15 went to Phil. Rehearsal.
Mad. Parepa-Rosa sang, which
accounted for a great crowd —
After Rehearsal, on my way up,
stopped in at N.A. & D. for a few
minutes, then to Studio — Found
the woman just finishing the
cleaning up — Had a Session
with Fautman & Wenzler &so, did
not get home until 7 o'clk. Well
at home, doing a little writing
& a little Reading — To bed at
11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### March Saturday, 7 1868

Up at 6.30 A.M. Mild, muggy day —
a washing thaw — & unlimited slush
under foot — Got to Studio at 9.15. Went
at once to Brevoort's Room to take French
lesson — Mlle d'Arnac anxious to get down
town — we did not have exercises prepared,
so the lesson was postponed — Set to work
to get Room in order for Reception this p.m.
Natural flowers — Sister Matt there to fix them —
At 12 Maud. Diven called — then Mrs Moses
Reach & daughter — All at work fixing up —
At 1 opened our door — Caught lunch
at intervals — a perfect Rush until
after 5.30. With difficulty could get our
Room to ourselves — Off for home at 6 o'clk.
Everything hurly burly & drive, bolted
some tea, then off to Philharmonic — there
before the doors opened & got good boxes —
Enjoyed the Mozart & Mendelssohn Sym-
phonies & Wagner's introduction to "Lohengrin"
very much — but did not go wild over
Parepa-Rosa — Geo. & I walked up 4th ave. with
the Kimball's & Emma Ireland — Home at 10.45
To bed at 11.45 —
