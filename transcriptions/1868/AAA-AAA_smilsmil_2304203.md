## Left Page

### October Monday, 26 1868.

Dismal day. Spent the m[orning]
packing + Receiving calls. Elis[on]
+ Sylvains Mulford + Jas. Carmell
called to see our work — Just
after dinner I went down town
to hurry up my new Trunk, which
by dint of plumying + scolding, I
got up to the house by 2 o'clk —
a fair, substantial looking
trunk + costing me $19.00 —
bid Fan + the other Mulfords
Good bye — + by 3 o'clk. was all
o.k. + dressed for the stage, had
a tolerably comfortable drive to New
Milford, getting there a few minutes
past 5 — Train came along at 5.50
got to Gt. Bend a little before 6 — took
tea at the Hotel + strolled about for an
hour or more, then in again + waited
patiently until near 11 when the Erie
Train came along — Got at once into
a Sleeping Car, Secured a double berth
+ passed the night as comfortably
as could be expected —

## Right Page

### October Tuesday, 27 1868.

Got up + dressed a little before 7 a.m.
Train got in about 7.10 — Pleasant m[orning].
On ferry boat, met Dr. E. J. Dunning and
Rose up town with him — Got home
about 8.15 — Folks surprised, altho'
they had expected us last night —
Waited about home until 11 o'clk, for
our trunks, as they did not come, then
went to A.B.N.Co. to Report. Saw
Messt. Gavit + good all + after much
consultation, three [illegible] for
[illegible] were put into my hands
Took lunch down town and got
home between 3 + 4 o'clk P. m. —
Spent the Rest of the afternoon
+ evg. unpacking trunks +
getting things put away — Cef-
Bradley made a short call
during the evg. — Got to
bed about 10.30 —
