## Left Page

### February Friday, 7 1868.

A lovely day, as I could judge
it, looking from my window.
Felt much appression in
my head during the morning
& worked on my drawing
with difficulty, but stuck to it.
About 3 o'clk P.M. went out
on back piazza & walked
up & down for nearly an hour,
enjoying exceedingly the fine
sky and air. Felt refreshed
& continued work as long as
daylight lasted. Then, for a
few minutes, worked in our home
gymnasium. Evely, Will & Joe
came in at 8.30 & stayed until
10 o'clk. It was after 11
when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### February Saturday, 8 1868.

Another charming day, but cold.
Finished drawing for N.B.N. Co and
mounted it before noon. It
is unsatisfactory to me, looking
"skinny" or meagre. I have done
it under very disadvantageous
circumstances. Maud Diven
called at 12, in sleigh. Offered
to take me sleigh riding. Matt
went with her for an hour. At
1.30 went to Studio. Young Bell
came & took a drawing lesson.
His friend, the nervous Englishman,
called & went away with him.
Got home at about 6 o'clk,
feeling tired & down hearted.
Evely spent lazily in my
big chair, Reading newspapers.
To bed at 10.30. During p.m. at
the Studio, Mr & Mrs Knudson called
& we had quite a long & chatty
visit from Mr. Harris Pardee.
