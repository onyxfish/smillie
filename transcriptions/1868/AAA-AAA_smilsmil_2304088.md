## Left Page

### March Wednesday, 4 1868.

Still cold, windy & cutting. Did
not get to studio until after
9 o'clk; feeling tolerably well in
the morning. Worked (19th day)
on Moat Mt., painting over the
Rocks in the middle ground
over which Runs a small stream
of water. Home to lunch at 1 —
back at 2 — my head beginning
to bother me again — Painted
away until 3.30 when Ge. & I
went into Brevoort's Room & took
our French lesson. At 5,
back to studio, but unable to
do any thing but clean up my
palette — Swept completely
used up — Bilious headache —
cold in my head — throat —
& lots of other things — To bed
at 10 — During the day, Ritchie
sent up to see if I could bite in
the 2d. line on his sky to-morrow —
I set monday.

## Right Page

### March Thursday, 5 1868.

In bed again, all day —
filled with a general disgust.
It seems as though the few mts
that I have, cannot be used
unless I pay some severe
penalty.

Got up at 5 o'clk P.m. &
took my breakfast at 6.30
P.m. Rolled in my big chair
until about 11 when I went
to sleep & slept until near
2 o'clk A.M. when I got up
& went to bed.
