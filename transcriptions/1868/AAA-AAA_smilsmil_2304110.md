## Left Page

### April Friday, 17 1868.

Bright mild m[illegible]. Went downtown
to A.H. Ritchie's to bite in 3° line
on sky of his big plate. Got there
at 9 o'clk A.M. & set to work at
once. Got through successfully
at 2 o'clk. Then rode up to
Academy of Music to Rehearsal
Philharmonic. Enjoyed again
as much as ever, the Schumann
Symphony, Mendelssohn's Mid-
summers Night Dream with Avon
Chorus & Weber's Der Freyschutz.
At 5 o'clk went over to see Avery's
collection of Water Color drawings.
There met Amelia Butler & Maggie
Gunn. At 6 went to N.A. of D —
At 7 — home. Found there Uncle
Willie, just in from Ottawa.
Spent the every chatting,
got to bed a little before 11.

## Right Page

### April Saturday, 18 1868.

Very mild, spring-like day. Up
early & got to Studio at 8.30. Studied
my French until near 10 o'clk, when
I went into Brevoort's Room and took
my lesson. After, not feeling well
did not get to work, but killed the
time until 12.30 when again Joe &
I went to B's Room & lunched with
him, Mrs B. Mlle de Asnoy, Miss Lizzie
Brevoort. Had quite a jolly time until
2.30 P.M. Painted on sky & distance
of a picture for Augers until about
4 o'clk. Reed of Boston called. At
5 went home. Eve[ning] with [illegible]
many, Matt & Kate went to Philharmonic
concert. Although my digestives were
very troublesome I enjoyed the music
exceedingly. The programme
was long and the audience
quite brilliant. Home at 11
& to bed at 11.45
