## Left Page

### August, Thursday 13, 1868

Cold, clear m[illegible] at 7 o'clk
Ther. 57°. Soon after bkfst. the
weather thickened again —
It is glorious for any thing but
sketching — Spent some time
with Colman + Tiffany chatting.
At 10 o'clk at my sketching pl.
a slight shower got there at
the same time — worked hard
until past one, getting the
tree passage in up[per] R.h.
cor. painted in — Walked home
in another shower. Afternoon
had glorious skies — tried to
paint one. 7×10 — Mr. Sambd[en]
of Phila., son of Gil. came this
afternoon — Had a pleasant
call from him — Every. I
finished my charade — Rec'd
boy from Polhemus with [illegible]
Spent part of the eveg. with
the Company in the parlor.
& got to bed about 11 o'clk —

## Right Page

### August, Friday 14, 1868

Lovely m[orning], promising a first rate
day to work — Miss Polland went to leave.
Every thing late, got to my work about 10.
Set to work painting in trees in middle
distance — smoke began to pervade the
atmosphere and at noon the mountain
hills were shut out from sight, the nearer [illegible]
being visible only in form, without drawing.
Strong S.W. wind blowing — got through with
my m[orning] work however, in a tolerably
satisfactory manner — Dinner very late,
near 2 o'clk. After dinner, a great change,
the smoke (for I smelled it) nearly blown
away — a game of croquet, when
Kellogg offered me a seat in his [wagon]
to go down the valley some 7 miles —
accepted — a glorious p.m. & enjoyed
the ride very much — got in just at sun
set. Eve[ning] a good deal of romping —
Virginia Reel — Scandal; & going
to Jerusalem — I felt so much better
that I took part in it — much excitement
over a bat that got in at the window —
got to bed about 10:30
