## Left Page

### July, Sunday 26, 1868.

Awake early, but knowing that I could get no bkfst before
9 o'clk I lay quiet, listened to birds & rejoiced in the flutter
of a delicious breeze, until after 7 o'clk. — Then got up & waited
patiently for my bkfst.

All day spent on one or other of the piazzas of the
house until after 7, when I met a young Relative of Miss
Porter's, from North Carolina,
we rode to the city — Home a
little after 9 P.M. Edws Parker
there from Po — Compelled
packing trunk, with trouble
got it shut & locked & was
just congratulating myself
when the hasp burst from the
lock — went to bed at 11
in a high state of disgust.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 27, 1868.
*at Poe's Hotel, West Point.*
*Room 53.*

Up at 5.30 A.M. & after dressing, at
once set to work to repair the damage
of last night. No stores open until
near 7 — bought two new locks and
tried to put them on, but before I
could finish, I had to start for 8
o'clk train, leaving Ge. to follow
with trunk on Mary Powell — I
met Mr. Menger as p. appointment
Reached West Point at about 10
went to Poe's Hotel & from there, we
climbed to Fort Putnam. I was
excessively disappointed to find
the atmosphere so hazy that I
could do nothing at all — To dinner
at Poe's at 1 — P.M. spent sauntering
about. At 6 we went down to landing,
Ge. came up on M.P. & Mr. Menger
went home by H.R.R. Ge. & I saw
every parade — took a walk &
went to tea at 7.30 P.M. Eve'g spent
in our Room No. 53 "laying off"
