## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 16 1868

Raining at 7 AM, when I got up, and promising a day of it. So determined to pack at once & start for Elizabeth town as soon as possible — by 10 o'clk we were ready, had paid all bills & Almon drove us to E. town. Miss Scull accompanying us, for the drive & some shopping — we stopped at D. Potter's to bid the Colmans good bye, at the P.O. & made a short call on Miss Mary Cook — at one of the [illegible]. By this time the weather was charming — a lovely blue — beautiful clouds and delicious breezy air — Got to E. town at 1.30 — did not get dinner until 2 — [illegible] clouded up again & blew very cold. I began to feel very much out of sorts — dyspeptically — hung about [illegible] in the p.m. took a nap until tea time — Dres[sed] felt better — time passed chatting with Williams's & Tweedy's & listening to Judson, of Phila. sing — Her mother, M[r]. bro's & a sister in law, a daughter of Gym[s?] are here — got to bid ab[out] 1 —

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 17 1868

Clear cold m[orn]g — Ice was lying around — some quite a ¼ of an inch thick — breezy too. So as I still felt under the influence of my yesterdays upset, I determined to work upon one of my studies in doors. Took the little study that I partly finished on the [illegible] of the mts. as seen from our camp, and worked over the lower part of it — could not get what I wanted — the middle distance being obstinately too much of one thing or another — too cold or too warm — too negative or too positive — after much musing, gave it up at 4.30 p.m. without getting out of my difficulties. Then Jl. & I took a short walk with Misses Belle & Meany Tweedy & getting in at tea time. Dr. Lewis & 3 sisters M[r] & Mrs Clay & another lady, all of them arr[ived] this noon. Dr. L. has just ret[urne]d from a trip to the Saugenay — very much delighted — Prof. Mr. Howard Williams, the fossil man, entertained us with a description of his ascent of Mt. Whiteface — got to bed at 10.30
