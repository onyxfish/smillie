## Left Page

### October Thursday, 8 1868

A cold raw day. 15° colder than yesterday, at times driving rain. Bkft. at about 9 o'clk. At 10 got to work with pencil going over sketch that I made of E-town from Buck Hill, set palette to work over the oil sketch of same subject, last Friday & at 12 o'clk went out for a walk — went through & about the new County Jail, now just finishing. At 1.30, dinner. At 2.30 so dark that I gave up the idea of working in color & tried to charcoal out an idea for a water color drawing. Slept at the better of me again & I did not wake until near 5 — another walk & tea at 6. At 7.30 with Gel. & amb. H. Called on Vic Dimock — back at 8.30 when Gel. & I went down to Fau M's — there played whist until 10.30 Home & to bed at 11.30 —

## Right Page

### October Friday, 9 1868

Glorious day. Up at 7. Bkft. at 8. Walked up to Fair Ground & enjoyed myself intensely, looking away over the peaceable stretches of distance to the faint blue hills beyond — Home & commenced work at 10 o'clk. Repainting sketch of E-town & mts. that I painted a week ago to-day. Worked hard and at 5 o'clk got it finished, in some respects satisfactorily. The middle distance is the worst part of it and all wrong. At 5 went up to the Fairground & met Gel. who has been painting there this p.m. — walked until 6 — tea, and at 8, went to Fau M's & met Katie & Hetty Searle & a Mr. Platt of N.Y. Played whist until 11.30 much to George's discomfort — got to bed shortly after midnight.
