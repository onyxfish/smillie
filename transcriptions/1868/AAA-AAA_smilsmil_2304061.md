## Left Page

### January Friday, 10 1868

Clear, very cold & windy. at
Studio at 9 a.m. Mr. Gavit sent
up Beckwith's die for me to go
to work upon. Got some small
canvases ready to work upon
& Reduced, on a 7×9, my large
Moat Mt. subject, to make a
small sketch in color. Home
to lunch at 1. Most of p.m.
used up by a long visit from
Leeds the Auctioneer. Got
Engraving table to Rights and
fixed up tools for an engraving
Campaign. At 5 o'clk went to
Mary Butler's to make arrangements
to go with her to Orange next Monday.
Home to tea at 6. Much of the
evey passed in discussing the
possibility of accomodating Mil
& Joe in our house. Went in to
Wills for a few minutes. Got
to bed after 11.

## Right Page

### January Saturday, 11 1868

Weather still continues very clear &
cold. At studio at 9 & soon had
a chance to find how much of
manual dexterity, as an engraver,
I had lost. Commenced work on
Beckwith's die "View of Albany" &
dry pointed & Re-entered much of
it, using the graver also. It as-
tonishes me that I take hold of the
tools with as much comfort and
freedom as though I was in full
practice. And it is now since I have
done any engraving of any importance.
Home to lunch at 1. Back at 2.15. Found
Young Bell waiting for me. He took his
drawing lesson. Post Belle called.
At 5, after touching up pf. of Delnoci's
die, went to Gymnasium & worked
about an hour, then home to tea. Every
the sensation, the new piano that Dr.
Throop sent up to Mary this a.m. Ditmn
came over & tried it & speaks well
of the middle notes. It is worth about
$750 or $800. To bed at 11.30
