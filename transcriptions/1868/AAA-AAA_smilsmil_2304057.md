## Left Page

### January, Thursday 2, 1868

Up rather late, owing to something somewhat headachy. Got to Studio at
9.30 & set to work upon 6×10 "White Mts
from Randolph Hill" — In a sort of a mess
with it, — Can't make my sky, mts, &
foreground harmonize & don't know
where the trouble lies. Home to lunch
at 1 — back at 2. Just before 3 o'clk
Maud Diven & her Sister Lea called —
left at 3.30 — Then I went to the barbers
& had my hair cut & head shampooed, on
Return, went into Ja. Hart's Studio. While
there P.H. came to the door. I sought an
interview with him & at last met him in
the hall, where I gave him a bit of my
mind". — He threatened to "let me have it
along side of the head". — I told him that
he didn't dare & that his attack upon
Father was contemptible & cowardly.
He then left. At 8.15 I went to the
gymnasium & worked for 1/2 an hour,
then home. — Evening spent looking
over some new books & English
papers — To bed at 10.45.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 3, 1868

A very heavy fog at 7.30 a.m. when I
got up & threatening more Rain — At
Studio at 9. Got to work at once upon
7×9 sketch "a view of the White Mts and
bit of the Saco, near Stillings." Worked
closely, for it is a heavy subject, until
1 o'clk, when I went home to lunch, back
at 2 & worked hard until near 5 — almost
too late to see — but I think the sketch
bright & pleasing. I think that I am
advancing in knowledge of color and
facility of execution — I only wish
that I could get a chance to work
upon subjects & sizes more pretentious.
These sketches, small pictures —
pot-boilers — are worse than child's
play. Evening spent reading
and writing dunning letters to
delinquent members of Water Color
Society — Louis Menger called
at the Studio during the morning.
To bed at 10.30.
The day cleared off after 11 a.m.
