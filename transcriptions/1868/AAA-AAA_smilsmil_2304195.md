## Left Page

### October Saturday, 10 1868

Superb day, breezy, but clear & cool.
about 9.30, with paper & pencils, went
out to Jal Carmalt's, hoping to be
able to get sketches from a fine horse
of his, but found on getting there, that
he had sold it. He was not at home. I
had a pleasant visit with Mrs C —
then went out & had a look at the
cattle and walked back to town.
Called on Carmalt at his office —
Dinner at 1.30 — after that went to
Tarbell's stable & sketched the
head of one of his blk mares and
bits of other horses until 5 o'clk.
Then home & walked with Gel.
until 6.30 — Tea — Uncle Wm at
Rush, stump speaking. Every at
7.30, with Gel. went to the Hotel
to call on Miss Mollie Groesbeck —
was out — Then went to Tom M's
where with Joe Wm., Sylvanus & [illegible]
I played whist until 10 o'clk —
Home & to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### October Sunday, 11 1868

Up late, of course. The weather seeming
settled into a very hazy, mild & Indian
summerish style. — Mel went to Presb'n
Ch. — met Fanny Jessup & Miss Dodge and
walked to Ch. with them — Henry Jessup —
am & last night with his new wife and
preached this mng from John 7. C.H 37 v. —
& John 4 c, 4 ty. v. He made an interesting
discourse & appeared to advantage. After
service walked through darkie town and
to the graveyard — then with Gel up to the
Fair Ground. Returning to dinner at 2.
After dinner a nap — Joe Wms called &
stayed from 4 until 6 — Tea at 6.45 — then
went again to Presbyterian Ch. to hear
[illegible]'s farewell discourse — He leaves
here Tuesday to sail for Syria on Saturday.
His discourse was descriptive of the
East — Rambling & very prolix — Service
from 7 until 9.45 — a walk about town
with Gel. then home & to bed at 10.30
