## Left Page

### January, Saturday 4, 1868

Dismal morning — not actually raining — but everything wet. Got to studio at 9 + set at once to work — Geo + I got everything swept, double swept + dusted up in fine style — Kate Drinker + Miss Duval called at 11.45. We were not ready for callers, but Geo. did the honors, such as they were. After finishing sweeping I got a good wash + having clean linen made a complete change + altogether felt as "fine as a fiddle". Opened the Room at 1 o'clk. + had quite a rush of visitors, quite astonishing to me considering the day — During part of the P.M. it was quite clear + very mild. Young Bell came after 2 o'clk + until 3.30. he visited among the studios after that — until dark — he threw back of the screen — I enjoyed the relaxation of chatting with our visitors very much. At 6 home to tea. Matt terribly exercised because a beast of a man had ruined her new dress by squirting something over it. Got a note from S. C. 7. Snedicor sent check for $79.25 — Result of Leeds'. Sale of 4 [illegible] pictures — Every spent lounging + Reading — To bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 5, 1868

A glorious, clear, cold, winter day — went to Rev. Hastings in the m'g + heard him preach his sermon for the new-year, as is his habit, giving his church, or congregation a text, or sentiment for the coming year. He chose Philippians 1st chap. 21st V. "For to me to live is Christ". Home to dinner. At 3.30 P.M. went up to the Park — wind high + my ears very cold, but I enjoyed it much — Home again at 6 — Every went to Rev. J.P. Thompson's + hear Rev. Jno Thompson, of the Ch. in 34 St near 6th Ave. He preached condemnation to me from the text — "Tho shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart and all thy soul and all thy strength" — After getting home Read awhile + to bed at 11 o'clk
