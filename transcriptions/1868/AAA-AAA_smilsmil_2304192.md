## Left Page

### October, Sunday 4, 1868

a glorious day — Up late —
mng went to church, but my
wits were so wool gathering that I
heard the sermon but indifferently.
The subject was the living "empel[e]
& the promise". I will dwell in them —
2° Cor, VI +16°. At 1 o'clk took lunch
& until 3 packed — then Geo and
I went out until 5, when we
returned to dinner. At 7
went to church and I'm afraid
was a poorer listener even than
in the mng. Home before 8 o'clk
& until 10 packed, when I finished
its for the night — and got to
bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 5, 1868

*Delevan House, Albany*
*Room 34 —*

Up before 7 — Bkfst at 8 — worked
at box of studies & things until it
was time for us to be off — at 9.45 bid
good bye all around & started off,
Ferguson & Smith, Reede driving up
as m did so — Got to West Port in
about 10 m. "Adirondack" got along
a little before 12 — Superb day, just
after leaving Port Henry had a very
good dinner — enjoyed the sail
through the River like, southern,
extremity of the Lake — got to White
Hall at 4 o'clk & at once took train
quite a crowd at Moreau, the
last day of the season at Lake Fil-
ferguson left us at Fort Edward —
at Albany a little before 8, went
to Delevan House — Parlor 34 — had
a good wash & supper — then walked
up to the Capitol & back to Hotel
when I wrote a letter to Father
& got to bed at 10.30.
