## Left Page

### February Saturday, 15 1868.

Very mild, overcast & thawing —
Did not get to studio until nearly
9.30 — Set palette & commenced
the finishing of 7×9 for Uncle Aaron
but felt very unlike work. Uncle
Wm called during m'f & walked
up home with me to lunch at
1 o'clk — I retu at 2.30 & insisted
up on my working. So I finished
Uncle A's little picture before 5 o'clk
— it should have been done before
lunch — for I had other work laid
out for the P.M. — Mr. Murphy, dog,
boy & friend, (all of the London Art
Journal & Daily Post I believe) called
near 5 — He (m.) has not fore possessed me
in his favor — Düring called & gave me
ticket to Thomas's Concert this even.
Tea at 6.30. Aunt H. & Uncle Wm went up to
Uncle A's. Went to Concert at 7.30 —
Very much pleased with "Coriolanus"
& the Schumann Symphony No. 2. —
Walked home with Düring & got to
bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### February Sunday, 16 1868.

after a night of unrest (the 3d, by the
way) was forced to get up about day-
light, by severe pain & movement of
the bowels — Curled myself up in my
big chair, where I took my breakfast
between 9 & 10 o'clk — Being quite
relieved of pain at noon — got up
& dressed myself. Cannot
account for this biliary perturba-
tion and feel discouraged —
Mrg overcast but afternoon
clear & pleasant — Walked the
back piazza from 4.30 until 5 o'clk.
Even spent Reading — bathed
my feet & got to bed it
