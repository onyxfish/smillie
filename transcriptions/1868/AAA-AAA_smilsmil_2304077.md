## Left Page

### February Tuesday, 11 1868.

Charming day — Made a change on N.B.N.Co's drawing & got to Studio just before 10 o'clk. Set palette & set to work again on Augero's Drop Curtain Study, painting in trees on R.h. side & part of fore ground. went home to lunch at 1.15 and back at 2.30 — Painted away until nearly 4.30 P.M. when Ella & Maud Diven called & with them I went out sleigh Riding — a fine turn out — two free driving blk horses a driver & plenty of fine Robes — Drove through the Park, out through & Harlem Lane — around by Uncle Aaron's stopped there for a few minutes then home by Bloomingdale Road. Reaching Divens at 6.30 — Delightful twilight, clear & cold — Ran from Diven's home, getting in a few minutes past 7 — all in a glow.
Eveng, again spent lazily in my big Chair & to bed at 10.15

## Right Page

### February Wednesday, 12 1868.

Beautiful day — clear & cold — At Studio at 9 — set palette & to work at once upon Augero's "Columbus". Alfred Jones called during the mg. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.15. Worked faithfully until 5.30, when I could see no longer & just managed to get the thing finished — I can hardly think it creditable to me, altho' I've enjoyed the practice, but I think little of a's composition, which I have not undertaken to modify — & have been much affected by the color & handling of his first painting, which I had to work over — Augero appears to be very much pleased, & today gave me a very nice Opera glass — Very tired — home to tea at 6.30 — Eveng spent in the midst of a family Mus-trd in my big Chair. Fred Smillie took tea & stayed all night with us. To bed at 10.30
