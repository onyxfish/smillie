## Left Page

### February Friday, 21 1868.

Ther. 35°.

A very mild, clear day — Spring-like
& streets overrun with shallow Rivers.
Reached studio just before 9 a.m.
feeling very miserable about the
head. Set to work upon sketch
a upon broken palette, that Mr. Brown
has bought, to finish it, but was obliged
to give it up & at noon Rose home
& again tumbled into bed, suffering
an outrageous headache for the
rest of the day — Got up at 9 P.M —
& sat in my big chair until about
2 o'clk a.m. when I went to bed.

## Right Page

### February Saturday, 22 1868.

Ther. 12°.

A very cold, clear day, wind cutting
from N.W. — A very trying change from
yesterday — Got to studio about 9,
set palette & commenced work
again upon palette sketch. After
a little, satisfied myself that my
head was not in a condition to
work — cleaned up & at 12.45 went
home to lunch. At 2 started for
Uncle Aaron's — by 8th Ave, R.R. — the
walk across from 8th Ave & 125th St. very
cold — Found no one in but Uncle
m — he was practicing Billiards —
played 3 games with him. he beat
me every time — At 6 started for
home — Saw fire works. 7th Ave & 43d — 5th Sts.
After tea went to Diven's & spent
the eve'g with the three yg ladies
Home at 10.30 — Got to bed at 11.45.
Mr. Ed. Parker of B — Rooming with Charl.
Mrs Smith & daughters Florence &
May at studio this p.m. Geo. I saw
them — also, Ned Dunning.
