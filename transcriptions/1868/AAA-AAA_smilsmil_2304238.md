## Left Page

### December Saturday, 19 1868.

Beautiful day — got to Studio about 9 — Found that I had left my key at home — had to wait until Geo. came — in the mean time amused myself building a fire for miss Bascome. The tree that I painted in yesterday was still so wet that I had no trouble in painting Right into them + so I spent the day at it — but feeling tired I did not work very closely — miss Mary Cook called about noon — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — about 4 o'clk young Mr. Phelps, Mr. I— mrs. Horace Robbins made us a call. Home at 5.30 — dressed + had tea — At 7.30 went to Appella Hall with Matt + Kate to a Private Musical Soiree by the Rivarde Musical Club — Geo. + Charl there with Belle + Mary Tweedy — all out together — Home at 11 — to bed at 11.30 —

## Right Page

### December Sunday, 20 1868.

a horrible sort of day — slow falling sleet that freezes in a thin film of glare ice over every thing making it really difficult + dangerous to walk in the street — Mrs. f went to Madison Ave. Baptist Ch. to hear Rev. Bridgeman of Albany. I was very much pleased — He preached from Psalms 37 "& 37." discoursing upon what constitutes a "perfect man" + "peace" — To-day D. I— succeeded in getting a tent out of Mary's neck that slipped in + has been in the cavity for a week. He is relieved + she is delighted + now expects to get better Right away. Took a nap from 4 to 6 — Evening wrote a letter to Mr. Mr. Maues. Got to bed about midnight —
