## Left Page

### April, Tuesday, 21, 1868.

A day almost like yesterday —
commenced Raining between 10
& 11 a.m. & kept it up for the Rest
of the day until after 6. p.m. —
Got to Studio at 9.20 & for an hour
or more wrote French exercises —
Then Mr. Kittell & Mr. Higginson (a
daughter of Stephen H. Tyng) called
about 12. Young Bell came & took
a lesson — at 1.15 home to lunch
at 2.30 back again — Rec'd from
Kate Fessenden Cards for "Founder's
Day" at Vassar College — Worked all
the p.m. until near 7 outlining with
Skts. Turp. & brown 20×36" View on
the Saco at North Conway."
Eve'g at home hanging paintings
in back parlor until 11 oclk —
at 11.30 got to bed, mill tired
out. Aunt H. stayed with us
all night.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday, 22, 1868.

A cloudless and very warm Spring day
got to Studio at 9.20 & set to work at
once (2nd day) on 20×36 Saco River &
by 12 o'clk had finished the outline.
Then wrote a note to Mr. Wm. Mam
enclosing my Private View Card to the
[illegible] A. of Fine arts. Home to lunch
at 1.15 — back at 2.15 and until 3.30
studied French & wrote exercises.
at 3.30 went in to Brevoort's & took
lesson. At 5. met with George down
to Snedicor's — did not get home
to tea until near 7.30 — Spent
the eve'g with a little Reading
a little writing — cleaning off
of drawing boards & got to bed
at about 10 o'clk.
To night Mrs. Knudsen gives
a party — am invited, but can't
help it — evidently am not
a Society man.
