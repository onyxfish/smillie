## Left Page

### October Friday, 30 1868.

Delightful day — up at 6.30
At 8.45 went to Studio to see a man about painting for him the portrait of some prize oxen. Did not see him but Augers showed me the photos and I agreed to try to do one of them. Home at 10.30. a.m. worked on outline of Slaughterhouse drawing (Saladers) for A.B. [illegible] all the rest of the day until 4.30 P.M. At 1.30 went with [illegible] & Registered for voting. At 5 o'clk. went to No. 65. 32nd St. and saw Judge Spear. He has a painting that he thinks is by Claude & desires to exhibit it in the Academy Fall [illegible]. Took a walk & got into tea at 6 — at 7.30 went to Emma Ireland's & spent the ev'e'g with her, talking over our Adirondack experiences. Left there at 10 — then home, stopped at Studio for stretchers — To bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### October Saturday, 31 1868.

Disagreeable weather again, being blustering & dusty, very. After [illegible] until 10 o'clk. Stretching my studies on the stretchers that I brought from Studio last night — then went to work upon drawing for A.B.N. Co. until lunch at 1 — After lunch went to Burling's Studio to talk over Water Color Society matters, preparatory to the Board meeting next Monday. Ret'd to my drawing at 3 & kept at it until 5 P.M. when, with [illegible], I walked to Central Park [illegible]ck — Ev'e'g, mounted George's Water Color Studies for him and got to bed about 10.30
