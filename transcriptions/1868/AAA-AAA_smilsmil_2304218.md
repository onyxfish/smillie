## Left Page

### November Sunday, 29 1868.

Cloudy \+ rather wintry, but a superb night \- My heard Dr. Hastings from Matt XI chap. end of 5th v. "and the poor have the Gospel preached to them". It was a strong missionary sermon to Raise funds for the mission City Churches \- Dined at home \- Passed the bulk of the p.m. in my chair, napping \- Mary \+ the Dr. at our house to tea \+ spent the eve't. I went to hear D.J.P. Thompson \- He gave his text somewhere in the 72°. Psalm \- but it seemed to be more particularly Grant's word "Let us have Peace" \- It was a repetition of his Thanksgiving sermon \- Father in bed to-day with a Return of his cold \- writing notes until 11.30 got to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### November Monday, 30 1868.

Rather bleak m'g lifting during the day \- At 9 went down town to see about the printing of our Studio Reception Cards or invitations \- Until 11 o'clk. Running back and forth from printers to paper man's \+ A.B. N. Co. Got to Studio at 12 o'clk Got things ready to go to work on my drawing, then home to lunch at 1 \- back at 2 \+ worked as long as I could see, to finish "Gaucho Guitar" \- Home to tea at 6 \- Eve'g Mounted A.B. N. Co's drawing, backed some of Mary's wedding cards, wrote to Mr. Jameson acknowledging the Receipt of her Memorial book of her son John \- Sketched a Dragon for Algero \-
