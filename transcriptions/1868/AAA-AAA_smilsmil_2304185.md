## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 20 1868

Up about 7.30 — driving wind
+ rain — to all appearance
the equinoctial — Soon started
a fire + by its pleasant warmth
leisurely dressed + then wrote
a letter to Uncle Willie — Did
not get breakfast until near
10 o'clk — Went to church + heard
a good simple sermon from
1st Cor. 6th Chap. 19 & 20th v. — "Bought
with a price" which was urged
upon us. Immediately after
service Mr. + Mrs. Clay + Dr. Lewis
visited our Room to look over
our studies — Wrote a letter
to Fan Mulford before dinner
at 4.30 — Walked on piazza
after dinner + between 8 +
9 o'clk wrote a long letter to
Mary — From 9 until near 11
in the parlor with the ladies,
then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 21 1868

Clear cold morning, developing
into a glorious day — too magnificent
to last — So cold that I determined
to work in doors — Got Room nicely
warmed + then set to work to finish
my water color study on the Lower
Ausable Lake. Mr. Clay + Dr. Lewis visited
me in the Mt, Dr. Lewis giving me a
commission for a water color drawing.
Shortly after 12 went out with J.L. + had
a glorious walk to Rice's falls — over the
stream + around over the hills by Wood-
bill. I was in a perfect delight — back at
4 to dinner — Afternoon again worked
on water color until about 4.30 when I
went off + had a walk alone on Buck
hill where I saw the sun set — Well,
caught at whist — Played with Mr. Clay,
(a very testy mortal, by the way) against
Mrs. Morrison + D.S. — until 10.30 o'clk —
Six games, + beaten every game —
Then my head ached + my feet were
cold + I went to bed at 11.15 very
much out of humor.
