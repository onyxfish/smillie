## Left Page

### Sept. Saturday, 26 1868

a struggling pleasant day —
dubious in the mng — fine at
midday — gloomy at evening
& lighting us to bed with a
watery looking moon — Mr. [illegible]
morrison left this AM. I got to work
immediately after breakfast on my
sketch of a slide & worked until
12 when I went out & walked for
an hour and a half with George —
Then dinner & again to work —
when at last I could see no
longer, Geo. & I again went out for
a walk & did not get in to tea
until late — My sketch in some
Respects pleases me — but it is so
far far away from my ideal that
on the whole it is discouraging —
Eve'g chatting with Dr. Lewis
until 9.30 — Then washing brushes
& clearing up until 10.30 when
I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sept. Sunday, 27 1868

Dull overcast day, threatening
rain — My went to church
"Be not pleased that ye have power
over Spirits but rather Rejoice that
your names are written in the
Book of Heaven" — was the text —
a discourse, good & hum-drum
as usual — After service, walked
a ways up the Water St. Road &
Ret'd by the Upper Road — Wrote
letters to Sister Matt & Uncle
Wm. Turrell — At 7 P.M. went to
church & heard a sermon from 1 John
2 chap. 14 verse — of course especially
directed to young men — Ret'd
home at 8 and for an hour tried
to write an answer to Corinne
Coleman — gave it up & chatted
with Dr. Lewis for an hour & got
to bed about 10.30 — cloudless
moonlight night, but of a very
sickly, dubious character —
