## Left Page

### Sept. Thursday, 10 1868.

*In Camp — Upper Ausable.*

Cloudy + threatening. but got things
Ready to go in to the Ausable Lakes.
About 9 o'clk the Colman party came
along + in half an hour we were all on
the Road — C's wife & sister — Tiffany, [illegible]
\+ I with Smith + Almon Reede as [illegible]
the 4 miles through the woods being bad
walking + warm. got to lower lake at
a little after 12 — built a fire + lunched
in about an hour, making a very heavy
boat load, we all went over the lake.
Showered while in the boat. Glorious
effects — all delighted — when we got to
the Camp, the Sun shone out brilliantly — warm work over the Carry —
all got to Camp about 4.30. I went
over to the Durand Camp + paid
them a visit, old Mr. D. 73 yrs. [illegible]
Carrie, Fred, Eugene + Miss Martins
are the party — They are disappointed
by the bad weather — The Mts.
are all under clouds — got to
bed + asleep before 10 o'clk.
Passed a very Comfortable night —
Rain all night heavily —

## Right Page

### Sept. Friday, 11 1868.

Cloudy all day with slight showers.
About 9.30 clouds lifted so that
we all went to work, a sort of
desperation — C. G[illegible] + I crossed
the lake + I painted a sketch
about 7×10 in., worked hard
until 1 o'clk — then Rec'd to lunch
again at my own sketch and
worked on it until about 3, when
the Durands Rowed me over
to my own Camp — There I
commenced another sketch of
same size of mts to the west
\+ worked as tight as I cd drive
as long as I could see — about
6.30 — The D's made us a short
visit — Before tea I took a Row
alone — water like a mirror. Every
thing most deliciously suggestive.
Tea by Candle light — Chatting,
lying around + whist until 10.30 —
our Shanties presenting some very
picturesque scenes — [illegible] water —
[illegible] umbrellas, as it was again Raining
and turned in at about 11 — asleep at once.
