## Left Page

### August Saturday, 29 1868.

Up at 6 o'clk. Finished sorting and
packing before 8 o'clk when I had
bkfs. Threatening a day of Rain — a
spatter falling about 7 o'clk. — about
9.30 got off for Bedee's. our party consist-
ing of Mr. Treedy, his daughters Isabell
& Mary & niece Jenny Treedy, Geo., self
& driver — before we started it began to
clear up & we got off amid excitement
& waving hdkfs from those left behind —
a hot & dusty, but altogether delight-
ful drive to Beedes where we arr'd
about 2 o'clk after making a short
call on the Colwans at Dr. Patters-
much charmed with Keene Flats
& delighted at Beedes — after dinner
went on to the hill back of house with
Mr. Enoch Lewis (He and the Whitney's m
detained here by sickness of Mrs. L.)
and got a couple of pencil sket[ches].
Evening spent out of doors chatting
and enjoying the moonlight until
after 10 o'clk — got to bed about
11 —

## Right Page

### August Sunday, 30 1868.

Mng. smoky again and unsettled
looking, but cleared up charmingly in
the course of the mng. at 10 o'clk, with
Messrs. E. Lewis, [illegible] Whitney & May Lewis
started out to walk to the Cascade on
Roaring brook, back of Smith Bedee's.
Quite a shower on our way there —
Charmed with the fall. It is about 400
feet high & very wild. Found that there
was fire in the woods at the top. Ret'd to
B's — Louis Tiffany there — the fire started
from his smudge of the day before — He
sent for Colman — I took a lunch &
with a boy of B's started for the fire —
Colman, Tiffany & Orlando Beede then
fighting fire — 5 of us with pails worked like
Beavers until near 6 o'clk — when we
got it subdued — If the fire had got beyond
our control, the destruction of the pictur-
esque wood have been fearful. Ret'd
thoroughly tired out. Gelwood made
his appearance this mng. having walked
from E. town last night — Charlie arr'd
there Sat. P.M. — Mr. L. & W. went on to
Lake Placid this P.M. — We't again
out of doors until after 10 — then to bed.
