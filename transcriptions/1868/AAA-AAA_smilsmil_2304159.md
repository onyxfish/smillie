## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 28, 1868

*Union Hall, Saratoga* — Room 65.

Up at 7. Soon after had breakfast.
at 8 saw a mil. parade & forming
of guard. Between 9 & 10 — light & heavy
artillery practice with battery
firing at target. Atmosphere very
thick & strong cool wind from N. E.
At 10.30 took steamer C. Vibbard to
Albany, where, after much careful
steaming & once just sticking in
the mud, we arr'd about 5.P.M.
Rushed to depot & got into Sarato.
Cars — behind time — would n't reach
for all the baggage — my trunk left —
Waited at Albany Junction for train
up & at Mechanicsville for train down
at Saratoga ½ an hour behind time. To
Union Hall & had tea at 8 — then
Sauntered about, went over to
Congress Hall where a "Hop"
was in progress — then to the
other Hotels, taking a drink at
the Washington — back to Congress
— there until 11, watching the
dancers & listening to the music,
then to the Union & to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 29, 1868

*Fort Wm. Henry Hotel — Lake George.* — Room 117.

Awake at 6.30. Up at 7 after a night of
sound sleep. Walked over the grounds
& about until 8 — quite a thick fog —
then breakfast. Then a chat with
Mr. Knowlton — Miss Emma Holt
& Frank Leggett. Sauntered about Congress
Hall & sat on piazza enjoying the
pomps & vanities until 11 — then,
with Geo. Strolled to the Springs, over
the grounds to the Indian encampment.
Saw a genuine gambler at his work —
back to Hotel at 1. Packed trunks
& bill & all ready to leave. Dinner
at 2. At 3 took train for Moreau. At
3.45 took Stage for Caldwell where
we arr'd at 6.30. The latter part of the
ride very pleasant but atmosphere
still very hazy — could not see more
than 2 miles out on the lake — tea
at 7 — at 7.30 went out alone and
Rowed for an hour — air delicious
& dim moonlight, very mysterious —
Spent remainder of even. on the
balcony listening to music & watching
the dancers. Met V. Anthony. To bed at 11.
