## Left Page

### March Sunday, 22 1868

Overcast, windy & Raw — Went to Rev. Thos. Hastings in the m'g & heard him preach from John. 14 "Chap. 15: V. "If ye love me, Keep my Commandments." He urged that God's love was shown in giving us commands and our truest love in loving obedience.

P.M. Spent at home, a little writing & considerable tearing up of old letters — Ev'g went to Madison Ave. Baptist Ch. & heard Alex. Vinton preach before the Y.M. men's Ch'n Ass. "For what would it profit a man if he should gain the whole world & lose his own Soul." a plain, straight forward discourse. After Service, Gio. & I walked home with Mr. E. Dodge Jun'r. and by invitation went in & stayed ber'p an hour. Saw Mrs. D — all extremely pleasant. Talked of the new Ass'n building just commenced. Home at 9.30 & to bed soon after.

## Right Page

### March Monday, 23 1868

Beautiful m'g, up bright and early, but did not feel equal to the m'g, as I had passed a restless night. Got to Studio at 8.30 kindled fire — set palette and finished outlining 16×26. "Moat Mt. — the Saco River" — but a headache, fever & what not, began so rapidly to get the better of me that at 1½ o'clk I cleaned up & started for home. Stopped & saw Sanin Gifford & [illegible]. Spent the p.m. in bed, getting no better quite fast — at 9 o'clk p.m. got my feet into hot water, & started a very successful perspiration — Rolled & swaddled myself up like a mummy & lay in my big chair until 4 o'clk a.m. when having got quite dry I crept off to bed.
