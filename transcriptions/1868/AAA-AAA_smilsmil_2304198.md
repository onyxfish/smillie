## Left Page

### October Friday, 16 1868

Gloomy mrng, but by noon, mild
& pleasant, thickening up again
at 5velt. & Raining before 10 ocl.
Ab 9 o'clk went to Coon's stable
thy working steadily, by 12 ocl.
had finished a good sized (13×[illegible])
study from a horse's head in Cray[on]
this a good likeness & pleas[ed] the
owner very much, that is, the own[er]
of the horse. Dinner at 1. at 2.30
went again to the stable to ge[t]
a study from the dog — but he m[as]
so uneasy that it was unsuccessf[ul]
I worked until 5 — then gave it [up]
walked until Tea time — at 8.30
with Geo went to Fan M's — there
until 10.30 then home & to b[ed]
at 11.30

## Right Page

### October Saturday, 17 1868

Snow storm early in the morning
that whitened the Roofs and grass —
heavily overcast sky & wind blowing
savagely — Ther. 30°. At 10 o'clk fixed
myself with extra wrappings & went
to Coon's Stable & spent the mrng
getting large sized drawing of the dog's
head — the dog & I suffered so from
cold as to make it very hard work
for us. Dinner at 1.30. Mr. Crane, a
niece of Uncle Wm's, her children & a
niece & Mr. & Hy Turrell took dinner
with us — Afternoon, finishing up dog's
head — walked between 5 & 6 — at tea,
same party as dined with us — but
they left at [illegible]. Nicholas Nickleby
until 8 — then girl & I went to Fan
Mulford's — there until 10.30 —
home & to bed at 11.30
