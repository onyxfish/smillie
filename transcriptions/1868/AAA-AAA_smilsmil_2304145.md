## Left Page

### June, Sunday, 28, 1868.

A glorious day — In the m't, went to Dr. Hastings & heard an excellent sermon from Psalm 42°. 2° u. m. thirsting for God — He spoke of the thirst as one that nothing earthly could satisfy — altho' surrounded by every earthly fulness, still craving as a figure, spoke of the water lily in the midst of the lake, yet thirsting & dying for the dews of heaven. — With Chark. went up to Uncle A's to dinner — Enjoyed the afternoon & every exceedingly — a glorious sunset. Between 8 & 9 o'clk Uncle A & Tunney walked over to 8th Ave cars with Minnie & me — got down home at about 9.20 and not to bed until 11 —

## Right Page

### June, Monday, 29, 1868.

Fine day, not hot — Up at 6. Engaged painting my sketch board — Started down town at 8.40 — went to Norton's & got Rubber cloth & got to Ritchie's at 10.30 — Worked at once burnishing — Lunched with father at 1 — then went to Higbie's & ordered flannel shirts for sketching in — Back to Ritchie's at 2.20 & worked faithfully until 6 — Home to tea at 7 — Every spent writing to Alfred Jones, Young of Boston a note of thanks to Andrew H. Green, Comp Troller of Central Park — Got to bed at about 11.30

14 days on Ritchie's sky — Tune 2 hours short to-day.
