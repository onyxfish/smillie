## Left Page

### February Sunday, 23, 1868

Ther. 40

Colder than yesterday. MNP
went to hear Rev. Hastings — He
preached from 23-4 v. 139 Psalm.
He tried to impress upon us that
there is a very mistaken sort of
self investigation. The idea, as I understood it was, not to strive to find
out what we think — what we are —
but what Christ is in us. "If me
watch our good intentions, they will
hide — if we nurse our Christian
graces, they will be sick — If we
feel our own pulses, they will always
beat irregularly." Dinner at home
Mr. E. P— with us — P.M. commenced
a letter to Fan M. Eveg, so cold,
& my throat troubling me, I did
not go out. Spent it reading
& did not get to bed until
just midnight.

## Right Page

### February Monday, 24, 1868

At studio about 9.30. Very cold,
raw & disagreeable — overcast &
fine snow flying, becoming a regular
snow storm by night-time — It was
noon before the Studio was warmed
up to 68°. I commenced Record
painting on "Moat Mt." from White-Horse
Ledge — 12th day of work on it — Home
to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 —
worked until 4.30 getting the sky
all repainted — after a fashion.
I cannot get the neutral, yet
full-color tones that I wish, and I
have too much drawing. W. Walter
Brown came in & took away little
sketch on broken palette, paying me
$15.00 for it — Eveg at home,
my throat bothering me. Dining
spent part of eveg with us.
After a little bothering with
my French lesson, got to bed
about 10.30
