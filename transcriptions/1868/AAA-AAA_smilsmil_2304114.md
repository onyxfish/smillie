## Left Page

### April, Saturday, 25, 1868.

Up at 6 o'clk. a miserable day, all with constant Rain & now & then a flurry of snow. Wrote a note to S.C.F. & studied french verbs — Got to studio at 8.45 — Kindled a fire. At 10. took french lesson — at 11.30 got to work on 20×56. (6th day) Rode home to lunch at 1. back at 2. & worked as long as I could see on painting — Got in the groups of trees on L.H. side — painted them very carefully but am not pleased with the colors. — too much of a bt. Sienna green — Home at 7. Eve'g spent studying french verbs & trans lating until 10 o'clk — Uncle Willie came from Poughkeepsie this m'f & stops with us — Mary Returned from a 2 or 3 days visit to Uncle Aaron — Got to bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### April, Sunday, 26, 1868.

Glorious day. Mrs went to Dr. Hastings — quite a jubilation on acct. of the paying off of the debt of the church — $50,000. Hastings in high spirits — "Praise God." sung in the middle of the sermon. Text, Isariah 26th 3+4: Perfect peace for him whose mind is stayed on God. Home to dinner. At 3.30 P.M. went out for my walk. Met Abm Polhemus driving his horse & light wagon. Got in with him & rode through Central Park up nearly to High Bridge — Returning. I left Polhemus in the Park and walked about home, enjoying exceedingly the charming afternoon, but annoyed by recollection of the work, so altogether unlike nature that is now on my easel. Home at 7 o'clk. quite tired — had tea — then a good nap in my chair — To bed at 11.45
