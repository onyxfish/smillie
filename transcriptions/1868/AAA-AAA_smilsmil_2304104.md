## Left Page

### April, Sunday, 5, 1868.

Up before 8 o'clk. Everything white
with snow. Sky squally & cold west
wind blowing. Charl. still continues
under the weather, the effects of cold
the Dr. thinks. Went to, & heard
Dr. Hastings in the M.F. preach from
Eph. IV ÷ 13°. He preached "perfect
manhood" — and "the measure of
the stature of the fulness of Christ."
Afternoon, feeling tired & sleepy,
gave myself up to it, very much
the same in the eveng., staying
at home & doing nothing. I have
worked hard last week, & expect
to work as hard the coming week
so I feel that I am not yielding
to indolence, but taking needed
Rest — To bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### April, Monday, 6, 1868.

Pleasant day, but windy & chilly
enough for March. Spent the
most at Studio Roughing out some
designs for Bk. Note drawings of
P.M. subjects. Took lunch
at Studio & at about 1.30 went down
town. Saw Mr. E. Gavit & had orders
to make two drawings. Walked
up to Brooks Bros., bought light overcoat
$5.—— then to Bond St. & called upon
Addie Dunning's for a few minutes.
Rode up to Studio at 6, took paste-
partouts over to Emma Ireland & then
home. At 7.30 at N.A. of D. there
until 10.15. Much Revision
being done in hanging. Home
at 10.30 & to bed at 11.
