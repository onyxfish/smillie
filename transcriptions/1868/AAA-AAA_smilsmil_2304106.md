## Left Page

### April, Thursday 9, 1868

Cold windy day. Up early & got to Studio at 8.30 but found myself more under the weather than at first I thought, with cold that has settled in my head — Spent the morning outlining But. note drawing of Sheep. Home to lunch at 1 — Aunt Olivia there, back at 2.30. Worked again for a while on drawing & at 4 while Miss Wordworth was at the Studio, went over to N.A. of D. There until 5.30 — then went to Ned Dunning's, to invite him to the house next Sat Eveg. Home at 6.10. At 8 went to N.A. of D — no one on hand until after 9 — Much confusion & diversity — the men kept at work until near 11, tearing down & hanging up. Got home thoroughly tired out. Got hot water & sat wrapped up with my feet in it until 12 o'clk., when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 10, 1868

When I got up at 6.30, a mid-winter looking snow storm in full operation. At least 4 in. of snow fell, some say 6 — After 9 A.M. it turned to sleet & during the p.m. it rained heavily — By eveg. most of the snow gone & stopped raining. After stopping at Studio & getting 7×9 "In the Caco" belonging to Uncle Aaron, went to N.A. of D. Worked hard all the mg. re-hanging water colors in Corridor. At 12.30, went to Studio & got lunch — Young Bill there, drawing — Back to N.A. at 1.30 — But little remained to be done which was finished up in about an hour. Loafed for another hour, met McSutee, then Retd to Studio — Saw Fairman & told him concerning the hanging of his picture — as the Committee had dissolved & information was not contraband. Home at 6.30 — Eveg spent doing nothing. Dr. D.D. Smith called with Dr. Throop & very carefully examined Charl., His trouble, more Enteritis than Gastritis.
