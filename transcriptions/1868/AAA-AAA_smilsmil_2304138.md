## Left Page

### June, Sunday 14, 1868

Clear day & very warm — Did not
get up until late, trying to find
the pleasure of lying in bed —
M'f, went down to Madison Ave.
Baptist Ch, & heard Rev. W. Elder
of Orange, N.J. His text was, Jacob
wrestling with the Angel — He gave
us a very good sermon on Prayer.
After service Charl & I walked up
to 8th ave. & took a car to Manhattan vist
Joe & Will at Uncle Aaron's — Spent the
p.m. under the big horse chestnuts
rest of the house. Ed — Dudley and
Seymour — juvenile admirers of
Tuney at the house to tea — Spent
the eveg on west piazza & to bed
at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 15, 1868

Awake at day break — Every thing
very charming, but did not get up
until near 6 o'clk — then got a bite
of bread & butter & after that took "Gyp"
under the saddle. Rode up to High
Bridge — inspected the new Reservoir
in course of construction — then back
to H'pt at 7.45 — Rather too hot for
pleasure — at 8.30 with Uncle A. Rode
over to 8th Ave & took car down — got to
studio at about 10 o'clk. & set to work
etching on die for the Ottawa folks.
[illegible] day — Ritchie called — home to lunch
at 1 — nobody at home — Matt & Kate went
this M'f to Po'keepsie — Mother & many
a visit — Annie Ballard at Nooram —
Back at 2 & etched on die until 6.30
taken by 8 Ave. cars, I went up to Uncle
A's — got there before me — Met there an
Atlanta, Ga., cousin El. V. V. — Spent
the eveg on East piazza — and
to bed at 10.30
