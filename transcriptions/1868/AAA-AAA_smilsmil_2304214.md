## Left Page

### November Saturday, 21 1868

overcast, Raw \+ threatening — [illegible]
Got to Studio just before 9 o'clk —
Allman there soon after — He
accepted the offer \+ paid me
$100 — then got to work on drawing
of the Arms of Iowa — for A.B. N. Co.
Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.15
\+ worked as long as I could see,
until 4.30. on drawing — Maj. n
Bundy called, gathering material
for an article for the Atlantic, was
also Dr. Fingsley \+ a couple of
friends \+ Ned Demming — also
Sam'l. Colman, who left tickets
to the N.d. of D. Private occ. Mon.
ev'g next for the Tweedys — at
5.30 I walked down there with
them \+ had a short \+ pleasant
call — then went to Snedicor's
\+ paid him $53.00 — Home to
tea at 6.45 — Eve'g — a little
Reading \+ not much of anything
\+ to bed about 11.30

## Right Page

### November Sunday, 22 1868

Cloudy, windy \+ cold — got up late,
feeling rather under the weather —
m'f went to hear Rev. Tho. Hastings — He
preached a most excellent \+ powerful
sermon from 1 John. Chap V. verses 4 \+ 5.
It was very manly \+ Christian in char-
acter to me — After Service, went
with Charl. up to Uncle Aaron's to
dinner — found there Aunt H. who
came from Montrose last Thursday —
After dinner a short nap \+ a little
before 5 Charl \+ I started \+ walked
down home getting in at 6.25 —
Eve'g went to hear J. P. Thompson
He discoursed on the "True Church
— where to find it" Taking for his
text Ephesians IV chap 4–7 verses.
Thought it good, simple and
liberal — walked home \+ that is,
to the cor. of 41st \+ [illegible] with Charley Adams
[illegible], Uncle Willie arr'd from
Poughkeepsie this m'g — Got to
[illegible] at 11.15 —
