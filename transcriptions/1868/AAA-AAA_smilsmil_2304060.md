## Left Page

### January Wednesday, 8 1868.

Up late, feeling headachy — weather
miserable — Did not get to studio
until 9.45. Commenced Charcoal
ing on Academy picture. 33×44½
"Moat Mt.- from White Horse Ledge"
worked away until 1 o'clk — Took
lunch at Studio & at 2 Rode down
to Uncle J's & had imp. of mouth taken
for "permanent set" of teeth — Then
went to Brooks Bros. & ordered suit
of clothes — then to E. J. Dunning's &
paid him bal. of $28.00. Then to
Schaus's & p'd. him $80.00 — Then to
Snedecors' — He has just sold a 7×9 in
sketch of mine with $12.00 for 60.00
a larger price than I dare ask. Then
home — Rec'd. note from Alfred Jones
enclosing vigt. for me to etch. At
8.30 went to N.A. of D. Opening night
of Pictures that have been to Paris.
American Contribution to Exposition
Home at 10.50 & to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### January Thursday, 9 1868.

A very cold windy day, with snow
squalls — Night remarkably clear
& cold. Got to studio before 9 o'clk
& set to work upon Charcoaling —
Between 12 & 1 o'clk. Miss Kate
Drinker & Miss Duval called and
I was kept until 1.30 when I went
home to lunch — Ret'd. to Studio
about 2.45 & during remainder
of p.m. worked at Charcoaling
getting my composition quite
into shape — about 4.30 Mr. E.
Gavit came in with drawings
& die pfs. for me to advise about.
He was very gracious & flattered
me & my work — Mr. C. P. Cranch
also called — went to Gymnasium
& worked for an hour. Found Uncle
Frank, Aunt Jessie at our house to tea.
They left about 9.30 — Joe & Will vis-
ited us until near 11 — Got to
bed soon after.
