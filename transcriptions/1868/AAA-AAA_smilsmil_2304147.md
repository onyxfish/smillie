## Left Page

### July, Thursday 2, 1868

Overcast wt, clearing off very hot at about 10.30. Got
to Pitchie's at 9.15 and put to work as usual, burnishing.
At 1 went to lunch, at 1.3 called on Gavit, had a talk
with him "concerning" wood drawing & business connected
with Father. Back to P's at 2.30 & stuck to it until 6.15
then home. At 8.30 called to see J.C.7. at Mr. Zabriskie's
131 W. 21st St. Saw her & Mr.
3 — Still very hot, but a
slight breeze — left at 9.45
On my way home cooled
off with an orange ice —
To bed at 11.

17 days on Pitchie's sky
full time to day.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 3, 1868

Another very hot day. Got to Pitchie's
at 9.20. An imp. had already been
taken from my sky — It disappoints
me very much — it looks roady, and
the ribbing or pattern shows in
spite of all the burnishing I have
done. Mr. P. under the circumstances
is extremely amiable and kind,
knowing that I have been unable to
finish paintings, that but for his
sky, I would. He offered to loan me
$300 in addition to the $250 that
he today paid me. I thanked him
sincerely, but my dread of borrowing
money is so deep rooted that, altho'
very hard up, I declined his offer.
About 4 o'clk — a heavy thunder storm
came up, but we got very little of the rain —
at 4.30 had a 2nd imp. of sky. At near 6
went home — had tea & at 7.30 took
stage & rode to Uncle Alvin's — Got
there about 8.30 — Geo. Matt, Will &
Joe all there — Billiards with Will
and got to bed about 11 o'clk —
