## Left Page

### August Friday, 21 1868.

Delightful day, though very warm.
A party made up for Mt. Discovery,
but as I had an incipient headache
I did not care to develop it by climbing
a mt. — So, went to my work on the
River, notwithstanding invitations. —
Painted all the m'f on foliage
of the oak tree, against the sky —
Got into a muddle — could get
neither color or touch — to dinner
at one, + back to study again in
about an hour. — Light of course
much changed, but I painted over
the m'f, observing the forms & color
about me. — Enjoyed exceedingly the
delicious air, color + all about me —
happy, just to sit + absorb the beauty
\+ fulness. — I feel at times, unworthy
the glorious privilege of living + enjoying.
Evef, among other things, amused ourselves
with Planchette — in answer to the question
"What does Mr. Smillie desire most in this
world" — it wrote — "Digestion." — "Is he enjoying?"
"Yes" — "To whom?" — "Art." "Will he ever
marry?" — "No" — There is my fate —
To bed about 11.30

## Right Page

### August Saturday, 22 1868.

After a very cool night, another warm
day. M'f, went to my study, about
9 ock. + after painting over the foliage
on upper R.h. Cor. — I went to work on
the butternut tree that crosses the
picture, but only got it finished
about half way down. At dinner time,
afternoon, took water colors and
in the meadow back of the house
made a sketch of Cobble and
other hills. — Altho' not successful
I obtained enough to encourage
and tantalize me. Evef, Mr.
Blackman the artist came along
on his way to leave — Ferguson and
he to go on, on Monday. — During
the evef we labored away at
some of the old games + got to
bed after 11.
