## Left Page

### February Saturday, 29 1868.

Squally day, but in the main, clear — wind strong from N.W. & quite cold. At studio's at 9.15 — occupied with french lesson until after 11 o'clk — Alfred Jones made his parting call — off for Ottawa to-morrow evef. Home to lunch at 1. back at 2.15. Worked as long as I could see, but did not get my stent done. Painted on R.h. fore-ground trees & back of them — I think this part of the picture is composed badly — the tree forms are confused. Young Bell took his drawing lesson — Pattison f. S' Sorus called to say goodbye. He is off for home — Uncle Wm J. called in the mf. — Evef at home Uncle A.N.V. & Junie to tea — a soirée dansante at Dodworth's — French & writing exercises until after 10 o'clk — To bed at about 11.

## Right Page

### March Sunday, 1 1868.

Got up about 9 o'clk. with a very heavy head — mf mild & clear, but by noon it became overcast and about 2 o'clk commenced snowing which kept up until dusk. Went in the mf & heard Rev. T. Hastings. He preached from the same text that he used last Sunday morning. 139 Psalm. 23 & 4th v. Continuing the same line of thought — study Christ do not study self. The desire of the Psalmist was, that his ways should be tried, proven — to see whether they were like the Master's. After service, rode up to Uncle A.N.'s by 8th ave. cars. Took dinner, chatted & got rid of time until 4.30 o'clk. when I started for home. Walked all the way in 1½ hour. The walking disagreeable — but many of the winter effects — in the minor key, very charming — Evef at home. doing nothing — To bed at about 10 o'clk —
