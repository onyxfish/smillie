## Left Page

### August Tuesday, 25 1868

A day much like yesterday, only not so thick in the afternoon.
My got to work on my study shortly after 9 o'clk. — Selected
a spot a little back of my ordinary seat so as to bring in a new bit
of foreground in the lower R. h. cor. and set to work to paint
it in. Returned to it again in the afternoon about 4 o'clk. May
Lewis & Daisy Sherman came with the boat & I had a little Row —
about 5. Misses Jennie & Belle Treedy gave me a call — half an hour later
Messrs Whitney & Lewis came along.
Mr. W. has paid me & my study flattering attention. I worked until after
the sun set. Ev'lf Rec'd a letter from Fan M. Passed the time
until 10 o'clk chatting in the parlor. Then washed up brushes &
some little writing and to bed shortly after 11.

## Right Page

### August Wednesday, 26 1868

Up early — Shortly after 7 a.m. —
Coleman, his wife, sister, father-in-law
& Louis Tiffany got off for Keene Flats.
The m'f bright & promising hot day,
which was fully realized. The Ther.
standing 80° at noon. After H'fst.
Mr. Mrs. Whitney & daughter & Mr. & Mrs.
Lewis & daughter got off for a Ride
through the mts & to some of the Lakes.
At 9:30 got off to my work. Spent the
m'f painting in tree trunks & the little
birch tree foliage. Dinner about 1 —
at 2:30 got off again with watercolors
to finish sketch of Cobble Hill commenced
a few days ago. Air very thick with
smoke, & sky full of fine cloud forms
giving some fine effects of the [illegible]
sive order. Finished my sketch
after a fashion. Ev'lf a violent
N.E. wind. Smoke thicker than ever.
In the parlor until 10:30. Got
to bed about 11.
