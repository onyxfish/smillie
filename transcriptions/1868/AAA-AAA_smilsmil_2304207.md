## Left Page

### November Tuesday, 3 1868.

A beautiful clear morning —
Immediately after bkf't, Father,
Gel. & I went to the polls in our
district, but so many men ahead
of us that we determined to wait
until noon. I finished Salady
drawing for A.B.N. Co., mounted it
& stretched paper for another draw
before 12 o'clk. Then went to N.A. of D.
& saw T.A. Richards — then to Studio &
saw Augero — then to Burling's & saw
him, then home to lunch. & at 1.30
Father, Ge. & I, went again to the poll &
after waiting an hour, got in our vote,
worked the rest of p.m. on Bull for
A.B.N. Co. — Eve'g. Quarterly meeting
of Water Color Socy. at Colman's
Studio — no quorum until 9 o'clk.
business until 10.45 — Gel. elected a
member. At 11 went to 5th Ave.
Hotel a crowd, but little excitement.
Home about 11.30 & to bed shortly
after midnight. At noon, Mother's
Shawl stolen through front window.

## Right Page

### November Wednesday, 4 1868.

Another delightful, mild day,
but I am very nearly used up
with a headache — Fought it
through, drawing on Bull for
A.B.N. Co. — at noon, on the
sofa for an hour, then again
to work — From 5 until 7.30
on the bed — at 8.15, after
getting a little brush up and
feeling somewhat better, went
to 152 W. 44th St. & spent the
rest of the eve'g. with Tom,
Sarah & Mr. Fessenden — Home
at 10 o'clk. & to bed at 10.45.
Gel. went to call on the
Treadys &c.
