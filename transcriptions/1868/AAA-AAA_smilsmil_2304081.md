## Left Page

### February Wednesday, 19 1868

Snow flurry until about 10 a.m. when
it cleared off — Rest of day, clear and
bright. Feel quite "played out," my
head feels light, my throat & bowels
sore & my back weak. — In my room
all the m[or]f, Reading, with a little
exercise on back piazza. — At 3 o'clk
went to studio & at 3.30 — with Geo.
& Mrs. Brevoort, took our first French
lesson, in Brevoort's Room, from Mlle.
d'Asnoé. It is now 15 yrs since
I commenced French. — In that time
I have learned & forgotten nearly
all that I ever knew of it. — [Petty?]
home at 4.30, feeling better for the
exercise. — Uncle Wm J. took
tea with us. Spent the evng.
brushing up on English Grammar
& studying French Verbs —
wrote a note to S. L. F. & got to
bed at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### February Thursday, 20 1868

Up at 7.15. Wea. clear, mild & Spring
like. — At Studio a few minutes
before 9 — by 10, at work upon 7×9
"On the Saco" — winding stream in
centre — trees on either side — beach
& bushes in foreground. — worked away
until 12.45 when I went home to lunch
— back at 2 — worked faithfully
altho' I felt weak & we had quite
a number of callers & loungers —
Gramins made a long call. Mr. & Mrs.
— Smith came in about [4?] & Father
a little before. Just before 5. I
finished up, somewhat to my liking.
At 5.30 went to gymnasium and
worked for ¾ of an hour — find myself
very weak. Home to tea at 6.30. Spent
evng. studying French — My throat
troubling me. Alfred Jones was
expected to spend the evng with us,
but sent word that he could not come.
To bed about 10.
