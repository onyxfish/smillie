## Left Page

### August Wednesday, 19 1868

*Hy. Ferguson arr'd at Mansion House.*

overcast mtf, and threatening Rain —
Did not feel very well. Clark, going
over to the mines offered to take me
along — Joined him gladly — Rode
first to "Nigger Hill," where on the top
they are working out some ore — then
on to Black Pond, where, after a row
on the pond, we took lunch — then
to Bert's, where there is quite a hole —
neither of these veins are being worked
for the sake of the ore, but only to
determine quantity & richness. I
procured some very fine specimens
of shot ore — The ore through here is very
rich yielding 80 p. cent of Iron,
but it is so crudely worked, that
the forges here — (there are no smelting
furnaces) but 50 p. cent of the 80 is
got out — Rode home over Simonds
Hill — Got a couple of outline sketches —
Home about 8 o'clk — Drew some
toilsome efforts at a Romp — to bed
at 10.30 — The day proved to be glorious
for our Ride — Sunshine and Shadow —
we drove about 26 miles —

## Right Page

### August Thursday, 20 1868

Rain last night — threatening mtf, air
close & sultry — Ther. 78° — last Sunday
night we had quite a frost — Enough to
nip potato vines in exposed positions.
Got to my sketching place about 9 o'clk
before 11 it began to rain — Sat through
it until 1 o'clk painting the branches &
twigs that lace over the sky — Then
home to dinner — P.M. heavy clouds
until 6.30 when we had a glorious
sunset — Spent the p.m. chatting
on piazza & making a water color
sketch, Representing first by Clarke
of our Charade Nightingale —
Every, again hard work to keep
the ball rolling — but the effervescence
is over — Got to bed about 11 o'clk,
very sleepy.
