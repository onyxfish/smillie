## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 1, 1868

A good specimen of an April day. Very mild, breezy and dusty, with
soft showers in the evening. Started for Studio at 8, but stopping at several
places, the barber's among others, did not get to Studio until about 10. Set to
work upon 16×26 (4th day) but made little progress as I felt very unlike
work. Aaron McCabe made his appearance at 1 went home to lunch with me.
After lunch I went to bed & slept soundly until 3 o'clk — then went to Studio. Found
that Mr. E.J. Woolsey had made a long call. Took French lesson from 3.30 until
4.30 — then at work again on painting until 6. Home at 7 — went to N.A of D.
at 8. & Reported myself for duty on the Hanging Committee. Messrs Huntington,
Whittridge, E. Johnston, E. White, McEntee
& T.A. Richards on hand. I worked
with Mr. E. White until a few minutes past 10. then home very tired & got to
bed at 11. My picture was complimented
& hung in a good place.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 2, 1868

Muggy mrf, threatening Rain. —
Until after 10 o'clk writing notices to
the press of our Studio Reception, next
Sat. Got to Studio at 10.40 & worked
until 1, on 16×26 (5th day) Home to lunch
back at 2 when it began to Rain
heavily. Young Bell was on hand
& took a drawing lesson. A.H. Ritchie
called, concerning the sky that I am
putting in for him on a large plate.
I painted in all centre & R. hand
middle distance & R.h. water, working
until near 6. The shower cleared off
beautifully about 5 — At 6.30 home to
tea, at 7.15 went to N.A. of D. through
a very spiteful shower. Worked
away at hanging pictures, almost
alone in Small West Room until
10 o'clk. then walked home very
much tired — got to bed at 11 —
glorious crisp moonlight night.
