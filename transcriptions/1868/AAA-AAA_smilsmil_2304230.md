## Left Page

### December Friday, 11 1868

Cold, overcast day, threaten[ing] more snow \- got to Studio at [illegible] to work at once on Charcoal drawing. much pleased with the working of the Charcoal \+ the paper (English Crayon.) \+ hope to work on a subject [with] effect that will bring me Cre[dit]. Took lunch at the Studio \+ at 2.15 went to Phil. Rehearsal \- Enjoyed the B flat Symphony [of] Haydn's \- but the "Semiramide" overture by Catill \+ the Symphonic Poem "On the mountain" by Liszt were very uninteresting to me. The last was a great disappoint-ment, for, admiring Liszt as I do, I had hoped for a treat \- Back to Studio at 4.45. Worked on drawing by gaslight until 7.30 \- then home \+ had tea quietly at 8 \- wrote note to Jo. Gavit and to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### December Saturday, 12 1868

Brilliant, clear \+ cold day \- got to Studio at 9 o'clk \+ worked diligently on Charcoal drawing until 12 o'clk. Then took a bite of bread \+ tea \- Fan M. came at 12.15 \+ at once we set off for the "Theatre Francais" \- getting there at about 12.40 \- Got two fine front seats in Box 2 \- Fan has never been or heard anything of this sort, so to her it was mingled shock \+ pleasure \- very Freitchy \+ funny. Out about 4 o'clk \- walked down to the S.N. \- there for ½ an hour \- at 5 started for Studio \- there at 5.30. met Meta Adams just going \- chatted with her 20 m. or more \- lighted up in Studio \+ tried to draw but soon found that there was no work in me \- Home to tea at 8 \- the Remainder of weft, lazily lolling in Easy Chair \- to Bed about 11 o'clk.
