## Left Page

### October, Tuesday, 20, 1868

Still dull and disagreeable — chilly,
raw & feeling like snow — [illegible] late
as usual — At 9 went to Smith's to
oversee Beck as he makes up some
sketching traps under my direction.
About 11 came home & commenced
another head of a dog in Crayon —
a combination of the studies that
I have already made — worked
until dinner time — after dinner
Read awhile Nicholas Nickleby then
to work again, but soon interrupted
by a call from Mr. Joseph Drinker —
Shortly after 4 walked down town with
him & again went to Beck's — There
until after 5 — then home — at 7:45
went to the Jessup's to make a call —
Saw Fanny & her friend Miss Borden —
& Mary Saulsberg & Huntting's wife and
4 baby — there until 9 — then went
to Fan. Mulford's — did not leave
there until near 11 — then home
and to bed — Bet. 5 & 6 o'clk [illegible]
Called on Mother Farrell — Mr. Crane took
dinner with us —

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday, 21, 1868

Gloomier than yesterday. Rain
beginning to fall about 1 o'clk & keeping
up steadily for the rest of the day
thereof. At 9 o'clk — went to see Beck
& my traps — have determined to have
a trunk made here so spent the
rest of the m[orning] Running about from
Smith's to Fordham's & from there to
Foster's & then to the Tannery to see
to getting all of the necessary material
& help — about noon — home to dinner —
worked on dog's head that I commenced
yesterday until near 4 o'clk, then
again went to Beck's — There until
after 5 — then home — Tea at 6 —
Evening spent writing & among
others, to Corinne Coleman. Rec'd
a letter from Will, answered
it by Return mail — Got to
bed at 11 o'clk —
