## Left Page

### June, Thursday 18, 1868

Another very hot day — got to Studio before 9 o'clk — worked on Ottawa die with graver
picking the etching into shape.
Enjoy sitting at my Studio window, overlooking Madison Sq. A pleasant breeze blowing
in all day & the songs of birds in the park, easily heard —
Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 —
worked on die until 5 — then fixing up about Studio until
7 — Home to tea at 7.15 — After tea, amused myself weeding
& cutting grass in back yard —
M. & Mrs. Node called for about ½ hour. Read until 9.30 —
got to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 19, 1868

Thick My, but cleared off to be another very hot day — Went down-town
in the My, expecting to find Ritchie ready for me, as agreed, but as he
had no pf. I could do nothing — Went to Pate's in Fulton St. & had etching
pfs of my Ottawa die. Poor pfs. Back
to Studio at 11.20 — Set to work to finish Wool Yard drawing for A.B.N. Co.
About 12. Young Haven came in — then
young Bell — gave him a drawing lesson
about 12.30. Emma Ireland & — Cook
called to see if I could join their
Adirondack Party for July 13–5 Aug 1st.
To my great regret, I cannot do it.
Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.15 — worked
all the P.M. on drawing & got it done.
Home to tea at 7 — Evng. Spent squaring
accts, mounting drawings & writing
a letter to Uncle Willie —
got to bed about midnight.
