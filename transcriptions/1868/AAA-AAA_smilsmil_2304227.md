## Left Page

### December Tuesday, 29 1868.

Another night of unrest —
Did not get to Studio until
10 o'clk & then felt like anything
but work — outlined or drawing
until 10'clk — & went home
about 2, for the day, feeling
used up. The weather, of
the meanest possible
composition — wind E. — and
a muzzle of fog & Rain —
Stopped at Flemings and
brought a copy of Dr. Lübke's
"History of Art" — 2 vols — $20 oo
an extravagant proceeding — but
I consider it a legitimate business
outlay — tools — or material. or such
like — Spent the P.M. between
a sofa & my bed — Every, as
bright as the day has been — Got
to bed at 9!

## Right Page

### December Wednesday, 30 1868.

and spent another miserable
night. In bed until noon.
During the M[orning], entertained
Mr. Jno. E. Gavit at my bed side
up at 12 with a sore & heavy
head — my throat being rather
better — Dinner at 1 — In
the afternoon prepared a
number of our Studio Reception
circulars, each with appropriate
little note & sent to Messrs. Whitney
& Lewis of Phila — Mrs. Hall & misses
Graupner & Curtis of Boston & to
several newspaper men in this
city — About 4 P.M. walked down
to Station "G." with them. Day
overcast & disagreeable — Eve[ning]
lay about, Reading some in
Lübke's History of Art — Gel. &
Charl went to the Speedy's — as
Miss Jennie [S.] is in town for a day
or two — Sister Mary still much
under the weather — Got to bed
about 10 o'clk —
