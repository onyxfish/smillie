## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 10, 1868

Pleasant day, wind from the East all
day, weather prospects unsettled.
After ordering a pair of boots at
Burgraff's — went to A.B.N.Co's & got
pf of my die. Still very unsatisfactory
— back to studio at 11. Burnished
tin some places, stoned down
the die — Home to lunch at 1 —
back at 2 — Gil was not called
upon to day for jury duty. I
felt so heavy & under the weather
after lunch as to be unfit for
work — About 3.30 Meta Gavit
came in — at 6, I put her into
a 5th Ave. Stage. Took no French
lesson, as none of us were prepared.
Home to tea at 7.15 — Spent most
of the eveg at Flemings — Dave
Smillie honored us with a short
visit — To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 11, 1868

The wettest, wickedest, windiest sort of
a rain storm all day long — got up with
threatenings of a headache — hung about
at home until 9.30 — then took Car and
rode down to A.B.N.Co. — there all day
working on die — also some patch
work on a back-die — at 1 o'clk.
went to Parker's to lunch & there
met Father — At 5.30, submitted
my die to Mr. Gavit — got but little
satisfaction from him, as he was
not in a humor to look at it —
it was passed however — so if I
get the amount I ask for it, I
must be satisfied. Walked up
from 10th St. stopping at 5th Ave. Hotel
to see if the Pierce sisters were there
from Boston — did not see them.
Home at 7.15 thoroughly wet. Mr. & Mrs.
Node at tea — quite a jolly eveg,
with them & their boy Harry.
To bed at about 11 —
Have spent 13 days on A.B.N. Co's die.
