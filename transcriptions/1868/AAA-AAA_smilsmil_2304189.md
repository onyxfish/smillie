## Left Page

### Sept. Monday, 28 1868.

A mild-like, gusty morning with spiteful flurries of Rain — my stock
of patient waiting almost used up —
Dr. Lewis + sisters got off in a Rain
storm about 9 o'clk — Shortly after, the
weather changed astonishingly, coming
off clear, breezy + mild — I had commenced
some water color drawing in-doors, but
packed up my oil traps + started for
Rico's Mill to get a study of Boulders.
Started one 9×15 + worked until 1.30
when I hurried home to dinner + came
back, but altho' I worked as tight
as I could spring, I could not
finish it before the sun went down.
Washed + cleaned up + back to tea
a little after 6. Very rather
used up, feeling tired + headachy.
Moved over to our new Room, No.
18 in the Mansion House + got
to bed a little after 10 —

## Right Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 29 1868.

Too blustering, cold + Rainy to work out
of doors, so accepted Neilson's invitation
to spend the day with him. Immediately
after breakfast — we started for Russia Forge + there saw
the hammering of "loot" — then over Simmond's
Hill, from which we saw glorious color, the
sun being out, — to Black Pond + then to Burt
Hill — There until 2 o'clk. + took dinner —
Then to Moriah + visited the wonderful
Iron Mines, went down 21" — "old bed" +
"Miller Pit." Exceedingly impressed by
what I saw. At 5.30 saw wonderful effect
looking over Champlain to Vermont and
Green Mts. Remarkable for color + light +
shade — Drove 6 miles along the shore of
the lake — Saw moonrise over the lake —
got to West Port at 7.10 — Tea at 7.30 and
until 10 o'clk, waiting for N. — who was
giving testimony before a Referee — walked
down to the lake — Glorious night, milder,
after the snow flurries of the day —
Shortly after 10 started for home, had
a fine, cold Moonlight Ride, getting
in at 11.30 — To bed shortly after 12 —
