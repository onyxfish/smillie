## Left Page

### May Tuesday, 19, 1868

Another dull miserable day, with drizzle enough most of the time to keep the walks wet. got to studio at 9, and setting to work on A.B.N. Co's etching, finished it before I went home to lunch at 1 o'clk. Back at 2.45 — went to Hillicks to order a frame, could not find him, — bought a small brush for stopping out — then to studio & set to work on B.A.B.N. Co's die — & etched on it until near 6.30 getting in a good big piece. Home to tea at 7 — Spent the every writing conjugations of french verbs & exercises — To bed at 10 — Mother is improving, but slowly — Her trouble is indigestion.

## Right Page

### May Wednesday, 20, 1868

Still bad weather — At 8 o'clk. went with father to Thomas's Rooms where father sat for a photograph — At 8.30 started for down-town, getting to Ritchie's at 9.15. Went right to work on his big plate, dry-pointing on the sky — Very tiresome work as I had to lie over the plate in an uncomfortable position. Rain commenced before noon — by 4.30 I was so tired & my fingers so sore that I could work no longer. At 5. with Mr. Ritchie went up to Union League Rooms to see his painting "Death bed of Lincoln" — at 6, went to studio — At 7.15, home to tea — Evening spent in mother's Room doing the whole of nothing — my Room being occupied — To bed about 10.

3½ days on Ritchie's sky.
Short time to-day — 1 hour.
