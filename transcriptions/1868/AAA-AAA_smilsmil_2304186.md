## Left Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 22 1868.

Unpromising mrng. which went
into a cold, overcast & blustering
day. Symptoms of a headache
which I fought against all day
not feeling color-wise. I worked
up a broad pencil sketch of
"Giants" that I started at Reed's.
at noon took a stroll for an hour
or more. Afternoon, worked a while
on & finished Giant sketch. At
4 o'clk. Started for the top of
Raven Mt. & by going straight for
it without troubling about a path
got up in 1 hour 20 m. from time
of starting from the house. There
for a few minutes, enjoying Lake
Champlain to the East & mountains
to the west. Took observations by
Compass of some land marks, and
at 5.15 started down, getting home
at 6.20, being gone less than 2½ hrs.
Every got into a long ante-[illegible] talk
with Mr. Clay, so that it was near
midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 23 1868.

Last night at 1.30 a.m. awakened by a
furious hurricane that seemed to
threaten to tumble our crazy old house
down about our ears. It blew hard
most of the night. Up about 7. Another
raw, blustering, overcast day. Set
to work & finished water color study
of Lower Ausable Lake. At noon,
with Misses Belle & Mary Tweedy &
[illegible], walked for 1½ hours. After
— Rec'd a letter (comical) from
Corinne Colman & after dinner
Smith Reede drove up with the
Colmans & Gilbert Burling. I
worked a while on my drawing.
[illegible] went up with a party to Split
Rock. I wrote a letter to Charl
acknowledging the Receipt of the
two P.O. Money orders for $50 each
from the Colemans. Tweedy, [illegible]
Williamses & Gilbert Burling spent
most of the evening in our Room.
Refreshments, an empty whiskey bottle &
one big English walnut. To bed before 11.
