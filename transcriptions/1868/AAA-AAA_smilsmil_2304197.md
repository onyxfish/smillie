## Left Page

### October Wednesday, 14 1868.

a very miserable thick, mizzling
day — Up at 6.30 — got fire started
& read until 6&[illegible]. at 8 o'clk. Co-
g went to Strond's Stable to tell
Briscoe that it was too dark and
cold to work — but B. was not on ha[nd]
walked about & Returning, wrote a
letter to Mary. At 12 again went
out & walked until 2 — after dinner
tried some drawing, but as my
head felt very uncomfortable
I lay down & slept until after
4 — At 5 went out & walked for
an hour — tea at 6.30. Ev[en]g
at 8 went to P.O. & got a letter
from Father — then to Fau M[illegible]
there until 10 — then home —
Read until 11 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### October Thursday, 15 1868.

Another mild mizzling day — Up
late & slept at 8.30 — Mrs[illegible] wrote a
letter to Father & about 11 o'clk after
drawing out a plan for a paint-
ing, oil colors, went to the Cabinet
maker Smith with it, to get Beck
to do it for me — Spent some time
there — then went to Fordham's to
see about a knapsack — Spent the
remainder of the m[orning] walking
about in the mud — Dinner at
1 o'clk — Between 3 & 4 feeling very
heavy took a nap for half an hour —
At 4 went to Tarbell's & Strond's stable
but their horses were out — then went
to Coon's Stable & got started a
sketch of his horse's head, & got a
small sketch from a fine New-
foundland dog, belonging to the
hostler — Tea at 6.30. From 7 to 8 —
Uncle Wm Read Nicholas Nickleby to
us — from 8 until 11 I wrote — to Charl
& Corinne Coleman — Then I went
to bed —
