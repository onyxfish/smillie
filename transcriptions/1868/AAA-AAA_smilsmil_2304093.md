## Left Page

### March Saturday, 14 1868.

Up at 7, feeling "springy" on my way to Studio, called on Brevoortwho has been laid up with a cold for 2 or 3 days — Got to Studio at 9.30 & found there Mr. Mc. Birney, Secy. prf. Men's Ch. Ass'n. asking me to call at Renwick's, 88 Wall St. at 11. A.M. Took French lesson. Mr. B. Geo. & I, in our own Studio — At 10.45, with Jo. Fairman started for Renwick's office. There met W. 2. Dodge, Junt. Mr. Birney, Whittndge, Renwick & Sands — Saw all of the plans and designs of the new Ass'n. Building — The 4th & 5th Stories are to be Studios — W. F. & I were consulted as to the Requirements — Took lunch with Father back to Studio at 2. Worked a while on Photo of Hy Fessenden — then Charcoaled 20×36 — "On the Saco, at N. Conway." Uncle Wm & aunt H. at Studio — Home at 7 — at 8, went to Tom Fessendens, 152 W. 44th — with Photos of W. F. Saw there Mrs. Warren, they seemed pleased with what I had done — then went to Diven's — there I met Geo. 4 or 5 ladies in — there until 10.15 — then home & to bed a little after 11 o'clk. —

## Right Page

### March Sunday, 15 1868.

Dull, warm wea. Did not get up until near 9, feeling heavy & headachy. Felt better after a bath and thorough scrubbing with horse hair mittens — went to D. Hastings's, he preached from Isaiah 44th chap. 5 v. "One shall say, I am the Lord's;" etc — He desired to show the danger of connecting oneself with the church through mere formality that from a spirit desiring to dedicate itself to the Lord — At 1 o'clk, with Geo. went to Uncle Aarons — Very mild & sunny — After dinner we strolled over the place & were in ecstacies over the beauties of form, color, light & atmosphere. Got home to tea at 6.30. Did not go out again, but spent the evening lazily, as my head feels heavy — To bed at 11 after a hot water foot bath.
