## Left Page

### December Sunday, 13 1868.

Another bright cold day. Up ab 9 o'clk. Went to hear Rev. D. Hastings — as usual. His text was Lamentations 1. Ch. 12. v. "Is it nothing to you, all ye that pass by" His Reference was to the "Communion" \+ the position of those that do not partake. He was very earnest, but I did not think altogether logical. Dinner at home. Mary \+ the Dr. with us. Afternoon about the house. W. St. Parker \+ B. Spent part of P.M. \+ Evelf with Matty. After tea I went down to 5th. Hotel and visited with Fan until about 11 o'clk. Then walked home \+ at once to bed.

## Right Page

### December Monday, 14 1868.

Overcast day. Got to Studio about 9 \+ kindled fire. Miss Devoy, a teacher, came \+ begged some lessons of me in drawing — especially for handling of the pencils. I gave her some hints. Worked on Charcoal drawing until 1 o'clk. Then home to lunch. Back at 2. On drawing until after 4. Walked down to 14 St. Then home. Tea. After tea walked up to Diven's in 52d. St. Saw Maud \+ Ella their Mother. There until 9. Walked down to 14th \+ 5th Ave. to Avery's. Its opening night. Quite a collection of artists \+ press people. A charmingly fitted up place with some pretty good pictures. A pleasant little supper at 11. Walked up home at 11.30. To bed about midnight. Have walked to day just about 9 miles.
