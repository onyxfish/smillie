## Left Page

### December Monday, 7 1868

wind \+ rain, a driving, blustering
outrageous day. Got to the Studio
at 9, kindled fire \+ Som got to
work on o'fen, painting in the
left hand animal. Sait's
his friend, Mr — called between
9 \+ 10 o'clk. At 1, in spite of slom
I went home to lunch. back
at 2 worked as long as I could
see. about 4 o'clk. At 5.30 it
had stopped raining. went to
Marry's in 34 [illegible] — there met
Matt. Kate \+ Charl — all home
to tea at 6. Evely, with Cham
went to the Theatre Francaise
\+ saw \+ heard "Genevieve de Brabant"
an extremely amusing, well
played, immoral piece.
The Singing, acting \+ scenery
I thought very good. It was
11.5 when over — walked home
\+ got to bed at 12.1

## Right Page

### December Tuesday, 8 1868

Cold, blustering sort of a day —
Got up with a headache, as is
my habit after being "out" the
eve'g before. Had pkg. to get
ready to send to Jos. Gavit — Albany
\+ some messages to do, so that I did
not get to studio until 10 o'clk —
then scraped out head for that
I painted yesterday \+ repainted
it — also the other of, working
like a good one all day in spite
of my headache. Think the of
I have painted to-day, the best I
have yet done. Home to lunch
at 1 — back at 2.30 — worked until
dusk — at 6 stopped again
to see Mary — to-day she has
had her nick lanced — found
her improving. Home to tea
at 6.30 — Eve'g looking over
libretto of Genevieve de Brabant
to bed at 10.30.
Young Bell was at the studio
drawing from about 11.45 to 7.15
