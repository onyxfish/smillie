## Left Page

### June Monday, 8 1868.

My overcast & foggy — mid-day sunny
& sultry — evef. thick, driving
sea fog — wind, as usual, from the
East & thereabouts. Got down town
to A.B.N.Co at about 9.30 and had pf
of my die — Up town again at
11 — Laid Rebiting ground on die
more to blacken the line pre-
paratory to burnishing than to get
Color — "Stopped out" and home to
lunch at 1 — back at 2 — bit and
burnished — at 6.15 I started for
Uncle A's — by 8th Ave R.R. Got to his
house at 7.20 — found tea waiting
for me — Geo. had gone up in 4.30 train
& had taken dinner with them at
at the regular time 5.30 — I spent
the evef. playing billiards with
Uncle A — & chatting with Miss [illegible] —
To bed at 10.45.
Will, Joe & Minnie went to Ft.
Washington this p.m. to visit Mr.
Bob. Hall for a few days.

## Right Page

### June Tuesday, 9 1868.

Did not wake until 7 o'clk — up at
once & out — Still, heavy fog — walked
about until bkfst time — 7.45 — Bkfo —
then took train 8.24 — Met Will, coming
down from Ft. Washington — Rode Right
down town, but before I got to A.B.N.Co
heavy Rain set in — got quite a wetting —
got pfs of die — a good deal of trouble
to get what I want — as the Co. has no
regular prover — The thunder shower
being over, got back to Studio at 11.30 —
worked with graver and dry-pointed
over part of sky. Calman called &
gave me pressing invitation to visit him
at Irvington — Maud Diven & Mr. Ried called
before I got to Studio — Home to lunch at 1,
calling to see Burling, but seeing Kuntze, on
the way — back at 2 — tremendous thunder
storm bet. 3 & 4 — Charley Adams came
to meet Meta — but she came not. I worked
on die until 7 o'clk — then home, sick, an
old-fashioned turn with my stomach
& bowels — went right to bed — Mr. & Mrs.
[illegible] & child, of Montreal, spent eve. at house
