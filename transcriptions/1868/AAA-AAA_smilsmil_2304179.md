## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 6 1868.

Up about 6 — did not get bk/pt. until 9 o'clk — Sunday in this region
is a lost day. Feeling half used up
with something in the headache my,
Sat around until after 10, when with
Gil. & Charl. went down to Dr. Patters
to see the Colmans — went with them
to a ledge of Rock near the house,
& enjoyed the fresh air & view until
near 1 o'clk. Then home to lunch
& at 2½ o'clk met Colman & Tiffany & m[r]
Charl. walked out to Chappel Pond.
A rough & interesting walk of 2 mi[les]
or more there. The pond, pretty and
suggestive, but presenting no str[ong]
points — found some fine specym[ens]
of Cedar on Mud pond — Got hom[e]
at 6. Meeting Mrs. Miss C. & Mr. Dun[h]
near Smith Reede's — Dinner & th[en]
until 10 o'clk doing a little of
nothing — then got to bed tired[,]
& sleepy — Rain falling at 10.

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 7 1868.

Heavy Rain all last night and nearly
all today. All hope of going to Lake
Placid given up. Charl, Gil. & I had
proposed going over to Placid to-day
Wilmington pass to-morrow & home
me. This plan being broken up, I
set to work & by 4 o'clk P.M. — had
finished up the water color study
that I got partly finished last wk —
m.f. I did not touch the color of
the mts. but worked in the water,
reflections & finished up middle
distance, So I still claim it as a
study from nature. About 4.30
as it had stopped raining & was
showing signs of breaking up, Gil.
& I walked down to Dr. Patter's to
see the Colmans — Found them
all in. At 6 walked back — had
tea, and spent the evg. as the
[illegible] usually go here, think-
ing up to-morrow's work — Got
to bed about 10 o'clk.
