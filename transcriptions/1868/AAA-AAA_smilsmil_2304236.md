## Left Page

### December Thursday, 17 1868

Pleasant mild day — almost spring like at noon. Got to Studio at 9.10 worked all the m'g outlining N\. 3 of Fat oxen. Alman came and paid me $228 00. being in full even for the one that I have not yet finished. At 1 went to [illegible] \+ had my hair cut. at 1.30 home to lunch. back at 2.30. Auger went with me to Bank \+ got Alman's check cashed. Spent rest of p.m. Repainting heads \+ other parts of oxen No. 2. At 4.30 went to Kelly \+ ordered panel stretcher for my large water color drawing. then to Troedler's for tracing paper, then to Snedicor's \+ Schaus's. on my way up stopped in at Artist's Fund Ex. back to Studio at 6.30. left there at 7. Home to tea at 7.45. Afterwards at Church Sociable at Dea. Philp's. Mr. [illegible] made a call as ever. Got to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### December Friday, 18 1868

Brilliant, clear day. quite mild in the m'g, but getting cold \+ very windy towards night. Got to Studio a few minutes after 9. Set palette \+ commenced painting on oxen No. 3. Miss Devry came \+ stayed for about ½ an hour. I Rubbed a strong glaze over all back of the oxen \+ locked the color in by noon. Bring Bell came \+ stayed from 12 until about 3 o'clk. I could not give him attention enough to call it a lesson. I stuck to my painting the while until 4.30 when I could see no longer. I had to leave about ½ of the foliage just rubbed in. At 5 walked down to 15th \+ 4th Av. with Miss Sterling. Called on Snedicor \+ [illegible] him $50. on acct. Home to tea at 7. new Suit home from Brooks. As I was tired, did little or nothing \+ got to bed at 10. Mary \+ her neck are better to-day.
