## Left Page

### March Tuesday, 24 1868.

Up at 6.30, feeling weak from
yesterdays experience but all
together better than I had hoped.
Determined to take things easy for
the day — got to Studio at 9.30 —
Young Bell got there a little after
10 and I gave him a drawing
lesson. He invited me to take a
horseback ride with him — So I
went home, got lunch at 1, dressed
& met him on 2 o'clk train for
Yonkers — got started on our
horses at about 3.50 & had a glorious
Ride in spite of the mud for near
2 hours. I was delighted & happy —
Rode out to Hastings & back through
the Neppervan Valley — Took 5.45 train
home, got in at 6.45 — Had a good
wash & Rub down & felt ever so much
better. Addie Dunning made a very
pleasant call at Studio in the m'g, Mrs.
Dart, Dan Sayer & Ch'r. Mayer in the p.m.
Eve'g writing & French — got to bed
at 10, but not to sleep until after 11.

## Right Page

### March Wednesday, 25 1868.

Quite a cold, windy day — Up at
6.30 — Got to Studio at 8.30 — Spent an
hour over my French exercises — Will
Black called about getting a damaged
picture Restored & I took him down to
Lazerus. Then got started on my
painting for the day, which was to
finish the last of my four 7×9's for
Snedicor — "On the Saco, near Conway."
At 1, home to lunch, back at 2.15.
Got my subject finished after a
fashion in time for French lesson
at 4 o'clk, which took until 5, then
I cleared up & took father's proof of
Hartford City to Gauthier to Passe-
partout. Stopped at Snedicors, then to
Studio — Got Jelly moved & went over to
Mr. Spring's — 25¢ — 7 o'clk — just in
time for dinner — Mrs. Ireland in
town — all very pleasant, a little
before 9 — Geo. Hawes & Addie D. came
in, at 9, I left — Straight home — a
little writing & to bed at 10.45. Rec'd.
check from Utica for $45.00
