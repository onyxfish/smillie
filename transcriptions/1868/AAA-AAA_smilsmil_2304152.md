## Left Page

### July, Sunday, 12, 1868.

As hot as ever — Up late & went
to Dr. Hastings' to church. Heard
a Rev. Mr. Pierson preach a
very good sermon from Isaiah
50 ch. 10 v. Very few out & all
apparently suffering from
the heat. Home to dinner.
Afternoon, wrote a letter to
Oscar Ireland — at 5.30 went
to Ireland's in 25th St. stopping
at the Episcopal Ch. in 25th &
near Madison Ave. Got to I's
about 6. Saw them all, took
tea & spent the eveg until
10 o'clk, the trip to the Adirondacs
— they to start to-morrow eveg,
much talked of. On my
way home, stopped at Haffy's
to get some cream to cool
off — Home & to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### July, Monday, 13, 1868.

One of the hottest days yet — unac-
customed to hot weather in the city
this is to me, just infernal — got to
studio at 9.15 — Ther. 92° & during the
day it reached 98°. Now, at 11. P.M.
it is 89°. Sat most of the day with
nothing on but my gauze merino
undershirt, the perspiration run-
ning down my beard and dripping
on the table — but I stuck to my
etching — Rode home to lunch at
1 — a nap & back to studio at 2.45 —
worked steadily until 7.15. Then
took a sponge bath & toweling &
at 7.45 went to Mrs. [illegible] mans
in 21st to see S, L, F. She went home
last Thursday with her mother.
Home to tea at 8.15 — Spent the
eveg until 11 o'clk. Preparing
my moist water colors. About
6 o'clk. Ogilvie, called at studio.
3d day on die for A.B.N. Co. —
