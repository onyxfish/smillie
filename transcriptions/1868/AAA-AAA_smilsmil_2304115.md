## Left Page

### April, Monday, 27, 1868

Joe & Will added themselves to our
family to-day.

a mild pleasant day. While at
bkfst. Rec'd. note from Fan. Mulford telling
me that she would be at the St. Nicholas
this P.M. Got to studio at 8.45 & set
to work (7th day) on 20×36. Painted in
the trees on River bank. R.H. Mr. Bell
came at 12 & took a lesson & gave me
$20.00. Art. went home to lunch —
back at 2. Worked until 4.30. Cleaned
up & sat & went to St. N. Found the
party, Ben. M. Fan M. Mr. Mr. Jessup &
Joe Williams, at dinner. I went out
to a Restaurant got tea & Rett. at
7.30 we all Rode up to Academy of
music. They had secured seats, so
I left them & went over to the French
theatre & saw "La Belle Hélène". I
was much amused & made use of
the opportunity as a french lesson.
Nothing can be said of the morals of
the piece, for it has not any, nor
have the members of the troupe any
I should judge. — Home at 11.15
and Right to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday, 28, 1868

Woke up with a nervous headache.
Did not get up until 8 — then took a cup
of strong coffee & some bread — got to studio
at 9.15 — So chilly that I kindled a fire —
weather overcast in mf., but clear during
p.m. — Painted sketch of broken palette
to give to Mrs. J. 2. a ward. — Fan M.
came to studio at about 10.30 & half an
hour after Mrs. Jessup came. I went
with them to N.A. of D — There until 1.
Then parted with them & I went home
to lunch — back to studio at 2 —
Worked on palette sketch until 5 o'clk
when I finished it — Home at 6.15 —
took tea at once & at 7.10 was at the
St. N. Visited with Fan, sharing with
Mr. Ned Cook until 9 o'clk when I
started for home — walked up &
got there at 10 —
To bed at 10.45
