## Left Page

### May, Sunday, 3, 1868

Up at 8 o'clk — mr[illegible], went to Dr.
Weston's, Baptist — 31st & Madison
Ave. D.W. preached a very good
discourse from Rev. XXII .17. After
the sermon 12 persons were baptiz[ed]
among them, Kenneth My Koff. The
ceremony was impressive, but as usual
distressing to me — I wonder when they
find nerve to go through the ordeal —
I never could do it — Home to dinner
at 1.30 — at 2.30 went up to Uncle
Aaron's — Met him & Charl in 125th
St. on their way to Ravenswood, so I
had the p.m. to myself — & enjoyed
it exceedingly loitering about. Uncle
A. Charl & Tony Ret'd at 7.45 o'clk.
when we took supper — The Rest
of the eveng until 10.45 spent in
chatting with Miss Porter — then
to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Monday, 4, 1868

Up at 6 o'clk — glorious mrg, got about
"gyp" before light buggy & drove up
to High Bridge & over & through the new
Reservoir. Returning at 7.30 — all alone
and enjoyed it hugely — Rather cool,
but birds & soft, misty mrg effects
charming — Bkpt. at 8 when the
laggards made their appearance —
Charl & I Ret'd to city in 9 o'clk train
I got to studio at 10 o'clk & commenced
drawing for A.B.N. Co. "Coffee, Tobacco
& Palm". With Prevost, went into Geo.
Innis's Studio was introduced to him
& me to lunch at 1 back at 2.15.
Divided p.m. between A.B.N. Co and
photos for S.C.F. at 5.45 went to
Sauthier's about basse-partouts —
Home at 7.10. Eveng spent bit;
writing & french exercises.
Mill Gavit came in later in the
eveng & stayed until 11. I got
to bed about 11.45.
