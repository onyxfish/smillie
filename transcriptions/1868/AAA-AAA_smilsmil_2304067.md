## Left Page

### January Wednesday, 22 1868

mother went this every to uncle Aaron
to visit for a few days.

Glorious day over head, but something
fearful under foot — Got to Studio
at 9 o'clk & set to work at once upon
drawing for N.B. Note Co. This I do,
partly for Father's sake and (I am ash-
amed to admit it) partly for money.
The subject is one of Darley's drawings
for the Ill. Lond. News — pub. in 1860 —
The Bnk Note people wanted Father
to wf directly from the wood cut. He
could not do it — So I made the drawing
copying the groupe, an Indian and
his horse attached by bears, adding
the accessories — I worked very hard
all day, going home to lunch, in the p.m.
A.H. Ritchie called with a pf. of his
sky, that I bit in for him yesterday.
It is not as bad as we thought. Every
took drawing home & worked up m
it until near midnight when I finished
it much to the satisfaction of Father.
That is my satisfaction, (together with $25)
but I would be better pleased if the whole
thing was original with me.

## Right Page

### January Thursday, 23 1868

The perfect antithesis of yesterday,
in a weather way — a dark, howling,
wet & miserable day — Rode to Studio
at 9 o'clk — feeling very much out of
sorts — Worked upon my Academy
canvas, outlining with spots — Turp.
Brown — until noon, when I gave
up to one of the most violent
nervous headaches that I have
yet had — So much for my over-
work on Tuesday & Wednesday —
It takes the conceit out of me to
find such a limit set to my
exertions — I lay upon the lounge
back of the Screen, in an agony beyond
words to describe until about 7, P.m.
when I was able to get up & go home.
Spent the evening in my big
chair until about 11 o'clk, when
I got to bed — but not to sleep
until after midnight.
