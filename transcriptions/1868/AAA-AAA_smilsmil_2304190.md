## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 30 1868.

a worse day than yesterday, heavy clouds, windy, with now & then a spitting of Rain — got a fire started in stove in our old Room & painted on my large study, 18×24, working over the fore mo & water at lower edge of study. worked at it until 4 o'clk when I called it done — I am very sorry that I have had to touch it in the house, but when I went to the spot in nature this mo. I found it so changed that there was no use of my going there again, so I did as little as I possibly could & have it hang together. — At 4.30 walked to the top of Wood Hill & enjoyed very much the wild autumnal wind, the mountains & the clouds. Crossed over & descended the N.W. side, returning a few minutes past 6 — Rec. a letter from Uncle Willie & Alfred Jones — Eve'g spent easily Rocking in my chair & chatting with the Williams's. Got to bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### October Thursday, 1 1868.

Clear, cloudless and cold — After breakfast went to Boulder Study near Rie's Fall — and altho' I found it very cold, worked steadily until about one o'clk, when I kindled a little fire & having my small teapot soon had a cup full of Borden Beef soup — with my toast & egg, & after, a cup of tea — altogether enjoying my lunch very much — At my study again & worked until after 4 o'clk, when the sun getting off me I got very cold & my hands so numb that I could not work. Finished my study after a fashion at 4.30 — washed up, packed up — got home about 5.30 — Glorious clear moonrise — but clouded over about 9 o'clk — wrote a note to the Treedy girls & got to bed about 11.30
