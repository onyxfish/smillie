## Left Page

### July, Friday 10, 1868

Overcast all the a.m — close &
sticky, with a fresh breeze from
the East. After bkpt, wrote a
note to Mary — got to Ritchie's
at 9.30 & worked until 12 trying
to burnish away the Rebbing
in the Ruling — at 12 went to
A.B.N. Co. & got transfer on die
of "Lassoing Cattle" — then to Trents
& bought a pocket compass — then to
Ridner's to get some gum ammoniac
Then to lunch & met Jaffuen — then
again to Ritchie's — there until 3.15
back to A.B.N. Co. & ground 3 etching
points — up to studio at 5. Frank
Reed made us a call. Fred Ireland
left a letter for me from his bro.
Oscar, now in Central America.
Home at 6.45 — spent the even'g
fixing gums & things for my
moist colors. To bed
shortly after 11 —

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 11, 1868

A very hot day — Ther. above 90° in
the studio. Got to work about 9.30
on my etching of "Lassoing Cattle"
for A.B.N. Co. — not altogether in
the humor for it owing in great
measure to the heat — Rode home
to lunch at 1 — back at 2, but
did not get really to work until
about 4 when I struck a vein
& stuck to it as long as I could
be, until 7.25 — Then, very tired, I
stripped, took a sponge bath and
a regular toweling — After my scrub
down, felt much refreshed & got
home & had tea about 8.30. Then
set to work preparing my moist
colors. At 9.30, went up & met George
at the Diven's — went with the girls
to Haffy's — cream all round — home
at 11 & resumed work on moist
colors — got to bed shortly
after midnight —
