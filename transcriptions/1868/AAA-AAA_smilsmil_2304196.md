## Left Page

### October, Monday, 12, 1868

A beautiful Oct. day. Up at 7,
& breakfasted late, giving me time
to take an airing in the garden.
At 9.30 went to Tarbell's stable,
as his black mare was out, I set
to work sketching an old Chestnut
horse, worked faithfully until
1.30 when, tired, discouraged and
chilled through, I went home to
dinner. After dinner, worked my
mf. sketches into better shape. At
3.30 called on H.H. Jessup & his new
wife to bid them good bye. & then
went to Billings Stroud's stable
& there labored with his mare
until 5.30 without being able to
finish a sketch of her fore legs.
Ev'g. G.O. & I called upon Miss
Julia Riley, out — left cards. Met
Insalla Torre at the gate — went
home with her & had a very pleasant
call until near 10 o'clk. — then home
& to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday, 13, 1868

Very pleasant m'f., but soon overcast
& so continued all day. Ev'g., being
very mild, still & dark. At 9 o'clk A.M.
went to Stroud's stable with water
color traps, & waited until 10.30 before
Briscoe, the darkey, came to hold
the mare & keep her in position.
Worked until 12 o'clk, getting her
fore legs, back & front view. I pay
B. 20 cts. per hour. Walked around by
the lake before dinner. At 2 o'clk
back to the stable, but Stroud took
the mare out. Then to Tarbell's, &
I had taken his mare out. Then
to the blacksmith's shop & around.
At 3 o'clk. Ben Mulford took me
over to his farm to show me his
stock. Took a running sketch of
a little bull calf. Walking and
sketching by its side as it fed.
Ev'g. great interest in election
returns. At Mulford's, playing
whist with Mollie Groesbeck, Fant Joe.
Were very unfortunate. Elisha M. and
Mr. Morris there. Home & to bed at 11.30
