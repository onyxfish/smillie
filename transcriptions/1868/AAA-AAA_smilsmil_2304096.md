## Left Page

### March Friday, 20 1868.

Felt tired & did not get up until 8 o'clk. Dull, windy
march day. Got to Studio at
9.45 a.m. & spent the morning
finishing R.h. cor. Rocks. Moat
Mt. (26½ days). Now I shall put
it away for a few days to dry.
Home to lunch at 1. Mother
gone to Mrs. Burgh. Got a note
from S.C.7, thanking me for
what I had done on photo. of Hy.
Back to Studio at 2 & worked
hard until near 6 — painting
in a little sketch, "On the Saco"
on a broken palette — as well
as I could see by an imperfect
light. I rather like the Result.
Evening at home, wrote to S.C.7
& studied my French lesson.
Snow commenced falling
before 9 o'clk P.M. — To bed
at 10.45.

## Right Page

### March Saturday, 21 1868.

A wild day. Mid-winter & March
combined. Snow & wind driving
furiously. Everything blocked up.
Got to Studio at 9.30 & found a young
snow drift on the floor and dislodged
ones back of some passe-partouts hanging
on the wall — all having driven in at
the ventilator. 5 of my large drawings
have narrowly escaped being ruined.
French lesson in the M.f., lunch
at Studio. P.m. spent Charcoal-
ing on 16×26" Moat Mt. from the River
Saco, at North Conway. Got part of
outlined so that I may commence
painting on it on Monday. Mr. Chas.
Marten came in late & made us
a long & very pleasant visit.
Home at 6.30. Still storming as
disagreeably as ever. Even.
spent Reading newspapers and
studying French.
