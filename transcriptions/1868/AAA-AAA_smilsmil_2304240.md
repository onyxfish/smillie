## Left Page

### December Monday, 21 1868.

Quite mild again — Mf, bright
& clear — clouded over by noon, but
ever pleasant. Got to Studio
at 9.15. Kindled fire & gave a
general sweep & fix up — At 9
miss Dewy came & stayed until
12. I painted in the ground m[?]
fat open No. 3 — Home to lunch &
, back at 2 — worked on grou[nd]
until 4.15 getting it painted in
after a fashion — Went to M[r?] [illegible]
at 4.30 to beg of him to accept my
position on the hanging Committee
Water Color Socy. He wont do it —
He doesn't like such troubles me
honors. Home to tea at 6 — Spent
part of ever looking over Darley's
"Sketches Abroad with Pen & Pencil" —
nothing in it, I think, calculated to
increase his Reputation — Mr
& Mrs. B.P. Baker called & stayed
until 9.30 — Shortly after 10
I got to bed —

## Right Page

### December Tuesday, 22 1868.

a mix'd sort of a day — Sometimes clear
& then overcast, flakes of snow falling
leaving us at last with a pleasant eve[ning].
Mf, went to Brooks Bros. with my
new coat to have some alterations
made — did not get to studio until
10.30 — At once set to work painting
[illegible] hand of — At 1, home to lunch
back at 2 — worked until 4.30
when I got the animal painted
in, the best one yet, I think —
home to tea at 6 — Gave it sent
up drawing of "Gaucho & Guitar"
saying that he would call to see
me this ever about changes that
he wishes me to make, but he did not
come — at 8 went to Lyric Hall
[illegible] at 41st & 42d St. to hear the
George Choral Union give Beethoven's
King Stephen & Meyerbeer's "Penitent" —
the singing I thought very poor —
Ret'd home at 9.30 & read until
near 11, finishing Darley's Book —
do not think any better fit than I did
last night —
