## Left Page

### March Sunday, 8 1868.

another mild, muggy day — Got up, as I expected, with a very threatening sort of a head; spent the day nursing it — Sat in my big chair by way of variety — adding spice to the variety, by napping & Reading and thinking of nothing in particular, getting Ready for my work to morrow on Ritchie's Plate —

Father is quite under the weather to-day, but at night appears better —

## Right Page

### March Monday, 9 1868.

Up at 6.30 A.M. very mild, clear day. Got to Ritchie's, 39 Park Row. at 5 m. before 9 o'clk & waited for him until 9.40 — Then, when he came, commenced operations, biting in the Close cross Ruling. The Ruling was not all that I could wish, but every thing went off very nicely & I had the biting finished at 3 o'clk P.m. Some time spent in Cleaning the plate off & at 4 o'clk went to A.B.N. Co, Saw Mill & Rode part way home with him — Stopped at Snedicor's & from there walked to Studio — Home to tea at 7 P.m — Eveng. Spent lazily — the house very warm & I very tired — To bed at 10 o'clk —
