## Left Page

### July, Thursday 30, 1868

Up soon after 6 - got things ready for
moving, bkfs., a run over the Ruins
of Fort Wm. Henry & at 8 o'clk off on the
Minnehaha for ~~Pitt's Place~~. The upper
end of the lake. The atmosphere, most
tantalizing, we could see but a few
miles & Mt. forms grew & faded away
as we went on. The air was delicious.
Reached ~~Ellstt~~, at 11 - at ¼ after 11 on
coaches for Ft. Ticonderoga. 4 miles
where we arr'd at 12.30. Dinner and
immediately after had a hot scramble
over the Ruins of Ft. Ticonderoga - got
down to the pier just in time for Stmr.
"Canada", overheated & like a full blown
Rose - at 1.30 off for Westport where we
arr'd at 3.30 & took a poor looking stage
for Elizabethtown. 9½ miles distant.
Got there at 6.30 after a somewhat
uncomfortable & unsatisfactory ride -
Homesick & very much disappointed
with the place. Put up at Simonds' home.
The young people tried to have a dance
but could get no music - To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 31, 1868

*Elizabethtown, Essex Co.*

Up at 7, bkft. at 8 - and at 9, transferred
our traps to new quarters up on the plain,
lodging in a house right opposite to, &
boarding in the house of - Kellogg - a son-
in-law of Simonds' - The atmosphere
if anything, thicker than before. Set
to work at once upon drawing for the
A.B.N. Co. Kept to work until 5.30 P.M.
- very close & hot. Late in the P.M. - a
decided improvement in the air, - able
to see all the Mt. forms about. Went to the
hotel to await the arrival of the Cook-
Kimball-Ireland party from the Lakes
& woods, where they have been camping
for the past two weeks. A heavy thunder
storm came up at 7.30 - I never heard
such a rattling clap of thunder as one
about 8 o'clk. Near 9, in the heavy rain
the party drove up, wet and jolly.
They were hilarious & running over
with the good times that they have
been having - a funny, bedraggled
looking crew - Took tea with them & sat
on the piazza singing & chatting until
10.30 - she home & to bed at 11.
