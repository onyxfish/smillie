## Left Page

### February Monday, 17 1868.

Dull morning, snow very like Rain before noon, which continued during the day giving another layer of slush under foot. At Studio at 9 A.M. Sore throat & half sick with cold generally. Painted in 7×9 sketch "On the Saco" — Groupe of trees on the left, River on right — just a glimpse — not in humor for work & so could get nothing in color that I wanted — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2. By working closely finished sketch at 4.30 —
Ever, about 8. Will sent in for some of us — Josie very sick. Father & I went in — Charé off for Beatsley — Found Josie in a sort of Hysteria — Will very much alarmed — Could get neither Dr. Beatsley or Gurney — got Wm. Thompson — he Reassured Will & at his, left the patient much quieter, Will thought she had Brain fever —
Home at 11 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### February Tuesday, 18 1868.

Clear & cold, but not able to enjoy it. Used up again with a tremendous headache, Sore throat & pains in liver, bowels & back. Passed the day with little cessation or mitigation of pain. About 8 p.m. Dr. Throop saw me & gave me Remedies. Got out of bed at 9 o'clk and into my big chair — dozed there until past 3 o'clk a.m. when I got again to bed & slept tolerably during the Remainder of the night.
