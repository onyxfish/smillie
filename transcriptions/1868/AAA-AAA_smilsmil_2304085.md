## Left Page

### February Thursday, 27 1868

Still another miserable day —
Snowing nearly all day — Got to
Studio at 9.15 & soon at work
on Moat Mt. (15 day). Glazed
heavily over middle ground Rocks
& painted Right into it. I now
feel at home again, having got
off from the mountains & on to
solid Rock — Being so much
more familiar with foreground
painting I am quite delighted.
I feel very hopeful that I will
make a creditable picture —
Home to lunch, through slush &
snow at 1 — back at 2 — & worked
until 5 o'clk — Home to tea at 6.30
Father much vexed at N.B.N.Co.
because they are not going to put
his name of the plate of Hartford
that he has just finished —
Spent the eve'g over my French
& got to bed at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### February Friday, 28 1868

Tried hard to be a pleasant day, but
hardly succeeded. Did not get to
Studio until 9.20 A.M. To work at once
on Moat Mt. (16 day). Painted over
the trees that come against the Mts
on the left hand & part of the middle
Rock — The work still progresses
smoothly & I'm in good humor
with myself. Took lunch at the
Studio & at 2.30 went to Philhar-
monic Rehearsal — Enjoyed it —
found new beauties in Wagner's
introduction to "Lohengrin" —
out at 4.15 — went to N.A. of D. & took
a look about & at 5 returned to
Studio — A few more touches on
my picture, Cleaned up & home
at a little past 6 o'clk — Eve'g
until near 9. Reading English news
papers — then Alfred Jones came
in, stayed until 10.40 — when he
left for the 11 o'clk Yonkers Train.
I got to bed at 12.
