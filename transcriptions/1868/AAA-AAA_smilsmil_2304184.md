## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 18, 1868

Up at 6.30. m'f very cold — In a few min
utes had a wood fire in little stove &
dressed very comfortably. Got some
of our things more to Rights & wrote
a letter to father — About 10 o'clk, had
palette set & everything ready for work
it being much warmer, went to my
place on the River & set to work to
Repaint the water — when I had given
a glaze over it all & a Rubbing in
of Color, the sky clouded over & the
wind blew so that my work more
was all endewise — I had to go much
to very much to my Chagrin, work &
guess work & Recollection. To lunch
at 1 & back to m'f study at 2.30 where
I worked until near 5 — my trouble
with my bowels still continues & gives
me much discomfort. Frank I read
& Mr. Clay went up to Hurricane this a.m.
& found snow enough to make a snow
ball — Ever[illegible] very sleepy — but after
10 got talking water Colors with Messrs
Lewis & Clay & did not get to bed until 11 [illegible]

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 19, 1868

m'f very pleasant, but with signs of
a change — Milder than yesterday,
but still cold to sit out, so that I did
not dare go to my work until near 10
o'clk — then set to work on foreground
of my River Study — Before noon it
had quite overcast & was rather breezy.
Back to dinner at 1, but did not
quit until near 1.30 — After dinner,
as my light was quite as good as
in the m'f, which was near no
light at all, I returned to my Study,
& worked until near 5 — My day's
work is unsatisfactory — In the foreground
I have taken some liberties with the drawing
to get in a form or two lying near, but
just outside of my study — I can see in a
very disagreeable way, exactly where I have
copied nature, and where I have patched
— have Every suffered again a good
deal from dyspepsia — altho' I am
extremely careful — After 10 o'clk got
chatting again with Clay & Lewis and
it was 11.30 before I got to bed —
