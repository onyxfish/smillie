## Left Page

### October, Thursday 22, 1868

The worst day yet, blowing col[d]
+ drizzly until about 2 P.M. whe[n]
it commenced snowing + kep[t]
it up quite briskly + steadily the
rest of the day. At 9 a.m. went
down town. Stopped at Fordha[m's]
then went on to Smith's where
I stayed until noon — then
home — Dinner at 1 — abou[t]
3, having finished letter to
Corinne Colman again mu[st]
to Fordham's + Smith's — [Beck]
finished up the last of my cutt[ing]
jobs, but the wood work of my
trunk was not finished —
Between 5 + 6.30 walked on the [Fair]
ground — enjoying the roar of the
wind and the bleak snow sto[rm]
until it was quite dark — Tea
at 6.30 — At 8 went to Fan's — [staid]
until 10.30, then home and
to bed shortly after 11 —

## Right Page

### October, Friday 23, 1868

A disagreeable day, with one or two
efforts to clear, but unsuccessful.
Spent most of the mf at Smith's shop
again, watching the finishing of the
[box of] my trunk, which being com-
pleted before noon, was taken
directly to Fordham's shop there to
be finished into a trunk. Went to
[Sayre's] foundry + got oak cleats, that
afterwards proved to be worthless.
At 1 o'clk got dinner — at 2 o'clk
went to Fordham's + found him
stretching on the Cowhide — he
worked hard all the P.M. — and
at 5 o'clk had it well finished.
Shell, Jel + I went to Mr. H + [?]
Painters to call on the Misses
Riddle, now staying there — Spent
a pleasant evef with them (Hettie
+ Mollie) Mr. D. + Mr. Mr Cooper —
home at 9 o'clk — Uncle [—]
read awhile Nicholas Nickleby
— at about 11, I got to bed, very sleepy
