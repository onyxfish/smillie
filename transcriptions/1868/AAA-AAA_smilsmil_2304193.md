## Left Page

### October Tuesday, 6 1868

Up shortly after 5, after many disturban[ces]
during the night from carts & busses & tor[ch]
light processions — at 6.30 had a good [breakfast]
and at 7.30 started on Albany & Susque[hanna]
R.R. for Harpersville — 120 miles — Early
m[orning] lovely, hills to the East, beauty [increased]
at 9 [o'c]l. Clouded over & being tired I [took]
a nap until about 11, when the weat[her]
improved & so did the Country, which
is very charming after coming upon
the Susq[uehan]na River — At 1.20 Reach[ed]
H.ville — as I had a headache, took only
a cup of tea for dinner — At 2 o'cl[ock]
started on what proved to be a doleful
sort of Ride — being 5 hours going 20
miles — got to Binghampton at 7 [o'c] —
Tea at Hotel near R.R. depot — Met
there Billings & Stroud — Suffered muc[h]
with my headache — 8.50 train late
15 m — got to Great Bend at 10 o'clk
and in 25 m — was in bed and
in 5 m. more asleep —

## Right Page

### October Wednesday, 7 1868

Up at 4.30, got a slight [breakfast] at 5.00
& at 5.35, by D.L. & W. R.R. Started for
New Milford where we arr[ived] at 6 o'clk
after about ½ an hour's delay. Stage
started for Montrose and after a
cool but comfortable Ride we got
there shortly after 8 o'clk a.m. —
Found Uncle Wm & Aunt H. Had not
yet had [breakfast]. So we took a com-
fortable one with them. From
10 until 12 o'clk — unpacking trunk
& settling things, washing, scrubbing
& dressing — Then walked through
town meeting & chatting with many
of the towns people — Back to dinner
at 1 — at 2.30, got at my work, but
getting tired & sleepy, lay down
to take a little nap & slept until
near 5 o'clk — another walk and
tea at 6 o'clk — at 8 o'clk Called
on Fan Milford — There until
10 o'clk — then home and to
bed just as the Clock struck 11 —
A pleasant day & quite mild.
