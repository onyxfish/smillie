## Left Page

### July, Friday, 24, 1868

Overcast, with a succession of sprinkles & spatters all day
long. Spent the day getting traps together, getting sewing,
mending etc, etc done.
After lunch went out & did a number of messages.
Every after tea went down to see Menger, 40 Bank St.
He is to go up to West Point with us on Monday. Then
went to 274 W. 11. 8th to find Mr. J. D. A. Ward — out of town.
Then home at 8.45 — Soon after commenced a steady
Rain. Got to bed about 11.

Mary got home from her trips with Uncle A. this P.M. at 4.30
Very much delighted with her trips & very tired.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday, 25, 1868

A very warm day, if exercising in the open air, but delicious if it can be taken
passively & bearing sweet traces of last night's rain — All day spent in
the labor of getting shirts & collars ironed, bits of mending & fixing
done & at last packing trunk and
valise. As years pass, no doubt I will find a way to reduce the magnitude of this work. At 3. P.M. went
to Uncle G's, but he had gone home,
then to Schau's & bought Bristol & 2
& a tin can, then to Gymnasium & got
my towel, then home — Took a bath,
my tea, & at 7.30, Geo & I started by
stage for Uncle A's, where we arrived
about 8.45. Passed remainder of
every with him & Miss Porter — To
bed at 10.30 —

Dor & Will went up to Bob. Hall's to spend Sunday.
