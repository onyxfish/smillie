## Left Page

### August Monday, 3, 1868

Awake at 6. Up at 7. Bkfs. at 8. Off for Hurricane Mt. at 9. After Riding about 6 miles, all of the way up hill, me got to our halting place about 11. The party consisted of 11. I had my water-color traps, umbrella & other things that made quite a load. After a Regular toil, for my headache yesterday was not in my favor — I Reached the top about 1. Settled myself to a subject & my lunch at once. Worked on my sketch with great vexation, owing to flies & inexperience until after 3. Then gave myself up to enjoyment of the grand view & a charming succession of atmospheric & cloud effects until 6 o'clk. When Dr. Lewis, Jes. & I, leaving Miss Poland on the top to stay all night, her camping comps. being just below, we started down. Reached the wagon at 7 & home a little after 8. The mt. to me, not near so bad as its name — something like Chocorua, but not half so good — the view to the E.W. very fine & to the E — Champlain was plainly seen away to N. & S. To bed at 10. well tired —

## Right Page

### August Tuesday, 4, 1868

Up at 7. Skpt. at 8 & soon after, got to work on yesterdays sketch trying to make something out of it — the camping party of last night, 8 in all — 3 of them ladies — got down home about 11 — they had no sunset to speak of — no moonrise & no sunrise this morning — they think that they had about an hours sleep & found it very cold even with their big fire, which we saw burning like a bright Red Star. — Worked on my sketch, mainly washing off what I put on, until Eveg. when it is still unfinished. About 8.30. went down to the Valley House, where we had quite a country fandango — old Simonds the host, led off with his daughter, in Money Musk to the music of two fiddles & a clarinet — It was unique to me — most of the strangers were present and all seemed to enjoy, it — was introduced around & now feel quite at home. At 11.30 Ice Creams — claret lemonade and cake — Shortly after 12 I got to bed.
