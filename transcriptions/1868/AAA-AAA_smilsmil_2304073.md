## Left Page

### February, Monday, 3, 1868

A very clear cold day, stinging
wind from N.W. Geo. & I got to studio
at 9. Fire built in a few minutes but
so little prospect of soon warming
the Room that I went down to
Brooks Bros & Co. bill of $70.00, then
to Sarony's & got pf. of photo of self —
then to Uncle George's — got my new
teeth, which to be frank, give me
little satisfaction, paid him $25.00
& back to Studio at 12 o'clk. feeling
extremely wretched — feverish and
chilly — pain in head, down my back
& all over — eat a part of my lunch &
then set to work to finish the "Laying
in" of my "Moat Mt." By 4.30 had the
canvas covered, did up my traps &
started for home — prepared in my
own mind to stay there some time. Dr.
J. called at 7.30 — I par-boiled my
feet for an hour — took his medicines &
at 10 o'clk Rolled into small bed, brought
down to my own Room, prepared for a
"sweat" but it didn't come — had a night
of dry, burning purgatory, instead —

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday, 4, 1868

Another clear cold day — In
bed all day, very weak, some-
what feverish & miserably sore
all over, especially in the head.
Dr. called in the morning —
About 3.30 sat up in bed and
wrote a note to Saml. Coleman
& fixed up my books & accts.
as Treasurer of Water Color Socy.
but not being able to prepare
a formal Report, sent everything
pertaining to my Office to Coleman.
The Annual Meeting is to be held
to-night at Walter Brown's Room
about to o'clk. feeling much better
& up, washed & dressed myself
& spent my evening in my Chair
& stretching some paper to com-
mence drawing for N. B. N. Co —
as soon as I am able —
Turned in to my little bed at
11 o'clk & passed a tolerably
comfortable night.
