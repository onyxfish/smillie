## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 11, 1868

when I awoke at 6. it was raining
softly — by 8 it had broken away and
after breakfast went to my sketching
place & during the m'g made
a satisfactory start, laying in my
sky & distance — About 11.30 it
began to rain again — and
before it came down steadily
I did not beat a retreat until
about 12.30 — Rain most of the
P.M. — Sent to N.Y. for gifts and
wrote a letter to Charl — The
girls that walked to Keene yes-
terday got home about 4. wet
& tired. We'f some excitement
over Jay Cookes visit to the house.
I spent part of the time writing
a charade & an hour with the
ladies in the parlor — — got
to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 12, 1868

Fresh, breezy m'g — after breakfast,
run down to the Valley House and bid
Mr. A.B. Marks & Miss Day good bye
as they leave to-day — Got to my
sketching place about 10 o'clk &
got to work at once & painted in
the bushes & bank on left hand
side — bothered with the light as
the sun was in & out — a couple
of slight showers passed over.
After dinner, spent a couple
of hours playing Croquet with
the girls — Afternoon superb —
Every quite cold — had a fire
in the sitting Room — At bed
time. Ther. 55°. Sam. Colman
his wife, sister, Father-in-law — Louis
Tiffany & 2 or 3 others arr'd this p.m.
proposing to stay some time —
I am much relieved of my
trouble to-day — Tried out some
Lamb fat & made application
while warm — with the prospect
of getting better & having a good time
my spirits have gone up.
