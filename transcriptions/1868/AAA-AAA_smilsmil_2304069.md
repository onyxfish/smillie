## Left Page

### January, Sunday 26, 1868

Dull day, mild & threatening Rain.
M.Y. went to Rev. Thos. S. Hastings. Saw
for the first time the Ceremony of installing
newly elected elders — P. Harwood Vernon
was one of them. It was very impressive.
H. preached from 1st John — 1 chap. 3? v. —
and was on the subject of Christian
Fellowship. Home to dinner — At
3. P.M., walked up to the Park — had
on a new pair of heavy shoes, that
brought me home early with sore
feet — went through some hot-
bathing operations with them
so that I did not think it wise
to go out in the eveng. At home
doing some Reading and some
writing — To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### January, Monday 27, 1868

Dismal weather — Raining all day.
Got to Studio a few minutes after 9 —
Kindled fire — Set palette with lots
of white & at last got started on
my Academy picture "Moat Mt.
from White Horse Ledge" — Much trouble
in getting sky color — but worked
indefatigably all the morning.
P. Swain Gifford called — Took lunch
at Studio at 1 o'clk — & then went
on with the mountain. Painted
until 3.45 when I went into Colman's
room & attended Council meeting
of Water Color Society. Mr. Hart
proposed a Sketch club and I
clinched it by appointing a
meeting next Friday Eveng at my
Studio. Got to gymnasium at 5¼ —
worked for an hour. Home to tea —
ate pretty well tired — Wrote
notes to different members of press
concerning our Reception for next
Sat. Düring came in at 9 & stayed
until 10 — I got to bed at 10.30
