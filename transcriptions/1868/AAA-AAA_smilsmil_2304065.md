## Left Page

### January, Saturday 18, 1868

Clear + moderately cold. Wrote a note to Ritchie + then went to studio. 9.30 AM, Set to work on 6×10. "After a Stormy day — On the Hudson above Po'keepsie" — to finish it + send it out to Utica. Worked on it until noon, when I went down to Uncle George's, to have more fitting of frising done about my mouth. Did not get back to studio until 1.45 Found young Bell at work on his drawing — He gave me $20.00. I worked on my sketch until about 4 o'clk — Then Copcutt (Francis) came in + made a call near an hour long — He has been traveling for 8 mos + was full of it — At 5 went to Gymnasium + worked about an hour — Every went to Cooper Inst. + heard Col. James Fairman lecture on "The Artist's Mission" — met Bellows + went with him to his studio — there met Mr. + Mrs. McEntee + ordeway — Left there at 10 o'clk — home and to bed at 11.45.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 19, 1868

Glorious day — Mrs. went to Hasting's Church + heard him preach from, 1. Corinthians 1. Chap. 18th v. "For the preaching of the cross is to them that perish, foolishness; but unto us which are saved, it is the power of God." — The Power of God — + Christ, the power of God, was his theme — As I felt quite tired, did not go out as usual during the afternoon — Took it easy in my chair + wrote a couple of letters. Every went to Dr. J. P. Thompson's. Somewhat disturbed by a drunken man in my seat + a man that fainted + was carried out. The Dr's discourse was to urge the serious minded that that was not enough + that there is great peril in "standing" just without "the gate of Heaven" — After service wrote a note to S. C. F — Did not get to bed until near midnight.
