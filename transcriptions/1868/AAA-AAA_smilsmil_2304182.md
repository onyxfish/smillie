## Left Page

### Sept. Saturday, 12 1868.

Awake about 5.30 after a splendid
nights Rest — to my astonishment with
threatening of a headache — Camp fare
must be too Rich for my moderately
dainty stomach — M[illegible] as unprofitable
as ever, so we determined to make Camp
immediately after Skip. G[illegible]. Louis I. [illegible]
took our sketching traps & starting ahead
went over the "Carry" to the lower Pond.
Very warm & wet. part of the time, Sun not
hot. Part of the Durand party Crossing at same
time. On lower pond we took boat & went to a
point about ½ way down where Louis & I set
to work on sketches, looking N.E. Before
me. Reached the point, the Rest of our party
had arr'd over the Carry & were bowling on
the boat — G[illegible]. at once. Rec'd with it. The C's
quite out of patience that we should have
gone on. Mrs C. Miss C. the D's all met at
afternoon — C. J. G[illegible]. & I Remaining — with Smith
Beede — again it clouded over & the wind
blew a going hurricane & now & then it
Rained, but we stuck to our work until 4.30
what with headache — wet feet, over nature & chill
I felt quite used up — but I got my study in
such shape that I can conscientiously
finish it at Beedes — at 4.30 we Rowed to the
end of the lake — at 5 started on our ½
mile walk over a horribly wet & muddy
track through the woods & did it in ½ an
hour — Smith Beede carrying our
heavier traps — Had a good cup of tea — felt
better and got to bed about 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sept. Sunday, 13 1868.

Did not get up until about 8 o'clk —
slept like a top all night — am told
that it Rained tremendously. Sky
full of splendid clouds — the view down
the valley, glorious — Purple & gold —
sunlight & shadow — the air delicious
so that I am ecstatically happy —
about the M[illegible] letting the wind blow
over me trying to Realize the many
blessings that I am enjoying — my priv-
ileges, my duties & my shortcomings &
little. to lunch at 1.30 — afternoon
the weather changed. wind shifting
to N. not coming off clear & cold — Black-
man & Ferguson spent most of the
P.m. with us — Rec'd a letter from
Matt — wrote her a short note in
Reply, and a long letter to
J. L. J. — got to bed at 10.30.
