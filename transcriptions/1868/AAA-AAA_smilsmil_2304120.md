## Left Page

### May, Thursday 7, 1868

Another dismal day. Cold windy & rainy, got to studio at 9. So chilly that we had to have fire. I commenced to work on drawing for A.B.N. Co. Wood subject. At noon went to Burlings with some copies of pamphlets published by Water Color Society to complain of the manner in which they are got up. He was not in. Saw Mr. Cranch in stead. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2. Young Bill took a drawing lesson in the afternoon. I worked on my drawing, but with little spirit. Home to tea at 7 o'clk — Heavy Rain during the evng & some thunder. I worked at my french until 10 o'clk, at about 10.30 I got to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 8, 1868

Cold, overcast day, got to studio at 9 o'clk was glad to start the fire up, that had kept in over night. Set to work on wood drawing and got outline finished by noon. Burling called about W.C. pamphlets — I had left message for him with Kuntze this a.m. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — I commenced washing in effect. Stuck to it until 5.30 when I walked down to Lauthiers — saw about toasse- partouts — then to studio, then home at 7.30 — Took tea and spent the evng looking over new English papers & writing french exercises — got to bed a little after 11 o'clk. Aunt Polly Van V. at our house all night.
