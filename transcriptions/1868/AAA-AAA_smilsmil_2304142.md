## Left Page

### June Monday, 22 1868.

Dull threatening day — but no
rain until near 7 o'clk. P.M. when
a slight shower fell — All the
m't at Ritchie's, burnishing on
his big sky — At 12 attended to a
number of messages — left my
wool-yard drawing with A19. N. [illegible]
Took lunch with father — After, we
went with to to Ritchee's to consult
concerning sky — We determined
upon another line — its angle &
width — Kept on burnishing until
4 o'clk when I laid a Ruling
ground — left at 5.15 — Walked
partway home, doing messages
by the way — Home about 7 — Matt
& Kate in from Poughkeepsie — Quite
a noisy time at home — Took
Refuge in my bed Room where I
wrote a letter to Emma Ireland —
To bed at 10.50.
9½ days on Ritchie's sky —
Full time to-day.

## Right Page

### June Tuesday, 23 1868.

Delightful day — warm — but not hot.
On my way to studio, met W. Knudsen
chatted with him, so that it was 9.30
when I got to the studio — Soon after
Mr. Pate came in about some engravings
& further delayed me — Took him down
to Jas. Hart's Room — Then wrote my
note to Emma Ireland — Near
noon when I at last got to work
Retouching on H. F. Fessenden
photos. Home to lunch at 1 — back
at 2.15 & worked on photos until
4.30 when I went with 3 of them
to Cauthier's for passe-partouts —
Stopped at Snedecor's — Schaus's
& Knoedler's — Back to studio at
6.30 — Home to tea at 7 — Eveg,
mounted large pencil study
of Hemlocks & "did up" some little
matters. To bed at 10.50
