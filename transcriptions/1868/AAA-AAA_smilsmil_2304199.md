## Left Page

### October, Sunday, 18, 1868

Did not get up until after 8 o'clk. Very brilliant, cloudless mrg + cold —
Bkfst between 9 + 10 o'clk — Walked out to the Lake with Uncle Mm back
in time for Church — went to [illegible] Baptist — Heard Mr. Cochran preach
from Romans 14 "C. 19" v. a very liberal, earnest + practical discourse. I
was at first much annoyed by the defect in his speaking caused by
some trouble about his palate. After service, met Fan M — went with her to
Hettie Searls + we all walked out to Salome Watrous's — I left them there
at 1.30 + back to dinner — After dinner a nap + then wrote a long letter to [illegible] —
Tea at 6.30 — at 7 went again to Baptist Ch. + heard Mr. L. preach from 1st Peter
1 ch. 24-5° v. — a better discourse, I think than the mrg. — After service, at
8.15 — Rl. + I stopped at Fan's — there until 10 o'clk — then home.

## Right Page

### October, Monday, 19, 1868

Disagreeable, but milder than yesterday. Spent the mrg until 11 o'clk
at Smith's Cabinet Shop, overlooking Beck as we worked upon a little
coin box that I am having made, hoping very much to reduce the
weight of my Sketching Traps. Then went to Kom's stable and finished
the body of the dog that I sketched some days ago — Dinner at 1 —
after dinner a nap — I am sleepy now a days — I do not
fight against it, hoping that I may get some advantage from
it. At 3 o'clk again went at the dog, getting a cat to hold before
him, so as to "get him up" in some style" — I got another sketch
of his head — at 5 went to Smith's, there until near 6 — Home to tea —
Evg spent listening to Uncle Mm read Nicholas Nickleby —
to bed at 10 o'clk —
