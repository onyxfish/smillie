## Left Page

### August Wednesday, 5 1868.

Up late, owing to dissipation of night
before — most delightful — about 9
by appointment went to the Valley House
& met Mrs. Marks. with her — Miss
& gel I went rowing on the Bogue —
It was very delightful & we spent
the m't until 12 o'clk on the water.
After, until near 1. under the trees
Chatting with our ladies & other
boarders at the Valley Ho. After
dinner took a nap — then finished
up Hurricane sketch, making a
poor, dry looking thing of it. more
some & found it tea time. To
my great gratification in the
eve't got a letter from Father.
Spent the eve't. being amused
by youngsters with games &
Geographical cards — I find
some very pleasant people here
& feel quite homesick at the idea
of going farther in among the Mts.
as we have determined to do —
Got to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### August Thursday, 6 1868.

Up at 6 — Rain falling sweetly. Took
my bottle of Sulphur Water & to bed
again & asleep until the breakfast
bell rang at 8 — then up in a hurry
paying my respects first to the Sulphur
Water & then to my blest — Spent an hour
or more at harness makers getting my
valise mended — wrote a letter to
Dr. Throop as I am suffering away
down in my cellar regions with what
is a new trouble to me. After dinner,
at 3 o'clk Mr. Lewis asked me to join
him & his family to take a Ride — went
over Simonds' Hill — The P.M. being very
fine I enjoyed the view from the hill
& the ride altogether immensely. We't
got half packed up & determined to go
to Keene to-morrow with, but hearing
that Colman is to be here We'd eve't
we will wait for him — Charades at
the house in the eve't Mr. Dr. Darrow
& her Sister Miss Morris being prime
movers — much fun — did not
get to bed until after 11.
