## Left Page

### April, Thursday 20, 1882

Cold & blowing great guns pretty
much all day — B'kfs. about 7.30 &
before 9 went to building. Dorsey
started clearing the places for the
cisterns & then I put him at dig-
ging holes for setting out trees —
The man came with stone for the
foundations of the cisterns. I went
over to Mr. Mulford's wood lot and
selected several young maples
for transplanting. Then to N. M.
& got his permission to take them.
Dinner at 12.30 — a good nap after
it — The heavy clouds broke away for
a time & it was beautifully bright
but the wind blew chill & clouds with
spitting rain covered the sky — after
being at building some time A. &
went to P.O. Tea soon after 6 — after
it went to see the teamsters Halpin, — not in, but
he came to the house about 9 o'clk.
Annie & I spent the evening
reading Prescott & playing "High-
low, Jack" — To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### April, Friday 21, 1882

Snow flurry before breakfast, followed
by a cold & raw rain. Continuous
cold & driving snow flurries all
day — when I got to house, after
b'kfs. I found that the Hawleys &
Dorsey had been there & all gone
away because it was too cold to
work — at which I was wroth.
Went down town — to the Tannery &
saw Foster — to the mill & saw Absalom
Stone about trees — met the Hawleys
in the street & we exchanged words.
Dinner at 12.30 — again to the house
The Hawleys at work laying foundations
of stone under cisterns — got them
done & cisterns in place by 5.30 — Tea
at 6 — at 7.30 walked out to Halpin's
to arrange with him for to-morrow's
work he has promised me — then to
A. Stone's & had more talk about
evergreens — About 9 when I
[illegible] to Courtright's — a little
Prescott before going to bed soon
after 10 — Night very dark, cold
& windy.
