## Left Page

### June, Saturday 3, 1882.

*Po'keepsie.*

Got up with a discouraging headache, but at it grew better afterwards. I went to studio's at 9.30 — a very pleasant day — & I worked hard, all the [illegible] on studio's extension drawings for Montrose & finally I got them finished & done up in a roll — Annie came about 12 & sat for Calverly until 1. Then she went home & I took my roll of plans to D. H. & W. Suffs — then home to dinner at 1.30 — Rested — then a little packing & we were ready to start for Po'k. We took Saturday special at 3.30 & after at last ride got to Po' at 5.20 & to the house at 5.35 — before we were expected — found Father suffering severely from effects of a fall he had at the Garfield Race House yesterday [illegible] — He fell off a plank at the front door, a height of about 4 fut & appears to have been generally wrenched — Tea some after 6 — Matt here but many the Mr. & the children out at Hillside — After tea went to Garfield Place House — only 3 family together — put Father to bed at 10.30 — there to bed myself at near 11.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 4, 1882.

There is a heavy thunderstorm before daylight — muggy & misty with some heavy showers until noon, then cleared to a delicious breezy day. Bkfs. at 8. Father did not rest well but seems better — did not go out in the [illegible], but had quite a nap. Dinner soon after 1 — then some writing. After it went out & walked with Annie to top of Hurst's hill — enchanting — breezy & bright — then to Hooker Ave. & Cherry St. Home to tea at 6 — Ed. & Matt there — Father groaning & very stiff, moving little & with great difficulty — got a plaster on his back & put him to bed about 10 — To bed myself at 11.
