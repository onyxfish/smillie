## Left Page

### November, Thursday 30, 1882

*Thanksgiving Day*

more snow in the m[orning] — after that very pleasant day — to studio at about 9.30. Hubert Herkomer called soon after with a lady & we had an enthusiastic talk about Etching & Printing. Then I got to work myself & after doing something on Cat. plate I pulled more b[ad] p[roofs]. Home to dinner at 1. back at 2.30 and worked until near 5 — then took a rest & home at 6. Thanksgiving dinner at 7 — Aunt Anna, Grace & Geo. J., Marie Cook, Ed[win] Cook & his wife at table with us. Evet[t]., the younger part of the party played Bazique until 10 — then all went home — we went to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 1, 1882

Raw, overcast day — nearly 10 when I got to studio — Did not do much besides stretching some of the Jap. pf[s]. Taken yesterday — tried to arrange some work for other etchings & lost some time looking for things so safely put away that I could not find them — Home to dinner at 1. Eliz. Home there — to stay overnight — about 3. A. E. & I went to Private View of Salmagundi Sketch Club system. at N.A. of D. There until after 4 — then I went to studio — home at 6 & had tea — at 8. A & I went to Wallack's & saw Mrs. Naughton in Honeymoon by Tobin — a tolerably well written play with intolerably stupid plot. Mrs. S[illegible] is not a particularly good looking woman — has not a good form, — is not graceful — does not dress well 'tis a very commonplace actress — my curiosity was satisfied but I was a long way from getting my $2.00 worth of fun out of it. Home before 11 — had a critical & descriptive talk — there to bed —
