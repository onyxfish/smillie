## Left Page

### June, Thursday 29. 1882.

Very delightful summer day —
after b'f'st. wrote some notes — Soon
after 10. to bank — then a little shop-
ping — then to studio & finished up
there — Home about noon and
to work at my trunk — Dinner at
1.30 — after that A. & I worked at
our packing until near 5 — then
to foot of 23d St & gust, by the skin
of our teeth, caught 5.30 boat to Cony
Island. Very pleasant sail. &
Reached Iron Pier a few minutes
before 7 — wandered up to Brighton
Hotel & there had supper — after it
met Wm Jessup — then down to the
beach & with a light surf & bright
moonlight over the sea on one side
& lingering twilight, every star &
myriads of restless forms & flashing
lights & babelish on the other, we
walked over the smooth hard
sand back to the Iron Pier took
9.15 boat back to the city — The sail
very pleasant, but too much tobacco
smoke — near 11 when we got to Pier &
11.30 when we got home. Right to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 30. 1882.

*montrose —*

Pleasant m'g, weather gradually
becoming overcast — Rain beginning
to fall about 5 p.m. — B'f'st. soon
after 8 — our trunk packing was
finished by 10 o'clk. — Then I went
to Bank with Osgood's check and
then to Jones' & bought an expensive
luncheon basket with crockery &
cutlery outfit — Then home & filled
it with luncheon. Left home at
12.10 Rosalie Butler there making
a call — got to Hoboken & saw
Gardinier & learned that freight
car had been sent off. Had trouble,
in getting Parlor Car seat — full — ate
lunch at 1.30 — again, at 6, after
leaving Scranton — got to Montrose
depot at 7.30. Tremendously heavy
rain — our freight car on the switch
a vehicle & team in waiting — a
ride to Town without discomfort —
got to Courtright's at 9 — Had a
cup of tea & some strawberries &
to bed soon after 10.
