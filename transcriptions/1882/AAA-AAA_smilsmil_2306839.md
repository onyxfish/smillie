## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 11, 1882

Miserable, gloomy, raw, rainy &
foggy day. Rain & wind during the
night, disturbing my rest. At 8
Annie & I went to bkfs. at C's — then
back to the house where the m[?].
until 11 was spent in superintend-
ing various small matters. Then
A. & I went down town & shopped
diligently until 1 o'clk. Then
I took Maggie's (the cook) dinner
up to her to cook & A. & I went to
C's & had our dinner — most of
the p.m. I spent unpacking a
hogshead containing dinner set
of china — then in our bed room
putting up curtains. To tea at
C's at 6.30 — back to house &
at 7.30 went down town to P.O.
&c. Got letters from Farren, Geo.
& Parrish — then home — wrote
a letter to Farrer & to bed at
10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 12, 1882

Still cold & cloudy — Up soon after
7\. At 8 went over to C's to bkfs —
back at 9.30. Terphas there. He
went back with me — got stove &
pipe & returning, put it up in the
Etching Room. All the m[?], about
superintending work going on.
Payne & team came up at 1 —
then to dinner — back soon after
2\. Watrous came with 3d load
of wood — Payne brought large stove
for furnace in Studio cellar. Mr.
Dav. Sayre called with Mr. Dickin-
son(?). Warner came & worked with
refractory bureau drawers. At
5.30 I lay down to rest, as did Annie
who felt too tired to go to C's for her
tea — Maggie made chocolate for her
& I went to C's alone — then down
town to P.O. & stores — home about
8 — Annie in bed. Read to me one
of Cable's sketches "Sieur George" —
to bed about 10.30.
