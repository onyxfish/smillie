## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 13. 1882.

Sulpurb day — cloudless & cool
with very fresh breeze. Immediately
after b'k'f. went out to look for a
subject & altho' the light was
not good, being too clear & hard, I
determined to do something. I
soon hit upon a spot not far
from the house. & got out my oil
traps for the first time in two
summers — I sketched in & from
about 10 until 1. I worked without
stopping, getting a result that was
both good & bad. Then dinner —
After that, at 3, with Annie & Miss
Small, went to wharf & took a sail
boat for Crowninshield's Point. Found
Mary Ireland at the Dexter house —
Mr. D. very pleasant — at 5 went
to top of Beacon Hill — then across
lots to Marblehead — through it to
Tucker's Wharf & were rowed to the
beach nearest Atlantic Cottage —
Tea at 6.30 — a note from Miss Abbott
inviting us to dine at Magnolia — Eve'g
reading papers — to bed at 10 —

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 14. 1882.

Overcast with strong southerly wind
Rain began to fall before 10 & con-
tinued all the aft. I had a
short walk, then worked on draw-
ings for lower hall — About 12.30
Annie & I walked up to P.O. with
a number of letters. I wrote one to
Cottrell. Dinner on our return.
Then a short nap from which we
were roused by a telegram from
Will Cook asking Annie to go to
Beach Bluff to superintend the
packing of things there, he & Sue
having ret'd to Cambridge where
she is today in child-bed. Annie
went at once — no rain but high
wind. I worked on drawings
until after 5 — then walked over
to the station & met A. in "a Barge"
She got out & walked back with
me — tea — Eve'g. very high
wind & bet. 9 & 10. thunder vivid
lightning, almost a Tornado —
to bed soon after 10.
