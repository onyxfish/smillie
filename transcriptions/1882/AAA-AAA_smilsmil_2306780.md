## Left Page

### June, Saturday 17. 1882.

*At Richfield — con. Dr Adams'*

Very hot day — got to studio at
9.30 + right to work upon Portland. O. Stuck to it + at 1.30
a boy came for it — not yet
done, so I sent him back with
instructions to come again —
then I continued my work +
finished at 4 — to my great joy.
Packed up drawing + left it to
be called for then home abt. 4.0
to dinner — after that a short
rest, then packed our bags
+ soon after 3 Annie + I went to
G.C. Depot, great rush of people
+ by 3.45 train started for Richfield — reached there shortly
before 6 — rode right up to the
house. Dr. A. still in N.Y. Soon
had tea — then sauntered in
the garden + took a stroll up
the main street to the old church.
Coming in I felt so warm that
I sought a quiet corner in parlor
while the others sat on the piazza —
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 18. 1882.

Rain during the night + in
the mf, a muggy, foggy mist
that at times was almost a niggle
Bkfs. at 8.30 — After that I walked
out on the road for nearly an hour,
then back + at 10.30 went with
Annie to the Episcopal Ch. Heard
Rev. Mr. Henry preach on the command to love — met Alden Weir
+ a younger bro. at the ch. — They
walked to Dr. A.'s with us — Alden
told Annie that he was engaged
to be married — Near 3 before
we had dinner — then the clouds
cleared away + we went out
to ride, Annie, Neelie + I — We
drove around for more than two
hours enjoying the sweet air very
much — Home before 6 — then
tea — after that a long saunter
in garden + on piazza — Later Dr.
Todd came in + Mr. Atwater + his
wife — chatting until near 10 —
then visitors left — To bed about
10.30 —
Ther. in the mf. fell to 64° — making
us feel uncomfortably chilly —
