## Left Page

### November, Sunday 12, 1882

mild & cloudy, with interval sunshine — Did not wake
until near 9 — Late bkfs — at 11 a. + I went to Brick Church
+ heard young Van Dyke preach his first sermon as Pastor from
Acts. 10th ch. 29. + 34 v. — on parts of them — a pleasant performance —
good, without brilliancy of thought or manner. After service A. + I
walked up the Avenue to 52nd St.
through to Mad. Ave. + stopped at
Dr. Yale's — not in — A. spent half an hour with Mrs. Y. — who is un-
well — then I gave up + we went home to dinner at 2 — Mother C.
dined at Aunt Anna's — After dinner read + later, napped —
at 6 went again to Dr. Y.'s + saw him for a few minutes — back
to tea at 6.45 — Early. Read awhile — wrote to Father,
took it to G.C. Depot — then to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 13, 1882

Another very mild day — Part of time sunny + very warm — before 6
p.m. wind + rain that continued during eve'g. Up late — To studio
by 10. most of mf. spent working up an old Croome drawing of Eagle
Ford, B.N. Co. A strange woman with a child called — said she was a daugh-
ter of Jno. Rolph — + a model — think she was crazy — home to dinner at
1.30 — back at 3 — Made a little experimental W.C. sketch — at 5
to Hotel Brunswick — Met Cone, Yale,
R.M. Hoe Jr., Gilder, Thos. Moran +
Betts — by appointment met Mr.
J. Seymour Haden + made preliminary
arrangements for his lectures — he was
very pleasant — Home at 6 — tea 6.30 —
Read papers, then worked on
plans for N.E. Room, main floor of
Montrose House until 10 —
to bed at 10.30
