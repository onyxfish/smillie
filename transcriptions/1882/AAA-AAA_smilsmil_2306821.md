## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 5, 1882

*St. "Providence" — State Room 21 —*

Warm day but a pleasant breeze blowing constantly — B&lp about 8. Soon after 9 started out — went to show Stone first — then to Louis Tiffany's & selected some glass — 11 o'clk. before I got to Fall River boat & with difficulty got a very poor room — then to Semon Bache's — D wave S. & bought glass for our bedroom, south window — then down to A.B.N. Co — there until 12.30. then to Charl's — saw him for a few minutes & at 1.15 at home, to dinner — after that, a short rest, then to Park Ave. Hotel & had hair cut — then home & at 4.30 Annie & I went to the boat. Started at 5.45 — delightful sail up the East River — after 15 or 20 m. waiting on the stairs, were able to get seats & had tea at near 8. Miss Humphrey on her way to Magnolia to join Miss Abbatt, sat next us — Eve'g passed listening to the band & promenading the decks — To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 6, 1882

*Atlantic Cottage. Marblehead Neck.*

Up at 6 o'clk. — B&lp on boat before 7. then walked to Ferry St. Station, Fall River, & took 8.15 train to Boston, getting there at 10 — By horse car, rode over to Eastern R.R. depot, bought tickets for Marblehead & left our small [illegible] & then started out for some small shopping & sight seeing. Wandered along Washington St. — went to top of Equitable Bldg. & at last, very tired & hungry, got to the Vendome, on Commonwealth Ave. at about noon — We waited until 1 o'clk. with great impatience, then had an "early dinner" which was nicely served but did not quite suit my paletta — About 3 o'clk we started again & walked to the E. R.R. & there took 4.05 train to Devereux Station — then rode to Mr. Stone's Atlantic Cottage, Marblehead Neck, getting there at 5.30 — Tea at 6 — wandered about as long as we could see — then in parlor — few guests — painfully quiet — To bed about 9.30
