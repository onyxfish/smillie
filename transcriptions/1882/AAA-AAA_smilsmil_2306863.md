## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 28, 1882

Quite cold with a snowy Rawness in the air — After 6 [illegible] Row down to Kimmel & Voigt in [illegible] 5 & got [illegible] from Rough Spot Cat. Plate — Then to studio & laid ground & Rebit — To dinner at Nelia & daughter down from Ridgefield — Returned same day — back to studio at 2.30 — laid ground & added some etching — At 5 to Newmans — Johnson's & other places — Tea at 6.30 — At 8, Annie & I went to N.A. of D. & heard Muybridge lecture on Animal locomotion, illustrated by wonderful instantaneous photos of horses, bulls, deer, dogs & men Running & by an instrument the consecutive photos were made to merge together & give upon a screen to give the exact action — a horse running with rider turning somersaults was a wonderful exhibit. He says all artists are wrong in their Representations of action of horses — granted, but if Scientifically correct they would fail to express action. Art has a province quite as much as truth — Galleries uncomfortably cold. Home at 10.30 — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 29, 1882

Snow all day — Got to studio before 10, after going to Wallack's & getting tickets to see Mrs. Laughing on Friday night. Then to work on Cat. Plate before noon. Took some [illegible] Home to dinner at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked at the Press as long as I could see — then cleaned up, had a little rest & home to tea at 6.45 — [illegible] did little else than rest myself in front of a pleasant fire — To bed soon after 10.

Geo went off to-day with Walter on a Duck Shooting exped. on Long Island.
