## Left Page

### July, Monday 17. 1882.

Breezy day — wind from S. E. —
a busy day for me. — To house
before b'kf't; Hamelin not at
work — hay'ing — Darkie Thompson
picking up wood — I went to
P.O. at 9.30 — no letter from Roberson
then to foundry. Then to studio
& wrote to Roberson counter man-
ding order for blinds — back to
P.O. at 12. — Through Boyd & Co
sent to another Binding Hampton
firm enquiring about blinds —
Dinner at 1 — men from foundry
at work setting pipes from the
cistern to pump & tank in attic.
Glass for studio's extension ar-
Tea at 6.30 — then to studio
Annie went to P.O. She Ret'd.
we sat together in studio
until it was so dark that she
could not find her hat — then
to C's & she read aloud to
me until after 10 — To bed
as usual at about 10.30

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 18. 1882.

overcast — Rain beginning to fall about
1 o'clk — increasing steadily until at
night it poured down in torrents —
A very busy day — Saypres men were
at work with pump & pipes & at noon
Burgess & Morris came to start
work on the oak stairs — I had my
hands more than full — Dinner
about 1 — a miss Shoemaker from
Wilkesbarre arr'd at Courtright's —
Tea at 6. 30 & after 7, in pelting
rain went to P.O. & then away
down to Payne's to get him to haul
stone to-morrow — Back to C's
after 8. Changed clothes & then
dozed in a shawl on sofa and
Annie read to me Cloister &
Hearth until near 10 — To bed
at 10.30.
