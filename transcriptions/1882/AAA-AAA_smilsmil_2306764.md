## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 16. 1882.

Delightful day — but my internal
weather does not seem to have
cleared yet. About 10 went to
studio & continued work on Etching
Room plans. Home to dinner
at 1 — back at 3 — waited for
Kurtz until 3.45 — then without
him went to Met. museum. There
he joined me at about 5 — with
Gen. di Cesnola — Annie also
came a little later — I looked
over the old pictures with K. with
a view to illustrating a hand book
but was not encouraged. At 5.30
A & I walked through the park & out
at 72d St. & by Mad. Ave. cars home —
Tea before 7. Eve'g, among other
things did some writing on a
"Steamer letter" for Emma — To bed
before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 17. 1882.

Sunny & pleasant in the m'g but
soon after noon a thick, grey film
spread over the sky & cold, raw wind
blew from the east. Before 6½ p., wrote
for awhile on Emma's letter & after
6½p. finished it; about 11 when
I got to studio — worked diligently
until 1 on montrose plans — Then
home to dinner — at 2.30 Annie
& I went to foot of Carleton St., S. M.
Servia (Cunarder) where we bid
Emma good bye — lots of friends there
— immense number going — tremendous
fuss of everybody, finding it chilly
we left before the steamer did — Went
to Styles & Cash, Printers 8th Ave & 14th S. Then
to Dewey & Keating — Tailors — Bdway near
4th S. & ordered clothes — Then home
at 6 — Tea about 6.30 — Eve'g, I
spent some time measuring lower
hall wall for Lincrusta Walton —
To bed before 11.
