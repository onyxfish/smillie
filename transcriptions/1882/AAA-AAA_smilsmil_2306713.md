## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 1. 1882.

Clear mf. about 10 m. & snow —
After bkfs — went to Renner's —
then to barber's & had my hair
cut. 11 o'clk nearly when I
got to studio. Found Mrs
Dustbury & Orange there with
copper plates & sketches — I
laid a couple of grounds for
her & when I got through it was
time to go home to dinner —
back about 2.45 & painted
a little snow sketch based
upon some observations made
in the mf. Home at 6 — then
tea — After 7 took a & her mother
around to Ned's — Scent on up
to 82; St— then in to Park & viewed
the obelisk by moonlight — its
wintry surroundings made it very
impressive — walked through the
Ramble & down home — got
to Ned's at 10 — home by 10.30
To bed soon after.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 2. 1882.

Charming day — clear & mild —
Snow melting fast; streets afloat.
Up early — bkfs before 8 — to Dr. H.W.
Bellows' funeral at 9 — there for
a short time at N.A. of D. then to
studio & commenced oil sketch
"A stranger in a strange land" —
the obelisk, snow, moonlight,
based upon my tramp in Central
Park last night — home to dinner
as usual & back at 2.45 — got
my sketch done in a very un-
satisfactory manner — Mr. Wissman
a Hartford etcher, called with some
specimens of his work — At 6.30
went to Fanny K's to tea, met a
[illegible] — Took them to singing club —
then to studio — prepared bkfs.
of Etchings "Snow Storm" & "Yosemite"
cedars to send to London Exftn.
Went for a— at 10.15 — Rose up in
4th Ave. car with Will, Jos, Minn,
Mr. & Mrs. Lounsbury & Two Robertson.
To bed soon after 11.
