## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 1. 1882.

A horrible storm of wind & rain. — Rode down to Rimm & Voigt's to learn what I could about printing on parchment. Got to studio about 11 — Jussing until near 1 — then home to dinner — about 2.15 went to Bank (2° Nat.) — then to studio & about 4, home again. Then had to go to grocers & glass ware man etc. etc. — 5 when I got home — then dressed — tried to make some petit ink dinner cards but too late — at 6.30 Mr. & Mrs. Ehninger & Mr. & Mrs. Saml. Colman arr'd — then Mary Ireland & Geo. Cook — at near 7. Mrs. Bliss Baker came & we had dinner — a very pleasant affair, it seemed to me — left table at 8.30 — guests left at 10 — to bed at about 11.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 2. 1882.

Delightful spring day — Bkfs. a little late — after it went to Newman & Capron's to enquire about locks — then to 2° Nat. Bank — there to Leavitt's to learn who had bought the "Regnault" "Automedeon" as I have a great desire to etch it. Leavitt not in, so I left a note for him & got to studio about noon. Home to lunch at 1 — at about 3.30 Annie & I went to Sherwood Studio Biuedy Recptn. Great crowd — bored our way about until near 6, then home — tea at 6.30 — at 8 went with Annie to her club — then to my club & wrote letters until near 10 — then for A. & took her & Emma to W. 38th St. Mr. Morse's — a pleasant little party — there until 11.30 — then home & to bed at 12. LaRoche called at house at 6.30 with my grinders.
