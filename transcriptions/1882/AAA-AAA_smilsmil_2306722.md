## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 21, 1882

Steady Rain all day, at times very heavy — thunder & lightning about 6 P.M — Got to studio at 9.45 — Spent some time over my printing press, cleaning journals & re-oiling it, then to work trying to get satisfactory p.p. from "A Lady of Cairo etching" — failed & went to dinner at 1 — back at 2.45 — & managed to do better in spite of the darkness. Gave up at 4.30 & cleaned up — It was 5.45 before I was ready to go to La Roche's — Saw him for a few minutes — 6.45 when I got home — Changed wet clothes & had tea at 7 — Evelf. Annie read aloud a chapter from "Tom Brown" & then I wrote to K°. T. Hammond — artist of Boston a Dutch uncle letter giving him some advice that he has asked for — To bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 22, 1882

A march-like sort of day, commencing with snow & continuing with bluster. Got to studio about 10 & to work at "proving" A Lady of Cairo" — very poor results — Home to dinner at 1.15 Mother Cook at Aunt Annie's — back at 2.45 & provied until near 5 — In 3 days I have managed to take 21 p.p. not one being really what I want — I cannot take what I want — my self & a professional printer succeeds no better, & yet I am sure that my requirements are not unreasonable — Home to tea at 6.45 very tired & much disgusted — Mary Willard to tea — spent the evelf. — I went home with her at 9.30 — to bed at 11.
