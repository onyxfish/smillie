## Left Page

### June, Sunday 25, 1882

Hotter than ever — did not go out of the house during the day most of the m'f. lying about — a little reading & a little writing — Dinner at 2 — then more lying about — managed to write to Ehninger, Osgood, Stephen Smith, old Kelly & father. Tea at 7 — Evening spent very much as the day had been — until about 10 — when I went to depart with letters — on my return a slight & patter of Rain — after a good towel bath to bed after 11.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 26, 1882

Hot, but not so much so as yesterday — B'k'p at 7.30 — At 8.30 went to D.L.&W. R.R. freight yards at Hoboken & saw Mr. Gordonier who assures me that we can get our special car through in spite of the Strikers — then to N.Y. by Barclay St. Ferry. Went to Bradley & Currier in Day St. about doors — then to Carl Reiffer's — not in — then to Char's — then to Selleck's in Fulton St. & bought shirt — then to Sharp's, 13 Bay, Ker[?] & bought Copper — then to Scoville Mant. Co — then to 2nd Nat. Bank — then to Studio & home at 1 o'clk — Dinner at 1.30 — to studio before 3 & spent afternoon there packing — between 3 & 5 o'clk. very heavy thunder showers. Annie, her mother & some others went to Coney Island — I worked until near 7 then home, quite worn out & had tea all the ev'f. writing & arranging things. A — Ret'd at 10.45 — to bed at 11.30.
