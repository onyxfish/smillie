## Left Page

### May, Thursday 18. 1882.

Unpleasant, overcast & chilly.
Part of the day the sun shone
but it failed to make it pleas-
ant weather. Got to studio
at 9.30 & spent the m'g work-
ing on drawings for interior
finish of Montrose &tching room
Dinner as usual & back at
3 & worked until 6. Annie
came for me & we went to
Dunlap's & got a hat —
Home at about 7 — had tea —
There, feeling tired took a
little nap on sofa — Then
as it had cleared brightly,
I walked to 5th Ave. & 57 &c.
& back — 10.30 — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 19. 1882.

Still overcast, cold & raw. After
4 p. Annie wrote to her mother in
Cambridge that we would not start
for Boston to-night. I went to the depot
with the letter & telegraphed also.
Then to Grady & McKeever's & got papers.
Then to studio at 10.15 — wrote a long
letter to Catnell & then to work on
plan drawings — Aunt H. came in —
Annie came at 12.30 & for half an
hour she sat while Calverly commen-
ced the modeling of a low-relief
of her profile — At 1 we went home
Aunt H. with us, & had dinner —
At 3 I ret'd to studio & continued
work on plans — Annie came & sat
an hour for Calverly — After 6 we
walked up home — Tea before 7 —
Then dressed & at 8.30 went to 31
W. 18th St. & called on Sarah Fessenden
Hammond — a very pleasant eve-
ning — walked home at 10.30
& right to bed.
