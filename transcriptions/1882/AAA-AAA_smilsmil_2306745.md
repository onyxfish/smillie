## Left Page

### April, Saturday 8, 1882

Very pleasant day — got to studio before 10 — S.W. Wood came in with F.H. Smith's call for a special W.C. meeting & some time was spent discussing it. Bridges, a model was waiting. Finally I got to work & painted from him an ill. for old oaken bucket until 12.30 — Home to dinner at 1 — Back before 3 — worked on sketch for [illegible] awhile — then on drawing of window sash for Montrose until afterto — Annie came in — we rode up to 72° 3'. & walked down 5th Ave. to 55th St. Then rode home & had tea at 7.30 — Evely. I wrote for S.W. Wood, a reply to Smith's call for W.C. Meeting — Then Annie read aloud to me — To bed after 11.

Mother Cook sent this every from Ridgefield.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 9, 1882

Overcast with a little sunshine rain at night. B[illegible]. after 9 — Soon after 10 Annie & I went to R.C. Cathedral — Seats on middle aisles 50 ¢ — Side aisles 25¢ — we got poor seats on side aisle and heard, without much enjoyment or profit, the Easter music & sermon — The mummery was pitiful — After service we went to Nee Buckinghouse & saw Will [illegible] — Home to dinner at 2 — After that, did some writing — At about 5 went to studio — on my way back, called on Gel — Home to tea at 7 — Evely. Annie read some aloud — I wrote a letter home & took it to depot after 11 — in heavy rain — back & to bed at 11.30
