## Left Page

### November, Thursday 16. 1882.

Clear, charming day — got to studio at 9.30 & worked all the m'f on A.B.N. Co. drawing — Home to dinner at 1.30 — back at 3 — worked until near 4 — then to Matthews' Auction Sale in 23d S'. — hoping to get a rug for $25 — that was sold (?) for $100 — at 5 to Century — met Yale, Hoe, Betts, Moran & Gilder & until 6.15 discussed a plan to have Seymour Haden give 3 lectures in Theatre of Union League Club — complimentary — then to the K's — Annie there & [illegible] grace — tea — then to Club in 14 S'. with them — then up home & dressed & at 9.30 at N.A. [illegible] Herkomer Reception — there for an hour — then to K's & got Annie & home at 11.30 — Right to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 17. 1882.

Miserable, wet day. Up late & got to studio at 10 o'clk — Spent most of m'f writing letters to Montrose — did a little work on A.B.N. Co. drawing "Huascar & Esmerelda" — to dinner at 1.30 — Edw'd & Mary Cook & chimés their new house to-day — Back to studio at 3 — the rest of p.m. spent in mounting drawings — at 5 went to Newman's & saw hardware that he is to send to Montrose — then to Reimer's — then home at 6.20 — tea about 6.30 — only Annie, Mother Cook & myself — a very quiet meal followed by a quiet even'g. Reading paper & playing High Low Jack with Annie — to bed at 10.
