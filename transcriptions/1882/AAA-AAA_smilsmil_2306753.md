## Left Page

### April, Monday 24, 1882

Brilliant, but still cold & windy.
Up before 7 — [illegible] at 7.30 — Casey waiting.
Pettis came with first load of muck.
At 8.30 went with Casey, Paine, his
boy & horse & Dorsey out to Mulford's
wood lot & until nearly, engaged
taking up ten maple trees — then
back & set two of them out, before
dinner at 12.30 — At 1 all hands
& Pettis & his team returned for
the remaining trees while I dis-
creetly rested from my labors —
Party returned in about an hour
& then I went out & worked with
them until 5.30 when we got
the last tree set much to my
satisfaction. The work has not
been done as well as I could wish
but hope for good results. Have
enjoyed the work — very tired
but not exhausted — Tea about
6.30 — Evel[f] — a little Priscatt, &
a little writing & making of memos.
for to-morrow's work. To bed at
10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 25, 1882

*Home again.*

Glorious day — up early — [illegible] at 7.30
Spent the m[orning] Racing about and
winding up affairs preparatory to
a start for home. The views from our
piazza, enchanting in their freshness
& brilliancy. Got through with all
my messages & back to Courtright's by
12 o'clk. & soon after had dinner.
At a little before 1, carriage came
— Janine, John Courtright & I Rode to
New Milford & took 2.38 Train for [illegible].
J.C. went as far as Scranton — Our R.R.
ride was not particularly disagree-
able except to A — who was made
sea sick by the swaying of the car
& for a time was more than uncom-
fortable. Got to Hoboken before 9 &
home at about 9.30 found Nelia
Dr. Adams, Katie & Mollie C — there —
To bed soon after 10.
