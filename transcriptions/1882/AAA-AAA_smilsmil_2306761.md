## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 10, 1882

*George's son born — 4 o'clk. a.m.*

Pleasant m'f. — weather growing more unpleasant until about 4 P.m. when a spattering rain fell & at night, steadily — Not feeling so well in the m'f. I did not go out. Mary (sister) came before 11 and made a call — after she left, & at about noon Geo came with the news that his boy was born this m'f. all doing well — Dinner at 1 — & about 2 I went to Annual Meeting N.A. of D. 37 or 8 members present — no interest — Geo was elected N.A. by 28 votes, having two to spare. Wordsworth Thompson & I went to the house to tell him — found him asleep in bed — then went in to see the baby & saw the mother as well. Then home & at 5.30 Rolled myself in blankets — tea at 6.30 — intense headache — to bed at 10 & got some sleep.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 11, 1882

Very cold & raw — very much out of sorts so that nearly all the m'f was spent in the big chair. Annie went to Fanny T.'s to lunch at 1 — I lunched at home alone — at 2 went to 34 S. & 39 ave. to see Mr. Douglass the Painter, then to studio. Chas. Kurtz called, also Douglass — also Mr. Koehler — I wrote one or two letters but was unable to work. Home at 6.30 — then tea. Ev'f. Reading — J.C. Nicoll made a call — To bed soon after 10 o'clk. Suffering very severely from headache.
