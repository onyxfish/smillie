## Left Page

### June, Monday 5, 1882

Cloudy mist. with sprinkles of rain — Ther. down to 60°. a fall of 20° since yesterday — B[illegible]. about 8 — at 9 Annie & I walked up town — telegraphed to Will — then home & wrote a letter to Catrill. Got out my work & started a drawing for a B. N. Co. — Cuban — mont & pleasure vehicle. Dinner before 1 — worked on drawing until about 4 then Annie & I went out & strolled about until nearly 6.30 — then in & had tea — Every in mother's room — Twilight — no gas until 9 o'clk — mother reminiscent — To bed at 10 o'clk — Heavy clouded again & cool — the weather has, after all, proved delightful.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 6, 1882

*at Home.*

Very cool & breezy — beautiful cloud effects — intoxicating day — Annie & I had a short walk before b[illegible]. After b[illegible]. I took another walk — up town. Back & to work at 10 o'clk on drawing — at 12.15 out again for a few minutes. Dinner at 12.40 — at 2 again to work, continued until 3 — then wrapped up my drawing — packed valise & at 4 o'clk. Annie, Mary, Kate & I went riding — Drove to Hyde Park & on our return stopped for a few minutes at Hillside & saw aunt Jessie & Emma — Uncle W. & aunt A. being in Canada — Home at 6.10 & had tea — At 6.50 bid our adieux — drove to Station & by 7.20 Saratoga Special Reached N.Y. at 9.20 — lovely every. effect on the River — Twilight did not fade until near 9 — Home at 9.30 — found Nelie Adams & Juliette Ireland.. Wrote a letter to father — Took it to R.R. Station & mailed it — then home & to bed at 11.
