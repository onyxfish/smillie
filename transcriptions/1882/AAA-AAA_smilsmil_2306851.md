## Left Page

### November, Saturday 4, 1882

Overcast w[illegible]. cold & raw. Up late to bkfp. After that, until near 11, writing a long letter to Sohst, a history of the wrongs I have suffered at his hands in trying to get Phil. Seats. Jan alpfeal to him for justice — then to his house, 47. Third St., left note — called at Acad[emy] of music — not there — then to studio saw Berrian & paid Rent up to Dec. 1st. — Home to dinner at 1.15 — Back to studio before 3 — Still writing for boy from Montrose — went to N.A. & spent rest of day light looking at the pictures there — a poor lot — again to studio — then home — Reat at 6.30 — Read Baker. Before I got to Century — quite a free meeting — had a very pleasant time — met Hubert Herkomer who is very wroth over his treatment by Custom House Officials. Left at 11.15 and walked up with Ferguson who was in a very unhappy frame of mind — home & to bed before midnight —

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 5, 1882

Charming clear & cold. Up late had bkfp. about 15 — went with Annie to Dr. Collyer's & heard him preach of the day of small things. Annie had a fit of faintness from which she quickly recovered, but which was anything but a pleasant interpolation into the service — Home at 12.30 and then out for a walk — went to Charl's (32, 47th) & saw his photo — Home to dinner at 2.10 — About 4 Annie & I walked down to 28th St. & called on Joe. Then, on our way back, stopped at Walter's — home at near 6 — Tea before 7 — Eve[ning] Reading & writing. To bed at 11.
