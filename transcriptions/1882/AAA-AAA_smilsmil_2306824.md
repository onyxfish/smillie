## Left Page

### September, Monday 11, 1882

Bleak, northerly wind, promising a storm — after a walk, tried to work on a drawing of lower hall for Cotrell, but felt very stupid over it, making no progress —
Dinner at 1 — Heavy wind and rain during the afternoon —
About 5 I dressed myself in rubber & started out & had a tramp along the coast, enjoying the driving wind & beating rain very much. Tea at 6 — Every. storm continued — almost a gale — dismal night — all to bed by 9 o'clk — A & J sat up until 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 12, 1882

Rain did not cease until near noon — wind strong from N.W. & heavy surf. About 9.30 A & J dressed ourselves for the weather & went out & spent the morning tramping from ledge to ledge along the coast enjoying the excitement of the rising tide & heavy sea. In places it was very grand — Annie had never before seen so fine a display. Back to house at 12.30 & had a very little nap before dinner at 1 —
After that, the storm having cleared broken & sky cleared, we sunned ourselves on piazza — then to work on Cotrell drawing — stuck to it until 5 — then, until 6 — A & J walked on the cliffs — wonderfully clear sky & very crisp atmosphere — Tea at 6 — another short walk by twilight —
Every. reading newspapers & a little writing — To bed at a little after 10.
