## Left Page

### August, Sunday 20. 1882.

Delicious day — Up late & bkfst
at 9.30 — then to house & at
about 11 Annie & I went up to
Warner's lot where we wander-
ed until about 1 — I was bother-
ed with a stupid headache.
At studio until 3 — then to
dinner — at 4 back & wrote
a letter to Ehninger — at sun-
down Mr. Westcott called and
introduced himself & a friend
Capt. Brady (I think) — his daughter
was with him — I went over the
house with them — at 7.30 went
to tea — back at 8 — wrote a
letter to Father. Glorious moon-
light — had a long walk with
A. on piazza, then in studio &
wrote until 10 o'clk — a letter
to Ehninger & one to Father —
then took A. to C's & went
to P.O. — back & to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 21. 1882.

Still bright in the mfg. P.m.
slightly overcast. Hamlin did
not put in an appearance —
had company, as he afterwards
explained — I followed up the men:
Herrick in Bay window — Lewis in wash
room — Morris getting out panels on
stair case — Burgess fussing with
the upper stairs & Cottrell on attic
stairs — Peter planing on the walls
of dining room & Backus tacking
up duck on walls of N.E. bedroom.
Dinner as usual, except that
as the C. company had gone & Munroe
was off on a pic-nic, at I & the two C's
were alone — I worked at sizing
the duck tacked up until near
7, then to tea — Evg. Florence
came in & we chatted until
near 10 — took her home & at
10.30 to bed.
In the mfg Mr. & Mrs. Park &
Mr. & Mrs. Atherton came & went
over the house.
