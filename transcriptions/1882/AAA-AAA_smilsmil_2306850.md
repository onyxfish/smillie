## Left Page

### November, Thursday 2, 1882

Bright day, much cooler &
windy — at night, cold — After
[illegible] stomach pain returned
upon me with such strength
that I went to bed where I
fell asleep until noon when
I awoke feeling much better.
then went to Bank, deposited
Chicago draft & returned the
$200 I borrowed of Geo. Home to
dinner — about 3 went to studio
— did nothing of importance —
had some callers — about 6 I
went to the K's & had tea. the
first Singing club tea of the season.
Emma & Grace not there. At 8
took Annie & Fanny to Steck Hall,
112 14 S. where they have their Re-
hearsals — then to 57 W 10 S. Nicoll's [illegible]
E Editing Club — considering our duty
to Seymour Hadeu — determined not
to undertake the management of his
lectures — as suggested by R. W. Gilder,
but to invite him & Herkomer to an
informal meeting of the club. At 10
Ret'd to Steck Hall for Annie — home
about 11 & then to bed —
Colman called in the afternoon —

## Right Page

### November, Friday 3, 1882

Pleasant, but cold — Got to studio
but could do nothing as my boy
from Montrose had not yet arr-
At noon went down to Walls.
& saw Chase about Phil. tickets.
Home at 1 — dinner at 1.30 — To
studio about 3 — Mr. Galt called
also others — no work done
but some writing — Home at
6.15 after going up to del. at
with letter for father — Tea
at 6.30 — later in the eveff.
Edward Cook & his wife played
whist against Annie & me.
About 10 I walked up 5th Ave
to 47th S. then home & to bed
at 11.
