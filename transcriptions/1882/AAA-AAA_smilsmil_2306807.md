## Left Page

### August, Sunday 6, 1882

The hottest day this season
I think — Ther. got up to 90° on
studio's piazza. B[l]k[?]. at 8.30 — to
studio's before 9.30 — Put up a
hammock on studio's piazza —
Annie read to me an excellent
sermon by Jas. Freeman Clarke
"Faith & Belief — Essential belief
concerning God" — Dinner at 1.30
again to studio's — a thunder
storm to the north, by giving no
shadow & a breeze tumbled the
ther. down to 80° — I wrote a letter
to Father — Tea at 6.30 — After
sitting on piazza for some time
we went to studio's — lighted a
student lamp & wrote. At about
10 when we lighted our lantern
& went to P.O. — then to C's
& to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 7, 1882

An exceedingly hot, sultry m[or]n[ing].
Evidences of thunderstorms appear
ed early & by 12 o'clk. we had a very
light spatter that connected two storms
one to the north, the other south. After
that it was hotter & steamier than
ever. Dinner about 1. Again to
the house — most of the time with
Burgess trying to make him under-
stand what I want in the way of
a platform at the foot of main stairs
at 4 o'clk — another spatter from a
shower south of us, but at 6 a
very heavy rain with some tremendous
thunder passed over us. Near[?] when
we had tea — rain at intervals
all the eve[ning]. Read Baker and
Annie read a little from "Cloister
& hearth" — To bed at 10.
