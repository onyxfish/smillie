## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 17, 1882

Heavy fog in the m'g. Sunlight in
the afternoon + Rain about 5 o'clk
+ later. Ace the m'g. Superintend
ploughing + grading — Dinner
soon after 1 o'clk. Just before
2 I started with horses + light
wagon for Montrose depot. The
air + sunlight, delicious. Got
to depot very easily in an hour
to a quarter — Train came along
on time 3.30. Fanny Kimball + Eliz.
Home and — we Rode back to town
— threats of Rain — got home at 5 —
in a shower — Tea soon after 6 —
Rest in studio — to bed at
10.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 18, 1882

Heavy, foggy m'g. Unsettled
weather — some sunshine in
the afternoon — with Petrus
+ Payne, superintending plow-
ing + grading — down town at
10.30 to P.O. Mr. A.B. Clark
was buried from H'f. Turrell's.
Dinner at 1 — looking after
men, indoors + out, until 3.30.
At near 4, Annie, Fanny +
Eliz. went out with me riding
over Butterfield's hill — got
excellent distant views of our
house — back at 5.20 — tea at
about 6.30 — then to P.O. Met
H'f. Turrell + learned that Aunt
H. + Uncle A. are in Town. On
my way home stopped there
+ made a call — the Rest of
evg in studio until about
10.30 — then to bed.
