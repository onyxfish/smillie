## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 29, 1882

*Pkeepsie Golden Wedding.*

Very pleasant day, altho' at times overcast. Before bkfst. went to depot
& gave Elfs. man orders — could buy no
tickets — back & had bkfst. — after
that again to depot — could get
parlor car tickets & checked valise
thru home & at 10.15 A & I went
to depot. Met Joe, Will & Minn
& with them went to Po'k. on
10.30 Train — Reached home at
about 12.30 & had a family
dinner between 2 & 3 o'clk. The
time fully occupied by A. & self in
directing greeting cards, of which I
took a lot to P.O. before 6 o'clk. Had
a little to eat & drink about 7 when
friends began to arrive. — The house
was tastefully decorated with evergreen
& flowers & ample refreshments were spread
in the Recpts. room. By 8 o'clk. the parlors
were thronged & all seemed to be enjoying
themselves — Father & mother quite as much
as any body — My bland Bourne sent a
poem which was read aloud by Rev. Mr.
Grant, the Baptist minister — By 10 o'clk
the last had gone & the Golden Wedding
Reception was over — For some time we
were engaged writing autographs upon
some cards that Annie & I had prepared.
It was after 11 before we got to bed
to arise midnight before we slept.
A greeting card will be found in
one of the pockets of this book.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 30, 1882

Up & at bkfst. at 8 — Some heavy
showers — later clearing — Before
8.30 Annie & I got away & to depot
before Geo. or Will, Joe, Minn & Walter.
By lying statements we were induced
to take a local Train that should
have got into N.Y. at 10.55 — it hauled
off at Montrose & let the City Elfs. go
by with the rest of party — they got
into N.Y. at 11.05 — we got in at 11.45
mad was no name for it — went to
studio — wrote a note about golden
wedding to Hitchcock of Tribune —
Home to dinner at 1.30. After that
wrote a note to Van Cleef of Herald.
After 4 when I got to studio —
did no work — Home to tea at
6.30 — Dr. Adams there — At 8 went
with Annie to her singing club —
then to Century where I wrote
a portion of a letter to Ferguson,
now in Mexico — went for Annie
at 10.15 — walked home — lovely
moonlight — To bed soon after
11.
