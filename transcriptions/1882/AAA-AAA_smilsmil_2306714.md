## Left Page

### February, Friday 3, 1882

Windy blustering m'f. with a
snow flurry — clear day afterwards.
Got to studio at 9.30 & spent
the m'f. writing letters to Boudet
about Etchings now in London
& to Hogarth & Sons about Etchings
to go to London. Brichen
came in about 1 — I signed
his papers praying hell a. f
D. for a fall & Winter 24 [illegible].
Home to dinner at 1.15 — back
at 2.30 — made to Colnell —
washed brushes & at 4.30
met Annie & her mother at Tiffany's
where we selected a $150 dinner
set; then to Arnold & Constable's
to order a hanging curtain for door.
Then home & tea at 6.30 — Eve'g
three Sloane Rugs were brought
home — Annie read aloud
Oedipus Tyrannus — To bed
at 10.30.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 4, 1882

Overcast m'f., snow began falling
about 10 o'clk increasing & by night
it was a fierce, cold snow storm. After
[illegible] went to W. Jarochi's — dentist in
5.45 — there until 11.30 — then to
studio — home at 1 — & at 2 Annie &
I were at Booth's Theatre to see &
hear Oedipus Tyrannus — & odd hash
& jumble of anachronisms — a greek
(by Riddle) & play acting of the modern
& most passionate sort with a setting
of De Payne's music that while good &
interesting is utter nonsense greek. I
was interested but hardly pleased,
certainly not satisfied. After the play
went with A. to Arnold & Constable's & there
bought another curtain — then to 4th Ave —
A tired night — to 29th St before we got a car.
took a. to Ned's — ret'd home. Then again
to Ned's & about 7 had dinner. Ned
[illegible] on Phil & Frannie — in at 9 — but [illegible]
Sprague at Fable — Later, Annie & Miss
Williams played 3 games of Whist against
Mary Cook & me — Home soon after 10 —
To bed before 11.
