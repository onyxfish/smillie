## Left Page

### November, Friday 24, 1882

Bright but squally — B[illegible]. At
7.30 — Charl & Inema Bourne got
off on the early train — At 8.30 Prof.
Van Ingen came. He, father & I
went to the new Vassar Institute
over the building to see what
sort of accomodation will or can
be given to the eight f fo. [illegible]
next month. Very poor. Then father
& I went to see Riessell & his Statue
for the Waterbury soldiers mon[ument].
Home at 10.30. I wrote a note to Miss
Abbatt — Dinner at 12.30. Afternoon took a walk out by the old
farm — on return took Annie out
for a short walk — Tea about 6 —
At 7.15 good-bye — 7.40 train to
N.Y. Parlor car to ourselves — in
at 10 o'clk — on time. — Home &
to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 25, 1882

Pleasant day, but windy & dirty.
Got to studio at 9.30 — myself spent
in Faking in[k?] from cat. plate of
Rough Spot. — Miss Walworth
came & I set her to work on her
drawing — Home to dinner at 1 —
After that, with Annie, went to Hehomer Private View of Paintings
& Etchings at Knoedler's — Back
to studio at 3.30 — Laid rebiting
around on Rough Spot & several
etching grounds. Home at 6 —
Tea before 7 — Even[ing]. Read to
Annie & her mother a little auto
biographical sketch written by
Father — brought from Pok — To bed
about 11.
