## Left Page

### June, Friday 9, 1882

Weather still pleasant, cooler but with strong wind. Soon after 9 went up to La Roche's to leave a piece with him. He is to begin at once a new piece for the under jaw. Then to studio & spent the n'f. arranging accts. with Geo. & writing letter to Catrill. Annie came, but as Calverley was not in she got no sitting. Home to dinner at 1.30. After 3 when I got to studio — spent the rest of the afternoon getting things in studio ready to leave for the summer. Home to tea at 6.30 — Rosalie Butler & one of the Church sisters to tea. Later in the evef. another of the sisters & Mr. Paul came — they stayed until 10 o'clk. R. B. just home after 18 months abroad — very quiet in manner but very interesting in comment & description. Mr. P. improvised a very little on piano. To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 10, 1882

Overcast & cooler. After b'kfst went to Louis Tiffany's & spent an hour with him looking over colored glass that I p'rh'ps use to select for Montrose House. At 10.45 got to Flint's in 14th St. & there met Annie. Bought a lot more of furniture, then to Bradford Willard's & ordered rods etc — then to Macy's and bought crockery — Home to dinner at 1.15 — To studio after 3 — worked about 2 hours on Portland, Oregon then to work packing files of catalogues etc — until 7. Home to tea at 7.30 — Evef. looking over old letters & arranging small matters generally. To bed after 11. Rain commenced about 7 p.m. & fell quite heavily for two or three hours.
