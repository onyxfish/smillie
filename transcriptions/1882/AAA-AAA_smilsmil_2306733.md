## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 15, 1882

Beautiful mtg. but gradually growing overcast — After bkft. started out on an electioneering work, went to Rondel's H.P. Smith's. Ferris. Murphy's & Bunner's studios — 1.45 before I got home to my dinner — Then a rest, then to Sherwood bldg in 57th St. & called on Wyant, DeLuca & Anderson (not in) — then to 23rd St & called on Bircher — then to studio for a few minutes — Home at 6.15 a few minutes rest before tea — To N.A. at 7.30 — an exciting meeting of the N.C. Society — 51 members present only 4 new absent — in spite of strong feeling & much sulppressed but temper there was no outbreak — F.H. Smith had assured me that a majority of the Socy was pledged to him but the vote was 20 to 31 against him — meeting adjourned about 10.30 — I got home at 11.15 and after a good wash, to get smell of stale smoke out of hair & beard got to bed.

## Right Page

*Minnie Smillie married G. Walter Pierce.*

### March, Thursday 16, 1882

About an inch of snow fell during the night — Horrible walking — weather cleared slowly during the day — Feeling something of the effects of last night, but got to studio about 10 o'clk — Wm H. Beard came in & made a call — I worked for about an hour on a pen sketch for Kurtz's Academy notes "Elaine" after Hovenden — Home to dinner at 1 — After that went to barber's & had my hair cut — to studio at 4 — a call from Miss Towne, Dr. & Mrs. Stevens — Home at 6 — tea, then dressed — at 7.30 carriages arrived and Father, Mother, Matt, Kate, Annie & I went to church cor 53rd & Mad. Ave — At 8.20 Hepworth married Minnie & Walter — then drove to Lounsbury's, 20 W. 58th St. where the wedding reception was given — quite a brilliant crush — supper before 10 — Father & Mother stayed through — home at 10.50 — Talked over matters & to bed about 11.50
