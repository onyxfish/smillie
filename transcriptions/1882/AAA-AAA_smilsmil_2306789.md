## Left Page

### July, Saturday 1. 1882.

Charming day, breezy & cool with a wonderfully beautiful
color over the distance — The mf was spent at the house
going over everything & consulting.
Dinner soon after 1 & then again to the house — The first
load of our furniture, etc, came about 4 o'clk, just after a
very heavy shower. There was some damage from water, but
the most serious injury was the result of ignorance in packing
upon the wagon, some of the [illegible]
being badly marred. Worked hard over it, rubbing & scrubbing.
Tea at 6 o'clk — Annie & I went
to house after it & enjoyed sunset — then to P.O. & Read papers
filled with acct of hanging of
Guiteau — To Courtright's about
9 — to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 2. 1882.

Clear, windy & quite cold — at
8 Ther. stood at 57°. At its warmest
it was 72°. Bkfst about 8 — Annie
& I went over to house — there
some time — Went to Presbyt-
ch. & heard the new minister. I
was not very favorably impressed.
Dinner soon after 1 — Wrote letters
during the afternoon to Father, Geo.
Osgood & Co., Colman, Belows,
Farren & Mr. R. S. Gifford — a
short walk with Annie before
tea at 6 o'clk. Evng. some
more writing — at 9 o'clk, took
letters to P.O. Home & to bed
at 10.
