## Left Page

### August, Monday 28, 1882

Overcast w.f. but clearing all day. Austin commenced
painting on window blinds —
I commenced cutting duck
for our bed room & Backus
put it up — afternoon, worked
for awhile on B. N. Co — at
5.30 feeling used up, started
down the hill to join Annie
who went to a Lawn Tennis tournament on the de Schweinitz ground
— met her returning — Tea soon
after 6 — on the bed for awhile —
then with C. & Mrs. Mrs. C. to Florence
L's where with Percy Read & Alice
Stroud & Miss Gilbert we played
whist — I felt like a boiled owl
— 11 o'clk. before we started for
our bed where I was glad to
get.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 29, 1882

Delightful day — all the morning
cutting out duck that Backus
tacked up in Cherry Room — also
with Herrick, in bay window —
Tried to do a little on B. N. Co,
drawing — dinner as usual
— and afternoon as usual, that
is, closely following up the men.
Wrote a note to Will explaining
why I had not yet finished
a B. N. Co's drawing — worked
on it for awhile — Tea at 6.30
after tf I went to foundry to
look at Birch that had been got
out for balusters — not satisfactory —
then to lumber sheds & picked out
some cherry — After tea Annie &
I went down town & got glue, Tacks,
etc — I paid Mrs. Runyon $5.00
for fine brick — back to C's. Read
paper & to bed at 10.30 — on our
way up town called on Mrs. Wm.
Jessup —
