## Left Page

### June, Sunday 25, 1882

Hotter than ever — did not go
out of the house during the day
most of the m'f, lying about
— a little reading & a little
writing — Dinner at 2 — then
more lying about — managed
to write to Ehninger, Osgood
Stelphen Smith, old Kelly &
Father. Tea at 7 — Evening
spent very much as the day
had been — until about 10 —
when I went to deposit
with letters — on my return
a sleight spatter of Rain —
after a good towel bath
to bed after 11.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 26, 1882

Hot, but not so much so as yes-
terday — B'kf: at 7. 30 — at 8.30 went
to D. L. & W. R.R. freight yards at Ho-
boken & saw Mr. Gordônier who
assures me that we can get our
special car through in spite of
the strikers — then to N.Y. by Barclay
St. ferry. Went to Bradley & Currier
in Jay St. about doors — then to Carl
Reiffer's — no him — then to Charl's —
then to Selleck's in Fulton St. & bought
shirt — then to Sharp's, 13 Bax[t]er St. &
bought copper — then to Scoville Manu-
Co — then to 2nd Nat. Bank — then to studio,
home at 1 o'clk — Dinner at 1.30 — to
studio before 3 & spent afternoon there
packing — between 3 & 5 o'clk, very
heavy thunder showers. Annie, her
mother & some others went to Coney
Island — I worked until near 7 —
then home, quite worn out & had tea
all the ev'g, writing & arranging
things. A — Ret? at 10. 45 — to bed
at 11.30.
