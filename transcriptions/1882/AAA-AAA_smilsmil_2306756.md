## Left Page

### April, Sunday 30, 1882

Charming day — Up before 8 and did a little writing before bkfs —
shortly after 9. At 10.30 Annie &
I went to 48th St — bet. 6th & 7th Aves
to hear Rev. Heber Newton preach
upon the effect of Darwins doctrines upon the Christian Ch.
He is an out & out evolutionist
& insists that it is entirely consistent
with the highest forms of Christianity.
Home soon after 1 — dinner an hour
later — About 5 went to studio —
wrote some notes & part of my
home letter to Father. Soon after
6 called on Geo & wife. Home
to tea before 7 — Self, Annie
read awhile aloud — finished
Father's letter & took it to station
about 10 — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 1, 1882

Charming day — Up before 7 & for an hour
before bkfs. wrote in preparation for
a probable discussion at the Special
Water Color meeting called for next
Wl. evef. Bkfs. at 8.30 a little
more writing & at 10 went to studio.
T.W. Wood there — was surprised
by a call from Wm Bliss Baker who came
to confess his forgeries in painting imitations of de Haas, Bricher & Bierstadt.
He seems thoroughly humbled — Annie
came in while he was yet there. We
gave the boy some good advice, which
I hope he may remember. We walked
home to dinner at 1.30. Ned there & at
the house all night — Annie in trouble
about change of servants — the old ones
leave today — Got to studio at 3.30
feeling very much out of sorts — unable
to work — Geo moving his traps into
Colman's old studio. Home to tea
at 6.30 — at 7.30 went to Up town office
of Herald with advertisement for
cook etc — back soon after 8. Evening
divided between reading & writing.
& to bed about 10.30.
