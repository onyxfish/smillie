## Left Page

### March, Friday 3. 1882.

Brilliant day, but windy + dirty. Got to studio before 10 + spent the whole morning writing letters, mainly about Montrose matters — Home to dinner at 1.30 — back at 3 — much of p.m. Packing case of Poet + Etcher [illegible] to go to Boston — Home to tea at 6.30 — Evening, M. & Mrs. Buffon of Boston called — after they left at 9.30 — I went to Bates drug store in 6th Ave, for some medicines — back at 10.30 — wrote awhile and to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 4. 1882.

Fine day — but unable to get up owing to a headache — the first since I was married sufficiently severe to keep me in bed — It was not a very bad one however, for at 1 I was able to take a little food & at 4 I got up, had a bath & dressed — lounged in big chair until 6.30 — then had tea. After that wrote a letter to Mr. S. A. Coale Jun. of St. Louis, Me concerning my desire to Etch after Regnault's "Automedon" which he purchased the other every. At 9.30 went down to the Century Club. Monthly meeting & had a pleasant time — a long talk with T. Hop. Smith concerning the Water Color Soc'y strife. Mr. Rob. Gordon bought Geo's Academy picture "View on the Merrimac" to fill a commission given him by a friend, to send to England about midnight, walked up with Geo. to 28th S— then home & to bed & to sleep before 2 A.M.
