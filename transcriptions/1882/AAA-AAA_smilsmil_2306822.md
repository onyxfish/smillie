## Left Page

### September, Thursday 7, 1882

Bright, pleasant day, with steady east wind. B.&b. at 8. Soon after, A. & I started out for a tramp & made a circuit of the Neck giving a tolerably close inspection to cottages & shore. Everything very much injured by drouth — dust every where — Back to dinner at 1 o'clk. After that, a nap — About 3.30 A. & I went to wharf & signalled for a boat which came & we went to Marblehead & for an hour or two tramped about there — I did not find any of the picturesque old buildings that I had expected to — back to tea at 6 — quite tired — found that Mary Ireland had called while we were away — Eve'g passed stupidly, reading newspapers — to bed at 9.30.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 8, 1882

Still steady East wind — luminous hazy mist. After b.&b. fixed up my W.C. traps & at about 10 Annie & I went out on our first sketching tramp for the season. Settled down upon a height back of the house — taking a view of Causway to Mainland & the old Devereux farm — an effect of color that was extremely pleasing to me — but I got very little of it — worked until near 1 — then to dinner — Thunder storms all about, but none reached us — At 3, horse & buggy came & A. & I drove to Will Cook's at Beach Bluff where we had a short visit with Will & Ivie. Then back & took a short turn about Marblehead — to house at 6 — after severe labor with the most indifferent beast of a horse that I ever drove — tea — then a short wander with A — then in — another storm threatened severely but did'nt come — a telegram from Geo. saying he'd be here to-morrow — to bed at about 10.
