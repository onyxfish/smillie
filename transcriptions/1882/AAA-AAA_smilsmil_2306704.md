## Left Page

### January, Saturday 14, 1882

Blustering, but not unpleasant day — a slight, March-like snow flying in the m'f. Annie walked with me to studio after b'kp', getting there at 9.45, to see W.C's before they go to the ex'b'tn. I did little during the m'f. except to finish a dog in Nellie's picture. Home to dinner at 1.15 — back at 3 + being tired + headachy after Rossell came + took away the pictures, I did little else. My Blk. W. is not finished. hope yet to get it in. W.P. Colman made a visit — I finished a few sketch of Evening Shadows for the Boston Art Club Cat. Home to tea about 6 — Ev'f. Phil Concert. Edith on from Phil — E. + A. went home + I to Century. Oscar Wilde the lion — much looked at + much quiet fun + satire — men don't like him — one said "It looks like a Magdalene" Flint said "She looks as much like a man as she can." It was dressed as usual — a male Bloomer. Home + to bed at midnight —

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 15, 1882

Beautiful day — late b'kp'. After looking over m'f. paper, in front of a charming wood fire, I started out for a walk — went through Central Park, beyond the Obelisk — on my way back stopped for a few minutes at A.V.V.'s dat Will's — Home to dinner at 2 — After that, a little reading + a nap. Edith went home by 5 train. Annie + I went up to A.V.V.'s and made a call. Home at 6.15 — tea soon after — Ch. Read aloud for an hour — then I wrote for an hour or more — then took home letter to G.C. depot — back + to bed at midnight.
