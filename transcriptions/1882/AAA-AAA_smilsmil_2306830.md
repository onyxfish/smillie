## Left Page

### September, Saturday 23. 1882.

Mild day, struggling without success
to clear. Several light showers.
I was quite used up by an un-
accountable dearcoah — Worked
all the m'f on lower half plans.
Dinner as usual & in the
afternoon again worked on
plans. Tea at 6 — Rather
dismal ev'e'g — all tired
of the stormy weather and
consequent imprisonment —
the storm being a most un-
interesting one — no fine skies
— no fine surf — I worked
from 9 until after 10 on plans
to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 24. 1882.

Another dismal day — Rain
& wind on waking in the m'f.
my trouble not relieved and
feeling quite weak & dejected.
Bkf't at 8.30 — About 11 went
to bed again & had a nap — Up
for dinner at 1.30 — Lying
about all the afternoon — Annie
wrote my letter home to father
to which I added a couple
of pages — Young Bassett called
for a few minutes at 6 — Finte
a fine sunset but wind still
N. of E — Tea at 6 — Ev'e'g more
lying about, chatting & reading
to bed about 10.
