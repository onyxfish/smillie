## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 4, 1882.

A real April day, struggling sunshine & shadow with many
showers at night. Got to studio
at 10 & spent most of time
getting cards in envelopes &
directed according to a list
sent down by Kate. Went to P.
O. station & to Bank & other
running about. Home to
dinner soon after 7. About
3.30 Annie & I started out,
went to Flint's in 14th St. and
selected 3 sets of bed Room fur-
niture etc. for Montrose. Home
& tea at 6.30. Geo & Valentine then.
At 7.30 A. & I went to Thalia Theatre
Bowery near Canal. Party of Collins
Mama & Julie S. Eliz. Howe, Geo. &
Val. joined us there. Strauss'
comic opera "The Merry War" very
well given, out at 10.45 — all went
in to Atlantic Garden & had beer
& pretzels — then home & to bed
at 1½.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 5, 1882.

Rain in the mrg. — overcast p.m.
& at night. Got to studio before 10.
Then to Kurtz Gallery. Varnishing
Day of Young Americans. Spent an
hour & a half looking at their new
exhib'n & chatting with friends.
Then to studio — wrote letters &
home to dinner at 1.45 — after
that my head ached so that
I lay down & slept until near
4. Then went to New Man's ab't
clocks for Montrose house. To studio
at 4.45 — worked on charcoal
sketch for "bed oaken bucket" & [illegible]
wrote another letter or two —
Home to tea at 7.15. Rest &
lounging on sofa. Annie
read aloud from Prescott & [illegible]
played upon Piano. To bed
before 11.30.
