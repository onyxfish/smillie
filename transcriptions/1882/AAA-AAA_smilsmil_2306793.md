## Left Page

### July, Sunday 9, 1882

Muggy & overcast — B'kf't at 8 —
Atg to house, here until 10.30
then with Annie went to Epis.
Ch. & heard Mr. Wainmer "Give
no thought to the morrow" —
After service, to our Room &
wrote awhile — Dinner at
1 — After that Annie & I
went to the house — Weather
cleared charmingely — I
tidied up the Studio, un-
packing some of the chairs
& made the place quite
home-like — Mr. Countright,
his wife & Florence [illegible] — made
us a call — also Ben Mulford.
Tea at 6 — About 7 returned
to studio & wrote as long as
we could see — then enjoyed
beautiful sunset — sat up on
piazza until near 9 — then to
our quarters — wrote to [illegible]
Took letters to P.O.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 10, 1882

Delightful cloudy, breezy day
— showers to the south of us —
The wife, as usual, went to
house after B'kf't — & then down
town, where I spent most of
the m'f. Got my last will
& testament from Wm. Jessup
& had it witnessed by Hunt,
Jessup & Saul Sayre — Dinner
at 1 — a long nap — to house
at 3.30 — general work —
Tea at 6 — Mrs. Dav. Sayre
& her sister in law, Mrs. Groshick
called & we went to the house
& until quite dark we went
over & about the house, then
down town, Annie stopping
at Dav. Sayre's while I went
in to P.O. — on my way back
I stopped for a — sat on
piazza until after 9 — then
to our quarters — Read
newspaper & to bed
soon after 10.
