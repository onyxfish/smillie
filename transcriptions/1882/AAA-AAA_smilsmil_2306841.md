## Left Page

### October, Sunday 15, 1882

Superb day, brilliantly clear &
balmy — Bkfs. at 9 — very soon after
Wm. Mulford came with two gentle
men (whose names I forget) wanted
to see something of the house. One of the
gentlemen was the Rep. candidate
for Gov. of Pa. I spent half an hour
with them. The rest of the m'f.
went in duties & loitering among
the trees until 12. Then in and
read until 1.30 — About 2 had
dinner alone as Annie was
suffering from head ache. I
took a nap after dinner — heard
numerous visitors on piazza but
did not go down to see any of
them — wrote until near 5 — then
out & rambled over Warner's lot
until after sundown — Ravishing
near 7 when we had tea —

## Right Page

### October, Monday 16, 1882

Delightful m'f. but overcast before
noon — a little rain at night. Bkfs.
soon after 8. Pettus commenced to
plough & with Payne, his team & boy
the work of grading was fairly com-
menced. Went down town about 10.
Saw Eldred, Thatcher & Clatrons about
the color of paint now being put on
school house — Home at 10.30, had
boy made, some tiles put in it &
took it to S&p. Saw Sattirals about
disputed bill — no result — home
again before noon — feeling out of sorts
in a dyspeptic way — ate very little
dinner — Spent most of p.m. in
overlooking Paine & Pettus, also man
who moved furnace coal from cellar &
replaced it with a smaller size — Rec'd
telegram saying that Fanny & Sib H.
would start for here to-morrow — on
bed until 6 — then tea — Evening
very quiet in steamer chair —
Had a game of cards with Annie
& to bed soon after 10.
