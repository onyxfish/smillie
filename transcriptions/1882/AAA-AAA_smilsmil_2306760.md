## Left Page

### May, Monday 8, 1882

Unable to get up in the m.f.
being feverish & having much
soreness in the chest & so hoarse
& to be almost unable to speak.
Sent for Dr. Yale who came at
11.30. By that time my symptoms
were better — but he advised
caution. I did not try to
get out of my bed until about
7 p.m. when I made a transfer
to big chair & blankets in front
room. Then had tea — Slept
much of the time. Annie read
aloud to me, my head feeling
much relieved. Father came
at 3 o'clk. He ret'd. to Po' by 4 o'clk
train — Geo. called at 5.30 —
I got to bed at midnight —
not sleepy as I had slept
during the day.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 9, 1882

Delightful day — woke up after a
tolerably restful night; got up before
8 — dressed & had my breakfast up stairs
— very much better — soreness in chest
nearly all gone, but weak & generally
sore. Will called between 9 & 10 o'clk.
Dinner as usual — Dr. Yale
came while we were yet at
table about 2 o'clk — About
4 Annie & I went out and
sauntered through Reservoir
Park — stopped at Park & Tilford's
— then home — Tea between 6
& 7 — Slept. Annie read
aloud one of Miss Thackeray's
stories. To bed about 10.30.
