## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 3, 1882

Weather variable & colder than yesterday — at times overcast —
the afternoon, very clear — About
9 went with Colman to his
house & was there more than an
hour, being very much interested —
Then I wandered off alone for
a time, & got back to Cottage
at 11.30 — Annie did not get
up until noon as yesterday's
work had over tired her — Sat in
front of fire until dinner at 1.30 —
About 3.30 — C + I went out in
his dog-cart & rode about look-
ing at the new & fine houses — back
at 5 — again in front of fire —
Mrs. C. played on piano — tea
at 6.30 — Hvelf., more piano —
chatting & to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 4, 1882

Str. "Providence" — Stateroom 59.

Glorious, cloudless day — Bkfst about
6 — an hour later, went to C's new ho.
& as he was not ready to go to [W.?] I —
Richards' with me I gave up the
trip for the [only?] — & went to the cliffs
& from the Bath Road followed nearly
all the way around, giving especial
attention to the houses, new or in course
of construction, of Gorlet, Cath. Wolfe, etc.
came out upon Bellevue Ave. at [Elznaga?]
court & back to C's — getting in at 12.20
very tired — Rested on lounge until 1.15
then dinner — more resting, again giving
up the Richards trip. Soon after 4, C.
came & took us all out to ride, went
a ways out on Ocean Drive — S[?] wind rather
too cool & brisk to be pleasant — drove
about & saw a number of the new build-
in at 5.30 — tea soon after 6, Col. Geo.
Waring joined us at table — made him-
self very pleasant — At 8.30 — hack — we
drove down to boat which came late
& did not leave until 10½ — after
leaving we went right to bed.
