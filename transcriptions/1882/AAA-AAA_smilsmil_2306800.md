## Left Page

### July, Sunday 23, 1882

Very delightful day, bright & breezy
& cool in the shade — B[illegible]: soon after
9 & about 10 went to the house —
most of the m[illegible], as I felt out of sorts.
I spent in big chair on studio
piazza, part of the time Annie reading
to me. Dinner at 1.30 — then again
to studio — lounged in big chair —
wrote to Father & Ge[illegible]. Mistrammed
visitors & went to tea at 6 — again
to studio — watered trees — Will
& Minnie Mulford, Alice Foster,
Miss Cook & Mr. Cortis came & went
over the house with us in the
twilight — lovely moonlight —
after 8 went to P.O. with our
letters — then to C's — Annie
read aloud to me until 10.30
— then to bed —

## Right Page

### July, Monday 24, 1882

Clear, warm day — over to house
before B[illegible] — & gave some directions
to B[illegible] — & back about 9. Trouble
with the stair-builders — Burgess
I think is stupid — he has confused
me & I have allowed him to
misdirect me — took the problem
in hand myself & ordered a return
to my original plan — To dinner
at 1 — back soon after 2 & made
a drawing & finish for lower
hall & reached a solution of
the stair difficulty — the day the
warmest one yet — To tea soon
after 6 & in about an hour Ret'd —
to studio. To P.O. — about 8 &
on our way up stopped at Wm
Mulford's — many young people
there — sat on piazza visiting
with the old folks until about
9 — then to C's & read paper
until after 10 — nearly when
we got to bed.
