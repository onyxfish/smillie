## Left Page

### May, Saturday 20. 1882.

Weather still unfavorable — rains but later it cleared to be delightful in spite of a chill in the air. Got to studio at 9.30 & worked closely all the mng on Montrose plans — Dinner as usual. — back at 3 & worked until 6.30. Then a walk in Mad. Sq. & home to tea at 9 — [illegible] Mr. & Mrs. Phillips Lockwood called — they left before 9 — At near 10 I went out & had a short walk — back & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 21. 1882.

Bright & warm mng. B[illegible]: at 8.30 — went with Annie to hear Dr. Rob't. Collyer. He preached a sermon on Elijah & drew a spiritual lesson from dyspepsia. Went home with a — then to studio — there until I went home to dinner at 2. After that, a lounge, then at 4 went with a. to Walter's & after half an hours visit there we went on to George's where we made a short call on Nellie & the infant. Then out to 6th Ave. R.R. & rode up to 8th Ave. & 72d St. Crossed the Park, out at 72d St. & 5th Ave. & home by Mad. Ave. cars at 6.45 — then had tea — the afternoon, overcast, very warm & a very little rain. Everf wrote a letter to father — took it to depot before 10 — then back & to bed.
