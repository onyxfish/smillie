## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 16. 1882.

A very hot day, but not without some hopeful promises of showers
in the afternoon that did not come — The day spent as
usual following up the men especially Backus who is filling
the wood in S.W. bedroom. It
is quite an experiment. Hamlin
got started on little chimney
on west side of house — Dinner
as usual — Afternoon as usual.
Tea as usual — Watered trees
— then A & J to P.O. & at about
6.30 we went to Bethel Ch. to see
the "Cake walk", an absurd fiasco
that was extremely funny from
its childlike mismanagement
& failure — absurd overdressing
of the negro girls — melodeon, bad
singing — Brisco performs very dimly
— execrable music by Brass band
— left at 10.30 — to C's & to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 17. 1882.

A cool breezy, cloudy day — When
I went to the house after bkfst was
informed that Hamlin was out of brick
— 250 only required, So Annie & I went
down town at once — bought the brick
of Stebbins, then went to Sayre's — saw
him & arranged to have him draw
them to the house before noon —
back to studio at 11 — to work on the
drawings for finish for bed room (any)
Dinner at 1 — again on drawing
— also watching Finker at work about
the chimneys — & Burgess who has got
the stair up to bay window platform.
Herrick working in bay window —
Tea at 6 — the Mrs. Courtright & children
picnicking on fairground — After tea
Annie & I went to P.O. then to delchmin-
ity's — robin — then to Dud Lathrop's
— then spent 40 m. buying tacks —
then up the hill & stopped at Dan
Sayre's & saw the Comstocks — to C's
at 9.30 — read newspapers & to
bed before 10.30.
