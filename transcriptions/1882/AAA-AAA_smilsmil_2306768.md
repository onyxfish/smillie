## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 24, 1882

Brilliant, beautiful day - Bkfs:
at 8 - then for a while walked Pigg.
with A - then wrote a letter to Cotrill
& before 10 o'clk. Rode by car into
the city with A & Mother C - when
there, they going their way & I
mine - Went first to Andrew & Son,
engravers. 5 Temple R. Saw them
& Hf. Apnf after my ill. of Old Oaken Bucket —
made my criticisms & had a pleasant
visit — then to Old State House & bought
tickets to Newport & State Rooms for Friday
night — then to Tremont S: & to Osgood's
about 2.11 — from there to the Common & saw
some of the Evolutions of a large number
(said to be 1200) of school boys under arms —
then to Boston Art Club & saw collection
of Water Colors — then by horse car rode
back & to Parker Ho. abt. to got dinner at
Tremont Ho. Café — then to Park Ho. & wrote
letter to Sam Colman — then to bos. of [illegible]
able Redp & wandering until 3.30 then to
Bowdoin Sq. & took car to Auburn — About
4.30 went with Will to see lawn tennis —
more wandering alone — then home
before 6 — dinner at 6.30 — at 7.30 Will,
his Mother, Annie, Kat & I went to
Sanders Theatre (a sort of Memorial Hall
Benefit Concert, Harvard Glee Club &
Pierian Sodality) (Instrumental) The
music & execution were neither of a
high order — some good voices but not
very fine taste or cultivation — amused until
10.15 — then home & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 25, 1882

Bkfs. before 8 - a sickly sunlight early
in the m'g, but by H'ps. time a grey
veil covered all, after 6 kfs. Annie &
I walked over to Elisha Mulford's in
Bodoim St, she Ret. to Will's, I went in
& had a very pleasant call of 3/4 of an
hour — then I wandered about and to
the Agassiz & Peabody museums where
I was immensely interested, especially
by the Mound Builder's skulls shown
in the Peabody museum — at 12 went
to the Square & met Will & with him
went to the Gymnasium inn — over that
had a very pleasant chat with Dr.
Sargent, its Director — out at one o'clk
raining hard — & so it continued all
the afternoon — lunch at 1.30 — a little
time in the parlor — then to our room
a short nap — Patterson I — called about
4 — Dinner soon after 6 — Evef. in
parlor chatting & looking over portfolios
of prints — Elisha Mulford & wife expected
but they did not come — at 10 a short
walk with A. on balcony — cloudless
moonlight & thin, luminous fog — to bed
at 10.30.
