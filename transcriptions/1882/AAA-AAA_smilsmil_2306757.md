## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 2, 1882

Partially overcast — windy and cold — out of sorts — got up early &
had a short time before bkfst —
at writing. To studio about 10 —
Tried to work on Portland O.
drawing, but too sore for it —
Dawdled about until dinner.
Fime about 1, then home — Back
to studio 2.45 — Mr. Fernando
Waller & Miss Horn called. Annie
also in for a few minutes — After
4 o'clk. I went to Spring Ricks
& Met. Museum in Central Park —
Had a hurried view & out at 6 —
Home in time for dinner, Wm & Mrs.
Coleman there — then we all went
to first concert of musical festival at
7th Regt. Armory. Some very grand
effects for eye & ear. M. & Mrs. C. are
staying at Grand Hotel — will dine
with us for two or three days —
We got home about 10.45 — to
bed near 12.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 3, 1882

Brilliant, breezy day — air very cool
in the streets, full of dust. Got up
feeling quite used up — to studio at
9.30 — Spent the m'f at studio with
callers and in finishing writing
my address intended for Water Color
meeting to-night. Home to lunch at
12.30 & at about 1.30 — Annie, Mr. Coleman
& I went to Musical Festival — enjoyed
the concert very much — a vast crowd.
After it, at 4.30, A. & Mrs. C. Returning, I
walked in the park — home at 6, then
a resting spell — Dinner at 6.30, Mr.
& Mrs. C. there — they again went to the
festival while I went to Special meeting
of W. C. 32 members present — Smith in
very bad temper — with no friends to back
him made a violent harangue — after
him I quietly read my address, which
seemed to fall with very good effect —
at any rate, my resolution justifying
the Board of Control which Smith has as-
sailed so vehemently was carried
without opposition — out at 10 — then
home & to bed before 11.
