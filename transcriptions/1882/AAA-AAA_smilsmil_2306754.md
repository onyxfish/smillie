## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 26, 1882

Overcast and rather raw — Got to studio soon after 9 — About 10 T. W. Wood came in + we had a talk about Water Color Soct'y matters + T. H. Smith's persistence in calling a Special meeting. Spent the rest of the morning writing letters etc. — Home to dinner at 1 and back to studio at 2.30 — Got out my Portland Oregon. drawing but did not get fairly started before Farrer + then T. W. Wood came in — a Board meeting called for 4.30 — I saw the members as they came + at 4.30 went to Chickering Hall to hear Rehearsal of Annie's Vocal Club — about 6 we Ret'd to studio — Board meeting just adjourned — walked home + had tea a little before 7 — Ely. $t. Nelia played whist against a. + J. until near 10 — To bed before 11 — Some heavy rain.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 27, 1882

Rain in the morning, dry but cloudy at night. Got to studio at 9.30 + tried to work on Portland drawing but it is so distressingly uninteresting that I could not make any progress — Home at 1 much depressed — back at 2.30 to meet Farrer with proof of call for W.C. [illegible] at my work doing little meeting nothing until near 5 — then as it did not rain I went out to do some shopping + get a little air — a drizzle came on followed by a semi-clearing. Home to tea at 6 — Dressed — into a company — $t. + Nelia, Ely, Howe, Julie Spring, Geo. + Valentine — at 7.30 two carriages — Geo. Valentine, Julie + Mother took in one, Ely. Annie + I in the other to Chickering Hall Concert of The Choral Club which went off very nicely — out at 10.30 Home + to bed soon after 11.
