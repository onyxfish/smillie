## Left Page

### June, Friday 23, 1882

Hot day, but not without a pleasant breeze — After bkfp.
wrote notes to Cottrell, Fattner
& LaRoche — About 10 went to
Studio — into Tiffany's & with Mr.
Mitchell went over selected glass,
weighing & measuring it — Then
to Studio, getting things together
for packing — At 2.30 went to
Bank — Home soon after 1 and
had dinner — Rested until
after 3 — then to Tiffany's and
packed glass — At 5.30 went
to Pike's about Thermometers —
again to Studio — there until
6.30 — then home — Tea soon
after 7 — Rested until after 8 —
then on window glass diagrams
until 11 — Mr. Chas. Collins
called but I did not see
him — To bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 24, 1882

A very hot day. Some writing after
bkfp. then to Renner's at 9.30, settled
up my acct. for the counter & inquired
about a frame for [illegible], as requested.
Annie met me there, went to Bates, the
druggists — then Rode to 73 Hudson St.
where we spent some time selecting
tiles — then to Semon & Bache's in Duane
St. & finished order for window glass, then
to Nippon Trading Co., [illegible] near Duane
St. — ordered 3 doz. lanterns (Chinese)
then home to dinner at 2.15 — After
that spent most of the afternoon
resting — near 5 when I went to
Studio — there for about an hour & a
half getting things together — Home
to tea at 7 — Thunderstorm to north of
us, we got only a spatter — At 9 o'clk
went to Studio with 3 doz. lanterns
& brought back box with 25 lbs. of
Tiffany glass. Had a [illegible]
to bed after 11.
