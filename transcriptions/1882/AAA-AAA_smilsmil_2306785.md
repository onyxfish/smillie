## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 27. 1882.

Charming day. altho' warm a delightful breeze blew steadily. After Bkfs; went to Lewis & Conger. Sloane's & Newman's — then to studio & spent most of the mg. assisted by Wm, the Janitor, in packing a large case & two boxes with things for Montrose. Home to dinner at 1.30 — After that a Rest — about 3.30 went to 29th St. & ordered Cat irons for front window — then to Tiffany's, Sincrusta, Arnold & Constable's & Price's — To studio a little before 5.30 — wrote to Osgood & Co. enclosing orders from Coleman, James, Bellows & Mr. R. S. Gifford. — then to Effs Co. with Copy of P. & E. that I Rd. to D. & Co. Home at 6.30. Tea at 7. Dwg. Connelly came and arranged to take charge of freight sent to Hoboken to-morrow — Fanny & Will K. called. To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 28. 1882.

Hot day. Overcast & a few drops of rain about 7 a.m. After Bkfs; went to studio — then to Am. Tile Co. Then to Semon Bache's — then to Varick St. to look for an earth closet man that I did not find; then to Himmel & Voigt's — then home at 11.30. pretty well used up with a headache — tied & strapped up bundles — Dinner at 1.30 — at 3 Gurney's Effs. came & took trunks & bundles — then to studio where he got cases etc. — then to Hoboken getting to freight car about 4.30 — Everything there except Heine's furniture. At 5 went to Heine's — furniture had been sent one ¾ an hour — back to car. furniture there & 4 men packing it into car — some of it properly covered, some not at all — the yard men bothering to get car out of way — done at 6.45 — very tired. Had hurling — home & had tea at 8 — Geo. came in from P'k. Stayed a little while — late, Geo. & tried cook & Valentine came — to bed at midnight.
