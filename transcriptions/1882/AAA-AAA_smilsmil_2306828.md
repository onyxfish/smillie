## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 19, 1882

A day like yesterday, breezy &
hazy — after bkfst. Spent some
time getting my oil traps in to
working order — It was nearly
10.30 when I started out for
a study north of the house —
worked hard until 1 — then in
to dinner — after that, a rest
before 3, out again to same study
& worked until after 4 when I
finished it. Annie came out.
I cleaned up & went with her
to walk — on our return found Mr.
& Mrs. Bassett at the house — they
made a short call — Tea soon
after 6. Eveng. Made a P.O.
stamp view of Marblehead to
send to Fanny K — on her birth-
day, to-morrow.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 20, 1882

Very warm day. Got up with a wretched
headache — for some time could not
determine what to do, whether to go to
bed or try to work — After 10 took a very
slow walk up to Castle Rock study
where I tried to finish pen sketch —
so much trouble with my pens that
I made but little progress. Annie
joined me before noon — thunder
storm threatening, passing to the south —
at 12.30 As I went to Club stable and
ordered carriage for this p.m. at 3 — then
to dinner at 1 — Mr. Chas. Bates arr. — announcing
that his yacht "Gracie" would soon be up from
Cohasset. I went to bed & a. countermanded
order for carriage — in less than an hour I
got up & with A — Geo., Nellie, baby and
nurse & Mr. B. went out in small boat — the
Gracie having gone around into the harbor
the men had to pull away around the neck
— we found the Gracie & a Bates family party
also Capt. & Mrs. Tower, former owner of the
yacht — the wind died out & anchor was
cast — we remained on board until after
5 — a slight shower fell, then cleared beautiful —
some of the party returned to Boston by R.R. —
we were put ashore & got to house in time
for tea — As I promised, if weather was fair
to go to Boston on the yacht to-morrow at
7 a.m. — Stilp'd evening & to bed before
10 o'clk., after having made arrangements
to get an early breakfast.
Mrs. [illegible] & Miss Small left this m'g.
