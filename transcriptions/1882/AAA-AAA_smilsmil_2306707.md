## Left Page

### January, Friday 20, 1882

Dull day, with now & then a
faint flurry of snow. Got to
studio at 9.30 — set palette &
to work. Repainting 12×20 —
"September Afternoon, with cows"
Painted a couple of years
ago — Changed the sentiment
entirely — Home to dinner at
1 — back about 3 — The first
sight of my morning's work
showed me that I was all
astray in color — Tried to correct
it — could not — Wrote several
notes — home to tea at 7 — sat &
Annie Reading Morris aloud
— about 9.30 I went out for
a walk — Stars — To bed
at [illegible].

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 21, 1882

Heavy Rain nearly all day — got
to studio at 9.45 — so dark that I
did no painting — Wrote to Cottrell
& other notes — had calls etc —
Home to dinner at 1 — at 2 went
with Annie to Booth's Theatre
& saw Mary Anderson in Gilbert's
Pygmalion & Galatea — Disappointed
in the play; it lacks sparkle and
fullness — The acting was poor —
Soon after 4, out — went to Gibbons'
& selected some furniture covering
for a Reclining chair — then in
to Bedell's & looked at crockery — then
to Tiffany's & looked at more crockery
— then to chair place in 14th St. — then
rode home — Tea at 6.45 — Evening
lounging and reading — To bed
soon after 10.

To day was astonished by an
invitation from the Socy of Am.
artists to send a painting to their
coming exhibition. Are they
growing more liberal or am
I growing broader?
