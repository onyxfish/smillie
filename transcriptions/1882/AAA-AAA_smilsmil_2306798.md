## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 19, 1882

Warm day with some showers. Got to the house about 9. Payne hauling stone for wall at East end of house — Stair men & Sayre men at work — everything seemed like hurry & progress — All the w.f. following up the men. Dinner at 1 — back at 2.30 — Clifford did a little work on the wall, a mere start, late in the afternoon. Tea soon after 6 — did not go down town but A. & I went again to house & there met Mr. Chas. Larthrops & Miss Hammond — went over the house with them of course — Stayed in studio until quite dark — then to C's & A. Read aloud Cluster & Hearth until 10 — to bed soon after —

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 20, 1882

Delicious cool day — got to house about 9 — let water on to the pumps & pumped some water up into tank & tried the fobs — Spent a good deal of time over the glass getting it into the sash under the bay windows in hall — Dinner as usual & the usual general oversight until after 4 when Aunt B. came — About 5, she, Annie & I — in the old cabriolet & the two blk. horses started for Youngs away south of town, after looking at stone there we came back by Wyalusing & Darkie town roads & had tea about 6.30 — after that I went down town & spent some time at Boyd's looking over locks & knobs — Back to C's at 9.30 — To bed soon after 10 — Mr. Harold Cortis of Brooklyn, N.Y. joined the family circle this eveg.
