## Left Page

### August, Monday 14, 1882

A pleasant day with some threats of showers — a wretched nervous headache ruined the day for me — so that I could do but little work — soon after noon I went down town to P.O. & to Boyd's. Dinner soon after 1 — In the afternoon did nothing but lie about the house watching the men until 6 o'clk — In the mtf. Hauling got started on taking down 8 or 10 courses from the top of the big chimney, which being laid last Nov. while it was freezing, was not safe — I spent part of mtf with him — Payne brought up 550 brick from Bums. Tea at 6 — Eveth. about 8 — Aunin & I went to P.O. — to Boyd's & then to Bethel (negro) church where they are giving a three days entertainment entitled "A camp in the Wilderness" — a very poor affair — back to C's at 9.30 — to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 15, 1882

A mtf of Aug. haze, but delightful air — To house about 9 — Aunin walked me up & down piazza for awhile for exercise — Then I fixed a hammock for her & I went to work at my drawings. Dinner as usual — Put in a days work, feeling better than yesterday — At 4 o'clk. A. & I went to the ball ground to see a game of base ball — the "Grasshoppers" agt. "a picked nine" — Pleasant breeze — shade — young people — mixed excitement — our friends being in the picked nine — until 6.30 — game over, our [illegible] badly beaten — To tea at 7 — Mr. Purle & Mr. Atherton called — Then A & I went to P.O. — started for the Bethel entertainment, but gave it up & went to C's — Read our papers & to bed soon after 10.
