## Left Page

### March, Friday 31, 1882

Clear day — cool, with strong wind & lots of dust. After 10
when I got to studio & commenced a sketch in oil, black & white,
for Miss Sartain, forwood sug-
from study of an old apple orchard
1.30 when I got home to dinner
about 3 when I got back &
worked during the afternoon
inspite of considerable dyspeptic
discomfort — near 7 when I
got home — tea — At 8.30 — at
Nicoll's Studio — Etching Club
meeting — went down to pro-
pose certain amendments
to the constitution with a view
to making us a club of workers.
— harmonious meeting — about
10 walked into home — beautiful
moonlight — to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 1, 1882

Overcast w&. with a little
rain — clearing later — very
windy & dirty — After b.f. went
up to Buckingham to see Will —
Too late — has given up going to
Cal. for the present — then to Lahock's
— then to Renner & Co's — then, at 11.30
to studio — worked on sketch of
New England apple orchard. Home
to dinner at 1.15 — Mother, Cook
& D. Adams went to Ridgefield —
back to studio before 3 — there
until near 7 — wrote a note to
Miss Sartain — then home to tea.
At 8.30 to Nell, for a few minutes
soon after — got to Century & heard
a portion of a very entertaining paper
read by Jno. Durand concerning
the early history of the Sketch Club —
"the twenty one" — etc — Predecessors of
the Century — With Geo. had a long
free talk with F.H. Smith concerning
the W.C. Socy squabble — no signs of
amicability — walked up home at
11 in a light rain — To bed at 12.
