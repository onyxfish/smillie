## Left Page

### December, Saturday 16, 1882

more cold, wind & flurry — got to studio before 10 & to work upon W.C. Lake 9o. — met man, then home to dinner. After that, at 2.45, back to studio & worked as long as I could see — Matt & Kate called in the m'f. Farrer called in the afternoon — At 4.30 I went to Terpple's, 23 E. 16th S. & saw him about Haden's visit to his city, then to Century where Haden com. met & discussed plans for his coming lectures — I was made Sec'y & Treas. of com. Home at 6.30 — tea — at 8 to Steinway Hall with Annie. Gounod's Trilogy "The Redemption" — Hall very cold so that I was out of humor. The work seemed to be rather tiresome in parts & generally common place — the orchestration was agreeable & some of the choruses very fine. Home at 11 & to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 17, 1882

Charming winter day, altho' somewhat gusty at times. Up & [illegible] soon after 9 — wrote letters until 12 — then walked up to Central Park, about here & back home at 1.40 — out with Annie for a short walk & back to dinner at 2 — In the afternoon more writing to Koehler, Osgood & Condit — At 5.30 Annie & I went to Kimball's to see Matt & Kate who are to return to Po'k to-morrow — accepted an invitation to stay to tea — Home about 9 & to bed at 10.30.
