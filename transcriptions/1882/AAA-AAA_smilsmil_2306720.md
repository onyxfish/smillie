## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 15, 1882

Clear & very mild, got to studio before 10 — much of the morning spent in framing etchings of "Lady of Cairo"; sold at N.A. Tried a little charcoaling, feeling for a picture — Went to N.A. & home to dinner at 10. Nellie am. from Ridgefield. Back to studio at 3 — framed another etching — took it to N.A. Then to Dr. La Roche's at 4.45 — there until near 6 — went to Bates' drug store & was detained some time. Home to tea at 6.30. The curtains made over by Arnold Constable & Co. were sent home & put up to-day — Nelly, Nellie, Annie, their mother and I played whist until near 10 — To bed soon after.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 16, 1882

Mild, overcast day — heavy rain at night — 10 o'clk when I got to studio — until 12 wrote letters — then to Hunter's, Optician — then to Bank (2°) then to Brentano's & to F.S. Church's — 2.13' S° with sketch of "Dec. Moonlight" for L'Art. Home to dinner at 1 — back at 3 — sent some drawings of sashes to Cotrell & at 4.15 at La Roche's in 54th S — on my way home met Uncle Willie in 38th S — home at 5.15 — then, with Annie went to Mr. Wetherbee's (& Mrs. Robt. Black) at 228 Mad. ave. Recep[tion]. Home soon after 6 — Haven Putnam, Murna, Grace & Fanny to tea — Haven left before 8 & then I took the girls to car — at 9.30 went to studio — fixed fine — then for A. to singing clues — home at 10.45 — having given up our original purpose of then dressing & going to Reception at Mr. Geo. Jones — W. 37th S. To bed about 11.30.
