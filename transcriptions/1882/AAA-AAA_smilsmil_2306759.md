## Left Page

### May, Saturday 6, 1882

father came down from
Po'k. upon a P.T. business.

Miserable day — cold & raw — Rains before noon — falling heavily until after 5 — then clearing — Got to Studio before 10 & spent the m'f on plans for Montrose Window Sash for Etching Room. Nearly 1.30 when I got to lunch. At 2.30 took Mother Cook to dep't. & started her all right for Ridgefield. — then to Studio at about 3.30 — Fenn waiting for me. Then Weldon came in — Gave Fenn a long lesson in Etching. Near 6 when he left — then home. M.&M.P. Colman to dinner. After that to last concert of Musical Fes'. Telegram from Father that he would be at house about 7.30 — the others went to the concert — I waited for him — he came at 8.15 — gave him his tea while he explained the A.P.T. has at last given indisputable evidences of insanity — He (F.) has come down to consult with Judson Thorpe. A.P.T. left the Terrace a week ago to-day. At 8.45 I left Father & went to Armory where I joined Annie — Frightfully hot — wash hall jammed by a wild audience — a sight in itself — tumultuous ovation to Thomas — out at 10.45 — Home soon after 11 & to bed about 12.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 7, 1882

Pleasant day — air still & strangely cool. Bl'k't. bet. 8 & 9 o'clk — About 10 I went with Father to Wills's & after a few minutes there walked back & up 6th Ave. with Will & Walter Pierce. Father & I went to 42d S. Presb'n Ch. to hear Rev. Payson & see Charles. I sat in gallery, Father down stairs. I cannot say that on second hearing W. P. pleased me any better than he did at first. After service, Father & I walked home. Then I went down to Geo's & saw him & Nellie — back — Chan at the house — dined with us at 2. Jack Downs also there. Geo. & Will came about 3.30 or 4 & a family Talk without Result of course, followed. All left before 6 — we had tea about 7 — after that I went out with Fathe. He went to Peddie's Ch. & I went to drug store & to depot with letter. Then Back — Annie read aloud for a while — To bed soon after 10.
