## Left Page

### May, Monday 22, 1882

*S/w Rhode Island. Stateroom 39 –*

Oppressively warm — very bright
& sunny — Latin, overcast & at night
on the boat, really cold. Got
to studio about 9.30 & worked
hard all the m[orning] on Montrose
drawings without accomplishing all I hoped. — Bet. 10 & 11 went
to Bank & at 5th Ave. Hotel office
bought tickets to Providence —
Home at 12.30 to pack valise —
Fanny & Carrie K. there — Packed
dinner at 1 — at 2.30 went over to
Champ's for my umbrellas — not done
— mad about it — then to studio — worked
until 4 — sent a letter to Cotrell with
drawings of some sashes — then to boat
and met Annie there — Started at
5.20 — could not get through tickets to
Boston but preferred this line because
we would not be disturbed so early
in the morning — very few passengers
as the Fall River line has bought the
through to Boston business — the boat
went very slowly owing to effects
of some experiments with the buckets
on the wheels — Strong S.E. wind and
threatening weather — too cold to
be enjoyable outside — Supper
about 7 — got rid of the rest by
wandering about until 10 — then
to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 23, 1882

*at Willie Cook's — Cambridge.*
*25 Appleton St.     mass.*

After a quiet night with a thin veil
of sleep, got up at 6 & found that we
were yet some ways from out Judith
& were laboring in a heavy S.E. swell
with cold driving rain — went back
to bed — there until after 8 then up
& dressed with some difficulty
owing to rolling of boat — Annie did not
get up until an hour later & about
9.30 we had help — then in quiet water
12 o'clk when we got to Providence,
6 hours behind time — pouring rain.
Hack to Prov. & Boston R.R. No train until
1.55 — Engaged a hack & drove to Mrs.
Frederic Condit's — 3 Bell St. Mrs. C. not
at home but directed to a house at High
St. where we found her & had a 15 min.
call — time to depart — Hackman tried
to impose upon me — failed — got to Boston
at 3.30 — a meal at Boston depot — then
horse cars out to Will's — very cold —
where we arrived about 5 o'clk — Will
out hunting for Summer cottage — came
home while we were at dinner, at
7 o'clk — I had had a walk up
to Auburn cemetery gate before
that — weather clearing — Evening in
general chatting until shortly
after 10 — then to bed.
