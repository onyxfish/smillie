## Left Page

### November, Friday 10, 1882

Nasty muggy day, almost wet — got to studio at 9.30 — cleared up, Partly, my etching corner preparatory to work, but was in no condition to do anything, and did not do much except get out a lot of studies & look them over for preparatory to some picture work. Home to dinner at 1 — at 2, went with A. to Phil. Rehearsal — After that went with her to Solomon's to look at curtains — Then she went home & I went to studio. Home at 6.30 — tea, later — Every. sat about until 10 — then to bed —

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 11, 1882

Very mild, cloudy wt. clearing at times during the day — Got to studio at 9.45 & before I left at 12 managed to do very little — Then I came home & found there Mr. & Mrs Neale of Phila. as I had 3 tickets took Mr. with Annie to Union League Club to see the etchings there. — McKim P. A. D. Shepard there — At 1.30 home to dinner which was served at 45 — After that, chatting until past 3 — Then Mr. & Mrs N. left us & A & I went to McGibbons, Arnold's & to Solomon's — looking at curtains — Bought them at S's — Then A. home & to studio — will there work work that he wants me to do — Home about 6 — tea at 6.30 — Then dressed & at 7.30 went to Academy of Music — First Phil. Concert — at 9.30, A. went home with Ed & many & I went to Lotos Club — Reception given to Seymour Haden — found the old gentleman very genial & apparently well pleased — a few gentlemen met at Mr. Howe's early in the everg — I could not go — was put upon a committee to manage Haden's lectures — a remarkable fine collection of Haden etchings shown by Mr. Keppel — Home at midnight — to bed.
