## Left Page

### February, Thursday 9, 1882

Rain commenced about 10 —
Spiteful storm with wind all
day & nearly all night — Got to
studio at 10 & spent the m[orning]
arranging [illegible] for autograph
edition of "Poets & Pitchers" — Home
to dinner at 1.20 — back at
near 3 — the rest of the after-
noon spent looking over my
sketches & planning for new
work. — Home at 6 — Fanny
Emma & Grace expected, but
the storm was too much for
them. — At 8 went with A. to
her singing club — then to studio
& wrote letter to Mr. Condit —
At 10.15 went for A. — home &
to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 10, 1882

Clear m[orning] & mild, followed
by high wind, snow flurries
& colder weather. — After b[reakfast]
went to Tiffany's to pick out
some darker colored dishes for
the new set. Annie met me
there. Then to the Lincrusta
office where we looked at their
wares — & at about 12 got to
studio — wrote to Catrelle — went to
N.A. with pf. of etching & home
to lunch at 1 — at 2, at Phil.
Rehearsals — after that, to Avery's
for a few minutes — then walked
up to Renner's — from there
home at 5.45 — tea at 6.30 —
wrote a letter to Mr. McLeod
Curator Corcoran Art Gallery on
the subject of Ill. Cats. At 9
Annie & I went to Mr. Robt. Gordon's
9 E. 38th St & had a very pleasant
call until after 10 — then home
& to bed about 11.15
