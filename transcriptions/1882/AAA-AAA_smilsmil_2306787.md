## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 27, 1882

Charming day. altho' warm a delightful breeze blew steadily. After bkfst, went to Lewis & Conger. Sloane's & Newman's — then to studio & spent most of the mng. assisted by Wm, the janitor, in packing a large case & two boxes with things for Montrose. Home to dinner at 1.30 — After that a rest — about 3.30 went to 29 St. & ordered Cat irons for front window — then to Tiffanys, Sincrousta, Arnold & Constables & Price's — To studio a little before 5.30 — wrote to Osgood & Co. enclosing orders from Coleman, James Bellows & Mr. R. S. Gifford. — Then to Effs Co. with Coley & P. & E. that I rl'd to D. & Co. Home at 6.30. Tea at 7. Bueff. Connelly came and arranged to take charge of freight sent to Hoboken to-morrow — Fanny & Will K. called. To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 28, 1882

Hot day. Overcast & a few drops of rain about 7 a.m. After bkfst. went to studio — then to Am. Tile Co. Then to Senon Bachis — then to Varick St. to look for an Earth Closet man that I did not find, then to Kimmel & Voigts — then home at 11.30. pretty well used up with a headache — tied & strapped up bundles — Dinner at 1.30 — at 3 Gurney's Effs came & took trunks & bundles — then to studio where he got cases etc. — then to Hoboken getting to freight car about 4.30 — Everything there except Flint's furniture — at 5 went to Flint's — furniture had been sent, only ½ an hour — back to car. furniture then all men packing it into car — some of it properly covered, some not at all — the yard men bothering to get car out of way — done at 6.45 — very tired. Had hurline — home & had tea at 8. Geo came in from Po'k. Stayed a little while — late. Geo & hired cook & Valentine came — to bed at midnight.
