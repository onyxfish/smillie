## Left Page

### April, Saturday 22, 1882

Still very cold & windy — after Bkf.
went to house & gave Dorsey directions
about digging holes for trees, then to
foundry — then to Stage office to
wait for Bills & Halpin — at 10.15
as H. did not come I went with B.
to his lot below Town to agree upon
where some dirt should be hauled
from. Then again on the hill & spent
some time staking out places for
planting trees. Dinner at 12.30 —
To house again before 2 — Punts
& Spike came from N.Y. The p.m.
spent making measurements &
giving directions to Catrell & to
Dorsey. With A. went to P.O. at 6 —
saw Casey & engaged him for Mon-
day. Tea at 6.30 — Felt so tired
that I spent it on the sofa — To
bed at 10.30 — Lovely, quiet
& cold moonlight night.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 23, 1882

Beautiful day, but cold & windy.
Near 9 o'clk before we had breakfast.
After that, went for awhile to house
then Annie & I walked out to Sayre's
& to Mr. Muelford's wood lots — met Halpin
he won't work for me to-morrow — scolded
him on & for an hour scrambled over
the hill looking for young maples to
transplant — back at 12 — then I
went to lower part of town to McWhinny
— then to Clifford's — neither could work
for me — then to Payne's — he promised to
be on hand to-morrow — then around by
old Foundry Road to depot Road to
Pettus' & engaged him to have dirt
& trees for me to-morrow — Back to
Courtright's at 1.30 — wrote letter to
Rumsey & Co. & to father. Dinner
at 3 — at 4 went to house with our
family — back at 5.30 — at 6 went to
Mr. de Schweinitz's (old H. Drinker
place) & took tea — Spent the eveg.
by pleasant wood fire until 9 —
then to C's & to bed very soon
after 10.
