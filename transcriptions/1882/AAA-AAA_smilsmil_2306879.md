## Left Page

### December, Saturday 30. 1882.

Clear, pleasant winter day — got to studio about 10 o'clk. After having written to Kimmel & Voigt that as I had given up all hopes of finishing my large "Rough Sport" etching in time for the [illegible] it would not do to go on with printing small plate for catalogue — I commenced work on large drawing 12×25. Causeway from Marblehead neck to Mainland. Tracing, re-tracing & outlining. Home to dinner & was Annie at studio about 4 p.m. Archibald McLeess surprised me by a call in the mf. At 5 went to Kleppel's then to "Century" where Gilder had a long talk with me concerning Academy & the Met. Museum scandal — To tea at 7 — wrote notes to Hoz, Betts & [illegible] & at 8 went to Grady & McK [illegible] there until after 9 — then home to bed at 10.30.

In the mf. got Thompson's & [illegible] signatures to Haden letter inviting him to lecture.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 31. 1882.

Bright, beautiful day — [illegible] at about 9 — after 10 Ed. Parker arr'd from P'k. I set to work uninterrupted — wrote a long letter to Koehler. After 1 walked down to studio back to dinner at 2. Afternoon, again writing — also had a consultation with Walter Cook about some Montrose plans — at 6 out, & walked up to Central Park & back. — Tea at 7 — after that wrote a letter [illegible] — took it to G.C. Depot. To bed before 11 — awakened at 12 by a tremendous racket of steam whistles & bells ushering in the new year — Vale.
