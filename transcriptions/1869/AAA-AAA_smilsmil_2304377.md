## Left Page

### Sept. Saturday, 25 1869

Muf sky very threatening and so too my head — but at about 8.30 things more promising, so strapping my pack upon my back I started for the Rim Ridge going. Riding up with C. who goes again away in to the upper gorge, where I would be glad to go with him, if I had no so many studies under way & the season so far advanced. Suffered a good deal with my head that, but am indifferent light, but stuck to it until now 2, when I made a pot of tea. Then worked until 5. but did not get my drawing done, as I had hoped to — on my way out, met C & the backboard. He carried my traps down — but I walked — Spatting rain on the way. Well Rec'd letters from Charl. Matt & father, also one to the party from the Tweedys & C. Dunham — Arl is still complaining & threatening going home with the C's next week — very childish, I think. Got to bed about 10 o'clk —

## Right Page

### Sept. Sunday, 26 1869

Weather threats at last made good — warm, steady Rain — going as the day advanced into a mild, driving, wind and Rain storm. Spent the morning writing to Charl. Matt and the Tweedys gives — Spent the afternoon fretting and rocking in-doors — dinner at 4 — Every growing rapidly colder — got to bed at 9 o'clk
