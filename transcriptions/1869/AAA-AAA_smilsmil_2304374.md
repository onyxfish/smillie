## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 19 1869.

Very warm, beautiful day. Up late + breakfasted late, as usual Sunday morning. Spent the m'f'g mting to Kate + killing time with the girls until about noon when I went out with Colman — we walked out beyond Smith Bedee's + climbed a clif at the foot of Camel's Hump where we enjoyed a beautif view + the delicious air. Got back to dinner at 4.30. The Rest of p.m. all Dayputting + sitting about the foot bridge. Charming moonlight night — all out on porch. Singing until aftng — Saying last words + finishing Kate's letter until near midnight, when I got to bed —

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 20 1869.

Up before daylight + 5 o'clk. all had Steps + at about 6 — Belle, Mary + Florence I ready with Clara Dunham started for New York. Colman + I were to go in to the Lower Ausable for the day — but the wind was so high, the sky so threatening + both of us feeling so unlike it, that we gave it up — I went to a group of Rocks just other side of the river + spent the m'f'g "cutting them out" i.e. chopping away some growths, + outlining my study — lunch at 12, about 1.30 Ret. to work + at about 3 was driven off by a heavy thunder storm, a succession which followed during the p.m. + eve'g — Read until near 9 o'clk — washed brushes Cleared up + got to bed ab 10 —
