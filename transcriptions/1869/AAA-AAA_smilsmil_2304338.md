## Left Page

### July, Friday 9, 1869

Wakened by flies about 5 o'cl — could'nt stand it — got up \+ went down to the back parlor — my big Chair \+ slept so soundly that I did not hear the bl[illegible] bell after 6 o'clk. Wrote to Matt at San Francisco \+ to Jennie. I [illegible] Did not get to studio until 10.45. Set to work biting Ruling of blue on Gorge's plate (14th day). All the Remainder of the m'f at it. About noon very heavy Rain with thunder — could not get home. Saml. Colman came in about 2.30 — I got home to lunch about 3 \+ back shortly after 4. The biting is not first rate tried over it, but suffering from nervous headache — I am beginning to feel very much driven. Home at 7 — at 9 again at studio \+ brought up traps to begin work at home. To bed ab[illegible] 10 —

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 10, 1869

A very warm day — my first to work at home. Started about 9 o'clk, by laying a Rebiting ground (1st day) on Ausable Lake, and the Rest of the day, Rebiting, which I accomplished with Reasonable success. Father gave Auger his [illegible] \+ last sitting — Eve'g brought home to me letters from the studio — one from Hegeman, one from [illegible] Henry T. Smith, with note of [illegible] from D. Huntington, offering to make arrangements for an endowment policy in Continental Ins. Co. for $10,000 — 10 yrs. half premium to be p[illegible] — saw pictures — will see about it — and one letter from Emma delayed at North Salem. Eve'g about 8.30 Gp. \+ I went down to see Mary [illegible] — were so harrassed by two old beaux that we did not get away until near 11 — on way home stopped at Jauch's \+ had Cream — then at studio \+ I brought home my big Blind To bed ab[illegible] 2 —
