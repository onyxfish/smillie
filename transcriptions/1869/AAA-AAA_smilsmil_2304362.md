## Left Page

### August, Thursday 26, 1869

Up at sunrise and out on the lake in the dug out, giving myself a lesson in paddling — The effect superb — Louis Tiffany suddenly made up his mind that he must return home, as he packed & at 8.15 AM. we left camps — Na for Patters — Geo. for the lower lake & Coleman & I for the "slide" again — I got to work about 10 o'clk — The sky cloudless, but wind in gusts very troublesome — worked on my study until about 3 o'clk — had lunch at 1.30 — from 3.30 until 6 worked as tight as I could on a sketch of the mts. to the west — Both studies in oil — Riled camps about 7 & had a late dinner — Found that Dr. Seur's & a party had been here. Welly, high wind, cold and a magnificent display of stars — lay about until at about 10, then turned in — Slept soundly until about 1 o'clk. then up to tend fire — again at 4 —

## Right Page

### August, Friday 27, 1869

*at Shurtliffe's camps — Sower ausable Saranac —*

Up as the sun was touching the opposite mts — went out on the lake & warmed up with a good row. Returning to camps breakfasted, packed traps & bid good bye to Bedee Camps at 9 o'clk. The weather glorious and the walk over the "carry" delightful — got to the lower lake, selected my subject at the upper end & got to work about 1 o'clk. on sketch looking to Indian Head with fine foreground. Coleman [illegible] above me, Geo. went on to the lower end of the lake — I worked my study until after 1, then lunched & commenced a study looking across the lake to a wall rock on Gothic — worked until after 4 — about 5 o'clk. Melville came up & Colman & me when we went down to the Shurtliffe shanty — dinner at dusk & an evening before the camps — "turning in" about 9.30 — Up at [illegible] o'clk to attend fire and again at [illegible] — right dark, windy & threatening
