## Left Page

### October, Sunday, 31, 1869

Up late — mu[illegible] went to Dr. Hastings' theard Rev. Henry Martin Scudder of San Fran[cisco]. His text was from Mark 5[th] the story of the Raising of Jairus' daughter — His address was peculiar \+ characteristic of the far west but interesting. After service went up to Uncle A's. getting there just after soup at dinner — spent the P.M. with Tyner, \+ a young cousin of Miss Porter — Wilcox — out of doors, going but windy \+ cold. Clear \+ with beautiful color — Teas at 6.15 — at 7.45 started for home, which I reached in an hour — got to bed about 11 —

## Right Page

### November, Monday, 1, 1869

Cloudless m[or]n[in]g, clouded over before 10 o'clk \+ for the rest of the day, dark \+ disagreeable with an attempt at sun about 3 o'clk — Got to studio at 9 o'clk \+ set to work on 5×14 "Edmund's Pond" — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.15 \+ painted again, finishing about 4.30, as I could see no longer — Result, a pale greenish bit of art, suggestive of biliany derangements — Home to tea at 6 o'clk — very stretched paper for a drawing and mounted large pencil study made last summer — Tom Snear[illegible] man called for a few minutes to bed about 10.30
