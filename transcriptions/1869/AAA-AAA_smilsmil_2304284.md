## Left Page

### March Tuesday, 23 1869.

Very Rainy stormy morning. In bed all day with a first-class, unmistakable sick headache — when not awake, in agony of pain \+ sickness, in a semi-comatose condition, dreaming barbarous things concerning my paints \+ painting — Father at home too, used up with a cold that seems to have settled in his head — Got up \+ washed, dressed \+ took the [illegible] about 8 o'clk P.m. — Spent the rest of the night until about 1 o'clk A.m., in my big chair — then off to bed. Lay there until after 4 o'clk when I got to sleep.

## Right Page

### March Wednesday, 24 1869.

Up shortly after 6 o'clk. \+ got to studio at 8 o'clk, feeling very ragged \+ demoralized — Bolstered myself during the day with coffee \+ spirits, so that I got my allotted work done — worked over middle distant foliage \+ l.h. side of picture (2nd day) Dr. F.W. Lewis \+ Mr. Jo. Boswell called in the m'f — I went home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — Afternoon Dr. Lewis again called with Mr. Ge. Whitney \+ Mr. Enoch Lewis of Phila. Messrs. W.\+L. Stayed until about 5.30. Mr. Whitney gave me a commission for a painting 20×24 — Mr. Enoch Lewis gave to get me — each one for 12×22. Mr. Whitney insisted that he should dine with them at the Albemarle at 6.30. He went over — \+ met in addition. Mr. S. P. Avery — we had a pleasant dinner \+ chat — leaving at 10 o'clk — Stopped at the studio for a few minutes — then right home \+ to bed at 10.30. Splendid day.
