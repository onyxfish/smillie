## Left Page

### May, Thursday, 6, 1869.

Pleasant my alths' last nighthreatums —
Breakfast at 7 — About 8 Mr. M. Gel & I
walked out to see the iron bridge over
the Schuylkill at Chestnut S — then to
the mount where we had ahumied
view — then over to the Fund Rooms, but
to early — then to Haseltine's Gallery —
there Mr. M left us & we Rel? to the [illegible]
building, calling on Kate Drinkin and
Van der & me boys' school — Miley and
Williams — Saw Dr. Lewis & bid him good
bye — at 11.45 got to Phila Academy and
met Mr. Whitney — with him went to
Earles Gallery — there met Mr. Farjes —
pres. of the Union League Club of Phil
and a great collector of pictures. m[illegible]
him & Mr. W — went to his house C[illegible]
of 7 & Vine Street — there very much
interested in the house — The pictures
and the man — It was nearly 3
when we took cars for Mr. Whitney's
house — there we had a pleasant
visit with Mrs. W — Miss Mary —
were taken to the dining Room and
library & saw many pleasant things —
a little before 3 bid the W's good bye —
Mr. W. has taken a strong hold of on my
affections — Got to Mr. Mairs just in time
for dinner — visited with them until 5 &
then Gis. went to call on the Judsons and
Williams's while I rode out to Green St. to
see May Lewis & bid them all good bye —
We got back to Mr. M's about 6 — took a hasty
cup of tea & then off to the West Phila Station
& took the 6½ 0 train for home — NY at 10.S
41 45. at 11 oclk — to bed just at midnight

## Right Page

### May, Friday, 7, 1869.

In bed all day with a dull,
sluggish bilious headache —
a fit ending to my Philadelphia
campaign — That trip I must
have enjoyed immensely but
for the miserable physical con-
dition that I was in — It makes
me altogether disheartened and
very much ashamed of my-
self — Did not get up until
9 oclk. Had still aching —
went to parlor — took a cup of
tea — Rolled in my blanket
until midnight — Then again
