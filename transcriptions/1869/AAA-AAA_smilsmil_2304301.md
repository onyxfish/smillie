## Left Page

### April, Saturday 24, 1869

Pleasant day. Feeling in-disposed to work. I went to Kehm's had my hair cut and enjoyed having my head shampooed. Getting to the studio at 9.30. Tommy Bell most of the mrf trying his hand at water colors. I painted a little sketch on a bit of French canvas that Louis Tiffany gave me. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — P.M. mounting paper for Meujer's drawing \+ working over sky on "Summer time" 12×18, in. — George's pupils going. Bruce, took his first lesson. Mr. \+ Mrs. Jas. Drake. Hegemau \+ D[illegible] Kinsply called. Home to tea at 5.30. At 8.30. Called on Miss Dewy at the house of her Sister, Mrs. Cobb. No. 57 London Terrace. She Read some of Robert Browning to me. Left at 11o'clk. Home \+ to bed at 12. P.S. Note \+ Cards for Founder's day from [illegible] Fessenden, at Vassar College.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 25, 1869

Very windy, dusty \+ disagreeable day. Mrs went to Dr. Hastings' \+ heard him preach from the words "Lord, what wilt thou have me to do?" — He does not think that Paul was a dyspeptic christian. Home to dinner — the wind \+ dust causing me to give up idea of going to Uncle d.o. — About 4 o'clk lay down on the bed \+ had a two hours nap. Eve'g went to Dr. J. P. Thompson's \+ heard a sermon on Adam's sin in the Garden of Eden. He is giving a sort genesis series of lectures — Glorious, cloudless, full moon night. Did not get to bed until about midnight.
