## Left Page

### February Tuesday, 9 1869.

a miserable day \- beginning to Rain towards noon \+ keeping up a sleety, mizzle until night. Got to Studio at 9 o'clk \+ set to work on a charcoal sketch 'ill\- the Tornado passage in Bonyauto among the Trees \- Home to lunch at 2 o'clk. having been kept by a call from Mr. Mrs Raynor \- Back studio at 3 \+ kept at my drawing as long as I could see \- Dr [illegible] \+ a [illegible] lady came in just at dusk \- Every at home \- Had settled myself for a good study but disturbed \- about 9.30 Tom Shearman came in \- the first time I have seen him in a long, long time \- He is the same old piece of goods \- He left at 11.15 and I got to bed at 12.05

## Right Page

### February Wednesday, 10 1869.

Rain all day \- at Studio at 9 o'clk. Miss Dewy came about 10 \+ stayed for an hour \- I finished \+ set drawing of Tornado that I drew yesterday \- I am rather pleased with it, as showing vigor \- Then set to work on illustration for Opening Lines among the Trees \- Home to lunch at 1 \- back at 2 \- worked closely all the p.m. \+ got it done \+ set \- this rather commonplace, I think, but likely to please more generally than the other one. Home at 6.30. Every did not feel well, but at work planning the illustrations until near 10. Mr. Parker of [illegible] calling on Miss Martin \- got to bed about 10.
