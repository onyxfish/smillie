## Left Page

### November Tuesday, 16 1869

Still so much under the weather that I did not get to studio until near 10 o'clk. Spent the mf Rubbing color over & otherwise preparing canvases to paint up in — A son of Carroll Dunham brought me a note from Mr. Coleman — C. is again very sick. The W.C. meeting to be held in his room to night, will have to be in mine — Home to lunch about 1.30 back at 2.20. Most of P.M. grinding a tube of Zinc yellow — Home at 6 — back at 7.30 — Were miserable with just enough soft snow to wet everything — 19 or 20 of the fellows turned out & we had quite a lively meeting the main business being an agreement to have our [illegible] election with the N.A. of D. — & the Reduction of dues for the year to $4.00 — Home at 11 — took a warm sitz bath and rolled into a bedful of blankets & comforters — Falling admitted bronchitis spent a sufficiently uncomfortable night.

## Right Page

### November Wednesday, 17 1869

Up at about 9 o'clk — Mild driving wind & rain — Felt too much used up to go out, so kept indoors until after lunch — At 2 o'clk went down to studio — by this time it had cleared up quite mild and spring. Spent the afternoon sweeping and fixing up studio — Home at 6 o'clk — Evett did a message or two, nothing more & got to bed about 10 o'clk.
