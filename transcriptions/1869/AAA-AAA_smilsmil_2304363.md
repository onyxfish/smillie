## Left Page

### August Saturday, 28 1869.

covered with ashes from mr camp [illegible]
Up for all day at 6 \- Threats of immed[iate]
Rain \- Breakfasted, agreed that if Rain
did set in before noon, we would mu[st]
\+ go down to Potter's. So Coleman mu[st]
up the Lake \+ Gil \+ I climbed to
the top of Rainbow fall, from its fo[ot]
\- a very hard climb but the view spl[endid]
grant paid \- we went up the strea[m]
about ½ a mile further then Return
\+ set to work sketching the view from
the top of the fall \- About 10.30 the Rai[n]
began to come down \+ soon after me[n]
Retreated \- got down to Camp very
met \+ there found Mr \+ Mrs Bak[er]
Miss Elliott \+ Orlando Ridee \+ ano[ther]
guide \- on their way to the upper lak[e]
\+ Mt. Marcy, stopping for shelter \-
Coleman \+ Melville soon after join[ed]
us \- As there seemed no hope for [fair]
work at 11.45 we set out for Potters \- M[y]
walk through the wet woods was ver[y]
beautiful \- At 1.40 we got to Potters \-
I had a good wash, a change an[d]
lunch, feeling first Rate \- At 3 I
lay down \+ slept for over 2 hours \-
Evening spent in general cha[t]
and council, getting off to bed a[t]
10.30 o'clk \-

## Right Page

### August Sunday, 29 1869.

Beautiful cloudy day, the
delicious and the atmosp[here]
effects most charming \- Every
body seems lazy and little
else is done but listless lying
about. I am taking a Regul[ar]
\- rest hoping to begin again
\- afternoon before
4 o'clk, wrote letters to Farber
and Edgar Sutphen \- Dinner
at 4, after dinner Sauntering
about until dusk \- Evening
all together singing until
about 9 o'clk \- got to bed
about 10.30 \- not until after
some of the girls had enjoyed
a bit of a Romp \-
