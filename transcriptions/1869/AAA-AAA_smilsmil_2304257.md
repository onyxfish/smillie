## Left Page

### January, Thursday 28, 1869

mild day — got to Studio at 8.45 — glazed over all the foreground \+ R.h. Cor of "Saco" 20×36 and painted solid over the beach \+ rushes — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 \+ painted as long as I could see — but could not get my work done — at 5.30 down to Snedicors — there until 7.30 writing out list of addresses — Home to tea about 8. Every writing addresses on envelopes — until 10 — Father \+ Mother at a Church Sociable at Mumps — Fanny Kimball \+ her two Woods called at the Studio this p.m. got to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### January, Friday 29, 1869

Very pleasant day — got to studio at 9 o'clk and by working hard, finished Ineed's painting by 12 o'clk — Rode home to lunch at 1.30 \+ at 2.15 Rode down to Phil. Rehearsal — Met there Miss Jennie Treedy with the other Treedy's — Back to Studio at 4.30. Found there the Card of Dr. F.W. Lewis — went over to the St. James to see him — Had a pleasant chat for about 20 m. then home \+ after a hurried dressing — Geo \+ I got to Mr. Thel. Roosevelt's at No. 28 E. 20th St. at 6.15 P.M. — at about 6.30 we sat down to dinner — Mr. R. at the head of table on his Right Dr. F.W. Lewis — Phil. F. Kensett, W.T. Blodgett, J.N. Smillie — Mr. Clark of Phil. N. P. Gray — Mr. Whitridge — Mrs. Hicks \+ at the foot of the table Dr. Ward — his in-law of Mr. R. — on Mr. R's left, Danl. Huntington — Howard Potter, Eastman Johnston — Jos. Clay, J.D.S. — J.D.A. Ward — W.E. Dodge, Jun. — Sam Hays — The dinner was sumptuous — the wines beyond my understanding. I felt complimented \+ embarrassed in such company — rose from the table about 12 — at 12.30 home with Mr. Dodge — got to bed about 1 —
