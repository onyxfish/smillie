## Left Page

### July, Sunday, 25, 1869

Intensely hot day — and I, very much used up — my stomach misbehaving shamefully. Most of the young people went to church — but I spent the most very quietly on the piazza, not counting a little nap that I took in my own room. After dinner, chatting until near 4 — when the ladies went to their siesta — near 5 — Emma — Fanny, Geo. & I went to the tops of a Rock about [illegible] m. walk from the house — but for the haze — the view would have been extended & pleasant — Pitt in time for tea at 6:30 —
Eve'g, walking the piazza and sacred music — but I was so much out of sorts that I kept very quiet, getting off to bed a few minutes after 11 —

## Right Page

### July, Monday, 26, 1869

After a very uncomfortable night up before 6 — urged by necessity — [illegible] at 6.30 — at 7 a carriage came to take those for 7.30 train. Gel & I made arrangements to go by 8.30 train — very gentle quiet rain — Mr. Hays & child with us — got to A.B. M. [illegible] at 10 — very soon got to work on the sky cross Ruling in George's plate (19th day) — Did not get finished until near 3 — then a heavy rain, went home — clear before I got there — stopped [illegible] Ruling all ready for biting — then gave up to my stomach — stretched myself on the bed. Eve'g sleeping in my big chair — [illegible] came in about 10 [illegible] and prescribed for me — got to bed at about midnight —
