## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 15, 1869.

Warm morning + cloudy, but after a time it cleared away + the p.m and night were delicious — Morning I laid a strong Rebiting ground over the whole of my plate (10 [illegible] days) + spent the day Rebiting, which I did vigorously + successfully. I got through about 4.30 and started for Mr. Beach's, but at our gate met Emma B. in her funny little pony cart. She wanted my strongest magnifying glass to aid her in botanizing — gave it to her + rode with her home, she ordered up a [illegible] + insisted that I should go [illegible] horseback alone — For 3/4 of an hour I Rode over Mr. B's place — among the woods enjoying it hugely — back to tea at 6.30 — about 8, Father + Kate drove into town — I spent the [illegible] writing to Kate + [illegible] from whom I rec'd this m'g a pleasant note + cards for Class Day at Vassar next Tuesday — Father + Kate got in about 10 o'clk — I put up horse \+ got to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 16, 1869.

glorious m'g clouding up and showering bet. 12 + 1 — + clearing off to a beautiful breezy p.m. + a cloudless moonlight night — Worked (11/2 days) with graver crossing + hatching generally — at 4 o'clk laid a Rebiting ground, varnished + at 4.30 dressed + went to Jn'l. Roosevelt went about with Mr. R. examined a new Reducer + then with Mr. [illegible] his nephew R. R. played Croquet until 6.15 — Then home + got tea Kate having gone in town to meet Matt who is expected up on the [illegible] — Uncle Willie arr'd bet'n 2 + 3 o'clk — After tea we sauntered over the grounds — Uncle W. and I using up a good deal of energy chopping down a young oak — at 8 o'clk Father drove in town for the girls — They got out here about 10 — Put up the horse \+ got to bed at 10.45.
