## Left Page

### July, Saturday 17, 1869

a most delightful day — I feel very well but indisposed to the slightest exertion — so I sat all the time on piazza, enjoying the cool breeze, such a delicious change from what I had suffered in the city — Mr. & Mrs. Calman most of the time with us — lunch at h— part of the time with C. in studio \+ part of p.m. very pleasantly spent talking Adirondacks and looking over a couple of part folio of Water Color sketches — from 2 to [illegible] [illegible] \+ C. of walking — at 4.30. bid Mr. C. good bye — walked down to shore \+ loitered for a while — at 5.3 took train for Yonkers — at [illegible] — at 5.40 took train to 152nd St. to also \+ see Mr. Torb[illegible] got there at 6 11 — Mrs. T. has gone on to Lake George, altho' she had made arrangements to accept our escort — at 7. 5 took train to NY, getting home about 7.45 — Tired \+ hot — did nothing during eveg. \+ got to bed at 10 oclt —
