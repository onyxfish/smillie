## Left Page

### April, Monday, 26, 1869.

Excepting wind \+ dust, a dilightful day — After 6k fo[r] — m[a]de a note to Kate Tessenden — Did not get to Studios until 9.30 — Set to work again on 14×18" Summer Time", but my m[i]f interfered with in various ways — Miss Devry at Studio — Home to lunch at 1.15 back to Studio at 2 — Finished Repainting my picture getting it to look pretty well for one — I have thought of giving it to Mary for her wedding present but I can hardly afford it — At 4 o'clk went with Son — Tiffany \+ at 4.20 we started for the Park on horse-back — Rid at 6 — had a splendid Ride — at 9, stopped to see Mary — \$4 [illegible] — Carried a little picture to her — Took Fea \+ spent the eve'g until 9 o'clk, Miss Carson \+ Miss Hewi[t] very pleasant — So bed at 10.[30]

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday, 27, 1869.

Delightful, but warm day until late P.M. — when it clouded over, giving us a dash of Rain between 10\+11 o'clk — On my way to Studio met D. Mulford \+ went with him \+ saw one of the "Spirit photos" — a m[r] Livermore \+ his Spirit wife — a transparent humbug — The light falling on the two faces from different sides — Got to Studios at 8.45 — Tried to commence Meuger's drawing of "West Point \+ the Hudson" — could not straighten out the composition to suit me — so set to work on Pats subject — Charcoal drawing and after fighting away again at the foreground at last got it somewhat to suit me — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — Charcoal drawing until near Rec[t] — pleasant letter from Mr. Mathis enclosing a very flattering notice (newspaper) of "In the Track of the Torrent" — Eve'g — 8.30 Called on Emma Ireland — \+ a pleasant call, as she kindly allowed me to talk about myself until 10.30 — Right home \+ to bed at 11.30
