## Left Page

### November Monday, 22 1869

Beautiful day — got to Stu[dio]
at 9.20. Kindled fire & got
to work after time lost through
visit of Burling & Snedicor on
"Indian Pass from Scotts" (2d day)
worked until 12. Then went
to 25 St. Bld[g] to see La Farge. He
was not in — saw Robbins, Gifford
& Tiffany — Home to lunch at 1.30 —
back at 2.20 & worked as late
as I could. des. & got laid [in] on
my painting — This generally
muddy & yellow — Mr. A. B.
Marks & their niece Miss Tayl[or]
called — On my way home
called on Mr. C. P. Cranch at his
house about Water color business.
Home to tea at 5.30. Ev[en]g worked
on Locomotive on Pacific R.R.
Bond until 10.30 — to bed some
after 11.

## Right Page

### November Tuesday, 23 1869

Disagreeable day, dark and
raining. Got to Studio at 9 o'cl[ock]
to work on 9×14 "Whiteface from
Placid" (3d day). Spent the [day]
chiefly getting it more harmonious
i.e. all of a color, a green — sky &
everything else. Home to lunch —
back at 2. About 3 o'clk.
having got over the painting commenced
charcoaling on 33×44". Dark
against day's "golden death" —
Home to tea at 6 — at 8, went to [the]
League Club, Preliminary meeting
to establish Metropolitan Art Mus[eum].
Mr. C. Bryant made a very interesting
opening address. Met there Gl. Whitne[y]
Mr. T. Richards — Had to leave at 8.45
to get home where we have a sort
of family pow-wow — as it is Father's &
Will's Birth day & the 1st anniver[sary]
of Mary's wedding — about 30 present —
Supper between 10 & 11 — got to
bed shortly after midnight.
