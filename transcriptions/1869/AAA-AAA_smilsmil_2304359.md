## Left Page

### August Friday, 20 1869.

Exceedingly close, sultry day. During the morning very heavy thunder storms which gave us fine exhibitions of both thunder & lightning with very much Rain, so that by noon the River had risen two feet. About noon a lull, but later more thunder storms & Rain. During the m'f I fixed up a sketch & wrote a letter to Van Zandt — also wrote to Father. Afternoon felt much under the influence of the weather and did little. Ev'g, weather cooler but giving little hopes — Mr. Colman read again until 9.20 — me got to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### August Saturday, 21 1869.

Dull overcast m'f — Immediately after b'kf't got off with Colman & [illegible] for the top of Giant's Leap which we reached about 10 o'clk. What with building a smudge & selecting my point to sketch from it was 11 before I got to work at outlines for water color on my 15×22 board, of the trees & rocks at the immediate top of the fall looking on the north side to the south — I worked until 1 when I went up the stream & joined C & I & had lunch when it began raining quite hard — hurried back, finished my outlines & all started for home. Out by 2 o'clk it lifted — I sauntered got to the house at 4 — touched up my outline & gave it a wash of orange — Ev'g Mr. C. Read [illegible] & I got to bed about 10.30. Ev'g cool — and after 9 o'clk glorious moonlight —
