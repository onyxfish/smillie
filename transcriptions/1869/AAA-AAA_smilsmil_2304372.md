## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 15 1869.

Up early and out on the pond in the fog —
Saw a fog bow + its Reflection in the water
forming a perfect circle — Bkft. about
7.30 — more venison — at 8. got off with C.
Placid, bound for the upper end — saw peculiar
prismatic effects on the water surface — Got
to upper end, 6 miles, at 9.30, lauded our
boat + striking through the woods, we
climbed to a Rock at the top of a hill
which took us an hour, so that it was
near 11 before we got to work — The view
all around from S.W. to S.E. with Lake
islands in foreground, splendid — I
worked until lunch (1.30) venison, on
sky — then until 4.30, in oils, on Rich —
a sketch — I made large outline with
pencil + at 5.05 started down, getting
to our boat in ½ an hour — Two other
boats with our party + guides, in waiting,
pulled down the West lake, stopping at
the Durand Camp. Got in, after a
glorious twilight + moonlight at 7.15 —
Home + dinner, tired + hungry at
7.45 — Splendid night, all out
on piazza — went to bed at
11.30 .

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 16 1869.

warmer yet, but glorious — After bkft.
trout + venison, off with C. at 8 o'clk
on a cobble mt. about [illegible] a half miles
east of the pond — Reached the top
an hour + a half + found a mag-
nificent view of [illegible] to S. + S.W. We sat
down to our work + stuck to it without
stop until 4.30 except to lunch
at 1.30 — I was more than usually
unsuccessful, getting no color at
all worth having — I got, however
a large + careful pencil out-
line — Started for home at 5.
getting in at 6 — Saw peculiar
effect of heavy clouds — sun
behind them — the under side
lighted up with a warm light
from beneath, probably a Re-
flection from Lake Saranac —
another heavy dinner of venison
and the most glorious night
that I Remember — Effects on Lake
of moonlight + cloud Reflections
wonderful — Rowing with the girls
until 9.30 — To bed at 10 —
