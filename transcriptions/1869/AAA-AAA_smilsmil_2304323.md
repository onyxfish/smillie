## Left Page

### June, Wednesday, 9, 1869.

Up shortly after 6 o'clk — M'lf clear
& cold — Bkft before 8 — & som after
Mr. Walker arr'd with his photo [illegible]
apparatus, — He worked until
10.30, getting what appeared to be,
3 very good negatives. Father & I
spent the Rest of the m'fg getting
tables & things Ready to go to work —
Dinner at 1 — more fresh herring [illegible]
contribution of Dorge, with the addition
to our dining table of quantities of [illegible]
very sweet, & serving almost as another
item on our limited bill of fare. — a
nap after dinner — then much time
spent, Rubbing down with sand,
the face of my big oil stone — a sketch
of some purple Iris — Tea ab't 6 — ab't
7, a walk along the Road to Beach
— then across to the River & down the
Track home, Father, Kate & I — waited
until R.R. Train & Mary Powell went
up, then back to the house — Sat
about until ten, then to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday, 10, 1869.

Cold & overcast, Rain beginning to
fall about 10. a.m. — & keeping but
quietly & steadily the Rest of the day.
Got to my etching about 9 o'clk (6[illegible])
working over the mountains and
distant trees — got along very com-
fortably except the want of a little
more light — Between 10 & 11 — as
it was quite chilly, I kindled
a small fire in the grate, for
the Rest of the day it was a great
comfort. Our bill of fare for to-
day was improved by the addition
of a Rice Pudding, but we missed
the butcher — Strawberries & herring
in abundance — Kate managed
to feed us very well, barring the
funny incongruities, not her fault.
Worked until 6 — then Tea &
pacing piazza until 8 o'clk —
Read for an hour or more Luebke
"History of Art") and got to bed
at 10 o'clk.
