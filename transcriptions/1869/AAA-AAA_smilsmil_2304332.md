## Left Page

### June Sunday, 27 1869.

Very sultry that but heard a good strong sermon from Dr. Hastings - 1st Corinthians 16th Chap. 13th V- "Watch ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men, be strong" - as this is his last sermon before his vacation of two months - it is a sort of parting charge - After service, went to Uncle a's via 8th ave R.R. - Suffered from the heat while walking across lots - Ate of Cherries before \+ after dinner. Chant \+ a. Mr. H. Scott there also. During the P.M. a splendid thunder storm - Tea at 6 - after tea, with Uncle a \+ Juney drove to the River House - 152d St. \+ saw Mr. \+ Mrs. Forh[illegible] at 8. Uncle a. left me at the Car[illegible]. During the Ride down, another shower - not quite over before got home ab 9.15 - To bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### June Monday, 28 1869.

Intensely hot day - got to studio ab 9.15 - Ther 90°. Commenced etching foreground trees on my [illegible] plate (7th day) Think that I have got into the spirit again, altho' I do not etch with the dash that I did on George's plate - a short \+ sudden shower at 1 o clk. when I went home to lunch - It steamed right away leaving it more oppressively hot than before. Back at 2.45 - Between 4 \+ 5 a very heavy thunder shower came up - It rained until about 6 - Shortly after, home to tea. Even\[illegible\] spent grinding up Strontian Yellow \+ Raw Sienna in oil \+ putting it into tubes - Found so much trouble with the Sienna that I did not get to bed until 12.30 — [illegible] comparatively cool.
