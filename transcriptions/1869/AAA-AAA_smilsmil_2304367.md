## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 5 1869.

Beautiful, cloudless day, but
not tempered by a pleasant
South wind — All lazy or
tired so that we did not have
breakfast until after 9 o'clk.
Spent the day until dinner
time at 4 o'clk lying about
on the shady side of the
house — After dinner
more lying about and at
sunset all went down to
the River — Eveg until
9 o'clk, singing — Jo[hn]
off to bed at 8.30 —

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 6 1869.

Threatening Rain, altho the
mist was very charming — Up
shortly after 5 o'clk. Charge
got off with D. P— for West Port
— home at 6.30. It was 8.20
before C. + I got off for the
to A "giants leap". which we
reached about 9.30 having
stopped to sketch by the way.
C. went on up the stream + I
did not see him again until
near 5 P.M. — As it was cloudy
I worked all day on my W.C.
study at the top of the fall —
about 5 — Coleman came along
at 5.30 after having fixed my
[illegible] in Rubber blanket to keep
me started for home — Eveg
some whist again — chatting
etc + to bed at 10.30. Night
very dark + high wind from
the South —
