## Right Page

### October Monday, 4 1869.

Vaughan House. Room 42.
Whitehall —

Stuck by Lingo! Up about 5.30, up
and shortly after 6 got started with
old Pat & his buckboard for West Port. It
has Rained heavily nearly all night and
rained without intermission all the way from [West]
P[ort]. I did it want to start, but I knew that
in a few hours the River would be up so
that I could not cross it & so, persisted
in getting off at once — old P. certainly drove
like all possessed & I grieved for Fanny
the mare — I was so Rolled up in Rubber
blankets etc. that I got but little wet but
the mud & water flew was a caution.
had a Rag carpet over my trunk & on that
almost mud enough for a potato bed — we
got to West Port shortly before 11 & the boat did
not come along until near 12. In the mean time
got a pail of water & a broom & washed my trunk
and Rubber blanket — It ceased Raining
shortly after leaving West Port. Commencing
again at Benson's — We got to Whitehall
about 4 — all the water very high & every
evidence of a great freshet — I was told at
once that no were stuck indefinitely. Communications cut off in every direction —
with the most of the passengers I went
to the Vaughan House — with a Mr. Vegder of
Pittsburgh & a young Dr. Noell of Phila —
went about to see the sights — every thing in
commotion — Canal boats in confusion
tugs & barges breaking their fastenings — the boat
stuck here, per force, making
various enquiries concerning any possible chance of getting away — it was exciting
the rush of water under the of the bridges
a very fine sight — Tea at 6 — poor enough
& intolerable — the canal element —
boatmen, drivers, etc. mostly French Canadian
in quite great numbers — Part of evening in
parlor with Mr. D. N. & Miss Stone of P[ittsbur]gh —
Mr. Veeder proposed that we should take
double Room together — I agreed — went
to bed at 10 o'clk & Upper part of house
quite pleasant and slept good —
