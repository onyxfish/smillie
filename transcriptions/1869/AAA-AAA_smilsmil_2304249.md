## Left Page

### January Tuesday, 12 1869.

overcast, windy and cold —
Did not get to studio until 9.30
Fire out — kindled it \+ then to
work on drawing. Spent the
day over R. h. Trees \+ the Shadow
below them — Find it difficult
to work the shadow down low
enough, \+ yet retain color
\+ Transparency — Home to lunch
at 1 — back at 2.15 \+ worked until
near 4.30 — At 5 went down
to Avery's \+ saw him about
selling my White Mt. drawing.
Then to Knoedler's \+ Snedicor's.
Home to tea at 6.30 — Well
spent, Lazily as I feel tired.
Mary is now called well
by the Dr. To-day he says that
he will not dress her neck any
more — It is about 13 weeks since
the beginning of the trouble and
since it was lanced —
got to bed about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### January Wednesday, 13 1869.

Charming day once more, got
to studio at 8.30 \+ at once to work
on drawing. Rec'd. letters from
Dr. J. W. Lewis \+ Mr. Joseph Clay — Phila.
concerning Water-Color drawings \+ the
Exhibition. Miss Devry came \+ sat
awhile looking over my drawings.
At 12.30 I went down to Knoedler's
\+ got some paper on which to mount
my drawing — Rode up home to
lunch at 1.15 — back to studio at
2 — worked until 4.45 getting
the middle passage of Rock and
trees on the left, nearly finished.
Dr. Yale came in at dusk — I went
in to the N.A. of D. to see if Clay's
\+ Lewis's drawings had any — At
6.30 home to tea — Eve'g writing
notices to members of the W.C. Soc'y
got to bed about 10.30
