## Left Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 21 1869

a Regular Equinoctial storm day, wind & rainy, but warm — after breakfast, set to work Repainting the sketch in oil that I painted last Wed. of Lake Placid — It is of so little use to me as I left it then & my Recollection still so strong that I think it best to Repaint it — Sat at & worked until about 4.30 when I took a walk along the road with C —
Grl. has been on the bed all day complaining of dyspepsia. My sketch is much improved with plenty room for more. — Ever cleared off pleasantly & beautiful moonlight — Chatted with the Colmans washed brushes and got to bed at about 10 o'clock —

## Right Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 22 1869

All day clearing up — Mist, clouds very low & heavy — by ever all beautifully cleared away, beginning at the north — worked all day, Repainting oil sketch that I got from the Cobble last of Nash's on thursday of last week — Sat at & worked until 4, getting it finished to look much better than it did before — Then went out to walk and sauntered about until after sunset, watching & enjoying the wonderful color — Colman out shooting, but he got only one Clapse, a beautiful bird — Got a letter from Mary about Montrose, dated 12th inst — Itself sitting about until 9.30 when I went to bed —
