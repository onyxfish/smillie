## Left Page

### June Thursday, 17 1869.

Glorious day again — morning Rideit \+ burnished over plate getting it as far as I can carry it without a proof \- 12 days on it \- after dinner Commenced the etching of my Upper Ausable plate (1 day). Worked over the distant mountains, experimenting with dotting — Uncle Willie left for P. Keepsie \+ Montreal \- at 4 o'clk \- At 5 I set to Chops down some small trees on the mound in front of the house and got quite warmed up on it \- at 6 \- at 7 down to the River Charming every \- at 8.30, with Father \+ the girls, drove out to Hyde Park \+ back at 10 \- Hard work, whipping \+ driving the miserable old beast of a horse but a superb, cool, clear night \- Put up the horse \+ got to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### June Friday, 18 1869.

Very warm day and I almost used up with a headache \- After breakfast spent an hour with the girls, put a side-saddle on old Dolly the mare, let them ride about the place, an exercise much like riding on a camel \- Commenced work about 10 o'clk (2d day) on my Ausable etching, but had little spirit all day \- much concerned as to whether I am doing right in dotting the mountains \- Before we had finished taking tea Parker came \+ shortly took Matt off riding, they did not return until after 9 o'clk \- glorious night, got to bed soon after 10 o'clk.
