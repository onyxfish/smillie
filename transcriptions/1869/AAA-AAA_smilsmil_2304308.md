## Left Page

### May, Saturday, 8, 1869.

Pleasant day, but feeling forlorn
the effects of yesterday — Got
to studio ab't 9 o'clk — but
unable to bring myself to
work — Tried it on Meuger's
West Point drawing, but to
very little purpose — Went
home to lunch at 1 — back
at 2.30 — Again tried to
work on drawing — was hing
orange tints over it, making
it stronger but destroying
much careful work — Mr.
Saml. Colman called — Home
to tea at 5.30 — to Phil. at 7 —
last concert + Schumann's
"Manfred" with Edwin Booth —
Immense jam — but I do not
like the piece — The declamation
+ orchestra are inharmonious —
the audience did not seem to
think so — Ge. + I walked home
with the Tweedy girls — Home at
11.35 and to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday, 9, 1869.

Pleasant day — morning went
to Rev. Thos. Hastings + heard him
preach from 51st Psalm 12th verse.
He enlarged upon Christian joy
and thought him a mean man
who was always praying and
never singing — Services opened
with Skoyd's Te Deum which
was very well sung but ruined
by the voices was able organist.
Home + at 12.30 started for Uncle
[illegible] where I am — Shortly before 2
wandered about with Uncles W m
+ [illegible] + Tuney until tea time at 6 —
After tea a discussion on
Spirit manifestations which
detained me until 7.20. Home
at 9.30. Chatting until late
so that it was near midnight
when I got to bed.
