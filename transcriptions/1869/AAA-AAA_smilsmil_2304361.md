## Left Page

### August Tuesday, 24 1869

Up when the sun was just tipping the tops of the opposite mts. I never
saw anything more lovely than the sky, the moon, stranded on the pale
Coast of morn" — the smooth water
and its reflections of cool purple &
Rosy light. got back to camp about
7.30 — at 8.30 had bkft — and with
Souis Tiffany got off for the big slide
that comes down on the Camp. I
spent the mng climbing & making
slight pencil sketches in small
book — Ret? part way down, & lunched
at 1 o'clk. Coleman then joined us
& we spent the p.m. together — I
made large pencil drawing —
The atmospheric effects of Colm
over the mts were wonderful — but
later the sky became quite overcast
with clouds from the South — got
back to camp about 6.30 tired — from
sore & dissatisfied with my days work —
found Mr Dunham in camp. harmony
just come up — our entire party
are now here — Two of our guides
entertained us with a sort of Circus
exhibition of their strength & suppleness —
Monroe Holt coming out ahead — He
is 6 ft 3 in. high & weighs 194 lbs — we got
to bed about 10.45

## Right Page

### August Wednesday, 25 1869

Up at 6 o'clk and found as I had feared,
all overcast & threatening Rain. After
a hasty consultation the ladies all
agreed to go home at once to avoid
the possible wetting — at 9.30 they
all got off — Gil & I took the boat
& rowed about the lake enjoying
the wonderful effects of cloud & shadow —
Souis & Coleman at work on the "carry" —
Suddenly about noon a very heavy
shower came up, when we hurried
back to camp, well wet — I rowed
up & got Souis — Coleman came
along about 2.30 o'clk — the Rest
of the p.m. a succession of showers.
Melville Potter our Rum-dining
guide Ret? about 4 o'clk, drenched
with loads of provisions generally
wet and coughed — in with the po-
tatoes came the mail — among other
a letter from Matt, at San Francisco so
at sunset it broke away splendid
by — giving us great hopes for to-morrow.
[illegible] Whitley & Cooker — [illegible] greas-
ing boots and writing to Matt until
10 o'clk when I turned in, and
had a passable night — although
towards morning I was cold —
