## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 27, 1869

Very hot day, pleasantly tempered with breezes \- Up at 8 o'clk \- after a night with performances not altogether of my selection \- very weak \& entirely without energy \- but got at the biting of cross ruling on Georges sky (20th day) \+ had it done by lunch time \- About 2 o'clk a heavy thunder storm passed over us \- while Reading a very interesting letter just rec'd from Matt dated San. [illegible], July 17 \- At 3, went down town with plate \- filled in water with sealing way, laid a ground \+ ruled perpendicular cross \- Did not finish it until 6.45 \- Home to tea at 7.45 \- After tea in my big chair until 9 o'clk \- Then varnished plate and went to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 28, 1869

Overcast sky and cool breezes, comparatively. Spent the m'f, biting ruling over the mater on Georges plate (2nd day) afternoon, burnishing over sky and distance, hoping now, that it will look on the proof anything as it does on the plate, I may call it near finished \- About noon trouble commenced again with my bowels \- I worked until 7, but could not quite finish the burnishing. All night I was up at intervals of near an hour each \- By morning I was very weak.
