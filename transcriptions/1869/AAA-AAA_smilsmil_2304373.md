## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 17 1869.

Beautiful mng, but clouds hanging
on mt. tops to the south. Threatening
more venison & trout for [illegible] & at
3 o'clk started with Dibble & [illegible]
Melville & buckboard for Wilmington
Pass — broke nigh wind & spring soon
after starting, which changed me
from back to front seat. Drove
slowly & did not get into the pass
until 11 o'clk — Hardly up to my
expectations — altho' very wild. At
[illegible] got to the falls, just at the
outside, east, of the pass — Stopped
an hour for lunch & to sketch — I
pitched in on a large sheet of paper
as I was much pleased. Drove on to
Jay. & there D. changed his 3 seat wagon
for one with 2 seats & a board without
springs upon which I pounded home
the remaining 12 miles — Belle and
Mary Tweedy, by turns, sharing the other
seat — at 3 [illegible] commenced to spatter
and we had more or less of it until we
got home, about 6.30 — all tired and
hungry — The Patter's had a good
dinner for us & after a quiet chat
we all got off to bed early —

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 18 1869.

Cloudy & pleasant all day, but
my jolting had made me very
uncomfortable about the head,
so that I was not able to go out
to work — Stayed in doors and
during the mng. penciled over
the sketch I got of the Mitrauable
Falls in Wilmington Pass — and in
the afternoon I worked slightly
over the pencil sketch that I
got day before yesterday from
the Cobble East of Nash's, looking
south — Splendid moonlight
night — Rec'd a letter from
Clare & a paper — [illegible] Rec'd
one from Father — got to bed
about 11 o'clk — by our time here,
which may be an hour wrong
either way.
