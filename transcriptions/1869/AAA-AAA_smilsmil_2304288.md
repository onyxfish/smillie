## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 31, 1869

Beautiful day, but windy — Got to Studio at 8.15 — Father walked down with me to see our Academy pictures — Russell came for them about 9'o'clk — M\[illegible\] spent touching on die [illegible] of Steamships and Sheep's head for Father — and making a drawing on wood for Kelly, the "Canvas-man" — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 afternoon, worked over small water-color sketches, preparing them to send to Phila. M\[illegible\] M\[illegible\] J. Hays", the great american animal Painter" called on us — Home to Tea at 7 — every tired out — Oliver I needy 'in the parlor with the girls — so I went to bed at 9.30

Miss Deury drew at the Studio during the m\[illegible\].

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 1, 1869

Fine day — overcast toward ev'g \+ raining at night — Got to Studio at 8.20 worked all day on a water-color drawing, that am trying to do in a bold sketchy style, a view on the Saco, for the coming [illegible] in Phila. This I may call my 2d day on it, altho' heretofore I have only worked at odds ends of time on it — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — Found Young Bell waiting for me — Set him to drawing — Harry Tenn called — add Dr. Kingsley — I worked until 6 o'clk — Home to Tea at 7 — ev'g spent looking over back numbers of Ill. Lon\[illegible\] news for materials for a Steamship drawing — got to bed about 11 o'clk.
