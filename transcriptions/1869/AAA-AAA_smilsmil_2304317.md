## Left Page

### May, Friday 28, 1869

Got to the Studio at 8.45. weather very cool. Ther. 65°. \+ threatening Rain. Set to work finishing Tracing of Geo's \+ my subjects for Pate (2d day) \+ getting every thing Ready for Transfers. Miss Deu [illegible] came to Studio. but not to work as she is suffering from Hay fever or something very like it. Home at 12.20, lunched \+ at 1 started for Pate's. Got both subjects transferred onto the etching grounds, by 2.30 \+ then went to A.B.N.Co. where I Ruled the lines (limits) on the plates. bought varnish \+ got back to Studio by 5. Mr. Knudsen called. so did Misses [illegible] Stout, Arnold \+ Goadby. Varnished margins of my plates \+ am Ready to begin etching to-morrow. Home to tea at 6.30. Evef. Spent Reading \+ writing. Got to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 29, 1869

Very Cool and overcast. Mf, clearing up before noon \+ getting very warm. Got to Studio at 8.45. and from got to work on my etching (2d day) George's subject for Mr. Pate. It was some little time before I could get the "hang" of the large mok glass that I have borrowed of Father. Mr. Meuger called about 10. he is pleased with the drawing of [illegible] bird that I have made for him. Mr. Riduer then came in with some dry colors. Then Fossil Williams, when I had to Rush to meet my appointment with Fred Duvan, he finished me off by 12.30. Home to lunch back at 2. Miss Arnold at the Studio also Rondel, Mr. \+ Miss Bolton. also some one to buy a picture for a wedding present. worked on my etching until near 7. Then home to tea. Uncle Millie just in from Ottawa. Evening chatting \+ to bed about 11. Drawings from Phila. arr'd. home (at Studio) about 4.30 P.M.
