## Left Page

### July Friday, 23 1869.

Still pleasant, but over cast — worked on Georgia plate (18th day) — with me home I stoned down much of the surface, Reducing the Ruling, I expect now to Refresh it by Rebiting — Dragged portions of the notes Reflections & bit it — at [illegible] laid ground over sky and distance for cross Ruling — Father brought up my plate with bits of blue Ruled — We have all been exceedingly shocked by the news to-day of Frank Ballard's defalcation — $63,500, a terrible blow to all — Every again at my water color — bop — to bed at 11

## Right Page

### July Saturday, 24 1869.

Another hot day — got started early biting in bits of blue on a usable plate (19th day) but so anxious to get color enough & out of practice in such biting that I over bit it very much — sent the Mrs., stoning it down trying to Reclaim it, but no use — Father home at noon — He & mother went to Plainfield, N.J., to spend Sunday with Uncle [illegible] — After lunch, went to A.B.M. Co's — got [illegible] of sky & gave plate to Doe to send it out — at 4 o'clk went to [illegible] — He has no money. at 5 to foot of Chambers St — went over big Sound Str. [illegible] Prov. — dence — [illegible] came & we took 5.30 train to Patterson, N.J. Drove out to Dr. McClellan's & were greeted by the Kimballs & Irelands — Spent the eveg on piazza and got to bed about 11 —
