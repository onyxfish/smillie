## Left Page

### April, Thursday 22, 1869

Charming day — got to Studio at 8.45 and set to work on
12×22 (5th day). Repainting on the mountains — better
it but not improving it as I know that I should — At
noon, obliged to give up by a headache, & without mak-
ing to finish my mts —
went home & to bed — where I stayed until 6 o'clk —
Then got up & sat around until 11, when [illegible] came
in from Mr. [illegible] Rogers where we were invited to
spend the eveng. Wrote a short letter to Fanny M.
and got to bed about 1 o'clk a.m.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 23, 1869

Pleasant day — got to the studio at 8.20 & set to work (6th day)
12×22 — finishing the second painting — going over the left h-
nt and the water. It is now suffused with a sort of Mars
yellow tone, but I expect to cor-
rect that in the next painting
by a liberal use of blue —
Home to lunch at 1.45 and
rode down to Phil. Rehearsal
at 2.15. Enjoyed the Mendels-
sohn Symphony very much,
after Reh. Mary & Belle Tweedy
& I went to the Somerville
gallery & saw Avery's pictures.
Back to studio at 6 o'clk —
Home to tea at 7 — evening
spent reading Manfred.
To bed shortly after 10 —
Miss Devoy at the studio
this mng.
