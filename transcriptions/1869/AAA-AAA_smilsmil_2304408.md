## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 30, 1869

weather considerably mixed, but mostly Rain — It tried hard to clear up about 11.a.m. Got to studio at 10 \+ to work with Apts 1 wk. \+ brown outlining 33×44" Dark against dark golden death — (2º day) — "Moat Mt. or 'Lifting of the Clouds'" was sent up from Snedicor's — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.15 after making a call at Bogardus's Gallery — worked as long as I could see but did not finish outline — Granning \+ a Mr. Fulton called during the P.M. — Home to Tea at 6 — Eve: worked until 9 o'clk, finishing outline of Locomotive \+ Render on Pacific R.R. Bond drawing — a long talk with father \+ to bed at about 11.15

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 1, 1869

Charming day — Got to studio at 9.15 after meeting \+ walking with Miss Hattie Stone of Pittsburgh — At work outlining on 33×44. (3º day) Home to lunch at 1.30 a little sketching Car wheel trucks at the Harlem R.R. depot — back at 2.30 P.m. continued outlining — Fanny Kimball \+ Woods called about 4 \+ I had a very pleasant visit, after them Maud Diven came in \+ I went home with her, getting home at 5.45 — Father home with a headache. After tea, Read for an hour, then worked on R.R. Bond drawing until 9.45 — got to bed at 11
