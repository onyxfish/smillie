## Left Page

### March, Tuesday, 9, 1869.

Delightful day \- Started from the house shortly after 8 o'clk, making some purchases for lunch to-day \- did not get to the studio until almost 9 \- Spent the m'g painting on 24×40 (9th day) going over the matter very unsatisfactorily \- Bur Mulford called \- also 6 or 7 ladies from Boston, bearing a card from Ordway \- at about 1 o'clk our adirondack lunch party arr \- Mr. \+ Mrs. Sam'l. Coleman \- Misses Cram Coleman, Howard, Belle, Mary \+ Jenny Tweedy \- a noisy party \+ apparently a happy one \- While at lunch Arvine Ballard called \- After lunch Mr. Samudin \+ [illegible] lunch party left about 3 o'clk \- at 3.30 I went Riding with Mr. Forbes to Studio about 5.30 \- Did some fixing up \+ at 6.45 home to tea \- Spent the ev'g until 10.30 at Dering's listening to his music \- Got to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday, 10, 1869.

a perfectly demoralized \+ demoralizing day \- Did not get up until after 8 o'clk \- Raining \+ blowing hard \+ spitefully \- Got to the Studio at 9.45 \- feeling so unlike work that the whole morning passed \+ did little or nothing \- Miss Lewy came shortly after 12 \- just before 1 I walked home to lunch \- did not get back until 2.40 \- Afternoon painted, but without any spirit, on the foreground of my 24×40 \- on the small tree-trunk \+ stick stuff \- Home to tea at 6.45 \- Ev'g Spent cutting linings into a book that Joe had borrowed \+ damaged. House uncomfortably hot \+ glad to get to bed at 10 o'clk \- Storm still keeping driving \- mother went with Mary to Newark yesterday to visit Nette [illegible] \- not home yet \-
