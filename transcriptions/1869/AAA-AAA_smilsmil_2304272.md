## Left Page

### February Saturday, 27 1869.

Clear, crisp & windy — got to Studio at 8.15 & at work at once on A.B.
Co's drawing Arkansas State Arms. At it closely until 10 clk when
I went home to lunch — back at 2.10 — Eaton, his wife & two other
ladies called — worked on drawing until near 5 o'clk — walked around
by 25th St. leaving a Phil. Circular for J. C. Nicoll — then home at 6.30 —
Had tea & spent most of the evening fooling over arms drawing which
does not at all please me — Mr. Thos. Hooker called at the house —
Kate in Jersey City. So with Matt, I had the benefit of his
call — Gil went down to the [illegible] — I got to bed about
11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### February Sunday, 28 1869.

Glorious clear cold day — My, went to Dr. Hastings & heard him
preach an admirable sermon from 139 th Psalm "I am fearfully
& wonderfully made". He considered it in a spiritual sense, our capabilities for good or for evil — our virtues & their alternate vices. At
noon, with Gil, went up to Uncle A's to dinner — Uncle Mr. & aunt H —
there — a nap from 4 to 5 — then home — tea at 6.30 — Evett went
to Dr. Thompson's (Jos. P.) & heard him on the relations of a good
Christians to existing governments — making it for a practical
application — Home at 9.30 & to bed at 11 —
