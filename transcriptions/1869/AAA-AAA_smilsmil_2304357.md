## Left Page

### August Monday, 16 1869.

Dull, threatening morning \- Got through packings \- fixing up of trunks \+ off at 8 45 \- our team, farm horses just off grass, were drive provokingly slow \- One or two rather showers near noon as we were in the Keene pass \- Into Keene Valley shortly after 12 and about 3 o got to D. Patten's where we were greeted with a shout. It was insisted upon that we could stay \+ by crowding up Room was made \- lunch at once \- then unpacking of trunks \+ settling into new quarters \- The party now at D. P's \- is Mr. Dunham \- Mr. \+ Mrs. Samuel Colman \+ Misses Corinne Colman \- Clara Dunham \- Belle, Mary and Florence Tweedy, Annie Tiffany \- Sue Tiffany, Geo. Clark and myself \- About 4 o'clk \- most of us went off to take a walk, but a little sprinkling \+ threatenings of more soon brought us home \- Dinner at 6 and Evening until about 9. Mrs. Colman Read to us from Mrs. Stowe's "Old-town folks \-" all got off to bed soon after 10 \-

## Right Page

### August Tuesday, 17 1869.

Very much the same sort of day as yesterday, a little more promise to be more aggravatingly broken as the day advanced. After bkfst \- for some time, putting up nails and stowing things in our cubby hole of a room \- about 10 o'clk got off with sketching traps \+ joined C. \+ S. at the foot of a Ridge not far from the house \- Did nothing but sport a piece of paper \- introduced to a Miss Elliott who is here painting \- Back to lunch \- Afternoon the wholes of us went to a cliff farther than the mt \- one, but it threatened Rain so that after some time picking berries we Returned \- Even't sitting around until after 10 \- then to bed \-
