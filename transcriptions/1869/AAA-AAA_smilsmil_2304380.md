## Left Page

### October Friday, 1 1869

Yet another superb day — Very like a weather breeder — Up at 6
& off with Dr. P. at 6.30 to join
the C's on the boat at West Port —
got to the top of Giants Leap a
little before 9 ocl'c + to work at
once on W.C. Study finishing it
at 12.30 when I cooked my lunch —
To say that I am lonesome doesn't
express it — The woods never before
seemed to me so desolate. At 3
I went away up the Ravine &
2 got outline of what I some time
ago made oil sketch of — Save
down, another slight Crayon
sketch & at head of 2d falls another —
about 4.30 started down getting
home shortly before 6 — Dined
alone, spent the evng in my
Room looking over studies &
working with Crayon — Dr. P.
did not return from W.P.
I got to bed about 10 —

## Right Page

### October Saturday, 2 1869

Up shortly after 6 having spent a
tolerably comfortable night dreaming
of Ravines, big Rocks & Fall painting —
Staying all alone in this wilderness
doesn't seem such a terrible thing
after all — The weather threatens of
last night still continue — bright
clear, but wind very strong and
gusty from the S — got to work
on my study of Rocks near the
house about 8.30 — as I work on
the big Rock, squatting, my umbrella
tied to a long stick, I had to lay
for the sun from 11 until 11.30 —
went home about to lunch & back
to work at once, getting through
about 4 ocl'k — when I got back to
the house, found him possession
of a peddler — a Mr. Gattling — He
had persuaded Mrs. P. to spend
some $60 or $70 of her hard earned
dollars — about 5 the Dr. Ret. from
West Port, bringing me a note from
& a letter from Sid. Hay, that
has been lying at 2 towns for near
a month — Gattling put his trap
in the big Redone — his horse & wagon
in the barn & to stay over Sunday —
had hard work to get rid of my
guest & went to bed ab't 9.30
