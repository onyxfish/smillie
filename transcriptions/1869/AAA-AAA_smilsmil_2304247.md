## Left Page

### January Friday, 8 1869.

Clear + very mild day \- Up at 7 \- made a note to Burling + got to the Studio at past 9 \- Commenced 3d Sky on my Water Color \- working very cautiously with light washes of Antwerp + Cobalt alternating for my blue \- + Y. ochre Neut. tint + Indigo for my greys \- Had to do considerable stippling in some places along the edges where I had washed + sandpapered \- Miss Devry came in the morning \- Worked until 1 \- took lunch at the Studio. At 2.20 went to Phil. [illegible] \- very much crowded \- with the noise confusion + crowding for seats, my appetite for the music was quite destroyed + I regretted losing the time from the Studio \- Before it was over, went with Joe to Stewarts, Snedico's + Chickerings \- put her in a car + then met Fanny Kimball + walked home with her \- then to Studio \- home at 6.30 and until 10.30 writing addresses on Reception Circulars \- Got to bed just before 11 o'clk \-

## Right Page

### January Saturday, 9 1869.

Very mild \- at times overcast + threatening Rain \- Got to Studio at 9 o'clk and at once got to work on the Sky of my drawing, working with patience but much disheartened \- By my working so much over the paper \- washing out + sandpapering the texture has become as granulated as to destroy the distinctive beauty of Water Color washes \- I regret it exceedingly \- as I wished to present the characteristic charms of Water Color not such a mixed mess + evidence of my ignorance \- Home to lunch at 1 \- back at 2 \- + worked until near 5 \- J. J. Griffen called about 4.30 PM, then my aunt \- About 4.30 Bayard Taylor came in + made a call of about ½ a hour \- Home at 5.30 \- dressed, took tea + with Matt + Jo went early to the Phil Concert \- an outragious [sic] rain but by dint of hard work secured good seats, but suffered from the heat + confusion \- much general dissatisfaction with Breming's management \- From these reasons but half enjoyed the Concert \- walked home with Miss Hows to Emma Ireland's + then to the Kimballs with Will + Fanny \- Home + to bed at 12 \-
