## Left Page

### February Thursday, 11 1869.

Thick, heavy mist, clearing off before noon, when there was a slight, April-like sprinkle — lovely skies —
Got to studio at 9 — Commenced a 3d drawing for the Bryant Series — No. 11. — Toning Bell came + I gave him a lesson in water colors, making a slight color sketch for him — Home to lunch at 1.45 back in an hour —
Two ladies — one of them a Mr. Benedict called to see my drawings — I finished my Bryant drawing about 4 o clk —
Mr. Forbes then came in + I worked on one of her drawings until after 5.
Then went to G.P. Putnam's to explain my plan of "Illustrating" among the trees — He is afraid that it will cost too much — Want of time troubles me more than the want of the money.
I do not see how I could get it through this year — Home to tea at 7.15 —
Eve'g. putting lettering on No. 13 —
Got to bed shortly after 11 —

## Right Page

### February Friday, 12 1869.

A glorious day, but too much like spring — got to studio at 9 o'clk at —
Miss Dewey came along + drew for an hour. I was engaged upon my 24×40 charcoal drawing " On the Boquet". Corinne Colman called just as I was going home to lunch —
Got off at 1.30 — back at 2.30. Began continuing "On the Boquet" n canvas with brown — worked until 4.30 —
Then went to Lauthier's + ordered 3 passe-partouts — from then to G. P. Putnam's — not in — then to Inedicoi's — then to Studio at 6.30 — Fixed fire, went to Schmitt's — got drawing-board —
Home at 7.15 — Got tea — visited with mother for 15 m — then to Burgraff's — then to 41 W 48. St. to see Fan M — not in — Home
the Rest of eve'g until 11 o clk fixing up drawing-board —
Got to bed at 11.30 —
