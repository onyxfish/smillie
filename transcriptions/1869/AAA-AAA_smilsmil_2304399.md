## Left Page

### November Friday, 12 1869.

Still fine weather — got to
studio at 9.15 & to work again
on Moat Mt. painting on the
R.P. middle distant trees — much
interrupted — Mr. Hill, a mill
owner, for whom I have made
a drawing (of his mills) took up
an hour talking about little
changes — all might have been
said in five minutes & I left to
my work — at 1, home to lunch — from
there to Phil. Rehearsal — Matt
Kate ahead of me — Did not enjoy
myself as usual. By the boxes being
sold & I too poor to buy I did not have
my seat. After Rehearsal walked up
to Studio mth Belle & Mary Tweedy & Clara
Dunham — from there to Synagogue —
5th Ave & 43d S. Heard Vespers, C. D. went
home with her father & I went by [illegible] my
home with the Tweedy's. At 6.30 back to
studio — cleaned palette — washed up &
& home to tea at 7.30. Evening worked
awhile on Hills drawing and on
Locomotive for Pacific R.R. R[oad] drawing
to bed at 10 —

## Right Page

### November Saturday, 13 1869.

Pleasant m[orning], but clouded over
about noon — got to studio at 9.15
to work on "Moat Mt." (9th day)
painting in l.h. middle distance
trees — Alman came in at 1 o'clk
then Swain Gifford — Burling
in between 10 & 11 — Home to lunch
at 1 — back at 2.15 — difficult to
see to paint so dark — Mr. Kellogg
of Utica, with sister & Bro. called —
he would have bought if I had
had anything finished — Katie
[illegible] called & two one ladies
to see about taking lessons of George —
at 5.30 took little painting of Edmunds
and around to Bogardus' new Gallery —
Home to tea at 6 — at 8 went to
call on the Tweedy's — there until
11 o'clk — walked home, stopped
at Apollo Hall (church fair) & found
Charles & Miles Palmer — walked
together — Home at 11.30
to bed at 12 —
