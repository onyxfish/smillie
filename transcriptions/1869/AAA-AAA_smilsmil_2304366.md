## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 3 1869.

a beautiful day — the whole party, girls and all went to the top of "Giant's leap" and spent the day — I got off at 9.15 — ahead of the Rush after waiting ½ an hour for them — got to the top of the [illegible] at 10.20 \+ set to work on water color study (13×20 in) that I outlined some time ago — worked until 12.30 when I joined the party, a short distance up the stream \+ lunched with them — back to work at 1.30 — Terribly bothered with sky and distance — at 3 gave it up, as my light was all gone — \+ went away up the stream — got a Rattle of a sketch \+ back at 4.30 Started at 5 — all home at 6.30. Miss Elliott spent the day with us — Every very quiet — Played whist with Mr. Dunham, Mr. \+ Mrs. Colman — to bed a few minutes after 10 and slept Roy illy —

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 4 1869.

a superb day, but quite warm. at 8.15 Colman \+ I got off to the Giant again — I took up my old traps — got to work at 9.25 — I commenced a study of the first fall back of the leap — the Rock Masses, fractures and color please me — worked steadily until 12.30 when I had it all laid in — then we lunched — at 1.30 I was about to commence a study of Rocks \+ trees near my one, but as C. had gone up the stream I suddenly determined to follow \+ started on at 2 getting to where I had sketched yesterday, at 2.30 — I immediately set to work and by 5 had laid in a view of the gorge — The afternoon clouded up so that my light was not good — at 5.20 we started down — left my traps in my Rubber blanket above head of the fall — got home at about 6.45 — very tired and hungry. Every — washed out my brushes and note —
