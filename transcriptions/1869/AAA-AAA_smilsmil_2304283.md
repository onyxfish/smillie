## Left Page

### March, Sunday, 21, 1869

Beautiful clear day — cold with feeling headachy \+ tired out spent the day snoozing about in chairs \+ sunning myself on the back piazza. Went to hear J. P. Thompson. He gave the 3d of his series on the Creation, treating this well the question — Is man an outgrowth of nature (Darwin \+ Huxley) or is man separate \+ above, to rule over nature? — Some one has been criticising J's pronunciation of the word "Epoch" — He pronounces the E long — He opened his discourse with a characteristic \+ bad tempered explanation — giving a variety of authorities on both sides \+ stating that he was educated before it was fashionable to clip the word — was always thankful for suggestions — very sarcastic, and in his discourse pronounced the word with offensive distinctness. ## Right Page

### March, Monday, 22, 1869

Glorious day — up rarely — had my bkfst. \+ got to studio just before 8 o'clk — at once set to work on 24×40 (19th day) painting the foliage over the sky — I have all along dreaded this part and its difficulties are fully equal to my apprehensions — Again I see so much that must be done that I am almost hopeless of getting it done by Apl. 1st — Home to lunch abt — wrote notes to D. F. W. Lewis \+ Danl. E. Saint — back to studio at 2.30 \+ worked as long as I could see — Home to tea at 6.45 — Evg. with Jss. went to Fanny Kimball's — Saw them all but Will — Saw the Cousin Miss Nola Cargill — Played parlor croquet with Woods — Home abt 11.30 — overcast \+ flakes of snow flying — Got to bed about 12.
