## Left Page

### Sept. Monday, 13 1869.

*at Scott's - North Elba*

glorious day - up at 5.45 &
after mending, piquing, fussing &
breakfasting got off with Willard
Dibble at 8.15 for this place. The
party is Mr. + Mrs. C. Misses Bell,
Mary + Florence Inneedy, Miss Clara
Dunham, G. E. + J. with Miss Corinna
Coleman + Chas. Smith on Patten's
buckboard. The day very warm
at Keene met old Mr. Durand and
family with Miss Walters on their way
to Wilmington Pass. Got to Edmunds
Ponds - a sketch + lunch at 1.30 near
the bank of the lake — The 2 E. had
on Round Pond, was to me very fine
walking through the woods we ate
lots of berries, Moose, Goose, black
+ blue — got to Scotts + at work on
the view looking to Indian Pass
by 3.30 P.M. C. G. E. + J — I endeavored
to work with ole Color + pegged away
abit until 6.15 getting nothing worth
the trouble. The views + color about
was something marvelous. Dinner
at 6.45 + a very excellent one.
Evening as beautiful as the day out-
a moonlight of rare beauty
walking, singing + chatting, getting
to bed about 10° —

## Right Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 14 1869. Lake Placid

*at Nash's - Bennett's Pond +*

another superb day — Up at 6.
adopted, got outline sketch of Miss
+ at 8 off for this place. 5 miles. with
a cart wagon, the party walking and
riding by turns until we got to John
Brown's — I made a sketch of house
+ surroundings but did not go to the
grave. C. + wife Florence S. G.E. + J.
rode on, the Rest of the party went
up to the house + grave — We got to
Nash's about 10. I at last, used up
with a headache + went right to
bed where I lay until 5 o'clk when
feeling somewhat better I got up +
went over to Lake Placid — Did not
go upon the lake, very much
disappointed at what I saw — Bennett's
Pond + the Mt. views about very charming
Dinner of Roast Venison + steaks.
Evening I rowed upon the Pond
in a very light boat that carried
but two, taking Belle, Mary + Clara
— The moonlight perfect, north-
on lights strong & water like glass
every thing lovely — went to bed
about 2.30
