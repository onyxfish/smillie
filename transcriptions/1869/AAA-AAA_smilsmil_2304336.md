## Left Page

### July, Monday 5, 1869

Beautiful day, clean breezy and cool \- Up at 6.30 \- Hcfp's at 8 \- Preparations for picnic until 10.30 when we started for the woods a half mile or so from the house \- We numbered about 15 \- Great tribulation over a clam chowder \- Dinner about 2 \- Plenty of good spread \+ informal jollity around loose \- Broke up between 4 \+ 5, when Mary, Belle \+ Jenny Tweedy, Ge. and I went off to a hill top for a view \- Home shortly after 6 \- Tea \- then a visit again to "Duntaun", on return family company \+ much clamour in getting off fireworks for the children \- Ev'g quite chilly \- Later a game of "Jerusalem" "for fun" and t'was fun \- then an effort at a square dance \- Shortly after 10 \- Company went away, but the home party did not have their fun out until 11.30 when I went to bed and slept splendidly until

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 6, 1869

Wakened at 5 o'clk \- up \+ dressed \- Really chilly \- pleasant mood fire in the dining Room \- Hcfp's at 6 o'clk \- at 6.30 Messrs Benedict and Nicholls, Mary \+ Belle Tweedy, Ge. \+ I go off for N.Y., escorted to the Danbury depot by Jennie Tweedy Miss Lottie Kellogg \- Heavy Train \- at Norwalk numbered 17 Cars, all full \- Behind time getting to 42d St. at 9.30 \- \+ to 26th at 10 \- Too late for Belle to take the 10 o'clk train for Irvington as she had promised \- Ge. went home with the girls \- I got to studio at 10.20 \- Etched awhile (day) but so sleepy that I took a good nap on the lounge \- Home to lunch abt \- back at 2.30 \- Finished etching \+ proofed plate for biting \- At 6, went to the Irelands \- Saw Miss Mary \- I wished to make arrangements for visiting them at Patterson \- then spent an hour inspecting the new building of Yng mens Ch's Ass'n \- Home to tea at 7.30 \- Ev'g being tired, did nothing \- got to bed about 10 \-
