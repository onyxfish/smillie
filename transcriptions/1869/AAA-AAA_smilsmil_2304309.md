## Left Page

### May, Monday 10, 1869.

Splendid day — Before going to
the Studio, called on M. Dan-
Huntington — to withdraw my name
if possible, from the list of can-
didates for Academician — too
late, however. Got to Studio
at 9.20 + set to work on West
Point drawing — (6th day) Feel
disheartened — the paper works
so badly, and it promises to be
a failure — Home to lunch
abt 1 — back at 2.30 — worked
until 7 o'clk P.M. — getting
into a very much better
frame of mind — quite hopeful.
Tea at 7.30 — at 8 went to
152 M+4 — Tom Tessenders
+ spent the evn't until 10 o'cl
with S.C.T — then home and
to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 11, 1869.

Clear very warm day — got to studio
at 8.45 and at once got to work
on West Point drawing (7th day)
but all the m'f, altho' feeling
very unlike work — Home to
lunch at 1.10 — back at 2.30
Mrs. Poreront called and
later, Maud + Ella Diven —
Worked on my drawing until
6.45, getting it almost done.
While in many respects better
than I had hoped to get it,
is still generally unsatis-
factory + must always be so.
Home to tea at 7.15 — at 8.15
at Seenley's Studio, 82 Fifth Av
Water-Color Society meeting.
No quorum out — Resolved
ourselves into a Board of
Control Meeting + transacted
business connected with Burling's
trip to England — Home at 11
and to bed at 11.15
