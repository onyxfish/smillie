## Left Page

### May, Sunday 30, 1869

Rain during early m'f, but ceased before 9 o'clk — Mug — went to D'r Hashtup's \+ listened to an old School Pres'n, Rev D'r Vicky or something of that sort, from Steubenville O — His text was Romans XII" 15". His delivery was so bad that even when carefully listening I coyed not understand the half of what he said — Home to dinner. After dinner Read until between 4\+5 o'clk — then walked up to F[illegible] Park \+ sauntered about enjoying immensely the birds \+ the flowers — got home at 8.15 — took tea and spent the ev'g Reading from Cobbe on "Religious Duty". Much pleased with her style, but not always with her matter, as now \+ then the Unitarian gets to be too strong to suit me — got to bed about 11.30

## Right Page

### May, Monday 31, 1869

Very warm day, so they say, but I know nothing of it as I have been in bed all day — got to studio at 8.30 — [illegible] made a call, but I soon got to work at my etching — Shortly after 9 discovered that I was partially blinded so made calculations for a headache — At 10.30 had to go home \+ right to bed where I stayed until about 7 P.M — when I got up \+ took [illegible] — wrote \+ rec'd a letter from Fan Mulford — sat up until after mid-night when I went to bed
