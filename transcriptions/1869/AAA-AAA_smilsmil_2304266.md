## Left Page

### February Monday, 15 1869.

Dark & Rainy m'g — drying up about noon — got to Studio at 9.20 — kindled fire — Miss De came & drew for an hour, Alfred Jones made quite a visit — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — Painted all day (1st day) on 24×40 " "On the Boquet" — By painting into a very strong glow & using my Bl& Walnut palette I think that I have made a strong start — I am painting it in very broadly, hoping to avoid some of my usual faults of niggle. Mr. Forbes & misses Sterling & [illegible] made us a call late in the p.m. Home to tea at 6.30 — at 8.20 Gel & I left our cards at Mr. Shel. Roosevelt's — then went to No. 35 E 22d St & called on Miss Jenny Treedy — Char. joined us at 9.15 — left in Company at 10.15 — Right home — clear Starlight night — got to bed at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### February Tuesday, 16 1869.

Bright for a time during the m'g but soon clouded over, making another dark day — got to Studio at 10 o'clk — at work at once on 24×40 — Home to lunch at 1 o'clk — back about 2.30 — Found Fan M. had been waiting for me for about an hour. She goes home to-morrow P.M. — Bid her good bye — Painted until about 4 — getting middle distance & water covered — Spent an hour or more with Mr. Forbes & Miss Sterling, giving them some hints in drawing — Home to tea at 6 — at 7.45 at Miss Cook's 92 W 10th St. Private Theatricals — "Three Points of the Law" and "Your life is in danger" — Very cleverly played indeed — Emma Ireland was exceedingly good as widow Smylie — Gel. Cook was one of the [illegible] — Mr. & Mrs. Jno. Hawes present — Home to bed at 11.30
