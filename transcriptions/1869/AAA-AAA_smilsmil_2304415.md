## Left Page

### December Thursday, 16 1869

Miserable day, driving wind & rain from 10 until
about 5 P.M. then heavy fog
& very mild — later in the eve
brilliant moonlight night — got
to Studio about 9.20 — Too dark
to paint, so I made a drawing
for Geo Steiner, for a label for
some patent yeast powder —
in water colors — It was a poor
thing, mainly owing to a very
miserable piece of Bristol
board that I worked upon —
S.D. Hay called during the
m't. Home to lunch at 1 — back
at 1.40, worked until after 1,
Home to tea at 6 — Well worked
until 11 o'clk — as hard as I
could drive, writing addresses
on Water Color Exh'b'n Circulars
(Berling being sick) — Then to
bed —

## Right Page

### December Friday, 17 1869

Charming mild day, but the
streets in a horrible condition
with mud — Got to Studio at
9.25 & at work on 1, twilight
(7th day) finishing the foliage
down to the Rocks, almost —
Lost some time posting circulars,
sticking the stamps etc. etc.
at 1 went to Kimball's to give
Fanny Geo's ticket to Y.M.C.A.
Studio Receptions to-night — Then
home to lunch, back at 2.20
worked until 4 then down
to Phil. Rehearsal — in time
to hear the Jubel Overture —
[illegible] there — at 5 back to Studio.
Home to tea at 6 — at 8.15 went
with Matt to Y.M.C.A. Bldg
to the opening of the Studios.
A very pleasant affair, saw
lots of friends — left shortly
after 11 — to bed. Down
after 12. —
