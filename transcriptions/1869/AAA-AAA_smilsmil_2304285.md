## Left Page

### March, Thursday 25, 1869

Another charming day — got to the studio at 7.30. AM, & returned to work on 24×40 (21st day) — worked over the distant mts, the water & tree trunks across it — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — afternoons work somewhat interfered with by callers etc — but more than all by the fact that such long hours use me up before the day is done — I am working under a head of steam just up to the bursting point — Home to tea at 7 o'clk — Did nothing during the eve but wash a few brushes — got to bed at 9.45

## Right Page

### March, Friday 26, 1869

Disagreeable N. E. Rain storm all day — got to studio at 8.15 & set to work on 24×40 (22nd day) glazing strongly over the foreground R. h. corner & then painting over it. I consider it so much improved as to be quite hopeful again that it will be a creditable work — Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.30 — while away Dr. F. M. Lewis called — Very dark P.M. — Managed to work after a fashion until near 6 — Home to tea at 7 — Welf washed brushes — Read some geology & got to bed about 10 o'clk — At noon wrote to Uncle Willie for Moccasins — & to Mr & Mrs Hy Marks, inviting them to the studio to see our pictures
