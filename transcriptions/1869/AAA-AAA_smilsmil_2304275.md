## Left Page

### March Friday, 5 1869.

Clear, cold, beautiful day \- got to the studio at 9.15 \- at work a
24×40 (7½ days) painting tree
trunks and foliage \- miss Dea[illegible]
came + drew for an hour \-
I worked until 1.30 \- then home
to lunch \- at 2.15 Rode down
to Phil. Rehearsal \- enjoyed
it very much \- at 4.30 went
to Gauthier's (Passe-partout)
then to Snedicors \- then to
the Studio \- getting there a
little before 6 o'clk \- Set to
work + by 7 o'clk had things
all Ready to go at weeking [illegible]
to-morrow mrg. On my way hom[e]
stopped in 34th St. + left invitations
with Miss Corson for to-morrow's Re[ception]
\- then to Burggraff's \- then home
Tea at 8 o'clk \- Evely tired
\+ did nothing but get rested
for to-morrow \- got to bed a
little before 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### March Saturday, 6 1869.

Up bright + early + got to studio
not before 8 o'clk \- swept, dusted
scrubbed + cleaned house generally
until 11.30 when, very tired + dirty
went home, took a bath, dressed
myself, eat lunch + feeling much
refreshed + very clean, at 2.15
got back to the Studio \- a miserable
snow storm had made the mf gloomy
unpromising, but before 3 o'clk it had
quite cleared off winding up the day
with a strong N.Westerly gale \- Our
Reception was a success + some
was very pleasant \- got went home
at 5 o'clk. I did not get away until
6 \- Joe waited + walked up
with me \- at 7.15 I went to Phil. Con[cert]
Charl, Matt + Att, Mary + the Dr. were
all there, so I had no trouble about a
seat. Enjoyed the Concert very much
altho' tired \- walked home with [illegible]
got to bed about 11.30 \- Mrs. [illegible]
Raynor, Mr. Forbes + Miss Sterling
sent us bouquets for our Reception
