## Left Page

### Sept. Thursday, 9 1869.

Heavy Rain during the night with change of wind to the
N.W. making the weather cooler, constant showers
all day long in dreary succession — as my traps are
all up in the "Grant" — I borrowed oil colors of G[illegible]
and painted over the Rough color sketch that I
made from the slide on the Upper Ausable Lake.
There was little in it to speak but I question if I made it
much better — It is a cold green all over and looks
like a Tea Tray — Eve[ning] very quiet and got to bed
about 10, 30

## Right Page

### Sept. Friday, 10 1869.

Up before 6 to bid "good bye" to
Mr. Dunham who started for N.Y. Weather as gloomy
as ever — cold and desolate.
Clouds very low — but hardly any Rain — Again worked
with oil Colors — in the m[orning]. Painting sky on study
on Lower Lake + p.m. the same on another study,
getting them Ready for [use] if I can get in to the
lake again — Feeling the need of exercise, about [3]
o'clk. I met off with the rest of the party to pick
black berries. the p.m. showing signs of clearing — Spent
the eve[ning] Reading + got to bed shortly after 10.
