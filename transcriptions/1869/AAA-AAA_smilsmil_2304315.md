## Left Page

### May, Saturday 22, 1869

Pain in the m[orning], but stopped before I went to the studio at 8.45 o'clk — Got to work again upon 12×22 w. "Lower Ausable" 8½ days) altho' last night I thought I was done with it — worked all day at it [and] improved it, I think, very much. It has been much more deficient to manage than I had expected — now I think it one of the best, altho' one of the most wintersome things that I have done — I am disposed to flatter myself, but I expect to get very little praise [for] it. Lunched at the Studio and at 1.15 went to Fred Durand's — back at 2.30 — worked until about 6 — Home to tea at 7 — at 8.30 must to see Mary — visited with Miss Cor[son] at 10 walked home with [father?] — To bed at 11.15 — This morning Rec'd — a letter from Dr Squire in Phila Enclosing check for $30[0] for "Water Color," "Track of the Torrent["]
