## Left Page

### April Friday, 30 1869.

Clear, crisp day — m[illegible] after
writing note to Mr. G.P. Whitney
& rewriting note to Watson [illegible]
to studio abg. 15. Worked (3° d[ay])
on drawing of West Point, most
of my time being given to the
sky & distance — As the [illegible]
is soft and my washes cloud,
I have to stipple & hatch to
get tints flat. Miss Devry dr[ew]
in the m[orn]. Home to lunch
at 1.45 at 2.30 got to Phil. R[illegible]
at 4 walked with Belle & Mary
I ready to Knoedles — at 4.45
back to studio & worked on
drawing until 6.15. Home
to tea at 7 — Mother & Charlie[s]
Ret[urne]d from Uncle A's — Eve[ning]
Reading "Phantasmion"
by Sara Coleridge — got to
bed about 11 —

## Right Page

### May Saturday, 1 1869.

Miserable day — blustering, cold,
N.W. wind — Rain commenced about
o'clock a.m. & kept it up all day
night with slight exceptions —
Got to studio at 9.15 and spent
an hour kindling fire, sweeping
& clearing up — then to work (4° day)
on drawing of West Point — move
slowly & timidly much to my
disgust — I wonder if I could work
bolder & fuller of color — it is thin
weak from want of knowledge — I
[illegible] Home to lunch at
1.5 — back at 2.15 — worked until
near 6 — very dark — home at
7.15 — Eve[ning] I was to call on
Mrs. Sterne Morse at the Westminster
Hotel, but the storm was too much.
Spent the even[ing] Reading
"Phantasmion" by Sara Cole-
ridge — fell asleep in my
chair about 10 o'clk & got
to bed at 11 —
