## Left Page

### August Friday, 6 1869

Ther 56° about 6 oclk this mf.
After a good night of rest felt
very well this mf. & all ready
to begin biting & ruling over my
ausalle plate, which I did
at about 9 oclk (2d day) —
Father & Faid at home & worked
on George's plate. (23d day)
(2d day of Father's work.) My
biting to all appearances pro-
gressed satisfactorily until
late in the p.m. when the [illegible]
over the lake got all into holes.
on removing the ground, none
of it proved first rate — I am
completely upset & would
give anything to flee the whole
thing & find rest — I sometimes
lose faith & believe than an
inevitable ill luck attends me.
Did nothing during wef. but keep
up a miserable thinking — Letter
from Geo. this mf. — Mother &
Kate went riding with Mr. Norton
this p.m. — To bed at 10 —

## Right Page

### August Saturday, 7 1869

Still very unusually cool — Up at 6.30
and as soon as dressed, at my plate
(2d day.) The water on lake very badly
bitten in holes & dots. Set to work &
by 11 oclk had, by dragging heavily a
blunt dry-point through the lines, so
far restored it that it looks, on the
plate, tolerably well — Then commenced
burnishing — worked over the sky,
even stoning a part of it — At last
got the whole thing looking so
much better that I felt re-assured.
About 5 p.m. took hold of George's plate
& again stoned all over the sky —
Father worked an hour or more over
it before dark — The great solar eclipse
was a perfect success — Some few
clouds for a time threatened, but all
that was to be seen, we saw — We
went down to the Meady's — Belle and
Mary start with old Mr. Dunham
for the Adirondacks next Monday
well — I had hoped to go with
them — Home at 10.45 & to bed.
