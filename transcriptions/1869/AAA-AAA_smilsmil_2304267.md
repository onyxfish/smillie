## Left Page

### February Wednesday, 17 1869.

Changeable sort of a day, windin up with a beautiful night — Got up with a headache — Bound to fight it through, got to studio at 9.20 \+ set to work on 24×40, (3d day) Roughly painting in the R.H. foreground corner — But at noon was obliged to give it up and Rode home — just able to Rob into bed — for an hour on two in Real agony — sick \+ nervous — Up at 6.30 — took a quiet supper \+ felt better — Every Mr. \+ Mrs. D. \+ Knowlton called — Many. Matt \+ the Dr. in Jersey City. Father, Gre \+ I proved to be so interesting that Mr. \+ Mrs. K. stayed until 10.46  I got to bed about 11.30
Commenced snowing about midnight.

## Right Page

### February Thursday, 18 1869.

Snowing \+ threatening a bad day — but between 9 \+ 10 o'clk it cleared very bright \+ making a fearful slop underfoot — Got to studio about 9.30, feeling rather ragged after my headache of yesterday — Got to work painting on 24×40 — finishing up odds \+ ends in the first painting in — as I used but half a day yesterday \+ but half to-day this makes but the 3d day — Spent part of the Mng \+ most of the P.m. trying to draw a foot (plaster model) for Mr. Forbes — Home to dinner at 1 — \+ to tea at 6 — At 7.15 with Gre started for Brooklyn to call on Corinne Colman. Had some trouble in getting her address but got to the house at 8.25 — Spent a very pleasant eveg until 10 10 mths Misses Colman \+ Howard. Home at 11.30 \+ Right to bed.
