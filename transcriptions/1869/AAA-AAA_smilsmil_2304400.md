## Left Page

### November Sunday, 14 1869.

Weather rather mixed — M[orning] overcast — noon very pleasant — P.M. overcast & at night, on coming out of church, [it was] trying to snow & rain. — M[e] went to Dr Hastings & heard him preach one of his earnest sermons from 1st Cor. 1ch. 6v. Dinner at home — at 3 o'clk walked up to Central Park & about — home at 5.30 — Eve[ning] went to Madison ave. Baptist Ch. & heard Wayland Hoyt preach an anniversary [illegible] sermon (the 1st) of an ass[ocation] for the promotion of lay preaching. His text was Matt. 27th Ch. 51st v. & Acts 8th Ch. & 4th v. Home at 9.40 & to bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### November Monday, 15 1869.

Cloudy during the day — P.M. quite threatening — [illegible] morning right night — got to studio at 9.15. Built fire — Jack Kemp called — then Charley Adams — I felt very much under the weather — stiff & sore with cold, unable to work — At 11.30 went over to the Ch[urch] Ass[ociation] Bldg. to look after George's rooms. He has about made up his mind to abandon one. — Saw Eaton, Kuntz, Sarine, Tiffany, Tait, Wm Hart and Wyant. They went to see Mrs Rumy. Home to lunch at 1.40, back at 2.20 — Drew [a] outlined 12×22, Webster — a miserable lake — Home at 6 — after[noon] went to W. Buel, Jesus's — [illegible] for [half] an hour at Dickie's Riding School — then to the opening of Bogardus' new picture gallery. Saw a lot of fellows — Home at 9.40 — to bed at 10.30
