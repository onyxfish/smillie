## Left Page

### January, Monday 4, 1869

The worst day yet, muggy, foggy, slushy, drizzling, misgling
and miserable — Got to the
Studio about 10.30 — Alman
had been there before me and
taken away "Fat Cattle No. 30"
I worked on outline of large
drawing — Rode home to lunch
at 1.30 — back at 2.15 — worked
until 7 o'clk, finishing the
outline by gas light — then
Rode home — Every spent
Resting — got to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 5, 1869

as miserable weather as ever —
Rained hard during the m'f.
Went to Truedlie's, Snedecor's and
Schaus's to buy brushes, colors etc.
Bought a sable for which I paid
$3.00 — Back to studio at 11 + at
once commenced on drawing
by putting a smooth wash of
Yellow Ochre + Madder Brown all
over it — Sylvarnus + Elisha Mulford
called — Rode home to lunch at
1.30 — back at 2.15 — worked until
near 5 o'clk on the sky — got
in lots of crude color — am
determined to get it strong and
dashing if I can — Home at
6 — Until 8.30 copying a list
of names + addresses — to send
out our Reception invitations —
Rec'd pleasant note from Mr.
Enoch Sewrs — Ned Pitcher made
us a long call in the afternoon —
Mary has had her neck lanced in
a new place to-day — got to bed
about [illegible]
