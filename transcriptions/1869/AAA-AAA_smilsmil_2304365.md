## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 1 1869

Up about 7 after a good nights rest — weather, cold, cloudy \+ windy — so we all concluded to work in doors — \+ spent all the m'f putting into shape the sketches that I got yesterday — after lunch, the whole party went to a lidge of rocks about ½ a mile from the house, where me had a fine view of Ausable pass — I commenced a small W.C. sketch of it but was driven off by a shower at 4 — hurried home \+ finished it after a fashion by 6 o'clk. The girls rigged their hair up over birch bark cornucopias on their heads about 2 feet high, topped with gay flowers \+ leaves, looking \+ acting like wild during the evening, getting up a grand concert on combs — when everything from Italian tunes to [illegible] of opera was sung or played in the most Ridiculous manner — I thought I should burst at one time \+ tears Rolled down my cheeks — Mr. Dunham, Mr. \+ Mrs. C. and I played whist and all got to bed by 10 o'clk — Chase still here, complaining of trouble in bowels.

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 2 1869

Gloomy day — clouds low and no chance for work out of doors. — Spent the m'f making over sketches — writing to father \+ Receiving the calls of Mr. \+ Mrs. Baker, Miss Elliott, a ypt lady — staying at Min Bedee's \+ painting — Lunch at 12 clk \+ about 2, as the clouds had lifted somewhat, Took water color traps \+ large board \+ went to the River near Hin's \+ spent the Rest of the p.m. outlining subject, so that I may be Ready to go at it ever does clear off — The study of the cascade \+ large boulders — at 5, packed up, feeling chilly \+ damp and started for home. Dinner about 6.30 — evelf quite quiet one as compared with last night — Got to bed at 10.30 — Rec. a couple of papers from Mr. Faulkner. [illegible — ~4 words] [illegible — ~3 words]
