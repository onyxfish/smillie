## Left Page

### December Saturday, 18 1869

Another miserable mild day of wind & Rain — got to Studio at 9.15 — wind driving down my chimney filled the room so with coal gas that at 11 o'clk I gave up, too dark to paint also, and went to Knoedlers. Then called on Sandf'd Gifford. Saw his Eastern Sketches. Then at Hays — McEntee, Whittredge, Jamt Thompson & Shattuck — Guy. De Haas, J. Scott Griswold & Lambdin nabim — Rode home at 1 & went to bed with a very severe nervous headache — slept until about 5 & at 5.30 got up feeling much better — very fine moonlight — went to Fleming's & to Rumygraf's — Read from 10 until 12.30 — then bathed my feet & got to bed shortly after 1 —

## Right Page

### December Sunday, 19 1869

Fine day, cold & breezy — My f ment to Dr Hastings & heard him preach from 27th Psalm — 1st v — dwelling particularly upon the need of a Stay & Trust in the Almighty — after service — Charl & I went up to Uncle A's — dinner — and enjoyed the beautiful sky effects. at 4.30 started for home. I got out of 8th ave Car & 80th St & walked home, getting in a little before 6 — Tea & at 7.30 at J. P. Thompson's. His text the words of the Prodigal in his return to his father & from it drawing the lesson of true Repentance & necessary frame of mind. Mrs Gart met & Adams here walked with them to 5th ave. R[ode] — Stopped at Ch. of the Messiah, 34th St — 4th ave — packed — heard close & sermon & "Shining Shore" grandly sung by the immense congregation — then to Studio & got dose of Mica for stove door — Home at 10.15. Ollie Streedy there — I got to bed about 11.20
