## Left Page

### December Sunday, 26 1869

Dismal Rainy day — Did not get up until 10 o'clk —
Did not go to Church, but spent the mrg. lounging.
Dinner about 1 o'clk — the afternoon spent much
after the manner of the mrg., did some little
Reading, but hardly of what might be called a
profitable sort or "Sunday Reading" — I looked over a
large portion of Aldrich's "Story of a Bad Boy" and was
much amused. — Eve'g went to D. P. Thompson's &
heard another Prodigal Son Sermon. — His treatment
of God's Love taking the portion when the Father saw his son while
yet afar off & ran & fell on his neck & kissed him — After
service, home — Aunt Margaret visiting — near 11 o'clk again
when I got to bed —

## Right Page

### December Monday, 27 1869

Dark, damp & mild, at night a foggy drizzle — Got to studio
at 5.40 — built fire & soon got to burnishing on "Continental" tint
plate, worked on it until about [illegible] getting it to look on the plate
my beautiful — then laid ground
traced & transferred little Temple in centre — varnished about [illegible] &
at 1.15 home to lunch, back at 2.20 & at work etching Temple —
managed to get it etched and necessary "stopping out" before it
got to be quite dark — Fixed my
wall & got every thing Ready to
"bite" the first thing tomorrow mrg —
home to tea at 5 — Eve'g engaged
scouring & tailoring my from old
overcoat & trying again to get
a sketch to please W. Frank
Scott — Gee & Samuels came
called upon me for a few
minutes — After writing
to Scott, I got to bed about
1.30
