## Left Page

### Sept. Monday, 27 1869.

Cold, blustering day with squalls at intervals. Traces of snow on the ledges of the mountains. Spent the morning working on Ausable Gorge drawing, fixing up bits that needed finish etc. Lunch at 1 and after worked over the sky of Mt. studies at the tops of Giant's leap. about 5.p.m. started off for Ausable Gorge \+ was much impressed with the effect of the great volume of water rushing through there being now quite a freshet on way home met miss Elliott \+ Kt? with her to Phin Bideis then home at 6,30 to dinner. Eve'g spent in chatting at 9 30 made up hot a [illegible] Root with wine \+ to bed somewhere after 10.

## Right Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 28 1869.

Clear cold \+ windy day. At 9.30 a.m. started with Coleman for the top of Camel's Humps \- went into the woods back of Phin Bideis. at first very easy climbing, but after a time got into low scrubby growth of Birch \+ evergreens. very heavy wet moss all over the tops of Mt. frozen stiff \- found icicles 3 feet long \+ 2 in thick \- the tops of high mts about white with snow \- after very hard work. Reached the top at 1 o'clk \- wind blowing a young hurricane very cold \- hard work to get a bite but got one at last \+ cooked our lunch \- with much difficulty got a pencil sketch of Gothic Range \- the views very grand \- the gothics especially \+ to the S.E. over a great wilderness. At 4 nearly frozen, started down got to the house at 6.15 \- on the way [illegible] gave me a "talking to" for my "assurance", as he called it, for taking lead in the woods \- He claims to be oldest \+ have most experience.
