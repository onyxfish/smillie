## Left Page

### August, Sunday 22, 1869

Superb morning - almost too good to last - Bkfst. late at 9.15 - Myself, Mr. Dunham - Read Episcopal service \+ a sermon by H\. F. Ward Beecher "The problem of Joy \+ Suffering in Life" getting through a little before 12. - We took a cup of Beef tea \+ all started off for a walk - went up to Smith Bedes, over to Phin Beckes through a beautiful wood \+ down home to dinner at 5 o'clt. - After dinner greased boots \+ packed traps to go into camp to-morrow - Evenf. from 8 to 10 - Singing hymns. - Beautiful moonlight night - got to bed about 10.45.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 23, 1869

*In Camp - Upper Ausable - Adirondacks -*

Superb day. Breakfasted \+ Ready to start before 8 o'clt - when Holts team has to be at the house - It did not come until near 9 - we all got off in short order \+ had a glorious tramp through the woods, altho' the Road was very muddy, to the lower lake, which we reached, about 12.15 - There met Mr. Shurtliffe who has a hut near the old dam - we went to his spring, got out our lunches, he made a pot of coffee, we drank it up \+ gave him 3 cheers \+ a tiger \+ went on - the Row over the lower lake very pleasant and heavy loading we got over the carry with all our luggage in one trip, getting to the upper lake about 3 o'clt and at last, all into camp about 4 o'clt. Some time spent getting fresh boughs of spruce \+ hemlock for beds and then a last dinner for which we were all prepared - Evenf. sitting about a roaring big camp fire singing about 9 o'clt Mr \+ Mrs Coleman, Misses Tiffany \+ Terrence medy went out on the lake. The Moonrise was superb beyond anything that ever I saw, and long to be remembered \- water absolutely still and reflection perfect - we all got to bed about 11 and slept through the night tolerably -
