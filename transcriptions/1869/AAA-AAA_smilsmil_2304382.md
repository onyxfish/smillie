## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 5, 1869

*[written at top of page:]* at the Troy House - Troy. Room 49 —

Up at 7 after a good nights rest, but with a very uncomfortable headache —
It has rained heavily all night &
is raining to day — The water in the
er & things look blacker than ever —
Took a cup of tea & kept quiet for
a while — feeling better walked about
& kept my interest up. Borke[?]
away before noon (the weather) & think
Mr. Veeder went up on some high ground
East of town & saw the flooded valley
stretching for miles — after dinner
a new excitement about a train
down — a train did come up from Port
Edward about 4 o'clk & at 4.30 We
me all started down — The fields for
miles all afloat, in many places
the canal tow path & all, entirely
outmerged — In places where the
water had subsided from potatoes[?]
the potatoes all unearthed. And over
Cornfields covered with water, pumps[?]
floating merrily. Passed cautiously
over the numerous breaks newly put[?]
got to Saratoga almost on time —
There made up new train & proceeded
very cautiously until 9 o'clk when
me got into Troy — could get no farther
So the whole party, 15 of us came
to this house — having a jolly time —
Supper at 10 o'clk — The Albany
& so Quebanna Road so badly
broken I am told that it will take
a week to repair — It seems that
I must go to N.Y.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 6, 1869

Up at 6.30 to take 7.30 boat down to
Albany, but no boat went, docks all under
water — Party broke up together, I bid Veeder
good-bye & the rest of us went to depot &
took 8 o'clk Train, getting to Albany at
10 — Day-boat "Vibbard" not yet gone —
all excitement & confusion — a rush
the boat — docks & adjacent streets
all under water — Temporary bridge
to boat — great crowd, so that I felt
nervous — at last, a few minutes
before 10 got started — glorious day,
clear & breezy almost cold — went
drawing along like smoke, when near
Hudson, broke Rudder Chain & drifted
roadside down the River, at one time
almost on shore — great times getting
small boat off upper deck — detained
about ½ an hour — then on again —
enjoyed sail, but had a miserable
expensive dinner — got to 34.[?] —
about 6.30 — D. Noel & wife went on. Mrs.
Gallagher met her husband on the dock.
Stone & Fuller took 9 ave cars & I walked
home — Matt, Charl, Geo. Will & Joe. The
family — George very miserable —
After tea, spent the eve, chatting and
at 10.30 got to bed —
