## Left Page

### February Sunday, 21 1869.

Still disagreeable — went to the Mad. Ave. Bp'n Ch. — heard Rev. Mr. Bordman of Phila — I was interested as we crossed to Liverpool in the same steamer July 1862 — I had a few pleasant words with him after service — He preached from the words "If any me love not Christ, let him be anathema — Maranatha."
He seemed to give very general satisfaction —
walked home at noon with father — and dined — about 4 o'clk P.m. — in a miserable wet mizzle, walked up to Central Park, Returning at tea time
Eve'g did not go out, wrote notes to T.F. Reed Boston. M. Gee Whitney Phila \+ a letter to Dr. Lewis Phila.

## Right Page

### February Monday, 22 1869.

Still miserable weather — damps and mizzling until night when it rained right down — Got to Studio at 9.20 — Commenced painting on 12×22 "Lower Ausable Lake" (2d day) Miss Devry came about 10 o'clk. Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.15 — worked until about 4 when I finished my sky. Home to tea at 6.30 — at 8 o'clk went with Geo to the Rogers' 106 W. 42d St. Spent a chatty eve'g with Mr. Mary Griffits \+ two of her Sisters — Home at 10.20 — got to bed shortly after 11.
