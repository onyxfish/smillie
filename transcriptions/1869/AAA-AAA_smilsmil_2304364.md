## Left Page

### August, Monday, 30, 1869

*Shurtliffe's Camp —*

Up at 5.30 and much disappoin[ted]
to find it heavily clouded over
and little promise of a good
at. Early breakfast & ready to
start in for camp at 7 oclk.
but it commenced raining
wind from N.W. Coleman, G[illegible]
& I waited until 9 o'clk when
things looked more promising
& with Melville we all started
in for camp — we took the
walk easily, sketching by the
way & getting in about 12 o'clk.
Had lunch, & then as it was all
clouded over again with no pros[pect]
of sunlight for work — Coleman &
I took boat, went to the foot of
me the rock walls on the North
side of the lake & spent the p[.m.]
climbing & sketching — Several
showers, but none wetting until
on our way back at 6 — when it rain[ed]
heavily — Every more rain and
wild wind — until about 9 o'clk
lying in our shanty, chattin[g] —
Then turned in — almost a gale all ni[ght]
the night & sound of wind very imp[ressive]

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday, 31, 1869

Up at 4+ to make fires. Very cold — weather
unpromising — showers & wind. G[illegible]
started for Potters immediately after
[illegible] and C. & I, taking the boat,
pulled up under "the Pulpit," anchored
sketched, while it rained — then we
landed & climbed up a very wild &
dangerous place to a narrow ledge
on the great Gothic Wall, where, in
another shower we took sketches
looking to the N. & S. — very grand
subjects to my mind — Near 1 oclk
we descended to our boat. I was
thankful to get down without break
ing my neck — but dis may ed to
find that our boat had got loose &
floated away — after a horrible toil
along shore, over rocks & debris of all
sorts for about ½ a mile, we espied
it lodged among some dead wood — took
luckily in another shower & at 2.20
pulled across the lake to Indian
Head & climbed up to under the
cliff — disappointed in the view —
Down & back to camp at 3.45. Found
[illegible]'s horse waiting. — Slung our traps
on him & at 4.15, in another shower
started for Potters, where we arrived
at 6.30. Feeling tired after all my
days work, trudging up with 5½
miles through the woods —
To bed at 9. Charl did not get home
