## Left Page

### June Monday, 29 1874.

Intensely hot day — Bkfs. at 7.30.
About 8.30. went to studio and
fussed there most of the morning.
Downtown & around just before
dinner, which we had some
after 1 o'clk. — Until 4.30
with Cotrill in studio fitting
big sash — A very violent
storm of wind & rain & a very
little thunder & lightning passed
about 3 o'clk. Again down-
town & back for tea at 6 —
Every gossiping with the young
people in the house — Thru down
the hill — back — more chaff
until 10 o'clk — then to bed
at 10.30.

## Right Page

### June Tuesday, 30 1874.

Glorious day — clean and cool —
Bkfs. at 7.30 — After that, went
down town to the mill & around
trying to coax trinke etc. etc.
until 12 o'clk — then up to studio
finished measurements — before
dinner at 1.20 — Sat chatting
until 3 o'clk — then gave up to
heaviness & spent one hour
in the bed & accomplished
nothing before tea at 6 o'clk.
So, my day has not been
well spent — After tea, with
trinke, turned over all [illegible]
oak lumber, sorting [illegible]
send to the mill to-morrow.
Did not get through with
him until 9 o'clk, & after-
ward very cold — then went
into Mr. Dud's — watched the
girls play back gammon
until 10 o'clk — then to bed —
Got a letter from Father.
