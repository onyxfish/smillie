## Left Page

### March Saturday, 21 1874

Up late. For a wonder, without a headache — but it was about 11 when I got to studio. To work on Marquette Lake, glazed my boats furiously & pitched in — made a great change in general appearance & for the better. Home to lunch late and near three when I ret'd —
Painted until 5 o'clk — Mr & Mrs. Browning & Miss Shattuck called — Then a Mr. Munns with a note of introduction from [illegible]. Home to tea at 7 o'clk — at 8.30 at P. Aveni's — all out but the son — in at 9 & then I stayed until after 11 — we were all so agreeable — Home & to bed about midnight.
Belle Tweedy at Studio this P.M. with long letter from Geo.

## Right Page

### March Sunday, 22 1874

Windy, but a beautiful day. Up late & did not get to Dr. Hastings's until 11.40. After service rode up with Mrs. & Josie Jessup, to 5th Ave. & 59th St. Then walked the rest of the way with them to 78th St. & Madison Ave. — resting for a few minutes — Walked through the park to 6th Ave. & took cars home. Dinner at 1.45.
Headache growing much worse all the P.M. & lay on sofa until 11.30 when I went to bed.
