## Left Page

### July Wednesday, 29 1874.

More disagreeable weather — Got up with a headache. After [illegible] went down town. Kit? — With Cotrell's man & got out oak [illegible] door panels — then down again [illegible] C — at work on door. At 12.30 at home & lay down in bed until after 1 o'clk — then bid Mr. Iyler good-bye — She went away on a visit — 1.45 when I got dinner — after 3 o'clk went to "Republican" office & read [illegible] of Mormon letter then again to Cotrell's — then to mill & ordered moulding — home to tea at 6.15 — Weather cleared — Welf, called on Miss Blackman about Joe Smieth [illegible] material — then to Fan M's — Whist till 10 — home with Gus S — to bed at 11.15 Glorious moonlight & very cool.

## Right Page

### July Thursday, 30 1874.

Glorious day — Ther. 60° at 6. a.m. — [illegible] near 8 o'clk — Welf spent in Studio with Elisha Mulford — down at Cotrell's shop — at the moulding mill — to — Riding [illegible] old horse, that won't do for George. At noon went out with Hy Syler over his lot to see if there was a bit I could buy. Dinner at 1.30 — all the P.M. spent tacking painted muslin on the wall, stretching more & priming it — George helping me — Tea at 6 — a tea-fight under way at W.J.'s — not invited — after tea, again at work in studio — then until 8 o'clk — Welf in Mrs. Dud's parlor until 10 o'clk — then home & to-bed —
