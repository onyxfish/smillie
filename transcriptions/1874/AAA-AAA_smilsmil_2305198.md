## Left Page

### March Saturday, 7 1874

Horrible storm of snow & sleet — went to N.A. & D. to see Brown to try to straighten out the business of settling sales. Then went over to Tiffany's & to [illegible] got to studio about 11 o'clk & found Mrs. Munson daughter, & niece waiting for me — they took an hour of my time & lots of advice. Then Falconer came in & the took another hour — at 1.30 I took lunch — Had hardly got to work on Aldine Almanac block before Darley came in — there until near 4 — then Jas. Hart — then I got Miss Woodward to sit for me while I drew a figure — then Ferguson came in — I abt I lighted gas & put new glass on "Old Birch Study" for Mr. Whitney — Home to tea at 7.45 — Many had been to see B.C. & I determined to call also, although it was late. Hurried through tea & at 8.45 got to the Walsh's — 80 Clinton P[lace] — a pleasant, old fashioned ch[at] — left at 10.30 — to bed at 11.45 —

## Right Page

### March Sunday, 8 1874

Very windy & snow-squally — [illegible] went to Dr. Hastings. Heard him preach from St. John. ch. 29. V — After service walked up to 59th St. & 5th ave. with Allie Diven. Then home to dinner — after that, a nap. Then wrote to Bunce & to [illegible]. Through at 5.30 — rode up to Jessup's — Madison ave. & 78th St — Took tea there & spent the evening until 10 o'clk. Walked down with young Watson. Home at 10.30 & to bed at 11.30
