## Left Page

### October Wednesday, 7 1874.

No rain yet, altho' I certainly expected it. Worked all the mng & at last waded through my muss. Dinner at 12.30. Worked awhile on George's packing & at 3.15 went to studio to meet Jas. Wright on appointment made by Will. He came about 4 & talked about business, but it amounted to nothing for the present. Ferguson came in & agreed to pay $25. p. month for a share in my studio — at 5 rode to Hayek's — 44th S. & 10th Ave. — Caught in the rain. To tea at D's soon after 6 — At 8. went over to the house & until 10 taking down my bookcases. To D's at 10.25 & to bed right away —

## Right Page

### October Thursday, 8 1874.

Raining very lightly when I got up. At 8 o'clk. at the house. R. did not come until near 9 & then arranged to come later if the rain held up — At 10.30 he came with a two-horse covered truck & 3 men. We hustled things & by 11.30 had the first load up in studio. At 12.20 had lunch at D's & at 2.30 my second load was up in studio. One little spatter of rain came but no damage was done. All the p.m. until 6 o'clk. I was trying to arrange the chaos of confusion — then to dinner, thoroughly tired out. Rain commenced falling heavily between 4 & 5 & continued until 10 — At 8 Charl came for me & until 11 o'clk we were strapping & marking boxes & packing George's traps — Then I went to D's & to bed at 11.30. But it was after midnight before I got to sleep. I was so tired.
