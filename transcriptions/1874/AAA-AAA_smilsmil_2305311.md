## Left Page

### October Sunday, 25 1874

Dull m[or]n. until afternoon then cleared to the same charming weather that we have been having for some days past. B[rea]k[fas]t at 8.15 after kindling a fire in studio wrote to Nicole. Then to Episcopal Ch. Heard a sermon from the pastor that made me thinks more of him "why is the house of the Lord forsaken?!" After service met S. & walked with her until 1.30. Dinner at 2. mote all the afternoon until 4.30. At 5, took Whiskey Stamp drawings to the S[ub]. p[ost] office. Tea at 6. Every mote letters in studio until 9 o'clk. getting [illegible] finished. Then in to W. [illegible] until 10 [illegible] then S. walked down to the P.O. with me. To bed at 11. Glorious night.

## Right Page

### October Monday, 26 1874

Very mild foggy m[or]n. At work arranging & packing. Carried all my traps into W—[illegible]'s house so that Krink may move my building whenever he pleases. Dinner at 1.15. About 3 it had cleared to be the loveliest of days when S. & I went up into the fair grounds. We loitered, sauntered & lounged until almost sunset. It was very delicious. In soon after 5 & worked until 6. Then tea. Played a couple of games of whist. then into Krink's — the most vexatious & tantalizing fellow in the world. I am trying to arrange with him to move my building. I did not get rid of him until near 9. Then to P.O. Then back to W.[illegible]. There until 10. Then to bed at 10.30.
