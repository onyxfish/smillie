## Left Page

### August, Thursday, 20, 1874

Very hot day — ther 90° —
Catrell at studio until 11 o'clk
then through with his work
& I got everything cleared
out. Went down the hill at
1 o'clk — Dinner soon after.
Got pkg. from Will with
materials for new drawings —
Got out my curtains & hung
them — moved table & got
things ready to work — The Mrs.
Boggs visited the Room. After
an early tea, with Gratz and
Gee. Rode out with Misses Gratz,
Lily Jessup, Saulsburn & Carrie
Boggs. In at about 8 o'clk —
made letters to Will & to Father
— After 9, went into Fan's — Had
two games of whist — Home &
to bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### August, Friday, 21, 1874

Extremely hot & dry. Between
my horse, a bath and sheer
weariness, most of the morning
was lost — The Boggs's left for
home on short notice at 1 o'clk.
We had dinner at 12 — At 1½
I went down the hill & got
muslin upon which I painted a long Programme affair
for Isaac Relay to use at his
"Reading" to-night at the Widow
Jessup's. I did not get through
with it until after 5 o'clk —
About 6, a brisk wind storm came
up with some rain — At 8 — all
went to Mr. J's where Isaac R.
gave his Hohenlinden Readings
in aid of Syrian Sufferers —
Tickets 50 ¢ — Parlors well filled.
Out at 10 & went down to Fan's —
a jolly time there until 11 — 
then home & to bed — Very
heavy rain during the night.
