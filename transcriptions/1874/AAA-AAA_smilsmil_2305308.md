## Left Page

### October, Monday 19, 1874

Very cold and gloomy m[orning] — another touch of gloomy [weather] —
but it cleared again before noon, very breezy & very bright.
Got to Studio at 9 o'clk, but it
was quite 10 before I started
on "A bit in MacMillan's Woods
North Conway. Sue H. came
but I begged her to give me
grace, as I had got fairly into
my painting work. Dinner at
1.20 — Painted until after [1]–
30 — About 5. went over to [Mollie]
Sayre's for [?] — then we walked
to the tops of hill west of town &
enjoyed splendid sunset view — went
across lots & out on Brewster Hills,
thence home by the road. — Tea
at 6.30 at H[?] Tyler's — there
until 8.30 — then down town for mail
— met Ben M. & with him went to
Gus S.'s — the Lathrops, girls & two or three
young fellows there — Whist until [?].
To bed at 10.40.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 20, 1874

Delicious day — and ideal Oct.
day. — 10 o'clk. before I got to my
painting on "wood scene". A bit
until Sue came — then I worked
upon her from 11.30 until 1.30 —
then both went to dinner at
Mr. Dud's. At my work again
painting at 2.30 & kept at it until
4.30 when S. came and we
walked out beyond the fairground
over Warner's hill & across lots to
Brewster hills road & then home.
Very lovely, sad & mied twilight,
moonlight merging into sunset.
In at 6.15 — tea — & at 8 again
went out with S. — to call on Kitty
Bentley, Miss Brown there. Out
again at 9. Charming moonlight —
walked almost out to Warner's,
then in. — I left at 10 o'clk and
to bed at 10.30
