## Left Page

### June, Thursday, 25, 1874

Clear + very warm, but delightful
breeze blowing. Went to [illegible] office
\+ bought tickets — arranged for baggage
\+ boxes at studio — got iron strap
\+ at studio's at 10. Calve very
helped me fix boxes. Got
them off at 1 o'clk. Bid my
good byes + went down B[road]
way + changed drawers, bought
yesterday. Thru to Stewart's —
then home to lunch at 2 o'clk.
All the P. M. + eve[ning] cleaning
out closets + packing trunk.
Jea at 6.30 — Uncle A, Emma
in — got through at 10 o'clk
\+ to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### June, Friday, 26, 1874

*Montrose.*

Up at 6 o'clk. M[orning] very close, muggy
\+ overcast. B[rea]k[fas]t at 6 3/4 + at 7.10 left
home after completing our good byes.
Got to foot of Christopher St. at 7.50. Crossed
\+ had to wait for nearly half an hour
before we could get in to our train.
Off at last at 8.30. Soon clear weather
but very warm. On reaching top of
Pocono Mt., delightful change — after
leaving Scranton, met our first shower
— passed through two or three before
reaching Montrose Station at 3.30.
Started off in stage, full, at 4 o'clk
\+ had two more showers before reaching
town at 6.15. It was after 7 before
we got into quarters at H[illegible] Tyler's — were
washed + at tea in M[illegible] Lathrop's —
Eve[ning] chatting with boarders, then
over to Studio's, a short call on Anna
H. — then down-town — Saw Fan M.
\+ a lot of other fellows — then home
to bed a few minutes after 10.
[illegible] got through showing very
little fatigue.
