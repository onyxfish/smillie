## Left Page

### July, Thursday 23, 1874

Another glorious day — after a restless night, got up sore and lame. Worked for a couple of hours in studio siruing the piece of muslin that I have tacked upon the wall, then worked on Florida arms until 12 o'clk. Then saddled "Major" & rode him for ¾ of an hour. He stumbled again two or three times. Dinner at 1 o'clk. After that, gave up to my sleeping & napped until near 4 o'clk — then finding that my experiment on studio wall was a failure I spent more than an hour soaking the muslin off. Tea at 6 — about 7 out again on Major & rode until 8 o'clk — washed and dressed — spent an hour in Mrs. Dud's parlor — To bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### July, Friday 24, 1874

Delightful day. Spent an hour after [illegible] with W. J. S. burning into caterpillars & then worked until 12 o'clk finishing Florida arms — Then went out on "Major" for a walk — my neck very stiff & the pain at the base of [illegible] very great — every jar hurts it — so did not enjoy my ride. Dinner soon after 1 o'clk. The pain in head increased so that I went to bed & went down town about 5.30 — Tea at 6.15 — After that prepared drawings in pkg. Went to Eyp. with them, wrote letters to Van Zandt, Will & Charles. Then from 10.30 until 11.45 read news papers — Got to bed about midnight.

Joe Mrs got up pic-nic in Salt Spring to-day — I could not go —
