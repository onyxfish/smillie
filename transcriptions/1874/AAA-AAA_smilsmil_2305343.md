## Left Page

### December, Monday, 28, 1874

Rain and fog. B'kf. at 8.30 —
Disinclined to walk in the
mth. Got to work finishing
up an old pencil study com-
menced at Lexington, Catskill
Mts. in 1860 — Dinner at 1 o'clk.
Afternoon spent outlining on
"Idle Hours". About 5 o'clk. I put
on my big boots & tramped
out in the mud — went down
the South Road thro out Academy
St — then into town — back to
tea at 6 — Evef. at 8 o'clk. with
Geo. & Mary, went in & called
upon our next-door neighbors,
Mr. & Mrs. Saml. Thorne — Mrs. T.
very chatty — Mr. S. very full but
not unconscious. Home at 9
& to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday, 29, 1874

Black, blowy and overcast.
Started with color on "Idle
Hours" — not very successful in
my beginning. At 12.30, walked
in town & got piece of bluestone
that I had ordered for Father
last night. Dinner at 1 o'clk.
Afternoon, worked as long as
I could see on drawing — at
5 o'clk. I walked in town with
Kate. I took a pkg. with decs,
down to the "Miller" Steamboat,
for Father — Back to tea at 6 —
Evef. Mr. Halen a draughtsman
"Hug" called & spent the evef
until 9.30 — I amused myself
for awhile with one of Verné's
books & got to bed about 11.
