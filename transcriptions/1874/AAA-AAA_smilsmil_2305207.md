## Left Page

### March Wednesday, 25 1874.

Very fine day, but still windy.
Up late & did not get to
studio until 9.45 — Then
to work on Raquette. Painting
over the water — Home to
lunch at 1.45 — back at
2.30 — Picture much im-
proved, but I'm in a
regular mess from want
of time — can't get the color
I want. Uncle A — J. Graydon
Johnston — Mother & Willie, at
the studio. Home to tea at
7.20 — Kate Ret[?] from
Montreal, after an absence
of 9 weeks. Evening spent
fussing & lounging — To bed
at 10.15

## Right Page

### March Thursday, 26 1874.

Overcast & mild — slight showers
about the middle of the day —
Got to studio at 9.30 & to work
on Raquette. Most of the a.m.
working with model on figures
in boats. Home to lunch at
1.30 — back at 2.30 & spent
the p.m. fussing — Allie
D. & Josie J. came in for a
few minutes — also W. O'Kelley
of the Herald. Home to tea
at 7.30 & spent the eveng
doing absolutely nothing —
To bed at 10.30.
