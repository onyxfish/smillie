## Left Page

### January Friday, 30 1874.

Up late, overcast day — but not unpleasant. After some writing, went to N.A. of D — then to Giffords Rooms saw G., Tiffany & Nicoll. Rec'd a very irate letter from Mr. G. A. Gilbert. His drawings are hung in bad places. Home to lunch at 1.15 — at 2. at Phil Rehearsal — after that, went to Century Club to see about George's dues. Then again to Y.M.C.A. to see the fellows on W.C. business. Home to tea at 6 o'clk — Ever, more writing & got to N.A. of D. at 9.30 & helped James arrange his accts. Home at 11 & to bed at 11.45.

## Right Page

### January Saturday, 31 1874.

Overcast m'g but pleasant p.m. & for'n's night. Did not get to studio until after 10 — then man from the "Graphic" came to get permission to use his camera in the galleries — then Dr. Mulford came — got fire started & cleared up somewhat. Home to lunch at 2 — Some more writing & back at 3.15 — Panton came in — then Mrs. Jessup — Josie & sister in law — more cleaning up — Home to tea at 6.30 — Ever't, went to N.A. of D. at 9.30 — arranged accts. At 10 at Metropolitan Museum of Art in 14th St. There until 11 o'clk. Home with the [illegible]. Home at 11.30 — to bed at 12 [illegible]. Father very much under the weather.
