## Left Page

### July Monday, 27 1874.

Overcast m[orn], most of the
m[orn] spent getting up a frame
upon which to stretch the
muslin covering for my walls —
Catrill's man came up with it
& nailed it up. Miss V. Boggs
& others paid me a visit — Dinn[er]
at 1.30 — P.M. — almost a clear
[off] — My stiff neck & back ha[ve]
made me feel very uncomforta[ble]
all day — The P.M. spent clearing
floor & piling lumber out of the
way that Catrill may finish
the floor — Tea at 6 — [illegible] went
downtown. Fan M. Rett[?] from
Saratoga, there until 9 o'clk —
then up on the hill — awhile at
Azur[?] & [illegible]'s — Home soon aft[er]
10 & read Beecher-Tilton until
near 12 o'clk — Then to bed—

## Right Page

### July Tuesday, 28 1874.

Rain, very spiteful, all day
long with short intermissions —
Cotrell & boy at studio until 11
planing over floor — I re-stretched
muslin that I sized yesterday
[?] — I mixed colors to paint it
Dinner at 1.30 — Afternoon
Austin oiled the floor and I
finally got color so that I
painted the muslin, but
it did not dry so that I might
know how I had succeeded —
Tea about 6.20 — Again in
studio, cleaning up paint-pots —
went down town at 8.30 and
after going for the mail, I
stopped at Fan's — Played
whist until near 11 — Home
& to bed at 11.30
