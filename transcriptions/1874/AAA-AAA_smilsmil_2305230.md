## Left Page

### May, Sunday, 10, 1874

Up between 5 & 6 with a sort of Cholic — the songs of birds
hardly repaid for the dis-
comfort. Back to bed & got
to sleep — Up to bkfst at 8 —
Hotter than ever — Felt
so slimpsy that I did not
go into church — Wandered
over the place a little — very
little. Dinner at 1 — until
4 o'clk, in library window —
Then dragged about for an
hour — Tea at 6 and again
the very until 9 was spent
out of doors — Then in —
got to bed about 11 —
Ther. as high as 93° in
the shade.

## Right Page

### May, Monday, 11, 1874

Very much cooler — 48° at 6
a.m. Bkfst. between 7 & 8 o'clk.
Near 10 o'clk. Uncle W. & I started
into town. Got to depot just as
the train came in with Mother &
Charl. Then with them we drove
about, looking at houses, winding
up with the Eastman house — got
to Parkers at about 1 o'clk & had
Dinner — at 2.30 Charl, Ed, Matt,
Mother & I drove to the Buckingham
place & went over the house & grounds.
I walked back alone — went to depot
& telegraphed for Drawing Room Seats.
Then up & called on Carrie Fonda —
Notea at 6 o'clk — & at 7.30, Mother,
Uncle W., Charl & I started for
home where we arr'd. at 10.10 P.M.
Much talk of house & to bed
near midnight.
