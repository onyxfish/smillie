## Left Page

### August Friday, 28 1874.

glorious m[orning] — could not
resist the temptation, so I
rode over to Hart Lake with
Ben M. Grnsie L — Mr. Wm M.
Miss Walbridge & Miss Sellmy —
There is an encampment of
[illegible] U.S. soldiers there — Back
at noon — wrote a letter to Will
& mailed it at 1 o'clk. Dinner
at 1.30 — a long chat after
dinner with Miss Gratz — on
to studio at 2.45 — my head
troubling me & very much
confused — Did no work worth
mentioning — At 4, gave up
trying — went to stable — out
with Geo. at 5 — a good ride
& in at 6 — Tea — then for an
hour at Mr. Wm Cooper's — then
wrote a letter to Father —
at 9 o'clk. at Fan M's — had
a romp there — home at
11 & to bed soon after —

## Right Page

### August Saturday, 29 1874.

Very pleasant m[orning]. Soon after
when I got to studio & set to
work on arms for a Santa Fé [illegible]
Dinner soon after 12 o'clk. — at
back to studio Mr. Gratz & son
daughter left at 1 for Phila &
[illegible] Mrs. Taylor went to Scranton
at 12.30 — back to studio at 1 —
Cora L. Miss Gilbert & Geo. came
in between 2 & 3 o'clk & were
detained by a shower — Young
Riley called later — I finished
drawing soon after 4 — Then went
to stable — Geo. & I rode until
6.30 — Tea about 7 — Made a call
at the Riley's — left at 9.15 — met
the Mrs. Gns L. the Stronds and
Jessups all blacked & dressed —
went to Fan M's & to Mrs. Sear's
with them — had a regular lark
ab't 9 — again to Fan's and played
whist until near 11 — then home
& to bed —
