## Left Page

### May, Friday, 8, 1874.

Pok eepsie —

Up about [illegible] feeling exceedingly
shaky. Went up to Mrs. Parkers
& had B'kf't soon after 8 —
About 9 Father, Ed. & I started
out. Went to Meinerts & Corlies & got
a list of houses, met there old Mr.
Corlies & went with him to So.
Liberty St. & to Carroll, Hamilton
& other sts — looking at houses
& lots. Then down to the Eastman
houses — delighted with them —
At 1.30 got back to Mrs. P's, very
tired & hungry — Dinner —
Then a nap — Between 3 &
4, Father, Ed. & I again started
out & went to the northern part
of town. Disappointed — Back
at 5 — got open carriage & driving
matt — again we drove to the
Colton, Eastman, Corlies and
King houses — Back at 6.35 —
Tea & Father started for home
in the 7.35 Train — Met
Emma Bourne — She called
at the house — Spent the
even't in sleepy talk & yawning —
To bed at 10.30. Very cool
& threatening Rain, but it cleared
very pleasantly.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday, 9, 1874.

Extremely close, hot, windy dirty
day. Awake early & enjoyed hearing
the birds sing — B'kf't at 7 — at 8.30
Ed. went with me to Blast Furnace
& here I sketched until near noon
getting material for drawing for
A.B.N. Co. Then went for St. &
we walked up town together.
After dinner very much used up
by the weather — made notes to
Jan M. & Hy. Tyler & worked over
sketches of the mf., About 3 o'clk
went out & took a walk. Tea
at 6 — At 7.30 Uncle Willie's man
with horse & wagon came for me
& I went to Hillside — Oppressively
warm — we sat outside & chatted
until near 10 o'clk — Got to bed
about 10.30
