## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 18, 1874

Very warm. All day with C. finishing the many little
things that have to be done
& yet takes time. Went down
town, — got a lot of money [illegible]
from the mill — Then called at [illegible]
fan M's & bid Miss Smith
good bye. Dinner at 1 o clk.
Party drove over to Salt Spring —
I worked in Studio until
near 5 — then saddled "Tom" &
I rode out about 4 miles &
met the Salt Spring party [illegible]
turning — In at 7 — Tea at 7:30
— went down town at 8.30 —
about 9.30 called at Mr. Cross-
man's & bid the Deals good
bye. To bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 19, 1874

Another hot, dry, dusty day —
All day in Studio watching &
directing C — who is now bringing
matters to a close. Most of the
time he spent over the mouldings
at the front door. I was a good
deal out of sorts — trouble with
bowels; so that I could not
work — Dinner about 6 clk —
the afternoon, a [illegible]
of the mt. Sea at 6. Well
could not go to Nina Jessup's
party — but called for a few
moments on Jan & her friends,
Misses Alling & Walbridge —
thence up the hill & [illegible] played
two games of whist with Mrs [illegible] —
at 10, to my Room & wrote
letters to Charl & Mary — down
to P.O. after 11 o clks — To bed
just before midnight —
