## Left Page

### March, Thursday 19, 1874

Muggy, muddy & overcast with a
dash of rain in the mfg. Got
to studio at 9.30 & to work
trying to paint in boats in
Racquette Lake, but I cannot
get the color to harmonize —
Snedecor made a long call —
Home to lunch at 1.30 — back
at 2.45 & painted until 5 —
cleaned up & at 5.30 went
over to the Palette — [illegible] Met
Kurtz & went over his building
with him — at 6.10 at Kimball's
Annie & the Cooks there — also
Alf. K. & his wife. About 7, Will,
Fanny & Mr. K., Ned, Annie & I
went to Steinway Hall to concert
of Vocal Society of N.Y. Weather
clear but very close — Home
at 10.30 & right to bed —

## Right Page

### March, Friday 20, 1874

Better weather — Cooler, but
not yet clear. About 10 when
I got to studio — at work all
day, with little effect, pothering
over the figures in boats in
Racquette Lake picture.
Home to lunch at 1.30 — back
at 2.45 after dressing for my
every work. At 6.20 went to
the Kimball's — tea & at 8.30
went with Fanny to Annie Cook's
where was a company of graduates
from the 12th St. School of Design.
Had a very jolly time — the
Romberg [illegible] symphony was
given — an essay by Ella
Russell & the Ballad of Lovel
& Genevieve — was given with
character illustrations — a very
clever frolic. At 11.30 went to
Geo. Hearns — 12.45 — just in time
for their supper — left about 12 —
& rode up in 6th Ave. car with
Miss Parlan & her bro. Home
about 2.30 & right to bed —
Frame for Racquette Lake
came today.
