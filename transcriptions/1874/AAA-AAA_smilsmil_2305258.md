## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 7, 1874

After an uneasy night — awoke in great distress which culminated at 7 o'clk in a grand bilious cascade — nearly a quart of yellow bitter fluid was thrown from my stomach — then the bowels acted almost as violently — After that I was exhausted and slept until 11 — then was washed & dressed & Mrs. & gave me my bkfst — at 12 o'clk. Glorious day — the afternoon was spent sitting in the open air. At 5 went out riding with Mr. Mercer, Biddee Grossbeck & [illegible] — called at Mr. L. Searles — Back to tea at 6.30 — got a couple of little dainties & put my lumber in my studio as there were signs of change — It was after 9 before I got to Joe [illegible] who had a little party — Smeds Searles & Jessups — Joe rioted as usual & it was 12 o'clk before I got to bed — Heavy thunder storm during the night —

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 8, 1874

Very hot, muggy day — Bkfst at 8 o'clk — At 10 o'clk went down to Frink. It seems as though I was going to have trouble to get him & Strange to do my work. Soon after 11 went to Crandall's & young C. showed me through his block factory — Up to my room at 12. Read, wrote. &c. Dinner at 1.30. I can hardly tell how the P.M. got away from me, but it was near 5 when again I went down to Frink & Strange — No answer yet — Told them I must have it to-morrow — positively. At 6 o'clk. From the top of [illegible] Lathrop's house, watched the approach of a superb thunder storm that came down upon us in great fury while we were at tea. About 9 o'clk — went to Mr. Wm. [illegible] where a few friends were invited & had a very pleasant time — Home at 11.30 & to bed just at 12 —
