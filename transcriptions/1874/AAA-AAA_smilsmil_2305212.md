## Left Page

### April Saturday, 4 1874

Threatening m't with flying sun.
Up. Early & got to studio at 8.30.
Worked very faithfully all the
m't on Darley's outlines, with
graver & Drypoint — Som after 12
started for down-town — Weather
clear, but cold, windy & very dirty.
Got to A.B.N. Co at 1 o'clk — Had
a p'f — of outline — Lunched with
the Directors — Started up-town
at 2.30 — Stopping to see Bunce —
Dewey (Sailor) Lauthier, Snedecor,
Haseltine Collection — J.G. Brown
at 10th St Studios & the Frost pictures
at Somerville's — Got to Studio at
5.30. Home to tea at 7 o'clk —
a long letter from Jol. Evening
Reading Taine's "Pyrenees"
To bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### April Sunday, 5 1874

Very cold — clear & windy
m't — clouded over before noon
— snow & rain at night.
m't, went to Dr. Hastings' &
heard him preach on the
Resurrection — from St. Luke's.
24th Ch — 2 — 6th v. After service
walked with Charle & Ed.
down to Mad. Ave, B.p'tn, Ch. &
then home with Kate. Dinner
at 1.30 — Kindled fire in the
furnace after dinner — a short
nap. At 5 o'clk. went up to
Willie's — from thence, at 6 — to
Uncle A's — Tea, with Ida
Thayer & Minnie Rayner. At
9 o'clk, in a sleety storm
started for home — walked
three blocks with Mr. Thayer
& daughter — Home & to bed
at 11.
