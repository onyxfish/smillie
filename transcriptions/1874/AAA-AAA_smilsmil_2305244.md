## Left Page

### June, Sunday 7, 1874

*[written above:] See Tuesday 9th*

*[written below printed header:] Monday 8th.*

Ext[remely] hot day — of course
feel weak after yesterday's exper-
ience. Got to studio at 10 o'clk.
Started Alabama Arms drawing
for A.B.N. Co. but did not do
more than ¼ of a days work.
About 12.30, Rode down to Dave's,
thun went to Sauthner's. Got
mounting board & rode up
home to lunch at 1.30 — back
to studio at 2.30. Ther. got
up to 86°. Didn't make out
much work. Katey Monterey
came at about 5 o'clk & I gave
her a lesson in laying etching
grounds. After 6 o'clk — while
painting for Miss Woodward I
got a lot of Venetian Red on my
breeches. Had to take them off
& wash them. Home man for
tea at 8.15. Eve'g. Uncle
Willie an[d] — [illegible] over Uncle
W's "Casillear". To bed at
11.15

## Right Page

### June, Monday 8, 1874

*[written above:] Tuesday 9th.*

Still very warm, but delightful
breeze blowing in at my studio
window. Got to studio at 9.30
made believe work on Arkansas
Arms, but did not much. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — after a call
from Uncle Willie & Dining.
Back at 2.30 — P.M. until
5 o'clk, giving Miss Scott her
fifth lesson. Very heavy thunder-
storm came up. She & her mother
beat a hasty retreat. Annie
coils picture came in from
N.A.[D.]. Mstr Calverley. I
set to work & straightened the
frame which had sprung badly.
Home between storms at 7.30 —
just got caught. Tea at once.
Eve'g, made paste & mounted
W.C. drawing & Smelting Furnace.
Heavy storms until 9 o'clk.
To bed at 10.30.
