## Left Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 15 1874.

Again overcast & strong winds from S. E. — great hopes of Rain —
After bkfst., Rode Tom & led Bub
to water at the Jail trough — all day —
then Rode away out to the mill
beyond the lake & watered them
there — got to studio at 10 &
got Readis, Sent Arms to S/fp, 5 —
wrote letter to Van Zandt — gathered them off — Pottered all the
afternoon on Race & succeeded
only in Rubbing out all that
I had done & more too. At 4.30
went to Stables & after grooming
Tom, had a good Ride with [illegible].
Tea at 6.30 — At 8, went up and
bid Mrs. Clark good bye — near
9 in at Jan's — Mr. Woolsey there —
& at 9, at L. Searle's — took
Sue Holt home — at 10.20 — a
slight muzzle of Rain —
To bed at 10.45 —

## Right Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 16 1874.

Rain at last — sweet & slow —
Got to studio at 9 & after a
time got started in a better
mood on my "Race" & worked
along feeling much encouraged.
Dinner at 1.15 — Back to studio
at 2 & worked steadily until
near 5. Blocking in suggestion
of effect as well as form —
Did not go horse back on
acct. of the Rain — but gave
my horse a good grooming. At
6.45 at Dau. Sayre's to tea —
about 20 there — At 8.45 went down
town to P.O. & stopped for ¾ of an
hour at Jan's — Back to Dau's &
there until 11.40 — then home &
to bed just before 12 —
