## Left Page

### November Friday, 6 1874

A charming day — got to [illegible]
at 10 o'clk. & until 12.30 worked
with Grover on dies. at 1 o'clk.
at Continental Co's — got [illegible]
presented bill & got check for
$30.— Then to Mechanics B[an]k
then at 1.40, lunch — Then
to Phil. Rehearsal at 2.30 —
After that, with Fanny Kimball
walked up to Knœdler's —
Back to studio at about 5.
Stretched canvases & to tea
at 6.30. Grand Row — in the
mr[s]. I had engaged 3d story, from [illegible]
for 3 weeks for Charl — not until
when he rel[urned] to the city he would
be able to complete the arrang[ment]
for the winter — Half an hour la[ter]
Alderer rel[urned] from Europe & want[ed]
his Room — In an instant, Mrs. D[illegible]
on her head for fear that she wou[ld]
lose the letting of her Room for the
whole winter — I went to her —
Then Mrs. Blake, hoping to make
some satisfactory arrangement b[ut]
it was no use — To bed at 11 o'clk

## Right Page

### November Saturday, 7 1874

Charming day — got to studio
soon after 9 — Spent the m[ornin]g
Charcoaling subject — say "Lake
Placid" on canvas 11×18. To
dinner at 12.30 — After that went
to Kehnis — had hair cut and
head shampooed — Then went
down to B[roa]d way & 44th St. to see W[illegible]
Henry Cox — Not in — back to
studio at 4 o'clk — Stretched
3 canvases & more charcoaling.
To tea at 7 — after calling on
Mrs. Blake & Aldgner. Even[ing]
went to Beingraff's — and down
to studio with a lot of things —
Got back at 15 & then to bed.
