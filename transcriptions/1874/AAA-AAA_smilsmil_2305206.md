## Left Page

### March, Monday 23, 1874

Up at 8.30 — feeling very flimsy — Got to studio at 10.15
Kindled fire & tried to get to work but distress & dis —
Fraction in head too great —
Shurtleff came in about noon
& near 1 o'clk, Mr. Heam & Miss
[illegible] of Phila — They stayed until
2.30 — then I went home to lunch
— back at 3.15 & went over to Tiffany's
Gifford's — Jno. Baker's — Colyer's and
D. Johnston's — then to Palette supper
then to Dewey's. The Tailor — [illegible]
& Snedecor's — S. begged so for my
"Lost" for his sale that, at last,
I consented. back to studio, fix it
fin for the night — & home to tea at
7.45 — Eve'g — Read news paper —
wrote note to Emma D — Fanny K —
& Annie C — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 24, 1874

Very cold, windy day — Got to
studio at 9.35 — Spent all day
fussing over Raquette picture
& making very little progress —
The sky is so cut up with little
clouds that I cannot suppress
that I am quite hopeless over
it. Home to lunch at 1.30 —
back at 2.30. At 5.30 Father
came in — also Ferguson and
Janine — Home to tea at 7.15
Eve'g, W. & Mrs. Jacob Wykaff
called — Read my news paper —
wrote a note to Jno. W. Nichols
& got to bed at 10.40
