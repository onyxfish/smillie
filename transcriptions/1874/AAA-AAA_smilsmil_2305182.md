## Left Page

### February Sunday, 1 1874

Very cold, clear day — Did not get up until about 9.30 — Late when I got to Church. (Dr. Hastings'.) Heard a good sermon but unfortunately was not in a condition to listen to it. After service went down to studio, fixed fire, wrote a note to Mr. Brown & carried it over to N.A. of D. Home to dinner at 1.30 — Spent an hour or two in bed but got no nap. At 5, started out and walked up to Jessup's — 78th St. & Madison Ave. There to tea. Left about 8 o'clk & stopped at Uncle A's. Home at 10 o'clk. Wrote an answer to an article in the "Post" — went out & posted it at 12.30 — & to bed at 1 o'clk. A.M.
Very cold & superb moonlight.

## Right Page

### February Monday, 2 1874

Cold with snow & wind. Got to studio at 10 o'clk. Found my fire all right. Spent the morning picking up things and getting ready for work on Appleton drawing. At 12.30 went over to Tiffany's & then to N.A. of D. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.45. Two ladies, Mother and daughter, called & spent most of P.m. with me. They want a large picture. Ferguson came in — then Nicole & Tiffany. They left at 6 o'clk. & I got home to tea at 6.30. Evening, looking over newspapers — cutting out notices — etc — etc. Father still very much under the weather. Got to bed about 10.45.
