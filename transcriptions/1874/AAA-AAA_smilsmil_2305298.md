## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 27 1874.

Weather somewhat disturbed — strong wind from S.E. — threatening a storm or drought — can't tell which. Went to Baptist Ch. & heard a young Irishman deliver a very clumsy address — went to studio after service & read papers until dinner at 1.30 — After dinner again at studio & wrote to D.S. [illegible] until 4 o'clk — then Geo. came — went with him up to Fain [illegible] — met there Fau. Gus S. & others — joined them — walked down town with them — tea soon after 6 — went, wrote until 8.30 — then with Geo. down town — stopped at Fain's — Home & to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 28 1874.

Over cast all day — and so was my head; for I suffered much from oppression & confusion — got to studio at 8.30 — stretched a canvas 11×16 & charcoaled a subject suggested by the sun set that I saw from Fire Hill a few days ago — At 11 o'clk. gave up & went down town — had my hair cut — back & worked a little while before dinner. P.M. again at studio & worked on fire engine drawing until 4.5, then to stable & out with Geo. until 6.5 — tea at 6.30 — Rec'd a letter from home urging me to come to N.Y. & attend to my moving. At 8.30 at Fain's. Home & to bed at 11 —
