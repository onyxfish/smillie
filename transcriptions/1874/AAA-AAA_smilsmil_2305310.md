## Left Page

### October Friday, 23 1874.

Another superb, dreamy Oct.
day. All the m'f, from 8.30 until
11 o'clk., dickering with H. & [illegible]
when at last we came to an
agreement concerning lines —
on my deed from Post I claimed
a revision of our agreement & got
considerable additional land —
and still, pay a large price.
At 11.30 went to studio with Hem.
S., & got sketches from her hands
on my whiskey drawing. At 12.30
went up on Warner's hill with S. —
nothing could be lovelier than the
color & atmosphere — back at 1.15 —
then dinner — from 2.30 worked on
Whiskey drawing — about 5 o'clk — a
lot of Montrose ladies called — I did
not get through with washing up
until near 6 — at 6 had tea — Mgt.
to Gee & to Will — ab.g Ret'd from [illegible]
to W. J. J's — Superb moonlight night
— with S. — until after 10 — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### October Saturday, 24 1874.

Stormy m'g. but cleared before
noon into a mild, silvery day,
the very Perfection of autumn.
Worked all the m'g. until 12
o'clk on Whiskey Stamps drawing
then walked with S. up onto the
Warner hill — back & c't & through
fairground. Ret'd at 1.15 — then
to dinner — P. M. worked until
near 5 — then down town for
a board to pack drawing —
Lovely ev'g. Tea at 6 — Ev'g.
called on A. S. Post — At g. at
Azur Bathrop's. Will M. Fred.
C. Beals, Gus S. Lillie J. & Alice
Stroud there — Whist until near
11 — Took Lillie J. home — then
to bed — Did not sit up to see
the eclipse —
