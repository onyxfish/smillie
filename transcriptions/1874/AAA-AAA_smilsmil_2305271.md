## Left Page

### August Sunday, 2 1874

Bright fresh, cool day with
many showers. Mᴴ, went to
Presby Ch. & heard Dr. Taylor at
our house. He preached a very
good sermon from Luke.
After service, went home with G[illegible]
went down to Methodist Ch. & got
house key from her mother there —
then went to Fan's — then home to
dinner at 1.30 — After that read
paper for awhile — a short nap —
then mate for awhile — at 5
went out & walked — Tea
soon after 6 — Evey, for awhile
with Fan & Gus, then to my room
& wrote to Fanny K — at 10 for
a time in Mrs. Dud's parlor
& to bed about 11 —
Evey cleared very cold —
Ther. on porch 55° at
10.30 P.M.

## Right Page

### August Monday, 3 1874

Gloriously clear, cold day —
Up early & over to studio before
bkfst. Cottrell there & commenced
work in earnest on front door —
Bkfst at 8 o'clk — Then again
in studio until after 9 — then
groomed colt & with Gratz &
Gel went out horseback until
11 — Men in & mate awhile fin-
ishing 2d Mormon letter for the
Republicans — Walked down to
the office — Mr. & Mrs. C. Groes [illegible]
left for Jersey City at 12.45 — Dinner
at 1.30 — In studio until after
4 — again down town — at 5 went
out with Gil in Tyler's buggy &
rode until 6.45 — Tea at 7 —
Mrs. & Miss Gratz arr'd from Phila —
Evey grooming horses & clean-
ing stable, walking with girls &
writing until 9 — Then to Fan's
& played Whist until 10.30
Home & to bed at 11.15
Mrs. & Miss Gratz arr'd this P.M.
