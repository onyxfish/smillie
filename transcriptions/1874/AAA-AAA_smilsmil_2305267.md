## Left Page

### July, Saturday, 25, 1874

wind blowing strong from S. E. —
air balmy & moist — I felt
very stiff & sore down neck & back
so that I could not work — have
taken cold. Wf, Rode over to
farm with Leu, Searle Lent, & Dink.
Air delicious — but rain will come —
back at 12 o'clk. Did some
writing on Mormon letter — Dinner
at 1.15 — much time lost chatting
after dinner — Finished M. letter
& took it to Republican office.
On way back, stopped at Wm Jessup's —
There until nearly — then home
to tea — Every at Ben Mulford's.
Julia Read, Alice Stroud, Gus
Seymour Lilly Jessup & others
there — Had a Regular boys
romps with them. Home at
10 o'clk — to bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### July, Sunday, 26, 1874

Overcast mf — up late — [illegible]
about 9 o'clk — Wf went to the
Baptist Ch — Rev. Mr. Murphy
of Des Moines, Iowa — preached
a sermon, level as a prairie,
from 1st Cor. 2d. ch. 9th v — Dinner
near 2 o'clk — Rain about
3 — To my Room at 4 — Wrote
a note to Emma Beach &
commenced one to Fanny K. —
Tea at 6.15 — Then gathered
in the parlor until near
9 o'clk — To bed about 10 —
Raining quite steadily —
