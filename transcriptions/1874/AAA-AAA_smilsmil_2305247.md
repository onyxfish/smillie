## Left Page

### June, Saturday, 13, 1874

Delightful, fresh & spring-
like day! Got to studio at
9.30 — Mr. Leonard Scott called
at about 10.30 — Paid me for
his daughters lessons, $30.00
At work sorting things
until afternoon — then home
to lunch — back at 3 —
at the same work all
the P.M. — except when
interrupted by calls. Katy
Greatosey came at 5 &
I showed her how to say
Rebleting grounds — At 6.30
went over to Union League.
Home to tea at 7.30 — at 8.30
went to Union Sq. Theatre
— Palmer out of town — Went
in & saw "Led Astray" — Wonder
how it has proved so popular —
Home at 11.30 — to bed at
midnight —

## Right Page

### June, Sunday, 14, 1874

Pleasant day, I suppose,
but I don't know, as I
was in doors all day with
a headache — Got up
& took tea with the family
at 6.30 — Sat in my
chair until about 11, then
commenced clearing out
my funny drawers, sorting
old letters, etc. etc. until
10 o'clk — then to bed —
