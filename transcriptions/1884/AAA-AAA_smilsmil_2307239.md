## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 3, 1884

Beautiful day. Got to studio about 10 o'clk & spent the mg in arranging in frames exhibits for Union League, Century & Salmagundi — Annie came at 1 & I walked home with her — lunch back at 2.30 — Painted over frame for charcoal sketch to bring it into harmony with color of the paper — also pasting up backs of frames etc. — At 5 went to Gradip's — Home to dinner before 6 — Eveg., about 8.30 with Annie went to Champney's by invitation & met there quite a number of people — among them Mr. & Mrs. Moscheeles Mr. & Mrs. [illegible] & Mr. & Mr. Boyessen — a Mr. Barnard inflicted an unpublished story upon us which he read in wretched manner from the MS. I pray that the unprotected public may be spared. We got away a little before 11 — home & right to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 4, 1884

And yet another beautiful day — Got to studio about 10 — Met Mrs. Saml. Colman & walked part way with her. Found the steam fitters had been in my room & had uncoupled radiator to move it away from the window — To work on small sketch of Normandy Cliffs, painting in the foreground — Sam. Colman came in & we chatted for more than an hour — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 3 & painted as long as I could see, but did not quite cover my canvas — Then to Donneli's & gave directions for them to send lamps & standard to the house — then to Bis bea's to have a binding root fixed — then to Keppel's, but neither he or Macbeth were in — then to Wiinderlich's to see about his selling some of my etching pts then home to dinner at 6.15 — Eveg. I read aloud — part of Hamlet & part of Dowden's essay on the play — then a couple of chapters from Village on the Cliff — To bed before 11.
