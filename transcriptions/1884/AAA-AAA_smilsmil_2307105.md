## Left Page

### March, Sunday 2, 1884.

Light snow most of the day — Up
late — Lounged about doing nothing
in a most reprehensible way
until 2 o'clk. when we had
dinner — Miss Williams, with
Carleton & Catherine took dinner
with us — Ed & Cook & wife being
in Phila. More lounging &
doing of nothing until near
5 o'clk. when I went out for
a walk — on my way home
stopped at Charles' — but he was
out — Tea after 7 — Evening
wrote a long letter to Father,
giving extracts from Helen Newton's
"Right & Wrong uses of the Bible" —
also, notes to Parrish & B.N. Ticknor —
At 11, took them out & posted
them — To bed about 11.30.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 3, 1884.

Pleasant m'g. but p.m. dark with
snow squalls. Up late — Help. At 9
to studio soon after 10 — got "Pond's
outlet" up on the easel — oiled it
out & got to work on the sky; after
fussing over it for some time I "went
in" & repainted most of it very
solidly — Home at 1.15 — dinner —
back before 3 & worked on sky &
distances until near 5 — Then
up to Dickel's & rode from 6 to
6.30 — Home at 7 — Tea — evening
spent loafing. To bed at 10.30.
