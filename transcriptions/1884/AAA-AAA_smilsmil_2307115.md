## Left Page

### March, Monday 24, 1884

overcast mtf. Clearing, very warm before noon — To studio soon ft 10 — wrote to Stroud & Smith & sent check for $62.50 — also to Cyrus Davis. Then to work on Chilian port & stuck to it until I went home to dinner at — Annie being on her back, & the cook gone, I had duties that kept me from studio until after 3 — Then again to work on S°. Am. drawing & by 5 o'clk. had finished it — Then to Up town Tribune office. Diet Kitchen business — Home at 6.30 — Walter came with drawings for Mirror panel, main hall — Montrose 7.30 when I had tea — Every read aloud closing chapters of the Newcomes.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 25, 1884

Beautiful spring-like day — got to studio very late — Annie still obliged to lie on her back — a sore trial to her patience — Ceres waiting at studio for me, hurrying up his work — Mounted drawing & some photos. Home at — dined — back at 3 — note from [illegible] — at his request went to n.a. — back to studio & set down ten sheets of Japan paper to take off to-morrow — Then again to n.a. with materials to begin making sketches for K's catalogue — but so much to look at & fell about that I accomplished nothing — Home at 6 — Every read aloud until Annie fell asleep — To bed at 10.30

Concert in aid of Diet Kitchen at Chickering Hall — much disappointment to Annie that she could not go.
