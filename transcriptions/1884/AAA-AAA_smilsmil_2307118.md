## Left Page

### March, Sunday 30, 1884

The nastiest sort of a day, owing to bitter cold, high wind & no end of flying dirt. Up at about 9 — about 11 went to N.A. Home to dinner at 2 — about 4, again to N.A. & home before 6 — Tea at 7 — Annie began to have pain. I wrote my letter home & at about 9 went to Pool's stable & got coupé — Rode up to Yale's — not in — down to 30th St. & Mad. Ave. for him — gone — Then to 160 W 34th S. to Miss Mabie's — for a nurse — none — Then to 335 W. 28th S. & found Miss Jane — Took her home with me — Then again to Y's — brought him home with me — discharged coupé — went for Dusk after 11 when he came — Annie was then delivered of a 2½ month fœtus — miscarriage the result of a fall in the street about 3 weeks ago — at 2.30 Dusk left & Yale went to bed up stairs & I went to my bed.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 31, 1884

Brilliant, beautiful day, though still rather windy & cold. Up at about 8 — Dr. Yale breakfasted with me — Annie appears to be remarkably well — It was after 10 before I was able to leave the house — Thro to N.A., reworked until 1 o'clk on a sketch after Guy's painting — Then home to dinner. At 3, again to N.A., but as Kurtz did not put in an appearance, I did nothing — at 4 went to studio & at 5, home, where I had a good long nap — Up before 7 & had tea — Well with Annie. Nicole came at about 9 & made a call of about half an hour. To bed before 11.
