## Left Page

### March, Saturday 8, 1884

Wretched dark day — ice covering
everything — rain falling — Got to
studio before 10 & to work on
view near Marblehead. Changing
some tones in the sky, & repainting
all the foreground — Van Cleef
called & we had some sparring
over a recent article his. pub.
in the Herald, on Etching — so that
I got home to dinner late —
back at 3 — had got foreground
too cold — so, worked over it again
for an hour or more — At 5, went
to Dickle's & rode for ½ an hour.
Tea at 7 — eveg. Annie read
from the Newcomes — To bed
at 11.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 9, 1884

Perhaps the most horrible
day yet — a quarter of an inch
of clear ice covers everything &
rain falls & freezes incessant-
ly. At 9 o'clk — several thunder
claps with lightning & heavy
rain — at night, sleet & later,
snow. Bp. soon after 9 —
Read Baker & wrote letter to
father — then a nap, and at
2 — dinner — afternoon, more
reading — soon after 6 — went
to Walter's for a few minutes
about Montrose plans — then
to G. Delbt with father's letter.
Tea at 7 — eveg. Annie read
aloud for awhile — To bed
before 11.
