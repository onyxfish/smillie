## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 5, 1884

Another nasty day — 1/4 of an inch of ice over everything — clouds + fog — a little struggling sunshine at 3 o'clk — then more fog + a niggle of rain. Got to studio at 10. Got out a canvas x upon which was an old sketch — in a few chalk lines indicated in a new form the composition of "The Pond's meter" + then to work with color — by 12.30 had laid in sky + foreground — then home to dinner — back at 3 + by 5 had roughly blocked in the rest of it — an improvement on the work of yesterday — Young Corum called with note of "intro" from father — Rested in big chair from 5 to 6 — then home — tea at 7 — Evef. Annie again read aloud from the Newcomes. To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 6, 1884

Muggy, sloppy + warm — Steaming fog by day, niggling rain at night. Got to studio at 10 + worked for a little while on "larger" Pond's out-let" — then, Relish on No. 30 — Home [illegible] — back at 3. Finished Re-biting + took a close wiped proof in black on white paper for the Harpers — to be used for a photo-eng. — to be published in the [illegible] — Wm Park, of Montrose, + a younger brother called after 4 P.m. — Home a little before 7 — had tea. Evef. uncomfortably warm. Annie read aloud for awhile — To bed at the usual time.
