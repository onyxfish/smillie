## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 18. 1884.

Pleasant day — Got to studio at 10, but feeling unfit for work, at about 11 I went down-town. To Charl's — & saw him — Then to Continental Fire Ins. Co. — & enquired about the cancellation of my Montrose policy — Then to A.B.N. Co. — but did not see Will — Then to Walter's & saw plans in progress — Then home to dinner at 1.10 — Then at 3 — to studio — hung about there for a time trying to work on an A.B.N. Co. drawing at 4.30 went to Jessup's & Co's with pantaloons — have to be altered — Then to Schubert's & got two sets of Henschel tickets & home about 6. Read libretto of Princess Ida — Fame. Ka at 7 — At 8 — at 5 Ave. Theatre — Gilbert & Sullivan are beginning to "run emptins" — to use a bit of rural slang — The work is not without cleverness, but it is a very mixed form of beverage. Home at 11 — to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 19. 1884.

A horrible day of soaking, driving rain — To studio at 10 — for awhile worked on tracing from photo., for A.B.N. Co. of a Chilean Port — Then, with oil paint, making alterations as instructed by A.B.N. Co. on a marine sketch by Harry Chase — Home at 1 — dinner — feeling out of sorts, I did not return until 4 o'clk. & then finished alterations on Chase nt. Annie went to a musicale at Mr. Villard's — In the m.f. I went again to Schubert's & changed location of our Henschel seats — Home, driving, heavy rain — at 6.30 — Eve'g — Annual Water-Color meeting — but, having premonitions of a cold, thought it best not to go out. Annie read aloud until 10 — To bed before 11.30, having written notes to S.J. Guy & Dillaway of Detroit.
