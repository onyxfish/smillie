## Left Page

### July, Saturday 26. 1884.

Dull mtf. with some struggling sunshine. After bkfst. Rubbed a grey background onto millboard with boats and went out to them again, hoping to be able to finish them up in some satisfactory way — Company all the mtf. — Dr. Oliver of Boston — H. f. Bacon & a friend of his & Mr. Mrs. C. F. Reinhart who have arr. this mtf. from Dieppe. I did not get much of a study — Home to lunch at 1 — out again in the afternoon with Baker & pencils, worked on a group of boats until 4.30 getting very much chilled — Then Annie came & we took a tramp out on the Havre road for a mile or more & back part way over another road. & then across lots into the Grand Val. To dinner at 6.30 — Heavy Rain — Eve'g went in doing small duties — reading, cleaning brushes, etc. etc — near 11 when I went to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 27. 1884.

Another day of sunshine & shower. About 10.30 Annie & I went to the old church to mass but got there late — after that we walked up the Petit Val some distance — then across lots to an old road that led us into the town Beuouval — we had been led along, from one point to another until we reached the town — we walked through to near the church — then turning at right angles we went through fields again to the edge of the cliff — Two or three showers fell — Then beautiful sunshine — magical effects of color over land & sea — walked home along edge of cliffs — some wild places for descent — a short rest in the church of Notre Dame de la Garde & then home at 1.30 — lunch — & then a nap — wrote part of my letter to Father — To dinner soon after 6 — after that went to the butcher's — milk woman's & baker's — Eve'g finished letter home — To bed before 11.
