## Left Page

### December, Monday 1, 1884

Dull day. To studio before 10 — Found invitation from Union league to send
W.C's next week. So I spent part of the m'tf touching up some of last summer's
studies & sketches & then cutting mounts.
Home at 1 — Fanny K. there — she took lunch with us. Back at 2.30
& until 4.30 pasting mounts on my studies — then to Reichard's for a few
minutes to take another look at the Xmas card designs — then to Grady's
& until 6 fussing over frame designs.
Home at 6 & had dinner — part of the ev'tf. fixing knobs on parlor bedroom
doors — then read aloud awhile to Annie from "Village on the Cliff" — Som
after 10 the boy had a tantrum — pure temper apparently. He kept it up
with short intervals until 1.30 when I took him up & put him to sleep — then lay awake myself until nearly
3

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 2, 1884

Charming day — of course I felt rather busted after last night's experience. Got to studio soon after
10 — pasted mounts on a couple of my studies & soon after 11 went to Am.
Art Ass'n. galleries & spent an hour there — then to Reichard's for a few
minutes to look at designs for Xmas cards.
Home to lunch soon after 1 — about 2 felt so tired that I went to bed and
had 1½ hour of solid sleep — then to studio & fixed things there for the
night — then to Grady's & selected the mouldings & ornaments for my big frame
He is to have a section of it made up.
Home to dinner at 6. At 8 with Annie
at Star Theatre & saw Irving's Hamlet —
a packed house. Tremendous enthusiasm
at culmination of scene of the players before
the king — but I am too cold blooded & find
too many things in Irving that are not agreeable
to me. Miss Terry's Ophelia was beautiful
& touching — met Sam'l. Colman & wife in
the lobby on our way out. Walked home —
glorious moonlight — in at midnight
& right to bed.
