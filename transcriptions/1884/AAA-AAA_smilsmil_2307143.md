## Left Page

### May, Monday 19, 1884.

another delicious day with sum
mer sea — had my hot water at 7
— my salt bath at 7.30 & my bkp.
at near 9 — the rest of the m'g was
spent in fancying canes until near
noon — lunch at 1 & spent some
time after that trying to get
a nap. As the day waned it
got to be almost uncomfort-
ably warm — lounged about
until about 6 & then, dinner
after that, with Annie, Jeb &
Nellie, watching the sunset
on upper deck with a — Mr. & Mrs.
Gordon until after 9 — delicious
eveg — very hot & close in our
stateroom & no air coming in
at the open port hole — down
in the stoke-hole the ther. stood
at 140°. To bed about 10.

The run at noon, 324 m.
A Baltimore steamer passed

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 20, 1884.

Cooler, growing to be a real cold
wind shifting to north, but the sea
quieter than ever — The boy slept
well & we passed a comparatively
quiet night — my salt water bath
at 7.30 — bkp. at 8.30 — family duties
& some walking filled the m'g
until lunch at 1 — after that
had a much uninterrupted nap
until 3 — then again on deck —
almost uncomfortably cold — no
sea and not a cloud to be seen.
Eveg — a mock Trial in the
smoking room & some singing
in saloon by Mr. Wash'n Nathan
& Mad'e Dottie — To bed about
10 — The boy giving us a very
good night — but he is an un-
ceasing fidget during the day.

The run at noon, 336 m — m'ts
a Bremen St'r passed — P.m. — a
White Star St'r.
