## Left Page

### May, Thursday 1. 1884.

Pleasant day — Got to Studio at 10 — Tried to work — could not — so, I spent the mtg — getting in order things about the studio preparatory to leaving for the summer — Home at 1 — did not get back until after 3 — Geo. brought in a copy of Com. Advertiser with letter concerning Wm Bliss Baker to which I at once wrote a response — Then came 12 × 22 frame which, being to flaming, I spent the rest of the afternoon in going over portions of it with india ink — Home at 6.30 — dressed — Tea at 7 — Mother Cook came at 7.45 & wife Annie & me went to Chickering Hall to concert of Choral Club — Home before 11 — & right to bed —

## Right Page

### May, Friday 2. 1884.

Pleasant day which grew to be exceedingly warm — Rec'd a letter from Wm Bassett saying he would take my Painting of "Rough Sport" for $120 — To studio at about 10 & to work on Rough Sport which, by a little general painting, I very much improved — Home at one. Headache — so I rested awhile after dinner & did not get to shop until near 4 — Finished "Cedar meadow" & a "Bit on Noble Ho. Neck" — washed up brushes & home soon after 7 — Strange, portentous yellow light after sunset — Strong smell of smoke as if burning stubble — Tea at 7 — Wrote to Cottrell & Payne — Read a little of Guenn. To bed about 11.
