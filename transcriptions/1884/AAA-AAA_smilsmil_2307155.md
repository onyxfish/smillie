## Left Page

### June, Thursday 12, 1884

Delightfully clear warm day, but
my head, badly clouded. After [illegible]
between 10 & 11, Annie & I got away
to Braquani's Studio. I took [illegible] of
my etching after his painting & he signed
there for me. He was very agreeable &
gave me 3 photos from paintings by
him — apologized for not entertaining
us in his house, explaining that his wife
was out, preparing for an early trip to
Switzerland. Back to hotel at noon &
had lunch — after that, at rest — at 3
Geo. & I went out & hunted awhile for
studios of Bacon & Middleton — finding
them so far off, at 4 we went to the Salon
& worked at that until 6 — then to hotel
& dinner — [illegible], Mr. Frank Benson,
called with note of introduction from
Mr. Wm. Bassett of Boston — stayed
until 10 o'clk — where I tried to go
to bed but Jas. Jmt. engaged my
attention until near 12 —

## Right Page

### June, Friday 13, 1884

Clear & very warm — after a troubled night
up late & did not have [illegible] until after
9 — went to market & got some splendid
strawberries — after [illegible], domestic duties
& then, not feeling in trim for work, at
about 10.30 I started out with Annie &
Bella to give the boy a trial in his new
carriage — After that, Annie & I went to the
flower market at the Madeleine, bought
some flowers & hazed about until near
12 — then to hotel & in due time had
lunch — in the [illegible]. wrote to Charl. & to
[illegible] about Montrose [illegible] — After lunch
a disturbed nap — After 3 — a., Geo. & I took
[illegible] to Jardin des Plantes & spent some
time there — then, by letter & [illegible], down
the river to Auteuil & back to [illegible] at
P. dela Concorde & to hotel at 6.15 & dinner
[illegible], makes Guide to Paris etc. until
9.30 — To bed soon after 10 —
[illegible] Miles lunched with us, guest
of Geo. & Nellie.
was not allowed to sleep until after
12 — nor between 2 & 4 — horrible
night.
