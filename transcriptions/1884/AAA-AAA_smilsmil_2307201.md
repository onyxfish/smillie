## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 16. 1884.

Bad night again — Did not get to
sleep until near 4 o'clk instinctively
naturally — felt very shaky during
the day — Out at 9.30 with Annie
we got to Nat. Gallery at 10.15 and
after an hour & a half there I was
obliged to give it up — on my way
home stopped at Swan & Edgar's
& bought underclothes — then home
& rested until dinner at 1.15 —
At 2.15 Took cab to 22. Old Broad
& drew £50 — then to Fickling & Sons
Tailors 7 New Broad St. E.C. where I
ordered a suit & light overcoat — then
'bus to Bloomsbury St. where I called
on Dallmayer about Chare's photo
lens — then to British museum until
5 — then by cab home & rested until
tea at about 6.30 — Evef. Annie
read aloud from "Village on the Cliff"
I wrote to Drexel Harjes & Co. for Frank
& a note to Geo. To bed about
10.45 — very close night.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 17. 1884.

Another very warm, heavy day — about
9.30 Annie & I went down to Westminster
Bridge — in St. James' Park met Mr. Sartain
& sat down & had a chat with him. Then
to the bridge & by boat to London Bridge — After
walking awhile through the close, dirty streets,
took cab to the Tower — found many changes &
re-arrangement since my visit 22 years ago —
Ab't 2.45 took cab home — Mr. Satchell arr. a
few minutes after us — Dinner at 1.30 — after
that I took a nap — At 4.30 out & spent
an hour or more wandering & looking into the
shop windows & doing a little shopping —
then home — tea about 7 — At 8.30 it being
very close & warm, Annie, Mr. S. & I went
out for some ice cream — Went to Queen's —
a sweet place in Berkley Sq. — none — then
on to Piccadilly — found a shop with sign up
but no cream — then on to the St. James & found
some poor stuff there — then home by bus —
wrote a note to Dallmayer & posted it — Took
Mr. S. over to her room at No. 1 about 9 —
to bed before 11.
*Ther. reached 83° to-day.*
