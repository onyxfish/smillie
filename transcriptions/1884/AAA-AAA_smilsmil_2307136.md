## Left Page

### May, Monday 5, 1884

Mild, pleasant day — duties kept me at home so that I did not get to studio until near 11 — Spent the rest of the mng — packing up & getting mounted etchings ready for Grady — Dr. Chs. Alm[illegible] Smith of Chicago called & I gave him a card to go to Century Club to see the pictures. Home to dinner at 1.15 — back at 3 — again at work over etchings until after 5 — then to Bisbée's in Waverly place, about boots — on my way back stopped at Century & wrote letter to Ehminger — Home at 7.30 — tea — Eveng. wrote letters to Stroud & to Richter — To bed before 11.

Ritchie came to studio to-day & bought $10× of Jap. paper. —

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 6, 1884

Nasty day — heavy rain and strong wind — got to studio at 10.30 and worked diligently at everything — pasting paper over frames — & putting things away generally — Home to dinner at 1 — back at 3 and continued the work until after 5 when I went to McKeever's shop — then around to the store where I was engaged about frames & etchings until near 7 — then home to tea —. Eveng. — wrote to W. Geo. Whitney, to sister Mary — etc — Jas. Junr. very fretful — between teeth & vaccination he is just at present having a hard time. I got to bed about 11.30 but my night's rest was very much broken.
