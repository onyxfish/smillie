## Left Page

### September, Sunday 28. 1884.

overcast — after about 9½ hours of sleep
I got up with a heavy head. B'kfo. at 9 —
it was near 11 when Annie & I went out
for a walk through the uninteresting
streets — back at 12. Lunch at 1.30
About 3 we all went out for a ride
to Kenilworth & had a look at the
ruins from the outside — then past
Guy's cliff to Warwick & from our
carriage surveyed the castle & back
to hotel at 5 — Table d'hote dinner
at 6 — a very good dinner but wretched
service. then until 8.30 getting the boy
to sleep — he has not been quite well
again this p. m. Finished my
letter to father & to bed before 10.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 29. 1884.

Heavy rain when I awoke — dismal prospects
for the day, but it cleared by 10 A.M. deliciously.
B'kfo. at 8.30 — about 9.30 Go°, Nellie, Sneddon, &
I get off in a "trap" for Warwick — went through
the castle & the grounds & climbed Guy's tower.
Glorious sunlight over one of the most beautiful
landscapes — superb Cedars of Lebanon — the
lawns & River Avon — one of the most charming
landscapes in England. Out at 12 — a. Returned
to L. by tram car — we rode on to Stratford
8 ms farther — got to Red Horse Inn at 1 o'clk
ordered lunch & then to Shakespeare's birth-
place — looked through the house & museum
then back to Inn & had lunch — about 3
when again we started — drove down to
the church & saw the tomb — Ch. decorated
for Harvest thanksgiving services yesterday
poppies, wheat, apples, potatoes etc. then
through Ch. yard to River — back to Manor
St° soon after 5 — having had a delightful
drive of over 20 m. Table d'hote dinner
at 7 — after it awhile in drawing Room
To bed at 10.
