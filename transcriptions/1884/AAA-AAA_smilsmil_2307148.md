## Left Page

### May, Thursday 29. 1884.

Overcast & quite cold — up at 8 & bkfst.
before 9 — at 10.30, Ypo, Nellie, Annie & I
drove over to Chatsworth — 4 miles —
went through the building, remembering
much that I saw 22 years ago — In the
gardens the Rhododendrons were coming
into bloom, promising in a week more to make
a wonderful display — The Duke being at home,
we did not see all of the apartments — after
a drive up to Edensor, then back to the Peacock
at 1.45 & had an excellent dinner after which
I had a very sound nap out of which I was
awakened at 4 to go to Haddon Hall — The children
& Bella went with us, a short drive of 15 m. —
In spite of the cold we enjoyed it very much
& after going through the Hall I walked back
part of the way through the fields, getting to the
Peacock at about 6 — Little Jim has been unable
to keep his last meal on his stomach — teeth, I
think — went to station & arranged for seats
to-morrow m'g. Tea soon after 7 — There was a
chat with our Manchester clergyman
friend — to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### London — Waterloo Hotel.
### 85 Jermyn St.
### May, Friday 30. 1884.

Up before 6 — Still overcast, raw & chilly
Before bkfst. at 7 went out & made sketch
back of the house. Had a good bkfst. & all
got away without hurry on 8.06 train for
London — Clouds broke away & we had some
very pleasant sunlight over a delightful
variety of landscape — Little Jim did
again — this time his bowels went back
on him & he got into a horrible mess — got
to London at 11.55 & took a room in the
Station (Midland) Hotel. & had dinner
abt. — after waiting more than ½ an hour
for it — a splendid hotel — About 3.90°.
& I took a Hansom & drove for 2 hours from
one place to another seeking rooms, we
used up the list we had (9 places) and
at last, in sheer desperation, we settled
at above address. Got our luggage together
all on one cab & we drove from Midland to
Waterloo getting into quarters before 7 P.M., all
very much tired — had tea — then I went
out for a walk — passing through Pall Mall
was startled by a tremendous explosion at a
few minutes past 9 — just after I had passed
St. James' Square — it proved to be a dynamite
blown into the area of Junior Carlton Club —
great excitement in the streets — I
got back to the hotel as soon as I could —
to bed soon after 10.
