## Left Page

### September, Monday 22. 1884.

Beautiful day — with shower at
5 pm — Spent all the m'g with Annie
in a shopping expedition — went up
Regent St. & down Oxford, buying a
trunk, table cloth & nap'kins — calpe
for mother & other things — went to Winds.
& Newton's & then to 71 Queen's Sq. where
Annie got her sack — Q. & U. not in — then
home to dinner at 1.30 — nearly 3 when
I took 'bus for the Tailors where I
tried on my new clothes & ordered more
met Ge° there — At the Bankers drew
out what remained on my letter of
credit — Smart shower while on my
way home. Tea at 7 — at 7.35, on the
spur of the moment — Annie & I went to
the Prince's Theatre, where we saw "Called
Back" a piece so intensely melo-dramatic
as to verge closely on burlesque — the Theatre
is a very pretty & comfortable one — Ladies
cannot wear hats & can have refreshments
served as they sit in their places — ushers
all young women — out at 11 & walked
home — to bed shortly before 12.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 23. 1884.

Another pleasant day — about 9.30 started out —
went first to Charing Cross Station to enquire for
trunk forwarded from Paris — then to Tailor's & tried on
new suit of clothes — then to 109 Leadenhall St. & got a
permit to visit St. Katharine & London Docks — were no'd
to the docks about 11.30 — went through Crescent
cellars & saw 4 acres of Port & Sherry — some of it had
been lying there 18 years — light fungus on cellar roof — only
to be found in wine vaults — then to Ivory floor — in
preparation for quarterly sale — magnificent display
of tusks — then to cinnamon floor — preparations for
regular sale there also — the bark bound into compact
bales & ends smoothly cut off — then cloves — nutmeg —
camphor — wool & other places — at last, after about a
mile of wandering out at Thames Entrance to Shadwell
Basin — walked up to Commercial Road & took 'bus got
home at 1.30 — dinner — nap — at near 4 Annie
& I rode to visit His, Mrs. & took a hurried look, then
to So. Kens'a — Mrs. & looked very generally through
that collection — out at 6 — home, very tired —
had tea — Trunk from Paris arr'd — 11/ 9d pence — may
purchases sent home — got to bed at about
10 o'clk.
