## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 8, 1884.

Fine day with light shower between 4 & 5 P.M. —
Up at about 7 — went out & bought butter in the
market place & bread — had our bkfp. about
8 & in spite of a hitch or two, enjoyed our new
quarters very much — Spent the mt. unpacking
my trunk to he very bottom — the first time since
I left home — also in changing Annie's room —
After 11 Geo. & I went out — to Bacon's — Mr. B.
took us to a carpenter & Geo. ordered stretchers
— then to our quarters & soon after 12 to lunch
at the hotel — After that, feeling very heavy
I went to bed — & so did Annie — About 3
Mr. Bacon called — About 4 Geo. & I went
out for a walk — went to the church where
I was delighted, especially with one interior
view — had to return to avoid a shower — to
dinner at 6.30 — after that Geo. & I climbed to
top of cliff N.W. of town — to church & signal station
Delcon's ever effect over the water & moonrise
over the land — Returning, we found A. & N. on
the "Plage" where we walked for awhile —
In soon after 9 & to bed at 10.45.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 9, 1884.

Pleasantly hot with showers in the
afternoon. — Up bkfp. as usual at 8. &
then some time getting my water
color boy ready & paper stretched —
It was near 11 when I went to the
church yard & began a study of the
tower of the old church — to lunch at 12
after that wrote for some time. Part of
a letter to father — after getting ready
to go out to work a shower came up,
& I went to the beach with Annie &
we sat under the awning & read to me
until the rain passed off — Then we
wandered on to the stream that pours
through the pebbles where a great many
town's women were washing clothes — a very
novel & entertaining sight — Then, the tide
being out, we went on to the foot of the
cliff & under guidance of a boy, over the
sea weed covered rock, under the great
arch — a wonderful sight in the brilliant
sunlight. Back to dinner at 6.30 —
Between 8 & 9 — in the twilight, Geo. & I
took a walk out on the valley road
To bed at 10.30
