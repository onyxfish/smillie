## Left Page

### June, Monday 30. 1884.

Pleasant & cooler day — after 4½ᵖ — Annie
Nellie, Geo. & I went to d. Berville's —
25 Rue de la Chaussée d'Antin & shopped
for easels etc — then to Edouard's. 8 Rue
de Pigalle, for colors — then, at 12, back to
hotel & had lunch — At 2.30 Geo. & I
again started out, went to 34 Rue Lafay-
ette & got moist colors — then to Vieille's —
35 Rue Lavare & got canvas — back
to Berville's & bought easels etc — and
got to hotel at 5.45 feeling as though
we had done a full days work — found
Tiberce de Mare waiting for us. A
pleasant chat with him about him-
self — his wife & childrens — his partner & his
partner-in-law. J.P. Healey. Then dinner —
Eveth. for an hour we were in Geo's
rooms [illegible]      & wife there with a fine
India shawl just purchased.   It
was 11 o'clk when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 1. 1884.

A very pleasant day — about 10 o'clk. Annie
& I got off for the Rag Market in the Rue
du Temple — we had some fine bargaining
with the sellers for some old curtains and
remnants & finally came away with one
old curtain & two remnants at a cost of
17 francs, we then took bus & went to the
Cath. of Notre Dame & walked through it —
then out to the Morgue, where we saw three
bodies, two men & a woman — then took
voiture & back to lunch at 12.20 —
after that a rest, then wrote a note
to old Mr. de Mare in answer to his note
rec'd yesterday eve'g. also one to Tiberce
About 3.30 out with Annie, the boy & Belle —
to Hotel Biuda & left a note for Miss Hall-
owell — then to a high point of the Mont-
martre district, where, from an old Mill built
at the close of the 13th century we had a splendid
view of city & country — then to hotel before 5 —
A. & I went out & bought brass candlesticks
before dinner — Eveth. a pleasant call
from Mr. Edwin Blashfield until 9 o'clk
It was 12 o'clk. before 12 o'clk. before
Master James allowed us to go to bed &
then during the night he behaved like all possessed
