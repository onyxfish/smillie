## Left Page

### August, Friday 1. 1884.

Brilliant mtf. - very quiet and warm. I went to the top of north cliff, opposite Signal Station and painted on the sketch that I made last night. I worked with all my might until after 1 o'clk but could not cover my canvas. I thought that I had done pretty well, but Geo. Cirdeulty was not of the same mind when I showed it to him — After a late lunch I rested for awhile & at about 4 with Annie. Took my Watercolor to the Church yard & worked until after 5.30 — this p.m. Geo.'s nurse girl ret'd. to England. After dinner, as I did not feel comfortable, having a good sized pain under my belt, I kept quiet — washed up brushes & did other chores & to bed at 10.30. Young Danforth called on us in the evtf. Bacon came in about 1 o'clk.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 2. 1884.

Bright mtf. — but just as I was about ready to start for the top of the cliff to finish yesterday's sketch, a sea fog blew over us that effectually barred the sun for the rest of the mtf. As my dyspeptic fits quite uses me up for exercise I stayed in doors & sketched upon canvas a subject that I sketched in pencil a couple of weeks ago — a bit in the little farm yard at the cliff. Lunch soon after 1 — Geo. at home with us, being also used up with dyspepsia. About 3.30, it having cleared, I went to church yard with my W. C. study — overcast again soon after I got there, but I worked until near 6 & finished my study, after a fashion. Dinner soon after 6. Evtf. again quietly at home — feeling rather better than last night — To bed soon after 10.30.
