## Left Page

### December, Saturday 13, 1884

Raw & overcast, with a slight covering of snow on the ground in
the early m'g. Pleasanter later in the day. Got to studio at 10
& to work on the foreground of big picture — Home at 1 — back at 2
& worked for two hours getting in about half only of the foreground
— none of the Rocks or immediate L.H. corner — then washed up &
home at 5 & took a rest until 6 — then dinner & at 8 to Phil.
concert — home at 10.30 &
sat by fire for awhile reading
— near 12 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 14, 1884

Bright m'g, but later overcast
& very raw — Up late — Read
news paper until Charl came
at 11.30 — we walked down to
Century club to look at exhibit'n
of photographs by amateurs — a
liberal display of Charl's work
there — met a number of friends —
the No. 1. Johnstons — de Forrests —
Miss Marquand (the photographer)
Mr. Tuckerman etc — Home at 2
& Charl took dinner with us.
I slept from 4 to 6 — then wrote
my letter home & soon after 7
took tea — after that to J.C.D.
with letter — Stopped at Mother
Cook's to tell her about time table
to Bridgeport. 9 o'clk before the
boy got to sleep — then wrote
awhile & read. To bed at 11.
