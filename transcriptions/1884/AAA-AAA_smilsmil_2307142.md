## Left Page

### May, Saturday 17. 1884.

*Stateroom 171 - 4" 35, City of Berlin*

Up before 7 - a good sleep - before 8 - &
we all away from the house also before 9 - Got to steamer with baggage
& baby at 9.20 - found many friends
on the vessel to see us off - with books,
fruit & flowers - we left the dock also 10
& passed the Hook & discharged pilot
at 12.10 - Glorious day - all anticipating
with some anxiety the result of
a big swell outside - Took a good
lunch soon after 1 - no sea - no
discomfort - Stateroom trunk running - after some trouble got the men
to work at the hold. Trunks & it was
soon found - Then contentment
reigned - After 6 when we took
dinner - much time consumed
in preparing the boy's fodder - he
seems to be in hilarious good
humor - but Bella was quite
used up from the start early this
mft. we all turned in soon
after 10 -

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 18. 1884.

Another deliciously quiet day,
but my head felt very heavy on
getting up - By request,amine officiated at the piano during service
in the cabin - then came lunch
after which I was obliged to give
up to my headache entirely which
became very violent - by 6 o'clk I
was in an agony between nausea &
noise from machinery - movement
of the ship & others in the stateroom
with me - about 10 o'clk I was
able to climb up to my own berth
& at 12 was able to get up & undress - About 2, before I got asleep,
the boy commenced & I had him
until after 3 - I did not get a wink
of sleep until near 6 when he
kindly allowed me about a quarter
of an hour
