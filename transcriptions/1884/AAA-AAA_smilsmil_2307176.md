## Left Page

### July, Thursday 24, 1884.

Very heavy rain in the mg, with appearance of continuing all day, so
I set to work in doors on a small painting of the subject of which I tried
to get a study yesterday afternoon —
The day proved to be showery with
intervals of delicious sunlight — I went
out on the Terrace & had a breezing before my lunch at 1 oclk. After that
again at my work until near 5, getting
only part of my canvas covered, in
a way that gives me some hope that
I can make something of it — Then
went out to walk with Annie — went
out on the Havre road — & up to the large
farm, then across to top of cliffs — Showers
north & south of us — glorious sky — very
strong wind — To dinner at 6.30 — and
washing up, etc — & to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 25, 1884.

Another rainy mg. Devoted
myself to working in doors on the
painting I commenced yesterday —
Took an airing with Annie just before
12 — lunched at 1 — At 2.30 went
out on the beach & worked until
5 on an oil sketch of boats — much
difficulty from sun & wind & the bad
working of my mill board — Then went
with Annie for a short walk up to the
culminating on North cliff — Dinner about
6.30 again — Nellie arr. from Paris —
after dinner I went for Dr. Fredelin to
come to the boy who has continued
troubles with his bowels — he came
between 8 & 9 — says the difficulty is
catarrhal (?) & gave a prescription that
I took to the druggists & had prepared —
then washed brushes — Read some [illegible]
Bacon's scraps & to bed before 11.
