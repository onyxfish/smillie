## Left Page

### January, Sunday 6, 1884

Very cold — very clear — Up
late — bl[illegible] — at 10 — almost
used up with a headache.
Lounged about until after 12
then walked up to Jennie's —
found her in the kitchen —
trouble with the cook. Home
at 1.45 — Dinner soon after 2
— Calverley came as invited —
Stayed until near 4 — I
then took a short nap —
Before 6 — went to Will's. Find
that things are not going as
smoothly there as could be
desired — both Mien & her boy
rather out of sorts. Home at 7 —
tea soon after — Well. wrote
letter home & took it to G. C.
Depot at 9.30 — To bed before
11.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 7, 1884

Another clear, cold day — To
studio before 10 — Spent some time
putting etchings into frames
then to work on "Stray Lambs",
getting on slowly over foreground
washing out & then in. Home
at 1.15 — back at 2.45 — worked,
as in the mf. — The last thing
I did being to wash out as
cleanly as I could a portion
of left hand lower corner so as to
be ready to begin anew to-morrow.
Mr. H. P. Gray called just at dusk.
at 5.30 I went to Glenham Hotel
& called on Mrs. Stuart Beatty — saw
also her daughter — Stuart & Dimock
scarce. Home at 6.30 — tea — on
lounge for some time — wrote
a note to Mrs. Beatty asking her
to excuse ceremony on Annie's
part & to make the first call —
After 9.30 went out & posted it and
took a short walk — Read a little
of The Republic of Plato & to bed
before 11. Did not get to sleep
until 1.30 — At 2. up with Jas. [illegible].
