## Left Page

### February, Monday 11. 1884.

Nasty weather again — perhaps
worse than ever — an inch or more
of snow, fell last night — & soon it
was a dirty slop over everything.
To studio before 10 — To work at once
on 24×40 "The Road to hellwood lot"
an old canvas, painted some
years ago. Changed the composition
materially & painted it after sunset
effect — Went to D.L.&W. exp. with
plans for Montrose, then home to dinner
abt — Annie at a lunch party at her
mother's, to Carl Sprague & wife — To
studio again at 3 & worked until
5, getting the canvas nearly cov-
ered — Cleaned up & took a rest —
Home before 7 — tea soon after —
Eveg. Annie read aloud from the
"Newcomes" — To bed before 11.
Raining hard.

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 12. 1884.

Fog, mist & cloud again — To studio
soon after 10 — Richter — the etching
printer of Phil. called & urged me
to let him have an etching plate
for a venture of his — 12 plates to be
pub. next summer, I to take my pay
out of the profits, if any — I did not
give him an answer. Worked on 24
×40 — Home to dinner abt — Emma
took dinner with us — back at
3 — Sister Kate came in — came
to N.Y. yesterday — staying with
Jennie — I worked until 5 — &
got things to hang together, but on
consulting with Geo. concluded
that I have some grave faults
of composition to correct — Rested
& home to tea at 7 — Every
day just very troublesome — so
accomplished but little —
I wrote a note to M. Condit
concerning his selling my Lake
George picture —
