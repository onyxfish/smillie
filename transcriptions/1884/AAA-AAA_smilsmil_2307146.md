## Left Page

### May, Sunday 25, 1884.

Another change — & a delightful one — cloudless sky & pleasant breeze.
Up soon after 6 — had my bath at 7.30
& 8.30. at 8 — Annie not able to get
up owing to severe headache and
lame back — she kept her bed un-
til 2 o'clk — then up & on deck. I
had many duties to perform, but
managed to get about an hour of
sleep before I had lunch abt. 2.0.
During the afternoon I wrote a letter
home — Annie on deck for some time.
Dinner at 6 — delicious evening —
on deck with Annie until after 9 —
much of the time with Mr. Gordon.
The Alaska passed us on her way west
at about 8. A fog came up about
midnight after which we had the
music of the fog whistle with little
cessation — the boy slept through most of
it but managed to behave badly again.

Days run, 338 miles.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 26, 1884.

Up at 6.30 — fog — whistling & sounding
— creeping along we got opposite the
mouth of Queenstown Harbor at about 9
where we lay & whistled for a tender
which came along in an hour or more
— Took off passengers (mostly steerage)
luggage etc — & at last — at almost
12 we got on our way again — The
day grew to be bright & beautiful —
the afternooneverf — being particular-
ly pleasant — The Channel being as
smooth as a river — after dinner
on deck with Annie until after
9 — then below & attended to domestic
matters — about 10 went on deck & had
a good walk in the dim Firelight.
To bed at about 11.30 — and had [illegible]
a poor night's rest between noises
aboard ship, incident to coming in port
and the boy.

Days run on the Chart, marked
279 m. to Queenstown.
