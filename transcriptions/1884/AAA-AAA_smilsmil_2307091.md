## Left Page

### February, Sunday 3, 1884

Charming day — B&b: soon after 9 — lounged about until after 12 — Then went to Charls — he has been confined to his room most of the past week by Rheumatism. Home to dinner at 2 — after that, a nap. At 5.30 went over to 34th St. & saw Wille — Ninim & her boy are getting along very slowly. Home & had tea about 7 — Rueth wrote note to Ticknor — Chas. H[illegible] Hart — & letter home. At 10 went to G.C. Depot — To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### February, Monday 4, 1884

Nasty day. overcast, raw east wind. spitting rain at night. Got to studio soon after 10 & at once to work on "The Pond's outlet — Montrose" — & did not stop until I went home to dinner at 1 — back at 2.30 — bothered by a call from a Mrs. Lasar — one of John Rolf's daughters, who gave me a message direct from God & wanted me to pay her a dollar for it. Stuck to my work until after 4.30 — could see no longer — had covered canvas but had not got the tone I want — too hot. VanDyke came in (& Studio) & I went over to N.A. with him to look at the etchings — back again to studio & "cleaned up" — home at 6.45 — Tea soon after 7 — Rueth, Annie read aloud for an hour from "The Newcomes" — To bed about 11.
