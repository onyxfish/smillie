## Left Page

### June, Thursday 26. 1884.

Pleasant & growing warmer — At 10.30
Geo & I started out — went to 179 Bone?
Perciret had a pleasant time calling on
Chas. Sprague Pierce until 11.40 — then, by
'bus, back to hotel. Annie & Nellie in late
from a shopping tour — At 2.30 Geo & I, by
'bus, went to the Luxemburg — Rode by the
side of a Priest, who spoke good English & with
whom I had an interesting conversation on the
subject of Republicanism, French & American.
From 3 to 5 in the galleries — was disappointed
at finding fewer pictures of high order of merit
than I had expected — The galleries are
exceedingly poor in lighting & accomodation.
Before dinner, Mr. H.f. Bacon called on us,
just rece'd from Étretat. After dinner
some time spent in looking over the
purchases of the day — another quiet
evening. To bed at 10, but called
up by master James for an hour
or so.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 27. 1884.

A hot day — At 10 Geo & I went
to H.f. Bacon's Studio by appointment
& spent the wef with him looking
over his studies & talking about
Étretat. Back to hotel in time
for lunch — After that took a long
nap. At 4 went to Palais Royale
& had my hair cut — back to hotel
& dressed in full Regalia — dinner
at 6 & at 7.30 Annie & I walked to
the Opera House — a short distance —
we had admirable seats 63 & 65 in the
amphitheatre — [illegible] churly was
well given & from 10.30 to 11.45 we had
the Grand Ballet of Sylvia — a spectacle
such as I never before witnessed. The
house is very grand. The vestibule and
foyer, wonderful — I enjoyed the whole
entertainment very much & with Annie
wandered all about through the lobbies
etc — back to hotel at 12 & to bed
at once.
