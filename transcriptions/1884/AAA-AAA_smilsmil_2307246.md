## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 17. 1884.

Overcast — to studio before 10 — with
help of James & Calverly got boxes of
paper & other heavy things into corner
where stove used to stand. To great
advantage of general appearance of
things — then to Am. Art galleries. Sketch
exhib., varnishing, met there Koehler,
Mallory & others — after 2 to Salmagundi
— met there Miss Maggie Woodward & others
Home to lunch at 1.15 — to studio at 2.30
made pastes & pasted together three
trunk boards upon which to mount
large W.C. Fanny Mulford Connell &
Souire Kling called. — About 5 I left
& walked home by way of 5th Ave. — in at
5.30 — Father just arr— from down town.
Dinner soon after 6 — Overf. started to
teach Father the letter game when Mother
Cook, Geo. & Valentine came in — then [illegible]
Oakey & Mr. O — they left at 10 — I
got to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 18. 1884.

Cold, overcast day with light, fine snow
beginning to fall about noon. To studio
before 10 — Rev. Mr. Lawrence of Syracuse,
late of P.K., met me at the door. Mr. Ernest
Knauff also called — Father came in
soon after — & stayed until after 12 —
then he went down town. I worked
on "The Edge of the wood" — painting portions
more solidly & adding a number of
sheets. Home at 1 — back at 2.15 worked
until 4 — home at 6 — dinner — after
that; sat in dining room & for an
hour, Father & Annie & I had games of
"Word making & taking" — then Geo. came
in — I kindled a fire in Father's bed-
room as a pleasant surprise for him.
Geo. left before 10 — then I fixed Father's
night cap & took him to his room all
bright & warm which pleased him very
much. To bed before 11. after writing
a letter to Connell.
