## Left Page

### July, Saturday 12. 1884.

Up at 6.30 — cooked a chop for myself &
had a good North American breakfast —
Ready to go to my work soon after 8 — Brisk
shower before that & as the day promised to
be unsettled I started out with pencils.
On my way up the South cliff stopped & made
a sketch — then to Folp & made a small one &
then on to farmstead & made one under the
apple trees in the farm yard — Near 1 o'clk when
I finished — walked down in intensely hot
sunshine — had a light lunch at home about
1.40 — after that a rest — Between 3 & 4 Annie
& I went to the old Church Yard & I got
fairly started on my W.C. Study when another
thunder storm came up & drove me off — back
home at 5.30 — To dinner soon after 6 —
quite a heavy shower — From 8.30 to 9.15
on the Piazza with Annie — beautiful sun
set & lovely sky — then home & until
10.30 occupied with domestic duties —
then to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 13. 1884.

Extremely hot, thunderous day — Up
at 8 o'clk — had my bkft. about 9 — Several
showers in the m'g & some heavy thunder.
About 10 I went out on the beach and
for some time watched the boats as they
came in & were hauled up on the beach.
Then home & went with Annie to the
beach & watched the bathers, but it was
too fearfully hot & glaring light, so I beat
a retreat at 11.30 — The rest of the party
went to the Hautville for their midday break
fast — I prepared my own at home at 1.
After that wrote awhile to Father & abt 15
went with Ge. to the Episcopal ch. That
is in the valley just outside of town. Found
a neat, new little building & a congregation
of somewhere between 50 & 75, nearly all women —
one man preached a very common place
sermon & we returned by a pleasant walk
a little before 6 — then to dinner. After
that, Annie & I spent some time on the
Piage — Mr. Danforth spent most of
the eveg. with us.     To bed at 10.30
