## Left Page

### March, Thursday 6, 1884

Cleared to be a delightful spring
day overhead — wretched, under
foot. To studio before 9 to work
on foreground & sheep "Pond's outlet"
went over it with a crackle of
brighter color — also worked on other parts.
Home at 1 — dinner — after that was going
with Annie to Reception in Sherwood Bldg
studios — great bustage — water in cellar —
had to go for plumber instead — To
studio at 4 & worked for about an
hour — then a rest. Home at 6.30 — In —
wrote a letter to Cottrell — dressed —
at 9 went to Wm. Rob. Gordon's Recption
as usual — good company & exceedingly
pleasant — Matthews Arnold, the lion —
His face & manner is very unpossessing to me. Had a chat with a
son of Edwin Arnold's, just arriv'd — much
Talk among the centenians of the fitness
of the Gordon house for Century needs —
Home at midnight & right to
bed.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 7, 1884

Dismal day with occasional
spittings of rain — Up soon after 8
feeling the worse for wear — stomach
the seat of discomfort — ate a light
bkfst, feeling no better, decided
to stay at home — Spent most of
the morning on lounge — a light
dinner soon after mid — Spent
the afternoon much as I had
done the morning, except that
I did some reading. Every[illegible]
Annie read for awhile aloud
— To bed at 10.30 — Rain falling
& freezing.
