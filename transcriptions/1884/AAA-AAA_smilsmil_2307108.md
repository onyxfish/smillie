## Left Page

### March, Monday 10. 1884.

Bright day, with a wincing into in the air — To studio before 10 & to work on "Pond's outlet" — oiling it all out and painting generally over it but without being able to improve it as I wished — home to dinner at 1 & back before 3 — Geo. came in & sat with me & instigated me to heighten light & color in various parts of the picture greatly to its advantage — mounted some etching pfts that I have orders for. This mf went to N.A. & saw Galt about pfts sold at the late exhn. Home by 6.30, having walked up Mad. Ave. where Barnum's procession was forming. Annie's old school teacher to tea, with two Misses Clinch, Rosalie & Helen Butler, Aunt Anna & Eurica J. & Mother Cook. Later in the evef — Edwd Cook & wife, Walter & wife — Geo. Cook — Miss William — Mr. Emerson, Mr. & Mrs. Herbert Morse — Old Mr. Gibbons — Ge. & Nellie came — company left before 10.30 & we got to bed about an hour later.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 11. 1884.

East wind & overcast — To studio before 10 — Part of mf — getting etching pfts into frames. Annie came down to see my picture & we walked home together to dinner — Kate came in also. Back to studio at 3. Packed a pf. of Pebble Head Neck Causeway to send to Richter of Phila. Made part of a pen sketch of Pond's outlet for Kurtz's Academy notes. Will come in between 4 & 5 o'clk. about Bank note co's work — Then I went to Hps. Co. & to 10th St. Studio Bldg. with etching pfts. Home & tea at 7 — At 8.30 went up to Charlie's to comfort him. His engagement with Ida Provost was declared "off" last friday night — I am very sorry for the poor fellow — his comb is badly cut — but Geo. & Nellie were there & we gave him lots of good advice. Home at 10 — to bed before 11.
