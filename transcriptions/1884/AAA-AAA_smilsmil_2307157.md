## Left Page

### June, Monday 16. 1884.

overcast & growing cooler until night —
then some rain — To market as usual & got
strawberries — Helpi etc — & at 10.30 went on
a hunt for Mr. H.F. Bacon — found his
studio & apartments at 157 Faubourg S. Honoré
near Rue de Berri — He was not at home
having gone to Étretat, but had a pleasant
call with his wife — Back to hotel at 12 &
had lunch — after that, a rest & before
I went to Salon, where I worked until
after 5, completing my circuit of the galleries
— to hotel at 6 & had dinner — Evelf.
Wm. Lippincott called to tell us what
he had learned of Crecy — a little country
place that he recommends for sketching.
I promised to go down there with him
to-morrow if it does not rain — Later,
Mr. Wilson de Mega called & spent
the evelf with us until 9.30. To
bed before 11 — & up at 4 a.m. to
sit with Master James for 3/4 of
an hour while he recovered from
a tantrum.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 17. 1884.

overcast & quite cool — Up at 7.30, went
to market & got strawberries — Helpi at
8.30 — at 9 Lippincott came for me &
we drove to R.R. Station (d'Est) and
took 9.40 train to Esbly, getting there
a little before 11 — then on top of shaky
old omnibus & got to Crécy soon after 12 —
went at once to Ma? Cinot's & had a shocking
bad breakfast & then looked about. The
house presents some desirable features —
the "gardens" are pleasant & the River at the
rear quite picturesque — had a talk with
Mad. as to terms — was not favorably impres-
sed — two doubtful women at table — walked
about the town — charmingly quaint — the
water ways & women washing clothes — at
3 started in omnibus to return — quite cold —
enjoyed the ride — through several little
towns — Picturesque horses, wagons & drivers —
left Esbly at 4.27 — got to Paris at 5.45 & to
hotel at 6 — dinner — Evelf. Stanley
Middleton called. To bed before 11.
