## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 27, 1884

*North Western Hotel - Room 194 - Liverpool -*

Beautiful day — Bath soon after 7. Bkfs.
at 8 — before which time we were surprised
by the arrival of a tug for passengers & their
luggage, we lying at anchor outside of the bar
from 3. AM. Everything hurly burly & great con-
fusion, as the tug had been expected by no one
supposing that we would go up with
the tide at about 10 AM. We managed to
hustle things together & were the last ones on
board the tug at about 9 — & in a little
more than an hour we reached the Cus-
toms Landing Stage — there the passengers of
another steamer arr. just before us, causing
a delay to us of at least an hour — I got the
small parcels passed & Gel went with them
& the rest of the party to the North Western Hotel
& establishing them returned to me — I got
the trunks all passed & at 12.30 we up
to the hotel & at 1 had dinner — At 3. Gel &
I went to Inman office & secured seats & rooms
for return — & did other little messages — back
in an hour & had a nap — then repacking — Tea
after 7 — Evg. finished repacking & got to
bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 28, 1884

*at the "Peacock" Rowsley -*

Pleasant day — altho' not very bright
& almost cold — Bkfs. before 9 — then
Gel & I took a cab & drove with our tks.
trunks to Inman Baggage Office — then to
Inman Pass. Office — Saw Mr. Kensey — no
news of Annie's lost watch — then to Cus-
toms Landing & got Bella's bag & bundle
then to Bank of Liverpool — & to a brokers —
back to Hotel about 12 — finished pack-
ing & had lunch at 1 — Saw all the
Gordons & bid them good bye — at
2.45 we left the hotel with all our
belongings in or on a bus & drove
to Midland R. R. where we took 3
o'clk train to Rowsley — At Millersdale,
where we changed, saw Marquand, [illegible]
got to R. at 5.11 & went at once to the
Peacock, a charming old house of
Mrs. Cooker, Proprietress — Miss Dawson
in charge — we were delighted with
everything — the outside of the house & the
gardens being very quaint & picturesque
after tea Annie & I strolled about —
Evg. chatted with a Manchester cler-
gyman & to bed soon after 10.
