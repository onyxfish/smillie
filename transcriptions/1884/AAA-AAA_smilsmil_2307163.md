## Left Page

### June, Saturday 28. 1884.

Another hot day, making us anxious to get
through with our work here & out of Town —
Wrote a note to Mr. G.P. Healy asking for Tibure
de Marc's address — to Drexel's & got more
money — then with [illegible] out to C.F. Reinhart —
147 Boul? Villiers & had a pleasant call with
him until near 12. Then to R.R. depot at Place
Courcelles & by ceinture to Gare St. Lazare — &
thence to hotel at 12.30 & had lunch — After
that, a nap — & at 3 went with Annie &
Nellie to their dressmaker in the Rue Rivoli
M. [illegible] — waited for more than half an hour
for them, then went to Louvre & wandered
through the Marine museum to where the
Regnault & Delacroix pictures are — with them
until 5 — then took seat on top of tram car &
rode along the quay to the Bastille — & thence by
'bus by the Boulevards to the Rue Caumartin
& hotel — a very good way to see one phase of
Paris. Dinner — eveng. still very warm.
& feeling tired, got to bed before 10.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 29. 1884.

A hot day, but cooler at eveng. About 10 o'clk
I went to the Hotel Binda, just off the Ave. l'
Opera & saw Miss [illegible] who ret? from
Switzerland last night — there until
12.15 — then to hotel & had [illegible]. After
that wrote letters until about 4 o'clk
when Annie & I went out for an airing.
we climbed to the top of a 'bus & rode
along the Boulevards to the Bastille —
there took Tramway & rode along the
ways to the island, then crossed
over to Notre Dame — on our way, passed
the Morgue & went in — no bodies exposed.
Then to the Cathedral, which we found
closed — then to the flower market where
we found exposed all sorts of birds, cats,
dogs, rabbits, rats & mice — we took
voiture & back to hotel at 6 — & had dinner.
Eveng. finished writing & to bed soon
after 10.
