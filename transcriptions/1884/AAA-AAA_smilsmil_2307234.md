## Left Page

### November, Sunday 23, 1884

A dismal like sort of day with promise of a howling storm which came along at night time — not cold but with plenty of wind & rain. Bkf. at 9 — part of mng. arranging the books in parlor book cases — Mr. Sprague sent. of Buffalo called — dinner at 2 — about 3.30 I felt so heavy that I took a nap of an hour — Before I wrote part of a letter to Father congratulating him upon reaching his 77th Birthday — Tea at 7 — after that finished my letter & at 8.15 went to depot with it & posted it — The weather was so bad that at last Annie determined not to go to the Morse's where we had been asked to spend the eveg. — Her cough still continues harrassing — the boy's has not improved — I read aloud for a little while — to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 24, 1884

Clear, cold day — To studio at 10 — fire in stove out — delay while fat. kindled it — after 11 got to work on a small color sketch for my big "Normandy Cliffs" painting — at 12.30 went to Bank & drew a little money — home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked until 4, getting my little sketch done in a way that encourages me — Then to Newman's & to Grady's — ordered 2 or 3 small frames — Home to dinner at 6 — At 8 Annie & I went to mother Cook's — Will — Sue & Nelia there — at 9 we went to Walter's — home at 10 & to bed soon after 11.
