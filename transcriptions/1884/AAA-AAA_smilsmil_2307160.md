## Left Page

### June, Sunday 22, 1884

Delightful day — very cool — in the
afternoon a smart shower with thunder.
Up late — after 8 — so that, after going
to market & getting my b'kft. it was
9.45 — At 10.45, with Annie & the boy
I rode to 21 Rue de Berri — American
Chapel — Annie rode on, for an airing
for herself & boy — I went in & heard Rev. Mr.
Beard preach a good sermon & heard
familiar hymns sung. After service I
went into No. 5 — Detaille's & de Neuville's
Panorama of the battle of Champigny —
a startling piece of realistic work.
To hotel at 1.30 where I prepared lunch
for myself. After that, wrote a letter
home — After the shower I took a short
walk with Annie & went into the Madeleine.
Dinner at 6 — Ev'g. lounging about
— To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 23, 1884

Very pleasant day. After many delays,
sometime after 10 — Geo., Nellie, Annie
& I, on top of Boul't. Haussmann tramway
car went to the Trocadero, hoping to go to
the top of one of the towers, but to our disgust
found that the elevator did not run until
after 1 o'clk. We went to a cemetery, just
opposite & wandered there for awhile, then
back, as we came, in time for lunch. After
a rest, Annie & I went to Theatre Francaise
& bought tickets for the ev'g. Then I left
her & met Geo. on the quay & we went
over to the School of Fine Arts & along the
way looking at the old shops — then took bus
to the Odeon & for awhile in gardens of the Lux-
embourg — back to hotel at 6 & had dinner — At
8 we all went to the Theatre Francaise — a stuffy
old place where we had 9 franc seats, &
could see, for the people who sat in front of us
& almost stifled, from heat & foul air — There
were three comedies on the bill, we waited
to see two (see pocket of diary) — There was little
action & much dialogue — at 10.45 we left for
hotel — at 11.15 I went to bed in little room
not having Jot. just. I went & held him
from 3.40 to 5.10 when again I went to bed —
