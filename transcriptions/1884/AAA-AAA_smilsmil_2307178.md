## Left Page

### July, Monday 28. 1884.

Charming day — spent the morning on the beach making a small water color sketch of some boats — Home to lunch at about 1.15 — after that worked over my sketch until near 4 o'clk. Putting in a boat that had been moved away while I was making my sketch — etc etc —
Then I went out with Annie &
walked up the hill to the little village of S. Clair — The afternoon was warm & sunny but very hazy —
we saw many charming pictures looking over the broad fields of ripening grain — before our return the sky was all overcast & a cold wind was blowing from the sea — To dinner soon after 6 — then rains — Evg, reading until 9.30 — To bed soon after 10.30

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 29. 1884.

overcast with clearing later, but never quite fair — Raw, damp wind from the sea. [illegible] feeling very seedy after a bad night's rest — worked for a while on W.C. sketch of some boats — then with pencil & paper went part way up the hill & made sketches looking south, over town & cliff — to get the composition for a drawing that I hope to make — Back at 12.30 & cooked a bit of steak for myself — After that took a nap — & after that, yet feeling very unlike work. I read for a while — also, went out to the market place to watch a french "Cheats John" — or Stiverant auctioneer, on the tail of his cart. While the others went to dinner I prepared for myself a simple supper, hoping to get the better of my indigestion — Evg — Annie & I had a little High, Low, Jack. To bed at 11 but master James got into a tantrum soon after & I did not finally get settled for the night until 1 o'clk.
