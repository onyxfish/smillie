## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 19, 1884

overcast day, with a niggimg
rain at night. Got to Studio
before 10 — & to work repainting
the little picture that I painted
last Spring — "A view near Marble
head — or Crowninshield's Point —
overlooking the Sea" — Covered
it solidly, making it much
lighter & cooler in tone. At
12.30 went to Bank — then home
— Emma J. dined with us — back
at 2.30 & before 5 finished the
painting — greatly improving it,
I think. Then to Dickee's and
Rode the "Brown Cob" mare for
20 minutes — walked home — took
a scrub down & sponge bath
& had tea at about 7.30 — a
rather stupid evef. wrote
a note to Richter.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 20, 1884

Rain in the mtg, but soon cleared
with strong wind from W. & N.W.
To studio before 10 — To work. Re-
painting old picture of 3 cows
in a meadow, done a couple of
years ago or more. Home at 4
back before 3 — Worked as long
as I could see, getting landscape
all in & one cow — an improvement
on the old work but nothing
very creditable. Home at 6 ½.
Tea — evef. Young W. Ordway
called — after 9 o'clk, Annie
read aloud for an hour —
then to bed.
