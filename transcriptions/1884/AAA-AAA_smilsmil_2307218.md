## Left Page

### October, Monday 20, 1884

Clear & very warm — Got to studio
before 10 & spent the m'f. arranging
contents of book case & its drawers.
Home to lunch at 1 — back soon
after 2. & spent the rest of the day
trying to get other things in order.
Home to dinner at 6 — the first
experimental dinner at 6, instead
of in the middle of the day — About
8, Mr. Nash, our neighbor in the rear
called about the fence between us —
Later Mr. Thos. H. Wood & a friend called
about Phil. Rehearsal tickets said to
be in my name — the same that
Mr. Hoadley badgered me about last
winter. To bed at 11, but J. just
required my company for a little while
at 11.30.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 21, 1884

Another very warm, clear day — Did
not get to studio until 10 — Spending
some time with carpenters & plumbers —
Ordered a pair of boots f O'Neil on 11th
Ave. Spent the m'f. overhauling
closet & the piles of truck therein —
Home to lunch at 1 — back at 3
& by 6 have got things so far in order
that I hope to get to work at some
thing more artistic than house clean-
ing tomorrow. Home to dinner at
6 — at 7.30 had a call for half an
hour from our opposite neighbor and
fellow passenger on the City of Berlin
last May, Mr. Levy or Levitt, (I don't
know which) wrote notes to Veré — to
Mr. Ticknor — to J. Neely Jnr. & to Am. Art
Assn. To bed at 10.30.
