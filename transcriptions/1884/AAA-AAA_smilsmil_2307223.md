## Left Page

### October, Thursday 30. 1884.

Dismal rainy day — carpenters got out of the house at noon for a while — the plumbers got through on W[illegible]. I got to studio at 9.30 & spent the m[illegible] working over two of my last summer studies — home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — afternoon on studies & mounting some of the large charcoal sketches — worked very steadily all day — home to dinner at 6 — Eve[ning]. Reading — to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 31. 1884.

Over cast, with rain & drizzle. more or less wet — Got to studio about 10 & to work on oil study from Cliff near Telegraph Station looking south — also worked on other studies in general way — home to lunch at 1 — back before 3 — worked on study of [illegible] boats & house at 6 — Dinner party — Julie & Preston Spring — Emma J — Geo & Walter C & Mr. Valentine — all Cleveland men — Blaine demonstration at night — a detachment in front of the house for more than an hour waiting for their turn to fall into the grand procession down 5th Ave. The noise of Bands of music filled all uptown streets until after 2. A.M. I went to bed at about midnight.
