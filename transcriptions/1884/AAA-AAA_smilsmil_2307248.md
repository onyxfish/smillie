## Left Page

### December, Sunday 21. 1884.

The nastiest sort of a nasty day —
snow in the nth. followed by
rain that poured down by night
time — a late blfs — & did not
go outside of the house all day.
Dinner at 2 — & snooge in the
afternoon — tea at 7 — Reading
aloud & some writing in the
evef. & to bed at 11.45 —

## Right Page

### December, Monday 22. 1884.

Gloomy day, dark with fog &
rain — After 10 when I got to
studio — Definitely made up
my mind not to try to finish
large W.C. in time for the Exbtn —
so, set to work planning some
smaller work. A cousier of Stephli
Parrish called, wishing for some
instructions in W.C. Stretched
paper — home to lunch at 1 —
back at 2.30 — P.M. so dark
that I could do but little work.
I pasted some unbound works
into neatly kep. to put away —
At 5. went over to Louis Tiffany's
& called on Saml. Coleman & wife —
Annie met me there — They return
home to-morrow. Home to dinner
at 6 — Evef a. Read aloud from
"Village on the Cliff" — finishing
it — to bed about 10.30.
