## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 2. 1884.

Grey mtf. — showery day — with passages of beautiful sunlight &
cloud effects — Afternoon. went
to beach & made notes of figures
there — fishermen — everybody talking
of the cremation last night, on the beach
of a young Bombay man — one of a party
of Indians, students at Oxford, who are
spending their vacation here — He died
suddenly & last night, at midnight,
with great secrecy, his body was burned
by his companions, at the foot of the cliffs.
Stt Bacon was present & describes it as something very dramatic. At 11, Annie came &
we went up to the crucifix where I
made a hasty pencil sketch — Night
wind — home soon after 12. — Dinner
(or lunch) at 1 — after that, again on the
beach & made more notes of figures —
Annie again came — a lot of letters from
home — a tremendously heavy shower just
before 6 — So dinner soon after — & after that
home — Evening, stupidly doing nothing
until after 10, then to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 3. 1884.

Brilliant, beautiful mtg. — I went to my
w.c. apple tree study & spent the mtg.
at that & at about 12.30 finished it and
think that altho' it is not very good, it
is the best w.c. I have done this summer.
Dana (of Phila.) worked near me & Dr.
Oliver (of Boston) not far off — Annie
came for me at about noon. — Dinner
(or lunch) soon after 1 — Then, as my
head felt uncomfortable, I took a nap.
Afternoon, chilly & overcast. At 3 o'clk.
Annie & the boy, Bella & I went out for
a ride — went out on the Grand Val road
to near Villanville by a loop & then back
on Grand Val road. The air was rather cool
for comfort — the sky was leaden — now & then a
drop of rain fell — the boy was cross & the
horse was tired — but we had as good a time
as could have been expected — back at 4.30 —
then rain began to fall — I did not go to dinner
with Annie but prepared a light meal for myself at home — steady rain in the eveg. — Before
10 the boy began his pranks & at 12 allowed me
to go to bed.
