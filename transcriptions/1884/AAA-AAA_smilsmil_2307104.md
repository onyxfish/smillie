## Left Page

### February, Friday 29. 1884.

Extremely cold, with fierce
wind — Ther. down to 5°. To
studio before 10 & towards retouching
sky on old Sep. afternoon picture —
Willie came in with photos of a
subject that he is anxious to have
me draw — Spent some time —
Home at 1 — back at 3 & worked
until 5 on foreground, without
being able to get what I wanted.
Home before 6 — Man from Grady's
hung some etchings in Reception &
Dining Room — High tea soon after 7.
Emma, Grace, Mary, J., Sley, Home,
Chas. Collins, Willie, Geo. Cook &
Jno. Valentines — All seemed to
enjoy themselves — They left
abt 10.30 or before!! — & we got
to bed at about 11.30.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 1. 1884.

Not so cold nor so windy as yes-
terday — but still a very cold day —
Up early — Before 9.30 got to E.
& W's — the Printers, in Canal St — and
spent the rest there by the side
of a printer, getting nine started
upon the proving of several of
my etchings — Home before 1 —
To studio at 3 — made a charcoal
sketch on canvas of "Fallow Field"
subject & put it in with trunks.
& Brown — out of sorts — went over
to Water Color Mtg — last day —
Home before 6.30 — tea — Miss Williams
with us — Miss Toshay came afterwards
— They, with Annie went to hear Walter
Arnhold's last lecture in this country.
Later, I saw him at the Century —
where I went before 9 & stayed
until after 11. To bed a little
before midnight.
