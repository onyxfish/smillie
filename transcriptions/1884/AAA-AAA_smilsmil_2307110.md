## Left Page

### March, Friday 14, 1884

Nasty day, with driving drizzle,
heavy rain & light snow — To
studio before 10 & to work on S.A.
statue — Finished it by 12 o'clk &
then mounted it — Went to bank
& deposited $202.50, the proceeds
of sale of "Stray Lambs" — Home at
1 — dinner, & at 2, at Phil.
Rehearsal — Annie following
soon after — After rehearsal
went to Jessup's & selected a
suit of ready made clothes —
also, a coat for horseback riding.
Then to studio & home at 6.30 —
Tea at 7 — Everg. Annie
read aloud from "The Newcomes" —
To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 15, 1884

Nasty, wet m'f. Soon clearing to be
a beautiful day — To studio before
10 — Stephenson, a young man from the
A.B.N. Co., called with a card from
Will & took up some time — The
rest of the m'fg. was taken up
with writing letters — etc — Home
at 1, after having gone to Fifth
Ave. Theatre & secured seats
for "The Princess Ida" on Monday
night — After dinner, Mr. Murray
called & when she left, Annie & I
went to 290 5th Ave., where the
Pastel Club makes its first exhibit.
After that, went to [illegible], in 14th St.
& looked at furniture — Then to
Macy's — A. went home & I, to studio.
Home at 6.30 — Everg. to Phil.
concert. Walked home at 10.30
to bed soon after 11.
