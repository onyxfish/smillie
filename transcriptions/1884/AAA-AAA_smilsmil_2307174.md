## Left Page

### July, Sunday 20, 1884

overcast & windy — Rain in the m'g.
I spent most of the m'g. in doors
& shortly before 12. went on to the
north cliff & wandered for some
distance finding some very interes-
ting foreground stuff — Home to
lunch soon after 1 — Afternoon,
wrote a letter home to Father.
Sat on the beach awhile before
dinner with Annie — Dinner as
usual & a short marketing stroll
after it — Ev'g., finished letter
to Father & got to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 21, 1884

Wet, windy & unpleasant — very fine
rain nearly all the m'g. & heavy wind
all the p.m. — Worked in-doors, making
charcoal sketch on canvas from a
pencil sketch & tinting it with color,
preparatory to painting it out of doors —
Lunch at home at 1 — new arrangement
J.D. & his family left (or lunch) at the hotel —
Annie & I lunch at home — had a breeze
from M.S.J. as I was preparing lunch.
— After noon, read awhile in H'y Bacon's
book — then Annie & I went out — & as the
tide was low, went under the great
arch & around to the other beach — I
went on under the second arch & made
a couple of pencil sketches — we climbed
zig-zig steps to the top of the cliff and
then home at 5 having had a delightfully
exhilarating walk & climb — Dinner soon
after 6 — after that, for awhile on the beach
where there was quite a heavy sea —
Ev'g wrote a letter to Arthur Turnure.
