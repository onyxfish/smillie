## Left Page

### March, Sunday 16, 1884

Slight snow flurry in the m'g.
then a splendid day — Bkfst.
soon after 9 — reading & lounging
until noon. Then walked to
Central Park — about there &
back at 2. Miss Woods &
Miss Abbott there — dinner
at once — our guests left at
4.30. Then with Annie, & the boy
went to Mother Cook's in 39th St.
Back at 6 — I took another short
walk, on my way back stopped
in at Walters to see sketch of
a plan for Montrose. Home at
7 & had tea — Ev'g. wrote letter
home & one to de Schweinitz —
At 9, went alone to Mother Cook's
saw Edith Wright there & had a
little talk concerning music &
art — also to G. C. D. circt. letter —
Then home & to bed soon after
11.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 17, 1884

Pleasant day out of doors but
unpleasant enough for me, as
I was in bed with an old-
fashioned headache until about
6 p.m. when I got up and
had something to eat — Eve'g.
Annie read aloud from the
"Newcomes" for awhile — I
did not go to bed again
until about 1.30.

Annie got tickets for Circus did. Changed to tomorrow eve'g.
