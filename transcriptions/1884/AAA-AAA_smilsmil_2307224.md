## Left Page

### November, Saturday 1. 1884.

Pleasant day — about 10 when I got to studio & spent much of the mng.
in putting away the many things out of place & in preparing for the repainting of the old picture "Evening Shadows" 33×44 & other work.
Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 & charcoaled on 20×29 "Palaise d'Aval, [illegible]" — for water color.
Home at 6 & had dinner. Will. K. called at 8 about a walk on Election day — at 9 I went to the Century monthly meeting & had a very pleasant time — walked up home at midnight — Remains of the Cleveland & Tammany processions everywhere. Right to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 2. 1884.

Mild, overcast day — fine moonlight night. — [illegible] before 9 — spent the mng. until 12.30 reading & writing, then out for a walk — met Tom Jessenden & walked with him for half an hour, then on up to Central Park & out at 72nd St. & 5th Ave. gate — Home to dinner at 2. — Spent the p.m. with Annie & the boy — he seems well but coughs very severely — our fears are very strong that it is whooping cough. Wrote letter home & took it to G.S. Depot — then to Studio & fixed fire — home at 7.15 & had tea. — Dwelt more reading & writing & to bed before 11.
