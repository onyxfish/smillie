## Left Page

### July, Friday 18. 1884.

Superb mts — brilliant sky effects following a rain — About 10 o'clk. I determined to go to the top of South cliff with my oil colors & try to get a sky effect — nearly 11 when I was ready to go to work — from that time until 1, I fought against every inconvenience, especially of wind — I strapped umbrella to my leg & anchored my easel, but everything tugged for freedom — At last I gave up, perfectly worn out — got home at 1.30 & had lunch — a rest after that — then worked awhile on mts study & got it into some shape. Then lay down & took a nap — up a little before 6 & went to dinner — Eve'g. looking over H. H. Bacon's book "A Parisian Year." Later Walter Davis called. — At 10 o'clk, when ready to go to bed — Jas. just got into a tantrum which kept me until 11.30 — & up again from 4 to 5 — [illegible] like all possessed.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 19. 1884.

Grey mts — head feeling unlike work. I went out with umbrella & sketch book and walked up the hill, N.E. of Town, between the great & the little valleys — found some charming stuff for studies — walked until I came out upon the road to [illegible]. Then returned & crossed country to the road in the little valley and so home at about 12.30 — Just a heavy shower of rain fell during my walk. Took a rest before lunch soon after 1. — Spent the afternoon trying to paint a sky seen out of my windows — at 5 went out to the beach with Annie — until 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. I walked along the beach to the tunnel to the top, on the north side of town — Home at 9 — made up the accts for the week & discussed them.
