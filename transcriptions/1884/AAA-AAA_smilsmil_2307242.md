## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 9, 1884

Fine day with two rain squalls in the afternoon — To studio at 10 &
altho' I did not feel in working trim I determined not to put off
any longer the commencement of my big painting — so I pitched into the
sky &c. — Kate came in at 12.30.
Had a letter from Mary concerning father's
condition — he is much run down & she
is worried about him. Home to lunch
at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked on sky
as long as I could see — feel quite
encouraged — Home at 6 — Dinner —
from 8 to 9 o'clk. Annie read aloud
from "Village on the Cliff" — I wrote a
letter to Father urging him to visit
us — Then went to Nathan Cook's with
Annie — Mr. Sprague, Miss Williams, Nellie,
Ed. & Mary, Walter & Mary & Valentine
there — Bazique until 10.30 — home
& to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 10, 1884

Beautiful day — To studio at 10, still
out of sorts but pumped all the energy
that I could into the sea of my big
picture — Plumbers at work putting
ventilator pipe from my basin to the
roof — Home at 1 — back at 3 — worked
until 4 — then rested — home at
6 — Chase & Kate to dinner — Chase
described his sudden illness during
last night. At 8.30 we went to
the Mad. Ave. Theatre & saw
the farce of "The Private Secretary"
by C.H. Hawtrey — It was very
absurd & we had a hearty laugh.
Out at 10.30 — Home before 11 &
right to bed.
