## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 5, 1884

Bright & breezy — Got to studio before
10 & to work on 33×44 repainting
on the trees — Beman came & I paid
my rent — Home to lunch at 1 —
Paperhangers commenced work in
back parlor-bed-room — Back to studio
at 2.15 & worked until 4.30 — then,
as I could see no longer, I cleaned
up & home before 6 — After dinner
called on my neighbor about hanging
his clothesline on our fence — About
8 Annie & I went to her mother's —
just home from a visit to Boston.
Will Cook with her — Ed[?] C. & his
wife — Marie — Geo. & Valentine there —
Home at 10 — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 6, 1884

Clear & colder — 10 o'clk. when I
got to studio — To work on 33×44
generally — fussing about the
figure of the milkmaid in partic-
ular — Home at 1 — back at 2.15
& repainted the figure entirely
making it quite light — Could
not see to work much after 4 —
went in to Tom Smith's for a
while about 5 — he is sick and
alone — Then to Schans's & saw
again Daurat's painting that
I saw in the Paris Salon. Spanish
groups of singers "The Duarlette" —
Home to dinner at 6 — Busy —
Geo. Cook came in for a little
while — Read aloud a chapter
in "Village on the cliff" — To bed
about 11.
