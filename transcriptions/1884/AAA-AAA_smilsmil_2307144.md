## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 21, 1884

A different kind of day — cloudy,
w. n.easterly, considerable mo-
tion of the vessel, to which many
succumbed. Gel among the no.
The duties of the day were many
— when not engaged trying to help
Gel, Bella, our nurse — preparing
food for the boy & sick — I walked
the deck against a strong wind
& got pretty well tired out.
Trouble with the engines — a
hot journal — had to slow up &
lost some miles — a ship on the
horizon in the mist but no other
sign of life — we got to bed
at about 10.

The run at noon 331.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 22, 1884

Clearer day — wind a little fresher
& more due east. The boy behaved
very tolerably well during the night
& I got but little sleep. Bella
so sick that she did not put
in an appearance until after
8 — I enjoyed my bath as usual
& a. & I had our [illegible] before &
after morning duties I crept
into my bunk & had a nap
that refreshed me — Gel got
up on deck for a little while
but was soon back in his bunk
— very wretched — about noon a
Spanish steamer passed South &
going in same direction — lunch
at 1 — another nap — feeling headachy
so that I was in no humor to walk
in the strong wind — ate little dinner —
Evening in saloon with Cap. Watkins
& Gent. Thomas of Portland, Me. To bed
at 10.30 & had a first-rate night &
so did the boy.
Day's run — 312.
