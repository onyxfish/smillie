## Left Page

### August, Friday 29. 1884.

Brilliant m'g — work two or three showers which did not prevent
me from putting in a good m'k work on my apple tree study in
W.C., which I did not quite finish but got so far along that
I am encouraged to think it is the best I have done this summer —
Home at 1.30 & had lunch — After-
noon — Annie & I went up on the
St. Clair Road — high wind — I took
pencils & paper & made sketch of
a group of trees & bit of distance —
Then we went across to road in
Petit Val & home just before 6 —
As I did not feel very well, I did
not go to hotel to dinner but got
tea for myself at home. Eve'g
quietly reading papers — near
11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 30. 1884.

A regular drenching storm —
worked all the m'g on unfinished
study in oil of boats on the beach —
about 12 o'clk. went out for a little
while for an airing but was soon
driven back by the rain — lunch
soon after 1 — & then again at work
on my study which I finished at
about 4.30 — then, feeling uncomfort-
able about the head, I put on my
rubber coat & went out for a walk
up the Grand Val & then by little
road to the right; up the hill through
woods towards Tuiles — out on Havre
road & home at 6 — Then to dinner
with Annie — Eve'g. Annie
read aloud to me from Century Mag. —
to bed soon after 10.
