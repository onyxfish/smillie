## Left Page

### August, Monday 25, 1884

At last, a storm — the first one since we
came here — gentle rain in the m'g.
increasing with wind until at night
it was a regular driving norther. After
b'kf. I went to the beach & wandered
there for some time, then in & for awhile
I worked over some of the pencil sketches
in my pocket sketch book. Lunch
soon after 1 — & then I took hold of an
unfinished W.C. Study of old thatched
cottage & finished that up. About
5 M. & Mrs. H.F. Bacon & Miss Bryant —
(daughter of Wm. Cullen B.) called to see
our studies — a very pleasant call —
they left at 6 & we went to hotel to
our dinner — after it I went to P.O.
to Mechanics Library for Annie & down
on beach to take a breezying & see the
rising sea. Eve'g. read paper for
awhile — to bed about 10.30 — storm
quite severe.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 26, 1884

Brilliant breezy m'g. thought at first that
I could not work out of doors but when I
went with Ge°. to the farmyard on the house
& croquet I found some old apple trees
so sheltered that I returned at once to
the house & got my W.C. traps. I tried
to work on a piece of paper that had not
been stretched & could not do it, so again
I ret'd. to the house. at 11.30 & got paper &
pencils & returning to the orchard made
a pencil sketch of a much contorted tree.
Home at 1.30 & had lunch. — the afternoon
was bleak & cloudy so that I did not
try to work out of doors — Annie & I wandered
about for awhile & bought some photos.
To dinner soon after 6 — Eve'g. squaring
up accts. with Ge°. who is preparing
to start on his Holland journey to-morrow.
To bed before 11.
