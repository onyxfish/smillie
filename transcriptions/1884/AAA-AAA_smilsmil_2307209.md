## Left Page

### October, Thursday 2. 1884.

*on board S.S. "City of Chicago" State Room 17–20*

Beautiful day — Belp at 8.30 — nearly 10 before Annie & I were ready to start out — went to the Walker Art Gallery & spent the m't after 12 there. There is no permanent exbt'n — also a very large Autumn exbt'n, numbering over 2000 works — oil, water col & etchings — classified. From Royal A. — Promo Institute & W.C. Soc'y & Soc'y of Painter Etchers — consequently I saw many pictures that I saw in London in the Spring. The exbt'n of etchings very poor — Pennell & Parrish seeming to carry away the honors in number & position. Dinner at 1.30. The last hours employed in squaring up & getting our 7 trunks & as many more bags and bundles off. To the Pier at 3.20 — tug took us to steamer at 4 — met W. Galt on board, & at about 5 we started. Jolly. The Welsh mts did not show fully — clouds — eve'g a dash of rain. Then fine moonlight. Got to bed about 10 but the devil took possession of master James & neither of us had much sleep during the night. Our State Room is comfortable but I do not like the arrangement of the ship generally & it is overrun with water bugs — Capt. Watkins kindly gave us seats at his Table.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 3. 1884.

Beautiful day — took my bath at 7.30 & my Belp about an hour later — don't like the cooking as well as on the Berlin — nor our service — very little sea — ship steady — got to Queenstown at about noon & expected the new arrangement of accelerated mail service to go into effect, but it did not — we lay there until 4 for the mail — We left Rock point at 4.55 — Capt. offered to let us go ashore — much amused by the boat loads of women with wares of all kinds, from apples to bog-oak bracelets & Irish lace, that assailed the ship & climbed on, even, by ropes, hauled up into her — a wild, picturesque horde that took possession of the ship & spread & hawked their wares. A heavy mail, bags — very speedily transferred — very glad when again we got started. Annie used up by a sick headache & had to go to bed. Dinner at 6 — beautiful firelight & moonlight — I spent much of eve'g in napping — at 11, Bella came & lay down beside the boy — Annie in my berth & I spent the night on a lounge in the dining saloon.
