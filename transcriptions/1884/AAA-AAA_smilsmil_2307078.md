## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 8, 1884

Dark & dismal — snow fell for a little while after 7 — then rain which froze as it fell — at night, it came down in torrents with driving wind. Took things easy as my head did not feel strong, & did not get to studio until nearly — worked on "Stray Lambs", putting in the bit of foreground that I washed out yesterday even'g. At 2.30 went to Richard's, to look at some paintings — then to Dist. Tel. Co., to make complaint then home to dinner. At 3 went to Kimmel & Voigt's, 242 Canal St. and started the printing of small plate for Etching Club cat. Then to studio at 4.30 — home at 6 — Even'g loung-ing about until after 10 — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 9, 1884

Overcast; raw & cloudy with partial clearing in afternoon — 10 o'clk. when I got to studio — worked on "Stray Lambs" — generally toning & getting it together. Home to dinner at — back before 3 + worked until about 4.30 — At near 6 walked down to Wunderlich's — went in & took a look at the J. C. Farrer pictures — exceeding bad — met him there & with W. had a little free-trade-in-art argument. He, of course, is a free-trader — I favor a specific duty of say $100 — which will be practically nothing on valueable works & prohibitive on cheap trash. Home to tea at 6.30 — Even'g lounging on lounge — To bed at 10.30
