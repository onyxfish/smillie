## Left Page

### December, Friday 5, 1884

Charming day — clear, mild and tender — but I felt quite mistrust & unfit for work. Got to studio at 10 & worked under pressure on color study for my big picture, going over the foreground —
Tom Smillie died at 1 o'clk. Calvert his step-mother & step sister being with him — the body was sent to Albany by 11 P.M. Train — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked until after 4 — About 5 went to Wünderlich's with p.p. from 10 of my etched plates & arranged with him to try to sell for me — then up to Grady's about frames. then home to dinner at 6 — about 8.30 went to Mother Cook's where a doll-dressing was under way — Did not feel well enough to go to the Salmagundi entertainment at Sarony's — Home at about 11 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 6, 1884

Dismal mtg, soon going into a down pouring rain & at night, a gale of wind — I got to studio at the usual time — I did some work on small canvas, "A bit on Marble-head Neck". Home to lunch at 1 & after that, feeling very much out of sorts, I went to bed & had a nap of an hour or more — then went to studio & cleaned up things for the day & home soon after 5 — Dinner at 6 — Everyone at home altho' it was century night — the wind & rain beat upon the windows so furiously that I glad to stay in — To bed at 11 —
