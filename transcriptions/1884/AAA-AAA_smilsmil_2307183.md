## Left Page

### August, Thursday 7, 1884.

Still clear, warm & very quiet — about 30
got away to my work at the farm on
the A mont, or north cliff. Worked very
steadily & by 1.15 had covered canvas —
but failed to get what I hoped for — a
single setting study, strong & bright in
color. Mr. Kittle came & commented, as
did a French painter with two of his
young lady pupils — Home at 1.40 &
had lunch about 2 — at 3, being very
tired I lay down & at last fell asleep
& did not wake until 5 — dressed
& to dinner soon after 6 — After that,
at home, waiting for Bellas until 8.30
then Annie & I went to the Casino
& saw a little French play — After that,
an operetta was given but it was so
hot that Annie gave out & we left after
only a couple of songs had been sung.
Walked for awhile on the Casino
Terrace with Bacon — Home at 10.30 —
The boy had a squalling spell, so that
it was 11.30 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 8, 1884.

Another very hot day — got up feeling
very tired in the head, but prepared
to go to paint in a sketch from part
way up the hill, looking back over
a part of town, the beach & to the cliff
Aval — but just as I was ready to start
a thin fog blew in from sea, so that I
was obliged to give it up — I commenced
to repaint a study of boats, on millboard
but alas, the fog cleared & I went up
to the place but found the color was
so unsatisfactory that I returned at
once & set to work trying to paint the
sketch from memory — lunch about 1.30
& again to work until after 4 — when
I was entirely used up & had produced
a very weak sketch — Hung about until
after 6 — then to dinner — After that, on
the beach until 8.30 when Geo., Nellie, Annie
& I went on a search for ice cream — found
signs but no cream — in before 9 —
