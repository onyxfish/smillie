## Left Page

### July, Sunday 6, 1884.

Delicious day — after a fairly good night of rest, up at 7.30 feeling a little headachy — but in the course of the m.f. it passed off — Had coffee and rolls about 9 & before 10 started with Henry Bacon & — Danforth, a young man from Boston, for a stroll on the hills — had a delicious time, going through old farm yards — across broad downs & to the tops of the cliffs where there was a wonderfully beautiful color & atmosphere over the sea — back to hotel for lunch at 12.30 — H o'clk. came very soon & Annie & I went out for a ride in a little basket wagon for an hour & a half — a short walk after that & dinner at 6.30 — Geo. & family arr. at 6.45 — After dinner he & I walked to the tops of the cliffs & back abt 9.15 — glorious twilight & moonlight — To bed at 10.30 —

## Right Page

### July, Monday 7, 1884.

Pavillon du Perrey — 2e Etage —
Rue Charles Bourde[illegible].

Another clear, beautiful day — hot in the sunshine with fresh breeze blowing — again up with headache which passed away — Had our coffee before 9 — then running about with Bacon & W. Gay — looking at houses to let — found some quaint old ones but none of them quite large enough — back & with Mad. Bas, an agent; Annie, Nellie & Geo. we went about about until noon, looking at a number of places — in proportion to picturesqueness & quaintness they were inconvenient. We had lunch at 12.30 — then a conference & determined to take the apartments above designated. Entirely new & very pretty & convenient but in no way artistic or picturesque — out with with Mad. B — then with Annie & Nellie selecting cribs for the youngsters — at 3 met M. Boissey, the proprietor, at Mad. B's — & we signed a lease — then to hotel & packed up — It was 6 before we got our baggage to our flat — dinner at 6.30 — busy until after 9 getting things in order — To bed at 10.30 — very tired.
