## Left Page

### February, Thursday 7, 1884

Yet another nasty day — rain most of the time, with fog —
Got to studio at 10 — worked with pen on inks. Etching
No 30 for Harpers — then took it
to Galt at 11 a. f.D — Spent an
hour looking about — then again
to studio & got [illegible] of Etching
"from my study door" & took it over
to Galt — then home to dinner
— did not get back until after
3 — then took two more inks
for No 30 — upon which to make
ink movements — Home before
7 — Welf, Annie read aloud
awhile — then I wrote a long
letter to Parrish — to bed about
11.30.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 8, 1884

Another dreary, dismal day — & I felt
much like the weather — Got to studio
at 10 & worked with pen for awhile
on inks — of No 30 that I took yesterday —
Then, spent an hour getting things put
away — soon after 12 went to 2° National
Bank — then, at 1, home to dinner. found
Mr. Hervé there — She took dinner
with me — Annie, at 1.30, went to a
lunch party at Mary (Ed.°) Cook's —
I went to studio, soon after 3 and
worried for some time over plans
for interior finish of Montrose bay window
without accomplishing anything —
At 5 went to Grady & W.C. cleever's and
ordered 23×40 frame for "Pond's outlet"
Home at 6.30 — from 8 to 10. Reading
with much interest a package of
Addie Dunning's old letters — To bed
at about 11.
