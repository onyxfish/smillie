## Left Page

### August, Saturday 9. 1884.

Uncertain m[illegible] — overcast with light
shower before 10. Soon after which it
cleared hazy & very warm — The boy
had a sudden diarreah this morning
which continued all day growing worse.
Before 10 I went up on the north cliff
to the crucifix with pencils & paper
& made sketches of the crucifix look-
ing west. My head feeling heavy,
I worked with great difficulty —
Home & had lunch about 1.30 —
Afternoon I felt so miserable that at
last I gave up & went to bed &
did not get up until near 5 —
To dinner soon after 6 — from 8 to
8.30 on the Terrace — Then home &
attending to duties — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 10. 1884.

Perhaps the hottest day so far this sea-
son — & yet a pleasant air was stirring
all day — Before H&p[illegible] — went for the D[octo]r. as
the boy's diarreah still continues — no him —
but he came to the house just before 11
after the boy had gone to sleep, looking and
acting very well — D[octo]r. says there is yet no
cause for alarm — After H&p[illegible] I took him
out in his carriage for 3/4 of an hour — Then
I wrote a letter to Charl — Out for a short
time before lunch which I had at 1.30 —
After that I wrote a letter to Father.
Before 5 went out with Annie & the
boy — In at 5.45 — the boy's trouble con-
tinues — did not get to dinner until
6.20 & found our places at table taken —
at which we raised a row — I had to sit
along & had a very poor meal. There
is great pressure now; "the season" is at
its height — Eve[ning] very warm — for a short
time at "Plage" — Then at open windows at home —
To bed about 10.30 — Thunderstorm with hail
before the m[orning].
