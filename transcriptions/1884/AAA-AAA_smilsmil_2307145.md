## Left Page

### May, Friday 23. 1884.

A summer sea again — wind unchanged in direction — less in force.
Had my bath with open port hole
& bright sun light. Breakfast at 8.30
Apl — ate a chop & got up on deck
quite himself again — Bella also
better — The day continued to grow
brighter & quieter until at evening
it was a dead calm — the sea very
smooth — sunset effects of clouds, pass-
ing showers on the far western horizon
& reflections over the water, very beautiful,
continued on deck until 8.30 — then
went below — relieved Bella & brewed
the boy's fodder for the night. After
Annie went to bed I went on deck
until 10.30. Delicious night — Jupiter
dropped a long trail of light over the
wonderfully smooth water — Venus
was hardly less brilliant — the air,
a dead calm.

Day's Run 317 m.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 24. 1884.

A very different day — overcast, with
raw wind — about 4 it began to
thicken & rain, sometimes nearly —
wind quite strong from the east &
at night, a nasty fog, with steam
whistle blowing until 1.30 a.m.

At night we had a concert in the
saloon, during which a subscrip-
tion was taken up for a Seamen's
Orphan Asylum at Liverpool, raised
£18.2°. Most of the singing was by
the Makeson family. Some of it was
good, but most of it exceedingly poor.
Especially by W. Chas. M. & his Sister
Mrs. Nathan. Mr. G. M. did well, also
Ma. Dotti. After the concert, Jas. jun.
continued his, instigated by the fog
whistle. I sat with him in saloon
until about 1 — did not undress
until 4 — his behavior was simply
fiendish & undoubtedly instigated by
the devil. By 6 a.m. I may have got
about 2 hours sleep. all Told.

Day's run 333.
