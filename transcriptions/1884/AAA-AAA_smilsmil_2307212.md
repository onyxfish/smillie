## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 8, 1884

Dull & overcast with fog part of the time so that we enjoyed the music of the fog-whistle — I spent the day as usual — Up at 7 — semi & bath at 7.30 — then, cracker in hand, a stiff breezing on deck for half an hour — this m[orning] with Prof Ewert — then my blkp. — then duties to the boy — a nap — lunch — writing on letter to father — then on deck with Annie — change of wind & weather — clear sunset — making the boy's every meal — dinner — along chat at table with the cap[tain] — another breezing on deck with Annie — down at 8.10 — the boy's meal prepared for early m[orning] & then to bed at 9.30 — Soon after 10 [illegible] Jim[?] gave us a furious serenade, lasting about 5 min. & again soon after 12 — expecting a night of it I slept in my clothes but he was quiet until 4 when he demanded & got his bottle & then slept until 6 — so that we got a fair night's rest

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 9, 1884

Another monotonous day — overcast most of the time — mild & little sea — the steamer going very steadily — Up at usual hour — bath at 7.30 — then blkp. — walked the deck part of the time with [illegible] of Ewert — wrote on letter to father in the afternoon, bundled in a rug in a chair for some time — Dinner at 6 — wind changed with a shower — Every in cabin quite gay — cards & [illegible] disputations while the young people in the music room rehearsed for to-morrow eve[ning]'s entertainment — It was hard to realize that we were out at sea — Did not get to bed until 11.30 — boy quite restless so that I was up several times with him & got no sleep after 6. a.m. —
