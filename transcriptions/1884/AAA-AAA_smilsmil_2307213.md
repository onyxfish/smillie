## Left Page

### October, Friday 10, 1884.

Glorious mrg. Bath at 7. & before
bkfs. had a fine breezing on deck.
Spent some time writing a letter
to Father. About noon — clouds
& light showers — the St. Laurence
passed near, going E. Nearly all
the p.m. spent in a long nap. a
cloudless sunset — dancing on deck.
At 8 o'clk. Concert in saloon. Passing
off pleasantry — no startling effort of
talent — Galt sang very well — general
jollification — To bed at 11. About
the boy began his Fantrums & we
were not allowed to sleep until
near 1 — then our drunken Irish
neighbor, Breslin, came down &
for an hour was exceedingly boistrous with some of his companions.
& was so upset that I got no
restful sleep during the rest
of the night, as Sat. just commenced
again at about 4.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 11. 1884.

Brilliant & beautiful day — Bath
soon after 7 which much refreshed me.
Then bkfs. — & much of the mrg. on deck.
no sea — little wind & very mild —
then had a good long nap & lunch
at 1.30. During the afternoon wrote more
on my letter to Father, bringing it down
to our sailing from Liverpool. After
dinner Mr. Benj. Franklin Pier also took
occasion, in a grand eloquent way, to present
to Capt. Watkins a baker bearing signatures
of the passengers expressing their gratification
etc. — to which Capt. W. rose & said, quietly,
"Ladies & gentlemen, I thank you" — an attempt
was made to get off some speeches with no
success. Feeling very tired A. & I went to
bed early — about 9.30 — very hot & close in
the cabins — the engines stopped for a short
time — said to be owing to a hot box — soon after
10 the boy began his pranks & behaved in a
perfectly exasperating manner all night —
we hardly got any rest & in the mrg. were
quite jaded out. Ran very
close to Nantucket Light Ship about 2 o'clk.
