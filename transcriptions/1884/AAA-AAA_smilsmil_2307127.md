## Left Page

### April, Thursday 17, 1884

Bright, breezy day — after a bad night with the boy, up late & had help — after 9 — after 10 when I got to studio — Painted on 23×40 "old wood road" — Home at 2 — back at 3.30 & painted until 6 — near 7 when I got home, very tired — Tea & then rested until about 8.30 — Then dressed & soon after 9, to N.A.D. Robt. Gordon farewells — an extremely complimentary & enjoyable affair — abt 11, supper in South Gallery — very well managed — a fine sight — Two little sketches — one by Henniessy [illegible] — the other, a graceful [illegible] by Gordon — I left at about 11.30 — Home & to bed soon after midnight.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 18, 1884

Cloudy, breezy & cool — Up late & with a threatening head — Went to Grady & McKeever shops in 42d St. then to store — Then to Newman's. Then hunters & about 11 got to studio — at 12, a note from Lisle & at once I went down town to see about Steamer stateroom, but did not accomplish anything definite — at 1.30, home — dinner — & at 2.20, Philharmonic rehearsal which, having a fine Programme, I enjoyed in spite of my headache — got home about 5 — St. Adams & his boy there, also Yale — at 5.30 I got to bed & in an hour Bedstead & Bureau from [illegible] arr. Tea soon after 7 — Coupé at 8, when Annie & I went to the 3d & last Henschel recital which we enjoyed exceedingly — my head behaving in an amiable manner — Home soon after 10.
