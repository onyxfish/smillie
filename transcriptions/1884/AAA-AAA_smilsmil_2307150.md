## Left Page

### June, Monday 2, 1884.

A remarkably pleasant day — up before 7 — the boy having passed a pretty good night. B'kf't about 9 — at 10 went to [illegible] of Royal Inst. of Painters in Water-Colors & worked faithfully at it until 12.30 — Geo. & Nellie part of the time there with me — Beautiful Galleries & an interesting exh'n, the pictures that pleased me most being by Walter Langley — Then to hotel — Annie had been to see Dr. Wiltshire, but failed — J. [illegible] does not improve, having much trouble with his bowels. Had a short nap before dinner at 1.45 — After it Annie & Nellie went out to Bedford Park & called on Mr. W.D. Conway, while Geo. & I went to the Grosvenor Gallery & worked until after 5, when we gave out — more interested in the Grosvenor than in the Royal Acad'y — met Montague Marks there — Then, alone, I went to Hyde Park & spent some time looking at the riders on horseback — the crowds & the Rhododendrons — then to hotel & had tea about 7. Geo. got apartments in a neighboring building. To bed soon after 9 — at 9.30 Dr. W. came unexpectedly & prescribed for J. [illegible]. Promising to call to-morrow mng.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 3. 1884.

Overcast, with a few drops of rain about 1 — up at 7 after an uneasy night with the boy — nearly before we get b'kf't — at 10.30 Dr. Wiltshire came & reassured us about the boy — at 11 I went to Royal Academy & worked faithfully until 1, where, very much used up I ret'd to hotel & took a short rest before dinner at 1.45 — After that struggled for some time again to get a rest, but there was too much confusion — Soon after 5 Annie & I with the boy rode in a hansome to the Albert Memorial, Hyde Park & beyond — on our way back, stopped at 36 Hertford St — 7 J. Seymour Haden's — he was out & I left a card for him — then back to hotel at 6.15 — After 7 Geo., Nellie, A. & I went to a Restaurant in Regent's S't & had tea, a better meal than we get at our hotel — what remained of the eve'g was spent in caring for the boy — To bed at 10.
