## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 22, 1884

Very fine day — nearly 10 when I got
away to my work — oil study of South
cliff, from a point a short distance up on
the batte — worked faithfully until 1.30
then home & had lunch at about 2 —
after that, a short rest — then finding
that the tide was setting right — I took
pencils & paper & went under great arch
of South cliff, & sitting on rocks bared by
receding tide & covered with sea weed, I
made a sketch of cliff with second
arch & foreground of rocks & pools of
water — back at 5.30 pretty well tired
out. Dinner & its usual followings —
For some time walked on the [illegible] with
Annie — then in, & wrote a note to Mr.
Arthur Furnure. To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 23, 1884

Beautiful day — but got up with a pain
under my belt, so that I did not feel able
to go on with my oil study commenced
yesterday — made sketch on canvas of the
subject I sketched yesterday p.m. & then
rubbed a glaze of color over it — Near 11
went with Annie along the beach, north, to the
tunnel & path to top of cliff — At the top, about
2 — I stopped & made pencil sketch — Annie
went on & I followed in an hour & had
lunch at home — This mff. Nellie went to
Paris alone to see her dressmaker. After
lunch, I prepared my oil traps & at 2.30
went to the arch & had to wait some time
for the tide to go down so that I could
get to my place to work — I found 'em
very damp work, the tide, naturally, leaving
every thing very wet. I worked away without
stopping until about 5 — then home & to the
hotel to dinner. Eve'g., between 8 & 10,
thunder showers — Wrote a note to Harbell
of Montrose about the Cooper lot & also
a note to father to accompany it —
So bed about 10.30.
