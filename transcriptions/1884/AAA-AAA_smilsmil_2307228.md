## Left Page

### November, Sunday 9, 1884

Brilliant day — heavy frost last night — B'kf't at 8 — Chatting with Father & Mother until after 10 — then I wandered off to Fox's lot where the Vassar Bros. Hospital is now being built — then across to Livingston's woods — gathered a Nov. bouquet of weeds, seeds & sticks. On my way back met Father & with him again went to the Vassar Hospital bld'g — then home & after, went up to Baptist Ch. I met Mary, Kate & Aunt Huldah — we walked home together — Mary, her Bessie & aunt H. going to Garfield Place. Dinner at 1 — towards 3 I went to Mary's there for an hour — then to Terrace — Mary with me — Ed. came in — Father read to me his letters to A. P. [illegible] — Tea at 5.40 — left at 6.15 — at 7. took 6.50 train & got in to N.Y. on time at 9 — Home — wrote a few words to Father & R.L. and then to G.C. Depot & mailed them — To bed at 11.

Found the boy progressing — his cough very severe.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 10, 1884

mild, pleasant day — 10 o'clk. when I got to studio — again set to work on small painting "Near Marblehead" — repainting foreground, making it both lighter & stronger. Rec'd. from Brom Stoker 4 tickets — gave me two but Jakey was so afraid that we would contaminate her with whooping cough that for peace's sake I returned them. — Home at 1 — back about 2.30 — & continued the repainting until after 4 — Dinner at 6 — about 8 Annie & I went to opening of the Inaugural Exh'n of the Am. Art Ass'n Galleries. Very crowded & frightfully hot — left soon after 9 — went to N.A. of D. — Autumn Syst'm — there until near 10 — met Laura Hearn — her son & a daughter — also Wm. Lippincott — then home & to bed about 11.
