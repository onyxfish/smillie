## Left Page

### April, Thursday 3, 1884.

Cold day — high wind & cloudy —
After 10 when I got to studio
but I got to work on an oil
sketch — a fallow field — &
worked without interruption
until near 1 — then home to
dinner — back at 3 — Wash.
Wilson came in — then Miss
Peck & Cooker Union — but, by
working until 6 — I got the
canvas covered & feel quite
hopeful of a good final result.
Home at 7 — tea — evening reading
— & writing letters to [illegible]
& Phela — & Clarence Crittenden of
Rochester — nearley 11.30 when
I got to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 4, 1884.

Cold — high wind & lots of flying
dirt. Got to studio at 10 & for
an hour or more worked on
12×22 charcoal sketch — a
bit near Montrose — sketched
last Oct. Then to N.A., Varnishing
Day — There until about 12.30 —
then home to dinner — Back
to studio — worked a little while
on sketch — also mounting some
etching pp — Then to Ward's fur-
niture store & to Flint's in 14th St.
to look at some furniture — my
eyes nearly put out by dirt,
nearley 7 when I got home — tea
at 8 at Chickering Hall — the first
of the 3 farewell Henschel Concerts.
Geo went on Annie's ticket, very
charming — Saw Colman & wife
there — Home at about 10 &
to bed.
