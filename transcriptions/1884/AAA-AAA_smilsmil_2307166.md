## Left Page

### July, Friday 4, 1884.

The hottest day yet — all the with. Spent
at the Bankers & in packing trunks —
lunch at 12.30 — at 2 o'clk. with Annie
& Nellie went to their dressmakers and
tried on their dresses — then Annie & I
went to the Louvre & had a general
stroll through the galleries — at 4.30 we
strolled along the Rue Rivoli & bought
some photos — also a couple of grammars
— then rode to Havre Station — engaged om-
nibus for to-morrow, & tried to engage a R.R.
carriage or buy tickets but could not. —
then back to hotel, doing a little
shopping on the way — after dinner
Annie & I went out to the Boulevard
& had some ice cream at a café & after
that, in the twilight, rode out beyond the
Arc d'Etoile — a vast throng of conveyances —
back at 9.30 — dresses came — confusion —
about 10 the boy began to misbehave & he
continued it until 11.30 when he quieted
down to a good night's rest & let us go
to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 5, 1884.

*Hotel Hauteville — Étretat — Room 29*

Another very hot day — up at 6 o'clk. &
to work packing — rolls & cup of tea at 8 —
then taken aback by the announcement that
Nellie was not well enough to travel to-day
— we decided to go on alone. Annie went into
N. to dressmakers & got back at about 11 — got
our packing done & had lunch at 11.30 —
at 12.30 bus came & Geo. went with us to the
station to see how things were done — 81f. 81c.
for our 3 first class tickets — 10f. 80c. extra for baggage —
started at 1 — got to Les Ifs at 5.8 & bus to Étretat
at 6.30 — the R.R. ride very hot & dusty but exceedingly
interesting — many crossings of the Seine — the
views of Rouen very fine — then a change of scene
— before reaching des Ifs, a thunderstorm — rain
continued until half over our bus ride — the
country extremely interesting with its old
quaint farm steads — to healthy looking tops
& cool, sweet air. Charmed with all that I
saw — got to hotel at 6.30 & at once to our
room where we tried to wash off some of the
dirt — then dinner — after that gave the boy,
who has behaved wonderfully well, his dinner
& then Annie & I went out for a half hours
walk — in to bed soon after 10 —
