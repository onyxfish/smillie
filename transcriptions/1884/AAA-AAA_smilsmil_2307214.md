## Left Page

### October, Sunday 12, 1884

*at home.*

About 4 A.M. off the bar — lay there — passed
Sandy Hook at 6.20 & at 7. off Quarantine —
Health & Customs H[arbor]. Officers came aboard & the
Lucy M. Starrin alongside — I got up at 6 & had
my bath & at 7 my blkfst — then finished packing
things. It was just about 9.30 when we left
the "City of Chicago", passengers & their luggage all
having been transferred — delightful sail up the
bay, altho' it was very warm. Got to Barge Office
before 10 — then a delay of about an hour before
the luggage was all brought in & I could get my
6 Trunks, 1 valise, 4 bags, 1 Roll of Shawls & Rug
& two steamer chairs together — then had an officer
detailed & the search was [promptly] made — my
studies were taken out of my trunk, an appraiser
called & we were taken to Deputy Collector William
who soon made everything right — the searcher was
very civil (he was a [Cornow?]) & at 11.30 we were chalked
"all Right" — G&C Cook & Valentine got down about 10.30
they took Bella & Jas. [Jr?] home with them — Arrived
I followed — getting to Mother Cook's at 12 in company
with quite a heavy shower — weather exceedingly
muggy — Ther. 77°. I tried to telegraph to Father —
no office open — Dinner at 1.30 — At 2.30 home, and
waited for trunks which came at 3.30 — more showers
Opened trunks & did some unpacking — then on
bed for an hour — Soon at two to — again to Mother C.
Brought Bella & the boy home & returned to tea —
Policeman called to say there was a light in the
house — I then went to relieve Bella. Two
streets in a weak endeavor to put things to
rights — to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 13, 1884

Still very mild & pleasant — went to Mother
Cook's for our blkfst at 8.30 & an hour or so later
returned to the house & the work of unpacking.
In the course of the m[ornin]g, Emma J. came in
& spent some time with us — She lunched
with us at 1.30 at Mother Cook's. Before we
left there, to our great surprise, Kate and
Ed put in an appearance — just down
from P[eek?]k. to see us — She went to the house
with us. Between 3 & 4 I went to studio —
Before 5 Kate came in & She, Ge[o]. & I went
to 126 E. 28th St. & were joined by E[d?]. Kate
decided to stay in N.Y. until to-morrow —
At 6.30 I went to Mother C's & took dinner —
Before 8, to 38th St — podging until 11, then
to bed —
