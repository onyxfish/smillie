## Left Page

### December, Thursday 25, 1884

Brilliant m'f. — cold day — after 10 when I got to studio — Got out a 10×14 W.C. sketch that I made last Spring — "A bit near Montrose" — & worked the rest of the m'f over it — nearly 1.30 when I got home to lunch. After 3 when I went out for a walk. To Central Park & saw the Sleigh Riders — Home at 4.45. Time fully occupied by many entries until 6 o'clk. Then a rest of 15 minutes — then dressed — just before 7 the Cook family came — Mother Cook & Katchen (Will's daughter) & [illegible], Walter & Marie — Ed [illegible], Mary & Miss Williams. Dinner a few minutes after 7 — a very good one I think. At table for about two hours then in parlor & amused ourselves for another two hours playing at a child's game that Mary had brought around "The Fish Pond" — To bed a little before midnight.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 26, 1884

Bright, cold day — Up late — after 10 before I got to studio — continued the finishing work on "A bit near Montrose". Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.45 & worked as long as I could see. Then pasted paper on backs of two mounted drawings — & put them in press. Then went to Grady's to give orders about frames — there until 6.20 — home at 6.30 & had dinner at 6.45 — even'g. divided between work as a stroker, lounging on sofa & writing a note to W'm Jessup concerning the proposed public road through our lane at Montrose. To bed before 11.
