## Left Page

### February, Sunday 17. 1884.

Back again to the flood — overcast
until after 10 — then pouring rain
for the rest of the day. B|Up.
before 9 — then some reading &
writing — Before 2, Kate came —
then George — the Edw Cook, his
wife & Miss Williams — at 2.15
we had dinner — a very good one
— up at 4.30 — Chark came in —
all stayed until after 5 —
after 7 we had tea — I had
written my letter home.
Before 9, through a pelting rain
storm, I went to G.C. Depot with
[illegible] — to bed before 11 — but up
agains at 11.30, for 3/4 of an hour
with obstreperous James just.

## Right Page

### February, Monday 18. 1884.

Overcast, threatening rain & mild.
To studio before 10 — got out a
canvas 20×30 & charcoaled a
subject of which I have been
thinking lately — upright — cedars —
a hillside & sheep — I don't know
what to call it — Pattison came
in while I was at work and he
recognized it as an "abstract com-
position" — Home to dinner at 1 —
back before 3 — worked for awhile
on sketches — but feeling very unlike
work — at 5, went to Grady McKewin's
shop to see a frame underway for me.
Home before 6.30 — tea at 7 — [illegible]
Annie read aloud from the
"newcomes". To bed at 11 —
more rain.
