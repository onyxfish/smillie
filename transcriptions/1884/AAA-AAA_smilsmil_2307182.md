## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 5, 1884.

Another cloudless day, but not so
quick & got away to my work at 9.30 —
commenced, in oil, my study within
the enclosure of the farm on the cliff.
I sat just inside of one of the gates &
from my point could see seven sketching
umbrellas. How many more there were
I do not know. I was much bothered
by my light & by the wind — I got quite
chilly but I stuck to it until 1 — then
with canvas more than half covered,
I started for home — lunch at 1.30 —
very tired — Took a short rest & at
3.30 went to my W.C. subject of yes-
terday — Too tired to work happily
on it — Annie came for me & at 5.30
I went with her — Dinner soon
after 6 — After that, I went alone up
on the cliff to the crucifix to study the
twilight & the rising moon — delicious
eve'g. Home at 8.30 —

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 6, 1884.

And yet another cloudless day — a
fisherman's fête day — at 9.30 Annie
& I went to the church to see procession of
fishermen & hear them sing a cantata — a very
prosaic affair — the fishermen do not look well
in their "store clothes" & their cantata singing is
my bad — The mass was common place except
a portion sung by the tenor of the Casino —
After that we went to the beach where we met
the Misses Smith & Walker — Home to lunch
at 1 — Soon after 2 I went out to the village
of St. Clair where I made a little W.C.
sketch of entrance to a farm yard — The
wind blew quite hard, but I sat in shade
of trees & did not put up umbrella — The
color all about was delicious — Annie
came up for me before 5 — Home & to
dinner at 6.15 — Eve'g. much amusement
on the Maine Square — booths with games —
a poor old gymnast & two girls — wooden
horses etc. I went out to see them from
8.30 to 10 — To bed at 11 —
In the m'g before bkft — Geo. had a fainting
fit but soon recovered — I rec'd my letter
of credit & 1500 francs from Drexel, Harjes & Co.
