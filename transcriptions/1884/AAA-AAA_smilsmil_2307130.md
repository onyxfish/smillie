## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 23, 1884

Brilliant, beautiful day — 70
studio soon after 10 & to work on
12×22 "The Edge of the Wood" —
Home to dinner at 1 — Soon
after 2 got to N.A. — meeting of
Exhibitors to vote on award of
Clark & Hallgarten prizes — a
very good tempered meeting
116 present — The balloting was
tedious & continued until about
4.30. Prizes to Blaine — Moeller —
C[illegible], Turner & W[illegible] Bliss Baker —
To studio & painted until near
6 — New home — Evg. with Annie
went to see Irving as Shylock
— a play put upon the stage
with great care & good judgment
but cannot say that I enjoyed
Irving's presentation of the part.
Miss Terry was very graceful.
Home at 1.30 & to bed without
delay.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 24, 1884

Overcast, with some rain — Got
to studio at 10.20 & to work on
small picture, Painted more than
a year ago, about 6×15" a bit on
"Marblehead Neck". Repainting
it generally — Interrupted by a
visit from [illegible] Pate, who has just
come to town, — also Carrie Hertzel.
W[illegible] Pate called to see painting
"Yosemite Falls & Merced River" —
To bank & made deposit for
Annie — then home to dinner —
Edith Congler there. Back at 3 —
finished painting — Home at
6.30 — very tired — found Annie
in like condition & not feeling
in mood to go to the Wagner Concert
at Met. Opera House to-night — So
[illegible] took the tickets to [illegible] Cook —
Home — Tea at 7 — evening with
news paper.
