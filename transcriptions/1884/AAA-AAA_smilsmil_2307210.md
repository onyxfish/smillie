## Left Page

### October, Saturday 4, 1884

Beauteful day — with a few hours overcast — Routed out of dining saloon at 5.30 — then to my stateroom — Bella to hers. boy awake — after a considerable struggle got him asleep but I got no rest — Annie not able to get up — I got my bath at 7.30 & felt better but with very many duties it was quite 9 before I got my help —
I got some nabs during the m'f; lunch after 1 — Annie got up to it & then out on deck. Evening observed the beginning of the moon's eclipse which was soon clouded over — to bed early & had a fair night's rest.

Left Rock Point yesterday at 4.55 — the Run at noon to-day, 274 m.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 5, 1884

Weather thickening — some showers — Between 4 & 5 in the afternoon a sudden squall struck us coming suddenly around from the N.E. with heavy rain — sails aback and great scurrying for a few minutes — I got on my rubber suit & enjoyed the excitement on deck. To bed early — a rather heavy night but it did not disturb my rest — & the boy behaved tolerably well also.
