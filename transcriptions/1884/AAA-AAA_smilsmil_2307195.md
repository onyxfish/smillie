## Left Page

### September, Thursday 4, 1884

Around gale with tremendous sea — came up very suddenly about 6 A.M. & raged by 10 — a very grand & exciting sight — Annie & I rigged ourselves in Rubber suits & went out on the beach & up on the cliff d'Aval to where we could see Presca run through the Mannepорte — wind so strong that we could hardly stand against it & Rain pricked our faces like needles — Home at 10.30 & spent rest of m'd. Packing — At 12, by invitation we went to M. Bacon's to breakfast & to meet a friend visiting there, Miss Clifford — a very pleasant time until 2 when we returned home & resumed our packing — Storm continued until after 4 — then Rain ceased — clouds broke away — wind still high — went to Ma? Bas to pay our rent; she asked 30 francs per day for since Sep. 1. — we declined & sought M. Boissaye but could not find him — attended to various messages — Annie dined at hotel — I supped at home — Very. more packing — wrote letter to Geo. After 9 — with A. went to beach to see the sea — full moon — grand sight — Home — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 5, 1884

*Havre, on board the Channel St. "Wolf".*

Glorious day — bright & breezy — Up at usual time — After blép — H.H. Bacon called — about 9.30 A. & I went to the Maire — saw M. Boissaye & soon reached an amicable settlement of the extra rent difficulty. Posted letters to Geo. at Amsterdam — Pet? to the house & finished packing & at 11.45 went to call on Dava — he out but his mother — had a visit with her & home at 12.15 — Annie & I cooked lunch & ate it at 1. At 2 the omnibus came & at 2.15 we bid good bye to Mr. Maubert and to Étretat — a splendid team of horses took us at a brisk trot over the 19 miles in 2½ hours — stopping but once & only walking up the hill out of Étretat. I enjoyed the ride very much — beautiful skies — got to le boat at 4.45 — secured our berths, or shelves — went to office & bought tickets to London — then to Hotel Continent el" where we had tea & waited until about 8 then to boat & stowed the boy who behaved splendidly — full moon — glorious night — afloat — beautiful effects of sky & water — on deck until after 10 — very breezy & considerable motion — Then to my shelf — one of the passengers snored so that at 1.30 I went up to saloon & lay there the rest of the night.
