## Left Page

### September, Monday 8. 1884.

*First Ave. Hotel London — Room 440.*

Unpleasant weather again — overcast, with
quite rain from time to time — It was 10.30
before we were ready to go out — then I got
a bath chair, as Annie did not feel very well,
& she & the boy were dragged about in it while
Bella & I accompanied on foot. We went all
about the Cathedral & into the Bishop's garden —
the funny little noseless man dragging the chair
& explaining things in a way that we could not
understand — at about 11.30, the chair was sent
back to the hotel with Bella & the boy & A. & I went through
the the Cathedral, Chapterhouse & cloisters — then
to a shop & bot. a few photos. Then to hotel — Packed
up — lunch at 2. Settled bill & at last off
on 3.5 train for London — dismal afternoon —
due at 5.17 — but near 6 when we got in — great
hub bub at station — excursion trains — drove to
Grand Hotel — no rooms — sent us over to 1st Ave. Ho.
— a new house, kept by some company on High
Holborn — a fine new place — electric lights —
aesthetic glass & furniture, etc. etc. — Tea after
8 — I sat in reading room until 9 — then
wrote on Father's letter until 10 — To bed at 11.

Trunks sent up from Southampton; instead
of being left in cloak room were sent to J.S. Morgan
& Co's, much to our inconvenience & expense.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 9. 1884.

Dismal sort of day but not wet. Bkft.
at about 9 — After 10, called two hansome —
Sent Bella & the boy off for an hours ride
in one & in the other A. & I rode down
to J.S. Morgan & Co's to look after our stray
baggage & to get letters — got letters from Father
& Co. Annie got one from her Mother — Then
we drove to Charles St. to look for lodgings
& after going to several places took the
2d floor at No. 46 — Mr. Pratt's, but a short distance
from Piccadilly & almost overlooking Green
Park where we hope the boy will get his
daily airing. Then back to hotel at 1 —
After 2 before we had our lunch —
Later in the afternoon I sauntered about
Lincolns Inn Fields & courts of the Temple.
Felt pretty well tired out. Tea after 7 —
For some time sat in Reading room —
& read some of Lamb's Essays — "Benchers
of the Inner Temple" etc. — in the afternoon fin-
ished letter to Father & posted it —

To bed at 10.
