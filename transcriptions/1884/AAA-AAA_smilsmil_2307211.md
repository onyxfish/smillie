## Left Page

### October, Monday 6, 1884

Fair wind & square sails set but
the day was wet much of the time.
the sea being with us we went along
very steadily with some fine pitching
in the n'lf. a steamer — the captain
said "a tramp", crossed closely
across our bows, making a fine
appearance pitching in the heavy
sea. The day monotonous — In
the ev'g, for awhile in the saloon
chatting with Prof. Evert —
Out on deck for awhile at 11 — night
almost clear — then in & to bed —
— boy behaved well until 3 — then
awoke & would not go to sleep again.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 7, 1884

Overcast with a few light showers — in the
afternoon after 4 o'clk. another change of
wind & clearing sky — brilliant moonlight
at night. B't b — soon after 8 — feeling very
seedy after my night's — or rather morning's
experience — the boy waking at 3 o'clk. &
refusing to go to sleep again — A fine
sea during the n'lf. Got a little
nap — & another in the afternoon — the
day as monotonous as usual.
Ev't got early to bed.
