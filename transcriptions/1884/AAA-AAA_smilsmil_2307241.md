## Left Page

### December, Sunday 7, 1884

A mild, spring like day — perfectly
clear m'g — cloudy afternoon — Still feeling
shaky — about 11 Annie & I walked
down to Century to see the pictures —
met Moschelles & wife — Collins & [illegible]
& others there — walked back, stopping
for a little time at Mother Cook's — saw
Nellie & her two boys there. Home to
dinner at 2 — after that spent a
short time making an exchange
of bedsteads in Rooms on 3d floor —
Then a rest — then [illegible] called
& Walter C. Tea at 7 — wrote
letter home & to Cotrill — went to
G.C.D. & posted letters — to bed
before 11.

## Right Page

### December, Monday 8, 1884

Fine day — to studio at 10, not feeling
much like work — [illegible] for nearly
all the m'g — on little "Baviering" "A bit
on Marblehead Neck" — scratching out
& painting in one of the sheep — then
to work on little sketch, 4 × 6 — in
oil for my picture in W.C. "S Fray
Lambs". Home to lunch at 1 & back
at 2.30 — worked on S Fray Lambs
sketch — Miss Humphreys & a
friend from Newport called — Home
at 6 — at 6.30, with Annie went
to Ed[ward] Cook's in 37th S. to dinner.
Mrs. Sprague & Miss Williams there.
Evening spent in playing Bazique.
Home at 10.30 — to bed at 11.
