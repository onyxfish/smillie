## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 23. 1891.

*Eliz., Sally & Mary Cook arr.*

Foggy & cloudy m'f. that soon cleared into a breezy, Brilliant day growing quite cool towards eve'f. Inspite of my good Resolutions I could not work in my studio — all day was spent out of doors at work about the place. In the afternoon I picked some large berries (straw), boxed them & took them down-town at 4.30 just in time to miss the stage over to little Depot — so, I walked over & expressed them. Then home & forked a large mess of Strawberries — At 5 Herrick drove down to D.&H.W. Station & got Eliz., Sally and Mary Cook, getting back to the house at 8.45. The youngsters were as lively as crickets & were glad to get something to eat. At 9.30 I went to P. O. with notes to Geo., Charl & Shirlaw — I sent to Shirlaw a tracing of his painting that he might retine the nude figure under the drapery. To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 24. 1891.

Another brilliant, breezy day without clouds. Again good Resolutions to begin my studio work were given to the winds &, instead, I was out of doors all day — working in the m'f. & drawing in the afternoon. It was nearly 3.30 when Miss J— Annie, Walter's Mary, Ralph & I started — drove to Dr. Norris's — all out — enjoyed the view from the piazza then home by way of Eek Labis — getting back at 7.15. Eve'f. after a little time over our news-papers, Annie read aloud part of me of Janvier's stories. To bed at 10.45.
