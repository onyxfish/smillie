## Left Page

### May, Saturday 2, 1891.

Another pleasant, warm day. After bkf. I went to G.C. Station & bought tickets for Annie & Kate. While doing so, by a jugglery that I could not understand, while buying tickets to accomodate a stranger who was in too great haste to take his place on the line, there being a great crush, I lost $3.40 whether I was cheated or the money was stolen from me I do not know — the confusion was so great & movements were so rapid. A. & f. took 10 o'clk. train to New Haven to remain there over Sunday — to go to-night to a play given in German by a number of young girls Katie & Mollie Adams being among the [illegible]. I got to studio at 10.40 & to work biting on jade & Lippencott's plates — at 12.30 went to Bank & made deposits for Annie & self — Then to G.C. Station & saw ticket-seller. He said he had made his cash bal. for the mfg & had no trace of my $3.40. So, I left, a sadder & still no wiser man — Home & had lunch — Then a short nap & got to studio at 3.30 — Finished the biting of jade plate & progressed with Lippincott's plate — Home at 6.30 — dinner — Eve'g. at 8.30 went to Century — monthly meeting — there until 11.15 — then home & to bed before 12.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 3, 1891.

Rain during the night — heavy showers between 9 and 11 A.M. then the weather gradually cleared until in the afternoon it was delightful. Up at 7 — had my bath & then back to bed again. Up for all day after 8 & had bkf. at 9 — Jim complained of being sick at 11, I darkened his room, got him to bed & he slept until 1.30 — Before that time Ralph & I went to the Century & saw the pictures — Home at 2 & had dinner — About 4, with Jim & Ralph I went to the park & they had a fine time climbing over rocks — After 6 when we got home — tea before 7 — Eve'g. I read & wrote — a letter to Herrick & a note to Cousin, Editor of Montrose Democrat commending a project to set out trees on Public Ave. Chave came in for an hour — He spent yesterday at Po'keepsie. To bed before 11.
