## Left Page

### October, Sunday 25, 1891

A wonderfully brilliant, beautiful day — mild, still & cloudless. I was out of doors all day. After bkfst. I went to the garden & was there until near noon. Then I set to work upon the Virginia Creeker vines on E. side of house and worked until 2 o'clk. putting in wire netting in places where the vines had broken down the old strong attachments & cutting out dead wood — Dinner at 2 — After that again at same work & stuck to it until after sun down — Tea before 7. Evefg., hanging about — rather uncomfortable. A little reading, wrote to Barney Carey ordering some stone.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 26, 1891

A very mild day — overcast in early mfg clearing to a happy sunny day before noon, with a light drizzling rain after 10 ell clouds breaking away in two or three hours. I got to work in Etching Room soon after bkfst. & bit a light aqua-tint over 5"×7" "A deserted brick yard". Then began the biting of aqua-tint 5"×7" "an old dam" — for [illegible] Mrs. This, together with watching & directing Herrick who is putting up mirror over Studio wash-stands, occupied me for the rest of daylight. At 5.30 went down town — back at 6.30. Evefg. spent wrestling with Sunday's & Monday's Tribune.
