## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 4, 1891

Rain fell last night & at intervals all
day — some of the showers very heavy —
especially between 8 & 9 p.m. After a
bad night I got up feeling weary and
depressed — very unlike working. I
spent some time after bkfs. gathering
a lot of fine lettuce for Mr. D. Lathrop
— then to garden. It was after 11 when
I went down town — there to Mr. S's with
lettuce — Home at 12.30 — Dinner soon
after — Made a fire in studio and
worked for 2 hours on jade plate
laying very fine mezzotint ground over
it — Before 6 again went down
town — Tea before 7. Eve'g. spent
some time over letter from W. Blaney
of Boston giving along description,
with illustrations, of his methods of
Electro-Etching. — Then took a hand
with Miss Joshay & Annie in a new
game played with dominoes.

work on jade plate 2 h.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 5, 1891

Pouring rain all the m'f. & fog & mizzle
all this afternoon. — I got to work in
my studio on jade plate before 10 & after
waiting for some time for the rain to
lessen or abate I went down to the garden
& gathered vegetables, getting so wet that I
had to change all my clothes — then worked
until dinner at 1 o'clk — and again after
that, laying mezzotint ground with fine
tool — I am getting the hang of the work
which is distressingly mechanical, and
after a time may be able to lay as good a
ground as a professional. — Parke came
& sat with me for an hour or more discussing
my spiritual condition & the education of
his son. Before 6 we walked down town — At
the Exp. Office I got pkg. from Kepkeel & Co. with
Photo & oil sketch of one of the old missions in
Cal. — I am asked to make an estimate of
cost of etching it in 8" × 16". Tea — Eve'g. I wrote
to Macbeth, & K. & Co. saying that I would like
to do the piece in aqua-tint or mezzotint for
$275 or $300 — but not to etch it — At 9 o'clk
took letter to P.O. Home — looked over news
paper and to bed after 10.

work on jade plate. 4 h.
