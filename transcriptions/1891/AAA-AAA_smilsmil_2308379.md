## Left Page

### February, Sunday 15, 1891

Bright, pleasant day, but wind from
S.E. Did not get up till late — Help — after
9 — sat awhile reading newspaper
then out with Ralph & had a little
walk — back at noon — then to Union
League club meeting [illegible] on the
way, Geo. joining us soon after. A
great lot of Claude Monet's on ex[hibi]tion
which I tried hard to understand
— had along talk with Mr. Lawrence
concerning them — They are very dis-
agreeable to me. Home at 2 —
dinner — Geo. & Emily came in &
took dessert with us — about 4 o'clk
I lay down & had along nap. Tea
after 6 — Chas. spent part of p.m.
with Kate — Eve'g. wrote a letter
to Peter Coople telling him that I
purposed making a change next sum-
mer & hiring Apollos Stone in his stead —
then read until 11 & to bed.

## Right Page

### February, Monday 16, 1891

Wet, mild day — got to studio at 10:30
after going to Lyceum Theatre & getting
seats for Friday eve'g. & then to bank for
Annie. So dark that I did not like to
go on with more serious work, so I
worked upon the two old plates, from
[illegible] desk, lake Geo.° & "my neighbor
Eldred's Barn" with a hope of making them
worth something. Home to lunch and
back — spent some time varnishing
plates & then made paste & mounted
a couple of etching refs. sold at the A.
Expo[sition]. Home before 6. Dinner —
Eve'g Annie & Kate went tohelppera
I read awhile & then tried to work
upon some drawings for Herrick at
Montrose — so hot that I gave it up
& went to bed to get cool. Annie
came in about midnight.
