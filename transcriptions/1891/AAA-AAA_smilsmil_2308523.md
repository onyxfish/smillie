## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 2. 1891.

milder — latter part of the day overcast — It was 10 o'clk. before
I got to studio & at once to work upon biting San Luis Rey — Sometime
before I got hot water — acid warm — big tray heated — etc — etc — The first
exposure to the acid proved that the soft ground (unsmoked) was laid imperfectly & that the paper was stretched so tight when the drawing
was made as to take up more or less ground all over the plate —
There was nothing however to give serious trouble — Home at 1.30
— back at 2.30 — worked at biting until 5 but did not get it done —
Took a walk up to 50th S. — 45 Am.
trying to get rid of acid fumes —
Home at 6 — dinner — Eve'g wrote a letter to S. R. Koehler.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 3. 1891.

mild & pleasant — afternoon clouded —
Got to studio at 10 & at once to work on
biting San Luis Rey — Home to lunch
at 1.30 — Annie used up by a sick
headache — back at 2.45 — found
Mrs. Blackburn waiting for me — She
spent most of the afternoon taking
her second lesson — I managed
to look after my Rey plate and
finished the biting at 5 o'clk. I
took plate for S. R. Koehler to Adams
Exp. at 4.30 — Saml. Colman came
in on my return — He has just
come to town & will share my
studio for two months — It was
nearly 6 before I got plate cleaned —
Then home to dinner — Eve'g with
papers — garden book & writing
a short note to Koehler. Chas.
Collins called for a few minutes.
