## Left Page

### October, Friday 9, 1891

Brilliant day — I got to work in good time in the morning in studio painting 11"×22" San Luis Rey mission, Cal. Before 1 o'clk. I had painted in the sky & tower — then out for awhile & sunned myself until dinner at 1.30 — After dinner the donkey was harnessed & Annie with Jim & Ralph & Herrick's boy, Wesley, & I, started for the brick yard — the youngsters & A— rode by turns while I walked. I made a pencil sketch of the tumble-down shed of Boyd & Cooley's Kiln with a pool of water in the foreground. About 5 we started for home. On the way I stopped in & saw Thos. Warner. Home at 6. Eve'g. worked awhile on sketch made in the afternoon while A— read aloud to me a story by Brander Matthews.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 10, 1891

Mild & pleasant, tho' much of the time overcast — gorgeous sunset — sickly moonlight. Got to work soon after blks— on San Luis Rey mission oil sketch & painted until near 1. Then took a little airing before dinner. Afternoon, Annie with the boys & donkey again went a fishing. I worked until near 5 but did not quite finish my painting. I then went down town & met A. & her party and with them returned at 6. After tea I read news papers — then wrote a letter to S. R. Koehler.
