## Left Page

### September, Monday 7, 1891

Up at 6 o'clk. expecting to go to Berwick with Young Woodier. Rain had been falling & heavy clouds covered the sky, so I gave it up — soon after went to R.R. station & saw Young W. for a moment — then on to site of new station where much work is being done — home at 8 o'clk. & had bkfp. Got in a good three hours work on jade plate working on second ground & beginning upon the more artistic part — dinner — At 2 o'clk. went down Town & met with Y.D. Com. & Street Com. to look over the ground of proposed improvement on Public Ave. Got about an hours work on plate before we went out riding — Charl. so stiff from Rheumatism that he could not go with us. Back at 6½. Eve'g. with papers until 10. min to bed.

4 hrs on jade plate.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 8, 1891

Cold day, raw & overcast in the m'g. but clearing to be wonderfully clear & beautiful in the afternoon. I made preparations for a good days work in studio but was mistaken in my calculations — much time was given to directing the men from R.R. who brought up many loads of fine dirt. Park brought Mr. Rapallo & their R. & we went over Warner's lot — the fair ground & then to the house — I got a chance to work for about an hour before dinner on jade plates — At 2.30 I went down Town to see Dan'l. Sayre & E.J. Ball, Clerk of County Com'r. about getting earth to put in front of Court Ho., but when I saw Page, boss of men at R.R., I could not make satisfactory arrangements — Got to work again on jade plates about 4 o'clk & worked until near 6 — Then down town Eve'g. Charl. spent it with the Parks — Bessie went with Bessie P. & Katie to a party given by the young Posts — after 9 o'clk I went to P.O. & then to P's — Charl. was taken up to our house in a carriage & I walked home. To bed before 11.
