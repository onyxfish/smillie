## Left Page

### June, Saturday 27. 1891.

Very cool, clear & windy — Ther. at 8 AM
55°. Sec the w[illegible] directing men.
Part of the time with Henick making
plans for new horse & cow stables —
then with Butterfield who came
& filed the teeth of the horses — then
with Stone & Coyle. After dinner
I trained creeping vines — and
at 4 started out for a drive —
taking Miss J — getting Elizabeth
(Walter's) at Mr. Garots & picking up
Annie & Ralph at the lake, where
they had been with the boys fishing.
We drove until 6.15. Eve'g
after a brief time with newspapers
(the eve'g does not begin until 8.30
as I am us ually engaged watering
the flower beds until that time)
I wrote in my garden note book
until 10.30 — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 28. 1891.

Warmer than yesterday. — Still bright
& breezy — Bkfs't before 9 — After d'd
went down to the garden & was there
for some time. — After 12 o'clk, I lay
down for a rest — Ralph had a
severe fall upon the piazza while
playing with Sallie & bumped his
nose & cut his upper lip so that it
bled freely & he looked very gory.
He yelled & was utterly uncontrollable
after washing until the blood
ceased to flow he fell asleep in
Annie's arms & did not waken un-
til after we had had dinner —
Then his face was much swollen.
At 4 o'clk, Miss Toshay, Annie,
Mary (Walter's) Jim, Ralph & I went
riding — back before 6. Eve'g
the children did not get to bed
until 8.30 — I then read awhile
& got to bed at 10.30.
