## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 10, 1891

Overcast w/f. with a very little light
rain — thick weather until afternoon —
wind rising — at night, a gale, whistling
& screaming — some rain before 10 p.m.
After bkfst. Coyle came up to ask if the
should go on with the tree work on the
green — they were afraid it turned rain —
All the more reason, I told them, to go
& complete the job — which they did —
two of the largest trees were set out in
better manner than any of the others,
& most of my time during the day was
given to the work. I saw Alex.
McCollum & Azur Lathrop concerning
the Conklin wood lot — A.L. made me
sick of the whole thing & mad at
myself. Brinewall worked in the
garden gathering roots & then shook
rugs. Evening I worked on tracing
of Saunders Key mission, correcting the
drawing & perspective of tower.

A tremendous fall of Rain
during the night with very
high wind.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 11, 1891

*B. G. Northrop's lecture.*

Am overcast w/f — wind shifting
to S.W. & W. — mild & clearing — a
glorious, clear moonlight night.
I spent the m'f. in getting my
studio things in order for leaving
or packing as might be destined.
Immediately after dinner went
with Abeles Stone & marked half a
dozen Maple trees to be taken up
for Pullis Ave. Then home, got team,
went to Station & at 2.40 Train
am. bringing B.G. Northrop — I took
him through the principal streets
of town & then home. He was left
to himself for some time to attend to
letter writing. Tea at 6.30 — we had
invited A.B. Smith & wife — & Azur
Lathrop & Florance, but they failed us.
Mr. N. was somewhere at the table
us illuding of his appearances that I was
led to effect an entertaining and
instructive lecture in the evening. We
went to the Universalist Ch. at 8.15
and found an audience of perhaps
75 people — I introduced B.G.N. — a
for a little more than an hour he spoke
in a rambling, inconclusive way
that was exceedingly disappointing
to me. I had hoped for something practical
that those who heard could take some
thing away with them to think of — I'm
afraid that it was a rebast of dry
husks — We all went to Dan Sauter's
& there left B.G.N. to remain over
night — He leaves at 7.30 Am. Home
& to bed soon after 10.
