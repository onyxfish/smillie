## Left Page

### April, Thursday 16. 1891.

Warm day - wind S. & E. - Got to studio at 10 - to work painting on 20 × 24.
Repainting sky & going again over
the distant mts. Home to lunch
as usual & back - Afternoon I
gave up to drawing upon some
Montrose plans for Herrick.
Home at 5.30 - dressed - Belle
& Mary Tweeday had am'd. So had
the Adams girls - we sat down to
dinner at about 6.15 at 7.45
Belle, Mary, Annie, Kate & I went
to Chickering Hall - Concert of the
Choral Club - we spread ourselves
over many seats & kept them until
Walter, Louise, Edw'd, Mary, Kate &
Mollie, Emma & Mary Ireland -
Eliz Howe & Jno. Valentine came
in - the colony settled itself - The
Concert was very enjoyable - After
it Charl joined us - the Tweedays.
Annie & Kate went with him to his
room, I went home & to bed, but
had to get up to let them in at 11.40.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 17. 1891.

Still very warm - still east wind and
still no rains - Soon after went with
Annie to McSteny's in 3d Ave, to sign a
transfer of some Western lots to Walter.
Back & bid the T's good bye at 10 o'clk.
McAdams gives rec'd. to New Haven at
9 o'clk. I went to studio & painted
for awhile on 20 × 24. Home soon
after, while Annie & Kate took Jim
& six other youngsters to the circus I
took Ralph with me up to the Central
Park. Went to Met. Mus. of Art - closed -
then we sauntered across the Park to
Mus. of Nat. His. & spent an hour there.
Home at 5 - I wrote letter to Herrick.
Then rested until dinner at 6.40
Evg. Annie & I went to the Eden
Musée & saw Otero the Spanish singer
& dancer & the Jap. troupe - the juggler
dropped one of his three burning torches
& we narrowly escaped a panic.
Home at 10.30 & to bed.
