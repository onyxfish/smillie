## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 4, 1891

*Annie & Jim returned.*

A winter day — overcast most of the time & cold with a feeling of snow in the air — I gave up the day to Village improvements — wrote final note to W. B. [illegible] Norton arranging for his lecture a week from to-night — then to the newspaper offices & wrote notices for publication — Saw Safford & engaged Universalist Ch. Bld [illegible] — Saw Coyle & engaged him to go to work for the Soct to-morn & went after Stone & Rutan and Hill — while down at the lower end of town I called & saw Thos. Warner who is very low with heart trouble — Home before 6. Tea. Even at 8 o'clk. went to station — train 20 m. late — Annie & Jim on board — A — quite used up by a heavy cold that has its seat in head & throat — To bed before 10.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 5, 1891

A raw m'f. with fine snow filling the air — but it was not cold and as the day advanced the weather improved — a fine, starlight night. Annie quite used up with her cold, so that she did not get out of bed until about 3 p.m. I got out about V. I. Soct work soon after bkfs. Coyle & two others (names unknown) spent most of the m'f. in the wood with the team getting a load of leaf mould — Afternoon, Coyle & another man dug holes on the green for the transplanting of trees — most of the time I was there measuring spaces & driving stakes — Even'g I went to the P. O. at 8 — then to Stone's and to Stoddard's, Fanny Reed's & Dan. Sayre's It was 9 o'clk. before I got home.
