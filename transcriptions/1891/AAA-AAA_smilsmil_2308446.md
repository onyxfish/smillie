## Left Page

### June, Monday 29. 1891.

Beautiful day. My head bothered me to such an extent all day that I did not attempt to work in the studio — some time with Herrick laying out lines in cow stable & with Rutau as he worked with mortar & cement in the manure pit. In the afternoon, Miss J., Annie, Sallie, Ralph & I rode from 4 until 6.30 — the twilight was spent, as usual, watering the flower beds about the house — myself — spent about an hour over the news papers & to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 30. 1891.

A very warm day — about 7.30 I went out to new barn & superintended the beginning of the spreading of the cement floor in the manure pit by Rutau — again, after bkfs., spent some time there — and before dinner, after a horrible mess, got the loose tiles in place over bed room fireplace — in very bad fashion however — after dinner picked a lot of choice strawberries & packed a box which I sent down by exp. to Miley Cook. We had our tea early — at 6 o'clk — & at 6.40 Miss J., Walter's three girls & I went out riding & found it very delightful — back a few min. before 8 — then watered flower beds & at 10 seated myself to read letters and newspaper — a letter from Mary — also one from Walter in reference to plans for library room in 38th S. To bed soon after 10.
