## Left Page

### March, Friday 13. 1891.

Dull, damp and muggy — wind rising at night as though preparing for clear weather. Got to studio about 10 and to work on 18×30. Brought it all out with Siccatif oil & then went over the whole thing, bringing it together & brightening the high lights. Home before 1. lunch Annie lunched at Mary Cook's — back to studio at 2.15 — To work on 14×22 & treated it as I had done the other in the m'f. For a time I was quite confident that I was doing fairly good work but when later I drew my curtains & lighted my gas my work went down & then I saw that my work was only commonplace & conventional after all. Communed with Geo. for a while & then went in to see Champney's pastel head from a new model. Home a little after 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Charl dined with us & later took Mary & Kate to the Opera. Annie & I had a quiet time at home reading.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 14. 1891.

Bright, cold, windy day — a flurry of snow in the ev'g. To studio soon after 10 — spent such time as I could get pursuing over my three paintings for the N. A. Mary & Kate came before I got to work & then Schilling — later, a young woman canvassing for subscriptions to a book & then a poor sinner seeking instruction in etching. Home at 1.45 — back at 2.30 & painted upon "Hazelbrook in April" — improving it I think very much. Charl came in at about 5 o'clk, walked up town — at Cor. of Mad. Ave. we met Mr. Thos. Sturgis & he walked over to my door with me. Dinner at 6.30 — Charl dined with us — At 8 we all went to the Garden Theatre & saw and heard "Love & War" — a very commonplace presentation to a very poor house. Out at 10.30 — Home at 11 — & nearly midnight when I got to bed.
