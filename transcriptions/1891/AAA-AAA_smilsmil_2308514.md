## Left Page

### November, Thursday 12, 1891

Bright for a little while in the
m'f. — then overcast & rain before
11 — quite a good deal of rain
fell before a clearing about sun-
set. I spent all day in [illegible]
& kindred work after having, at
10 o'clk, driven to Perry Conklin's
& got a surveyor's plat of his wood
lot, then to P.O. & to Bank & saw
Gilbert the surveyor & arranged
with him to go to the Conklin lot
on Sat. m'f. if pleasant. It was
after 5 p.m. that Jim, Ralph
& I went down town — Evf.
I wrote to Walter Cook

## Right Page

### November, Friday 13, 1891

Cloudy during the day — magnificent
moonlight night. After 8[?] went down
town & spent some time directing the
men setting out trees on Public Ave.
I also superintended Herrick in tak-
ing some tall maples west of the house.
Then I got to work in doors at my
clearing up & packing being much
impeded by a stupid headache.
I wrote to T.H. Smith & C.Y. Turner.
After dinner I again went down
town to look after trees & on my
return began to pack things together
into my big box. Before 5 I again
went to look after the tree planters
& then walked out to Perry Conklin's
& told him that it was impossible
for me to settle the wood lot purchase
& that he might clear it or leave the matter
open until next Spring — Home soon
after 6 — Evf. unable to do anything
owing to headache. About 9 went
down town to P.O. & to the drug stores
