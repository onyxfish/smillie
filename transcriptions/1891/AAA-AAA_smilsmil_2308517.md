## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 18, 1891

Clear, cold & windy. After attending to some matters at home, A. let me take Mrs. Bowder to the studio to clean the windows there, after getting her started I went over to Bank & Safe Deposit & cut A's Nov. Coupons — then went downtown to Nassau St. & got the Nov. int. on W.&S.&W. Inc. bonds — then to 2 nat. bk & deposited it — then home to lunch at 1.45 — It was after 3 when I got to studio. Mrs. B. worked as long as she could see — 5 o'clk — then I went home — a little rest — & A. & I went to Fred. Ireland's, in 49th St., to dinner — after I left, & went to Water Color Socy meeting at N.A. of D. — an uninteresting affair. T.W. Wood, Pres. of N.A. of D., asked me if I would deliver an illustrated address on etching before the N.A. Schools — I gave no decided answer. Home at 10, 40 — Annie had been to the house, could not get in & had gone to [illegible] in 57th St. leaving a card — I went there, got her, home & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 19, 1891

Pleasant day — not so cold nor so windy. Clark, plumber, called at house to see about putting in some gas pipes for new connections. Walter came — It was 10 when I got to studio. Found a german woman scrubbing the floor — sent by Mrs. Bowder — went to Union Transfer Co. in 22d St. & then to Brooks Bros. & bought a ready made overcoat & suit of clothes, then to bank & again to studio. Helped the woman awhile by moving furniture. Went in to Tiffany Co's — saw Louis and gave him Walter's sketch for colored glass in the hall. Home to lunch at 1.30 — had it alone — back to studio at 3 & worked until 5 — then home — a little rest & soon after 6 — A. & I went to the Ireland's in E. 17th St. & there had dinner with Emma, Mary & Grace. They were very agreeable & in excellent spirits — Later, Chas. Collins & Miss Josephine Wisner came in — we left at about 10 — home — to bed at 11.
