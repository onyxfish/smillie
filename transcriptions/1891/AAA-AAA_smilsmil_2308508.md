## Left Page

### October, Saturday 31. 1891.

*Annie & Jim went to N.Y.* [written above printed header]

Most of the day was cloudy, with grey, luminous atmosphere — At times the sun shone out very pleasantly.
I was busy about the place until 10, then downtown, to Bank, Post Office Etc. At 11, home, sewed out with Jim, he on the donkey, I on Selim. The donkey could not and be much faster than Selim could walk + Selim was not used to the saddle + was awkward. Home at 11. 45 after a ride that could not be described as altogether success. Annie & Jim had lunch at 12 + I drove with them to the Station where they took the 12.30 train — to get to N.Y. at 3 o'clk. then to go to Edw'd. Cook's in 37 St. The funeral of Aunt Anna was this mnt. A — Expects to Return W'l C. N. — may leave Jim w— not. + drove about until 1, with [illegible] to exercise the horses — went to Broadhead + he again examined Sam's [illegible] leg + says that he is improving — Dinner at 1.30 — It was near 3 when I drove with Temp on [illegible] over to his wooded hill side that he was [illegible] me to buy — 35 acres for $500 — we tramped through the woods more than an hour — was delightful — Really very fine woods — enjoyed it very much — offered him $400 he said NO. He left me at P.O. at 5 o'clk.

Ralph & I had our tea alone (of course) at 6.30 & I spent the evng. Salins with the Nov. "Century" & newspapers.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 1. 1891.

A bleak, howling day — growing colder — wind from S.W. to N.W. — at night some snow flurries — As I did not get a very good night's rest — It was late before I got up or nearly 10 before I finished bkfs. Then I went down to garden — after that trimmed some grafted apple trees — also Blackberries & Raspberries — Then walked out to Jones' Lake to look at some young trees for transplanting — home at 1.20 — dinner at 2 — after that, rested awhile. wrote a letter to Annie before tea — After tea wrote to Geo also to Mr. Vickery to the Secty. (Trumpff) of Milwaukee Ind. Exps. Ass'n — took letters to P.O. at 9 o'clk.
