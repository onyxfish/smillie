## Left Page

### June, Thursday 25. 1891.

Cloudless, hazy, breezy. Hot.
Very soon after 6 [illegible] - a new pain
developed itself in the Region
of the Stomach - it was severe
enough to make me very un-
comfortable & I spent most
of the [illegible] on the bed. Ate
a light dinner & kept about
until after 4 when with Miss J.
Annie, Walter's Sallie & Ralph I drove
over to Bissell's & got butter - thence
home at 6.30. The [illegible] being warm
we sat upon the Piazza until 10
o'clk — then to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 26. 1891.

A very hot [illegible], abortive thunder storms
in the afternoon & very cool, breezy after
[illegible] feeling somewhat better than
yesterday but weak & depressed. Kept
about all day out of doors — tried to get
started on my studio work but had
not sufficient energy. Wrote a note
to Mr. J. Pierpont Morgan. Watched the
progress of pointing the masure [illegible]
with cement by Rutan — concluded
that I had better use mortar & cement
& sent Henick & [illegible] for more lime &
sand — So, we did not ride — much
of the afternoon was threatening in
character — a very little rain fell but
the storms passed north & south of us.
Eve[ning]. Annie & I went to [illegible] Sayre's
house — now "Rosemont Cottage" — a small
boarding house, & called upon Jessie
Edgar (Comstock) — home at 9 — a little
reading.
