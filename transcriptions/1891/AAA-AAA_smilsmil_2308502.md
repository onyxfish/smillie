## Left Page

### October, Monday 19. 1891.

Overcast m'f — Rain at night —
but a nice day full of beautiful
sunshine & color — Got to work
in etching room immediately
after bkfs. & commenced another
day of worrying with aqua-tint
grounds on "Pratt Ins." Plate,
sometimes getting beautiful
granulations over nearly all
of the plate but with some defect
so serious as to spoil all —
did not give up the fight
until after 4 o'clk. At 5
Annie & I went down town &
had a short walk — Eve'g
I wrote a note to Charl asking
him to send me a bottle of
Atwood's Alcohol, to renew the
aqua-tint ground fight — took
it to P.O.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 20. 1891.

After raining steadily all last night
it continued all day & into the night
the wind rising in the afternoon.
A bleak & very dismal day — I got
to work soon after bkfs — upon 5×7"
etching "An abandoned Brickyard —
for Etching Club cat. Stuck to it
very closely but did not get it
quite finished — At 4.15 I should
have gone down to D.B. Smith's office
to attend a meeting of Executive
Com. N.D.Socy. but my acid was hot
& every thing ready for a 20 m. bite, so
I waited & went to Smith's at 5 o'clk
no meeting. Eve'g, the wind
& rain outside made me feel as
though I were at sea or in a light
house. I read for awhile Mrs.
Custer's "Following the Guidon" — then
my newspaper — & to bed before 11.
