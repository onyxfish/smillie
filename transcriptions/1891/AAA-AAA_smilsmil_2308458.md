## Left Page

### July, Thursday 23, 1891

Light showers in the early m'g.
Threats during the day with two
or three light dashes — Again, as
usual, out of doors until noon —
then, awhile before dinner. I
sketched in charcoal "A home
upon a Hill" — an upright — a
farm house & barn & groups of trees
on top of a hill — from sketches in a
road near Brooklyn in this county.
In the afternoon I made a larger
charcoal sketch of same subject
upon at 21" × 31" canvas. Welf
wrote notes to Walter Cook on
the subject of alterations in 38
S— house. After 10 went to P.O. with
it — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 24, 1891

Heavy rain last night — cloudy until
about 11 — then, lovely day — a brisk little
thunder shower at 3, & another much more
brisk about 5 — I was in the garden
with the men until after 11 — then to
studio & worked upon 21×31 until
dinner — In the afternoon set palette
& rubbed in a scheme of color in
oil & turpentine — At 4.30 the carriage
being ready — Ralph & I drove down
to Sam. Sayre's to get Annie & Miss
Tashay — the storm being so immi-
nent we set to the house & Miss T.
took tea with us — Before 6 Ralph,
Jim & I went down town. I shopped
about for some small kegs in which
to pack down string beans in salt — Tea
about 7 — before 8 Annie, Miss T. & I
went to Chas. Sayres — saw Mr. & Mrs. Wright
& others — later, A. & I called at Judge
[illegible] Jessup's & saw Louise — others out —
Home soon after 9.
