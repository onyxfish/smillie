## Left Page

### July, Monday 27. 1891.

Delightful day — quite cool —
Ralph woke at the usual time
in the n't., complaining of stomach
ache & continued to grow worse
until at night he had quite a fever
& was evidently very miserable, so
that at 8 o'clk I went to see Dr. Rich-
ardson about him & get advice.
I worked about out of doors until
11 — then to studio, set palette &
got to work painting on 21"×31".
Dinner at 1. I painted in the
afternoon, with interruptions, till
4.30 — then out riding. As Annie
had to stay with Ralph I went off
& took Miss Foshay, Mr. Courtright &
Florence Lathrop with me — back
soon after 6 — tea — Eve'g reading
newspapers from 9 to 10 — then to
bed.

Gretta went to her home for a
two weeks visit, her sister Bessie
taking her place.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 28. 1891.

Bright n't., overcast before noon, with
threats of rain — clouded afternoon.
Ralph was up during the night in the
n't., altho' appearing to be better was
evidently weak & far from well — he con-
tinued to improve during the day.
I did not get to my studio work,
painting on 21"×31" until near noon,
& did but little before dinner. In
the afternoon I did even less — at
at last lay down upon the bed with
Ralph & we both had a little nap.
Before 5 we went riding, picking
up Annie at Ayer Lathrop's — meeting
Mr. Comstock. She got in & rode with
us. Home soon after 6. Eve'g
after reading for a while wrote
notes to H. E. Barnes concerning
studio rent & to Mr. U. R. Blaney
asking a description of the process
of corroding etchings by Electricity.
