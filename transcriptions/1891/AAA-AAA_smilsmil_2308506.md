## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 27, 1891

A howling day — snow scurries chasing one another all day long.
I worked in stretching room biting "An old dam" — aqua-tint, with
many interruptions, most of them from Herrick who was putting up the
mirror + wood work over basin in studio. Shortly after 12 I went down
town for a breeze — In the afternoon I got off by 5.50 a box for Aunt H.
with a lot of Raspkemy plants —
About 5 o'clk. I went out for another walk. Eve'g. after my newspaper
I wrote to Fred Smillie in answer to a singularly good turn [illegible] note
Rec'd — to-day replying to my note a few days ago telling him that I
have had Father's working-desk, that Father had given to Fred, sent here
as it had been lying for five years in
Uncle Willie's coal shed, neglected
and abused.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 28, 1891

Cloudy m'g. but soon cleared to be a brilliant, quiet day — I got to work
on the biting of aqua-tint "An old dam"
but was much interrupted — just by the finishing of the mirror corner
over basin in studio — I oiled the wood
\+ rubbed it down — At 11 I took
Selim to Barney's + got a saddle,
saddle-cloth + bridle + then rode
him home — In the afternoon I
had to look after Stone, also Herrick,
who is stretching canvas for ceiling
of Reception Room in 38th S. About
5.30 I went out + had a brisk walk.
Eve'g., newspaper — wrote a note
to Mary + a check for Equitable
Ins. Co.
