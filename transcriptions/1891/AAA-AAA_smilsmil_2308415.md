## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 28, 1891.

Very pleasant day — clouds & breezy growing cooler at night. It was 10.30
when I got to studio. To work on
jade plate — etching in Teak wood
base. Home at 1.30 — lunch — back
to studio at 2.30 — Annie, Kate &
the children went to Bedloe's Island
& Statue of Liberty. I etched until
near 5 — Went in to Tiffany's & saw
Mr. Mitchell about partition. That
they purpose to put up in hall way
by my studio door. Then went to
Exhib'n of Soc'y of Am. Artists — met there
Chase and Kenyon Cox — walked up to
58th St. with Cox. Home at 6.15.
Before dinner set out some plants
in back yard. Dinner before 7 —
Eve'g, a little reading. Charl
came in. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 29, 1891.

Another bright & beautiful day — A.
& I made a mistake & got up at 6.30 &
were down for b'f'st. at 7 before we found
it out. To studio at 9.30. The bldg in a
state of great confusion — every body moving —
I am the only one who does not make a change.
Frank Waller & I agreed to an exchange — I
to give a painting for a large easel plus.
I etched on jade plate & very nearly finished
the Teak stand. Home to lunch at 1, back
at 2.45. Coleman came in to discuss
the resignation of Dan't. Huntington as Pres.
of the N.A. of D. I worked until 4.45. Then
went to Vantine's & met Annie — we did
a little shopping — back to studio at 6 —
washed the brushes & cleaned palette
that I used in giving some final touches
to 20×24 "Mt. among the Adirondacks".
Home at 6.45. Dinner. Eve'g a little
reading then wrote a note to Miss
Frances Smith, a newspaper writer,
answering questions as to my views on
the subject of woman's dress — answered
that I do not think that my education
as a landscape painter especially qual-
ifies me to act as a judge — A short walk
before going to bed at 10.30.
