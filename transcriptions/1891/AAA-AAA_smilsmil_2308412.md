## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 22. 1891.

Overcast wtf. & quite cool — a very little
Rain fell about 10 A.m. then it cleared
& the afternoon became very warm —
At 9.30 P.m. & until after midnight
thunderstorms with heavy rain — Got
to studio about 10. Exp. man delivered
from F.o'K. Father's letter-press & foot rest that
used to be under his working table. I got
to work upon Jade Etching — dotting outline
a depressing work — Home at 1 — back at
2 & found Mr. Benson, Lossing & youngest daugh-
ter waiting — they made quite a visit &
left with me a fine specimen of Miss Lossing's
Illustrations with pen — worked again on Jade
Plate — Saml. Colman came in & selected
some Dry-point p[late]s. that he insists upon buy-
ing — Home soon after 6 — Dinner before 7 —
Wrote letter to Herrick & got the pkg. of Childs
seeds [?] to him — Charl came at 8 & took Kate
to call on Miss Butler (daughter of Mrs. Allen B.) &
at 9 we joined the three in Charles' Room —
Delay in getting carriage — it was after
11 when we got home & 12 when we
got to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 23. 1891.

Overcast wtf. cool for a time and then
humid & hot. A very heavy shower
with thunder after 4 P.m. — and again
after 8 o'clk. Got to studio at 10 & to work
on Jade plate. About noon mounted
Colman's Etchings — Home soon after
& back before 3 — again worked on
Jade plate. I wish I could be sure
that I am doing this plate in the right
manner — that would give me a little
more heart for the desimal load of purely
mechanical work that I am putting upon
it. As I work, almost my only incentive
is the hope of some money when I get it
done. The darkness during the shower
interfered with my work. Home at
6.15 — dinner. Wetf. Alice & Fred came
in for a few minutes — A — to make an
appointment to bring miss Morley to the
studio to look at some Etching proofs.
Annie read aloud to Kate a play
in German. I studied Henderson
on cold frame & greenhouse culture.
Charl came in for awhile — I got
to bed at 10.30
