## Left Page

### April, Sunday 12, 1891

Overcast nearly all day — very mild
& spring like — a light shower about mid
day — B[illegible] at 9 — Read paper for
awhile — then out for a walk up
5th Ave. to 80th S. Home at about 12.30 —
Wrote a letter to Mr. Benson J. Lossing
— dinner at 2 — Walter & Louise
came in — also Charl — & later
Saml. Colman — A little before 5
Annie, the boys & I walked down
to George's & met him & Nellie on their
way to the N.A. Jim stopped with
Sheldon & the rest of us went to the
galleries & spent nearly an hour
there — then home, picking up Jim on
the way — Tea at 6.30 — Read
awhile after that — & before 9
o'clk, went to the Union League &
saw the Water-Color Exh[ibition] there —
Poor enough — Home before 10 &
right to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 13, 1891

Delightful, balmy spring day. To
studio before 10 o'clk. Soon followed by
Kate & Ralph, who has come for his last
sitting to Champney. Annie came later.
Colman came & spent an hour or
more in repairing a study that he has
given me, made on the Nettherlan in '66.
I worked on "A Pool. Oct.", repainting
sky & drawing the whole thing together.
At 1 Annie, Ralph & I walked home —
lunch — back at 2.30 — Painted until
near 5 — Colman came in again —
Before 6 I went in to the Ortgies gallery
to see the Turner & Lippincott pictures
there to be sold Tues?, & Friday — met J. & d. —
& Bolton Jones. Home to dinner — Self
Annie & I went to Scottish Rite's Hall
at 8 o'clk. & heard Miss Annie Besant
lecture on "London — Its Rich & Its Poor".
Disappointed — had expected something
more intellectually clever & powerful —
So far as she presented her Theosophy it
is only emasculated Christianity and
her in talking of Wealth & Poverty she is
a blind demagogue. Out at 9.15 —
Home & to bed at 10.30.
