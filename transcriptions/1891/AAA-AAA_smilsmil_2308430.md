## Left Page

### May, Thursday 28, 1891

Pleasant & very cool — To studio at
10. Mrs. Dalzell soon called — she saw
ge? about lessons for her nephew — I spent
the mtf. in gathering things & packing
About noon Mr. S. R. Koehler came in —
& later, C. Y. Turner. It was 2 o'clk.
before I got home to lunch & 3·5 when
again I got back to studio. I
worked away until about 5 — Then
to Rose & Co. & paid $75.00 on acct. f
my bills — Then to D. B. Butler's and
paid him $75.00 & I sent $25 to the
Treas. f Century Ass'n. — Home soon after
6 — dinner before 7 — at 8.30 went to
Mtg. f D — adjourned meeting of N.Y. Art
Guild — a continuation of a wrangle
over a new constitution under their
act of incorporation — A question of
Parliamentary law that could not be clearly
strated by either side was hotly discussed
without the slightest progress — so dry so
I crept out & walked up town with
Stanley Middleton — To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 29, 1891

Heavy rain in the mtf., that ceased about
10 o'clk — muggy, niggly sort of air after that — To
studio about 10.2 continued, in a desultory sort
of way, picking up things — Geo. F. Kelly came
in at about noon & wanted permission to
publish in his "Am. Etcher" my article
in Etching Club "Publication" — I assented
if he could get permission of Club officers
as the publication was copyrighted. He
stayed until 1·5. Then I went home to
lunch. After that I discovered that in
making deposits at the Bank for Annie, on
Wedy, I had someway failed to put in a $30
coupon — I hurried to the Bk., but could not get
no trace of it — I hope to find it later. Then
to Newmans & bought a lot of hardware
for Montrose — Then to studio — there until
[illegible] walked home by way of Union League
Club & left a note for Charl. — Home to dinner
at 6.45. Evef. Charl came in and
stayed until after 10.
