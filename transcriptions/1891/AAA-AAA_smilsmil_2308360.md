## Left Page

### January, Thursday 8, 1891

Bright, crisp day. Got to studio at
10.15 — worked for a little while on
dry-point sketch of geo — then spent
the rest of the m't. making checks
for Montrose accts. & writing to Herrick
Home soon after 1 — lunch — feeling
very unike work it was 3 o'clk. before
I got back to studio — then wrote notes
to F.S. Church, C.A. Ratt & C.F. Chapman
Champney brought in Alex Harrison
who is interested in dry-point work.
Coleman came in for a few minutes
before lunch times, still sick but
enough recovered to be out. At
4.30 went to Kurtz's — not in — then home
where I found Dr. Yale — The operation
to be performed on Ralph is again post-
poned until Sunday. Before Yale left
Mrs. Parke came in to say that she could
not come to dinner. I had a short
nap before dinner time. — Evening
Annie went to her Choral Club re-
hearsal — I sat & read news papers.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 9, 1891

Very pleasant day. To studio at 10 &
to work upon a small water-color,
"A barn-yard study," commenced
to wash in the color & by 4.30 had
gotten everything but the cow as it
seemed to me in a fresh, vigorous
way. Schilling came in about Etching
club matters. Home to lunch & back
at 3 o'clk. It was near 5 when I went
to Kurtz's — he had gone home — then to
D.B. Butler's & ordered a frame or two —
then to C.A. Ratt's home with a note — then
home — dressed — Mr. W. G. Parke came
about 6 & then Elig. Howe. Dinner at
6.30. Charlie had been asked but did
come until after 8 — we had a very
pleasant eve'g of chatting & soon after
10 I took Elig. home & Charlie took
Mrs. P. to her hotel. To bed about 11.
