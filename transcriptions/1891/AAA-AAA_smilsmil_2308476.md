## Left Page

### August, Friday 28, 1891

Cloudy m'f. & warm, growing clearer & cooler until ev'f, when
it was almost cold. Got to work in studio about 11 upon Jade plate
still cleaning with scraper, stone & burnisher — more work after dinner
— soon after 4 we went out for a ride — After that I went down
town to work up Village Improvement — went to Judge Searle's & saw him
& then to Judge McCallum's and saw him — Both agreed to be present at the meeting next Tues. ev'f
& endorse us. When I got home found Mr. & Mrs. Parke there making
a call — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 29, 1891

Very bright, cool m'f., growing somewhat cloudy after 12 — Got through with my
morning duties as soon as possible & about 10 o'clk went down town to secure
the Universalist Ch. bldg. for our V. I. meeting
next Tues. ev'f. — Went to Dewey, Mink &
Hanley & got their assent (Representing Band)
then to "Republican" & got notice in. On my
way home met Mrs. Dud. [illegible] with a fine basket of plumbs for us — I worked at potting
runners from White strawberry plants & then
about among fruit trees until nearly 1 —
Dinner — a few minutes after 2 we got
away for Pine Hill — Bessie went with Norman
Parke to Dimock — Parke, with his four horse
team & big "democrat" overtook us before we got
to Fairdale. We went on to a height beyond
the hill we usually go to & had a fine view,
then drove on & around upon the Creek Road
again. Home at 6.20 — Ev'f. with newspaper.
