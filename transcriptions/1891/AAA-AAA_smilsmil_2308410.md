## Left Page

### April, Saturday 18, 1891

Very hot — wind S. & E. — but no sign of rain — To Studio soon after 10.
Spent most of the m'f. depainting a small sketch for Yale. about on the Brie at Cressy — Home & back for lunch — finished the Yale painting — then washed up my brushes. was interviewed by Miss Frances Smith, on the subject of Woman's dress from an Artist's point of View. Charl came in for a moment —
Home at 6 — dinner — Eve'g. Annie Kate & I went to Y. M. C. A. Hall to a Morse entertainment — "A Kitchen Operetta" — libretto by Lucy Gibbons Morse — music by Jos. Mosentahl — performers — the boys from Mr. Morse's school — the girls from Miss Gibbin's school — an audience of friends & patrons of the schools — a very jolly, clever little entertainment —
Soon after 8 a heavy thunder storm came up, with hail, some of which fell through the sky lights down upon the audience — Home at 10 o'clk — in a very light rain — the last dribblings of the shower — & straight to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 19, 1891

A charming April day — but very uncomfortably warm — Up late — Kef'. at 9. 30 — About 11 Ralph & I started out — went to Union League Club and saw Charl — made appointment with him & then to The Century & spent half an hour with the Inshaw paintings then down to Studio — Charl soon came & I sat for some photos — one with Ralph in my lab — was left at 1.20 just as Nellie & her boys came.
Home — dinner before 2 — Charl came in for awhile about 3 — At 4.30 Annie Kate, Jim & Ralph went off for a trip in Riverside Park & I took a nap.
Annie & party ret'. about 6.30 — tea at 7 —
At 8.30 Annie & I went to Walter's and examined & approved plans for reconstructing hallway in our house. Home a little after 10. Found Charl with Kate. To bed before 11.
