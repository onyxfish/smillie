## Left Page

### September, Monday 21, 1891

*Katie + Bessie T. left us.*

Another very warm day, the ther. getting above 80°. After bkp. I drove over to little R.R. depot, taking Anne + Bessie — Katie going with Jim, Ralph + the donkey — there we met Parke + Bessie P. The three girls are to go to Glen Summit to stay overnight with Mr. Atherton + to-morrow to go on to Scranton when they will join the Parke family. After that A. + I drove about town attending to various small matters — I went to Thos. Warner's house, he is very sick, + made a short visit — Home before noon. Dinner at 1.30 — after that I made another crayon study of a girl's head. I am beset by a desire to do this thing in dry-point for the Etching Club Cat. + at the same time I am quite sure that it is not wise for me to select such a subject. At 5 went down town — and among other places to the barber's + had my hair cut. Tea at 6.30 — a very warm, close eve'g. that passed very stupidly. Wrote a note to Stewart Bautey asking him to agree to the proposed improvements on Public Ave. + got to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 22, 1891

Another hot, dry day. After bkp. finding that Herrick had had two apple trees cut down without my consent + had sawed off half a dozen great limbs from the noble beech tree in front of his house I was angry + told him so. It put me out of humor for my work all day. I worked over jade plate, clearing up the teak wood stand at intervals going over to see how Stone + Rutan got along cutting down three maples at the side + front of the Herrick house. The horses spent nearly all of the day about the house filling themselves with grass. At 5.30 Ralph + I went down town + got back to tea shortly after 6.30. Eve'g I wrote a note to S.R. Koehler answering questions concerning mezzotinting for Miss Hale. Took it to P.O. at 9 o'clk.
