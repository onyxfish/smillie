## Left Page

### July, Friday 3, 1891

Overcast w'f. — wind from east —
clouded most of the day — cleared
about 3 — Heavy thunderstorms passed
north & south of us about 8 p.m. — we
getting only a dash of rain. Until
11 A.M. I worked out of doors — then
to Etching Room & finally made a
a start at laying a mezzotint ground
over my dotted outline after jade vase
for — um — Bishop. Worked until 1 — then
dinner — & again from 3 to 4 — my
success was not brilliant, but I
think I can get a ground sufficiently
even & of good texture — At 4 we
went out riding, keeping watch of
clouds in the west where we could
hear muttering thunder — Home at 6 —
Eve'g. again the inevitable news-
paper & a little writing

3 h. on jade plate.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 4, 1891

A dubious opening, in a weather way.
Heavy clouds & strong N.W. wind — later
it cleared partially — beautiful clouds
— beautiful sunshine & brilliant atmos-
phere. After some garden work I went
to my etching room & worked a laying
mezzotint ground on Bishop's jade plate
& found that I have much to learn in
that line. It was near noon when
I went over to the Warner woods where
Annie, Miss F. & all the children were
having a pic-nic. I built a fine
place & a fire & cooked chocolate — At
1.20 I ret'd. to the house & had a quiet,
christian-like dinner all alone — Ret'd.
to the woods at 2.30 — there until near
4 — then home & worked another hour
on jade plate — Before 6, went down
town — After tea, worked for a while
fixing up Cos. — After 8 set off our fire
works — some good rockets & fine wheels
& also of other stuff — Mrs. Garst & Mr. Richards
came over for a few minutes — then left
taking their children with them — Later —
Annie read aloud F.H. Smith's paper
in July "Century" — To bed at 10. 15

3 h. on jade plate.
