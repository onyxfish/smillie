## Left Page

### June, Saturday 13, 1891

A cloudless day, very warm in the sunshine but delightful in the shade. Ther. in-doors did not get above 72°. The m'f. was busily spent out of doors, most of the time at work putting up wire netting & trimming vines on the west front of the house. After dinner I put my bureau in order also getting out cushions, curtains & such things. After 4 I loafed about out of doors. The train being engaged hauling sand. Old N.W. Hotchkiss came in the m'f. & made up a lot of mortar preparatory to work on manure pit & foundation of new barn. After tea I worked about out of doors until 8 then down town — on my return for about an hour, I sat on piazza enjoying the delicious ev'f. air and the moonlight — then to bed — but for some unaccountable reason, got but little sleep — dozed between 1 & 2. Then Ralph became wakeful & kept me from sleep until 4 — then again I dozed until after 7.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 14, 1891

A very hot day — withering in the sunshine, but with an air so pleasant that in the shaded parts of the house the Ther. got but little above 70°. Up late — & did not have help until about 9.30 — after that, until noon, I worked upon the vines over studio — after 1 I had a short nap in the studio. Dinner at 2.30. In the afternoon wrote a letter to Geo. & looked over some of my bank accts. Ev'f. wrote a note to Shirlaw asking him to send to me his painting "Reflection" with outline studies fit for my guidance. Took letter to Post office — then sat on piazza enjoying the perfect moonlight until after 10 — then to bed.
