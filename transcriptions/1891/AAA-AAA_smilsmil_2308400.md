## Left Page

### March, Sunday 29, 1891

Overcast m'f. gradually clearing until it was a charming day.
B'kfst. at 9.30 — it was nearly 11 when
I went to see Geo. B't. Swift there
— was pleased to find Geo. much better
— home at 12 & with Annie walked
over to B'dway & 44th S. & called on
the Cooman sisters — on our way home
stopped at S. Bartholomew & heard some
music — Uncle Willie & Aunt Agnes
waiting — they dined with us & at 3 o'clk
we all went to Collyer's Ch. where the
children had an Easter service — Jim
& Ralph got pots with flowering plants
after leaving the pots at home, Annie
the boys & I walked up to 58th S. — We
went into the R.C. Cathedral & heard
some music — then home — Tea at
6.30 — Evely. Kate went to church
& Charl brought her home — I wrote
a letter to Herrick — Geo. & Emiley
came in & left before 10 — To bed
before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 30, 1891

Lovely spring day — To studio at 9.30
after a call on Geo., whom I found improving —
I painted on Charl's picture until 1 o'clk
then home — Alice Ireland lunched with
us — Annie, Kate & the youngsters went to
the Museum of Nat. His. & the Park in the
afternoon — I went to Woman's College
of Medicine in 15th S. to see the Bronze Tablet
sent by Baines Tracery Co. — It is in two
pieces & otherwise unsatisfactory — then
went over to Lockwood Daforrest's — 7 E.
10 S. He is Secy. of the Co — made my
complaints to him — then spent some
time with him looking at studies & in his
parlor & dining room — To studio at 4.30 &
painted for an hour — on my way out
met Mr. Chas. Whitehead — he insisted upon
going with me to see Geo. — then we walked
up 4th Ave. & he bought oranges & grapes & sent
back to Geo. — Dinner at 6.30 — Evely. reading
a work on Hardy flowering plants pub. by a
Pittsburgh firm of Florists — To bed at
10.
