## Left Page

### November, Sunday 8, 1891

A wonderfully lovely day. After bkfo. & morning duties I hitched the horses before the open wagon & at 10.30 drove to the different churches & left notices for the Northrofo lecture. Then, Annie the boys & I, rode to the Conklin wood lot, hitched & blanketed our horses & spent two hours loafing through the woods. It was exceedingly delightful. In the woods the leaves hang heavy upon the trees yet. Their gentle rustling & swimming spinning, showery falling was to A. & to me very enjoyable. Home at 2 & had dinner — then out pruning vines until near 5 — then a nap before tea. Sue [illegible], wrote a long letter to S. R. Koehler & before 11 o'clk took it, with other letters to the P.O. Then to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 9, 1891

The Indian Summer weather still makes heaven here but there is an ominous change in the wind that moans as it comes in shifting gusts from the east. About 10 o'clk I went to the green & found Stone, Coyle & the two Woods, at work taking up two of the largest trees. I went on down town & arranged with Raynes for a heavy pulling team as the trees with their great balls of earth are very heavy. At noon I went to Perry Conklin & found him in bed — his bad leg having broken loose again. After some talk I told him that if I found everything as described I would take his 35 acres of woodland at $500. Home to dinner. After that went to the green where I found them having a great time in trying to get the biggest tree out of the hole. It was nearly 5 when I got home. Rhinewalt worked in the garden pulling turnips etc. — at 5.30 lay down for a rest — Tea at 6.30. Eve'g wrote a short note to Sam Calwars.
