## Left Page

### April, Monday 20, 1891

Another brilliant day & delightful, being cooler than the preceding days.
To studio before 10 & spent the m'g in a general cleaning up, having got through with my painting for this Spring & making ready for a new campaign of etching. Mrs.
Geo. Moss (Emma Peale) called to get some advice concerning three paintings by her br. J. T. P., that she wishes to sell. Home to lunch & back as usual. Again worked at cleaning &
putting in order. At 6 went to 49 W. 37th S. & saw the three paintings.
Home at 6.30 — dinner. Mrs. [illegible] Summ'n
Ireland came & took Kate to rehearsal
of Mendlessohn Glee Club. Anniet & I
spent a short time quietly reading
& to bed before 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 21, 1891

An uncertain morning clearing to pleasant weather again. To
studio before 10 & after awhile got to work upon Mr. Heber Bishop's plate, a
representation of a piece of jade. I have determined to make an outline very carefully of dots — bite it — & over that lay a fine megatint ground. It will be a vast & tedious amount of purely mechanical work — but Mr. B. wants an exact representation — not a work of art — and he is willing & able to 'pay' for it — Home to lunch about 1 — Mr. Colman had left for me a ticket to the Mendlessohn Choral Club concert to-night. Back to studio before 3 & worked on jade plate. Home soon after 5 — dressed — dinner — at 7.30 walked down to Chickering Hall — 25th Anniversary of the Club. A large number of the retired members took part — a very fine concert — an enthusiastic audience. Mossentable had a rousing approbation after his 'Thanatopsis' Music. Mr. & Mrs. W[illegible] Bispham sat back of me. Home at 11 & to bed at about 11.30
