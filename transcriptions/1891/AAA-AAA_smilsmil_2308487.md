## Left Page

### September, Saturday 19. 1891.

Cloudy & uncompromising at first, but by
10.30 it cleared & was a delicious day.
Parke came up with his big wagon
& four horses & a party & took Annie &
[illegible] & [illegible] away for the day on a pic-nic. They
went to Salt Spring & had lunch, then
to Silver Lake & got home at 7.30.
I stayed at home with Ralph and
worked in the studio trying to work
out a crayon study of a girl's head &
dry-paint for Stehring Club Cat — but
after hard work until 3 o'clk. I had
to acknowledge that I had failed.
Ralph & I dined alone at 1 — at 3
I got the Donkey & we went off for a
ride — went to the Jessup farm where
I made a sketch — home at 6 — while
we were taking our tea, the others got
home from the pic-nic — Before 9 o'clk
Mr. & Mrs. Parke, Young W. Simpson and his
fiancé — Geo. Norris & Charley Post came.
They left before 10 o'clk. & then we
went to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 20. 1891.

A very warm & very pleasant day.
After breakfast I spent some time with
the horses, watching them while they
fed themselves with clover in the
young orchard. Then I went to the
garden & wandered about the place
until about 6 o'clk. Then a short nap
& dinner at 2 — wrote a little while
after that & before 4 went out for a
ride — on the Wyalusing Creek Road
passed — Tyler's barn burning.
Home at 6 — Tea. Eve'g. wrote
notes to Ross & Co., J. H. Miles, Benj. Austin,
Alex. Schelling & J. H. Smith. Took them
to P.O. before 10 o'clk. Then home & to bed.
