## Left Page

### February, Saturday 7, 1891

Wet unf. growing worse until it culminated in a horrible storm of sleet. The rain did not begin until near noon. To studio before 10 & to work making a transfer of the Morgan tracing to the plate — not very successful but I determined to use it. Then spent some time in arranging my plate. Tracing, photo, & mirror in most convenient manner for work — Home at 1 — Sheldon, Lawrence, Swift, Lawrence Peck, Merrill Bishop & Jim were taken by Annie & Kate to the Museum of Nat. History this m'f & after that had a substantial dinner — It was a noisy and jolly affair — the weather having no effect upon the youngsters' spirits — Got to studio about 3 — varnished plates & spent some time making large acid tray water tight. Home before 6. Dinner — Every Annie Kate & I went to Philharmonic & heard a delightful concert — I left them on my way home at 5th Ave. & 39th St. & went to the Century Club monthly meeting. Walter Cook elected a member — Had a fine time until near midnight — Then home. Experimented with some immense capsules filled with castor oil — managed to swallow four of them — lay quietly in bed for an hour or more & was then obliged to be quick in my movements — peace until near 6 am.

## Right Page

### February, Sunday 8, 1891

Dull & unpleasant until late in the afternoon when it began to clear & grow colder. Bkfs. late — lounged with newspaper — wrote a letter to Apollos Stone at Montrose asking him to take Coyle's place next summer. Then took a nap to make up for loss of sleep last night. Dinner at 2. Before we left the table Chard came in & spent the afternoon — about 4 o'clk. Jim & I went out for a walk — went to Century & spent some time there — then a short walk & home. Tea at 6.30 — Unf. Geo. & Nellie came in & stayed until 10 — To bed soon after.
