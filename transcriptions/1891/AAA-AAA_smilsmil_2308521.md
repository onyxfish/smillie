## Left Page

### November, Saturday 28, 1891

A sort of skim-milk m'f. overhead
— cloudy in the afternoon & slight
spittings of rain at 6 o'clk. Work
in the house detained me until
about 10 o'clk — then to studio —
made up my mind that I must
lay a new ground on Santinis Rey
plate — & I did it — then got tracing
on tissue paper pasted on card board
mat. Tacked that over plate, sprayed
it with water thro' an atomizer —
the tissue stretched as tight as a drum
& I commenced drawing — Home to
lunch at 1.30 — back before 3 — more
drawing — had a call from Schoff,
the Boston Engraver — after I lost
light I mounted photo. of Rey Mission
& got some [illegible] in press — Home at
6 — dinner — Jim has at last got
through with his visit at Edwards
& dined with us. Another quiet
ev'g in our bed room —

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 29, 1891

A cold, raw, unpleasant day — The
Ther. standing at 24°— at 10. Am.
Bkf't late — fire in library where
it was very comfortable & where I
sat during the m'f. toasting my
toes & reading the news paper until
near 1 o'clk — Then I walked up to
Central Park & back. Stopping at
Yale's & seeing Mr. Y. Yale came
to the house at about noon & said he
had some [illegible] to show me — As he was
out when I called I did not see
them. Dinner in the laundry at
2 — in the afternoon I had a nap.
Tea at 6.30 — Ev'g. I wrote to
Gifford, Mary & Mrs Beecher —
To bed after 10.
