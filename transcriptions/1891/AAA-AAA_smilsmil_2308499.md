## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 13. 1891.

Ther. 29°. last night.

A quiet, brooding day — now and then a gleam of sunshine & now and then a tearful spatter of rain. My work in studio was interfered with by stomach discomfort caused by a strain while at work setting out strawberry plants yesterday. I could not go on with my painting so, I made tracing & reduction — 5"×7" for Etching Cat. plate "Deserted Brick Yard" & laid ground on the plate — in the afternoon I made camera-lucida reduction of San Luis Rey Mission — At 5 went down Town on general business — most of it connected with the Village Improvement Soct. meeting to-night. Tea at 7. At 8 went to the meeting at Universalist Ch. Bldg. — not more than 12 or 15 present — but we had a meeting as required by our constitution. Home at 9 —

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 14. 1891.

A very pleasant day — growing milder — a lovely moonlight night. It was near 10.30 when I got to work painting on 11×16 oil sketch "An old dam". It was half painted & I completed it. Took a little airing before dinner & trimmed dead stuff out of the Ampelopsis vines — after dinner, painted until 3 — then drove out to Rev. W. Raynor's — about a mile from Town, & looked at some young maples to transplant to Public Ave. — then drove south of town, below Carey's, to see some trees there. Home before 6. Even'g, wrote notes to the mason in Wilkes Barre in answer to his bill for topsiring chimneys & to Lindenborf in N.Y. in answer to his bill for repairing a lamp more than a year ago — took notes to P.O. — To bed at 10.30
