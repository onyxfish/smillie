## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 9, 1891

A delightful day - breezy and bright.
Very warm sunshine & cool breeze -
The day passed in constant work at
all sorts of things about the place -
in vegetable garden - flower garden
& putting up Studio awnings.
Annie got a note from Edith
Wright announcing her wedding
to take place at Germantown
on Friday next — Annie at once
decided to go on to the wedding.
I would require a more hearty
invitation. About 4 o'clk. we
went out riding - Seeing Mrs. Dan.
Sayre as we passed her house, we
invited her to join us - and she
did so - Home soon after 6.
Evg. Reading newspaper and
to bed before 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 10, 1891

Warmer, but a delightful day. I
was not up to the weather - I have
not yet become acclimated & feel
stupidly uncomfortable - still, I
kept out of doors & about all the time
busy about one thing or another.
In the afternoon instead of going
riding sent Herrick & Rutans to the
little depot & got cases from M.f. -
all but bbl. of jars. I spent the rest
of the afternoon unpacking - After
tea I walked down to P.O. Florence
Lathrop joining me & together we went
down & up the hills - on my return
I stopped there & made a short
call -. Evg. I spent a part
of the times with my newspaper &
then Annie read aloud until
after 10 some amusing chapters from
"Inside our Gate". To bed ab 10.30

Harry Tyler died to-day at about
1 o'clk. after a lingering & extremely
painful illness.
