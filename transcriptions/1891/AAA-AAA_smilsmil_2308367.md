## Left Page

### January, Thursday 22, 1891

A horrible storm of wind & rain — broke away late in the afternoon — my head & throat was in such a condition that I did not dare go out of doors — So I gave up & spent the day prowling about the house like a prisoner — About 3 o'clk, after much trouble, I got a messenger boy who took a note to Nicoll & Jones at the N.A. of D. In the afternoon Annie succumbed to a very severe headache & went to bed — I spent a miserable eve'f sneezing & mopping tears — Chard came in at 9.30 with a lots of photos. to show Kate. I went to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 23, 1891

A very pleasant day but I did not go out of doors — After a very uncomfortable night I awoke in the m'f with a heavy headache. I took my b[illegible] in bed & did not get up until about noon — then I wrote some notes — about the middle of the afternoon Schilling came bringing M.S. of Yachting Club Car — In the m'f sitting in bed I had written notes to him to Nicoll & Jones, to Kurtz & to Geo — & had sent a messenger out with them & the rec'[illegible] bringing prints of portraits from Kurtz & letters from studio — After I left I wrote a new copy of the cat. getting through by 6. dinner at 6.30 — Eve'f I wrote notes to Hopkins & DeVinne & Co. to Herrick & to Horace Brewster of Montrose then read for a while & to bed about 10.
