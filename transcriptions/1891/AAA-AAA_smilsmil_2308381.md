## Left Page

### February, Thursday 19, 1891

*Genl. Sherman's funeral.*

Bright, pleasant day — Got to studio at about 10 & soon to work on Morgan going over it & revising it all — About 12.30 I walked up 5th Ave to see the crowds that had already begun to gather — Home soon after I had lunch. Annie had secured a window in 5th Ave. for the youngsters — in a little store bet. 36th & 37th St. I went with them & got there at about 3 o'clk. The procession did not get to 37th St. until 3.30 we had an excellent view of the caisson & body, the escort, the Pres., Cabinet, Army & Navy officers etc — Posts of the G.A.R. kept filing past for an hour — then we gave up & returned home without seeing the military. I lay down for half an hour to rest — then up & dressed. Soon after 6 Annie & I walked down to the studio — I put more on portrait in Champney's studio — then to Mr. Ehningers where we had a delightful little dinner — at 8 — Mr. E., A & I rode up to the Garden Theatre & saw Sara Bernhardt in Cleopatra. Altho' I could not understand the French it was a very intense performance. Sara is a tiger cat and an artist — but far, far from my ideal Cleopatra. The waits were very tedious — It was almost 12 when the curtain fell on the last act. we took Mr. E — to her coupe in front of the Bruns wick, then home & to bed — just before 1 o'clk.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 20, 1891

Snow in the early m'f. — then sleet & at last, drizzle. To studio soon after 10.30. Spent the rest of the m'f. until 12.30 writing instructions to Herrick & a note to Koshler — then went to the Ortgies gallery to see some of the things to be sold this P.M. for acct. of R.W. Weir estate. Home, lunch — at 2.30 Rel. to the gallery — met Mr. T. & Alden Weir there — very few buyers — I wanted a portrait of Raeburn, painted by himself, aug?— by Weeker, but lost it thro' clums'iness — Home about 4 — had a good nap before 6 — then up & dressed — Dinner at 6.45 — Chas. Collins, Emma Ireland & Charl— were our guests — at 8 we all went to the Lyceum Theatre & saw a very funny farce-comedy "Nerves" trans. from the French by Comyns Carr, we were all very much amused. Home before 11 & right to bed.
