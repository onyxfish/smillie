## Left Page

### June, Thursday 11. 1891.

Another hot day with delightful
breeze — A late bkfs. 8.30 — and
a busy day — much of the time
with Herrick directing several
little jobs indoors and doing
myself many things out of doors.
At 4.30 Annie, the boys & I
went out riding & did not get
back until 6.45 — then, after
tea, as a shower was coming,
I set out a number of Marion
Cos plants — The rain came at
about 8 to my great joy as the
low water in some of our cisterns
has been a matter of much concern
to me. Eve'g., newspaper
again, as usual — with an ex-
cursion, for variety, to the various
cisterns to see for myself that
the water was running in all
right. To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### June, Friday 12. 1891.

*Annie went to Germantown.*

Early m'g overcast but by 10 o'clk. it
cleared very warm. At 9.45 took
A. over to little depot & shortly after
10 she got started on her way to Ger-
mantown to attend Edith Wright's
wedding, so suddenly determined
upon for the morrow. On returning
I drove through town & attended to
some messages. The rest of the
day was divided between out-of-
door work & getting things in order
in studio & etching room. After
sunset I set out a number of lettuce
plants — Butter-cup & Henderson's N.Y.
After that I wrapped myself in a
shawl & sat upon the piazza enjoy-
ing the balmy air & the young moon until
9 o'clk. Then studied Walter's plans
for our library-reception room in 38th St. &
read newspaper. To bed at 10.
