## Left Page

### January, Monday 26, 1891

A streaked day — at times bright — at times overcast — at no time really unpleasant. Got to studio about 10 — then to N.A. & there met Nicoll & Jones — went to Kurtz's & was promised the Colman & Chapman blocks at 3 o'clk — there with N. & J. to Vautines & saw Mr. Raymond who turned us over to one of his young men & we spent an hour selecting stuffs to decorate the corridor — then to Lauckner's to get an address & home at 1.30 — lunch — after that & rest — then to studio at 3 — then to N.A. & there met Schilling & Rankin — then to Kurtz's & got the two blocks — Colman's portrait — not as satisfactory as the first one — at 4, to DeVinne's — Mr. Hopkins had been called home — I got proofs & then home soon after 5 — pretty well tired out. Dinner at 6.30. Every. completed proof reading & posted. Revised sheets to DeVinne. Annie read aloud an amusing story by E. E. Hale in Feb. Harper & we got to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 27, 1891

Very pleasant day. Got to studio at 10.30 & to work making ready to print a two pfp from dry-point sketch of George's head — but at 11 a boy came with note from DeVinne & Co. saying that Kurtz's portraits could not be printed in time to deliver on Friday — I was completely upset & went at once to DeV's where I compromised by getting them to hurry a part of the edition & take time on the rest — back to studio before 12 & worked until after 1 — then home — lunch — a rest — & to studio at 3. Annie walking with me to 29th St. I worked as long as I could see — Nicoll & Jones coming in to consult about W.C. Soc'y decorations — took 6 pfp in the afternoon & 4 in the af. — Dinner — Every. corrected more cut. pfp. & mailed them — wrote note to Horace Brewster & sent check for 3 tons hay — To bed before 10.
