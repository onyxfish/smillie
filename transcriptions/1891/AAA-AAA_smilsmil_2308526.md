## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 8, 1891

A brilliant, beautiful day —
Moran's men came at about 9 o'clk
to crate my old book case to send
it to Po'k. They made such a
mess of it that I had to stay with
them until it was done — It was
after 11 before I left the house —
I took Ralph with me & went
to Union Storage Co. leaving order to
call for book case. Then to studio
Home at 1 o'clk. Storage men came
for book case about 2 & had a great
time getting it out of the house — I
got to studio at 2.30 — Laid etching
ground all over Ray plate. Mrs.
B. came & we completed the
biting of her plate — I varnished
my plate — Home at 6. Eve'g
Annie went to her Choral Club.
I wrote notes to Mary — C.H. Bar[?]
Cabinet maker in Po'ke & W.S.J. Hyd[?]
Prs. of Phil. Socy — concerning my fill[?]
of Phil. Programmes.

*Mr. B. — 4th*

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 9, 1891

Fine day Aftert[noon?] went to the
paper maker's, Warren, Fuller & Haugh
in 42d. St — & selected a number of
samples & had them sent to the
house — About 10.30 when I got to
the studio — Worked on Ray plate
until after 1 — then went to Sand.
Colman's apartments in 25th. St — &
saw him for a few moments — then
home — It was near 3 when I got
back to studio — Miss Lossing
called. I worked as long as I
could see — then out, going to a
number of places — For ther. — to
Pach's, to Bridgeman's & to Vantine's
where I spent near an hour wan-
dering about & looking at things —
Home at 6 & with Annie went to
Edwd Cook's to dinner — Jim went
with us. I brought him home at 8
& then set? — Most of the eve'g
looking over a book of old Eng.
portraits — Home soon after 10.
