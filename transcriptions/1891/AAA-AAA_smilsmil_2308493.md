## Left Page

### October, Thursday 1. 1891.

*went to Scranton.*

Brilliant day. Took 7.30 train, with mine
& went to Scranton intending to go on to Bound
to see the Wooden horses, but at Scranton station
met Pardee who had rec'd Telephonic message
from young W— saying that his mother in N.Y. was
taken suddenly ill & his father had gone on to her —
so, we went with P. we had a ride about
town before a lunch soon after 12. It was
not long after 1 when P. & J. started on horse
back for the top of Ball Mt. — not less than
6 m. It was a pretty warm ride, on the whole
very pleasant, the day being wonderfully fine.
we got to the top about 3.30 & saw all of the
kingdoms of the earth — a very wide stretching
panorama. Started back at 4½ & got
to the house a little after 6 — tired but not
exhausted. Had a sponge bath & dinner
at 7. From 8 to 9.30 we sat in front of a
pleasant wood fire & chatted — then
to bed.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 2. 1891.

*went to Glen Summit.*

Up at 6.30 — A grey day — pleasantly fresh
but not cold. Bliss: about 7 & just before
8 took Train for Wilkesbarre & Glen
Summit, or Summit Glen, as it is
to be named — got there before 10 & was
met by Mr. Athreston — W. & Mrs. P. A & J were
the party — on our arrival a buckboard
& team was ordered & we drove at once
with Mr. Athreston to Bear Creek over
remarkably good roads — much of
the country is wilderness that has been
repeatedly burnt over — where the
timber is standing it is very fine —
we got back to dinner before 2 o'clk
& after dinner, just as we were preparing
for a stroll Mr. Worden drove up — She
took A. & J. out for a short ride to the top
of the mt. On our return we found Mr.
[illegible] just in from Wilkesbarre — after a
little visiting together we sauntered to
the station & soon after 5 took train
to W-barre where we made close con-
nection over D. & H. to Scranton — we
got there about 7 — had dinner before
8 and to bed at 10 — very tired of
course.
