## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 25, 1891

Very dull day — warm & enervating. Rain commenced about 2.30 — some heavy showers. To studio at 10 & got started painting — Repainting an old study sketch made at Montrose. Annie brought in Ralph & he played in the studio while she & Kate went to the Water-Color ex[hibition]. She took him home at 12.30 & I followed soon after 1 — lunch — back at 2.45. Finished the repainting & now feel that I have got started upon a fortnight or so of painting. Rested for awhile — home soon after 6 — dressed — Ev[elyn], Ed[ward] & Mary, M[iss] Valentine & Himma S. dined with us — Mary Willard was detained by a headache. Chal. Collins came in at about 8.30 and we had a very pleasant evening until near 10.30

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 26, 1891

A nasty day — mild with heavy showers in the m[ornin]g — growing cooler — snow and slush in the afternoon — the last of Annie's Thursdays — a few callers in spite of the weather. To studio at 10.30 — to work painting upon a canvas 14 × 23 that I laid in at Montrose a year ago last fall. Altho' the day was very dark I managed to paint very nearly over the whole of it. Rec[eived] a pleasant note from Mr. T.L. DeVinne sending check in payment for the two proofs of [illegible] & enclosing a copy of his microscopic book "Brilliants." Home to lunch & back at usual time. It was nearly 6.30 when I got home to dinner. Eliz[abeth] Stone dined with us — Ev[elyn], Annie went to rehearsal of her Choral Club. Eliz[abeth] left with her. I wrote a note to Mr. De Vinne & then worked on a plan drawing. Hammock seat, for Montrose until 10.30 About 11 when Annie came in — then to bed.
