## Left Page

### January, Sunday 18, 1891

A dull & disagreeable day. As my throat has been + threatened unpleasant things I did not go out of the house. About 11 Yale came & we had a very trying struggle with Ralph — we could not hold him still enough to have the stitches removed — & so, after two trials, he was given chloroform. At this the poor little fellow became frantic & fought as for his life — the operation required but a couple of minutes — it was 15 or 20 m before he came out from his stupor after which he seemed bright & playful. He was dressed for the first time in a week before his dinner. I spent the afternoon with him. Charl came in before dark. Tea before 7 — Ralph sitting at the table with us. Eve'g, reading and to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 19, 1891

overcast, clearing late in the day. Got to studio at 10.30 feeling very uncomfortable about the throat. Spent some time getting some small p.p. into frames for young Woodin. Then St. Gerster came in & then Schilling. Then I worked for a few minutes on the dry-point sketch head of Geo. & home at 1 o'clk. Back at 2.30 — to N.A. & then to Kurtz's where I was much vexed to find that little or no progress has been made with the Etching Club portraits — Then to Saint. Colman's — met Mr. Louis Tiffany — Mrs. Fred Ireland & Swain Gifford there — again to N.A. There until dark — to studio — sent autographs of etchers to Moss Sug. Co. Home at 6 — dinner — Eve'g divided between reading & writing out some Etching Club cat. notices that are to be printed — To bed at 10.
