## Left Page

### November, Monday 2, 1891

Bright m'f but very cold & windy —
clearer & less windy as the day passed.
Got to work printing — Took p[roof] of
5"×7" "aqua-tint" "An old dam" — much
of it, on the water especially, is over-
bitten. It was near 1 when Ralph
& I went to the Cider Mills with two
big bottles & got a gallon of cider for
8 cents — Dinner at 1.30 — after that
with Stone who is pruning fruit trees
on Herrick's place & transplanting some
small trees — then took some proof
of "A deserted Brick Yard." At 6 went
downtown — Tea — Ev'f. wrote a
letter to Annie & took it to P.O. at
9 — Some work in my garden [illegible]
& then to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 3, 1891

A cold & cloudy day — got to work
in printing room very soon after b'fast —
worked with burnisher on aqua-tint —
tint that I bit over the whole of 5"×7"
"A deserted Brickyard" — making cloud
forms in the sky & lights upon the water.
Took several p[roof]s before dinner —
after dinner rested awhile then
again proving some plates —
worked until nearly 5 o'clk. then
cleaned up & had a brisk walk
on piazza until 6. After tea wrote
a short note to Annie — took it
to P.O. There met A. B. Smith and
conferred with him concerning the
Northrop's lecture next week — then
to Dau. Sayre's & saw Mr. S. on the
same subject. Home at 9.
