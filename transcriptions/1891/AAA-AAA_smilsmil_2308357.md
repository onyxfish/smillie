## Left Page

### January, Friday 2, 1891

A nasty wet, mild day — very depressing — Got to studio at
10.30 — Got from George a piece of paper yesterday upon which
I planned a Water color — I commenced the washes in a
mad sort of way & sponged them out, trying to work somewhat in the
Dutch method — but I did not
make any satisfactory progress.
Home to lunch & back as usual —
Eve'g — at 7 o'clk. Annie, Kate
& I went to Ed. W. Cook's & dined
with Mr. & Mrs. Bishop, next door
neighbors of theirs — pleasant
— home before 11 — then to bed.

Mr. Warren Kimball died
to-day.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 3, 1891

Brilliant winter day — clear & cold —
Did not get to studio until 10.30 and
got to work upon my Water color "A
back Road" — Home at 1 & back at
2.30 & worked as long as I could see
without success enough to make
me feel that I have been justified
in taking the time. This forced
upon me as a fact, more strongly than
ever, that to be successful, I must
devote all of my energies to one thing.
I can never be more than mediocre
while I divide my attention between
oil painting — water-colors, all kinds of
etching & engraving and gardening.
My New Year's Resolve is to stick to one
thing — but what shall that one thing
be? At 5.30 went to see [illegible] Coleman
who is recovering — Home at 6 — dinner —
Eve'g Mrs. Keeler & her son came in
for a short call — then I read Janvier's
Aztec Treasure House — To bed at 10.30
