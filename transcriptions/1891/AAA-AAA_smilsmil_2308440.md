## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 17. 1891.

Very bright for a little while in the m'g
then clouds & great humidity — a most
depressing day — Evidences of thunder storm
all about — a gentle rain commenced
about 3 o'clk & continued until near 6 —
Slight showers later. Jim seems to be quite
recovered from the effects of his fall yes-
terday. I spent most of my m'g with
Hotchkiss at the new barn making a
cement bottom to the manure pit —
The gathering under Selim's jaw has broken
& discharged freely all day — But for the
rain Herrick would have taken him to
Butterfield's for Professional advice.
In the afternoon I wrote to Walter Cook
at length concerning the proposed alter-
ations in Reception Room in 38th St. At
5 I walked over to the little depot in
the rain & met Miss Joshay on the arrival
of the train — got her into stage & walked back.
After tea I walked down town to P. O.
etc — Eve'g, chatting & newspaper
to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 18. 1891.

A remarkable change in the weather.
A cold, driving north east storm — with
steady rain nearly all day — Ther. below
60°, glad to have a fire. Had one
on the hearth all day. After breakfast
for some time at work transplanting
flowering plants from one bed to another.
Then superintending Mr. McCauley
who was cleaning Studio & Etching Room
& Rutan who was cleaning my presses.
After dinner had a nap, then resumed
my house cleaning work — little rain
in the afternoon. After 5 I transplanted
a number of Nasturtiums to the bed in
the lane. Then went down town — back
to tea at 6 45. Eve'g, Annie read
aloud for a little while — then news-
paper & then to bed soon after 10.
