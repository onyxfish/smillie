## Left Page

### September, Friday 11, 1891

Charming day. Got started at about 10 o'clk. painting sketch x . "An old mill dam" from pencil sketch made day before yesterday afternoon. My intention at first was to make a 5×7 aquatint after this for the Pratt Ins: but as the subject develops I am afraid that the composition will be too complex. As I felt out of sorts in a strange way I was not able to finish the sketch. I worked upon it until about 4 o'clk. Then washed up & went down town — to new R.R. Station & around — did not get home until 6.30. Tea — Had my first real tussel with Ralph who refused to do as he was bid. He took a half dozen or more spankings before he yielded; after which he was as smiling & jolly as ever. Of the two I suffered most. Eve. with [illegible] with newspapers. Men wrote a note to Rose & Co — & to bed before 10.

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 12, 1891

Another delightful day. At 10 o'clk went down town to meet the County Commissioners in reference to the care of the Public Green by the N.D. Socy. I met two of the three at the Court House and they promptly agreed to my requests. Then to new Station & N.M. R.R. Home at about noon & was able to do a little studio work — preparing boards for painting — before dinner. All three of the commissioners & clerk, Ball, with Mr. Courtright came to the house bet. 3 & 4 o'clk, and we went over the place to give them an idea of my abilities as a landscape gardener. They did not stay more than an hour. It was nearly 6 when Annie, the boys & I went to the new R.R. Station to see the progress. Then I went to P.O. & other places & home to tea at 6.40. Katie & Bessie Throop taking tea at McClarke's. Bessie rode on horseback with Norman this m'g. Eve. quietly with newspapers. Marvellously beautiful moonlight — the girls came home after 10 with Young Warner & Chas. Sayre. To bed before 11.
