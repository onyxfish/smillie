## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 29. 1891.

overcast in'g. A heavy shower from the south soon after 10 o'elk. Rain fell heavily until after noon — then wind shifted & there was a partial clear-up. Clear, starlight night.
After b'kfs. I set to work transplanting lettuce — was driven in by the shower, then worked upon jade plate until near dinner time — after that, finished transplanting and then went up on fair ground to look about. Then gathering & putting up Poppy seeds. At 6 went down town & did not get back to tea until near 7. Eve'g, wrote a note to Budworth & son about pictures for Chicago — then finished, with pen & ink, working drawing of frame for fire wood.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 30. 1891.

*Kate came from Scranton.*

A brilliant, cloudless day. The bricklayers came at 9.30 & got to work on the studio chimney & got it done by noon & then left to attend the fair. My time until near 11 was taken up with many duties, then started down-town met Ben Wueford who told me that my art com. were to meet on the fair ground at 10 o'elk. so. I went there, met Mrs. Ben W. — & worked awhile with her — then home & ret'd to fair ground with Annie & Ralph. Home to dinner at 1 — had a nap while Annie went to station to meet Kate who came up from Scranton to stay here while I go to Berwick & Scranton on a short trip. Kate, Annie, Ralph & I went to the fair ground & were there nearly 2 hours. Then I went down to P. O. Tea at 6 ¾. Eve'g — very quiet.
