## Left Page

### July, Sunday 5, 1891

A bleak, cheerless day — cloudy
with cold N.W. winds — some spitting
rain & a shower about 6 p.m.
Spent the m'f out of doors — in the
garden & about. Dinner before
2 — & after that again out & about.
In the course of the afternoon I
wrote to Charl. — Just after
tea I was seized suddenly by
an attack of pain such as I had
June 25th. As near as I can locate
it it is in the meatrus well down to
the diaphram. The pain has a cramp
like character & comes in severe
spasms. After an hour it abated
somewhat & I wrote notes to
Mr. French of Inst. of Fine Arts, Chicago
& to Rose & Co.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 6, 1891

A charming day until about 4.30
when clouds backed up from the N.W.
& a few drops of rain fell — later,
clear again. Out of doors until 11
a.m. then to etching room & to work
upon jade plates until dinner at 1.
Afternoon, worked until near 4, then
we went out riding & got back at
6.15 Early ev'g. W. & Mr. McCallum
made a brief, formal call. Ev'g
with newspapers. To bed at 10.

3 h. on jade plates.
