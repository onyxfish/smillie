## Left Page

### May, Thursday 14, 1891

Pleasant w'f., cool + overcast afternoon — To studio soon after 10. Spent some time writing letter to Sam. Coleman giving acct of Academy dinner — Then to 2°. Nat. B'k. + opened a new acct for Annie as Treas'. Then to 42 W. 15th and called on Will H. Low to ask him to make for me a carefully corrected outline of Shirlaw's "Reflection" — but he begged off — after a pleasant chat walked up home to lunch. It was near 3 before I got to studio. Sat down to the Morgan plate and studied over it — find that the "bite" is in the main very successful — parts of the face will require rebiting where the line is already of good size — the acid has acted beautifully all over the plate. At 5.30 I went to Stern's + met Annie + we bought a folg. screen for Montrose. Home at 6.30 — dinner — Swell. Geo. Emily + Jno. Valentine called — Tobed ab't 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 15, 1891

At last, the blessed Rain — overcast m'g, rain falling in light showers before — a cessation until about 4 when it fell mainly in showers throughout the night. Got to studio at about 10 + spent the m'g working on Morgan plate — a little about the face, the background + the coat, with graver + burnisher, making test spots so that, when I got home in the afternoon, I could see the effect of my work upon the parts. After 12 Frank Walter + wife came in to look at some of my work to get an exchange for a fine English easel of his. They stayed until 2.15 without our reaching a conclusion — Then home to lunch pretty well used up by a coming headache. At Kimmel + Voigt's at 3 o'clk. + got two [illegible] from Morgan plates — very much what I had expected — To studio at 4 + tacked them down, then to Hutchinson's — in 27th St — a message for Annie — raining hard — then home and to bed entirely used up.

Charl came + took Kate to theatre.
