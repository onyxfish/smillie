## Left Page

### December, Thursday 24. 1891.

A day of wind & rain — exceedingly heavy rain in the afternoon — none after 5.30 p.m. Got to studio after 10 — laid a ground over Rey [illegible] & prepared to reinforce parts by line etching — Tacked down [illegible] to stretch that K & V. took yesterday — wrote letter to Gifford — Home about 1. Floor polishers at work — carpet men & gas fitters & man putting new hardware on front doors — Sent boy to Rockwood's & got Yale's negative — Got to studio before 3 but was not able to do much it was so dark. After 5 went to Photo-gravure Co. with Yale's negative — saw Edwards & Aut[illegible] & two of the plates — promising well — Home at 6 — dinner — Eve'g devoted to getting stockings ready for tomorrow — A little before 10 went with Brinie to Edwards — There for a few minutes & then home & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 25. 1891.

A dull, cloudy day — disagreeable, but a great improvement on yesterday — After bkfs. some time was spent in taking our X mas presents out of our stockings — Then, at about 11, I went to Charles' lodgings & to the U. A. club to learn at what times he went to N.Y. yesterday as the 7.30 train was smashed at Dobbs Ferry & seven people killed. Then I went to find Valentine's in 32° St. saw him & invited him to the house. Then to studio — Home at 12 & for more than an hour I was carving cold turkey tongue. The guests arr'd: Edw'd & many and five children (all but Jessie) — Geo. & Emily Cook — Jno. Valentine — Geo. & Willie & three children & nurse — so that we sat down to tables in dining room & library eighteen in number, not counting Edw'd's baby — Things seemed to go off very smoothly, Gretta, Barbara & Mary Moody helping at the tables — After dinner some of the children sang & played on the piano — Later, Kitty, Emma & Grace Ireland came in for awhile — all gone at 4.30 — Then I took a walk to 5th Ave. to 72d S. — Tea about 7 — Eve'g, after a little reading I wrote notes to Yale & Thos. Moran & to Agur Saturnk from whom this m'g. I rec'd an offer (for Yale) of $100 for the horses — I wrote that I would ask for cash.
