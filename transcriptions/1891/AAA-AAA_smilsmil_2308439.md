## Left Page

### June, Monday 15. 1891.

*Annie ret? from Germantown.*

An exceedingly hot day. I spent a part of the aft. with Hotchkiss who has commenced "pointing" the foundation walls under new barn. Peter Coyle also commenced work on the road & paths. I commenced to put up more wire netting & hanging verandah over front door for vines to run upon. After dinner, it was so hot & I could not induce the young ones to play in the shade so I went with them under the hemlock trees & while they played I wrote a letter to Mary & Kate. At 5 I drove to the little depot for Annie. I had to drive about & wait for more than half an hour before the train came, bringing Annie — then we drove through town attending to various small matters & then home. After tea A. & I sat on piazza — a heavenly night — & she told all that she knew, which is exceedingly little, about McCarthy or Carty, Edith's husband — I think that Edith has, with scant courtesy, imposed a man upon the family, who has a record requiring explanation. His first wife got a divorce from him on the ground of infidelity — he made no defense — got an exasperatingly impudent note from Char. By great effort fixed my boxes & wrote a bantering reply — To P.O. at 10.30 — To bed at about 11.30 —

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 16. 1891.

Hotter than ever — Ther. in 2° story rooms got up to 86°. I don't know how high, heat out of doors. I spent much of the aft. training vines about the house & getting the tangle out of them. Before noon threatenings of thunder storms are along the western sky. We had the relief afforded by shade during the afternoon, but only a few drops of rain fell — showers passing north & south of us.

In the afternoon I set out a number of lettuce plants & worked over strawberry bed picking a small mess — the first.

Jim had a fall from the hammock & gave his head a severe bump with the usual results following shock. Sleep & stupor — repeated vomiting & at times, thick, unintelligible speech. He recovered about nightfall & got to bed in a natural, rational condition. Evening the clouds passing away we had another glorious moonlight. I went down town about 8 — after that, Annie & I sat on piazza until after 10 — Then to bed.
