## Left Page

### August, Saturday 8, 1891

Very warm & close — hazy atmosphere —
a thunderstorm passed north of us about
2 o'clk. Did not go out riding — too hot.
It was 11 o'clk. before I got my
garden work done — then to studio
& worked on "Sweet Pea" dry-point
until past 1 o'clk — then dinner —
After that in new barn with Henich
planning details for new stables,
until almost 4 o'clk. Then again
to studio & worked upon dry-point
until near 6 — getting it finished
so far as I can see without a proof.
Wandered about the place until
tea time — after tea went down
Town — back about 8.30 — Evening
promenading piazza or reading
newspaper.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 9, 1891

A very close, muggy day, overcast
most of the time with now & then a
stray drop of rain — I think it is
preparing for a drouth. Spent
the mfg until 12 o'clk. in the garden
& trimming vines about the house —
Then wrote a letter to Mr. Vickery of San
Francisco about a proposed plate of the
Mission San Luis Rey Ruin. — also a note
to Mr. Macbeth — Then dinner — after
that wrote notes to Rose & Co. & to J. H.
Mills — Rode from 5.30 to 6.30 — Then
tea — Eve'g wrote a letter to Henry
R. Blaney of Boston acknowledging his
letter describing to me his Electro-Etching
process — About 10 went to P.O. with
our Sunday mail. Then home &
to bed — Thunderstorm passed near
us between 10 & 11 P.M. a few drops
of rain falling.
