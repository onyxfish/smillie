## Left Page

### March, Thursday 19, 1891

Feeling a little less miserable
to-day than yesterday, so that
before noon I was able to get
up & dress myself. I was des-
perately weak & so tender & sore
amid-ships that I could not stand
up. I managed to write a note
to Dr. Gerster & to Chas. Platt.

In the evef. Annie went to
her Choral Club rehearsal and I
crawled back to bed at 8 p.m.
Geo. & Nellie came in later.
Geo. came to my bed room but I
was too weak to talk to him &
he soon left me. I did not
compose myself for my nights
sleep until Annie came in about
11 o'clk.
Yale called about noon.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 20, 1891

Dull day with rain commencing
about the middle of the afternoon. At
night, some very heavy showers. I
breakfasted in bed, getting up later.
For a time I felt encouraged but
discomforts seemed to thicken as the
day went on. I continue very weak
& am in constant dread of a recurrence
of the pain of [illegible]. Yale came in
before noon & I had a pleasant visit
with him. After lunch Annie went
to the bank for me & dep. Etching Club check.
She also got my mail from Studio which was
bulky, Mr. Koehler's Chaney Cat. being a part
of it. Evef. early, I wrote to Dr.
Gerster (he sails to-morrow) & to Klackpel & Co.
Annie went to Mr. Rob't. Underwood Johnstone's to a meeting of the "Trio" to which we
had been invited. She went with
Walter & Louise — Louise played.
Annie came in soon after midnight.
