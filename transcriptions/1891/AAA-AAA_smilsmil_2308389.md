## Left Page

### March, Saturday 7, 1891

overcast mf., clear, mild afternoon — got
to studio about 10 — Kate + Ralph were
ahead of me — went in to Chambney's +
he soon started upon his pastel portrait
of Ralph — I rec'd to studio a note aut
or two. Miss Lossing came, with her friend
Mr. Hatch, bringing a couple of examples
of first attempts at etching. I laid a ground
for her that she might see how it is done —
then Annie came + took Kate's place with
Ralph. I then went to the Bank with W.
C. Soct. check — + to Kurtz's + got photo of Shirlaw's
painting "Reflection" — then to studio + walked
home with Ralph. Lunch — Katie + Mollie
Adams with us — At 2.30 I went down to
Fishel, Adler & Co. in Fulton St. with the Shirlaw
Photo — Saw F., his partner A. is now in San Fran-
cisco — he could not give a definite answer
without consulting him — Evidently the
photo — did not appeal very strongly to
him — went to Beekman St — + bought a
lot of Blotting paper — then to Studio — then
home at 4.30 — lay down until 6 — Dinner at
6.45 — Geo. & Smiley dined with us + later took
our Phil. Tickets + with Kate went to the Concert
at 8. I went to the Century — my two little
pictures were badly hung + looked, to me,
shocking — crude in color + clumsy in
brush work — I was humiliated! It
was after 11 when I started for home —
Walter Coale left me at his door —

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 8, 1891

Overcast mf., rain began early
in the afternoon — a wet night. Bkft
after 9 — Kate + Annie went to the Baptist
Church in 46th St — near 5th Ave — + at about 12
o'clk. I followed with Ralph + heard
the last of the sermon — then we walked down
to the Century + looked at the pictures and
sauntered thro' the house. It was 1.30 when
we got home — dinner at 2 — It was after
4 when I lay down to get a rest + about
6 when I got up, out of humor because
I had been disturbed so frequently — tea
before 7 — then dressed + with Annie was
at Mr. Lewis' 112 E. 16th St — where about fifty
were invited to a musical entertainment —
beginning at 8.15 + over at 10.15 — quite a model —
Mrs. Bowman sang + Mrs. Roper played upon
the piano with great power + skill — the Programme
was a Quartette, for strings — Beethoven — Op. 18 —
four songs by Mrs. Bowman, delightfully sung —
Lulaka — Mendelssohn — Das Erste Lied — Grumann —
Mondnacht + Frühlingsglaübi by Bohn — then
a Romanza for Viola, written + played by Otto K.
Schill with admirable piano accompaniment by
Mrs. Roper — so well received that another piece
was given in answer to the encore — Please
a Scimitate of Schumann's Op. 44 was the
concluding piece — Mrs. Roper + Messrs
Danuseuther — Miele — Schill + Hartdegen —
After the music there were light refreshments
+ a little chatting. We left before 11 — home
+ to bed at 12.
