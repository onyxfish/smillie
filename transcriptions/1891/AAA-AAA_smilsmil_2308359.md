## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 6, 1891

Overcast but not unpleasant day. It was after 10 when I got to studio
& to work upon my W.C. "A back road". Stuck to it until lunch
time — then went to Bank — drew a little money — went to Putnam's &
exchanged Annie's Xmas present, a gold pen, for a Waterman's fountain pen
with which I am now writing. Home to lunch — back at 2.30 & by 4 o'clk
had finished my drawing. Then to Davinne's & saw Mr. Hopkins — arranged
terms for printing Etching Club book — back to studio & wrote notes to Church, Colman,
Platt, Chapman & Lathrops. It was 6.30
when I got home but I managed to get
dressed in time to go with Annie to
Walter Cook's about 7 where we dined —
guests Mr. & Mrs. Hoyt, Ed. [illegible] & Mary Cook.
A very fine dinner — but it did not
stow away in an entirely satisfying
way to me owing to some internal
cussedness of mine — we got home
before 11 — Charl had taken Kate
to the theatre — I sat up for them
& then got to bed before 12.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 7, 1891

Fine winter day — Got up feeling a
little the worse for my dissipation
last night & anxious in advance con-
cerning the dissipation tonight. Got
to studio at about 10.30 & spent some
time in getting the room warm. Then
I worked for awhile on background
of dry-point sketch that I am making
of George's head from life. Before 1 o'clk.
went to Kurtz's with photo of Lathrop
& learned that Platt & Chapman had
already been there to sit for photos — then
home — lunch — back at 2.30 — Geo. sat
for me for awhile & I got some more
work done on dry-point — Gill of Springfield
came in to see him — but not to see me —
Home at 5 — lay on bed for an hour — then
dressed & with Annie at 7.30 at Dav. Oxford's
45 W. 19th St. (Bessie Riley) very cordially rec'd.
by Mrs. L., introduced to her husband — The
guests were Mr. & Mrs. Carpenter — Mr. & Mrs.
Howard Russell Butler — Mr. & Miss Lawrence —
The dinner was choice & delightfully
served — & the conversation was lively
if not profound. Mr. Lord, who hardly
looks to be as old a man as he is, was
quite jolly & Mrs. Lord was resplendent.
It was after 11 when we got home — Charl
still with Kate — discussed dinner party
& to bed at midnight.
