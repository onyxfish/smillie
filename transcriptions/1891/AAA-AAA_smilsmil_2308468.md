## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 12. 1891.

Close [illegible] & overcast by threatening
clouds — quite a shower at noon &
some lighter ones followed. — after
which the weather cleared quite
cool — My time was all taken by
out of door work until 12 — then
worked on dry-point Hollyhocks
until dinner — after that more
dry-painting & then I began to pre-
pare for printing to-morrow — cutting
zinc plates to fit the coppers. At
about 6, Parke came with W. Jones
of Scranton & we went over the place
& the studios until after 6.30 — then Tea.
Evelf. I went through the misery of
dressing for "calls" & we called on
W. & Mrs. McCallums & on W. & Mrs.
Griffiths (miss Vail) Home soon after
9 — then I worked until near 11
preparing paper for printing —
then to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 13. 1891.

Fine day — with every effort to get
to my studio work early it was after 11
before I was able to begin taking [illegible]
from my two dry points — "Dark & Light
Double Hollyhocks" & "Sweet Peas". —
Worked until dinner time — after
that, for some time with carpenter
arranging the drainage parts of the
floors of box stalls. Then again at
printing till 4.30 when the horses
were at the door & we went off &
had a ride, getting in at 7 —
Tea — after that, a little rest — then
dressed & with Annie & Katie
& Adams went, at 9.30, to the Rink
where a ball was given by
Pratz, (who called to see us in the
m[illegible]) — music from Scranton — about
a hundred people there — dancing
by cards & ice cream. met a son
& daughter of Elisha Mulford's there
& had a very pleasant chat with them.
Home at 12.30 & to bed, of course.
