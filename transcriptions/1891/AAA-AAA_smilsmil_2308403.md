## Left Page

### April, Saturday 4, 1891

"Antigone". To New Haven.

Light shattering rain in the m[orning].
Up at 6.30 - packed bag - B[rea]k[fas]t at 8
Annice & I went to G.C. Depot - Jim
acting as our escort, just before 9 - met
Eliz. St. & Emma J. - all bound for New
Haven - A. 2 & E. had seats in parlor
car - none for me - but got to N.H. all the
same - the Downes' carriage waiting -
no seat for me, but I got to McAdams'
house all the same - walking up in
a chilly, spitting rain - lay down before
dinner & had a short nap - Dinner at 1 -
the Downes' sent up a ticket so, quite
unexpectedly I was able to go to see &
hear the Greek Play "Antigone" given
by a number of ladies of New Haven -
All the parts taken by ladies - it was fine
to see the chorus of old men bearded & [illegible]
with the long, bare arms & hands of women
& to hear them sing with feminine voices -
The stage setting was simple & charming
& the costumes in excellent taste with
some slight exceptions - The groupings
were very artistic - the color effect of the
whole being very agreeable - as to the
acting none of it was very good, nor was
any of it very bad - Some of the ladies were very
handsome in their picturesque costumes - The
chorus & the orchestra were fairly good.
Out at 5 - I walked up to the A's in a light
spiteful sprinkle of rain - Tea at about 7 -
Ev[enin]g general chatteraton until 9.30
then to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 5, 1891

A pleasant day with cold, N.W. wind.
Up at about 7.30 & had a fine walk before
B[rea]k[fas]t - soon after 8 - Chatting for some time
before a pleasant fire - Reading news[pape]r -
then out for another walk - walked to the
top of "Mill Rock" & back soon after 12 -
then lay down on bed & had several naps
before dinner at 1.30 - Emma J. - Eliz.
H. & Jenny Whitteig dined with us. Mr.
Downes & the children came in just as
we had had our dinner - They all soon
left & I went out & in my wanderings
found some "pussy willows" & gathered a
bunch of branches - Back & had another hour
on the bed - soon after 6 - Annice & I went
to the Downes' to tea - in the eveng. Mrs.
Thayser & Miss Kitty Jenison called and
we had quite a lively time, being re-
freshed by ice cream & ginger ale - Back
to the Adams' soon after 10 - the A. girls &
Roger having been at the Downes' with
us later in the eveng — & to bed at 11.
