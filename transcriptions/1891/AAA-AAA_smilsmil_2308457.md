## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 21, 1891

*miss Frueauff went home.*

A delightful day — cool & breezy.
I tried to get to my work in studio
but one thing after another kept
me out of doors until dinner time.
After dinner I spent some time
with the men while they moved
the old cow shed, making of it a
wing to the new barn & carried
down to the foot of the garden the
surrey to the cowshed to be used
there as a shelter for certain things.
At 4.30 we went out riding, stopping
for Miss Foshay & taking her along —
going to Fairdale & home by the
bridge a little before 7. Miss F.
took tea with us. Evg. Mrs.
Comstock & Mrs. Tracy of Honesdale
came & spent a part of the eveg
with us, Miss F. going back with
them — To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 22, 1891

*To day (July 22d) on 10 A.m. Train.*

Another delightful day — and very
little work done in the studio. Miss
Frueauff left on the 10 A.m. Train —
little R.R. Susie going with her to
the station, Herrick driving — On his re-
turn I drove down town, leaving speci-
mens of Cos lettuce with A. B. Burns &
Mr. Christman. On my way back
met Mr. Smith & Mr. Warner — they rode
up to the house with us & then we went
over a part of the Warner farm & talked of
the possibility of my buying a few acres
adjoining our property to give more pas-
turage to the cow — nothing definite
achieved — Dinner — for the first time
in a long time our little family alone.
Tried to do some work in studio but did
not succeed — a good deal of aimless
wandering about — no riding — Went
down town between 7 & 8. Evg.
reading news paper again as usual.
