## Left Page

### July, Friday 17, 1891

*Mrs Satchell & daughter left*

Rung up by our French Alarm Clock at 3.30 A.M. & got little sleep before I had to get up at 5.30 — Beautiful mt'f — Pleasant day but very hot in the sunshine. Blefs — at 6.15 — MW. S. & Edith — Annie, Miss J. & Jim, with Herrick for driver, started at 7 for the Montrose depot. Annie & others back about 10.30 — I got to work on Jade plate soon after 10 but had interruptions — & a short nap just before dinner at 1 — worked again an hour or more in the afternoon — Mr & Mr Carke came after 4.30 with Helen & Team & made a visit. Tea at 6.30 — At 6.45 we went out for a short drive — in before 8 o'clock — Then spent some time watering plants altho' indications made me hope for Rain — Sat on Piazza until 10 — Then to bed.

3 h. on Jade plate.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 18, 1891

Rain, continuous & at times very heavy — I was out & about until 11.30 — men at work moving the privy from the foot of the hill up to new position over manure pit at new barn — In spite of the Rain they got it on to the trucks & up to its place & then abandon it. I worked for an hour on Jade plate — then dinner — Afternoon, a fire in the studio made it very pleasant there and I worked about three hours doing nearest mechanical work cleaning margin up & outline of rose — hard work as the mezzo-tint tool seems to have entered deep. Rec'd a note from Macdonough saying that Doe may clean the plate for me.
After tea I went down town and attended to messages. Evening, after newspapers Annie read aloud for a while — To bed at 11.

4 h. on Jade plate.
