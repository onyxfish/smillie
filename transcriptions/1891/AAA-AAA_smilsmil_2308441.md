## Left Page

### June, Friday 19, 1891

A Raw, chilly day with fog & between
1 & 2 o'clk. a shower. Some heavy rain
last night. I spent the mft out of
doors, part of in the garden. After
dinner I took a nap & then went
out & spent the rest of the afternoon
Transplanting nasturtiums & lettuce
Shirlaw's painting "Reflection," arr-
Eve't. with newspaper — then wrote
notes to Shirlaw & to Mr. Chas. Read —
took them to P.O. at 9.30 — After
that read awhile a book on
Entomology. Trying to learn some-
thing concerning pesky caterpillars.
To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 20, 1891

Another day of cloudiness & easterly wind
— growing warmer. Spent a good part
of the mft transplanting more lettuce
also, bossing the laying of the brick wall
on west side of cow stable. Time in
the afternoon was divided between
cleaning the strawberry vines in the cold
frame, of their superabundance of leaves
& training Creeper vines on Piazza.
After tea, worked as long as there was
light over flower beds. Eve't
read newspapers for less than an hour.
At 10 o'clk. half a dozen young darkies
came up & serenaded us — a new
Glee club. To bed before 11.
