## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 11, 1891

Brilliant day — As Annie & Kate had to go out I took Ralph down to the studio with me. A. came for him before 11. A son of Wm Harts, with Fischel, Adler & Co. came with a request that I call upon the firm this p.m. — I made a gelatine tracing from Lippincott's Water-Color "Girl's Head" 9×12 of which I am to make an etching, to please myself. Home to lunch at 1 — about 2 I went to Avery's Gallery — then in to see the spealy pictures at Ortegies' — then to the Grolier Club & saw a collection of proofs of etchings — then to 94 Fulton St & saw Fischel. He had there a painting by Young Watrous that he offered me to etch. I declined with thanks — then to Kimmel & Voight & signed 25 proof Goldsmith's Daughter — then to studio came — then geo & Nellie — then Walt & Louise & soon after we sat down to dinner which seemed to pass off pleasantly — Mr. S. left at 9.30 — the others remained until after 10 — to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 12, 1891

Dull day. Got to studio a little after 10 & after writing a confee of notes & acknowledging receipt of check from Pratt Ms., Brooklyn, payment for the plates done last summer, I got to work upon the Morgan plate & by 12.30 had laid in the coat on l.h. side of plate. Then to bank — then home — lunch at 1.30 — at 2.30 went to Centring and left copy of "A Publication by W.Y. Etching Club" — then onto H.D. Allens — W. 47th St. & was in his chair from 3 to 5 o'clk. & had a hard time having a filling rubbing away down upon the fang of one of my teeth. Walked up 5th Ave to 69th St. then home. Dinner at 6.30. Eve'g. Annie went to Choral Club Rehearsal — I read, until 10 o'clk. — Whistler's "Gentle art of Making Enemies". Then to bed.
