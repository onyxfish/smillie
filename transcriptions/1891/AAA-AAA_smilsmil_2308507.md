## Left Page

### October, Thursday 29, 1891

A lovely day — hazy & mild — a perfect Oct. day. Annie rec'd
news of Aunt Anna Ireland's
death at East Southhampton yester-
day. I got to my biting on Pratt
Mrs. plate early but was much
interrupted. Stone got plants of
Sweet Briar from Saul. Brewster and
White Peony Root from Allen's.
Herrick stretched canvas for ceil-
in 38' 8: & I sized it — gave it
another coat in the afternoon
& did considerable pruning.
I worked on plate until near
5, then down town. — Evey
wrote a long letter to S.R. Koehler
detailing my aqua-tint trials.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 30, 1891

Another mild & delightful day.
I got to work at 10 o'clk. finishing
the biting on the Pratt plate, but,
as usual, was much disturbed
by other things that required
attention. At 11 I went down town
for the mail as Annie was anxious
to know if anything further had
arrived from the Irelands — there
was nothing, so she does not go
to NY. today. I worked for awhile
before dinner sawing off the top
of a vigorous young maplee that is
coming up between us & the sunset.
After dinner I cleaned ground from
Pratt plate. At 4.30 went down
town & with A.B. Smith drove stakes
for the tree planting on Public Ave
Evey. I finished letter to
S.R. Koehler.
