## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 14, 1891

A delightful, very warm day — clear until late afternoon when the sky was clouded now & then, in the eve, a few drops of rain fell. To studio at 10 — & to work painting on an old picture "A memory of the Connecticut" trying to make something out of it. I also repainted the sky in "A Pool" & worked greens into other parts. Home to lunch after having gone to the Safe Deposit — back to studio at 3. Painted a little while — then rested until 6 — home — Dinner at 7 — Geo & Ninety, Eliz, Home, Jno. Valentine & Chase dined with us. At 8 we all went to the 5th Ave. Theatre in 24th St. & saw & heard a medly amusing play "Alabama" — a study or sketch of southern characters — It was fairly well acted taken all together. We walked up home — Ed & many Cook joined us at the theatre — To bed shortly before 12.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 15, 1891

Uncomfortably warm day — overcast in the afternoon, growing suddenly very chilly at 5 P.m. — To studio at 10 and worked all the m/f over sky & distance in 20×24 "The Gothic Mts. from Roaring Brook a Giant of the Valley". — To 2d, Nat. Bk. & then home at 1 — Katie & Malley Adams arr'd from New Haven — lunch — At 2.30 at Dr. Allen's 55 W. 47th St. & had an unpleasant hour while he put a filling in one of my front teeth — At 4 rode down 7th Ave. to 15th St. & went to 359 W. & enquired after W. S. B. Koehler — hearing that he was sick — he is in Washington — then wandered down to the wharfs & to the New Washington Market & at last got to the studio at 5 — home at 6 dinner at 6.30 — The Adams girls dined & Ed & Cook's. Well. Annie & I went around there — Walter & Louise there. We stayed until after 10 —
