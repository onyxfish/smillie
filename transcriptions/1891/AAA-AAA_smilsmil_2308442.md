## Left Page

### June, Sunday 21, 1891

And yet another very disagreeable day — mild but exceedingly damp, with S. E. winds, heavy showers & between times heavy fog & driving mizzle. I did not get away from the house except when I went down to the garden in the m'f. I spent some times washing the bottles & putting them away in my dark room — also in getting printing paper in order & transfered from studio closet, where it has been all winter, to drawers in printing table. Eve'f I commenced writing up my Garden mem. book. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 22, 1891

Foggy m'f. gradually clearing — wind shifting to the west. Clouds & some tremendously hot sunshine. Just before 6 a very heavy thunder storm with floods of rain that filled our cisterns. I spent all of the m'f out of doors directing & working myself with the men — in the garden & at the new barn. At about 4.30 we started out for a drive but when we got near S°. Montrose the weather suddenly became so threatening that I at once turned about & got home just in time to escape a ducking. It rained until after 7 then I wandered about for an hour to see what damage had been done. A beautiful sunset & rainbow. Eve'f after reading newspaper I spent some time in writing up my garden mem. book. To bed at 10.30.
