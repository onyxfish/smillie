## Left Page

### August, Saturday 22, 1891

*Katie Adams left us.*

A perfect day — up before 6. H[illegible]. At
6.30 & at 7 Annie, Katie the boys & I started
in open wagon for Montrose depot. The
morning was delicious & we all enjoyed
the ride mightily — got to Station in
1h. 10 m. Train on time — Goodbye, [illegible]
Drove home slowly — going up long hill
to Button's & then across on ridge to old
road — home at 10.30. I took file of
old war Tribunes down to G. W. Lyon's
to have them bound & attended to
other matters, then home & had a nap
before dinner — feeling useless in
the afternoon I spent it out of doors
directing the carpenter about the new
barn. Evely Annie & I went to
Daniel Sayre's to lay plans for the
Village Improvement Soc'y organization
meeting to be held next Tues. Evely.
He was sick in bed but we talked
the matter over with Mr. S — Home
about 9.30 & to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 23, 1891

A series of heavy showers all day
commencing about 9 a.m. Heavy thunder
showers between 2 & 3 o'clk. I was out
of doors all the m'f — in the garden during
the heavy rain — From noon to dinner at
2 and & lounged. Afternoon I wrote
notes to Yale, to Charl — to Mary and
others — Evely I wrote, without finish-
ing, a note to Independent Republican
concerning Village Improvement — Went
down to P.O. with letters at 10 o'clk.
