## Left Page

### February, Monday 9, 1891

Dismal wt., before noon sleet began to fall & before night it was horrible, growing warmer with heavy showers. Got to studio about 10 — too dark to see to etch but busied my self writing notes & making such preparations as I could for the etching of the Morgan Portrait. Home to lunch a little before 1 & at 1.30 went down-town to 62 Wall St. getting there at 2.10 to attend (as a Trustee) a meeting of The Sauce Creek Cattle Co. met there Mr. Geo. Burnham — W. Daylath, Horde & Kendrick — meeting continued until near 5 o'clk. It was decided to liquidate the herd, sell the Sauce Creek property & feed cattle at the Central City station. Home at 5.30 — dinner at 6.30 Eveg. Annie & Kate went to the Opera & at 8 o'clk I went to S. P. Avery's — after a short visit with his mother & two daughters I went up to S. P. A.'s Room & until 10 we looked over etching [illegible] — especially Flameng's — studying heads & portraits — then to Messenger services & arranged to have a boy sent for [illegible] &c. at midnight thru home & to bed & lay awake until they came in —

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 10, 1891

Warming day — got up with a headache. To studio at 10.30 & commenced laying in the background of the Morgan Portrait. I went over it all first with my multitpee point of four needles, then, with a large Rat-tail file point, I laid in a bold single line arrangement. Soon after 12 I went to the N. A. of D. & there to bank & made a deposit. Home to lunch at 1 — after that went to bed & did not get up until after 5 — then dressed — dinner — Evening, Annie & I went to Chickering hall and there met Emma J. — 2°. concert of the Mendelssohn Glee Club — we got good seats in the gallery & enjoyed the concert very much — A. & I walked home getting at 11.15 & there to bed.
