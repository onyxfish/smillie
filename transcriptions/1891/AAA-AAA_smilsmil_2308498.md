## Left Page

### October, Sunday 11, 1891

A cold, bleak day — wind from
the north — It was nearly 11 when
after my regular morning work
Annie, the boys & I started
out for a tramp. We walked
first to the new Engine house &
the coal pockets of the N.Y. & [illegible]
R.R. then by road to Harrington
mills & thence along the track for
a mile or so. Returning across
lots & the road, getting in at
2 o'clk tired & wind blown. After
dinner I had quite a nap — then
wrote a letter to Walker Cook —
then walked down town & saw
Rutan & Stone — back at 7 & had
a cup of tea — Eve'g. wrote
a long letter to Mr. Herbert Beecher
of Port Townsend, Washington answering questions concerning etching.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 12, 1891

**Ther. fell to 23°. last night.**

A very still, bright & cold m'g
with evidences of a severe frost
every where. The day grew milder
& became overcast & brooding — a
very rich sunset & twilight. Now
that the flies are again clear the
fires all burned brightly & in-doors
was very pleasant — I went down
town at 9.30 — got to work painting
at 11 o'clc. on San Buis Ray mission —
Stone worked all day getting straw —
very bed (where poppies & sweet peas
grew) ready for the placing of plants,
taking out a great lot of stones — I
painted until 4 — then worked
with him until 6.     Eve'g
with news papers & wrote a short
note to Chase.
