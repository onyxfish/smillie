## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 3, 1891

A dismal day with some very heavy showers about 1 o'clk — mizzle + fog
for the rest of the day — Got to studio at about 10.30 intending to begin on
a course of painting but my head was so uncomfortable + I felt so
generally under the weather that I fussed about, dampening + putting off
etchings in press — writing notes,
etc. until about 11.30 when I went
over to the N.A. of D. There for an hour —
then to Kurtz's — thro' to 2°. Nat. Reut —
then home — lunch — it was 3 o'clk
when again I got to studio — little
was done until 5.30 — went to 116 W
41st S. + left Nattress's photo. for Mr. Snell —
then home — dinner at 6.30 — every
lounged in big chair + read news-
paper.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 4, 1891

*Dined at Howard White's, Orange.*

Very cold, windy day — got to studio
a little after 10 — Some time before I
got the room comfortably warm. After
writing two or three notes I made a
start on my two plates, the Morgan
portrait + the Bishop's jade, commenc-
ed tracing the two on gelatine — at
12.30 started for Dentist H.D. Allen
155 W. 47th St — he kept me until 1.30 —
took an impression of part of my mouth —
It was near 2 when I got home to
lunch — after that, dressed — to studio
after 3 — worked until 5 — then walked
down to 18th St — got onto a "blue car" and
Annie almost immediately followed me.
Rode to Christopher St — ferry + took 5.45
train for "Mountain Station" getting there
about 6.30 — Very warmly rec'd. by Mr.
+ Mrs. White + Mrs. Parker — Sat down
to dinner at about 7 — we had a good
time — all hands being jolly until
1.30 — took 9.37 train in to N.Y. + got
home very comfortably before 11 — A
there was still a good fire in the grate —
sat down + read a short story in cutting
[illegible] + got to bed about 11.45.
