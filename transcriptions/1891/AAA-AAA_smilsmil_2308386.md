## Left Page

### March, Sunday 1. 1891.

A bright, cold winter's day with
strong, cutting west wind. The Ther.
fell to 18° before we went to bed.
Up late in the m'f. H'f'. at 9.30
Sat by dining room fire reading till
after 11. Then, with Jim & Ralph went
to studio & thence to W.C. [illegible] where
I met Dielman & his wife & miss
Mc. Chesnay. It was 1.30 when we
got home. Dinner at 2. The A's
dined at Ed'w'd Cook's. At 4 I went
out for a walk. Up Mad. Ave. to 57th.
across to 7. Ave. to see the new Music
hall — then to Central Park (met Nicoll)
across the Park to 72d S. & 5th Ave — up to
77th S. to see new Synagogue — then
home at 5.30 — 5 miles in 1½ hour —
Tea at 7 — Eve'f reading in Harper
for March until near 10.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 2. 1891.

Very cold clear m'f. Ther. as low as 6°.
Partially overcast in the afternoon. Got
to studio at 10.5 & after an hour or so
got through with painting on "Bit in
Riverside Park" & then took up sketch
painted at Montrose last summer "View
in the Brie near Cressy." Yale & I make
an exchange. That is, he circumcised
Ralph & I give him the sketch. At
about 1 went to S'ffs. Office with drawing
for Hammock seat & sent it to Herrick.
Then home to lunch. The Adamses gave
it? to New Haven by 10 o'clk. Train. Back
to studio at 2.30 — Painted on Brie sketch
until after 4 — washed brushes & went
to N.A. at 5. Home at 6 — dinner —
Eve'f, Annie & Kate went to Opera.
I sat & read until after 9 — then
Charl came in & sat until 10.30 —
then I went to bed.
