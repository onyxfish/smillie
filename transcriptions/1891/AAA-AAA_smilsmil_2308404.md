## Left Page

### April, Monday 6, 1891.

*Back to N.Y.*

A very pleasant day - her. at 28°.
very early in the m'g. Up. before 7 - H'p.
before 8 - A. + I said our good byes and
walked to the Downs' before 9 - at 9.10 with
Emma + Elie' - we rode in the D. camp'
to the R.R. Station getting there at 9.25 - the
train on time 9.30 - very crowded - away at
9.35 - Annie left us at Bridgeport to visit
the Irelands there - we got to N.Y. at 11.40
I went home at once - all were there -
lunch soon after 1 - To George's before 3
I found him much improved - then to Studio
then to N.A. - none of my pictures sold.
A Mr. Woodward introduced himself
to me + we had a long conversation about
paintings + painters - Then to Binder-
man's in 19th St - + ordered some seeds for
Montrose - Then to Klipple's + saw some
Dutch Etchings - Met Cha'. Kurtz there +
we walked over to 3d Ave. at 8.52 - Then
I bought acids + measuring glasses - To
Studio after 6 + home at 6.30 - Annie got
home at 5.30 - Dinner - every. a little
reading + to bed at about 9.30.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 7, 1891.

A pleasant day - with wind + clouds.
Stopped at George's before I went up to
Studio. To work on 20×24 "A bit of
brook with some Sheep" + made quite
a radical change, painting out some
dark small bushes that hid the brook + throwing
the centre of shade beyond the brook, to
the advantage of the picture, I think. To
lunch at 1 - after that spent some time
writing out list for Henderson seeds - About
3 I walked with Annie, Kate + the boys
over to Lepic Hall to see the youngsters
dancing class. Was amused + pleased
in watching Jim - At about 4 o'clk went
down to Courtlandt St. to Henderson's + bot.
15 dollars worth of seeds + books - then up to
Studio - cleaned up + home a little after
6 - A telegram had just been rec'd from
Mr. Koehler saying that he was too ill to
come to dine with us - Entirely being at the
house, Annie impressed her to send a
messenger boy after Geo. Cook - We waited
dinner for him until after 7 - then sat
down. It was near 8 when he came, looking
pr[e]ttily - no message having reached him
he joined us. Later in the even'g, Charl came
in - I got his check for $270 - payment
on the picture I have just finished for
him. Annie, Miley, Geo. + I tried a game
of whist — Combs and left us soon after
10 but I sat + read my garden books
until after 11. — To bed at 11.30 -
