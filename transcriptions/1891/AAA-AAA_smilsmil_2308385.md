## Left Page

### February, Friday 27, 1891

Bright & cold with some snow flurries.
Horrible underfoot. The two inches of slush
froze & this a.m. began to thaw — but it
did not all melt. Got to studio about
10.30 & to work painting on 14×23. At
12.30 I went to Kurtz's with "Reflection"
Shirlaw's painting, to have it photographed.
Then to bank — then home. Lunch, then to
studio at 2.30. A daughter of Benson, Lossing
& another young lady waiting for me — to ask
questions about etching. I finished the painting
on 14×23 & then worked on the old study
12×17, that I painted over on Wed.
I repainted a group of trees in middle
distance & worked until 4.45. Then
went in to see Miss Abbott, whose father
died on Sunday & was buried on Wed. —
Home at 5.30 & had a nap before dinner.
Katie & Mollie Adams with us. Every
they, with Annie & Kate, went to the Opera.
I had a good time alone reading — then
I worked awhile on drawing for hammock
seat & at 10.30 went to bed.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 28, 1891

Some bright sunshine & some cloud with
strong east wind. Got to studio a little
after 10, followed soon after by Annie, Jim
& Ralph, & a little later by Kate & then Katie
& Mollie Adams. It must have been
nearly 11 when Annie, Jim & I went into
Chaimber's & he soon got to work upon
a pastel sketch of Ralph's head. I
soon left them & got to studio & got to
work painting upon a small upright
that I laid in last spring, a little passage
in Riverside Park — owing to interruptions
I did not make much progress. It was
after 1 when we started for home — lunch.
Near 3 when I got to studio. Painted
until 5. Chase came in — then Mr. Chat.
Whitehead. It was 6 when I got home
— dressed — at 7 we sat down to dinner —
The Adams girls — Fred, Ireland & Alice,
Churchill Carmalt, Kate, Annie & I. We
were not as hilarious a company as in
old days, when Emma & Mary were
in great form but we had a pleasant
quiet time. At 10.20 Fred, Alice
& Carmalt left us — then to bed.
