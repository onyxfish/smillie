## Left Page

### March, Thursday 5, 1891

Bright, beautiful day — went down to Chambers St — Dept. of Public works, & paid Croton Taxes, in arrears. Then to 23°. St. & stopped at Art Guild. Rooms t saw J Harrison miles — Then to studio & got started painting soon after 11, worked on 20×30 "A meadow creek" — until 12.30 — Then to Bank — Then to Kurtz's & got painting of Shiplaws — Then to N.A. & got remaining pfs. of etchings in frames & took them to studio. Then home ab't .30 — lunch — To studio at 2.45 & painted until 4.30 — am not proud of my work — it is purely a studio painting, conventional and artificial — a work of "chic" — I had hoped to do better — At 5.30 I went to Kelpael's with 4 copies of Etching Club Publication — W. Jordan paid me for some etchings sold — Then to Vantine's & got a small hot water kettle. Home at 6.30 — Dinner — Eve'g. Annie went to her Choral Club Rehearsals. I read awhile — then some Montrose work —

## Right Page

### March, Friday 6, 1891

Bright pleasant m'g. — overcast afternoon & eve'g. Got to studio about 10. feeling unlike work but I painted a little on 48 × 30 "A meadow brook". I wrote two or three notes & at about 1 went to the bank — then home to lunch. After lunch a severe pain in stomach came on & I lay down on my bed for half an hour — Then rode down to Vantine's with my new, leaky tea kettle — Then to studio. W. Jones of Scranton came in to learn something about etching as he wishes to deliver a lecture on the subject — Then Dt. Yall came — After they left I put some etching pfs. in press & then wrote a letter to Heinck — Home soon after 6 — dinner — Eve'g wrote a note to N.A. Warner — Read awhile & got to bed soon after 10.
