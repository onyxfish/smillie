## Left Page

### March, Monday 9, 1891

A dark, mizzling day with very heavy showers & thunder & lightning at night. Got to studio at 10.5 & spent some time getting things ready to do some printing. It was after 11 before I began to take proofs from an old plate upon which I have recently worked "From Dean's Gap, Lake Geo." Nothing went right — not surprising when the weather & the condition of my head were considered — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.40 & printed until after 4 without accomplishing anything satisfactory — It was after 6 when I got home, well wet by a showery deluge — Found Mary, just ham' — from P.O. — Charle was in for a few minutes — dinner — Evel't, Mary & Kate went to the Opera — Annie & I kept house — she napped & I read a little — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 10, 1891

Brilliant, beautiful day — Got to studio at 10.15 & to work as soon as possible. Proving my Shirlaw mezzotint. I got along very satisfactorily — Before 1 I walked up 5th Ave. and stopped at Muet. Dist. Mess. Co's office, near 37 St. to scold about the failure of the messenger to meet Mary & Kate at the Opera House last night. To lunch soon after 1 — Mary & Kate in Brooklyn — back to studio before 2.30 & finished proving the mezzotint, having taken 11 pts in three kinds of paper — Just as I was cleaning up Mr. J.H. Jordan of Keppel & Co. came in & after considerable dicker he bought half a dozen of my pts for $20.00. Sleep $4. Hollyhocks $4. Aqua-tint $3. Dry-point portrait $3. Pansies $2.50. Soft-ground $2. Etching $2. Was much depressed to learn through him how little I could hope for from Keppel in the way of practical encouragement to do artistic work. Home soon after 6 — dinner — evening Dillving & his wife called — then Geo. & Nellie at them. Char. — A great jabber was kept up until 10 o'clk. To bed soon after that.
