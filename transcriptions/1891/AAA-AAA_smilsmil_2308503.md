## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 21. 1891

A cloudy & windy day, not altogether
unpleasant — a little driving rain
about 11 o'clk. At 9.30 I went to
A.B. Smith's office & there met a
quorum of the B.J. Exec. Com. — to con-
sult concerning the improvements
on Public Ave. It was 11 before I
got to my work. Finished biting
on 5"×7" etching "A deserted Brick
Yard" — cleaned plate — laid a
soft ground on Saudin's Rey plate.
Then Parke came — He & Mr. P. up
from Scranton for the day — they stayed
to dinner — after that I hitched up
& with P. drove to the Blacksmith
shop of Broadhead to consult him in
the matter of Sam's lameness. After
looking the horse over he thought he
could better him by treatment &
I consented to let him try it. Back
home at 5 — a little work in studio
& at 6 walked down to Rutam's to
see him about work to-morrow —
then home to tea. Evening
wrote notes to Fred Smillie — Aunt
Huldah.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 22. 1891

A dismal, cloudy day with much
wind & very cold. Stone & Rutam
returned to work to-day — the horses
were required to work the roller, so
Sam was not taken to be blistered —
I got to work soon after 10 & took 3
prints from 5"×7" plate. "A deserted
Brick Yard". Then laid a ground on
it & prepared for biting. Went
down town at 12.40 — after dinner
drew more lines on 5×7 & bit them
— cleaned plate — made aquatint
solution — then my regular chores
& it was 6 o'clk. before I was
ready for an airing — walked down
town & back. Evening, sitting in
warm room before a pleasant
fire listening to roaring wind &
rattling casement. Wrote a
note to Charl & read newspaper.
