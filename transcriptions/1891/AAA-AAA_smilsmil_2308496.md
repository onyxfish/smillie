## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 7, 1891

Heavy rain all day long — at times coming down in deluges. Another mt passed & I did not get at my studio work but was directing Herrick & writing to Parke concerning the sale of the horses & on Village Improvement business — After dinner I spent some time in the new barn directing the man who was lining the mangers with gal. iron — then made a working drawing for small closet & mirror over wash basin in studio. At 5 o'clk. went down town, caught in very heavy shower — back at 6 — Evg. after my newspaper I read aloud for awhile & [illegible] from Harper for Oct.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 8, 1891

A little cold sunshine in the mfg., then a bleak, raw day — wind N.W. and many spray-like showers. Got to work in earnest on San Luis Rey mission painting 11"×22". Got it carefully outlined in the mfg. and in the afternoon rubbed in a scheme of color in transparent pigments using turpentine, siccatif & oil as a vehicle. At 5 went down town, taking the boys with me — back at 6 — Evg. at 8 went to Mr. Daut. Sayre's to a meeting of the Executive Com. of N.Y. Soct. — no quorum — Mr. Sayre, Mrs. Williams, Mr. Judge Searle, Miss Fanny Reed & H.B. Smith there. We got through the routine business of passing bills Rendered — Home soon after 9 — wrote a short note to Geo. Hook & to P.O. To bed soon after 10.
