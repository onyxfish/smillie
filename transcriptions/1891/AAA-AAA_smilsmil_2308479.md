## Left Page

### September, Thursday 3, 1891

Overcast w'f., some bright, hot sunshine in the afternoon — a dash of rain soon after 6 P.M. — Starlit eve'f. Spent all day out of doors, in garden — working among white strawberry plants — and clearing out among poppy beds. Dinner soon after 1 — Some time with Stone & Rutain directing the digging of drain across carriage road — At 4.30, Annie, Kate, Bessie, Ralph & I went out for a ride — just as we got back to town there was a sprinkle of rain, a smart shower for half a minute & then a hold up until we got home. Tea — Eve'f with newspaper until 8 —

## Right Page

*Charl came from Lake George.*

### September, Friday 4, 1891

Muggy & overcast w'f. — then some hot sunshine & overcast again late in the afternoon. Immediately after bkf., Bessie, the boys & I went to the M't L. Depot & met Charl as he came in on the 9 o'c train — after my duties in the garden I went with him to the site of the permanent M't L. R.R. depot & had a talk with the boss carpenter — There is promise of a pretty little station prettily situated. Dinner — Spent some time directing Stone & Rutain in building drain across carriage road and soon after 4 o'clk. Charl, Kate, Bessie & I went off for a ride — back at 6 — dressed & at 6.50 got to W. G. Parke's where we had been invited to take high tea. As usual, it was a meal very pleasantly served — after it we chatted, the most of the time being taken by Charl and P— in stories of the far west & border experiences. Home before 10.30 —
