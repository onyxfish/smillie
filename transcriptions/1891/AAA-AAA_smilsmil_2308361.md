## Left Page

### January, Saturday 10, 1891.

Delightful day - clear & mild - got to Studio before 10 - C. A. Platt came soon after to give me criticism of the proof sent up by Scribner - Got to work on "A barn-yard Study" which seemed to run along very successfully Nearly went to Kurtz's about Etching Club portraits - Home at 1.30 - back to Studio at 2.30 - & worked until 4 when I set my drawing aside as finished - Dr. Gerster came in for half an hour - I rested for a while & home at 6.15 dressed - dinner - Eve'g. Annie, Kate & I went to Phil. Concert. I could not enjoy it as I was in such nervous anxiety over the operation likely to be performed upon Ralph to-morrow - my mind would constantly revert to it in spite of myself. Concert over at 10 o'clk - A. & K. left me at the Cor. of 5th Ave. & 40th St. & I went to the New Century Building - 43d St. near 5th Ave. - to attend the first meeting of the Club there - a very full meeting - the house was very draughty & cold — much of the interior is yet unfinished — some ferment among the artist members over the condition of things in the gallery. I was more interested in arranging with Yale for to-morrow than in anything else - My Cliffs of Normandy had a splendid place - so did "The Passing Herd." - I left at 11.30 - commenced to rain just as I got home - to bed soon after 12 & to sleep after 1.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 11, 1891.

*Ralph was circumcised.*

A dull wet day with a gale of wind in the afternoon - a quiet, foggy eve'g. Bkfs. soon after 9 - About 10.45 Dr. Yale came bringing Dr. Humphrey with him - Preparations were promptly made for the operation but it was 11.30 before they were ready to begin. Ralph submitted with the unquestioning sweetness of a lamb until the ether cone was placed over his face. The struggle then was brief - Dr. H. attended to the ether & Dr. Y. performed the operation, which was much retarded by violent vomiting while under the influence of the ether - It was 3/4 of an hour from the commencement to the end of the operation. As Ralph recovered he had further vomiting raising nothing but mucus. The poor little fellow was in great distress but after a nap or two fell into a very quiet sleep which must have lasted nearly an hour. During the afternoon he was wonderfully patient & good altho' at times evidently suffering from nausea. From time to time he had spoonsful of hot milk or cold water. About 6 he ate a cracker & drank some milk - It was after 8 before he went to sleep.
