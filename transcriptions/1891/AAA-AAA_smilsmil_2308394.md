## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 17, 1891

*An attack of gastralgia*

Bright, pleasant wth. After my usual domestic duties, on my way to the studio, I called on C.A. Patt at his home & had a pleasant chat with him. Got to studio about 10.30 & at once to work upon Chas's painting. It was soon after 11 when I felt the first twinge of a pain that I knew too well — a neuralgic pain in the stomach. I cleaned my palette & started for home, getting there before 12 & in great pain. I curled myself up upon my bed & a messenger was sent for Dr. Yale. He did not come until about 2.30 & then gave me a half dose of opium by Hypo-dermic injection. It quieted pain & he left — but in an hour or two the pain returned more severe than at first & again Yale had to be sent for. It was after 8 p.m. before he came — He gave me a second Hypo-dermic, full dose & I was soon under its influence & the rest of the night was spent in stupor.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 18, 1891

Have only a nightmare memory of this day — By Yale's advice a dose of Castor oil was got down me that in the afternoon added to my miseries of head ache, nausea & stomach pain. Yale came in the morning & spent a short time with me.

I did not get my clothes off and my self properly in bed until about 5 o'clk. this morning.
