## Left Page

### March, Monday 23, 1891

A pleasant day with considerable struggling sunshine. B&P. in bed.
Up at about 10 o'clk. Afternoon went
out on Park Ave. & was much refresh-
ed by half an hour of sauntering.
Lunch at 1 — after that rode up to Dr.
Gale's — with him for some time —
then walked home. Read for an
hour. Then out again for a short
walk — then in & rested until 6.
Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g, reading
for awhile & writing to S.R. Koehler.
Geo. & Nellie came in for a few min-
utes — they, with Kate, went to Charl's —
also I went to G. Central Station
with K's letter — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 24, 1891

Overcast w'g with some appearance of
rain but the afternoon cleared charming &
mild. Got up to my breakfast — not long
after 10 o'clk. Ralph & I went to the studio's
where we were until noon. I got the Pratt
Mr. plat's done up so that if Mr. Koehler
calls while I am away he can get them
without trouble. I attended to some other small
matters; then we walked home. Lunch at
1 — about 3 I started out to walk up town.
Met W. Woodin in 42nd S. Told me that his
wife has so far recovered that she may
see company. Walked on to 57th S. & 8th Ave.
Carole Beckwith not at home. I left a p.p.
of "Bunch of Pansies" for him — then on to Swain
Gifford's — made a call on him — then to 7th Ave
gate into Central Park & out at 5th Ave. Bus
down to 42nd S. — went to Ottendorfer's & had
hair cut & head shampooed — Home at 6 —
Rested until dinner — after that — read
— to bed before 11.
