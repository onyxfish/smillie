## Left Page

### October, Saturday 17, 1891

A wonderfully beautiful day, clear
& very mild — I was with Putam
until 11 o'clk. superintending the
transplanting of some Pinus [illegible].
Then I got to my studio work —
made a transfer. 5"×7". "A deserted
brick yard" & outlined upon soft g[round].
"An old dam" — the latter for aqua-tint for
Pratt's his. I then bit soft g[round] — outline for
3m, found the ground biting very ex-
tensively & cleaned plate — the out-
line is sufficient for my purpose.
Spent the most of the afternoon in un-
successful efforts to get an aqua-tint
ground on the "dam" plate — it would
crackle — gave up in disgust at 5:00
& went down town — tea at 6.30 —
Evef very stupid with a headache.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 18, 1891

A breezy day — with many clouds —
the middle of it was delightful
for rich color & atmosphere — B[reakfast]
at 9 — it was after 11 when with
[illegible], Annie & the boys I wandered
out through the quarry, over Warner's
lot on to Sayre's hill & over that
looking for ground motives for the
N. J. Socy. It was 2 o'clk when
we got home. Dinner — after
that I had a nap in the studio
— then a little work out of doors
& a walk — Evef. I wrote a
long letter to Yale — also to young
Wordies. It was 11 o'clk. when
I went to P. O. & nearly 12 when
I got to bed.
