## Left Page

### April, Friday 24, 1891

Brilliant day — cool & delightful. I got to studio before 10 & to work on the Jade plate — still dotting in the outline — wrote note to Uncle Willie & sent him $1.50. About 12.30 went to Fifth Ave. Art Galleries (Ortgies) — Varnishing Day Socy of Am. Artists. My little study from Nature has been hung in a good place & I am now a going man — Home to lunch at 1.45 — To studio at 3 and on Jade plate until 5 — then Jc° came & with him I again went to the Socy of Am. Artists 54th. Met — Kenyon Cox — Ochtman — Bacher — Howard R. Butler etc — also Dalph who had been duly commissioned to offer me the nomination of Pres. of the N.Y. Art Guild — Respectfully declined — Home at about 6.30 — dinner — Evefg, some reading. Charl came in with pts from photos that he took last Sunday — He got an excellent one fine — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 25, 1891

Bright mfg. but clouds soon gathered & the wind blew strong & raw from the N.W. At noon one might have shivered in an overcoat. To studio before 10 & worked on Jade plate, still dotting outline. Schilling came & left his etching Club Acc't. with me — Miss Smith called again to interview me concerning a woman's dress. Home at 1 — lunch — a juvenile party to Van Cortlandt had been planned for this afternoon. It was so cold that I advised it be abandoned but was overruled. At 2 Smrie, Kate, many Cook & eight children started but only went to Central Park & were glad soon to get home — I went to studio & worked, in spite of headache until after 5 — Charl came in — Home at 6 — dinner — Evefg, reading in front of a pleasant fire & to bed soon after 10.
