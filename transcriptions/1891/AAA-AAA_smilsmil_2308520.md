## Left Page

### November, Thursday 26, 1891

*Thanksgiving.*

A dull day, with some rain about the middle of the day & also more at night. Spent some time at home after bkfst. undoing what the carpenters did yesterday afternoon — taking tacks out of canvas on ceiling where it was puckered. Then to studio & worked on vermilion tracing on tissue paper for soft-ground etching of San Luis Rey. Home at 1.30 & had lunch — a little more work on ceiling. Then I lay down on the bed & had a nap — At 4, dressed & at 5. Annie & I went to Walter's for a Thanksgiving dinner — Children, the cousins mostly, were the diners. After dinner the children gave entertainment in their Marionette theatre in the attic. The children planned & managed; so far as it was plan or management, & to me it was not very funny even for children — Home soon after 10 —

## Right Page

### November, Friday 27, 1891

A bright, pleasant day. It was after 11 o'clk before, with a carpenter's help, I got the ceiling canvas finished — that is, tacked into place satisfactorily. Then I went to Douglas's — to market etc & to studio — then in to Tiffany's & saw sketch for light in hall — Doris took me down into the cellar & made the selection of glass for the different parts — Home to lunch at 2°. Ralph & I lunched alone — To studio at 3 & before I got started upon my work Yale came in with kfp. from some of his plates — then Schilling came in & together we made selection of [illegible] "Panorama" plate for Etching Club cat. It was no longer light when I left — Then I cleaned up my desk — cleaned ground from Rey plate & made ready to lay another — put some dry-point kfp. in press & home before 6 — Had a simple dinner in the laundry & quiet evening in our bed room at home.
