## Left Page

### October, Monday 5, 1891

*Kates left us.*

Bright, mild & pleasant morning. Before 10 o'clk. The sky was overcast, wind cold from N.W. — showers of light, misty rain — afternoon, colder & clearer. B'k'p. before 7 — at 7.15 the children & I rode over to R.R. Station with Kate who started for Wf. there to meet Mary, the two then to go to Ridgefield to visit Jae., I got Stone & Fontain started on setting out new Asparagus plants then to work in studio starting sketch of Cal. Mission of San Luis Rey. At noon went down town — dinner at 1.30. Afternoon work interfered with by having to boss the laying of stones in drain gratings on road. Eve'y wrote notes to Walter Cook & Jno. Boyle & Co. concerning stuff for the Reception Room ceiling in 38th S—

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 6, 1891

Cool, bright m'f, growing cloudy before noon — milder & overcast afternoon with indications of rain. After B'k'p. I lighted a fire in the large stove in the lower hall — & was driven out by the smoke — examination showed that the bricklayers who Topped the chimnies a few days ago had Tumbled so much debris down the flue as to block it up completely — It was 11 o'clk. before I got it cleaned out — then I had to direct Rutau in laying gutterways to the new drain under the road — At noon Ralph & I went down town — Back to dinner at 1 — my headache bothered me so that I could not work in studio but directed things out of doors until 4 — then down town — back at 5. Azur [illegible] Took a critical look at the horses — Sam's lameness he says is serious & he advises me to sell them if I can — Eve'L. wrote to L. M. Yale. Rain before 10 p.m. — continuing heavily during the night.
