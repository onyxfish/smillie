## Left Page

### May, Friday 8, 1891.

A mild, overcast day. S.W. wind & indications of rain. Katie Adams started for New
Haven by 9 A.M. train — 10 o'clk. when I
got to studio — Had pails of hot water brought
up & acid warmed preparatory to beginning
the biting of the Morgan portrait — Just
as I was about ready to begin there was
an incursion of plumbers & carpenters
who insisted upon ripping up my floor to
get at some stopped-up pipes — I was obliged
to give up my work & went to N.A. & tried to
look at pictures there — At 1 went to G.C.S.
& interviewed the ticket seller there concerning my troubles of last Sat. m'f. Home
& had lunch — Geo. & I walked down to the
studio before 2.30 — Plumbers & carpenters
gone — again I got started on the biting of
the Morgan head & got two bitings — The
acid took hold very nicely & once more
I am hopeful. Geo. went to Ridgefield
at 4 o'clk — Home to dinner at 6.30
Kate & I had a very quiet time —
Ev'f. I wrote to Annie & Herrick —
I got to bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 9, 1891.

A coppery, hot day. Ther. got up to 84°
in the shade at 3. P. M. Got to studio
about 10 & right to work upon the biting
of Morgan portrait — Before 12 went to
the Bk of the Metropolis & deposited
a lot of Dietkitchen money for Annie —
then to Wunderlich's & looked over some
very interesting late English Mezzotints
& some German etchings — Again to studio
— home at 1.30 — near 3 when I ret. to the
studio — at my biting until 5 — washed
up — walked in Madison Sqr. — home
at 6.30 — a modest dinner for Kate &
me — Ev'f. at 9 went to Century Club
& heard an entertaining discourse by
Rossiter Raymond upon Egypt, Ancient
& Modern & its Redemption under
English influence & work — with
stereopticon illustrations — after
this, wandered awhile & had
some brief chats — then home at 11
& to bed —

This m'f. Rec'd. a box of beautiful
flowers from Mr. Beach — Some novel
poppies — yellow, deep orange & copper
colored.
