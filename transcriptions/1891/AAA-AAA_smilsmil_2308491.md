## Left Page

### September, Sunday 27, 1891

Another 80° day. Very hot, very dry
& very misty. Bkfst. at about 9 o'clk.
It was after 10 when C. & I went
down to the garden. I was there
until nearly noon. It was after
2 when we had dinner — then
dispersed ourselves to find cool
spots in which to pass the after-
noon writing, reading & napping.
About 5 o'clk. we went out for a
short drive & came in soon after
6. Eve'g. I completed a letter
to Geo. & about 9 went to P.O. Also,
stopped at W.H. Jessups & saw my
Woodin about our contemplated
trip to Berwick next Thurs. On
my way home, Azur Lathrop's ho.
being open I stopped there to see
him — he had gone to bed, but I spent
a few minutes with Florence &
two of her lady friends — Home at
about 10 — it was 11.30 when we
got to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 28, 1891

*Chas. Collins left us.*

A very charming day — breezy and
cooler — the haze of the past few days
almost cleared away. Soon after
10 o'clk. I had the horses and we
went out for a ride — we jogged
about until nearly 12 — then home.
At 12.15 Collins, Ralph & I went
to the new R.R. Station, from which
trains start for the first time, and
C.C. took the 12.30 train to go to
Buffalo. Then R. & I went down town
& transacted some business & on our
way home stopped at A. Lathrop's &
talked with him about oats & things
— then to dinner. I worked for a
little while on jade plate & then for
some time gathering seeds. Tea at
6.30 — A very quiet eve'g with news-
papers.
