## Left Page

### May, Sunday 24, 1891

Quite cool & part of the time over-
cast — singular & impless and
weather — my back exceedingly
lame at first in the m'g. — gradually
improving as the day wore on — I
was able to saunter about — & near
noon Geo. Annie, Jim, Ralph & I
took a walk to the Smiths, by a back
lane & around by a [illegible] — Dinner
about 2 — In the afternoon Nellie
took Annie off for a drive while
I warmed myself in front of a very
pleasant hickory fire. — After
tea my back felt so limber that,
in the twilight, I walked down the
street, past the old Addoms place
& back — The rest of the evef. all
hands were rather snoozy — To
bed at 10.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 25, 1891

*home again.*

Pleasant day — at times overcast. Ralph
wakened us very early — not later than 6 —
Geo. went to N.Y. by early train taking Jim
with him so that he might get to school
early — Annie & I waited over a train and
had bkfst. at 8 — Nellie drove Annie & Ralph
to the train — I walked — off at 18. At Bronx-
ville met Alden Weir with Nathrop (Mark)
who had been spending Sunday with him.
We got home at about 11.30 — I did the
marketing etc — & then rested until 1.30
— lunch — to studio about 2.30 — Finished
a slight tracing of Shirlaw's "Reflection"
Calverly came in. went to his studio and
saw his bas-relief portrait of me — I think
it is very good — At 4 went to Shirlaw's
with photo. & tracings — not in — spent half
an hour with his wife — then gave it up —
went to 17 Gramercy Park & saw specimen of
the Spurr. wood varnir for wall covering — then
to Bridgeman's & got a garden syringe — then
to studio — Home at 6.30 — dinner — every
Annie feeling used up went to bed very
early — I pored over newspapers and mag-
azines until 9.30 — To bed at 10.

Mary Cook had a son early yester-
terday m'g.
