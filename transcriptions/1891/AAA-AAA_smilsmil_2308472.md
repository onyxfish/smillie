## Left Page

### August, Thursday 20, 1891

A threatening sort of a day — wind from S. E. very strong with many clouds.
We had a late bkfst — all feeling
somewhat seedy after the excitement
of the past few days — But we got
in a good lot of work clearing away
all evidences of the play & the party.
Very soon after dinner I went down
town & attended to several matters
there, with Ralph, wandered over
to the new R.R. & for a while watched
the progress of the work there — then
home & had a rest — after tea I
again went down town to P.O.
& to pay some bills connected with
our entertainment — About 2.30
Herrick, with team & taken wagon took
Gretta, Bessie, Katey & three of their friends
to Silver Lake, Gretta's home, leaving
Bessie there — They had a good time
& got back shortly after 11 they say — We
were too tired to wait up for them &
went to bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### August, Friday 21, 1891

Lowering clouds until near noon, then
some heavy showers, continuing at intervals all the afternoon. Change of wind
& signs of clearing in the evg. I
spent the mf at work out of doors —
Starting the day with a 9 o'clk. bkfst.
While in the garden just before noon
Annie, Katie A. & Jim came down — I
caught the donkey for them — she bolted
suddenly & I hung on — she threw me &
dragged me a short distance, bruising me
& giving me a good shaking up. I caught
her again & whaled her. Dinner at 2 —
About 4 the bruised foot & leg (the right)
got to paining me so severely that I lay
down in studio until near 7 when we had
tea — I was too lame to walk so Annie
& Katie went to the Rink alone to see a
sort of play in which Chas. Collins & a live
goose are the principal actors. They
got back at 10 o'clk — To bed soon
after.
