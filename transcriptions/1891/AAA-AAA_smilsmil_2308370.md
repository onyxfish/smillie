## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 28, 1891.

A pleasant day with a milky sky. at 9 o'clk. started for Kimmel & Voigt's
& got there before 9.30 — Started going V — Taking proofs an tinted Imperial
p/p. of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" — took rags of my own & showed him what I
wanted by working myself — He soon took a very fair proof & I left him, rode
up to 2° Nat. Bk., then in to Kurtz, then
• NA, Home to lunch soon after 1 — then
a short rest & again to DeVinne's
where I spent an hour, from 11 to 12
in the m[orning] trying to get the work done —
Mr. Hopkins is away again & there is
great confusion — Afternoon saw the
last proofs & orders were then given to
push ahead. Then to studio — Mounted
a p/f. of "Sleep" to frame for my Wood in.
Dr. Yale came in & we made a trade
for a small oil sketch of mine — "Crécy
en Brie" — to Square off Raskin's [illegible].
Then I went again to NA & met Nicoll
& Jones about decorations — Home
before 6 — dinner — Eve'g I wrote
one or two notes when Chas. Collins
came in — pleasant chatting until
after 10 — then to bed —

Got a pleasant note from Thos.
A. Janvier & one from his wife thanking me for cards to Water Color Show.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 29, 1891.

Disagreeable day — some rain in
the m'g — mist & fog in the afternoon —
got to studio at 9 30 — wet paper
& prepared for printing — & then
spent the rest of the m'g writing
notes — Home at 1 5, lunch — This
is Annie's first "afternoon at home" —
back to studio at 2 15 & took some
satisfactory p/p. from my Etching "A
bit of brook", etc — I sent my bill to
Koehler for the Pratt plates #189.°°
Home before 6 — Found Mr. Sprague
& Miss Williams, the last of 5 callers
there — dinner — Eve'g Annie
went to her Saturday club — Geo.
came in & made a short call —
I got to bed before 10.
