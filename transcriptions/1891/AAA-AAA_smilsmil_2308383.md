## Left Page

### February, Monday 23, 1891

Bright, very pleasant day — got to
studio about 10 planning to begin
a spell of painting but before I got
started Schilling came in & then
Dr. Gerster & between the two of them,
no doubt considering it a holiday, it
was after 12 before I was left alone.
I wrote one or two notes & then home
to lunch — back & worked on 9×12
plate, "Girl's Head" after Lippincott.
Drew in the lines over the face &
got the plate ready for biting. Then
mounted a number of etching pps.
By the way, Charl came in just
before 1 & I sat for two profile photos
to aid Calverley in making his bas
relief of me. Home after 6 — Dinner
Ev'g. I collapsed & after reading
for awhile I went to bed at
about 9.30.

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 24, 1891

Mild, with some pleasant sunshine
— threats of change — but no rain fell
until about 11 P.M. Got to studio
soon after 10. Could not get started
on my painting but busied myself
for a time "stopping out" little ends of
lines & faults generally on my plates
now ready to bite — Afternoon went to
out & deposited check Rec'd from Wm H.
Woodin $119.50  Home to lunch — At 2.30
to Grolier Club & saw Rembrandt Etchings.
Then to studio — Dr. Gerster came in for awhile
then to DeVinne's with two pts. of Goldsmith's
Daughter — then to Shirlaw's & got 3 autographs
upon 3 pts. of "Sleep" — then to Keppel's & left some
pps. then to studio & then home — Dinner.
Eve'g Annie, Kate & I went to the Boston
Symphony Concert (Nickish) at Chickering
Hall — House packed — enthusiastic audience
The Wagner "Waldweben" was given a 2d
time in answer to a demand — Pachmann had an ovation & becoming
excited indulged in some grotesque
pantomime — A & K. rode home.
I walked — To bed after 11.
