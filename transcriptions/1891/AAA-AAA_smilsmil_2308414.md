## Left Page

### April, Sunday 26, 1891.

Delightful day. After an uncomfortable night, owing to headache, I got up after 7 & had my bath. Then, after dressing, lay down & had a nap before bkf. at 9. After which I feel much better. About 11, after Annie, Jim & Ralph had gone off with a party to Vancortlandt, I walked up to the Central Park & sauntered & sunned myself there. Home at 1.30 — dinner at 1.45. Annie & Ralph came in just before 2. Chark came in before we finished dinner. At 3 Annies went to Cooper Union to join her Choral Club in singing at a free concert given under Adler influence for the enjoyment of the "poor." Kate & Chark went up to Washington Bridge & I went to bed & had a nap. Before 5 I wrote a note to Mr. [illegible] objecting to his putting a partition across the west end of the hall way near my studio door & Ralph & I walked down to the Studio & left the note under Mr. [illegible]'s door. Home at 6. Geo. Smiley & [illegible] Valentine there. Tea at 6.45. Eve'g some desultory Reading. To bed before 10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 27, 1891.

Lovely spring day, growing to be very warm in the afternoon. Ther. in the shade getting up to 79°. To Studio at 9.30 — got to work upon Jade Plate & sketched the m[illegible] dotting outline, getting through with that part at last. To Safe Deposit & 2° Nat. Bank & attended to Annie's matters for May 1st. Then home at 1.30 — Fanny Trumbull lunched with us. It was after 3 when I got to Studio. Soon after, Alice Ireland & Miss Morley called — I showed them a number of etchings & Miss M. brought a pf. of "Old Cedars — Coast of Maine". Then Saml. Colman came — he took four pf. of dry-points. I did no work in the afternoon. Home at 6.30 — dinner at 7. Eve'g. Some news paper reading. Then wrote a letter of instruction to Herrick — mailed it & to bed at 10.
