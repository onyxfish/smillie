## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 24, 1891

Breezy, pleasant day — Louis Tiffany
called at the house about 9.30 & looked
over things giving advice concerning
the shade & color of cartridge wall [illegible].
Then Walter came & it was 11 o'cl
before I got to studio — Said cold
ground on San Luis Rey Mission plat[e].
Dr. Ferster came in soon after noon.
I took Ralph with me to the studio
where he amused himself at Water-
color painting — Home to lunch at
1.30 — It was near 3 when I got
back to studio & worked for awhile
tracing San Luis Rey upon tissue
paper, in red, preparatory to soft
ground etching — At 5 went to [illegible] &
[illegible] — dismal — home at 6.15 & then
with Annie to Fred Ireland's where
we dined — At 8 went with A — to Mr
Green's — 39th St. & Lex. Ave. There to Hodgson
Hall, 45th St. & 5th Ave. Choral Club [illegible]
— left them there & went to Dr. Yale's —
Spent the eve'g with him looking
over some of his recent work — waited
for A. + Mrs. G — at 10.15 — then home

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 25, 1891

Overcast most of the time, without
being unpleasant. I spent the day
at home with carpenters putting up
the canvas on library ceiling — the men
were tolerably tractable & the canvas
was got up fairly well. I left them
before 3 & got to studio just in time
to meet the young woman who wishes
me to take her as pupil in etching.
She had specimens of her work. It
ended in a partial agreement between
us. No steam & too cold that I
could do no work — visited with Geo.
Home soon after 5. Emma D. came
in. At 6.30 A. & I went to W. 42nd St.
to dine with Geo. & Emiley — meeting
Walter & Louise — 6th wedding anni-
versary — a very good dinner and a
pleasant eve'g. Home at 10.30 and
soon to bed.
