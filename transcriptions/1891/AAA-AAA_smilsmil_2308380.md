## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 17, 1891

Cloudy, dark & damp — some slight showers
falling — very mild — After b'kfst. went with
Annie to Woman's college. East 15th S. and
Livingstone Place. Then we walked together
uptown, stopping at Sinnett & Annend's, meeting
J. C. Nicoll there — then to studio at 11 — Got
to work biting what I had drawn upon
the two old plates yesterday — Soon after 12
I went up to Allen's in W. 47th S. — She worked
at my teeth until 1.30 — Home at 1.45
back to studio at 2.45. Met Kelly of Art
Review, full of plans & hopes — he took
much of my time — Then Dr. Pfoster came
in — I did not get my work quite done
altho' I stuck to it until 6 — Then
home — dinner — [illegible], too hot for
anything — Read newspapers until after
9. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 18, 1891

Clouded until 10 when the sun shone
out — wind rose & it grew rapidly cooler.
9.1 — To studio at 10 & had Rose & Co. send it me
to Mr. Morgan for the portrait of his father which
was brought to me at 11 o'clk. I set to work at
once drawing in the lines of the face and
worked steadily until 1 o'clk. when I went
home to lunch — back at 2.30 (S'gz. Howe
lunched with us & went with Annie to
the meeting of the W'd. P.M. club.) I
finished the laying in of the head about
4.30 — went to N.A. with p'f. of etchings —
then up 5th Ave. to Ortgies's gallery — there
until 6 o'clk. looking over Rob't. W. Weir
collection of old books, engravings etc. — &
a few very poor paintings — Dinner, but
I could not eat, a headache having
developed. Kate dined with Annie Ballard
& etc. — Soon after 9 — Annie read aloud
to me and O.W. Holmes essay on Old age.
