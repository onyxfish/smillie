## Left Page

### April, Friday 10, 1891

Dark day with some attempts at rain.
Got to studio after 10 — a call from Male
& then to work on "A Pool — October"
repainting the foreground — Home to
lunch & back at usual time and
painted until 4.30 — then cleaned
up & took a short walk getting
home before 6. Rested before dinner
at 6.30 — Read awhile after & 
& at 8.30 went to Annual Meeting
of N.Y. Etching Club. Cha². Mielatz's
studio, "Alpine" Building — Broadway
& 33d St — a small attendance — no
quorum — but we went ahead and
transacted business all the same —
re-electing our officers & arranging
for an ex[hibit] next year & a publication
under my direction upon the lines
of this year. It was 11 before we
broke up — Home at 11.30 — Right
to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 11, 1891

Dark day with wind & heavy rain nearly
all day — After bkf. went to his grocers and
butchers in 3d Ave. & then returned home.
Spent the m'f. completing a long
letter of instructions to Herrick concerning
the garden work — flowers & vegetables —
& also notes to Mr Plumb & to Paske.
Lunch aftn't — more writing — about
4 & went to studio — Chase came in
& we spent a few moments with
Geo. Home before 6 — Taking Kate's
new Pratt Institute hat (Rec'd. from Nellie)
with me. Dinner at 6.30. Eve'g
Annie, Kate & I. Splashed in a heavy
shower to the Met. Opera House & heard
the last Phil. Concert of this season — The
Piano playing of Adele Aus der Ohe was to
me simply marvellous. In answer to an
encore she played with amazing brilliancy
an arrangement of the Spinning Chorus from
the Flying Dutchman. At the close of
the concert Thomas Rec'd. an ovation
& was loaded with flowers. We
splashed home through another shower
& got to bed before 11.
