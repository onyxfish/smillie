## Left Page

### August, Sunday 16, 1891

A perfect day — See the morning out of doors. It was after 2 o'clk
when we had dinner and at
4 we started out for a ride —
we stopped at Dr. Richardson's
& asked him to come to the house
bet. 7 & 8 o'clk. to see Jim who has
been quite out of sorts during the
day — we drove over to Brooklyn
passing North Pond & returned by
the valley road, East Bridgewater &
the old depot Road — getting home
at 7. 15 after a very delightful
ride — Dr. R. was at the house ahead
of us, — Minks Jim has taken cold —
late tea — eve'g wrote to Mary,
to Thos. E. Jones of Scranton etc. — and
at 10 o'clk. went to P.O.     Then
home & to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 17, 1891

Very pleasant day — Got through
with morning duties as soon as poss-
ble then to work clearing up studio
— no small job — began to feel
out of sorts before dinner time and
during the afternoon was quite used
up — Mary Sayre & young Wariner
came up at 4.30 — & at 5. Katie A.
& I went out riding & took them
with us — Annie remained at
home expecting "A cup of tea" company
We got from our ride at 6 & found
that A. had waited in vain — Tea.
Eve'g Louise Jessup & her bro. Geo.
M. McCausland & Benj. J. Chandler
came & until 9. 30 Rehearsed "A
cup of tea" —     Then we tried ex-
periments in lighting studio and
to bed soon after 10.
