## Left Page

### May, Monday 18, 1891.

Pleasant, cool w'f. Got to studio
about 10 & soon had a big fire in
the grate &, drawing up a table,
I sat before it all the morning,
doing some writing & feeling pretty
stretched. Home at 1 — lunch,
& then made up my mind to
give up again — Spent most of
the afternoon on the bed — Had
a very unhappy experience im-
mediately after dinner — before dessert
— the result of a large dose of castor
oil taken only an hour before. The
entire contents of this individual
were promptly & unceremonously
evicted, so to speak & I went to bed
very empty & very unhappy to pass
a wretched & very feverish night.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 19, 1891.

Pleasant day — Yale came in the
m'f. in answer to a summons —
but my fever soon abated and I
was advised to keep patiently in
bed until the irritation in head,
throat & chest had subsided. The
day dragged monotonously and I
got up & dressed late in the after-
noon, going down to dinner in the
ev'g. Later, Chas. Collins, Geo.
& Charles came in & stayed until
10 o'clk. I got to bed before 10.30
