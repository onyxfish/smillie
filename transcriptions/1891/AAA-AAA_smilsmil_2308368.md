## Left Page

### January, Saturday 24. 1891.

Delightful day — cloudless & mild. I got up with a headache, my throat & the inside of my head feeling very raw but at 10 o'clk. I started out — went to Studio & from there to the N.A. of D. — after correcting some matter in the Etching Club Cat. I went on to Kurtz's — the portraits were ready for me but I declined to accept Colman's & Chakman's — he agreed to deliver two new ones on Monday — Then I went to DeVisme's & saw Mr. Hopkins & made all possible arrangements for haste. Then to "The Churchman" office to enquire after process block of "The passing herd" that was loaned to them in Feb'y '89 — A young man promised to look it up — walked slowly up town getting home at 1 — lunch — Did not go out again — in bed until 3.30 — then with Ralph — Every Sallie had a birthday party at Walter's — Jim went at 7 — after dinner, about 8 — Annie followed — I read until 11. Rain commenced a little before that.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 25. 1891.

Dismal snow & slush until near 11 after which it cleared overhead. It will be some time before I can clear under foot. Ralph's later. Of course I did not go out of doors — all the m'f I read news papers or wrote letters — Dinner at 2 — more lounging — reading & writing until after 4 when Clark came in — Tea at 6.30. Eve'g Annie read a paper by Mrs. Stockton aloud — then read Feb'y Harkers until 9.30 — To bed about 10.
