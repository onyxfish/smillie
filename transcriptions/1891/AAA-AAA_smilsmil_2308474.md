## Left Page

### August, Monday 24. 1891.

*M. & d. R.R. commenced running trains into town to-day.*

Heavy clouds in the m'f & very warm
So. wind - before noon there was much ex-
ceedingly hot sunshine. From 3.30 to
7 o'clk - there was an almost continuous
downpour of rain with some thunder.
At 9 o'clk. Annie, the boys & I went over
to the new R.R. station to see the train
come in. There was a great crowd, shout-
ing & firing off cannon crackers. The band
played & speeches were made by Hunt
Jessup & W.D. Bush. It was quite ex-
citing to see the big engine come up to
the station. Spent the m'f about the
place giving directions — went down
town at noon. Dinner at 1.45, with
the mess until the rain came at 3.30
lounging until tea at 6.30 — went
down town just before 8 — Went into
R.R. jollification at Court House — stayed
there a few minutes — Rather a lugubrious
jollification. Home before 9.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 25. 1891.

*Village Improvement Soct organized*

Pleasant day — Had an engagement to meet
Danl. Sayre at his house at 9 A.M. but Mrs.
Burroughs (Della Raynsford) called to ask us
to postpone it until 12. I wrote a note to the
md.t Republican & A. & I drafted the form of
a Constitution for the Village Improvement
Soct of Montrose. At 12 we went to Danl.
Sayre's & talked for 3/4 of an hour with him
& his wife concerning the suitableness of differ-
ent persons for office in proposed Soct. Home
to dinner at 1. Went down town & put
big posters in P.O. window announcing
meeting at Danl. Sayre's — went to many
of the stores & invited the proprietors. Home
b'fore 5 — tea at 6.30 — At 7.30 A. & I went to D.
Sayre's — we were not the first ones. Thirty
or forty came. A very pleasant & animated
meeting. Wm. Jessup chosen temporary chair-
man. I was on com. on organization & read
my form of constitution — It was adopted,
section by section, with very little modification.
The com. on nominations brought in my name
as pres'. I fought against it seriously but it
was useless & I was promptly installed.
Soon after 10 we adjourned until next
Tues. ev'ef, not having entirely completed
our organization. Home at 10.30 and
to bed.
