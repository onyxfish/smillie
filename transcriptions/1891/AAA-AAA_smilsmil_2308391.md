## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 11, 1891

Beautiful day - bright & mild - got to studio about 10 & spent some time putting studio in partial order. Then set palette & worked on "A bit in Riverside Park" — At 12.30 walked up to Dr. H.D. Allen's, 155 W. 47° S. — was detained by him until 2 o'clk. Then home — lunch — To studio soon after 3 — worked more than an hour on 18×30 — sand papering & scraping the sky — Then to Van Tine's with W.C. Soc'y. bills. — Thru to Klckpel's — got a "pansy" proof for Beckwith — looked at the de Gros etchings — then to Wünderlich's & saw the strange collection of Watchman's vagaries in oil & pastel — Bought apf. of a small plate — Reproductive etching — To studio at 6.15 then home — dinner — Suef. Mary & Kate went to the opera. After reading newspaper I worked for awhile on plans for a window sash for opening on the stair at Montrose — To bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 12, 1891

Disagreeable day — rain beginning at about 11 & continuing steadily until far into the night. At 2.15 A.M. was awakened by severe pain in stomach — probably gastric neuralgia — was up & seeking remedies for some time — got to bed again with hot water bag & at last to sleep but in the m'g felt assured up that I did not get up until near noon — Dr. Yale came about 10 — Through the rest of the day I kept up & about altho' my head distressed me a good deal — In the afternoon I arranged a lot of Montrose bills, papers, checks etc — and in the rus'g I arranged back files of Punch, Ill. Lond. News, Harper's Mag. & the Century — Annie went to her Choral Club Rehearsal & Mary & Kate went to bed — Mary had been to Newark to see Nette Suttshen — I got to bed at about 10.
