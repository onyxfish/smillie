## Left Page

### November, Friday 6, 1891

A brilliant beautiful day. Stone, Coyle and two Wood men
dug & worked at changing the places of maples on the Green.
They finished digging the holes, 12 or 13" & got two of the trees in place
but not finished. I did little else all day but direct them.
A hard days work and very un-
satisfactory too — in view of the
fact that my own work is horribly
behind hand.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 7, 1891

Gorgeous day — warm, sunny and
still with delicious color — Again
nearly the whole of my day was
given to superintending setting
of trees on Public Green. Thankful
if I had to give time to such work
which was during such beautiful
weather. At noon I made
board protectors & put them
about 5" × 7" plate & print for
Etching club & sent them by
Exp. to Kimmel & Voigt. At
5 o'clk. being very tired I lay
down & rested until after 6 —
Eve'g. I wrote notices of the
B.G. Northrop's lecture to be read
from the pulpits to-morrow.
