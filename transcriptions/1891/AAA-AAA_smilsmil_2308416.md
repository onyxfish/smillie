## Left Page

### April, Thursday 30, 1891.

Pleasant, growing to be very warm. To studio at 10. Finished Jade Etching & Spent the rest of the morning varnishing it, the LipSpincott and the Morgan plates. To lunch + back as usual. Spent some time in the afternoon going over the Lipp-incott plate with a point. Home to dinner at 6.30 — Ev. elf. at 8.30 at N.A. of D. — Special meeting to act upon Constitutional amendment changing Hanging Com. from Alphabetical succession — To 3 elective members with as large Com. of Selection. Some other Con. amendments were voted upon without success. I Took Part, as E. Johnson & Mrs. Moran in favor of the Ass't & younger painters. The Hanging Com. business was revolutionized & altogether the winter was lively & interesting. No remarks were made concerning Dan. Huntington's Resignation. Left at 11.15 — home & to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 1, 1891.

Pleasant & warm — Did not get to studio until after 10 — New got things into order for biting & commenced operations on the Jade plates and the etching after ahead by LipSpincott. Calman came in to bid me good-bye — He goes to Newport this afternoon. Home to lunch at 1 — Annie at Yonkers attending Unitarian League. Back to studio at 3 & continued the biting until 5 o'clk. Horrible confusion throughout the building — A gang of men commenced tearing down the east end of the structure. I went down to Bridgeman's & ordered Peonies, Tomatoes & Celery Plants sent to Montrose — Home at 6.30 — dinner. At 8.30 I went to Century & met J. C. Nicoll & together, as auditing com. we went over Schilling's acc't with the NY. Etching Club — then sat & chatted with him until 10.5 — home & to bed.
