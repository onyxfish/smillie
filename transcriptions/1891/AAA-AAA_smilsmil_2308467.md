## Left Page

### August, Monday 10, 1891.

A very hot day — Ther. got up to 84°.
Thunderstorms passed south of us bet.
7 & 8 p.m. & a little rain fell. I
hurried through with my out-of-door
work in the m't. — At 10.30 Mr. & Mrs.
Wright & Mr. Sirdell called & I spent
some time with them but was able
to get started upon a dry-point of
a bunch of Hollyhocks — Double — white
& very dark — upon a 9" × 12" plate — a
little after 11 — worked closely until
dinner at 1 — & again in the afternoon
the rain prevented our taking a
drive — after 7 I went to P.O. Eve'g
it was so close & warm that I did
not light the lamps until 9 o'clk.
Then looked over news paper —

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 11, 1891.

*Katie Adams came.*

Very hot again — thunderous clouds
by their shade made the afternoon
tolerable — Heavy thunderstorms passed
north & south of us about 7 p.m. giving us
two or three dashes of rain — a heavy blow
& some very vivid lightening — through all
of which Katie A— rose on her way from
the depot. In the morning spent
some time directing the laying of the
stable floor & then to work on Double
Hollyhock dry-point — Dinner at 1 —
more time in stable & more work
on Hollyhocks — At 5.30 went down
from taking Ralph — at 6.30 while I
was preparing for the coming storm, Mr.
Courtright & Florence Lathrop called —
then tea in the midst of the confusion of
the storm — at about 8.40 Katie arrived,
neighbor wet & frightened, her trunk coming
by the stage more than half an hour later.
She was in very good spirits & we all
chatted until 10 o'clk — Then to bed.
