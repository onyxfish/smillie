## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 16, 1891

Cool, cloudy & breezy. It was 10.30 when I got to studio, a[s] many things to look after — Got to work promptly biting aqua-tint on Ray plate but was interrupted by Schilling who came with [illegible] a plate for Etching Cat — a very poor thing, I think. Then Thos. Moran came & my m[orning] was much broken. Home soon after 1 — back at 2.30 & bit as long as I could see — then to K & V.'s in Canal St. with the two Moran plates — Home at 6.

Even[ing], most of it given to writing a letter to Schilling telling him that I do not think his plate good enough to represent him.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 17, 1891

Clear, windy & cold — 10.30 before I got to studio — Took Ralph with me — I spent what remained of the m[orning] in biting the aqua-tint upon the Ray plate — floating & painting with an acid brush, working up the landscape part & leaving the sky. Home about 1.30 — back at 2.30 & Resumed biting. Moran came in with Sarony negatives of himself & wife. I worked as long as I could see — slow work, using the mordant with a brush but more satisfactory time than a series of stoppings out. Dr. Ferster came in with a plate, an old man's head, drawn in but not bitten. Soon after 5 went to Photo-gravure Co. 137 W. 23d — & left the Moran negatives — then home — Dined in our dining room — no carpet & every thing in confusion — but a great improvement on the laundry. We lunched in the dining room — our first appearance there — Annie not at home. Even[ing], wrote notes to A.&S. Anthony & to Yale & read newspapers.
