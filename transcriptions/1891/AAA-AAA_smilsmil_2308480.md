## Left Page

### September, Saturday 5, 1891

Rain in the morning until after 11 —
Sulky, cloudy weather during the rest
of the day. After my garden work got
to my studio work and by sticking to
it at last got all the mezzotint ground
cleaned away from the outline of Jade
rose & the margin cleaned — This
has been a very trying piece of hard,
dirty & purely mechanical work. Re-
quiring great nicety & care — Dinner
— afternoon, worked on plate until
near 5 o'clk. Then went down town
& took a short walk beyond — Tea
at 6.30 — Charl. with the Parkes.
After tea, had to go down town
again; back at 8 — Read until 10
& then to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 6, 1891

Charming day — B.H.P. at about 9 — after
it went to garden & was there for about
an hour — then, while the others went to
walk I wrote a letter to S. R. Koehler.
Dinner at 2 — at 3.15 we all got
away in open wagon & drove over to
Brookleyn & back by South Pond — a
delightful drive, getting home at 6.30
Tea — Ewell, Bessie Parke & Katie
Nursb came, followed later by W. &
Mrs. P — Just before 9 I went to Mr.
W. H. Jessup's to see young Woodin
about going to Berwick with him for
a day to look at his new house in
course of construction — Home at 9.30
to bed at 10.30 — Charl quite
used up by a sudden attack of
rheumatism in right groin.
