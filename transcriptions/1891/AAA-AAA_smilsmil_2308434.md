## Left Page

### June, Friday 5, 1891

*To Montrose.*

A brilliant & beautifully cool day. Up soon after 6 - Hkf. at 6 45. at 7 Supp. men came & got trunks & cases. Mary Moony came to help the girls & lock up the house. Everything worked well & a few minutes after 8 we left the house without having been at all hurried - got to Christopher St. Ferry at 8.35 - checked baggage at No. 3 to New Milford. Then to Hoboken where we had to wait less than 15 m before going up on our train - found all R.R. employees with whom I came in contact had their instructions concerning my party & I felt like a nabob & as though I owned the train. 17 minutes at Scranton & had a hurried dinner - got to New Milford at 2.38 - a trifle over five hours running time from N.J. with four stops. A fine vestibuted train with all the modern improvements. Herrick and Pengo met us & at 4 o'clk. we got to Montrose after a cool drive. I had lots to look at & was kept busy running about as long as there was daylight. Tea about 6.30 — Suey, some reading & unpacking of trunk & to bed about 10 o'clk. — Shivering cold.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 6, 1891

**Ther. here last night to 39°**

A very cool, pleasant day - overcast in the afternoon with a few, very small drops of rain. I spent the whole day out of doors going over the place making myself acquainted with the status of things. I am disappointed in many respects — I miss Coyle's well kept roads & a certain professional touch about the flower beds & vegetable garden. I am afraid that this is owing to carelessness that our supply of rain water is very short. The day was so cool that I kindled a fire in the big hall stove & had a good fire on the hearths of dining & bed room. I went downtown at about noon and again at 7.30 — spent a little time reading newspapers & to bed at 10.30
