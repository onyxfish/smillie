## Left Page

### August, Thursday 6, 1891

Close & muggy — cloudy mtf. Sunshine
in the afternoon with with a sunshine
shower at 4.30. I got to work about
10.30 & spent rest of mtf. rocking a
fine ground over jade plate —
Dinner at 1 — another hour in the
afternoon "rocking" — the horses
were at the door at 4.30 when quite
a shower fell from what seemed to be
a clear sky — we had to wait about
20 m. for it — then we drove down
to Mr. Lathrop's & got Mr. Hasey & then
drove for an hour — Home at 6.15 —
tea before 7 — Evntf. wrote note
to Dr. Yale — & one to John Doe, en-
closing $6 — payment of his bill.
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 7, 1891

Very muggy & close — clear mtf. gradually
growing thicker until about 7 P.m. when
a light thunder shower came up, followed
during the evntf. by others. After 10 o'clk
when I finished my out of door duties &
got started upon a 5×7" Dry-point of Sweet
Peas. Stuck closely to it until dinner
at 1 o'clk — & again in the afternoon for
about two hours — At 4.30 we went
out riding — much interest in the M.
& I. R.R. — the rails are now laid to the
Boro' lines & crowds of the town's people
were out watching the progress of the
work. We got in at 6.45 — tea —
Evntf. we were invited to a little
card party at Mr. Richards' & [illegible] but
the rain prevented our going — instead
I amused myself by putting string
beans down in salt for winter use.
To bed a little before 11.
