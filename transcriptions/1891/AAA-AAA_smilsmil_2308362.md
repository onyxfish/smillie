## Left Page

### January, Monday 12, 1891

A breezy, cloudy day — after 10 when
I got to studio — my time much
wasted by trusting Butler's promises
about frames for my etchings and
water-colors so that I accomplished
ed but little during the m'f. Home
to lunch — after that went down
to DeVinne's & saw W. Hopkins &
made some progress — then to Shir-
law's with K.P. & "Mezzotint" & "Steep"
with which he was very much pleased.
I saw in his studio a small painting
that I liked very much & moved like
to mezzotint it. Then I went to Bott
studio & from there home. Ralph
is wonderfully docile but the changing
of the dressings is a very sore matter for
him. Evef. St. Yale came in for
a while having dined somewhere in
the neighborhood. I wrote a note
to W. Koehler & at 10.45 took it to the
Grand Central Station — then home
& to bed.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 13, 1891

Delightful day — brilliant, cloudless
& pleasantly cold — got to studio soon
after 10 & spent the day getting etch-
ings & water-colors into frames — Home
to lunch & back as usual — In the
afternoon Josephine, Dr. Will's widow,
surprised me by a call — she is looking
remarkably well & was very agreeable.
As she left Elig. Home & her niece
Lillie Hess of Chicago, now on her way to
Paris for art study, called. I had been
to Kurtz's to see after progress of Etching
club photos — portraits. Home at 6 —
dressed — dinner — Evef. Annie, Kate
& I went to Chickering Hall — Boston Symphony
Orchestra. Nickisch conductor — & heard an
extraordinary & delightful concert — the best
orchestra playing, I think, that I ever heard.
After — A. & K. rode up in omnibus — I
walked — & got home before they did —
to bed soon after 11.

Ralph had trouble with his constipated
bowels this m'f. & Annie had a scene
with him from out of which all em-
erged serene — He seems to be re-
covering from the operation nicely —
