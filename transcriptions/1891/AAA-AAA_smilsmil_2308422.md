## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 12, 1891

*Annie & the boys set? home.*

Cool day & overcast. It was 10
when I got to studio & at once to
work continuing the biting of Morgan
patrick — a long exposure for black
coat — wrote a letter to S.R. Koehler.
Home soon after 1 — lunch — back to
studio at 2.30 — again put plate in
acid bath after going all over coat
with multiple needle — A little
before 4 went to Jersey City & met
Annie & boys at 4.42 on their return
from Haglebrook — we rode up town
together & I got out at studio & fixed
things up there & home at 6.30 — Dinner
Eve'g, lounging & reading until
a little after 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 13, 1891

*N.A. annual meeting and dinner.*

A very pleasant day, bright with cool
wind from the east. To studio about 10. got
my Morgan plate into the acid for the last
time — gave it a good hour during which
time I wrote letters. Then took plate
out of bath & cleaned it — washed up —
It was then after 12. went in to see
Louis Tiffany for a few minutes — then
home before 1 — lunch — & to N.A. of D. —
at about 1.45 — Annual meeting — a
remarkably full one — 58 present — much
anxiety among old & young men as to who
would be W. Huntington's successor — When
it came to the actual voting, however,
matters straightened out to the satisfaction
as it appeared, of everybody. Wood was made
pres. F.D. Millett, vice pres — & old & young
were happy — F.S. Church & I were tellers &
we had a very busy time. With very little
intermission, the dinner followed the business
meeting — 57 sat down — Wood made a short
speech after dinner — followed by a long, rambling
talk by Millett & a lot of advice from Chase —
wrought up by Whittridge, Mr. Huntington
had an ovation — for minutes there was
wild excitement — a pleasant thing to see.
Me. F. User spoke well — all broke up
before 11 — [illegible] & I walked up home
at 11.30 & to bed before midnight.
