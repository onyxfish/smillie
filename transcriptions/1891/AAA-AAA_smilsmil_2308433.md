## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 3, 1891

Another hot day. Overcast and at
of a few drops of rain — but the weather
soon cleaned — Some light packing in
the m'g & after blch'; attended to the
routine messages & got to studio at
10.30. Finished packing my big case.
At 11 Mr. Jas. Burt called to look at
small paintings but did not see
anything particularly pleasing to him.
He wants to buy my "Study" now at the
Boston Museum. Home before 1 and
had lunch. Annie, by invitation,
lunched at Aunt Anna's. I did
some packing in big home chest and
at 2.30 kept appointment with Dr.
H. D. Allen — 155 W. 47th S'. Home before
3.30 & before dinner had about com-
pleted the packing of big chest.

Ev'g. Annie & I, by way of a
rest, went to Mad. Sq. Garden to a
Gilmore Band concert. The large
number of people present looked
few in the immense place — The
organized noise of the band however
was considerably larger than the
building — if it could have been re-
duced by half it would have been
much to the advantage of the enter-
tainment. We left before the
concert was quite over & got to bed
before 11.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 4, 1891

A bright & pleasantly cool day. Some
packing — then, after 10 started out upon
my last round of messages before our
start for Montrose. To studio & wound
up matters there — arranged with Rose
& Co. to care for my pictures — went to
Hamm on Hills' — then to Union Storage
Co. & home soon after 12. Lunch at
1. Afternoon, packed my trunk and
then, with great deliberation looked
about for something to do. We have
never before got ready for our Spring
flitting with less hurry & pressure —
toward ev'g. Rain began to fall.
Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g, Slig'. Home
came in & then Charl — & we had
a chatty ev'g until 10 o'clk. Slig'.
was carried off by a small Dis. Mess.
boy. Charl. took care of himself
& Annie & I went to bed. Rain
falling quietly & with dubious promise
for to-morrow.
