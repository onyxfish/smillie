## Left Page

### July, Monday 22, 1872

*on board St'mr St. John -*

Has memory lost its seat in your distracted globe?
After a very heavy rain, a superb morning \- Cool \+ clear until 10 o'clk. Running about doing up little errands, then in \+ packing until noon \- Kate hard at work making over my flannel shirt \- worked on a Locomotive &
by Paul Dixon for Uncle W \- Lunch at 1 \- again in drawing until 3 \- Bathed, dressed, packed bag \- took tea and bid good bye by 5 o'clk \- on the boat met all the party Miss Mary Cook Gil \+ his sister Annie Cook, Miss Edith Wright \+ her father, Will \+ Fanny Kimball, Miss Elizabeth Howe, Emma N. Goddard \+ myself \+ Had a special supper served to us at 8.30 \- and, just as we were passing West Point, a moonrise \- after passing Po'Keepsie went to bed at 11.15 \- Stateroom 9 \- Will Kimball sharing with me.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 23, 1872

*at Champney's*

Up about 5 o'clk \- Bkft. on board of St. John at 6 \- off at 7 o'clk, on Renslaer \+ Saratoga R.R. \- Oscar Moreland having joined us. Very heavy train \- got to Saratoga at 8.45 and away on Adirondack R.R. at 9.45 \- little interest; got to Riverside, or Folsom's Landing at 12.15 \- there were met by wagons \- Rode 7 miles to Pottersville \- there at 1.30 \- dinner, short notes written home and then change of rig \- at last, in camp costume, we got away at about 4 o'clk \- Rode 8 miles to Champney, getting there at about 6.30 \- Tea between 7 \+ 8 \- a very good one \- quite heavy showers \+ a wet eve. Much fun \+ bilting on Miss Mary Cook's sneezing \- Got to bed about 9.30
