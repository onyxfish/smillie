## Left Page

### January Wednesday, 24 1872

Up early — glorious mng — got to the "Evell Mail" office at 9 o'clk — Saw Mat Bundy — then to Colton's — got a copy of my Chromo — horrid — then to Sutton's — not in — then to A.B.N. Co — then to P.O. with Money order — again to Sutton's — not in — again to Evell Mail to see Townley — not in — then to D. Appleton's \+ Co — saw Bunce — then to Anthony's \+ sent a couple of hours selecting photos — then to Scribner's saw the Editorial \+ Faff of the Monthly \+ then to Walter Blackman's and at last, at 3.30, home — tired \+ hungry — the Rest of P. M. looking over my new photo's — and so during the even'g, got to bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### January Thursday, 25 1872

Superb day, windy, clear \+ cold. Do not feel like work yet. Commenced cutting on my drawing "Sunlight on a Shadowed Path" — Spent the afternoon trying to re-write my letter "The Story of the Saints" — Even'g, at 7.45 at N.A. of D. Private view of the 5th annual collection of Water Colors. — appeared to be a very well pleased crowd. my poor little sketches took more attention than I had expected — Home at 11 o'clk. \+ to bed som after.
