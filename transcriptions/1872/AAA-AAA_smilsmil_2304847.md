## Left Page

### April, Thursday 11, 1872

Superb day \- Abq o'clk \- at 22d Regt Armory in 14th St to arrange pictures in gallery \- Tremendous work \+ bother, helping Coleman \- Had my lunch in my pocket \+ did not leave the bldg until 4.30 when I went to see the pictures at John Taylor Johnston's \- Reception \- especially "The Slavery" by Sumner \- met him a number of pleasant acquaintances \- Back to 14th St at 5.45 \- There until 6.20 then home \- Wrote some press notices Joe \+ Will at the house \- To bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### April, Friday 12, 1872

Charming day \- Got to Armory in 14th St \- at 8.30 \- met Coleman \+ got to work hanging \- at it until 12 o'clk \- Rode up home \- lunched, and back at 1 \- and did not leave the building until after 7 o'clk \- got home at 7.30 quite worn out \- After tea, in my chair, napped \+ paid for it after I got to bed at 11 o'clk, by lying awake an hour or more \- Passed an unrefreshing night \- dreaming about pictures that would not stay hung.
