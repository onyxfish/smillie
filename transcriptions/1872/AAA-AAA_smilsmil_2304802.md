## Left Page

### January, Saturday, 6, 1872

Glorious day — Got down to Marine Court in Chambers S\. at 9.30 — to find no court for the day — found Judge Gross in his office and after hard pleading was excused for the term — Then to Continental Ins. Co. then to Seaman's Savings Bank then to A.B.N. Co — then up to Leavitt's, Snedecor's \+ Palette oyster. Home to lunch at 1.45    By this time, day overcast \+ dreary — Afternoon felt tired out and did little else than common study of head from Katte — Suelf went to Phil. concert which I enjoyed exceedingly got to bed at just midnight. Charl at home quite sick.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday, 7, 1872

Superb cold day — Myself went to Dr Hastings' — Text Prov. 9" Ch. 10" v," The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom". The text for the year is "The hope of glory" — Home to dinner — During the p.m. finished a letter for the Mail. at 5 walked up to and about Central Park — very cold — Trs to tea at 6 — Suelf Reading \+ writing — to bed at 11.15
