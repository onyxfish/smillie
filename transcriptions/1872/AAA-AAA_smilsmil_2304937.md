## Left Page

### October Monday, 14 1872.

*Williamsport. Pa.*

Steady Rain — Spent the m'g until after 11, packing
& clearing up Blue Room,
preparatory to final leaving.
Had a game of billiards with
Jack & the Cap't. — Ab't lunch
Started ab't 20 for depot — gun
driving — Cap't. accompanying —
Sun & Rain struggling. Train due
at 1.50 — got away at 2.20 — detained
by heated Journal — lost half an hour.
Some beautiful effects of autumn on
mist, sun & sparkling rain — as
we passed Alba, hills tops well
sprinkled with snow — thundering
down heavy grade, steep hill on me side
River on the other — making up time —
a loud crack underneath Parlor Car —
Conductor jumps for bell Rope — Train
brought to a stand still — a spring bolt
gone — through the Ralston valley —
very narrow & wild — Wm port at 5.15 —
made up 15 m's of lost time — Stuart at
the depot — walked home with him
— very bless antly greeted — Tea at once
Twelf, Stewart went out — back at 11.30 —
Josie & I chatting — to bed at 12.

## Right Page

### October Tuesday, 15 1872.

Overcast m'g — B'kf't. about 8.30 —
It was after 10. when Josie B. and
I started out. Sauntered down town,
shopping & looking about us by
the way — went to Stuart's Office —
he joined us & we went through
more streets — across the R.R. along
High St. down by the Herdic He & R.R.
Station & so home about 1 o'clk. The
air very cold & raw — Dinner soon
after 1 — The S & I went to a number
of the great lumbering mills. Stuart
left me & I wandered about from
one mill to another until near
5 o'clk. when I Ret'd home —
Tea soon after 6 — Stuart
not home to tea — a neighbor,
a lady, called — left at 9 o'clk.
Played Cab. Jack with Josie
until near 11 — Stuart Ret'd
after 10 — To bed at 11.30.
