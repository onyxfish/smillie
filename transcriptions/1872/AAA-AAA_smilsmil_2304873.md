## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 4, 1872

Quite a chilly, windy day. Rain commenced about noon \+ heavy all the P.m. Got to studio at 9 o'clk. Started on Evening sketch for Borse \+ got it painted in by 1.30 — Pretty good — Home to lunch at 1.45 — Started writing a letter to the Mou but feeling sleepy, lay down \+ stayed there until 5 o'clk. Then went to studio \+ cleared up. Home to tea at 6.45 — Evng, writing Reading, etc. — etc until 11 o'clk — to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 5, 1872

a dismal cold day, with mud \+ rain like March. Got to Studio at 4.15 and set to work Repainting Mt. Lafayette, 10×18, for Watkins of San Francisco. Miss Bascomb came \+ drew. I worked until 1.15 — then home to lunch, back at 2.30 \+ worked until 5 when I got the thing finished — Home to tea at 6.30 — Evng wrote a letter to Ferguson \- mounted oil sketch by Sam. Colman, washed brushes — bathed my poor feet" and got to bed at about 11.15 feeling out of sorts. Spent a miserable night, alternately shivering and burning. Next morning
