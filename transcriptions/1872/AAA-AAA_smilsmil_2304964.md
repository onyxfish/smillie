## Left Page

### December Wednesday, 11 1872

Clear, windy & cold. Got to
studio at 9.30 & worked on
Cloud's Rest block until 1.15
then home to lunch — Mary &
her boy set off for Po'keepsie on
the 10.30 A.M. train. Clarence
Cook called at studio at 11.30
& me Fallad water color mat[erials]
for an hour. Back to studio
at 2.30 — worked on block as
long as I could see but owing
to interruptions, did not get
it finished — The Editor of the
Arcadian called first, then
Davidson — then Meta Adam —
then Nicole — then Sam Tiff[any] —
I walked up home with Nim[mo] —
he gave me tickets for the opera —
Tea at 6.30 — Charl & I went
to the Academy & heard Succ[a]
in the Merry Wives of Windsor —
charmed by Succ[a] — Home
at 11.30 — to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### December Thursday, 12 1872

Got to studio about 9.20. Finished
drawing "From Cloud's Rest" and
spent rest & w[rote?] "Reading
up" & making notes for a
[illegible] article — Mr. Walter
Brown called about 11 o'clock —
later in the day he sent me
a ticket for Union League Club
meeting to-night. Home to lunch
at 1.30 — back about 2.45 feeling
very much under the weather.
Cut down stretcher & prepared
charcoal sketch for Matis
medium picture. Home at
5.45 — Gave up to headache
& fever — parboiled my feet &
to bed about 10 — Passed the
night in high fever & general
misery — neither asleep nor
awake but dreaming fantastically.
