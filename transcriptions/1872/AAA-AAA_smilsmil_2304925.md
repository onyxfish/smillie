## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 20 1872.

Mr. D, Cap S, \+ May went to Watkins this mf. Waked me up at 5 o'clk \- but went to sleep again \+ did not get up until 8. Bkfs. near 9 \- High wind \+ cloudy sky \- Allie not well. Fussing about until near 11 \- They out to carriage house \+ worked with crayon from the grey until 1 o'clk \- Lunch at 1.30 \- After 3 when I went to stable \- John washing carriage just where I wanted to work \- 4 when I started oil study of grey's fore legs. At 5.30 got away on Dandy, went up to Horseheads \- a great trot back after sunset, cloudless every sky. Bath. Sea in library at 7.30 \- Folks back from Watkins at 8.30 \- Evef, billiards with Cap \+ Jack \- To bed at 11 \-

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 21 1872.

Just the loveliest sort of a day, aetho perhaps a trifle too warm \- but I did not find it so, sitting in the stable yard working over first study that I painted from Dandy. Painted until after 1 o'clk \- then called to dinner. Got to work again about 3 \- painted in grey's fore legs \- worked in carriage house. Soon after 5, saddled "Dandy", Rode below town towards Baden \- delighted with meadows \- groups of trees and glimpses of distant hills \- in the under twilight effect of a Sep. evg just after sunset \- commenced climbing the hill \- soon had the loveliest view overlooking Elmira away to the hills on the far side of the valley \- over all, a pure, luminous sky, broken only by one brilliant cloud \- the Chemung reflecting all. Rode on over the hill \+ down by the water cure \- got in a little before 7 \- met Miss Dickenson \+ Bray \+ to tea \- cards until after 10. To bed a little before 11.
