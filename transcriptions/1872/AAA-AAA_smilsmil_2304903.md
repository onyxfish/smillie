## Left Page

### August Monday, 5 1872.

Up at 5 o'clk — took a real swim, going out beyond my depth. Back to bkfst. at 6 — dismal jollity — Every thing packed & all away from camp at 9.15 — Morning without a cloud — air like champagne — but not exhilarating enough to make us happy. Old camp carried out to the middle of the lake & sunk with dire accompaniment. Down among the dead men — Yunwa thinks that Peach Lake or any other lake is but a Ragbag to her soul." — The last sight of Racquette through the River like an inlet Lake a gate way into Paradise — got to Forked Lake at 10.15 — a short halt then over the lake to the push camp — Saw two boats go over the Rapids — I carried Henry's boat about 50 lbs, nearly ½ a mile — Yoke hurt me — otherwise not bad work — Miss Edith & Annie [illegible] shouldered it for a little way — Will K. carried it nearly a mile — got C. [illegible] launched at 1.5 at end of Long camp — The Rivers more beautiful than ever — got to a camping ground on Song Lake about 3.30 clk — Oscar I. never left us to go out as we came in — Pitched up in our camp site — Tent put up for the girls — table arranged, but owing to delay in baggage coming up, did not get dinner until 7 — Pork — Hominy — boiled eggs & hot biscuits. Built a fire on blocks of dead wood logs — sat there until 10 o'clk — I took a short row with Misses Cook & Howe — common place compared with Racquette — To bed (!) about 10.30 — on the bare ground — No boughs under us, the only luxury, an accommodating hole for my hips — hardly any fire — wind quite high but warm — in a grove of white birch that almost roared — but I liked it —

## Right Page

### August Tuesday, 6 1872.

Up at 6.30 — sky overcast & threats of rain — new to the lakes — bad beach & Rodolph water — but went in & tried my swim — got beyond my depth & for a few moments was rather scared — Relieved S & G when we had bkfst — Two or three showers but too slight to wet. All the party off for new camp ground soon after 11 — but all supplemental party of Annie, Yunwa, I, all Cook & myself started at the same time with Elbert & David for Owl's Head — Rowed up the lake a mile or two — entered a small inlet hauled up the boats & struck into very fine woods. Crossed several small, cold streams, that to us, proved very refreshing — In 1¾ hours from the boats reached the top, a broad fine cleared table of rocks. The view all about was superb. The day, beyond description — The blue distance melting into the atmosphere beyond, seemed no less intangible than the air itself. Racquette shimmered in the lap of far away hills lovelier than ever and now so rich with pleasant memories. Long Lake stretched also a river. Clear pond was at our feet — miles beyond that, then Rock & [illegible] — Forked South Pond & others that I do not now recall. The wide stretching wilderness of unbroken forest was very impressive — I stayed upon the top one hour forty minutes. I took two sketches — Started down at 2.40 — Reached boats at 3.41. & then at once started down the lake, with a [illegible] [illegible] and does not please me — There are [illegible] & poor farms — very much at variance with the spirit of our journey — Do not forget view of distant Adirondack Mts from Owl's Head. The Row down the lake increasing in beauty every moment — clouds fading away & afternoon sky growing ever until a cloudless even & crescent moon filled the measure to its utmost — Got to our new camp opposite Camp [illegible] at 6.39 — embowered in trees — Picturesque, but not so open & breezy as our camp upon Racquette — a good square meal by candle light — but no trout or venison — Everyone all very quick in camp. The fires burnt low & got to my blanket shortly after 11.
