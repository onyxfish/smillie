## Left Page

### April, Wednesday, 3, 1872

Charming springlike day — Got to studio at 8,35 — wrote for a while \+ then to work, painting over "Dark against day's golden dark". About noon Miss Dawes \+ a young lady friend called — Spent a very pleasant hour. Home to lunch at 1,30 — back at 2,15 — A day of processions — Military funeral of Genl. Anderson in the mtf. Dances in the afternoon — Painted until 5 — wrote until after 6 — Home to tea at 7 — At 8,30, called on Wm. A. P. Stokes to beg picture for Homeopathic fair. Very pleasant call — Home at 9,30 — To bed at 10,30.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday, 4, 1872

Very pleasant day — Got to studio at 8,40 — worked most of the mtf. on photo of Mulford's father — At 12, 15 went to Kneedler's — saw pictures, met Mrs. Wm. Martin's niece \+ bought bristol board — Home to lunch at 1,30 — back at 2,45. Spent the P.m. trying to work on Architectural drawing for A. B. N. Co. — Home to tea at 7 — Evg. went out on fair business. Called on Butler, W. L. Andrews \+ Jas. Fraser. Very pleasant call at F's, but made up my mind to do no more of this work — Home at 11,10 — To bed at 11,45.
