## Left Page

### August, Friday 9, 1872.

At Adirondack Hotel Keeseville.

Up at 5 after a very hot night — Room right over the kitchen — Mitt Will \+ Gio went out to swim \+ was much refreshed — much amused to find what a miserable, shabby place it was that had appeared so beautiful the evef. before — Bkfst at 6.30 and about 7.30 off in two lumbering old stages. I sat outside — most fearably hot and the Road very uninteresting — some fine views of White face \+ Saranac mts. Reached the Toll Gate .25 miles at about 1.30 — a bread \+ milk lunch — lots of thick milk — Tormented by black flies, we sat about until 4 o'clk — The Road as uninteresting as ever, except occasional views of White face \+ the mts. until we at last reached Ausable Forks — they time after children middle distances — after the Forks kept alone Ausable River. Beautiful sun set \+ views of Whiteface — but who reconciled to the outrageous plank Road got to Keeseville at 8.15 — all 4 us tired but nobody cross — even when we found that our baggage that had been sent from Pottersville had been greatly damaged by water — Miss Edith's loss was most serious. Supper at 9 — all of us were weighed Mr. Wright had lost 6 lbs. W. C \+ G. C. had also lost — most of the ladies had gained from 1 to 4 lbs — I gained 3 lbs. On average gain of the party was out 4 lbs — Chatting in parlor until 10 — rolled out 11.30

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 10, 1872.

At Leland House. Schroon Lake. Room 24.

Up at 6 — scurrying about until 7 — their help — at 7.45 all off on Stage for Port Kent. Mrs. Beers and her party of 7 cut on the Stage. We have been off on a little jaunt for a few days \+ have now Returning to Elizabeth town. Clear mist and strong breeze — Very pleasant ride — got to landing, 5 miles — just in time for Steamer Oakes Ames — a very breezy sail over to Burlington — There at about 9.30 — took a short walk to a hill near the landing — disagreeable part of the town — very hot and no place to desk — Go back to dock — waited until after 11 — boat behind time for the "Adirondack" due at 10.45 — again on our way — the sail down up the lake delightful altho' hazy on both sides — all of the Mts. almost could be seen — thunder storms over Adirondack mts. the green Mts. "such stuff as dreams are made of" — most fort abt 5 elt — dinner Crown Point at 2.30 — another subdivision of our party, leaving of heart strings — met Fannie Miss Annie \+ 3 asked for Schroon Lake — 26 miles — a thunder shower south of us \+ promises of plenty more from all around — made the rest of preparation me could — just before 3 — if in 2 seat wagon that Leland Road sent for us — at my fell came me of the heaviest squalls that I ever was out in — could not hold my eyes down — as it came over the hill — to ask face was terrible — a blue-back turmoil of clouds that seemed to roll over \+ over — it was over in 10 or 5 minutes — just at the close me got under cover — Re arranged things — found that me were not seriously wet. Held on — spirited horses — but the road sad — most of the country tolerably plain \+ cultivated — more so than I had expected — corn \+ oats generally laid flat by the storm — more Rain at intervals, settling into it quite steadily at 6 o'ck — many views \+ glimpses of Paradox Lake which must be very beautiful. Under favorable light. Of course, under such circumstances [illegible] but little of the Road — got to Leland's about 5 feeling mite chilly — found Mr. Kimball, Family settling — big house — new \+ full of people — charades under way — met three or four of value \+ the emptied of Colonis. Wilt \+ I had supper about 9 — the girls in their room — Hot Whiskey \+ to bed soon after 10.
