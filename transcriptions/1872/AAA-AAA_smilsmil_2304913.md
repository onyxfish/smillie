## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 27, 1872

Beautiful day, altho' part of mt[illegible] looked rather dubious \- about time even a slight sprinkling 4 P am. After Hef?, at packing \- mult [illegible] by 11 o'clk. Dinner abt and then the P.M. went by somewhere and nothing was done, altho' I made an effort or two to work on one of my blocks \- [illegible] at 5.30 \- all good byes said \+ away at 6 \- foot of Chambers St. at 6.40 \- boat away at 7 \- very heavy Train \- Knew nobody on [illegible] \- off at 7.30 \- a very stupid sort of an evng, hardly Relieved by a little episode of professional gambling in next section.
Turned in about 11 and had a night of tolerable Rest.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 28, 1872

Willow Brook \- Elmira \-

Up a few minutes before 5 \- near Elmira \- hurried up \- on time \- [illegible] at 5.20, went to livery stable for hack when Jack, Allie \+ Miss Josie Jessup came up with me \- drove right to Gut D[illegible] \- chilly \+ foggy \- must not forget the glorious Sunrise effect that I saw from the cars \- walking about for a while to get warm, then dressed \+ left about 8 \- in library about for an hour or more, then to work on block "wreck" until near dinner time, 2 o'clk. After dinner, donned flannel shirt \+ with Allie, Josie \+ Jack went up on the hill near the house \- did not return until near 7 \- tea about 7.30 \- Evng a number of young people came in, among them Mr. Reed (Jut.) \- In billiard Room dancing etc, until after 10. To bed about 11.
