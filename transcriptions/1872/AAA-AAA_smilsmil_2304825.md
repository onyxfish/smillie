## Left Page

### February Wednesday, 21 1872.

Mild, pleasant day - got to studio at 9 o'clk. Very much out of sorts - head confused and Rheumatic pains down my back \+ legs - Did not feel able to go on with the painting of W. Ges Whitney's picture, so commenced charcoaling in 24×40". Wt. Clark "High Sierras" - about noon VerPlanck came in \+ abt Miss Cassandra Parker \+ sister - we met her on Pacific R.R. Home to lunch at 1.45 - back at 2.45 V.P. came in again. At 4.30 went to Appletons to see Bunce then to Anthony's - Schill \+ Dinau's - Snedecors \+ Leavitt's - back to Studio \+ home to tea at 6.30 - Self attempted to mount large Watkins photo - gave it up after one - got to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### February Thursday, 22 1872.

Fierce, March-like day; beautiful overhead, but boisterous and dirty below. Got to studio at 9 o'clk and commenced the Repainting of W. Whitney's picture (7th day) W. Mortimer Smith came in - took a fancy to the 8×13 that I painted the other day - Told me to finish it up for him - McKrudson called at 12.45 - Home to lunch at 1.20 - back at 2.30. W. Bunce called - had a long talk concerning Yo-Semite and publishing - then Charley Adams - then Mr. Dart \+ niece - Got over sky and part of mts. Home to tea at 6.30 - Dressed \+ at 8.30 at St. Nicholas Hotel to call on the Misses Parker. They Ret. to Conn. this P.M. Very cold - walked up - Stopped at Leavitt's gallery - met S. Shattuck, Bradford, Ryder, etc. Home at 9.40 - wrote a note to Uncle Willie and to bed about 11
