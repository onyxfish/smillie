## Left Page

### October Wednesday, 16 1872

Weather still dubious in the mty. Bkfst at 8.45 — Soon after 10 — went out with J — to take a short walk — the weather clearing so charmingly that we did not return until noon, having sauntered down, or up, or out to the bridges that cross the Lycoming. Dinner at 1.30 — Near 3, when J + I started for the hills north of the house. The day was now superb — almost without a cloud, and the air fresh and exhilarating went away to the top of the hill — very fine view in all directions — up & down the valley, over Mty port & up the Lycoming — A superb sunset — J + I tried hard to sentimentalize and repeat poetry — in at 5.30 — Tea soon after 6 — Then with S to Herdick He — Met Mr. [illegible], he rode home with us — there found Mr Maynard — Chatting & story telling until after 9 — then they left — S, J + I at Cal. Jack until 11 — To bed at 11.45. A wonderful moonlight night —
Very heavy frost —

## Right Page

### October Thursday, 17 1872

Pleasant mty, but signs of change in the weather. Bkfst at 9.30 — Some trouble to determine what to do with the day — I settled to my work, tracing drawing of Bridal Veil Fall — worked 2 hrs from 11 until near 1 — Just before 12, Stuart + Josie went to a wedding. Returned in half an hour — lunch and soon after 1 we all started off in a barouche, drove to Celletany then out along the Susquehanna (west branch), past the great boom to the Linden R.R. bridge — then crossed to Nigh Road — Rain commenced falling — drove along the hill sides — charming drive, through Newberry & home at 5.30 — Tea at 6.30 — Evening reading "Kathrina" & playing Cal. Jack until 10.30 — To bed at 11 — Some heavy rain with thunder & lightening —
