## Left Page

### July, Monday 8, 1872

Delightful day with thunder storms passing near us ab noon. Got at my Big Tree block, and altho' this the fourth day, I worked on it until 5 o'clk, \+ yet it is not finished. It will be financial ruin' if they all takes as long time. Dinner abt 1 \- At 5 o'clk, I got saddle on the horse \+ went away up the East Road, then first road to left, then next to right, then to right, going about a triangle \+ home at 6.30. Every thing was glorious \- air \+ views superb \- a great thunder cloud giving shade most of the time. The horse was tolerable \- Every, sitting about as usual. My right eye has been troubling me all day, felt as though sand was in it \- Got to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 9, 1872

at Mr. Delancy Verplank's. Fishkill.

Up about 7 \- eye still troublesome. Sky overcast, but broke away by 10 o'clk. \- mvf spent packing in a very leisurely sort of way. Dinner about 12.30 \- at 1.30 Carriage from Po' \- came \+ me went into town \- left the girls at Mrs. Parker's \- went through the new Public Library \- and at 3 o'clk. Took train for Fishkill, got there at 3.35. Found old Mr. Verplank \+ Miss Kate Rogers waiting for me \- at once to the V's \- Young V. mother \+ sister left this a.m. Young Mr. V. doing the honors of the house. Every. drove to the top of Reese's hill \+ had a glorious view altho the weather is threatening \- Tea at 8 \- Every. chatting \+ to bed at 11.
