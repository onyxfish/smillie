## Left Page

### July Friday, 26 1872.

After a good share of sleep up at 6.30 — overcast + threatening sky. The fellows took boats + went in for a swim at 7 — Quite 8 when we had bkfp. Venison brought over from Forked Lake by Mr. Wright's son. Rain commenced early — Spent most of the mng from about 9 until after 1, with Emma J. out on the lake in the Rain, and skirting along the shore — Three delightful hours. — a cup of tea + crackers. Busy preparations for dinner until 4 o'clk — a great dinner of broiled brook + Lake Trout and venison — Soon after 5, went with Geo. Cook + David Rice (guide) a mile + a half down the lake to fish at an inlet — a very heavy shower, but prepared for it — fished — no luck — superb effects of cloud + Sunset light, never saw any thing a grander or more majestic — heavy thunder clouds swept over — darkness + rolling thunder — back to camp about 8 o'clk — Well, many Rain, all in the Ladies shanty — much fun with singing and Charades — Mr. Wright especially funny in Racquette. (Rack-eat — Racket) Inspector (Com-Spectre) — etc. Fine fire — good light and splendid effects — got to bed at about 11 — a first rate sleep. Mary Cook again calls for Nitchel.

## Right Page

### July Saturday, 27 1872.

Superb morning. at 7 went out for our morning bath — made a little progress in swimming. a Royal bkfp. at 8 — About 9.30, three boats got off for an excursion on the lake. Emma J. + Fanny K. in my boat. Pulled away south past Constable Point, around Osprey Island, then to Murray Island + about among smaller isles + Rocks, enjoying to the utmost every charm and beauty of cloud + distant mts, the beautiful color of the water, the vistas opening as we passed point after point and the tall and graceful trees that lined the shore. Lunched on crackers at o'clk in a little cove out of the wind — then started home — The wind high, from the north and the water very rough — all of us more or less wet but "not afraid" — got to camp at about 3, after a long pull + a hard pull of at least 10 miles — but it was a good pull — Found Nitchel almost ready to start for Long Lake — a hurried mail was written — I sent a note to Father and then general preparations for dinner which was served soon after 4. Although very unfortunate, not being able to get either venison or trout we all had plenty to eat and of course, enjoyed it. Sunset and twilight on the water. Emma sang for us, german, french + scotch ballads — could it help getting sentimental — The man would not be mortal who could enjoy such a season without emotion. Back to camp after 8 — A quiet evening out with the ladies in their shanty — to bed at 11 — Miss Cook again calls for fire.
