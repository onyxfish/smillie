## Left Page

### Sept. Monday, 2 1872

Fresh, cloudy mng. again — Up at 7 o'clk —
After bkfst — Genl. D. & Prof. Jane off for Buffalo —
much discussion as to whether we sh[ould]
go to Watkins Glen — Walked & wrote
a letter to Josie Kently — About 11.30
rode to depot — Sprinkling rain —
Train late 12.30 — Flying Rain —
could not make up our minds
to go & at last got on to the train
without making up our minds —
Reached Watkins a little bef[ore]
2 — at once to the glen, by horse car
about a mile — ¾ of the way through the glen
reached Glen House — a 3 mile like cottage,
hanging on to the side hill — tolerable dinn[er] —
our party of 8 — Misses jessup — McDundie — Au[nt?]
Cap[t]. & Mrs. Liscomb — Bray Hall — Jack & [—]
about 3.30 on through the valley & the
weather cleared charmingly so that
we had beautiful effects of light
& shadow through the gorge which
was finer than I expected — out
at 4.45 — over the hill & by Lake View
Ave. & had a very fine view of
Seneca Lake — then down to depot — had
an hour to spare — hired a little steam
boat — water quite rough — but jolly —
Back to Elmira at 8 — a before me[?]
Reached the house & had supper
— very tired — nearly 11 when I
got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 3 1872

Up at 6.30 — Beautiful morning.
Bkfst. at 8 — After bkfst — Rode
into town & purchased wooden
platter for May's Wooden Wedding
— back about 11 — worked on it,
cleaning & preparing — then
note to Will Kimball until
dinner, soon after 1 — After
dinner went with Cap[t]. up on
the hill — Ret[urned]. & sketched out
subject on platter — then went
to barn & sketched from the
grey horse until 6 — then
took a walk & back to tea at
7\. Eugene & wife to tea — Susy
Pin Poole with Bray Hall, Cap[t].
& Jack until 9 — then with the
ladies until 11 — To bed at
11.30.
