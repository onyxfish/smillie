## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 16, 1872

Mng. still cloudy with very strong disposition to shower — went to town by 8.30 a.m. Stage. Stopped at Burgraff's \+ got big boots — then home — then to Arnueds't \& Co got flannel — then to Kuntze and ordered 3 photos of Miss Arnold who died a few days ago — then to Miller's \+ ordered shirts — then to Norton's — saw Charley Burnham — then to Rubber Store — then to 34th Boway \+ bought a blanket — then to Cook \+ Valentine 39\+41 Wallcum — then home at 1 — Lunch — Did a number of little messages near home \+ caught 3 o'clk Stage to A.N.Y.'s — there at 4 — worked on outline of Merced Gorge until dinner at 6 — Eveg. wandering about; air very breezy \+ fresh \+ moonlight charming —
To bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 17, 1872

An accessively hot day; thunder storms all about, two or three of which passed over us — Mother arr'd from 41\+45; at about 9.30 a.m. \+ Rete home at 7.30 — I got to work on drawing of Sentinel Rock and finished it — then on "Descent into the Yo.Se." \+ finished that about 3 o'clk. — Lunch abt 1 — I took up "Merced Gorge" \+ worked on that until near 6, when we had dinner. — Charl visiting Townsend at Rye — I spent the eveg. alone on west piazza until near 10 — then Junie joined me — Talked until near 11 — then to bed. Glorious moonlight.
Sick \+ uncomfortable during the night.
