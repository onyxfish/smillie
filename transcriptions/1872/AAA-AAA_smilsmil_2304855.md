## Left Page

### April, Saturday, 27, 1872.

Charming day — got to Studio
a few minutes before 9 and
at once to work with graver
on Head (6½ days) About
1 o'clk. Elisha Mulford came
in — Stayed until 1.30 —
Home to lunch at 1.45 — back
at 2.45 — Miss Bascomb at
Studio, drawing — Worked
until 6 — At about 7 went
to Mrs. Hyde's — 195 Madison ave.,
where Belle & Mary T. were —
There until 7.45 — took them
to a 6th Ave Car — then Home to
tea at 8 — At 9 o'clk. went
to Fair — last night — Home
at 11.30 — to bed at 11.45.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday, 28, 1872.

Delightful day. Mdf went
to Dr. Hastings & heard him
preach from a triple text,
John 20th ch. 29th v., 2nd Cor. 5th ch. 7th v.,
and 1st Peter 1st ch. 8th v. Home
at 12.15 — Father, who has been
at home for two or three days,
very miserable, is to day much
better. Afternoon, arranging
things until 4 o'clk — then to
bed & slept until after 6 —
Eself went to B'dway Tab. &
heard Dr. Taylor — a very
interesting sermon from a verse
in the Parable of the Unjust
Steward, concerning faithfulness
in little things — Home
at 9.15. Read to Father "Wanted
a Religion for the Hindoos" and
got to bed at 11.30
