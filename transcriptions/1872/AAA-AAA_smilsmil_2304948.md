## Left Page

### November Thursday, 7 1872.

Heavy Rain during the mng.
Got to studio at 9.20 — For
a while, picking up things
that still persist in lying
about, then commenced work
on Yp. Senide block — "Bathers"
Having forgotten my lunch,
at 12.15, went home for it, back
in an hour & made a cup of
tea — Worked until 4 o'clk.
At 4.20, went to Dewey's, the
Tailor, & tried on coat. Walked
over to 116 Clinton Pl. looking for
Miss Parmenter, a model — Then
to Seavitt's Art Rooms and saw
the Belmont Collection — then
to studio — fixed fire & home
to tea at 6.45 — Evening
wrote two or three notes —
Read newspapers and to
bed at 10.

## Right Page

### November Friday, 8 1872.

Weather varying between very
fine and dubious — Got to Studio
at 9.10 — to work on "Bather" block.
Mr. Genl. Diven with little Eugene
came in about 11 o'clk — and so
did Mary Tweedy. Dr Hitchcock,
a friend of George's, also called.
It was near 2 o'clk before I got
a chance to prepare lunch,
then to Phil. Rehearsal, which
I enjoyed very much. After,
walked up to Studio with Belle
Tweedy — at 4.30 — Worked a few
minutes — washed up things &
soon after 5 o'clk — went over to
the Hoffman Ho. met all the
Owens — & all the Jessups, young
and old. Home to tea at 6.30
at 8 o'clk. Charl & J. went to
the Am. Institute Fair — Home
at 10.35 — To bed about 11.20
Glorious moonlight night.
