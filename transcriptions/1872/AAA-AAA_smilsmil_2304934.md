## Left Page

### October Tuesday, 8 1872

Cold, cloudy & windy — so I gave up idea of working in the stable & stuck to my Cathedral Spires until 4 o'cl. having a lunch at 1 — Got the drawing almost finished. At 4.20 off on the grey. Rode into town — got mail — letter from Charl & Matt about Philharmonics — I'm ordered home forth with — I Rode on, down River Street to the mill & the foot of the hill — went up Road to Right & over into valley beyond — very rough & steep — part of the way through a wood after sundown — out at last on Walter Cure Road & home about 6.30 — having had a superb Ride — sky almost cloudless & young moon brilliant. Evef, Bray Hall & Miss Dickenson called — billiards with Bray — They left at 9 — I Read papers until 10 — To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### October Wednesday, 9 1872

Superb day — After bkfst. wrote letter to Matt & note to Miss Kimball concerning Philharmonic matters — At 9.30 saddled the grey & Rode in to P.O. Stopped at Mrs. G.M. D's to tell her that a telegram had been Rec'd announcing that her husband had arr'd — Back about 12. Worked on blocks until 1.30 when I had lunch — Nearly 3 before I got to work in Stable painting hind legs of the grey. At 5.30 with Mr. D — went to Aleck's by invitation — to tea & spend the evef — After tea, Mr. Aleck & I went to call on Mary Reed for half an hour — back, cards until 10.30 — then home & just about midnight when I got to bed —
