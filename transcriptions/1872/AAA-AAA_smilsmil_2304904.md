## Left Page

### August Wednesday, 7 1872.

Up at 5. Charming mist rosy mist curling from the water — water like glass — took boat \+ went out for an hour. Think the lower end of long lake very beautiful — back at 6 \+ went with Will K. for hrs. Mr Swiim — Helps — about 8 — went out in boat \+ got two sketches — then back \+ party broke camp at 11 — In Henry's boat with Miss Annie C. a mile to the outlet and 7 on Racquette River — then a carry of 1/4 miles — went down to a full at other end of carry — waited for a long time owing to delay in getting baggage over — the fall at lower end of carry most picturesque — Reached that about 2.30. Then to Mother Johnston's \+ started in boat at 3.40 — 7 miles on Racquette River again — water like a mill pond — water Maples, graceful as elms leaning over the banks and standing in good order, looking more like an orchard than a forest. Then turned into Stony Creek a most unnecessarily crooked stream, terribly scarred by fire — 2 miles — then Stony Creek Pond, then to Spectacle Ponds — without interest. Wagons at carry, boats \+ baggage pulled on them \+ hauled one mile to Corey on the Upper Savanac — cleaved most of the distance \+ road an ordinary wagon road. All now on the carry, picking huckleberries and misbehaving in a very pleasant manner — Reached Corey's about 6.30 — soon after went out to row on the lake with Annie \+ fanny — evening \+ new moon very beautiful \+ rowed around two of the islands \+ back to house at 9. Heavy dinner of venison — a quiet hour or so, after — then to bed at 10. Shared with Will K. Rolled ourselves in our blankets \+ slept on top of the bed.

19 miles of Rowing \+ 2 1/4 of carry

## Right Page

### August Thursday, 8 1872.

Up at 5.30 — at 6 went out with Will to swim, had a good time — cloudless sky, except to the W. — Back to helps. at 7 all got away at 9.5 — In Henry's boat with fanny K. The pull up Savanac very interesting — so many islands and glimpses of distant mts. Thunder storm threatening — storm passing over Whiteface — grand — storm passed north of us to our great joy. Reached Norton house kept by Col. late Hough. at 10.45 — at 1.5 got started on 4 mile carry — a very comfortable road through a wood terribly defaced by fire — Reached the end at 12.25 — a dismal mud hole where we waited until 1.20 regretting all the time that we could not be enjoying the beautiful view of mts away to the south that all the more charming under the beautiful sky that we left behind — Then boats at last came. Up at 2 we went on — in half an hour pulled into [illegible] pond — Mild, Hatless Kenrick greeted us on landing. Little to interest except view of Whiteface \+ other mts. Short delay meeting boats \+ baggage on two sleds then off again \+ the carry through beautiful woods — Up \+ down hill \+ around about — 2 miles — stopped on the way and lunched. Reached Upper St. Regis just about 4 — First sight very disappointing — low shores and much burned over — improved as we went on. A long bay to N.W. very inviting — pulled across \+ short part into shallow outlet \+ but a few rods into Spitfire pond — a matter of no consequence — through that into a low, banked swamp-like outlet of about 1/4 of a mile, then into Lower St. Regis — Everybody Bridgeroo! Huroo! as we came in sight of Paul Smiths — pulled across the lake, 6 boats abreast, \+ landed with much effect — at once to our rooms, washed \+ dinner at 6.30 — 55 minutes of solid eating — then out on the lake — young moon — mirror like water \+ in the dish light beautifully wooded shores — all entered into the spirit. The result was two delightful hours — singing back at 9 — more Aurora. Met Will Gillendar introduced me to some ladies — To bed at 11. 20
