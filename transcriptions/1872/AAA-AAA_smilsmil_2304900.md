## Left Page

### July, Sunday 28, 1872

Heavy fog when I awoke \- Enjoyed our blankets until near 8 \- Then the fog had lifted, the sun shone bright \+ the water was like glass \- made a little more progress in swimming \- Slept. at 9.30 About 10.30 five boats got off \- sky clouded \+ strong wind from the south. Went first to the old Camp ground of four years ago \- as I had never been there before, I indulged in no sentiment. Then Rowed over to the outlet into Elizabeth Lake \+ through to it \- chartured water lilies \- Rowed to upper end \- a shallow pond, separated only from Racquette by a narrow strip of bush-covered sand \- the outlet a shallow tortuous stream about 8 or 10 yds. through which we poled our boats \- pulled home, fresh breeze \+ water tolerably rough \- got in after 4 oclk. All at work for dinner \- hominy \- Chocolate etc \- etc \- It was 5.30 when we when we finished dinner \- Every in shanty and on the Rock at the waters edge \- until near 11. \- Then a shower of Rain \- then all to bed \-
Miss Cook calls mildly for fire.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 29, 1872

Did not get up until near 8 oclk. the morning overcast \+ threatening Rain \- made slight progress in swimming. Before Slpt. went over to Beech's Island with Fanny K. Got some huckleberries \+ wild Roses \- Found Emma J. and Will K. on the island, also a gathering berries \- After 9 when we had Slpt. \- Between 10 \+ 11 Will \+ I went over to the island \+ picked berries until after 1 \- then back \+ several of us set to work Concocting cake, minus eggs \+ other essentials. A nut cake, iced with sugar decorated with red raspberries \+ chocolate, crisped so as to read "Edith \- 21" and a chocolate jelly cake was the Result. Quite a heavy thunder storm came up about 3 oclk. and from that, it settled into a steady Rain. About 4.30 a brood of young ducks appeared near Camp \- old coote \+ I went off in a boat after them \- it rained hard \+ I had a very heavy jaunt \- the duck were too young to fly, but they went through the water like mad \- C. fired at them the only charges he had (4) but they got away. Back to dinner, very warm \+ somewhat tired. Enjoyed a hearty meal of boiled huckleberries. Oscar J. \+ W. Wright went off at noon with two of the guides to stay out all night hunting deer \- Oscar Came back at 8. Evening, birthday festivities. a procession. miss Edith Crowned. Did the way Norma \- like bearing the cakes \- with graceful gravity she performed her part well \+ was the leading figure in a Remarkably picturesque scene \- Shortly after 11 to bed \- I heard nothing from Miss Cook Rice during the night.
