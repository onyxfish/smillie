## Left Page

### June, Monday, 10, 1872

Suffocating, muggy + overcast. After bkfst. worked on drawing of house, out of doors, until near noon — lunch at 2.15 — at 12.30 with Uncle A. — drove down to the city in buggy. Just escaped a very heavy shower. at 2, I left him at Union Sq. + drove up Fifth Ave to 85th St. Called on Josie Jessup to learn something of the D'wens — Then to McVee at 3.20 — Worked until after 5 — Then dinner at 5.30 — a glorious clear off. Gave "Nero" — the dog — his last meal. Read papers and got to bed about 9.30.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday, 11, 1872

Glorious day — bright and breezy. George left for his Happy Land — Plainfield, J. at 9.30 AM. I worked on drawing, part of the morning out of doors; then in, until lunch at 1 o'clk — Afternoon, worked until after five o'clk. Then dinner — Junie had a caller, a Mr. Gile of Baltimore whom she met on the Pacific R.R. last winter — At 7 o'clk with Uncle A. I Rode down to 59th + 5th Ave + saw Chas. Cornish — back at 8.40 after a charming drive — at 9 went up to my room, spread comfortor and pillows at window + "mooned" for an hour, then went to bed.
