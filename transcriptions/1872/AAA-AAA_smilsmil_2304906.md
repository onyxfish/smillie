## Left Page

### August Sunday, 11 1872.

Up at 8 o'clc — Considerable Rain
during the night. Sky broken, but
thunderous & showery. B'kf't at 9 —
Spent an hour upon piazza —
ab't, sauntered off with Will &
until near 1 o'clc discussed a
variety of subjects running all the
way from syringes to eternal damn-
ation — as we sat in the shadows
of a birch tree & overlooked the
lake. Dinner at 1 — out with
the K's on little balcony — letters
from Campston Read aloud —
About 4 o'clc — gave up and took
a nap for an hour — Tea at 6 —
In the twilight walked out
with Will — on our Return, stop-
ped at the church and joined
in a general sing. Back to
hotel soon after 9 — walking
piazza & chatting until after
10 — 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### August Monday, 12 1872.

Up at 7 after a very Restless
night. Heavy fog — more or less
cloudy all day. Hot & muggy.
B'kf't at 8.30 — At 10 got away in
boat with Fannie — Annie C. & Will
for a Row up Schroon River. Water
high & current very strong — Pulled
4 miles, to first bridge — 12 o'clc —
then turned & floated down — In
at 1.30. The Schroon, a smooth flowing,
rakish stream, from 30 to 60 feet wide, very
tortuous, low banks lined with overhanging
water maples & alders, with occasional
small clearings — in places, a wilderness
stream — then suggesting New England —
The boats, after one lake experience, some
thing absurd — great, clumsy coal-ino
things, barbarously colored Red, white
blue and orange — but the pulling was
inexpressibly easy — everything considered
Dinner just before 2 — For a while
after — camping in no 9 — Then Fannie
Annie C. Carrie K. Will — Woods & I got
into the boat & pulled over to the island
called "Isola Bella" — a wreck of a place
owned by Bayard Clark — Said at first built
up by Col. Ireland but now unoccupied —
a shabby old house — crazy, crooked and
mean in its caricature of something
better — the same spirit pervading every
other part of the island to the detriment
of its natural beauties — Threatening storm
hurried us home, before 6 — Tea before 7 —
very wandering about piazza — No
light — moon & little promise of
better weather for to-morrow — To bed
at 10.30
