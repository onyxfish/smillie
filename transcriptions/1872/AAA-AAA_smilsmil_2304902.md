## Left Page

### August, Thursday 1, 1872

*Camp on Blue Mt. Lake.*

Waked at 4 oclk - charmed by an
indiscribable effect: Pines dark against
a northern sky, lighted by aurora — to the
east the slender crescent of the old moon
fog lying on the lake and in the foreground
the great smouldoring fire that made the
nearer tree trunks glow with ruddy hue,
In spite of all that however went to sleep
again & snoozed soundly until 6 o - then
our swim — then a bkft. of trout, venison,
Partridge, Squirrel - fried bread, potatoes
hot bread, coffee, tea, etc - got away from
Camp for Blue Mt. lake at 9.45 - The sky
cloudless & the water without a ripple -
five boats & five guides - pulled at once
for East Inlet or Marion River — Emma
& Annie Cout in my boat, David Rie Pull-
ing — The inlet one continuous unalloyed
pleasure — gathered many lilies — The
upper end a beautiful alley way of
overhanging trees — Got to the camp at
12.30 — 5 miles from the lake - the carry
only 100 Rods. Near 2 when again we
took boats, a very short pull on Oty River
brought us to Utowana Lake — the first portion
a perfect field of white lilies — the boats
soon appeared to be loaded & every body was
decked with them — passing Slim Point came to
the body of the lake — at upper end entered upon
an extremely narrow passage — then opening —
low trees on one side — tall grass, like meadow
on other — perhaps a mile of it — water shallow
but wonderfully clear — then out on Eagle
Lake, fine views of Blue Mt. always ahead
of us — Clearing on [illegible] — ned Buntline — at
upper end entered a short river — Poled
through very narrow rapids — under bridge —
past rows of graceful Arborvitaes — out on to
Blue Mt. Lake. The old Mt. looming up grandly
— the 30 islands a marvel of beauty in the
afternoon sunlight — sailed Straight [illegible]
admiring aground, beside cold Spring [illegible]

## Right Page

### August, Friday 2, 1872

at 4.30 P.M. - Prompt arrangements for
camping — fires — bough beds — tent up
[illegible] Deputy — tried a sl[illegible] Feb of
boats on the beach — Then went Rowing
with Edith W, Fannie C, about among
the islands — back to a great supper of
[illegible] crackers at 7 - beautiful sunset —
in boat with Fanny K. until 9 - Will Emma
& Miss Howe in another — brightfire on beach
over the fire to lake, blackness of darkness —
stars thick in black sky overhead — singing
until near 11 — Guides under the boats — Will
& J in confabulation until 11.30 — then, rolled
in my blanket for the first time, I slept —
under the open sky. — Up at 6 — threats of a
shower — hurried toilette — bkft. served on
a large log on the beach — a shower — all
clad in rubber or water proofs — much fun —
some extra provisions had been brought up
from Eagle Lake — Clear weather again —
went out on the lake with Will K — Met
Emma & — pulling with Mr. Wright against
a high wind & rough water — beached boats —
transferred loads — Emma with Will —
Coute Mt. W — we made a vast circuit
of the lake — the weather changing altho'
still threatening — Back to Camp before
10 — a few minutes after, all off for home —
the Return through the Rivers. No less
delightful than yesterday — Reached
the old J Carry soon after 1 — lunched in
bread, cheese & raw pork fat — Very heavy
thunder storm — boats turned over — Every
preparation made — exceedingly picturesque
and funny. The Roar of Thunder & Rain — amid
it all soon after 2 — Mr. W. Mitchel & J in one
boat prepared to stay all night fishing —
still hunting — long some more & saw that &
settled into a heavy pour — caught 7 fine
trout — storm so heavy that all noise of a
hunt at night was given up — Mr. W. fixed
himself in bow of boat for a chat & any show
offered. J. Mitchel paddled — I killed mosquitoes —
the River interminable and yet not enjoyed —
the novelty. — Carry 30 to 50 ft. wide — lined with lily
pads — pickerel weed — thin tall grass. Even bushes —
alders, birces & many other growths. Reins burning —
[illegible] — many places quite a display of flowers — at
12 or so put back larches, Arborvitæ — balsam
pine etc — out to the lake about 7 o'clk then
chanced places & Mitchel rowed us home —
Grand change in weather — sudden, breaking
away of clouds — brilliant sun — rainbow forming
complete arch & in parts double —
