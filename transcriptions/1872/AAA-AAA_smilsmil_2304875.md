## Left Page

### June, Saturday, 8, 1872.

Very close, muggy day. Heavy headachy feeling so that I could not work. Arranged my water colors but could not do it. Loafed about the place — Lunch at 1 — Still loafing, unable to work, until 4 o'clk — Then, with Uncle a. + Junie Rode down to the City. At Studio, met Rossell + sent my big "Twilight" home — with Uncle a. Called on Bob Hall in 34th St. Stopped at home + back, through Central P. to Uncle a's — glorious P.M. — got in at 7 — dinner — Went on piazza — To bed at about 10 —

## Right Page

### June, Sunday, 9, 1872.

Glorious day. Up at 7 — Bkfst. at 9 — on piazza until 11 — Then wrote a letter to Meta Adams. Dinner at about 2 — After that, with Afghan + pillow, on a bench under the trees until nearly 5 o'clk. — Junie had a whole raft of yng fellows among them, young Turrell. He + the two Halls, stayed to tea — J., until 9 o'clk. — Went to our Room at 9.30 — Sat on the floor, watched the setting moon + reflections on the water, and talked a practical sort of sentiment until shortly after 10 — Then to bed.
