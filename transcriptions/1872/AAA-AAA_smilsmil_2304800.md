## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 2, 1872

A glorious day — Felt better in the m'g, at 9 o'clk. ment. down town to Marine Court, Part 2° 32 Chambers St. where I am summoned as a Juror. Court adjourned until to-morrow. Back to Studio at 10,30 \+ found that I could not work — Mr Redfield \+ daughter of Phil. \+ young Cheeseman called — then Mrs. Thos. J. Hunt; Home to lunch at 1,30 — Made a Rag lay figures, dressed it in Millie's clothes \+ worked from it in water colors until 4,30 then to Studio — Home to tea at 6 — Felt very much used up. Ment to Dr. Percejo for a few minutes, home at 8 20 \+ to bed at 10 40

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 3, 1872

A miserable day, beginning with snow and ending with a heavy rain — but not more miserable than I — Began the day with a headache and wound up with more thereof. Unable to do anything all day — Slept most of the p.m. on Sofa — towards evening my headache assumed more of the Bilious form — Did not go to bed all night but lounged in my chair or on the Sofa —
