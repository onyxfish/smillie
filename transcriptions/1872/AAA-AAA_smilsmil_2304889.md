## Left Page

### July Saturday, 6 1872.

Heavenly morning — B[illegible] soon after 7 — Up a chump — Tree, sawing limbs \+ rating cherries until 8.30 — then in [illegible] at my work, worked all day very steadily but did not get my Big Tree block finished. Dinner about 1 — worked until after 5, then went out to tree chopping. Tea at 6.30 — Some more chopping. She gives went into town at 8 o'clk. \+ back with Uncle W. Shortly after 9, I amused myself walking about until their arrival — Uncle W. just in from our house — brought a letter from Father — I got to my bed at 11.20 —

## Right Page

### July Sunday, 7 1872.

Another delightful day — not yet too warm — The morning was spent, I hardly know how. Ed Parker walked out from town getting here about 9 — We spent the day with us — for about an hour lying on top of the High Rock — Dinner at 1.30 — Afternoon sauntering about the place until tea time — tea at 6.30 — Then all went to the River — there until near 8 — at 8 drove Parker into town — back at 9, and to-bed at 10.20 — [illegible], wrote a letter to Father,
