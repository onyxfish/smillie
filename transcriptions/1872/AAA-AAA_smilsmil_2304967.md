## Left Page

### December Tuesday, 17 1872.

Charming day — was waked up
about 8 o'clk — A.M. Found myself
feeling remarkably well — Got
up, washed, dressed & had
breakfast. The morning was
spent in reading the newspapers
and gathering together my notes
and materials of western trips.
Had my dinner at 1.30 — Most
of the afternoon was spent
in playing agreeable, first
to Suise, then to Fanny Mulford
who called about 3.30.
Tea about 6 — Evening, I
read until near 9 o'clk —
To bed about 10.20

## Right Page

### December Wednesday, 18 1872.

Miserable day with snow and
rain — up about 8 o'clk —
after a good night's rest feel-
ing much better in general
condition. Delavce called
before I got my breakfast.
Spent most of the mf-
reading. Lunch at 1.30.
Afternoon taking large
waltkins photos of from
their mountings — Tea
at 6.30. Ed Parker came
in for a few minutes on his
way to the six o'clk. Train.
Poetry, more fussing with
photos. Some reading and
some lying about — To bed
at 10.45
