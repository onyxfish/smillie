## Left Page

### July, Thursday 4, 1872

The day began as hat as ever born after 9 oclk. I got to work on Big tree block — worked badly as I felt oppressed with heat \+ sleep — At noon, threatening thunder storms. Dinner, near two, First storm came on soon after — during the afternoon two or three others finally settling into a quiet rain which lasted until near 11. Worked on block — Uncle W. prepared to go to N.Y. This eveg, but at the last moment gave it up. Eveg, until 9.15 spent on front piazza — got to bed about 10.30 — A delightful change in the atmosphere.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 5, 1872

Still very warm. Uncle Willie left for the city of N.Y. som after 8.15 — worked on my block Big trees until 12 — then off for a stroll — did not have dinner until after 1 — worked faithfully until after 6 — slow progress — I am not sufficiently familiar with drawing on wood, to have the amount of confidence necessary to beget despatch — Tea about 7. Parker \+ sister came while we were at tea — Allie was left with us — Ed. \+ Watt went off until near 9 — we went down to the River — Beautiful sun-set. Wrote a note to Verplank telling him that I would be at his place next Tues. P.M — To bed at 10.30 \+ slept glorious until daylight.
