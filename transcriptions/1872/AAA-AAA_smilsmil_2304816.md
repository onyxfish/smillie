## Left Page

### February Saturday, 3 1872.

Tremendous storm of sleet, snow and wind. Up late \+ got to studio at 10.30. \+after a little general cleaning up of last night's performances I varnished \+ touched again on a number of my last summer studies \- Prepared lunch at 1.30 \+ after, spent the afternoon working on other studies.
Home to tea at 6 \- well very stormy \+ at 7.45 was at Philharmon. Very jolious out in doors and enjoyed the concert very much. Walked up 4th ave. with Emma \+ Oscar Irelands \+ the Rest of the way home with young Kimball. In at 10.45 \+ to bed at 11.45

## Right Page

### February Sunday, 4 1872.

Delightful day but thawing so as to make it very disagreeable under foot. Went to Dr. Hastings \+ heard him preach from Acts 18th Ch. 21st V. "I must by all means keep this feast". It was Sacrament Sunday and his sermon was a very earnest appeal to believers outside of the church \- Home to dinner \- Read and napped during the afternoon \- tea at 6 \- and in the evg, revised some of my writing on the Mormons, for the "Mail" \- Uncle Willie went on to Phila. by midnight train \- got to bed at 11.20
