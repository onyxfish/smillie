## Left Page

### July, Tuesday, 30, 1872

A superb day \- all late again. A swim, a chod-kow and Help\- about 9 \- Mr. Wright came in som after from his sons camp on Little Forked \- Letters were written to send out by his guide. After that I went out on the lake with Nwma J \- Skirted along shore, gathered lilies \+ sketched until after 3. Another glorious day for memory. After return Retouched sketches \- built a lily pond \+ tried to make myself generally useful. Greatest element in camp caused by the arrival of Charley \+ Albert with the half of a deer, a 2 year old buck that weighed about 175 lbs. A Royal dinner of broiled venison steaks at 5.30 \- Gel C. Wile K. \+ Oscar J. did not return from fishing until after 7. Twilight went out in boat with Edith W. \+ Annie C. Solid enjoyment. A fire was built on the Rock beach at camp, the water like a glass \+ the effect of Reflections of fire \+ fire\-lighted trees wonderfully beautiful. At ntd, Charley Sabattis went out in his boat, gave us imitations of startled deer \- the cry of deer when caught by does, bay of hound, owl, loon etc. \- Lighted Jack \+ Spadled up to us, absolutely by most noise. After that, went out with Will K. \- fascinated by the stillness the suggestive blackness of the water Reflecting the stars that danced \+ shimmered in inky depths immediately beneath us. On shore a fire had been kindled on the Rock shelf upon which we land \- The effect as seen from the water was charming \- The pam [illegible] in slant its on the flat rocks, all bright in the fine light launched \+ [illegible], their voices sounding singularly clear over the still smooth water. Rolled in as usual after 11

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday, 31, 1872

Yet another wonderful day. but of course all late, as usual \- The regular swim \- I still improve a little \- Wpt at 9 \- 10.45 when we all got off for Browns Tract Mlet \+ Eighth Lake. Hilton chan. I paddled to the Inlet \- Nwma J. \+ Geo C. in the boat. Delightfully fresh breeze \- none of its delights lost upon any of us. Upon a little island at the mouth of the inlet, Charley \+ Geo C. pulled up the neet, about 4 miles of the most unmitigated ly tortuous stream that I ever went \- der \- not wide enough to go through without striking the oars on either side, it stretched, Right \+ left, glossy black water that Reflected rich as cloude mirror the Roses \+ Lilies that lined its sides \- banks \- head none \- the Reeds \+ bushes Rose to a height of perhaps 4 or 5 feet \- at some distances back were Ranged the larches \+ over all a sky fleckled with brilliant fleecy clouds to counter balance the beauty of this scene that in many ways strongly suggested the tropics, we were almost balanced by vast swarms of the gauntlest hungriest musquetoes that ever assailed me \- Got to the camp at 1.15 \- lunched on crackers \+ then on a mile more than a mile to 8th lake, which me reached at 4.45 \- a pretty sheet of water but with nothing particularly picturesque \- Big Buck in a boat \- 250 to 300 lbs. At to camp near 6 \- hungry as hawks \- a venison dinner soon prepared. Evening \- more delicious than any before \- Edith W. \+ Annie C. took me out on the lake \- beautiful sky \+ Reflections that doubled every glory \- Rolled in my blanket in the bottom of the boat I enjoyed a pleasure that was new to me \- an absolute surrender to the witchery of the hour without one single fleshly discomfort. After 9. went out alone \- but the stillness was too much for me \- I felt oppressed by it \- in my blanket for the night at about 11.30
