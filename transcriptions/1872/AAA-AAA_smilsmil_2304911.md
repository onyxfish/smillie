## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 21, 1872

Up a little before 8 \- Heft \+ at work soon after 9 \- meather sweltering \- muggy and cloudy. Finished Merced Gorge \+ commenced drawing "Half Dome from Merced River" \- worked until dinner time \- 1 o'clk. \+ again from 2.30 until 4.45. Phaeton ordered for 5 o'clk. Thunder storm interfered \- waited \- Tea at 6 and about 7, Emma \+ I started out for Lake Wacksabuck, some miles distant. Lovely, misty twilight \+ starlight \- by 8 o'clk., very dark \- hard work sometimes to follow the road \- picked it out until we reached the lake \- very indistinct view from a height \- suggestions finer no doubt than daylight would show \- drove down past Hotel. Mun turned \- directed by North Star \+ [illegible] of the compass very unsteady \- Moon at last managed to rise N.N.E. by N. \- Emma taking our bearings by the North Star, now cloud covered \- Moon soon covered \- moonlight feeble \- so were our efforts to get home \- Sobered down \+ reached Vail's in a very rational frame of mind at 10.20 \- nobody but the stable boy to greet us \- To bed at once \-

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 22, 1872

Clearer atmosphere here \- but no less hot \- after 8 o'clk. Heft. got to work on "Half Dome" at 9.15 \- my spent very pleasantly with all of the J's \- Emma Reading the lively Encyclical letters of H. H. Dinner at 1 \- work again at 2.15 Between 3 \+ 4, quite a tornado of wind \+ rain with thunder, lightening \+ hail \- at [illegible] packed my bag \- just before tea \- and at the same time a very heavy thunder storm set in \- preventing my going to depot \+ continuing for more than an hour \- Accepted the situation philosophically \- Subserv every sunset, twilight \+ storm effect \- waiting or not \- piazza \+ then in parlor \- Emma sings \- a very long Scotch ballad \+ bids us good night before she commences \- will meet us at the breakfast table \-

Got to bed at 10.30
