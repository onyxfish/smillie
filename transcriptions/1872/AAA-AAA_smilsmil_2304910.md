## Left Page

### August, Monday 19, 1872

Up at 7.30 — a very hot mg. Sky soon filmed over \+ breeze from the west making it very agreeable when not moving. About 10 o'clk. a place having been prepared for me in one of the rooms, I got to work upon drawing on wood". Merced Gorge from Nevada fall" — Sleepy \+ stupid but kept it at it with tolerable faithfulness until dinner at — at my work again at 2 \+ continued until 5 — getting my block well advanced. Tea about 6. Then general trouble moonlight ride for the evef. — Shall it be given up? — Showers about — (we had a shower between 3 \+ 4 P.M.) \- the vote \- compromise — all go out rowing\- on the water until 9.30 — warm, but very lovely — beautiful moonlight about 10 o'clk — To bed about 10.45

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 20, 1872

mg overcast and heat not so oppressive, altho' still very hot. About 10 o'clk. got to work on "Merced Gorge" — worked until dinner time — 1 o'clk. — hotter \+ hotter. at my drawing again soon after 2 \+ continued at it until 4.30 — Then with Emma J., in Mr. Baring's Phaeton drove over to Miss Lib. Howe's — there to tea — full table — two Miss Irelands from Harlem — \+ John \+ wife — \+ others. Good old-fashioned \+ profuse. About 9 o'clk great, four-horse wagon drove up — Vail \+ 20 or more of his boarders, big \+ little. Miss H. Emma \+ I joined them. Rode to Ridgefield — no moon — rather foggy. Reached R. before 10 o'clk. — Everybody in bed. Great times looking for ice cream or any thing else — waked up one store keeper — drank up all of his Soda water \+ Root beer \+ carried off a large part of his candy. They to a shoemakers — Woke him up \+ took all of his ice cream. Tableau. Cream in a wagon by moonlight — took all of his peanuts. On the way home, an old folks' concert — funny old ballads — Emma on her high horse — moonlight struggled through about midnight — left Miss H. at her home. Emma \+ I took phaeton again \+ followed big wagon home. Light sparring over dangerous ground. Watching and being watched in camp. Home about 1 o'clk — brilliant moonlight — to bed about.
