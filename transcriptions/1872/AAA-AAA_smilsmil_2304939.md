## Left Page

### October Friday, 18 1872.

Rain — Breakfast at 9 o'clk & about 10. got to work on "Bridal
Veil" — worked pretty steadily
until about 1, when I had a
light lunch — and again at
my work — Mrs. Clara Parker
came in & spent about an hour
after 3 o'clk — During her stay
quite a heavy thunder storm.
Cleared very soon after — Josie
& I walked down town at 4.30
Returned at 5.30 — Dinner
at once — Stuart & his father
having tired of waiting, had
commenced without us.
Every Stuart went to see a
Theatrical Representation —
Josie & I visited until after
8 — then walked in the moon-
light for an hour — Returning,
we played Cal. Jack until
Stuart's Return — Packed
Trunk — to bed at 12.30 —

## Right Page

### October Saturday, 19 1872.

Up at 6 — glorious mrf. Bkf't. at 7.30
at 8.20 Stuart, Josie & I started for Catawissa —
R.R. Station — I got off at 9 — Enjoyed the
scenery along the west Branch, Susquehanna —
beautiful color & bright day — About 11.30
when I crossed the first of the Catawissa
bridges, of which I think there are five —
Some portions of the Road exceedingly
mild & picturesque — Soon after I when
we reached Jamauend, the end of the
Catawissa R.R. — on to the Lehigh Valley
R.R. — weather changed to cloudy &
threatening — Very much interested
in the evidences of energy and teeming
life. the many R.R.s — canals, furnaces,
mills, etc. etc., culminating at Mauch
Chunk where we arr'd soon after 2.
20 m. for dinner — then on — Showers of
rain & streaks of sunshine — At
Catasaqua passed the wreck of a
coal train, done up day before yesterday —
Still wondering at the R.R. trains &
the furnaces & mills — Reached Easton
about 3.30 or 4. From there, over the
N.J. Central R.R., comparatively un-
interesting so I gave up to sleep and
rest — Got into New York at 7.15 and
up home at 8.05 — Father, Mother, Matt
& Kate up at Will's — I got my tea &
spent the evening with Geo. & many —
got to bed about 11.30
