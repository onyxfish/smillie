## Left Page

### Sat. March 15, 1902

**Weather:** Raw, cloudy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw, cloudy day - to studio after 10 laid ground on Morgan plate to draw in new face \+ add to the background. fussed for some time with Morton p.p. taken yesterday - About 12 went to bank with Scribner check for $104 - Thanks to Mr. H. d. Smith - \+ then to Scribner's \+ thanked Mr. Smith. While there met Florence Grey \+ had a very pleasant visit with her - They - her mother \+ Leila Martin - are living at Princeton \+ she gave me an urgent invitation to visit there - Then back to studio \+ packed up my printing things \+ took \+ home to lunch. to N.A of D. immediately after \+ had a long session with the etching class - bit up \+ printing - Connolly has done the best work then Atkinson, Popkieu \+ Stern - there is not much to hope from the others - Home at 6 - very tired - a few minutes rest before dinner - Walter Throop, a cousin of the girls, dined with us - leaving soon after dinner. I went to Camera at 8 to meet the other members of the Finance Com. of the N.A of D. - de Jondet - Watrous \+ Gifford. We discussed the matter of investing funds on Central Trust Co. in certain R.R. Bonds - - agreed to buy $25000 specified bonds \+ meet again next Sat. night - Home soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 16, 1902

**Weather:** Raw \+ disagreeable  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw \+ disagreeable - Rain falling after 3. p.m. I had my bkf. after 10 - Read newspapers \+ about noon went to studio \+ changed Morton p.p. in blotters in press - Then to Charls' \+ wound his clocks as requested - Then a short walk on Park Ave. Dinner at 2 - after that wrote a letter to Rev. P.C. Williams concerning Ralph \+ his temperamental disposition to criticise \+ sit in judgment generally - Had a long nap before supper at 6.30 - Lent some reading \+ writing.
