## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 9, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy clouds \+ high southerly winds that at times blew a gale \- Rain came early in the afternoon \+ fell in torrents \+ almost continuously \- the wind dying out. I spent some time with Moody on the laying of stone bounding path from house to Annex. Trumbull came before noon to say that he wd. be ready to come back to work in two or three days \- then I went to hotel \- doors had arr. on stage from Prescott \+ Co \- I saw Wm. Etkin \+ Katie \- Home to dinner at 1. After it worked for some time on drawings for caps \+ casings for piazza posts \- then gave up to headache \+ stomach discomfort. About 6 o'clk \- dressed in Rubber suit \+ went to hotel with Telegram to Miss Lanes friends at Tupper Lake \+ Telephoned to Ford. Beede that owing to the storm Miss L. wd. not go to Racid in the evt. It was a wild night \- The rain was falling heavily \+ the water rushing down the roads \+ paths furiously \- Back in time for supper \- spent most of evt. in studio, warm \+ dry big big stone, reading news papers.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 10, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy day \- but fine \- cloudless \+ cold moonlight night. After bkf. got studio in order \+ laid out Borden work to make a beginning on view in library looking from N. end of room to south windows. After 11 went to hotel for mail, then to Scott Brown's \- met Mary, Kate \+ Charl at Reserve entrance \+ we went over the road that led us over shoulder \+ Moon Mark to back of Obenkynis \- At O's, made a brief call \- home in time for dinner \- Afternoon I worked on Borden \- an outline on 12×18 millboard \- At 4 I went off with Ralph \- him used up by a cold \- we went to top of High Rock by Iron bridge \+ got excellent view of the River \- the Lark in its hill \+ Moon Mark behind, new to me. Then we walked down the valley some distance \+ home before 6. Evening Bessie, Miss Lanes \+ I, on invitation of Mr. Felix Adler went to his house \- to hear Mr. Percival Chubb read from Tennyson's In Memoriam \+ discourse thereon. It was a family gathering of Adlers, Goldmarks \+ Obenkynis \- Miss Stewart \+ Miss Miller \- being with Mr. \+ Mrs Weston who might be called almost (the Adler family) the only outsiders there \- Mr. Chubb is an enthusiast \+ was rather prolix \- but I was sufficiently interested not to suffer much from that \- He discoursed about the claims of the work to rank with efforts of Milton, Goethe \+ Dante \- outlined the development of Tennyson's argument of immortality \- Reading illustrative passages \- The reading was introduced by Lawrence Adler playing on the piano, with much cleverness
