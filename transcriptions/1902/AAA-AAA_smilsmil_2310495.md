## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 5, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a cold night a brilliant, cloudless day. I had a fairly restful night \+ got up with a clear head. The drugs I took last night gave me a pretty thorough clearing out. I kept quiet all day feeling that I had not yet quite recovered from the effects of my two-day headache spree. In the afternoon I loafed with the others on the south slope of our hill \+ on Windy brow getting a good sunning. I made a big fire in the m.f. in studio \+ at 11 went to hotel \+ got mail \+ telephoned to D. Laught to come up to see Kate Leonard who has an attack of lumbago. Then to Mr. Scott Brown's to enquire about supplemental help if needed. While there met Rev. M. Taylor \+ wife — I knew him many years ago in Montrose. Home to dinner — Miss Daue \+ Charl dined at Magher's — Otis brought a load of earth \+ a load of gravel. Moody spent the P.M. with him getting the gravel.

Evey Mr. Baudine called — Miss Daue was very amusing — I like her very much — she is entirely unaffected, sensible \+ has a keen enjoyment of fun.

Jim tramped over the Gothic trail alone making a day of it — getting back about 5 p.m.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 6, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Went up Hopkins —

Another clear day with very strong southerly wind \+ growing hazy as the day advanced. After bkfs. Jim went with Hatcher \+ Willie to lower Ausable \+ spent the day there. At 10.20 Ralph \+ I started for the top of Hopkins. We took it slowly. The walk is a long one \+ the grades are generally comparatively easy — but the woods are not fine except in a very few spots — there is much second-growth — also wind-slashes. Resulting in general untidiness. At 1 o'clk. we got to a sheltered place where we found sunshine, water \+ logs convenient as seats — Here we stopped for half an hour \+ had our lunch. We got to the top at 2.15. It was something of a surprise to me as I thought that there was yet quite a mile ahead of us — we came out upon a ledge of rock which proved to be the summit \+ the tripod was but a short distance ahead of us — the view to the south west is very fine but the gale blowing from that direction made it difficult for us to look in that direction. We found a cleft in a rock where, somewhat sheltered we rested for 5 min. — Then, at 2.30 we started down, following the trail to near the foot — briar patch where we came upon an open patch where we got some very fine blk. berries — home at 5. I had a rub down \+ rested for an hour before a substantial supper \+ felt little fatigue after my unusual exertion. Wey with the family \+ to bed about 10.
