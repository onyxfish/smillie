## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 27, 1902

*The seventh anniversary of Annie's death.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A damp, unpleasant day, weather improving at night — some writing kept me at home till after 10 — then to Clausen's \+ ordered passe-partout for Rf. for Borden — then to studio — Jates Stern came — about 1m after 12 a fearful explosion shook the bldg. Stern \+ I went out to find place \+ cause — followed crowd up Third Ave. to 39 St. \+ then west at Lex. Ave — we saw the first of the shattered windows — at Park Ave. most of the glass in the windows seemed to be blown out \+ when we reached the cor. \+ could look up the ave. to Grand Central Station the ruin of windows seemed complete — everything in The Murray Hill Hotel, Grand Central \+ Mt. Union Manhattan Eye \+ Ear Hospital in the way of glass was wrecked — window shades, curtains and broken sashes hung from every opening — The great glass clock dials on the Gd. Central were blown in \+ glass wreckage covered the sidewalks — It was impossible to get information but it was evident that there must have been an accidental explosion of a large amt. of dynamite somewhere about 41st \+ Park Ave — Home about 12.30 \+ found that one of Mary's windows \+ one of the laundry windows was out of course there was great excitement in the house. Lunch soon after 1 — to studio at 2.30 — putting things in palace till 4.30 — Walked up to 42d St. immense crowds — home \+ had a rest before dinner — Dinner at 6.30 — Charl. came in to see how many of our windows were broken — lost Geo. \+ Molly came —
