## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 4, 1902

**Weather:** Overcast & rather raw  **Temp:**

At 9 o'clk. went to J.P. Morgan's - servant told me he had not yet come down to his bkfst — sometimes did not come down till 10 — so I went to studio — back at 9.30 — servant told me he came down in great rush just after I left & had already gone. — I went to studio & busied myself there till 11 — then I went to [illegible] & got reduction of Borden's outline — then walked up Broadway to 34th S. & called on Maurice Sterne about etching class. home at 12.30 — lunch — to studio at 2 — worked on Morgan head as long as I could see — till 4.30 — then got out paper for proofing & wet it down for printing to-morrow. Walked awhile on Park Ave. before dinner — [illegible] wrote part of my letters to the boys — M. Mo[illegible] Fagnani & Miss Bessie Lane are invited to dine here to-morrow eve't. As I could not write them I am taking time by the fore-lock.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 5, 1902

**Weather:** Snow, hail & sleet  **Temp:**

Rec'd. Mr. Fagnani & wife dined with us.
Calles on J. Pierpont Morgan.

Snow, hail & sleet — beginning sometime during the night & continuing till about 11 a.m. — then the clouds broke — there was sunshine late in the afternoon followed by frosty moonlight — It was very cold & early in the day the wind was a gale — the storm was short, wide spread & des-tructive. At 9 o'clk I went to M. J.P. Morgan's & sat for half an hour reading a book that I took with me — About 9.30 he having finished his bkfst. was invited into the library — I was rec'd. with ceremony — I showed him pf. of my etching — he at once called his daughter Anita who was later joined by Mr. M — saying they knew more about such things than he did — my etching was criticised as a portrait & objections were made to the eyes — the mouth & the chin — other photos were brought out — I was given a number of very vague suggestions — to studio soon after 10 — M. Sterne came with a lot of sketches made by applicants for entrance to N.A.D. etching class — a poor lot — we selected eleven for admission — I did a little work on Morgan plate before I went home to lunch — back before 2.30 & did some more work on plate — home before 5 — after wetting down papers to take up to-morrow — Rested till after 6 — then dressed — Dinner at 7 — Rev. Mr Fagnani & his wife came — Bessie Lane was invited but sent regret — sick — Mrs F. was in very fluent humor. M. F. everyone it quietly. there would have been a torrent of talk if Bessie Lane had been present — M. F. brought his camera & took a snap flashlight group.
