## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 23, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dubiously overcast m& passing gradually into a bright, sunny day with wind a trifle raw my influenza seems to be no worse for my outing last night. To studio before bkfp. \+ prepared things there for Mr. Kane who is to begin the house cleaning to-day. After bkfp. I wrote a note to W. T. C. Williams informing of Mr. Wheelwright's safe passage of Com. re Admiss. of last east. Then to studio. Some times there with Mr. Kane \+ Tho. Roland. Then home \+ wrote to E. C. Stedman \+ to Scott Brown. I had my lunch with Bessie. Mary \+ Katharine try out. More note writing after lunch. Then to studio \+ after a time went to Brooks Bro. Then down to Union Sq. \+ to Keppel's where spent some time looking at Abbey's "Deserted Village" drawings \+ some prints — Home at 6 — Dinner — Relf with Baker till near 9.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 24, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \+ raw. I spent most of the m& at Studio. Mr. Kane finished cleaning there but there was lots for me to do in arranging things. Home to lunch at 1 — After that I went to Brooks Bro. \+ arranged about the cleaning \+ repairs of my overcoat. Then to studio \+ most of what remained of the afternoon was given to writing letters to Jim \+ Raekh. Home to dinner — Relf. Chase came in. [illegible — entire passage]
