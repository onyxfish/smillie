## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 14, 1902

**Weather:** fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine day - Trumbull did not come - informed Dennis that Moody was to supersede him on the 23d - to which he offered no objection. I made new frame \+ stretched \+ Kalsomined more muslin for ceiling of my bed room - At 11 went to Hotel, got mail \+ engaged horse to drive down the valley this p. m. Rec'd notice from Wilson that he had deposited $125 to my C. with Second Nat'l Bank. Dinner at 1 - at 3 went to hotel - got horse, poor enough, \+ a very shabby old top buggy - \+ with Ralph, drove first to Mr. Notman's where I had a pleasant call - Then to D. Merle Smith - a very agreeable call on him \+ Mrs. Smith met Rev. M. Irons there - Saw Trumbull at work - upbraided him - Then to W. Allen Marquand's no one at home - left card - Then to D. Laight's saw Mrs. L - Then home at about 6 - tea - \+ Wea. in my own Room, napping \+ reading news papers.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 15, 1902

**Weather:** clouded m'g  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded m'g., heavy showers before noon. cold, n.w. wind - clearing afternoon - cold brilliant moonlight night - I was hardly able to get up in the m'g because of a headache that was more or less disabling all day. Trumbull came in the m'g, the larger part of the day spent putting the irons upon the corners of north face of side over Piagga. This part of the house was not "framed" according to drawings \+ instructions I sent to Branch, the builder - I have always considered it structurally weak. saw planning further strengthening - After 1 - got this with this he worked in my Room putting up a part of cornice \+ I put up two small pieces of muslin on the ceiling - In the afternoon we were surprised by a call from Katchen Cook who has been camping with Ida Ogilvie \+ outh fus and now with the Goldmarks - we invited her to visit us about Sep. 1st \+ she accepted - Every \+ I hung about the fine - The card, hard moonlight having no attractions for me now. Catharine Hardenburg \+ one of the Russ boys came in about 9 o'clk.
