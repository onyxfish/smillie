## Left Page

### Wed. May 14, 1902

*Kate went to Po-keepsie*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day but I got up feeling somewhat out of sorts after my last nights experiences. It was late when I got to studio + I did little else than clean a casserole belonging to N.A., itching Dep't. which I had used there in making up a lot of etching ground. Had an early lunch at home — Kate left for Po'keepsie for the season. I got to N.A. + D. before 2 — Annual Meeting — Harmonious — nothing of especial interest except perhaps that 5 new academicians were elected — filling the limit of 100 — The last name on the list was Cecilia Beaux. (printed in the Trib., Beaux) Meeting adj'd at 5.30 I went out + had a delightful walk on Riverside Drive + in the Park — back before dinner at 7 — I was placed on the right of J.G. Brown, v. pres't. who was on the right of Pres't. Dielman — one of alive of veterans — Tait was on my right + then Bristol — all calling for soft bread because they could n't chew the crusty rolls — The dinner was good + followed by the usual speeches — Miller was even more frothy than usual + seemed to release himself greatly — he amused the others. Backwith did well in an earnest address + Will Low took considerable time — I slipped out early + got home at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 15, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still remarkably fine, cool weather. I got up early + immediately after b'kf't. went to K. + M., the printers, + got 4 india p'ts. from Morgan plate. The result is not encouraging. There is something about the eyes that is wrong — On my way up town stopped at 2d Nat'l Bank + deposited Steel dev't. check — at studio. Tacked down p'ts., then home to lunch. After that I went to Greenwood. The trees + grass + flowers were very beautiful. I found the new inclosure of granite posts + bronze rail satisfactory — also the eight headstones — Mackellar, who did the work joined me at the lot. On my way out I wandered through the old parts of the grounds, by Sylvan water, Port's mound + grave of the Indian girl Do-hum-me, + was surprised to find that many of the old trees were gone — It is many years since I was in that part of the cemetery — Home in time for dinner at 6.30 — easy. Went to Charl's + got check for $250 — part pay m't. for Greenwood work by Mackellar. Home at 9.30 — Carson came + in addition to the rubbing gave one a little "active movement."
