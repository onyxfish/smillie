## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 12, 1902

**Weather:** Damp m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Damp m'f. \+ rain threatened during most of the day — mild moonlight night. I got to studio about 10 and wrote a note to Mr. Stedman announcing my desire to throw aside the Morgan plate \+, if he will give me a little time for a respite I will etch a new plate giving the head only, the size of the original photo. Then I cleaned house until noon — then home — lunch at 1 — At 2 I was at Mr. Borden's — saw him — worked on outline of library, looking south till about 4 — Then sauntered slowly home feeling very low in both mind \+ body — Rested for an hour before dinner — after dinner wrote to both Jim \+ Ralph — short notes to Mr. Barhydt \+ to Mr. Otis — I went to Freese's drug store \+ then to studio \+ home at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 13, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The clerk of the weather made a poor effort to smile but it might almost as well have been tears. To studio about 10 got express man to take Tribune file from the studio to house. I worked all the m'f on Borden outline — home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — \+ worked till 4, finding it very hard work to see — then went looking for gas logs \+ andirons — went first to a store on Bdway \+ 27th \+ 8th Sto — then to Wanamaker's — then home — dinner — Eve \+ f. Charl came in — I wrote to Mrs. Kent \+ sent check to Edwin H. Lee for Maurice that I got from St. Hubert's stables at Keene Heights.
