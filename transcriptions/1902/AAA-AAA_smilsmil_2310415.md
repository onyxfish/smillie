## Left Page

### Thur. March 27, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another fine day. To studio before 10 + worked all day on portrait of father (second) Painted in monotone. About 12.30 went to No. [illegible] Gallery (late Avery) + saw Bolton Jones pictures. Then to Durand-Ruel's. Then to lunch. To studio at 2.30. Sieleck took Morton photo; framed, back to the [illegible] Gov. I worked on portrait till after 5 — then to Buckingham hotel + saw Mr. Kent + her two daughters. Home to dinner at 6.40. Eve'g at 8.30 went to Edw. Coale's to talk to him on business — Mr. Kent + her daughters there — Home at 10.

Jim has been out of sorts all day — Temp. 102 1/5° at 1 o'clk — 102° at 10 P.m. I went to Dr. Celley's — he came at 4 P.m. + prescribed castor oil.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 28, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came from Hackley Hall.

Dull w'g. Rain commenced after noon + fell freely at night — with high wind. To studio before 10 + painted face of father's portrait — Home to lunch + back at 2.30 — finished the painting + then had a rest. Then home. Ralph came down from Tarrytown on an afternoon train — found that Jim's fever had increased + after dinner, feeling somewhat worried, I went to Dr. Celley's — he had just gone to hospital — saw Mrs. C— arranged to report early in the w'g. Eve'g at home — at 10 o'clk — Jim's temp. was 102 3/5°.
