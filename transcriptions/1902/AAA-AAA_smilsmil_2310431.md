## Left Page

### Mon. April 28, 1902

**Weather:** Charming day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

a very uncomfortable day for me - with heavy headache - influenza that made fountains of my eyes & nose + a severe intercostal neuralgia on my left side
I went to studio for a few minutes at 10 - then to Miller's & to Douglas's - + made some preliminary arrangements for changes to be made in attic - then to studio - + at 11.30 went to Scribner's with K.+V.'s bill for framing p\[illegible\] (15) of Borden place - then to Calverly's - then home to lunch - Gave my head a rest until after 3 - then to K.+V. for p\[illegible\] from students work - not ready - H\[illegible\] was printing some of them - then to Westbury - + Rode up to Abingdon Sq. - went to     Hudson S\[illegible\] + saw a 60 gal. oil tank - satisfactory for Keene use - Then walked all about the old, familiar 12\[illegible\] S\[illegible\] Region + up 5 Ave. + home at 6.30 after going to drug store + taking a Castor oil cocktail — no dinner — Quiet, more strolling in the streets + reading.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 29, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day very disagreeable in spots - cloudy in \[illegible\] a smart shower about the middle of the day - again at about 5, continuing into the night. My cocktail reported at 10.30 last night - again at 12 - 2 A.m. 4 + 7 - again at noon + at 7 p.m. with great quantities of catarrhal mucus - I got up + breakfasted at 9.30 - waited for Locke till 10.30 - he did not come - Then to studio - Note from D.L.+W. R.R. stating that my missing standing press awaited my order - went to Jackson's Eq\[illegible\]. - with it - washed printing caps + sorted paper, etching p\[illegible\] - etc - To lunch at 1 - Young Dock came at 2 + I arranged with him about curtains to hang bet. dining room + library - at 3 went to Barnard Club. Carnegie Bell. 56\[illegible\]\[illegible\]: + heard Jefferson's talk about the stage - actors + acting - It was informal + very entertaining - he is evidently an old man but he is bright + witty - after his talk he asked for questions + a number were handed to him written on slips of paper - he showed amusing adroitness in his answers + captivated the women who formed about 19/20 of his audience - I met Emma Ireland there - Then I went to the fake 46\[illegible\] by Students League - 57\[illegible\] S\[illegible\] - a very noisy, social affair far below the level of others of past years - then to exhibit of Socy. of Am. Artists - light was very poor - Rain commenced as I went out — Thee to Dr. Cilley's + told him that I must have treatment of some kind - I could not continue taking drugs to induce the bowels to move - He said he would arrange for a consultation to-morrow at 3. Home in heavy rain - dinner at 6.30 — very much troubled with eyes - could not read.
