## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 15, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate Ret. to Po'k.

Very fine day \- spent part of melf. getting things put away \- then went to see Mrs. H. Draper in 36 S. about Thos. Reynolds who has applied for place as furnace man etc \- She spoke well of him \- then to studio \- had a talk with Maynard about giving up studio \- he is completely discouraged \- then to coalyard of Daires. foot of E. 37. St \- saw D \- he is optimistic \& think strike trouble will be over soon \- then to Scribner's \- Mr. Smith not in \- then to Second Nat. Bk \& home \- lunch at 1 \- after that went to Miss Bradley's \- saw her \- She spoke well of Thos. Reynolds \- Kate left for Po'k on 3.30 train \- until near 5. fixing things in drawers \& closets \- then to studio \- to Ryan's \& to Van Palin's hardware store in 42. S. \- home to dinner at 6.30 \- Eve. Reading \- Chas. came \& took the girls to his house to spend the eve.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 16, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day \- Step. at 8.30 \& after that was engaged in my room \& about the house till near noon \- then I went to Thos. Roland's house \- 306 E. 34. S. \& left a note engaging him to work for me, caring for furnace etc. Dinner at 1 \- after that trying to arrange my desk \& papers till about 4 o'clk. Then to studio \- to Ryan's \& to Scribner's where I had a pleasant half hour with Mr. H. Smith \- then to Schlemming's bindery in W. 23. St. \& saw my bound files of Tribunes \& other papers through the Civil war \- 1860\-5 \- then home \- tea at 6.30 \- Eve. at 8.30 went to Edw. Cook's \- Charles Calkins came in a little later \& we had a pleasant eve. \- till near 11 o'clk \- then home \& to bed.
