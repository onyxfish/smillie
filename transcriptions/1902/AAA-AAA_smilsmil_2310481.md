## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 8, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Steady rain thro' most of the night \+ till about midday \- showers the evef. \- then clearing \- Trumbull did not come \- I worked hard all day \- finished putting up piece of sized muslin \- my bed room ceiling \- Stretched another piece \+ sized it \- \+ stained mouldings for alcove \+ my bedroom \- About 6 o'clk. went to Harry Beede's to hurry him about some iron work he is to do for me \- then home to supper. Evef. \- Mr. Maghee came in \- Bessie played on the piano \- after m\- left I read Paolo \+ Francesca by [illegible]

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 9, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day \- but I was somewhat out of sorts \+ unable to make best use of it. Trumbull did not come \+ that disarranged plans \- I tried to do some work on my ceiling but the abdominal strain was too severe upon the weaker parts \- By mf. mail I got letter from E. C. Stedman \- slowly recovering from a severe illness \+ much out of humor over the Stock Exchange Hist. Co. business \- Wilson has decieved him \+ the berated me for writing in my letter to him that if he could do nothing to expedite payment to me then I would have to take measures that would \- I wrote at once what I hope may prove to be a conciliatory letter \- Jim \+ Raelph went up Northmark \+ got back to dinner \- At 2.30 I rode out to the Gill brook Reservoir with M. J.H. Maghee where we left the wagon \+ walked up to the lumberman's camp to investigate a matter of sanitation \- when I was there some days ago I was satisfied than sewage from that camp ran into Gill brook above the Reservoir \- Today it was proved that I was correct \- Maghee \+ I walked all the way back stopping at the new dam now almost completed on the River about half [illegible] from entrance to Reserve \- there we met Mr. Moffat \+ told him of the result of our investigation \- at entrance met Scott Brown \+ he promised to cut off Gill brook water at the reservoir \+ depend upon Mackenzie brook entirely for supply \- Then home soon after 5 \+ rested before supper \- evef. some reading.
