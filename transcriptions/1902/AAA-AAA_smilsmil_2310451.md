## Left Page

### Sat. June 7, 1902

**Weather:** muggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

A muggy day with two or three showers. Blcfs' at 9 — got letter from Mary — the party got to the Ark in very good shape + were having delightful Oct. weather. I got to house about 10 — soon went to studio + arranged with Ryan to repair kitchen range then worked at packing away + clearing up in studio till near one — then home for an hour — lunched with Charl at 2 — had trouble with my watch + went to Bayerdorfer's with it — then to studio — Taking down, shaking, dusting + folding curtains &c then to Bayerdorfer's at 5.30 + got my watch then home — This m/f I called up Wilsm of Stock Exchange Hist. Socy He said Stedman was expected on Monday — made appointment for Wed. next — says he has a check for me. To Charl's to dinner at 7.30 — later he went to the Theatre + I went to Century — monthly meeting — very few there — Nicoll gave me a note of introduction to Asa D. Gallup, Manager of Lake Placid Club — as I have some thought of going to Keene Heights by that way — back to No. 29 early.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 8, 1902

**Weather:** muggy, close m/f  **Temp:** getting up to 85°

Just before noon a sudden change — a smart shower + quick clearing with wind which by 3 o'clk. blew at the rate of 70 miles an hour — Ther. falling very rapidly — getting down to 49° at night. I got up late as per agreement with Charl. and breakfasted with him at 10 — It was not until after the shower that I went to 110 then wrote till after1 — lunch with C. at 1.30 — again to house + had a nap, then wrote to Mary — to Ralph — to f Beede + to Station Master at Westport about pianos — then a short walk + to Charl's to dinner at 6.30 — Quet reading till Carlson came at 9.30 — then the usual work with him till near 11.
