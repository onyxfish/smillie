### Sat. Aug. 2, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy Rain last night \+ again this m'f. \- a succession of showers thro' the day \- a little sunshine \+ brilliant starlight night. \- I accomplished little during the day \- wrote to Elly. Howe \+ some other notes \- fussed awhile with photo. Printing \+ got another sized strip of muslin up on my bedroom ceiling. The weather was such that I had no chance to walk. Trumbuee \+ Adams did not come \- Otis hauled more stuff on to my road. Eve'f. \- I had another pull at Silas Marner. [illegible — ~8 words]

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 3, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant m'f \+ a lovely day till late in the afternoon when a shower passing to the south of us gave a little spatter \- Bkf's at 9.30 \- the rest of the m'f was spent writing notes \+ printing \+ developing some photo. portraits of self. Among others I wrote to E. C. Stedman to see if he can hurry up payments for Morton \+ Morgan etchings \- dinner at 1.30 \- At 3.30 Jim, Ralph \+ I started for a walk \- we went in back of Scott Brown's \- crossed the river \+ by an old wood road went to J. H. Waghee's upper clearing \- saw deer tracks on the road \+ a red fox in the clearing \- we went into the woods at the N.W. end of clearing \+ tried to find an old wood road, of which we had been told, by which we could come out in the valley half a mile or more north of the Beedes. We did not find it \+ came down by a stream in parts very rough \- very wet \+ very thick with undergrowth, at last, when near the ends, getting upon the road we had been looking for. Home before 6 \- had a rub down \+ change \- then fell asleep on my bed \+ had supper after the others. Eve'f. finished reading Silas Marner \- in some ways it disappoints me. As a story it is sad
