## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 9, 1902

**Weather:** Clear, Cold + Windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

did not get down to hlp. till after 10. wrote a note to Mary Cook + after 11 left it at her door — then to studio + opened windows for airing — then had a short walk on sunny side of Park Ave — then to Charles + home at 1. wrote draft of note to Curtis + Cameron. Dinner at 2 — After 3 went to Mary Cook's + had an hours quiet talk with her concerning some family affairs. Another short walk on Park Ave + then home + had a nap till near 7 — then supper — Evening much broken up. Sheldon came in bet. 9 + 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 10, 1902

Lizzie + Jessie Smillie dined with us.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine winter day. clear. pleasantly cold + moderately still. To studio at 10. Ther. 55°. I had to battle with the heater in the cellar some time before the studio was reasonably warm. To work at the biting of Morton + Morgan — used Dutch mordant — found that many lines on the Morgan plate, drawn with dashing needle failed to bite — then I used nitric + water + that took hold all over — home to lunch. Back at 2.30. Finished Morgan with nitric \+ cleaned plate — Dutch mordant on Morton but did not finish him — At 5 went to Mr. Blashfield's reception — 48 Central Pk. So. great crowd — I did not know her — she did not know me — I looked thro' the rooms — saw no one I knew — then left — went to Plaza Hotel to see Mr. Beach — not in — then home — Lizzie + Jessie Smillie dined + spent the eve'g with us —
