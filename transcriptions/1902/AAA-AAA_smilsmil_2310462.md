## Left Page

### Tues. July 1, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day after a night of heavy rain. After bkf. Ralph \+ I went to ferd. Beede's \+ got a horse \+ buckboard \+ rode down the valley to D. Chas. Daight's — to have him look at the little finger on my right hand. Some foreign substance or infection has got in at the root of the nail \+ since last friday there has been swelling, pain, \+ a little superation. I have poulticed it. He advises me to continue it. I had a pleasant call with him \+ a very pleasant ride getting home after 12. Dinner at 1 — About 3 R. \+ I went to P.O., To Scott Brown's — called on Mr. Robinson at his cottage \+ then we walked to the top of Roaring Brook falls. Home at about 5.30 — tea at 6.30. Eve'g, M. \+ Mrs. Maghee came in for a little while. Got a letter from Jim, written Wednesday — at Alice Ireland's. Kate probably started from Po'k this m'g. If so, is at E. town to-night — Telephone out of order \+ could not communicate with her.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 2, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate arrived —

The first warm day since we came here. No fire on the hearth \+ doors \+ windows all open — altogether a very lovely day — but toward eve'g — indications in the sky foreboded a change in the weather again. After bkf. — I went down to ferd. Beede's \+ was fortunate in meeting D. Daight — I showed my finger to him — he was non-committal \+ said dress it with vasaline for a day or two. Then to Harry Beedes \+ home at 10.30 — Kate came at 11.30 from E. Town where she stayed last night. Dinner at 1 — To hotel with letters before 3. M J. H. Maghee introduced me to D. Maghee, his wife (no relation to J.H.) \+ I sat on the Piazza for an hour or more \+ chatted with them. Then home — my finger troubling me a good deal. I dressed it with an ointment Kate gave me — Antiseptic sistine. I could do little but dodge about. Eve'g, with newspaper for an hour or so \+ then to bed.
