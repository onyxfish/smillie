## Left Page

### Tues. March 11, 1902

**Weather:** mild & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild & pleasant spring day. Got up early \+ was able to get to studio soon after 9 - got plates \+ went to Plimmel & Waight's - Henry took p\[h\]p\[hoto\]s for me from Morton plate \+ from my etching of father at Poughkeepsie, 1842. I am not satisfied with the Morton p\[hoto\]. Think he has pressed them too roughly - To studio before 12 - got p\[hoto\]s between blotters \+ under light pressure. Had a sun walk on Park Ave. \+ home at 1.5 to lunch. To studio again at 2.30 - had a job to get photo. of Morton into frame \+ pasted up ready to return to N. Mu. At 4.30 went to Tomelle to get photo reduction of portrait sketch of father - Then to Wanamaker's \+ ordered hickory table for Keene Piazza - Then to Riker's - 25th St. \+ 6th Ave. \+ home at 6.20 well tired - dinner at 6.30 - Eve\[ning\] after reading my news paper, at 8.30 went to Fred Ireland's to bid Mollie Adams good bye - she is to sail for Genoa to-morrow - met Edw\[ard\]. \+ Mary Cook there.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 12, 1902

**Weather:** pleasant & mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant & mild. Spent the m\[orning\] at studio working on Morton plate - \+ the afternoon in scraping out the face on J. P. Morgan plate - dismal work - Eve\[ning\] went to regular meeting of N. A. f\[u\]nd - at 109\. S. \+ Amsterdam Ave. This morning at 9.30 I met Lockwood de Forest \+ W. Manuel at Safe Deposit - 2nd Nat\[iona\]l. where we went over the securities of the N. A. f\[u\]nd. \+ verified the accountants statement. There was a quorum at the Academy meeting \+ routine business, unimportant, was transacted - over by 10 \+ I got away as soon as possible \+ home at 11.
