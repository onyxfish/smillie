## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 5, 1902

**Weather:** Stormy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Stormy day - S.E. wind - heavy, low flying clouds - much mizzle & some showers - high wind at night. I awoke at 6 o'clk & in my bed wrote a letter to S.C.f. Hammond - I saw in paper a couple of days ago death notice of her sister, Mrs. Warriner. Bkfst. at 9 - packing up things about studio \+ putting away things preparing to flit to town. Dinner at 1.30 - afternoon, much the same kind of work - altho' I have felt at times during the day very uncomfortable in an indescribable sort of way. The discomfort seeming to cover stomach & bowels. I walked on north piazza for some time enjoying storm effects. Supper at 6.30 - Evef reading in studio & down stairs chatting with Mary.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 6, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain last night - more in the n'f. Strong southerly wind - sunshine & rainbows at intervals during the day. Evef wind changed suddenly to the north with roaring gusts. Moonlight \+ brilliant starlight. My stomach & bowel trouble, whatever it is, troubled me much during the day, quite unfitting me for work & depressing me so that I felt that I should get back to town as soon as possible - then Mary told me, for reasons that were satisfactory, that I must choose between Wed night & next Monday - then I took a good dose of Bismuth & began to feel better - About noon went to Harry Beede's & got blind fastening - Dinner at 1 o'clk. After that, went to P.O. & got letters - one from Ralph telling of his good fortune in catching Empire State Exp. at Albany & so getting to N.Y. on schedule time. Then home & worked at getting things in shape to pack - After 5 when I again went to Harry Bede's with blind fastening to have him correct a mistake - an interesting walk up the hill with roaring wind \+ starlight. Tea at 6.30 - Evef Read newspaper for awhile - Magbee came in for awhile.
