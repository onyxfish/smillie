## Left Page

### Sun. April 20, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

went to Tarrytown \-

A very pleasant day \- opening with fog \+ clearing to be cloudless \+ misty with a cool east wind \- Ups at 7 \+ had a good scrub \- bkfp. at 9.30 \+ got away on 10.45 train getting to T-town at 11.45 \+ to Hackley Hall a few minuutes after 12 \- Ralph had gone off a walking \+ did not get back till just before dinner at one \- I sat at Head Masters table on Mr. Williams' right. Ralph was at another table \- After dinner he \+ I went off for a stroll \- we went all over the new buildings \+ prowled about in the woods in the vicinity \- back to Hackley at 4.15 Service in Chapel at 4.30 \- Mr. Williams bro. Rev. M. Wright. delivered an address or sermon \- To catch my train I was obliged to leave before his address was finished \- I walked down to station \- met \+ spoke to Dr. Jacobi \- to N.Y. on 5.37 train getting in on time at 6.25 \- Tea \- Eve'g \- some newspaper \+ wrote a note to Dielwan about Abbot.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 21, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm day \- ups at 7 after a very unrestful night. Physically I feel very much discouraged \- I am so full of discomforts \+ disabilities \+ am steadily growing weaker \- To studio at 9.30 \- Trouble with fire \- Ther. at 64° \- so, I went to Kimmel \+ Voigt with about 40 lbs of plates, etched by students of N.A. while there learned that there is to be an auction sale of etched \+ eng? \- plates by order of executor of the late Wm. Pate \- at Silo's \- 43 Liberty St. \+ that in the cat. is my plate of Goldsmith's Daughter \- I went to Silo's to see about it \- Saw Mr. Keeler \+ he referred me to Mr. Cor. C. Pate \- of Robb \+ Pate, 100 Wm St., the executor \- I went there \+ had a pleasant interview with Mr. Pate \- he did not know anything about the plate \- it was no doubt a blunder \- according to his schedule of assets \- he has 19 p?. of Goldsmiths D\- \+ wd. like to sell them to me \- but I declined to buy \- then he offered to exchange for an oil sketch \+ I said I would think of it \- he took me up stairs to Drug Club to show me a lot of paintings there \- a poor lot to my mind \-. Then, at noon, I Reed to K\+V \- got Borden pp. \+ Plate Library No. 1 \+ went to studio \- Home to lunch at 1 \- after that, I gave up to my feelings \+ felt ill after 3 \- then to studio for a little while \- at 4 went to Wanamaker's \+ bought a screen to be put at the head of Ralph's bed at Hackley Hall \- then to Mitchell Vance Co. \- to get glass globe for Hall light \- to studio at 5.30 \- Maynard gave me $50 on acct. of studio Rent \- Signed the India p?p. of Borden Plate \- Library No. 1 \- \+ home to dinner \- Eve'g wrote to Ralph \+ after 9 went to Charles' \+ met a Mr. Dice there \- a Chinese sick merchant \- Home at 10.30.
