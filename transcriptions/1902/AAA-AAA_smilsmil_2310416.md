## Left Page

### Sat. March 29, 1902

**Weather:** Nasty, rainy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nasty, rainy m'f. Up early + went to D. Cilley's at 8 o'clk. + reported that Jim's temp. was still above 100°. He came to the house at about 9 + after looking Jim over very carefully said he found no evidence of anything more serious than a bronchial attack. There was enough of that to acct. for the fever. It was 10.30 before I got to studio — had intended going to Gov. Morton's with pl. of etching for his approval. I spent an hour or two touching in places on portrait of father — home at 12.30 + had a half hour rest after before lunch — After I went to Na. f P. Etching Class — Sunshine — then heavy shower with thunder — I worked very hard taking pf. for the boys till 5 o'clk. then walked across Central Park — a lovely early spring afternoon after the shower — Home at 6.30 — dinner — Eve'g at home — Jim still in bed + very impatient — Temp. a night 101 3/5°.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 30, 1902

**Weather:** Sunny + pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunny + pleasant until late p.m. — thickened with a little rain — starlight at 10 — D. Cilley came at 9 am. — said Jim was so much better that he might get up + sun himself on Park Ave. I had bkf't. before 10. + after "Ralph + I went to studio + cleaned the windows — then to Chas's + wound his clocks. then to Central Park + saw the flower show in the green houses — home at 2 — dinner — I had a long nap — tea at 6.30 — S'r'l Tom Cook came into see Jim — I went to the Buckingham at 8 to see the Kees's — they were out — then to Century — Saw Maynard + Turner about Na. f P. Etching Class competition — also Gifford — Home before 10 — wrote to E. C. Stedman.
