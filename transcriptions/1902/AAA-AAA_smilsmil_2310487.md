## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 20, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day \- at times rather close \+ almost uncomfortably warm \- a heavy later in the day \- S. E. wind \+ threats of storm \- Trumbull engaged getting out long strikes. Dennis act\- to his work \- or rather, to his loafing. After 11 I went to St. Hubert's \+ had the barber cut my hair \- then home to dinner \- Afternoon drew plans for mantel piece in my bed room. Evett. Ralph \+ I went to St. Hubert's \+ heard a little Jap. with limited supply of English, give brief description of a series of colored photo. slides of Jap. scenery \+ costumes. He has been a teacher of something at Cornell \+ Princeton \+ is to return to the Scientific School \- University at Tokio \- to teach there. Home about 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 21, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy showers commencing before 8 a.m. with thunder \+ lightning \- so dark until after 9 that Trumbull \+ Adams had difficulty in seeing to work. \- clearing slowly during the day with some fine atmospheric effects. Adams came to my room before I was out of bed. he came to kalsomine my bed room ceiling \- I followed him \+ Trumbull closely all day \- I mixed the colors for A \- Besides coloring the ceiling of my bed room he went over most of the painted ceiling of main room, much to advantage the stained portions of wood work. Trumbull worked most of the day a mantel piece in my bed room \- also putting up shelves in attic store room \- Evett. Bessie \+ the boys went to St. Hubert's where some southern School darkies gave a begging exhi[illegible] \- I loafed at home.
