## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 22, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, sunny on't., with beautiful cloud almost from a clear sky + april like. The first shower came about the middle of the m't.. Others followed, the rain at times coming down heavily — several showers in the afternoon, some with thunder — Ev'f., steady, quiet rain for sometime. — Maghee says he can stand it to have it rain on rainy days but to have it rain on fair days is too much for him. Adams came in the m't \+ worked all day, completing Kalsomining \+ main room ceiling — the painting of my chimney-breast in bed Room and staining \+ shellacing strips for my bed-Room ceiling. Trumbull came in the afternoon \+ worked till 6. — Ev'f Mary, Bessie \+ the boys went to a play given by native amateur talent at Eggleston Hall, Keene Valley — Mr. Beede \+ his team took them down — a head ache spoiled my ev'g \+ I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 23, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another April-like day. sunshine \+ showers — heavy \+ frequent enough to keep everything wet — Thundershowers passed north of us in the late afternoon. Trumbull came in the m't. \+ I kept close to him all day as he finished putting up the wood work in my bed room ceiling — the Room now looks quite finished \+ is greatly improved in general effect. I planned \+ made drawings for the doors \+ partition that I intend to have put up separating my basin alcove from my bed room. Dennis Gano left my employ to-day — he is altogether too slow. Before 5 P.m. I walked down the valley about a couple of miles — stopping at Harry Beede's \+ at Delprand Hale's — Home at 6:30 — a short rest — then supper. Ev'g some reading of newspaper \+ loafing.
