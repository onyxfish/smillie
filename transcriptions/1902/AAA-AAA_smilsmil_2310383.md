## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 20, 1902

**Weather:** Bright m'f - overcast late in the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Spent the m'f at home writing letters etc - etc - \+ at noon went to W.P. Allen in W. 47. St. to tell him that my mouth is still so tender that I cannot let him into it \+ I canceled my engagement for Wed. indefinitely - lunch at 1 - \+ to studio at 2.30 - fussed around till 4 - Then a short walk on Park Ave. \+ home \+ had a rest before dinner Eve'f, Mary, Kath. Eliz. \+ I went to the N.Y. Theatre \+ saw Floridora - a foolish, spectacular piece that has been running for more than a year - It has song \+ dance, some legs \+ lots of bare bosoms - Ryley was absurdly grotesque in one part - there was no real acting - most of the singing was bad \+ there was no noticably good dancing - I cannot account for the popularity of such a play - It was after 11 when we got out \+ 12 when I got to bed

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 21, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

K. \+ E. went to Saratoga.

nasty day with storm of driving wind \+ rain at night. To studio at 10 \+ after 11 I went to K. \+ V. - Canal St. \+ got Borden book plate - Then to Scribner's \+ delivered to Mr. Smith my plate - The small book plate \+ the title pages for 2d. Vol. Borden Cat. Then home - The girls, Katharine \+ Bessie, started on the 1 o'clk. train for Saratoga to visit the Maghees. Lunch at 1 - After that, as it was very dark, the weather unpleasant \+ my eyes smarting \+ burning I lay down on my bed with wet cloths on my eyes \+ spent the rest of the afternoon there - dinner at 6.30 Eve'f - eyes very troublesome but I wrote short note to Jine \+ sent him some money.
