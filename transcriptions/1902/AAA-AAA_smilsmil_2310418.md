## Left Page

### Wed. April 2, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*[written above entry: Jim ret? To New Haven.]*

Another cloudy, windy, cold day. To studio about 10 \+ continued work on Morgan Head. About noon went to Connelle's new place 30-32 E. 21st S. \+ left my oil sketch portrait of father to be photo'd. — Then to bank \+ made deposit \+ got my will to take to Clarence Smith — then home to lunch — Again to studio \+ worked till after 5 — then a short walk — then dinner at 8 o'clk — Ralph \+ I escorted Jim to the Grand Central Station where he took train to New-Haven. A messenger came with note from E. C. Stedman. I went to his house — 115 E. 16th S. — before 9 o'clk. Taking ph. of Morton — I think that in some way it was a disappointment to him altho' he said nothing of the kind — I judge so only because of the way he looked at it, saying nothing — Later he said he was pleased with it. I sat \+ chatted with him, his wife \+ Miss Fuller, his Sec'y., till about 9.30 — then home.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 3, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still clouded, windy \+ cold — Immediately after bkfst. I went to W. Borden's. W. 56th S. \+ had a short interview with him — He is not willing that I shall make a view of a corner of his library as I proposed, he wants all of the south end — the part that does not appear in the place already done. This complicates matters as it makes another uninteresting subject with much work \+ little time to do it. I walked down 5th Ave. \+ stopped at the Century — got Stedman's note \+ then to studio —. Breadner, the plumber at Fort Henry, came in to talk about work at Keene. I pointed a pf. from Morgan plate \+ was disheartened to find that I have made another failure — I have not got a Likeness — Home to lunch — back at 2.30 — worked until near 4 — Then went down town to 21 State St. Office of Clarence B. Smith — \+ had an hours talk with him about codicels to my will — \$500 to Greenwood Cemetery for perpetual care of my lot — limitation of interments — \+ a trusteeship for the boys to hold the estate for them after they come of age — giving them the income till they are 25 \+ then for the next five years transferring to them each year 20% of the principal. This is for protection to them \+ a gradual introduction of them to refuse possession \+ management of their property — I then went out \+ had a short walk on the battery — Home to dinner — eve'g with newspapers before the parlor fire.
