## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 10, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'f, but with all signs of coming change — wind S. E. — sky overcast later in day — very light spatters of rain in afternoon. Mary in bed all day — disturbance of stomach \+ bowels. was up vomiting \+ nursing during the night. Bkfs. at 9 — about 11 I felt so heavy \+ tired that I lay down on my bed \+ slept till 12. 30 — Dinner at 1.30 — Wrote part of a letter to Sam. Colman. After 4 o'clk — Ralph \+ I walked out to the new dam in course of building on the River a half mile or more from entrance to reserve. Back home before 6 — I took no supper — on my back until about 8 — Then up \+ read some trashy stories till after 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 11, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Shower about 8 a.m. — Then attempts at clearing — heavy showers about 3 — 4 o'clk p.m. — a fine sunset \+ clear, cool night — I got another large piece of muslin up on my big room ceiling — It stretches my gizzard \+ nearly breaks my neck to do this work \+ has strained my stomach so that it is quite sore. I finished my letter to Colman — Trumbull came in the afternoon \+ we got up, successfully, the panel ceiling in alcove, main room. Eve'g I loafed about with Sat. \+ Sunday newspapers.
