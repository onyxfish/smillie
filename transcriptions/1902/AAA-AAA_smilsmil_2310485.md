## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 16, 1902

**Weather:** Brilliant, cold \+ cloudless m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Before noon there was a shower \+ during the after-noon half a dozen of them — Thunder with an April-like sky \+ April-like temperature. — Trumbull did not come. I spent all day, practically, upon a poster announcing an advertisement for next Tues. eve'f for the benefit of the Keene Heights library. Between 7 \+ 8 o'clk. p.m. I took it to the hotel \+ delivered it to Miss Robinson at whose request I made it. Ret'd home very soon \+ spent rest of eve' with newspapers. Rec'd two interesting letters to-day — one from Sam'l. Colman — the other from Stedman.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 17, 1902

**Weather:** A fine day but windy \+ too cool for Aug.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkf'- soon after 9 — Spent m'f tidying my bedroom \+ studio \+ writing — wrote to E. C. Stedman. After 12 I went to Hotel \+ got Sunday m'f mail — then home — dinner at 1 o'clk. — Soon after 3, with Jim \+ Ralph, I walked the Reserve Road nearly two miles to the River Canon Trail — which we found to be so blind that we soon lost it, but keeping on thro' the woods we came upon the River out a few rods below the little foot bridge — which we crossed \+ Ret'd by trail on west side getting home at 6.15 I rested till near 8 o'clk — in fact, had a good nap — changed clothes — had a Rub down \+ then a light supper. Read for an hour \+ then to bed.
