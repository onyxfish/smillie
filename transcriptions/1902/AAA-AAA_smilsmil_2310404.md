## Left Page

### Mon. March 3, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow \+ Rain in the early m't. but it soon passed \+ was followed by sunshine. Later, clouded over \+ was cold \+ raw at night. I got to studio at 9.30 \+ worked on Morgan plate till 12.30 — then to 2° Nat'l. \+ made another loan — bringing the total up to $3000 — This is very depressing to me and gives me much anxious thought as I try to work out some plan of living within my income — I might do so by giving up my home — taking a small apartment or going to a hotel or boarding house — but I cannot face the thought of giving up the home where Annie lived \+ died \+ where I wish to die — Income from the estate is shrinking \+ I cannot earn enough to make good the deficiency — It seems as though there is nothing for me but to drift helplessly — To studio again at 2.30 \+ worked till after 5. went to Avery's \+ saw Will Low's pictures "In a painter's garden" — very unsatisfactory to me in color \+ execution. His catalogue is, I think, a bit of complacent affectation — The whole affair is very characteristic of the man. Evef spent over my accounts.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 4, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

H. Maghee dined with us.

Pleasant day, but variable. To studio at about 9.30 after going to Miller's \+ to Ryan's \+ paying the bal. on their bills. Worked all the m't on Morgan plate. I am in a mess over the likeness. It is a Sphynx-like face. It is full of meaning \+ bold without any strong markings. So far I have been able to get only a coarse \+ vulgar face while in the large photo there is a pervasive nobility \+ refinement. At 12.30 went out for a saunter — went to Clausen's \+ Ochme's — Home to lunch at 1 — to Studio after 2 — worked on Morgan head taking proofs from time to time — I feel encouraged to hope that I may yet be successful in getting a likeness — about 6 went out \+ walked about the streets till 6.30 — then in to dinner — Mr. Maghee came to dinner \+ later took Katharine \+ Bessie to dinner Theatre Evef I read Century for March.
