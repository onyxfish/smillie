## Left Page

### Mon. March 17, 1902

**Weather:** Dull day with light rain.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkf. I stopped at Dr. Cilley's — not in — then to studio \+ fussed about there awhile — then to Am. Art Ass'n. \+ spent the w'l till noon looking over the Sam. Colman collection of Curios — Jap. & Chinese textiles, potteries, bronzes, carvings \+ prints. met Colman \+ Gifford there — then to studio \+ home at 1 to lunch — At 2 was at W.W.C. Borden's 85 W. 46 St. \+ saw him about proposed Ill. Cat. of his coll. of paintings. He seems to have some vague, vast plans but wishes me to formulate them — It is agreed that I am to call upon him soon \+ submit to him a plan for a cat. of his art works giving size of work, a color & character generally — I went at once to Scribners \+ had an interview with Mr. Smieth — I am to think over the matter \+ see him again on Wed. P.M. Yale came in while I was there and together we walked up Fifth Ave — we went in to Montross' — I was introduced to him \+ we had a very pleasant chat together as we sat and looked at a couple of Tyron's recently finished paintings — I left Yale at 37 S. \+ went to studio \+ from there to Dr. Cilley's — had a 3/4 hour talk with him but got little comfort — I left with him specimens of urine — was advised to eat some rough or bran like food — but got no guess of what the trouble may be that is carrying me steadily down — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g looking over Ill. books with Katharine \+ Bess, reading newspapers etc.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 18, 1902

**Weather:** Bright, cold \+ windy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkf. — wrote some checks to pay bills \+ a note to Clarence Smith — to studio at 10 \+ worked till 12.30 on pen sketch made in W. Borden's library last April showing the end that was back of me when I made the view that I engraved — I spoke of this to Mr. B — when I saw him yesterday \+ he said sharply — why didn't you etch it — it could have been used — \+ Mr. Smith said to me later — it is not too late just yet — so, I am finishing the sketch thinking it may be the basis of a free-hand etching. Had a half hour's walk on Park Ave. before lunch — After lunch wrote a note to Oscar Omland \+ to studio before 2.30 — Maurice Sterne came with a portrait plate just etched \+ for a couple of hours or more worked upon it at my desk — I worked with pen \+ india ink on Borden sketch till 5 — Then I went to Am. Art Ass'n. \+ spent nearly an hour looking over the Jap. prints in Colman's collection — I would like to get one or two as mementos — Home after 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Spent eve. in comfortable seat in front of the fire —
