## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 30, 1902

*Cornelia Adams died at New Haven — this eveg.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An unpleasant day — snow in the air clouds overhead \+ bad walking underfoot. At studio I commenced work on the Morgan \+ Morton heads — got gelatine — tacked down photos \+ traced on the gelatine the Morton portrait — At 12.30 went to 2? Nat'l. Bank — then to Clausens \+ got passe-partout for the Borden India pf. that I am to give him — It was almost 2 o'clk when I got home to lunch \+ 3 when I got to studio — the rest of day light was absent getting the pf- pasted into the passe-partout. It was 5.30 when I went to the reception Mr. Keese \+ the Misses Gavit at 104 E. 22? S'— met there Meta Gavit Adams \+ had a talk of bygone days — Home to dinner at 6.30. Eveg I should have gone to the Reception given to Wm. Coffin at the h.a., but I developed such a headache that, the weather being bad, I could not go away up to 109? S'— Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 31, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An old fashioned snow-storm to judge from appearances — snow commenced early \+ kept it up all day but much did not fall — I got to studio after 10, scraped gelatine tracing of ef. gov. Morton — prepared it for transfer \+ laid ground — home to lunch — back before 3 — got an excellent transfer \+ then varnished the plate \+ made all preliminary preparations to start the etching — Home \+ 5.30 — dinner at 6.30 — Eveg. wrote to Jim \+ to Ralph \+ mailed letters at 10.30 — I sent to them some ill? papers picturing the wreck made by the dynamite explosion last Monday.
