## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 24, 1902

**Weather:** Dull day with rain at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day with rain at night. To studio at 10 — finished scale drawing of monument for Greenwood lot — then got out a lot of unfinished oil painting work \+ planned getting something done in that was, especially with a view to getting finished some of the many wedding presents that I owe to my nieces \+ nephews — Home to lunch at 1 — at 2 went down town — first to Tax office \+ got tax bills for this year — then to Clerk of U. S. Circuit Court or jury notice — was excused on acct of my age, being so near 70 — then to Montgomery's in Fulton St. \+ got little 3" x 3" steel anvil, polished face — hammer, punches \+ callipers, an outfit for the erasing \+ hammering up of work on etched plates — then to Clarence B. Smith's — 21 State St. \+ gave final instructions for codici to my will. To Studio at 5 — then home — dinner — Avery was at Charles for a little while to consult with him about monument —

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 25, 1902

**Weather:** A nasty rainy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty rainy day — \+ I got up with a nastier headache than usual. It was after 10 when I left the house — I went to Carrere \+ Hastings to get a permit to enter the enclosure of the Public Library buildings now being erected on Reservoir Square. W. Carrere had just come in — he at once insisted upon acting as my escort. We must have spent quite an hour going thro' the temporary buildings where I saw the draughtsmen at work \+ then got a realizing sense of the vast amt of drawing \+ redrawing that has to be done when such a monumental work as the planning \+ erection of this library bldg. has to be done conscientiously \+ with every provision of care. It astonished me when I saw how many times each part was drawn, revised \+ redrawn at many different scales — then we went to the modeling rooms — my astonishment was greater when I saw that almost every part is modeled in clay \+ plaster after the drawings are made — here again there is revision \+ remodeling leading frequently to modification of original plans — interior as well as exterior work is modeled — entire rooms, hall \+ stairways with ceilings complete — mirrors on the floor to reflect the ceiling — sections of ceiling (for instance) being repeated upon a larger scale 3/4" = 1' — \+ again the detail being given full size — $48,000 had been appropriated for this work alone. The man at the head of it being an enthusiast — Carrere then took me over the building which is not yet all up to first floor line — a considerable portion of marble basement wall is now up. Then I went to F. J. A. Ward's 119 W. 52d St. to enquire about Paul Bartlett, proposed by him for membership in Century — Ward is in Ohio — then to studio at 12 — home at 1 — lunch — then gave up to my headache \+ went to bed —
