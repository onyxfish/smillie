## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 22, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A thoroughly nasty day. damp \+ dark with light rain at times. After blfp. made an office call on Dr. Cilley. Then to studio \+ spent what remained of the aft. in Finishing plt. \+ like work. Home to lunch at 1 \- again to studio \+ at about 4 what to day in the Hospital Second Ave. 17 \+ 18 St. Open to Public To-day. I went from garret to Cellar \+ was much impressed. It was 6.40 when I got home \- dinner \- Well at 8.30 at meeting of Com. on Admissions \- Century Club \- first meeting of Com. this year. As it was my introduction I found it very interesting. Prof. Van Army [illegible] was made chairman. It was an unusually short session \+ adj. at 10. The room was badly ventilated \- much tobacco smoke \+ my eyes hurt very much so, I left immediately after adjournment \+ went home.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 23, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ cool. To Studio at 10 \+ Painted till 12 on Table in Borden library \- Then to Scribner's \+ saw Mf \+ L. Smith about young Mf Jessup \- he proposed him for membership in the Cent. 15 months ago \- Then to 2°. Nat. Bk. \+ made 3° loan \- $750 \- in all $2500. Then to Avery's gallery \+ home a little before 2 \- lunch \- Then to N.A. of D. \+ met a number of my etching class \- saw some of their pen work done last Sat. \+ Lectured them upon the necessity of careful and exact work \- got away about 4.30 \+ directly to Tonnele's \- 15° St. \+ got photo. reductions of Morgan \+ Morton \+ took them to studio \- After 6 when I got home \- dinner \- Well. very uncomfortable with headache but managed to do some writing.
At 11 went to drug store \+ had a castor oil cocktail \- was up at 1. 2. \+ 4 am.
