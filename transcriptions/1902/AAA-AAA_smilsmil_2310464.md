## Left Page

### Sat. July 5, 1902

**Weather:** Pleasant but clouded, warm & threatening showers  **Temp:** [illegible]

Miss Alice Lane left us.

Pleasant but clouded, warm & threatening showers - There was a little spattering in the afternoon - Miss Lane left immediately after bkft. Katharine & Bessie rode as far as Dr. Daight's with her - my finger was in such bad shape that again I went to Dr. Magee at St. Huberts - He thought it sh'd. be cut & said that he was sure Dr. Daight was entirely competent - Then I went to Mt. VanHoorn's & visited awhile with the old gentleman - Then down to feed B's & arranged to have a horse immediately after dinner - Warner then came with horse & buckboard & took me to Dr. Daight's - but he w'd not cut my finger - said, poultice it till Monday - I was much disappointed & ret'd home - my mouth still troubles me but less than yesterday - Ev'tf, Reading

## Right Page

### Sun. July 6, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant summer day - the first since I came here when it was comfortable to have all doors & windows open & to sit out on the north piazza without protection - I got up at 6.30 & until 9 o'clk was engaged bathing & exercising being bothered much by my maimed right hand - the little finger of which remains obstinately sore - seemingly from day to day neither better or worse - I loafed about during the m'f & wrote some letters & nursed my finger - dinner at 2 - Later I went to the hotel & tried to open communication with Dr. Fester at Raquette but found no telephonic connection - Then Jim, Ralph & I climbed the wood road that Rogers Bro's are making to the great slash between Noon Mark & Dix - it was a longer & harder pull than I have yet taken & I did it without discomfort - Home before 6 - Tea before 7 - The family spent the ev'tf on North Piazza - Chinese Lanterns - I read till 9 o'clk -
