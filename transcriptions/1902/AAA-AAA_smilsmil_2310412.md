## Left Page

### Fri. March 21, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

House fronts slipped into subway.

Pleasant springlike day. To studio before 10 \+ before noon completed the drawing in on plate of portrait of father \- Varnished plate \+ prepared for biting \- or, more properly \- having laid a solid ground over the whole of the plate I saw about through such lines as were not sufficient bitten as it was impossible to get a satisfactory rebiting ground on it \- At about 12.30 went to Park Ave. for a walk before lunch. found that a portion of sidewalk on east side between 37. \+ 38th S'. had slipped down into Rapid Transit tunnel \+ two stores \- No° 55 \+ 57 had gone down into the hole \- while at lunch about 1.30 \- a large portion of the fronts of these two houses, followed the stoaks down into the tunnel hole \- Bessie \+ I went out \+ watched the crumbling \+ desintegration for some time but saw no large masses come down. It was exciting. To studio at 2.30 \+ bit the work of the mt \+ then worked with graver till near 6 \- a short walk \- then dinner \- Evelf wrote to Charl \- to Jim \+ to Ralph \- before I went to Grand Central Station \- superb moonlight night \- large gangs of men with flaring lights shoring up the falling House fronts \+ a procession of carts dumping earth \+ stones into the hole. It was an extraordinary picture.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 22, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day. To studio about 9.30 and spent the mt. working on etching portrait of father \+ taking prints \- Results very discouraging \- I have made serious errors in the drawing of portions of the face \+ I do not like the characters of the lines \- It was 1.30 before I got home to lunch \- Immediately after that I went to N.A.D. etching class. Had a long afternoon printing from plates by the students \+ got home after 6 very tired \- Evelf quietly at home.
