## Left Page

### Mon. May 26, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day — after bkft. went to Miller's about floor to be laid in attic. — to studio before 10 — spent the day taking p.p. on different kinds of paper \+ experimenting with inks \+ different ways of working. Got some pretty good p.p. I think I shall submit a p.p. of plate as it is for approval. I took no india p.p. to-day. I had a call from Scheutening the binder — He is no longer in partnership with Adams. He got an expressman \+ took away the box with Civil war file of Tribunes — To lunch at 1.20 miller came at 2 — It was 2.30 when I got to studio — Continued printing till 4.30 — Home at 5. Rested till 6.30 — dinner — Eve'f wrote to Uncle Willie that I hoped to make a flying visit to Po'k. next Sunday. Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 27, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced falling about 10 p.m. last night \+ continued with little intermission till afternoon to-day — clearing \+ cooler weather at night. All day at studio, most of the time at printing with a result that is almost elation. I have not been able to get what I want in the way of a likeness or of workmanship on the Morgan plate but at last I have pulled through what I hope will be a passable job — Among other things I have learned how the plate must be printed \+ was able to take some satisfactory p.p. on india \+ jap. papers — a printer can follow my directions easily. Home to lunch as usual — It was nearly 6.45 when I got home to dinner so tired that I did not dare to sat at that time but rested on my bed till about 7.30 — then I took my dinner. — Carlson came at 9.30 — I told him that I was very tired — so, he gave me passion treatment only which seemed restful to me — but I did not get to sleep till aftermednight. — Mary \+ the girls went to Dr. Stepen Smillie's \+ spent the evef.
