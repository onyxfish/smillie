## Left Page

### Fri. June 13, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came from Tarrytown \-

Very hot day winding up with a heavy thunderstorm about 10 p.m \- Bkfst. at 9 \- then to Miller's \+ then to studio \- To work at once to fake some p[illegible] of Morgan plate \- thinking that I can please myself better than Mr Voigt was able to \- I had some difficulty in starting but after working till 2.30, without lunch \- only a few crackers \- I got 7 prints \- 3 on thin Jap. \+ 4 large paper India, none of them quite what I wish but nearer to it than Voigt got \- If I could have another day I c[oul]d get it \- at 3 I went to Chas's \+ rested \- then a Telegram from Ralph saying he would arrive at 4.25 \- I met him at Station \- then to studio \- Tracked down proofs \+ cleaned up \- To 110 at 6.30 \- Ralph there \- E[illegible]. Mann, had just left his trunk \+ bicycle \- then to Chas's \+ had dinner at 7 \- Evely., music \+ chatting till 10 \- then to bed with thunder storm accompaniments \-

## Right Page

### Sat. June 14, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast with cool east wind \- About 7 \- before getting out of bed something got into my left eye giving me much discomfort which increased to such a degree that soon after 8 I went to Dr. Alley's \- he was not in but the young doctor sharing the office removed from my eye a small speck, apparently a coal cinder \- He says there is now a great deal of such trouble owing to the amt. of soft coal burned in the city because anthracite is not to be had because of the coal strike. The bkfst. \+ after that with Ryan \+ his men at 110 having the water turned off from upper part of house \- tank cleaned \+ traps [illegible] with glycerine \- Then to 2? Nat.? But \+ arranged for possible over-drafts during the summer \- then to Brooks Bros. \+ bought a suit of clothes \- Called at 110 Mad. Ave. to see Elis[illegible]. Howe \- not in \- Then to Charles \- lunch at 2 \- Geo. came in for a few minutes \- wrote a letter to Mary \- went to 110 with Ralph \- there I had a little rest \- then to studio and spent an hour on some final work on Morgan's p[l]ate \- packing there till 6.30 \- dinner at 7 \- Evely. with newspapers \+ Chas'. orchestrelle music till after 10 \-
