## Left Page

### Fri. March 7, 1902

**Weather:** Bright pleasant m'f. — overcast p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio about 10 — spent the m'f working upon Morton Plate \+ printing a couple of india p'fs. to submit to Mr. M. \+ to Mr. Stedman. — Home to lunch after 1 — then down town — went first to Mr. Clarence Smith's law office, 21 State Street, to see him about possible Personal property tax — not in — then to 60 Cedar St. \+ saw Mr. Van Felt, gave him a list of the Real Estate holdings of the Ireland Real Estate Co. — \+ talked with him of the possibility of converting it into holding that while paying a good interest will stand a good chance of increasing in value — He is to let me hear from him later — Then to Fuchs \+ Lange — 29 Warren St. \+ got a lot of gelatine sheets for tracing — then to Sands \+ Vandwater St. — \+ got some old Colbert plates that he has cleaned up for me — Then home soon after 5 — tired — \+ had a nap before dinner — Eve'f. wrote to Jim \+ Ralph —

## Right Page

### Sat. March 8, 1902

**Weather:** Pleasant for a while in the m'f — Rain com-  **Temp:** [illegible]

menced about 3 p.m. \+ continued heavily thro' the afternoon \+ eve'f. I spent the m'f at studio working on Morton plate. After taking p'fs. yesterday eve'f. I saw that some reinforcement was necessary in parts to bring it together — what I have done this eve'f. will, I think, greatly benefit the general effect. Home at 1 — lunch — then to N.A.\+D. — Etching Class — full house — all on hand because I had promised to print \+ they all wished to see what their work looked like on paper. — I took with me blankets, rags, ink \+ my tools — I damped paper \+ printed from ten or a dozen plates — then worked on two or three of them with dry-point, graver \+ scraper — It was a very busy afternoon \+ I did not get through till after 5 — 6.15 when I got home — Charl came in for a while in the eve'f \+ I got to bed early.

Mr. Clarence Smith called at house about 5.45 — I did not get in till 6.15
