## Left Page

### Thur. April 10, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mean, nasty day — raw — cloudy \+ spattering rain. To studio at 9.30 — stopped out the Morgan head that I had drawn in yesterday then put in some broad lines on the background intending to bite them up strong. Varnished the plate to be ready for the acid at 11.15 started for N.A.f.D. — got there a little before 12 — saw most of the Etching cl. \+ their plates — criticised \+ advised as to the biting — mixed acid for them \+ started for home about 1. had lunch \+ to studio at 2.30 — bit background \+ then worked with graver on the figure till 6 o'clk. Carleton Cook was married at 4.30 this P.M. in the church cor. Mad. Ave. \+ 35th St. but I could not go, either to the wedding or to the reception that followed at the bride's home in 59th S. I had a short walk before dinner — after dinner a little time with my paper then wrote to Scott Brown \+ sent him check for P.W. Otis — \+ sent check to Bursar of Yale College — curate to June.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 11, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dubious m'g. clearing delightfully — light rain after 6 p.m. After bkfs. wrote part of a letter to Ralph \+ got to studio at 10. Worked with graver on Morgan plate till 1 o'clk — then home to dinner (lunch) \+ after that went to N.A. \+ spent the rest of p.m. with etching class — most of the competition plates have been bitten — some of them are very encouraging — \+ some are perfectly hopeless. I think that Connelly stands 1st \+ O'Malley 2d. I stayed till 5 — then walked across the Park to Mad. Ave. \+ 98th S. \+ rode home. Finished letter to Ralph — Dinner at 6.30 — About 8 went to Mr. Barhydt's — found her packed up \+ almost ready to start for a summer in Europe — She is very sanguine about the etching class — is quite sure it has come to stay \+ if she had not been interrupted by other callers I think she would, or might, have made some proposition to transfer the fund (Baldwin) to the care of the N.A.f.D. She said the time had come \+ she w. talk of it when she ret. from abroad — Walked home getting in at 10 —
