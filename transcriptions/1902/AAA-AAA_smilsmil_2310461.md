## Left Page

### Sun. June 29, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day free late in the afternoon when it cleared from the north. Up at about 6.30 but did not get my bkf'. till 9 — then fussing about the house till 11 — then R. \+ I started for a walk — we went up the road past Openheim's to the old Noonmark trail \+ then up the new logging road now being made up thro' the notch between Noonmark \+ Round Top to a great wind slash that is to be cleared of its fallen timber. We found the walk very interesting. Home at 1.30 — dinner at 2 — Maghee came at 3 o'clk. I took Miss Lane out for a ride \+ Mr. Maghee came \+ took Katharine — I podged about the house — read a little \+ walked the piazza till tea at 6.30 — Evelf Harry Beeds came to tell me that Trumbull could come to do carpenters work for me in about a fortnight.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 30, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant m'g. — looking really like a final clearing up — a milky sky soon came over the sky growing denser — in the afternoon rain free steadier — After bkf'. I wrote some notes then Otis came \+ I spent an hour or more with him going over the place \+ planning improvements — It was about noon when R. \+ I went to Hotel \+ then a short walk. Dinner at 1. — until after 4 putting up bamboo shades, straightening up trees that I transplanted last fall — like work — Then Trumbull came to arrange about doing carpenter work for me \+ I was with him for some time. Tea at 6.30 — Evelf with newspapers.
