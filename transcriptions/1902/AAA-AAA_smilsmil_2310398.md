## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 19, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

my cathartic dose routed me out of bed before 2 a.m. \+ for nearly three hours I had a very hard time - vomiting \+ purging - It seemed as though nothing but the outside walls would be left - I got a little sleep before 8 o'clk - could take no hlps. Sent for D. Cilley He came after 11 - did not think that I required any especial attention - Suffered all day from very severe headache Late in the afternoon braced up \+ wrote a note to Sect. of Century Com. on Admissions in behalf of Henry Jessups.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 20, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant weather in the m'f clouding over later - after a night of head ache at 7 o'clk. I felt that I could not get up but a full dose of Acetanelid \+ Caffiene enabled me to do it. After a very light blcf. I went to D. Allais, W 47. St. at 9.30 \+ was there for an hour - he filled a tooth for me. Then to studio - wet paper \+ lighted gas under soapstone on printing table - then went to Charity Organization Socy. \+ made enquiries about a begging letter-writer. E. B. Rowland, about whom Oscar Ireland had written to me - not found. Come to lunch - Kate, Mrs Cook, had prepared a bird prune - I ate it - it was my undoing - then to studio \+ took prints from Morgan and Morton - Morton came out in satisfactory condition but Morgan was a disappointment - It will require much careful treatment - Probably I shall have to take out the whole of the face - the etched parts bring ones better - then home and tried to get a rest - at dinner could eat but little - that bird was fluttering within me - after dinner, about half an hour - I went all \+ pieces suddenly - having horrors \+ discomforts of all kinds - I went to bed at 9.30 completely used up, after taking a cathartic dose of Carter's liver pills -

After leaving studio I walked to Alexanders 5th Ave \+ 16 S. for shoes \+ back home - 3 miles - without discomfort.
