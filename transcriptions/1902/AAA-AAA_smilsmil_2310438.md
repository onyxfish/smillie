## Left Page

### Mon. May 12, 1902

**Weather:** Overcast but pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast but pleasant. To studio at 10. worked on Morgan plate all day — Faking pf. \+ working between. I am much out of sorts to-day \+ cannot work as I wish to — I cannot take such prints as I used to — cannot etch — cannot bite — etc. etc. — Soon after 12 I went to Scribner's — Saw W. W. Smith \+ showed him the Pach Photo. \+ reported Borden business to-date. Then home to lunch. To studio at 2.30 \+ worked till 5 — Then out — saw Carlson going to D. Cilley's \+ I followed him in — but the D. had no advice to give — I feel that I need some advice or some treatment sorely — I am slowly but surely losing in every way — I feel that I am facing a not far distant end — I had a short walk \+ home — dinner — after that a while over every paper. At 9.30 Andrew Carlson came \+ went over me pretty thoroughly, leaving a little before 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 13, 1902

**Weather:** Overcast m.f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m.f. rain for about a couple hours before noon. Then gradually cleared \+ pleasant. To studio at 10 \+ worked on Morgan head till noon — Then to W. C. Pate's, 100 William St. \+ took to him the little painting "near Crowninshield's Point, Mass. Coast" painted some years ago. Then back home to lunch. After lunch went to Nat. Acad \+ Design \+ spent some time auditing accts preparatory to Annual meeting to-morrow. Left there at about 5 \+ walked down by the Park to the Pamlico — near 70th St. Saml. Coleman's quarters — he was out — then home — dinner at 6.30. Eve\[ning\] at 9.30 Carlson came \+ gave me the usual treatment. Had a high-daddy night — Hyper-acidity, nightmares \+ like pleasing incidents.
