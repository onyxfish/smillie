## Left Page

### Wed. June 11, 1902

**Weather:** Mild & overcast with some gleams of sunshine until about 2 o'clk when rain began to fall & at times came down quite heavily till evening. There was some thunder during the afternoon. I had an ugly headache in the m'f & did not have Charl's till near 11 o'clk — then to house — to Dorph to Miller's & to the studio — It was near 2 when I went to Century & had lunch — caught in rain on my return to studio — I worked awhile on Morgan plate & made preparations to print — washing printing cloths — cutting paper etc. to Charl's, before 6, in pelting shower. Ge. came & at 7 we dined — I felt very heavy in the ev'g & tried to read with little success — a disagreeable feeling in my head akin to dizziness, bothering me & unfitting me for either work or play.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 12, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A muggy day, growing hotter — Bkf. at 9 — Then to 110 — to laundry & to studio — after 11 I went down town to 30 Broad S'. & saw Mr. Wilson, Manager of Stock Ex. Hist'. Co — I gave him a late pf. of J.P. Morgan plate & he expressed himself greatly pleased & called in others to share his pleasure — but he put off the payment of my bill saying it was with the Auditing Com. & w'. be p'. next week. Then I went to Russell & Louieu Co. in Chambers S'. but my hardware order was not yet filled — then to studio — then home — then to Century & had my lunch — then wrote to Kate — then to Charl's then to Wannamaker's & completed my Keene purchases there — then to Vantine's & got some mill work for Keene — then to Studio & wet down paper for p[f]s of Morgan head to-morrow then home, rested half an hour — then to Charl's Ge. came & we dined at 7. Carlson came at 9.30 & gave me the usual rub down & exercise — his last visit — this experience has cost me $63[illegible] so far.
