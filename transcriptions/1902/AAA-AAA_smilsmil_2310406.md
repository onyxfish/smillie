## Left Page

### Sun. March 9, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day but quiet + niece. I got down to my breakfast soon after 10, read news paper till about noon - Then rose up to N.A. f D. with umbrella that I borrowed of Davis yesterday - then back to dinner - Had a long nap in the afternoon - tea at 6.30. We'f wrote a letter to Miss Charlotte Kent, informing her that I would mail to her, to-morrow, my Montrose Garden book of record. At 10 I went to Charle's + spent nearly an hour with him - then home + to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 10, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful cloudless day + mild - after bkfst. wrote to Mr. Clarence Smith telling him the object of my call on him last Friday. I want to know if I am on The Personal Pay Rolls - + if so I wish to be got off - To studio after 10 - made paste + mounted a pencil sketch - preparation for a water-color drawing - "in Pincian Hill looking over Piazza del Popolo" - There were many things lying about that I put away + at 12.30 I went to Station H. Lex. Ave. + 44 S. + mailed my Montrose Garden book to Miss Charlotte Kent. Then home to lunch - After that to studio at 2.30 - mounted + stretched large drawing boards preparation to making some scale drawings of work to be done at Keene next summer - At 3.20 went to Calveer's + sat for him till 5 o'clk - [illegible] he modeled a collar + neck tie on my Remodeled head - or head's neck, Rather. Then to Pompajo - Then to Tonnelle's at 5.40 - place closed. When to Alexandre's + tried on new shoes - Home at 6.30 dinner - no letter from Ralph - a long one from Jim that was to me very depressing - He has flunked in his Trigonometry exam - is dangerously low in Greek + German - He writes this to me very frankly, for which I am glad, Tho' it distresses me. I felt very blue during the evening while Bessie played on the piano.
