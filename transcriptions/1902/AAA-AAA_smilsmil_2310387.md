## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 28, 1902

**Weather:** Overcast but not unpleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast but not unpleasant. I had so much writing to do that it was late when I got to studio. I was kept busy till lunch time in rearranging things after the thorough house cleaning. Spent the afternoon at much the same sort of work. Home before 5 \+ rested before dinner — Eve'g. I dressed after dinner \+ at 9 rode up to Tiffany's 72d S. \+ Mad.. Ave. Met Alden Weir in the car. Tiffany's entertainment was a very pleasant one with lots of "swells" from the various walks of city life — lawyers, doctors, musicians, poets artists \+ just ordinary \+ pleasant fellows \+ a few minutes of quiet chat with Abbey — At 11 30 I walked down with Walter Cook \+ br. Geo. Oct. left us at 56 S. \+ Walter left me at my door.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 29, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A considerable snow storm — another day with little accomplished — more letter writing in the m'g \+ to studio before 11 — more fixing up — I find much to do in assorting, tying in paper \+ putting away the heterogeneous contents of closets that have not been disturbed for 2, 3, or 4 years. Home to lunch \+ did not return to studio. My dissipation last night made me very heavy \+ I slept a good part of the afternoon — Eve'g. I was engaged writing.
