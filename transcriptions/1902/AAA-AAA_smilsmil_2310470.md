## Left Page

### Thur. July 17, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Peculiar weather - misty, lowhanging clouds - wind from S. W., growing warmer - showers about \+ some thunder in the afternoon - fine moonlight night. much of my time during the day spent oiling \+ staining new work in my room \+ laying out the ceiling for muslin panels. Dinner - It was about 4.30 when Ralph \+ I started out for a walk - Jim was away fishing - we went to Mossy Cascade - I got back at 6.30 warm \+ tired - I lay on my bed \+ Rested till about 8 o'clk - then had a light supper. Eve'g all out on South piazza enjoying the moonlight - Mr. \+ Mrs. J. H. Maghu came \+ joined the worshipers.
It was after 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 18, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

High wind all day - cloudy but not cold. I was kept pretty busy all day - Trumbull made the frames upon which to stretch the muslin for ceiling of alcove off main room \+ my bed room. I wrote to Eliz. Howe inviting her to make us a visit. I restained portions of alcove off my bed room. Afternoon a per "dowie" \+ unlike exertion - I painted \+ stained sashes for plate glass in main room alcove. B. Haight, his wife \+ her sister called. Eve'g passed very stupidly.
