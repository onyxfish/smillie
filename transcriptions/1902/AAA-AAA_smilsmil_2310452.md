## Left Page

### Mon. June 9, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant beautiful day \- B\[illegible\]. at 9 It was after 10 when I went to 110 \- Allen's plaster man had commenced work in second story hall cutting out the defective parts where the floor of last winter passed through \- He made a dreadful mess. I spent sometime with Allen \- then to studio \- I did not have a regular lunch but got some crackers \- at 1 I had Valhuenfeld's man take large packing case from studio to house \- I worked at studio getting my painting materials together till after 4 \- then I went to Arnold \+ Constables \+ bought drawers there to Mr. Cutcheon's \+ bought hats. Then walked up to 37th S. I had a short rest before dinner feeling uncomfortably inert \- unwilling, \+ to some extent, unable to move. The eveg was passed reading to them to bed rather late.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 10, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Tarrytown \-

Dubious day \- pleasant at times in the mf \- threatening at times in the afternoon \- light rain about 7 p.m. The most of the mf was spent writing a long letter to Jim in answer to me rec'd last eveg. I feel that it was necessary to write it once \+ so, took valuable daylight. It was, with reference to his standing in his studies \+ the disposition to be made of his time during the coming summer \- it is very hard to explain to him, in a way that he cannot misconstrue, my wishes in reference his mental attitude as a student, not to free my ambition for him. Shortly after noon I went to G.C. Station \- got a light lunch \+ took 1.10 train to Tarrytown getting there soon after 2. Met the? \+ Mrs. Jacobs \+ Mr. King \- Srinlaw of Mr. Goodhue at station \+ we went \+ together to the Hackley Hall Commencement exercises \- Met Rachel promptly \- Exercises followed soon after \- declamation by several boys \- fee. Mr. Savage made an excellent address to the youngsters \- its tone \+ purpose pleased me much \- it was followed by Mr. Williams who gave diplomas to the upper class. Then we all went to the new buildings where the corner stone of the [illegible] Savage House was laid by Rev. M. Savage \- [illegible] P.S. \- we made a morning [illegible] cent \+ [illegible] addresses. Then the class ivy was planted \- It was then 6.15 \- a shower threatened, so I did not wait for the celebration on the lawn but walked down to station \+ took 6 o'clk Traie \- met Geo. \+ Mrs. Jacobs there \+ Rel \- to the city with them \- I got to Charls' a few minutes after [illegible] 7 o'clk \+ had dinner \- at 9.30 Carson came over \- had dinner our usual work \+ I got to bed at 11 \- . These two erasures are the result of trying to write something predetermined while thinking of something else.
