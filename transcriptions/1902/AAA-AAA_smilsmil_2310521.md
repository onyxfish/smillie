## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 29, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Unpleasant day \- cloudy, windy \+ cold \- Got to work at studio on Morgan place with graves \- After five months rest I now see much that can be done to imp. more it. I began first upon parts of the figure where I have intensified the depth of shading \- Home to lunch \+ back at 2.30 \+ worked on plate till "lost light" \- Then had a short walk on Fifth Ave \- dinner \- Evely Charl came \+ took Mary, Kath., Elis., Nelle to the old Academy of Music in 14th St. when we saw "The Ninety \+ Nine" a drama in which one of the leading characters is a real locomotive engine with steam enough to whistle \+ make the driving wheels go round \- it was really a very effective production \- The play was amusing \+ as a work of art was on a level with E.L. Henry's paintings \- There was no remarkably good acting \+ little that was remarkably bad \- on the whole it proved better than I had expected It was 11 before it was over \+ midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 30, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- some flying clouds \+ a few drops of rain late in the afternoon. Worked all day on Morgan plate \- After my work in the afternoon I went down to Alexander's for my shoes that were to be done last night \- not done yet \- Home to dinner at 6.30 W. \+ Mrs. Maghee came \+ took Kath. "Leigh" to dine \+ then to theatre \- Evely Charl came in.
