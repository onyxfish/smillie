## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 17, 1902

*Eliz^th Howe arr.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day, less hazy than yesterday. Ralph had more fever this m'f — 101°. As I transferred him to my bed where he stayed all the m'f — he spent the P. m. \+ eve'g in reclining chair in studio wrapped in a blanket. At 9 P.M. his temp. has fallen to 100°. I think his trouble is tonsillitis in mild form. I am still out of sorts \+ did little more on Borden outline than wash a part of it out because some of the proportions were incorrect as I found after careful measurement. About 10 am. I drove with Emma down to the village \+ did some shopping for Mary. In our return we went to the hotel to bid Wm \+ Katie Elkin good-bye — not in — we walked a way into Reserve \+ on return to hotel, found the E's in \+ stayed with them till near 1 o'clk — they go out to Port Henry on their way home this afternoon — then home to dinner \+ we found Eliz'th Howe there just in from E-town — Part of P. m. I was useless because of condition of my head — most of the time I was with Ralph \+ in the eve'g. Maglee \+ Sherwood came in the eve'g.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 18, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'f., overcast before noon — stormy looking moonlight \+ a little rain — after 6hps. I worked for a while on Borden outline \+ at 11 went with Emma \+ Eliz'th. to the top of Roaring Brook fall where we found Charl \+ Katharine Throop — home to dinner at 1.15 — After dinner more work on Borden outline \+ at 5 I went out alone for a walk. I went into Reserve on the road for a mile \+ out, making, to the house, 3 miles — \+ enjoyed the walk. Met W. G. Neilson who has just arr'd \+ had a rambling sort of talk with him. He gave me a copy of his Annual Report as Pres. of Adirondack Reserve, just out. Home to tea at 6.30 — Eve'g read Neilson's report \+ news paper.
