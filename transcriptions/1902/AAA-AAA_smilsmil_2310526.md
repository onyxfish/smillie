## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 10, 1902

**Weather:** Charming day — mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

It was 10.30 before I got to studio — met Schlueing, the binder at the door — he took $10 off bill for binding file of Civil War papers — then I set to work on Morgan head \+ fussed over it all day without being able to get a satisfactory proof — this is awkward as I have engaged to submit a pf. to Stedman to-morrow. Home to lunch — back at 2.30 worked till 4.30 — then to Jackson's. 17th St. \+ Broadway \+ "forced" "Gas logs" — then to Keppel's \+ looked at a collection of old engravings that he has there — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Evelyn Charl came in — Kate had been to Murray Hill Theatre this p.m. \+ was so much pleased with Ralph Stuart's appearance in "The Romance of a Poor Young Man" that she urged me to get tickets — Just before 10 I went to theatre \+ bought tickets for Friday night.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 11, 1902

**Weather:** Delightful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 o'clk — all morning pursuing over Morgan plate trying to get a satisfactory pf. to take to Stedman — Home to lunch at 1 \+ back at 2 — by 4 o'clk I got a pf. that I racked to a board \+ took it to Stedman at the Sherwood Studios — 3\. 8 10, 57 [illegible]. Stedman is not yet quite satisfied with the eyes — not yet quite. The face, as a likeness — a work of art — our vaunted technique is nothing short of a dismal, humiliating failure. S. was very nice about it \+ wrote a note for me to take to Morgan with a proof of the plate for his criticism. I have had a mind to start a new plate. Home to dinner at 6.30 — Evelyn Charl came in — I wrote short notes to the boys with reference to the Harvard-Yale foot ball game to come off at New Haven on the 22d.
