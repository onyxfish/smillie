## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 25, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cool m'f. after a cold night - wind chg. from north to south in the afternoon with promise of a weather change to-morrow. Mr. Otis \+ Moody worked together all day hauling earth for top dressing \+ gravel for walk. - I decided, after 11 kfs., to go up Hopkius to which Emma S. assented but Eliz'. Howe did not dare undertake it. Emma, Ralph \+ I started at 10 o'clk. \+ after a very leisurely walk got to the tops at 1 o'clk. The day was ideal - cool \+ quiet - we spent two hours on the tops - had coffee heated over a spirit lamp - Egg - deviled ham \+ calves-head jelly sandwiches - cake \+ fruit dessert - enjoyed with perfect comfort while lolling on moss grown rocks - We explored the rocks \+ woods near the summit \+ enjoyed the grand view to the fullest extent \+ at 3 o'clk. started down, getting home at 5.30. I had a good rub down \+ a short rest before tea, or rather, dinner at 6.30 - Well chatting \+ to bed early, where I lay \+ read for an hour till 10.30

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 26, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Stormy day. Moody left for the day after doing m'f. chores. I went to Post Office before 11 - \+ the m'f. slipped away without my having done any thing worth while. Dinner at 1 - I did a very little work on Borden outline \+ a number of little things about the house - had a long walk on piazza - Tea at 6.30 - a sleepy eve'f. \+ to bed early.
