## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 13, 1902

**Weather:** Fine day, but cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, but cold & windy. To studio at about 10 & to work with graver on Pierpont Morgan head — Maurice Sterne came in for a while. Home to lunch at 1.30 — after that, to 2°, nat°. Buh to studio at 3.13 & worked till near 5. A short walk on Park Ave. & home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Estf. George came in.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 14, 1902

**Weather:** Pleasant day & milder — with no sign  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day & milder — with no sign of snow storm predicted by the Weather Bureau. Got up with headache that stupified me to a great extent during the day. After bkf't I took checks to Douglas, Miller & Ryan — paying the D— bill in full & leaving small balances unpaid on the other two. It was about 11 when I got to studio — worked on Morgan head — finding it much more difficult to manage than the Morton one being much less definite in drawing. After lunch worked till near 4 o'clk — then walked down to 51 W. 10' & had an interview with E. M. Ward to arrange that our classes at the N. A. of D. shall not interfere. As it is he complains that his students are running away from his class, neglecting their work there, to do work in my Etching class. Then went to Maurice Sterne's studio 935 B'dway, where he has an exhibit of his etchings & paintings — Home after 6 — East — wrote long letters to Jim & Ralph & got to bed about midnight.
