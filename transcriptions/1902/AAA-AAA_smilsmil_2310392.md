## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 7, 1902

**Weather:** Clear, Cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, Cold & windy — after 10 when I got to studio — worked on J. P. Morgan Etching till lunch at 1 — & again in the afternoon from 2 30 to near 5 — finished the drawing in. I am hopeful that I will get two creditable plates done soon Had a short walk & a Rest before dinner Easf wrote letters to Jim & Ralph. Charl came in for awhile — to bed late.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 8, 1902

**Weather:** Overcast — very cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast — very cold & windy — after 10 when I got to studio — most of the mf was spent working with crayon on sketch portrait of father — from photo by Chase — I varnished plate of Morgan & Morton & am now ready to begin biting — Home to lunch at 1 — after that went to Nat'l — Etching class — full — working with pen from an old Indian — no really good work is being done — two or three seem to be really making intelligent effort but have not as yet accomplished much. I left there soon after 4 — went to Mr. Olcenhym's Riverside drive & 109 St not in — then to Sam Colman's Central Park W. near 70 S. Mrs C. is now under X Ray treatment — Home at 6 — dinner — Welf. Charl & I went to Century for Prof. Mr. H. Rees's lecture "Some Recent Results of Celestial Photography." As a lecture it was dismally disappointing to me — Mr. K — is neither a good or an interesting speaker — He told nothing that was new to me but the photos — that were enlarged upon a screen were intensely interesting & I had never before seen so fine a display of photos of Sun, moon, stars & nebulae — I had a short talk with him after the lecture & asked some questions but failed to get any enlightenment. I remained and chatted with one & another till near midnight.
