## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 1, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A close, muggy day — a real dog-day — with three showers — very short but quite heavy. Trumbull worked all day on north piazza braces. Moody, under my direction tore up board walk to annex \+ commenced the laying of the low stone wall that will retain the east side of the path. I felt the depression of the weather \+ was on my bed from 12 to 1 o'clk. Then dinner. Afternoon, I wrote a long letter to George concerning his plans for the winter \+ offering him some suggestions as to living in some little town near the City — also as to the possibility of my letting him have the studio now occupied by Maynard. Tea — eve 7, all sit on north piazza. I divided time between newspapers \+ walking on piazza.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 2, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Crisp, cool, beautiful day with some very high gusty wind about noon. Trumbull at work — most of the day on North Piazza — we spent some time planning the location of a building near annex to be used for general storage purposes. After bkf. much time was given to laying out the new path from house to dining room. Then I wrote a note to E. C. Stedman in answer to his of 21 ult. \+ went to hotel \+ got mail. Kitchen Cook, Katharine, Bessie \+ Jine went up Noon Mark, taking lunches with them. Dinner at 1. Afternoon, some more planning — about 5 all hands went over to Maghee's to bid good bye to old W. Vaulthorn \+ to Mr. Vaulthorn, his daughter, who leave for town to-morrow m'f. Home to tea at 6.30 — found Willie Cook there — most unexpectedly arrived from some camp down east — the result of misunderstanding the plans of Kitchen who was to leave to-morrow at pecting to meet him in N.Y. City. We made the best we could of it — Willie occupied Jine's bed \+ Jine slept on one of the cushioned bunks in main room.
