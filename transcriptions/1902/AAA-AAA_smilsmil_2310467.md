## Left Page

### Fri. July 11, 1902

**Weather:** Brilliant - clear - cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 6 + for two hours exercised - rubbed + bathed - much of my time given to Trumbull who has trouble with frame for plate glass in my bed room window as it is warped - after bkfs. I went over to mr. Maghee's + got some clematis plants - weed + job - which I set out in various places about the house - Otis came + commenced to uncover the cess pool that has caved in - It was such a mess that I told him to cover it up till next spring - then he is to dig it out + timber it up + cover it properly - John Otis brought a load of 3" planks + covered it over temporarily - He also brought a load of stone to commence the walk poorhouse to annex - I wrote to Wilson telling him my patience was about exhausted waiting for payment - fetching of Morgan + Morton + Jim + Katharine went + top of Hopkins - Dinner at 1 - About 3 Ralph + I went out for a scramble - we went to the cliffs due east from the house on the other side of Beede Brook + climbed to the top of the second tier of cliffs, above the Smith Beede sugar camp - There is no trail + we found it all exceedingly hard bit of work - the black flies made us almost wild - but the view was very satisfactory - we then crossed over to the south end of the cliff + came down on Beede Brook back of Putnam's camp - + home by Ghost's walk - at 5.30 - Jim Ralph + I had a fizz of Koka-Cola - Tea at 6.30 Eve. Miss Davis + mr. Magee came in for a few minutes - at P.C. - I had cramps in my legs pretty severely - but did not feel at all fatigued -

## Right Page

### Sat. July 12, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful summer day. I got up feeling very well after my yesterday's tramps - notwithstanding loss of sleep last night - I did not get to sleep till about 2 o'clk this nt. - probably because of Koka-Cola that I took when I came in from my tramps. Trumbull worked all day in my bed room + at last my plate glass window was got into its place + running smoothly. When I unpacked the case having the three plates of glass I was disgusted to find that one of the plates was broken into small pieces - I cannot tell who is to blame for it. About 10 I went to Hotel - got mail, then to Scott Brown's + got a little white lead. I got a pleasant letter from Dr. Gerzier who writes that the trouble on my little finger is "a Runaround." Dinner at 1 - Afternoon very busy leading the sash frame, getting the glass into it. Then staining + painting it - I was very tired before tea time - + had a short rest - After tea Katherine Wardenburgh came over + the two K's + Jim + Ralph had Ping Pong -
