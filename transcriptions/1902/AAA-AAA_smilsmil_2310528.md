## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 14, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A sort of Dresden fog in the mf. which later cleared to a nice sunny day. To studio about 10 \+ worked on Borden outline Till lunch time — then home — at 2 o'clk. I went down town to new Chamber of Commerce bldg. to which Col. Turner had given me a card of admission — It is a remarkably interesting bldg. not completed — Turner had charge of decorations — I found a number of Artists \+ Connoisseurs there — the collection of portraits is interesting but, with very few exceptions, they are poor paintings. The library is charming — after about an hour I left — went to Wall St. \+ saw Charl. — then home \+ wrote notes to Jim \+ Ralph — dinner — Wetf. just as Mary K. B. \+ I were starting for the theatre the Charl. came \+ joined us — he got a Pros. box, seat changed Bessie's seat \+ took her with him — The play was "A Romance of a Poor Young Man" — It was amusing tho' given in a very ordinary manner — The hero (manuel) sneezed, after violent efforts to suppress it, during the death scene of old Laroque, \+ the house enjoyed it, laughing unreservedly. During an intermission the orchestra played "Susie Woosie" a very popular song from the Weber \+ Fields Vaudeville farce of Twirly Wirly. A very peculiar effect was produced by the audience humming the air along with the orchestra — It was not singing but a very singular, all pervasive humming that could not be located — it was in the air — seemingly voiced by no one.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 15, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another sad-eyed mf. changing later to faint beaming smiles of sunshine. I got to studio about 10 \+ worked for an hour on Borden outline — then to bank \+ made deposit — then into Scribner's — saw Mr. Senith for a moment — then a look at some fine bindings on exhn. — then back to studio \+ worked till 1 — then to lunch — back at 2.30 \+ worked till 4 — then loafed on Park Ave. for an hour — mild \+ muggy — any exertion was an effort — Dinner — Evg. at 8.30 went to Charl's \+ he gave us Schubert's 9th Seymphony on his Orchestrelle — home at 10.30.
