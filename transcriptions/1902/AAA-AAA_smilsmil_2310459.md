## Left Page

### Mon. June 23, 1902

**Weather:** Cloudy \+ quite cool  **Temp:**

Bkf't. at 8 \- Dennis Garneau came \+ I set him to work on a series of jobs \- then I spent much time writing a letter to Jim, answering two from him that I found awaiting me. I went over to the hotel about noon Telephoned to West port Station about freight here \- then to ferd Beede's \+ saw him about bringing freight in \- then to dinner at 1 \- after that wrote a couple of notes \+ at 3 went to hotel \+ mailed them \+ got my mail that came in about 2. Afternoon busy arranging things in-doors, raking pine needles off from newly seeded grass, etc. Tea at 6.30 \- Eve'g. developed suddenly something like influenza \+ went to bed with a streaming nose.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 24, 1902

miss alice Lane came.

**Weather:** A day of showers \- the m'g was cloudy  **Temp:**

\+ the afternoon was a succession of showers with occasional thunder \- the wind grow-ing colder. After bkf't. for some time raking pine needles from off the young grass. \+ working about generally. It was near noon that ferd. Beede came with Miss Lane, from Po'keepsie. Dinner at 1. wrote a short note to Jim. went to P. O. before 3 \- \+ ret'd with mail \- \+ loafing \+ raking pine needles again \- Tea at 6.30 \- Eve'g. the girls went over to spend a couple of hours with Mr. Maghee. I read newspapers.
