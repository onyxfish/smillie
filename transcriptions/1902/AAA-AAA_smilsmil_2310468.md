## Left Page

### Sun. July 13, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very fine day — but I was not able to enjoy it fully because of a headache that I got up with \+ that hung on me most the evef. Bkfs. at about 8.30 — I podged about the house all the m'f. fixing locks, curtains \+ like small chores — Dinner at 1.30 — Afternoon, considerable loafing on the breezy piazzas until after 4 — then I gave up to my headache \+ was on my bed till 6 — On piazza for some time — then to Studio \+ wrote to Miller the carpenter about broken glass.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 14, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very hot \+ muggy — thunderstorm late in the afternoon \+ two heavy ones before 10 p.m. After bkfs. I got to work with Trumbull \+ finished drawing of the seat under my east window in bedroom. I worked with him more or less during the day. He is not a very good workman \+ is not intelligent in planning his work from beginning thro' to the end. Dinner at 1 — before 3 went to P.O. with letters to mail — got money order for Sparklets. The boys were with me — we started into the Reserve but soon gave it up because it was so hot \+ went back home. Tea at 6.30 \+ self. watching thunderstorm \+ reading newspapers.
