## Left Page

### Thur. May 8, 1902

**Weather:** Superb day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day — to studio at 10 — bit the plate of O'Mally's — that is, the fact that he drew the other day on his etching of a costumed figure — \+ then bit the portions of face of Pierpont Morgan that I have now drawn in for the third time. — For the rest of the day I worked on the face with gravers. At 12.30 went to Bank \+ deposited W. C. Pate's check — then home to lunch. After that worked on Morgan head till 4 o'clk — then home \+ worked with bks wash on a figure sketch by Katharine putting in background of sky \+ sea — then walked down to 23d. S. \+ paid bill of Horton's — Home to dinner at 6.30. Eve'g. Read my newspaper, then prepared for Carlson who came at 9.30 \+ gave me nearly an hour \+ a half of massage.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 9, 1902

**Weather:** Brilliantly clear — high wind \+ quite cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliantly clear — high wind \+ quite cold. Spent the m'g at studio printing — took two or three proofs from O'Mally's little scene \+ some proofs from the Morgan head, working on the plate between proofs — I cannot get what I wish in the Morgan face but think I am now nearer to it than I have been. It was 1.30 when I got home to lunch — after that I went to N.A. of D. \+ there met Mr. Maxwell \+ went over the accts. with him, preparatory to Annual meeting on Wed. Walked thro' Central Park finding it uncomfortably windy \+ quite cold. Dinner as usual — Eve'g. wrote to Dielman, at his request, statement as to Status of Baldwin fund — then to Jim \+ Ralph — to G.C. Station at 10.30 — mailed letters — then to bed
