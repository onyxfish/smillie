## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 26, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The boys came home for Thanksgiving

Another nasty day - Raining more or less all day - to studio at 10 - found that Stedman had been telephoning for me yesterday & this m't. Just as I was getting to work at painting he came in - much troubled because J. P. Morgan is to sail for Europe on Dec 5th making it most desirable that my etched portrait of him shall be finished at once & submitted to him for criticism before he leaves - I promised to do my level best & at once set to work scraping the face from the plate - it was an all day job to get it entirely erased, the coarser clean erased portions of plate hammered up, ground laid & gelatine tracing made. MacKellar took part of the m't arranging for the cutting of gravel mon't to be put up on Greenwood lot. Home to lunch at 1 - back at 2 & worked till about 4 - then it was so dark that I could no longer see to work - home & had a rest before dinner. Jim & Ralph came home during the afternoon for their Thanksgiving vacation - both went out at once to do "shopping". Dinner at 6.30 - We & Ralph went with a young school fellow to Wallack's Theatre to see "the Crisis" - I went to regular meeting of Com. on Admissions at the Century - the meeting was so protracted that I left soon after 11 o'clk - knowing that a longer session in the badly ventilated room would unfit me for the work that I must do to-morrow - I got to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 27, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thanksgiving - Ralph [Ret?] to Hackley Hall

Dull & dark all day altho' efforts to clear commenced before noon - I got to studio about 10 - got a successful transfer of gelatine tracing of Morgan head and drew it up on the plate under many disadvantages, chief among them being a sort of nervous hopelessness of being able to do any better than I have already done. I cannot tell till it is bitten whether it is better or worse - Varnished back of plate & home to lunch at 1 - to studio at 3.30 & completed varnishing plate, now ready for the acid. Eve'g dined with "the family" at Charle's. Rec. his wife & three boys were there - Mary & her [Bessie?] (K. was sick) & Kate - Walter, Pierce & Minnie & [her?] mother (young Walter was at the Theatre), my two boys & I were the guests - at 9.25 Ralph, Val & I went to Grand Central Station - the boys took 9.40 train for Tarrytown & I returned & completed my dinner - It was nearly midnight when we got home.
