## Left Page

### Wed. July 9, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful m't. very warm - weather changed gradually - rain in afternoon \+ ev'g. my ills are growing gradually less - stiff neck \+ headache gone - soreness of mouth, less - \+finger healing altho' much swollen \+ inflamed. Trumbull came about the middle of the m't \+ brought a small chest of tools \+ cleaned out under north piazza for a workshopp. I bossed the work - stretched paper to make plans - printed photos. \+ toned them \+ got rid of time in other ways. Dinner at 1 - afternoon T- hung the slat doors on upper piazza - Dennis dug holes \+ filled them with sod, manure \+ loam in preparation for transplanting vines from W. Maghee. Ev'g. some reading \+ to bed early.

Trumbull came
worked part of day.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 10, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy \+ continuous rain during the night - clearing during the m't - growing quite cool - brilliant afternoon \+ moonlight ev't. - Glad to have a fire on the hearth at night - Trumbull worked on seats in main room \+ finished them in the m't Men to work at getting in new sash \+ plate of glass in my room - finding several unexpected difficulties to overcome. I had all that I could attend to in looking after him \+ Dennis \+ laying out work for them - After dinner I went to hotel for mail. \+ at 5.30 Ralph \+ I walked to P.W. Otis's to stir him up about work promised but not performed. Then tea - ev't. newspapers \+ a promenade on piazza before going to bed.
