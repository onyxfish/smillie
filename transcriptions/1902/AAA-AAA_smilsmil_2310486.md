## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 18, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Early m'f., dubious weather - but cleared before noon to beautiful day - fine quiet moonlight night. Trumbull came \+ worked all day putting up cornice in my bed room - I put up the last two pieces of muslin \+ sent for Adams to complete the Kalsomining I followed Trumbull most of the day but made time in the afternoon to wander for some time over the hill called Windy brow. Eve'g. Mr. \+ Mrs. Maghee came with a couple of young men, Mr. Muirs \+ m[illegible]. I spent the eve'g. in my studio when not walking in the moonlight on the upper piazza.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 19, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the early m'f. \+ again at about 8 o'c after which it slowly cleared to a beautiful day - brilliant cool moonlight - Trumbull did not come till 10 o'clk - most of my m'f was devoted to writing - wrote to Imma Ireland \+ to Chas. Collins - Afternoon I worked with Trumbull \+ successfully dampened, stretched \+ tacked in place a sheet of sized burlaps over the chimney breast in my bed room - then Ralph \+ I had a little ramble over Windy brow. Eve'g. Katharine, Elizabeth \+ the boys went to an entertainment at St. Hubert's managed by Miss Robinson \+ others for the benefit of the Keene Heights Library. The old folks stayed at home.
Dennis also stayed at home — too many green apples.

I had a serious talk with Jim this m'f. Bessie has been tutoring him in German \+ he has behaved badly — He will not take any interest in the work.
