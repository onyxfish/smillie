## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 24, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day — one of the most charming of the summer for cloud \+ sunlight effects over the valley — I lay long in my bed in the m'f. watching the wonderfully beautiful effects of sunshine \+ rising clouds over Giant — much of m'm'f given to writing a letter to Mary Coati — About 1 went to Hotel for mail but at 4.30 it was not yet distributed — so, Ret. to home \+ dinner — After noon, with the boys I went to Harry Reede's — then we walked around by Indians camps — Kae Hillard's, Weston's, the Hotel \+ home —
I took no supper till 9 o'clk —
Late in the afternoon old W. Van Horn \+ all of his double household \+ three or four others gathered on the north piazza — Jim \+ the girls went to the Maghee camp in the evef.

Cha's. Moody commenced his engagement

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 25, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. but the inevitable shower came about noon with no recurrence later. Trumbull got to work on partition to board up across alcove in my bed room, separating the plumbing section from the sleeping part. I wrote notes to bro. Charl to W. S. Miller \+ after 11 went to P. O. with them. Then down to Harry Reede's \+ home at about noon — Dinner at 1 — Afternoon I chased Trumbull until about 4.30 then started with the boys for a walk but was called back to meet W. Allan Marquand who had come — He made a pleasant call of 20 m. or half an hour — Then with Jim \+ Ralph I walked out on the Chafee Pond road about a mile \+ a half — home to tea at 6.30 —
Evef. loafed with news paper —

Moody this m'f begged off for a couple of days to get in hay that is out — He will do the chores —
