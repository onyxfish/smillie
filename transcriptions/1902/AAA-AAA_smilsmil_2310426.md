## Left Page

### Fri. April 18, 1902

**Weather:** Fine day, but very cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, but very cool — Got a letter from Jim begging for money — sent him a note + check for $10. To studio at 10 — + spent the day in most discouraging work on Morgan head — I cannot get the likeness — Afternoon I went to Brooks Bro. + bought a strong overcoat + pair of trousers. Evef wrote to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 19, 1902

**Weather:** Beautiful day but very cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day but very cool — To studio at 10 + put another day on the Morgan face — I have given up the hope of making it a workmanlike piece of etching or engraving — I have yet a fragment of hope that I may make a passable likeness — I find now, when too late, that I failed to comprehend certain qualities of effect in the face — the gradation of light + shade from top to bottom + the delicate shadings that give the anatomical structure, consequently I did not plan the work correctly + lay in my underwork intelligently. I'm too old now to learn. I had my usual amt. of loafing about the streets + bought a hat + pair of gloves. Home to dinner at 6.30 — + ruef with a double copy of the Evey Post.
