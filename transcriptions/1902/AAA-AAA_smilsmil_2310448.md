## Left Page

### Sun. June 1, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day, but warm — Up at 6 — Bkfst. before 8 — Then to G. C. Station — met Charl \+ together we went to Po'k. on the 8.45 train getting there at about 10.30 after a comfortable ride — we walked up the hill \+ sauntered down Market St. to Eastman Terrace \+ then thro' Garfield Place \+ finally reached Uncle Willie's about noon — all out at church except Kate who was waiting for us — we went through the house \+ had a visit with Kate before the others came in — The old folks seemed to be surprisingly well — Uncle W. is feeble but walks without crutches. Aunt A. is quite deaf but makes no complaint \+ Aunt Jessie hobbles about with a face that looks 60 in spite of her 80 \+ odd years — she is terribly crippled by rheumatism — we had dinner about 1 \+ after 3 Charl \+ I walked about to see the improvements in the Southern borders of P'k — we went to Springside \+ spent some time on the grounds trying to locate old trees \+ etc. Back to tea at 6 — Took 7 o'clk train — an immense one in two sections — crowded so that we had difficulty in getting seats — got home at 9 —

## Right Page

### Mon. June 2, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another fine day — hot — Bkfst. at 8.30 — many things kept me from studio till 11.30 — I consulted with Mary about kitchen provisio[ns] for Keene \+ went to Steinmenfeld's \+ ordered a lot of things. Then to Mark's with Ralph's coat \+ to studio. Worked on Morgan Plate till 1 o'clk. getting it ready for Proving to-morrow m'g. Then home to lunch — again to studio — laid rebiting ground over part of Morgan \+ gave it some acid — then I worked for some time making a good bevel on the plate — home at about 6 — found M'r Holt there in answer to a note that I wrote him this m'g. I asked him a number of questions about Credent who was in command of his squadron when it went to Porto Rico — Dinner at 6.30 — W'f Geo. came in for a little while. Carlson came at 9.30 \+ we went through the regular performance.
