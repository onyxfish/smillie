## Left Page

### Mon. April 14, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mable Cooley ret? home.

more clouds - less sunshine + con-
siderable wind with much dirt.
To studio at 9.30 - made gelatine tracing
of Morgan head - transferred it to plate
\+ drew in the face with point - This for the
third time - + I feel very shaky over it -
varnished plate - laid rebiting ground
over a portion of O'Malley's plate that will
be much better if it gets more color — and
an etching ground over Connelly's plate
that needs a few added lines in some
places - Then had a walk on Park Ave
before going home to lunch - To studio
soon after 2 - working with acid till 5 o'cl.
Rebit on O'Malley's plate + added the lines to
Connelly's plate + nearly finished the biting of
the Morgan head - then walked down to
Steinways w. 14th S. + made enquiries as to Pianos
in moving piano to Keane Heights + leaving it there
over winter - was told it could be moved with
little risk but they did not think it could be left
over winter in a cold house without detriment.
Home to dinner at 6.30 - Mable Cooley left this
afternoon - Evening as usual - the Post for
an hour or so - then a little letter writing -
then a half hours walk on Park Ave - + to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 15, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely day - to studio at 9.30 + worked till
after 12 on Morgan head - with graver and
dry point - Then to Ins. Locke's - about cur-
tains + cushions for Keene that he is fixing
Then to lunch - It was 3 when I got to
Studio - owing to headache - worked till
5.30 - got paper wet for printing to-morrow.
Then a short walk on Park Ave. + home
to dinner - Eve\*. Charl came in for
a while - + then George - I wrote letter
to W. Will Jenkins of 11 new Court, Lincolns
Inn. London. W. C. in reply to letters from
him asking for pp. from Am. Etchings to
be reproduced + publ? in The Studio - of
London. I cannot now think of any
thing I have that I would be willing
to send him - + do not know of other
etchers who are more likely to have.
