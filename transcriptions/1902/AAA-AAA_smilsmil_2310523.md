## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 4, 1902

**Weather:** Very pleasant, mild \+ hazy  **Temp:** [illegible]

It was after 10 when I got to studio — I worked till near 1 o'clk — trying to get a good print from the J.P. Morgan head — then I gave up in despair — It is a perfect ogre — an unmitigated failure — I cannot decide what to do about it. I have not courage enough to scrape the head off the plate — \+ that is what I should do — At 1 o'clk. I went to the Polls in 87th just east of 5 ave. \+ voted for Odell — then home to lunch — after that I worked over my accts till near 3. \+ balanced my bank acct. Then I walked up to Geo.'s studio — Madison Ave. \+ 60th St. \+ found him in — Nellie came in soon after — we chatted together till near 5 o'clk then G. \+ I walked in Central Park till near 6 — some lovely twilight views — then home — dinner at 6.30 — At 7 Bessie Jane came \+ with the girls went to Chas's to dinner — later in the eve'g. Mary \+ Kate followed there.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 5, 1902

**Weather:** Overcast m'g — clearing to mild, quiet \+ hazy  **Temp:** [illegible]

When I got to studio I studied the prints taken yesterday in comparison with the large photo. \+ concluded that if the defect in the eyes could be corrected I might save the rest of the face so I at once scraped out the eyes \+ laid them in again with the graver. Home to lunch \+ continued the work in the afternoon \+ took some prints \+ also took courage — I may yet pull it thro'. A short walk \+ then home at 5. Had a rest before dinner — Eve'g. Kate \+ I went to Murray Hill Theatre to see "Him on the Bristol" — Mr Donnelly took the part of the widow O'Brien \+, big as he is, did it fill it. The whole affair was low grade \+ commonplace — Two acrobats, Scott \+ Wilson, may be excepted — they were good but not exceptionally so. Home at 11 o'clk.
