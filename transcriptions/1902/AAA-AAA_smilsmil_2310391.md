## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 5, 1902

**Weather:** Very clear & very cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very clear & very cold - to studio about 10 & found studio uncomfortably cold so that I could not begin my etching - for an hour or more I worked at starting a portrait of father based upon photo - taken by Charl on father's 75th birthday - After 11 I started etching of 24. Jos. Morgan & soon became so interested that I found it hard to stop for lunch - I walked awhile on the sunny side of Park Ave - to studio again at 2.30 & worked on etching till 4.30 when I had every thing drawn in but the face - Then I went to Keppel's to get his advice upon a point concerning my proposed mezzatint plate after Wiles' Julia Marlowe He advises against plain prints - take 200 fine p[illegible] - then destroy the plate - Home to dinner at 6.30 - We[illegible] with newspaper until "Wooly" Carmalt came - then I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 6, 1902

**Weather:** Bright, cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold day - to studio at 10 - so cold that I went to 2d. Nat. Bk instead of going to work - deposited Scribner cht. payment for Borden plate - also Ryau cht - then to studio - worked for awhile on portrait sketch of father started yesterday - Then on etching of Morton & finished the laying in of that - then had a short walk on Park Ave. & home to lunch - back to studio & started the etching of J. P. Morgan - worked till 4.30 - then home & had a rest before dinner - We[illegible] was preparing to dress to go to Mr. Oppenheim's music Party with Bessie when she gave out, saying she was too sick to go - then I was also too sick & I wrote & mailed a note to Mr. [illegible] - stating the case - later our neighbors Mrs. Mrs. Wedel came in & made a pleasant call.
