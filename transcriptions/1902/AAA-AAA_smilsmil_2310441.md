## Left Page

### Sun. May 18, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up rather early, for Sunday — finding a pleasant day I decided to go to Tarrytown to see Ralph — As soon as dressed I went to Charl's \+ telephoned to Hackley Hall \+ asked Mr. Williams if I might dine there — home \+ had left — took 10.45 train — Ralph was at the station \+ together we walked up the hill — finding it a pretty warm day — we loafed about till 1 o'clk then dinner — After that R. \+ I walked to the new buildings \+ about in the woods till 4.30 — then back to "Chapel" — Russell Bellows addressed the boys — I had a short talk with him after it — again R. \+ I went off — he returned to his supper at 6.30 \+ I went to 6.54 train \+ got home at 8 — had something to eat — read newspapers \+ to bed early —

## Right Page

### Mon. May 19, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Foggy, raw weather with a very little drizzle — To studio at 10 \+ worked for a couple of hours on Morgan Place — I see that now I have got nearer to the proper expression of the eyes — Mr. Andrew M. Adger called with letter from Fred K. — [illegible] introducing him — He wishes to take lessons in Etching \+ spent some time talking about it — Home to lunch at near 2 \+ back in an hour — worked on Morgan till 4.30 — then to Gleason's — Cor. Houston \+ Mercer — for Glass Globe for Hall light ordered nearly a month ago — not done yet — walked up home — dinner at 6.30 itself — Charl came in — he was followed by W. Aug. Meyer — Carlson came at 9.30 \+ was with me till near 11.
