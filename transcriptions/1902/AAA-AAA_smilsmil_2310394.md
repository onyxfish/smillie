## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 11, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine cold winter day. To studio at 10 - worked on portrait of Morton - finished the biting - then with graver. Soon after 12 I went to Calverly's - he is about to make changes in the bust he made of me - to put a less formal neck-tie + coat collar on me - Home to lunch at 1.30 - to studio at 2.30 - worked till 5 - then home - made some change in dress - then to the reception of Mr. Graham Lusk - one of Louis Tiffany's daughters - a delightful little woman. Then to 101 E. 69 St. hoping to find Clarence Smith at home - I wish to consult him about personal matters - not in - walked home - dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g went again to C. Smith's - again not in - walked down to 38th St - to Charl's to tell him that Mackeller had been to see me to-day about monument etc for Greenwood - home before 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 12, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - altho' cold + cloudy. To studio at 10 + spent the way working with graver on Morton, getting it ready for a proof. It now looks, on the plate, quite finished - Home to lunch at 1.30 - back at 2.30 + worked till 4.30 - Went to Clausen's + found him alone + in very talkative mood. He told me some strange stories about art-dealers + their methods - especially of Durand-Ruel - who sells foreign pictures - mainly modern French impressionists - taking in exchange American + other paintings - these he sells to Clausen very cheap - an Innis, for instance, from Potter Palmer at one fifth of what P. P. paid for it - Question - what profit did Durand-Ruel make on the French picture - most part of the eve I loafed in front of the fire - then, a little writing
