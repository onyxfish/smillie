## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 2, 1902

**Weather:** Overcast & milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast & milder — to studio at 10 \+ painted till near 2 o'clk. on Borden Library. Then home \+ had lunch — then to Private View — N.A. of D. — Fine Arts Bldg — 57'5: I think 1 one of the most interesting exbns of the Academy that I remember. The portrait of Miss Marlowe by Irving Wiles was the surprise of the exbn to me. I walked down to 43d S. with Harry Watrous — Home before 6 — dinner. Eve'g at home enjoying quiet of fireside — [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 3, 1902

**Weather:** Cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold & windy — some rain & snow fee last night \+ froze so that this m'g the conditions under foot were very bad — I went to Douglas's — Miller's \+ Ryans before I went to studio \+ left checks. Painted on Borden interior till 12.45 — then to Bank — nearly 1.30 when I got home to lunch — (Jonne Bamberger — a Morse school fellow of Jim's — lunched with us — To studio \+ painted as long as I could see — then home \+ had a rest before dinner — Eve'g. Jim, Ralph \+ I went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw Hoyt's Brass Monkey — laughable nonsense — not very improving to taste or manners.
