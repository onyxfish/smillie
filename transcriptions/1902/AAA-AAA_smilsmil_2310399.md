## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 21, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim \+ Ralph came home

A day of rain \+ sleet \+ snow \+ high wind I did not get up till afternoon having been much disturbed after 1 a.m. by the dose of Castor Pills \- feeling very weak \+ wretched. Ralph was not expected before dinner time but he came about 3 o'clk when I got up to dress \- I went to the parlor where there was a pleasant fire but feet so out of sorts that I went back to bed before dinner. \- Jim came from New Haven \- a surprise to us all \- the storm increased towards night \+ a leak developed in the roof at exit to leader. Jim went to the roof to shovel away the snow but only seemed to make matters worse \- soon the water came faster \+ at last poured down the walls thro' Ralph's room, down the hall way: tho' Mattie's room, flooding his bath room o fairly, at last, obliging her to abandon it \- Everything was topsy turvy \- Mr. Wedell's man came \+ I hoeveled more snow off the roof \+ pots, pans, pails \+ jugs were placed about to catch the running water \+ carpets were torn up \- I was in bed \+ could do nothing, but was sure that if I could go on the roof I could find some way of diverting the floods \- Dr. Cilley called about 4 o'clk, he seems to think that my trouble is indigestion without complications \- a confusing diarroea has kept up all day. \- I feel extremely weak \- There was much excitement thro' the house all night \- many of the boys were emptying pots \+ pans \+ jars at intervals \- I tried to compose myself to sleep at about 11 o'clk in spite of the running water at my bedroom door that sounded as tho' a croton pipe had broken.

Dull, disagreeable day with some snow \- clearing late at night. Numerous \+ whistling fire engines added to the other noises \+ confusion in the house roused me at about 2 a.m. the sky to the south seemed ablaze \- it was a fearful sight.
