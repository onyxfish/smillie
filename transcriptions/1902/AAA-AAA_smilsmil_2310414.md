## Left Page

### Tues. March 25, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day — crisp + cool — Got a much disgusted letter from Jerd. Beede — He is mad because of the letter I wrote him about a week ago concerning coal — also one from Scate Brown saying that Otis was going to have four Tons for me altho' he knew that Beede had already hauled it for me — So I wrote to all three — That kept me till near 11 o'clk — To studio + found that D. Gerster had telephoned to me to say that he would be at the studio with a friend about 2 o'clk. I worked till 1 o'clk. putting studio in order. I have a very bad habit of rarely putting a thing back in its place after I have used it — so have to put things to rights semi-occasionally — To lunch all — Salus — all hands went to Newark to lunch — To studio at 2 — D. Gerster came with friend — Think the name was Rosengarten. + until after 4 they looked over proofs of my work — At 5 I went to meeting at Hotel Manhattan, Fraternity holders on Park Ave. + all adjacent. A full meeting — Jas. Stokes steering — Read a very thin-witted statement — meeting organized by electiing A. H. Green Chairman — J. W. Devoe Read Resolutions — Very little discussion — It appears that tunnel on east side has been carried without permit or knowledge of authorities or property holders, under the [illegible] line — Com. of [illegible] appointed empowered to stop tunnel work + make investigation — find who is to pay for the damage. Adjourned — a short walk — then home to dinner. Quiet every at home doing some writing.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 26, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim arr? from New Haven.

Charming day — went to Krummel + Voigt's immediately after bkft — Had four p[hoto]s. taken from Gov. Morton plates — quite satisfactory — but was kept waiting so long + the place was so cold that I was quite chilled — I would not wait to see the proving of the Borden library plates commenced. 15 idia, large fo[illegible], are to be taken — but walked all the way up to studio getting pleasantly warm. Packed the Jan p[hoto]s down + then home to lunch — Found Jim there — just arr. from New Haven — home for Easter Holidays. After lunch went to barbers + had hair cut + head shampooed — then to Dr. Celley's. To get result of last analysis of urine. He says the result is — Normal — my age being taken into consideration — very few casts — a trace of albumen — closest tests — no sugar — Indices, evidence of improper intestinal fermentation + an insufficient amount of urea — Then to studio + arranged + assorted prints till near 6 — Dinner — Evely at 8.30 went to Century — meeting of Com. on admissions — Home at about 11 o'clk —
