## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 8, 1902

**Weather:** Quite cool - overcast most of the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

again I have tackled Morgan. This struggle is humiliating \+ very depressing to me — so much resultless labor \+ anxiety, when time is precious, over a face that should \+ could have been done in two or, at most, three days argues an exceedingly small measure of ability \+ sure decadence. I worked with graver — \+ without abi[lity] — doing \+ undoing — \+ taking prints to make manifest my continued failure. At 12 I went to Schencking's took bindery \+ saw the son \+ asked for a reduction on my bill for binding files of War Tribune. — referred to the father — then home to lunch to studio at 2 \+ worked till 5 — then to Wanamaker's to look for basket grate for my bedroom — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Mary at Charles' — Eve[ning]. George came in for a little while.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 9, 1902

**Weather:** not altogether pleasant - overcast most of the day with raw, northerly wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkp — at about 10 — headachy — It was after 12 when I went to Central Park \+ 105th St. \+ saw the Chrysanthemum show in one of the green houses there — then walked down to 72nd S[t]. \+ mad[e] Ave \+ took car — stopped at Chase's \+ got time — Dinner at 2 — after that walked up to 57 W. 57th S[t]. — Chase's Art School, \+ saw Portrait of him by Sargent — then to Studio \+ wet paper for printing to-morrow — then home \+ rested till 7 — then wrote a note to E.C. Stedman — then to the Irelands in E. 17th S[t]. \+ saw there Catharine \+ Mary who have just ret[urned]. from their journeyings abroad — Home before 10 — Chase there.
