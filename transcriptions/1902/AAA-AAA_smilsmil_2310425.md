## Left Page

### Wed. April 16, 1902

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — had troubles last night on account, this m'g. before 4 o'clk. which put me out of sorts + left me with an aching head all day — but I nevertheless put in a full days work at printing — most of the time was given to plates by Etching Class — O'Malley, Connolly, etc. Last Sat. I thought the first prize w'd go to C — but now, after proving their plates, O'Malley's comes out best. Both of the plates are very interesting to me — I took two prints from Morgan — the new head is still far from satisfactory — I stopped work after 5 + amused myself walking about the streets till after 6 — then home to dinner — a couple with a very heavy head. wrote to Prof. Comfort of Syracuse asking for the return of lectures loaned to the museum there — also to E. P. Pafee asking him to ship to me the boxes + crates of mine in Mr. Kent's barn.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 17, 1902

**Weather:** lovely spring  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely spring day — got up with headache that finally floor'd me. I went to studio + made pkg of Etching class plates preparatory to taking them to the printers — then I made pkg of Elson Samuels proofs + went to Adam's 44 p. in 142 St + sent the pkg to Boston — then I wandered about the streets till 1.30 — then I went to drug store + had a castor oil cock tail, then home + went to bed — the act was very slow in boring its way through taking from 9 to 12 hours — the last bout being at 12.30 A.M. at 10 P.M. I had a bowl of hot clam broth — soon after get up — to bed at about 1 A.M.
