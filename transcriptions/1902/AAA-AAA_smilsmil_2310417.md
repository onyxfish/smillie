## Left Page

### Mon. March 31, 1902

**Weather:** Sunshine - showers + wind - not a very pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Taken all together after 4&p. I went to H. Joo. Morton's + had a very satisfactory interview with him - He expressed himself as very much pleased with my etched portrait of him - likes the expression better than that of the original painting by Bonnat - Then to Buckingham + saw Mr. Kent for a few minutes - then to studio + at last got to work on Morgan plate drew in new lines on face + extended the background - varnished plates - home to lunch - back at 2.30 - bit in work of the mf - cleaned plates + worked with graver till 6.15 - then home to dinner - Evey with the boys - Jim is somewhat better \+ Temp. a little above 100°. in the m'f + 99° at night - owing to his being out of sorts - + the Rain, we did not go out in the evef

## Right Page

### Tues. April 1, 1902

**Weather:** Cloudy, windy + cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary went to Po'K. for a visit

to studio about 9.30 + worked all day on Morgan portrait. Spent some time getting a new graver ground into satisfactory shape - then spent all the time working on the face - At noon went to Scribner's + saw Mr. Smith. He told me that Mr. Borden is not willing to have his paintings taken down - out of frames - + out of the house, to be photo'd - So the ill. cat. of his paintings is given up - but he has given me an order to do three plates - that is - one of the south end of his library - The part that does not appear in the plate I have already done - one of the new library room, jotted up in the old music room - one, a view in the picture gallery - came to lunch - then to studio + worked with graver on Morgan head hopefully - altho' I have not yet got quite what I want. I had a short walk before dinner +in the Evey took the boys to the Eden Musee - a very stupid sort of an entertainment - I had expected some amusement from the cinematograph affair but they were so bad - jiggling + jerking about so that my eyes were almost blinded - + then, they were run so rapidly - but for such defects they would have been amusing - It was about 11 when we got home
