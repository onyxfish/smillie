## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 10, 1902

**Weather:** Nasty day. wet snow falling.\*  **Temp:** [illegible]

was at Allens at 9.30 \+ was there till noon. He took the metal cap from one of my teeth \- cutting the tooth down considerably \+ excavating a cave on the faug\[ht\] pacity below the gum \- filling it \+ then placing a new cap over the tooth. It was a very painful process \+ when he let me off for the day I was quite used up. Something went wrong with my watch \+ it stopped for 50 minutes. I went to Prayerdorfen's \+ he removed a tiny black speck of something from the interior saying it had possibly got between the wheels \+ stopped the movement. Then home to lunch \- then to Studio \- but I felt so uncomfortable that I cal\[led\]. home \- went to bed \+ had a restful nap. Eve\[ning\] I wrote to Jim \+ Ralph \- mailed the letters before 10 \+ then went to Charles' there till 11 \-

\* the snow was no more than a few flying flakes \- there was a little sunshine in the m\[ornin\]g \+ a little starlight at night.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 11, 1902

**Weather:** Disagreeable day \- m\[ostly\] dark,  **Temp:** [illegible]

damps \+ threatening \- at one time a snow flurry \- Spent m\[ornin\]g at Studio most of the time preparing Asphaltum \+ Burgundy Pitch to make Etching gr\[ound\] at the class this afternoon \- Alice McCane called \+ for half an hour we talked of family matters \- cross currents \+ ill winds. She came to urge me to have Jim come on from New Haven to attend Catharine Cook's wedding as Mary Cook had set her heart on it. Home to lunch at 1 \- there to N.A\[cademy\] \+ met a lot of young men who wish to join Etching class \- I made up a lot of Etching ground while they made free sketches from old Geo. Duffy. All of the sketches were unsatisfactory \- slovenly \+ sketchy \- I told them that I was going to insist upon close carefully studied drawing \+ that next Sat. they must make from life some outline drawings. It was about 5 when I left. I had intended to go to Mr. Louis Tiffany's Reception but felt too tired \+ dirty \- so went directly home meeting Katharine \+ Elizabeth on Mad. Ave. \+ s\[ee\] Returning from Tiffany's. Dinner \[at\] 8.30 to Annual meeting of Century \- Spent a pleasant eve\[nin\]g till near midnight. Was elected member of Com. on Admissions for a term of three years \-

First session \- Etching class.
