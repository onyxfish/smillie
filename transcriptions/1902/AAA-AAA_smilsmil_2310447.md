## Left Page

### Fri. May 30, 1902

*Decoration day.*

**Weather:** Bright but strong southerly wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright but strong southerly wind made it muggy in spite of the moving air — Got up with a headache that made me very uncomfortable all day. I spent the m'f at the studio fussing about but not able to do anything that amounted to much — finished drawing of seat arms for Keene — Home to lunch \+ after that I gave up to headache \+ spent the afternoon on my bed — at dinner time ate my lunch \+ even'g wrote letters to Jim \+ Ralph under much discomfort.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 31, 1902

**Weather:** Beautiful day, brilliantly clear, breezy \+ cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day, brilliantly clear, breezy \+ cool — waked at about 4.30 — headache worse than yesterday but under influence of drugs I was able to get up. After bkft. I went to Miller's with drawings for seat arms \+ left them with him — then to studio — then to Steinway's \+ left order to send for Annie's piano to box \+ send to Keene — then to K. \+ V's — Canal S'. \+ made appointment for next Tues. m'f. Then to Wanamaker's \+ purchased a lot of things for Keene — Home to lunch — after that spent two hours on my bed nursing my head — then to 42° S'. \+ bought some Hardware for Keene — then to Century \+ paid semi-annual dues — then again to studio \+ home to dinner at 6.30 — with Mary looking over my under clothes \+ other things for Keene. Carlson came at 9.30 \+ gave me good work.
