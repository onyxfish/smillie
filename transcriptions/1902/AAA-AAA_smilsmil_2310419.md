## Left Page

### Fri. April 4, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still mildly unpleasant - a headache that has lasted for three days made me determine to do no studio work to-day. I did some writing after bkft. - then with Ralph went to Mad. Sq. Garden \+ after standing on a long line for some time succeeded in getting two good seats for the Circus to-night - Then we went to the bank - then R- left me \+ I went to Scribners \+ got from Mr. Smith the large photos of Mr. Ordenis Library \+ gallery - then to Calverly's \+ sat for him till 1 o'clk - he is modeling a new bust of me - then home to lunch - After lunch - my head still hurting - I had a long nap that greatly relieved me. After 4 o'ck - I went with Ralph to Rogers Peet \+ Co's \+ he bought a shoring suit - then to Baerendorfer's - then home - Dinner at 6.30 Eva \+ R \+ D went to the Circus at Mad \+ g. garden

## Right Page

### Sat. April 5, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

mary ret. from Po'k;
chase ret. from Florida.

me mild sunshine - Passably pleasant. After bkft. went to N.A. 4 S. - 5th Ave. \+ 109 S. - to arrange with etching class for the competition next week. Found a full class at work drawing upon the plate from model. - I arranged details \+ am to be at the school next Monday P.M. Left there at 12 \+ walked across the park to Mad. ave. \+ 102. S. \+ thence home by car - lunch - feeling very heavy \+ headachy much of the afternoon was given over to sleep but first I went to studio for the second time scraped out the head of J.P. Morgan on etched plate - It was his perhaps that I found so depressing that at half past three I had to go home \+ to bed - dinner at 6.30 - Eve \+ at 8.30 I went to Charl's for a few minutes - he got back from his Southern trip this afternoon - then to Century - monthly meeting - had a fairly pleasant time - home at midnight -
