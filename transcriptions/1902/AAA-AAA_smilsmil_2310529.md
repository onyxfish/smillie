## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 16, 1902

**Weather:** overcast, as usual, with some weak sunshine later in the day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I took the 10.45 train for T. Town — got there on time — walked part way up the hill \+ got up on the Trolley car that overtook me \+ rode to gate at Hackley Hall, getting there a little after 12. Dinner at 1 \+ after that I had a conference with Mr. Williams in his study concerning Ralph \+ his studies. I told him how anxious I am that R. shall be thoroughly taught the foundations or elements of a general knowledge \+ that I feared he did not have enough of such simple \+ rudimentary studies as Arithmetic, Grammar, Geography. He looked over the report of last month that I gave to him \+ said that he thought that I was in some measure right — he is doing nothing of all now in the way of mathematics — he has not yet got a place in the chemistry class — nor in mechanics — this is partly the fault of school schedule of recitations, causing interference of recitations \+ partly, I fear, owing to delay \+ forgetfulness on part of W. W. — he promises to take action at once — our interview was pleasant \+ he gave me a copy of his most recent translation — The Æneid — After that R. \+ I wandered in the wood \+ went into the remodeled barn where a lot of the boys \+ some of the masters were playing at [illegible] base ball — at 4.30 we went to Chapel — I slipped out at 5 — walked part way to station in pleasant twilight — \+ by 5.36 train home at 6.30 — tea — Evay wrote some notes etc —

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 17, 1902

**Weather:** Dull day with a raw north easterly wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Peter came from Regan's \+ put in gas log in fire place in my bed room — This kept me in till about 10.30 — then Prof. Stahr came — he had been to studio — the first I have seen of him since his return from Spain — he has been abroad some years nearly — he is yet very enthusiastic about etching — he went to studio with me \+ looking over p.p. of work by N.A. students consumed the rest of the m'g. Then I went to Second Nat. Bank \+ home to lunch. After that I went to W. Borden's in W. 56th S. \+ worked about an hour on half a routine. Then home feeling very much out of sorts — Dinner — Avery \+ Charle came in —
