## Left Page

### Sun. May 4, 1902

*Jim returned to New Haven*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day \- B\&p\. before 10 \+ the m\+f got away from me by bits before I knew it \- went to studio \+ cleaned windows \- Then to Charl's \- Then to Century \- there a short walk \- then home to dinner at 2 \- Afternoon feeling the loss of sleep last night I fully make it up between 3.30 \+ 6.30 \-
Evef. Geo\. \+ Charl came in \- I went to R.R. Station at 8 o'clk with Jim \- at 9.30 Carson came \+ I had my first massage \- It was 10.45 when he finished \- an agreeable way of taking exercise \-

## Right Page

### Mon. May 5, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm day \- m\+f at Studio \- most of the time writing letters to Lockwood de Forest \- J.T. Breadeuer \+ others \- At 1 o'clk. went to bank \- then home to lunch \- After that went to Mr. Borden's in 56th S\. \- Preparatory to taking photographs up there to take photo. of the music room Transformed into library annex \- from there to Lenox Library to see some prints from plates by Father recently acquired by the museum \- a miserable, dirty, bedragged lot of worn out imps. Weitenkampf not in \- saw his assistant \- then walked to Met. Mus. \- Spring Reception \- met Mr. Gerster \- a throng at the Museum \- met Geo\. Hearn at the door \- Was glad to get into the new Hall \- It is very noble \- Elevell. of course was there \- as entertaining as ever \- Posing as Curator of Sculpture \- Saw a number of people that I knew \+ got home at 6.30 \- very tired \- Dinner \- evef., some reading \- Charl with us part of evef.
