## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 29, 1902

*Emma J — Eliz. H — left us.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another disagreeable day. Clouds low down in the m'f + some rain. signs of clearing about noon — some pleasant sunshine — then again dark clouds. B+P. at usual time. at 9 Emma + Eliz. left us — Emma to go to Stockbridge. Eliz. to Elizabeth, N. J. Charl + Raegh packing so as to leave to-morrow. I went to P.O. at 11 + got letters from W. Williams of Hackley Hall saying that owing to strikes of mechanics the buildings will not be ready for the opening of school before Oct. 8 — this will delay the departure of Raegh — After some running about arrangements were made by which the servants leave for N.Y. on Friday + two native women take their places to remain + serve till we can close up here satisfactorily — the servants in the mean time cleaning house in 38 th St. This promises a satisfactory solution to what has threatened to be a difficult problem. To P.O. again at 3 o'clk — To-day I did considerable work on The Boden outline — about 5.30 I went down to Harry Beede's to learn what progress he had made in getting out lumber for us — he says he is ready — if Trumbull is not here to-morrow I shall send for him. Moody did chores only to-day on acct. of bad weather — Ev'y did some writing.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 30, 1902

*Charl left us.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant but cloudy m'f. Remarkably mild afternoon, heavier clouds + at night, rain. About 9 o'clk. Charl got off in good shape for N.Y. I had a day of tolerable activity in general preparation to close up here for the season. At 11 o'clk went to P.O. then to look after the work that has been started on retaining wall on dug way road — found Ducharm digging the sand + throwing it over road bed — Mr. Otis hauling stone. Trumbull came for a few minutes in the m'f. I did not see him — in the afternoon his nephew hauled three loads of lumber up from Harry Beede's mill. Moody helped him — he also cut down a number of small pine trees near the house. I met W. + Mrs. Natruan on the road, in the afternoon. + was greatly surprised to learn that Sam. Coleman has just arr. at the Tahawus House — with trained nurse — he went to Arizona alone + was very sick there. Ev'y writing —
