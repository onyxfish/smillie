## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 8, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow all day long, stopping at night This m't at 8½ a frightful accident occurred in N.Y. Cent. RR tunnels at 56' S'. by which 17 people were killed outright \+ many more terribly maimed. It has given me a great shock, coming so soon after my two boys had passed over it. I got to studio at 10. \+ spent another day painting on Borden library \- It is slow work \- there seems to be a spell in the thing that holds me I cannot give it up \+ it seems as tho' I am never to finish it.
Evef. \- as usual, in front of a fire, reading.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 9, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day \- bad walking \- It was after 10 when I got to studio \+ again I spent the day painting at Borden library \- Home to lunch \- back soon after 2 \- painted till 3.30 \- cleaned up \+ went to NA of D\. 109' S'. \+ Amsterdam Ave \- to meet prospective Etching Class \- ten presented themselves, with others enquiring \- I arranged to meet them on Sat. p.m. of each week \- then walked down Central Park West to 77\-8 \- where Samuel Colman has apartments \- saw him for a few minutes \- Mr. C. is wonderfully easier \- can see friends \- not a case of recovery, but retardation \+ relief from pain. Resulting from drugs \+ wise nursing. Home to dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. with newspaper \+ to bed early.
