## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 27, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild pleasant day \- dreadful under foot \- to studio at 10 \- before I got to work Mackellar came \- I went back to the house with him where his book of photos of monuments was \+ it was nearly noon before we got to the end of our discussion about monuments etc \- He is to come again to-morrow \- I worked on morton plate \- Home to lunch \+ back at 2.30 \- I took prints \+ worked between times \- It was after 5 when I took an india proof that seemed to me to be near finish \- Then walked for half an hour in Park Ave. \+ home to dinner \- Evelf. Chase came in \+ we agreed upon the style of enclosure \+ head stones for Greenwood lot \-

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 28, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

J. H. Maghee lunched with us. Amesatwater went home.

A beast of a day \- high wind and heavy rain. \+ I got up with a beast of a headache \- \+ that made the deuce. I worried through the mf \+ managed to "put in" half a day on Morton \- when I got home to lunch, found Maghee at table \- a surprise \- he has just come from Saratoga \- After lunch it rained so heavily \- was so dark \+ my head hurt so that I went to bed \+ had some sleep \- About 5 I went to drug store \+ got a Castor Oil Cocktail \- took no dinner \- Evelf I wrote to Jim \+ Ralph \- Chase came in \+ we talked about head stones, enclosing posts for Greenwood lot \- Mackellar was at the studio this mf \+ I gave him the order for enclosing iron rail but left the matter of head stones open \- Went to Cpt. Cent. Station before 11 \+ posted letters \- Castor oil began to get in to work before midnight \- I did not undress or go to bed till 4 a.m.
