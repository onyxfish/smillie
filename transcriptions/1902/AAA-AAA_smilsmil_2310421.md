## Left Page

### Tues. April 8, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy wind \+ rain all day long \- to studio about 10 \- Then to grocer \+ Butcher \- \+ taking advantage of a lull in the storm, I went to Connell's in 21st S; \+ got photo. reduction (No. 2) of the portrait I painted of Father. Thence to Second Natl. Bank \+ back to studio a little after 11 \- cleaned the surface of scraped out face on Morgan plate \+ preparatory to laying ground \- Home to lunch at 1 \- back at 3 \+ worked with graver on the Morgan figure \- laid ground \+ got together materials for sketching interior at Borden's house to-morrow m'f. I telephoned the N.A, telling the Etching class that I would be there at noon to-morrow to criticise their preliminary drawings \- After 6 when I got home \- dinner \- evef., reading \+ paying accounts \- bal \+ check book etc [illegible — ~3 words] [illegible — ~3 words] [illegible — ~3 words] [illegible — ~3 words]

## Right Page

### Wed. April 9, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very little sunshine in the early m'f. Then clouds \+ heavy rain before noon. After bkf. I wrote notes to Walter Cook \+ Clarence Smith \+ got to studio about 10 \- made gelatine tracing of Morgan head \+ soon after 11 started for N.A. \- got there at noon \+ met a number of etching class students \- commented on their preliminary sketches which are all meagre \+ unsatisfactory \- \+ advised them as to treatment in drawing upon the plate as they will this afternoon \- then home to lunch at 1.2 to studio at 2.5 Transferred Morgan tracing to plate \- \+ drew it in with point \- very unsatisfactory \- shall clean it off \- I am quite disheartened \- gave up at 4 \- walked to Soct of Am. Artists 246 W. 57 S. \+ spent an hour there \- did it find much to enliven me. Eastman Johnson came in \- have not seen him before this winter \- was quite shocked to see how old he looks \- he is uncertain on his feet \- he tells me that he will not take any more orders but is getting his affairs into shape for the end \- perfectly cheerful \- nothing morbid \- Home to dinner at 6.30 \- Read news papers \+ to bed early.
