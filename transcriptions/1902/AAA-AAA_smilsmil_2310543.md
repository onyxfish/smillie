## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 16, 1902

**Weather:** Pouring Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pouring Rain all day with a good deal of wind — It was 10.30 when I got to studio — found the Ther. at 77°. I could not stand that. So I opened the windows as much as I dared — so that rain should not come in — \+ went to Second Nat. B Nk. \+ then to Scribners \+ saw Mr. Smith — then home at 12 lunch at 1 — then to studio \+ till 4.30 was putting things in order preparatory to taking hold of the Borden work again — home at 5 — dinner Eve'g wrote to W. Scott Brown \+ sent check for taxes at Keene that for the year mount up to nearly $90 —

I wrote other notes \+ sent checks in paymt. of bills.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 17, 1902

**Weather:** Clear \+ cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came from New Haven —
Kate came from Robertsie

Clear \+ cold — It was after 10 when I got to studio — found studio Ther. at 56°. — Retaliation perhaps because I complained yesterday because the Ther. was up to 77°. saying that I would rather have my studio too cold than too hot — I at once started up the fire but it was near noon before the Ther. got near 70°. I was, consequently much delayed in beginning work on the Borden library No. 2. I commenced, with water-colors, to put in an effect on the platinum print reduction of the 12×18" some time I made on mill board — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 \+ after working awhile concluded that I could not make a success in that way — then I rubbed a glaze of warm tone — oil tints — over my 12×18 pen sitting, preparatory to beginning to paint it in oil to-morrow — at 5 went to Wanamaker's \+ bought note paper \+ miscellanies for the boys Xmas — Home at 6.30 — Jim got in from New H — just before me — dinner — Eve'g I felt quite under the weather.

B. ½   last mem. of B. work — Nov. 19x
