## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 28, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

tuite cold, very windy \+ consequently very dirty in the streets. After bkf°. I wrote a note to Mr. Williams repeating my request that he give Ralph more study work, especially in Mathematics — the boy has asked for it \+ I have asked \+ Mr. W. has promised — but, so far, has not performed. then to studio \+ got things ready for biting. Then to bank \+ got a loan — then to Jay Office — Chambers S°. \+ paid taxes — then to studio — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 \+ worked till near 5 \+ finished biting the head of J.P. Morgan — then walked up \+ down Park Ave. for half an hour or more — then home — dinner at 6.30 — Eve°. I went to Walter Cook's to congratulate them. Sallie Cook Brown had a boy baby yesterday m°. I saw Walter \+ Louise \+ Louise gave me all of the particulars of the accouchment she having been present.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 29, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — I got up with severe headache of neuralgic character — It troubled me at my work very much through the m°., gradually wearing off — I got to studio about 10 \+ worked until 4.30 (when I could see no longer) at Morgan head, using the graver. then I went home \+ rested for an hour \+ a half — Dinner at 6.30 Eve°. Jine went to an entertainment at Mary Cook's \+ I went to the Century, stopping for Chave on my way — Mr. G.W. Ritchey of Yerkes Observatory gave us an intensely interesting talk on the Revelations of Photography in Astronomy illustrated by a great number of remarkably fine photos. He is a fluent speaker \+ explained so simply \+ fully that it was not necessary to question him. He is an enthusiast in Astronomic photography \+ thinks that the field is of unknown extent \+ but just entered — He showed many amazing [illegible]'s — It was 11 o'clk. when I left.
