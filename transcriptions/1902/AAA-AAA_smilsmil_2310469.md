## Left Page

### Tues. July 15, 1902

**Weather:** Misty, overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Misty, overcast m'f. developing into a series of thunderstorms winding up at about 6 o'clk with a violent gust of wind followed by a rapid fall in temperature \+ a bright moonlight night. In bed, in the m'f, before getting up, I Revised the plan upon which Trumbull is constructing seat by window in my bed room \+ started him upon the work before I went to bkfs. A busy day \- wrote to W. P. Q. of Quebec \- made some progress in drawing plans \- directed Trumbull's work \- Mr Maghee adjusted my small lawn mower so that I could use it \+ after cutting grass on North terrace I raked over \+ seeded the half-grassed ground under the pines at the south front of the house \+ was very tired after it. Eve'g some newspaper reading.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 16, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A splendid bracing day \- cloudy, breezy \+ cool, followed by a superb \+ quiet moon-light night. While in bed, before getting up, I devised another change in window seat \+ before bkfs. started Trumbull at it. I had a busy m'f. looking after T\-, working on new plans \+ writing to Miller \+ to Charl. Bessie \+ the boys tramped to the Giants' Wash-bowl in the m'f. \+ dinner was delayed till 1.30 \- after that I went to Hotel \+ mailed letters \+ then walked in the Reserve to the 2 miles Mark \- enjoying it very much. Home before 4.30 having walked 5 miles without feeling fatigue. Oiled \+ stained some new wood work \- Trumbull finished the seat. Tea at 6.30 \- Eve'g with newspapers mainly.
