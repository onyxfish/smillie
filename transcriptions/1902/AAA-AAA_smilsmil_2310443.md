## Left Page

### Thur. May 22, 1902

**Weather:** Bright day, growing warmer  **Temp:**

To studio at 10 \+ spent more dreary hours trying to make something of the Morgan plate. At 12 went to Bank \+ deposited E. M. Cooks checks. Then home to lunch - to studio at 2.30 \+ for two hours painted \+ worked on Morgan without getting satisfactory results - I am altogether discouraged \+ am ready to abandon the plate as a bad job - It has been a very expensive one - Home at 5 \+ rested till 6 - then dressed - dinner at 7 - Wm. Mulford \+ Woolsey Carnall came on invitation of Katharine \+ Eliz't. We left the table at near 9 \+ I went at once to Emma Ireland's \+ was there for about an hour. She accepted invitation to visit the Ark at Keene next Summer \+ named the latter part of Sep't.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 23, 1902

**Weather:** A sweltering hot day  **Temp:**

To studio at 10 untill 12 worked upon drawing of end arms for seat in large room at Keene - then went to Bank - met Geo. There - home to lunch - After that went to Russell \+ Erwin large Hardware dealers in Chambers street. Had bronze knobs fixed \+ left order for some other things needed - then to studio, getting there bet. 3 \+ 4 - worked on arm drawings untill near 5 - when I satisfied myself that I had made a mis calculation as to proper proportions - so that my work for three or four hours is lost - home \+ rested till dinner - Even'g. writing to Jim - newspaper \+ preparation for Carlson. D. \+ Mrs. E. P. Smith made a very pleasant little call, coming in just after a dash of rain that caught me when I went out to mail letter to Jim. Carlson came at usual time \+ gave a pretty thorough treatment.
