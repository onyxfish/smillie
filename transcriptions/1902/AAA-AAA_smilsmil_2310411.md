## Left Page

### Wed. March 19, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

High wind \- weather bureau reports 72 miles an hour \- snow flying most of the day \- very light \- none remaining \- At studio before 10 \- made gelatine tracing of portrait of father \+ f small head of J.P. Morgan \- laid ground on plate for father's portrait \- after lunch I transferred the two tracings successfully \+ drew the lines upon father's portrait till after 4 \- then I went to Scribner's \+ spent nearly an hour considering various plans \+ propositions for the Borden et. of pictures \- nothing was decided \- I am to make enquiries about Photo-gravure \+ other processes \- Home at 6.15 \- We\+ wrote to Scott Brown for Beede \+ J.W. Otis about hauling 4 tons of coal to house at Keene Heights \-

## Right Page

### Thur. March 20, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Milder than yesterday \+ less wind \- partly clear \- partly cloudy \- To studio at 9.30 \- continued work drawing in lines on plate \- father's portrait \- got all in by 11 o'clk \- then varnished plate \+ got ready for biting \- at 12 went to Sou. Art Ass'n \+ saw Kirby \- he gave me address of Boston firm that does good Photo-gravure work \- then to Scribners \+ saw M. Smith for a moment \- home to lunch at 1 \- back to studio at 2.30 \+ spent the p.m. biting \- used nitric. with which I am not familiar, \+ because of that I did not get all that I wished \- the nitric bites a white line \+ is so much more rapid than Dutch Mordant that I do not know where I am at. It will give me added trouble \- a short walk before dinner \- We\+ wrote to the Boston firm \- A.W. Elson \+ Co \- 146 Oliver St \+ to miss Evalyn Lee who wants some help in painting a portrait.
