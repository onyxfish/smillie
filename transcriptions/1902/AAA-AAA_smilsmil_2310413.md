## Left Page

### Sun. March 23, 1902

**Weather:** Cloudless & very warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless & very warm - Got up with a beast of a headache that made me miserable all day - went to studio & opened windows to air the place - then to Charle's \+ wound his clocks - then rode to 110th St walked to cathedral of St. John & went into the little chapel where services are held - Then, over to Riverside Drive & walked down to Soldiers & Sailors Mon. at 89th St. This part of the city is developing with wonderful rapidity & is now exceedingly beautiful in spite of many unfinished parts. Home to dinner at 2. Had sleep until 6 - got up with severe headache continuing - at 6.30 went to druggists \+ got, & drank, bottle full of Citrate of Magnesia - Then to studio & closed windows - after & my headache had so far passed that I went to Edw. Cook's - saw Edith who has last every - her first appearance at home since her Typhoid fever last fall - She looks better than I had expected - but has a very thin head of hair - I saw also Carleton \+ his fiancée Miriam Goodwin - C. stroked his adolescent moustache as complacently as ever & Miriam was as vivacious & pretty as ever. Home soon after 9 - Read Mrs Gilbert's Reminiscences of the Stage. Cat. Mag. began its work about 11 - I did not undress & go regularly to bed until 2 a.m.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 24, 1902

**Weather:** A fine mild day growing much cooler towards night  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine mild day growing much cooler towards night. To studio rather late - worked for awhile on print of Father's portrait & satisfied myself that any farther time spent on it would be wasted. Soon after 11 went to Am. Art Ass'n & saw Kirby & enquired about processes suitable for getting up a fine ill. cat. of pictures. He showed me some interesting work by the Boston firm of Elson & Co. - Then to Scribner's - saw W. Smith & left with him a number of p'fs of my work in different styles also Elson samples - I told him what I had learned of processes & he is to consult with Mr. Borden - Home to lunch at 1.30 - to studio - made start on new portrait of father - made gelatine tracing from photo. \+ enlarged it by squares to size of etching - This took till after 4 - Then went to R. \+ V. \+ arranged to have the Borden p'f. taken on Wed. mtg. met Reich there, the etcher. Walked up this' the widened Elm. St. - It is a dreadful place & a disgrace to our city - Every I got to bed before 10 o'clk.
