## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 18, 1902

**Weather:** Dull day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day - After a very unrestful night got up with a beast of a headache that clung to me all day - To studio about 10 \+ worked on Borden outline. Home to lunch \+ gave up to my headache \+ was on my bed for the rest of the afternoon until after 5 - then out \+ sauntered on Park Ave. for awhile. Dinner at 6.30 - We\+ went to Mr. Barhydts - 40 E. 70' St., by appointment \+ was with her till after 10 - She has agreed to pass over to the custody of the N.A. f\+. the principal of the A.H. Baldwin fund in R.R. bonds. This is to be done as soon as her business adviser, who is now on his way home from Europe, returns to the city.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 19, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A light shower about 9 am. after which the weather cleared sunny \+ mild. To studio after 10, still suffering from headache all over. Worked on Borden outline - Home to lunch - back at 2.30 - Maurice Stone came in with some p\+s of his recent work \+ we talked of the N.A. f\+. Etching class \+ he agrees to take it for the bal. \+income of Baldwin fund after the payment of the 1\+ \+ 2\+ annual prizes - that is, for $125\+ the season. I finished Borden outline \+ took it to Tonnelle for photo. Reduction. Dinner at 6.30 Eve\+ my head distressed me so that I did not go to meeting of Water Color Socy.
