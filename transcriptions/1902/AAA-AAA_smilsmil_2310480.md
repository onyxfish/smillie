## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 6, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Lanes left us.

Heavy rain during the m't. commencing at about 1 o'clk \+ continuing until the afternoon \- about 1 o'clk \- midday \- very dark \+ with heavy pouring rain, some thunder \- clearing at night. Up at 6 o'cl. the Lanes got away at 7 \- Jend Reede taking them to West Port. I had b[illegible] soon after \+ was all the m't. very busy sizing \+ Kalsomining \- I made up new Kalsominine \+ put a coat on the alcove frame \- also on chimney breast in my bed room \+ on part of ceiling \- Part of the afternoon I slept \+ made up some of the loss of last night \- Eve't. I loafed.

Otis rolled the road in the afternoon.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 7, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clearing, with cold northerly wind. Trumbull came in the m't. \+ we worked together all day \- most of the time on ceiling for alcove which was finished \+ ready to put up \- I Kalsomined the whole thing again \+ got some novel effects \- T. \+ I put up a section of muslin on my bed-room ceiling \- \+ I commenced some experiments with burlaps on chimney breast in my room. Mary \+ the girls went to a tea at Dr. Laights in the afternoon \+ we had tea at 7. Eve't. I feel stiff \+ tired after my day of work in the cold wind on north Piazza.
