## Left Page

### Tues. July 29, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — Trumbull worked all day on balcony in front of alcove window \+ almost finished it — Jno. Otis did some work on the road \+ I was busy all day on ceilings — I kalsomined the one for the alcove as it is at present nailed to the laundry rafters but it was not successfully done — came streaked. Then I took down the two strips that I put up in my bedroom yesterday — had Trumbull make a new frame — then I restretched each piece \+ kalsomined it \+ put one piece up successfully — Eve'g I was very tired — dozed awhile and read a little of Silas Marner.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 30, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful warm breezy summer day — I spent much of the m'g. \+ part of the afternoon with Ralph experimenting with his portrait lens \+ studio lights — I arranged several sittings of self \+ the two boys — a dozen in all \+ after dinner R. \+ I developed them — a disappointment owing to several causes — miscalculation of exposure — focus — movement — etc. etc. I had much trouble in getting strips of muslin, prepared yesterday — on my bed room ceiling — Trumbull worked on balcony at my bed room windows — Otis did some work on road — Eve'g in studio, napping \+ reading newspapers.
