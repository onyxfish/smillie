## Left Page

### Fri. July 25, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Peculiar weather — misty clouds hanging on the Mt. tops — formless clouds shutting out sunlight much of the time — \+ wind blowing making a sighing, soughing sound in the fomes. Trumbull came this m'f. \+ a good part of my day was spent with him. He got the plate of glass into its frame \+ the frame into its place in alcove in main room. This did some work on road. I wrote a scolding letter to Wanamaker \+ Kalsomined some of the muslin that is for my ceiling. Mr. \+ Mrs. D. Magee called in the afternoon \+ the girls — K. \+ B. \+ Miss Lane went to Mr. Shaws for an afternoon \+ tea — Well, loafing.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 26, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still peculiar weather — nevertheless, a pleasant day, — parts of it very delightful. The feminine contingent — Mary, her girls, Kate, Mr. Lane \+ her daughter started at 9 o'clk. for a days outing on the Upper Ausable — with Ford. Reede \+ Pond as guides. Trumbull came \+ I worked with him all the m'f. — Getting a part of my bed-room ceiling tacked up \+ the alcove stretcher muslin Kalsomined \+ nailed to ceiling of laundry where it will be finished. Dinner at 1 — Jim, Ralph \+ I — a very good one. After 3 o'clk. we three started out for a walk — went over Reserve Road to Reservoir on Gill Brook \+ then up a very bad wood road, for about half a mile to a camp of four or five log houses — lumbermen who are at work clearing up a bee "wind slash" of pulp-wood some distance farther on — neither picturesque \+ pleasant — dirty \+ foul smelling. We got home about 5.30 — I had a change of clothing \+ a rest — Supper before ) when the others came — Eve'g. the Boys \+ I carried up to the house some lumber that had been left at the gate — Then read newspapers.
