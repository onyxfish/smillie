## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 30, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull m'f. Rain commencing about 2 o'clk. \+ falling quite heavily during the afternoon \+ eve'f. I got up about 7 \+ had bkfp. at 9 — about 10.30 I went to studio \+ worked on Morgan head till about 1.30 when it was ready for an imp. I wet down paper \+ had a little walk before dinner at 2. Jim dined at his Aunt Mary Coale's. The afternoon being dull \+ rainy I had a long nap. Tea at 6.30 Jim ret'? to New Haven on the 8\. 10 train. I read \+ wrote till 11 — then to bed again.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 1, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cool \+ pleasant — 10 o'clk. when I got to studio — got ready for printing but it was noon before I took first print. I was disappointed of course — I expected to be — but it promises better I think than the last head — Soon after 12 I went to 2° Nat. Bank \+ then home to lunch. To studio soon after 2. Found young Stahr at the door — he brought some prints from plates that he etched while abroad. I got to my work as soon as I could but did not succeed in getting any satisfactory pf. Paper too dry — paper too wet — etc. etc. etc. I stuck to it till after 5 — wet down paper for further trials to-morrow. A short walk — then home — dinner — Charl came in in the eve'f. \+ several things that I had planned to do were left undone.
