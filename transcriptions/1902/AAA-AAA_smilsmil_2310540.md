## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 10, 1902

**Weather:** Dull, windy + cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

at 9.30 went to J. P. Morgan's + asked to see Miss M. — as she appears to be the one who is to criticise + pass my etching. I did not see her but the elder woman whom I have not yet located. There was confusion in the house for some reason + the incognita took my print + showed it to M. M. + Reported that the chin was not yet satisfactory — still too broad + square — if I could come back at 1 o'clk. I could see Miss M — I then went to studio — very cold — no coal — Ther. 55° — worked with my overcoat on — tried to make the desired changes in chin. Took two or three proofs of head at once + took one to Miss M. — at 1 — She is not yet satisfied — Home to lunch, much discouraged. — At 2 o'clk — went down town — to Clarence Smiths + executed Will — or Rather, codicil to Will — then to 30 Broad. + left pl. of Morgan — no one in but boy — there to Geo. Read's — Van Telf N. T. — in Then to Edw. Cook's — there till 4.30 going over the Ireland Real Estate Co's acts as Auditor. Then to studio — Coal had come — then home. Dinner at 5.30 — Evell wrote to Stedman + before I went to Charl's — Mary + girls there — home at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 11, 1902

**Weather:** A nasty day — sleet + freezing  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 — Spent the mf. fixing proofs that I took two or three days ago — taking out tacks, trimming etc. — Then I worked for awhile on Morgan's chin trying to produce the effect desired by Miss M — That is, to make the chin appear smaller + more square — a difficult matter because of direction of lines already in + the amt. of fussing already done over the work. — Home to lunch at 1 + back soon after 2. I feel so assured that I have M's chin as desired that I set down a few sheets of Toronoco paper for pfs. to-morrow — I spent much time washing old printing cloths a dirty + out of the usual laundry way — potash + boiling suds. — Home to dinner at 6 — Evell lounging + reading.
