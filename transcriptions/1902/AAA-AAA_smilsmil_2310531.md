## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 20, 1902

**Weather:** mild & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild & pleasant. To studio at 10. Allison came from Kappel's to see if I could match a certain kind of satin faced p.p. paper that he wished for proofs from all Whistler plates that he has. I had some of the very paper that I got from Saml. Colman in 1879 but would not part with it. I worked on a scale drawing of monument for our lot in Greenwood Home to lunch at 1. At 3 went to 106 W. 55th S. To see Irving Wiles who is m'na of P. School Com. to arrange about Etching class — he was not in. I met Frank Fowler in the hall & went into his studio for a few minutes — then up to the Park & at 4 o'clk to the Barnard Club Rooms in Carnegie Hall — a Piano Recital by Pascal with some singing by Miss Boese. I was not greatly impressed — Had a very pleasant chat with Mrs. Frank Brownell. Home at 5.30 — dinner at 6.30 — left; after my newspaper wrote notes to Irving Wiles & Clarence Smith.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 21, 1902

**Weather:** mild & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild & pleasant. To studio at 10. Took a sudden determination to paint — am so tired of all this rubbishy work that I have been bothering over for some time past trying to make a little money. Set palette & got out a canvas upon which I had rubbed in a sketch — a mt. view suggested by Keene Valley from my studio balcony. Painted till 1 — then home to lunch — back at 2 & painted till 4 & got my sketch 8½×14" done — Tolerable — Frank Millet came in for a few minutes to see Maynard's studio — possibly he may rent it. Then I went to Irving Wiles' studio & talked with him about Etching Class & N.A. Was introduced to Mr. Day — a young painter — then to Fine Arts Bld. 59. S. — Private View — Water-Color Club — a very interesting exhib'n — Met Harry Penn there & went around the exhib' with him. Home at 6 — dinner — Eve'g wrote to Jim & Ralph. At 9.30 went to Charles' — Mary & the girls there. Home at 10.
