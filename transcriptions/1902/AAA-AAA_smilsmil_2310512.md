## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 9, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day after a cold wet night — all day a series of squalls with light spatters of rain — not enough to wet things. Trumbull \+ Chase came \+ worked as usual be. I did a little finishing work in doors — The men worked at the retaining wall on the road \+ made good progress. Moody worked with me in attic clearing that up \+ then helping carpenters. I made some progress in getting my personal effects ready to be put into my trunk. Dinner at 1 — then to P.O. then to Felsenheim Chapel \+ saw Harry Deeds — then down the hill \+ saw Winch \+ then Mr. Ferd. Bead — \+ then to studio \+ was busy there until dark. D. Adams came to see about painting job \+ made promises — Tea at 6.30. Catherine Hardseburgh took the place of Katharine Throb who was brought home by W. Maghee about 8.

T \+ C 1

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 10, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day after a freezing night — not a cloud all day — brilliant sunshine and pleasantly warm. After bkf. had to go to Scott Brown's to secure some long wood for large fireplaces — then down to see the men at work on road wall — then to the house to look after Trumbull \+ Chase — then with Moody worked at cutting out trees till noon — wrote letters to Jim \+ Ralph — Dinner at 1 — then to P.O. — again to men on road wall — again setting trees with Moody \+ in at 4.30 \+ had a pleasant fire in my red room where I spent the rest of the P.M. \+ the evening reading newspapers and painting — wrote to Dielman about etching class.

T \+ C. 1
