## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 6, 1902

**Weather:** muggy, moist \+ overcast.  **Temp:** [illegible]

a very little rain during the day \+ a very little more at night. Another day of worrying at studio over the Morgan plate — taking proofs \+ fussing to get something that is too elusive for my aged faculties to grasp. Between 5 \+ 6 p.m. I took a short walk but the weather was so enervating that I did not go for dinner at 6.30. Evelf. Katharine was to go to Symphony concert with Charl but too free use of gasoline for cleaning purposes made her violently sick. Bess went to Charl's to dinner to take K's place but before dinner was over she too was sick from like cause — so she came home \+ I took her place \+ went with Ch. to Carnegie Hall \+ enjoyed some old true music (Beethoven) A tone poem by Rich. Strauss was rather beyond me, however.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 7, 1902

**Weather:** cloudy \+ coarser.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. \+ Mrs. J.H. Maghee dined with us. nothing accomplished to-day — too much discouraged to try to do anything on the Morgan plate — many notes \+ letters to write, so I gave much of the day to that work — I did not go to studio in the afternoon but stayed at home \+ gave most of the time, after writing a letter to Jim \+ part of one to Ralph, to sleep. I dressed for dinner \+ at 6.30 Mr. \+ Mrs. Maghee came \+ we had a reasonably good dinner \+ an agreeable ev'f. I finished my letter to Ralph \+ mailed it \+ Jim's letter before 10.
