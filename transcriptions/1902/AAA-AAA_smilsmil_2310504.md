## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 23, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day for action — some sunshine — some clouds that looked showery \+ hosts of flies that buzzed \+ stuck persistently — Rain \+ wind at night — I have been fighting against headache \+ some sort of influenza for several days. Last night I was very restless \+ uncomfortable \+ about 4 A.M. I got up \+ dosed myself with quinine \+ whisky — I have been very uncomfortable all day \+ unable to work. After 5 o'c'p'. I went to Mr. Cook \+ D. Drummond. Sec'f Com. on Admissions Century — then to P.O. — Dinner at 1. Afternoon Ralph \+ I took Emma \+ Elig. drove over the River trail from below Maghee's to back of the hotel \+ then home. Katharine \+ Elig. were off all day with the Maghees \+ again at night at what they call a "camp fire". R. \+ I, with our guests, had a quiet ev'g. at home.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 24, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Day opened overcast \+ unpromising — wind from north — cold — clearing slowly to fresh \+ pleasant. My cold somewhat better. The girls \+ Emma \+ Elig. went for a walk in the Reserve \+ then to Chapel Pond after I had gone to the P.O. Ralph \+ I walked down the road a mile or more to where we saw, some days ago, a hornets' nest — to find that some one had destroyed it — then back in time for dinner — After dinner I went over to H. Dubois's cabin to see Mr. Jr. Burrows who is a visitor there — His only son is to marry a daughter of [illegible] tomorrow — I had a pleasant half hour with old D. Dubois \+ Mr. B — then home — Jr. Otis drew two or three loads of earth to spread over bare places on my hill to take grass seed. Moody helping — Tea at 6.30 — Ev'g Emma played on piano for us a little.
