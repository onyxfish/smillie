## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 3, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph \+ the servants left. Rather cold in the early m'g \- a fair day but not a proper clear-up yet \- a clouded \+ stormy looking night with considerable wind \- Up at 6.30 \- bkp. at 7.30 \+ at 9 Ralph, Kate, Leonard, Jane, Banks \+ Lucy, Rene, the servants got away in fine style. Emma Otis \+ Rose Edmonds came soon after \- the one to rene the other to cook \- Trumbull \+ Chase made good progress on the little building getting the sills \+ cedar posts \+ floor beams laid \- Otis brought a few loads of manure from his own stable. Dinner at 1 \- a little family of four \- at 2 I went to P. O. \+ among other letters got one from Charl describing his trip to N.Y. the D. N.Y. train frequently missing the N.H. Central at Albany obliging passengers to take away train getting to the city at midnight. It worried me a good deal as I thought of possible complications that would upset Ralph \- I was able to do but little in the afternoon because of stomach trouble \- after Supper a stupid evef.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 4, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cold m'g \- afternoon cloudy with cold north wind \- Still feeling out of sorts I was not able to accomplish much \- Trumbull \+ Chase worked on small bld'g. Otis hauled manure \- one from his own stable, making five from that place \- afternoon he hauled three, making 9 in all from hotel stables. At noon I went to ford Beede's \+ got my overcoat \- home \- dinner at 1 \- Bessie went up Mt Hopkins with m's. Mackay \+ one of her daughters \- I went to P. O. at 2.30 \- then to Reserve and walked away up the road \- Supper at 6.30 \- Eve'g, Bessie played awhile on piano \+ after that I read Frank Stockton's Kate Bonnet.
