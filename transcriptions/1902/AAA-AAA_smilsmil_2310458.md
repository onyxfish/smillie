## Left Page

### Sat. June 21, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain with wind till after 3 p.m. Late in the afternoon the clouds broke away & the sunshine out. I got up at about 6.30 - The rain began to fall at that time. Bkfs. at 7.30 - Stage came at 8.15 we then drove over to R.R. Station \+ got two passengers \+ a dog - The curtains of the stage were all down but the wind drove the rain in upon us \+ we had a very uncomfortable ride. The roads were execrable especially about the Cascade house, where we got out for a few minutes, \+ thence on to near Keene Centre. It was 12.30 before we got to Keene C - where Reed's team stood waiting for us. We made the transfer after a little delay \+ found the most uncomfortable part of the drive was up the valley with the wind and mizzling rain driving in our faces. It was 3 o'clk. when at last we got home \+ found shelter \+ comfort in the Ark. We soon had our dinner \+ hot coffee - then I took a look over the house \+ partly unpacked my trunk - went to bed soon after 10 carrying a dose of whisky \+ quinine -

## Right Page

### Sun. June 22, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright cool day - got up feeling none the worse for yesterday's exposure. Breakfast after 9 - Later Ralph \+ I walked over the place \+ down the road taking note of changes during the winter which prove to be much less than I expected. We went over to St. Hubert's Inn \+ found a small army of men \+ women at work preparing for the opening for the 25th. Mr. doe, the new manager, came yesterday. Dinner at 2 - supper at 6.30. I don't know what became of the time tho I went to bed after 10 -
