## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 27, 1902

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'g., wet disagreeable afternoon. Moody managed to put in a full day. After bkf't. I worked for a while on Borden on time — at 11 went to hotel — got mail \+ brought home two window sashes sent up by Whitehead. Dinner at 1 — Afternoon too wet to go out I worked on Borden out-line for a couple of hours. It was near 7 o'clk when Mary \+ Charl arr'd having spent last night at Bluff Point hotel. Then we had supper — Ev'f. much Talk over the Montreal \+ Quebec trip — M. \+ C. being greatly pleased.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 28, 1902

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ wet — a little struggling sunshine for a short time in the afternoon. The day passed without incident. Like many another unpleasant day. walking on piazzas — writing, reading, chatting. About 4 P. m. Emma \+ Katharine thro'p. Charl, Ralph \+ I went to the Reserve \+ walked to the flume. Rain commenced to fall gently \+ continued for some time, but we did not get wet — the walk was really much enjoyed. Tea at 6.30 — Before 8 Fera Deede came \+ got the trunks of Emma \+ Eliz't. Ev'f. Emma played on piano \+ sang some old time songs.
