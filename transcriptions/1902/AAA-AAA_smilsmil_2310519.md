## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 25, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - bright + mild. Spent the m'f at the studio - most of the time about going over a past of my stock of old drawing paper + tying it + wrapping it in paper to protect it from dust + abrasion + then filing away upon a top shelf - fine old paper - I wonder what will become of it ultimately - Home to lunch at 1 - was surprised by a call from a man (Smith) says he is Sect'y of Ex Gov. Morton + asks for delivery of photo. that I ret'd to him months ago, after having etched plate of Stock Ex. Hist. Co. I think Silleck ret'd it for me some time last April. Part of afternoon I was at home packing away things in closets then to studio - there till near 5 - Home to dinner - with newspaper + to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 26, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast + rather raw - Rklp'. before 9 - took 10.02 train + got to New-Haven + 10 m. after 12 - about 15 m. late. I went at once to the Pekin's - 477 Prospect St. + found Mollie Adams there. Dinner at 1 - Jim did not come till after dinner - he had been at the station when my train came in but we missed each other - About 3 o'clk - we all started for Jim's room - Durfee 211 - Katie, Mollie, William + dog, Jim + - we stopped at "Commons" + one of the waiters visited us about + down to regions below, boilers, dynamos, bake ovens, kitchens, store rooms and cellars - then to Jim's room - there met his room mate, Lewis Bamberger. After a little the others left + Jim, B - + I held the fort till about 5.30 - Then Jim + I walked up to D'. Elkins' and had tea there. D'. had one of the new boxes for developing photo. films without need of a dark-room - an ingenious device that he explained to us. At 7.30 Jim + I left his house - walked down to street car + rode to R.R. Station - 8.10 train came along about 5 m. late - + got to N.Y. on time 10.a Mary + the girls, with Charl, came along just after I got in - from dinner at his house.
