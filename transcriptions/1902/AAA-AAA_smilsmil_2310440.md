## Left Page

### Fri. May 16, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still fine weather, Allen, the mason, \+ his men, came \+ commenced breaking hole thro' east wall in attic bedroom for a window. Miller, the carpenter, sent window frame, sashes etc. It was nearly 11 before I got to studio — Before 12 I went to 2d. Nat. Bank — Then to Schkel Schleuning \+ Adams 256 W. 23d. St. Book binders \+ gave order to bind my file of Tribunes from 1861 to '65 — Then home to lunch — Did not go to studio in afternoon — wrote letters at home — dinner at usual time — Eve'g wrote to Jim \+ Ralph —

## Right Page

### Sat. May 17, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

went to Greenwood with Charl.

Warm, cloudless \+ hazy — Spent the mf. at studio \+ for nearly two hours worked with crayon on pf. of Morgan — My failure with this portrait is because I failed to appreciate correctly fundamental tone relations very general in character — If I had time I would not hesitate now to begin a new plate from same original — Soon after 12 went down town — went to Read's in Cedar St. \+ saw Van Pelt for a few minutes — then to Charl's \+ together we went to one of his down-town clubs \+ had a nice little lunch in swell style — after that we came to Greenwood via Wall St. ferry — out to our lot where we spent some time in inspecting MacKellar's work — on our way out to the lot \+ on our way back we looked at a number of monuments with a view to selection for our lot. Home soon after 6 — dinner — Katharine off with Will Mulford — they came in when we had nearly finished our meal — M. was invited \+ he stayed \+ took pot-luck — Carlson came at 9.30 \+ I had my regulation rubbing.
