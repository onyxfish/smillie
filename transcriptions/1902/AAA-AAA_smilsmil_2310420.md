## Left Page

### Sun. April 6, 1902

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f, but before noon the sky was veiled  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'f, but before noon the sky was veiled — rain began to fall about 3 o'clk \+ continued quite heavily till after I went to bed. Up at 8 \+ had my sunday m'f scrub — bkfst at 9.30 out 11 Ralph \+ I started out — He had his camera — we rode to 10th S. \+ 8 Ave. walked up to the Cathedral — he took some shots at it — then over to Riverside \+ down the drive to Soldier's \+ Sailor's mont, then to cars \+ home at 1.30 — dinner at 2 — after 3 went to studio \+ closed windows — then to Walter Cook's \+ spent an hour or more chatting with him, Louise \+ Edw?. — then home — found Geo. Val. \+ Charl there. Tea at 6.30 — Ev'f reading.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 7, 1902

**Weather:** Leaden clouds — cold, raw easterly winds and rain at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

Leaden clouds — cold, raw easterly winds and rain at night. After bkfst some writing — then to bank — \+ at a little after 11 to studio \+ oiled \+ o'clk cleaning surface of copper where I had scraped at head of Morgan. After lunch went to Etching Class at N. A. found a full house — fifteen students at their preliminary drawings from the model — old man with fine head — in a costume that may pass for old Dutch. It was all satisfactory so I left them \+ walked down Central Park west (8 Ave) to Sam. Colman's \+ made a call on him — his wife is suffering greatly now — then home \+ had a good rest before dinner — Ev'f, went to station with Ralph \+ saw him off for Tarrytown on 8.35 train — then to Charl's for a few minutes \+ at about 9 to Chas. Collins, by appointment \+ spent the rest of the ev'f with him \+ his sister looking over old prints that he has.
