## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 28, 1902

Kätchen Cook came \-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day \- almost cloudless \- warm, with refreshing breeze \- wind changing to S. E \- Trumbull at work all day putting up braces \- 5×5" on north piazza \- Moody worked all day clearing up about the front of the house \- most of the time on the road \- I spent a good part of the w.f. carving letters \- The Ark \- on a long board that is to be the centre of the rustic cedar gate at road entrance \- At noon went to hotel got mail \- met Mr. Howell \+ walked with him up Reserve Road for a short distance Scott Brown gave me $10 \- for use of horse mower on golf links last season \- Home to dinner at 1 o'clk \- Kätchen Cook there just am? from her camping in Pleasant Valley \- afternoon looking after the men tea \- Ev. f., news papers as usual \-

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 29, 1902

Reception \-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Showers from 5 to near 7 a.m. \- very humid, misty \- light showers about noon \+ again at 2 \- sunshine \+ shadow after that but no rain \- All the w.f. I was with Moody \- most of the time transplanting a group of small evergreens to the S. E. cor. of the house \- Dinner at 1 \- after that preparations for the function of this afternoon \- a general "tea" for which Mary \+ the girls have issued more than 90 invitations \- The house has been decked with green branches \+ wild flowers the north piazza being quite gorgeous with golden rod \- for. Rye \+ such flowers \- A great many came but many, no doubt, did not come fearing showers \- A crate of peaches from Chas. came late in the p.m. They were in very bad condition \- fine, large peaches but not one altogether sound one among them \- After tea Mary, Ralph \+ I cut out all of the unsound parts, pealed \+ sliced them \+ put them on ice \- Sorry work \- Ev. f. some loafing \+ fighting o\? r again the skirmishing of the day \- then to bed.
