## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 7, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

D. Elkin & Katie called.

Heavy rain nearly on't till about 8 o'clk. after which it cleared rapidly - a couple of light showers - but in the main, the day was beautiful. Bkfp. about 9 - after that we were surprised by a call from Katie Elkin & her William, just arr. from Montreal after a quite extended Canadian Trip - They spent the m'f with us & at 12 went back to hotel, Chark & I accompanying them part way as we went onto the Opechupnis where we had a pleasant call at Mr & Mrs. O. Home to dinner at 1 o'clk. after stopping at hotel for mail. afternoon the usual crowd came between 4.30 & 6 o'clk. Tea at 6.30. Evening went between general chatter, pacing piazza & reading newspapers.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 8, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day - but my digestive apparatus was not attuned to it. I wrote some notes in the m'f & at noon took them to hotel & got mail. Then to Scott Brown's & got on the track of Enoch Sanders & interested Scott in getting out some stone suitable for steps - then home to dinner. D. Elkin dined with us - Katie E. having gone to Hopkins. I was obliged to yield to my physical discomforts after dinner & was on my bed till near 4 o'clk. Then Sanders came with a load of stone from the Reserve suitable for steps - Scott B. came soon after & all worked till 6 to get the stones in place in the walk from house to dining room - D. Merle Smith & Mr. S. made a very agreeable call about 5 p.m. & expressed great admiration for the Ark & views therefrom. Tea at 6.30. Eve'f Kitchen & Willie came to say goodbye as they are to leave early to-morrow.
