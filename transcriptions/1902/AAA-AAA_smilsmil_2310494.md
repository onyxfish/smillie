## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 3, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kätchen & Willie Cook left — Miss Clara Lane came.

A very fine day I am told — I was in bed all day with the most severe headache I have had for years. I did not get up till about 9 P.M. \+ to bed again at 12 — Trumbull came \+ put in a day finishing the work on north piazza \+ then left to return when called — Rob. Otis \+ team worked in the afternoon with Ch. Moody hauling stone \+ earth for terrace road to ice house. Kätchen \+ Willie made arrangements to board for a week or more with Mr. Weuch, at the foot of our hill. Miss Clara Lane arr. — soon after noon to commence her visit.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 4, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain with thunder about 5 a.m. Heavy showers till near 8 — a sullen clearing — some pleasant sunshine later in the day — high wind \+ more rain at night. I got up with another kind of headache — severe in the back of my head — sort of neuralgia perhaps — It bothered me so much that I was obliged to give up — it \+ I went back to my bed soon after dinner. Rob Otis \+ team with Moody at work all day accomplishing very little — could not find such stone as I wished \+ hauled earth that I objected to.
