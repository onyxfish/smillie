## Left Page

### Tues. June 3, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A most uncomfortably hot + muggy day - Ther. above 90° - + humidity above 70°. I got bkfs. a little earlier than usual + at 9.20 was at K. + V's in Canal St. - too early - it was some time before Mr. V. was ready for me - I tried my best to coach him to take off the Morgue pace like my proof but to my great regret he did not succeed - he could not get a perfectly even + round crisp + the delicate lines while drawing up the shadowed parts a very little - on the other, less important, parts he got a vivacity that I have not yet got in a proof from the plate. At noon I went back to studio with the 6 or 7 pfs. that had been taken + put them between blotters - home to lunch at 1 - then to studio + at 4 went again to K. + V's + got the remaining pfs. - not yet to take a dozen on thin gaps. To studio + put pfs. between blotters - home to dinner - Eve: J. wrote to Ralph + to Ge. Cook - in answer to Q.C.'s appeal I sent him the final payment for his books - $50 - many little things were arranged with Mary preparatory to her starting for Keene Heights to-morrow - I went to Q.C. station + had all the trunks checked - all is now done that can be done preparatory to the start.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 4, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Caravan starts for Keene Heights Ark.

A charming day - bright, breezy + growing cooler - up at 6 - bkfs. at 7 - + at 8.15 I led my caravan of feminines - six in all - + started them on their way north - getting them into their seats - the servants forward - Mary + the girls in Pullman seats - as the regular summer train is not yet on they will have to change cars at Albany. After that I walked up to Mr. Bordie's 56th St. his house closed - then home - to studio - to Scribner - saw Mr. Smith - then to 2d Nat. Bank - cut coupons for summer, months + left them with bank - then to Mr. Cutcheon's + bought vowels - then to Schuiling about binding Ptg. files - then to Century + had lunch - after that went to barber's + had hair cut - then to studio - there till about 5 - wrote part of letter to Jim for his birthday on the 6 - + to Summa Ireland. a short visit at home - then to Char's - out for a short walk before dinner + met Martin our neighbor of 112 - had along Falk with him - he has sold his house for $5000 - a fabulous price - to C's again at 7 + had dinner - loafed until 9.30 when Carson came + gave me good work for more than an hour - then to bed + had a fair night's rest.
