## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 1, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine cold, clear, windy day — commenced the year by feeling better than I have for some days past — to studio about 10 — finished the cleaning \+ varnishing of "Dark against day's golden death" — then I made three camera-lucida reductions from photo of J. Pierpont Morgan showing three arrangements — 3/4 fig. — 1/2 fig — head alone — to show to Stedman \+ to Wilson — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — Got ready to resume painting on the unfinished oil sketch of W. Borden's library — I wish to turn this in with the other materials as a part of the etching of the place — Then walked three miles on Park Ave — cold \+ windy — in at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Waller de Forest Smillies daughter — Jessie Lamb — dined with us She has come to the city with her little boy to have a surgical operation performed on one of his legs — After dinner she went with May \+ others to Charl's — I remained at home \+ wrote to Jno. W. Wilson \+ others
