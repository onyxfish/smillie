## Left Page

### Thur. April 24, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Much cooler — a pleasant day — went to W. Bordens \+ was there sketching the So. end of his library till near 12 — then walked down to Century \+ wrote notes to Elson \+ Co. Boston — W. Morton, f. Brush \+ Pencil \+ to Clarence Smith — then home to lunch \+ to studio at 2.30 \+ worked till after 4 opening packing cases \+ taking out contents — Had a rest before dinner — Sue \+ Charl came in \+ consequently I did not get any part of my letters to the boys written.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 25, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Quite cool — wind east — clouded over later in the day — To studio about 10 — spent [illegible] unpacking some of the boxes rec. from Montrose, John helping me \+ getting those boxes filled with books into the cellar — Home to lunch \+ back soon after 2 — one box containing many kinds of paper — also boards \+ blotters for standing press \+ a number of [illegible] including about half a dozen Goldsmith's Daughters was quite damp throughout \+ much paper \+ some proofs were much injured by mildew — I worked at assorting this material till after 4 — then to Mitchell \+ Vance Co. \+ rec'd the shade I got there the other day — then home \+ had a rest before dinner. Sue \+ unmailed my bust by Calverly — it is criticised but is generally approved — Sheldon came \+ took Mary \+ Katharine with him to a Columbia Glee \+ Mandolin Club Concert — I wrote to Jim \+ Ralph.
