## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 6, 1902

**Weather:** mild day \- misty m'f.  **Temp:**

To studio & painted on Bordeu library till 12.30 \- then to bank & to Scribner's \- got a book for Ralph \- home to lunch at 1.30 \- to studio at 2.30\. Painted till after 4\. \- then home & went with R\- to G. Centrale Station \- where he started for Tarrytown on the 4.55 Train \- Then to Barber's & had hair cut \- Home at 6\. dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g Reading.

Rec'd. a note from Wiles asking me not to write to Curtis & Cameron till he has made an effort to relieve himself from some of the bonds or restrictions of his copyright transfer to C.&C. \- As it states the copyright covers all sorts of reproductions\- I don't believe they will release their hold \- he will have to submit my proposition to them offering to do the plate for them as the publishers & take my pay from sales.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 7, 1902

**Weather:** nasty day \- a little sunshine m'ts m'f.  **Temp:**

then overcast & later just enough snow & rain to make it wet underfoot\. \- To studio & painted on Bordeu library\. Home at 1 \- & at 1.30 at H.D. Allen's, 47th 5t. to have him look at my teeth \- made an appointment for friday m'f at 9.30\. \- then to studio & painted as long as I could see \- then to Fussel's \- 4th ave & 17th St. & paid bill for window shades \- then to Bayardorfer's & paid bill \- then home \- dinner \- Jim & I went to G'l. Central Station & he took 8 o'clk. Train to New Haven \- I Rec'd. home \- eve'g reading.
