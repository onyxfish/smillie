## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 12, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Icestorm covered every thing with a thin glaze making travel difficult. Snow at night. To studio at 10 \+ made a long morning taking half a dozen p\[late\]s of the Morgau[illegible] on Thin Connors Paper. It was about two o'clk. when I got home to lunch. After that was detained for some time by a young man from the Knickerbocker Publ. Co. 156 5th Ave. N.Y. who are getting up a Biographical [illegible] \+ tried to rope me in for $1.00 for a full page portrait — but I resisted. It was so late when I got to studio that I could not do more than damp inks. \+ put them in press between blotters. It was 6.30 when I got home to dinner. Eve\[ning\] wrote to the boys.

This m\[orning\] after b\[reak\]f\[as\]t went to Charl\[ie\] for a few minutes — one of his speculations in Wall S\[t\]. resulted in a severe loss to him yesterday.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 13, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A typical winter storm day — snow, cold \+ wind. The wind was cutting \+ the snow was dry, small \+ granular, striking upon the wind on panes with the sound of sand. To studio about 10. Some time changing p\[late\]s between platters \+ boards. Then, after looking at Morgau[illegible] I decided that it w\[oul\]d be best to take out a portion of the chin the lines of which did not seem to suit me \+ were, by much working over and changing, more or less rotten. So I scraped out a portion — hammered it up \+ then laid it in anew with graver much to the improvement of that part of the face, I think. Home to lunch before 2 \+ back in an hour — mounted a p[late] taken on thin [illegible]. \+ prepared it to sub- mit to M\[r.\] Morgan Monday m\[ornin\]g. To dinner at 6.30. Eve\[ning\] I was glad to fix myself in my easy chair before a clean cut fire instead of accepting a card of invitation to Lotos Club to see some very fine landscapes. Charl came in \+ stayed till 11.
