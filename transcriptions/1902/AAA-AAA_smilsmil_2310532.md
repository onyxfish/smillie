## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 22, 1902

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f - clouded afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

a smart shower between 5 & 6 p.m. in which I got caught — To studio at 10 — Painted all day on a 10 x 15 canvas upon which, years ago, I had painted out of doors a very rough sketch of Jones's lake Montrose — I went over it paying little regard to what was under \+ got a fair result — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 \+ worked till 4.30 — then I cleaned up — Went to Clausen's \+ ordered a 10 x 15 frame — Was caught in the rain \+ had to run all the way home — dinner — Ev'g as usual — newspaper \+ a little writing.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 23, 1902

**Weather:** Quite cool — windy \+ consequently dirty  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkf't. before 10 — Then reading newspaper — then out \+ prowling about the streets — went into Charles's — home to dinner at 2 — Afternoon Clarence Smillie \+ wife (Catharine Cook) called — Ev'g Geo. \+ Sheldon came in — I wrote to Sarah f. Hammond — to Mr. Barhydt, Sterne, Mackellar \+ Ralph — After 9 went out \+ walked up \+ down Park Ave. for half an hour or more.
