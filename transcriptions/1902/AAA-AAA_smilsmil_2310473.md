## Left Page

### Wed. July 23, 1902

**Weather:** Cloudless m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless m'f., lovely atmosphere - then beautiful clouds - heavy shower bet. H + 5 o'clk - + after 10 cloudless moonlight. Much of my day was spent with Adams who pasted heavy paper on ceiling of my bed room preparatory to the muslin that is to be sized + kalsomined - Trumbull did not come - Jno. Otis with his team worked on my Road. I wrote to Cath. Cook Smith in answer to her note + told her that we would have room for her Clarence early in Aug. Bet. H + 5 p.m. Ralph + I started out for a walk up the camp road now building thro' the pass between Noon Mark + Round Top - but we turned back + had got as far on our way home as the hotel where we were caught by the shower + sat on piazza with D. + Mr. Magee - Jim went fishing, taking his lunch with him to Grant's washbowl - came home about 6 having caught nothing. Evey the J.N. Maghees came over + spent a chatty eveg - on the forazza but Ralph + I had a quiet time by ourselves reading in the studio.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 24, 1902

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant during the m'f. with threats of change - clouded afternoon - thunderstorms about - some light rain fell on us - evening very lowering - clouds settling low + wind moaning - I spent most of m'f. with Adams getting the ceiling of my bed room covered with sheathing paper. Before noon I went to Mr. Beede's + got Wanamaker pkg. that has been at his house since day before yesterday - it was thoroughly wet - took it home + opened it - more errors - I sent for 1¼" curtain poles - 1½" size sent, for which the rings I have are too small - I sent for light chord + pulleys for bamboo shades - + cloth's line is sent - no pulleys - the lacquer on portions of the brass had changed to opaque + white owing to the wetting. The party did not go to the lake camp as expected owing to threatening weather. Dinner at 7.30 - afternoon Ralph + I stretched muslin on frame for decors + I made up some glue size + effectively sized the muslin on a small frame - I spent eveg in my own room reading news papers.
