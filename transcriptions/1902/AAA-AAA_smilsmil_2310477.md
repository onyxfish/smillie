## Left Page

### Thur. July 31, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm & muggy, with many clouds and thunder — Rain fell quite heavily for a little while in the afternoon & again in the eve'g. My m'g was divided between stretching muslin on a frame for my ceiling & taking prints from the negatives taken yesterday — two portraits of Ralph were the most successful. Mr. VanHorn dined with us — an exchange for Katharine who dined with the VanHorns. I Kalsomined the muslin stretched in the m'g, & then gave up to a headache & lay upon my bed — no supper — & uncomfortable thro' the eve'g.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 1, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a very uncomfortable night, owing to headache, I got up feeling quite used up & had hard work getting thro' the day — which was disagreeable — close & muggy in the m'g early — then some very hot sunshine followed by heavy rain with thunder about. Adams appeared in the m'g & got part way done with priming the two balconies just finished by Trumbull when the rain came — He then tried to paint the ceiling of muslin on stretcher for alcove &, I'm afraid, has made a mess of it — Trumbull left early in the afternoon & will not return till early next week. At 10 a.m. I went to hotel & then to Scott Brown's — I fussed about all day but was not in condition to accomplish any thing. Eve'g. Mr. Maghee came & there was the usual séance on the north Piazza — I read newspapers.
