## Left Page

### Mon. July 21, 1902

**Weather:** Heavy showers.  **Temp:**

Pleasant m'f., clouds + sunshine + rather muggy - Trumbull did not come to work - Ralph + I stretched muslins on frames preparatory to sizing + kalsomining to be put up on my bed room ceiling. \- + I worked awhile upon some drawings of plans - about 11 - R. + I went to Harry J. fed. Beedes + then around by Putnam Camp + across Golf Links to Scott Brown's - there to Holt there home to dinner - Rain commenced soon after - gently at first but in the course of the afternoon there were tremendously heavy showers, with thunder - the rain fell in torrents + floods of water roared about us - After the worst of it - I put on my rubbers + went out to see the wreck of my road which had been nicely rolled this m'f. - It is much washed + gullied - I think Repairs may cost $50 or even more - I met W.G. Nelson looking over his damages - The rain held up for an hour or so about 6 o'clk + then resumed + is now, at 10.30 coming down heavily!

## Right Page

### Tues. July 22, 1902

**Weather:** mr. Lane + her daughter arr. Adams painted.  **Temp:**

Continued bad weather - low flying clouds + light showers during the m'f. - sulky, clearing weather in the afternoon. I was busy during the m'f. on plans + little jobbing around. After dinner I went down to Harry Beede's + he took order to get out some iron work for me - angle braces. Ralph was with me - then we went to Otis's to see John - we found him at work on W.G. Nelson's road that has been about as badly washed by yesterday's storm as mine - we got home before 6 - Mrs. + Miss Lane got there a few minutes before us - They expressed themselves much pleased with their ride over from E. down - in spite of the weather. Tea at 7 - I felt much out of sorts + in the eve'g flocked by my-self in my studio - Jim + Ralph went to the hotel to an entertainment given by a hypnotist.
