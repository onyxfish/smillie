## Left Page

### Tues. May 6, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - but nevertheless I was all day under the influence of a trying headache - To studio at 10 - & finished the scraping out of portions of Morgan's face & laid ground on plate - also on a plate of O'malley's who came in later & etched the portion (feet) that I scraped from Jequne on zinc by him. I found in Repairs pkg. from Wm. C. Pate - two steel plates, Upper Ausable &"On the Susquehanna" - & 18 pfs. from Goldsmiths Daughter. The pfs. supposed to be all first class, I found to be 24 the 30 printed on Tronoco paper & having both Autographs - one mounted on mat, with auto's & five on large mts. pfs. with both autos - the rest were defective pfs. or experiments in printing or paper without auto's & of no value - so much for the pfs that I bought in a poke. Home to lunch & after that I went to Clausen's but did not see him - Then to different galleries & saw a lot of uninteresting painting - Then to Pach Bro's saw Meyer & arranged to meet him at Borden's to-morrow at 3 - then to Charity Org. & home & had a rest before. I dressed & went to Clarence Smith's (Cath. Cook) to dinner - 124 E. 81st S. Cor. 4th Ave. - Time 6.30 - but Clarence did not come in till 7.0 but that seemed to cause no inconvenience. The ev'g passed pleasantly - later Clarence & I discussed the text of the Codicil to my will drawn as I instructed & at 9.30 I left L. Dolson - my masseur, came at 10, I was ready for him & he gave me an hour of good rubbing -

## Right Page

### Wed. May 7, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

was married 21 years ago to-day.

Overcast & muggy - To Studio at 10 - worked with crayon for awhile on a pf. of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" - lowering tone of flesh & quieting disturbing lights in the sky — changes that I propose to make on the plate. Weitenkampf came in for a few minutes - then I drew the lines on Morgan face & varnished the plate - & varnished O'Malley's plate - Home to lunch at 1 - To Borden's in 56th St at 2.45 - Meyer his assistant from Pach's came soon after & for nearly two hours we worked to get a photo. plate of the Central Room — difficult because of conflicting lights & want of light. After that I walked home - wrote a letter to W. C. Pate expressing dissapointmt. at the character of the prints described as 18 pfs. "The Goldsmith's Daughter" — Dinner at 6.30. About 9 o'clk went to Charles & Read to him the codicil to my will prepared by Clarence Smith - as he is named as trustee with Walter Cook.
