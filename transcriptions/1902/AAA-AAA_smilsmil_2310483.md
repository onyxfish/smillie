## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 12, 1902

**Weather:** Cold N.W.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold N.W. wind blew all day — many clouds — a fine Oct. day — Trumbull did not put in an appearance. Otis did some work on the road. I made up some Kalsomime \+ sizing \+ prepared the section of ceiling on stretcher — I made a scale drawing for some iron braces for north piazza \+ about 12 went with it to Harry Beede's but he was not at home — Dinner at 1 — Jim \+ Ralph went up Giant Nubble — Taking lunch with them, starting soon after 10 \+ returning soon after 3 — I put up section Kalsomine'd this m'f \+ about 4 went with Jim \+ Katharine over river trail from below Maghee's to near the hotel — found it in very bad condition from fallen trees. Walked up to a targe[?], alongside of trail, in the woods, at which a man was firing, \+ before we discovered our danger — The man skeedked away \+ I took target \+ carried it to Mr. Maghee — Home about 5 — Stretched another section for ceiling \+ Kalsomine'd it — Tea at 6.30 — [illegible] loafing — Bessie played on piano during twilight — very pleasant —

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 13, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

went up Noon mark.

Superb day — Pleasant N.W. wind, neither cold or hot — Ralph insisted upon my trying to climb Noon Mark — I doubting my ability, he confident — I consented to try on condition the he give me time first to put up a section of ceiling muslin — Katharine \+ Elig[?]'s went with Maghee on a days excursion to climb Colvin — Jim went fishing in Reserve — Ralph \+ I started on our climb at 11.º after I had tacked up the section of ceiling — We took it very deliberately \+ I felt little dis comfort — Just before 1 o'clk we got to the shoulder, about half way up, \+ there we spread ourselves upon a bare rock — warm sun — clime — sheltered from the wind \+ commanding a fine view of the valley — we took 35 m. for our lunch at 1.30 started for the top which we reached at 10 m. before 3 — having made the climb (not counting lunch time) in three hours — R. has done it in an hour. We found sheltered places on the top \+ enjoyed an hour there loafing \+ gazing at the splendid expanse of Mt. [illegible] to the south pus — at 4 o'clk. we started down \+ taking it very easily we got home at 3.35 — I at once rolled myself in a blanket on my bed \+ lay there till a little before 7 — Men I got up — Changed under clothes after having rubbed myself with whisky \+ alcohol — dressed — \+ am alight [illegible]. At 7. o'clk — [illegible] in spent in my bed room in my easy chair before a small fire — Chas. Moody came \+ I engaged to have him come \+ work for me after 23d [illegible]. Read news papers \+ to bed soon after 10.
