### Sun. July 27, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced falling early + continued during the n'f — a misty clearing in the afternoon — starlight night — Rkp. at 9.30 Until near dinner time, engaged sorting + arranging a great lot of material — locks, knobs, nails, + screws + all sorts of things that have been or may be wanted about the house + putting them in labeled boxes in the closet off my studio — Dinner at 1.30 — The Maghees, Vauthorns + Hardenburg's + Mr. Dooring came in the afternoon — I tried to tack up some of the sized muslin on my ceiling but was not successful — Eve'f, walking piazza + trying to read Silas Marner — my eyes trouble me — I find difficulty in concentrating my attention + thought + then get sleepy. ## Right Page

### Mon. July 28, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day — brilliant, breezy + warm — The most of my day was spent in efforts to put up two long strips of kalsomined muslin on my ceiling without puckering on one edge — the strips were 11 feet long + 20 in. wide, sized + kalsomined in one piece + cut length-wise thro' the middle — the work nearly broke my neck + was not satisfactory after all — Trumbull worked on the little balcony under the alcove windows of large room — Mr. Otis hauled some coarse gravel for the road. Jim was off all day at Chapel Brook + caught 14 trout. After 4 o'clk. Ralph + I went to Mossy Cascade + back to tea at 7 o'clk. Eve'f, very tired — sat in twilight till 9 o'clk — then to bed.
