## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 7, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delicious m't, mild + sunny — soft south wind — growing overcast after noon — Rain soon after 4 that fell very heavily + steadily till after 7 — then clear starlight — Have felt much better to-day + was able to keep in good work — most of the time with Moody trans-planting trees. Jul. Otis drew 3 loads of manure + one load of laundry wood (1/2 cord) + stopped at 3.30. I got in several Spruce + balsam trees north of north terrace + a couple of flat cedars by east Piazza — after dinner I went to P.O. \+ got mail — . Ev'g. Read news-papers.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 8, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a cold, stormy night snow lay white on the tops + slides of Giant — the m't. cleared deliciously, balmy in sheltered places in luminous sunshine — The afternoon was again clouded + sullen — there was some moonlight later, Rain. I spent much of the day with Moony getting evergreen trees + transplanting them about the house — Otis, with two men from E-town began laying the retaining wall on the new road bank, two teams hauling stone — After dinner I went to P.O. + then to Scott Browns — I kept out of doors + about all the afternoon in spite of unpleasant weather — Tea at 6.30 Ev'g. by by fire reading.
