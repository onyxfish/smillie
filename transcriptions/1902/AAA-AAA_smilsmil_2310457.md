## Left Page

### Thur. June 19, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull until after 9 - then rain commenced + fell very heavily for more than an hour, after that the weather cleared slowly to mugginess + at night it came off exceedingly pleasant. I got up at about 6.30 + soon after 7 went to 110 + worked at packing my trunk till 9 - then to C's had bkf. It rained so heavily that I did not return to the house till after 10.30. I got a carpenter from Miller - he headed up the barrel already packed + planed off the old markings + on crated boxes. By noon I had trunks very nearly packed + the freight boxes already to ship. Then R. + I went out - to news man - ordered Pic. - Pat sent to Pleasantville 15¢ - then to P.O. Station f. then to Bank + drew $200 in small bills + coin for dinner use. Then to Bordman + p's his bill - then to Jackson's + arranged with him to send for the trunks + freight - then to G.C. Station + bought RR tickets to Lake Placid - but could get no section in sleeper - was told to return at 5 — then to Century with R. - had lunch - paid + core - then to 110 - Jackson at door - took freight - to return for trunks at H. ## Right Page

### Fri. June 20, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - awoke before 7 after a comparatively comfortable night. Got up - our "sleeper" did not go beyond Saranac Lake - so, at 7.25 we transferred there to a day coach + got to Lake Placid about 8 - Drove at once to the Club. + presented Nicoll's letter to Mr. Ira Gallup, manager - we had bkf. before I met him - was very cordially rec'd. He handed me over to Mr. Allen, his athletic man + he took us about - then R. + I walked to the Stevens House on the other side of the lake + back to Club soon after 12 where I had a rest till dinner at 1 - after dinner R. + I went out in a boat for an hour - then we walked to the Ruisseaumont Ho. + after a look at the interior of the house we went down to the lake shore + got a boat + rowed for nearly two hours crossing the lake nearly to the White Face Mtn. + back - then walked to L.P. Club - R. + I lay upon our beds + each had a nap - then R. went out for awhile in a canoe after our supper at 7 - Early, a nice lady entertained us for awhile in singing - chatted with a stranger - read news-paper + to bed soon after 10 - in a very pleasant room upon exceedingly good beds - the manner of serving the food, while it is good, is somewhat peculiar - Mr. Gallup has been very attentive or, at any rate, affability so.
