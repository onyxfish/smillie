## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 4, 1902

**Weather:** Clear & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear & cold - to studio before 10 & painted on Borden interior until 11.40 - then down town to 30 Broad St. & saw Mr. Wilson - he said I must see Stedman, so I rode up town & saw S - at his apartments at 16th St. He cannot decide upon the size of J. P. Morgan etching without further consultation - then to Geo. B. Post's - 33 2, 17th St - & saw Mr. Thomas & from him got another print from a reduction from large drawing of front of new Stock exchange - then home to lunch at 2 o'clk - then to studio & painted till after 4 - then to Irving Wile's studio - 106 W, 65th S. - talking about Julia Marlowe's painting for which I have taken a fancy & would like to make an important mezzotint translation of it. As I feared, I found that he had agreed with the Copley Print Concern of Boston - only a week or so ago - to let them have the copyright - Wiles assured me that he was extremely sorry - he will prefer to have the painting reproduced in blk & white by mezzotint engraving rather than by any photo-process. I said if I could devise any plan by which it would be possible for me to make a mezzotint plate after it he would do all he could to aid me in carrying it out - He gave me the address of Curtis & Cameron - Pierce Bldg. Boston, Mass. . . I think I shall write to them & try to open negotiations with them.
Then home - dinner - ever pretty much used up with headache.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 5, 1902

**Weather:** Cool, pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool, pleasant day - rather windy - up late & help - after 10 - to studio at noon & opened windows to give it an airing - then walked on sunny side of Park Ave. till near 2 o'clk - then went to Charl's for a few minutes & home to dinner at 2. After dinner had a talk with Jim & Ralph - then a long nap - Tea at 6.30 - Ever wrote to Mr. Mr. Wilson & then a letter to Curtis & Cameron - Pierce Bldg. Boston - Copley Print people - to whom Irving Wiles has transferred the copyright of his painting of Julia Marlowe. - In my note I made a proposition to C. & C. that w. enable me to make a large mezzotint plate under their copyright - then I mailed the note to Wiles for his approval & to bed late.
