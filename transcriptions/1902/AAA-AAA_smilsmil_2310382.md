## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 18, 1902

**Weather:** again clear & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

again clear & cold — Got up with my eyes much inflamed — right eye bloodshot — Got card from K. &c. that S.Y.V. would be ready to go on with extra india ph. from the Borden plate — so I hurried, getting there soon after 10 — he had already started — Took six ph. — I ret. to studio &until 1.30 was engaged backing them down — home to lunch back at 2.30 — at 3 was too tired to go to N.A.D. etching class — so I went home — lay down on my bed & had a nap — up at 5 — dressed & at 6.30 was at Lotos club on invitation of W.E.Co. to share the dinner given in honor of E.A. Abbey — There was a very full house, over 200 sitting down to dinner — Before dinner I was hustled into a small room where a little group was — photo — Abbey & Lawrence forming center. David Bispham & Jon A's Right — Lawrence — Lotos Pres. W.T. Evans & bro. Geo. on his left — Geo. & I were end men — A Reproduction is to appear in Tribune of to-morrow — The dinner, per se, was good — The speeches, after, were sometimes amusing & sometimes both amusing & good — the best of the evey was E.C. Stedman's — Dillman's was unexpectedly good — he made some points quite neatly — Lawrence was, of course, facile & Abbey in Reply, was becomingly modest — all of the speakers were laudatory without measure — Praise was spread over Abbey with a shovel & rubbed in with a broom — At the close, Abbey was again upon his feet to say a few words — I wondered what he could wish to say for I had seen that he was squirming under things that were said of him — With eyes downcast & considerable hesitation he said a few deprecatory words & then — "gentlemen, I shall go from here a very humble man —" He was evidently sincere & could hardly have said any thing better.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 19, 1902

**Weather:** Still clear & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still clear & cold — B'kf's. at 10.30 — to studio about noon & opened windows to give it an airing — a short walk in Park Ave — then to Charls — Home to Dinner at 2 — about \+ George came & we went to Studio that he might see the Borden painting & sug'd men home — Charl came in & the 4 ge. stayed till 6.30 — Tea — W.F. Sheldon came & stayed till near 10. I have done little else than loaf all day altho' I have much to do.
