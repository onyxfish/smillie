## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 14, 1902

Catharine Cook married \-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine clear cold day \- Got my smaller div? \- This mo'g \+ for some time after 6bf: was engaged writing checks, pay't of bills \+ notes. It was nearly 11 when I got to studio \- worked on Borden painting for an hour \- then to Scribner's \+ left with Mr. Smith the pp \+ title pages of Borden Cat \- then to 2°. Nat. Buk \+ home to lunch \- to studio after 2 \+ painted till 4 \- then home \+ with Jim went to the church, 35' St. \+ Mad. ave. at 5 o'clk. We went into a side seat but Tom Cook saw us \+ haled us up to the front to seats reserved for the family. There we witnessed the sacrifice \- the church was decorated to some extent with the Xmas greens etc \- all desirable seats were occupied by a suitably dressed \+ interested audience. The bridal party was reasonably prompt \- not more than 15 or 20 m. late \- The march down the aisle was stately \+ almost painfully deliberate \- the bride was composed, evidently under severe restraint \- but no less evidently, aforiously happy \- \+, of course, very handsome \- The mother was both proud \+ happy \- After the ceremony Jim \+ I went to the house \+ found it crammed \+ overflowing \- Jim went in but I could not \- many, me giving me a verbal invitation, said that no invitations would be sent out \- only a very quiet affair. The Throop girls were not invited \- I feel that this is an affront to me \- Jim reports that there was a fine supper \+ every preparation for a great crowd. Home \- dinner at 6.30. Lucy Chase came in \- later Eleanor \- Katie \+ Wm Elkin \+ the ev'g passed pleasantly till 10 o'clk Jim Ret? to New-Haven by the 8 o'clk \- train \-

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 15, 1902

Eleanor Cook ret? to Boston \-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

much milder \- pleasant day. To studio after 10 \+ painted on Borden Library till 11.45 \- then to H. D. Allen's in 47° St. to have him examine my mouth which has given me much pain since his work last friday. He made some changes \+ promised relief \- home at 12.45 \- lunch \- Immediately after I went with Eleanor to the train \+ saw her seated in her P.C. seat on 2 o'clk. train. Then to studio \+ painted till 4 \- at 5 I went to 23° St. \+ did some unsuccessful shopping \- home at 6 \- dinner at 6.30 \- a stupid headachy ev'g. Many has kept in her bed all day owing to a cold.
