## Left Page

### Tues. April 22, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably warm day, getting above 80°. I went to studio at 9.30 \+ to work on Morgan head. Maurice Sterne came in bet. 11 \+ 12 \+ stayed for some time at 12.30 I went to 2\. Nat\. Bank \+ then home to lunch \- to studio at 2.30 and took infos. of \+ worked on Morgan. I have got it at last so that it looks something like him but technically the head is very bad \- bad texture \+ bad line \- I am much ashamed of it \- but can do no better now. I shall lay it aside for a few days \+ try to forget it. Home at 5.30 \+ rested till dinner at 6.30 \- Every Chase came in \- Then young Wm Mueford \- he left at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 23, 1902

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably hot day \- Ther. getting up to about 86°. I got up feeling very rag-ged \- having had little sleep after 3 A.m. I determined to stop work \+ take some rest. About 11 o'clk I went to Scribner's taking the Borden plate. I saw W. H. L. Smith intending to tell him that I could not promise to let him have the second Borden plate before I left town, but he anticipated by saying that he had told Mr. Borden that it would not be finished before the fall \- That was a great relief to me \- I felt that a load had been lifted from of me. I should have written that a [illegible] I rec'd notice from [illegible] \+ W. freight office that my boxes \+ crates had arr\. from Montrose \- I went to Jackson's \+ p \- \+ left order to get them \+ Then I went to barbers \+ had my hair cut \- There to studio, got Borden plate \+ to Scribners, as I have written \- Then to Silo's Sales Room in [illegible] S\- got there just as cat. No. 61 \- Goldsmith's Daughter. was reached \- no plate \- passed \- I saw W. Wm C. [illegible] \+ left with him a bid for W. G. [illegible] "Susque-hanna River" \- my eng. \- $2.50 \- I saw some plates sold for 75¢ each. Then home to lunch \- Studio at 2 \- clearing up till after 3 \- Then [illegible] came with the Montrose freight \- my big standing press not there \- but the printing table was \+ that I sent up to N. A. of D. Then home \+ had a rest before dinner Eve\. at 8.30 Century Com. on Admissions \- had an agreeable eve\. \+ after the meeting a pleasant chat with Mr. Howard Mansfield about the eyes at the Grolier of Haden's etchings. Home after 11.
