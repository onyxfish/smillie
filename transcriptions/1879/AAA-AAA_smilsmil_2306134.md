## Left Page

### January, Friday 24, 1879

Mixed day — clear mrg, cloudy
P.M. — Got to studio at 9.45 — made
pen sketch of [illegible] Tiffany's picture —
then to Morris (3m) to Knordler's —
Schreyer's — Quartley's — then to Harker's —
at 2.30 had lunch — then to G.
P. Powell's — then to Richardson
the engraver — then to Sackett's —
then to the Photo Eng. Co. — then
again to Knordler's — saw young [illegible] —
talked with him about eng. —
— at 6 reached studio — the whole
day having been given to the
W.C. Ill. Cat. Think I have got
ahead of the Graphic people, as
a lot of the cuts were put on Harker's
press to-day for next week's issue —
Tea at 7 — at 8.30 went in to Emma
J's for a few minutes — then to [illegible] —
there until 10, — then to studio
— there until 10.45 — then
to 37th St. & to bed —

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 25, 1879

Bright pleasant mrg — got
to studio about 9.30 & for a time
at work upon Cat. Sketches, then
to N.A. & at 11.30 went down town
to Sackett's — back at 1.30 after
having arranged the red for printing
for cover — Had dinner & again
at N.A., worked as long as I
could see — then to studio and
had a short rest & tea at
6.30. After that, dressed & at
about 9 went to Union League —
Showing exhn of pictures — Morris —
Turner up stairs — all by electric
light — very fitful & unpleasant in
its color (the electric light) — at
10 went to 37th St. & to bed —
Very high wind & very cold —
