## Left Page

### July, Monday 21, 1879

Still very pleasant weather. After my warning last night
I have worked very carefully to-day — In fact, I am not
able to work more than carefully. Chas. Rec'd. to N.Y. by
6.30 train. I spent the m'g
laying ground & dragging
parts of Osgood etching also
adding parts. Dinner at
12.30 — Afternoon, biting.
Tea at 6 — Every. went to
A.P. I's & got some medicine
then a walk with Father —
the rest of ev'g in Rocking
chair & to bed at 10.30.

Rec'd. a letter from S.R. Koehlen
praising my etchings very highly.
He says my Jacque is "a glorious
piece of work" & he defys any
Frenchman to beat it.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 22, 1879

Muggy warm m'g. After bkf'st
wrote along letter to Koehler —
then a short walk — did not get
to my printing until 11 o'clk.
Then to work proving Osgood
etching — Dinner at 12.30 &
at 2 again at work. About
4 o'clk. thunderstorms began
to come one after another followed
all the rest of afternoon & most of the
night — very violent — White house's
shoe factory struck by lightning there —
grand sight — livid light — one part of
the heavens on fire, the landscape
looking like a Red sea — the other
part of the sky like a pool of ink
while the roar of other storms coming
on about us was terribly portentous.
I got but 11 pfs. Tea at 6 — very
much fit on my bed — To bed
for the night soon after 10.
Rec'd. pfs. of "Up the Hill" from
Juengling.
