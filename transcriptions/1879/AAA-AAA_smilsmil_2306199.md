## Left Page

### June, Thursday 5, 1879

Very warm but with a pleas-
ant breeze — Having laid a
ground over up the Hill I etched
over many parts of it — I also
worked on Water Color Hay Ride
near Peekskill — putting in
Chickens & getting it ready
for Century as Sat. evef. To
lunch at 2 — Biking etching
& fussed over plate all the
rest of P.M. overcast P.m.
Thunderstorms. W. & N. of us — tea
in studio between 7 & 8. Heavy
thunderstorm from 8 to 9 —
Wrote a number of notes & at
10.30 to 37° S. & to bed at 11.
By that time the rain had
almost ceased.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 6, 1879

Charming day — fresh & breezy —
Afterbkfst — went to Bellevue's to
make arrangements to make sketch
from his Hebert — Then to Studio
to work on etching "Churchyard"
To lunch at 1.30 — Went to Efs.
with Shattuck's pkg — Continued
etching — Miss Culbertson called
& Delnoce & Treadaway & others.
My mordant being very strong
had ground all pulled up and
I had to clean off my plate
before it was all etched over —
Had my tea about 8 o'clk. Then
went to Mrs. Ireland's 331 E.
17th St. W. & Mrs. Coleman there —
Back to Studio at 9.45. Heavy
rain with wind, thunder & lightning
between 5 & 6 P.M. Evef. very
breezy & almost cold. T. 37° S.
& to bed before 11 o'clk.
