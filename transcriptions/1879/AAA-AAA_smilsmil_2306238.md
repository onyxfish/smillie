## Left Page

### August, Sunday 24, 1879

Delightful day but my head in a
dismal condition. Up late & help' at
9 oclk — after that went out along the
shore & scrambled on the rocks, part
of the time with my eyes shut, to
relieve the pain back of them —
Back to hotel about noon & wrote
a letter to Sam. Coleman & one to
Miss Hallowells of Chicago &c.
Then to dinner — after that I
gave up to my headache & lay
upon the bed until 6 o'clk. Wm.
May came & invited us to tea.
Gee went, but I kept on my
back until later, then to parlor
& heard Wm. May preach a short
sermon from Romans 13. ch. &
14\. v. — Life is a hard road any way
& we should be careful not to
put an offense or stumbling
block in the way of our brethren.
To bed before 11.
