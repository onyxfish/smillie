## Left Page

### June, Friday 13, 1879

Charming day — immediately after b'clp. Started out to look for Wright, went to a number of places but could get no trace of the scamp. Met Clare Brainerds in the Street — learned that he had been swindled by Wright — found that he had been to Dr. Guerusey in 23d St. & offered one of my pictures for sale — Dr. G. gave me a number of names of men that W. had sold books & pictures to — I called on them. Don Cornelius Vanderbilt — At last went to Dr. Marcy's & found my Owl's Head picture — learning that W. was expected soon I waited tin half an hour. W. walked right into my hands. He was unabashed — A friend of Dr. M's has bought owl'sHead, arranged that the check was to be paid to me — W. offered me an order for the Rough Sport but I told him no, he must make me to it — so we went to 2. R. R. offices, foot of Duane St. & there I got it — Back to Studio at 2 o'clk & had lunch — little done during the p.m — had tea at studio & at 8.30 q.p. & I went to Anna Cook's — there until 10.30 — then to 37. 5t & to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 14, 1879

Altho' warm, a delightful breeze stirring — got to studio's at 9.30 & to work on my etching after Jacque. By 1 o'clk. I had completed the etching & biting — cleared plate & went to luncheon — then back & to work Faking p.p. — About 5. J.W. Wood came in and I took out the head of his Man of Peace, in the cabinet. There laid two grounds for him — worked until nearly there — had tea in studio — Bellows came in — at 8.30 went to Miller's & got umbrella, then to Kimball's to see who of them had gone to Shrewsbury — there until 10 — then to studio, then to 37 5t — & to bed soon after 11.
