## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 1, 1879

Clear + very mild — Before
6&p- Read some of Monaldini's
niece — after, read some more
then up on the hill + spent
the rest of the mtg up trees
sawing out dead wood +
painting wounds. Elisha
Mulford called on me —
Dinner at 1.15 alone as Mr +
Mrs. W. J. J. are out somewhere
dining — after dinner finished
"S. Monaldini's Niece". Went to
my lot + spent the afternoon
climbing trees + painting
over bare places. Tea at 6.30
I went to Courtwrights —
no one at home — then to Wm
Jessup's — there until aftrng —
then to Dan Sayre's — there
until near 10. To bed at 9.30
Ther. 85°.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 2, 1879

Still very pleasant weather.
After breakfast I had a walk
\+ spent a little time on my hill
then got out Bruce's work and
commenced a large sketch
of Mirror Lake — Stanza VIII — could
not feel like work but stuck
to it until dinner at 1. After
that tried it again but it was
no use, my head hurt me +
I felt generally incompetant, so
again went to my hill + fussed
among the trees until near 5 —
then caught uncle W's horse "Ben" —
got him saddled + bridled + went
out for a ride — perfect failure —
he is as high as a church, as hard
as a mill gate, stubborn as a mule
\+ slower than time — besides all this
he is a bad stumbler — soon got back.
Tea after 6. Twelf, again went to Court-
wrights + again failed to get in — then to
Widow Jessup's — Henry J— there — to a Fast for
Syrias on the 11th. There until aftrng —
then to W. J. J's + to bed soon after 10.
