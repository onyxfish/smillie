## Left Page

### September, Saturday 27, 1879

fair night's rest, everything
considered. Blkp. on board boat
at 7 — Reached dock about 8 —
detained by baggage which came
on Newport boat. Then to D.L. +
W. R.R. checked trunk, then to Photo
Eng. Co. (67 Park Pl. then to studio —
then to Gilmore Garden + spent
an hour + more watching the
walking match between Powell
Weston + others — six day match
this being the last. Then downtown
saw Charle for a few minutes, then
had lunch, then saw Will for a few
minutes, then to D.L+W. + off on 1 P.M.
Train — got to New Milford at 7.37.
no stage, had to hunt around for
team — away at 8.20 + got to [illegible]
at about 10.20 — chatted awhile
\+ to bed at 11.
Glorious moonlight night.
Rain before morning.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 28, 1879

Dull overcast day. Blkp.
at about 9 — after that went
to my studio — place very
forlorn looking — overgrown with
weeds — lightning destroyed some
of my finest hemlocks — dead —
wood in the tops of one of my big
maples. Families of one kind +
another with a number of the
smaller trees that I have set
out, about all the milf, dinner
at 1. Mrs. Clark + Bard there —
out again among my trees
in the afternoon — walking about
town until 6. at 7 went to Baptist
Ch. with Uncle Wm. M. Sherwood
the minister, made a pleasant
discourse. Heavy thunderstorm
during service — out at 8. W.J.
S. — Mrs. Clark + Bard to tea at
8.30 at W.J.S.'s — general chatting
until after 10 — then company
separated — to bed at 11 —
Beautiful night.
