## Left Page

### October, Friday 3, 1879

Another very pleasant day — not quite so warm as yesterday.
Up at 7 feeling like a boiled owl having had but little rest & feeling
weak & discouraged. After [illegible],
went to my lot & waited until
10 o'clk for Robt. West. Then he came
& chopped down the hemlock killed
by lightning. As I was unable to work
upon Bruce's work I spent the mng
about my studio & fussing with my
trees. Dinner at 1 — After that
again at my trees, cutting out the
tops of a big beech, got so warm
that I could not go riding with
Uncle Wm. Went down from it
3.30 — then up to the house & sat in
big chair warming my back until
tea time. Well, sat in same
chair until 10 o'clk, then to bed.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 4, 1879

Charming mng. After a very restless
night, went out & walked in the garden
[illegible] at 8.15. Then wrote notes to
Charl & Belle [illegible] — mailed them at
10 — then to my lot. Robt. West and
helper cutting up the hemlock. I
spent part of mng. in beech tree
cutting out the tops — A rest before
dinner at 1 — then again to my
beech tree & quite more myself out
getting out the tops. Sawed it off when
it was 10 in. or a foot through, so it
required considerable engineering
to get it down without injury to
me or the tree. Tea at near 7 —
[illegible] in big chair reading
in [illegible] & to bed before 10.
