## Left Page

### May, Saturday 24. 1879.

A very pleasant day — got to studio about 9.30 and to work on sketch of page block for Harper's Weekly of sketches on the Saguenay. Lunch at 2 — worked on sketch until 4, then with [illegible] to foot of Barclay St. & missed train boat by 5 seconds. Fred Smillie then waiting for us. Took 4.30 train & went with him to his home at East Orange. took tea there — then 7.13 train to Newark — crossed to Market St. Station & took 8 train to Elizabeth — there took 8.30 train to Plainfield & got to Uncle G's house about 9.30 — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 25. 1879.

Charming day — Up about 7 & had breakfast soon after 8 — after that sauntered out on R. R. & about until 10. then went with Uncle G, Aunt Ollie [illegible] & the Girls to church & heard the Rev. Mr. Ketchum upon Doctrine & incidentally, the necessity of a formulated belief or creed. After service, again to Uncle G's & had dinner about 1.30 — at 3 I walked up to the Tweedy's & spent the rest of P.M. with them on their piazza — back to Uncle G's at 6 & had tea. during which a charming shower fell — later it rained quite steadily — After an eve'g of chatting, got to bed about 10.
