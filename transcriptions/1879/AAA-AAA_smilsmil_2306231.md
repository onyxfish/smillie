## Left Page

### August, Sunday 10, 1879

Brilliant blue day, with fresh
cool breeze. Spent much of the
mf on the rocks near the surf
Reading the "wreck of the Grosvenor"
also did considerable walking
about until dinner time at
1 o'clk. After that, for a time
on piazza — then to my room
& wrote a letter to J. M. Falconer.
About 4 o'clk a party went over
to Rafe's Chasm — on the rocks until
about 6 — then back by the shore
& had tea — sweet music on
piano by Mrs. McKenzie & singing
by Mr. Beate. Near 11 when I
got to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 11, 1879

Still cloudless — up with a mis-
erable headache. After [illegible] went
to the pier to see a party off on
an excursion in the steamer
"White Giants". I was invited &
joined. Made for Thatcher's Island
climbed to the lantern of one of the
lighthouses — inspected signal service
station, then again off, rounded
Cape Ann & rowed into Pigeon Cove
som after 12 — went to the
Hotel — met Mrs. Huleman (Daise Hartrack)
dinner at 1 — after that went with
Miss Dunham to a piece of Pine
woods & enjoyed breeze, shade, &
view of the scenery for half an hour —
then a walk about & at 3.30 again
we were off — Entered Annisquam
river & out through a short canal
at Gloucester & home at 6 o'clk. After
a very pleasant if not exciting
day — tea on piazza most of
the evef & to bed shortly after
10.
