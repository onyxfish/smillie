## Left Page

### November, Sunday 2, 1879

Mild, charming morning —
got up feeling much better.
ate a light help, & spent the
mrt reading & going about
the house. Dinner at 1 o'clk.
at which I took a wine glass
full of sweet cider and in 20
minutes I had a spasm of colic
such as I never suffered before.
The Dr. was sent for, but it was
all over in a quarter of an hour
and during the rest of the day
I felt very easy — Afternoon
bleat & overcast. Matt & E2 to
tea — they left soon after 8 and
before g it began to snow heavily.
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 3, 1879

Quite a fall of very light snow
during the night. Father got
away for N.Y. city at 8 a.m. The
weather soon cleared. The snow
melted & the wind blew. I
packed all my etching material
into valise then went into town
to Hps. Office, to Matts & to Man's.
Home then to Dinner at 12.45 —
P.M. spent loafing & making
pen sketches in a letter until
about 4.30 — Then I went to
Prof. Ritter's & made a call on
Prof. & Mad. R. — then walked to
Main St. & Hps. Office. Home to tea
at 6. Svef at 4.30 called on Mr.
W. Hasbrouck — all out, then made
wedding call on the daughter,
Mrs. Jackman, at 25 Cannon St.
Home at 9.30 — to bed at 10.45
