## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 11, 1879

Disagreeable day — Got to studio
a little before 10 — bothered with
a pain in the back of my head —
Set palette — & commenced a
sketch 14×18, of a bit of Livingston
lane — Po'k — as I saw it Holiday
week — Rain commenced before
noon — Lunch at 1.30 — Worked
as long as I could see & got
my sketch finished — getting
some of the color effect that I
was after — Annie — [illegible] lady
love of years ago, called, seeking
his address. Heavy rain in
the eveg — Tea at 6.30, wrote
& fussed until after 8 then to
N.A. there met Mr. Hurlbut
& Mr. Hatch, of Cleveland. — Mr.
H. fancies my large twilight —
To 37 S. at 10.30 & to bed.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 12, 1879

Overcast wlf., but gradually
cleared, until in the evening
it was beautiful weather — Got
to studio a little before 10 &
soon got to work repainting an
old sketch 8×12 — a view of [illegible]
Mt. [illegible] Co, Penna — Smeared
it down with a [illegible] & went
to work de novo. To lunch after
2 o'clk — the back & painted
as long as I could see, getting
my sketch done — a very good
one for me. It bears no resemblance
to the original either in color or
composition — Tea at 6.30 — then over
to N.A. to see who had bought my
"Diven's Creek" — Jno. H. Sherwood
called about a new studio building.
At 8.30 mention to Coleman's — musical
entertainment — there until 10. There
with Dinsa — Julie S. & Fred — Went up
to the Fulton's. Received — £ 60 S.
there until 11.45 — a very pleasant
time — then rode down to 38 S. with
the Cook's — to 37 S. & to bed — at —
12.30
