## Left Page

### May, Friday 30, 1879.

Rockaway —

Charming day — Got to studio
som aftrg — Grand display
on 5th Ave., formation of Decoration
Day Procession. Abt 1.5 went to
Emma J's — with her to [illegible]
of E. 34 St., meeting by the way
her Mother & sister Mary. Mr. &
Mrs. Caswell & Mrs. Blake — we
crossed to Hunter's Pt & took
11.15 train for Rockaway, reaching
there at 12.35 — my first visit — we
went to "Atlantic Garden" — one
visitors — had everything to ourselves —
we rolled in the sand & loafed
until near 2 when we had a
grand lunch served by Mr. G.
under the cedars — table cloth
— napkins, dinner cards & bill
of fare — at tables, on Battin [illegible]
cloth until near 4 — Ret'd to
N.Y. by 4.30 train — met Mr. & Mrs.
Saml. Coleman, just Ret'd from a
days visit to Easthampton — Got
to N.Y. about 6 — home with Mrs. Blake
then went to Emma's — there until
8.15 — then to Anna C's — there until
a little after 10, then to 37th St &
to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 31, 1879.

Very hot day — After bfst. went
to Anna C's to tell her that I
could not go on the Yeltville
walk proposed for to-day —
then to studio & to work on
Etchings, all day making
outlines on tracing paper &
transferring them to my grounds.
Very much troubled with a
diarrhea that commenced
yesterday — but managed to
"put in" a good day's work.
Tea in studio about 7 & at
9 with Geo. & Bellows, got
to Dr. Yale's, 438 Mad. Ave,
informal Etching club meeting
to consider illustrating E.C.
Stedman's Poem "The heart
of New England" — to 37th St.
after 11 & right to bed.
