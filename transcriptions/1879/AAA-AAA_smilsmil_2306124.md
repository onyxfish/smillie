## Left Page

### January, Thursday 2, 1879

Snow all day — afterblkp: went to Mitchells (druggish) & at 10,30 to work on my etching with burnisher & graver — At 12,15 out for a walk in the snow. Dinner at 1 — after dinner, proving my plate & found no end of troubles — the subject is so different from anything I have yet done that it requires very different proving — clouds cleared away, but a gale of wind — air full of flying snow — went out for a walk & enjoyed the hub-bub for awhile but a sudden prickling pain in my left eye sent me home quickly — Tea soon after six. Most of the even spent with a cloth wet with Pond's [illegible] on my eye — Wet down paper for printing to-morrow — to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 3, 1879

The wind still holds high carnival — My eye gives me no trouble but I do not dare go out — Afterblkp: laid re-biting ground on foreground of etching & the rest of work was absent rebiting — dinner at 12.45 — Afternoon, proving & at 4.30 got a proof on Japan paper that seemed to me very near what I am after — Well, grinding in & fixing more paper until 9.30 — wrote a note to [illegible] & had a [illegible] tel. Mess. come for it & take it to [illegible]. Before going to bed found that during my walk yesterday even I had frosted slightly my right cheek & on the chords of the neck under both ears. To bed at 11 —
