## Left Page

### May, Monday 26, 1879

Up at 6.30. Delicious morning —
Rb up about 7.30 & in to N.Y. by
8.30 train. up to studio by 10.30
found letters to answer which
took some time. Then finished
up Saguenay sketch and
commenced one of Quebec
& picturesque bits about —
Lunch after 2 — worked
on Quebec sketch all P.m —
Tea about 7 — at 8 went to
155 W. 21 St — & saw Leila Martin
about Easthampton — L.I. — at
8.45 to Fanny K's — Quartette
playing — Mr. Coleman. Fanny
Emma J. & Anna C — Charming —
there until 11 — then went
home with Emma & Mary J —
key forgotten — gives in dark
studio vestibule while their gallant
goes to the haunts of above, gets
his key — opens doors forgives —
returns key & gets to 37 c 8 — &
bed after midnight.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 27, 1879

Charming m'g with Easterly wind.
at 9 o'clk. at Grand Central depot
met Anna Cook & Mary Ireland &
went to Dobb's Ferry. Signaled
for the ferryman who came at 10.15
in his sail boat — we sailed with
him for nearly an hour when he
landed us at an old dock at the
foot of the Palisades & we climbed
to the top by the bed of a brook. we
reached the top a little before noon
& found a subject for study & settled
myself — At 1.15 had lunch — weather
changed — high wind & by 3 some rain.
we wandered through the woods delib-
erately & by 4 got to Sneeden's Landing.
crossed to Dobb's Ferry under double reefed
mainsail — then walked to Hastings &
took 5.16 train to N.Y. getting in at 6.5 caught
in heavy shower on way to studio — found
two letters from Chas G. Loring of Boston
museum of fine arts — thanking my boy
of etching "Cedars by moonlight" & offering
$100 for painting "On the Hill Top" — I [illegible]
accepting — tea at 7 — every "in studio
until near 10 — then to 37. 8 — & to bed.
