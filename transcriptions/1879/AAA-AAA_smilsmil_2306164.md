## Left Page

### March, Thursday 27, 1879

Miserable east wind & rain. Got to Studio's at 10 o'clk. but failed to get to work before 12. At 1 o'clk Mr. Lord came, then Mr. & miss Mitherbee, I devoted my afternoons to them this being their last day. Mrs. W. paid me $30.00. At 5 o'clk I went to 31st S. & dressed & at 6.30 at Mr. Adams, 125 E. 36th S. a pleasant little dinner party to Misses Eliza & Helen Ripley & Mr. & Mrs. Whiting & Wheelan Reid & Mr. & Mrs. Chas. Mitchells were among the dozen who sat down to dinner. At Table from 7 until 10.30, left soon after 11 & to bed by 11.30. [illegible — ~5 lines]

## Right Page

### March, Friday 28, 1879

Very charming day — after bkfst went to Mr. Adams for my glasses that I carelessly left upon the dining table. Mr. A. & the ladies were in the parlor & I made a short, informal & very early morning call. Got to studio about 10 — m'f was largely wasted in a variety of ways. Got to work on pen sketch of Fort George, Lake George, for the Harpers. Took a very light lunch in studio about 2.30 — then out for a short walk. Worked on pen sketch until after 6 — then out for another walk. Tea at 7.30 — soon after 8 went to Colman's — Mrs. Jno. E. Williams there — with daughters. Mrs. Colman & her Nephew played the 9th Symphony — left at 10.15 to studio — wrote a note to Father & Mother congratulating them upon their wedding anniversary. Then to 31st S. & to bed at 11.45.
