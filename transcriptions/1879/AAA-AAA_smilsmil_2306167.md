## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 2. 1879.

Clean, cold wintry day. Got to studio shortly after 9 & to work on "The Butters Pantry — Camp Manhattan —
lake Geo." for Harpers. At 11 o'clk plates presses arrived on Hoe's Truck — it was 2 before they were delivered — then I had lunch — Mr. Geo. Whitney & Miss M. called — I worked until 6 — then to Anna C.'s & had dinner — she was suffering from head ache but was able to go with me to Wallack's where we joined a party of Mr. & Mrs. Colman — Mr. & Mrs. Louis Tiffany — Geo. S. & [illegible] — and saw "A Scrap of Paper" — Got to 37 St. at about 11.30 — To bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 3. 1879.

Bright, pleasant with followed by showers; a heavy snow squall & a cold, blustering night —
Got to studio about 9.30, worked for awhile on "The Butter's Pantry" — at 1, with Geo., went to the Albemarle & lunched with Messrs Geo. Whitney & [illegible] Lewis. Back soon after 2 & then the machinist came & put up my printing press. Worked on sketch of [illegible] Dave [illegible] until about 6 — at 6.40 at Anna Cook's & took tea — after leaving her at Arnold's — made a short call on the Paynes — at 9 at Mr. Adams' & made my dinner party call, there until near 10.30 — (Miss Helen Ripley there) Then to 37 St. & to bed at 11.
