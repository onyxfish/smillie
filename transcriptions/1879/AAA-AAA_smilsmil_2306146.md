## Left Page

### February, Monday 17, 1879

Very pleasant mrng. but before
night, before noon a miserable
storm set in, first sleet & rain
then snow. Got to studio
before 10 & prepared to begin
work on 33 × 44 — Adirondacks.
Got my big frame up on its
legs & washed the face of the
picture — then to work, painting
over sky & distance. At 1 o'clk.
Miss C. came & spent the
p.m. painting. In the evng
the snow was so heavy &
I felt so under the weather
from a cold that I did not
go to Mrs. Dr. Lusk's Reception
but instead went to Coleman's
where Mr. C. read to us the
Benjamin article in Harper's
for March on "the Tendencies of
American Art." To 34th St. at 11
& right to bed.

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 18, 1879

Overcast & miserable day. Got
to studio a little before 10 and
to work on 33 × 44 — painting
as loosely as I could over the
middle distance — Don't feel
a bit like work — Lunch as
usual about 2 o'clk. About
4 Uncle Willie came in and
later, Father. Uncle W. has come
to the city to attend the burial
of the body of Robt. Hinshelwood
just brought from Boston where he
died a day or two ago. At 6.30
I went to Walter Cook's — cor. 5th ave.
& 14th St. to dinner — Surprised to
meet there Anna C. & Emma S. &
Will K. We sat down to a French
dinner at 4 & were at table for more
than two hours — then for an hour
or more talked over plans for an
Adirondack house. Home with
Anna at 11.30 — then to 34th St. & to
bed at midnight, but not to
sleep until after 1.
