## Left Page

### August, Saturday 2. 1879.

Extremely hot day — Bkfst. before 8 — about 9. Mr. Dodge rowed Gel.
Lyman & me over to Kettle Island.
I spent much of the morng. in the
hollow of a rock, giving myself
up to enjoyment of surf & air —
Afterwards joined Mr. Dodge & Miss
Knowlton (Reporter of Hunt's Talks on
Art) — back to dinner abt. 30 — after
that, a short nap — then with Gel.
walked to a piece of woods some
distance from the house. Suffered
very much from the heat. At last
found a pleasant place on the
rocks & remained there until
near 10 o'clk. Then to Hotel and
had tea at 9. On the rocks
from 8 to 10 watching moon &
tide — then to hotel for a short
time & watched the haps —
then to bed abt 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 3. 1879.

Exceedingly hot day — Bkfst. bet.
8 & 9. — Service in the parlor at 10.30
by a resident minister — an old
man & a very blue Presbyterian. He
got through at 11.45 — then more
loitering on piazza — dinner soon
after 1 — then wrote to Durham
& Chicago, Mamma J. — & home —
About 4 Gel. & I started to go over
to Rafe's Chasm but gave it up
before starting. Some of the time
was very pleasantly spent on
the Jouns Cottage piazza — Tea
about 7 — another evening on the
rocks watching tide & moonlight
with the Jouns, Mr. McKenzie, Miss
Shaw & others. Back to room &
to bed shortly after 10.
