## Left Page

### September, Monday 29, 1879

mild, overcast with clearing
into a very pleasant day.
Mrs spent running around
& writing letter to George.
Dinner at 1 — after that
reading & writing until after
3, then went to studio &
fussed there in doors & out,
climbing trees & doing carpenter
work. Rev Mr. Sherwood came
over & visited me for a while
when I was in the top of my
big maple getting out dead
wood — to tea at 6. Suey
went to P.O. called on Ben M.
& saw his autophone that enables
him to hear without trouble — then
to Court House & for a time listened
to Galusha A. Grow. then to D aunt
Sayres & at 9 to W. J. S's — read
newspaper until near 10, then
to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 30, 1879

Up early, delicious mf., walked
out to Sutte's depot before breakfast
at 8 o'clk. After that down
town & about until 11, then
tried to get to work on B nice
work but could not. Then
went to my lot & until
dinner time sawed dead
wood out of the top of big
maple — after dinner, took
a nap & then went riding
with Uncle [illegible] — in at 6 &
had tea. After that down
town & read newspapers.
Soon after 8 went to Mr. Cooke's
young ladies out but sat by
9 o'clk — there until 10, then
to W. J. S's — all in bed, so
I went there too & lay
awake until after midnight.
Beautiful moonlight.
