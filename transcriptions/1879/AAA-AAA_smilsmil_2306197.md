## Left Page

### June, Sunday 1, 1879

Hotter yet — Up late, 11½ —
about 10 — then to Dr. Hastrup's
Heard him give out a first
rate text. "The unsearchable
riches of Christ." I must
confess that that was as
far as I followed him.
After service rode down
to studio — Ther. 92° then
rode up to A.P.V.'s & took
dinner there. At 4 rode
down to N.A. & spent an
hour or more in those
baking hot galleries taking
a "last look" — then to studio
— tried to write — could not
Got a little to eat & at 8.45
went in to Coleman's —
there until shortly after
10 — then to 37th St & to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 2, 1879

Much cooler & overcast.
To studio before 11, having
first gone to the Scoville Manf.
Co., then to E. & H. Anthony for
a glass tray & some Asph. Varnish
then varnished my plates
& tried to get to work but
had several callers —
out to lunch at 2 o'clk.
more callers in the afternoon
so that it was not until
4 o'clk. that I could get
started upon my etching
"Up the Hill" — worked until
after 6 — then had a roll
& some strawberries — after
7 went out to Mad. Sq. — and
there was caught in a
shower — later went in to
Emma J's — Geo. joined
me — there until 10.20 —
then to 37th St. & to bed at
11 — Heavy rain during
every tonight.
