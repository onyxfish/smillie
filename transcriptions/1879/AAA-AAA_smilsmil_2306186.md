## Left Page

### May, Saturday 10. 1879.

Still wonderfully fine weather. Got to studio's soon after 9 and wrote Etching club notices for Monday eveg. Surprised by a call from Kate — worked a little on Hay Ricks — just as I was preparing to go out to lunch W. & Mrs. Wm Hammond (S.C.H.) came in — I chatted with them for 20 m. — then excused myself — went to restaurant — had two chops broiled & to 9.C. Depot at 1 — met Walter Cook & wife — Aunce C & his Howe. Ate lunch on Dob's Ferry Dock — then to Snedden's Landing — Climbed to top of Palisades — meeting the Braces — glorious weather — the walk & views delicious — Reached the Ferry opposite Yonkers, after a walk of about 7 miles, about 5.30 — Met Genl Cook & Valentine there — Crossed River in one boat — some of the party got my net missed 6.51 train — went to a Restaurant & had oysters, ham & eggs, beer, coffee, etc, there until 9 — still to depot and took 9.26 train, reaching 9.C. Depot at 10 — then to 34th St — & to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 11. 1879.

Still charming weather — after Hfp. down to studio's, then to Dr. Hastings & heard a good sermon from him — after service, went with Charl to see his new quarters in the Buckingham — then at 1.15 to the Hertzells & had dinner at 3 went to 37th St — & had a nap — then to 45th St — & 5th Ave. & called on Jno. Sherwood — not in — then to Studio & wrote home — & at 6.30 to Mrs. Wm Dart's — 44 Park Ave tea about 7 — soon after 8. excused myself & went to N.Y. Hotel, getting there at 9 — Saw W. & Mrs. Wm Hammond & John Gilbert (Kate F's husband) there until 10.20 — then walked to 34th St — & to bed at 11. Change in the atmosphere — cold, raw east wind with almost a fog.
