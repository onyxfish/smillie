## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 26, 1899

**Weather:** Charming day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. Immediately after bkf. I went to Walter's \+ had a talk with him about W. Edwards etc. — then to studio \+ bothered over the Keene dinner. I'm afraid that I'm going to get into another big building scrape — at noon went to N.A. \+ entered in the C. Trust check book the deposit of C. Vanderbilt's gift of $10.000 to the N.A. — then to O'Brien's \+ left in N.A. printing order — then home to lunch — again at work on Keene plans until 4 — then to cor 15th S. \+ Irving Pl. \+ made application for gas for Etching Studio — then to Keppel's \+ there met Dielman — Blum did not come. We turned over some p/f until 6 o'clk. laying aside a half dozen or more at prices running from $15 to $30 — then after 6, I went to Walter Cook's where Eliz. Coffin was holding the last of her series of Receptions. Mr. Ja. Morse walked home with me \+ I told him of my intention to send Jim to some out-of-town school next year — he did not approve ardently — Dinner — Every a little Reading \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 27, 1899

**Weather:** Clear, much colder \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fourth anniversary of Annie's death.

Clear, much colder \+ windy. I spent the m'f at studio dividing my time between working on Keene plans \+ writing into a book back accts of N.A. p.d. Before I had a promenade up \+ down Park Ave. for half an hour, then home to lunch \+ to studio at 2 where I divided my work as I had done in the m'f. At 4.30 I walked down to 14th S. \+ up B'kway \+ 5th Ave. home. Dinner. Every newspaper — reading to Ralph and marking the boy's linen.
