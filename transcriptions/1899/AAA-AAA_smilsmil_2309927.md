## Left Page

### Mon. July 17, 1899

**Weather:** Overcast \+ damp  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \+ damp \- some sunshine from noon to 3 o'clk \- a good shower from 5 to after 6 o'clk. M\*f. I wrote a letter to Mr Maghee of Keene Heights \- Afternoon I finished plan drawings for large window, main Room, Keene house. My leg was less troublesome to-day \+ I got about without aid of my crutch, a good deal. There has been a marked improvement in my bruises within a few days \- Ralph was stung on his right knee by some insect \+ the knee swelled very much \+ was so sore as to lame him. I bathed it with a lotion that I have been using \- Belladonna, Chloroform, Ammonia &c.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 18, 1899

**Weather:** Superb day \- breezy \- cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day \- breezy \- cool \- beautiful clouds. I feel the good influence of it very decidedly. wrote quite a long letter to Walter. Cook asking him to give some attention to the pavement that is to be laid at 110 \- also inviting advice as to where Ralph shall be sent to school next winter \- I wrote to Orlando Beede \+ sent him plans for the big window in Main Room at Keene Heights. I went down town before noon \- Rusan came to the house, mended leak in Tank \+ barn, etc. \+ took away broken Sanitas W.C. to try to mend it. Dinner at 1 \- Afternoon I went down town again, onfoot as before \+ home about 5. Evef\. newspaper \+ some reading to R \- the knee has not troubled him to-day altho' the swelling continues but much lessened.
