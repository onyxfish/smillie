## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 5, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Curious weather - fine m'f. in the course of the m'f., a change + a little sprinkle of rain between 2 + 3 o'clk - after that it soon cleared — beautiful night. Some time with the Painter then awhile on plan drawings for Aqua-tint box frame. - About 11 went to R.R. station + got information about trains from Binghampton to West Port — then to foundry - saw Young Beach + conferred with him about the iron work for Aqua-tint box - Home about 12 + had a little Tennis exercise with Ralph before dinner — after dinner felt so heavy that I did not get to work right away - on plans from 3 to 5 — then R. + I went to P.O. feeling draggy. Eve'g - feeling more so - Ralph read to me for awhile.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 6, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - brilliant with strong cool west wind. I got up early + did some writing before breakfast - among other things I finished drawing for iron work for Aqua-tint box + Herrick worked on the frame part of the day. I took drawing to Beach foundry — after dinner I had more notes to write + then I got fairly at my trunk-packing - I went to P.O. about + + back + almost finished with trunk soon after 6 - then rested until tea time — Eve'g. Miss Wilhemina Harris of Binghampton arr. on late train to make the girls a short visit. Eve'g, - chatting + Reading.
