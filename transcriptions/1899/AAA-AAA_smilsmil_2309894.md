## Left Page

### Fri. May 12, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A charming day — After bkfst. I went to Miller's — the carpenter — not in — then to studio \+ then to N.A. where I was kept until near 1 o'clk — with members — my Treas'. duties \+ laboring with Grumins over accts. Lunch — rested awhile \+ then got ready for a bicycle ride. Ralph \+ I started at 3 — I went to Borrmann's \+ changed my Christy saddle for a Garford upon which I rode with increased comfort — we went to N. Park \+ loafed there — then to the new N.A. School Bldg. — then to Riverside Drive \+ home thro' Park at 7 2? St. getting in at 5.30 o'clk — after a very enjoyable ride. The Park is very lovely now — Rested until dinner time — miller came in at 6.30 for a few minutes — a quiet evg \+ to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 13, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, raw day — showers in the after part \+ evg. I spent much of the mng \+ part of the afternoon struggling with Grumins over the Acct. trying to get a balance without success — He knows nothing about book keeping and easily gets tangled — It worries me greatly. Evg. I went to Century — Early part of the evg. C. Herschel — a civil engineer, I believe, a member of the club gave an address on Frontinus \+ the Water Supply of old Rome — with stenoptic illustrations. Later the Bldg. Com. had a meeting \+ agreed to recommend to the Council Blashfield to take the place of Dielman who steps up higher by virtue of his election as Pres. Home at midnight
