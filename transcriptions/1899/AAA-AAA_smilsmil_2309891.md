## Left Page

### Sat. May 6, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another mild, milky day — awoke about 3.30 with severe headache \+ got little more sleep before I got up at 7.30 — After breakfast I went to N.A. \+ spent the mng there with Frannis working over the accts. Frannis is very slow \+ little help to me — there were interruptions by calls. Nicoll, Ward \+ others, to talk over developments in relation to coming trouble at annual meeting — Home to lunch soon after 1 — I got to Weehawken ferry, foot of 42d St. before 3 o'clk. \+ waited in a malodorous atmosphere for E.P. Genter \+ Yale who came at 3 — we took boat crossed to Weehawken — then trolley to the Boulevard on top of cliff \+ then walked south to Mr. Karl Bitter's Studio. Dr. Killiam was to have accompanied us but was detained by a case. When we got to the house, Bitter had been called to N.Y. so were left to the care of his Italian serving man who showed us over the entire establishment, from cellar to roof. The place is interesting \+ picturesque but did not excite my envy. Home at 6 \+ rested until dinner at 7 — Soon after 8 I went to Cantine \+ was there until near midnight. Great ferment among the N.A.s — Wood is working hard to keep his place \+ gather about him in the council a collection of fossils of his era. On the other hand, Nicoll \+ I worked to organize the opposition, of which there is plenty if it will only work together — these is the trouble — Petty jealousies splits the opposition up \+ it counts for nothing — we tried to get a union on Dielman for Pres. Beckwith V.P. \+ De Forest for Pres. — I do not know with what success.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 7, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm hazy mng. growing clearer \+ cooler in the afternoon — After a good nights rest got up with a clear head at 8.30 — Eighteen years ago Annie \+ I were married in this house. After bkft. I read awhile — then Ralph \+ I went out for a short walk but it was too uncomfortably warm — from 1 to 2 o'clk. I rested — then dinner — after dinner I went to N.A. getting there before 4 o'clk. Frannis was there \+ I worked until 6 — then I had a walk — getting home before 7 — tea — Mr. Adams to tea with us — in the eveng G.C. \+ Nellie came in — I wrote a letter to him, Ralph sitting by my side \+ writing — At 10.30 I took it to G.C. Station \+ mailed it.
