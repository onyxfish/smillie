## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 23, 1899

**Weather:** Superb mt.  **Temp:** clear + very cold — heavy frost last night.

Up before 6 — bkfs — at 6.30 \+ away in the stage at 7.10. Head waiter \+ waitresses for fellow passengers — The valley very beautiful in the early mt. light — enjoyed the walk up Spruce mt. Got to E. town 10.10 Stage for West Port waiting. Got away at once \+ reached R.R. station at 11.0 Train on time 11.38 — Stopped at Ti. at 12.30 — half an hour for an unsatisfactory dinner on board steamer "Vermont" — Got to Albany before 4 — changed at once to H.R. RR — 4 o'clk. train for N.Y. Weather changed, cloudy \+ milder. at Po. K. 10 m — oyster stew \+ sandwiches at counter — Reached N.Y. on time — 8.15 — went directly to 110 — found Jennings waiting for us on front door step — gas lighted — beds made etc. Charl. soon came around \+ spent a half hour with us — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 24, 1899

**Weather:** Singular weather — So. Easterly wind —  **Temp:** quite mild — overcast — very heavy shower

at 11 — no more rain but cloudy the rest of the day. We got up late — it was 10 when we went to Grand Union Restaurant Park Ave. \+ 42d. St. — home at 11 just before the down pour of rain — I was tired and had a head ache that increased until at night it was very severe — After writing a letter to Mary I went to studio and then walked down to 5th Ave. \+ 23 St. — an immense crowd there watching the workmen engaged upon the Dewey Triumphal Arch — walked up 5th Ave. \+ then home — Charl. \+ Geo. met me at my door — Geo. came in for a few minutes — Then I went to bed \+ remained there until 7. Then I went with the boys to C's \+ had dinner — At 9 I went to Century hoping to meet Cauldwell to talk of my duties as Paris Supt. Jury for Etchings \+ Engravings — learned that he was out of town for the day — Ret'd to C's — he played on his orchestrelle until 10.30 — Then, the boys \+ I went home \+ to bed.
