## Left Page

### Fri. May 26, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - clear + warm with southerly wind. After bkfs. talked with neighbor Martin + painter Douglass - Then to studio - Mr. W. Bayard Cutting came soon after to ask that Ralph might spend a couple of days next week with his son Bronson at Oak-Dale. He was very affable + we had a pleasant chat about boys - Then I set to work upon h.a. accts to unravel some of the Knots + brought things up to Dec 1. - all right. At noon went to etching studio + found S Fahr + Sterne at work, both in trouble - The hat of the model is not satisfactory and they cannot fake Rats to suit them - I remained with them for half an hour + think that they got some helps. Then home to lunch - To studio at 2.30 + worked for an hour on h.a. accts. Then home + after H. Ralph + I got away on our wheels - we sauntered a part of the way thro' the Park + lounged under Wistaria covered arbors - but the drive was so full of carnages - so we turned onto 5th Ave. - at 82d. St. + went up to Mt. Morris Park - Then across to Grant's tomb + thence home - getting in at 6.15. I rested until near 7 - Then had dinner - Evening, after my news paper, I read awhile + Ralph \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 27, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate went to Po'k.

Very warm day - pleasant altho' looking showery - a shower in the eve'g after 8 o'clk. After bkfs. I went to Spalding's + got a the cyclometer - to Lincoln Nat. Bnk. + met Watrous + had a talk with him about h.l. matters - Then to Camiene + Hastings about new blue - Then to studio + worked on h.a. accts until 12 - I have just learned that Grannis is off on a Three days fishing excursion without asking me about it - His books are not kept up to date - he cannot now get them ready for the Audit immediately after June 14 + Miller will of course, as chairman of the com. make it very unpleasant for me - It shall be my duty to make it somewhat unpleasant for Grannis - At noon I went to etching class + found Sterne + Fahr working on their plates after Mr. Brunner who was posing for them - lunch up - Kate went to Po'kee'sie for the spring by the 3.30 train. I went to studio + worked on accts. So far, while I have met some perplexments, I have worked them out all right + am not bothered by any serious hitch - It is simply drudgery that Grannis should do instead of going off fishing - The most serious trouble is that he is incompetent and a muddler. Home after H. + Ralph + I went out on our bicycles - The sky was overcast but it was lovely in the Park which was swarming full of people of all kinds - May parties - games on the lawns - music on the mall - endless processions of fine equipages - horseback riders + cyclists galore - Adennis, Charl \+ Bessie met us + returned with us - Dinner before 7 - eve'g Read awhile + Ralph \+ to bed at 10.
