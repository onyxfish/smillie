## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 6, 1899

**Weather:** Glorious day \- bright, sunny & cool  **Temp:**

After 4&p. I walked up to Fine Arts Bldg. in 57 st. stopping at Simpson's (6th Ave & 43d St.) on the way \+ ordering a case of Burgundy. At the F.A. Bldg. I met Mr. Fayson Mr. Nicoll of the Commission for Paris Exposition. Mr. Wolf, the wood Engr. of Com. on Etchings \+ Engravings was already there — we waited for half an hour or more \+ then Mr. French, another wood Engr. \+ fellow com. man came in \+ after a little delay we went into the gallery to look at the works sent in — after further delay Mr. Drake of Century Co. came \+ then being 4 of a com. of 5 (Ch. Platt being absent) we organized — the wood engravers promptly put in Drake as chairman. To my surprise I found that the only etchings out in were 4 by Blanes — some exhibitors took plates and Alfred Jones, head of Washington — I at once expressed a determination to resign from the Com. as there was nothing for me to do — the etchers had taken no interest. I was unwilling to assume the responsibility and do the work of making a collection — after some urging I stayed \+ helped in the selection of a lot of [illegible] for wood engravings — About 12.30 we had arranged to meet again next Sat. at 3.30 — I went to studio \+ home to lunch at 1.45 — A young man from Beaman, Everett \+ Choate, lawyers, came to see if I could give testimony for the A.J.S. — abt. the boy who came near being hurt by the barrel of stone bullets made a year or more ago. To studio \+ wrote Report for J.A. on bills of [illegible] — then to Century Co. in 17 St. — saw Drake \+ told him that I had decided to Resign from Com. — He protested but I think he was pleased. Then to Lockwood de Forest in 10 St. — saw him \+ gave him Report on the [illegible] bills \+ talked about [illegible] business. Then walked home — making nearly 6 miles for to-day. Dinner at 6.30 — Susy read awhile to Ralph who is rather out of sorts — cold or indigestion I think — \+ then to bed early.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 7, 1899

**Weather:** Brilliant day  **Temp:**

Chas. Collins came in after 6&p. on a message — we had a half hour chat — then I went \+ voted taking Ralph with me to show him how it was done — Then to studio — wrote to Mr. B. Cauldwell a note resigning my position on Com. on Etchings \+ Engs. for Paris Exp. \+ a letter explaining my action — wrote to Callanan Bros. Then I dampened all the [illegible] that I took last week \+ put them in press. Home to lunch at 2 — After 3 when I went to studio feeling very much out of sorts, so that I could not work. I picked over some india (chinese) preparatory to more printing — Home after 5. Dinner at 6.30 — Susy with Ralph. Then some writing of acct.
