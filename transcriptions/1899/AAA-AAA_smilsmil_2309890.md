## Left Page

### Thur. May 4, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - Cloudless, cool + breezy Very soon after bkft. Nicoll came in \+ we had but a few minutes talk together when H. C. Underhill of the Macknight Flintic Stone Co. 150 Bdway came to talk with me about a new pavement infront of the house - while we were talking, Mr Marlen, of 112 - came out \+ together we talked of having a new pavement infront of both houses - Then I hurried away to Mr. Jennings office - Mills Bldg \+ there met Beckwith \+ Nicoll \+ with Mr. Jennings we talked over the Trust Deed \+ the proposed amendment to the Constitution creating a Finance Com - until after 12 o'clk - Home to lunch at 1 - after 2 I went to 2d. Nat'l Bk \+ from there to N.A. - Grannis too busy to work with me on accts. for Annual Report - he will get me into a mess presently by not being ready - There home \+ at 3.45 Ralph \+ I went out on our wheels - we went thro' Central Park to the N.A. building \+ I went thro' it - The lathers are at work inside \+ the tinners outside - Then we went over to the Riverside Cinder Path up to \+ around Grant's Tomb - down Path to 104th St. Boulevard to 72d 5th Thro' Park to 5th Ave. \+ down Mad. Ave. home at 5.40 - alpt. strong wind on way home - Changed. - Rubbed \+ Rested before dinner at 6.30 - quiet eveg - a little reading to Ralph. To bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 5, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Good day - growing colder - east wind - After bkfst. I went to N.A. met Nicoll there the head some surprising information as to stories floating about a pressure on Wood who agrees to resign in favor of Mr. Hart for Pres. - etc - etc - to me improbable, as I don't believe that anything or anybody could induce Wood to resign. Went to 2d Nat'l Bk \+ then back \+ was with Grannis until after 1 going over accts. as he has transcribed them - He is neither very clear headed or correct. Then home to lunch - Back to N.A. at 3 \+ with Grannis until 4.30 when Ward Miller came - As Robbins declines to serve on audit com. they could not go on with the work until that vacancy is filled but there was an hour of very unintelligent \+ unsatisfactory talk on the subject. Home at about 6.30 - dinner - Katharine \+ Bessie went to Newark this P.M. on a visit over night. Read a little to Ralph \+ as my head troubled me went to my bedroom at 9. I to bed \+ soon after.
