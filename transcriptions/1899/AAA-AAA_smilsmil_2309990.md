## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 24, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day \- clear, cloudless \+ quiet. Got up with a headache that clung to me all day \- to studio at 9.30 \+ worked with grover on Mr. G. plate until 12\. \- Then to Yale's \+ after a conference he puts me on the old course \- calomel \- Then a clearing out \- then a course of Sal. \+ Soda with Soda bicarb \- This may help my rheumatics, but the greatest amt of my distress now-a-days comes from hyper-acidity of the stomach\. \- To lunch at 1.30 \- after it my headache got the better of me \+ I was on my bed until near 5 o'clk. Then to Dewey lamp store about repairing student lamps \- Then to Brooks Bros. about changes required in new coat \- then to studio \+ wet down paper for printing to-morrow\. \- Home to dinner at 6.30 \- \+ after my newspaper I read awhile to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 25, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day \- To studio at 9 \+ worked faithfully all day on Mr. G. plate. I had printing outfit in order \+ took proofs and worked between times with grover \+ burnisher \- all of the work being on the face. To lunch at 1.30 \+ back at 2.30 \- Stopped work soon after 4 \+ then walked up to Central Park \+ to the lake at the head of the mall \+ then back home\. \- Dinner at the usual time\. \- For awhile in the even\+ read to Ralph from R.L. Stevenson's "Kidnapped."
