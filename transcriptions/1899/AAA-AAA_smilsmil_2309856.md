## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 23, 1899

**Weather:** Brilliant day, pleasant air in spite of the Fang imparted by melting snow.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 \+ after some writing set Palette \+ worked all day on a sketch that I painted at Montrose, Oct. '95 — a sort of an autobiographic sketch — "Into the Valley" — autumn — eve'g — a Road leading down into a valley in shadow — an old man going down the Road — alone — not a very cheerful subject — I think it is rather strong, for me. Home to lunch \+ back at 2.30 — Davis came with a man \+ took away the 22 framed Etching pf. for the Etching studio — Dinner at 6.30 Chare dined with us \+ took Katharine T. to Symphony Concert — Mr. Mayor came \+ gave Jim two hours of tutoring — Jno. Valentine called on the ladies — I spent part of the eve'g writing up my accts.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 24, 1899

**Weather:** Brilliant crisp winter day  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 \+ to work at once painting upon a sketch that I painted two or three winters ago — after a walk in Central Park — late Twilight — snow upon the ground — eve'g sky before me — full moon behind me — a hopelessly ambitious subject — but one that interests me so much that I am satisfied to work at it even though I am sure to fail — Had a half-hours walk on the sunny side of Park Ave. before lunch — to studio at 2.30 — Painted until 4.30 — then went to Carrere \+ Hastings — saw Mr. Brainerd — Then walked up 5th Ave. to Temple S. Manuel — went in and enjoyed Vesper service — home to dinner — At 8, with the boys, walked down to Mad. Sq. Garden to see the effect of moonlight on the tower — home at 8.30 — Read to them until 9 — [illegible — entire passage]
