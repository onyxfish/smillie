## Left Page

### Thur. June 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sale of N.A. fd. to Met. Life completed.

An exceedingly hot, uncomfortable day. To studio at 9.15 \+ worked at some checks etc, the last batch of bills approved by the council \+ ordered paid — about 11 went to N.A. \+ from there to Wall St. went to Charles office \+ at 11.45 to Lee \+ Lee's, where I found Wood \+ Watrons with Davis \+ the M.A. seal. we waited until 12.40 before the Met. Life Co representative, Mr. Lawyer, came with the checks after arranging preliminaries — we went to the U.S. Trust Co \+ paid the Phillips mtg of $68000 — then to Central Trust \+ made arrangements with Mr. Hyde for the recognition of the new N.A. Res\. and Treas\. — then back to Lee \+ Lee's where forms were prepared to meet requirements of 2\. Nat. Bk \+ Safe Deposit Co. It was then 2 o'clk — so I hurried up town \+ deposited in 2\. Nat. Bank $166700 odd dollars. The Police Annual Parade blocked my way but I got to N.A. \+ then home at 3 \+ had a light lunch. Then being very tired I had a nap \+ after it went to McBarbeis \+ had my hair cut \+ head shampooed. Then to N.A. \+ put seal in safe. Home to dinner at 7 — Geo. Cook \+ Emily there — also Emma Ireland we had a very pleasant time until near 9 when our guests left, to go elsewhere — I pothered with one thing \+ another until 11 before I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 2, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot, scorching hot all day without mitigation with windows \+ doors open all night — the Ther. did not fall below 80° \+ during the day got up to 86°. After bkf\. — an Ass. Editor from the White pub. house called with biographical sketch of me \+ tried to get me to subscribe to the 12 vol. work for $120. To studio at 10.30 \+ worked on N.A. work until 12 — then to 57 W. 10th St. with checks for T.W. Wood to sign — went to Dielmans — at 4½ and had a talk with him — Home to lunch at 1 — again to studio — more N.A. work \+ at 5 again went to T.W. Woods — he signed a check for me — as Pres\. he has to countersign all checks. To studio and worked there until 6.30 — then home to tea — we have dinner in the middle of the day now. Everett Chase came in for a while — I wrote a hasty note to Jim \+ took it to G.C. Station \+ mailed it.
