## Left Page

### Mon. June 19, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright day - pretty hot + a severe one for me - pain being constant + upon attempt to move, very severe. D. Adlen called about noon + in the afternoon Dr. Gerster came + cheered me for a time - Mr. Manuel came + set to work upon his accts. in my bedroom. He continued his work from 9 until about noon - then he left to go down town - Jennings still continues with me - He has brought a Rattan lounge from 10 + sleeps in my Rooms upon that

## Right Page

### Tues. June 20, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot, uncomfortable day. Heavy thunderstorms in the eve'g about 7 with showers following. The air was soon perceptibly cooled + the night was comfortable. Mr. Manuel came before 9 + worked all the m'g. I managed with Jennings's help to slip out of my bed into a big chair alongside where I sat for some time bolstered with pillows - It is dreary work though - pain follows every movement + it is hard to find a position in which there is not more or less discomfort. I am improving but it is a most aggravitingly slow improvement -
Jennings with me again all night
