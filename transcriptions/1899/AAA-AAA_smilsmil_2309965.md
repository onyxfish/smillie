## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A really cold day \- offs? about ½ o'clk \- wrote a letter to Mary \- Val \+ Gordon came in to see Ralph \+ I took a stroll down 5th Ave. feeling stiff \+ headachy \- Met Eliz. Howe [illegible] \+ walked back with her for some blocks, then resumed my walk to 23? 5th then home \+ had a lunch after 1 o'clk \- About R\- \+ I walked up to 50th St. to Cutter's \+ called at his house hoping to be able to see him but he was out of town for the day \- Then Ralph \+ I walked on up 5th Ave. to the Park \+ to the menagerie there \- then down home getting in soon after 5 \- It was a hard walk for me \- I was so stiff \+ sore all over \+ the pain in my head \+ neck so great. I set awhile before the fire \+ at 7 R. \+ I went to Charl's by invitation \- Mr. \+ Mrs. Tom Adams taking their fare well dinner there \- Mr. A \- kett? from a year abroad last Tues \- we had a very good dinner \+ pleasant evef until 10 o'clk \- then home \+ to bed \- pretty late hours for Ralph.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 2, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph entered at Cutter's school.

Very fine day but unusually cold \- up at 7.30 \- offs? soon after 8 \+ at about 9 o'clk \- Ralph \+ I got to Cutter's school \- 18 E. 50th St. after waiting 15 or 20 m \- we were shown into Mr. C's room \- He was very affable \+ took me over the establishment \- I was soon won over \+ left R \- to make his beginning in "Cutter's school" \- one of the first boys that he met was one of his Miller School fellows \- I then went to Gales \- then to the house \- to 2? Nat. Buk \+ to 41 W. 10 St. where I spent an hour with Dielmann. Home at 1.30 \- lunch. wrote some letters \+ attended to some business matters \- after 5 R. \+ I walked down to 4th Ave. \+ 20th St. for some lamp wicks for Montrose \- At 7. we went to Charl's \- His Adams friends have gone \- we dined \+ spent the evef with him \- home at 10 \+ to bed.
