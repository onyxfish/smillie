## Left Page

### Fri. July 7, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day following the electric storms of yesterday - I got about more than usual - climbed down to the berry terrace \+ inspected things there I looked over the seeds in the shop \+ found a number of kinds that I wanted. I spent some time planting Nasturtium seeds in my Studio window boxes \+ about the border of the Peony bed. Dinner at 2 - after that I had a long nap. I then wrote a letter to W. J. N. Maghee - Eve\[ning\] wandering about piazzas - then reading newspapers \+ to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Sat. July 8, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain in the early m\[orn\]t - clearing partially about 9 o'clk - superb sky by 11 - light showers in the afternoon. Bissell came about noon bringing a lot of chickens I bought 2 doz. varying in weight from ½ lb. to 2 lbs. for $6.00 \+ a brood of 13, about a week old, bought for a dollar, birth-day gift to Ralph most of the afternoon was spent by Spouse in constructing a house \+ yard for the hen \+ her 13 chicks - I Rec'd notice from 2nd Nat'l B\[an\]k that my Cent. Pacific Coupons July 1st $250. had been ret'd "no funds". This is a great trial to me as I had had no intimation that trouble was brewing there. I wrote to Charl about it, \+ sent him a note of introduction to W. B. Daffan, of The Sun - He wishes to meet him in behalf of some young woman who is doing journalistic work. My side \+ leg are still very painful but I can trace a slight \+ slow improvement.
