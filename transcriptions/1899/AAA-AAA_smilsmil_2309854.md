## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 19, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day in spite of snow chilled wind \+ slushy streets — Enjoyed my Sunday scrub \+ had bkft. before 9 — worried awhile over N.A. accts — then went out with Ralph for a walk — a fire at the cor. of 6" Ave. \+ 48" St. diverted us for awhile — Then we walked on to 57" St. \+ back home getting in just in time for dinner at 2. After dinner more worrying with accts. Geo. came in \+ sat with me for awhile about 5. I went out \+ had a short walk — Then to Mary Cook's \+ answered her appeal for $5.00 for the Diet Kitchen by giving it to her — then to Walters \+ talked with him about Lawrenceville School. — He is inclined to throw cold water on it — favors a more "nobby" school — St. Marks or Pomfret. — I am discouraged — Home to tea at 6.30. Eve'g. some reading to the boys.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 20, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day overhead \+ quite mild — I got to studio about 10 \+ worked for an hour or so, cleaning glasses \+ putting [illegible] pfs. into frames — Then I went to N.A. where I found considerable work for me. It kept me until 1.45 — then home to lunch — after that feeling very tired I rested for an hour — Emma Ireland came \+ I had a talk with her about Jim \+ the Lawrenceville School. — She objects to the school — says that young Ed. Spring went there for a time — she disapproves of the system — Then I went to 145 W. 58" St. Mrs. H.H. Villard's — a Diet Kitchen Concert, out at about 5.30 — then went to Architectural League Sysm \+ had a hurried look thro' the galleries — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g went to Council Meeting \+ got home early —
W. Mayou came \+ spent a couple of hours with Jim.
