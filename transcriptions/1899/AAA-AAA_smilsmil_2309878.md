## Left Page

### Sat. April 8, 1899

**Weather:** Bright m'g. \- cloudy later, with threats of rain at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent the day at the studio working on zinc plate \- sketch of my front face \- laid ground \- added a little new work on the face \- bit it in \+ fussed in various other ways over it \- to discover at the end of the day that it has all been wasted time. The result is not worth the outlay \-
About noon I went to Carrière \+ [illegible] \+ saw Messrs Carrere \+ Brainard \- about the progress of new building \+ some details to be observed in the etching room. At 5 o'clk. Beckwith \+ Watrous came to my house by appointment \+ together we went to the house of Mr. Jennings \- Cor. 39th S. and Park Ave. To consult him about the formation of a trust fund for the N.A. \+ D. \- to take the place of the present maintenance fund \- the object being to get the fund into such shape that it cannot be diverted to other uses than maintenance \- we were very pleasantly rec'd. \+ spent nearly an hour with him \- I made an arrangement to meet him at his office down town next Tues. at 2.30 P.M.
Eve'g. I went to Mrs. Darby d's \- 40 E. 70 \+ spent an hour with her \- she is in very miserable condition \- recovering from a severe illness \- we talked of the Baldwin Fund \+ she promised to send me a check for the etching class soon \- walked home \-. Found Claire there \- chatted with him until 10.30

## Right Page

### Sun. April 9, 1899

*Ralph ret'd. from New Haven.*

**Weather:** Pleasant but cloudy \- some sunshine in the early part of the day \- air mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- after b\&p. I read my paper for a little while \- then for some time I was delving among some old papers of Annie's \- Twelfth St. School memoirs \- old compositions \- etc. very interesting \- an almost complete series of letters to her mother written while we were abroad in 1884 \- Then out \+ had a long walk \- getting in just before dinner at 2 \- afternoon. I had a long nap \- at 6.30 went to P.R. Station \- Ralph arr'd on time at 6.45 \- in good shape \- Tea \- Eve'g I read a story to him \- then wrote a long letter to Catherine Adams Elkin about Jim arranging to have him stay with her until the end of [illegible] May and continue his studies with tutors \- I mailed it at P.R. Station before 11 \- Read a number of Annie's old letters \+ to bed at 12.
