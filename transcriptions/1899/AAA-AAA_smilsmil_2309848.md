## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 7, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another dull day — after o'clk. went to M. Chas. Barney's — across the way, and talked with him about the summons sent to the N.A. \+ to the Portrait Show people in the complaint of the boy who came near being hurt by the pulling over of the stone balustrade in front of the N.A. \+ who, through a shyster lawyer claims $5000 damages. It was 11 when I got to studio — painted until 1 on Capri — lunch — afternoon, completed the repainting — then went to 4 W. 22. S. \+ made preliminary examination \+ measurements for putting in a gas stove. Then to stove place on Broadway \+ to Gas Co. on Fourth Ave. Disagreeable snowy night. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve\. at home.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 8, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snowing nearly all day — To studio at 10 \+ painted on small Capri on panel. Very discouraging work — my long spell of professional idleness has worked very greatly to my detriment — Home to lunch \+ continued my work in the afternoon until near 5 — Then to N.A. — met Hart \+ Dielmann there — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Eve\. M. Mayor came \+ gave two hours to Jim — I spent some time in studying pamphlets etc, left by M. Thos. Adams, descriptive of the Lawrenceville School — \+ I have quite fallen in love with it \+ have almost made up my mind to send Jim there.
