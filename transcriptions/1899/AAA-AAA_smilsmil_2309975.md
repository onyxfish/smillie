## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 21, 1899

**Weather:** Fine day \- breezy \+ rather cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

to studio at about 10 \- Mrs. Kane had cleaned the stairway \+ hall when I got there \- I set her to work scrubbing the studio floor while I moved things to give her a clear track. I did a lot of clearing up of drawers \+ of putting things in place in closets \- about 12.30 I had got the floor cleared \+ she was scrubbing the last section, so I went home \- put some of my things there away \+ had a rest before lunch at 1.30 \- After lunch I fooled away some time over various things \- at 4 o'clk. Ralph \+ I got away, he on Jim's wheel \- I on my own \- we had a pleasant ride in Central Park \+ got home soon after 5 \- I had a rub down \+ change \- Then a young man called at the request of M. Gherardi Davis, so he said, \+ persuaded me to go with him to the polling place \+ register. Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve. I gave some time to Ralph \+ his lessons. Charl came \+ took Mary \+ the girls with him to his house.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 22, 1899

**Weather:** mild \+ very pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up soon after 7 o'clk \+ had a thorough grooming \+ bath \+ shampoo \- then back to bed \+ did not get up until after 9. \- late bkft. About 11 Ralph \+ I went out \- first to Fred Ireland's \+ had a little talk with him about a college for Jim \- then I decided to have him prepared for Yale \- then R. \+ I rode to 106th St. \+ 3d Ave. \+ walked to Central Park \- sauntered there until 1 o'clk \- then took 5th Ave. car. \+ home at 1.30 \- lunch \- wrote a long letter to Jim \+ one to Mr. W. B. Olmsted \+ Pomfret school \- All of my family dined at Charl's at 6.45 After dinner Ch. \- Mary \+ the girls went to church to hear the last of the Rev'd Mr. Jaunce \- R. \+ I kept house at C's \+ when Geo. came entertained him until the return of the church goers \- R. \+ I Ret'd home at about 10.
