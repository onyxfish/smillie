## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 18, 1899

**Weather:** Very warm & clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm & clear. I worked all the mf on Jacobi etching — Bessie Parke & her friend miss Kingman came to lunch with us & left almost immediately after. I wrote to Second Nat'l. Bank — to Douglas & to Douglas Adams, concerning more Paint for the Keene house — I worked on Jacobi — & after 5 I went to P.O. Ev'g. very quiet & mild. Bessie came down stairs but was not allowed to go out of doors for fear of taking more cold. I paced the piazza & read newspaper.

Wet down paper to take 1st p'f. from Jacobi to-morrow.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 19, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day — Ther. got up to 84°. in my studio. After b'kf't I got to work at my press & some time was spent in getting it into service from first grinding ink. I found in a jar some that Kimmel & Co. had ground for me not less than four years ago. There was a hard cake outside, but inside I found a tough paste that I was able to reduce with oil & it makes an ink that has some desirable qualities, to my mind. At last I got to my printing & took 3 prints before 11 o'clk — The result was somewhat of a surprise as it showed more color than I expected. After dinner I worked with the scraper, in preference to the burnisher, & reduced the color in a number of places — also did some dry-point work on high lights — worked until 5 — Then to P.O. Got a long letter from Korkler. a depressing act of the physical condition of him self & wife. Evng. hazy moonlight. Very warm I spent most of the ev'g in big wicker chair on piazza.

Three weeks since my accident — no pain in my hip joint but a stiffness in back, down & on the pelvis & extending to muscles of the thighs that makes walking difficult — There is a sore spot in my side still but it does not inconvenience me at any time.
