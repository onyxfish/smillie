## Left Page

### Wed. March 15, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nasty day. cold with heavy rain most of the day. To studio at 9.30 - worked for a while on W.C. "on the Susquehanna" - It was started on its way to Phila. at noon. Sterne \+ Olinsky came with their plates \+ I gave them an hour - Home to lunch \+ back - Afternoon wrote to Mr. Gherardi Davis, Assembly Chamber, Albany concerning Tax bill, N.A. \+c - \+ to Franz Branch giving instruction concerning the Keene house - Also gave some time to clearing up - Home after 6. Dinner - Evelf. Read aloud Barker's article on liquid Air in McClure's Monthly. - To bed early.

Mr. Mayon spent a course of hours with Jim.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 16, 1899

**Weather:** clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, windy \+ rather cold. To studio rather the worse for a headache \+ spent the m'g writing - I have a lot of arrearages of letters and business that must be attended to \+ during the eve'g I am rarely in condition for such work. At 12 I went to N.A. \+ worked there until after 1 - then to w. 30th St. trying to find zinc sheets suitable for Etching experiments - got some small sheets too thin. Nearly 2. when I got home to lunch. The afternoon spent experimenting with the zinc plates - getting working surface \+ laying ground. To N.A. before 6 - dinner at 6.30 - Evelf. worked awhile writing up personal accts.

Mr. Mayon gave 1½ hour to Jim.
