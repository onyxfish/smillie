## Left Page

### Mon. April 24, 1899

**Weather:** Mild, clear & very spring-like  **Temp:**

\- To studio about 10 & had letters to write that kept me until after 11 - then to City Hall & at 12 had a hearing before Mayor VanWyck concerning the amended bill transferring exemption from taxes from old to new N.A. Site. Beckwith & I did the little talking that was done - Wood & others stood around - were treated civilly & dismissed without any assurance as to what he would do - then walked with B - himself & talked about the difficulty ahead in finding a new Treas. for N.A. - I wanted B. or N. to get together some of the serious N.A.s & have a consultation - I fear the election of Gel. Hall, which I think would be a serious blow to the N.A. But both B - & N - seem inclined to the drift policy - Home to lunch at 1 olk. To studio at 3 & again had writing to do - Stone came & I gave some time to him - Miss Abbott called - I had a short rest before dinner - Eveng. to Council meeting - Pay the way at 5 o'clk - & rec'd an urgent note from Carrere & Hastings & saw C - Schmid a member of Assembly had been there to advise prompt action to get N.A. bill (the one before the mayor to day) through the Assembly - I don't understand it - at Council I read letter Rec'd this P.M. from Farrington, offering to assist me in balancing the Academy accts. Legrand Benedit sent in an application for the Cat. advertising - which was followed by an acrid discussion I cannot seem to make plain to the other members, all of them - what is so clear to me - that B. should have a chance to pay his debt to the N.A. - but Hart opposes him bitterly & Wood, Watrons, Weldon & Church followed him - & he carried his point - Home at 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 25, 1899

**Weather:** Very warm hazy day  **Temp:**

\- up early & immediately after 8½ - before 9, went to Mr. Wm. Lee's in W. 36 S. He got out of bed to see me & I told him about Schmid's visit to Carrere & my fear that there might yet be some develment somewhere. He seemed to think there was little chance for it - then home & wrote some letters & after 10 went to Carrere & H - to inquire about electric lights in the new bldg - then home - & then to N.A. where I spent some time in looking up the cost to the N.A. for the past two years of School lighting by gas & by electricity - About 3 I went to etching class in W. 22d St. Stone - Storks & Kelley there - & the young girl model Bertha Reiner came and I assisted the boys in arranging a pose & then left them - home to lunch at 2 - after B when I got to studio - worked until 5.30 drawing plans for the biting sink - etching table & printing table for the Etching room in New Academy School. Then went with it to C & H - & left it there - Took a short walk but between the enervation of the day & the lameness of my back (I've been wearing a porus plaster all day) I did not get much enjoyment out of it - Dinner at 6.30 - Eveg. Read a little while to Ralph, then to bed.
