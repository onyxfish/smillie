## Left Page

### Tues. July 25, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

East wind still threatens \+ nothing comes of it. Rain enough in the m'f. to wet the piazzas \- a little sunshine in the middle of the day \- m'f. I wrote a letter to Roland Muelford with reference to Jim \+ his studies \- \+ I wrote to Jim about his underclothing \+ care of himself. About noon went down-town \+ got back to dinner late \- Frank Herrick worked about the house all day on odd jobs. I planned a re arrangement of my work. Table drawers \+ got some thin mahogany for the purpose of carrying it out \- went down town again about 6 \-

## Right Page

### Wed. July 26, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Several light showers during the night \+ early m'f. Cleared before 9 \- hot day with strong westerly breeze \- Trimmed vines about piazzas a good part of the m'f \+ directed Herrick who was working about the house. Went down town at noon \- back to dinner at 1 after stopping awhile at Daw. Sayre's \- Afternoon, working at various odds \+ ends about the place until 5 \- then down-town again. To-day I have discarded my stick. Eve'g Mary \+ her girls went to an entertainment at Mrs. Shaefer's \- I sat with kate on piazza until after 9 \- a very warm, quiet night \- To bed about 10 \-
The camp uniform for the boys came to-day \+ I forwarded the p\&p to them by Friendsville Stage.
