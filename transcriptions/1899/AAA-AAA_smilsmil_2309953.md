## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 7, 1899

**Weather:** Superb day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - Very cool mt'f, but warm at mid-day - The mt'f was spent finishing up matters \+ at 12.20 Jim, Ralph \+ I got away in train for Alford \+ from there to Binghampton where we arr'd at 1.50 o'clk. Went to The Arlington \+ had a fairly good dinner. Then Fireman's parade - at 4.15 train on D\+H - left for Albany - got there without incident at 8.45 - As we were crowding out of the door three or four fellows made a rush - one pretended to have caught his coat on the knob \+ in the jam an attempt was made to take my pocket book from my hip pocket - I felt the hand under my coat \+ clapped my hand on my pocket book \+ so saved it - It was so clumsily worked that the trick failed - The pre-arranged event was transparent - had it not been I would have lost. Went to Kenmore hotel by bus \+ was assigned to 243 \+ 243½ - a good sized bed Room, with smaller bed Room opening off from it - also bathroom \+ W. C. Then I went out \+ had a short walk - To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 8, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy thunderstorm at 2 a.m. up at 5.15. had bkf't at 6 \+ got away on 6.50 train - cool mt'f clouded all day - Got to West Port without incident very soon after 11 - drove at once to New Windsor - getting there at 12.30 - met W. W. Neilson \+ Mrs. W. Neilson \+ hers in \+ daughter on the piazza - on their way to St Hubert's. Had dinner at 1 \+ a good long sleep after it, as I was tired after my short night. After 5.30 Chas. arr'd from Lake George. He has been sick for some days - bowel trouble - \+ was complaining of severe pain in the chest, probably Rheumatism, which continued to grow worse until he went to bed at 11. After his arrival we took a short walk together over old ground - Supper at 6.30 - Eve'g with newspaper \+ walking on piazza.
