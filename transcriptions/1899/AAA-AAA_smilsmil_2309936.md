## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 4, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day with thunderous in-dications all about from m'f until night \- Storms all about but none passed over us. \- I wrote a letter to Ralph \- Then I painted out the background on the en-larged negative \- Jacobi \- At 12 I went down to P. O. Dinner at 1. \- afternoon I cleared up etching room, preparatory to laying grounds etc. \- Wm \+ Stone with man \+ team spent a good part of the day getting in the crop of oats raised This season on the swamp lot. At 6 went down town to P. O. \- After tea, at 8, I went down for a third time Eve'g I wrote to Ryan \- 2°. Nat' Bvl! \+ Adams, the house painter, Keene Valley

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 5, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

misty, muggy m'f. \- Somewhat sunshine in the middle of the day. \- at 4 p.m. thunder storms passed south of us \- sky generally overcast \- a very little rain about 6 o'clk \- clearing later. After b'kf. I laid a ground on plate for Jacobi etching. \- Then I traced photo. on gelatine. After dinner I took Jacobi negative, with back-ground painted out, to photographers \+ took an experimental print to see the result of my work \- Then home \+ spent some time in grinding my tools \+ getting them into condition for work. \- Then I varnished the gelatine tracing. Eve'g. Reading \+ walking on the piazza.

Seven weeks to-night since my spill in Central Park.
