## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 28, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow last night did it amt. to much — less than an inch fell. The day was overcast until evg. when it cleared. To studio at 9.30 — worked at my press until near 2 o'clk. Taking five pfs from Mr. G. Plate — not one of them being satisfactory. There are portions of the face that are actually worn out by much rubbing, scraping \+ changing. Altogether I am heartily sick \+ ashamed of it. Home to lunch at 2 — The boys off to Nerly cum Theatre — a treat from Mary Cook — They dined there in the evg. Mary went to Po'k by afternoon train. After lunch went to Fine Arts Socy. Bldg. Varnishing Day of Na of D. Spring Exh. Did not meet many of the members — The show of pictures impressed me as uninteresting — I do not now Remember one painting that impressed me strongly as being good. Watsons had a talk with me about a scheme he has of saving the Socy of Am. Painters — that is, that d. is to do the Awakening \+ then buy lots \+ build alongside of the fine arts — all members of the Socy of A.P. to be made Associate members of the U.L. — It amused me, being a poor fragment of the H.B. Butter Scheme I urged upon him Na. Six years ago \+ that he [illegible] then strongly opposed. Andrew Carnegie this afternoon came in soon after \+ Watsons \+ I talked with them. C. said that I had Recouted from my faith in the Butter-Carnegie scheme, but I assured him that I had never lost my faith in it. At 5 went to studio for half an hour — then home — a small family at Dinner Table — Kate, Katharine, Bessie \+ Self — The usual quiet evg. — The boys coming home at about 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 29, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold wintry day — After bkfs: wrote a note to Andrew Carnegie to assure him again that I had not changed my mind about the H.B. Butter Scheme. Then to studio, then to Kimmel Tvoigt's with print of Jacobi head \+ got a bid for printing (\+ paper) at $10 per 100 — then to Dept. of Water Supply — Park Row — \+ paid Water Tax for the year — then to Lee & Lee but met Mr. Lee in Bdway. I gave him Martin's letter with reference to his moulding in front of his house projecting over my front \+ the promise used to put it in shape for Record. Then back to K.\+ V's \+ then to studio. Home to lunch at 1.30 — Roland Mulford called \+ I saw him. To studio at 3 \+ for two hours was cleaning up. Then home — Dinner — Jim dined with Tom Cook \+ then went to some dancing class party with Tom — I read aloud for awhile to Ralph.
