## Left Page

### Tues. July 11, 1899

**Weather:** Charming m't  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming m't - overcast p. m. with threats of showers that did not come.
I wrote some letters after b'kft. and at about 11 o'clk. went down town on my own legs. It was slow work but not so painful as it was annoying.
I transacted business in various places - had a long talk with Edgar Turrell about the rebuilding of the burned "Village Hall" - got home at 1 o'clk. - dinner - then a nap - then a little more writing \+ at 6 o'clk. I went down town again - I met so many \+ told the story of my accident so often that it was 7 o'clk. before I got home to tea - Eve'g with newspaper.
Kate Leonard is so crippled with Rheumatism in legs \+ feet that Dr. Gardner had to be called to see her to-day.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 12, 1899

**Weather:** Lovely m't  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely m't - Thunder showers in the afternoon. The boys went at 10 o'clk to the Episcopal pic-nic at Heart Lake - Katherine \+ Bessie went at 12 o'clk. returning at 7.30 - Mary \+ I had a quiet time at home - I wrote letters in the m't - Dinner at 1 - a nap during the thunder storm - more letters -
