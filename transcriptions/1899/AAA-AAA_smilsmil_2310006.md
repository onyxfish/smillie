## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 26, 1899

*[written above header: 'Gene Atwater stayed all night with the girls.]*

**Weather:** Fine winter day \- clear + cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine winter day \- clear + cold \- up at 30 in obedience to drugs \- my headache passed with other things, got to bkp. a little late. Wrote notes until 10.30 \- then to studio \- Spent an hour in putting a new graver into shape to use \- then some more fussing work on "Mr. G." + took a print \- Home to lunch at 1.40 \- back at 2.30 + worked as I had in the mg until 4.30 \- then washed up + put down large paper for proofs to-morrow \- then to O'Brien's printing office in 23d St. with copy for my N.A. Treas. Report that I got from Grannis only yesterday. I left it there as a "Rush" order \- Then took a walk up 5th Ave. + home to dinner after 6 \- Gene Atwater had arr. from New Haven during the P.M. \- to stay over-night with Bessie \- to go to the "Coming out" party of Sallie Cook \- a display of party dresses + o'clk \- Jennings their escort \- as he went away with them he asked Mary if he should bring back a receipt for them. Charlie came in at [illegible] + photos of my place at Keene Heights \- I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 27, 1899

**Weather:** cold + raw \- overcast + threatening snow  **Temp:** [illegible]

cold + raw \- over cast + threatening snow which came at night. It was about 10 o'clk when I got to studio \- another day wasted on the Mr. G. head \- most of it in fruitless experimenting in printing \- It is difficult for me to get ink of the composition best suited for this work + I have worn the plate considerably in trying many combinations of black, brown + oil \- medium + strong. To-day I opened the bottle of "Turner Brown" that I got in a dry powder from Newman's \- Schosp, Lond. in 1892. I ground some of it up with black + boiled oil + the result was very peculiar \- I could not get a sound print with it \- this is a serious disappointment to me as I have been holding this Brown in Reserve for especially fine work. Home to lunch at 1.30. Gene Atwater rel. to New Haven in the afternoon. I went back to studio at 2.15 but gave up printing at 4 \- went down to 23d St. \- Home at 6 \- Tom Cook dined with us. Eveg. I did some work at my book cataloguing \- Kate dined at Charlie's \-
