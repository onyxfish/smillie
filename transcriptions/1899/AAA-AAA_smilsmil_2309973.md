## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 17, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After the high wind last night the bad weather came - showery mtf. - clearing as we traveled to N.Y. - very mired. Up before 6 \+ got to work as promptly as possible packing my trunk in studio - bkfs. about 8 - then lots \+ all sort of things to do - but I managed to get everything into my trunk \+ things generally cleared up - It was near noon when the bus came for us - to Alford about 12.45 - Milk Train due at 1.15 an hour late - some heavy showers while at Alford - a dreary wait - When at last the train came along it was decided to run it on the switch \+ let the Syd. Ren past - by time table the 1.15 train was to get to Scranton ahead of the Syd. \+ there were to transfer to it - as it was the Syd. was flagged to stop at Alford \+ we at once got seats in Parlor Car "Richfield" \+ had a very comfortable Ride to N.Y. - getting in to Hoboken about 7.30 \+ to the house at 8.30 - we had a light meal - Joe came in for awhile - trunks arr. soon after 10 - I unpacked mine \+ it was about midnight when I got to bed.

A singularly silvery luminous moonlight effect as we crossed ferry to foot of Christopher St. -

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 18, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy showers in the early mtf. - followed by a muggy \+ very warm day. I got up before 8 \+ had bkfs. about 9 - then loafed for some time with newspapers - then to studio - sorted some of my things at home. \+ had a short nap before lunch at 1.30 - During the P.M. I had another nap. It was about 5 o'clk. when I went to studio with my etching tools etc. Then sauntered down to 4th Ave. \+ 23? 5. \+ bought a sharp pointed scalpel \+ a knife for cutting corns - then to the Dewey Arch \+ up 5th Ave. \+ home. Dinner at 6.30 - Eve'f. divided bet. newspaper \+ Ralph's lessons for the morrow. To bed soon after 10.
