## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 12, 1899

**Weather:** milder & partially overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

milder & partially overcast — To studio at about 10 & worked on Keene plans until 12 — then went to agent, 11 W. 34th St. & put my name down for the studio in 22d. St. for my class of Etchers. Then to S. P. Avery's & saw the F. H. Smith drawings & then to Durand-Ruel's & saw the Bondin paintings. Home to lunch soon after 1 — To studio again at 3 & worked on Maynard's Ex Libris plate until 4.30 when Mr. Kingsbury came & together we grubbed thro' the N.A. minutes of meetings — Council and general — for two years, to get a chain of evidence as to the intention of the N.A. to build — to be presented at Court next week. It was 6 o'clk when he left — Then I walked for half an hour & home to dinner at 6.30 — a quiet evg. at home, Reading.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 13, 1899

**Weather:** Very disagreeable day — dark, cold & wet  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary Ret'd. from Po'keepsie.

Very disagreeable day — dark, cold & wet. Sleet & snow fell last night, freezing in a film of ice over everything & making walking very difficult. Rain continued during the day adding to the discomfort & difficulty. I went to studio at about ten o'clk & spent the m'g writing letters to Mollie & Katie Adams & others — Home to lunch — and at the studio spent the afternoon as I had the m'g. The Rain having ceased for a time I took a short walk before dinner — Evg. at home —
