## Left Page

### Fri. June 9, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary \+ the boys went to Montrose

Sunshine for a little while in the m't then it clouded over \+ was slightly cooler \- distinctly cooler in the afternoon \+ ev'g. when there was a light fall of rain \- B'kf't at 7.30 \- Soon after 9. The C'pp. man came \+ got mules \- then R \+ I went to Spalding's \+ I got some golf clubs for him \- then home. I attended to various matters in getting things all ready for a start \- At 12. Mary, Jim, Ralph, Kate, Leonard, Jane Banks \+ I went by street car to foot of Christopher St \- then to Hoboken \+ I got the party off on the 1 o'clk train for Montrose in good shape. Then home, where Mary Mooney was still in charge \+ had my lunch \- I then took a little nap \+ after that did some writing until after 6 \- then to Charls \- dinner after 9 about 9 when we left the table \- Chas, Thos. Adams \+ self \- in library until 10.30 \- photos of Montrose \- etc \- etc \- \+ then to bed \- a very comfortably, cool night.

Got a letter from W.G. Neilson \- official \- as Pres't of Adirondack Mt. Reserve \- giving me permission to lay a half inch pipe to Highland cottage to connect with Gill Brook supply \- no tanks allowed \- 1st faucet $5.00 \& f. 4oo 3d \+ all subsequent ones $3.00 each \- no promise to insure supply \- \+ no promise for any thing beyond the present year \- Upon such uncertainty I replied that I could take no action, but would study the water problem from all points when I got to Keene.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 10, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day \- overcast \+ comfortably cool \- up at 7 \- B'kf't at 8 with Adams \- to house at 9 \- wrote to M. J. \+ Maghee at Beedes \+ then act to Douglass \- Man. Storage \- Brooks Bro's \- to Met. Life \+ saw M. M't. Ecker about plans of the Met. Co. for old M.A. M'f. with a view to renting galleries \- then to N.A. \+ saw Davis \- then to studio \+ saw Maynard \+ told him of my interview with M. E. Then to Laundry, Hahnefields, Owen's, Charl's \+ to Century at 1 o'clk where I had a comfortable lunch \- Home after 2 \- \+ worked at writing until about 5 \- then put new Hunt saddle on Bicycle \+ went out for about half an hour \- Riding on Park Ave. and adjacent streets. To dinner at Charl's at about 7 \- a quiet ev'g \- so cool that he played for awhile on his orchestrelle. To bed at about 11.
