## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 30, 1899

**Weather:** mild & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild & pleasant - got up with a beastly headache that struck tome all day - I printed from Jacobi \+ M.P. G. - but was not successful - everything seemed to go wrong \+ I was disgusted. Home to lunch \+ back \+ printed until after 3.30 - I wrote my resignation as member of Bldg Com. N.A. p.s. \+ took it to Maynard. He took it without comment \+ I made no explanation - I don't think it was needed. Got home at 5 \+ rested on my bed until 6 then up \+ dressed \+ got to Charls' at 7.10. At 7.30 we sat down to table - all my family - all of Geo's family - Josephine, Wells wife, Minn, her husband \+ her boy - 15 in all - we had an excellent dinner served by two Union League darkies \+ we sat at table until 10.35. Then Charl furnished music for us on his orchestrelle - It was after mid night when we got home — feeling in no humor to go to bed. I read the Dec. magazine until after 1 o'clk - then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild day with a little rain at night. I got up \+ had help. a little before 10 feeling pretty well after the gorge of last night - It was quite 11 before I got to work printing. I had some large heavy plate paper wet \+ ready for India p.p. So I persisted altho' I did not feel at all like work - the result was unsatisfactory of course. Home to lunch \+ back before three took two p.p. from M.P. G. - plate on thin paper that came out all right. Spencer Coon came to interview me about the talked of combination or "Salon" Sytèm. I could give him but little time - \+ besides, it was a subject that I did not wish to talk about to a newspaper man.

Dinner at 6.30 a quiet eve'g at home - Charl came in for a little while.
