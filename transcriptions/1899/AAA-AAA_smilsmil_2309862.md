## Left Page

### Tues. March 7, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Almost another blizzard — a high wind + four or five inches of light soft snow made the city look as though we were in the depths of winter again — After 10 when I got to studio. About noon I went to Linser + Amend's + got Nitric Acid then to N.A. + left word for Etching class to come to my studio this p.m. + I would superintend the biting of their plates — Then to Park Ave. Hotel and made a short call on Mr. Geo. Iles, to see some prints that he has recently purchased, Cole's Voyage of Life — very poor. Lunch at home — 1.30 — Studio at 2 — four of the etchers came + I was with them until 5.30 — Then to Many Cook's — to Charl's — + home to dinner — Every worrying over the balancing of my check book.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 8, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day — To Charl's at 9 o'clk — then to studio — got painting "My in Adirondacks" ready for Athletic Club exhibit — then to 2d Nat'l Bk + got a loan of $250 — then to N.A. got check books + to studio at 11.30 — To work on my Report to be read at N.A. meeting this evef — at 1 o'clk — a short walk + then home to lunch — After that, Miller the carpenter came + we went on the Roof to plan some way of ventilating through the air shaft without letting the snow in — then to studio + continued work on Report — Between 3 + 4 o'clk. Hale + Gerster came in + wanted me to go out with them "for fun". I wished to but had to finish my report — we had a good time for a while — then they left + by 5 o'clk I finished my report — then walked up to 5th Ave. entrance of Central Park for an airing + back — half an hour of rest before dinner — Eve'g at 8 o'clk. went to regular quarterly meeting of N.A. — fairly full — fairly interesting — I left at 10.30 + walked to town with Nicole + Walker.

Mr. Maryon gave 1½ hours to Jim this eve'g
