## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 13, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another quiet, luminously misty, mild \+ quiet Oct. day \- In the sunshine it was very warm \+ in the still air the leaves came floating down like lazy butterflies. I was late getting up, feeling very groggy \- A miserable little boil on my lips among the hair of my moustache is really making me feel wretched all over \- my lips is so swollen as to be very inconvenient. After bkfs. I was with Herrick directing the crating of my aqua-tint pop-over \- then down in the lot to look after the men at work there upon the stone wall \- then to the lane to see the painters at work upon the fence. Then down town to P.O, etc. \+ then, up a hemlock tree that has grown up to interfere with our view of sunsets \+ I cut some 12 or 15 feet of his top off \- after dinner I took a little rest \+ then finished sorting apples \- wrote two or three short notes \- ferreted the trees that had been cut in the mf. \- again with Herrick \- at 5 went to P.O. Had a talk with Cooley who wishes to make a bid for the plumbing of the Keene Ho. \- then again to lot \- then worked with Herrick until 6 folding \+ stowing away rugs. Every some newspaper \+ more stringing of lamp wicks.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 14, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ And yet another day like to two predecessor \+ followed by a superb moonlight night with a refreshing breeze from the S.E. I got up at 7 after an unsuccessful night owing to the trouble on my face. Hfst. a little before 8 \+ before 9 I went to Dr. Gardner's \+ saw him \- says the trouble is caused by an abnormal nasal secretion which coming down into the hair of the moustache sets up an irritation at the roots that results in general disturbance \+ many little pimples \+ suppuration. The larger one on my lip he says is a part of that disturbance. He gave me a preparation to be used with an atomizer to spray the membranes back of the nose \+ a local wash for the lips. Then I went \+ saw Mr. Cooley \+ left with him plans of Keene house \- he wishes to figure on the plumbing \+ make an estimate. Then home \- Herrick writing \- he helped me in various ways, in packing a box with blankets, hangings etc \+ a bt. with the tennis outfit, hammocks, etc \- also, headed up apple bbls. \- I marked all of the bbls. \+ boxes. Afternoon many had along ride with Daud. \+ Mrs. Sayre. I got thro' a good deal of work after 6 went down to P.O. \+ got newspaper \- Tea at 6.30 \- Eve'g, an hour with newsp. \- Then stringing wicks \+ some newly cleaned lampft. Then I walked for a while on [illegible] enjoying in spite of my decrepitude, the wonderful moonlight.
