## Left Page

### Tues. May 2, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 75

Heavy thunderstorm from 7 to 8 p.m. More uncomfortably hot than yesterday. After bkft. I went down to Court, suit of N.A. agt. Baumann for Rent. Before I went Nicole came in to propose a plan of campaign for the Annual meeting — but I told him of last night's fracas + then the fat was all in the fire — I told him that I had determined to call up at the next Council meeting my resignation of Oct. 3. + insist upon its being accepted then. At Court Baumann's lawyer begged for another adjournment but Kingsbury opposed — the Judge add. it to 1.30 this p.m. Then I went up to Academy — met Blum there who urged me to retain Pres.-ship but I told him it was impossible. Home at 12. Rested until 12.30 — then lunch — then to Court — case called — no defendant — judgment given by default. Home at 3 — for the rest of the afternoon I wrote notes of various kinds. Dinner at 6.30. Even Read a little to Ralph — then read a short note to Jim enclosing his monthly check — at 9.30 I went to G.C. Station with it.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 3, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fresh in f, growing cooler through the day until at evg it was quite sharp. I had some writing to do + then a number of messages, so that it was after 10 when I got to studio — there callers came one after another. I could do nothing + it was near before I could get away — Went to Roome's in W. 34t St. + paid May Rent of frething studio — then home to lunch — then to studio + wrote until 4.30 — then to N.A. + attended to some business there. Home at 6. Dinner at 6.30 — newspaper + a little reading to Ralph — Headache.
