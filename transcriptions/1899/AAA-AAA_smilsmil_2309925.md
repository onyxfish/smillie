## Left Page

### Thur. July 13, 1899

**Weather:** Damp, overcast & chilly morning.  **Temp:**

during the day it cleared somewhat but was more or less overcast & cool all day — I suffered a good deal of pain in my injured side and feel generally out of sorts & depressed. I did but little work during the day — stretched paper & prepared to draw plans for the detail of large window in Keene house — got an interesting letter from Mr. Maghee describing present condition of the house. Eve'g read aloud to Ralph for awhile.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 14, 1899

**Weather:** Superb day — a day to live in such  **Temp:**

weather, if one is not maimed — I did not suffer so much discomfort as I did yesterday — yesterday's suffering was no doubt caused by the Rheumatic character of the weather. I did not accomplish much but just lounged about. Dr. Gardner came in the afternoon & looked me over with some care & then told me that he did not think that I had cause for fear or discouragement — told me of some of his experiences, from which I gathered that I shall be lucky if I recover from the effects of this accident before next winter. Kate came on the eve'g train from N.Y. The girls & boys went to station & met her. Eve'g Jim read two amusing skits in dramatic form, written by his school fellows Henry Plummer & Ned Thurston — They were two very realistic portrait sketches of Jim & I lessened the truth. That Jim is a "dawdler" came to me with great force & I could not laugh as the others did.
