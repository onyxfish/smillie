## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 11, 1899

**Weather:** Clouded & chilly  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded & chilly, growing somewhat milder towards night — high winds during last night — up at 7. after bkfst. went to the house & the mf was spent there in investigation. About noon a carpenter named Cox came from Jay (16 miles away) summoned by Besde to work for me. After lunch he took his tool chest to the house & I saw him there & explained what I wished done. He did not work at all to-day. I worried most of the afternoon over a lot of hardware, trying to make it tally with a list written list that I got from Black, said to have been sent by the Russell Irwin Co. I could not find the half of the things in the list. Well. The Porter came to Charl, having learned his name, & told him that there were some cases for him in the store room — Charl passed him to me — I went with him to the store Room & there was my missing hardware — the man in charge said it had been there a month —
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 12, 1899

**Weather:** Clouded  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded mf & threatening — but clearing during the day — late in the afternoon a gusty shower passed north of us — we got a very little of it — after bkfp. I had the lost hardware sent over to the house. There I spent most of the mf. unpacking & checking it off from a list sent with it. Cox, the carpenter came & commenced work in the large room — ceiling up the E. sides & then laying a portion of the floor. Lunch at near 2 — after that Charl & I went down to Scott Brown's & had a chat with him. we stopped at Mr. Coffin's cottage & there met Mr. Nottman, a niece of Saml. Colman, (Miss Howard) & other ladies. Then to Pine Knoll & completed the house cleaning of 2d Story — throwing the larger pieces of board out of the window & burning up the smaller ones in fire place of mg ms room. After the floor was swept the place looked almost finished —
Dinner after 7. Beautiful night.
