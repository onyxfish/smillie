## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 24, 1899

**Weather:** Clouded  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded o'clk. clearing before noon to a pleasant, warm day. After b'fst: wrote to agent at Saranac Lake to put $3000 fine Ms. on Keene house. I worked for awhile on Jacobi \+ then put it aside. I shall not work any more on it here. Some time getting my tools ground \+ in good order — Then I got out crayon study of Mr. Gerster's head \+ the plate that I bit in more than two years ago — After the biting I did very little on it being discouraged — After dinner I took hold of it again \+ worked until 5 o'clk — Then to P.O. \+ a walk afterwards. Evett. newspaper — walking piazza \+ Kate Douglas Wiggin's "A Cathedral Courtship."

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 25, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day — Thunderstorms south north of us in the west. which was exceedingly quiet \+ close, but no storm reached us before bed-time. Morning, I wet down paper \+ ground fresh ink \+ then took two india prs of Mr. Gerster — I decided that I must take out the background in front of the face — it was too formal in its arrangement of lines \+ too dark — so I set to work with scraper \+ ayr stone — wrote a letter to Jim — dinner — took J's letter to P.O. As it was then near time for afternoon mail to come in I waited for it. After 4 when I got to studio — worked until near 6 — Then for half an hour cut "black-knot" from plumb trees — I found time during the day to pick pears \+ put them away \+ to wash some printing cloths — Evening, loafing \+ reading.
