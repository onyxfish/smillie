## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 30, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day — have not yet quite regained my physical equilibrium — so, did not do much work in the m[orning] but loafed about watching the men at work Relaying tiles — putting tin upon the ends of upper piazza — decking + Herrick, casing plumbing work on lower piazza — went down town about 11 — at noon took three prints from Mr. G — place — after dinner worked awhile with crayon on one of the prints — I loafed on the upper piazza \+ out on the Tennis C[ourt] watching the girls play — at 5 went down town — Every — Reading in the new magazines.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 31, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The boys return from camp.

Fine day. I got up with a severe headache after a bad night — I did not attempt to work on my plate but most of the m[orning] was spent making glue paste \+ covering a large box, made long ago for laying dry aquatint grounds with strong muslin — At 12.30 I took a little to eat \+ at 1.45 I started, with Mary, Catharine \+ Bessie, for Camp Choconut. We got there at 4 o'clk. \+ found Jim \+ Ralph waiting impatiently for us, all of the other campers having left early in the m[orning]. We saw Mr. Carmaet \+ Mrs. Winlock \+ started for home at 5.15 \+ got in at 7.45 without incident except the breaking of a breeching strap on the big hill near Mrs. Muelford's. We had our supper soon after \+ all hands being more or less tired we got to bed early.
