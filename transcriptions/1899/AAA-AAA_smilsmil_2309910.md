## Left Page

### Tues. June 13, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm day \- filmy overhead at times \- very sticky late in the afternoon \- I got to the house at 9 \+ after doing a little work I went to N.A. \- by good luck found Grannis there \+ got from his safe, not yet moved, all the ins. policies that young Smith cause to me for yesterday \- stopped at Fuessel's about window shade \- then home \+ worked till noon \- have commenced now upon the assembling of my various N.A. accts \+ the incomes of making the combination agree with the balances given by 2? Nat \- the first go was not a success. At noon went to Rayno's \+ left order, with white silk grenadine, for summer neck ties \- then to studio \- got some N.A. documents \+ saw Maynard \- then to Century \+ got lunch \- home at 2 just in time for the excitement of fire on Martin's Roof \- my neighbor on the east side of me \- Engines were rushing up in front of the house \+ a crowd had assembled \- my front door was open \+ a young man from Fuessel's on the stoop \- said the painter had let him in \+ firemen were going into Martin's house with hose \- I went onto my roof \+ found the fireman on Martin's roof tearing up the tin \- there was a little fire underneath that was put out with a pailful or two of water \- The fire started no doubt, in the clothes of my painter spread on Martin's roof to protect it from paint spattering \- but as he was working at my skylights he knew nothing of it until a woman from a 3? St. house called to him \- one cloth \+ some Rofte was burned \- Painter admits that he smoked after his lunch out \- says he was in the shade of tank at No. 106\- I think the fire started from some carelessness of his but he knew nothing of it at the time \- About 4.30 I went out \+ had a bicycle ride in park getting in at 6 \- I was warm \+ tired \- Rested \- changed clothes \+ got to Charles' to dinner at 7 \- busy \- looking over photos \-

## Right Page

### Wed. June 14, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very sultry, disagreeably hot day \- to house at 9 \- Have got into a perfect muddle over my N.A. accts \+ am now much worried by them \- At one time I had them as I thought, proved to May 5th \- but now I am completely befogged \- At noon went to 2? Nat \- out \- but one check is still out so, did not get the deposit books of the three closed accts \- then to Century \+ had lunch \- a shower before 2 o'clk. made every thing steam \- to house at 2 \+ worried most of the P.M. only getting deeper into the mire \- About 3 \:clk Digester came \- he wants me to etch a portrait of Dr. Jacobi \- which, of course, I shall be very glad to do.
