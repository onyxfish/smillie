## Left Page

### Mon. July 3, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day tempered by a pleasant breeze. At times it seemed as though we might get a thunderstorm, but the day passed without it. Adams took hold of the matter of laying out the tennis court in earnest \+ went down town \+ bought measuring tape, lime \+ line \+ before the day was finished the court was completed \+ ready for players. He also bought me a very good bottle of Claret from the druggist who feared it might not be good as it had been in his cellar for 10 years. He offered, as a superior article, a sweet, fresh made Ohio wine but B\- preferred the old Medoc. Well. Charl took the boys down town \+ bought a lot of fire crackers, fire balloons, etc for them. Later Mr. \+ Mrs. Dant. Sayre \+ Mrs. Jessie Comstock Edgar came \+ spent a chatty hour with us. I got out some chinese lanterns. The piazzas were pretty.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 4, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thos. Adams Ret\[urne\]d. to N.Y.

A very hot day, but tempered by a very pleasant breeze that blew all day. At night thunderstorms passed south and west of us. No active work was done by any of us. About 11. The town parade came up to Fair Ground \+ we saw it at F.G. entrance. The "Fantasticals" were a poor lot \+ a dismal attempt at fun. At noon Adams left us to return to N.Y. Dinner about 1. Afternoon under the trees. The boys amused by their small cannon, firing at small wooden forts. Evening Mrs. Jessie Edgar \+ her little girl. Miss Macdstok \+ a little girl, with two others, came by invitation to see our fire works. We sat on the upper piazza while Charl set them off at the foot of the lawn in front of the house. There was a very good display of rockets \+ fine balloons. The company stayed until about 10 o'clk. The night was oppressively close.
