## Left Page

### Sat. July 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day — my night was not a restful one, altho' it was so cool that I lay under two blankets. Adams was ecstatic over his nights rest and delighted with every thing about the house \+ place. I feel so lame \+ sore that I got about very little — I made a tour of the grounds about the house \+ barns. Jessie, the cow, was delivered of a bull calf in the afternoon — altho' in honor, undoubtedly, of my arrival I waived the compliment \+ named the new arrivee, Tho. Adams.

Nothing of interest — we ate our meals \+ loafed — Well, Ralph read aloud to me for awhile.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 2, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day — but quite bearable when the heaviest work of the day was to swing in a hammock on lounge in a big chair. Adams \+ members of the family went to church in the morning but I clung to the shady piazza. I am constantly \+ depressingly lame \+ sore, but I can note a steady improvement from day to day. Adams has devised a plan for next Summer — he \+ his wife \+ his friend Mabie \+ his wife are to hire my Montrose house — he has asked for the "Refusal" of it. Charl says he is very much in earnest.
