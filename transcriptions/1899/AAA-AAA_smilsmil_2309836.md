## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 14, 1899

**Weather:** Milder & not quite so wet as yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio & spent the m'f. writing notes in reply to some queries sent to me by strangers & which have long been in waiting — Lunch — & again to studio. Packed Etching proof "Cedars by Moonlight" to be sent by Exp. to Rev. Mr. Benson of Phil'a. Pasted my Water Color on the "Susquehanna" in frame ready to send to Exbt. At 4.30 I walked down to Lockwood deForest's, 7 E. 10th St. but he was not in. Then walked home — Eve'g feeling very uncomfortable — Sore throat, pain in back & head, etc — went to bed early & passed a wretched night.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 15, 1899

**Weather:** Bright, pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

I did not get up until after 10'clk — & then I got up feeling very seedy — Dinner at 2 — After 3 o'clk. Lockwood deForest came & we had a talk about N.A. matters for an hour or more. I asked him if he would consent to accept the Pres't ship. He would not say "yes" — nor would he say "no" — but, unless he learns something that will scare him off I think he will eventually accept — Tea at 6.30. Eve'g, as usual — some Reading to Ralph — & some reading by Ralph —
