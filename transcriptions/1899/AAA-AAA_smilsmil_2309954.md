## Left Page

### Sunday Sept. 10, 1899

**Weather:** Charming day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - Bkft. at about 9 - all the m/ about the house \+ around with Charl who made a number of photo. exposures to get house \+ surrounding country. It was about 2 o'clk. when we had dinner. Afternoon we walked out on Ausable Road \+ spent about a couple of hours on that - Then I wrote a long letter to Mary. Mr. Wetherbee, aunt of Emily Taylor of Po'k. came to me to tell me of the announced reugagement of E. T. Supper at 7 o'clk - Had a telephone chat with Katie Elkins who, with her husband is now at Adirondack Lodge \+ expects to come over here on Wed. after a walk on piazza with Charl. to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 9, 1899

St. Hubert's Inn - Room 229.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day - have got up feeling better - we had bhft. after 9 - \+ later went over to the house \+ we sat upon nail legs on my bedroom loggia amusing ourselves \+ listening to the song of the Pines. and we determined to make a start for St. Hubert's Inn - Our baggage had gone on by the 8 o'clk. stage - we did not get started until 10 - in a light, two seat wagon - The ride was delightful - the atmosphere was wonderfully luminous - It was 1 o'clk. when we got to the Inn \+ after getting our rooms we had lunch - After that, as promptly as possible, we went over to Pine Knoll \+ visit the house \+ annex - of course, as I expected, there was much to vex \+ distress me but on the whole I was able to be somewhat philosophic - The material is sometimes good of its kind but generally it is very ordinary \+ the workmanship is always very common - Dinner at 6 30 - Evening I was welcomed very pleasantly by a number of the friends of last summer. we went early to bed - our room is a large double bedded room in the attic.
