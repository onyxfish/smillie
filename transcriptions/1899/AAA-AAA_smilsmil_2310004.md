## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 22, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another mild, sunny day — to studio's about 9.30 — Another day of discouraging work on the Mr. G. Head — trying to get the shaded side of face in transparent shadow with correct modeling — I know that I am putting a great amt. of misdirected work on this plate, hopelessly trying to make something that was not in my mind when I first etched it — It is neither an etching or an engraving — The lines on the shaded side of the face are not intelligently laid in to represent modeling & texture — by repeated working over them I have got the whole side of the face into a muddy mess with out texture or meaning. I worked with graver until near noon — Then I took a proof. At 12.30 went to 2nd Nat. Bank — Home at 1.30 — lunch — Then to studio & worked until 4.30 — Dinner at 6.30 — Swamp Art Editor of N.Y. Times (L.W. Crippen) called to get some information concerning the story that has got out that the N.A. is seeking a Municipal Site — In the Tribune of mf. was an interview with Beckwith — In to-nights Post there is an item — It is a pity, I think, that the story has got out in the form it has, crediting Mayor Van Wyck with permission to approve a site in one of the new parks of the city — Clare came in for a while.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 23, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast & raw, with east wind. Up before 7 — was at Harlem R.R. Station — G.C. — at 7.45 to meet Bronson Cutting & friend to tell them that Raefer was not well enough to go to Bronx Park with them — I waited until 8.10 for them but they did not put in an appearance — Then I went to Dr. Yales — saw him & asked him to call at the house & see both the boys who are suffering from bronchial troubles — Then home & got some breakfast about — Then to Hebson's Real Estate office & there met Edw. & Mary Cook & George & Emily — together we executed a deed conveying our right & title to certain lands in the City of Cleveland, O. Taken by the city from certain dock lands owned conjointly by us, for the enlargement of the River harbor —. Then to studio & got to work upon that everlasting Mr. G. Head — I fussed for sometime with graver & took prints & after 12 took a large paper proof — which was a failure & sent me home to my lunch in a dejected state of mind — Went to studio & took two large paper proofs but neither of them was quite successful — so I cleaned up & at about 5 o'clk went to Putnam's & spent nearly an hour & a half hunting for books to suit my taste to give to the boys, Kate, Mary & other girls. I got for the boys two fountaine pens & two books — a book each for the others — home to dinner at 6.45 — Eve. fo. — as usual —
