## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 3, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - up at 7 - Jennings late - we had to scurry for our bkfs. Ralfsh got away to school at 8.30 \+ I did not see him again. I went to Jackson's \[illegible\] but they w\[oul\]d not promise to get my trunk to foot of Christopher St. before 3 o'clk then to Westcaths - nor w\[oul\]d they promise but they gave me a numbered receipt upon which the Del\[ivery\] bagage man w\[oul\]d give me a check for my trunk finished packing at 9.30 - but many other things kept me busy - writing to Jim \+ to others \+ giving directions to Jennings \+ going to Studio for Dr. Yale's copy of Dodd, Meade \+ Co's Publication of Rembrandt \+ which I told Jennings to take to the Dr. At 11. 45 \[illegible\] man came for my trunk \+ at 12 I started for the ferry getting there at 12.30 - no trunk - I went in without it - no incident on my way to montrose - the car at times uncomfortably overheated - Got home at about 8 o'clk. found Mary \+ Katharine expecting me. This only. Bessie - Kate \+ Kate Clinard \+ Jane went to N.Y., passing me on the way - Mary has made an arrangement with Mrs. Van Auken to come to the house every day \+ cook for us, for 75.00 per week. To bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 4, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delicious day - brilliant \+ cloudless and pleasantly warm. When the train came in at about 10 I went to station hoping to get some news of my trunk - nothing heard of it. Then I went to foundry \+ saw young Beach. he had the iron band for my Aquatint Ground box ready - all finished up by lathe work in spite of my directions to make it as simple \+ inexpensive as possible. I was much astonished \+ provoked when he chg\[e\]d me $20. for it. Took it home \+ spent some time down in swampfield with Speuse looking at the work done \+ laying out new work. After dinner Katherine helped me pick apples. At 3 I again went to station - my trunk had come - Then to [illegible] Office \+ to P.O. Pruning Trees \+ unpacking trunk until tea time. Eve\[nin\]g. I read aloud to K. \+ Mary one of Joel Chandler Harris's Sketches in Scribner. Mr. \+ Mrs. Daw\[son\]. Sayre, Jessie Edgar \+ miss Mackintosh came in \+ chatted for an hour - To bed soon after 10.
