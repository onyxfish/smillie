## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 15, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

And yet another — to-day the Ther. in my bed-room got up to 78°. I got up at 8 o'clk. — with a wretched headache that stuck to me all day but I pulled thro' after all fairly well. Bkft. about 9 — it was near 10 when I went down to the swamp-lot \+ for nearly two hours work at pruning a great wild-rose tree and an apple tree that was grafted some 3 or 4 years ago — then to the house \+ until near 3 o'clk worked at clearing up etching room \+ studio \+ packing some boxes of various things to go to N.Y. My face gave me almost as much trouble as my headache — the small boil on my lip continuing to discharge \+ swelling about a bit little. After dinner I lay down on my bed for an hour — then I wrote to Jim \+ Rush \+ to O. Reede about the Spp. Chq of $14.30 that was put into the bill I paid Bet. 7 \+ 8 o'clk. Mary \+ I walked down to P.O. with our letters. Then I read Tribune for Sunday — about 9 o'clk — had a light meal \+ to bed after 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 16, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine breezy day until eve'g. when about 6 o'clk. it clouded over \+ the wind blew strong \+ cool from the east. I had a very busy day — soon after bkft. went down to the lot. Geo. Walker \+ another man commenced work on the ditch to drain a spot that has been wet all thro' the summer. Hibberd \+ Roberts got to work on the wall rather late — Dickson \+ his team also at work. I got my trunk down into studio \+ gathered every thing that was to go into it. At noon went down to P.O. Dinner about 1:30 — Saw Dr. Gardner — thinks my lip is pro-gressing slowly but favorably. I need encouragement. Afternoon, again went down to lot — packed all of my etching materials \+ at 6 went down town \+ paid all the bills agt. me except one — It was 7 when I got home to tea — Some time making up my accts. Then until 10 — stringing wicks \+ fixing the lamps to leave. Soon after that to bed.
