## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 5, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

light fall of snow, continuing all the morning. Bkfs. at 4.30 - Writing until 1 o'clk - Then took my walk in the parlor until 2 - Then dinner Then again wrote until after 4. Then went to Century, looked at the pictures, met Jno Cauldwell and talked to him about the Paris Exposition of 1900 - Then walked up to Central Park \+ back - Tea at 6.30 - The usual evef at home.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 6, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull \+ disagreeable - some snow fell at night. I worked awhile on Cabin painting \+ at about 12 went to N.A. where I found considerable work for me to do in writing up check book \+ making out checks - It was two o'clk before I got home to lunch. I went to studio long enough to clean up palette \+ then again to N.A. \+ worked there until near 6 - Then home - dinner. At 8 went to N.A. Council Meeting - An animated but short meeting - Beckwith as Chairman of Ways \+ Means Com. Reported his interviews with Crocker Mayor Van Wyck \+ proposed getting up a bill to be presented to the State Legislature praying for $350,000 from the State, for the N.A., on the ground that it is a public institution. This was agreed to \+ the last unemployed man on the Council was put upon his Com. - B. was pleased - "now we've all got something to do," said he, \+ let's do it." Home before 11 -
