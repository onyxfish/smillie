## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 20, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot \+ cloudless day - a specimen drouth day. The Ther. got up to 87° in various parts of the house - in my bed room it did not fell below 72° last night - hot dry evef., a little breeze from the east springing up at about 10 p.m. I got up at 7.30 \+ enjoyed my bath room. \+kp; at 9 - I loafed about, hardly leaving the house - reading \+ writing - I wrote to Walter Cook, Underhill of Flintie Stone Co. To Orlando Beede (sending him $500) to stone mason \+ to Katz, an art dealer. Dinner at 2. It was after tea - at 8 o'clk. that I went down to the P.O. with letters - the rest of the evef. on piazza.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 21, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 88°

A very hot day - strong breeze but that was hot too - About 5.30 thunder storme with high wind \+ moderate amt. of rain which continued to fall until near 8 olk. Derrick came \+ put up W.C. Tank in my bath room. I was with him for some time. Kuan came in the afternoon \+ made water connection with Tank \+ to my surprise it would not fill - not more than 3 or 4 inches of water rose into the Tank. I printed pfe from Jabah's etching and worked all day, between provings, on the plate.
Evef. at 8 o'clk. went to P.O. \+ got my Tribune - walked the piazza for some time. Then read paper.
