## Left Page

### Sun. July 23, 1899

**Weather:** Grey m[illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Grey m[illegible]. burning off to a hot, sunny day with light east wind that grew strong at night \+ quite cool. I spent part of the m[illegible]. pruning plum trees \- had a talk with Frink about buying his house \+ lot if he could get for me the back end of Munger's lot that adjoins his. Wrote part of a letter to Bro. George \+ had a short nap before dinner at 2 \- afternoon, finished letter to Geo. \+ Took a walk over the Warner hill going around the Reservoir \+ Fair Ground. This was the first walk, for walk's sake, that I have taken since my accident \+ I am well pleased \- I am very stiff \+ some what sore but the walk was accomplished with little discomfort. Evelf. Read Sunday Tribune \- very lonesome without the boys \- to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 24, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

East wind all day \- overcast morning looking much like rain, but by the middle of the day it cleared beautifully clouding over again before night. My m[illegible] was spent working out of doors \- get about quite freely now \- I limp but have out little dis comfort from my leg. Wrote a conflee \+ notes before dinner \+ after dinner went down town \- for a while at exercises of laying corner stone of Baptist church \- letters from Jim \- Ralph \+ Roland Munford.
