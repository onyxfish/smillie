## Left Page

### Mon. May 22, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph Ret? from his visit to the Cuttings. More shadows than sunshine to-day. After bkft. waited for Mr. Martin to come with his plan for a front stoop — but he did not come — then waited for Mr. [illegible] Carrere \+ H — He came at 10.15 \+ I went to studio with him \+ showed him my sand — finished well — then found Sterne \+ a young woman model waiting for me — The y\+ woman wishes the next pose for Etching Class. Sterne worked on one of his plates while I went on with my work on N.A. accounts — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — Sterne came again \+ bit his work of the morning — the young woman also came with two or three hats, trying to free my requirements — I worked on accels — until 4 — then home — Ralph, who had Ret. in the mf from his visit with Bronson Cutting, bringing a fox terrier bitch, went out on his wheel with me — we went down fifth Ave. to 10th street — a difficult bit of riding at times, of which we were duly proud. \+ called at 41 W. 10th to see Dielman, but could not get in — then to Carrere \+ Hastings \+ found Dielman there — had a consultation about the finish on wells for N.A. school bld\+ — then Ralph \+ I went on up to the Park — going around the Mall \+ then home — a sprinkle of rain — in at 6 — I did not take my dinner until 7 — Eve\+ Mr. \+ M\+ Mann called to see me, wishing to get testimony to use ag\+t. J. A. L. Gardner — then Charles came in — \+ Thos. Adams — all out at 10 — \+ then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 23, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — showery sky — short shower at 2 o'clk — cloudless moonlight night. About 3 o'clk. A.M. was awakened by great nausea \+ for an hour lay with the sweat running off from me — then the barrier gave way in my bowels \+ got relief \+ from 5 o'clk. slept until 7. Got to studio at 9.30. \+ made things ready for biting — Sterne came \+ got to work \+ at about 11 Stahr came \+ he too set to work biting his plate — I kept an oversight of the two \+ did some work in posting up N.A. accts. that I have not yet been able to make come out in exact accord with the balances shown by check books — at 12 I went to N.A. Grannis not there — Home to lunch after 1 — to studio at 2.15 Sterne \+ Stahr were there — both of them have done very creditable plates — just now I think Stahr is ahead — Both of them are very much interested and in earnest. I went to Bormannis with his saddle \+ got my own — then to Miller's about Keene Hardware — then out \+ had a short ride on my bicycle. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve\+ I wrote a long letter to Branch at Keene \+ to M\+ Man — took the Branch letter to G.C. Station \+ mailed it. Then home \+ to bed at 11.
