## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 30, 1899

**Weather:** Clear & very cold \- Ther. as low as 7°  **Temp:** [illegible]

I wrote some notes after bkfst. & got to studio about 10 \- There, after a little searching I found the plate that I could not find yesterday \- Madison Sq. Garden & tower from the east, in 27° St. I got out original drawing & bkfst. taken in '96 [illegible] not until noon \- Then to 2d Natl. Bank & to Putnam's where a bought another fountain pen for Ralph \- Mary having taken his stud. which he did not like \- Then home to lunch. At 2, with Jim & Ralph went to Murray Hill Theatre, Lex. Ave, near 42d St. & saw the play of "Men & Women" by deMille & Belasco. It was a fairly amusing play fairly well done \- the house was full & seemed to enjoy it \- It was about 5 when we got out \- it was too unpleasantly cold & windy to make walking a pleasure, so we went directly home \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g, some reading aloud to Ralph \- Charlie came in for a little while.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 31, 1899

**Weather:** Cold clear weather continues \- up at 7  **Temp:** [illegible]

Had my scone \- back to bed \- up & bkfst. att'g. Read my news paper Then tackled & went thro' the job of taking down \- rearranging and piling back again upon the tops of closets a great number of unbound vols. of Ill. London News \- much \- Harper \- Century & other publications, wrapped with brown paper & labeled.
Before dinner I went out for a short airing \- to studio & to Gr. Cent. Station \- Dinner at 2 \- an uncomfortable & disturbed nap on the dining room sofa \- Then writing. Eve'g among other things I read aloud to Ralph & Jim Mark Twain's article on Christian Science publ. in The Cosmopolitan Mag. I don't know how much they understood of its method & its madness \- They were amused by parts of it.
