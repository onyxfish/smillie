## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 26, 1899

**Weather:** Brilliant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant \+ quiet day — Ralph \+ I took 10.40 train to Fordham \+ walked to the new Zoo gardens in Bronx Park. We were much interested altho' the work is all in an embryotic state. We would have felt amply paid if we had seen nothing more than the Polar bears in their Tank of Water. It was very funny. Took 1.30 train back \+ got home 5 m. late for dinner — After dinner my head ached so that I gave up \+ was on my bed from 3.30 until 6.30 — Well again read to Ralph — at 9 we went to Grand Central Station \+ got free time — Then I wrote a letter to Stahr, in Munich, \+ one to Mr. Barhydt.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 27, 1899

**Weather:** Very pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant, mild \+ quiet day — After bkft. went to studio for a few minutes, then to 2d Nat'l Bk. where I made a loan of $750 to pay Taxes that must be paid before Dec. 1st. Then down town — stopped at 290 Bdway \+ subscribed at Times, London, office for a set of Encyclopaedia Britannica — 25 vols. half bound at $107.10 to be pd. in monthly instalments of $5.10. Then to Tax Office \+ pd. Taxes at home \+ Studio v/p amt to $669.70. Back to studio at noon \+ worked with graver on face — Mr. G. plate, until almost 2 — Then home to lunch — back at 3 \+ worked until 4.30 — Then walked up 5th Ave. to 59th St. and back home — dinner at 6.30 — Welf wrote a note to H.C. Underhill — Macknight \+ Hustic Stone Co — concerning coal hole covers that have been cemented into pavement.
