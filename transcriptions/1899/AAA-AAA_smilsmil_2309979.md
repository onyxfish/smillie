## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 29, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable wet day - rain commenced bt. 10+11 a.m. + continued, sometimes in showers, until evef. Mary decided to go to Scarborough on the Hudson to see Minn (Mrs. Walter Pierce, who is at home there alone to-day + much under the weather - She left the house to take 10.45 Train - this left me alone at home - I Read newspaper + wrote letters - lunch at 1.30 - afternoon, more writing - Wrote to Callawn Bros. accepting (with a modification) their bid to put plumbing in the Keene Heights house. I went to Jas. H Morse's bet. 5+6 o'clk. p.m. + saw Mrs. Morse - Told her about the boys - + why I had sent Ralph to Cutter's School - Then as Mary had not, arr'd at 5 o'clk - as expected, I went to Charles + telephoned to Scarborough + found that Mary had gone to Sing-Sing to take a later Train - She got home at 6.30 - dinner - Evef. at home + to bed early -

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 30, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool + pleasant - Ralph came home with Mr. Cutting soon after 9 a.m. + then to school. I got to studio early + finished cleaning up the student's etched plates - I find that I have 27 from which to take p.p's. + a number more that are not worth that trouble. I got through before - lunch at 1.30 - after that went to 2° Nat'l + fixed up matters for the end of the month - then to N.E. &D. Amsterdam Ave. +109th St. to look thro' the acct. of Lee+Lee who have just sent in a batch of bills that Rockwood deForest has asked me to audit. I was engaged upon this work until after 5 - Then walked down Central Park West (8th Ave.) to 100th St. then across, thro' the park to 5th Ave. +90th St., seeing some fine effects looking west over the great Reservoir - Took Mad. Ave. car at 88th St. - home at 6.30 dinner - Evef. with R.+his studies until after 9 — Then balanced my check book to agree with 2° Nat'l Bk. figures - + to bed.
