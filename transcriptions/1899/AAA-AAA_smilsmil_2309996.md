## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 6, 1899

Lizzie \+ Jessie Smillie dined with us
Jane Diring died at 2. A.m.

Cold, cloudy \+ blustering — a little flurry of snow at one time — I got to studio at about 10 — Etched in \+ bit additional portions to background on M.P. 9 — plate overbit it, of course, \+ had to scrape \+ burnish — worked awhile with graver on Stem plate, sketch of a girl's head. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — about 3 Dr. Gessler came \+ he spent the rest of day light. He makes various criticisms of M.P. 9 head with respect to likeness — so I shall have to work more on it — At 5 I went out \+ in spite of cold \+ wind had a fine three mile walk on Fifth Ave — Home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Lizzie \+ Jessie Smillie there — At about 8 o'clk they went with Mary \+ Kate to Ch[urch]

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 7, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A milder day — At bkft? rec? from C.H.A. Diring a note from stating that Jane Hinshelwood, his wife, died at 2 A.M. yesterday. She has been sinking for some time past under a complication of disorders, Rheumatic gout, Bronchitis, Kidney trouble etc — for some months her mind has been much affected. After bkft. wrote to Middlehill — Thistle Stone Co. \+ some other notes — Then to Diring's, but he was out — To studio \+ until lunch time at 1.30 worked on M.P. 9 — head carrying out suggestions made by the Dr. yesterday — Then home — At 2.30 went to Chas. Calverly's \+ sat for him until 4.30 — he is changing the neck, wear \+ coat collar on bust that he modeled from more than a year ago — Then to Col. Turner's — 35 W. 14 St. to talk about the stage fitting of Village Hall, Montrose, Pa. — introduced to Miss Thorke — who attends to decorations in textiles — On my way home stopped in some of the galleries to see pictures — dinner at 6.30 — Quiet even\[ing\] \+ to bed early.
