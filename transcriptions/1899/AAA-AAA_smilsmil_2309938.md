## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 8, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another superb day — cooler — at night had a little fire in dining room. Got with a headache that had disturbed me thro' the night — it bothered me more or less all day. About 10 o'clk. went down town with Mary \+ Kate — I went to photo. \+ got prints (Jacobi) — then home — mounted two prints \+ put them in press. Afterward I worked with crayon on one of the prints for a while in the afternoon. I was obliged to give up to my head ache. At 5.30 I went down-town. got a letter from Ralph — but none from Jim — Last evef. Mr. Wm. Mulford, who had been at the camp on Sat., told me that Jim had been sick, indigestion or bowel trouble — Ralph wrote that he was recovering from an indigestion — It worries me somewhat that he has not written.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 9, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still superb weather. I worked for some time on Jacobi portrait — on tracing etc. etc. Mrs. Kate Riley (Parker) and her daughter Emma came early to dinner — some time with me in studio after dinner — Then I wrote to Charles — to Geo. \+ Ed.d Cook — to Douglas Adams \+ to Jim. After 4 o'clk I went to P.O. \+ to photographers where I got a print of Jacobi from wrong side of negative — then back \+ cleared up printing Table \+ got press ready for work — The Riley's had been prevailed upon to stay to tea — after that Mary \+ the Girls escorted them to their lodgings —
