## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 27, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim started for Pomfret School.

Fine cold day \- bkft. before 9 o'clk \- some scurrying around to get Jim ready for his start \- Tom Cook came in to see him while we were at bkft. At 10.30 Jim, Ralph \+ I with Jennings carrying Jim's heavy bag went to G. C. Station \- There we met Mr. Plummer \+ Deny together with a Pomfret master \+ a lot of boys \- They all got off in good style at 11 o'clk \- Then I had a talk about boys with Mr. Plummer \- Then to Miller's \- The carpenter \- There an hour about hardware sent to Keene \- Home \+ Ralph \+ I had our lunch at 1.30 \- afternoon, wrote to Many \- went to Ryan's \- failed to see him \-
Jennings gave us a very good dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g \- Fred Ireland came \+ we talked of a school for Ralph \- \+ I consented to his calling upon Curtis to see if he had a place for him in his School \- after he left Joe came in \+ stayed until near 10 \- Then to bed

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 28, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cloudless day \- Ther. 60° to 65° in shade \+ strong westerly wind \- After 9 o'clk when we had finished bkft. \- an hour with Tribune \- then Ralph \+ I went out together \- to Dode's about new fire pot for furnace \+ to Ryan's \+ to Studio where I experimented a little on floor stains for Keene house \- Then to 2d. Nat'l. Bk \+ made a new loan for $500. Then sauntered about Dewey Arch \- up 5th Ave. \+ home \- Jennings served lunch at 1.30 \- To-day I have been so stiff in every limb that I get about with a good deal of discomfort \- it is very discouraging to me. I feel as though I w'd never be limber again. In the eve'g when at the studio I went to Maynard's for a few minutes \- after lunch lounged in front of fire for some time \- Then to Douglas' \+ \- to Miller's \- to Studio, to Dewey's book store \- 4th ave. below 20th St. then again to the Dewey Arch to mark progress \- up 5th Ave. \+ home about 6 o'clk. Ralph had been up town to see his school fellow, Gilbert Brome \- Jennings served our dinner at about 7. Eve'g loafing before fire.
