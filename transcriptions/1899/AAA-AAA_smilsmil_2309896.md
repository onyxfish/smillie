## Left Page

### Tues. May 16, 1899

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f, but cool - Rain between 2 & 3 o'clk - Then clear - Rain again at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After b'kf't went to office of Corporation Counsel Ward - waited for Mr. Lee until 10 o'c + was waited for W. Ward until after 10.30 - when she came we had an interesting talk about the N.A. \+ tax matters. He was communicative \+ frank - said that in the proceedings before Justice Nash he expected a decision in favor of the N.A. in which case he would appeal it. With reference to tax levied on old property for 98. he said there was no ground for that \+ he would have the matter settled before we passed title on June 1st - Then I went to a Hardware store in Chambers St. \+ met Miller the carpenter and spent an hour or more selecting hardware for Keene house. Then home to lunch. Wrote a note to Mr. Magbee at Keene - At 4.30 - as it had cleared pleasant, Ralph \+ I went out on our wheels \+ had a charming ride in the Park getting home at 6 - dinner at 7 - Evef I wrote a letter to Branch -

## Right Page

### Wed. May 17, 1899

**Weather:** Variable weather - cool - overcast - sunshine - show-ers.  **Temp:** [illegible]

uncle Willie \+ Aunt Agnes came from Po'K.

After b'kf't went to N.A. There for half an hour then to Etching class. Sterne, Stehr \+ Olinsky there \+ under Cloud posing in full regalia. The boys were working upon preparatory studies. I remained with them for awhile, then back to N.A. \+ worked there until 1 o'clk on checks to artists from sales of pictures from 44th St just closed - Home to lunch. Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. put down from Po'K - At 2 back to studio Stehr \+ Stern came with old plates \+ new \+ I worked with them until 5 o'clk - Then home \+ rested until dinner - Evef all hands went to Charl's - I left with Ralph at 10 \+ ret'd home.
