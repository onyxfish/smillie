## Left Page

### Thur. June 29, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A souper8 day after the heavy thunder storm at 1 A.m. mentioned on preceeding page - The m't was overcast for a time but cleared brilliant, beautiful + cool - About 10 I went with Jennings to 110 \+ then to studio - Douglas called to see me about paint + oil for the Keene house while I was at bkp. From 110 I went to studio + worked there until 12.30 when I got everything packed up + ready to go into my trunk - then I Ret'd. to 29 - had a short Rest + my lunch - At 3 Ret'd. to 710 + set about the packing of my trunk - It was very hard work - Yale came in while I was at it + spent some time chatting. It was 6.30 when, at last, I locked my trunk, thoroughly tired out, + went to 29 \+ lay upon my bed until dinner at 7.15 - Adams + his son Newton dined with us - It was near 10 when Mr. August Meyer came in - I went to bed soon after + left him, Charl. + Adams talking

Dr. Allen came in about 1 o'clk. + gave me a look over - he stripped me and carefully examined hip joint + chest. He says progress is satisfactory + he sees no trouble ahead.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 30, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightfully clear, fresh day - following another tiresome night - After bkp. I went to 110 to wait for Jackson's Exp - Jennings came to relieve me + I went for final visit to Studio - picked up last things there + Ret'd. to 110 - Upp. man not yet called for trunk - near 11 - Sent J- to see what was matter - after impatient waiting, Upp. came at 11.40 - Then I went to 29. + with Charl. started in Coupe at 12.30 on foot to Christopher St.- Met Adams there - my trunk did not arrive until the last moment, but we got away on time - Drawing Room car full + our seat on the sunny side - but we did not suffer much owing to the fresh breeze - We had a lunch served at about 2 - nothing of interest during the trip - I bore it very well - We got to Montrose at 7.35 + found all the family at the station - The boys + I rode home in a barouche - Charl, Adams + the family walked - Everything about the place looked very well - We had a good meal + then loafed + chatted until after 10 o'clk - when I got to bed tired out.
