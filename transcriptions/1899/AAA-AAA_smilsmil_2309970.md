## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 11, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A grey day, rather luminous in the m'f., growing heavier as the day advanced — very quiet + mild. After bkf. I had an interview with Hibbard + his mate with reference to laying stone wall. They promise to begin to-morrow + lay an extra good wall for $1.00 per Rod. I wrote some letters after bkf. + went down town about 11\. worked for some time with chisel + mallet on dead wood on an apple tree. Dinner about 1\. much of P.m. spent in attic sorting truck left by Jim + Ralph — Some of it put away — some to be taken to N.Y. Then I gathered in the studio some things to be packed + sent to Keene next Spring. Down to P.O. after 4. Saw H.f. Turrell + he agrees to let me have small gore flank at entrance to lower lot on The Road. [illegible] Tea at 6\. Eve'f, reading some old [illegible] that I found in a box in studio attic also, putting wicks into the lamps that have been cleaned to leave until next year.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 12, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 76°

Very warm — cloudless + misty — at 5 P.m. with all doors + windows open the Ther. in dining room stood at 73°. In my bedroom it reached 76°. wonderfully beautiful hazy moonlight.
Hibbard + Roberts got to work on stone wall in lot but before I had finished my bkf. a man came to beg for one of them for a few hours — I went with him to the lot + reluctantly gave consent. — The two were away all the afternoon — a commencement was made on H.f. Turrell's cor. on the Road. Taking the 16 × 24 × 32' as agreed. I cleared out the attic of East closet, studio — Rearranging boxes and putting away — hammock — chinese lanterns etc. etc — went down town at 11\. — back + sorted apples and put them in barrels until dinner at 1\. Continued work in studio closet until near 5 — Went to P.O. Read my newspaper until 6 — Then tea — Eve'f. fixing lamps — stringing wicks etc. to be ready for next season. In the attic + studio closet I found among a lot of "fossils" some of the rough drafts + letters written by me to Sarah Jessenden more than 40 years ago — there were some of Sarah's letters also — I Read a number of them with intense interest + lived over again the experiences of nearly half a century ago. This new reading with the judicial advantage of the many + varied years of life since they were written leaves her as much an enigma to me as she was then. I cannot decide whether one she was playing with me as a fisherman may play with a hooked fish. I was pleased to find in some of my letters to her so clear an expression of my feelings + the impulses that governed my action, or rather inaction — I was sick + oppressed by timed doubts of my ability to take my place in the struggle of life — I was torn by contending emotions by a pure love + admiration which only intensified the fears that filled me — She had all the feminine intuitions + I am glad now to be satisfied that she knew me through + through.
