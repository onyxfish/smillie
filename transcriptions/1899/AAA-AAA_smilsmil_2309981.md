## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 2, 1899

**Weather:** Brilliant cloudless \+ cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 \+ got to work trying to get some good pfs. from students etched plates but for some reason, inexplicable to me, I could not get satisfactorily bright, sound pfs. I worked until after 12 — then to Second Nat. Bank \+ drew money for Mary \+ the servants. Lunch at 1.30 — man came from Wanamaker's \+ I subscribed for a Century Dictionary — Got at my printing again about 3 \+ stuck to it as long as I could see — after 5 — getting some better results — but leaving me, on the whole, rather discouraged — a short walk \+ then home to dinner — Rea? from the Pomfret School Report for Oct — of Jim's rating — am much discouraged — the rating is A.B.C. etc — A. standing for very good. He does not get any thing above B\+ \+ from the down to C. \+ D — in "Sacred Study" (?) he is marked "failure." What shall I do with the boy? Eve'g I gave some time to Ralph who is uncomplaining but is discouragingly slow in his studies — Charl. \+ Geo. \+ Nellie came in, but being very tired went to bed at 9.30.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 3, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunshine for a little while in the m'f, then rain began \+ it fell heavily at times thro' the day — I got to studio soon after 9 and to work printing — I have not got "the hang" of it yet; it is so long since I have done any serious work in the way of printing. In the course of the m'f, however I improved \+ at last "caught on" — home to lunch before 2 \+ back to studio before 3 — my printing rags were now getting well primed \+ "fat" \+ I had got a good combination of blacks (hard \+ soft) \+ oils (medium \+ strong), so I took pfs. from the two large competition plates, "Thunder Cloud" (Indian) by Sterne \+ Stahr \+ got satisfactory pfs. I cleaned up at 5 \+ rested, being very tired, until 6.30 — then home to dinner — Kate dined with Charl — Eve'g I had a short session of reading to Ralph \+ got to bed early.
