## Left Page

### Wed. July 19, 1899

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \+ chilly m'f. but later the day was superb - breezy \+ brilliant - I wrote to Charl, to Adams, the painter at Keene, \+ to Beede. I also spent some time marking a lot of Ralph's clothing, in preparation for his start for camp Choconut to-morrow. I kept about on my feet a good deal altho' I felt lame \+ sore. Evening I read a little to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 20, 1899

Took Ralph to Camp Choconut.

**Weather:** Showers  **Temp:** [illegible]

Showers in early m'f. - chilly, overcast m'f. with wind from the east - burned away before noon - Afternoon two thunder showers passed near, giving us a light sprinkling - most of the m'f. was spent getting Ralph's things together \+ packing his trunk - Dinner at 12.30 - at 1.30 we started for camp Choconut Rings's team - many, the two girls, Ralph \+ I - man driving - we had a comfortable drive - in 2 h. 10 m. Found Jim much out of sorts, headache \+ nausea. - Left him curled up in his bed. My first visit to the camp - The im'p'm was the half nakedness of the campers, wearing only sleeveless jerseys - running trousers, long stockings \+ sneakers. I am afraid that my boys can't stand it - Jim seemed pleased but gave no expression. Saw Mrs Munford - her daughter, Mrs Winlock, a friend, Miss Childs of Phila., a trained nurse who is to have an eye to the boy campers. Showers threatened, but that was all - we started on our return trip at 5.20 It was a trial to me to part from Ralph for whom my yearnings are peculiarly tender - He invites closer relations as Jim does not - A cool drive home, getting in at 7.40 - Tea was awaiting us - To bed at 10 - glorious moonlight night.
