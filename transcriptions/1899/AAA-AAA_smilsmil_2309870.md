## Left Page

### Thur. March 23, 1899

**Weather:** Rain in the mt'f - clearing in the afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the mt'f - clearing in the afternoon - In studio at 10 \+ worked all the mt'f with crayon upon proof of "Goldsmith's Daughter" taken yesterday, finding much more to do than I expected - I have toned down all of the light part of the flesh giving sense of color \+ modeling - also quieting parts of sky - I think that I can improve the place greatly - Lunch at 1 - back to studio - worked on pf. of Genl. Greenon comparing Voigt's pf. with the one that I took I find that mine is much the best pf. - I spent some time in getting out proofs of other plates \+ help and for a campaign of plate work - at 5 went to NA - Home after 6 - dinner every with Ralph \+ to bed early -

Mr. Mason spent 1½ hours with Jim I am not at all satisfied with the result so far \+ must make a change. I cannot decide where the trouble lies. Jim does not like him - says all of the time is given to unsuccessful attempts on the part of M - to work out problems in algebra - no time to Latin or Greek -

## Right Page

### Fri. March 24, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Emma Bourne came from Po'k.

Fine day - Spent the m'tf at studio working on old etching plates \+ getting my hand in so as to begin on a piece of etching. Home at 1 - Emma Bourne there - came down to visit for a couple of days - At 2 aut to Central Trust Co. Wall St. met Mr. Hyde by appointment \+ talked with him for nearly an hour about what is the best policy for A.P.D. in caring for its invested funds - As "meas" - I wish to have them in as safe a shape as possible with the least trouble on the "meas" - Then went to Levi P. Morton's \+ had a short talk with him - Got to studio at about 4 - then to Etching class - Stahr - Stone \+ Kelley There - they had got through drawing from model \+ Stahr was biting his etching - then to Am. Art Ass'n to see the first annual exhibit of Am. Landscapists - a lot of paintings - mostly in groups, by about a dozen painters - some very good pictures - met Kost \+ Wiggins there - at the door, met Chas. Miller who enquired when his duties as director were to begin - Then to NA - then to Studio - t home at 6.20 - Mr. Thos. Adams to dinner - took the girls to the opera again - Charl came at 9 o'clk - \+ took Emma \+ Kate to his house -
