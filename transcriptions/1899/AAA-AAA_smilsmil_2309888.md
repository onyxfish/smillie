## Left Page

### Sun. April 30, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day - after an unrest-
ful night I got up at 11 - about noon
I went out for a short walk \+ found
myself uncomfortably weak. - Dinner
at 2 — at 4 I went to N.A. by
appointment \+ met Beckwith, Brown
\+ Nicole \+ we talked over the Jennings
Trust Deed \+ some other matters until
near 6 — Then I sauntered home
very carefully — M. Tho. Adams
took tea with us at 6.30 — Every
I wrote a letter to Jim \+ Ralph took
it to G.C. Station to mail — Then
to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 80°

A very uncomfortably warm day —
After [illegible] went to Itching studio - met
there Sterne, Stahr \+ Olinsky \+ a young girl
whom I understand is a sister of Olinsky's
she is a student in the N.A. school - ties
to pose for the Itching class who are to-day
to begin their competition for the Baldwin
Prize. From there to 2? Nat. Bank - Then to N.A.
home at noon. I had a rest before lunch. —
After it I commenced writing the Report concerning
the de Grand Benedict advertising business - I found
it troublesome to arrange \+ I stuck to it very closely
until 4.30 when I finished it. Then at about 5 - I
lay down upon my bed \+ rested until 6.30 - dinner -
at 8 I went to Council - not a full meeting - Wood -
Hart - Beckwith - Dielman - Church - Weldon - Bro-
geo \+ Self - Things ran along, ordinarily - until we
came to General Business - I took first opportunity
to present my Benedict Report - The result was very
surprising to all parties - Jas. Hart insisted that it was
an attack upon him \+ finally lost his head completely
abused me in the coarsest - most insulting \+ vehement
manner — It was a pitiful exhibition - His florid face
was very pale - his voice was pitched high - almost
tearful \+ he shook all over — He failed to intimidate me -
I was too easy perhaps to get mad at him - \+ that only
made him madder - he said I was a passionless oyster
that I deliberately planned to make his life miserable
was always ready to knife a man from under the table -
He went on in this way until Dielman called Wood's attention
to the improprieties of permitting it to go on without rebuke
This outburst expressed to me many of his peculiar
actions towards me during the past two years - He has
made a Bogey of me \+ with distorting vision has made
every word or work of mine mean studied insult
antagonism to him — He hates me with a hatred
that has been growing, unknown to me, for a long time -
I am really sorry for him - There was a disagreeable
use to me about having the sympathy of the other mem-
bers - from Wood \+ Weldon I would not expect it - but
