## Left Page

### Mon. March 13, 1899

**Weather:** Dull day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day — spent the mf at studio finishing the preparation of some Asphaltum varnish \+ getting my etching department into order for a campaign — about 12.30 went to N.A. — saw my Water Color \+ ordered it sent to my studio. It needs some more work very much \+ as it has been asked for by The Phila. Art Club \+ the Chicago Art Ins. I wish to put the needed work on it before it starts on its journey. Home to lunch. — to studio — at 4 o'clk — went to Mr. Davis on Grammercy Park — a Reading by Hopkinson Smith for benefit of Dick Kitchen — much amused, of course — out at about 5.30 — to studio — home to dinner. Ev'g Council — N.A. — It was 11.30 before I got home —

Mr. Mayor with Jim.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 14, 1899

**Weather:** Pleasant — clouded m'f — clear afternoon much cooler  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant — clouded m'f — clear afternoon much cooler. Worked all the m'f on my Water Color "On the Susquehanna". and I think improved it very much — Went to N.A. at 12.30 — Then to lunch — Then to studio for a short time \+ to Etching Class at about 3 o'clk — on my way there met Thos. Fogarty, a member of my Etching Cl. at the league some years ago — says he is still much interested in Etching but he finds no time to work at it. Stahr, Stone, Dlinsky \+ Prince there biting their plates — making some funny work of it — with them until 5 o'clk — then to studio — there until 6 — then home — Mary \+ the girls in great excitement because Kate had gone out about 10 A.m. \+ had not yet returned — She came in at 6.20 having been off having a good time with the De Angeli's. After dinner I went to Charles' — there until 10 o'clk, most of the time discussing the Kansas Smelting Co. deal that is now under way — There is a gigantic consolidation of Smelting interests of the country going on and this advice is that I hold on to my stock \+ go with the swing — Home at 10 \+ to bed.
