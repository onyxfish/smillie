## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 24, 1899

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull mt., rain commencing to fall at about noon, increasing until night when it came down heavily — I worked all day on plans for Keene house getting all done that is necessary (for the house proper — but not for the annex) before putting it into the hands of the architect's draughtsman — Home to lunch + back + worked until a little after 4, when I could see no longer. Rested until near 6 — then home. Dinner at 6.30 + a quiet ever. I read awhile to Raelph.

A heavy thunderstorm at 11.30 p.m.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 25, 1899

**Weather:** Clear, cool + breezy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cool + breezy. Got to studio at about 10 + spent the mt. puzzling over plan for annex to Keene house — a separate building for dining room + kitchen with bed rooms for servants, laundry, bath rooms etc. Home to lunch at 1.15 — Soon after 2 went to Carriere + Hastings — saw Mr. C — + left with him the rough plans that I had drawn for the Keene house. He has promised to let one of his boys draw the plans in ship shape manner for me. Then to studio + worked on plans until after 4 — then home + dressed — went to Afternoon tea at Dr. Janeway's — 36 W. 40 St. — Mr. + Mrs. J — \+ Mr. Tho. J — there for 20 m. or so, + then to Ed Mr. Cook's, where Catharine has the last of her series of teas — all of the family, + more too, there. Then home to dinner — Mr. Chas. H. Edwards called late this p.m. to see me, with card of introduction from Walter — Mary + Jim saw him + told him that another candidate (Mr. Mayon) had already seen me — whereupon Mr. Edwards slofted — about 7.30 Mr. Mayon came + I told him frankly that Fred Ireland + Walter Cook had been working at cross purposes + that I would have to see them before I could go any further — I then went to Walter's but he was out.
