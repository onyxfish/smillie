## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 14, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary's "Tea"

Early m[orning] overcast by thin filmy but later it cleared to be a wonderfully fine day — quiet, very cool. Ruam + his helper commenced work this morning on putting water closet into my bath room. Had bkpt at 7.30 because of the entertainment that is to come off this P. M. from 4 to 6 — Mary has sent out cards to bet. 50 + 60 towns people + summer boarders for an "afternoon Tea". I got to work early continuing the biting of Jacobi. The Mordant (Dutch) that I am using was mixed in the summer of 1896 — it bites a beautiful line but I think it works very slow — I was very deliberate + did not get through until after 4 o'clk. I was gratified by the result when I cleaned the plate — Then I went to my room — changed my dress + came to the company who were scattered every where. I saw a number to speak to — notably Mr. Winlock (Nuelford) from whom I learned much of Jim + Ralph at Camps — There was quite a delegation from Camp Choconut. An informal tea at 7 in the Cherry Room miss Riley with us — Every I went to P.O \+ got letter from Jim — their news paper.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 15, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another wonderfully beautiful day — air cool but very warm in the sunshine. I built a fine in studio but my time there was mostly given to writing to Mrs Winlock — Walter + Jennings — about 11 I went to [illegible]. met Edgar Turrell + Dave Supre + we inspected the bldg of the new Village Hare, now in progress. I objected to sills under hearths in front of fire places + orders were given to cut them down — I worked with graver on Jacobi plate. I chose try to finish the plate with as little us. of it as possible.

Glorious sunset + moonlight night. I had a long walk on piazza, of which I never tire.
