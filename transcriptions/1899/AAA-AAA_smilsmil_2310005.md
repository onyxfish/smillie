## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 24, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Tremendously heavy rain all the m[orning] with wind from the east, which shifted, \+ in the afternoon blew strongly from the west \+ cleared a into cold. I got up at 7 and had my scrub \+ rub down - then to bed \+ got up to bkf't at 9 - I was bothered all day with a headache that increased as the day wore on. Jim helped me for some time in the work of preparing a catalogue of all the books in the house. Dinner at 2 - late in the afternoon I walked up to Central Park \+ 5th Ave. then back \+ went to Diions[?] in 41st S[t]. Spent half an hour or more with him - a sad time. My headache became so violent that I had to go home \+ to bed. I got up about 10 o'clk., having had a little sleep, \+ went to bed again at midnight.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 25, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold \+ windy - Got up late after a headachy night; my enemy still clinging to me - much discomforted all day. Ralph helped me for awhile with my cataloguing work after the distribution of xmas presents after bkf't which took an hour or more. At noon I went to Ed[ward] Cook's \+ gave to him a couple of books, found among mine, that his father had given to him many years ago, \+ to Mary, a pitcher bearing the name of Thomas Cooke that has been in the Cook family many years. Our dinner came off at 2.30 - all of our family - Geo. \+ his family \+ Chas - 13 in all. We sat at table until 5. a very pretty table \+ a very good dinner - all were quiet out seemed to enjoy it. The table was decorated by a pair of silver candelabra given to Mary by Charl. The breakfast table was decorated by a new set of dishes presented to me by the family from Ralph. I passed a stupid eve'g owing to my persistent headache \+ got to bed early.
