## Left Page

### Fri. March 31, 1899

**Weather:** Pleasant & quite mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant & quite mild, To studio at 9.30 & wrote until near 12 - then to Barbe's & had my hair cut - lunch at 1. Afternoon to Etching class - Stuhr, Stone, Kelly & Perrine There - drawing from model - old woman at prayer - I was with them until near 5 - then to Newman's in W. 29° St. then took elevated R.R. to 104° S°, then walked up to N.A. site & looked at school bldg now in progress - then home by way of Electric Trolley - two transfers - 35 minutes Dinner - Early to bed early - with headache that has been bothering me all day.

Private view at N.A. fD.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 1, 1899

**Weather:** Partly cloudy - windy & colder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Partly cloudy - windy & colder than yesterday - another m'f given to writing most of it in going over accts for some years back of the expense of Dozier house in Montrose & money paid to W\. S. Peuse since he has been in my employ. At noon went to N.A. & attended to business there - Home to lunch - Emma Ret° to Po'k in the afternoon - I went to studio \+ wrote until after 4 - Then out & walked about & after 5 home & had a rest until dinner - Emma Ireland has given the boys a theatre party treat - Jim & Ralph to select a companion to meet her & others at the Murray Hill Theatre to see "Our boys" - Jim invited Ned Thurston & Ralph invited Gilbert Brown, son of mines - They dined with us - Eve'g I went out to find Ghirardi, Davis & Mast Eloberg - Assembly man & Senator to see if I could hasten the N.A. bill at Albany. Could not find either of them - went to Beckwith & spent an hour with him. Then to Century - met Mr. Maghee - of S. Hubert's & talked Adirondack house I also met Fred Ireland & he said very positively that I was making a great mistake if I did not take Jim away from Morse's school immediately - He did not give me satisfactory reasons or explanation \+ without them I don't think I shall follow his advice -
