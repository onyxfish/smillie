## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 13, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A blizzard. Snow falling steadily all day — high wind — Ther. below 10°. Nobody went out — no school for Jim on acct. of Holiday (Lincoln's birth-day) I would not let Ralph go out because his cheeks were so badly frost-bitten on Sat. They are very red \+ much swollen. I stayed in all day \+ devoted my self to writing up Estate Receipt \+ Expenditure accts that have been badly neglected. Charl came in for awhile in the afternoon — later M'. Thos. Adams came in \+ insisted that it was a pleasant day \+ that the Throop girls should not waste tickets to the Opera, bought by them — His contention resulted in his dining with us \+ escorting Katherin \+ Bessie to the Opera in the eve'g. a quiet eve'g at home — did not go to Cornicie meeting.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 14, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright day — weather much moderated but still very cold. After bkfst. I worked for some time on my accts, then to studio for awhile. Had a little exercise on the sunny side of Park Ave. before lunch — after lunch I went to Canvera \+ Hastings \+ spent half an hour with M'. Ford \+ the young man who is working on the drawings for Heene House — then walked down 5th Ave. stopping at Boussod, Valedon \+ Co. \+ saw the Bridgemans — a pitiful decadence from his earlier work — then in to Tooth \+ Co's \+ saw the Rangers — one very fine picture — the top's Becky Cole's Hill — worth all of the others together — Then to Gas Co. \+ ordered two gas heaters for the boys bed rooms — there to N.A. \+ looked at the Water Color Exbn. — Did not find much to please me there — my own picture lacks simplicity \+ force to a very great degree. — Then to Studio \+ spent some time in the cellar, nursing the neglected fire into life — Home after 6 — dinner. Eve'g before the fire.

"Becky Cole's Hill".
