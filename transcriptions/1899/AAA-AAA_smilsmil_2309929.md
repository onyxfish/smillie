## Left Page

### Fri. July 21, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day with a strong breeze. glorious moonlight night. very quiet.
In the m'f I wrote notes to Roland Mulford - to Jim + to Ralph - After that I made a circuit of my farm - the first time that I have been able to do it - every where I find the want of intelligent care. Every thing - house, building + grounds cry out for my attention + money.
Dinner - I packed the Tennis Racquets \+ balls to send to the boys at camp -
Ev'f. so nice + the moonlight so beautiful that we all sat on the piazzas until after 9 - some, until after 10 — then to bed. -

At 1.15 am, Bessie had me get to look after a man who was trying to saw his way into one of her east windows. I went + found that a mouse in the wall, was making considerable noise gnawing at the wood work - [illegible — ~5 lines]

## Right Page

### Sat. July 22, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very warm day - cooler in the ev'f. About 11 o'clk. went down Town. Sent Tennis Racquets to the boys a Camp by Friendsville Stage driver - had hair cut. afternoon wrote part of a letter to Gz°- Five weeks to-day since accident -
have had a very comfortable day so far as pain in left leg + side is concerned.
Ev'f. after 9 with news paper. [illegible — ~20 lines]
