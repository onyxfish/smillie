## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 10, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Day opened with warm showers - then muggy, misty weather - thunder all about during the afternoon - the edge of a shower passing over us at about 6. I got started on the drawing in of the lines on the copper of Jacobi portrait this mth. found my hand much out of practice - I a want of confidence generally that made the work laborious \+ unsatisfactory - The transfer from gelatine tracing was not altogether successful as the ground was broken in places. After dinner I felt so heavy that I could not work. So, I took a nap. Cwrote a letter to Charl \+ took it to P.O. at 5 \-
Eve'g — as usual.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 11, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

muggy, sticky \+ smoky - easterly breeze all day - After bkft. I went to Village Hall - saw Ed. Jones (darkie) \+ — Welch - the transfer and made bargain with them to have all the burnt stuff - wood cinders \+ ashes - To my lot, entering from Lower Road - fill a depression there - One load was hauled - then "had some words" with Welch \+ he ceased work - I returned to studio \+ worked on Jacobi plate - I have drawn all in \+ am much disappointed - it is not at all what I hoped to do - After dinner I looked it over carefully \+ stopped at different parts. Rec'd. a note from O. Beede stating that he had not yet got the hardware for the new house that I ordered thro' Miller two months ago. Wrote to Miller, Beede, E.M. Cook \+ Jenning's \+ at 5 took letters to P.O. Eve'g. again went to P.O. at 8 o'clk. Eve'g. wrote a letter to Ralph.
