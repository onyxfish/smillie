## Left Page

### Sat. March 11, 1899

**Weather:** A dull, unpleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, unpleasant day — After bkft. went to studio + from there to Lea + Lee's, 20 Nassau S. Getting there in time for appointment at 10.30 — with W.H.L.L — until after 1, consulting about The new Trouble with Tax assessors. I signed an elaborate affadavit drawn up by W.L. to meet the case — also one for the answer to Baumann answer to suit for rent. I was very much wearied when I left — Home to lunch at about 2 — + after that Orlando Beede came + I spent half an hour or more with him — Showed him plans for Keene house — he came to ask me to write to Neilson to ask permission to have some of the building materials hauled over his place before the snow melts — Then I had a short rest + went to 41 west 10. S. + saw Dieleman to urge progress by Building Com. — Then to Nicoll's — 57 W. 10. S. W. + M. Com. meeting — There until 6 — walked up home to dinner — Eve. at 8 went to Century where Prof. Geo. F. Barker delivered an intensely interesting address, with experiments, on Liquid Air. It was in the picture gallery which was too small to accommodate all of the members + their friends who came. Prof. B. had a can with about 10 galls. of the Liquid air made by Chas. Tripler — he threw it about as though it were water + showed its marvellous effects in many ways — It seems to me that as a new developer of energy it will prove a greater and more beneficent agent of Revelation + progress than Steam — after taking a Kettle of it boil on ice + the same Kettle, on a fire, freeze a glass of water Champney said he thought Hell quite as possible at the North Pole as at the

## Right Page

### Sun. March 12, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An April-like day — sunshiny + showery and very mild — a heavy shower about the middle of the day + a Thunder shower bet. 7 + 8 P. m. — Bkpt. at usual time — wrote letter to Branch + one to W.G. Neilson asking permission for Branch or Beede to have building material over part of his ground at Keene while there is snow — also, sent to Nielson, as Pres. of Adirondack Reserve a formal application for water service. Went to studio + put some Asphaltum in not cutting to make varnish for Etching Class — Then took a little walk — Dinner at 2 — some more printing — Then to studio for a few minutes Then to Am. Art. Ass'n. To see the collection of de Montval's remarkable paintings — met Rockwood de Forest there + had a talk with him about succeeding me as Treas. U.A. ft. Caught in a shower on my way home. Tea at 6.30 — Eve. with Ralph — Jim went with Bessie to church.

Equator.
