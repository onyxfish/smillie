## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 3, 1899

**Weather:** misty, muggy, cloudy m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

misty, muggy, cloudy m'f \- some hot sunshine about the middle of the day. Strong breeze during the afternoon \- Thunder all about \- threatenings \+ a little dash of rain about 7 P.M. \- High wind at night. Up at 7 a.m. \+ had a good rub, scrub \+ bath \- During the morning I did some pruning of fruit trees \- wrote several notes to different people \+ a letter to S.R. Korhler \- dinner at 2 \- loafed in big chair on piazza much of the afternoon \- Went to P.O. at 6.30 when thunderstorms were most threatening \- Evely again on piazza in big chair \- Then read for an hour.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 4, 1899

**Weather:** Very cool, clear day  **Temp:** [illegible]

K. \+ B. gave a dance.

Very cool, clear day \- Pruned fruit trees \+ attended to other things about the place \- worked for a little while on plate for aqua-tint dry-ground box frame. About noon Ralph \+ I went down town to P.O. etc \- Afternoon I helped to arrange piazzas for the dance that Katharine \+ Bessie are to have in the evely \- with the painters who are at work on the So. gable. at 5 o'clk went to P.O. Evely. the House looked very pretty \- cleared for dancing \- piazzas lighted by chinese lanterns \- bamboo shades down \- chairs, rugs etc. The air was very cool \+ I kindled a fire on the hearth \- 20 young people came \- Miss Perego was engaged to play for them \+ they all seemed to have a very good time at 10.30 messrs Edgar, Comstock \+ m left to drive over to New Milford to take midnight exp. to N.Y. \- That broke up the party \+ at 11 all were gone.
