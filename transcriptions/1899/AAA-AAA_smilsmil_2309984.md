## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 8, 1899

**Weather:** A very mild day, partially overcast.  **Temp:**

Spent the m'f at studio working on some copper \+ zinc etched plates cleaning them up — went to 2. Nat Bk, then home to lunch — To studio for an hour — more picturing over of Chinese India, preparatory to printing — at 4 o'clk. home \+ rested for an hour or more — then dressed \+ at 7 went to the Architectural league in 57th St. The Regular Monthly meeting \+ dinner — The invitation stated that there would be a discussion after dinner on "The merits \& demerits of united Art Exhibitions in N.Y. City." I went very much in the dark as to the character of the meeting \+ the discussion — where I found that I was one of a few guests of honor seated at the Pres't Table facing the other tables I began to have fears — Chat. Stewart Smith — J. Hopkinson Sm. J. Carroll. Beckwith \+ others were guests \+ after the dinner there was a discussion opened by Vice Pres't Lamb followed by speakers called upon by the Pres't Gibson — The proposition was:— would it be wise \+ practicable to have a great Salon System of art works of all descriptions by all art organizations \+ others working together — Beckwith followed Lamb, approving — F.H. Swift followed with a very characteristic address \+ took the cake — C.S. Smith followed — all taking the affirmative — there were several shorter affronts interpolated — Then the Rev. Mr. Chamberlain made a very florid address \+ after it, altho' I had told the Pres't that I could not speak, he called upon me — as a result, I had to make a very lame excuse \+ beg off — it was very mortifying \+ irritating to me — I walked down with S.S. Smith to his home in 47th St. \+ then home, meeting Mr. Wurdee in 5th Ave. I got in at 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 9, 1899

**Weather:** mild \+ pleasant — sunshine \+ cloud.  **Temp:**

I got to studio at 9.30 — not long after that Emily Cook came in \+ made quite a long call — I fixed bevels on some plates \+ otherwise prepared for another printing bout — wet down a lot of paper, etc. etc. It was nearly 2 o'clk. when I got home to lunch — I had planned to take a bicycle ride with Ralph in the afternoon but feeling very much out of sorts I went to my bed \+ had a nap. After that R. \+ I took our wheels to Borrmann to have the valves fixed. Dinner at 6.30 — After my news paper I read awhile to R — After I went to my room I got hold of some old letters which kept me up until nearly midnight.
