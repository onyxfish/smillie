## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 7, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day, brilliant - almost cloudless - crisp, cool \+ breezy - \+ wonderful in color. After bkft. finished taking down bamboo shades - Then to Herrick who is at work in shop finishing aqua-tint ground box - then for a time about with pruning shears - Then wrote notes to Callanan Bro. \+ to Fredt. Marvel. To P.O. after 11 - then more work pruning - Dinner at 1 - after that Herrick completed the cutting out of top of Ash Tree \+ I heated Tar \+ went about among the fruit trees applying it wherever there were wounds - To P.O. about 4 - Picked apples until near 6. Tea at 6.30 - Evef. spent some time getting wicks into large double lamp. Read newspaper \+ did a little writing.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 8, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some sunshine during the mf. leaden grey clouds overcast the sky in the afternoon - wind from east \+ night with a little sprinkle about 7 p.m. Bkb. between 9 \+ 10 - from that time until 3. I was out of doors much of the time - made Folkshire measurements of fence bet. my land \+ My Turreels where I propose to have a wall built - also of gore that I would like to have at entrance from lower road. I did some pruning \+ cutting away of diseased parts of trees - At 3 had dinner - after that wrote a long letter to Jim — a shorter one to Ralph - after 7 Mary \+ I walked down to P.O. with our letters - After 8 we had a light refection - newspaper - etc - etc until aferg.
