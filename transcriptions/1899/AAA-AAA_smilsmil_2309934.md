## Left Page

### Mon. July 31, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day — brilliant, breezy & cool — After breakfast I got to work on portrait of Dr. Jacobi + stuck to it until near noon — then I went down town to P. O. + home soon after 12. Painters put a coat of color on the decking on east + north sides of house. After dinner I went down to the lot where Speuse was cradling oats to see how he was getting on — Then to my work + kept at it until 4.30 There is a subtle something that I can not get in the Jacoby portrait — my lines are correct but the result is not satisfactory — then I went to my berry patch + picked black berries — Then tea time — Early eve'g. I walked on piazza for some time — then read until near 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast until 9 o'clk — then cleared — cloud-less & warm — I was routed out early by movement of bowels + again later — discomfort continued thro' most of the day. Worked part of the day on Jacobi portrait — I wrote to Charl & to Jennings. + did some loafing in hammock on piazza — Went down town just before 6 — got letters from Jim & Ralph at camps — Opening & Reception "Country Club" in the little red Sayre house near Jones' Pond. Events as usual — news paper etc.
