## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 16, 1899

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate came from Po'k.

Pleasant day \- Got a letter from nobody inparticular, when I come to think, but having to go to Meyrovitz's for my new glasses it was 10.30 before I got to studio. I got to work on head of M.W.G. \+ all this I suffered from headache all day. I put in a full days work changing considerably the drawing \+ the face by extending the limits \+ shading \- home to lunch \- at 5 o'clk. having cleaned up \+ bring very uncomfortable owing to my headache I sat in my big chair \+ dozed until nearly 6.30 — then home to dinner \-
Every I had intended to go to the Lotos Club, Geo. having given me a card \- but the conditions of my head prevented \+ I loafed in front of a fire at home. An interesting eclipse of the moon well seen from parlor bay windows.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 17, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day \- damp \+ raw with a sense of snow in the air \- occasional sprinkles \- walks wet all day. Before [illegible] got up \+ had my scrub \- then back to bed \+ had an hour there before I got up. \+ kept at 9.15 wrote notes to Art Editors \+ Post \+ Tribune asking them to visit N.A. \+ School to see p[illegible] of etched work by my pupils \-
Geo. Cook came to see me about keeping his books that have been among Dunnie's books for years past — he is now in very straitened circumstances. After 4 I walked up 3rd Ave. to 50th st to look at a building offered to N.A. as security for a loan of $27000. Then to Dr. Yates \+ was there until after 6 \- Then home \- tea \- eve'g wrote a short letter to Jim \- Bro. Geo. came in for a while \-
