## Left Page

### Tues. May 30, 1899

**Weather:** Cool, bright  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool, bright mt after the shower last night but it rapidly grew warmer + in the afternoon it was hot, hot, hot. To studio at 9.30 and commenced the transcription into the bound Cash Book of N.A. accts. for the fiscal year ending to-morrow as I have arranged there. It will be a record of the entire financial transactions of the N.A. for the year past, but not in regular ledger form. Sterne came + was at the studio nearly all day biting his plate. He showed a good deal of intelligence in his management of the acid — nevertheless he got some bad fore biting on the face which will give me some work to remove it. Home to lunch at 1 — after that feeling the shortness of my hours last night. I was sleepy + I indulged in a short nap. Then to studio — Spencer Coon came + wanted to interview me but I put him off until to-morrow mtf. Home at 4.30 + at about 5 I went out on my wheel — While loafing on the Casino terrace I met Rob't. Underwood Johnston — also out with his bicycle — + I had a pleasant chat with him. The park was wonderfully beautiful + the immense throngs were a sight to see. I did not get home until 6.30. Had my dinner at 7.30 — Every wrote a note to Katie Elkin — mailed it at g. central station — + then to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 31, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot, muggy day. After bkft. wrote a note to Mr. W.B. Cutting in answer to his request that the term of Ralph's visit be extended to next Monday. To studio at 9.25. Coon met me at the door — Nit interviewed me on N.A. financial prospects until 10.30. At 11.15 I was in Wall St. Went to Central Trust Co. — Saw Mr. Hyde + made preliminary arrangements for the introduction of my successor — then to office of Morton, Bliss + Co. — + there met Beckwith + McCall — Got ex Gov. Morton's signature to subscription form to be attached to Trust Deed — then to Jacob Schiff's office — Saw him + got his signature — then to W.E. Dodge's — Saw him + got his signature — then to Mr. Jenning's office — Saw him + gave him the form with signatures — then we went up town on Elevated R.R. + I got home to lunch at 1.30 e.a. Afternoon I worked on copying N.A. accts. into bound Vol. About 5 o'clk. I went off on my bicycle + got home at about 6.30 — It was 7.30 before I ate my dinner. Old Kate the Cook, got on her high horse + said that I'd have to keep better hours or she'd leave. Went every + to bed early.
