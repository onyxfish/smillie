## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 18, 1899

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast day — To studio immediately after 4½. Ed Cook came \+ talked over some matters of business, relative to Cleveland dock property \+ Williams-burgh dock property — I spent the morning in a general clearing up — just before 12 Russell, from Budworth's, came \+ took the framed etchings pf. those that I had taken from Student's plates, to N.A. pf. — I went home at 12.30 \+ rested an hour before lunch — at 2.15 I started for N.A. \+ was nearly an hour getting there. Maurice Stern was there \+ he spent the remainder of the P.M. helping me arrange \+ hang pf. — Under my direction Davis \+ a halper hung the framed pf. — Stern \+ I got the sale \+ Baker pf. on the wall \+ I pasted the thin paper pf. on the pf. making a very interesting epase I think — Stern was evidently much pleased by the appearance of pf. from his plates — I got home before 6. \+ home before 6.30 — dinner — took a copy of Annie's will to Ed. Cook as requested, for use in the Cleveland dock transaction, as requested. Then home \+ read to Ralph from Stevenson's "Kidnapped."

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 19, 1899

**Weather:** Very heavy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy showers in the early mg., none after 10 am. but clouded \+ damp all day — Pain \+ discomfort all day — intercostal rheumatism in front of me \+ lumbago back of me \+ general misery inside of me \+ a headache in addition. I feel old \+ disconsolate — most of the mg. spent in assorting the contents of my closet that have been piled in one corner of my room — periodicals \+ books — London Punch, Harper, Century, St. Nicholas, Round Table \+ others for years back — arranging them in files to wrap up accessory \+ feet with other files ready for binder — Dinner at 2 — from 3.30 to 4.30 I rested on my bed — then I went to Beckwith's studio by appointment to meet Ways \+ Means Com. Only J. G. Brown came — we considered the replies rec'd from members of N.A. to the Tait plan of a public sale of pictures contributed by the artists for benefit of building fund, as not one fifth of them had responded it was thought best to drop it. Home to tea at 6.30 — then had a long stretch of reading to Ralph from "Kidnapped."
