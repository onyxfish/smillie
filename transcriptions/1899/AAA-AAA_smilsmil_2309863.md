## Left Page

### Thur. March 9, 1899

**Weather:** Disagreeable  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day - Raw + overcast - To studio at usual time - worked at various things until 11.30 - Then went to Carrere \+ Hastings + for an hour was with them both having the plans of the new Academy bldg before us, + discussing some suggestions of mine with reference to the lay out of the ground floor. I was much encouraged by the very intelligent + helpful suggestions from both of them - Home to lunch - then to studio + spent the rest of the afternoon preparing my etching room for work - After 5 o'clk went to Eimer + Amend's + got herbs \+ Sal Ammonia - Home to dinner Early - Read awhile to Ralph - Mr. Mayor spent 2½ hours with Jim

## Right Page

### Fri. March 10, 1899

**Weather:** Very fine  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day. At studio I wrote a letter to Franz Branch at Keene, N.Y., reciting my version of our interview at St. Hubert's last Sep. when we reached a verbal agreement with reference to the building of my house at Keene Heights - My object in writing this letter to-day is, that if he assents in writing then we will have an agreement in black \+ white. At 12 I went to 4 W. 22d S. + met Stahr + Perine - S. was doing what he could in the way of laying grounds for the other members of the class that is to meet this afternoon - I went home to lunch at 1 - about 2 messenger from Snedea came with the surprising news that she \+ had just been learned that the assessors of last year + this had assessed the old site, that has been exempt for more than 30 years, for taxes - last year at a valuation of $275,00 dollars - This year $325,000 - making a bill of taxes of more than $11,00 - went to studio - pictures from Montauk Club Ret? Then to Etching class - Stahr, Stern, Perine, Slinsky + Kelly there - I was with them until about 4.30 then to N.A. + had a look at the Water Color Show which I found very interesting - There to studio - home at 6 - dinner - Eve. newspaper - reading to Ralph + completing the correction \+ balancing of my 2d Nat. Bk. acct.
