## Left Page

### Tues. June 27, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day altho' warm. I suppose that my condition is improving from day to day but the gain is so slow as to be really discouraging. About 10 o'clk. I went with Jennings to 110 \+ worked there for some time getting things together \+ down to the dining room. Back to 29 before 1 \- \+ had lunch at 1.30 It was about 3 that Jennings went with me to studio \+ there I worked for an hour \+ a half. Then to 29 very tired. I went to Owen's \+ to Randall's \+ on my way past 10 met Mr. \+ Mrs. Martin, our neighbors of 112. He explained to me that he's going to have a new front put on the house. I had a nap before dinner. Chas. Collins came in \+ we had a chatty time until 10.30. It was 11.30 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 28, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast most of the day. Thunderstorm about middle of afternoon. other showers in the eve'g. heavy thunderstorm at 1 A.M. showers continuing until about 5 A.M. Ther. fell to 54°. 60° at 10 a.m. Got up at 7.30 after the most restful night so far, but pretty bad for all that. At 9.30 called Hausom \+ with Jennings went to 23d S. \+ 4th Ave. \+ bought crutch. then to 2d Nat'l Bk \+ fitted up my affairs there \+ then to 29 S. 38. at 10.30. de Forest came soon after \+ we talked over the audit etc. he excused me from accompanying him to 109th St. \+ N.d.fp \- to meet Manvel. I attended to some writing until lunch at 1. at 2, in a light shower went to the house \+ continued my work there until 4.30. Maynard came in for awhile. Had a nap before dinner. wrote a short note to Mary. Eve'g very quiet. Chase played some light music on his Orchestrelle. Tabed at 10.30
