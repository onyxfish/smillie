## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 29, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day in spite of the predictions of the official weather prophet - pleasant temp. - gentle breezes - warm sunshine \+ beautiful clouds. Bkf. at 8.30 - Fred Ireland came soon after with a note from Cutter saying that there was room in the Jun. class for Ralph - At 10 I went to Millers \+ settled some of the Keene hardware points - home - wrote notes to Rhand Beede \+ Douglas Adams, Keene Heights - Lunch at 12.30 \+ at about 1 o'clk - Ralph \+ I started out to see the Dewey Naval parade - went to 42d S. \+ 6 Ave. Elevated R.R. \+ Rode to 104th S. walked over to Riverside drive, then up to 108 St. down the stairway into Riverside Park where an open hillside gave us a very fine position to see the sights in spite of the crowd. The view was quite unobstructed down and up the river - The crowd was simply tremendous - no description could give an adequate idea of it - We got to our position before 2 o'clk \+ then had to wait about an hour for the procession - at last it came - the Olympia with Admiral Dewey leading followed by half a dozen battle ships \+ Cruisers - a fleet of Torpedo boats \+ Revenue Cutters, transports \+ the like - We could plainly see them go up to opposite Grants tomb - turn, fire a salute \+ come down the River again - It was a brilliant \+ exciting scene \+ quite beyond description - the excursion boats \+ tugs, black with crowding humanity, swarmed over the River like flies - each one making some part of a terrible \+ discordant din - The Olympia stopped opposite to our position \+ the parade passed in review before her - each vessel as it came up being greeted with booming \+ screeching indescribable. About 4 it got to be monotonous \+ we made our way out after much crowding - we went to No. of a new school, others met us. We went thro the [illegible] \+ were much pleased - then to 9 "Ave. \+ 104th" St. took train to 135 \+ 45 - walked across to Madison Ave - took Trolley \+ got home at about 6 - Before 7 Jennings gave us a very good dinner of broiled Chicken - Read ever Post \+ got to bed tired \+ early - hard day before us to-morrow.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 30, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Splendid day - but very cool - pleasant in sunshine - cold in shade. Bkf. soon after 8 - about 10 I went to Mr. Whaley's to go up \+ take another look at our seats \+ learn the way of approach - Found a man there with diagram selling seats for $2. $3. \+ $5.00 Front seats at $5. \+ seats where I bought 2 at $5 each selling for $93. There were two front seats left \+ I tried to get an exchange but she would not do it on any other terms than $4.50 extra - as the view from my seats was cut off by a big flag on one side \+ an awning on the other I felt obliged to yield to what I considered an imposition - my two seats cost me $14. At 11.30 I made two sandwiches \+ with peaches \+ grapes we went to our seats at 12 - The head of procession came at 1.30 - in the meanwhile the crowd in front of us was swaying \+ revolving in a manner that confused me at times - there were so many women \+ children that I felt sure some of them must get killed - an ambulance stood at the Cor. of 36 St. Two women \+ a boy I saw carried to it. The crowd was good natured but there was a very unruly element in it - reckless \+ dangerous - There were several megaphones in the hands of ambitious shouters \+ a continuous din was kept up - at last, at 1.30 the head of the parade reached 36th St. \+ the screaming, shouting \+ confusion that accompanied Dewey's carriage was indescribable - Sousa's Band preceded but it could not be heard - Every body was wild - these outbursts were repeated upon the appearance of favorites - the parade passed along quickly making few halts of more than a minute or two at a time - We watched it until 4 - 2½ hours, \+ then as the sun had gone behind the houses opposite it was blowing very chilly we left our seats \+ went home - in many ways it was a fine show but it was also crude mob-like in many ways \+ I was not greatly impressed - The scene yesterday was much more impressive - From home I started out at 5 with Ralph intending to go to Cutters but stopped at Dr. Yale's on our way - He was in \+ he told me such things about Cutter's school \+ of Dr. Gerster's opinion of it (his son John went there) that my plans were quite upset - I decided to sleep another night over it - Home at 6.30 - Jennings had dinner ready for us - \+ by a comforting fire reading Eve. Post.
