## Left Page

### Sun. March 5, 1899

**Weather:** Disagreeable  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable \- overcast m'f., showers later & heavy rain at night. at about 1 o'clk. I went to Dr. Yale's, but he was not in \- then to Century Club & spent some time looking at the paintings there. Home to dinner at 2. The remainder of the afternoon was divided between reading & napping. Between showers went out with the boys & had a short walk before tea time \- Eve'g at about 9 o'clk. went to Charl's \- Gel had been to my house with Val. late in the afternoon \- in the eve'g he was at Charl's \- It was 11 o'clk before I got home.

### Mon. March 6, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

a superb day To studio about 10, laid varnished the plates that Etching class had prepared for biting \- washed brushes etc. etc \- then to N.A. & to 2? Nat'l B'nk \- Home to lunch at 1.30 then to studio & painted for a little while in the foreground of 20'x 24' \- M'f in the Adirondacks" \- Home at about 4.30 \- met Grace Ireland who told me of the death of Kipling's daughter \- a terrible Tragedy \- Went to Carrere & Hastings \- saw C. & talked with him about the Bill at Albany to transfer freedom from Fapalions from the old site to the new one \- It seems that it will be difficult to do it in an honest, straightforward way \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g. Council meeting \- Mr. Dainek Huntington came in & was asked to sit down informally with us \- he did so & there was interesting talk about the advisability of some sort of a semi-public function to get some of our new rich m[e]mbers who are comparative strangers in the Society of the City into the toils of, & acquainted with the N.A., its work & its needs \- It was after 11 when I got home.
