## Left Page

### Wed. July 5, 1899

**Weather:** Clouded m'f - muggy + rather chilly.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charles Ret'd. to N.Y.

Clouded m'f - muggy + rather chilly. A heavy thunderstorm about the middle of the day + another, lighter one, about 7 P.m. All hands up early - bkf: at 7 + Charl. got away on 8 o'clk. Train - It was a long ev'f. + rather dreary to me as, owing probably to the weather, my leg was unusually painful - I had a long interview with Wm. Spense concerning our pecuniary relations - + finally settled up on $26.50 as a lump sum that would settle all claims up to next Sat. July 8th. After that I had a long interview with Abolles Stone + had no difficulty whatever in settling all matters with him -

Ev'f. my leg felt easier + I walked awhile on Piazza - but the ev'f. was very long - I read awhile to Ralph + was very glad when at last 10 o'clk. came + I went to bed -

I have walked a very little to-day without my crutch.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 6, 1899

**Weather:** overcast most of the day, with two or three showers + considerable thunder.  **Temp:** ull - ev'f. delightfully fresh.

My leg was very painful + the weather was depressing to me - I slept in the m'f. + again in the afternoon - Sprout came with ten Plymouth Rock chickens + after some bargaining I bot. them for $2.50 I wrote a short letter to Katie Elkins - Ev't. I read aloud for awhile to the boys + to-bed at 10.
