## Left Page

### Wed. April 12, 1899

**Weather:** Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced before 5 - wet mf. - but a lovely, balmy, sunny afternoon - I was barely able to get up feeling very ragged after my night of headache - a little btr - after 9 - Mr. Roland Mulford came \+ we talked of Jim \+ Pomfret school - I am much disappointed to learn that he does not expect to teach there next winter - To studio \+ got started at printing at about 11 - from Sternes two plates - one of which I think very clever - from my etched plate - Girl's Head - after Home to lunch \+ back at 2.30 - took [illegible] from my etched plate - Girls Head - after W.r. Lippincott - cleaned up \+ out at 5 o'clk - Rode up Lexington Ave. to 110 St. \+ walked over to Amsterdam Ave. \+ had a look at N.A. school bldg. in course of erection - Home at 6.45 - dinner - eve wrote a letter to Jim - took it to G.C.A. [illegible] \+ mailed it - then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 13, 1899

*Dr. Gerster's Pig dinner.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful spring day - very mild - put on my spring overcoat. To studio \+ cleaned up after printing operations of yesterday. Mounted one of the fab. [illegible] of Lippincott head. Preparatory to retouching the face very carefully to guide me in my work \+ progress. Then to N.A. to see how Frannie is getting on with making up the accts of the year - has not yet got really started \+ seems to me to be very slow in comprehending my circiple place - Home to lunch - at 2.30 at Mr. Jennings' office, 15 Broad St. He had had an interview with A.M+ \+ M.S. Trust Co. \+ was assured that very liberal terms would be offered by Trust Co. Mr. J. promised to draw up a deed of Trust \+ form of subscription very soon. Then I returned to studio. Did a little work on proof, Retouching the face with crayon - Home at 5 \+ had an hours rest - To Dr. Gerster's at 7:clk Fule there acting as host, taking the place of D.G. - who was detained at hospital examining candidates. Saw Mrs. G. for a few minutes - It was nearly 7.30 when G. came - The guests were Dr. Yale - Dr. Otto Liliane - Had Billet \+ two musical gentlemen, Messrs Billet \+ Wetzer - Dr. G's son John sat at table with us making eight - We had a good dinner \+ a very pleasant, informal time - I sat between Yale \+ Billet - It was after 11 when Yale \+ I left - I Rode with Y. to his house \+ then walked home - Found that Katherine \+ Bessie had just got up a little before, from Charls, bringing. Olga Waste were there - She stayed over night.
