## Left Page

### Sun. May 28, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably warm day - Ther. on north side of house at 3 p.m. 87°. Thunder storms about in the evef. The pavement got a wetting at 8 p.m. - but that was all.
Up soon after 6 - had my rub + bath + then to bed again until 8.30 - bkf. at 9 -
A severe headache came on about 11 + I was useless until near 1 - Then I wrote some notes etc - Dinner at 2. About 4 I went to W. 45 st. to call on Sam. Colman + wife - they went to Newport on the 15 - Then I went to Emma Ireland's - + visited with her until near 6. I took to her some of Annie's old 12th St. School M.S. Records that I found not long ago - From there I went to George Cook's - 122 Mad. Ave. Sawhim - Emily was under the weather. Then home. Tea at 7 - Crazy Read awhile to Ralph - then wrote to Jim - also notes to Geo. Cook + Emily + to Emma I - asking them to dine with us next Thurs. evef. Then to G. C. Station with letter to Jim - Then home + to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 29, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph went to the Cuttings

An unpleasant kind of a day - overcast - with chilly east wind that made one shiver - at the same time very little exertion wd bring on perspiration. Between 11 + 12 p.m. - a smart shower with thunder + lightning. After bkf. I went to Carrere + H. - saw Mr. Clements about some work on new bldg M.A. - Then to Wm Miller's + to studio - worked on N.A. accts. + at last have them all satisfactorily harmonized + clearly recorded. Stahr + Sterne came with their plates, drawn from Mr. Brimmer + set to work at once varnishing them - Stahr, being the most impatient of the two hit his + worked until 6. p.m - Just before 12 I went to N.A. - saw Grannis + had a rather angry interview - I was much displeased that he should be so indifferent, apparently, about getting the books of acct. ready for the Auditing Com. He is hopelessly in arrears + makes no effort to recover himself - on the contrary, without doing anything to me, he went fishing on Friday + at - He says that I have compared the accts. as recorded in Check book so that he does not know how to post them - an excuse only + a discredit to his intelligence. Home to lunch at 1 - To studio before 2 + coached Stahr in his biting - Ret. home before 4 o'clk + saw Ralph started for the Cuttings - He is to remain until Thurs. - Back to studio - there until 6 - then home - Dinner at 6.30 - at 8. to N.A. last meeting in the old bldg. - J.G. Hart presided - full council - J.G. Brown + [illegible] DeForest there. The business was mostly routine - There was better sentiment, altho' Reference to the fact that it was our last meeting was made several times - we loitered until 11 + Auld lang Syne was sung as we left the Council Room. As we went into the street a heavy shower was falling - I was provided with umbrella + got home without a wetting -
