## Left Page

### Thur. July 27, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm, delightful breeze - Thunder-storms north \+ south of us from 1 to 3 o'clk. Then clear again - cool eve'g. Katherine \+ Bessie went with D. Sayres party to Salt Spring - pic-nic - I spent some time hunting over my stock \+ paper here for a sheet suitable to make Dr. Jacobi's portrait on - At about 11 I went down-Town \+ home at 1 - Herrick. working in attic - afternoon I made paste - mounted paper for Jacobi portrait - went down town at 6 - Eve'g, as usual, with news paper.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 28, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - a good part of the m'g was spent writing letters to Jim and Ralph - about noon I went down town to P.O. etc. Afternoon I arranged my scale for the Jacobi portrait \+ fixed my pins \+ squares of thread (No. 200) over photograph \+ paper. Herrick worked about the house - part of the time over the attic making openings in north \+ south ends to permit a movement of air through, \+ I directed - The usual eve'g.
