## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 11, 1899 [Tuesday. 12.]

**Weather:** mild & muggy  **Temp:** —

mild & muggy — a very stormy, wet mf clearing in the afternoon — To studio soon after 9 — spent much of the mf. continuing a synopsis of N.A. accts. for last year, with intention of presenting it to-morrow eve'f at the meeting — the rest of the mf. went to Mr. Guy C. Bipler, ass. Editor Nat. Myclopedia of Biography — white publisher — agt my better judgment he talked me over until I consented to have a portrait inserted — for this I subscribed for the work to be completed in ten or a dozen vols — half bound at $10 per month — total cost about $120 — I am vexed at myself for allowing him to talk me over. To lunch at 1.30 after I had worked awhile in pastels on some red Yosemite W.C. studies — Mable Cooley left at 3 o'clk. I got to N.A. at 4 o'clk. & met there by appointment Mrs Barhydt — I took her to the Etching studio & showed her the pf2 from the Etchings by Sterne & Stahr — She appeared to be very much pleased with what she saw & what I told her — After she left I got some figures from the check book for my Report — Then I went to 10th St — met J.G. Brown — saw Nicoll in his studio — then to dockwood de Forests & talked with him half an hour — he advised me not to read any report — announce only that my report had been duly prepared & approved by the accountant & the auditing com — & to this I finally assented — Home at 6.30 — dinner — Eve'f — I loafed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 15, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** —

A day that opened windy & cloudy after some light showers during the night — & then cleared bright, windy & cold — I got to studio at 9.30 — wrote to Jim & to S.P. Avery & printed from Jacobi head, in plain paper, from Mr. G — plain print — & from a little sketch head from by Maurice Sterne — Home to lunch at 1.45 — back at 2.45 & used what was left of day light in clearing up & clearing up. I am discouraged & do not feel at all like work. If I could finish the Mr. G. head to my liking it would relieve me of a great load. Eve'f.
