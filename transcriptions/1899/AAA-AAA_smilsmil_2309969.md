## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 9, 1899

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast mg, looking very promising for rain - but by noon the day was beautifully bright - clouded up again before sunset - day very mild - I had fine lighted in the studio in the mg thinking that if the weather was bad I would do my in-door packing - instead, I was out of doors all day engaged upon work of all sorts. Herrick finished my aqua-tint-ground box \+ frame. I went down to P.O - about 11 - paid some bills - got screws for Herrick - pruning trees until dinner - afternoon for awhile in lot when Speuse \+ Dickson \+ his team were hauling f wall stone to the lot limits - then to P.O. oiled Herrick's wood-work on Aquatint gd. box. Tea at 6.30. wrote to Reede about hardware matters - articles to be Ret'd. to Russell \+ Erwin Co. — \+ articles yet missing, probably stolen.
Suffered great discomfort from acidity of stomach — awakened at 3 a.m. by pain \+ nausea - after a dose of Carb. Soda it passed away.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 10, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A marvellous day - opening with a luminous mist - it passed into a cloudless day - violet blue sky - a wonder of color - T mer. 69°. in the shade. - After bkft. I spent some time ainting - then to P.O. much time spent looking for bbls. to pack apples in - also, with young Turrell - having him go with me to the end of the old Tyler lot to show him the little gore of land that I wish his father to give me in return for building 450' of line wall on my side of the line - he approved but could not speak for his father who is, he says, "very peculiar." Dinner after 1. Then Warner came \+ painted 2d. story piazza decking - then tarred trees - then oiled the wood-work of my new aqua-tint ground-laying machine - then down to P.O - and back by way of old Tyler lot - finished picking apples - went again to P.O. at 5.30 - Tea after 6 - Mary had had another ride with Mr. \+ Mrs. Dav'd. Sayre. — Eve'g as usual - newspaper \+ writing.
