## Left Page

### Sat. March 25, 1899

**Weather:** Dull, raw, mean day \- Rain at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio \+ worked over some old etched plates \- Got out the partly finished plate \- Girl's head \- after Skipkin \- Catt \+ worked on that until noon \- then went to Fifth Ave. Trust Co. Cor 43d. St. \+ saw Mr. Laudell to whom I gave Mr. Morton's card of introduction, \+ to him I talked about taking care of N.a. investments \- then to Douglas' in Lex. Ave. \+ home to lunch. To studio \+ worked on head until 5 \- then out \- sleety Rain \- home \- dinner at 6.30 \- Went with the family.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 26, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow on the ground in the mf. \- dull until the afternoon when it cleared cold \- Bt ft at usual time \- at 10.30 went to Fred Mclands \+ had a talk with him about Jim \- He advises me to take him from Morse's school at once \- have a tutor for him until we go out of town \+ send him to Pomfret next fall \- then I took a little walk, going to Studio to look after the fire \- Home at 1.30 \- dinner at 2 \- at 4 went to Chase's \- met Mr. Meyer, of Kansas City Smelting works there. Then a second walk \+ got home at 5.30 \- . Then I lay down on my bed \+ rested until 7.30 \- Then I had a little to eat \+ at 8 went to Jco. Hearn's \- 46 E. 69th St. \- in answer to his invitation \- several people there \- Dunean \- of Bric a Brac fame \- Mr. \+ Mrs. Blakeslee \+ Hearns married daughter \+ her husband \- . I had a quiet talk with Mrs. H. about working on the old painting of mine "The lifting on the Clouds" \- Hearn says it is her picture \+ she must decide \- I asked her to come to my Studio \+ I would show her just what I wished to do \- she said she would \- I left at 10 o'clk. and walked home \- passing the ruins of Windsor Hotel \- very picturesque effect \- men working by flaming gas lights \+ moonlight.
