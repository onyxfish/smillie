## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 3, 1899

**Weather:** Over cast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Over cast m+f, that for a few minutes gave a delusive promise of good weather + then turned out about as nasty a specimen of a day as could be made — first sleet, freezing as it fell — then a little snow, then a drizzle of Rain. I went to studio — worked for a little while on the small sketch of Capri — Then I painted for an hour on "Ruins of San Luis Rey Mission," changing an architectural line in the composition that has bothered me for a long time — went home to lunch + back to studio about 2 — Got out my larger painting, "Capri from near Naples + painted over portions of sky + sea. Home before 5. Found quite a crowd there — The Rooms looking very pretty in the light + decoration — There were many old time people, some of whom I had not seen in many, many years, — Mary Polhemus + her sister — Annie Ballard + her daughter — Thos. Shearman + his wife — &c — The three girls had with them, to "assist" the Misses Glave, Holt + Cooley — Emma Ireland presided at the table — Chas. + Mrs. Adams came in after 6 — at [illegible], with them + Ralph I went to Charles' + had dinner — at 9.30 carriage came — R — + I went home in it + Then I took Marie Champney (who, by the way, was one of the assistants) down to her home Fifth Ave Cor. 15th S — I got home soon after 10 — + to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 4, 1899

**Weather:** A brilliant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant day — blue sky — no clouds. Keene, cold wind — Nicoll came in after 1[illegible] about N.A. business — To studio at 10.30 — worked on larger Capri until 12 — then to O'Brien's — to N.A. + to 2nd Nat Bank — It was nearly 2 when I got home to lunch. It was 3 when I got to Carrere + Hastings — just closing — Mr. Brainerd The only one in + he could not tell me what I wanted to know. Then to studio — Some general work there until near 5 — then home + rested until 6. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g Mr. Mayon came + had his first session with Jim, as his tutor. I am much concerned at what seems to me to be a very bad mental attitude that Jim has been getting into for some time past. I hope that Mr. M. will be able to break the spell that seems to be on him now. I had intended to go to The Century in the eve'g but as the girls had gone to Charles, I found it easier to stay at home with Ralph + read to him — Mr. Mayon stayed until 9.30 — two full hours —
