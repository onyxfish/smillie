## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 2, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine day but I was bothered all day with an aching head - something of a Rheumatic sort. To studio at 9.30 \+ all the m[illegible] fussing over p[illegible]. from the Jacobi \+ Gerster plates - dampening the thin p[illegible]. p[illegible]. \+ putting them between blotters in the press. Working on an india p[illegible]. of Mr. G. with crayon I decided to make a change in the back ground which has never pleased me. Home to lunch at 1 to suit Kate's convenience - afternoon my head was so uncomfortable that I gave up to it \+ went to bed \+ had a long nap. Up at 6 feeling somewhat better - dinner at 6.30 - We'f went to Photographic Salon, management of the institute at Berkely Institute, 19 21 W. 44 S. \+ was very much interested in some portrait photos that I saw there. Pine Mac Donald of Albany. \+ by Rudolf Eickemeyer of Yonkers. Then to Century \+ had a very good time there until midnight - then home with Chas. Collins who left me at my door.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 3, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day. Had my ocnb about 7 - back to bed - okf[illegible]. at 9.30 - Soon after 11 Ralph \+ I got away on our wheels - \+ made the round of Central Park - just 10 m. by my cyclometer. Saw an exciting Runaway in the Park \+ crowds about the damaged Parties. Home before 1 o'clk. I was pretty moist - had a rub down \+ change \+ read Tribune until dinner at 2. After dinner wrote notes to Howard Russell Butler \+ W.H. Warner. After 5 took a walk up to Central Park \+ on my way back stopped at Yale's and found him in pretty miserable plight. There until near 7 - then home \+ had a light meal. We'f wrote a letter to Jim at 9 o'clk. Took it to G.C. Station - then to Charl's where Kate \+ Bessie had dined - heard some Wagner on the Orchestrelle - home at 10.30
