## Left Page

### Fri. April 28, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

another brilliant — wind easterly but pleasant — I am much out of sorts from my cold which holds me in the head, by the throat \+ away down in my chest. to studio at 10 — Spent an hour going over what I had written on the Benedict business \+ then to N.A. where I worked on acct? until 1.30 — then home to lunch — While at lunch Benedict came — went to studio with me but he could not, without his books explain what I wanted to know of the growth of his note indebtedness — so he is to come again to-morrow m'f. About 4 I sauntered up 5th Ave. \+ to the Exh. Socy of Am. Artists — there until near 6. then sauntered home again \+ found Katie Elkin waiting — she brought very satisfactory news from June — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef all hands out to theatre with Charl \+ Adams — Ralph \+ I alone — we had a game of Halma — then to bed — I bring very much "stuffed up."
Recd form of Academy Trust deed from Mr. Jennings through Beckwith — to put the N.A. maintainance fund beyond the reach of diversion,

## Right Page

### Sat. April 29, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ warm — I was not able to get out of bed until about 6 P.M — Temp. at 7 A.M. a little over 99° \+ at 10 P.M — a little under 99°. sore throat \+ chest \+ severe head ache — A friend Benedict came at about 11 o'clk \+ spent some time with me — he brought mem. of his notes given to the N.A. \+ of their extension but his memory of the transactions is not very clear \+ his records are not complete — This distress me in the investigations that I am making as the basis of a Report that I wish to be complete \+ unassailable.

I got up about 6 \+ went down to my dinner — Evef all hands went out for a cafe \+ again Ralph \+ I were left alone — he read awhile to me from "The Courtship of Miles Standish" — To bed soon after 10.
