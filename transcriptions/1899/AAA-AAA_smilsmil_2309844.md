## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 30, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, breezy, cold day \- a snow flurry last night left a thin film of snow that was soon blown away \- When I got to the studio, Maynard was wrestling with leaking valves on the condensor of the hot water heating apparatus \- I stopped it temporarily \+ then got to my work \- Set palette \+ commenced painting on a small panel an off hand sketch of "Capri, from near Naples" About 12 went to Higgins' \+ left order for man to attend to leak, which he did in the afternoon \- I went to N.A. then home to lunch at 1.30 \- Ralph came in from school very much out of sorts \- I fixed him in my bed, with hot water bag &c \- To studio \+ worked until 4.30 \- then home \+ rested until dinner time \- Ralph, by that time had developed considerable fever, so I went in for Dr. Putnam \+ he came while we were at dinner \- He thinks the trouble is with his digestion \+ gave a course of Calomel \- At 8.15 I went to Council meeting at N.A. \- a short session \- I got home about 10 o'clk \- Ralph's bed had been arranged alongside of mine

At 9 A.M. I went to Dr. Janeway's and told him of my miseries from excessive acidity of stomach \- He advised Nux Vom\- 10 drops before meals.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 31, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day, cloudy \+ raw with a little snow \- Ralph still had high temp. so, before bkfst I went in to Dr. Putnam's to ask him to come in, which he did before 10 He could find no evidence of any thing but a trouble with his digestives \+ ordered Citrate of Magnesia which gave the boy a pretty thorough clearing out\! He was kept in bed all day \- but at night his temp. still stood at nearly 101°. I got to studio at about 10.30 \+ altho' very uncomfortable from a headache I worked on the little oil sketch of "Capri from near Naples" \- Home at about 1.30 \- after lunch my headache had so far got the better of me that I lay on my bed until 4.30 \- then I went to studio, cleaned my palette \+, then, inspite of the disagreeable weather, took a moderately long walk \+ home to dinner at 6.30 \- Crane came in the evg. \+ I decided to invest in his Mr. Meyer's Smelting stock pref. Ralph's temp. still kept up above 100° so I gave him a dose of Rhubarb \+ Soda.
