## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 20, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day \- feeling better than yesterday but tender throat \& hoarse \- Went down town getting to Court at 10.30. The whole m'f was spent in waiting \- All hands present, at 12.40 the case at last, was called \& F.B. Lee made his presentation of it. Adjourned at 1 \- Brainerd, Maynard Geo \& I went to the Astor House \& had a very good lunch \& back at 2 \- case resumed immediately \- Jas. M. Ward for Tax Com. \- a shrewd able man \- after his Reply to B.F.Lee, on further argument Judge (or Justice) Nash allowed testimony to prove that The Academy had, in good faith, pressed the work of getting plans \& preparing working drawings for the buildings to be erected \- I was called \& got along well enough until Ward took hold of me. He very soon got into a line of questioning that showed his drift \& made me nervous \- He asked if the N.A. occupied its property on 23d St \- as owner or tenant \- an awkward question to answer \- whether it rented to galleries \- whether students paid fees \- whether the instructors were members of the N.A. \& if they rec'd pay for their services \- all of them questions with a plain intent \& with due regard to facts, difficult to evade \- However, all things he treated me very well \& the Judge was patient, considerate \& kind \- Brainerd followed me \& was a rattling good witness \- Maynard \& Geo were not called, the judge saying it was unnecessary \- Result \- There is no question of fact \- it is now a question of construction of law \- does the enumeration of exemptions named in the law cover the N.A. ? \- Lee \& Lee are directed to furnish a brief in ten days \- Home \& had a rest before dinner \- Every at home with the boys \- the others at Chars with Uncle Willie.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 21, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day \& milder \- At b'kf't. Rec'd a note from Lee \& Lee stating that an engagement had been made for me to meet W. Gherardi Davis at his office, 44 Pine St. I went to it \& L. 20 Nassau \& with B.F. Lee \& Kingsbury went to 44 Pine \& met Mr. Davis \& talked with him for some time about a bill for the benefit of the N.A. to be passed by the legislature of N.Y. remitting taxation on its new site \& buildings to be erected. He took the bill \& promised to look after it in Albany. I was pleased by the quick \& intelligent interest that Mr. D \- showed in the bill \& am hopeful that he will be able to push it thro' without trouble \- Then I went to Tax office \& found that the valuation of the site has been again raised \& is now \$112000 \- doubled in two years \- Then to N.A. \& attended to some business there \& home to lunch at 1.30 \- After that a rest, then to Con. Gas Co. \& to Etching Class Studio, 4 W. 22d St. to see what Shake things were there \- Then to Nipples, 20 E. 16th St. Met Dielman \& Blum there \& we worked until 6 selecting proofs for the Baldwin Fund. N.A. Then home to dinner \- Everf Chas. came in for a while evidently elated over the shake that some business ventures of his are getting into \- All hands got to bed early \-

Uncle Willie Ret'd to Po'k. This P.M.
