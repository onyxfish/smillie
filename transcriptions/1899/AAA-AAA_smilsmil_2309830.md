## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 2, 1899

**Weather:** Fine, cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine, cold day — after bkft. wrote some N.A. letters \+ got to studio soon after 10. Spent a good part of what remained of the mng biting an "Ex Libris" place for Maynard. Also did some work on my W.C. "on the Susquehanna". Home to lunch at 1.30 — to studio before 3 — fire in heater had burned out so I had to light it again — Cut my W.C. so that it would fit the frame that I have for it \+ signed it \+ put it in the frame. After 5 o'elk. I made a New Year call on Walter Cook's family — saw them all \+ more too as Mr. Sprague was there \+ Carl Sprague \+ his wife. Home soon after 6 — Bad news from Dr. Adams at New-Haven who is seriously ill with Grip.
Evet. Read to Ralph \+ then had a game of Halma. Charl came in for a few minutes.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 3, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dr. D.A. Adams died this mrg.

Pleasant day growing constantly milder got up with a headache that was a discomfort all day. To Dr. Allen's in 47th St. \+ made appointment with him for To-morrow p.m. at 2.30 — met H.B. Butler \+ walked a couple of blocks with him. Then to studio \+ worked for a little while on W.C. then to N.A. \+ to 2nd Nat. Bk — then home to lunch. Found there a telegram from Roger Adams saying "Father died this morning, Pneumonia, little suffering Mother ill, Grip, so funeral private". Alice Ireland came in \+ lunched with us, on her way to New-Haven. My headache troubled me so much that I lay on my bed until near 5 — Then to meeting of Building Comt. at C. \+ H's office — the plans of temporary building are now complete — At 5.30 went to Mr. Yale's \+ home at 6 — Dinner at 6.30. Evet. a game with Ralph — Then he read a little from Dickens to me. I wrote a letter to Lee \+ Lee with reference to Frank Fowler's picture to know the extent of the Academy's obligation when a purchaser fails to come to time.
