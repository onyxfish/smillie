## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 16, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderful day for brilliancy + beauty. not a cloud in the sky all day — At night the moonlight was ravishing. I spent some time after 8bp'. writing to M. J. H. Maghee — + to the boys — Then to work on Jacobi with graver + dry point. I should have taken a pf. immediately after biting but my printing outfit is not in good working order it is so long since it has been used, so, I am putting off the day of trial. I gave some of my time to watching the plumbers who are at work in my bathroom — but I "put in" a good days work on Jacobi. About 5 o'clk I went to P.O. then walked down So. Main + seeing Mrs. Courtright on her piazza I stopped there for awhile. Evef. at 8 o'clk. Dr. Gardiner came to see Bessie + I Rose with him to his office for some medecine for her. + Then walked home. — paced piazza during much of the evef.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 17, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Camp Choconut.

Another fine day. Plumbers came, stayed half an hour + found they could do nothing without a carpenter, + Heinck cannot come until monday. I worked all the mfg., with graver, on Jacobi. Had a short call from Miss Kingsland who had driven up from Parke's farm with Greene P— asked her + Bessie P— to come to dinner to-morrow. Had dinner at 12.30 + at 1.30 Katherine + I started in a one horse buggy for camp Choconut — suffered less from heat + dust than I had expected + in spite of a stumbling horse we got over there in 2¼ hours. Found the boys very well + very happy over the capturing of a young woodchuck that they had just dug out of its hole + killed. At about 5.30 we had tea + sandwiches on the piazza of the Muelford house — two of the young counselers present. At 6.10 we started for home + had a very pleasant sunset, twilight + moonlight ride getting in at 8.20. It was about 9 when I had a little to eat. Read my newspaper + got to bed.
