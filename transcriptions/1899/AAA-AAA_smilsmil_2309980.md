## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 31, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate came from P'k.

A very disagreeable day - high winds \+ heavy rain - I went to Studio about 10 \+ skent the m'f. Preparing to prints tomorrow - most of the time cleaning \+ picking over Jap. Paper \+ Chinese india. lunch at 2 - As I did not care to get another wetting in going to Studio I stayed at home, having enough to do there - instead of doing it however, much of the afternoon was spent slacking. About 5. Mr. Schee, Ass't. Art Director, Paris [illegible] came to tack of Etching exhibit - As I expected not enough good etchings to make a satisfactory ex[illegible] have been offered \+ if an ex[illegible] is to be made the com. will have to find the material.

Dinner at 6.30 - Kate, who came before lunch, dined \+ [illegible] the ev'f with Ch[illegible] I worked with Ralph until after 9 - This work discourages me - neither of my boys seem to have a faculty for study. R- is mentally quick at every thing but his lessons - They seem to be a "grind" in sp[ite] of me \+ he works without giving any evid[ence] of interest.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day with some wet - To studio early \+ spent the m'f. filing off the sharp edges of copper \+ zinc plates of students - in grinding printing ink \+ getting press ready to print - Home to lunch at 1.30 - by the way - as a part of my m'f work I wrote a 9 page letter to Jim - back to Studio before 3 \+ printed 9 pfp. from several of the plates - It was feeling about to get things as I wanted them, but none the less work. At 5.30 I took a short walk, then home to dinner - Suel'f. share came in for a little while - Then Will Parke \+ wife came in - they left a little before 10 - then to bed.
