## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 5, 1899

**Weather:** mild, quiet day, part of the time a grey, brooding overcast day  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild, quiet day, part of the time a grey, brooding overcast day. Up at 6. \- had bkfs. at 7 \+ at 8 went with Mary \+ Katharine to the R.R. Station \- K. went to N.Y. I ret. to the house \- much of the mnt. was spent sawing out the top of an ash tree by the side of the drive way that is growing so high as to impede the view \- then gathered apples from the trees in the pasture \+ also did some pruning on the trees. Dinner at 1 \- after that, Spenser cut down a Ball wood tree growing next to the Ash, that I cut the top out of this mnt. It was such a rank grower that it had got beyond possibility of my trimming it into shape, so that I could see over it from the piazza, so I had it cut down. Went down town at 4 \- got mail \+ Baker \- then back \+ continued to gather apples until near 6 \- Tea at 6.30. Eve'g reading news paper \+ writing.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 6, 1899

**Weather:** Dull mnt., rain seeming to be imminent  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull mnt., rain seeming to be imminent, but a slow improvement went on until about 3 P.M. The weather cleared beautifully. Up at 7 \- commenced my work out of doors about 9 o'clk. Later, went down town. Paid H.W. Beach bill \- left shoes with Camp \- then to P.O. \+ home before 12. Picked apples \+ did some clipping \+ pruning. Dinner soon after 1 \- then took down awnings on Studio Bay window. After 3 went to P.O. \+ to Herrick's \- after that picked apples until nearly 6 o'clk. getting some of the finest "Northern Spys" that I have yet picked \- then took down some of the bamboo shades on piazza. Tea about 6.30 \- Eve'g, news paper until 9 o'clk.
