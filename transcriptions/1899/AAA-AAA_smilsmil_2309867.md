## Left Page

### Fri. March 17, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day - Carpenter commenced work on vestibule in basement hall - at studio I wrote until 11.30 - then I walked to Dr. Alleen's - W. 47 "St. \+ he examined my teeth \+ said there was nothing for him to do. I sauntered about until 1 - then lunch at home - Then to studio \+ then to Etching class in 22ᵈ S'. Thunder Cloud the Indian as model. Staho - Sterne - Kelly \+ Perrine there - I was with them until 5 o'clk - then walked up 5ᵃ Ave. to the Windsor Hotel - 46" \+ 47. S'. which had been burning more than two hours - a tremendous conflagration with wild stories of panic \+ loss of life - there was an immense crowd but I managed to see most that was to be seen - No studio - to Edw'? Cooks \+ then home to dinner at 6.30 - a short eve'g \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 18, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark, foggy \+ wet - at studio during the m'g \+ wrote half a dozen letters \+ notes of one sort \+ another \+ home to lunch at 1.30 - after lunch my headache so far got the better of me that I was obliged to go to bed - did not get up again until after 5 - then I went out \+ had a short walk in murk \+ fog - I got within a block of the ruin of Windsor Hotel - great crowds there. Home to dinner at 6.30 - head more trouble - some in the eve'g - to bed at 10 -
