## Left Page

### Wed. June 21, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day - cool, clear \+ breezy - I got up at 7 - very stiff \+ discouraged after a tolerable night - which, nevertheless was better than any of the nights since the accident - With Jennings I went into bath room \+ he gave me a satisfactory bath from my chest plaster bandages down to my feet. Mr. Manuel came before 9 \+ worked until after 12 - back before 3, worked until 5 \+ got everything at last to balance perfectly, greatly to my gratification - At breakfast I got 5 Montrose letters - from Mary, the girls, \+ the boys. The day was very tedious of course - Dr. Gerster was out of town \+ sent his youthful assistant - Dr. Allen came about 4. I wrote to Perry - 2d letter - he does not at once agree to Manuel for Auditor - I wrote to Mary in the eve'g. Sheldon \+ Val. came in to say good-bye - they go to Cotuit to-morrow - Some club men came in to see Charl - When I went to bed I dispensed with my back rest \+ slept propped up by fellows alone.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 22, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 68° at 9 A.m.

Still lovely weather - very cool - Charl says that his registering Ther. fell to 58° last night - I had a fairly good nights rest - Manuel came before 9 \+ worked all the m'g then left \+ did not return - I wrote a long letter to deForest - also notes to Stahr \+ Steme Dr. Adams came as I was writing, at about noon - He took off my adhesive plaster bands \+ cleaned my skin. he was encouraging in spite of the general pain I suffered - I really feel much discouraged. About 4 30 Jennings fixed my bed \+ I had a good nap - After 6 he helped me down to the Library - \+ at 7, down to the dining room.
