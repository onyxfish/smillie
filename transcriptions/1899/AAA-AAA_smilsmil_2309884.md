## Left Page

### Sat. April 22, 1899

**Weather:** Very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day — I got up with a beast of a headache — after having suffered from it all night — had bkft about 10 — Nicoll came in then \+ we had a talk about N.A. matters — Then I went to N.A. \+ attended to some business — then rode up to 109 St. \+ Amsterdam Ave. \+ went all over the School building now erecting. Home at 1.30 \+ after lunch lay down upon my bed \+ slept most of the p.m. — Dressed for the eve'g. — before dinner Soon after 8 went to N.A. — Reception to Advisory Com. Galleries closed to the public — not a brilliant success — Invitations not sent out soon enough — 650 of them many — Regrets — hardly half came — few millionaires — I went about with W. Seligman Bishop Potter came in too late to make the Remarks promised — but W. Wm 2. Dodge spoke very acceptably — after him Pres. Seth Low — after that an exceptionally good supper served to the guests, as they sat, by many waiters. Chase was there with Mr. Aug. st mayor — It was after 12 when we walked up home together.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 23, 1899

**Weather:** Very mild, pleasant spring day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild, pleasant spring day — up early — had my scrub — bkft — at 9.30 newspaper \+ after 11 Ralph \+ I got away on our bicycles \+ made the circuit of the Park — went to new N.A. bldg met Steene there — Home at 1 — Rested until dinner at 2 — Afternoon, had a nap — wrote a letter to Catherine Elkin — after tea read awhile to Ralph — then wrote a letter to Jim — at 10.30 took it to G.C. Station — \+ to bed soon after.
