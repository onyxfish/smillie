## Left Page

### Sat. June 17, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thrown from my wheel in Central Park.

A very warm day - Mr. Fred^t. morval the accountant came before I had finished breakfasting - I took him to my house + showed him my acct book for N.A., check books etc, + explained my way of keeping them - He spent an hour or more with me + left after making an app^t to come monday m^g \+ carry the investigation thro' to a finish - I tried to help matters by continuing some of my tables of analysis - at 2 I went to the Century + had lunch - after that, again to the house + plodded at same thing until almost 4 - Then I took my bicycle + went to Charls' He was in but did not care to go out with me, so, I went alone - I rode up through the Park - out at 110^t S^t \+ to N.A. + drove - there I met Bolton Jones + F.S. Church - I was detained for some time + then started down thro' the Park on the East Drive, at the 90^th S^t Gate - I decided to go out + down 5^t Ave. - to get home quicker - coming from the north, on the west side, many vehicles \+ bicycles passing up + down, I saw what seemed a chance to get thro' across the stream going up on the east side of the drive, so I made a jump for it, getting farover on the left side of the Road - at that moment a man on bicycle coming down 5^t Ave. swuing into the Park at a good clip + was + bushes intervening, we did not see me another until an instant before meeting - I cannot tell clearly what happened - before I could think I was flung violently into the grass - possibly, the man, to save himself, put on a spurt + bowled me over - at any rate, he went on + I did not - I was stunned for a few seconds - The breath being knocked out of me I was helped to a seat by a young man and thought, at first that I would soon recover + be able to go on - A few minutes proved to me that that was not possible - so the young man got a Coupe' for me - my wheel was put on the box in front + I started for Charls

## Right Page

### Sat. June 17, 1899 *(continued)*

my left leg being quite useless I had difficulty in getting into the carriage, but once there, rode without inconvenience - Got to Charls a little after 6.30 - After I got in the effect of shock began to show itself - Charl. Adams + Jennings were in + I was got up to my room + into my bed as promptly as possible - Charl went for Dr. Allen + came back with him in a very short time - He said that I am recovering from shock - no bones broken, altho' he was not quite sure about one of my ribs - I found that when in bed I was not able to move myself - my chest was bandaged with adhesive plaster so that I could not fully inflate my lungs - Jennings took charge of me + stayed with me all night - After 10 o'clk Charl telephoned to Dr. Gester, asking him to come in to see me to-morrow - I passed a very painful, sleepless night.

### Sun. June 18, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day - but a very uncomfortable one for me - Jennings has to help me in every movement + not one can be made without great, sometimes exquisite, pain - I layed a little at times during the day, + having got my back Rest from the house + propped me up with pillows - I managed to write two letters describing my accident, one to Jim, the other to Ralph - Dr. Allen came, + later, Dr. Gester - To my gratification neither of them think that I am seriously injured - Every body is kind + attentive to me but the day was very long in wearing away - Jennings slept on the floor by the side of my bed last night - He brought a wicker lounge from the house + slept in that - Brigao S^de^ Cook + his wife came to my bed side in the eveg.
