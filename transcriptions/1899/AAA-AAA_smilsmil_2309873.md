## Left Page

### Wed. March 29, 1899

**Weather:** Dull m+.  **Temp:** [illegible]

At the Mayor's office.

Dull on+. with light snow flurries — High wind in the afternoon — Will Parke came about 9.30 — went to studio with me \+ then down-town. I went to City Hall \+ met other members of the Council \+ at 11 o'clk. sharp, we had a hearing before Mayor VanWyck with reference to bill to exempt N.A.of D. new site — from taxation — just passed by the legislature. He promptly discovered that the word free, preceeding schools, in the old bill had been omitted in the new one. \+ asked all explanation — we had none to give — he said he would veto the bill unless that word was inserted — \+ we promised to have the word put in — He treated us with entire civility — I went at once to dear Lee — saw both — they explained that the word had been dropped intentionally because recent enactments were much more liberal than the laws in force when the first bill was passed \+ to have the word in the new bill might restrict privileges granted by later laws — Letters to Senator Nate A. Elsberg \+ Assemblyman Gherardi Davis were dictated \+ sent — In the afternoon I wrote to the same gentlemen asking if a new bill could be got through with the word free inserted — to studio at 12.30 — home to lunch at 1.5 — to studio at 2.30 Kelly came \+ I laid sketching ground for him \+ a solid ground for Staler — Then Parke came \+ with him Miss Mable Kingman who was visiting at his house in Savannah when I was there last summer — We had a long chatty session — Some writing after they left — a short walk — too cold \+ windy to be pleasant — home soon after 6 — At 6.30 Wm. Elkin \+ Kateg came \+ dined with us — a pleasant eveg — at 10 they left —

## Right Page

### Thur. March 30, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

w— ether variable — early m+ overcast — then high wind with flying clouds. later, clear, quieter \+ milder. Waited until 10 for Beckwith \+ Nicoll who were to call for me — see three to go to lawyers Jennings about N.A.of D. Trust — don't know why they did not come — Then to studio \+ wrote until 12.30 — then out \+ walked until after 1 — then to lunch — At studio and continued my writing until 4 — then went to Rob't Reid's Studio — Reception — 142 E. 33d. St. and looked at his [illegible]. Then to N.A. — varnishing day — \+ saw a number of the artists \+ attended to some work — home after 6 — Dinner — At 8 went \+ Mr. Jas. Herbert Morse's to tell him what I had decided to do about him — but he had gone to Cotuit. — Then to Dr. Yale's \+ spent the rest of the eveg with him.
