## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day, very cold, with strong biting wind from the north. Bkft. at 9 - then some writing - It was noon when I went to studio for a few minutes - then I walked for awhile on the sunny side of Park Ave - but the wind \+ cold made it too unpleasant - so I went into Charl's \+ was there until near 2 o'clk then home to dinner - About 4 o'clk I walked up to Dr. Gerster's 34 E. 75th St. - a two mile walk - no one at home - then I walked back \+ on my way stopped \+ saw Mr. Yale who gave an encouraging acct. of the Doctor's condition - Dr. Gerster operated on him last Friday for appendecitis - Home after my four mile walk very cold but otherwise in good condition - Tea at 6.30 - Eve \+ some reading to Ralph - wrote a note to Dr. Gerster. Charl came in for a few minutes.
