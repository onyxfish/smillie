## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 22, 1899

**Weather:** mild, overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild, overcast day — with some sunshine in the mtf. I got to studio at 9 30 — Maurice Sterne som came to express his gratitude for what I have done for him in Etching. S.W. Jewitt, agent for owner of stables on other side of alley came in to see if I wd. share expense of repaving part of court. I told him that his side was in very bad condition \+ shd. be repaved — when that was done, if any part of my pavement did not conform to a proper grade I would have it relaid —. Then I devoted the rest of the mtf. to taking pfz. from Jacobi, Mr. Gerster. To lunch at 1.30 \+ back at 2.30 — worked with Grover on Mr. Q — until after 4 — then rested in my big chair until after 5 — then a short walk \+ home to dinner. Evelf Ge? came in \+ sat with me in my room until near 10 — Mr. \+ Mrs. Marshall being in parlor with Mary \+ girls.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 23, 1899

**Weather:** Pleasant, altho' overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant, altho' overcast, most of the time — after writing a letter to Jim to studio at 10. 30, \+ spent the day at work with Grover on head of Mr. Q. To lunch at 1.30 \+ back after 2.30 — worked as long as I could see — until almost 4.45 then out \+ walked up 5th Ave. to Central Park \+ on my way back found Dr. Yale in and out of sorts — a cough \+ trouble with the wound from surgical operation a year or so ago — while there Dr. Gerster came in — I wanted to talk with Y— about my own condition but had no chance — Home at 6.30 — very uncomfortable — trouble with stomach — acidity — I ate a very light meal — discomfort increased — a heavy sort of stupor came over me \+ I lay down upon my bed \+ slept. It was near 11 before I got up, undressed \+ went to bed. At 12.30 was routed out by movement of bowels \+ again at 2.30.
