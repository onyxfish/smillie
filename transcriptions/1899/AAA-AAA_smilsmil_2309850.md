## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 11, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another exceedingly cold day — I was able to get up for bkfs: but felt very weak \+ seedy. It was 11 o'clk when I went to Carrère \+ Hastings's \+ was there until near 1 o'clk. going over the drawings for Heene house — The prof. architects have improved my drawings, or designs, from their point of view of symmetry, but I do not approve — They would spoil some of my pet interior effects to get windows or other openings into symmetrical relation from the outside, for which I care little — Then went to studio to look after the fire — then home to lunch — I spent the afternoon in dining room writing up N.A. acts — Evely — Kate Katherine \+ Bessie went to Charles where Emma J— is to dine — Mary followed later — Seny Plumme dened with us \+ he \+ Jim afterwards went to a theatre in Harlem to see "Secret Service" — there is nothing like being young — Ralph \+ I had a quiet eve\+ together.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 12, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold with light snow falling all day — water pipes frozen — \+ general discomfort. All up late for bkfs: I did not go out of the house all day but busied myself with writing \+ reading — among other things I wrote to Branch, Beede's carpenter telling him that the architects computed that I w\[illegible\] require 16000 brick for my two chimnies \+ directing him to buy that number as promptly as possible for me so that they may be hauled before the snow goes.
