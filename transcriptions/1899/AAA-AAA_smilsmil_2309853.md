## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 17, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mild, dull day, ending with rain at night. About 10 o'clk went to C. \+ H's and spent nearly an hour with Mr. Ford \+ Ingalls, The young man who is making the drawings for the Keene house. Then to studio \+ soon to D. B. Butler's \+ the M. A. Home to lunch \- after that to barber's \+ had hair cut \+ head shampoo'd. To studio at 3.30 \- fire out \- rekindled it. The rest of the afternoon I was arranging light moulding frames that I have, to put the newly purchased etching p.p. for the etching class in. Dinner. Evey Mr. Gilles came with a card of introduction from Uncle Willie. He stayed until nearly 10 30

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 18, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright for a little while in the m'g \- then dull, rain p.m. \+ wet. Most of the m'g at studio getting frames ready for etching p.p. for etching class. Near 1 o'clk. went to D. B. Butler's to hurry up some glasses \+ backers. Then to Gas Co. about stoves for boys' rooms. Home to lunch. Afternoon at studio came across an oil sketch painted some years ago \- an attempt to represent a winter twilight \- full moon rising at back \+ casting shadows over snow in foreground \- an effect noted in Central Park. I became interested, set palette \+ worked on it as long as I could see. Dinner. Evey with the boys at home.
