## Left Page

### Tues. April 4, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A much pleasanter day than yesterday — To studio about 10 \+ spent the m'f. drawing in on Zinc plate for etching a portrait sketch of myself — Before 1 o'clk Mr Fredt H. Ecker of Met. Life Ins. Co — called to Talk with me about N.A. matters — He would like to get from the N.A. some sort of a proposition to hire the galleries of the old building for a year or two but I told him that it was not at all probable that the N.A. would make any such proposition. Home to lunch at 1.30 — It was near 3 when I got to Etching class — Stahr \+ Stem there, biting \+ printing — making some wild work — I stayed nearly an hour — then to studio \+ varnished my Zinc plate to be ready for biting to-morrow — then to Roome \+ paid Rent of Etching studio — then to Durand-Ruel \+ saw the exhibit of "Ten Painters" — a very disappointing show. Took a walk \+ home to dinner — Evey at home — wrote to the boys — mailed it — \+ to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 5, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — bright, cloudless \+ breezy — To studio at 10 \+ started biting the head of my self that I drew upon a Zinc plate the other day — Was delayed by having to mend my bellows — Yale sent me a note from the Colonial Press, asking for prices for half a dozen small etched book plates — At noon I went there — 109–111 Fifth Ave, but was not encouraged to hope for much — Mr Browne, having the business in charge, was not in — Then to N.A. \+ home to lunch — P.M. I continued biting — using Dutch Mordant which bites an admirable line but very slowly — I did not get the biting quite finished — Dr. Gerster came in \+ invited me to a stag party at his house to dine on Roast Pig a week from to-morrow night — I walked for half an hour before dinner — Evey podged about \+ to bed early.
