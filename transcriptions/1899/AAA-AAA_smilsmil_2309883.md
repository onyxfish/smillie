## Left Page

### Tues. April 18, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming spring day — at 9 went to Mr. Fred. Jennings' - 86 Park Ave. Cor. 39th St. \+ saw him about form for N.A. subscription paper - then to studio - got Jim's bicycle ready to supp. to him - worked awhile on Crayon head - Then to Adams & p. in 42nd St. - there sauntered down 5th Ave. stopping at different picture places - went to 2nd Nat. Bank. \+ home to lunch at 1.30 - It was after 3 when I got to Etching class - only Sterne \+ Kelly there, biting some of their work - I was with them for more than an hour - then to N.A. - got invitation for Sat. eve'g for M. Jennings - then to studio \+ worked on Crayon head until 6 - then a short walk - Dinner at 6.30 - went with Ralph \+ new paper until 9 - then to Charles where Kate had gone to dine - John Jessup's there - later, Mr. Meyer of Kansas City came in - It was near 11 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 19, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another fine day. To studio \+ much of the m't was spent working over some old printing ink that I have had in a jar for a number of years. It is very hard but still reducible. I think the time was well spent because I have found that such ink has peculiar qualities that I consider valuable - At noon went to N.A. \+ to 2nd Nat. Bank \+ then home to lunch. After lunch I wrote to Branch at Keene, giving him directions with reference to the house he is putting up there for me. He says that the foundation wall is now laid \+ main building \+ most of the digging for basement \+ chimney. At 4.30 I went out, with Ralph, on bicycle - my first ride since last Summer - got along fairly well. In at 5.30 - 6 mile ride - Dinner at 6.30 - Mollie Adams dined with us - I took her to Fred Ireland's at 10 o'clk.
