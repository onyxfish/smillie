## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 1, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

about an inch of snow fell last night — To-day has been brilliant, with a biting cold wind — Ralph's temp. on awakening was normal but he stayed in bed until about 10.30 when the house had got warm. He still complains of occasional pains in the stomach — I worked at home until 11.30 writing notes, making out checks \+ otherwise attending to business. Then I went to studio for a few minutes — then to O'Brien's, the printers in 23? S. — to the N.A. \+ to 2? Nat'l. Bank — Then to W. J. Roome's — 34. S. \+ p? Prof. Rust on Etching Class studio in W. 22?. S. — Then home to lunch — After that, to studio \+ looked over the pkg. of Etching p.p. purchased for us from Baldwin Fund. — Home at 4 — dressed \+ at 5 went to Y. Gov. Morton's House, 681 Fifth Ave — meeting of Ways and means \+ Advisory Com. — Mr. Morton, Schiff \+ W.m E. Dodge of the Alt. Com. — put their names down for $5000 each — That was the most important bit of business accomplished. Home at 6.30 — dinner — every Charl. came in — I gave him check \+ bonds to pay for the Kansas smelting Stock — He was in a state of excitement over his success so far in managing a "deal" with the Arlington wool mills \+ others — a matter involving one hundred million dollars.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 2, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day — Painted on "Capri" all the m'g — it turns out badly — home to lunch at 1.30 — After 3 went to Na. \+ drew check for Nepple. Ralph with me — then to Etching Studio — \+ W. 22?. S.! VanderPoince there — I told him how to arrange things — then R. \+ I went to Alexandre's Shoe store in 6th Ave. \+ got him a pair of shoes — then we went to Nepple's \+ from there home at 6 — Dinner at 6.30 — after that went to studio \+ cleaned up palette — Mr. Mayon came at 8 o'clk and I arranged with him to take charge of Jim \+ his studies at Mr. Morse's school — Mayon is to come three eve'gs in a week giving 1½ to 2 hours each eve'g. at a chr. of $2.00 per hour — to begin Sat. eve'g. — Eve'g. Ralph read a Pyle story to me.
