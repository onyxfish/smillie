## Left Page

### Wed. May 10, 1899

Annual meeting N.A. f.D.

A charming day. went to studio after Bkft. \+ wrote until about 11 — then to N.A. \+ got some figures from the records that I wanted. \+ home at about 12 — met some of the N.A.s who were very anxious to know who sent out the ticket headed by Dielman — of course I knew nothing about it. I had a light lunch at 1 \+ went to N.A. getting there before the Academy lunch was over — meeting called to order soon after 2 o'clk — unusually large number present — 60 I think. Things went as usual until the election of Officers. Then the fight was on — Before the balloting Jno. F. Weir made a speech, much interfered with by Jas Hart as presiding officer, \+ nominated Jno. LaFarge — He was very severe upon Wood — Miller nominated Wood \+ then 1st ballot — Wood led with 26 votes — LaFarge had 7 \+ I think Dielman had 16 — no choice — 2d. ballot Wood lost heavily — LaFarge held his 7 votes \+ Dielman went ahead of Wood then Wood backed out \+ on the 3 ballot Dielman went in on 33 votes, a few more than necessary — LaFarge still held 7. Then for Vice Prt. there was much difficulty in getting a candidate to stand — Beckwith refused — Sodel Needle — at last J.G. Brown consented \+ he went in very much to his gratification — Lockwood DeForest gave his consent to run as Treas — \+ was elected by a handsome vote — my Bogey, Geo. H. Hall, had but one vote — The result was a great relief to me \+ very satisfactory — After that I paid little attention to the proceedings \+ went out \+ walked in Mad. Sq. — Dinner after 7 — a good dinner — the Room hung with Vautine rugs etc. looked very well. — I had Parry on one side of me \+ DeForest on the other, Haseltine in front of me \+ beside him foul-mouthed Dolph — of course there was speaking — Wood made believe that he was glad to believe that he had been defeated \+ thanked the members for it. — Dielman touched him up with whitewash \+ smoothed him off with soft soap — Brown aired himself to his great contentment — he is sure that he is not only the best painter but the most popular as well in the N.A. — Dielman's impromptu speech was very good — After the friction of the afternoon the dinner was very pacific — but aside from that it had no virtues — Home soon after 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 11, 1899

Dull W'f. Rain came on about the middle of the W'f. — I went to studio without umbrella — wrote some letters \+ soon after 11 had to run home through a light Rain — by noon it was falling in torrents — at 12.30 I prepared myself and went to Ralph's school in 42d S. \+ brought him home. During the P.M. weather cleared brilliantly, but a headache came on that made me incapable of work — At 5 walked to N.A. — Granniss gone — home again — Dinner at 6.30 — about 7. a brief \+ very heavy shower — Wm Y. at about 9 Gifford came \+ spent an hour looking over specimens that I have of D.W. fine Water Color paper. I got to bed soon after.
