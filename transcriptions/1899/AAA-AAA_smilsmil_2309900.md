## Left Page

### Wed. May 24, 1899

*Aunt Jessie & Emma arr. from Po'k*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — up at 7 & wrote letter to Orlando Reede before bkft. — Soon after I went to N.A. Lockwood & Forest came in & I spent some time explaining to him the books & system upon which I had kept them — At 10.30 I got to Etching Studio & found Stahr & Sterne with Mr. — as model — She took pose while I was there & the boys commenced their drawings in blk & white — Then I went to 2d Nat'l Bk — then to studio where I laid grounds upon two plates for Stahr & Sterne Home before 1 o'clk — Raefels school mates Bronson Cutting — Gayer & Minnick & Gilbert Browne came by invitation & lunched with him & at 2 o'clk — I went with them to the Broadway Theatre & from very good seats saw Bronson Howard's Spectacular play "Shenandoah" given by a fair to middling company — The boys were amused & so was I — after the play I put the boys on their way home & Ralph & I went to our home — I had a short rest before dinner — Geo & Nellie & Charl dined with us to celebrate Aunt Jessie's birth-day — her 80th, she being born the same day & year as Queen Victoria — except for the crippling of her hands & feet by Rheumatism — Aunt J — seems to be wonderfully well & in good spirits — After dinner Geo & I went to N.A. Special Meeting for amendments to Constitution — providing for a Com. on Finance & declaring that the new officers elected at the annual meeting shall take office on the first Monday in June. — There was no opposition — a meeting was called immediately after adjournment to consider Report of Care Brandts Com. on the advisability of putting up temporary galleries on new site — The debate was spirited but it was decided to defer further consideration — Home at 11 & to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 25, 1899

*Aunt J. & Emma Ret? to Po'k.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day. To studio immediately after bkft., made out checks for the last of the pictures sold & them to N.A. — from there to Etching Class and found Stahr & Sterne at work upon drawings from Mrs. Brunner who was posing for them — then went to Wanamaker's to buy a number saddle — but learned that no more are made — Then to studio & home to lunch at 1 — At 2 o'clk. put Aunt Jessie & Emma aboard a Lex. Ave. car starting them on their way home to Po'k — & then to Studio — Sterne & Stahr soon came & it was 3.30 when they left — they had been studying with much interest a number of Etching Pfs. from fine plates that I showed them. Then I went home — saw Minnie Pierce — & at 4 o'clk Ralph & I went out on our wheels — went to new Academy bkft — went all over it — & then on to Grant's tomb & home at 6 — near 7 when I had dinner — A quiet eve'g. & to bed early.
