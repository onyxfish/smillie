## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 16, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day \- Brilliant moonlight night \- got to studio soon after 9 \- The whole mtt. spent in getting about 30 [illegible] from my students etchings into shape to show at The N.A. house warming next Monday evef. \- A short walk before lunch at 1.30 \- Afternoon I went to Durand-Ruel's \+ saw the Raffaelli's paintings \+ etchings (Heaven save the mark.) I read his aesthetical introduction to his catalogue \+ the absurd praise of his etchings \+ am decided in my conviction that it is all "Rot" \- to use the slang of the day which is quite good enough for it \- Then I went to N.A. 109 St \+ after a careful look at the Etching studio \+ consultation with Davis decided the manner in which I shall put up the [illegible]. Left there soon after 5 o'clk. \+ walked down thro' Central Park \- the effect of the rising full moon on the one hand \+ the sunset sky on the other was very beautiful. \- I walked across to 5th Ave. \+ 98 St. \+ went thro' to Mad. ave. \+ took car at 94th St \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. I went to Edwd Cook's at 8.30 but all were out \- then home.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 17, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- after bkft. made up a package of heavy traveling rug \- Sheldon's tuxedo suit \+ bedroom slippers for Jim \+ had Jennings take it to the [illegible] Office \- Then wrote a note to Jim \- \+ one to Maurice Sterne \- Then to Studio \- Telephoned to Budworth to come to studio to-morrow mtt. for etching [illegible] to be taken to N.A. Then to Fredericks. Sullivan St. for a sale of white paper to use on wall of Etching room \- had none \- went down town \- to Tax Office \+ got Tax bills for house \+ studio amtt. to over $660.!! then to Nassau St \+ got Excelsior Diary for next year \- then to 127 Fulton St. \+ got roll of paper \- then to Studio \- Rolled 6 yds. of the paper on the floor and began to arrange the etching [illegible] not as I wish to hang them \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.30 \- young Russell, from Budworth's came for etchings but I was not ready \- I got framed etchings out \+ cleaned the glasses of some \+ otherwise busied myself until 4.30 \- went out \+ wandered to Knoedles \- just rearranged his store. 34 St. \+ 7 [illegible] ave \- met Miss McChesney there \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. had a good deal of stomach pain but in spite of it I read some Stevenson to Ralph.
