## Left Page

### Thur. April 6, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — cloudless — mild but not enervating — went to Miller, the carpenter, before I went to studio — then continued the biting of etching — between the slowness of the mordant \+ the time waiting for varnish to dry, noon came before I knew it — then to N.A. \+ to Bank where I talked with Cashier Case about a loan to carry on the Keene house — He told me, as Chark did when I went to his house this m'f after 6kfo:, that money was so tight now that I could not get it under 5 or 6 % — which is bad for me — Home to lunch about 1.30 — then to studio \+ finished the biting of etching — worked on the fine with graver — cut down for after for printing to-morrow — then walked up 5th Ave to 67 S: met Geo: \+ walked over to Lexington Ave. with him — then home to dinner — Rec'd a letter from Katey Elkin inviting Jim to continue his visit there indefinitely — Eve'g I wrote reply accepting invitation until I can decide what to do about Jim's continuance at W. Morse's — Katherine Throop told me that some time ago young Jas. Morse told her that Jim really didn't know any thing about Greek — if that is true he had better leave his school — Posted letter before 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 7, 1899

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast — rain commenced about 3 P.m — very heavy, with wind, at night. To studio at 9.30 \+ got my printing dep't. in order \+ took print from portrait etching of self — Took four prints before 1 o'clk. working on plate with drypoint \+ graver between prints — then home to lunch — at 2.30 went to Etching class — Perrine the only one there — no model had been engaged — I was provoked \+ discouraged. back to studio \+ resumed work on plate. More prints \+ more work until 6 o'clk. I have trouble with the nose. Eve'g I wrote a letter to Jim \+ at 10 o'clk went to G.C. Station \+ mailed it — then home \+ to bed.
