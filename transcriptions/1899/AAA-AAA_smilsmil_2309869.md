## Left Page

### Tues. March 21, 1899

**Weather:** Clear most of the day with cold, raw East wind.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear most of the day with cold, raw East wind. I got to studio + finding it uncomfortably cold went to N.A.T. There met Beckwith + Nicoll — was tasked for some time of N.A. business when I determined to go down town, partly for the N.A. + partly for self — went to 2° Nat. + got my Central Pacific Securities + then to Cheyer + Co. Broad S.! I had them stamped accepting the plan of Reorganization — then to Central Trust Co. + saw McHyde — made app't to met him on Friday at 2 to talk of some sort of Security Trust for N.A. then to Lee + Lee for some light on other N.A. matters — then home to lunch at 1.45 It was about 3 when I got to Etching Class — Stahr, Sterne, Keely, Olinsky + a stronger N.A. student there — they were biting the work of last Friday + were so anxious to get prints that I Retd. to studio — ground ink + at 4 Retd. to class with ink blankets — paper — slab of glass + Muller. I was with them until 5.30 — then to studio — fire out — at home to dinner at 6.30. Eve. a letter from W. W. E. Dodge that quite upset me. The effect of the Site Com. Report seems to have been to anger him agg.t the N.A. He says he is sure that if the Advisory Comt. had known of this episode they would not have accepted positions on the Com. + he would not have subscribed to the new bldg. This makes a very critical situation — There must be something that he misunderstands on the could not take such a position. I at once wrote him a note, hoping to let the matter in another light before him + at 9.30 left it at his house I about 9. Dr Wm Elkin + Katy Adams (his wife) came + we had a pleasant call from them until 10.30
Mr Marton gave him — [illegible]

## Right Page

### Wed. March 22, 1899

**Weather:** Rain + fog.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain + fog. Up late + got to studio late. I wrote a supplemental note to Mr. Dodge — + mailed it — at lunchtime I Rec'd from him a note in answer to the one I left at his house last night, written before he Rec'd my note of this m'g. His note to me was very pacifying as he admits making a mistake + promises that I shall go no further. I accomplished little at the studio + got home to lunch at about 1.30 —
Immediately after, I rode down to Kimmel + dicts — 242 Canal St. taking my two plates, "Goldsmith's daughter" + "Gen'l Greene" — There was considerable time lost in preparation for proofs — There Henry Roig took two p/p — from the mez'atint of Gen'l Greene — He was evidently at loss in managing my heavy ground + deep scraped lights — I had to take hold + give the finishing touches on the plate myself. He had better fortune with the other plate — but it was after 6 before I started for studio with four p/p. There I tacked them on boards and got home to dinner at 6.50 — Mr Adams dined with us + went with Katherine + Bessie to Opera in the ev'g — I did some reading + got to bed about 10 o'clk —
