## Left Page

### Saturday, April 20, 1867

Morning overcast and threatening Rain. At Studio a little after 8 o'clk. Had a regular sweep out and dust up. At 10.30 went to last Phil. Rehearsal — There met S.C.7. & a Miss Dwight, walked with them as far a Snedecor's — Then home to lunch — At Studio about two o'clk — Last P.M. Reception — very few callers — Lizzie Munroe among the first. A little after 3. S.C.7. & Mrs. Sharpless came went with them through the Studios & at 5.40 went with S.C.7. to Bdway Cars — then home, very much tired, took tea & with Geo. Mary, Matt & Kate started right off for Phil. Concert. Enjoyed immensely the 2d movement in the Jupiter Symphony — the "Bury d'amour" from Romeo & Juliet and Tannhauser — Mills also was splendid — wonderful.

Heavy thunderstorm during the night — clear p.m. but it Rained during the evening.

## Right Page

### Sunday, April 21, 1867

Pleasant day, mild & cloudy. Headachy during the morning, so, until dinner time sleeping in my big chair. Afternoon about my Room putting some drawers to Rights — etc, etc — Every night at Bdway Tabernacle & heard Prof. Hitchcock preach a short sermon from Elijah's exhortation to the children of Israel — "Choose ye this day whom ye will serve — God, or Baal" — Very forcible and practical — The organ blower not at his post at commencement of last hymn — Quartette choir sang one verse & part of 2d without accompaniment — wind at last — organ woke up gently — choir & organ in perfect tune — feather in cap! choir met Mrs. Kimball — Fanny & Will K— walked home with Fanny — went in & had very pleasant call until a few minutes after 10 o'clk P.M.
