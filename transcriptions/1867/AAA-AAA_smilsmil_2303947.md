## Left Page

### Thursday, June 13, 1867

Up at usual time, clear and promise of a hot day — After bkft strolled around until near 9 o'clk — then wrote a letter to Geo & gave it to the stage man at 10, reft to take into town to the P.O. Then set to work outlining on Hartford daf until dinner time 1 o'clk. After dinner again a nap — bad practice. From 2.30 till 5.30 worked on outline — then went out to enjoy a little of the afternoon — Tea at 6 — delicious evening — with birds & flowers and beautiful colored clouds. Mr. E.P. came along about 7 with horse & buggy & took Matt riding — they Ret'd about 9.20 moonlight. glorious. Father sent by E.P. the die that he has just sketched & billen to N.B.N. Co. At tea we had our first Strawberries of the season, fine, large & good flavored — there is some fun living in the Country, altho' this especially aggravating to be without either a horse or a boat. To bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Friday, June 14, 1867

Lazy as usual — did not get up until 6.30. After bkft. & a short ramble — as my feet have been hurting me very much. I gave nearly an hour to them, sitting with them in very hot water while I read Ruskin. think that the effect (if any) on the brain (if any) is good. Then commenced, at last, drawing some flowers on Rosalee Goodman's white wood box — worked until a little after 12, then went down to the River and amused myself until 1 — then home to dinner — After dinner repeated the bad trick of a nap. & at about 2.30 got to work again. Have rather surprised myself at the measure of my success — in water color — on wood — & flowers — all more or less novelties to me — worked until just upon 6 — then a little saunter and tea — After tea went with Matt to the River, at near 7. E.P. his sister Allie & Mr. Darling arr'd in a Row boat from P.B. Matt took a sail with them for half an hour or more when they came ashore, went up to the house while I took the boat & rowed until their return from 8.10 until 9.20 — Much distressed hearing of fearful accident to little "All Right."
