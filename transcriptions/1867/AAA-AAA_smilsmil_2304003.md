## Left Page

### Thursday, October 3, 1867

Awake before day light — throat very sore head all tight from cold — Up at 7, feeling like a "stewed monkey" — Wind blowing again, a young gale — quite cold & cloudy — Geo. & I about in despair concerning our work. During m'g I penciled on "Crest of Chocorua" — but head hurting I went out to walk — wind blowing so hard that I gave it up. — Misses Settlebale & young Bart-lette called about 12 & I saw our studies — Afternoon again worked on "Chocorua" getting it nearly finished — Evening, Geo. Rec'd. a letter from Maud, Divan I was very much used up by my cold, spending most of the eve'g sneezing & groaning — To bed at 10.30 & passed a miserable night —

## Right Page

### Friday, October 4, 1867

Up at 7 — Beautiful m'g, cool cloudless & without wind — but too cold for me to dare to sit out of doors — After b'k'f't took Study of Mt. Washington under snow, down to meadows & made outline of a foreground then Ret'd to the house & sitting at the window Repsainted sky, mountains & ice — Misses Littlehale & cousin gave a call at noon — afternoon I continued painting in same study & got it finished about 5 o'clk — when Geo. & I took a long walk down past Eastman's & over a wood road through pine & birch woods — very much charmed by the sober colors & grays — My cold is still very troublesome unfitting me for work completely. Evening until after 10 o'clk playing Euchre with Mr. Parsons & daughter — Then to bed —
