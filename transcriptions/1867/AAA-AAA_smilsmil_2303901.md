## Left Page

### Saturday, March 9, 1867

True to itself, the day can hardly be called pleasant —
made engagement with White for this p.m.
Mrg., went to Phil. Rehearsal &
enjoyed the orchestral pieces
leaving at 11.20. Went to Snedecor's
& Schauses then up to Studio —
Put things somewhat in order &
at 1. went home to lunch — 1Pet2
to Studio at 2.10. A slight flurry
of Snow. In spite of weather, quite
a number out. Gave to Mr. Jas Frazer
my Sketch of Street Car over crowded.
Throop called at 3.30, went with him
to White's the Dentist — There over father
under the influence of nitrous Oxide gas
he had all of his upper teeth (16) out.
Operation successful, altho' the supply
of gas fell short. At 4.30 Pet2 to Studio
there until 5.30 then home — at 6.15. with
Geo. went up to Diven's — took Fan & Maud
to Phil Concert. Enjoyed immensely
Beethoven's 2d Symphony & Liszt's "Tasso" —
also Chopin's 2d Concerto for Piano & orchestra
with Mills at Piano. After — "Delmonico's" —
no Rain, plenty of mud — home at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Sunday, March 10, 1867

Most miserable sort of a day —
wind & Rain from N. E. — Father
in bed with Packing head ache, his
old trouble — but up at 4 o'clk & for the
rest of day & eveg., very well. —
Went to Hastings' church in
the mrg. — Excellent discourse
upon Parable of the man without
wedding garment — "For many are
called, but few chosen" —
Afternoon, slept for two hours —
Dr. Throops here to tea & in the
eveg., and part of the time,
Kenneth Wyckoff —
