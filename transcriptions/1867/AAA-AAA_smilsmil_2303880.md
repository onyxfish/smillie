## Left Page

### Saturday, January 26, 1867

Snowing when I got up — but soon
tired of that, & all day, except
that it was quite cold, it was
more like an April day, than
mid-winter — now all clear & bright
now all clouded over — Mrs
at 10 o'clk went to Uncle G's & got
my teeth — they are much imp'd
— then to Phil. Phil. Rather tedious
as I missed 2d & part of 3d movement
of Symphony — but enjoyed "Fingal's
Cave" — met S. B., & Emma Richards —
walked to 22d St. & 6th Ave. met D. B. —
Right home & worked hard all p.m.
finishing "Gaucho" — after all my
work, find so much in it that is
wrong that I will make a new
drawing. Ev'g Phil. Concert met
Dorman Fam. M. & Amanda D. Nannie
Pleasants — Bessie Riley & lots of
other friends. Irelands, Kimballs
etc etc. Enjoyed Concert medium
Concertos (1 for violin & 1 for piano)
bore me — these no exception —
Throop walked home with Mary.

## Right Page

### Sunday, January 27, 1867

Clear, cold & very windy day.
Mrs went to Hastrup's church
was very much pleased
Subject — "Christ within one,
is life." Met there Will and
Joe. Afternoon, among other
things, must record that I did
violence to my conscience by
stretching a piece of paper
on which to make a drawing
to-morrow. Every did but
little better — wrote a letter
to Addie Dunning — did not
finish it — Throop took
Mary to church —
