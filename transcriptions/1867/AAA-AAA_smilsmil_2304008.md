## Left Page

### SUNDAY, OCTOBER 13, 1867.

Another dismal, dreary day —
Up at 7 — Indulged hopes most of
the m'g that it would clear up —
As far as the clouds lifted showed
a good sprinkling of snow on the
mts. My cold better, but I did not
dare to go into a cold church —
walked along the Androscoggin
on the Berlin Road a mile or more
— Scenery Rather bleak — Back
+ took dinner at 12.30 — Squalls
passing over Randolph Mts. all day —
drizzling over us at about 3 P.M. —
After Reading + writing some, wrote
a long letter home to Mary —
Evening spent hopelessly, doing
nothing much in particular —
To bed at 10.30 + slept well during
the night.

## Right Page

### MONDAY, OCTOBER 14, 1867.

Drove 42 miles today —

Up a little after 6 — bkfst at 7 — a
heavy white frost over everything — At
20 m to 9 got started, wind very strong +
cold from N — Clouds flying + clinging to
tops of mts. From Randolph hill, scene glorious
all of the mts covered with snow — got sketch
of Madison + others — fine views of Carter notch
— gravated all the m'g by clouds that just
hid the Summits — but the gorges in the snow,
half light + sunlight — grand — Road horrible
until we turned on to Jefferson Hill Road —
Reached Waumbeck H.o. at 11.50 — Drove on to
Jefferson Mills (12.40) hoping to get dinner, could
not — on to Whitefield — 2.45 — got dinner —
From Bray's Hill, before reaching Whitefield
the White Mts were magnificent — Clouds
almost all gone — day perfectly delicious —
got a sketch — also one of Franconia Range
At near 4 started for Bethlehem — had
some tip-top views of Lafayette and on
ascending the hill going into Bethlehem — had a sight of the White mts
beautiful beyond description — niti-
ous a cloud + all Rosy in the afternoon light, we watched them until
we Reached Dinclairs Hotel just as
the sun set at 5.30 — Superb moonlight — my trouble in the head seems
to have gone, as if by magic — To bed
at 10 o'clk + slept soundly all night.
