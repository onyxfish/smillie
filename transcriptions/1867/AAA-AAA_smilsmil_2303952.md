## Left Page

### Sunday, June 23, 1867.

Again a charming day — at about
9.20 started for town on foot, getting
in to church 10 m or so before service
but feeling decidedly uncomfortable —
went to 1st Cong. + heard — Corning. Matt
XIX — 13–15. and was interested in a sermon
concerning infant baptism — He was
especially severe on those who hold
the dogma of original sin — Said
that Christ had no water — merely a
laying on of hands — Rite was not prac-
ticed in the early history of the church —
+ that it was a great mistake to consider
it a legitimate successor to the Rite of
circumcision — thought that it was a beautiful
dedication — should be the spontaneous
desire of every parents heart + should be
practiced as such — The discourse, while
interesting, did not impress me as profound, or
thorough — Went to Dr. Hasbroucks + took dinner
at 4. P.M. Called on the Fonda girls — there
until 5.40 — met again to Dr. H's — took
tea — at 7.30 he got up his horse + buggy +
took me home — went to bed at 10 o'clk
feeling quite sick, after an uncomfortable
day — Some of my old fashioned qualms
have got hold of me.

## Right Page

### Monday, June 24, 1867.

Charming day, but very warm. Up late
got a late bkft. nearly 9 o'clk — No paper
(news) until eve'g. Got started on "Sunset,
from under the Hemlocks." 7×9 — Have an
idea in it that pleases me + as I have been
able somewhat to catch their color, think
I will keep the sketch until next winter
when I will paint a picture from it.
About noon Mr. Roosevelt sent me an
invitation to play croquet, take tea + row
with him this eve'g. Hurried through with
my sketch + at 4.30 went to his house — The
lawn — shadows, fountain — air — delicious —
two games — he won one — I, the other — brightly
sparkling river at 5.30 with hardly a cloud
overhead — tea at 6 — his mother in town — his wife
doing the honors — first time that I have met her —
a little after 7 went down to the River — each
in single shell — pulled down to Po'keepsie —
everything glorious, sky, air + water — pulled
home at near 9. to bed at 10 — but overheated
or over excited, slept very little during the
night — Rain began to fall gently
about 3.30 A.M. —
