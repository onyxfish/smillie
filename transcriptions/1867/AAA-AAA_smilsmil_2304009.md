## Left Page

### Tuesday, October 15, 1867

Drove 28 miles today

Was awakened at 5.30 to see the effect of dawn behind the White Mts covered with Snow — Very Rosy sky & very cold blue Mts — Sky clouding over & threatening — Got off for Franconia at 8.15 — over the first hill, got sketch of Lafayette — tremendous long hill down to the valley, then tremendous long hill up to the Notch — Soft half sun light and charming color — Got to Echo Lake about 10.30, got a sketch & drove on to Flume Ho getting there at 11.30 — put up horse & walked to Flume — while there it began to sprinkle — back to house at 1 & got dinner — Codfish, pork & potatoes — after dinner walked to pool — caught in heavy shower — got off at 2.30 — very dark & threatening while passing through the Notch — as we commenced to descend the Mt. the Rain came on & it Rained heavily until we got back to Bethlehem — just before 6 o'clk — had much trouble with our horse — a mean beast & hardly able to get up the long hills — we were well protected from wet & cold & got in very comfortably — To bed at 9.30, pretty well tired out.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, October 16, 1867

Drove 20 miles today
At Crawford Ho. Cottage.

Up before 6 o'clk — weather promising but not clear. After a first rate hearty bkfst, got off at 8.15, but forgetting Thompson's waterproof, had to go back for it, a delay of 20 m. Got a charming glimpse of Mt. Lafayette's Snow Covered Crest, bright against the blue & above the clouds, but about the Mts. it thickened up & we had but a limited view — otherwise, the day was charming — Roads, shocking — cut a mile or more up Fabian's old path looking for Upper Ammonoosuc Falls — couldn't find them. Reached Crawfords at 12.15 — ordered dinner & walked to the gate of Notch & back — dinner at 1.65 — & at 2 started to walk up Mt. Willard — walked very slowly & reached top in about an hour — The Sensation of the trip so far. Without being a subject for a picture, it was exceedingly impressive — got a poor sketch & rode to house at 5.45 — Large house all shut up — 2 men & 2 women in the Cottage — 2 horses in the stable — but no way to go up Mt. Washington — Such stories of dangers & snow & ice are told us — as we are now in a great hurry, we have given it up —
