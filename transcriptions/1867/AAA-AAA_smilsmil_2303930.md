## Left Page

### Monday, May 6, 1867

Up early but feel very slow. Day uncertain what to do —
At last, clears off rather sullen-
ly — tried to commence painting
upon 10½×18 for Augers — but
could not. Went out into Dark
& sat in the sun — then to N.A. of D.
& Fredecors & Schauss — Home at
1 — Afternoon felt better — out-
lining 3 or 4 subjects for [illegible]
on Mill boards 6×10 in. Toomey
with Biddy came — then Uncle A —
At 5 — I went over to N.A. of D —
there met Misses Woodworth & May
Diven — I stayed until 6.45.
Evening in at Wills for a little
while — Home early & to bed
shortly after 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, May 7, 1867

Overcast day — morning until 11 o'clk
outlining small subjects for oil
sketches — then set palette & com-
menced 10½×18 "Storm coming" for
Augers. Home to lunch at 1 —
worked until after 5 P.M. getting
in all the sky & mountains — Uncle
Willie, Aunt Agnes & Belle from Ottawa
came this m[or]n to stop. Evening at
6.30 Sam. Coleman came to house —
took tea with me in my own room —
at 7.30 we went down to the Studio
monthly meeting of Water Color Socy
in my room — Tremendous Rain.
not many out — Exhibition next
fall discussed — Some wishing it
in connection with N.A. of D. Fall
exhibit — others wishing independent.
Burling, Fredericks & Self a committee
to get information — Hooper, Anthony,
Fenn, Fredericks & Burling at Studio
until midnight — then went home
through heavy rain, smoked out
and tired out — To bed at 1.50
& asleep, maybe an hour after.
