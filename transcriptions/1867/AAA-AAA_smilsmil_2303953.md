## Left Page

### Tuesday, June 25, 1867.

Up late — Steady Rain — wrote letter to Father & sent in to town to P.O. Got Pa at 10.45 — Read it — & Ruskin — set palette & got "Inlight" from a suggestion near Manhattanville all ready to begin to paint upon at dinner time 12.30. The sketch is 6×10 — at 1.30 commenced painting and at 6.30 declared the sketch done. I think it about as good as the rest. The landscape has too strong a Reddish or brown tendency while the Clouds in the sky are too strong in color. I hope that H— will think he has got $15.00 worth. No Rain after 3 o'clk — We found 2 young Chipping birds on the ground — had fallen out of their nest in a rose bush 12 feet — on ground in Rain 8 or 10 hours at least — still living — held them in my hand until they got quite lively — no old birds around — put one in another Chipping birds nest & the other in a wrens nest, both right by the house — both birds appeared to accept the orphans — I will watch them with interest. To bed at 10 o'clk & slept soundly until 7. To-morrow

## Right Page

### Wednesday, June 26, 1867.

Got up with a headache that has made me uncomfortable more or less all day. The day pleasant between 5 & 6. P.M. overcast — Letter from Father & Mary, in the m'f. Mother went into town by Mrs. Stage & Pete at 2 P.M. After getting my paper from 11.40 train, spent rest of m'f on sofa. After dinner — stretched a couple of 8×10 canvases — & setting my palette about 3 & painted, in about 2½ hours, 7×9 in sketch "Near the Clouds" — this now better than it was. Worth, I hope, $15.00. My little orphan Chippkitties appear to be thriving — I cannot see the one in the wrens nest — but all appears to be going well. At 7 o'clk, by appointment, went to the river & met Jn. P— went out in single shells — he pulled down to Kaal Rock about half way — as I felt unlike work & heavy mist or almost drizzle came on at a few minutes after 8. I came in in quite a little shower — J. P. came in at 8.30. Home ¼ before 9 & to bed a few minutes after.
