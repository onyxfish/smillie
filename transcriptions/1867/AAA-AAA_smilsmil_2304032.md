## Left Page

### Monday, December 2, 1867

Disagreeable m't., dull tired
heavy, promising snow, which
came about noon — just enough
to cover the ground — then tried
to clear up. All day at work
upon my sky — altho' I had
determined to commence a
new drawing — Colman disua-
ded me, and I am trying to
recover what has been done.
Home to lunch at 1.30 — back
to Studio through smushy
snow — Clearsky immediately of
P.M. Spent working over m't.
Eve'g, Mr. Wm. Main at house.
Mat Mills for a little — got Rece.
cards — Eve'g spent getting them
stamped with initial S. — then
directing envelopes to friends
\+ writing a few notes — among
others, to E.J. Woolery, esq. — Mr.
Main stayed with us all night.
I did not get to bed until
after 1 o'clk A.M., very
much tired out.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, December 3, 1867

Lowering day — threatening, but
doing nothing — Off to Studio before
9 o'clk. Stopping at a number of
places to leave cards for our
Reception next Sat. When I got to
the Studio found Geo. + Mr. Main there
ahead of me — Most of the morning
used up distributing tickets to the
fellows in the building + hearing
their several complainings and
advisings — Conclusion — When
a fellow is found to be a willing worker,
the others all load him + then kick him.
Home to lunch at 1.30 — Afternoon
spent trying to get a tracing of Photo.
of Boston — to make a drawing for
A.B.N. Co. — Before 5 o'clk left Studio
\+ on way home distributed more tickets —
The same on way down to meeting of
Water Color Soc'ty at Dr. Haas's Room —
a pretty full + spirited meeting —
Especially in the money way.
Rec'd dues from a number of the
members, Returning with over
$160 in my pocket — To bed at 11.30
