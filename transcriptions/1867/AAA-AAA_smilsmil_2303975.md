## Left Page

### Thursday, August 8, 1867.

Up about 6, after a disturbed night — my
mist got to itching so at one time that I
thought I would go wild — By advice of
young Haven I got some bean leaves and
tearing & breaking them up, applied them
with Relief — Eat a light bkfst. being still
unsettled about the stomach — After some
misgivings, determined to keep on with my
study. So at 9 o'clk took my seat in the
midst of the swg, but careful to touch
none of it — painted in the water. — Thunder
storms threatening — after dinner — about
2 o'clk the Kimball party — 15 in all — arrived
at the Kearsarge Ho — sent right over for
us — my arm in such condition that I
could not work — discharging freely a thin
watery humor — Spent the p.m. with the
Kimballs & Cooks — They have just made a
general tour of the mts. Every, a hop,
at the Kearsarge Ho — just took a look in —
quite gay — got to bed about 11 — Mr.
Mrs K — occupied adjoining Room — not
a Room at Hotels — town Running over
full. Every walked with & to Mr[s].
Stuart on to hill back of Champneys.

## Right Page

### Friday, August 9, 1867.

Up before 6 o'clk — the Kimball party
got off at 6.40 — Skies heavily overcast —
almost threatening Rain — Cleared up
just before noon — Saw Dr. Marceau of
N.Y. at Kearsarge Ho & asked his advice
concerning my arm — now very much
swollen & discharging very freely — the
eruption has extended almost all the
way around my arm & from the wrist
half way up to the elbow — Dr. M. tells
me to keep quiet — out of the sun and
patiently to let it run its course — As
whatever I do with my arm irritates
it, I have followed his advice to day &
remained in doors — Young Curtis &
his cousin left this mng — So Joe & I are
the only masculinities in this Spinsters'
Retreat — Every took a walk beyond
McMillan's with Miss Helen Haven, then
back & spent the rest of even[ing] on [illegible] —
