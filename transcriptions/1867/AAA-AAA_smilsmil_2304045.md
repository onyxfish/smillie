## Left Page

### Saturday, December 28, 1867.

Early mf overcast, but soon cleared off charmingly — Got up with a heavy head that bothered me through the day, at times quite using me up for work — In the mf, painted a 3? sky on 7×9 sketch". Mt. Lafayette, from Echo Lake." Got back from Pilleck & went home to lunch at 1 o'clk. Ret'd at 2 & found young Bell waiting for me to give him a drawing lesson. Morse the drawing master & his wife called — Got Haseltine's sketches off to him — & finished 7×9 sketch — Took a nap on the lounge, from 5 until 6 o'clk & then home to tea, feeling better — Uncle A. & Tommy there — great excitement over a soiree at Dodworth's to-night — At 7.45 Gio & I with Dining, went to J. Wrey Mould's where we heard Hayden's Symphony in D, Ruy-Blas— Simarimas with several other pieces on 2 pianos — Met Mr. Powell and heard his daughter sing — also J. R. Thomas in a buffo song — Arouled on his violin & Tiyton, violincello — Music over at 12 m — then supper — the whole thing quite, unique & some things very funny — Mould's parlors, for instance, no carpets on the floor — nails & wooden scribbied over & drawings tacked about — Home at 1 o'clk AM. & to bed at once.

## Right Page

### Sunday, December 29, 1867.

Fine clear & seasonable day — Up rather late, but to church, (Hastings) early — Enjoyed the music & heard a fine Christmas sermon from Luke 2nd chap. 15th verse. After service, met with Charl up to Uncle Aaron's — Got there just in time for dinner — After dinner, as my head still troubled me, took a little nap — then until sundown wandered over the place, enjoying intensely beautiful effects of form & color — Sky clear — distance, purple & orange — tree forms, clear, crisp and delicate — Relieved in p.m., sunlight golden & brown — At f. Charl & I walked down B'way to 59th st — at lower end of road getting much mud — At 59 st. took 1 cars home — Tea & discussion with Dr. Throop concerning "Revivals" — Then to Thompson's & heard him preach from 1 Chronicles 1st chap, 1 v. "Adam, Seth, Enoch" — 3 typical names — At first rather heavy to me, but probably my own fault — Then home and to bed at 11 o'clk. P.m.
