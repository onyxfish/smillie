## Left Page

### Thursday, October 17, 1867

Drove 25 miles to day —

Up at 6.30 o'clk and had [illegible] at —
mrs. delightful — after talking over
the possibilities of getting up Mr. Wash-
ington, we finally gave it up. Reluct-
ly of course + at 9 started down the
Notch, home. Nothing can describe
our wonder and pleasure. The col-
was marvelous — the greys of the
cliffs — the Russets of the foliage
+ over all that fine October atmos-
phere — Gel + I could not find
vent for our happiness — I took a
couple of sketches in the Notch +
a little after 1 o'clk Reached
Stilling's where we took dinner — at
2.30 we started on — enjoying as
much, without the wonder — the valley
of Upper Bartlett — we drove over the
Humphrey's Ledge Road, crossing
Conway Meadows at 5 o'clk — Just
as the sun was setting — a Royal Cl[oze]
to a Royal trip — Was much pained
to Receive a paper from home
announcing the death of H. P. Fessenden
— a letter from Snedicor telling me that he had
sent money by [illegible] — but no money to be found —
Finish bed + sent letter home.

## Right Page

### Friday, October 18, 1867

A perfect Oct. day. of the mild and
intoxicating kind — I slept late, as I
was tired + did not have [illegible] until
near 8 o'clk. Spent most of the day
getting things together + packing —
Have been much mortified and
vexed by the delay of my money, now
9 days since it was sent from N.Y.
I have had to make arrangements
with Thompson + Eastman, to whom
I owe money — at 3.30 had everything
Ready — had my good byes said +
at 4.10 bid a sad adieu to North
Conway — It never looked to me more
enticing, bewitching — The hills + mts.,
Everything was of wonderful color —
It seemed to me that I never saw so
much before that was Charming —
we were driven down to Conway Cor, to
take Stage to Portland to-morrow. [illegible]
as we approached Conway Cor. the sun set
behind Chocorua Peak — until 6, we
walked about much delighted with
all that we saw — after tea nine
about until a little after 9 where I
went to bed — The day has been very warm.
