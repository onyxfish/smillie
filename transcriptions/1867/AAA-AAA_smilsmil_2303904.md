## Left Page

### FRIDAY, MARCH 15, 1867.

Glorious day, but cold —
Mrr. got started on Hillyer's
Paving again, with charcoal,
so that it was 11 o'clk before I
commenced to paint upon
"Hudson above Pokepsie (7th day).
Home to lunch at 1 — Maud
Diven at Studio on my return
at 2. Painted over water &
distance — entirely out of kep-
ing with sky or anything else —
disgusted — but can't do any
better — encouraging — At
5 o'clk went to Durand's (Fred)
Schaus's, & Snedecors — While
away Hennessy called at
the Studio. Evening
wrote to Uncle Willie.

## Right Page

### SATURDAY, MARCH 16, 1867.

Raw cold morning. by noon
Snow falling — continuing steadily
during the rest of the day & night.
Mrs. Young Bell came to Studio
& took lesson & paid me another
$20.00. Painted (18th day) upon 16×22
"Hudson River view". niggling over the
detail of distance & water painted
yesterday not yet dry. Putin
tug-boats, sloops, etc. Home to
lunch at 1 o'clk. Afternoon, in
spite of the storm, quite a number
of visitors, but Geo. & I worked on.
Meta Javit, lady friend & Charly
Adams came in about 4 o'clk
& visited with us for about an
hour. Evening, not feeling well,
but sat up until after midnight
Reading Chas. Reade's "Good Fight"
