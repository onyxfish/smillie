## Left Page

### Saturday, November 30, 1867

A clear, almost cold mrg.
& windy — by the afternoon it
became quite squally, cloudy
over & clearing off — & giving us two
little snow flurries — At night
very cold — All day hard at
work at my drawing, very much
discouraged — In spite of me
I cannot overcome the tendency
to a disagreeable purple —
Swain Gifford & Burling called
in the mrg — Bell came at 1 o'cl
& took his 3d lesson — I went
home to lunch at 2 — Every
in at Wills for a few minutes
& got print of Boston to make a
drawing for A.B.N. Co — Then to
Studio at 9.30 with large drawing
board — back at 10 — Sat and
Read awhile — then to bed
at 11.30

## Right Page

### Sunday December 1, 1867

Glorious day, quite cold, clear
bracing — Mrg. went to Rev. Hastings
ch, and heard what I knew, & at times
to be an excellent discourse from
Rev 7ch.17 V. I was particularly annoy-
ed by my mind wandering in spite of
me to the work of the week and plans
ahead — Immediately after service
walked up to Uncle A's — through
the Park, doing it in one hour & 38 m.
Will & Joe went up there to stay
until tomorrow mrg. About 4.30
started for home, walked down the
5th ave & did it in one hour & ten m.
3 miles for my afternoon walk —
Everg. with Gel. & Charl. went to
Bdway Tabncl to hear Newman Hall
who sails for home next Wcl. The
crowd was tremendous & only by dint
of persistent squeezing did we get
inside of the door — Heard an animated
discourse from "For God so loved the
world" etc etc" — aimed particularly
at the doctrines of election — pre-
destination — etc — etc — Had a run
home with Charl at 9.30 & then
right to bed.
