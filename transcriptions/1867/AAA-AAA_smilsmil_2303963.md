## Left Page

### Monday, July 15, 1867

Up about 6 — charming fresh m[orning] —
Finished my letter to Emma Ireland
& wrote notes to Alfred Jones & H.A. Loop —
Matt went into town by 10 o'clk stage —
I went down at 11 to R.P. for pasees sa[id]
J.R. & his wife at boat house — Painted
6×10 — Autumn — "Over the Hills in Pennsylvania"
for uncle Willie — In my desire to get
glow & transparency of color I have
painted too thin — so that on looking
closely it looks glazey & unsubstantial —
Altogether, though, I think it strong & as
good as anything that I have done here —
A little before 5 had another regular try
getting ice out of ice-house & up to ice-box
in house — at least 400 lbs — thoroughly not thoroughly —
tea at 6 — & at 7 went out in shell with J.R. —
3/4 of a mile & met Van Kleek & Johnston — Pet[?] to
R's & then on again to P. — tide against us, but
grand pull back — great old moon — & water — fine
pictures — in at 9 — after a pull of 7 miles — found
Willie Aspinwall camping out on the point, his
boat in the cove — little shelter tent & serving
boy with him — W.A. — Maybe about 15 yrs of age —
Matt came home in 3 o'clk stage — Even
George went to Mrs. Beach's — visited an hour —
I sat in at 9.30 & to bed after 10 —

## Right Page

### Tuesday, July 16, 1867

Overcast with slight sprinkle of rain
in the m[ornin]g — Clouds at times lifting, then com-
ing again — Bet. 3 & 4. quite a mizzle — then it cleared
giving us a most glorious evening — worked all
day finishing up half a dozen or so of sketches —
At 5.30 cleared up & a few min. before 6 had
tea — After tea went to River to take my
share in a little boat race between J.R. —
& Bailey, one of the Harvard Scientific
crew, this year — Roosevelt's Point, quite gay —
the R. family & many friends, horses & boats
making a picturesque crowd on the rocks
& under the trees — Rev. Hegeman of P.O. — one of the
judges — Rev. Corning in J.P's new boat — I in
Comings old one — two or three boats up from P.O. —
About 7.15 at the firing of a gun they got off — J.R.
pulling splendidly — Bailey flustered & spatter-
ing — Corning joined the race & came in 3 min.
ahead — J.R. beat B. amiably but a boats length — time
26 m 54 sec — distance 2½ miles — after the Race
J.R. B. Corning & I pulled over the course again — it
was glorious — full moon — twilight — rippling water —
Catskill Mts — & Hudson Highlands — in at about 9.30 —
went up to R's — house met Mr. Hegeman's family —
Miss [Joey] Jewett also "mosqueto" — At 9.30 left — met
gel at our gate & with him walked to Beach's brook —
met Matt & E. Parker out riding — back aft[er]
pulling & enjoying to the full the glorious view —
to bed at 10.45 — and a night of very sound sleep —
