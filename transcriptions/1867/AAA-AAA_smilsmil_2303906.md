## Left Page

### Tuesday, March 19, 1867.

Another cloudless day —
At 9 o'clk. A.M. took my seat
in Fred Durand's chair — had
a small filling put in one of my
lower front teeth — left at 10.15 —
went to Snedecor's — then to Studio
— near 11. A.M. set palette & got
to work on "View on the Hudson"
(10th day.) Home to lunch at 1 —
P.M. continued to paint over
R.h. corner, but progressed very
slowly — have felt very miserable
all day — my stomach is in a
very qualmish condition —
Feel discouraged and at
times think of giving up my
trade & going to stock-breeding.
Eveg. at home charcoaling
some ideas for Hazeltine's
sketches — To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, March 20, 1867.

And yet another cloudless day —
Mrg. spent half an hour or so
outlining in Pets Turfs — threw
three Hazeltine sketches, then
set to work (11th day) upon "View
on the Hudson" — painting over the
foliage in R.h. corner — Feel
miserably sick & have hard work
to stick to my easle. Home to
lunch at 1 o'clk — At 4 o'clk.
Kate Smillie came to Studio &
with her I went to White and
Glenny's, where under the in-
fluence of Nitrous-oxide gas she
had two back teeth put. Ret'd
to studio, cleaned up & then home.
Eveg. went to National Academy
of Design. There until 10.40
looking over books — Home at
11 and then to bed.
