## Left Page

### Tuesday, April 16, 1867.

Steady down-pour of rain until between 4 & 5 o'clk P.m.
Got up at 9. a.m. used up with a headache — got through toilet
operations & breakfast, when I spread myself out for the
rest of the day. In my big chair until 4.30 when I
prevailed upon myself to take something to eat.

Spent about reading and writing until after mid-
night — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, April 17, 1867.

Up early and off to studio —
weather threatening — Rain before
noon — then threatening again.
Worked all day (10th day) without
the first glimmer of success, on
sheep in 14×18 "Seeking the Shade"
At night disheartened — went
with Charl to Academy of Mus.
& heard Zampa — First time that
I have been in the Academy since
the Re-building — Effect not pleasant
to me — flimsy & barn-like — Scenery
stage effects — shameful — Felt
as I always do at the close of an
opera — wonder if grown up people
really are amused — it amuses
— why? The finale — the earth
swallowing Zampa & his Marble
Bride, with the eruption of Mt.
Vesuvius — very funny — Trap-
doors & Roman candles — sport
for children — Home at 11 o'clk
glorious moonlight night —
To bed a few minutes before
midnight.
