## Left Page

### Saturday, August 10, 1867

Up bright & early to bid good bye to the
Havens — mother & daughter & Miss Sarah Thompson
— off in the Centre Harbor Stage — Wt. as
usual, overcast until near 10 o'clk —
when it cleared off not very unusually
hot people here say — my arm more trouble-
some than ever — discharging very freely.
Spent the mrt watching thunderstorms
moving over the mts north — After-
noon, wrote to Charl. Matt & Ja. Hart.
New boarders came — Mrs. & Miss
& Miss Williams — am not conscious
of anything stirring within me —
Wind changed to N.W. and it came off
10 or 12° Cooler — Went with several of
the girls & helped weigh them — got
Chocolat and Caramels min made.
Miss Grausnер played for some
time on the piano — So the eveng
went until near 11 when we got to
bed.

## Right Page

### Sunday, August 11, 1867

Superb day — one among a thousand
and literally it has been altogether lovely —
but I've been real blue about my arm —
Pains like a scald & looks shocking —
& discharges so freely that everything
put about it is saturated in a short
time — Spent the morning on piazza
after dinner I went to Kiarsarge Ho,
& saw Dr. J. M. Marceaux of N.Y. went to his
room & he examined my arm — Says it is
running the usual course, but slowly & ad-
vises Elm bark poultice & afterward "Black
Wash" — a preparation of Mercury & Lime water
— went to the Dr & got some bark — then with
some of Thackeray's Essays that Mr. Loring
lent me, spent most of the p.m. in the grove
near the house enjoying myself very much,
having been so reassured by Dr. J. M. M. After
tea walked again to the Dr with my
prescription, found him in — promised the
lotion to-morrow — then Jed and I walk-
ed on beyond the Intervale House enjoy-
ing the glorious moonlight — Every
yard of the road seemed to have its
picture — home about 10 & then to bed,
with a bark poultice on my arm.
