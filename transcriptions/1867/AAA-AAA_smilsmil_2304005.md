## Left Page

### Monday, October 7, 1867

Up at 7 — Mf charming & had almost determined to start on our trip to the Mts but about 10 o'clk the wind began to blow, continuing to blow harder until the p.m. when it blew a young hurricane — at 10 I took paper & pencils — took a sketch of Moat & Lodge from Poudells Road, then went on, past Mrs. Kith's, to Kiarsarge village, then home through the woods; called Cathedral & Forest Sanctuary — a glorious walk — After dinner went to meadows & got on time on Academy board of Mt. Kiarsarge but wind so high that I could do nothing — Called on Mr. Champney he was out — then home — Spent the Ev'g Reading Ruffini's "Vincenzo" about 9.30 Stage arr. bringing Mr. Bemis — To bed about 11 — awake during the night with head all stuffed up so that I could hardly breathe — It seems impossible for me to shake off this cold —

## Right Page

### Tuesday, October 8, 1867

Up rather late owing to my Restless night — Mf again fine & again almost determined to start for the Mts but my cold is still so severe, unfitting me for enjoyment — the Mts clouded — wind cold & by 10 o'clk again growing to a gale — Gel has some sketches to make & I wish to paint a sketch of Kiarsarge — we determined not to start today — I kept the house for my cold's sake, & painted a sketch 9 × 12 of Kiarsarge, trying to get the effect as I have been b of Sunset upon the mt — worked until after sunset, producing but a weak effect. At 7.30 Gel & I went to the Kiarsarge Ho. to bid the Littlehales goodbye — they go to Boston to-morrow — Pleasant visit, with invitations to call on them at 44 Bowdoin St. Returned at 9 o'clk & for an hour & a half Read Vincenzo — Got through the night suffering the usual amount from my stuffed up head.
