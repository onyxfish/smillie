## Left Page

### Thursday, September 5, 1867

Up at 6.30 pleasant morning —
Exercised myself about until
about 10 o'clk A.M. when, doing up
my lunch I started for the day —
Finished the painting in of large
Moat Mt. Study, worked on it from
11 until 3 o'clk — Lovely color, extr
hazy atmosphere — enjoyed my
lunch very much as I lay on
the ground under my umbrella
watching light & shadow on Moat —
Louis Tiffany surprised me at
3 o'clk — just arr'd from Crawford H.
At 3.30 went to study on River —
Clouded over — but worked without
sunlight until 6.30 — Almost dark —
very much tired when I got home.
Every Jel. + I went to the Mansarge
\+ spent an hour with Tiffany, then
home — Leila Martin called on Mrs.
Hall — Sweedish dance + games —
Rec'd letters from Mary + Throop —
Hinshelwood is bothering Father —
wrote at once to Father — put letter
in P.O. & got to bed after 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Friday, September 6, 1867

Up about 6, after a very Restless night —
Felt fevered, nervous and so generally
out of sorts as to be entirely unfit for
work — think it owing to overwork & having
caught cold. Day overcast — Mrs. f., for a
time played Croquet & sat about the house —
every body making preparations for a trip
to Chocorua — desperate — determined to
go with them — Won George over to join —
All agreed before dinner — dinner at
about 3.30 — m got off — Mr. & Mrs. French —
3 sisters Peirce — Mrs. Hall — Misses Graupner,
Curtis & Littlehale, Messrs. Reed, Crocker and
driver in Eastman's large wagon with four
horses — Mr. Binny, Jel. and I in small
one horse wagon — we made the 14 miles
to Nickerson's in about 3 hours — The Clouds
covering Chocorua, so that we had no
p.m. beauties of approach to enjoy — Had N's
house all to ourselves — only one boarder —
Selected our Rooms — had a poor tea, then
by invitation went to Mr. Cowings & spent an
hour or two — Misses Graupner, Curtis & Littlehale
stay there all night — Back to hotel & to bed
before 10 o'clk — Bombed but had a tolerable
night's Rest — Prospects for to-morrow — dubious.
