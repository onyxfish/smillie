## Left Page

### Wednesday, August 14, 1867

Up a little after 6 o'clk with strong promise of headache — Raining with a look as though it meant it for all day — but thunder before 9 o'clk & soon after breaking up into a cloudy, sweltering fly-sticking dog-day. Part of my Miss G[illegible] playing piano to us — my headache almost leaving me. My arm still my trouble — Kept it in ret pack until afternoon, then as more eruption seemed to be coming out nearer the elbow, and the skin was all off at the mist, did it up dry, a short nap after dinner. Then wrote a letter to Will — Eve'g on the piazza — inspiration + objects of most attention, as usual, the Cats. Much sensation because Gel caught + handed to Miss Green the white cat — by its tail. Miss Kate Peirce especially much moved — about 9 o'clk a sharp shower — To bed at 10.30 and passed a wretched night owing to the general irritation + itching that has come out ever my body.

## Right Page

### Thursday, August 15, 1867

Up at usual time — did not until after — Sky heavily overcast + threatening Rain — About 10 o'clk Gel + I started up the Road walked as far as Pittman's boarding ho. about 3 miles — Saw Mrs. Wm Martin and Cela — visited about ½ of an hour sat, go started to Return — Raining thickly got about a mile on our way back — as far as the "Intervale House" — when the Glen coach overtook us, + getting into it, we Rode home — after dinner a nap — chatting + Reading during the Rest of the P.M. — Every spent with animation, playing "Old Maid," "Muggins," and "Slap-Jack" with cards — To bed at 11 — To day I have had the upper part of my arm, by the mist, done up in a dry hdkf — it is healing — but ½ of my from elbow to mist, where new eruptions are threatening, I have it bound in Cloth wet with solution of Sugar of lead — but the bright red eruptions have spread over me — back, front, arms + legs, and at times set me almost wild with the itching.
