## Left Page

### Monday, August 26, 1867

Delightful day — slept soundly until near 8 o'clk a.m. then bkfst.
About 9. started off for the day with water-color traps & lunch — waded the River & established myself before a couple of fine old willows with the ledges rising purple behind them — got to work about 10 o'clk —
Spent remainder of my making Camera-lucida outline & trashing in the sky — eat lunch at 1 o'clk —
worked all the P.M. until 5.30 very faithfully, never leaving my umbrella all day long. As this is my first water color study, I am very much encouraged — got home at 6.30. Evening spent in games having special reference to the departure of Mrs. & Miss Blood tomorrow m't. Rec'd letters from Will & Father by night mail — To bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### Tuesday, August 27, 1867

Photo. of "Three Elms" & inmates taken at 10. a.m.

called at 5 a.m. but knew nothing until 6 — Just saw, from my window, Mrs. & Miss B — Start off on Centre Harbor Stage — waved my towel to them — got bkfst as soon as I got down stairs. The day clear, but very milky, or grey. Myself, commenced color (water) on study of tree trunk & meadows that I outlined last m't. Worked until after 12, with very small measure of success. At dinner & at 2, a large wagon with 9 or 10 of the girls drove over to Diana's baths — as they passed near to my sketching place on the other side of the River they offered me a ride & I accepted — I got to work on my old willows before 3 o'clk & worked until nearly 6 — but failed to finish the study — as I had hoped — delightful & very charming walk home over the meadows after sunset. Girls did not get home from their bathe trips until about 8 — Evening spent with parlor games until the mail arr'd. I Rec'd a letter from Ogilvie — Had just Rec'd my letter 3 weeks old — To bed before 11.
