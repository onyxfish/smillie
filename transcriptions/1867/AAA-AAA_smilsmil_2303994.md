## Left Page

### Sunday, September 15, 1867

Glorious day. — Morning, went to Congregational Church and heard young Eastman, of this place preach — text: "Come unto me all ye that are heavy laden" etc — etc — Immediately after service Miss Williams, Mrs Hall, Geo & I took a walk through the meadows — Returning just before dinner.
After dinner wrote a letter to Matt — Evening spent in our usual (now) occupation of talking and gazing at each other until 10 o'clk when we went to bed —
This mrg a stove was put up in the parlor & a fire started much to the comfort of all of us.

## Right Page

### Monday, September 16, 1867

Fine morning again, for a wonder. I hardly know what to make of so much fine weather — Went to my ivy bed Study. It was delighted to find everything, temperature, light & color, just right — Set to work on water, & just got everything painted in roughly, when a strong S.W. wind blew up that so increased that I could do nothing — Water that at first was like a mirror — now all in streaks & ripples — Canvas flapping & umbrella almost inside out. Gave up at 12 o'clk much disgusted.
P.M. Early, quite milky — then later clouded over — Took pencils & paper, went across the meadows to the River & got two, careful outline sketches — Spent a part of the evening working with the pen upon one of them. —
To bed at 10.50
Mrs Story or Storer, of Chicago, who has been here about a week, went in the Crawford House Stage this mrg.
