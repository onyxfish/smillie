## Left Page

### Friday, April 26, 1867

Fine day I believe — In bed all day with a miserable sort of a headache — for what reason I can hardly imagine, unless that my head, tired of waiting for an excuse to ache, determined to accept my pleasant evening at the Kimballs as sufficient excuse.

Up at 8.45 P.M. and rolled in my big chair until after 2 A.M., then to bed again.

## Right Page

### Saturday, April 27, 1867

Morning fine — felt rather used up — Jones Bell at the Studio at 9 o'clk & took a lesson — paid me another $20.00 — Spent the m'f. charcoaling subjects for Hazletine's sketches — Rain commenced about noon — At about 12.20 Addie Dunning came in & made us a call of ½ & ¾ of an hour — Home to lunch and back in a heavy rain — Before 4 it cleared off bright. Outlined on a millboard 6×10 in. O. Dunning's Croquet Ground. & put a scumble over sky on little sketch for Addie D. Uncle Mr. Turrell made us a visit. At 6 o'clk went with sketch in frame to 21 Bond St. (Hawes) & left it for Addie. Eve'g part of time Mary & Dr. Throops in my room — warm discussion with Father & all round on the sin of dancing — Father seems to see damnation in every figure of the Lancers while the Polka step is but a graceful way of jumping down to Hell —
