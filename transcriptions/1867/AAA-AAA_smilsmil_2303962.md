## Left Page

### Saturday, July 13, 1867

Clear, cool, & breezy — wind from N.
After 6 &pt. went to River, water quite rough
— took out boat & got oxygenized for the day.
Enjoyed an hour exceedingly, rocking in
the rough water — air like strong wine —
waited for "paper train", then home & to
work about 11. Painted in 7×9 sketch of
old Hemlock trunks & roots at the edge
of the River — J.R.'s cove. In some re-
spects have got nearly what I wanted —
in others, think I see where I have failed
& can do much better at 2d painting.
If I can get it to suit me, will give it
to Samuel Coleman. Back at 7. Father,
Matt, Kate, Gel & I went out in the boat, m
pulled across the River & up along shore, ris-
ing above Beach's — then down. Sunset, truly w
moonlight very lovely — girls singing & father happy
at 8.45 came in J.R. getting out his shell — asked
me to join — did so at once — leaving boat I took
another shell — had a glorious pull of 4 miles —
sky without a cloud — moon near full — as
we pulled, facing the moon — the reflex from
our boats were like dancing flame — wonderful
and enchanting — Running over with pleasure
— in at about 10 o'clk. — got a little chilled
waiting — then a hard run through the woods
up to the house — Did not go to bed until after 11 —

## Right Page

### Sunday, July 14, 1867

Did not get up until 9 o'clk — quite tired out
with the exercise of yesterday. — Glorious
morning — air still cool & bracing — my hasps,
at this threatened with a headache — as I
write this, my feet are in warm water.
Tried a very comfortable lounge on back
piazza & spent all the m[?] there & part
of the afternoon — about 4 o'clk went
up the Cherry Tree, & spent nearly half
an hour there to the great satisfaction
of my stomach — about 5. went to the
River — a little after 6 took tea and
then gave ourselves up to the enjoyment
of a glorious sunset and following
moonlight — Gel walked into town
at 10 a.m. & back about 8 — we sat
for a time on Piazza & sang — So
cool that I put on my Raglan —
& did about 10.
