## Left Page

### Monday, July 29, 1867

Up before 6 — mrf clear, but still
close — Finished my letter home
about 10 A.M. went out on Croquet Ground &
had a game Annie Cook- Oscar J. + me agt. Mr. K—
Will T. + Geo. our side beat. then another game
dropping Miss C. + Mr. K. again we beat — dinner
time — After dinner much discussion
as to what we shall do — It is so warm —
threatens showers + I have but one quite close
with me — so I don't care much to go off on a
tramp — party spreads itself under an apple [tree]
across the road — further discussion — a new
Croquet ground laid out, the question of
"what shall we do" not yet settled — shower
coming — girls don't care — they get ready and
go off in the rain — Some of us remain to christen
the new ground with a game, but were driven off
by the rain that did the Christening itself — After
the rain about 5 P.M. Woods Will K. Geo. + S
started after the girls — found them by a big fire
on a high ridge with a most glorious prospect
before them — Coasting up through Franconia Notch
great storm going over — every ground effect —
a gleam of sunlight across the picture growing
into a broad glory — all of us in ecstacies, as well
we might be — The girls quite wet from their walk in the
Rain, but we got up dry — Shower passed over without
reaching us — nearly 8 o'clk when we got home + had tea
Pretty singing + talking + to bed after 11 o'clk — Slept
like a top.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, July 30, 1867

*on board "Lady of the Lake" — Lake Winnipesaukee.*

Up at 5.30. cloudless mrf. Bkfst at 6.20 —
off in Blair's wagon at 6.50 — adieus from bedroom
windows + balcony — Reached Plymouth at 7.25 and
train started at 7.30 — Reached Miers at 8.30 + went at
once on board "Lady of the Lake" — Reached Wolfsborough
at 10.30. there but 5 minutes + held to Miers at 11.30
The 7.30 train from Boston came up a few min. past 12 + we
went right on to Centre Harbor — Dinner served on boat but
we did not take any — The Lake disappointed me. There are
some beautiful stretches of water + distant Mts. but it is
altogether tamer than I had expected. Reached
Centre Harbor near 1 o'clk — Rushed for Centre House
+ got dinner — After dinner great squabble for outside
seats on the Coaches to North Conway — at last settled —
Geo. on one Coach — I on another — Off at 2.30 — glorious
breeze — old Chocorua peak my pet as I rode
around 3 sides of him — Driver, good-natured, old
customer + outside load quite jolly — At Madison,
nearly 7 — very hungry — while changing horses Rus-
hed into a neighboring house — surprised a woman and
captured a tea — on — quite cold + dark when we
got to Conway — got inside of one of the coaches +
Rode remaining 5 miles to North Conway, getting
in there a little after 9 o'clk P.M. — Got
Room No. 26 at Kearsarge House + turned in at once —
asleep almost before I could get to bed.
