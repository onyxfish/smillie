## Left Page

### Tuesday, November 12, 1867

Driving wind & rain nearly
all day — Still feel much crippled
by the pain in my chest. So spent
the most of the day finishing
up some of my summer pencil
studies — Had lunch at the
studio — Afternoon Charcoaled
on 10×18" "Mt. La Fayette from
Bethlehem Hill" — Evening
at home, expecting Mr & Gavit
word being sent me that he
was to call — but he did not
come — Finished Reading
Hayes' "Open Polar Sea"
To bed at about 11 o'clk. —

## Right Page

### Wednesday, November 13, 1867

Clear, Cold, blustering day — At
studio a little before 9 — Met Gavit
on his way there — he wants me to
make two drawings for the A.B.N. Co
and gave me photos to begin one
Albany, at once — Painted in
5×10 sketch of "Mts Madison, Adams
& Jefferson, from Randolph Hill" — I
intend making a drawing in water-
color of same subject 12×18 in, this
sketch to be my key of color. I
have not succeeded as I hoped
& will have to repaint it to get
nearer to what I want — Isaac Riley
called just before 1 clk — & before he
left I. D. A. Ward — So that I was late
to lunch at home 1 hour. Afternoon
rushing to get my sketch finished, Mr.
J.P. Brevoort gave us a call just
at dusk — At home, father brought up an
etching pl. of Hartford — I stretched paper
for my drawing, then Geo & I, at 8.30 went
to 332 Lexington Ave & called on Mr. & Mrs.
Isaac Riley — Home at 10.30. have suffered
today some unmistakable twinges of
rheumatism — nice work for me. —
