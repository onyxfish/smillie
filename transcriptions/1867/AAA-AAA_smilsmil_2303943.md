## Left Page

### Wednesday, June 5, 1867.

Another very hot day — went to
Studio, but so used up with a
headache that I came home
at noon & went to bed — Sleeping
all the afternoon. Matt
went by boat to Po'keepsie this
m'g. Father & Mother follow
at 5 o'clk. P.M. — I hope to go
up next monday — we will
try to live a Hill Side, which
is but half furnished — It will
be a sort of Camping out —
Evening all of us lying
listlessly about the house —
windows all open & no lights.

## Right Page

### Thursday, June 6, 1867.

Tremendously hot day. Ther. at
Studio 88°. Knees very weak — Tried
hard to paint a 7×9 sketch, from
a pencil study of Evergreen Tree-
trunks for Sam'l Colman — but did
not manage to get it done — was
somewhat demoralized by visit at
2 o'clk of Mr. Jas. Fraser & his friend
Mr. Southwick — Mr. F. says that he
does not like "Passing Shower" 14×20
that I have painted for him, but very
kindly, offered me a check for $100.00
as accomodation until I could paint
him one — without knowing where the
money for my summer trip is to come
from. I refused with thanks — Mr. S.
bought my little Moonlight for $40.00
frame & all — poor compensation for the
loss of $156.00 — Spent the eve'g
Reading Du Chaillu & figuring up to
find how rich I am, in pictures
unsold ($916.00) Money expected ($421.00)
and debts ($1448-.) I am worth
$111.00 less than nothing & am plan-
ning a trip to the White Mts on such a
purse.
