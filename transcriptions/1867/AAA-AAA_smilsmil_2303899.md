## Left Page

### Tuesday, March 5, 1867

Still disagreeable. Worked all day on little
watercolor drawing — Quite an experiment — failed
entirely to get the spirit of the illustration — no "dim
old woods" about it — but a very weak, flimsy sort of
a tangle —

Evening at 7.45 went up to Divens with Geo. & got Fan &
Maud & returned home with them — Joe & Will spent the
Eveng with us — Every thing quite jolly — at 10.30, pickled
oysters and coffee — Geo. &
I took the girls home at about 11 o'clk — Clear Star-
light night — To bed a little after Midnight.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, March 6, 1867

Overcast and disagreeable.
Spent the morning working over my experimental
water color — Home to lunch.
Afternoon tried to work out a new design for
"Gauchos Lassoing Cattle." It is an upright vignette drawing
that I made for the A.R.N.Co some weeks ago — not pleased
with it, I need to make a new drawing — trouble in
drawing the horse — Fan,
Maud & Capt. Liscombe called also. Sanford Gifford —

Eveng. 4th Regular Monthly meeting
of Am. painters in Water Colors — at
Saml. Colmans Studio — Very
few out & but little business
transacted — Made acquaintance
of Farrer, Fenn & Burling —
Got some of them into our room.
Home at 11 o'clk — Miserable
slushy, sleety evening.
