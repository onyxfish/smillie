## Left Page

### Saturday, January 12, 1867.

Glorious cold cloudless day.
My spent between Reading
Owen Meredith's "Evan Harrington"
and trying to make something
out of the material for a
drawing that Mr. Gavit left
with me last evey. — After
dinner at a little after 3 oclk.
went to Studio & found the Sat.
P.M. Reception in full blast.
Halls & Studios thronged, but
the large majority, merely
inquisitive ladies — I doubt
if there were any buyers.
Stayed until after 5 oclk.
walked home with Mr. J. R. B—
& Miss Porter — then on to my home.
Spent all the evey until
11 oclk, very foolishly Reading
to the end. "Evan Harrington" —
As usual — Angry with myself
for my trouble — went to bed
with tired head & tired eyes
but untired body at midnight —
but slept well —

## Right Page

### Sunday, January 13, 1867.

Steady down-falling snow
all the mf — p.m — leaden
& very wintry looking. Mostly
in doors most of the day —
took a walk of about a mile
between 12 & 1 o'clk & enjoyed
it — Did my duty as carver
at the dinner table.
Afternoon passed lazily.
Some family singing, then
tea — more lazy and then
to bed.
