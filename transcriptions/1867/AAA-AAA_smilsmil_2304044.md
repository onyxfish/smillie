## Left Page

### Thursday, December 26, 1867

Very mild, freely thawing day — Go to Studio at 9.15 after making a call
at the N.A. of D — Set to work upon an old 7×9 in. sketch that I painted
some years ago, & finished it up for Haseltine. Colman came in at noon & I gave him a 7×9 sketch
of an old hemlock tree, payment for a sketch that I got of him some
time ago. Home to lunch at 1. Tommy V.V. took lunch with us —
then back to Studio & by working closely until 4.15 — as long as I can
see, finished sketch — Grandins called. Took the bit that I painted yesterday
down to Snedicor's. Ordered frame for my forthcoming Academy picture
33×44 in. Home to tea at 6 —
Matt & Kate in at Will's, helping to finish up his Christmas dinner — I spent
the every Reading & trying to study up a subject for the A.B.N. Co. out
of a lot of South Am. photo's that [illegible]
Gavit sent up to me. To bed at
10.30

## Right Page

### Friday, December 27, 1867

A miserable sort of day, still mild & muggy. Early m'f. clear, by by noon it showered quite briskly.
Rest of the day overcast & threatening.
Got to Studio about 9 & soon got to work upon 15×10 in sketch for
Haseltine — the last of the lot —
"Mt. [illegible] & White Horse Ledge, from North Conway" —. Home to lunch
at 1 — back at 2, and by 4
had sketch finished — Went then
to Silleck's & ordered a toy to pack sketches in to send to H. —
to-morrow, if possible. Home to tea between 6 & 7. Mother & Mary got in just before 8 o'clk.
from Newark, where they have been, spending the day with Nette & [illegible] — Spent most of my
every looking over the news
paper — Still a victim to the
sour temper of my stomach —
Irving spent a short time with me —
To bed at 11.
