## Left Page

### Wednesday, May 8, 1867.

Wicked, Spiteful, N.E. Rain
& wind Storm all day. At
Studio at 9 — but feeling the
worse for last night — worked
(2nd day) on "Storm Coming" &
got it all in — This defective
in lines of Composition &
is merely tolerable in Color
this discreditable that I have
to write such criticism against
myself, for more time & care
would very much modify
such defects — but like every
body else in this part of the
world, I work under pressure
of a given number of pounds to
the Square inch — Every
at home — part of the time in
business Conversation with
Uncle Will — To bed at 10,30

## Right Page

### Thursday, May 9, 1867.

To day we were to have gone to Yonkers
to spend the day with young Bell — but at
6 o'elk when wakened, the weather
was too threatening to think of going
so, went to Studio & painted a sketch
5×6 in. for a little frame that Snedecor
lent to me — Geo. says that he likes
it, but to me it looks bilious — Young
Tiffany lent to Ge. & me a couple
of Shareholders tickets to Academy
of Music to see the Japanese jug-
lers & gymnasts — we went & had
a splendid position in one of the
lower proscenium boxes — Words
are entirely inadequate to express
my admiration & wonder at the
skill dexterity & endurance shewn
by these strange people. Little
"all right" is a perfect fascination
to me. Nerves and muscles must
be trained to an extraordinary
degree. The daring & perfectness
of their displays is consummate.
& their music very funny, also their
lucid description of things.
