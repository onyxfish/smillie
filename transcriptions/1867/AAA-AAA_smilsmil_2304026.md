## Left Page

### Wednesday, November 20, 1867

Yet another fine day. Growing very much milder — part of p.m.
overcast, but toward even, again cloudless — wind still up & dusty — At Studio
about 9 — Some time wasted, washing up dishes — then set to work on
7×12 " Great Rock, near Summit of
Chocorua" — for Dolph's Sale — I
Regret now, painting it — While
I think that there is some of us
good work put upon it as I know
how to do — it lacks qualities
that will make it popular and
saleable in an auction. It is
rather heavy in color & middle dist.
is bad — At 4 o'clk went to Leeds &
to see him — did not succeed — On
my way home, bought a lot of moist
colors — Some proved to be as hard
as bricks — Even, after mounting
drawing of Hartford, for father, went
with Gil down to Brevorts — there
met Edwin Forbes & wife — She is quite
a pretty young woman — he is crippled —
did not leave until 11 — home to bed about [illegible]

## Right Page

### Thursday, November 21, 1867

Cloudless, pleasant day — we have
had a remarkable run of this weather.
B.B.N. Co. want me to go to Albany drawing
right away for $200 — Can't do it —
altho' I want money badly — In at
[illegible] Hart's Room & met D.M. Carlin —
very talkative & more than an hour
wasted — Got started on my water-
color drawing 12×19 in — "The White
Mts from Randolph Hill." Borrowed
foreground studies of J.M.H. — Got the
subject outlined on the paper &
washed in the sky. Did not know
exactly how to manage it and
should not wonder if I had got
myself into some trouble.
Young Kipp called in late in the
afternoon — Even at home &
helping the girls, Mary & Matt, to
hang the curtains & shades in
my Room — got to bed about
11 o'clk
