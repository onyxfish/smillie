## Left Page

### Thursday, April 18, 1867.

Glorious cloudless day, but very windy — At the Studio a little after 8. A.M. outlined with Opt. Turp. a couple of small subjects — then worked the rest of the day on a little 7×9. bit on the Hudson (1½ day) that I intend for Fred. Durand if he likes it — It is about as good as anything I do. But without anything like brilliance of color & "chewed-up" in handling. At 10 o'clk. A.M. occupants of our Bldgs' Studios met in Pratt's room & determined to close the Sat. P.M. informal Receptions next Sat. Home to lunch at 1 — Every Geo. Matt. Charl & Kate went to see Hartz the Illusionist — I stayed at home — Read new English papers & went to bed at about 10 o'clk P.M.

## Right Page

### Friday, April 19, 1867.

Delightful day — not a cloud. Outlined with Opt. Turp. & brown a 6×10 subject — Then finished the bit on the Hudson — 7×9. working until 11 o'clk upon it — making a long day & a half on it — Then commenced again upon "Peeking Shade" 14×18. (1st day.) Worked over the sheep & generally over all — I think it much improved — altho' it is over worked — heavy & monotonous in color tending to green — The greens, I think, well toned, but there is no proper compensation or balance of other colors — I hope Mr Tracy Harris will like it. Miss Badgley called at Studio. 4. P.M. About same time S.F. called at the house. Every very much tired — in my big chair, Reading — To bed at 10 —
Ned Dunning at Studio when Miss Badgley called.
