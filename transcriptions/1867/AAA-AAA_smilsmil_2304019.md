## Left Page

### Monday, November 4, 1867

Father still so under the weather that he did not go down town to-day.
Bleak and rather Novemberish morning — went to Uncle George's
& left my teeth, that had more than they could do Saturday
night, biting & chewing candy.
I called upon Mr. A. B. Durand at his Residence & Studio 91 Amig[?]
Found him at home very pleasant —
met there Mr. Nelson, painting and as I was leaving the house met Carew
Durand. Back to Studio at nt 11.30
Home to lunch at 1.30 — Afternoon
spent penciling on view from top
of Chocorua, looking north. O[illegible]
called during p.m. Brevoort brought
in a Mr. Norton & Mrs. Thompson from
Farmington, Conn — At 6, I went again
to Uncle G's & got my teeth — Wife &
Gel & I went to No. 26 E. 35th & called
upon Miss Sarah Thompson of Portsmouth, N.H. She spent a week with us
last summer at the "Three Elms." Had
a chatty, pleasant visit. Home
at 10.30 & soon to bed — weather
has cleared up charmingly.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, November 5, 1867

Beautiful clear morning — got to
the Studio before 9 o'clk and set to
work stretching some small canvases
which together with a long call from
Sam. Colman consumed all of the
morning — Home to lunch at 1.30
there met Mrs. Nesbit & Mrs. Parker.
After lunch, went to Polls & deposited
my Republican vote — then to Studio
outlined & prepared for painting two
little 7×9 subjects — "Mt. Lafayette" and
"[illegible] on the Saco." Late in the afternoon
three young ladies called & we had a
very pleasant visit — Before leaving,
two of them announced themselves as
daughters of Waud, the Artist, of Philadelphia. Attended Regular monthly meeting
of Watercolor Society at Wm. Hart's Studio —
most important business, the Resignation
of Rawson. Treat & election of J. D. Smillie
to fill the vacancy — This puts me in
the Board of Control — Home at 10.30 —
Father came up from down-town quite
sick — something Colicky — had a bad
night of it — To bed about 11.
