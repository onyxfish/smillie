## Left Page

### Wednesday, March 27, 1867.

Snow flurry in the morning. Got up with a miserable headache — at the Studio set to work (16th day) upon 16×22. Graying things about the foreground — There is little that I can do now. The painting should be put aside for a month, instead of being sent to an exhibition, then I could finish it intelligently. Home to lunch at 1. Then rode down to Uncle George's — Paid him $30 for my teeth + walked to Studio. Young of Boston called. Eve. Will came in with circulars for Snedecor + Charl brought home Geo. W. Peck to stay all night. I spent the evening writing a letter of good advice to Fan Mulford — To bed a little after 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Thursday, March 28, 1867.

Overcast and blustering March day — At Studio worked all the morning on a little painting that I first painted in a year or more ago. for Mr. J. R. Brevoort. Looking across the River — Sun half an hour high + behind clouds. — very black + heavy — tried to remedy it + succeeded in getting a somewhat luminous, but very monotonous result. Home to lunch at 1. Afternoon painted over sky on little Monument Mt. sketch for Addie Dunning.

Evening, Henry Ferguson, by invitation at the house — up in my Room — also Dr. Throops. Stayed until 11 o'clk.

Started from the house at 7.30. A.M. went with circulars to Snedecor — got to the Studio at 8.30.
