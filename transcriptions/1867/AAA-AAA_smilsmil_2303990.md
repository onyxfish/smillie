## Left Page

### on top of Chocorua —

### SATURDAY, SEPTEMBER 7, 1867.

Up at 5.30 — Sky all overcast, but Chocorua
peak clear — Got a sketch from the house
before bkfst. Bkfst, mainly pork & onions —
At 7 all in wagons & drove down to Mr. Cowing's —
took in Misses Cowing — Graupner — Curtis & Littlehale
then on a mile or two farther to Hammonds, at
the foot of the Mt. at 7.45 started up the Mt.
(all but Mr. French & Mr. Crocker) with Hammond
& Nickerson as guides & to carry traps — as
we reached the top of first or Bald Mt. the sun
shone out & from that time we had a glorious
day — Took three sketches on my way up and
reached the Summit with the rest of the
party at 11.15 — or in 3½ hours, easy work —
all of us ecstatic — the crest one of the most
imposing pieces of Rock work that I ever saw —
Beats anything in Central Park — Got on the
sunny side of a rock, out of the wind & took a
sketch — then dinner was called — never enjoyed
a meal more, sitting on a small rock platform
precipices on either side — the sea to the South E.
& an ocean of mts. to North & West — 18 lakes &
ponds in sight — After dinner I took 3 more sketches
8 in all — & at 3.30, after hard work & but little
play we started on our way down, taking it
very easy, as we did on our way up — & reached
the foot at 5.45 — or in 2¼ hours — Got tea at Hammonds & at 6.30 Miss A. Pierce & Miss Littlehale and I
started ahead in small wagon — glorious ride
twilight deepening into moonlight — no clouds
& aurora borealis — Reached home at 9 o'clk
Large wagon 5 minutes after us — One of the greatest days that I ever enjoyed — Chocorua
as a mt. every way superior to Kearsarge, 19 of the
9 feet in height — Wild — Sterile — Grand — then the
crest — an everlasting hill of granite, set upon a grand
mt. All of this was enjoyed at my Rate & 3392 ft.
head — Some dancing around, then to bed at 11 o'cl.

## Right Page

### SUNDAY, SEPTEMBER 8, 1867.

A perfect day — cloudless, breezy, fresh — no
one admits being tired or complains of
yesterday — Bkfst. late & all at the tables — the
fun of yesterday not died out yet — Went
to church in the m'f & heard some one
begging on behalf of Freedmen's schools —
having no money with me & the seat being very
uncomfortable, slipped out at 11.30 & went
home — Wrote part of a letter to Matt — Have
found that our new boarders of 2 or 3 days ago are
Mr. Pitkin & son of New Haven & niece of Boston, Mr.
Parsons & her daughter arr'd. yesterday while
we were away. At 4 o'clk went with a number
of others from the house, in large wagon to the top
of Thorn Hill Road & over to Jackson. From
the Ridge of Thorn Hill Road, saw sun set, effect
very charming — waited for nearly half an hour
then down to Jackson & around home by the
regular stage Road — having some delic-
ious moonlight riding before getting home
at about 8.8 clk — a late tea — feeling
tired I went to my Room & stretched
myself on my bed — Miss Graupner played
some classic music — at 9.30 down stairs
again — walked for some time, enjoying the
evening air & moonlight — To bed at 11.
