## Left Page

### Sunday, March 3, 1867.

Pleasant m[illegible] but chilly —
went with Charl to Hastings
Church & heard him preach
Concerning those who would not
go to Heaven themselves & also
deterred others by their example.
After service Charl & I went up
to uncle Aaron's via 3ave & R.
After dinner wandered along
the River, through the woods &
over the place generally until
4.30 o'clk — weather changed —
bleak & blue — a little after
5. Pete home — Dr Throop at
tea — I written to Wills at
about 8.30 & got impts of
Snedecor's Note place[s] —
To bed at 11. About an
inch of snow fell during
the night.

## Right Page

### Monday, March 4, 1867.

Weather again of the old
miserable stripe. Spent
most of the day making a
charcoal crayon drawing to
illustrate some lines by Alice
Carey "Pictures of Memory" — the
idea — "dim old forest." —
Home to lunch — Late in
the afternoon made nittens
of drawing to make a small
water color drawing for Mrs.
Rutherford's Album.
Evening at home.
Another spatter of snow
during the night.
