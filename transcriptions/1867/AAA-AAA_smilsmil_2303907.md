## Left Page

### Thursday, March 21, 1867

A vile day — Snow commenced falling in the mrg & by noon an inch of horrible slush covered the pavement. Snow & Rain ceased during the p.m & a juvenile hurricane took their place. Painted (12 day) on "View on the Hudson". Still on the foliage of R.h. corner — I doubt if the color will stand well as I have painted & repainted one painting too soon after another. Home to lunch at 1. P.m still pegging at the bushes. Mand Divery at the Studio in the mrg. Welf at home — Dave Smillie presented himself at Divring's at night in the character of a drunken vagabond, and got a dollar to pay for his night's lodging.

## Right Page

### Friday, March 22, 1867

Blowing great guns all day. Painted all day (13th day) on Hudson River picture, with more feeling for it than any day since I commenced the 2d. painting. Painted over foreground trees & Rock in l.h. foreground. So far feeling unwell & unlike work my painting upon this picture has been merely compulsion. I have now finished the second painting & next Monday hope to begin the 3d. — Too soon, but I am trying to get it finished for the N.A. of D. Exhibition. Home to lunch at 1 — Welf wrote a note to Uncle Willie — Aunt Margaret came in & so did Will & Josie — grand family confabulation as to what is to be done with Dave — unanimous conclusion — We don't know. I am to write to the Binghampton Inebriate asylum for information; To bed at 11.
