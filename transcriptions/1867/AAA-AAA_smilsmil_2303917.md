## Left Page

### Wednesday, April 10, 1867.

An end to our fine weather
at last — Overcast — all the
p.m. trying to rain, mizzled
& misted part of the time —
Commenced painting "The
passing shower on the mountain"
14×20 — (2d day.) Got the sky
in quite satisfactorily before
going to lunch at 1 — on return
painted in all the foliage
except the principal evergreen
trees — Rather green & heavy, but
I am pleased with my days
work — Every, mounted for
the 2d time, little oil sketch
for Addie Dunning & Cloud
Study with Monument Mt. —
They had peeled up, after mounting
with glue on mill board, prepared
for oil painting, so I tried it
again with paste, on same board.
To bed before 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Thursday, April 11, 1867.

Fine weather again in the as-
cendant — At work early upon
14×20, "Shower", painting in the
principal groupe of evergreens.
(3d day.) Color tending, as usual,
to blackness — but the drawing, I
think successful. Every, Uncle
Aaron, Tooney & Aunt Huldah to
tea. After tea, went down to a little
fair at Madison Ave. Bap. Ch. — there
for a few minutes, then with George
went to Loyal League Club — by invitation —
Senator Sherman of Ohio there, made
a little speech — never heard him
before — Rather disappointed in him
as a speaker — After the speech,
a supper & a little more speech —
A few pictures were exhibited,
loaned for the occasion. I
sent only a little "Twilight" belong-
ing to Mr. Gnatony — 6×10 in—

Aunt Polly & Jerusha Van took
lunch at the house.
