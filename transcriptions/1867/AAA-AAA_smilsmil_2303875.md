## Left Page

### Wednesday, January 16, 1867

Beautiful bright morning
My birth-day I believe, but I
hardly dare think how old I
am — we'll say no more about
it. Took a mg. walk down to
34 St + around by 5. Ave — Worked
during the mg. making new
outline for A.B.N. Co's drawing.
At noon went to Bohemius +
got Broma etc — fixing up
some for lunch — Birthday
festivities. Afternoon until
dark finishing outline. Then
went to Mr. Ben. Jessup + gave him
my ticket to N.A. of D. Reception
to-morrow eveg. Walked up
5\. Ave to 47 St. then home —
Eveg. Read a little story to
folk at home, comfortably in
my Room —
To bed between 11 + 12 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Thursday, January 17, 1867

Heavy snow storm + high
wind. Splendid winter evening.
Father went down-town at about
noon. Snow continued falling
all the mg. — flying + drifting
all day — Worked with tolerable
faithfulness all day upon A.
B.N. Co's drawing. Between
5 + 6 o'clk P.M. Rigged up +
went out for a walk — got
as far as B. 30. + 6. Ave — and
between snow drifts + the
wind gave up + Returned.
Eveg. all in my Room again.
Father hanging pictures, no
one went to N.A. of D. Reception.
Dr. Dunning called at Studio — I
of course at home — Clafes
called with p/p of Marshall's
\+ Carpenter's heads of Lincoln.
Wants a puff for Marshall —
no doubt he would like it at
the expense of Carpenter —
got nothing — will call again.
