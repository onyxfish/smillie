## Left Page

### Sunday, July 21, 1867

Mrf overcast & almost cold, with strong wind from the N. Father made his appearance before any of us were up in the mrf. — Up later than ever — bkfst about 9 — weather clearing up delightfully fresh & breezy. Spent part of mrng. with Father & Gel walking Road to gate and talking. Part of afternoon, getting things together for packing. At 3 o'clk Gel and I took Mr. Beach's boat home. Glorious wind strong — water rough — we enjoyed it exceedingly — altogether a great day for our last one here. Walked back on the track — getting home about 5. Before tea, got my trunk nearly packed — after tea went with Gel to River — at 8. went up to Roosevelt's — all gone to church — went home — until near 10 o'clk — all on piazza singing. Every cloudy & quite cool, threatening us for to-morrow. Finished packing my trunk & got to bed about 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Monday, July 22, 1867

Up at 7 — Mrf overcast — Every thing comfortably ready for starting at 9 o'clk — stage came along punctually at 10 — all adieus already done — drove direct to wharf — 10.30 — to wait 2½ hours — went to Parker's — wrote a letter to Millie — at 12 went to R.R. Station & got lunch — at 12.30 on the dock & waited until 1 — both boats came up at same time — tide up — down boat waited for up boat to make the landing — at 1.10 got off on the C. Vibbard. Charming sail — very fine studies of cloud forms & water reflections — Landed at foot of 34th St. at 5.20, walked home — Joe Seutin invitation for us to take tea with her, accepted — Nell quite sick — left at 8 o'clk — Evelf in at home doing up lots of little things until 11 o'clk, when very tired, I went to bed & slept soundly through the night — quite a heavy shower as I went to bed.
