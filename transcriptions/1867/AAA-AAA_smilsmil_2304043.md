## Left Page

### Tuesday, December 24, 1867

Up late — not until 8 — feeling rather under the weather, which is not a very inspiriting order, — dull & wind from the East. Got to Studio about 9.30 & worked languidly on some of the little sketches about — devoting most time to the 7×9 in "Mt Lafayette, from Echo Lake" — I am trying to get my last batch of 5 sketches off to Haseltine before New Years. Home to lunch at 1 — Afternoon pretty much spent collecting from the fellows in the building to pay for Reception Cards. Launt Thompson gave us a call — At 5 o'clk went to Snedicor's & did not get home to tea until 7 — Spent most of the evening at Will's arranging a lot of my Stereoscopic Slides in a new holder that he has just got. Saw there D.T. Albertson — Home & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, December 25, 1867

A dull day for a Merry Christmas — did not get to Studio until 9.30 — Set to work upon a 7×9 sketch. a bit of bank & Hemlock Trunks & Roots on the River side at Mr. Roosevelt's. — painted in last June. — About 12.30 Rich? Dorrman called — Just before 1 o'clk Walter Brainerd came down from Joe Still with an invitation for us, to dine with them — Respectfully declined, but I went home & got a good chicken dinner, then hurried back to Studio — Dr. Throop dined with us — Worked faithfully as long as I could see & by 4.30 P.M. got sketch finished after a fashion — my stomach is giving me no end of trouble now. Home about 6 — At about 8 o'clk went in to Will's — Raining hard — remained there until after 10 — a lot of the Brainerds & several Smillie's there. — To bed at 11 o'clk.
