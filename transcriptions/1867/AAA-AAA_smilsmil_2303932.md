## Left Page

### Friday, May 10, 1867

A day, in a weather way, hardly one thing or another. Very windy,
cloudy & cool. Painted until
nearly 6 o'clk P.M. faithfully
upon a circle 8½ in, "Noon
on the Hudson." In some respects
I like it — in others I am afraid
it is false in color & tending to
conventional prettiness.
Rec'd a note from Tracy Harris
saying that had he known last
Feb. that I was going to paint
his picture, he would have countermanded
it — as he is going to
Europe — as it is, he wishes me
to send it to some dealer & have
it sold on his acct. I do not
know yet how to treat him — goodness
knows — I must have money —
but I think I will be proud and
release him from all obligation
to me. I think he is a petty, mean
man. — Every at home. Spent
an hour or so in at Willis.
To bed at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Saturday, May 11, 1867

Delightful mg with promise of
a charming day. Before going
to studio wrote a note to Mr. Harris
releasing him from all obligations
incurred in giving me a commission
to paint him a picture. Young
Bell took a lesson. I finished
two 6×10's — "Autumn p.m. in Central
Park" & "Mfg on the Susquehanna
near Binghamton" & worked a
little over several other sketches.
I have 7 now ready to send to
Haseltine. Left Studio at 12 o'clk
had hair cut & a good shampoo —
Home to lunch. P.M. Spent in
doing up messages. Saw Selleck
& Tynan & Seitz, Snedecor, Greene &
Leeds concerning costs & exhibition
— information that I keep as
Chairman of Com. on Exhibiting
of Water-color Society. Home
after 7 o'clk well tired out.
Evening Reading newspapers
& other heavy work. During
P.M. no less than 3 real April showers.
