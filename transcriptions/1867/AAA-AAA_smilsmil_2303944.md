## Left Page

### Friday, June 7, 1867

Still very ravin, but not so hot as
yesterday — Ther. 85° in studio —
Worked under strong urging, all
day, finishing 7×9 sketch for
Colman (2d day). This, to me,
a singular looking thing, evidently
very wrong in color somewhere,
but in my present imbecile state
I cannot tell where — At any
rate, it is painted carefully, and
is not without some good points.
Mr. Wm. Main made us a short call
at about 2 o'clk — Misses Goodrich,
Davis & Wheeler made us quite a long
call, later — Work is done under
protest — Lazying is done under protest —
everybody is protestant in every thing
now-a-days — Young Bell called to
say that he cannot come to-morrow —
Eveng — a little more DuChaillu —
an hour in at Will's — then home
& at about 11 — to bed. Tom Gillelan
called in evng for a little while.

## Right Page

### Saturday, June 8, 1867

Up late & feeling headachy — Strong
easterly wind & spitting rain, at times
enough to wet the sidewalks. At
studio about 10 o'clk — Gave a coat of
siccatif to circle "mrg" & "evng" and
to "Coming Shower" & fixed them up to
have Snedecor send for them. Did
a little painting on & about noon on
Southwick's sketch. About 12.30 Mr. Main
came in — Went over to N.A. of D. with him —
took lunch & then went down town. Saw
Mr. Southwick, delivered my picture —
got my $40 00 — Went to W.E. Dodge Jr. —
then to A.B.N. Co. — then to Leeds & Miner —
then to N.B.N. Co. where I got $96. for father —
Rode up to Snedecors — Saw my pictures
from Phila & made arrangements with
Snedecor, concerning the paintings
I will leave with him during the summer.
Heavy Rain at 5 — Ret'd to Studio
& until 7 o'clk — engaged packing up
things to get off to Poughkeepsie Monday
mrg. Chare went up this P.M. Every
steady rain — Spent most of evng
reading Du Chaillu — To bed
shortly after 10.
