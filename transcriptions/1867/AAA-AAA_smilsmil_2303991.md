## Left Page

### MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 9, 1867.

Up at 5. go to see the French party off — also
Miss Pittee — cloudless sky & glorious sunrise
about 6 o'clk — the party got away in large of
extra, for Centre Harbor — Joy be with them,
they were a jolly set — George & I walked
down the Road about two miles, then back —
After bkfst, Miss Graupner offered me a seat
in or with a party just going to Carter's Notch —
Accepted it, & a little after 9, started off — Two
Misses Howe — Misses Howland, Haymard
Curtis — Graupner — ~~Miss~~ Miss Robbins Mrs. Keist —
Miss Howe, an aunt of the Misses Howe, form-
ing the party. Went around by Dundy Road
to Jackson, then on to the Notch — getting
there at just 1 o'clk — took lunch at once
by Wild Cat Creek — then a sketch of the Notch
then at 3, on to the last house, where a
boy took us through the woods to a ledge
where we saw up & down the glen, & towering
above, Mt. Washington — Clouds had blown
up & gave a fine effect of illuminated vapor —
got a sketch — then all home — reaching there
at 8.30 — Took tea — Lida Martin spending the
day with Mr. Hall. About 10, George drove home
with her — I went to bed, well tired out, and
slept like a top —

## Right Page

### TUESDAY, SEPTEMBER 10, 1867.

Up before 6 o'clk — Sky all overcast, got
my bkfst at 6.30 — had my lunch prepared
before any one else came down to bkfst, had
started with color box & traps for my work —
got a Ride out to pine woods near
Mudgett's and there, a little after
8 o'clk set to work upon a moss
covered Rock Study — no light — part
of time had up umbrella to keep
off the spitting Rain — worked until
4 o'clk, getting a tolerably success-
ful sketch, but naturally enough
it is very flat, wanting the sunlight.
Afternoon, wind blew up & by 5 o'clk
it was quite broken away. Walked
home through the meadows, getting
in just before tea & very tired —
Eve'g spent telling ghost stories —
got a letter from Jno. Snedecor
enclosing check for $50.00 — To bed
about 10.30. Lovely moonlight
night.
