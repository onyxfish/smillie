## Left Page

### Thursday, August 22, 1867

Up at 6.30 after a tolerable nights rest —
up once to use my brine, altho during
my climbing & exertion yesterday I suffered
no in convenience — Day very close with
a filmy overcast. Spent the morning
writing a long letter to Mary —
Afternoon excessively close
and muggy, thickening up
more like rain — I stayed
at home working over some of
my pencil sketches & starting
a letter to S.C.T. Evening
spent upon piazza, doing,
as we have found out how to do,
nothing in particular — mail
late and as I sat up for it, it
was past 11 before I got to bed.
Rec'd. by the mail a letter from
D. Throop.

## Right Page

### Friday, August 23, 1867

true to its record of six weeks past,
in this part of the country at least, it
has rained, off and on, all day. I
spent the m't, most of it, fighting
off a headache — with partial suc-
cess — worked over Jackson Falls
pencil study & after dinner got
it finished and set it. Then wrote
a letter to Throop which took
until near 5 o'clk — then a walk
of about an hour over the meadows
on the drift Road to Upper Ford.
After tea, a little writing, then
joined those in the parlor at
their games — all engaged writing
poetry of Rhapsodies as they call it,
until after 10 o'clk — mail late
again — 11.30 when I got to bed.
