## Left Page

### Thursday, March 7, 1867

Just as miserable a day as could very well be compounded. Sleet in the mf — heavy rain & wind at noon with 2 inches or more of slush under foot. Mf, finishing up two sketches of "Gipsey" (horse) that I made at Uncle Aaron's a year or more ago. Home to lunch at 1 o'clk — P.M. Charcoaling to get a satisfactory design for new drawing of "Gaucho Lassoing" — Couldn't get what I wanted & at last determined to make changes on the old drawing, so I rubbed out the whole of the Gaucho & a good part of the horse — blocking out proposed changes in charcoal.
Every quietly at home.

## Right Page

### Friday, March 8, 1867

A glorious clear day at last. All the mf working on Gauch. drawing. Got it at last to look presentable — I will again offer it to the Co. & hope that they will have no fault to find — Home to lunch at 1 o'clk
P.M. worked a little more on drawing — made Camera lucid. reductions of Charcoal caricatures — & charcoaled a new caricature upon female hair dressing & bonnets — Tooney, Uncle A. & Aunt H. at Studio bet. 3 & 4 o'clk — Fau Mulford called at near 5 — Ge & I walked up to Divers with her. Saw Mr Mulford & others — Every new parlor furniture came home — Wrote a letter to Mr. Mr. Main —
To bed at 11.30.
