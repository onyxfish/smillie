## Left Page

### Wednesday, July 17, 1867

Up late — that is, not until 7.40 — a splendid clear & cool mrg. Got to work shortly after 9 o'clk and by 5 o'clk P.M had painted my 18th sketch — 6×10. "Evening on the Hudson, after a stormy day." It is after some pencil notes that I took a few evenings ago of a very fine sunset sky and its reflections on the water. — Father & Geo. speak very well of it, & say that it is "strong" — After tea dressed & went over to J. Ro. to get k. so that I could get my oars in his boat house — Ushered in to quite a tea table full of strangers — took a second tea and was excused with Mr. — went to boat house together — Met McKeil & Stevens — J. R. had arranged for a pull 2½ miles with M. C. K. — The R. family & friends on the point again — J. R. beat his last [illegible] time 4 m. 50 sec. — M. C. K. the Champion of the River, pulled splendidly — Father, Geo. Matt. Kate & I in Beaches boat pulled about, up & down & across the River — Moon Rise from behind a bank of clouds, the delight of us all — lunging & rowing until nearly 10 o'clk — then home & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Thursday, July 18, 1867

Up late again — sleeps someday now — Sky overcast & quite cool. Got to work after [illegible] on 7×9 sketch that I call "Alone" — An old hemlock tree, in relief against an evening sky — from a pencil study that I made at Montrose last Oct. I am rather pleased with the treatment, in some respects, with the color of the thing — finished it before dinner — Smart shower about 1.30 & more or less of rain, the rest of the p.m. After dinner, to work on 7×9 "Late afternoon among the Catskills — from the tops of Buttermilk Ravine" — This I have finished, to give to Jno. A. Roosevelt for his many civilities to me during my stay here at Hill-Side. — At 4 P.M. at work on 6×10 "Bit on Hudson River Shore, near Poughkeepsie" and in an hour or so had finished it for H's alt time — at 5.30 took tea & at 5.50 my quite a heavy Rain. Geo. & I walked with Father down the R.R. track about a mile & a quarter — He is going to N.Y. City to finish a die & expects to be gone until Sat. night. Shortly after getting home it stopped raining — by 10 o'clk it was moonlight — but quite cold. Spent the evening cheerlessly reading some miserable stories and got to bed chilled through at 10.15
