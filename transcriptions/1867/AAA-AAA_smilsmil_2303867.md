### TUESDAY, JANUARY 1, 1867.

Dismal, overcast day, snowing during the m'g. Felt pretty well myself — but father quite miserable and at work of course — altho' the house is not yet fairly "to rights" — the "girls" determined to receive such friends as called, informally. Bro. Will called early & spent some time with us. Aunt H. & I kept my room during the day, Uncle Aaron, Gilbert Petit, Dr. Tivols and others overflowing it in the p.m. Ike Riley gave me a call — The Girls received but very few. George made 18 & chart about 45 calls.

The sensation of the day however was Dr. S— but of this, nothing must be written. Between 10 & 11 o'clk I got my bed fixed & slept through to the morning without waking. Aunt H. went home with Uncle A. and Toony between 9 & 10 o'clk P.M.
