## Left Page

### Monday, January 28, 1867

Clear, cold day — Set to work in the M'f— to plan a new arrangement for drawing of "Gaucho" — but after several attempts in charcoal, gave it up, & set to work again up on the old drawing; washing out different parts & redrawing the. Worked faithfully all day & at last got it to do, after a fashion. It is wrong — but I don't know where. At dusk walked to Studio & home with Geo. After tea, prepared to call on Fan M. & Maud D. when they sent word that they were going out. Then Geo. & I went to the Ireland's & spent a very pleasant eve. Had some fun Recording a bet concerning probable changes in the Sectarian views of Addie D. — & Geo Hawes two yrs. after marriage — Geo. & Mr. Spring & I bet that Addie will become a good Presbyterian.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, January 29, 1867

Another clear, cold day — worked a little while on wagon drawing, then at 11 o'clk went down town to A.B.N. Co with the two drawings — wagons & Gaucho. The Gaucho drawing made considerable stir among the Co. folks. They are pleased with the novelty & action — but enquire as anxiously as I do for the admitted fault. No one can determine where it lies. However I agreed to make a change in the fore legs of the horse & took the drawing home. Drew $6p.oo for drawing wagons. Spent P.M. making changes in Gaucho's horse. Eve'g with Geo. went to Diven's in 52d St. & had a good time with Fan M. & Maud. Mary D. was tired out — all last night on Erie P.R. Mr & Mrs Corey there — also Mr Ed. Cook & a lady — left for home at 10.40 o'clk.
