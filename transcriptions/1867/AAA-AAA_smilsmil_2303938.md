## Left Page

### Friday, May 24, 1867

Cloudless mrg. — but bleak & windy
afternoon, clear again at eveg. —
Very much tired out from yesterdays work — at Studio a little
after 9 a.m. Found letters from Geo.
Hawes & Addie Dunning. Got to
work upon sketch for Haseltine
7×9 — "Alone" — An old broken
Hemlock against a twilight sky
worried it out after a fashion
but did not get done until after
6. P.M. Then so tired out that I
stretched in a big chair and
slept for more than an hour —
Home about 8 o'clk — after tea
still too tired to go to J. Q. A. Ward's
68 Hammond St. where Geo & I
had been invited to see a few
friends — I Regret it very much.
Spent the eveg writing to
Addie & went to bed at 11.

J. H. Dolph made us a long call
concerning a sale of pictures that
he proposes to have next Dec.

## Right Page

### Saturday, May 25, 1867

Another clear mrg. cloudy P.M.
& rainy eveg. with great guns
of wind & loads of dust. Young
Bell took his lesson this mrg.
I spent the day (1½ days) on
moonlight 6¾×10½ — or "Night"
in my series of Mrg, Noon, Eveg. and
Night — I glazed it all down &
painted over it, very much to
its advantage I think — At
about 4 o'clk I went to Snedecor —
he has sold my little 5×6 in
sketch that I painted a few
days ago — it nets me $22.50
As I had painted it for him that
he might sell an odd sized and
old frame, had quite a discussion
with him as to the propriety of his
charging me a per centage, but
he cho[se] it — Eveg at home
& to bed at 10:30
