## Left Page

### Friday, March 29, 1867

Clear windy day with clouds of dust. Off to the Studio at 7.40 a.m. with Father to see our Academy Pictures — Jed. Soory followed — Gave the Room a thorough sweeping, dusting & putting to Rights — through at 10 o'clk — Tired — sat about until dinner time — home to lunch — Afternoon, glazed top of sky (16×22) — 17th day — with blue & scumbled bottom with white — & glazed down the water again — I call it done — Miss Wadleigh called — So did Mother & Mary & Meta Gavit — & Joe — Will's wife — walked home to Will's with Meta — Do/ went in to Will's — but saw M. G. but for a few minutes — home & to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Saturday, March 30, 1867

Glorious clear day, but very windy. Before 9 o'clk a.m. Mr. & Mrs. Mr E. Dodge Jun'r called at the Studio to see my "View on the Hudson looking South" — Both expressed themselves very flatteringly, & Mr. D. besides accepting the picture giving me permission to exhibit it in the N.A. of D. gave me a check for $182.00 — $150 for the picture & $32 — for the frame — Mrs. Mr. Martin & Leila of 21st. Called — No work done — home to lunch at 1. P.M. Reception. Miss Armstrong of Honolulu came with Will Kimble — and Miss Meta Gavit, to whom — at 2.45, I acted as escort to the Philharmonic Rehearsal — much pleased. hurried back to the Studio — our pictures out to the Academy at 4 o'clk — At 7.20 started for Brooklyn — reached the Institute at 8.30 & hear to the close Mr. Hart's lecture "The Field & the Easel, with illustrations." A very good lecture very badly Read — Met Ferguson & with George, got home at 10.40 o'clk.
