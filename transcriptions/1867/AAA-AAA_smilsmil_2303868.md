## Left Page

### Wednesday, January 2, 1867.

Pleasant clear morning, a monotonous day — Spent half an hour pacing the back piazza in the sun and felt very much refreshed by the exercise.

After dinner went to Fleming's — got some papers & a copy of Carpenter's "Six Months at the White House."

Spent the Rest of the afternoon and evening assorting & fixing up files of Son. Ill. News for several years back. Got to bed at about 11.30 Pretty well tired out.

## Right Page

### Thursday, January 3, 1867.

Bright, pleasant day, but Rather chilly. Up very late in the [illegible] — had my steps in bed — so that it was 11 o'clk before I was Ready to take my morning tramps on the piazza —
Dinner at one — A little after 2, D. T. called with a carriage & wished me to ride up to Manhattanville with him, — had an appointment with Uncle Gel — could not go — but Rode with him 15 or 20 min. Rice & Matt & Hate went with him, calling at Uncle G's — Uncle G — came bet. 4 & 5 o'clk and took imps. of my mouth — the upper jaw in plaster — the lower in wax, not a pleasant process.

Recd. a letter from Addie Dunning. Eve'g passed lazily, Father writing to Walter at Ottawa.

To bed bet. 10 & 11 o'clk
