## Left Page

### Tuesday, July 9, 1867

Rec'd letter from Fan Mulford — mailed
July 5th — has been in somebody's pocket for
3 or 4 days — Lovely morning — a late breakfast, that
is, after 8 o'clk — After bkfst — went to ice house
& had quite a time again getting onice &
filling big box in the house — After 9 got
palette & got Ready for work — At 10.30 run
to R.R. for Paper — Saw J.R. at his boathouse —
Ret'd home at 11.15. Worked until dinner
time without being able to get sky or any
tones to please me, after dinner worked
until 3 o'clk, when I gave it up. I do
not understand that at times I am
more than ever, a perfect booby at my work —
Seeing a storm coming I started for Beach &
had tremendous work getting the boat out of the
cove — tide out — after wading, tugging & hauling,
Succeeded — broke a tholepin — had to pull forward
— down by the head — tide against me — storm
coming — working like a horse — at 4.30 made
fast at Doyle's — Hurried home through Rain —
plenty of wind & quite cold — at 6 again went
down & fired boat in P's cove — home in Rain —
tea at 7 — at 7.20 Pat came to see if I would
Row down to meet J.R. who has just gone to [illegible] —
I went — almost dark — quite Rain — the
flood — not motion enough in water to make
boat dance — Spirit of Scene, fine — got a
scare from a Rain com that jumped from the
water not a boat's length ahead of me — met
J.R. below half way mark — pulling home — I turned
in at nearly mid in time to walk through the
woods in heavy Rain — jolly time — Rained
most of the night — To bed before 10 o'clk —

## Right Page

### Wednesday, July 10, 1867

glorious, cool, breezy, cloudless morning —
went down to the boat after bkfst. and found J all
right. Father went into town by 10 o'clk Stage. I
felt well tired, even sore, from yesterdays ex
ertion in Rowing — So of course was rather
lazy — but got to work upon the little 6×10
that stuck me yesterday & before dinner
got a sky & distance that I think has
something good in it. While at dinner,
Father walked in, having walked from town.
Did not do so well during afternoon on
my painting, & at 4.30 again gave up in
disgust & went over to J. R— Played
game of Croquet — badly beaten as usual —
took tea with him, as healing to my
wounded feelings. Effort made to muffle
his baby, but its head would hang out of the towel.
About 7 we drove into town — he to attend a
Club meeting — I to amuse myself. Was
with him part of the time — bought Rope and
padlock for Beachie's boat — Got a letter
from S.C.J. and read it — Called on
Lydia Parmentier — very pleasant — at
9 o'clk went to J.R — found him ready & at 9.30
home — found Matt just from N.Y.
