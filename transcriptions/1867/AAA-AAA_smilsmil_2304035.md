## Left Page

### Sunday, December 8, 1867

Clear, Cold and windy — a fine winter day. Morning went to [illegible]
Hastings church + heard him give an excellent charge to all
Christians, concerning their vocation
Ephesians IV — 1st. — Emma
Bourne spent the day with
us. After dinner, took a
nap in my big chair and
at 4.30 went up to Central
Park + had a glorious walk
between twilight + moonlight
Air very cold + wind quite high
but I was very warm — home at
6 — Every Geo. and I went
to J. P. Thompsons and heard
him give a historical lecture
on the general Religious condition
of Spain from Early time down
to the present — out at 9.30 —
On my way home stopped in
at Wills + got Boston drawing to
add Steamships — home and
to bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### Monday, December 9, 1867

Splendid clear cold day — At
Studio before 9 and got fire soon
started but it was some time
before the Studio got warmed —
for about an hour was engaged
putting in Steamships on Boston
drawing — then set to work again
upon Water Color drawing "White
[illegible] from Randolph Hill" — washed
out of my blue, some cloud forms
\+ put in clouds of a brownish color
much to the general advantage
of the drawing, I think — home to lunch
at 1.30 — To my mortification, heard that
people called Sat. night, misunderstanding
our invitations to the Reception —
Afternoon spent upon middle
distance foliage — Kate went to
Jersey City with Emma B. to spend
a day or two — Every I wrote to
Matthew Vassar, asking the loan of a
drawing or two from the College Gallery
for our Water Color Exhibition —
To bed at 9.40
