## Left Page

### Friday, August 16, 1867

Up between 6 & 7 — Rain falling as softly & steadily as thought it was the sweetest thing in the world and the most desirable, and through the day it fell without cessation — Little m nothing done during the m't, as I felt out of sorts after another uneasy night — took a nap before dinner — afternoon, felt better & spent most of it Reading "Parson Garlands daughter" by Miss Muloch. It interested me in some respects it pleased me, but altogether, I had formed a higher estimate of Miss M — Every all in parlor & dining room playing "going to Jerusalem" etc & afterwards "Raphsodies" — on the whole the Raphsodiacal effusions were clever — several of them quite clever — but Miss Curtis was allowed the palm — the Championship — all to bed, tired at 11 o'clk — Then has been but little advance with my arm at the wrist — it is slowly healing, but toward the elbow it seems still inclined to erupt — The bright spots still go on over my body & I am a perfect martyr to the itch — [illegible] 3 out of the spots are bright & large — but most of them are more like mus quite bites —

## Right Page

### Saturday, August 17, 1867

Up as usual & found it Raining, as usual — this enough to make sick a strong man — from 10 until 3 o'clk it was a horrible, sticky, fly day — Run out scorching, then a mizzle of Rain — Spent the m't Reading in North Am. Review "The Judiciary of M. City" said to have been written by Thom. Shearman — & "Russian Serfs & their Emancipation" — After dinner a party was got up to drive to Jackson Falls 8 miles — a large wagon — 4 horses, & small wagon, 2 horses — at the gate — all Ready — heavy Rain comes in & it pours without intermission from 3 until 5 o'clk — Breaking up shower from 6 until 8 o'clk — new boarders, Mr & Miss Fuller arr— in the midst of it, drenched — amused ourselves during the eve'g with more games — at nine beautiful moonlight, with mist variations — a run on the Road with some of the girls & then to bed at 11 o'clk — To-day I've suffered much with the itching eruption which is mostly from my hips down, my shins being the Grand Centre — My arm is now all day tis getting stiff & hard & decidedly better.
