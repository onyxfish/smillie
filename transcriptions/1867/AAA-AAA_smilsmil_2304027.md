## Left Page

### Friday, November 22, 1867

Overcast at last, Rather mad looking something like a storm.
At Studio about 9 o'clk & set to work again upon 14×18 Ham
picture and soon worked it as far as I can carry it now.
I am very sorry to have to put out such a looking thing for date.
Mr. Choate & Miss Gibbons paid us quite a visit. Prevost brought
in Mr. Norton of Farmington.
Home to Lunch at 1 o'clk. P.M.
painted a new sky on 7×9 sketch
of Echo Lake & Mt. Lafayette. At
4.30 P.M. went to Coffee's Plaster
Cast Establishment to see about cast
to send to Alfred Jones. Then called at
Snedicor's. Saw Mr. Hart. Eve'g at
home, wrote to Alfred Jones. Falconer
& Van Ingen — Gilbert Pettit came
in from Milton about 7 o'clk and
stayed all night. To bed about
11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Saturday, November 23, 1867

Miserable Rainy day — at Studio
at 9, worked a little while on water
color drawing — long enough to satisfy
me that I have spoiled the sky,
through ignorance of manner in washing.
Then went to 10th St. Bldg & called on
Mr. Hart. Spent most of the m'g with
him looking over Aaron Penley's book
on water color drawing, & consulting
with him concerning his experiences
of paper, colors, etc, etc. Called
on A.D. Shattuck — then to Knoedler's
& bought some frames, then home to lunch.
Back to Studio at 2.30 & found young
Bell waiting. Gave him his second
lesson. Also worked a little on
my drawing. Eve'g a little
family dinner party. Joe, Will,
Minn, & Uncle Aaron with us. It
is Father's & Will's birth day. Father
is 60. Will is 33. An immense
Turkey was sacrificed with usual
attendants. As were two mince pies
that were dropped on the floor just as
they were being brought to the table.
To bed about 1 o'clk.
