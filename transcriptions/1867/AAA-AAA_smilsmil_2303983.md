## Left Page

### Saturday, August 24, 1867

Mists hung on the mts most of the
m'g so as I could not go on with Mt.
Mt. Study. I spent the m'g getting a
very careful Camera-lucida outline
upon my water-color block of an old
tree trunk & meadows beyond that I
expect to finish in watercolor. It
has good stuff for near study — afternoon
I started with portfolio & pencils, made
the River & walked directly to the foot
of White Horse Ledge — a good two miles
I think the most direct way — Climbed
about a third of the way up on a ledge,
found a view of Charsarge & Echo Lake that
pleased me — set Camera & took it. Thunder
Rolling when half finished — could not see storm —
hoped that it would go back & the Mt. caught
in it — tremendously rough scramble down —
tumble after tumble — thoroughly wet in
five minutes — Thunder, wind & rain
among rocks & trees — very fine — then the
walk through the dismal, pine woods —
welcome meadows of the intervale —
marched Right through the River and
Reached home just as the Rain ceased
my appearance very much laughed at.
Son got a change & my supper fell Right,
evening spent as usual and to bed
about 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sunday, August 25, 1867

Up between 6 & 7 — m'g clear & crisp
with wind from N. W. went to m'g service
and heard young Todd of Boston. Dant. 6th
10th — a very good discourse, taking Dan-
as a high type of Christian fortitude &
power — After service wandered off to
the hill back of the church — Dinner
at 2 & at 3¼ o'clk. P.m. Jes and I
started across the meadows for White
Horse Ledge — waded the River & got there
at 4.30 — at 4.40 started up through
bushes & over Rocks. Reaching the top at
5.25. The view, glorious — a beautiful
combination of lines, looking over Harts
& Humphreys Ledges to Carter's Notch.
on the top 20 m. at 5.45 started down
Reaching the foot at 6.10 — Did not get
home until 7.50 — altho' our walking
time was but 55 m. Felt tip-top &
enjoyed my tea ever so much — after
tea Miss Graupsner played a movement
or two from Beethoven's 2d & 7th Symphonies.
Arrangements made for a trip to Chocorua
tomorrow — to my great vexation, I can't go —
to bed at 10 o'clk. after a grand day.
