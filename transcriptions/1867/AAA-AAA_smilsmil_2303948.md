## Left Page

### Saturday, June 15, 1867.

mf overcast — got up with an unmistakable headache. The Result of last night's rowing. Until 9 o'clk had no hope of saving the day — gave my feet a scalding, keeping them in water as hot as I could bear, & got unexpected Relief to my head. At 10 30 went down to R.R. to get paper — no Herald — saw J.A.R's "Times" — glad to see that "all Right" is Convalescent. Ret'd home & got to work on Rosalie's box — Dinner, at 1 — worked p.m. until 5 o'clk on vines etc on box — slow work — at 5 walked up to Mr. M. S. Beach's hoping to see Mrs. B — or Mr. Sullivan, the farmer — saw neither — walked home by R.R. track — getting in a little after 6 — Tea — gentle Rain — walking on piazza until 9 o'clk — To bed at 9.30

## Right Page

### Sunday, June 16, 1867.

Up late — mf muggy, overcast and threatening rain — Had intended to walk to town to hear Howard Crosby, but at 9 o'clk little sprinklings changed my mind — between walking & reading a life of Duncan Dunbar by his son in law, per. Chaplin, the mf passed. At 1 o'clk there was a brisk, heavy shower with one clap of thunder — leaving the weather more & muggier than before — more little sprinkles during the p.m. — Thunder all about us. Read Part V of Jaime's "Christ History" — "Human Systems of Religions Truth" — with much pleasure — Write letters to Fan Mulford & George. At 5 o'clk the affair of the day — weatherly breaking — came off — in the shape of a finely made Thunderstorm — it rained heavily for nearly an hour then continued gently & persistently until sometime about midnight — Walked Piazza until about 9.30 — To bed soon after —
