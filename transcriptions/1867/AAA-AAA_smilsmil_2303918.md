## Left Page

### Friday, April 12, 1867

Fine day, but as usual, very windy. At the studio before
9 o'clk — got started at once upon "Shower" 14×20 — (4th day.)
Painting in foreground rocks
getting my first "painting in" finished. I think very successfully.
Got through early at 5 o'clk
or a little after, went to Schaus,
Snedecor's and Kelly's — Home
to tea late + well tired out.
Evening doing very near to
nothing — To bed at 10.30

Young Bell called to say
that he could not come
to take his lesson to-morrow

## Right Page

### Saturday, April 13, 1867

Glorious day — at the Studio
early — again painted a sky
on little sketch for Addie D —
This is the 3d one — For some
reason the others seem to have
blackened. For the present
I think it is again very much
better — At 1.30 went to N.A.&D.
Varnishing Day. Both of my
paintings are on the line and
hung better than they deserve. My
portrait hangs right above my
Dodge picture — as I stayed but
an hour — got but an imperfect
idea of the Collection. Home to lunch,
off + right back to Studio — worked
upon little "Milight" of Mr. Gratoni's
that was at League Club Thurs.d night
adding much needed grays. At
3 o'clk with Gil went to Phil. Rehearsal.
Home at 7 o'clk worn out + too tired
to go over to Brooklyn to hear Mr.
Jas. Fairman's lecture "the Aesthetics
of art" — much disappointed.
He has already read about 2/3's
of it to me.
