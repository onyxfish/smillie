## Left Page

### Thursday, January 10, 1867

Miserable sort of a day —
but I feel very well — Went
to Dr. Dunning's to keep my
engagement with Fred Durand
at 11 o'clk. Sat in his chair
until after one o'clk, he diligently at work all the time — I was
surprised to find how entirely
I have neglected my teeth —
The 9 that I have on my lower
jaw he has put in fine condition, with one temporary
gutta-percha filling in my
left molar — Stopped at Schans's
on my way home — dinner late,
tired & spent p.m. in big
chair. Uncle J. called in for
a few minutes but did not bring
my teeth with him. Evy & Jo.
& Fate went to a little party
given by Louise Dua in 23d St.

## Right Page

### Friday, January 11, 1867

Cold clear day — Uncle A. & Aunt
A. called at the house about 10. a.m.
I went down town with Uncle A.
went to A. B. N. Co. to see about some
drawings that I hoped to get —
Saw Mr. Gavit — to my chagrin, got
the promise of but one — Mr. G. promising
to call with photos, etc. in the eveg.
Went searching through Nassau
Ann. Beekman & Fulton Sts. for a
man, Hollingsworth that used to keep
back files of Soul. Ill. News — Trying
to get a missing No. H. has left the
Country & at near 2 o'clk I gave
up for the day. Rode up to Uncle J's
& after much waiting got my teeth —
Of course, much disappointed — they
hurt me as bad as a tooth-ache and
look horrid — this is the beginning of
sorrows — home between 3 & 4, completely tired out & very hungry — got
some tea & toast. J'd it in the eveg.
with some photos of Buenos-Ayres
wagons — miserable trying subject
for me.
