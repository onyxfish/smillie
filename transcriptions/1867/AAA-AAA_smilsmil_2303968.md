## Left Page

### THURSDAY, JULY 25, 1867.

*at the "Ocean House" Newport*

Cloudless m'g got off the boat at 6.30 & took omnibus to Ocean House — Bkfst. at 8 o'clk. then up to our Rooms 109 & 107 — lay off until near 10. Fine view out to sea from our windows — both rather headachy. on the balcony listening to the band until 10.30 then walked slowly down to "the neck" nearly 2 miles — some bits of good Rock & very fine surf — walked along the beach for a mile or more — at 12, caught in a sudden shower — took Refuge on piazza of neighboring house — in a few minutes started for Hotel, but within 2 or 3 00 yds of it, had to give up & take shelter on another piazza from a very heavy thunder St. did not get to Hotel until 2 o'clk — got a cup of tea & went to bed — at 3, dinner, but could not take it — headache — another thunder St. Up at 4.30 & walked home the "old Round town" — hurried in from 4th shower — again to bed — up for tea at 7.45, feeling better — band played from 4 till near 6 — Miss Brainard sang in Parlor — band again from 8 until 10 — very few guests in the house — promenading the hall — but one set of Lancers danced — every thing quiet — at 10.30 — Ther. 65° Rain at night — to bed at 12 — soon asleep & good nights Rest —

## Right Page

### FRIDAY, JULY 26, 1867.

*at the "Parker House" Boston*

Called at 5. A.M. — up at 6 off for "the Beach", in a steady Rain — back to Hotel at 7 — got Bkfst — Bill $13.00 then to depot & took R.R. "Old Colony Newport" line to Boston — 62½ miles. fare $2.00 — passed through poor & uninteresting country arriving at Boston at 10.25. In Room 164. Parker House & [illegible] A.M. went at once to Atheneum, walked over the Common, went into Childs's Picture Gallery then to J.H. Young's Studio, 16 Tremont St. — got permit to enter closed Athenæum Gallery — spent an hour or two in them, until past 3 o'clk. Had a Royal dinner at 12.30 — walked through the streets — "the Gardens" — "Back Bay" — Library & lots of strange places — Met in Washington St. Nannie Pleasants & the Drinks — mutual surprise — pushed on to Bunker Hill Mont. Climbed it — back to Faneuil Hall — then to Hotel — cleaned up, enjoyed very much our tea — at 8 o'clk called at Tremont House — Saw the Drinks & Nannie for ½ an hour — then after some trouble found the Winthrops House & spent from 9 until 10 with Mr. Young & some of his friends, enjoying some music — walked the streets again — in at 11 — well tired & very well pleased with Boston — The Parker House — & ourselves
