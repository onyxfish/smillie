## Left Page

### Friday, February 15, 1867

More mean weather. If not raining, trying very hard to, & showing every inclination to disagreeableness. Worked upon "Lasoing" getting in first washings before I went home to lunch. On Return at 2. found Miss Louise Dua having a quiet chat with Geo. — no less pleasant after my arrival — Stayed until 2.40 — A little after 3. Maud Diven called in — Stayed until nearly 4 — Later, Young Ridner & then Mrs. Greatorex introduced Mr. E. Cunard, the Steamship man — agreeable. Every, as invited, went up to the Diven's. Had tickets to private view of a large picture by de Haas, but the girls seemed to have no appetite, so stayed with them at home until 10.45 — Sick-looking moonlight —

## Right Page

### Saturday, February 16, 1867

Just as miserable a day as possible — almost — Myself divided between giving Young Bell his 4th drawing lesson & working on Lasoing drawing. Young Bell gave me $20 for 4 lessons & offered me $40.00 but I told him that my conscience would not allow me to take that amt — At 3 P.m., went to 10½ Phil. Rehearsal at Steinway Hall. Enjoyed all of it. Beethoven's 2d Symphony, Liszt's [illegible] & Mozarts. Magic Flute. Saw Addie Dunning & the Irelands & the Kimball's. Invited them to spend next Tuesday eve'g with me — walked up to studio with Fan M. & Maud Diven — Rain & mud. Left Studio at 5.30 Geo. Rode up to 52d St. with the girls. Eve'g hard at work mounting drawings — at 11 o'clk a cloudless moonlight night.
