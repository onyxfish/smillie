## Left Page

### Sunday, November 10, 1867

mild, disagreeable day — at one
time in the morning looking as
though it would clear, then
thickening up, blowing during
the afternoon and Raining
in the evening — not feeling
well, did not get up until about
8 o'clk a.m. After my bath &
bkfst did not feel much
improved, so I filled my big
chair most of the m'g — as
like programme for the p.m.
Evening much distressed by
a severe pain in my chest
that has been increasing since
the m'g. I do not know whether it
is cold or flatulency — so I
did not go to Thompson's as I
wished to. After 9 o'clk got
interested in Kane's Arctic Ex[p.]
and Read until after 11 o'clk
then to bed.

## Right Page

### Monday, November 11, 1867

Very mild, Sunny Indian Sum-
mer day — but I'm quite used
up with a severe pain in my chest,
went to Dr. Thropp's before going to
the Studio, Saw there Dr. D.D. Smith
they seem to think my trouble, Rheum-
atic & gave me Remedies accordingly.
Left Many & Mrs Minans there — Back
to Studio about 10. a.m. & spent the
m'g until 12 Stretching Canvases.
Then called on Burling, on Water-
Color Society business & on P. Swaim
Gifford. Home to lunch. Many Teitzen
here — back to Studio at 2.30 and
spent p.m. outlining 2 or 3 little
subjects on Canvas. Late p.m.
had a call from Ogilvie —
Evening Reading awhile in
Hayes Open Polar Sea. After 8 o'clk
Mill, Josie & Clare came in — the
rest of the every until 10. 30
spent in jabber —
