## Left Page

### Friday, November 8, 1867

Mild, Indian Summer sort of a day — up early and at Studio before 9 o'clk. Got palette set & to work at once upon 8×11 in sketch of "Mt Washington, Tuckerman's Ravine & Botspur, from a ledge on Carter Range looking across Pinkham Notch; as seen Oct 14.'67." A little almost as large as the sketch. The top of Mt W— is snow covered, mist wreathes in the Ravine in the middle picture over the blue sky. A foreground of bright autumn foliage & rocks fills the R.h. foreground. I have painted it to be sold at auction — it is said to be very good for me. Went, for a few moments, to Banvard's to see the spectacle of "The Devil's Auction". Saw some pleasing spectacular effects, Fairy grottos and palaces & Fairy dancers — but was most affected by the evident desire of a large number of women on the stage to appear as nearly naked as possible — at least half a dozen must be congratulated upon their near approach to success. One woman, nearly naked, I thought the finest, noblest form I had ever seen — she was large & I admired her grace & suppleness — Home at 11 o'clk — and right to bed —

## Right Page

### Saturday, November 9, 1867

Still quite warm — in the morning promising another clear day, but before 10'clk it rained a little, clearing during the p.m. and coming on to Rain steadily during the evening. Got to the Studio before 9 o'clk and set to work painting 7×12 sketch of "Great detached Rock near the Summit of Chocorua". Fought with a headache most of the day. Home to lunch at 1 o'clk. On Return to Studio found Young Bell waiting to take his drawing lesson — He spent the p.m. drawing; the first lesson of the Season. An Irish gentleman, a friend of his, called & went away with him at 4.30 P.M. Went at home until 9 o'clk. Reading Dr. Hayes' "Open Polar Sea" — then went in to Hill's & was there until 10.30. Ret'd home, washed up some brushes, wrote a little & got to bed about 11.30.
