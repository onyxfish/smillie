## Left Page

### Thursday, September 19, 1867

Up shortly after 6 & took a breakfast alone
about 7 — wrote a Chockaroo note
to the Misses Peirce in return for
one, sent by them from Summits,
Mt. Washington. Then had to mend
seat to Camp stool, so that it was
after 9 before I got off to my Ivory
bid — Painted in leaning trees &
parts of general foliage — worked
with a good deal of comfort until
1:30 then home to a late dinner —
After that set to work of large study
of Moat Mt. painting out my effects
on mts & making it afternoon,
also introducing some clouds into
the sky, as I saw them — Think
the study much improved — after
working out of doors until the sun
got too low, worked on study
in-doors until I could not see
every, a little writing & some
sitting around — Rec'd a letter
from Mlle & went to bed about
11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Friday, September 20, 1867

Hanson, the Postmaster here, cashed my
$50.00, [illegible] check this eve.

Up at 6.30 — Clouded & promising a
dull day — Re-wrote Chockaroo
note to the Peirces & made sketch
in it before 9 o'clk A.M. at which
time the clouds lifted and I went
to my Ivory bid study & worked from
10 until about 3 o'clk, very much
interfered with by strong S.E. wind.
So that I did not get done all that
I had laid out for my day's work —
After dinner, took traps and
went out back of the house and
worked like a good fellow on
a sketch of autumn color, 8 × 10 in
got it done after a fashion by
6 o'clk — It is very crude — may be
of some use to me — at any rate
the time was far from wasted — It
was so windy & hazy that, to my regret,
I could not work in my large Moat
Every playing the Civil to Mr.
Hall who goes home to-morrow.
To bed about 10 o'clk, Some Rain
thunder storms passing around us
Very sticky, warm weather.
