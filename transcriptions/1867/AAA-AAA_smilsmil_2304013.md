## Left Page

### Wednesday, October 23, 1867

Up before 7 — clearing off cold & windy from west — Mfst. before 7.30 at 7.45 Reed & Binney called for us & we walked until 9.30. Seeing many Streets new to us — Packed valise & paid bill at Parker Ho. About 10.30 called on Miss Graupner. She gave us tickets to hear the Great Organ — bid her good bye — Walked around by Mr. Todd's new church & went inside — then to Music Hall. Heard B. J. Lang play for an hour. a noble instrument — but not so marvellous to me as it appears to be to some people — Dinner at 2.30 took train on Boston & Worcester R.R. to N.Y. Afternoon clear & cold — at about 6 o'clk. got a sort of tea at Springfield — about 8 Reached New Haven & at 10.45 Reached N.Y. 8¼ hours Running 236 miles — Got off train at 42? St. & Reached home about 11 — found father Mother & Charl up — So much gossiping to be done that we did not get to bed until after midnight —

## Right Page

### Thursday, October 24, 1867

Another clear & beautiful day, but rather cold. Trunk & valise came to the house early & at once set to work unpacking — went to studio's about 11 o'clk — called on Jas. Hart & saw his studios & those of Theresa, his wife, was much pleased — Home to lunch & did not get off again until 3 o'clk — Called on Gilbert Burling at his studio, Relative to Water Color Soc'ty. Then went on to Snedicor's — saw him & talked business with him — according to him — a gloomy look out ahead — met Wm. Hard & Guy — were at home — Dr. J. on hand to welcome us — got off to bed early & tired. about 10 o'clk.
