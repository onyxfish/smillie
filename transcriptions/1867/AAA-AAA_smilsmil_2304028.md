## Left Page

### Sunday, November 24, 1867

A very disagreeable sort of a day —
no rain, but mild, muggy and
threatening. Mrs went to Dr
Hastings Ch. 1st, Ephesians.
2 Ch. 13 v. A very earnest & able
discourse on being "made nigh
by the blood of Christ" —
Until 4 o'clk. getting some things
straightened up about my
Room — then walked up to
the Park & about until 6 o'clk.
After tea, went in to Mills' —
there saw Gavit & got from
him some horrible photos to
make from them a drawing
of Boston. Begged for something
better — it was promised to me.
Went to Dr Thompson's & heard him
discourse upon some of the mistakes
that have been made by the Prot-
-estants and lessons that may
be learned from the Papists.
Service over at 9.30 — then home
a little writing & to bed at
11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Monday, November 25, 1867

A day of the same description as
yesterday. Every body complains
depressing influences — Did not
get up until late — almost 10 o'clk
then I got to the Studio — Worked
on Water Color drawing, Recklessly
because as I have destroyed the
[illegible]. I am not deterred by fear
of marring the Rest. At noon
home to lunch — back at 2 — Found
Bobbett at studio — he stayed an
hour — About 3 o'clk — the Military
(his Evacuation day) came along —
a muddy display — At 3.45, met
Saml. Colman, went to Burlings
Studio — to a meeting of the Council of the
Water Color Society. Home at 6. At
8, went in to Mills' — at 8, with Geo.
went to Emma Ireland's — Found
her just going out with Will Kimball —
but he very gracefully yielded, spite
of our protests & Emma Remained
at home — Spent the rest very pleas-
antly with her & Mrs. Spring until 10.30
Mr. Edw. Spring introduced later in the
evening. Home & to bed after 11.
