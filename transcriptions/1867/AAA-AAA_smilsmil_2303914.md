## Left Page

### Thursday, April 4, 1867

And yet another glorious day. Sky cloudless, breezy but very warm — Out with my light spring overcoat — none needed. Painted on "Seeking Shade" (7th day) working over foliage terminations three-trunks on either side — I am working against a strong tendency to monotonous green — Stone of Catskill — a sort of painter, called and — Fenn, a draughtsman — Home to lunch at 1. Worked until 5. then walked down to Schans's with young Teel. Eveg. Throop in my Room with father, Mary, Matt & Ge. until 9.15. Throop reads portions of "The Fairy's Search" by Mr. E. [illegible] & I Responded. Reading Drake's "Culprit Fay" & other things — To bed at 10 — Mother went up to Uncle Aaron's with him to stay overnight.

## Right Page

### Friday, April 5, 1867

Rain during the morning, by noon, stopped — Afternoon and eveg. perfectly clear again — at the Studio early — too dark to continue on "Seeking Shade" — commenced a "Twilight" on 7×9 in. & had it painted in by 1 o'clk, when I went home to lunch — Sky poor, but the rest I think above my average. P.M. Commenced another 7×9 — a "Hudson bit" — Worked until 4·45 to 6 o'clk — got it very nearly finished — pretty well tired out pleased with my day's work — Eveg. Roaming about the streets, in at Fleming's — Dickel's & other places — object merely to distribute my blood properly — to get as much in my heels as in my head — Home & to bed by 11 o'clk.
