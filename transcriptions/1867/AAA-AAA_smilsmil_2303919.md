## Left Page

### Sunday, April 14, 1867.

A Spring like day, with strong Easterly & Southerly wind all day. Sky, at times cloudless, then all overcast with a thin film. — M.f., went to P.º ray Tab.ᵉˢ and heard Prof. Hitchcock on the Probable whereabouts of the Soul between death & the final Judgement. He thinks that there is a medium state, neither purgatorial nor probationary — It may be on this earth, where the unclothed soul, knowing pleasure or pain, & doing the Lord's will, awaits the last great Assizes — At noon went with Charl up to Uncle Aaron's & there took dinner & tea. Spent the P.M. Rambling about — at 6.30 got home — too tired to go out, went to bed at 9.38.

## Right Page

### Monday, April 15, 1867.

Very warm and enervating. At the Studio early & kindled a small fire, which I afterwards repented. Stretched a couple of Canvases with charcoal — sketched 6¾×10½ in a Moonlight River bit, and, 10½×18 in, a Storm subject for Augers, to pay for the portrait that he has painted of me & a small head of apple girl that he has given to me. Set Palette & got started painting Sheep on 14×18 in "Seeking the Shade" — Home to lunch at 1. On return met Maud Diven & her Sister Ella. Called at the Studio for about an hour — feel very lazy. Worked ½ a day on Sheep. (9ᵃ day) Ever.f went to Opening of 42ᵈ Annual Exhib.ⁿ of N.A. of D. — Tremendous crowd. got there at about 9 — left at 11 o'clock. Hot, dragged and dissatisfied.
