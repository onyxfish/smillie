## Left Page

### Wednesday, November 6, 1867

Cold, bleak, windy, dusty, dark and dreary November day — taken in bed all day after a very bad night — at Studio about 9, and commenced painting little [illegible] sketch of "Echo Lake & Mt. Lafayette" — I tried to use my black walnut palette — it is too dark and causes me extra trouble in painting the sketch, but I succeeded in getting something tolerably satisfying. Dolph called in the afternoon talking up his sale that is to come off soon, I promised to paint two or three little sketches for him — Every [illegible] and I went to the Kimball's 22d St. Found them in & had a very pleasant evening until 10.30 o'clk then home & to bed at 11.30 —

## Right Page

### Thursday, November 7, 1867

A mild like pleasant morning but afternoon thickened up & threatened to be disagreeable Father at home, not able to go down town. After dinner he gave us a visit at the Studio. I spent the day Charcoaling drawing in mts. Spts. Turfs — three sketches — "Mt. Wash[ington] under snow, from the Carter Range" 8×11 in — "Great Rock near Summit of Chocorua" 7×12 — and "a bit in artist's Brook — Mt. Adams in the distance" 10×14 —
Evening spent at home, I read Canto VIII of "The Faerie Queene" — and a little in Hay's "Open Polar Sea" — to bed early —
