## Left Page

### Tuesday, June 11, 1867

Lovely day — Up a little after 6
& bkfst. Shortly after 7. — Lounged
about, dressed up & with sev. fut-
unable to do as I pleased, because
my trunk is still among the missing.
At 10 sent in for it by Stage driver —
Spent rest of m'g until dinner
time with Charl on High Rock, am-
using ourselves building a big fire —
Dinner a little after one — then a
nap, & then waiting for the Stage
until 3 o'clk when it came along
&, oh joy, with my trunk — all right.
Unpacking & putting away things
until after 5 — wrote a note to
S.C.?. Charl walked into town —
got horse & buggy & with a lot of gro-
ceries & Ed Parker, drove up at 6.30 —
deposited his groceries & continued
his ride until after 8. then Returning
they took tea & at about 9.30 they drove in to
town, Charl to stay all night & take 6.30
train to-morrow m'g to city — Lovely
moonlight night — About 10 when we
went to bed.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, June 12, 1867

Up about 6. Charming m'g — walked before
breakfast which we had about 7.30. After
bkft. Sauntered down to the River — out on
Roosevelt's Point & along the track — Did
not return until after 10. — worked before
dinner about 1½ hours outlining day of
Hartford for N.B.N.C. — After dinner, a
nap & then about 2 hours work on day.
At 3 o'clk Stage man brought letters
from Uncle Willie — Bro. Will & Geo. Geo.
tells me that Mrs. Rutherford has been at
Studio — got the Alice Carey Album drawing
that I made for her & has taken a fancy
to my little circle "On the Hudson" wish-
ing to buy it. Afternoon clouded up —
threatening rain — we need it very much.
Read for about an hour Ruskin. — After
tea down again to the River — about
& very slight rain — Sat on piazza
until 9.30 — to bed a little before 10.
