## Left Page

### Sunday, July 7, 1867

Very warm day, more or less breezy with, at times, clouds that show that the atmosphere is still surcharged with moisture — morning — stole cherries picked peas & wrote to Emma Ireland Dinner late — Spent afternoon lying around, Reading & writing to Uncle Willie — a very lazy sort of a day — After tea, Father Gel— Kate &us & I went to the River & sat upon the Rocks until 8 o'clk — enjoying cloud forms, sky colors & Reflections on the surface of the water — all very fine — Returned to the House & spent Remainder of Evening, until 9.30 on Piazza — moonlight charming and evening breeze Perfectly delicious. We all got to bed about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Monday, July 8, 1867

Weather acts as though it would like to Rain but succeeds only in making a very warm day of it. After H.fst., with Gel walked to Mr. Beach's hoping to get the boat Sullivan had promised to put in the water for us — but we were disappointed — back on the R.R. — waited for papers — J. R. varnish one of his shell boats, then home to work on 6×10 — old Hemlocks on River side — dinner at 1.45 — hungry — J. R. sent & at to invite Gel & me to play croquet — with him this p.m. I got my sketch indifferently in — at 5 with Gel met over — in half an hour played part of a game — tea announced — went in — everybody very amiable — at 6, we were all alone — Mr. R. sen'r & jun'r going into town also J. R. to a boat club meeting — At 6.30 Gel & I went home — Changed dress & again walked up to Beach's — boat not yet in the water — walked back. Met Kate & Father — waited on the "point" — saw 4th of July fireworks in Po. — Moonlight — cloudy — Some charming effects on the water, Reflections of sky — fire-works etc, home after 9. — Cleaned up palette and brushes & to bed before 10 —
