## Left Page

### Sunday, August 4, 1867

Up a little before 7 — glorious clear breezy mf — cloud effects over the Mts delicious — Bkfst about 9 o'clk — Sitting on piazza until church time — then went to Congregational Ch. & heard Dr. Storrs of Brooklyn — gave us a very good, but rather rambling discourse — Psalm 4" 6" — church very full & some excitement when Mr. Scheuick of N.Y. had an Epiliptic fit — After service walked back of the ch. on the hill & then to Artists falls from thence through the woods home, getting into all sorts of bogs & tangles — in at 2 then dinner — Glorious afternoon — enjoyed it until 4.30 when Geo & I strolled over the meadows — in at 6.30 then tea — after tea some chatting on piazza — All Conway walks up & down the dusty road — Very glorious & cloudless — Geo & I walked again — back at 8 — more talking until 9.40 — Chilly — walked again until 10 — then in & to bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### Monday, August 5, 1867

Got a glimpse of sunrise — charming — back to bed & did not get up until near 7 o'clk — Mrs — at Karasarge House — Saw Stephenson & wife off for the Glen House, then back to bkfst — About 9 o'clk got all things ready & Geo & I started for the meadows to begin our first study for the season — I selected a junction of Artists Brook & Saco River — got pencil outline with aid of Camera-lucida upon 12×18 canvas & got outline about half done with Epls turps & brown by 1 o'clk — then went home to dinner — 2 o'clk — after dinner finished skimming Mr. Mowatt's Mute Singer — very flimsey — about 4, off again over the meadows — selected a spot to make study of Mote Mt. & Washington Ridge — then on along the banks of the Saco — a mile or two — home at 6.15, very tired & warm — atmospheric effects very charming, storm threatened from 2 until 4 — then cleared perfectly cloudless — Excitement of the evg, Tableaux by young ladies & gentlemen at Karasarge Hall — Tickets all sold — So Geo & I didn't go — Chatting on piazza until after 9 — to bed at 11.30.
