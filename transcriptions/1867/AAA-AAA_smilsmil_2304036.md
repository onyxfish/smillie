## Left Page

### Tuesday, December 10, 1867

Dismal morning — Snow falling as though in for a regular storm
but before noon it cleared off and almost cloudless, thickening up
in the p.m. and after, a glorious moon-light night. At the Studio by 9
and worked all day on drawing white Mts. Still encouraged —
worked on the foreground, now the whole thing is beginning to
come together. Home to lunch at 1 — Many has gone up to
Uncle Aaron's to spend a few days. At 4.30, went to Snedecors
Gallery and took a look at the Dolph Collection of Pictures, then
to Passe-partout near — then to Snedecors, — met D. Johnston &
walked up home with him. Every at home — trying to clear away some of the Rubbish
that has collected about my Room to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, December 11, 1867

Dull day — threatening snow — Stopped at Schmitt's on my
way to the Studio and ordered strainer to mount my drawing — Got to Studio
9 o'clk. — Worked all day on drawing — on the middle distance and over the foreground,
washing out portions and drawing them in again — Still feel encouraged — although
I wish that I had time and opportunity to Re-draw it —
Home to lunch at 1.30. Every delayed until after 6 before got home. — At 8 went in
Willis's and with him and Joe went to Brainerd's in St. Luke's — met there Jno. A. Gavit —
Played billiards until after 11 o'clk — Then walked home alone — got to bed about 12.
