## Left Page

### Saturday, November 16, 1867

Windy, dull & threatening morning
but before noon it had broken up
so as to be almost a fair day.
At the Studio early, after a walk &
talk with Will, & putting my H. note
in Station G. Worked hard on
drawing of Albany & got if finished at
1¼ o'clk when I went home to lunch.
On my way back called on Burling —
tried to see him this mng, but only
succeeded in seeing the bro. Farrar.
They now have their Room arranged &
some characteristic beauties on the
wall. At Noon saw also P. Strain Gifford.
On Returning to Studio saw yet a little
more to be done on my drawing. Then
went to call on Pawson — out of town —
went to N.A. of D. — back to Studio at 5 —
then home. Mounted drawing — at 6.30
off to Philharmonic — got 3 good boxes —
saw E. Ireland & Kimballs — Then Addis
Dunning & G.P. Hawes — gave them seats
in one of our boxes — Spent altogether
a very pleasant Evng & enjoyed the
music — Home & to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### Sunday, November 17, 1867

Still blustering, disagreeable weather.
mg went to Hastings Ch. & heard
a very good discourse upon the
Publican's Prayer — "Lord — be
merciful to me, a sinner." —
Dinner at home — after dinner,
with Gel & Charl, went by invitation
across the way to see a bridal wreath
of Wax Flowers just finished by a
Miss Jenkins — a very creditable
performance — They told the whole
story of beauty, innocence — etc. etc.
I cannot wax more eloquent than
they — Then went to Will's with Charl
there an hour then up to Central
Park & walked until 6 — Early Evng.
Mr. H. or somebody else for him, Returned
my note, under the front door — all written
over the back — unopened & addressed
to Father, thinking, I'm sorry to say, that
he is the sender — Dilving came in &
kept me too late for Church — about 9
went in to Will's with my drawing, there
met Jno. E. Gavit & old Brainerd. Think
that Jno. E. G. liked the drawing — Home
at 10.30 and to bed —
