## Left Page

### Monday, June 17, 1867

Up late again — that is about 6.30
Day overcast, muggy + thunderous.
After bkfst. did some wandering
around — Stealing Strawberries, etc.
At 10, gave letters to Stage man
at 10.30 went down to R.R. but no
"Herald" — not even a "Times" for
Roosevelt. Then home + again
at work on Rosalie G² 6×[illegible]. Putting
Daisies + Red Clover on the front of it.
Afternoon, got a double Nasturtian
+ added that, finishing 3rd day &
out, for the present. At 3 o'clk.
Rec'd. letters from Charl. Ge² and
Fan Mulford. After tea, at about
7.30 went over to Jno. Roosevelt's, me[t]
him just starting for the River. Joined
him + at his invitation took bow oar
in his 2 p² oar shell — + pulled down
the River — out until 8.15 + had a
glorious time — Enjoy the excitem[ent]
amazingly — The exhilaration of
pulling in a shell, Relieves the
muscular exertion of all sense of
drudgery. Home at 9 — to bed at 10.
but not asleep until near 12.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, June 18, 1867

Up up until 7. another day of same
sort — with sputterings of Rain — Matt
went into town at 10 o'clk by Stage.
At 10.30 again went to R.R. hoping for
"Herald" with the success of yesterday —
got to work at my painting about 11.
Worked until 12.30 — dinner at 1.
To work again at 2 + finished my
7×9 Sketch, Evergreens, Lake and
mountains at 5 o'clk — I am pretty
well pleased + hope to be able to
paint the Rest of the Haseltine
sketches as comfortably here —
About 4 o'clk quite a Tornado
came up from S.W. with heavy
Rain + Thunder after which it
went into a steady Rain for Rest
of afternoon + evening. Got to bed
about 9.30.
