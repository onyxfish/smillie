## Left Page

### Saturday, May 18, 1867

Still April like — Cloudy & mudy.
Young Bill at Studio & took his lesson
paying me $20.00 — I worked in
a helpless sort of a way (2nd day)
upon "Edge of blue woods" that
I painted in some few weeks
ago for J. Q. A. Ward — 7×12 in —
Alfred Jones took a fancy to
it, offered to buy it & I have asked
& obtained Ward's consent —
Home to lunch — P.M. felt so
useless, that between 4 & 5 o'clk
went up to Central Park — took
a row on the lake & walked
until 8 o'clk — Enjoyed myself
nicely — everything charming —
full moon & no clouds — new
cut grass — flowers — birds — all
sweet — Supper at 8.30 — Geo.
gone to Divens — followed him at
9 o'clk & stayed until 10.20 then home
& to bed — Father Uncle Willie aunt
Agnes, Charl, Matt & Kate at the
Academy of Music seeing the
Japanese.

## Right Page

### Sunday, May 19, 1867

Rather cold, cloudy, & windy —
Myf went to Hastings & heard
him preach from the text —
"Wherefore, by their fruits ye shall
know them" — An excellent
discourse, teaching in the first place
care & fairness in selecting the fruit
and next the fallacy of judging a
system or Religion by the fruit of an
individual — As he summed up "By
our fruits, ye shall know us, but
not the Religion of Christ, that is
to be judged by him" — Took
dinner at home — then a nap &
at 5 went up to Central Park where
I sauntered about until after 7
o'clk — Spent the eveg. at home
Read a discourse by Army[illegible]
on Christian Union — did some
writing etc. etc, & did not get
to bed until near midnight.
