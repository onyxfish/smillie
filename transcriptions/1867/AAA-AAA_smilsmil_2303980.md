## Left Page

### Sunday, August 18, 1867

Glorious day, but very warm — went in
the M+ & heard Dr. Storrs of Brooklyn
— a very earnest & excellent Discourse
from 27 & Chap, 30th v. Little by little,
the progress of the world & all that is human
— So too, with the growth of the heart for good
or for evil — Got home in such distress
from my skin trouble that I went to my
room, undressed myself, took a partial
towel bath & stretched out upon the bed
until dinner time at 2 o'clk. Afternoon
breezy & delicious — after some Reading, at
4 o'clk went to Pine woods — found there
Mrs. Loring & Miss Green — chatting with
them & writing to Brevoort — home at 6.
After tea all out on piazza watching
the approach of a thunder storm — the
whole west lit up by constantly flashing
lightning giving promise of a grand
storm — but it parted & passed north & south
of us about 10 o'clk, giving us only a little
Rain — At 11, when I went to bed beautiful
moonlight — Poor nights Rest for
some reason.

## Right Page

### Monday, August 19, 1867

up a little before 7 — charming M+, at
9.30 — 12 of us in two wagons with two drivers —
8 in one wagon & 4 horses — 3 of us in 2 seat
wagon & 2 horses. Started for Jackson Falls —
went over Thorn Hill Road and had a
delightful drive arriving at the Falls at
about 11 o'clk — 8 miles. Discharged cargo
at once at falls — wagons Returning,Carrie me to
[illegible] — small one home — Until 1 o'clk I wandered
about, up & down stream — met Lila Martin — at
1 took Lunch — bread & butter & hard boiled egg —
then made Camera-lucida sketches of top of
fall — & one looking over valley to Moat Mt —
I do not think the falls fine — at about 5 o'clk
we got under way again — driving home by
the Regular Stage Road — we stopped at
Goodrich Fall, which are quite consider-
able for height & volume of water — a mill dam
at the top destroys their picturesqueness —
After delay of half an hour — we got on again
& had a very pleasant drive home, altho' all
crowded into one wagon — back at 7.30 —
Spent ev+, working on sketch from top of
fall — Rec'd a letter from Mary & a paper
from Will — To bed about 11 o'clk.
