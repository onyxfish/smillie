## Left Page

### Tuesday, July 23, 1867

Up about 7 — cloudless m'g & promise of very hot day — at 7¾ — went to B°may House & got a first rate breakfast, then went to Studio — to Silleck's — & to Snedecor's — sold to him 4 of the sketches that I painted for Haseltine @ $20 each — then to Schaus's & got material — then down town — got lunch at Berry's — then to N.B.N.Co — made out my bill for Hartford drawing — $119.00 — & got check — paid prem. on Life Ass. at Equitable Co — at A.B.N.Co & had chat with Deluroe — at Menger's — Kuoy's — Brooks Bros & bought pantaloons & rest. Then to Kelly's — got canvas & ordered stretchers — then home at 6 — at 6.45 went to Bergman's, got tea — then to Studio brought home some things — 8 o'clk — D[r]. Throops at house waiting for me — visited until 9 — then packed trunk & wrote until 10.30 — then to bed after a very hot & [fatiguing] day. But did not get to sleep until near 1 o'clk a.m.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, July 24, 1867

*on board Newport Steamer "Newport"*

Awake at 5 — kicked about in bed until 6.30. then got up & finished packing trunk — went to B°way House at 7.15 & got bkfst, then to Burggraff's — Upp. Res. man's & to Mills — Mill is suffering from dysentery — then to Jennings & Schmitt's & Bergman's & had Rosalie Goodman's box filled with fine candies — 2¼ lbs. then to studio & took H's sketches to Silleck to box — then to Snedecor's & Schaus's — Stalberg anxious for money & closed my acct. — then to Kelly's & got stretchers again to Studio — met there Clinton Ogilvie — to my great surprise — just home from Europe last night — got off Uncle Millie's two 6×10 to Silleck to box & boxed up R.G.'s box — then home — Upp. Man called for trunks at 1 o'clk — then went into Mill's & got lunch — wrote letters to Haseltine — Rosalie G — & finished one to Uncle Millie — at 3.45 locked up the house & at 5 o'clk started from foot of Murray St in Steamboat Newport — chart. Seems us off — after much tribulations got a $2.00 State Room — fare $4.00 — tea $1.00 — Gen. R.S. Butler a passenger — at 6.15 took tea at a stew booth — had partridge — very good table — an immense number of passengers — Glorious breeze — & walked about until 10 — then to bed — N.G.B. but little sleep — owing to its being very warm from ground — swell — got to Newport at 3.15 a.m. — great hubbub — after which I got to sleep & slept until 5.30 or 6. a.m.
