## Left Page

### Friday, September 13, 1867

Lovely mt. again. After bkfst.
Rode about with Mc Millain, hunting
for a large Canvas. Found one at
Randall's — about 12×20 feet. R. harnessed
up his horse & carried Canvas, my traps
& self to the Ivy bed where I was found-
ed some 6 or 8 weeks ago. Spread the
Canvas out over the Ivy, & shortly after
9 o'clk got to work, painting in with a
good deal of comfort the lower R.h. Cor.
earth, grass & driftwood. Again I
begin to think that I will be able to make
a good study of it. After dinner Geo.
& I complimented the ladies of Three Elms
by taking them riding over the Humphrey
Ridge Road around home by Bartlett.
Misses Peirce (3) Curtis, Williams & Mt. Hall. Geo.
& driver & Mc Millain's large wagon & 4 horses.
It was clouded over, very dusty & before we
got home once or twice spattered rain, but
I think the Ride was enjoyed. Out from 4
until near 8. Everybody quietly in doors —
out of doors, strong N.W. wind and Rain.
Gloomy prospects for the Peirces, who
leave tomorrow mt.. Got Rec'd. a letter
from Father. To bed soon after 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Saturday, September 14, 1867

Up at 5.30. Mt. perfectly clear & very
cold — Bkfst at 6.30. The Peirces off on the
stage for the Glen House at about 8 o'clk.
They may go up Mt. Washington. After their
departure waited about until after 9 o'clk
for it to grow warm enough to sit at my
study out of doors. Then started for my
ivy bed — from the meadows waved a fare-
well to the Martins as they passed down on
a special coach to Centre Harbor. At
my work suffered much from strong N.W.
wind & cold — stuck to it, working over sky
& distance until 12 o'clk, when, benumbed,
gave up. Spent p.m. Repainting the
work that I had done in the mt. (work-
ing in the house.) After 5 o'clk set to work
chopping wood to get warm, & got warm.
Sunset with Mrs. Hall & Geo. went to the
[illegible] of the house & waved farewell, as per
agreement to the Peirces, supposed to be
at Tip Top House on Mt. W. — & waving in
return to us — Spent the evening gab-
bing in concert with the rest of the
household and got to bed about
11 o'clk.
