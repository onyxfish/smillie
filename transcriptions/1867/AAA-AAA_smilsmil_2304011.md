## Left Page

### Saturday, October 19, 1867

*Preble House, Portland. Me.*

Up at 5.30 + off at 6 in Portland stage, the air fresh + cool + sky without a cloud. Glorious sun-rise when half way to Fryeburg. Delighted with effects on the way. at 7.15 took bkfst at 7.6.9. Then above entered upon a Road that with but few exceptions was uninteresting all the way to Gorham. Me. At Hiram we stopped + saw the Great Falls of the Saco. The Country thereabouts was fine — many oaks + the color wonderful. Toward noon it got to be uncomfortably warm. Reached Cornish before 12, but did not get dinner until a little after. Had the honor of eating at the same table with a burglar and officers. at 12.45 drove on. Reaching Gorham at 4 just in time for train on York + Cumberland R.R. Reached Portland at 4.40 + drove at once to Preble House. Had a cracking headache + at once went to bed + with slight exception slept from 5 P.M. until 6 A.M. — a feat that I do not think I ever performed before —

## Right Page

### Sunday, October 20, 1867

Up at 7 feeling very well — another cloudless but hazy day. Bkfst at 8 — a very good Bill of Fare, but not much of a bkfst — which will answer in a general way, I think, as a character for the house. After Hcfst walked to the Observatory at the Eastern end of town over the "burnt district" + along the wharfs — taking up at church time at Rev. Mr Walker's Dutch Refmd Ch. State St. + heard him preach + excellent discourse from 1st Ep. John. 2d Ch. 17 v. The building crowded. Mr W's last Sabbath as pastor — inflamation of knee joint has kept him on crutches more than a year — delivered his discourse sitting. After service walked to western end of town. Promenade + through several of the finest streets. Dinner at 1. At 2 we started out again completing, I think, the sights to be seen in Portland. Much pleased with State St. look at Residence of Wm Pitt Fessenden — Neal Dow + others. Eve'g wrote a short letter to S.C.7. To bed at 10.30
