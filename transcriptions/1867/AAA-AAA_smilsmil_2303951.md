## Left Page

### Friday, June 21, 1867

A day lovely almost beyond any other —
clear, fresh & almost cool — Awake at
4.30 but not up until near 6. a little
walking — some writing & at 7.30 bkft.
Mrs spent adding some yellow daisies
to Rosalie G's box and outlining part
on Hartford dg. Afternoon outlined
more on daf. then got outline ready
of Buttermilk Ravine from top — 7×9 —
Bet. 3 & 4 went up East Road to spot
I had selected & got careful Camera
Lucida outline for a water color
study. Returning at 6. Tea a little
after — then went down to River worked
until just 8 getting study of Rocks
that rise from the water. On Return
found that Kate had come — E. Parker
having brought her out from P. —
After much general gossip —
got to bed just before 10.

## Right Page

### Saturday, June 22, 1867

A day like yesterday clear & delightfully
cool in the breezy shade. Right away after
bkft. worked an hour on Hartford daf. —
then got "Sunset among the Mts. from top of
Buttermilk Ravine — Catskills" 7×9 — (Title
rather larger than the picture) ready
to begin to paint — went to R.P. & got the
paper & at about 11.30 began to paint —
got in sky & mts. before dinner at 12.30 —
after dinner worked another hour on
daf. & at 2.30 commenced painting
again — finished it a little after 5 o'clk —
in some parts the color is crude, in
other parts heavy, but altogether, I think
this an advance — Cleaned up & tea
at 5.30 — then with Father walked along
the R.P. beyond Crosby's — Father goes
to N.Y. this eve'g by P.8 — propeller — he
expects to be gone until next Thursday —
glorious walk — left him in very
good spirits — got home a few m.
after 7. Sat around until after
9 when I started for bed & got there
a little before 10.
