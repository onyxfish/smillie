## Left Page

### Tuesday, April 30, 1867.

A worse day than yesterday. Mr. Winans at Studio from after 9 A.M. & gave me check for $100, for my "October in Northern Pennsylvania." Spent the m'f on "A Waterfall — Catskill Mts" 6½×9 in — trying to make it worth $15.00 for Haseltine.
Afternoon spent on cutting a number of sketches. 6×10 & 7×9. for Haseltine.
Even'g on my way home from Studio at 7.10 stopped at Mr. Wm. E. Dodges Jun. to see about the payment for little frame ($17.00) — Both Mr & Mrs. D. made themselves so agreeable that it was 9.30 before I could get away — Got my supper before 10 o'clk. Got up at Diven's — Sat & read until after 11 o'clk — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, May 1, 1867.

and worse yet than yesterday. After stretching a couple of 6×10 canvases, got to work upon a sketch (6×10) that I will call "Afternoon on the Housatonic" — got it painted in by 4 o'clk P.m. I think it tolerably good in color — better, perhaps, than my average.
Thunderstorm & very heavy Rain during m'f — held up while I went home to lunch — heavy Rain again during p.m. —
Uncle Wm. at Studio. At 5 o'clk went to Snedecor's — paid him Winans' check for $100 — & Dodge's for $17 — & had credited to my acct. $19.2.50 for work done by Am. Bnk. Note Co. — Bal. still against me $62.50 — Evening in at Wills for half an hour, then home — Father Sorting die p'fs. in my Room — To bed a little after 10 o'clk.
