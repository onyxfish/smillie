## Left Page

### Thursday, May 16, 1867

aunt aphia & her son Georgy made their appearance at our breakfast table this A[M].
weather, April-like & enervating.
Feel unable to stand up at
my easle — Spent the m[orning]
outlining 3 or 4 7×9 subjects
for sketches — Home to lunch
by general advice, took an extra
pair of pantaloons & started for
Uncle Aaron's — Got there a little
after 3. P.M. Got Gipsey under
the Saddle — went up to High Bridge
Gip behaving pretty well shying
jumping & bolting occasionally
but showing obstinacy but once.
— back a little after 5 P.m. —
Geo. Uncle A. & Tooney came in 5.30
Train — Dinner about 6 — then I
spent an hour or more practicing
on Billiard Table — Every[one] spent
chatting and to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Friday, May 17, 1867

Warm gentle Rain at 6 a.m.
when I got up. — At the Billiard
table again until 8.30 — then
breakfast & at 9.10 took Stage
for city. At Studio at 10 o'clk —
weather clearing up. Morning
did little else than set palette
& varnish over "Peeking Shade" 14×18
Home to lunch — Afternoon.
forced myself to work & worked
over lower sky — foliage & sheep
day's finishing at last, I hope,
and finally this dreary picture.
It looks very bad to me — but I hope
to sell it, nevertheless. Evening
at home, & in at Will's for an
hour — industriously doing
nothing.
