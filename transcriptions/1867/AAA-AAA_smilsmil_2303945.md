## Left Page

### Sunday, June 9, 1867

Miserable, cold windy & rainy
day — up late & lying around
the house all day until the
eveng. Got things together
Ready to pack. Evenin
went to Dr J. P. Thompson's and
heard a Sermon from Mat
7 — 26-27 v. Concerning buildi
upon proper foundations.
After the Sermon went to
Studio — got a number of
things there, carried them
home & packed trunk all
Ready to be off the first
thing to morrow mrg. To
bed about 11.45

## Right Page

### Monday, June 10, 1867

Up at 6 o'clk — Cloudless mrg — bkfst at 7 —
went to see Express man about trunk — prom-
ised to be on hand — At 8. started for foot of 34
St. got there at 8.30 — no trunk — "Daniel Drew"
on time at 8.45 — still no trunk & went off with-
out it — Glorious sail, but rather cool & windy
through the Highlands — very fine — Arrd at
Poughkeepsie at 1.30 — found Charl waiting
for me on the dock. Saw Ed Parker & stopped
for a moment to write a few words home concerning my
trunk then with Charl went to R.R. Station where I got
a lunch — then to Northern Hotel where we waited until
2.05 for the stage out to Hyde Park. In about ½ an hour
Reached Hill-Side there Recd with joy into the
bosoms of our family. Found Father, Mother &
Matt quite jolly — About 5 Charl & I walked
up to Mr. Beachis — I took a look at pony
Tom thinks that Mr. B. has given me an order
that I may get him to use, if I wish — He seems
hardly bigger than a goat; so, thanks Mr. B —
I will not use him — Sauntered home by Rail.
Tea at 7 — then sitting on piazza in the
moonlight until 8.30 — too cool — then ill-
uminated house — 2 candles in bottles — at 9
went to bed. Father & I in a bed (selfish) Mother
& Matt on the floor & Charl on a sofa
