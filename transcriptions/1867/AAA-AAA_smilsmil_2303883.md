## Left Page

### Friday, February 1, 1867

Gloomy overhead & terrible
under foot — Got to Studio
Rather late in the morning.
Commenced painting another
6×10 — "Grain-field near Lenox"
worked faithfully all day
& was Rewarded by getting
a little bit of color Rather
more agreeable I think than
my average — Between
5 & 6 o'clk P.M. walked down
to Schau's & made a few
purchases — Got most mis-
erably sick — Rode home
in a car & passed an unhappy
eveng nursing one of my
old fashioned bilious upsets

## Right Page

### Saturday, February 2, 1867

Miserable sort of a day. Rain
drizzling & falling at intervals. —
Tommy Bell took his 2d lesson
in drawing — He had a drawing
of a deer's head, copied by him from
a stuffed one — I got Auger's deer
head, & worked over Bell's drawing
practically to explain what I wanted.
After lunch went to Derby's Rooms
to see Rosa Bonheur's "Horse Fair" and
other pictures, then to Leeds' Rooms
to see Avery's collection, to be sold
on Monday. Ret'd to Studio at 4.
In spite of weather, had quite a [illegible]
of callers. — home in miserable
Rain. At 8 o'clk Geo. & I went
up to Diven's — Saw all of the girls
& Jan. M. — all had colds in our heads.
— also candy & ginger snaps. Ret'd
home at 10:30 — quite a thunder storm
— bright lightning & heavy thunder.
Found Throop visiting — to bed
at midnight, as usual.
