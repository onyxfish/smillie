## Left Page

### Saturday, December 14, 1867

Rather a dismal day — Sun still overhead and plenty mud
foot — Did not get to Studio until near 10 o'clk. Worked over the sketch
of drawing with very thin opaque color adding, I think atmosphere
+ tenderness — Just before noon
mounted in on my Strainer —
Forming Bell came about 1 o'clk
and I did not get off to lunch
before 1.30 — On Return gave
attention to Bell. In Return
Rec'd $20 00 — To my extreme
disgust the paper on my Strainer
began to wrinkle. The drawing
looks so much better since mounting
that I have Rec'd many Compliments.
Every, went to Dolp his Sale —
2d Swelf. Pictures terribly slaughtered — My Mt Washington. 8×11, brought
$31.00 without the frame. My "Glimpse
of Lake Mackinac" was bid in —
Home at 10 o'clk. & to bed
at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Sunday, December 15, 1867

Raw, disagreeable day — My,
went to Hastings and heard him
preach from the Text Colossians
1:13, "Who hath delivered us from the
power of darkness hath Translated
us into the kingdom of his dear Son" —
be enlarged upon the power of
darkness, Spiritual & physical, and
upon light. He was good and I
was interested, altho, as usual now,
annoyed by mental wanderings to
the Studio — Afternoon, did not
go out for my walk — Evening,
clear, rather windy and cold —
went to D Thompsons and heard
him in earnest appealing "Why
will ye die?" — dwelling upon
the death of the Soul and how
to secure its life — on my way
home stopped in at Willis for
about an hour — then home
and to bed at 11 o'clk —
