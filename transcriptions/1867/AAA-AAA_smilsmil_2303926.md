## Left Page

### Sunday, April 28, 1867

Cool day but clear & delightful. Mrf. walked down to Madison Ave. Bap[tist] Ch. with Br. Will & his wife — Heard Prof. Arnold of Hamilton Th[eological] College on our interest and share in other men's sins — It seemed to me a moderately able, & in no way original discourse. After service, transacted some peculiar business with Messrs. Winans & Tracy Harris. Then with Geo. went to Uncle Aaron's to dinner — The p.m. spent as usual, with Uncles, & & Mr. Turrill & Soony, Rambling about — met & heard Converse, the great [illegible]. Fenian Head Capt. Col. Roberts. Geo. Ret[urned] home about 5 o'clk P.M. I stayed to tea, & did not get home until 8 o'clk — Did not go to church but spent what remained of the evening at home.

## Right Page

### Monday, April 29, 1867

Cold, disagreeable day, at times almost raining — Kindled fire again at Studio. Painted all the mrf. on 14×20 "Seeking the Shade" (11½ days). Painting out two or three sheep — painting in one & bushes, etc. at foot of big tree — Also outlined one or two small sketches for Haseltine. P.M. at same work until 4 o'clk then went down town — At [illegible] N. Co. Saw Chas. Burk & had a plain talk with him — hope now that we understand one another better — Evening called (with Geo.) at No. 21 W. 15 St. On Miss Florence Smith — at about 9.30 went to No. 16 E. 29 St. where met Mrs. Geo. Yewell entertained some friends. Introduced to Geo. Baker, N.A. a number of artists present — pleasant evening — left at 11.30 hurried home & got to bed just before midnight.
