## Left Page

### Saturday, November 2, 1867

Up at 7.15 — weather still very pleasant — got to studio before 9. Finished pencil sketch of "Crest of Chocorua." It is very neat + pretty, but not strong. I am in constant anxiety + trouble because my work is marked by such littleness + gives such small evidence of strength or breadth. — At 12.15 went up to studios, Bdway + 32 st. Called on Kuntze, 44 Fam[?] and Miss Walters. Back to studio after dinner + worked on sketch for Chocorua, looking north. — Had a number of callers late in the p.m. among them A. H. Ritchie — Every at 8 o'clk. went with Geo. and was Registered — Spent a little time at Fleming's + then until 11 was in at Wills — There met Mr. E. Gavit — he was in a civil mood — home + to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Sunday, November 3, 1867

Up about 7.30 — had my bath + breakfasted at 9 o'clk — went to Jos P. Thompson's — communion service — Text: "Why call ye me Lord — and do not the things which I have commanded you" Met Mr + Mrs. J. R. Brevoort + sat with Geo. M. Lyon. — Dinner at home. At 3.20 went up to Central Pk. + spent the rest of the p.m. until 6 o'clk. walking about. Weather thickened up + before 7 it commenced to rain — Father has been very much under the weather to day most of the time in bed. — Every went again to Thompson's + heard him give a sort of religio-political historical address — concerning especially the relations of Protestantism — Papacy + Italy — an absorbing question of to-day.
