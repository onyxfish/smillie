## Left Page

### Tuesday, October 1, 1867

Up at 7 — Cloudless sky & wind still very high — Ther. low — 45°. The snow covered mts were lovely — fixed myself up at a window & spent the m'f painting the snow tops — 9×12 in — tolerably successful — At noon, walked with Gel an hour or more over the meadows — got a newspaper from Father — Afternoon a thin film of cloud came up — just enough to spoil my plans of painting a large Moat Mt. Study from one of the windows — So worked with pencil on "Crest of Chocorua" at 4 o'clk — between discomfort from cold in head & overheated Room I gave up — went to Kiarsarge Ho. — Read papers — then walked then chopped wood & at last took tea. After tea took a lesson in Euchre from Miss Parsons. Rec'd a letter from Patent — went to bed soon after 10 o'clk bunged up with a cold —

## Right Page

### Wednesday, October 2, 1867

Up at 7 — M'f clear, quiet & mild promising us a pleasant working day — but it soon thickened up — at 10 o'clk, deeming the day hopeless, for our oil Studies — with our pencils & paper & lunches, Gel & I started for the White Horse Ledge — Sprinkle of Rain before we got there — We climbed up as near the face as we could get, having a very Rough time of it, but amply Repaid by many glorious passages of Rock Autumn Color & distance — I got 3 or 4 Rough Rock Sketches. Before we Reached the tops, about 3 o'clk — quite a shower — waited under shelter near half an hour — then on — got outline of Moat Mt. & part of Ledge from Summit — a view off towards Carter notch — at 5 o'clk started on Return, getting home in about 1¾ hours — Closing, I think a well spent, certainly pleasant day — well tired, but penciled on Rock Sketches to bed at 10.30
