## Left Page

### Tuesday, February 19, 1867

Weather still fine — Went with Ward, in N.Y. Saw his "Indian Hunter" in Bronze — It is being boxed to ship for the Paris Exhib'n — Saw to Launt Thompson's Napoleon — Left Studio at 11 a.m. Called upon C.C. Griswold — then upon Cheunger — went over to 10th St. Studios & called upon Guy — Wm. Hart — J.G. Brown — Hubbard — S.R. Gifford — Shattuck — McEntee (not in) — Launt Thompson & Julian Scott — At 1.30 took 6th Ave car down town. Stopped at Hulthen's for Geo. Took umbrella to Smith's to be re-covered — took lunch — went to Leggett's, bought "Stubbs on Horse Anatomy" — went to A.B.N. Co. with drawings — a little yet to be done on them — Left with them. Stopped at Snedecor's — walked home 6.30. Ev'g stretched paper for another drawing — abt. 1 p.m. went up to Diven's to meet George — there until 10.30 — home to bed — tired.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, February 20, 1867

Miserable snowy wintry day — Streets all filled up again with snow — Feel very lazy after my days work yesterday — All the work of the day was correcting the two drawings just made for A.B. N. Co. At noon, weather so horrible — wind & snow, that I borrowed a little bread of Brevoort & did not go home — Ev'g took drawings in to mill & made drawing of Josie's new boots with her feet in them — 9.45 or near 10. Nieta Gavish & her Father came in from an Agassiz lecture — All tired out — home & to bed at 11.30.
