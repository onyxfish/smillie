## Left Page

### Saturday, June 29, 1867.

Up at 6.30 — Glorious, cloudless m[orning] —
Father made his appearance just as I got up — having come up on the [pro]p[er]
last night — He brings me the good news
that my "Passing Storm" 14×20 is sold —
Took my "Ruskin" & went up to gate at 8
o'clk to wait for Stage man to give him
message — at 11 found that he had passed —
went down to P.R. and got paper — Read
it until noon — then stretched transp[arency]
of "Hartford" for my drawing. After
Dinner, felt tired or lazy I did not
get to work until late, but succeed[ed]
in getting three of my 7×9 sketches
finished — "Afternoon" — "Evening" & "Hemlock
on the River bank." — I stretched a
couple more of canvases to fill out
my complement. After a late tea
wandered down to River & about — to
bed at 10 — My little orphan Chipping
bird that did so well at first in the
strange nest disappeared & I can find
no trace of it — probably after its
fast it was overfed & so killed —

## Right Page

### Sunday, June 30, 1867.

Clear day — very hot but very happy —
Up late — bkfst about 9 — with sketchings,
umbrella & pillow, spent a couple of hours
in the shade of trees on the little hill in front
[of] the house. Spent rest of m[or]n[ing] in doors. Read —
[It was] a quaint old book published 1707, written
[16]75 by Rob. Barclay & presented to Chas II
[as] an apology for the True Christian Divinity, as the
same is held forth, & preached, by the people, called
in scorn, "Quakers". Dinner at 2 — Rest of the
afternoon out in the woods — altho' intensely
warm, a stiff breeze has blown all day from
the S.W. — at 5, being at the "big Rock", picked
my pockets full of straw berries — more than
enough for tea — After tea & sunset, went
with Kate down to the river — there about an
hour — home, & Father, Mother, Kate & I sat
on piazza & sang & chatted until 9.30 —
to bed at 10 — & then a very warm
night of it.
