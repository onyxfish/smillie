## Left Page

### Saturday, February 23, 1867

Promise of something better in the weather way than we have had for some Saturdays past — a delusion. — a miserable wet snow flurry about 4 o'clk —
Worked all day on 16×22 (5th day) painting in L.h. lower corner. Got my canvas covered, but had to work through all the confusion of callers, of whom there were quite a number, on our Regular reception afternoon — In some respects my painting pleases me — in many, I'm disappointed — It is on a very high key & almost weak — but I hope to get something sunny out of it — Evy went alone to Steinway Hall & heard Thomas's 4th Symphony Concert — Heard "Suite in Canon form" J.O. Grimm — March & Chorus from "Ruins of Athens" Beethoven (Chorus of 80) 2d Episode — Lenau's Faust-Lists — And the grand "Symphony Eroica" — The 2d & 4th movements, especially the 1st, as grand to me as ever.

## Right Page

### Sunday, February 24, 1867

Trying hard to be pleasant weather. Felt unaccountably sick in the mg, so did not go out. Indoors all day — As a proof of my indisposition, I ate no dinner —

D. Throop spent a good part of the evening with me, as he did not feel especially well, or like church going himself.

Sat. Feb. 23d — Young Bell came late — about 11 o'clk & stayed until about 1.30 — Taking his 5th lesson.
