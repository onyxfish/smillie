## Left Page

### Saturday, March 23, 1867

Singularly enough, taking
other Saturdays for a rule,
to-day has been a pleasant
day. Mrs began a little
sketch in oil 6×9 in. intended
for Hazeltine of Phil. Young
Bill came & took a lesson —
went home to lunch at 1,
— back at 2 — a little sweeping
and dusting & then we were
ready for visitors — large
crowds — everybody — tried
to work on my 6×9 — no use —
so, got it roughed together
and left it. George's picture
was very generally admired.
Had a "season" with a number
of Mak. Mears' young ladies —
— home about 7. Tired out —
Did nothing during the eve'g
but lounge in my big chair.

## Right Page

### Sunday, March 24, 1867

Dull and overcast — morning
went to Madison Ave. Baptist
Ch. I heard a stranger very
imperfectly, preach concerning
the Law — Moral, Ceremonial
and Political.

At 3 o'clk. P.M. walked
up to Central Park & about
until about 5 o'clk. when,
worn out, I returned home stopping
in at Will's. Mrs. Ed. Parker
of Po'keepsie & Dr. Throop,
took tea with us — I did
not go out in the evening,
my miserable stomach
being too much for me —
To bed at 10.30.
