## Left Page

### Thursday, February 21, 1867

Still heavy snowing. Got to
Studio early & commenced
painting upon 16×22 in up progress
"On the Hudson" intended for Mr.
Wm. E. Dodge Jun. (3d day)
Painted in sky & scraped it
off & painted it over & over after
a while getting it into some
sort of a condition to leave —
Went home to lunch. Snowing
very heavily — P.M. Painted in
extreme distance — hills & Rivers.
Fan & Maud called in the P.M.
bet. 4 & 5 o'clk. Eve'g Mary, Geo
& I went to Mr. Tracy Harrises in 46th
St. Geo. & I went up with carriage
to 52d St. & got Fan & Maud at H's
about 9.15. 30 persons in all —
Supper at 11 o'clk. Carriage
came at 11.30 — Started at
11.45 — Home at a few minutes
past 12 — Altogether a pleasant
eveng.

## Right Page

### Friday, February 22, 1867

Washington's Birthday — Holiday for
most folks — N.W. promising Rain,
but cleared off, at night blowing cold
& wintry. Morning at Studio, paint-
ing (4th day) over distance & water of
16×22. Ned Dunning called at
12.30. D. Throop at 12.45 — by previous
agreement, went to dinner with him
at St. James' Hotel. D. D. Smith joined
us. Pleasant dinner. Met I. O. Eaton
in Jal. Harts Studio — Called afterward
on us — Rich? Dorman & Matteson called
— hardly away before Fan Mulford came
in — & at 4.30, Maud Diven — Worked
tolerably well through the whole of it,
painting in R.H. Corner. Walked
home with Maud D — Eve. with Fan.
Eve'g from 8.45 until 9.50 in at
Bro. Will's — old Brainerd there &
Mary Sutton (Hasbrouck.) Had quite
a jolly time
