## Left Page

### Friday, October 11, 1867

Up before 7 — everything shrouded in damp grey clouds & my head so stuffed up that I could not speak without blubbering — a pretty frame for, & sweet time to go journeying for pleasure & sightseeing — Mr. J. off for Boston at 9.30 a.m. — Gee & I "free monarchs" etc — I had soon to give up to an outragious headache & [spent] all day on the sofa in parlor — Evef, was so far recovered as to amuse myself until after 10 o'clk, looking at the illustrations & Reading bits [of] Richardson's "Beyond the Mississippi." Got to bed before 11, but spent as now is usual, an uncomfortable night.

## Right Page

### Saturday, October 12, 1867

Up at 7 o'clk. and found to-day like yesterday — only, more abundant. Very much discouraged — this costs us our intended visit to Tuckerman's Ravine — my trouble in the head, little if any, better — Spent most of the mf. over Richardson's book & walking the piazza — had dinner at 11.40 — no appetite — Spent afternoon penning a sketch from Mr. Hayes which grows in my favor — I feel a desire to paint a picture of the subject. Evef, Gee managed to get some Boston papers — which served for a short excitement — we have nothing to grumble at, however, in-doors — we have "the parlor" to ourselves, an open fire place & a cheerful wood fire — and the services of the entire household, as far as we need them. Our horse has nothing to complain of either. Spent the Evef, Reading a little — writing a little & lying about a little — To bed at 10 & had a comfortable nights Rest
