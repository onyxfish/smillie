## Left Page

### Wednesday, September 11, 1867

Glorious cloudless day, worked for a little while after bkfst, penciling on "Crest of Chocorua" — At about 10 o'clk — light all right, set to work bravely on large Study of Moat Mt. & the Ledge — worked myself into a fit of the blues — Mr. Marten & Lida gave me a call in the mg. Afternoon Repainted small study of Moat & Ledge, with small comfort to myself — it may be better than first painting, but not much — Evely, very much in the dumps — Mr. Marten again at the house, waiting for M. M. Lida & Frank M. on horseback — as Frank had to wait & Lida go on, I rode home with her to Pittman's about 9 o'clk — glorious moonlight walked home — feeling first rate got in at just 11 & went right to bed.

## Right Page

### Thursday, September 12, 1867

A day like yesterday — Up before 6 — bkfst. at 6.30 as most of the folks are going on a long drive to Swift River. They did not however, get off until near 10'clk — I rode with them down to McMillan's ford — there found a subject, Ret'd to the house at once, got my traps back again just about 10'clk — worked until 1.30 getting on with tolerable success a sketch of Mc Mls ford. The wind got so high before noon as seriously to incommode me — After dinner at 3 o'clk went to study on the River worked on that until near 6 o'clk finishing the first painting in. Evely with Ges, walked down to Eastman's & called up on Miss [illegible] of N.Y. City — very pleasant. At 9 Ret'd home — Party in from Swift River — also the [illegible] — Ges Rec'd a letter from Father, Miss Curtis Read to us the Ghost Story in Harper, then at 10.30 we went to bed.
