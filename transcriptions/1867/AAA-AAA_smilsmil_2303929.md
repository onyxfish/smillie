## Left Page

### Saturday, May 4, 1867

Fine day, but windy & cold — At
Studio ¼ before 9 o'clk & found young
Bell waiting — He took a less'n —
was delayed until after 10 o'clk
before I got started up on a 6×10
sketch "in Central Park." an
afternoon, Autumn effect. Home
to lunch at 1. Finished my sketch
at 3 o'clk — Think it worth all that
I will get for it — $15.00 — At 3.30
went out riding, with young
Tiffany, through Central Park & out
to McCombs' Dam. Very cold on return.
got home at 5.30 — went with Ge. Matt. [illegible]
& Emma to Phil 25th Anniversary Concert.
Beethoven's Symphony, No 5 splendid,
especially 2d movement. Rev. Franklin
Johnson orated to us from 8.40 till 9.5.
Then Liszt's "Preludes" — glorious —
After 10 — Frithjof's Saga, by Seidenfrans
& orchestra — 50 minutes long. although
tired, parts I enjoyed immensely.
Concert over at nearly 11. — every-
body pretty thoroughly tired out.
Right home & to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### Sunday, May 5, 1867

In bed until 8.30 p.m. with
a miserable headache —
pains shoot in every direction
through my head, until my
brain is fairly cobwebbed with
them — At 9 o'clk took
a warm bath & sat in my big
chair blanketed until 2 o'clk
a.m. when I crawled off to
bed.
