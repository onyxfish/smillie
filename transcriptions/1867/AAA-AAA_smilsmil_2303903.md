## Left Page

### Wednesday, March 13, 1867

Not quite so disagreeable as yesterday, Rain holding
off until the afternoon — Went with Father to dentists at 9 o'clk
he had two more teeth extracted
— All Right — At Studio Re-painted
portions of Cloud Study & Monument
Mt. 8×12. Painted at Lenox last
July — Improved very much, I
think — Home to lunch at 1.

Afternoon, very dark — Started
Charcoaling "Recollection of Hillyer's
Ravine" 24×40 in. & got very
much interested — Worked until
after 6 o'clk. Home during
the evening, doing little
else than read back news.

## Right Page

### Thursday, March 14, 1867

Clear, cold, wintry day.
M'f, charcoaled for a little while
on "Hillyer's Ravine" — then commenced
second painting on "The Hudson
above Poughkeepsie" — 16×22 — got
into the sky & into a muddle
when at 1 o'clk I went home to
lunch. Afternoon, again
at it & got the sky, blue and
clouds, painted in after a
fashion — It does not please
me — After 5. P.M. again went
at my Charcoaling, in which
I am much interested, and
worked as long as I could see,
until after 6 o'clk.

Every at home, more
lazily spent even than
yesterday evening.
