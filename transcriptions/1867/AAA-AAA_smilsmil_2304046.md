## Left Page

### Monday, December 30, 1867.

A cold, windy, half-clear day.
Got to studio about 9. Kindled
fire and started 7×9 sketch from
"Grannis." Mt. Kearsarge from White Hore
ledge" — Old Mr. Riley of Montrose
made us a pleasant call about
noon — Home to lunch at 1 o'clk
& back at 2 — Worked as long
as I could see & at about 4.50
got sketch finished — I think
it about up to my average —
but painting & finishing one of the
sketches in a short day, is to
me a pretty severe test — At
5 o'clk went to gymnasium &
worked about 3/4 of an hour. Much
depressed at finding so little strength
or endurance in me — Home to
tea at 6. Found Tom Gillelan just
leaving — Even'g spent Reading
— for an hour with my feet in hot
water — To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Tuesday, December 31, 1867.

A very cold day, with leaden
clouds overhead. Got to the Studio
at 9, but it was so cold that it was
some little time before I got to
work. Painted all day, on a
sketch-study, that I painted in
Conway last Oct. of Mt. Kearsarge
from the Meadows — at sundown.
Home to lunch at 1 — back at
2 — & worked as long as I could
see, on my sketch — I think
that the day has been profitably
spent — Wife went to Vanderlip
& Taylor's — did a little New Year's
shopping — then home to tea —
Spent the even'g until near
11 o'clk. Reading About's "Man
with a broken ear" — Snow
commenced falling & at 11 o'clk —
3 in. Reported on the side-walk —
So the old year goes out —
I got to bed at about 11.50
