## Left Page

### Thursday, May 2, 1867

Delightful clear day —
Had a long call at the
studio in the mrf from
Alfred Jones — on for a few
days from Ottawa. — At
11 o'clk — commenced painting
"Morning on the River" 7×9.5
and at 5.30 got it done —
I like it pretty well — for
altho' it is not what I wish,
I can see how it should be &
how very much to improve it.
Evef Uncle's Mr Turrell +
Aaron + Aunt Huldah — un-
expectedly to dinner — At
8.30 called upon Mary Dutton
at her mothers in 23d St. Saw
the old lady + 3 daughters — At
9.30 called on Leila Martin —
21st St. + there met Bro G.L. Mission — Scranton + Wilbur. Stayed
until 10.30 — then walked
home in a sprinkle of
rain. To bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Friday, May 3, 1867

Unpleasant mrf, but by
11 o'clk cleared off, cloudless
but cold — Felt rather "played
out" — Took a 7×9 that I rushed
in some weeks ago + spent the
mrf finishing it — Some good
things in it — but on the whole
rather cold + colorless — Home
to lunch at 1. Found Gilbert
Pettit's wife + Mary Teitjens +
her son Fred. P.M. finishing
up two or three sketches for
Haseltine + outlining the cliff
of Monument Mt on a panel
6×8 in. — At 5 P.M. went to N.
A. of D. with Gel. Met and
had pleasant chat with Sibbie
Clinton (née Burnham) stayed
at Academy until near 7 o'clk.
Evef at home writing
out a S.S. Fair Report for
Mary — To bed at 11.
