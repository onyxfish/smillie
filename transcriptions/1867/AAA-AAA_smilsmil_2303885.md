## Left Page

### Tuesday, February 5, 1867

A Real April sort of a day. went to Studio in a smart shower. In half an hour it was all Sunshine, too through the day. Painted in the foliage + tree trunks on "Shade" picture 14×18 — Am somewhat pleased with the color which I think fresh but tending to blackness as does nearly every thing that I do. At 4 oclk P.M. left Studio + took 4.30 train to Manhattanville, Dornan, Geo + I. — Carriage at depot — drove to Uncle A's where Mary, Fau, Maud + Matt were awaiting us. — Dinner at 5.30 — all quite jolly — billiards, a little dancing + some cards. Carriage at 9.30, Dornan, Matt, Maud, Geo, Charl + I rode to 8th ave. took 10 o'clk car. — out at 59th St. — walked to 6th ave. — Cars — G. Maud + I. out at 52nd St. — D. Matt, Maud + Charl went on — home at 11.10. — Just heard that Priscilla Pore has a son. Had a Jolly time at Uncle Aaron's

## Right Page

### Wednesday, February 6, 1867

Clear, bright, Spring-like day. At the Studio at 10 AM. + finished painting in "Seeking the Shade". I don't like the color of my sheep, nor of any other part in fact, but I hope to improve it very much in after painting, as I think that it is in suggestive shape. Annie Ballard called about 4 o'clk. So did Nimans, + so did Teel + a friend, + so did Addie Dunning + George Hawes. Addie came to the city last Sat. We at 8 oclk went to Burlings Studio. N.Y. University. 3rd Regular monthly meeting of Am. Painters in Water Color. About 20 present. After I left (near 9 oclk) I was elected a member. Walked up to N.A. of D. with Bellows, Must + Van Elten. Bellows + I returned to Academy. Social meeting of N.A. of Arts. Punch + cigars — near 11 o'clk J. J. A. Ward asked me to go with him to monthly meeting of Athenaeum Club. — went — more punch, cigars with oysters + salads. Looked at the few pictures + many fellows for ½ an hour then walked up town with Mr. Walter Brown
