## Left Page

### Monday, August 12, 1867

another superb day — my quite
cool — before bfst got pulv. Elm
bark oiled silk & some muslin —
after bfst, made up new poultice
& dressed my arm — the drawing of
last night has advanced it. Rap[idly]
about 10 o'clk accepted invitation of M[rs]
Hill & risk her & Mr Blood & daughter
went up to artist's fall — Spent mo[st]
of the m[orning] there & under the [falls] —
not feeling so well after dinner
took a nap — The Remainder of
the p.m. in shadow of trees in front of
house trying to Read — Eve[ning] at
Kearsarge Ho. learned that Wm Her[rick]
his wife & his Sister Carrie had pass[ed]
through town this afternoon on their
way from the Mountains to Centre
Harbor. At night I Rec'd. a little
from Throop enclosing some musl[in]
Got my "black wash" from the
D[octor].

## Right Page

### Tuesday, August 13, 1867

overcast, wind blowing strong from
the S.W. which toward evening got
around to the S.E. — clouds threaten-
ing Rain — Mrs cleaned poultice
from off my arm & determined that it
was "done" enough — arranged cloths &
applied the "black wash" — the skin
is off from considerable patches on
my wrist — I do not like applying so
much mercury, so in the afternoon I
washed it all clean again & applied
cloths wet only in cold water — I feel
miserably fevered again & there is
much pain & a sore spot in my right
arm pit — besides little bright Red itch-
ing blotches on my arms, legs & body —
I am forlorn — Mrs & afternoon
spent about the house — Just before
sunset, unusual and charming effect
over Mte. Mt. & ledges. Reflected light from clouds
through thin film of vapor — Eve[ning] passed in
almost on piazza feeling feverish & uncomfort-
able until near 10 o'clk — then feeling better —
wind blowing strong & moving around to N.E. with
a spatter of Rain — To bed after 11.
