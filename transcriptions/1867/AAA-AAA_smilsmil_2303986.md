## Left Page

### Friday, August 30, 1867

Cold, cloudy, windy m'g. up about
6 o'clt + penned up on sketches of
Mt Washington that I took yesterday
P.M. Finished it before 10 o'clt, then
Geo + I with pencils + paper walked
up the Road beyond the Interval
Ho + took outline sketches overlooking
meadows to the ledges + Moat Mt —
Did not finish until about 1.30 — walking
home, getting in just before a violent
wind + rain flurry, that cleared away
again in about half an hour — got dinner
at 2.30 + at 3 — as it was all clear but windy,
I took my water color traps + went across
the River to my willows — hardly at work
before it clouded up again + at 4.30, we
had another flurry + clearing — went home
disgusted + until dark worked with pen
on m'g sketch — Eve'g Geo + I called for
½ an hour on Mr. F. W. Edmonds at Cliff House —
Then home + had quite a jolly meeting +
waiting time over the young ladies that are
to leave to-morrow m'g. Rec'd letters
from Rosalie Goodman, thanking me for her
box + from Charl — To bed a little
after 10 o'clt — well tired.

## Right Page

### Saturday, August 31, 1867

Up at 5.15 to see the Misses Hill + Miss
Hall off in Centre Harbor Stage — Mr —
all + misses Grace + Kate Peirce, and I
rose down the Road with them about
2 miles — 5 coaches, glorious cold m'g
(ther. 54°) + some excitement — walked back
reaching Three Elms before 7 o'clt — As
I can see very well Moat Mt + the ledges
from our upper piazza — I commenced, at
last, my Moat Mt Study from there, sitting
in the hall — I could see tolerably well and
worked with some comfort, getting in the
sky + mts. At noon, 2 new boarders came —
middle aged ladies — don't know their names.
Afternoon, painted in sketch on board
afternoon effect over Mt. Mt. ledge
+ meadows — from piazza — worked in
great distress, could n't see for glare
+ reflected light — but got it done by
6 o'clt — after a fashion — Eve'g Geo
+ I walked to the upper Whittaker's +
called on Misses Waring + Dr Horne.
Night clouded over and dark — sat
up for the mail + Rec'd a letter
from Jas. Hart at Lancaster N.H. —
To bed before 11 o'clt
