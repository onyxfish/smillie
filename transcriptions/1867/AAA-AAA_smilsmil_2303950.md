## Left Page

### Wednesday, June 19, 1867.

awake at 4.30, but had not courage
enough to get up. So a little more
slumber until about 6 when I got
up — glorious mng & by 7 perfectly
cloudless. walked out on East Road
enchanted by perfect beauty of the
Catskill Mts. Stood looking at them.
thinking of the serenity, purity & greatness of which they are type — of the
many glorious good times that I had
enjoyed among them, now impossible
to re-enjoy — of the passing away of
things of time & sense, until the tears
stood out upon my foolish cheeks.
Ride to bkft. late — 7.30. Martha arr'd from
town about 9 o'clk. I went down to P.P. and
wandered over the place until 11. Commenced
old Hemlock on River bank — 7×9 & at 5
o'clk. was done. Tea before 6. went to P.P.
hoping to meet Jno Roosevelt & take a Row
with him in his shell — glorious evening
River like glass — No John — went up to
house — he in town — did not return until
bet. 7 & 8. Stayed with him until 7.30 —
then walked out on Road to near end
of Mr. Beach's place — Returning,
about 9.30 — got to bed a few min.
before 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Thursday, June 20, 1867.

awake again at 4.30 — up at 5.30. Glorious
morning. While at bkft. Jno. Roosevelt rode
up on horseback and invited me to come
over & play croquet with him — went at
8.15. Finished one game (he beat me) a little
after 9. Then we went to River. Smooth
as glass, sky overcast with thin film —
Taking each a shell-boat, at 10 we started
down the River — my shell was 30 feet long
15 in. broad. 8 in deep & weighed 57 pounds.
we pulled down to Po-(Kale Rock) & back —
very easily — 1¾ hours. on Return home
took a bath & rub down & at 12 set palette
& rubbed glaze on Twilight on River 7×9
Dinner at 1. At work again at 2 and
at 6 had my sketch finished — in
some respects, very satisfactorily
At 7 went to the River with Matt &
sat on Rocks until 8. Sky overcast.
no wind — River smooth — air close.
