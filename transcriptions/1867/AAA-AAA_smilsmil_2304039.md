## Left Page

### Monday, December 16, 1867.

Beautiful day — quite mild &
thawing in the sunlight. Spent
all the m'g. getting my drawing
of the Strainer & at Schmitt's getting
the panel upon which to mount
it anew. Miss Covell called with
miss — a niece of Rembrandt
Peele, of Phila. Jasper Corpsey
called & paid water Color dues.
Home to lunch at 1.30. My
frame for large drawing came
home this m't. 'tis too wide & very
unbecoming. So after lunch, Rushed
to Purdy's — 15 [illegible] & ordered a smaller
blk. walnut moulding. Then Return-
ed to Studio & Remounted drawing on the
panel. Mr. Craig called & paid his
dues. At 5. went to Purdy's & got
moulding. Cost me $1.75 & looks
much better than the frame that
cost, maybe $20.00) Eve'g at home
very lazy. Will — Joe & Clare
with us. Got to bed about
11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, December 17, 1867.

Overcast day, threatening Rain,
at which an attempt was made
about 3 o'clk. Morning spent
getting drawings properly in
their frames & Ready to send
over to the Academy, & getting
the Studio somewhat to rights.
Home to lunch, stopping and
seeing the Farrars. Gifford and
Crauch. Afternoon very dark —
nothing done — went over to the
N.A. & D. for an hour. P.S. Gifford
& Crauch called at Studio about
4 o'clk. Home to tea at 5.
Evening spent lazily in my
own Room — with lots of hubbub
about me. Matt, Kate & Mother
sewing. Did a little Reading.
Got to bed shortly after 11.
