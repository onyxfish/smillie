## Left Page

### Wednesday, April 24, 1867

Miserable, Raw, cold, disagreeable sort of a day — mizzling drizzling sort of misty Rain setting in between 2 & 3 o'clk P.M.
Lighted fire in Studio — Young Bell spent the day (from 9 until after 4) drawing — I have had a very hard days work, sticking closely at it from about 9 until after 6 o'clk — painting in a sketch 9 × 12 in. "Evergreens" for J. Q. A. Ward — I Rather like it. Ward saw it & says he likes it & Jas. Hart speaks well of it. For some days past I have been working with a good deal of satisfaction & Really feel as though improving.
Evening, claimed the privileges of one tired out & did nothing but a little newspaper reading. To bed at 9.30.

## Right Page

### Thursday, April 25, 1867

A Real April day, altho' rather cool — Sunshine & Showers — Feel Rather "played out". Morning spent a couple of hours or more repainting all the foreground on 9 × 12. "The Haunt of the Eagle" — Removing a cartload or so of coal black — Then glazed & painted upon sky "Evo[?] — on the River". 7×9. Home to lunch at 1 — Afternoon finished the painting and did a little stretching of canvas etc. At 4.30 Ge. & I went to 10th St Studio Bldg to see Bierstadt's "Great Domes of the Yo Semite" — Mr. B — left cards for us at noon — I think it his best — so far. At Studio again at 5.30 — Maud Diven there. Ge. & I walked up to 6 Ave & 41st with her — Emma Bourne at the house [illegible]. Ge. & I by invitation at Kimball's to play Table Croquet — Ge., Woods K— & Miss V. Nelson agt. Fanny — Will K— & myself — Long game, but we beat — then a 4 ball game between Will K. & one — by luck, I beat — home to bed a midnight
