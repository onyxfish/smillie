## Left Page

### Thursday, November 28, 1867

Thanksgiving day & a miserable one — Dark, Rainy & dismal all
day. Up late & did not get to the
Studio until after 9 o'clk. Set
to work at once upon sky of
drawing & used up the day
Repeating washes of blue. The
paper (Real Creswick) acts badly,
probably my fault — the blue
granulating in the texture of the
Paper & destroying all effects of
atmosphere — besides, in spite
of me, a heavy, purplish color
destroys the tone of my blue &
gives me a touch of the blues of a
charming description. Frederick
Pondell called just before I run home
to lunch & Ned Dunning called
in the afternoon. Evening at home
drawing a Sketch in Crayon
suggested on Reading Jean
Ingelow's "Divided" — To bed
a little after 10.

## Right Page

### Friday, November 29, 1867

Very much like yesterday, dark
& dismal the Rain commencing
about 2 o'clk & keeping it up nearly
all the afternoon — Worked
away again on drawing — If my
sky was only near Right, I
would be very hopeful of making
a creditable thing of it — Home
to lunch at 1 — Afternoon
young Tiffany came in and
bid us good bye — he starts
for Paris to-morrow, & expects
to spend the winter in the
Studio of some French master
& return in the Spring —
Every at home — a little
Reading & a little writing
& to bed at 10 o'clk —
