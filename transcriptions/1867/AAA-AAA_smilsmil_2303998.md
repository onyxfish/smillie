## Left Page

### Monday, September 23, 1867.

Up at Sunrise — but too cold to
stay up — Up for the day about 7 o'clk
Very cold and windy — waited
until near 9 o'clk + finding that we
could not work about oil studies
with any comfort — Geo. + I took a
horse + wagon tour lunch + went
over to the ledges — hitching our
horse + sketching as we went
along — we went around by Bart-
lett — then to Jackson + home
over the "Thorn Hill Road" — I
got four sketches — disappointed
at not finding a sketch on
Thorn Hill — Lovely color + effect
but the lines, merely as lines,
ungraceful + unpicturesque —
Snow on Madison + two or three
other points — Home after
sunset, tired + hungry — Got
to bed a little after 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, September 24, 1867.

Up at about 7 — another cloudless
beautiful day — After bkfst. outlined
a little caricature "Chockaroo" for
Mrs. Hall — then set palette + worked
indoors on Reflection study in water —
tiny bed study — at 10.30 went to the spot
and fought away at the water until
near 2 o'clk — amid Ripples, Reflect-
ions + Transparency, I cannot tell
the color of things — Much annoyed
by Rapidly Rising wind — After
dinner, went back to Kiarsarge
House to work on Moat Mt. study —
fought with the wind about an
hour then gave it up — took pencils
+ paper + got camera-lucida
outline of bend in River below
Artists brook — on my way home
met Champney + had a long chat
with him — Evening, strong
south wind + clear — did
nothing in particular until
bed time.
