## Left Page

### Monday, April 22, 1867.

Real April day — Heavy shower
in Mrf — First class thunder
storm in afternoon — Before
going to Studio called at Kimball's
to invite Fanny & Will to the house
this eveg. Mrf Painted in a
moonlight on the River 6½×10½
— all in by 1 o'clk — Bell came
and took a lesson — Home
to lunch. P.M. altho much
delayed by darkness during the
thunder storm, painted in "Mrf
on Hudson — Highlands" 6×9 — think
on the whole a creditable day's work
Jas. Hart told for the first that Friday
last he was made father of a little girl.
At 8 o'clk P.M. went down for Fanny &
there until 9 — up home with her & her bro
Will — Mr. Jessup (Benj.) & wife — Nettie
John & Robert at the house — Chatty
evening — but I had one of my sick
turns & was made very uncomfortable
at near 11 — the Jessups left — at 11.40
the Kimball's left & I got to bed
immediately.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, April 23, 1867.

Glorious day — Rather cool and
windy — got to Studio at 8.15 &
set to work at once upon 6×10
that I call "On the Susquehanna"
By 1 o'clk had it painted in — I
think it one of the freest bits of
color & handling that I have done
So Hart & Brevoort say — Home
to lunch at 1 — Afternoon work
ed for half an hour on "Mrf on
the Hudson" & another hour on
the outlining of 10½×18 that I intend
painting for Augers — A little after
3 o'clk went down to Kelley's, Schau's
& Snedecor's — & Knoedle's, then to
N.A of D — there met Mrs. Wm. Martin
niece and daughter — Lucy Lawrence
Miss Woodward & Gel. left at 6 o'clk
to Studio — cleaned up — they home
Eveng Reading Dr. Brown's "Spare
Hours" a little writing & to bed
at 10.30.
