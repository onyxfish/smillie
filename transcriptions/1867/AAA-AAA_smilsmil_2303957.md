## Left Page

### Wednesday, July 3, 1867

Another cloudless m'g — tired — did not get up until 6.40. Wrote
a letter to George after bkfst —
got it off by stage man & then to
work. 3d day, at Hartford drawing.
Had hoped to finish it to-day, but
it is too much for me — a want
of Confidence & dash in the first
trashing in, as usual, has Resulted
in a certain, characteristic tame-
ness, where I had Really desired
what is known as "brilliancy" — After
dinner Jno. P. sent Pat to see if
I would play Croquet this afternoon.
At 4.30 or nearer 5 — I went over
& had two games — each winning
one — at 5.50 went to the River & met
Rev. W. Corning & his bro. Chas. — They
had just Rowed up in single shells
from P's — feeling the heat very much.
At the River, at 6.20 I left them.
Returning home. — Evening spent
industriously Resigning myself
to the situation. — By 10 o'clk.
succeeded & got to bed soon after.

## Right Page

### Thursday, July 4, 1867

Up at 6.30. after a very close, warm night —
late went into town in the 7.30 stage to spend
the "Glorious Fourth" & next day. — After a bath
& bkfst. I went into the ice house & tired
myself pretty well out quarrying out ice
& lugging it to the great ice-box in the Cellar.
Got at my drawing at about 10. At 10.30
went for paper & Rell a little after 11 & 
did not get to drawing again until near 12.
Dinner at 1.30 — At 3 — Tremendous success
of thunder, hail & windstorms, lasting
almost without cessation all the p.m.
The storms gyrated in circles, starting
from N.W. Hail fell in flat discs, some
nearly an inch across & in smooth balls
some half an inch through — it came in
liberal quantities & was followed in
a few minutes by a tornado with a
small deluge of water. — Thunder incess-
ant. At 6 P.M. J. P. Called for me to
Row down to P's with him. Shaved him &
work — met him at boat house at 7 — just
in time to be caught in another storm —
both Ran home — Rest of evening
pacing about. — To bed at 9.45 —
