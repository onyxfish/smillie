## Left Page

### Tuesday, September 17, 1867

Up before 6 o'clk to see Miss Curtis off in Centre Harbor Stage.
To my mortification, Stage just got away from the gate as I go down stairs — Day very mild. Clouds thickening as the day advanced — No Ride Rain, but drizzle & mist — Spent an hour or so writing to S.C.7. then set to work penning on sketches of yesterday, worked closely but did not finish them. In the m'g Miss Harvey of N.Y., Dr. Gorham, Misses Littlehale & Miller called on Miss Granspner — misses H. & G. played duetts on Piano. Enjoyed Beethoven's 2nd Sym & Van Weber's Jubilee Overture —
Afternoon from 5 to 7. walked with Gil. out to Autinvale Home & back — Felt very much under the weather — dyspepsia — lay down upon bed after tea, slept until 10 — then up long enough to Read letter from Brevoort then to bed.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, September 18, 1867

Very close warm day — Up at 6.30 finished my letter to S.C.7. in the morning — Spent Remainder of time finishing pen Sketches of yesterday at 11 o'clk. Clouds Rolled up, giving us a very hot muggy day — as the weather was evidently very unsettled & I was still under the weather I did not attempt to go on with any of my oil Studies — but at 3 o'clk — with Mrs Hall I went up to Artist's Ledge. Lost my way going up & had a sharp, short clim nearly up the face — a quarter of an hour on the ledge — then I found the path & Returned easily a few minutes after 6.
