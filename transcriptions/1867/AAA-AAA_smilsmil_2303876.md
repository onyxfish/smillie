## Left Page

### Friday, January 18, 1867

Glorious day but very cold,
with a large fire in my grate
could not get Ther. above 5[?]°
during the [illegible]. Worked all
the day upon ABNlo's drawing
getting it nearly finished. At
about 4 o'clk P.M. Fan Mulford
& Amanda D'ven called at the
house. F.M. came to NY. on
last Tues. I walked home
with them, went in for a few
minutes — then alone on to
Central Park — stinging cold —
got my ears touched by frost
& hurried home — Rubbed
them well with snow & cold
water — no great harm I
guess — Evelf. Will came
in — went home with him &
saw Gavit — They go to Stock-
bridge to-morrow for a sleigh-
ride — Pete home — Mounted
ABNlo's drawing — & then to
bed.

## Right Page

### Saturday, January 19, 1867

Another glorious day — cold
but not so cold as yesterday —
Mf. worked on drawing until
noon — finishing it I hope —
After dinner took drawing to
Studio — then went on to Phil.
Rehearsal — Enjoyed amazingly
Overture to Fingal's Cave — After
Rehearsal, Returned to Studio
— not so many visitors as last
Sat. Had the good fortune
to sell my upright Autumn 10×14
to Mr. Allen Dodworth for $90.
— This clears off back Rent & leaves
me a bal. Evelf. Jel. Matt,
Charl & Kate went to Pease's
Concert, Steinway Hall — Dr.
Throop spent the evelf. with —
I wrote to Uncle Millie.
