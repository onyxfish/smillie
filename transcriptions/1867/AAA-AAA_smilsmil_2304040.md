## Left Page

### Wednesday, December 18, 1867

The first day of the hanging of drawings of Am. Society of Painters
in Water Colors — First annual [illegible] Saml. Coleman + I, to do the work —
Got to N.A. of D. about 10 o'clk A.M. + found that the men had just got
the wall clear for us — The drawings piled all about in apparently inextricable
confusion — but we pitched right into the work — A box from Mr. [illegible]
Boston came during the p.m. We kept 3 men at work until 5 o'clk P.M. Then
with a splitting headache I went to studio, made a cup of tea, drank it + ate two
slices of bread + stretched myself upon the lounge until 7 o'clk — Then went to Academy
and continued the work until 10 o'clk — Got home at once + to bed by 11 o'clk,
well tired out — Slept badly during the night continuing my duties as a member of the
hanging Committee until morning —

If one undertakes to perform such duty conscientiously, the trial, vexation + care, can
hardly be over estimated —

## Right Page

### Thursday, December 19, 1867

was waked at 6.30 — Got off to Academy before 8 o'clk — Among the first there — At
work at once — Sometimes keeping 6 men going — Valuable, desirable + large drawings
continued to arrive until 3 o'clk P.M — A large box came from Phila. about noon —
English drawings from W.J. Linton arrived later. At noon, Colman, Burling + I
got together + had a good dinner; back again + at work — Owing to good things coming in
so late, Rehanging had to be done — We had not expected to occupy the corridor — It had occupied
all available space in the East Room, even to the curbace, the Council gave us permission to extend, which
did, at last occupying all of the Corridor except a small space in the S.W. Cor. —
At 7.30 P.M. we got done after 19 hours of hard work + hanging 278 pictures — Then went
home — took tea, Rested a little, Re-dressed + back to Academy at 9.15 — Opening to
Members of N.A. of D., of Water Color Society and press — a pleasant time + met some
good men. Mr. Rosenberg very much dissatisfied with the hanging of his drawing — At home
at 11.30 + to bed — Gilbert Berit [illegible] at night —
