## Left Page

### Wednesday, September 25, 1867

Up at 6.30 — Weather very threatening.
Clouds low, blue & cold — At 9.30
went to dry-bed, determined, if
possible to finish my study, having
the drift-wood yet to paint over —
Sunlight for a few moments struggled
through rifts in the clouds, just busy
to show me the effect — quite benumbed
by the Cold & wind — At 11.30 had to pack
up traps quickly — Storm coming down
from N.W. Got in at 12 — a few minutes
before the Rain — Continued work in-
doors on study — finishing about
until 4.30 when Geo. & I walked down
the Road about a couple of miles — The
Rain having ceased — Evening
went to Cobblers — Doctors and
Express — got piece of drawing
paper — When I got home, Miss
Graupner played on piano for
us — A letter in Clay me from
Mrs Hall — Sent her Caricature
to her — Got Rec'd. letter from Tom M.
To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Thursday, September 26, 1867

Up about 6 o'clk — Chopped wood for
half an hour — then [illegible] — then wrote a
letter to Charl — Clear, cold & very
windy day — At 9 o'clk seeing that I
could do nothing with my oil studies
I took lunch, pencils & paper & walked
down the Road about 3 miles to a place
that I had already picked out — Got a
sketch of bend in River & Chocorua
& two or three more, River bits in the
immediate vicinity — Did not get
through with them until after 3 o'clk
then, benumbed with the Cold I started
home following the banks of the
River — a very winding way — Soon
got warmed up — Stopped to get
another sketch — got home just at
Sundown — pretty well fagged out,
blown away & hungry — Evn'g wrote a
letter to Snedicor — Misses Littelhake,
Mrs Payton & Mr. Chase came to
hear, & heard, Miss Graupner play
on piano for nearly an hour —
Got to bed about 10 o'clk —
