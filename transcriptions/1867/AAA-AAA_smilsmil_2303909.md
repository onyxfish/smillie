## Left Page

### Monday, March 25, 1867

Mxf, threatening Rain, but broke away before noon
& by eve'ff it was quite pleasant.
Worked again upon 16×22
"Hudson River" (14th day) com-
mencing upon the sky, glazing
over the blue with Madder Lake
destroying a tendency to green —
Scumbling a warm tint over
the lower sky & glazing with
black, Madder Lake & Mars yellow
the water & distant hills — The
water is still too high in key.
Eve'ff at home Charcoaling
"Clytie" of which subject
Loops is painting a picture —
I have stolen Loop's figure
& mildly caricatured the king.
In my effort, literal to the
text, I have Represented
Clytie as "Taking Root".

## Right Page

### Tuesday, March 26, 1867

Glorious day, — Cloudless,
rather windy, but quite mild —
at 8.30 Rode down to Bleecker St.
Called on Lauthier, Paid Fain M's
bill ($15.00) at Krauss — called upon
Gifford — P. Swain — at his Studio. cor.
B'away & 4th — on Snedecor — & then took
a look at a collection of paintings at
Leeds' Rooms to be sold to-night —
At Studio at 10 o'clk. Found there Mrs.
Misses Ella & Maud Diven — then
Mrs. Winan's called & approved of her
picture — Worked (15th day) on 16×22 —
Home to lunch abt. Worked over l.h.
& forground Rock — Long letters from
Clinton Ogilvie at London & Mr. J.M.
Main. Mt. & Mrs. Knudsen at Studio
at 6.40 called at house of Wm. E. Dodge Jr.
& saw him — made arrangements for him
& Mrs. D. to call at Studio to see my
16×22 "Hudson River". After dinner
went up to Diven's where Gel. & Mary
met me — Ella & Maud out — Saw May
& younger sister. Home at 10. To bed
at 11 o'clk.
