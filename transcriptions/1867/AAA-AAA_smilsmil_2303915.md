## Left Page

### Saturday, April 6, 1867.

Clean, crisp & windy — my at the Studio at 8.30 Young Bell came & took a lesson. I painted in another 7×9 — from a night, overlooking water at a distance — am quite pleased & in the humor for work. Strange that after working in real misery on a picture of so much importance to me as Mr. Dodge's picture — unable to do anything to please myself — I should suddenly take a working streak & be able to do better work than anything that I have yet done. P.M. Had a very pleasant reception at Studio. Evef at home until 9.15 — then went into Will's was smoked out as usual, in about 3/4 of an hour. Home & to bed by 10.30. Mother came home from Uncle Aaron's in the afternoon. Throop, Mary & Matt drove up for her.

## Right Page

### Sunday, April 7, 1867.

Another fine & windy day — morning, went to B[r]wy [illegible] Ch. & heard Prof Hitchcock on the great mysteries of the Bible. The lesson he drew was "Humility, wise discretion and gracious obedience." — Home to dinner. Afternoon, napping until 3 oc[k] then went up to Central Park & walked about until after 6 — Home to tea pretty well tired out — with Geo, went again to hear Prof Hitchcock. Text, Matt. 5th. 47: "What do ye more than others". A very able discourse treating of the Christian Character broadly and as a power.
