## Left Page

### Sunday, March 31, 1867

Glorious cloudless day and quite warm — overcoat uncomfort-
able. Mrs. heard Prof Hitchcock
13th way Tabernacle — Confess that owing
to difficulties within & without
I did not properly hear the discour.
After service, met Charl & rode
by 8th Ave cars up to Uncle Aaron's.
Took dinner & spent the afternoon
Roaming about — delicious — at
20 m to 6 started for home —
Evening again went to 13th way
Tabernacle & heard Prof H.
Acts 26 Chap. 26 v — Agrippa's
belief and yet, unrepentance of
sin — a sermon that came home
very severely, to myself — After
service went into Mill's for a few
moments — met there old Brainerd
Will Gavit, Meta & Charley Adams.
Home ab 9.20 & to bed ab 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Monday, April 1, 1867

Pelting Rain in the morning —
worked all day on illustration
of Miss Alice Carey's poem for
Mr. Rutherford's album — drawing
the vines & mounting the central
drawing upon Bristol board —
This is the 3d day that I have
devoted to this work to be given
to Mr. R. Home to lunch at 1.
Afternoon cleared off delight-
fully — At 6 o'clk drove up
to Uncle Aaron's met him, Tommy
& George — Took dinner and
stayed all night — From 8
until 10 o'clk, P.M., playing
Billiards — to bed at 10.30
miserably sick.
