## Left Page

### Wednesday, October 9, 1867.

Drove 29 miles to day — at Lancys — Gorham N.H.

Up at 7 — In desperation at last determined to start up on our [W?]
trips — We soon got through with our adicus — Mr. Parsons being particularly kind on parting, & at 10.30, with a little grey horse of Eastman's we started —
Mild day, south wind & mackerel sky, all promising rain — Before noon it clouded over quite thick, but the mts
tops were not covered — we enjoying the ride very much — eat our lunch at 1 ock as we rode along — horse behaving badly, so that we thought he would break down —
at 2 stopped & gave him some oats — better —
at 3 Reached Glen Ellis Fall — much pleased —
Rain began to fall — hurried on — some grand pictures, know, & storm clouds in Tuckerman Ravine. Reached Glen House at 4. determined
to Rest horse & push on — had full view of all the mts — gave horse Corn & at 4.45 on again for Gorham, Reaching Lancy's at 6 o'clk — 29 miles for the day. Two gent[lemen] from Boston Gillies & Dawson —
took tea, spent evef chatting & at 10.30 to bed — weather still looking dubious.

## Right Page

### Thursday, October 10, 1867.

Drove 12 miles to day.

Little sleep after day light, my cold troubling me — Clouds breaking away & promising a good day — Before 6.45, walked to town &
back — 2 miles — B[reakfast] at 7.30 — at 9, W[?] G & friend Geo. & I started for the tops of W[hite] Hayes — going through the meadows back of Lancy's — by boat across the River — then up until we struck the Regular bridal path —
Reached the summit a few minutes before 11 — a very disagreeable climb to me — owing to my cold, chilling & overheating — nose & ears stuffed up — Strong N.W. wind on summit —
a tone Sketch — atmosphere very milky —
Started down at 12.15 — got home at 1.30 — altho' I saw much fine color & some fine Rocks & bits —
altogether I was disappointed — At 3 o'clk — G & I drove down to Lead Mine Bridge —
By the time we got there, sky quite overcast but mts. clear — we each got a sketch — drove a little farther on & Ret[urne]d home at 5.40 —
Mr. G's friend Richardson, went west on the 5.30 train — Spent the evef with G. & Geo. chatting until 10.50. then to bed & passed another miserable night.
