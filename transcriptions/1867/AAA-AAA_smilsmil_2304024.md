## Left Page

### Thursday, November 14, 1867

Clear & windy, thickening up and blowing harder during p.m. but clearing
again beautifully during the eve.
Had a miserable days work on
the outlining of drawing of Albany
— a poor subject at best — & the
photo very imperfect. Had a
number of callers, among them
Johnston (maj. mc.?) Dalph, Tiffany
and Will Chandler. Evening
with Gel went up to Divers,
in 52d St. Saw the old lady
and Allie (getting, by the way, to
be a very pretty girl.) Miss Helen
was out to the theater & Amanda
is at Towanda. We left there
at 9.30 — then home — a little
Reading & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Friday, November 15, 1867

Charming wr. — at Studio before
& commenced at once upon
drawing of Albany — (2d day) Had
lunch at the Studio — and at
2.30 went to Philharmonic Re-
hearsal — Enjoyed immensely
the Pastoral Symphony and felt
more than ever puzzled at the
fascination of Sistres music
for me. I feel that it is mid-
almost barbaric — but its
power is immense — some
passages have a weird energy
that thrills through & through me.
Mazeppa was Rehearsed to-day.
Eveng. at home (after a few
minutes at gymnasium) writing
business letters to members of Water
Color Society. Mr. P.H. — met
father in 5th Ave. this eveng. and
addressed him in a character-
istic — I felt obliged to write a
short note to Mr. H.

To bed at 11 —
