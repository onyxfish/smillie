## Left Page

### Sunday, January 20, 1867.

Miserable dull day threatening another storm — Did
not go out to Church as
I have an addition to my
cold. Father kept me company
in the mf. Toward evef.
a regular snow storm came
up, that by midnight was an
old fashioned out and outer —
fine, dry snow, sifting & drifting
into & through every crack and
cranny. Father went through
it all to church in the evef.
Throop spent the evef. with
Mary. To bed before 11 o'clk
— nearly suffocated during
the night as I had done the
night before, with a stuffed
up nose.

## Right Page

### Monday, January 21, 1867.

Storm continued through
the morning. Everything presents
splendid wintry pictures.

Worked yet a little more on
A.B.W. Co's drawing during the
mf., deepening the effect.
Afternoon drew with lead
pencil the old jagged spruce
trees & the rocks on the crest
of Monument Mt., of which
I had made an outline sketch
in July 1865. Evef. worked
on same until bet. 10 & 11 o'clk.
Then to bed fearing a renewal
of the suffocating experience
of the past two nights, but my
nose rose superior and I
suffered but little inconven-
ience.
