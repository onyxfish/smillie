## Left Page

### Friday, December 6, 1867

Dull, threatening weather day —
Got to Studio before 9 + pitched
into Boston drawing getting
it done by one o'clk — I think
considering the materials I
had to compound the drawing
from, it is rather creditable, alt
its tendency is to flatness and
weakness — at 1 — fixed up a
lunch + at 2.30 went to Phil.
Rehearsal — Heard with great
pleasure Schumann's Symphony
in C, 4.45 when Rehearsal was
over, found it Raining — Ran to
Snedecor's + [illegible] man —
then to Coffee's plaster shop + [illegible]
Casts for Alfred Jones — then to Studio
then home — Evef took drawing in to
Wills — showed it to him + Mr. E. Gavi[illegible].
Returned home + mounted it — wrote to
Jones + Wm Hart + a lot of Receipts
to members of Water Color Society.
Worked hard until 11 o'clk
then to bed —

## Right Page

### Saturday, December 7, 1867

Got to the Studio at 8.45 and
at once set to work to get every-
thing in order for our general
Reception — George + I swept out
then I spent more than an hour
in the floor mending the carpet —
By 12 o'clk, we were all ready,
dusted + arranged, but visitors
did not begin to come before 1. —
The afternoon was squally, at
times clear, then a flying snow
flurry, but the number of callers
seemed to me quite large — At
times the crowd in our Room was
so great as to prevent getting around
altogether. The excitement was
agreeable to me. Evef, found
Emma Bourne at home to spend
Sunday with us — went in to Will's
for a few moments — then to Fleming,
then up to Diver's — visited with Mend
until 9.30 — then home — Glorious Moon-
light night — Reading and
to bed at 11 —
