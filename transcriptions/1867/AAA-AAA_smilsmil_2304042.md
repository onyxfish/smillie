## Left Page

### Sunday, December 22, 1867

A most miserable sort of day. Snowing hard when I got up
which was not until about 11
o'clk. Remains of my headache
still hanging low down at the
base of my skule — Took a light
slept about 12 & my dinner at
1 — Bleak, blowing & raining
during the afternoon — Wrote
to S. C. 7. About 5 lay down
& got a little nap — tea at 6 —
Evening quietly in my room
& finished letter to S. C. 7 —
got to bed before 12 but lay
awake for a long time —

## Right Page

### Monday December 23, 1867

Up early but did not get to the studio
until about 9 o'clk. Made first
went with Colman over to the N.A. of D.
to see about some changes in hanging
the drawings & to see to some new draw-
ings that have come in since the
exhibition opened — was kept until
near 11 o'clk. then back to studio
& Repainted sky on 6×10 oil sketch
that I made to guide me in my
large water color drawing. On my
way home to lunch stopped at Wood's
Gymnasium, got 4 tickets for the per-
formance to-night & walked up home
with him (Wood) P.M. — Spent in
Repainting mts & middle distance of
sketch but did not get it finished.
At 5.30 — home — wrote to Van Ingen on
Society business & balanced my accts
at 7.30. Matt, Kate, Charl & I went
to the Gymnasium & spent a pleasant
evening — Jas. went up to Diven's —
Home & to bed at 11.30
