## Left Page

### Thursday, December 12, 1867

Heavy Snow storm all day — a very severe storm — all travel stopped or seriously impeded — Snow drifts every where — At the Studio at 9 worked all day upon drawing, getting it more harmoniously together. Lunch at the Studio — Rec'd a letter from old Matthew Vassar in answer to my application for the loan of one or two drawings in the College collection, for our Water Color Exhibition. Eve'g, storm still tremendous. And Dolpbs sale commences this eve'g — After struggling home, spent the evening cosily & thankfully in my room enjoying the fire & many comforts. Reading & chatting until 10.30 o'clk when I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Friday, December 13, 1867

Very cold day — most of the time clear. After getting paste ready making other preparations for mounting my drawing, got to Studio a little after 9 o'clk — Worked away upon my drawing finishing generally — Rubbing out some Evergreens in middle distance — simplifying lines & broadening the effect, wherever I knew enough to do it, until 2.15 when I went to Phil. Philharmonic, heard Schumann's Symphony. &c. — then to Knoedlers — got paper — Saw Snedecor & [illegible] — [illegible] man — Then to Studio, my ears showing the effects of frost — after getting strainer from Schmitt — mounted paper over muslin on it — getting home to tea about 7 o'clk — after 8, went in to Wild's — then met Gavis with one of his microscope — there until 10.15 — then home and to bed at 11.30.
