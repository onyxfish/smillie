## Left Page

### Wednesday, July 31, 1867

*Kiarsarge House, North Conway*

Woke at 5 with a racking headache.
At 8.30 got up with great pain, dressed
& got down stairs — perfectly pure, clear
mrg — went in to breakfast, but could
not eat it — gave up & went to bed
where I stayed all day —. In the
afternoon Geo. went riding with Mr.
& Mrs. Stephenson, fellow passengers
with us outside on the coach, yesterday.

With all my pain I was very sick,
but fortunately for one slept nearly
all day —. At 8.15 got up and at 7
was able to take tea — after tea walked
north of the town with Geo. for about a
mile, returning about 8.30 — Met a
gentleman who spent last night on top of
Mt. Washington — he says water froze & that they
had quite a cold time of it — went to bed
about 11.15 & got to sleep about midnight.

## Right Page

### Thursday, August 1, 1867

Awake at 5.20 and up about 6 — had bkfst.
at 7, then set about finding a boarding house —
Looked at rooms in the house of Thompson, a
br. of the proprietor of the Kiarsarge House — met
to the McMillen house & saw McM. — Talked
with him & then went to Mr. Champney's — a
Boston Artist, who has a house here — We intro-
duced ourselves to him & were very well Recd. —
went with him to his sketching ground for the
day, then bid him good mrg. Returning to hotel,
took horse & buggy — drove to Thompson's & took a
Room at $16 on the week, for the two of us — then drove
out to the ledges — visiting "Diana's bath," "the Devil's
Glen," "the Cathedral" — & "Echo Lake" — very
much pleased with our drive — the distant
mts., the ledges & the meadows of the intervale —
back at 12.30 — packed up trunks & cleared our
room — dinner at 2 — some picking of teeth
& watching a pic-nic party get off — then had our
trunks transferred to our new boarding place & spent
most of the p.m. getting to rights —. Tea at 6.30 —
everything agreeable — about a dozen boarders, most
of them young people — walked down to P.O., met Miss Black-
well — was told that the Martens are at the Pittman
House — Spent most of the evening at Kiarsarge
House — Rell at 9 — chatted with two [illegible] ladies
of our boarders, don't know names — to bed at 10.40
