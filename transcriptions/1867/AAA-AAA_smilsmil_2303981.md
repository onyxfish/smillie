## Left Page

### Tuesday, August 20, 1867

A very fine day, altho' rather clouded
& windy — Spent the morning wandering
over the meadows, up & down the brook
seeking for just what I want in the
way of a sketch — did not find it — also
with a shovel, tried to remove, or cover up
the ivy that poisoned me, that I might
finish my study — did not succeed — too
much to do — After dinner, at 3 o'clk
Geo and I must up Peaked Mt. Gobups
without trouble, in about an hour & a half
to the shelving Rock — jigged & slid over
it to the ledge — The afternoon delicious
effects wonderful — Got a couple of
sketches — one of Chocorua & another of
mote & ledges — Mt. Washington under
a cap of cloud, but the view up the valley
very fine — Waited until sunset — very
much delighted — Saw it from the highest
peak — Got home after 8 o'clk — tired —
Spent the evening chatting however —
To bed before 11 — Have suffered very little
to day from my poisoning — but was up once
during the night & had to rub my legs
with strong brine — in which I have found relief

## Right Page

### Wednesday, August 21, 1867

*On top of Kearsarge Mountain*

Up shortly after 6 — all ready & off, 10 of us in one
large wagon for the foot of Kearsarge at 8.30 — got there
in about an hour — At 9.30 started up the Mt. Misses
Greene & A. L. Peirce on horses — I & the guide afoot —
the rest of the party following — We reached the sum-
mit at 11 o'clk — not at all fatigued after a glorious
climb — atmosphere just the thing — view, of course,
beyond description — got a fire going & tea pot
over — Remainder of party reached top at 11.55 — had
our dinner in 2nd story south room of tumbling down
house — very jolly — every thing & every body on the floor —
from 10 clk until nearly 6 — hard at work taking
camera lucida sketches — then we had tea out
on the Rock — if every thing else was great, what can
I say of this time — but few clouds — color marvellous —
slanting light & long shadows — the shadow of
Kearsarge, a perfect cone, stretching away to the
east & just as the sun set plainly seen by all for
2 or 3 minutes, extending up above the horizon. It's
place on the sky — the sun slowly sinking — all
of us in transports — the sky, melting horizon &
many lakes to the east, the loveliest picture —
Sun set at 6.30 — at 7 we started down, very unwilling
to leave. Were nearly ¾ of an hour reaching the woods,
all the way enjoying the waning day & evening in the
valley — wood quite as dark as any cellar — much
fun & time & mud in getting through — out of the woods
at 8.35 — five minutes longer getting down than getting
up — the 3 Misses Peirce, Miss Green & myself rear party —
found Emerson & the wagon with Mr. Blood very impati-
ently waiting for us — a jolly drive home reaching
the Three Elms just about 9.30 o'clk — nobody tired
of course — all quite frisky — girls only sorry that they
had not stayed on top all night — Rec'd letter from Matt —
a new boarders, Mr. & Miss Hale — both young — To bed at 11.
