## Left Page

### Saturday, October 5, 1867

Steady, down-pouring Rain and my cold worse than ever — my stock of patience is being rapidly exhausted — a few more such days & I will be homesick — Braved the storm after bkfst & went to Kearsarge Ho. hoping to find money by Exps from Snedicor — no money — then went to P.O. & got a letter from Father — then home & put up easel in parlor & worked all day on Large Mout. Mt. study trying to get it into some sort of presentable shape. I have made some grand mistake upon it, but as yet do not know enough to know where, or what it is —

Evg. borrowed Severn's "Days Ride or Romance of a Life" & Read until 11 o'clk — with very little satisfaction so far as the book was concerned. Rick — a short letter from Mary — To bed at 12 & slept soundly through the Rest of the night.

## Right Page

### Sunday, October 6, 1867

Up at 7.30 — a clear, cold, very windy morning — altho' I slept well during the night, my misery is more gen-eral than it has yet been — Throat very sore, ears stuffed up and head aching — went to Kearsarge Ho. before bkfst — no money yet — After bkfst went to bed & slept all the morning. During the afternoon wrote to Kate & to Snedicor — Took tea in a little S.E. Room with a pleasant open fire, abandoning the large, cold & cheerless banqueting hall — About 7.30 Preusman Van Elten & Nicols called — Saw our studies and stayed until about 9 o'clk — They have just come from the mts and are on their way to Ny. City. At 10 got a pailful of hot water — bathed my feet for 15 or 20 minutes, took a big dose of whiskey & hot water — went to bed piled lots of clothes over me & slept pretty well until the mng.
