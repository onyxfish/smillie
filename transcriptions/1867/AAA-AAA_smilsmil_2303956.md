## Left Page

### Monday, July 1, 1867

Another fine day — not so warm as yesterday. Wrote a letter to Charl George. I waited for stage mail — from [illegible] 10 o'clk — then went to P. P. for paper — Train 10.30 — waited until after 11 — then up to the house — just as I got there train passed — R.R. and get [illegible] paper — mf all gone in such work — bfore dinner, commenced on "Hartford" drawing & got the sky washed in — worked faithfully all the afternoon. After tea went down to the River — hoping to find Roosevelt & get a Row — unsuccessful — then also to the house and out on the East Road — delightful — far pleasanter on the hill than down by the River — Mother & Kate to bed early — Father & I pacing piazza — to bed at 10. — Showers during the night.

## Right Page

### Tuesday July 2, 1867

Glorious mf — up before 6 and out on the East Road — the Catskills Mts as noble — inspiring in their messages as ever. Kept early, & to work early — that is, shortly after 8 — worked at drawing until I heard paper train — went, got paper — Saw P. at his boat house — Spent nearly an hour with him — back again to work. Stuck to it until 5 o'clk — making tolerably satisfactory progress — Mr. Parker came out in afternoon stage bringing letters from Gel & Matt. Took my tea alone at 5 o'clk & at 5.45 was waiting for P. at his boathouse by appointment — at 6 — were off in single shells, for P. to see a Scull Race — 3 rowing boats — Saw it — no excitement — Sky cloudless — air like wine & water like oil — no wonder that I enjoyed myself — pulled better than ever in my shell — altho' that is not saying much — In at 8.20 during a glowing, dying twilight — "day's golden death" — Found E. Parker at the house — horse & buggy — took his mother away [illegible] — we got to bed at 9.45.
