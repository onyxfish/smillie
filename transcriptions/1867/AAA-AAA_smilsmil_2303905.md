## Left Page

### Sunday, March 17, 1867

Miserably out of sorts, and
the day in accord with
my humor. Suppose
that I have taken cold —
feel old & sore besides,
being decidedly dyspeptic.
Snowing heavily all
day — There must have
fallen at least 8 in. of snow
Did not go out during
the day. The whole house
very much interested in a
Southern gentleman (Virginia)
who shoveled our walk for his
breakfast & 35 cents. As it
continued to snow he returned
in the afternoon & again cleared
the walk, refusing at first 25
cents that was offered him, but
afterwards taking it. Good looking
young, & apparently well educated —
Something very wrong somewhere —
nevertheless, interesting.

## Right Page

### Monday, March 18, 1867

Marvellous day for clearness —
Cold and wintry. After lighting
fire at Studio, went to Schaus's.
Pete at 10 & until 11.30 at work
packing Uncle Willie's little picture
to Express to him at Ottawa. Set
palette at last & started work
(9th day) upon "Hudson River View."
Home to lunch at 1. P.M.
painted over foliage on left
hand side of picture. Express
man came, & got top 9 pictures.
Uncle Mr. Turrell called about
4 o'clk —
Eveng. went with Ge. to call
upon the Kimball's in 22d St.
Found only Mrs. Woodsk. at home.
Visited with them a few minutes
then Ge. went to Mr. Martin's in
21st St. & I to Butler's in 23d St.
Mary not at home — Saw Amelia
and Maggie Gunn. Left at
10.25. Home & to bed at 11.30
