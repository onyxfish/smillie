## Left Page

### Friday, January 4, 1867

Another charming day —
Up. bet. 8 & 9 o'clk At about
10.30 a.m. Dr. J. called & again
I Rode out with him for maybe
half an hour Collecting bills —
Pd.?? and then walked down
to the Studio — Saw several of
the "fellows" & was pleasantly
greeted. Called on Jas. Hart and
Breevort — walked home about
one o'clk. — After dinner
spent some time trying to put
one of my closets in order —
tired myself out — at dusk
took a nap — fresh at tea-
time. — Eveg. Father & Mother-
in at Willis. Mary at Dürings
Geo. at Prayer Meeting — Matt
& Charl Skating — Kate & I in
my Room Reading — To bed
at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Saturday, January 5, 1867

Dubious m'g. in the weather
way, but by noon, Charming.
At 11.30 went down to Uncle Geo's
by appointment to get the "set"
of my teeth — There until 1.20 — Rode
home & got my lunch at near
2 o'clk — Sat awhile & then at
2.45 Started for Philharmonic
Rehearsal. Heard Volkmann's
Symphony in D. Minor — 1st time.
Like 2d & parts of 3d movements —
& Mendelssohn's "Fingal's Cave." Then
thought it about time to start for
home — 4.30 o'clk P.M. Stopped
at Studio — Saw Mr. Walter Brown, Mrs.
Greatney — Colman, Rogers, Fairman
& several others & was pleased to
Receive their Congratulations. My
frame for my portrait came in to-day
— a very showy affair — Augero is quite
pleased — Rosalie Goodman & Mr.
Mrs. Cherry called at Studio to-day, I
did not see them — Geo. did —
Eveg. killed outright, stretched
upon Sofa — To bed at 10 o'clk.
