## Left Page

### Sunday, September 1, 1867.

Day overcast & threatening
Rain which did not come — for
a wonder — Evening it rather
broke away — all day fight-
ing with a headache & lying
about the house until about
4 o'clk P.M. when I started
out over the meadows & walked
along the river a mile or two
was much charmed with
the walk — many studies and
effects — back to tea about 7.
Spent the evening on the
piazza — to bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Monday, September 2, 1867.

Up a little after 6 o'clk — clear, cold
morning — Stretched a 12×18 canvas,
washed brushes, took a walk & had
bkfst. before 8 o'clk — after bkfst.
worked until near 11 o'clk upon pen
sketch of Moat + Ledges from beyond
the Intervale House — then, the light
being right I took my large study of
moat & the Meadows, & taking position
near the barn painted over mountain
& ledges until dinner time, 2 o'clk —
after dinner again painted on it
until 4 o'clk — then took 12×18 in
canvas, went to the River & got
sketch of a study that I intend to
make in the P.M., looking up the
River, with large ledge reflected —
home after sunset half an hour or so,
quite tired with my days work —
Every quite jolly — 4 of the girls have
spent the day at Carter's Notch & Mrs
[illegible] & Miss Greene go away to-morr-
ow. Strangers of Sat. left this mng.,
Mr [illegible] & wife & 4 gentlemen & a lady
arrived this evening. To bed at 10.30.
