## Left Page

### Monday, June 3, 1867

Up before 6. A.M. after a night of considerable Rolling — nice a wind & sea. Very heavy Rain — very much belated — before about 7 — misty, but luminous &
very breezy — delicious — enjoyed the pure air hugely — did not get to the dock until 10 o'clk — 4 hours behind time. Went at once to N.B.N. Co to settle business & get further instructions — Up home at noon — bath & general change — then lunch — feeling very good — After lunch — took a long snooze that quite used up the afternoon —
Heavy shower at 5 with light Rain until 7. Eve'g, for an hour or so, in at Will's — then wrote a note to Mr. Beach & to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, June 4, 1867

a clear & confoundedly hot day. Did not get to Studio until 9.30 Soon got to work upon a 6×10 for Uncle Willie — "A glimpse of the Cats Kills, from near Po'keepsie" — Think that I have a pure, true color'd sky, but "snuffy" foreground. I have to remedy it in a 2d painting. Miss Woodworth spent a good part of the afternoon at Studio. Fred Durand called & took away his little picture. Burling called in the m'g, & Colman in the P.M. To night is monthly meeting of Water Color Society — but I went to second Concert of Harrison's Grand Musical Festival. & heard with great delight Mendelssohn's "Hymn of Praise." —
The orchestra was almost up to Philharmonic & the Chorus, beyond anything I have heard before — The 2d movement, Allegro agitato, charmed one as much as ever — The Duet & chorus, "I waited for the Lord" was deli[ghtful] — was encored & the Choral was grand. The crowd was immense & the heat suffocating. Met Irelands, Kimballs & Cooks — walked home with Emma Ireland.
