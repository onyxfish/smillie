## Left Page

### Tuesday November 26, 1867

Close & muggy during the m[orning] about noon it cleared off very
warm — Later in the afternoon, all
overcast again & very dark. Eve[ning]
clear & breezy. Got a new drawin[g]
board, 18×30 in. & stretched a n[ew]
half sheet of Creswick Paper on it,
upon which to begin a new drawing.
Worked, slap-dash, over the one
that I consider spoiled, trying
to learn something to aid me in
making my next drawing. Used
body color all over the sky, & by
the end of the day, began to think
that I was learning something.
Miss Porter & Tooney came in at
3 o'clk & stayed until nearly 4.
Even[ing] I went to call on the
Butlers, in 23d St. Saw Amelia.
Man[,] their mother were out to church
but came in at 8.30. After that spent
a very pleasant two hours, chatting
over old times & various subjects.
Home & to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, November 27, 1867

Delightful day — clear and mild.
Set to work on new drawing —
13×21 in. — the same subject as last
attempt — got it all on to the
paper, outlined with pen & first
wash of warm orange tint done
before 10'clk — Miss Minnie Van-
doren called at 12.45 — her first
call since her return from a 7 mos.
trip in Europe. At 1.30 home to
lunch — found Aunt Jessie there.
P.M. — Repeated washes of Cobalt
over my sky — got them very flat
& am encouraged — Young Runce,
pupil of Mr. Hart called after
4 o'clk. About 5 I went to Snedicors
& ordered a frame for my drawing.
Home at 6.30. Even[ing] went in
to Wills for a little while. Home
again at 9 o'clk — Some writing
to bed at 10.30
