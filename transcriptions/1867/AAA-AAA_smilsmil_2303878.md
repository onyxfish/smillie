## Left Page

### Tuesday, January 22, 1867

A sort of a mixed up day. Clouds + sunshine + chill and
thaw. About 10.30 went down
town to A.B.N. Co. with drawing.
Saw Mr. Goodall, many little
points of criticism, all of
detail — only vexatious.
Stopped at Brooks Bro's on my
way up + ordered a suit of
clothes — cutter not in — will have
to call to-morrow. — did not
get home until 2 o'clk. After
dinner Rested — then pencilled
on an autumn study of trees
that I got partly finished in
Montrose last Oct. — Every
Man out with Dr. T, until 9 o'clk. —
Rest of eve'g he spent with us. —
Tried unsuccessfully to stretch
paper to make another drawing
for A.B.N.Co. — Gave it up and
got to bed near midnight.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, January 23, 1867

Delightful day — My Uncle A.
called + took mother up to his
house to stay over night. At 11
A.M. I went down to Brooks Bro's
was measured — on my way up
stopped in to see French pictures
at old Derby Rooms. This is the 2d.
Annual Exhn under auspices
of a French Etching Club — and
very inferior I think, to the first.
Stopped at Uncle George's to have
something done with my false
teeth — he knocked out the front
ones and is going to replace
them. — Home to dinner at 3 —
very hungry + very tired. Rested
after dinner, then charcoa[l]ed
a little on my new drawing — a
Gaucho on horseback. Eve'g quite
a lively time — Matt had a
call from a Mr. Williams — and
Hy Foster called, also Dick
Dorman — he was very jolly
+ weighs 178 lbs.
