## Left Page

### Saturday, July 27, 1867

*At J. C. Blair's — Plymouth, N.H.*

Up at 5.30 — baggage ready — breakfast — bill [illegible]
$3.00 for Room — with our meals, this is just $4.25 [illegible]
day each. — Hack to Maine & Boston R.R. Station & at 7.30
started for Plymouth — day very pleasant — Country
uninteresting until past Concord N.H. — then glimpses
of mts & at last lakes. Passing along the shores of Winni-
pesquam — Long bay — Winnipesaukee & Mtkawa[rra] —
with charming views of Mountain & water for more
than an hour. — Plymouth at 12.50 — quite enough
of R.R. travel for one day & very hungry — the Penn-
sewasset House beside the track — Rushed in with
Oscar Ireland (he found us in the cars 2 or 3 hours before)
& got dinner — then took places on top of a
Franconia Stage & at about 2.30 reached Blair's
about 3 miles from Plymouth. — The Kimballs,
Irelands, Cooks & Nelsons all out waiting. O I
was expected — & a boisterous welcome given — [illegible]
was quite jolley. — A little wash up — then about
the house — down to the Pemigewasset River — poled
a boat — Later, croquet and at 7.15 — tea. —
Evng, I feel very tired, almost used up.
Singing & piano music by the band &
to bed at 10 o'clk — Mrs. Spring insisting
upon our taking her Room.

## Right Page

### Sunday, July 28, 1867

Up at 7 o'clk — day overcast & mild. A
dozen of the party went to Church, a mile
or two from the house. Still feeling tired
I remained at home — getting a nap,
a letter home partly written. After
dinner, a great deal of "lying around" —
very little disposition shown on the part
of any one to move. — very warm, with
sudden spatterings of Rain — more like
an April day than July. — A number
of us, with waterproofs spread under us
spent a good part of the afternoon on
the River (Pemigewasset) bank — with
at times a little paddling in the boat.
The evening until after 10 P.M. spent on
the piazza singing Sacred music —
We had determined to leave this early
tomorrow mrng, but owing to the good-
natured & flattering determination of our
friends, we have agreed to Remain
until Tuesday mrng.
