## Left Page

### Sunday, October 27, 1867.

Up at 7 — Beautiful day — In the mng went with Charl and heard Dr. Hastings — John 3. 18.
a most excellent discourse —
At 12.30 Charl. Geo & I. by 8th Ave.
R.R. went up to Uncle Aaron's —
getting there a few minutes before
2. Dinner at once. Miss Porter,
the new Governess introduced.
Spent the afternoon wandering
over the place, much delighted
with the Fall effects — a little
after 5 o'clk P.M. we started for
home, getting in, in time for tea.
After tea, went to Thompson's
Bro may Tabernacle Ch. & heard the Rev.
Newman Hall of London — "Lord
increase our Faith!" Church crowded
& had much difficulty in hearing him —
his voice is not strong, nor does he speak
very plainly — His address was
forcible, but nothing wonderously profound.
Such, as of course, would be desirable food
for my gigantic intellect — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Monday, October 28, 1867.

Weather thick & rather dubious — Up
before 7 — At Studio a few minutes after
9 — Jas Fairman came in & Read
to us his Recent Correspondence
with Wm Hart — also portions of the
original M.S. prepared by W.H. as
a lecture on art — The quarrel bet-
ween the two, bids fair to prove a very
disgraceful one to the profession —
All day at work getting studies
fixed up & things Ready to begin
my winters work in Real earnest —
Evelf P.M. 3 o'clk — Wm Hart made us
a short Studio visit — From 5 to past
6 — Wenzler visited, ventilating his
peculiar theories concerning Color
He is sure it will be Reduced to a
Science, as much as thorough-bass.
Evelf, Father Read to me his in-
teresting correspondence with Mr
Robt. Hinshelwood — Insolent and
ignorant on one side — plucky
& comparatively quiet on the
other — To Bed at 10.30.
