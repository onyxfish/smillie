## Left Page

### Wednesday, December 4, 1867.

Clear cold day — under the weather with a headache and very unlike work. Part of morning used up distributing the last of Reception cards among the Studio. Home to lunch at 1 — on way back, stopped at Burling's to straighten up some Water Color Treasury Matters — on Return to Studio found Sanford Gifford — Spent rest of p.m. getting Boston outline on paper for A.B.N. Co's drawing. Every Spent getting my Water Color books into shape. To bed at about 10 o'clk. Louis Menger called in the [illegible] & gave me a Commission to make him a Water Color drawing about 14×24 — $200.00 — View from West Point, up the Hudson — To be Chromo-lithographed by Day & Son on Hanhart, London — Quite a feather in my Cap.

## Right Page

### Thursday, December 5, 1867.

Early mf, dull with a promise of snow, but it cleared to be another delightful winter day — Got to Studio rather late — 9.30. Set to work up on Boston drawing & had it outlined before I went home to lunch — Burling called during mf. On my way back to Studio stopped at 6th National Bank. Cor. 6th Ave. & 35th St. & deposited $196.04 as Treas. of Water Color Society — So opening an acct. Afternoon — washing in effect in drawing but could not get it finished as I had hoped to — At 5.30 went down to Dolphs & to Snedicors & did not get home to tea until 7 o'clk — Mr. Arnoldi of Ottawa took Tea with us & spent the eveng. He is a pleasant young fellow — about 36 & a native of Canada — To bed at 11.
