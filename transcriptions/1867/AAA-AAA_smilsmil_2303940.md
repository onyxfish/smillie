## Left Page

### Tuesday May 28, 1867.

A very warm day — Ther. in
Studio reached 79°. In little
humor for work. Worked 6th day
on "Coming Shower" 14×20. painted
over foliage and foreground — I
have now come to the end of
my Rope & altho I see much
that I would like to have done
I don't know how to do it — I
thing that I will call it done.
Stopped work at 5 — went home
got tea & at 7.15 Geo & I started
for Brooklyn to call on the
Beaches — After much trouble
in hunting for the house we found
it at 66 Columbia St at 20 m
to 9 o'clk — Had a very pleasant
call with Mr & Mrs. B & Miss Sumner
B — There until nearly 10.30. Home
by 11.45 — To bed a little after
midnight — Heavy thunder storm
between 3 & 4 o'clk A. M.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, May 29, 1867.

Very warm & enervating day — Ther.
in Studio got up to 84°. Painted
in "Catskill Mts & Hudson River from
back of Hyde Park" — 6×10 in. Hard
work to stand up to my paste.
but by persistency got through by
5 o'clk P.M. Of course I have
tried for vastly more than I have
been able to get. The Result
instead of color & light — is metallic,
dull & rather weak — Evening
Geo and I went to Kimball's
where there was a little musical
entertainment — the last meeting
of their Singing club — Quite a
number present — at least 50 — and
a very pleasant collection of glee,
etc etc were sung — Cream etc
served at near 11 — Company broke
up at 11.30, walked home in a
Raw mizzle — what weather —
To bed a little after midnight
