## Left Page

### Sunday, September 29, 1867

Up late — not until near 8 o'clk —
Another cloudless day. but warmer
& more smokey than any preceding —
The heat is Really uncomfortable —
Hung about until church time —
then went to Congregational ch
& heard the Regular pastor
Rev. W. Kimball preach "Blessed are
the poor in Spirit, &c" — After
service, Ret'd. to the house & sat
on upper piazza, enjoying very
much the day until 1 o'clk —
afternoon wrote to Father and
about 4 o'clk went walking with
Geo over the meadows — It
was quiet, delightful & very warm —
Ther. 74°. [illegible] wrote a short
note to Mr. L. A. Hall enclosing
a letter that had come here for
her — Some gossiping & then
to bed at 10 o'clk — Sky overcast &every promise of a
change in weather.

## Right Page

### Monday, September 30, 1867

Up about 7 o'clk — Rain falling and
a miserable cold wind blowing
from the north — a little flurry of
snow & at 11 o'clk the clouds lifted —
a glorious spectacle was given,
of the White Mts., literally white
& snow capped — Wind strong and
cold from N.W. As I had worked all
the m'g on one of my Saco pen
sketches at 12 o'clk I walked up to
the pine woods beyond Whittaker's
followed wood Roads to my great
delight, getting home to dinner after
2 o'clk — again worked on Sketch
until I had finished it, then at
5 o'clk again went out, with Geo.
to the pine woods, enjoying
the Roaring wind & the glorious
foliage — In at 6.30 — Evening again
worked some with the pen — Got off
a Chockarooster Caricature to
Miss Curtis — The Pittams,
Mother & Son left this m'g —
To bed at 10. wind blowing a
hurricane & like to tip the old
house over.
