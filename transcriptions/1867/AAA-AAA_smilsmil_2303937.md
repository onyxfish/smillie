## Left Page

### Wednesday, May 22, 1867.

Clerk of the weather not hired yet —
another miserable, east wind and
rainy day — Uncle W. Haunt agnes
started for Montreal early this m[ornin]g.
At Studio until after 10 am, arrang-
ing & putting things to rights — then
got started again on "Coming Show[er]"
14×18. (5th day.) I worked over foliage
but not as fully in the spirit as
I could desire — Home to lunch —
P.M. Conant — Art Critic of Janes'
gave us quite a long call, so
that I did nothing — at 4 P.M.
went to Brooks Bros & settled bill —
called a Putnam's, Schauss's and
Snedecor's — also called in & saw
Rothernel's "Court of Lincoln" —
Every tired — Father & Mother
in at Wills to dinner — I Read
& wrote a letter to Fan Mulford —
getting to bed toward midnight —
[illegible — ~4 lines]

## Right Page

### Thursday, May 23, 1867.

Up at 6 A.M. M[ornin]g cloudless — Bkfast
at 7 — Gel & I off in 8 o'clk train for
Yonkers. There met young Bell & his
two younger brothers — went to his sail boat
at once crossed to the Palisades — there
a few minutes after 10 — Saw a pretty
cascade — Climbed to top — Amused our-
selves tumbling down big Rocks — glorious
views — Return equally — Rather exciting —
got quite wet — Sky overcast & blowing
quite cold — Went up to Bell's house —
met Father, Mother, Aunt & Sister — dinner
about 1 — Enjoyed it & like the family —
Wnt with Mr. B. to his garden — about
3 P.M. Young B. Gel & I drove some miles
up the Nepperhan Valley, then across
to Sunny Side then to Irvington, called
on Sam[ue]l Colman — not in — then to Bell's —
did not get in until after 7 o'clk, very
cold & headache — Warm fire & tea
soon set me to rights — at tea table until
9 o'clk — all things very pleasant — B's
elder br[other] introduced — Bid all good
night & took 9.52 train home, pumped
off at 42nd St at 10.30, to bed before 11.
