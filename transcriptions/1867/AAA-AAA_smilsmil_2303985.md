## Left Page

### Wednesday, August 28, 1867

Cloudy, windy m'f, threatning Rain.
I could not work out of doors, so I
worked all the on't faithfully on
water color study of willows, working
up parts defective and of which I
remembered exactly the color, or
the drawing. — Afternoon entirely
overcast — I took pencils & paper
& went up the Road a mile, or
more & made a camera-lucida
outline, overlooking the Saco, meadows
beyond, the ledges & Moat Mt. I
was much annoyed by Rain
which kept falling in little showers
every few minutes — got my
sketch finished a few minutes
after 6 o'clk — then home —
Steady Rain in the even'g & most
of the night — Miss Grant's new
Ieutertained us most of the eveng
with music on the piano —
Phs. of photo's of "Three Elms" &c.
in the even't much comment of
course — one is pretty good — To bed
at 10.30

## Right Page

### Thursday, August 29, 1867

Disagreeable day, with showers in
the morning so that it was out of the
question to go out to work — worked
in doors penning on sketch that I
took a Jackson of Moat Mt. ten days
ago — worked all of the m'g & part
of the afternoon upon it — At about
4 o'clk, as the clouds had lifted
from Mt. Washington Joe and I went
up the Road about 1½ or 2 miles &
got a sketch of Mt. W— and the
upper Intervale — home late, as
I did not finish my sketch until
after 6 o'clk — Singular Sunset — wild
like with unusual & almost ghastly color.
Strange blues — gloomy purples & lurid
reds, to say nothing of dangerous looking
yellows — Met Miss Waring on the Road —
she spoke to us — We met her last summer
at Lenox with the Goodmans. Every
steady, heavy Rain most of the time —
I was rather forlorn — up at Stansarge
house — then back to "Three Elms" —
Summer gone & no work done —
To bed at 10.30 —
