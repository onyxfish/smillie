## Left Page

### Sunday, February 3, 1867.

Still miserable weather —
did not get up until near
11 o'clk A.M — So bringing
my breakfast and dinner
near together — Felt "mean"
all day — still under the
weather from my Friday's
upset — Afternoon wrote
for a little while — Every
went to Frothingham's — the
Unitarian + would-be success-
or of Boston Theodore Parker —
but he did not preach — then
went to Bdway Tabernacle
+ heard an excellent discourse
by Prof. Shedds on self-seeing
or Subestigation — After
services finished writing
letter to Addie Dunning.

## Right Page

### Monday, February 4, 1867.

Miserable Rain all day
long — Geo down town to see
about his jury duty. His Court
adjourned for one week. I
commenced painting up on
14×18 in. canvas — Round top —
"Seeking a Shade" — Sheep under
at tree — painting it for Mr.
Tracy Harris — Got in the Sky,
distance + middle distance.
Nothing very brilliant —

Shary and Fanny Mulford
by arrangement went up to
Uncle Aaron's to spend a week.

Geo Kate + I spent the
evef. in at Will's.
