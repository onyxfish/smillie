## Left Page

### Friday, October 5, 1866.

Up early — Sat in the parlor & wrote until a little before 8 o'clk — then took 35 minutes walk & was in in time for bkf. — Some croquet with Mr. Harris — then took paper & pencils & went to Sayres Ridge just beyond Warner's — after wandering through the woods & enjoying the marvellous morning, took a camera lucida sketch of a Hemlock. Returning to dinner at half past one. After dinner a little more croquet & then again took pencils & paper & with Geo. Fan M. & Maud D. went on to Post's Hill back of Drinkers & got a sketch of a groupe of trees there — then went on through the woods to Maple grove, down to the Wyalusing & by the Road home. Evening spent penciling on afternoons sketch.

## Right Page

### Saturday, October 6, 1866.

Another great day, but not up to yesterday. milder, more milky & like September. Up at sunrise & swaddled in overcoats wrote for an hour or more to Addie Dunning. a short walk & then bkfast — after bkfst. of course croquet — took traps & with Sarah [illegible] went again to Sayres Ridge. Some of the Autumn effects of color too great for endurance. Will I ever be able to get studies of anything of the kind? The severe frost has made short work of color glory this fall — the leaves are coming down very rapidly — worked until 1 o'clk on Hemlock. Afternoon at 3 o'clk Geo. & I took Fan M. & Maud D. out Riding. on South Road — Road to Elk Lake — then across to road that brings us back into town over Butterfields Hill — back at 5 o'clk P.m. & left the Girls at Wm. Mulford's where they are to take tea. S.L.F. out riding with the Hamers. took her to Mr. M's. at 6 o'clk when she is to take tea. — Continued letter to Addie until 9 o'clk when Salomy W. & Mrs. Streeter called — Geo went for Sarah & brought her home to see them. at 9.30 escorted them down the hill — at 10.30. to bed.
