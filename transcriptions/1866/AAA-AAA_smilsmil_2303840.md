## Left Page

### Sunday, November 18, 1866

A day, between — between for weather —
part of the time overcast, again almost
clear. Gel. & Charl. spent p.m. Evef.
& night with Mother at Uncle Aaron's.
Mf. I went to J. P. Thompson's Ch. and
heard a very able controversial sermon
from Prof. Hitchcock on 1st verse 1st Chap. of
John. After service, walked down
to Studio — Dinner at one. Again
to Studio & fixed a little upon "Octo[ber]" —
that is to go to Brooklyn Reception the
1st thing to morrow mt. Home at 4
o'clk — Kindled a fire in dufrygnac
— Evef. went with Father & the
girls to Madison Ave. Baptist Ch.
& heard Prof. Robinson — text
"My kingdom is not of this world" —
Very able I thought and I
enjoyed hearing him very much.
After Ch., we all went to the
Studio to take a look at the
pictures to go to the Reception
to morrow — Home at about
10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Monday, November 19, 1866

Threatening Rain, but did it make
out — Pottered awhile in the mt.
at mixing tints for back parlor ceiling
but gave it up without accomplishing
any thing — Then went around to the
Hebrew Club House in 42d St., just
built to see Augers, who is painting on
the ceiling of the Ball Room — climbed
to the scaffolding where he was at work
& was much interested watching him —
went all over the building from garret to
cellar — got to Studio at 11 o'clk —
then attempted in oil colors to get tints
for ceiling — wasted time until 3 o'clk.
The Rest of a short p.m. used up, painting
upon "Eagles Nest" 9×12 & watching the
Police turn out at Inspector Carpenter's
funeral. — Kipp & Will Chandler at
the Studio. Evef. spent between
Fleming's Book Store & Düring's —
D. played some Beethoven music
for me — especially some Dead
marches. Uncle A. called in
early evef. & many went home
with him to stay all night.
