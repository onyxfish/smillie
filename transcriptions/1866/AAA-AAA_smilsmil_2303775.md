## Left Page

### Wednesday, July 11, 1866.

Another superb day. Before 8 o'clk A.M. commenced a pencil
study from a Lamb's head, the manner
of which, having consenting, her force, to
do without it. At about 10 o'clk. went
down to the Elm trees & pushed in a
sort of a sky on the 14×24 in. study. I
feel as awkward as a beginner. Addie
unsuccessful in finding her watch —
Afternoon commenced second Elm
study 12×18. Making worse work than
in the morning. Gave it up about
5 o'clk — went home, & immediately after
supper, Addie rode up on her little mare,
I got a saddle & bridle on the black colt
& started off on a ride with her, the colt
behaving tolerably well, everything con-
sidered. The even'g was charming — at
about 8 o'clk went over to the Dr's to
escort Emma to the Church Sociable
at Miss Lavoisa (?) Tuckers. — Met
there, for the first time, Mr. Tockermann,
Miss Cook & others. After a very
warm even'g, Reached home
(Smith's) at 11.30 o'clk. P.M.

## Right Page

### Thursday, July 12, 1866.

A day "cram-jam" full of good things with-
out flaw or wrinkle — At about 8 o'clk A.M. got &
walked over to D.D's — Arr'd at 20 m. past 8, found
Addie & three writing impatiently — Emma having gone
in (milking) to the P.O. Got under way at once, on Reach-
ing the P.O. were told that Emma & walked on towards
Lebanon — drove on — R'd went home again to the Dr's and
found that E. had again started for town — whipped up
& found her just beyond the "triangular square" at 9.15
got off for Lebanon Springs — A thin haze tempered the
sun & made our drive over delightful, altho' at times
it looked like Rain. Arr'd at Columbia Hall at 11.45
found Mr. & Mrs. Kimball & Misses Carrie & Fanny on one
of the balconies — warm & pleasant greetings all around.
All went up the hill back of the House & sat there
until 1.30 — although we had complete pic-nic
arrangements — Mr. K. would not allow it — so we
all dined at the Hall at 2 o'clk — After dinner music
in the saloon, which though not good, was extremely
enjoyable to me, as we sat upon the broad front
balcony, looking over a beautiful valley exquisite
in dreamy haze of sunlight, for every trace of wind cloud
had disappeared. At 4 again climbed the hill
back of the house, at 5.15 started for home by Pittsfield.
The view from the Mt. immediately east of the Hotel
very charming — stopped horses & climbed a little
eminence by the road side — Reached P— at 6.45
just after sunset with fine view of Saddle Back &
other mts — stopped by Roadside & had an evening
pic-nic — very good. Air soft & balmy — color of
hills & sky, cast & west beautiful — horses free, and
all in good humor, is it a wonder that to-day's leaf
is silver bright with a gold edge. On the way home
had conversation with Emma that gave me a new
sightlines her deeper & more serious nature — Drove
up to the Dr's at nine o'clk — Miss A & those waiting
us — parlor & at 10 o'clk got & I started for Smith's
