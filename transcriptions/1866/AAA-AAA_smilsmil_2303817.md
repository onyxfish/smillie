## Left Page

### Wednesday, October 3, 1866.

Overcast, with heavy wet fog, almost a
drizzle — a little after 7 o'clk walked out
to the lake — returning by Lower Road to
Methodist Ch. then by Foundry Road & Jos. Drink's
home. During the morning worked
upon pencil study of Hemlock on Five
Hill — at noon Sarah, Gel & I walked
for an hour — Afternoon again
worked upon & finished Hemlock
study. At 3 o'clk took Tarbell's
horse and went out upon Salt Spring
Road about 2 miles to Myron Mott's
for baking apples — at a neighbor's, Har-
rison, got half a bushel — put them in
a bag & threw them across the horse in front
of me — awkward load — but cantered
home all right. Continued ride after
delivering apples, until 5.45 — Afternoon
cleared off gloriously — cold & perfectly
clear — Evef., folks went over the
way to call on Mrs Comstock — spent
from 8 until 9 o'clk with Josie Bentley,
then, until nearly 10 o'clk called at
the Comstock's or Sayre's — Home
early and to bed by 10.30.

## Right Page

### Thursday, October 4, 1866.

Heavy frost last night — mf perfectly
clear & cold — enjoyed walk very much.
Late bkf. again — discouraged — cannot
effect a reform — set up easle in the
parlor & charcoaled until noon upon
[illegible] for Mr. Winans. Tried to find
a horse that Sarah might ride with
me this p.m. — without success — after
dinner, Mr. Harris & I played two
games of Croquet — our overcoats
on — At 4 o'clk — took Tarbell's mare
& had a good gallop of an hour &
a half — warmed the mare up,
but my hands very numb from the
cold wind — nevertheless — great —
met Josie Bentley & Gel Bentley, driving —
out riding — they went with me over the
road that I went day before yesterday —
atmospheric effects very different
but yet splendid. Fan M. & Maud
D— arr'd this evef., from Scranton.
Gel & I there from 9 until 10, then
home & chatting in the parlor with
Mr & Mrs H. & Sarah until 11 o'clk.
