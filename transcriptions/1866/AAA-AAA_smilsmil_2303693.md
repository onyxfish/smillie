## Left Page

### Tuesday, January 16, 1866

Such a miserable m[orning] as only the clerk of the weather
of N.Y. can get up. Rain &
sleet, freezing as it falls —
Set to work. First day painting,
5th day since beginning, upon
the sky of my Academy
picture. Had a tall time
of it & at last, getting it within
long gun-shot range, I left
it. A sky, 4 feet long, & from
12 to 15 in broad, is quite a
surface for me to go over.

To-day, my birthday —
I hardly dare count up how
old I am — too anxious am
I to forget, that I could not
till without counting. I
have not accomplished
what I should at this
my age —

Eveny at the Studio
fixing up.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, January 17, 1866

Fine winter day — First thing, this
m[orning] after reaching the Studio,
[illegible] & I set to work and gave it a
regular house cleaning — getting
things charmingly fixed up.
Between 11 & 12 o'clk Mr. W. Martin,
& daughter Lila, Miss Sarah Martin & the
misses Holt, attended by a darkie
woman, with basket, dishes & eatables
made their appearance — At about
one we spread the table, made
tea & sat down to lunch and
a right jolly time I think we had.
Mr. Griffetts & Mr. Verplanck also
called — and too, Miss Meta Gavit.

In the evening went to the first
Reception of the N.A. of Design.
Crowded of course, and no
startlingly good pictures. Met
Miss Dunning & for the first time
her intended Mr. Ehinger —
I like him — Had a very
pleasant chat with Miss
Florence Smith. Sent a note to
Robert Hinshelwood.
