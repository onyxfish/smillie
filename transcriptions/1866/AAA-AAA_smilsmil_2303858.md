## Left Page

### Sunday, December 30, 1866.

Bright, clean morning. Feel very well, everything considered.
Dr. [illegible] called about noon to see Father who is again sick in bed,
— probably the same cold that he took some time ago — showing itself now in fever & trouble in
his throat. I stayed to dinner. I was indulged with some chicken
and squash pie — all but the paste.
After dinner walked in the sun on the back piazza for
half an hour. Enjoyed it exceedingly and was very conscious of its stimulating effects.
Father up in the even. Throops called and took Mary to church.
After church, quite a visit in my room. To bed at 11
and a tolerable night of rest — I trust that I am getting along.

## Right Page

### Monday, December 31, 1866.

Dull dismal day again and snow falling — The last day of
the year. I might sermonize and get doleful — but all of my
energies at present, are directed to trying to be thankful.
Throops called in for a few minutes after dinner, but his
patient had flown. — Father was down-town — permitted to
go after promising to bring up work enough to keep him
quiet at home for a few days.
Amused myself part of the afternoon pen sketching "Perils
of the Season" — a party of Skaters tumbled in a heap. — Between
6 & 7 o'clk p.m. Aunt K. arrd from
Montrose. Evng spent in considerable hubbub visiting
with her — To bed at 10.30
and another night of fair
Rest.
