## Left Page

### Tuesday, January 2, 1866

Only half pleasant to-day.
At the Studio I outlined
in color four of my small
Charcoalings — all bits on
the River — besides working
some changes on my Carp'e
canvas. Addie Dunning
called at the Studio just
in time to take lunch
with us. We talked over
again her engagement to
Mr. Effinger. I am not
yet by any means reconciled
to it — Later, Mr. Jas.
M. Hart (Theresa Gorsuch)
called. She was alone
and we had quite a jolly
time — Every at home.
Mary + Tech? Dorman
spent the every with me
in my Room.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, January 3, 1866

Still disagreeable weather —
overcast & raw, yet not really
cold — At 10 o'clk A.M. saw Mary
off by Train to Poughkeepsie —
At 11 — went to Loop's Room, to
sit for my Academy portrait.
about noon Henri Appy the violin-
ist came in & little painting
was done until lunch time.
A little after two, again sat
for Loop when he finished paint-
ing in the face — In half an hours
more he had the back ground
painted in — The beginning
is to me bold and satisfactory.
Being now 4.30 o'clk, I wrote
a letter to Uncle Mr. Turrill —
then dinner & rest at home
writing regrets to party
invitation from Miss Florence
Smith and thanks to S.C.F.
for her invitation to Geo. &
self to spend a night in
Orange.
