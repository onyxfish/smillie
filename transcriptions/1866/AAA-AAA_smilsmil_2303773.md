## Left Page

### Saturday, July 7, 1866.

Intensely hot day — Mrs. just after breakfast, Geo & I went down to the pond back of the house — shoved out in the flat boat, gathered white lilies, paddled across & found some charming shore bits, lily pads etc. etc. Between 9 & 10 a.m. Ret. to our sketching ground of yesterday & found Addie, Emma & the Dr. Picked out a subject for Addie, not a very satisfactory one, & she began upon it — we worked until 12 & went our different ways to dinner — a little after 1, a sharp thunder storm came up — Geo & I went down & Rescued the Canvases — on Returning, in the midst of it Addie, Emma & the Dr. came up — in a few moments it was clear & we Ret. to work — All the P.M. showers passed about us, & one or two light ones over us, but we worked on until nearly 6 — the heat at times being almost suffocating — After tea a glorious & perfect double Rainbow. Every walked over to the Dr's & got a letter from home. My work accomplished amounted only to partly outlining in spirits my two studies

## Right Page

### Sunday, July 8, 1866.

Still very warm, but tempered by a delightful breeze. Drove to the Presbt'n Ch. with Mr. Mrs Smith in the Am. & heard the Rev. Mr. Smith (no relation) preach a very good discourse from Matt. V. 6. After service accepted invitation to dine with Dr. D. — The walk down the hill, Charming — though not delightfully breezy & thunder muttering & threatening over the hills in the west — Dinner table conversation, "Inde in art, as exhibited in the desire to suppress ones own pictures, because not as successfully painted as desired" — Dr. says that it is pride on my part because I would like to destroy my Academy Picture — Thunder storms around us & over us from 2 until after 6 o'cl. At 6:30 walked over from the Dr's in a gentle Rain, with prospects of more to-morrow. Every wrote a letter to Jas. R. Brevoort.
