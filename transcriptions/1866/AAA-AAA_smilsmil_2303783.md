## Left Page

### Friday, July 27, 1866.

Unpromising morning, heavy fog which did not fairly lift away until near 10 o'clk — Still real but had a good nights rest.
Worked over the foreground of Elms (14×24) 8½ mrf. of course in great tribulation — too green &
yet not green enough. Afternoon painted in background trees on R. hand of Elm study 12×18. (7: day)
Thunderstorms plenty, but back fine to the N.&E. worked until after 6 o'clk. having suffered considerably from oppressive heat. Mrs.
Tappens, a neighbor, wealthy, etc. wishes us to play drawing master to her two daughters & two young lady friends, for a few weeks.
The proposition was made day before yesterday, through Addie —
Am thinking about it.—
Every writing to Matt.

## Right Page

### Saturday, July 28, 1866.

Mrf warm, cloudy & threatening more thunderstorms, but went to work again upon Elms, 14×24. (9 mrf.) & painted in two of the large trees — the mrf was too short & I had to leave a large part of my work but very roughly rushed in. Afternoon, storms so threatened that I brought my traps up to the house.
Afternoon given up to storms, the heaviest passing west & north of us. Spent the time reading & working a little upon my sketch for Emma. About 8 o'clk after the last shower Geo & I went down to the ore I camping white lilies — Emma &
Ned off riding — a new arrival, Mr. Fox of Portland Mr. — friend of Ned's — tauny [illegible] there to spend the night. Party very proud of the success of the Harvard Scientifics, but evidently, tired.
Felt miserably under the weather myself. On our return home found old Mrs. Smith, just Ret'd from a visit somewhere on the Hudson. The first I have seen her.
