## Left Page

### Monday, July 9, 1866.

Chilly, clouded day — Myf
started out with Mr. Smith &
Gel to look about through the
woods — hoping to find Studies.
Smith wished to show us a natural
Rock basin that he thought very strange —
about 9 o'clk got to the pond, took
the boat & paddled about, gathering lilies & trying to sketch until
after 11 o'clk. Afternoon, feeling
unwell, caught cold — started out
alone & walked away up on to
Bald Head & about, looking for
Studies — gave up the idea of any
study from such a high place.
After tea went over to the Dr's &
had half a game of Croquet with
Emma & Gel — beaten. Then
walked up to the P.O. with Addie,
Emma & Gel. Girls in quite a
gale — had heard some gossips
from Rosalie Goodman that pleased
them. Rita, sat awhile by a fire
in the Library & got a little before
ten; started for home.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, July 10, 1866.

Clear fine day — Ther. early in the
mf stood at 52°. Immediately after
bfst. Smith had his unbroken black colt at
the door waiting for me to try him. They all
expressed astonishment when after a little
handling, I got quietly upon his back, almost without opposition, & trotted him up
& down in the yard. Went down to our
Elm Studies, where Addie & Emma Dorn
joined us. Addie commencing a new
study of Elm Trunks with back ground of
distant bank & foliage. Got both of
my studies for myf & afternoon. Ready to
begin upon tomorrow. Girls not out in the
afternoon — By invitation, to play Croquet
with Miss Rosalie Goodman at 5 o'clk —
went over to the Dr's for Addie & Emma &
found that Addie having lost her watch
in the myf, was off in the fields looking
for it — Gel, Emma & I went on to Mr. G's
where we spent from 6 o'clk until 7. Delightfully, looking at his old house, his
