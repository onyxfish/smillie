## Left Page

### Monday, December 10, 1866

Clear cold winter day — I had hoped to get to the Studio this M'f — but biliany derangements interpose. My head & throat are very sore & my liver is behaving itself very badly. For a little time, bundled up & went out under back piazza to walk, run & exercise myself. Feel so much discouraged about my physical condition as to be willing to go into almost any quack folly — probably off of such unfortunate mortals as myself that quack humbugs make their living. Jane Divin was strongly recommending to my almost willing ears, a Clairvoy aut at No. 42 Irving Pl. At 11.20 P.M. went to my own bed with Jel for the first time since last Tuesday even'y. Slept tolerably well — Dr. Throop called in the even'y.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, December 11, 1866

Fine, clear cold wintry day — Awoke again with heavy headache and sore diaphragm. My liver still ill behaved. As with yesterday spent a good part of the day in useless efforts to read — Tried my old favorite subject "Egypt." — but Egypt was no go — I had n't head enough. Walked again under the piazza. Dr. J. called in the p.m. Tried to interest myself with the men working about the house. — Eve'f, enjoyed a bit of steak at tea. Throop called again & took many to Army Pages to meeting.
