## Left Page

### Wednesday, December 26, 1866

Delightful, clear day —
Got up at about 8 o'clk A.M.
with miserable headache
which kept me in a sort of
stupid, half awake & half asleep
condition all the m'g.
Throops called between 1 & 2
o'clk & found me, no doubt, very
much out of humor. I begged
for a change of practice, &
he gave me powders to stir
up the liver. I told him
that if I was no better in a
week from now, I wanted to
call in Dr. Bartlette. Mother
& Mary went to Snedecor's & Schmius
about Cornices — ordered furniture
at Schmitt's. P.M. I felt better
& Ev'g, quite well — Enjoyed my
supper, with grapes sent in by
Issie. & after, had a chat with
Father. The Remainder of the
ev'g spent visiting in front
room. To bed after 10, asleep at
1 o'clk A.M. Sleeping tolerably well
through the rest of the night.

## Right Page

### Thursday, December 27, 1866

Miserable day again, only
fit weather for people to be sick.
Quite a snow storm during the
day & a tornado of wind at night.
Up between 8 & 9 o'clk A.M. feeling
quite well, but soon my headache
came on & again I was a victim
for all day. Dr. J. called between
12 & 1. Advises me to take the
run of the house & to eat as freely
as I dare, so on the strength of
that had 3 oysters for my dinner.
Afternoon given up entirely to
headache, which seems to be altogether
nervous. Tied my head up & slept
through it. Ev'g much better —
Joe & Will called in for a few minutes.
Geo. went to Long Marten's in 21st St.
Went to bed at 1 o'clk A.M. Up at
2.30 to attend to a little something —
Up again at 5 o'clk. Scared & shivering
— chill followed chill until in about
half an hour I got up, dressed myself —
Rolled myself in blankets, put pillows
in my big chair & fixing myself, slept
there until 7.30 to-morrow morning.
