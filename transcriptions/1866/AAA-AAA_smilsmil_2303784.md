## Left Page

### Sunday, July 29, 1866.

Very pleasant — cool morning. Drove in to the Cong. Ch. and heard Mr. Neale, a former Pastor. Text. Numbers 17: 7" — discourse an eulogy upon Moses, and a lesson of meekness, faith & devotion under discouragements. After service called & saw Carrie Kimball & Father — bid them good bye as they intend starting for Springfield to-morrow mf. At 1 o'clk. rgut with a violent head ache to Mrs. Sears — very kindly she made me a cup of tea & toast, & while they went to p.m. service, I stretched upon the sofa — much better at 3 o'clk. when Mr. S. drove up for me. took no dinner but stretched myself again until 5 o'clk. when I got up quite Relieved. We [illegible] walked with Gil around the Road by Higginson's & then to the D[illegible]. where I passed a pleasant evg. By inadvertance or grace on the part of several young men, we enjoyed the company of the y[oung] ladies alone for nearly an hour.

## Right Page

### Monday, July 30, 1866.

Close, muggy mf. — heavy, sluggish, wet & thunderish looking clouds hanging sullen & threatening. Painted all the mf on 2. hand elm & large groupse — 14 × 24 (10° mf.) with my usual degree of insuccess. Mr. Jn°. Cheney & young Richard Goodman called to see me where I was at work. Between 2 & 3 o'clk. a heavy storm came on — been threatening for two hours — rained steadily until 4.30 — & even then seeming loth to stop. Kept away until after 5 in the evg. Such weather is horrible to me, I feel like a sponge, a jelly-fish — a tad-pole — it is as though we had gone back to the rough house & mud stage of creation. — I am sick. Before 5. went over to the D's — & spent an hour there — took to Emma the little Philopsena sketch that I have painted for her — believe she liked it. Returning, took tea with Geo. & the old Lady, the Smiths in town attending a lecture, walked the Road until 8.30 — then killed the rest of the evg. most miserably. Oscar I— ned D. & Mr. Fox left the D's to-day — the K's did not go, as Carrie was not quite well, they are still in town at Mr. Arnold's.
