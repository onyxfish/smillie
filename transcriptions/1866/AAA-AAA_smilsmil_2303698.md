## Left Page

### Friday, January 26, 1866.

Fine day. Thawing freely in the sunlight. Threatened by a headache in the m'g, so, went to Knoedler's + the gallery in 10th St. Studio Bl'd to see pictures. Set palette about noon and set to work upon Academy picture, painting over sky + distant mountains. (6th day) Feel quite encouraged — altho' I have not yet got within gunshot of what I started for. Father took his tea again at the Studio + for the first time, slept there. Eve'g I went to Mr. Despard's no. 70 W 31st St. to take part in Reading Talfourd's "Ion". Present, Mr. & Mrs. D— Mr. Greatorny. Miss Parker + Mr. Douglas. Left at 20 m. to 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Saturday, January 27, 1866.

Glorious day — Hard at work all day (7th day) painting in the far bank of the River, on my Academy picture. Feel much encouraged — am much disposed to finish too carefully at the first painting — I am warned against it as a mistake by the older painters. At about 4 o'clk p.m. took large brush + set to work massing and painting broader, what I had already done — also puddling greys, (cool) into the shadows already too warm.

Katy + took her 8th lesson. Eve'g went to Phil. Concert with Geo. + Mr. & Mrs. J.R. Brevoort. Called in at the Studio after the Concert, to see how Father was getting along and stayed until near midnight.
