## Left Page

### Saturday, March 31, 1866.

Overcast, Raw & chilly with sprinklings of Rain until about 4.38 when a Regular storm began. Did nothing during the day but loafy. Mrs. Choate took her 6th lesson, Katy G. her 15th lesson — At 1 o'clk — Misses Dunning, Ireland and Kimball came by appointment to take lunch with us — Had a very pleasant informal time — after lunch, Read my journal kept during our visit to Senoe last July. They left at 3 o'clk — Shortly after Mrs. Wm Martin, & daughter called & in 15 or 20 minutes after Russell came & took away our Academy pictures. Then came the heavy Rain — we waited until 5 o'clk — got went out, got a carriage, when we all adjourned to Mr. M's — took dinner & stayed until nearly 10 o'clk — The house in 41 W. L. simply to-night.

## Right Page

### Sunday, April 1, 1866.

Characteristic Spring day — fresh, rather windy — Cloud & sunshine — new bonnets & finery — the day may be voted a success and the opening auspicious. Went to Dr. J. P. T's in the morning & heard an Easter Sermon — afternoon scally-napped about, in Brevoort's Room, taking a nap at the Studio & walking the streets — Self again went to Dr. S's and heard a Sermon to young men. Walked home with Mr. Gavit then stopped in at Miles to see Father & Mother for a little while.
