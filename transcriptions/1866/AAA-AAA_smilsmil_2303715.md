## Left Page

### Thursday, March 1, 1866

overcast, muggy & threatening.
Under the weather again.
Set my palette, but did
nothing all day — Languid,
head achy — used up.
Miss Dunning called in the
evng to ask the escort of
Geo & self, for herself and
friend this evng to the
2d Reception of the N.A.D.
Geo & I took dinner with
her & her father, then walked
to Mr. Vaug's — 16 W. 24th St. and
met miss Rebecca Shepard — then
at about 8.30 o'clk — went over
to the Academy — Fought the
jam & press of "Beauty & fashion"
until after 10 o'clk, trying to make
ourselves believe that we were
seeing pictures & hearing music —
— then, home with the young
ladies — and home with our-
selves.

## Right Page

### Friday, March 2, 1866

Kept my bed until 10 o'clk this
mrng, just for fun — Got bkfast.
at the studio — Worked (1/4 day)
upon Academy picture, painting
middle distance trees, left hand.
I am working now without a
particle of enthusiasm — I am
Sorry to Say — If I cannot
break up this state of things, my
picture must be an utter failure.
Evng, went to Dr. Dunning's & heard
Mrs. Julia Ward Howe Read a paper
upon the Differences & Relations of
Philosophy & Religion, before some
30 or more Ladies & gentlemen.
It was to me, very abstract & profound,
& from the single hearing, I must
confess that I gathered little.
After the Reading, a few minutes
after 9 o'clk — I escorted Miss D —
to Mr. Vaug's in 24th St —
Heavy Raw mist, but no Rain.
