## Left Page

### Tuesday, July 31, 1866

Fine breezy morning — at 8.30 o'clk. went to the Elms & found Addie already established at her mf's work. I went on with my 14×24 (w. mf's) making a little headway upon right hand large elm. At 12 M. Addie, Emma & I went to Mr. Tappan's, concerning the young ladies & drawing lessons. — Mr. T. was out. After dinner took 12×18 in. study & went to work (8th day) but found that my color "crawled" so, owing to the hard glazed surface of the sky, & the sky was painted all in back of the tree, that at 4 o'clk I gave it up & went & had a Royal tumble in the lake. Afternoon, glorious. Ev'g, went over to the D's, found all pleasantly seated in the Library & D. about to Read aloud "Ecce Homo". Our arrival disturbed him & after a little he went off to bed without bidding us "good night" — & out of humor I fear, altho' we begged him to read. I stuck some sketches in Addie's large Album, made a slight figure sketch, then Retired — all for lorn.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, August 1, 1866

Pleasant mf's, down under the Elms before 8 o'clk — all things ready when at 8.15 Addie came along. Geo. & I got for an hour & a half & talked of things interesting perhaps to nobody but ourselves — Grievances & such things. At 11. Emma & Miss Apthorpe came. Miss A. & Emma took up warmly with me some of the topics I had been discussing with Addie. Faith, Hope & Charity — the sum & substance of this life in our relations with one and other. Afternoon as it was cloudy & windy took canvas & outlined (12×18.) Chestnut tree in oatfield. At 4 o'clk. on my way to another study, met Miss Pralie Goodman on horseback looking for me, she told me that her mother & others were at the house. Returned met Mr. & Mrs. Cheney, Miss Cheney, Mr. G & two children. Showed them Geo.'s studies, some of mine & pencil drawings over which they grew flatteringly enthusiastic. A very pleasant call of over an hour — In the eve. by appointment Geo. & I went for Addie and Emma & went to Mr. Packertham's where we spent a very pleasant evening, with Mr. & Mrs. P. & two Misses Loup — good music on a very fine Piano — Ev'd drove O. wind & from the D's home, quite heavy rain. Afternoon, I called with Addie upon Mr. Tappan, saw her — Mrs. Mrs. Hosker. Consented to give lessons in drawing at her house. Commence to morrow.
