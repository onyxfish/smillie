## Left Page

### Sunday, August 12, 1866.

Superb morning. Rode in to Ch.
heard Mr. Smith preach. Prov.
4th Ch. 18v. "The path of the Just is a
a shining light." etc. etc. — An
excellent discourse. After service
walked home with Fred. Durand.
Geo. Hawes & Addie Dunning. Sat
awhile upon the D's Piazza, then
walked on to the Smith's. Got a lunch
& wrote a letter to Father — dinner
after 4 — then wrote awhile to
Mary — afterwards — a walk
with Geo. along the lake shore.
The weather decidedly changed
& threatening. To bed a
little after 9. P.M.

## Right Page

### Monday, August 13, 1866.

Soft warm Rain until noon —
clouded & showery afternoon —
worked faithfully until nearly
5 o'clk upon 10¾×18 in. Sky Study —
am satisfied that I have learned
something, but what I have learned
I am afraid will not be found on
my canvas. — Feeling stirred up
I started out for the colt — caught
him after he had jumped two
fences. got saddle & bridle on him
and started out — behaved tolerably
well until we met a woman in a
long black cloak — he jumped,
reared & burst his saddle girth — as
he continued restive the saddle slipped
from under one and I was obliged
to spring to the ground, all of which
was accomplished very prettily, —
so I flatter myself. Walked
home with the saddle under my
arm. Geo. walked with Geo.
until after 8 o'clk — then home,
walked the piazza until after 9.
Then in & to bed by 10.
