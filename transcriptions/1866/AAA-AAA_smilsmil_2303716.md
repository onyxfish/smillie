## Left Page

### Saturday, March 3, 1866

Fog & rain in the morning. Worked (20th day) on Acad. pic. painting middle trees, middle
distance — got over but little as Mr. Choate took her 2d lesson — commencing with color & Katy G. took her 12th lesson. I Eve'g I took my little Hyde Park picture 6½×9 in to Ochans & offered it to him for $30.00 to be Cr. on my bills. Stolberg wishes to see it by day light & Ge. and I took tea at Miss Dunning's miss Emma Ireland being there to form the "Lenox Quartette" — We had a very pleasant eve'g telling stories & recalling good old times — Waited upon Miss I. to her home, then went home ourselves without waiting.

## Right Page

### Sunday, March 4, 1866

Mild & pleasant w'f. March is doing the lamb. Got to Dr. P. P.'s late, but heard part of an excellent discourse, combatting especially some of the modern Philosophico-Religious ideas. Took a cup of tea with Mr. Mrs. Brevoort after dinner, then went around to the Studio & gave it the regular sweeping that it has been crying for for the past month — then home & got a good bath & change — tea — a few minutes chat with Father & Ge. & then went alone to Cooper Institute to hear A. P. Putnam of Brooklyn on The Future State. He preached a pleasant doctrine of ultimate Salvation to the last man — no one to be lost or damaged.
