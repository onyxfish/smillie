## Left Page

### Monday, October 29, 1866.

Steady pouring Rain commenced at about 10 o'clk. A.M. & kept it up without intermission all day.
J. S. [illegible] — [illegible] [illegible]
and [illegible] [illegible] at the [illegible]
[illegible] of [illegible] [illegible] by
my getting things together to pack my trunks. — down the hill paying bills — called in at Fanny Mulford's &
there met Josie Beutly, went up the hill with her & again at my trunks —
after dinner, through Rain & mud, down town again. Settled all bills & a 5 o'clk. Up the hill again — ¾ of an hour at the trunks & then went to Fan Mulford's to tea, with her until 8 o'clk then got horse & buggy & took her up to Uncle Wm. Rull & got J. alone Watrous — night, black as ink — gale from the East & heavy Rain. Put horse in barn —
Josie Beutly, Del. Raynsford & Vic Dimrock with Joe Williams, Dan. Sayre, Rev. Milford & [illegible] Tyler came & danced until after midnight. Joe Wm's took home Fan &
I alone & the horse & buggy — to bed at 7.30
Wild axe handle & Molly Grubs at 3 o'clk A.M. Don't know why.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, October 30, 1866.

But little Rain & wind chopping around up between 7 & 8 o'clk. After [illegible] finished packing, went down town, bid some of the folks good-bye — took out an Accident Ins. for $5000 vs. "Railway Pass. Ass. Co." Hartford.
Up on the Hill — more bidding good bye — at 10.30 Stage called — Off all Right. Rw. W. —
But only passenger besides ourselves —
Cold, windy Ride over to New Milford, but after all much to enjoy in the leafless tree forms — dead ferns & underbrush & effects of mist — New M. at 12.10 — poor dinner —
[illegible] Hotel — a short walk & then waiting at depot — Train due at 2.07 — arr. at 2.10
Tea at Delaware Station at 7.30 — Train delayed by a coal train ahead — Harriston Junc.
[illegible] m. behind time — Train on Jersey Central, waiting.
Due at Jersey City at 10.35 — did not get in until 11.85 — got up home at 12.15 — found all in bed, having given us up for the night.
