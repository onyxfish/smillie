## Left Page

### Thursday, April 26, 1866.

Overcast and windy —
Commenced upon a little
(6×10 in) Canvas an evening
effect — In the afternoon
Mr. Greatorex came in
saw it, liked it, and paid
me $20 00 for it — All very
pleasant —
Ev[en]g at home.

## Right Page

### Friday, April 27, 1866.

Glorious day — worked all
day finishing up "Noon" 6½×9.
for Mortimer Smith — and
"Under the Bough". 7×12 and
"River Scene" — 6×10. for Louis
Menger — I have them
now in such shape that I
can present them as finished,
but I feel that they are hard
cases — I regret especially
that from pecuniary necessity
I am obliged to do such trivial
little things — hurried at that.
Eveng spent a few minutes
at Wm Martin's Esq. 155. W 21. St.
& a few more minutes at
Dr. Mills', where Meta Gavis
is staying to-night.
Father has not been so well
to-day — suffering from head-
ache.
