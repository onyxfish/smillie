## Left Page

### Monday, June 25, 1866.

Another Specimen brick of a day, sent down to us from Paradise. — Think I managed to enjoy it — Very warm but quite breezy. — Played croquet again from about 10 until dinner time — After Dinner Miss D. drove her Father to West Stockbridge to take cars for N.Y. — Miss D. ret'd about four o'clk. The day if possible more delicious, langour breathing and satisfying than any yet. — Sat for an hour or more upon the Croquet ground, looking to the South, over the little lake + to the mts beyond. — thankful as for the Sum of all enjoyments, in that I was alive. A few minutes after 5 o'clk Miss D. took me over to Lenox Furnace — then to Lee Centre + then home — arr'd a few minutes before 8 o'clk — A late tea, & then one of the most magnificent Storms with lightning + thunder, such as I do not Recollect to have seen or heard for many years.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, June 26, 1866.

Still another glorious day — After more Croquet, Misses D. + I— Gel + I — with Sketching traps, materials, + books, went to a little bower, prepared by Misses D. + Aphorpe, under a Hemlock tree — We Sketched until near 10 o'clk Miss Ireland Reading to us. — After dinner — a little finishing up of Sketches + then more Croquet —
About 5 o'clk. Misses D. + I. with Gel + I started out, drove around the East Side of Lake Mahkeenac to Curtisville + the little lake, called Mountain Mirror + sometimes Echo Lake — Returning home just between Sunset + Moon Rise. — After tea, as musquetoes were troublesome, built an old fashioned Smudge in front of the house + for a time enjoyed the smoke of red clover tops — Walked about the house + away out on the Road, "turning in" a little after ten — bfst. in the mfg being ordered at 6.30 + and a horse back Ride immediately after.
