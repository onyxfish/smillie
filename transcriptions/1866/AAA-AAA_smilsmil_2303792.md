## Left Page

### Tuesday, August 14, 1866.

a mild edition of yesterday
— all but the Rain. Got up
with a miserable headache —
feared for the day — no breakfast.
Colt standing haltered Ready
for me. — got saddle on but broke
again — strapped it on with a sur-
cingle. — went over to the D's met
Addie just Returning from the P.O.
Proposed a farther Ride — went
to top of West Stockbridge Mt., through
a wood Road & out back of Bristed's
Colt behaved Remarkably well —
Got home about 11 o'clk. Afternoon
finished outlining with Spts. turp. &
Brown, oatfield Study that until now
has only been up on the canvas in
pencil lines — Then went out & out-
lined upon Academy Rd Rattlesnake Mt.
part of Mon. Mt. & the lake. at about 5.
made unsuccessful attempt to see Mrs. Jaffin
concerning my class, then went on to
Bristed's to see Ehringer. Met E. & a Mr.
Sedgwick on the Road. Went home with
E. & saw his work — then across lot to Smith.
Delf on my back — trouble below stairs,

## Right Page

### Wednesday, August 15, 1866.

Steady heavy Rain until noon.
Mut out to the barn & found Gideon in
trouble concerning some stairs that he
was attempting to make to go up to his
loft — took measurements — got angles
& worked all the M'f. finishing a
very Respectable & serviceable looking
set of steps that are to stand in
memory of one. Afternoon soft
& warm until near three o'clk. when
the Sun mastered — wind changed
from S.E. to N.E. — N. & N.W. fine
clouds & showers about us. Geo.
Hawes made us a pleasant call to
see our studies, bet. 3 & 4 o'clk. — Soon
after bro. Ge. walked over to the D's
& I put saddle on Colt & Rode over.
Saw only Miss Apthorpe. Colt behaved
better than ever — R'd home at 6 —
Early evef. Ge. H. brought over to us
letters & paper — from Matt. many &
Charl. Evef. in doors, old Mr.
Sears & his daughter Nancy
adding a little variety.
