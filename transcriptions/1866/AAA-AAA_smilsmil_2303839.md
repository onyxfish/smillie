## Left Page

### Friday, November 16, 1866.

Fine day — Cool and fresh —
Mr., mounted Locomotive
drawing & pottered a little
over it — Then set palette &
+ fixed + worked over "October"
making 5½ days for painting
+ about 2½ for drawing.

Mother came in town during the
day, Father returned with her
to stay over night — Gel,
Gel and I went to Tom Jessuden's
153 My 4 St. + spent the evef.
with J.C.T. + the family + their
cousin "Portland Tom" Jessenden.
Things quite "lovely" etc, etc.
Home a few minutes after
10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Saturday, November 17, 1866.

Clear, windy + quite cold — half cloud
half sunshine. At the Studio at 9 a.m.
Got things fixed up somewhat for the
day + at 9.45 went to Phil. Reh. at
Steinway's. The Schumann Symphony
No. 2, I could stand, really enjoying
portions of 3rd movement, but the Rest
was quite beyond me. Feeling chilly,
went down to Scharfenbergs then to
Reed's Galleries, cor. 12 St. + B'd way. Saw
Reed's + the Benson + Homer collection
to be sold to-night. Returned to
Rehearsal + there met J. C. T. At the
close of the Beethoven Concerto for piano
we left + went to the Studio where Gel
joined us — had a pleasant visit
until 1.30 — P.m. finished Locomotive
+ Sheep's Head drawings. Mr. Greaton
brought in Miss Wheeler + 2 other ym's ladies.
Evef. with Geo, Mary, Matt + Kate, went
to Phil Concert. Liked the 3rd movement
in Schumann's Symphony a little better than in
the mf. + so far got hold of an idea in Listz's
that I really enjoyed it. Bristow's
Columbus interested me, but I cannot
think very much of it. Met S. Throb at Concert.
