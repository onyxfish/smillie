## Left Page

### Saturday, March 17, 1866

The patron Saint of the beast Irish has a fine morning — I wish that my hatred for the
Devil might be as positive —
it would be healthy. Mrs
Choate took her 4th lesson —
Katy G — her 14th? I painted
upon "From under the bough"
(2nd day) — got over all but the
tree — Afternoon, a heavy
snow squall lasting but
a few minutes — It snowed
in upon us Mr. Mr. E. Dodge Jnr.
He saw, liked & bought the
little picture that Mr. Reed
did not like yesterday —
R— was to pay one $30.00 —
D— will pay me $40.00 —
Ev'g at Studio —

## Right Page

### Sunday, March 18, 1866

Clear, cold, windy muter
m'g — p.m. overcast and
milder. Went to Dr. J. P. J's
in the m'g — heard him preach
a begging sermon for their mission
school up town — took up a
collection of $5520. Before
2 o'clk p.m. with Geo. went to
Mr. Choates to dinner by inv'tn.
93 W. 21st — met Mr. C & a younger
he Mr. C, her mother Mr. Sterling
& miss Vaughn. Had a very
pleasant dinner & afternoon.
Singing by Miss V. & conversation
left at 5 o'clk — Ev'g went to
Madison Ave. Baptist Ch. to
hear Dr. Robinson of Rochester
on "The Relation of Bible and Church"
was suffering from a regular fit
of dyspepsia, so did not enjoy
it, as otherwise I might.
