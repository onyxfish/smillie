## Left Page

### Saturday, August 4, 1866.

Early, mrng pleasant — went
to the Elms a little after 8. Addie
did not come until after 9 o'clk.
Had a frank & satisfactory conversation
with her, which explained some things
that I had only been able to surmise
before. Penciled on some white Pine
tops that rise from the woods just
beyond the Elms. Near 12, Rain
began to sprinkle & me repainted before
the day. Afternoon, Geo. drove with
Mr. Smith to Stockbridge. After working awhile with the pencil, at 3
o'clk. I started out to "do" Rattlesnake
Mt & I did it, very satisfactorily
to myself in 3 hours, hastened
somewhat by a very heavy thunderstorm that reached us a little after
6 o'clk. I was much pleased with
the Rocks, or ledges — Hemlocks and
Birches on the Mt — parts quite
warmed me up with old Catskill
Memories — Evening at home
writing — Geo. to Mary — and
I, to Matt. High wind & cold.

## Right Page

### Sunday, August 5, 1866.

Cloudy, windy and cool. Walked
in to church at the Rate of 2 miles
an hour, enjoying very much effects
of sunlight & cloud shadows. At the
Cong. Ch. heard Rev. Mr. Matoon
preach a lachrymose sermon upon
a text from Eccl.

Took lunch at Mr. Sears & again
went to church & heard Mr. Smith
(when I was not asleep.)

Dinner, writing & talking finished
the rest of the afternoon and
evening. Went to bed early, to
get rid of ourselves. Night
very cold, dark, wet and windy.
