## Left Page

### Friday, September 21, 1866.

Very heavy Rain and wind all
day long — Second day of Snog.
Co. Agri. Fair — Dismal work —
throngs of country people and stock
from miles around splashing along
the muddy road looking about
as happy as drowned rats. Band
played sweetly under a leaking shed
— Ice cream served on the grounds.
All efforts to make a "Fair Day" of it
fell through about noon — Kept in
doors until after three o'clk when
G.J. and I went down to Fan Mulford
Found there Amanda Diven, Elisha M.
& wife & other members of the family —
Took tea-dinner with them at 5 o'clk
& spent the evef. with them until 9 o'clk
— a game of whist & some romping
filling up the evef. After 9 o'clk
went down the hill again to the
Post Office. To bed again at
Mr. Lathrop's, where by arrangement
we are to sleep until Mary goes
home.

## Right Page

### Saturday, September 22, 1866.

Breezy & cool, clearing up — By the
afternoon it was glorious & cloudless.
In the M.f. Mr. Wm. Jessup came to the
house with muslin for me to make
banners for a Republican Mass Meeting
to be held here next Monday — I am
on the Com. on "Banners & Inscriptions."
Called in at Fan's at 10.30 o'clk for a
few minutes — Rest of a.m. getting up
banner frames & sketching muslin —
P.m. hard at work on "This is the way
we'll treat the 'r Clymer" — Heister Clymer,
Dem. Can. for Gov. flying in air, hoisted by
charge of mortar (oct. elections) loaded with
4000 Repub. Maj. After J. Mary, S.C.F.
& I took a walk for ¾ of an hour — After
tea spent the evef. working on 2d banner.
Andy Johnson holding in his arms, baby-wise
a little monster. Copperhead — Jackass and
Devil. "This is my Policy." I am told it's Ma
& Seward & Raymond it's Dry Nurses" — G.J.
took tea at Mr. Jessup's with Fan Mulford &
Amanda Diven — Came in at 11 o'clk.
