## Left Page

### Wednesday, May 16, 1866.

Another fine day — In the M'f. painted a little sketch 3½ × 7 in for Miss Georgiana Gordon — then painted a 2d sketch 3×7 in a little storm bit, that I have given to Mr. Jas. Fairman to pay him for a sketch that he gave me — At about 5 o'clk. P.M. Mr. Capocutt came and insisted upon my Riding with him — So drove in style — fine horses before — darkie behind — through Central Park. Returned about 6.30. Clouded over & quite chilly — Rain with thunder & lightning comm'd. at about 8 o'clk. My eyes troubling me, I spent the even with a cloth wet with cold tea spread over them.

## Right Page

### Thursday, May 17, 1866.

Rain pretty steady during the day — M'f worked a little while on the Storm sketch that I commenced yesterday — then finished up the little 8×10 in picture "In the woods, on the Hudson" for Mr. W. E. Dodge Jun. Finished by 2 o'clk. & then outlined subject 9×15 in — for Mr. Jas. Earle of Phila —

Day so chilly that we had fire at the Studio.

Even at home — Father is apparently much improved. Says that he has worked on his Plate 6 hours to-day, and is quite sprightly. Deacon Phelps called & spent a good part of the even with him.
