## Left Page

### Thursday, October 25, 1866

Still bleak and quite wintry. Blue sky tried to show itself, but was unsuccessful. Got to my painting rather late in the mng. Painted in Centre Tree on 10×14. At 4 o'clk proposed a walk out to Jas. Carmalts. Sarah, Gel. & I went — pleasant, brisk walk — Mr. T. Mrs. C. out — left cards — Returning, met them both opposite Judge Warner's, visited in the Road awhile — then called upon Del. Paynesford at Judge Warner's — then home to tea — After tea went down to Fanny Mulford's — Popped chestnuts & corn until 9.30 — then escorted Fanny Seymour home. Home myself by 10 o'clk. Dan Sayre, Jor. Williams & his cat calling at the house. Away about 10.30 — to bed after 11.

## Right Page

### Friday, October 26, 1866

Another wintry day — windy mth slight snowy flurries. After my walk in the mng got to work again painting upon 10×14. Finished all that I can do upon it this painting — put in bare trees & little work about central groupe — afternoon. Fanny Mulford came about 3 o'clk & stayed until 5 — visited with us in the parlor mean time I painted up a little sketch "Delaware River above Easton" that I intend to give to her — Escorted her home & took a walk through a cold, drizzle. Eveng. Jor. Williams brought up a little surprise company for a dance — Fanny Milford, Del. Paynesford, Salome Watrous, Josie Benty — and Dan Sayre. Ben. Mulford & Addison Watrous making the party. We had a very pleasant time & danced until about midnight. Uncle Mr. & Aunt H. seemed to enjoy it. After, Gel. & I had a spirited controversy with Sarah. She enjoys joking and flaring mightily — if she has the best of it.
