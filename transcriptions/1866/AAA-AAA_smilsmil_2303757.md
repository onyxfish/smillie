## Left Page

### Tuesday, June 5, 1866.

Muggy, mussy, miserable day — feel very like a Rag, and a wet one at that. Painted all day ( day) upon Wood Scene 10×14 in. for Mr. Jas. Earle, dragging greys & light color over it. Trying to get the blackness & tawness out of it. Rec'd a letter from Addie Dunning — Eveny. Geo. & I spent at Mr. Ireland's — 25th St. Miss Emma has decided to go to Europe on the 15th of this month — we are to go with her, if possible.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, June 6, 1866.

Muggier, mussier & more miserablem than yesterday. Weather fit only for Cholera cases & Croton-water-bugs — Called on Dr. Wm. Thompson in the mng. & spent nearly two hours with him consulting professionally. My dyspepsia he considers altogether nervous, & so places particular stress upon Recreation and pleasant Society. Worked half a day ( day) on 10×14. Still continuing the greying process. Eveny. spent an hour at Düring's, hearing him play.
