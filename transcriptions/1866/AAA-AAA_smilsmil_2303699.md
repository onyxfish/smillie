## Left Page

### Sunday, January 28, 1866

Overcast, chilley and Raw,
Went to Dr. Thompson's in the
mng, but getting there late
heard but part of the sermon.
Afternoon, after dinner,
took a cup of tea with Mr
& Mrs. Brevoort — then at the
Studio & prepared Father's
tea for him — with Geo.
went to Dr. Thompson's in
the eveng — After church
again at the Studio and
then home to bed.

## Right Page

### Monday, January 29, 1866

Again chilley, overcast and
disagreeable generally —
Mng went down town, taking
with me small picture "Bit on
the Hudson" 7½ × 10¾ in. to Mences
to send out to the Utica Exhibition.
Went to C.B.N.Co. & got proofs
of Graham's Sheep [illegible], also
Transfer of Beaver. P.O. stamps
for Uncle Willie. Met Robert
Hinshelwood in the Street was
accosted by him, but took no
notice of him. Went to No. 1
Wall St. & saw Father, his big
plate & third proof from it —
Its condition I think admirable
but am afraid that he will find
his time to finish it (2½ months)
short —      P.M. — quite useless
from nervous use-up & headache.
Eveng, Geo. slipped & sprained
his right ankle — slightly. Spent
the eveng with him & Father
at the Studio.
