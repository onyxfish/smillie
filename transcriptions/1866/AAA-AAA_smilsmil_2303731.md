## Left Page

### Friday, April 6, 1866

overcast, early mrt — hot
& clear about noon — Rain
by 3 o'clk — Painted all day
(3d day) on "Path by the River"
10×14 in. Glazed heavily
upon it, warm Rich transparent
colors. Yellow Madder — Madder Lake
Antwerp Blue & Bitumen. The
first painting is too dark for
such treatment — as it carries
parts down too near black. I
glaze with Siccatif de Haarlem
& as it gets tacky, paint right
into it — Eveng went to Mr. Wm
Blodgetts, No 56 W 25th St., to see
his pictures. Most of the painters
were there — no ladies — Geo.
& I left before 10 o'clk to go
to Meta Gavits — After we left
Mr. B's — a fine supper was
served — Songs were sung
& there was a jolley time
generally.

## Right Page

### Saturday, April 7, 1866

Cold and Rainy — Painted
(4th day) on "Path by the River"
getting over the foreground —
Mrs Choate came & took
her 7th lesson. Katy G. her 16th
lesson — Mrs G. asked me
for something definite concern-
ing my terms, & I had the imped-
ance to mention $5.00 a lesson
which she declared to be satisfacto-
ry.
At 3 o'clk. went to Phil.
Rehearsal to hear Beethoven's
7th Symphony — then Returned
& again went to work —
Every at home.
