## Left Page

### Sunday, July 1, 1866

A model day — cool, clear and bright. — We walked up to the Methodist Ch. with Gel arr'd late but heard a part of — Miller's discourse — He is the Regular pastor — expected to hear — Cookman — a former pastor. After service, walked with Misses D. & I., Gel & Ned, into Mr. Aspinwall's grounds to wait until afternoon service. There met Thuinger, Gel. C. Authon & three ladies — At 1:30 again met to the Methodist Ch. & heard Mr. Hokman — quite a telling & showy discourse — Tept. the invitation to come & drink of the Fountain of Life, in Revelations. Dinner at 3 o'clk. with considerable discussion upon the discourse of the p.m. — the rest of the p.m. Reading & conversation. Ev'y D. Read from the Psalms & the 19th of St. John — then some sacred music. — then general writing of letters from 9:45 until 11:30 o clk — a short piazza walk with Emma & I. & then to bed.

## Right Page

### Monday, July 2, 1866

Clear & bright but warmer than yesterday. The m'f all used up with visitors & croquet. The D. took a hand with us in croquet. The Addie D. & I. made a side & were beaten after a very respectable fight. Chas. Astor Bristed's daughter & a child, limping, came over horseback, & afterwards, Mr. Sub. Thuinger, with whom we had more croquet, making a very late dinner, whereat the D. was vexed — It spoiled our plan of driving to Pittsfield with Ned to set him on his way to New York. Miss Apthorne drove over to West Stockbridge with him at 2 o'clk . — P.M. while sitting on the piazza with the girls, yawned, & had to pay a tremendous penalty — put off the piazza, rolled in the grass & generally discombobolated. Then sauntered into some woods to get an outline sketch on a little brook. — then up to the P.O. Ev'y Dr. Addie, Emma & I went about 2½ miles — Furnace Road to a Church Sociable at Mr. Thos. Sedgwick's — quite a lot of pretty girls — Bed home about 11. very jolly.
