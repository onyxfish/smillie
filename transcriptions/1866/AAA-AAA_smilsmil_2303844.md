## Left Page

### Wednesday, November 28, 1866.

Muggy disagreeable day, with
Rain and wind — Quite under the weather myself — part
of the [illegible] unfit for work with Neuralgia in the left side of my face.
Finished outline upon 10×14 in
panel & outlined two little subjects
6×10 in. for Uncle Willie. Then
got out 16×22 "On the Hudson"
for Mr. W.E. Dodge Junt. & studied
on that for awhile. Kelly
sent up my 20×36 in Canvas for
my Grain field picture to-day.
Even at home looking over
my file of Ill. London News —
& trying to get some of my traps
in order —

## Right Page

### Thursday, November 29, 1866.

More like April & June than
Nov. Muggy & damp — now black
dark & Raining — in a moment,
all clear & glistening in bright sunlight — Still uncomfortable about
my face — Worked all day on
9×12 Sunset that I painted last
June & called "The Eagle's Nest" —
I have Repainted it to send to
Albany for Sale —. After morning
service (it being Thanksgiving day)
Father gave us a call — at about
2 o'clk Eel went up to Manhattan
ville to Uncle Aaron's where there is
some jollification — I don't feel able
to go — At Mr. Gait's Request, I put
a wash over a part of my Sheep's head
drawing — Between 3 & 4 o'clk P.M —
Ned Dunning gave me a call of an
hour — at 4.30 went to Artists Fund [illegible]
& stayed until 5.30 — Tea at home
with only Father, Mother & Kate. Every
trying to get closets, etc, to Rights.
