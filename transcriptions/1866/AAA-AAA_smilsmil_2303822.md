## Left Page

### Saturday, October 13, 1866

Fine mrg — Clear & quite mild —
almost used up with a headache
— moved about gently, went to the Gris-
mill, & down town — About 11 o'clk went
to bed & slept soundly until near 3 o'clk —
got up feeling much better and
took my dinner. At 3 o'clk Mr.
Mrs. Harris — Gel & Sarah went out
Riding — I bothered about until
4 o'clk trying to get a horse to ride.
At last borrowed Dr. Gardiner's horse
Goodwin's saddle & [illegible] Tyler's bridle.
Trotted out to Jas. Carmalts — only his mother
at home — Trotted on, around the lake,
the cross Road near the mill over to Silver
Lake Road and into town. Trotting back
& forth until near 6 o'clock. Horse behaved
well — fat as a pig, trot very short & uneven.
Eveg. went up to Mrs. Bards for a short
time. — Ret'd with Sarah intending to
make a call or two — but laziness &
indifference got the best of us — So
Sat & Read until 9 o'clk. then
took a walk down the hill —
back & to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sunday, October 14, 1866

Clear, cold mrg. Soon clouded
up & blew hard all day. — Mrg,
went to the Presbyterian Ch. Mr.
Miller undertaking to explain to
us the Relations of the Almighty
to evil. After Service, with Sarah,
stopped in to Fanny Mulford's
for half an hour. Afternoon
Sarah, Gel, Mr. Mrs. Harris & I walked
to the Ridge beyond Warner's and
under the lee of a stone wall admired
the fall pictures. Sarah, Gel & I
then walked around the cross road
from the Salt Spring Road to the Silver
Lake Road & then over Brewster's Hill into
town — getting there at 5.50 o'clk.
Eveg went with Sarah to the Presb'n
& heard Mr. M. draw a parallel between
the pilgrimage of the Israelites and
Christian Progress. After Service went
with Sarah, Vic D., Miss Doolittle, Ad. Watrous
& wife, Hy Foster, Dan Sayre, and Jes. to
the Zion Ch. of darkies — Quarterly
meeting — too many white people — ark
moved not move — dismissed at 9 o'clk
— all home.
