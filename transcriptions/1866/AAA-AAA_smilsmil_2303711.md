## Left Page

### Wednesday, February 21, 1866

Another fine day. Rec. from Academy notices that they will give another Reception next Thursday wor. Mch. 1st pictures to be called for, Monday Fb. 26th. So I just set to work & finished "Recollection of Hyde Park Scenery, on the Hudson" 6½ × 9 in. (2 days.) I am told that it is as good as anything that I have done. Evel. Gil. & I went to adjourned meeting of artists at N. A. of D. having reference to the Paris Exposition of 1867. It was stated, upon revised measurements that there would be 2100 feet (sq. feet) of wall room — 1824 have already been applied for. Lutz, Gray & Hicks waxed indignant at the limited space. The Report of the last meeting Committee was accepted, but the meeting refused to adopt their suggestions. Kens. presided — whole thing farcical.

## Right Page

### Thursday, February 22, 1866

Yet another glorious day, but am miserably under the weather with something very like influenza. Worked (13th day) on Academy picture on the trees & meadow on top of left hand hill. Am in a peck of troubles now seeking for a gray that is clear & silvery yet not cold. Worked away until 3 o'clk P.M. Then went to see French pictures in 10th St. Studio Bldg. From there to Mr. A. B. Durand's. Had a very pleasant visit with the old gentleman & his son Fred. Saw the new Catskill Mt. picture. Mr. D. is complaining much this winter of lumbago — he looks feeble. Being such a glorious day & a holiday the streets were crowded with people & gay with music, soldiers & flags. Went in to Brady's to see his war photographs — got medicine from D. Bartlette. Evel. Jau fires works at Madison Sq. & wrote to Sister Mary.
