## Left Page

### Friday, June 29, 1866

Weather still very fine — Ned & I
played again, but before finishing
the horses were ready & Miss D. & I
started off horseback to find a
boarding place for Geo. & I for three
or four weeks. Mr. Smith, with whom
Charley Dunning this boat crew are to
stay (5 fellows) agreed to take us at
$11.00 p. week — Returning, played two
games with Ned — winning one — So, am
Champion & entitled to the Mallet.
After dinner, Misses D. & J. — Geo. Ned,
& I — went to Lee Centre and visited
the middle one of "The Forest Paper
Mills" — Mr. Garfield, the proprietor
was an exceedingly agreeable and
interesting guide. His three mills
finish about 300 tons of writing paper
p. year. Every cloudy and
quite cool — Enjoyed a walk
and talk with Miss Emma d.
after tea;

## Right Page

### Saturday, June 30, 1866

Cool and pleasant — Early mf.,
fixed up Croquet ground with Ned.
At about 10 o'clk. AM. Miss Rosalie Goodman
came by invitation, to play Croquet
with us. She is a remarkably good player,
and all our hopes (Ned's & mine) of beating
her were incontinently smashed — we
were disgracefully beaten — and much
made fun of besides — Dr. Dunning arr.
his mt. a little before 8 o'clk — Miss D.
having driven over to Pittsfield for him.
After dinner, at about 3 o'clk — Miss
D. on horseback. Miss J. Ned. Geo. & I in
the wagon, we drove to Lenox Furnace
crossing the River — down towards Lee,
then turning & going up the Mt. — through
a mild pass, by "Roaring Brook" — back
over the mts. & out again by New Lenox
& so home at about 8 o'clk — Evg.
played whist. Dr. D. & Miss J. against
Miss D. & I — our side beaten in three
straight games — then, between 10 &
11 — we had a good walk by moonlight.
Sky, cloudless — air, clear, cool
and quite crisp. Fire in Library —
