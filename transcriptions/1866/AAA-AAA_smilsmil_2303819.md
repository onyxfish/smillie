## Left Page

### Sunday, October 7, 1866.

Very mild, cloudless &smoky day. Up early &short walk before bkfast. Sauntered up to "Fair-ground" before church &enjoyed intensely the Autumn Sabbath, the tolling bells etc. etc. Got to the Presb'n Ch. late, but heard the sermon from Mr. Miller on "Completeness in Christ" after my service waited through communion which was very impressive. Joseph Williams was rec'd into the Church upon profession of faith. At 4 o'clk, miss Sarah &her went up to the Rocks on Fair-ground &were there joined by Mrs. Mr. &aunt H. Mr. &Mrs. Harris. Every-one went with Sarah &miss H. to the Presb'n Ch. I stayed at home &finished letters to Mary and Addie Dunning — took them to the P.O. at 10.30 — to bed by 11.

## Right Page

### Monday, October 8, 1866.

Another fine morning — but breezy — While at Croquet with Mr. H. Ge. passed in a carriage with Fau. Maud &Sylvestre &go't boys — Mrs. Comstock called &took Sarah riding. I worked awhile on one of my drawings — at near 11. Ge. Del? with the girls, asking me to go on the hill with them. Took sketching traps, &went up to Warner's lot &got an outline of some foreground rocks. After dinner, more croquet until 3 — when I got Tarbell's horse. Mr. H. had ordered a vehicle of Searle at 3 o'clk. did not come until 3.30 — just as about starting Mrs. Jas. Riley &sister called detaining us until after 4. — Meantime I rode up &down &about — started at last accompanied carriage around the lake — then separated. I went over a cross road to Silver Lake Road. Fine sunset — got in quarter to 6. Every-one until 9 o'clk at Fau. Mulford's, then home.
