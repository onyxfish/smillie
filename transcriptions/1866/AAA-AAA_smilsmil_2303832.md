## Left Page

### Friday, November 2, 1866.

Delightful day — Gel & I went & registered ourselves as voters — first business of the day — Then went to Studio — got fire kindled & went to fixing up Studio. Had a long call from Carrie & Fanny Kimball — while there Lila Martin called, left Sarah Martin's card, telling us that she (Sarah) was to sail for France tomorrow — wanted us to call. Home to dinner at 1 o'clk — After dinner started to go to Martin's in 21st St — Met Sarah, Lula & Gil in the St. Escorted them to the cars — then with Gel went to the Kintalls, to see the new decorations of the house — Ret'd to Studio about 4 o'clk — there until 6 — then home. Saw them later in the eve'g — went in to Mr. Wells & met Mr. Gavit & one of his Sons — met a "their feller" Charley Adams — there until 10 o'clk — Tobacco smoke, ice cream & every body very funny the characteristics of the evening.

## Right Page

### Saturday, November 3, 1866.

Rather Raw, with haze promising a change in weather — waited about the house in the m'g for painters &c — at the Studio by 9.30 — Among other things, spent most of the m'g charcoaling on "Noon" with sheep, 14×18 in — at 1, went home to lunch — about the house with carpenters & plasterers until about 3 o'clk — then went down to 2d Phil. Rehearsal at Steinway's Hall — first time in it — a great uncomfortable barn-like place — very little interested in the music, as seemed to be the case with most others — getting acquainted with the place. Met Emma, & Fanny K — Mrs. Spring — sat with them all around — After Rehearsal went home with Emma — Eve'g Gel & I spent at Mr. Harris's in 46th St. with Mr. & Mrs. H., Mr & Mrs. Ryckoff and Miss Baldwin — home at 10 o'clk —
