## Left Page

### Thursday, January, 18, 1866.

Mild day — Streets all running
water. Clouded m'g clear P.M.
In the m'g had a call from
W. E. Dodge Jun't. — He bought, for
his friend Mr. Stedman, last Aug-
of Jno. Snedecor, my painting of
"From the Top of Buttermilk Ravine"
He now wants something of same
sort for himself, & commissioned me
to paint a 14×20 in. pic. for him.
This is very flattering & encouraging
to me. P.M., went down-town &
saw Pres't Dunbar, Mr. Chas. Jewett
of Con. Bk. Note Co. concerning this
fracas of Mr. Hinshelwood's & his by
Locomotive die. I am all right
with them. Afterward, met R. H.
himself at Uncle W's 95 Liberty St.
The bold crazy farce was again
enacted on his part, bombast &
insolence, when Uncle W. attempted
to effect something of an under-
standing. It was pitiable —
Ev'g at home.

## Right Page

### Friday, January 19, 1866.

Another mild day. Clouded
m'g & clear p.m. Commenced
(4th day) outlining "Smash up"
drawing for National Bk. Note Co.
Afternoon Rec'd. by City Post
a letter marked Official Business
& directed in a strange hand.
Of course I opened it, & found
it a letter from R. Hinshelwood
coarser & more vulgar, if possible
than his first effort. He shows
himself to be most pitifully ignorant
ungenerous & unmanly. I merely
underscored the misspelled words
& sent it back to him, without word
or comment. Ev'g by invitation
went to young Prof. Draper's. No. 13
E. 30th St. where we met a pleasant
little conversation party of 18 or 20.
Draper is an old Hobbie School fellow
of mine. Mr. Capscutt was very
attentive & Mrs D. & Miss Broadlaw
very agreeable. Met young
Griswold there. Uncle Willie
called at Studio about 5 o'clk P.M.
