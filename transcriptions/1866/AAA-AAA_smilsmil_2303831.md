## Left Page

### Wednesday, October 31, 1866

Fine morning — House in terrible confusion — Carpenters, painters
& plasterers from cellar to garret —
no part of the house properly habitable.
Most of the mng about the house
seeing what is or is to be done. While
at bkfst table Recd a note from Addie
Dunning, among other things, telling me
of her engagement to George Hawes.
About noon, Uncle Aaron's coachman with carriage called for Mother
& she went to spend a few days at
Manhattanville. Afternoon, with
Gel went to Sugar's & looked at some
wall paperings — then went to Studio
called upon Mr. Greatorey, Brevort
Augers & Jas Hart & saw their Studies
afterwards went to Snedecors & Schau's.
Eve spent miserably over a
poor fire trying to get warm and
to find comfort in some aching
grinders that were troubling me.
Mary at Danny's — Gel Matt & Kate
in at Will's — Charl at Co. drill
and Father in bed —

## Right Page

### Thursday, November 1, 1866

Clear, Crisp day — went to Studio at
a little before 9 o'clk & there by appoint-
ment met a woman that with me set
to work & by noon we had things looking
quite clean & pleasant — swept, scrubbed
& washed — also dusted — Home at one
o'clk & took dinner — Charl at home
with a touch of bowels — Spent the
remainder of the afternoon looking
after the men about the house.
Evg. Gel & I took some traps
along to Studio, then called upon
Addie Dunning & Emma Ireland
at the Ireland's in 25th St. Both gals
ladies very serene — Addie evidently
very nappy — her Father called in
for a few minutes — Ned Dunning
came in & stayed until we left,
at about 10.30 — Home, & to bed at
11.30
