## Left Page

### Friday, October 19, 1866.

Still they come, the glorious days of
October. Up early & nearly finished a
letter to Sister Matt before breakfast.
A little after 10 o'clk A.M. took pencils &
paper & started for woods on Mulford's
Hill — nearly 2 miles off — quite a warm
tramp — M[oun]t[ain]f[ull], breezy, hazy & perfect —
enjoyed myself hugely — in the woods
under the hemlocks & out among the
tall, almost leafless maples & beeches
— on a stump watching the leaves come
silently down. Home at 1.30 — very tired —
dinner at 2 — short nap after dinner.
A few minutes after 3, Uncle Mr. Sam[uel]
H— in their vehicle & Sarah & I on Tarbell's &
Meeks' horses, went out on Friendsville
Road to Dutch Hill, then across to Creek
Road at D[r.] Cornell's, then straight home.
My mare trotted beautifully — did not
get in until 6 — all rather tired —
after tea — all asleep in parlor until near
8 o'clk — then Sarah & Gel went to Mr. G[eorge]
Bentley's & did not get in until 10.45 —
I worked on one of my M[oun]t[ain]f[ull] sketches —
bare maple group — the other sketch
a clump of hemlocks.

## Right Page

### Saturday, October 20, 1866.

Another day "more so" than any preceding. I
had intended visiting Elisha Mulford at Lake Side to-
day, to get a study from the head of his Merino
Buck — I would have gone on horseback in the morning
& Ret[urne]d by moonlight — but I did not feel quite
equal to the 22 miles there & back, so, with regret,
gave it up. Spent the M[ornin]g penciling on maple
groups that I got yesterday M[ornin]g. Between 11 & 12
went up to Warner's lot & finished study of rock —
then until after one — just heavenly — the dream
& gleam in the atmosphere beyond my capacity
to do justice to, or enjoy enough. P.M. penciling
on various studies & setting studies & sketches
already completed. About 5 o'clk called
upon the Misses Biddle & had a chatty
pleasant call of nearly an hour. On
returning home, Sarah not yet Ret[urne]d from [Judge]
Warner's — went out there — found her at tea —
did not disturb her but Ret[urne]d immediately, enjoy-
ing a moonlight equal in loveliness to the day
it follows. After tea, at about 8 o'clk, Sarah
came in, having walked in with Del Paynesford
& Salome Watrous. The Rest of ev[enin]g doing nothing —
at 11 o'clk took a short walk with Sarah
then to bed.
