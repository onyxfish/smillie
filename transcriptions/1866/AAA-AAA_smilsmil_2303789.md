## Left Page

### Wednesday, August 8, 1866

Still very windy so that I will not
venture to set up easle + umbrella
went into the woods again + worked
faithfully all the m[?] on the
Hemlock Study of yesterday. Made
good progress, getting it nearly
finished — Afternoon overcast
+ cloudy, at times looking very like
Rain. Worked again with the
pencil, for about an hour on the
Hemlocks + then until 5 o'clk.
upon the Pine tree tops. Wind
still troublesome unless in the woods
tin the woods, Musqueloes worse
than the wind — Yesterday I
built a smudge — but of the two
evils, Smoke + Musqueloes —
I am as yet undetermined
which to Choose —
Eve'g, lying around in-doors,

## Right Page

### Thursday, August 9, 1866

Steady, soaking Rain all day
long. Painted in, upon 10½×18 in.
canvas an evening sky— cumuli in
shadow, etc, that I had taken notes
of the other evening — very imperfect
ones I found, when I came to try
to paint from them. The result
of this painting is strong but very hard
+ cold + black looking. The time
I have tried to Represent is after
the sun had been down about half
an hour. I penciled a/s, in Addis'
sketch book, which Geo. has for a
few days, one of her unfinished
sketches at Dobb's Ferry — a little
winter River bit. Measured
Mr. Smith's piazza — found that
131 lengths of it made a mile +
that I could pace a mile pleasantly
in 20 minutes — So had a pleasant
walk of between 3 + 4 miles. Every
so damp + chilly that Mrs S. had
a little wood stove brought into the
sitting Room + a fire kindled in it.
Rec'd through P.O. invitation from Mrs.
Rackermann to spend to-morrow with her,
