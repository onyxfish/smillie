## Left Page

### Friday, May 18, 1866

Chilly, & Raw with Rain in the morning — worked all day painting in picture for Mr. Jas. Earle of Phila. 9×15. Worked in that pleasant frame where it seems impossible to get color tone — or any desirable quality — but at last get it all in. — Cold & tending to black. Evens, for an hour in at Dr. Wills.

## Right Page

### Saturday, May 19, 1866

Cloudless day & very warm. Painted in (1st day) Mortimer Smith's "Morning" 6×10 in. It seems strange that yesterday I should have worked under such discouragement — feeling at night, that I not only knew nothing about color, but that I never would — yet to-day I have worked quite happily & with an assurance that it will come if I stick to it patiently. The work I did yesterday was boyish & discreditable — of what I have done to-day, I am quite proud. The composition is very faulty, but I think the color in refined quality of grey, without absolute coldness is superior to anything I have yet done.

Katy G. took her 22d lesson. Mr. [illegible] P. Reed sent up $50.00 for his little painting.
