## Left Page

### Friday, July 13, 1866.

Broiling hot day with wind blowing a young hurricane —
My, finished pencil study from Lamb's head — Could
not get umbrella + fixings to stand in the wind — So at
about 11 o'clk. with sketchbook went over to the Dr's — Accepted
invitation to stay to dinner —
Smiths all away. Strawberrying.
From 3.30 until 5 — played Croquet
with Emma — then started
out with the three farm hands
Addie, Emma + Geo. to give
a grand final look for Addie's
lost watch — Unsuccessful.
Eve. had lots of fresh
straw berries for tea, + then
sat up on the piazza with
Gideon + talked horse, while
watching a thunder storm
come up, until 9.30 o'clk.

Storm arr'd during the
night some time.

## Right Page

### Saturday, July 14, 1866.

Still hot with little wind. My
again at my 14 × 24 study (4th my)
Repainting sky + getting in a couple
of the further elms — Am
almost in despair at my
unsuccess — The girls did
not come up to-day.
Afternoon, worked again over
sky on 12 × 18 in. study (4th day)
I have to wonder at times at
the utter absence of ability
to do the simplest things in
painting + study — My work
so far, would disgrace a beginner.
Eve. gathered water lilies +
abt. 8 + 9 o'clk went over to the
Dr's     D. D. Ned. + Geo. Hawes
arr'd from N.Y. this m'g by way
of Pittsfield.
