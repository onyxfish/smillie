## Left Page

### Sunday, June 17, 1866.

Overcast + dull — a blue heavy day. Myf went to Thompson's Co. + heard an old Prof. Somebody from an Ohio College — did not consider myself highly edified — but that might have been my fault. By noon it began to sprinkle + by 2 o'clk it was fairly started on a Regular afternoons Rain. Myf wrote to Miss Danning.

## Right Page

### Monday, June 18, 1866.

Dull cloudy mng. but very warm and muggy — An idea of the state of the atmosphere may be had from a published statement, that taking 100° as Saturation — there was an average of 83° last week, with a mean of 70° of temperature.
Worked all day (1½) on A.B.N.Co's drawing "Ohio arms" — Got it penciled in and commenced to wash in with India Ink. Eveyf Uncles Geo + Willie at the house.
