## Left Page

### Saturday, December 22, 1866

Dull, disagreeable day — and a miserable one to me indoors. Neuralgia + pain in head continues — Spent the day in a semi-stupid way, half awake + half asleep — Dr. J. came in the mrf. Yesterday P.m. for the first time I had some of Borden's Extract of Beef — it makes quite a palatable tea — That is to be my stand by now — In the Evmf. Gel brought in to me a pleasant little note of condolence from Mr. J.R. Brevoort. Father still at home and in bed all day, but says he is much better. A miserable night of stupid dreaming again — I do not get 4 hours sleep out of the 24.

## Right Page

### Sunday, December 23, 1866

Dark, dull and dismal. Father spent the day with me in my Room. Throop called in the mrf. Will called too, bringing me a characteristically pleasant letter from my dear Uncle Millie in Ottawa. His References to humbugging wasting time about the studio I think simply insulting. If I had humbugged a little more I might not have been on my back to-day. Got through the night tolerably well, by spending more than half of it in my chair.
