## Left Page

### Sunday, March 11, 1866.

Overcast & blustering — out
of sorts generally with cold
headache — did not get
up until 10 o'clk — went to
studio & got my breakfast
at 12 m. Between 3 & 4 P.M.
went down to St Nicholas. Met
Fanny M. Josie B. Kate Searle, Amanda
Diven, Salome Warner, Sylvester M.
Joe Williams, Cook, Hitchcock
& another fellow — all save Cook
took dinner — quite an affair.
Later in the evng Priscilla,
her husband, Katy Bunty
& Dick Dorman came in.
At 9 o'clk went to 34th St.
with Katy B — then to the
studio — met Gel — and at
10 went home to bed.

## Right Page

### Monday, March 12, 1866.

Commenced Raining in the
m'g, and continued at inter-
vals during the day, making
away at about dusk. Painted
all day (4th day) on Mr. Reed's
little picture. I call it done
now. Its tendency to blackness
is not altogether corrected —
and, especially in the foreground,
it is too much cut up by detail.
Evng Gel & I went down to the St.
Nicholas — but the Montrose party
were all out — Opera — L'Etoile du Nord.
We went up to Metrop. & saw Priscilla
& her husband for a few minutes.
Gel then went on home & I went to
the Broadway Theatre to see Owens
in The Victims & Forty Winks.
It is very funny, & I did my
share of laughing — but it
would be tiresome to go
more than once in a very long
while.
