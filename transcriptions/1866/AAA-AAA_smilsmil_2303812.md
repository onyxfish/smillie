## Left Page

### Sunday, September 23, 1866

Superb day — M'f. went to the Presbt. Ch. + heard Mr. Miller discourse on Secret Sins — After M'f. service, Geo. Mary & I walked around by the Foundry Road home to dinner. After dinner, at about 3 o'clk. Sarah, Mary, Geo. and I walked down the Nayglusing Road to the falls back of David Hinds. found the stream quite full, the falls on their good behavior and everything lovely. Ret'd. home by Road through Darkey Valley, getting back a few minutes after 6 o'clk.
Ev'g. Sarah, Geo. + I went again to Presbt. Ch. + heard Mr. Miller draw morals + lessons from Martha — too much cumbered with cares.

## Right Page

### Monday, September 24, 1866

For a wonder, another fine day. At 7 o'clk A.M. people began to come into town to attend Repub. mass meeting here to day — Set to work. Right after breakfast to finish banners — got through about 11 + distributed them — once among the New Milford delegation that stood on the top of our hill waiting for a chance to get into town — Went down town + found a perfect sight. a Regular Running River of rivers of men, women, horses + wagons pouring into town — drums + fifes — a brass band — banners + flags — quite exciting — at one time Public Ave. a mass of 4 horse teams + heavy wagons loaded with men — after dinner, speeches on the green — Judge Pitkin, Landon + Coates from 1.30 until 5.30 P.M. Took girls to Courthouse — could not hear — wandered about. Crowd expected to hear J. W. Forney — sick — Ev'g. Torch light procession — seen from top of hill, over green — torchlights 3 sections of it + Roman Candles + Red fire in front of Mr. Jessup's, very fine indeed — Went down to Joe Williams' — I, Ct., Mary, Fanny, Armand D., Geo. + lots of others there — crowd in front of Tarbell's and another speech from Landon then a bond fire on the green — boys tossed in a blanket — 12 + 15 feet up. Great fun — young man on a bit — glorious moonlight.
