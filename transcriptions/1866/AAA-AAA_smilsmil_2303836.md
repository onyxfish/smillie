## Left Page

### Saturday, November 10, 1866.

Charming day and very mild. Worked again all day (6th day) on "October," glazing foreground & painting into it & upon the centre rocks & tree. — Not yet what I had hoped to make of it. Alfred Jones from Ottawa, called in the m'g. Home to dinner at 1. Kate & Toony V.V. dined with us. P.M. at Studio, Addie D. — Called & Mrs. J. D. A. Ward and the Ward himself. — We & Gal. and I spent pleasantly at mr. Irelands. E. 25th St. Addie D. was there, but no Mr. Geo. Hawes. Finished up a little sketch for her, taking me until about 20 m. to 11, when we left.

## Right Page

### Sunday, November 11, 1866.

Dull overcast day at about 10.30 A.M. started for Uncle Aarons at Manhattanville — walked through Central Park, out at 110th St. & then across lots over 4 miles. Reached Uncle A's at 12 o'clk. Wandered down to the River in the woods much delighted and charmed with effects & color. — Dinner at 1.30. Quite a family party of us. Mother, Gro. Charl. Matt, Kate & myself. Afternoon spent chatting & frolling — all went home at 5 o'clk. Charl and I wandered about the Convent of the Sacred Heart in the twilight, returning to tea at 6 o'clk. — After 8. Charl & I Retired to the Billiard Room — till it not in dark — but — as I had never struck a ball upon a Billiard Table, Charl and I knocked the balls about for a while experimentally, satisfying our Consciences by doing no counting, & I may add, no play that could be counted. — To bed at 10.30 o'clk.
