## Left Page

### Friday, February 9, 1866

This mng., about 6 or 8 inches of beautiful white snow clung to every thing. Trees, fences and spires were fantastic wonders, under their beautiful, clinging load. By noon however the mild temperature and a slight rain had very much modified the beauty — By night, locomotion (pedal) was about as pleasant as it may be supposed to be in the grand canal of Venice. All day painting (1st day) on little 6×10 in for Menger. Miss Dunning came in the mng., drew, took lunch with us, and drew some more, (with a lead pencil.)

Ever again at the Studio with Father.

## Right Page

### Saturday, February 10, 1866

Muggy, misty, drizzly, dreary day. Mng., worked a couple of hours or so, on Beaver P.O. stamp for N.P.S. I hope that now it is finished. Burnished also for an hour or two on Graham's Sheep die for Continental Bk. Note Co. I hope that too is finished — P.M. went to 10° Philharmonic Rehearsal — Afterwards went with Miss Dunning to see Bierstadt's "Storm in the Rock Mts." Concluded that we didn't like it much. Not grand enough to suit us. Ever until after 9 o'clk at Studio with Father.
