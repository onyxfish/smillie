## Left Page

### Monday, April 16, 1866

Pouring down Rain until toward evening when it held
up, kindly, no doubt for the
Private View, N.A. of D —
All day did little or nothing,
so biliously under the mark,
made believe work somewhat
upon Guano drawing —
Afternoon charcoaled a
sketch, "Old Spruce Trees
by Twilight" — Every went
to the Private View, got
there about 10 o'clk —
Strutted up the great Stair-
way with all the assurance
of "conscious loveliness, but
felt" less so" when I discovered
that I had forgotten to Roll
down my pantaloons, that
had been turned up on
account of the muddy walking.
Met Geo. and Mary there and
Returned home at about 11 o'clk.
Hy Foster & Fred. Warner
called at the house this eve[ning].

## Right Page

### Tuesday, April 17, 1866

Pleasant day — overcast, but
not cold. Myself went down to
Mr. Ball's to get him to call.
Father is quite used up. Cold,
Dyspepsia & lots of things.
Feel still out of sorts my-
self & did but little on the
guano drawing. Every
at home — Aunt Agnes
took dinner with us.
