## Left Page

### Tuesday, July 17, 1866

Up at 5.30 again — very warm. Ther.
76° — At 6 o'elk Geo. Hawes & Ned D—
Came along & we went down to the
lake to bathe — Took a swimming-
lesson & for the first time was able
to swim any — Swimming a few yards
at a time — much delighted — At
8.30 went to work on Elms, 14×24)
(5th mf.) & again worked over the sky
& got in the left distant grove &
trees & meadows — Suffered severely
from heat. Before noon a thunder
storm just missed me — Afternoon,
thunderstorms all around & high
wind — So used up by the heat that
I did nothing but make one little
outline sketch — During the p.m
discovered that I am really gray —
not a few gray hairs, but too many
to count. Shortly after 6 o'clk
a fine wind, Rain & thunder storm
came up, going off into a soft
Rain storm of a couple of hours.
Spent the evng at home writing.
Addie sketched in our usual place &
this p.m. with Emma, but did not meet them

## Right Page

### Wednesday, July 18, 1866

Pleasant mf. and cooler —
Spent an hour or two with Smith's folks
in the Barnyard trying to get studies,
but had to give it up — I killed a lamb
and I secured its head, for study
purposes & commenced a pencil drawing
from it — At about 10, went down to meadow
& worked up on 14×24 (6th mf.) painting in
the foreground grass, up to the middle
fence — disheartening. Afternoon
again threatened with thunderstorms —
Grand sight — one moving north from W. to E.
— one south, moving N.E. — both sweeping
on magnificently & meeting a little N.E. of
town — the turning, seemed to move in a circle
to W. returning upon us, a perfect tornado
of wind & Rain a little after 4 o'clk. All
over in about 1½ hours. Tea at 6 o'clk. Mr. & Mrs.
I. going to see — Geo. walked in to town to see the
Kimball's, who arr. this mf. I stopped at the
D's, played croquet in the wet grass, waited
for tea, as they all promised to go with me to
the K's — After tea, more Rain — So Oscar I—
and I went on & met Geo. in the K's parlor —
Staid until 9.30 when M. S. called for us —
we rode & drove home, through a steady Rain
distance, about 1½ miles — [torn/illegible]
