## Left Page

### SUNDAY, AUGUST 26, 1866.

Up before Sunrise — Heavy fog —
Got basket & plate with 3lbs of butter
went over to the D[illegible] — Nobody up — Enjoyed
Sunrise — fog — fog-bow — fog effects
very much — The mystery of the fog
making it like walking in a new Country.
Went to Church (Cong.) & heard Dr. Taxton
of the 1st Presb[yteria]n. Ch. N.Y. (12th St & 5th Ave.) Text
Matt 27 cl. 22 v. "What shall I do with
. . . Was very much pleased
indeed with the discourse. Very able &
eloquent — Aside from the tone of the
thing, the form was excellent — His handling
of language surprised me. P.M. writing
home — Eve[nin]g at 7.30 Geo. & I walked
into town with our letters — Beyond
the P.O. met Geo. Hawes, Addie & Emma
Returning home from eve[nin]g meeting. Robbed
Geo. H — of Addie & went with them. Took
tea with Addie & Emma, at near 9 o'clk P.M.
— then bid Mr. Mrs. Ireland & Emma
Good-bye — they start for Stockbridge
to-morrow m[ornin]g. Expect to stay there
until Mr[illegible], then visit in N.Y. State. Glorious
moonlight night — Perfectly cloudless
if possible more beautiful than last night.

## Right Page

### MONDAY, AUGUST 27, 1866.

Clear, bright m[ornin]g, very warm in the sun
but the air stirring, quite cool, like late Sep[tember].
Worked all the m[ornin]g (17½) on Elms 14×24.
Painted the chestnut on extreme right hand —
will now leave it for some days & then hope
to finish it in a single sitting. Between 10
& 11, Rosalie Goodman & Miss Cheney came
to my sketching place & invited Geo. & me
to Croquet & tea to-morrow eve[nin]g. Very
pleasant call. Afternoon, worked
until after 4 o'clk (3? p.m.) on old
hemlocks, then went to Mr. Tappen's
& met my class. I urged strongly
that if we expected success in
sketching from nature, we must
not expect to sit in arm chairs, nor
tables & such like conveniences
upon a pleasant piazza — we must
go out & find subjects — The ladies
could hardly be persuaded, but
agreed to go a few yards from the
house, sit under a pine tree & sketch
a hickory. But little accomplished
3rd lesson. Eve[nin]g Rec[eive]d letters
from Mary & Fan Milford. In
doors doing most nothing.
