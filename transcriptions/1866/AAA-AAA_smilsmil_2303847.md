## Left Page

### Thursday, December 6, 1866

Don't know what sort of a day —
All day Rolling in a perfect agony
in my big chair — Pains in my
head, my mouth — my body —
all over — Dr. Throop came at
about 10.30 & left Remedies. —
Mother & Mary brought down a small
iron bedstead & fixed it up in my
Room — About 5 o'clk p.m. I
managed to crawl from my
Chair to it — at about 6.30 went
quietly to sleep, with 3 exceptions
when waked to take medicine, slept
until 1.30 a.m. Then awoke quite
clear & almost without pain. — fever
almost gone. Still kept wet cloths
on my head — got up at 4 o'clk &
sat in my Chair for 3/4 of an hour —
then sleepy again, went to bed and
slept until 7. a.m.

## Right Page

### Friday, December 7, 1866

Awoke feeling pretty well — pain
still in my head — after a very
light and sloppy breakfast, got
up & undressed myself — washed
myself — combed & brushed my
hair, got on a clean shirt & collar —
went to bed like a Christian
where I stayed patiently all day
& night — Dr J — called after 11 o'clk a.m.
Day glorious & Spring like —
Sun shining full up on my windows
Room altogether too warm — cold
in the throat in the evening — inter-
fered with my sleep & rest during
the night. Made best meal of
fine cut Chicken with mashed potato
& toast soaked in gravy — almost
too rich.
