## Left Page

### Tuesday, April 24, 1866

Cool windy day — went down town in the mng stopping to see pictures — at about 12.30 commenced packing up Father's things at No 1 Wall St — pertaining to the Bierstadt plate, prior to their Removal to No 20 W. 4th St — got all things Ready & started for the Studio before 3 o'clk.

For an hour or so, during the p.m, worked on a copper plate of R. A. Gifford's, giving him some practical lessons in free-hand etching.

Every at home — Uncle Frank, Aunt Jessie & Emma arrived at the house to-day to stay until they start for Canada.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, April 25, 1866

Delightful, fresh & spring-like day — all day hard at work upon Gifford's etching, biting, dry-pointing etc, etc — G seems to be quite pleased at my success — also finished etching an old subject — "The Myst", that I commenced Dec. 1860 —

Every at home.
