## Left Page

### Thursday, August 16, 1866.

Cold, windy & cloudy mt. Ther. 50° — at 7 o'clk. Soon cleared cloudless & blew hard & cool all day long — went with the men to the Hay field & Run the Mowing machine until 10 o'clk. Then took pencils & paper & went to Bullard's woods to finish old Hemlocks. Found Addie under the Elms jumping up & down to keep warm — proposed a Ramble — took it — got warm — every thing jolly — Geo. overtook us — Ret'd home a little after 12. Between 2 & 3 o'clk Miss Apthorpe & Addie, on their way down to see Emma Ireland stopped to see our Studies & Sketches. At 4.30 I went to Mr. Tappen's & met my class Misses Tappen (2), Carey & Hooper. Sat up on Southern Piazza & sketched away at apple trees, Mtn, Mt. etc. etc. until 6.30. Every. walking with Geo. Beautiful new moon — effects over the lake most charming — walked the Piazza until near 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Friday, August 17, 1866.

Very cold mt., Cloudless windy day like late Sept. About 8 o'clk took the Colt & went over to the Dr's to see if Addie would go with us to Rattlesnake Mt. — she could not — went on to town & took Colt to blk. Smith's & had his fore feet shod — first shoes — behaved very well — Rather lively at one time. home again & after 10 went down to old Hemlocks & finished them. P.M. worked a little on mt. study, in-doors & at 3 o'clk feeling unlike more work — day very windy but glorious — cool & cloudless, & again took the Colt & started out upon a Saunter — went up on the Stockbridge Road 2 or more miles, then turned to the left upon a lee Road for a mile or two, then again to the left, back of Rattlesnake Mt. then home & was perfectly delightful. Every. walked across lots to Mr. Goodman's — found him Miss Rosalie & Miss Cheny at home — spent a pleasant Eve. until 9.30 o'clk. Heard that Emma Ireland is at Dr. D's. Addie told me that she was expected & that she had invited the Loop girls & Ehminger there to tea.
