## Left Page

### Tuesday, September 25, 1866

In spite of Prognostications, weather still fine — Mr. Tracy Harris arr. last night about 7 o'clk from N.Y. by way of the Bend — Mr. Coles & Geo. against Mr Harris & me. Played Croquet a good part of m'g — only two games — our side won both — Afternoon spent lounging around, part of the time planning improvements upon house with Aunt H. with a view to getting a Studio added — various little walks during the day. Evening, all but Mary, unwell with a cold, went to the Court House to hear Josh Billings lecture — Josh was wise, foolishly witty & ridiculous — the Room was comfortably full, but the audience soon bubbled up & Ran over — Altho' all that he said I had Read in his book — the laughing was infectious and I enjoyed myself very much.
Clouded over & moonlight slipped out.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, September 26, 1866

Rain at last — early in the m'g, but held up from 9 until about 3, when it took a "fresh bolt," making very Raw bleak weather. M'g, went to Carpenters & got stretcher fixed for 10½ × 18 canvas that I intend painting for Mr. Winans. A short walk with Sarah before dinner. After dinner, fixed for horse back ride, but the Rain caught me — Afternoon spent jolies autty in doors — Geo. took tea at the Mulford's — After tea, went down there awhile — Later in the eve'g, Pete. home & found Joe. Williams, Dan Sayte and Ben Richards bidding Mary good-bye.
