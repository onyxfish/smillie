## Left Page

### Saturday, September 29, 1866

Rain was prognosticated for to-day, but it did'nt come — a mild, quiet, overcast mild, sad sort of Sep'. day — but delicious in its mildness & sadness. After [illegible] one game of croquet with Mr. Harris. then devoted the rest of the m'f to preparing plans for Aunt H's proposed addition to the house. While at dinner Ben M. Fan & Maud, stopped for me to go out to the farm with them — promised to join them on horse back — ¼ before 3. Mr. & Mrs. Harris — Gel. & Sarah drove down the Wyalusing Road to the Falls — half an hour after, on horseback I started for Mulfords farm where I found the girls in the orchard. — They in the buggy, I on horseback — we went on, out on the Summerville Road, crossed through the woods back to the New Milford Road & then home, I stopping in some woods about a mile or more from town — home at 5.40. — Rain threatenings dispersing — Luminous sunset — a short ride with Miss Julia Riley — after tea, every body very stupid & sleepy — went down for the mail at 9 went in to Fan's — more sleepy — Ellen to & Matt. Many home safe — to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Sunday, September 30, 1866

Glorious morning — clear & bracing, yet mild. Morning went to the door of the Presb'n Ch. with Sarah, as escort, then went to the Baptist Ch. heard their pastor, Mr. Moore, explain the doctrine of "Justification by the Grace which is in Redemption." Dinner at 2 and at 3.30 Geo. Sarah & I for a walk went out to Maple Grove back of Drinker's then to the Wyalusing Road, across to the Meshoppen Road & then up, back of Mr. Gel. Benting's & so home. — Eve'g went with Sarah to Baptist Ch. heard from Mr. Moore a sermon to very young people — we were advised not to play marbles for marbles, nor to pitch quoits, as it seemed to excite a sort of thirst — etc — etc — After every service wrote a home-letter to Father.
