## Left Page

### Tuesday, December 18, 1866.

Beautiful clear day. Woke at about 9 a.m. feeling quite myself after a comparatively comfortable nights Rest. Had bkfst in bed at 10 o'clk. Up at about 11. The Rest of the day & every in my big chair. Annie Ballard gave me a call about 2 o'clk. Dr. [illegible] came in while she was here. Expressed himself satisfied with the progress. Have been able to Read considerable nonsense to-day in the shape of stories. Joe sent me in a glass of Currant Jelly. Mary made up for me a lot of wine jelly. I have Oranges & figs & wine that the Dr. tells me I may use sparingly. So I hope that the pleasant stage of convalescing is about setting in.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, December 19, 1866.

Another clear day. — Disturbed considerably during the night by the cold in my head and difficulty in breathing. Felt tolerably well during the day & for a time interested myself sorting my file of Ill. London News — find myself very weak. Dr. T. called a little before 2 o'clk. P.M. & thinks that I am improving slowly but I am hardly conscious of it. Every. Mr. Morrell called again & saw most of the family. I went to bed a little after 11.
