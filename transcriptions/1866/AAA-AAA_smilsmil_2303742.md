## Left Page

### Monday, April 30, 1866

Fine day, quite mild and no fire in the studio. Head quite came and light-like from yesterday's work — So did but little on "Parliament Buildings — Ottawa (1st day) for Uncle Willie.

Father is better to-day — had his plate & traps fixed up in his room at home — but was unable to do any work, of course.

I spent part of the even in at Will's.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, May 1, 1866

Spitting, Sputtering Rain until about noon, when it set in steadily — Quiet even Rain during the night.

Still out of trim for work & fooled, 2d. day, over Parliament Bldgs.

Every at home + in at Will's.

Mr. Earle, of Phila was at the Studio to-day, and bought my "Wood bit on the Hudson" 10×14 in — for $50.00. + Commissioned another 9×15 — He also gave two commissions to George.
