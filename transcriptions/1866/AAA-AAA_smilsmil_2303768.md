## Left Page

### Wednesday, June 27, 1866

The weather apparently not yet
tired of its good behaviour — Up
at 6 — bkfs. at 6.40 & Misses D. & I.
and I off on our horses a little
after 7. a.m. Miss D. Rode the off
Roan. Miss I. the ynt. Mare Jenny
& I the high Roan — Mff. dulling
but a slight clouding soon covering
the sun & a fresh breeze blowing, the
Ride was delightful — Went first to
town — took the Road to Richmond —
turned to West Stockbridge — at an old
School house turned off. & by Riding
across lots at 9.30 Reached Bald
Head — Ret. by Road to W. Stockbridge
& home — at 10.30. All of thy afternoon
and evng. devoted to thunderstorms
and Rain. At about 5 o'clk. P.M.
Ned Dunning made his appearance
very unexpectedly from Nyf.

## Right Page

### Thursday, June 28, 1866

Cloudy mild mff. Threatened
with a headache — but it did n't
quite come. Geo. & Miss D. drove
over to Lenox Furnace early after
bkfs. while Miss I. Ned D. & I
played Croquet over which we
got quite excited — Afternoon
again at it — Ned D. with two balls
& Miss I. against Miss D. Geo. & I —
Fortune clinging with unwonted
Constancy to our Side — Ned &
I then played a Champion
game, best two out of three — I
beat once — the other [illegible] games tomorrow
Evng. found a pleasant little
fire in the Library hearth, and
later had music & singing.
