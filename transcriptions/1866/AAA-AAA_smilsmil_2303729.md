## Left Page

### Monday, April 2, 1866.

An april sort of a day —
most of the time overcast,
at times, sprinkling — In
no humor for work — In the
mrng, Miss Dunning came
& finished her drawing —
In the afternoon, Gel. &
I took our brooms & set to work.
The Studio got a regular over-
hauling. Went home, took a bath
& scrub & ate a good beef-steak
of Mother's cooking — This
being our first day again to-
gether, all is confusion — no
servants — no food — no "nothing."
Spent the eveng at Mr.
Thom. Fessenden's. no. 82
W 48th St. with Tom, his wife,
her mother (Mrs. Apgar) and
S.C.F. Left for home at
20 m past 10 o'clk — Bright
moonlight.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, April 3, 1866.

Glorious Spring day. All
day dusting, washing up,
picking up & sorting over things
at Studio — Towards night
things began to look quite or-
derly & cleanly. Mary
and Matt arrived at
about 5 o'clk P.M. from Poughkeepsie.
Eveng spent with much
Racket at home.
