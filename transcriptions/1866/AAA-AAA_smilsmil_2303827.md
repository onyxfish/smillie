## Left Page

### Tuesday, October 23, 1866

A Real glorious, October morning — Cloudless, clear & crisp. Before bkfst. Char coaled a subject upon panel 10×14 in. — Distant hills, bright, Autumn color'd foreground trees — Same theme very much as the 10½×18 that I painted yesterday. Walked after bkfst and before 11 o'clk got started on painting — by noon (rather, dinner) had sky and distance painted in.

After dinner, at 2.30 went down town, got Tarbell's & Week's horses & Sarah & I went out horseback. The sky all heavily overcast & cold wind blowing — went around lake, over the Babcock Road down to the old Watson's home & then back by road by Jessup's farm. Beautiful Sunset — Rode from 3 to 5.15 o'clk. Even'g with Sarah & Gil to Fan Mulford's. She arr'd from Elerina this p.m. — Rec'd by, ran some chestnuts from Maud Diren. Spent the ev'g popping them — Rec'd home at 10 — a short moonlight walk with Sarah then to bed —

## Right Page

### Wednesday, October 24, 1866

Cold, bleak, windy novemberish day — N'th, finished note to Addee Dunning. Took a walk out to the lake & back — at 11 o'clk commenced painting (2nd day) upon Autumn subject, 10×14 in. Considerably interfered with by getting interested in one of Miss Braddon's trashy novels, "Lady Audley's Secret" — In painting, got in foreground & all except prominent tree. — Afternoon at 5 o'clk went with Aunt H. & Sarah to call on Mr. Miles — not in — on Fanny Drinker — not in. — Sarah & I then went on a walk — called up on D. & Mrs Dimock — saw both — Pleasant call of about half an hour. — home a little after 6. Even'g spent finishing up the novel. — got through by a little after midnight. — Very angry at myself for wasting precious bed time & eyes sight upon such work — promise myself that I will not do it again, for a good while, at least. A letter from Mary, — complains of house upside down — Carpenters — plumbers, plasterers, etc.
