## Left Page

### Sunday, April 8, 1866.

Miserable snow & sleet —
Mrs went to Dr. J. P. S's
heard a discourse upon
the church as a family
institution. — Afternoon
wrote to Fanny Mulford.
Even cleared off — again
went to J. P. S's & heard
something concerning marrying
and dying in heaven.

## Right Page

### Monday, April 9, 1866.

Glorious day — Painted all
day (1st day) on half-circle top.
7×10 in. for Mr. Lewis B. Reed Jun.
my second attempt to please
him & his wife — This time I
think that I will "fetch them"
as I have used an abundance
of blue, red & yellow — It is
more fully charged with color
perhaps than anything that
I have yet done — but not un-
pleasantly or trickily so, I
think. I am now getting so
much better acquainted with
my brushes & material that I
work with much more boldness
& freedom. Spent most of
the eveng at Mr. Tracy Harris's
No 46 W 46th St. with Mr. H —
Mrs. Wyckoff & a gentleman
& his wife — Strangers to me.
