## Left Page

### Saturday, July 21, 1866.

Chilly, cloudy day, very pleasant
for riding or light exercise, but
too cold, to sit out in the fields and
paint — my idea. Myf. walked
over to the Dr.'s with the basket in which
came our green peas yesterday —
was tempted by Croquet to tarry —
Played one game with Emma.
Did no sketching, but wandered about
during the Remainder of the morning
looking up material — Afternoon
as it was clear for an hour or two
went down to Elm Study 12×18 in
(6th day) — Sun soon under clouds again —
painted in small middle distant elms.
Find it decidedly pleasanter painting in shade than in sunlight. About
4 o'clk had an aggravating glimpse
of "our party" going off to enjoy themselves.
The Kimballs, Irelands & Dunnings
going somewhere — don't know where —
Geo & I, walking after tea, met
them Returning. Seemed to be
happy — and why not? Got in
from our walk a little before 9 o'c.
Remainder of the eve. at home.

## Right Page

### Sunday, July 22, 1866.

Beautiful mrf and rather cool, so
thought I would walk in to church
rather than ride — The walk was delightful, but rather too warm — Met the K's
walked to the Episcopal Ch. with them —
went to Cong. Ch. there met the Dr. & party —
Sat with Ned & heard a very good sermon
from Mr. Smith — "Buy the Truth & sell it not."
After service, called for half an hour upon
the K's — at one, took what was called a lunch
at Mr. Sears — viz — one piece of ginger bread.
Went to afternoon service at 1.30 — found
it heavy, & confess to having forgotten the
text — Dinner at home at 4 o'clk —
wrote a little while then wandered
off with Geo. Evg. killed in aimless, purposeless talk with Mr. Gibson.
Weather decidedly threatening
for the morrow.
