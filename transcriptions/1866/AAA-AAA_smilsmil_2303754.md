## Left Page

### Monday, May 28, 1866.

Morning close & almost sultry.
afternoon blustering & rather
chilly — Worked about
half an hour on "Denver City"
& then a little after 10 o'clk
yielded myself to Augero, who
worked almost without inter-
-mission until 3 o'clk — by
which time he had painted
a very good likeness of me.
Size of canvas 12×14 in —
Then went down town to Am.
Bnt Note Co — with "Denver City."
Called at Menger's. His man
backs out of the two little commissions
7×12 that he gave Jno. & I —
— don't know what will come of it —
Went to Pats's & got p/s. of some
little free-hand etchings by
Colman —

Every at home.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, May 29, 1866.

Cold and chilly — Rain
commenced about noon and
continued until about 7 o'clk P.M.
Felt very unlike work — oiled out
& glazed upon Mr. Harris's pic.
worked somewhat over the sky.
Mr. Wm. E. Dodge Junr. and his
bro Chas. called — Mr. D. expressed
himself much pleased with the
little painting for him, & gave me
his check $57.00 — $40 for the
picture & $17.00 for the frame.
Then Mr. LutherOuntee called
& paid me $75.00 for my "Denver
City" drawing — at about 4 o'clk
getting out of patience with myself,
I took a 9×12 in. canvas and
slapped in a Sunset effect, to
accustom myself if possible to
the free use of positive color —
my tendency being to monotones
and grays —

Evening at home, except a
few minutes at D[illegible]'s.
