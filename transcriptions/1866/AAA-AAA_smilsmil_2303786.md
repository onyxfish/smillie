## Left Page

### Thursday, August 2, 1866.

Very unpromising mrg., at 5 o'clk.
still Raining — wind S.W. soft, warm and
wet — but by 10 a.m. it was clear & windy.
Clouds glorious all day long — Most
of the mrg. in bed nursing a head-
ache — & until 3 o'clk, lying around.

Then dressed & at 4.30 presented
myself at Mrs. Tappan's — Soon
astonished at quite a stir. Two
pony phaetons were driven up, and
two brace of young ladies, charming
in their light muslins & silks were
added to the little bevy already
gathered. Eight young ladies with
Mr. J— started under my escort
to find a suitable sketch for the
afternoon. I selected a groupe
of Chestnuts but a few rods from
the house, in the shade of which
we arranged ourselves up on the
grass & then set to work — Some
at the tree trunks, & some at a gate
& so we worked until 6 — when a
heavy & blt. windstorm came up,
light rain in the west. Gel. went with
mr P. beyond Curtisville. I walked the [illegible]

## Right Page

### Friday, August 3, 1866.

Cold, blustering & blowing: worked in
doors upon sketches in my sketch-book
until after 9 o'clk — then went down to
the Hemlock trunk on Dr. D's place that
I nearly finished, June 26" — Finished
it, & a little before 11, went up to the house
& just met Addie, Emma & Geo. Haws
coming to find us & have a little Ramble.
I Returned with them & on the top of Smiths
met Geo., we went into Bullard's woods —
Addie sat down & commenced a sketch
of some old Hemlocks — the Rest of us went
on to a thick growth of small pines, & gen-
erally through the woods — Ret'd to Addie
& a little after 12, after a pleasant hour,
separated — Emma went this p.m. to Mr.
Butler's, near Stockbridge, for 2 or 3 weeks.
P.m. outlined old Hemlocks in Bullards
woods — & at 4.30 went to the Dr's. — met Addie
She was just going into town with Geo. Haws.
I finished trimming Elm tree — Removed
dead wood, ladder, etc. etc. & left a little
after 6. Welf., Geo. & I walked until
after 8 o'clk out on the W. Stockbridge Road,
& at 9.30, went to bed very tired —
