## Left Page

### Saturday, September 1, 1866

Muggy, warm morning, with quite a strong breeze from S.W. Worked (2nd mf.) on 11×14, Study of pine trunks getting over another quarter fit, am Rather better than yesterday I think — After 11 o'clk. Rosalie Goodman came to where I was sketching, bring a box upon which I have promised to draw some vines and flowers. Had a pleasant call of about half an hour. Afternoon worked upon oat field study (4th P.M.) painting in pines upon L.H. side. Had a sharp shower of about 15 minutes duration at about 3 o'clk — gathered myself & traps under my umbrella — Have worked to-day under great disadvantages, clouded & muggy, with occasional short bursts of sunshine — On Returning to the house at 6 o'clk, found Rosalie G. & Miss Warner — stopped to see our studies — had another pleasant call — as in the M. — Geo. sharing it with me. Every[one] walked the piazza until 10 o'clk. Very strong, wet South wind — slight shower — bright lightning & thunder storm passing to west & north of us.

## Right Page

### Sunday, September 2, 1866

Rainy morning — at 6.30 a.m. went to the P.O. to see if there were any letters for us — nobody up — waited awhile — saw one of the servants & got from her two letters & a paper — Father sends us $50.00. Did not go to church, but stayed at home, mainly to write letters — wrote to Father, Marg., Aunt Agnes & a little note to Meta — a visit — Afternoon it cleared off charmingly & at about 6 o'clk. went down to the pond & gathered a lot of water lilies —

Evef[ning], tramping the Road with George, and then the piazza.
