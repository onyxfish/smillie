## Left Page

### THURSDAY, SEPTEMBER 27, 1866.

Up at 5.30, glorious sunrise. Man got off in the Stage for Montrose Station and N.Y. at a little after 6 A.M. going in Company with Col. Coles, his mother & Miss Boyd, intending to stop over night at the Water Gap. Morning very warm & without a cloud. At a little 9, I got Tarbell's rel. horse under the saddle & Mr. Harris got a team from Goodwin's with driver Mr. H & S.C.F. we met off Riding on Silver Lake Road about 3 miles then back through the Valley to Wyoming Road. Geo. went off Riding with Fan M. & Amanda D. Dinner & then more Croquet — then putting a Room to Rights, changing bedsteads & transferring things from Mr. D. Lakosta's. Afternoon, with Fan M. & Maud D. & Geo. Called upon Mrs. Mr. Cooper & Fanny Jessup. Went to tea with Fan M. & spent the eve'g until 9 o'clk — R'D home & for about an hour Read Baker's "Sources of the Nile", then to bed, sleeping for the first night at home. Quite an excitement about 1 o'clk — Fire started in Sayre's Grist Mill — Soon put out, with small damage —

## Right Page

### FRIDAY, SEPTEMBER 28, 1866.

Another glorious day. Enjoyed my walk before breakfast much, the air still, frosty & cloudless. After bkfst. a game of Croquet & at 10 I got my horse & saddle & rode out to Jas. Carmalt's — not at home, but saw Mrs. C. & with her went about & looked at his famous bull "Duke" — the cows, horses, etc., etc., & then spent half an hour in the house looking at photo's & chatting. Home at 12.30 — after dinner, more croquet & loung-ing & at 4.30 a walk up to Warren's tot. Returning (Mr. Harris, Geo. S.C.F. &c.) at 5.30. Between 7 & 8 o'clk P.M. went down town for the mails & spent an hour or more at Fan Munford's. Mr. & Mrs. Mr. Jessup & Fanny R-mon calling — considerable movement — elegant, to the wise — over the back of the sofa, for instance — small chairs, stools & sofa pillows very frisky — Amanda D. — bad headache, obliged to lie on the sofa — me too — etc. etc. Home at 9.30 & soon to bed.
