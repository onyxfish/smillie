## Left Page

### Wednesday, September 19, 1866

Muggy, sticky & close, mix. Rain and sunshine all day. Heavy rain in the evef. Spent the day quietly finishing packing, clearing up, & reading. Ther. 79°. wonder where the strength has gone to that I enjoyed last week. In-doors until after 4 o'clk, then went out & finished up a few remaining messages. Calling into Geo. at the Ireland's. Saw W. & Mrs. Spring. The rest of the family expected to-night. Evef. Mr. Harris sent word that he will not be able to go with us to-morrow. Vexed that I did not start this mf. Geo. & I went again to the Ireland's. All home but Mma. — met there Ned Dunning. — Home early. Mr. main [illegible] from West Point and with us all night. To bed early.

## Right Page

### Thursday, September 20, 1866

Up at 5.30 — overcast & rainie. At 9 o'clk started on Jersey Central R.R. for Montrose. at 11.45 Reached Junction in 10 m. train on D.L. & W. R.R. got off — Delaware Station at 12.50 — 15 m. for dinner — Rain at Water Gap — miserable all the way to top of Poconos Mt. — one bank of fog at the Summit — of course nothing to be seen — descending the Mt. many fine suggestions among the old pines dead & alive — swamps, bogs, trailing moss, scarlet vines, breathing mist, spectral trees etc etc. — Reached Scranton at about 4.30. — More heavy rain before getting to Montrose Station at 6.30 — Nine grown persons & two children in the stage — interminable ride from depot to town — got in cold, tired till nattered about 9 o'clk P.M. — Expected at Uncle [illegible]'s. P.L.T. arr. yesterday evef. about 11 o'clk. Went over the way to Mr. Dad. Lathrop's to bed.
