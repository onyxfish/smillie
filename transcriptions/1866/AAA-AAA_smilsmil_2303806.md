## Left Page

### Tuesday, September 11, 1866

Morning clear, but clouded over before 8 o'clk — Worked in doors until 9 o'clk upon Pine Study 11×15. Then took Elm Study 12×18, trout out — Worked all the m'f — by noon it began to Rain & poured down steadily all the afternoon & very. P.M. Continued working in-doors, upon Elms — working over the entire subject, ground & all — This is a great way to finish studies from Nature, but I plead with myself, stress of weather —
Mr & Mrs Smith off black berry all day — Mrs & Geo off with Addie to the Aspinwall place.
Eveg Geo & I quite comfortable working over our drawings.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, September 12, 1866

Morning very threatening but suddenly about 9 o'clk it blew off very windy & quite broken up. All the m'f worked upon large chestnut in oatfield study. In the afternoon went out with it — Staked umbrella, tied down easle & strapped on study but it was of little use. After an hour & a half I gave it up & came in-doors & continued my work. Worked over it generally so as to leave it, making the day upon it.
Eveg at about 9 o'clk, George and I walked to the Goodwin's there found, in addition to the family, Mr. Jno. Cheney & niece the third Miss Cheney that I have met. Spent a very pleasant eveg, in part discussing dyspepsia with Mr. G — Home to bed after a 4 mile walk (2 m. each way) at 10.45 o'clk.
