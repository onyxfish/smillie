## Left Page

### Wednesday, January 10, 1866

Glorious winter day, quite mild by comparison with the days that have gone before. Painted all day on view on the Hudson (1st day) 8×10 in. Round top, for Mr. Lewis B. Reed Jr. So far it pleases me better that anything that I have yet done. I fancy that it shows more knowledge of color & nature. Breevort and Miss Dunning took lunch at the Studio with us. Spent the eveng very pleasantly at Mr. Ireland's — No. 50 E. 25th St. with Miss Emma T Mr. & Mrs Preston Spring. — Later in the eveg, with Mr. & Mrs. Ireland and Mr. Oscar J. —

## Right Page

### Thursday, January 11, 1866

Clear, mild day — Painted in (1st day) "a Recollection of Hill Side, Hyde Park" 6½×9 in. Because Jas. Hart does it, tried Cadmium in sky, making a new key. I cannot work it to my satisfaction. Cadmium with me, makes a blue leadeny — with J. H. it does not. It is one of the mysteries of Relative color. I got the whole subject painted in with tolerable care & success. The Composition, if it can claim to be so much, is very faulty. Miss Pardee, her cousin & a gentleman called in the p.m. also Mr. Copcutt and Miss [illegible]. In the eveg on reaching home, I found a letter from Robt. Hinshelwood, so vulgar and abusive & yet so childish in orthography, writing & composition as to make it quite absurd. I fear for the poor man's wits. Evening at home.
