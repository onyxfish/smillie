## Left Page

### Friday, April 20, 1866

If yesterday was a weak-ender — to-day is a perfect killer — The ther. has reached, in our Studio, to day, with windows, door & ventilator all open, & no fire — 81°. Finished Guans drawing, about 4 o'clk — Miss Fanny Payson & sister Mrs. Gillman & Misses Julia & Betsy Riley called at the Studio — had a pleasant call of about an hour —

Late in the p.m. went over to the N.A. of D. — Met there Miss Leila & Sarah Martyn — Every at home — Father still under the weather — we have had quite a scare about the breaking out of dark spots upon his back — As yet Dr. Ball seems hardly to know what to make of it —

## Right Page

### Saturday, April 21, 1866

Very Sultry — Ther. again above 80°. Mrs. called upon Mrs. Choate to say that I could not be at Studio during the Mrs. She arranged to come next Wk & gave me a check for $50.00 — Katy g. took her 18th lesson. Went to Phil. Rehearsal at 10 o'clk — Met S. L. F. & Mary Butler — walked over to 14th St. & 8th Ave. with them. Returned to Studio, spent the afternoon idly. Went home at 4 o'elk & enjoyed a bath — Storm came up at about 5 o'clk with thunder & lightning. With Geo. Mary & Matt, went in the even'g to the 5th & last Phil. concert of the season. Uncomfortably warm — but the Beethoven 7th Symphony, grand —

Father is still complaining very much of neuralgic pains in his head & is confined to bed —
