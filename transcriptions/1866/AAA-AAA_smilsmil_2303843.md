## Left Page

### Saturday, November 24, 1866.

Pleasant day — at the Studio at 9.30 — Got another unfinished Study of last summer — The Pain of Smith's flock and worked the most of the day finishing it in Charcoal & crayon — Altho' out of drawing for I could not get the beast to stand a moment, I think it valuable to me and effective — Afternoon Charcoaled up on "Noon" 14×18 for M. Harris, drawing in the Sheep. Every hard at work again in my Room hanging my pictures — Got through about 11 o'clk and went to bed with my throat tied up in a wet towel, as it annoyed me much with heat & soreness — It has troubled me for several days past.

## Right Page

### Sunday, November 25, 1866.

Clear bracing cold day — Real winter & quite windy — Feeling used up, Rather, did not get up until near 9 o'clk — Kindled a fire in my Room & invited the family to join me. Invitation accepted — Did not go to Church as I considered myself an invalid. Charl went to Uncle A's after m[orning] service & [returned] at 7.45 P.M. — Between 4 & 5 o'clk P.m. I went out & took a walk — Evening spent with Mother between reading a little & writing a little — wrote two pages to Jan Mulford that I enclosed in George's letter to her.

During last night a flurry of snow, this m[ornin]g early a white spread was over everything. By noon no traces of it could be found.
