## Left Page

### Friday, June 15, 1866.

Very hot clear day. Ther
84° in the Studio.
Commenced upon "Beaver
Drawing" for Uncle Willie
& worked all day like a
good fellow, finishing it
at about 6 o'clk — I
think it somewhat bold,
free & tolerably successful.
Mrs. Greatorex paid me
the $40 - for my little
"Wood bit", that a few
days ago she asked &
for until next Sept. To-day
she says that she prefers
to pay at once. —
Evening at home.
Took home my portrait by
Augers which seems to
give unusual & almost
unexpected satisfaction.

## Right Page

### Saturday, June 16, 1866.

Another day, off the same piece
as yesterday. Commenced
drawing Ohio State Arms for P.B.N.
Co. — having to introduce figures
(supporters to the shields) I got
camera lucida outlines from
Gel — Fairman & Augers — did
not work quite so vigorously
as yesterday. & so, only got
my subject partly pencilled
in — Every Gel Many and
I went to call on Mr. Benj.
T. Jessup. No 28 E. 39th St.
We found Mrs. J. slightly indisposed
to saw her in her Room. Mr. J.
showed us over his great new
house with great satisfaction
& Nette looked more charming
than ever, having just Retd
from a days Ramble in the
Country.
