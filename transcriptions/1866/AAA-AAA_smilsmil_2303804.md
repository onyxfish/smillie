## Left Page

### Friday, September 7, 1866.

Pleasant morning — run down to the elms at 9 o'clk and worked (5° mrf) upon pine tree Study (11×15) getting it all painted in — will work a little upon it in-doors and then call it done. — About 10 o'clk the sky filmed over & by noon it was thickly clouded over & blowing from S. E. After dinner feeling the need of exercise Gel and I started for Rattlesnake Mt. Enjoyed the tramp very much and got a couple of pencil sketches. Got home after 5 o'clk pretty well tired out. Gel especially, as he had been on his feet all the mrf, over to Goodman's & Dunnings, playing with the girls — Wife spent between working on my p.m. sketches & pacing my beat on the piazza. Before we got home it sprinkled, during the mrf it rained & in the night it rained heavily. To bed at 10 o'clk, with the idea that Sketching weather will never come again.

## Right Page

### Saturday, September 8, 1866.

Heavily clouded & Raining in the mrf — warm & close, by 9 o'clk wind changed, and for the rest of the day it blew like a little fury. Clouds broken & flying. Worked all the morning in-doors on large Elm Study, 14×24 (mrf) finishing it at last. I painted extremities to the large trees — took out some of the brown color in parts & finished over the chestnut tree. Afternoon worked upon my pencil studies — especially those that I made upon Rattlesnake yesterday. — At 5. P.M. took the colt and had a ride out upon the W. Stockbridge Mt. Road. Colt rather unruly. On Returning, about 6, found Mr. Goodman & Rosalie just leaving. Gel had been doing the honors, showing our studies, etc, etc. Every Gel & I went to Mr. Bullard's and made a call. Saw Mr. & Mrs. B. & Mr. B's mother, Mrs. Norton, wife of Prof. Norton of Boston. — Spent a very pleasant mrf, altho' Mr. B. seemed disposed to talk nothing but pictures. — Left at 9.30 o'clk — home & read newspaper until near 11 — then to bed.
