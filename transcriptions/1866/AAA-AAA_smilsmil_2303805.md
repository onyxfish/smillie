## Left Page

### Sunday, September 9, 1866

Cold, blustering morning. A little before 9 a.m. started for church. Walked out to Higginsons then toward Lenox, crossing into Mr. Bullard's wood, wandering about in them, then through Dr. Dunnings pine woods out to the Road again. Met Addie at the gate by Sedgwick's wood & walked up to town & church with her. Mr. Smith preached an excellent discourse from Prov. 10.22. Gel. & I, after Mr. Service walked home with Addie & then on, getting caught in a light shower. After lunch, ran down to the pond & gathered Lilies. Was caught again in a heavy shower but had my waterproof, under which I took Refuge in the end of the boat. Took the Lilies to Addie & rec'd many thanks — Ret'd home just in time to get some dinner. Then walked again with Gel. on the West Stockbridge Mt. Road. Eve'g, note to Matt.

## Right Page

### Monday, September 10, 1866

Up before Sunrise — Glorious Mg. At 6.10 was at Dr. Dunnings with my sketching traps & set to work at once with Camera-Lucida upon Sketch from the cor. of Sedgwick's woods — then commenced one looking over the Croquet ground to the pond, — before finishing — invited to breakfast — accepted — good — then finished sketch. Gel. meantime waiting for Camera lucida — he & Mr. Smith bound for Monument Mt. At 9 started with Addie for the Elms — worked all day, Mg. & p.m. on Up Right Elm Study 12×18. (1 day) — At noon almost perished with the cold — white frost last night. P.m. much warmer — after 4, met my class & gave them 9th & last lesson — I working all the time on Miss Hooker's sketch — At 6.20 went to Dr. D's by invitation to take tea. Gel. not there, did not come until after 8 — took tea without him. Gel. Havens & sisters went to Boston Sat. noon. Spent the eve'g working up Miss Hoofer's Sepia Sketch — Started for home at 9.50 tired out.
