## Left Page

### Sunday, December 2, 1866

Most glorious day — In the m[orning] a little twinge of neuralgia, but went to Dr. Hastings church in 42d St. and heard, to me, a very earnest + excellent discourse on "Regeneration — God's share in it and man's share." — After service took a walk up 6th Ave to 52d St. + back — dinner at 1 o'clk. P.M. walked from 3 until 5 o'clk in Central Park and enjoyed it very much. the day was so delightful — air pure, fresh + cool enough to be bracing, but not cold + for the time, I was free of pain. Everg. Mr. Ed[ward] Parker of Poughkeepsie took tea + spent the evng at the house. This young man appears to admire Martha + Martha appears to reciprocate — I earnestly hope that the case will not prove fatal — Went to [Belnay?] Tab[ernacle] + heard Prof. Shedd. "Watch" a good sermon — Walked home with neuralgia + went to bed in real pain — but rested well during the night.

## Right Page

### Monday, December 3, 1866

Another very fine day — feeling quite well — as I expect, with Loop, to draw from life this afternoon. I call it a broken day as I have nothing that for a half day I can work to better advantage upon. I spent until 10 o'clk coloring in water colors a Photo. taken in Montrose of several of the young ladies there, for S.C.F. Rather sorry looking work — but I can't be great at every thing — ahem —
Afternoon, our model did not come — disappointed — but charcoaled a subject upon a 9 × 12 canvas for J. Q. A. Ward. one of his selection, for which he promises me a cast of his "Freedman" — outlined charcoal with Opts Turp — At 4 o'clk neuralgia got hold of me again and until 6 I suffered severely — walked home — found the family in back parlor abusing the paper that has just been put up — feel very bad about it myself — took a dish of wheaten grits + then as my flying devil took possession of me again. I went to bed + stayed there until 10 o'clk — when, quieted I got up + wrote this.
