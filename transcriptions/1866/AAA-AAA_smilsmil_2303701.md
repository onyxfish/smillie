## Left Page

### Thursday, February 1, 1866.

Fine day. Commenced again upon Academy picture (8th day)
Painted in middle distance trees from Righthand end to middle.
Am doing my very best to avoid
colorlessness & blackness —
Result of days work unsatisfactory
— Evng, feeling dumpish
went around to Christy's
Minstrels, 24½ St, next to 5th
Ave. Hotel. Besides songs,
dances & jokes, saw Desdemona
— The Black Prima donna —
& Double Bedded Room —

Found some things to
laugh at, but was not
montrously diverted.

## Right Page

### Friday, February 2, 1866.

Still unusually fine weather.
In desperate mood, started,
(9th day) upon the middle ground
meadows, determined if possible
for me, to get some light & color.
— Covered over considerable
space & succeeded in getting
a fine prevailing tone of orange
over wood & meadow — That
may be color in one sense of
the word, but am not quite satis-
fied that it is an orthodox land-
scape color — Try again tomorrow.

Evng at 9 o'clk met Geo.
by appointment at Mr. Harris Pardee's
No. 10 W. 47 St. Miss P. prepared
for a party — we finished our
call with the "old folks" — Mrs.
D. Wm. Thompsson (Mr. [Post]) Mr.
Paulding, years ago of Kingston,
& others.
