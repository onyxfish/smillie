## Left Page

### Thursday, October 11, 1866.

Mild, but overcast. Slept as usual very late. After Slept croquet with Mr.
Harris + writing a letter to Mary.
Dinner early — went down town, met Fanny M. + Maud D. Returned home with them + then with Sarah
age. + Mr Mrs. Harris, went on up to the Fair Ground. Tried to listen to Elisha Mulford, who delivered the annual address — hard work, gave it up — found some sport in the mule Race. Admired very much Mr. Rachel Mulford's Merino Buck — Stayed about the ground until near 6 o'clk, walked home with Fanny M. then Returned + got Sarah + Salome Watson.
Every passed lazily at home — being rather tired.

## Right Page

### Friday, October 12, 1866.

Miserable Rain and wind —
Returned in Spts. Turp. October afternoon 10¾×18 in. At noon, took a walk down town. After-
noon, charcoaled upon upright panel 10×14. A miserable day out of doors and indoors.
Evening, sat moping about the house, when all went to bed —
& then went down town to Fanny Mulford's + there had a jolly time until after 11 o'clk — played Whist — D., Ben., Jan., + I — drank cider + committed various other excesses. Amanda D. is high spirits.
