## Left Page

### Thursday, March 15, 1866

Foggy dull morning from
which the day hardly recovered.
Got to work late upon the
lower sky of my Acad. pic.
(24 day). I now regret very
much that I have touched
it at all while feeling so much
under the weather. The sky
altogether is in a bad state —
muddy, colorless, sunken in
in parts, & bearing out in others.
Spent the eveng at the Studio
part of the time in Fairman's
Room. Went home very much
disgusted, bought a box of
Podiphyllian Pills, & am bound
to put myself through — Too
much of this thick weather
has got into my head.
Got a notice from Com. of
Jurors to step up and pay
a fine of $100 — as delin-
quent Juror — Hope they
may find it — I can't.

## Right Page

### Friday, March 16, 1866

Hard, pouring-down Rain
all day. Tried a bottle
of Citrate of Magnesia in
the mrng. — Have had
more than my hands full
attending to myself — Feel
better & clearer — al tho' weak.
Did not set my palette at
all, but just loafed about
stretching canvases & touching
drawings — Lewis B. Reed Jr.
called — does not like the
picture that I have painted
for him — So I have determined
to try another — Recd. a note
from S. C. F. & answered it —
Also a long call from Dan
Dayre — Eveng at the
Studio writing —
