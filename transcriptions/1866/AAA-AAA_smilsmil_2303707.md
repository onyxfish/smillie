## Left Page

### Tuesday, February 13, 1866.

Beautiful mng. p.m. clouded over — Worked all day (2½ days) on Wood bit 10×14 in
got it all painted in — not much color — tendency rather to tones & greys — but think that I can finish it creditably — Afternoon Miss Lizzie Putnam, sister, cousin and somebody else called —
Eveng. after dinner called upon Mr. & Mrs. Jas. M. Hart and found them both out, then tried Miss Leila Martin 155 N. 21st St. with equal success, then went to Odell's organ factory 7th Ave. bet 21 & 22d St. where Dr. Grant's organ was being tried — Soon tired with crowd & heat + started for Studio. Passing Christy's Minstrels at about 9 ock I went in & was amused for an hour.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, February 14, 1866.

Pleasant mng. with Rain at noon — at night, a steady pour. All day used up with a headache — the effect of a cause — Cause — oyster pie, administered yesterday eveg. at dinner.

Eveng. so far recovered, that by invitation, I went to N.A. of D. An informal meeting, having reference to American pictures to be sent to the Paris Exposition of 1867. This is the first meeting of artists that I have attended, and if it is a characteristic meeting, as I think it is, then I must say that I think they are a wonderfully unbusiness-like set of men. The meeting was called for 8 o'clk. It was near 9, before any movement was made towards organization. a chairman (Shumway) & two Seclys were appointed — nothing being accomplished, or like to be at 9.30 o'clk — I left —
