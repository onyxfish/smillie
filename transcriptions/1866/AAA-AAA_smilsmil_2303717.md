## Left Page

### Monday, March 5, 1866.

Blustering, windy day —
Worked all day (21st day) on
Acad. pic. on the large oak
tree & small birches in the
middle of the foreground —
Am very anxious to get it now
in a state to leave for a few
days, so that with a fresh eye
and hand I can begin at the
sky and work over the whole
of it. Eve'g. Gel. & I went
to Mr. Ireland's, 55 E. 26th St.
where their singing club had
a sing. Met Miss Florence
Smith & Miss Bellows, daughter of
Rev. W. B. of Sanitary Com. fame.
Also two or three others, not
too numerous to mention, but
I do not know their names.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, March 6, 1866.

A glorious day, but very windy.
Worked (22nd day) on Acad. pic.
and finished my 2nd painting
over it — At present it is a perfect
hodge-podge — I intend to give
it a rest until next Monday
then pitch in again. I
hope but fear — Eve'g at
Studio, wrote a note to Sister
Matt & one to Mr. Chas. Jewett
asking for money from C.B.N.Co.
On reaching home found the
money there waiting for me.
This $80.00 has been owing to
me since last Dec. I
counted it in among my
Receipts for that month,
as it was then earned & I could
have had it if I had then
asked for it.
