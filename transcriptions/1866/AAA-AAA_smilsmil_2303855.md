## Left Page

### Monday, December 24, 1866

Still miserable weather, Father went down-town however.

Dr. Says that I am better, & certainly, in several respects I do show improvement. I trust that it will continue.

After my dinner, feeling sleepy, I lay down upon my bed and slept for nearly two hours — at 4 woke confused & with much pain in head — from 5 to 6, had a splitting nervous headache — tied my head up tight as I could with bandages — Got relief after a cup of tea at 6 o'clk. Evening passed along in the dark.

About 11 o'clk. undressed for the first time since Friday, went to bed & passed a tolerable night.

Geo. & Charl. went up to Uncle Aaron's to spend Christmas at about 8 o'clk. P.M. Uncle A. called here in the mrng. with a statement of money affairs between him and Father. Quite Satisfactory.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, December 25, 1866

Bright & promising in the mrng. by over-cast afternoon — Not a very merry Christmas to me — not so well as I was yesterday and pretty thoroughly discouraged. I am not altogether satisfied with Throop's treatment — & yet have no special confidence in any body else. I am fagged out & just ready to give up the fight — Spent the day like all other days, rolled up in my blanket in my big chair — Father at work all day on a die that he brought home with him yesterday. Matt and Kate in at Will's to dinner — Geo. & Charl. Returned from Uncle Aaron's at about 6. P.M. Gilbert Pettit & wife spent the evng. at the house.
