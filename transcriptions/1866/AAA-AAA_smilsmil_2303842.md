## Left Page

### Thursday, November 22, 1866.

Disagreeable Rainy day —
Detained at home — did not
get to Studio until near 10 o'clk.
Set to work upon drawing for
N. B. note Co. — Missouri State Arms
(1st day) and nearly finished
it after working all day very
faithfully at it — Miss Church
gave us a pleasant call in the m[orn]. She has taken a
Studio cor. B[roadwa]y & 30 St. At
3 o'clk. Jel went home, used
up with a cold. Eve[nin]g,
Schmitts bro had been at the house
& fixed up & put up my large book
case — transferred my books from
Mother's Room & piled them into
the case — worked hard until
near midnight

## Right Page

### Friday, November 23, 1866.

Another dark disagreeable day
did not get to Studio until after
10 o'clk — Worked for a little time
on a drawing of Father, taken with
Camera Lucida by P. Hinshelwood in
1842 — worked up by Father then finished
by me — I am quite proud of it — It
represents Father in shirt sleeves and
high boots, standing by pig pen with pail
in hand — The top of house just beyond —
Recollecting the circumstances attending
taking of sketch, it is to me very interesting —
Finished Drawing of Missouri Arms
before I took lunch — P.M. Spent
finishing in Charcoals & Crayon a
study of Lamb's head that I commenced
in Lenox — About 3 o'clk P.m., my new
Bookcase came home from Schmitts —
Evening at work until after midnight
arranging my books in the two
cases — Think that my Room will
be charming — when finished —
