## Left Page

### Friday, March 23, 1866.

Cloudless mng, overcast by noon + raining by 5 o'clk p.m.
Worked all day (28th day) on Acad.
pic, painting over middle distance
trees + meadows — am painting
the brown of positive green out
+ getting what I consider a
more harmonious + atmospheric
grey — Evng, after sketching
paper for Guano drawing for
A.B.N. Co, at 9 o'clk went
to (W. Despards in 32d St.
to take my part (Malcolm
+ 1st Murderer) in Reading
Macbeth. Misses Van Doren —
Dunning + Parker were there —
Mr. Despard, Greatorner +
Mr. Douglas, Despard + myself —
got through about 11 o'clk
and went right home.

## Right Page

### Saturday, March 24, 1866.

Charming day. Worked (29th day)
on Acad. pic, coming more forward
on middle distant trees + meadows,
— am now nearly over them.
Mrs Choate took her 5th lesson —
Katy G. was too unwell to come.
Miss Dunning came + drew
for an hour or so, took lunch.
Fanny Kimball called — so
did Mrs. Woodman (Lucy
Durand), afternoon went to
Phil. Rehearsal — was delighted
again with Beethoven's 7th
Symphony. Music may not
be a moral agent, but I
always feel that I am a better
man after hearing such music
— Grant White to the contrary
notwithstanding. Evening
at studio, charcoaling Guano
subject for A.B.N. Co. and
washing out dirty brushes.
