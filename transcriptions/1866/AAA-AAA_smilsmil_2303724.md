## Left Page

### Monday, March 19, 1866

Raw, + overcast, — during the middle of the day, some Snow + Rain — Commenced again upon acad. pic. (25. day) For 3 days the sky has been under a coat of fine French chalk, and in the Sun as much as I could get it. To-day cleared off + set to work — feel quite pleased — I think now that I see my way clear — painted all over the sky, a very thin painting. Miss Dunning spent the mng at the Studio drawing and took lunch with us. —
Ever Mrs. Jas M. Hart and Mrs. Jas. R. Brevoort called late in the afternoon. —
Every at the Studio with Father.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, March 20, 1866

Threatening in mng with heavy Rain during p.m. Worked all day (26th day) painting over extreme distance + River and about half way along on opposite bank of River — I work more slowly than I wish, but am now getting along with tolerable success. The distant bank has been painted in but once, (as I go over it now, even though I paint it lighter, I find it hard to avoid marring the transparency + dash of the first painting is not easily retained, (by me at least) in finishing — Every at Studio Reading Macbeth. — Dorman called + stayed until near 10.
Rec'd a letter from T.H. Wood of Utica, enclosing $150.00. ($100 — for Geo — $50 — for one) for 3 little paintings that we sent to the Utica Art Ass. Exhibtn.
