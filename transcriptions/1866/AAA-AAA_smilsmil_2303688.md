## Left Page

### Saturday, January 6, 1866.

Not so cold as yesterday. A fine winter day. Continued "outlining" on the "Hudson" (4th day)
but did not get it quite finished. Mr. & Mrs. Brevoort took lunch
with us at the Studio. We had a splendid loaf of Brown Bread
Home made, that miss Dunning sent up to us yesterday, good
tea, milk & sugar. Apples, Roasted or baked and Prunes
stewed by ourselves. In the matter of these little dishes,
I am becoming very proficiens. Katy Greatorex took her
5th lesson in drawing, from me. In the every., after
dinner, went down to Mr. Dunnings.
Miss D. was just going to her little singing circle — Chatted
with her but a few minutes —
after she left, stayed with her father 15 or 20 minutes. Then home
and to bed.

## Right Page

### Sunday, January 7, 1866.

Brindy, squally, flurrying and sun-shinily wicked miser
day. Quite a snow flurry drove from the N.W. during the
m'f.; all day the wind was cut like a knife — As I
am suffering now from neuralgia, owing primarily to my childish
and real terror of a dentist
I have not gone to church at all to-day. The sudden
transitions from cold out of doors, to warm in doors gives
me severe pain. I am comfortable so long as I am by a
fire. So have been in nearly all day Reading & writing.
Have enjoyed my cozy Room & bright fire particularly.
Eve'g passed pleasantly in conversation with Geo and
writing to Father.
