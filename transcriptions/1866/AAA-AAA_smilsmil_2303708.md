## Left Page

### Thursday, February 15, 1866.

The artists last night, after Punch,
\+ sandwiches + appointing a Committee
(Church, Edwin White & Cropsey) to tell
them what they came together for,
adjourned until next Wed. eveg.

Splendid day, but rather windy.
Bro. Will called at the Studio. He
has no duties down-town to-day, on
acct. of Mr. Geo. W. Hatch's funeral.
He brought me a letter, sent
through Uncle Willie from that
crazy man Hinshelwood — I told
him to put it into the fire. Sat
all the Mng to loop for Academy
Portrait. He painted some upon
the hair + face. Afternoon, went
down-town to Continental's with
die + drawing of sheep subject
eug. by Graham. Left bill for $80.00

Eveg. called upon Miss
Florence Smith at 254 W. 23d St.
Ges joined me at 9 o'clk —
— a very pleasant evening —
for us.

## Right Page

### Friday, February 16, 1866.

Clear and cold — All day
painting upon "View on the Delaware"
10½×18, for Mr. Tracy Harris (3d day)
It is one of the poorest beginnings
I think that I have painted this
winter — To-day I have painted
over the sky + middle distance —
It is better, I think, but not much,
being all too cold and green.

Eveg., went to Winter Garden
to see Edwin Booth in Richelieu.
I am no admirer of Theatricals —
and to me, their tragedies always
approach too nearly to farce.

Portions of the Scenery I considered
very fine, except the villainous
wronging of the Rights of perspective.
Booth's "get up" was excellent, but
he was not the cunning old man,
"taking out the lion's skin with the
foxes." He ranted + raved —
It did not please me even so
much as his Hamlet — It was
all "Theatrical". House, full.
