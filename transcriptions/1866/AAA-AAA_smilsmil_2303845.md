## Left Page

### Friday, November 30, 1866

Still showery and clear — dark &
light. Very difficult to work. —
Made an outline of a River glimpse
upon a circle of about 8 in. for a
frame that I have had for some
years — Then commenced outlining
with Op.te Turp. + brown upon 16×22.
"Hudson River." I am very much
interested in this subject + hope
to be able to paint it upon the
canvas as it is already painted
in my mind — but it is very like
trying to whistle an imperfectly re-
membered tune — at the first audible
note away goes the coarse phantom —
so in my painting — the first visible
daus upon the canvas, blinds me to
my ideal picture that fades away
like breath upon a glass.

Ever at home, trying to get
some things to Rights.

## Right Page

### Saturday, December 1, 1866

Gusty, blowy + disagreeable day to me.
Shortly after getting to Studio's, young Mr.
Bill of Yonkers called + renewed his
application to be taken as a Pupil in
drawing + expressing his willingness to pay
even $10.00 pr. lesson — he is amply able.
I agreed to give him a lesson every Sat.
m'g for $5.00 a lesson — he sat right
down + took his first lesson — I worked
upon outlining 16×22 "Hudson River"
finishing it before 3 o'clk when George
+ I went to 1st Phil. Ph. of 2d concert —
I listened to Beethoven's 4th Symphony
but neuralgic jerks took me in the face
so that I could not remain to hear the
"Carnival Romain" of Berlioz + a con-
certo for Camille Urso ther Violin —
Walked home — felt better, sat down in
my big chair, had a little nap before
dinner and spent a very pleasant
evening at home; looking over
old letters.
