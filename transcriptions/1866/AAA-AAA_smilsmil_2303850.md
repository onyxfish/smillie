## Left Page

### Friday, December 14, 1866

Blustering, dirty & disagreeable day — Stayed about the house until after 10 o'clk — then got disgusted at myself & started out. Went up to Bedford's & got a bottle of Hastings Dry Sherry — Returning home took a little fit & then went on to the Studio, feeling very miserable — got there but unfit to work — Curled myself up in the big chair until 1.30 — then went out to a Restaurant in 4th Ave & got a plate of Rice pudding — Retd to Studio sat in big chair again until after 3 — then roused myself — Rode home went right to bed — high fever — every — Swallowed a lot of piping hot flax seed tea & bathed my feet, or, parboiled my feet in hot water — went to bed at 12 M & passed a very wretched night.

## Right Page

### Saturday, December 15, 1866

Beautiful day, judging by what I can see out of my window. Kept my bed until after 1 o'clk when I got up feeling unexpectedly better — The Rest of the day I spent in my big chair in my own Room — Mrs. Tracy Harris called & saw me. Carpenter and painter at last got out of the house. Carpets came, but no one to put them. Leeds called this [morning] at Studio but saw no one — So did Bell, my pupil — Gel being attending to Phil. Rehearsal — The first of our informal Reception at our Studio Bldg. took place this [evening] — Gel says that things went very nicely — Every all off to 2d Phil. Concert — Charl took my place & ticket — I have heard nothing of this concert —
