## Left Page

### Thursday, January 4, 1866.

Fine day overhead. In bed with a sick headache until noon when I managed to get up. Made myself a cup of tea and eat some Roles. Finished my letter to S.L.7. and at 3. P.M went to the Studio. Charcoaled on canvas 10½ × 18 in. a View on the Delaware, near Easton Pa. for Mr. Tracy Harris. Mr. Greatorey brought into our Studio a troop of young ladies from Miss Haines school.

## Right Page

### Friday, January 5, 1866.

Clear, crisp and very cold. Finished Charcoaling & outlined "on the Delaware" — Commenced outlining with [illegible] Turpentine and Caledonia Brown, my Academy picture "On the Hudson, above Pok̈eepsie" — Was considerably interrupted and did not make Satisfactory progress with my work — Miss Fanny Payson & Miss Kate Drinker called in the afternoon. Later, J. Q. A. Ward came up with some 3 or 4 friends. Spent the evening until after 10 o'clk with Mr. & Mrs. Brevoort.
