## Left Page

### Wednesday, February 7, 1866.

Raw chilly + overcast —
threatening Snow. Mr. Jacob
Ryckoff visited us in the m'g
to look at some little pictures
by Colman + Breevoort. Sat
to Loops for Academy portrait
from 10.30 until 1 o'clk. —
Then again a few minutes
in the afternoon. The
Rest of the day wasted with
Callers, little jobs + domestic
house-work about the Studio.
Evelf called, with George,
upon Miss Dunning. Met
there besides her father + brother
Ned. Mr. Rackermann and
Fred Durand. The evelf
passed pleasantly altho'
Miss D. was remarkably
quiet.

## Right Page

### Thursday, February 8, 1866.

Drizzle + mizzle, overhead
about — and a marvel of
slipperiness underfoot —
Spent all of the day Charcoaling
and outlining subjects — most
of the day upon 14×20 in. up-
right canvas "Old Spruce Trees
+ Storm among the Mountains".
The trees are almost literal
transfers from a pencil study
that I made on the Hudson
above Poughkeepsie, June 1863.
The charcoal drawing
pleases me + I hope to be
able to paint it without
destroying all of its spirit.
Evelf at the Studio char-
coaling upon other subjects.
Heavy Rain in the evelf.
