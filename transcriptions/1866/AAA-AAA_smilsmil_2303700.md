## Left Page

### Tuesday, January 30, 1866

Very disagreeable day. Raw and misty — a little sleet in the evng., and bright moonlight at 11 o'clk P.M. Etched in Beaver P.O. Stamps and bit it in". Felt used up with a sort of influenza feeling. In the evng. instead of going to Miss Pardee's, No 10 W 47 y., I mote a Regret — so did Gro. and I took them up and delivered them myself at 9 o'clk. They were home, spent a comfortable hour along side of the fire amusing myself with bits of Gil Blas and then to bed.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, January 31, 1866

Glorious day — Worked hard all day with Graver, burnisher and Rebiting on Beaver. I hope that it is finished. I have begrudged the time that I have had to take from my Academy picture and am now all anxiety to get at it again. Father came up to the Studio between 4 & 5 o'clk. to go to Mr. A. W. Shepard's to dinner. Evng. Glo. & I joined him & there met Mr. Mead, Mr. & Mrs. Howells, just from Venice & two or three others. We were particularly interested in a collection of Photographs that Mr. H., who has resided in Venice 4 yr., has brought home with him — Mr. S. amused us with his Stereoscptions. Left a little after 10 o'clk.
