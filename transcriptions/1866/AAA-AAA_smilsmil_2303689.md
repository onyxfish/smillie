## Left Page

### Monday, January 8, 1866

"The oldest 'inhabitant'" is out with a statement that to-day is the coldest day that New Yorkers have known for sixty years. But with all due deference to said O. I — I think that I remember a spell about as cold 3 years ago. It is said that the Ther. fell to 20° below zero. It was certainly very cold and we put our noble stove to the test to warm up the Studio. The sky is without a cloud & the air cuts like a knife. Set my palette for the first time since Oct 2d — & painted a little sketch for Mr. Brevoort. It is very crude, but I hope to make something of it. Everg at home.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, January 9, 1866

Not so cold, but more disagreeable owing to the wind — Clear cloudless sky. Painted in, (1½ day) View on the Delaware 10½ × 18 in. for Mr. Tracy Harris. Got it all out of harmony. Sky too Red — distance too green — all, lacking brilliancy — but of course I hope to make something out of it. Everg. go and I spent the eveg at the Grammercy Park House with Mr. Chas. Martin and daughter Sarah.
