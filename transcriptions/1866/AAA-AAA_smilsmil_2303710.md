## Left Page

### Monday, February 19, 1866.

Pouring down Rain all day — Storm broke away about 6 o'clk P.M. — t at 11, all was clear Starlight. Miss Dunning spent a good part of the day with us drawing — I painted (1st day) on upright "Noon" 6½×9 in. Altho' feeling still much under the weather, I am tolerably pleased with my days work. To-day I should have attended Superior Court, being summoned as juror — but I will risk a fine of $25.00

N Sat. Mrs Choate called to get me to take her as a pupil. At first I Refused, but at last she persuaded me into a half consent — Eveg at 9 o'clk went around to Mr. Wm Martin's 155 W 21st met Gel. to Staid until 20 m to 11 — then home to bed.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, February 20, 1866.

Glorious day. Worked all day over sky and distant mountains on my Academy picture. (12th day) Rubbed a very dry scumble of warm tint — white, Jaune Brill., Mars yellows & in parts, Cadmium, over the entire sky — then painted the higher lights etc — As I Rubbed the scumble off with a dry Rag — it was so thin and dry, that in an hour or two it all "went in" & looked like the dickens. Painted over the distant mountains, but am not at all pleased with the color. Eveg Gel. & I went to Mr. Rutherford's — Cor 110 1st & 2d ave. Saw her Autographic & artistic album as far as it is finished & again promised to add our contributions.
