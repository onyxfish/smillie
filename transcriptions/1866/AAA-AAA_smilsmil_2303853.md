## Left Page

### Thursday, December 20, 1866

Bright, clear & very cold day.
Got up a few minutes after
9 o'clk A.M., after a most
purgatorial night, at times my
breathing through my nose being
entirely stopped. In the m[orning]
when I got up, fairly worn out
and discouraged — feel now
that I am not getting along &
cannot get along — with a
presentiment that I am to
be much worse before I am
any better. Dr. J. called bet[ween]
3 & 4 o'clk P.M. & tried to en-
courage me. Rather heavy
work — Bro. Will called in
for a few minutes in the
even[ing].

## Right Page

### Friday, December 21, 1866

Bright & sunny — In bed all day
with miserable pain in my
head besides, a pretty severe
attack of neuralgia in the left
side of my head & jaw. Got
up at about 5 o'clk. P.M. Had
a shiver while washing & soon
after was in quite a fever —
Between 7 & 8 o'clk. when Dr. J.
called for the 2d time to-day,
with all things, & in addition, a
stuffed up head, so that I
could hardly breathe. I felt
miserable enough — Thought
that I was going right into
my anticipated Typhoid fever.
Small iron bed-stead brought down to
my Room again — passed a miserable
night upon it. Father at home,
all day & in bed — General cold with
fever, & added, Tonsilitis.
