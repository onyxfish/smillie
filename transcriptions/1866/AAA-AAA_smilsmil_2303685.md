### MONDAY, JANUARY 1, 1866.

A most miserable day —
Raining steadily until after
noon. Geo and myself
were at the Studio all day,
until near 4 o'clk P.M. I
was engaged charcoaling
upon my Academy picture
(1st day) 25 × 48 in — This
composed from my summer
studies, being almost a view,
looking from the "East Road" over
Mt. Hope, Mr. Jas. Roosevelt's place,
& down the River to the Hudson
Highlands. At my work, I was
quite happy in my recollections
of some of the glorious days of
last summer — In the evening
called upon Misses Ireland —
Kimball, Blanche & Leila Martin,
Julie Martin, Lizzie Munroe,
Meta Gavit, & Mrs. Frank W.
Ballard. Mrs. Wm M. Smillie &
Mr. Jacob Wyckoff — Sister
Mary was with Mrs. W. at No. 20. W.
41st St.
