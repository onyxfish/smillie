## Left Page

### Monday, September 3, 1866.

Very fine morning. Walked over to the D°s at about 8.15 a.m. with some of the lilies that I gathered last evening — waited until Addie came down, after breakfast, prepared more lilies & sent along (Mr. S's col'd man) to Mr. Tappen's with them & my compliments to the young ladies. Commenced a pencil study from four of the lilies that I had kept & worked on it until 9.30. Then went to the Elms & worked on my pine study (5, mf.) Addie there before me. She gave Geo. & me an invitation to tea. To night afternoon, painted in chestnut in oatfield study (5×t.m.) and at 4.45 got to Mr. Tappen's & found all four of the ladies waiting for me. They objected to Resuming old sketches — too damp — agreed to go to meadow across the road, worked until after 6, accomplishing as usual, nothing much — hurried down to D.D's a little after 7 took tea & passed a pleasant chatty evening, the P. appearing to be in very amiable mood. Wrote home a little before 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, September 4, 1866.

Had yesterday, probably, all she good weather that can be granted to us this — Rain commenced before 9 o'clk a.m. & continued during the day — warm very little air stirring — After chopping my clint of wood — took breakfast, then finished the penciling of the white lilies that I commenced yesterday m/f — then gathered some Golden Rod Blackberry vine tone or two other things and spent the rest of the m/f. making drawings from them — After dinner, set to palette & accumulated worked over the skies on oatfield study & Elms (14×24) giving to both, I think, more air & tone. at about 4 o'clk Took up & finished pencil study on brook that I worked upon last Thursday. at 6 o'clk, finished my days work & walked the piazza until tea time. Evh. Rec'd a little note from Mega giving acknowledging & note with pen sketches that I sent down to her yesterday — more walking on the piazza & then to bed at 10 o'clk.
