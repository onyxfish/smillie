## Left Page

### Wednesday, May 30, 1866.

Pleasant day after the rain of yesterday. Commenced again (2º day) at 9×15 for Mr. Jab. Earle — Repainting sky and distance — got it much warmer, but the distance far too strong —
Evely. Aunt Mary Gillelan & Tom took dinner with us. Quite a party at the house to bid Aunt Jessie good bye — She starts for Canada tomorrow morg. — Went around to the Parsons to see Nanny Pleasants, but they were all out — After 9 o'clk made a Charcoal sketch, trying to illustrate the closing scene in Hugo's "Toilers of the Sea".

## Right Page

### Thursday, May 31, 1866.

Another fine day — Worked (3º day) on Mr. Earle's 9×15, finishing the 2º painting of it — Painting as I do now without a particle of interest or desires, only under the whip of necessity — it is no wonder that so far I am not very Successful — Aunt J— Emma — Will Gillelan, his wife & two children got off to Ottawa all Right — at 7.45 — this morg. Evely went up to 172. 4[illegible] to call on Misses Duvall & Kate Drinker — both out —
