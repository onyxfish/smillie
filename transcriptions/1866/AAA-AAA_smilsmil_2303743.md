## Left Page

### Wednesday, May 2, 1866

Windy m[orn], and threatening —
but before noon, cleared off
charmingly. Worked very
unhappily (3d day) on tracing
Parliament Bldgs. Ottawa — got
the tracing finished —
Everf went over and paid
to Mr. Jos. M. Coburn, for Father
$3,000 — on the house No. 20
W. 41st St. Then called at
No. 139 W. 22d St hoping to see
the Riley Girls, of Montrose,
but they were out.

## Right Page

### Thursday, May 3, 1866

Fine day — Rather breezy
and cool — Retraced the
outline of the Ottawa Bldgs
upon the Bristol Board (4th day).
Extremely trying work — both
to eyes and patience.
Everf at home, not feeling
well.
