## Left Page

### Wednesday, August 22, 1866.

Up a little after 5 o'clk — mr. [illegible] bit low & scudding clouds all about &
wind S.W. Walked over to the Dr's with
Rosalie G's umbrella as agreed, — before
6 o'clk — not a soul up — called ca-back
-back — & Addie came rushing down
thinking somebody was sick. Bbt'y got
bkf's at 7. Wrote a letter to Matt
then worked upon an India ink
drawing that Miss Ellen Tappen is to
copy. At 11 o'clk took the colt & rode
in to town to P.O. Mr. Hill & another y'g
man called to invite us to a Town Pic-nic
at Mirror Lake — 3 miles off — too far to
walk tho way to ride. Addie and
Emma went but we did not. P.M.
worked again, finishing drawing, until
after 5 o'clk, then Geo. & I sauntered
off until 6. Eve'g, charming
moonlight until near 9, when it
clouded over. Walked the Road
& piazza until near 10 o'clk —
then to bed. Just after tea,
went down to the pond & gathered
white lilies.

## Right Page

### Thursday, August 23, 1866.

Steady pouring Rain all day long.
Between 5 & 6 o'clk, went over to the
Dr's with white lilies — too early, again
— saw no one — hung lilies & card on verand-
ah post. Between 9 & 10 a.m. Mr.
Davis came with invitation to us
from Addie to spend M'g or p.m.
with them — as we were started
upon our work, promised to go over
in the afternoon. Painted in a
sky from pencil sketch upon my
14×24 in. Then Study, much to
its advantage, I think. At
3 P.M. Geo. & I went through much
mud & rain to D. D's Found Mr.
& Mrs. Ireland there. Drew designs
upon Lebanon Needle book cover for
Emma. Then some games upon the
piazza & hall — then tea — then
drawing upon pin cushion covers for
Addie. Geo. Haws Read an Irish
story & Recited a Negro sermon much
to the amusement of the Company —
then a little singing "by the band"
"good nights" all around & at 9:45
a walk home through mud but dry overhead.
