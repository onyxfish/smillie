## Left Page

### Monday, October 1, 1866

a genuine Oct. day. Soft, warm & hazy.
Agreed that we have a picnic to Fire-
Hill — after much running about & mending
of pantaloons, at 10.40 we got off — Uncle
Wm. Aunt R. & Sarah, in uncle Wm. buggy —
Mr & Mrs. Harris, their Son Edw. & Cel.
in a hired carriage & I on horse back
on a big, stiff & short trotting mare. — We drove
down the Wyalusing Road about 6 miles to
Depew's Mills, then turned sharply to the
left & climbed an unmitigated hill for nearly
2 miles, arriving at the summit at about
10 clk after a hot drive. Put horses in the
barn of a man named Bertholf. Got cream, milk
& butter & between 2 & 3 had a Royal dinner
in a meadow under the shade of a big tree —
glorious stretches away to South & West. After
dinner, sketched a Hemlock top — then got up
horses & at 4 started homewards — over the
Ridge, getting into town over Butterfield's Hill —
— Some delicious afternoon effects — got home
at 5.40 — all Right — the day a success.
Every spent until 9.30 with Fau M &
Mand D. Mr Brewster there & Elisha M & his
wife — Fau & Maud go to Scranton &
Wilksbarre to-morrow for 3 days.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, October 2, 1866

Yet another superb day — Up early &
walked out to the lake — Very fine picture
cloudless sky — wreathing fog, & autumn color
— felt well & enjoyed it — Off & late —
croquet with Mr. Harris — then worked
until noon on pencil sketch of yesterday.
Nap of an hour before dinner — all right —
dinner late — More croquet — at 3
met after Mr. Lyman's Poney for Sarah
to ride — got it & Tarbell's horse for my-
self, but we did not get started until
near 4 — mishaps at starting — Saddle turned
— sudden dismounting — all right — pony
pig headed & hard — Sarah discouraged —
Return home & the children play with
the horse, while I finish my ride alone —
delighted with afternoon effect
over distance & hills seen from road
that runs through Sayre's farm to the
Silver Lake Road — Ret'd at 5.45
taking home the pony beast —

Every quietly in doors doing not
much of anything in particular.
