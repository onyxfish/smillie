## Left Page

### Saturday, August 18, 1866

Glorious cloudless day, warm and
slightly breezy — Got to my work under the
elms, a little before 9 o'clk (14° mf.).
At about 10.30 Addie & Emma stopped,
on their way to Mr. C. E. Butler's — Their
visit was about 20 m. long. Just a
fortnight since Emma went to Mr. B.
— was very glad to see her. Resumed
work & painted away upon largest
Elm of the groupe but made very
slow progress. Afternoon, as there
were slight clouds, tried to paint
in sky on oatfield study (2° day)
but at 4.30 o'clk gave it up as un-
successful & rubbed it all together
a sort of general grey. Then went
to Mr. Tappens as before agreed — Found
excuses from Misses Hosper & Carey — gave
to the Misses Tappen, in-doors a lesson
in brush handling in India Ink (4.°
lesson) This is better than taking them
out, for not one of them is far enough
advanced to sketch/study from Nature.

Eve.g Geo. & I walked the Road nig
8.30 — after that I continued my walk
upon the piazza until near 10. then bed

## Right Page

### Sunday, August 19, 1866

Overcast mf. Up early & had
a half hours walk before breakfast.
Walked half way in to town, when Mr.
Smith's vehicle overtook me — went
to Con. Ch. & heard Mr. Smith preach
from Rev. 19. 12. "And on his head were
many Crowns." A good discourse
upon Christ & his many honors — Met
Miss Apthorpe & bid her Good-bye,
as she leaves Lenox to-morrow mf.
Walked home with Geo. after morning
service — Rained most of the way.
The old lady got us lunch — then
wrote a little note with pen sketches
to Emma Ireland — dinner at about
4 — Rain very heavy until near
6 — wrote to Mary but did not finish
the letter — Evening spent talking
with the Smiths & pacing the piazza.
