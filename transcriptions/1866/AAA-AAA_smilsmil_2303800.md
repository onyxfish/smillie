## Left Page

### Thursday, August 30, 1866

Cool, clear morning, worked from 7.40 until 9 o'clk, penciling
vines from nature, then took pencils & book & went over to the
brook beyond Dr. D's, where some time ago I got an outline of a
subject. Stopped for Addie —
her father & husband from N.Y. — could not go then. Went on — worked faithfully until after 12. Addie came a little before 12. Had a splendid
walk home to dinner. Afternoon took old Hemlock pencil study
& worked upon it until 4.15 (4.
p.m.) then went to Mr. Tappen's.
Gave to Helen & Mary a lesson
in the Library — penciling from
a small branch of ash leaves.
On my way home was overtaken by
Meta Gavis & Charley Adams, driving
from Pittsfield on their way home
to Stockbridge. Had a jolly
visit from them on the piazza
— showed them some of air-
sketches etc etc. Evening
spent gadding about.

## Right Page

### Friday, August 31, 1866

Mild, pleasant day. My., from
until 9 o'clk, penciled upon brook
study, then went down to the Elms
& commenced a study 11×14 in.
of the lower parts of some pine trees
upon the edge of the wood. Intended
to make it a sketch of a single
setting but only got over about one
quarter of it. Have got a promising start, I think. Afternoon
got started at last upon the hills
in "oatfield" study, (3rd day) After
working about an hour, it clouded
over and I worked until 6, without
sun. Got in the line of hills and
middle distant trees. The
fence in the foreground, for which
in the oatfield I left a space un-
painted, has all been pulled down,
so I will have to invent it —

Addie came up to work under
the Elms this m[orning].

Eve[ning], until 9 o'clk, walking
the road with George.
