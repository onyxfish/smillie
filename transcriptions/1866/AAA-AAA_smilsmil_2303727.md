## Left Page

### Tuesday, March 27, 1866

Delightful day — cloudless and Spring like. Worked
(31st day) over Acad. pic —
Scumbling over parts of sky,
the blue, etc — the distant
Mts — the opposite side of
the River — the farthest
meadow, left hand side
& all the middle distant
trees — The whole thing I think
now hangs much better
together. I glazed and
painted also, Righthand
foreground trees, getting
them much Richer & freer
than the G. N. Every
finished lining our Shadow
boxes — then went up to
Will's to see if Mother had
arrived — but she stayed
all night at Aunt Jessie's
— Jersey City.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, March 28, 1866

Beautiful m'g, but overcast
at noon, and between 6 & 7
o'clk p.m. it commenced
to Rain. Worked (32d day)
on Acad. pic, painting over
foreground & about generally
toning & heightening the effect.
The past few days have
made a great change in
the general effect & color
& now I am quite flattered.
Every. until after 9 o'clk
at Studio then went up
to Will's & visited until 11
with Father & Mother.
