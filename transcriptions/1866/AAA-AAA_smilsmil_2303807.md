## Left Page

### Thursday, September 13, 1866

Glorious morning — after bkft. went to Mrs. Crosby's + got our washing + settled with her. Then to the D's and with Addie came home, the D. following us — Showed off all our studies + summer work, with which he seemed pleased. Then Addie + I went to the Elms. She finished her study + I worked upon up right elm 12×18 ( [illegible] ).
P.M. worked upon same study until 3.30 — then with Ge. tramped to the high Ridge on W. Stockbridge Mt., back of Bristid's. Every thing just perfect — views Ravishing, etc. etc — waited upon the Ridge until after sunset then came down through the woods. Got home at 7.15 well tired, washed, changed, took supper + then by invitation went over to the D's feeling first rate — D. was out, — Addie, then + cheerful fire on the hearth — worked upon slight sketches that I had taken in the afternoon + got home at 10.15 P.M.

## Right Page

### Friday, September 14, 1866

Clouded over, after bkft. got a Rooster's head + commenced a small pencil study — About 8, started for the Church hill in town to take pencil sketches but it Rained by the time I Reached Dr. Dunning's — stopped there + found them at bkft. Got home at 9.15 + finished two studies from Rooster's head. Rained heavily most of the m'g. P.M. at 2 o'clk again started for town. Succeeded in getting two sketches looking over the town + to the S.W. Sprinkling once. At 4 Pet's, stopping at Mr. Sear's + the P.O. Got the D's mail and stopped a moment with it on my way home. Between 5 + 6 o'clk Mr. Jul. Cheney + niece, Mr. Goodman and Rosalie gave us a farewell call. — Very enthusiastic. — Heavy shower in early ev'g, then glorious new moon + all clear. Getting things together, to leave Sunday, until 10.30. During the night more heavy Rain and driving wind.
