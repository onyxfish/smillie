## Left Page

### Thursday, July 5, 1866

Bkfst again a little after 6 am, then Geo. & I went hunting up sketches — spent the mng mostly on the margin of the large lake — then through woods & meadows up to the house & back over the hill to the small lake or pond — Found much, that was tantalizingly near being first rate — In the woods & on the lake margin — we were obliged to get too near to the objects. I found at least two subjects (wood & lake bits) that I wish to do. After dinner, penciled upon a little sketch in my book & at 3 o'clk. Geo. & I again started out, prospecting — we went through woods, over hills & meadows, but did not find exactly what we wanted — After 6 o'clk while at tea. Dr. D. Addie & Emma stopped a moment on their way home from Stockbridge — we were invited to go with them to see fireworks at Mr. Rackermann's but were too tired — Eveng spent writing to Father.

## Right Page

### Friday, July 6, 1866

morning overcast but very close and warm — walked over to the Dr's taking with me the first white lilies from the lake back of our house. After some delay, Addie & Emma started out with me, we went to the woods on the large lake and after some wandering found geo, still unable to settle upon any subject — All four continued peregrinations until dinner time when 'twas determined to begin upon some elms at the edge of some woods quite near our house — The girls could not come again in the afternoon so Geo. and I started our subjects — I found that I could outline mine with the aid of the camera-lucida, so I outlined one 14×24 in and another 12×18 — both Elm subjects with little bits of distance. Eveng walked over to the Dr's and sat chatting upon the verandah until half past 9 o'clk — just before going to bed the Ther. marked 80°. Pretty warm for the Berkshire hills.
