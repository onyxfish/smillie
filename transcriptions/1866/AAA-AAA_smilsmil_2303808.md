## Left Page

### Saturday, September 15, 1866

Up a little after 5 o'clk. Very cold and windy. Pitched right into packing and at 8 o'clk had trunk & bags locked and breakfast eaten. At 8.30 saddled colt & trotover to meet Addie by appointment. Got started at 9 o'clk. Horses very frisky — went at once to top of Mt. Stockbridge Mt., took wood Road to left & after breaking & thrusting through brushwood & over logs, came out upon open rock & enjoyed fine view — retraced Road & got down back of Bristeds — took another wood Road & went up to Buck's meadow. — Home at 11.30 — Addie much pleased — glorious Ride. Dinner at 12 — Started for State Line at 1 — Reached it at 2.25 — Addie & Davis arr. at 2.45. Train arr'd at 3.15. Bid good bye to Lenox & Berkshire for the Summer of 1866. In 40 min. Reached Chatham. At 4.30 Harlem Train came along. For miles had glorious views of Catskills — The day has been great — at 9.30 Reached No. 20 W. 41st. St. & found all glad to see us — Talked and gossiped until near midnight, then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sunday, September 16, 1866

Glorious day — clear & quite cool. Geo. & I stayed home during the m'g to visit with Mother about — went down to the Studio to look about. Returning home, stopped in to B's may Tab — Just heard dog. Afternoon walked in Central Park and enjoyed exceedingly the delicious day. Even'g with something of a headache went for a while in to the Wills —
