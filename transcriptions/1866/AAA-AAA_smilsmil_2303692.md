## Left Page

### Sunday, January 14, 1866

Cold windy day — In the m'f went to hear Jos. P.
Thompson — Heard the
concluding discourse of
his course upon the Relations
of worldly wealth & prosperity
to Christian life.
Afternoon, took a cup
of tea with Mr & Mrs Brevoort
after dinner, then went
home & wrote for an hour
and a half — then tea
and an evening comfortably
at home, over a pleasant
fire, Reading & writing.

## Right Page

### Monday, January 15, 1866

A very fine day & quite
mild. Clouding over in
the afternoon, & by nine
o'clk snowing so that 4 in.
or more fell before morning.
Painted in (1st day) another
little "Wood bit on the Hudson"
7½ × 10¾ in. Was so much
interrupted by calls that it
was with difficulty that I got
it all in. & then, but indifferently.
Mr Meuper called & gave to Jo.
& myself Commissions each to paint
a little landscape 7 × 12 in.
Price $40 —. Miss Dunning
lunched with us. In the
evening went with Jal. to
Mr Wm Martins. 155 W 21st St.
& had quite a lively time
with Misses Lila & Blanche M.
Misses Holt Miss Drake —
Mr & Mrs M — & a brother of Miss
B —. I was not well — so was
rather stupid, I'm afraid.
