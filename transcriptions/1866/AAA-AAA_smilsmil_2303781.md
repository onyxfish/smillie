## Left Page

### Monday, July 23, 1866

and threatened to some
purpose — On getting up, fond
a good drenching Rain in
process of doing itself — Tried
easel & palette & painted in a
sketch, 8×12 in, of some cloud
effects that I made note of the
other day — Am rather pleased
with the Result— wonder why I
can paint with such facility in
doors, the so paralyzed, so far as
work is concerned, out of doors —
Sunshine & heavy Rain all day.
At 4.30 Gel & I went to Dr. D's — I
got Jaws & set to work trimming
one of the elm trees south of the
house — Geo. visiting with the girls
in-doors —

Ride home in the Rain, took
tea & killed the evening.

The Dunning party, I believe, rode
to W. Stockbridge in the eve'g with
Dr. D. who is bound for N.Y. — The
Ride must have been cold, but was
no doubt pleasant — to all
concerned.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, July 24, 1866

An unexpectedly clear bright morning —
But as it was still chilly out of doors, &
I was anxious to go on with some in-door
work, I painted a little sketch 6¼×8½
in, based upon one of my Monument Mt.
sketches of last year. Gel pleases me
by telling me that it is the best thing
that I have done. I have painted it
with the intention of giving it to Addie.

At 4.30 got the blk. colt & started out
with him under the saddle — went around
by Rattlesnake Mt. & back past Mr. Goodman's —
near there, a dog sprang out upon me. colt
kicked & away went the saddle girth — kept
my seat, but had to get down — Rode to Mr.
G's & borrowed his saddle & then back
home by 7 o'clk — tedious Ride. but the
p.m. just as lovely as it could be —
Eve'g Gel & I walked in to town with
letters — called upon the P's & found
them all in — was introduced to a very
pleasant lively Miss Smith & her cousin
Miss Ogden, sister to Anna Cora Mowatt —
The Dunning party walked to Roaring
Brook, so I am told, after 5 o'clk. P.M. —
distance about 12 miles — moonlit night
