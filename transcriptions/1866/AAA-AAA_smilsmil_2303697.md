## Left Page

### Wednesday, January 24, 1866

Dull day threatening snow. My outlined a little River bit
6×10 in. that I charcoaled in yesterday & also a wood bit
10×14 in. At 12 o'clk set palette & worked the rest
of the day on "wood bit +River" 6¾×10¾ in. to be sent to
Utica. (2 days) Think that I have lost none of what was
good in the first painting & have improved parts that
were not good. Father arrived in the City this p.m.,
fixed up his tea for him at the Studio. Spent the ev'g
with us at home & slept at Düring's — across the street.

## Right Page

### Thursday, January 25, 1866

Dark, disagreeable day — My worked over small branches & leaves — sharpening
& finishing generally on "wood. &+River" (2½ days) —
Afternoon worked upon Mr. Thompson's picture of "Fall
upon West Brook — Catskill Mts" Added tangle to foreground,
breaking up the stiffness of line & painted in a Rainbow at the
foot of the fall. I borrowed the picture for the N.A. of D.
reception & saw so much that I wished to do, that I have just
retained the picture to do it.
Part of ev'g in at Wills with Father, who slept again at Düring's.
