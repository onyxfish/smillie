## Left Page

### Tuesday, August 28, 1866.

Cloudy, mild day. After waiting some time, to see what it would do in the weather way, took pencils & paper & went to the turn of the Road north of Mr. Smith's, & made a sketch looking S.W. over Lake Mahkeenac, then commenced a 2° sketch, looking N.W. but before getting it finished, it commenced to Rain — After dinner it rained quite heavily, worked in the barn with Mr. S. Carpentering until after 3 P.M. Then dressed & as it had cleared up after a fashion, went over to Mr. Goodman's, getting there a little after 5 o'clk — George followed me — Had a game of Croquet with Miss Rosalie in the wet grass — then another with Geo. & Miss Cheney added, but did not finish it. At 7.15 Addie, Geo. Hawes & Charley D. came along. Had tea — and after tea had a game of Squails — at 10.30 walked home by a sick looking moonlight.

## Right Page

### Wednesday, August 29, 1866.

Morning very much after the pattern of yesterday — Gathered some trailing vines — Wild Buckwheat &c to, & made pencil sketches from them. About 9. Addie called at the house — Geo. & I went with her to the woods — She & Geo. sketched, I hunted around, doing nothing but climb trees — Afternoon, breezy & cloudy, but much clearer — worked again upon some pencilings, & at 3 — took traps & went over to the D's — by 5 o'clk had finished a slight sketch of the house from the S.E. — taking in some of the largest trees — Rosalie Goodman & Miss Cheney came up & with Addie, Geo. H. & Rid Goodman, we had a game of Croquet. Our side, Addie & R.G. & I — badly beaten. Eveng, marching on the Road with George.
