## Left Page

### Wednesday, October 17, 1866

And yet some more glorious — The m[illegible] until 10.45 spent bidding good-bye to Amanda Diven & Fanny Mulford, who went to New Milford by Stage on their way to Elmira. Worked until near one o'clk. drawing on sketch of Lenox, then spent about an hour on the bed, as I felt quite tired out. Dinner at 1.30 — all ready for a start — horseback, when I found that Tarbell had played me a trick again, going off with my horse. — Ran around until nearly 4 o'clk when I got off on Searle's horse "Billy" — a stiff gaggling, shambling thing. It was like a hobby-horse without rockers & had a movement inside that reminded me strongly of a fanning mill — Got Week's horse for Sarah & started down the South Road. Round the Meshopsen — first turn to Right — through charming wood, on to South Road again beyond Latrobe old farm & home — just about 6 o'clk. So me had ambled & giggled over about 6 miles in 2 hours — a distance that I could have walked on foot in 1½ hours. Every all at home, yawning in a most interesting manner. Joc. ## Right Page

### Thursday, October 18, 1866

mrng clouded over & almost threatening but by 10 o'clk it broke away & at noon was delightful. Between bkfst and 10 o'clk. down town attending to a variety of things, looking for my lost umbrella, etc — Returning home worked until dinner time on pencil studies. After dinner, as I was disappointed about getting horses, I took pencils & paper & went out to Jas. Carvalto — Aided by Mrs. C. I worked for an hour to a half trying to get a study of the head of the Bull "Duke". While he was securely fastened on one side by ropes from the ring in his nose, Mrs C. kept him as nearly as possible in position by a long pole fastened to the ring in his nose from the other side. I got two indifferent pencil studies — he was so very uneasy. P.m. lovely, warm, still & clear beyond description. Every, moonlight to match. Went to Presb. Ch. with Sarah to hear something about Freedman's Aid Soc. — don't know, nor wish to know, the man's name — inexcusably tedious — home & to bed about 11 o'clk
