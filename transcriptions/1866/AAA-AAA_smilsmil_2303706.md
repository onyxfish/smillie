## Left Page

### Sunday, February 11, 1866.

Mng. glorious overhead, but terrible underfoot — went
to B'way Tabernacle and heard J. P. Thompson. It was
one of his exhortation sermons and was, of course, admirable.

Afternoon, overcast — at home & part of the time
napping in my big chair.
Even. again heard J. P. T. —
"Blessed are they that
mourn" etc, etc.

After services, went down
to studio. Found fire out —
Rekindled it & Read
"On the Limits of Education".
A lecture by Dr. Bigelow
until near 11 o'clk — then
home to bed.

## Right Page

### Monday, February 12, 1866.

Pouring down Rain — Snow
and ice Rapidly disappearing
— more like April than Feby.

Miss Dunning came to draw
at about 10 o'clk. Commenced
copying one of my Summer oil
studies — Took lunch with
us. — I commenced painting
an upright wood scene 10×14 in
— by Even. got it about half
painted in (1½ day). Think
it so far, quite up to what
I have done so far. Even.
Spent a little time after
dinner with Mr & Mrs. [illegible] Brewoo[?]
Reading Orpheus C. Kerr's papers.

Then went around & paid Chas.
Schmutt, the cabinet maker
$61.50 that I owed him —

Then to studio & spent an hour
or so with father — then home
& to bed —
