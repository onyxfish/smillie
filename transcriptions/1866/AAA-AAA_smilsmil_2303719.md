## Left Page

### Friday, March 9, 1866.

March has not done blowing
about it yet. If possible
this is about the worst day
yet. Joe Williams of Montrose
called this mf. Eleven of the
young people of Mr. made up the
Bridal party of Priscilla Torre and
are now at the St. Nicholas and
Metropolitan. P.M. Joe W. and
Josie Bentley called. Worked
all day (3d day) on Mr. Red's little
picture — It looks very dark —
Evey Ofc. and I went to
the St. Nicholas — but the yg
people were all out enjoying
themselves. So we went to
Bryant's Minstrels to laugh
at their nonsense — walked
home through a perfect
hurricane of wind, dust
and dirt.

## Right Page

### Saturday, March 10, 1866.

Glorious day overhead, but
still very windy and dusty.
Mr. Choate came to take her
3d lesson, Katy g. her 13th lesson.
I started them both upon their
mr's work & left them to go
to Phil. Rehearsal. Returning
at 12 o'clk. overlooked & worked
upon Katy's drawing & painted
upon Mr. Choate's painting
until after 1 o'clk — Then took
lunch & at 2 o'clk started to find
the Montrosians. Went to Metropo-
litan Priscilla — to St. Nicholas, no
nobody — Men up to the Derby
Gallery to see French pictures —
disgusted — back to Metropo. &
found Priscilla & Fan Mulford — now
with Fan to St. N — Saw Salome W —
Mr. & Mrs. Hurd. Rte to Metropo.
— then to dinner — Evey at Phil.
Rehearsal Contest (4s)
