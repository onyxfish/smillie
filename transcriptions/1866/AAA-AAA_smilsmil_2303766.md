## Left Page

### Saturday, June 23, 1866.

An almost perfect day, waked at about 4 o'clk A.M. & heard the birds morning service — then went to sleep again & did not get up until so late as to derange breakfast matters. At 8 o'clk, Miss D. went alone over to West Stockbridge for her Father, Returning with him at about 10 o'clk A.M. Then until dinner time, inspected corn-ploughing by machine — the young turkeys — hens, chickens & garden. After dinner, were successful in doing nothing until nearly 4 o'clk, when we started Croquet — Miss D & Gel. against Miss J & myself. We played two games & came out just even. Besides M[illegible] work mentioned all four of us, with the Dr. wandered out to the Rye field & into the Pine woods, Drove into town for the Eve[nin]g mail with Miss Ireland — Eve[nin]g wandering about in moonlight. Means to give Miss D. my photo. Never to quarrel with her again —

## Right Page

### Sunday, June 24, 1866.

A day no less perfect than yesterday in spite of prognostications of bad weather M[illegible] D[illegible]. Miss D & Gel. started, walking for church. About half way, I found an Elm tree, shade & a seat, I begged to be left to enjoy alone the pleasure. I sat there until after 11 — the air — view — songs of birds & tolling bell just heavenly. went up to the church — walked home with the party — & finishing to me, a delightful M[illegible]. After dinner gave up to lazy enjoyment of the day — do not recollect for years such unmixed pleasure. Later in the afternoon, Misses D & J & Gel & I went down to the Pine woods where met the Dr. — played with the Newport calf — visited with the old mare & had a good time generally until supper. After tea, more wandering about in the moonlight — pacing the Verandah — etc. etc. but the party so little the victims of Romantic lunatic sentiment as to be able to Retire in good order at about 10.30 P.M.
