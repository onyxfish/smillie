## Left Page

### Thursday, June 21, 1866.

All day (very warm & cloudless) doing up the messages that must be attended to before starting for the Summer.

Mrs. about the Studio, P.m down town, Saw Menger — A.B.N. Co. &c. — &c. Got $40.00 for drawing Ohio Arms. Sent "Eagle's Nest" and a little 6¾×9 in canvas "Cascade among the Catskills" to Snedecor. We called for a few moments at Mrs. Ireland's & there met Ned Dunning. Returning home commenced packing about 10 o'clk P.M. — finishing at about 1 o'clk A.M.

## Right Page

### Friday, June 22, 1866.

Up at 6 o'clk — Pkpt. & B. by the 8 o'clk Boston train over the New Haven R.R. Reached Bridgeport at 10.08 — & then took the Housatonic cars at 10.30. Took dinner at Kent at 12.30 & reached Lenox Station at about 3 o'clk P.M.

The day had been delightful, cloudless & warm — but from 10 am. weather threatening to be seen in the east. When half way between Lenox Station and Lenox Village — met Misses Dunning and Ireland out driving. They anticipated us at the Hotel so as to give us the advantage of a ride from town to the Dr's house. Just as we all got fairly in, the threatening storm came down splendidly — it continued sprinkling & wet-looking through the evening. Met Miss Apthorp for the first time. Spent the even very pleasantly with some walking on the piazza — some music & general resting & chatting to fill in the spaces.
