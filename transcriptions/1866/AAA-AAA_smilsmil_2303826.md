## Left Page

### Sunday, October 21, 1866.

Not quite so mild as yesterday and with decided threatenings of change. Felt rather under the weather myself — so stayed in all the m'f, except a little walk on the Fair Ground before service. Wrote to Father and a note to Addie Dunning. Galusha a. Grow took dinner with us at 2 o'clk. Conversation almost entirely political — He has been stumping in Penn'a. and is now going into the state of N.Y. He stayed until about 4 o'clk. P.M. quite windy and overcast — loafed about indoors — Eve'f went with Sarah & Ge. to the Pres. [illegible] Ch. & heard a S. Post in the absence of Mr. Miller — Rather tedious, I must confess. After every service, a walk around by the Foundry Road — then home — some more writing & to bed about 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Monday, October 22, 1866.

Dull overcast morning — the change seems to have come at last — wind blows & the few remaining leaves come down. At noon a spirt of rain then a change again — P.M. Clouds broke away & glorious sunset — mild and clear — moon rise to match — Set palette in the m'f & painted in "October among the hills of Northern Penn'a." 10½×18 in. intended for Mr. Horace Winans — Painted in the parlor with all the disadvantages of imperfect light — light grey paper'd walls, etc, etc — Got the sky cold, & distance too colorless, but on the whole am pretty well satisfied with my day's work. Eve'f went across the way to Mrs. Dud. Lathrop's with Sarah & George & there met Del. Raynesford, Mrs. Streeter, Salome Watrous, Josie Beutly & Vic. Dimock — Dan Sayre & [illegible] Williams coming in late. At 9 o'clk. Ge. went down the hill with Sarah & I escorted Salome & Mr. Streeter to the Hotel — joined S. & G. walked around by Foundry Road then home. To bed at 10.30
