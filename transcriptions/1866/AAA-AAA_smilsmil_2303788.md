## Left Page

### Monday, August 6, 1866.

Dark, bleak morning — at 9 o'clk
Geo. and I started out + walked
past Mr. Belden's under Bald
Head — then struck across mead-
ows + woods up to a high ledge
of rocks — the climb was exceed-
ingly rough + very wet. Reached
the tops a little after 10 — was
disappointed in the view — went
on through the woods to Bald Head
— Saw the Catskills + Mt. House — then
back home — a bath then rested
until dinner. After dinner
sketched in-doors + Read until
4 o'clk. — Addie D + Miss Dana came
up for us to go down to the small
pond with them, went down + helped
get them afloat, then Returning
went to Mr. Tappan's for my class.
Only the two Misses T. were there. Gave
them a lesson in-doors on the use
of pencils + brushes generally —
Ever Mr. R. Goodeman lectured on
Wit + Humor. But feeling tired I
stayed home + went to bed early.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, August 7, 1866.

Clouds and sunshine + very high wind.
Rather cool to sit quiet out of doors, but
delightful for light exercise. Took pencils
\+ worked under the Elms, drawing some
of the White Pine tops in the wood beyond.
Very much inconvenienced by the wind.
At 11 o'clk. D. D. Addie, Miss Dana + Mr. + Mrs.
Trimble came along. went with them into
the wood — Addie worked up on her pencil
study of old Hemlocks. Afternoon, as
the wind was still high, went into the woods
\+ started a new pencil study of the bottoms
of the old Hemlocks. Worked until near
5 o'clk P.M. — then by invitation went
to D. D.'s to have a game of Croquet —
Addie + Mrs. T. off visiting — Miss Dana
asleep. — played with Mr. T. — near
6 Addie + Mrs. T. came — Geo. + I invited
to tea — Stayed after more than enough
hesitation — After tea Croquet — Geo.
Hawes, Miss Dana + Mr. T. against Addie
\+ me (two balls) — played until it was
quite dark, but our side beat — The
rest of the even'g after a little singing, we
all told ghost stories. D. D. went
to N.Y. this P.M.
