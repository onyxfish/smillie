## Left Page

### Saturday, May 26, 1866.

Pleasant weather still continu[ing]
but there are threatenings of
change — Mr. [Kuntz], for whom
I am making the Denver City
drawing called this morning,
Expressed himself pleased.
Worked all day (4th day) upon
it striving to get some effect.
It looks very pale-faced &
washy to me.

Eveg at home, mounting
the drawing — Rec'd a letter
from Father — he is well,
but evidently lonesome.

## Right Page

### Sunday, May 27, 1866.

Very heavy & squally Rain
during the mrg., clearing
up before noon — Afternoon
quite pleasant. Almost
used up again with a head
ache — so that all day I
sprawled about in-doors.

Afternoon wrote a few words
to Father — Heavy Thunder
storms between 10 & 12 o'clk
P.M.
