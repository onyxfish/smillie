## Left Page

### Monday, February 5, 1866.

Cold, clear & bright — Finished the first painting (11th day) of Academy picture the last being the right hand corner foreground trees.

There is much now in it that gives me encouragement altho' at present it is all out of keeping, the sky cold, the landscape hot and yellow — middle-distance strong, foreground weak etc, etc — Mr. Rec'd. a note from Robt. Hinshelwood, enclosing the envelope in which I sent my note of Jany 16th to him, Requesting me to add Mr. to it & return it to him. — Put it in the stove — P.M. Father handed me a letter mailed from Newark. N.J. Care, Am. R.R. Co. — It was another long letter from R.H. — I handed it to Geo. to Read, and if there was anything very entertaining in it, he was to communicate it to me — But he Reports that it is simply foolish. Eve'g at home with Father, Mother & Kate.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, February 6, 1866.

Fine clear cold day. Mother and Kate started at 9 o'clk. A.M. for Montrose. Painted all day on a little River & meadow bit (6×10 in.) for Mrs. Greatrex. It is to be finished in one painting, that her young lady pupils may the more Readily copy it. In the afternoon, she came in, saw it & expressed herself much pleased & put down at $20.00 bill for it.

A very good days work for me. The painting I think the most Successful single-paintings that I have done. Eve'g visited Miss Broadlaw at my Dr. Jns. Draper's — No. 13. E. 30th St. Spent an hour & a half pleasantly & at 9.30 Ret'd to the Studio, wrote a business letter or two, then home to bed.
