## Left Page

### Friday, January 12, 1866.

Dull, lowering day, thawing and very sloppy under foot.
Painted in (1st day) "a Bit in the woods — Banks of the Hudson."
I am quite pleased at what I consider my success in getting
delicacy & transparency of color with freedom of handling.
I find such difficulty in getting natural color without painting
very low in tone that my painting is generally heavy, thy want
of experience in manipulation and engraver's tendency to
finish makes my work hard &
metallic. To-day, I think a movement in the right direction.
Even Rained hard, went to the N.A. of Design to hear Dr.
Rimmer of Boston lecture on Artistic Anatomy. The Room
was overcrowded & suffocating so that about 9 o'clk, the lecture
was brought to a premature close.

## Right Page

### Saturday, January 13, 1866.

A sort of an April day, very mild, muggy, & towards evening,
showery. In the mng worked for an hour or more. & finished
outlining (4th day.) my Academy pic.
Then set to work upon "September
10½×18 in. belonging to Mr. Jacob
Wyckoff. (7th day.) I worked about
a scultite of black out of it, painting grey tones all through it —
This now I think, the best
painting so far that I have done
but it will hardly be popsulars.
It suffers in some respects by
contrast with its mate, picture
painted by Geo — a very light, grey
Autumn picture. I will
ask Mr. Wyckoff to let me
sell it for him. In the
afternoon went to Phil. Rehearsal
In the mng Katy G. took her
6th drawing lesson.
Eveng at home.
