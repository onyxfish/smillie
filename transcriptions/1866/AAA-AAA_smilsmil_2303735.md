## Left Page

### Saturday, April 14, 1866

Overcast and chilly, with mizzle in the eveng. "Varnishing
day" at the N.A.D. Mr. Choate
came & took her 8 lesson. Katy
G. took her 17. — At about 11
o'clk went over to the Academy.
Geo. & I found our pictures hung
first rate — Mine really looks
better than I had expected.
Ret'd at about 1 o'clk. — Painted
for Mr. Choate nearly an
hour — then got lunch and
ret'd to the Academy again
to take a quiet look at the
pictures. Ret'd to the studio
after 5 o'clk — Eveg at
home. Made a chalk
drawing "Varnishing day."

## Right Page

### Sunday, April 15, 1866

Breezy day, rather overcast.
Mrng went to near Jo.
P. Thompson — Afternoon
took a long walk in Central
Park — Eveg, too tired to
go out, much to my disap-
pointment as I had wished
to hear J. P. T.'s farewell
sermon before his departure
for Europe — He expects
to sail next Wednesday
with his wife and two
daughters —
