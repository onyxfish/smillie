## Left Page

### Saturday, February 17, 1866.

Clear and very cold — Myf, sat again for Boof, about an hour and a half — He painted over the coat, very much to the advantage of the portrait, taking away much blackness. It is still too black, though. Katy G. took her 10th lesson — Mrs. Choate called & insisted upon being Rec'd as a pupil. She got no definite answer, but promises to come again.
P.M. Charcoaled, upon a Storm "among the Catskills" (1 day) finished the outline in turpentine. — Also, "under the bough" 7×12. & commenced with charcoal a little "morning" 6×10 in. for Mortimer Smith. Wolf at the Studio washing up — Dorman came in & spent an hour with me — at 9.30 went home met Miss Baldwin, now residing with Mr. Myckoff.

## Right Page

### Sunday, February 18, 1866.

Overcast myf, Rain beginning at noon & continuing through the night — Myf. went to Thompson's and heard an excellent discourse on taking heed to my ways — but my head hurt & I have an influenzaish fever upon me —
After dinner, went to the Studio and passed a miserable afternoon napping it in my big chair — pain in head much increased — at 5 o'clk went home — prepared for myself a cup of tea — tried to write but had to give it up.
