## Left Page

### Wednesday, June 13, 1866

overcast and muggy, with
several showers during the
day — Night, Thunder storms
all about and over us.
Worked all day (1/2 day)
on the Twilight 9×12 that I
painted in one afternoon
some two or three weeks ago —
By perching an eagle upon
a prominent crag & building
a brush heap nest & laying
four eggs in it, I have produced
a subject that I call "the Eagle's
Nest" — quite novel — I am
told that it is pretty good for
me — Evey went to Emma
Ireland's to tell her that we
hoped to start for Lenox about
the middle of next week — She
goes next Friday — then went
to 162 W 22d St. and spent
the rest of the every very
pleasantly with Mr. & Mrs.
Jas. M. Hart.

## Right Page

### Thursday, June 14, 1866

Still overcast — muggy, chilly
hot, Raw and Sticky — with
occasional showers — Set my
palette in the m'fg, but had only
time to paint in my Eagle when
Augero announced that he was
ready for the 2d sitting on my
portrait — I sat for him until
4.30 P.M. — making a hard day of
it — He has produced, I think,
a very excellent likeness —
After sitting, I stretched paper
for some drawings that I am
about to do & did up some little
"chores" about the Studio.
Evey at home doing the
whole of nothing.
