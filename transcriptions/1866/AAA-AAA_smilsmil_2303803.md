## Left Page

### Wednesday, September 5, 1866

Another, foggy, muzzling drizzling day.
Got up with a confirmed headache,
fought it out all day, part of the time
on the bed & part of the time at my
work. Set palette & painted over
sky, worked generally on distance
& middle distant trees on upright
elm study 12×8 (day) Have it
all ready now to go to work upon
out of doors. Provided it ever
clears up. Afternoon cleared.
Very warm south wind. & sky
full of clouds — Gel went out but
soon came in unable to work —
He went over to Rosalie Goodman's
but she was out — I was unable to go —
Eveg, before 8 o'clk, very heavy Rain &
wind storm passed over us from N. W.
to S. E. — heavy thunder storm south of us
— over in about an hour and a half,
— passed the eveg. pacing a
narrow strip of the piazza & trying
to keep out of the rain — to bed
at 10 — feeling pretty well used
up.

## Right Page

### Thursday, September 6, 1866

Up bright & early with a dull cramp-
ing in my vest pocket — Morning clear
cold and windy. Right after breakfast
Gel started off with Mr. Smith — He is going
to the River at Seurs depot to make
some pencil sketches. At 9 o'clk went to
the elms & worked (4½ nof) upon fine
tree study, painting in foreground & small
hemlocks — The wind blew very hard, at
times carrying away my umbrella and
throwing over my easle in spite of me,
but I worked on, in spite of it — At
noon Rec'd. a salute from some ladies
on a load of lumber. Afternoon
took a short time at old hemlocks
with the pencil, — at 4 o'clk went to
Mr. Tappens with the young ladies
continued the sketches commenced
last Monday — I took Miss Hooper's
sketch & worked upon it & the others
overlooking, until sundown. Eveg,
got a fill of fine fresh blackberries
after a proper rush, set to
work pacing the piazza —
glorious, clear, cool, quiet
eveng.
