## Left Page

### Thursday, April 12, 1866

Still mild and clear. Painted (2° day) on "Noon"
6½ × 9 in. for Mortimer Smith.
Think that it is in some
respects now the best little
thing that I have done yet,
altho' inclining to blackness.
To-day am rather under
the Spring weather. At
4.30 o'clk went to Mr. Jno.
Taylor Johnston's — No 8. 5th Ave.
Invitations Read from 2 to 6 o'clk.
P.M. Found a spacious house
fine suites of Rooms — a library —
a charming gallery, very
choice pictures — plenty of
artistic "big bugs" — Church —
Darley — Bierstadt — etc., et.
& plenty of table-truck. Cham-
-pagne Punch, so on & so forth.

Went after 9 o'clk at Mr.
Jno. E. Gavitt's.

## Right Page

### Friday, April 13, 1866

Mf threatening, with sputtering
of Rain — Cleared off cloudless
between 10 & 11 o'clk — Jno. E. Gavit
called at studio about Guano
drawing. I commenced the
outlining but am quite used
up by Spring weather, vertigo
etc. etc. — So got it but
partially completed.

Went at home.
