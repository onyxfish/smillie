## Left Page

### Monday, October 15, 1866.

Glorious day — after at first — went to Smith's Cabinet shop & spent a couple of hours or more fixing up a stout frame work for a Coffee mill in which to crack wheat — In this Carpentering most of the morning was occupied. After dinner at 2.45 Mr. & Mrs. Harris — Geo. & Sarah in a carriage — I on Tarbell's mare, got started down the South Road — went to the 5th Road that turns to the Right on leaving town — went up a hill, let down bars, crossed a meadow to a higher piece of ground & enjoyed a glorious view — the Carriage lead then I Ret'd home & I went on to Sarah walking — notion — Continued across a mile or two — was Rewarded by some Charming views — horse good — day good — country good — me good — everything good. a Ride ever to be Remembered — out until after Sunset. a good wash & change — tea — eve'g at home. Vic Dimock & Mrs. and Lathrop called — 9 o'clk to Fan M's — there until near 11 — Whisld. Cocoanut Taffy & some Pombine.

## Right Page

### Tuesday, October 16, 1866.

Some more glorious — all the m'g seriously at work hunting up a horse for side saddle. at length made agreement with Ed. Weeks to use his mare every fine P.M. for a week or so. Dinner at 12 & at 1 o'clk Mr. & Mrs. Harris. Nurse & Children started for Scranton & Wilkesbarre, where they will stay for a day or two. Geo. Rode down to the depot with them. Sarah & I on horseback & Uncle Wm. & aunt H. in their old buggy, we went on the Summerville Road to the top of the long hill beyond the mill pond, then crossed by road through the woods to New Milford Road, then on & across to Plank Road by Hart Lake — Met Joe Williams. in Wood Road, he left us beyond Hart Lake, then met W. & Mrs. Comstock out driving. — Left Plank Road near Chamberlain's & got onto it again by Toll gate — then home, at 5.45 — Started at about 2.30 — a most glorious ride — all serene and well pleased — the new horse under side-saddle doing well — I Rode Tarbell's horse. Oct effects never to be forgotten. Eve'g at 10 o'clk. went to Fan M's out at Ned Bacon's — oysters — Met them — moonlight walk — home at half past 11. & to bed.
