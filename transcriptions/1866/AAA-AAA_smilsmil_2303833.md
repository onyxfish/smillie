## Left Page

### Sunday, November 4, 1866.

A novemberish sort of a day — half overcast with chilly wind — At about
10.30 Started for Uncle Aaron's at Manhattanville + walked it, about
4 miles, getting there at near 1 o'clk.
Found Mother waiting for me. But Uncle
A. had driven down to the City for me,
as my headached. I walked about
until dinner time which was 2 o'clk.
Had a very good dinner + enjoyed
it, being quite set up by a very strong
cup of tea. Chatted until near
5 o'clk, then with Mother + "Toony"
walked out awhile. At 5.15 Started
for home. Walked over to 8th ave +
124th St + took Cars to 59th St., then
walked home — walking two miles +
riding 3 — getting home at 6.20.
Folks at tea. Eveff went to
B'dway Tabernacle + heard Prof.
or Dr. or Rev. — Shedd — Must confess
that I was more occupied thinking
of other things than of what he said,
so cannot say critically what
I thought of his discourse.

## Right Page

### Monday, November 5, 1866.

Cold and windy. About the house
for some time with the painters — see-
ing to tints for ceilings etc, etc. At
about 10 o'clk went down town, stopping
at picture places, the printer of "Rocky
Mts" etc. etc. Went to Constable's the paper
hangers. Returning, stopped at different
places looking for paper for parlors.
Home to dinner at 1.30. After dinner
again with the painters — they have tinted
my ceiling — have come to the conclusion
that I don't know much about it. At
3.30 again went to Studio. Addie
Dunning made us a call, between 4
+ 5 o'clk. Home to tea at 6. Matt +
Kate, gone to Uncle Aaron's. Eveff,
with Joe went to Mr. Diven's, No.
W 52d St. Amanda out at a concert
with her bro. Her Father, Mother,
sister May + friend Miss [illegible] — J.
Elmira introduced themselves to his
+ we had quite a chatty evening
until 9.30 o'clk — home + found
Dr. Throop — went to bed at 10.30 and
left him still visiting.
