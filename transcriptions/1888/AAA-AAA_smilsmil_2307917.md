## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 7, 1888.

A bright & beautiful day — very hot in the sunshine but a refreshing
sweet breeze blowing constantly.
Uplat, feeling seedy so that I
did not attempt any studio work.
my head too uncomfortable — I
worked until near noon putting
up wire screens for vines to run
upon on the west front of the house.
Dinner at 1. After that a nap.
Then a long ride, discovering a
new road N.E. of us — It was a
very delightful afternoon for driving.
Home at 7.15 Tea — a late evef.
Annie read aloud some Homer
& we got to bed abt 1.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 8. 1888.

*Mary ret. to Buffalo with her children*

Very hot with a steady south wind
In the mf. I laid an etching
ground on my plate for "The
Goldsmith's Daughter" Etching
after Danl. Huntington. On
acct of Mary's departure we had
dinner soon aftr 12. — Herrick
drove them ner to New. Milford,
starting a few minutes before 1.
I took a nap between 2 & 3 &
then again to work on tracing
of Goldsmith's Daughter getting
everything ready for transfer
to morw mf. Tea soon aftr
6. between 6 & 7 o'clk — a
delightfully refreshing thunder
shower. After it I went
down Town to attend to messages
Evef. Reading newspaper,
& to bed about 10.30.
