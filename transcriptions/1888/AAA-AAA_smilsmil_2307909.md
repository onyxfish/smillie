## Left Page

### July, Sunday 22, 1888.

A delightful day altho' somewhat warm. Up late — Bkf. about 9 — I lounged about + wrote quite a letter to Mary — Dinner at 2 — out under the maples with the rest of the family — then a little more writing — Soon after 6 the horses were brought to the door + Sue, Katchen, Annie + I went out for a sunset + moon rise ride — we went to Fairdale + Ret. over the Ridge Road + Butterfield's Hill — getting in soon after 9 o'clk — having had a very delightful ride — then the party had cake + milk — It was 11 o'clk when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 23. 1888.

*Sue + family started for Ridgefield*

Up before 5 o'clk — a charming day — Bkf. at 6·5 — mall came at that time + took away the trunks + the party got off with Herrick at 7 — taking 8·20 train to NY, + hoping to catch 5 o'clk train to Ridgefield. I spent the rest of the day doing up "chores" that have been neglected for some time — Part of the mt. downtown — a quiet dinner at 1 — I must confess that it was very restful to me — the constant noise of the children got to be annoying. Spent most of the afternoon in studio fixing curtains + clearing out Bay window — Tea at 6·30 — Eve + putting water on young trees + such work — then, nearly an hour on Piazza in the moonlight.
