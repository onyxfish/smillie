## Left Page

### October, Saturday 6. 1888.

A horrible day of fog & heavy rain —
my neck continued to get much
worse, sent for Dr. Richardson who
came at dinner time. In the
m'f I was able to do a little
work on drawings for oak bye room
window seat. In the after-
noon I was not able to do any-
thing & by 6 o'clk. was nearly
wild with the pain in my neck
& the impossibility of finding any
position that would give a moment
of comfort. Took an anodyne
(powder that Dr. R. left for me) went
to bed & passed a troubled
night.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 7. 1888.

Did not get out of my bed —
the weather reported to be dis-
agreeable — very heavy & stupid
all the m'f. Some pains at the
base of my skull, exquisitely pain-
ful — like an exposed nerve — I
spent the day alternating short
naps with intervals of fruitless
efforts to find a position in which
I could lie without pain — again
sent for the Dr. He brought a little
Veratras unguent & applied it to
my neck. Another night of
trouble.
