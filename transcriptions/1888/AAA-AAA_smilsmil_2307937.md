## Left Page

### September, Sunday 16, 1888

Strong east wind during the night — spitting rain in the mg & heavy showers in the afternoon, settling into a steady wet — As usual I spent some time in the garden among the vegetables — then took a nap — then tried to fix a loose tile, over bed room fire place — more tiles came down, making bad worse, gave up in disgust & had dinner at 2 — after some working about, when it settled down to rain I settled down to write. Tea at 6.30 & in the eveng I wrote again finishing letters to Kate, Chas, Geo, Stokes & Bro & partly writing one to Genl. A. S. Diven — on the death of his son Eugene & his daughter-in-law Mrs. Geo. Diven — To bed at 10.50.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 17, 1888

A very warm, cloudy wt. Rain began to fall afternoon & continued heavily during most of the afternoon & evg. Chas. Wright arr'd from Ballyhack before 10 o'clk. & at 10 Herrick got away with the Greys for New-milford taking Edith & her cousin both bound for Phila. I got to work on my dry-point "Poppies" & took four proofs, working on the plate between pfs. Dinner at 1 & after that took four more pfs. I think that now I will do nothing more to the plate — before tea had a rest — after tea went downtown & attended to various messages — Evg read papers & to bed about 10.30.
