## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 4, 1888.

A very hot day & clear — [illegible] after
8 — To work on Burns etching, laying
out a sketch for the lettering, much
of my time however was spent with
Charl in arranging the rooms for
photos. Charl lunched with the
Parke's — going at 12.30 — returning
at 3. I tried to lay a rebliting
ground but used up the last drop
of my turpentine without success —
being 4th of July — no stores were open &
I had to give up. With Charl
again who worked at his photograp-
ing until after 6 — Tea at 7 — Eve —
Annie & I called upon the Woodin-
house at 9.15 — Charl went to call on
[illegible] Lathrop. About 11 where
we got to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 5. 1888.

*Charl Returns to N.Y.*

Early in the mfg, that is, about
7 o'clk. thunder storms began
to threaten, — a very few drops fell
— the sky continued clouded and the
air was close & hot. Went down
from about 9 A.M. Charl got away
for N.Y. about 10 o'clk. To drive over
to New-Milford — I laid rebliting
ground on Burns & fussed with
rebliting until the afternoon — then
I set to work with graver and dry-
point — Thunder storms were as
thick as black berries in the after-
noon — one or two passed over us
about 4 o'clk — As soon as they had
passed the family went out riding
— the air was deliciously cool and
fragrant — Back before 6.30 — then
tea — at 8 went downtown — back
before 9 — Read news papers — &
Whis­tler's "10 o'clk".
