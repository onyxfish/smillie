## Left Page

### June, Monday 18, 1888

As hot as ever — to studio before 9 o'clk & by 11 had my big case packed — S. R. Koehler came in at about 10, 40 — & just as I was leaving the studio, Bernian, for whom I had been waiting for a week, hailed me, but I had to rush to meet Mary & children at 11, 15, & so, made an appointment with him for to-morrow, P.M., then went & met Mary & attended to her baggage & home at 11, 30 — Spent the rest of the [illegible] finishing packing of papers & books — then lunch — the afternoon (except when I napped) was spent getting things together — Dinner at 6, 30 — a full table — Eve'g. S. S. Parker put in an appearance — Mr. Winans called & I nearly finished packing large case — dressed Annie's breast — the raw surface still spreading & to bed after 11.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 19, 1888

*Kate, Mary & the girls sailed for Bremen today.*

A great improvement in the weather — a cool breeze — B'kfst about 8 — before 9 went to studio, finished packing, screwed down my boxes, went to Gurney's &c — then to 2nd Nat. Bk & deposited $[illegible], Rives' check — then, at 11, 30, to Hoboken to No. German Lloyd's St. Emis — where, soon after 12, I met Charl. & party & Tom Shearman & wife. The vessel was crowded — At 1 o'clk. precisely the steamer started, our folks getting off in very good spirits — Annie, Charl. Ed. P. & I (with Jim) ret'd. by carriage. A & I stopped at Flint's & bought another ink & a bureau for Montrose — then home to lunch at 2, 30 — at 3, 30 ret'd. to studio — Bernian called by appointment & we talked about repairs to studio — I finished up last things at studio and then home at 6 — dinner — Ed w't. Cook with us — Eve'g I packed big case until 9 o'clk — about 10, dressed Annie's breast — in condition of which again concerns me very much — to bed at 10, 30.
