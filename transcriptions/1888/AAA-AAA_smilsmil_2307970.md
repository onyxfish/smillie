## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 21. 1888.

A clear, crisp winter day — Got to studio at 10 & worked until near 1 on India ink drawing, portrait of Annie's father — then to Bk's and deposited $175 just Rec'd from the A.B.N. Co. Home to lunch — back to studio at 2.30 & worked until near 5 on portrait drawing — A plumber came in to look at the water basin complaint having been made of bad smells in the building. I sent check for $150 to Wm Benian. After leaving studio I met C.A. Platt & with him went to Ortgies Gallery to see Etchings published by Socy of Am. Etchers — a very poor lot. Thence home to dinner — Evett at 8.30 — to meeting of Am. Water Color Socy. Home at 10.30. Annie spent the evett at her bro. George's.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 22. 1888.

Cold & clear — To studio at 10 & spent the m'f finishing the drawing of Annie's father — home to lunch at 1 & back before 3 — Spent the afternoon in looking over some of the unfinished oil paintings that I have & in making preparations to begin work on some of them — At 4.30 went down to W. J.D. Waring's — 19 E. 16th St. to get a statement from him of sales made of miscellaneous etching [illegible] that I let him have last June. The sum total reached the grand amt of about $14.00 where, from what he had told me of sales I had expected about $100 — then to Bisbee's for new boots — not yet done. Home — dinner at 6.30 — Evett in front of a fine fire — part of the time Annie read aloud from Robert Elsners — To bed at 10.
