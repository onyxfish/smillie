## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 10, 1888

Brilliant cold & windy — Ther. fell
last night to 27°. My neck very much
better — I bundled up & spent some
time out of doors — at one time raised
Jim's Kite for him — I did some work
on drawings for oak bed Room window
seat but got stuck at one part — I
was much bothered by my eyes
& at night found the right one con-
siderably congested and a strange
looking spot, like a solidified tear,
on the cornea, midway between
the left corner of the eye & the iris.
It did not hurt me but I was
much depressed — not knowing
what is or may be I do not know
what to look forward to — I
spent the every on big chair,
Annie reading aloud — To bed
at 10.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 11, 1888

much milder — overcast until evef when
the clouds to some extent broke — My
eye was a source of constant anxiety
to me all day but really caused me
no real inconvenience or any pain.
Had Herrick kindle a fire in studio
furnace — also in etching room — I
most of the day I spent in getting
my things ready to leave for the
winter — About 3 o'clk. Dr. Richardson
came to see my eye — he thinks it
a gathering of serum under the conjun-
ctiva — that it will not be absorbed, but
must be opened — he says wait & don't
be alarmed — Mr. Woodin & her niece
called — Then I wrote to Yale — Spent
some time with Decker directing the
washing of the horse's legs —

Ralph, who for the past 14 months has
been walking with the aid of a finger, started
off on his own account Today, walking con-
siderably, all alone & evidently enjoying
it as much as anybody.
Evef. Annie read aloud for awhile
To bed at 10 o'clk.
