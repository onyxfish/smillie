## Left Page

### June, Friday 22, 1888

Started for Montrose,

The hottest day of the season so far.
96° in the shade. Up soon after 5 —
but it was after 7 before we had our
Help! Every thing was done without
hurry-scurry (except that I had to go
to the butchers for our bb) and we
got away about 8°. Geo. Cook locking
up for us. Annie, Jim, Ralph, Greta
to in carriage to Hoboken — Kate
& Mary by street-cars. Gurney's
me took boxes & trunks at 7, 70
We did not suffer extremely
on the cars & the children did very
well — Ralph was perfection. Got
to Ne. Station at 2.55 Herrick & our greys
there for us & a team & wagon for the
girls & the trunks — the ride was ex-
tremely hot & dusty — it was after 5
when we got home — just as we got
in a spatter of rain — the southern
edge of a thunderstorm passed over
us — we had tea before 7 & got to
bed before 10 — Annie does
not seem to have been injured
by the fatigues of the day.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 23, 1888

A very hot day with a short but
furious storm of wind, rain and
thunder in the afternoon — Up early
& found many things to do about the
house — It was about 11 when at last
I got down town to attend to messages
At noon Herrick met me with the
team & took me home — Dinner
at 1 — Afternoon, until the storm
came, with Herrick putting up
awnings & piazza screens. At
6 I went down town with Jim —
Got a letter from Kirby saying that
my prices for etching August Bonheur
($200) was satisfactory — Tea about 7.
Evet'g, a little reading — Spent
half an hour dressing Annie's
breast, which to me seems to be
getting along satisfactorily — To bed
soon after 10.
