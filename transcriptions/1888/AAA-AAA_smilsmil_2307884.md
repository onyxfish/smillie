## Left Page

### June, Saturday 2, 1888.

Very pleasant day — up early & had bkfst. soon after 7 — then to Am. Spts. Co. with Mary's bundle — then to Prady & [illegible] shop & turned over the [illegible] & frames am. from Co'k. — then to Knordlie's & spent some time with Mr. Roose looking over Etching [illegible]. It was almost noon when I got to studio — To work on drawings & finish to 3d window in oak bed room at Montrose. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked on drawing for Herrick until after 6 — It was almost 7 when I got home to my solitary dinner — After that, sat & read until nearly — then walked down to Century Club — It was about 11 when [illegible], Nicoll, Dielman, Wood, Church, Freer & myself & the Doct'y of Am. Etchers met Mr. J. D. Waring in a small room & discussed the matter of giving bonds — (W. to Ass'n.) until after midnight — then walked up home & to bed at about 1 A.M.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 3, 1888.

Glorious day — made a mistake & got up at 6.30 & did not find it out until I went down for my bkfst. at 7.15 — wrote part of a letter to Herrick — took a short walk & at 8.30 had bkfst. Then read until 10 — then to studio & made sketch of wash room ceiling for Herrick — soon after 11 went to Century Club — met Frank Fowler, Ht. Ferguson & Dorrett & sat chatting with them until after 12 — then home, no one in. Kate & Mary off on a spree to Coney Island — I got my lunch as agreed & after it had a little nap — then wrote to Herrick, Tarbill, Ben Baldwin (about Mrs. Crossman's piano) to Wm. Douglass and to Mary — out about 4.30 & went up to Central Park, met Charl. at the Belvidere and we sauntered through the Ramble to 72d. St. gate & took 'bus. I got home to dinner at 7.15 after it. Read until almost 9 — then Charl. came in — stayed until near 10 — then I went to bed.
