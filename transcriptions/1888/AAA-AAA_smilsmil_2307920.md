## Left Page

### August, Monday 13, 1888

A bleak, cold, cloudy, windy
day. Fires were burned all
day in large Room & Studio —
I spent a good part of the
my etching upon "Goldsmith's
Daughter" — & again in the
afternoon until 4 when we
went out riding — I got A.
& Jim at Agur Lastrak's & we
all bundled up as though it
were winter — I wore a heavy
overcoat & it was not too much.
Home at 6.15 Tea — Eve'g
Reading Sunday & Monday "Trib"
To bed before 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 14, 1888

Glorious day — clear & cool. Ther.
fell to 47° last night — After bkf'st
went to Beach, Brown & Co. to see if, in
their machine shop, they could fit a
new box to an axle on the carriage
— then attended to several messages
— got to work at my etching (Gold-
smith's Daughter) at 11.15 & worked
until 1 — & after dinner until 4 —
putting in the sky — then out to
ride with Annie & Jim — went
to the Christman's — found only
Mr. C. at home — Took him out
riding with us — home at 6.30
Tea — Glorious moonlight night
— Eve'g Read newspaper until
9 — then Annie read aloud
Cable's "The Negro Question",
until 10 o'clk — then to bed
leaving the remainder to be
finished at some other time.
