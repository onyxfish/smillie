## Left Page

### January, Sunday 8, 1888

Dull day, but no rain fell — The temperature quite mild until towards eve'g. when it began to clear & grow colder. Bkf. late, as usual — Then m'f spent in Reading & some planning for a studio printing Fable — About 1 took Jim out for a walk. on 5th Ave. met Will and walked a short distance with him — Dinner soon after 2 — About 4. Walter called with his children & with Annie & our boys we all went to Ed. Wm. Cooks in 37' 5. There Ralph became so sleepy at once that I rid home with him. Had a cup of tea after 7 — again a little planning on printing Fable — then wrote a couple of short notes — At 10 went to J. C. Del[illegible] — then took a short walk — home & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 9, 1888

Dull day — moderately cold — To studio soon after 10 — Spent some time at work upon the Rowsley drawing & then got it into to frame — It is a poor, common place thing; such as any one with a steady hand could learn to do in time. Then I blocked out, in charcoal, the design for another drawing, about 7×13 — cows up on a slope of hill side, feeding — then home & back at 2.30 — The rest of the P.m. was spent pottering over frames and lists & such things — At 5 went to N.A. & saw Farner — then to Station 7. 3d. Ave & 28' S. & mailed a copy of Huntington's Durand to Jno. A. O'Neill. Washington. Then to McKeever's. 42d S. near Broadway. Then to the butcher's. 3d. Ave. & 36' S. then home & had dinner at 6.30. Eve'g. Read aloud Rob. Louis Stevenson to Annie until Charl came in about 9 — then I worked a little on printing Fable plans — To bed about 11.
