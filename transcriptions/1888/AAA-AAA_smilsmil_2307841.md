## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 6. 1888.

A bright, cold, windy day — It was 10.30 when I got to studio. To work at once biting in my Stokes etching — "What o'clk. is it?" and Geo's invitation plate — At 12.15 went to Kurtz's & got small photo. of Alma-Tadema picture — then to Bank & deposited checks to co. of Annie & self — then to Parkinson's & ordered a 2d. doz. of photos. of children — then home to lunch — wrote to W. Marvin about delay in getting large photo. of the Alma-Tadema — To studio at 2.30 — Miss Verdaval came & worked on charcoal sketch of a head — I worked on my plates until near 6 without finishing the biting of them.
Dinner at 6.30 — Evelf. Charl came in & stayed until near 10. A season with Ralph, who went all night without his food rather than take it from a bottle. He surrendered in the mts. I got to bed before 11.30.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 7. 1888.

Overcast mts., clearing into a bright, breezy day — After 10 when I got to studio & resumed my biting operations. Soon got Geo's little invitation plate bitten in & ground cleared off. Then Mr. Marvin of Scribner's called & we had a talk about the designs in starting me on the Tadema plates — also, about the chances of having it etched larger — also, about some etched portraits of musicians wanted for a new work. It was nearly 1.30 when I got home to lunch — back at 2.30 — finished biting "What o'clk. is it?" & left studio soon after 5 — some time in Geo's studio — went to Grady & McKeever's to see about frames — home at 6.30 — dinner — Evelf. meeting at N.A. to discuss amendments to Constitution, but I stayed at home with my family & rested — got to bed at about 11 —
