## Left Page

### September, Sunday 2, 1888

A very pleasant day — for some
time in the garden — then about
the place until 11 — then tried to
take some photos — of the children —
experimenting with Carbutt plates
then developing my work until
2 o'clk — not very successful. Dinner
— went out Riding at 4 — back
soon after 6 — Evef. wrote letter
to Kate — took it to P.O. — at 9 o'clk.
To bed soon after 10 —

## Right Page

### September, Monday 3, 1888

A mildly overcast day. closing with
a very beautiful sunset — Up at 5.30 AM.
Help at 6.30 — Sophia Ireland left at 7
A.M. with Will Parke for depot, D.J. & W.
to her return home. Coyle came to his
work feeling sufficiently recovered from
his recent sickness — I worked about in
the garden & elsewhere until after 10
when I went to the foundery & purchased
a plow — then to P.O. — home at 12.15 —
dinner — about 3 o'clk. had the horses
hitched to the new plough but their
prosperity has spoiled them — they
refused to plough & I had to give it
up. In the aft. Mr Roger Searle
came & talked strawberries & cultivation
of the soil with me — in the afternoon, when
out for our ride, I drove over there & saw his
grounds & garden — Home at 6.30 — Tea —
Evef. Mr. & Mrs. Woodin called. left
at 9 o'clk. after which I read newspapers
until 10 o'clk. Annie used up with a
headache — she was desperately sick
from 10 to 12 — after which she rested
quietly — Rec'd a letter from Kate at
