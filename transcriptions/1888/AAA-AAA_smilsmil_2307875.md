## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 15, 1888

Cold, Raw, & Raining until every
when again the weather cleared.
Got to E. R.R. Station at 1 45" 6" — at
about 10 20 & there met Prof. Currier
& Deaf & Dumb Asylum — to which place
we drove — After a short interview with
D. Peet I spent most of what remained
of the m[?] with the Art Instructor, Mad.
La Prince looking over the work of the
pupils. then again with D. P., who was
very Kind & interesting — brought up a
young man to tell me some stories by signs
— very amusing — Home at 1:5 To Studio
at 2 45 & Painted on 20×24 until 6.30.
then home. Annie was able to get on a
dress on & go down to dinner — the first time
in three weeks — Ev.[?] Charl came
in for a time — I wrote a letter to [illegible]
& also the biographical notices of [illegible]
for Weitenkampf. To bed soon after
11.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 16, 1888

Cold & Raw with considerable Rain
during the day & a short clear up before
sundown — To studio at usual time but
felt very unlike work owing to a headache
Tried to drive myself to do something —
new & cheap frame came for the obelisk
painting & I painted a little while on
that picture — then a while on Lake George
study — Gifford came in about noon and
made a call — home to lunch & back at
2.45 — Made charcoal sketch on 15×24
for a pot boiler to help sell an sea frame
— fussed around until after 4 — then
went to Chas. Platt's studio — 115 E, 25° S: —
& sat with him for more than an hour —
back to studio & washed brushes — home
at 6.30 — Annie, doctor & nurse says, is
getting along remarkably well — Dinner —
headache and the cold makes my
ev[ening] rather miserable.
