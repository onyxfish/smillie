## Left Page

### July, Friday 6, 1888

*Sue & family came from Cambridge*

Delicious cool & breezy day. I worked
all the m'f on Burns etching with
dry point & graver — dinner at 1 —
Herrick went to Montrose depot to
meet Sue & family at 3 o'clk. I went
on with the work on etching & about 3
o'clk took p'fs one of which I dried
& sent by mail to Calverley in the
eve'g. It was after 5 when Herrick
arr'd with our visitors. Rob't. (Sue's
eldest son) arr'd later on the stage with
some of the baggage. The party were
all very tired, having come right
through from Cambridge — Tea
about 7 — Eve'g it was so mild
that we sat out on the piazza until
after 9 — Early to bed.

Had the grass on our meadow
cut this afternoon for hay.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 7, 1888

A warm, hazy day, with strong, dry
wind from the N. W. I took several
india p'fs from the Burns etching — The
ink was old & unsatisfactory — the ink's
were bright & hard. I worked at
it until dinner time at 1.

After that I spent a good part
of the afternoon loafing on a stone
wall under a big maple, watching
Herrick, with the greys & Coupe — getting
in the crops of hay in our meadow.
Then for a time worked on the re-
construction of the lettering on Burns
etching. It was about 5.30 when
with Sue, Katchen & Rob't I started
out for a ride — back at 7.30 — Tea.
Then I cleaned up things in the
printing room — cared for the horses —
dressed, & with Annie at about 9 o'clk
we went to Mr. Christiansen's where a
few friends were invited to aid Miss
[illegible] good bye. A pleasant little
c— Home at 11 —
