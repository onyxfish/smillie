## Left Page

### December, Saturday 15, 1888

A brilliantly beautiful day — got it with a wretched headache to which I was obliged to succumb after lunch — It was 10.30 when I got to the Studio — I packed 3 plt. of the [illegible] Tadema plate & one of my dry point & [illegible] went to Koppel's & showed them to him — was very warm in his praise of both plt. but did not offer to buy the dry point — then I went to Radtke & Haukers — I gave Mr. L. a plt. of the Alma Tadema — He gave me two plt. of English Etchings some time ago — then to Scribners — Mr. Marvin not in — saw Mr. Thomas — gave him a plt. & left Mr. M's a him. I was well rec'd — but my plt. was [illegible] looked at — there home to lunch — Marran Carter White there — then I went to bed — got up before 5 — found Mr. Joseph [illegible] of Bridgeport had come to stay over [illegible] to attend the funeral of Mrs Clinch monday mng. Dinner at 6.30 — then I was able to sit up — but Annie would not go to Mr. & H. Moses (Lucy Gibbons) a small company to meet Mr. [illegible] much to my disappointment as well theirs. To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 16, 1888

Arrived. Pleasant day, altho' somewhat overcast — Bkft. soon after 9 — about 11 escorted Annie to the church door & then, with Jim, walked on to the N.A. of S. & took a pretty careful look (the only one that I have had) at the Fall [illegible] that closed yesterday — It was a dispiriting sight to me — It was about 1 when I got home. Wrote a letter to Mr. Woodin à propos of the framed etching plt. that I have prepared for her. Dinner at 2 — just as we have finished, Charl. came in & I went with him to see the 69th St. house that we may take in exchange for the Terrace (Po'k) property. I am not sanguine of making a good thing out of the bargain, but almost anything is better than the P'k house. After going thro' the house (we met Kurts the photographer in it) we wandered out to West Side Ave. & Riverside to see the improvements — Home at 5 — Wrote a letter to Miss S.T. Halowell — then with the children until near 8 — Tea — & then again to work writing — which I continued until 10 o'clk — To bed at 10.30.
