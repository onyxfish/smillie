## Left Page

### June, Sunday 10. 1888.

*Marie, Walter Cook's wife, died.*

A very disagreeable day — Hot & muggy — alternating between sunshine and showers. Up at 7.30 — The day was spent with Annie in charge of the children as Greta went off at 10 A.M. to make a day fish — Before noon Annie went to Walter's & found that Marie was so seriously ill that she was not expected to live through the day. I spent what time I could in writing notes — Soon after 1 I went to Union League Club & with Cheve went to his room to see his new arrangement of pictures — Met his friend Will Lane — Home to dinner at 2. Later Walter's three children came — more visiting in the afternoon — Geo. & Emily came in after 6. Alice Ireland took away letters — many — Edw. & Gogo stayed over night with us. A cup of tea at 7 — Tonight met 9 o'clk with Ralph before he would go to sleep — About that time Geo. & Emily came in to say that Marie's end was very near — About 10 Walter came (with Mr. Valentine) to say that Marie had quietly passed away between 9 & 10 o'clk. I then went to G.C. Depot with letters — To bed about 11.

Marie is supposed to have died of cancer of the stomach.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 11. 1888.

Hot, humid & breezy. R[a]lp — at about 7.30 — Walter's children took the announcement of their mother's death with loud lamentations & ate no breakfast. I got to studio's about 9 o'clk. & found the fire-escape man waiting for me impatiently — After much scolding I gave in & let his masons in to knock half a dozen holes through the wall & upset things generally. I had to do considerable clearing away of furniture & curtains before they could get to work. Then I went to Kimmel & Voigt's & got my plate & [pf?] of "What O'clk is it?" again to studio & then home to lunch — Edith Wright there — after lunch — to studio — the masons through with their work in my room by 4 o'clk. Then I added to the remarque on Stokes' plate — Caldwell called before 5 & we went to Stokes where I delivered plate & proof & was fool enough to take his note at 4 months. But he promised to give me his check to-morrow if I did not like the note — with Caldwell went to Knoeders & Wunderlich's & saw a lot of etchings — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Annie in bed with a headache — Mrs Walter's Mary stayed with us. Every with the children who, one way & another took most of my time until 10.30 — To bed at 11.
