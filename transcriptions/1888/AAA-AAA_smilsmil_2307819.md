## Left Page

### January, Sunday 22, 1888.

Very brilliant & very cold — Ther.
down to 6° in the mf. Bkf.
about 9 — Sat by dining room
fire until after 11 — Then Geo.
came & together we went up to
73d St. — Saw Joe, Minn & Walter —
While there Dr. Guernsey came
in & we talked of the probable
cause of Will's death — He said
that W. had a weak heart — that
business strain & excitement was
too much for him, anticipating the
annual meeting on Friday the died
of Angina pectoris. — I walked home
along through the Park — getting in
at 2. — most of the afternoon with
Annie & the children — Many
arr'd from Po'k. at 6.30 — Tea
after 7 — Charl came in the eve'g
& we sat, chatting, until after 10.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 23. 1888.

*Will was buried To-day.*

Pleasant mf. — clouded & rain after-
noon. Bkf. about 8 — immediately after
a many went up to 73d St. At 10.30
Kate, Charl, Annie & I went to to the Mt.
Arthur Baptist Ch. in 57th St. near 6th Ave.
The funeral was to be at 11 — Mr. A. & Hepworth
made addresses in addition to service read
& singing by a quartette — a very large no.
of people were present — the number of
men was especially notable — To me, it was
a touching tribute to Will's lovabliness — The
services were over at about 12 — we stayed
for some time to see & speak with many old
friends — then home — lunch at 1 — Mary
Kate & Charl with us — At 2.05 Charl &
I went up to Woodlawn — Walter & Minn —
W's bro. & his wife — Junior & Chas. Gunther —
M. A. Shepherd & Col. Porter went with us —
The body was placed in the Receiving Vault. We
ret'd to the city at 4. I walked down to Studio
& back — Mary ret'd to Po'k. at 3.30. Dinner
at 6.30 — Eve'g. Charl came in —
no gas, owing to the cold — we chatted
until after 10 — then to bed.
