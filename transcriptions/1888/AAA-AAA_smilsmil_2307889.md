## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 12. 1888.

Breezy, bright & cooler — Bkf. before 8 —
Moran, the carpenter, came to arrange for
the partitions in the cellar. To studio at 9.30 —
then to J. A. Stokes & got check from him for
$100. Payment for my plates — Then to bank
& deposited it. To studio & spent the rest
of the mt clearing things to get away
for the summer. Charl sent up some
estate papers for Geo. & me to sign before
a Notary — at 1 o'clk. got Geo. & signed them
then home to lunch — found Ed. &
Mary Cook there, just in from Buffalo. To
studio at 3 & continued the work of the
mt. Home at 6 — Sue, Cook there, from
Cambridge — Dinner at 6.30 — Eve. very
much broken up with calls etc. Charl
came in & W. Williams to see Mary —
& Valentine & Geo. Cook — Edw., Mary,
Sue & Walter's Mary stayed with us
over night. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 13. 1888.

*Walter's wife was buried —*

Delicious bright & breezy mt. Bkf.
early — I went to studio & spent
the mt in [illegible] curtains & own
& generally getting things in summer
shape — Home to lunch at 1 — about
the house until about 2.50 — then with
Annie went to Walter's, 135 E. 37th S. —
where a few friends & Relatives met
& a very simple funeral service was
read by Dr. Collyer, after which there
was a dismal wait for nearly half
an hour before train time — a special
car took us up to Woodlawn Cemetery —
the grounds were wonderfully beautiful
with great masses of Rhododendron in flower.
The same car brought us back to 7th C. D.
at 5.50 — We had dinner at 6.45 — Mary
& Edw. — Sue & Nelse dining with us —
Mary & Edw. & Sue stayed all night. In
the eve. I packed away some of my
back Nos. of Ill. London news — To bed
before 11.
