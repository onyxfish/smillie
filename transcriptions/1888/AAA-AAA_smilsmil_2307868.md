## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 1, 1888

Heavy Rain in the m'f. overcast after
noon & ev'f. The Dr. came before 10
& I was present when Annie's breast was
dressed. I was horrified to find it a
crater-like looking wound more than an
inch across & nearly an inch in depth.
Then the Dr. explained to me that it was
more a carbuncle than an abscess. It
was after 10 when I went to Bellevue Hos-
pital — saw Miss Perkins & arranged for a
trained nurse, Miss Church, who came at
about noon. I went to studio for a few
minutes — at 12 rode up to Yale's — waited
½ an hour for him & then talked of Annie's
case — he reassured me — home at 1 —
Dr. Blackwell & Stimpson came at 2 &
held a consultation — Dr. S. tells me not to
be alarmed — at 3 I went to studio — then
for a couple of hours — then home —
Dinner at 6.30 — Kate dined at George's
Annie feeling more comfortable — Dr. B.
came in before 9 o'clk — Eve. & Geo.
& Charl came in — also Geo. Cook &
Chas. Collins. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 2, 1888

A raw, cloudy day — Dr. Blackwell came
soon after 9 & Annie's breast was dressed
she had a very good night's rest — I
had so many things to attend to that
it was near 11 when I got away from
the house — stopped at studio for a
moment — then to Deposit Co. & got cert-
ificates — took them to Drexel, Morgan
& Co. Wall St; paid final assessments on
Chesapeake & Ohio stock — then home at
1 — waited until nearly 3 before Dr.
Stimpson came who, with Dr. Blackwell
dressed Annie's breast & said that things
looked better. It was just about 4
when I got to studio — worked until
6 on large Water Color "A Group of Cows"
softening lines & work of that sort — then
to Einmer & Amend's cor. 18th St. & 3d Ave. —
Home at 6.30 — dinner — Eve. Kate
went out with Charl — Geo. came in
for a few minutes — I sat with Annie
& read — To bed soon after 10.
