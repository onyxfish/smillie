## Left Page

### August, Saturday 11, 1888

Very cool morning & cloudy —
gradually growing warmer
again & threats of Rain passing
away. Up late — spent some
time after bkfst cutting out the
old wood from the Raspberry
bushes — at 11 went down town
with Annie — called a Court Ho.
& consulted Clerk about Cornish
vs Tyrrell — other messages — had
True, the Jeweller, solder 4 needles
together that I may try them on
my Goldsmith's daughter etching —
After dinner worked on etching
until 3 — then a nap & soon after
4 went out with the family and
had a ride — on Return, at the
P.O. got letter from Mary — Lucerne —
July 29th — Eve. Reading news-
paper & a little writing. Rain
at 10 — heavy showers from 2 A.m.
until mng.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 12, 1888

Rain until about 8 A.m., mists &
clearing until 11 — a charming day
until after 4 when the weather
began to thicken again — Rain
commenced to fall before 7 P.M. &
continued steadily & quietly. In
the mt. I wandered about the
place for some time, then wrote a
long letter to Kate — Dinner at 2 &
then some more writing. Soon
after 4 the family went Riding
& ret'd a little before 6 — then Tea.
Eve. on Piazza for some time
then wrote a letter to J. Q. A. Ward
inviting him to visit us & started
a letter to Ehninger — To bed at
10.30 — heavy thunderstorm at
that time, passed near us &
much rain fell during the
night.
