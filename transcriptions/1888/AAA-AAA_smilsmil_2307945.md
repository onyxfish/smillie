## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 2. 1888.

*Geo. & family ret. to N.Y.*

Cold, disagreeable day, growing worse as the day advanced —
Cloudy with some driving showers in the afternoon — Geo. & family
got away for N.Y. a few minutes after 10 A.m., Henrick taking them
to New Milford for the 11.55 train.
I got to work at the biting of my Huntington etching & finished
it about 5 o'clk, & then for an hour or more was engaged cleaning
the plate — darkness came on so that I cannot know yet what
sort of an effect I have got —
Our little family had a quiet tea soon after 6. Evtf. with newspapers
& writing notes to Emma Bonwne,
Litte & Bridgeman, nurserymen —
To bed at usual time.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 3. 1888.

Very cold & bleak — a succession of snow squalls all day — The Ther.
fell last night to 30°. I spent
the m/f working on "The Goldsmith's
Daughter" — with Emery Palmer and
burnisher — Dinner at 1 — after
which John Doyle came & spliced
the rope for our hoisting tackle for
lifting fire wood to the bed room
floor — then I got to work pre-
paring to take p/p. to-morrow. W.
Parker came in & made a lengthy
visit — then Mrs P— At 5.30 I
went down town & got back at
6.20 — Tea — Evtf. — newspapers
& a little writing — sent check to
Giles Watrous.
