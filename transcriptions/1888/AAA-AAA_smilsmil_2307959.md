## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 30. 1888.

Bright, breezy & pleasant. after my
messages it was near 11 when I got
to studio — after being there for a short
time I went to the N.A.A. 6.2.25¢
& saw Thos. Kirby — then to 18th S. and
saw Lauckner about the Ranger
etching — then home, on my way stopping
in 2. 32d. S. & engaging Mr. Duff to come
to studio & clean up for me this p.m.
lunch — to studio about 2". the Gold-
smith's Daughter" arv. by Exp. soon
after — Mr. Duff came at 3 & slaved
away at things until 5 15 managing
to make a fairly even distribution of
dirt. Home about 6 — dinner at 6.30
Evett. all hands tired & little was
done. I found Shipper's receipt for
our goods sent as freight from New Milford
& sent it to Union Transfer Co. Wm
Douglas came in the evtg. & we
went over the house together looking
for painters' work to be done.
Annie read aloud for a little while.
To bed soon after 9.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 31. 1888.

Very pleasant day — Got to studio
about 10 Mr. Duff there cleaning the
windows. Went to Union Transfer Exps. &
then to 2d Nat. Bk. made a loan on
security of an Erie R.R. Bond anticipating
income of Jany., to pay indebtedness at
Montrose. Ret. to studio & worked until
1 o'clk helping Mr. D. clean up. Then home
to lunch — back at 3 & worked at the
same work until after 5 — freight arr.
from Montrose — on my way home stopped
to see how Geo. felt. He has been used up
to-day with an unusual & violent head-
ache — He had gone to bed & I went to
Dr. Wm Swift & asked him to see Geo. Dinner
at 6.30 — then unpacking things & hanging
pictures until 9 — then with Annie to
see Walter — there until 10 — then home.
To bed at 11.
