## Left Page

### June, Friday 8, 1888.

Still delightful weather — spent the m'f wandering about & with the children — Annie & I went to see an old house at a short distance from Dr. Adams's. It is for sale & we thought it might be a good thing for Geo. Smillie. After 12 I took a nap. Dinner after 1. Between 3 & 4 the old mare Fanny was hitched up & Nelie, Annie & I rode over to Alecw Weir's house to find that they will not be there until Monday — we returned by a different road getting in at 6 o'clk after a very pleasant ride. Then tea — after our evef. of the usual chat. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 9. 1888.

*Home again.*

Very pleasant & very warm — Annie & I concluded that we had better start for home in the m'f. so we took the 9.30 train & after a very hot, dusty and tiresome ride got home a little before noon. I went out & did some marketing etc — it was after 1 when we had lunch — At 2.30 I got to 2d Nat. Bk & made a deposit for Annie. Then to J. & C. Johnston's & bought some shirts etc. After 3 when I got to studio — Geo. & Nellie came in. I found that the [illegible] men are awaiting my return to commence knocking holes through my walls. I wrote Beman a note on the subject. Did not accomplish much else at studio — home before 6 — Dinner — Evef. at home working to get things in shape for a start at Montrose — we were much shocked to learn that Walter's wife, who has been ill for some weeks, suffering apparently from some gastric trouble, is likely soon to die. Charl came in about 9 o'clk, with another Oregon mf. Co. Bond. We got to bed at 11.
