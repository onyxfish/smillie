## Left Page

### November, Friday 9, 1888

Warm, wet day — with some heavy rain about 10 A.m. Depressing weather. Ralph, besides his cold, seems much troubled by his teeth & evidently feels very miserable. To Studio before 10 & got started on A.B.N. Co. drawing — Statue of Dom Pedro I with buildings and distant mts. a very picturesque subject but I have to compose it & I find it very troublesome. Home to lunch at 1 — Mr. Justice left us for home at 2.30 — then I ret'd — to Studio & worked as long as I could see — then for some time sawing & splitting up some old packing boxes, for kindling wood. Home at 6 — Eve'g looking over old Scnd. News not & snoozing in the bed.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 10, 1888

Dark & muggy with frequent deluges of showers culminating in a thunder shower at about 4 P.m. Spent a part of the m't. & of the afternoon making tracing & then retracing the So. Am. subject for A.B.N. Co. drawing "Statue of Dom Pedro I" with mountains & architectural surroundings — I got home at about 6 P.m. & was met by Annie who told me that Jim had had a very heavy fall about 3 o'clk — from the arm of a chair — striking the back of his head. He had been in a heavy sleep for nearly 3 hours, waking only to vomit. Yale had been sent for — I waited until after 7 & then went to Yale's house — just missed him — Ret'd & found him with Jim who seemed to be waking up — Y. said he was suffering from shock & was coming out of it naturally — He had a mild mustard plaster put on his stomach after which vomiting ceased & Jim got to be quite lively — He was got into his own bed before 10 & had a quiet night.
