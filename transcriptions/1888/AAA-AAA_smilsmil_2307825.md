## Left Page

### February, Friday 3, 1888.

Brilliant, beautiful day — to studio soon after 10. Spent the m'f working over W.C. studies made 20 years ago of the two Ausable lakes. with the idea of having them framed for the Montrose house. Wrote a letter to Herrick concerning lumber for barn — Home at 1.30 back at 2.30 — Soon after crate arr— from Montrose with movable closets. I spent the afternoon unloading & screwing them up in place — It is a very orderly arrangement & it makes me sad, to think that I am getting everything about me into such good working shape when I am so far on in my life with little hope of being able to do worthy work. I wish I had a 20 year old boy to whom I could pass it all over, & enjoy seeing him make good use of it. Home at 6.30 — dinner. Ev'f. Read for awhile — Charl came in about 9 — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 4, 1888.

A beastly day, beginning with snow — then rain that froze as it fell followed by a thaw & a mizzle that no umbrella could protect against — To studio soon after 10 — Spent the m'f finishing 20 year old W.C. study of lower Ausable Lake — Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — worked until 4 — then took off my coat, finished putting up the closets in dark room and then knocked to pieces the crate & sawed it up for fire wood — This took me until 6 — Then home, pretty well tired — Dinner — Ev'f. Reading until Charl came in — He stayed until 11. It was 11.30 when I got to bed.
