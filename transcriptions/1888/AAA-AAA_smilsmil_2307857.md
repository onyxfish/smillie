## Left Page

### April, Saturday 7, 1888

*[written above header: Charl elected a member — the "Century"]*

Delightful day — got to studio soon after 10 & spent the mng. with dry-point & graver, on the Tadema plate — Annie & Jim came in at 1 & we walked up home to lunch. Back to studio at 2.30 — worked on plate, with some interruptions — miss Verdenal came & worked on her drawing — A member of the Union League Art Com. called for information concerning etchers — At 5.30 I covered my printing table & blind — Home at 6.30 — dinner, Kate & the children (many's) Ret? to Po'k. on the 3.30 p.m. train. Evett. went to Century. Charl was elected a member — About 11 walked into town with Dr. Yale & Rev. D. Marvin Vincent. To bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 8, 1888

Still beautiful weather, although rather cool. Up late, as usual — after bkfst. Read paper for awhile — It was about 11.30 — when Annie & Jim & I went to N.A. f.d. & spent some time looking at the pictures — then to Century & home to dinner at 2 — many dined with Mr. Hertzell. About 3.30 Annie, Jim & I went to the Yandel Gallery, 5th Ave. & 19th S. — Private View of Exhi'm of Soct'y of Am. Artists. After a look around the galleries we called on the Kimballs in 22nd S; Home soon after 6 — Tea — Evett. wrote a note to Beu Muelford & part of a letter to Herrick — Charl came in & spent part of the eveg. with us. To bed soon after 10.
