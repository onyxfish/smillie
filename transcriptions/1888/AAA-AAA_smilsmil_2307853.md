## Left Page

### March, Friday 30, 1888

A bright, mild day — Got up feeling
very seedy after a night spent
battling with a nose that wasn't
worth a cent for breathing purposes.
About 10 Geo. came in to enquire
after me & with him I walked
down to the studio where I superin-
tended the unpacking of my new
printing table & putting it into
place. Kelly of the Art Review came
in & I signed 25 pfs. of the Etched
plate that he bought of me last Spring.
It was almost 2 when I got to Gal's.
He kept me waiting until nearly 2.30
& then it was almost an hour later
when I got away & home to lunch. I
spent the rest of the afternoon in-doors.
Ev'g. finished letter to Herrick. Then
Charl came in & stayed until after
10\. It was near 10 when Edith & Marion
Wright am. unexpectedly to stay all night.
About 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 31, 1888

Bright & pleasant, altho' somewhat
breezy. I got to studio soon after 10
but my head was very uncomfortable
& continued to increase in discomfort
throughout the day. I got my mordant
bath all ready to go on with the biting
my Tadema plate but did not dare
trust myself with such important work.
I could not bring my wits together — nor
see properly out of my eyes — so, I spent
the m'f. varnishing my big wooden
trays & such odd jobs. Home to lunch
at 1 — back at 2.30 — In the afternoon
S. R. Koehler called when my head was
hurting me very much. By the way —
at noon I went over to the Bank & cut some
coupons & deposited them for Annie — &
to Knœdler's. I accomplished nothing
in the afternoon — Home soon after 6 —
dinner — Ev'g. most of the time
lounging on the bed — By the way — No. 2 —
Edith & Marion ret'd. to Phila. some time
during the day — To bed before 11.
