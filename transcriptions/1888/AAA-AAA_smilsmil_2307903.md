## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 10. 1888.

Another pitilessly dry day. I
was at work training vines about
the house until after 10 — then
to work & carefully laid out the
Text on Burns plate — Dinner
at 1 — a rest, then again to
work tracing Text onto gelatine
when Mrs. Sarah Sprout & her bro
J. Wesley Little called & took
considerable of my time. Mr.
J.W.L. has commenced the study
of Art. About 4.30 started out
for a drive taking Sue, Kutchen,
Eleanor & Rabi. — The horses behaved
admirably — back at 7 — Tea —
Sueff on Piazza, with lights in
Chinese lanterns until after
9 — then lighted up & did a
little writing. 11 when I got
to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 11. 1888.

A very hot dry day with threats of
thunder storms nearly all the p.m.
— wild wind storm from 7 to 9 p.m.
when at last part of a thunder storm
passed over us at about 10. I
worked about out of doors until 10,
then to work on Burns — finished
the gelatine tracing, transformed it
& + then dinner at 1 — After
that etched the Text & coat of
arms & varnished plate. Carriage
at the door at 4.30 — Annie, Sue
& I rode about not daring to go
far for fear of a coming storm.
— home soon after 6. Tea at 7.
After that Rabi. & I went down town.
Eveff, a roaring gale & wind.
Got a letter, through Charls, from
Kate — while the others played
cards I read the newspaper.
To bed about 10.30.
