## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 20, 1888

Muggy & overcast, with some heavy showers —
to studio at about 10 & to work on Tadema
etching which I stuck to until after 12.
Chas. Kurtz came in & wished me to
make some drawings for him for his
academy notes, but I told him that
it was impossible. Soon after 12, went
to Am. Art Galleries & saw the Kruseman
Van Elten Paintings & the Hamilton
Gibson Water Colors, drawings & proofs —
then to Bank for Annie & home to lunch
at 1.45. To Studio at 2.45 — work interfered
with by poor light — Home at 6.30 —
Dinner — Eve'g read lectures,
and to bed at about 10 o'clts.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 21, 1888

A wet, windy day — Got to studio at
a little before 10 & to work on Tadema
etching to which I stuck very closely.
Home to lunch & back to studio as
usual. In the afternoon T. W. Wood
came in to ask me to be sure to be
at the Annual meeting of the Water
Color Soct'y. Home to dinner at
6.30 — Ralph seems to be very miserable
with his cold which has downed him
up completely — At 7.30 I went to
W. C. Soct'y meeting at N. A. of D. The
matter in which Wood was interested
was postponed until the Nov. meeting.
I got home soon after 10 & found there
Chas. Collins who had been enter-
taining Annie part of the eve'g.
To bed about 11.
