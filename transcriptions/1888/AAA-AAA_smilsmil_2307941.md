## Left Page

### September, Monday 24, 1888

A brilliantly beautiful day &
night. In the mtf. To our surprise
the lumps discovered in A's breast
last night had nearly disappeared.
I worked on Tomato vines until 11.
then wrote a letter to Mary &
one to Charl. Dinner abt. Some
more abt of door work & a little
photographing. At 3.30 we went
out riding — Annie — Geo. Nellie
& I — to South Pond. Home a few
minutes after 6 — Tea — Jim seems
to be out of sorts — has a slight er-
uption on his face — complains of his
eyes & is very hoarse. At night
he seemed to be a little feverish.
Evg. Reading newspapers —
magazines & caring for horses.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 25, 1888

Cloudy day with threats of rain —
as usual, spent some time in the
garden — gathering tomatoes. Tarbell
came to me there to talk about
the horses. It was near noon
before I got to my work biting
the Huntington etching. Dinner
abt — Coyle & Decker at work
with team hauling dirt from
west of the garden & spreading it
on the spoke N.E. of strawberry
bed — we did not go out riding
& I continued at my biting
until after 5 — Tea early — after
it Geo. & I went down town on
messages — back at 7.30 — Evg.
reading. Jim's condition seems
somewhat improved but he is
evidently out of sorts in some
way. Gentle rain as we went
to bed at 10 o'clk.
