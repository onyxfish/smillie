## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 4, 1888.

Mild, overcast day with a little dusting of snow, at night — To studio at the usual time — 10 o'clk. To work on W.C. Sketch — "Near Rowsley — Derbyshire" — feeling much discouraged by the appearance of my work so far — was not able to do much to elevate my spirits — Home to lunch soon after 1 — back at 2.15 — Worked until 4 — then wrote letter to Herrick — Fixed up roll of drawings + plans — Took them to 4to. at 5 and then to McKeever's in 42d St — Home at 6.15 dinner — Most of the evely Read aloud to Annie from Jany No. of Century — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 5, 1888.

Bright, beautiful day — To studio at usual time & to work on Rowsley drawing — did not get home to lunch until 1.30 — Annie walked down with me after lunch at 2.30 and I went on with my drawing getting it finished in spite of quite an interruption in the call of Messrs Radke & Sauckner, Print Publishers, who came to settle details with me for the etching of a plate, about 15 × 21, after a Water Color drawing by Ranger. About 5 I walked down to 14th St. & then up to 50th & 5th Ave. & home — Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'ff Annie read awhile aloud, and then I worked upon plans for a printing Table — To bed about 10.30.
