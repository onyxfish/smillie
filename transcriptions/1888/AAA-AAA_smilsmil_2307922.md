## Left Page

### August, Friday 17, 1888

*miss Sophie Ireland came to visit us —*

A thunderstorm at 4 A.m. after which steady, & at times, very heavy rain fell until after 3 P.m. I was used up with headache & other troubles & did not get up until noon. At 1 o'clk. Herrick drove to the D.L.&W. depot taking Annie — all bundled up in the carriage. They returned a little before 5 having had a very bright & pleasant ride back, bringing miss Ireland with them. Tea soon after 6. Even[ing], for a time on the piazza — later, in doors. Annie read aloud a magazine article to us — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 18, 1888

A very pleasant day, altho' partly cloudy. I did not get up until 10 o'clk. owing to my continued headache. Did some work at fixing wire for the vines on west front piazza. In the afternoon with Annie, Sophie Ireland and Jim we rode over Butterfield's hill. Showers passing to the north of us. Every A. read aloud a Burroughs magazine article & we got to bed soon after 10.
