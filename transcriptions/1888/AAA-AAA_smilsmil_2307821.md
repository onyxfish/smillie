## Left Page

### January, Thursday 26, 1888

Bright, cold & windy — After 10 when
I got to studio — again to work on
the interminable Printing Table plan
& finished them — Then to work
on details for oak bed room ceiling
at Montrose. Home at 1 — Kate
& I entertained Tom & Louise —
Jim's cousins — while Annie went
to lunch with their mother & some
friends — Just as I was leaving the
house at 2 o'clk., Belle & Mary Tweedy
came in — they visited with Kate
until 3.30 — I got to studio at 2.30
& finished up my plans — Tom
Fessenden came in for a little while.
At 5.30 I took plans to Adams Exp. Co.
Home before 6. Eve'g. Charle
came in & we all chatted together
until about 10 — To bed by 11.30
up again at 12.30 — Domestic
exigencies — To be again at 1.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 27, 1888

Very cold, clear & windy — To studio
at 10 & to work on X water-
color — Cattle subject — putting
in first washes — The paper works
better than the other large cattle
subject done a few days ago — I
put in the washes vigorously and
by 5 P.m. had the paper covered —
cattle sketched — But I see where
my want of constant practice results
in muddy or uncertain color & want
of quality — my sky (clouds) is too hot &
when I try to cool it with blue it makes
it heavy & muddy — my greens lack
vitality — in short I am not a first
class artist — Home to lunch at 1 &
back at 2.30 — Sent check to Equitable Life
Ass. Socy & wrote to Azur Lathrop & Herrick —
At 5.30 went to Knodler's & got some colors.
Then home — dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. Staff
View — Water Color Socy, but I did not go —
Stayed at home quietly in front of the
fire & wrote a letter to Gen. Diven thank-
ing him for box of oranges rec. yesterday.
