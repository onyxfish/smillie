## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 29, 1888.

Clear & very hot day — up late — To
studio before 10 — most of the m'g
spent with Etchin'g pfp. — writing to
Guéckünst about Mr Rive's photo-gravure
— getting pfcf. Ready to send by Exps.
to G— Then to J. Stokes — Publisher, with
mts. of "What o'clk is it?" — To Exps. Office
& to Bank & Safe depoSit Co. Home
soon after 1. Lunch. Then Rode to
Canal S. — to Kimmel & Voigt's with Stoke's
plate — met Snedecor there with pfp. of
Vaultèn's big Etchin'g after Jas. Hart.
Ordered Printing Roller — back to studio
at 4.30 — At 5.30 to McKeever's shop
with Tadema pfp. to be framed for
Mary S. cok. Home for dinner at 6.30 —
Eve'g. Read news paper — Heavy
thunderstorm between 7 & 8 o'clk —
Charl came in before 9 for half
an hour. I wrote a note or
two & got to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 30. 1888.

*Decoration Day.*

Another hot, muggy & overcast day —
To give Greta & Jim a chance to see
the procession I did not leave the
house until after 10. Then to studio
set palette & Repainted fore ground
on 15"× 24"= "on one of the Harbor Islands —
Lake Geo." making it lighter & clearer
in color. Home to lunch at 1. Gurney's
Exps. had delivered my big blk. walnut
Table from Po'k at Studio — chairs at the
house & framed engravings at McKeever's.
Did not get back to studio until
3.15 & then had very little work in
me. Fussed over the m'g work.
Home at 6.30 — dinner — Eve'g.
a little reading — a little writing
& more loafing & to bed at 11.
