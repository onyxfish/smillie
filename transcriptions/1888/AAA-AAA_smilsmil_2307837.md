## Left Page

### February, Monday 27, 1888

Bright mid, then overcast — air very raw with a few flying snow flakes. To studio about 10 — got my etching table into order and commenced work on "Competeting Blowers" — Rec'd a check from Water Color Soct'y, went to Bank & deposited it — also to Kurtz's & saw silver print from his large photo. from Alma Tadema's painting "Reading from Homer" — then on up to Ortgie's gallery to see the Spencer pictures — met Mr. Hitchcock of the Tribune & we talked of the decadence of Etching. Then in to see the pictures — some fine ones — shown with every advantage — Home to lunch at 1.15. Back to studio at 2.30 — worked on etching altho' almost used up with a headache — Home before 6 — walked up with Champney — Dinner.

Ev'g some reading — To bed at 11 & passed a very uncomfortable night.

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 28, 1888

Unable to get out of my bed owing to a beastly headache. It was 6 p.m. when I got up and ate a light meal — we spent the ev'g in the parlor until 10 when Annie went to bed and I kept vigil in the nursery lounge. It was after midnight when Ralph woke for his supper — about 1 when I put him back in his crib and 1.30 when I put myself to bed.

moran, the carpenter worked part of the day in 2d story W.C. fixing a broken chain.
