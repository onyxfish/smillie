## Left Page

### April, Friday 13, 1888

Charming bright but cool day —
To studio before 10 — Wrote a note
to Herrick & sent him check for his
month's wages — Then worked on the
Tadema plate until after 12 — Then
went over to Fd. Stokes' with plate
& pf. of "What o'clk. is it?" It is not yet
finished but he wants a few imps. for
canvassers immediately — Home to
lunch at 1 — back before 2.30 & worked
on Tadema plate until after 4 —
Then, my head & eyes feeling very tired,
I rested awhile — Wrote notes to Edw.
Bierstadt, Ticknor & Co., Gutekunst,
Boston Photo-gravure Co., & Forbes & Co.
inquiring about Photo-gravure
Reproductions in the interest of Mr.
G.L. Hives — Home soon after 6 — dinner.
Evelf. Gretta & Mary, the waitress, went
off on a spree on tickets that we had
given them to Barnum's while A. & I
stayed with the children — Ralph is very
much bunged up about the eyes & I wrote to Yale
to come in to see him to-morrow. Charl came
in for a little while — Annie read
aloud "A Roman Singer" & finished it —
To bed at about 11.30.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 14, 1888

Disagreeable mf. showers — heavy
shower about the middle of the after-
noon — To studio before 10 — & spent
the day working with dry point on
the Tadema plate. Home to lunch
& back as usual — worked until 4.30
when my head hurt me so much
that I gave up — but it was nearly 6
before I got away from the studio — Took
a short walk — Home at 6.30 — dinner.
Evelf. Annie & I went to Geo. Cook's
in 39. St. where we played whist for
an hour — home soon after 10 & then
to bed.
