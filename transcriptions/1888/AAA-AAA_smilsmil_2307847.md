## Left Page

### March, Sunday 18. 1888.

Bright day — most fit given up to
domestic duties — Ralph has a very
heavy cold which keeps him coughing
& sneezing & so stuffed in the head that
he can breathe only through his open
mouth — consequently he is troublesome
& requires care — Annie's condition
makes it impossible for her to care for
him as if she were well — So, I tried
to do my share. About noon Annie
Jim & I walked down to George's and
after a visit there, & seeing most of the
family we walked back — Dinner
at about 2 — Afternoon, more nurse
work. About 7 had a cup of tea.
Evef wrote to Mary to Chas. H. & Hart
& to Geo. L. Rives — At 10 went to P.G.D.
with letters — Home & to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 19. 1888.

Very bright & pleasant — To Studio some,
at 10 & got fairly at work with my
point, at last, on the Klua-Fadawa
etching — Home to lunch. At usual
time — Soon after 2, Dr. Stimpson came
& met Dr. Emily Blackwell & had a consul-
tation concerning Annie's breast — & to
my exceeding great relief Dr. S. Pro-
nounces it positively, a deep seated in-
flammation of the glands and nothing
more serious, as in my anxiety I had
feared it might be — So, before 3 I
returned to the Studio with a com-
paratively light heart & worked on my
Fadawa until almost 6 — Then a walk
& home to dinner — Evef. P.o. & Emily
Cook spent it with Annie while I went
to Century Club to hear Muy Bridge on Animal
Locomotion — It was just the same as
what I saw & heard from him at the N.A. of
D. 3 or 4 yrs. ago. Interesting in some ways
& horribly uninteresting in others. He is a
mere idea man & utterly without appreciation
of the Spirit or object of art. I left soon after 10
while he was yet speaking — Home and
to bed soon after 11.
