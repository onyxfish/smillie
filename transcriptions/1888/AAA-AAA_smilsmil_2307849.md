## Left Page

### March, Thursday 22, 1888

Blustering & cold with quite a snow flurry between 1 & 2 p.m. To studio before 10 & put in a hard days work on the Tadema plate. — Etching in the three central & principal figures — To tell the truth, I am rather pleased with the way in which I have done them — The work may be mechanical but not more so than is required for an exact reproduction of the drawing & the painting — Home at 1 — Margaret Parker White there to lunch — back at 3.30 & worked until near 6 — then a walk & home at 6.30 — Dinner — Evening read news to Alpen — Annie read aloud a short story from Harper's for April —

## Right Page

### March, Friday 23, 1888

Very cold — clear & blustering — on my way to studio, about 10 o'clk, again I ruptured a small blood vessel in my right eye — showing clearly that I had blood in my eye. — When I got to studio found water running through the ceiling from Mr. Daniel's room — Puddles on my etching Fable — no damage — fortunate & inexplicable. I etched until 12.30 — then walked up to Dr. [illegible]'s — Mad. Ave. & 49th St — Saw him — did not seem to think that the trouble in my eye was anything very serious — Home to lunch at 1.45. To studio a little before 3 — & worked until near 6 — Nicoll came in with a project for subscriptions to buy one of H. Van Elten's paintings at his auction sale to-night — Home to dinner after 6. Spent the even't on lounge nursing my eye with hot water applications — Charl came in for a little while. Did not get to bed until 12 o'clk, owing to Master Ralph's interference & the same cause got me up before 5 o'clk. To-morrow mtf, after which time I did not get to sleep again.
