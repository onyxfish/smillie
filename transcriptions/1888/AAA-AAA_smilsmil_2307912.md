## Left Page

### July, Saturday 28, 1888

A lovely day, cool & clear with a beautiful sky — I worked pretty hard all the mf on my Tadema — burnishing & then taking some Jap. paper pfs. for the first time getting something perfectly sound and precise in line without being too hard. Parke came in & sat with me some time while I was at work. After dinner I took two more pfs. & at about 4.15 Edw., Mary, Annie, Jim & I rode out to Cope's & around by Elk Lakes, getting home at 7.15 after a charming ride. Eve.g Edw. & I read alternately, aloud, portions of Bryant's & Derby's Trans. of Homer's Iliad. After 11 when we got to bed.

Rec'd — along & very interesting letter from Kate at Lucerne — July 15th.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 29, 1888

An overcast mf, clearing bfor it to a charming day — a heavy shower passed over us between 4 & 5. P.m. I wrote for awhile in the mf & at about 11 we all went off for a stroll in the woods & went over to the hill back of the little Sayre house & sat there for some time, under the trees, overlooking the lake — Home to dinner at 2 — Afternoon, again writing — a letter to Kate — After 5 we went out for a short ride. Home at 6.45. Tea — I spent the eveg in the nursery with the baby & finished my letter to Kate — Geo. Chase & Kimmel tonight. It was 11 o'clk. when I went to the P.O. with letters. Quite a heavy shower fell while I was out.

The "Strunk," as Tom calls it, went into the trap & this mf early Herrick took him beyond the barn & shot him to death.
