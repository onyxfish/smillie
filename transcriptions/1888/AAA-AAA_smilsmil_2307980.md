## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 11, 1888

A downpour of rain most of the day — very dark and gloomy. My cold distressed me very much and I did not dare go out of doors but kept myself as carefully housed as possible. I wrote at last an answer to Jim Shearman's letter Rec'd. last may — Dinner at 6 — At 7.20 Annie & I started for Chickering Hall, the rain having ceased & the young moon shining dimly — In the vestibule of the hall we met Emma J — went in & got good seats in the gallery & then waited half an hour for the Mendelssohn Glee Club to begin their first concert of the season — It was exceedingly enjoyable & I did enjoy it in spite of feeling very wretched. — It was nearly 11 o'clk when we got out. Emma had an escort & we walked up home & got to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 12, 1888

clear, windy & cold — got up late but feeling none the worse for the exposure of last night. Got to studio about 10.30 — did not attempt to work — did a little at assorting my Alma-Tadema proofs — went to Second Nat'l Bk. & effected another loan, this time for $800 to meet outstanding debts — then to Daut. Pike's — then to Brooks Bros. & ordered a blk. frock coat. It was after 2 when I got home to lunch & 3.30 when I got back to Studio. Found Yale there — he remained until after 5 — Saul. Colman came in again — I accomplished nothing — Home at a little after 6. Dinner — about 8 went to N.A. of D. Quarterly meeting — voted on Kemp's resolution excluding glass from the exhibitions. carried — then in to Water Color Soc'y meeting at 9 to discuss T. H. Smith's proposition to have a Decorative Art Soc'y fancy costume ball in the galleries of the [illegible] the night before Private View — The room was fearfully hot & foul, so I did not dare stay — I heard the case stated & the discussion started & there being a quorum & one over I slipped out at 9.30 — home & to bed at 10.30.
