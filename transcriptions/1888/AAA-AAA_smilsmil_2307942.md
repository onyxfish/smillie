## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 26, 1888

Brilliant day until about 4 o'clk.
when it clouded over — clear over[illegible],
until 11 out of doors — much of the
time in the garden — Then to work
at my biting — Parke came in &
made a call — Geo. went out paint-
ing. D. Richardson came, examined
Jim & said he did not think that
there was anything serious ailed
him — Dinner at 1 o'clk. We
tried to get away at 2.30 — but it
was near 3 when Annie, Geo, Nellie
& I, escorted by Parke on horseback
got started, for Pine Hill — we overtook
his carriage with Mrs. P. Mrs. de Schweinitz
& Miss Lord — Norman & Bessie in a light
wagon — got to Pine Hill at 4.30 & had a
very fine view — came back by Ridge Road —
lost half an hour going back to look for the
P's who must have taken a cross road
home — Home at 6.30 — Tea — Chores kept
me busy until 9.30. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 27, 1888

Clouded over. clearing pleasantly
by 10 Am. breezy & growing cooler.
With Coyle & Decker for some time —
drawing earth from west of the garden
& grading it over the slope N.+E. of the
strawberry patch. Some time in the
garden — got a mess of green peas.
To work at my biting at about 11 —
Dinner ab't 1 & at biting until near
4. S.H.p. [illegible] am. with Raspberry
& Bell berry bushes & hollyhocks. So,
I went out & worked with Coyle
setting them out until 6 o'clk —
Tea at 6.30 — Even'g in Etching
Room until near 9 — had my plate
in the acid for 20 m. To bed at 10.30
