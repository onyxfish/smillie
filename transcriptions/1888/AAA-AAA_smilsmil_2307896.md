## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 26, 1888

Pleasant cool day, partly cloudy.
About 4:20 P.M. — a light shower.
Up late — all day bothered
with a disagreeable headache which disabled me
from working on Calverley's Burns.
Part of the m't. down town attending to small business. Dinner
soon after, then unpacked
some furniture rec'd from Flint.
After 3 Barney came with the
new harness for the horses & for
an hour or more he was engaged
fitting it to them — then had an
exceedingly short drive to test
he felt — driven in by the shower.
Soon after 6 went down town —
Tea at 7. Very cool ev'g — so
that we had a little fire on
the hearth. To bed soon after
10 — Annie's breast still seems
to be improving.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 27, 1888

A very cool day — At 8 A.m. ther.
below 60°. Fire on the hearth very
acceptable at half time. I had
fire in studio all day. Got to work
on crayon sketch of Calverley's statue
of Burns of which I am to make
a small etching. Mr. Baldwin came
with piano — A.1. Carey came about
the new culvert that is to be built
at lane entrance — Dinner — After
that worked on sketch until 4 o'clk.
Then made p't p' & took it to Esp. office
for Calverley — met Herrick, returning
with horses & carriage from harness maker.
Got in & drove Teague for a few minutes
then home & took the family out for
about 3/4 of an hour — In at 6:15 then
tea — about the place until 8 o'clk.
Then wrote notes to Calverley & to
Charl, from whom we got a telegram
announcing the arrival of Mary, Kate
& Nat at Southampton — all well after a good
voyage — After'g went to P.O. —
To bed at 10:30.
