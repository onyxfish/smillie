## Left Page

### August, Monday 27, 1888.

Overcast with growing colder &
more cloudy until after 4, when
the sky cleared beautifully —
Coyle came up in the m't and
gathered the vegetables — the first
time that he has been well enough
in a fortnight — I worked awhile
in the m't on the A.B.U.Co. drawing
& after dinner again I worked
until 4 — Soon after, we went
out riding — all bundled up in
shawls — to Fairdale & home over
Fairview Hill. After tea, some
time with news paper — then Annie
read aloud Mrs. Jos Pennell's very
readable article in Harper —
"Our Journey to the Hebrides". All
very sleepy & to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 28, 1888.

A delicious day — clear & cool —
worked on A.B.U.Co. drawing until
12. then, my eyes & head hurting
me very much I went out to
the garden & spent an hour
there with the Tomatoes & squash.
Dinner at 1.30 — again on drawing
until 4 — then drove to Fairdale
& back over "the Ridge" stopping
at Shay's & getting Annie's sun
umbrella, lost by her three
weeks ago & picked up in the
road by his hired man.
Home at 6.30 — Excitement in town
over the suicide of Burgess, keeper
of the Tarbell House — Got letter
from Geo. — surprised us by writing that
he had ri— a letter from Mary written
from Venice. Eve't. A. read aloud
until 9.30 — To bed at about 10.
