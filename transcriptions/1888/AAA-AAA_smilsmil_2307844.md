## Left Page

### March, Monday 12. 1888.

*Great Blizzard —*

When I got up was amazed to see everything covered deep with snow which was still falling while the wind howled furiously — It was after 10 when I started for the studio. I soon found that I had undertaken a very severe task. It was extremely cold — Ther. down to 11° & 12°, wind blowing a gale & snowfalling in blinding drifts. No cars of any kind, surface or elevated, were running — & very few vehicles of any kind were about — after noon hardly any one ventured into the streets — Stores were not opened — all business was suspended — N.Y. City was paralyzed. I worked all day on my large Water Color "Group of Cattle" Miss Abbatt kindly gave me a little lunch so that I did not have to start for home until after 5. I set out with some fears — three times the wind carried me over ice-covered, wind swept pavements in spite of my every effort — I was blinded & the breath almost taken from me — I got as far as the Park Ave. Hotel when I stopped in there for a few minutes to get my wind — then walked up to 38th St. through the tunnel — In my struggle with the elements I ruptured a small blood vessel in my right eye, and a clot & blood settled under the cornea, adding much to my beauty & filling me with anxiety concerning my ability to finish the Tadema etching. Evng. sat listening to the howling storm — no lights in the streets.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 13. 1888.

So used up by my busted eye that I did not attempt to go out. Kept myself as quiet as possible hoping that the clot in my eye would be absorbed as promptly as possible — The weather continued very bad — high wind, very cold & much snow flying — Chas. came in about 6 — having walked up from Wall St. He took dinner with us — & then his departure at about 8 — Annie read aloud awhile — It was about 11 when we got to bed.
