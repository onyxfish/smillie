## Left Page

### September, Thursday 20, 1888.

A little bright weather in the m'g
after that, cloudiness — Rain at 4
P.m. in showers & again between
8 & 9 P.m. quite heavy — with
thunder. All of the time that
I could get through the day I
spent in biting the Huntington
etching. I am surprised to
find so many lines in the
sky & face — more or less all over
the plate, in fact, that have not
cut & do not bite — it will give
me considerable work to make
them up. Dinner at 1 — Herrick
got my derrick put up on 2° story
piazza. At 5.30 — went down Town
— Jim with me. Tea at 6.30 — another
evn'g of yawning & sleepy con-
versation. Charl sent me a
letter from Katie through, from Geneva.
To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 21, 1888.

Cloudy & chilly — evn'th. clear & cold.
After bkfs. I spent considerable time
in the garden working among the
tomato plants. It was 11 o'clk. before
I get to the house. I got to work
again at the biting of "the Goldsmith's
Daughter". Dinner at 1 — After it,
some time with the men super-
intending the putting up of big stove
in lower hall & the stove in chem-
ber room. Continued biting until
4 o'clk — then with Annie — Nellie
& g°. went out riding — bleak
overcast. home at 6. Tea at
6.30 — Evn'g given up to "chores"
— work in "dark Room". (Charl has
sent to me some fresh Stanley plates)
& Reading newspapers.
