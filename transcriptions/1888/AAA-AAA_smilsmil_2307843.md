## Left Page

### March, Saturday 10. 1888.

Bright, spring-like day. To studio a
little after 10. At 10.30 went over to Fifth
Ave, cor. 29th S. & called upon Rajon — I gave
him a pf. of my Bridgman's Etching " A Hard
Bargain" — He put his autograph upon my
pf. of his etching after Millais — "A Tower
Warden". Back to studio at 11.30 & found
that Witty Clark of Scribner's had been there. So
I went to Scribner's & saw Mr. G. & Mr. Thomas, head
of the Subscription Dep. He wishes the Tadema
etching made ½ inch larger — i.e. 8½ in. long.
I then went to Kurtz & gave orders for a new
reduction from the large photo. Home to lunch
soon after 1 — To studio at 2.45. Spent most
of the afternoon mounting some pfs. of the Durant
portrait etching. At 5.30 started for home.
Dinner at 6 & at 6.30 Annie & Jim went over
to Charl's Room to see the Barnum Circus parade.
Gretta & Mary also went out for the same
purpose — to see the parade. I remained
& was nurse until the return of A. & J. Even'g
in nursery reading — To bed at 11 — A troublous
night — the baby up at 12 — & 2 — & 5 — much
trouble with his bowels — constipation.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 11. 1888.

Dull day with a little light rain at
times during the afternoon — falling
steadily at night. Up late — 6 pfs. after
9 — spent the morn. with Annie and
the Children. A — suffering from
a headache in addition to the dis-
comfort from her breast which does not
get better. Dinner after 2 —
About 4 o'clk. I went down to George's
& saw Nellie & his new boy No. 3 — there
for about an hour — then to studio and
took my box of Water-Colors, then walked
down to 18th S., up Bdway & 5th Ave. to 44th S.
through to G.C.S. — dropped Geo's letter to
Kate into the box, then home — a cup of tea
at 7. Eve'g. Reading — Rain set
in & fell with increasing heaviness
until midnight, when it suddenly
turned to snow & the trees tumbled.
