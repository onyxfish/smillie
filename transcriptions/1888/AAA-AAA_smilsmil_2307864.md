## Left Page

### April, Saturday 21, 1888

A cold & cloudy day — Got to studio at 9.30 & after finding a fire, to work on Tadema plates finishing with graver, dry-point & burnisher the faces & other parts that I etched & bit in yesterday — Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — worked until about 5 and then, feeling very tired I went of for a walk — Home soon after 6 — Dinner — Eve'g. to the [illegible] Phil. Concert with Annie — I felt so wretchedly that the concert was almost spoiled for me — but I managed to get some enjoyment out of the Pastoral Symph, my Overture to Tannhauser & Chopin's funeral march, arranged for orchestra by Pres. Thomas, & played in memory of J. W. Drexel — After Concert Geo. & Char. walked home with us.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 22, 1888

A cold, breezy m'g — cloudy with signs of April showers that did not amount to anything. A milder afternoon — B'kf't about 9 — It was 11.30 when Annie, Jim & I started out. We went up to 73d. S. & called upon Jo. — she was spending the day with the Gunthers. Then we walked down through the Park & out at 59th S. & 5th Ave. Riding thence home — went in to Walter's for a few minutes, then home to dinner at 2 — About 3 took a very short walk — Mr. Watson called & got me to sign a protest ag'st [illegible] Tunnel under 37th S? Then I walked down to Salmagundi Club. 123 Fifth Ave. & saw a few pictures — there to [illegible], where I met Ward (J.Q.A.) & had a long chat with him — then to studio & wet paper to take pf. to-morrow — then home at 6.30 — a light tea — Eve'g. reading until 9.30 when S. S. Avery came in with another [illegible] protest — we chatted until 10.45 & soon after went to bed.
