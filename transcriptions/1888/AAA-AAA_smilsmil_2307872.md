## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 9, 1888

Overcast & very warm — sunlight struggling through & a few drops of rain falling — up late — after 10 when I got to studio — Set palette & commenced repainting old oil study 15×24 "On one of the Harbor Islands Lake George" — I went about reckless by — At 1 went to [illegible] & deposited $200 — payment for Tadema etching — Home to lunch — St. Stimpson met Dr. Blackwell at 2 o'clk — expressed himself as entirely satisfied & dismissed himself — To studio at about 3 & painted until 6 — Home to dinner at 6.45 — Suet wrote a note to Kate — Read awhile — very hot — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 10, 1888

Overcast, hot & muggy — A heavy shower at 2 & thunder storms about us most of the afternoon — To studio at about 10 & repainted foreground of "Harbor Islands" making it much broader in effect and lighter in color & making, altogether, a pretty good thing for me. Home at 1.30 — after lunch did not want to see the Dr. Annie feeling very much better — To studio before 3 — wrote to Herrick & Smith at Montrose & to the Boston Photogravure Co. — At 5.30 went to Adams Exp. with [illegible] Rec — this [illegible] from the B.P.G. Co. with original drawing of Mr. G.S. Rives & two proofs — Then to J & C. Johnston's & bought a shirt — then to Bridgman's & paid bill — then to Pastel [illegible] at Wunderlich's — then to Huyler's & got a box of candy — then to studio & cleaned up palette — home at 6.45 — dinner —
