## Left Page

### August, Thursday 9. 1888.

Delightful day growing cooler all the time. — I got up with a headache that grew worse with the day.
In the a.f. I succeeded in getting a first rate Transfer — bl'k lead from gelatine tracing — f. "The Goldsmith's Daughter" on to my etching ground + in spite of my headache I made a start with my paint — After dinner I went to bed until 4 o'clk. Then with Annie — the children + Greta went riding. Seeking Rye Straw without success. Home at 6.30 Tea. Eve'g useless from headache — to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 10. 1888.

Cool, clear & crisp — beautiful day — Still suffering somewhat from yesterdays headache — Kept out of doors until near 11 — then to work etching on "Goldsmith's Daughter" — Dinner at 1 — again etched until 4 — then out with family on another hunt for Rye Straw — failing to find any — this m'f Herrick took the horses and brought in a small load of oat straw. Home at 6.15 Tea — Eve'g. reading newspapers — etc. Rec'd photo from A.B.U. Co. a S.c. Am. subject for me to draw.
On dressing Annie's breast, at bed time, it was found to be healed, all over — for the second time — now we will watch anxiously hoping that at last the good work will be permanent.
