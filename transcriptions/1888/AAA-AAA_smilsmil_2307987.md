## Left Page

### December, Thursday 27, 1888

Very warm & wet — rain most of the mtf — to studio's at 10. At work at the mtf. Podging about. Finished a drawing of Annie's father and the mounting of it — then some winter. Home to lunch at 1. back at 2.30 afternoon very dark — wrote up minutes of last meeting of Nominating com. Home before 5 o'clk. in very heavy shower — got a rest before dinner —
At 8 o'clk — starlight & much cooler — with Annie rode down to 14th S. — left her at Steinbeck Hall (No. 11 E.) rehearsal of Choral club — then on to Century — attend meeting of Nom. Com. Dr. [illegible] Mr. Jos. W. Harper & Mr. Stephen Olin [illegible] — an interesting meeting until 11 o'clk. when having completed our nominations we adjourned, sine die. walked up home — found Annie there. The [illegible] messenger escorted her to the stoops — had been there 15 or 20 min. — having forgotten her latch key. To bed just before midnight.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 28, 1888

Very pleasant, bright day — to studio at 10 — Fr. Otis came in to talk over a proposed change affecting Qo. Rives & E. E. Stearman — He saw for the first time my "Cliffs of Normandy" & was very much impressed by the picture — spent the rest of the mtf cleaning my study which has got to be very dirty. Home to lunch at 1, stopping at [illegible] & leaving an order for three small frames. Back to studio at 2.15 — Annie walking down with me on her way to a Choral club rehearsal — again at work on house cleaning — very tired. Home at 5.30 — dinner at 6.30. Eve'g had intended to go to Am. Art Ass'n and hear Verestchagín talk about War, but was too tired — Remained in front parlor fire & read until after 9 — To bed before 10.
