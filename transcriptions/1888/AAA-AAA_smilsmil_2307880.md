## Left Page

### May, Friday 25, 1888.

Cloudy but not unpleasant — did not leave
off. until near 9 — after that about town
attending to many little matters — Rode
behind Wright's team of horses — remark-
ably well broken in some respects — too high
priced. Got up to the house about 11. Looking
after things outside until 1 — then to P's
to dinner — P. arr. from Hopbottom, with
his man & horses, just as we had finished
our bkft. After dinner I went up to J. W.
Tarbell's to see his team of grays & we
rode behind them for more than an
hour. They come nearer filling the bill of my
requirements than any other teams that
I have yet seen — about the house with
Herrick until 6 — then to P's to dinner — after
that Ja. Carmalt arr. with Norman P—.
& about dusk Herrick came with
of So. Montrose this team of grays. I took
a very short ride after them & they were
discussed by Parke & Carmalt. — Every
we talked horse & family until 10 go —
then to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 26. 1888.

*Home again.*

Dull day, Rain commencing about
10 A. m. & continuing until about 3 P. m.
Bkft. about 8 — at 8.30 went out — up the
hill to Tarbell's — Saw him & after a short
conversation agreed to buy his team
& a $40.00 double set of harness for
$500 — Then down Town. Bought D. L.
& W. R.R. mileage Ticket — at 9 o'clk. went
to So. Montrose with Herrick — Saw Hunter
& had a short ride after his young team —
I prefer Tarbell's old team — on our way
back stopped at Carey's quarry & dickered
with him for flagstones — in town, stopped
at the Parkes & bid them good bye —
then up to the house for a few minutes —
then drove to D. L. & W. depot — much
of the time in a heavy Rain — Train
on time at 12. 52 — dinner at Scranton —
Hoboken 6. 45 — & home at 7. 45
& in — & every, chatting with
Annie until 10 — then to bed.
