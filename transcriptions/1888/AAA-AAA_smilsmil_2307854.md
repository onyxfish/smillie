## Left Page

### April, Sunday 1, 1888

A very pleasant Easter Sunday until
after 3 o'clk. in the afternoon when
there were some light showers — after
which it remained clouded, but no
more rain fell. Bkfst. about 9. The
m'g was spent with the children
about 12.5 Annie & I started out &
walked up to 53d. S. & home by way of
5th Ave. Taking a look at the bonnets.
Dinner soon after 2. Greta's after-
noon out, so we were again with the
children — I managed to write notes
to Charl. Drexel Morgan & Co., 96th S. Rives
S.R. Koehler & later, in the eve'g, a
letter to Mary. It was after 9 before
finally Master Ralph was kind enough
to go to sleep — then I went to G.C.D. with
letters — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 2, 1888

A mild & pleasant day — Got up
feeling but little better than yester-
day. It was 11 before I got to studio
as I went first to Moran's — the car-
penter & to Valkenning's in E. 45th S.
to get marble slab for my printing
table. I worked until 1 o'clk. at
stopping out on the Tadema plate —
home to lunch at 1 — Jim was very
sick at the table & seems to be
thoroughly upset. Back to studio
at 2.30 — Got Water Color "A Group of
Cows" touched up & into its passe-
partout for [illegible]. Then again stopping
out on etching until 5 — did not get
into the bath at all to-day — Then
to Gurneys &p. in 22d S. met Koehler
in the street — Ran to NAofD & looked
at the pictures until 6 — then home —
Jim seems to be better — dinner —
Eve'g in parlor, for the first time in
some days (or evenings). Annie read
aloud from "A Roman Singer" — Charl.
came in for awhile — To bed about
11.
