## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 1. 1888.

Bright & cloudless — thawing —
To studio at 10.15. Spent some
time getting a few ph. of the
Durand Portrait etching in
press — then to work on large
cattle drawing, finishing around
generally — about 1 went to bank
— then home to lunch — back
to studio at 2.30 & worked until
near 5, getting my drawing into
a state where, for the present
I will leave it. Rec'd. letters
from Sam't. Coleman (Santa Barbara)
& Sarah Fessenden Hammond (Newton)
Walked to Union Sq. (after stopping
at Water Color extn.) & thence, by 5th Ave
up home — dinner at 6.30. Eve'g
Charl came in for a few minutes —
He told us of the death, by Typhoid
fever, of Enoch Lewis' youngest daughter
Lottie — To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 2. 1888.

Brilliant day — Did little work at
the studio — stopped at barbers & had
my hair cut — it was 11 o'clk when
I got to studio — got out an old W.C.
study of Upper Ausable Lake & worked
at it until almost 12.30 — Then
to 2d. Nat. Bk. & Safe deposit Co. — It
was 1.20 when I left there & went
home to lunch — Got to studio at
2.20 — Found A.B.N. Co. men there
with my Renovated printing press —
The machinists worked until 4.30
putting it up. At the same time
¼ of a ton of soft coal was put into
my box — I worked until 5.30
putting things into order — then home
— dinner — Eve'g. Geo. & Hriley Cook
came in for a little while — also
Geo. & Nellie S. — & Charl — To bed
soon after 11.
