## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 23. 1888.

*At W.G. Parke's — Scranton.*

overcast & threatening — to studio
before 10 — wrote to Mr. Rives — then
etched little Remarque on "What
o'clk." plate & took an imp. of
the plate — cleaned up & at 2.30
went to Bank for Burnie — Home
to lunch — Packed my travelling
bag & then had an hour to play
with the baby & read newspaper —
left home soon after 3 — to Chris's —
S.I. ferry — h.D. L.&W. Station. Hoboken —
met Parke, his daughter — his bro. —
Mr. Taylor, a new york lawyer, &
Mr. A — Scranton — we had a very
pleasant journey getting to Scranton
at 9.30 — Had a good lunch on the
train — Drove out to P's — found
them in confusion preparatory to
a start to-morrow for Montrose — &
Mrs. P— quite tired out — To bed
at 11.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 24. 1888.

*Montrose.*

up at a little after 6 & before B&P's —
wrote a letter to Annie — B&P's at 7.30 —
after B&P's a short ride on horse back —
rather lively for a few minutes — Soon after
Mr. Simpsons's team — one that I am to look
at with a view to buying, drove up — Mr. Taylor
came in & we (P., T., & I) drove down town — the
black horse, a beauty. Then left the others & went
to D. Boyd's stable — his darkie got out his team
of mares, light in weight, dark in color & well matched —
we drove for more than half an hour — then to Jar-
dwin's stable & he got out a team & drove about —
we had a look at the Memfield Team, very handsome
large Kentucky horses, but far too expensive — then we
looked after another team, highly recommended but
could not find them — nothing decided — at 12.20
went to depot & got my dinner — Later, was joined
by Mr. Parke, her two children — two maids and
[illegible] trunks, etc. etc — soon after I we went
on the train, getting to Montrose station at 2.30 —
where a team in waiting took us to town — got to
Mrs. P's — at 4.30 — wrote a note to Annie & walked
in — then up to our house where, until 6, I
went about looking at things in front of doors,
making a [illegible] a genuinely satisfactory stare,
men at work on the framing of the new barn. —
Back to Mr. P's & had tea — Romp about
some time about among the store men making
various enquiries — after that, sat & chatted
with Mrs. P. for awhile — To bed at 9.30 —
