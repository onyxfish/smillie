## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 15, 1888.

A pleasant day — overcast in the afternoon with threats of a change. In the mtf. Herrick took carriage to the foundery shop & had a new axle box fitted — I spent some time in the garden (Coyle has been sick since last Sat.) getting vegetables — when Parke found me — It was nearly 12 before I got to work etching on "Goldsmith's Daughter" — Dinner at 1 — Etched again until near 4 — interrupted by a call from Woolsey Carwalt — In the mtf. Mr. Franck called upon me — About 4 Churchill Carwalt called with a message from his mother — At 4.20 we went out riding — taking W. Carwalt with us. Home at 6.30 — tea — Evtf. with newspaper until near 9 — then Annie completed the reading aloud of Cable's Negro Question. To bed soon after 10 — Shower during the night.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 16, 1888.

A very hot day with strong southerly breeze. I worked during the mtf. & again in the afternoon at the etching of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" putting in the face. A very severe trial as I have no experience to guide me — it is altogether a matter of study of the works of other men — feeling & theory. In the afternoon, soon after 4, a light shower passed over us — At 5 I had the team harnessed & we went out for an hour — Evtf. Reading news paper & lounging on piazza — Got a pleasant letter from Ward in answer to my invitation to visit us here. This morning I had Stephen Dorsey do the stable work as I want Herrick to work at his carpenterng.
