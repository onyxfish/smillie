## Left Page

### February, Thursday 9, 1888

After a very uncomfortable night of headache I was unable to get out of bed in the m'f. I did not get up until 3.30 — After 4 I went to studio — then home feeling very shaky — Dinner at 6.30 — Went in big chair in front of a big fire & to bed soon after 11 —

Saw Will Kimball when he called to see Kate at 11.30.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 10, 1888

Very cold — overcast — in the afternoon snow which fell until 9 p.m — Got to studio at 10.30 — my studio very cold — lighted a soft coal fire in the grate. Mrs. Clement Woodise, with her mother, came in & spent most of what remained of the m'f. She bought 3 tiny etching p'fs. Home at 1 — lunch. Tom & Louise Cook lunched with Kate & me. Annie lunched with Mary Sprague Cook & friends — I went in to Miss Faukmoringe with Phil. Rehearsal Ticket then Kate & I went on to Rehearsal, leaving Jim & Tom at Kindergarten by the way — Kate & I sat in Char's seats — after Rehearsal went to Grady & McKeeve's & ordered two frames — then to Newmain's & ordered some hardware — then to studio — then home at 6 — very cold & snow driving — Charl came in sick with a headache & took toast & tea while we took dinner — he stayed until 10 — I wrote a note to Marvin expressing my pleasure at the prospect of etching Alma-Tadema's "Reading from Homer."
