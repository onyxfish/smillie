## Left Page

### December, Monday 3, 1888

Pleasant day — got to studio at 10.30
& painted awhile on Lake George
study — awhile on the Huddle Woods,
then got out an old painting of
"the ocean from near Crowninshield's
Point — Mass. coast". I sand papered
the sky & sea to get out brush marks
& then painted upon it. Home
to lunch miss Foshay at lunch as usual. — About 4.30
went up to Grady's about stretching
frames, then rode down to Waverly
Place — to Bisbee's, to try on my boots
— not ready — walked home. Katie
& Mollie Adams there to spend the
week with us — dinner — Evef.
Annie read aloud Shakespeare's
Winter's Tale, or rather, half of it — as
we have tickets to see Mary
Anderson in that play on Thurs.
evef next.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 4, 1888

The day opened dark, with snow squalls
before noon it was bright & sunny — several
snow squalls following — to studio at 9.45
— painted awhile on "the Ocean from near
Crowninshield's Point" — Then got out the
painting that I laid in for Charl. five
years ago — "Evef. Belt W., Lake George."
& worked upon that. — Home at 1 —
back at 2.30 & painted until 4 getting
the painting nearly finished — & I
think making a tolerably good thing
of it — About 5 went down to Bisbee's
& tried on my boots — accepted them
as a fit — walked home stopping
at the butcher's & grocer's & making
my purchases for to-morrow — Dinner
at 6.30 — Evef. Gel. Cook & Emily
came in for a short call — later,
Charl came in, staying until 10.
Then Annie resumed her reading
aloud & finished the Winter's Tale.
To bed at 11.
