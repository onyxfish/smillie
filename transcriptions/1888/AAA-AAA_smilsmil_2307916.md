## Left Page

### August, Sunday 5, 1888

A very sultry, sulky sort of a dog
day Sunday — did not get up
till late — help — after 9 — Spent
most of the day writing letters
— every body suffering from the
oppressive, humid heat — the
ther. did not get much above
80°. Sky overcast with thunder
ous clouds — Dinner at 2 —
Some more writing — to Mary
— S. W. Marvin — Kimmell & Voigt
— Thos E. Kirby — Century Club
Steward etc — a little after
4, horses at the door — we went
out for a short Ride — when
about a mile from home a heavy
shower suddenly dropped down
upon us & we got in pretty wet.
Tea at 7 — Ralph, quite out of sorts
— apparently an indigestion.
Wrote to Chave & at 9 took
my letters to P.O. After that, a
little reading & to bed shortly
before 11.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 6, 1888

An exceedingly disagreeable day
— hot & humid, with overcast or
cloudy sky. In the mfg made a
proof of [illegible] plate & a plain [illegible].
Took it to [illegible] Office & sent it to
McMarvin & Scribner's Sons. Attended
to other messages & home at 12 — Dinner
at 1 — In the afternoon decided, at
last, to sign all the [illegible] sent to me
by Stokes & in a letter tell him how
poor I consider them — It is useless to
fight ago; it he will not pay for good
[illegible] & his printer cannot do any better.
So I signed them in disgust & sent
them to him taking a vow that I would
do no more work for a cheap John —
Tea soon after 6 — At about 6.45 we got
away upon a drive & rode until 8.30 —
Late as usual. It was very warm until
a little before our return — Remainder
of evef, unharnessing horses & atten-
ding to chores about the house. Nearly
11 when I got to bed — an uncomfortable
sleepless night.
