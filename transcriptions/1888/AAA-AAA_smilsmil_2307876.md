## Left Page

### May, Thursday 17, 1888

A brilliant, beautiful day — Mrs
carey had a little scare. Thought that
Jim was in for Scarlet fever — sent for Gale
who pronounces it Rose Rash or German
measles. After bkft. wrote a short
Report to Dr. Isaac Lewis Peet. of Deaf &
Dumb Asylum — then to studio & to work
on a canvas 15×24 — a bad bother to
fill a frame that I want to sell —
Home at 1 o'clk. Lunch — Annie
had made up her mind that she
was so near recovered that she
no longer needed Miss Church, so,
she settled with her & we parted — She
has proved in every way agreeable —
worked hard & finished my painting
at 6.30 & home to dinner at 7 completely
tired out. Annie & I spent the
evg. in the nursery, I. most of the
time on the bed — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 18, 1888

Another dismal & disagreeable day — overcast
dark, raw. at times wet. To studio at 9.45
but did not feel at all like work & so, in
spite of efforts to do better I did nothing.
Gale came in & I spent part of the morning
mending his etching points & getting them
in working order for him. Home at 1 —
Lunch — after that, went to Kimmel Vogt's
in Canal St. Got my pfs. of my Tadeuma
etching & was disgusted to find that with
but one exception they are all poor, even
to downright badness — They are not even
sound prints — To studio at 4.30 — wrote to
Mr. Thomas of Scribner's Sons asking for the Tadeuma
plate that I may work upon it & get more pfs.
— also to W. Rhines — took his pfe. & 50 pfs.
from Boston Photo-gravure Co. to Siffy's office &
submit to him — then home at 6 — dinner.
Evg. in the nursery, with Annie — did
not go to the Phi Sigma entertainments
(theatrical) of Mr. J.H. Morse's boys —
To bed soon after 10.
