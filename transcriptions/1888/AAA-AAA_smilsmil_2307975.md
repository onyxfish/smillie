## Left Page

### December, Saturday 1, 1888

Bright & pleasant. To studio at
10 & spent rest of the morning
painting on "The Huddle Wood"
Home at 1 o'clk — lunch — soon
after 2 Annie, Jim & I started
out — first to 2°. Nat. Bult — Then to
J. & C. Johnston's & looked at a few
carpets — then to Arnold & Constable's
& saw, by daylight, some of the carpets
that we saw there last evef, & then
to Sloane's & selected a dining
room carpet — then to the Venetschgi
collection of pictures & spent an hour
or two there — then Annie & Jim went
home & I went to Union Sq. to find a Wood
carpet store — home at 6 — dinner — Eve
Charl came in & at 9 we went to the
Century Club — a very fine meeting &
discussion & muddle over the matter
of empowering the management to pur-
chase a new site — about 11 when the
meeting adjourned — met Ehninger there
just off from Steamer — talked with Mr.
Heber Bishop about some etchings — after
midnight Yale, Geo. Charl & I walked up.
To bed abt 1 o'clk. A.M.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 2, 1888

Brilliant day — up late — bkf —
after 9 — spent the mf with the
children — about 1 o'clk. Jus. Val-
entine came & we walked up to
59° 8' — then back to dinner at 2
Eliz. Howe joining us at the table
V. left immediately after dinner.
Eliz — left after 4 — I walked down
with her — then to 5th Ave. Hatel and
spent half an hour with Ehninger.
Home soon after 5. wrote to Kate —
Mary Willard came in & took
tea with us. Ralph out of sorts with
another cold — coughing — This mf
Annie took Jim to Sunday School —
his first visit — Eve Annie
read alone from Rob'. Elsmere &
we got to bed at about 11.
