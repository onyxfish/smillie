## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 5, 1888

A dull m'g until after 10 o'clk. when it cleared to be a bright
& mild day — To studio at 10.15 &
commenced painting on a small
canvas, laid in a long time ago
& for which I have a frame — titled
"the River's Source". Home at 1 — Annie
& the Adams girls lunched with Alice
Ireland — I got back to studio at
2.30 & painted until after 4 — then
I made measurements for some
etching frames & before 5 went to
Brady's but they had no such patterns
as they had promised to have ready
for me — I ordered frame for "The Pool
in the Woods". Home at 6 — dinner
— a quiet ev'g. Reading — wrote
a note to Uncle Willie about final
payment on the Greenwood lot.
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 6, 1888

Wet early in the m'g, a brilliant
day after 11. Got to studio at 10.45
& to work painting on "the River's Source"
or "Headwaters" — Home to lunch & back
& painted until 4 — cleaned up — Took
a half hours rest & home at 6 — Dinner
at 6.30 — Eliz. Howe joining us — at a
little before 8 o'clk. Annie, Eliz, Katie
Mollie & I walked to Palmer's Theatre.
Bdwy & 30 St. & saw Mary Anderson in
"A Winter's Tale. It was a very de-
lightful entertainment, but mainly
because it was so beautifully put up on
the stage — The play in itself is not very
interesting to me & the actors were a
commonplace lot. Mary Anderson
scored all the points — Her dance in the
shepherds fete was one of the most pic-
turesque & graceful things I ever saw.
She was a living creature released
from the sculpture of a Greek frieze.
The Statue scene was a remarkable effect
— The pose based upon the Venus de Milo
— Home soon after 11 & right to bed.
