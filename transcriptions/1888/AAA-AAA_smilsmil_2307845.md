## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 14. 1888.

Bright early on[ly]. Then overcast with more snow — Evelf. again clear with rising ther. My eyes still looks badly &
is some discomfort to me, but I determined to protect it & go to studio, which I did at about 10.30. Kurtz sent second reduction of the Tadema & I cautiously commenced a tracing upon gelatine giving my eye as little work & as much rest as possible. — Home to lunch at 1. The streets, with the barricades of snow, are a sight to see. Back to studio at 2.30 & worked until near 5. Then took my half dozen of mounted [pfs] of the Durand etching to W. Huntington & got his autograph upon them. — Home soon after 6. Dinner. Evelf. Read a little but spent most of the time pacing the floor or sitting & holding my head — all for the sake of my poor eye. — To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 15. 1888.

Brilliant, mild day — Snow running away in rivers of water. Added to my trouble with my eye I am just about used up with a blind headache — Got to studio at 10.30 — some time writing notes then, outlining or tracing the Tadema on gelatine. At 12 went to 2d. Nat. Bank & Safe Deposit Co — then to Bank of Metropolis Co. 15th St & University Pl. & made deposit to Co. of Diet Kitchen — Home to lunch at 1.15. To studio again at 2.45 & in spite of blood shot eye & headache continued to trace the Tadema. At 5. [put up] [pf.] of "A Glass with the Squire" & "Portrait of Durand" & took them to Mr. Walter D. F. Day 36 E. 34th St. — then to Grady & McKeever's & gave order for another Water Color frame. Home at 6.30 — dinner — Evelf. was so used up by my headache that I went to bed before 9 o'clk. — Suffered all night with it.
