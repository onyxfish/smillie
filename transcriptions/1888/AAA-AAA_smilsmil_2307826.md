## Left Page

### February, Sunday 5, 1888

A lovely day, almost Spring-
like in its mildness — Got up
with a mean headache —
B'kp't at 9 — Read paper for a
while — then wrote until 12
when I went out with Annie
& Jim — we walked down to
W.C. Elptin. A there lost 5 m —
then she & Jim went on to Aunt
Anna's — I stayed awhile longer
among the pictures — met Winslow
Homer & talked of his intention to
etch large "A Voice from the Cliff" —
Home about 1.30 — Dinner soon
after 2 — then more writing — to
C.H. Hart, Jas. Macdonough, S.R.
Koehler — Herrick & sister Mary —
It was after 7 when we had tea —
about 9 I went to G.C.D. with
letters — then a walk — It was
11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### February, Monday 6, 1888

A beautiful, bright winter day — to
studio at 10 & worked until 11¾ on
A.B.N.Co's drawing Banco de Cubico
Chile — then went to 29 W. 26th — Photo
Gallery & met Annie & the children
& had photos of the children taken —
out at 12 — went to Bank — then to
Snoardcer's & at 1 home to lunch —
At 2.30 Annie, Kate & I went up to the
Met. Museum of Art — I thought it was
Annual meeting, until I got up there &
then found that I was just one week
too soon — In the car we met Eliz. Howe,
her sister Mrs. Hess & her sister-in-law — We
all went to the Museum — looked at pic-
tures until 4.30 — then out — Kate & I
walked through the Park to 59th St. 45th Ave.
then Kate took 'bus, I walked on home —
Dinner at 6.30 — Chase with us — used up
with a cold — Stayed through the evng —
I spent much time arranging old
files of Ill. Lond. News.
