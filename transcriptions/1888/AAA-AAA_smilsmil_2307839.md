## Left Page

### March, Friday 2, 1888

Up at about 4 A.M. with Master Ralph & after I got to bed did not sleep until after 6 — so, I felt rather "seedy" again during the day which was raw and unpleasant, with, at times, electricity attempts at rain — To studio at nearly 12 after having made a visit to Pajon at 2.64 Fifth Ave. cor. 29th St. where he showed me two plates with ground on & portraits nearly drawn in — He was very agreeable & we spent half an hour in lively conversation. He will etch portrait of Mr. Rives, about 6×9 in. for $1000 — if Mr. Rives is willing — After leaving him I went to Vautine's & bought two jars & cream — to the studio — then on to Metropolis Bank for Annie & to Dr. Blackwell's with a note — then to studio — There I wrote notes to S. R. Koehler & Clark — a lady from Orange came in to make enquiries about Mr. Manly — Home to lunch at 1.30 — 2.45 when I got back — Etched, subject to several interruptions on "Competitive Blowers" a title that I shall change to "What o'clock is it?") Mrs. Verdcnal & her daughter called & later Howard White called. He came to talk a little about Will's affairs, he being one of the trustees. I worked until considerably after 5. It was after 6 when I got home — Dinner — After that I went to George Cook's for a rubber bag — not having what I wanted, bought at a drug store & Annie applied it filled with ice, to her breast that is caked & troubling her. Evening I read aloud for awhile to Annie — To bed after 11 — about 3 A.M. a repetition of Ralph's evidences — all hands up with him — He quieted & went to sleep at about 4.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 3, 1888

Rather a cold & windy day — The cold increasing at night — In the very early a.m. sleet fell & the streets were very slippery. By the time the sun shone out & at noon there was no trace of the ice & sleet. To studio soon after 10 & by noon had completed the drawing in of "What o'clk is it?" But having no asphaltum varnish I could not prepare my plate for the acid — Home to lunch at 30. Back at 2.45 & about the afternoon cutting paper & mounts for Etching pts. & fussing with the pts. themselves in getting them ready to mount. It was 6.45 when I got home to dinner — Both Annie & I sat in the parlor & read — Geo. Cook came in for a few minutes — Annie's trouble with her breast does not seem to progress or diminish — She has kept ice on it most of the day & eve. Did not go to monthly meeting of "Century" — to bed soon after 11.
