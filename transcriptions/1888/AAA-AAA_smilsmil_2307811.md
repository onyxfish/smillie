## Left Page

### January, Friday 6, 1888

Dismal day. Overcast morning —
Before noon sleet began to fall
& the pavements were soon cov-
ered with ice — later snow fell
— then again sleet. To studio
before 10 & to work on the fore-
ground of "Feeding by the Way side".
Having washed it all out I
put it in anew — Home at 1 —
back at 2.30 & again worked on
drawing altho' much hindered
by the darkness — Home at 6.
Dinner — Eve'g. I read for
awhile aloud to Annie & then
she read aloud to me some of
Stevenson's Essays. To bed
about 10.30.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 7, 1888

Dark, wet day — near 10 when I got
to studio — Got to work again on "Feed-
ing by the Wayside" & fussed away
all the mt'r over it — benefitting it
in its poor way I think. Home
to lunch & back at usual time —
then I worked some on the Rowsley
bit but it was too dark to see —
Miss Howard, Emma Bourne's friend,
who is studying at the M.A.f.D. School
& at the Hague called — about 5 I
walked up to Grady's & ordered a
small frame. Home soon after
6. Found Annie feeling quite
used up & miserable — Dinner.
Eve'g. I read aloud to her for a
little while — She went to bed at
9.30 & I prepared soon after 10 to
follow suit by Master Ralph made
other dispositions — It was more of
a fear than a necessity for he did
not behave very badly after all, but
I slept (not much) with my clothes on
until 5.30 A.M.
