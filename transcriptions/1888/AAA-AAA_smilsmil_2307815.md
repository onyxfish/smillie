## Left Page

### January, Saturday 14, 1888

Bright, clear day. To studio at 10 —
Very soon finished 5½ × 13 — adding
some crude, cool color in the fore-
ground. Men took it over to the N.A.
Men to Bank. Back to Studio at 11.30
— Yale came in for a few minutes.
I set to work preparing studio for
the cleaners & Printing press that
was to be sent up to-day from the A.
P. N. Co. Home to lunch & back
at usual time. The afternoon given
over entirely to breaking packing
boxes into kindling wood. & fixing
shelves in closets. Rested for awhile
before going home at 5.30 — Dinner at
6.30 — At 8.30 went to Annual Meeting
Century Club — Horribly close & hot — a
free meeting, drawn out by an opposition
ticket. I voted the regular ticket
(which was beaten) & then home at
11 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 15, 1888

Dark, rainy day — Up at 8 — Bkf —
spent some time reading — then
worked awhile on working drawings
for printing table for studio. Dinner
at 2 — About 3 took a nap that
lasted until near 5. Geo. Eniely
& Valentine came in. I wrote
letter to Mary — & one to Herrick —
& a note to Jne Durand in Paris
about etching pt. of his father's por-
trait. About 8 had a cup of tea.
At 9 went to R.C. depot & mailed
letter — on my return home Charl
came in. He left soon after 10
& I got to bed shortly after 11.
