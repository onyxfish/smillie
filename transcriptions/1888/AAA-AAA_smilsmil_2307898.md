## Left Page

### June, Saturday 30, 1888.

*Chare arrived from N.Y.*

Cool, breezy day — cloudad most of the time with threats of showers at times. In spite of a head ache I worked all day on etching of Calverley's Burns. I got a very good transfer the first thing in the m'g & by 4 P.m. I had it etched — Then we went out Riding, wondering why we had heard nothing from Chare who is expected to come from N.Y. on the 1 P.m. train. On our return from our ride, at the P.O. we got a note from Char. saying that he would start as expected — so, we sent Snell right away to depot for him. Tea at 7.30. Chare arr'd a few minutes after 9. We chatted until nearly 11. It was 11.30 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 1. 1888.

Another cloudy, cool day, clearing charmingly in the afternoon — the day slipped away as many another Sabbath day has, without anything serious being accomplished. We had btlf — at 9 — & dinner at 2 & rode from 4.30 to 6.30 — after tea W. Parke came in with Mr. Brown of Scranton. Eve'g wrote a note concerning Uncle Willie to Frank Wittenkamp — a sub-editor of Appleton's encyclopedia of Biography. Very tired & sleepy — To bed at about 10.30.
