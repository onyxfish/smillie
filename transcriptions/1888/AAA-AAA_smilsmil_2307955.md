## Left Page

### October, Monday 22, 1888

Ther. fell to 28° last night — the day much milder & quiet — For an hour or two the sun shone in the middle of the day — but again the sky was overcast in the afternoon & it looked very much like snow. At 10.30 Jim & I went down town & until noon I was running about settling up little matters — After dinner I worked until 5 o'clk. getting the vines netting up at W.W. cor. of the house & fixing the running vine upon it — Sherrick cleared out studio closet & took the boxes with books etc up to the attic. Before 6 I went down town — Iwell. Dan Saupe & wife called — after 9 o'clk. I got to work packing big house box. After 11 when I got to bed, no word yet from Gretha.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 23, 1888

Heavy fog all day long — no rain but everything dripping wet. I spent much of the day finishing drawings for the table & seat at south window of oak bedroom — went down town at noon & again at 6 — Iwell. I worked until 10 packing big house case — Kate & Mary, our domestics, are all ready to start for Hy. To-morrow w'f at 6 — we to follow on Thursday — No word yet from Gretta — mysterious & very provoking as it doubles Annie's work.
