## Left Page

### April, Thursday 19, 1888.

Slight, filmy overcast, a mild day with a chilly feeling — To studio before 10 — Spent the m'f with graver on figure lying in foreground, on Tadema plate — Home at 1 — To N.A. of D. before 2.30 — Annual meeting — a very full one — as usual, the air was foul from want of ventilation. When it became unbearably hot and close, then the windows would be thrown open & then there were draughts. A lively meeting — much interest in the matter of electing Deweing & Shirlaw as Academicians & Blum, Chase & Minor as Associates — Dinner about 6.30 — very crowded — very hot — a good dinner & some mild amusement after — Songs by J.G. Brown, C.P. Cranch & Geo. Yewell — Stories by Geo. Butler and Louis Lang — The Merchant of Venice by Sellstedt & Remarks by Frank Millett, Ed. Ward & Jno. F. Weir — Broke up at 10.30 — then home & to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 20, 1888.

Rain in the m'f — clearing in the afternoon — bright moonlight at night. To studio at 10 — for a wonder my head was clear in spite of yesterday's dissipation — I worked with dry point & graver in the m'f. on Tadema plate — & laid ground & varnished the plate before going home to lunch at 1.30 — It was 3 when I got back to studio. Etched in some small bits that had been left uncovered in the first etching. Mr. Thomas, of Scribner's, called to learn the state of the plate — I bit in the small patches of work & cleaned off plate — It was nearly 7 when I got home to dinner — Eve'g Annie read aloud a part of Minnie Putnam Jacob's article on the education of her child —
