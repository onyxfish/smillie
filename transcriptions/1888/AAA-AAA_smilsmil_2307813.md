## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 10, 1888

A light fall of snow last night with a sleety rain this mng. clearing brilliantly before noon, Then, horrible walking. To studio at 10 & to work on 7×13 — Cattle on a Hillside — or "Attending to Business" as they are all intent upon their feeding. I finished outline & then commenced laying in the color — Home at 1. Emma J — lunched with us — back at 2·15 & worked as long as I could see, getting it well advanced. — At 5 went to N.A. Then walked down to 14th St — up Bdway to 5th Ave — on to Central Park & then home — nearly 4 miles — Eliz. Howe Ed. & Mary Cook dined with us — Ge. & Emily & Valentine came in the evef. — They left before 10.30 — To bed soon after 11.

In the afternoon Mr. [illegible] from Ketchel the print publisher, called to learn my lowest price for making an etching 14×20" after Winslow Homer's "Voice from the Cliff" — I told him $800.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 11, 1888

Bright & cold. To studio before 10 and to work on 7×13 drawing & got it pretty well finished before I went home to lunch at 1 — After lunch went to bank & got a check book for Jucie — To studio at 2·45 — Passe partout for 7×13 am — found that I had made my drawing one inch too wide — after a time determined to cut the inch off the foreground, which I did & before I went home I mounted it upon a piece of trunk board — Home soon after 6 — dinner — Evef. I worked awhile upon plans for forming table. & I read awhile — To bed about 10.30.
