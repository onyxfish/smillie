## Left Page

### September, Thursday 6, 1888

Another brilliant day — Spent some time in the mtf. working with Coyle & Decker in getting the ground for Strawberry bed cleaned up, worked with pick & crowbar — at 12 went hunting for a platform wagon to borrow — found it at Mr. Woodin's. And had Decker hitch the greys to it & take Dozier over to Roger Searle's & get Strawberry plants. They got back at 3,30 with 125 — we went out Riding at 4 — back at 5.30 — finding it almost too cool to be pleasant. The plants were all set before 6 o'clk. Tea at 6,30 — Evtf. Reading & lounging.

During the day I got Etching Room all ready for the biting in of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" — mixed mon Mordant & tested my large wooden tray — glass syphon, etc. etc.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 7, 1888

Bright mtf. — afternoon overcast — rain at 6 p.m. I spent a part of the mtf. in garden & with Coyle & Dozier who completed the setting of the strawberry bed at noon. At 11 o'clk. I went down Town — met Annie — back soon after 12 — Dinner at 1 — Afternoon. Decker went to Mr Baxter's & borrowed stone boat & spent the afternoon moving the Stone taken from the strawberry bed. I picked some Palsies — took them into Etching Room & commenced a dry point after them — worked for awhile with Decker. Tea at 6,30 — Evtf. with newspaper & then a game of Bazique. To bed at 10.30.
