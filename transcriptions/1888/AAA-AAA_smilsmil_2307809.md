## Left Page

### January, Monday 2, 1888

A very pleasant day — To studio at
10 o'clk. Most of the day about
working over "On the Eastern Shore —
Maryland" — improving it in
some ways & inquiring it in others.
It is not at all what I had hoped
to make it — then worked awhile
on the cloudy day one — & after that
washed out the foreground of
"Feeding by the Wayside" which I
find, on getting it into the frame
was much too heavy. Charlie
came in at dusk & with Geo. we
walked down to 14th St. & then up to
38th St. I got home soon after 6. and
found there Geo. & Finley, making a New
Years call — nearly 7 when we had
dinner — Evely I read aloud for
awhile to Annie — & then worried with
the plans for a printing Fable —
To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 3, 1888

Overcast m'f. with a few flying flakes
of snow but before noon it cleared very
pleasantly. To studio at 10 o'clk. and
to work on sketch — "An Inlet, Chesapeake
Bay" & finished it before the eve'g.
Went to Bank before 1 o'clk. & home
to lunch about 1.30 — near 3 when
I got back to studio. Fredk Stokes
came to me India-ink sketch "Com-
petitive Blowers" accepting the proposed
terms for Etching. Filling out of sorts
I went out before 5 o'clk. & had & into
a long walk, getting in to dinner at
6.15. Eve'g, wrote notes to Messrs
Stokes & [illegible] Rives — also reading.
To bed at 11.
