## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 21, 1888

fairly pleasant day, altho' at times unpromising — To studio at 10.30 and after a little work finished the india ink drawing of Mr. J.S. Rives — cut a mount for it, pasted it & put it under weights — Home to lunch soon after — after having gone to Bank & safe deposit & got some money & Chesapeake & Ohio securities. Back to studio at 2.45 & spent some time clearing up about my etching table getting ready to go to work there. Dinner at usual time — At 8 took Annie around to [Bro.?] Geo's in 39th St & then I went to N.A. of D. — Special meeting called to rescind the resolution of the Council to admit glass over paintings at the Spring exhbn — 22 for glass · 19 against. I voted to uphold Council action because it was too late now to back down — am opposed to glass over the pictures — it is beneath the dignity of an Academy exhbn — it is more beneath dignity to back & fill & change courses after having made such an announcement — & when the Exhbn pictures have almost begun to arrive. Home at 10.20 — Then to bed — During the night Kasper had his first regular squalling spell,

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 22, 1888

Brilliant & delightful day — To studio at 10.30. Packed Rives drawing to Effs. to Washington & wrote letter to Mr. R. — a second note to Col. Elliott F. Sheperd. Did considerable clearing up. Charle came in at about noon — He & [I?] walked up town with me at 1 o'clk. Lunch — back to studio at 2.30 & continued the work of the m.f. until after 4 — There to Upps. with Rives P.P. Office closed — Walked down to Keppel's & spent some time looking over the collection of Buhot's etchings & was very much interested — Home at 6. Edith Wright arrived before dinner & stayed with us — In parlor until near 9 when I retired to write for awhile on Coleman's letter. Gave up before 10 — Domestic duties until 11 — then to bed.
