## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 11. 1888.

Cloudy & fresh — To studio at 9.30 —
For some time engaged trimming
& fixing the plt. taken yesterday —
then to work on "What o'clk. is it?"
etching, to get true shapes to take
a print to show Mr Stokes — worked
with dry point & burnisher — Home
after 1 — to lunch — back at 2.30 &
continued my mornings work until
4.30 — then wet down some paper
to print on to-morrow. Left Studio
at 5.30 — in the hallway met Chat.
Baker & Ret. to studio with him.
He took away my small oil paint-
ing "A bit on Marblehead Neck" to try
to sell it. It was 6.45 when I
got home — dinner — Evef.
again Annie read aloud from
"A Roman Singer".

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 12. 1888.

A nasty day with wind & showers
clearing beautifully about sunset.
To studio at 9.45 — worked for a while
on "What o'clk. is it?" & about noon
took four imps., working a little on one
of the faces between imps. It was
1.45 before I got home to lunch &
after 3 when I ret. to studio — Then
I tracked down, to stretch, the four
imps. taken in the m'f., after
which I wrote some instructions
for Herrick — walked down to 14th
St & then home by 5th Ave. by 6.30 —
dinner — Evef. Annie read aloud
from "A Roman Singer" until 9 — then I
took her to her bro. Geo's in 39th St — no
one in — brought her home & then I went
to Union League Club to see Etchings
by Women Etchers of America — Great
Tariff meeting of the Club — so that I could
not see all of the works — home soon
after 10 & right to bed.
