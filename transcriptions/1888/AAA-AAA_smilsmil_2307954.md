## Left Page

### October, Saturday 20, 1888

Brilliant & cloudless m'f until about 10 when great banks of cold & windy clouds came down from the N.W. During the day some snow flakes flew — some fine color effects of a sombre character seen from studio's bay window. Got to work upon plans for window seat & table in oak bed room & spent most of the m'f upon them — After dinner had a nap & then again to work on drawings as long as I could see. Went down town at 6 — then tea — most of the ev'f in dark room developing some goose photos that I took in the m'f. Ralph again troubles me — after 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 21, 1888

Ther. fell to 29° last night — all day it blew very hard & cold — dull laden colored clouds covering the sky. Did not get up until late & spent some time getting the seven fires (not counting the kitchen fire) to do their level best. Worked in studio for a time on plan for table & seat in our bed room — then wrote a letter to Katie Throop. Dinner at 2 — at 4, with Jim, went to Heindi's & looked at his barn & arranged to have it fixed up so that the team of greys can be kept there this winter. Home about 5. Ev'f. wrote to St. Chas. W. Allen — to Postmaster of Station 7, N.Y. Annie wrote to M'f, grocer, milk man, plumber, etc. & at 9 I took letters to P.O. very cold & windy. To bed at 10.30.
