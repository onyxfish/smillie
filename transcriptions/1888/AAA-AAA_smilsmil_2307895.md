## Left Page

### June. Sunday 24. 1888.

A very warm day but delightfully tempered by breezes. Bkfs. after
8 — the mf spent rambling over the place & lolling about the house,
doing a little reading & now and then making an effort at writing.
Dinner at 2 — a little nap and more writing — The edge of a thunder
storm passed over us at about 4
P. m. — At 5 Herrick came up
with the group — At 5.25 Annie
Jim, Ralph, Petra & I started
on a ride — altho' warm the air
was exceedingly fragrant and
we enjoyed the ride very much.
Home at 7 — tea — About 8 another
shower came up & lasted for an
hour or more. — I completed my
letters — to Geo., Charles & Calverley.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 25. 1888.

A delightful day — breezy & comparatively cool. Bkfs. at 8 — went
downtown about 9.30 & did not get
back until after 11. Cases from
Studio arr. and I unpacked
them — Dinner at 1 — Afternoon
Parke came up on horseback and
spent some time — then Bessie P.
came with two of her friends —
May Wickham and [illegible]. At last
I got started on Calverley's Burns
statue sketches but did not make
much progress. At 5 o'clk. we
went out for a ride & got in at
6.40 — then tea — Sat about
until after 8 — then wrote a
letter or two. At 10 dressed A's
breast, which seems to be improving — & to bed at 11.
