## Left Page

### December, Monday 17, 1888

A heavy & persistent fall of rain all day — quite mild — overf. light fog, no rain. Got to studio at 9.30 — to work mounting etching plt. & touching on plt. f "What o'clk. is it?" Dr. Daw. Stimson came in at 12 & stayed until 1 talking over Century Club matters. He is a strong partisan of Lawrence's. Home to lunch — back & worked over my plt. until 4 — then in big chair & had a comfortable nap when Miss Abbatt came in — chatting until 5 — then home — Dinner at 6.30 — At 8 to Century Club — to attend a meeting of Nom. com. W. Harper's sister died a day or two ago, so there was no meeting — home at 9.10 & A. read aloud from Robt. Elsmere until 10.30 to bed at 11.

A. went with Mrs. Jo. Ireland to the clinch funeral at the Romish Cathedral at 10 — Mrs. J. was able to be in time for the noon train & ret'd — to her home

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 18, 1888

Bleak, windy & cold. To studio at 10 — again at the work of mounting etchings with various retentions. Chawfsney introduced W. Halstett of Boston who has a new Photogravure process. He spent some time with me — then Manly. The etcher came in with things to show me. I got plt. ready for Miss Hallowell & took it to Exp. office at 12. Then to miss Seggett's with two plain copies of "Ports & Etchers". Home at 12.30 — lunch at once. Emily Cook, Miss Williams, Edna & Tom there — at 1 o'clk. started for the Met. museum of Art. having in charge Annie, Emily Cook & Miss Williams — there was a great throng — & we met many friends. The Mendelssohn Glee club sang two pieces & Dr. Prime delivered a long address in addition to some shorter ceremonies — we got home before 5 — Evef. wrote my letter to Kate to go by steamer to-morrow m'f, mailed it — then Annie & I read aloud from Robt. Elsmere until 10.30 — then to bed.
