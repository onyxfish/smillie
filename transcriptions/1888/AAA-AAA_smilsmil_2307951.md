## Left Page

### October, Sunday 14, 1888

Fog in the mtfs. no rain but heavy clouds & wind — at night
a fine moonlight — I got up
with my headache & was quite
useless during the mtf. Parke
sent Norman up to tell me that
Dr Norris had not yet Ret'd. to Phila —
so, at 3 o'clk. Herrick drove Annie —
Jim & [illegible] down to his place with a letter
from Parke. The Dr. rec'd me very well —
gave me his careful attention & then
pronounced the trouble in my eye a sort
of Herpes resulting from lack of constitu-
tional tone & advised bracing up — He
prescribed three 1/8 gr. doses p'd day of
calomel — three 3 gr. doses of quinine —
some whiskey & aloric acid wash — To
me this is depressing — it means that
Herpes has come to stay. Dr. N. would
not accept any fee. Our ride was a
cold & muddy one — Home at 5.45. Tea
soon after — evef wrote a note to
Yale — Annie read aloud for awhile.
Went to P.O. at 9.30 — then home & to bed
at 10.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 15, 1888

Heavy fog in the early mtf. clearing
before 10 — 2 or 3 hours of very pleasant
sunshine — the afternoon leaden &
dull — at night, some faint moonshine.
I made use of the mtf sunshine
& got a number of photos of the
three geese that Conklin left here
a week ago last Sat. Spent an
hour in the dark room before dinner
— as soon after dinner as we could
get away — about 2.15, Annie, Jim,
the baby & I, drove down to Mrs. de
Schweinitz's & took her & her baby out
to ride — back at 4.30 — At 5 again
I went down town & got the mail
& the medicines prescribed by Dr.
Norris yesterday — most of the evef
was spent in the dark room — this
mtf. Gretta went home to make a
visit, Annie having to do her duty in
the mean time. To bed at 11.
