## Left Page

### July, Monday 16, 1888

A lovely day, if we were not praying for rain — Ther. 60° in the mt., making a fine pleasant — it did not get above 72° in the studio. I spent a good part of the mt. down town attending to little messages — home at 12. Got studio into a little order — dinner at 1. Got out Alma-Tadema pht. & place & to work upon it; I see clearly what is needed to improve it greatly. At 5 went out riding with Katchen, Eleanor, Willie & Jim & did not get in until near 7 — then tea — Mr. de Schweinitz & Misses Lord & Dwight called — lovely moonlight night — To bed about 10.30 —

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 17, 1888

And yet another dry day, altho we were not left without hopes of rain eventually — the wind blew strongly (& cool) from the east & at times the sky was overcast, but in the afternoon it was as bright & clear as ever. I spent some hours of the day at work upon the Alma-Tadema "Reading Homer" with graver & dry point. In the afternoon about 5 Annie, Katchen, Rob., & I went out riding — went to Stone Bridge Creamery & got some cream — home at 6.20. Tea before 7 — After that went over to Tarbell's — & talked with him about hay & horses. Saw Mr. T. & her daughter. Annie, Katchen & Rob. went to the Jessup's then to Baptist festival — They got home about 9 o'clk.
