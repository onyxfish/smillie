## Left Page

### March, Sunday 4, 1888

Clear, cold & windy — got up late
& did not get through b'kf't until
near 10. Spent the rest of the m'f't
with Annie & the children. At
12 walked down to the Century
& saw the pictures — then, to
D'r. Emily Blackwell's & saw her
concerning Annie — Home at
1.30 — dinner after 2 — later,
took Jim out & showed him
the site of the recent fire — 42nd S't.
& Lex. Ave. Then home & took
a nap. A little to eat after 7 —
Evett wrote to Mary & a letter to
S.C.F. Hammond. To bed about
11.30.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 5, 1888

Raw & overcast with a free of about
an inch of snow at night. Got up
with a headache that made me un-
comfortable all day. Got to studio at
about 10.30. Spent the rest of the m'f't
varnishing my plates and the big wooden
biting tray that I brought from Montrose.
To lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — but
little accomplished in the afternoon.
Miss Verdeval came & commenced a
drawing from a cast. W. Kelly — editor
of the Art Review made a long call.
I managed to get two or three p'f's
mounted. At 6 went to Adams Exp't.
& sent p'g. with p'f. of S. R. Koehler at
Nat. Museum at Washington. Home at
6.30 — dinner — Evett wrote a letter
to Mr. Geo. L. Rives — & one to Herrick.
Tried to go to bed soon after 10 but
an attempt to induce Ralph to
take his late supper from a bottle
postponed the hour of rest until
midnight.
