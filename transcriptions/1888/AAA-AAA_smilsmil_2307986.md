## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 25. 1888

A delightful day — clear & very mild.
Bkfp. before 9 — after that the presents were
opened & inspected — Jim & Ralph at
high glee — Spent most of the rest of
mf with the youngsters & at about 1 ock
went out with Jim & had a short walk.
At 2 o'clk. we sat down to a high lunch.
Bouillon — oysters — chicken. Charlotte Russe —
cakes, oranges, grapes, chocolate & coffee.
Twenty were at the Table — 10 adults & 10
youngsters Ed² & Mary Cook & their 5 children —
Ge. & Hinely Cook — Ge. & Nellie Smillie and
2 children — Two of Walter Cook's children
C. remained at home with Ed² who is
just recovered from chicken pox.) Chat,
Smillie & Miss Williams, Annie — myself
& Jim — Gordon & his nurse — Ralph & his
nurse — Lawrence Swift — & his mother
joined us later — all seemed to enjoy the
lunch — after that the youngsters played until
near 5 o'clk. when Chas. & I presented the
greatest Moral & Intellectual Show on earth —
a poor magic lantern etc — strung along
with what pretended to be a story —
It was
enjoyed by the children & all got away
before 6 with still plenty of energy left.
I then wrote an addition to my Sunday
letter to Mary giving an account of the dinner.
At 7 we had, some tea & then I took Mary's
letter to Station 7. Then home & spent
the rest of the evening reading — to bed
at about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 26. 1888

Still warmer & clear — To studio at
10 & spent the mf mounting drawings
& that sort of thing — Home at 1 o'clk &
after lunch Rode down to 10th S. Met
Lockwood de Forest at his door & had a
chat with him. Went to Nicoll's & spent
more than an hour talking over Century
nominations — From there to Kelpbel's & to
Wunderlich's & got cats of etchings. Then
to Century club & spent some time there.
To studio at 5 — Geo. came in & we
talked Century nominations until near
6 when he walked up to 35th St. with
me — While we were at dinner
Uncle Willie came in — he joined
us & stayed overnight — Chas. came
in before 9 o'clk. & stayed until
10 — general chatteration — To bed
at about 10.30.
