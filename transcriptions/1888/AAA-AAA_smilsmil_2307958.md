## Left Page

### October, Sunday 28, 1888

Disagreeable day — muggy and
overcast with rain in the after-
noon & evening — We got up late.
I spent a part of the morning
reading — after 11, bandaging my
eye carefully, Jim & I went out
for a little walk — down Fifth Ave
around Madison Sq. stopping at br.
Geo's in E. 28th S. Saw Nellie, Geo. in
the mean time being at our house.
Home about 1 — Afternoon & evt
wrote to Kate — Charle — Gilbert &
James (Mr. men. Montrose) and to
Ticket Agent, D. L & W. R.R. Bingham-
ton — Annie read aloud for awhile
& we got to bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 29, 1888

A fairly pleasant day — clouded in
the evef & a few drops of rain — I
went to Dr. Noyes at 9 Am. & was
there until near 11, most of the time
in the hands of his Associate, Dr Hunter,
who was trying to fit glasses to my
eyes — Dr. N. says my eye is getting
on nicely — then attended to my
domestic messages & went to Union
Storage & Transfer Co. about our goods
shipped from New Milford last Thurs.
Home to lunch — To studio for awhile
in the afternoon — went to Adams Exp
Co. in 23d S. about non-delivery of the
painting "A Goldsmith's Daughter" —
Home at 5.30 — by appointment, to meet
Mr. Moran the Carpenter — Dinner
at 6.30 — Evef Annie & I spent
at Edw. Cook's — E. 37th S. — Home
at 10 & to bed. [illegible — entire passage]
