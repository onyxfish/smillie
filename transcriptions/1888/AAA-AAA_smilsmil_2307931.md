## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 4, 1888

Bright m'f. but soon overcast. Cloudy & cool with east wind
through the day — Smitts started painting the barn & out buildings.
Could not get any one to plough
my berry plat, much to my vex-
ation — After much work out of
doors I go in to studio & varnished
back of "Goldsmith's Daughter" after
I had completed the "drawing
in" of the lines — then varnished
the inside of the large wooden
tray — Dinner at 1 — again
with the men — At 11 went
out with the team, Annie & Ralph.
Great "doings" in Town — Veterans'
Camp on the Fair ground and
Firemen's Parade — much so-called
music — horses excited — a short drive —
tried to find O'Brien to get him to plow
a strawberry bed for us — back at 5.30
went down town — talked with Deans
about raising the valuation on our property
here for taxes — he is an assessor — then went
to Mr. Raynesford to get a team to plow —
home to tea at 6.45 — every. Read
news paper & wrote a note to
Jas. Carmalt — To bed before 10.
A small spot of supperation appeared
on Annie's breast to-day —

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 5, 1888

Brilliant, beautiful day — quite
cool — As we went to bed early
last night we got up late this
m'f. Bkfs. at 8.30 — O'Brien came
then with his team & commenced
ploughing the patch for strawberries
etc — all day the work was kept up
at it, ploughing, dragging & picking
stones — I spent a long time in the
m'f. in the garden, chiefly among
the tomato vines — I hardly went
into my studio at all — Down town
at 12 with Jim & has his hair cut —
Home to dinner at 1 — Again at work
in strawberry patch — The G.A.R. Veterans
have a camp on the Fair ground and
had a surprise & sham battle last
night & a pitched battle to-day at 11.
our horses got so wild with the noise
of cannon & guns that, altho' harnessed
did not dare take them out — I went
to the fair ground where I met Annie
& Jim — back home in 3/4 of an hour and
worked on strawberry patch. Picking
up stones until near 6. again down
town — Tea at 6.30 — every. Reading
news paper — wrote to W.S. Little of
Rochester, ordering berry vines.
The opening on Annie's breast has
spread a little but from appearances
I am hopeful that it will go no further
