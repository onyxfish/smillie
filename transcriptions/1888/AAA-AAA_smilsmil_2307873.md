## Left Page

### May, Friday 11. 1888.

*To Po' Keepsies.*

Dull m'f. light rain about noon. It
o'k — a steady rain by 3 o'clk. — Took
Train at 8 A.M. — Met Geo., Nellie & the baby.
Po'k at 10.30 — Rode right to the Terrace —
spent what remained of the m'f. in going
through the house & giving directions about
the sending to me of my things — Mary
& Kate & Geo. arranged for the division
of some of the furniture & old carpets.
Went out & took a short walk — Dinner
at 12.30 — finding that we had got
through with our work we determined
to return to town by the 4.50 train
which we did — very heavy rain —
got to N.Y. at 7 — then home & had
a light supper — Eve'g with Annie
& to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 12. 1888.

Dull day — rain in the m'f — up soon
after 6 — Ralrph gave us a bad scare —
fever — 102½°. Pulse 180 — Respiration 60 —
sent at once for Yale but he did not come
until noon. The poor child was distressingly
sick — unable to hold his head up & moaning
most of the time — it was 11.30 when I got
to studio — Home at 1 — Stayed with the
boy until near 5 when he showed eviden-
ces of improvement — then again to
studio — home at 6.30 — Dr. Yale came
in in the eve'g. Miss Church took
charge of Ralph until midnight, then
Britta's relieved her. Annie continues
to improve — To bed before 11.
