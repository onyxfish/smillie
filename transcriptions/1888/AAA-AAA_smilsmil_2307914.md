## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 1. 1888.

A delightfully cool, clear day after the rains of last night. See the mtf. worked a proving tadema plate — taking 5 true fals. pfs. & two plain mfs. Dinner at 1. Then took a resting spell & at 4 o'clk. went out for a drive which was very enjoyable, the air was so fresh. back at 6.30 — Tea. Eve'g Annie read aloud a part of the Iliad while I signed a number of pfs. (shocking bad ones) of my plate; "what o'clk is it?" — As usual it was 11 o'clk. when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 2. 1888.

Much of the day was cloudy as though it meant rain, but nothing came of it — I worked most of the mtf. Trying to write a letter to Stakes & Bore about the bad printing of the pls. of "What o' clk is it" — & selecting such of them as were altogether too bad to sign. At noon I walked over to the lake & met the rest of our families who had been there to fish — they were much elated over a catch of three very little sun fish — we walked home, the youngsters getting a lift part of the way by Mr. munger. Dinner at 1. A rest after dinner & a rough ride from 4 to 7 — tea — Eve'g more Iliad & to bed at 11.
