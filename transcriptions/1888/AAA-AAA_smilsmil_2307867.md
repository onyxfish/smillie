## Left Page

### April, Sunday 29, 1888

An uncomfortably hot day — the Ther.
getting up to 85°. Geo. Cook came at
9 o'clk. & took Jim away with him
on a little excursion — to be back at 1 —
I had bkfst. at about 9.30 & spent
the day indoors with Annie & the baby.
Annie has no fever but feels very miser-
able — Dr. Blackwell still shakes her head
& talks about malarial or intermittant
fever — The breast trouble progresses very
slowly. Dinner at 2 — between 3
& 4 I got a nap. Charl came
in for a few minutes — & then Geo. &
Emily Cook. A cup of tea at 7 —
At 8 I went to G.C.D. with letter
to Kate — then walked down to studio
— wet up Water-Color box & got bkf. & plate
of Tadema picture — then home at 9.15 —
Soon after Edith & Marion Wright came in —
they told all about the arrival of Ernest
& his bride on the Aurania — They staid
with us over night.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 30, 1888

Clear, but not so hot as yesterday.
Edith & Marion left us at 8.30 A. M. —
About 9.30 I went over to Grady McKurie's
with Water-Color drawing by Saunier to be sent
to Miss Davis (Jas. Wright's fiancée) as a wedding
present — then down to Scribner's & delivered
the Tadema plate to Mr. Marvin — It was
after 11 when I got to studio — I then
got out my materials & commenced
two Water-Color sketches, 3" × 5" — for the
menu cards for the Water-Color dinner
next Thurs. Home to lunch & back
after 3 o'clk. having waited some time
for Dr. Blackwell who did not come.
Kate amd. from Po'k. I worked on the two
sketches until after 6 when I took them
over to Weldon — 222 W. 23d. St. I was near
7 o'clk. when I got home & found Dr. Blackwell
& [illegible] — they had performed another oper-
ation on Annie's breast — Annie still under
the influence of Ether — She was much
under its influence all the eveg., but
I got her settled for the night & to bed
myself about 12.30.
