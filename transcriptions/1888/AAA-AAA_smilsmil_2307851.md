## Left Page

### March, Monday 26, 1888

Dismal day — wind & rain — got up at 8 with a very heavy head — general influenza & troublesome eyes — very soon determined to follow the doctor's advice & take a day of rest — Part of it was spent upon the bed but the most of it was with the children — Most of the day Annie spent on the bed — Late in the afternoon she got up & went into the nursery where she stayed until 9 — after which we went to bed — Dr. B— came in the afternoon & dressed her breast. Evef there was a little desultory reading aloud from Sunday's Tribune by Annie. To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 27, 1888

A nasty, raw day ending wet & rainy. Got to studio at about 10 — very much under the influence of influenza & a sore throat but I managed to finish the Fadema etching & varnished the plate preparatory to biting — Then home to lunch at 1 — owing to press of domestic duties it was 3 before I got back to studio — I got my etching "What o'clk is it?" with second ground & sand paper ink ready for the acid & had the Fadema twice in the bath — Home at 6.30 — Evef, a dreary one — being very uncomfortable from cold in the head — Annie was able to sit up but evidently did not feel very bright — I sent Greta to bed after her dinner & until 10 o'clk I sat with the children — To bed before 11.
