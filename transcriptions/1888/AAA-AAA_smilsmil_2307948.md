## Left Page

### October, Monday 8, 1888

An exceedingly disagreeable day — wind & spiteful showers & rain all day long. I was barely able to get up about the middle of the [illegible]. feeling very weak & light headed — the pain in my neck was less but was quite enough to cripple me completely — To me it was a day of misery — by a great effort I was able to write a short letter to Chard who expects to start for the far west, on a pleasure trip, all [illegible] [illegible]. D. R. came in the afternoon but had no further advice to give — Evett. Annie read aloud & I got to bed shortly after & had a better night.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 9, 1888

Very cold — Everything frozen hard last night. I felt much better & got up to my [illegible]. A bleak, cold, windy & cheerless day — About to Parke came in with Misses Wickham & Cunningham and made a short call — I kept up all day gradually improving. I managed to write quite a long letter to Mary — also, a short note to Emma Bourne, accompanying Natie's [illegible] letter to Chard — a short note to Geo. with an accompanying note to Charley Platt asking him about an etching enterprise that was started last spring — Evett Annie went alone to the Parke's where they have a small company — I read newspaper & did a little writing.
