## Left Page

### May, Monday 7, 1888

A very pleasant day — To studio before 10 + to work on Ill. C. drawing "The Passing Herd." Home to lunch at 1:15. At 2 Dr. Stunkson + Blackwell came — Both expressed themselves as quite satisfied with his progress made — The sloughing must continue for some time yet. Annie has a good appetite — sleeps well + keeps up her spirits wonderfully well —
Back to studio before 3 + worked on drawing until almost 6 — then to Eimer + Amend's + bought some Anti-septic gauze — Home at 6:45 — dinner — Evelf. I read awhile to Annie + sat with the children.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 8, 1888

Overcast all day — light rain at 6.30 — Got to studio before 10 + to work on "A Passing Herd" until 2.30 — Then to bank + deposited check &c. from Chas. Baker in payment for oil Painting "A bit on Marblehead Neck" — $45 — Then to 42d. St. + 7 ave. McKeever's shop to hung up "Herd" frame. Home at 1.30 — as Annie seemed very bright I did not wait until D. Blackwell came — To studio's at 2.45. Rossell, [illegible] carnau, came for "the Herd" about 4.30 — the frame having arrived an hour before — sent it to Uniondrague + then worked on other large drawing "A group of cows" with opaque color — I think improving it very much — Wrote to Dr. Purdy Sec'y of League Art Com. — asking if I could send "fronts" two + took it to his residence 3 or 4 Mad. ave. — To dinner at 6.30 — Evelf. Reading papers until [illegible] — then to bed.
