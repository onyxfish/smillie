## Left Page

### December, Friday 21. 1888.

Pleasant mft. clouded afternoon
with a snow flurry in the early eve. To
studio at usual time. — Got out
water colors & a little sketch laid
in & washed off years ago & started
work upon that. — I re-modeled the
whole thing in composition & color scheme
& worked faithfully until 1 o'clt. — then
home back at 2.30 & worked alone
as I could see. At 4.30 went again
to Dan. Pike's to see if I could get some
Magic Lantern slides — again he had forgotten
them. To O'Neil's & got part of the photos he
had promised — copies of my drawing of
A's father — etc. — home — dinner at 6.30 —
Eve'g A. read aloud from Rob. Skinner for
awhile — before 9 I took her to Walton's
left her & ret'd. home — Charl soon came
in — about 10 A. returned & at 11 45 I
got to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 22. 1888.

Very bright & very cold — Ther. at 8°. It
6 A.M. & not much; if any, above 15° at
any time of day — To studio about 10 &
spent most of the mft. finishing the
water-color sketch of yesterday — Home
arr'd after 2.30 — where I got back. I
mounted my sketch in the afternoon
& managed to do a little of other work
but I had many visitors — Dr. Otis, on
Century business — Ferguson — to urge
his claims to a position on the Com. on
Admissions — J.W. Champney — Charl
& a man from Pike with Lantern slides
— plumbers etc. etc. At 5.30 I went
to O'Neil's & got some more photo. prints.
Then to Brooks Bro's & tried on coat and
home at 6.40 — dinner — At 8 A. & I went
to [illegible] — expecting to hear Veretschagin
talk on "War" but he didn't talk — so we
went to the Kimballs & spent the eve'g
very pleasantly there — Home soon after
10 & right to bed.
