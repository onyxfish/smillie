## Left Page

### November, Friday 23, 1888.

A very cold day — so cold that the
masons did not work at painting
on the east wall. I got to studio
at about 10 — set palette & commenced
another repainting on 20×24 "Gothia
Mts from Roaring Brook". Home at 1 —
back at 2.30 — Rec'd a note from Mr.
G.L. Rives — Ass. Sec'y of State — asking
me to learn if Geo. Butler would paint
a portrait of Sec'y Bayard — if so, for how
much. I painted as long as I could
see — then S.R. Koehler came in &
we chatted until 5.30 — home before
6. dinner — Ruth, Annie &  I
went to the Casino & saw & heard "The
Yeomen of the Guard", Gilbert & Sullivan's
latest — I was somewhat disappointed.
The stage setting is very commonplace —
the company was not good — some of the
fun (the fault of the actors) was clownish
& the words & music were without novelty.
There were several very fine passages
however — enough to send us home
in good humor. To bed soon after
11.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 24. 1888.

Cold, cloudy & raw. After last night's
dissipation I did not feel good for
much & did but little at the
studio — among other things I fussed
for awhile on a problem that Champney
brought in to me — translating a rain
bow spectrum into its equivalent in
B.k. &white — to aid a professional friend
in some photographing experiments.
Also went to N.A. trying to get Geo. Butler's
address — home at 1.30 — Afternoon Annie
went with Mary Cook, her brother & Aunt to
a matinee at the Lyceum Theatre — I
went to Brady's, trying to get Geo. B's address
— there to A.A. Ass'n — & from there was sent to
51 W. 10th St — from there to the Holbein Studio
in W. 55th — no Butler — saw Geo. Innes, his
son in law Hartley & J. Rondel — went to
Sherwood Bldg. Then to Waverly Place — to
Bisbee's shoe shop. my boots arrived — did
not take them — then to Century — got B's
address — 154 W. 55th St — then to studio —
6 o'clk — home at 6.30 pretty well tired —
Eve'g Annie & I, both being tired —
nodded & read in front of fire until 9.30
— then to bed.
