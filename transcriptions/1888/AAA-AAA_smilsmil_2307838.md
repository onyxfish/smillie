## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 29. 1888.

Overcast most of the day & rather raw — feeling quite used up after my yesterdays experience — Got to studio about 10.30 & for some time was engaged writing notes & attending to bills & checks — then to work on etching of "Competitive Blowers" until about 12.30 — then to Safe Deposit at 22 Nat. Bk. Dep. my A.B.N. Co check & Cassels check. Cut Annie's coupons & deposited them then home to lunch. Back to studio at 3 & etched until 5 — then lead-puttied the open seams in the bottom of a wooden tray for acids. Home at 6.30 — dinner — Besides feeling much out of sorts myself, Annie has trouble with her breast & Ralph's bowels continue obstinate. So we were somewhat forlorne as we spent our evg. in the nursery — Gretta sent out. To bed soon after 11 — just as I was going to sleep Ralph awoke & I sat up with him until almost 1 o'clk & up again with him at about 4.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 1. 1888.

mild & overcast with dampness almost amounting to rain at night. Did not get to studio until 10.30 — feeling pretty seedy after last night's experience — wrote a letter to Parkes & then etched on Competitive Blowers until 12.45 then to lunch, first going to 42d St. & Ley. Ave. where the Rittier & Stymms factory was making a tremendous conflagration — home at 1.30 Surprised to find a lunch party. Mr. Sprague & Mary — Emily & Nellie — It was 3 o'clk. when I got to studio — to find that I had missed a call from S. R. Koehler. Mr. Jno. L. Rives' sister-in-law Miss Kean, came with my drawing of Mr. R. all very much pleased with the likeness — only a few touches desired — there I made under Miss K.'s direction — At 4.30, went to Kelsell's & got him a note of introduction to Rajon — then to Wünderlich's — then to Radtke & Saackner then to studio — then home — dinner. Evg. Mr. & Mrs. Emory called — after they left, Annie read aloud for a while — to bed soon after 11.
