## Left Page

### November, Monday 19, 1888

A nasty day — wet + warm — Ther. 55°. Got to studio at 10 and commenced work on a drawing of Annie's father from a photo-enlargement from an old daguerreotype — Traced + re-traced it + washed in background. Geo. came in with a letter from Charl. Santa Barbara Cal. Nov 12th enclosing along dreary letter for Kate — Home at 1.30 — lunched alone — at 2.30 went to Mary Cook's — Annie + Jim there — also Mrs. Sprague + Miss Williams. Read Charl's letter — then to studio — posting the letter on the way — met Yale at studio door — he visited with me until Calverley came in + he stayed until dark. The only work that I accomplished was to get 6 [illegible] of "Reading Homer" into press. Home at 6 — Eve'g, reading until after 9 — then devoted my time for an hour to going over Boyd + Cooley's bill — wrote letter + check + to bed at 10.45.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 20, 1888

Bright, cool day — to studio before 10 — then, taking drawings I went down town to the A.B.N. Co — Spent some time there with Freeland — Shepherd + Jones. At 11.30 went to Drexel's Banking House about Annie's Chs. + his certificates — To studio soon after 12 + had time to make a few washes on the portrait drawing of Annie's father — then home to lunch. Back at 2.30 + worked as long as I could see in drawing — At 5 went to Wunderlich's to look at frames for etchings — met Washington Wilson and walked with him to 38th S — home to dinner — Eve'g went to Col. Turner's — 35 W. 14th S. supposing there was to be a meeting of Soc'y of Am. Etchers — the meeting was at 4 P.M. — walked up to the Casino + got libretto of "The Yeomen of the Guard" — home at 9.15. Read libretto + to bed at 11.
