## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 24, 1888.

Bright, crisp day — To studio at 10, as
usual — To work on large working
drawings for Printing Fable that I
want Herrick to make for me — I
want to get them finished & off as
soon as possible — wrote a letter to
Herrick — at 12.45 went to Bank —
then to Newman's — then home —
lunch — to studio at 1.20 — again
worked on Printing Fable drawings —
wrote a note to Sarah Leggett [illegible] —
Uncle Willie's came in for half an hour —
Shortly after 5 went up to 73d St. & saw
Jos & Minn — find them sorrowing in
a very natural & healthy manner —
Walked home thro' Central Park — a
wonderfully beautiful Twilight and
moonlight — Dinner at 6.30. Eve'g.
Read aloud from Feb. Harper — until 9
when Charl came in — To bed
at about 11.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 25, 1888.

Dull mf. — Snow about noon — falling
to the depth of about 2" — at night heavy
rain — a strong freeze before mf. — the
natural result being one of the worst
specimens of frozen slush that I ever
saw. To studio about 10 — much
tempted to meet Charl at Vautine's
to look at Oriental bargains — but,
instead, I tried to paint a Firelight,
moonlight & snow — and did it
succeed — Home at 1 — back at 2.15
— after getting my painting into shape
to leave. I worked for awhile on plans
for Printing Fable. Home before 6 —
Dinner — Eve'g. Read aloud to
Annie & Kate a sketch by James in
the Feb. "Harper" — To bed about 11
with something — I don't know what —
crosswise in my gizzard.
