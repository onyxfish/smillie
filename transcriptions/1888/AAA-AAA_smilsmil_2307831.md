## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 15. 1888.

Very bright, exceedingly cold and
windy. To studio at 10.30 & worked
on Sc. Am. or team drawing for A.S.
N. Co. until 11.45 Then Rec. a note
from Sam. Marvin of Scribners — went
right down there — Saw Mr. — arranged
to etch a plate for them after Alma-
Tadema's painting "Reading Homer" now
in Met. Museum. Then walked up
home to lunch — back to studio at
2.30 — worked on drawing until after
4 when I finished it — Then made
paste & mounted plain photo. ints.
portrait of Mr. G. L. Rives & put it in
press — Home to dinner at 6.30 —
Eveth. Chas. Collins came in and
stayed with us until 10.30.
Ralph again out of sorts — heavy
cold & obstinate constipation —
near midnight when I got
to bed & up again about 1 o'clk.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 16. 1888.

One of the coldest days of the
winter — Very clear & windy — Got
to studio soon after 10. Spent some
time writing a letter to Herrick.
Then to work on photo. of Mr. Rives
that I mounted last night. Washed
in a background of trees — Home
at 1 — back soon after 2 & worked
on portrait until about 4.30 —
About 5 went to N.A. of D. & spent
an hour looking at the etchings on
show there — met Yale & we walked
a few blocks together — Home to dinner
at 6.30 — Eveth. again wrote D. K.
addresses on pamphlets for Arnica.
