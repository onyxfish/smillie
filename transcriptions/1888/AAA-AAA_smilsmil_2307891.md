## Left Page

### June, Saturday 16, 1888

Another very hot day — no relief from last night's thunder storms. The atmosphere very humid & much of the time clouded & threatening. I got to studio before 10 & to work at packing — Mr. [illegible], Waring came in & I spent some time with him going over my stock of [illegible], from which he made a selection & took them away with him. Home to lunch soon after. Kate had arr. from [illegible], the first stage of her journey across the sea. Ret. to studio before 3 & packed diligently until 6 — Home at 6.30 & dinner soon after. Chas. came in & spent the eveg. with Kate. About 8 Annie went to Walter's with Edw. and I followed her in about half an hour. Home soon after 9 — Sat with Chas. & Kate until 10 — Then dressed Annie's breast which within a day or two has shown a disposition to be troublesome in healing — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 17, 1888

And yet another exceedingly hot day — Did not go out of the house until eveg., when I went to G. C. Depot with letters. I worked during the day at packing back Nos. of Ill. Lond. News, Punch, Harper's Mag., & Caulting. I wrote notes to Marvin, Kirby & uncle Willie — At 2 o'clk. dinner. Edw. & Geo. Cook, Emily, Jno. Valentine & Kate Smillie dined with us — the afternoon passed in working & lounging — Tea at 7 — & the eveg. went as the day had done — To bed about 11 after giving Annie's breast a dressing with ointment that Dr. Blackwell gave her when she called on her this mtg.
