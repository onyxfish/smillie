## Left Page

### January, Monday 16, 1888

Bright, clear & growing colder.
To studio before 10 — then went on
to Bank — then to Nespels — in 16"
5" — & talked with him about the
large etching (15×20) proposed
of Homer's "Voice from the Cliff"! The
Result was most disheartening to
me in view of the poor trash that
forms the bulk of his stock in
business. I ask too much to suit
him — then to Johnson & Faulkner's
helping to get some curtain stuff
for Montrose — About 11.30 when
got to studio — worked until 1 on
portrait of Mrs. Rives — then home to
lunch — back at 2.30 & for the rest
of the afternoon planned & continued
a cattle subject (W.C.) 20×24 — At 5.30
went to Yale's & met Gifford there —
Home to dinner at 6.30. Evef
Read awhile — then had a
game of cards with Annie —
robed at 10.30 — & so ends my
55th Birthday.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 17, 1888

Overcast with snow beginning to
fall before noon & continuing heavily
until night — Got up with headache
— to where I got to studio — In spite
of feeling out of sorts I pitched into
my big cattle drawing & slapped
in what seemed to me strong washes
with a big brush. Ames Faullart came
in & made a breezy call — Home at
back at 2.15 & worked about
as long as I could see — W. Hauckner,
the print publisher, called to see
if he could interest me in a big
plate after James Hart — but I could
not be interested — Home at 6 —
Dinner — much of the evef spent
fixing curtain & pole in dining room.
Then Annie read aloud Oatrons
of Stielmans's Article on Ruskin in The
Century. To bed about 11.
