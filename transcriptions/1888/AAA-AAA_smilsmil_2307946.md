## Left Page

### October, Thursday 4, 1888

milder, but still cool enough &
breezy — Overcast m'g — gradually
clearing — to be overcast again before
sun down — I worked all the
m'g at taking first p't. from the
Huntington etching but was not
brilliantly successful. After
dinner I pulled a third proof —
an india (the two of the m'g were
j'k) but I managed to spoil the
india pl. At 3 we went out for
a short drive — a stiff neck in-
commoded me very much. I
left A. at Mr. Jeff. Griffiths — to call
& got home before 5 — I immediately
went to etching room & remembering
what I had learned in taking large
p'ts. succeeded in taking the only
good pl. of the day — tea at 6.30 —
Ev'g. went down town for mail
etc — home about 8 — Reading
until after 9 — to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 5, 1888

mild, milky day — sunlight struggling
with indications of rain — my stiff
neck very troublesome — awhile in the
garden & directing Coyle about some
flower beds & transplanting — then,
before dinner did a little work of
a preparatory nature on a squib that
Duine is getting up for the 10th Anniversary
of Margaret Parker White's wedding —
after dinner, again with Coyle until
4 o'clk. then to work with pen &
ink making sketches on the margins
of A's parody of a part of Joe's Wedding
Bells — After tea, the work being
finished, I took it to the P.O. in a
heavy rain — got my feet wet —
brought home corn meal & had
a big poultice made for my neck
& went to bed with the back of
my neck well plastered.
