## Left Page

### June, Thursday 14, 1888.

Cool, overcast m/f — with muggy afternoon & thunderstorms in the even.
Up early & had a 7 o'clk. help. Before
8 went with Sue to the depot & gathered the 8 o'clk. Train to Boston
Mary &c. to Buffalo. Before 10 & Nellie
went back to Ridgefield at 4 P.m. —
It was nearly 10 when I got to studio
& spent the m/f in dusting & cleaning
curtains & getting things ready for
packing. Home to lunch at.
Nellie there. & Emily — back to studio
at 2.30 & until 6 o'clk. continued the
work of the m/f. Then home. Ed &
Cook took dinner with us — my even.
was broken between calls from Parke
& his br. Charles & later from my bro.
Chas. About 10.30 I got to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 15. 1888.

An oppressively hot day. Ther.
up to 85°. Thunderous atmospheres
& at night heavy thunderstorms
until nearly 11 o'clk. Had an early
help; & after it went over to Grady's
& ordered two cheeny frames for water
color studies for Montrose. The old
home frames reengravings, re-ordered
& renewed were sent to their new
home (10 2. 38 St.) this m/f. To studio
at 10 — most of the m/f. Taking down
curtains, shaking, switching & folding
them, wrote to Mr. Rives & to [illegible]
Sent by Exp. drawing & Q's specimen. Proof
to Mr. R— then home to lunch at 1. Used
up with a headache & went to bed.
Got up at about 4 to spark Jim & pack
up a box of silver for storage with the Gor-
ham Co., was able to eat a little
dinner & in the even. pack some
back nos. & Punch — but at 10 I went
to bed with my head aching out-
rageously again.
