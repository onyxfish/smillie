## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 18, 1888

Strong east wind brought hopes of rain — a shower fell at about 4 in the afternoon, after which it cleared again.

I worked on "Tadema plate" in the mtf with graver and dry point — While at the dinner table a disagreeable sort of blindness came upon me, followed by headache — I lay down for an hour or so then wrote to Calverley acknowledging receipt of $100 for Burns' etching. At 5 went down town — called on Mr. Franc, at the old Beuity house — he is an artist from Wilkesbarre. Home before 7 — Jim, Willie & Eleanor had been to a children's garden party at Mrs. J.S. Carless's —. Every general chatting until 10 — then to bed after dressing Annie's breast — which, last Sunday evening again showed a little break in the newly healed surface — which now seems to threaten to reopen.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 19, 1888

At last, the blessed rain. It fell during a good part of the night heavily in the mtf until after 10 — during the remainder of today it was mild & cloudy. Before 6 p.m. a heavy thunder storm after which rain fell gently until 6 p.m. I was a good deal out of sorts with a headache but managed, after a little work on the Tadema plate to take a working pf. of it before dinner — It was about 4 p.m when Sue, Eleanor, Rob, & I went out to ride — as we came thro' town hurrying to get ahead of a thunderstorm we met Annie, Katchen & the boys — they bundled into the carriage with us & we got in just as the storm broke upon us. Tea. After that, went down town for an hour. Saw Palmer & chatted until near 10.
