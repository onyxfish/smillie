## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 24, 1888

Heavy Rain during the night &
a fierce blow in the m'f which
lasted all day — Awoke at 5 15
— did not get to sleep again — Nate
& many got away in the 6 15 Stage —
Mr. Cum came to do the work for
the day — Prettas came along at
noon — excusing herself by saying
that she had mis read Annie's
postal telling her not to stay later
than Sunday — I spent the m'f in
packing & looking after various
things — went down town about 1 —
back in an hour — dinner at 1 —
again packing cases & trunks
until after 5 — three down town &
back to tea at 6.30 — Finished my
packing before 9. but it was 10
when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 25, 1888

*Family Returned to New-York.*

A wonderfully beautiful day — Up before
7 — all hands very busy — everything going
smoothly — At 7 30 Pope's team came &
loaded up with our boxes & barrels & trunks —
in all, we had 15 pieces weighing about
a ton — he got away a few minutes after
8 — we had our bf — & picked up last
things & left the house at 9.30 — Drove to
P.O., left town at 9 45 — the roads in very
bad condition but we got to New Milford
without trouble at 11.30 — our freight and
baggage just ahead of us — train on time —
11.53 — Ralph very much frightened & did
not get pacified until we reached
Scranton — Annie nearly used up by
a headache — another row about Parlor
car seats — I wrote for them last Tues. but
the Porter had heard nothing about it — we
got seats & might have got through with
tolerable comfort if the car had not been
so overheated — Got in to N.Y. a few min.
late & sup to the house at 6.30 & found
Kate & many waiting for us — Had tea
at 8.30 the trunks came & the children
were got to bed — Ralph still much
excited — gave him a quieting mixture
before he would go to sleep —       we
got to bed some after 10.
