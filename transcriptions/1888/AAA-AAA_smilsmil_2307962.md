## Left Page

### November, Monday 5, 1888

Pleasant, mild day — Little matters kept me about the house until after 10. At the studio I kept myself busy in putting finishing touches upon my house cleaning & in dampening & putting into press some of my last summer etching proofs. After 6 when I got home to dinner — After that Ge came in for awhile. It was 8.30 when we went out together we parted at 28th St. & 4th Ave & I walked on down to C.Y. Turner's studio. 35 W. 14th St. — a meeting of Socy of Am. Etchers — Moran — Turner — Dielman — Sartain — Pratt — Van Elten & Hamilton there — nothing accomplished — Soon after 10 Pratt & I left & walked uptown together — I got home at 11 & found myself locked out. Annie got out of her bed & let me in.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 6, 1888

Election day. Opened wet but very soon cleared, the day proving pleasant & warm. Wrote a note to Charl in the w.f. & mailed it to him with letters from Mary & Kate. It was 11 o'clk before I got to studio — very much used up by my cold — I accomplished no work before I returned home to lunch. Nearly 3 o'clk. when I got back to studio & the rest of the afternoon was spent sorting the contents of drawers. Home before 6. Evely attended to some Montrose bills & other writing — quite used up before I got to bed with cold in the head — sneezing etc, etc.
