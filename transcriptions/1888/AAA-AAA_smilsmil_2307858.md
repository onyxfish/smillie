## Left Page

### April, Monday 9, 1888

Beautiful day — to studio at 9.45
& worked all the m'f on Tadema
plate with dry-point — Home to lunch
at 1 — after lunch went to Jackson's
factory in 28th St. beyond 2nd Ave. and
after some looking about found a piece
of soap stone 2 in. thick — had it cut
the size I wanted it for my Printing Table —
11½ × 17¾ — & paid $2.00 for it — then
to studio — there to Gat Co's office. Cor.
15th St. & Irving Place. To see about bonds
that Annie has that will mature in 1st
of May — waited 20 m. for Pres. or Treas.
but could not see them — then on to
Kimmel & Voigt's & got ink & paper —
Met W. Geo Hunter there & he promised me
a proof of his latest plate — to studio
at 5.30 & wet down paper to take proofs
in to-morrow. Home at 6.30. Dinner.
Mary had been to see Josephine (Will's
widow) during the day — Evef. finished
writing a long letter & instructions to
Herrick. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 10, 1888

Overcast & threatening — Rain commenc-
ing about 12.30 — falling in heavy showers
& driven by an east wind. Got to studio
at 9.30 & spent some time getting things
put away & preparing to print. Then
took 3 plt. from Geo's invitation plate
that I worked over a bit. Some time
with Geo. arranging a list of nominations
for A.A. & A.N.A. according to new plan.
Many came in between 12 & 1 — at
1 went to Bank & drew some money for
Annie — then to "R. Stokes", the publisher —
he was engaged & I did not see him —
home to lunch — many Ret'd to P'k.
by 3.30 train — I ret'd to studio at 2.30 &
took 4 India plt. & 2 plain imps. from the
Tadema plate & two imps. from "What o'clock
is it?" It was 6.30 when I got things
cleaned up & I was ready to start for
home. Dinner — Evef. Annie read
aloud from "A Roman Singer" — To bed
soon after 10.
