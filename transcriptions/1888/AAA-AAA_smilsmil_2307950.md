## Left Page

### October, Friday 12, 1888

A dismal day — very windy at times
& bleak — some rain very early in the
m'g & a very wet fog in the afternoon
with rain again at night — my eye
shows no change & causes me little
inconvenience but it worries me —
I worked all day in etching Room
& studio fixing things up & getting
ready to leave for the winter —
I varnished over many things with
asphaltum varnish. Ev'g.
Annie read aloud some of Fiske's
assays — the storm clouds broke &
for a time we had beautiful moon-
light with quite mild weather.
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 13, 1888

Bleak, heavily clouded & windy — a
depressing continuance of bad weather
through much of the day rain fell
freely — I had a good fire in the
studio furnace & etching room &
there I worked all day getting
things put away — some to re-
main here through the winter
& some packed to take with us.
In the afternoon Parke called
for a few minutes & I almost
determined to go with him to
Scranton on Monday to see an
oculist there — At about 6 o'clk
I went down town to P.O. the
first time this week — Got a
reply from Yale to my letter of [illegible].
He sent me a prescription — an eye-
lotion. I sent down town & got
some & rested my eye while
Annie read aloud to me —
All day I had been bothered by
a dull headache which I took
to bed with me at 10 o'clk.
