## Left Page

### March, Friday 16, 1888.

Dull day with light rain in the afternoon late. Did not get up until near 11 — then had a light b'kf'st. About 1 I went to studio & was able to work during the afternoon — finished Tadema tracing — scraped it successfully, filled it with red — laid etching ground & set press for transfer. Wrote a letter to Herrick & other notes — Home about 6 — Annie seems to be quite despondent & out of sorts, her trouble in the breast progresses so slowly & without any encouraging signs — Dinner — Ev'g, read Baker awhile & then held my head in front of the fire. To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 17, 1888.

Bright, pleasant day — Got to studio soon after 10. Cleared ground laid yesterday p.m. off from plate — it was brittle — got other ground & laid. Two more before I got a satisfactory one — Spent the m'g laying grounds & getting my transfer which, at last, I accomplished successfully. Home to lunch at 1.30 — After lunch took Jim to play with little Peck boy in 37th St — then to studio about 3 — getting a handled hand vise on the way — Spent some time clearing up & then made a start on the Tadema etching — Putting in the distant sea — feel very timid — am afraid of being too mechanical. Made W. Rice's drawing into p'h'p & took it to Adam's Ep. Co. in 42d St. & sent it to Schoff to study and estimate upon — Home at 6.30 — Dinner — Ev'g, read aloud for a time to Annie. Dr Emily Blackwell called upon her to-day & admits that the persistency of her trouble in the breast puzzles her & proposes a consultation with Dr Weir on Monday — This alarms me — I grow nervous in my dread of new sorrows.
