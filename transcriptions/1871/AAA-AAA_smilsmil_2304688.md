## Left Page

### June Wednesday, 7 1871.

Southern Hotel St. Louis —
Room No. 258.

Up about 5.30 Had Bkfst about 7. Mr Whittredge got off on his train, Kansas Pacif. at 8 o'clk — we got off on our train, Northern Miss. at 8.30 — This days Travel was of the dreariest and most uninteresting kind — a low, poor, flat country with miserable looking houses, nothing suggestive of thrift \+ comfort. — We stopped for dinner at 2.30 and for tea about 7.30 — both poor meals. About 10 we got into Kansas City and there Transferred ourselves at once to the Denver Pacific R.R. getting our Pullman compartment without trouble. — Mr Whittredge joined us a few minutes later at State line — before 11 we were all stowed in our beds — I was sick and so had only a tolerable night.

## Right Page

### June Thursday, 8 1871.

Pullman Car "Prairie Queen"
Kansas Pacific R.R —

Up about 5.30 after a few hours of sleep \+ feeling decidedly out of sorts. nausea with some pain — Spent the early m'f on the Rear platform, enjoying as much as my condition would permit, the novelty to me of the beautiful Prairies, the low clean hills \+ the wooded River bottoms. Bkfst a very poor one, at 8.30. About middle of A.M. got to the prairie par excellence. to me a wonderful sight — Saw one prairie wolf, a number of Buffalo — one herd [illegible] ran along with the train for some distance. had a number of shots fired at them — von hit — many antelopes a few large sort of Rabbit \+ innumerable prairie dogs — About noon I was completely used up with headache \+ the rest of the day was on my back — Slept well during the night — waked once \+ saw a superb effect of moon light over the Prairie — my bed a convenience of the most agreeable kind.
