## Left Page

### February Wednesday, 15 1871

Charming day overhead — horrible underfoot. Got to Studio at 9 oclk about 10 my [illegible] came (6" less) and commenced a new sketch. I got out Uncle Willie's drawing of Niagara \+ die eng'd. by R.H. and commenced work on it to make something of it if possible. Home to lunch at 1.40 — back at 2.30 and worked on die (½ day) as long as I could see. Delbit\- Hay made a long call — Home at 6.15 — at N.A. \& D. at 7.30 \+ had another inimitable \+ interesting lecture from Rimmer — after that Swain Gifford (first time that I've seen him since his Return) Louis Tiffany \+ I went up into the galleries then over to Studio — then up 5 Ave. — leaving them at 38. St. \+ home abo. To bed about 11 —

## Right Page

### February Thursday, 16 1871

Cloudy and clear — mild — water running everywhere — got to Studio at 9 o'clk and to work on die (1½ day) part of a.m. with graver but most of the time trying to lay a Re-biting ground. Dabbers etc all out of order. Home to lunch at 1.45 — back at 2.45 — Rebit — very rough — then graver again — Jno. Snedecor called — then Mr. Jno. Whitney and then bro. Will — at 6 oclk. went in to Mr. W. \+ dined at the Albemarle — at 7.30 Ret'd. to Studio — fixed up and washed up and about 9 went to N.A. \& D. \+ met Mr. W. again — There until near 10 — walked to Albemarle with him — home at 10.15 — to bed about 10.45.
