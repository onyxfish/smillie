## Left Page

### January, Sunday 22, 1871

Clear, windy day, getting colder into night. Ther. 10°. Ice over everything from Rain + sleet last night. M.f. went to D'r. Pn't. Hastings. He preached from 1st Cor. II Ch. + 14 v. After service walked down to Studio, fixed fire + then home. Dinner about 1 o'clk. At 3 o'clk again went to Studio. Picked up things about and home again at 5.30. We\. M.f. at home, very lazy and saving myself for hard work to-morrow. To bed at 10.15 after a serious talk with Father.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 23, 1871

Very cold, overcast m'f. Got to Studio at 9 o'clk. Fire all right. Spent half an hour cleaning glass in frame, then to work on drawing (20 days) gummed portions of horse — and at once got in a mess as it destroyed a quantity of grey that I wished to preserve + threw all out of keeping — washed gum off and worked hard for two hours to get things back in original place. Snow commenced about 10. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.15 — worked on distance + ground. Kate + Mrs. Pierce called. At 5 o'clk — James Mulvaney took my drawing to N. A. of D. — went over with him — no one there — for a moment saw collection — put my drawing in place — now that it is finished, feel very much dissatisfied and put it forward with much misgiving. Undoubtedly it has good work \+ shows some knowledge — but to me, it shows more want of knowledge than anything else —. Home to tea at 6 o'clk — every in the midst of a family racket, doing nothing — To bed about 10.15
