## Left Page

### June, Tuesday, 27, 1871

at White \+ Hatch's \-
Room 2 \-

Up at 4 o'clk \- a hurried bkfst. and off at 5. 17 in ton on stage \- the sun already up that. Until 10 o'clk. when we struck the Merced River, one unwaying monotony of plain with a cloudless sky \+ pitiless sun, Ther. 90° no tree or shrubs, great clouds of dust \+ the wind with us. Got to Snelling's at 11. There Cap. Webb had fixed for us a claret sangaree. Six miles further on struck the foot hills \+ at 1.30 got to Hornitas where we had dinner \- immensely hot \+ dirty, all of us \- Here Uncle a. missed a bag, containing his jewelry (why did he bring any on such a journey) At once he hired horses \+ cut back to Snelling's. After dinner we pushed on \- the new vehicle little better than a lumber wagon. Got in to Mariposa at 6.30 almost hammered to pieces \- took tea \+ at 7.15 in a very fine mt. wagon we started for White \+ Hatch's where we arr. at 9.30, completing about 85 miles after nearly 17 hours of fatigue. Stood it remarkably well \- all fairly good humored \- The moonlight glorious \- the house charming \- to bed without delay about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday, 28, 1871

A very hot day \- all kept as nearly cool as possible. Resting, preparatory to another start. Had our bkfst. at 8.30 \- our good opinion of Mr. Hatch increases \- Dinner at 1.30 \- Under the trees on my blanket, snoozing. Later, at the Saw mill \- tea after 7 \- at 9 Uncle a. arr. on the stage. He went to Snelling's \- changed horses, then on to Modesto, getting there at 10 P.M. finding his bag. Stretched himself out until 2 o'clk A.M. then back, having in the 2 days ridden about 200 miles \- The chase after his bag being about 125 miles \- All to bed soon after 10 o'clk.

Aunt Margaret died to-day, in New York.
