## Left Page

### June Monday, 5 1871.

Got up about 7 \- Bkfst at 8 and at 9, out to see what we could. Intensely hot. Went to the Public Library \- then called on Mosler \- not in \- then took Main St cars out over the hill to Corryville, but for the inevitable smoke we would have had a very fine view of the city \- Enjoyed the Ride. Back to Hotel at 1.30 \- So hot \+ tired that we did not think it advisable to try more. Dinner at 2 \- then packed \+ ready. At 3 W\- Whittredge appeared \+ we got off in the 3.30 train for St. Louis. All right until we got to North Vernon where an engine had run off the track \- nearly 3 hours detained but enjoyed the new scene \+ sensation \- Got to Seymour, Ind, for Tea bet. 9 \& 10. Then had our beds made up \+ about 11, turned in.

## Right Page

### June Tuesday, 6 1871.

on the Ohio \+ Miss. P.R. \- 6 o'clk \- A.M. \-

Raining heavily \+ the country as flat as a pancake. Rain held up when we reached St. Louis about 9.30, went to Barnum's \+ got Bkfst. \- disgusted \- then went to R.R. ticket offices \- Showery that \- then, finding that we would have to stay over night, we transferred our quarters to the Southern Hotel \- Clearing charmingly \- Rode alone to Lafayette Park \- back to the Hotel \+ had dinner about 3.30 \- then to the top of Court House dome \- then with Geo. \+ W.\- W. to Mercantile Library \- then alone along the River, to the new bridge, just building \- \+ watching the loading \+ getting away of the Steam boats \- back to hotel a little before 8. Mr. Cole of St. Louis visiting Mr. W \- \+ girl \- a cup of tea \+ plate of strawberries at 8.30 \- Prof. Botta at the same Hotel. Self, wrote a note to Pattison \+ got to bed about 10.30
