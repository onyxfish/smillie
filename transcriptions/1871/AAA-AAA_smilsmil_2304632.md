## Left Page

### February Saturday, 11 1871.

Bright pleasant day — Soon after 9 o'clk. presented myself at M. O. Roberts 107 Fifth Ave — on business of A.B.N. Co's — M.O. not presentable, but was very affably Rec'd by his son-in-law, Ames VanWart — went into Library and Gallery — then told that M.O. would see me in an hour — Went up to Studio — then back at 10.30 \+ saw the great man. Not over courteous — showed him my drawing of Locomotive — he told me that it was "as common as the nose on my face". Down to A.B.N.Co at 11.30 — Finished my business with them then called on Frothingham. World N— \+ Thing Smith Continental N— Home to lunch at 1.45 — at Studio at 3 just in time for perspective — on at 4. Gave Miss Willets a lesson in drawing on wood — Refused young Macy as pupil in water-colors \+ Rec'd a visit from Louis Tiffany just home from his Egyptian Trip — Home to tea at 6.15 — went to Tiffanys at 7 and met Louis at N.A. of D at 7.30 — Brewort's first lecture on perspective — went off nicely — Home at 10.20 and to bed soon after 11 —

## Right Page

### February Sunday, 12 1871.

More snow — blustering stormy morning — Used up with a headache — after hd[illegible] — Spent the m'f on Sofa — Took no dinner but went to bed and stayed there until Tea-time when I [illegible] — Father very much under the weather also — Every I read Grey's anatomy until near midnight — To bed at ab't 5 a.m. —
