## Left Page

### October, Saturday 7, 1871

Very pleasant day — Got to studio at 9 o'clk — commenced etching on die — finished it at noon — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — But in etching — am torn in the conflict between need of money and desire to work into a presentable shape, my studies \+ sketches of the past summer. Bank note work is hateful to me — my poverty \+ my infirmities are hateful — I don't know that I will ever be able to rise above them — At 5.30 went to Kelley's \+ left one for stretchers — then to Brooks Br. — then, at 6.45 — home to tea — Eve'g, scouring around among things at home \+ to bed about 11 o'clk —

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 8, 1871

Delightful day — W\+ went to P. Hastings — heard him preach from the first verse of Romans XV.\+ — as usual, vigorous and excellent. After service, walked about enjoying the air \+ sunlight — Dinner about 1 o'clk — after that, a nap — at 3.45 went with Geo. to St. Thomas's where we heard a sermon from Dr. Morgan \+ delightful music. After service, walked with Chas. up to 64th St. \+ 5th Ave. — to see Uncle C.'s new house, then down through the park \+ home to tea at 6.30. Eve'g went to Dr. J. P. Thomp.'s — heard him — walked home with Dilling — talking with Geo. \+ Chas. until 11 — to bed about 11.30
