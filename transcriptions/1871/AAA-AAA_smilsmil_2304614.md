## Left Page

### January Wednesday, 4 1871.

More colic before daylight — up a little before 8 o'clk — Got to Studio at 9.45 and until 11 — dusting \+ fixing up — then at Tracing of my drawing (6 days). Colscutt called \+ arranged to go with him to-morrow P. M — Home to lunch at 1.10 — day gorgeous — back at 2 — Very soon Misses Josephine \+ Virginia Johnson called — Miss V. stunning in appearance — long explanation of the N.Y. Genl. Grain. Imbroglio of last Summer — at 3.15 went to George's studio \+ with him \+ Belle Many Tweedy went up to 3.20 Ward's \+ saw Statue of Shakespeare — jolly — walked with B. \+ M — to 25 St — part of the way with H.H. Dyer — then to Studio at 5. J.T. Johnston \+ wife came in — lighted up \+ pleasant visit until 5.45 — Home to tea at 6.15 — went to 4th Rimmer lecture at 7.30 — after lecture, went up to Gallery \+ there met lots of folks enjoying the new Derby lot of Paintings — Had a good time until 10 — home at 10.15 — corrected my Rimmer notes by going and to bed at 11.20 —

## Right Page

### January Thursday, 5 1871.

Overcast and mild — Got to Studio at 9 o'clk — after calling on Colscutt to try to beg off from engagement this P.M. — Set to work on drawing (6½ days) finishing Tracing \+ beginning on Retracing. Worked steadily until 12.30 — then home — dressed — lunched — at 2 o'clk. went to Harlem R.R. depot — met Colscutt and at 2.30, Train to Harlem — then Carriage to Mr. Arnold's house — about 2 miles — Found Miss A. \+ Miss Maggie Seubbs and enjoyed a visit until near 5 o'clk. Ret. by carriage to Harlem — then by 3d. ave. cars — C. invited me to dine at League Club with him \+ then to see Jefferson — but I begged off — Home at 6.30 — Tea. At 7.20 went with Charl. to Booth's Theatre \+ Saw Jefferson as Rip Van Winkle — was very much pleased — So Really Simple, unaffected, funny \+ affecting at once — out at 10.50 — Home at 11.15 — to bed at 11.30
