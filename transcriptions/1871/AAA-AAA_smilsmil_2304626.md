## Left Page

### January Saturday, 28 1871.

Dull \+ overcast with snow at night. Got to studio at 9.20
\+ worked for an hour or so on
"Chaldpeel Pond" then to work on
12×22 "Indian Pass" - Painted
thinly over sky and distance \+
solidly over foreground, Trees
changing the form entirely.
Home to lunch abt. 30 \- back abt
2 \- \+ worked until 3 \- Hy Peters
Gray called for a few minutes.
At 3 went in to Perspective class
\- there until 4.30 \- washed up \+
put on my "thinking cap" until 6 \-
wanting a subject for a picture
to follow up the success of my
water color \- Home to tea at 6.30
Edw. Parker arr. about 8. P.M. \- Engraving
"Charcoaling" Ichabod Crane \+
the Headless Horseman" \- To bed
soon after 11 \-

## Right Page

### January Sunday, 29 1871.

Dull day and more snow \- Six inches
must have fallen during the night \-
Mrs. Newt to Dr. Hastings \+ heard
him preach from 1st Tim. 6th Ch.
12th 13th \+ 14th v \- the discourse turning
upon the words professing \+ confessing \-
He doesn't like the word professor
as applied to a Christian, but confession,
and talked learnedly, but interestingly, of
original greek \+ translations. Home
abt 2 \- dinner about 1 \- a disturbed
snooze in my chair during p.m. \-
unwell, throat sore, so stayed at
home. Read \+ wrote letters to
Wm. J. Richards \+ uncle William \-
To bed about 11.15
