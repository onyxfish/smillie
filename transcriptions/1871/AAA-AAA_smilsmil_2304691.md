## Left Page

### June Tuesday, 13 1871.

Up soon after 5 o'clk, cloudless. At 7 with Messrs. Dwight, Nimiam, Porewer \+ gel. with \- Pratt as guide we started for the tops of Grey's Peak. We rode horseback about 14 miles getting to our stopping place at 11 \- lariated horses \+ started for tops \- Heard thunder, clouds coming up \- climbing with great difficulty, the air so thin, \- half way up, caught by snow storm \- passed when I reach the tops \- Glorious effects of storms about us but the great view lost \- Strange electric phenomenon \- we all fizzed like beer bottles \- hair stood on end \- strong smell like sulphur matches \- Alarmed \- hurried down \- horses gone all but one \- note from Mr. B and gel. that they had gone on to hunt the horses \- met them \+ horses \- long ride back \- very tired \- Barton House at 8 o'clk \- to bed at 9 \- Slept well \-
