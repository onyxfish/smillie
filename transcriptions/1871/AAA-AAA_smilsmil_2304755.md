## Left Page

### October, Sunday 29, 1871

Brisk breezy and cool \- [illegible] went to D. J. P. Thompson's to hear what had been announced would be his farewell sermon, but he stated that it was a mistake. He will however very soon leave for studies in Germany and Egypt. He read a well written paper thanking his people for what they have done for him \- then preached a regular sermon from a text in Deut. Dinner at \- at 3.30 went to St. Thomas's and heard the fine music as usual and a very good sermon from Morgan \- VOk, 6¼ 13¼ After service, with Ge walked up to the Park \- through to 6th ave. \- home \- Tea at 6 \- Evef J wrote until 10 o'clk \- to bed at about 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 30, 1871

Beautiful day. Got to studio about 9.45 \- Kindled fire and then set palette \+ as promptly as possible got to work on my Chicago Contribution 12½ × 16 \- "Evening among the High Sierras" worked until 1.30 \- Mr. Davis, with note of introduction from Kate Drinker called about noon \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.15 \- worked as long as I could see getting it all in \+ tolerably satisfactory. Home to tea at 6 \- Evef writing Committee notices, a note to Miss Rebecca Jones, washing brushes and entertaining During \- To bed at 11.30
