## Left Page

### March Thursday, 9 1871.

Dull, foggy morning — got to studio at 9 o'clk and to work on etching of Colorado Arms. Kept at it pretty steadily until near 1 o'clk — Home to lunch at 1.30 — Back at 2.30 — Mild, Spring-like and clear — Etched until 5 o'clk getting very nervous over the flat work. At 5 o'clk — Armstrong came in and posed for a little while but as both of us were tired nothing was done — I swept \+ dusted \+ home to tea at 7 — Queer, feeling very much used up. At 8 went to Studio for drawing of Niagara — Father worked some on die — Got to bed about 9.30

## Right Page

### March Friday, 10 1871.

Heavy Rain at 7 a.m. — more rain from 9.30 until about 11 — Clear and very warm afternoon — Got to Studio at 9. Misses Tiffany (12") Ripley (13") Mauny — Jones (7") \+ Whiting (1) came \+ worked away until 1.30 — Home to lunch at 1.45 — back at 2.30 and finished etching of Colorado Arms (2½ day) — Stopped it out \+ Ready for biting — About 5.20 Armstrong came in \+ she posed for me for about half an hour — Home to tea at 7 — wrote a letter to Uncle Willie. Mr Van V. of Georgia called — At 9 went to Knoedler's \+ saw Eastman Johnston's new picture "The old Stage Coach" — At 10 got John Rogers' — 43.S. — great crowd to see his Rip Van Winkle — Home at 11 — Joe \+ Will there — To bed at 11.30
