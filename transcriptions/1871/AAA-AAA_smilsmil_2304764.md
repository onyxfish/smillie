## Left Page

### November Wednesday, 8 1871.

a Raw and wintry sort of day, over-
cast during the mng. Got to studio
about 9 o'clk. Set to work on
"lifting of the clouds" \+ by 1 o'clk
had quite Repainted the sky —
Wordsworth Thompson came in
about noon — Then Rossell for the
Chicago pictures so that it was 2
when I got home to lunch and
nearly 3 when I got back to studio
worked as long as I could see on
painting, getting over the mountains
\+ middle distance. Geo A. Leavitt
called. — About 5, Townley came
in — He wants me to write the notices
of Chicago exports to be published —
Home to tea at 7 — wrote five
notices \+ mounted miss Jones's
drawing \+ washed brushes —
got to bed at 1 o'clk a.m.

## Right Page

### November Thursday, 9 1871.

Charming day \+ much milder
than yesterday — Got to studio
at 9.30 \+ to work on "Lifting of the
Clouds" — At it until after 1 o'clk,
then getting list of Chicago pictures
\+ answering a note from Leavitt.
Home to lunch at 1.50 — back at
2.40 — a characteristic squabble
between Jas. Hart \+ Pratt. Painted
until 4.30, when I could see no
longer, getting over the foliage and
rocks of the farther foreground.
Home to tea at 6.30 — Superb
crimson Northern lights. Worked
until nearly 11 o'clk, mounting
my oil studies. — To bed at
11 15.
