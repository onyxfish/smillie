## Left Page

### June, Sunday, 11, 1871.

I sit in the shade of a pine tree that sings to me a glorious hymn as the west wind sweeps through its branches. Below me lies Idaho and the winding gorge through which runs the road to Georgetown. Beyond stretches the "Snowy Range", glistening white in this glorious atmosphere \+ above all is a sky of purest blue, unspotted by one cloud. AM Returned to the hot soda springs \+ took a bath \- 113°. Fahr. back to hotel at 12. Dinn about 1.30 \- as I felt tired \+ unwell, took a nap. Things all packed ready to leave by the stage for Georgetown \- It was nearly 5 o'clk before it arr'd. The ride extremely dusty but the scenery much finer than any yesterday. Got to G. town at 8 o'clk \- Geo. had left the coach 2 miles from town \- I walked back for him \- got quite out of sorts \+ went to bed at 10 Really sick \- I am sure the climate as well as the food aggravates my stomach trouble.

## Right Page

### June, Monday, 12, 1871.

At Barton House \- Georgetown, Cal.

After a miserable night, up at 6 o'clk. Felt better as the day advanced. At 8 o'clk, went with Geo. to Stewart's Mill \- there met Mr \+ Mrs. Nordhoff \+ spent the mf in an extremely interesting manner going through the mills \+ having the process of working the silver out of the ore, shown and explained to us. Climbed with Mr. Stewart to one of the veins just opened. Back to Hotel at 1. Found there Messrs Dwight, Miriam \+ Brewer. Dinner at 1.30, at 2 went to stable \- got horse \+ light wagon \+ with George drove down the valley 6 or 8 miles \- got two or three sketches. Back about 6.30. Tea at 7 \- Slight thunder shower. Engaged horses to go up Grey's Peak to-morrow mf. To bed about 10.
