## Left Page

### July, Monday, 3, 1871.

Up about 6 \- Bkfst about 7 and at 7.30 we all got off, horseback, for Lamon's Garden \- There for a short time, then to the Yo\-Semite fall \- Climbed as far as I could at the base of it \- then to the Saloon \- Cobblers all around, and back to dinner at 1. Reading the Heralds until near 4 o'clk then with Cap. Webb took horse \+ cantered over the meadows and along the River to Bridal Veil fall. Climbed to the very foot \+ was much pleased \+ well for my trouble \- saw some splendid circular Rainbows on my way back, met George, we wandered over the Meadows, along the River \+ under the trees getting in about 7 o'clk \- Tea about 8 \- Eve'g Chatting with different ones \+ to bed about 11.15. Thunder Storm threatening again during the P.M. \- nothing in it.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday, 4, 1871.

Anniversary of Am. Independence, very hot at that \- Bkfst. about 8 o'cl. Spent the m'g Reading \+ trying to write a letter to the "Mail", but gave it up at 11 o'clk, as "no go", went down to the River but soon driven back by a thunder Storm that gave us a very welcome soaking and cooling. Dinner about 2 \- After that horse Races in honor of the glorious day \- a motley crowd of native Americans, native Indians \- negroes \+ Chinamen \- in number about 80 not counting horses \+ dogs. 3 mo short, exciting Races then all over \- Some grand effects of sound by firing of a loaded log. At 3.30 started off, went beyond Hutchings \- then climbed back of Black's, then along the River bank looking for studio \+ home a little before 6 \- tea about 7 \- wrote without accomplishing any thing, until near 11 \- then to bed \-
