## Left Page

### January Monday, 2 1871.

Colic before daylight — Up at 5.30
feeling sore & unable to move — on
the sofa until 11 when I washed &
dressed and at noon started out
with Charl, calling on Mr. [Jessup's]
& Mr. Savony in 52d 8. He then left
me & I went on alone until about
3.30 when I got home again — off
at 4 o'clk after eating an apple [baked]
& a couple of crackers — The day
overhead, overcast — underfoot
very slushy — I worked away
until 9.45, when, for the 2d time I
called at Mr. Hyde's 195 Madison
ave, when the Tweedys were — Con-
sidering my condition I got through
the day wonderfully well & enjoyed
it, Really — My list (in pocket of book)
numbered over 50 — made 27 calls —
Home a little before 11 — To bed at
11.30 —

## Right Page

### January Tuesday, 3 1871.

Bright cold day — Another fit of
colic about 5 a.m — all day my
stomach very sore — Got to studio
at 10 — Spent the mng. kindling
fire, sweeping & putting things
to rights — Home to lunch abt —
back at 2 — worked on tracing of
large drawing (5½ days). I find
difficulties increase as I get
further on — Mr. Forbes, then
Mr. Knudsen called — About 4.30
I finished sweeping up —
Home to tea at 6.15 — Evening
spent reading "King Henry
IV — Second part" until 9.30
To bed at 10.30.
