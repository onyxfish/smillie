## Left Page

### August Monday, 28 1871.

Sotyone Valley —

Up late, bkfst. soon after 8 — Spent the m'f in arranging trunks, repacking, etc. and wrote a letter to Many Truedy — Lunched at 2.15, after I had written a short letter to Sister Mary — At 3, Gue & I set out for the boat at 3.30 — we started in's Sacramento for Donahue — 34 miles — at heade of bay — Very rough \+ foggy crossing the channel but after passing the head found it charming — Enjoyed the day — the clear sky, the mts., the water, pelicans, gulls, etc. Exceedingly — got to Donahue soon after 6 — Cars to Healdsberg, where we arrived about 8 — Went to Sotyone Hotel — Tea at 8.15 — to Room \+ bed in a little house near by. Walked about a while and to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### August Tuesday, 29 1871.

Geyser Hotel — Room No. 1.

Up 5.30 — Bkfst. soon after 6 — at 6.45 started in open wagon for the Geysers — got to first station at 7.45 — waited there an hour until horses \+ wagon were changed \+ Mrs. Poole, a good looking, dashing Californian had time to get up, dress, pack her trunk \+ eat her breakfast. The m'f had been very foggy \+ cold, now it was exceedingly hot \+ me toiled very slowly up hill — The Ride over "the hog's back", very exciting, 4 in hand, driven by the dashing Mrs. Poole. Down the hills to the Geyser Hotel, something tremendous — got in near 1 o'clk — Lunch soon after — The heat of P.M. chatting with Mrs. P — at 4, Gue \+ I went through the Canyon — back to dinner at 6 — more chatting until 8 — Then with a party I again went through the Canyon by moonlight — glorious night — back and to bed about 10.30
