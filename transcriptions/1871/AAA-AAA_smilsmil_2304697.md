## Left Page

### June, Sunday 25, 1871.

Another splendid day, but cool
\+ very windy of course — Bkfst.
late — a short walk after + then
to Church (B. Scudder's) with
Matt + Geo. Heard Rev. Alex.
Thompson + Uff. Matt, 1st ch. 1st v.
on the genealogy of Christ. After
service, went in to Chinese S.S.
\- had an interesting class of me.
back to hotel at 2.15 — feeling under
the influence of a cold — a hot
cup of tea + off on a long walk —
went to Woodward's Gardens.
a rowdy turn-out — back at 5.30
Tea at 6.30 — a snoozing sort
of eve'g, slightly disturbed by
a call from — Staples — finished
letter home + to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 26, 1871.

Ross House — Room No. 20 —
Modesto.

Cloudless, cool + windy. Spent
this day very largely — breakfasting
about 9 o'clk + spending the
morning completing packing —
Had lunch at 2.30 left the
hotel about 3.30 — ferry boat to
Oakland pier about 4 o'clk. Train got
about 4.30 with all our bags + baggage
and our party, Uncle G. + Jinnie, Cap-
ons Webb + daughter Hattie. Matt Geo
\+ J. — Changed at Lathrop just after
sundown + got to Modesto, the end
of the R.R. at present, at 8.30 — The
Ross House is just opposite the depot —
Modesto is a few scattered, frame
buildings in the midst of a burnt-
up plain — Glorious moonlight —
Had quite a chat with the proprietor
of the hotel about his old
friend (— W. M. Hart) + fellow apprentice.
Got to bed about 10 o clk.
