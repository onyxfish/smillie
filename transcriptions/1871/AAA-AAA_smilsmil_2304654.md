## Left Page

### March Monday, 27 1871.

Miserable day \- mizzle \+ rain \- got to studio at 8.30 \- kindled fire \+ worked a little \- then down to Commissioner of Juries at 9.30 but cored'nt make any thing out of him then to Clerk of Court \- no better luck. At 11 o'clk \- Court of Genl. Sessions \- [illegible] I answered to my name \+ with a lot of others, was excused for the day. Back to studio at 1.45 \+ worked on Colorado Arms (6½ days) just as tight as I could spring until 6.10 lunching at the Studio \- I consider conscientiously that I have "put in" a full day \- worked with lozenge graver \- Home to tea at 7 \- letter from Matt \- Well feeling very tired \- but put pins in Annie Tiffany's drawing board \- Got to bed soon after 10 \-

## Right Page

### March Tuesday, 28 1871.

Clear, Splendid m'fg, cloudy, cold midday \- superb night \- Got to Studio at 8.15 \+ dry pointed on "Colorado Arms" (7½ days) until 10.15 \- then down to Court of Genl. Sessions \- discharged at 11.20 much to my delight \- then to Cpt. B. M. Co. Went with Goodale to Jo. Am. house \+ got correct drawing for Arms of Bancode Trujillo \- back \- furnished overdée \- left it to be proved while I went with father to dinner at 1.15 \- back at 2 got [illegible] \+ at Studio at 3.15 \- laid a ground on die. \+ made corrections on Trujillo drawing \- Home to Tea at 7 \- well fixed drawing boards of Misses Ripley \+ Whiting \+ carried my big wooden lay figure down to Studio at 10 o'clk Go to bed at 11. 15.
