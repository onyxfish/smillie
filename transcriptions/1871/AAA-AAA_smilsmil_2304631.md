## Left Page

### February Thursday, 9 1871

Another miserable day — Got to studio about 9 o'clk and to work on locomotive (7th day) worked until ) — then home to lunch — back at 2.20 \+ worked until 4 but did not get it finished as I had hoped from 4 until 5.15 amused and disgusted by the parade under my windows, through slush and mud of the Irish Rabble of N.Y. to greet the Fenians that England wont even take the trouble to keep in prison — At 6 went to Brooks Bro— then to Prof. Rood's — 34 E. 15th S. Home to tea at 6.45 — made paste — mounted two sketches — then made up Water Color acct. \+ wrote W. C. letters until 11 o'clk — To bed at 11.40

## Right Page

### February Friday, 10 1871

Glorious day — got to studio at 9.30 \+ spent the whole A.M. until 1 o'clk with my pupils (5th lesson.) then, at 1.30, home to lunch, back at 2.25 P.M. worked on locomotive drawing (7½ days) and at last got it finished — W. W. Richards made me a long and pleasant call — At 4 o'clk — stretched paper for Miss Tiffany — Home to tea at near 7 — Eve'g mounting locomotive drawing and writing to Falconer \+ Van Ingen To bed about 10.30.
