## Left Page

### December Saturday, 30 1871.

Very disagreeable day — could not induce myself to go to the studio or undertake work of any description — a dead and lethargic feeling having possession of me — I mettled but without strength — could not complain of any specific pain, but simply, my machinery would not go — Spent most of the m'g in my big chair — After lunch meybuls to "Stratoning" — then to Y. M. C. A. Bldg \+ called on Gifford, Tiffany, Hart, Eaton \+ Miss Walters — then down to McCream B'way \+ 11th St. bought gloves — then home — Ev'g, much used up with stuffed head \+ nose — etc — etc — To bed at 11 o'clk, and passed a very miserable night.

## Right Page

### December Sunday, 31 1871.

A worse day than yesterday — muggy and foggy — Up late feeling not so well as yesterday. Did not go out of the house but spent most of the day on the sofa — used up with a cold and biliary derangements, the root of the difficulty being too much work and too little play — As the year goes out I feel tired and disheartened — I have accomplished little during the past year — all work comes slowly from head \+ hands, thereby losing much value while ahead I see no glimmer of light — I am not unconsious however that I have very much to be thankful for and still working and striving, I hope to be more worthy. To bed about midnight.
