## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 1 1871.

Up before 6 o'clk, after a pretty hard night —
blkfst, & at 7.30 we started in a handsome
turnout for the New Almaden Quicksilver
works, 12 miles distant — the mtt foggy
& quite cool, cleared as we got to the works
at 9 o'clk — got permission to visit the mine
& drove in to the top of the hill, a steep climb
of 2½ miles — ¾ of an hour. Soon after 10 o'clk
went into the great Eastern Tunnel under
the guidance of a little urchin, 13 yrs old, and
followed by 2 or more Mexicans — we spent
an hour wandering through the passages
climbing down into black holes & stumbling
in clear like darkness — was intensely interested —
drove down the mtt to the works again in 12 m.
Had a letter from Staples to Mr. Randal — Sub —
Very polite & showed us through the works
which are very simple — at 11.15 went to the
little hotel & got a lunch, besides drinking
of their Vichy Spring — at 12.50 started back
to San Jose getting in at 2.30 — found Uncle A.
& Capt. Webb at the Auzerais House — all took
3.20 train for San F, getting in to Grand Hotel at
6.30 — dinner at 7 and at 7.45 Gil and I went
to the California Theatre where we met Mr.
Porter, painter of the scenery — He took us in
giving us good seats & sitting with us — The
play "Ready" & scenery about the City of San F.
and the Yo-Semite — back to hotel at 11.30
and to bed at 12 — after a very hard
days work.

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 2 1871.

Up about 8 o'clk. mtt. Rather a sore head —
Blkfst there until 11 o'clk. writing,
getting duds, wash etc in order and
settling accts with Gil. Then at the
same business with uncle a. for an hour —
considerable complication — About
noon started out; called at Mr. Ashburner's
office, hoping to see him & ask questions — out of
town, but saw there another gentleman who
answered me — then to Genl. Hardenberg's to
ask more questions — not in — then to R.R.
ticket office to ask more questions previous
to selecting route home — then to 842 Jackson St
to make arrangements with policeman Woodruff
to show us China town — again to Hardenberg's
— not in — then at 3 o'clk, back to hotel — so
under my headache that I went to bed
& slept until 6 o'clk — then up — dinner
at 7 — at 8 with Gil & Frank Staples met
Woodruff and went through China town —
into some of the worst dens I ever saw —
thieves and prostitutes — Restaurant & kitchen,
theatre and joss house — gambling & policy
shops — Back to hotel at 10.30 — to my
room at 11 and wrote until 2 o'clk A.M.
Eyes hurt me — Rather scared for fear
of granulation.
