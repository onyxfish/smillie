## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 29 1871.

Bkfst at 8 — when we got up it was leaden overhead and sprinkling — Poor prospect for Watkins Glen — After bkfst all agreed to give it up — Maud, Gil \+ I went out to ride — Up to Water Cure — over the River etc — Called on Mr. Reed (Jut.) not at home — the clouds broke away — clear, cold \+ very windy. Back at 12.20 — Lunch at 1 — At 2 went off with Mr. D. and wandered away to the top of the hills — over \+ around — getting in near 6 — Splendid tramp — Saw Seneca Lake many miles away. Dinner soon after 6 — every again in billiard Room until 11 — To bed at 11.45

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 30 1871.

Beautiful morning, very clear after a cold night — Up at 7.30 — but did not have bkfst. until 9 o'clk. amused ourselves about the house, stables \+ gardens until time for us to go to depot. Maud, Jack, Gil \+ I Rode to depot to take 11.30 train — found that it had run into an oil train \+ no one could tell when it would be along. Ret'd to Mr. D's — lunched at 1.30 \+ spent the p.m. on piazza until 4.30 when Maud Gil \+ I took horse cars to her bro. Geo. Diven's where we took dinner at about 6.30 — After dinner — Mr. Gil D — drove Gil \+ I to depot to take 8.10 train to Ny. — Train late — beautiful moonlight so I paced up \+ down the platform until 9.30 when the train came along — found our section all ready for us (Mr. D. had telegraphed) on the last car. The evening Star — got to bed soon after 10 and would have enjoyed it very much if I had not suffered a good deal of pain —
