## Left Page

### July, Sunday 23, 1871.

Cloudless and very hot. Rtfo: late \+ the most spent at the saw pit writing a letter for the "Mail" \— Dinner after 2 the feature of which was some excellent ice cream. After dinner, more writing until about 5 o'clk when Uncle A. came after me, \+ with him \+ George S walked to the upper of the meadow below Hutchings and enjoyed evening effects over Cathedral Rocks \— Tea about 7 \— Very enjoyíng, the moonlight and correcting the M.S of the day \— To bed about 10 45

## Right Page

### July, Monday 24, 1871.

Up before 6 o'clk, to have an early start, but owing to Ridgeway's want of preparation we did not get off until 9.30, all considerably out of humor, the day, glorious, Getting to Register Rock, Matt, Geo \+ S walked up the ladder Trail \+ rejoined the horses above the Vernal Fall, got to Snow's at 12, stopped but a short time, then on near the top of the Trail, the three pack horses being just ahead of me, \+ tied together, the hind one backed \+ pulled the middle one over the trail with him \— never saw a more fearful sight \— one horse rolling over the other \+ down the steep incline of broken Rocks \— a marvel neither horse was seriously injured \— While working to get one up Uncle A. was knocked over a Rock while Ridgeway's leg was caught under the horse \— For a few minutes things looked very serious, but we managed to Roll the horse off R. and then get him up \— a delay of near an hour Re-backing \— Got to the valley about 2 o'clk. Dinner at 3 \— Sauntered up the valley until near 7 \— Eve[nin]g, we all cooking [in the] promise \— Wandered down to the River by moonlight \— got into our blankets at [illegible]
