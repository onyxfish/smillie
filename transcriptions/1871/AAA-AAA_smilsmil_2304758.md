## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 13 1871.

Up at 7 — bkfs at 8 — Feel rather tired —
About 9. with Matt & Ge. went to H.G. [illegible]
store — I left them there and went to the
Union National Bank & introduced my[illegible]
to Mr. Connell the Cashier — Engaged to
fan Mulford — to my surprise met there
Charley Adams. with him went to the
Washington St. tunnel and the Rive[r] —
Left him then & met Matt & Ge. & went to
the Crosby Opera House Gallery — then to
the Academy of Design — there left M.
\+ G. and hurried back to Bank — Mr C.
took me to the Clearing House — then the
Vice Pres. of the Bank took me to the
Chamber of Commerce — Parted at
12.30 — went to the hotel — mended my[illegible]
\+ packed it — then to dinner — a very good
one — Rice note from Mary Treedy — At
2 o'clk. Charley Adams came with Campl[bell]
\+ we drove about the city — Wabash and
Michigan ave's. — to the Water Works, thro[ugh]
North Dearborn S. — Lincoln Park, Uno[?]
Lasalle S. — Tunnel etc. — back to hotel [at]
4 — Settled up — at 5.15 left by M. C. and
Great Western R.R. Pullman Hotel Car —
Enjoyed our tea, served in our [own]
Section very much — and to bed about
10 o'clk — Up at 3 as we crossed at
Detroit, to see boats etc. — Then to bed —

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 14 1871.

on Pullman "Arlington" Michigan Central — Cataract House — Niagara — Room 157 —

About 6.30 when I got up — Pleasant
[illegible], but really cold, got our bkfs.
[a]t 8 & 9 o'clk — Journeying & enjoying
everything very much — Passed through
London & Paris & had our first views of
Lake Ontario at Hamilton — Think the
G.W. R.R. is one of the best laid roads
I ever traveled over — Got to Susp. Br.
a little before 1 — Transferred to Erie
R.R. cars — then on in 15m. at Niagara —
Got Rooms, washed up & to dinner
at 3.30 after I had taken a preliminary
run to the Falls — After dinner, with
Matt & Ge. went to head of falls, then
to the foot, walked to Susp. Br. and
back, then about town until 7 o'clk.
At 7.45 went to tea with Matt —
at 10. went out alone on piazza over
the Rapids — in the darkness, their
voice came up to me as it had not
before, my ears being calloused by
the din of 3000 miles of R.R. travel,
not to mention several cities by the
way — very very cold — Fires
in the grates — and very acceptable —
To bed about 10.30
