## Left Page

### March Saturday, 25 1871.

Another glorious day, got to studio at 9 o'clk and set to work repainting on "Lake Raid" 12×22 in, Mr. Snock Lewis's pic. which I have borrowed to send to Spring Exhbn N.A. of D. — went over all of it — making it very much more delicate and I am afraid, chalky — still in spite of its losing color, I'm sure 'tis better — Allie Diven called + Mrs. Jessup + Miss Joies. Home to lunch at 1.15 back at 2 + painted until near 6. Ned Pitchin called — Home to tea at 7.15 — Evening drawing on "Banco de Trujillo" (1) until after 11 o'clk. To bed about 2 —

## Right Page

### March Sunday, 26 1871.

A sickly sort of day, attempting to be good or bad, hard to say which, until between 5 + 6 P.M. when it commenced to rain — At 9.30 a.m. walked to studio at 10.30 went to Madison Ave, Baptist Ch. Heard Dr. Elder from the text describing Christ as growing in grace and knowledge and stature + in the favor of God + Man — Baptizing after service — walked home with Kate. Dinner at home — Reading after — then a nap from 3 to 4 — at 5 walked down to Will's in St. Lukes Place — Home at 6.30 — Wrote Matt a letter to Matt and to bed about 10.30.

Sleet storm covering the ground white with slush, at 10 P.M. —
