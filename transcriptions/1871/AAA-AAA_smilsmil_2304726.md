## Left Page

### August Saturday, 12 1871.

a charming day — got off to my work before 8 o'clt. Study of Cathedral Rocks and worked until after 1 o'clc getting it finished in a very unsatisfactory manner. It seems to me that I work tremendously hard only to disgust myself with my efforts.
Afternoon at 2.30 went over to my study of the Domes and after working until sun down I finished that in a manner hardly more satisfactory than the first one. Home to tea at 7.30 — got a copy of the N.Y. World Mail — July 25 with my first letter in it. Evng wrote a letter to Minn, washed brushes and got to bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### August Sunday, 13 1871.

Still the same glorious weather. Late bkfst then reading the newspapers until near 10 o'clc — went up to see the horse race over the new track — back in about one hour — wrote until near 2 — then went to the River and took a bath — back \+ had dinner — wrote from about 3.30 until 6 — "Mail" matters — then paddled an hour on the River — Evng wrote to Father, Matt \+ Simma [illegible] — got to bed about 11 o'clc.
