## Left Page

### Sept. Saturday, 9, 1871.

Up about 4.30 after sleeping soundly. Got to Ogden about 6. Transferred to U.P. R.R. Checked trunk, then into depot and by next time, away soon after 5 o'clk. W. \+ NW. Hamlin have the section adjoining us. Spent a good part of the m[illegible] in observation car going through Devil's gate canyon Weber \+ Echo canyons. I got no dinner at Wahsatch. All the afternoon going through a most dreary sagebrush country. The sky overcast and dismal \+ the air very cool. Tea at Bryg[illegible]. Beautiful sunset. by twilight went through the peculiar green River country. About 5.30, quite a shower. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Sept. Sunday, 10, 1871.

Up at 6. W[illegible] quite cold. Clouds lying low. Rolling country. Rather bare[illegible] — a great improvement upon that of last night. Saw many antelopes. In one herd, 24 — in another 15. Bdpt. at Laramie at 9 o'clk. W[illegible] clearing beautifully until we got towards Sherman when we ran into a very raw, wet fog or mist. Fire kindled in our car. Reached Cheyenne at about noon. Miss Parker \+ Capt. Bryant left to go down to Denver. An antelope playing about the station. All the afternoon, monotonous, rolling country. My headache getting the better of me between 2 \+ 4 had quite a long nap. At Sidney at 5.15. but took no tea. as my headache was increasing. To bed about 8.45.
