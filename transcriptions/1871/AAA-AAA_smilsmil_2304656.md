## Left Page

### March, Friday 31, 1871

Sulperb day again — Got to Studio at 8.30 \+ worked for an hour on "Lake Placid" 12×22 — improving it, I think, decidedly. with scumbling \+ glazing. At 9.30 Misses R— (19) J (18) M (13) J (13) \+W (7) came also the model — They worked until about 1 o'clk — Home to lunch at 1.30 — at Phil. Rehearsal at 2.30 — enjoying it very much — back to Studio at 5 — lettered tablet to go on my 12×22 frame. About 6 — Billing came in — we walked home at 6.30 — Rueff went to Baumgraf's — then to the Jessup's 50 W 52d — Spent a very pleasant eve't until 10.30 — Allie driven out, calling on Annie Lowther — Home \+ to bed at 11—

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 1, 1871

Threatening mt — drizzling day \+ atrocious night of driving sleet \+ Rain — Went to Studio — prepared "Lake Placid" for N.A. of D. \+ then downtown to A.B.N.C. at 10.30 — Spent most of the A.M. with Goodall receiving instructions for drawings. Lunched at 1.30 with father — worked on "Colorado Arms". Until 3.30 — then alterations (9 days) in "Arms of Ecuador" drawing until 5 clk — 5.30 for home — stopped at Studio — home at 6.15 — tea at 6.30 — Aunt Jessie gone to Brooklyn. Jamma still with us — Down to Phil. Concert at 7.45 — Enjoyed the concert exceedingly — after it walked up home in a horrible storm — to bed at about midnight.
