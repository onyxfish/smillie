## Left Page

### October Saturday, 21 1871.

Delightful day — made some notes on acct. of Chicago Relief Fund, so that it was 10 o'clk. when I got to studio. It was then some time before I got my window & desk fixed up so that I could go to work — I also called on several in the building for Chicago contributions. Atwood called at noon — Home to lunch at 1.30 — W. Ford, Baptist min­ister of Montrose at lunch — back at 2.30 — worked on etching (5¼ days) — Father & Mother came to studio between 3 & 4 o'clk — Soon after, Allie Diven, Annie Sowden and sister & Josie Jessup called. Home to tea at 6.30 — Read awhile aloud — then writing Chicago Com­ business — About midnight when I got to bed —

## Right Page

### October Sunday, 22 1871.

Another charming day — Myf went to Dr. Hastings & heard a stranger — After service walked down to 31st St. with Allie Diven & Josie Jessup — Home to dinner at 6 o'clk — at 4 — at St. Thomas's good music as usual, and rather a better sermon than usual, I thought, from Dr. Morgan. After service, walked up to the Park — Twilight & moonlight contending most deliciously — the air still & mild — Home to tea at 7 — Evef. mate some and got to bed about 11 —
