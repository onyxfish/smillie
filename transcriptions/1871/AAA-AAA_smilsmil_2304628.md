## Left Page

### February Wednesday, 1 1871.

mild, overcast day — everything running underfoot — got to studio at 9.30. Misses Ripley & Tiffany came a little after 10 (2d. lesson) arranged books & palette on table gave all my mf to them — Home at 1.30 — used up with a headache \+ went to bed — up at 7 — took some Tea \+ at U.A. of D — at 8.10, Rimmers 7th lecture — abg. went up into the galleries, there met the Kimitalls & the Tiffanys — went to studio at 10. home at 10.45. and working on notes of lecture until 12.30 — To bed a little before 1 o'clk — mild, hazy moonlight night, got to sleep about 3.30

## Right Page

### February Thursday, 2 1871.

atrocious day — missfeing & muggy. Left miss Tiffany's umbrella at her door \+ got to studio at 10 o'clk — unfit for work. Spent most of mf carefully lettering a Fallet with quotation \+ title of Browning's poem at 12.30 went to N.A. of D. \+ fixed it on my frame there. Home to lunch at 1.30 — Jane Hinshelwood came in \+ delivered herself of some crazy bassline. Uncle Frank Bourne is exceedingly ill Small Pox, probably with complications Back to studio at 3 — did nothing during P.M. — but Read up anatomy — at 6 went to Dechamp's to price small lay-figure horse — $75.00 gave it up — then to Mrs. Ford's with her gauntlets — there until 7 — home at 7.30 — and stretched drawing paper \+ wrote water color notices until 10.30 To bed at 11.15 —
