## Left Page

### December Tuesday, 12 1871.

Splendid day — Up late again
\+ did not get to the studio until
9.45 — then to work on one of
my Summer studies \+ worked
until 1.15 — then home to lunch
— back at 2.30 \+ worked on 16×20
"On the Saco" until 4.30 getting
it into better shape, I think.
Home to tea at 6 — Gilbert Petti-
surprised us while at the table.
He has just arr'd from Lexington
\+ Reports his mother as very low —
at 8 o'clk — at N.A. of D — to hear
Page's first lecture on Art — a
very full audience — and a
very empty lecture — Walked
up with Louis Tiffany — Home
at 10 o'clk — a little Reading
— some brushes washed and
to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### December Wednesday, 13 1871.

Overcast wf — got to studio at
9.20 — to work first up on 16×20
on the Saco" — fussing over the
water. Then on 10×15 — W. Lafayette
for Watkins — Seitz came in
\+ wanted a little sketch, as
a Christmas present for a friend.
I owe him for mounting boards.
At noon a miserable sleet storm
commenced that kept up for
the rest of the day — Home to
lunch abt. 1.5 — back at 2.15 —
Worked on circle for Lanthier \+
blocked in some changes on
24×40 — on the Bouquet — an old
Academy picture of 2 or 3 yrs
ago — Home to tea at 6 — well
mounting maps \+ studies until
10 o'clk, Then some Reading
\+ to bed abt 1.15
