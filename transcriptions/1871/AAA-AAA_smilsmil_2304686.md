## Left Page

### June, Saturday, 3, 1871.

Burnett, Ho. Room 55 —
Cincinnati — O. —

Day uniformly hot, otherwise mixed. Up at 5.30 — 8tfs. Train late but got off about 7 a.m. The Ride over the Alleghanies very exciting — fine hill scenery before Reaching Pittsburgh at 10.30 — got away from P. about 11 — the view of it, crossing the bridge \+ from P. — extremely picturesque — got to Denniston, for dinner — about 3 oclock — poor affair — Countryministrations and disappointing to me — got to Columbus — tea — about 7 — Country still poor \+ flat — fixed pillow \+ stretching myself out in my palace compartment, enjoyed moonlight \+ cool every breeze. Then went to [illegible] got into Cinn'ti about 11 — bus to the Burnett House \+ to bed just before 12 —

## Right Page

### June, Sunday, 4, 1871.

An immensely hot day — did not get up until near 9 — Bkfst near 10 \+ at 10.30 started out with Gill — went first to Methodist Ch. Cor. 7th \+ Smith St. Heard singing \+ prayer — then went to Papist, Unitarian, Campbell, etc, etc, etc \+ at last to Baptist in G's \+ Vue — Heard sermon \+ back to Hotel. Gill went in while I went to the River, Suspension Bridge \+ wandered over one of the strange (to me) River boats — back to Hotel at 2.30 very tired — dinner at 3 — Shower with thunder — napped until 5. Then took horse car over the bridge to Covington \+ back — very delightful — tea at 7 — lounged on porch until 9 — wrote letter home —
