## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 19, 1871

Very hot. At 8.30 got off to my work —
went about a mile or more down
the River & painted a study of
some of the cliffs on the South side.
Made no outline, only a rough charcoal
one & no frotté, painted very rapidly
& got done at 2 o'clk. Much annoyed
by changing light, at last clouded
over, thunderstorms N & W. & made
almost wild with tormenting flies
& ants — Eat my lunch, there,
being no light for afternoon study,
& feeling very tired, sauntered
home at 3.30, taking little sketches
by the way. — At home, finished
crayon sketch of South Dome —
Rec'd. letter from Kate — July 3d.
Uncle A. went to Peregoy's just
for a trip. — Sucy. loafing, wrote
a few words to the Tweedy girls
— Rain on going to bed at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 20, 1871

Overcast & threatening day — at 9 o'clk
off with water color traps for the Upper
end of the valley — went beyond
[illegible] & climbed a point on
Glacier Rock & got pencil outline
of the valley & Domes — then on & on
getting some hasty notes in my little
sketch book. At last got to Illilouette
Canyon & turned up it, climbing
until 12.30 when I stopped & took
my lunch — then on until I got to
a point where I had a fine view of
the Vernal fall. Spent about a couple
of hours on a water color sketch, then
climbed to where I could see the
Illilouette fall. At 4.30 started back
getting to the house in about 2 hours.
The climb up the Canyon, as hard
as anything I have found here. — Bath
& towelling, then tea. A note from Uncle
[illegible] wont be back to-night. Evening
resting and to bed about 10 o'clk.
