## Left Page

### May Tuesday, 2 1871.

Charming day — got to Studio at 8.30 — Putting Room to Rights until 9.20 — then gave it up \+ to work biting crossing &c on Car die (3 days) until 10 clk — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — worked with graver until 6 o'clk — then again at house cleaning, until near 7 — very tired — Home to tea at 7.30
Back to N.A. of D. — Water Color meeting at 8.30 — no quorum — went up into the gallery \+ at 9.45 walked up with Smillie [illegible]
— Home at ten \+ to bed about 30
Rec'd letter from Ferguson — He is still in Chili, but expects to start for Ecuador soon.

## Right Page

### May Wednesday, 3 1871.

Threatening all day, but Rain did not come until about 9 P.M. Got to Studio at 8.30 — worked until 9.30 trying to get my Room in order, then to work with graver on Car die (3½ days) \+ at it until 12.30 — then home, worked a little on Georgie's drawing, lunch \+ back to Studio at 2.15. Misses J. (21) M. (20) \+ W. (15) there until 5.30 — Miss Ripley made a call, Miss Bascomb called to ask me if I would give her a few lessons. Home at 6.30 — cut \+ mounted drawings for Miss Whiting \+ made a note to Dr Lewis — To bed soon after 10.30
