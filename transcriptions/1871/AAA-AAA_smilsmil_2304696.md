## Left Page

### June, Friday, 23, 1871.

Cloudless + windy. Up late + H\[illegible\] about 9 o'clk. Spent the m\[illegible\] packing + arranging for a start for the Jo-semite, planned for this p.m but about 11 o'clk. Ge announced that he was not able to go — a consultation followed + it was determined to postpone departure until Monday next. Lunch at 2 o'clk. — then out with Uncle Aaron to Telegraph Hill + roamed about the city. Left me at 11.30 and until 6.30 I wandered about alone through the Chinese quarter — then back to tea — Eve\[illegible\], entertained by a call from a very hale old Englishman. W. Wilson of Malta + New Zealand on his way home to England after an absence of 43 yrs. He played his flute + seemed very well pleased. To bed about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday, 24, 1871.

A pleasant day, Late H\[illegible\] again the most of the m\[illegible\] Spent with Matt about among the Chinese stores, bought a pipe + tobacco for Charl — back at 12.30 — lunch at 1.30. Afternoon went with Uncle A. to the China + Japan Steamship dock + went over the Steamers "China" + "Great Republic" — back about 5 o'clk — wrote letter home. Tea about 7 — Eve\[illegible\]. with Uncle A., Matt, Jimmie \[illegible\] Peace + Staples, went to the Emmerson Minstrels, very much amused. back + to bed about 11 —
