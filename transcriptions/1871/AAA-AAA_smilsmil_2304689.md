## Left Page

### June Friday, 9 1871.

Here I sit with a superb view of the Rocky mts. stretching before me \+ fanned by a cool breeze fresh from their snowy tops \- walked this A.M. early enough to see the sunrise over the prairie \- it was cloudless \+ superb \- slept again, then was awakened by Mr. W\- to see the mts \- one of the most glorious sights I ever saw. 4 antilopes were scudding along over the plain quite near to the Train \- Got in to Denver about 9 \+ at once to A.M. H\#. Feeling much better than yesterday \- Bkfst antelope steaks etc \- then went to P.O. found note from Coleman \- off on a trip to the Garden of the Gods \- then to Stage Office bought tickets to Georgetown then back to Hotel \- about the streets much interested in the strange mexican teamsters \+ their trains \- also the Indians, auction sales etc \- wrote letters home \+ to Matt \+ note for Coleman \- Evg\# walked out to Cal. \+ I stk. with Mr. C's boots \- back feeling very much under the weather again \+ to bed about 10 o'clk \-

## Right Page

### June Saturday, 10 1871.

Up at 5 o'clk after a miserable night \- feeling worse than ever \+ hardly daring to start on the stage, but I was desperate \+ could not wait \- Got off at 8 o'clk. with a heavy load \- the sky cloudless \- Soon forgot my misery in enjoyment \- the drive over the plains 12 or 15 miles to foot hills \- irrigation \- then the Vernon Canyon \- hot \+ dusty \- higher, the flowers, then the parks, peculiarly clean \+ cultivated looking, with views of the snowy Range \- Dinner about 2 at Mr. Wheeler's. a very good one \- Then the tremendous descent of Floyd's Hill to Clear Cr. \+ the drive through that wild gorge all worked through by the miners, until at a little after 4 we Reached Idaho where we determined to stop over Sunday. Very glad that we did it. Met Messrs. Dwight Brewer \+ Merrican. also Mr. Nordhoff\* \+ wife \+ two children \- all pleasant tourists \- Evg\#, chatting \- a walk to the hot spr. \+ to bed about 10 o'clk \-

\* Nordhoff \- .
