## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 27 1871.

*"Willow Brook" — Elmira —*

overcast on getting up in the
m'f, but clouds broke away, giving
a cloudy, breezy day. [illegible] at 8
then arranged odds & ends, so as to
get away in the 1 o'clk stage. at
10 o'clk went "down-town" — bid-son
good-byes, little "chores" &c — back
at 11.30 — wrote a note for au Mulford
took it down to P.O. at 12 — back to
dinner at 12 15 & off in stage at 1 —
At Jat. Carmalt's, Miss Churchill & Mr
Schaut[illegible] got onto the stage — m escorted
them to Utica & Binghamton & started
them for Utica — our Erie train late —
got away at 4.40 — got to Elmira at
6.30 — Maud Divew waiting for us
with Carriage — Drove to Willow
Brook — Dinner about 7.15 —
Spent the even'g with the family
very pleasantly until 11 o'clk —
Then to bed —

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 28 1871.

[illegible] at 8 — Heavily overcast
& quite cold — Spent the morning
riding about Elmira with Maud,
Ella & [illegible], I driving —. Back
soon after 12 — Lunch abt —
about 2 off again, Maud, [illegible] &
I in close Carriage — Called
on George Divew — Saw his wife,
children & house — Then on
Eugene D—, ditto — ditto —
Home at 6.15 — Dinner soon
after, most of the evening
spent in the billiard Room
"playing" "pool" with the girls —
To bed at 11 — with the
understanding that if it is
fair to-morrow, we are all to
go to Watkins Glen —
