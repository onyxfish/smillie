## Left Page

### April Saturday, 22 1871.

overcast, with occasional spatters. At 845 called on Louis Tiffany at his home, Madison ave. \- Saw his sketches made in Spain, Italy, Egypt, etc. \- Got to Studio at 9.30. Worked on "Mules, S.A." Mr. & Mrs. Posea, from San Francisco, Acquaintances that Matt has made there, called. \- Geo. painted in my Room \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.30 \- Worked on drawing as long as I could see, but did not get it finished. Home to tea at 7.20, Aunt Jessie at the house. \- Eve'g, looking over London papers. \- To bed about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### April Sunday, 23 1871.

Superb day \- cool, clear \+ fresh. Mr. [illegible] 9 \+ 10 o'clk. walked up to the Park, about there for awhile, then down to Dr. Hastings \- too late for the text \- but his subject was the necessity of loving God, not from fear of punishment nor from desire of Reward, but because he loved us. \- Home to dinner. About 3 o'clk, with Geo. went to St. Cloud Ho. from there, with Belle \+ Mary, drove to St. Thomas's Ch., Cor. 53d & 5th Ave. \- Heard Dr. Morgan \- a fairly heavy Sermon about a "Christian letting his light shine like the Sun." \- Music, as usually very good. After Service, walked up 5th ave. across the Park \+ Geo. \+ the girls Rode down in [illegible] Ave cars. I walked in park until 7 o'clk. then home to tea. \- Eve'g, napping in my big chair until 9.30. \- Then wrote letters to Dr. Lewis \+ Matt and got to bed at 12.45
