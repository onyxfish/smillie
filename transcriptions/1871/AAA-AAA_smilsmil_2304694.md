## Left Page

### June, Monday, 19, 1871.

another tremendously hot day. Up at 7.30, bkfst at 8. Sat about until 9.30 then took water colors + went to hill back of Brigham Young's + made color sketch looking over lake to N.W. Then tried to get one of the Mts to S.E. but the sun broiled so that I could not see. at 11 gave it up — went to Brigham Young's. Rec'd me very pleasantly + gave me his autograph in "The Book of Mormon". Back to dinner at 1. At 2.45 left Salt Lake city for Ogden, got in at 4.45 + full an hour before we got away on C.P.R.R. Took a lunch — Superb thunder storm effects over Salt Lake + distant Mts. The Mts on our right, with sunset effects very fine. Dark when we reached Promontory — wrote a short letter home and to bed about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday, 20, 1871.

Up at about 5 o'clk. Before bkfst. at Elko. 8.30, passed the Humboldt Mts under a fine effect of thunder storm. of which we had several at different times during the day. The scenery generally of a very dreary, + depressing character. Sometimes the mt forms fine but the everlasting sage brush \+ alkali ever brought the question "what is such a country good for?" Dinner at 1.30 at "Battle Mt." much of the day running along the Humboldt River — About 6 o'clk tea at Humboldt. Very clean + satisfactory. Chinese waiters. The last thing we could see at night was the Humboldt Lake or Sink — the body of water where the River is lost after running 300 or 350 miles — To bed after 10 o'clk.
