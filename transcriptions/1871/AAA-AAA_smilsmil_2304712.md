## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 25, 1871.

Up at 5.30 all complaining of a very cold night. The grouse was cooked again but did not get done in time for bkfst — All got off for "Cloud's Rest" about 8 o'clk said to be 7 miles away, hard climbing for the horses, 4000 feet, got to summit at 10.30. The view superb, the grandest thing of the kind that I have yet seen. Took a number of outline sketches in my little book. The rest of the party left before Gue \+ I did, We worked until 12.30, got to camp at 2.15 — Had dinner at 3 — grouse ruined by flies that got at it. At 4, Matt, Ridgeway \+ I. Rode to the Cascade at upper end of valley — Climbed on foot to 2d fall — very rough, no trail — got there at 6 o'clk — back to camp at 7.15 — tea at 8. Every sitting around \+ to bed at 9.30

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 26, 1871.

Up at 6 after a very comfortable night's rest. All hands busy until near 9 o'clk when we broke camp \+ started over the new trail from the Little Yo-Semite to Glacier Point being the first party of tourists over it. Portions yet unfinished made the walking sometimes ticklish. The scenery from this trail is the most savage \+ grand of any that I have yet seen. We named one view "rock, 'Aeolus Point'" — got to Glacier Point at 2 o'clk and lunched near water a few hundred yards from the point, at 3.30 started for Sentinel Dome, had a grand sight and then on to Peregoy's where we are — a little before 6 o'clk — went to the hill back \+ saw a wonderfully beautiful sunset over the distant mts — tea soon after 7. A funny incident; a Reverando, who followed us over the trail, criticised very freely the aptness of the title Aeolus Point not knowing that we were the guilty ones. a moonlight walk and to bed at 9.30 — a glorious day \+ night.
