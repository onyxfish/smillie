## Left Page

### December Monday, 4 1871.

Overcast, drizzling mf \+ much milder \- Clouds broke away \+ part of P.M. Sunny \- Blustering cold night with some sleet. Up late \+ feeling tired \- Did not get to studio until 10.30 Spent most of what Remained of the mf "touching" pf \- of Father's die of R.R. Depot. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.15 \+ worked as long as I could see on a little circle "Autumn", that I painted about a year ago \- DeWitt C. Hay made us a call about dusk \- Home to tea at 6 \- Surf at 8 Rode down to "Palette" club, 2d ave, \+ 3rd; Monthly meeting \- Home about \+ to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### December Tuesday, 5 1871.

Another immensely cold day \- as I had to write Committee notices it was 10.30 before I got to the Studio, then I set to work on "the Saco River \+ Mountains at N. Conway" 16×26 \- going over it all, harmonizing etc etc \- Alfred Jones came in at about 12.30 \- Home to lunch at 1.40 \- back at 2.30. meeting Belle Treedy \- Mrs. Forbes called \- going to England France S. Africa, India, China \+ goodness only knows where else \- worked until 4.15 \- Mrs. Verplanck called \- at 4.30 went to Perry's Studio \- back at 5.30 \- Home to tea at 6.30 \- Surf wrote a letter to Chicago Com \- got to bed about 10.45
