## Left Page

### October, Thursday 19, 1871

Overcast and quite cold, clearing during the day — got down-town at 9.10
& to work etching (4th day) — at noon
wrote a note to [illegible] Ferguson &
another to Townley accepting position
in Life School of Palette club — at 1, went
to lunch, then to C. B. N. Co. saw the pr[ess]
& Mr. Nacashmi, a Jap — got photograph
for Geo. to make drawings. Then back
to work — etched until near 5 o'cl
then home — Tea at 6.30 — At 7.30
walked to N.A. of D. meeting of Gen[eral]
Com. of N.Y. Artists Chicago Relief fund —
was appointed Secretary & worked
until 10 o'clk — then home & to
bed about 11 —

## Right Page

### October, Friday 20, 1871

Beautiful, breezy and crisp day —
at 8.30 started for down-town — Stopped
at Brooks Bros. & had to wait there
until 10.30. In the cars met W. Redfield
of Phila — Stopped at Windle's & saw
J. M. Falconer — It was 11.30 when I
got to A. B. N. Co. — Suffering all day
with a headache — Uncle George
came in to see Fred — Out to lunch
about two o'clk — Took notes to
Schenck & Johnston, auctioneers —
at 5.45 left office with drawing,
tools & tools, to work at Studio — Rode
with Young Thompson — Stopped at
Studio — Home a little before 9 —
Father & Mother at home — arr'd at
3. P. M. — Found a ticket from Mrs. Durand
for the Moulton concert — Steinway
Hall — went — very much entertained
— Home at 11 — To bed at 12 —
