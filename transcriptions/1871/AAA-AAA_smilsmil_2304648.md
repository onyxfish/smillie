## Left Page

### March Wednesday, 15 1871

Horrible weather again — mizzle until about 10 o'clk \+ then for two hours, heavy Rain — dry during the p.m. \+ Raw mizzle again at night — finished letter to Dr. Lewis \+ got to studio at 9.30 — worked on drawing (1½ day) until near 1 o'clk — Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.5 — Misses Ripsley (14) Tiffany (13) Mauing (8) Jones (8) and Whiting (2) took lesson — No model came, much to our disappointment — Worked until near 6 — washed up the dishes \+ home to tea at 6.30 — Telegram from Matt, at Rochester — at 7.30 at Rimmer's 13th lecture — Home at 9.30 — wrote a note to Bellows \+ got to bed about 10.45

## Right Page

### March Thursday, 16 1871

Still horrible weather — got to studio at 8.30 and worked for awhile on Miss Tiffany's drawing, finishing it \+ cutting it from the board — then to work on Insurance drawing (2½ days) — my anxiety to have it as good as I can do makes me work timidly on it — Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.15 \+ worked until after 5 — About 5.45 walked down to Wills, borrowed "dress coat" \+ home to tea at 7 — Out to W \+ thus A.C. Clarke's. No. 8 W. 22d St at 8.45. Music from 9.30 until 11 — large parlors crowded — very close and very warm — excused myself at 11.30 — home \+ to bed at 12 — Mrs. C. proves to be the adopted daughter of Dr. Percy — until I got into the parlor I did not know whose invitation I had accepted.
