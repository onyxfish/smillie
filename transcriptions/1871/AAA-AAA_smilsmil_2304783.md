## Left Page

### December Saturday, 16 1871

Charming day — got to studio at 8.45 & at work on 9×12, a sketch for Southier. Interrupted by various visits, among them Lewis B. Reed. Home to lunch at 2 o'clk — back at 2.45. Worked until 4.30. Rec'd a note from Dr. Lewis of Phila telling me that at last, my Water Color "How they brought the Good News from Ghent to Aix" is sold — for $250 — Paying com, his loan & interest, I get $17.60. Home to tea at 6. Welf wrote the Dr. a long letter & got to bed at 11.30 —

Learned to-day that aunt Sallie Pettit died last Thurs. p.m. This has been expected for some days.

## Right Page

### December Sunday, 17 1871

Dismal day — a slight fall of snow during the night —
Melf went to Dr. Hastings's & heard him preach from the last verse (1st) of the 27th Psalm. Dinner at 1. — Slept in my chair until 4.30 — Then walked up to the Park — home at 6 — tea. Welf wrote on mail letters to bed soon after midnight.
