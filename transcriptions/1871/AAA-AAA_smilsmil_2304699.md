## Left Page

### June, Thursday, 29, 1871.

Up soon after 5 — bkfst at 6 \+ off in the stage for Clark's at 7 \_ Very hot, but the ride very interesting. Timber improving constantly. Up hill 10 miles — then down 4 — arriving at Clark's at 11 o'clk. Dinner at 1 \+ lounging the rest of the day as it was very hot. Wrote quite a long letter home before dinner. Sea at 7 — Glorious night. To bed about 10 o'clk.
Very much interested, Felkins with [illegible], a hunter, who was by accident shot through his body a week ago to-day. The bullet went in above \+ to the left of the umbilicus \+ out at the back, and yet he is about declaring himself nearly sound.

## Right Page

### June, Friday, 30, 1871.

Up at 5.30. Glorious mtf. Delayed by bkfst not until 6.45 — an hour later our party got off for the big trees, each on a disjointed looking horse, that with ropes around their necks, for halters, seemed more like candidates for the gallows than the companions of pleasure seekers. In two hours we reached the trees — No one can appreciate them at once — it is hard to get a realizing sense of their bulk. The "Grizzley Giant" I think the grandest \+ the Forest Queen the most beautiful. Lunch before 12. Set to work on Water Color sketch — thunder storm threatening. The gloom \+ thunder very fine — At 2, mounted again, made tour of the grove, came out on precipitous point — view — then on down, getting to Clark's at 5 o'clk — worked for an hour or more on sketch. Tea near 7 and to bed about 9 o'clk.
Up \+ out, about 2 o'clk A.M. glorious moonlight, but that was not what I went to see.
