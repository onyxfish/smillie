## Left Page

### October Thursday, 5 1871.

A mild, semi-hazy day — got to studio's at 9 o'clk — feeling very lazy but did my best to work, biting the ovals that I had ruled yesterday.
Home to lunch at 1.45 suffering from pains in the stomach much like what I had last Sunday — back at 3.15 and spent what remained of the P.M. working over some of my sketches in water color.
At 5.30, went to Geo. C. Allen's with Uncle Wm's chain — then to Brooks Bros \+ ordered a suit of clothes — Home to tea at 6.30 evef. Read one of C. King's High Sierra articles, ("Atlantic") and wrote to Mr. Albert Post \+ Uncle Wm. Turrill —
To bed about 10.

## Right Page

### October Friday, 6 1871.

Heavy, steady rain during most of the m'f. — Got to studio's at 9.30 \+ worked on A.B.N. Co. die — laying a ground \+ getting a tracing on gelatine — Still feel very much under the weather, suffering constant pain in the stomach — Home to lunch at — back at 2.30 and spent rest of p.m. finishing up some water color sketches in my pocket sketching book — Upon the lounge for a while, then home to tea at 6.30 — Steady rain — evef. looking over my photographs \+ reading Bowles' Sumone the Rocky Mts — To bed about 10.30
