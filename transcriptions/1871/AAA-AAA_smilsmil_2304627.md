## Left Page

### January, Monday 30, 1871.

mildly sunny day — Everything afloat — Got to Studiés at 9 o'clk. Kindled fire and then got very much interested charcoaling a Sketch, 16×26 in "Ichabod Crane + the headless horseman" — Spent the day on it — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30, after stopping at 6th National Bank + closing Water Color Society acct. — P.M. Mrs. Watsons made me a long call — worked until it was very dark — 5.20. Home at 6.20. Stunning notice in Evest Mail — of W.C. drawing. Writing dunning notices to members of Water Color Socy until about 11 — To bed soon after.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 31, 1871.

Miserable, dark, mild day, Rain beginning about noon + falling steadily the rest of day + night. — MYf, went to Station "9" 149th St. + 7th ave. — took care + Rode down to Nehlig's, Returning him his book, called on Earnest Parton. then to Knoedler's. bought drawing paper + got to studiés at 11 — found a note from Wm Hart — wants to see me — at noon went to his rooms — He wishes to write notes to leading art men to interest them in Water Colors. then to N.A. of D — then to Schmitt's — then home to lunch at 1.20 — back to studiés at 2.30 — Stretched paper for Misses Ripley and Tiffany — did little else — so very dark — Home to tea at 6.10 — back to studiés at 7.10 — there to meet Wm Hart and Burling — Hard at work writing until 10.30 — Home at 11 and to bed at 11.20
