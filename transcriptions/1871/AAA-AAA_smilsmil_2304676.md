## Left Page

### May Sunday, 14 1871.

Another charming day — Did not get up until 9 o'clk \+ spent the rest of the a.m. in devotion to soap \+ water — Dinner at 1.30 — at 3.30 P.M. with Ge. went to the St. Cloud \+ then with Belle \+ Mary J. went to St. Thomas's Ch. after service, walked up to 5th Ave \+ 72d St through the Park to 7th ave — \+ down home at 7 o'clk — Spent writing to Matt \+ Mr. Enoch Lewis — to bed about 11. 30

## Right Page

### May Monday, 15 1871.

Beautiful day — got to studio at 9.15 — Finished in a little time the etching on Engine die and prepared to bite (3½ days) when Mr. Hart sent for me to go over to Sileck's \+ see the broken condition of frames just in from Phila. Back in a short time \+ worked at biting until 1.30 — Home to lunch — wrote an appeal to Members of Water Color Society to make greater efforts. Back to studio at 3.10 — P. Yale called — at 5 Mr. Hart called — got my etching bitten in \+ die cleared off at 6.30 — Home to tea at 7.15 — at 8.30 Rode down to 16 St. Luke's Place with Will's dress coat — not in, back home at 9.35 — Wrote letters to Dr. Lewis \+ Maud Diveen \+ got to bed shortly after 11 —
