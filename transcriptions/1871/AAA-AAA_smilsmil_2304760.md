## Left Page

### Sept. Friday, 15 1871.

Did not wake until 7 — Raining with promise of same for all day — Before we were dressed Chas. Reyden's card sent in — here with his sister \+ Miss Walker — Bkfst. at 8 — then with R. \+ party for a while — then out with R. \+ "did" Goat Island, getting pretty wet, — back to Hotel at 11 — wrote letter to Uncle A. — At 2.5 gel \+ I went by R.R. to Suspension Bridge \+ met Miss Benedicts \+ the Treedy Girls, just in from Chicago. Ret'd to Cataract Ho. at 2.30 — dinner at 3.30 — At 5 o'clk all (7) started in two carriages — Crossed Susp. Br — drove up to Table Rock — back — Goat Island Sun. to, Terrapin Tower — The Three Sisters \+ back at 6.30 — pleased, in spite of the weather — not inharmonious with the spirit of the fall — Tea at 7.30 — went walking on piazza over the Rapids — Annie Cook, Mother \+ bro. at Hotel — To bed soon after 11 —

## Right Page

### Sept. Saturday, 16 1871.

Wakened from a sound sleep \+ routed out of bed before 5 o'clk — Wf cold \+ overcast — Bolted breakfast and off in b. 15 train, Erie R.R. — Ran along the Niagara River quite a ways — Buffalo at 7 — Changed cars — crowded, close \+ dirty — driven into a Drawing Room car. The country flat until between 8\+9 when we ran through valleys, sometimes narrow, at times broad, varying all the way to Binghampton, where we arr'd at 3.17 — had just time to transfer trunks \+ then off on Del. Lack. \+ West'n. — got to New Milford at 2.30 — then stage to Montrose — Clouds broke away and it was very fresh \+ clear — Got to Uncle Mr at 4.30 — To our great disap pointment, found that Mary had been gone a week — Having had no dinner, enjoyed even the meal \+ got to bed at 9.30 very tired.
