## Left Page

### April, Saturday, 8, 1871.

Hazy, breezy, but very warm. Got to Studio at 9.10 \+ until 12 o'clk engaged stretching paper \+ Tracing "Llanna" drawing. Then at 12 started out, met Fanny K, took stage, Rode to the Battery, there met Cooks, Irelands etc \- 1 o'clk Staten Island ferry boat — to Laven Landing then R.R. to Huguenot which place we reached soon after 2 o'clk \- walked about a mile to some woods by a little stream — lunched — then picking the Mayflowers (Trailing Arbutus) \+ otherwise enjoying ourselves until 6.10 Return train — delightful twilight sail up the bay \- N.Y. about 7.30 \- all got into a 4th Ave bus (11 of us \- 12 in the party, but one went to Brooklyn) \- dropping out by 2 \+ 3 \+ until I left Emma J, \+ Mr. S. at their door \- Home about 9 o'clk \- Tea and to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday, 9, 1871.

a Singularly Hot day. Ther 85° in the shade — everybody gasping. Myf went to hear Dr. Hastings who preached from "Col \- 3° Ch \- 1st v°". afternoon loafing \+ trying to keep cool until 4 o'clk. \- Rode up to Central Park \- about there until 7 o'clk \- then Rode home. Evef wrote a letter to Matt \+ got to bed at 2.
