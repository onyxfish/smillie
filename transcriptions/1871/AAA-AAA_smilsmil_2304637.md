## Left Page

### February Tuesday, 21 1871

Dull morning — an inch or two of snow — Got to Studio at 9.30 somewhat demoralized by last night's dissipation and more by the Reported Results of last night's Sale of the first annual Artist's Sale — Did'nt work very hard, W.f or b.M. on Niagara (2½ days) at 12.30 went to Knoedler's for W. C. brushes etc. — Home to lunch at 1.45 — Aunt Jessie \+ Emma spending the day at our house — back at 2.45 — worked until 5.50 — then to Snedecor a number of other messages — and home at 7.45 — Tea \+ then the rest of well fixing drawing boards for my pupils, doing some writing etc — and to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### February Wednesday, 22 1871

Bright cold day — Got to Studio at 9.30 — got a message from Dodworth that if I wish to Retain my Studio I must wait upon him personally — got very "mad" and if my work for an hour or more — Worked for an hour or more on little drawing of Union Dime Savings Bk for A.B.N.Co — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.10 — Misses Ripley \+ Tiffany there before me (8th lesson) — Misses Maury \+ Jones came after (2d lesson) — Mr. Tiffany came for Annie — All left soon after 5 o'clk — Home to Tea at 6.45 — at Nel. \+ D. Rimmer's lecture at 7.30 — after lecture — m'te Brevoort \+ Calverley met to Schmullig — had some Lager — Home at 9.45 — mote a note to Uncle Willie and to bed at 10.45 —
