## Left Page

### March, Thursday 23, 1871

a sick sort of day with faint attempts at sunshine, got to studio at 9 o'clk feeling very tired. Etched over lower face of shield "Colorado Arms" (8½ days) \- bit \+ Resist \- at 1.45 Rode down to Emil Seitz's \+ made arrangements with him for a lot of fine, old English mounting board. Home to lunch at 1 \- back to studio at 2 \- Geo. Wood of E. Town surprised me with a call. at 5 o'clk went to Y.M.C.A. \- meeting of art committee of which I am Secy. Back to studio at 6.20 after a pleasant chat with Elbert Munroe. Home to tea at 7 o'clk \- Well still very tired but made paste \+ mounted drawing for A.B.N.Co. \- Telegram from A.S.S. at San F. \- all well \- also a letter from Matt at "Shermans." Got to bed about 10 o'clk \- Father worked all the evening on die of "Niagara" \-

## Right Page

### March, Friday 24, 1871

Superb day \- Got to studio at 8.30 worked awhile on Miss Ripley's drawing. Before 10 my "Lambs" all came along \- Miss R. (17) S. (16) J. (11) M (11) \+ N (5). Misses R. J. \+ N. drew from the life \- in crayon. Abt. 15, home to lunch at 2.30 \- at Phil Rehearsal which I enjoyed exceedingly \- Back to studio at 5 \+ worked until 6 on Miss R's studies which I finished with many odds \+ ends, engaged at studio until after 7 \- Home to tea at 7.30 \- Eveng. worked awhile on drawing Arms Banco de Trujillo" (½) \- Wrote letter to Uncle Willie \+ packed die of "Niagara" to go to Ottawa by Exps. to-morrow \- got to bed at 12 o'clk \-
Rec'd. notice to serve on jury next Monday \-
