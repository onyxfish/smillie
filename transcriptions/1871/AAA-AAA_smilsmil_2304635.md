## Left Page

### February Friday, 17 1871.

another beautiful day — got to studio at 9 o'clk — fixed up the Room — about 10 misses Ripley & Tiffany came (7th lesson) and about 11, misses Maury & Jones (1st lesson) — Very busy with them all the M[orning] until after 1 — Home to lunch at 1.40, at 2.30 at Phil. Rehearsal — Didn't find much to enjoy but the 2d movement of Schumann's Symphony (Op. 97) After Reh. with the J's, Matt, Kate & [illegible] went to Somerville Art Gallery — then to N.A. of D. Water Color Exh. at 5.30 — back to Studio, where I cleaned up & fixed fire — Home to tea at 6.45 — Every Reading — Will & Minn to tea — Weather changed — again Raw & mizzling — To bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### February Saturday, 18 1871.

A shocking day of wind & Rain. Got to Studio at 9.30 — Very dark — at times could not see to paint, but worked on the two 8 in. circles that I am going to send to Schenck's sale (3d day for the two) Home to lunch at 1.40 — back at 2.15 — and worked until 3 o'clk — then went into Brevoort's (Perspective) until 4.15 — the worked a little more, finishing the circles after a fashion. Post Dodge called — then Matthews the auctioneer. at the Studio until 7 pottering about, cleaning etc — then to Schmitt's & ordered drawing boards — home at 7.45 — tea — Every Reading papers — Father suffering severely with neuralgia — Got to bed soon after 11 — brilliant night.
