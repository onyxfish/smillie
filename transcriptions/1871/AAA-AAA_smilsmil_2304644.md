## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 7, 1871

Sulerb day — got to the studio at a little before 9 o'clk — Spent the m'f on "Niagara" (3½ days) bothering mth Rebiting \+ grounds — at 12.30 went to N.A. of D. \+ got Catlin's N.A. Indians for costumes — then to Silleck's then to Studio, Miss Whiting \+ mother there — am to add Miss W. to my list of pupils — Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2 — Resat on die — Mr. B. Skaats came in \+ insisted that I shall give him lessons in Water Colors — from 5 to 6 o'clk drew from a little English girl (don't know her name) an enigma to me, pretty, modest \+ seemingly virtuous — Home to tea at 7 — wife marking clothes for Matt \+ writing Johns — Aiken and Reed — all picture exhibition men — To bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 8, 1871

m'f started bright, but mizzling rain 9 o'clk — setting in Regularly about 4 P.M. — Got to studio at 8.15 — Skaats came about 8.45 \+ stayed until 9.30 — persisted that I could not take him as a pupil \+ he gave it up. Worked all the m'f on "Niagara" (4 days) mth graver \+ burnisher — Home to lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.20 — Miss Ripley there (12th lesson) \+ Misses Maury \+ Jones (6th lesson) but no Misses Tiffany or Whiting. Mr. Franklin called — I had chance to do a very little on die — Very dark P.M. — Pupils gave up before 5 o'clk. Home to tea at 7 — at Rummer lecture at 7.30 — a\[illegible\] went to Y.M.C.A. gymnasium — got key for my closet \+ wood promised to try to find me a male model — Home at 10 o'clk — still miserable mizzle — To bed at 11.15
