## Left Page

### August, Saturday 10, 1878.

A continuance of the same weather —
heavy clouds threatening rain &
a scalding hot sun — B'kfp — about
8 — After that M. & Mrs. C — Miss H. &
Mr. Coles, the J's, Miss Cushing &
Geo & I went over to the Giant's leap
Spent much of the m'f there &
upon Chappel Brook — Back shortly
before dinner at 1 o'clk — After that
with Misses Hillard, Cushing, Nellie
J. & Mr. Coles Took a rough & tumble
through the woods to the top of the
Rocks overhanging the Beede Farm —
back about 4 o'clk — barely in time
to avoid a ducking from a heavy
shower — then took a nap & was
up in time for tea at 6 o'clk — out
of sorts from bad food & in much
pain, Stomach & bowels — After tea
went over to Honeymoon Cottage
with Miss Hillard & Mr. Coles &
called upon the Bride & groom,
Mr. & Mrs. James — back before 9 —
Some dancing in the Parlor — a
little promenading at Piazza &
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 11, 1878.

Delicious m'f — but weather
evidently still very fickle — B'kfp —
at 9 — After 10, with Mr. Colman
went to Riverside & spent the
m'f on a big rock, chatting &
writing — She & I wrote a note to
Belle & Mary Sweesy urging them
to join us here. Dinner at 2 —
then much loafing until
near 5 when Mr. Coles, Colman
Misses Hillard, Cushing, the
Jacobs's & Colman, S Farrow
Geo & I, started out for a walk
we got to Chappel Pond Brook —
met Mr. Mallony — M. & Mrs. C —
Miss Cushing & Geo did not cross
the brook — the rest of us went
on to Sunset Ledge where we met
the James's & Warners — a fine
climb — back to tea at 7 — very
alto, cloudy had some fine
moonlight effects — 11 o'clk.
before I got to bed.
