## Left Page

### July, Friday 5, 1878

Charmingly breezy & comparatively cool m'f. Much of it
was passed on piazza of W. Richards
where we met Rev. Mr. Courtney, W.
& Mrs. Ethan Allen — Mrs — Richards
& Miss R. — Dinner at 1. W. Howard
Williams, the fossil of E-Town memory
being dug up for our entertainment.
About 2.40 we started for Peekskill —
Nicoll having a headache, did not go.
Drove part way over old P— Hollow
Road & had some charming views.
Attempted to get to Beecher's house
by the old Kittredge road but was
stopped by Beecher's new house —
Sent wagon down the hill & walked
across the lawn to Beach's — Mrs. B.
at home (4.15) but going to N.Y. by 5.7
train — insisted upon our riding to
depot with him — found wagon at gate
foot of hill — sent that back to Nicoll —
At depot we were joined by Beecher
who went to N.Y. with Mr. B — Had
a little chat with him about his
projected trip to California — We
took 5.18 train & got to [illegible] at 6.25 —
Right home to tea — Crake or flood
don — his wife died yesterday m'f
of hemorrhage — To bed soon after
10.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 6, 1878

Very warm — Had a call in
the m'f from Wallace Bruce.
Got to work again on Anthony
block — Am discouraged — have
got it too black — Dinner bef.
1 — again at block & stuck
to it all the afternoon without
improving it. Mr. & Lord's
funeral at 4 o'clk. Father
one of the bearers. Tea
at 6 — Strong, cool breeze
blowing in the ev'g. Much
of the time walking in the
parlors. Then a walk
out of doors — To bed at 10.
