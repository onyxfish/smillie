## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 19, 1878

Fresh, charming day — got
to studios about 9.30 and
spent most of the mng
arranging papers, etc
for my framing at Po —
then writing on Society
matters (W.C. of course) —
abt. went ment to Knudlers
with Geo — back at 2 had
lunch. Mrs & Miss Sackett
called — more mating —
at 6 called for a few minutes
on Walter Brown — then to
thek's, to tea — there until
8 — then to the Klutz Gallery
& a little before 9 to Studio
wrote on my annual Report
until near 9.30 — then
to 41 ½ S— and to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 20, 1878

called on Jasper Cropsey — 4 W. 27 S— at 10 a.m.
Pleasantly breezy day. Got
to studio soon after 9 and after
a little five, settled down
to work on my annual report
to W.C. Socy — at it until lunch
at 1.30 — Soon after 4 finished
work on it, feeling nearly tired, I
spread myself on lounge for a few
minutes, then walked up to 41 S—
and on my way back, at 5.45, at
549 5th Ave. (Cor. 34 S—) & called on Ja.
Symington (F.H. Smith's partner) and
telling him that it was impossible
for Smith to be elected Treas., advised
him to induce S. to Resign better surmely
under five than submit to a humiliating
defeat — He was glad I had called
but did it think Smith would be
defeated. At 6.15 at Germans to
dinner — at 7.30, with Geo, we walked
down to M.C. Rooms. The Smith party
all there — and I felt blue & nervous.
Soon after 8 a quorum present and I
called the meeting to order. Jasper
first showed itself on the approval of
the minutes — then a disgraceful &
ill-mannered interruption from Wm
Magrath while I was reading my
report — then a few spiteful words
from Silva, as member of nominating com-
then Smith surprised us all by declining
to run for Treas. — in favor of Robbins.
Peace reigned for the time & the election
passed off very quietly. Wood, elected
Pres. unanimously — Robbins Treas. and
