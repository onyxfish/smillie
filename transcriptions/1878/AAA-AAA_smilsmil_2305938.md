## Left Page

### January, Sunday 6, 1878

Clear windy & cold — B[illegible] —
about 9. 30 — At 10.15 went
up to Yale's & saw him — He
orders a blue pill — Then
to D? Hastings — This year he
discontinues the practice of a
motto kept for the year but
preached from Romans 8th ch
24th v — "By Hope are ye saved"
After services, a short walk.
Dinner at 1.30 — Near 3 o'clk
when I got to Studios — very
cold there — Wrote out part
of Ferguson's letter from Cairo
for the Post — At 6 went to Nellie
Jacobs' — Tea about 7 — Spent
the eve'g there until 8.45
Then to studios — wrote a [illegible] in
home — To 41st St & to bed
about 11. Took a blue
pill.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 7, 1878

Fine day — 9.30 when I
got to Studios — To work on
figure in Orchard — Started
with color, felt very awkward
as I have not touched color for
a year. Was unsettled all
day owing to my p[illegible] last night
& catarrh of [illegible] a this m'g.
Lunch at 2 — At 4.30 Board
of Control meeting, W.C.S. — in
my studios which continued
in session until after 6 — It
was 7 before I had my tea — at
8 went to Council meeting of
N.A. of D — There until 10 — Then
to Century Club with J.W.
Wood & Geo. Hall — The old
boys had a Christmas tree
& a great romp, celebrating
"melfth Night" — The whole
thing was very grotesque &
humerous. I stayed
until midnight — then
trotted up to 41st St and
into bed shortly before 1.
