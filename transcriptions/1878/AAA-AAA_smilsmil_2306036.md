## Left Page

### July, Thursday 25, 1878

Pleasant day — felt tired
after the pull of yesterday —
made letters until after 11 —
then fixed myself to start
Harper page view from top of
Bek Mt — but could do nothing
— perhaps it was laziness —
I was not uncomfortable, but
think I was tired — Dinner
at 1.30 — The afternoon got
away from me as the my
did & I had no progress
to show — Tea about 7 — a
threatening evening — in
the parlor & on the piazza
as usual — Cobbs sang for
us & there was a little
dancing — I obed down
after 10.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 26, 1878

Disagreeable m'f, which grew
into a stormy day which at
night was a perfect howler.
Tried to get Readey interested
in my Bek. Mt. top view but with
little success — got a general
effect. Rubbed in with pencil
but could not get the group
of figures to suit me. Dinner
bet. 1 & 2 — Rain in the afternoon
prevented me from getting exercise — Evef. Mr. Butler ret'd
from Bek. Mt — where in spite of the
storm, he held his meeting, bringing
with him Mr. Colvin — said he
had had a first rate time — found
Colvin very interesting — Every
a dance, or hop, in the parlors —
four doleful musicians with
fiddles & horns could not drown
the roar of the storm outside —
got to bed before midnight —
thought the Roof would be
