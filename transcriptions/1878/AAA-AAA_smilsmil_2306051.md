### August, Monday 26, 1878

*In Camp. Upper Ausable —*
*Bishop's Camp.*

Brilliant, cloudless + fresh mtf.
Up at 6.30 + began to arrange for a
days excursion into the Ausable
Ponds — Blkp — at 7 + at 8 we got away
Mr + mrs. Colman, Eastman Johnson
Nellie + marie Jacobs, Bella Tweedy,
Miss Coskine, Geo + S — with Brown
+ Montay as guides — got to lower
lake at 10.30 + without delay to the
camp — just as we were making the
landing across a very swift current
Montay broke his oar — Brown with
his heavy boat could not get up the
stream between the two troubles we
were delayed about 3/4 of an hour —
at the landing met Miss Van Sturwood
+ party — over the camp at 1.30, more
delay from high water, at Upper
Pond met Monroe Holt + a party —
on the shore of the moment we decided
to stay over night + Holt lent us an ape
2e Rubber + 2 woolen blankets + some
tin dishes — we gave him a note of
what we wanted from Beede's +
he promised to return at once + send
some one in his place with blankets
+ provisions — Montay left us also to
return + we went on to camp — after
all sorts of delays we finally got our
dinner a little before 4 — much enjoyed —
The rest of p.m. + early evntg passed in
boats + fixing up camp — at 8 o'clk.
Brown returned from camp — no word of Holt
or any one else for us — we got up a
supper of scrambled eggs + Toast which
was eaten by the camp — fire — between
9 + 10 we had a brush fire — Johnston
very jolly — all hands as well — at 11 we

### August, Tuesday 27, 1878

lot of us — Brown at our feet — I was pinned
between Johnston + Geo — Brown snored
so did Johnston — so I kicked one and
nudged the other at short intervals
all night — The weather was cool + our
blankets thin but Brown kept such a
roaring camp fire going that we really
suffered from heat — with a yellow Birch
log for a pillow there was little sleep —
Up early — Lovely effect over the lake —
dreamy, perfect quiet — washed my face
in the lake — dried it with my pockethandkerchief
— combed my hair with my fingers, then
took a little pull in the boat — Blkp —
about 7.30 — at 8 in grand Haste came
ring some provisions — a very scant mess
and most satisfactory + pleasant concerning
the failure of any one to get through to us
last night — After Blkp Mr. Van Sturwood
came + made us a call — then we all
went out + rowed up the River about
3 miles — Back to camp about 11 — Dinner
at 12 + at 1 got away on home stretch —
glorious effects that made us all very
unwilling to leave — After getting to the
lower end of the lower lake, Mr. Inuse
+ Geo went on while the rest of us went
to foot of Rainbow fall — Johnston very
much pleased — About 4 o'clk we got
upon our way making slow progress
owing to mud + fatigue on the part of some
of the ladies — Return to Beede's at 6.15
— so ended our two days of camping
without a towel, or bit of soap — a hair
brush or a tooth brush in the party, with
a short supply of blankets + three meals
served from Rations for one meal — I
had reason to be glad that I was such
a liberal provider — Had a good rub
down + toweling with entire change
then I felt like a brick — Tea was
served to all very late — not before
7.30 — Evtng on piazza until 10 o'clk —
there to bed —
