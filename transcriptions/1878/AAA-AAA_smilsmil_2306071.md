## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 9, 1878

Heavy & wetting scotch mist with driving S.W. wind — Blcb about 8 —
Before the table was cleared H.W.B.
came over & invited the entire family
over to his house — to come right
away, bring our knitting & stay to
dinner — He sat & chatted with us
for nearly an hour — very free in
his criticisms & comments — Tom S.
getting his share — About 10 we all
went over to his house — Weather
improved so much that I went
if I had a walk with Emma &
Miss B— Back to house soon after
11 & dinner soon after 12 — H.W.B.
again in very good spirits & after
dinner we sat at table a long
time, he drawn out by my questions,
giving an account of his English
experiences, where he went then
during our war, & telling characteristic stories — After dinner Emma,
Miss B— Mrs. Shearman, Geo. &c. went
to the brook & through Carhart's woods
for a walk — Sky overcast & after
the walk I felt tired & lay
down for a rest — Tea at 6 — After
that, H.W.B — Col. B. & Tom S. came over
to the house — There had been quite
a shower — Thunder & lightening in
the distance — H.W.B. & Tom immediately
planted themselves & continued a
discussion that they were having —
Col. B. & I sat on piazza — then with
the ladies, as it had cleared beautifully
we went over to B's house — then
Emma, Miss B— Mrs. S— Mrs. Col. B —
Geo. & I went on a moonlight walk
down the hill past [illegible] — dog
back about 10 o'clk — bid the Beechers
good-bye — went to the Beach house
& to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 10, 1878

Up soon after 6 — charming fresh
mng. Blcb — at 7 & at 9.45 — Emma
Miss Bullard, Geo. & I. started for
depot — I took 12 train to N.Y. city
got there at 9.25 & went at once to
the H. give 21 W. 37th S — to see about
rooms for the winter — then to the
barbers — then to Hunter's & got a
stronger pair of eyeglasses — then
to studio — saw Calverley — then to
Coleman's — no one in — then by
3d ave. elevated R.R. down Town —
to Sharpe's & bought some copper plates
then to Equitable Bldg. & saw Philip
Wilson about my Continental Life
policy — then to Abn. Co. — to see Miller
then had lunch & at 2.15 at Harper's
saw Parsons — there until after
4, going over my lake George sketches
with him & Alden — got enthusiastic
over the specimens of engraving
shown to me & promises made —
got $35 — for expenses at lake George —
then by elevated 3d ave. R.R. — to 23d
St. met Mrs. Ferguson on the car — just
quite from Europe — called on Mrs.
Preston Spring — Emma — out — then
again to studio — saw Mrs. Calverley —
then to Coleman's — saw Corinne &
her baby — also Charley — Mr. & Mrs.
Howard there to dinner — we sat
at the table until 8 o'clk — then
I bid Corinne & Charley good-bye
& walked to R.R. depot — took
8.30 train — got to P. at 11 — there
home & to bid just about 12 —
glorious moonlight night —
