## Left Page

### October, Monday 7, 1878

Wonderfully beautiful mrf. B[illegible] soon after 7 — as
Cha. B, wife Rit? to Mf, by 8 —
Train — all the mrf. out of
doors — Rambling over the
hills to the north — Back
to dinner about 1.30 — as
soon as that was finished
all off for a ride — Munia
& Ge together — Mr. Beechum
Miss Bullard & I (with driving
after H.W.B's fast horses — Rode
to a bit of wilderness south
& climbed a high rock —
Quite overcast — Back
to tea at 6 — Very quiet
every. Talking of house
building — the Ladies
to bed early — I wrote
to father — Rain falling
at 9 — I got to bed before
10.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 8, 1878

Cloudy mrf. but pleasant —
breeze stirring — B[illegible] about 8 —
Soon after 9 — Munia — Miss B — Ge.
I got away on a "Random Scout"
going first through Carharts woods
then through a Cedar lane, then
across by Peekskee Reservoir and
back through Mr. Beechy's woods — at
about 1 o'clk — the day then perfectly
glorious — had got two sketches and
found quantities of fringed gentians
& other wild flowers — Dinner at
1.30 — before dessert Mr. Will Beecher
came in for a few minutes — after
dinner I sat about & read for
awhile — then my head feeling heavy
I lay down upon my bed — about 3
H.W. Beecher came over and I
[illegible] & had a chat with him on
the piazza — at 6 we all went over
to his house — there met his son Col.
Beecher — Tom Shearman & his wife —
our tea was a very informal and
pleasant affair — Tom S — led off by
recalling some amusing incidents,
giving imitations of the prayers of Dr.
Isaac Tayer & Deacon Baron Ranch —
H.W.B — was in very good humor and
much amused — in the garden the
conversation was rather a theological
discussion between H.W.B. & Tom for some
time — then it branched off to military
matters which waked up the Col — me
took a look through part of the house
bid them good night about 10 —
Then as the night was a glorious
one I took a little moonlight
walk by myself & got to bed a
little before 11.
