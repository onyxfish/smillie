## Left Page

### March, Monday 11, 1878

Dull misty, later in the day — mizzle & at night, rain —
feeling very uncomfortable
after yesterday's headache. I
did not get to studio until
10 & then soon went to N.C.
Then to Y.M.C.A. & called
on Gifford & Abbey & saw D. Yale
most of the way going away
from me — to studio to lunch.
The Tuesdays mrs Coleman
Did nothing much until
made us a cake,
near 5 — then down to Reinhart's
Saw him & then to 51 W. 10th St.
There saw Shelton — then to
Studio — Tea at 6.30. Etching
Club should have met in the
evef, but I forgot to send out
notices — Gifford Shirlaw Abbey
& Reinhart came & made a
very enjoyable evef in art
chat. I went to N.A. Cormac
at 8.45 — back about 10 — & found
the party still enjoying themselves
seeing — It was very near
midnight before I got to 41 S.
then right to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 12, 1878

Horrible stormy day — wind
& rain. Got to Studio at 9.30
& to work on Lake Placid — run
over nearly the whole picture
modifying & bringing things
together — think I improved
it altho' the whole thing is
very much overworked. —
Landis at 2 & family came
in & gave a special invitation
for this evef. Nicoll came
in & quite upset me with a
letter from F.H. Smith — It was
nearly before I got to K's later
in the evef, quite a number
of invited guests arrived to
hear a Miss Sargent of Boston,
sing. She is about 18 yrs old &
has a strong, masculine tenor voice
— it was a very interesting evef if
nothing more — Mr & Mrs. Coleman
& Minna were there. At 11.30 walked
home in a thunderstorm with annie
cook — to 41 S. & to bed shortly
after 12.
