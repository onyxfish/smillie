## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 6, 1878

Snow flurry in the mng. followed by snow most of the day — about two inches fell — I found myself out of sorts, & did not go out. All day worked over etchings — Rebit portions of Peekskill Haystacks & strengthened some parts of Pollard Willows. Dinner at 12.30 — Got press ready & took pfs. of my larger etching "Jefferson S". & also of the small ones — worked until tea time — 6 o'clk after that, fixed my blind & did some burnishing on Haystacks & took more proofs — About 9 o'clk. I gave that up & went to P.O. with letters — Sat by fire on my return & did not go to bed until near 11.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 7, 1878

Cold, windy day with tendency to snow squalls — don't feel quite so sore to-day in middle section. Took a short walk after bkfst — then after a little time over etchings got at Bruce's work. — Started ill for 3°. Stanza. — Dinner, after a short walk — Charming weather, worked at drawing until 4.30, then went to Parker Mills & gave a check of father's to Ed. P. — then to Armis's home to tea ab. 6 — after that wrote a note to D. Yale & a card to Geo. & a little after got to Prof. Ritters — met Mrs. R. for the first time — had a very pleasant chatty time until near 10. Mrs. R. proving herself a very clever, versatile person — I wish she were less of a painter, went to P.O. with my letters then home & to bed at 10.30.
