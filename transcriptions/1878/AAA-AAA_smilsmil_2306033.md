## Left Page

### July, Friday 19, 1878

Splendid day — Routed out of bed in the mtts by a call at 5 o'clk. Attended to & slept until near 7 when I was again called to go up Black Mr. Bdp— about 7.30 & an hour later got away on the meteor. Had a charming sail up the lake, stopping for a time at Mr. B's cottages building north of Hewlitt's, then to Hewlitt's landing — there for a while & then to Blk. Mt. Point. Reached at 11.30. After a little delay a very good lunch was served on board, Mr. Mr. Butler joining the party — at 12.50 Mr. Cyrus B— Mr. Colt & myself started for the top of the mt — 2300 ft high & 4 hard miles of climbing. We were soon followed by Cap. Tubbs who has charge of the road now being built to the top — During the first mile I thought I would have to give up — the heat was so intense & we could reach of the breeze — we came to some water & I laved head & hands & drank, lemon juice & squeezed into the water, only once again we chased water on our way. There was very little shade, the mt. having been burned over — the grades were very steep & oney now then did we have a breeze — the air moving from the north while we climbed the south side of the mt. Several fine views to S.W. on the lake from ledges — parts of the road reminding me of Sierra trails. Reached the summit at 3.20 — 2½ hours — Glorious breeze — very fine view of Adirondacks, Greene mts — all of lake Geo. & bits of Champlain — two sketches — to N & S — Started down at 4.40, got to boat at 5.50 — Stopping at Hewlitts on way back, saw for a moment Delyore & Ginsch — got to Rogers Rock soon after 8 — about 9 when we had tea — a Regular dance in the evening which the young folks kept up until after midnight — I went to my room & stayed there going to bed soon after 10 — pretty tired — that & the dance music kept me awake.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 20, 1878

Another wet day — feeling tired — spent the morning in my room writing & fixing up sketches. Dinner between 1 & 2 o'clk. Changed room to No. 49 — double bedded — Ready at 3 o'clk. to go up the lake with party, but the meteor did not come along until 5. then a party of us went on her to Black Mt. to get the men there & at Weston Point, who return to their homes every Sat. night. Delightfully cool on the water — charming every effects. After dark it was delicious — the shadows from the mts were so solemn & the starlight rippled in the lake like ribbons of silver — we got to Rogers Rock dock at 10 — at the house had a little to eat — then Cobb gave us some — [illegible] of Blue Beard — some comic songs in the parlor — at 1.30 went to bed.
