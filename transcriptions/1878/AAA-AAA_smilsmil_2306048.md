## Left Page

### August, Sunday 18, 1878

Heavily overcast with thunder-
ous looking clouds — near
9 when I had b[reakfast] — the
trip up Dial — deferred on
acct. of the weather — I read
& wrote during the morning —
a slight shower soon after 12 —
Dinner at 2 — about 4 P.M. —
Misses Hillard & Cushing, Mr.
Coles — Storrow & myself started
for Washburn's flume — but a
heavy shower caught us &
we took refuge in the Washburn
house by the old Patten place.
Ret. to B's about 7 & had
tea — Eve. on piazza. Service
in parlor from 9 until near
10 — to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 19, 1878

Overcast & threatening. Miss [illegible]
Cushing & Storrow left at 9 a.m. —
The Colemans, Bakers & Williams
made a day of pic nic to top
of Roaring Brook falls — I went
across my lot & commenced a
new study of Rocks & water, an
up-right — Back to dinner
at — at 2 Mr. White left
& then I returned to my note
& painted until 6 — At times
a few drops of rain fell — then
to tea feeling quite used up.
Eve. wandered about until
after 10, then to bed — a wet
night & heavy rain before
morning.

Alonzo Parker & — [illegible] are
of Conn. — am? this evening
from Lake Placid — brought
messages from Belle & Tweedy.
