## Left Page

### Memoranda

A parson had been ventilating some heterodox views before his congregation — he did away not only with hell, but by questioning immortality — He demolished hell & doubted of heaven — Soon after, meeting one of his parishioners who was generally considered a hard case he questioned him as to the impressions his discourse had made on him — The man was hardly pleased — the doubting had been too broad — "I tell you", said he "I'd rather go to hell than go nowhere"!

Many of our young artists stand in about the same relation to the N. A. & its grade of Associates — they say, "don't abolish the grade — 'tis hell, but we'd rather go there than nowhere
