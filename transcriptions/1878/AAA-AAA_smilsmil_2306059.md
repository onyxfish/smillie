## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 11, 1878

Rather a dismal sort of day. Starting with a light rain
enough to frighten the Jacobs
from starting on their way
home this m'f'g. Not feeling
able to work — much of the
m'f'g was spent lying
around, Reading, visiting
in Miss Hilland's Room
planning a Club House, to
be built up here & called
the Eyrie — Dinner at c'm
1 — then awhile on bed —
then finished letter to Miss
Durfea — weather thick
& showery — tea about 7 —
eve'g divided between
my bed, the piazza & the
parlor — got to bed for
the night between 10 & 11.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 12, 1878

Up before 6 to see the Jacobs
off — they got away with Mr.
Longstreet — Miserable m'f'g
mizzle & rain until after
10 — wrote out Constitution
for Keene Club, who are to
live in the "Eyrie" — About
11, with Miss Hilland & Mrs.
Cole, James & Hooper, went
to Peny Knoll — big pain
under my belt — Dismal
dinner soon after 1 — Part
of P.M. on bed — Showers
during the afternoon —
Dismal tea at 7 & dismal
eve'g in parlor — Rain
out of doors & pain in-doors
— to bed at 10 —
GCo went to P.O. in the
afternoon & managed to get
his horse & buckboard safely
over a deep way near Stetson's
