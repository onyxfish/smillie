## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 13, 1878

Dull foggy & wet. On my way to studio stopped at Miss Duncie's for a few minutes — got to shop soon after 10 — wholly unfit for work but I fussed over a little oil sketch 4×6 in. that I painted for E.N.J. years ago. After 2 when I took lunch weather cleared to a mild spring-like day — worked until after 4. Nicole came in — I cleaned up & soon after 5. went to Q.A. Anderson's — 14 S. & 4 av. — not in — then to 35 S. W. 15 S. & delivered an etching to W.J.J. Barr. Got to studio's about 6.30 very tired. Rested until after 7 — then tea — then writing until near 10 o'clk — then to 41 : S — & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 14, 1878

Overcast m'll — Rain commencing about — Got to studio a little before 10 — after having a very disagreeable passage with F.H. Smith at Renier's — He is a Rhinoceros made of brass & nothing can ever make him a gentleman — Set palette & to work on 9×12 view from Owl's Head on Long Lake "Adirondacks" but when the rain came 'twas so dark that I gave up painting & set to work charcoaling on 25×40. Lunch after 2 — worked until near 6 — then a nap — tea at 7 — at 8 went to Johnam's & made a short call, then to Mr. J.J. Williams' — found then Cottier & Inglis — left at about 9.45 & went to Union League Club monthly meeting — at 11 a few of the men speeches I heard were by two deaf & dumb men — Humphry Moore & Prof. Carlin — They told very funny stories by signs — at 11.30 walked to 41 S. with G[illegible]. Pleasant moon light — to bed about midnight.
