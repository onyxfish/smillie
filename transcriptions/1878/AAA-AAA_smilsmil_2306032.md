## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 17, 1878

*[written at top: Roger Rock House — Room 14.]*

Bkf. I soon after 8 — after that met Mr. Harbert about 9.30 we got away with him for a drive the mtt, overcast & at times quite cool & threatening — the Roads very heavy & dusty — Mr. Harber very pleasant — Drove over to the little town of Warrenton — then across to the lake & back to the Hotel. at 1 o'clk — could see but little of the lake owing to the smoky haze. Dinner at once — at 2.15 on Dr Garonski's started for the lower end of the lake — the breeze quite strong from the south & the atmosphere rapidly grew clearer — at times when the wind was directly aft we suffered very much from heat. Got to R. Rock House about 5.30 — Mr. Cyrus Butler & party on his steam yacht up the lake but returned about 7.30 — Tea at 6.30 the evening passed after introduction in chat with different members of Mr. B's party — Mr. & Mrs. Snow — Miss Snead — Miss Butler at — Mr. B — Took hold of my illustrating business very warmly & has promised to aid me. To bed about 10.30, a shower passed over us during the early even—

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 18, 1878

A very hot day — after bkf. — got out sketching traps & with Geo. went to cottage back of the house and sketched until 11 — then alone I climbed Roger's Rock — followed an old trail until I got lost, then had to tramp until 12.30 before I came out on the brow of the Rock much farther south than I had intended — Made a sketch — then along towards hotel & got another — got to hotel at 2 — dripping wet & very hot — could not take anything to eat until about 2.45 — after dinner finishing up sketches & resting — Tea about 6.30 — Went on piazza & in parlor — some dancing by the young folks — playing upon piano & singing. Mr. Colt of Newton carrying off the honors of applause — Chatted with Mrs. Rich. Butler who arrived last even. with her daughter & niece, Miss Potter — Mrs. Payne & two daughters, young Milburn & others — To bed about 11.
