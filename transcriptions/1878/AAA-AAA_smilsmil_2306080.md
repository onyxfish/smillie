## Left Page

### November, Saturday 2, 1878

Very pleasant day — but do not feel comfortable in the head — a discomfort that I cannot explain & that is very depressing to me. After bkf. went to Bruce's & showed him a card &c°. from Jo°. then around through Conklin's — met Van Ingen & went into his studio — 10 when I got home — then to work on The island of Arran, for Bruce. At 12.30, to P.O., at 1 dinner — then again on Bruce's work. Had a very pleasant call from Prof. Ritter of Vassar College about 4 o'clk. After 5, Father & I went out for a walk — tea soon after 6. Every fussed over Bruce'll's until about 9, then out & walked until 10 — then in & to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 3, 1878

Superb mtf. Took my time at exercise, rubbing & bath — did not get bkf. until 9 o'clk. Soon after 10 went to Stabee & ordered carriage to take Mary & children home — Katie is quite sick — seems to be a low fever of intermittent character. While the church bells rang, I wandered out to the outskirts of town and was completely lifted out of myself by the glory of the mtf. The enjoyment for a time was intense. After quite a stroll I returned & went to church cor. Cannon & E. Hamilton (Wheeler's). Communion service — after that went to Waring's then home to dinner at 1. After that read until near 4 o'clk — then out & walked until near to — then tea — Father brought in a young man Ge°. M. Laing — a one-armed stranger who had an interesting story concerning the loss of his arm from gangrene by inoculation while assisting at an amputation — Father then went to church — Stayed at home & wrote part of a letter to Mr. Whitney — to bed at 10 —
