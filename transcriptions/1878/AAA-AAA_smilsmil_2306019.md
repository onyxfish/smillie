## Left Page

### June, Friday 21, 1878

Another warm day & milky. Up early & fussed & bustled & had bkfst — at 8 — Charle got away for N.Y. by 8.30 train. I worked in-doors on "View of Hudson River, from Top of Anthony's Nose" — for Harper's Weekly — went up Town & attended to some messages at 11.30 — Dinner about 1. Transferred outline to Harper Block & sketched small proof of an etched die — which I am to finish as a drawing for A.B.N.Co. Tea between 6 & 7 — About 8.30 went to Matt's & at 9.30 — Returned with Kate — To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 22, 1878

Heavy rain until near noon — mizzle & showers later with a heavy blow & shower at 10 P.M. Started an etching on zinc of Cedar trees — about 3 × 6 in. Up Room. Etched a bit, progressively — tried to bite with Dutch Mordant but it would not act so, at 11, had to go in town & buy Nitric acid — Then got along first rate — 1 of acid to 10 of water. Dinner about 1 — Got my etching & biting finished about 3 — then at once set to work to take bfst. Getting something near what I wanted on dark, thick Japan paper, I took ten proofs, worked until 7.30 — tea at 8 — then lounged about until 10 — then to bed.
Rec'd note from A.V. St. Anthony directing me to go ahead with a drawing on wood: ill. the "V. Longfellow's 'Preludes'" —
