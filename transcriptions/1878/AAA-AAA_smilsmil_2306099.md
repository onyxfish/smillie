## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 10, 1878

Beastly day — dark with wind & heavy driving rain — Up late —
9.45 when I got to studio — to work on drawing "View from top
of Rogers' Rock". & finished it during the aft — altho' much
of my time was given to laying etching grounds for others —
out to lunch after 2 — the afternoon so dark that I could hardly see
to work but I slapped in a sketch in brown on blue paper
of "Approach to the Huddle." Mr. Adams & two other Utica gentlemen
called — At 5 I went out for a walk — Tea at 7 — clear during
the eve'g — wrote notes to James Fredericks & Kate Riley. To 37th
St. at 11 & to bed at once.

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 11, 1878

Overcast & windy — Got to studio at 10 & to work on "Approach to
Huddle" for Harpers — Worked until near 2 o'clk — then out
to lunch — on my return tried to get down to Harpers
with my drawings but one interruption succeeded another
so that at 4 o'clk. I gave it up. Rees. Mr. Gesner of Linden,
N.J., called with Miss Armstrong who wishes to take lessons in
W.C. — Nicoll came in & I did his etching partly — At 5 went
out for a walk — back at 6.30 took tea — at about 8.30
[illegible] & I called up on Miss Kate
Hilland at 33 W. 39th St. — near
11 when I got to bed.
