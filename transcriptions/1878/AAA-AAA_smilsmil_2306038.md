## Left Page

### July, Monday 29, 1878

Bright pleasant mtg. Got up before 6 & had a very hurried bkfp — with Mort. Butler & Joe Milbank — was hustled away on the Meteor at about 6.45 — got to Blk. Mt. Point a little before 9 — with Mort at once prepared to ascend the mt. on horse back — my mare lay down under me & otherwise behaved so bad that I was obliged to take another horse — there a man had to go ahead of us with an axe & chop the road clear of fallen trees that had been blown down by the wind of Friday night — after 2 hours we reached the top of the mt. at 11.30 — taking our horses to the tip top — the trail where they were blasting was in very bad condition but the horses went over it very nicely — the view was perfectly clear but a thin filmy began to spread over the sky — I verified and corrected the sketches of the other day — at 1 o'clk ate our lunch — then made several foreground sketches of the top — between 2 & 3 we started down — stopping & sketching by the way — parts of trail more trying for horses to get down than to get up — at 4 o'clk — got up on horses bound for the foot of mt. — got to boat at 4.45 — Boat started about 5 — sky overcast with film just thick enough to enable us to observe the eclipse of sun. Two of Capt. Tubb's daughters on board — got to Rogers Rock at 7 — then had tea — went on piazza telling the story of the day & listening to other stories of horses & bulls — etc. etc — to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 30, 1878

Gentle rain — Bkfp — at 9 o'clk — then some exercise on piazza — trim notes until 11.30. Then commenced outting & retracing ups on block view from top of Black Mt — Dinner about 1.30 — after that worked on sketches of yesterday, made pen sketches in albums for Misses Lillie and Nellie Payne & Miss Potter — then finished cutting on block — The Rain continued with short intervals all day — Tea about 7 — went promenading piazza with Miss Mellick & Miss Marion Butler — & Reading Tribune — the parlor deserted at 10 o'clk — then the gentlemen had a bat hunt — to bed at 10.45.
