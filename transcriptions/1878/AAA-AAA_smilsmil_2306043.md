## Left Page

### August, Thursday 8. 1878.

*Mansion Ho. Elizabethtown — Room 11.*

Up soon after 6 & finished packing of trunk. Glorious
mf. B'fst at 8.30. About 9.30, with a number of friends
went to boats & billiard room & made sketches of figures
until near 11 — at 11.40 the Horicon came & we had a
fine send off by Roger Rock friends — from Baldwin to Addin
junction by R.R. — then on to "Vermont"
steamboat — waited for 3/4 of an hour
before we got off — then dinner at
1.30. Clouds banking up in the
N. & W. Rain before we got to West
Port. Rode over to Elizabethtown
in a pouring rain — at the Mansion
Ho met lots of friends who urged
us to stay — Miss Kate Hillard arrived
from Keene — Rain continued until
Eve'g, then cleared to a glorious
moonlight — Promenaded upon
the piazza until after 10 — then
to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 9. 1878.

*Beede's, Keene Valley — Room 40*

Heavy thunderstorms & rain
during the night — pouring rain
all the way until about 11 — mate
a short note to Mrs. Watrons at R.R.
Ho with sketch of our being struck
with mud at E-Town — also a
short note to father — Had an early
dinner (12.30) & at 1 got started
for Beede's, with Mr. Mallory, driving
by Benson — The sky was yet filled
with clouds & the road with mud
when the sun came out it was
scalding hot — but the atmosphere
was so humid that we had
wonderfully beautiful effects —
Roads in very bad condition — washed
out in some places, bridges down,
rivers up, but we got to Beede's
in 4 h. 10 m. The ride having proved
very enjoyable — the rain fell upon
us — Miss Hillard & her friend Mrs. Coles
ahead of us — Tea at 6 — the Colwinas
& Misses Jacobs pleased to see us. Eve'g
on piazza until near 11, then to bed.
