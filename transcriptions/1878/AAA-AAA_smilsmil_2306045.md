## Left Page

### August, Monday 12, 1878

fine mtf, but evidently no change yet for the better in the weather. About 9.30 Coleman & I got away for a days tramps - we followed the Pond Road for about 2 1/2 miles, then through the Road to the River — found it too high to cross to the Ravine we intended to explore, then pushed on through the woods towards the Lake — we got into the most horrible jumbles, rocks & swamps, windfalls & all other kinds of wood abominations — at last, about 12.30, after a most fatiguing pull we came out upon the Road but a few rods from the Lake — By the drift wood at the old dam I kindled a fire made a pot of Soup & broiled a steak — the dinner was enjoyed very much — during it quite a shower passed over — About 2.30 we climbed to the top of Rainbow Falls — another shower — a little before it commenced our Return. At 4.15 left the lake & trudged back over the Road which was excessively mirey — light showers shortly before getting out of the woods met Nellie [illegible] — [illegible] — got to B's at 6 — Had a good rub down, a short rest then tea — Beautiful moonlight night on Piazza promenading until 9.30 then to bed well tired.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 13, 1878

Beautiful mtf, but weather evidently treacherous. Misses Hillard & Cushing, Mr. Cole & White went to upper Pond to camp for a day or two. I got at my oil Traps Ready for work. Ab't 1 went to Rock at River but was driven in by a heavy shower about noon — Rec'd note from father, Anthony & Mr. Edwards of Camp Manhattan — note to Anthony & to Wallace Brice. Dinner soon after 1 — after that, a Rest on my bed, then note to Parsons — Prospected for a study to begin to-morrow. Mr. Coleman's friends, Mr. & Mrs. Fisher, Baker & the Williams girls arr. from Placid — Tea at 6.30 — Cloudless moonlight night, but Raw & cold. Ther. 56° before 10 o'clk — To bed at 10.30.
