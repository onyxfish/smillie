## Left Page

### Tues. March 3, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — to studio at usual time — that is, 10 o'clk — varnished acid pink, filtered new acid — heated water \+ acid \+ got things generally in order to begin biting Borden plate, which I did after lunch — I made but two immersions during the afternoon \+ acid seemed to work perfectly.
At 4 o'clk. I went to H. D. Allen — Boulevard \+ 74th St. He made some improvements in the set of lower teeth. From there I walked home getting in at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Ivel Ga: came in \+ told us how he had been defrauded by young Mills who has practically robbed him of a painting \+ frame — $185.00 Carlson came at 9.30

B. 4 h.
C. 1

## Right Page

### Wed. March 4, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Foggy mf. clearing later to pleasant, sunny day. To studio before 10 — To work on biting of Borden plate — made very slow progress as I "stop out" very carefully. At 2.30 went to Lock's \+ paid bill of $6.50 then home to lunch, \+ again to studio \+ continued the biting till 4 — Then I worked for half an hour on little painting for Sallie Cook Munroe — then to Emma Ireland's, getting there a little before 5. Besides the three Ireland Sisters, Eliz. Drove was there, also Mary, Kate, Katharine \+ Bessie — The object of the gathering was to reminiss, so to speak, of matters concerning "the Ark" especially \+ of other things generally — At 6 I walked home — dinner — Ivel Rec: a note from Jim — asking my advice. A youth of extreme High Church tendencies, named Holstiple, of Hudson W.t., has not held upon Jim \+ is striving to proselyte him — he has got him into some sort of a church class \+ now advises him to be confirmed \+ it is upon this point that Jim asks advice — I wrote him at once that upon such subjects he was not sufficiently informed to take such action — I expatiated somewhat on the fallacy of a man-made God such as these ritualists have made \+ advising him to take care that Holstiple doesn't blind him altogether. At 10.30 I went to R. C. Station and mailed the letter — Met J. S. Church \+ walked up with him — then home \+ to bed.

B. 4 h.
