## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 8, 1903

**Weather:** Dull, cold m'f. \- clearing later \- Brilliant cold moonlight at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 \+ worked on Borden etching till 12.30 \- then to Douglas' \+ paid $100 on acct. of his bill \- then home to lunch \- Katharine \+ Bessie gone to Summit N.J. to stay over night \- to studio at 2 \+ worked on Borden tile after 4 \- then went to Davies Bros., Coal merchants \- \+ got there a dismal forecast. There is little coal to be had by the dealers who have to hunt every where for it. It is now $10 \[illegible\] a ton \+ may be double that before long. Home at 5 \- too cold \+ windy to walk out of doors \- so I exercised in my room \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Evetf wrote a note to Jim \+ went to G.C. Station \+ mailed it.

B. 4½ b

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 9, 1903

**Weather:** Very cold \- with high wind \- Carson came at a little after 8 \- gave me more than an hour of massage \- of course I was much belated \+ did not get to studio till 10.30 \-  **Temp:** [illegible]

I worked on Borden plate \- home to lunch \- \+ in the afternoon etched till 4 \- then I walked down to Tenth St. Studio bldg \+ saw Dielmann \+ arranged with him to meet me at Mr. Barhydt's on Monday and to arrange for transfer of Baldwin fund to N.A. of D. \- then walked home. Minna \+ Mary Ireland there, calling \- Dinner \- Evetf Wm. Mulford called \- Charl came in \- I wrote to Ralph \- Mr. Barhydt \+ Catharine Ireland \- at 10.30 went to G.C. Station \+ mailed letters.

B. 4½
