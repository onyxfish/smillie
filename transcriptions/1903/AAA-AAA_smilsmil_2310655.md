## Left Page

### Sat. July 25, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day - very gusty. clouding over in the afternoon, heavy rain storm at 6.30 with gusts so strong that at times I almost feared that the house w. not be able to withstand them. showers \+ gusty outbreaks followed all the eve. I got up in the m.f. without headache but so very weak that I could hardly drag myself about. The sun was very bright in the m.f. \+ Ralph \+ I did a lot of photo- printing to have the work nearly all spoiled by some evil that we cannot locate. good prints w. be clouded yellow or come out a sickly yellow grey - provoking. Adams came \+ painted the cared posts on north piazza - some of the color was washed off by the showers. - In spite of showers \+ wind Ralph \+ I spent the eve. in the Rattan lounging chairs on the north piazza wrapped \+ hooded in steamer rugs - The roar of the wind \+ the spray from rain in the darkness were strong reminders of steamer deck experiences.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 26, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Windy day - cloudy \+ clearing in the m.f., bright p.m., - growing cooler - cloudy, breezy \+ quite cool at night. Got up feeling much better - had my rub-down \+ bath \+ at 9, my o'clk. - wrote to Wanamaker, Miller, Trumbull etc \+ about 1 went to hotel \+ got mail. It was after 1.30 before we had dinner. Katharine Hardenburgh dined with us. It was about 4 p.m. when Ralph \+ I walked down the road to the Iron Bridge, climbed to the top of the rock there - continued up the trail to the old Bross wood Road \+ by that out to public Road - thence to bridge to The Wood \+ Ogden houses - passing the old Dr. Potter house where the Colmans, Tweedy's, Tiffany's, Geo. \+ N boarded 4 years ago - at Mr. Wood's house we got up on the Mossy Cascade trail \+ by it, out upon public Road at Iron Bridge \+ thence home at about 6 having loitered comfortably about 2/2 or 3 miles - pretty good considering my condition yesterday \+ the day before - at Mr. house we found the Magleas, Mrs. Doring, etc \+ Supper at 6.30 - after that there were fine clouds \+ striking effects - I made a rapid sketch of one \+ then made chalk outline upon canvas \+ hope to paint it to-morrow - a little news paper reading later in the eve.
A very cold, windy night - Ther. fell to 50° in my bedroom - I had an unrestful \+ uncomfortable time -
