## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day interm't passing to rain at night. I got to studio before 10 + in about an hour had drawn my last lines on the etching of the Borden plate. I can hardly realize that the long + depressing stage has at last come to an end. I at once prepared the plate for the bath + then set to work preparing my Acid Room for work. It is in a dreadful condition — a good deal of general picking up + putting things in place had just to be done — then dusting + cleaning then repairs to wooden sink which has developed a crack in the bottom thro' which water runs — then I made up a new lot of Mordant + in the afternoon sometime was given to pulverize it as platinum + making varnish. Had lunch at home as usual after a little promenade on Park Ave. — Ever I wrote to the boys + Charl, who was here, mailed the letters for me.

B. 3 h.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 28, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Carlson —
Had my stomach washed out.

A phenomenal storm of Rain + wind — all traces of the late snow storm were driven out of the streets by torrents of water hurled by furious wind. I did not get out of my bed till just before dinner time, late in the afternoon. I was wakened before 5 A.M. by severe pains in my chest — continued for an hour or two — About 8 am. I sent in for Dr. Putnam who soon came — examined me carefully — said the seat of the trouble was my stomach + advised Savage — I assented + he said he w'd. Return about 11. I took a very light bkfst. + waited — it was near 12 before he came with his apparatus — little time was lost — the tube was passed down my throat into my stomach with little trouble + less discomfort to me than I had expected + I was filled with warm water — this was syphoned off + I was filled again + again emptied — the ordeal could not have been less than 15 m. — it seemed to me a full half hour + I was greatly relieved when the tube was with drawn — About a quart of the liquid from my stomach was taken away for Dr. Osborn to analyze — About 1 o'clk. I had a chop + things for lunch — as my headache continued I did not get up till near 6 — + then went down to dinner — Eve + I got back to bed before 9 o'clk — was there when Carlson came — He gave me more than an hour of massage.
