## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 31, 1903

*Ralph came home. Katharine T. " "*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant \+ very mild \- To studio bet. 9
\+10 o'clk \- Ret'd. to the house about 11 \- Ralph
just in from Tarrytown to do a little shop[ping] \-
He went with me to 2d. Nat'l. Bank \- then
I went with him as he bought collars etc.
then home for lunch \- After that I
went to studio \- pasted tissue paper on
large frame that I raise at big window
when I am bothered by reflections from the
windows in houses opposite \- washed
brushes etc \- Ralph came for me at 5 \-
I went with him to Boormanns. he
took his wheel \+ ordered a Coaster brak[e]
on it \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. loafing
about \-

Sent two ph's. from dies by Father to Uncle
Willie.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 1, 1903

*Ralph Ret'd. to Hackley.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm \+ sunny \- Bkfst. at 9.30 after
my usual scrub \- At about 11 Ralph \+ I
Rode up town to 130th St. ferry \- went up on
via-duct \- then to Grant's Tomb \- then to
Columbia \- to Morningside Park \+ took
car for home at Central Park West \+110th St. \-
Dinner at 2 \- Soon after 4 I went with
R. to G.C. Station \- he Ret'd. to Tarrytown on
4.27 train \- Then I Ret'd. home \+ Rested
till after 6 \- no supper \- wrote note to
Uncle Willie \- \+ one to Registrar of Columbia
University for Entrance Requirements for Ralph
\- also a tentative form of circular to send
to etchers soliciting exhibits for St. Louis
[ex]p'n. To studio at 9 \+ closed windows
\- then sauntered in the moonlight for half
an hour or more on Park Ave \- then home.
