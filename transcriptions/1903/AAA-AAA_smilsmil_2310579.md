## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day — overcast thawing \+ raw — I got to studio at 10 \+ put in a day on "Borden Place" — Maynard came in \+ gave me $100 on acct. of back rent which I rec. joyfully. Home to lunch at 1 — after that went to bank \+ deposited his check after I had given the boys money to go to see \+ hear "the Sultan of Sulu" — to studio at 3 \+ etched till 4 — then, having a great deal of distress in my chest I gave it up \+ went to The Century to see the Shirlaw paintings — too dark to see — home at 5 — discomfort in my chest increasing — I cannot describe it — don't know what organ or organs are involved or whether it may not be some muscular affection like Rheumatism — I am wondering now if all of my miseries of the past year or two, that are becoming so numerous \+ indescribable are not from Hypochondria — at any rate I accept the warning \+ I have been inexcusably dilatory in setting my house in order. There are a number of things the, for the memory of my father \+ for my own sake I must do — I hope that I may yet have time \+ strength. Carson came at 9.30 — the distress in my chest had passed before he came \+ I took his treatment with comfort.

B. 4 h.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 22, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine winter day — bright \+ cold — thawing in the sunshine — Bkft. about 9.30 — Loafed about during the m'f. feeling somewhat better than I did yesterday. At 1 o'clk. went to studio \+ opened windows — went then to Charle's — met the boys who had been out for an airing — Home to dinner at 2 — afternoon, Mr. VanHorn called — at 4.30 I went out \+ had a short walk on Park Ave — then to studio, closed windows — then to Century to see Shirlaw's paintings — met him there \+ his wife \+ her sister — had a long chat with them — Mr. S— says she wants Maynard's studio \+ I say she can have it if she will ask him for it. I wish she would. Home at 6 — Tea at 6.30 — At 8 went to G.C. Station with the boys. Jim went to New Haven on the 8 o'clk. train — Ralph \+ I waited in the station — had a chat with Hero Schmit of Hackley Hall — at 8.35 the Hackley contingent got away \+ I Ret'd home \+ for the rest of the ev'f tried to find consolation in the newspapers.
