## Left Page

### Sat. June 13, 1903

**Weather:** An April day — sunshine + showers  **Temp:** [illegible]

I took 9.30 AM Train for Tarry Town — met Ed. Cook at Station + we went up together. 2 + I went from Station up to Hall in one of the two trolley cars that were waiting — a large crowd — The first exercise was in Goodhue Hall. Gift of the Key p — transferred from Mrs. C.C. Goodhue to Mon. Carnell D. Wright, Pres. of Trustees — his acceptance was an admirable address — he in turn turned them over to Head Master Williams — his address was rather slow + heavy — A hymn was impressively sung — then we all went to the Minot Savage House where another Key Presentation Ceremony was gone thro' — Baldwin the Pres — making a short address — travelling praise + compliments upon Mr. Hackley who sat by — unblushingly — Rob. Collyer made a prayer in which he conveyed some information to his hearers + addressed to the Lord. Then we adjourned to the Parlor of the Philip Savage House where we had more Keys + a passionate address from Minot J. Savage — It being then about 2 o'clk — the dining room was thrown open + lunch was served — Ed. C — went home — just in time to be caught in a shower — At 3 we Rel? to Goodhue Hall for Commencement Exercises — Rev. Merle Wright addressed the graduating class in a way that was delightfully stimulating to me. After these exercises the class day was planted + at 4 a large number of the guests went to the Athletic field where the boys had Races — Running jumps — High jumps — Pole vaults — Potato Races — throwing base balls, et c. — A light shower, soon allowed by sunshine caused something of a stampede before 5 o'clk — I walked down to Station + took 6.54 Train getting home before 8 — had a light supper — Eve'g, recounting the events of the day — I should have noted that as a part of the Goodhue Hall exercises, before the Carnell Wright address Rev. Tho. Slicer spoke admirably — \+ was introduced himself to me + asked me to call on him at his home 156 E. 35 St. S.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 14, 1903

**Weather:** Heavy showers — clouded, breezy + cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfst — before 10 — some writing — then to Studio — there for awhile — a short walk between showers — dinner at 2 — After that, some more writing. + Reading of news papers + at about 4.30 walked down to W. 8. S. No. 17, + called on Mrs. Giles, daughter of Wm. Main — saw her + her daughter, Ellen, + gave to her notes of introduction I have written to Messrs. Arthur Scribner — R. W. Gilder + Ripley Hitchcock — Mr. Giles showed me some of her paintings — It was after 6 when I started for 331 E. 17. S. I found the Ireland girls at their supper table — I had a very pleasant ev'g with them till 9.30 when I started for home — caught in a very heavy thunderstorm.
