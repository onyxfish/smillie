## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 1, 1903

**Weather:** A mild, quiet \+ brooding sort of day \-  **Temp:** [illegible]

clouds hung misty \+ low upon the mts, that were at times enveloped in a luminous, mysterious haze \+ then, in gloomy shadow. After bkf. I gathered a great quantity of corn \+ sent a mess of twenty choice ears of "Country Gentleman" to Mr. Maghee. After that Moody went with me down to the Maghee garden, where, until near noon, I took up strawberry plants \- I was much disappointed to find that all runners had been cleared from his extra late french variety. I got about 80 plants of the Brandywine variety. Then home \- then to hotel \- mail, not to come in till 1 o'clk \- new arrangement. Then home \+ had dinner at 1.30 \- after that I worked with Moody till near 5 o'clk, setting out the plants got in the mts. . Then to hotel \- mail. Home about 6 \- Rested till supper, about 7 \- Evg. Reading \-

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 2, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during last night \- clouds low \+ heavy till middle of aftn, when wind changed to north \+ the weather cleared cool \+ beautiful \- Trumbull came \+ finished his work \- I was in garden after bkf. \+ gathered two baskets of corn, one of which I sent to Tracy at St. Huberts. I did some work in gathering things up in studio \+ printed from negatives of self that Ralf took a few days ago. Dinner early, so that Mary could go with the Maghees to their camps on lower Ausable. I went to hotel \+ got mail immediately after dinner \- back \+ worked at the picking up of things \- later went with Moody \+ got some wild blackberry roots \+ transplanted them to my berry patch \- Supper at 6.30 \- Evg. our servants had "a party". I wrote notes \+ attended to some business affairs.
