## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 5, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouds hung low \+ there was a chilly wind from N. W. until well on in the aft when the clouds broke away \+ there was some sunshine \+ a milder air. I wrote to Mr. Kent, after bkfst., concerning cold frame sashes left at Montrose. \+ dawdled away some time in the garden, got first mess of corn. Afternoon, a little late, I went down to Ford. Beades's \+ with him went to his muck bed to see if it was the kind of stuff I wd. like to put in my garden \- it did not look altogether satisfactory to me \- I told him to haul a couple of loads on Monday and put it on my ploughed ground so that I may judge of it after it has drained out. Home to supper at 6.30 \- Evef., reading a frontier story by Gen. King.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 6, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant nt after a very cool night \- Ther. fell to 38°. Soon clouded over \+ was more or less cloudy \+ cool all day. I was in garden till 10.30 \- got a fine mess of peas \+ also some vines are badly struck by mildew \+ (also) beans, fine peas, fine squashes \+ fine carrots \- corn still struggling, near to finish, with cold weather \- About 11 went to Wes. Otis's \+ saw Rob't. \- engaged team for work to-morrow. Then to Mr. Openhy nes \- the first time I have met her since Mr. O's death \- She spoke freely of him \+ his tragic end \- Then to hotel \+ got mail \- home soon after 1 \- dinner \- Mr. Hradenburgh, Rautom \+ other members of that family came to 4 o'clk. tea as usual. We adjourned to the garden \+ then I went with them to the Hemlocks, their home \- Saw Mr. M\- Evef. the young people went over there to "Camp fire" \- I had a quiet evef. Reading.
