## Left Page

### Mon. June 15, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another dull day with frequent showers, some of them very heavy. The m'g till 12 was taken up with writing - going to G'd. Cent. St'n. to look after R.R. seats for Mary \+ to Douglas's - At 12 went to Higgins' - Horton's, Richter \+ to Scribner's - Mr. H'd. Smith has gone to the country for a short vacation so, my Borden business will have to be settled with him by correspondence. - Home to lunch at 1 - To studio at 2 \+ met Higgins who looked over my heater in cellar - it is leaking - Then Chas. Kurtz came in \+ other callers - so that my afternoon till 5 was gone - Then home - dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g Charl came in - I wrote a letter to Jim \+ mailed it at G'd. Cent St'n. before 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 16, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ quite cool - All the morning running around - first to Davies Bros. \+ ordered 20 tons of coal - to Hankinson's \+ paid bill - To Second Nat'l. Bank \+ cut coupons till Nov. 1. \+ left them with bank to collect - also left a $1000 bond as security ag't. overdrafts - Then to Alexander's but not finding what I wanted went to Coward's, in Greenwich near Warren - bought pair \+ ret'd. to Alexander's \+ ordered a pair - Then home to lunch soon after 1 o'clk - having been from the East River \+ 37th St. to Warren near the North River - Rested after lunch till 3.30 - then writing till near 6 - Then, as Ralph had not yet appeared - was to have been in at 3.30, I telephoned to Stackley \+ learned that the school had been detained for a day as a bit of discipline - Mr. Fred. [illegible] to dinner - I accepted an invitation from Charl to dine with him - Woolsey Carnact. called on the girls - Mary left the girls with [illegible] \+ him \+ came over to Charl's - we ret'd. to the house at about 9.30 - I had Rec'd. a note from Jim to which I wrote a reply \+ mailed it at G'd. Cent. St'n. before 11. This was in reply to his reply to my note written last night.
