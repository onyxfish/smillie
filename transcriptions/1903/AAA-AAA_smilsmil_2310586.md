## Left Page

### Sat. March 7, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, muggy day with rain at night. worked all the m'f on Borden biting \- Hometo Lunch at 1 \- back at 2 \- at 3\. Dr. Gersler \+ Lustgarten came \+ spent the rest of the afternoon with me looking over my p\[illegible\] \+ sketches \- W. Lustgarten bought a copy of my aquatint, "Old house at Boulague" for $10\. \+ asked me to make a selection of proofs that I have for sale that he may see them \- Home at 5 \+ Rested. Till dinner at 6.30 \- Evef, before 9 went to Century meeting \+ found it so entertaining that I did not leave till midnight \+ got to bed at about 1\.

B. H h.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 8, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal, dark, rainy day \- bkf\. at 10\. went to studio at noon \+ spent an hour cutting away the many proofs, sketches etc, that I brought out yesterday to show to Dr. Gersler \+ Lustgarten \- Then to Charl's \[illegible\] home to dinner at 2 \- About 4 I gave way to my feelings \+ went to bed \+ "made up" the sleep that I lost last night \- I was disappointed at not going to visit the new Studio Bdf. in 67. S. near Central Park west as invited by Rob\[t\]. V. V. Sewell. Tea at 6.30\. Evef. Revised biographical sketch of Father, sent to me by M\[r\]. Howard Brown. for Biographical Dict\[y\] of the U. S.
