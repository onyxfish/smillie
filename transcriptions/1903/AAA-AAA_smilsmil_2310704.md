## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 29, 1903

**Weather:** Very pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — I went to studio very early — Walter Cook came in for a few minutes to speak of Arnold Brunner who passed the Com. last night. I commenced the repainting of one of last summer's beginnings. x I worked till 1 — then to lunch — After that I went to N.A. of D. \+ there arranged with Mr. Sis about the enrollment of candidates for the Etching class. Then back to studio getting there about dusk — cleaned palette \+ home before 6. dinner — Wrote to Ralph \+ Geo. Hutton — at 9 went to studio for half an hour.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 30, 1903

**Weather:** Very mild \+ pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bessie Rel? from Po'k.

Very mild \+ pleasant. To studio at 10 \+ to work on the painting of yesterday — A short stroll on Park Ave. before going into lunch soon after 1 — to studio again at 2.30 \+ painted till 4.30 — then cleaned up — had a little rest — then a stroll on Park Ave. \+ home at 5.30 — Read We'f Post till dinner at 6.45 — We'f. wrote letter to Jim — none to Ralph as I expect him home to-morrow — Bessie Rel? from Po'k — this afternoon — used up by severe cold — Katharine remained behind — I went out \+ mailed letter to Jim after 9 o'clk then, beguiled by the mild moonlight I lounged up \+ down Park Ave. till near 10.
