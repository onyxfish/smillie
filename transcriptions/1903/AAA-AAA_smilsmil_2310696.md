## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 13, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary Ret? from P.ok.

Charming day \- bright, sunny \+ warm. Bkf't. at 8°° I busied myself about the house all the m'f. Mary Ret? from Po'keepsie before noon \- lunch about 1 \- after that went to the barber's \+ had my hair cut \+ head shampoo'd \- to the great benefit of my appearance \+ an added comfort. I mailed to Ralph his watch \- When I got home D't. Hale came \+ I had a protracted visit \- I went over my case fully \+ he asked for a day or two to consider it \- I have had much trouble in getting about to-day \+ am entirely unfit for work of any kind. Freight from Westport came this p.m. Dinner at 6.30 Eve'g. unpacked bicycles \- some reading.

Mary notified Christine \+ Blenda that she w? not want their services after this month.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 14, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another bright mild day \- Gas man worked with helper all day in the kitchen making new connection from Gas Range to pipe between Metre \+ Regulator attachment put on by Gas consumers Assn. Trouble in butler's pantry \- water backing up from soil pipe \- m'f. I went to studio \+ then down to 10''S. Studio Bldg, hoping to see Dielman \- not in \- the only one I found was Guy, \+ with him I chatted for nearly an hour \- Then walked up town stopping at Kepple's \+ having a chat with him \- then to Scribner's \- saw Wt-off \+ Smith \- then to studio again \+ home. Lunch after 1 \- afternoon at home \- unpacked box of freight from Keene \- went to Ryan's \+ Thomas' \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g. went to Tom Fessenden's \- 154 W. 44''S. \- \+ visited with Sarah Fessenden (Mr. W'm Hammond) Ge? came in later \- we had a nice \+ chatty time. Sarah looks very old but stee retains much of her old time vivacity. Ge? \+ I left just before 10 \- Then home.
