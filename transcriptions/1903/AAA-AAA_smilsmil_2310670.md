## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 24, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day — I continue to improve slowly — After bkfs. when Ralph \+ I were about to commence a campaign of Telephone \+ Electric bell wiring the Elkins came \+ took the boys off with them for a tramp over the River trail on West side. So, I spent much of the mtg. printing photos. To Hotel at noon — dinner at 1. Afternoon R. \+ I took up the work of boring holes for wires \+ planning the Tel. \+ bell equipment — we worked without making much progress till 5 — Moody got the old iron [illegible] from Mr. White's. At 6 R. \+ I went to Fred Beede's \+ wandered in his woods looking for the black muck that he says he has \+ is desirous of selling to me for garden. If we found it we did not know it — Home at 6\.30 — supper — eve. f. some newspaper reading — some coming over R's. plans, on paper, for wiring — \+ some writing —

Kate walked from her bed to her chair on the piazza unaided \+ spent the afternoon there — Dr. came at noon but says he will not come to-morrow unless sent for.

Jim went with the Elkins to their place in New Russia Valley — the other side of Giant about 3 m. from E-town.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 25, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced falling quite early in the mtg. \+ from 7 to 9 fell heavily. wind from E. — light mizzle after that — clouds hanging very heavy in the afternoon \+ at night — Ralph \+ I had a good days work getting bell wires in place in the house — we had to crawl on our bellies under the house \+ thread wires through floors \+ partitions — we got batteries in place, wires up \+ rung front door bell at about 5 o'clk. Earlier in the afternoon W.H. Neilson sent requesting me to meet him at Scott Brown's — I did so \+ we signed deeds exchanging triangular bits of land by which my entrance gate now stands upon my own ground. I went to hotel at noon \+ again in the afternoon on my way home from Scott Brown's. I built a good fire in studio in the mtg. \+ Kate spent the day there in a wicker reclining chair or lounge. — The Dr. did not call to-day — She is regaining her strength very satisfactorily.
