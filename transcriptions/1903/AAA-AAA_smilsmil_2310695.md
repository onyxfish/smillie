## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 11, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Tarrytown \-

Weather still disagreeable \- cloudy +
threatening but rain did not fall till
after 5 p.m. I got up at usual time,
went thro' usual Sunday m'g functions.
had bkf't + at 10.45 took train for T. town \-
I walked most of the way up to the school
before the Trolley car overtook me \- got to
school soon after 12 \- Raelph out, so I
sauntered about alone for some time before
he returned \- Dinner at 1 \- on right hand
of Mr. Williams \- after dinner, coffee in parlor
where I had an interesting chat with Mr.
[illegible] history teacher + successor [illegible] W. Brush
After that R \- + S wandered over the grounds
to the site for the new Skating Rink, the new
beautiful bld'g + the Chapel now in course
of construction \- at 4.30 to afternoon service
in Good hue stall \- Mr. W. S. Miller + wife there
\- I slipped out to catch 4.50 valley car but just
missed it \- then I walked down to station +
just before I got there was caught in a heavy
shower \- I took 5.33 train \- got in at 6.30 \-
had supper at home + at 8 went to 141 E. 37th S
to Congratulate Edna Cook upon her engagement
to Mr. Mintsley \- he was there + I extended my
congratulations to him as well \- Mary Cook
is visiting at Walter's at Bellport L. I. \- saw Syd
+ Tom. Home at 9.30 + to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 12, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded m'g \- rain in afternoon \- dry
eve'g. After bkf't went to studio for a
few minutes \- then to 2d. Nat'l Bk. + made
deposit; then sauntered up 5th Ave. stopping
at picture galleries \- home at 12.30 lunch
at 1.30 \- then for a time, putting away
things + reading \- at 4.30 I yielded to
my feelings, lay down upon my bed +
slept for nearly two hours. Dinner
at 6.30 \- Mr. Fred Young dined + spent
the eve'g with the girls \- at 8.30 I went
to Dr. Yale's \- found him in \- + was with
him till 10.30 \- much of the time he
was showing to me photo. prints of his
work \- He spoke very quietly, evidently
under restraint, of Roy, his death + work that
he had been engaged in \- it was very trying
to me \- I sympathise so keenly with him \-
knowing what an irreparable loss the
death of this only son, a companion just
growing into manhood, must be to him \-
I told him, just as I left, that I thought
my physical condition was grave + I
wished him to call on me at an early
date to give my case a most careful +
searching consideration.
