## Left Page

### Wed. July 15, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of showers — becoming persistent at night. Ther. last night fell to 57° in my bedroom — I had a wood fire all day in my stove in studio. I wrote a couple of letters in the m'f \+ at about noon went to hotel — got mail — interviewed Mr. Tracy concerning the possibility of my getting my family washing done in the hotel laundry — Mrs. T. thinks it can be arranged after consultation between Mary \+ herself. Dinner at 1 — afternoon in studio, writing up some of my old acct. books. Tea at 5.30 after Ralph \+ I had tramped around the block. Ev'f some desultory reading. A fire on the hearth made it comfortably warm in the large room.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 16, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day opened with sunshine \+ clouds — soon after 4kf't light showers began to pass over from the west \+ later in the day there were many, some of them quite heavy — so that the ground got a pretty good drenching. I got up with a headache, so severe that I was practically disabled all day. I worked with Ralph at printing photos from the negatives he developed yesterday. About 11 went with Mary to the hotel \+ she interviewed Mr. Tracy on the subject of having our family wash laundered at the hotel — then home — dinner — about 3' went to P.O. \+ then walked on Reserve Road nearly out to the lake — to the Schille Trail — \+ home about 5.30 a walk of about 5½ miles — had a rub-down \+ change — a rest — then supper — Ev'f — loafed.
