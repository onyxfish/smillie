## Left Page

### Tues. March 17, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Carlson.

A fairly pleasant day - altho' foul weather was predicted by the Weather Bureau. At 10 went to Studio + worked steadily with graver till 12.30 on Borden plate. Katherine Throop had a model + worked in studio. At 12.30 I went to Lewis + Conger - W. 42d St. I left the cup of Spirit Lamps to be soldered - Home to lunch at 1.15 - to studio at 2.15 + worked till 5 - Then Rode to Central Park W. + 86th St. - then walked in the Park for nearly an hour - then out on East Side + took car at Mad. Ave. + 84th St. + home at 6.30 - dinner + Eve'g with newspapers till 8.30. Carson came at 9.15 + gave me treatment - especial attention being given to abdominal movement and exercise.

B. 5 h.
C. 1

## Right Page

### Wed. March 18, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Miss Chadwick to lunch.

Pleasant day - In spite of a headache that made me miserable all day I was able to put in a fair days work on Borden plate. To studio at 10 - worked till near - then home - Miss Nathalie Chadwick there to lunch - back to studio at 2.30 - Carson came at 3.30 + gave me a half hours abdominal treatment - Later Maurice Stone came in - I worked till 5 oclk. putting in about more than four hours work - Then I rode up to 84th St. + Central Park West - went into Park and around large Reservoir - out at 90th St.- Thro. 91st St. + at Carnegie's door met H. Russell Buster, who insisted upon my going into his house (next door to Carnegie's) which I did - Talked for a few moments with him about federation Scheme for a great, Union Art Bldg. - saw his wife + boy - left at 6.35 - got home at 7 - dinner - Eve'g. did not feel able to go to Water Color Meeting - annual - election of Officers - J. Q. Brown will not be re-elected, probably - loafed in big chair. B. was Re-elected - if I (and another fellow) had been there + C. McCall w'd have been elected.

B. 4 h.
C. 1/2.
