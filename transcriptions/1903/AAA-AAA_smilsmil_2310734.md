## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 28, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine, cold day — after a night of discomfort was unable to get up in the m'g. Kept in bed till after 12 — then got up, dressed \+ at 1 o'clk had a little nourishment —
In spite of feeling wretched I clung to the hope that I would be able to go to the Golden Wedding of F. Baker \+ Priscella Goodliffe at 162 Summer Ave, Newark \+ I arranged that Ralph should go with me as an attendant — but at 5 o'clk, I was still far below par — The Ther. stood at 25° \+ I reluctantly decided to take no risks \+ stay at home. — Then R. \+ I went to G.C. Station \+ I telegraphed my regrets to Mr. Baker — Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g wrote a note to Bessie Lane in reply to her request for address of [illegible]

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 29, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More snow during the night \+ in the m'g — clearing to fairly pleasant weather. I wrote to Mr. Baker after b'f'st. To studio before 11 \+ painted on 16 × 18 " "Munderhead" Ev'g\! The frame for it came yesterday \+ I was able to see it in frame \+ judge of effect \+ requirements. Home to lunch \+at 1.30 Ralph lunched at Edw\. Cook's \+ with them went to "Babes in Toyland" (Theatre) I ret'd to studio's before 3 — painted for an hour \+ then cleared up — lounged in big chair till near 6 — then home — dinner — Jim dined with his comfret Alumnus at Delmonico's — Ev'g. Charl came in.
