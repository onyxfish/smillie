## Left Page

### Thur. July 9, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day - Ther. got up to about 90° except in protected places. a lively breeze all day, but a dry \+ hot one - Trumbull worked most of m'f fussing over water-closet seat hinges - In the afternoon he got at casing posts on the piazza - Ralph \+ I spent much of the m'f in putting up hooks \+ hanging bamboo screens on north piazza. Dinner soon after 1 - Christme's face very much better - I went for mail about 3 \+ then to Scott Brown's where I met Wm G. Neilson \+ had a pleasant chat with him - home before 6 - After supper seeded terrace north of house with new grass seed \+ then R - spent some time spraying it thro' new hose - Glorious full moon moonlight - In spite of heat I did a little writing in the ev'g.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 10, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A muggy, sunny, cloudy, breezy, close day - all by turns - a smart shower passed over us at about noon - others passed north of us early in the ev'g. Until about 11 I passed the m'f. looking after things \+ writing notes \+ making a plan of my bedroom closet doors to show White Vaughan \+ Co. why I cannot use the lock I got of them \+ the sort of lock I wish them to send me - Then to hotel - met Maghee - Talked Egypt, Ancient \+ Modern, and then walked back with him - Dinner at 1 - Finished letter to W. Vaug. \+ Co. \+ to hotel soon after 3 - home \+ wrote letter to Jim - Supper at 6.30 - ev'g. seeded a portion of the ground under pines in front of house \+ then spent some time watering with hose the ground north and south of the house - Then loafed about till bed time - the girls entertaining the Misses MacKay -
