## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 12, 1903

Katharine \+ Elizabeth rec'd from Annapolis.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine weather. To studio before 10. Painted on Canvas upon which I worked yesterday — Maynard gave me a check for $150 - back Rent — a great relief but far too little to help me out of my present trouble — my bills now standing are more than $700 in excess of what is in sight — I see no way out except to give up my home 1105. 38th st. Lunch at 1.
I worked a little while in the afternoon — then cleaned up — sauntered for awhile on Park Ave — home. had a rest before dinner \+ in the evef. went with the girls to Chas's \+ looked over many of the photos he brought home with him.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 13, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very pleasant weather. After bkft. I spent most of the morning writing notes \+ letters that I have been neglecting for some time past. Then I went to the bank with G. W. Maynard's check — then to studio till 1 o'clk — then home \+ after lunch went up to N.A. of D. where I arranged with Mr. Diis to call a meeting of the Etching class to organize for work.
Fred. Stahr was there doing some work for himself — I left before 4 o'clk — as I was nearing home I saw that there was unusual excitement in Park Ave. bet. 39th \+ 40th St. I asked a Policeman \+ he told me that Andrew H. Green had been shot to death at 1.30 — on his door step (91 Park Ave) by a negro. Dinner at 6.30 Evef. wrote to Jim \+ Ralph. mailed letters after 10.
