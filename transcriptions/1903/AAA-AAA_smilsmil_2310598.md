## Left Page

### Tues. March 31, 1903

**Weather:** Sunshine \+ shadow day —  **Temp:** [illegible]

sent for Dr. Putnam \+ he came at about 10. He advised a clearing out with castor oil \+ told me how to take it in a little whiskey. It went down successfully \+ did its work without much discomfort to me. I feel quite easy during the day. Read a lot of Oliver Horn — it disappoints me — Smith has worked too hard over it — lacks spontaneity — too much attempt at fine writing. Want. Sam. Calman came in \+ made a long \+ interesting call — Ge° also came in.

wrote to Butler proposing to defer dinner engagement for a fortnight

D. P.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 1, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came home.

A lovely day as I could see it from my window but I had to keep my bed all day. I awoke feeling quite fresh after a good nights rest but before long the bowel discomfort returned \+ I sent for Dr. Putnam who came before 11, gave a prescription for assafatida (?) pills \+ said I must continue in bed. Kate Leonard \+ Jane Banks left my service to-day. Kate was much taken up \+ I felt very sorry for her. Two swedes came to take the places vacated. I spent the day as contentedly as I could reading \+ writing. I was much shocked to read in the Eve. Post of the disappearance of Mr. Adolph Openhym \+ his probable suicide by jumping from High Bridge. At 7 o'clk I was much surprised by Jim's unexpected appearance — said Dr. Osborn of New Haven said he had the mumps \+ advised him to get home at once. I sent him in to Dr. Putnam who said he did not have mumps but a form of sore throat with some fever \+ told him to go to bed \+ take care of himself.

D. P. came to me in the m'g
Jim went to him in the eve'g
