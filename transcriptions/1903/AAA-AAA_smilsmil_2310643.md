## Left Page

### Mon. June 29, 1903

**Weather:** Showers in the middle of the day. Some sunshine in the mt — some at evefg. \+ clear, cold starlight \+ a young moon at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfst. at 8 — work about the place \+ in my room till about 11 — Then Ralph \+ I walked out to Chappel Pond — The desolation after the fire is very sad to see. A light rain began falling as we got to the Pond \+ continued as we walked home — Got in rather damp — changed some of my clothing \+ had dinner soon after 1 — Jim \+ Katharine off most of the day fishing — got nothing but wet — I spent the P.m. in doors — Tea at 6.30. After that Ralph \+ I went to Scott Brown's. I paid bill for Wood \+ Coal — Home at 8 — Mr. Maghee \+ Catharine Hardenburgh there —

## Right Page

### Tues. June 30, 1903

**Weather:** Brilliant mt for a little while — then overcast with light rain — growing warmer — afternoon sunny — evefg., Portentous clouds \+ smart shower about 10 p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfst. at 8 I spent the mt (after making the tour of the place) in writing — to Mimbull — to Waster cook — to Botanical Garden, Bronx — \+ to Katie Elkin — To the last, a long letter — mainly concerning Jim. Dinner about 1 — afternoon I went to hotel with letters \+ got mail — after reading newspaper on hotel verandah I went to Scott Brown's — met Rob. Neilson just coming in from camp — had a 3½ lb. speckled trout — then met Wes. Otis \+ arranged to have two or three lengths of gal. iron pipe added to my pipe to run it down to my garden — then Scott ordered the pipe from West Post by telephone — Then I walked a mile \+ a half into Reserve — The beauty of Mill Brook greatly injured by the flood that rushed thro it on the breaking of the reservoir dam — Home at 5 after a four mile walk — Read papers — tea at 7 — More wandering over the place then on piazza with Ralph — he talking chemistry much over my head — but I, much pleased — To bed soon after 10 —
