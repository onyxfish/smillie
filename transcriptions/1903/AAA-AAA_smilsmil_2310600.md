## Left Page

### Sat. April 4, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wind \+ heavy rain in the m'f. Toward noon snow flew quite thickly in big flakes for an hour \+ more \+ it grew rapidly colder — clearing during the afternoon \+ bright, cold moonlight at night. I had a very hard time with bowels in the night — up at 12 - 1.30 \+ 3 o'clk — with much pain — I got up \+ had bkfst. at 9.30 — the swelling on Jim's throat is going around to the Parotids in true mumps style which seemed gratifying to Dr. P — when he called about noon — I hung about all day feeling a good deal out of sorts \+ good for very little — Weitenkampf of the Print Dept. of the Public Library came to see if he could get a promise of proofs from my work for the Library —
Ev'g. Charl came in —

Dr. P— once.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 5, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a night when the Ther. went down to 28°. To-day followed brilliantly clear, stimulatingly cool \+ rather breezy. I did not get thro' the night without disturbance — up at 4 o'clk — \+ again at 8 — after which I felt weak \+ discouraged — Dr. P— came at 10 o'clk. before I was dressed. He found Jim progressingly favorably — After I had my bkft. Ralph \+ I went out \+ we sauntered about for more than half an hour on the sunny \+ protected side of streets, I beginning to feel stronger — Dinner at 2 — later R. \+ I went out again — Jim had gone thro' a course of Calomel \+ Seidlitz — Tea at about 7 — M. Young took tea with us \+ spent the ev'g. doing much singing —

Dr. P. once.
