## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 17, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced about 10 a.m. commencing gently, wind \+ rain increasing till about 1 o'clk when both were driving furiously. Then a gradual clearing \+ by 4 o'clk. there were streaks of sunshine down the valley. I gathered corn from the garden after 4\[?\]. Mr. Loring. Hardenburgh \+ Vanstrom came to bid Kate good bye \+ I sat for some time \+ "chinned" with them. The result was: — M.f. — nothing accomplished. Dinner at 1 — the afternoon was as unproductive of results as the m.f. After 4 I went out to see how badly my road was washed. Surprised to find no damage done. Supper before 7 — Eve'g. reading.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 18, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate left for Po'keepsie.

A very fine day — breezy \+ cool — almost cold — all hands up early — \+ at 8.20 Kate rode away from the house with the Woodwards bound for Westport \+ Po'k. They live near Uncle W. \+ so if need be, can see Kate to her very door. The day was all that could be desired for her journey \+ she started off seemingly in very good spirits. Rec'd. telegram from her at Westport saying that so far she had got along feeling "fine". After my m.f. work in garden, picking peas (last mess probably) \+ corn — I wrote part of a letter to Charl — At 11.30 I walked with Ralph out on Chappel Pond R\. — got a couple of snap shots — Then to hotel — got mail — \+ home before dinner at 1. Schilling there but he soon left. Bessie had been walking most of the m.f. with Maghee — he was asked to dine with us \+ accepted. Afternoon I wrote part of letter to Geo — after I had finished letter to Charl \+ posted it at hotel. Mr. Macintosh \+ daughter called \+ I let their horse eat part of my front piazza rail — at 5.30 Ralph \+ I went to Amy Blade's — not at home — then down to the iron bridge \+ back in time for supper — Jim went up Colvin — all day.

Otis \+ team at work on road.
