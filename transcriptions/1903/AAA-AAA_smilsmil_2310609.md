## Left Page

### Wed. April 22, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - becoming overcast late in the afternoon - To studio about 10 \+ worked on Borden plate till 12.30 - went to see the works of 10 Am. Painters at Durand-Ruels - a very poor lot according to my view - Home to lunch - back at 2.30 worked till after 4 - then went to ly bu. of Soct. of Am. Artists - 57th St. especially to see the work of Ben Foster - not very inspirit. ing to my mind - walked home \+ had a half-hour rest before dinner - Eve'g at 8 - went to Century - Com. on Admissions \+ had a very long session - an unusually full meeting - but one absentee - nothing exciting - I did not get to bed till 10'clk.

B. 4 h.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 23, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An overcast day with wind getting around to the east - some sunshine in the early m'f. To studio at 10 - worked till 12.30 - Uncle Willie, Aunt Agnes \+ Diring came in at about 11 \+ stayed half an hour or more. I had a short walk before lunch \+ back to studio at 2 and continued work on Borden plate till 5 - A lot of George's studio belongings were sent down \+ carried up to Maynard's studio - he is moving his things to the east end of his studio preparatory to the putting up of a partition - At 5.30 I went to Charity Or'g Soct'y, Fourth Ave. \+ 22d St. about my charities subscription - Home at 6 - dinner at 6.30 - Mr. [illegible] dined with us \+ spent the eve'g - later, Mr. \+ Mrs. Hardenburgh, of Jersey City came in.

B. 5 h.
