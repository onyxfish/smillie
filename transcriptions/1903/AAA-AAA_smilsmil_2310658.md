## Left Page

### Fri. July 31, 1903

**Weather:** Chilly, cloudy, windy — clearing at night to clear, cold \+ brilliant moonlight.  **Temp:** [illegible]

The most of the w\+ was spent with Ralph in getting sharp sand from a road bank below the house — washing it — then mixing with it cement \+ filling the spaces between bricks on hearths in Main Room \+ my bedroom. Adams came in the m\+ \+ spent most of the day painting piazza posts etc. — Dinner at 12.30 to give Christine \+ Blenda an afternoon outing with Tucker, the golf master at St. Huberts. They went to top of Roaring Brook fall \+ farther. Later Ralph \+ I went to hotel for mail. — with Moodie directing his work laying cobble stones along the edge of the road way up to house. Bird. Berse came \+ I paid his bill — I wrote to Ryan — sent check to Shovan. — Tea at 6.30 — after it R. \+ I went to hotel with letters — Mete for about an hours, most of the time chatting with Mr. Alexander. — home before 9.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 1, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. Rockwell \+ Miss Jones dined with us —

Glorious day — I spent much of the w\+ out of doors — \+ considerable time in-doors printing photos. from some of Ralph's negatives. — About 11 went to hotel \+ got mail — Mr. Rockwell \+ her sister, Miss Jones — (daughters of Alfred Jones) came by invitation \+ dined with us at 1.30 — with them some time after dinner. It was about 4 o'clk. when Ralph \+ I started for the village — we walked to Mr. Estes' \+ made a purchase or two, then onto the Country Club — my first visit. It was the regular Sat. afternoon tea — A number of people, old \+ young — male \+ female, were there, eating cake, drinking tea \+ gabbin' — I added a nice to the confusion \+ it about 6 rode home with Mr. Maghee, Mr. Boornum, Bessie, Katharine Hardanburgh. Ralph Boornum \+ Ralph S. — Bessie R. \+ I got out at our gate. Supper. — Ever\+. loafing about till bed time —

I was much shocked to learn, thro' the newspapers, of the sudden death of Dr. Gales' son, Leroy Jun\+ aged 17". This must be a crushing blow to him \+ his wife.
