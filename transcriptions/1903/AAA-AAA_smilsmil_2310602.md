## Left Page

### Wed. April 8, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another wet day — so dark that I was not able to see to work at times during the day — to studio at 10 worked on Borden plate awhile — \+ for some time at work on proofs of father's early work that I am preparing to mount — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — awhile on B— plate — \+ then at mounting pfs. Home soon after 5 — Dinner at 6.30 Eve'g, Charle came in —
Jim seems to be improving steadily \+ has no appearance of swelling about the face — \+ no pain. has been up \+ dressed all day, but still kept a close prisoner in his little room — hard luck which he submits to with singular equanimity

B — 3 h.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 9, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant m'g — showers late in the afternoon \+ eve'g. After 4&p. I went to Dr. Putnam's to ask if Jim could be released from quarantine — He says he may now go out in the streets but should not mingle freely with the family for five or six days yet — Will he che- me $5.00 for this as a professional visit? To studio at 10 — worked on B. plate — home to lunch — back at 2.30 — worked till 5 — but not all the time on plate — cutting mounts for pfs. — Then went to O'Neil's — 6' Ave. \+ bought Pajama suit for Jim — Home at 6.15 — dinner — Eve'g, out of sorts — indigestion — Jim \+ Raefsle were out together, morning \+ afternoon. Jim took his dinner in my bed room \+ he \+ Raefsle spent part of the eve'g there — R. reading aloud —

B — 4 h.
Dr. P — ?
