## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 3, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim ret. home.

Cloudless m'g. \+ very mild - clouds followed \+ a very pleasant day - a mild \+ beautiful moonlight. In garden as usual after bkf. I had planned to go up River trail, west side, \+ make a sketch of some rocks, but time got away from me \+ I put it off till the afternoon - then it was so cloudy that I gave it up \+, instead, made a blk \+ white study, from upper piazza, of one of the big pines. Went to hotel at noon \+ again at 5 o'clk - then walked over to Wes. O'his \+ engaged his team to come \+ work for me - then home at 6.30. Sup/per Jim came in by stage from E. town just before that. Eve'g. all the young folks went to hotel to hear some Tyrolean singing \+ Zither playing - my head bothered me \+ I spent the eve'g. moved this lounges in the main room, sleeping - to bed at 11 feeling better.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 4, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm \+ summer-like day - pleasant breeze blowing - bright moonlight - heavy thunderstorm between 8 \+ 10 p.m. then more moonlight. It was 10.30 am. before I got away with Ralph to go to some rocks on the River-trail that I wish to get a sketch of hoping to get aid from it in reversing the lower left hand corner of my 33×44" "The River's Source" - We stopped at "The" Janeway's - saw him, his wife \+ his two little daughters there his mother \+ her friend - It was nearly 11.30 before I got to my rocks. Ralph took two or three photos. \+ I made a slight crayon sketch - It was nearly 12.30 before we started back. got out of Reserve at 1. Stopped at the hotel \+ home at 1.30 - ate a very light dinner - a little nab after it - Mr. Weston came bringing Mr. Harris of Montreal - an artist, stopping at East Hill. He met Mary \+ Kate at Munich. I did little else than loaf during the rest of the afternoon after they had gone. Supper \[before?\] - Eve'g. Reading - Katharine \+ Bessie went to lower lake to attend afternoon - also ties \+ have a moonlight row with Maghee - They came home through the storm \+ got in pretty wet but well pleased, of course.

The first eve'g this season when it was mild enough to sit out on piazza without wraps, as we did before storm came up.
