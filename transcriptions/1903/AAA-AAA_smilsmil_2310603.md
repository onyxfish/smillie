## Left Page

### Fri. April 10, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - after bkft. wrote letter to Mrs. Thurber at Washington - It was almost 11 before I got to work at Studio on Borden plate - Katharine there with Ralph for models - At 12 I went with R. to Rogers, Peet & Co. where he bought two suits of clothes - I went to look for Decomp, the plaster cast man in 33d. St - but he has moved - I do not know where - Home to lunch at about 1.30 - to studio at 2.30 - worked awhile on B- plate - at 5 went to Clauseuis about more mounting boards - Home at 6 - Dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g. Charlie came in - Jim had a discussion with Bessie concerning the teachings of the Anglican Church \+ the proselytting purposes of Father Burgess of New Haven \+ Holsapple, a classmate of Jims - She is much wrought up over it thinking that Jim has been hopelessly ensnared by them - Jim seems to be improving physically \+ has been out a large part of the day -

B. 3 h.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 11, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - with rain at night - Got to studio at 10 o'clk. Worked on Borden plate till after 12 - Then went to Clauseuis to see about mounting board - then went into Durand-Ruel's to see some paintings there \+ home to lunch about 1.30 - At 2.30 Ralph \+ I at Mad. Sq. Garden Theatre \+ saw the Fifteenth Cent. Morality Play "Every man", the Title part being taken by Edith Wynne Matthew-son (or some such name) - a very interesting reproduction by arrangement with the Elizabethan Stage Socy of London - It is getting to be quite a fad here now - no piece of certain whimsicalness I found it impressive - Every man carries the honors - We got out at 4.30 \+ walked home - There I had a rest before dinner - Eve'g R- \+ I went to the Century (Jim walked up Fifth Ave. with us) where we heard Prof. Putnam discourse about the ancient mound builders of the Ohio Valley - for nearly two hours he talked continuously - some of the time interestingly - He showed a great number of pictures on a screen - The effort would have gained much by condensation - I met D. Wm Carnalt of New Haven, there - he sat with Gale behind me \+ found refuge in sleep during a part of the lecture - We got out at 11 \+ hurried home thro' a light rain - that became very heavy soon after we got in.

Prof. Fred's Ward-Putnam - on Ancient Earth works of the Ohio Valley \+ the art of the Builders.

B. 2 h.
