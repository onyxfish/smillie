## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 22, 1903

**Weather:** Wea.  **Temp:** Ther.

In bed all day \-

A lovely day, bright + warm with strong S.W. breeze. I had trouble during the night with my bowels + decided that the safest place for me w. be in my bed + there I stayed. When F.S. Lombard came to see Kate about noon I had him come in to see me \- says the trouble is an inflamed condition of the mucus membrane of the Colon + prescribed a combination of Salol, Seltyd + Bismuth \- every 3 hours, which Jim got for me at the hotel later. He disapproves of Castor Oil very decidedly. Ferd Beede called on me later. The barrel with electric + telephone + supplies was delivered this mf + unpacked by Ralph. During the p.m. I began to improve.

Kate was put in my big folding chair + spent an hour + a half on the upper piazza.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 23, 1903

**Weather:** Wea.  **Temp:** Ther.

A very fine day. After a quiet night I got up + had bkft. about 9 o'clk. I moved about cautiously, fearful of a return of bowel trouble \- but the improvement continued. I wandered over the place + almost over to the hotel + then back to dinner at 1.30. I wrote a short good bye note to Charl, who is to sail on Tues. mf. The day passed without anything being accomplished. In the evef we were surprised by a call from Wm + Katie Selkin who had driven over from their quarters on the East side of Giant + had taken a room at St. Hubert's for the night.

Kate got into her chair on upper piazza without difficulty + spent more than six hours there. The D. came once to-day.
