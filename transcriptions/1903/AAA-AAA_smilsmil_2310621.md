## Left Page

### Sat. May 16, 1903

Emma Bourne rel. to Po'k.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - cooler air - After bkfst. went to Wanamaker's \+ bought shirts \+ collars - then down to Kimmel \+ Voigt's - Canal St. \+ got some pp. on white paper for Etching Class plates (for "Brush \+ Pencil") \+ a few of the Zinc plates - then to Studio at a little after 11 \+ worked on Borden plates till 1 o'clk. - then home to lunch - Jane \+ Mary Hinshelwood took lunch with us. All preparations made for Emma B. - to start for her home on the 3.30 train. Cab came for her at 3 - chairs at station to take her to car - Lizzie \+ Jessie Smillie there to accompany her to Po'k - Ed. Parker to meet her at train there. She is extremely weak but free from pain. I got to studio at 2.30 \+ worked on B-plate till 5.30 - then wandered in the streets till 6.30 - then home to dinner, Stupid every - all hands appeared to be very sleepy \+ went to bed early - Jim went to the Cooks but they were all going out \+ in sheer desperation he came home \+ went to bed - I lay on my bed till 10.30 then got up \+ wrote this.

Vondrous called at Studio in after noon.

B \- 4½ h.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 17, 1903

Jim rel. to New Haven.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still dry - I got up with a headache that made me quite useless all the mng. After bkfst. I was on my bed till 2 o'clk. getting considerable sleep \+ feeling much better - ate some dinner. After 3 o'clk. Jim \+ I rode up the Boulevard to foot of 130th St. - Fort Lee Ferry - then we climbed to the viaduct \+ walked north \+ onto the Boulevard - then South to Columbia College \+ spent some time looking at the grounds \+ buildings - then to cars \+ got home at 6. At 8 I went with Jim to Gr. Cent. Station \+ saw him started for N.H. - then to Charl's \+ got correct time - then home \+ wrote to W. Jno. Howard Brown \+ to Chas. Moody.
