## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 2, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Emma Bourne lunched with us

A delightful day. To studio about 9.30
\+ commenced the second painting on
x — wet effect — cumulous cloud +
rising moon. Alice Ireland called
to consult with me about varnishing the
portrait of old Geo. Ireland. At 12.30 I
walked on Park Ave. — home to lunch at 1.
Mauda + Christine had left — Jane Danks +
Katy Bradley were in their places. Emma
Bourne lunched with us — on her way to
New Haven. To studio at 2.30 + continued
painting — Vondrous called with some
sketches for etching — a story of a club of
young Art Students + a request for permission to use printing press at N.A.of D. At
about 5 I went to Brooks Bros. + bought
a suit of clothes — on my way up 5th Ave.
met bro. Geo. + we walked up together.
Home to dinner at 6.30 — Evet with
newspaper + loafing.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 3, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still charming weather — To studio before
10 + continued painting on x till 12 o'clk
then to polling place on Sixth Ave. bet 37 + 38
St. + deposited my straight Citizen's Union
vote + vote for canal enlargement. Then home
— lunch at 1 — back to studio — painted till
4.30 — cleaned up — home at 5 — Rested till
6 — dinner at 6.30 — Evet reading till 9.
Then to Century to learn how the election
had gone — was disgusted on finding that
it was all Tammany. Then I walked
down Fifth Ave. to Mad. Sq. — Charming
moonlight night — balmy + luminous —
great crowds of people swarming the
streets making all sorts of hidious noises
— mostly with tin hornes — masses of
people in the open space, 23d St. + Fifth Ave
— I was ashamed of my city + sick of
my kind. Home at 11.
