## Left Page

### Mon. March 9, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty foggy wet day — to studio at 10 and continued biting the Borden plate, at times new work over the old — I cut a monot for the proof that Dr. Lustgarten bought on Sat. \+ then went to Durand-Ruel galleries \+ saw a sell? of Cecelia Beaux's Portraits, which to me are disappointing \+ not up to her level — met Edna Cook there — on going out it was raining heavily — she had no umbrella — I had \+ escorted her home — then to lunch — back to studio at 2 — worked till [illegible] — home at 6 — dinner — Eve \+ wrote to Dr. Lustgartner \+ to Mr. Patter Palmer of Chicago who has written me enquiring prices of etched portraits — At 10.30 mailed letters \+ as it had stopped raining I walked a mile \+ a half on Park Ave — then to bed.

B. 4 h.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 10, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Carlson.

All day the weather tried to clear without brilliant success — there was still considerable fog in the air \+ a bit of rawness — otherwise it was reasonably pleasant. To studio at 10 — handicapped by a headache — made slow progress with my biting — no doubt using up much time quite uselessly — At 12 went to Clauson's — then to Knoedler's \+ saw the Chase Portraits — met him \+ Geo. Hearne there — During the few minutes I talked with Chase I was much impressed by his evident nervous \+ physical energy — it made me sick for myself — With an engine in good order running under such pressure it is not surprising that he turns out such work as he does — then I went to the Am. Art galleries \+ saw the Clarence King collection combined with two others — Home to lunch at 1.30 — to studio at 2.30 \+ worked till 4 — then I walked up to 656 Madison Ave — above 60° St. to Dr. Lustgartens \+ left pf. that he bought on Sat. then to Ga.'s studio, or. 60° St — Nellie was there painting — met her sister, Mrs. Swift. All was feeling out of sorts — he went out with me \+ we walked in Central Park till 6.15 — met his son Gordon as we came out — I got home before 6.30 — dinner — Charl came in later — Carlson came at usual time —

Elizabeth went to New Haven in the afternoon to visit Katie Elkins.
B. 3½ h.
