## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 29, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold blustering m'g, that as the day advanced softened + grew more benignant. a night of splendid moon light followed. Bkfp. late - everything cold - every body cross - the servants were out last night till 1 o'clk this m'g. I kindled fire in my studio - then in garden then wrote letters + commenced picking up things preparatory to leaving for town. Went to hotel at noon - got satisfactory note from Ralph at Troy. Dinner after 1. Mary gave out again + went to bed. I worked about Studio, did some painting on front Piazza etc - The Maghees all came over to 4 o'clk. Tea - I went to hotel + got overcoat that Ralph wore away yesterday - Rested from 5.30 to 6.30 Supper - Ev'ey wrote letter to Emma Ireland - learned to-day that Sam'ls Coleman married his trained nurse at Newport about a week ago.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 30, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful mild, sunny day - autumnal color wonderful - After bkfp. - in garden - another bigness of corn - "Country Gentleman" very fine. Then to Studio + finished along letter to Emma Ireland. At noon. to hotel + got mail - Mary got up about the middle of the m'g. + altho' she kept up all day she was very quiet. Dinner after 1 - after dinner it was so mild that I sat on studio balcony + made study sketch of a pine tree - About 3 o'clk. feeling the need of a walk - I went over Windy brow to Wes. Otis' + thence up trail to top of Roaring Brook fall - the walk thro' the woods brought back vividly memories of autumns many years ago. The outlook from top of fall was gorgeous - from there I climbed down the face of cliff to a new trail to basin half way down the fall - I found it quite interesting + followed it out to the old trail - on my return stopped at Mr. Weston's house closed - they left some days ago - then to Prof. Adler - found him at home - we had a talk about NY's municipal elections + the prospects - He seems to be more hopeful than I am - He walked with me to my gate. I had a little rest before supper. Ev'ey a quiet time with my newspapers.
