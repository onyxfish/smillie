## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 12, 1903

**Weather:** Pleasant day - clouded p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - clouded p.m. - After doing regular marketing I went to Portrait show at Am. Art Ass'n. Galleries - To-morrow will be last day. I wished to take last look at the Van Dyks - the Villiers belonging to W.C. Whitney is, to my mind, one of the noblest of portraits, as a painting - It was near noon when I got back to studio - painted on foreground of Passing Showers till 1.70 - Then home to lunch - Charl gave his Boston Symphony tickets for this p.m. to Jane \+ Mary H. - They went to concert - I went to N.A. of D. \+ met but 3 members of my class, which drew from model this m'f. Scholl, Aronson \+ Mrs. Levy - I worked on plates by Mr. d. \+ A — left before 5 \+ went to studio - cleaned palette etc - Then home a little before 6 - dinner at 6.30 - Every my head troubled me so that I did not go to Century as I had intend ed - went to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 13, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wind \+ Rain in the m'f - Till near noon - Then it cleared rapidly to a bright winter day - Up before 8 \+ had my Sunday m'f grooming - Bkfs'- before 10 \+ a loaf with newspapers till 12 - Then to studio \+ opened windows - Then, after a short walk, to Charl's - found him at bkfs'- sat there till 2 - Then home to dinner - most of the afternoon given to a nap - At 5.30 went to studio \+ closed windows - Then walked down to the Irelands 2.17th St.- took tea with them \+ spent the evening - later Mr. Kipling \+ her daughter Lillie came in \+ there was a chatty time till 9.30 when they left. I stayed till 10 - Then walked home.
