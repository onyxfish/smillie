## Left Page

### Sun. March 1, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A gusty, cloudy, rather cold day with some pleasant sunshine — Did not get up till near 9, feeling rather stale. I went thro' my usual bathing & rubbing. While still at it D.P. came to say that he had been unable to see Dr. Ogden last night & so, was unable to give the preliminary report concerning stomach contents as he had expected — I must now wait till Tues. for full report — As he stayed for but a moment & asked no questions & gave no advice I am wondering if the call will be counted as a visit. Breakfast about 10 — It was after 11 when I went to studio & opened windows for ventilation — then I walked on Park Ave. — counting blocks — until I had walked 2½ miles — then home & at 2 had dinner — Wrote a note to D. Celley telling him of the stomach washing incident. To studio at 4 & closed windows & then walked 2 miles on Park Ave. Then home & had a little nap before 6 — 4s\. & Charl came in & I saw them before tea at 6.30 — Eve'g reading

Dr. Putnam one visit?

## Right Page

### Mon. March 2, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, rather windy & rather cold. I got a late start in the m'g & did not get bkfst till 9 o'clk. After that I wrote till near 11 — then to studio. I worked for awhile at making new a short time varnish & mending leaky sink — then to bank & made a deposit. On my way stopped at Calverley's and had a visit with him — Home to lunch at 1 & to studio soon after 2. Continued m'g work till 4 — then went to D. H. Allen's "Ausonia." He had lower set of teeth ready for me — he tinkered & filled at them for some time — at last he declared them to be a perfect fit & I left feeling as though I had a mouth full of oyster shells — walked down thro' the Park — home at 6 — Dinner — a dismal function for me — at 6.30 Eve'g — after my newspaper I wrote for some time trying to evolve a set of Rules & regulations for the N.A. of D. Etching Class.
