## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day — high wind \+ heavy rain throughout the mt holding up in the afternoon \+ clearing at night — I had my bkfs. at 10 \+ loafed away the rest of the mt. Dinner at 2 — \+ loafed away the rest of the afternoon — my excuse being a stupid sort of headache — no supper — We" wrote to Scott Brown — D. A. Adams — Clarence Stirling \+ wrote checks settling several accts — for a while in parlor with the boys — then out posted letters \+ had a little promenade on Park Ave.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded, but pleasant — to Studio at 10 and to work on 16×20" Cumulus, "Evening" — I practically repainted all but the field of blue \+ the light part of the thunderhead, changing the color for the better, I think. After 12, went to 2d Nat. Bk, then home to lunch \+ at 2 back to studio \+ worked as long as I could see — a prowl in the streets \+ home soon after 5 — had a rest before dinner — after that, at 8 o'clk. with the boys, went to Charlie's — found that he was entertaining his friend Mr. Tom Adams with whom I will not associate — so we set? home — Jim then read aloud to us a skit, "The Green Knight" written by his Prof. in English. Later, Ralph suffering from a cold, was put to bed after treatment — warm bath — hot lemonade \+ whiskey \+ 6 grs. of Quinine —

Rec'd. an interesting letter from Bessie — at Hamburg.
