## Left Page

### Mon. June 1, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — Early in the m'f I took a dose of Citrate of Magnesia \+ it cleaned me out — But left me feeling limp \+ wretched — I got to studio about 10 but was unable to work — I went to 2° Nat'l. \+ deposited checks \+ coupons \+ mailed to Mackellar a check for $400, paymt. for monument. \+ then home before 1 \+ again gave up to my headache \+ spent the afternoon till near 6 on my bed where I got up feeling better — Mr. Maghee came in — on his way to G't. Cent. Station to take night train for West. Port — He has terrible stories to tell of forest fires very near to St. Huberts \+ the Ark — he says that every man in the district is out fighting them. Mary's plan has been to go to Keene on Thurs. but now she postpones that date till she can hear from Mr. Maghee who is to write to us at once after he gets to Keene — Mr. Young dined with us — Bessie was able to do her share of entertainment in spite of the fact that this m'f she had a Wisdom tooth extracted — It was just coming through \+ has given her much pain.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 2, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool \+ bright — Went to Charl's after bkf't. \+ got large printing frame for photos. Then to Studio at 10 — Miller waiting for me — To consider repairs on stair — then to work on Borden plate till 12 — then to 2° Nat'l. Bank — Home to lunch at 1 — to studio at 2.30 \+ worked on plate till near 6 Then walked up town with Ge°. to 57° S't. — home at 6.45 — dinner — Mr. Young with us — Sue \+ Charl came in \+ to Mary \+ the girls (by previous appointment) to Mad. Sq. Garden — A uss' entertainment, "Venice" — I remained at home with newspaper \+ accts.

B — 5h.
