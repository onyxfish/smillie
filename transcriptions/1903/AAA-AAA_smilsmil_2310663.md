## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 10, 1903

**Weather:** Brilliant m'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant m'g. until nearnoon then it was clouded till towards ev'g when again it came off clear & cold. I was engaged most of the m'g printing from some films develafsed by Jim from snapshots while on his camping trip — nothing very good or very interesting. At noon went to hotel for mail — then dinner. Afternoon I tried to work in studio but my head troubled me so much that I gave up to it at last \+ went to bed for an hour or more to Mr. White's at 6 to secure some old iron pipe for my telephone conduit — then to hotel \+ to Scott Brown's \+ back to supper. Ev'g Dr. Elkin surprised us — he had been to camp on the upper lake with Katie \+ Mollie \+ others, but preferring a bed in-doors he had left them \+ came down to the hotel — he spent the ev'g with us — Mr. Maghee \+ Catharine Hardenburgh came in for a tim

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 11, 1903

**Weather:** Heavy rain early  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain early in the m'g with some thunder — other showers during the day \+ now \+ then thunder — warm \+ rather muggy — clear at night. I kindled fire in studio intending to put in a days work there but other things interfered — about 10.30 I went over to Mr. Maghee's meeting Wm. Elkin on his buckboard on his way up to the house) \+ spent an hour looking over back nos f "The Studio" for an idea for pyro-work on panels for my bed room mantle — met the Misses MacIntosh there — At noon went to hotel \+ got meat for dinner \+ home at 1 — dinner at 1.30. Wm. Elkin dined with us — afternoon I again went to Maghee's \+ looked at more "Studios" but did not find what I wanted — Home to supper. Ev'g Ralph \+ I in studio — he drew to scale the fire place \+ chimney breast of main room forme to use in planning to finish. For a time we were amused by the singing of darkie songs by M. Young.
