## Left Page

### Mon. April 6, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fairly pleasant day — filmy over-head + milder — up at 4 — again at 7 — my condition discouraging — went to studio + was able to work for about an hour — then home + lay on my bed — After lunch D. P. came in. He says that Jim is so much better that he will not call again — as to my case he says he has made a mistake in prescribing Asafadita — he now says stop it — After 3 o'clk I felt well enough to go to studio + worked on Borden plate till 6 o'clk — then home after a short walk on Park Ave — Dinner — Ever quietly — in front of parlor fire.

Dr. P. once.
B. plate 3 hours.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 7, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm, rainy day — To studio about 10 — laid ground over Borden plate filling in all the lines — then went to 2° Nat. Bank + made deposit — back to studio + until near 1 o'clk, opening lines by drawing point thro' them to rebite — Home to lunch — back at 2.30 — very dark after-noon, so that I did not work more than a couple of hours. The rest of the time I spent in taking proofs from Father's In memoriam scrap book + preparing to mount them between boards — worked at that till near 6 — then I walked on Park Ave — the rain having ceased for a time — In at 6.30 — dinner. Eve'g. Charle came in — I was with Jim a part of the time.

B. plate 3 h.
