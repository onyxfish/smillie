## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 8, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — I began it by taking a quantity of Citrate of Magnesia \+ was kept fairly busy all day. I had my bkfst. at 9 — Read till after 12. Then out \+ sauntered in the sunshine on Park Ave. Met W. \+ Mrs. Sidney Smith, walked home with them \+ had a chat at their doorstep. Dinner at 2. Read "Recollections" R.H. Stoddard \+ put out from 4 to 6 lost in a nap — supper. Eve'g went to Walter Cook's \+ saw the family — Sallie \+ her husband there — home at 9 — bro. Geo. there — Resumed my reading of Recollections to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 9, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another lovely day. To Studio at 9.30 \+ tacked Vesuvius — it was a hard all-day stand-up fight — \+ I came out of it pretty well licked — I painted again \+ over sky-distance \+ sea but did not get within gunshot of what I wished — I could not get the colors or tones that I wished — it all looked to me horribly poor \+ amateurish. home to lunch from 1.20 to 2.30 — worked till 4.30 — then to barber's \+ had my hair cut — a short walk on Park Ave. home at 5.30 — Emma Ireland there — would not stay to dinner — Eve'g loafed over paper till 9 o'clk — washed brushes.
