## Left Page

### Wed. June 17, 1903

**Weather:** overcast & cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph Ret'd from Hackley.

overcast & cool — light showers in the afternoon & eve'g. The first Tribulation was the breaking down of Severina, the laundress — seemed to be state of hysteria — decided that she cannot go to Keene. I went shopping — bought suit of clothes underclothes at Brooks — under clothes at Arnold & Constable. Rattau for mending chairs — & home to lunch at 1. Ralph home from school — detained with others because of the misbehavior fore of the graduating class who hoisted a Thundermug on the weather signal. Afternoon I attended to many things — went to Hinton's with dark Room lantern — dinner at 6.30 Eve'g. I went to G't Cent. St'n — Got R.R. Tickets & had baggage checked for the party to-morrow. I had an interview with Severina & she frankly told me that she had been in some sort of an asylum for 13 mo's but declined that nothing was the matter with her — from her talk I was satisfied that she is still & somewhat demented. In answer to my questions Christine Told me of some of Severina's queer sayings & actions — She thinks her misfortunes are all tracable to a witch in Sweden whose malign influence still controls her here.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 18, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary & servants started for the Ark. Jim came from New-Haven.

Gentle rain all night — when I went to bed I was uncomfortable — increasing till 12 when I had a paroxysm of vomiting followed by purging — at 1 o'clk. I had a second distration & at 3 o'clk. a third — after which there was very little of me left — during the rest of the night there was more or less discomfort & when morning came I was unable to get up. Mary & her girls and Blenda & Christine got away on the 8.45 train. Jim surprised me by coming in from New Haven at about 10 a.m. I spent the day in bed suffering from a severe headache. Kate Leonard came & will stay & provide for the boys & I leave — After a talk with Jim it was decided that he should return to New-Haven, pack up & come back here on Monday next. Jim \+ Ralph dined with Charles & he came in for awhile later in the eve'g.
