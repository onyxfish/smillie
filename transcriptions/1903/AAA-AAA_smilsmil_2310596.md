## Left Page

### Fri. March 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day — I got to studio before 10 \+ worked on drawn plate till 12.30 — a walk on Park Ave. \+ home to lunch — back to studio at 2 — worked till 3 — then Carlson came \+ gave me a half hour — then worked till 4 \+ home — not feeling equal to a tramp I commenced writing a letter to Ralph — Mr Geo. F. Cummings called \+ introduced himself as a son of Thos. Sears Cummings \+ said that he had been up as a candidate for membership in Century for two years \+ wished to know why he didn't get in — the fact that he was a son of T.S.C. — the founder of The Sketch Club — the parent of the Century to his mind seemed to cover every thing — he took nearly two hours of my time. Dinner at 6.30 — Evelf. \+ Nellie came — also Mr. [illegible] who practiced singing some music to Bessie's accompaniment on piano. I finished letter to Ralph \+ wrote one to Jim — Out at 10 — mailed letters — had a short walk then in \+ to bed.

B. H.h.
C. — 1/2.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 28, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant m'g — overcast afternoon with threats of rain — sudden change at night — colder with wind \+ rain —
I feel too much out of sorts to think of work — I went to studio for a while — then to Walter Cook's office in 29 "S": \+ looked over some ill. books on Wood Carvings — to select some bits for Keene house — then to barber's — had hair cut \+ shampoo — home to lunch at — afternoon, I went to Allen about my teeth — then to Soc'y. of Society of Am. Artists — met Mr. \+ Mrs. H.R. Butler there — invited to dine with them next Wed. Home at 6 very tired — After dinner trouble with my bowels began — Carlson came in the eve'g — gave me general treatment, avoiding the abdomen.

C. \+ t.
