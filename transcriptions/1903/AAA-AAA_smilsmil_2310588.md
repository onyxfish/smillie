## Left Page

### Wed. March 11, 1903

**Weather:** Rain during the m'f — clearing during the afternoon, fine moonlight night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the m'f — clearing during the afternoon, fine moonlight night. Got up with a head ache that continued until late in the afternoon, when it seemed to clear away with the storm. Clarence Smith called at the house at about 10 AM. \+ left my new will which I am to copy in Auto graph — Katharine went to studio at 9 to meet her model. I got there at 10.30 \+ continued the biting of Border plates — Home to lunch at 1 — back after 2 — finished biting \+ cleaned plate — not altogether successful — some of the lines have bitten rough on portions of the flat tint work where the biting is rather strong some lines have not bitten as strong as others, as though the point had cut differently — a very unusual result in my experience — worked on plate with graver till 5.30 — then to Clauseus — then walked up 5th Ave. to 50th St — then home at 6.30 — dinner — Ever Connected with newspaper till 9.30 — Then wrote for awhile on a biographical sketch of father for a Boston publishing house.

B. 5h.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 12, 1903

**Weather:** Mild, pleasant spring-like day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild, pleasant spring-like day — Got to studio at 10 — Katharine ahead of me again with Model. I worked with graver till near 1 o'clk — then a promenade in Park Ave. Till 1.15 — lunch — back to studio at 2.15 \+ graver again till about 4 — made preparations for printing to-morrow — went to Dr. H. D. Alee's at the Ansonia \+ by filing away some portions of under set of teeth he greatly added to my comfort. — Walked down thro' the Park \+ so home — a little more than two miles — getting in after 6 \+ somewhat warm \+ tired — dinner — news paper. — Carlson —

B. 5h
