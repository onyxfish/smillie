## Left Page

### Mon. March 23, 1903

*Miss Taylor ret'd. to Polk —*
*Carlson —*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beastly dark, foggy, wet day. To make it pleasanter indoors Joe had a big fire in studio furnace \+ my Ther. up to 82° when I got there. I had to work all day with often windows I worked from 10 to 1 on Borden plates — Home to lunch \+ back at 2 — worked Till ten min. of 6 — a long day — Mrs. Potter Palmer called in the afternoon about etched portrait of her husband. It is wanted in a hurry — so, I cannot do it. The weather was so persistently bad that had a very short week only — Dinner at 6.30 — with newspapers Till 8.30 — Carlson came at 9.5 \+ gave general treatment.

B. 7 h.
C. 1.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 24, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunny, foggy, warm on [illegible] — overcast p.m. heavy shower about 6 p.m. To studio before 10 \+ reworked for nearly two hours on Borden plate — then went to Am. Art Ass'n. \+ saw the Twachtman \+ Colman pictures \+ the Burritt etchings. Met Colman there — also Schilling \+ Carleton Chapman. It was 1.30 before I got home to lunch — \+ 2.30 when I got to studio. Carlson came \+ gave me a half hour of abdominal treatment. I worked about one hour on the Borden plates — then went to N. A. Allen's "the Ansonia" — B'dway \+ 74th St. He made some changes on lower teeth. Met one of Kate Parker Risley's daughters \+ her cousin Miss Dodd. Walked home thro' the Park. Stopped at Century \+ looked over letters to Com. on Admissions for Wilton Lockwood — also for Andrew Carnegie. Home at 6.30 — dinner. newspapers.

B. 3 h.
C. 1/2 — Since paymt. Feb. 26 —
