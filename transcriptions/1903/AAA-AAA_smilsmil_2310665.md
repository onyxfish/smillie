## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 14, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day altho' the middle part of it was clouded. The girls, Frances Payler \+ Fred. Young went to the Lower Pond. I spent the day there. I had a number of things to look after — Two teams of Fred. Reede's were hauling gravel from the River bed to the public road outside my gate — but at 11 Jim, Ralph \+ I started for the "Hubble" on Giant. We stopped for a few min. at the top of Roaring Brook Fall — then on over the giant trail to the Washbowl trail, \+ by a new \+ unworn trail we passed north \+ east of the Hubble, skirting the burned part till we got to the tops at 1.30 — we spent a delightful hour \+ a half in the Rock \+ enjoyed our lunch of coffee, hash, cheese \+ marmalade sandwiches immensely — at 3 o'clk we started down, going over the old trail to Washbowl, all new to me — some of it extremely wild — from the washbowl down to the Road — most of the way thro' burned wreckage of forest — to the Road at 4.20 \+ home at 5.30. I feel surprisingly well — had a sponge bath \+ rub down \+ at 6.15 lay down on my bed \+ Rested till when we had dinner — Aunt I Rolled myself in my Rugs on lounging chair \+ Rested again till 9.30 — there is for a while \+ to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 15, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate seriously ill.

Glorious day — Katharine came to my door at 6 A.M. saying that Kate was in great pain \+ wanted a doctor. I went to Moody, waked him \+ sent him to hotel for Dr. Lombard who came about 7.30. He said she was very seriously ill — a weak, arsenic heart — that there was an element of Angina pec. in the pain she was suffering — Blenda \+ Christine overslept themselves \+ it was 8.30 before we got our bkft. This was pretty hard on those of us who had been up since 6. The medicine left by Dr. L. could not be kept on K's stomach — she had several vomiting spells which greatly weakened her — at 10 I went after the Dr. \+ found him on the golf links — he directed that the remedies begin as of Enema — He came again to the house at about noon \+ found K. was worse again \+ said that she was in an extremely precarious condition — we arranged that she should have remedies \+ nutriment thro' the bowels \+ said that she must have a trained nurse — Miss May who is here, a friend, Nurse is a daughter of Dr. Dubois's — who has long been an invalid was induced to come to meet an emergency — She came after the Dr.'s third visit after 4 P.M. The Dr. came again about 8 P.M. \+ Reported that she was greatly improved since his last visit — Miss Maze was fixed in the hall way on my folding chair — one of Reede's teams hauled gravel for me all day — Moody helping much of the time. Jim \+ Ralph went up to North Mark in the M't — I went down to the Country Club in the afternoon — Jim went over to the hotel in the evng.
