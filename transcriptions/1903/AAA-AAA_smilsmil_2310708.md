## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 6, 1903

**Weather:** Bright along in the afternoon, after a cloudy, breezy  **Temp:** rather cold m[illegible]

Bright along in the afternoon, after a cloudy, breezy \+ rather cold m[illegible]. To studio before 10 \- got out old painting 33×44" \- resuvius from near Sorrento \- was amazed on seeing what a poor, colorless thing it is \- set to work at it \+ scubbed in a new sky \+ dropped all the distance about three inches \- Home to lunch \- The girls (K. \+ E.) went to Annapolis by m[illegible] train to visit Emily Taylor Kellogg for a few days \- To studio again \+ painted till 4 o'clk \- cleaned up \+ home at 5 \- Rested till 6 \- then wrote part of letter to Ralph \- dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g finished Ralph's letter \+ wrote one to Jim \- At 8 o'clk. went to Bormann's \+ got R's wheel with its new "coaster brake \- At 10. went to [illegible] C.S. \+ mailed letter.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 7, 1903

**Weather:** Bright, breezy \+ quite cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, breezy \+ quite cold \- up late to bkfs'. head troubling me \- added to that, some symptoms of influenza \- wrote a letter to C.S. Farrington \- Response to his of Oct. 6" \- telling him that Representative of Am. Surety Co. had called on me \- but when he told me that he had information concerning C.S.F's dishonest practices it [illegible] I told him that I did not need to tell him anything as proof was on record, available if the Co. desired it. I added a few remarks that could not be misconstrued \- To studio at 10 \+ worked on Vesuvius rubbing in strong glazes over the whole thing. Home to lunch at 1 \- two long \+ very interesting letters from Charl \- To bank before 3 \- then to studio \- worked a little while \- home at 4 \- in bed till 6. Eve'g did not feel well enough to go to Century. After going to Borman's \+ the druggist's I stayed at home \+ read \+ took Calomel Tablets.
