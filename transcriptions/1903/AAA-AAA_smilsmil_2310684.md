## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 19, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. went down to 32° last night on piazza outside my window — A brilliant, cloudless day, delightful in temperature —
After bkfst. I went to garden \+ gathered a fine basket ful of corn (26 ears) Wax beans, the last of the season — lettuce, the last of the season, Chard, carrots \+ summer squash. The frost of last night does not seem to have done any damage in killing. Of course it retards the ripening of corn \+ other vegetables — The wax beans are now too old \+ the later plantings are not yet ready for table — The lettuce has given out — no more planted. The last sowing of Champs. of England peas is now in fine flower, pods setting but they will not mature. Chard still growing — After garden work I crawled under the house \+ fixed up electric wire running there — then for some time changing place of electric bell in upper hall — then a little sunning — then dinner — afternoon I was so much out of sorts that I did not attempt to work but wandered over the hill looking for young balsams to transplant. The catarrhal trouble in my bowels is causing me much inconvenience. Schilling \+ Miss Goldmark called late in the afternoon — Supper at 6.30 Evell. I had fire in my bed room \+ sat there

Otis \+ Leane did not work to-day for me

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful cloudless day — Bkfst at 9. After that the medicine I took last night gave me a bad quarter of an hour two or three times over, leaving me very seedy — \+ very nearly used up with a headache. I managed to gather a big basket full of corn — then I raked up pine needles on new seeded ground in front of the house \+ at noon I went to Scott Brown's \+ gave him all my garden corn \+ other vegetables that may be good for anything after I leave here — Then to hotel, got mail \+ home to dinner. After that I rallied \+ later went to Harry Beedle's \+ then to the iron bridge. Supper before 7 — Evell., Jim preparing for his departure early to-morrow mg.
