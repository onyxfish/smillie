## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 7, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fairly pleasant day — In studio at 10 \+ worked on Borden plate till 1, then to lunch \+ back after 2 \+ continued work on etching till after 4 — then I gave up tired — I walked on Park Ave. awhile then home \+ had a rest till dinner — Eve'g went to Century — monthly meeting — had a pleasant eve'g — a long talk with J. Q. A. Ward about Paul Bartlett, the sculptor, nominated by Ward \+ passed for non-resident membership at the last meeting of Com. on Admissions — W— is an enthusiastic admirer of Bartlett \+ very indignantly denied several stories current to the disadvantage of B.        Home at midnight —

I delivered Charlie's painting, "A Back Road" — at his house.

B. 5 h.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 8, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet day — heavy rain during the m'g — clearing at night — After my Sunday m'g work had my help'. at 10 — loafing about the house till dinner at 2 — \+ sleeping from 4 to 6 — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g Geo. came in — at 7.30 I went to Ed.W. Cook's — about an hour — arranged for meeting of Ireland Real Estate Co. for Wed. eve'g — At 9.15 Carlson came \+ gave me mass— age till 10.30.
