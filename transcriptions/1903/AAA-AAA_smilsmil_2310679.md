## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 11, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very brilliantly beautiful day - my headache \+ collapsed feeling still clings to me. After bkf. I spent some time in garden, gathering a mess of corn, the second this season. I wrote to Peter Radeker, at Montrose, Pa., for the cold frame sashes that Mr. Kent is willing to let me have. I went to hotel before noon got mail - worried Tracy about freight that I cannot get from Westport - gave Dr. Lombard check in paymt. of his bill ago. Kate \+ me - then home - dinner - \+ did a little photo. printing \+ finally gave up to my headache \+ was on my bed till nearly 6 - then up - out to see what Otis \+ Moody have accomplished on my road to-day - Mr. Howell with me. Supper - Evelf. arranged light \+ cushions comfortably in bay-window of main room \+ read. \-

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 12, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, but rather too warm for comfort. I spent the usual time in garden after bkf. To hotel for mail \+ while there spent some time \+ money trying to arrange by telephone for transfer of Kate's R.R. seat from Thurs. to Friday, so that she may go to Pok with the Misses Woodward. I wrote to Ryan about the Electric Batteries, unpacked them m.f. Two of the four jars are broken \+ so is the bell \- also, wrote to Radeker of Montrose for the cold-frame sashes that Mr. Kent says I may have. After dinner again went to hotel \+ worried \+ waited, using up a considerable portion of the afternoon, trying to get Curtis, the ticket agent at Plattsburg - but had to give it up. Advised to try again about 7 P.M. After supper I again went to telephone \+ some one in the Plattsburg ticket office (not Curtis) told me (so I understood him) that Kate's seat had been changed to Friday - N.E. next to the Misses Woodward - Then I went to Highland Cottage \+ saw the Misses W - then home - Evelf. reading.
