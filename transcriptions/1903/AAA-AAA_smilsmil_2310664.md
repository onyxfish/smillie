## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 12, 1903

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy - Streaks of sunshine & then mizzles of light rain - altogether, a disagreeable day. I was with Trumbull much of the day superintending the putting on of finish on my bed room mantel piece. Beede got to work hauling sand onto Public Road near entrance to my gate, to fill a mud hole where I had told him to put stone & gravel - he insisted that he was doing as he had been told \+ we had some heated words - he finally cooled off & said he had been hasty - I went to Maghee & got permission to have stone \+ gravel from the River bed on his place. Then to hotel for mail - Afternoon, Trumbull finished my bedroom. Dr. Elkin came over in the mts. after all except Jim & Ralph had gone for a week. It took him a long time to decide that he did it know what to do \+ he went away saying he might come back to dinner but he did it. Eve. there was a "masked ball" at the Hotel for benefit of Leave Library & Macghee fitted out Katherine \+ Jim - K. in a Jap. costume \+ Jim turbaned \+ [illegible] in something oriental. The others remained at home - I read till 10 - Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 13, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - clouds & sunshine - Strong N.W. wind. Jim & Ralph went to the top of Giant \+ back about 5 p.m. Trumbull worked during the mt. only - \+ so did Beede. I was busy with the the two most of the time - went to hotel for mail before noon. Dinner a little before 2 - late because the girls, Miss Taylor & W. Young took a long walk - to Beaver Meadow \+ all \+ back by River Trail - West side. Afternoon I went out Chapelpee Pond Road & got a slight sketch of the cliffs to help me in finishing my painting "The Rise of the River" - Ret'd. by way of old [illegible] Road Trail which has been ruined by the fire - a sad wreck. Supper about 7 - Eve \+ loafing about. Young singing
