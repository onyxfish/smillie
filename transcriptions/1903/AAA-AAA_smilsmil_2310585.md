## Left Page

### Thur. March 5, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — a little rain about the middle of the day — To studio at 9.30 and continued the biting of the Borden plate — Home to lunch — worked in the afternoon till about 5 o'clk. Then went to Payesdorfer \+ got my watch — Then to Clausen's about mounting boards — Then home. Dinner at 6.30 — Mr. Young, a young man engaged to a Po'K. friend of the Leo's K. \+ L. dined with us — he is studying for the ministry \+ sings very agreeably — Carlson came soon after 9.

Jane \+ Mary Hinshelwood lunched with us.

B. 5½ h.
C. 1

## Right Page

### Fri. March 6, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day — To studio at 10 \+ continued the biting of Borden plate — At 12.30 went to 2°. Nat. Bank — Home at 1.15 Mollie Dayre there to lunch — To Studio soon after 2 \+ worked on biting till near 5 — Then to Barber's \+ had my hair cut. Then to Mary Cook's to beg to be excused from buying a $3.00 set of Diet. Kitchen Concert tickets — Then home at 6 o'clk — dinner — Roelf. Charl came in — I wrote to Jim \+ Raefen.

B. 5 h.
