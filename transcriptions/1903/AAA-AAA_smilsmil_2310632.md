## Left Page

### Sun. June 7, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The blessed showers continued at intervals thro' the day — at times the rain fell quite heavily. but all together I do not think that much water fell. It was near 10 before I got my bkfs — Got a Telegram from Mr. Maghee saying that the Ark was as saf as anywhere, which was measurably assuring. Went to studio \+ spent some time touching upon pf. of B-Plate. Dinner at 2. Mr. Van Horn called about 3.30 \+ gave us some later \+ fuller information from Keene. I wrote a note to Maghee \+ mailed it at 9? Cent. Station at 5. Then I went to Tom Rowland's — 306 E. 34 S. \+ left message for him to call at Charles to-morrow m'g.
Early part of evef I gave way to drowsiness — from 9 till 11 I wrote notes.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 8, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, with light showers — a single clap of thunder about 6.30 P.M. Rain during the evef. To studio soon after 9 with scrub woman who worked till 1.30 scrubbing down stairs \+ hallway at studio. I worked on B-plate till near 11 — then to Scribners \+ showed proof to Mr. Smith who expressed himself as entirely satisfied. Then I saw Mr. Arthur Scribner. Stated the case of Miss Ellen Rose Giles \+ asked if there was any chance in his establishment for her as Proof-Reader etc — His answer was discouraging — then I went to Putnams \+ saw Bishop P— \+ presented the case of miss G — to him — He was entirely frank with me \+ was more discouraging than Mr. S — Then to studio just before 1 — detained by scrub woman \+ did not get home to lunch till 1.45 — back to studio at 2.30 \+ worked on B-plate till 5.30 — then walked in the streets till 6.30 — Home to dinner — Mr. Young dined with us \+ spent the evef.

B. 4 h.
