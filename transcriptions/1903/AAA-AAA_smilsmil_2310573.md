## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 9, 1903

**Weather:** Cold, windy + cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, windy + cloudy — I got to studio + put another day on Borden Place drawing the lines on the arched ceiling a beastly, mechanical job — I have made a mistake in attempting to make it an example of mechanical work — it is not worth the exertion — it will be an example of misapplied + useless ability — Home to lunch + back about 2 — worked on Place till 4 — then a little work on mounts for prints — then walked down to Wanamaker's \+ paid bill — then walked home — Bessie went to Po'k this afternoon — Dinner at 6.30 Eve'g. I wrote to D. Wm. L. Elkin — to Jim, sending him money — \+ to D. H. D. Aleen —

B. 4 h.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 10, 1903

**Weather:** A very lovely day, bright, quiet + mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day, bright, quiet + mild — I got up with a beast of a headache that stuck to me all day to such an extent that I was unable to work — At about 10 I went to Wm. S. Miller's — he took me to the old church old in 40th St. that he has been reconstructing. On the ground floor, in the rear, I found Thos. Hastings' (Mr.) carriage house + stable — the front part of the bldg. has been arranged into two studios with living apartments — one studio is immense + is occupied by a sculptor, Tonnetti [?] the other, smaller + higher up, is occupied by Will H. Low — I visited with him for more than half an hour — after 11 when I got to studio — until 1 ock. + cut mts + am'g? Etchings for mounting — lunch — at Studio till after 3 — Telephoned to Clarence Smith — then went down town — went to A.B.N. Co. + got from Freeland a caliper that he has been keeping for me — then to Saunthis \+ conferred with him about my Will — there had a half hour stroll on the Battery — then to studio at 5 — there till 6 — then home — dinner \+ my Carlson came.
