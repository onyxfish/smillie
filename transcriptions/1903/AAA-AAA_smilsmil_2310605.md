## Left Page

### Tues. April 14, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beast of a day — Rain & high wind
till eveg. To studio about 10 — worked
on Borden place till 1 — then home to
lunch — back at 2 \+ worked till
4.30 — then till 6 o'clk. on etching
mounts — as I had then stopped
Raining I tried to get a walk on
Park Ave. but the wind nearly
took me off my feet — dinner
at 6.30 — after 8 I went with R—
to Gr. Cent. Station — he ret'd. to
Hackley School on the 8.35 Train
\- then home — Chase came in.

B. 5 h.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 15, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another day almost as bad as yesterday —
Rain, driving wind, nizzle & mist — To studio
at about 10 \+ worked till 1.30 — then home
to lunch — continued work on Borden
place till 5 o'clk — from 2.30 — then, till
6.30 I worked at mounting old proofs of
father's plates — Home to dinner —
went to Gr. Cent. Station with Jim \+ he took
8 o'clk. train to New-Haven — then I ret'd.
home — Eveg. I wrote out a statement
of Maynard's acct. I told him this
mog. that I was going to divide his
studio, as originally planned, making
two of it, \+ that I would let him have
the eastern half — the other I proposed to
let bro. Geo. have — Maynard now
owes $900 back rent.

B. 5 h.
