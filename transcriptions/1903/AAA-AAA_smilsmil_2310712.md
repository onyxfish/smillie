## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 14, 1903

**Weather:** Rain in the m'f — clearing at noon clear & much cooler at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 & painted all the m'f on 14×18 — making radical changes in the sky — making it brighter & more simple. Home to lunch at 1.30 — 'gene Atwater there to lunch — back to studio — worked awhile on old painting — Hollyhocks: the original after which I made magazine plate. — Stretched it to fit a frame that I have. — Cleaned up before 5 — had a promenade on Park Ave. — then home — read paper till dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g I was much out of sorts — lame all over — feeling weak & much dejected — to bed at 8.30

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 15, 1903

**Weather:** A fine clear, cool & breezy day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfst. at 9 after bath & rub-down — Tribune till about 11 — then out & walked up & down Park Ave. & around till about 1.30 — then went into Charl's — got correct time & home to dinner at 2. — After that wrote a long letter to Catharine Elkins concerning her proposal to give Jim a coming-out dance at the Lawn Club, New-Haven, the week after Thanksgiving & defending myself ag'st an implication that I have been too close with Jim in money matters. At 5 o'clk. went to Ed.'s? Coaks — all out, but saw Mrs. Sprague in a pleasant half-hour visit. Then to the Irelands 331 E. 17 th St. — went thro' the motions of taking tea & spent the eve'g with them till 9 o'clk. — Mr. Valentine came at about 8.30 \& I left him there.
