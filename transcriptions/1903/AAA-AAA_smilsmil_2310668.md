## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouds \+ rain in the m'f — lovely afternoon — more rain at night. I had a beastly time last night. was wakened by some sort of nightmare bet'n 12 \+ 1 o'clk — heart beating like mad \+ I, scared at something, I did not know what. After nearly half an hour my heart was still running at 90 per min. by actual count. It was nearly an hour before it fell to 75. I had a second movement of the bowels — evidently, an indigestion — at 4 a.m. the whole experience was repeated with less violence. The discomfort in bowels continued but I had no further movements — but had bad headache — did not get out of bed all day — Young \+ Miss Taylor postponed their departure till to-morr[ow]

Kate continued to improve — D'. came but once.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant m'f. followed by a delightful day. We were aroused at 6 o'clk to get a 7 o'clk bkf'. so that Frances Taylor \+ Fred. Young could start before 7. 30 for West Port \+ Co'H. Tera Beede sent up Crawford \+ his team I felt very weak but hopeful after yesterdays experience. After the visitors got away I went down to the dining room \+ got some bkf'. Then out to garden \+ picked a large mess of peas. I moved about very carefully — Then did a little photo. printing — Jim \+ Ralph went to the top of Snow mt. Mr. Maghee took dinner with us. then took Eleg'. away somewhere — She did not return at night. Jim \+ Ralph went to Keene Centre on their wheels. my bowel trouble rec'. \+ I was again very uncomfortable. I managed to write a letter to Charl who is to sail for Europe next Tues. m'f.

Kate continued to improve. — D'. came once.
