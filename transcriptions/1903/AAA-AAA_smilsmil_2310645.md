## Left Page

### Fri. July 3, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim left us — started on camping expdn

Splendid day — brilliant, breezy + cool — Jim started at 9.30 for Keene Centre where he is to meet the Placid Stage — Spend the night at Placid + to-morrow at 2.30 meet Willie Cook + two others at Clear Lake Station + with them go on to Rainbow Lake which is to be their base of Supplies during a camping expedition which may last a month or six weeks, if their expectations are realized. — I worked awhile on drawing plans for my bed room mantel piece — then with Ralph to where Scott Brown + O'Fs were laying pipe to Neeson's — then to hotel — then to Wruch's — paid him — then home — Dinner — afternoon, some work out of doors — cutting away decayed wood from trunk of large pine tree south part of house — O's came + put together pipe that leads water down to garden — Tea at 6.45 — watered portions of garden — lettuce plants that I had set out etc. — Evef. in studio, Reading —

## Right Page

### Sat. July 4, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — but I was handicapped all day with more or less of a headache. I got up at 5.30 + started Ralph, with his rifle, after some crows that were in the garden — but he got nothing but an early start. I did little more than wander about in the mtf. Ralph + I went into the Reserve + went up the stream to the bathing dam constructed last Summer part of which has been carried away by recent floods. — most of the afternoon I was on my bed — Evef. all hands, except Kate + self, went to Maghee's. I read until about 10 — then, to bed.
