## Left Page

### Sat. April 18, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beautiful day - to studio at 10 \+ worked till 1 - on Borden plate, biting second etched work. A short stroll before lunch at home - back to studio \+ continued biting till nearly 6 o'clk - a short rest before dinner \+ after 8 p.m. went to Century \+ heard a lecture from Fred. E. Ives, on Color Photography up to Date. I did not learn as much as I had hoped to, but some of the demonstrations of effects of superimposed transparent colors on the screen were very remarkable - After the lecture I had conversations with different artist members concerning candidates before Adm. Com. for nomination - Home about 11.30.

B 6 h.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 19, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another mild \+ very pleasant day. Bkfp. at 10. Got to Bolton Jones' studio - No. Clinton, W. 42d. S., soon after 11 - Had along talk with him about Ben Foster, his candidacy before Century Adm. Com. \+ the effect of the fact that he is the Art Critic of Welf Post - Before I left Mr. \+ Mrs. J.D. Woodward came in - It was after 12 when I left. I walked about till about 1.30 - Then to Charl's \+ got correct time many other gues there - home to dinner at 2 - After 4 I rode up to 80d S.- walked to Central Park - around the great Reservoir - \+ down on the West side thro' to 59 7S. \+ Mad. Ave. where I took car \+ got home at 6.45 - Eve'g both Geo. \+ Charl came in.
