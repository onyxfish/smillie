## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 6, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

W. fred. Young this fiancé arrived at noon — Jim returned soon after.

Disagreeable day - no rain - but mist at times + wind strong + persistently south. I spent much of the day out of doors. Scott Brown came in the m'f - to set stakes at corners of the two triangles of land that G.W. Neilson + I are to exchange - he to give me the triangle at N.W. corner of my lot in exchange for a similar triangle that I am to let him have at the S.W. cor. of my lot - I went with Otis Brown + saw the iron pike stakes driven. Trumbull came + worked. Mr. fred'k Yorns + Miss Taylor of Po'k. to whom he is engaged, came in stage about noon — Miss Grace Buck of Po'k came with them — she is to visit the Misses Woodford now occupying Highland cottage. I went to Hotel with star mail - dinner at 1.30 - about 2 o'clk - before we had finished our meal, Jim came alone from Placid. Afternoon, I went down to find Beadle's - saw him + arranged to have him work on Public Road near my gate to fill a mud hole + change grade - I wrote note to Sylvester nye about dumping refuse from the roadway into the hollow near gate. Tea at 7 - Ev'f. some reading.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 7, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m'f. for a little while - Then clouding over - growing cooler - north wind + light showers in the afternoon - most of the m'f printing photos from films by Ralph. As my head bothered me so that I felt unable to do studio work I busied myself out of doors. After dinner Jim, Ralph + I walked out on the Chapper Pond road to the Fern Caves - now all burned over - a most dismal place - a light spatter of rain fell as we walked home ag'a very cool north wind.
Ev'f. some reading. The young people enjoying the moonlight + a camp fire at Maghee's.
