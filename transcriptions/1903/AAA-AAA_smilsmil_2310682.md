## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 15, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably hot day — with a sharp \+ short thunderstorm in the afternoon, followed by a very mild eve't with a pleasantly fresh air — After bkft. in garden — got 24 ears of corn, all fairly good — but none of them really fine specimens — Then I wrote a rather long letter to Mr. Kent \+ at noon went to hotel for mail \+ supplies — home to dinner at 1 — Afternoon the Maglees made parting call on Kate, Mr. M— came up with the others — Otis \+ team \+ Moody worked on the road — Otis ran my Road Roller into the bank \+ wrecked it temporarily — I accomplished but little during the P.M. wrote to Bridgeman, the seeds-man — Eve't. again lanterns lighted on North Piazza \+ family sitting out there

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 16, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day — warm with a refreshing breeze — Soon after I got through with my mornings work in the garden Louis Dorb called — He came to Neene Valley yesterday \+ walked up to the Heights this mft. Seeing my name on my gate at the road he came up to the house, not sure whether it was he or me he had met — We had a pleasant, chatty time for an hour — Then I finished my letter, started some days ago, to Mrs \+ J. Smith \+ took it to the hotel \+ mailed it — Rec. a belated letter from Chank — written before the letter Rec. by Mary two or three days ago — dinner — much of the afternoon was spent cutting iron staples out of one of the beeches by the Road side near my gate — these were put in some years ago by Wm G. Neilson — to hold a wire fence — the wood is rotting about them \+ I have to cut them out to save the tree — Supper before 7 — Jim \+ Ralph had been down the valley, on their wheels, \+ had a swim — In the eve't Ralph was used up with nausea \+ headache — Some of us sat out on north Piazza till near 9.

Otis \+ team on Road.
