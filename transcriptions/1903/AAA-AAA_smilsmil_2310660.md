## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 4, 1903

**Weather:** Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain all day, with strong south wind. Very little work done out of doors - Moody before the rain came on heavily worked a little over the ground that he seeded yesterday. I attended to some correspondence after bkfp. \+ then got to work on 14×18 that I laid in some time ago. This is the second painting - worked till 12.30 - then to hotel with mail \+ for mail - hom at 1 - dinner. Painted till after 5 \+ then cleaned up \+ washed brushes - Tea before 7 - Evef. till 9 I was rolled in my rugs in lounging seat on north piazza - Resting.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 5, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still wet, but not a heavy rain like that of yesterday - Rather a mizzle with occasional respites - strong south wind - After bkfp. I picked peas \+ beans in the garden - The first "mess" of Champion peas from our garden - Then set palette \+ to work repainting sketch of Hollyhocks, painted in Montrose some years ago. 9½×13. The original of a magazine that I made \+ sold to Kelskel - Painted till noon - Then to hotel \+ got mail - dinner before 1 o'clk. Trumbull came in the afternoon, brought another man \+ fussed about - Put knob in my bed room closet door - Put sash in Moody's window - etc - I fussed over painting till near 6 - then washed up Evef. with Raelph, arranged list of Teleph. \+ electric bell supplies for service between house \+ annex - Then wrote a note to Regan requesting him to get the articles \+ send them to me.
