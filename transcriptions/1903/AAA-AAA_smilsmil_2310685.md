## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 21, 1903

*Jim left for U.l.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another lovely day - cloudless \+ warm. Up at 6. Jim had his bkf't at 6.30 \+ at 7 started for Westport to take 1.46 train to Troy \+ there to take St. "Saratoga" for U.l. - expects to stay at Ed.w? Cook's To-morrow night \+ on to New Haven all old. We had our bkf't before 8 \+ after a time I went to work, with Chisel \+ mallet on beach tree near Gate extracting iron staples \+ cutting out decaying wood. Ralph \+ I made a number of measurements about garden \+ other places. Dinner at 1 \- After that a rest \+ then again at work on beech trees - About 5 Adelaide Mayhee came \+ insisted upon my taking part in a Birth-day procession in honor of her Mother, so I went up with her \+ carried the birth-day cake on my head \+ in half an hour returned to my work \- Tea at 6.30 - Even. some music on piano \+ newspaper reading -

Rob Otis's team at work to-day with strange driver - O. off after stray cows. Team hauled ledge stone for wall near gate - also, one load of manure which was spread over new ploughed ground to prepare it for berry vines or bushes.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 22, 1903

*Down sick.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day - worked most of the m't at the beech trees at the turn of the road near the gate cutting out the old iron staples \+ the decayed wood about them - Went to hotel after 12 o'clk - dinner at 1 - after that I heated tar \+ tarred all the exposed places on the trees that I have worked upon - When I got through with this work - about 4 p.m. - I felt extremely uncomfortable in a general indescribable way - This m't. when I got up I had evidence that my bowels were clogged with mucus \+ I administered an enema of tepid water \+ rested. I had considerable trouble before I got a clearance \+ felt better after it - but was weak. When I got to my Room, about 4.30 P.M. I had more trouble with my bowels - a loose evacuation with mucus tinged in spots with blood - I felt very queer \+ lay down on my bed - one half an hour a chill came on - I then undressed \+ had hot water bags put at my feet \+ stomach. It was half an hour or more before the chill passed \+ the fever came - At about 9 I sent Ralph to hotel for Dr. Janeway Jr. as there is no other physician in the neighborhood - He declined to come \+ told R. to go to his son Theodore when R. went to him, he too seemed unwilling but said he would be over presently - he did not come till 9.15 when I had given up hope of his coming \+ was preparing to treat myself - my temp. was then 104º \+ my pulse 119. He gave me a mixture that had opium, camphor \+ something else in it \+ said he would come again to-morrow m't -

I passed a wretched night, of course.

Tris's Team at work hauling ledge stone to-day.
