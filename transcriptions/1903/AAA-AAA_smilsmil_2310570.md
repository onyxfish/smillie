## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 3, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant, sunny, mild day until later in the afternoon when it clouded over. To studio about 10 \+ put in a fine hour day on Borden etching \- I find that sitting at a desk so absolutely quiet \+ with nerve tension because of the fine mechanical work is extremely trying to me, my week of vacation seems only to emphasise this fact \- home to lunch at 1 \- back soon after 2 \+ worked till 4 \- then I paraded the streets till near 6 \+ then home \- dinner at 6.30 \- Carlson came at 9.15 \- massage for one hour.

B. 5 h.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 4, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nasty day \- dark, foggy \+ wet \- to studio after 10 \- was able to do a short days work only on Borden plate \- Home to lunch as usual \- back at 2 \+ worked till near 4. Went to Clauson's to see if I could get a 20"×30" frame to put Chasl's Plt in for a Century exhibit as C\- is not willing to let me have his frame for the purpose \- found an old french frame that Clausen said he wd. try to fix up for me. \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Well I worried for a good while over the balancing of my bank pass book \- I cannot account for a slight discrepency \-

Thunderstorm about 7.30 P.M. after which the weather cleared.

B. 3 h.
