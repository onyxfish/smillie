## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 28, 1903

**Weather:** Charming day. growing cloudy towards evef.  **Temp:** [illegible]

with threats for to-morrow. Again the day was given over to the telephone enterprise. After bkft. R. \+ I about an hour or more in the garden — then to work stringing the wires thro' the iron pipe, connecting pipe ends by coupling \+ getting the pipe into the trench. I went to Maglees \+ got another pipe wrench to use with mine — then to hotel. Dinner at 1. After that we resumed work \+ at last connected batteries \+ telephone. Mrs Vauttome happened in \+ she was the first to speak thro' it to me in the Annex. I find that it requires more battery power.
Evef I wrote to Ryan for more batteries.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 29, 1903

**Weather:** A wet day, rain setting down after 10  **Temp:** [illegible]

giving me chance to gather vegetables from the garden without getting wet. I had Woody pick a large basketful of very fine string beans \+ take them over to M. Tracy at the hotel. I went over for mail etc at noon. The work of completing the electric bell \+ Telephone wiring went on. Ralph sticking to it forever closely, getting all connections in good working order — considerable work has yet to be done in protecting the wires at exposed points. Dinner at 1. I had a fire in studio \+ Kate spent the mff there. After dinner she was dressed \+ went down stairs — the Dr. joined her there when he made his last call on her. Evef I did some reading \+ writing in studio — Katharine \+ Ralph went to the hotel to hear the [illegible] ton negro singers.
