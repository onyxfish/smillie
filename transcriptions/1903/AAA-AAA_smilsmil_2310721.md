## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 2, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Light fall of snow in the m'g + again in the ev'g. cloudy, raw day. After doing marketing I went to Bayerdorfer + then to 2° Nat'l Bank — There to Portrait Show Am. Art Ass'n — + paid $1.oo for 3 tickets — the only concession allowed to artists — Regular charge $1.oo for single adm'n. Some remarkably fine old pictures, especially the noble Vandyke belonging to Wm. C. Whitney. Also a lot of exceedingly commonplace stuff — there till after 2 — then to studio + home to lunch at 1. Immediately after that went to Nat'l D — Etching class — there till after 4 — home at 5 — too tired to go to Afternoonlea given by Mrs. Lockwood deForest for the coming out of her daughter Judith — sent cards — wrote a note to Lockwood — Dinner at 6.30 — ev'g after my newspaper worried till 11 o'clk over Bank acc't — getting worse the longer I worked at it — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 3, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snowfell during last night + a part of the m'g — about 4" in all. The remainder of the day cloudy + raw. I got to studio at 10 \+ to work on "A Pool in the Woods". Stuck to it till 1 o'clk. Gifford came in at about noon — home to lunch — back at 2.30. I had scubbed heavy glazes over my work carrying the whole tone down + getting some qualities that I thought desirable. I determined to do as little as possible upon it + let it go believing that I could not better it by more work — so, I slapped on a few daubs of solid color over the glaze — to-morrow we shall see what it looks like — left studio a little before 5 — weather + walking so bad that I had no walk — went home + had a nap instead. Dinner at 6.30 — ev'g, more worrying over my bank book — the bank credits me with $40 more than I can acc't for — I wonder where it came from.
