## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 5, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a wild night of wind + rain the mt seemed still, close + very warm - it seemed as though a deluge was imminent - but before noon the weather cleared charmingly - The air became fresher + more bracing + the night was a brilliant, cloudless moonlight. It was a day of hard work - After bkfp. the cold frame sashes were uncovered + showed less broken glass than I expected - then to garden + then to work - In the course of the day I got most of my things in order, ready to pack. I with Moody's help I got the piano serwaddled + the attic store room packed - Dinner after 1 - Men to hotel + got mail - worked till 5.30. then had a rest before supper at 6.45 - Every. Packed box to go to N.Y. by freight -

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 6, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day - Up before 6 + Backed, finishing freight box + commencing on trunk - Bkfp. at 7.30 - then picked two baskets of very fine corn + sent them to Mr. Maghee - These cleaned out "Rec. Bat." jars + with Moody packed + locked up attic store room - besides doing lots of other things - It was 11 before I got at my trunk again - After 12 Jere Beede came for trunks + I was ready - I then dressed + a few minutes after 1 I went over to Maghee's, the family having preceded me. There we had dinner + at 2½ I went to hotel + got mail - Thence to the Ark, followed immediately by Mr. + Mrs. Tracy + Misses Anderson + Moody - all went hastily Thro' to north piazza - then to garden to give Tracy a look at the corn - Beede's team was waiting. Many Christmas + Blenden got in - bid good bye to the T's + at 3 we drove over to the Maghees - got Kath' - \+ Bessie + left there at 3.15 - the drive over to Z-Town was a succession of delights - the foliage color + the sunshine being wonderfully beautiful. I walked more than half way up S Prince Hill - Then, as we were crowded - put Taylor (our driver) on the back seat with the servants, took Bessie on front seat with me, + so drove into Z-town, getting to the Windsor at about 6.30 - The last of the drive by the uncertain light of a rising full moon - we soon had our supper - I tried in vain to get Curtis of D. + H. R.R. at Plates by phone - Read newspapers + to bed at 9.30
