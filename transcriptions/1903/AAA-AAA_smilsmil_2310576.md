## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 15, 1903

**Weather:** nasty weather \- sleet & snow all day  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- get up at 7 \- exercise & scrub \- bkfst. at 9.30 \- before dinner I wrote for a while trying to formulate rules & regulations for Etching Dept. \- Na f D \- but my head was too heavy & muddled for such work \- dinner at 2.30 \- loafed about indoors till 4 \- then gave up & went to bed \- no supper \- rest & my head troubling me so that I could do nothing \-

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 16, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katharine & Bessie Ret. from Po'k.

nastier weather than yesterday \- Raw & sleety \- heavy snow storm at night. I did not get to work on Borden plate till about 11 & continued till 12.30 \- then to 2°. Nat. Bk & deposited U.S. Steel div. \- then home to lunch \- after that determined to take a play spell in the afternoon \- went to Mad. Sq. Theatre, 24th St. & bot. ticket for Beatrice Herford's Monologues at 3.30 then to Scribner's & had a chat with Wt. H. d. Smith \- looked over some books & then to the Theatre \- I had an excellent seat in third row from front. Miss H \- gave us "The pay station Girl" \- "A professional Boarder", on encore "An English woman packing her box" \- "At the Box Office" & "The Sociable Seamstress". She was exceedingly clever & very funny \- It was near 5 o'clk. when I got out \- went to Alexander & got my shoe \- then to Buskink's \- then home \- Katharine & Bessie got in from Po'k about 6 \- dinner at 6.30 \- evg. S wrote a short note to Raepa & sent check for $11.53 pay mt for a set of drawing instruments \- mailed it \- & went to Freas's for Pepsin etc \-

B. 1 1/2.
