## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 16, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came home for the holidays \-

fine cold day \- wrote note to Emma \- This m'f, after bkfst. \- did not get to studio till near 11 o'clk \- Spent Rest of m'f making two little pen sketches for a Calendar that Oscar J.'s wife is getting up for him \- Home at 1 \- while at lunch an old man called who proved to be F. Baker, husband of Priscilla Goodliffe of ages ago \- They live in Newark \+ are to celebrate their golden wedding on the 28th \& he came to ask me to put in an appearance then \- for half an hour we talked of old times \- after lunch went to my class at N.A. of D. \- It was with them till 4.30 \- walked half way home across the Park to Mad. Ave \& 37. St. \- feeling unusually well \- home at 5.30 \- Jim there, just down from New Haven \- dinner at 6.30 \- Ev'f, enjoying the warm fire \- Jim went for a short visit to Alice Ireland's.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 17, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine day \- after bkfst. went to grocer's, butcher's \+ studio \- then Fine Arts Bld'g where I spent the day with the Com. on Selection going over about a thousand paintings offered for the Annual Exh'n of N.A. of D. H.O. Walker was Chairman \- Ben Foster, Sect'y. we lunched on the premises \- \& ceased work about 4.30 \- I walked down to studio \- Took my painting "An Adirondack Lake" to Clausens \- then home \- letter from Mary in which she writes enthusiastically of their pleasure during the voyage \- none of the party sick \- everything inspiriting \- The letter was written on ship-board \+ mailed at Plymouth \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Ev'f, with Jim, went to Ed'd Cook's.
