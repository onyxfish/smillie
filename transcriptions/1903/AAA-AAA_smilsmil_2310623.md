## Left Page

### Wed. May 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate went to Po'k.

The hottest day yet — two heavy thunder storms late in the afternoon — barely enough rain to wet pavements — no mitigation of heat. To studio at 10 \+ worked till 1 on Borden plate — Maurice Stern called — also Hugh Douglas — Home to lunch — back at 2.30 — Plumber worked most of P.M. clearing waste pipe from Leo's studio. I worked on B. plate, taking prints with working between prints — It was 6.45 before I finished \+ washed up. Then home to dinner — Eve'g. quite used up — bowels "went back on me" — Third time this spring — Got to bed early. Kate went to 10 k. on 2.30 Train

B-7 h.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very hot but a better quality in the air. a mitigating breeze afternoon \+ eve'g. To studio at 10 \+ worked on Borden plate till 1.30 then home to lunch — back at 3 \+ worked till 5.30 — Had hoped to get plate ready for pf. to-morrow but found too much to do. Walked the streets for half an hour or more before dinner. — Eve'g newspaper till 9.

B. 5 h.
