## Left Page

### Fri. May 22, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A shower about 9 am. led us to believe that the hoped for change in weather had come — but in an hour or so it was steaming hot again. After bkfs. wrote to Fred. Stahr + along letter to Fred. Morton. Editor Brush \+ Pencil — It was 11 before I got to studio — worked till 1 — then home to lunch — on my way back met Dr. Yale + chatted with him for nearly half an hour — no — that was on my way to studio at 11 — Afternoon spent some time making pkg. of Etchings to send to Brush \+ Pencil, Chicago — Worked on Borden pl. till 5.30 — then to Freese's \+ paid bill — then to W. S. Miller — 40's then to Adams Exp. Co. + sent pkg. of Etchings — then wandered till after 6. Then home — dinner about 7. Mr. Fred. Young dined with us — I wrote to the boys — went to Gd. Cent. Station at 10.30 mailed letter + home — Pleasant cool breeze in street but little of it got into the house

B. 4h.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 23, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day — brilliant + cool — to studio at 10 + worked on Borden Pl. till 12 — then went down town to Chambers St. to the Mvin n[illegible] Co. — to get an electric push-button for studio — but saw — could not find what I wanted — then home to lunch — to studio at 2.30 — again on B — plates, about 2 hours — interrupted — Rondrons came with Atelier's tools that he had bought + Hookeila came with a plate that I allowed him to bite in my acid room — At 5.30 Ge.? + I walked up to Century Club — here for a few [illegible] home to dinner a 6.30 — Eve'g. Mr. J. H. Maghee came in — also W. Fr.? Young \+ a son of [illegible] Stephne Smith + Chase —

B 4h
