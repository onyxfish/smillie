## Left Page

### Mon. July 13, 1903

**Weather:** Cloudy most of the time during the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy most of the time during the day - soon after bkfs. I went down the hill to Harry Beede's to enquire why his wife had not come up, as promised, to do the washing - saw her - Harry thought it too far for her to walk before beginning her days work - saw Orrin Beede + he said he w'd. take her up on buck board. - She is to come up to-morrow. Then home - finished some writing + went to P.O - then to Scott Brown's - then home - dinner - after that - fixed handlebars on wheel but could not find brake rod - + so, did not dare ride down the hill + to town as intended. Practiced for awhile over the grassy ground + level bit of road - find that I have lost all my confidence + skill in riding - another sad evidence of age. Fixed up W.C. hinge - took it to P.O. + mailed it to J.P. Ryan - made drawing of push-button to send to Ryan - for studio street door - for Geo's studio - Wrote a note to Ge°. Then in garden with Moody for awhile - The fawn came up to us - supper - absent even't on north piazza - Rolled in my blankets - napping till near 10 o'clt.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 14, 1903

**Weather:** Bright m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m't. - clouded before noon - when a sudden squall came, passing over in a number of showers - hail with some - cloudy afternoon - light shower at 6.30 - Even't so cool that fire was lighted in large room. After bkfs. Katharine, Bessie + Ralph started for the top of Hopkins, taking lunch with them - they reported on their return at about 3.30, a smart dash of hail when near the mt. top. They got very wet - I gathered a full mess of very good peas + a mess of chard from the garden - then I wrote to Mr. Stf'd. Smith + to Jo. P. Ryan + at noon went to hotel + got mail - went to Scott Brown's + saw him - then hurried home to dinner just escaping a shower. Afternoon I worked for awhile arranging the cobble stones, brought yesterday m't., along the edge of road in front of house, separating gravel from grass. Tea - even't. Reading.
