## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 26, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*miss Buck left.*

A very pleasant day, growing cloudy before night. To studio at 9.30 to work painting on Charl's upright landscape which has been in my possession now (or a year or more to repaint. I have grown so tired of the Borden plate that I must have a rest, so I am getting a rest by working harder than ever at painting. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 Son after Nathalie Chadwick came, just from Catskill with her cousin Miss Armstrong. — I gave her a note of introduction to Frank Jones + took her up to Maynard + introduced her — + I gave her a lot of advice. She is a very sweet looking girl + attractive in manner. I painted as long as I could see — till 5 — then cleaned up + went to 119 W. 23d St Gas Consumers league (?) + complained that since their attachment had been put upon my meter I did not have enough light. Then home at 6.15 — dinner at 6.30 — Eve. Read news paper + loafed — after worrying awhile over my accts.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*The eighth Anniversary of Annie's death. Carlson.*

Dull, damp + foggy. To studio at 10 + for a time tried to paint the white cow in the picture I painted for Charl some time ago, but could not get the detail drawing as I wished owing to the very rough underpainting — so, at last I made a smooth surface with fresh paint + a palette knife + set it aside — then I took up the monochrome portrait of Father that I painted about a year ago — it is on board, finished in vignette form. I changed it + effect entirely by finishing it out to the edges of the board. Home to lunch + back at 2.30 — worked at on portrait — then a rest — a short walk. Home at 6.15 — dinner — Eve.
