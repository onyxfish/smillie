## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 26, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully beautiful mfg., clouded at noon - looking like showers in the afternoon. In garden till 10 o'clk - great crop of peas \+ beans - corn flourishing but backward for want of warm weather. Then R. \+ I resumed our work on Telephone wiring. we laid out a line for pike, measured the pike \+ then took it to Maghee's - \+ left it - he not at home. At 12 went \+ got pike, he having cut \+ threaded it. Dinner at 1 - afternoon Rob't. Otis came \+ ploughed a strip north of garden where I expect to put berry vines of various descriptions. He put two loads of manure upon it \+ ploughed \+ harrowed it in. Tea at 6.30. Ev'g. - striped.

Kate continues to improve - spent much of the day on piazza - \+ Ev'g in studio. D'. came at noon - will call again Sat.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious mfg. - clouded mid-day - clear, cold ev'g. I was up at 6. A.m. with severe head ache - which cleared in the course of the mfg. B'kf't. at 8 - after that in garden till after 10 - Then, with Ralph working at Telephone wiring until near noon - then to hotel for mail, groceries \+ some pike couplings. After dinner resumed our work \+ stuck to it all the afternoon. great trouble in getting threads, for pike through which half a dozen wires are to run between the two houses, dug under \+ among the roots of the big pine trees - then to get the wires threaded thro' the 17 lengths of ½" iron pike \+ the couplings screwed on without cutting the insulation on the wires - By 6 o'clk. we had got but little more than half the wire down in the pipes. Mr \+ Mrs Alexander, of N.Y. called during the P.M. - their first visit to the house. Tea at 6.30. Ev'g read newspaper for a little while \+ to bed early.

Kate continues to improve.
