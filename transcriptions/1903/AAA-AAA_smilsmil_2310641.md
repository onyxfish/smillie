## Left Page

### Thur. June 25, 1903

Willis Cook appeared.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day with one or two brief \+ sickly efforts at sunshine — Up at 5.30 \+ had a long pull at copying off my new will. Bkfp. at 8 — after that to studio \+ attended to various matters in the neighborhood. Then went to Miller's \+ got carpenter to help me pack boxes — we worked together till 12.30 getting two cases p kd. for Keene — then lunch — after that finished the copying of my Will \+ at about 3 started for down town — to Mt. News Co. in Duane St. — to D \+ H. Courtaudt S. \+ bought mileage ticket — then to a picture store \+ got pipe couplings — It was 4 when I got to Clarence Smith's \+ nearly 5.30 when I got through with the revision of my will \+ had it formally signed \+ witnessed — then to studio at 6 — got some things together there \+ home at 6.30 — found Willie Cook there — he has come to arrange with Jim about their projected camping trip — his coming \+ staying at this particular time is very awkward — He \+ Jim went to a Restaurant \+ Ralph \+ I went to Charles — Geo. came in while we were there — Home at 9.30 \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 26, 1903

Left N.Y. for the Ark.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm, pleasant day, much to the delight of Kate Leonard who was able to get out all of "ke wash" \+ have it dried properly \+ put away in the linen closit. I got up at about 6 \+ got considerable work done before bkfp. — at 8 — until about 11 Packing Trunk — then to studio \+ small matters in the neighborhood — had my hair cut — went to Miller's \+ gave order for another small packing box which was brought to the house. The carpenter helping me pack it — lunch at 1 — It was just after 3 o'clk. when the App. man came \+ took trunks \+ boxes — Then I went to Deposit Co \+ put my Will in my box — Then to Tiffany's \+ got six pieces of glass for my bed room door at house — then to R. Cct. St. \+ had trunks checked for West Port — then to Alexanders — 6th Ave. \+ 19th S. \+ got shoes that had been made for me \+ home at a little after 5 — Then had my settlement with Kate — went thro' the house taking a final look at things — there is a Restaurant in 42 S. with Jim \+ Ralph \+ got something to eat — Willie Cook ret. to Pompton some time in the afternoon — At 6.40 we went to Station \+ there met Augusta, the laundress. \+ at once went into the Train \+ took our places in the two sections that I had secured some days ago — our journey was uneventful — our beds were made up before 10 P.M. \+ we then turned in for the night.
