## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 1, 1903

**Weather:** Singular weather — Cloudy & rather warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ close — threatening showers — one little one came before 10 p.m. As usual I spent considerable time in the garden. Then I went down to Ferd. Beede's to see if I could not get him started again to work on my road — then to Harry Beede's then to Albert Winche's \+ for nearly an hour was with him in W. Maghee's garden. Home to dinner at 1 — After that went again to Winche's \+ with basket \+ trowel took up about two dozen strawberry runners, then home \+ set them out. Supper at about 7 — Evef. loafing about

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 2, 1903

**Weather:** Brilliant mt.  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- luminous haze — then showery clouds — no showers — almost cloudless moonlight at night — the first we have seen of this moon, now eleven days old. The mt f. was spent between the garden \+ printing photos. I went to hotel for mail after 12 — home to dinner at 1.30 afternoon took Moody down to Maghee's garden \+ got about three dozen strawberry plants — it took us until 6.30 to get them into the ground. Supper. Evef. some reading \+ some napping.

At noon I telephoned to Wm. Otis, in New Russia valley — a neighbor of Wm. Elkins — to learn, if I could, something of the whereabouts of Jim from whom I have heard nothing since he left with D. E. \+ Katie, ten days ago — I expected him home Sat. evef. Mrs. Otis said she believed there was a young man from Keene stopping with the Elkins — that was all I could learn.
