## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 9, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a night of unceasing rain the day was the heaviest \+ most continuous down pour that I remember. It was so heavy that I did not attempt to go out until it held up at about 4 P.M. I spent much time in writing two long letters to Jim \+ Ralph. When I went out in the afternoon I went to studio \+ saw Geo. who told me that Maynards bro. had come to the studio looking for M - who had not been seen since some time Wed. He got into the studio \+ found Geo. W. there in a sort of dazed condition. Bro Geo. telephoned to the Century \+ after awhile Mead — Mead White \+ McKim, came \+ took Geo. W. M. away in a cab — I went to 5th \+ 3d Ave. \+ Registered — then to G.C. \+ got time tables — then home — Jane Banks called \+ saw Mary to-day \+ offered to return to my service as Cook — Tom Rowland called \+ gave up his place — he brought Patrick Conefry of 326 E. 35th — who agreed to take the place at $18.00 a month, caring for house \+ studio. .
Dinner at 6.30 — early. some Reading.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 10, 1903

*Po'keepsie — 60th Anniversary of uncle Willis's \+ aunt Agnes' wedding —*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy mt. followed by showers \+ cold rain. Mary, Bessie \+ I went to G. Cent. Station, met bro. Geo. there \+ we went to Po'k on the 10.30 Train. Showers began to sweep over us before we got to Peekskill — from Po'k Station we rode to Uncle Willie's house, 39 Hooker Ave. \+ found all hands well \+ in pleased anticipation of the simple \+ informal festivities of the day — we were rec'd. with enthusiasm — Uncle W. \+ aunt A. were in very good spirits — \+ so were aunt Jessie, Martha Earl \+ Cinna Bourne — There was much preparation for a dinner, Tom Gillelan \+ wife \+ Mary (Tatjeu) Burling \+ possibly Lizzie \+ Jessie Smillie being expected — at 2 o'clk they had not come, so we sat down to table — we were all surprised \+ greatly pleased, while yet at table, by arrival of cablegram from Charl. at Saltburg congratulating the bride \+ Groom — Mary Burling arr'd. in time to have a part of the dinner — later, Tom Gillelan \+ his wife came at least, Lizzie \+ Jessie Smillie arr'd. Telegrams \+ flowers kept little W. busy running to the door — he was as pleased as a child \+ showed it in many ways while aunt A. smiled passively thro' it all — Uncle W. sang a song — double quick marched up \+ down the rooms \+ even danced a little — He is a surprising example of health \+ spirits at 90 years of age. A supper \+ more festivities were expected in the eveg. but Geo. \+ I had to leave soon after 6. to catch the 6.40 train — we were joined by Bessie at the R.R. station. The floods have disarranged the time-tables. The 6.40 was late — all passengers were directed to take the "local", leaving at [illegible] — we got to N.Y. at 9.30 — went home — got something to eat \+ then to bed
