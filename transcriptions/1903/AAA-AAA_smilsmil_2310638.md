## Left Page

### Fri. June 19, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim ret. to New-Haven —
Halsey Joes dinner at Joto's.
Over cast, with showers — one or two feeble attempts at sunshine. I got up to 6h.p. after a night of good rest but feeling very shaky. Jim went back to New Haven intending to pack his belongings there \+ return next Monday. Severina came saying that she had been to D. P.'s \+ that he told her it would be all right for her to go with us up to the mts., but I told her that I could not take her. She was very quiet but broke out when she went to the kitchen \+ scared Kate Leonard by her ravings that she came up stairs to me for safety. Ralph went down to her \+ presently she quieted \+ left. Later R. \+ I went to Dalm's. Charl had already been there. P. promised to have a laundress ready for inspection on Monday next at 11 a.m. Then to bank — then home — lunch at 1.30 — Chas. Kurtz called in the m.f. to make sure that I would be at the Joes dinner to-night — The afternoon was spent in getting things together for packing. I had a rest before I went at 6.30 to the Joto's at invitation of Prof. Joes to meet members of Advisory Com. at Art Mattus S.t Jouis Exp'n 1904. Delman, Baer, Fleming \+ Wolf were there. With Joes, Kurtz \+ self, seven sat down to Table — a very simple good dinner — no formality. Joes showed his plans of galleries \+ explained what he hoped to accomplish. Everything seems to promise good results. J.Q.A. Ward was expected — also Walter Cook, but they failed to come. We had a chatty time till 10.30 when we left the Table \+ dispersed. I got home at 11.
Ralph dined at Charl's.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

constant rain thro. the night — nearly 4" has fallen in the past ten days. Overcast with several showers. Rec'd. letter from Mary in the m.f. Went to Charl's at 9 \+ with him to Union League Club. Met Mallory there — gave him Miss Giles' address — says he has been looking for such a person. Spent some time writing letter to Mary. Then to studio \+ with Peter went up on roof to look for leaks — found very bad state of things — many places where the tin is rusted thro' — lunch at home at 1. I found enough to do till about 7 when R. \+ I went to Charl's to dinner. There till near 10. He played for us. Then home.
