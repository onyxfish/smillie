## Left Page

### Sun. June 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain most of last night — overcast, muggy m't. — a heavy downpour bet. 12 \+ 1 — after that, slowly clearing — Bkp't. about 9 — The m't. spent picking up things — much time given to arranging accelated bills \+ other papers. — I went to G't. Cent. S't. to make enquiries about seat for sister Kate — finished letter to her — About 2. Ralph \+ I got a light lunch for ourselves, Kate \+ Leonard being given a holiday — then I wrote letter to M't. f't. W. Morton \+ at about 4 Ralph \+ I rode up town to 145th S't. \+ 8 Ave. — then walked over to Boulevard \+ down to Riverside Viaduct — over that \+ then down to Fort Lee ferry \+ there took cars home — to Charl's at 6.35 — dinner — home about 8.30 — Pottered about till 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 22, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jime came from New Haven

A little sunshine in the m't. — after that cloudy \+ cool all day — After bkp't. went to studio — got Bordue pl. trimmed \+ done up with large photos — went to Scribner's with them — Mr. Smith not yet Ret'd. — to town — left a note for him — then to Richter's \+ selected Burlaps for studio hall — then to Dahn's \+ after a half hour of waiting saw Augusta Carlson who made a favorable impression — she is to call at the house to-morro m't. Then to Douglas's about studio hall — Jime arr'd. from New Haven — we lunched at home at 1 — I rested awhile after it — Then to Dahn's — to Bridgeman's for seeds \+ to Wanamaker's where I spent the rest of the afternoon making Keene purchases for Keene — home at 6 — went to Chas' to dinner at 7 — spent the ev'g there — home at 10.
