## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 23, 1903

**Weather:** A fine day \- high wind.  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day very weak \+ with severe headache \- When D. The'. Janeway came at about 10 A.M. my Temp. was normal \+ after listening to my heart with stethiscope he said I had a very good heart for a man of 70. He advised a diet of Quinine, Bismuth \+ boiled milk prescribed.

Blt. berry Roots from Mr. Kent, \+ Strawberry \+ Raspberry Roots from Bridgeman arrived.

Alis' team at work.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 24, 1903

**Weather:** Still fine weather.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a fairly restful night I was able to get up in the m'g \+ go down to bkfst. but was miserably weak \+ giddy \- After bkfst. I fixed wicker lounging seat on East balcony in sunshine \+ out of the wind \+ spent the m'g there, rolled in blankets, reading. About 2 I had a light dinner of boiled rice \- After dinner, for about an hour, I superintended the setting out of berry plants \- then passed the job over to Raefer \+ Moody \- About 4 o'clk. I went to my room \+ was on my bed till 6.30 \- Then more rice \+ festooned milk \- Several times during the day I have had uncomfortable sensations in bowels \- Eve'g reading newspapers.
