## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 6, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful winter day - bright & cold Bkfo. soon after 9 - Read newspapers \+ loafed about till 12 then out. went to studio - opened windows - then to Century \+ looked at pictures - then walked up to 60th S. \+ Fifth Ave. - then home at 1.45. dinner at 2 - wrote a note to Mr. VanHorne with pen sketch of Noah inviting certain sinners, in the flood to come out \+ dry themselves off a bit. I had a short rest - then went to studio - closed up - \+ walked down to Emma Irelands, getting there at about 6 - we sat \+ chatted till 7 then I took her to Mr. Adolph Lewis's in E. 16th St. where I left her \+ walked up to Edw. Cook's in 37th St. \+ there I spent the rest of the eve'g. with old Mr. Sprague \+ most of the Cook family, including Carleton \+ his wife \+ Edna \+ her fiancé - Home at 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 7, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine day - to studio before 10 - worked on painting "Cumulus at Sunset" X painted the foreground getting lots of black out of it - home to lunch as usual after going to bank to have a discrepency explained that bothered me in balancing accts - got what I wanted. to studio soon after 2 \+ worked as long as I could see - had a little constitutional on Park Ave. \+ then home a little after 6 - dinner - Even'g writing up accts, checks for bills, etc.
