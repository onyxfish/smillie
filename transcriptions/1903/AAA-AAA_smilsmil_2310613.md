## Left Page

### Thur. April 30, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very hot \+ enervating day. I got up \+ went down to bkfs. but felt very listless \+ shaky I hung about the house all day \+ kept very quiet \- no Return of the bowel troubles \- I did considerable writing during the day \+ went out twice for very short walks \- I wrote extended instructions to Chas. Moody at Keene Heights concerning Kitchen Garden matters \- The seeds were sent to him on Tues. Evey Mary \+ her girls went to a Glee concert \- I did some reading

## Right Page

### Fri. May 1, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cool \- sudden fall in Temp. a heavy frost in the country \- I got to studio about 11 \- found Ge. there \+ carpenters at work putting up partition across Maynard's studio \- I did a little work on Borden plate \+ home to lunch \- Put in a couple of hours on Borden plate \- when Bronson \+ Scholles (Etching students) came with their competition plates \+ I worked over them \- I showed the boys a lot of proofs \+ they were pleasingly enthusiastic \- It was 6 o'clk before they left \- Then home \- dinner \- Evey I wrote to Jim \+ Ralph \+ got to bed at 11.

B 2 h.
