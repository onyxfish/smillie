## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 9, 1903

**Weather:** Warm with strong south wind. \+ indications of coming rain, clouds hanging heavy in the lake defile while it was clear overhead  **Temp:** [illegible]

At 9 o'clk the boys went to hotel \+ went to upper lake with the Elkins \+ Cooks expecting to spend the day there but at the lake it was so threatening that all returned just after dinner \+ the E's \+ C's drove right onto E-town. I spent most of the m'g in the garden - To hotel about noon. Miss Woodward dined with us — that is with Mary, Katharine \+ me, Bessie being sick. The boys got home just in time for ice cream — Immediately after that we went out to the brush heap that had been hauled out to a hole just over my line \+ mW.g. Neilson's land. By hard work we got the whole of it burned up by 6.30. I was completely used up \+ went to bed very much out of repair — with heart running at 120 per minute \+ with splitting headache. I did not get really quieted down to rest all night.

Otis \+ team worked on road — all day Moody helping part of the time.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 10, 1903

**Weather:** Warm southerly wind all day \+ hazy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up after a restless night with severe headache \+ feeling very weak — after bkfs'. I wrote a note to Mrs. W.G. Neilson explaining to her how it was that I burned my brush heap on her land \+ apologizing for it — sent it to her with a basket of beans. Later in the m'g she made a pleasant call, with her son Winn. I was unable to do any thing all day \+ just lay around, weak \+ miserable. During a part of the afternoon I sat \+ bossed Moody while he dug a shallow gutter at the side of the road \+ paved it with small cobble stones. Ralph too has been complaining of bowel pains all day — Jine \+ Katharine spent the day on the lower lake with the Maghees fixing up the new camp that has just been built for Mr. M. Eve'g a north Piazza — Thunder storm \+ short shower about 9 P.M.

Otis \+ team half a day.
