## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 2, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day \- B.B.T. at 8.30 \- Before 11 Ralph + I started for a tramp. We went into the Reserve \- He had his camera + made a number of exposures. We went up the road as far as entrance to McLaren Trail + then followed that over to the River + crossed on the foot bridge there + Ret. by West Side River Trail. I made numerous mentae notes for the finishing of my painting "The source of the River" \- I hope to make some pencil color notes before the season is over. We got home at about 1.30 \- I had a rub down + then a rest + did not have my dinner till 3d afternoon The Maghees came over as usual after Church service

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 3, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day \- Bessie + Ralph went with Mr. Maghee up Gothics + Ret. late in the afternoon having had a glorious time \- I was busy out of doors all day after writing some notes. among them one to D. Yale \- + one to Fred. Morton of Brush + Pencil \- Ferd Beede hauled several loads of rotted saw-dust manure + I had Moody spade up the ground under the pine trees in front of the house where I have been trying to get grass to grow for two years. he mixed the manure thro' + + seeded a part. Otis came + fixed basin in one of the wash closets + changed faucets in butler's pantry. The fawn came to the hill + I followed it to the clumps of trees on the crest of Windy-brow. She was so little disturbed that she lay down + chewed her end while I stood not more than 6 or 7 feet from her, watching. Eveg. fooling with newspapers.
