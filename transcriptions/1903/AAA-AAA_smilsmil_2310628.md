## Left Page

### Sat. May 30, 1903

*Decoration Day -*

**Weather:** Cool — cloudy most of the day — light showers before 9 + about mid-day.  **Temp:** After 6[illegible].

I wrote for some time making a fair copy of new form of Will — About 10 went to fifth Ave to see military procession + into way to Park strauca + inauguration of Genl. Sherman statue by St. Gaudens — I missed the first, + most interesting division — the Regulars under Genl Chaffee + the war veterans — Home at 10.30 — Charl had arranged for an outing at Botanical + Zoological Gardens but the threatening weather was too much for it. I worked about the house, getting my Tribune War file into its case in the Store Room — To studio after lunch + worked at arranging things there till about 4. Then Rode up to Central Park W. at about 96 S. + loitered, going to north field where there was an immense crowd of people — it was a very interesting sight — there were whites — blacks + yellows — Jews + gentiles — most of them young — many of their children — There was a good deal of horse play, but no disorder that I saw — Most of the Tennis courts had players + I saw some good playing inspite of hampering crowds — I wandered about till after 6 — Then home to dinner — . At 8 I went to Clarence Smith's — 81 S. + Lexington Ave. + consulted him about some slight changes in my Will. I found Mrs. Frank Brownell there — I left at 9.30 + walked home — a little over 2 miles —

## Right Page

### Sun. May 31, 1903

**Weather:** Cool day — fair, but not without threats — a little very light sprinkle of wet now + then.  **Temp:** Up early to accomodate the girls who are to have an outing to-day — help — about 9 — After that I walked up to the Park + took a critical look at the equestrian Statue of Genl. Sherman, unveiled yesterday. I think I would like the angel of Peace better if she had no wings + a little shorter neck — + the horse if he were less anatomical, especially as shown in his hind quarters — The General's collar is not "Regulation" — it is detrimental — but I suppose St. Gaudens must have had some authority for it — The group has a very fine forward movement +, altogether, it is surely something for the city to be proud of — I do not think it well placed — it should have been out on the open Plaza — I wandered thro' the Park up to 72 S. + then home — about 3 miles in all — after stopping at Charles' + getting correct time. Dinner at 1 — After that I gave up + spent the afternoon on my bed — Every[illegible] I went to Clarence Smith's + consulted with him concerning some changes in new will that I am preparing — Mrs. Frank Brownell was there — I left at 9.30 + walked home from 81 S. + Lexington Ave. — about 2¼ miles — Gave up to my headache again — All of this was on Sat. every, as already recorded — I did not get up from my bed but lay there till midnight — Then, as Blenda + Christine had not come in I went down + sent Severina to bed — I did not get settled for the night till 2 A.M. — The girls were out all night — I took 1 2/10 gr. of calomel —
