## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 25, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast most of the day with a chilly feeling of snow in the air — Up soon after 7 \+ had my bath \+ rub-down — bkfp. at 9. Read my paper — then to studio — there for some time cutting \+ mounting photos. It was about 12 when I started for a walk — I went to Century — saw Sewell's seven lourettes "Culsie \+ Psyche", there on exhibition — Had a talk with Nicoll about Etchings \+ the St. Louis Exb'tn — then up 5 th Ave. to Central Park \+ home to dinner at 2 — after that, Read awhile three had an hours nap — then to the Irelands in E. 17 th S., Emma \+ Grace out — saw Mary for a few minutes then walked home — had supper — then finished my Sunday paper.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 26, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A Cold, blustering day — I got to studio about 10 o'clk. \+ practically spent the m'f trimming a lot of old photos. of geese. Taken years ago in Montrose, \+ pasting them on a piece of mounting board — I should have been engaged upon something of more importance. An individual from the Surety Co. came in to question me about Farington. He told me that he knew that F. had been dismissed from the M.A. because of irregularities in June '97. I asked him to excuse me \+ told him that he had already enough information to direct him to official records open to the public. He said the Co. did sometimes bond men who had gone wrong if they could be satisfied that there had been a genuine reformation —
\+ home to dinner at 1 — at 3 went to fall opening of Met. Mus. of Art — met Frank Waller \+ daughter also Miss Nathalie Chadwick — I wandered about till 5 — then walked down 5 th Ave. to 72 nd S: \+ then rode home — dinner at 6.30 — R. self wrote to Sarah Hammond (S.C.?) to Scott Brown Ralph — etc — after mailing my letters I had a little walk up \+ down Park Ave.
