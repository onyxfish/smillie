## Left Page

### Wed. July 1, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Showers in the mt. clearing very muggy \+ warm in the afternoon \- About 10 a.m. all hands turned out to watch the "white fawn", now getting to be sorrel in color, as it wandered about the place \- It came slowly to where the women were grouped on the road way east of the house \- I stood at some distance \- after coming to within about 30 feet of the women it turned \+ walked quietly past me not more than 10 feet from me \- it played about for 20 m. or more, then trotted away \- All day it was about the golf links \- at 5 p. m. it was in port \+ the hotel \- During the day Ralph shot \+ killed one of the little skunks at its hole near the trail to the road. \- During the mt. \- I worked in plans for details for mantel piece in my room \- \+ swam in the afternoon \- I wrote to [illegible] \- went with Jim to E. Hubert's to help him plan his journey to Raisins Lake to meet Willie Cook \+ others for a camping party \- After tea we went to Ferd Beale's \+ engaged horse to take him to meet Placid stage at [illegible] Centre on Friday \- Then I went to hotel \+ telephoned to Kate at Windsor Hotel \- E. town \- She left P'k. this mt. Eve'g. Reading.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 2, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate arr. from Po'k.

Beautiful mt., growing very warm \- Showers \+ high winds in the afternoon \- Cloudy \+ quiet eve'g. In the mt., my corn-crowned bunion on my left foot was made so sore by my attempt to extract the corn that I was made lame for the day \+ could do but little walking \- I worked in studio on drawings of detail for mantel piece for my bedroom \- Kate arr. from E. town \- where she spent last night; just before dinner \- Afternoon I continued my pen work \- Tea at 6.30 \+ eve'g. rolled my rug on lounge on piazza \-
