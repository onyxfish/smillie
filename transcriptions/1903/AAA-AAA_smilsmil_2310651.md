## Left Page

### Fri. July 17, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day - After bkft. wrote to Jim \+ to Arthur Trumbull - Spent most of the morning with Ralph printing from photo-negatives. It was near noon when I went to hotel - When I got back to the house I found that Mollie Adams had been there with Curtis Walker \+ his sister - They ret'd. to the house after we had had our dinner - They had driven over from where they are staying in New-Russia Valley \+ returned there in the evef. Ralph went down to Keene Valley village on his wheel \+ I went to Harry \+ then to ferd. Reed's - to Mr. Winch's \+ to the hotel garden where I got three fine heads of lettuce from "old Tom" - Then home. \+ from there to hotel again for mail - home - tea 6.30 - evef. sleeping \+ reading -

## Right Page

### Sat. July 18, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ cool, threatening Rain which came at evef. in light showers, continuing steadily in moderate Rain thro' the night - Trumbull came \+ worked all day casing Piazza posts, month. I came to the conclusion that it was time for me to do something, so I made a little fire in studio - set palette \+ commenced painting a sketch, without any very definite idea, upon a mounting board 13½ × 18". I worked closely till about noon - Then to P.O. Got a letter from Jim which assures me that he is having a good time in camp. On my way home met the fawn at the Polley way gate - it followed me over to the dok. Ralph got a couple of photos at it with his camera. I painted on my sketch till near 6 - Then again to hotel - home in time for supper - Evef.: I had a nap in studio - then Read till bed time.

Trumbull worked all day.
