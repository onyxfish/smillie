## Left Page

### Sat. June 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The porter roused us at about 3.30 A.M. train about 20 m. late — we got to West Port before 4 — found young man with herd Beede's teams waiting for us. Got our trunks \+ bicycle on the baggage wagon \+ then we rode in a very comfortable three seated vehicle over to Elizabethtown getting there before 6 — The m't was cloudy \+ quite cool — At the hotel we waited till 7 for our breakfast \+ at about 8 started on our ride over to The Ark. Augusta, the laundress, was much interested in the fires. \+ the streams. I watched for evidences of the great fires, but it was not till we began the descent into Keene valley that we saw the burned patches over the mts — It was distressing — the culmination came when we got to where we could see Noon Mark — Round Top \+ the Knubble at the foot of the Giant — It will take generations of men's lives to repair that injury — The soil upon which the forests depended for sustenance was largely organic matter many times very shallow upon the rock — sometimes this was entirely burned off — \+ of course, till there is a new supply there can be no vegetation — we got to The Ark at 11.15 \+ found Mary \+ the girls effecting us — everything about the house was charming — Soon after 1 we had a good dinner afternoon going over the grounds \+ inspecting things generally — Some time unpacking trunk \+ getting things into my room — [illegible] in big room enjoying the warmth of wood fire —

## Right Page

### Sun. June 28, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day — brilliant, breezy \+ cool — up before 7 — made fire on hearth — had my bath \+ then bkfst. at 8.30 — the m't was spent putting my things away \+ wandering over the place — dinner at 1.30 — after that I wrote a note to Charl \+ took it to the Hotel where I met Tracy who has the management of St. Hubert's this season — then more dawdling about — had a short nap — Mr. \+ Mrs. Maghee came over about 5 o'clk. with Adelaide \+ Catharine Hardenburgh — Supper at 6.30 — then on Piazza for an hour or so — then some writing in my room. \+ to bed at 10.
