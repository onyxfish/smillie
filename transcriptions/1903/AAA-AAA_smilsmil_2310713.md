## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 16, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable dark, wet day — to studio at usual time \+ painted \+ fussed over "Hollyhocks" till about 12, \+ then to Second Nat. Bank — then up Fifth Ave. — Stopped at Clausen's \+ home to lunch at 1 — back to studio — very dark — did a lot of little odd ends \+ home at 4.30 — Rested — dinner — then for an hour or more fussing with electric battery-bells wont ring — but I made them do it. then, at 9, settled down to my newspaper. Charles came in —

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 17, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark wet day — to studio about 10 — wrote notes to Jim, Kimmee \+ Vaigt \+ Emma Ireland. Then got out an old Hollyhock study about 10" × 14" — Painted years ago in Montrose — cleaned it, oiled \+ out — extended the edges to make it fit a frame I have \+ glazed down parts of it til, to me, it seemed quite presentable. — Home to lunch at 1 — after that my head was so troublesome \+ the day was so dark that I lay down on my bed till about 4 — Then to studio \+ was there till about 5.30 cleaning up \+ making photo. solutions — Then home — dinner — with paper till 8 — then, for some time, writing up old acct. book. — Charl came in for awhile.
