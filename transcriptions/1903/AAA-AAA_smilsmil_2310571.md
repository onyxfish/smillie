## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 5, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Carlson.

Cold, windy, cloudy day, with light snow flurries — awoke soon after 4 o'clk a.m, with severe headache which disabled me all day. I wrote a note to Mr. Dexter Tiffany of Rainsfield after bkft. — This is the last Reception day for going Beck T. + his bride. — At studio I spent most of the day cutting mounts for prints. Before noon went to Clausen's to see about old french frame that he has could fixed up to put on Charl's painting for Century album. Sat. night — the frame is too dilapidated. — Home to lunch + back to studio — there till after 4 — then home + had a rest before dinner. Every Carlson came at 9.30 + gave me the usual treatment.

A gale blew during most of the day sometimes at a rate of 65 miles an hour, so local weather Bureau reports. At the tall Flat-iron building women were thrown down by roller gusts + a messenger boy was blown out to the middle of the street, under an auto-car, run over + killed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 6, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

lunch party at home. — Louise Jessups — Anna "(Mr. Woodin) — Jessie Edgar.

A very pleasant day — mild, quiet + sunny. To studio at about 10 + worked on Border plates till after 12. — then to 2nd Nat'l Bank. Home at 1.30 — the girls had invited to lunch Louise Jessups, her sister Anna (Mr. Wm Woodin) + Jessie Comstock Edgar — we sat at table till nearly 3 — then I went to studio + worked till almost 5. — Then I went to Buskirk's — 6th ave. near 34th st., + ordered a die to mark my plt. for Public Library Print dept. — Home at 6 — Dinner — Ev'g, wrote to Jim + Ralph — at at 10.30. mailed letters + had a walk before going to bed.

B. 4 h.
