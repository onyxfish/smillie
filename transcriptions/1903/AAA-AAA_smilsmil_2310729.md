## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 18, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came home for the holiday —

Fine cold day — Got to Fine Arts Bldg in 57th St. soon after 10 — The Com- got tho' with its work soon after 11 \+ adjourned to meet on call to award prizes — Geo. \+ I walked down to studio — got there at about 11.30 — I laid ground on a small zinc plate \+ prepared to make an etching upon it as an object lesson for my class — I took the pencil sketch made at last class meeting by Miss Goldthwait for her etching \+ in the afternoon, after my lunch, I etched it after a manner that I thought would easily come within the comprehension of the class. The drawing took about 20 m., but the delays of varnishing \+ biting took all the daylight of the afternoon — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Ralph arr. from Hackley at about 2 o'clk —
Every I wrote to Merry — Mr. Gessler \+ Harper Bros — Enc. of Am. History delivered to-day — Vol. 10 injured by having been wet.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 19, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day — To studio at usual time \+ worked on my etching after Miss Goldthwait's sketch \+ then took a print of it — Home to lunch at 1.30 — Then to N.A. Etching class \+ worked there till 4.30 — then home, dressed \+ had a short rest before dinner — After that, on the Roof for a while watching the fireworks set off at 8 o'clk from the new Williamsburgh Bridge, formally opened to-day — Then, with Jim \+ Ralph, to Charls' \+ from there to the Century where we heard Prof. Chas. F. Chandler of Columbia U. — give a very interesting lecture on the Electro-Chemical Industries at Niagara Falls — Jim was nearly asleep part of the time, \+ Ralph was severely critical of the Prof's chemical joke giving a formula for the production of Whiskey from Coal — About 11 when we got home.
