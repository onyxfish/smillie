## Left Page

### Mon. April 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, mild spring day — to studio before 10 but my work was interfered with by finding the Ther. at 83° — Result of Jor's carelessness — Until 12.30 I worked on biting on Borden plate — Then went to Walter Cook's to enquire about the manner in which partition, to be put thro' Maynard Studio, should be supported — Home to lunch at 1.15 — to studio at 2.30 — finished biting \+ cleaned plate soon after 5 — Then to Clausen's about mounting boards \+ home at 6 — dinner — Evef went to Stag Recep\[tion\] at Water Color Exhib\[ition\] Am. Art Ass\[ociation\] — Some interesting work but not enough to be remarkable. Home at 11 — Geo. walked up with me.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely day. To studio soon after 10 \+ worked with dry point \+ graver on Borden plate till 1 o'clk — Then home to lunch — back to studio before 2.30 \+ continued work on plate. Had a very pleasant call from Dr. Estelle Merrill (a friend of Mr. Thurber's) \+ a young friend whose name I did not catch — Later Carl Wingate, one of the N.A. Etching class, called with his composition plate. I got home at 6 o'clk — dinner at 6.30 Evef, Charl came in — I copied a portion of my revised Will, of which I am preparing an autograph copy for formal execution.
