## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 22, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, raw, wet day. Up at 8. had my scrub \+ breakfast at 10. Read news paper for awhile — then transcribed from father's "A Pilgrimage" till 12.30 — then to studio \+ from there to Charles' to get correct time. Then home to dinner at 2. Wrote again on Pilgrimage till after 4 — then slept till 6.30 — had some supper. Eve'g resumed my writing transcription omitting parts of the original too private or personal to be put into print, condensing parts \+ playing editor in a deferential way. About 9.30 I went to Charles' where the rest of the family were — there for about an hour — then home.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 23, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — cloudy till p.m. then some rain — To studio about 10 \+ painted on "A coming shower". x Till near noon. Then Sam\. Colman came in to borrow my camera lucida to experiment with it. He stayed for some time \+ talked freely of his marriage \+ what induced him to such action — he said that he could not live alone — he would have died if he had persisted — he could not eat, nor could he sleep — he was in deplorable condition — now, under the care of his trained nurse wife he is as well as so old a man could reasonably expect to be. Home to lunch — afternoon spent in general work about the studio \+ making some things ready to Etching Class students — Home before 6. Dinner — J.H. Maghee with us \+ took Katharine \+ Bessie to Theatre. Charl came in later — I loafed.
