## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 22, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day in spite of threats of a light snow flurry in the mts. It was 10 o'clk when I got to studio & commenced the repainting of "Passing Showers — Evening". I went generally over all of the cloud part of the sky, painting most of it in much higher key & with more color — Home to lunch at 1 & back at 2 & painted till 3.30. At this season I am much bothered in the afternoon by reflections of sunlight from windows opposite — then I cleaned up & went to Kimmell & Voigt's — 242 Canal St. & got paper & ink for Etching Class — Home at about 5 — had a rest before dinner at 6.30. Evng. with the boys went to Charl's & looked at his photos, till 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 23, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day. 10 o'clk. when I got to studio — spent mf repainting portions of clouds in "Passing Showers" — making them yet higher than I did yesterday — cleaned up at 12.30 — Home to lunch at 1 & there to Nat, getting there at 2.30 — With class till 4.30 — working upon their plates & taking pfs. Home at 5.30 — Rest before dinner — Evening, with Charl, went to Century & heard Walter Damrosch give an "Explanatory Recital" at the piano, of Wagner's Parsifal. In the library — an unusually large attendance of members — very interesting — D. told the crowd that it is a work of deepest spiritual & religious significance & explained the meaning of the several prominent characters — He gave the many "motifs" that constitute the musical language of Wagner & upon the piano showed how some of them are developed or expanded in the progress of the play or communicated — He is evidently an enthusiast. His voice is unpleasant & nasal but his enunciation is clear & his choice of words is good — His delivery was conversational when not playing — sometimes he played & at the same time fell into a sort of intoning becoming impassioned until voice & piano were in strife.
