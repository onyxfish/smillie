## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 7, 1903

**Weather:** went up Moon Mark.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very beautiful day \- at bkft. the boys expressed a desire to take some photos during the day + I agreed to go with them up Moon Mark \- as Rob. Otis + team were at work (1st day) hauling brush away from near the house \- + small cobble stones to make gutter by road side. \- + as Warner + team were hauling what is claimed to be muck to put on garden it was some time before I could give them instructions for the day + arrange other matters. \- It was 11.15 when we started \- going up new trail which we found to be very steep + hard \- the beauty of the woods is quite destroyed by forest fires of last spring \- the summit of mt. is a ruin + the outlook south where most have a fine view + fine was, was a sad one. We got to the top at 1.30 \- 2¼ hours \- + at once found a spot somewhat sheltered from the cool north wind + ate our lunch \- a very generous one. It was after 3.30 before we started down + 5.15 when we got home \- 1 3/4 hours \- I had eaten too freely of lunch + drunk too much coffee \- stomach somewhat upset \- my legs were also much strained + had some crampy discomforts. \- I had a bath, rub down + change of clothes + then lay down on my bed + did not get up again till near 11 when I got up + undressed. I had some leg cramps that were painful. With my student lamp at my head, I read.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 8, 1903

**Weather:** Wm + Katie Elkins + Co. + Winey Cook called.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cloudless + pleasantly cool after frost last night. I put Registering Ther. out on our piazza + in the mt. it marked 32°. After bkft. the boys + I went out + spent some time in burning brush in the hole near S.E. cor. of my lot, on Neilson's land. Then the boys went off for the day on an Exploring expedition on Snow Mt. + I looking over garden to see damage done to squashes + beans by frost \- other vegetables do not show any injury yet. also, looking after Rob. Otis + team, hauling material for road \- At 11 I went to hotel for mail + for D. Lombard, for Kate who is now having trouble with her bowels \- Dinner at 1 \- Bessie + Maghee went up Giant. Rather late in the afternoon we were surprised by a call from Dr. Elkins + Katie + Co. Cook + Winey. After going over the house we strayed over Windy bow + then to hotel where they are to stay to-night + go to Upper Lake to-morrow \- came to supper at 6.30 \- after supper worked in garden for some time fixing newspapers over the squash vines as there are abundant evidences of more frost coming to-night.
