## Left Page

### Fri. April 24, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant, mild day. To studio at 10 \+ worked till 1 on Borden plate. Then home to lunch — my head aching I thought best not to work on plate, so, soon after 2 I started for Green wood Cemetery, getting there in 3/4 of an hour. I went to our lot — from there I walked across the cemetery to the eastern gate \+ there saw Mackeller in his office. He said that he had rec'd. notice that the monument w'd. be shipped from the quarry Ap. 15 — but had heard nothing since — Then walked back to 5th Ave. Entrance \+ thence home at 6 o'clk. — Dinner — Eve'g wrote to Jim \+ Ralph.

B. 3 h.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 25, 1903

**Weather:** cloudy & mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ mild — Carpenter came at 8 o'cl \+ got to work at some jobbing until I had my bkf't, then I gave him further directions \+ got to studio before 10 in time for Silleck's men who came \+ until after 11 we were engaged getting my big book case down from Maynard's room \+ setting \+ up in my studio. Then I worked on Borden plate until lunch at 1 — To studio at 2.30 \+ worked on plate for a couple of hours — Miller came \+ took a look at where the partition is to be set in Maynard's studio. I worked for sometime rearranging things \+ cleaning up. Home at 6.30 — dinner — Eve'g. the girls went to Charl's — later D. Mr. Stephen Smith \+ son came \+ I took them to Charl's — I stayed till after 10 — then home \+ to bed.

B. 4 h.
