## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 13, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day - an enervating warmth - uncomfortable if exercising - Bkft. at 9 - after that went to garden \+ picked 28 ears of corn - 12 of which I took to Mr. W.G. Neilson - from there I went to Mr. Openlyn's - Alex. Schilling there - we spent what remained of the mf. in chatting on piazza - After 12.30 S. \+ I walked over to hotel where I got mail - among others, a diary letter from Charl. to Mary - the first we have heard from him since he sailed on the wlf. of 25 ult. At time of mailing he was in London. I wrote a note to Curtis - ticket agt. D.H.N. at Plattsburgh - about Kate's seat - then home to dinner - Afternoon I fooled \+ lounged around - the usual afternoon tea \+ Mahjees - Evef. Confing about - a little reading \+ some time on South piazza with all doors \+ windows open the Ther. did not fall below 71°. in my bed room during the night - it got above 80°. during the day.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 14, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm mf. - later in the day tempered by light clouds - pleasantly cool evef. After bkft. in garden till after 10 - little things about the house - took my time till dinner. After that I got my tar pail filled \+ heated the tar \+ made application to some of the trees about the house - then for some time I sketched pines from North piazza - to help me if I attempt to paint "Sunset among the Pines" - \+ then I looked after the work that had been done to-day on the road by Otis \+ Hearn \+ Moody - a small showing, as usual. Supper at 7. Evef., paper lanterns on North piazza - the first time this summer - the family sat there while Bessie at the piano \+ Katharine with her whistle, discoursed music. The evef. passed pleasantly.
