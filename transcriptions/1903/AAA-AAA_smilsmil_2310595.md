## Left Page

### Wed. March 25, 1903

**Weather:** Cloudy, windy, rather cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy, windy, rather cold — to studio before 10 \+ worked till 1 on Borden place — home to lunch \+ back at 2 — at 3 Carlson came \+ gave me abdominal treatment for half an hour — I then continued work on B. place till 5 o'clk — then I went to Scribner's taking with me the part of Goupiel publication "Gerome" — photo gravure that belonged to bro Will — Mr. H. Smith was not in — left it with Mr. Hatch — then to Rogers, Peet \+ Co. \+ bought a Derby hat — Dinner at 6.30 — at 8.30 to Century — Com. on Admissions — a protracted session — midnight when I got home —

B. 5 h.
C. 1/2

## Right Page

### Thur. March 26, 1903

**Weather:** Fine bright day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine bright day — to studio at 10 — worked on Borden place till 12. Then went to Walter Cook's office. 29th S. near 5th Ave. He was not in — walked up the Ave., stopped at Tooth's \+ looked at paintings — home at 12.45 — wrote a note to Clarence Smith — lunch at 1 — to studio at 2 — worked till 4 — then rode up town \+ left at Mrs Dodd's house, 308 W. 73d S. the photo. of Kate Parker Riley, \+ family, taken many years ago at Montrose — then walked up Riverside Drive to 97th S. then to Boulevard \+ rode home — dinner at 6.30 — Sue \+ Charl came in —

B. 4 h.
C. 1
