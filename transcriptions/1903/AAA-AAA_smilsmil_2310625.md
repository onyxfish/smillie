## Left Page

### Sun. May 24, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

to Greenwood \-

Very cool day \- leaden sky \- Bkf. at 9.30 \- then wrote to Sus. Sterne \- D. H. D. Allen \- Clarence Smith \+ W. Main. It was near 1 o'clk when I went out \+ wandered about the streets till near 2 \- then to Charl's \- got correct time \- then home to dinner \- Soon after 3 Charl \+ I started out \- went to Studio \+ saw George's Room \- then by Elevated R. R. to Greenwood \+ went to our lot. McKellar has set up the central monument which is quite an imposing mass of stone \- we are satisfied. We returned as we came getting home at 6.30 \- Ev'g. went to Ed. W. Cook's \+ saw Tom \+ from him got information of improved condition of Mallie Adams \- then to Charl's \- there till 10 \- then home.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 25, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine weather again \- Got up with a beast of a headache that at last drove me to bed immediately after lunch. I got to studio at 10 \- George moving his traps from storage warehouse into his room \- As I was unfit for work I went to Klunnel \+ Voigt on the chance of getting a pf. from my Borden plate \- I was successful in getting Henry's \- to take a couple for me \- enough to satisfy me that the plate, measured by my hopes, is a failure. I have tried too hard. The work is too close \+ mechanical \- that makes it expressless \- In many parts the biting has failed to some extent, for causes beyond my understanding \- so the work fails from a purely workmanlike point of view \- artistically, it never had a chance \+ I made a serious mistake when I undertook to make it a specimen of mechanical \+ technical excellence \- I went to studio, backed down my p/p. \+ then home \+ to bed \+ did not get up again.
