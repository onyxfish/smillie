## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 8, 1903

**Weather:** Pleasant mild day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant mild day — After 10 when I got to studio — Got out "Passing Showers" x painted a couple of years ago \+ set to work on that to get some of the black out of it. To lunch at home \+ then to bank \+ made deposit — nearly 3 o'clk when I got to studio \+ painted till 11. Mr. Wingate came in to question me about "The Catskills — Cole — The Hudson River School of Landscap-ists — etc — he purposes to write a book on some subject — Saw Colum came, Ret. my camera-lucida — we had an interesting talk concerning his domestic matters as we sat in the dusk. — Home after 6 — dinner — Evey went to call on S. P. Avery — not at home — Ret. home \+ did some writing —
No news yet of the Patricia — I had expected it to-day surely.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 9, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable till late in the afternoon when a gale of wind \+ rain struck the city, the worst of it continuing for three or four hours. I got to studio late \+ busied myself in preparation for the afternoon work with N. A. Sketching class. Just as I was leaving for home, for lunch, an agent of Harper Bro's caught me \+ made such liberal offers that I was induced to contract to take an Encyclopaedia of Am. History in ten Vols. The work has been very favorably noticed by the press — Then home to lunch — \+ then to N.A., where I had a busy afternoon — took twelve prints from as many places — It was after 6 when I got home — had a wet time. Dinner — concluded that I had had enough of the N.A. for one day, so did not go to quarterly meeting in the evey but spent it quietly at home loafing. No word yet from Sister Mary \+ party — The n.y. papers reported the arrival of the Patricia at Cherbourg.
