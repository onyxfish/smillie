## Left Page

### Sun. March 29, 1903

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up late after a disturbed night. About 1 I went to studio + then to Charls' — There till dinner time, then home — + had dinner — about 4 o'clk. I went to studio + for about an hour I worked at arranging to mount some of the larger + more important proofs that father pasted in his "In Memoriam" Scrap Book — Then my bowel trouble came on again + I went home + to bed. Had a bad time thro' the night. Charl + Ge. came in the eve'g — sat with me + had a long talk about giving up his studio. I told him I would give Maynard warning.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 30, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day of high wind + heavy rain — I sent for Dr. Putnam + he came about 9 o'clk. during his office hours — after he had been with me a few minutes he was sent for — he went + returned about 11 o'clk. — decided that my bowel trouble was a form of indigestion — advised me to keep in bed + take no solid food. I followed his advice — amused myself by reading Oliver Horn (Hopkinson Smith) + got along in comparative comfort till late in the afternoon when my troubles returned again. I had a bad night.

Butter wrote changing dinner night from Weds. to Thurs.

Dr. P — ? does he chge. for two visits.
