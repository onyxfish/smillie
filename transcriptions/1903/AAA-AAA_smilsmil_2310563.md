## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. VanHorne & her daughter lunched with us — Carson —

Clouded + rather raw — growing less cold. To Studio at usual time — worked on Borden Till 11.30 — then to Am. Art Assn. + looked at the Marquand Exhibit till near 1 o'clk — I was not as greatly impressed as I expected the collection into various departments was not so very superior or so complete. I met there Edna Cook + her friend M. Murphy. Then home. Mr. VanHornet her daughter Katharine there to lunch. I got to studio before 2.30 + worked till 4.30 on B. plate — Walked for a while on Park Ave. Dinner at 6.30 Eve'g wrote reply to note rec'd. from Mr. Chadwick (Celia Halcott) + a note to Ralph. Carson came at 9 + left at 10.45

B. 3½h.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the m'g. clearing in the afternoon — It was after 10 when I got to studio — I am almost continuously "out of sorts" nowadays + hardly feel fit for work at any time — I worked on Borden plate (most depressing to me now) till 1 o'clk then home to lunch after a short walk on Park ave. To studio at 2.30, worked till 4 o'clk, + was then obliged to give up on acct. of an acute attack of flatulent colic — I got home + worked over myself till I got relief — then I went out to Durand-Ruel's + saw MacMonnies paintings + bronzes — the bronzes I admired — also, some of the paintings, but others rasped my nerves + color sense — then to Koe's + saw Hopkinson Smith's W.C.'s then to Knoedlers — then to D. Putnam's — there till after 6 — He will probably do something in the way of washing me out. Dinner at 6.30 — an uncomfortable eve'g. Did not feel able to go to the Annual Meeting, Water Color Socie—

B. 4h.
