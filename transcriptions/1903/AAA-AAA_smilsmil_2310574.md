## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 11, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

had teeth extracted.

A beastly day \- fog & mist & rain \- I got to studio at about 10 \- I could not at first see to etch & worked about at odd matters. Later it was lighter & I worked on Borden plate until 1 \- drawing in the flat tints on ceiling \- Home to lunch \- back at 2 & was able to see to etch till 3 \- I had determined to go to D. Celley & get him to go with me to Hasbroucks & have my teeth out as C's hour does not commence till 4 o'ck \- I had to resist almost an hour \- so, I worked on Etching mornings \- till 4 went to Dr. C's \- he did not come me till 4.35 \- Mrs. C \- entertained me part of the time \- at last he came greatly to my relief \- I had begun to fear that it w? be too late to go to D. H's & I could not bear the idea of having to give up the job now that I had set my heart on it \- He went with me without delay after telephoning to H. that we were coming \- the rain poured down & the wind blew but we got to 62 W. 38 a little before 5 \- work was exceedingly prompt \- him less than five minutes my last six teeth were out without the slightest consciousness on my part \- they, being entirely without teeth I asked Billy & Hasbrouck if they could recommend to me a some good cut nose. Dr. Celley walked home with me but I felt no effects from the gas \- Had a supper of "spoon victuals" at 6.30 \- Evg. Charl came in \- to bed early.

B. 3 h.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 12, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lincoln's birthday.
Carlson.

A very lovely day \- mild & clear \- very generally observed as a holiday. I got up feeling none the worse for yesterday's spree & have remarkably little soreness in my mouth. To studio at 10 & worked on Borden plate till 12 \- Then went to 132 W. 34th St. & called on Maurice Stern \- arranged with him that I am to visit the N.A. f. D. etching class on Sat. m'f. \- then home to lunch at 1 \- To studio at 2 & etched till after 3 \- then rode up town to 81st St. and delivered at 124 E. \- my little painting for Catharine Cook Smith. Then walked over to Fifth Ave. & down, along side the Park to 59th St. then rode home & had a nap before dinner at 6.30 \- Evg. I wrote a little note for Catharine Smith. Carlson came at 9.15 & gave me the usual treatment.

B. 3 h.
