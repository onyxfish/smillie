## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 19, 1903

**Weather:** Very clear - very cold - wind not so strong as yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very clear - very cold - wind not so strong as yesterday - to studio at 10. Painted all day - Commenced on little pictur painted some time ago, for Sallie Cook Munroe - a memory of Keene Valley. I soon had done on that all that I thought necessary - Then I took up an old painting "An Oct. afternoon" first painted out of doors on Montrose fair grounds - I worked all the rest of the day on it - went over it generally repainting much of it solidly. I think it much improved. - I went home to lunch as usual - after washing my brushes I had a short walk on 4th Ave. - went to G.C. Station & got timetable - Home at 6 o'ck - dinner - Ev'g. Frank Beatty came in for a little while. Carlson came at 9.30 -

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 20, 1903

**Weather:** overcast m'g. clearing to fine weather much milder than yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast m'g. clearing to fine weather much milder than yesterday - to studio at 9.30 worked on Borden plate till 12.30 - then home & feeling out of sorts I rested till lunch at 1 o'clk. After that dressed & went to G.C. Station & took 3.35 train for Tarrytown. met Nellie & Gordon at the train & sat with lady who proved to be Mrs. Bevin, also on her way to Hackley to attend the Dramatic entertainments. Train very slow in getting through the tunnel which was filled with smoke & steam - we were considerably behind time when we got to Dobbs Ferry, five miles from T-town, where we were switched into a side track & after a long wait learned that there had been a train wreck at T-town - a passenger & freight train, blocking three tracks. we were due at T-town at 4.20 but it was after 6 when we got there - Nellie, Gordon, Mr. Bevin & I rode in a sleigh up to Hackley Hall & got there after the first play & just as the second play commenced - "teasing a mother-in-law" Val. Smillie took the part of Mr. Oat muff & Spelman that of his wife - the play was funny & fairly well done. Val was the star. After the play supper was served. It was full & good - There was a large attendance. I telephoned to stable - two sleighs were sent at at 8.15 - Nellie could not be found at that time, so Mr. Bevan, his son Victor & [illegible] - Jim, Rachel & I went to the station & caught the 8.46 train. Jim found us at the school at about 6.30 having come to [illegible] New Haven & taken 4.12 train for T-town. This train was detained by wreck as mine was - on our Return we saw the Remains of three freight cars over the river bank, burning - Home at 10.

B. 3 h.
