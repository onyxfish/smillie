## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 14, 1903

**Weather:** not so cold, clear & windy as yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

not so cold, clear & windy as yesterday. to studio at 10 \+ worked all day on Borden plate — home to lunch 1.30 to 2.30 on B. plate till 4 o'clk — then made glue-paste \+ mounted three oil sketches on trunk board — walked on Park Ave till 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Eve\[ning\] — newspaper \+ letter to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 15, 1903

**Weather:** Dull day, much milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day — much milder — to studio at 10 — worked on Borden plate till 12.30 — then walked awhile on Park Ave \+ went to Durand-Ruel's \+ saw a coll. of portraits painted by Mrs. Adelaide Cole Chase, daughter of Foxcroft Cole of Boston — after the manner of Sargent — some of them I thought excellent — others I did not like — home to lunch at 1.15, to studio at 2.30 — on B. plate till 4 — then for an hour worked at relining the Mrs. Doughty painting that I am going to present to N.A. of D. \+ some other sketches — walked on Park Ave. till 6 — then home — dinner at 6.30 — Eve\[ning\]. Carlson came at 9.30 \+ gave me treatment till near 11.
