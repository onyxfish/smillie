## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 12, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold day — to studio at 10 & worked till 4.30 (barring lunch at home) on Borden plate — Dinner at 6.30 & at 8 went, by appointment, to Mr. Warchydts & met there Tielman & Frank Jones (acting Treas. N.A. of D.)
Mr. B. presented a paper drawn by her lawyer, Transferring in Trust to the N.A. of D. six $1000, 5% R.R. bonds to be known as A.B. Baldwin Fund, for the advancement of the art of Etching — This fund, when now in the possession of the N.A. is to take the place of the $200 per annum that, for some years past has been given by Mr. B — the int. will be $300 which will improve the scale of the work. We left at 9.30 — I walked home — D. & J. took car.

B. 5 h.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 13, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very cold, clear day with strong westerly wind — to studio at 10 & put in a day on Borden etching — Home to lunch at one o'clk. after a half hours brisk walk on the sheltered & sunny side of Park Ave. & back to studio at about 2.30 — at 4.30 I went to Tho. Moran's studio, 26 W. 22 St — he was out — left for him some photo-gravure prints, portraits of himself & wife — then to Scribner's & paid small bill for books — then home. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. Carlson came at 9.15 & gave me massage till 10.30.

B. 5.
