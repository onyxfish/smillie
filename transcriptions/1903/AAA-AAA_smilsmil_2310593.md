## Left Page

### Sat. March 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very wet day, some of the showers being unusually heavy. To studio at 10 \+ worked till 1 on Borden plate. Then home to lunch which I took in my bedroom, as the Throop girls were having a girl lunch — some half dozen girls. After lunch I went to Carnegie Hall to the 7th (last) Matinee of the Boston Symphony Orchestra — Charl's ticket — I heard an Overture by Georg Schumann — a Rich? Strauss Concerto for the violin, played by Hugo Heermann \+ the superb 9th Symphony of Schubert's. It was wonderfully played \+ I enjoyed it very much.

After the Concert I walked down 5th Ave. with Charl — home at 5 o'clk — had a rest — dinner at 6.30 — Mr. Young, engaged to Miss Taylor, our visitor, dined with us — Just after 8 o'clk I went to Century \+ heard Pro\. David Smith of Columbia lecture on ancient Arithmetics with Stereoptican illustrations — He showed photos of the earliest known numerals — the basis of what are called Arabic numerals — but which are about 4000 years old \+ were found in a cave in India — he told of the slow growth of the science — its earliest literature and the peculiar devices to get results in multiplication \+ devision — There were many illustrations of ancient M.S.S. — from printed books of 15th century \+ later — He is a ready speaker — full \+ running over \+ sincerely interested in his subjects — He made it exceedingly interesting. After the lecture I had a very pleasant time chatting with Janvier about the Hudson River School — \+ with others — Home just before 12 — very heavy downpour of Rain.

B 3 h.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 22, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet, disagreeable day — Bkfs\. before 10 — I tried to do some writing but soon after 11 felt so drowsy that at last I gave up to it, lay down on my bed \+ slept till near 2 o'clk — then had dinner — Mr. Young dined with us. Afternoon, went to studio \+ spent some time looking over things there — home about 5.30 Geo\., Nellie \+ Gordon there — also Mr. Kellogg (Emily Taylor). Miss Frances Taylor \+ Mr. Young — Tea at 6.30 — We\+ I went to Charl's \+ was there till near 9 o'clk.
