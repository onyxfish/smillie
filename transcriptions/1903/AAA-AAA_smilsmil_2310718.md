## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 26, 1903

*Thanksgiving Day. Jim Ret. to New-Haven.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at about 10. Splendid day. bright, breezy \+ cold. Spent some time mounting some of my portrait photos — one with Noah II inscription for Mrs. VanHorne. Then I laid ground on Copper Plate — made tracing on gelatine from Photo Reduction of the drawing I made years ago of Mr. Arpad Gerster's head — Out for a walk at 12.15 Home to lunch at 1 — to studio at about 2 — Transferred tracing \+ traced it thro' the ground, making, in fact, a dry-point outline — then I cleared off the ground \+ began to work in an effect by dry-point — I worked till 4.30 — then home — had a rest till 6 — then dressed \+ at 7 went to Charl's with Jim \+ Ralph — Mary, Kate \+ the girls went ahead of us — Dinner was served at about 7.30. Besides my family, Geo., Nellie \+ their three boys went there — Josephine Smillie \+ Walter \+ Minnie Pierce — 16 sat down to one table — of course it was a fine dinner well served — There was at times considerable noise \+ some exhibitions that lacked refinement but all seemed to have a good time — we got up from table at 10.15. I went with Jim to G. Cent. Station where he took 11 o'clk Train for New-Haven — then Ret. to Charles — home a little before 12.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant \+ cold, but not so cold as yesterday. To studio at 10 \+ worked till 11.30 on dry-pt. commenced yesterday — then I went down to Receiver of Taxes, Chambers St. \+ got my Tax bills. The Reform administration more than doubled the value of 110 E.38. Raising it from 2150.00 to $44 000 — \+ the studio bl'dg from $7500 to $11000. increasing my Taxes on 110 from $477.42 to $622.01 Studio Tax decreased from $170.50 to $155.50 The net increase being $129.59 Then to 2d. Nat'l. Bk. \+ got a loan of $500. making $1300 borrowed during this year. — Then home at 1.30 — lunch — \+ to studio a little before 3 — continued work on dry pt. head till 4.30 — Then went to 308 W. 26th St. C. A. Gildersleeve's Sons \+ bought Remnants of printing blankets for Etching class to amt. of $4.75 Walked home getting in about 6 — dinner — Every Ge.? Cook \+ Hively came in — also Cha'. Some confusion in preparation for the start to-morrow mt 4.
