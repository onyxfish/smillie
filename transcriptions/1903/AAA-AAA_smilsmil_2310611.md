## Left Page

### Sun. April 26, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Hackley School.

An overcast day but mild + not unpleasant. I took 10.45 train + got to Tarrytown at noon — behind time. \+ to Hackley 15 m. later. Ralph met me at the gate — I was pleasantly welcomed by Mr. + Mrs. Williams + later by the other masters + their wives — I sat with R. in his room till dinner at 1 — + we discussed the situation — he wishes to join a new class in Algebra but as his time is already filled asks to drop his Latin, as a good point, the end of Caesar has just been reached — Mr. W. is not quite willing to have him give up Latin yet, but admits that he must have mathematics — suggests dropping French — But French + German are among the Exam. Requirements for entrance to Columbia Scientific + R. has Latin enough for that. I think R's position is well taken — had a talk with Mr. W. after dinner + left him still undecided — Then I went with Mr. Moore + R. to his workshops + saw R's mechanical drawings + work. Mr. M. is enthusiastic over R — so indeed is Mr. W. + other masters — R. + I then took a walk + were back in time for service at 4.20 — After that, another walk + back for tea at 6.30 — After tea, R. + I went with Mr. Gage (Latin teacher) to his Room + again the situation was discussed till 8 o'clk — Mr. G. — arguing for retention of Latin if possible but admitting that mathematics could not be ignored — He proposed as a compromise that R. join the Algebra class + until the end of this term, attend his Latin class without prep. — hoping that by some process of absorption he may be benefited — R. went with me to the gate, I got the 8.30 train to N.Y. + home by 9.30 — I wrote to Sheldon for a copy of Columbia Cat-

## Right Page

### Mon. April 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day — Soon after 5 a.m. I was routed out by a return of bowel trouble + was made uneasy all day — It was about 11 when I got to work on Borden Place — Geo. came in for a while — Home to lunch at 1.30 back at 2.30 + worked till 4.30 — Then went to Bridgeman's, in 19th St., and ordered garden seeds for Keene — on my way home stopped in 20th St. — Had a pleasant visit with Dan. Huntington now permanently crippled — the result of a street accident about two years ago — he was evidently much pleased to see me — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Mr. Cornu joined us — Eve'g, Charl came in — I wrote to Mr. Williams describing my conference with W. Gage — also to Ralph + sent him the Columbia Bulletin that I got from Sheldon.

B. 4 h.
