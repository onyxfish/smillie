## Left Page

### Thur. May 28, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Again a little tantalizing rain in the m'g. \+ after that, muggy heat \- I got up with severe headache \- had W.H. \- late \+ got to studio later \- I felt so unequal to work of any kind that about 11 I went to N.A. of D. Taking p'p. up that belonged to the Academy \- I Ret'. to studio bringing a number of copper plates that Mr. Barhydt left for me to dispose of \- Home to lunch \+ after it rested for awhile \- Carpenter came \+ fixed my bed room window. After 3 when I got to studio \- Ryan's man finished his part of work changing gas light in studio hall \- I worked at the arranging of pamphlets etc till near 6 \+ then wandered about the streets for awhile Home to dinner at 6.45 \- Mary \+ I dined alone, K. \+ E. having gone to Brooklyn to attend the ordination services of the Rev. Fred. Young \- I fooled away the eve.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 29, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

No rain yet \- pleasant \- Before going to studio at 10 I went to Jaygas \+ to Miller's \- At the studio I continued the work of arranging contents of closet \- pamphlets \- cats, etc \- \+ contents of drawers \- paper, mounting board etc. Sterne called \- also Aleen, Thomason \- Home to lunch Emily Taylor (Mr. Sew'. Kellogg) lunched with us \- Tony. The carpenter came \+ put up closet box in storeroom for my files of War Tribunes. To studio at 3 \- much of the rest of the afternoon was given to Mrs. Giles, a daughter of Gov. Maine, who called to see if I could give her points to help her find a market for some of her painting. A short walk in the streets with Geo. before I went home to dinner \- Charl came in the ev'g. I wrote to the boys.
