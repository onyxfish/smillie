## Left Page

### Tues. June 23, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very wet day - almost continuous rain - cold N.E. wind. Augusta Carlson the laundress, came at 9 o'clk. \+ arranged to meet me friday eve't. when we start for the mts. - I wrote to Mary about her - also to Ferd Beede - Then I went to studio \+ worked there till lunch time - then home - The afternoon was spent in work about the house until about 4.30 when the rain held up a little - then went to G't. Cent. Str. \+ got two sections on Sleeper for Friday night - Then to Hindly's - \+ got dark-room lantern - Then to Buskirk's for Cash book - then to Alexanders about shoes - then home before 6 - to Charl's at 7 - dinner - home at 9 - did some writing.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 24, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain all night - wet m'g, gradually drying till at 10 P.M. I saw some stars. Bkf't. at 8 - Gathering things together at home till near 11 - then to studio - To grocer - To P.O. Station f. gave summer address - then to 209 Bowery for leather castors - To K.N. \+ got Borden plate - to Vantine's \+ got tea-pot - to Scribner's, saw M. H. L. Smith - delivered plate - signed India \+ Jap. pf. - got $150 on acct. - To Second Nat'l. B'k - then home at 1.30 \+ had lunch - after that I rested - Then to Hardware store in 42d S. - bought hardware for Keene - Then to studio - got colors, brushes etc. - Then to stationers, to druggists, \+ home at 6 - Read evef. paper till 7 - then to Charl's to dinner - At 9, with the boys, went to studio - got things, took them home - then to Charl's with a pkg. of prints that father had selected for him \+ finally, at 10, home for the night.
