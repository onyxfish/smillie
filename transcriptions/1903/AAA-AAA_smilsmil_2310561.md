## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 16, 1903

*my 70th Birthday.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant mild day — to studio at 10 \+ worked on Borden place till 12 — then feeling unfit for work I loitered on the sunny side of Park Ave — Lunch at 1 — After that I gave up to my discomforts \+ spent the rest of the afternoon on my bed, getting some sleep — Up at 5 — finished letters to the boys \+ at 6 had dinner — Ineena, Mary \+ Grace came promptly — I managed to get through rather dismally — I thought I made a surprise when I announced my birthday — but after dessert, a cake with seven candles — one for each ten years was brought in — After dinner the Delands, Mary, Kate \+ I went to Wallacks 30 \[illegible\] \+ a B away \+ saw the Duchess of Dulham — an absurd jumble of nonsense with many catchy airs — Howlan was as broadly comical as ever — he is very funny but I wish he were less of a clown — Mme Gertrude Quinlan was as wild \+ rushing as when I last saw her two or three years ago — the play was showily put upon the stage — the singing was fairly good — While amused I could not but feel that it was all much below my ideal, the Mikado or Patience — 11.30 when we got home.

B. 2 h.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 17, 1903

**Weather:** Clear, windy \+ cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

It was nearly 2 o'clk this m'g. when I got to sleep — \+ 5 o'clk when I awoke, after which I got little more sleep, consequently I got up feeling little rested. To studio at 10 \+ worked on Borden plate till 12.30 — then a short walk on the sunny side of Park Ave. \+ to lunch at 1.15 — After lunch, feeling the need of rest, I lay down upon my bed \+ had two hours of sleep — At 5 I went to studio \+ then again my usual walk on Park, Ave. Dinner at 6.30 — Ann Sutphen came to dinner with the girls \+ left, to meet her father, immediately after — Eve'g I went to Century \+ heard Dr. W. H. Ward's on the engraving of Seals \+ Gems in the Early East — It was little more than a succession of stereopticon photo. slides \+ impressions from seal cylinders — very much jumbled (to me) chronologically — with inadequate description — much enthusiasm over beauties that I could not find together with many free \+ fantastic theories as to emblems \+ meanings that failed to carry conviction to my mind — After the lecture I chatted with Meing — had some very interesting talk from Haven [illegible] as to Gen'l. Greene \+ police matters — Home about midnight.

B. 2½ h.
