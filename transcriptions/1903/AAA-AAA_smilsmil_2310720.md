## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 30, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - after 10 when I got to studio - I have undertaken to do the family marketing \+ that sometimes make an appreciable hole in my morning time - I got to work on "An Adirondack Lake, 14×18 - \+ painted all of them f (what was left of it) then home to lunch after a short walk - to studio at 2.30 \+ worked as long as I could see on the little "October, after a stormy day" - then to Clausen's \+ ordered two frames 14 × 18 \+ 12×18. Then home at 5 \+ had a rest before dinner at 6.30 - Eve\+ wrote two letters to Mary - one addressed to the Staumburgh Hotel, where her party will stop for a day or two, on the presumption that the steamer Kron Prinz Wilhelm will get to Hamburg on the same day that the Patricia does - The other, care Morgan, Harjis \+ Co - Paris - Charle came in - I wrote a note to Jim. with check - Then I went to Charle's \+ till 10.30 was looking over photo-prints from negatives that he took while in Europe last Summer. Some of them were very beautiful -

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 1, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day - to studio at about 10 \+ worked on 12 × 18 "Approaching Storm" till lunch time. Chas. Kurtz came in \+ got my signature to appear to State legislature for enlarged appropriation for art exhibit to St. Louis Exp\[osition\]. Home to lunch after going to Miller's about lap-boards for etching class - Back to studio \+ worked on "Pool in the woods, eve\+" as long as I could see. Then to Brooks Bro\+. \+ bought a pair of Trowsers - then home before 6 - dinner at 6.30 - Eve\+ worring with bills, accts. \+ balancing bank acct.
