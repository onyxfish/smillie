## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 10, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clonded m't, clearing to a mild \+ very pleasant day. To studio at 10 to work painting on "Coming storm" \+ I painted till near 1 - then, as Jennings could not coat Ralphie's wheel I went up to Borrmann's \+ arranged with him to do it - then home to lunch. To studio at 2.30 - painted till after 4 - then went to Exp Co. to have R's wheel sent for. Then a little walking - home at 5 - Read paper till dinner - Eve'g Reading Charities Report on Juvenile Court - corrected some notes.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 11, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charl arr. on St. Hohenzollern.

Pleasant day. To studio at 10 - painted on an old panel - done years ago - a bit of the ram shackle buildings at Atlantic City - I christened the painting at the time it was done: "Our Coast Offices". I repainted the sky solidly. About 11 I rec'd. telegram that the Hohenzollern upon which Charl sailed from Genoa Oct. would reach her dock at 2.30 - home to lunch at 1 - at 1.30 went to foot of Christopher S. - crossed to Hoboken \+ got to docks of North German Lloyds at 2.15 The steamer came up at about 2.30, was worked into her dock \+ passengers came ashore at about 3 - I exchanged salutations with Charl who was on upper deck as the vessel was coming up to the dock - The Custom Ho. nuisance was soon over but it was near 4 before luggage was piled on carriage \+ we started for home - unloaded at Charl's house. Then he went with me to 110 - I went to studio \+ cleaned up. Charl \+ George dined with us \+ later we all went to C's house \+ were there looking at photos \+ other things till near 11.
