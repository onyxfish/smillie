## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 8, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - Trumbull came \+ I spent much of the m'f with him laying out the work on mantel piece in my bed room - He was completely at a loss \+ I not only bossed the job but I did much of the work - About noon I went to hotel for mail - Dinner at 1 - all hands off for entertainment at the Country Club - Trumbull got the mouldings \+ carvings for my mantel piece fitted \+ I made up a stain \+ applied it - Eve'g. some reading.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 9, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain nearly all day - strong south wind - Bkf't. at 9 \+ after that I kindled fire in studio \+ when it got comfortably warm I commenced the writing of a letter to Mr. H'y Kent at Montrose. This occupied much of the m'f till dinner at 1.30 - Had considerable dear exercise on the piazzas \+ a little more writing on W's. Kent's letter - The usual Sunday afternoon tea \+ strong contingent of maidens - I took no supper but instead, walked the piazza - Eve'g. some more writing - Very luminous moonlight - more walking on South piazza.
