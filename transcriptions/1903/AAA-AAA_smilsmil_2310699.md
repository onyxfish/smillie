## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 19, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

miss Buck left.
Kate came from Po[?]

Unpleasant windy m'f. clearing into a fine day \- Bkf. at 8.30 \- After that I went to 2°. Nat. Buck \+ then to studio \- uncomfortably cold \- Fussed about, cleaning up \- Could not build a fine m' heater because Higgins took away gauge. Nicole came in \+ we talked of making collection of etchings for St. Louis Fair \- Then home to lunch \- back at 2.30 \+ met Patrick there \- he shook rugs \+ promised to clean the windows to-morro. Ives at N.a.f.D. called me up on 'phone \+ told me that the Surety co. had sent a note to N.a. with a printed form of questions concerning C. S. Farrington \+ that the list \+ Request of Surety Co. would be considered at a meeting of officers of U.a. to be held in Mr. Diedman's Studio \- W. 10th St. at 4.30 \- I walked down to D's \+ found him in conference with Talsey Ives \+ Chas. Kurtz concerning request that N.a. apply to State authorities having charge of a proposed appropriation to aid exhibitors for St. Louis Exposition, for control of a portion of that fund to apply to the Art exhibit from N.Y. Later Jones, Watrous, now, de Forest came in \+ it was agreed that there w' take action \- Ives \+ Kurtz then left \+ the Surety Co's request was considered \- J and C.F.D. letter to me \- It was agreed to reply to the 2.1. Surety Co. saying the N.A. desired to be excused from answering the questions. Then I went to Wanamaker's \+ home to dinner \- After that took Ralph's wheel to Borrmann's \- some newspaper reading \- then to bed early.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant \+ milder, so that it was warmer out of doors than in the house. Bkf. at 8.30 \- After that to studio \+ till 11 o'clk I was fussing about, Ther. at 58°, writing for Higgins, to attend to heater in cellar, \+ for Patrick to clean the windows \- A little after 11 Patrick came \+ by 12.5 he had the work done \- I got the place so in order that I could have gone to work if the studio has been warm enough \- Higgins did not put in an appearance \+ at 1 I went home to lunch \- After it I felt quite unfit for effort of any kind \+ it was not till after 4 o'clk that I went out. Took note to Moody up to P. O. Lex. Ave. \+ 44th \- Then to studio \- then to Higgins', 430 4th Ave., left note for him. Then to Dade's, paid bill, \+ home at 6 o'clk. The plumber at work on W. C. second story \- Dinner before 7 \- Evening with newspaper.
