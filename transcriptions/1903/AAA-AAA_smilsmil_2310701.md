## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 23, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day - Rain after 3 p.m. \+ thro' the even't. I went to studio after 6 o'c \+ fussed about there till after 11 - then home \+ wrote part of a letter to Ralph. lunch at 1.30 - At 3 I went to Nat. Arts Club in W. 34th S\. \+ had a long talk with Ives \+ Kurtz concerning the Etching exhibit, its location \+ environment at St. Louis Exp\. Will Low was present \+ got the dimensions of various galleries \+ corridors - All plans \+ [illegible] positions were, of course, purely tentative. Home about 5 - finished Ralph's letter - Dinner at 6.30 - Even't. The house looked quite like home again, the carpets having been laid during the day. I finished letter to Jim \+ mailed both (to R. \+ J.) before 10 -

During the day I had interviews with Patrick Conefry - paid him $4.00 \+ discharged him - Tho. Rowland - paid him $4.00 for work done before he discharged himself \+ Ernest Jennings whom I took into my employ, vice Patrick C.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 24, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very much cooler - cloud \+ sunshine - to studio after 10 - The water gauge had not yet been sent to studio so I went to Roberts on 4th Ave. below 32d St\. to get it - went back to studio \+ put it on heater \+ turned on the water - It took some time to fill the system \+ let out the air - Jennings was there \+ when the water was all in he kindled fire. Home before 1 - lunch after 1 - for some time I was quite used up by a headache that had come unannounced - I gave up to it for a time after lunch - At 3.30 went to studio - fussed about there till after 5 - then took a short walk with [illegible]. Home at about 6. Dinner near 7 - Fred's Young \+ Woolsey Carmalt dined with us informally - long letter rec'd. from Charl at Innsbruck - Spent the even't in my room.
