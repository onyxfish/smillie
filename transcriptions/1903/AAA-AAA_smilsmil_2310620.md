## Left Page

### Thur. May 14, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m't. clearing into a warm cloudless day. To studio at 10 + worked on Borden plate till 1 — then home to lunch — back soon after 2 + worked till 4.30 — then feeling very tired I went home + slept till 6.30 — then up for dinner — W. Fred't. Young dined with us + later took the two girls (K. + E.) out. Charl came in. Emma Bourne was well enough to get up + came in to the parlor for a few minutes in her wrapper.

B. 5.30 h.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 15, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came home.

Very mild + pleasant — some heavy clouds that looked almost like Rain. I worked at studio from 10 to 12.30 on id. plate — then to 2d. Nat'l Bank + home at 1 — lunch. To studio at 2 + worked till 4.30 — then home + had a rest till 6. Jim came from New Haven at 3.30 — he has engagement with dentist to-morrow m't. We have very discouraging news from Mollie Adams — Typhoid, Pneumonia — + both lungs affected. Dinner a little earlier than usual — got my letter to Ralph finished + started for N.A.D. at 7.15 getting there at 8 — Presentation of prizes to students — Miss Chadwick got Hon. mention in her class — Antique — Head. Zimmermann got 1st Prize in Etching class (+ a lot of other prizes — Schlee got 2d prize + Andresezky got Hon. men. Power O'Malley got a lot of prizes + Maurice Sterne got the Mooney Scholarship of $700 per. an. for two years study abroad — The ceremony was interesting + the students were comically enthusiastic. After the awards — there was dancing — then I came away — Rode down town with Dielman, Low, Wiles + Frank Jones. Home at 10.15

B. 5 h.
