## Left Page

### Sun. July 5, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant mth. clouded over by 10 a.m. \+ light showers after 3 P.M. Wetf. clouds lifting. Up at 6.30. Ther. in my bed room 60° \- Kindled a little fire \+ enjoyed my rub down between sunshine \+ fire glow \- Bkfp. at 9 \- mth 3 loafed about \- wrote to N.L. Smith sending bill for Borden plate No. 2.
Dinner at 1.30 \- owing to Rain Ralph \+ I lost one walk to the burned district. I had too much headache to enjoy reading or work of any kind \- prom\- enaded piazzas \- Had a talk with Augusta, the laundress, about her home sickness \- She has said that she can not stay any longer \+ must go back to the City. She told me that she we try to hold on. The Swedes had a jolly time at night, with half a dozen men callers, concertina music \+ singing. There may be too much of it \- To bed early \- still headachy.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 6, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain last night \- clearing commenced in the early mth. \- a glorious day followed \+ a cool \+ cloudless moonlight night. From my east window, at about 6.30 A.M. I saw a fawn pass across an open space on the wooded hillside a few hundred feet away \- altogether, it was a pretty sight long to be remembered. I spent a good part of the mth. writing directions to Wm. Miller \+ at 11.30 went to hotel \+ mailed letter \+ plans to him \- Sat upon hotel pi\- azza \+ read newspaper \- then home \- dinner \- a loafing afternoon \- About 5 Ralph \+ I went to hotel \- then walked around the block \- i.e. \- down by the Putnam Camp, to the north \- up by the new Road \- tea at 6.30 \- after having stopped at the hotel garden \+ got from the old gardener a lot of lettuce \- Wetf. \- some loafing on piazzas \- \+ a little reading.
