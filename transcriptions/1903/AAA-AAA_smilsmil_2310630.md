## Left Page

### Wed. June 3, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm day. Ther. 83°. to studio at 10.30 - to work on Borden plate - home about 1 - lunch - back at 2.30. worked till 4.30 - \+ did a little photo printing between times - At 4.30 went to K.P. in Canal St. \+ arranged to be there on Sat. m'g to have p.p. from Borden plate - Then walked up Elm St. to see the march of improvement there \+ to look over the Striker's battle ground. Watched subway work for some time in Astor Place - Then rode home. dinner - Josephine Smillie dined with us. after she left Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 4, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Smoke from forest fires hangs over the whole state - in this city it is so heavy as to diminish the sunlight greatly, the sun looking like a copper ball - The smell of the smoke is very strong making the eyes smart. Accts from Keene Valley pub. in the papers are very disquieting - Dr. Daight is said to have left his house at night because of the falling burning embers. I went to studio at 10 \+ worked on Borden plate till 10 - home to lunch - back at 3. \+ fussed about till 5 - then strolled in the streets till after 6 - then home - dinner Ev'g. with news paper till Charl came in -

This was the day fixed for Mary \+ the rest of the family to start for Keene Heights, but the start is now indefinitely postponed because of the forest fires.
