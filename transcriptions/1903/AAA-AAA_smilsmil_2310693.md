## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 7, 1903

At home in E. 38th S.

Disturbed during the past night by cramps in the legs \+ other discomforts — About 5 Am. a sudden shower raised a racket on the tin roof of Piazza under one of my windows — Up about 7 — bkfs\. about 8 \+ at 10.30 we got away for West Port — I drove — Clouded day but not unpleasant — got to R.R. station about 11.40 \+ spent the time before arrival of Train at 12.20 getting trunks checked — freight billed \+ mileage books bought — Very soon after getting under way, or at near 1 o'clk. we went into Dining car \+ had a satisfactory dinner — at Albany we had a long wait of 40 min. Till 5 o'clk — when we were hitched onto a Western Exp. \+ went thro' without stops free near the city when we were stalled by trains ahead \+ got in to G.C. Station at 8.30 — half an hour late — went at once to the house — mizzling rain — Kate, Leonard \+ Jane Banks there — making things as pleasant as possible for us — we had a light supper — our trunks came after 9 — \+ then we got to bed

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 8, 1903

Rain in showers until about the middle of the nt — when it set in steadily \+ at time came down in floods — some heavy thunder during the evef. I got up it 7 \+ went at 8 o'clk. to the newsman \+ to the grocer — it was not till 8.30 that we got any bkfs\. — it was after 10 when I went to studio — I saw George — then home in the rain that increased so in violence that I did not go out again but emptied my trunk \+ wrote part of a letter to Ralph — a modest dinner at 6.30 — unpacking, reading newspapers \+ writing in the evef.

The fall of Rain to day is given as over 6 in. the lower portion of the city flooded.
