## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 18, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day though cloudy most of the time. — I got up at the usual time feeling very weak & seedy but I managed to keep about all day growing stronger. — Ralph & I went to Maghee's & then to White's where we saw the old iron piping that we may have for our electrical subway — We went into the house & had a pleasant visit with Mr. White. — Men to McHolts Home to dinner about 1 — After that went to hotel again — The hauling of gravel onto the road goes on slowly. — Beede's team hauling it from Chappee Pond. — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. Reading —

Kate sat up in her bed for about 15 m. Extremely weak. — The Dr. came to see her twice — Miss Mars came & spent some time with her in the eve'g.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 19, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very variable weather during the mtg. — bright at first — thin clouds down over Giant — then a hazy, muggy dog-day kind of weather with very violent thunder storms after 4 p.m. continuing in rapid & violent succession until about 8 p.m. There was hail & tremendously heavy rain that cut the roads up & deluged my room. — Trumbull came in the mtg. — Put in 2 large plates of glass in alcove & finished up his work about 3 p.m. No team from Beede. — I worked about out of doors but the proximity of thunder storms unfitted me for studio work & writing letters.

Eve'g. Jim went to an entertainment (Book Party) at Mr. Nielson's. — Young Miss Taylor made preparations for departure to-morrow, dependent upon conditions of the roads, bridges etc. — The roar of the river on one side & Roaring brook on the other was very impressive when heard at night.

Kate got thro' the day fairly well, making no marked improvement. The Dr. came to see her twice.
