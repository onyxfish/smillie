## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 25, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful fall day — mild, quiet \+ sunny. I got up after a somewhat uncomfortable night, not having physical energy enough to keep my bed warm. Bessie \+ Ralph went off for a days tramps up Gothics. I moved about carefully, picking the last mess of peas for the season — I wrote a note to Mr. Kent \+ rested for a short hour before dinner — After that I went down to the gate to see how Otis was getting on hauling stone for the wall. Later I walked over to Scott Browns \+ arranged with him to Telephone to Silas Cross at E. town to learn if he could come to me for two days next week to lay wall. Then home \+ transplanted one of the strawberry plants sent by Bridgeman the roots encased in clayey soil that I removed by soaking in water. Bessie \+ Ralph came in at 6 pm after a very successful day — Tea before 7 — Eve'g, loafing about.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 26, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day of the hazy, lazy Oct. type. I spent most of the m'f out of doors, much of the time garden. At noon went to hotel — Dinner soon after 1 — Then Ralph \+ I spent some time in studio, I taking several photos. of him — Then I transplanted the strawberry plants I rec'd. from Bridgemans. Scott Brown sent over \+ got a load of corn stalks for fodder — part, the stalks from which I had gathered the corn — \+ part, the last planting upon which the corn will not ripen — To-day I gathered the first mess of "shoe-peg" corn. Tea at about 7 — Eve'g, podged about at little odd jobs.
