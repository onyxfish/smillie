## Left Page

### Sat. May 2, 1903

*Emma Bourne came.*

**Weather:** Bright \+ pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasant \- Got to studio at 10 \+ worked till 1 \- Then home to lunch \- Emma Bourne there \- came down from Yonkers \- At 2 o'clk. I went to Madison Ave. to see the Parade of the Police Dept. It did not come along till after 2.30 \- I got an excellent position on steps of church cor. of 35th St. The Parade was imposing because such a compact mass (5000) of men physically splendid \- I did not wait for the whole procession to pass \- Got to studio soon after 3 \- found man with ton of coal \+ laborer waiting \- Ryan's shop shut up \- could not get cellar window open \- after a long delay I pried the padlock off \+ the coal was put in the bin \- Then Otto (Etching Class student) came \+ I gave him his plate \+ some time \+ after him John J (another student) came with his plate \- Calverly made a long visit \- ge\? spent the P.M. at studio painting \- I went home at 5 \+ had a rest before dinner \- Eve\+ went to Century \- monthly meeting \- had an agreeable time till after 11 \- The Chauncey tragedy was a sad note \-

B\-3h.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 3, 1903

**Weather:** Clouded day \- some sunshine \- showers  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded day \- some sunshine \- showers at night \- Bkfst. at 9.30 \- later, I went to studio \+ wrote there for a time \- Then I had a short walk before going to Charli's dinner at 2. At 3 went to N.A. of D. School, 109. St met Sterne \+ was there looking over the work of the etching class till near 6 \- Then walked about two miles on my way home \+ rode the rest of the way \- getting in at 7.30 \- had no supper \- Eve\+ wrote out my tentative plan for management of N.A. Etching class under Baldewin fund \+ expenditure of its income \+ mailed it to Dielman
