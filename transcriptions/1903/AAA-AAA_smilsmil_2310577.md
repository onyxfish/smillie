## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 17, 1903

**Weather:** 10 in. of snow  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow ceased falling in the n'f — gradual clearing — bright afternoon — high wind growing very cold — It was late before I got to studio — Etched on Borden pl. till near 1 — then to Douglas's \+ paid him $150 on acct. — then home to lunch — back to studio soon after 2 \+ etched for nearly an hour — then went up to Harvard Club — Carnegie Bl'f. 7 "Ave \+ 56"S. \+ heard Chs. Battell Loomis read a number of his comic skits \+ sketches — He is a cadaverous, melancholy looking man of middle age with no evidence of humor or fun in his face — He read in a clear, unaffected manner — made his points without forcing them \+ in Irish dialect was very good — His feminine audience was greatly amused. The reading was over at about 5 \+ I walked from 56'S. down to E. 39"S. to Ed M. Cook's where I met Walter \+ George C — \+ we discussed the matter of five ins. \+ Ireland Real Estate Co — Home at 6.30 — dinner — We \+ Clark came — At 9.30 Carlson came \+ gave me a full hour of treatment.

B 3h.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 18, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold with high wind — Ther. as low as 6° during the night — 22° the highest — To studio at 10 — Etched on Borden plate — Home to lunch at 1 — back soon after 2 \+ etched till 4. At 5 went to Buskerk's — 6 "Ave. 34-5" S? then to Toath's Gallery \+ saw Ranger's paintings, or rather, Paintings by Ranger — then to Clausen's \+ ordered a couple of small frames for Etching pfs. \+ some mounting board — then to studio — some one has been meddling with the heater fire — opening draughts — \+ it was burned out — so, unwilling to leave the bl'f without fire during such weather I kindled it afresh \+ waiting to see it well started, it was 6.30 when I got home. Dinner. Eve'f, loafing before the fire with newspaper — bad habit.

B 4h.
