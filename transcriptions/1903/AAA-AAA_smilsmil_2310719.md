## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 28, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary, Kate - Katharine & Bessie sailed for Hamburg on the Patricia.

Beautiful day - Up early & bkft. before 8 - much confusion. Preparation for start on ocean voyage - Mary flustered. Bessie sick - Kate nervous. Katharine mildly moved. Carriage & baggage wagon came before 9 - the seven trunks, half dozen suit cases & two or three rolls & rugs & wraps were gathered together & put into the wagon. At 9.15 Ralph \+ I started for steamer - met Charl on his way to the house - we went to Christophers'. Homy & Hamburg Am. docks in Hoboken - we went generally over the steamer - inspected the two state rooms for our party - felt a strong desire to join them - Charl and wife, women & baggage half an hour after us & managed every thing - had a letter to the Captain from an officer of the Co. strongly recommending the party to his care - the Cap. was very agreeable about it - seats at the table were secured - only 42 first class passengers - the boat is immense - slow - for passengers & freight - not in a hurry - pass. accommodations crowded - Mr. Magee, Adelaide & Hatt. Van Brome came to see them off - Bessie save going to the dock too late to get aboard but tossed good byes & kisses from the dock where Charl, Ralph & I joined her. We watched the steamer till she floated down stream, then we ret'd. to the City - went to Tax office & paid taxes - $622.01 on 110 - $155.50 on studio bld. Then a walk to Brooklyn bridge - then to studio - then home to lunch - soon after 2 I went to N.A. of D. \+ met members of Willing class - laid grounds, ground paints &c - home before 6 - Ralph had been to Keith's - my treat - dinner at 6.30 - a very quiet affair - as was the evening that followed - to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 29, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph Rel'd. to Stackley

Overcast m'f. - snow in the air - cold & penetrating - a few tiny specks fell - clearing late - fine moonlight night. It was near 10 before I had bkft. I read news paper & tried to do some photographic printing. At 1 o'clk Ralph & I went out for a little airing. We went into Charl's for a few minutes. Home to dinner at 2 - loafed about during the afternoon - Tea at 6.30 - Charl came in for a few minutes - Evelf. went to station with Ralph who ret'd. to Stackley on 8.35 train. Then Rec'd. home & spent rest of evef. reading.
