## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 30, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very high wind during the night which continued during the day \- The m'f opened wet but that soon passed \+ it grew colder \+ clearer till night. It was late when I got to studio \- I got out an old painting "in the woods near the Huddle, Lake George" \+ commenced repainting that \- Lunch at home, usual time \- afternoon again painted \+ completed work on "Huddle Woods" \- for the present at least. Went to Clausen's \+ ordered frame for little painting for Sallie Cook \- then home \- excitement at Chat. Barney's corner \- 3875 \& Park Ave \- His house shows signs of settling into the Rapid Transit Tunnel \+ emergency men are showing it up \+ the two houses adjoining on north side \- worked all night \- Dinner at 6.30. Ev\+ I wrote to the boys \- mailed letters before 11 \- then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 31, 1903

**Weather:** Clear, cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold \+ high wind \- neuralgic pains in my face troubled me thro' the night \+ consequently I had hlf. late \- \+ got to studio about 10.30 \- I worked during the m'f on Charl's painting "A back Road" \+ put in white cow \+ other details which finishes it for the present \- Home to lunch at 1 \- After that I felt so out of sorts, mainly because of continued discomfort in face \+ loss of rest last night that I gave up to it \+ lay upon my bed till about 4.30 \- then to Studio \+ cleaned up there \-
Dinner at 6.30 \- Ev\+ I went to Century where Dr. Carldemcholtz discoursed to us of his explorations in North western Mexico \- He was not a fluent speaker \+ tho' he spoke with a marked German accent his English was admirable \- His photos were interesting but not always good \- that could not be expected \- he limited himself to descriptions of two tribes of Indians \+ their cave dwellings are to be found in more extended shape in book \- adding now \+ then a little dry humor. I left the club immediately after the lecture \+ went home.
