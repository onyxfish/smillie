## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. Up after a wretched, un restful night, the pain \+ other discomfort having been continuous till about 5 A.m. I went to studio about 10. Cleaned up palette \+ things left yesterday \+ then went to Keppel's where I selected a few prints, Armand-Durand reproductions after Rembrandts, etc. for the N.A. Etching class. Then to Einver \+ Amend's to order Acid etc for N.A. class — Then to studio \+ home to lunch. To studio again at 2.30 — spent the rest of the afternoon making up a lot of Etching ground for class. Before 6 I went to Alice Ireland's — 40 E. 5th \+ got Jewitt portrait of Geo. Ireland, to clean — had a talk with Alice about Jim — took portrait to studio. Then home to dinner late — Evey wrote to the boys — Charl came in about 9 with a lot of photo negatives — I begged off — too tired to look at them.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 21, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day but cold. Immediately after wdp. I went to N.A. 4th. to meet etching class, lay grounds \+ prepare for first work next Wed. p.m. I had expected Stahr to be there but he was not. I met many difficulties owing to lack of necessary tools or materials of one kind \+ another \+ was obliged to give up, leaving to Stahr the work of laying grounds when he comes with rollers, etc in the afternoon. I walked down thro' the Park to Mad. Ave. \+ 76th St. thence home by car. lunch — to studio at 2.30 washed the Jewett portrait of Geo. Ireland \+ then "brought it out" by rubbing a very thin coat over it of a mixture of Siccatif de Harlem. linseed oil \+ distilled Sp.ts turpentine. I had a job in cleaning up the vessels in which I made etching ground yesterday. Wrote it about 5. Rested before dinner. Mr. Fred Young \+ his wife (miss Frances Taylor) took dinner with us — Evey. I went to Charles' \+ was there till near 11 o'clk — looking at ten or twelve dozen negatives — Taken by him while abroad last season.
