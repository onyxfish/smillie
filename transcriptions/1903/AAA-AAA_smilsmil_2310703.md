## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day but still rather too breezy to be comfortable. After bkft. Packed Ralph's Eider-down + took it to Am. Exp. then to Miller, the carpenter - then to studio getting there after 10 - Set palette + started work on one of the sketches made last summer at Keene 14"×18". Home to lunch at 1 - back to studio's at 2.30 - Painted - Maurice Sterne came + showed me a lot of etchings (life on the beach at Coney Island) made by him last summer. Cleaned up at about 5 - then to Clausen's + ordered 14"× 18" frame - a short walk + home at 6.20 - dinner - Katharine + Elizabeth have gone to O.K. for the Taylor-Young wedding - Swett, Geo. + Emily Cook called - I took Jim's wheel to Boormann + brought Ralph's home.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 28, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day. To studio about 10 o'clk. Continued Painting on 14×18 - Home to lunch - back + painted till near 4 o'clk - Fredk. Strahs there came in to learn something about starting the etching class at N.A. of D. I had a short walk - then home + rested till dinnertime - after dinner I went to Com. on Admissions - Century - The name of Geo. Cummings came up \+ was heavily black-balled - It will be a great surprise to him when he learns of it - Recess at 11 o'clk. - when I excused myself + went home.
