## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 30, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another day like unto yesterday. It was dull + leaden till about 10 o'clk. when the rain began + continued during the rest of the day. I was in the garden during a large part of the m'f + in consequence we had a notable dinner of vegetables. peas, beans, Beet Tops, carrots + lettuce - The beef was good - so was the ice cream. I was for a time in studio after dinner, reading - about 4 I went to hotel + got mail - then some pacing on piazza deck. Eve'g. Reading newspapers - Took my eve'g meal about 9 o'clk. For a little while working on plans for decoration on my bed room mantel piece

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 31, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'f. - for a time during the fore-noon there was promise of clearing - but later it clouded over + about 5 o'clk. rain came o'lee quite heavily during the eve'g. For some time after bkf. I worked in the garden. Then as water was running into the tank in driblets I went to hotel stables + to Scott Brown's to learn the cause - learned only that since spring floods the pipes had been more or less choked by dirt of various kinds, + almost every body had complaints to make. Got mail + then, while at hotel, got, by telephone, message from Chas. that he had arr'd safely at Plymouth, England. Dinner at 1. After that, as the water supply seemed to have ceased altogether Raeph + I started out to get at the cause. Went to Highland Cottage, where our pipe starts + found they had no water. The troubles was beyond them - Miss Miller had no water - troubles beyond her. Wesley Otis, who knows all about the pipes (a complex matter) was away - not expected before Wed. After a great deal of running back + forth + enquiries I was told of a cut-off that could be reached by a little digging outside the hotel. engineer found as small stick so jammed in the pipes. When removed we had full water supply again. I got home before 6, not wet but uncomfortably damp - changed clothes, supper, Eve'g, reading newspapers.
Rec'd an amusing letter from Emma Ireland-Davis with Julean Cheney at Indian Neck - Brawford, Conn, where she has found an odd sort of a public house, built over a boat with a big sign on top "The Ark" - she sends it to me as a suggestion to me when I weary of my Ark here.
