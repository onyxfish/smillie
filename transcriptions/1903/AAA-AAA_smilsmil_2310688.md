## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 27, 1903

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'g. with S.W. wind \+ threats of coming storm \- At 2 o'clk. a heavy thunderstorm came up suddenly \- short duration \- after that it cleared pleasantly \+ milder copper. I had my bkfs. at 9 o'clk \- at 10, in garden got a fine mess of Stowell's Evergreen \+ Country Gentleman corn. It was nearly noon when Raelph \+ I went into the dark room \+ developed a dozen of his films. I think there are two excellent portraits of himself \+ a good view of the Ark. At 1.30 had dinner \- Mary confined to her bed by a bowel trouble. At about 4 I went to Maghee's \+ returned. Some Nos. of the Studio, borrowed some time ago. After a short visit there I went to Hotel \+ got mail \- back home after 5. It was 6.30 when we had supper \- I had a pleasant little fire in my bedroom \+ there Ralph \+ I sat \- he reading \+ I writing a letter to Jim.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 28, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph left for N.Y.

A very disagreeable day \- fitful, stormy sunshine \- sweeping, shivery showers followed by bright sunshine \+ rainbows. Cold \+ cloudless moonlight at night. Up before 6 \- went to hotel at 7. \+ by getting into the stage there secured a seat for Ralph \- stage more than full of servants leaving \- I changed seats with R. \- at the gate where he \+ Katharine were waiting. Bkfs. at 7.30 Trumbull came \+ worked all day putting up winter shutters over plate glass \+ protecting exposed. electric wires where they were likely to receive injury \- I spent some time with him \- also in garden \- then, much of m'g. taking photo. Prints from the negatives developed yesterday. At noon went to hotel \+ was caught in hard shower \- D. T. Mr [illegible] on Piazza of Highland Cottage \- one small umbrella between them \- the D. walked with me to the hotel. Dinner at 1.30 \- the afternoon was fooled away \- Tea at about 7. \- Evely wrote several notes \+ a letter to Ralf.

Otis put in some work.
