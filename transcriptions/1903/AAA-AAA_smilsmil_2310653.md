## Left Page

### Tues. July 21, 1903

**Weather:** Several Thunder showers  **Temp:** [illegible]

Several Thunder showers — some of them very heavy — practically a wet day. Mollie Adams \+ Curtis Walker called soon after 11 a.m., just before a heavy shower, to take Ralph with them into camp at Upper Lake for a couple of days — his stiff neck was excuse enough. I worked on the painting that I commenced yesterday \+ after pretty steady work got it painted in by 6 p.m. Did not go for mails in m't owing to rain — went after dinner \+ then to W. Maghee's \+ got a Cat. of Electric supplies, which R. \+ I studied over in the evef. in preparation for installment of telephone \+ bell communication bet. the two houses. Very tired in the evef. but after a nap read news paper.

Trumbull finished casing piazza posts.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 22, 1903

**Weather:** Heavy showers, with much thunder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy showers, with much thunder, all day long. I got out of the house but once when Ralph \+ I went to hotel about 11 a.m. My work in doors was on Petty Cash acct? most of the time. I made a start on rewriting the m.s. of Father's "Pilgrimage". I propose to put it in such form, by leaving out what is purely private, that I may have a very limited dition printed for which I have already etched two portraits — the Quebec one by Heaton 1827 — \+ the full length Po'keepsie. 1840 — I hope to etch a third, after an oil sketch I have painted, based upon the photo. that Charl took of him at his desk on his 75th birth day.
