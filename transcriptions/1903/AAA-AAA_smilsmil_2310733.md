## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 26, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow in the mt. \- a heavy squall with unusual darkness for a few minutes at about 9 a.m. clearing shortly to a bright \+ an increasingly cold \+ windy day. Ther. Ran from 32°. in the mt. to 8° at night. At 10.30 I went to N.A. 34th— 10.57 S. \+ met other members of com. of Selection \+ awarded prizes — exceedingly disagreeable duty for me. I did not thing exhibited was of such preeminent \+ distinguished excellence as to be worthy the distinction of selection for prize. It was 12.30 before we got thro. with it — On my way home stopped for a short time at conflagration \- 52°. 6. \+ 7. Ave — The old "Adelphi" — bitter cold — Lunch soon after 1 — the afternoon I gave up to resting as I find that I have little ambition \+ no reserve. Dinner at 6.30 \- at 8 went to the New Lyceum Theatre. 45th S. near 7th Ave — with Jim \+ Ralph \+ there met Emma \+ Mrs Ireland who presented us with the tickets. We saw Mr Gillette in Barrie's farce-act "Pantasy" "The Admirable Crichton". A story of a butler who with an Earl \+ his family, in a private yacht, is wrecked upon an uninhabited tropical island — The butler being the superior party comes naturally to the top in the course of three years \+ social Relations are awkwardly reversed. \- The butler as governor directing the Earl \+ his nephew in their menial employments \+ making love to the eldest daughter. This gives Gillette a fine chance to display his strong hold in acting \- that is, his colossal egotism — his Ready assurance \+ quiet superiority. There is no perceptible differance between his Sherlock Holmes \+ his Admirable Crichton \- Emma gave him \+ Myra ticket \- she invited Allean Wollman — we could not find Emma's cab after the performance, we all took a Broadway Car \- E. \+ Mrs — insisted upon going on alone, so we took a transfer at 34th [illegible]

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 27, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow in the mt — clear before noon — cold, but less windy — Bkft. about 9.30 \+ loafed about till near 1 — then to Charlie's got correct time — called for a few moments at Ed Wds Cook's to tell them there that Catharine's portrait is hung in N.A. 34th — Dinner at 2 \- at about 4 the boys \+ I went to Emma Ireland's — met the Misses Emerson there — left at 5 \+ walked home — Supper at 6.30 — Eve'g by parlor fire, Reading.
