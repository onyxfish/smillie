## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 10, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cablegram Rec'd. from Mary — went to Boston Symphony Concert.

Fine winter day — to studio \+ commenced repainting Passing Showers — going over what I painted on Tues. last. Home to lunch at usual time after a little walk \+ breathing in Park Ave. Afternoon I did not feel in working time so I cleaned up at 3.30 \+ later went down to Wanamakers to do a little shopping — walked up home \+ had a Rest before dinner — Charl. Ret'd. from Boston last night. At 7.45 he came \+ took me to Boston Symphony Concert at Carnegie Hall — Heard Midsummer Night's Dream — Heard it first 56 years ago — 1847 — at Castle Garden — Heard Melba sing Handel's "Sweet bird that shun'st the noise of folly" with flute obligato — a remarkable show piece but no heart or warmth in it — We walked home I got to bed about midnight —

At 2 o'clk. I got message from Charl. — He had Rec'd. Cablegram from Mary announcing safe arrival at Hamburg — pleasant voyage — none of them sick.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 11, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up after an unrestful night with a very unpleasant feeling in my head \+ an uneasiness below the belt — Got to studio before 10 \+ for some time wrote a letter to Mary — I was able to do a little painting on Passing Showers — About noon M. Snyder, a young fellow from Chicago, came in \+ introduced himself. I know some rather clever dry-point work that he has done — I had a short walk before lunch — after lunch, feeling unequal to studio work I finished letter to Mary, then lay on my bed till about 4.30 — then to studio — cleaned up — another short walk — home at 5.30 — Read newspapers till dinner. Evef. wrote to Jim \+ Ralph.
