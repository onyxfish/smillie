## Left Page

### Sun. July 19, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very wet day — some heavy showers alternating with scotch mist. Bkfst. at 9. Ralph had such a stiff neck that he did not get up to bkfst. The soreness lessened so that he got up \+ dressed before dinner. I kindled fire in studio stove — much of the evt was spent in marking bed linen, towels, etc, to go to hotel laundry to-morrow. Dinner at 1.30 — Afternoon for some time I wrote up back accts. Petty cash. In spite of the weather Mrs. Hardenburgh \+ her grand daughter, M. Maghee \+ Adelaide \+ W. Billy Atkinson called for afternoon tea. If I may judge from what I hear the latter was not altogether a welcome guest. Tea before 7 — Part of every before a wood fire — Part, again writing up Petty Cash.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 20, 1903

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, some showers — I painted part of the day on a sketch 12½ × 18½. Munshine worked on casing piazza posts. I went to hotel for mail at about noon — Got telegram for Ralph from Mollie Adams asking him to join their camping party to-morrow — Upper Ausable — His neck is limbering out slowly — but, with weather such as we are having he will be better off in a comfortable house. Mr. Maghee met me about noon \+ we went to D. Dubois' room over the chapel. We waited till the old gentleman took his noon day observation \+ made his calculation — then we set our watches by correct time — to hotel \+ got mail — home to dinner — Afternoon Maghee came \+ ran wires from house to annex — fixed bell in the Butler's pantry \+ attached a dupee of Ralph's dry battery cells. I did some painting but was out of sorts \+ not in the humor for it — Every stupid \+ nappy.
