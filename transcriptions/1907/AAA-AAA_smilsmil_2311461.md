## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 8, 1907

**Weather:** Misty, mild, cloudless day \- Fine overhead. dreadful underfoot  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfst. at about 9 \-
Spent most of the mt arranging photos
in scrap books for the boys \- at noon went
out for a walk but did not go far \- the air
was not pleasant in spite of sunshine \- nor
was it invigorating \- Had my dinner alone
at 2 \- a long nap between 3.30 \+ 5 \-
A little to eat at 6.30 \- Evef. Charl
came up \+ spent the evef. Jim
ret'd from Yonkers before 10 p.m.

My Rheumatic arm much better.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 9, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not a pleasant day \- overcast \+ rather raw.
\- I concluded that it would be better for me to
stay at home \+ finish the arrangement of photos.
of last summers journeyings in the scrap books
for Jim \+ Ralph \- I got that work finished in
the mt \+ after lunch took the two scrap books
or albums down town to Cullen's \+ left them
there \- the prints to be pasted in as arranged
by me \- met Charl there \- I had intended
to stop at Wanamaker's on my way up town
to make purchases but felt so out of sorts that
I gave it up \+ went directly home \- I don't
know what particular kind of ailing is pending.
