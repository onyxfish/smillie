## Left Page

### Sat. July 13, 1907

Oxford, Mitre Hotel — Room 16

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal day — fog + rain most of the time — not a gleam of sunshine — Up a little earlier than usual + took 9.35 train that left from our basement — the run was uneventful — we had a fine compartment to ourselves + really enjoyed our excursion — We had a good table d'hote lunch after 1 o'clk — having the dining hall, 1st class, entirely to ourselves. At 5.30 we reached Birmingham + by cab drove over to G.t. Western Station + made close connection with 5.45 train + got to O— at 7.20 — Drove at once to the Mitre + had a late dinner — After that we wandered about till after 9 — Then in + to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 14, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day, but the warmest we have had since we landed. Ther. in my room got up to 70°. Bkfst. at 9 — after that we started out for a walk — went down past St. Aldates street, past Christ Church College down to Christ Church Meadow + so to the River, we tracked past all the College house boats or barges — then up to the Broad Walk + thro' Rose Lane to High St. + Magdalen College — we went over the bridge then back + thro' the quadrangles or gardens of the College, then along High St. in + out of college grounds wherever we could get access — We got thro' from Queens + All Souls to New College + into the Slike where we had a fine view of a large part of the old city wall — then into Holy Well St. + along to Parks Road + then down Catharine St. past The Bodleian Library, Brazenose out onto High St. + to the hotel — it was then after 1 + we waited for lunch at 1 — that was in every item a cold lunch — I cannot recommend the Mitre — After lunch I had a rest + then out again, up thro' the Broad St. + to Balliol — the gardens of which are extensive — there I met Stevens, a Rhodes Scholarship man, all a classmate of his — he was very civil — took us into his rooms + told us interesting things about manners + customs here among the colleges — At 4.30 we left — the boys one way, I another — I went to the Cathedral + got an excellent seat — Service from 5 to 6 — The boy singing + music were good but I did not get very enthusiastic — After service I spent some time going about the interior — an interesting + beautiful old — Then out + some more wandering + got to hotel before 7 — At 7 a meagre dinner was served — After dinner we went out again — streets thronged with idlers — we went up St. Giles to the Martys now — + took a look at The Randolphe Hotel where Annie + I stopped while here — back to hotel + I wrote to Emma J — + to bed
