## Left Page

### Tues. June 25, 1907

**Weather:** Dance. 342 m.  **Temp:** 69

Superb day - clear, almost cloudless, + warmer than yesterday - the Ther. figures that I give are the max. in our state room. Bath at 7 - bkfst. at 8 + much of the m'g spent sunning myself on the after-deck, away at the stern, with the third-classers - a very respectable lot + not so crowded as the "firsts" - That part of the deck is from my point of view preferable to any part set apart for the exclusives we past. Mrs. C.C. Cooper (Emma Lambent) a cousin of Miss Abbats, + together some of my care indirectly, introduced herself + then her husband Colin Cooper to me - It is many years since I last saw her + I would not have known her if she had not given her name - We had a pleasant chat of old times - I spent some time in the gym - lunch at usual time - more loafing fore + aft + a little more work in the gym - From 4 to 5 I had a sound nap - a good dinner at 7 with "Blue Danube" Waltz - "Il Trovatore" + Moonlight Serenade to carry me back thro many years of Reminiscence. Eve'g, the upper Promenade deck on Port side was dressed with flags + extra electric lights + the band started a dance at about 9 o'clk. That was heartily followed up by the young + some of the middle aged people - In a Reel that at times became strenuous, Jim + Ralph, side by side, took part with two sisters - I watched them with interest - With hilarity all about, they both were very serious, Ralph was as grave as a judge + thro' it all I did not see a smile on his face - I wish they might have some of the effervescent joyousness of their mother who enjoyed her sport with unconscious spontaneity. The dance continued till 11 o'clk - The moonlight over a smooth, rippling sea, was superb.

I saw whales sporting.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 26, 1907

**Weather:** . 363 m.  **Temp:** 71°

Monotonously fine weather - smooth sea + mild breezes - bath + breakfast at usual time - spent a large part of the m'g. sunning myself on the upper deck aft. 3° cabin - the people are very different-decent - I meant to write - preferable, as companions, to many of the obese specious of "the chosen" among the "firsts" whose jewel adornments make their uncomeliness more evident. I Took my Registering Ther. on deck + was surprised to find it cooler in our state room than out on deck - Temp. varies very little - standing most of the time 69° + 70° - one night it fell to 64°. Lunch - There are two Wilson families on board - father, mother + two daughters in each - I took the four daughters over the vessel from stem to stern - first Officer Riehle joined us - I had a nap in the afternoon - dinner at 7 - most of the evening I was in the Social Room Reading - a fine moonlight night - To bed at 11.
