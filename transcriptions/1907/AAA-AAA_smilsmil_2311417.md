## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 9, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 7, apparently none the worse for last nights experience — It was almost 9 before we had bkft. After that we took a cab + drove about the city for a couple of hours — went across the River by the new temple of Justice — Large + imposing past San Angelo to St. Peters — did not go in — then in to S. Giovanni in Laterano — went in there + spent some time going about — interested in the musical bronze doors of the baptistry — that give various resonant notes as they are swung back + forth — By the way, we went up on the Capitoline Hill + around by the Colosseum + in there on our way to S. Q— then on to S. M. Maggiore + in there then to the Via Nazionale + thro' various streets past various sights back to hotel soon after noon — lunch at 1 o'clk — After 2 we drove to Capitoline to learn that we were too late to go into the museum that closed at 3.30, we went into the Aracœla church, then a look at the Tarpean Rock — then to the Tabularium where we were allowed only 15 m — consequently could not go to top of tower — then we went down into the Forum Romanum + with great care, book in hand we went over more than half of it piece by piece. The afternoon was lovely — a deliciously cool air was moving + there was considerable shade — in the sunshine it was unpleasantly hot. I enjoyed the air, the light + the deliberate way in which we were taking it all in — It was about 5 when we left the grounds + took cab back to hotel — a rest before dinner at 7.30 — We + for a time in general room — a party of young Am. women at the piano \+ practicing new steps in dancing after their days work of sight seeing + shopping.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 10, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still fine weather — clouds appeared over the Albanian hills in the afternoon — Up before 7 + did some writing before bkft. after 8 — At about 9 we started for St. Peters + the m[?] was given to it faithfully — we went to the top of the dome — did not go into the ball — on our way down a young American girl coming up said to her companion — See that old Man coming down the steps — she had mistaken me for one of a party of Italians + spoke quite loud — as I squeezed past her I said — a-e-e-s — but I'm not dead yet — Whereat she threw a fit, as the boys say — then we did the church carefully, then by then to the sacristy, taking everybody — then into the Vatican \+ went wherever we were allowed to go there + paid a man at every door — out at 12.30 + drove to hotel — from the dome top the Pope's grounds seemed dried up — when I saw them in March years ago they were luxuriantly green — after lunch, a rest \+ at 2.30 we started out with our chariot — drove to St. Clements — couldn't get in — too early — then around the Colosseum to the baths of Caracalla — too early there + had to wait a few minutes before the custodian came — we wandered for half an hour among those Cyclopean Ruins — then on to the catacombs of St. Calixtus — we went thro' with two Philadelphia (?) ladies + an amiable monk — No material change since I was there 15 yrs ago. that I noted — we stopped at the tomb of Cecilia Metella + explored it — then on over a very dry dusty jolty ministering road — at a high point we had a panorama of the Campagna — its aqueducts, monuments + the mts. beyond — then, turning to our left we rode a long way over a very rough \+ dusty road — at last reaching the paved Via Tuscolana — we entered Rome by the Porta Asinaria — we went out by the Porta Sebastiano — we drove again to St. Clemente + were successful in getting in + were taken down into the old church under ground by a pleasant young Irish priest — this old cellar church is lighted by electricity — excavations + Restorations have been extended since I was here 15 yrs ago — Then we had candles only to aid us in our explorations — then we drove to hotel getting there at 6 — I had a good rest before dinner at 7.30 — a little loafing after that + to bed early.

Rec'd telegram from Charl — family at Pyrmont — I hope that Katharine is not sick again.
