## Left Page

### Fri. July 19, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m'g but by noon it cleared + the afternoon + we'f were delicious - Up at 7 - got the boys up after 8 - bkfst. at 9 - at 9.45 we went out in an open carriage + drove about the city for nearly two hours, stopping at the Hotel de Ville + going thro' all after permissable - then to the Cathedral + heard a portion of m'g mass - then drove through the Boulevards + to the Palais de Justice - a huge structure sitting alone making it very impressive - it stands upon a commanding height - From a balcony we had a fine view over the old town - then drove to Hotel - 11.30 - The boys went off to do shopping + I took my camera + went about taking snaps + shots - I went into the Musée Royal of Ancient Paintings + sculpture - interesting + extensive - the problem of perfect lighting is not solved here - altho' it is good in some of the galleries - To hotel for lunch at 1 - after 2 we went thro' the park - then by Tram out to the Palais de Cinquantenaire - I got a several snaps at what I hope will prove to be effective pictures - We went thro' the Architectural + Decorative galleries hurriedly - then back in the Park, there was music by an excellent band - Lohengrin overture was well given - I left the boys there + returned to hotel + had a short rest before dinner at 6 - Eve'g we went down to the Market place + Hotel de Ville very impressive in the twilight - thro' several shop streets, bought some photos - then to the Bourse + there entered an odd four-horse bus + was hauled up the hill to hotel - then again to park to get moonlight effects - went to the Theatre or garden at the W.E. Cor. of it - here I left the boys - went back to hotel - to bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 20, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Dom Hotel. Cologne. Room 53.

A beautiful day - bkfst. at 8.30 - then I went out with camera + got two more views + bought five 5×4 spools. At 9.30 we left the Hotel by bus + went to R.R. Station + there, under direction of a very competent Porter got a carriage to ourselves + had it all the way to Cologne + were threatened with intrusion only twice - we left at 10.10 + got to C. - at 3.30 - a good ex press - At 12.15 we went into the dining car + had a very good lunch for F. 3.50 each - At Vervey the Train stopped while our luggage was inspected by Customs officers - a mere formality - one trunk only was opened + our suit cases were not removed from our carriage, nor looked at. - The country thro' which we passed was, with the exception of one small section, was very flat + under high cultivation - Grain, looking very heavy, was ripe + being cut. The green of vegetation was brilliant + the golden yellow of the grain was in charming contrast. At Cologne, by bus we went directly to this Dom Hotel - my telegram was with others on the office desk but no assignment of rooms had been made - we were given two rooms on the entrance - mine is, as at Brussels, directly over the front door - They are reached by a small + dark stair way - They are mean looking in every way. My room is marked 4 M. - The boy's room, with two beds is 7 M. but that does not make them satisfactory - After getting our luggage into our rooms we went over to the Cathedral Service was in progress, so we could not go all around - we wandered aimlessly thro' the streets for a time, then I ret'd. to the hotel - Drew £30 - Thos. Cook & Son - I wrote a short letter to Mary - The boys came in at 6.30, or 7.30 by Cologne time + we had supper at about 8 - Then out again for awhile - The boys went in + I ret'd. to hotel - Read news papers - Then out alone strolled down U. Goldschmid Str. to quarter market, brightly lighted + thronged with promenaders - to hotel - to bed at to 30
