## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 4, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain all the m'f. Some sunshine in the afternoon — I woke up with a head ache — got up late \+ had bkfs'. at 10 — immediately after that I aut to D'. Tuttle's \+ together we went to Hoboken by Christopher S'. ferry \+ to Ham. Am. docks to learn there that our steamer Prinz August Wilhelm was at the foot of 25 S'. North River so, back to ferry \+ crossed to 23°. S'. — Met M'. Maghee on boat \+ Adelaide — the rain was furious \+ we were drenched when we got to the dock \+ on the vessel — we found her small but with promise of comfort — we saw her under every disadvantage — D'. T. left me on 23°. S'. — Car \+ I got home at about 1 — changed my wet clothes — rested for an hour \+ had lunch at 2 — the afternoon past in doing odds \+ ends of things that had to be done. I diner at 6.30 — Ralph \+ I alone — Jim dined out with some sort of a club — R. \+ I worked with our Bromide enlargement experiments \+ made some progress, but it was little — we were encouraged however to believe that none of the difficulties we met were insurmountable to look at it.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 5, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — like Spring — Bkfs'. before 9 o'clk — when preparing to go to studio Herbert Smith called about fine ins. on my possessions in appartment \+ detained me for some time. Then to butchers' \+ grocers' \+ on to studio — saw Ryan about the Repair of water leak under pavement — Then to Globe Werniche Co — saw manager \+ he promised to send up for the half dozen Extension library units that have been in my hall way two or three weeks — Then to Wanamakers \+ bought several articles for house — Then home at 1.30 \+ had lunch with R. — who had been up for two hours but had had no bkfs'. He is evidently not feeling well — The mumps seem to have subsided — After lunch I took a short walk with him out of doors — Then I had a Rest — After an hour \+ a half — 3. to 4.30 — with Miss Murphy, reading to her from Father's M.S. autobiography, she taking it down Stenografically — Dinner at 6.30 — R. \+ I. Jim out at class dinner — Eve'g R. \+ I continued our enlarging experiments using the negative taken at St. Hubert's last Sep'. We came near to being successful — After 9.30 R. said he felt feverish — I took his temp. \+ found it 102°/10° pulse 96 — He has not been well for some weeks \+ I am anxious about him.
