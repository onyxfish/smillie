## Left Page

### Wed. July 3, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day, cold \+ raining \- I awoke as usual at about 4, A.M. after a fair nights rest my head ached furiously \+ I took Acetanelid \+ Caffien I fell asleep again \+ woke about 8 o'clk \- feeling better I got up \+ started the boys \- It was after 10 when we finished our bkfp. Then to Ham-Am. office \+ had the matter of my missing camera in the hands of an official there \- Then by two penny tube to Holborn \- then thro' 11 to another one \+ went to the Bank \- By this time it was raining hard \- After a little trouble we found the Deutsche Bank \- London Agency 4 George Yard \- Lombard St. got £50. \+ left our address. Then out \- took Cab \- Rode thro' Victoria S. to the Embankment \+ so up to the Carleton Hotel \- Stay may [illegible] It was then after 12 \+ I decided that it we not be safe for me to go out again so I had fire made in my room \- Every body is complaining of the Arctic summer \- one of the papers this eve. says that London will lose £1000000 because of it. I had a Table d'hote lunch in the Hotel with the boys and then to my room where I finished my letter to Mary. As the weather seemed to have cleared at 6.30 I went out \+ met the boys in the street \- Together we went to the Criterion Restaurant \+ had a Table d'hote dinner there \- It was good \+ cost a trifle more than the hotel lunch \- that is, a little short of $5.00 for the three of us, the boys taking wine \- After that they went to some variety show \+ I ret. to hotel \+ spent the eve. by the fire in my room \- I have taken small doses of [illegible] \+ [illegible] 3 or 4 times \- the boys got an atomizer \- some Alboline \+ Listerine \+ troches for me \- I think I am holding my own but not much more \- Nutmeg, this mrng was the same as yesterday \- my throat is very troublesome.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 4, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some sunshine to-day \+ a very cool wind \- a little rain, of course \- Awoke at 4 o'clk. usual headache \- got to sleep again \+ did not wake till after 9 \- It was 10 when we got bkfp \- after that we went out \+ wandered down by the guards \+ saw a little function \- don't know what it was. at 11 \- Then thro' to Whitehall to Houses of Parliament went thro' Westminster Hall, then over to the Abbey \+ spent the remainder of mrng there \- Then back to hotel \+ had lunch at 1.30 \- Then the boys went to the Tower \+ did some shopping while I went to Royal Academy system \+ left at 4.30 completely tired out \- The exhn. was a surprise to me \- it seemed almost unqualifiedly bad \- Of course I understand that there are dialects in the language of Art \+ that the home dialect is very different from that of England, France, Germany, China or Japan \- nevertheless everything seemed poor to me in conception, technique \+ color \- Sargent has gone wild \+ Shannon was away below his mark \- the works by men I know seem to have lost their cleverness \- two landscapes by [illegible] Parsons \- 4 or 5 feet long \- hung on the line were as slick \+ niggled as anything that R.W. Hubbard ever did \- I found nothing that appealed to me as having distinction either in thought or deed \- The galleries were jammed \- got back to hotel \+ lay on my bed till 6.30. Then the boys came in \- we went again to the Criterion \+ had dinner \- it was 9 when we came out \- I went to my room \+ the boys went to the Metropole to call on the Wilsons \- I did some writing \+ then to bed.
And so ended the Fourth of July.
