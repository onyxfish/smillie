## Left Page

### Wed. April 24, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - high wind - To studio after 10 - Packed up the Hinshelwood pls. in box made by Millers & busied myself among my own pls. till after 1 o'clk - Home to lunch at 2. At 3 Westenkamp called me on phone & asked me to come to him at the Print Room, Lenox Library - I walked over there a pleasant tramp across the Park - The principal matter of discussion was concerning mounting boards - & something that shall meet all requirements, durability, color, texture of surface & cost - I shall have to come to W's aid in this matter - Then we discussed the plans for housing in the new library bldg. I consider them lamentably inadequate - The rooms are too small & lighted wholly by sky light - I must use Carreg - It was 5 o'clk when I left & walked home - a short rest before dinner - After dinner I went to George Cook's, 23 E. 46 S. - Taking my painting of "Coming Storm", as a present to Emily - Check of $50. To Geo - in payment for writing up my books of accts - Home at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 25, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

University Glee Club concert.

Warm, Hazy Spring day - B'kfst as usual & got to studio a little before noon having spent most of the m'g going over the new building of the N.Y. Pub. Library under guidance of Mr. Ward who is in charge under Carrier Hastings - I went first to the office of C. & H. To have a look at the plans & W. Billerod Brainerd sent me to the building direct - Following Ward I climbed thro' the open iron net-work of the book stack on up to the third story under the roof & there I gave special attention to the rooms that have been set apart for the Print Dept. They are fine rooms but a very serious defect is in the lighting which is by sky-light only - the sky lights are not over the centre of the room - I think that the need of a sky side light for study of prints is imperative - a small room, near the S.W. corner room, has a window with west light opening upon a court - If this room could be secured for the Dept. it would go far towards supplying the need of a side light - Home to lunch at 2 - after that I wrote a letter to Westenkamp - urging him to my secretary to get from Pr. Billings the order of the little room with the west window -
Dined at 6.30 - Every fine & I went to the University Glee club concert at the wedding Astoria - There was little of the glee music in the concert - There was an orchestra of about twenty pieces - the Orange musical society a woman's chorus of about 120 assisted - the glee club & the 60cy had separate numbers on the programme but were joined together with the orchestra on the final number "Hail Bright abodes" from Tannhauser - It was given with great spirit but I thought it rather noisy & ragged - The opening number - "and sighing" by Grieg - by Glee & Sanders orchestra I thought was very fine - The solo by Warrenwrath, a robust barytone was good - After 11 when we got home
