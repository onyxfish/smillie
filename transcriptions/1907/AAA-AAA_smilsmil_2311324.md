## Left Page

### Tues. March 5, 1907

Tichmore Hotel, Room No. 330 - Port Antonio
76 miles from Kingston

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A smart shower at 6.30 when I got up - then sunshine - There were threats thro' the day - heavy showers were seen over the mts. but on the whole it was a fine day - Bkfs. at 8 - got away for Warton at 9 - 10 miles - very interesting ride - first thro' a home like looking country, rolling hills - woods - green fields \+ stone walls in the valleys - then a climb over Mt. Diablo - some rather heavy grades with much twisting \+ turning along hill sides overlooking gullies until the crest was reached when there opened before us an immense stretch of low country, apparently forest covered, with mts. beyond - showers were passing over parts of the mts - sunshine was on the low country - the effect was very impressive - the descent was rapid - the sharp turns were many \+ our driver swung around them at a high rate - at one we narrowly escaped collision with team coming up - the pole came within a few inches of poking into my ribs. Warton is the end of a railway that runs to Spanish Town \+ there connects with road to Kingston - the ride was interesting for a short distance from Bogwalk, where we passed thro' the Rio Cobre Ravine - we got to Spanish Town at 12 \+ drove to Rio Cobre Hotel for lunch \+ found it in possession of a tourist party - it was useless to attempt to get lunch there - our driver took us to a boarding house \+ there we got coffee, eggs, bread \+ butter \+ fruit - drove back to station for 2.30 train to Port Antonio - late one hour - disabled engine - we got away at 3.30 - a long \+ interesting ride till we got to the hill country - passing thro' a region of chocolate hills, the gorges filled with bananas \+ cocoanut palms - we came to an upland where the stretches of banana groves was amazing - from this the descent was easy till we came suddenly out upon the sea at Anotto Bay. It was then sunset - we ran along the beach for more than an hour - a very light surf breaking at our feet - some times we passed a little island, some miles by tunnels thro' promontores - we went thro' 22 tunnels altogether, most of them up in the hill country. Reached Port Antonio at 7. Great confusion - drove to Tichmore - cleaned up a bit \+ dinner at 8. very good - Eve'g. loafed about piazzas - orchestra - this might be a hotel at Newport or any other seaside resort in the U.S. - up to date in all respects - electric lights - great public halls - wide piazzas - first class elevator \+ bedrooms of all grades from fine suites to singles about 10×15. - A small news paper pub'd. every day giving latest general news \+ locals.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 6, 1907

called on the Nitchies.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not a pleasant day - cloudy, with threats of rain - there was considerable sunshine - the most unpleasant characteristic of the day being mugginess - it is of the kind known here as a "Norther." Up at 6.30 \+ wrote till 8 when I had bkfs. Then I had my hair cut in first class style - then I wrote to Chas. giving expression to my work at the Ham.-Am.-laine who have changed steamers - the Aug. Wilhelm is not to call here on her way to Cartagena - wherefore the Sigismund, a new vessel of the same Hreidenich is to call \+ then go to Colon \+ not to Cartagena then to Port Simon \+ home - this cuts Cartagena out of our trip \+ we lose the stateroom of our selection - we do not know where they will put us - Then Dr. T. \+ I sauntered into town - an evil smelling dirty place - it was so muggy that we soon got to hotel \+ loafed till lunch at 1 - again we loafed till after 3 - then we drove out to "Folly Point" - to the Alfred Nitchell place - we had a very pleasant visit with Mr. \& Mrs. (Annie Tiffany) Nitchell - Mr. M. has been unwell for the past three weeks - up for the first time to-day - the estate is a very large one, covering the end of the promontory on the south side of Port Anthony - We passed the lodge keeper thro' iron gates flanked by low buildings \+ drove up to the house - a wide spreading rectangular flat roofed building reminding one of what old Pompeiian buildings might have been - we entered the basement \+ sent our cards \+ were promptly invited up - the immense square court, roofed \+ with 2nd story gallery all round was a delightful surprise - the best ruins about E. \& W. \+ this court was open on the N. \+ S. sides - with doric columns supporting the walls - it was tile paved \+ open to the roof - to the south we looked out to the bluest of sky - a few cocoanut palms intervening - to the north we looked across the bay to the Tichfield Promontory \+ the Blue Mt. Range rising high beyond - it was really ideal for a tropical home - after our visit we drove over the grounds \+ to the light house \+ the Temple of Æolus - then along the cliff \+ out at a far gate \+ then back to hotel - Dinner at 7 - \+ an eve'g. of loafing - the daily paper honored me by a notice which was almost a copy of Who's Who - biographical note - Dr. Skelton, the Resident hotel physician introduced himself - a Ridgefield man - knows George \+ Rosa - quizzed me by our family resemblance -. wrote part of a letter to sister Mary before going to bed - have found two ticks on me - lots of fleas but no mosquitoes.
