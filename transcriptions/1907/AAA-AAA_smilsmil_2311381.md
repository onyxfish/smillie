## Left Page

### Sat. June 29, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 60° 64°

A squally day — overcast mt. — wind rising — sea rising — p.m. clouds formed \+ broke — a few gleams of sunshine. — more spurts of Rain in flying showers — Eve't. very heavy wind \+ Rain in dashes — altogether not a pleasant day \+ yet not without features that were very stimulating — The sea was fine to look upon \+ some waves came aboard — but the ship was as steady as a N.Y. ferry boat — At night I went away forward — deck wet with flying spray \+ the air full of shrieks \+ howls, from cordage, the open smoke stack \+ the many funnel-like ventilators it was very impressive. I did not have my bath in the mt. — my throat is troubling me \+ I fear to take more cold by exposures. I spent some time writing to Mary \+ Charl — some time in gym. and at other times doing some vigorous walking — In the evening the Com. gave us an impromptu sort of entertainment in the Zimmer — Piano \+ violin playing, recitation \+ dancing — a 16 year old Jewess who has already taken my attention because of her vim \+ grace in dancing, danced a sort of Spanish dance with castanets to piano music — a graceful performance — as an entertainment it served to pass the time — Some good piano playing by a professional was marred by the howling of the wind \+ the flapping of canvas over the skylight.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 30, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 59°-61°

A fine mt. — clear, brisk wind \+ beautiful clouds. The afternoon grew to be more squally \+ wind rose — at night some dashes of Rain — I tried temp. out of doors — 55° in shade, 65° in sunshine — while it stood midway in stateroom — I wrote parts of letters to Mary \+ Charl \+ had a short exercise in gym. There was service in dining saloon at 10.30 but I did not attend. I did a considerable amt. of brisk walking during the afternoon — \+ had a nap. Captain's dinner at 7.30 — as usual, much display — decorations \+ doers — I dressed for it \+ in spite of precautions, suffered from cold in the draughty saloon — The dinner was protracted \+ lasted till after 8.30 — there seemed to be a general disposition to make speeches — A German consul made one of considerable length — The Cap'n responded \+ three or four followed — Towards the close it was bedlam — far from an orthodox sabbath — everybody was in noisy good humor \+ after dinner the racket was transferred to the promenades where grown men \+ women acted like foolish children. While this was going on there was a concert in 2d cabin for benefit of Sailor's widows \+ orphans.
