## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 24, 1907

**Weather:** Storm all day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Storm all day - did not go out of the house Up at 7.30 - had a scrub - then bkft. at 8.30. most of the day was given to trimming the photo prints (236) that I have had made for Albums for Jim \+ Ralph - souvenirs of our Eurapean travels - I was to have gone to the Tweedy's at Plainfield for lunch to-day but the weather was too outrageous. Dinner at 2 - afternoon \+ eve'g, some writing \+ some reading \+, with Jim's help, hung some pictures.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 25, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cheerless m'g - clearing gradually cold \+ windy - To studio soon after 10 - To work on a very hasty sketch made years ago, out of doors at Montrose, in a lot back of the fair ground - it has some suggestive qualities that I like. \+ I have tried to bring it together with as little additional work as possible - I hopes that I have bettered it - At 1 30 went to Lock's to arrange about putting up some curtains at home - then home to lunch - at 2 - At 3.30 went down town \+ saw Clarence B. Smith \+ consulted with him about proceeding against the garage nuisance that has been established in the stable adjoining my studio bldg. We will write to Tillinghast demanding an abatement as far as possible of the nuisances complained of - then I went to Herbert Smith's about Insurance - he was not in - then to Wm Cullen's - then walked down to Bowling green \+ by subway home. - Jim did not come home to depnner - detained at office by work - I dined \+ spent the eve'g alone -

I rec'd. from W. Louis A. Holman of Needham mass. a note \+ a copy of Appleton's Mag. in which is an article on Am. Etchers with ill. My old Cedars is reproduced - I got a very flattering notice - which, perhaps, leads me to think that the article is just \+ unusually correct \+ discriminating - He complains that it has been sadly cut.
