## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 13, 1907

**Weather:** overcast mt.  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast mt. growing milder altho' still cold - pleasanter in the afternoon + milder. I had bkfs'. at 9 - After 10 when I went down Town - went to Colombian Consulate + had my passport stamped, sealed with Colombian seal + signed by Colombian Consul, all for $7. Went to Colombian Bureau of Information 15 Whitehall St. + got a little information. Then to Consulate of Costa Rica + got considerable information + at last to Studio getting there at 1 o'clk - Prepared + ate lunch - then finished preparations for printing - ground ink + wet down paper + left for home at 4.15 When I got to 79th St. it looked so inviting at Riverside Park that I walked there for some time + home after 5 o'clk - a rest before dinner with Jim \+ a quiet evg. - wrote a note to Abraham Turk -

Rec'd. Reply from J. B. McQuire Director of The Corcoran Gallery of art to my letter offering The Cliffs of Normandy to the trustees at a nominal price to be fixed by the trustees - my note was 11th. The Reply was prompt on the 12th The board has over-stiffened its appropriation + has discontinued purchases for the time being - They might have had it for $10 - but — It is trying to outlive the fashion of ones time - To be placed a back No on a top shelf - It is a law of nature + if one persists in living beyond his time he must take the consequences - it is of no use to philosophize — the fact is depressing —

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 14, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A colder colored sun shone thro' a thin haze for some time this mf. - but later a raw fog settled down + made things out of doors about as disagreeable as possible - Bkfs. at 8 o'clk - to studio soon after 10 - To work at once upon getting first print from portrait of Father. I got a very good one but the result was disappointing - it is overbitten + I shall have some trouble in reducing it. After taking a couple of plain prints I worked with dry-pt. \+ graver on the plate - I had lunch at 1.30 \+ at 3.30 I went to Wanamaker's + bought a bath robe + a light cab - Then home at 5. Had a rest + at 6.30 dinner with Jim who went to drill later - I wrote to Herbert Smith + loafed over news papers + to bed early.
