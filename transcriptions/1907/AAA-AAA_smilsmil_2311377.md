## Left Page

### Fri. June 21, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day - bkfst at 8 - Had a long m[orning]
at home - wrote to Bessie a short note - \+ to
others - Jim came home to lunch at 1.30
having finished with the Western Electric
Co - After lunch I went to 2°. Nat[ional bank]. - then
to Meyrowitz, then home - Then I had a rest
Then some time writing checks \+ arranging
accts to be left — Dinner at 6.30 - Evening
getting everything sorted out \+ laid alongside trunk \+ suit case. To bed about 10.30

## Right Page

### Sat. June 22, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day - up at 6.30 \+ commenced packing
trunk at once in earnest - bkfst at 7.30 - after
that the work went on - I wrote a note to Miss
Charlotte Kent \+ settled with Deardon for rent
till Oct 1° - also with Kate Leonard \+ Mae
Bradley - By 11.30 we were all ready - then
we had a light lunch - the carriage was in
waiting - our luggage was carried down to it
\+ at 12 we left the house - drove down Bway
\+ 8° ave to 23d S- ferry \+ got to steamer just before
1 - a great crowd but got our luggage into our
stateroom without trouble - Charl arr[ived]. Soon after
\+ George at 1.30 - Apl Kinny \+ Lloyd Holsapple came
to see Jim off - Geo. \+ Charl left with the last of the
visitors \+ the vessel backed out of its pier about 2.15
I wrote notes to Emma J. - \+ to Charl \+ they
were sent back by pilot - Sea exceedingly
smooth - a light silvery haze, like a veil, over
all - we got many things out of our trunks \+ hung
them up - I wandered from stern to stern - Dinner
at about 7 - every seat taken - much confusion \+ air
very close - it was fortunate that I secured seats days
ago - Every beautiful, luminous moonlight - sea like
oil - with the boys on deck away forward at bow where
the effects were very fine - This ship is a wonderful
monster - to bed soon after 10 - from 1 o'clk, for two
or three hours the fog whistle was blown at short
intervals but it did not succeed in disturbing me to
any considerable extent.
