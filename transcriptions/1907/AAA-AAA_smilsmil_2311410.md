## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 26, 1907

Axenstein - Grand Hotel - Room 69.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm + almost cloudless day - Up early, had my bkft. + then set out on a prowl to get snap shots + information - went around by the River, after a call at Cook's office - To see some of the old parts of which I failed to get photos the last time I was here - many of them have been Renovated + Restored until they are no longer Pic-turesque + some have been completely torn down + the height upon which they were built has been dug away - I bought some Abuela photos + 6 Rolls of film + with the boys went to N. Ger. Lloyd SS. Co. for information - Then to hotel - again I went out + got some more snap shots - back + had lunch at 12.30 - Then I sat + wrote to Mary till it was time to start for the Train - by Hotel bus - Our train got away at 2.10 - The Ride was hot + dirty - the Ride by the lake was not interesting - soon we came to the mts - while going thro' a tunnel the carriage door opened while Jim was trying to close the sash + he was almost pitched out into the tunnel - a narrow escape from a frightful tragedy - the Lake of Zug was seen to great advantage - when we got to Arth-Goldau we waited for 20 m. - we sh? then changed to another train as we found when after starting the guard told us that the train we were on did not stop at Brunnen - we w? have to go on to Fluellen. We got there a little before 6 - went to a Restaurant + got something to eat - At 6.53 Ret. to Brunnen - Then walked to the station of the Brunnen-Morschack Elect. Bahn + went up by the last car - 7.55 + got to The Grand at 8.5 - The Proprietor told us that the house was full but he gave the boys 41 - a beautiful Room overlooking the Lake + sent me to 69 on the floor above, temporarily, as the Room was engaged for the morrow - About 9 o'clk that he + Katie should lunch with us to-morrow - a letter + card from Jim Shearman + a Telegram from Chase were waiting for us - Charl's Telegram was fm Englisher Hof - Hildesheim - 24 ins. I got to bed before 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 27, 1907

Changed to Room 17.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Will + Katie Etkin lunched with us.

Another lovely warm day - Bkft. at usual hour, then loafed about taking a number of snap shots and finishing a letter to Mary till after 11 - then Katie + Will arr? + the rest of the day was practically given up to them, loafing - part of the time on the different terraces, a part with W. Keeler, an old time Ridgefield man, I believe, whom K. + W. knew - we went out to the "Glacier field" + I saw the strange glacier + waterworn rocks that have been uncovered there for a considerable space - we sat for some time on the balcony of the boys' room, No. 41 - lunch at 12.30 - more loafing - coffee + beer under the Chestnuts + later K. + W. left for their quarters in the Waldstetter Hof. Brunnen - Then I had a half hour on my lounge - dressed - dinner - some time looking over the latest rec? news papers - + to bed.
