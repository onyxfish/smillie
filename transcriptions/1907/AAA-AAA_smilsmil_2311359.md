## Left Page

### Thur. May 16, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain thro' a good part of the day — It was very heavy in the early m'f. \+ again in the afternoon — I got to studio soon after 10 \+ put in a long morning on father's portrait — I had lunch in studio after 2. The later afternoon was spoiled for work between the darkness, which at times was very great \+ a visit from Vondrous. The rain fell so heavily that I was held a prisoner till near 5 o'clk — then I went home \+ had a short rest before dinner. While at table I rec'd. Geo. Cook's check for ten thousand dollars, my share from sale of Cleveland dock — Even'g I wrote several notes, enough to keep me busy till bed time.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 17, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast all day till late in the afternoon when there was hazy sunshine — To studio before 10 after stopping at Charl's for a moment. I spent the day at work on father's portrait — laid ground, worked over portions of back ground \+ bit the work in — I went to bank \+ deposited my Breland R. E. Co's check at about 1 o'clk — then back to studio \+ worked till about 4 — then up Town \+ sauntered along Riverside Park \+ Drive, impressed by the naval display — Two Jap. war vessels, three fine examples of our own battle ships \+ four torpedo boat destroyers, all lying in the Hudson off this part of the city — Dinner at usual time — Even'g wrote part of letter to Prof. Marquand —
