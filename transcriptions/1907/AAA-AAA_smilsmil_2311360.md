## Left Page

### Sat. May 18, 1907

**Weather:** mild with some hazy sunshine at times  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild with some hazy sunshine at times
I got to studio before 10 \+ worked on father's
portrait till 1 o'clk. Then home to lunch at
2 o'clk \- amused myself after that by
reading for an hour \- then a rest \- then
a saunter on Riverside \- Dinner at 6 30
Well I completed my draft of letter to
Prof Marquand.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 19, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very close, warm day with southerly
wind \+ veiled sunshine \- I got up at 7
\+ busied myself with scrubbing till after
8 \- Then b'kft. Then I made a fair copy of
my notes to Mr. Marquand concerning
his occupancy of the Ark for the coming
summer \+ mailed it to him. Then I went
out \- it was too warm to walk \- I sauntered
along Riverside \+ in the Park till near 2 o'clk
Then in \+ had dinner \- after that read
for some time "The Blazed Trail" by Stewart
Edward White \- It does not please me \- is labored
is not spontaneous \+ genuine \- It does not have
the brutal force \+ directness that takes hold
of me in Jack London's best work \- Then I had
a rest \+ after that more sauntering in the
Park \- great crowds to see the war vessels \-
four U.S. boats \- Two Italian \- Two Jap's (they
sailed to-day) \+ five U.S. gunboats. \- In \+
had tea at 7 \- Well commenced a letter
to Katharine P. T.
