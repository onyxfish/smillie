## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 25, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - after a fairly comfortable night I got up feeling that my condition was imp\[roving\]. The painful spot (distended gallbladder) has disappeared \+ the discomfort at p it fstomach has lessened greatly, consequently I am again hopeful - I spent practically all of the m\[ornin\]g. working over the problem of my bank acct. being still unable to bring to an agreement the figures in Pass book \+ my check stub - The difference is $150.05 - after a long hunt I found the 5 cents but the $150 still remained - At last, it seems that there is no entry on my check stub of K. \+ O. confons last Jan\[uar\]y - my last bal. was Jan\[uar\]y 4th '07 - unreasonable length of time - altho' for some reasons I still have doubts that this is a correct solution it seemed entirely reasonable \+ I gladly accepted it.
Then I went out \+ had a walk on Riverside, then home \+ had lunch - Afternoon \+ again in the ev\[enin\]g. I worked over some mounted photos \+ some negatives \+ prints sent up from Cullen's - In the afternoon I had an enjoyable walk in Central Park - Dinner at usual time. Ev\[enin\]g Jim went to his club -

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 26, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day. and I feel much better than yesterday - After 6½p\[.m.\] I wrote to Mr. Scott Brown about the putting to rights of things in the Ark. - After 11 I went out \+ walked up Riverside drive to 108th St. then back thro' the Park - a good 2½ miles - getting home before 1 \+ not feeling fatigued. a record gratifying to me - lunch - afternoon I gave some time to photographs. \+ had another walk in the Park at about sunset - Dinner 6.30 Ev\[enin\]g. adding to the embellishment of our rooms.
