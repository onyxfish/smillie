## Left Page

### Thur. May 2, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

weather still overcast \- wind east \+
chilly \- Bkfo. before 8 \- at Charls before
10 \+ left with him the long letter just
Rec'd from Bessie, mailed from Ragusa.
Then to studio \+ spent the m'f painting
again on Hazlebrook \- Home to lunch
at 2\. Afternoon wrote letter of instruction
to Earl Moody at Keane Centre \+ to M.
Wyckoff at Holyoke asking if he can
give me information concerning paper
manufacturers that will aid me in my
seeking for the proper mounting board
for use of Print Dep't of Public Library.
Then I went over to Central Park \+ had
a walk \- my observation of spring foliage
effects disgusted me with my efforts on
Hazlebrook \- I'm "way off" as the boys say.
Dinner at 6.30 \- Prof P. [illegible] called me
up on phone to know if I was disengaged.
He got here at about 8.30 \+ till near 11
we were looking over \+ discussing photographs taken while we were in Jamaica
\+ further south.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 3, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal m'f., overcast \+ raw \- late in
the afternoon it cleared cold \+ windy. I got
to studio before 10 o'clk \- no fire \+ Ther. at 60°.
I went to the cellar, cleared out furnace cut
kindlings \+ made a fire \- Of course the studio
was cold all the m'f., but I busied myself
making ready to mount some photos \+
other like work \- \+ then home at 2 o'clk
\+ had lunch \- Afternoon I tackled the
Roth etchings sent to me by him some
time ago \- I looked over them care-
fully \+ critically \+ then began a letter
to him \- or rather, it was begun two or
three days ago \+ I continued it \- Dinner
at 6.30 \+ after that I finished writing my
criticism to Roth \+ mailed it \- Then to
bed \-
