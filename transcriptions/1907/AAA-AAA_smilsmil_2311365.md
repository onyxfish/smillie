## Left Page

### Tues. May 28, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant out of doors, so reported, but I did not find it so indoors. At 5 A.m. my temp was 102³/5°. Pulse 110. \+ my frontal sinuses giving indications that they were preparing to enter the melee. This condition of things was very depressing to me. \+ I think it disturbed D. T. as well. I gave up all hope of being able to go to New-Haven next Sat. In the after noon wrote a few lines to Ralph telling him about it. I sat up long enough to give my bed \+ room a good airing
At 2 A.m. I had a severe wretching time but as nothing was in my stomach I raised a little mucus only. D. T. says this was probably the effect of the Calomel. At 5 30 I took Ct. Mag. \+ at 9.30 my bowels were cleared.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 29, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day — so reported — I kept in my bed all day till about 6 p.m. Then I got up \+ sat in my chair till I turned in again for the night. At 5.30 a.m. after a fairly comfortable night my temp. was 101³/5°. Pulse 100. My head ached badly but all other symptoms were better. D. Tuttle came at about 11. I slept a good deal during the day. At 9 p.m. temp. was a trifle above normal \+ Pulse 78. Young Jones shaved Jim's bed with him. I sat up for awhile in the eve\+.
