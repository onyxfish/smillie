## Left Page

### Thur. June 27, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 70°

fine weather continues but with some signs of change for a time during the afternoon the sky was clouded over — the sunset was fine but the night till 10 o'clk was dark — after that I was in bed — Ther. in Room stood steadily at about 70°. at 9.30 p.m. I took it out into what seemed to me a very cool air but it would not fall more than 4°. The functions of the day followed in regular order. There was much activity among the young people practicing for an entertainment tomorrow — games, shuffle board. sack races etc & a dance to follow. I spent sometime on my favorite place on after deck in conversation with a german barber on his way home on a visit & a young frenchman of Cherbourg — just as interesting as the average man on first promenade. Eve'f I read awhile & loafed about rather uneasily as the breeze was rather too cool for my comfort to sit out on deck.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 28, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 62°

Overcast & cooler — a little more wind & water ruffled — First Officer says we are making fewer miles because we have lost advantage of Gulf Stream — the day passed much as the days before — except that I was more or less uncomfortable all day because of cooler temps. Ther. in our Room stood at 62 in the afternoon — I went to 4 p.m. mf afternoon — wrote part of letter to Charl in the mf. Afternoon was largely taken up with shuffle bd. games in tournament, honors wonby
Later the potato races made much amusement
