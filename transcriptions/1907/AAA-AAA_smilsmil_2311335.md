## Left Page

### Wed. March 27, 1907

*Ralph came home for Easter vacation.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day - Up at 7 \+ had bkfs. with Jim at 8 - The m'f was elapsed in arranging my money matters \+ other affairs that had accumulated during my absence. About 1 o'clk - I went out \+ had a short walk on Riverside \+ in the Park. Lunch at 1.30 - Then I went down town to Cullen's \+ was detained some time there waiting for Wm. C. He insists that the condition of my camera, that gave me so much trouble while I was down south, was the result of some accident to it after it left his hands - I insisted that no fall or sudden jar could cause a screw to fall out if it had been put in place properly, \+ further, that it had had no fall or other rough treatment while in my hands. I then went to P.O. Customs \+ &c - \+ saw Mr. Warner \+ got Rose of Rolfe's etchings that had been held up some time ago - Then I went to studio at 4 o'clk. \+ saw Geo. T Maynard - Then home at 5 - found Ralph there - he having come down to spend his Easter Holidays - Ms. Jim \+ I had dinner at 6.30 - They went out for a stroll later in the eve'g. \+ I, feeling tired, went to bed at 9.30 -

## Right Page

### Thur. March 28, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - looking showery at one time after noon - Ther. got up to 64°. at 2.30. Bkfs. with Jim at 8 - m'f I attended to various matters till after 10 - then to studio - worked awhile on portrait of father \+ varnished a couple of paintings that Annie bought years ago in Europe - Home to lunch at 1.30 with Ralph - After 2 we went down town first to Ham. Am. Line to enquire about Steamers for Europe in June \+ return in Sep't. Every room, 1st class, taken on the S.S. we wished The Lincoln. To sail June    Then we went to White Star Line \+ then to German Lloyd but with no better success. Then we went to Charl's office 50 Wall, \+ found with him Mr. Julian White - After he left we discussed the Steamship situation \+ C. gave me a card to Mr. Lawson Sandford of North Atlantic Conference who, he thinks can find a way to help us. At about 5 o'clk. we all went up town by sub way - C.\+R. getting off at 42nd St - R. \+ I had dinner at 6.30 Jim had Company mess dinner at the armory - I got to bed early.
