## Left Page

### Wed. July 17, 1907

**Weather:** A chilly clouded m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

A chilly clouded m'f - but the afternoon was charming - sunny + much warmer - I was up early but the boys were not ready for b'fst - till nearly 9 - Then we went to Cathedral Despairing of getting fine weather I made a number of exposures with open diaphram \+ lengthened time - we went in to Cathedral \+ went thro', with a verger + a mob - certainly there were more than a hundred in the party we went thro' Choir, crypt, cloisters, chapter Ho. \+ all - We had lunch at the hotel about 10'clk a meagre affair - confusion because of a tourists party of some 25 or 30 - After lunch as the weather was clearing rapidly, I went again to the cathedral grounds + waiting for gleams of sunshine got again all the subjects, + more, of this morning. I went into the Cathedral about 3.30 + sat in the nave for about half an hour listening to the service - owing to the peculiar construction of the building, the choir was obstructed + hidden from view - This made the effect of the music more impressive to me \+ I enjoyed it greatly - out at 4 - + continued my wanderings till after 5 - then to my room + rested - the boys were off perambulating - At 7 o'clk - we had dinner. The courses were so meagre that I had to ask for more - after dinner the boys went out, but I did not - spent the even quietly indoors.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 18, 1907

Brussels. Hotel de L'Europe - Room 12.

A dull day throughout, a light sprinkling of Rain in the ev'f. Left Canterbury by 9.5t train - owing to the mistake of taking train from London at Charing instead of Victoria we were obliged to continue by same line to Dover, around about way - Scheduled to reach D- at 10.53 - boat time 11 - with the length of the pier to run as the train does not go onto the pier - at station before last I asked the guard if we could make the boat - looking at his watch, said he thought we might, then shouted to engineer: "Hurry up - party wants to catch the boat". At Dover we hustled - could not register trunks to Brussels for want of time - a big porter, with all our luggage on a truck started down the pier on a run + we followed - after about two minutes I thought my legs would give out, yet kept on - three going pluckily, had been handed in, the last was held for us - boat left 11.5 The crossing was smooth - boat small - motion enough to make some of the passengers sick - to Calais in 1 1/4 hours - then because our trunks had not been registered for Brussels they had to be examined + registered at Calais - again we hustled - had a good run + were there fast on the train - all of this cost a little more than $2. more - Don't make unnecessary mistakes - they cost time, trouble + money - our side of 134 miles was without interest - we got to B- at 5 - drove at once to hotel + there found the manager smilingly awaiting us - telegram &c - my rooms escorted on Mezzanine floor, overlooking the square - Table d'hote dinner served at 6 + 7 o'clk - we were hungry + declared for 6 o'clk - was lied up, rested for a little while - then dinner - after that went out with the boys to the Park - very near - Then I ret' to the hotel, the boys continuing their wanderings - I wrote a note to Mary + got to bed before 10.

To-day Chase planned to sail from N'f' to meet us at Heidelberg on the 28!
