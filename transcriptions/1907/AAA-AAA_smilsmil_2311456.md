## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 28, 1907

**Weather:** Thanksgiving - Ralph sat? to tt. H  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dubious weather again in the m'g. before noon it had cleared pleasantly. I had my bkf't. before 9 but the boys combined bkf't \+ lunch at 10.30 - After 11 we all went off together \+ walked up Riverside to about 120'S'. Then Ret'd. to 110'S'. where I left the boys to continue their prowl \+ I ret'd. home by subway. Lunch at 1.30 - Afternoon reading \+ retouching photos - Charl came at 5 o'clk - looked over photos that he had not seen yesterday \+ at 6'5 we all started for George's home - Subway to 181'S'. then aanedu car to University, getting to the house just before 7. Soon after we went to the table - George's family - mine - \+ Charl. Nellie gave us a very good dinner. We left a little before 10 - made good car connection - Charl \+ Ralph went on to 42'd. 5t. R. to take 11 o'clk Train to New-Haven. Jim \+ I stopped off \+ home about 10.30

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 29, 1907

**Weather:** went to Plainfield.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright m'g. but a sulky afternoon. When I got up in the m'g \+ found promise of a fine day I determined to make the deferred visit to the Tweedie's in Plainfield. I telephoned to them \+ was answered that my call w'd. suit their convenience. I had my bkf't. - went to barber's \+ had hair cut - \+ to Station foot of W. 23'd. S' - 11.20 boat to Jersey City to Plainfield by N. J. Central at 12.'3 Mary was waiting for me at Station. We walked to the Tweedie home, 125 Crescent Ave \+ there met Mr. Tiffany - Belle \+ Florence. We chatted for half an hour or more, then had lunch - then more chat till about 3 o'clk. Then with Belle \+ Mary I went to the church \+ saw the Louis Tiffany window in memoriam of W. Mr. Tweedie - Jos. \+ Ollie. Then together we went to station \+ I took 4 o'clk. Train to Jersey City - slow train - getting there before 5 - In the grey twilight had a wonderful view - the N. Y. skyscrapers all lighted up! From that point it was something quite indescribable - the mystery of the city light - the indefinable olds. \+ the wide spangled of lighted windows combined to make such a sight as probably could nowhere else be found in the world. Home at about 6 \+ had dinner alone - Jim remaining at office for overwork - he came in later - quiet evening.
