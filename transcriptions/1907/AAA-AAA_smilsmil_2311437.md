## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 21, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine cold day but I was not able to go out \+ enjoy it — I passed a fairly comfortable night — Kate insisted upon my taking my bkfs. in bed — I did not get up till after 10 o'clk. — I do not feel that there is any improvement in my condition — I was able to keep about all day — finished the assorting \+ marking of my negatives — I was able to eat lightly at lunch \+ dinner. I wrote a long letter to Ralph — After dinner I called D. T. on 'phone — he said apply a mustard plaster — Kate made one \+ I wore it all the ev'f. got to bed at 10 with a very hot side — for a time it seemed to increase the discomfort extending it widely but before m't. it quieted \+ I got some rest.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 22, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine m't. — clouded middle of the day — fine again late afternoon — Up at about 8 \+ had bkfs. at table — then called up D. T. \+ asked him to come \+ see me. He came soon after 11 — I stripped \+ he gave me a very careful examination as I lay upon my bed — He says it is clearly a case of distension of the gall bladder \+ tells me to keep up courage \+ hope for the best — it may subside — He was with me for more than an hour — Then I went out \+ loitered for half an hour on Riverside Drive — walking is painful. Then in to lunch — At about 3 o'clk. I went down town to Guilieu's taking my 5×\+ negatives with me \+ left them with him with instructions to print — Home refarest — Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'f. Jim read aloud for awhile — I got to bed early.

I got, to day, a very pleasant note from Mr. Marquand concerning the Art \+ their summer there.
