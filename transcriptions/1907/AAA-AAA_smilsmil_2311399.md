## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 4, 1907

Munich Grand Continental Hotel

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An almost cloudless day \+ very warm - in fact, hot \- Up at 6.30 - got the boys up \+ had bkfst. before 8 - At 8.30 our luggage was taken across to the station \+ after some tribulation we got away on the 9 o'clk. Exp. in a through car. The tribulation was caused by the throng at the weighing counter where porters \+ tragers jostled to get their luggage in ahead of others - \+ later by the delay of our trager in getting to the platform with our hand gepeck \+ securing seats for his - until he came we could not be seure which part of the long train we go thro' Munich - however, we got good seats in a compartment with one other passenger - a frenchman, M. J.C.N. Forestier of Paris - a commissioner of Parks \+ Roads, as I understood him, on his way to Vienna, to study Parks \+ recreations for the People - he being much interested in such matters \+ having ideas of his own, some of which were set forth in a little pamphlet of which he let me have a copy - He spoke a little English \+ was very amusing - He said he did not think I was an American because I did not speak thro' my nose - We set over the R.R. by which we came as far as Karlsruhe - then turned S.E. - after passing Stuttgart - at 1 o'clk we went into the Speisewagen \+ had a table d'hote dinner - it was crowded \+ very close \+ hot - 86°. As we passed Ulm we had a good view of the Cathedral - We reached Munich at 5.20 - 15 m. later went at once by 'bus to hotel \+ were given good rooms on the ground floor - mine has bath room attached - We were all very dirty after our long ride - we washed up - boys had a bath - then we went into the hotel restaurant \+ had a supper that cost 24 m - $6°. This is a bang-up hotel - then we went out \+ strolled on Maxymillian Pl. - Till about 9 then, as the boys had not had enough they went on \+ I ret'd. to hotel \+ to bed before 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 5, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, warm day with thundershowers at night - Had bkfst. together before 9 \+ at about 10 started out in a cab with general instructions to drive us around \+ show us the streets \+ fine bldgs. of Munich till we told him to return to the hotel - he completed the circuit very intelligently I think, as we followed his course on the map in about 2 hours. Starting out he took us up by the Glas Palast - the Basilica, Königs-Platz - the old \+ new Pinakotheks - then down thro' the Odeon Platz, past interesting bldgs. - thro' Hof gartin, past Königs Residenz, then into Prinz-Regenten Str. - past Nat'l. Mus. across the bridge to the Friedens Denkmal out to Regentur Theatre, back thro' Maxymillians Anlagen down to the Maxymilianum, across the bridge down the length of Maxymilian Passa, a noble Passa, to the old part of the city, past the Rathaus - then thro' Sendlinger Thor platz to the Theresien Wiese Renn-Bahn, to the Bavaria \+ Ruhmeshalle - there we went up inside of "Bavaria" \+ on getting down told driver to return thro' Sonnen Strasse to hotel - we got there after 12 - cost $2" - Dinner at 1 - it was nearly 3 o'clk when we drove to the alt. Pinakothek \+ spent an hour \+ a half there looking at the old pictures - the Pourtrais ptgs \+ the antique - I did not find much refreshment there - do not think the collection is rich in any of the old Masters - Reubens perhaps is shown at best, but I dont admire him any - Ret'd. to hotel at 5.30 - The boys went off \+ rested - at 9 we went to the Hof Bräu Haus. A new sensation - I have seen nothing like it before - on the street level we entered a low, cellar-like place where crowds of men \+ women were sitting on benches at bare tables \+ drinking beer - the vaults were low. Reaching a crowded opened upon a paved court with fountain - beer casks on end answering for tables while the crowd, mostly men, stood, drank, ate the bread, cheese, sausage - they brought with them, \+ smoked - the air was close \+ reeking - We went up stairs into a great hall, lighted by great chandeliers - the semi-circular Roof \+ two walls profusely decorated in low tones by Wagner - there were long tables, bare, where dinners \+ endless beer were served - The hall was crowded \+ a better class than below - whole families were there, eating \+ drinking - apparently every body in the best of humor - it was rather an inspiring sight - The boys enjoyed it entering into the humor of it thoroughly - it is said that 25 fifty-two gal. casks are emptied there every night - we remained till near 10 then started to walk to hotel - thunder storm coming up - began to rain - got a cab then to hotel.
