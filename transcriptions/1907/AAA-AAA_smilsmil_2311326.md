## Left Page

### Sat. March 9, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** Ther. 68° 83°

A fine day but very hot during the middle of it — I was aroused very early by the movement of the luggage of passengers leaving for the ships sailing at 8 o'clk — my luggage was taken with that of the others at 6 o'clk. I got up at 7 \+ had bkfs. at 8 after making several snaps shots at the mts. that were entirely clear — After 9, with the D⁴. I went to town \+ to McTtam — Am. wharf — after waiting for the Prinz Siöjes mord to get away we saw the head man here of the H.A. Co. \+ he said he did not know where the Aug. Wm. was but he expected her to come in some time in the afternoon \+ probably she w⁴. sail about 10 a.m. to-morrow. This was discouraging to us — so we went thro' the wrecked portion of the town \+ took a number of snap shots — I got away from the D⁴. \+ Ret⁴. to Hotel before he did — He came \+ we lunched after 1 — The afternoon was tiresome because it was too hot to do anything — at 5 we went to town again — The Aug. Wm was in \+ we went aboard of her with the Purser — \+ found No. 5 reserved for us — It was after dark when we Ret⁴. to hotel — Sat. is Market day \+ the crowds of negroes was immense — The cars were jammed full \+ we had an experience we got to bottle after 7 \+ had dinner at once after that I feel heavy \+ tired \+ I got to bed early — It was very hot at midday on the white dusty streets of Kingston, but at night it was pleasantly cool. My Registering Ther. got down to 68° at night in my bed room — practically out of doors — \+ up to 83° at noon same place.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 10, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Onboard Prinz August Wilhelm.
Room 5.
Capt. Bode.

A fine day — Up at 6.30 — out soon after 7 — wandered over golf links 'till 8 o'clk \+ then had bkfs. with D⁴. T. We paid our bills at the Rate of $5.oo a day. The same as chg⁴. at Richfield which is vastly superior. The two cannot be compared in any way — We took 9.15 car into town \+ got to steamer at about 10. Went on board at once, took possession of our Room, got trunks on board \+ did some unpacking — lunch at 1. \+ at 2 o'clk. we left the island of Jamaica — The wind Rose \+ before night we had considerable swell. The usual loafing about — only 20 passengers, most of them for the round trip back to N.Y. only four Tables, two on each side, set for dinner which was much better than what we got on the island, always excepting the Richfield — We hear unpleasant stories of small pox at Cartagena \+ possible quarantine, if we stop there, at Port Limon — I went to bed at 9 — our Room is on lee side — port hole open but Ther. did not fall below 80°. Went to 84° during the day.
