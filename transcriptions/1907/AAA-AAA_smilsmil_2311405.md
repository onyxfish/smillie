## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 16, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Room 133 —

Heavy rain all the mt. — ceased before noon \+ late in the afternoon the clouds opened \+ a few beams of sunshine got thro' — as the clouds lifted it could be seen that the mts. down to abt. 5000 700 feet of our level were white with new fallen snow — Up at 7 \+ had bkfst — as the prospect of our getting away very soon were poor — our Rooms expensive \+ cash in hand limited. I went to the office \+ asked to be changed to cheaper Rooms. Nos. 6\+7 are 40 Kr. per day — we were given 130 \+ 133 on the floor below at 16 Kr. per day — the Rooms are comfortable but furniture is scant \+ everything, except the beds the 2° or 3° class — I wrote a long letter to Emma S. but did not finish it — then went out at about noon \+ had a prowl every where — damp \+ cold — lunch at 1 — menu. Pork \+ cabbage in all forms \+ cold meat — I could not abide that so, went to Restaurant \+ had an omelet that was like leather \+ as salt as brine — I think the food service here is very poor — afternoon I had quite a long prowl with the boys out to the three springs — back to hotel. \+ slept for an hour — dinner at 7.30 — Every S sat in large hall, admiring my fellow creatures, reading a W.W. Jacobs story \+ listening to the music — After 10 went to my Room \+ prepared my trunk for a start to-morrow early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 17, 1907

**Weather:** Stelvio Pass — 1' of snow — Ther. 39°  **Temp:** 9050'

S'. Maria Münster Fahl Schwitzerhof — Room 4.

Clouded day with a few streaks of sunshine \+ light, misty showers for a little while in the afternoon. Every — very pleasant — Up at 6.30 — boys up an hour later — bkfst — \+ at 8.45 we left the hotel — for a time it looked as tho' we were to have a fine day — the sun shone out \+ our way up thro' the evergreen timber was charming — Beyond that — it was bare \+ wild, with Road winding back \+ forth, ever rising — we made but one stop on our way up — Franzenshöhe — we got to the top at about 12.30 — a cavalcade of us went up \+ met a cavalcade of others coming up from the other side — tourists detained by the two days of bad weather — I think there must have been as many as 40 or 60 vehicles of all kinds there at one time \+ not less than a hundred people — great confusion in the Restaurant — a long time before we could get seats \+ longer before we could get served — the meal was eatable \+ sustaining — Before going into the Restaurant we climbed to the top of the Dreisprachenspitze — 275' above the pass — snow \+ mud made it very disagreeable — the view was bleak to the last degree — of course we saw nothing of the Ortler, but saw a party that had just come in — I met D. \+ Mrs. Williams up there — I had to take some photos — but under the conditions fear that I was not successful — the view over to the other side was not so fine as on the maj— side — it was bare, often covered with small drifts of snow — we all to the house \+ waited long for dinner — at last, after more than two hours of waiting we got started — It was very cold — 39° with wind \+ little flurries of snow and rain — I was very glad that I had on my heavy ulster \+ muffler \+ overshoes — for some distance we followed the Requeen \+ they went down — then turned \+ descended thro' the trees \+ in every way cheerless Val Munauza — after Reaching timber the character of it changed for the better — especially when the sunshine out — The last part of it was fine — before we emerged from the woods \+ drove down into S. Maria in the Münster Thal — at about 5 o'clk — we got to the Schwitzger hof — a new \+ good sized bldg on the out-skirts of the town — our Rooms were large, very nicely furnished \+ very pleasant — the dining Room was unusually large — the plumbing was modern — but there was neither gas or electricity — we took a prowl thro' the village \+ into the country beyond — Ret. \+ had a good supper at 7 — there being no light or warm Room, \+ I being tired I went to bed at 9.
