## Left Page

### Mon. April 8, 1907

**Weather:** Rain last night — wet m'f, slowly clearing in the afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night — wet m'f, slowly clearing in the afternoon — Ptbfo. alone at 8 — Jim so much under the weather from effect of his cold that he did not get up till after noon — I too felt unable to go out till the afternoon — I absent the m'f making photo prints \+ in writing a fair copy of inventory of contents of Ark for Prof. Marquand. Lunch at 1.30 after noon I went out for a walk — strolled over to Mt. Mus. of Nat. History \+ spent a couple of hours or more there — then home — Jim up \+ dressed — we had dinner together — Evening dragged itself slowly away \+ both of us got to bed early.
Rec'd. from Bessie the two Rosaries for presentation to Kate Leonard \+ Lucy Kane — Kate seemed to be greatly pleased when I gave them to her.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 9, 1907

**Weather:** Snow fell steadily  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow fell steadily, I don't know how many inches, all day long — wet, soggy, a perfect slop. Ptbfo at 8 — Jim is completely bunged up by his cold but insists that he must go down to his business where there is work awaiting him that needs his attention. I gave a good part of the day to completing the Ark inventory for Prof. Marquand — I had lunch at 1.30 \+ a long nap in the afternoon to make up for loss of sleep in the early m'f after 4 o'clk. — when I had a headache so severe that I hardly hoped to be able to get up during the day. Jim came in after 6 somewhat wet \+ bedrabbled — I hope that he has got no harm from going out — Dinner at 6.30. The usual ev'f exercises — that is, some desultory reading \+ a little writing of one kind or another.
