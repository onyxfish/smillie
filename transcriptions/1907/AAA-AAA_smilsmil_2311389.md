### London. Carleton Hotel - Room 169. Mon. July 15, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A really delightful day - sunny + warm - The official figures to noon were 68°: max - 57°: min. Up at 6.30, making a mistake of an hour I rang for hot water + brought the night porter - he thought I was sick - one shilling for my mistake - We had blf: at 8.30 - Paid bill - £4. The most expensive hotel, comforts + conveniences considered that we have yet been in - To station + took 10 o'clk Train for London - got there at 11.15 - To Carleton at once - + were given our rooms on first floor - Then to Ham - Am. office + to my great joy got my camera - went out + took some snap shots - Then I told the boys to go off - get their lunch + see as much as they could - I went to a little bakery + had a pot of tea + graham Rolls - Then back to hotel + had a nap - Then out with my camera + loafed in the St James Park - To hotel at about 6 - unpacked trunk - got letters from Mary + Charl - wrote to Mary - went to Cook's + left orders for 3 first class tickets for Canterbury, Dover, Calais - Brussels, Cologne, The Rhine + Heidelberg - The boys came in at 6.30 - We went to Criterion + had dinner - Then to the Palace Theatre of Varieties. It was a long programme of 19 turns, several of them of little interest. Corvin, Ventroquist, with impressive face + mouth slightly opened, without a facial quiver, made a wooden manakin sing songs + carry on a heated discussion with him - Walter Kelly was amusing as a Virginia Judge - Yvette Guilbert gave us half a dozen [illegible] Two in English - A Bargain + The Key of my Heart - She was intense in articulation + facial expression - but the culmination of all was the skirt + color dancing of Mlle Dio - It was marvellous - She was something ethereal - hours + stage were dark - stars of light scintillated - lightning played + thunder rolled as she came up from a burning pit - for a couple of minutes it was hell - It seemed as tho' there could be no illusion - her skirts were flames - They leaped 20 ft high + played about her as she danced a wild dance - Then she seemed to disintegrate + parts of her were carried up in flame to fall back again - Then the flame died down + went out - She

### Canterbury. The County Hotel - Room 56. Tues. July 16, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another blessed warm + sunny day - Had blf: about 9 - we started out on top of a bus. I hoped to get some street scenes with my camera but soon found that that was hopeless except when the bus was standing still + then it did not stop where I wanted to take a shot - we got off at Westminster bridge + I advised the boys to go off by themselves + report at the hotel not later than 2 o'clk - + I w°. prowl about afoot with my camera - they went to 30. Kensington + other museums in that region while I went to St James Park + got some shots there - To hotel soon after 12 - I wrote again to Mary + did some Repacking - At 1 o'clk I went to my little bakeshop of yesterday + had a light lunch - Then to hotel + resumed packing - The boys came in soon after 2 o'clk - + by 3 we were ready to start. The porter had told me that a good train left Charing Cross Station at about 4 + we w°. get to Canterbury at 6.15 - We got to station at 3.50 + found that the first train w°. start at 4.32 was slow + w°. get to C - at 7.15 - The Great Eastern + Chatham service is we were made acquainted with it is very poor - The firsts are no better than some of the thirds on the Midland + Gt. Northern - + some of the seconds + thirds that we saw were miserable, untidy looking cars - We got to C - at 7.15 - at once to hotel + washed up - Then we had a very common place dinner - Then out + wandered up to the Cathedral + walked thro' the grounds in the twilight - back to hotel after 9 + to bed at 10 -
