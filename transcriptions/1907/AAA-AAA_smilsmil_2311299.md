## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 14, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another miserable day, rain + raw fog. Bkfp. at about 9 + as I had considerable work to do in preparing my check stubs for Geo. Cook to write up my books for the past five years I decided to remain at home + do that work — Lunch after 1 o'clk. It was 4.30 when I commenced to dress to go to Louis Tiffany's reception — It was after 5 when I got there. Mr. dusk. Miss Tiffany + Miss de Forest rec'd in the large parlor or Reception room while the crowds flocked to the studio above where Louis rec'd. The effect was, as usual, weird + fires blazed in the central fire places — innumerable oriental lamps failed to light the gloom overhead + a pipe organ in the gallery was made to reel off rag. time music — most ludicrously inharmonious with surroundings so sombre + suggestive of any thing but Rag-time — At 6 o'clk I went to Presb'n Hospital + saw Jessie Smillie for a few minutes. Then home — Jim was late + we had dinner at 7 o'clk — Evef I continued work on check stubs.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 15, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Shocking weather till afternoon — then it blew cold + the pavements dried. Bkfp. at 9 — after 10 when I went to studio — had my lunch there — worked on etching of Father till 4 p.m. — then rode to 72d St — walked to Riverside Park + thro' the Park to 90th St. The clouds had not yet broken away but the wind was strong + cold. Got home at 5 + rested till dinner at 6.30 Evef came news of destructive earthquake at Kingston, Jamaica — Dr. Tuttle called me up on 'phone, + so did Charl — they think that overruling powers must be opposed to Jamaica trips. Evef I worked on accts.
