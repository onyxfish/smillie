## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 4, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day \- bkft. before 8 \- after that went to Dr. Tuttle's as he said he wished to make a careful examination to know whether my gall bladder enlargement had subsided properly \- he said he found the conditions satisfactory \- then I went to Meyrowitz, Optician in 42d St. \+ had him adjust the glasses, made last June, to my nose. Than to the Frank maker, Schuff \- then to Borsemann's Dep. Ave to see if he w'd. take two old bicycles that are in my studio cellar \- but he has gone out of the bicycle business \- then to Douglas's \+ was there for some time \- then to studio, there till near 2 o'clk \- then home \+ had lunch \- afternoon with my photos, \+ writing a letter to Bessie \- a short walk before dinner \- Eve'g. finished \+ mailed letter.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 5, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Election Day.

Mild \+ pleasant. Bkft. after 8 \- read newspaper till after 10 \- then Jim \+ I went to the poles and voted \- as I disapprove of Parsons' fusion of Rep. with Hearst's Municipal League I voted a split ticket for Judiciary candidates, some Rep. some Dem. \- as recommended by the Bar Ass'n \+ Citizens' Union \- then we took a walk for about an hour \- lunch at 1 \- after 2 we went up to brother George's \- University Ave \- away up town \- house faces south \+ overlooks the athletic field of the N.Y. University \- It so happened that a foot ball game was on bet. N.Y.U. \+ Rutgers \- began at 3 \- we saw the first half from the windows \- Nellie was the only one at home. At about 3.30 Nellie walked with us to the Hall of Fame \+ then down to R.R. Station, Putnam Div, we took 4.17 train \+ got home at 5. After dinner I went to 331 E. 17th St. \+ spent the evef. with Emma \+ Mary Ireland
