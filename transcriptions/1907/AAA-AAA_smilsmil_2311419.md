## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 13, 1907

Bertolini's Palace Hotel — Room 69 — Naples —

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day — several sharp thunder storms in the early afternoon. Up before 7. Bkf't at about 8 — after that I went to Allaura's \+ bought a doz. photos. At a little before 10 we left the Hotel after seeing every fellow who popped up — we got to station \+ had trouble in getting a satisfactory compartment — we had to take one from which we could not get to the Restaurant car — as the first stop after leaving Rome was after 1 o'clk — we had to take seats in the dining car — we got away on time at 9.50 — amused ourselves as best we could till 12 when lunch was served — There was a crowd \+ the meal was served in slash dash fashion — soon after I we stopped at a station — we left the dining car \+ went to the comp't. where our hand luggage was \+ sat there with two Italians till we reached Naples on time at 3.10 — the ride was hot \+ dirty, when it was not raining furiously as it did three times — the passage over the Campagna was dreary, every thing being dry, burned up \+ covered with dust — the engine pulled slowly on the up grade till we reached the mts giving ampleetime to gaze up on receding Rome \+ the astonishing lines of aqueducts — the mts are not imposing after Switzerland — agriculturally the country seemed to be miserably poor — the showers seemed to be confined to the mts — after passing them everything was again dry as we approached Naples. Vesuvius loomed up dimly \+ the Sorrento shore could hardly be seen — There was some delay in getting away from the Station \+ it was about 4 when we got to the Hotel — a remarkable place — perched on a height reminding me, in a way, of Axenstein — we washed up \+ at 5. Ralph \+ I went to Cooks \+ bought round trip tickets for Pompeii, La Cave, Salerno, Parstum, Amalfi, Ravillo, Sorrento \+ Capri — about $28 for the three — I bought some films, then back to Hotel \+ had a rest before dinner at 7 on the open terrace — the wide bay — the young moon — the lights in the city below \+ the cries \+ composite noises combined to make it very interesting, not without furnishing a common place dinner Ev'ef. — I wrote in the writing Room.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 14, 1907

Cava. Hotel de Londres — Room 14.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day. Thunderclouds piled high in the afternoon \+ there was considerable thunder but at night it was cloudless, serene moonlight, cool \+ very pleasant — Up at 6 \+ Revised my packing taking many things from suit case \+ putting them in trunk \+ putting in suit case only such articles as I most need — Bkf't. before 8 — left Hotel at 8.30 in two single cabs — R. ahead with two cases — Jim \+ I behind with one — a long drive to the station. Train left on time — 9.55 — some confusion — more passengers than seats — got to Pompeii about 10.30 — went at once to entrance \+ paid 2.50 each, entrance. I engaged guide at 2 f. an hour for about 2 hours we tramped the blazing hot streets following him — I think we saw nearly everything — at about 12.40 we went to the Hotel Suisse \+ had something to quench thirst — about 1 we had a 5 f. lunch \+ at 2.30 train to Cave, getting there in about an hour — drove at once to Hotel \+ were given pleasant Rooms — I had a nap \+ then a general wash and change of underclothes — I interviewed the manager — the Vozzo who had this hotel when we were here Ap't. 24. 1893 — is now at the Cappuccini, Amalfi. The Manager got the Register of that date \+ I saw my autograph \+ my endorsement of the Hotel — Dinner at 7.30 — a noisy party of French-Italians — Ev'ef. I went out on the terrace from our Room \+ was charmed by the moonlight over the mts — the cool air — the night song of insects — It made me homesick as I thought of the view, under similar conditions, from a point south of the Ark — looking to Noonmark \+ the Ausable Pass —
