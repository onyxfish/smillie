## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 8, 1907

Ralph ret. to New-Haven.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark, dirty, raw \+ wet day. The real wet part did not commence till afternoon \- Ralph \- before 9 \- R \- got up soon after \- says he feels pretty well but the Ther. shows that he has a temp. of 99 4/10° \- the coughs a great deal \- It was 11 o'clk. before I got to studio then had a lengthy interview with Ryan concerning a new lease \+ giving him more cellar room. I fussed about in studio till 1.30 \- then home \- lunch at 2 \- then wrote a letter to Belle Tweedy \- Geo. Cook \- a short letter to sister Mary \- Dinner at 6.30 \- after that R. \+ I went to G. Cent. St.; \+ I saw him started of 8.02 train for N.H. Then I walked over to Hotel Astor. Bdway \+ 44th St \- Annual Meeting Zoological Soc. after formal business Mr. Rich. Tjader gave an interesting acct. of his slaughter of wild animals in East Africa \- he did not make it clear that it was altogether for scientific purposes \- to me it seemed sad \- almost wicked to kill such magnificent specimens as he showed in snap shot views of elephant \+ giraffe \- He, (Tjader) is a blond norseman \- 6' 2" tall \+ very fluent in broken english \- he spoke for near an hour, till 10 \- the room was a handsome one on the 8th floor, front, \- an audience of 150 or 200 perhaps \- met Chas. Collins \+ Mr. Walton \- Pres \- Hornaday introduced himself to us \+ we had a pleasant chat before the meeting \- home at 10.40

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 9, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, windy \+ cold. It was near 11 before I got to studio \- F. Tuttle came at about 12 \+ I showed him the Cliffs of Normandy \+ other works in oil, Water-Color \+ Etching, all of which interested him \- it was after 1 oclk when he left \- I told him that Schmelck \+ the Ham. Ave. lines had told me this mtg over the phone that we could have Room 5 on the Aug. Wilhelm \- from Jamaica south and back \- I prepared my lunch in studio \- later I had a call from Nellie \- I did not accomplish much in the way of clearing up confusion at the studio \- at 4 o'clk. went to Man Storage to enquire about Chippendale settee \- was delivered to dock last July \- went to d \- settee there \- then home at 5 \+ had a rest before dinner at 6.30 with Jim \- he was used up by his dance last night \+ went to bed after 8 \- I wrote a note to Jack Downs \+ to Katie Selkin.
