## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 5, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day. B\+Bs. away after 8 — then set out on foot — went to Duomo, Giotto Tower \+ Baptistry. Latter blt. much interior scaffolding. — Then to the Bargello — went thro' all the galleries — then to the Badia — Church opposite — then to Palazzo Vecchio. The loggia dei Lanzi \+ the Uffizi — There we went thro' all the galleries, picking out the single \+ double std Fan'd pictures. After 12. being pretty well tired we rode to hotel — had a short rest \+ lunch at 1. — After that I wrote a letter to K. P. T. — Then we rode over to the Pitti palace \+ went thro' those galleries — we were too late to get entrance to the Palace apartments — Then we went into the Baboli gardens \+ wandered there for a time, then out. Took cab \+ drove up the Viale dei Colli (over Viale Machiavelli — V. Galileo — to San Miniato al Monte — then to the Piazzale Michelangelo — Took a look over the city, down the Arno \+ across to Fiesole — Then down the Viale Michelangelo, across the River — to Santa Croce — it was closed — after 6 — then back to Hotel at 6.30 — we were all tired \+ rested till 7.30 — then dinner — Then the boys went off somewhere — \+ I went to bed early — a serenade from 10 to 11 under my window. Three good voices — two men \+ a woman — as I was not sleepy — only tired — it was not bad.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 6, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very bright pleasant — uncomfortably hot in the sunshine — the blue overhead without a cloud \+ wonderful. Jim did not get up till late in the afternoon — an indigestion \+ trouble with his bowels — Raekeh \+ I had B\+Bs. after 8 \+ having done what we could for Jim left him — took cab to S. Croce \+ went thro' that establishment very deliberately looking at frescos \+ tombs — The Cloisters, Pazzi chapel, Chapter ho, Refectory etc — the large chapel has undergone external improvements to the detriment of its picturesque ness in some details — then cab to San Marco \+ that too was done without haste — I did not get up as much sentimental interest over the Savonarola relics, his cell \+ other apartments as I did when, after careful coaching by Annie, I went thro' them with her 15 yrs ago — Then we walked to a barbers who had on his awning "American Shampooing" — This led us to hope he could speak a little English — but not a word — so, we had our hair cut in Italian \+ rest? to hotel by cab. found Jim sleeping \+ not yet ready to get up — We had lunch — About 3 — R \+ I went to Borgo S. Apostoli N\° 5, on a hunt for Ernest Roth — for the past two years I have sent letters to that address, care Serafino Marridi — \+ he has got them — It is a very mean place — a narrow street with tall buildings, a short distance from the Arno \+ parallel to it. We went from street to garret, up the dirty stone stairs \+ had peeks into some squalid apartments, but got no trace of Roth or Marridi — then we wandered about in the neighborhood — went into the \+ San Michele \+ did the Cimabue \+ its canopy altarvm thoroughly; inside \+ out; an attendant taking us into it, delerue — then to San Lorenzo \+ did the Capella dei Principi \+ the sacristy — then to Alinari's \+ bought some photos — then to hotel by cab feeling unaccountably tired — lay down on my bed \+ slept soundly till after 7 — Then Jim had felt well enough to get up \+ we went down to dinner — Evening with newspapers — another serenade before I went to bed.
