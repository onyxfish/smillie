## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 12, 1907

Landeck - Post Hotel - Room 16 -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — up at 7 after a good rest. air filled with light mist — clearing, a white blanket was over the plain + clouds covered the great Wetterstein top — I tried to photo. it — we had our bkf. + at 9.30 started on our way over the Fern pass. The ascent commenced at once, + we soon had fine views back over the plain + to the mts. — The ascent was gradual — boys walked a good part of the way — I did a fair share — The top of pass is about 5 m. from Lermoos + is 3970' above sea level — we passed several small lakes, deep sea green, far down below us — also Fernstein Castle — The descent was very rapid + winding — in a small lake near the foot, on a peculiar mound, the ruins of Sigmundsburg Castle — Then on to Nassereit at about 12.30 where, in a garden with many pedestrian tourists we had our mid-day meal — it might be picturesque, possibly nourishing, but it was not enticing or homelike — we waited here for a little more than an hour, then onto Imst — from N — on the valley widened + the views up + down were fine but a light wind was with us, the road was dusty, it was very, very hot + at times we were almost smothered — Imst lies a mile or two from the R.R. station — we got to station a few minutes after 3 — Train came along at 3.30 — bummel-zug — no first class — so we rode 2. to Landeck, getting there at about 4.10. Rode a long way to Hotel Post where we were cordially greeted by Herr Müller — He had been already advised from Schenkers of Landau contract — he had his porters look at my trunks + they reported that they were not too heavy + I was promised a good Landau + driver for to-morrow. Found awaiting us a letter from Mary + a telegram from Charl from Hotel Wildbad, Rothenburg, Bavaria — M's letter is dated Aug 8 + says that they may leave R. on Monday, 12d + go on to Eisnach, no address given — C's telegram asks why I do not telegraph to Mary — he forgets that he promised to telegraph his address to me as soon as he had one — It will be hard work for me to find him now — Rested in the eve + to bed soon after 9.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 13, 1907

Hochfinstermuug Hotel — Rooms 35–36 —
3730 ft above sea — 2 B m. — from Landeck —

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — very warm in the middle of the day. Up soon after 6 — did some writing before bkf. at 7.30 or 8 — wrote to Eidlitz of Schenker's Bureau telling of our treatment by Alpin Hof proprietors — Telegraphed to Charl. at Rothenburg, hoping to catch him there — Then there was a little rearrangement of contents of trunks + stowing them on Landau — a very fine turnout — our Host Müller very anxious to have every thing arranged to our satisfaction — no objection from any one regarding our luggage — We got away in good style at 9.30 — our driver, Albert Stief of Meran, on the box — The ascent began at once, the road cut in the side of the precipitous rock — the Inn rushing below us — progress was slow at tho' our team was a good one — after a few miles of this kind of road + scenery we reached a valley + at 11.15 reached Ried — 9½ miles from Landeck. There we stopped for an hour — we loafed about, sitting under big lindens. Till 11.45 — Then we had an ample lunch + at 12.20 got away again — our ride continued thro' a fertile valley with high mts — not particularly interesting — sun scorching hot + dust with us + at times — after about 10 m. the road crossed the Inn — the valley narrowed — the road was hewn in rock — there were tunnels + avalanche galleries — tored mts — we reached Hochfinstermuuth, 23 m. from Landeck — at 4.15 — we were at once shown to our rooms — mine has a balcony that overlooks the wild valley + stream — got a camera shot at it at once — Then with the boys descended the steep zig-zag path to the river — along way — the boys objecting to my undertaking it + predictably dire results — I resisted — the light was gone from the old mill + bridge but I tried a shot at it — we made the ascent slowly + I got back to hotel without detriment — Then I rested till dinner at 6.30 — after that walked back on the road for nearly a mile, then three minutes to a terrace that commanded a fine view — then back to hotel — I looked over some comic German papers till 9 then to bed —
