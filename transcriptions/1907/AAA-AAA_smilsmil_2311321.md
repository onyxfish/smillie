## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 27, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 79

Constant Spring Hotel \- Room 48.
Kingston \- Jamaica.

A superb day followed by moonlight to match. \- Up at 6.30 \- just rounding the east end of Jamaica \- the Blue Mts rose beautifully in the morning light. \- Bkfs. at 8 \- at 8.30 we were approaching Port Royal at the end of the hook like formation, the Palisades, that enclose Kingston Harbor on the south \- We had clear view of the wrecks of Princessen Victorialuise \+ Prinz Waldemar lying near her \- We rounded the Point \+ entered the harbor at about 9 \- got up to the H. A. dock at 10 \+ went ashore at 10.30 \- Passed the customs promptly \+ were then Taken in charge by a runner for Constant Spring Hotel \- We took charge of our baggage \+ got us a hack \- we drove to the Nova Scotia Bank \+ got Am. money chge. into English \- The devastation of Kingston seems to be complete \- we got to the hotel before 12 \- On comparing my watch with hotel clock I was surprised to find that we were exactly alike \- N.Y. time \- went to my Room \+ took some of my wardrobe out of my trunk \+ had lunch at 1 \- I had my hair cut in my bedroom before that \- the barber shop is in the wrecked portion of the hotel \- After lunch finished letter to Chas \+ wrote B.T. went to P.O. Mail for us. to-morrow \- we wandered thro the wrecked streets \+ took a number of snap shots the sunlight was fitful as heavy clouds covered the mts \- \+ for a few minutes there was a dash of rain the wind was gusty \+ clouds of mortar dust swept thro' the streets \- If the brick \+ mortar had been better there would have been less loss of life \+ property I am sure \- we went thro' Jubilee Park \+ saw how the darkies were living there under tents \+ in the most miserable of huts \- At 5 o'clk we ret'd to the hotel \- I had a rest \- at 7 o'clk we had dinner \- nothing to brag of \- Before the earthquake there were 250 guests \- now there are about 50 \- one wing only is at all safe \- my Room is a sort of gallery or verandah \- closed on one side by slat blinds \- no windows or glass \- thro' the blinds I pass to a little loggia from which I have enjoyed an enchanting moonlight \- looking out over level fields with Palms \+ other semi Tropical trees to the mts. beyond \- B. T. had a little walk about the grounds, 9 P.M. \+ heard many strange sounds.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 28, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 82°

Ther. lowest last night 69°\. highest to-day

A charming day \- awake before 6 after an unrestful night \- the bed was hard \+ uncomfortable. I got up at 7 \- changed clothes etc \- had bkfs. with B. T. at 8 \- After that took some views from the top of the Central tower of hotel \- so much wrecked that it cannot be used \- It was near 10 before the P. \+ I went down to town \- we rode around to the Race Track where a great number of blacks are camped under all sorts of shacks \+ tents \- The conditions are very insanitary \- I got a number of pictures \- B. T. left me to visit a friend of his \- After I had completed my work I got back to hotel at about 12.45 \- At 1.30 we had lunch \- At about 3. I went down town again to get shots at the Parish church \- It was 4.30 when I got back, pretty well tired \- we have planned to leave here to-morrow \+ go to Montego Bay at the west end of the island \- then by points get to Port Antonio by sea \- Leave there Friday \- get back here \+ sail on the Aug. Wilhelm Sat. noon \- We cannot take our trunks on the island, paint \+ I am puzzled to know in what shape I can carry the articles I shall need \- probably see them up in my carry-all \- Dinner at 7 \- not a satisfactory function \- The night another superb exhibition of moonlight glories \- my loggia is a delight \- little air \+ shining \- The fountains plash \+ trees \+ crickets chirp as they do in northern zones \- I heard many bird notes very early this m'g but one bird has been quite constant throughout the day \- very much like our cat bird \- a sweet, charming succession of note \- hardly enough to be called a song \- are the darkies say it is a nightengale \- but it does not sing at night.
