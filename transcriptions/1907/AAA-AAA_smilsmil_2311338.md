## Left Page

### Tues. April 2, 1907

went to Casino with Ralph

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day & growing milder — Up at 7 o'clk p. before 8 — Still feeling much under the weather from indigestion — did not go out of the house till eve'g. Rec'd a very pleasant note from Mr. Meyer of the Ham-Am. line stating that he had reserved State Room 50 on the Lincoln, June 22°, for me to which I replied thanking him — I wrote to Prof Marquand telling him that I was paraphrasing an inventory of the Ark contents for him — sent N.A. of P. cards to Finnan Ireland \+ Jesse Smillie. — Lunch with R. — about 1 o'cl — I kept very quiet during the afternoon & my discomforts gradually decreased, so that after dinner Ralph & I went to the Casino Theatre \+ saw Doris Maun in the White Hen — I am led to wonder, almost always, when I go to a theatre or palace of amusement how little is needed to please an audience — there was 3 hours of noise, rant & hurry burry without a single witticism or anything really droll — Maun, as a German is in a state of constant excitement & misunderstanding, the slender story under all being unreasonable to absurdity, but not the delightful absurdity of Gilbert — it was purely absurdity — There was not much good singing & one woman gave a graceful gowned dance — not high kicks — cake walk or cau-cau — It was near midnight when I got home & to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 3, 1907

went with Jim & Ralph to New Amsterdam

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — Bkfst. before 8 — my indigestion seems to be passing — I spent the m'f. at home. Mr. Meyer called me up on the 'phone to say that he had sent to N. German Lloyd on my behalf & had got the refusal of Room 303 on the Kronprinzesse, sailing from Naples Sep. 20° & asking if that w'd. be satisfactory to me — I answered that that w'd. be an entirely satisfactory arrangement thanking him. After lunch R. & I went down town — first to John D's. to a man if & jeweller, then we inspected some of the foundation work in progress for some great buildings on Dey & Cortlandt St. — then to W. C. Cullen's with my camera & the photos, bought when South, to be mounted. I got developed photos, prints of southern trip — then home. Dinner out, Jim, Ralph & I went to New Amsterdam Theatre & saw "Moulan" this new play, "The Grand Mogul." It was amusing in a mild way, I cannot say more for it. I hark back to my Gilbert & Sullivan models, or even the Offenbachs, & find a great gulf between them & such plays as are offered now. There is little or no wit — music of common place order — a great crowd of half or quarter dressed chorus girls who run or trip thro' what may be called squad or company dances.
