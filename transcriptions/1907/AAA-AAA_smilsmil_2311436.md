## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 19, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day \- up at usual time \+ help before 8 \- but the dis comfort in right side was so considerable that I did not go out in the am. I wrote to Prof Marquand \+ spent some time over the arrangement of last summers negatives, selecting those from which I wish to have prints \- the No. of prints I wish \- separating them in groups \+ writing No's upon them \- At 1 I ate a very light lunch \+ then went to Riverside Drive where I loitered for nearly three hours \+ saw a long procession of Infantry, cavalry \+ artillery pass — 1½ hours — \+ then almost as many Veterans, Civic Societies \+ German shooting clubs, on their way to the unveiling of the equestrian statue of Genl. Franz Sigel at 106th St. Then home \+ had a rest \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Jim tired after his parade \- Eve \+ J. continued the work with my negatives.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 20, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded \+ chilly \- rain in the late afternoon \- clearing at night \- I got up at 8 o'clk \- feeling miserable \+ unhappy \- The dis comfort in my right side seems to locate itself more definitely \- I called Dr. Tuttle on phone, learned that he would be in his office \+ soon after 9 I went down there. I stripped \+ he probed all about the sore place \+ having learned its exact locality he said that apparently there was trouble with the gall bladder \- advised calomel this afternoon \+ Phosphate of Soda \+ Salycilate of soda in a glass of hot water to-morrow before rising — Then home \+ spent a doleful day. I could not walk about because of the discomfort. I had a light meal at dinner time but no supper. Eve \+ Jim went to Mary Cooks to tea \- I was invited \- I was able to do some work assorting my photo\- negatives \- I got to bed with a hot water bag after 10 o'clk.
