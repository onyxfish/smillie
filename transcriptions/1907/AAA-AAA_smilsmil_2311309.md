## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 3, 1907

**Weather:** clear, cold \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- Bkfst. at 9.30 - The improvement in my condition still goes on \+ in spite of wind \+ cold I went out for a walk at about 1 o'clk, but I took the precaution to keep a half- to my nose as a sort of shield or wind break to protect my over-sensitive membranes. Dinner at 2 - Afternoon I mounted some of the photo. prints made last night - Charles came in later \+ left after 6 - Tea at 6.30. Evef I wrote a letter to St. Alfred Jerome Brown [illegible — ~30 words]

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 4, 1907

**Weather:** Dull raw m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Light snow falling from noon on - I got up \+ had bkfst. soon after 8 - headache \+ trouble with right eye - bloodshot \+ aching - I did not feel able to go out but it was necessary for me to go to the bank so I went down there at about 11 \+ got matters into proper shape - then to D. B. Bueters on 4th Ave - then to Baycorderfer's at 4.10 - then I gave up the idea of going to studio \+ took subway train at 28th St. for home - light lunch at 1.30 \+ then as head \+ eye were painful I was on my lounge for some time - Jim did not come home to dinner \+ I took little to eat. Evef balanced my back acct.
