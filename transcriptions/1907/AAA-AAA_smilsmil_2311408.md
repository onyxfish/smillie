## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 22, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed

A cloudless day - The boys went up Piz-languard, 10700'. I waked them at 5.45 + they started before 7. I ret. to my bed feeling much out of sorts, very cold - nausea - after 8 I went down + had [illegible]. I found a stove with fire + sat by it + read till after 10 - then feeling very miserable I went to my room + went to bed after getting my rubber bag filled with hot water + put at my feet - with this amt. of comfort I got warmer + dozed - The boys ret. soon after 12 + reported having a good time - a hundred or more people on the top - the entire panorama of mts clearly visible except the Ortler which was a small, pleasy cap - About 5 p.m. my telp. was 102. + while I was sure that my trouble was indigestion I thought the conditions warranted calling a doctor. This was done + Dr. Paul Gnedig responded - He prescribed calomel + no solid food before to-morrow evg. The manager of the hotel made a pleasant call when he learned that I was sick + ordered a commode for my convenience when answering calls during the night. It was a weary, disquieting experience - I had felt so unusually well yesterday, doing a lot of work, without fatigue.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 23, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded day, but not unpleasant - I kept my bed till 5 p.m. - a dull headache kept me uncomfortable - fever had disappeared before noon - The boys amused themselves tramping about, going over to St. Moritz in the afternoon - I had my meals served in my room - Tea + an egg in the m[illegible] - oat meal gruel at noon + tea, an egg + toast at 7 - The temperature here is a great discomfort to me - it is fine for out of door exercise, but in-doors, when it goes down to 46°. at night + does not get above 60°. during the day I long for some warmth or a place, a stove or heater of any kind where I can warm my hands or feet. On Wed. it was unusually cold - the ther. in my room, facing south + sunshine pouring in, did not get above 46° at noon - The air is delightful to breathe + I do not wonder that some claim for it curative effects - Pontresina is 5910' above sea level - about the height of the top of Mt. Washington. Fire in stove in boys room in the evg.
