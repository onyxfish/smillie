## Left Page

### Tues. July 9, 1907

**Weather:** Durham - County Hotel - Room 39.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A few beams of scant sunshine - more shadow \+ some rain - Up at 7.30 after a somewhat troubled night - wrote before bkft. which I had with the boys at about 9 - They went out immediately after. I saw the cortege of Court dignitaries \+ others start from Guild hall for the Minster - I followed \+ met the boys in front of the Cathedral but we decided not to attend the function inside being told that no part of it was especially interesting - we waited about outside \+assing around the building - then in it \+ went thro' all that was down in the books (guide books) - after getting out \+ buying some photos - we went to the Nat'l Provincial Bank of England \+ got £30. Then we ret'd. to hotel \+ had lunch - I don't like The Provincial Hotels of England in the matter of feeding - I am already out of sorts in a dyspeptic way since leaving London - its much so cold meat, pickles \+ gooseberry tarts - At the Hotel I met Dr. Stimson \+ his daughter, also on their way north - After lunch we went out again to the Ruins of St. Mary's Abbey, where, one sat, afternoon 45 years ago father \+ I heard a band play \+ watched the crowds of holiday people - I did not brave them toРеменias as we were driven back to hotel by Rain At 3.15 we left the hotel - drove to station \+ got the 3.30 train for Durham, getting there at 5 - There was a little sunshine on the way \+ much more rain - for most of the way we had an entire first class carriage to ourselves - a very pleasant way of travelling - we had a long ride to hotel - got into our Rooms - then went out \+ found our way to the Cathedral \+ visited only 6 o'clk was quite dark owing to heavy clouds - there were very few inside \+ the effect in that vast interior was very solemn - Then we wandered thro' the cloisters \+ out to the gardens - The Rain drove us back to the Hotel \+ at 7 we had dinner - Dr. Stimson at same hotel. I found a fire ready to kindle in grate in my Room - so I sat here to it \+ had a very comfortable evening - The boys were out again till dusk -

## Right Page

### Wed. July 10, 1907

**Weather:** Edinburgh - Bedford Hotel - Princes St. Room 17.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little sunshine in the m'g - then clouds \+ showers - in the late afternoon, after we got up into Scotland the weather cleared delightfully \+ for a little while we had a clear sky \+ sunshine - Up before 8 \+ had bkft. at 9. Soon after that we went up to the Cathedral but could not go thro' it because of m'g service - we sat \+ was a part of the service \+ were much interested in the acoustic effects of the intoning \+ choir responses - The organ effects were fine - Before going into the Cathedral we went thro' the Castle - very interesting - we met Dr. Stimson \+ he introduced us to his daughter \+ Miss Elliott, accompanying them - Together we went thro' the library with a very agreeable \+ interesting ranger - The Bishop had a supplemental service after the Regular m'g service \+ again we were prevented from going around the Cathedral - so we went to a photographer \+ I bought some photos. Then to Hotel \+ had lunch at 1 o'clk - Then arranged to take 4.30 train to Edinburgh. This enabled us to return to Cathedral \+ these we went there \+ went carefully over all parts that we had not already seen, under direction of a competent verger. Then to hotel with time to spare - Settled bill - satisfactory - The beds after were fairly good - left at 4.10 for Station - Train 4.30 - got prompt to our places - had to change at Newcastle - got another - only in corridor car - also dining car of which we were the only occupants - had a good table \+ hot dinner at 6.30 while running along side the sea - We got in to E-- at 8 o'clk \+ told Cabbie to drive us to the Windsor - He startled us by saying that the W-- had gone out of business more than two years ago - then told him to go to Royal - There we were told that they did not have three beds unoccupied - There is a great crush in E-- at this time because of a great Agricultural Fair that is held here every seventh year - also because The Prince \+ Princess of Wales are here - Then we were taken to The Bedford Hotel on Princes St. opposite Allan Ramsay's mon'm.- here we got the last three beds in the house. Three men slept in the drawing Room. We were tired but as it was still light we walked to the foot of Calton Hill \+ then back to Hotel \+ to bed at 10.
