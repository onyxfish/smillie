## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 18, 1907

**Weather:** Dismal day \- dark m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal day \- dark m't., Rain commenced \& fell lightly \& intermittently after that \- I got to studio soon after 10 \- Maynard in a state of mind because the painter had not come to give his floor a second coat of varnish \- I telephoned to Douglas about it. Emma J. came at 11 \& till 12.30 we looked over Annie's old music sorting it to be distributed in various directions. Then I got out my old painting of three cows that I am going to work over \& give away \- I scraped \& sand papered the sky \- Home late \& had lunch late, just before 3 o'clk \- After that I accomplished but little, the afternoon being so dark. Some reading \& some resting \- Jim came in a little late \- Then dinner \- Eve'g \- Some picture hanging \- Had a note from Belle Tweedy, invitation to Jessie Smillie \& me to lunch with the T's sometime this week \- phoned to the Presb'n. Hospital \- Jessie away for a fortnight. Mary Cook phoned to me that Sue Cook is coming to visit her for a week \& asking me to care.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 19, 1907

**Weather:** Bright cool day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright cool day \- Bkf't. at 8 \- went to Brooks Bro's. closed on acct. of a death \- walked to studio \- there at about 10.30 \- set palette \- got out old painting with three cows \- painted in a new sky \- Emma J. came in \& finished the work of yesterday \- assorting Annie's old music. Home to lunch at 2 \- Then worked till near 4 on negatives \- Then I went to Presb'n. Hospital \& saw Dr. Fisher \- He told me that Jessie Smillie broke down six weeks or more ago, under a severe attack that was said to be indigestion \- distended stomach \& impeded heart \- when able she went to Washington \- He thought she would be back in a week or two to take her place again \- I walked across the Park getting home at 5.30 \- Dinner at 6.30 \- At 8.30 Jim \& I went to Carnegie Hall \- Celebration of the 25 anniversary of Charity Organization Socy. Addresses by Pres. Rabi. de Forest, Mayor McClellan \& Gov. Hughes \- Our seats were away up in the Press Circle \- we could hear what Hughes, McClellan \& de Forest said but could not catch what Schiff, Cutting \& others said \- we left before the speaking was finished \- The addresses of Hughes \& McClellan were forcible \& instructing \-
