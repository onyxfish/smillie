## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 8, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine cool day \- up before 7 \- after a night that was not altogether restful \- Probably because of too much pictures I suffered a "brain storm" or something of that kind last night, lying awake \+ feeling uncomfortable in a nervous way \- I decided then to refrain from pictures for some time at least \- had my bkft. at 8 \- then writing \+ reading newspapers about to the boys were ready \+ we started out, going first to the Frauen Kirche \- then by tram to the Maximilianaeum which was closed \- got a shot from Terrace in front \- then to the Hof Brauerei, one of the largest breweries in the world, got a pass from the office \+ a guide took us thro a portion of it where we saw racking beer in vats \+ down into freezing cold cellars where we saw the beer going into barrels. After this we walked down Max. Mr. St. to the Hof Brauer Hs. \+ thence to New Rathaus where we took automobile cab direct to hotel \- dinner at 1 \- \+ immediately after we ret. to Rathaus \+ went thro new one \- I was much impressed by the large mural painting by Piloty \- the History of Munich in the Town Council Hall, as admirable hall \- then to the Old Rathaus \+ saw the old hall, not much to see \- then back to hotel \- the boys went on, I rested for awhile \- then to Schenkers, agents for Cook \+ Son \+ made preliminary enquiries for tickets on to Naples \- then to a shop \+ bought a number of photos. Munich is a fine city but it is not pictures of me \- so, I found few city views that pleased me. About [illegible] I went with the boys to the Lower Brau Keller \+ had dinner \+ spent the eve'f there \- a most remarkable [illegible] Recalling in some degree, memories of Hallensee Terace, Berlin \- a Garden, extensive, theabley shaded by trees \- a Terrace \- without cover, a higher one under cov \+ a third, uncovered, at the top of the building \- there are large halls within the building \- accommodation for 8000 \- There was an immense throng \- after 8 it was almost impossible to find a seat. All sort of people \- families came bringing their provisions \- bread, sausage cheese \+ the invariable big white radish \- we had a decent dinner, no tablecloth \- soup, fish, roast beef \+ beer for 4m. each \- an excellent band, 60 or 60 members, gave a concert, divided into military \+ orchestral music \- Concerts three times a week \- 60 [illegible] \- adm. concert nights \- after our dinner we wandered about among the crowd \- a sight well worth going along way to see.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 9, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day \- warmer than yesterday \- up \+ bkfo. at 8 \- after 9 I went to Schenker \+ Co. 16 Promenadaou Plats. Tourists Agents, Representing Cook \+ Son, \+ fell into the hands of M. Alfred Eidlitz \- Director \- I explained to him my Itinerary \- he is a gracious Hebrew. I think, \+ quickly caught my ideas \- the Result being that I gave him a transcription of my Itinerary from here to Naples \+ he said that if I w\. Return after N. o'elphe we have a complete little forms \- then I ret. to hotel \+ with the boys walked out to the Hof Garten and King's Residence \- to the Antique museum where we spent some time in looking at arms etc. \+ climbed to the top of the dome from whence we had a fine panoramic view of Munich \- back to hotel at 1 \+ had dinner \- after that the boys went off and I went alone to the new Rathaus where I took a number of 3 snap shots \- then down Kaufinger Str. to Karl's Thor \- then around to the hotel at 4.30 \+ Rested for an hour \- then to Schenkers \- Mr. Eidlitz had a complete Itinerary \- with R.R. Train times for best trains \+ departure from each stopping place, also ones for Saudan for no ride from Haudeck over the Stelvis \- He was extremely amiable. Together we went over the Itinerary \+ I modified the time table at two of three points \- making it more comfortable \+ convenient as to meals, arrivals \+ departures \- then I drew £70 \- 1424.50 M \+ paid him D23. 60 \- then to hotel at about 6 \- the boys came in before 7 \- I urged them to go away \+ get their eve\. meal where they pleased \+, being tired, urged Remain at the hotel \+ have a light supper \- This was done \+ I spent the eve'f quietly in my Room.
