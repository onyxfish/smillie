## Left Page

### Fri. June 7, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, but with an element of uncertainty about the weather \- Bkfst. at 8. I got "Vinyards near Ravello" off to Reiter's who is to send it to Bessie Parke Ballantine as a Wedding present \- Her thinks on was born a short time ago \- I went to Arnold & Constable's \+ exchanged some of the undercloths I bought there yesterday \- Then to Alexanders \+ to Cammayers \+ bot. a pair of bed room slippers \- Then to 2 Nat'l Bank \+ spent some time in arranging my financial matters for the summer \- It was 2.30 when I got home. \+ then lunch \- then a rest \- then out with Ralph \+ had a walk in Riverside Pk. Dinner at 6.45 Evg. \+ Jim went to Prest. Haskeltbal to see David Harris. he was taken there to-day. \- S'phoned to Jessie to look after lim. \- Ralph \+ I had a quiet evg. together.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 8, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Princeton Ball game.
Jim goes to camp.

A very pleasant mild day \- Showers predicted by weather bureau threatened but did not materialize \- Jim got his bkfst. about 7 \+ started for his Armory \- four companies of 7th Regt. going down to Ft Hamilton to camp for a week with the Regulars \- Ralph \+ I left the house at 9.30 \- went to foot of W. 23d St \- 10.25 boat, Penn. R.R. to Jersey \- 10.44 Special Yale Club Train to Princeton where we arrived just before 12 after a short hours run \- Train went into the yard \+ not up to station \- we walked directly up to the Campus \+ into the Public Street \+ then took a hack \+ rode out to Prof. Marquand's \- a very extensive \+ lovely place, was told that no one was at home \- left card \+ started back \- had gone some distance when we were overtaken by Prof M. on his bicycle, who had raced after us \- we turned back on his urgent invitation \+ 2 to his house \- Mr. Marquand returned soon \+ we were very cordially entertained \+ had a delightful lunch at 1 o'clk \- a niece of Prof. M's \- daughter of Dr. Terry \+ her husband, (I missed his name) came as we sat down to table \+ later we were all taken to the field (Mr. M. did not go) \- our seats were excellently placed opposite first base \- Play was called soon after 3 \- after an exceedingly picturesque parade of graduates \- The Red devits were very effective \+ made a strong color note in a beautiful picture \- Their car bearing head devil with big Yale bull dog on a spit, timed, baited \+ prodded by attendant devils, over Red fire was funny \- the Buster Browns \+ other class costumes combined to make their section of the bleachers exceeding fine in general effect \- the singing was fine at times, impressive \- "Old Nassau" was given with exciting effect \- As we sat in the midst of the Yale cheering section I cannot tell how effectively "Bright College Years" was given \- It was about 5 when the end of the game came \- the Princeton Rush over the field was very exciting \- we walked with the crowd direct over the college campus to the R.R. yard where we boarded our train \+ had to wait till 6.15 before it started \- I wished we might have had time to have walked over the grounds \+ seen more of the university buildings; our run to Jersey City took 65 min \- Then we had to wait some time for a boat \+ got home at last at 8.30 \- Kate soon had a good dinner ready for us \- \+ at 10 o'clk \- we got to bed pretty tired.

Beat at ball game, North stand, 2d Row. Sec F. No 23.
