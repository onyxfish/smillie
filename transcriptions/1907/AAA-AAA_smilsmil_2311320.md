## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 25, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

287 m. at 12 m.

A lovely day — almost cloudless, with light wind \+ so mild that I had to change my heavy coat for a lighter one — I spent much time at bow \+ stern — saw flying fish for first time this trip — steamer passed west of us going north — I wrote part of a letter to Mary Cook \+ part of one to Charl. I did not feel inclined to eat at lunch time, so I lay down \+ slept soundly till 3.30. I took some photos. of ships \+ one of sunset. That used up Spool No. 1 — Then I examined camera \+ found that shutter speed was not controlled by lever — all exposures were at least 1 sec. Consequently all were over-exposed. Owing to absence of screw \+ looseness of front plate the "lever attachment" had slipped out — It is too late to-day — I hope to be able to fix it to-morrow when I can have bright sunlight to see by — of course I am much disturbed by this experience — Passed San Salvador light at about 7 p.m. — after our dinner — Capt. thinks we shall get to Fortune Island to take on our cargo of negroes at about 3 am — Glorious moonlight night. Port hole in state room open all to day —

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 26, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

275 m. at 12 m.

A very lovely day. bright sunshine, few clouds \+ a Temp. a little above 70°. Capt. officers, \+ stewards all in white duck. We reached Fortune Island at about 3 A.M. \+ took on board a number of negroes for labor. At about 5 p.m. we rounded the eastern point of Cuba — a curious, barren \+ wrinkled land. Rising plateaus crossed by deep cut gullies — the mts. to the west were very effective in the sun set haze. A headache \+ my anxiety about my camera interfered with my enjoyment of the day — For nearly two hours in the m't. Dr. [illegible] \+ I worried over that camera before we could get the shutter — the diaphram \+ the bulb connections to work in their several attachments \+ connections — But at last we were successful \+ now the various functions seem to be correct in working. I spent some time writing part of a letter to Charl — Dr. Kubin sits at my left at Table — he is not communicative. Mr. Pedro Martinto sits opposite with one of his three little ones — his wife has not been able to come to Table — he impresses me very favorably he is so quiet \+ gentle with the child. The dinner was something of an affair — long strings of paper flowers \+ vines were wound about the sconces \+ looked effectively from points in the ceiling — At night the moonlight was glorious —
