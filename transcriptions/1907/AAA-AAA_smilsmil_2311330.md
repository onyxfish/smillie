## Left Page

### Sun. March 17, 1907

**Weather:** Cool to cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool to cold - cloudy most of the day with some stray bits of sunshine - Up before 7 - down a little before 8 & learning that there was to be a military mass at the Cathedral at 8 I went there in time to see the soldiers file in headed by the full band - The band had its music stands placed in front of the altar & the conductor took his place in the centre - The soldiers stood with arms grounded, down the centre aisle - they were all barefooted - The band played three or four pieces, none, it seemed to me, quite sacred - one was surely a two-step - another was an imitation of a bag-pipe, the big horns doing the drone - The congregation was large - the central seats being filled with women - a few bonnets were present but the larger number wore silk shawls, fringed, of various colors, white, pale creamy buff, pink & black over their heads - After service I [illegible] to hotel & had bfst. after that a gentleman we met in the hotel office took us to see the coffee beneficiary - the largest in this country - a place where the coffee bean goes thro' all preparations for market - where it is shucked, macerated, dried on cement floors, picked over and polished. Mr. Lynett of Scranton went with us - back to hotel after 11 pretty well tired - I rested - lunch at 12.30 - after that I went alone to coffee beneficiary again with camera & got some snaps & shots under unfavorable conditions - then to Central Ave. & took tram & rode out to the Savanna - saw some English [illegible] base practice - & got a snap at the mts. Then, by tram to Rudd's & got the photos he has printed for me - then to hotel & rested - at 5. with Dr [illegible] - went to Minister Merry's - at the legation & saw a daughter for a few minutes - then, by tram to the widow Fernandez where we were most cordially rec'd. They have been patients of the Dr. at Presbyterian Hospital - The son Joachim was under his care for a long time - The father Mauro [illegible] - was very prominent in politics & educational matters - The eldest son, Mauro, is a practicing physician a graduate of Penn. University - The daughter, Adita (diminutive of Ada) a young woman, perhaps 20, was very entertaining with her Spanish-English, talking with intense vivacity with eyes & hands, facial expression & gesticulation - she was very amusing - Miss Batchelor, a caller, a English girl was vivacious but not so unaffected - we were invited to stay to dinner & had a very good time - very adroit & one & [illegible] the water color sketches & Joachim played the piano - A sister of Mr [illegible] Principal of [illegible] High school came in later - at [illegible] to hotel under escort of Johaim.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 18, 1907

**Weather:** A glorious, cloudless & cool m[illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious, cloudless & cool m[illegible]. After a very good night I was up at about 6 & had bfst. [illegible] - It was later very deliberate - but I managed to get to station at 8 - Bill at Mimsiriol Hotel [illegible] a day, U.S. cur. That cur. goes here as promptly as Costa Rican - The United Fruit Co. is King here - \+ pays its employees in U.S. gold - I secured a good seat on the right side of car then went out & took snap shots at the town - We got away at [illegible] - The Ride over, the upland & divide down to the forest & River came on the Atlantic side of the mts. was delicious - The atmosphere was luminous - the forests were surprising to me - to the tops of the highest mts. they were as heavy as the best parts of the Adirondacks - in no places showed any evidence of forest fires - I am told that they are unknown - The River [illegible] the Reyen was as surprising and impressive under sunlight as they had been under clouds on Friday. I took several snaps while we were going out & doubt if I get anything worth the trouble - We stopped at a station for "breakfast" at about 11 - & there were detentions for one kind & another that were trying to the patience - when we got down to the low level the heat was oppressive - We got to Port Limon soon after 4 - without delay got on the steamer - The loading of bananas was in full operation - The machinery of it was very interesting - & I hoped to get a photo of it but while I was putting a new film into my camera - the work was stopped & the machinery was with drawn - We got away from the dock at about 5 o'clk - Then I washed up & had a dinner for which I had a keen appetite - Surf. M. [illegible] Smith introduced me to his father, Revd. Smith, his chair being next to mine & I passed a pleasant eve. chatting with him - he telling many interesting stories of his life - To bed at 11.
