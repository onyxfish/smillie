## Left Page

### Sat. May 4, 1907

**Weather:** Ralph came from New-Haven  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain last night + early this morning. By noon it had cleared rather muggy but it grew colder + windier before night. I had bkfst at usual hour - wrote to Mr. Wyckoff about mounting board - It was near 11 when I got to studio - I worked till 10'clk mounting large photos - + other work of that kind. Men to 2. Natl Bank - then to Denksy + Carroll + ordered card plate + cards - It was 2 o'clk when I got home + I had lunch soon after - Before 3 o'clk Ralph came + later Jim - they went off together for a tramp - I wrote to Wettekampf + to Mrs. Tilton, Treas. of N.Y. Diet Kitchen Ass. 2. I had a short rest before dinner at 6.30 - at 7.45, with the boys I went to Herald Sq. Theatre + by appointment met Charl in the lobby - my treat - The play was the English musical Entertainment - "The Orchid" - It would be hard to classify it among plays - it was almost vaudeville in character - with some very ordinary singing + a good deal of amusing dancing - Eddie Foy is a good clown - Frye & Fraguea was amusingly funny in a capable way - She made a hit in "No Wedding Bells for me." Wm Rock did some rather remarkable dancing in a gruesome make up as a wreck of a man returned from orchid hunting in Peru - Taken all together it was very nonsensical - amusing, hilarious. Melville Ellis was a good specimen of are all round variety man - his acting was passable - so was his singing + in playing the piano he showed surprising dexterity - The performance was over at 11 - on our way out we met Rob't Cook + his wife + Eleanor Cook Jones + her husband - I have not met him before - To bed after midnight.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 5, 1907

**Weather:** Ralph Ret'd to New-Haven.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, pleasant day but cool for the season - Up at 8 - had a bath + bkfst before 10' - The boys made their appearance shortly before noon + together we had an hour's stroll on Riverside before dinner at 2 - It was nearly 4 when again we went out + by subway went up to 168th St. - then walked a little further up + across lots to the Boulevard drive - then down to where the Riverside Drive is in course of construction + over all sorts of impediments we went down to 145 St - then by subway home at 6.30 - Tea - Ralph Ret'd to New-Haven by 8 o'clk train - I did some writing + got to bed early.
