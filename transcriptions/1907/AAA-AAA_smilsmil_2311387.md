## Left Page

### Thur. July 11, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day as days go here - There was a brief shower at about 9 a.m. after that it was dry \+ sometimes sunny - After 6 o'c'. we walked up to the Castle walking thro' the Allan Ramsay garden - we took a guide at the entrance of Castle \+ he showed us about with his toncal address accompaniment - after reaching the top he turned us loose \+ we saw the Scotch Regalia \+ several interesting dungeons - altho' weather was misty the views from the highest points were very fine - we wandered out \+ at the head of High St engaged a man with a one horse open vehicle to take us down High St to Holy Rood showing us the places of interest by the way - we stopped at the Parliament House - Supreme Law Court of Scotland, \+ went into the great Hall where Advocates, etc in wig \+ gown were marching back \+ forth in consultation with clients - Then on down \+ out at Holy Rood - which was closed to the public - because H.R.H., the Prince of Wales is to dine there to-night - Then on \+ thro' the old Town to Potter Row - N. 11, Hawtbns close where father was born 100 years ago, Nov. 23 - The old houses have been pulled down \+ more modern tenements built in their place - in the wall of N. 13 an old stone was set with this inscription carved on it - God be Praised for his good gifts - 1629 - to which I said Amen - Then we took a long drive out to the Agri. Show - It covers a large \+ miles of open outside the City - The throngs were immense an excellent chance to see the Rural Scot we saw the Prince \+ Princess \+ military escort drive thro' the gds we got to notice \+ had lunch at 1.15 after it the boys went off \+ I went to Nat. Gallery \+ spent a couple of hours there - Then to hotel \+ rested for an hour - Then wrote letter to Mary - took at 6 P.O. - Dinner at 7 - After that we walked down Princes St to the end \+ around the foot of Castle Rock nearly to the Grass Market - It was now after 9 - we ret'd thro' the Park or gardens after a pleasant \+ interesting walk \+ to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 12, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Edinburgh. North British Station Hotel. Room 30

A dull \+ threatening day but rain did not begin to fall till after 8 p.m. - Up at 7.30 - b'fast at 8.30 - then out - went to N. Br. Station Hotel \+ engaged Rooms - This is the largest hotel of its kind in Scotland - it is really an immense affair - very imposing in size \+ structure. We then ret'd to the Bedford - paid bill - Ret'd to N. Br. - I got into our Rooms - Then out \+ went into church yard at the foot of Calton Hill - Saw vault of Rob't Burns \+ Lincoln monument - then up to top of Calton Hill - No view - every thing lost in haze - then down \+ walked over North Bridge - back \+ down Princes St \+ I got several photos - To hotel - Rested awhile \+ had lunch. After it we again went out \+ took seats in a large open vehicle - 4 horses \+ rode out to The Firth of Forth Bridge - 8 miles - to Queen's ferry - The first part of the road was uninteresting - The after part picturesque - The drive took a little more than an hour - alighted under the bridge - very big \+ as ugly as it is big - walked on \+ into the little old town \+ at 4.10 started back in an automobile bus - getting over the road in 45 m. Then to hotel \+ I had a nap - we had dinner at 7 - very satisfactory to the boys - our Rooms are immense - The boys have a beautiful double bedded room - The dining Room is a noble big one \+ very attractive - After dinner we went out but were driven in by the rain - an orchestra played acceptably in large central Room - I read newspapers - wrote to London bankers \+ telegraphed to Mitre Hotel in Oxford - To bed after 10.
