## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 15, 1907

**Weather:** Fine clear, cold day  **Temp:**

Philp'. at 9 - wrote a note to Director McGuire, Corcoran Gallery asking if my offer of "Cliffs" had been communicated to the trustees - To studio at 10.30 \+ worked on portrait of Father till 4 o'clk - Taking my lunch in studio as usual. Then I went to Arnold \+ Constable's and bought socks - Then to 6" Ave. \+ shopped for Watch guard - Walked to 42". S' \+ Bdway. \+ there took subway home - getting in at 6 o'clk. Dinner with Jim at 6.30 - West, loafing \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 16, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:**

Ralph came from New-Haven.

A disagreeable, overcast, raw day. I had a very unpleasant experience last night - Soon after midnight I woke, my heart running like mad - for awhile I could not count the beats but after a time got on to it \+ counted 110 to the minute - I was somewhat scared but propped myself up in bed \+ waited - I had had no scare dream \+ woke slowly to a realization as to the work my pump was doing - I waited \+ in 45 min. the pulsations had subsided to 90 - Then I went to sleep \+ slept till after 6 - I had considerable pain in stomach \+ bowels - at 10 o'clk pulse was still at 70 \+ I felt very shakey - Called up D'. write - told him of my experience \+ the said he thought it an attack of indigestion - I had philp'. at 2 but did not feel able to go out till after 10 - I had rec'd a notice from P.O. Station F that a pkg - was there held for me - the duties $9.75 were paid - I went to Station F \+ found that the pkg - was a case of etchings by Ernst Roth sent from Florence for my criticism - So I told the official. He said I wo' have to go to Customs Dep'. in P.O. City Hall Park - I went there \+ met a very decent acting deputy col'. Customs Bureau, who says he does it also but he writes poetry - with him I arranged to have the etchings sent back to Roth at Florence \+ I will write to him giving instructions about his getting a consular certificate - I left 35 cents for that purpose - Then to studio at 1 o'clk - lunch - worked on my plate till 4 - Then home - Ralph had arr'. from New-Haven \+ then gone out - He came in later - Jim phoned that he would not be home to dinner \+ asked R- to meet him at the Garrick theatre at 8.15. So R- \+ I had our dinner \+ then R - went to theatre \+ met Jim - I wrote a letter to Roth \+ to bed early - not having quite recovered from last nights illness.
