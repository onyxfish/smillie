## Left Page

### Tues. June 11, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mildly pleasant day \- Bkfst soon after 8 \- wrote a letter to Bessie with enclosure to her mother giving an illustrated statement of the reception \+ presentation of the blessed Rosaries for Kate Leonard \+ Lucy Kane. It was after 11 when I went to No. Germaus Lloyd office for information concerning the return ticket that I have \- Then To Wanamakers \+ bought some shirts \- home at 1.30 \+ had lunch before 2 \- Afternoon Deardon came bringing his decorator \+ painter \+ went thro' the apartment and laid out the work to be done during the summer \- I had a short walk before dinner \- wrote to Ralph \- Evelf took down curtains \+ hangings \- I called Jessie at Presbt'n Hospital phone \+ enquired abt. David Harris \- the doctors fear that his trouble is Typhoid, but it has not developed sufficiently to make them sure.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 12, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another pleasant day \- bkfst at 8 \+ spent the m'g stripping my rooms of curtains \+ all hanging things \- Deardon brought in a great number of samples of wall paper \+ I selected two, one for hall way \- the other for my bed room \- About noon I went out \+ had a walk on the drive \- lunch before 2 \- After 3 I walked across the Park to Presbt'n Hospital \+ saw Jessie \+ Miss Hicks \+, for a moment, David Harris \- He is in for Typhoid fever, so the doctors say \- Before 5 I went in to Lenox Library \+ saw Weitenkampf for about half an hour, then walked up Fifth Ave. \+ took a look at the north wing of the Met. Mus. of Art, now in course of construction. It is very disappointing to me \- it does not harmonize with R. M. Hunt's bold facade \- it impresses me as flat, without sufficient relief. Then across the Park \+ home \- dinner before 7 \- Evelf. Mr. \+ Mr. Fred'l. Dielman called \+ we had, what seemed to me to be, a very pleasant evelf. Rec'd a short letter from Jim, in camp at Fort Hamilton \- he is having an interesting time \- I wrote to him \+ to Ralph.
