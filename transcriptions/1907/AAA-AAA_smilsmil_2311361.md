## Left Page

### Mon. May 20, 1907

**Weather:** overcast m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast m'f. but in time the wind got around to the west \+ the weather cleared beautifully bright \+ cool — I started for the studio at about 9.30 \+ stopped at Charl's \+ left with him a letter just Rec'd from Katharine from Fiume — I worked till ½ o'clk printing \+ working between prints on Father's portrait — Then I had lunch in the studio — then more printing \+ work \+ at 4.30 I left the studio — went to Riverside Park \+ wandered there till 6\. Then home \+ rested for half an hour — Then dinner — Ev'ey Jim went out \+ I wrote again on letter to Katharine.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 21, 1907

**Weather:** Pleasant day — cool \+ fresh  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — cool \+ fresh out of doors. Chilly in the house. — Up at 7 — To studio before 10. Spent the day working on Father's portrait — Printing \+ then working on the plate — I think that now I can see the end — It is very much overworked \+ not at all what I hoped to make it — Progress owing to many interruptions has been too deliberate. It lacks the snap of spontaniety \+ enthusiasm — Were I younger I would etch another one. — I had my lunch at about 2 o'clk. — Carle ton Cook came by appointment at 4.30 about the Greenwood Cemetery lot purchased by his Grandfather Cook in 1840. All of the three or four generations have a legal claim on it \+ he is trying to get release — The boys \+ I will sign off. I continued my work till after 5 — wet down paper for pfs. to-morrow. Home at 6 \+ had a short rest. Josephine \+ Smillie came in at 6.30 — but would not stay to dinner — we had dinner at 7 — Ev'ey was spent, most of it looking over Melville, from \+ tree books, 3 vols — just Rec'd from Doubleday, Page \+Co.
