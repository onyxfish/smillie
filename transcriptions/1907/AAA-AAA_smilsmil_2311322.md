## Left Page

### Fri. March 1, 1907

Montego Bay Hotel, Room 2.
miles from Kingston.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — up at 6. delicious morning — I re-packed my trunk from the bottom up. to leave it at the Hotel until I return. In my carry all I put such articles as I shall need for the week to come — Then I walked out about the grounds till 8 — Then VKfp. with P. T— then settling acc't d. 1.2. 7.3. — at 9 o'clk we started in buggy for train — getting there in 40 m. with 35 m. to wait. ticket office not yet open — great crush — negroes whites — not pleasant — Tickets to end of line 15s. first class — our car, Delaware make, was combination — 1/3 first class, 2/3 third class — no second class on the road — owned by Government. There were but 18 places in our car — quickly filled with cries for more — we had a big Costa Rican negro for neighbor. we got away on time. 10:15 — for two hours the ride was very uninteresting — but the air was pleasant \+ little dust. When we entered the hills, scenery changed for the better — elevation high overlooking wide valleys — no cultivation — no towns — but miserable little shacks every where — The 80000 negroes on the island do not gather in towns or communities — But it was not till we entered the Cock pit country that the scenery became singularly unique — I never saw anything to compare with it. The country was a wilderness of upheavals — small mts like a choppy sea — without any general trend that I could see — deep ravines, precipitous sides, all clothed with semi Tropical vegetation — the R.R. Ran in sharp curves well up on the mountain side, the wheels constantly shrieking — It was a succession of Rapidly changing view — impossible to get with my camera. After 3 we Ran thro' a heavy Rain storm \+ then, after passing Montpelier we came out upon a lovely long panorama of views, over a great valley to a Range of moderately high mts with great variety of outline — Here I saw a Realization of the Jamaica pictures that Church \+ Mignot painted years ago — The sun was in Right Relation to the scene \+ effects were perfect — Tall Trees loaded with trailing vines \+ parasites, a great riot of vegetation suggesting Tropical jungles — then a broad billowy valley, misty \+ melting in the sunshine \+ beyond all the great waves of the mts all vibrant in the sun light — From this we soon passed out to the western slope from which we saw the sea — then Montego Bay \+ its islands — in a few minutes we were down to the end of our journey — at 3.30. We met the manager of Hotel at the Station \+ he rode with us — the House Stands on a hill overlooking town \+ bay — is clumsily built \+ finished about 150 years ago, probably for some old English sugar planter \+ has been very little changed — has few Rooms — cannot be Recommended as a hotel — A fair dinner at 7, then on piazza watching moonrise (but it Rose in wrong place for picturesque effect) — The sunset over the sea was fine — we have mosquitoes — they do not sing \+ seem to be harmless.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 2, 1907

Falmouth Hotel — Mr. Jacobs.
22 miles from Montego Bay.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. Up before 7 \+ did some writing before bkfst at 8 o'clk. After that we loitered about till after 10 — Then took a hack \+ drove about Town — Market day — great crowds of negroes — very few whites to be seen — I took a number of snap shots — back to hotel at noon — The whole town out — vehicles, horses, harness \+ driver, about as miserable specimens as could be imagined — a trace broke \+ the driver got out his Repair Kit, pieces of old Rope \+ soon it was all right — the horses, small rack of bones, are kept on a run all the time — at 1 o'clk we had lunch — at 3.15 we started for Falmouth — our Rig was, if possible worse than the one we had in the mt. — The harness, if it was ever new was made for a horse as big again as one of our team — it was tied in knots to shorten it, \+ The breeching, having no cruppers slipped off the animals entirely, This was Regarded as of no consequence by our driver — he flet the miserable little beasts on a run all the time \+ they made the 22 m. in 5 m. less than 3 hours — The Road is almost level all the way \+ very good except in spots where broken stone had been newly spread — It runs for most of the way along the sea shore — There was no sea on our left. There was any approach to Surf — it broke our from shore the water being very shallow — the tideline is less than two feet. There is little that is picturesque along the Road, we passed a few spots that I wish have liked to catch with my camera — combinations of cocoanut palms, Spanish bayonets \+ Mangroves with bits of sea — we passed one extensive cane plantation \+ two or three small sugar mills — also some considerable cocoanut groves — After ninety, at 6 we drove into the market place of Falmouth — the wind having been in our faces all the way \+ the sun on our backs — on a drive it was positively refreshing — we got down at the Falmouth Hotel — Mr. Jacobs, on the Market place — a strange old house built for a Residence. The ground floor was one one great Room entered directly from the street. Then was a number of Tables, with white Table Cloths \+ dishes — dining Room in fact — at one corner was a stair way — I cannot describe the large Avon, or Salon, up stairs, with its cheap cotton lace curtains \+ its poor, pretentious furniture — all rooms were connected — no hallway — my Room was about 20 ft sq. to the peak of the Roof, 16 ft. — my base had a very poor one with mosquito net — the posts were very troublesome — we had dinner at 6.30 — we ass said of the better — Then the P. \+ I sat on the porch till moonrise — we went to beach to see it over the sea — The effect of the place upon me was uncanny \+ with no one. But hacks to be seen \+ the low, mean buildings, the squalor \+ poverty of the place I felt as tho' I were in Dahome — went to bed early \+ slept cold \+ uncomfortable — all night — two mosquitoes under the net took their fill out of me.
