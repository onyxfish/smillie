## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 10, 1907

*Garmish — Alpen hof — Room 115 —*
*2350 above sea level*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A red hot day — thunderstorms after 9 p.m. Up a little before 7 — had a bath \+ bkft. at 8 — then newspaper — then to Shenker's to see Fidlitz about some details — then to Neuhauser St. No. 16 \+ got 5 rolls of films — then to hotel — some time arraying my acc't \+ getting E. Mark, Kronen \+ franks properly divided as I have drawn enough in three currency's to meet all demands till we get to St. moritz when the Deutsche Bank is Represented. Then packing — that had to be done from Bottoms up \+ as I was already tired \+ that I found it hard work. I did not finish till 12.15 \+ at 12.30 went in to lunch — at 1.30 we left the hotel — it is a good one, rather expensive, but I have had there more home comforts for less money than at any other hotel since we left home. When I got to station saw that my trunk was not on bus — bus was sent rattling back for it \+ came in time but it made some confusion \+ uneasiness. There was a great crowd — many climbers in picturesque garb with Alpen stocks \+ ice picks — there was but me in first class carriage — we shared it with an English lady \+ her companion — a Dutchess or Earless probably — but we got on very well together — left at 2.10 \+ got to Garmish at 4.42. The ride was fearfully hot 88°. by ther. at first the country was very flat \+ uninteresting — we passed a large lake, the Staffelsee \+ came in sight of the mts. we ran directly for them \+ entered a valley about 3.30 — scenery not particularly wild — grade constantly up — at Garmish 2300 ft. above sea — a fine group of ben ranged mts. faces the town which is a nice one, [illegible] west of Partenkirchen — we went at once to the Alpen hof \+ were give Rooms on top floor with balcony facing the mts. already mentioned — The matter of Dander was at once taken up with the hotel manager \+ objection was made to our two trunks as being too heavy — The carriage man was called \+ there was a lengthy discussion several members of the hotel corps taking part — The drive is about 40 m. The usual time of starting 7 am. I objected to doing of all in one day — carriage man said he w'. take two days for it \+ I w'. pay 30 m. extra — 3 m. added for Trinkgelt — to this at last I assented but it was agreed that if the weather was fair we c'd. start for Lermoos at about 3 p.m. to-morrow — with the boys I strolled out but soon the boys took to loose. They came in \+ we had supper after 7 — I got to bed early but did not like my bed. pillows a yard sq. \+ hard — light bed clothing \+ a square feather bed on top.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 11, 1907

*Lermoos — Drei Mohren Hotel — Room 4*
*3245 feet above sea level.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain until near noon — sultry — change for better later \+ we decided to go on at 3 o'clk. I was up at 7 after a restless night — the bed was especially German — two big flat pillows a yard square filled upon a mattress that was made thicker at the head, a sheet \+ light blanket \+ then a thick, heavy feather bed, a square, not long enough to cover out shoulders — far too warm to sleep under it \+ not warm enough to sleep without it — so, I was in trouble all night — I had bkft. at 8 \+ spent the m'g. writing \+ studying up guide books \+ maps — the boys got up about 11. I had taken a short walk in town — met them on my return — then we walked over to Pfarren Kitchen — but found nothing particularly interesting — returned \+ had dinner at 1 — by this time the weather was bidding fair \+ we made preparations for our start — at 3 o'clk our landau came — a really handsome dark blue turn out with handsome horses \+ a boy driver with jaunty green hat \+ feathers — now there was a sudden change in the weather — the clouds lowered \+ a cool breeze brought the smell of rain — nevertheless we started — the hotel outfit stood about to wish us a Keleas out journey — for half an hour there was little rain drops which kept us in constant fear — but that passed — the clouds lifted \+ when we got to lermoos the sunlight streamed over the plain at the foot of the mts. For a long way our drive was by the side of a stream \+ thro' tall spruce woods till we came to the frontier at Griesen where two young soldiers sat at a gate — one addressed us in German \+ something else which the boys did not understand — he smiled indulgently at our ignorance — then spoke to our driver — I offered him my trunk keys, he shook his head \+ we drove on having passed our customs exam. The valley thro' which we had been driving closed till at last there was little more than room enough for road \+ stream — we saw surveyors stakes all along which we supposed to be for the continuation of the R.R. from Garmish — at last the valley opened \+ we could see the great mts. that surround the plain of Lermoos, we got to hotel at 6.15 — were at once shown to our rooms, very large with balcony looking east over plain \+ mts. a clean \+ inviting place. In our road we stopped at a great haus to give our horses a rest. I loaves of Rye bread — a large party of excursionists, many bicycles \+ a band were having a good time (the bicycles because they were having a rest.) after our arrival \+ putting things in our rooms we went out for a stroll — very meal at 7.30, soup, liver \+ rice, mutton \+ potatoes — I could sleep at once after it \+ get into bed at 9 o'clk. In my window — the great, bare rock mt. — Wetterstein Gebirge — [illegible] — Zugspitze — 9740 ft. — S. Schneeferner kopf 9410 \+ Wetters Thr. [illegible] 8860.
