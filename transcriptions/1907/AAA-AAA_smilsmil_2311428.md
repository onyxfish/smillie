## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 1, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

less sea - clouded m'g - then some clearing \+ promising weather with increase of wind \+ sea in the afternoon \+ again some spiteful showers. It is getting to be rather hard work to get rid of these days - it requires considerable strength just to keep about when there is so much movement of the vessel. The event of the day, or ev'f., was the presentation, for one night only, of the skit "D innocents Abroad" for which Miss Rebecca Jane Stoker is mainly responsible, but it is shared in lesser degree by Mrs. Marion T. D. Barton \& W. S and. Adams - it is a potpouley but of nonsense with no plot or story - all farce - I read the MS. \+ expected flat failure - every thing was confusion \+ uncertainty up to the last moment when it was decided to make the Presentation in the forward dining room, \+ hands turned in \+ by stretching curtains from bed rooms \+ lines of electric lights it was made pretty possible. There was a full \+ sympathetic "house" - first requirement. Then the artists were sympathetic \+ vivacious - the two ladies, Mrs Perrin of New Orleans, as a "designing widow" \+ Miss Smith of N.Y., as a "Butterfly Am. Girl", were very pretty to look at \+ amusing - Mr Ephraim Jenks, who was not a lady, (W. W. M. Curtis) \& Mr. Jenks (J. M. Heend ook) were well made up, every thing considered, \+ carried their parts well - \+ too, the Italian Prince in disguise (H. O. Counter - Jim took the part of Reggie Tompkins) a basket Am. boy - the summit of Mr. Blane, carefully constructed \+ duly labeled, where the Prince was corralled by the jeeminues was an effective scene - \+ so was the last where the widow demonstrates her affinity \+ falls into the arms of the Prince. The young daughter of a Siamese missionary - gave us a "Spirit Dance" (Eleanor Collins) \+ Donald des Granges \+ d. T. Tiggers gave a Vaudeville skit "For why I am a fool" Messrs Frank Prietter, Sarasint Curtis sang a couples of Quartette songs \+ a collection yielded $49.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 2, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cool - high wind - considerable sea \+ some nasty showers. The mid-day portion was fine notwithstanding high wind \+ falling Ther. but the latter part was nasty \+ I had to get out my steamer ulster. Jim was quite used up with a nasty cold. I got rid of a part of the day by sleeping \+ began to find the trip irksome - Cap't Dinner was served in the ev'f., minus the Cap't., perhaps because he could not fill his seats in the two rooms at the same time - it was a mild affair \+ went off decently - The ev'f was spent in the Social Room which, owing to cold weather outside was well filled - I tried to read Marie Corelli (Thelma) but found more amusement in watching my fellows - Again we had the Siamese Spirit Dance.
