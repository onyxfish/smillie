## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 2, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Strassburg - Hotel National - Room 7 -

Mng rather unpropitious - hazy & chilly - but later it cleared warm - Up before 7 \+ did some writing, also arranged for packing before 8.30 when we had bkfs. - The rest of the mng till 12 was practically given up to re-packing trunk & suit case from the bottom - I was surprised by my bill for seven days - 45 fr. M. - I claimed that I had asked for pension rates, to which I was entitled. He took off 2 m. but that left 23 m. of errors & overcharges - Mary & Chas. pd. 12 m. - of the errors - the remaining 11 m. were all Table items - resulting from confusion of waiters - not intentional, so I let it go - but it obliged me to draw money, so Charl \+ J, after lunch, when I had said my good byas - started ahead of the bus - went to the bank - I drew £ 50. \+ Repaid Charl 100 m. that I borrowed from him - Then on to Station where we met the boys \+ our luggage - we got away at 3.45 Ran to Appenwein - got there at 5.45 \+ at 6.10 Took another train that got us to Strassburg at 7 - The Hotel faces the square opposite station, away from the heart of the city but all parts easily reached by electric trains that run every where. We at once went to our Rooms, washed \+ brushed \+ being late for Table d'hole we had our evening meal in Restaurant — After that we strolled thro' some of the older streets that were very attractive to the boys - to the Cathedral — very impressive — I left the boys at 9 - Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 3, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm \+ pleasant day altho' somewhat vexatious for photographing because of the variable light - Up at 7 - bkfs. with the boys at 9.30 - Soon after that we went to Cathedral with guide went into crypt \+ wherever else we could get. we watched the clock for a time - Then the boys went up the tower while I loitered about till they came down. Bought photos \+ films - Then went to Cathedral to hear the cock crow at 12. - There was an immense crowd - all doors to Cathedral, except small door by clock, were closed - consequently, many who came could not get in as that corner of the nave was screened from other parts of the building - I managed to get just inside the door, but the boys could not follow. - The cock crowed reasonably well, but his wings were as funny in their metalic flapping as they were 45 years ago - Ralph timed the crowd coming out - it took 8 m. Then we Ret. to Hotel \+ had dinner — after that, the boys Ret. to Cathedral \+ climbed to top of Spire - Reporting that it had not been done in the mng. - I took my camera \+ went to the Canals \+ got a number of picturesque subjects - having to wait impatiently sometimes for the sun to shine out. I Ret. to Hotel at 4 \+ had a nap - Then some writing - The boys came in at 6.30 Supper at 6.45 — Well the boys went out \+ I loitered about the Reading \+ the writing Room -
