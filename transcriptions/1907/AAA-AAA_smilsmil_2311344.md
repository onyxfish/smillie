## Left Page

### Sun. April 14, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day, altho' unseasonably cold \+ windy \- Bkfs' at 9 \- before 10.30 I went out for a walk \- up Riverside to 116th St \- then to Columbia \+ inspected the grounds \+ buildings \- then to Morningside \+ down to Cathedral \+ continued my inspections \- then to Bdway \+ home by subway \- Dinner at 2 \- Ril. Kinney classmate of Jim's dined with us \- \+ later they went out together \- Charl came in about 5 \+ left soon after 6 \- He had written out a fair copy of the itinerary that we planned \- the other eve \+ advises me to cut it freely as too much work has been laid out. I dressed \+ at 7 was at the Irelands 331 E. 17th St. They were at tea \+ I sat at table with them \- all in \- a pleasant eveg till ten, then home.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 15, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day but still very cool \- Bkfs' with Jim before 8 \- then I spent some time writing a letter to Harry Watrous, Cor. Secty N.A. of D \- a formal presentation to the Academy of my collection of catalogues \+ other documents interesting to the N.A. It was 11 before I got to studio \- Finished putting stamp mark on the Academy pamphlets \+ made all into a big bundle ready for delivery \- A gentleman who did not give me his name called \+ told me that he had prepared (so I understood him) a sketch of [illegible] life \- he was a classmate of his \- he has tried unsuccessfully to get it published in some Review or magazine and asked my opinion as to having a limited publication of it \- I advised it \+ said I would be willing to contribute \- It was 2.30 before I got home to my lunch \- then I rested \+ later went out \+ had a walk \- dinner at 6.30. The eveg I gave to unsuccessful efforts to get some good velox prints from a negative of Mr. Jacobs for George.
