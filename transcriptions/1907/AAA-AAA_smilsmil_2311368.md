## Left Page

### Mon. June 3, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very disagreeable day - Rain all the m't. by 3 o'clk. the walks were dry, but the clouds hung heavy - I then went out \+ walked for more than an hour on Riverside. I had my b'k'f't. at 8 - I wrote to W. Wyckoff of Holyoke - Lunch after 1 o'clk - Some reading in the after noon when not out walking - Dinner with Jim at 6.30 - Eve'g he went out \+ I wrote to Ernest Roth at Florence. Italy.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 4, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant mild day - B'k'f't. at usual time - At about 10 o'clk. went down town to Ham. Am. office to make final payment on S.S. tickets to Europe \+ back - misunderstanding with clerk - he chg'd. $110 - I that it sh'd. be $105 - I p'd. latter aut. agreeing to pay the extra $10. if I was wrong. Then to Charles' - there for some time - Then to W. C. Cullen's \+ p'd. big bill after protesting some small items - Then up town to Aleyander's - tried on shoes ordered some time ago - Then to 2d. Nat'l. Bank \+ left order for $5000. Penn. R.R. "new notes" - also $5000 N.Y. Central notes - These run for three years, are a little below par, \+ yield about 5 3/4 %. - I buy them from sale of Cleveland dock \+ choose them as a temporary investment to tide me over the next three years (when they mature) which I am pessimistic enough to think are going to be years of unrest \+ some distress - Home to lunch at 2 - Lunch - Finding that I was in error about S.S. Rate for Room of Pres't Lincoln - I again went to Ham. Am. offices \+ p'd. the $10 - Home before 5 - a rest before dinner Jim came in with David Harris who stayed with him all night.
