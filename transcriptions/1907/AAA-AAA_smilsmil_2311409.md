## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 24, 1907

**Weather:** Cloudy but not unpleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy but not unpleasant - The early m'f was bright but cold as usual. I got up before 7 feeling fairly well after a quiet night. - After bkfs. I went out with the boys intending to take a short walk but soon found that I had no strength. I was in an old state of collapse, if that condition is possible. - I cannot understand this state unless I have overexerted myself very considerably - I do I have been unconscious of it - it is possible that the stimulation of this high, pure air has betrayed me - up to Wed. else I was feeling unusually well + after my Montanatsch climb felt so well + untired that I spoke of it to the boys in the a.m. It is mortifying - disgusting. - The manager of the hotel tells me that my experience is not unusual - I have had a chill he says. He told me that since he came to P. he had had no less than half a dozen + that there were 14 here in the house in the same condition. - There was a little poor comfort as this for me. - We went to the bankers (Juo sons) + I drew £50. Then rec'd to hotel + hung about there all day. I lunched with the boys but in the ev'g. I went to the Restaurant + had a light meal. - At 5 p.m. we kindled a fire in the big stove in the boys' large room + spent our ev'g there reading aloud from an old magazine. - The boys complain of the cold. My Ther. varies between 45° + 55° fine temp. for young + healthy persons to exercise in. - We got our trunks + suit cases packed ready for a start early in the m'g. - Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 25, 1907

**Weather:** Albula  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up before 6 - a beautiful fine m'g - a glorious day - We went down to bkfs. at about 7 + after that completed the last items of our arrangements. Paid bill - entirely satisfactory. 27 f per day for one very large room - four windows, corner, beautiful outlook, two beds + handsome furnished + small room adjoining with one bed - 378f in all for six days - so many attendants however that it cost me 32f in tips to get away. - Omnibus left at 8.30 - a long ride to Samaden - at 9.20 the train came + we got a fine 1st cl. compartm't. to ourselves - I felt extremely shaky + distressed over my condition but the excitement of the wonderful scenery very soon overcame it in great measure. - The Albula R.R. The Rhætic line Bahn, is a stupendous work - hardly less exciting than the wild mts + gorges through + over which it makes its way. - We soon plunged into the Albula gorge after leaving Samaden - The first tunnel 3¾ m. long we went thro in 10 min. From Preda down to Berguen, 3½ miles, we ran down 1330 ft thro 5 spiral tunnels + over fearsome gorges. - The mts were of the wildest description - Towering rocks + very little snow. Berguen is in a little green basin, with fine hotel + then came The Bergüner Stein or gorge. - Remarkable on both sides + a succession of surprises till we came to Filisur - after passing Alveneu we saw, under beautiful light, the fine Schafflose waterfall. Tiefcastle is a considerable town. - Beyond it The Solis bridge spans a tremendous gorge, 275' above the torrent rushing thro' a plume below. After a little comparatively open valley, we came to the Schyn Pass, a deep ravine where we ran from tunnel to viaduct in rapid succession - at last, at 12.30 we got to Coire 2445' above sea, having descended about 3500' from Pontresina. - The atmosphere was decidedly balmier. We went at once to the Steinbock hotel - opposite station + had a very good dinner - as we had to wait till 2.55 for our train on the Swiss Federal R.R. The boys went off on a route while I sunned myself in the pretty little garden of the hotel. I got quite thawed out + felt that sunshine together with the bracing new life into me - our train came on time - again we got a fine comp't. to ourselves in a new car + enjoyed the picturesque scenery, the mts receding as the valley widened till we got to Sarganz - then we were upon our route of 1904 - Passed Wallensee - then a long, uninteresting ride along the S.W. shore of Lake Zurich - we got to 3-4our hotel at about 6 + were shown to our very pleasant rooms, 152.3 - The boys have a large cor. Room looking out to the lake. - I rested for half an hour - then, with the boys went to the Tone halle where we had dinner on the Terrace. - At 8 there was a concert + the garden was illuminated - it was exceedingly pretty. - There was a great throng of people + the music was very good. - I waited + heard an overture to Rienzi - something by Handel, very familiar, but do not recollect title + a selection from Carmen. - Then feeling tired I left the boys + went to hotel + to bed at 9.
