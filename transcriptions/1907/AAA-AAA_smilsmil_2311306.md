## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 28, 1907

**Weather:** Bright \+ cloudless overhead but nasty underfoot  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ cloudless overhead but nasty underfoot. I got up late feeling worse than for two m'ts past — heavy aching in the head — eyes tender — the frontal sinuses very sensitive — throat sore \+ atemp. of 99.75°. I called up Dr. Tuttle \+ he advised a sea voyage \+ Phenacetine \+ Salol in the mean time. At noon, feeling better I went to studio, got some photo. materials, ret'd home \+ had lunch before 2 — was good for nothing in the afternoon — Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g Jim went up to Cortlandt Park skating with some fellows — I amused myself fixing a new electric plug to take the place of the one that blew out in my enlarging box some days ago.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 29, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm day — during the middle of the day the clouds grew thin \+ some weak sunshine got thro'. Bkf't. before 9 — Very uncomfortable during early m'g hours, from 4 am till I got up at 8 — but rather better than yesterday — I kept closely indoors all day nursing the raw membranes in my head — Bro. William's widow, Josephine, called about 5 o'clk \+ stayed till after 6 — I am very sorry for her — Jim came home \+ his friend, Harry Joved, took dinner with us at 7. After that they dressed \+ went to a dance getting back at 2.45 — Gould stayed all night with Jim. I spent the eve'g trying to get some Velox prints from negatives taken at St. Hubert's last summer but the plates I had had been opened some time altho' to time limit had not expired \+ it was queered so that I got nothing.
