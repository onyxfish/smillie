## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 9, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day everywhere except under foot \+ hlp's - before 9 to studio after 10. Spent the time till after 1 o'clk. in washing out old printing cloths \+ doing some much needed cleaning up. Had lunch at home after 2 o'clk - After 3 o'clk. I walked over to Saml. Colman's - 267 Central Park W. Cor. 87th S. "The Cascade" - Found him in \+ had pleasant call - gave him 4 of his etched plates that have been in my possession for some time - He told me he had been very unwell during the past winter - he looks thin \+ ill - It looks to me as tho' he \+ I are having a neck \+ neck race to the finish. Mrs. Colman came in - a picture of health - dressed in creamy white crêpe like stuff - She is, to me, enigmatic in face \+ manner \+ It was near 5 when I got home - a rest, then dinner alone - Jim dined with me to Bontellons in 36th S. \+ went to an amateur theatrical performance. - I did some experimental photo-printing.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 10, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Light fall of snow last night - overcast till after 10 a.m. then it cleared sunny \+ mild - clouded over quickly about 4 p.m. \+ there was a short shower about 5 after which the clouds broke away. I got up at 7.30 \+ had a cunning \+ thorough scrub \+ then went to bed again for half an hour - Up for all day \+ had hlp's at 9 o'clk - It was after 11 when I went out for a walk - went up Riverside to 116th St. everything afloat from melting snow. Home to dinner at 2 - After that fussing with photo's till Chase came at 4.30 - he left at 6 - Jim came in soon after \+ we had tea at 6.30 - Evg. with newspapers \+ writing.
