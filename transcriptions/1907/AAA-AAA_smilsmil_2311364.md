## Left Page

### Sun. May 26, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A despicable day \+ of evil memory. Cold \+ Rainy - a few flakes of snow, it is said, were seen floating about - I was not able to get up \+ had to send for Dr. Tuttle some fever \+ a good deal of general missery that found pointed expression in larynx \+ bronchia - Fortunately my head escaped in large degree \+ I was able to read. I slept also \+ so made up for the sleep lost last night Of course I worried greatly as to how long this condition is likely to last.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 27, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another disagreeable day - Rain, \+ in the afternoon, thunderstorms. My temp. still holds at about 101° and my head is sufficiently clear to permit considerable reading - but the time passed dismally. Dr. T. came as usual about noon - I did not get out of bed all day - At night I took 1 gr. calomel [illegible — entire passage]
