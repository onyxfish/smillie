## Left Page

### Fri. April 26, 1907

**Weather:** mild, overcast day  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild, overcast day \- Bkfs' early \- left house at 9.30 \- went to Charl's \- got letter from K. P. T \- just rec'd \- to studio soon after 10 \- wrote note to D. Billings of Public Library presenting the Hinchelwood prints \- was busy over various things till near 1 o'clk. Then to Adams' s'pp. in 42d S'. with box of H\- pfs. for Print Dept. of Mus. of Fine Arts, Boston \- Then home to lunch \- Afternoon wrote note to Emil Richter, Curator of Print Dept. Boston museum \+ copied the list of 74 pfs. The awnings on south windows put up \- Showers \- but I went out \+ had short walk \- dinner at 6.30 \- Evening wrote to Raelph.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 27, 1907

**Weather:** overcast m'f., \+ much cooler  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast m'f., \+ much cooler \- growing clearer \+ milder \- Bkfs' before 8 \- to studio after 10 \- cleared my desk \+ to work on father's portrait etching. \- Home to lunch at 1 o'clk. \- at 2 o'clk. went by sub-way to Fulton St. Then to Pier 14. Hudson River \- to Red Line S.S. Iceland \+ there found Wm \+ Katie Selkin \- his cousin, Mr. Murphy, starting for Europe \- Mollie Adams soon joined us \- Me 2's expect to be gone till Oct. \- Wm hoping to gain Tone by a stay at Carlsbad \- We spoke of the possibility of our meeting \- the boys \+ self with the 2's \- somewhere in Germany next summer. \- Mollie \+ I left the steamer at 3.20 \+ went up town by subway, separating at 42d St. I went to studio \+ worked on plate till a little after 5 \- Then home after a short walk \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g quietly alone.
