## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 1, 1907

Hotel dela Ville - Milan - Room 218.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm, close day - clouds hanging low - occasionally, light rain - till we got thro' the S. Gothard Tunnel \+ into the low country when it cleared very warm - I was up at 6 o'clk - finished my packing, had our bkfst - settled bills \+ left on the 8.37 car for Brinnen - cab to R.R. station - slow train, no 1st class - at 9.23 to Erstfeld's where we changed to fast train from Lucerne at 10.20 - great confusion - impossible to find seats - after some delay a car was added \+ we got a compt. at the end of the last car with open corridor - so, we had excellent advantages for seeing - After leaving E - the scenery soon became interesting, loops, spiral tunnels, viaducts, torrents \+ waterfalls following each other rapidly - Then came Göschenen the entrance to the great S. Gothard Tunnel \+ the starting part of roads over St. G. \+ Furka pass. We were just 15 m. in going the 9¼ miles thro' the tunnel - coming out at Airolo because we could not come out any where else - On this side the change in scenery \+ weather was very marked - again we have tunnels, loops \+ gorges opening at last into the beautiful valley of Ticino - as we approached the head of Lake Maggiore, going up the grade of Monte Cenere we had a splendid panoramic view of the broad, level valley \+ the Ticino entering the Lake at Locarno - Then tunnel thro' Lugano - About this time we had a train dinner \+ the boys met Mrs. Waite \+ Miss Hathaway \+ as sent up on Pres. Lincoln - we got to Chiasso on the border at 2 o'clk - Custom House - waited 20 m. or more \+ could learn nothing about how or when the examination would be made - \+ in queue we went on - from here to Milan road flat \+ uninteresting - got to M. about 3.30 - to Hotel dela Ville - out at once - went to Cathedral - went to Roof \+ to top of spire over central dome - 3180 steps - met Mrs. W. \+ Miss H. on the Roof - after coming down - I left the boys \+ cat to Hotel \+ rested - the Porter then told me that our trunks were detained by Customs Officials at station because they had not been examined - I gave him my keys \+ after the trunks arrived apparently had not been opened - Dinner at 7.30 - on entering the room at first table sat Mr. \+ Mrs. Atherton \+ daughter \+ Dr. \+ Mrs. Chas. Parke - warmly greeted - after dinner we chatted together till nearly 11 o'clk -

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 2, 1907

Bologna - Hotel Brun. Room 4-B

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, pleasant m'g. - heavy thunder showers in the afternoon - Bkfst - after 8 - met the Atherton's \+ Parke's in court, bid them good bye. After bkfst went to Cook's \+ drew £50 - Then to the Brera \+ did that hurriedly - then out, took a cab \+ drove to S. Maria della Grazie, took a look at the wreck of The Last Supper - then to hotel at about noon - lunch \+ a few m. past 1 started for R.R. station. Our train was a very long one - great bustling crowd - with difficulty we found seats in a first class carriage - two already in - a sort of corridor car with six seats in our compartment - Showers commenced soon after we left - we ran thro' \+ past two or three - from Parma on to Bologna - the rain was continuous - at times very heavy with almost constant thunder - we had an interesting example of first class R.R. travel in Italy - our train was a slow one - leaving M. at 1.40 \+ getting to B. at 6.30 - from start to finish no official of any grade entered our compartment - when the rain was heavy it leaked thro' the roof of the corridor \+ soon that part was a puddle - at stations there was a wild rush for seats - they filled our compartment \+ stood in the corridor - I think we were the only ones in the crowd entitled to first class - there were two persons - a fat man \+ a dirty stinking fellow who sat next to me - others got in \+ rode from one station to another standing - there were no guards to keep passengers in their proper places - they could ride where they pleased - tickets being taken up at the station on going out - for nearly two hours our comp. was a stinking mess - part of the time the windows had to be closed to keep out the rain - altho' signs prohibiting smoking \+ spitting were all about, just before we got to M. the fellow next me fished out of his pocket the stump of a half smoked cigar \+ lighted it \+ the fat man lighted a cigarette \+ the dirty man coughed \+ spit up on the dirty floor - it was disgusting - we got to the hotel at about 7 - dinner at 7.30 - Stannie \+ I went here 15 years ago with the boys - an interesting old bldg., palace of the Ghisilieri - built in 1491 - After dinner we had a walk thro' some of the streets.
