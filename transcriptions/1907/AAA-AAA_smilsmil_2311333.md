## Left Page

### Sat. March 23, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Remarkable weather continues - at about 4 o'clk this m'f I was wakened by a faint light coming in at the port hole. I got up and peering out saw a vision of perfect quiet - the sea lay glassy, without motion - a pale light from the moon, in its second quarter, over all - sea merged into the rose violet sky at an invisable horizon - in the east, low down, Venus glowed golden \+ from her a trembling trail of golden light fell over the sea \+ lay across my bed. The only evidence of life or movement was the rhythmic throbbing of the ship's heart - It was deeply impressive to me - There were no clouds in the sky during the day - The air had in it a refreshing flavor of the north \+ I loafed in the sunshine with keen enjoyment marred only by the dull headache that has depressed me for the past two days - I think that the sudden change to the cool air of San Jose \+ return again to heat has brought on an attack of catarrh of stomach \+ bowels. I read the Bismark - also Conan Doyle (waste of time) "the Hound of Baskerville" - \+ wrote more on letter to mary. In the afternoon \+ night a sort of mist, not a fog, fell on us, \+ at night everything was very damp. The moon could be seen overhead thro' it all.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 24, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A continuation of the remarkable weather of the past three days - no clouds, a quiet sea \+ very mild - At bugle time this m'f. the band played three old hymn tunes, one being that to which "Nearer my God to Thee" is sung. I was up \+ on deck when it was played, looking out over the waters to the misty horizon - it was a favorite hymn with father \+ as I listened I could hear his voice \+ see his rapt face \+ I thought of the shortness of life, our loves, our hopes, our sorrows \+ our passing - The day was passed in usual fashion, loafing, reading, sleeping. I read a portion of Bram Stoker's "The Jewel of Seven Stars" - an effort at the mystic that does not appeal to me at all - Lovely moonlight - I walked the deck for some time in the afternoon \+ eve'f. The D. \+ I exchanged snap-shots at each other on the upper deck in the afternoon. Eve'f was Cap't's dinner - a very tame affair. The Cap't was toasted by Gen'l. Sooy Smith \+ he in turn said a very few words \+ drank to the passengers.
