## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 10, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful clear, cold day \- Bldp'. at 9. Jim used up by a colic that took him during the night, purging \& vomiting with much pain \- in bed all day. I went to Tho. Lock's \+ then to studio \- all the rest getting things ready for Truckman who is to call for them to-morrow to take them to the Wellesley. I prepared my lunch at 1.30 \+ after that till 4 o'clk. I worked with graver on portrait of father \- then home \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Jim was able to get up \+ take a bowl of clam broth. Eve'g I worked over act \- especially those of Geo. Maynard

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 11, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day \- Jim was able to get up \+ go to his business this m'g. I had bldp'. before 8.30 \+ got to studio at about 10. I had a hard piece of work in getting "Cliffs of Normandy" down stairs \+ nailed into its frame. Budworth's men took it away before noon. I wonder if the com. will allow it to go to the Corcoran [illegible]. Locks' Truckman came \+ carried a lot of pictures etc up to the Wellesley. I had my lunch in studio about 1.30 \+ worked on etched portrait of father till 4 o'clk \- then up town to 79th St. \- Walked in Riverside Park enjoying the sunset \+ twilight over the River. Dinner at 6 \- Jim went to inspection \+ drill in the eve'g. I wrote to Ralph.
