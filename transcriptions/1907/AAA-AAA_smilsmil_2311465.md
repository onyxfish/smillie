## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 16, 1907

**Weather:** Clearing but not pleasant - clouded -  **Temp:**

a very little sunshine + much cold, raw wind. I got up to 6h45'. I completed the transcription of the close of father's M.S. leaving out the parts that seem to me not such as should be printed for the public eye - It would be an intrusion within family precincts - Father did not write for print - nothing was farther from his mind probably - I try to think what he might have been willing to have published - Before 1 o'clk - I went out + had a short walk - I did not enjoy it - the discomforts in mid-Region continued in somewhat less degree than yesterday after lunch I walked again - Then I had a nap - Dinner with Jim at 6.30 - Eve'g we rearranged the Globe-Wernicke book cases in the hall.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 17, 1907

**Weather:** not a pleasant day - cold, cloudy + gusty.  **Temp:**

Ralph came home for Xmas Holidays.

I managed to get to studio for a few minutes in the m'f. No new developments in the Garage business next door - I got home at about 1 o'clk. + had some lunch - Later I started out for a walk, hoping by brisk exercise to drive off the heaviness that oppressed me. Twenty minutes satisfied me that I was getting worse rather than better \+ I hurried home - Presently Ralph came in from New Haven - I sat with him for a little while - I recognized the approach of a chill + got to bed as promptly as possible - old Kate got hot water bags + bottles about me till I felt as though on a funeral pyre - D'. Tuttle was called + said he w'd. be along about 8 o'clk - When he came the chill had passed + my Temp was about 103°. He had no definite remarks to make - The night was of course an unhappy one, but before the m'f. the fever had passed.

To-day Katie's niece, a married daughter of Mr. Brady's + sister of Katie, died after lingering illness -
