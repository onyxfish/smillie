## Left Page

### Sun. July 7, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day began badly in London \+ ended charmingly in Cambridge — Up at about 8 \+ wrote to Katie A. Elkin — To Charl \+ to Mary — I called the boys at 9 \+ we had bkfst. about 10 — after that we set to work at our packing \+ it was after 12 before everything was cleared up — at 1 o'clk had lunch — paid bills \+ all the fees I could think of \+ then had nearly an hour before time to start for train — We left the hotel at 2.45 \+ drove along the embankment \+ Victoria's Street to the Liverpool St. Station, Great Eastern Railway — Our train left at 3.30 \+ we had plenty of spare time — we left exactly on time — the country most of the way was flat \+ uninteresting — we got to Cambridge on time — 4.55 \+ drove at once to the Bull Hotel — a dingy, old fashioned place — its recommendation being that it is in the midst of the colleges \+ walked in \+ out, thro the courts, back \+ forth over the bridges \+ were delighted with what we saw — the afternoon was beautifully clear — we returned to hotel at 7 \+ had a poor dinner — Out again after that \+ covered more ground till 8.30 — then feeling tired \+ lame in the back I ret'd to hotel — The boys went on — I don't like this lameness in the back — I'm afraid it means mischief.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 8, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant mng \+ a dark, rainy afternoon clearing at evening — I got up after 7 — packed \+ then went out for a walk about town \+ back up to Madeleine College — went into the courts there then back to hotel — windy \+ cold — had bkfst. with the boys left at 10.4 only — a run of less than half an hour — we walked up a gentle incline to the Cathedral, going into the college grounds on the way — the Cathedral is imposing but not beautiful to my eye — the number of disasters from falling towers, beginning as early as the 15 or 16 centuries — the walls out of plumb \+ the great rods of steel with immense turnbuckles, binding walls together made me think that there must have been much faulty construction at first, or else treacherous foundations — we climbed to the top of the great tower 237 steps \+ had a view as wide as the atmos. — place we permit over an absolutely flat country — without the haze it would make a fair sized parade — I said so to our guide \+ he said, do you have no trees in America? We had a decent lunch at Lamb's Inn, adjoining Cathedral, \+ by bus took 12.40 train to Lincoln, getting there at 2.32 — We took 'bus' White Hart Hotel \+ had a long climb up to the Cathedral — we were left at the door — did not go to hotel — made appt. with man to meet us at the door at 3.25 which left us a scant 3/4 of an hour to "do" Lincoln — it was wicked — it was getting to be very dark — the interior looked gloomy but, with assistance of Ringer who rushed us around, we saw many things of the wonderful details that we w'd not have seen if left to ourselves — it is a superb Cathedral inside \+ out \+ its position on a rise of ground in a vast plain is very commanding — we regretted leaving it greatly — There is a little old glass in it that is fine but there is a great lot of modern glass that is atrocious — let us hope that it will mellow in the course of two or three hundred years — The bus was at the door \+ by it we caught the 3.36 train — Heavy Rain on the way — Changed at Doncaster \+ got to York a little before 5 — Drove at once to Harkers \+ as soon as possible had a fire made in my room — Rested till dinner at 7 — the boys roaming in the meantime — I spent the sofy in my room nursing my fire \+ resting — the boys came in at 10 — then I [illegible] bed —
