## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 2, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some snow last night \- a disagreeable m'f clearing windy + cold \- I did not go to studio \- stayed at home \- wrote a letter to Mary + for a time worked arranging a lot of my last summer photos in albums for the boys \- About 12 I went out + had a long walk \- lunch at 1.30 \- after that attended to some bills \- more work on the albums \- a rest + before d out again + promenaded back + forth on the long terrace on Riverside drive south of the soldiers + sailors mon't. Eve'f effect over river very fine \- Dinner alone \- Jim detained down town by a rush of business in his dept. He came no before 9 + had his dinner there \-

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 3, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day \- snow commenced at about 11 o'clk. Got to studio soon after 10 \- found that the auto fellows next door had again smashed my cellar door + had made a small lake of graphite toil in the road way by my front door \- I went to them + found a class in the second story + a young man, Miller, instructing them in Running auto's \- I assailed him but he said he was not the responsible party \- Then I went to phone + called up Clarence Smith \- but got little comfort or advice from him. Then I went to Bureau of Combustibles in 67th St. was told that the garage had been examined \- + some demands would have to be complied with before it got a licence \- Then I went to Pres't Hospital to see Jesse \- Ret'd after days ago from Washington where she has been Recuperating after a sudden attack of illness about two months ago \- Then to Lenox Library + spent some time looking at the [illegible] of Jacquemart Coloring \- then I walked home in the snow \- lunch at 2 \- then I arranged photos in the two scrap books that I am preparing for Jim + Adolph for Xmas \- I had dinner alone at 6.30 \- Jim came in at 8.30 + had his \- Eve'g wrote to Herbert Smith \- the fire Ins. Exchange + made out some checks \-
