## Left Page

### Mon. July 29, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At last, a real summer day, — bright + warm. The Ther. in my room rose to 76°. I did not change my under wear but put on my lightest suit of clothes + felt really comfortable for the first time since we reached land. I was up before 7 + did some writing till 8 o'clk. when I had my bkft. alone. Then I wrote several pages of a letter to George. By 9 o'clk. other members of the family were up \+ after 10 we all of us went down, thro' the delightful wooded paths into the town + there we wandered thro' the streets, taking snap shots \+ into stores making purchases. We saw lots of students in their caps of various colors + faces slashed \+ cut + in various stages of healing — some of them still bandaged + plastered + some to recently uncovered as to be disgusting. These marks were all borne with evident satisfaction + swagger — a despicable condition of things I think. Back to hotel at 1 + had dinner. Very soon after that all of us but Katharine + started out, went to funiculare + by it to Konig Stule. How very different from the time 45 years ago when father + I climbed to it by foot path thro' the woods. Climbed, as then, to the tops of the tower + enjoyed the wide spreading view. Mary would not go up. About 4 we returned to the Schloss Station. I sauntered on thro' the woods to the hotel, the others stopped at the Terrace Restaurant where there was music — I had a nap before dinner at 7.30 (Souper, rather). After that we retired to Chas's room + till 10 were on his balcony. He, Kate \+ I took photos of the fascinating view of town and river at 9 P.M. before daylight is all gone + after the electric lights have been turned on. We set our cameras on the sill of his window + with open stops made exposures of 6, 8 + 10 min.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 30, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain + wind during the mng. — clearing in the afternoon. I had my bkft. at 8 — after that spent some time reading newspapers — then wrote an installment in letter to George. The family together for a time. Before 1 o'clk. Mr. + Mrs. Biensson arrived from Wiesbaden. W. B. is a German Australian wool man with whom Chas. has close business relations. At 1 o'clk. we had dinner. C. at a table with the B.s Afternoon, Bessie with the boys + I went to the castle + "did" the interior under the direction of a guide. At about 5 Bessie + I ret. to hotel, the boys went down to the town. I had a rest before supper. — Evng. Charl, the boys + I had quite a long walk up the road from hotel. Later, with the family + Mr. B. in Damen Zimmer, table at 11.
