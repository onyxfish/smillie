## Left Page

### Wed. June 5, 1907

**Weather:** Rain last night — overcast day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night — overcast day — thunderstorms in the afternoon with heavy showers — clearing late — Bkfs. at 8 — with Jim — Harris did not get up till 10 being unwell — I wrote to Scott Brown then went to studio + worked there putting things away for the summer till after 1 — Home + lunch at 2 — Harris gone — called me on 'phone from The Wellington, Bdway + 55. S. to tell me that the doctor told him he was in for grip or something of the kind — had taken rooms at hotel + w. be sick there — So I feel much out of sorts spent much of afternoon on my lounge — woman from dock's fitting summer slips on furniture — Jim dined alone — I went to Charles' + had dinner there + later talked over details of itinerary for next summer — I took with me a pf. of father's etched portrait — he does not like it + was far from complimentary in his remarks — all of which was very depressing to me after my weeks of anxious work. Home at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 6, 1907

*Jim's 24th Birthday. Ralph came from New Haven.*

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — I went to 2°. Nat. Bk. + paid or 5 m Penns. R.R. 3 year gold notes + 5 m H.R. N.Y. Cent. 3 year gold notes — paid for by proceeds of Cleveland dock sale. Then I went to Brook's Bro. + bought a steamer ulster, then to Amack + Constable's + bought underclothes — then up to studio — there for a few minutes then to Schuff's in 42°. St. + he declined to allow anything for the unused valise that I bought of him 2 years ago — so I told him to send it back to me — then to Meyrovitz for my spectacles — the man in trying to fit the frame to my nose chipped one of the glasses + I had to leave them — then home after 2 o'clk. — had lunch — I did little else than rest for the rest of the afternoon — Jim came in at 6.30 + we had a birthday dinner which had been prepared for us — the cake was brought in with dessert + the 24 pink candles were lighted — as Jim had to hurry to the Armory early after dinner the cake was not cut — Ralph came at 8.30 — hungry + had a belated dinner — Jim came in about 10 — then the candles on the cake were relighted — the cake was cut, the boys sampled it + ate the last of the ice cream — To bed at 11.30.
