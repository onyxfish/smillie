## Left Page

### Mon. March 11, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 79°–83°

Fine day so far as weather was concerned, but the breeze was unpleasantly stiff on our beam + the swell increased as the day went on - at night we rolled considerably + shipped some big seas to the discomfort of the negroes on deck, men women + children shipped at Kingston for Port Limon. The day was tedious to me - partly, no doubt because I was not feeling well - my food is giving me some trouble. I got a chair from deck steward for the rest of the voyage + loafed in that till 10 - then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 12, 1907

Savinilla + Barranquilla —
15 miles from S —

High wind all day - the intensity of sunlight being thinly veiled at times - In the early m'f we approached Savinilla + came up to the long, very light pier at about 7 - the bay is very broad + shallow - the shores low + barren looking, every thing tinder dry + no rain for months - I had my bath as usual at 6.30 - bkfst at 7.30 - train out at end of pier + got away with most of the passengers at 8.30 but when I got on land I loafed about so long that some of the visitors scattered about + at last the train went off + left four of them behind - the town is a gathering of mis-erable huts - the most forlorn looking place I ever was in. It was after 11 before we got to Barranquilla on the Magdalena River - there was a noisy rabble of drivers of poor, starved looking beasts, horses + mules before all sorts of vehicles - one negro who spoke a few words of English was almost mobbed by our passengers in their efforts to secure his services as interpreter between them + the quiesome drivers - by his assistance I secured a driver + two little mules in front of a two seat top wagon, to drive about for one hour for one dollar - there is no currency but paper - a dollar of it represents one cent of our money. Consequently - we paid for our ride #/100 dollars of Colombian currency. The streets are unpaved + in some of them the sand was so deep that our little mules could hardly pull us thro' it - The houses are low, rarely more than one story - stuccoed, white washed + thatched - There were no bldgs of interest - we went to a bit of a canal where a lot of boats were lying, loaded with produce + an open market was under way. We got back to R.R. Station in less than an hour + then I wandered along the river front trying to get satisfactory snap shots at the many sternwheel boats + stern wheelers of the western type - I missed a no. of good group pictures by not being quick enough - Soon after our train started on its return + after many halts it got to our steamer at about 3 - I got some sandwiches from the steward + then rested for awhile - our R.R ticket cost 44 cts each way. Dinner at 6.30 - we left loitering about + talking with a character, a bode an adventurer from away up the country.
