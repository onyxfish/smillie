## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 8, 1907

**Weather:** A bright m'f. pleasant later & light rain at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at about 10 o'clk — then to Clausen's & got a couple of sheets of picture glass — back & put print of "Master Dawplon" in frame — It is an early print but, nevertheless, worn — so I went over it very carefully with crayon, intensifying the darks & adding greatly to its effectiveness. While at British museum Print room I very carefully studied the beautiful ph. of this fine plate, a gift from Jno. Cousens — from it I learned the imperfections of the print I have. Home to lunch after 2 — I had little disposition to work during what remained of the afternoon but in the ev'g wrote to Ernest Roth.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 9, 1907

**Weather:** Pleasant day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio before 9 o'clk. Got closet ready for Douglas's man who came soon after & stained wall & floor — Lock's carman came & took up to the Wellesley a lot of framed pictures & books — I did considerable cleaning up work, hanging curtains, etc. I was after 2 when I got home to lunch — after that I spent some time in getting "The Chief's Return from Deer Stalking" into its frame, all cleaned & pasted up — At 5 I rested till dinner — Eve'g Jim went to the Armory & I worked getting many of the things brought from studio into place.
