## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 24, 1907

**Weather:** Cloudless & very cold  **Temp:** at 6 a.m. Ther. Touched 1°. at noon it was 8°.

I got up later than usual, feeling somewhat out of sorts \- bkfs'. just before 9 \- then went down town to Ham. Am. office. Got plans of Priz Eitel Frederick \+ a pass to go aboard to-morrow \- then walked from Bowling Green up to Hall of Records \+ was relieved of $9500. of my $10000 personal assessment \- then to studio at about noon \- until 4, except for lunch, I worked at clearing press corner of studio preparatory to doing some printing \+ getting some things stuck away in closets. Home at about 5 \- a rest before dinner at 6.30 \- Jim went to dine \+ as I felt tired \+ headachy I pottered about doing nothing in particular altho' there are a number of things that I should do at once.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 25, 1907

**Weather:** A miserable sort of day  **Temp:** [illegible]

about an inch of snow fell last night \- the a.m. was foggy \+ milder \+ the afternoon was darker \- the gloomy fog thicker \+ a mizzle of rain froze as it fell covering everything with a thin layer of ice. \- It was about 10.30 when I got to studio at 11.45 I went to Pier 55 \- North River \- foot of 25th St. \+ there met Dr. Tuttle \- together we went over the steamer Prinz Eitel Frederick \+ had a look at Room 17 which is secured for us \- Altho' conditions were unfavorable we were pleased with what we saw \- It was 1.15 when I got back to studio \- I had lunch \+ then, till 4 o'clk, worked on a device to hold negatives in my enlarging box \- I also boiled out some old printing cloths preparatory to some printing \- Home at 5 \- a rest \- dinner \- Everg arose to Ralph.
