## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 15, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up soon after 6 - lovely day - had bkfs. early \+ started from hotel at 8 o'clk - train left at about 8.20 - We passed Salerno \+ changed trains at the next Station, Battipaglia - here we had an uninteresting wait for half an hour - then on over a country growing more \+ more uninteresting till, at ab. 10.30 we got to Paestum - I should have noted that the R.R. made a rapid descent thro' a picturesque gorge from Cava to Salerno. There is no town at P. - a R.R. Station only - a walk of 10 m. down a walled lane brought us near to the Temple of Neptune - where we were stopped by a gate \+ keeper - Being Sunday we passed free but, to my disgust, our cameras were taken from us, a dozen or more sightseers came down on the train with us. We loafed for some time about Neptune, the air from the sea near at hand being delicious - then over to the Basilica as it is called - After about an hour we went out, getting our cameras \+ finding some good points of view outside \+ taking snap shots - Then we went onto the Temple of Ceres - here we found no impediment \+ for some time had the ruins to ourselves \+ took photos - In the shade of one of the pillars we seated ourselves \+ opened our lunch bag, brought from the Cava hotel - 3 plates, 3 knives \+ 3 forks, 3 tumblers, 3 napkins - cold chicken, beef, boiled egg, cheese, peaches, pears, figs, nuts, a bottle of Red Caferi \+ a bottle of weak coffee premier. We had as sumptuous feast, al fresco, very comfortably - as we finished, a half dozen young one appeared \+ took every remnant of food - \+ there was a good ant, \+ the empty bottles - then we sauntered to the Station \+ got there an hour too early, because of an error on a little time table given us at Salerno - The train came at 2.30 - 6 m. late \+ we had a tiresome ride back to Battipaglia where we changed train \+ got to Salerno at 4.45, half an hour late - Our chariot, with suit cases, was awaiting \+ we got away without delay - driving furiously at times, three horses - the Road in very poor condition - The last part of the drive, 12½ m. by moonlight - got to hotel at 7.40 \+ had dinner on the terrace at 8 - only one other guest, a queer atis, with his courier, came with us from P. I had a chat with Mr. Vozzi, son of V. of Cava \+ nephew of my old Vozzi - then with the boys went to the extreme end of the terrace \+ sat there for some time enjoying the moonlight.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 16, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not a pleasant day but it might easily have been worse - fine color at sunrise but grey clouds soon spread over the sky. Before bkfs. I went about the old place \+ took a number of snap shots with poor light. Bkfs. at 7.45 \+ from 8.15 at 8.30 we got away for Ravello - As we started a few small drops of rain \+ deeper gray in the sky did not promise well. We drove back to Atrani \+ there commenced the climb thro' the gorge which, tho' wild, does not have, to my mind, the picturesque grandeur \+ quality of the Swiss \+ Tyrolean nts. - apart of the way up we drove up the tofs of the landau as the sprinkling of rain had increased but before we reached R. we got \+ down again - We went at once to the Pension Palumbo \+ saw a daughter of Mr. P., her mother being unwell, \+ her Swiss husband. She took us thro' the house, \+ over the terraces - every thing very familiar - then to an old adjoining palace that she \+ her husband are fitting up for a Dependance Palumbo - an extensive \+ interesting building - as there was thunder \+ promise of a shower we hurried away \+ did not go about the town. The Cathedral tower stood familiar, but the tower of the old church of which I made a careful pen study years ago has fallen in - I missed other bits of ruin - We had not got down half way to Atrani when the shower caught us \+ it rained heavily till we got to Amalfi - before 12 o'clk - Told coachman if it did not rain at 2 o'clk. to come back for us - lunch at 12.30. The rain ceased - paid up - bid W. V. good bye \+ descended at 2. It was not till 2.40 that we got away - The P.M. was gray with a few patches of sun shine on the sea - The ride is as wild as ever - the sea as wide \+ blue - the Rocks as threatening, \+ for many of them tower above Tower the road with wide fissures that give an unstable look to the masses - the road is very tortuous \+ not so wide or as good as the Swiss roads - at times grades rising high above the sea giving wide stretch of view \+ again down almost to sea level - our vehicle was a shabby \+ chatty affair - our team three poor horses, a big one in the middle - the driving was reckless - I was thankful that we got safely down thro' the Atroni gorge where over a wet road in driving rain he drove furiously. He had no mercy for his horses - The descent to Sorrento was uninteresting - we got to the Victoria Hotel at 6 \+ I had a rest before dinner at 7.30 very fine gusto - every J wrote a letter to the ladies".
