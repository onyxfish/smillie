## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 16, 1907

New-Haven.
Yale - Princeton foot ball game.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day up early - hurried away after bkfst. \+ got to G. C. station in time to take a Special that left at 10 m. before 9 - \+ we got to N.-H. at 10 m. before 11 after a very comfortable ride - an immense train \+ packed - other trains special \+ Regular, ran at intervals of about 15 m. Till noon - I went at once to R's Room \+ found him there - his Room mate's father \+ mother soon came in \+ we chatted for a while - at about 12 we went to Commons where a foot ball dinner was served to students \+ their friends at one dollar each - at about 1 o'clk we started for the field \+ got there without trouble - our seats were good on the east side, south of the middle line - The game was called promptly at 2 \+ 5 m. a.m. just as the teams were lining up - every one of the 34,000 seats seemed to be filled - It was a wonderful sight - The singing \+ cheering was something tremendous \+ makes the blood tingle to think of it. The play was a rushing one from the start the Princeton team winning the first half 10 to 0 to the amazement \+ disgust of all Yale's men. The Yale team came back in the second half, evidently determined to do or die - \+ they did - The game from start was intensely exciting - not a stagnant moment - The new rules more open \+ there was much play that was wonderful as exhibition of sure dexterity \+ cat-like quickness. Cox \+ Tad Jones did some amazing running \+ dodging - The excitement of the 34,000 onlookers, the roars of applause - the college yells \+ the college songs made an ensemble that would have made hot the blood of the coldest cucumber that ever walked on two legs - It is impossible that one who has never lived thro' such a scene can by any means imagine it - when Yale scored 12 \+ the whistle blew the wildest excitement followed - Yale took the field \+ danced their satisfaction over it from end to end - the Princeton contingent stood solid in their places, cheered every man in their losing team. Then bowed their heads to sing Old Nassau - The Yale men stopped their dance \+ standing before the Princeton men cheered them - it was a thrilling passage - it was a wonderful day \+ a wonderful game - I am sure it will be historic - We walked into town with the army - there I left the boys - took a car \+ rode out to the Elkins's home — had dinner \+ spent the night with them.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 17, 1907

Rel. to N.Y.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day - up soon after 7 - Had a two mile walk before bkfst. at 8.30 - I was very lovely - the cloudless m't - the pure still air - the rime of frost over every thing. Ther. 34°. I enjoyed exceedingly - After bkfst. I walked down to Woolsey Hall arriving there at 10 - There is a "university service" held in Sundays in the Hall at irregular intervals, when all the Departments, Academic - Sheff. &c. join in one service - The students were privileged to bring their friends "just this once" \+ many young girls sat in the body of the house with the students. Service commenced at 10.30 - every seat was occupied - the singing was very effective - the Wesley Hymn" Come, Thou Almighty King" - "Tune" Italian Hymn" was given with a fine swing - also the Boone Hymn" flung out the banner - let it float" sung to Waltham was fine but the culmination was in the Doxology - that was mighty - The address was by Mr. [illegible] Matt interested in Yale Young Men's Christian work - He has returned from a Convention of Christian workers of all nationalities held in Tokio. He told some remarkable stories of Japan's enlightenment \+ the wide spread Oriental interest in education - students from Tibet, Korea \+ interior China flocking to the new University at Tokio - went to Ralph's Room 383 Berkley, \+ sat there for an hour enjoying the warmth of a sunshine \+ a good wood fire - then we walked to Katie's \+ at 1.30 had a dinner - After that we went out back of the house \+ there was some sport with the new toy "Diabolo" - At 4 I felt sleepy and went to my Room \+ had a nap - the boys left before I came down - Tea soon after 6 - I left the house at 7.10 - took Winchester Ave. Car, got to station at 7.30 - soon after the boys came in - just \+ I returned to N.Y., on the 7.52 train getting in to G.C. Station at 9.35 \+ up home at 10 — I got to bed at about 11.
