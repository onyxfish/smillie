## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 17, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mauch-Chunk \- Electric Mule.

A lovely Indian Summer day \- mild \+ hazy. Rlp\. before 8. Then to Penn\. R.R. Station foot of W. 23d\. St\. at 9.30 \- met Charl in 27d\. S\. car \- we took the 10.14 for Mauch Chunk \+ got there at 1.15 We had something to eat at the R.R. Restaurant lunch counter \+ then started down the track \+ after a 14 of a mile or so branched off to the tow path of the canal \+ another 14 of a mile brought us to where Thompson \+ his party were at work setting up a small electric mule on a piece of temporary track \- there we were introduced to a Westinghouse electrician, Mr. Richards, Mr. Condit, a machinist from Jersey City \+ Mr. Edw\. W. Drinker, canal traffic manager Leigh Coal \+ Navigation Co\. Mr. D\. is connected with the Phila\. Drinkers of old Montrose \- was from boyhood familiar with the name of Smillie, he said, \+ was tracked of the Drinkers, \+ Eedles, Rilays \+ other Montrose folks. Mr. Condit is father of the girl who married Wm. Gillelans eldest son \- young Girard was just completing the setting up of a small electric mule just rec\. from abroad where it took a gold medal at Milan last fall \- a larger one, also French, was not in working order, consequently our coal-laden boats that were in waiting could not be used for demonstration as expected \- we took a half mile ride on a canal boat drawn by an electric mule \- then came back to where young Girard was with his little mule \- he turned on current \+ skipped away in perfect trim. We then took a couple of boats in tow successfully. The afternoon was lovely \+ this part of the Lehigh gorge is picturesque \- but the light was too hazy for photo\. \+ \- but I took some photos of the mule \- it was about 4 where we all piled on an electric mine engine \+ rode us to a foot bridge \+ there crossed to Mauch Chunk \- Thompson led the way to a hotel where the party had drinks \- at 5 we crossed to Station on other side of river \+ then waited till 6 o'clk\. for N.Y\. 2.40 \- Thompson, Richards, Condit, Charl\. \+ I rode on it \- we had dinner on the train I was uncomfortable on the ride back \- got in late it was near 11 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 18, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another fine Indian summer day \- Up early \+ hlp\. before 8 \- After that I wrote to Scott Brown. It was after 11 when I got to studio \+ I pottered there till 1 o'clk accomplishing very little in the way of clearing up. Trouble that has developed in the stomach has bothered me \- then I went to 2d\. Nat\. Bank \+ drew some money \- Stone to lunch at 2 \- after that, assorting \+ arranging negatives till about 5 \- then feeling used up I had a rest \+ nap\. Cullen sent up the photos that I bought abroad last summer, mounted \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Evening looking over the 125 mounted photos.
