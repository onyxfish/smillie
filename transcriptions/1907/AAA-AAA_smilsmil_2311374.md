## Left Page

### Sat. June 15, 1907

**Weather:** Warm + pleasant — Ther. getting up to 78° at noon — bkf't before 8 o'clk.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio after 10 — putting things away + studying old guide books. Till 1 o'clk — then to Randall's + bought neck-ties. Then to Treess's + ordered 10 lbs Sal Ammoniac sent to St. Hubert's — then to 110 E. 38th St. + went thro' the house which has been almost entirely rebuilt + largely extended — very little of the old house remains — then home — lunch at 2 — Jim came along about 3 — sunburned + completely tired out — He was quite enthusiastic over his experiences of the past week — fortunately for him he was detailed for in-door Telephone work during the rain on Friday — I had a short walk in Riverside Park before dinner at 6.30 — Jim went to bed soon after + I turned in early.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 16, 1907

**Weather:** Went to Po'keepsie + returned.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright sunny day + uncomfortably warm. Had my bath at 7 o bkf't after 8 — at about 9.15 went to Grand Central station + bought two tickets to Po'keepsie + return, also two Parlor car seats. Then waited till near 10 when Charl came + we took 10.05 train, due at Po'K about 12.15 but delays on the road prevented us from getting there till near 1 o'clk — got up to 39 Hooker Ave. as soon as possible — family at dinner — thought we were not coming — Aunt Jessie, Simma, Harry Smillie + trained nurse at table — Uncle W. — Aunt Agnes having dinner served up stairs — we had dinner + then went up + found the "old people" finishing their meal — Uncle W. well — have finished his 94th year if he lives till Sept. We found him remarkably bright + cheerful + while we were with him there was no evidence of loss of mind — he was quick + amusing at repartee + full of his old gracious courtesy of manner — at times he is not so clear — our visit seemed to cheer + clear him very much — Aunt A. seems to be dazed + is very deaf — Aunt Jessie is terribly crippled by rheumatism + the growth in her breast, supposed to be cancer, spreads very slowly + discharges — but she is mentally very alert — we chatted till shortly after 3 — then took trolley car to station + waited for 3.45 train — late — got to N.Y. city after some suffering 6 — went to Charl's — dined with him — then spent some time reviewing my itinerary of travel + added some important notes — I went home at about 10.
