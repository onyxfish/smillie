## Left Page

### Sun. April 28, 1907

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast - rather cool - up at 7 - had a scrub \+ shampoo - Bkft. at 9 - I spent a good part of the m't arranging the photo-negatives of my last southern trip - At 12 I went out to Riverside \+ walked till near 2 o'clk - then in to dinner - After that I indulged in a nap - Charles came at about 5 - \+ stayed till 6 - Then I went out \+ had a half hour walk - Then tea at 6.30 - Evening. I did some writing \+ some reading \+ worked for some time upon classifying \+ numbering the photo-negatives of my last trip.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 29, 1907

**Weather:** Overcast \+ cool m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ warm - I had my bkft. in bed - last night at about 11 I had an unaccountable attack of pain in the region of the stomach - while located in the "pit of the stomach" it did not seem to be a stomach pain but rather a sort of neuralgic pain - I thought it might be a kick of the solar plexus. It kept me pretty miserable till after 1 o'clk - then I got to sleep - I got up at about 10 - I rec'd. a formal note from Trustees of N.Y. Public Library accepting the Henshelwood job. \+ with it a very gratifying informal note of thanks from R. Billings the Director. In it he wrote that he had seen the note I wrote to Westernkawkf concerning the necessity of having a room with side light for study of technique of prints - \+ he said my reasoning was correct \+ he thought there w'd be no difficulty in the way of adding the small room with west window, named by me, to the suite of rooms already allotted to the print dep't. I got also a note from Emil Richter curator of Prints Dep't Boston Museum of Art, acknowledging receipt of my letter to him with list of Henshelwood p'ts - but the boy had not reached him yet. I had a short walk before lunch - Afternoon I wrote note to Dr. Billings - also to Weiten, Kampf. I spent some time studying the etching p'ts sent to me \+ some time ago by Ernest Roth - I am puzzled to know how I can write to him criticism that will be perfectly clear - I had quite a long walk before dinner - The budding trees \+ brushes in Riverside Park \+ the soft, hazy atmosphere is perfectly delicious - I wish I were an artist \+ could paint what I feel - Dinner - Eve'g. loafing with news paper \+ magazines \+ to bed early.
