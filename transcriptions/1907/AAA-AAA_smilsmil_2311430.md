## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 5, 1907

**Weather:** Pleasant, but rather windy + cool for me.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I am going thro' a process of acclimatization that, judging from experience, seems to be necessary. Something like a cold has got hold of me, + affects head, eyes, throat + down upon the bronchia. I get up feeling depressed after a restless night. It depresses me to think that, after feeling so well during the summer + doing so much, that I so promptly fall into old ruts on my arrival home. Jim + I had a late bkfst. + I spent the most indoors clearing up. A short walk on Riverside before lunch at 1 o'clk. After that went to Ham. Am. Line for Pass to Hoboken Dock to meet Charl when the "Blucher" comes in. Office closed + I ret'd. home unsuccessful. Dinner at 6.30. Jim went out + I spent the evef alone.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 6, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charl arrived.

A very fine day. Soon after 7 a.m. a Telegram came saying that the Blucher w'd. dock at about 8 o'clk. So Jim + I dressed in haste. Kate gave us some coffee + we left the house at about 7.40 + got to the Ham. Am. dock at 8.30 + were told that the B. had just left Quarantine. It was a full hour before the steamer made fast + her passengers came ashore. Charl among the last. Val. Smillie was on the dock. It was some time before C. could get his things together + a further delay before he got an Inspector. Jim + I left at about 11.30 + got home at 12. + had dinner at 1. I spent part of the afternoon in unprofitable napping. At 7 o'clk. Charles came. We had tea + then till after 10 we talked of our experiences last summer. [illegible — ~2 lines]
