## Left Page

### Thur. July 25, 1907

**Weather:** Clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heidelberg - Schloss Hotel - Room 29.

Clear & very warm till evening - then clouds came up in the west - I got up early & got every thing ready for a start - Bkfst at 9 - then out with the boys & took quite a long walk thro' the public park - took a number of snap shots - back at 12 \+ with Jim went to barbers & each had a hair cut & shampoo - 1 m°. - at 1 had dinner & then in waiting or reading room till 2.30 - Then we got away, bag & baggage for R.R. station - got there too early & had to wait more than half an hour for our train at 3.20 - The Run was hot & uninteresting - a very flat country much grain being harvested - got to Heidelberg at about 5.30 - after some delay we were started in the Schloss Hotel 'bus which took a full half hour or more to climb the hill - were much disappointed, on reaching the hotel, to find that Mary & her girls had not yet arrived & there was no word for us from her - The hotel people had Rec'd. my letter but apparently had paid no attention to it - we were given good rooms, but they were at the back of the house - we went out on the terrace at 7, for supper - no table d'hote, we waited for more than half an hour for beef steak - it came at last - it came at last - Then I went into the reading room & spent nearly an hour chatting with a Detroit Lady, travelling with her daughter - we met her first in Brussels & then in Cologne - To bed before 10 -

Found letters from Katie Elkin & Mrs. Caroline Satchell - Mr. S's note was an invitation to 4 o'clk tea last Friday forwarded from the Carleton.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 26, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary & her girls arrived -

Heavy rain last night - a muggy, misty m° \+ clearing in the afternoon - much humidity - Bkfst at 9 - rec'd a card from Eliz'. from Basle saying that they w'd get to H. at 6.30 - looked at rooms \+ selected a large cor. room on our floor, overlooking Town & castle - superb view - for the girls - I wrote a letter to Katie Elkin at Bad Kissingen - & then went out alone, the boys being away, & had an extended walk thro' a fine old wood on the way to Wolfsbrunnen - Back at 1 & had dinner with the boys - They went off again & I wrote to Mr. Satchell - I was unfortunate enough to have a row in my domestic digestive apparatus that made me very uncomfortable for the rest of the day & evg. I kept in my room till after 7 when I had "supper" with the boys - I arranged with the elevator boy to let me know when Mary came - finished my meal & yet no announcement went out - made enquiries but nobody knew of the arrival of three ladies - by 8 o'clk I grew uneasy - Head Porter made it pleasant by surmising that they had got into a wrong carriage & may be had been carried over to Strassburg, or maybe up to Darmstadt - I started to walk down the road when Mary hailed me from an upper balcony - they had been in for half an hour & selected rooms directly opposite ours - They soon came down - we had a continuous jabber till 10 o'clk - There were two telegrams from Charl - one a wireless sent before reaching Plymouth - the other from Plymouth saying that he w'd get to H. at 6 p.m. to-morrow -
