## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 4, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

About 3" of snow fell during the mg — clouded all day. I did not go to studio but at home worked on Preface that I propose to place before father's "Pilgrimage" if I ever get that in shape to print. Lunch at 1.30 Afternoon I read awhile \- wrote two or three notes \+ revised \+ rewrote some of the mornings work. About 5 o'clk I went out \+ had a brisk two \+ a half mile walk. Dinner alone at 6.30 \- Jim came in after 8 \+ had his dinner. Eve\[g\], reading \+ clearing up one of the closets.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 5, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold winter day \- bkf\[s\]. before 8 \- got papers from Clarence Smith \- printed regulations of Fire Dep\[t\]. for garages \- \+ copies of his correspondence with Tillinghast \+ a statement of present status \- Thinks it possible to get injunction \- wrote to S. \+ ret\[d\]. such papers as he asked for \- To studio at about 10 but had to give some time to interviewing stable men in the court to secure their testimony if needed. I worked on "Down into the Valley" till about 1 o'clk \- Home \+ lunch at 2 \- wrote to Tillinghast \+ sent bill for second glass broken in cellar door \- also to Tolfree, owner of garage bld\[g\] \+ to Herbert Smith sending him schedule upon which the Ins. Sy. bases my rate of insurance. At 6.30 went to Charls. dinner at 7 \+ at 8.30 to Carnegie Hall. Boston Symphony Orchestra \- 2\[d\]. Concert, D. Karl Muck Conductor. Brahms Symphony No. 2, an old friend was very finely given but the great success of the evef was the Beethoven Concerto No. 5 with Paderewski at the piano \- It was a wonderful performance. I think I never before heard the combination given so perfectly \- It was a great treat \- walked home after concert.
