## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 30, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dr. Tuttle dined with us.

A very pleasant day — waited for work till 9.30 — then to studio, stopping at D—s in Lex. Ave. — After 10 when I got to studio — To work on a painting "laid in" in Oct. '95 — "Down into the Valley" — painted on it last Wed. I think I have bettered it now — then I worked for a while on an old painting "In the Huddle woods, Lake George" — \+ I think I bettered that. At about 1 o'clk. I walked to bank. 5th Ave. \+ 23d. S\. then home at 2 — had lunch — The afternoon was passed — reading, writing, arranging photos. for Dr. Tuttle's visit this eve\[ning\]. \+ napping \+ walking on Riverside — D. T. came \+ we had dinner at 6.30 — the eve\[ning\] was spent with photos. until 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 1, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A sultry day again — a raw, wintry wind from N.E. prevailing — I did not get up till 8 o'clk — then a scrub \+ b\[reakfast\]. at 9 — after that I read till after 11. Then, Jim a\[ppearing\], we went out \+ walked on Riverside till 1.40 then home \+ dinner at 2 — after that some loafing \+ a nap. Wrote a letter to Mr. Louis A. Holman of Needham, Mass. acknowledging receipt of his note of 25th Nov. \+ copy of Appleton Mag. for Dec. with article by him "The Etchers of America". Did not take any supper — wrote part of a letter to Mary.
