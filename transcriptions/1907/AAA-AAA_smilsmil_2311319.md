## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 23, 1907

**Weather:** cloudy with considerable sunshine, wind with us \+ temp. gradually rising  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- Up soon after 7 \+ out on deck \- men cleaning away the ice, flushing the decks \+ cleaning up \- many of the water pipes on the boat were frozen in N.Y. \+ much trouble followed. Btls' \- at 8 \- the day was passed in promenading the decks \+ resting in State Room. The temp. in it varying from 62°. to 65°. in the ev'g getting above 70°. I don't know what it was out of doors, probably 45° or 50°. In the ev'g I went out to the top of the wheel house aft \+ for some time enjoyed there the brilliant moonlight the effect of which was to make more vast \+ mysterious the "weltering waste" about us \- The vibrating sound of the wireless Telegraph overhead caused me to look up to the two points where the pale electric sparks were playing. It was awesome to me there as I realized that out of space, I knew not from whence was coming intelligible messages from the uttermost parts of the earth \- The Cap't. says he has just communicated with the August Wilhelm 600 miles away on her trip north.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 24, 1907

**Weather:** sunshine \+ many clouds, very strong wind blowing from S.E. \+ so chilly that my heavy overcoat was not uncomfortable  **Temp:** [illegible]

I did the usual amt. of strolling about \+ napping. Before bkft. I got out my camera to take a snap shot at a curious atmospheric effect \+ then found that Cullen's man in cleaning the lenses left out a screw in replacing the plate in front that holds controlling mechanism for diaphragm \+ time \- as a result it was loose \+ movable \- I stuck a wooden peg in the hole where the screw should be, tied string across it to bind it down \+ finally added some strips of adhesive rubber plaster. I hope that it is temporarily fixed so that it can do good work. The clouds cleared away so that at 10 o'clk. there was beautiful moonlight. Our Chilian friend Sr. discoursed to D.T. \+ me in smoking room about his Japs. experiences till after 11. Steamer passed north to the east of us to-day.
