## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 27, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully beautiful day but rather too warm for comfort — spent most of the day on boat deck — finished reading Cap't Erie + think it a clever work, the "local color" seems to be genuine + must have been studied on the spot. There was a "ball" after 9 o'clk. on the Starboard upper promenade deck — enclosed by canvas + decorated with flags + many colored electric lights — the high temp. was rather too much for some of the dancers — Jim came out of it with face streaming with perspiration + said he was wet to the skin — I went to bed early with a digestive upset that I charge in part to some stale Turbot served at dinner + some diabolical sauce or flavoring on dessert.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 28, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, misty day — after an uncomfortable night I got up before bkfs't. time + was on deck for a few minutes after which I retired to my cabin without bkfs't. I took a compound cathartic pill that kept me going for the rest of the day. I had no stomach pain but a constant discomfort somewhat like nausea — by midday my temp. was 100°. + later our cabin, altho' port was wide open, seemed so close that I asked Jim to see the doctor + learn from him if I could sleep in the hospital where I could have more room + air — the doctor came to me + said the hospital was for sick people only — so I contented myself in my own bunk. He spoke very little English + gave me powders to relieve my head + a tonic to take tomorrow before meals — fever gradually passed away + after 10 p.m. I was not disturbed + got thro' the night fairly well — no food all day but a couple of bottles of water.
Dr. Aug. Grütter.
