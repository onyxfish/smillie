## Left Page

### Thur. May 30, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Decoration Day.

After a reasonably restful night I awoke before 6 with temp. a trifle below normal \+ pulse 78. I had an early \+ light bkft. in bed \+ got up \+ dressed at about 9 o'clk. The day was a beautiful one \+ from my window I watched the parade go up Broadway as long as it interested me \- Jim made his first parade with the 7' Regt. D. Tuttle came before noon, seemed satisfied with conditions as he found them \+ gave me my discharge. I spent a considerable part of the afternoon in my bed \- then some writing \+ Reading \+ so got rid of the evef.
After parade Jim went to West Point to a ball game.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 31, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fairly pleasant \- some sunshine \- but it was unseasonably cool \- Bkft. in bed \- did not get up till 10 o'clk \- I went out two or three times in the course of the day, but not far, as I found the wind too cool for me. I wrote to Scott Brown \+ to Mr. B\- \+ to Earl Moody. After my three days in bed I feel much like a derelict \- I drift about, feeling helpless, purposless \+ dispirited \- Jim came in \+ we had dinner at 6.30 \- Evef Charl came \+ spent the evef till after 10.
