## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 2, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Edw. Cook's funeral.

Pleasant for awhile in the m't — then clouded over — Bkfs. after 8 \+ then got away as soon as I could \+ went to Miller's where I was detained some time explaining drawings for photo. enlarging box — I got to the Edw. Cook house just after Dr. Collyer had commenced reading the service — After service I went up stairs \+ joined the members of the family — then to studio there for a time then home to lunch — After that I went to Fourth Ave. near 24th \+ got some electrical supplies — then to Bayerdorfer's about clock — then to studio \+ at 4.30 went to Millers \+ got the box that was ordered in the m'f. It proved to be pretty heavy as well as cumbersome by the time I got to subway station \+ I was lucky enough to get a corner \+ a seat \+ eventually reached home in fair order. Dinner — run the eve'g R. \+ I got to work of experimenting with box \+ found the measurement for some further additions to the box — I was surprised at the amt. of heat developed by the 50 candle electric light — It may prove to be one of the most serious difficulties.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 3, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Horrible day, raw, dark \+ wet — Bkfs. before 9 — spent sometime after that I writing \+ attending to acc'ts. It was 10.30 before I went out to market, then over to P.O. Station, Columbus Ave. \+ 84th St. — met there Miss Kate Hillard — then back home to get umbrella \+ overshoes — It was noon when I got to Miller's in 40th St. Saw Fred \+ explained to him additions I wished to have made for enlarging box that I set up last night — then down town to Cullen's \+ got Bromide paper etc — then to studio at 1.15 Prepared my lunch \+ then made further preparations for experiments this eve'g. At 4 o'clk. went to Miller's — the work was done \+ I carried it home getting there at 4.45 I had a visit \+ after dinner Ralph \+ I again got at our experimenting work — I made up solutions \+ made partial prints on some very old Bromide paper. we struck snags some of which we were able to remedy — we were disappointed but not discouraged when we went to bed just before midnight
