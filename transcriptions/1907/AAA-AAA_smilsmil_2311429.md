## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 3, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely m'f. bright, clear \+ not too cool. The late afternoon was misty so that when we approached land we could not see it. I spent a part of the m'f. packing up \- there was much speculation as to whether we could get in to night the general impression being that there was only a fighting chance \+ a waiting to see the rim when but up at noon \- Hopes were high when we saw that we had done minus valley well \+ that we had but 71 m. to make \- At 5 o'clk. we took a pilot \- the mist so thick that we could see Sandy Hook but dimly \+ Coney Island not at all \- we got up to Quarantine after 6 \+ were delayed there for more than half an hour while the steerage passed the Port doctor. At this time we had dinner \- we got up to our dock by the time that was over but it was 8.15 before we had warped in, made fast \+ got ashore \- we carried ashore our hand luggage \+ were fortunate in getting our two trunks with little delay \+ in the mean time I had telephoned to Kate to be Ready for us \- The Customs search was soon over \+ we got a handsome bob \- Failed one that took us \+ our baggage to the Wellesley for $7 \- Kate Leonard \+ Katie Bradley were ready \+ Jones \+ welcomed us heartily \- our apartment looking very bright \+ cheerful after its Renovation.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 4, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable wet m'f., clearing in the afternoon. we got up \+ had bkf'. before 9 o'clk. The rest of the m'f was spent in distributing things \- I finished the letter written aboard ship to Mary \+ before lunch went to P.O. sub-station on Columbus Ave. got stamps \+ mailed it \- Lunch at 1 o'clk \- Ralph left immediately after it to catch the 2 o'clk train to N. H. I went down town to W. U. Tel. office \+ left order to send me notice of the hour when the Blucher w'. dock \- She is due Sat. or Sunday \+ Chas. is on her \- Jim \+ I had a lugubrious dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g he went out \+ I busied myself with accumulated mail matter.
