## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 14, 1907

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day — light snow flurry in early m'g. but it soon cleared to a bright, breezy \+ cool day. To studio at 10 — set palette \+ spent m'g repairing a landscape painted by Ciceri, bought abroad by Annie many years ago — it is on panel, surface cracked very badly, showing white — After working till 1.30 I got them all covered \+ now the painting is presentable, as it was not before. Home to lunch at 2.30 — During what remained of the p.m. before sunset at 4.40 I worried over the balancing of my bank acct. Then out \+ had a brisk walk on Riverside. Dinner a little earlier than usual to accomodate Jim who went to drill — I wrote to Ralph \+ a note to Emma J. \+ found other things that kept me busy till after 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 15, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day — To studio at 10 — Spent the m'g. retouching some defective negatives of last summer \+ of 1904 — Douglas's man came stained Maynard's floor \+ painted wall in my closet — Jennings came — said he could not do my work — brought another man \+ I installed him at once. Home to lunch soon after 2 o'clk. Then continued work on negatives, pasting skies on over exposed negatives that were streaked — using the stump. About 4 o'clk. I went out \+ walked on Riverside — lovely sunset \+ misty Indian summer effects — a rest before dinner — Even — making preparations to go to New-Haven to-morrow to Yale-Princeton foot ball game.
