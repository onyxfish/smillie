## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 5, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

11 in. of snow — official

A heavy fall of snow last night — this m'f — very cold \+ high wind — wide spread \+ tying things up generally. I had to content myself indoors at home — I passed the day in nursing my eye — writing up accts — Reading, writing letters \+ sleeping. I am glad that I'm not a prisoner for life — at least I hope I am not — Jim was to dine at Mary Cooks \+ take Louise to a concert to-night but Mary w'd not let her out such a night \+ called me up on 'phone — then I 'phoned to Jim. He came home to dinner \+ having two tickets to the concert went later to the Yale Club to see if he could find any one there to go with him.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 6, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright day with cold n.w. wind — Bkfs: at 9 \+ went out at 10.30 — went to studio got some things there — then to drug store then home \+ had lunch at 2 — the most of what remained of the afternoon was given to fixing negative frame for enlarging box. I had dinner alone at 6.30 — Jim did not come home. — Eve'f. made a list of goods \+ chattels for insurance.
