## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 20, 1907

**Weather:** Cloudy but pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy but pleasant till near 4 o'clk — then a thunder storm. Rain continued thro' the evefg \+ it grew rapidly colder. I got up early \+ wrote a letter to D'. Notte before I had bkfs. also to Duck \+ son — Keene Valley with check in pay mt. of plumbing bill. The boys had arranged to climb Piz Languard — 10715' — but as it was very cloudy in the early mg. that was given up — they had a late bkfs — \+ then we all went away on one of the wood paths across the stream Flatybach or Bernina — at one place there is a rocky gorge thro' which the stream rushes in cascades \+ waterfalls — the boys went on, I sauntered back, going down into the ravine — we all got back to hotel in time for lunch at 12.30 — after that, at 2.30 we took carriage \+ drove up the Roseq Val. to near the glacier foot. The road is narrow, not bad, but not in class with the wonderfully fine highways — it was not particularly interesting — we got to the restaurant in 1¾ hour — then we had to walk more than a mile to get to the foot of the nearest glacier, over a wide stretch of grass \+ gravel — the light over the snow was beautiful but clouds were rising rapidly threatening to eclipse it \+ I hurried on to get past the shoulder of a near mt. to get a shot at the Bernina Alps which rose in splendor, but the clouds came up so swiftly that it was shaded \+ a mist passing over it before I could get into position as I wished — then I became aware that showers were imminent, \+ that I had better make tracks for the shelter where I had left the vehicle — the boys, more venturesome, pushed on \+ I turned — I did my best; but before I got within ¼ of a mile of shelter the shower came not heavy at first, fortunately, so that I got but little wet — I could see the boys coming on a run \+ was very anxious lest they should over went or get very wet — They got in before the heaviest of the shower — very much blown but not very wet — at about ½ an hour the rain passed — a woman came in on a donkey completely drenched \+ bemoaning the destruction of a flower garden that — we drove back in 55 m — had a rest \+ dinner at 7 — Ev'g too cool for comfort — To bed soon after 9 — to get warm.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 21, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day but cold as Greenland, with strong wind — the Ther. in my room, with sunshine coming in at south windows, at noon stood at 56°. I was up at 6.30 — so cloudy that the boys would not attempt to climb Piz Languard. I wrote a letter to Mary, addressing it to Morgan, Hargest Co. the boys Rec'd P.O. cards from Mary \+ Katharine from Cassel — they are now on their way to the Hague \+ Katharine having broken down is to stop at Pyrmont with friends. We breakfasted together after 8 — about 10 we started for a walk \+ went towards St. Moritz to a small lake, Statzersee — a pleasant walk in spite of the cold — back to hotel soon after 12. lunch at 12.30 — at 2 we rode to the foot of the Morteratsch Glacier — 40 m. ride, then in 1¼ hour we went to the foot of the glacier — went into the ice grotto \+ climbed the rocks on the right hand, or W. side of the glacier — it is a wonderful place \+ under the brilliant sunshine the snow mountains were dazzling — the Roseq was tame compared to it — \+ we had no thunderstorm to mar the trip. On our return we stopped at a photographer's store \+ parted with some francs — then to hotel — about 5.30 — I had a rest — then, as we found that dinner dress was practically universal, we got into our glad rags \+ went to dinner at 7 o'clk — after it I sat for a while in the Salle when a band of Italian Itinerants were singing \+ making characteristic music — then I strolled outside trying to enjoy the moonlight — but it was too cold \+ the electric arc lights interfered — then to bed to get warm — I have felt surprisingly well for the past few days \+ this ev'g, after a long walk in the mg. \+ a considerable climb at the glacier I do not feel at all tired.
