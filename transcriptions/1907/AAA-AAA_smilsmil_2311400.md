## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 6, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another bright warm day with thunderstorm \+ considerable rain during the night. I had my bkfs! alone - some writing \+ reading of news paper after - Then, with the boys rode to the New Pinakothek \+ spent the m'f there - It was hard work - But few of the pictures impressed me \+ the wonder grew as I went on, why the greater part of these paintings are thought worthy of a place on the walls of a nation's Art treasure house - The majority of the landscapes I wd not hang on the walls of a room that I had to live in - The two large Piloty are masterpieces of their kind \+ make Kaulbachs big canvasses look sick - Thüsselda before Germanicus \+ Seni before the corpse of Wallenstein - Gabriel von Max's Apes as Art Critics was an interesting picture - I was much taken by Dagnan-Bouverets Child - also a little Water color by Boldini "Two friends" - I saw "Weavers", an early work by Walter Gay - I cannot like the Lenbachs - came \+ hurriedly thro' paintings on porcelain \+ the antiquarian coll'n Then to Hotel \+ had dinner. Afternoon we went to the Secession Exb'n in the bldg. opposite the Glyptotek - It was amusing, enraging \+ depressing by turns - The coarseness, vulgarity \+ brutality of the work is too much for me - naked women, hideous in form \+ color are the chief outcome of Secession inspiration - coarse \+ vulgar all the way through - in conception \+ execution. The boys were greatly amused - I left them \+ went to Hotel \+ rested - About 7 we went to the Minster Haus - a place not far from the hotel where we had our eve'g meal sitting out of doors under an awning - there was no music - a quiet place - back to Hotel at about 9.30 \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 7, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain \+ thunder last night - rain continued till about 11 - P.M. cleared - I did not get up till near 8 - had my bkfs. read newspaper \+ walked the boys after 9 - It was after 9 when I went to the Glyptotek - I was there till 12 \+ went quite carefully thro' all the halls - I am surprised that the collection is not more important - the two bldgs., the Glyptotek \+ the Exh'n bldg. of posites, exteriorally promise so much - The interior of the G. is to my mind, in decoration rather shocking - The walls are all rudely painted to represent marble - this content with imitation rather than the real thing is, I think, characteristically German - The colors, gilding of the ceilings are garish \+ the Cornelius frescos are dismally - much of the antique work has been freely Restored - and in some cases at least statues have heads that do not belong to them - much of this work is credited to Thorvaldsen - In the large Roman gallery I was much impressed by a small double statuette sitting groups of man wife said to date 1100 B.C. The modeling on the faces is singularly sharp \+ delicate - To hotel at 12 - dinner at 1 - After 2 the boys \+ I went to the Basilica of St. Boniface - then to the Münchener Jahres-Ausstellung 1907 (glass palace) - 2265 pictures of every description from great epics, or efforts, on wide spread, yards, of canvas to grotesque sketches for fugende Blätter - Oil, Water-color, Guache, Tempera, Pastel, Etchings, Aquatints \+ many new processes - we set about doing the galleries according to a line drawn on the plan - by 5 o'clk. I had got thro' one half of the 79 galleries \+ was so completely used up that I had to go back to my hotel - the boys continued \+ came in after 6 almost as much used up as I was - The impression made on me by the Exh'n is, on the whole, one of depression - I cannot now recall a single painting that stirred me as a work of art, technique or mind - I remember many that abnormally [illegible], grotesque or vulgar - sometimes downright brutal - there were a few clever blt.-white drawings for Reproduction but the mass of them were wired things illustrating tales of purely German character - there were a very few good etchings \+ many process prints that I could not understand - Eve'g we went to the Minster Haus - a beer garden, without any garden, where there was music - we had supper \+ remained till 9.30 - then to hotel -
