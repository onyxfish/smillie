## Left Page

### Fri. March 15, 1907

Imperial Hotel — San José — Costa Rica — Room 14

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rainy almost all day — coming down hard at times — a partial clearing at sunset — Bath + rub at 6.30 — B\&b at 8 — soon after, before we made fast to the wharf, the Port Doctor came on board + said that notwithstanding our clean bill of health, acting under general instructions, he quarantined us + ordered that no one should be allowed to go ashore — The Captain was greatly incensed + the Purser was sent ashore to telegraph to authorities at San José — The rain was falling pictully + the outlook was dismal — but before 10 word came that we were released — I scrambled my things together — + passed Customs examination which is very thorough — + paid 10 centavos, or 5 cents on my 5 Kilos of luggage — I got to train a few minutes before it started at 10.30 — The rain had ceased but the clouds were unbroken — for a number of miles our way was along the sea shore — a fine surf was rolling in — there were many cocoanut palms + thatched huts up on stilts — trees, with surf + sky + sea reflecting sky formed strangely picturesque pictures — Pulling, we came to a considerable extent of pestiferous looking swamp country — a strange luxurious pale growth stood in the water — very tall trees stood covered with trailing vines + parasitic growth — In the matter of lush tropical foliage + jungle business my stock of evocative pictures was greatly enlarged — Then we came to the Rio [illegible] — from that, most of the way we followed its windings thro' a Ravine like valley more or less wild + deep — some times we looked down to it hundreds of feet below, + again, we ran along side of it — The stream is turbulent, not navigable at any part, + in volume about equal to the Ausable River — The forests on the hills along the River were noble + varied greatly in color — we did not see as many banana plantations as I expected — when the down train passed us, we lay on a switch for more than an hour — At last we emerged — a fine Rain drenched forest + got to a high upland country — A rolling, wide field country enclosed by stone walls — the air was very cool + we might imagine ourselves in New England — It was almost dark when we ran down into the broad valley of San José, over 1000 ft above sea level — 102 m. by R.R. from Port Limon, a 7 hours trip ordinarily — we were 9 — The [illegible] of our journey — The Road is very poor in construction + appointments — We drove at once to Imperial Hotel — a great crush — were told when we got to desk that there was not a bed to be had — told to go over to the International, an Annex — went there — no rooms — more flying about — back to Imperial + at last found — put in two very poor rooms, but, as the clerk told us, better than nothing — at about 8 o'clk. we had dinner — a fair one, I got ourselves to eat — we had had no proper lunch on train but I had some bread + a bottle of Kola — After dinner we went out — I shopped for a hat, found a fine Panama, $1.40, in good — did not buy it — made in Ecuador — Then we went to the Public Square — Music by a very good band, all wind, of about forty — we wandered about till 9 o'clk — then to hostel + to bed

## Right Page

### Sat. March 16, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day but a trying one for photographic work as it was very cloudy during the m'f + windy — After a fairly comfortable night I got up + had b\&b at 8 — the B. + I wandered out exploring + later, with cameras went about the Cathedral, Nat. Theatre + up to a Park on a hill to the east of the city where we had a pretty good view over the town, When we separated, went to H.N. Rudd the photog. + there I spent an hour selecting photos. Then to hotel after 1 o'clk. + was told that I was too late for breakfast; the hours being from 10 to 12. However, I got some bread + butter + coffee + then, as there was considerable sunshine I went out with my camera again + took snapshots at places that I had shot at in the m'g. I got back at about 4 pretty well tired + rested for an hour — then, finding a lot of buzzards on the tiles near my window I took snap shots at them + while so engaged Knoeland my registering thermometer, a birthday present from Annie, off from the window sill to the floor + broke it — much to my sorrow — Then out for a short walk — back at 6 + had dinner with the Dr. — Coast after dressed (or not) for exhibition in honor of the Am. visitors in town, especially for the benefit of a party of Capitalists to whom some concerning some mining property — I was much surprised to find W. Sory Smith one of the party — had a little chat with him — He is to return to N.Y. on the Aug. [illegible] — Late the U.S. Minister + his family came — the Dr. Knew two of the Marry daughters — I was introduced — one was introduced to Minister Marry + invited to call at the Legation — we rec. to hotel after 9 o'clk + then to bed.
