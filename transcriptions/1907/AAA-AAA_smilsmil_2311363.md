## Left Page

### Fri. May 24, 1907

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy part of the day \+ rather cool. Up at usual time \+ after bkfs- went down to foot of W. 79th S: where the cruiser "Newark" \+ five torpedo boat Destroyers lay \+ by a little assurance \+ persistence I went aboard \+ spent an hour in a very interesting \+ instructive exploration. I was allowed to explore below \+ saw engines furnaces - bunks, officers quarters etc - etc - The men were all bright \+ civil \+ one young fellow was particularly entertaining telling of hard times during heavy weather on the way north from Cuba. When I got back home I set to work on letter to Katharine \+ stuck to it till I finished \+ mailed it \+ had lunch \+ then went to water color eyen in 57th S: my first visit \+ it closes on Sunday. Walked home. Dinner - Eve'g I wrote to Raefer.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 25, 1907

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant but unseasonably cool - Bkfs: before 8 - soon after 9 went to Meyrowit'z's \+ got the spectacles just made for me - Then to Schuff's \+ bought suit case \+ steamer trunk - Then to studio \+ dawdled about there feeling much out of sorts because of a general influenza feeling. I trimmed pfs. of portrait of father - They look very unsatisfactory to me now. I cut a mount for one. Home at 1. had a rest \+ lunch at 2. Afternoon went to W. C. Socy S45tin. Was introduced to W. G. d. Berg of Seattle - Director of Washington State Art Ass'n etc - Met E. M. J. \+ had a pleasant visit with her. Walked also home. Dinner alone at 6:45 Jim has gone with Bordman to Ridgefield to stay over Sunday. I spent the eve'g loafing with magazines.
