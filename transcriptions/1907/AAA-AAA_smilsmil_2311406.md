## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 18, 1907

**Weather:** Ther. 56° in my bedroom  **Temp:** 4910'

A perfect day — almost cloudless & delightfully cloudless cool - no dust - It is too bad that on the Stelvio, the culminating point of our trip we should have had disapointing weather — We got away at 9 o'clk - The ascent was gradual from the start, up a cultivated valley to Cierfs - a little beyond that, the land became barren - covered with a scrubby kind of pine & then the winding up over the Offen pass began — I am not enthusiastic over the pass. It is neither very wild or picturesque - it was about noon when we reached the top - The character of scenery changed very soon after passing over, we entered timber & had stretches of Road till we got to the Ofenberg Inn at about 12.30 Here we stopped for 2¼ hours & at 1 o'clk - had a dinner with 9 others - Diligence passengers - 8f with wine - it was eatable - From here on the Road was fine & growing in interest - The descent was constant - the timber getting better - On the plateau of Chamfs & Sech it was fine & the Road ran in long stretches - farther on we got into a great defile crossing some fine Ravines & at last in a tremendous deep Ravine, La Serra, we came upon the Spöl which ran hundreds of feet below us, roaring between perpendicular cliffs - If not so picturesque as the Finstermuny gorge it was far deeper & wilder - it was simply tremendous as we skirted the narrow shelf above it & was the sensation of the day - A little after 4 we entered Zernetz & drove to the Bären hof & got into our rooms, I was tired & spent an hour on my back on my bed - a fairly good dinner at 7 - no light, but candle, & no heat, I went to bed at 9.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 19, 1907

**Weather:** Wea.  **Temp:** dependence. 5910'

Another perfect day - clear, light wind & pleasantly cool - Up early & had my bkfs' - alone - The boys soon followed - & we got away on the last lap of our drive at 9 o'clk. The drive was not particularly interesting, but light air made joy - As we passed Samaden we had a look up the Albula Valley thro' which we expect to go in a few days - We got to the Roseg Hotel, at about 1 o'clk - were courteously rec'd - by the manager - The hotel proper being full we were given rooms in the dependence - a bldg. almost as large as the hotel & connected by a covered way - the Rooms are very fine - The boys have a large corner, double bedded Room with four windows to S. & W. - mine adjoins - view up Roseg Val. & across towards S. Moritz - we cleaned up a bit - then had lunch in the Restaurant - after our arrival I paid Alfred Volbouraker for 3 days extra time & a good tip, we parted affectionately — At about 2.30 we walked about ½ a mile to station of funicular Muottas Murnigl being exceptionally clear we had wonderfully fine views up the Roseg Valley to S.-Z. - over S. Moritz & lakes to S - & into the Albula Valley N.W. - the boys climbed to a higher point but I was content to remain where I was - We came down by 5.30 car (Runs every 30 min.) & walked to hotel getting in at about 6.30 - a very short rest & then we had dinner at 7. - at least the boys did - I feel too tired for dinner - So at about 8 o'clk I had a light meal in the Restaurant - I got to bed early because I was tired & cold.
