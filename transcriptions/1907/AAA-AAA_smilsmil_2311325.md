## Left Page

### Thur. March 7, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - up at 6 - wrote part of letter to Mary - Bkfs. - with B. T. - at 8 - After it a confab on piazza with B. Shelton About 10 B. T. \+ I rode out to Blue Hole. It is a charming drive with the Hole as an excuse, not needed, at the end of it. Most of the way is along the shore, parts of which are interesting - I got two or three snapshots of what I think should prove to be good pictures - Back to hotel at 12.30 - Lunch at 1. After it I wrote more in letter to Mary - a headache that had been bothering me all day increased so much that I lay on my bed for an hour \+ then I went without my dinner Evef. finished my letter to Mary, mailed it \+ after a walk outside got to bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 8, 1907 Room 48

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Constant Spring Hotel - Kingston.

Fine day - cool enough till we got to Kingston - very hot then but cool again in the evening. Up at 6. Bkfs. at 7 - bill at the rate of $5. a day - item of livery very reasonable - left hotel with regret at 7.45 - Train left at 8 - The ride along the coast to Annotto Bay, 30 miles, very fine - had some glimpses of fine cloud \+ atmospheric effects over the high mts. inland - got to Kingston at 12.30 - went at once to Ham. Am. Office \+ was much gratified to learn that our Aug. W. will arrive here to-morrow p.m. \+ sail for Cartagena \+ Port Limon according to contract. Then rode out to Constant Sps. Hotel - got our old rooms \+ had a miserable lunch - we sigh for the Lichfield - Then I had a nap - then wrote letter to Mary Cook \+ to Charl - Then a stroll over the fields - the fervent, melting heat of mid-day had passed \+ it was very pleasantly cool. Then a very poor dinner \+ lounged about till 9. then to my room \+ rearranged baggage \+ to bed at about 10.
