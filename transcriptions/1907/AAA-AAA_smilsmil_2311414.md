## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 3, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain held us prisoners to-day - It was a steady drizzle with occasional short intermissions - After bkfp. we went to a church near the hotel but ret. discouraged from further wanderings - Later, Jim went off alone \+ was gone all the m'f. I wrote a letter to Mary \+ then had a long \+ interesting conference with the Proprietor, Mr. Frank - He took Ralph \+ me over portions of the hotel to show us rooms that were still of the original proportions - immense rooms, very high ceilings - as I wrote yesterday it was built in 1491 \+ occupied by a prominent family two generations about a hundred years ago a fire destroyed a considerable portion of it \+ it was then enlarged. Mr. F. purchased it 45 yrs. ago \+ has made changes from time to time until now it is very different from its original form - At about 1 Jim ret. \+ we had lunch - at 2 o'clk altho' it was still raining quietly, I had a cab - we drove to the Academia \+ hurried thro' the collection of pictures there without finding anything that interested us. The gem of the collection, so called, is S. Cecilia by Raphael - I cannot see the merit of it - parts are, I think, very bad - it seems to have been mercilessly Restored. Then we went to the leaning Tower \+ started to climb the Fallon one, Asinelli - I got part way up the crazy, dirty thing \+ then gave it up - the boys went to the top while I waited - Then we went to several churches seeing nothing that interested us \+ at 4 we returned to the hotel \+ I improved the time by resting - Dinner at 7.30 - The boys spent the even. at the Billiard Table - I strolled about little streets - no rain - Had a very bad scare on my return - could not find the book in which I had my letter of Cr. \+ about $200 in notes - It was like a dreadful nightmare till suddenly I remembered that last night I had put it between mattresses of my bed - a very unusual precaution - ordinarily it is in my suit case, locked, at night.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 4, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Florence - Grand (Continental) Room 94.

A fine day - Spent the m'g roaming about the Town, Taking snap shots \+ buying photos. We went out to The Margherita Park - then by tram back to hotel - I asked the Porter to Telegraph to the Grand Continental at Florence - he looked over his list of hotels \+ said there was no such hotel - I looked for it in my Baedeker - no one there - puzzled - Lunch at 12.30 - left at 1 - Train left at 1.40 very long \+ full - had to take seats in smoking car - Italians seem to pay little attention to "no-smoking" signs were up all about expressly forbidding smoking in corridor or placing luggage there - no attention was paid to them - The ride, altho' diversified by many tunnels \+ viaducts was not interesting to me - The mts. \+ streams are not picturesque, or where there were no mts. or streams the country was as flat as a pancake. We got to F-- soon after 5 - a great rush out of passengers - We got into a Grand Hotel 'bus - \+ inside of it was painted Continental - so, we had landed on our feet - The Porter told us that Continental had been dropped from the title for some time - The hotel is a very fine one - There are few guests here now - not the season - We have two good sized \+ pleasant rooms - 2nd floor - with bath room between - open to both - for 28 fr. = $2.60 per day - We had a wash up \+ then out for a walk along the Arno to the Ponte Vecchio - a wonderful sunset - a picture of brilliant sky - bridges, domes, distant mts - Reflected on the mirror-like surface of the River - a scene never to be forgotten - Back to hotel at 7.30 - dinner - some time with newspapers - then to bed - At the hotel we found letters fro K.P.T - Bessie - Bro. Geo. \+ a card from Mrs. Elkin.
