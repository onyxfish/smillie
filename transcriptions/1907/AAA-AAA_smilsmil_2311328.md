## Left Page

### Wed. March 13, 1907

**Weather:** Hot day, hazy in early m'g — clear later  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot day, hazy in early m'g — clear later. Up at 6.30 + had my bath just as we were entering the long, sound-like sheet of water that leads to Cartagena — we got up + made fast to the wharf at about 8 o'clk + at 9 we went by carriage up to the town which is at the end of a promontory + almost surrounded by water, our hired passports were entirely useless. The wharf end is nearly half a mile from the town + the steam car runs back + forth at short intervals except during the middle of the day — one darkie, labelled "Tourist Guide", having a little english, acted for all of us + thro him we got a hack at a dollar an hour + drove all about the place, inside + outside of the walls — It is a peculiar + interesting place — there is no fine architecture but some of the old churches are good in mass + the balconied windows + fronts are picturesque — In the cathedral there is a very fine pulpit of marble, carved in high relief, said to be 300 years old — I got a lot of snap shots + when I prepared to put a new roll of films in to my camera found that I had brought over a fully exposed roll — of course I was wroth, but there was nothing for me to do but submit — so we went to station + had to await there for more than an hour as I did not care to walk that distance under the fierce sun. I wished to save myself for another turn in the afternoon — Lunch at 12.30 + back to town at 1.30 — the [illegible] parade did about on the ramparts + thro' the streets till near 3 o'clk — finding some exceedingly interesting bits — then we rec'd to the ship which did not get away till near 5 o'clk — I got thro' the days tramping + work in very good shape, feeling better than at any time since our start from N.Y. Dinner — then we[nt] on deck, part of time with l' [illegible] — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 14, 1907

**Weather:** A lovely day, mild bland breeze + easy swell  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day, mild bland breeze + easy swell. Bath at 6.30 in cool sea water + good rub down. I loafed much of the day reading Condensed Novels — new Burlesques by Bret Harte + found them very clever + amusing. To-day has been a most enjoyable one for the pleasure of living in such air + on such sea.
I find that we have about 40 monkeys + lots of Macaws, Parrots + such like on board, they are the property of crew, stewards etc who are taking them to N.Y. to sell there
