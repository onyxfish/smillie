## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 7, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - cloudless - very hot in the sunshine - to-day I am used up as Jim was yesterday - Perhaps peculiarities of climate may have something to do with it - Routed out of bed bet. 1 \& 2 a.m. again at 4 \& at 6 - The clearing out was so complete that I was much weakened \& did not feel able to go out till in the afternoon the boys went with me \& we called on Miss Constantine. 8-10 Via Solferino - We were warmly greeted \& she related a number of incidents in our visit here 15 years ago showing how well she remembered us - we did not see Miss Fack. I ret\. to hotel \& kept very quiet till I went to bed - Both of the boys however were on the go all day altho' Jim was still complaining

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 8, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another fine day - like to the others - after a quiet night I got up feeling better \& had bkfst. at 8.30 - As we were making preparations for a start for Fiesole a note from Miss Constantine was handed me - her private carriage \& liveried driver was at the door at our service for a ride \& a boy handed an invitation to lunch at 1. I wrote a polite note of thanks \& we started for F. Cab to Duomo - then electric tram in 50 m - to F. We got up there soon after 11 \& went at once to the old Roman ruins, theatre \& baths. We went up to the terrace of the church S. Alessandro \& had a look at the wide valley view \& the City of Florence - a sense of glaring light \& heat was the dominant one - then we ret\. to the Piazza \& took return car \& got to the Duomo at 12.45 - Cab to hotel - \& got to Miss Constantine's a few minutes after - I had lunch there - was greeted effusively by Miss Fack - I left soon after lunch - ret\. to hotel - packed up \& at 4 o'clk - we were ready to leave - At 4.30 the bus left - when we got our luggage thro' \& every thing on the station platform we were told that the train was 1 1/4 hour late - there was a large crowd - a large procession were evidently "firsts" on their way south to take steamer - The boys got Am. Magazines \& we sat on our suit cases \& waited - When at last the train came it was a very long one \& full - there was a rush - a squeeze getting off \& getting on \& it was over in a few minutes - we were lucky in getting a comp\. in which there were but two - French corridor train - 6 seats in comp\. We got away at 6.15 long after we started passengers less fortunate than we were passing up \& down seeking to adjust themselves - At 7 we had a wretched dinner - having secured "first series" we were hustled to make way for succeeding series - then we ret\. to our car the torture vigil commenced - the seats are so constructed that it is impossible to find ease or comfort in trains - by midnight, when we got to Rome, my neck was nearly broken - we had trouble in getting a fiacre - one at last \& were taken to the hotel Russie 'bus \& found that the Russie is closed but the Angleterre, run by same proprietors takes the business of the R - till it opens, Oct 1 - no other way for it - we went to the A. where pleasant Rooms were waiting for us \& after a wash got to bed at about 1 -
