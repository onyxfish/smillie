## Left Page

### Mon. June 17, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another close day — hazy sunlight — Ther. got well above 80° — Bkfs'. after 8 — after 9. started for the Ham-Am. docks at Hoboken — got there + on the new S.S. Pres'. Lincoln + at once made efforts to find the Chief Steward as I was anxious to secure seats at Table, there being about 80 less seats than there are 1st class berths — + the ship going out full. I loitered about + visited various parts of the ship meeting old stewards who claimed to have sailed with me before — the bath steward of the Prinzessen Vic. Luise will our bath steward on this trip — I went all thro' the 1st 2nd 3rd + 4th cl. pass-quarters — the vessel is a wonder in many ways — our state room is one of the most desirable on the ship — at last I saw the chief Stewards + he told me that word had come to him from the office not to locate any dining room seats till he got lists from head quarters — So, I crossed at Barclay St, went to main office, saw Schmolke + got the seats I wanted — also located our steamer chairs — then to 2nd Nat'. Bnk. — + got my letter of Cr — then to Neyrowits — 42nd S. — + got my glasses — then home + had lunch at 2 — then a good rest — then a long letter to Scott Brown + sent him checks — some other lesser notes — Dinner at 6.30 — Gil. Kinney dined with + spent part of eveg with us. I found work to do till 10.30 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 18, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another uncomfortably hot day — Bkfs'. before 8 — wrote a short letter to K. P. T. — + to some others — after 10 went to Wanamaker's + made some purchases of silver plated spoons etc for the kitchen — then to Dock's for his bill — then to studio — there for some time — then home to lunch at 2 o'clk — a rest after that — then writing again — to Frank Herrick, Earl Moodie + others — Ralph came in just before 6. Dinner at 6.30 — Eveg with the boys + to bed after 10.
