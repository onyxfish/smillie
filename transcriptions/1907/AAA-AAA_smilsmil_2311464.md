## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 14, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A horrible day \- Snow \- wind \- sleet, rain After a more than usually quiet night I overslept myself till 8.45 a.m. I was much surprised \+ on waking felt very well \+ was hopeful that I w'd. soon be out of the woods. I had bk'p. in bed \- Then up, washed \+ made change of underclothing \+ by that time found that Kidney, liver \+ stomach were still a strong "combine" ag't. my comfort \+ well being \- Then I called up Dr. Tuttle \+ asked him to favor me with a call sometime this m'f. He came before 1 o'clk. \+ after a careful examination said he th't. my trouble was from a stoppage in the gall bladder \- It unfits me for work of any kind \+ makes me wretched. It was near 2 o'clk before he left, "hop ing" that the gall bladder would "clear up" soon without trouble as it did before \- if not \-? I loafed about, unable to do any thing of the many that I could think of \+ wished could be done. I Jim came in before 6 \- dinner at 6.30 \- I ate some broth \+ custard \- I have eaten no meat or solid food since Monday \- Jim slept quietly in his chair all the eve'f.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 15, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark \+ disagreeable with more or less rain which greatly reduced the snow upon the ground \- I had my bk'p. in bed \- Then up \+ dressed \- I got out M.S. of Father's auto. biography, "A Pilgrimage" \+ the unfinished Type written "edited" copy \- about half a dozen pages at the close of the M.S. remained to be transcribed \+ to this work I devoted myself \- Jim got up \+ at 2 o'clk I indulged very carefully in a light dinner. The trouble in my mid region still remains very obscure to me \+ physically discomforting. Eve'f. Charle came up \+ sat with Jim \+ me till 10 o'clk.
