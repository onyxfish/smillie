## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 30, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Light snow in the mt till about 10 am overcast all day. I was wakened before 3 A.m. by discomfort in head — breathing thro the nose was painful \+ so it continued with occasional fits of sneezing with watery discharge till I got up to get rid of myself soon after 7. B\[illegible\]. before 8.30 — discomfort in frontal part of head constant \+ depressing. Temp 100 4/10°. I called up Dr. Tuttle \+ had a talk with him over the 'phone. He thought I w\[oul\]d. be better in bed but I told him that was more than I could stand. I had a short nap before lunch at 2 \+ again at about 4. By an effort I was able to write part of a letter to Katharine P. Throop. I read a little but on the whole was useless \+ very miserable. Jim came in soon after 6 — dressed \+ went to dinner at the Burlinghams. I had a light dinner alone \+ loafed about during the evef.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 31, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ sunny day — last night was more restful to me than for several nights past — less pain in head \+ less fever — but I thought it safer to stay in doors \+ take no risks. I had B\[illegible\]. before 9 o'clk. \+ spent the mf. with newspaper \+ writing part of a letter to K.P.T. I rec\[eive\]d. one from her this mf. from Naples. Lunch at 2 — my room is very bright \+ sunny \+ I paced it like a caged bear, hoping that I was getting some benefit from the warm sunshine. Jim \+ I had dinner at 6.30 — later he went to drill. I did some more writing \+ got to bed early.
