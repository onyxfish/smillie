## Left Page

### Thur. March 21, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day - scarcely a fleck upon the sky \+ the sea growing quieter every hour - During the n't we passed Hayti dimly seen to the east \+ Cuba was in sight, to the west, most of the afternoon. \+ at sundown we passed her eastern extremity - To-day we feel a touch of the north in the air. I spent much of the day loafing about with Bismark his reflections \+ "Reminiscences", lent to me by Saagy-Smith. I found it very interesting. Eve't the starboard promenade was very prettily dressed for a dance, flags draped to form an enclosed room \+ electric lights strung along - The band played \+ there was dancing. I spent some time writing to Mary.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 22, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudless day over a quiet sea till eve't when, for a short time, we met a long swell. Up at 6 \+ in my bath-robe watched the debarkation of the forty negroes that we took on at Fortune Island on our way down. Fortune Island seen from the ship is a long, lowlying shore with palm trees - Then had my bath \+ at 8, my bkft. About 2.30 passed San Salvador \+ mentally paid tribute to Chrs. Columbus - much of the time during the day was given to writing to Mary \+ reading Bismark. I have been quite out of sorts for the past two or three days - probably catarrhal trouble of stomach \+ bowels, resulting from rapid changes of climate - It has marred very much my enjoyment of the perfect weather we have been having.
