## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 21, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At noon 197 m. from Naples 783 from Gibraltar.

A very mild \+ pleasant \- sea perfectly smooth \+ the steamer perfectly steady \- but with a motion, elastic, not vibratory, that makes it difficult to write \- Much of the day was spent watching the human cattle that crowd the steerage \- At feeding time they are as interesting as a menagerie \- I find that we have no time given for our m/f bath \+ can get no satisfactory explanation from Ober Steward or bath steward \- I got one at 7 but the boys had to go without \- A correction is promised to-morrow \- I dont like the cooking \- Evf. a glorious moonlight \- have never known any thing finer or milder in the tropics. A crowd on the boat deck, where there are many chairs, enjoying the outlook \+ the full moon.

### Sun. Sept. 22, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Noon 359 m. 424 from Gibraltar.

Still very warm \- water very smooth \- wind with us \- nothing worth recording during the day \- naps, lounging \- reading \- lovely moonlight night \- many gathered on the boat deck \- coolest, pleasantest place on the ship \-
