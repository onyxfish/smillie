## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 28, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An unpleasant day. At 5 a.m. my temp. was normal. I remained in bed till noon. Mr. Talfree called me up on 'phone but I had to send word that I could not leave my bed to go to 'phone, so I do not know what he wished to say to me. I wrote a note to him in the afternoon. I got up before 1, washed \+ dressed \+ had a light meal. Temp. up again a little later in the day. D. T. came after 4 \+ says that in appearance, in every respect, he thinks I look better. I wish I could feel better. Ev'f. Jim went to dinner of his 7th Reg't. Co. Ralph \+ I had dinner, I making a pretence of eating. Later R. went to a party given by Mr. Woodin. I loafed around.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 29, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, pleasant day. I got up \+ had bkft. at about 9 o'clk. Temp. normal but it fell off a little during the day. I moved about a good deal during the day but did not dare to go out-of-doors. D. Tuttle called me up on 'phone in the afternoon. I tried to write some letters but could not do so, nor could I do much reading. I ate a very light dinner with the boys. Afternoon Charl came \+ spent an hour or two with us. Ev't Ralph by invitation took supper with the Edgars. He came home early but was evidently well pleased with his call. Jim went down to Mary Cook's.
