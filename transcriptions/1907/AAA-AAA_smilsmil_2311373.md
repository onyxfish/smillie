## Left Page

### Thur. June 13, 1907

**Weather:** Very bright pleasant mt. — Clouded p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

a light sprinkle of rain about 6 o'clk - nothing more - B\&P. before 8 - busy all day at home clearing the decks for carpenters, painters \+ paperhangers - got all pictures down \+ stowed away in closets — all hangings dusted \& folded - lunch before 2 - work continued in the afternoon - went out for a walk after 5 o'clk - shortened by a sprinkle of rain, not enough to wet the walks - dinner at 6 30 - Eve'g marking clothes etc.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 14, 1907

**Weather:** A disagreeable wet day - Rain last night until 11 to-night  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- I hope that Jim will get no damage from it - Before 10 o'clk. went to D. Tuttle's with specimens of wine for analysis - then down town to Ham. Am. office \& got card to admit to Pres. Lincoln S.S. on Monday - then up town to studio \& worked there packing away things till after 1 - Then home to lunch - Afternoon at home writing \+ putting away things - Eve'g went to Charle's \+ had dinner at 7 — After that studying maps \+ perfecting itinerary for next Summers proposed Travel - Home before 11.
