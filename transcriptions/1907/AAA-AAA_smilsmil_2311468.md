## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 22, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — I had bkfst. before 9 o'clk. After that read paper + wrote on letter to Bessie — the boys got their bkfst. before 11 + then to church. Before 1 o clk. I went out on Riverside Drive for a walk — I met Jim + Ralph + we continued our walk up to about 100th St. Then home + had dinner at 2. After that, read awhile — then a nap — then again writing on Bessie's letter — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. the boys had a good deal of "Diabolo" practice — Ralph is very expert at it — more reading + to bed early.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 23, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A tempestuous day — a very high wind and excessive rain made havoc in the city + on the bay. I was sure it would not be wise for me to go out in such weather, so I reluctantly stayed at home. I felt little enough like going out if it had been a fine day — my head was heavy with dull pain + I had the discomfort of a presentiment that further ills were pending — However, I was up + dressed + set myself to work to finish a letter to Bessie though commenced some days ago — It was dismal work in every way, I could not think, I could not write — but the letter was finished after a fashion + mailed in the afternoon. By that time I had developed a temp. of 100 2/5. I have no appetite + eat very little — I got to bed early — the boys went out.
