## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 26, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day except when up \+ wrapped in blankets I sat in big chair before fire while my bed \+ room was aired \- my fever was down very much in the m't. but gradually increased till in the afternoon it got above 101°\- However, I was fairly comfortable during the day \+ was able to read a good deal. Took a "day off" with H'y. Van Dyke \+ Read Hopkinson Smiths latest. D'r. Tuette called before noon \+ made careful examination \+ said it was satisfactory \- took away specimen of urine \- About 8 o'clk called up on 'phone to know how I was getting along. Ev'g. Jim went to a club dinner. Ralph at home with me \- Geo. called me up to tell me that Geo. W. Maynard was to marry Miss Brownell at 9 o'clk this ev'g.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 27, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At 7 o'clk this m'g I was 1° below normal later in the day it crept up \+ at 4 p.m. it had risen to almost a degree above normal again I read much during the day. D'r. T. came at about noon \+ brought a good report of analysis of urine \- said he found improvement in pulse \- At the time of his visit my temp. was about normal \- but it raised later in the day. Ev'g. Jim went to a club dinner but came home early under the weather \- Ralph did not go out. I discovered that I have not yet paid taxes on my studio building. I wrote to Clarence Smitts asking him if he could get the bill \+ send it to me so that I could pay it before Jan'y 1st.
