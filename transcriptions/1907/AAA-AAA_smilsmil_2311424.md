## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 23, 1907

At noon 360 to Q- 64 — Gibraltar at 5 p.m.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A change in the weather — strong breeze with us — white caps — but no swell yet — clouded — showers in the afternoon — I had my bath before 6 \+ spent the day reading, loafing \+ sleeping — There was a smart shower before we got to Gibraltar — when we dropped anchor the Rock was well covered by a cloud \+ for a time we could not see across the strait but later the clouds lifted \+ for a little time we had a clear view — There was the usual excitement about getting ashore — the usual delays \+ the usual crowding — The tender made but one trip \+ every bit of standing room was filled — the boys \+ I went ashore altho' it was late \+ the weather still threatening — we found the markets closed \+ the streets very muddy from the recent rain — The main street was swarming with people, lots of turbaned, bare legged \+ tuniced orientals giving an element of picturesqueness to an otherwise commonplace looking street — we slid about in the pasty mud for a time, bought some curious fruit \+ some remarkably large \+ sweet Malaga grapes \+ then returned to the tender where we had to wait for an hour \+ a half in considerable discomfort as there was little protection \+ no comfortable seats — After 7 the passengers began to return \+ soon the boat was swarming with them \+ their purchases — In the short time allowed the amount of "shopping" done by the women was amazing for diversity of character, quantity \+ quality — At 7.45 — Long after dark, the tender started for the steamer \+ in less than half an hour we were aboard — Dinner had been delayed till almost 8.30 when the K.L. got under way — Later in the evening fine moonlight.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 24, 1907

Noon 225 — 3005 m. to N.Y.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded \+ showers during the m'g — afternoon clearing charmingly — a breezy day — air very bland — ocean swell causing a gentle heaving fore \+ aft — I was called by bath-steward at 6.30 but did not get up owing to headache till about 8 o'clk — The day was passed in lazy fashion — much of the time up in the boat-deck. Eve'g in writing room reading short stories by Bret Harte.
