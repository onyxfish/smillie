## Left Page

### Mon. July 1, 1907

Plymouth \- Duke of Cornwall Hotel (323 m) Rooms 29. 30

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day, but I was in no physical condition to enjoy it \- my head ached miserably all the air passages in head \+ throat were much irritated \+ I was very despondent because my miseries were so like the beginnings of my recent downfales. I kept about all the time but could not get warm \- At 2 o'clk. we sighted the Scilly light \- at dusk we were off Eddystone light \+ at 10.30 we were up to Plymouth \- It was some time before the tender came \+ considerable time was lost before it was made fast \- a number of passengers \+ luggage came on board, that took more time \+ added to the confusion \- the Steward got away with our small pieces \+ assured me that he had left nothing \+ I trusted him \- The sail from Steamer to wharf may have taken 15 or 20 m. It was cold \+ windy \+ I was unhappy \- The Customs Exam. was orderly, prompt \+ perfunctory \- Ralph's suit case was the only one of our things opened \- Then R\- went ahead to the hotel to secure Rooms as it was too late to think of going on to London \- we had two very comfortable Rooms \+ got to bed at midnight \-

To add to my miseries I found that my camera had been left in my state room on the steamer \- while I blame myself severely for my carelessness, I know that it was because of the condition of my head.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 2, 1907

London. Hotel Carleton. Rooms 577\-578.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 64°

Very pleasant day in spite of several light showers \- I awoke at about 4 Ack. with a headache so severe \+ all air passages so irritated that I feared that I would not be able to go on \- but I took Caffiene \+ Acetanilide \+ after 9 got asleep \- woke at 8 \- my temp. 4/10 above normal \- headache much better \+ irritation of breathing passages much less \- got up hopeful \+ had help \-
After 9 \- Just missed getting 10.30 Exp. to Exeter but to way train at 10.40 \- By the way, I should say that the Duke of Cornwall Hotel was satisfactory \- Rooms large beds \+ [illegible] good \+ charges regular \- we got to Exeter at 12.45 \+ had just an hour to see the cathedral \- took cab \- much hurried at Cathedral, to our great regret \- back to station \+ took 1.45 Sup. to London \- no stops \- a de luxe train "The Cornish Riviera Sup". We had a fine first class compartment \- corridor car, all to ourselves \- after 2 o'clk the boys went into dining car \+ had a good meal at cost about equal to home charges they were much pleased. The country we passed thro' on our way to London was very charming much of it showing complete finish in its cultivation \- we got to London on time, 4.45 \+ got our luggage on top of a four wheeler \+ drove direct to the Carlton \- we were assigned to Rooms on the 5th floor windows opening on large court, very quiet \+ satisfactory \+ well furnished \- I surprised the chambermaid by asking for a fire in my grate \- but I soon had it \+ began to thaw \- we dressed in black suits \+ white linen \+ at about 8 o'clk presented ourselves at the door of the great dining Room \- we were politely told that we could get what we wanted in the Grill Room \+ a flunky showed us the way \- our dinner cost us 27 shillings \- the Grill is a long room in the basement \- rather stuffy \-
After dinner I went to my room \+ fire \+ the boys went out for a lark \- I found at the hotel letters from Mary, Katharine \+ Bassie \+ Hattie Elkin \- also telegram from Plymouth saying no sign of my camera there \- then I sent a telegram to Head Steward of S\. Lincoln at Hamberg \-
