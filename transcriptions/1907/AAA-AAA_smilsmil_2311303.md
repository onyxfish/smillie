## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 22, 1907

**Weather:** Dull + overcast till eveg.  **Temp:** [illegible]

where the weather turned clear, windy + cold. I got to studio between 10 + 11 o'clk + till 1 o'clk — I fooled away time trimming mounting + retouching photos. Then prepared lunch — After that worked on portrait of father till after 4 o'clk — Then went to Mr. Geo. Walton Green's Reception, 127 E. 39th St., cor. Lex. Ave. A number there — had a pleasant call — Meta Faint Adams was there — She is spending the winter with Mr. G — Her sister Julia came in before I left. Had quite a chat with Helen Baxter. Home at about 6 — After dinner, at 7.45 I went to Mendelssohn Hall — Annual Meeting of Am. Geo. Socy —, met Charles Theres — There was a lecture by Howard W. DuBois, an engineer, on the Grand Canyon of the Colorado with many illustrative lantern slides — The lecturer was not stimulating + the pictures were disappointing as a whole, though there were a few admirable exceptions — after 11 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 23, 1907

**Weather:** Brilliant day — genuine winter  **Temp:** Ther. 12° at noon — drizzly

Got to studio after 10.30 — I worked on father's portrait till nearly 3 o'clk — then had a very light lunch + soon after 4 o'clk. rode up to 75 E. 8. + called on St. Gerster + was fortunate to find him at home — We had a very pleasant chat for half an hour or more — He is to get the press I sent to him from the Mets last Oct. in readiness to set up, then he will inform me + I shall boss the job. From there I went to Mrs. Barhydts, 40 E. 70th St. + spent an agreeable half hour there chatting with a Mr. Van Winkle — Then across to Presbyt. Hosp. + saw Jessie Smillie for a few minutes — Then walked across Central Park at 72° d— clear, exceedingly cold moonlight night — the cold cutting wind in exposed places in the hurt my face + ears severely — To escape it I rode home from 72° St. + Broadway — home a little before 7 — dinner — Eveg. loafing —

Ther. fell to 6° at 10 p.m.
