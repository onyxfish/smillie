## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 25, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hardly a pleasant day - clouded \+ misty with now \+ then light flying showers \+ now \+ then bits of pale sunshine. I had my bath before 6 \+ went to bed again - b'kf't at 8. until later in the afternoon I was more or less uncomfortable owing to a dull headache but I managed to get rid of the day. Reading, sleeping, walking \+ writing part of a letter to Mary - After dinner a number of the young people got at the piano \+ made merry by a lot of incoherent singing of wide range, from Funiculare to Rock of Ages. More movement to-day - stiff breeze at times with some sea. There was a mild sort of rebellion in the steerage this p.m. against the quality of the food given them \+ great lots of macarroni, beans \+ bread were strewn maliciously over the deck. The fourth officer attempted to quiet them \+ there was some spirited discussion between him \+ the leader who was loudly cheered by his fellows.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 26, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Showery day - breezy - but not cold - a moderate sea along heaving motion in the ship with much creaking but no rolling - portions of the day were very fine \+ as usual, I spent much time on the boat deck. It was after 3 o'clk. p.m. when we sighted St. Miguel to the N.W. of us - Showers were passing over it \+ for a time it looked as tho' we w'd see but little of it, but the showers passed \+ we skirted along the southern shore so close that with our glasses we could easily see the houses, fences, roads \+ even people - Part of the time it was in bright sunshine altho' the tops of the highest mts. were cloud capped - We had an excellent view of Ponta Delgada, its buildings, the breakwater \+ the shipping behind it. The Cap't. had a map of the island displayed on a table where we could study the geography of the island - we cleared it after 6 - \+ that was all we saw of the Azores. Miss Hooper loaned me a book, Cap't. Eri, a Cape Cod story written by Joseph Lincoln - I found it very amusing \+ read till almost 11 - Then I went to the boat deck \+ was much impressed by a sad old moon, not far above the rolling waters, in a clouded sky.
