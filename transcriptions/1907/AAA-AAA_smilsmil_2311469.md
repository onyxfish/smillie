## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 24, 1907

**Weather:** Bright & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasant — with temp. I was pleased to find my temp. normal — but as I was assured that something inside of me was going wrong I telephoned to Dr. T— \+ he said he w'd come up in the course of the day — Temp. commenced going up again slowly \+ I was greatly discouraged. At about noon I went out \+ walked up \+ down on the sunny side of the Ave. for 20 minutes — I was unable to concentrate up on any thing — I could not read, nor could I write, so I just loafed about miserably — annisance to my self \+ every body else, I felt. The doctor came after it — he seemed unwilling to hazard any guesses — He made as careful examination of me — heart, lungs, stomach, liver \+ gall bladder \+ of my right kidney \+ of my bowels — \+ still he had nothing definite to prescribe or say — At his suggestion I sent out \+ got some Lacktose to make buttermilk — I had no dinner \+ a temp 101½°.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 25, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal Christmas day for me altho' it was bright \+ pleasant out of doors — when I got up after a few hours of restful sleep in the late m't till 8 — my temp only 2/5 above normal — so, I got up, dressed \+ had bkft', like any ordinary, normal morse altho feeling "queer" About 1 o'clk. I went out with Ralph \+ walked on the sunny side of West. End Ave. up to 94th St. \+ back — my temp. having gone up to 10 2/5° — At 2 o'clk we had dinner — a perfunctory performance for me — after that my temp. rose rapidly till 6 o'clk. when it stood at 104°. At 5 I had gone to bed — The doctor was called \+ he came at 7 — By his direction Kate gave my feet \+ legs a fine, hot medicated bath \+ a mild mustard plaster was put on my throat \+ chest — I took a dose of Acetanelide, Caffiene \+ Quinine \+ was soon much relieved — The night passed quietly but with little sleep till some time after midnight.
