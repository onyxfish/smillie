## Left Page

### Mon. March 25, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

last night at about 11 o'clk, the wind began to blow hard head on \+ a big sea soon rose \- we pitched heavily all night \- In the m'f the sky was clouded, the wind continued high \+ cold (46°.) \+ big seas came aboard running in cascades from forcastle to main deck \- it was quite exciting \- all were out in winter overcoats \+ wraps \- I had my bath at 6.30 \+ bkfst at 8 \- I tried to get some photo. snaps at the seas but do not think that I was successful in getting anything \- a considerable part of the m'f was given to packing my trunk \- I took another turn at it in the afternoon. finished it \+ then celebrated by taking a nap. The sun came out before noon \- the wind went down \- the sea followed suit \+ at night we were trudging along in the usual fashion \- a cloudless moonlight night, moderated sea \+ wind \- \+ so cool that a heavy overcoat \+ muffler were necessary to comfort \- I did considerable deck walking \-

## Right Page

### Tues. March 26, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 6 o'clk \- Raw \+ overcast \- Ther. down blow 40°. Everything damp \+ just positively wet \- Later in the day there was some bleak sunshine, but overcast again at night. Bkfs \- after 7 \- got up to quarantine after 8 \+ had some delay there till the Doctor came aboard \+ kindly passed us \- Then, up the bay \- customs officers coming aboard at Ellis' Island to get our declarations \- the ship landed us at the United Fruit Co's dock \- K9, I pier, by the Battery \- We got our luggage past the officers at 10.30 \- then R. T. \+ I separated \- he went home by Elevated R.R., I went by subway \- I got home at about 11 \+ found everything there looking beautifully clean \+ inviting \- I found much matter of all kinds piled up \+ awaiting my attention \- I had lunch at 12.30 \+ after that a nap \- then I resumed the work of going over papers \+ accts., unpacked my trunk \+ put away my things \- Jim came home to dinner but went out to a dress drill \- After 8 Clark came \+ we talked till near 11 \- then to bed \-
