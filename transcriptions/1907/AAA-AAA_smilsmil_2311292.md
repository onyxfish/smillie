## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 1, 1907

**Weather:** Cloudy m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'f — but cleared beautifully later — Bkf. alone at 9 o'clk — Later I went out for a walk — air mild — wind southerly — walks wet \+ unwilling to dry — I kept out for some time \+ in at about 11 — wrote part of a letter to Ernest Roth — At 2 o'clk Jim, Ralph \+ I had a big Turkey dinner after which I had a rest — Ralph seems to make slow progress with his mumps. he has kept in doors all day \+ makes no complaint of pain or discomfort, devotes himself to reading — I read Thr'd Jack London's "The Call of the Wild" — After 4 o'clk I went down to 174 E. 37th to call on Mary Cook she excused herself \+ I saw Tom — He told me that his father died without pain or struggle in his chair as he was preparing for bed — from there I went over to Walter's \+ saw him for a few minutes \+ from there to Charls' \+ was there till 6:15 then home \+ had tea with the boys — later Jim went out — I spent the ev'g reading —
