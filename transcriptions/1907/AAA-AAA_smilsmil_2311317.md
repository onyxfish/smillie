## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 19, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal day — Rain \+ sleet began to fall at about noon — Raw atmosphere. Ups soon after 7 — bkfs. at 8 — Got note from Bureau of Customs, M. M. Wagner. saying that plan agreed upon on Sat. could not be carried out owing to complicated requirements of Customs \+ postal laws — I wrote to Roth \+ to Wagner — I wrote Codicils 1 \+ 2 for my Will, bequests to K \+ B — Throop \+ to Kate Leonard — At 11 I went to studio saw George — signed leases with Ryan \+ then home at 1 — Lunch soon after — The afternoon was spent in preparations for southern trip — Dinner with Jim at 6.30 — much of the evening was spent in getting my painting "The Adirondacks" hung \+ from Roaring Brook — to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 20, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still dismal, but not wet to-day — Ups at 7.30 \+ had bkfs. soon after 8 — Rec'd. letter from Mr. McGuire — Director of the Corcoran Gallery of Art stating cordially that the Art Com. accepted "the Cliffs of Normandy" on my terms with thanks — the price is to be confidential \+ the picture is to go on record as purchased by the Trustees — I was busy at home till 11 — then to 2d. Nat'l. Had trouble in getting witnesses to the two codicils that I added to my Will yesterday — I thought a Notary wd. be sufficient — I had to get Mr. Hy. L. Smith \+ a young man, Mr. Johnston, from the bank — I got home at 1 — Lunch \+ busy again during the afternoon — Jim did not come home to dinner — Eve'g. Charl sent up a long letter just Rec'd. from Bessie — I got my things together to put in my trunk \+ to bed at 11.
