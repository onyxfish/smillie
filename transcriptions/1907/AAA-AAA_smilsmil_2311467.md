## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 20, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hardly a pleasant day — not much sunshine — none of it warm. I got up \+ had bkfs' at 8.30 — feeling pretty seedy. Ralph was up early \+ we chatted together for a good part of the m'f. I wrote some letters bearing on the Sniffan Court Garage business. Then an went out \+ had a walk on Riverside Drive. Cullen sent up the two photo albums with last summer's work for the boys — The work has been done with shameful carelessness \+ I am disgusted — I shall be ashamed to let such work go out or into their hands — I wrote to Cullen telling him that I thought I would not accept the work — Afternoon Ralph went off for a tramp — I did some more writing \+ had a nap. This P.M. Kate's niece was buried \+ Kate went over to the Cemetery — Mary Moody stayed to take her place — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef. the boys went out — I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 21, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day, cloudless, cold \+ rather too breezy for me — up early \+ had my bkfs'. with Jim. I had some letter writing to do — got a letter started to Bassie — \+ about 11 went out \+ had a two mile walk on Broadway up to 101' S'. \+ back — feeling fairly well — but digestive conditions are still far from good. Ralph got up late \+ went down town, met Jim, had lunch with him \+ the two were together till they came home to dinner.

I spent the afternoon taking another walk on Riverside Drive — a nap — reading — \+ some more writing. Dinner at 6.30 — Evening the boys went out — Charles came in \+ stayed till near 11 — we talked of "Teddy" — of the "Mule" — water-ways \+ the demoralization of business \+ finances.
