## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 28, 1907

James Shearman \+ wife called -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Again a fine day - warm, but not too warm for me - It was hot in the sunshine, but in my room, sunshine pouring in all the afternoon the Ther. did not get above 72°. After bkft. the boys went to Lucerne by themselves to have a spree while I remained to receive Jim Shearman \+ wife who had Teleg'd. that they w'd. arrive at 2 - I spent the m't climbing in the park back of the hotel, getting some good snapshots \+ on returning to the hotel I wrote for some time on a letter to E. N. S - commenced at Traföi - Lunch - Promptly at 2 Mr. \+ Mrs. S - am'd - the greeting was warm \+ till 4.35 we reminisced \+ swapped common place ideas with enthusiasm - It was exceedingly pleasant 2½ hours for all of us I am sure - After they left I had a rest - The boys ret'd. at 7 - Dinner - Eve'g. There was an entertainment Cinematograph \+ sleight of hand in the large hall - some of the cinne'. pictures were amusing in a mild way.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 29, 1907

The Burgenstock

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Altho' the sky was not propitious we started for the Rigi Kulm on the 9.55 car down - but when the bottom (Brunnen) was reached we gave it up \+ went to the Waldstetter hof instead - There we saw the Telkins \+ when the 10.55 boat was in sight we agreed that a trip to the Burgenstock would be as good as we could do, weather conditions considered - We went by the boat to Viznau - changed to a smaller boat \+ then to Kehrs iten - at the landing we took the funicular car that carried us up to the top of the Burgenstock ridge - here there are two or three or more large hotels - Rain was beginning to fall - we went into the Restaurant \+ had a satisfactory meal - that over, the rain ceased \+ we walked out on the path along the face of the cliff - one of the most remarkable that I ever was in - it is about four feet wide \+ is a dug way - that is in parts it is along one of the steepest inclines that it is possible for trees to grow upon - at these parts it was beautiful \+ surprising - at other parts the path was cut in the cliff face \+ the look down to the water 2000 ft. below was dizzying - I never more fully realized the meaning of a bird's eye view - at the end of this walk, about 20 minutes from hotels, there is an elevator that goes up the face of the cliff to the top, between 2\+300 ft. higher - we got very near to this elevator but the threats of rain were so imminent that we turned back \+ got to the hotel just as it came on again - A car was just going down \+ we went with it - The cliff walk was a sensation an iron rail runs at the edge of it - otherwise parts of it could not be safe to walk upon - Before the Return boat came the Rain had passed - the sun shone out \+ we had a pleasant sail back - altho' rather cool - there were some fine cloud effects over the Burgenstock, seen from Viznau - From Brunnen we barely caught a car up to Axenstein \+ got to hotel at about 7 - dinner at 7.30 - newspapers in the eve'g. \+ to bed early -
