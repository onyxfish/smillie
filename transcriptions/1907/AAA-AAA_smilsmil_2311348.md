## Left Page

### Mon. April 22, 1907

J. 2. A. Ward \+ dinner at Nat. Arts Club.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright \+ pleasant day — less cold \+ windy than yesterday — Up before 8 — At 10 took my watch to Bayerdorfers to be cleaned — then to studio, mounted ten 4×5 photos. that I have printed for W. Wyckoff of Holyoke \+ thus till about 1 o'clk. worked over the Hinshelwood pf that I am to send to the Boston Mus. of art. A messenger from the Pub. Library Print Dept. took away that Dept's share of them — pfe. \+ I went home to lunch at 2 — Spent the afternoon at home — At 6 I dressed \+ soon after 7 was at the Nat. Arts Club — Gramercy Park. Dinner to J. Q. A. Ward \+ in honor of Am. Sculptors — 141 guests, male \+ female. — Before dinner Ward introduced me to his wife, at table I found my seat was next to her \+ in a choice position in front of the Raised Table where her husband \+ the speakers sat Mr. W. is a stout, amiable looking woman but I did not find her interesting altho' I made tentative efforts in different directions — Dan. C. French sat also with his friend Miss Longman, a sculptress whose work I do not know — on my left sat Mr. Richard H. Hunt, son of Rich'd. H. — ; who was agreeably chatty. Beyond him sat S. C. Stedman — I was in very good company. The dinner was good, as such dinners go \+ there were 141 diners — it was rather late before speaking began. S. Karucci Trask, Pres. of the Club made a short address of greeting \+ then appointed Lorado Taft as toast master. He made a good address, lauding Ward without stint — Ward replied, the bulk of his address being written — done bits of characteristic humor \+ some common penn modestly delivered — C. Howard Walker, a Boston architect spoke well concerning Sculpture \+ Architecture — Then \+ Hutchinson Smith got on his hind legs \+ made a rattling speech — lest, Sculpture \+ Painting — he abused buyers for their neglect of Home Art. Rev. Jonest Elias followed — Sculpture \+ Worship — then A. M. Shepard as finished, lawyer-like address — Sculptees, Citizens hep-then, after midnight, Chas. R. Lamb — Sculpture \+ the Sculptor a gush of adulation of Ward — To Bed after 1 o'clk

## Right Page

### Tues. April 23, 1907

Walter Cook Junr — married

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, damp \+ raw with a wet mizzle for an hour or so from 4 to 5 p.m. — I got up before 9 o'clk. feeling some effects from my dissipation last night — I did not go to studio but wrote to W. Wyckoff of Holyoke \+ sent him a lot of solio prints from photos taken on my last trip. Kate Leonard went to Springfield for a visit of a couple of days — I had lunch at 1 then went to Grand Central Station \+ took 2.15 train to Pelham — a great crowd of the Cook Relatives, connections \+ friends went on the train to P — \+ there we took carriages, busses, etc \+ were taken a quite a long drive to the little church at Pelham Manor — There, after a moderate wait the ceremony of marriage was witnessed by a gay crowd that filled the church — Margant Carmichael Roper \+ Walter Cook Junr. were made man \+ wife — then by conveyance we all got to the Roper Residence, a small house that was jammed full completely — The Receptionwas soon got thro' with — \+ then a look at the display of presents of silver, glass, etc — etc — these I hung about for some time \+ at last, started to walk to the station \+ was caught in the mizzle \+ got quite damp before a Trolley car came along \+ I rode in that — a crowd returned to town on 5.35 train \+ we got in at 6.15 — I was a few minutes late to dinner — Jim went out \+ I loafed till bed time —
