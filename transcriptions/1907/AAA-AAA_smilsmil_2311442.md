## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 31, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day. To studio at 10 — woman there scrubbing — I then went to Macy's. Bdway \+ 35 St. Taking broken cut-glass fruit dish \+ left it to be mended — then back to studio \+ was there till 3 o'clk — Jennings was there for a time cleaning windows \+ scrubbing sidewalk. — Then I left the woman still at work \+ went home \+ had a rest — At 6.30 I went to Chas's to dinner \+ spent the eve'g. after it in looking over prints from photos. that he took in Germany last Summer. He has some very beautiful ones — Home at 10.30 \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 1, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — Dep. before 8 — got to studio at 10 \+ found the cleaning woman, Mr. Bassac. there — I worked with her till almost 2 — then I left her, went home \+ had a late lunch — then worked with photo. prints, trimming them — Ralph came in from New Haven at about 4 \+ for an hour I showed him the various prints I have of last summers work. Then I had a rest for an hour. Then dinner at 6.30 \+ after that, with Jim \+ Ralph, I went to the Garden Theatre \+ saw the Opera of Madam Butterfly — written by Giacomo Puccini — given by "English Grand Opera Co." A Jap. story — a young Lieut. U.S.N. takes a Jap. girl for his concubine — but she takes him seriously, loves \+ trusts him \+ when he leaves her, with promise to return she is faithful to him \+ waits confidingly — years pass — his child by her grows to be 3 or 4 yrs. old — when the Lieut returns with his wife — then wed. Butterfly is broken hearted \+ commits suicide \+ the little one is carried away to be cared for by the wife \+ the faithless Lieut. The house was crowded. The part of Mad. B— was taken by Miss Wolff — the Lieut by Stiles — The singing was good but unfortunately for me — I do not comprehend the musical language of emotion \+ consequently much of it failed to reach me \+ it seemed to be unnecessarily long drawn out — a bit tiresome, in fact. If I could have followed the text closely it might have been more interesting. The stage setting for the first Act was very pretty \+ the changing effects of light, good. It was 11 o'clk when we got out \+ home \+ to bed at 12.
