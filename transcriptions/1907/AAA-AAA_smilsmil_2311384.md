## Left Page

### Fri. July 5, 1907

**Weather:** fair + windy, some light showers of coarse  **Temp:** [illegible]

A late bkfst, + after 10 o'clk we started out, the boys going their way + I going mine. I went to Charing Cross Underground + thence to S. Kensing­ton + then to the Museum — the splendid new bldg, not yet occupied, completely disguised the Rambling buildings that I Remember.
I merely walked thro' a number of the many galleries + renewed my acquaintance with some old friends — + at about noon ret'd. to R.R. Station + went to back to Victoria Station — there I took Trolly car to Vauxhall bridge + then walked a short distance to the Tate Gallery. A fine building, facing the River, in a very shabby neighborhood — The collection of pictures is a noble one + I could not but wonder if this represents English Art, why are current as for so very poor + uninteresting — The Millais pictures interested me very much in the story they told of his evolution — just as I have been interested in the evolution of Turner, Constable + others — The Watts paintings are still away beyond my comprehension — The galleries are extensive + the pictures, compared to space, are not many — the walls are dull red — imitating grained leather — the problem of eliminating direct sunlight has not been solved completely — I had tea + rolls in the basement. I got back to hotel after 4 o'clk — Bought tickets for to-night at His Majesty's Theatre — Then had a rest — The boys came in after 6 — we dined at the Criterion. To Theatre at 8 — The last night of the Season — Bill, a hodgepodge résumé of the Seasons work — The Winter's Tale. Act 2. Scene 3. I have delightful memories of Mary Anderson in this scene — Miss Viola Tree was very charming as Perdita — but not Equal to M.A. The first Act of a Woman of no importance followed — I wish we could have seen the whole play — Tree as Lord Illingworth — Not impressive — Then Act II Scenes 2 & 3. Antony + Cleopatra — Tree as Mark Antony — Constance Collier as Cleopatra — neither of them moved me — too much rant — A funny one act play The Van Dyck — W.S. Penley Plat followed — Tree + Weldon Grossmith — Then Col. Newcomer — Tree as the Col. — Constance Collier as Ethel — Miss Frances Ivor as Mad. de Florac interested me more than all the others for naturalness + sweet simplicity.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 6, 1907

**Weather:** Considerable sunshine to-day. In fact, it was quite pleasant except for two or three gusty dashes of rain.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Are had bkfst. about 10 + then started out — walked down to St. James Park + thro' it to Buckingham Palace where we saw Guard mount + listened to the band play — then on to Hyde Park gate — in + walked half the length of Rotten Row — a gay turn out of horsemen, horsewomen + idlers — The idlers were a very showy lot + almost as handsome as the beds of flowering Rhododendron that formed a background — Then we walked over to the parade + for a time watched the inspection of a great lot of Volunteers — seemed to me there were several kept — Then we walked across to bridge over the Serpentine to the Albert Memorial — we were driven from here by a momentary gust of wind + rain + went to the Albert Hall opposite + went thro' it — then by bus — we rode up Piccadilly to Haymarket, bright sunshine — a fine sight — got off + had lunch at the Trocadero — After that the boys went to Houses of Parliament + I went to Cook's + got tickets to Edinburgh's oration at a cost of $83.50 a price which staggered me — Then 3 went to the Nat'l Gallery + wandered there till 4 o'clk — then to Hotel + Rested till 6 — the boys came in later — they had tickets to the Savoy — Prince + Peri — by Gilbert + Sullivan — We went to The Monaco + had a short dinner — then by cab to Theatre. Iolanthe is a very characteristic work of those masters G.+S. A new to each of us — We were greatly pleased — Ethel Morrison was queen of Fairies — Jessie Rose was Strephon's mother + Clara Dow was Phyllis — C.H. Workman was the Lord Chancellor + H.A. Lytton was Strephon — All of these parts were well taken, but the Lord High Chancellor carried off the honors, he is a good actor, a good dancer + a good singer — He sang the patter songs in a remarkable manner with clear enunciation — his dance with the two peers in Parliament Court was exceedingly clever + funny — We were out soon after 11 — to Hotel by Cab. + to bed at 11.45
