## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 21, 1907

S.S. Prinz Eitel Friedrich - State Room 22.
Capt. Gerdes -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ bitterly cold - strong n.w. wind - Up at 6.30 \+ had bkfs. with Jim before 8 - Then to work packing which I got through with at 10.30 - The Hack came at 12.45 \+ I got to the steamer at 1.30 \+ at the gang-plank that the sailing time had been changed from 2 clk. to 7 o clk. To-morrow m'g. That was a shock to me - Geo. \+ Charles came to see me off. Ch. soon went back to his office after arranging that I dine with him - D. Tuttle came \+ he. Geo. \+ I left the steamer - Geo. \+ I went to studio - I worked awhile on Father's portrait but still feeling chilly \+ demoralized - I went home, got warm there \+ then had a good nap \+ at 6.30 went to Charles \+ at 7 had a good dinner - At 9.30 he called a cab \+ took me in \+ back to the steamer. a very thoughtful \+ pleasant way of overcoming the difficulty of reaching the pier foot of 25th S't. Great confusion \+ noise that continued till after midnight as the engines hauled freight aboard \+ dropped it into the hold - Dr. T. \+ S turned in at 10.30 - I lay warm in my berth but could not sleep on acct. of noise -

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 22, 1907

34 miles run at 12 m.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At 7.15 am. we slipped away from our pier \+ were at last off on our Southern Trip - There were clouds \+ there was brilliant sunshine \+ a biting wind. I had a walk on deck before bkfs. at 8 - Then I wrote two short notes to Geo. \+ Chas. (Jim is in New-Haven) \+ sent them ashore, with the pilot - The rest of the m't. was spent loafing about the deck \+ putting things in order in our state Room - lunch at 1 - most of the afternoon I slept - Dinner at 6 - after that till 10.30 I slept again soundly. Then D. T. came in \+ I turned in for the night \+ slept some more - In that way I'm doing noble - smooth sea \+ very little motion in the boat.
