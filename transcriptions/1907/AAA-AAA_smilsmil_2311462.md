## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 10, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

I was up for a little while in the m't \- got note from Bureau of Combustibles saying that action was commenced ag't. Automobile school \+ garage in Sniffen court \+ asking if I w'd. give testimony if the case got into Court \- I telepht. to Clarence Smith also to the doctor for some instructions \- Then back to bed again \- I don't know what is coming \- Maybe grip \- My throat was very sore last night \+ I had fits of sneezing \- Took some quinine \- but whatever is coming did not give pause \- At 5 am. my temp. was 1° above normal \- at 5 p.m. it was 3° \- or 101½° \- Before I went into bed for the night Katie bathed my feet in hot water, soda \+ mustard \-

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 11, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very little fever in the m't but a violent headache with great sensitiveness to light \+ pain in the eyes \- I kept my bed \+ sent for D'. Tuttle \- he came in the afternoon as the headache was passing away \- thought it might be an indigestion \- was comparatively comfortable as I lay in my bed at night \+ hoped for better things on the morrow.
