## Left Page

### Wed. June 19, 1907

**Weather:** Very warm \- pleasanter than yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

a cool breeze \- Blft\. at 8 \- after writing letters Ralph \+ I went to Brooks Bro's \- got my bill \- to Stern's \+ bought an umbrella that folds up so that it will go into my suit case \- then to 2? Nat\. Bank \- drew $100 \- then to Cook's \+ got English money \- then to Rogers, Peet \- \+ got bill \- then to Meyrowitz \+ got glasses then home at 2 \+ had lunch \- After that R\. \+ I went out \- I took camera \+ made 3 exposures \- then took the roll to a place on Bdy\. near 80\. S\. \- left it to be developed then home \+ rested \- Ralph left after 5 to join Jim \+ go to Coney Island \- I had a walk before dinner \+ Dr\. Tuttle phoned me that he was called out of town \- so I had to eat dinner alone \- Eve'g \- I fussed about doing lots of little things.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 20, 1907

**Weather:** Fine day \- early Blft\.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Then engaged in general work, one item being the rolling up of the big rugs, after Kate had cleaned them, \+ covering them with for paper \- Then I went to Wanamaker's to see why the plated ware I bought on Tues. had not been delivered \- got bill \- ware promised this p.m. Then to studio \+ saw Maynard \+ Geo. The latter in good humor having rec'd\. word that the Met. Mus. of Art had accepted, as a gift, his large painting, "Evening" presented to the Mus. by Chas\. Then to Trees \+ got some drugs \- then to Meyrowitz for spectacles, but they were not ready \- then to Dr. Tuttle's \+ had a half hour talk with him about certain drugs \+ possibilities \- then home \+ lunch at 2.30 — then a rest \- more work \- at 6.30, with R \- went to Chas\.' \- Jim followed soon after \- We had a bang up dinner \- just before 9 I left them \+ went down to Emma Dryland's \+ spent the rest of the eve'g with her \- left at 10.30 \+ found it raining \- did some sprinting \+ got home at 11 but little wet. The boys came in a little later.
