## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 10, 1907

**Weather:** Dark, wet day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark, wet day \- bkfp. after 9 o'clk. Edna Murphy called me up on phone \+ asked Jim \+ me to come there to dinner to-day at 1.30 \- The rest of the m'g was given to Tribune \+ hanging pictures \- Jim \+ I went to 154 E. 37'St. \+ had a very pleasant time \- Mary was in excellent spirits \- besides Edna \+ Henry \+ Louise there was Alice Murphy, sister of Henry \+ Willie Cook, son of Sue. After dinner the young people went up stairs \+ Mary \+ I had a long confab by the parlor fire \- I left soon after 4 \+ went home \- more picture hanging \- a cup of tea at 6.30 Eve'g \- sent check for $12. to Civic Forum for two seats to the course of ten lectures \-

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 11, 1907

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- early bkfp. After that went to the Globe-Wernicke Co. \+ ordered one of their book-case "units" \- so that I may re-arrange those that I already have into a more satisfactory combination \- Then to Second Nat'l. Bk. Then walked to studio \- worked there till after 1 o'clk \- have now got the place almost cleared up \+ ready for me to begin work in it \- Then home \+ had a late lunch \- Then, feeling tired I indulged in the luxury of an afternoon nap. Rec'd a very kind note from Katie Elkins asking me to spend Sunday with them after the Yale-Princeton game on Saturday, to which I returned that I hoped to spend Sunday with her \+ William but had my fears about exposure at the game \- D'inner Eve'g \- arranging papers from last summer's trip.
