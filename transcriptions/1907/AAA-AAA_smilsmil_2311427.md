## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 29, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day, clear with a fresh Tang in the air that was refreshing after the warm weather of the past few days. I put on a heavier suit but found that the weather was hardly cool enough for that. I was very weak after yesterdays experience \+ slept both morning \+ afternoon. \+ was for a long time on the boat deck \- gradually improving in tone

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 30, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An interesting day \- Rain, with some breaks of sunshine \- high wind \- head seas \- warm atmosphere surcharged with humidity \- The boat did not roll but creaked \+ strained with elastic shiverings \- She pitched tremendously sometimes \- with driving wind \+ rain \+ humidity there was not a dry place on the boat \- Ropes for hand lines were stretched up \+ down the upper promenade deck \+ after wind \+ sea had increased we were not allowed to go on the boat deck. In the n't, while up there on the platform at foot of forward funnel a sea was shipped that ducked me so that I had to retreat to my cabin \+ make a change of clothes \- In the afternoon I was at the extreme stern watching the ups \+ downs \+ got another \+ more serious ducking from a sea that came over the stern while she was down \- again I had to make a change \+ as that left but one more possible change to my dress suit I was careful about further exposures. Ralph was almost drowned in a sea that caught him on the forward part of Saloon deck. Was in his best suit \+ rain coat \+ was thoroughly soaked. I arranged with deck steward \+ had our wet things sent to the laundry drying room. A good part of the crest of a wave came in one of the dining room windows \+ went almost half way across the room \- There were few at the tables.
