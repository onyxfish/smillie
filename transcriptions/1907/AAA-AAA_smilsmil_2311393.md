## Left Page

### Tues. July 23, 1907

Wiesbaden. Nassauer Hof - Room 425 -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day - Raining most of the time. In spite of the weather + my condition I decided that it would be best to go on. My throat trouble is much aggravated, palate elongated + great hoarsness - We got away from Coblenz on one of the large boats (Barbarossa) at 10°. Could see but a short distance up or down the river but had a fair view of the country each side as we passed along - our map was so complete that we could determine our exact location without trouble - Every league was marked on each River bank in large figures - + they were all on on the map - The picturesque interest of the Rhine lies between St. Goar + Bingen - + much of that distance we were in the dining saloon at our church - My recollections of the Rhine as I saw it in 1862 I find are very correct - there have been great changes of course - There were no Rail Roads on the banks in '62 - the vineyards have been greatly extended + also manufacturing interests - + the towns have spread out. The boats are very much larger + have more comforts. Saloon on main deck - Upper deck + a saloon below main deck. meals can be served at small Tables on each deck - meals from 11 to 2 - owing to weather, served in saloon main deck. We got our Table No. fort o'clk. + then took seats where we found our No. The meal was a good one for 3 M. for some time I was in lower saloon seeking a retreat from the weather - We got to Bieberich at 5.30 + hired a conveyance that carried us to our carriage to the Nassauer Hof at Wiesbaden - all the way up - uninteresting - 7 M. - were shown at once to our pleasant Rooms on 4th floor, overlooking the park - We had "Supper" at 7 - Then I went to my Room + Remained there while the boys went off for a prowl - I enjoyed a couch till near 10 - then to bed - a very good one.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 24, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day, somewhat cloudy in the m. but clearing to a cloudless p.m. + full moon night. Temp. about 68°. I got up very hoarse + throat much inflamed + troublesome - otherwise I am very well - Got the boys up after 8 + had bkfs. after 9 - Then we went out + wandered about - went to Kurhaus + paid 1 m. each for the privileges for one day - it is a very large building of great apartments - large Central hall + dome - a great Concert room on one side, a smaller one on the other + fifteen suites of reading + lounging Rooms on one side + Restaurant rooms on the other - furnished + furnished in lavish manner but not according to standards of taste with which I am familiar - I should say it was of the modern German decadent style - grotesque to my eyes in many ways - The gardens are lovely - There was an organ concert in the at it but we did not wait for that but went to the hot spring that I remember - everything is changed there, new building have been erected over + about it - drank a glass of the very unsmiling looking water - Then about the town - I made one or two snapshots but did not find anything picturesque. At the hotel + had dinner at 1 o'clk - then the boys went off + I went to my Room + had a nap - At 4 o'clk - went to Kurhaus garden with the boys + heard a concert by a military band - a great number of people there - After the concert more wandering about, the warm sunshine very delightful - At 7.30 we had Supper + then again to Kurhaus garden - another concert + more people - a great throng - I am surprised to see so little display of beauty of fashion - on the whole the people are very ordinary looking in dress + behavior - we see many Rheumatics coming - no doubt here for treatment - the scene was lively in some ways picturesque - The beautiful garden - the wonderful Elms, the great trees, the Lake, the throngs of people, the electric lights, the moon, the music - all went to make pleasure - we loitered about till 10 o'clk - Then I left the boys, Ret'd to hotel + to bed
