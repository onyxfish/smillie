## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 17, 1907

*Naples, Bertolini — Room 104.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day, having some thunder showers that did not interfere with us — Up soon after 6. bkf. after 8 — then sauntered about hotel grounds \+ outside — very little could be seen of Sorrento in the short time we had — The Steamboat Regina Elena came soon after 10 \+ after a short delay in shipping the passengers that were in small, hotel boats lying waiting, started for Capri — soon after a heavy shower, very local, developed over Capri \+ we had fears of a showery day — it passed like a vail before us \+ was soon followed by another the skirts of which just touched us — that too passed \+ at about 11 we reached the Marina — took on more passengers then into the cave, getting there in about 15 min. A fleet of 30 small boats waiting for us the boys \+ got into the first boat liked the procession the effect in the cave was pretty, as usual, when the whole 30 boats got in it was crowded \+ a babel — also our show — As soon as the last one got in we got out — an odd scene outside when the boats huddled about one boat where a guard took toll — 1.25 fr. from each pass. each boatman then demanded his "pourboire" — or "maccoroni" as they say in Italy — Then onto the Marina, getting there at noon. Got into a vehicle at once \+ rode up to the Piazza of the town of Capri — A funiculare from the Marina to the piazza is almost completed — we walked to the Quisisana Hotel \+ had lunch there — There have been considerable changes there since our visit 15 years ago — The dining room is larger, handsome \+ very new — After lunch we learned on enquiry that the Rock of Tiberious is too far away for a walk inside of two hours so we gave that up. reluctantly — nothing else that we knew of to take its place so we loafed about town for a while — finding nothing very entertaining we rode back to the Marina at a break neck pace — The Italian drivers seem to be a reckless lot — at the Marina we found only regular boats — So, we went on the boat nearly an hour before its time of sailing — it got away at 4 — then to Sorrento — then to Naples, getting there a little after 6 — There is no dock for the steamer — were put ashore in small boats — cab to hotel — getting there at about 7 — found letters waiting — Chase \+ attires — were given new rooms on floor below our other ones — dinner \+ then loafing on terrace — lovely moonlight — clouds all gone.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 18, 1907

*Vesuvius.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day for our excursion altho' clouded — it was quiet \+ comparatively cool — Up early as usual, about 6. we left the hotel at 8.30 \+ went to Cook's \+ bought tickets for ascent of Vesuvius — the 9 o'clk. Party was complete — we had to wait till 9.40 — then by cab we were sent to station of ferrovia circumvesuviana — had to wait till 10.30 — Train out to Pugliano after much deliberation at 11.0 then we got into a rickety old wagon — at Cook's called a "brake" \+ rode to start of Cook's Vesuvius R. R. Left at 11.50 — Electric — in 10 m. we reached the section of Rack \+ Pinion — grade at times 1/25. We got to the end of the Road at 12.30 — 2 hours from Naples going not more than 20 miles — Here we started to climb the cone — we had passed the last trace of vegetation \+ were in an indescribable waste of lava \+ ashes — there were 7 in our party — one woman — all started on foot but me — I strode a miserable looking grey horse — we were about 7 1/4 hour in getting to the Crater — at first the grade was gentle as we skirted the foot of the cone going eastward to Monte Sonia \+ along that valley when the trail turned directly back \+ in one great sweep up circled around the cone, to west \+ south — it tried my nerves — I do not have felt it on my own feet, but lifted up on the horse, on a trail dug into the side of a 45° angle incline, composed of loose lava gravel \+ ashes, gullied by rains \+ in places broken away or not more than 18" wide. I could not, as I looked down the constantly increasing depth \+ heard the stones rattling down behind me keep thinking of possible results should the beast make a misstep — The ascent was steep — 15 to 20° perhaps seemed endless — over the ocean there was a beautiful play of color, the air was very still \+ the surface reflected the lights \+ shadows of the clouds above in turquoise \+ opal tints — in its wide spread its effect was aerial — islands \+ promontories seemed to float in it — at last the cone top was fairly reached \+ at 1.30 we were on the edge of the crater. The ashes and were loose \+ deep in places — one boy, one of the party picked out a bit of lava so hot that it scorched his hand. It was such hard work for me that two stout dacoes hoisted me up on their shoulders \+ carried me to within a few yds. of the crater edge — we got up there at about 1.45 — the shadow of a cloud over the crater spoiled the possibility of getting a good photo. but we could see clearly down into the throat of Hell — the opposite wall was almost perpendicular \+ little puffs of steam were issuing from many fissures on the side. we could see down for hundreds of feet, but not to the bottom — that was hidden by some projections below us — it was a fearful spectacle. We left the top soon after 2 — got to end of R.R. in about half an hour — \+ in 20 m. to Gomino, the station of funiculare for lunch — Here we stopped for an hour \+ had lunch — at 3.30 we started down — got to Pagliona at 4. had to wait there 35 m. \+ at last got to Naples about 6 — cab to hotel — Rested — dinner 7.45 — Eve. on Promenade deck enjoying wonderful moonlight effects over bay city \+ mountains.
