## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 11, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another fine day - Thunder storms hovered so near to us, on the north, in the afternoon that it seemed sure that we w'd get a shower - but we didn't. The Porter tells me that there has been no Rain here for three months - \+ every dust covered thing bears out his statement - We drove to the Castello Sant' Anelo getting there just at opening time - When I was here 15 y'rs ago it was a barracks \+ not open to the public - now it is freely restored \+ we can go from bottom to top - exceedingly interesting in every way - one cannot help moralizing on the vanity of human pride \+ ambition when going over this desecrated monument - We were shown about by an intelligent guide - saw Papal State apartments, cellars, \+ dungeons with dreadful stories - then to the Vatican coll. of Antique sculpture - there we spent the Rest of the m'g. \+ back to hotel for lunch - at 2.30 we started out with our driver to make a round - Went first to the S. M. dei Cappuccini \+ went thro' the Crypt \+ saw the Remarkable examples of decoration in human bone work - Then to the Carcer Mamertinus - a most ancient place a subterranean hole where Tradition says St. Peter was confined \+ caused a spring of water to rise in the rock floor so that he might baptize his jailers - this is now nicely cleaned up, illuminated \+ lighted by electricity - Then to S. M. in Cosmedin - \+ saw the Bocca della Verita - now supposed to be an aesthetic cover to a sewer manhole - then to the little circular temple across the piazza - then to a place where we were shown an opening into the Cloaca Maxima - \+ then a four faced arch. Janus Quadrifons. Then a long drive down along side the Tiber to the Porta S. Paolo - \+ then a long way further along a drive way shaded by trees to S. Paolo fuori le Mura - The boys thought it would make a good skating Rink - then I met the Two girls I met on the Roof of the Cathedral at Milan \+ I chaffed with them for some time - then back to the city \+ went to the Pantheon \+ from there to the hotel - after a short rest, with the boys, went up the Spanish steps \+ out to the terrace on Pincian hill \+ saw the sun set - then back to hotel - dinner at 7.30 - Evening wrote to Mary in Public Room while a party of young girls, American, amused themselves at the Piano - one of them played exceedingly well - They also danced for a little exercise after their days work.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 12, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still fine weather - up as usual before 7 - I was after 9 when we got started on our sight seeing work - Drove to the Doria Palace - closed - wrong day - Then to Colonna - closed - too early - Then to Capitoline Museum - went thro' in something of a hurry - Then across the Piazza to Palace of Conservatori - in the Sale dei Conservatori a bunch of marriages seemed to be running thro' a legal mill - The coll. of Statuary \+ paintings in this Palace staggered us but we struggled thro' it all - then to the Tabularium \+ climbed to the top of the tower - then down \+ our chariot carried us to the Colonna Palace, now open - a short time here - Then to the Quirinale where we got permission to visit the state rooms in the palace this p.m. then to Baths of Diocletian - the Nat'l Roman Museum - Here we saw an endless list of statues, old frescos, etc. etc. spread out a great length - when 12.30 came we drove back to hotel - lunch - a short Rest \+ soon after 2 we went to Cooke's - too early - off at 3 - then to Quirinale Palace \+ were escorted thro' many grand festal apts. en suite about an inner court - The effect was rather pleasant - some of the saloons were imposing \+ we left agreeably impressed - then again to Cooke's \+ got some money - Then to the Palatine hill, dismissed cabby \+ devoted ourselves to the work of solving the labyrinthine puzzle of Palaces of Tiberius, Caligula \+ those other old fellows - In this we were greatly aided by a wheezing old fellow who found great amusement in describing the vaults \+ excavations as the Palaces of Caracalla - etc - He had but a few words of English but a large vocabulary in Pantomime - He made us understand that the palaces were not holes in the ground occupied by soldiers \+ slaves but structures high above \+ now destroyed - He was so familiar with Baedeker that he could turn at once to the page \+ put his finger on each description, so keeping us constantly informed - We went conscientiously over the whole field - The light was beautiful \+ the elevation sufficient to show the relations of the most interesting points in Rome. R. had his camera \+ took some snap shots - I cannot forgive myself for leaving my camera at the hotel - By 5 o'clk - I was tired \+ we Ret- to the hotel - I had a Rest before dinner - Eve'g wrote a note to Vedder - am disappointed that I have not been able to call on him
