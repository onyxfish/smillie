## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 6, 1907

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast all day & quite mild but with a raw feeling in the air - Up at 7.30 to see how Ralph was - his fever was less & he said he had passed a fairly comfortable night - I had my bath at 8.30 & at 9.30 called Dr. Tuttle & asked him to come up & see R - Bkpt. before 10 - T. came at 11.30 & gave R. a careful looking over & said that he found nothing of serious import - Jino Ralph & I had dinner at 2 - After that R. & I went out for a short walk - No, our walk was before our dinner — between 3 & 4 o'clk - I had a nap - Tea at 6.30. Eve'g R. & I continued our enlarging & took half a dozen prints 8×10 of my head & hair, three of them pretty good - After 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 7, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 60° at 2.30

A mild spring-like day - Clouded p.m. - the sunny one & made walking under an overcoat oppressively warm - Bkft. before 8.30. R. - & temp. not yet normal - 99½° - generally he seems better - After 10 I went to bank & left book to be balanced - Then to Electricians on Fourth Ave. & bought some fixtures - Then to studio - had to waste some time over the electric bells that would'nt ring - then fussed with my photo enlargements till after 1 o'clk an inexcusable waste of time - Then home - lunch at 2 — after that, more time given to changes of electric fixtures - Then a Rec'd. note from Katie Elkin saying that Mrs. John Downs died Sat. m'g at 7 o'clk - Dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g I wrote a short letter to Mary who is now in Rome.
