## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 7, 1907

**Weather:** clear, cold \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day starting at 19° on at getting more than 3 or 4° higher during the day — after 10 I started for down Town — went to Ham. Am. office \+ learned that the sailing day of S. Eitel Friedrich had been agains postponed — now it is to Feb. 21st — I then went to Office of the Royal Mail line — 22 State S. \+ learned that S.S. Trent w. sail for Jamaica on the 16" — got a boat plan \+ the refusal of a state room till to-morrow noon — then to 78 Broad, Colombian Consul — not in — then to Dr. Tuttle's — found him in — he does not approve of sailing on the Trent — so, we agreed to hold our passage on the S. Eitel Friedrich to sail on 21st. Home after 2 o'clk. \+ had lunch — Then wrote to Royal mail giving up reserved state room — \+ to Ham. Am. — Dinner at 6.30 — Sue \+ Jim went to drill \+ then to a dance — he came in at about 2 a.m. with Gould who stayed overnight with him. — I worked till 11 printing from Ark negatives to send to Prof. Marquand.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 8, 1907

**Weather:** overcast m.f., but by 10 o'clk. clearing to be a cloudless \+ very pleasant winter day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I had my help. Soon after 8 — then for sometime with newspapers — Between 11 \+ 1 o'clk. I walked for more than an hour thro' sunny streets — 79th St. to Manhattan Sq. then to Central Park W. \+ up to 91st St. There was some thawing but generally, the walk was very pleasant — Lunch at 1.30 then I mounted some of the prints I made last night — made a [illegible] of half a dozen views in \+ about the Ark \+ took them to P.O. station Columbus Ave. \+ 84th St. \+ mailed them to Prof. Marquand, Princeton, N.J. Dinner at 6.30 with Jim who spent the eve\[ning\] at home — I wrote to Prof. M — \+ to Ralph —
