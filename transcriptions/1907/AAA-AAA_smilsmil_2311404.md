## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 14, 1907

Trafoi Hotel - Rooms 6·7·
5080' \-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudless day - Up soon after 6 - boys up + had bkfs. at 7.30 - at 8 we got away in good style - The air was cool, almost sharp - The Road continued along the cliff overhanging the River + was intensely interesting - after a time we left the River but + entered a small lateral valley. A fine waterfall with stone bed. suddenly came in view + I asked our driver to stop that I might get a snap at it - he laughed + declined saying that the [illegible] was for Nauders + I caught photos of it - camera w. be confiscated + I might be imprisoned at Aleous for a fortnight - I didnt try to photo it. As the valley opened out we came to Nauders + a little beyond, by a small sheet of water (Reschendsee) we had our first view of the Oetler group. - As we went on we passed two other smaller lakes, Mittersee + Heidersee - as foregrounds to the distant mts. We passed over a broad high land untilled + like a moor - by a long zigzag descent we reached the valley, passing the old castle of Furstenbury, now occupied by poor families + the large Benedictine Abbey of Marienberg in the midst of trees, on a hill above it - Here we entered the Upper Vielschigan valley + soon came to Mals, still on the slope above the valley + stopped for two hours - had dinner in the theatre ball room of the Hotel - soon after 2 we started on again - the R.R. at present ends at this place - We soon reached the floor of the valley which is here very wide - the sun burned very hot + the dust was with us - It was not pleasant - after 3. we got to Spondinig - where we stopped for a few minutes for refresh-ment - here we left the road from Landeck to Meran + turned to the R. - to cross the valley + climb to Trafoi - I should have noted that on the high moor, the ground beyond the Rescchen sea, we crossed the divide of the watershed between the Black Sea + the Adriatic. The climb up the Trafoi Thal is not exciting - it is a steady pull of about 8 miles from Prad at the foot. Our driver got us to the hotel at 6.30 - the boys walked nearly all the way up - we were shown at once to our Rooms, 6·7 - on the first floor - with balcony + outlook to the mts + Glacier - 40 K. per day for the two - We washed up + changed our clothes - I had a half hour rest before dinner at 7.30 - not a satisfactory meal - Even some rather bad music - piano, violin + cello - The house seems to be quite full, but among the many that sat about it was surprising to me that there should be so few young + good looking ones - all are old + fat - to bed soon after 9 -

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 15, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant, but clouds hung over the high peaks all day, raising fears as to weather to-morrow - our driver says the wind is good + the barometer marks fair weather - but when the rain began to fall about 8 p.m, hope was at an ebb. We had bkfst. together after 8 - I spent the mf quietly in my room writing to Jim Shearman, Katie Elkin, &c. + looking thro' my several guide books for further information as to our line of travel - The boys went off on a random scout coming back in time for lunch at 1 - Ralph is developing a strong desire to do some real alpine climbing - Wt Elkin had advised him to climb the Oetler, saying he had done it + it was easy - The afternoon went as the mf did - I wrote + I went out + had a walk down to old Trafoi - The boys were off somewhere - Dinner at 7.30 - very unsatisfactory - after dinner, music - while sitting there Mrs Williams came + introduced herself + husband Dr W. She is a friend of Miss Roelker's - we had a pleasant chat - They are to leave in the mf. I dont know what we shall do - we must wait for good weather - If we do we must get into cheaper Rooms - 40 Kr. per day Room Rent is pretty steep -
