## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 22, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dark, foggy, wet m[illegible], the weather improving later in the day. I was to go down to Plainfield to lunch with the Tuesday's, but phoned to them \+ the lunch was set ahead to Sunday. I devoted the day to the photo prints from last summers negatives taken for souvenir albums for Jim \+ Ralph. Late in the afternoon I had a walk \+ after dinner I went to Mary Cook's - 154 S. 37 S. \+ there saw Sue Cook who is on here visiting. Rob\[t\]. Cook \+ his wife \+ Willie C. were there — also Walter \+ his Edw\[s\]. I stayed till near 10 — Then to studio \+ got framed print of Master Lambton \+ took it home -

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 23, 1907

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

father's birth-day - one hundred years ago.

Overcast but not unpleasant — to studio at 10.30 - much exercised by the goings on in the stable next to studio that has been let for an auto-garage - it is a menace to the neighborhood - gasolene is taken in by the barrel full \+ the fellows about are a rough lot \+ their behavior at night is reported as exceedingly bad. I went to Police station in 35 S. \+ was advised to send complaint to the Bureau of Combustibles in 67 S. I got out a lot of my old unfinished paintings \+ sketches with a view to finishing some of them - Home to lunch at 2 — Worked over photos of last summer Till after 4 — Then walked on Riverside drive for about an hour — then a rest — At 6.30 Jim \+ I went to Chars' who gives a dinner to celebrate the 100th anniversary of Father's birth — Charl, George, Jim \+ I were the celebrants — a good dinner honoring the memory of a dear good father — Home at 11.
