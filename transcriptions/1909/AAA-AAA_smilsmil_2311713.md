## Left Page

### Sat. April 24, 1909

**Weather:** Brilliant day \- cloudless, breezy + bracing  **Temp:** about 45°

To studio before 10 one of Miller's men came with my print closet. He spent some time fitting some moulding on my aqua-tint box table \- Jane + Mary + Sherwood came + between the carpenter + their visit little of the mf was left. I started the work of packing away my pfs. in the new closet \- Home to lunch at 2 \- back to studio soon after 3. + continued putting away pfs. Till after 5. Men home + had a rest before dinner with Jim. Evef alone \- Reading newspapers + the new magazines.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 25, 1909

**Weather:** Superb mf \- bright, breezy + bracing  **Temp:** [illegible]

I had my scrub early \- + [illegible]. Rather late. Then, feeling unusually well I started out alone + went by subway to the Broadway end of the Road \- 227° S. at entrance to Van Cortlandt Park \- all a surprise to me \- I had never been there before \- the wide stretch of the Park \- The picturesque diversity of the ground \- the trees + open spaces pleased me greatly \- about 9 o'clk I was first conscious of a discomfort in my right side in neighborhood of kidney \- it increased + when I reached home before 2 I was very uncomfortable \- Had dinner with Jim \- pain increasing so that I could not find rest in any position \- after 4 I undressed \- went to bed + sent for D. Tuttle \- he came about 5 \- my temp. Thee 100°2. He advised calomel but did not seem disposed to venture a guess as to the cause of the trouble \- he thought it might be gas in large intestine pressing on the damaged kidney \- by 10 o'clk the pain had passed \- but not the fever \- before 11 I took a dose of Ct. of Mag. + then slept.
