## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 23, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day with a good deal of rain \- got up before 8.30 after an uncomfortable night \- Stomach still very tender but quieting \- I managed to work rather steadily at arranging photo's of last summer. Trips in Album No. 2 \- also in taking another look over M.S. of father's autobiography in preparation to submit it to criticism of Geo. H. Putnam who has kindly consented to read it next Sunday \- I took some portions to a typewriter, cor. Bdway \& 81 St. \& had them retyped the originals being marred by interlineations \& changes. \- I spent some time reading Will H. Low's Chronicles \- my physical condition gradually improved \& at 6.30 I was able to eat a reasonable amt. of dinner

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 24, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain all last night \& almost continuously thro' the day \- I spent the mt at home \- wrote to The Post about proposition to build N.A. of D. Galleries on site of old Arsenal in Central Park. Other matters kept me busy till lunch at 1 \- After that I went to Second Nat'l \& got from Safe Deposit Jim's Cert. for 35 shares of Steel common \- Then to Charl's \- Wall St. \& had a consultation with him as to advisability of selling the stock which for a few days past has been falling rapidly \- Jim bought at 28 \- a short time ago it reached 58 \- now it is 41. Charl advised ag't. selling. Then I went to Cullen's \& left 2d. Album of last summer photos. to be mounted \- Then home after 4 o'clk \- A rest before dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. loafing with newspapers.

The Post printed my N.a. of D. letter \- pub'd March 2d \- A copy of it in Diary pocket.
