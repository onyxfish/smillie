## Left Page

### Sat. March 27, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant m'g\. clouded later \- light rain in the eve'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'g\. clouded later \- light rain in the eve'g\. My physical discomforts continue but not to a degree sufficient to keep me from going about \- Communicating by phone with Clarence Smith I learned that it would be best for me to go at once to his office to sign some papers with reference to action to get Reduction of Taxes on studio blt\. I went down \+ signed papers\. Saw W\. Hewitt there about Kate Leonard's case \- then Rodends To the Warniche Globe Co\. to see if I could find a piece of furniture suitable for keeping my prints in \- the 15×20 mes \- Could not\. Then I wandered down Centre S't\. to the bridge much interested in the tearing ufo of that part of the city in preparation for gigantic improvements \- by L\. Road went to studio \- there for a few minutes \- then home to lunch at 1.30 \- The afternoon I spent at the N.A. exbn \- an interesting show \- I was impressed by the fact that few of the works were by men or women that I knew \- many strong exhibits by people whose names were new to me \- \- Had much talk with Allison \+ dis \- Home after 5 \- a short rest before dinner \- Eve'g Jim went to Opera on invitation from Alice Ireland \- I went to Century hoping to meet some of the N.A's \+ get some information as to possibilities\. ation at meeting next Wed \- saw but few \- Had a chat with Collins \- Ja. Morse \- Dr. Emerson \+ Roeloker\. I got home early \+ to bed\.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 28, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the early m'g\. then slowly clearing \- During the middle of the day some pleasant sunshine \- then more clouds\. About 7 o'clk\. the Compound Cathartic that I took last night called for my prompt attention \- then to bed again \- Later there was another call after which, within half an hour, the sore \+ fast tender to the touch, under point of ribs on right side passed away leaving no trace \- Dr. Tuttle thinks there is some stoppage in a duct to the gall bladder \- At any rate, relief came to me \+ at once I felt that once more I was "on an even keel"\. I got up, had my cerel \+ then m'g bkft\. after 10 \- loafed with newspapers till noon then out \+ walked in Riverside Free 2\. It was a joy to me to be able to walk without discomfort \- Dinner with Jim\. After that we went out for a stroll on Riverside with the thousands of promenaders there \- It was a sort of pre\-Easter parade \- On our return I computed that I had walked not less than five miles to\-day without fatigue or discomfort\. It is surprising to me the promptness with which such attacks as I have just passed thro' pass off\. Tea at 6.30 \- Jim went out in the eve'g \- I read \+ wrote \-
