## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 21, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely mt., so spring like that I sat upon a Riverside Park bench for some time - clouded afternoon, rain at night - Had my bkfst after 9 - fooled away time reading newspapers \+ doing some photo. printing - then out for a loafing walk in Riverside Park - Dinner alone at 2 - Afternoon had a call from Will Parke - later Chas. \+ Mary came - tea at 6.30 - and evening - well, I did a number of things that have been awaiting my disposal for some time, then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 22, 1909

Washington's Birthday.
Jim got home about midnight.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Weather dubious in the early mt. but cleared delightfully. Rain at night. A slight discomfort in the region of the belt yesterday increased to-day, making me discouraged - I spent about a couple of hours in the morning loitering about Riverside Park \+ sunning myself on the benches - I had my lunch at about 2 - later I went out again - went to two or three apartment houses where I thought there might be sunny rooms facing south \+ west \+ made enquiries of preliminary character - at the bldg. cor. of 74th St. a very large \+ modern one, I found the proprietor, Mr. W. R. Stewart - He took me about \+ kept me for more than an hour - He took me to an apartment on the 11th floor, overlooking other bldgs., all the rooms lighted from south or west - It was delightful - The tenant wishes to sub-let - The place is so superior in finish \+ location that I am anxious to secure it if possible - The difficulty will be to get release from my lease. I got very tired after my long walk \+ in going about with Mr. Stewart \+ my discomfort that has been hanging about me for two days greatly increased \+ continued to increase till when I went to bed with a hot water bag I was in positive pain — it was pervasive in the region of the stomach \+ of a character that suggested neuralgia -
