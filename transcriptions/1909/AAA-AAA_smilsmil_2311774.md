## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 24, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day kept my bed all day feeling generally miserable — dictated to Ralph a letter to D. Tuttle detailing a plan to go by automobile under Ralph's care to West Port, The D. to take early m'f train \+ meet me at W.pt at 5 o'clk \+ then wait for 10.33 night train to N.Y. \+ asking him to Telegraph the earliest day he could come — Eve'g basket of fruit from Chave am.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 25, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very hot hazy day — I woke feeling unusually well — I had a mug of weak lime lemonade — that was because the D. had said that I needed fruit acid — but it upset things so that I could not take any blst. I got up \+ wrapped in comforters \+ blankets sat in easy chair in studio while my room \+ bed had an airing — Had some broth for dinner — A chill set in soon after \+ I was got to bed at once with hot water bags about me — the chill was a severe one — the Temp. following got up to 105°. Ralph gave me Sweet Sp's of Nitre twice \+ the Temp. fell rapidly — Telegram from D.T. saying he w'd. come up to-morrow — By 9 o'clk I felt easier — Then had sudden movement of bowels that have been closed for four days — a strange looking mess — much mucus — Some chicken broth at 9.30 — then to sleep. woke at 12.30 with stomach pain — Took Paregoric \+ campher — pain passed then a second chill came on \+ I had an hour \+ a half of misery — about 3 o'clk — Then fever — I dont know how high Temp. got — but by 8 am. Thurs. it had subsided to 101°.
