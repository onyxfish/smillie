## Left Page

### Wed. March 17, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A smart snow flurry in the mf. Soon cleared bright, breezy + cold. On my way to the studio stopped at Chasl's + saw Mary + girls. To studio before 11 + continued my work of taking acc't of stock of pfs. Till 1.30 - then home + had lunch - back to studio after 3 + worked till 5 o'clk - The number of pfs + variety of plates unearthed surprises me - I made discoveries of a number of lost things + doubt if I have yet got together nearly all of my work - Home at 6.30 + rested till 6.30 - Something very like neuralgia has made me very miserable during afternoon + evg - The seat of trouble is in the stomach. Dinner rather late - the Kitchen much upset over Mary Mooney's death + in making preparations for the funeral. Evg. Reading + writing -

## Right Page

### Thur. March 18, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day - at 3 A.m. I took a castor oil cocktail that I had prepared the night before + under its inspiration I got busy at 6 o'clk. This treatment was based upon the hope that by it the discomfort in stomach + thereabouts would be relieved - in this I was disappointed - To studio after 10 + worked among my etching pfs. Till 1.30 - then home to lunch. Not feeling comfortable in my internals and anxious to be in shape to go with Dr. Tuttle to D. Fisher's Lantern slide show of views among the West Indies - given in the Florence Nightengale Hall of the Presbyterian Hospital. I was on my back from 3 to 6 o'clk - Dinner at 6.30 - Evg I did not feel equal to the trip over to the east side + reluctantly gave it up - Jim went to his dvil, + I wrote notes to Tribune + Post setting forth that as the N.A. of D. had not as an organization, asked for permission to build in Central Park the institution should not be censured - It's favored by a group in the Academy but not as yet by the Academy as a unit.
