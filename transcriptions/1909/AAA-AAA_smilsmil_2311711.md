## Left Page

### Tues. April 20, 1909

**Weather:** Cold persistent rain  **Temp:** about 45°

To studio at 10 — no steam, no fire — went after Winston \+ had him start a fire \+ before noon the studio was more than warm enough. I painted on The Cathedral at Chartres till near 1 o'clk. getting over the whole of the middle \+ foreground. Now I shall put it aside, probably till next fall, when it will get another painting. Thence home to lunch much under the influence of the weather — spent a good part of afternoon on lounge — some time looking over Hinds' book on Engraving \+ Etching — Dinner with Jim both of us rather glum \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 21, 1909

**Weather:** A dismal day — cloudy, cold \+ raw morning  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain afternoon \+ evening at times heavily — to studio in the mtf. \+ painted on "Sunset on the Giant Adirondacks" — Home to lunch at 1.30 — much out of sorts — headache, discomfort in right side \+ Temp. 1° above normal — spent most of the afternoon on lounge. Tried to communicate by 'phone with Jim but owing to blundering somewhere I could not get him after after trying for half an hour. He had seats for himself \+ me at the Lyceum Theatre in 45° St. "The Dawn of a 'to-morrow'" — I wished to tell him that I w'd be unable to go — I told him when he came home to dinner \+ he tried to get someone on the 'phone who w'd go with him, but without success — at last I told him that I w'd try it — \+ we went — had good front seats in the balcony. The play was by Mrs. Frances Hodgson Burnett \+ altho' upon another social plane has many of the characteristics of Little Lord Fauntleroy — Glad (Miss Robson) is an impossible creature with a very obvious moral — she was an animated sermon \+ rubbed it in energetically at times — nevertheless I approved because the play was clean in story \+ in presentation — there were no décolleté shirts \+ naked bosoms. In spite of my illness I got thro' all right — a driving rain storm as we came out of the theatre — home in a taxi cab \+ Right to bed.
