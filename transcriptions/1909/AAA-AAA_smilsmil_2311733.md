## Left Page

### Thur. June 3, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Light a rain in the early m'f. then hazy \+ luminous — then sunshine — Ther. 76°. B'kf'. before 8 — To studio at 10 — made good progress catalog \+ die p'fs. Home to lunch at 1.30 — wrote to Mielatz — Read awhile — then walked on Riverside feeling decidedly better than for some time past. Home to dinner at 6.30 — Ev'n finished Willsow's Chronicle of Friendships — I have some definite ideas concerning it as a work but find it difficult to put them into language. I have been interested because more or less acquainted \+ familiar with persons \+ places described but he is prolix \+ much given to fine writing — that is temperamental — he is an ardent worshipper of R.L. Stevenson \+ St. Gaudens — he records with evident satisfaction his intimacy with many art \+ literary lions \+ hardly conceals his own self complacency. That jarred upon me as I could not forget his last mural decoration for the Wilkes-Barre Court House.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 4, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day, Rain (very wet) \+ wind — B'kf'. at 8 — To studio soon after 10 having stopped at G'o? Lentral \+ got tickets for New-Haven. At studio I continued \+ completed classification of die p'fs — Home at 1 — lunch — packed my grips — left the house at 3.15 \+ took 4 o'clk train to New-Haven getting there at 5.40. Ralph met me at the station \+ together, by hack, we went to Mr. Lekin's home getting there at 6 — Ralph stayed to dinner \+ spent the evening. Katie showed us many things rec'd. from Japan, a part of Mollie's effects \+ some remarkable photos of Canadian Rockies, taken by Mollie.
