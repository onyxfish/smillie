## Left Page

### Thur. April 22, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very charming day altho' it started dubiously \- To studio early, stopped at Millers \- he has my closet for prints almost finished. \- I painted on my old picture that was at first a view in the Huddle woods \- Lake George. \- I have painted it over a number of times + do not know whether it has been for better or for worse. It is one of several small paintings that I dab, or daub, at every now + then. \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- at 3 went down town \- Took a proof of Washington exposures to Cullen to develop \- went to Charl's new office + saw him \- then to Beckman St. looking for trunk boards to use in packing prints in the new closet. Found what I sought at 7/4 Beekman St. \- Meyer \- Then up town by subway to 72d St \- loafed for some time in Riverside Park \- Home at 5.30 \- a rest \- dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. some reading \- some writing, then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 23, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw, wet mt. \- after 2 P.M. clearing weather began + the late afternoon was delightful, clear sky + welcome sunshine growing stronger \- To studio soon after 10 \- Closet, by Miller, for etching pf. was not sent to studio because of the rain \- I got out a canvas that had upon it a first painting \- A Hill \- top "of Farm" \- done at Montrose years ago. I set my palette + worked upon it till 1.30 \- then home to lunch \- I had a long walk \- that is, a long walk for me now a\- days \- in Riverside Park \- perhaps three miles. Dinner with Jim \- Evef. \- after some reading I wrote to Ralph.
