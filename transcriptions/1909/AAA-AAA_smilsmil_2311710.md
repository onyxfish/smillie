## Left Page

### Sun. April 18, 1909

**Weather:** mild \+ spring-like  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- enjoyed a scrub \+ sheer hlp's after 9 o'clk \- some loafing with newspapers then out \+ loafing in Riverside Till 2 o'clk when I had dinner with Jim \- After that more loafing, yielding to "that tired feeling" so common at this time of year \- later went out for a walk \- went to "Columbia Court" \- Riverside \+ 115° St\. To see apartment of W. Lamson \- no one at home \- Then wandered about the Columbia, Barnard, Maun's, Teacher's \+ other educational bldgs \- some extensive ones now in course of building \- Home after 6 \- Willis Cook took tea with us. I was shocked to learn from him that his mother died last Feby. I cannot understand how it could be that I should not have known of it at the time.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 19, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sudden outbreak of spring \- Ther. got up to near 80° \+ there was a rattling thunderstorm soon after 6 o'clk p.m. I wrote to W. A. J. Parsons \- Print Dept. Cong. Library \- Washington, about mounts for prints for the library, asking if my boards would be permissable if cut down to regulation size. Then to studio \+ painted on Chartres Cathedral till 1 o'clk \- Lunch at home before 2 \- at 3 went to barbers \+ from there to Brooks Bros. \+ ordered suit of clothes \- Then to Scribners \+ bought copy of "A Short History of Engraving \+ Etching by A.M. Hind, one of the curators of Print Dept. British Museum. Home after 5 \- dinner \- Eve'g, exploring the Short History which covers 473 pp. In describing technical processes it is much like most of its predecessors \- it is very muddlesome \- singular how difficult it seems to be to describe clearly the very simple processes of engraving \+ etching.
