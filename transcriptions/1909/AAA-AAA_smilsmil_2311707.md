## Left Page

### Mon. April 12, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

met W. A. J. Parsons at Cong. Library

A very pleasant mild day, clouding over in the afternoon. The compound Cathartic pill that I took last night did not work satisfactorily this m'f & thro' the day I have had some fever & a good deal of dragging discomfort in stomach, side & around to right Kidney spoiling pleasure completely. After bkfs. we went out to the ground back of White House to see the children roll eggs this being their practice on Easter Monday. An adult can enter the grounds only if in charge of a child. Small boys lent themselves to strangers for a consideration. Then escaladed over the iron railing to offer themselves to a new comer & so on till held up by a policeman. Such an offer was made to me but I did not accept. Then we went to the Treasury Bldg & I dragged thro' that with Ralph & a crowd of women. nothing particularly interesting to me. Then to Hotel & rested till 1 o'clk. Lunch. Then to Congressional Library & met Mr. Parsons & with him a gentleman from Boston, a cover collector of prints. I did not get his name, unfortunately. we had a general chat of more than an hour & my Roofs had flattering consideration. Our conversation roamed widely. Mr. Parsons invited me to lunch with him to-morrow but I begged off being in no condition to lunch with any body. Back to Hotel before 5. Some fever, feeling at times so uncomfortable that I thought I wd. have to give up & go home at once. But after a rest I felt better & after 7 went down to the Grill Room & had something to eat. We 4 in our Room chatting with Ralph till bed time.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 13, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable windy day. overcast, Rain at various times during the day & evening. Up before 8. bkfs. at 9. About 11 went to Cong. Library. met Mr. Parsons. He showed me the methods of keeping the prints. he says Islanders are too expensive. He uses a book-shaped portfolio of three sizes, with flaps. He has a variety of ways of disposing of the larger prints. He piled a lot of Whistler's on the table, left us promising to return & that was the last we saw of him. At 1.30 we went to Restaurant in Attic & had a simple lunch. After that Mr. Roberts took us thro' the stack & other interesting parts of the building & later an attendant took us down to the cellar & all thro' the machinery sections. It was 4.30 when we got thro' all. Then over to the Capitol. we went over all parts of the bldg open to the public except climbing the dome. The works of art were depressing to me. The Leutze Westward was especially disappointing. After our rounds I was very tired & was glad to get back to Hotel & rest. About 7 I called up Cosmos Club & got C.Y. Turner on the 'phone. He said he wd. come around to see me later. Ralph & I had dinner & when we came up I found Turner in the lobby waiting. We talked together about N.A. of D. matters for about an hour. both agreed. Then he left & R. & I went to our Room & made Ready to go home by the 10 o'clk. Train To-morrow m'f.
