## Left Page

### Fri. May 28, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came from New-Haven.

An April day of sunshine & showers. about 2 o'clk. a very brief thunder shower — I was up & had bkfs. at about 8 o'to studio at 10 — Douglas's man had been there yesterday & varnished the interior of new Cherry closet for prints — I got out a lot of die. pfs. to begin assorting, classifying & destroying — I worked at it till after 1 o'clk — Then another man from Douglas came \+ I left him to give 2d coat of varnish to closet — Home to lunch — Ralph came along soon after 3 o'clk. Together we went to the Fine Arts Galleries — W. 57t St to see the four Mural decorations for County Court House at Wilkes-Barre. painted by Blackfield — Smedley — Low & Cox — of course the Blackfield led the others almost out of sight altho' it was dissapointing to me — but the others! — Cox was hard, formal, commonplace — Low was weak in design & feeble in drawing & color — d— has no feeling at all for color — Smedley was less offensive to me than either Cox or Low — altogether it was a depressing agt'm — A brief shower held us prisoners in the gallery for a time — Then home — T dinner — Evg. we went to Charel's — he played some light and inspiriting pieces on his new Pianola — We got home before 11.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 29, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came from Ansonia.

A pleasant day. April-like in cloud & sunshine effects till the late afternoon when the atmosphere cleared deliciously. I got up at about 7. 30 — bkfs. alone after 8 — at 9.30 went to Dr. Tuttle's to report progress & if possible get from him some theory in explanation of the peculiar pains & discomforts that are now nearly constant with me. He says that while I do not have a "floating kidney" it is a movable one subject to cloggings & distensions that by interference disturb other organs & consequently give me more or less of pain. The pain under my belt has changed character & location since yesterday — it is not so distressing but it is constant & more than annoying — I got to studio at about 11 — My print closet has been varnished inside & what remained of the outf. was spent in Reasranging prints in it. Ralph came in after 12 — Together we set home for lunch just before 2 — Soon after that he went to Gr. Central Station to meet Jim — I had a rest after & some reading — & wrote awhile on cat. of my pfs. Then out to Riverside park where it was very lovely — all but my pain — Home at 6.30 — found Jim & Ralph there — dinner — Evg. The boys went out to a theatre — I read newspapers & magazines —
