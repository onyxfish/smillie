## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 30, 1909

**Weather:** Jim went to Ridgefield.  **Temp:** [illegible]

About 4" of snow fell during night \+ m\+ the weather, mild at first, grew rapidly colder \+ at night it was dry winter weather \- I got to studio \+ had an unsatisfactory day in that most of the outcome was either unsuccessful or not worth while. \- Much time \+ trouble was given to one of the two Morgan (Junius) p\[illegible\] that I have on thin paper \- mounted by pasted edges on a heavy mounting board \- I got it off \- The p\[illegible\] was too wavy to leave as it was \- I now that it is off I dont know how to treat it \- I dont like such proofs when pasted flat to the backer \+ know of no way to keep them flat when pasted only at the edges \- I cleaned up alot of photos. of European scenes bought by Annie many years ago \- Jim came in to say that he was going to Ridgefield with Bradford Boardman \+ a party to have some winter sport \- I got home at 4.30 dressed \+ then rested till 6 \- Then to Charls's \- Dinner \- Evf \- he supplied us with tickets to a carriage \+ I escorted Mary \+ her gives to University Glee Club concert at the Waldorf \- An immense \+ gay crowd, as usual \- mostly young folks \- The singing did not commence till 9 o'clk \- The evening was Butler's musical setting to Tennyson's Bugle Song \- The words, to my mind, being one of the most charming English Compositions \- so ethereal in fancy \- so musical in words as to make melody subserflous \- The Result did not please me \- The descriptive parts not successful \- Imagine a chorus of 100 or more male voices breathing energetically \- dying \- dying \- dying \- It was breaking a butterfly on a wheel \- The Amherst College Song, in unison was given with great effect \+ it is a mighty song \+ was immensely stimulating \- It was 11 when we got out \+ had trouble in getting our carriage \- I stopped a few minutes at Charls's \- Home \+ to bed at midnight

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 31, 1909

**Weather:** Jim ret?  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold windy day with sunshine \- I got up late \- had my scrub \+ then hlp\- before 11 \- After 2 I went out on Riverside for a walk but it was too exposed \- the wind was cutting cold \- so I retreated to the protection of the west side of West End Ave. \+ continued my promenade there \- Dinner alone at 2 \- Gordon Smillie came in at about 4 o'clk \- I made a pleasant call \- Later Chas. \+ Kath. Throop came and spent an hour with me \- Jim came home at about 7 o'clk. \+ we had supper together \- Evg. Reading \-
