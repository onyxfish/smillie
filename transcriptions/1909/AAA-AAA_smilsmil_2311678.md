## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 13, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant mied day — Ther. getting up to 46°. By advice of D°. T. I took at Castor Oil cocktaie at 4 A.M. + "got busy" about 5.30 — I had a late bkf°. I felt weak in the knees during the day — I had a short walk on Riverside — busied myself at home — did some [illegible] printing on a paper new to me in the afternoon I went to D°. Tuttle's + left for him an interesting specimen of [illegible] — Eveng. had a sort of loafing time Jim went to Armory to shoot

Tom Cook married to Frances Wilcox at Buffals this evef.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 14, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast but not unpleasant till night when rain began to fall — I did not have bkf°. till after 10 — I had a comfortable walk of about 2½ miles on Riverside before dinner at 2 — I feel now as though once more "on an even keel" — but — for how long? In the afternoon I had another walk. Eveng. wrote to Geo. Heason thanking him for assenting to my request to do some work upon the picture of "Lifting of the Clouds" painted by me about 40 years ago.
