## Left Page

### Fri. June 11, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not pleasant - nor yet really unpleasant overcast with easterly cool winds in the m\. growing warmer with some glimmering sunshine in the afternoon. - Tenth anniversary \+ commencement at Hackley School. - I had hoped to go but did not like to take weather chances - On my way to studio stopped at 29 E. 38. St. \+ saw Mary about purchases for Keene at studio continued arranging ph\. from dies eng\. by Father - 2 o'clk. before I got home to lunch - Before I had finished W. Huggins (I think that is his name) called to consult with me concerning aqua-tinting \+ printing in color - He is a novice with some enthusiasm \+ a considerable equipment of valueless information. - Soon after he left Mielatz came \+ before he went away Mary came \+ later, Katharine - They bring unpleasant news about Elizabeth's eyes - The Surgeon is not satisfied with progress made \+ says she will not be in condition to start for the d[illegible] on wednesday \+ that means a change of plans \+ dates. - Dinner at 6.30 - Eve\. I wrote to Joseph \+ to Katie Elkin some acct. of the Braland Real Estate Co. muddle.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 12, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came from Ansonia.

A warm day - Ther. getting up to near 80º in the afternoon. - Bkfs. at 8 - to studio at 10 - finished arranging \+ putting away ph\. from dies eng\. by Father. - Home to lunch before 2 - lost some time taking a nap - wrote to Nye \+ Moody that the date for starting the Ark caravan had been changed. - Then out for a stroll on Riverside - Very lovely but too warm \+ languorous - It was 6.30 when I got home. - Found Jim there - down to spend Sunday here in N.Y. Dinner - Eve\. Jim went to Coney Island with Corwell. - I wrote some notes \+ read newspapers.
