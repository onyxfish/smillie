## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 20, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant day — growing much milder  **Temp:**

on my way to studio stopped a Charl's to leave a note sent to my address for Mavy — They were all at b\+p. So I went in \+ had a little chat with them — To studio soon after 70 \+ to work at once printing moonlight on old french paper. I worked with graver considerably between p\+s. also made changes in composition of ink \+ in manner of reg wiking. The paper used is not suitable but the 7 "blast p\+s. seemed to be all that I could expect under the conditions which I hope to improve. To-morrow by using thin Jap. paper — \+ after that grey China \+ french India — I had my lunch in studio \+ did not leave till near 5. Home at 5. very tired — Rested till. Dinner — 6.30 — Evef in big chair with Evef Post.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 21, 1909

**Weather:** A very mild sunny day. The Ther. getting up above 40°  **Temp:**

I got to studio at 10 \+ found the Ther. there at 83° \+ every window shut — Wruton said he found the heat at that measure but was content to shut off the steam. I had to walk the streets after I had opened the windows, till 11 o'clk. when the Ther. had fallen to 74°. Cha\[illegible\]. Collins called for a few minutes — I Resumed my printing of moonlight plate on thin Jap. paper \+ think I got some good p\+s. I had lunch in studio \+ a few minutes walk on Park Ave. I continued my work till 4 o'clk — then washed up \+ rested for half an hour, feeling tired. — Home at 5.30 — Dinner 6:15 — Jim went to dine r \+ I had part of an evef with phots. for boys' Albums —
