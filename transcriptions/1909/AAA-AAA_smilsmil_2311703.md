## Left Page

### Sun. April 4, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day. I did not get really awake till 10 o'clk. Then up — my mouth so sore that I phoned to D. T. about it — He said he wd. be in at 11.45 — I had my bath + bhfp. + got to the D's on time — he said the sore spot was smaller than when he cauterized it last Monday. He touched it with Nitrate of silver + advised me to dispense with lower teeth set for two or three days to give it time to heal. walked up home — with my Penknife cut away that portion of the hard rubber that galled the sore + for the first time in a fortnight ate with comfort. Part of afternoon spent reading. Had an invigorating, breezy walk in Riverside. Evef. — Tea at 6.30 — Evef. — Boys at home — a quiet time reading.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 5, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine breezy day. Before going to studio I went to Tiffany's — Mad. Ave. + 45th. St. + left a note in behalf of Otto Sandreczki — Then to Clausen's. To studio at about 11. I fixed up the Doughty painting to take to N.A. of D. Then home to lunch at 1.30. After lunch went, with Ralph, to schools of N.A. + left the Doughty painting with note for Watrous — Council meeting to-night. R. + I spent some time looking at the Academy collection of pictures. Then we went to the N.A. showing Exhib. in 57th. St. + spent some time there — met Miss Willard + Mr. Brownell. Home in time to have a short rest before dinner at 6.30. Evef. Jim + Ralph went out + I loafed + read.
