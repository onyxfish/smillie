## Left Page

### Wed. May 26, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another charming day \- bkft. after 8 \+ had another nosebleed while preparing for it, as I did yesterday mf. By snuffing up fine table salt it was stopped with little trouble \- Young Douglas came to studio with Roofer \- after examining the roof carefully arrangements were made for him to get to work on it to-morrow. I found it very difficult for me to get to work being so nerveless as to be incapable of fixing my attention upon anything so I set to work to arrange the many Bank-notes die pk. that I have \- while at the fad. E. Poefree, owner of plk adjoining studio came in \- a weak, purposeless complaining man, came \+ told me his wors for about an hour \- he has renewed, verbally only, his lease for one year of his plk to W.D. Sands, a man as mean as I. is weak. That means that there will be more trouble next winter when steam is wanted \- Down to lunch at 2 \- while at lunch my nose began bleeding again \+ it was nearly an hour before I could stop it \- Salt did it at last \- Then I lay quietly on my back on my lounge for an hour \- Then out to Riverside \+ loitered about there till after 6 \- The foliage, flowers \+ grass under the sunshine are very lovely now \- Dinner \- Eve\. reading Post \+ a little writing.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 27, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal day \- wet until late afternoon but heavily clouded all day long \- I had bkfst before 8 o'clk \- soon after 9 I went to Dr. Tuttle that he might take a look at my nose as there were some slight indications of bleeding again \- He examined carefully \+ said that he found no evidences of any thing promising further trouble \- The weather was so bad \- a heavy mizzle of rain against which an umbrella was small protection \- that I returned home \- I wrote to Spencer Nye \+ Earl Moodey at St. Huberts with reference to the reöpening of the Ark, but with little personal interest \- I confess to depressing forebodings as I look forward to a journey to the mountains \- I am given over to rheumatic pains \+ constant disturbance of stomach functions so that I have very few hours that are free from pain \- a light luncheon at 1.30 \- Headache \+ stomach ache made me useless for the afternoon \- from five to six o'clk I walked painfully on Riverside \- At dinner I hardly dared eat \- Eve\. in discomfort \- Why do I write these experiences? Really because I wish to keep a record of my progress down hill to satisfy my curiosity on the way, so that I may have a record of my decent \- That's morbid no doubt.
