## Left Page

### Sun. May 2, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim returned \-

A blustering day with rather more of clouds than sunshine — up before 7 \+ had my bath — then to bed again — up for all day at about 9 — bkfst. before 10 — loafed with news papers till 12. Then I tried a walk but owing to discomforts without \+ written I came home at a little after 1 o'clk — Dinner solus at 2 — The dull heavy feeling under my akron prevented another attempt at a walk — I loafed on my back some of the time — wrote a letter to Scott Brown tea before 7. Jim came in at 10 o'clk \+ I had something of a shock when he told me that he had engaged to go to the Ansonia Brass \+ Copper Co. starting in on two weeks from to-morrow as asso. Supt. at $15.— per week — hours from 7 a.m. to 6 p.m. I am greatly worried over what I consider his precipitate action — I do not think that he fully realizes the he has cut out for himself.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 3, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright windy day, unseasonably cold. Delayed in getting to studio. To work there till after 12 — the same bothersome arranging \+ cataloguing of pts — Then to Second Natl. Bank. Home at 2. a good part of the afternoon I loafed in Riverside Park, feeling somewhat better — Pain \+ discomfort amidships not so marked as it has been for some days past. Jim dined at a club housewarming — I had a lonesome dinner \+ in the evef went to Charl's \+ was there with Mary, her girls \+ him till after 10 — then home \+ to bed.

I told Shuler this m'f that probably I would hold on to my apartment for another year.
