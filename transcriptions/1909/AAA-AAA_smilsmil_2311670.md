## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 26, 1909

**Weather:** Clear, cold, windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold, windy — consequently a dirty day the snow having all disappeared leaving the pavements well supplied with desiccated horse dung \+ asphalt erosions — I got to studio at about 10.30 \+ set myself to work upon Moonlight — I have found a fine early pf. of it that is very discouraging to me as it shows that after several days work upon the plate I have now got it into a condition not so good \+ far away as it was when I left it in 1878 — it makes me realize how greatly age has robbed me — that I can no longer trust myself to work up to the standard of 30 years ago — for some time past I have been aware of this — but now the fact is forced upon me coarsely — \+ further, my last work at printing has proved to me that I no longer have strength enough to prove my plates — This means that I cannot clean up, or finish my unfinished work, but, practically paralyzed I must leave my studio efforts \+ effects in a awful confusion \+ incompleteness with little hope that there may have been enough done to secure for me a modest place among the workers of my time — I prepared plate for rebiting in parts — \+ a ground over the open field of the sky about the moon that I shall open the original lines to give them a touch of acid to bring back the effect as it was in 1878 — lunch at the studio — a \+ went to Millers in 40's \+ gave order for another panel — To studio till 4 ocl. New home — dinner — Eve \+ Jim went to a dance \+ I loafed helplessly.

As my digestion seems to be improving my kidney that for a very considerable time has been quiescent seems to be disposed to make trouble again.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 27, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

14th Anniversary of Sunnie's death.

Milder \+ quieter than yesterday, consequently pleasanter. I got to studio at 10.30 \+ spent the m'f. biting on Moonlight \+ hope that I have brought back some of the original force — but not all — an etching cannot safely be meddled with if it does not reach its mark at first fire — If the joke does not make its point — do not explain it — let it go \+ try another fly. I had my lunch in studio \+ fooled about there till near 5 — New home — dinner early as Jim has to go to a dress parade at the Armory in honor of Asst. Sect. of State Bacon. I loafed.

To bed with a temp. 100½ — Prescribed a Castor oil Cocktail in the m'f. prepared it \+ left it at the head of my bed —
