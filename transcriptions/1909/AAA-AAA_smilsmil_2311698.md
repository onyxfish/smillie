## Left Page

### Thur. March 25, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Great storm of wind \+ rain — heaviest during the mt. when 1 1/10" of Rain fell — a lull after the middle of the day — getting at it again later in the day — I felt so entirely out of sorts that I did not brave the storm but kept my room all day — general misery with head quarters in the stomach or thereabouts — When the storm held up at 1 ock I 'phoned to Dr. Tuttle asking him to come up — He came at 5.30 — stethesced me \+ made careful examination \+ said he could find no evidence of anything that need give alarm — It seems to be a functional disturbance — indigestion with consequent lodgement of gas where it could make itself most disagreeable — Altho' unfit for any thing I managed to read a good portion of T. C. Williams' Translation of the Arneid recently published. A bowl of gruel for dinner — Jim's night for drill.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 26, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day in spite of a snow squall or two in the mt. After a night of fair rest I got up feeling weak \+ still uncomfortable in mid region. After 10 I went to studio, going first to Lucy Kane's rooms. 706 3d. Ave., near 45th St. I arranged with her to come to studio \+ go on with the cleaning that was to have been done yesterday — She came before 12 \+ I left her there at work. Mary came in for awhile \+ Talked of affairs. I managed to get things together so that I could trust Mr. Kane to go on with the cleaning \+ at 12.30 left for home — Lunch before 2 — I had a walk in Riverside Park for about an hour after 3.30 — Read T. C. Williams' Arneid — W. Holme of Times came at about 6 \+ interviewed me concerning No. of D. \+ ste in Central Park. I had a frugal dinner alone at 7 — Jim dined with the "Betas" at Park Ave. hotel. My physical condition has improved since last night.
