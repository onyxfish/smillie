## Left Page

### Sun. June 27, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some thunder last night but no rain - much cooler - Ther. not much above 70°: overcast sky at times - a smart shower - some distant thunder between 10, 30 \+ 12 - I was up soon after 6 - my head uncomfortable - I had a walk before bkft - very lovely - bkft. at 8.30 - went to Ralph's room at about 9.30 - he, Jim \+ Bronson still in their beds - but they were soon up \+ off for their bkfts. At about 10 I went to Woolsey Hall - had two seats - one from Katey, the other from Ralph - Tried both \+ chose R's seat, which was next to Jim who came in late - from either seat the view of the Speaker was cut off by the ridiculous hats of some fool women - my hearing must be failing for I could understand so little of Pres. Hadley's address that I can give no report of it - The drift of it was: high ideals - honestly \+ a faith in fellow men that would bring out the best that was in them - we had to wait about in the lobby till the shower was over - then to R's room - there till 1 - then to the Sekings - all three of us - had dinner - on Piazza until 4 o'clk - then I went to my room \+ slept soundly till 6 - my head still very uncomfortable - Then tea - the boys stayed with us till 7.30 then they left, Jim expecting to catch a trolley car to Ansonia - I sat chatting with Katie about Religion Ethics \+ Kindred subjects.

Pres. Hadley's text was, Psalms 15th - 1-3. Lord, who shall abide in thy Tabernacle? Who shall dwell in Thy holy hill? He that walketh uprightly, \+ worketh Righteousness, \+ speaketh the truth in his heart. He that backbiteth not with his tongue, nor doeth evil to his neighbor, nor taketh up a reproach against his neighbor.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 28, 1909

Class Day.
Glee Club Concert.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot m\[orning\] with a haze in the air that foretold thunderstorms - I felt well \+ strong enough to take a stroll of considerable extent on the look out for a cottage that might be suitable for Mary \+ her girls - I found some offered for rent, some for sale \+ others in course of building - I did not get any figures but have been told that such houses as I looked at w\[oul\]d probably rent for from $45 to 60 per month \+ w\[oul\]d sell at from $5 to 8000 - To lunch at 1 - with a thunderstorm threatening wildly, Katie \+ I started for the Yale Campus at about 1.30 - the rain began to come down as we reached Woolsey Hall - we made a jump for the entrance \+ got in without being wet - There we had to stay till nearly 2.30 roaming about - sitting \+ chatting - Then the rain having ceased we started for Ralph's room but learned before getting there that the Class Day exercises were being held in the Hyperion Theatre - We raced over there - exercises already commenced but we got two good seats in gallery - the occasion was not inspiring to me - poor \+ addresses, not of very high order - After that we went to the planting of the class ivy - then Katey left, me \+ I went to Adminis[tration] Bldg \+ heard part of Pres. Hadley's speech to the class - then home \+ had a rest before dinner - Then Katie \+ I went to the Glee Club concert at Woolsey Hall - in cab - The concert was a very good one - I was much impressed by the Quartet - Munnigurode, a funny little fellow - clear, bell-like Tenor - Pomeroy, 2d Tenor - Howard, 1st bake - Tobin an Second base - a rich, melodious voice - Their pieces, all comic, were the hit of the ev\[ening\]. out at 10.10 - home by cab.
All night much disturbed by pain in Kidney so that I got little sleep - I drank, at intervals, a quart of water \+ took 10 grs of Benzoate of Soda \+ 10 grs of Urotrosin - I got a good wash out \+ disinfecting - by m\[orning\] the disturbance had disappeared
