## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 10, 1909

*Miss Anthony left*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day almost cold \+ with high wind \+ clouds \- winding up with a smart rain storm after 6 p.m. Miss Kate Anthony left in the morning stage at about 7 o'clk. I did not get up till after 9 feeling very weak \- got down stairs \+ wrote a letter to Dr. Tuttle describing my more recent miseries \- this kept me till after 11 \- Then the exertion seemed to have been too much for me \+ I had to go to bed with pain in stomach \- After 1 o'clk I had a bowl of junket. I spent an unhappy afternoon, part of it, rolled up in blankets on lounge on east piazza \- Evel\+ had bowl of broth before the fire \- loafed thro' the eveng. with the family. went to bed with mean stomach pain

In writing to Tuttle I told him of my condition \+ gave a brief resumé of attacks during the 35 days I have been here. Fifteen attacks in

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 11, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day \- cool, quiet \+ clear \- Up before 7 but as my room was warmed by a fire \+ it was cold elsewhere I had my gruel in my bedroom. Ther. in my room fell to 56\. last night. Before 10 Ralph moved my folding chair with cushions from studio to south piazza \+ there I spent the mo\+ quite delightfully, free from pain \+ wrapped in warm steamer rugs with Kate Douglas Wiggins' Rebecca on the swinging rest before me. At noon, or 1\. o'clk. Rather I had a bowl of mutton broth. In the afternoon I began to move about \+ at last got down the road to where the men were working on the wall retaining road \- Then on my chair again till after 4 o'clk \- Then to my bed room \+ had the nap that the flies denied to me on the piazza \- Mrs. Frank Dodd \+ daughter called \- I went to dinner with family \+ had a light supper \- Evel\+ Bessie \+ Ralph out star gazing for meteors \- brilliant night.
