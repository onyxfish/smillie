## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 30, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - awoke early after an unusually comfortable night - It was a long wait till bkfst. Then, with assurance, I ate some melon \+ in consequence suffered considerable discomfort till Dr. T- came - He gave me a mixture that brought quick relief - so that when dinner time came I ate my scraped beef with satisfaction - Jim came in bringing two sets of under wear that he had purchased for me - also a fine bunch of flowers. I wrote a letter to Mary \+ it was mailed before 2 o'clk. I recd. by mail my two fountain pens that I had left behind, but no scrap of writing - Had a long social visit from Dr. Mc Bains - also a formal call from Mrs. Christie, Dr. Sft. - A good bath \+ rubbing \+ then napping till tea time. Evening wrote on diary.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 31, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely sunshine out of doors - much dreaming not all of it pleasant, last night - awoke at 5 - long wrist till bkfst. after 8 - After bkfst. Roz gave me a big enema - soap \+ glycerine - the ordeal was trying to me - the evacuation was very complete - Geo. came in soon after \+ made an extended call. The enema made me feel useless, so that I couldn't get much enjoyment out of Geo's visit. As I have not yet heard a word from home since I left on Thurs. I wrote a short note - also a note to Shutter at the Wellesley enclosing check for rent for Septr. I ate a good dinner with relish at 1.30 but all the afternoon I felt very dopey \+ slept much of the time. At last a postal card from Ralph, without date came - said he was going up Grant - \+ that had to satisfy my longing for more - I was greatly disappointed - Letter from Jim - Charl sent a couple of books - A light tea - About 8 I got into easy chair \+ was wrapped in blankets. Before long I began to have trouble in stomach \+ got into bed - trouble increased - but after hugging my hot water bag for some time it subsided \+ I fell asleep.
