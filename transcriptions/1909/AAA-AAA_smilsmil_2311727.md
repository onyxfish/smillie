## Left Page

### Sat. May 22, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull cold day with rain much of the time. I had a fairly restful night from midnight to 7 am. I got up feeling thankful when I found that I had little trace left of yesterdays pain. I took a stiff dose of Cit. of Mag. \+ breakfast at 9 \- D. T. called me up at 10 \+ I reported that I was better. The bed-bug professor came \+ I had a long conference with him. On careful search he bagged one on my bedstead head board. He say that while we are away during the summer he will thoroughly fumigate the rooms burning 10 lbs of sulphur in each room. I wrote to Ralph. At 1 o'clk. I was induced to eat a very simple meal but it was too much \+ for the rest of the day I was in almost as much misery as yesterday. Jim came along at about 4 P.M. I was not able to give him a very jolly reception. At 6.30 he had a good dinner \+ I had some gruel. Cwelf Hugh Crowell came over to see him. I went to bed with temp. 2° above normal

## Right Page

### Sun. May 23, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day \+ chilly — The sun shone out before sunset. After a bad night I kept my bed \+ sent for Dr. Tuttle because my temperature still keeps from one to two degrees above normal \+ the region of my right kidney is very tender. The output of urine has some unusual peculiarities in pus mucus. The Dr. came at 12 — I get little comfort from his visit being quite sure that he has no well defined idea of the possible condition of my insides. Mary \+ Katharine came \+ made me a visit. I sat up in my bed \+ wrote to W. H. \+ J. Smith of Scribners. \+ I wrote to Aunt Jessie congratulating her for attaining to her 90th birthday, to-morrow. I got up \+ dressed in time to sit at dinner with Jim. I had some gruel. The afternoon passed dismally. Jim left at 6.30 to catch 7 o'clk. train for Ansonia. I read in the evening \+ did some writing \+ to bed late.
