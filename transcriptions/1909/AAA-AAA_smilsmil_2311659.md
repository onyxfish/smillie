## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 2, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — to studio at about 10 — to work on place of Cedars by Moonlight \+ after stoning that portion of the sky about the moon I ground ink — cut paper, set press \+ took a couple of prints to see how much I had reduced the line It was nearly 1 o'clk. when I got cleaned up \+ I was engaged to lunch with the Maynards at 1 o'clk. at 209 Duckman St. It was nearly 1.30 when I got there \+ Geo. \+ Nellie ahead of me — The M.s live in a snug little 6 room apartment — Mrs. M.'s mother, Mr. Brownell is with them — we had a pleasant, chatty time till near 4 — then I left — by subway to 103d St. \+ from there walked to Driveway \+ home — from 5 to 6 I rested — Dinner at 6.30 — Cornwell dined with us — Eve'g — The three went to Theatre \+ I busied myself in a variety of ways.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 3, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little sunshine in the a.m. dull p.m. wet eve'g. B.k.f. at 10 — newspapers — about noon went out alone \+ had a comfortable four mile walk on Riverside — In at 2 \+ had dinner with the boys — loafing afternoon — Eve'g went with the boys to Charles' \+ spent a couple of hours with him \+ the Throops — Mary \+ her girls.
