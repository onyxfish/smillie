## Left Page

### Thur. March 11, 1909

**Weather:** Bright day, high cold wind \- Ther. 30°  **Temp:** [illegible]

lower than it was yesterday. To studio after 10 \+ spent the m'f. overhauling \+ packing into dust tight parcels prints of various kinds continuing the clearing up of yesterday \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back to studio after 3 \+ for some time worked at caulking seams in the bottoms of large wooden trays in dark-room to be used in biting etchings, bleaching prints \+ such like work \- home soon after 5 \+ had a rest before dinner at 6.30 o'clk \- Jim did not come home as he had to be at the Armory early. I spent the evening writing some notes \+ reading.

I met Miss Agnes Abbott in the street at 1 o'clk. to day \- walked with her short distance.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 12, 1909

**Weather:** Very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- To studio after 10 \+ completed my clearing up there \- now I am ready to begin making a selection of prints to take to Washington for the Nat'l. Collection growing out of the collection of copyright prints. Home to lunch at 2. After 3 went to Pomeroy's, 3\+ 2. 23? 5t. \+ had a wait there while new pads were fitted on my truss \- It was 5 o'clk. when I left there \- rode uptown \+ walked on Riverside \+ in the Park till after 6 \- then home \- dinner with Jim at 6.30 \- Ev'g. wrote to Ralph.
