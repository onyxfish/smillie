## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 6, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph Ret'd. To New-Haven.

A fine day after the storm — still mild but the Weather Bureau promises an unusual cold wave by to-night — I got up feeling very seedy \+ had bkfs. at 8.30 — Ralph took 9:15 for New-Haven — I got to Studio \+ Resumed dry point work on moonlight sky — I prepared lunch in studio \+ after \+ had a walk on the sunny side of Park Ave — then Resumed work till soon after 4 o'clk — feeling better as the day went on — My trouble probably a form of indigestion — As the years go on these disorders take new forms — On leaving Studio I walked up Lex Ave to 59th S't. inspecting the progress on the N.Y. Central Station — then across to Broadway \+ by subway home — Dinner with Jim at 6.30 — eve'g. Reading \+ writing notes.

Ther 56° in the m'g.
Dropped 26° in 19 hours.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 7, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 21°

A clear cold winter day — but not so cold as the weather Bureau promised — To studio after 10 — worked awhile on Moonlight — then took an imps. — was encouraged — \+ shall leave the sky part and rebite a shallow place in the centre of picture — Prepared lunch soon after 1 — after that ground up the last of the Kimmel \+ Voigt ink that I have. Left the studio after 4 — Rode from 42nd to 72nd S't. \+ then walked farther than I found it pleasant in the wind \+ cold — Dinner with Jim — who went to drill in the eve'g. I was busy with newspapers \+ many small matters.
