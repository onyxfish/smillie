## Left Page

### Sun. July 11, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant mng. but before noon the weather thickened — later in the afternoon there were showers with some thunder round about. I got up feeling as well as usual but pains began in my right side before noon increasing rapidly after dinner — At about 3 o'clk I went to bed with hot water bags but could find no comfortable position — at 5 o'clk. my temp. having risen to 101½°, I asked Ralph to phone to Dr. Hubbard — He came before 6 \+ made a long call — He had nothing to add to instructions already rec'd. from Dr. Tuttle — The kidney pain was slowly passing when he left but I had a most distressing headache — at 7 I had a glass of Lactone Buttermilk — \+ survived a pretty dismal night.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 12, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mild day — overcast much of the time — I still had headache \+ temperature — so, did not get up — I kept in my darkened room \+ bed till about 4 — then up — very shaky — I wrapped myself in rugs \+ lounged on a long wicker seat till near 7 — then buttermilk \+ rice \+ again in my rugs \+ lounge till 10 — feeling uneasy all the time — When I got up to go to bed a stomach spasm came on that lasted for more than half an hour — no trace of kidney pain or soreness. I took some Paregoric — then to bed \+ presently fell asleep — at 3 A.m. I took my temp. 101¾° — a degree \+ 4/5 above normal — I wonder if I have been irrigating too much — Dr Tuttle warned me ago. Faking more water than my blood vessels could comfortably absorb \+ carry.
