## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 3, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Woke up feeling fairly well after a nappy night — that is I did not have any continuous restful sleep. For my bkfst. I had two soft boiled eggs \+ part of a cup of very weak coffee. In less than an hour I began to feel in slight form, some of the dreaded pain of the day before yesterday. When Dr. Tuttle came at about 11 the pain was severe \+ I begged him to begin treatment at once. He gave me some hot drougs \+ at to 3 mustard poultice — It took hold slowly — I could take no lunch — When Dr. T. came about 3 in the afternoon I begged for a hypodermic. He gave it to me, also a dose of Paregoric — In about an hour, after a few whiffs of chloroform I got partial relief \+ fell asleep. So far the pain had been very distressing but not so severe or long continued as on Wed. After troublous sleep I awoke \+ found pain less \+ I was in charge for the night of Miss Malone, Special, about midnight I was free of pain, but still very sore \+ could move myself in bed. From that time on it was constant improvement — I semi-conscious most of the time.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 4, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast part of the time but during the day a fair share of sunshine \+ fresh air came in at my window. I had grateful relief from pain thro'out the day \+ was on a light diet of Rice gruel strained — the routine visits of doctors \+ nurses were made \+ miss MacClive read aloud to me a considerable portion of the Des\' lists by Wm. J. Locke — Dr. T. came to me two or three or more time — I do not want for professional attention — In the afternoon I had long calls from Chas. \+ from Jim who came from Ansonia \+ who is now on his way to Hudson to visit Lloyd \+ols\' aspele. For my eve'g meal I had an egg on Toast. Soon after that trouble commenced in stomach \+ progressed rapidly — Dr. Tuttle \+ Roome saw me then — I hoped that the attack w\. be light \+ was given 30 grs of Soda bi-carb. in hot water. Relief for a little while \+ I fell asleep to awake before long with severer pain — more bi-carb. again relief \+ sleep. More pain — I begged for Paregoric \+ got it — very little relief — pain now persistent \+ severe — 3 o'clk am — I begged for Dr Roome \+ morphine inject. Routine \+ long wait before he came — He wished to evade responsibility — I was in an agony of pain — I begged — I got the morphia \+ a few whiffs of Chloroform — Then came relief \+ sleep.
