## Left Page

### Mon. June 7, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still charming weather - rather cooler - Up early \+ taking some crackers with me, had a delightful walk before bkfs. After that for some time with Katie - then packed my belongings - At 12 went to Ralph's room expecting to meet him there - waited till 12.30 - then out, telegraphed to Kate Leonard \+ did a little shopping - back to R's room at 1 - no signs of him - then ret. to the Elkins \+ had lunch - Hack came a little after 3 - to catch 3.51 train loads of time - on my way down stopped at R's room, but everything was as I left it - my mem. on table as I left it - evidently nobody had been there - then on to Station - train 17 m. late - Parker car very hot, crowded \+ stuffy - to N.Y. without incident - still late but I got home in time for dinner - Eve'g. writing checks notes \+ Reading.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 8, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fairly pleasant day, but with threats of change to the weather wise - Bkfs - before 8 - to studio at about 10 - Douglass' men at work painting outside wood \+ iron work. It was after 1 o'clk. when I left after a mornings work with my collection of die p[illegible]. Home to lunch - After that busied myself till 4 o'clk - then to Walter Cook's Office. 3 W.29.'S. - To meeting of Directors of the Ireland Real Estate B. to consider a complication that has suddenly developed \+ that promises lots of trouble - Under the new lease of the Winburg dock property to the N.Y. New Haven \+ Hartford Co. that Co. had commenced the preliminaries necessary to the construction of a dock - The commissioner would not grant permission stating that the N.H.R. Co. did not own the land under water \+ that the lot on land was improperly described \+ bounded in the deed - On investigation it is found that there was ambiguity in the deed's description \+ that there had been carelessness years ago in not taking necessary action to secure title under water - The matter has been put in charge of Title Guarantee Co. to straighten out previous surveys \+ transfers \+ appear to the state to give title to land under water - The R.R. Co's lawyer threatens cancellation of lease - Wearing some litigation may be before us. Home to dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g went to Charles \+ met Lizzie \+ Jessie Smillie there. C. gave us an exhib. of the possibilities of his new Pianola \+ then an exhib. of Lantern slides - I left soon after 10 o'clk - Home \+ to bed.
