## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 16, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A stormy day, cold wind \+ rain — some sleep between 5 \+ 7 — at 8 o'clk. my temp was but 1/5° above normal — a surprise to me considering the misery of the night passed — but altho' normal in temp. I was too weak to get up \+ there was nothing to invite in the state of the weather — most of the m'f was spent in regaining the sleep that was lost last night. The afternoon was spent loafing in bed with Sundays Tribune — It was about 6 o'clk. when I got up, dressed \+ went down to the big room where I spent the evng in an easy chair in front of the fire, part of the time doing some reading. To bed soon after 9.

Ralph sent a telegram, not by my desire, early in the m'f, to D'. Tuttle asking him to come up as soon as possible on Mr. Hardenbergs account — she has heard that D'. Tis coming \+ is very anxious to see him — I think she must be near her end.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 17, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Unexpectedly pleasant m'f. The sun shone out till towards noon when a veil came that thickened till rain came at about 5 o'clk growing steadily heavier into the night — a telegram from D'. Tuttle came before I got up stating that he w'. start from N'Y. this eve'g on 9.3° train — Spencer Nye was ordered to send a team to West Port to meet him at 6 a.m. to-morrow — I had a quiet serious talk with Hattie about the domestic situation — I said that I was unable to direct matters \+ I wished her to take directions from Mrs. Throop's in matters pertaining to the dining room \+ chamber work — this she promised to do. Old Kate is to be left free to rage \+ range in her kitchen. I wrote a note for D'. Tuttle for the Nye driver to take to him — Early in the afternoon I was fixed in my chair, well shielded by blankets, \+ sat there till the rain began, about 5., reading Josephine Daskam — then my stomach began to feel uncomfortable \+ I retired to the large room where, as the discomfort increased I took a dose of paregoric. The trouble abated. At 5.30 Nye's driver \+ team arr'd — got Nye's note \+ left in a heavy down-pour. — Eve'g reading \+ family confab till after 10 — then to bed. Heavy rain all night.
