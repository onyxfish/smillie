## Left Page

### Sun. March 21, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day but I did not get up till noon. I had an uncomfortable night till about 4 Am. Then the discomfort or pain in stomach was so much modi fied that I had some quiet sleep. At 9 I had a bowl of gruel as I sat up in bed — then I called up D. Tuttle on the phone but I do not think that he knows any more than I do as to what the specific trouble in my stomach is — After dressing I went out into the street for some air \+ exercise — felt very groggy — Dinner at 2 — a mess that I ate in great trepidation — Later Jim \+ I went out to Riverside Park \+ had a half hours stroll — then I felt that I had had enough \+ we returned to the house. — At 5.30 Charl came for half an hour — Jim \+ I had supper before 7 — Well he went out for a call \+ I wrote to Clarence Smith about Kate Leonard's case She holds Mary Mooney's Savings bank book \+ a blank check signed by Mary — of course she cannot get any thing on that now — Mary owed Kate an amount equal to the deposit in bank — $180.00 —

## Right Page

### Mon. March 22, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day but windy \+ dirty in the streets. To-day I feel quite recovered from the indisposition that made me feel so wretched Sat. \+ Sunday. The ways of the stumjack are fast finding out. To studio soon after 10. Budworth's men came \+ took the difting of the clorids to Geo. Haarn — I went on with my work with my prints till 12.30 — then to 2d Nat. Bank — Home to lunch before 2 — To studio again soon after 3 \+ worked till 5 — then home \+ rested till 6.30 — dinner — Evely as usual — newspaper \+ some writing.
