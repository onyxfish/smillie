## Left Page

### Fri. April 16, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant day — on my way to studio I called on Dr. Tuttle in 49th St. \+ described to him the sad condition of my machinery while on the Washington trip. — It seemed to be amusing to him — quite otherwise to me. — Then I walked down to The Century — 43d St. \+ spent some time looking at the collection of J. B. Bristol paintings — Then to Miller's shop in 40th S. Then to studio — left there before 1 — home to lunch — gave way again to the drowsy, heavy feeling \+ slept — wrote a joy letter to Mr. Yale — Another walk on the drive — made enquiries at some Apartment houses for rooms. It was near 7 when I got home — dinner alone as Jim dined with Miss [illegible] I had a very quiet eveg most of the time writing.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 17, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant spring-like day. — on my way to studio stopped at The Century \+ wrote a note to W. Geo. F. Kunz. 49 W. 57th St. Enquiring for information concerning the substitution of a reduced in size process-plate to take the place of the original mezzo-tint, after an example of jade, by me, among the illustrations of Bishop's monumental work on jade. — (That sentence is inexcusably clumsy but by a little mental effort it may be understandable.
After 10 when I got to studio — worked over the painting that I laid in some time ago of Cathedral at Chartres from the River — with scumbling \+ glazing preparatory to a repainting. Home to lunch at 1.30 accomplished little in the afternoon because of spring fever or something akin to it. Dinner with Jim at 6.30 — Eveg he went out — nothing to record for the eveg.
