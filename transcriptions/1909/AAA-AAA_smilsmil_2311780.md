## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 5, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some rain in the early m't \- later plenty of sunshine with cool breezes. I got some refreshing sleels before 9 o'clk \- fever all gone I think. When Dr. Turtle cut my rations down to 2 drachms of albumen every two hours \- I felt very much delpressed \+ the day lagged miserably but I got thro' it without any severe irritation. The general depression was caused I think by weakness \+ soreness in the lumbar region \+ about the right kidney. I got thro' the night comfortably without the attention of a special night nurse.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 6, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Decoration Day \-

Awake early \+, as usual, without pain \+ fairly cheerful \- The day, from all accounts is delightful cool \+ brilliant \- I got thro' my usual morning duties but was unhappy over my rations of Albumen \+ sugar of milk \- it was made less objectionable by combining it with some broth, beef or chicken \- Stomach pain came on gradually after 10 o'clk \- when it got to the stage of real pain I sent for Dr. Roome \+ by Chlorofom, gently administration istered \- so that I was not the least bit unconscious, but in less than 15 m. the pain had subsided. This was very satisfactory to me as I dread a Morphine habit \- I asked for Horlick's malted milk \+ got it \- much of the afternoon I lay in a doze \+ Miss McClive read aloud to me I did not understand it being too dopey \- Later in the afternoon Stomach pain came on, Dr. Roome is very busy man, he has 25 Typhoid cases to look after \+ it was not till 7 o'clk that he could come to me. He gave me a very light injection of Morphia, Magandra Solution, the lightest that I have yet had \+ soon I had relief. Charles came in for a few minutes \+ later Jim came in \- on his way from Huds on \- only for a few moments \- Miss Benjamin was assigned to me a night nurse \- I did not sleep much but had lots of cat naps.
