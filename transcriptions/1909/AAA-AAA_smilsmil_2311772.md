## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 20, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dr. Tuttle left.

A fitful day - hardly pleasant on the whole some sunshine + some showers with some distant thunder. I had my bkfst. in my room + came down to large room about 10 o'clk. I counted with the Dr. the cash cost of his trip from Wlf. city + return - it amounted to $32.00 for which I gave him my check telling that his charge for professional services he could put in his regular bill - I sat in big chair on east piazza for some time & Had a rest before dinner + then had a fine dinner of broiled chicken + pease + a glass of lemonade with a small stick in it. It went down very satisfactorily + gave no trouble till 5 o'clk - then I had a little pain in stomach - the smallest one yet - but I went to my bed with hot-water bag + there the Dr. left me when he started for home before 6 o'clk - Earl went home at 3 o'clk - promising to be back tomorrow p.m. I got up + had supper before 7 - sat with family + read.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 21, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The worst day yet - a chill.

Pleasant mfg + evng., but cold + cloudy between. I got up feeling about as usual + after bkfst. in my room was interviewed by Jas. Holt Canister + paid him $47 for 69 loads of stone - at the rate of $4" per day - paid Peary Washburn $24 for help at the ledge + Mr. D. Beade $17.25 for ledge rock at the rate of 25¢ a load. My troubles commenced at 3 o'clk. A.M. with kidney pain - that subsided by 9 o'clk - + I was tolerably comfortable till about 1 o'clk when trouble in the stomach began. Mrs (Dr.) Janeway called + I had a short chat with her - then I had a glass of hot lemonade with a little whiskey + paregoric in it — that eased the trouble so that before 3 I had some dinner prepared for me - but before I could eat it a chill came on + I got to bed with hot water bags at my feet + each side of me - The chill was a severe one - after that came the fever that flared up to 105°. At that time I did not know much of what was going on about me. Ralph + Bessie had ret'd from a walk to the top of Mr. Hopkins - he gave me 3 weet Sp's? of Nitre + in 40 m. temp. had fallen to 103¾°. Then he gave me 5 grs of Quinine + after awhile I fell asleep - at 10 o'clk I was comparatively comfortable - took a cupful of chicken broth — then went to sleep + had a very quiet night.

There was an unusual discharge of pus.
