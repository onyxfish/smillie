## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 12, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very cool day \+ cloudy much of the time \- at 1 o'clk. this m'g I was waked by a stomach pain \- it was not very severe \+ did not last long but it demoralized me\- I got up before 7 \+ with pleasant fire in my bed-room commenced the writing of a letter to Jim suggesting to him the idea of a poultry farm as a means of earning a living \- my b'f's. was brought to me \- later I went to south piazza \+ continued writing the letter to Jim \- came in at noon as I felt uncomfortable \+ went to my bed \- After 1 o'clk had a bowl of strong beef tea \+ after 2 o'clk had to turn into bed with obstinate stomach pain which kept me there till about 4\- I should have written that before 1 o'clk. I rec'd. a letter from Dr. Tuttle to which I made immediate reply urging him to come here for a few days outing \- From 4 I loafed about free.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 13, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- woke early feeling better than usual Before getting up I finished letter to Jim, suggesting that he think very seriously of Chicken farming. At the breakfast table I read the letter to the family \+ it was discussed \- Ralph does not approve \- thinks it undignified \- I spent most of the day on piazzas in rugs on lounging chairs About noon I went down the road to see the wall that the men were building \- That was about all the moving about I did for the day \- Afternoon \+ eveng in my big chair on north piazza feeling very hopeful \- Mr. \+ Mrs. Maghee came over in the eveng \- Mr. M \- engaged me in active conversation \+ tired me out \- To bed after 9 o'clk.
