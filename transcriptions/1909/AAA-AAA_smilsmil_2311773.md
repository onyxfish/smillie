## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 22, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day lovely beyond description - my temp. on awaking was about a degree below normal. After bkfs., while yet in bed, I dictated to Ralph a letter to Edna Murphy in answer to hers asking for herself \+ Henry an invitation to visit The Ark. - The impossibility of it under existing conditions was frankly stated in such way as I hope will not offend. - Later I got up, went down stairs \+ was rolled in my rugs in bigchair on south piazza \+ there I remained till after 3 o'clk - there I dictated to Ralph a letter to D. Tuttle discribing yesterday's experiences. Before 4, feeling tired, I went to my room \+ had a restful nap, coming down stairs at 6 when I had some tea — Later I had chicken broth — Ev'g with the others before the fire \+ to bed at 9.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 23, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, got up \+ about middle forn'g went down to South piazza, rolled in rugs. wrote a letter to D. Tuttle telling him that I had determined to go down to hospital before the end of the week — after writing the letter pain in Kidney commenced \+ great discomfort in stomach \+ bowels - for the first time since coming here I felt really sick — The neuralgia \+ Kidney pain did not affect me that way — Temp 101°. I got thro' the night fairly well.
