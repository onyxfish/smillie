## Left Page

### Fri. April 2, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant m\[illegible\]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m\+ \- clouding over later. an attempt at rain in the eve\+ \- To studio about 10.30 after stopping at Chas. Collins' for a few minutes to talk about the Academy meeting \- the most of the m\+ was spent in making design \+ working drawings for a case, or closet in which my p\[illegible\] \+ prints can be properly kept \- Home to lunch with Ralph before 2\. \- Part of P.M. working on design or plan for closet \- Ralph brought Bronson, his room mate, home to dinner Eve\+ , B\- \+ Jim went off together to some show \- R\- remained at home to nurse a troublesome throat \- I read \+ wrote some notes.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 3, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day \- a blk. fog. for a time in the m\+ suggestive of London weather. There was rain, but not heavy \- After bkfst I completed the scale drawings for a closet in which to keep my etching p\[illegible\]. Then to 34th St \+ Fifth Ave\- McCutcheon's to get help left there some days ago to be marked \- Then to W.S. Miller's, carpenter \- 40th St\- \+ left there the drawings for closet \- Then to studio \- left there at 12.30 \+ home to lunch \- alone \- Ralph went to meet Jim at Yale Club \- Afternoon I spent over my accts. \- Eve\+ The boys dined with Hugh Crowell \+ I dined alone. Eve\+ I went to Century Monthly meeting \- hoping to meet a number of the N.A's \+ get echoes of last Wed N.A. meeting \- very few there \- Rec'd. a number of congratulations as one of "the seven". \- Home \+ to bed at midnight \- Jim stayed at a Turkish bath all night \- Ralph came home.
