## Left Page

### Fri. April 30, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty wet raw day - To studio at about 10 I had a difficult mornings work in getting my collected p/p. into proper groups \+ in cataloguing all work done since 1888 The date to which my work was catalogued. The work seems to be getting more complex as I advance with it - A clipping Bureau sent me a clipping from the Washington Star of Apr. 24th giving a surprisingly flattering notice of my gift to the Congressional library \+ my work \+ grade generally. Mr. Parsons must have given special facilities to the reporter. Home to lunch at 2 - on my lounge for some time with again something like neuralgia in the stomach - Dinner with Jim. Eve\+ wrote to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 1, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annie's Birthday. Jim went to Ansonia.

Disagreeable weather - wet, raw, cold free eve\+ when it cleared - moonlight - May day bills, dues, subscriptions etc detained me after b\[illegible\]. so that it was 11 oclk before I got to studio - continued work in assorting \+ cataloguing p/p. Home to lunch at 2 - Afternoon, just podging - some reading — Jim went to Ansonia, Conn. To meet an engagement at 3 o'clk. with managing man of a mftre working co. who offers him a situation - Without knowing just what is offered to Jim I doubt the wisdom of going away from home to a little mfg. town in the country - It seems to me to be only blind groping for he does not know what. I wish he could select some definite line of work - some one kind of business \+ then set himself to work to understand \+ follow that. I cannot understand the vagueness, indefiniteness of his purpose - I wd. Rather see him tie up with a wholesale grocer or a manufacturer of shoes or any other line of staple demand than to drag along as he is doing - A lonesome dinner - Eve\+ went to monthly meeting of Century - Interesting discussion concerning possible improvement in management of the club - a good deal of rambling talk representing divergent views, some of it interesting, some funny, but nothing leading to formation or definite expression of the sentiment of the meeting - I thought remarks by Cecily Sloan more lucid \+ practical than the others - I sat with Chas. Collins \+ Dr. Fagnani - Home at 12.
