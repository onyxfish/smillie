## Left Page

### Mon. April 26, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another fine day — Got up feeling very [illegible] — Calomel \+ Cit. of Mag. acted imperfectly. Temp. sub normal nearly 1°. After 10 I ventured out feeling groggy \+ much depressed over the peculiar attack of yesterday — I can no longer have any confidence in myself — I tumble from one hole into another with small interval between. I got to studio \+ was able to do a little work there in arranging my [illegible]. Phoned to Clarence Smith about Tax Com. hearing at 2.30 to-day. He said he w°. have it postponed. — Home to lunch before 2 — Then gave up a considerable portion of the afternoon to resting on my lounge — Then a saunter in Riverside Park. At 6.30 went to Charl's to dinner — Jim did not dine at home. Battalion drill — Kath. \+ Eliz. throop in New Haven. Mary, Charl \+ self had a quiet eve'g. turning over the prints in the portfolio that Father left him.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 27, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still fine weather — my improvement continues. I got to studio at about 10 \+ to work in arranging prints — I had to go to 42d St. to get more fine wrapping paper — then to Douglas's in Lex. Ave. \+ got oil \+ obtain for my new cherry closet for prints. — I continued work at studio till near 1 o'clk — Then to Charl's \+ had lunch with Mary — back to studio soon after 2. \+ continued work till 4.30 — home at 5 — rest till dinner. Jim went out in the eve'g — is considering an offer of a situation with Ansonia Brass Co. at Ansonia, Conn. — I spent the eve'g with newspapers \+ writing.

Showers at night.
