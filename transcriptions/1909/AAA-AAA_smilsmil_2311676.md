## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 9, 1909

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day — Rather cold in the m't. — snow at night — to studio at 10 \+ to work on 10×14 panel "Chartres Cathedral from the River" — I made a very free sketch in semi-transparent color with a drying vehicle of Siccatif \+ turpentine — This will serve crudely as a key when I beginning the painting moil — I prepared my lunch in the studio \+ in the afternoon I worked among my prints getting them assorted, properly wrapped to protect from dust \+ marked it was after 5 when I got home — Dinner — Every alone \+ got rid of the time without accomplishing anything —

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 10, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day — rain \+ wind in the m't — about noon there was a balmy change with bits of sunshine — almost like April — about 2 o'clk. a small hurricane came across from New-Jersey \+ struck the city — two or three deaths resulting — I got up at 8 in bad shape — all functions more or less disturbed — uncomfortable to the extent of pain almost \+ discouraged — I drove myself to work arranging prints (189 photos) in one of the two Albums for the boys — I took advantage of the mid-day lull in weather conditions \+ went out on Riverside — but the effort was too much \+ I was glad to get back — an invalid's lunch — spent the afternoon on my lounge — no dinner — at 7 I asked Jim to 'phone to Dr. Tuttle — he came before 9 — could find nothing definitely wrong — good pulse — temp. \+ color — I took gruel while he jollied me — We spent some time looking over mounted prints from my last summer's work \+ I determined to make a struggle for a "fresh halt" \+ move on. then to bed.
