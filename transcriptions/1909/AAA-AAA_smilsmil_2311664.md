## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 12, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, disagreeable day - I got up \+ had bkfo. at 8 but was obliged to succumb to a headache more violent than any that I have had for a long time - my discomfort is unusual in its character, seeming to be a dumb balking of my digestive machinery- These disabling attacks are very depressing to me, - I know of no way of avoiding them - They seem to be the ever growing disabilities of age \+ a warning that it will be impossible for me to get even a small part of the work finished that I have planned to do before the final summons - I grieve over the quantities of unfinished work of all kinds that I must leave behind, some of which, I dream, might be worth saving.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 13, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunshine in the m'f. overcast afternoon at 4.30 A.m. I took Castor oil \+ responded to demands at 8 o'clk \+ thereafter - The headache of yesterday is gone but there is powerful weakness in the machinery under the belt - I breakfasted at 10 \+ hoped to get to the studio for a short day but did not have legs enough for the journey - I conclude that it would be wiser \+ safer to take another day off" as this such loafing is very depressing to me - while the sun was shining I made some experiments in printing from some negatives on Aristo paper without success - I just loafed in discontent -
