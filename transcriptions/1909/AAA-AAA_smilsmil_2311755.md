## Left Page

### Sat. July 17, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fitful day — quite cool — breezy — several showers — I had a restful night but felt very weak in the legs when I got up — had a nap in my chair after bkfst. Then wrote a letter to Dr. Tuttle giving him an acct of my various seizures beginning with June 25 — at New Haven \+ asking his opinion of the collection — Dinner soon after 1 o'clk — when I was conscious of an improvement in my general feeling — the improvement continued \+ I was able to take considerable exercise out of doors without discomfort before evg. I had my nap before 6 o'clk — The McKays — Mr. McK. \+ his daughter called \+ delayed Tea Time till near 7 — Evg. before fire gossiping.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 18, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright mf followed by a day of showers — a very heavy one, with thunder, about the middle of the day — my experiences commenced at 4.30 A.M. when I woke with pain in my right side. I commenced taking notes at once. found my temp. to be 97 3/5 — pulse weak at 72 — The pain increased \+ so did my temp. but it did not get above 101½ pulse 88 — By 8.30 I was able to communicate with the outside world, had a mustard poultice made \+ got in on by 9 o'clk — It soon got to work — The pain was lost under the burn of the mustard — At 1 o'clk. I had a light dinner — Temp. fell to a little below normal — then rose to 101 — but at no time was there any chill — By evg. I was without pain except for the burning but I feel languid, depressed \+ entirely without will power — I had a glass of buttermilk \+ passed the evg. in half sleeping.
