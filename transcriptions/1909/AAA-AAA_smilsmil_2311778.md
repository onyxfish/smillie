## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 1, 1909

**Weather:** Overcast mt. clearing later  **Temp:** [illegible]

Big pain.

Overcast mt. clearing later. I enjoyed a very simple b\+b\- \+ in due time after it, altho there was some objection on part of stomach, I had a good bath \- objection grew stronger as day advanced \- \+ by \+by I got started on as big a pain \+long continued as I had ever had in my long experience of Pain \- The doctors did not seem to be in much of a hurry \- I was given Carminatives twice at long intervals without effect \- during most of the afternoon I lay with the tension of pain growing till the abdominal muscles \+ the stomach were from \+hard to the touch \- I had to wait for Dr Tuttle who came in late \- It was torture \- I begged for morphia injection \- a weak mustard plaster had been applied over the stomach \- later a flax seed poultice replaced it having no effect. At last Dr. Tuttle ordered the injection \- I had three at intervals of half an hour or so \- in all about a 1/4 of a grain. It was getting late in the day before I began to feel any relief \- when the pain subsided Dr. Tuttle then gave me whiffs of chloroform, not enough to produce insensibility \- at last I fell asleep. Miss Yofret was with me during the night as a special nurse \- it was a long time before I had quieted down to a natural sleep \- that came before morning \-

Jessie Smillie arrived to-night from her vacation on the coast of Maine.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 2, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day so far as I could see \- I spent it in a blissful sense of absence of pain \- a mild form of Nirvana \- no physical body to vex me \- no mental care to disturb me. It was surprising to me that after being racked by such pain in a few hours afterwards the parts affected were not sore or tender to the touch. Dr. Tuttle was pleased of course but I am sure that my case puzzles him.
