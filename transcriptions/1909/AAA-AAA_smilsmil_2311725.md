## Left Page

### Tues. May 18, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still chilly weather + overcast. I got to studio at 10 + made considerable progress in getting things into their proper places but I'm an awful dawdler — I cannot do any thing with snap + energy. The mess I'm in at the studio could easily have been cleared up in one day. But, confronted by confusion I am powerless. After 12 I went to the Savings Bank on Union Sq. where Jim has an acct + I had the accrued int. added while I waited — Then walked up to 2d. Nat'l, 28 S. 45 Ave — + home to lunch at 2. After 3 I ret'd. to studio + was there till 5 — I took Priscilla Poor's etching "The Road to the Lake". to the Exp. + sent it to her. Home before 6. Dinr. Eve'g. I got letters from Jim + Ralph — I wrote to Jim + worked awhile at balancing bank acct.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 19, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded m'g but clearing delightfully. To studio at 10 — there till near 1 o'clk — To little purpose — wrote a short note to H.R. Butler — Home to lunch before 2 o'clk — Spent most of the afternoon in Riverside Park — Dinner solus at 6.30 — Eve'g got along letter from Jim describing the mill he is in + the work that is done there — He has not been assigned to any special duties but says he looks around + asks questions — I got a letter from W. Herbert Putnam — in reply to my letter to Mr. Parsons + acknowledging the receipt of ph. from Jade plate + the Sauders Rey Mission plate. — The letter is very cordial + assuring me that if I send, as I proposed, a selection of 25 or 30 ph. from my engr. + etched work, they shall be kept together in one portfolio — the James D. Smillie collection — all of which is very gratifying to me.
