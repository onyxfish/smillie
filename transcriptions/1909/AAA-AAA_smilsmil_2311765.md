## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 6, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - feeling much better altho' so weak that it was diffcuet to get about - Corwin Crawford came \+ was about most of the day - I spent some time with him - Ralph \+ I went over to the Van Horn's \+ spent an hour or more there - on my return I had a rest before dinner. In the afternoon I wandered some time in the woods - Hoet drew some stone but I do not know how much of the day he gave to my work. Ev'f all the young people went over to a "Camp-fire" at Maghee's.

In the afternoon I passed [illegible] quite freely - this gives me hope that the next attack of Kidney disturbance may not come at such short interval of time as the last

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 7, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The family went off for a day's picnic

Charming day, except that it was far too warm. The Ther. on north piazza at 4 p.m. stood at 84° with strong warm wind. Before 3 a.m. I was waked by Kidney pain - Capsolin failed \+ at 3.30 I made a mustard plaster \+ applied it - after a couple of hours the burning of the mustard smothered the pain. There was an outpouring of urine as in the last attack free of pus till some hours after the attack - but there seemed to be no febrile disturbance at the time. In the afternoon there was a slight rise. I did not get up till afternoon - had my bkft. \+ dinner in my room - after that I just loafed about, weak \+ disconsolate, unable to determine whether to fight it out here or give up, go to the city \+ get entrance to the hospital. The family got back from their picnic about 6 o'clk — earlier than expected. Well pleased \+ tired - Dinner after 7 - a short evening \+ then to bed.
