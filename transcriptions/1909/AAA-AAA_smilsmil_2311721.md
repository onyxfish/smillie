## Left Page

### Mon. May 10, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely m't though to breezy to be pleasant in the dry dusty streets — Some Rain in the afternoon — To studio after 10 \+ till 1 o'clk worked at taking pf. from a lot of frames that were piled away in a closet and putting them with the assorted collection of pf. I worked under every disadvantage of discomfort in all parts, as it seemed to me, of head \+ body. Home soon after 1 \+ had a bowl of clam broth \+ very soon after that went to bed \+ stayed there till near 6 o'clk. with dismal headache — At 6.30 took a little nourishment \+ Jim at dinner — Eve\+ he went to Armory for some of his togs — my head feeling relieved I podged about till bed time.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 11, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day — very cool — Strong west wind. To studio at 10 \+ continued my work with my prints but much retarded by my weakness \+ the stomach discomfort that makes movement painful — I gave up before 1 o'clk — Then went home \+ had some lunch — After that I lay down upon my bed \+ was there till 5 o'clk — Then I went out to Riverside Park \+ in spite of the stiff wind I kept about till 6 o'clk — Then home \+ had dinner with Jim at 6.30 — Eve\+ Jim went out to make calls — I wrote a letter to Scott Brown \+ read till after 10 — Then to bed.
