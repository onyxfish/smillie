## Left Page

### Wed. July 7, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day, but rather too cool for my comfort \- Ther. in my window marked 52°. I got up at 5 \- lighted my fire \- opened the blinds \+ let in the warming sunshine \- the back to bed \+ had a very pleasant time till 7 o'clk \- Then up \+ a good scrub \- B'kf's\- at 8 \- wrote to Geo. Chas. \+ Jim. Did not feel equal to exercise \- discomfort in kidney commenced before dinner at 1 \+ increased so that at 3 o'clk. I went to bed with a hot-water bag \- I drank hot water freely \+ took Urotropin \+ Benzoate of Soda \- Some fever \+ some real pain that did not wear off till well into the night \- I finally turned in for a ee night before 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 8, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Ralph's Birthday.*

Clear, high wind \+ rather more than cool except in the sunshine \- my kidney discomfort seems to have passed for the time but I am very weak \+ my digestions are threatening a strike \- There was little I could do but loaf about \+ read \- At dinner Katie Boadley had decked the fire-place in festive fashion in honor of Ralph's 22d birth-day \- I spent a large part of the afternoon reading \- Every enjoying pine \+ chatting \- After the others had gone to bed a spasm of stomach pain came on that was quite severe \+ lasted perhaps an hour \+ more without sign of letting up \- Then I took a dose of paregoric \+ in a few minutes had peace \- then to bed with hot-water bag.
