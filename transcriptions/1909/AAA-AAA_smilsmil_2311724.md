## Left Page

### Sun. May 16, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncertain weather in the early m't but clearing later it was sunny mild \+ ener-vating — at 6 p.m. there was a light shower \+ a Rapid change in temp. Ther. falling with chill easterly breeze — Up before 7 \+ had my scrub \+ back to bed for an hour or more. B'kf't. at about 9 — loafed with newspaper \+ book till noon — Then out to Riverside Park \+ loafed on benches there till 2 — Then in \+ had my lonely dinner — Then, yielding to that "that tired feeling" I gave myself up to sleep for nearly two hours — A little before 6 o'clk. I called on Miss Kate Hillard. at 111 W. 82d. St. where there the rain came. I borrowed an umbrella — home before 7 — no eve'g meal wrote to Clarence Smith \+ sent him check in pay'm't. of his ch'p. ag't Kate Leonard for collecting Mary Mooney's bank acc't. At 8.30 went to Chas's He has a new Pianola — Remarkable improvements over old one — can be tricked into Representing very closely the hand-made article of piano playing — Chas. is Rapidly getting the hang of it. Home at 11 \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 17, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant, without sunshine, decidedly cool with brisk east wind — B'kf't. soon after 8 o'clk. About 10 o'clk. Took Miss Hillard's umbrella back to her then to David Clark's Sons \+ bought Roses \+ Carnations \+ left them at 133 E. 36th. St. for Miss Mary Collins who to-day celebrates her 81st birthday — Then to studio \+ to-work trying to Reduce the disorder that is Rampant there — Ernest D. Roth came in \+ we talked for an hour or more about printing — At 12.30 I went to Second Nat'l. Bank — got cash for the check sent to Kate Leonard \+ made deposit on my own acc't. \+ home to lunch at 1.30 Afternoon Re't. again to studio \+ worked there till near 6 — Home in time for a lonesome dinner — Jim's absence seems to make a hole in every part of the apartment since — Eve'g. my newspaper \+ some writing kept me busy till bed time.
