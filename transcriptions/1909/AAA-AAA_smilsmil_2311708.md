## Left Page

### Wed. April 14, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Home again —
Ralph went on to New Haven.

A down pour of rain, with wind, all day. We had our bkfts. at about 8 — settled bills \+ left hotel in a hack at 9.20 — to Union Station \+ got away on the 10 o'clk. Exps. for N.Y. The ride was comfortable \+ depressingly uninteresting — For the first time since reaching Washington I was free of pain — That was almost as good as sunshine — We had a reasonably good lunch at 1.30 — Due at N.Y. at 3 o'clk — we got in at about 20 min. late — \+ to the house at about 4.30 — I had a good rest — \+ dinner with Jim \+ Ralph at 6.30 — At 7.15 J. \+ R. went to Gd. Cent. Stn. \+ R— took 8 o'clk train to New Haven — Soon after 8 I went to N.A. of D. meeting — Rode up in Amsterdam Ave car with Bolton \+ Frank Jones \+ other N.A.'s — The meeting was to elect Associates \+ Hanging \+ Selection Comttes. Very full meeting, N.A.'s \+ A.N.A.'s — I sat with Dav. C. French — The meeting did not come to order till after 9 — As the balloting was uninteresting \+ the room was full of smoke \+ very stuffy I soon slipped out \+ got home a little after 10.

Our Trip to Washington cost $107.25

## Right Page

### Thur. April 15, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day, weather gradually clearing till evef. I was late at bkfts. \+ felt very inert all day — I found some things to look after at home — a carpenter from Miller's among others — He was engaged upon general repairs from about 10 o'clk, till 5. I strolled for some time on Riverside Drive before lunch but it was an apathetic effort — part of the afternoon I slept — couldn't help it — averse to effort of any kind, mental or physical — A long saunter on the Drive again — Dinner with Jim — Evef he took his friend Boardman to Suptr. of M.A. of D. I went down to Charl's \+ spent the evef there. Smema Bourne there —

I recd. a pleasant letter from Mrs. d. M. Yale — written at the New Willard Hotel — arrived there just after we left — She informed me that her daughter is with her in good health, consequently my condolences were a little "previous" — "The lady who died was Dr. Yale's sister. Dr. Y's father was also Leroy M. Yale \+ a Dr — hence my error.
