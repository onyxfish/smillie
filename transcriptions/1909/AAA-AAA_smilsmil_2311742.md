## Left Page

### Mon. June 21, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An excessively hot day — I don't know how far the Ther. got up among the 80's — It was very oppressive — Bkfo. at 8.30 — to studio before 10 \+ worked till near 1 o'clk — clearing up in all sorts of ways — Then to Charles \+ had lunch — Bessie used up by sick headache — At 3 o'clk. with Ralph went to Wellesley — He soon left \+ I podged about till 6 — then ret'd to Charles — dinner at 7 — Eve'g. "gabbin" —

## Right Page

### Tues. June 22, 1909

Yale — Princeton Ball game —

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another exceedingly hot \+ humid day with threats of heavy showers in the afternoon which proved to be very light — not enough to drive the crowd away from the ball-ground. Ralph went with me to studio after 10 \+ did the heavy work in taking down, switching \+ folding the curtains there — I had a spasm of pain in region of stomach like others that I have had within the past fortnight — it lasted, like the others for about 20 m. To 242 38th St. at 1 o'clk \+ had lunch — At 2\. we started (R.\+S.) for the league grounds at 167\. St. Subway train jammed full — Had excellent Grand Stand seats — immense throng — anywhere from 10 to 15000 in spite of threatening rain — Sprinkling when we got to the grounds — very little fuss at any time \+ the blessed rain brought relief — Game called at 3.40 \+ it went on mercilessly till 6\. It was desperately contested \+ at the end all of the players were evidently fagged — Yale got two runs in the first inning — Princeton got 1 in each following — 3.4.4 — after that, till the 9th it was a string of 0's for both teams — The tension was intense — In the 9th Yale made a most exciting rally \+ made 3 runs — that took the heart out of Princeton who was unable to accomplish anything in the last half — The excitement was tremendous — there was but little singing but continuous cheering \+ racket. The crush at the subway station was greater than any I have been in since war times — We got to Charles before 7 — in ample time for dinner — Eve'g. very tired — tobed at 9 —
