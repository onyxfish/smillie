## Left Page

### Mon. July 5, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another day of delightful weather - Btfp before 9 - Rather a tiresome day to get rid of. All stores closed - streets singularly quiet for 4th of July \+ I did not feel strong enough for much exercise - I went out a little but for the most part hung about the house reading. At 1 o'clk - we had lunch at 2g. I wrote a letter to Charl explaining why we overstayed time originally set for leaving - also to bro. George \+ to Jim. It was about 7 when Ralph \+ I went to the Manhattain \+ had dinner - my meal being very simple. Then to 2g \+ remained till 9 when we shook the hospitable dust from our feet \+ went to the Grand Cent. Station \+ at once boarded our Train which started on time - 9.34 - I turned in soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 6, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouds \+ light Rain - Ther. from below 50° up - clearing after noon. We arr'd. at Westport on time at 6 o'clk - I had a reasonably comfortable night \+ got up feeling fairly fresh. By good luck there was a N.ye. Team there \+ before 6.30 we were on our way to E-Town - got to Kellogg's at 8 - had a poor btfp - \+ away at 9 - went by way of new Russia - several light showers - fairly good \+ interesting road till after passing the Huntsman's Home - from Yuba mill desolation by fire was complete \+ tragic - some passages in the gorge before reaching Chappel Pond were the embodiment of unlovely destruction - Got to The Ark in apparently good shape at 11.15 - The sun shone out \+ everything looked smiling \+ lovely - I had a nap before dinner at 1 o'clk. Afternoon I lounged about \+ had another nap. Eve'g meal at 6 - Eve'g chatting before a bright wood fire - To bed early.
