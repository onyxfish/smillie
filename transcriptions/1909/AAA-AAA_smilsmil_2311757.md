## Left Page

### Wed. July 21, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild \+ pleasant day — an off day for me as I feel very weak \+ generally uncomfortable I got up \+ had bkfst. with the others soon after 8 but after that lay about discouraged \+ listless — Crawford came with fly-screens for kitchen windows. I spent much of morning on north piazza on lounge wrapped in traveling rugs. Rec'd. letter from Dr. Tuttle. He advises me to give up \+ spend much time on my back. He also advises 15 or 20 grs. of strohpkin \+ as much more of Soda Benzoate every 24 hours — This is distressing to me as both of those drugs disturb my stomach \+ are, I think, the cause of much of my trouble there now — Young came to dinner \+ spent most of the p.m. singing — Katharine went to camp with the Maghees. I spent the afternoon \+ part of eve'g on north piazza rolled in rugs. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 22, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant m'g. Slightly overcast — but later weather conditions changed — the wind blew \+ moaned \+ before night there was some rain. Following D. T.'s advice I spent the m'g wrapped in rugs, on the north piazza — went down to dinner at 1 o'clk — ate very little \+ left the table with a big pain in my stomach — I got to bed at once — Took 1/4 gr. of morphine \+ suffered for more than half an hour — temp. got up to 101°. It was a dismal afternoon \+ eve'g for me with a hot-water bag for companion.
