## Left Page

### Sun. March 7, 1909

Ralph ret. to New-Haven —

Disagreeable day - wet + raw till evef. then it cleared mild + misty - Bkft. late - about 10 - The boys were up before 11 + went to church - I loafed about feeling rather uncomfortable in the region of the stomach in a way that makes me think some new trouble is brewing there - Dinner at 2 — After that Ralph + I had a turn with our photo-enlarging outfit - apparently getting fairly good results - About 5 Charl. came in + with him Ralph + I took a short walk in the twilight - Jim was out at tea time - Ralph + I had our supper together + he left before 8 to catch the 8.30 train to N.H. - I spent remainder of evef. reading -

## Right Page

### Mon. March 8, 1909

A wonderfully beautiful day - I was late + it was considerably after 10 when I got to studio - Confusion has been piling up there for some weeks past + I set to work to clear it up. Before going to studio I went to 2d. Nat. + drew some money - Stopped at Lock's for leather cushion - I collected a number of Hinshelwood + other pfs. + made pkg - Home to lunch at 2 - At 3.30 I walked over to Lenox Library + was there with Weitenkampf till near 6 - gave him some Hinshelwood pfs. + others by Pease, Dunnell \+ others - Walked home - getting in at about 6.30 - Jim had a friend to dine with him - Dorning by name - a bright pleasant fellow who is to sail for Antwerp in a few days -
