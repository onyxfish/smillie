## Left Page

### Sun. May 30, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudless brilliant day - mild with strong westerly breeze - Up after 7 - scrub, bkfst. at 9 - With newspaper till near 11 - Then the boys put in an appearance \+ before 12 we went out together. I have been more free from pain to day than at any time for more than a week past. We loafed about Riverside till 1.30, sometimes sauntering \+ at times basking on seats in the spring sunshine - The almost absence of pain made me very hopeful. Dinner at 2 - we chatted together till 4 - then out to Riverside again till after 6 - then in \+ the boys had something to eat - Eve'g they went to Charles \+ I, feeling that I had done as much - perhaps more, than was wise, remained at home \+ wrote a note to Catharine Elkin.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 31, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Boys left by 8 o'clk Train - Jim for Ansonia R.- for New Haven.

Very pleasant day - Ther. getting up to 75°. Bkfst. at 9 - The boys got up later - I saw a portion of Decoration Day Parade \+ got to studio before 11 - Engaged until 1.30 gathering all the Bank note die-pts that I have \+ sorting them in a general way preparatory to a careful classification. Home at 2 \+ met the boys going out - I had my lunch. My discomforts \+ hardships have been very discouraging all day - I can detect a change that to me seems like settling into a chronic condition - I wonder if I can get used to it \+ wear it without much external evidence - I read for awhile - rested awhile - then out to Riverside \+ wandered \+ loafed there till 6 o'clk. Then home - dinner a little earlier than usual to give the boys time to get their train at 8 o'clk - They left me at 7.30 — I loafed away the eve'g being in no condition to make proper use of it.
