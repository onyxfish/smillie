## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 4, 1909

**Weather:** Damp mild wt — clearing afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

Had W. Toffree on the phone — with reference to his tenant Sands in Sniffen court, next to Studio — T. says he will make good if Sand's does not, for Steam heat. To studio at 10 — that Jose Winton who has charge of the furnace had got the Ther. up to 80° — much to my discomfort. Ernest Roth came in \+ spent an hour with me. I got to work on Cedar, Moonlight — continued stomp \+ scraping — then used dry point — Home to lunch with Ralph at 2. at 3 we went down town to W. C. Cullen's — then by elevated to 23d St. Shopped for a book-case but did not find one to suit me — then to Electrical Supply Store \+ got fittings to put in hall, with flexible wire, so that I can see what is in the book-cases — S. Tikeman has sent home the last batch of bound books. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g the boys went to a theatre — I amused myself with bills \+ accts.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 5, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beast of a day — rain all day — much of the time in floods — I got to studio soon after 10 \+ worked a moonlight plate with dry-point \+ then took a print — it seems to be progressing in the right direction. It was 2.30 when I got home to lunch, decidedly under the weather — headache \+ fever — lying about. Unfit for anything — poor company for Ralph whose vacation ends this eve'g. He had planned to return to New-Haven to-night but gave it up on acct. of the storm. Jim dined at Mr. Villard's
