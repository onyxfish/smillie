## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 3, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day \- dark \- a fine snow filling the air that amounted to nothing when it fell on the ground. I went to Second Nat'l \+ fixed up my financial affairs there for the month \- Then to studio getting there after 11 \- I made preparations for taking a few phs from Yosemite plate to be used as frontispiece for the eight copies of my Yosemite article for Picturesque Am. \- many years ago \- printed on one side of the paper only \- I shall have them bound. Had lunch in studio \- At 3 went down town to Wm. C. Cullen's taking a full set of prints from my last summer photos, to be mounted on white boards \- also the large photos given to me by sister Kate \- Venus de Milo \+ the winged Victory \- to be mounted \- Home after 5 o'clk \- Dinner \- Eveng. alone.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 4, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull grey mg. that had cleared into a pleasant misty-like day \- The mg. looked so threatening when I got up that I decided not to go to the studio but at home to write letters \+ do other things that needed doing. I gave some time to writing a letter to Geo. Hearn \- After thanking him for the copies he sent me of Cat. of his pictures, foreign \+ Am. \+ his Ivory carvings I took up the matter of my old painting "The lifting of the Clouds" urging him to let me have it to clean \+ to paint over certain portions that have deadened because painted into a bitumen prattle he has refused this request before \- I wrote to Wm. Wilcox of Buffalo thanking her for her invitation to the wedding of her daughter Frances to Tom Crook \- lunch at home \- At about 3 o'clk. I walked to the Met. Museum \+ spent a couple of hours looking at the German pictures now there under the auspices of the German Govt. \- Many of the largest paintings are horribly aggressively bad \- the outcome of morbid \+ disordered minds \- I can understand it when a man here or there is degenerate or with diseased mind \- but I cannot understand it when such men \+ their works are honored by medals, prizes \+ positions as instructors in art institutions \- These pictures have been selected by men high in official German art \- some of them are from national collections \- the critical essay in the Catalogue prepared in Germany claims that that Nation, by this exhibit, demonstrates that, breaking all shackles of convention and tradition she has risen in new life to commanding heights. Some they are deplorable \+ desolating despite \- Franz von Stuck shows a corner of Hell \- Revolting picture. The most incomprehensible outreach is an immense work \- a Tryptich \- in distemper (properly enough) The Plague \- yet another of his works \- Head of a woman \- with black gloves \- is dangerously pleasing if you can get far enough away from it \+ half close your eyes \- but why go on \- there were good pictures there of course \- but these brutal atrocities blind your eyes to them. After seeing the pictures I had a pleasant case on Ab. Seely Kent \- then walked home \- Dinner \- Eveg., jimi dull night \-
