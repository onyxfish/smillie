## Left Page

### Tues. June 15, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - 10° cooler than yesterday - dryer & fresher - Up early, vkb. before 8 but it was 10.30 before I got to studio. Mary came said that at Gd. Cent. Office she was told that all parlor car seats on Friday mf. Train were taken - in distress - Told her I wd. go to office on my way up town & see what I could do. Made glue-flour paste very carefully & then as carefully mounted my already mounted photos of Venus Milo & Victory on heavy trunk board & got them in press. It took me till 1 oclk. hard work - then to Gd. Cent. & without difficulty bought parlor car seats to West Port for Friday mf. Then home - lunch 2 olk afternoon got trunk up from cellar for packing - went to Bdway near 36th St. & bought mileage ticket on D. &H - then to Charl's & delivered R.R.T car tickets to Katharine - Gordon Smillie Piere - we rode up town on subway. Dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g. had a promenade on Riverside - River smooth & reflective - very delightful - wrote to Estes & Moody - Keene - instructions

## Right Page

### Wed. June 16, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A few degrees pleasant warmer to-day - not oppressive except in subway. I went to bank before going to studio - thence walked up to Rogers, Peet & Co. & bought a summer hat - then to Clausen's & paid a bill of $3.00 then to Studio at 11. I made up a stiff glue paste - took the two photos that I mounted on trunk board yesterday out of press & then pasted manilla paper on the backs of them - That job kept me busy till 1 - then home & had lunch at 2. The old Fall clock has gone wrong again - The clock man came for it & took it away at 3 oclk. Busy packing things away - After 5 went to Grd. Central to get some train information then to Lucy Kane's 706 3d. Ave. - left instructions about her baggage - Home to dinner at 6.30 Eve'g. continued the work of packing things away for the summer.
