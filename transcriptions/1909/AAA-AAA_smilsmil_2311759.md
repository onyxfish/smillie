## Left Page

### Sun. July 25, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

miss Anthony arrived.

Unpropitious opening — promises of another stormy day but instead a lovely day followed. Had bkfs. in my bedroom — after that dressed — went down to large room \+ wrote to Dr. Tuttle describing the miseries of the past week. W. Young stayed to dinner. I had my dinner alone setting in the sunny window of the corner room. About 4 p.m miss Anthony arr. — driven up from Paradox Lake where she has been visiting with friends. She was not abroad by the Brooks \+ is here now on their invitation — a bright, happy looking young woman. Ev'g chatting before fire — later, a little moonlight roaming —

## Right Page

### Mon. July 26, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delicious m'f. I spent nearly all of it on the south piazza on one of the wicker lounges. The air was balmy with occasional strong breezes of exhilarating freshness. I wrote a letter to Dr. Tuttle giving him an acct of my physical condition during the past week. After Ease had taken it to the P.O. he ret. with one from the Dr. Chase has phoned to him that long ago I had had a malarial experience \+ so, the Dr. writes advising Quinine treatment. Dinner at 1.30. Then Ralph, miss A., Kath. \+ Eliz. went to Club Ho. \+ with Fred. Young went by stage out to lower pond to spend the afternoon \+ walk back. It was after 7 when they arr. for tea — I was threatened with a neuralgic attack — was very uncomfortable \+ apprehensive. I took Paragoric \+ got to bed with a hot bag — fortunately I escaped the attack \+ joined the others at about 9 — Young spent the ev'g with the girls out on the moonlit south piazza. He spent the m'f here singing most of the time. He is to start for home to-morrow m'f.
