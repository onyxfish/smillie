## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 8, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Much milder, a bright m'f. - clouded afternoon. To studio at 10. Mr. Sullivan there - she worked till 3 o'clk cleaning the place which needed it very much. Before noon I took my camera down Mad. Ave. \+ took three snap shots at the Met Life Ins. Tower, bringing in the Mad. Sq. Garden Tower the object being to show the melancholy belittling of the later for which I have great admiration. Then to 2° Nat'l. To studio \+ partook and lunch - worked till 4 o'clk. - then home. Dinner alone - Jim dined at the Cook's - to dance after it - I wrote to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 9, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \+ much milder. Domestic duties made me late — it was 11 when I got to studio. Then Mr. Polfree called \+ that took time. his tenant (Sands) in the garage adjoining studio is playing a pretty mean game trying to evade a part of the payment for steam that I am supplying for heating. The little work that I was able to accomplish injured to a considerable extent my moon light etching — a portion of the bold work in the middle ground proved to be shallow \+ to deepen it I laid a heavy ground - then dragged a point thro' the lines to open them - then bit with strong nitric which broke up the ground \+ injured the line. Dinner alone. - as Jim dined with his Regimental Co. - Well I went to Century — Annual Meeting — asked Geo. Haven Putnam if I might read portions of father's autobiography to him to get an opinion. He told me that he would rather have me send him the entire M.S. \+ let him read it in his own way which is exceedingly amiable \+ much more than I w'd dare ask of so busy a man. On my return home, altho' at midnight, I got out the M.S. \+ after looking over portions determined to cut it much before submitting it to Putnam.
