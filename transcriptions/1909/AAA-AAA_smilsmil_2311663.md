## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 10, 1909

**Weather:** Nasty  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nasty day - did not go out of the house light rain, mizzle \+ mied. - Up at about 8 - Bath \+ Shampoo - bkfst at 10 - a lot of time wasted over newspapers - Wrote a note to W. D. Sands enclosing form of agreement concerning steam heat - Dinner at 2 - After that tried to make myself believe that I wished to see the N. A. of D. Exbtn that closes this P.M. - I have not yet seen it - was unsuccessful - so, it closes without my approval - Loafing proved to be hard work under the conditions of the day -

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 11, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - to studio after 10 - on careful examination of moonlight plate I find that owing to the worn \+ rounded edges of the deeply bitten lines over which I laid a ground \+ thro which I ran a point, the acid had only destroyed the line \+ surface between lines - The remedy was necessarily heroic - I used Water of Ayr stone \+ rubbed all that portion of the plate down till I got a new surface with lines very much reduced - then I laid a strong ground \+ again re-entered all of the lines in preparation for an other biting by which I hope to bring it back to its original condition - I mixed fresh mordant \+ made preparation for carefully studied progress -
