## Left Page

### Mon. March 15, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine bright breezy day. on my way to studio stopped at Keppel's \+ we chinned together for some time while I looked at his exbn of 17th Cent. Etchers. To studio at 11. Began to gather from shelves \+ drawers, proofs of my work to make selections to mount for Museums. It was 2 o'clk. when I got home to lunch. Later I ret'd to studio \+ continued the work till 6 — then home \+ had dinner. Welf Jim went out with Hugh Coswell \+ I worked at enlarging photo. until after 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 16, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fair in the m'g — later, clouded with raw wind — To studio at 11 after stopping at W'm Miller's \+ having a long "gab" with him. I continued my work of yesterday which is practically "taking acct. of stock" of etching p'fs from my plates — I began with intention of making a few selections to mount for Public Library \+ the Nat'l. collection, at Washington but I find so much confusion, prints in so many different places, \+ p'fs from same plate scattered, that the best way will be to round the whole lot up \+ arrange what I have in an orderly manner — Home to lunch at 2. The domestic dep't. much upset. Mary Mooney is in a state of coma \+ supposed to be dying after an operation at the Eye \+ Ear Hospital. My afternoon was spent at home, not profitably — A walk between 5 \+ 6 o'clk — dinner — Eve'g went with Jim to St. Nicholas Rink \+ saw a Hockey game played between Co's J, \+ K. of the 7th Regt. — The first time that I had ever seen the game played — very interesting to me — Jim said it was an amateur game \+ was slow as compared with professional work. — I admired the quickness, dexterity \+ good humor of the players. Game stood 2 to 5 — Jim's Co, the 9th, being winner — after it we walked home from 66th St.

Mary Mooney died To-day at MetEar Hospital after an operation — an estimable woman — She entered our service as waitress 22 years ago \+ for years past has been a trusted stand by for us.
