## Left Page

### Sat. June 5, 1909

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day with mizzling mist enough to wet things at intervals all day — Up soon after 7 — bkfst soon after 8 — after that sat with Katie till near 11 o'clk — sending letter to Dr. Miller, in Japan, Mr. Shaffer \+ others giving details of Mollie's last illness \+ death — Before noon, as the weather seemed to brighten I went out for a walk to the north but a wet nizzle sent me back in less than 3/4 of an hour — While feeling very well I decided not to risk going to the Princeton-Yale ball game \+ exposing myself to the risk of taking cold — So, to my great disappoint ment gave it up — Lunch at 1 — Then Katie \+ William went to the game — I stayed at home \+ had a good time all alone — reading Mollie's letters that Katie has had typewritten — walking on the piazza \+ napping — Katie, Wm., Jim \+ Ralph returned before 6 — Dinner at 6.30 — Jim \+ Ralph left at 8.30, Jim to return to Ansonia — The rest of the eve'g I was much interested in looking over many of M's Canadian Rocky Mt. photos.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 6, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delicious day — did not wake till near 7 — up — dressed as soon as possible \+ had a very enjoyable walk before bkfst — without discomfort of any kind except some weakness in the legs — a condition that I did not hope for a fortnight or so ago — In to bkfst at 8.40 — a little late — The mg till 11 was spent in pleasant chat — then out with Katie \+ walked down to Whitney Ave \+ towards St. to see a new house building on H[illegible] Murchley's plans — then down as far as Pres't Hadley's house to see another of Henry's planning — There by car back again up Whitney Ave. to near the end \+ climbed the hill to near Prospect St. to see a third H\+ M— house — then home at 1 — just in time for lunch at 1 — a long walk without discomfort \+ much pleasure — After lunch I went to Ralph's Room at 2 o'clk. finding Jim \+ R— there waiting for me — As I had already done considerable walking I wished to avoid tiring myself — so the boys submitted good naturedly to loafing — we wandered about the campus \+ at last I left them [illegible] to 4.77 before 5 \+ then rested till dinner time at 6.30 — then the boys came \+ we had dinner together — They left at 8 — Jim to return to Ansonia — Wm., Katie \+ I chatted together till 10 — then, to bed.
