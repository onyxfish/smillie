## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 2, 1909

**Weather:** fine day \- unusually cool \- cold last night  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. outside, went down to 44°. At 2 o'clk. A.M. I was waked by severe pain in right Kidney \- continued to increase \- wanted a mustard plaster but did not know what to do \- if I attempted to waken any one all the women w'd. be awake in a minute wanting to know what was the matter \+ if mustard from the kitchen was got the three women in the annex would be in a tumult \- It was a sad prospect at 2 AM. to have to lie tormented by increasing pain for five hours till the household was up\- I got up \+ stoked the fire \- but it was an hour before I thought of the tube of Capsolin, a counter-irritant; that D'. Hubbard left with me \- I got up, warmed my bare body before the fire \+ rubbed the emulsion well in all over the region of the Kidney. It got to work presently \+ by 5 o'clk I had considerable relief \- it was like a mild mustard plaster \- By 6. o'll. I was so far comfortable that I fell asleep \- up to this time there was no chill, but I was cold \+ no febrile disturbance \- The family got up \+ went down to a late bkfs: \+ it was half past nine before it was discovered that I was not with them \- Then Zare came to me \+ I asked him to get me some bkfs. At about 6 I began to feel the effects of fever but it did not get above 100/5. The pulse was rapid \- near 100 \- I spent the day in bed miserably with head ache \+ fever which did not diminish much before night \- a peculiar feature as it appeared to me, was the great flow of urine bet. 2 \+ 6 o'clk \- then I passed 32 oz \- no pus \- after that pus appeared freely \+ from 6 am to 6 p.m. I passed another 30 oz \- more than 60 oz in 16 hours \-

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug 3, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day \- I had my bkfs. in bed \+ after it got up feeling weak \+ seedy but with only a trace of fever, little head ache \+ quiet in Kidney \+ Stomach. I had my wicker lounge arranged on the south piazza \+ spent the m'g there \- wrote a letter to D'. Tuttle \- had my mid-day meal served there \- Then I loafed about the house \+ at about 4 o'clk darkened my bed room \+ tried to get some sleep \- but it was too close \+ warm indoors so I ret'd. to my lounge \+ spent the rest of the day there till 6.30 \- then went with the family to the Annex \+ had my ev'g meal — Ev'g with the family till after 9 \- then to bed \-
