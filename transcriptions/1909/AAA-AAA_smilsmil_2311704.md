## Left Page

### Tues. April 6, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild spring-like weather — wrote to Clarence Smith about Kate's claim on Mary Mooney's bank acct. After 10.30 when I got to studio — owing to Winton's carelessness the Ther. there registered 80°. I opened the windows + cleaned out — went to 2d Nat. Bank — then wandered over to the new Penn. R.R. station at 7th-9th Ave's — 30th-32d St to see how the work was progressing — then back to studio — then home to lunch — after that went to Penn. R.R. ticket office + got time table — then to studio — mounting board from Bainbridge had arr. + I had a job unpacking it. It was 6 o'clk. when I got home — Dinner. Ralph dined with his classmate Kenfufee Evelf. I went to Charl's — there till 10 — then home.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 7, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A muggy, enervating day — Ther. getting up to 75°. To studio at 10.30 — Ralph came along soon after to see if he could help in getting ready for a start south to-morrow. I selected about a dozen [illegible] that I shall take to Washington for Print Collection of Congressional library — about noon we went to Penn. R.R. ticket office \+ bought two round tickets, Washington + back good for 10 days. Then to 2d Nat. Bk. + drew $200. Then home to lunch — at 3 o'clk Ret. to studio — Ralph cut 6 mounts in new boards + I pasted the [illegible] in bound with bond paper strips — We got through at 6 o'clk. making it possible for us to start to-morrow. Home at 6 40. The boys had their dinner — I was too tired + rested till 7.30, then had a light meal — Evelf. took things easy.
A tremendous blow of wind, reaching 70 miles an hour, passed over the city at about 10 p.m.
