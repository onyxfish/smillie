## Left Page

### Tues. June 29, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day, beautifully clear & fresh. Got up feeling weak & fevered but without pain. I took a little walk before bkfst. & for the moment, as it was quite cool, felt refreshed. After bkfp. it was manifest to me that I must save my strength, so I cut out the Alumni meeting that has heretofore been so interesting to me. I got some sleep to repair the loss of last night. Ralph came & lunched with us at 12.30 & soon after 1 o'clk we (Katie, R. & I.) took Trolly into the city, transferred & got out to the Ball field at about 2. We had excellent Grand Stand seats on a direct line with the plate & 3d base. About 2.30 the Parade came in twas very effective in the bright sunshine but it was soon over. Then came a long & very tedious wait. I was very uncomfortable & at times feared that I could not hold out through it. The game started at 4 & that revived me somewhat. It was a long game closely contested. The tension on me was severe when at 6 o'clk. the game ended. Yale winning 5 to 0. We got out after the usual wild dance over the field by the costumed classes. It was an unusually beautiful spectacle. Ralph left us in town as he expected Jim to spend the eve'g with him. When I got home I was nearly collapsed. At 7 o'clk. I ate a little & soon after had violent pain in the stomach. I retired to my Room & after half an hour was relieved by throwing everything from my stomach. Then I lay on the bed till 10 o'clk. Then Katie & Wm. came anxious to do anything possible for me. I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 30, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day — mild refreshing westerly breeze all day. After a restful night I got up at 7 o'clk & had a walk before bkfst. Pleased to find that my head was clearer — my legs stronger & stomach & kidney quieter than for a couple of days past. Katie & I took a car that got us to Woolsey Hall before 10 o'clk. We wandered about outside & then in. K. held the the seats while I waited on the balcony for the procession. Our seats were very good ones. Section A. Balcony. Right. 26-27. Everything was on time & the ceremonies were impressive. Not marked by any unusual event. They were over before 12.30. Then out. Katie went home & I waited about until the doors were opened for lunch at Commons. which was at about 1 o'clk. I hoped to find either Prof. Brown or Mr. Elkin to act as my escort. But failing, I walked in & took a seat as near the speakers stand as possible. Soon after, I saw Wm. & joined him at Table Reserved for Faculty. We had the usual cold lunch & at about 3 the speaking began. Hadley presiding. Sect. 4d. Was Dickinson was first called upon. He was amusing as well as interesting. His theme was: The problem of disarmament among nations. He thinks, as I understood him, that the madness must go on until the burden of it becomes intolerable & bankruptcy results. Then "the light" of faith & peace will dawn. He was followed by a few words from Pres. D. Wright. Then Bishop Lawrence of Mass. One of the board of Overseers of Harvard made a very finished address. The theme being "The Voluntary University" as opposed to the State University, arguing that the Voluntary University must be the National University. Then Pres. Taft spoke at length, making many hits & rousing immense enthusiasm. It was all over at 4 o'clk. Then I went to Ralph's Room. Saw him for a few minutes, then home. Had a rest before dinner. A quiet eve'g with Katy & William. Ralph went to New London for the boat Races tomorrow.
