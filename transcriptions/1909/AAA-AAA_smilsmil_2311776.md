## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 28, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day \+ things seemed to be going on swimmingly \- I was greatly surprised that I had no returns of my two familiar pains of the past summer. Had b\[illegible\] at usual time. \+ then bath \- \+ so on \- nothing of special int. thro' the day. At 9.30 P.M. Miss Giles wished to give me warm broth but D. T. had left instructions that I should have Zoolack \- \+ that I had \- Before midnight I was waked by stomach pains, very sharp, burning like acid \- after suffering for some time I rang for a hot water bag \+ got a warm one. \- another wait \+ I got a warmer one \- pain increasing all the time with constrations of Zoolack \- again I rang \+ got very deliberate answer. I begged for Bi-carb. Soda or something of that kind to put out the fire \- in time it was brought but it failed to put out the fierce fire \- another wait \- again I rang \+ begged for something to ease the pain. A liquid drug was brought \+ in 15 or 20 m. I had relief \+ then, at about 3 a.m., blessed sleep.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 29, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came.

I think there was rain in the m\[illegible\] \- but the afternoon was sunny \+ pleasant \- I woke feeling miserably depressed after last night's experience \- I was dopey \+ weak \- I ate very little at b\[illegible\] \+ lay lethargic all the m\[illegible\]. I begged that my bath might be cut out \- D. T.'s visit about noon gave me a little heart \- I asked to be excused from eating dinner \- but he urged me to try \- I did \- was no worse for it \+ after it had my bath \- Jim came in after that \+ sat with me till about 3\. Then he went to Chas.'s \+ had dinner with him \+ I went to sleep \- Eve\[illegible\] Jim came back \+ sat with me till 9.30 \- then I had my hot broth \+ then turned in.
