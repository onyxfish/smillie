## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 17, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant winter day - To studio at 10 - The Geo. Hearn painting "The lifting of the Clouds" was delivered soon after - I was much disappointed to find that it has yellowed very much - probably the varnish - much of the foreground is too brown. The varnish is very hard + difficult to remove - The matter of correcting errors + changes is complicated + I am worried about it - it is not as simple a problem as I expected - I puzzled over it much of the mf. At 12.30 I went to Macbeth's Gallery to see Paul Dougherty's paintings - met Drake of The Century there + chatted with him for some time - then to 45, 5. N. The Schuyler - by appointment with Mrs. Dielman + had lunch with her + her daughter left at about 3 - went to the Montross Gallery to see a lot of Schilling's work - painting + etching - Too much for my understanding - then to Manhattan Hotel where Ella + Viola Beach were having a Reception in Parlor 50 - a very pleasant affair - met a lot of people of the long ago - Pauline Burger + her daughter - Buckinghams + others - It was near 5 when I left - Home + rested till dinner with Jim at 6.30 - Well he went to inspection drill + I went to Chaols - made the proposition to Mary + her girls that they spend the summer with me at St. Huberts - + it was agreed to - Home before 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 18, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - to studio at 10 + got to work on the lifting of the clouds". I hope that my painting upon the hard varnish will not result badly - It will be difficult + unwise to remove the varnish - I commenced painting upon the trees on top of cliff, right side of picture - Taking out the brown + the dark spots - It is a pretty sad looking picture + hardly worth trying to save - but what I have done is an improvement. Mr. Tolfree came in for a while + I shared his woes in connection with his tenant next door - Then K.P.T. came - + after her, Mary - I gave up work for the day after 1 o'ck. + then home to lunch at 2 - After that I went to the Gallery of the Hispanic Society of Am. W-156, 55 and saw the paintings by Joaquin Sorolla y Bastida. These pictures are astonishing examples of bravura. They are admirably drawn - full of light + of strong color + apparently are done at one heat for there is no evidence of repainting or retouching that I could find - The color is crude but the values are true - The exhibition is in striking contrast to the German Expt. at the Met. Mus. It is brilliant, dashing - full of light + joyousness - one must feel exhilirated in the presence of such work - The German work is by comparison is clownish, crude, morbid + depressing - While I am moved to admiration in the presence of Sorolla's work I fear that its influence upon young students will not be for the best - it is so natural for the inexperienced to be carried off their feet by dashing facility + in their work try to begin where the old man left off - we are all, public + artists, running wild now after bold impressionistic execution - The tide will surely turn before very long -
Dinner with Jim - well he went to drill - I had a quiet evening at home.
