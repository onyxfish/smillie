## Left Page

### Thur. May 20, 1909

**Weather:** Some sunshine but more cloud — air cold \+ raw with promise of rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some sunshine but more cloud — air cold \+ raw with promise of rain — I got to studio at 10.30 \+ spent what was left of the morning cutting mounts — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back to studio at 3 \+ worked for two hours on a charcoal sketch trying to gather together material for an etching from notes that I had made from a dock on the Hudson looking up to the University Yacht Club Bldg, Riverside \+ Soldiers \+ Sailors Mon. Had a rest before dinner — In the evg I went to Chase's where he was giving an exh'n of Lake George Lantern slides made many years ago. I got home after 11.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 21, 1909

**Weather:** Dull cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull cold with worth rain in the afternoon and evg. To studio at 10 after stopping at Hugh Douglas' \+ W. S. Miller's — Geo. the carpenter from Miller's came at about 11 \+ worked till 1 o'clk Faking a twist out of one side of the new cherry closet made for my pfs. Misery began to descend upon me soon after he came \+ by the time he went I was hardly able to walk owing to pain thro' me from side to side \+ especially on the right about the Kidney. I got home \+ waited about for awhile the trouble increasing — At 4 o'clk I had a temp. of 100°. I went to bed after phoning to Dr. Tuttle — he came at 8 \+ made the usual examination finding a very considerable swelling, as in attacks heretofore — He admits that he is puzzled but thinks it may be owing to an atonic condition of a portion of the Colon where there is not tone or power sufficient to prevent stagnation \+ clogging that crowds other organs, the tender right Kidney among — I find that explanation quite as comforting as none. He advised a Compound Cathartic pill — At 11 I had a bowl of gruel \+ at 12, a pill, my trouble much less.
