## Left Page

### Wed. April 28, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An unpainting day - clear sun shine, but rather cool. To studio after 10 + worked with my p.p. Till near 12 o'clk - on my way to studio I went to Tiffany's to see Mr. Geo. F. Kunz - the "gem man", from whom I had rec'd. a note before I left home in the morning asking me to call + he w'd. talk with me about the Bishop's jade work + He was not in was expected at 11 - I ret'd. at about 12 + saw W. Kunz - He knew nothing about the substitution of a reduced process plate from my original mezzo. but in place of the original - said that was done before Bishop's death + must have been by his order - Bishops was beyond his depth in attempting to direct the production of such a work as he ignorantly hoped to make of his jade Catalogue + was easily the victim of bad advice. It is declared that by his order all plates were destroyed after the publication of the book - It enrages me to think of my jade mezzo. the plate being destroyed after but eight or ten p.p. were taken from it - Home to lunch at 1.30 - Before 4 o'clk. went down to Clarence Smith's office + got the bond that I have executed to secure the Bleecker St. Savings Bank agst. loss in paying Mary Mooney acct. over to Kate Leonard - also to Cullen's + left Washington prints there to be mounted - Eve'g Jim + I went to Mr. Dodd's in 77 St. I called on Kate Parker Riley + her two daughters - we met the three Misses Dodd - also Mrs. Hunt - Home before 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 29, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beast of a day - Snow, hale, sleet, rain \+ Ther. down about 35°. To studio before 10 - some time spent staining with oil + Van-Dyck brown the new cherry closet for prints - then for a time compiling a cat. of plates that I have etched + engraved since 1878 - Home at 1 o'clk - Afternoon was busy in a desultory way doing nothing of importance + watching the weather - Charl has given to Jim + me tickets to the University Glee Club Concert at the Waldorf this eve'g. The weather was too much for me - so, I did not go - Jim went to a dance last night after his call with me on the Rileys at Mr. Dodd's 333 W. 77 St. Crowell invited him - it was at Bretton Hall + as he did not get home till the small hours this m'g. he chose his bed rather than the Glee Club concert.
