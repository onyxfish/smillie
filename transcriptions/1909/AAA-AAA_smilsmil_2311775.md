## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 26, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

mg. opened with showers — I felt very weak \+ the chances for a start for N.Y. City seemed small, but as the m'g went on I grew in strength as the fever lessened \+ the clouds lifted \+ the sun shone out — I spent the m'g writing \+ signing blank checks to leave with Ralph. As far as I could think I left instructions with Mary who packed my suit case \+ had parting interviews with old Kate, Young Kate \+ Lucy Kane — At 1 o'clk I had some broth — about 2 I got up \+ dressed \+ at 2.45 got away in Nye's big motor — bundled in robes \+ bolstered by pillows. Ralph went with me — The ride was delightful \+ the jarring did not incommode me at all — the air was fresh \+ the sunshine was warm — the way we sped along over the good parts of the road was exhilarating. There was no trouble in climbing Spruce Hill — after we had passed Elizabeth Town we were caught by an exceedingly heavy shower, but thanks to the many rugs I kept dry — the sun shone out brightly at 4.45 when we got to Westport. We went at once to the Westport, the little hotel near the station \+ Ralph went up to wait for Dr. Tuttle who was to come on the train due at 5. It was delayed by a breakdown till 5.45 Then he came, I was delivered into his custody \+ R. started on his return to the Adks. I kept my room \+ on my back — The Dr. wandered about \+ I dozed — We tc. we chatted till near train time — 10.33 — then settled bill \+ went to station. Train about 10 m. late — Large bridal party had engaged all state rooms — but our darkie said his was not taken \+ for a time we flattered ourselves that we were in luck — but the conductor ousted us saying that the darkie was wrong — So we had to be satisfied with our section. No. 6. No time was lost in getting to bed \+ I had as comfortable a night as was possible under the circumstances.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 27, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Dr. \+ I lost no time in getting into a taxicab \+ up to the hospital — Charming day — we went direct to my room, without ceremony. \+ at once I undressed \+ got into my bed. Miss Isabelle MacClive had not yet arr. from her vacation but came at about 10 o'clk. She is my special nurse, a young St. Catharines, Canada, give. In the course of the m'g I was visited by the House physician, Dr. McBaine, a young man from the west, by Miss Brown, Head nurse in the corridor, by Miss Giles, night nurse, by Geo. For day orderly \+ Jos. Levy, relieving orderly — Dr. Tuttle came in on his regular rounds before noon \+ again in the afternoon. I had my broth at 8.30, my lunch at 1.30 \+ my even meal at about 7 — \+ a cup of broth at 9.30 — I feel somewhat gorged.

In the m'g I wrote a note to Ralph and mailed to him remnants of R.R. mileage tickets.
