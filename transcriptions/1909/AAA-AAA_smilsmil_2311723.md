## Left Page

### Fri. May 14, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm, enervating day with small thunderstorm at 6 p.m. Up late with about the same amt. of stupifying headache that I took to bed with me — It has hung on all day making it impossible for me to accomplish anything — It was near 11 when I got to studio — I did little else than pack the framed etching — the Road to the Lake, that I am going to send to Priscilla Beatty Poore at Scranton — I tried to write a letter to W. Parsons, Print Division, Congressional Library, Washington — but my head was not equal to it — so I carried the pkg. with Jade pf. \+ Sanduis Rey mission to Adams Exp. \+ sent it on its way without accompanying letter — Home \+ had lunch at 1.30 until 4 o'clk. I was on my bed — Then I went out to Riverside \+ loafed there till 6 o'clk — In, a few min. ahead of the thunderstorm — Dinner with Jim who spent his eve'g packing in preparation for leaving home to-morrow for his new venture in Ausonia — I cannot help looking forward wondering what the future has in store for him — \+ for me.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 15, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim left his home with me to-day.

Up at 7 \+ had bkfst with Jim — our last bkfst together as fellow occupants of our apartment — Jim gives no evidence of regret \+ misgivings concerning the step he has taken — I think he is really glad to get away \+ out of N.Y. city — I wrote a letter to W. A. J. Parsons concerning the two pfs. — (Jade \+ Sanduis Rey) that I sent to him by Exp. yesterday — Late when I got to studio. Did not do much of anything — in no mood — A little before 1 o'clk. went to Grand Central Station \+ there met Jim who left on the 1.02 train for Ausonia — others I bid him goodbye — then home \+ lunched — afternoon I went to the Water-Color Exhn \+ was much interested — greatly impressed by the Revolution in manner \+ method of Water-color work since I took heed of it first at the formation of the Society — There was an unexpectedly large \+ interesting class of etchings — Looking at pictures is tiresome work \+ I got home tired \+ was glad of a rest before dinner. Even'g I went to the Century where we had a lecture by a member of the club, name forgotten, who was with the U.S.S. Albatross on her southern Pacific deep sea sounding cruise — It was illustrated by lantern slides from photos. taken by him on \+ about the many islands that he visited. After the lecture sat \+ chinned for some time with J. H. Smith \+ Nicoll — home \+ to bed at midnight.
