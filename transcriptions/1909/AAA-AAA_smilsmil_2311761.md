## Left Page

### Thur. July 29, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A muggy day — not quite so hot as yesterday — much haze \+ cloudiness — After a few drops of rain I got up I was troubled with nausea for a time \+ did not go down to bkft. with the others — The unpleasantness passed \+ I had it later — The day was passed like its predecessors, in loafing — If there is any virtue in such living there should be some evidence of it soon.
Dinner at 1 — Box from Wanamaker came in the afternoon — Sent order to P\+T for coffee.
Evening meal at 6.30 — rest on So. piazza in the moonlight — no pain to-day.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 30, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright early sunrise — a heavy rain storm with some thunder, passed over while we were at breakfast — weather cleared soon after \+ we had a pleasant day — followed by brilliant moonlight.
I feel so much imp'd in condition that with Ralph I went to the Club Ho. interviewed Coughlan, straightened out his bill agt. me — Sold him my Road Roller \+ came as near as possible to coming to an understanding with regard to Nye's livery bill without seeing Nye. Then home \+ had a rest before dinner — After that Ralph \+ the girls went for a walk to Beaver meadow fall \+ back by River trail. Later in the afternoon I started down the hill to call on Widow Ford, Deedee — At my gate met Mr. Van Horn \+ Mrs. Loving \+ rode with them to the widows. Saw her \+ arranged to get ledge rock from her to build a retaining wall on my road. Then walked home by way of the old road taking it as carefully as possible — Then another rest — Tea at 7 — very cool eve'g — while the others sat on So. piazza I preferred to remain in-doors — altho' I took a short walk by moonlight which was to brilliantly cold \+ severe.
