## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 19, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant on the whole  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the afternoon \- more rain \+ high wind at night. To studio at 10 \+ worked till 12 on lifting of Clouds \- then cleaned up \+ went to Knordler's \+ saw a collection of about 80 water-color drawings by Jno. Sargent the portrait painter \- views of places visited by him \- they are exceedingly crude, some of them mere blots \- a few are strong \+ impressive \- really works of genius of high order but the greater number should never have been seen outside of his Studio \- the Brooklyn Inst. of Science \+ Art has purchased the lot for $20000 \- led by the present craze for slap-dash \- Home to lunch before 2 \- owing to rain I could not go out for a prowl so I amused myself by writing a letter to the Tribune about the new scheme of getting permission for the N.A. of D. to build galleries on the site of the old Arsenal in Central Park \- I opposed it \+ urged that the A. take its present Building Fund of about $200000 \+ put up galleries covering its 16 city lots in character like the Glass Palace, Munich \- the building in [illegible] Park, Berlin \+ others of same sort \- went to barber \+ had hair cut \- Dinner with Jim \- Eve. he went to "Studio" dance \- I wrote to Ralph \- then dozed till bed time.

Letter to the Trib. \- in issue of 22d. A copy of it will be found in pocket of Diary.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 20, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim went to New-Haven.

A fine day, tho' dubious at times \- cloudy \- sunshine \- breezy \- a combination of March \+ April \- To studio at 10 \- worked till 1 o'clk \- on lifting Clouds. Taking the hard harsh detail out of foreground rocks \- Home to lunch before 2 \- Jim there \- he left for New-Haven on 3 o'clk. Train to spend the holiday (22d) with Ralph. I had intended to go to Catharine Cook Smith's Recep. but got mixed up with other things \+ wrote Regrets instead. I had my dinner alone \+ in the Eve. went to the Century \+ heard Montgomery Schuyler discourse about "our four big bridges" \- I was disappointed \- It was a disquisition upon the relations of Art, Architecture \+ Engineering in the matter of bridge building, drawing upon our four bridges for illustration \- The address was rather turgid \+ the lantern illustrations poor \+ insufficient \- I sat with Hfd. Smith \+ Chas. Collins \+ had a pleasant time \- Home at 11.
