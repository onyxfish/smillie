## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 8, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hotter day \- clear, strong breeze & Ther at 87°. I had a fair night's rest & got up with a glimmering of hope as both stomach & kidneys were quiet. Had bklp. with the family \- Before noon I went with cautious fear down to the road to see what progress had been made in getting stone & preparing a bed for the wall. was not much gratified by what I saw \- Then slowly got back to the house finishing my activities for the day. Dinner at 1.30 \- I ate carefully what I thought was a very light, safe meal, but at 3 o'clk I was doubled up on my bed hugging a hot water bag \- The neuralgia was severe & kept me down till 6 \- Then I had a poached egg & after that, while I escaped neuralgia, was so disturbed in the stomach region that I sought the seclusion of my bedroom \- Ralph found me there &, in the dark, he sat on my bed & we talked till after 9 \- my temp. had been rising till, at 10, it recorded 101½° \- making me unhappy.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 9, 1909

**Weather:** chill  **Temp:** 103³⁄₅

Pleasant m'g \- Up & bklp. after 8 \- was about till after 10 \- Then neuralgia of stomach \- to bed with menthol embrocation & hot-water bag \- Pain continued for an hour or more Then I fell asleep \- Up about noon feeling relieved \- wrote awhile \- felt a chill coming on \- got to bed at about 1 o'clk. & shivered for more than an hour \- Temp. began to rise before 3 o'clk. by 3.30 it had risen to 103³⁄₅° \- Soon after that it began to fall & at 4¹⁵ it was 102⁷⁄₅. Then, acting on instructions from Dr. Tuttle I took 5 grs of Quinine \- At about 6 my temp. had fallen to 100³⁄₅°. I had a fairly comfortable night temp. gradually falling till the m'g when it stood a trifle below normal.
