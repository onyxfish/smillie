## Left Page

### Mon. March 29, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fair mixture of March \+ April - more sunshine than shadow - weak failure of combination of snow flurry \+ shower - Rain \+ snow during the night - Before 10 went to Dr. T's - he touched a troublesome canker in my mouth with nitrate of silver - Then on to studio but stopped at N.Y. Pub. Library \+ was there going carefully thro' the bldg till near 12 a very interesting time watching the progress of the work which, in parts, has advanced to the decoration. To studio for a short time then home to lunch - After that I went down to Eines \+ Amend's. 3d Av \+ 18th \+ got some rubber tubing - To studio awhile Home before 6 - a rest. Dick Boardman, a college chum of Jim's, dined with us \+ spent the ev'g - a pleasant young fellow - amateur at dry point.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 30, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A good deal of sunshine with some harmless snow-squalls in the afternoon. To studio soon after 10 \+ worked at the sorting of my pfs. Till 12.30 - Then to 2d Nat'l \+ attended to making deposit — Home to lunch at 1.30 - About 3 o'clk I decided to spend the rest of the afternoon out of doors for the good of my health - I tramped in Riverside till 5.45 \+ enjoyed it very much - Then a short rest before dinner - Ev'g Jim went out \+ I amused myself alone -

Otto Sandreczki, an old time N.A. Etching Class student of mine, came to the studio bringing some of his studies \+, without complaint, a hard luck story. He is a good fellow \+ has talent - I shall try to help him.

After lunch I sat for awhile \+ tried to arrange my argument ag'st the accepttance of the Arsenal site in Cent. Park.
