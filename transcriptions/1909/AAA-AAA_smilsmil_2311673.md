## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 1, 1909

**Weather:** A splendid cold winter day  **Temp:** Ther. at 5°

at 7 Am. we had fresh strawberries for dessert at dinner, which means, of course, that spring has come — my head ached so severely that I did not feel able to get up till near 11 — It was about noon when I got to studio, resumed work of cleaning a lot of etchings & photos. That have got badly grimed with dust while lying upon a closet shelf — Bkft. so late, I had no lunch left studio at 4 — went to Kekkeels & saw the Whistles etchings there — Had an extended & interesting chat with Allison — Home at 5.30 Dinner with Jim — Ev'g wrote to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 2, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant day & milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- to studio soon after 10 — found a bad tempered note from W.R. Sands complaining that I do not give him enough steam to heat his garage & insisting that it must be kept up to 65° all the time. I wrote him a line referring him to Mr. Polfree with his complaints & orders — Continued my work of cleaning photos & etchings — Had lunch in studio — and left after 4 o'clk — Home & had a rest before dinner — Ev'g attending to bills — wrote to Mr. Polfree — This m'g I met Mary Cook & walked a way with her — She told me that her Tom is to be married on Sat. 19th to Frances Wilcox — 641 Delaware Ave. Buffalo — The wedding is to be very private — invitations to be written. I found my invitation when I got home in the afternoon — a pleasant note from Mr. Wilcox inviting Jim, Ralph & me.
