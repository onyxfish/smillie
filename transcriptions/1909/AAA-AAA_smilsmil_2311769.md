## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 14, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Strong wind from south \+ clouas — Early in the m'g. about 5 o'clk. was aroused by pain in right side \+ around in lumbar region — Got up, made mustard plaster \+ applied it. It was a day of misery — when pain inside subsided then the stomach began trouble \+ between the two it was late afternoon before I was relieved of pain. There was some fever during a part of the day.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 15, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day — Strong south wind — Took 3 drams Castor oil early \+ was thoroughly cleaned out in the course of the day — I felt so very seedy that I did not get up till rather late in the afternoon — stomach threatening altho' I took only a little gruel \+ a little mutton broth — When I got up \+ dressed I went to the north piazza \+ was there wrapped in blankets like a mummy \+ stretched on my big reclining chair. The gloom of the twilight \+ the roar of the wind in the trees was immensely effective \+ I enjoyed it for some time — I had a beaten up egg \+ some gruel \+ then my trouble commenced \+ at last I gave up \+ went to bed with severe pain in stomach — not neuralgic, but a steady, distressing pain — I hugged my hot water bag \+ at last, worn out, fell asleep. At 12.30 I was waked by a chill — it was not severe — no involuntary twitching \+ shivering but it kept me awake \+ miserable till after 2.30 — then the fever followed — a match to the chill — perhaps 101°. I was awake till first gleam of day — then fell asleep
