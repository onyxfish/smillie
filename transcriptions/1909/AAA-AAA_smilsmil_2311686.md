## Left Page

### Mon. March 1, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming winter day. To studio before 10 o'clk \+ started painting "Chartres Cathedral from the River" \- I hope that I am right in thinking that I made a fairly good start, for me. By 1 o'clk I had painted the sky, cathedral \+ buildings that show over the middle distant trees. Then I went to Second Nat'l Bk, made deposit, then home, lunch at 2 \- then a rest \+ after that walked over to Central Park West, 87 St, \+ called on Sam'l Colman, his wife \+ his 13 month old son. I had a pleasant call \- the youngster is very bright \+ active. \- Home at 6 \- Dinner. Eve'g reading old school correspondence of Ralph including some interesting notes from the Rev. T. C. Williams To bed early.
Rain at night.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 2, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very disagreeable weather \- dark, with steady falling light rain \- I got to studio at about 10 after stopping at Charl's \+ asking permission to take my lunch there \- notwithstanding the lack of light I went on with the painting of the Chartres Cathedral \+ by 1 o'clk I had a considerable portion of the middle distance painted in. At 1.30 I was at Charl's \+ lunched with Mary \+ her girls \- After that I chinned with them till near 3 \- then to studio \+ cleaned up palette \+ brushes \- After 4 I went to Mary Cook's, 154 E. 37 St as invited to bid her good bye in unison with other members of the family \+ friends. Mary seemed to be in very good spirits \+ I had a pleasant time for an hour \- It was after 5 when I left \- At the subway Station in 42 St. I met Emma J\- on her way to the Cook's \- I turned \+ walked with her almost to the house. Then again to station \+ home. \- Dinner \- Eve'g. Jim went to Armory to prepare for the trip to Washington with his Regiment. \- I loafed the event away.
