## Left Page

### Sat. April 10, 1909

**Weather:** Clear, cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold & windy — after a very comfortable night's rest, up at 7.30 - Find myself groggy with a dragging feeling under the belt. Had a light bkft. with Ralph about 9 o'clk — Reading newspapers for a time then to Corcoran Art Gallery. I dragged thro' all the galleries except the hemicycle — the central galleries surrounding the statuary galleries on first floor are poor places for pictures — impossible to get far enough away from them — The Evans contribution hangs there now — the other galleries are good — rather low — but, as the pictures are not crowded they look well — I was surprised to find so good a collection of American paintings there — my Cliffs of Normandy has had a plate glass put over it \+ it hangs over Church's Niagara \+ could easily be overlooked — as it has a high horizon it is seen to a disadvantage when placed so high. — I was very tired when I left the gallery after 12 — Went to Hotel \+ rested till 1.30 — Then indulged in clam broth \+ hot Scotch — Then another rest — Then we sauntered down to the Smithsonian Institute — hurried thro' it — Then to the Nat'l Museum where there was much more to interest us — Went to the Graphic Arts Dept — that S.R. Koehler did so much for in organizing \+ found things so crowded — Fables shoved up against cases so that exhibits could not be seen — I found my exhibits but they were difficult of access for the reason given — we remained there till closing at 4.30 — Then wandered thro' an extensive market \+ out to Penn. Ave. \+ to Hotel — Rested till near 7 — Then we had a good dinner of which I was able to take sparingly — A party of three near us took considerable attention — one sleepily drunk another, a Kentucky Congressman, being noisily drunk. Every Ralph went out seeking adventure while I loafed about, wrote diary etc.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 11, 1909

**Weather:** Charming clear day, little wind  **Temp:** varied from 34° to 45°

Charming clear day, little wind but Ther. varied from 34° to 45°. Bkft. after 9 — after looking over newspapers Ralph \+ I wandered down to the Cafetoc — I had my camera \+ got some good snaps. Then we went to the Union R.R. Station \+ went carefully over it, then by Trolley back to Hotel at 12.30 — It was near 2 when we had lunch — A 3 o'clk. we went to starting place near P.O. of Arlington cars — missed 3 o'clk — had to wait till 3.30 for next car — dismal country to ride over till we got to the cemetery — grounds \+ woods there very beautiful — a long pull up the hill to the Arlington House — some camera shots — at Col. Liscomb's mon. among others — The mon., which is fine, surprised me I did not know that he had one here — we walked thro' to the Fort Meyers gate \+ there took car down to Georgetown — then across the river \+ again changed to car that took us to Hotel, but we got out at Lafayette Sqe \+ thro' White House Grounds. Then rested till 7 — Then dinner — After that Ralph \+ I went to Cosmos Club hoping to find Millet or Cy. Turner — not in — back to Hotel — wrote to Jim —

m.f. I wrote notes of condolence to Mr. deroqu[illegible] \+ to Will M. dow — The one has lost her daughter, the other, his wife.
