## Left Page

### Tues. March 9, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very disagreeable — Rain + nizzle all day — After 10 when I got to studio — for some time at work mounting the enlargement made on Sunday afternoon — Then, until Mrs. Kane came, I was putting things in order for her to get to work cleaning — I left her in charge — then home + had lunch after 2 o'clk — much of the afternoon went to waste over photos — Dinner at 6.30 with Jim — Eve'g. more waste of time looking for negatives that I had put away so carefully that I could not find them —

## Right Page

### Wed. March 10, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

meeting of N. A. of D.

A very much mixed day — m'g. opened wet + dark — soon cleared to warm, hazy sunshine the Ther. getting up to 67° — afternoon clouded with light showers — Eve'g clear, growing colder. At 10 o'clk — I went to D. Tuttle's — The distress in the region of my stomach is wearing upon me — I have little hope that he can help me but I find some degree of comfort in making the doctor a sharer of my discomforts — a practice more or less efficacious with everybody. Then walked to studio + worked till near 2 o'clk — Rearranging a lot of my prints of all kinds — Then home — lunch — returned to studio + dampened + put in press some pf. from Yosemite etching preparing them to be bound with the eight copies of Yosemite article written by me for Picturesque America many years ago — printed for me by order of W. Willie Appleton on one side of the sheet only — Home at 6 — dinner. Eve'g. went to quarterly meeting of N. A. at the [illegible] 109×55. Not a full meeting — Everything pro forma — general business — Then Chas. Miller called for a statement concerning the reported efforts of the N. A. to get permission to build on the site of the old Armory or Arsenal in Cnt. Park. Dielman explained at some length the progress of a scheme that has been pushed with great secrecy for months past — very few of the N. A's knew anything about it — Miller was not satisfied + put off one of his rambling buncomb declamations that led to some acrimony — Dielman assured us that so far nothing had been done that could bind the Academy + that in good time there would be ample opportunity for discussion — Home before 11.
