## Left Page

### Wed. May 12, 1909

Annual meeting - N.A. of D.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exhilirating sort of day, bright fresh \+ breezy - my physical condition is slowly improving \+ to-day I feel better than for some time past. To studio at 10 \+ worked among my p\[illegible\] - the arrangement of them being now practically complete. I went home at 1 \+ had lunch - \+ at 3 was at the N.A. of D. - The meeting had already been called to order \+ Pres. Dielman was reading his annual address. The meeting was a full one, as many as 50 being present at one time - D. gave a report, quite complete of the Central Park Sta episode without going into details of mentioning the names of those in the City government who are reported as having favored the project. Of those who lobbied for it at Albany - He frankly admitted what we all know, that there had been much work done that was necessarily secret but maintained that at all times the rights \+ powers of the N.A. of D. as an organization has been most carefully respected - the Park Sitters were vociferous in their endorsement - The leading incident of the meeting was the determined effort to re-elect Dielman after he had positively declined \+ his determined refusal to accept a practically unanimous election - It was at last made clear that he could not be induced to accept but three ballots had to be cast before his name was eliminated \+ Mr. W. Alexander was elected. A new departure was made when Cass Gilbert, Architect, was elected to the Council - The meeting adjourned before 6 to re-assemble for dinner at 7. The members drifted off in groups - I went out alone but was overtaken by Dielman \+ Mr. Weir - at the Cathedral we met a lot more - \+ we went to Morning Side Terrace - Then across to Riverside - it was a delightful evng. walk - The dinner being attended by Asso. members was a very large one \+ reasonably good \+ well served - There was no such horse play as at the last dinner - The speeches were of the usual stamp - old Bel\[illegible\] of course led off - Howard R. Butler Read an address urging upon the members loyalty \+ unity - Kenyon Cox made some sensible matter of fact remarks - The N.Y. Times came in for no end of abuse whenever opportunity offered - most of the fellows are boiling hot - I don't see why - it was only to be expected in a newspaper controversy - The Irishman who cracked a thin skulled appointment in a Donnybrook shindy expressed regret but wanted to know what the devil a man with a skull like that should be doing at such a place -
I left before 11 - The affair was not over.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 13, 1909

Kinney \+ Boardman dined with us.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - milder than yesterday - The strain of meeting \+ dinner yesterday made me feel useless for active exertion to-day - I have a very heavy head - It was about 10.30 when I got to studio \+ I managed in the course of the m\[illegible\] to pack a pf. of the Bishop Jade \+ a pf. of the Sandius Rey Mission in good shape to Exp. to Mr. Parsons of the Print. Div\[illegible\] - Congress\[illegible\] Library - It was about 2 o'clk. when I got home to lunch - After that I tried to write a letter to W. Parsons but my mental processes were not fluent \+ I had to give it up. I spent some time loafing in Riverside Park - We had dinner before 7 - Jim had invited his friends Bradford Boardman \+ [illegible] Kinney to dine with him - Hale gave us a good dinner \+ I think that possibly they had a pleasant eve\[illegible\] - altho', when too late, I realized that I had talked too much.
