## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 11, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, windy, March-like day — some stray gleams of sunshine. \+ some even. plumes. I had bhfo. at 8 — little or no improvement in my condition from yesterday. I completed the arrangement of photo. prints in one album. Then went out for a little air \+ exercise. Too much air for my comfort — \+ a little exercise was sufficient to cause a dull pain in the region of my unsound kidney — a little necessary food — at 3 o'clk. I went down-town to W. C. Cullen's \+ left with him the album \+ the prints to mount in it — Home after 5 \+ had a rest before a very frugal meal. Eve'g Jim went to drill — I loafed \+ to bed early. Temp. 10 p. m. \- 100/5°.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 12, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lincoln's Birth Day centenary.

The morning early, rather dubious but later the weather cleared charmingly \+ the sunshine was bright \+ inspiring. I got up before 8 o'clk. feeling better in some respects — Temp. normal — less discomfort in region of kidney — Before noon I passed a considerable amt. of pus. I worked in Riverside for nearly two hours \+ feel better for it — but later in the afternoon fever came again. Temp. rising above 100° with very depressing effect upon my spirits. I feel quite assured that these continuous attacks that prevent me doing work for so much of the time are evidence of advance of the kidney trouble, pus being retained \+ absorbed. If I am right in this assurance it means that I have reached the end of my work — There is nothing cheering to me in that thought — Gil Kenny came while Jim was out \+ sat with me for an hour or more chatting very pleasantly. Dinner — I called Dr. Tuttle on phone, describing my condition. he advised Castor Oil in the mf. I wonder how much of this is real — or how much is imagination —
