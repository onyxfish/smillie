## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 14, 1909

**Weather:** A bad day — Raw, slushy + drizzly  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bad day — Raw, slushy + drizzly — about 4 in. of snow fell last night — I got up feeling better than I did yesterday + determined to go to the studio — It was after 10 when I started I got to 79th St. — by Subway to 42d — went into the Lincoln Bank + made deposit for Jim — When I came out the Raw East wind beat in my face so strongly that I thought it unwise to push on to studio — home was the safest place for me — so I re-entered subway — went home + spent the rest of the day there working over the last lot of photo prints sent to me from Cullen's — Evef Jim went to drill.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 15, 1909

**Weather:** Sunny, mild + sloppy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunny, mild + sloppy — To studio at 10 30 — to work on Moonlight plate — I was hardly in working trim + made little progress. Prepared lunch in studio + at 2.30 walked to Second Mat. Bank, 5th Ave. + 28th St: on my way up stopped at The Montross Gallery + saw the Willard d. Metcalf landscapes — one that he calls "Spring" I enjoyed immensely — it is at high water mark in landscape painting I think — there are about 16 paintings on view but, from my point of view they are very uneven in quality. Then I went to Charles + spent about an hour with Katharine + Elizabeth — Then home. Dinner at 7 with Jim — Evef I worked at trimming the photo prints that Cullen sent up a couple of days ago —
