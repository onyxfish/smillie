## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 22, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant day \- very mild \- enervating  **Temp:** [illegible]

The streets in shocking condition \- flowing with muddy slush \- To studio at 10 \+ had a fussing day \- lots of little odds and ends jobs. It kept me fairly busy till 4 o'clk. To lunch left \+ put them between blotters \- to put away things that have been left lying about \+ to oil the cherry table made for my large aqua tint ground laying box \- lunch in studio. Ralph at home when I got there before 5 o'clk \- I got a rest before dinner \- well with Jim \+ Ralph went to Empire Theatre and saw the play "What every woman knows" by J. M. Barrie \- Maude Adams as Maggie Wylie and Rich? Bennett as John Shand \- The last has Barrie's humour \- is amusing \+ absolutely clean \- which is giving it high place above the average of suggestive silly plays that seem to be so popular now a days \- there is no adultery even remote \+ no passionnate love making. I think it is possible that some men may be as big fools as Mr. Shand is but no woman could be as calculatingly feminine as Maggie Wylie \- Such a play is not, of course, a realistic story of life \+ such a strand should not be demanded. The inability of Shand understand humour in life \- the gradual dawning \+ its sudden radiance at the last moment was an unexpected clever climax \- Bennett fitted his part well but Maude Adams is not able to carry the part of Margaret probably \- Her giggle, her little nervous gestures \+ her very bad movement in walking got onto my nerves so to speak \- I felt the want of dignity \+ else see her portrayal of the character \- Maggie was a reserved, self controlled \+ determined Scotch woman, not the woman of uncertain moods \+ little movements that Maude Adams gives us \- After 11 when the play was over \- went directly home \+ to bed as soon as possible \- my stomach made itself disagreeable during this event for some unknown reason \+ caused discomfort during the rest of the night \-

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 23, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, unpleasant day \- everything muggy \+ moist with now \+ then a little rain. I got up hoping to be able to go to the studio but at 10 o'clk resigned myself \- As diagnosed by me it is a mild case of gastritis, induced partly I fear, by some strain while printing a couple of days ago \- I hope not \- that it prove fatal to my plans for work ahead \- I loafed about with little life or energy \- Ralph got up about 11 o'clk \- \+ was company for me till he left at 1.30 to meet Jim \+ lunch with him \- They dined with Hugh Crowell \- In the eve'g I got up energy enough to attempt to do some photo printing using Royal Velox \- new to me \- not suitable for the negatives (from Round Tower, Windsor.) from which I printed \- consequently I went to bed tired \+ discontented.
