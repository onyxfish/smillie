## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 27, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant m[illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m[illegible] — but about 1.30 it tried to rain but not enough free to wet the sidewalk. Bkft. before 8 — To studio before 10 — Painted on a small sketch painted years ago in the fair ground at Montrose — making considerable changes, to its advantage I think — Cleaned up at 12.30 \+ went to Second Nat[illegible]. Then home to lunch before 2 o'clk. — Some reading \+ some sleeping in the afternoon — Dinner — Eve'g Jim went out — I wrote a note to Geo. Haven Putnam \+ then took to his house in 86. S. the M.S. that I had prepared, being selections from Father's Autobiographic Sketch together with my preface \+ addendum. G.P. has very kindly consented to look over the work critically \+ then advise with me about it.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 28, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — an attempt at a snow flurry in the afternoon did not spoil it. Bkft. after 9 — with newspapers \+ magazines till about noon, then Jim \+ I started out for a walk on Riverside — came in before 2 — then had dinner — more reading — then at nap — After 4 went to 154 E. 37 S. to call on Mary Cook who is to sail for Gibraltar on Thurs. She was resting \+ begged to be excused — I saw Louise \+ when chatting with her Jim came in — Before 6 we went to Charl's — saw them all \+ left at 6.30 — Home \+ had tea after 7 o'clk. — I spent the eve'g alone — more reading.
