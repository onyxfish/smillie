## Left Page

### Fri. March 19, 1909

**Weather:** Overcast \- light rain late afternoon \& eve't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfo. I wrote a note to Editor N.Y. Times concerning the Row over project to build N. A. of D. galleries on old Arsenal site in Central Park \+ left it at Times office on my way to studio. I resumed work of gathering all of my etching pfs. Together \- W. Baxter of Bainbridge's Sons came with samples of mounting board \+ we had a long confab over varying shades of white \- At last I ordered 125 boards, 30×40" to be cut into 15×20 \+ 20×30 \- With this lot I hope to get satisfactory mounts not only for my own work but also for much of Father's work at the Public Library Print Room \- I had my lunch at studio because Kate Leonard \+ Catharine Boadley wished to go to the funeral of Mary Mooney. Afternoon Mr. Holme of the Times came to interview me about N.A. of D. \+ Cent. Park project. He did not leave till 5 o'clk \- Then I got home as quickly as possible \+ rested till dinner at 6.30 Eve'g Jim went out \+ I amused myself writing to Ralph \+ reading news papers \-

## Right Page

### Sat. March 20, 1909

**Weather:** Fine day, but cold \& windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- It was after 10 when I got to studio but I made good progress \+ have now nearly completed the rounding up of all my etching pfs \- I have many more than I thot. It was 2 o'clk when I got home to lunch \+ I ate very sparingly having intimations of discontent from parts of my anatomy where complex chemical reductions go on \- I was not successful in warding off the threatened trouble \- Discomfort increased to actual pain \- My dinner, through fear was a perfunctory performance \+ before 10 o'clk I went to bed \+ nursed a very hot water bag. Of course I was unhappy but I made efforts, as energetic as possible under the circumstances to cultivate philosophy \+ stoicism \- The output was nothing to be proud of \+ my sleep was not of the youth, health \+ beauty kind.
