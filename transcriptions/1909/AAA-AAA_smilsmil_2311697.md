## Left Page

### Tues. March 23, 1909

**Weather:** A lovely day \- cloudless \- pleasantly cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ reasonably (remembering that it is March) quiet \- To studio about 10 \+ Resumed the work of the past few days but was seriously interrupted by a lengthy call from a poor devil engraver who comes to see me at long intervals \- He is well up in years \+ within his extremely limited field of perceptions is seriously enthusiastic \- His talk about the inspiration \+ incentive to high endeavor that comes to him when he takes a day off to visit print shops or picture galleries is pathetic \- I was inclined to be impatient but his gratification in talking \+ questioning was so evident that I could not deny him. It was near 2 o'clk when I got home to lunch \- after that the day was so fine that I loitered in Riverside Park \- down to 72d St\. Thence to subway \+ to Studio \- there till 5 \- then home \+ rested till dinner. Eve\+ Jim went out \+ I loafed over my papers.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 24, 1909

**Weather:** A lovely m\+\.  **Temp:** [illegible]

but rain before bedtime \- Bkft. before 8 \- to studio at 10 \- Rummaging in out of the way places for prints \+ cleaning out long neglected drawers \- a penalty for disorderly methods \- or lack of method \- Till near 1 o'clk \- Saw Mary for a few moments on my way to studio \- Home to lunch at 1.30 after that went to Mc Cutcheons \+ left there the doz. hdkf, bought in London, to have monogram embroidered on them \- Then to studio \- There till 5 \- home \- a rest \- dinner \- Eve\+ wrote some notes \-
