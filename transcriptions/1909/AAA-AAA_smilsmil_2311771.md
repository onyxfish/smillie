## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 18, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dr. Tuttle came.

Heavy rainstorm all day with mist around. I got up after a fair night of rest - bkfst. in bed - but as soon as it was properly warmed in large room I went down - D.T. got to the house soon after 11. in a very good natured frame of mind \+ assuring us that he had suffered no hardships by the way - I had a talk with him about my condition - he thinks that on the whole I am as well off as I w'd. be in the Presbn. Hospital \+ that another month here, even if no better than the past two months would not be likely to seriously impair my recuperative energies looking forward to a season of retirement to the hospital - In the afternoon D'. Hubbard called for him \+ they went to see W.W. Hardenburg. Even'g. I sat with the family taking part in the general chatteration.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 19, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day notwithstanding some clouds \+ a few misty drops of rain - Kidney pain started me up between 3 \+ 4 a.m. I made mustard poultice \+ had it on for about 3 hours - I was uncomfortable about the stomach \+ cured fake little bkft. D.T. watched me. I'm sure some things in my case puzzle him. About 9 o'clk. after the kidney pain had subsided the stomach pain began - with Paregoric \+ hot water bag I checked it somewhat - In an hour or so it subsided. \+ then I was able to take some chicken broth - After two o'clk. W. Maghee sent Frank, his coachman, \+ his two seat surry to take the Dr. \+ two others over the Reserva road to the lower lake. Mr. Frank Dodd came \+ made a pleasant call to enquire after my heels. While here the Dr. \+ party returned. I got up after 5 \+ for awhile sat on the north porch — Sat with the family thro' the even'g. \+ to bed late - 11 o'clk.
