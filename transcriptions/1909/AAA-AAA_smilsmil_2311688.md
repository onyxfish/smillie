## Left Page

### Fri. March 5, 1909

**Weather:** Bright, cold & windy  **Temp:** 28°

Bright, cold & windy — Ther. 28° — wind 50 m. according to weather bureau. I got to studio at 9.30 — Century men came & took lifting of "the Clouds" — Then I walked down to Putnam's in 23d. St. & after writing for some time I had an interview with Geo. Haven P— He approves of my plan of having a number of copies of father's auto-biographic sketch "privately printed" & after a few general directions passed me over to Mr. Savage who has general charge of that dept. of work — then home — lunch at 1.30 — after 3 went to The Century & from there to studio — there till near 5 — then home — Found Ralph there — down to dine with Bangs & go to theatre with him — I dined alone — Got P.O. card from Jim, Washington, Mch. 4" arrived 4 a.m. nothing doing — all well." As this statement of arrival is at variance with all newspaper reports — I'm afraid Jim may have written the card before he got to Washington — acct. as rea that the regt. got to W— too late to join in the Inaugural Parade — scheduled to arrive at 4 a.m. card post marked 7.30 p.m. He has no doubt had a hard time between famine & frost.
I spent a quiet ev'g at home.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 6, 1909

**Weather:** Cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold & windy — attempts at snowing at night. On my way to studio, quite late as I had remained at home to see Ralph — I stopped at Gas Co. Rebate Office, cor. 6th Ave. & 42d. St. & was there told that the Co. w'd in good time send me check for amt. due. Jim phoned to me at about 9 o'clk. from down town that he had got back safely from his Washington trip — At studio I found Ryan check & went to bank with it at about 12.30 — then home — lunch at 1.30 — Jim came home to lunch but Ralph did not. Ralph came to dinner in the ev'g. bringing young Bangs with him. After that we three went downtown by subway — I left them at 42d. St. & went to The Century Club. Geo. A. Hearn came before 9 o'clk & kept possession of me during the ev'g — not an unalloyed pleasure — He says that I have improved the lifting of the Clouds & expresses entire satisfaction. I am not quite sure that he could point out where or how it had been improved — Home before midnight.
