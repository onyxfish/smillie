## Left Page

### Wed. June 23, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day — About as all my days are now dividing time between the Wellesley \+ the Studio — Ralph went with me helping wherever he could — we had lunch at Charls At Studio I laid out painting material for next summer hoping that I may be able to do something at St. Hubert's. Dinner at 7 \+ even in general confab.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 24, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fearfully hot day — Ther above 90°. M\[illegible\] went to studio — made selection of material — palettes, colors, brushes \+ panels to take to the Ark for the summer — Ralph was with me — then to Wellesley taking palettes etc with me — After some work there R\[alph\] ret\[urned\] by subway to 23d S\[t\] — I bought colors — then to Scribner's — saw Mr. H\[illegible\] L. Smith \+ left with him a zinc plate that has been used in printing the etched plates for Bordeu's cat. Then to Bank — drew $100 — then to 38th St. \+ had lunch — After that to 440 West End with R — he packed his suit case to take to New-Haven with him I packed silver etc — Painters at work in the kitchen — On our return to 38th St. Stopped at Gr\[and\] Central — \+ bought mileage \+ section in Pullman sleeper for Sat. night — July 3\. for West Port. After buying it neither the seller in the Pullman office or the young men in Information Bureau could tell me how H.R. \+ ATH. trains connected or when Westport would be reached — Dinner at 7\. Even, tired \+ with intimations of trouble with stomach I went to bed early.
