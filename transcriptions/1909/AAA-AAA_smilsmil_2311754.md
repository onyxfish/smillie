## Left Page

### Thur. July 15, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 103 1/5°

A bright mf. — very warm day ending with thundershowers. I got up feeling quite as well as usual. I had a scrub with my hair belt — bkfs. at 8 — after that went about giving directions to Crawford \+ to Moody — Soon began to feel discomfort in Region of right kidney — This increased so rapidly that I went to kitchen \+ had Kate make up a mustard plaster for me — Before I could get to my bed room \+ adjust the plaster I was chattering with a chill — I got to bed \+ lay there shivering for an hour or so before the fever came — by 12 o'clk. my temp was 103 1/5°. By this time the mustard was getting in its hot work, but with it \+ the high fever — the kidney pain was lost — It was a dismal day for me \+ seemed to be interminable — by late afternoon my temp fell 2°. The improvement continued thro' the night \+ before mf. I got some rest.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 16, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day with a tendency to showers — A heavy thunder shower passed over us between 4 \+ 5 o'clk. I did not get up till about noon — my stomach is so much out of sorts that I can eat very little — Temp. a little below normal — no kidney pain — but I am very, very weak — Sat for some time in bay windows of main Room \+ had my bucket milk there — Afternoon. feeling somewhat stronger I went out \+ directed Moody in cutting down some trees near the house. Then came the shower \+ I had a nap — At 6 o'clk. I had a glass of lactone buttermilk — Eve'g. — Bessie played on the piano for some time.
