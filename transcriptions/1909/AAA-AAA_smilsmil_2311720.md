## Left Page

### Sat. May 8, 1909

**Weather:** Pleasant - rather cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant - rather cool - It was after 10 when I got to studio - stopped at Lincoln Bank \+ deposited to Jim's Co. the check that he got from sale of Steel common nearly $200 - by which he doubled his original investment. I got to work in one of my closets among framed pks. of my etchings \+ took a number of them out of frames \+ rearranged them with the other pks. Mary came in \+ then Geo - It was half past 1 o'clk. before I got away - lunch at home at 2 o'clk. - Ralph off with Jim. I spent a considerable part of the afternoon in Riverside Park. Home at 6 - a rest - the boys came in in time for dinner at 6.30 - Had been to a Ball game — Eve'g they went out together to some theatre \+ I loafed alone till early bed time.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 9, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph Ret. to New-Haven.

not altogether pleasant - nor yet so bad - overcast all day - a very light sprinkle of rain in the m'g. - We had bkft. soon after 9 o'clk. by agreement as we had planned to spend the m'g. at the Zoo in Bronx Park - The weather discouraged us - Jim \+ Ralph went to Church, R. thereby saving his Chapel mark. I loafed about till 1 o'clk \+ then went out to Riverside Park \+ continued my loaf there till 2 - then in \+ had dinner. The weather being more reassuring we started for the Zoo at about 3 o'clk. - by subway we got to southern entrance in 35 m. Then wandered till 5.45 covering the larger part of the ground in hurried manner. Home before 7 - Tea - At 8 o'clk. Jim, Ralph \+ self went to 42d St. R- took 8.30 train to New-Haven \+ Jim \+ I went to Chas's \+ there spent the eve'g till 10 o'clk. Then home.
