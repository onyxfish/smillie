## Left Page

### Fri. July 9, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day — cool + quiet — Up at 5. Kindled fire — opened windows + let in the warm sunshine — bolstered myself up in bed + read till 7 — A private Chivalry, by Francis Dynde — not a wise use of time + energy — Bkfs. at 8 — About the middle of the m'f I went over to the Club House — failed to find either Scott Brown or Mr. B — but had a talk with Augustus Coughlau, the manager — Dinner at 1 — Afternoon Ralph climbed Noonmark — I wandered about on Windy brow + elsewhere + did some Trimming + Pruning — Tea at 6.30 — + went like others.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 10, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely m'f — but the weather changed before noon. Thickening up + threatening but rain did not begin falling till about 9 P.M. Bkfs. at usual time — after it a young man — a carpenter — don't know his name — came, took some measures for lumber + then promised to come to work about the middle of next week. After 11 Ralph + I went to the Club. I ordered articles at Stone, saw Coughlau — also Hamilton Arabia — also Mr. Maghee, her husband + his new auto — then we ret. to the Ark with our mail. Dad Adams was writing for me with specimens of wall paper to put in Earl's Room. Dinner — little else than loafing in the afternoon — I got out steamer rugs + arranged them on lounge seats on north piazza. Tea at 6.30 — Went on N. Piazza till near 9 o'clk. Went indoors + soon after to bed.
