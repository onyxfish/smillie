## Left Page

### Tues. July 27, 1909

**Weather:** Warm \+ pleasant — a skim of haze covering  **Temp:** [illegible]

sky \+ distance at times - a good part of the m'f was spent watching Earl a work burning a great mass of brush cut from the ten or dozen trees that have been felled about the house recently. Mr. Openhym came to the house \+ made a very pleasant informal call - Dinner after one o'clk - more strenuous work as I sat on south piazza \+ directed Earl in raking up stray matter from road \+ under the pine trees - wrote part of a letter to Jim - Tea at 6.30 Immediately after it had severe pain in bowels for some time - it slowly yielded to the persuasion of a hot water bag - \+ I spent the remainder of the ev'g in my rugs on wicker lounge on north piazza - The rest of the family there - after 10 a walk down the lawn, south front of house to see the moonlight over the mountains.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 28, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The hottest day since I got here - a mist hung over the mts, \+ at times the sky was veiled by thin clouding - a pleasant breeze part of the time \+ then still \+ close. I luing about all day watching Moody at his work clearing up about the house the wrackage following the felling of a number of trees - I got thro' the day without discomfort till after Tea - then I had some pain in region of stomach \+ getting scared I took a dose of Paregoric which was a foolish thing to do following the excellent work of a dose of Castor oil taken at 4 o'clk. this m'f. Much catarrhal mucus was moved from the bowels \+ I should not have interfered by taking any form of Opium. The discomfort soon passed \+ I sat with the others in the beautiful moonlight on the south piazza.
