## Left Page

### Sun. June 13, 1909

*Jim ret'd. to Ansonia.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A muggy m't with some misty sunshine Rain in the afternoon — very heavy showers at about 7 o'clk — I got up at 6.30 — had a bath \+ shampoo — then to bed again — up at 8.30 \+ had bkft. at 9.30 — newspaper. Till after 11 — then Jim had his bkft. \+ together we went out \+ promenaded Riverside Drive — Rather exhausting. Dinner at 2 — Jim went down to Charles' regardless of the rain to see Mary \+ her girls — I spent some time writing out a catalogue of dies \+ etchings — my work — my dinner served for supper as well — Jim left at 6.30 to catch 7 o'clk train, just before the very heavy shower — a little deluge — fortunate for him as he had no umbrella with him — Self. busy about various things, especially in boxing \+ putting away mounted photos. of last summer's European Trip.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 14, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A rather dubious day that changed to be hot \+ humid — Ther. 84°. After stopping at Charl's to see Mary about traveling matters I got to studio at 10.30 — Mr. Huggins caught me before I got fairly started at my work \+ I showed him examples of wet ground Aqua-tint in proof \+ on the plate \+ explained to him lots of things — Then I started on the job of mounting the two large photos. that Kate gave me — the Venus de Milo \+ the Winged Victory of Samothrace upon heavy trunk board — Cullen's mounting on ordinary photo. board bring a failure, dampening them \+ put them in press to take the buckle \+ twist out of them preparatory to gluing them to the heavy board — Home to lunch at 2 — Had a rather strenuous afternoon inspite of heat in packing things away in closets — wrote to Nys — Moody, Kellogg \+ others in preparation for a move towards Keene on Friday — dinner. Self went to Charls — nothing very exhilirating there. Home soon after 10.
