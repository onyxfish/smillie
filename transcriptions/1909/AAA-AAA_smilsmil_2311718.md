## Left Page

### Tues. May 4, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day — on my way to studio stopped at Charl's \+ consulted with him as to selling Jim's Steel-common. At studio continued my work assorting \+ cataloguing my pfs. till 12.30 — then to Second Nat'l. Bank \+ got Jim's Steel Cert. from Safe Deposit — Home to lunch at 2. After 3 went to 74 Beekman St. Meyer's, \+ got some trunk boards for mounting large photos. \+ others to pack pads pf. to Washington — took them to studio after 4 — On my way home stopped at Clark's, the florists \+ ordered $37 worth of flowers sent to Josephine, to-morrow, the fiftieth Anniversary of her wedding. Then home \+ rested till dinner — Eve'g. I wrote a note to Josephine.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 5, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Light shower in the m'f but soon cleared bright, mild \+ spring-like — To studio at 10 \+ made good progress with pfs. of my work. I now have a complete chronological catalogue — \+ to day, as the pfs. from all my plates have been gathered \+ folded in separate wrappers, I have been affixing No's in their cronological order \+ placing them in the new closet in that order — It puts them into a very convenient shape for reference — Home at 1 — lunch — back to studio at 3 \+ continued my work there till 5 — then to Riverside Park and loafed there till 6 — Then a short rest \+ dinner with Jim — Eve'g he went out to make calls \+ I found work to do in preparing No's for work to-morrow.
