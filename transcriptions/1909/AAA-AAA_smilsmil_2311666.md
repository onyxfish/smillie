## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 18, 1909

**Weather:** Clear \+ cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful ice storm last night \- in the brilliant sunshine this m't. every stem \+ twig was glittering in a coating of ice. Ther. 20°. at noon \- much colder at night \- I got to studio at 10 \- worked on moonlight plate \- had to do considerable graver work to bring the newly bitten parts up to the other \- am now ready for printing. Had my lunch in studio \- left for home at 4 o'clk \- A rest before dinner with Jim at 6.30 \- Eve'g with photos. Trying to arrange albums for boys \- The prints from Cullen are, some of them, exasperating.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 19, 1909

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The weather bureau reports that before dawn to-day the Ther. fell to 7°. \- I know that I was cold in my bed \- When I went to the studio it stood at 12°. but grew milder as the day advanced \- the weather was fair \- I got to studio at 10 \+ first in a day of long hours \+ hard work \- I printed five prints from the moonlight plate working between each on the plate \+ experimenting with inks \+ wiping \- Prepared lunch in studio \+ after it walked in the streets for about 20 m. \- I have planned to do some serious printing of pts from the moonlight to-morrow \+ have set down some of my old French paper \- After 5 o'clk when I left studio \- Maybe I looked tired, because, in the subway a stout middle-aged woman kindly offered me her seat \- but no young man did \- it is singular, but when a seat is offered me it is almost always by a woman or young girl \- Home at 5⁴⁵ \+ rested till dinner \- Eve'g got more photo prints from Cullen \+ trimmed them for the boys' albums.
