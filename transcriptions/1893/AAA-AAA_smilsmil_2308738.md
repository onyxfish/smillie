## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 22, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still pleasant weather, altho' cloudy in the afternoon. We all had an hour or more of sauntering & breezing on the Promenade before dejeuner at 12.30 — After that, feeling stupid, I took a nap — & at sundown went out & walked briskly on the Promenade for nearly an hour — Fine sunset sky & very good surf — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef. I wrote part of a letter to Kate.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 23, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant, with some cloudiness & cool breeze — The little finger of my right hand has been troubling me for two or three days past — There seems to be a determination to gather at the root of the nail — with pain & heat. I poulticed it Friday night — seemed to be better on Sat — & to-day so disturbed me that I could not sketch or write — I went out with A. & the boys in the mfg & had a walk on the Promenade — got some flax seed & had a poultice on finger all the afternoon — Another walk in the afternoon — a wasted day — so far as work is concerned. Evef. finger was so much easier that I wrote for some time on letter to Kate — To bed with a big flax seed poultice.
