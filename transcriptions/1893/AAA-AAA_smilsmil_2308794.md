## Left Page

### Sun. May 14, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliantly beautiful day — Very warm
in the sunshine, with pleasant breeze blow-
ing. Enjoyed a scrub & change of clothes
& breakfasted after 9 o'clk — Then spent
most of the morning in studying all of the
plans of vessels & schedules of prices sent
to me from Cunard, White Star, North Ger-
man Lloyd & Am. Line of Steamers —
Determined that we should like "the Saal"
Aug. 26 or the "Allen" Aug 22d — Lunch
at 1 — loafed — late in the afternoon
we all went down to the Public Garden
where we enjoyed the breeze — a look-
out to Capri & some music by the
Band — Children's supper at 6.30 —
A. & I dined at 7 — Eve'g reading
guide book & writing diary.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 15, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — very warm but breezy — at about
9.30 A. & I took steam tram car & went on the
Corso to the point where, weeks ago, I commenced
a sketch, & with A. holding an umbrella over
me I finished it — Jim & Ralph joined us before
I got through. Then we went up the Stairway
to San Martino & got a slight sketch — Ret'd to
hotel soon after 12 & worked on my sketches
until lunch. After that we went to Cook's
to get a draft on Cologne for $250 Marks to
send to the N. German Lloyd's S.S.Co — Then to
a barber's where the boys & I had our haircut.
A. went for Miss Joshay. Ret'd with her — Took
the boys & I ret'd to hotel & wrote to the North
German Lloyds Co — naming certain Staterooms
that I wished on Saal or Allen, to sail Aug 22d
or 26th — also wrote to Hotel de Russie in Rome.
Then went down the Corso towards Philippo
& made a slight pen sketch — Back to hotel
at 6 — boys had their supper at 6.30 — at
7.30 A. & I dined at table d'hote — at
9 o'clk. by invitation we went to Cafo
Hartuell's Room with a few of my sketches
& talked about Indians & such things
until 10.30.
