## Left Page

### Tues. April 4, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disgustingly (to me) beautiful weather —
stretched out, a prisoner, in my bed
with the privilege only of looking out over
the blue bay to the mts. back of Sorrento —
Early in the morning a horrible pain in my
head came on — a combination of tic dol[oreux]
Rheumatism & lots of other things. It set me
nearly frantic. I felt as tho' a small vol-
cano was beneath the bridge of my nose,
preparing for an eruption — Dr. Scam-
in & tried to pacify me but did not ad-
vise any treatment for the head trouble
— all a part of me whole, he said, that w-
surely, in time, be reached by the caffeine
& Saly[cilate of] Soda. A dismal day for me —
Temp. went up to 101³⁄₅ & stayed there
from 12 until near 6 o'clk. & then it fell
slowly until it reached 100²⁄₅°. The
highest that it has yet been at that hour.
Dr. S— says I must stay in bed until he
releases me — says I am suffering
from Rheumatic fever.
Eve'g, a pleasant concert in the hall
down stairs, which was some enjoyment
to me — later Alvine read aloud part
of H[enry] James's "Point of View"

## Right Page

### Wed. April 5, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still bright weather — a feast of tan-
talus from my bed out of my window.
Awoke after a night of uncomfortable
dreaming. Temp 99³⁄₅ — very discouraging.
Violent pain in my head, more intolerable
than that of yesterday came on about 9 &
continued until after 11. Fearful that
it might proceed from some fault of secretion
or deep seated gathering, I sent for Dr. Scatti-
he came & reassured me. The
rest of the day was as dismal to me as
the preceding ones — Acting on advice of
the Dr. I sat in my bed, a shawl over my
head, breathing the steaming vapor from a
saucepan of chamomile flower tea — for
the time, at any rate, it was a relief.
