## Left Page

### Fri. April 14, 1893

**Weather:** warm, cloudless mt[illegible] — changing in the afternoon to much colder with high winds & storms of dirt.  **Temp:** [illegible]

my temperature record for the day the best yet — highest, at noon, 3/5° above normal falling to normal again at 6 o'clk — so that for 15 hours out of the 24 I am normal. Our neighbors, the Platts, got away for Rome at 1 o'clk. & as soon as their Room adjoining ours could be cleaned up we were put in possession of it — so now we have two Rooms getting the sun — of course we give up our inner room, No 15. Wrote a letter to Hemick giving him instructions concerning garden — also, a note to Elig. Howe. Jim occupied Annie's bed alongside of mine all day — a complete example of fretful impatience & irritability — I think he is getting better.

Eve'g. Annie read aloud from Don Orsino.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 15, 1893

**Weather:** Beautiful, bright day  **Temp:** [illegible]

About 11 A.M. I changed my quarters from my bed in my old room to bed in new room taking every precaution ag't. catching more cold. The change was a very great relief. I was much encouraged because at noon, for the first time in a fortnight, my temp°. stood at normal. With great care I ate a good beef-steak lunch — but, alas, at 3 o'clk. my temp°. had risen 3/5°. That is to 99° — but from that point it fell & at 6 P.M. I was again at normal. Then I went back to my own bed. During the day I wrote large part of a letter to Kate. Annie went out both m'g & afternoon taking Ralph with her. Jim is still feverish & is kept a prisoner, part of the time in bed. His behavior is far from saint-like. More Don Orsino in the eve'g.
