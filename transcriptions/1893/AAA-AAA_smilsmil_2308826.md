## Left Page

### Mon. July 17, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day. Taken altogether on acc. of the wind & drizzling showers — in the m.f, while A. was out shopping I took the boys & my sketch book & went by boat up the River as far as [illegible] & there made some slight notes — back to the house in time for lunch at 12.30 — In the afternoon I again went out over the same grounds taking notes & making selections hoping to be able to make one or more larger few studies. I was much bothered by the cold wind & the fine, spotting showers. Dinner at 7.30 — Evelf Jno. Durand came in & stayed until 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 18, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very breezy, cloudy & cool m.f. — clearing in the afternoon to be pleasanter — After bkfs. I went to the Port S. Louis & struggled against cold & wind & refractory pen, ink & paper until 12 o'clk, trying to get a few study of the rear of Notre Dame Cathedral — then home — lunch at 12.30 — After that I was going to the Luxembourg with A — but felt so uncomfortable below the belt that it was given up & I stayed in-doors nursing my misery. About 4 o'clk. A. went with Mad. [illegible], taking Jim, to a french Dr. (the A's one being out of town) to consult him concerning J's condition of bowels & stomach. I took my evening meal with the boys — " Evening I finished reading Ibsen's Doll's House — an idiotic thing to my way of thinking. I find no Revelations in it — no new doctrine concerning the elements that constitute a true marriage — What is it in the book that makes people talk? I am very dull of comprehension. As it is the only work by him that I have read, perhaps I need to read more of him before I can understand.
