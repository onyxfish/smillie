## Left Page

### Sun. June 11, 1893

**Weather:** Bright day — very warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright day — very warm — Thunder showers threatened in the afternoon but did not reach us until about 8 — Very bright lightning & heavy thunder — Steady Rain & a wet evening. After bfst — A. — 2, The boys & I went to S. Marco's but found that we were too late for mass with music — so we "did" the Cathedral, went into the Baptistry & around the galleries, outside & inside the building — Then we sent the boys to play & we "did" the Dodge's Palace very deliberately from the attic down to the dungeons — We were delighted — It is one of the most perfectly gorgeous Palaces that I have ever been in — Perfect in harmony & artistic richness of color. The Veronese paintings are wonderful & gave me great pleasure by their decorative harmony with their architectural surroundings — It was near one when we got through with the dungeons & the rooms seemed to dwell upon lovingly — much after that, a rest & at 3 o'clk we took steam boat for the Lido — a short sail of about 15 min — then tram car across the land strip to the sea shore or beach where we found a complete bathing establishment reminding us of Coney Island — After A. & E. got established in easy chairs, by a thatched roofed osier bathing cottage & the boys had got off shoes & stockings, I left them & ret'd to the city & then by steam boat went the length of the Grand Canal & returned getting to the hotel at 6.15 — A. & the boys having got in ahead of me. Dinner at 6.30 — Every day again wrote a little on Mary's letter.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 12, 1893

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast — at times very close & hot — late in the afternoon a fresh breeze sprang up with rain soon after, which continued until near 9 P. M. — after then very cool & fresh — After bfst — I finished my letter to Mary & posted it — Then, leaving the boys to amuse themselves at the hotel, at about 10.30 2 — A. & I started out — went to steamboat landing — no boats — festa of St. Anthony to-morrow — Temporary bridge on boats — the procession from S. Marco, across Grand Canal to S. M. della Saluta — so we took gondola to the church of S. M. Gloriosa del Frari which we found very interesting — especially the Titian altar piece, portions of which to my eye were very charming — Trans & Canova's tomb — very rich carved & inlaid wood stalls etc. etc — A funeral was under way & we waited & saw the body carried in procession out of the church with candles & incense, to the gondola hearse — mares in scarlet smocks — no mourners accompanying it — only one priest in a 2d gondola — then we ret'd to hotel & had lunch at 1 o'clk afternoon I finished pen sketch from our balcony & had a short walk before dinner at 6.30 — Every about 8.30 Annie & I went to the Casa Kirsche, nearby, to call on the sisters Torrey but we were too late for them — Miss Sliger sent down her card saying that being much fatigued they had retired for the night — back to hotel, left A. — & for half an hour or more walked on the Piazza San Marco.
