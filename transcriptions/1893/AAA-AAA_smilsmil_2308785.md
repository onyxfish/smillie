## Left Page

### Wed. April 26, 1893

**Weather:** Another cloudless day  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfs. I rigged up my umbrella in one of our two loggias & set to work making a rapid pen sketch of the view over Amalfi & beyond. To me, extremely interesting if not exactly the material (choicest) for a sketch. The sun blazed down into the corner where I worked & for an hour I baked & my eyes were sorely tried. Then I shut myself up in a dark room & stretched myself on a sofa until the equilibrium was restored. Then I went out on the Terrace & loafed until near lunch time. After lunch I had a long chat with S. Pozzi during which he gave me a history of the convent & the connection of his family with it for the past fifty years. Then Annie joined us & he took us to two apart of the old building — put up 700 years ago. We were also introduced to his sister who was very effusive. After that we went down town for a stroll with no definite object in view. We were at once besieged by hackmen & others — more persistent than leeches — then boys, & even girls, gathered about us, urging us to engage them as guides to the Cathedral — to the Campo Santo or a macaroni factory — we could not free ourselves from the pests who were worse than gadflies or leeches. Three clung to us professing to guide us to the Campo Santo, a place high up on the hill — a foul street winded bath Annie & me. The view was very fine & we went through some tunnels & climbed stairs as great as any thing we saw in Mentone or San Remo. But it was nasty — an open privy all the way, besides all other sorts of waste. We got down the hill by another stair way, very hot & tired — Annie stopped on the beach with the children and I climbed to the hotel by easy stages. Dinner at 6.30 — after Annie & I had a stroll on the garden terrace — a heavenly night.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 27, 1893

**Weather:** Hazy, overcast day — very still & warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfs. I went out on the garden terrace & commenced a pen sketch of the convent building & the cliff. I worked closely for more than two hours & got it more than half finished. From time to time I strolled up & down the terrace & rested myself. Annie spent the m'f on the beach with the boys. Lunch at 1.15 after that I rested until 3 — then again to work on my sketch & by 5.30 I had finished it. Then I again strolled up & down the terrace — most of the people in the house have gone — no one takes their places — the place is quite deserted. Dinner with the boys at 6.30 — A. at table d'hote Ev'g. A. again read aloud from Sant' Ilario.
