## Left Page

### Thur. June 15, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up for a few minutes at 4 - Heavenly night -
a beautiful calm - delicious color & ravishing
reflections - Back to bed & dreamed that I
was in Montrose & the house on fire - Up
at 8 - Light Rain - clearing soon but the
weather during the rest of the day uncertain -
After 10 J S started out with sketching things &
up Steam boat went to the Rialto where I spent
the rest of the m't making a pen sketch look-
ing from the bridge down upon the fruit market.
Walked back to hotel getting in at 1 o'clk.
Lunch - after that, a rest - then sketched
Zaccaria from 3 to 5 - then off for a tramp -
Steamboat to [illegible] landing & then for an
hour poked about the streets & canals
coming out on the Grand Canal again
near the R.R. Station - Steamboat back
getting to hotel at 6.40 - Dinner - every
all complaining of being tired - Do we
loafed on our balcony listening to the
band on the Riva not far from us, and
watching the lights on the water —
To bed before 11.

I saw a youngster about 10, fished out of
the canal this p.m. - badly scared & very
thoroughly wet — Children here do some-
times fall into the canal.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 16, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncertain sort of day - clouds at times & thun-
der showers about but only a few drops of rain
fell - In the m't. A, Z & S took Steam boat &
went to the Frari & the Scuola di S. Rocco - our first
visit to the latter - superb halls - two on the upper
floor - noble specimens of rich & harmonious dec-
oration - carved & gilded ceilings, great paintings
by [illegible] Interestos from frieze to high dado. There
& the from remarkable rich wood carvings.
The paintings, the free at in handling & technique
of any of the old paintings that I have yet seen.
Then we went into the little church of S. Rocco
adjoining but did not find anything that was
especially interesting to us - most Tintorettos &
then again into the Frari for refreshment of
memory & again admired the carved wood,
the [illegible] stalls, the gilded stone screen and the
many noble Titian. Madonna Cesaro - Then
gondola to the Grand Hotel to call on the
Grays & on Martin - [illegible] - went on & we,
after waiting awhile, learned that they'd
were not in - So, we walked to our hotel
buying fruit on our way - Lunch - After
that, Adline went with the boys to the Lido.
I had a rest & at 3 o'clk. went to my work at S.
Zaccaria, but the sun being very hideous &
the wind too fresh, after half an hour I sat - & the
hotel & continued my work there until after
5 - then out & prowled around until 6.30 -
Dinner - about 8 the Grays & d.m. came to see
us - about 8.30, the boys being already - A & I got
into their gondola with them & went to their
hotel where we passed a very pleasant hour.
A boat with very good musicians - singers, male
& female & instruments - came under the
windows (which were primo piano) with cushioned
sills - in an old Palace). Florence Gray knew them
& called out to them to sing some favorite and
characteristic songs & we had an ideal time -
the lapping water of the canal as an added
accompaniment to the music - the great form
of S. [illegible] della Salute rising before us & reflected
in the water - the old Palace room in which
we sat dimly lighted - For a few moments I
was able to give myself entirely up to it -
It was nearly 10.30 when we left & walked back
to our hotel, our steps the latter part of the way
being hastened by a light fall of rain!
