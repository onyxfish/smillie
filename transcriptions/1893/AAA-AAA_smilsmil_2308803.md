## Left Page

### Thur. June 1, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Festa, Corpus Domini — but a bad day for it — Some sunshine in the m'g but showers commenced before noon — the p'm decidedly wet — After bkfst I finished letters to Charl. — Then went to [illegible] Vecchio with Mr. Thurber to get some photos — with his camera but clouds came up before we could do anything & before we got home it began to rain. In the afternoon I took a nap to prepare myself for the work of the even'g — as most of our household had tickets for Opera and Ballet in the eve'g. Six ladies — Miss Constantino — Jack — Nettleton — Cutter (invited by A — to join us). Ely — Howe & Annie — They went over, immediately after dinner to the Politeama Fiorentino, a summer theatre on the Corso V. I was nearly near to our house. I waited until the boys were asleep & followed at about 9 & saw the larger part of a Comic Opera "La Educante di Sorrento" by Usiglio — The house & the people amused me more than the Opera did — The house very large & bare — the people in immense crowds — every row very free & easy, men smoking & wearing their hats — Entrance, 1 franc — we had best seats & paid 4 fr. for our reserved seat with two cushions — upper gallery 50 centimes — audience very noisy, shouting up approval or disapproval upon the instant — Some of the shouting sounded very funny to me — After the Opera the Ballet of "Rollé" was given in which Michaelangelo Tulus a prominent part and the scenery represented some well known scenes in Florence. It was a good show all this! I could not see it well from my seat — a great many on the stage — clever management of movement, color, etc — there was much scantiness of apparel & much high kicking — A lot of little youngsters played their parts very nicely — It was 12.15 when we got out — made home & right to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 2, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet day, rain falling gently most of the time — Bkfst — rather late — Then I wrote letters on three small notes & commenced a letter to Mr. Koehler — It was near noon when I went across the River & walked thro' some of the meaner streets there & got some slight sketches of horses, donkeys and their harness. Back to lunch at 1 — after that, a good nap to make up what I lost last night — Then, at about 4, A. & I went, in the rain, to Alinari's and spent an hour & a half looking at and selecting photos — After 6 A. went home in a bus & I prowled about until near 7 looking at buildings, streets, statues & churches (interiors) a second time to fix impressions — After dinner a short time in Salon — Then, in spite of my afternoon nap, feeling sleepy, I went to bed before 10.
