## Left Page

### Thur. May 4, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely day. A stroll on the terrace b[efore]
b[reak]f[as]t — after it I got to work on pen sketching
very promptly — until about 10.30 when,
with A — miss F. & the boys we went to the
gardens of Mrs. Reid, the widow of an English
man who lived here for about 40 years.
The house is the old Rufolo Palace — a delicious
very old, half ruin — We were charmed with
the gardens, its terraces & its views — from there
we went to the Cathedral & admired the
wonderful old pulpit of marble & mosaic —
Then on to the plaza & the fountain & back
by another path to the church of San Giovanni
where we saw another pulpit, not equal to
the one in the Cathedral, we went into the
Albergo del Roma, opposite, had a fine view
from a balcony & a drink of water & then traved
with some sort of tonic — then back to our house
to lunch at 12.30. After that I went down to
the cathedral & made a sketch from the piazza
in front — I also got some hasty sketches of the
tower from our terrace — at 4 o'clk. A — the boys
& I went on a walk thro' the town & out up on
a pathway that gave us delightful views
down the Ravine thro' which we drove up
from Amalfi — bet. 3 & 4 o'clk — thunder shower
passed over the mts north of us, after which
the air was much pleasanter — In the
afternoon I went into S. Palumbo's wine
cellars & he explained some of the processe
& wine making — at 5.30 had our eve[ning]
meal with the boys — Every feeling
cold & somewhat out of sorts I called
myself in my big rug & slept until A
came to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 5, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still charming weather. Clouds hanging on the
tops of the mts — air very warm but fresh — I
got to work soon after b[reak]f[as]t — first, in front of the
Cathedral. Then up in a terrace overlooking the
old edifice known as the Campo Santo — There
I worked until 12 — getting my sketch well
advanced, but far from finished. Then
to the house — got a short walk before
lunch at 12.30. After that I again went
out & got some very hasty pencil sketches.
At about 4 I went out with Annie & the
boys — we went first to the Church Loggia
or porch of Santa Chiara which is upon the
brink of a steep cliff. The sun was in just the
right position for the most beautiful picturesque
effect over one of the most remarkable views
that I ever looked upon — We basked in the
sunshine & revelled in the view full of en-
joyment in spite of the dirty & beggarly crowd of
young ones who insisted upon following us and
being more or less begging. The view from the
ravine — the wildly broken lines of cliffs & hills
rising to high mountains behind — the many
little villages — the masses of ruins piled and
perched upon heights seemingly almost inac-
cessible — & beyond all, the sea stretching far
far away — well — it was enchanting. Next
we descended a little & went through private
gardens to the Belvidere Cumbrone, a charming
nook, ending in a view from the edge of a
high cliff — Then back & down steep steps,
along a narrow path thro' woods at the foot
of the cliffs, returning to the house at 6 o'clk — by
way of the Campo Santo — Then I rested for
half an hour & had my beef steak with
the boys at 6.30 — after which I sat at table
thro' dinner, there being but two others, a
young English couple, besides our own party.
Very [illegible] worked for awhile on some of my
sketches.
