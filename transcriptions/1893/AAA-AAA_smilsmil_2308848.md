## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 28, 1893

**Weather:** Clear, uncomfortably warm & enervating  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, uncomfortably warm & enervating —
awoke soon after 5 after a fairly restful night
it was after 7 before I could get my bath &
after it I did not feel refreshed — Bkf. soon
after 8 — much of the m'f spent loafing
& sleeping — lunch at 1 — commenced to
read Marion Crawford's "Children of the King"
but it did not enliven me & I slept
more. The steerage passengers forward
had a good time dancing to Concertina
played by a retired U.S. Navy sailor on
his way home, a brother Far showing the
young Germans how to dance. Dinner
at 6 but we none of us devoted much
energy to it — lovely, charming moon-
light — The band played aft for the
second class passengers & they danced
— uncomfortably warm in our state
room.

Run at noon — 309 m.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 29, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day — almost cloudless & with a
delicious breeze — pretty heavy rolling in the
afternoon & ev'f. At dinner I was pretty
well ducked by a dash of water driven
in at the port hole behind me, which, altho'
closed was not screwed down close, at
the same time & in the same way my
bed was well wet. I lounged away
the day which was started early with
a bath before 7 — I had a nap before
lunch at 1 — & along one after it — Then
more loitering & loafing on deck, chatting
with Mason Bey & Mr. Delahoche, one of the
Pake's Chamberlains now on his way home
on a visit for the first time in 19 years —
There was also fun on the steerage deck
— dancing — another fine moonlight
night, but too much rolling for walking

Run at noon 395 m.
