## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 18, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy & windy — I got in a good
days work on "old Tower at Ravello" &
did not stop until about 4 o'clk —
Then I went down to Butlers & order
some frames & talked with him about
getting glass & plain mouldings for my
few sketches to cover them in the Century & Etching Club &c &c. Then to
N.A. f D. & consulted for a time with
Farrington. Then home to dinner.
Ev'g. went to Council meeting,
feeling a good deal out of sorts. The
discussion over the financial straits
of the N.A. f D. didn't make me feel
any happier & I left soon after 10,
before adjournment.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 19, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of wind & cloud & sunshine —
Took Ralph to school thro' a snow flurry — Then
to work on Old Tower — Ravello — until 12.15
then went for R — lunch at 1 — Miss
Voorhes came in at that time and had
some lunch — At 2 I went to N.A. f D.
& from there to 2° Nat. where I saw Case,
the Cashier & tried to effect a loan of
$5000 for the N.A. f D — I can get one for
4 months, but not for five years, as I wish
it. Then I went to Washington Wilson's &
had a pleasant chat with him — but he
had no money to lend — Then I went to
5th W. 10th St. & saw Wood & Nicoll. Again
stopped at N.A. & then home at 6 — Dinner
at 6.30 — while at dinner Sam'l. Mrs. Coleman
came in for a few minutes — Sue &c. A —
& I went to the Waldorf & called upon Mrs.
W. G. Parke — Charl came in — he introduced
us to Mr. Bolt, the proprietor, & we were
shown all over the building — Home at
10.30. — we met there Genevieve Wright
widow of Walter, Jas. Wright's son.
