## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 7, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely day — brilliant & mild — morning
divided between out of doors & work in
studio on some of my pen sketches —
Went down town in the course of
the m'f. — Dinner after 1 — Then I
felt heavy & did not recover until
I had had a nap. — Then I made
a scale drawing for a stone to bear
the name of Sayre to be placed on
the front of the building that Dan'l
S— is now building on Public Ave.
Took it down to him before 6 —
Tea — Ev'e'f. Reading magazines

To-day Gretta went home for a
visit leaving her sister Lucy in
her place.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 8, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More lovely weather — Late H'f.
Then Annie, Raeph & I wandered
over our pasture & down to the far
end of it to renew our acquaintance
with the swamps. I was out of doors
all the m'f., pruning trees & shrubs.
Dinner at about 2. — In the after-
noon Azur Lathrop & Mr. Gilbert,
of Ridgefield came & spent some time
in going over the house & place.
I worked until dark, pruning.
Eve'f. I wrote a congratulatory
letter to Uncle Willie & Aunt Agnes
their Golden Wedding anniversary
takes place next Tues.

I also wrote to Sittes, the Nurseryman,
an order for some trees and
shrubs — Went down to P.O. about
10.30.
