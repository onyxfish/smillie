## Left Page

### Sat. June 3, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy, with some sunshine but no rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

After help. A. & I went to the Bankers (Maquay Hooker & Co) & drew out the last $100 on the dravet, Morgaut & Co — letter of Cr. That expires on the 13th ins. — Then to Cook's office & bot. R.R. tickets to Venice — Then to Alanari to look at more photos — & there met Elie — who afterwards went with us to the Baptistry, the Duomo, then to the Pieza d'Annunziata & took a look at the ducca della Robbia Reliefs on the loggia of the Ospedale of the Innocents — Then into the Church of the Annunciation & the cloisters & had a good look at the frescos of Andrea del Sarto, so much admired by some professional critics — After this, we went our ways. A & Z went off together & I returned home — Much late, that the boys, off with Miss Cutter & the Thurber boys, did not get in until 2 o'clk — after we had finished our lunch. & A. & I had started out to find them. At about 2.30 I went to cloisters of Santa Croce & worked on my sketch there until about 5 but was unable to complete it — Then walked home — Dinner at 7.30 — Every for a time in Salon Reading newspaper — & for a time

## Right Page

### Sun. June 4, 1893

**Weather:** Again cloudy — some rain in the m'f, clearing after 4 p.m. charmingly  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rather under the weather — an upset of stomach & bowels, the result of an indescretion at table yesterday. I kept as quiet as I could, did not go out in the m'f. Worked with pen over some sketches in small sketch book — In the afternoon at about 4 o'clk. E., A., and I went to the P. Signoria to see the Tombola or public drawing of lottery — As the performance did not begin until 6 we took cab & drove in the Cascine until that hour — very charming — Then we stood in the Piazza Signoria until 7 watching the great crowd & the process of getting the numbers into the wheel & then the drawing of them out — As we did not fully understand it we did not get wildly excited but 7 we returned home by omnibus. Nighttime at 7.30 or later — After it Mrs Beason & her daughter came out & they, with E., A., & I took cab & drove to the P. Signoria to see the illumination of the Palazzo Vecchio — I was much pleased with the electric lighting of the principal streets — The effect of the ill. of the P. Vecchio & its Fale Tower was exceedingly fine — From underneath the tower appeared as though built of some translucent material & illuminated from within — very novel. We drove across the river with the B's to their Pension & had a fine distant view of the Pae. Vecchio — Then home at 10 & to bed —
