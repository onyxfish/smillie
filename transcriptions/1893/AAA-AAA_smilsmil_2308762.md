## Left Page

### Sat. March 11, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still the beautiful weather continues. Up a little earlier than usual & before the others were ready for help — I wrote (Don't a letter to Herrick.) After help — I went to Sligo's Pension & got from her the address Dr. Charles — New to the Bankers on Piazza di Spagna & drew £ 50 — Men spent the rest of the m't wandering about the streets — I got two or three photos. Lunch at 1— after that I went to Dr. Charles — 72 Via S. Nicolo del Tolentino — he listened to my story asked me many questions, had me strip & gave me a general examining & poking & then told me that he found no evidences of organic disease, my tongue indicated a much disordered condition of the mucus membrane of stomach & bowels — he did not think the spasms of pain were neurologic — what he said impressed me as reasonable this manner gave confidence — He prescribed morphia & Bismuth — Annie met me at the D's and together we walked to No. 7 Via S. Martino, passing the baths of Diocletian & into a new quarter of the city beyond the R.R. Station — the studio of W. W. Story — our cards were taken in — we were asked into an inner studio & son W. Story presented himself — a stout, jolly, medium sized, handsome man who greeted us very amiably & who took us into his sanctum sanctorum where we saw a stout lady friend who was calling on him & a clay Priestess of Pythia — a small draped figure, sitting & with outstretched arms & a sad face, seem to be in the act of invocation — with his red secret cap on his head & grey suit, he walked up & down smoking his cigarette, cracking jokes and making some pretty bad puns that seemed to put him in great good humor — after about 15 or 20 m — we bid him good bye. A. took cab & I wandered about until near 6 — then to hotel — dinner at 6.30 — congratulating myself that I had got through the day without pain, but it came on at about 8 and made the rest of the eveg disagreeable.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 12, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — clear & warm in the sunshine — very quite breeze — After an uncomfortable night got up late in the m'g & did not have bfst. until 9.30 — Sometime spent in Reading — then, with the two boys, (A. did not feel like going out) I wandered across the new bridge Margherita to the west side of the Tiber, taking a look at the great walls that, now far advanced, are to enclose the river — we walked a little ways along the high bank of the river away from the city — The air & the sunshine was delicious & I saw one subject of which I wish to make a sketch — We soon returned & I went up to the terrace on the Pincian Hill for a few minutes — then to the hotel to lunch — After that I had some pain & I lay down on the bed & fell asleep — at 3 I got up, my head aching very severely — Rheumatic — Resulting from having my hair cut yesterday m'g, as was nearly by Colm A. & I went out — The boys had gone with our neighbors (Colwell) with those daughters they became very intimate — we went thro' the Porta del Popolo & into the Borghesi grounds where we were delighted by many charming landscape bits — walks under live oak avenues — old fountains — bits of temples or lichen-covered statuary — The Italian Stone Pines are a feature here — then came some bits like English thickets — great crowds of people wandered everywhere, over the grass & under the trees gathering wild flowers — Roads were filled with vehicles — we saw the Queen pass three times — at one time her carriage was stopped where we stood & we had the best chance to study a Royal face — a very pleasant [illegible] one we found it to be. In the eveg. it ans [illegible]alk, before the boys got to sleep & we were able to go to the Michel Reis & in Villa Sistina (where Slige is now stopping) to hear some music, this being the regular event for it — we were none too late as the exercises did not begin before 9.30 — It was a very bright pleasant affair — Mad. M's family & their friends all appearing in force — There was some very good male singing (solos) a good violinist & a remarkable exhibition of what might be called serious music on the mandolin with piano accompt. — We left at about 10.30 — at long much later before I got to bed — under Dr. Charles' directions I made my first experiment with glycerine to secure a movement of the bowels.
