## Left Page

### Thur. July 27, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy but pleasant & cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy but pleasant & cool — got up feeling very tired & used up — have been sticking to my work a little too closely so, will take things easy for a day or two — After 10 when I left the house — walked thro' Hyde Park — went to the New Gallery in Regent's St. near Piccadilly Circus — & was surprised to find a small & very sane exhibit of paintings, with an average of artistic excellence higher I think than that of the R.A. — I went thro' it very carefully & had my lunch in the building — I left at 3 o'clk & walked down to Pall Mall to the Gallery of the Royal Socy of Painters in Water Colors & bot. a cat. Then to the Nat. Gallery and spent about an hour with my old friends there, then walked back up Regent's St. to Liberty's & at 5 o'clk. met Annie there & with her selected a piece of embroidery — then home on top of a bus — had a short nap before dinner at 7 — Nelly wrote a note to Alfred Jones.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 28, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy & muggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy & muggy nearly all of the day — Mr[.] & I went by underground R.R. to the City — just to Great Tower St. — Johnson & Sons, where I paid charges on trunks in storage & ordered them to be sent to the house — then to J.S. Morgan & Co — old Broad St. & got my etched plate — portrait of J.S. Morgan — Mr. Robt. Gordon in Scotland — then to office of Mr. [illegible] Lloyd & 3.5 — Grace Church & Fenchurch Sts & to find bal. on passage home. Then home by R.R. getting in soon after 1 — lunch — then I had a long rest — Annie went with the boys & had a row on the Serpentine — about 4 trunks arrived from Salmon & Son — I wrote notes of Mr[.] Durant & Mr. Gordon — then a walk in the Park & dinner at 7 — Nelly & Annie's [illegible] dress came home from Liberty's —
