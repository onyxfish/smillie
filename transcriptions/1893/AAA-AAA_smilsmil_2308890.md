## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 22, 1893

**Weather:** Mild, pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild, pleasant day — after taking R.
to school I got to my work on for some studio
— went for R. at 12.30 — lunch at 1 and
again I worked as long as I could see.
After H R. & I went out for a walk,
went up to 58th St. — & back — Dinner
before 7 — Emma, Mary & Grace I —
with us. Charl came in for a
few minutes after 9 — badly bunged
up with a cold — To bed after 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 23, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*went to Po'keepsie —*

Early m[orning], bright, but soon grew misty
& overcast — not really cold. Annie and
I took 10.30 train, after I had taken R. to school,
& with Mr. Geo. went to Po'k. getting there
at 12.30 — went right to Mary's house and
at 1 o'clk. we had a good dinner. This
is our old anniversary day. Father's and
Will's birth day & Mary & Matie's wedding day.
— A sad, rather than a joyous anniversary.
After dinner Geo. & I walked a mile or more
on the "South Road", beyond the cemetery. On our
return went to Uncle Willie's & spent some
time there with his family — the Hinshelwood
gives & Mr. & Mrs. D[illegible] being there as guests.
A black darkie, name for [illegible], was also there,
playing on the piano for Dining. Stood up
tea at Mary's at 6.30 — Eva[?], Uncle W. and
Aunt A. came, with Aunt Jessie, the H's
& the D's & Ed[?] Parker — This was our cele-
bration of Uncle W's golden wedding, that
properly took place after 10 Oct. Uncle
W. & Aunt A. seemed to be wonderfully well
& very happy. Uncle W. was as jolly and full
of fun as a boy — a joy to see — The evening
passed very pleasantly & after 10 we had
chocolate, cream & cakes — Charl came
up in an afternoon train, getting in before
6 —
