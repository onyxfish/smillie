## Left Page

### Sat. July 29, 1893

**Weather:** Cool, cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool, cloudy day with some smart showers. Feeling tired yet I took things
easily in the m[orning] & did not leave the
house until 10 — it was nearly when I
got to the museum & at work in the
Print Room still following up aqua-
tint study — looking over portfolios and
reading references etc. — After 10.4,
I took lunch in the museum lunch
Room & then spent some time over the
Nimrod Bronzes (bowls etc.) that are
decorated, as I think, by a bitten line.
Then again to Print Room — there until
near 5 — then sauntered home, just
after a shower, & had time for a short
rest before dinner at 7 — a pleasant
note from Mr. Rob't Gordon, saying that
J.P. Morgan had expressed himself to him
as pleased with my etching of his father —
Read aloud a short story to A —

## Right Page

### Sun. July 30, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Richmond —*

A pleasant day — that is, the sun
shone out several times & we had to
put up our umbrellas only once —
I spent the m[orning] in a lounge feeling
very stupid — lunch at 1 — Annie,
the boys & I went to Bishop's Road Station
& at 2.40 took train for Richmond, getting
there in about ½ an hour — hired an open
vehicle & drove around the Park, reviving
memories of long ago — Had we remembered
more clearly at first we would it have
taken the drive — it rained a very little as
we rode — on our way back — we got out at the
Terrace — went thro' Star & Carter Inn to the
Terrace — sunshine & the views very lovely —
Then we walked thro' the Terrace Gardens to
the River & there we hired a boat & a man
to row us down to Kew — it was breezy on the
river but the tide was full & we found
much that we thought very beautiful —
Then we went thro' the Kew Gardens & into
some of the houses — we had plenty of time
to saunter about — took 6.40 train back to
London & got to our house at about 7.20 &
dinner at 7.30 — short every — Read for
awhile — wrote a note to Mr. Rob't Gordon
& then to bed.
