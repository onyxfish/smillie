## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 30, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A great deal of rolling last night — so much that a pretty constant racket was kept up. The nt'f. was charming, in the afternoon the weather thickened somewhat & the long swell went down, we passed very close to a Cunarder going east. I felt a little more energetic & walked more, but I did not enjoy it really, & I had a little nap in the n'tf & a long one in the afternoon & in the eveg finished reading the second book since we started.

Run at noon 396 m.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 31, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day, at times almost thick — no sea — getting fresher & cooler in the afternoon — & clearer — but at night a spitting rain. I was up rather earlier than usual & under a new arrangement with the bad-meister I had my bath soon after 6 & then to bed for an hour. Before lunch I had a good, solid nap.

Jim was very much out of sorts about dinner time & soon after, the result of "errors of diet" — vomiting & diarreah with pain. Eveg I loafed about, napping in corners. About 10 p.m. some thick weather & a few toots on the infernal fog whistle.

Run at noon 394 m.
