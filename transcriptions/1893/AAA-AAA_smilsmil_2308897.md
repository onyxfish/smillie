## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 6, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. Ralph's temperature had fallen to a little above 101°. & after lunch, Temp°. normal, he was allowed to get up. I got through with my out-of-door messages, then to work with water-colors finishing incomplete sketches made while abroad. Suema J. came in the m/f & gave Jim his first lesson on the piano & stayed to lunch. Afternoon I continued my work until 5. Then I went to N.A.f D. & signed a lot of [illegible] & took a look at the fall exh[ibition] opened on Monday. Home after 6. Dinner at 6.30. Our neighbor, Mr. Martin, sent in a note stating that Jim had broken some of the glass in his dining room window during the afternoon, while snowballing with Tom, so I went in to see him & make my apologies. He was very agreeable & told me some amusing stories about our tenants, the Macks. According to his representations they were disreputable & disorderly. There an hour. Home. Charl came in & stayed until 10.45. He is recovering from a very severe cold.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 7, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

many ret°. to Po'keepsie

Fine day. Ralph was well enough to go with me to school this m/f. Then I got to work upon a water-color sketch "Campanile at Lugano" — body-color on grey tinted crayon paper. I was soon very much interested & worked closely until 2.15, went for R. Lunch at 1. Many ret°. to Po'k by 3.30 train. I worked as long as I could see but did not finish my sketch. Before 5 I walked up to 70th St. & 5th Ave. Met Ge° out walking with Gordon, we walked together for awhile. Home after 6. Dressed. & A. & I went to Ed. W. Cook's where we dined en famille. Later in the eve/f Jack Dorms came in. Home soon after 10.
