## Left Page

### Mon. May 22, 1893

*Florence. 10 Via Solferino.*
*Pension of Misses Falk & Constantine.*

**Weather:** Brilliant, delightful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, delightful day — late in the afternoon,
in Florence, we had two or three light showers.
Up at 6.30 — bkfst. at 7.30 — Paid up — fee'd up &
soon after 8 started for the station & got off
on time at 9 o'clk. I found the country
thro' which we passed unexpectedly interesting —
The color on distant mts. very lovely
&; in spite of dry weather, the green was very
fresh — In passing Lake Trasimeno the
country presented so many Rome-like
aspects — the distant mts. unfamiliar, New
England farm & color — the trees uncut & untrimmed — that I had a touch
of homesickness. Some of the mountain
scenery before reaching Florence was
very fine. I got to the end of our journey at
2.20 — in time & in good shape — we ate lunch
prepared for us at the Russia on the tram —
we Rose at once to our quarters, secured for
us by Elsa Howe, & met her on the way — Then
I set to to station for baggage — a struggle at the
house with Coachman & Porters who at first
charged us an aggregate of 12 francs & finally we
got off for 8.50, still a considerable imposition —
while our rooms were being put in order and
A. & Elsa. were having a good time "gabbin'!"
I sauntered to the D. Uomo & into it — I had
not expected to enjoy it — & I was not disappointed — The newly finished front is very disagreeable to me. Back at 5 — Rested — A.
went out riding with E—. Dinner at 7 —
Even in Salon — Mr. Thurburn & Minneapolis
showed me a number of photos. that he has
recently taken in Naples & vicinity —

## Right Page

### Tues. May 23, 1893

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f — overcast afternoon with light showers  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'f — overcast afternoon with light
showers before 6 — a heavy, brief shower about 8.30
P.M — about 10 A.M — A. the boys & I drove to P.O — &
there got letter from N. German Lloyds with receipt
for money sent & saying that we could have our
first choice on the Saale, sailing Aug. 26th — Then
we took a look at the Palazzo Vecchio and the
Piazza d. Signoria. Then we walked up the Via
Calzaioli & — shopping for shoes & hats for the
boys & thru through Piazza V.i.e. Emanuela to the
Via Tornabuoni where we did more shopping
for boys clothes — then down thro' the Porta Rossa
to & past the old market for more shopping —
then Rose home just in time for lunch.
Ralph is out of sorts — Trouble with bowels —
After lunch I was to go to La Certosa, the
old monastery 3 or 4 miles out of town where
the famous Chartreuse cordial is manufactured —
but the weather was so threatening that we
gave up the trip — instead, I jun & I walked
down the Lung Arnat to the Ponte Vecchio,
crossed it & went out to the Pitti Palace —
then back, recrossed the bridge, thro' the
Uffizi, taking a good look at things, up to
the Corso Borgo d' Albizzi where we looked
at the outside of some of the palaces, then
through to the D. Uomo — in & around that, the
tower & the Baptistry — then to S.M. Novella
& into that Church, then home just before 6 —
we were somewhat bothered by the
showers. The boys had their every meal
at 6 — A. with them while I had a nap
before dinner at 7. Even in the Salon —
