## Left Page

### Mon. March 27, 1893

**Weather:** clear, warm m/f. with pleasant cool breeze  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, warm m/f. with pleasant cool breeze that increased until the afternoon when it was decidedly marchey & unpleasant, altho' still brilliantly clear — the mts, islands & outline of the bay shimmering with lovely color. My cold in the head still very distressing — After 10 I went not alone & going very slowly so as not to get overheated & covering mouth & nose with m[illegible] ldrf as a respirator. I accomplished a very good walk — I went to the east up the Via Tasso to the top of the hill getting no end of fine views over the city & bay to Vesuvius & the mts beyond — then I turned eastward & came over a very uninteresting road through the village of Pomaro & finally down a long reach of steps to Posilippo via Naso not far from our hotel — just time to have a short rest before lunch. After that A — the boys & I went down the hill to the Public Gardens & to the Aquarium where we spent a long time looking at the many strange creatures there — the boys & I treated ourselves to a shock from the electric eel, which is not at all at all but a great & disagreeable looking fish — He was very much exhausted, nervously, having shocked a great number of curious visitors already today thereby greatly impairing his powers. Back to hotel before 5 — glad to get there it was so windy, cold & dusty out of doors. Had a chat with Mr. Bidder — then to our rooms and lighted fire & thawed out — then a rest before dinner at 6.05 — Eve'g Reading Baedeker Naples —

## Right Page

### Tues. March 28, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless day — not quite so cool or so breezy as yesterday — At 10 A & I, leaving the boys at home to play in doors, took steam tram car for the Museum — for some reason or other it left us near the top of the hill & did not take us down to the Museum — we tramped thro' the streets, finding the Neapolitans swarming, vociferous, having strong characteristic marks and, generally, dirty — our movements very superficial — Suffered from my severe cold I could not loiter with deliberation on such cold stone floors or in such a cellar-like atmosphere — We passed thro' the truly alien frescoes — the statuary, the bronzes, vases, gems, coins & household objects — For some reason not understandable to me, I was not permitted to enter the obscene gallery — It was 12.30 when pretty generally stiffened & congealed, I left the building & took cab back to hotel. Lunch at 1 — Aftn. the boys went out with the boys & we amused ourselves for a while & watched the great, white clouds of steam roll out of Vesuvius. We were at rest & before sunset out along & climbed to a height back of the hotel & got some delightful outlooks over the bay & to the mountains beyond. Back soon after 6. Dinner [illegible] — Every — a strolling band gave us a concert during dinner time & the early part of the eve'g after it — then I read & wrote a little —
