## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 15, 1893

**Weather:** A clouded m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

but glorious afternoon —
Mr. & Mrs. Gifford came in at about to o'clk.
just before — we all went into town together.
I went to Bank & got my $20 — changed into
francs — Then our part of the party returned
to Hotel to dejeuner — Immediately after — at
1 o'clk — 2 — A — the two boys & I took bus at foot
of garden & rode on to Public garden where
we were joined by Col. Davis & his daughter
who got into the bus with us & rode on to the
end of the route — Here we passed
thro' gardens & commenced a long climb
up a cobble-stone path, thro' beautiful olive
groves — we had some exquisite views
of the trees over a sea bluer than any
I have ever seen — lovely beyond words to tell —
in the shadow of the trees we looked out to
the east until we reached the crest of the
ridge & then we looked down, down, to the
sunny sea — with Monte Carlo & Monaco beyond
Soon we came to Roquebrune — a strange
huddle of extremely old houses clustered
about the remains of a picturesquely
situated castle — Col. Davis had his camera
& took some photos — & I made a very slight
pencil sketch on a terrace — some of the
passage ways that we went thro' — up steep
inclines — sometimes steps & under crooked
arches, were very remarkable — It was
delightfully exciting to me & I was much
pleased to find that I had sufficient
strength for the tramp — It was about 4.30
when again we reached the public road
at the foot of the mt. By bus we all to Public
garden where went with Col. & daughter to
their villa where we had coffee etc — & met
among others, Mrs. Stanbury of the celebrated
Stanbury Villa & gardens &c — east of Mentone.
we walked more than half way to our hotel —
then bus — in at a little before 6 — I dined
with the boys at 6.30 — Walt — lounging in
front of a pleasant little fire & to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 16, 1893

**Weather:** Bright & beautiful until near 11  **Temp:** [illegible]

then strong, cool
wind & thin cloud — stronger wind & colder in
the afternoon & denser clouds — After dejo —
I went out upon the Terrace — set up sketching
umbrella & commenced a pen sketch of a
subject there that has interested me very much
— but before [illegible] I was obliged to give up work it
became so windy & cold — then with the boys
I went off & had a little walk before dejeuner
at 12 — Immediately after that A. & E. went
to Monte Carlo to hear a choice concert at
the Casino — they failed to get up to the Casino
& then went on to Monaco & after wandering
about there set to Mentone about 6 o'clk —
I worked on my pen & ink sketch awhile &
at 3 I went out with the boys for a walk —
climbed a mule path starting from the
cemetery & went up the mountain for a
mile or more — some fine views but the wind
& absence of sunshine lessened the pleasures
& the beauty very much — we climbed
among olive & lemon groves & vineyards
almost as far up as there was cultivation
then found a path to an unfinished road
that led into the old road that led
to the town & by which we reached our
starting point at the Cemetery — It was
about 5.30 when we got in, all pretty
well tired. The boys & I dined at
6.30 — Early. I did a little reading while
[illegible] wrote, Fornie a letter to Kate.
I find myself unwilling, if not incapable
of making any mental effort — I am
still afraid that there is some serious
trouble brewing — To day I have wondered
if it may not be in the kidneys.
