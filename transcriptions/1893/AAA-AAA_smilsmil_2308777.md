## Left Page

### Mon. April 10, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A slight improvement upon yesterday. Just enough to keep me from
falling into utter hopelessness — S°.
Scottie came at noon & expressed himself so well satisfied that he said
he comes not come again before next
Thurs, unless sent for. He said, also,
that he had completed analysis of the
specimen of my urine & found it entirely
normal. This is a great comfort to
me. Annie rec'd a letter from Mrs. Fant
of Buffalo saying that owing to her condition
of her very aged mother they could not, to
their great regret, occupy the Montrose
house this summer.

Evely. Annie again read aloud
from Crawford's "Don Orsino."

## Right Page

### Tues. April 11, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very slow improvement continues — only
2/5° of a degree better than yesterday — less
pain & discomfort in the head. Neighbors on
our left, having front rooms, communicating
with ours, expect to vacate them on Friday & Sat.
Annie filed an application with the manager
for the room adjoining in place of the inner
soundless room that we now have. The two
sunny rooms will be much better for our
patiently hoped for convalescence. Jim
has coughed & complained a good deal today & has been kept carefully housed.
Annie & Ralph got out for awhile in the
afternoon. I rec'd a letter from Mary —
March 30° — & a cat. of Saml. Colman's Sale
from Charl.

Annie wrote a note to Gretta Sullivan
asking her if she & Katie Coleman cared
to return to her service when we get
back home.
