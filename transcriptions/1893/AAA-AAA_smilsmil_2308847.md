## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 26, 1893

*Our last day in London.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, tho' mostly cloudy — after
bkfst. I took a run with the boys in Ken-
sington Gardens, & then set to the house
& took things very quietly, packing — We
have four large trunks, a steamer trunk,
a basket, a valise, three hand bags, a
big grab-all & a bundle of umbrellas
& sticks — a formidable array. Annie
has her packing so well advanced that
she had but little to do — Lunch after
1 — Then she took the boys to Boynton's
Aquatic Show at Earl's Court & I completed
my packing which was generally distrib-
uted — at 5.30 I lay down & rested until
dinner shortly after 7 — There were a
few little things that I found to do in
the eve'g — To bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 27, 1893

*On board Steamer Saale.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 198  **Berths:** 195

Pleasant day, tho' mostly cloudy — Bkfst. at 9.
Before that time I had everything finished up —
trunks locked & hand bags all ready for a
start — after bkfst. we put up a lunch of cold
chicken & rolls & then, with Ralph I went out
to Kensington Gardens & had a half-hours ram-
ble over the grass & under the trees — The bus
was at the door long before 11 & our luggage
was after much labor piled upon it — we
had, at the last moment, to give up a small
"wash" that had been sent to a laundress on
Thursday, as lost; she claiming that the carrier
had not given it to her — at a few minutes after
11 we left the house — at 11.35 we got to Waterloo
Station & before 12 luggage & tickets had been at-
tended to & we were in our 1st class seats, but we
had to wait 25 m. more until starting time.
We had a pleasant run down, two others
sharing our carriage with us* — we at our lunch
at 1.15 & got to the quay at 2.30 — There were
not many passengers & in half an hour we got
away & steamed down to the Saale, 5 m? miles
below — we got to her in a few minutes & our
passengers, luggage & mails were transferred
on at 4 o'clk — the Saale turned head & steamed
out — It was just 5 as we passed the Needles at
the end of the Isle of Wight — There seemed to be
showers ahead but the afternoon was very
lovely, the air pleasant & the water smooth.
It could hardly have been pleasanter — at 6
we had a long drawn out dinner in a dist. dining
saloon — only a moderately full pass'r list —
about 80 first class. Every walked the deck
until I was tired out.

* Mason Bey & wife.
