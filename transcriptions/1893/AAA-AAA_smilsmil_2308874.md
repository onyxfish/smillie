## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 19, 1893

**Weather:** Heavy clouds during the m'f. and from the east — raw but not cold — Some sunshine during the p.m. — wind more southerly — pleasant eve'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Much of my time in the m'f. given to oversight of the men — some of the material for tanks + bathroom at the house, brought from the mill. — I worked in studio upon plans + laying out a plan to give me correct uniform angles in laying mezzotint ground. Went down town about 11 o'cl. Dinner at 1.30 — Afternoon, at work in the m'f — at twilight walk on the piazza — Stupid eve'g, owing to dyspeptic discomforts.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 20, 1893

**Weather:** Heavily overcast m'f. growing clearer all day until eve'g. when it was beautiful moonlight — quite mild.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent between 2 & 3 hours rocking a mezzotint ground on small 3" × 4" plate for W. S. R. Koehler. also did a good deal of work in some of the trees sawing out unnecessary branches. Work progressed on the barn sills & on the ditch for the pipe for water. At dusk W. Azur Darthra's came up to tell me that Mrs. H. H. Tyler, her her lawyer, had consented to sell her lot adjoining us for $500 — so, I agreed to pay that for it — A. L. to take the deed & transfer it to me some time during the winter.
At 7.20 this telegram was delivered. "Jim has hurt his eye with a percussion cap. Sight perfect but the accident may prove serious and we think you had better come home — Mary S. Cook."
Annie at once packed her trunk — I went down to the stage office & ordered stage to come to house in time for early m'g train —
