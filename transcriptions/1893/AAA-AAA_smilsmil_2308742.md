## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 30, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day altho' much of the time cloudy. Still feeling very weak & much discomforted — discouraged because I do not improve as each day I hope to — I went out for a little while in the m'f, but could not go far & did not enjoy it. In the afternoon A. E. & I went to the Municipal Casino in the Place Massena & heard a female orchestra discourse common place music in a very ordinary way — our tickets being good for the day we sauntered out & along the Promenade just before sunset then back again — I left them & back to hotel & gave the boys their supper. Every at about 9 o'clk — I went out & wandered thro' some of the streets — enjoying the simple moonlight — the crowds of people & the many café bands. I went again to the Casino noting people there — not nice — clouds of tobacco smoke & a good deal of a little gambling — 1 to 15 fr. on the little horses" and upon tables marked blue, red & black — my headache'd severely & I was glad to get out & into the open air — Back to Hotel at 10 — & to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 31, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright sunshine until about 4 o'clk — light breeze — delightfully warm. Got up at 7½ a.m. with very severe headache which had bothered me more or less all night — A strong dose of Bromo-Caffein gave me some relief — it was 10:30 when I went out to the Promenade & there I stayed with the boys, sunning myself, until after 12 — A fisherman on the beach had a large octopus in a tub of water, exhibiting it — After dejeuner, the boys went off with their French teacher & at 2 A. Ely & I rode to the cemetery on Castle Hill to see the tombs of Garabaldi & Gambetta — The first, we found in about ½ of the way corner of the cemetery — a very modest street with about an average amount of conventional wreaths and chicken wire & bead work — The Gambetta tomb made us laugh even while we recognized the sadness of the habit. Upon a plateau, the highest & most commanding position in the cemetery, stands a wooden pyramid about 15 or 20 ft. high — covered to its top on small sides with faded, weather worn & and ragged wreaths bearing inscriptions, most of them having convex glass in the centre covering some childish designs — the whole thing was poor, patry & mournful to the last degree. We then rode to the summit and enjoyed the view over the city, the seat the nice men on, down, past the port and out around the city nearby to the forts where we were much interested in the splendid show of armored war vessels, some 8 or 10 of them lying in the roadway — also torpedo boats etc. — we saw one torpedo exploded under water. On our way back we had some beautiful effects of sunshine from under a heavy cloud bearing down upon a very quiet, shimmering sea — in before 4:30. I took my tea with the boys & got to bed early.
