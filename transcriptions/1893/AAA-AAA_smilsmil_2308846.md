## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 24, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day, bright, cool breezy. I walked down to the Museum stopping at Newman's & giving an order for some Turner Brown, ground dry for printing — It was 11 when I got to the Print Room but by working diligently I got through with 2 or 3 of the Sam'l Cousins volumes — after 4 o'clk. I walked down to Trafalgar Sq. & to Cockspur S't. to Office of No. German Lloyd Steamship Co. — to see about R.R. Tickets to which I believe we are entitled — but this m'f I forgot to take with me my steamer receipt & nothing could be done without it — Then I walked home — along walk — & got in very tired — had a rest until dinner — Well! I packed until after 10 — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 25, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day — walked down to the Museum doing some shopping on the way — Bought penknives for two of the attendants in Print Room & a couple of neck ties for the young man who sit by the door & smiled upon me as I entered — also a cigar case of lizard's skin for Chase — at the B.M. I finished the Cousins Call — frezzatints, left a note of good bye for Mr. Cust, said my good-byes to the others & got away before 1 o'clk. — Home & had lunch; in the afternoon Annie took the boys to Barnum's show. I went again to the No. German Lloyd Office & succeeded in getting 3 1st cl. R.R. tickets from London to Southampton — while standing in Trafalgar Sq. making a sketch Wm Magroth surprised me — he too had been sketching on the Sq. — has been on this side for 2 or 3 years — we had quite a long chat — then I walked up to Soho Sq. — confectid. purchases & paid bill & bid Mr. Mills goodbye. Then home by bus & rested until dinner at 7 — evening I looked over my Cousins notes & extended them somewhat by adding a Resumé.
