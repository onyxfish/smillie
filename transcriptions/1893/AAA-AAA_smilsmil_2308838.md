## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 8, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

to Windsor Castle & Burnham Beeches.

an almost cloudless & uncomfortably
warm day. Got to museum at 10·5 & worked
on my hunt after early aqua-tints until
12. – then home. Lunch at 1 – at 1.50 we
all took train from the Paddington Station
for Windsor getting there at 2.30 – we went
at once to the Castle – climbed to the top
of the Round Tower, after we had gone thro'
St. Georges Chapel & the Prince Albert Memorial
then we spent a little time on the Terrace
& at 3.30 took horse & wagon & drove over to
Eaton where we went into the Chapel &
then to the play ground – after which we
drove to Burnham Beeches, getting there at
4.30 – the road, in parts, was very pleasant –
under noble elms & between hawthorne hedge
rows. The beeches we found to be wonderful
old trees, of course. We turned our horse and
boy out of the road – we got down & enjoyed
a half hour ramble very much – at 5 we
drove to Slough, most of the way over the road
over which we came & got to St. before 6 –
our train was due at 6·03 – it came 10 m. late
& waited 15 m – but we got in to Paddington
at 7 – only 20 m. late – dinner at 8 – very
tired & hungry – a very short eve –
Annie read a short story aloud.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 9, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot, cloudless day – Got to Museum at 10·15
& again to work upon this aquatint field that
grows as I work. I am beginning to doubt if it
is worth the harrowing & the time that I am
giving to it. Lunch at 1.30 & then out for a
very short time – went to Mudie's & got a
book for Annie – Worked at museum until
4 – then, on top of a 'bus to Uxbridge Rd. Station
– walked to Goulding's – saw his son but he
(T.G.) was out of town – so I had my long
journey for nothing. – Home, getting in
at 5.45 – rested until dinner at 7 –
Eve., working on my notes – Rec'd. letter
from Kate.
