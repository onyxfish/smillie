## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 6, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another overcast, raw day. Ther. down about freezing point all day — clear starlight at night. Sly. H. came at about 10 & at 10.30, She, Annie & I went to the Russian Chapel in the Rue Dame to attend ceremonies celebrating the Russian Xmas — a small building, odd in appearance — I do not know that it belongs to any order of architecture — gaudily decorated — some paintings — a great number of candles — carpeted from & very few chairs — all remained standing during the ceremony in which five very richly robed priests took part. The ceremony was very & infascinating by the priests & responses by a male choir of very fine voices but imperfect training — a very high treble boy's voice — pure & sustained was very lovely — a gong was an impressive interposition — & a sort of chant by the five priests, two of whom had fine voices, was impressive, if not more. The service was over before 12 — a large part of the congregation pressed to the altar to kiss a very ornamental cross held by one of the priests. Then we went home — lunch — Annie went again to the dentist and I took the boys & went to the architectural gallery at the Trocadéro. The immense casts there of great doorways & like architectural works are very wonderful — as a fine school for a student — a large number of blouses & sabots there, reading their newspapers & getting warm. At 4, closed. Then we went out, down Kellier to the Champs Elysées — around the Eiffel Tower & then home. I rested — Dinner before 7 — Evening I wrote to Prof. Collins telling him that I had just rec'd a note from Mr. R.W. Giesen.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 7, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant, slightly hazy day & much milder in the [illegible]. I walked down to the Bibliothèque Nationale. Rue des Petits Champs & Rue de Richelieu. There I enquired of the Secretary & he told me that if I brought a card from the Art Section it would secure for me the privileges of a student in the Print collection — then I went to Cook's — Rue de l'Opéra, & got P. & M. Time Table. Then home to lunch — found Elegh H. with Annie who had been with the boys to the Lake in the Bois to skate — Elegh lunched with us — it was 2.30 when again I started out — crossed the Pont d'Iéna, took an Ave. Rapp tram & rode out to near the Luxembourg Pal. & then went in & saw the paintings & sculpture — was much surprised to find new buildings in which the collection is placed — when we were here last it was housed in the old Palace — the new galleries are finely lighted — I had but a very short time & could only glance at a few of the works — I recognized some old friends & missed some — I was delighted to find Whistler's Mother, in one gallery, vis a vis to Sargent's Carmencita — There were several pictures that affected me very disagreeably, one being a grand full naked woman by Mouliet — There were two great canvases by E. Detaille that greatly disappointed me — I not Elegh. H. Then together we saw thro' the galleries which closed at 4 — I then put her into a bus & went to the Panthéon — too late — then to St. Etienne & heard part of a sermon — then down the Boul. S. Michel along the quai to Pont Neuf & took Main car at the corner & rode to the Pont de l'Alma & thence home. Dinner before 7 — Well, spent some time over a French service set that I bought this [illegible].
