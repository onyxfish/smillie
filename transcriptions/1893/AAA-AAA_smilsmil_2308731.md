## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 8, 1893

**Weather:** Overcast & quite mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast & quite mild — Bkfp. bet. 9 & 10 o'clk — Then I sat & wrote — letters to R. W. Gilder — To Prof. Calvin — C. F. W. Mielatz & Johnson & Sons — Lely & Hattie K. came to take the boys home with them to lunch — Tleg. Home came & lunched with us & then we hurried away to the Theatre du Chatelet to a Colonne concert — our seats — the best we could get, were away up in the Premier Amphitheatre — hard, cushionless benches, narrow & restricted seats — 4 fr. each. This is the 19th year of these Concerts conducted by M. Ed. Colonne — The orchestra numbered 94 — chorus, mixed, 100 — one leading Soprano, Mlle Berthe de Montalant & five male voices — Warmbrodt, a wise tenor, & [illegible] — Tournets — Doraillier & Gallois — The piece was by Hector Berlioz — "L'enfance due Christ" — Trilogie Sacré — It was a very interesting & satisfactory performance to me — There was no brass — no straining of chorus against orchestra — all parts were well within the limits of their power & all showed sentiment & training & some of the effects were beautiful & wonderfully well done. Scene VI in Part 3. The invisible angels, Mary and Joseph — the angels were behind the scenes & sang their message & then their retreating voices as they went heaven-ward grew fainter & fainter until singing Hosannah! Hosannah! until the breathless house could barely hear the last far — far away note. It was deliciously done — The "finale" "Choeur mystique" & the Angels' "Amen! Amen!" Should have been heard in absolute stillness, without a movement for five min. after the close. It was spoiled by impatient people & slamming doors. We got out of the stuffy, dirty old theatre, big and overflowing with an immense crowd at 4.45 — walked to Maniway at dinner — E. & A. Rode, I walked all the way home. A great feast to-night at Mlle. Arnavon's — crowded table — a number of invited guests — Slez — Mrs K — Lilly & Hattie among them — Pretty good dinner & some very sweet champagne — a regular comb followed in the salon — Mlle Arnavon appearing to be in great spirits — I retired to our room at about 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 9, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very mild day — thaw & mud — sunny o'hf. Rain in the afternoon — Annie went to the dentists immediately after bkfp — Jim, Ralph & I soon followed — went first to Cook's office & bought tickets for Nice — then to dentist & met A — then out & did a little shopping — leaving Ralph with A — Jim & I sauntered home, getting there just in time for lunch — Annie went to the K's in the afternoon but I stayed in & loafed — did some packing — dinner at 7 — quietly. Reading newspapers.
