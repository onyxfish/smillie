## Left Page

### Wed. May 24, 1893

**Weather:** Bright, fresh mt.  **Temp:** [illegible]

afternoon some heavy clouds & showers passing to the north — After H[illegible] — we started out with a cab & went first to Deman & Co — & arranged to have them send for our now superfluous baggage & ship it to London — Then to a shop & bought a basket in which to pack some of the baggage — Then to the church of S. Croce, an edifice that interested us very much by peculiarities of construction — to frescoes and to tombs — Then into the cloisters, the refectory & the small chapel of the Pazzi — In these cloisters, the architectural forms, the vegetation, the sunshine & the birds delighted us — I have come down to the Uffizj where A— left me & went on the boys started the R[illegible]. We'll. I walked hastily thro the immense corridors & the many galleries filled with wonder & admiration & wishing that I were a young man able to settle here for a term of study — I feel that the old fellows were so sober & serious that I have little patience with the vagaries & grotesqueries of the advanced young men of the day. It was just 1 when we got to the Pension — lunch — after that, a rest. At 3.30 E., A., & I took cab & drove to the Porta Romana — dismissed our coachman & then walked up the Vale Mach[illegible]avelli — at this part a fine avenue of cedars & cypresses & live-oaks — We had some very charming views, after quite a long walk we got to the fortress & church of S. Miniato al Monte, now a Cambo Santo — very interesting building up on a beautiful height overlooking the city — a little lower down we went into the church S. Salvatore del Monte about which are arranged noble cypresses — then on down to the Piazzale Michel angelo — a very extensive terrace — in the centre of which stands a bronze copy of M. Angelo's "David" — from this terrace there is a wonderfully beautiful view of river, city, valley & distant mountains — the light was lovely — the air fresh & bracing — the perfume of flowers delicious & the song of birds most delightful — we lingered a long time — A. & E. had coffee in the Cafe. Then we continued our walk through the Vale Michelangelo & crossed the river by the Ponte in ferro & thence by cab to our Pension getting back just before 7. — Rather breezy & cold — Dinner — Eveg. I wrote as called to Kate & marked it.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 25, 1893

**Weather:** Overcast but not unpleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

some smart showers in the afternoon that prevented our going out until 3.30 — After we took a cab — & A. & O. drove to the Piazza chapel — burial place of the Grand Dukes of the Medici family — a great Octagonal, Dome covered building, encrusted by many colored marbles, frescoes & gilt work — Then into the new Sacristy, another Medici mausoleum — there are monuments by Michelangelo & one being quite unfinished — Then went outside and into the church of Jan[illegible] & after a circuit inside we went into the cloisters — From thence to the Duomo where we looked only at the outside — then to the Baptistry — we sat for some time enjoying the interior which is to me very noble — after looking at the Bronze doors we walked down the Calzajoli to the Old Market, this being flower day — being late we did not see much of a display — then to the Uffizj where A— left us & set to the house while I & [illegible] went in — we stopped, but a few minutes in the Tribuna & then through the long passage way over the Ponte Vecchio, to the Pitti Palace — On the passage way I had hoped to see a finer collection of engravings — it is very inferior. The galleries in the Pitte are really Regal — noble & I was intensely interested in seeing the originals of so many famous pictures made familiar to me by reproductions — Necessarily it was a race thro' the galleries & we had to get back to the house at 4. — Then lunch — after that A. & I packed basket & got it to a [illegible] for Lauro's men who came for them at 4 o'clk. They are to be sent direct to London — Showers prevented our going out as we had planned. I wrote to Norddeutscher Lloyd & to Herrick. At 3.30 — it then having cleared charmingly A. & I took cab & had an hours ride through the charming Cascine on Park very near our house — just outside the city limits, lying along the river — the wild free growth & dense under brush was very fine — on returning I feel so out of sorts that I did not go to dinner but had a simple meal in my room and went to bed.
