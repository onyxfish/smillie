## Left Page

### Thur. March 9, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still charming weather. It was nearly 10 when we sent Jim & Ralph to the Pincian Hill to amuse themselves while A. + I made a tour. By bus we went down the Corso stopping up at the Casa Vita went into the church of S. Ignazio — nothing noteworthy — I was interested in the High Relief sculpture over three of the altars, sculptured pictures in excellent harmony with architecture — I wonder that such effects have not been more frequently used. Then to the Pantheon, which, of course, was to us intensely interesting — of course I bowed at Raphael's tomb walked around the rear of the pile & saw the remains of the Thermae of Agrippa — then across the Piazza to the church of S.M. Sopra Minerva — the only ancient gothic church in Rome. — There saw M. Angelo's Christ with the cross wearing a metal shoe to protect his foot from the wearing kisses of the devout faithful & bearing his nakedness covered by some bronze drapery. Then to the Piazza Navona where we sat a while looking at the fountains & resting — then to the Ch. of S. Agnes — thence went to Corso Vittorio Emanuele — took cab — rode to the Ch. d'Gesù, principal Ch. of the Jesuits, & one of the most gorgeous in Rome — then to the Teatro Marcello, a tremendous old ruin now occupied by blacksmiths & others, then to the S.M. in Cosmadie — on the Bocca della Verità, where we were deeply interested in the Pagan temple & Christian church — & here also saw the Temple of Vesta — Then, past the Palatine Hill & Roman Forum to S. Pietro in Vincoli — we did not see the chains of S.P. but we saw M. Angelo's Moses looking very lonesome. This completed our morning work & we got to hotel at 1 — lunch — headache that quite used me up — at 4 o'clk Ely — A. the boys & I went to the Pincian gardens — leaving the boys we went into the gardens of the Medici adjoining — they were awfully delightful in the late afternoon light — their fullness of shade, the singing birds, etc. — the Terraces were lovely — it was an hour of delight. Then to Pincio — got the boys & to hotel at 6. Dinner at 6.30 — Unwell, in spite of headache I read & wrote.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 10, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightful day — At 10 A. + I met Ely on the P.du Spagna — went to P. Venezia & there took tram car & had a long ride out thro' the Porta S. Paolo to the church of S. Paolo Fuora le Mura — founded in 388 but as it has been almost utterly rebuilt since a great fire in 1823 it presents, in its interior, no appearance of age — only suggestion of antiquity is to be found in some early mosaics upon an arch over the altar & in a frieze over the Tribune. It is very large & cruelly cold with its polished marble floors & walls. In case of a brilliant ceremony & function the cold greys of the interior might give good foils to bright color but as we saw it, it was dishonestly bare, cold & sulky. The cloisters were far more interesting & there we amused & warded ourselves for some time. The space is small & no care is spent upon it — at 2 we took tram car to return. Then a pain came upon me in stomach & bowels that was very discouraging — it seemed to be more decidedly neuralgic than previous attacks — can it not come from these cold churches & galleries? Home to lunch at 1 — Pain continuing I took a dose of Dr. Kendall's medicine & lay down for an hour, then I got up feeling relieved & worked until 5 upon W.E. sketches of Richmond blossoms — Then I went out with the boys and walked by the Tiber, across it — around the Castel S. Angelo, near S. Peters — again across the river & here to the hotel. Dinner at 6.30 but it was no pleasure to me — very uncomfortable evening & much discouraged. Read Hans Basdeker.
