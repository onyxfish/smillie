## Left Page

### Thur. April 6, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a very comfortable sleep I woke at 4.30 A.M. & taking my temp. was much pleased to find it normal. I turned over & slept until near 7 — then again took temp. & found that it had risen 3/5°. To my great disquiet — The rise went on during the day in spite of every care, reaching 101/5 at 3 — the morning from 9 to 11 being hours of great pains in the right side of the head with facial neuralgia — my head is at no time free from pain — with irregularly recurring pain in the m[illegible] & fever in the afternoon & constant loss of strength I am much depressed.
Eve'g. Annie read aloud from a work by Marion Crawford "Don Orsino".

## Right Page

### Fri. April 7, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temp. normal again this m'g & then rising as before. I thought that I should go wild with the pain in face & head which did not begin to abate until after 11. Dr. Scotte came & tried to make me comfortable by assurances that there was nothing unusual in my case & that he was confident that the fever would pass away in two or three days — but I have no faith in his promises — I cannot rid myself of the impression that the situation is a very grave one.
Before 12 o'clk. I had my bowels cleared (as do now every other day). Annie changed the sheets of my bed & I got some refreshment in that way. Letters were rec'd from Kate, Fanny K., Rosalie Butler & Charl— more pain in my head again in the afternoon.
