## Left Page

### Mon. July 3, 1893

*Toblach, Gr. Toblach Hotel — Room 71.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day altho' much of the time we watched the weather anxiously. Showers passed very near us but none actually fell upon us. After our coffee A. & S. the boys & I started out upon a walk. H. & E. The boys soon settled to play in the stream & we continued on enjoying the sweet, cool air, the birds & the flowers & returned by way of the new Hotel Falena — in town we stopped at one of the Industrial Schools — no one at work but we got two or three small bits of iron work — Got to hotel about 12 & did up our packing before dinner then dinner — then bills settled & fees — Omnibus ready for us about 3.30 but we delayed starting for a few minutes to see whether a coming shower would reach us or not — it did not — at last our many adieux were said & we started at 3.45 in a good landau with a strong pair of horses. It was not long before we entered the narrow defile at the R.R. side of the valley & crossed by the Ponte Alto over the remarkable deep gorge of the Falizon. The climb to the Pass was very fine — dense forests of spruce & pine — gigantic masses of rock arising thousands of feet, many times in outline, & sometimes topped with snow. The color too, at times, was very rich — down the other side there was a succession of fine views, wild and grand, the effect enhanced by a threatening storm — The Croda Rossa & Monte Cris Fallo with its glacier were notable forms — we stopped at Schluderbach, a large hotel, where the horses were taking on for 2.0 m. We walked on — it was now 6 o'clk — the air was very cool & we were within 1 m. of Toblach — The valley became again a straight defile — at 3 and & there was a fine side gorge into which we looked & had a fine view of the great pinnacled & Cathedral like rocks. This Zinnen, nearly 10,000 feet high — from thence on the news became more & more common place — we got to Toblach at 7.30 — Hotel very near the R.R. Station — both 1/2 to 3/4 of a mile from the town — wide, flat valley, Dolomites on either side — The impression in the clouded every light not very inspiriting — Hotel large, very pleasantly situated & excellent — we were in time for a late Table d'hote.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 4, 1893

*Innsbruck, Tiroler Hof — Rooms 63 & 85.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious m'f. When we went out & had a chance to look about us we soon concluded that Toblach was a charming place & the ground Toblach Hotel very beautifully situated. The Station R.R. is 2 or 300 yds. in front of Hotel — a little before 11 we walked down to & train 20 m. late — paced up & down, enjoying the sweet air — the sunshine & the wide valley & the forest covered Mts rising immediately behind the hotel — when the train came we were fortunate enough to get good seats in a through car for Innsbruck — the ride was an exceedingly interesting one — the character of the scenery entirely changed from that on the Cortina & Belluno side of the mountains — the valley so well cultivated & fertile, the forests on & below the peaks so grand — in time the valley narrowed & we had some fine gorge scenery at times — till at last we entered the innumerable ramifications of Franzensfeste, in a narrow defile, unimaginable, the sound thinking we ate our lunch in the cars — beyond this we had exceedingly wild scenery at times as we climbed the Brenner Pass — as we approached it seemed as where the R.R. makes a sharp loop and returns upon itself as it climbs the Mt — a thunder storm came up & without altogether destroying our view of some glaciers it refreshed us by its coolness — and at last Brenner, the top of the Pass, 4470 feet above sea level — from this the descent was very exciting — most of the time we were high above the post road descending the precipitous sides of the valley — I am sure that passengers went from the R.R. than could be seen from the carriage road. The wonderful scenery of the landscape as it fell from the high Mts to the farmed & cultivated country was very charming — The stream was always retained, the wooded cliffs upon its sharply sinuous sides & the bright fields above meet — it seems never to be forgotten — It was about 7.30 when we got to Innsbruck — went to the Tiroler Hof very near the Station — established ourselves & I got my evening meal with the boys & while A. & E. dined at Table d'hote we wandered about the quaint old town — went to the P.O. & got a lot of letters — a letter looking about in the city — Showers came up & passed into a freshly steady rain that continued most of the night.
