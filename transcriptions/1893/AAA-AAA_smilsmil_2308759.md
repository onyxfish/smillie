## Left Page

### Sun. March 5, 1893

**Weather:** Still cool & very bright & much less wind than yesterday  **Temp:** Bkfs. about 9

— it was after 10 when A & I, with the boys, started for St. Peters — There we made the rounds of the interior very deliberately & got back to dinner at ½ o'clk — After that I had a bit of a nap — at about 4 o'clk — A — E — the boys & I went out for a ride — Drove thro' the Villa Borghese grounds — delightful old trees — great crowds of carriages & people of all sorts wandering over the grass looking for Spring flowers — The queen in her carriage, with crimson liveries passed us — like a good democrat I doffed my hat to her — After awhile we drove to the Pincian hill & made the circuit there, finding greater crowds of carriages & people — A fine bull was playing us — At 5 o'clk we got down at the door of the Church Trinita de Monti & went in to hear Vespers sung by the nuns — Mendelssohn wrote several compositions for them which they are supposed to sing beautifully — then just as the sun is setting the front doors are thrown open & the sun shines straight in with wonderful effect — The music may have been written by M— but it was not well sung — the intoning by the priests at the altar was & uselessly barbarous — when the doors were opened the sunshine did not materialize as the sun had already set — The interior is very common place and altogether I did not think Trinita de Monti much of a success — On our way home was much impressed by a view of the dome of St. Peters — silhouetted against the cool sky, in a setting of frame of almond blossoms on a tree that grew upon the slopes of the Pincian hill — the glory of the past — the dying day, seen through the budding flowery meshes of spring. Dinner with the boys soon after 6 — Evening spent from 8 to nearly chatting with Mr. Parker Mauier an artist from Washington, D.C. who is nowhere on his way to Naples — I says he presented a note of introduction to me in Npl. Sometime in 1878.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 6, 1893

**Weather:** A very pleasant day  **Temp:** Bkfs. before 9

— as soon thereafter as we could get away Annie & I went to the Colosseum & spent the afternoon there with a crowd of 30 or 40 following Signor Ol. Spadoni about the ruins & listening to his explanations & lectures, which on the whole was very interesting also — I thought that he indulged in much more repetition — He did not give us much that was historical — it was more descriptive of the building as it was in its early conditions, the character of the flooring, machinery, fights of animals & wild etc — etc — We did not get through until nearly 12:30 — then we took cab & back to hotel where we had lunch at 1 — after that we had a rest — it was not until 4 o'clk that we all started out & walked first to the Quinnal Palace where we gazed upon the old Greek equestrian statuary & the fountain & listened to the playing of a band — then on to the Trajan Column & to the Capotocene Hill where we saw the Dioscuri — Marcus Aurelius etc, etc — Then we went down to the excavations & then back to hotel just in time for our dinner — Every pleasant — a good fire on the hearth & I pored over guide books until bed time.
