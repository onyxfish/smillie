## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 14, 1893

**Weather:** A dull, windy, raw & disagreeable day, winding up with a half-blizzard snow storm.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After my morning duties, Taking R- to school, marketing etc. I got to work on my Munn drawing, by 4 o'clk. I got it finished and then, in the snow storm I Trounced down to 153 E. 15th St. to an adjourned meeting of the N.Y. Etching club, taking half a dozen of my mounted pen sketches with me. — Quorum — financial affairs of the club were discussed and an assessment of $10— per member, to pay debts, was agreed to. — Question as to advisability of having an expt. came up then — Reports of new plates, very discouraging — so few of them — Then I brought out my pen studies & offered to let the club have 25 or 30 of them to help make an expt. They were very well recd. & it was agreed to have an exhibition. Then I started for home on a trot & got there at 6 o'clk — brushed my hair & put on a clean collar & with Annie started for the Ireland's in E. 17th St. where we were invited to dinner — a very good dinner & a very pleasant eveg. of course — Home before 11.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 15, 1893

**Weather:** A very nasty day, heavy rain in the early mfg., filling the streets with slush. Rain more or less heavy all day. Fog in the afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Went to school with Ralph then to work on a new drawing — a bit of an old tower at Ravello, getting it in shape on the paper for color. After R- at 12.30 — together we went up Lex. Ave. to 47th St. to see a stable on live — lunch — after that I mounted two of my drawings — & then, some writing — Eveg. at 7 o'clk. Sam Coleman & wife & Walter Cook & Gorge came to dine with us. C. was suffering from a severe cold — He expressed a desire to see my sketches & the Morgan portrait — the others joined — & again I was showman.
