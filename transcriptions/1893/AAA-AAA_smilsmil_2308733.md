## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 12, 1893

*Marseilles — Hotel du Louvre et de la Paix — Rooms 79, 81*

**Weather:** Brilliantly clear — bitterly cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliantly clear — bitterly cold with furious wind — Bkfs. at 8.30 & at 9.20 left our hotel
& in a very few minutes got to R.R. Station
where, after a little trouble we got a coupé
compartment to ourselves & kept it until
the end. By rolling ourselves up in our
rugs we got along comfortably — at [illegible]
we started on time at 9.51 & got to
Valencia at 12.04 — 25 m. for a very good
3f. dinner (or déjeuner) the proprietor sending
us away with a big bundle of figs, oranges
& cakes — Then on until 5.42 when we got
to Marseilles — after the sun went down
I suffered from cold — after dark the country appeared to be snow covered & when we
got into the city the streets were wet & sloppy.
By bus at once to [illegible], a very large & showy
one, & were at once shewn to the rooms set apart
for us — had fire made at once — very dinner —
[illegible] got the boys to bed — sat before the fire
& read & wrote awhile — to bed at 10.

Passing Avignon we had a good view of the
city walls & the ancient Palace of the Popes
& as we passed Arles we saw the
ruins of a Roman Amphitheatre.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 13, 1893

**Weather:** cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m[orning] clearing until night, when it was cloudless — milder than yesterday not still uncomfortable —
ably windy in exposed places. After bkfs., toasted
ourselves in front of our wood fire until 11. Then we
all went out — walked down to the old port or basin
thence thro' Cours Pierre Puget to the Promenade or
Garden by some name — a bit of irregular ground
from a steep hill side, laid out in walks & terraces and
having luxuriant growth of trees & shrubbery — It
must be, with its outlook over the ocean, a delightful
place in summer — under the light fall of yesterday's
snow, it looked rather dreamy — Thence we climbed
to the new church of Notre Dame de la Garde — part of a
fortress upon a considerable height — The view was
very fine — over the city & over the water — our view to
the N.E. was obscured by smoke & vapor — The church
itself is, I think, architecturally ugly — its walls, to the very
ceiling, were thickly covered by votive offerings and
paintings of poorest description — while there, the big
bell in the tower boomed out twelve with tremendous
clamor — It was 12 o'clk. when we reached the hotel
& then we had a hearty lunch — Then read newspapers until near 3 when Annie, Jim & I went out
on an afternoon walk — just to the Arc de Triomphe —
in the Place d'Aix — a very poor part of the city — thence
by the Boul. des Dames to the Rue de la Joliette where
we had a good view of the shipping & the sea under a setting sun — then into the [illegible] unfinished cathedral — I do
not at all like it — it gives little, half ruined churches by
its side — thence through some very narrow old streets, up
stone stair ways & down others to the Rue de la République
to our hotel — Here I left A. & J. & started in quest of the
Palace of long [illegible], led astray by Murray's map
of the city — the Palace was quite remote from the
spot where I was led to look for it — After [illegible] where I
got back to the hotel — the boys had their supper at
6 — I ate nothing, not feeling like it — A. & I dined
at table d'hôte — Every one sat chatting before our
very pleasant wood fire until near 10.

The Hotel du Louvre et de la Paix is a very good, and
moderately high priced hotel — central location, on
one of the best streets — good cooking — good service &
everything new & in excellent order — We had 2 Rooms
on 4th floor — left — 4 beds — 25 f. per day — Constant wood
fire & b. Bkf. 4 f. Dinner 8 f. — Our bill was 114.80.
