## Left Page

### Wed. March 15, 1893

**Weather:** A clouded day — a light sprinkling of rain just before 1 o'clk.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfst — Annie went with Ely on a sight seeing expedition — I determined to take a rest & remained at home to work upon sketches & do a little writing. While in my room, about 11, Mr Colwell came to say that Ralston, while playing in the next room with his little girl, had fallen over in a faint — I found him in Mr C's arms in a dazed condition, but with eyes, pulse & temperature normal. He had tumbled into the fireplace & was smeared with a red wash, dust etc — I got him upon our bed where he soon fell asleep & after an hour woke quite himself again — Lunched at 1 — Soon after that we all went off by cab, across the river to the Villa Farnesina where the public are admitted on the 1st & 15th of each month, to two galleries when are some much admired ceiling frescos — in the smaller hall is a wall fresco "Galatea" said to have been painted entirely by Raphael in 1514. I confess that the whole thing was a disappointment to me. From this Villa we walked to the Church of S. M. in Trastevere, the interior of which we found to be very picturesque & interesting — are much moved by these old churches that are in such large part constructed from the spoliation of Pagan temples — I like to see the columns of varying sizes & orders with patchwork, frescos, etc. Then we went on to the Ch. of S. Chriscoco — last restoration more than 250 years ago — that does — passing — two largest porphyry columns in Rome — then across Portici Quinbale & into the ghetto thro the Portico of Octavia — was much interested in the doorway of an old junk shop — lintels carved marble of elaborate design from the old temple — Then into the Ch. of S. M. in Campitelli — Then took cab & back to hotel before 5 — Had a rest before dinner at 6.30 — Suet ff. Some writing — This mng I wrote a short letter to Mary.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 16, 1893

**Weather:** Bright mng — cloudy afternoon — up early & wrote some time on a letter to Derrick before bkfst.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfst Ely came & we three went to S. [illegible] in stations where we spent the rest of the mng going through the ch. & cloisters — the Baptistry — the Scala Santa & the museum — on the whole the mng was a disappointment to me — the group of bldgs is not fine or impressive — the E. facade of the Ch. is fine but it overlooks a mean & barren waste — the interior is a hodge-podge — an exceedingly matte ceiling over the [illegible] nave, with mean, painted orncts washed walls — the Transept is complete in its finish — the antique is everywhere restored to a condition rather better than new — the fine old pavement in the nave interested me. The cloisters are also much repaired & ruined architecturally by the common superstructure added. The Scala Santa, being partially covered & concealed by a wooden colouring seemed to me not unlike a flight of North Ave steps with their winter covering on — the museum was interesting but had nothing of overwhelming interest — By rushing things we got through by 12.30 — Then cab & back to hotel — dinner at 1 — taking time in the afternoon I sat at window of hotel & worked on new sketch of Civicina villa — at 4.40 being clueled I went out for a walk taking Jem with me. Went down to the Pantheon & thereabouts — went to the Fa. da Pietra & saw the Chamber of Commerce, built by Hadrian — old Temple of Neptune built by Hadrian. Back at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — most of the evesy spent studying up in guide book what I had seen during the day & in reading newspaper.
