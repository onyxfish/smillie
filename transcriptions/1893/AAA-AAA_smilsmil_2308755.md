## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 25, 1893

**Weather:** More Rain — a bleak, dull m[orning]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More Rain — a bleak, dull m[orning] — rain falling steadily until about 1 o'clk — after that it gradually cleared & at night it was brilliant & cloudless moonlight. At 11 I paddled through the rain, along walk, to the druggists to get my Rendell prescription renewed, as I had used up the medicine put up at Newton & I so wish to give it a fair chance! I was kept long waiting — went to P.O. & into a photo place & got some photos. Back to hotel at 12.30 — lunch — about 2 A — the boys & I started out for a walk — we went to the Hotel di Roma & got Sly — & then on, through the winding alley & channels of the old town up to the summit of the hill — there we found most of the buildings in ruin, the effect of the earthquake of 1886 — The summit is crowned by the church of the Madonna della Costa — The views were fine, but I liked most the tree covered ruin back of the town upon the tops of which rested the clouds of the storm just past — we descended by a road that by a long detour took us up the Vallone de Fraucia on one side, across a bridge & down on the other side — Some parts of the way were very picturesque — we got back to hotel before 5 & I rested until 6.30 — then the boys & I got our early meal — Slivje joined at the table d'hote. Early I read & loafed — A. & I went out for a while to enjoy the moonlight under the palms in the garden — there was a fine sea on & the moonlight & the palms were very lovely but they were also very wet so we did not stay out long.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 26, 1893

**Weather:** went to Bordighera & lunched with Mrs. D. Tiffany & Florence Tureedy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day but somewhat clouded — Slept. Home came at about 10 o'clk. & then our entire party sauntered down to the station to take the 11 train to Bordighera — got there in about 25 minutes & seeing the Hotel Bellavista from the station we walked to it in about 15 m. & met Mrs. Tiffany & Florence Tureedy just returning from church — we at once went with them to their villa & sat upon the terrace with them enjoying a lovely stretch of landscape — over palms & olives to the bay of the sea beyond which were beautiful mountains from stretching west north — the distant terraces, beyond Nice, being faintly visible — It is a view entirely different from anything else that we have seen upon this coast — At 12.30 we went in & had a very good luncheon — the Villa is a dependence of the Bellavista Hotel & adjoining — after lunch we all went out for a walk through the old town which we found small & picturesque — then to a boulevard that led us back to the rocks on the beach where we saw some fine breakers — we got to the R.R. Station & had to wait for some time for our train, but we sat upon benches on the sunny side of the station & had a good time until 3.45 — bid our friends goodbye & in half an hour were back in San Remo — We bought more photos & climbed again the steep, narrow streets & at last, soon after 5, got to our hotel pretty well tired out — At 6.30 I had a refreshing dinner — got our [illegible] bill — scrutinized it & paid it — & made all arrangements to make an early start in the morning.
