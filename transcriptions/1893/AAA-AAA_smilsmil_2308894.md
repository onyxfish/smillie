## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 30, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day. Had a short walk before b'fast — about 10.30 or 11 o'clk Annie, Katie, the boys & I started out for a walk. We went north until we came to the high, wooded ridge, upon which the Blake house stands. This we flanked, going around, over the rocks & thro' the wood to the west & north of it & coming out near Whitney Pond & thence home at 12.30. I had a short nap before lunch. Rheumatism commenced to show itself in my left shoulder with uncomfortable sensations in all of my other members. Lunch at 1 — after that, Annie, Katie, the boys & I walked down to Mr. Jennie Whiting's on Whitney Ave. Took a look over the new house. Then Ralph & I walked down to the "Green" where I purchased some Tabloids of Salycilate of Soda hoping to modify the increasing rheumatic pain. Home at 5.30. Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g Reading & chatting until 10.30.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 1, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Home again.*

A dull, disagreeable day, with some heavy showers between noon & 5 o'clk. I got up with an exceedingly lame left shoulder & arm — Rheumatiz — and as there was nothing to invite me out of doors, I stayed close by a pleasant fire & nursed my aching arm. It was 3 o'clk., in a pelting shower, that we drove away from the A's — and took the 3.30 train to N.Y., getting in on time at 5.30 — Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g, wrote to S.R. Koehler & at 10 went to Grand Central Station with it.
