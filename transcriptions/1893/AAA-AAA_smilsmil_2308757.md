## Left Page

### Wed. March 1, 1893

*Rome, Continental Hotel. Rooms 29–80–*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sulperb day. clear sky & warm sunshine. Bkfo. about 9 o'clk. – It was after 10 before our party started out – we walked directly to the Duomo & there put ourselves into the hands of a guide who helpped us through the groups of buildings – of course I would prefer to go through such places alone, taking my time & studying only what most interests me, but when hurried a good guide is a help. We went deliberately by thro' the Duomo – then got tickets at 1 l. each to enter the Compo Santo – Here we spent some time – an interesting museum requiring study for its proper fullest enjoyment – thence to the Baptistry. – Here I was treated to a new sensation – the achoing reverberation of a chord of three notes sung by our guide D He first sounded, with a burst, a crescendo, a low note – then when the whole vault was vibrating & quivering with it he sounded the next note, less energy – finally – then the third & highest note – the blending of the three while still preserving their individuality forming a delicious all pervasive echow tone that was delightful to me – Then we went to the Leaning Tower & Climbed to the top getting a glorious view the atmos. being particularly clear – but making the towers of Leghorn & Spezia on the main – others across the great Pisan plain to the Tuscan mts. that were very beautiful with brilliant snow-capped tops & hovering clouds – It was hard to leave. – Déjeuner at Hotel at 1 o'clk. After that again we went out & wandered about, going to the house where Galileo was born, etc & etc. Back in time to leave Hotel at 4. Trains on time – Got at about 4 45 – Charming twilight – last rays of sun light up in the mts – moon, one night before the full – Plain set in the sea – good views of Elba and other islands – Brilliant moonlight – They we made ourselves comfortable as possible & put the boys to bed by placing pillows under their heads as they lay up on the seats where they slept soundly until after 11– We were fortunate in having a compartment to ourselves save a young Englishman on his way to Ceylon & new Zealand – We got to Rome at about 11, 20– went at once to the Continental – a big Murcler's hotel, across the plaza from the R.R. Station – were shown at once to our rooms, fine large ones – en suite – and by midnight got to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 2, 1893

*Changed to Rooms 61–62.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day – Bkfo. about 8.30 – It was near 10 when Annie & Alex. got started on a voyage of discovery, looking for quarters during our stay here – they took Ralph – I took Jim & we wandered to the Pincian Hill, or rather to the Scala di Bagna, where I got my just view of the dome of St. Petter's – then down the stairs to the Piazza di Spagna where at a book store I bought a map of Rome – then to P.O. & got a letter from Mary tone for Ralph – [illegible]. – Then down the Via Nationale & by a cross-cut the Via Cavour & to our hotel getting in at 12.15. A. & E. got in at 12.30 after an unsuccessful search. E. got in about 1 o'clk. in which lunch a la carte – Then for some time A. & E. were arranging a plan for a further search for lodgings & I wrote a short letter to George. – A. & E. started off again at 3 & I, with Jim & Ralph, started out for a walk – I soon found that I was more than tired – it was a very unpleasant cause of exhaustion but there was none of the positive stomach or unpleasant feeling over the bowels that made life miserable to me at Nice & Mentone – After wandering through the fine church S. M. Maggiore – then back to Hotel before 5 & got a bit of a nap before I went with the boys for their every walk from after 6 – It was 6.30 before A. & E. ret.? All having taken a quarter at the hotel – Sussa – but E. was unable to locate himself – They dined? takes 2 hours – every part of the time in Reading Room – later, settling accts. with Alex. – To bed at 10.
