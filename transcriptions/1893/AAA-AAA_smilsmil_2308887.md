## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 14, 1893

**Weather:** mild, pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild, pleasant day. After taking Ralph to school I set? to the house & spent the m'g in working with the pen upon some of my Venetian studies until 12½ when I went for R., brought him home & then hurried down to the N.A. to met Alfred Jones, but he had gone a few minutes before I got there — back home & had my lunch & again to work with the pen. About 4 p.m. Durand came in & we chatted for some time about his book "The Life & Times of A.B. Durand". I have agreed to etch a portrait of Dunn Reed for it — about 5 I took a nap. Dinner at 6.30 — immediately after it Dessauer came in & peddled some table cloths & napkins to Annie & Evat?. I wrote a number of notes — to Jones, Jordans, Kurtz, Farrington & others.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 15, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'g, clearing to a cold & windy afternoon. Stopped at Chas.'s Rooms (after leaving R. at school) to make enquiries concerning Susq'a & Western R.R. stock. Then got to work in dining room upon my Italian studies with pen & ink. I worked as long as I could see upon two studies of the Rialto. Annie lunched at Walter's. Bet. 4 & 5 o'clk. R. & I went to the N.A. p's. I wanted to see Farrington on "Treas." business. There was a stereopticon show or" in the lecture room — an "Illustrated Talk on Prairie, Mt. & Shore" — views along the Canadian Pacific by Chas. W. Skinner. We were amused meanwhile by some of the pictures — then I had a brief talk with F—, then home at a little after 6. To learn that A. & I were invited to dine at Walter's — dressed in a hurry & shared a good dinner with Mr. & Mrs. Babcock — Carl Sprague — Edw'd & Mary & Walter & Louise. I was not in good shape for such an entertainment. To bed at 11.
