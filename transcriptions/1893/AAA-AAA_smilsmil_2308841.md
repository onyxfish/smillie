## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 14, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably warm day — Got to B.M. at 10 o'clk & had to wait until the headman, Pro Tem, came with the keys to the cases — Prof. Colvin is now away on a vacation. I spent some little time winding up my aquatint researches & then got started on the Liber Studiorum series of proofs. It was great fun — Eight cases of them with Rawlinson's descriptive catalogs. At 4 o'clk — I walked to the of [illegible] in New Bond St — about the magnifying glass he is making for me — then I continued my walk on to home getting in after 6 — a sewing lady at work for Annie — Dinner at 7 — very unsatisfactory — somebody will have to be talked to. Swan? — I wrote for some time on a letter to W. S. R. Koehler.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 15, 1893

**Weather:** new Southgate —  **Temp:** 87°

The hottest, steivingest day yet — The Ther. was somewhere up above 80° with a heaviness & sultriness such as I have rarely felt at home. Got to the museum at 10.20 & at once set to work upon the Liber Studiorum plates which I find immensely interesting — far more so than I had expected — Annie came along later & finished her translations concerning Saint Non & de Prince. I had lunch at 1.30 & at 2.30 went to King's Station, Great Northern Ry. went onto Finsbury Park and waited there until the 3.33 Train, then onto Southgate (New) or Colney Hatch, to see W. Sidney James about getting a magistrut pole rocker — when I got to his house I found that we had ridden from F. Park in the same R.W. carriage — We had a very pleasant time, for an hour while he explained to me the device, which he says is not his, but an old idea improved by an [illegible] friend of his — I made drawings & measurements the refused to accept any royalty. I got back to Station in time for the 4.57 train which was 14 m. late — drove to King's & thence by underground R.R. — home a few m. before 6 — Dinner at 7 — Mr. [illegible] was talked to "this mg & showed the good effects this eve up."
Sley I wrote a note to Mr. James asking him if he could have the castings of his pole rocket made for me.
