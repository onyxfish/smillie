## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 18, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very hot but somewhat tempered by breezes — quite comfortable at night —
Feeling better in the m'f. I went to the museum, getting there at 10.30 — I
finished Liber Studiorum & by lunch time had hurried thro' Liber Veritatis.
Then Mr. Cust brought me some very interesting old prints, done with sharp
point & hammer — & after them I saw the von Siegen prints — also Prince
Rupert's & W. Vaillant's — Mr. C. had to send down to the mounter's for the
prints — Soon after 3, feeling used up,
I rode home stopping at barber's & having hair cut etc. — Then home — rested & wrote
before dinner — loafed during the even'g.

For 11 days the max. each day has been above
83°. 20-day. max. at Westminster 93° —
at Royal Observatory 95°2; in the sun 146°3/10
the lowest, last night, 72°.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 19, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still warm — but a delightful change in the weather which is very considerably cool-
er — & a fresh breeze blowing. Got to Museum
at 10.30 & spent the m'f, reviewing some
Austrian aqua-tint work — Louis Marvy's
soft ground etching & reading up Jul. C.
Smithe's historical notes on mezzotinting.
Lunch as usual & then wrote notes —
prom'd with until about 3.45 — then
having got to a stopping place & feeling
tired I stopped work. Went to Newman's
Soho Sq. — Shut up — Then to Ross (Optician)
Bond S. — Shut up — Then Thro' to Hyde Pk. —
walking all the way home. Annie gone
to Putney to call on the Satchells there.
Rested until dinner at 7 — Evening
reading newspapers & writing a note to
Mr. F. Goulding.
