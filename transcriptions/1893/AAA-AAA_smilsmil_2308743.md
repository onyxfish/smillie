## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 1, 1893

**Weather:** A delicious day, cloudless, still and mild.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Feeling a good deal not fit to — still — headache & temperature slightly above normal. After bkfst. I dragged myself out & wandered to the high ground west of the city where I made a poor little sketch of a chapel. Back at 12.30 — Dejeuner. In the afternoon, Eliz., Annie & I took a ride, going along shore, west, as far as "California" — Back at 4.30 — I took dinner at Tableau? hate. Evef. with some recent "Punch"s & "Review of Reviews" loaned by Mrs. Mc. Future. Between 9 & 10 o'clk. Z., A. & I went out for a short stroll to enjoy the moonlight, the street crowds, the café orchestras, & a brief walk on the Promenade overlooking the sea. A very high wind blowing —

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 2, 1893

*Entrance of King Carnival.*

**Weather:** Still glorious weather — Bright sunshine — just warm.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfst. Took sketch book & went to the old part of town & wandered about there, in Cathedral etc — finished a little sketch looking up narrow street to the Cathedral — Then to Fish market & became interested there — then a walk & back to déjeuner — After that I set to fish market with Camera. Lucida & made a couple of pencil outlines — Then back wrote a short letter to Kate from whom I rec'd a letter this m'g. After dinner, Eliz., Annie, the boys & I went out to see the arrival of King Carnival who was announced to come with his suit at 8.30 — Brilliant moonlight — an immense crowd — we went first to the Place massacua where the illumination effects were very fine — then retraced ourselves part way up the Ave. della Gare & took position — The procession came along on time — an indescribable babel — no end of torches & lights — no end bands & noises of all sorts — men & women in all sorts of dresses on foot, on horseback and in chariots of which there were a number, one or two with gigantic figures — Every body seemed to be good natured. It was throughout a piece of boys' play — was saw the whole display without discomfort & as soon as the crowd had dispersed sufficiently we went back to the hotel — then I again went to the crowd — to the square to put an hour wandering about — the most popular idea of fun seemed to be carrying an immense, [all? nose?] capering, screaming & making all sorts of hideous noises —
