## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 29, 1893

**Weather:** Cold waves — Snow flurries — some cheerless sunshine  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold waves — Snow flurries — some cheerless sunshine — my pain kept me awake a good deal thro' the night & in the m'f I did not feel like getting up, so I had my b'kfst. in bed — but by 11 o'clk. I got very tired of that & for a change I got up. Dressing, a difficult matter — feeling so sore all over, I thought it best, in view of the necessity of my being able to keep up, to send for Dr. Gardiner — He came bet. 1 & 2 o'clk. He advises Salol, & a liniment which he would prepare. Dinner at 2, after & Pretta went to the D's & got medicine & liniment. — I could only lie about, in great discomfort any way — Tea at 7.30 — I wrote a letter to Annie & completed the one to W. Koehler — The girls took them to the P.O. for me.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 30, 1893

**Weather:** Bright, cold m'f, but soon cloudy with still a little of the snow squall disposition  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold m'f, but soon cloudy with still a little of the snow squall disposition the night, fine. Again I stayed in bed until 11 a.m., taking my b'kfst. There, the pain in my back still very severe. I was able to accomplish very little in the m'f, but feeling better in the afternoon. I did some clearing up in the studio, getting things together for packing in trunk. The supplemental trench was finished at 9 a.m. & the plumbers came & put in the pipe. Williams left at noon & Hill finished up at 3. Putnam & Oakeley's son-in-law (don't know his name) came on this m'f & worked on the foundation wall at east end of old barn — Derrick still working alone, tearing off a portion of the roof & getting up rafters for the bathroom. It is evident that he has trouble enough to understand my orders without undertaking to transmit any of them to a second person. Will Packer came in to studio about 5 o'clk. — going away in 6.20 train — I gave him something to eat & had a short visit before he left — Evening I wrote a letter to Annie.
