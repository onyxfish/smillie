## Left Page

### Tues. March 21, 1893

**Weather:** A delightful day — clear, bright & warm.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up early & at last managed to finish my letter
to Herrick — after Bkft. We came in — A. and I
decided to go to the Capitoline museums — she
did not care to accompany us — so we went
alone & the boys played on the Pincio — we
raced thro' the two museums, I am sorry to
say, in true tourists style — It is too bad —
it grieves us, but what can we do else, at
our ages & with but one life before us? We
saw the originals of many grand & familiar
works & to them we made obeisance as we
passed. Lunch at 1 — after it a short rest
— at 2.30 Melger children again came for
our boys — at 3.30 I went to the "Michel" for
Annie — in Elig's room — the boys there,
having come to grief — Jim getting his finger
badly hurt — & then deserted by the K. boys.
The finger being bound up the boys went
with S. to buy photos. & A. & I went to a
very interesting shop & bought spoons — & then
to another & bought a lot of photos. It was
5.30 when we got back to the hotel — Met
the King driving on the Corso. Dinner at
6.30 — a stupid [affair?] — Reading news, [Baedeker?]
& dozing — went to bed early to get rid
from myself.

At 12.30 — the night watchman waked us up to
give us a telegram just rec'd. It was rather
startling — but proved to be only an enquiry from
Parker's Hotel, Naples, concerning the time of our
arrival there.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 22, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

[Superb?] day — Up at 6 — wrote a letter to Walter
Blackman before bkft — After bkft. Annie went
off with the boys to get their hair cut & took Jim
to the dentist's where, for the fourth time a filling
was taken out & put in again — Correspondence —
it seems to pass their victims on from one to the
other. I started off alone going down the Corso
to the Pa. [Naugia?] a stopping in at every church and
place mentioned in Baedeker — Met around to
the Capitoline hill — over the [Tarpeian?] Rock — into the
church S. Maria, [Aracoeli?] where I treated myself
to a look at it & Santo Bambino — that blessed baby!
It was much amusing — by the ceremony of the showing —
the lighting of candles — the unlocking of doors — the
running out on a little Railway of the box in which
the doll is placed — more doors to be unlocked — then
the exposure of the stupid little image — at last the
reverent kissing of its feet by the attendant monk
& then the wiping up of the kisses with a dust cloth.
It was all pitifully absurd to me. The interior of the
church was interesting but I did not think it as picturesque
as the S. M. in Trastevere — then I crossed the Pa. to the
Via del [Campidoglio?] entered by a side door the
[Tabularium?], in the vaults of the Senatorial Palace —
very interesting in themselves. I then I climbed
to the top of the Campanile of the Palazzo del Senatore — a hard pull up 200 steps — but the view was
exceedingly interesting & beautiful — one of the finest
to be had in Rome. After a good long look, back
to hotel in time for lunch — After that A. & the boys
went off to meet Elig. & make an afternoon of it —
while I worked on a pen sketch of the Pincian Hill
from a window in the hallway. I worked closely
until 5.4[?] — then I went out & walked on the
terrace until 6.15 — Dinner at 6.45 — Every
arranging for a visit to-morrow to Albano &
Frascati — also, worked on pen sketch and
finished it.
