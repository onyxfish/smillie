## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 20, 1893

**Weather:** A lovely day — cloudless & quiet  **Temp:** Blp. at 9.

But it was nearly 11 before I got to my place in the flower market, with Water color box — and commenced to paint the study for which I made the pencil outline yesterday — I used my new Newman color box & colors &c. of course felt unacquainted & so did not work with freedom & rapidity. I worked until 12.15 not getting half through — somewhat chilled — walked rapidly back to the hotel, getting quite warmed up. After dejeuner we all went out, got a carriage & rode out to Cimiez about 2 m. north of town — up on the hillside — a very old Roman town — once the capital of the [illegible] Maritime Alps. Here we saw the ruins of an old Roman Amphitheatre — 70 yds. × 59 yds. Some things I enjoyed — the sunlight on the road & walls — the roses & geraniums — the orange trees loaded with fruit — the cactus & eucalyptus. Also, the old olive trees but there were no picturesque old buildings — no bits that I wanted to sketch — I was surprised to find so much snow yet lying unmelted in places where the sun almost did not reach it. We got home before 4 o'clk — & then I went to the Promenade & walked until sundown — In at 5 & seated until dinner. Forty. Three strollers, a man & two women — supposed to be Tyroleau, gave us an entertainment in the long corridors, consisting of Tyrolean songs — zitter — guitar &c. and a characteristic dance. Their performance was over at 9 — then to our fine side & easy seats & by the bright light of our lamps. Seeg. accompanied us —

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 21, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another bright, sunny day — Had a short, sharp walk before bkfp. It was again late — 10.30 — when I got to my work at the flower market — as might be expected, being so long away from my work, my first attempt now is very weak & uncertain — especially in the very difficult matter of trying to represent a crowd of people in action — I stuck to it until 12.15, then folded my tent & quietly sloped, getting back to the hotel just in time for dejeuner — After that Anine wanted with a decayed Baroness to arrange, if possible, to have her take charge of the party in the afternoon. Talking French to them — A. offered 18 fr. but she wanted 20 — As A. wanted a Bonnie rather than a Professor, no arrangement was made — We all went to the Pl. Masséna — thence took tram for the Port — from thence walked up the Boulevard Carnot, by some of the finest villas about Nice, with terraced gardens — brilliant with roses, geraniums & oranges, to the end of the promontory of Mont Boron, thence up to the hotel of that name — Here Anine's foot hurt her so much that she determined to turn back with Seeg. & Ralph while Jim & I went on up & around the summit of the mt. & down again to the Boul. Carnot. This we successfully did, getting some fine views off over the sea & also over Villa [illegible] — The coloring is not inspiring & the buildings are not picturesque — The modern villas is so noisy, sometimes horribly plensy ugly & startingly light in color. So far, to my great surprise, I have found the villa architecture of Europe, that's at these show places, pretentious & inartistic! I no longer think that in the way of nobleness we (Americans) take precedence in our villa architecture. The gardens are usually very lovely. It was after sundown when we got to the Port & took tram to the Pl. Masséna — reaching the hotel at 5 o'clk — Every A. & I sat before the fire & snoozed until 10 o'clk — altho' a small dance was in progress down stairs.
