## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 28, 1893

**Weather:** mild & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild & pleasant day — the morning being quite spring-like. After usual duties in the m'f., got to work at my sketches — after lunch Miss Abbatt called & we spent some time talking of things that have happened since we went abroad — I worked until 4 then taking half a doz. pen sketches I went to Shirlaws to ask for space at the next Century Meeting to exhibit him F.H. Smith & Champney are ahead of me, so I put in my application for the Jan't meeting — I talked critically at my sketches — some of it I did not understand — Then he showed me some of his work — & all of that I did not comprehend — The sum total was to me discouragement. Then, on my way home stopped at [illegible]'s & spent an hour studying Zorn's etchings & they added to my depression — Home! dinner — Eve'g I wrote to T.J. DeVinna concerning Etching Club matters & [illegible] at 10 I made 6 and mounted ½ a doz. pen sketches — To bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 29, 1893

*New Haven — at Dr. D. L. Adams'*
*459 Prospect Ave.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — took R— to school & there to work on pen studies until I went for him at 12.20. Lunch at a little before 1 o'clk. Then Annie went to her Wed. Afternoon Club — I again worked on my pen studies until [illegible]. D[illegible] came in & gave his final instructions for the etching of the Jerman Reed portrait. He expects to sail for France on Sat. next. Then the boys & I went to the Station & with a crowding throng pushed thro' to the train — In spite of our efforts we could only get very indifferent seats in the second section of the 4 o'clk. train. Annie joined us 10 m. before 4 & our section did not get away until 4.10 — our car was over crowded & most uncomfortably close but — we got to N.H. about 6.20 or more than half an hour late. Rode direct to the Adams' — supper — Eve'g, general chatting & to bed about 11 —
