## Left Page

### Wed. March 29, 1893

**Weather:** milder & quieter than yesterday, but not so bright & clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfs. wrote for nearly an hour on letter to Kate, then, as A. did not feel like going out & she wished to give the boys some lessons, I went off alone. By tram-iculare I went to the Plateau of Vomero & then to the Castle S. Elmo where I loafed to my heart's content — The views over city & bay were very interesting — Naples altogether, Naples is a squalid city, unclean & picturesque — the mass of its inhabitants seem to have but an imperfectly developed sense of order or cleanliness & to be born mendicants. It is a city of hawkers — I never heard so many street cries — every thing wanted can be bought in the street from men & women who are carrying their stores upon their heads or have them up their backs of miserable donkeys — The voices of the street hawkers as they came up to me on the walks of Castle S. Elmo made a chorus as curiously characteristic as anything that I ever heard — 89 long flights of steps I got down to the Corso V. Emanuele & thence, by easy stages to the hotel in time for lunch at 1 — After that I rested until 3 — then A. Still feeling unequal to a walk, I went out with the boys & had a long tramp along the water front to the great basin where for a time they studied the ships & boats of various patterns — Back to hotel at 6.30 — Rested — dinner at 6.30 — Eve. had a long chat with Miss Glauckner who, with her sister, [illegible] from Capri this afternoon.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 30, 1893

**Weather:** very warm hazy, quiet [illegible], cloudy afternoon — bright moonlight night  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfs. I finished & mailed a letter to Kate — then leaving A. with the boys & acting as school marm, I went along the Corso V. Emanuele for about ¼ of a mile to a point where there is a very picturesque view over the city and to Nisino — I worked there with pen & ink until 12 o'clk — getting only a foreground corner done — Washed back & had lunch at 1 — after that I rested for some time, then worked for awhile upon my sketch of the mts. Annie went out for a walk among the shops, taking Jim — I worked until after 5 & then with Ralph, walked up the Via Tasso to the top of the hill — Back at 6.30 & had dinner — Eve. discussing plans for moving as I do not get rid of my cold here as I had hoped to — It is too cold & windy — better go on to Amalfi & bake awhile there & then come back here & finish our visit — I spent some time reading a copy of the Trib. Rec. to-day.
