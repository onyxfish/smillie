## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 9, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming weather still continues. High wind, westerly, bringing down much foliage, but not at all cold. I was much of the day out of doors trying to find men & a team to work for me, but every body seems to be busy. In the afternoon Stone & Kenyon, with horse & wagon & Henick, also with horse & wagon, went to the woods & gathered a lot of leaf mould. Just as we had finished our tea, Will. J. Parke came in, having driven up from Scranton. He sat down, had a bite & then spent the eve'g with us until near 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 10, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cloudless, quiet & nice — a superb day — a joy to be out of doors. I was much about, directing — Hill digging — Stone at work among the strawuries & Henick tearing out the stalls in the old barn, to get at the decayed ills. I went down town before noon, worked for awhile on plans for water tanks. Dinner at 1.30 — Parke came with his team & took Annie & Ralph out for a ride. More work on plans & then out of doors with pruning shears. Down town before 6. Tea at 7. Eve'g., wrote a letter to Kate & forwarded with a letter rec'd. from George.
