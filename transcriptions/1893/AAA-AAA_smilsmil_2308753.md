## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 21, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of steady, down pouring rain from about 10.30 until near 5 o'clk — I had a comfortable night — but after 7 a.m. had considerable discomfort & sometimes down right pain — I had a very good chafe for my help — at 11 went, through the rain, to a druggists with Dr. R's [illegible] also got more photos. & back to hotel & had lunch — Afternoon, tried to work a little with water color but made a mess of it — also read a little. Between 4 & 5 went into Slig. H's Room where she dispensed coffee, hot water & cakes. Dinner with the boys at 6.30 — Every I read part of the time — E — spent part of the evey. with us.
Bright starlight night.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 22, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day, the afternoon especially bright & fresh. Had a little pain in the [illegible] but was more discomforted by as curse of weakness. — I got ready to go out to the old town to make one or two slight sketches when Mr. Gifford came in — after a short call he walked back with us, he returning to his hotel & I going to the small sq. in front of the Cathedral & making a slight pencil outline sketch — To Hotel at 12 — I did not take my mid-day meal until after 1 — After 2 I went out & made another slight sketch then ret'd to hotel & went out with A., E., & the boys & wandered about — A very fine & heavy surf commenced last night and has been running all day — We went along the plage to look at it — many people there — in one place a part of the parapet & sea wall has been broken down and washed out & much of the shingle has been washed up & thrown over the promenade — We went up on to the parapet of the jetty or Break water & there the immense dashing waves were very imposing — To hotel at 5 — a rest — dinner with boys at 6.30 — every loafing.
