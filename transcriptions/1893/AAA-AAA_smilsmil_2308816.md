## Left Page

### Tues. June 27, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Belluno. Hotel des Alpes. Rooms 25. 26.*

Charming day, altho' at times very warm. BkBp. at 8.30 + taking things very quietly we got away from the Bella Riva into Gondolas (A. E., Mrs & Miss Beeson, the boys & self) at 9.30 went thro' some canals, new to me + got to R.R. Sta. at 10.15 where we soon had everything properly attended to & a carriage secured to ourselves. The train did not leave until 10.45. So having time, I went into the Scalzi church near the station, a fine exhibition of bad taste. Train started on time. Last look at towers & campanile of Venice from the bridge over the lagoon — along side open perfectly level country until we reached the great Moraine at some distance from the foot of the mts. — tunnel. Soon after 12 got to I neves of there changed to another train that started after a delay of about 20 m. — we soon entered the mts. going thro' a pass by the side of River Piave — some of the scenery was very fine — narrow passes & then very wide valleys with beautiful environment of fantastically shaped mts., very grey & bare, but no snow. We had a very satisfactory lunch in Lucas. Got to Belluno at 4.05. Hotel near the station — everything very nice, pleasant Rooms ready for us. I sat down at once & made a little pen sketch from our window. Then A. & I lay down for a few minutes rest — then out for a little stroll before our dinner at 6. After that Annie the boys & I wandered about town, which we found interesting, until about 8 — glorious moonlight night. To bed at 9.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 28, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 7 & 19

*Cortina d'Ampezzo. Hotel Aquila Nera. Rooms 7 & 19.*

Brilliant day — at times, very hot. Up at about 6 + prepared for our days drive in a leisurely manner. BkBp. at 7.30 + at 8.15 we started. A., E., the boys & I in a landau, Mr. & Miss Beeson with a single horse. We had been assured that, starting at 8 we would get to Cortina at 12.30 — have dinner & rest for 2 hours & get to Cortina at 6.30 — never believe these fellows — we got to Longarone in two hours & there our driver stopped for 3/4 of an hour & then told us they could not get to Tai before 2.15 — so we told them to stop at Parasha, where we arrived at 12.30. Here, at the Corona d'Oro we had a substantial lunch & a most refreshing rest in cool, darkened rooms & then on a little after 2 — getting to Borra before 5 and stopping there for nearly 3/4 of an hour to give the horses a rest. Some of the party had milk & bread. We got to the frontier at about 7 & went through a long custom house examination & at 8.15 reached our hotel at Cortina, having been just 12 hours on the way — 3 of them resting. The first part of the ride was thro' an open valley — then it narrowed the mts. rose high — peculiar & interesting in form but not inviting for pictures. The wind grew stronger as the valley narrowed & blew with no sting, was no shadow & at times much fine dust which we found to be very trying — we were very not uncomfortable when we got to our dinner place — just beyond that is a sharp, zigzag ascent for 2 or 3 miles or so — where the sun got low enough over to give some shade. I wrapped a Venice & losing my light overcoat slipped off from front seat to the road — bustled with an hour or two later, too late to return for it. We passed near Titian's birth place. Pieve di Cadore — on a height to the right — then thro' the most picturesque part of our ride thro' the villages of Venas, Vingo & Vodo — &c — very fine looks & gorges — picturesque & dirty villages! Beyond Borra we had quite a walk before we were overtaken by our carriage — as the night grew cool I mourned the loss of my overcoat & had to wrap myself up in shawls — at the border we were told by Customs officer that the boys & della Martin had been there enquiring for us. It was still twilight when we got to Cortina — The G's welcomed us. Rooms were ready but there was considerable delay before we got dined & put to bed — 10 o'clock before the boys got to bed — I followed promptly but the ladies of my party went out & had a little moonlight walk.
