## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 25, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouds & sunshine — brisk, cool winds from N.W. — a very invigorating
day. Hill & Williams at work on the pipe trench. Stone & Rutau on the
wall at east end of old barn, and Herrick tearing out the sidings, eaves
etc. at east end of house for the new bathroom. I spent much of the
m'f on the roof problem for said bath room. To P.O. at 11 & got a very
satisfactory letter from Annie. afternoon I scraped for about two
hours on Koehler mezzatint plate.
After sunset Ralph & I had a walk.
Eve'g I wrote a note to Charl
& about 9 went to P.O. with it.
Brilliant moonlight — I took quite
a walk about Town.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 26, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful, bright m'f. — wind changed
to the east — overcast during most of the
afternoon — fine sunset. Rutau & Stone
at work in the field getting out stone —
Hill & Williams digging trench for water
pipe — Herrick at work tearing down outside
work on piazza where bathroom is to be. I
went downtown in the m'f & got a letter
from Annie. Was able to do a very little
work on M. Koehler's plate. At 3 o'clk. I
went down to Cooley's & got two plumbers
to come up & put the water pipe into the
trench — it is lead pipe in several pieces
& the ends have to be soldered together.
They managed to get it into place before dark.
It is bedded in Kelun. Evening,
I wrote a letter to Annie & took it
to the P.O. at about 10.
