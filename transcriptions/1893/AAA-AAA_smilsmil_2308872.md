## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 15, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another nowher last night — but the wind came from the opposite quarter, striking the other side of the house — & I was so tired that I slept. The day was heavily clouded with strong wind & gusty showers — cold & disagreeable. Good fires made it very pleasant in-doors. As the weather did not invite me to stay out — as I had no workmen to direct — and as it was quiet & inviting in the studio, I yielded to temptation & made a water-color sketch from my pencil sketch of Cloisters of Capucin monastery at Amalfi — I got my out of door exercise by walking on the Piazza. Evef. I wrote a note to W. R. Vickery in answer to one from him asking me if I would sign more pl[ate]s of the San Luis Rey plate.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 16, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day opened with a succession of snow flurries, the snow, light & wet & clinging to everything. Before noon all trace of it was gone. Clouds covered the sky until after 4 P.M. — wind blew very cold — brilliant sunset & cloudless evef. Cold, bright moon. I spent the mf. in studio tinkering over the sketch made yesterday. In the afternoon, with the men. Setting out the trees [out?] that arr[ived?] this mf., write [stone?]. Still digging on trench in the middle of the road to the barn for water pipes. Truck with two teams drawing dirt from the mill grading on east lawn, while Herrick was fixing a device of mine to catch the urine from the cow stable. Worked out of doors until after sunset — then had a brisk walk on the Piazza — Tea — Rec[eive]d a letter from George — Evef. I loafed, & to bed early.
