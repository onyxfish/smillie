## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 26, 1893

**Weather:** Clear, cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold & windy — I had a short walk after bkfs. Taking Ralph with
me to the butcher's & then going down
to D. B. Butler's on 4th Ave. Then home
& to work on "Campanile at Belluno".
I made first washes of color just
before lunch — worked until after
4 o'clk. P.M. Then I went to N.A. &
arranged for Council meeting this
eve'g. Home — Dinner at 6.30 — At
8 I went to N.A. Special meeting.
Much anxiety & trouble over our
defeat. T.W. Wood is naturally very
anxious to arrange about artists
going to the N.A.'s for power. He
dreads the ordeal of facing them with
any such request. Above all things
he desires to retain the Presidency
of the N.A. & he fears the effect, but
he was obliged to submit and it
was unanimously agreed that a
special meeting of the N.A.s be called
for the 2d Wed. in Jany. to provide ways
& means to meet the deficit reported
to them at their last quarterly meeting.
I left at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 27, 1893

*Annie went to Germantown — wedding of Marion Wright.*

**Weather:** Brilliant & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant & cold. At 9 o'clk. Annie & I
left the house — went to Jersey City by Desbrosses
St. Ferry, & then she took 10.10 train to Phila.
I ret'd. home & at 11.30 got to work upon
"Belluno". Lunch at 1 — after it,
worked until after 4 — then to N.A. &
signed a lot of checks — then to Bway
& 11th St. then home. Dinner at 6.30.
Bessie Throop dined at St. Wm. Cook's.
Tom Cook dining with us.
Eve'g Reading, chatting & writing.
