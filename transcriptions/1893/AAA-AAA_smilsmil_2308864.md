## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 29, 1893

*Last day at the fair. On Sleeper "Lockport" &c. Lake Shore & Mich. Central*

**Weather:** Dull, raw day

Dull, raw day — Sifo. man came for our trunk before we were out of bed — HHp — about 8 — after which I wrote a note to Mr. Brown & enclosed $25 — not down in the house but a servant. Then Af — went to fair — I went to Englewood Station, found trunk & checked it — then I went to 4th & that [illegible] — in French section — shift dept. We made a hasty review of the French pictures that we said last entry by electric light — after 12 we did the fair — Goodbye & by Stewards R.R. — we went to Cottage Grove Station & to the Delaware Ho. where we had lunch — after that we got a thorough warming in front of the fire in the parlor — and shortly before 3 cab started for Englewood — Train 12 and hours late — when it came along in sections, the 2d section being the "Sleepers" — we got on that — room got settled into our section — which fortunately proved to be in a new and very fine car. The train was very heavy — not a vacant seat & dining room car — not enough to accommodate the passengers — buffet on our car, from which we got our evng meal — Turned in at about 10 — had a very comfortable night.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 30, 1893

*at home again*

**Weather:** Pleasant day, growing finer until evening

Pleasant day, growing finer until evening — got in to Buffalo on time — 6.30 by Chicago time, 7.20 by Eastern time — trans. at once to D.L. & W. — got baggage checked & Drawing room seats — in to Mansion Ho. & had HHp — left Buffalo 9 o'clk. 2.45. Really enjoyed parts of the ride — the valley along by Dansville, etc — at 1 o'clk after many naps, we had a light lunch served from our buffet — got to Binghamton on time — 2.10 — in A. & S, having 4 hours to wait, wandered down to the Chemung River, crossed it & along to the river section of it & the Susquehanna — had back this the business portion — "Passing the" Stone of some town — saw that Vernon, far beam was giving a matinee of the musical farce comedy, Star-light — we went in — got rid of all hour — while listening to most villainous music & looking at a most vicious & depraving spirit silk-eldt covered mites — we wondered what the mothers & sisters who sat with us witnessed it — thought of it. The influence such shows throughout the country must be hurtful — After getting out we went to the Arlington & spent the rest of our time there until we went to D.L. & W. station — 6.10 train on time & without trouble we at last reached Montrose at 8 o'clk — Herrick waiting for us at the station — got promptly to the house & found all in waiting for us — Kate & Katherine, the boys, Gretta and Natie, all ready with welcomes — we had a light supper — then a good deal of talk — and to bed about 10.
