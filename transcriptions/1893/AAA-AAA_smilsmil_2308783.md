## Left Page

### Sat. April 22, 1893

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast most of the mtf. but clearing
very beautifully about noon — delightful
weather until about 4 o'clk. when clouds
again covered the sky — a shower passed
south fus, giving us a few drops, at about
5.30. I loafed about during much of the
mtf. going out for a short walk with
Ralph at a little before noon. Lunch at 1.
Soon after 2 we all went in a cab to
Camaldoli, an old monastery where there
are still ten or a dozen white gowned
monks — The drive to it is along one bunch
of it is through streets or high walled roads &
is uninteresting — but the latter part, along
high grounds with occasional views over
wide spreading country to distant mts is very
fine. The monastery is at the extremity of a
very high, sharp & singular spur of mountains
the ground falling away from it in every
direction — the panorama is very wide &
beautiful — much of the city of Naples is hidden by intervening high ground — but the sweep
from Vesuvius on the East to the Gulf of Baia on
the west is grand & the too topographic to be
a good picture — The establishment — the old
buildings & gardens — was delightful — & the old
monks in white, with kindly, weather-burned
faces were also pleasant to look upon — as no
women are allowed within the grounds, Annie
& the boys walked around the outside while
I availed myself of my privilege as a man —
we found it quite along back from where we
left our cab to the monastery — it was nearly
an hour before we returned — at about 4.40
we started our ride back, getting in by
the Via Tasso at about 5.30 — all rather tired —
a rest — dinner at 6.30. Every — newspaper —
a little writing & to bed early —

## Right Page

### Sun. April 23, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Somewhat mistif in the mtf. but soon cleared
to delightful day — clouding again from the E. about
4.30. Took my bfst. in bed — read awhile, then
got up & had a very thorough rubbing down and
a whisky bath — then again a long rest — did a
little picking up of things, preparatory to packing, & to church at 1 o'clk — at 2.34 we took
train to Pozzuoli — getting there at 3.06 — hired
a cab at the station & drove at once to take
Lucumus where they make a choice round of
oysters & then to Solfatara where I was much
disappointed — I had expected a bit of wild, smoky
landscape — instead, nothing could have been tamer
or more uninteresting — we returned to Solfatariana
then turned towards Baja going to a turn of the
road just beyond the Bagni di Nerone, from
which point we had an admirable view of
Baja — the castle & the bay — Then we drove back
to Pozzuoli & going up the hill to the amphitheatre
— but without entering the enclosure, a good
view of the Temple of Seraphs. The amphitheatre
was to be exceedingly interesting, being in many
ways far more picturesque than the vast bulk
up at Rome. we were obliged to go through it
hurriedly as we did not wish to lose our
train at 6.30 — we got to our hotel before 6
& had a rest before dinner — Evening,
Annie & I completed our packing; paid
our bill & paid the servants — & got to bed
at 10.30 pretty well tired out.
