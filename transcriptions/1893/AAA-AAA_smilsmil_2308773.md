## Left Page

### Sun. April 2, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hatefully beautiful weather as I
gaze out upon it thro' the window.
By 3 P.M. my temp. reached 101°
the highest point yet. Dr. Scotti
came in sometime after that time and
made a fair effort to reassure me
but a fever patient, 60 yrs old & far
from home & its comforts — knowing
that he is not the only sufferer thro'
his misdeeds, requires something
more than words. The Dr. seems
to be sound & conservative & was
kind enough to explain to some extent his proposed line of treatment.
He wrote a prescription for powders of
caffeine & Saly. fl Soda, to be taken 3 times
a day. It was 6 o'clk. before I could
get the powders — then I took one &
at 9 o'clk., a second — & then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 3, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The two doses of caffeine & Sal. fl Soda that
I took at 6 & 9 yesterday P.M. kept me awake
until bet. 3 & 4 this mng — a perfectly
quiet wakefulness — at 6 o'clk A.M. I took
my temp. & my equanimity was established
when I found it to be normal. It stood
the same at 9 A.M. this mng. I was
greatly elated — got up, washed & dressed
myself & curled up on the sofa & wrote a
note to Dr. S— He sent back a reply that
in any event I "must not leave my bed."
Too late — the mischief was done.
From 9 o'clk. my temp. went up like
a rocket & at 3 it stood at 101⁹⁄₅°. The
highest point yet reached — by 10 o'clk
P.M. it has fallen only ⁴⁄₅° — to 100⁴⁄₅°.
I went to bed very unhappy &
spent some time writing (mentally)
a codicil to my will & several farewell
letters to friends.
