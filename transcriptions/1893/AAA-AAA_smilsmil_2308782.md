## Left Page

### Thur. April 20, 1893

**Weather:** Hazy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hazy mng but gradually clearing to a charming day — Got up & had a good time washing & dressing — Then had bkfst. in my bed room. About 10.30 I went out for a walk & for nearly an hour & a half sauntered to the west on the Corso all my head membranes were very tender giving me some pain so that I had to keep a hdkf. over nose & mouth most of the time but I found my strength returning very satisfactorily. On getting in I rested until lunch — after that I went to hospital & paid Dr. Scotti's bill — $90. At 3 o'clk. we all went out in two horse carriage & drove to the Palazzo di Capodimonte — The place was closed to visitors owing to preparations for coming festivities but we were allowed to drive through the "Gardens" where there were some very lovely wood bits & long avenues under overarching trees — a view out over city & bay, from a terrace where a large live oak grove was very fine — Drove down through the city & around by the Bledo — & out a Lucia & the Public Gardens back to hotel at 5 — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef. Telegram came from Amalfi saying that we could have only north rooms at Hotel Cappucini — I answered that they would not do & asked where we could have south rooms — all of which is very disgusting to us.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 21, 1893

**Weather:** Delightful  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day. I went down to bkfst. for the first time since the commencement of my illness — after that wrote notes to Jas. I wound in Paris & Jos. Pennell in London asking for the closing dates of the great Paris & London Exbts. Then A. Ralph & I went out for a walk — went down to near the Pzze. dei Martiri & thence, up a zig-zagging flight of steps, delightfully picturesque thro' gardens & old places to the height that culminates in the Pizzofalcone — I had not strength enough to explore further, so we turned back and got to the hotel at about 12.30 I being quite tired out — I had a rest on my back for half an hour before lunch — after that I had another rest & about 3 we all went off on a ride, going up the Via Tasso to the top of the hill, then along the Posillipo ridge of the hill. The afternoon was very delicious — the sun warm, but not hot; the wind cool but not cold & the blue atmosphere over the water & distant mts., indescribable — The views of either sides from the ridge of the hill are very charming. It was 5 o'clk when we got back to the hotel — A telegram from Amalfi saying that possibly we might have rooms there such as we wanted, to-morros evef. I rested until dinner time — my walking has made my feet & legs very lame — after dinner read newspapers in reading room for some time — to bed early.
