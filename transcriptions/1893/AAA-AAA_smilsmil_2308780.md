## Left Page

### Sun. April 16, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day, warm, still & sunny. Getting desperate over my condition. If I did not have a Ther. & take observations I wouldn't know that there was anything abnormal about my temperature. I'm in a bad hole & believe that I have tried patient waiting long enough. About 10.30 I got up, wrapped in big rugs & shawls & went into the other room & sat, stretched out, upon a sofa in the sunny window where I could see old Vesuvius & much of the bay. I finished & mailed a long letter to Flats & a short note to Charl. — now & then I did a little washing — at 2 o'clk. I had a roast lamb lunch — after that I had a very short nap. It was 6.30 when I got back to my bed — Temp. a uniform brave getting to 99¾° at 3 P.M. No improvement, but in a general way I feel better for being up & about. Jim seemed to be better to day & was much better tempered. Annie had two outings in the course of the day with Ralph. A fleet of armor clad vessels maneuvered in the bay to the great joy of the boys. Evening, more Don Orsini.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 17, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded in the early part but soon bright & as sunny as ever. I believe that my Ther. is playing tricks with me — my temp. is recorded as higher than it has been since last Wos? — & I don't believe that it is really so. I know that the Ther. is defective — it makes me feel uneasy. I kept in bed until Dr. Scotti came soon after 12. He assured me that there was nothing to discourage me & made an appointment for me to call upon him at the Hospital next Wos? P.M. Then I had my bowels cleared — lunch — & at 3 o'clk I got up & dressed & pranced about until 5 o'clk. when I undressed & went back to bed again. Jim continued to improve & went out with Annie for a little while in the afternoon.

Eve'g Annie again read aloud from "Don Orsino".
