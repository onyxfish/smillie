## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 2, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — mf Teleg? to Annie that our start was postponed until to-morrow & soon after Rec'd Telegram from her asking us not to come until to-morrow So — we agreed — I spent the day looking after the men & my packing — Herrick & Sly got the roof boards on in the [illegible] & the Tinners came in the afternoon & very nearly completed the job. Stone finished up his work about the place & I picked up odds & ends all over. I went down town in the [illegible] from 11 to 12. Evely went down again just before 6. Wrote a note to Kate & podged about until near 10 & then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 3, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*New-York. 110 E, 38th St.*

Heavy rain in the very early morning, ceasing after 7 & clearing very gradually. Up soon after 6 — bkfs at 7.30 & soon had things all ready for a start so that for some time I had to sit about waiting for the bus. I spent some time with Herrick giving him last directions — The Tinners came to complete the soldering of the roof, but it was too wet. Katie, Ralph & I left the house a little before 12 — at the R.R. Station I found [illegible] waiting for me — He delivered to me two deeds — one from Fanny Tyler, conveying to him the [illegible] at adjoining our place on the S.W. The other from him, conveying the same property to me. I went into the station with him — gave him a check for $100 — an acknowledgement of debt for $450 — which completes the purchase. Train started at 12.15 — stuffy, hot car — an intolerable length of time getting to Alford — wait there 20 m — neck run to Scranton — no fire in car — windows stale — cold & draughty — horribly slow — got to Scranton at 3.15 — Three hours going 45 m. by R.R. Wait at Scranton for 35 min. — Then take Erie & Pullman seat — Stoveler with Rapidity & comfort — never so to NY. by that combination of trains again. [illegible] car held it — got to the foot of Christopher S. at 8 o'clk. car to 14 S. Stopped by political procession — walked to 4th ave. — car home — getting in at about 8.40 — Annie waiting — House in a discouraging condition — but we got a little to eat & went to bed pretty tired. Annie, Ralph & I slept in the nursery — third floor, front — Jim slept at Memy's.
