## Left Page

### Fri. April 28, 1893

**Weather:** Overcast m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m't, gradually clearing — afternoon bright, warm & breezy — clouded eve't.
I worked for a time on sketch of monastery after b'kf'st. It was after 11 when A. + I
went out for a walk along the new carriage
road in course of construction west of
Amalfi. Back before — luncheon — after
that I took a nap. — Then out with
A. + the boys for a walk. Went down
to the town & into the Cathedral which
establishment we went thro' thoroughly.
Then to a maccaroni factory — boys and
beggars worse than fleas — there seems
to be no way of driving them away — they
are such a nuisance as to make any going
about really a trial. Went to a Pharmacy
& got Dr. Scotti's prescription of Caffiene
+ [illegible] Soda powders. It was near 6
when we got back to hotel. — Found letters
from Charl., Kate & Jno. Durand for me, & a
little note from Sumia [?] — for Annie. — I had
a dinner with the boys at 6.30 & A. dined
at table d'hote. Eve'g I wrote part of a
letter to Charl.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 29, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some rain at last, but not much — enough
to spoil the day for my work. It was a
mild misty m't. with very light showers, not
at all unpleasant for walking on the Terrace.
Before 10 the clouds began to roll off & the sun
shone out. I rigged a protection ag't wind
& sun at the cor. of the house & worked for
some time upon a sketch there — managed to
get considerable walking before luncheon time
at 1 o'clk. After that I made two pencil
outings — one of old well, the other of the clois-
ter — when the sun shines I hope to get
some color on them. — It was about 4 o'clk.
when A., the boys & I all started out for
some explorations — we soon got lost in
the labyrinths of stairs & tunnels, which, as
usual, we found very nasty & tiresome. — We
got down to the main street & then went on
up the mill valley & ravine where there are
a number of paper mills — we went into
one & saw a part of the process of paper
making — The very rudest, simplest forms
of hand working turning out commonest stock
for home consumption — The interior with
figures etc. was very picturesque — so were
some of the buildings on the mill stream.
The day was warm but the wind was chilly
& fitful & I did not enjoy my walk at all.
Back to hotel at about 5.30 & then rested
until dinner at 6.30.
