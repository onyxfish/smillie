## Left Page

### Fri. July 7, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy m[orning]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hotel Victoria. Basle. waiting for 9 P.M. Train for Paris —

Cloudy m[ornin]g. that soon cleared into almost cloudless & very warm day — After being in bed for to hours got up feeling much Restless. It was 9 o'clk. before we went to our V[illegible] — it was after 10 when with A. & the boys we started out to look about the place — Spent sometime feeding the ducks & swans — Men to the bridge over the Rhine — then to the Cathedral — curious & interesting but not beautiful — then to the old hall in which Huss was tried & condemned — then A. & I went to hotel & I went to Rath haus — a fine court — then to Hotel & enjoyed the cloisters with the very clever frescos of Carl Haberlin — the last (yet at Augsburg red) one, Kaiser Wilhelm &c° — by the Grand Duke of Luxemburg on his visit to the hotel in 1840 having portrait of Painter & wife in it — we had a substantial luncheon after 12 but 1.30 took the Train for Basle which proved to be slow & tedious as the weather was very hot — Musecany could hardly be called interesting after what we have seen in the past few days — it might have been the River Delaware in New Jersey instead of the Rhine — just below Schaffhausen we had an excellent view of the falls of the Rhine from the R.R., which commanded a fine view of the Valley. I was pleased with the picturesque old town of Laufenburg on the Rhine not far from Basle — we got to B— at 6.38 on the wrong side of the River & to save time took cab across to the Central Station at the Victoria Hotel, opposite. we had our evening meal & enquired for a telegram expected from mad. Costado. we were offered a bundle of letters & English papers for Mrs. & J. Snillie — who's sake? Then I went to the office of the Wagon lit & bought three bets to Paris paying 75 & 75¢ for them — then had to go to R.R. Station & buy supplemental tickets, making our II class tickets into I's — paying 56¢ 85¢. our night journey costing 132¢. 60¢. We started at 9.10 and got the beds made & the young ones into them as soon as we could but A. & I could not get to bed until after we crossed the frontier that gone two o'clock Customs H[ouse] examination — This took place after 11.30 & was a farce — then we tricked ourselves in for the night — we had a 4 bed comp[artment] to ourselves, securing entire privacy — and very good beds — but by Rattling banging & noise was very great — in spite of which we slept — The Toilette & W.C. accomodations were not all that was promised — the furnishing of the car was extremely simple & plain — unlike those of the [illegible] Salon dit I — classe.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 8, 1893

**Weather:** 17 Ave. du Trocadero  **Temp:** 3me [étage]

Paris at Mad. Desmarestts.

A very close, hot day with thunderstorms in the afternoon, the weight of which passed near us. Soon after 6 A.M. the Sleeping car Porter awakened us — after all, we had passed a tolerably comfortable night — the boys slept like tops — it was about 6.30 when we reached Paris and at once we went into the Custom H[ouse] dep[artmen]t & found our two Trunks that had been sent on from Innsbruck but the valise that started with us from Constance had not arr[ived] — we soon & after the C.H. formality then had our baggage piled upon the top of a cab & started for Mme. Costado's rue Marçaux — There Mme. C. informed us that one had given up her appartement & was to leave Paris, but she had arranged with her mother to take us, so we drove at once to 17 Ave. du Trocadero, near by — before 9 o'clk. were in our Rooms, small but comfortable — convenient, looking out upon a court — Then to ordinary getting — after some washing & changing linen & at 9 o'clock, quite refreshed, I was ready to begin the business of the day — after a short rest I walked to the Champs Elysées to the Salon des Beaux Arts there — & there I spent the with we went [illegible] 2 when I [illegible] — to our quarters — which at 12.30 — after that I had a sound nap for an hour or more and at 3.30 I again went to bed[illegible] and worked conscientiously looking at almost everything until 6 — the hour of closing — There are 4355 paintings & 443 sculptures, [illegible] objets d'art & architectural drawings — for the most part a dreary lot to me — so little do I find of the art, fresh art, to which I was educated 30 years ago — To me this is fantastic, ignoble, insincere — The taste of it is nauseous after Florence & Venice — However, I devoted myself to it with all of my strength trying to understand it & to adjust and at 16 o'clk. was much pleased to find my [illegible] much more tired in my feet than in my head — evidence, I thought, that after my recent journeys & irregularities, I must be in pretty good physical condition to be able to do so much with so little discomfort. At 7 o'clk — dinner. Mad. Costado, a son & a daughter being added to the family party — Very little but french spoken, at times it was very amusing to me as this I also made me feel that I am ignorant & stupid — very — we all got to bed early.
