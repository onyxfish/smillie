## Left Page

### Sun. July 9, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — much fresher & cooler
than yesterday. B'kfo at 9 — Annie went to
the R.R. station & at the missing valise and
got back to the house with it at 12.30 — I
spent the m'tg. reading newspapers
& doing some writing — after lunch
Annie & I went to the Salon Champ
de Mars & again I worked there very
faithfully until 6 o'clk — the result
being that I am no better pleased
with the efforts than at first — I regret
now that I did not see the other Salon
of the Champs Elysees — that closed
June 30th — That, I am assured, was
more representative of the art (French)
of to-day — The great majority of the work
in the Champs de Mars Salon seems to
me to be very crude & immature in
character — it does not at all have
the impress of erratic genius — only
the vagaries of imperfectly educated
young men — or even, boys.

Again Mad. Costado, her son & daughter
dined with us — an eve'g of chatting
& to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 10, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant & moderate day — After B'kfo
Annie & I went to the American Embassy
in the Rue Galilée & without ceremony or
trouble I got a note from the Sect'y to the
chief of Administration, Bibliothèque Nationale
asking an extension to me, an Am. citizen,
of the privileges of the institution — we then
went to Dreysel, Harris & Co's & got some
letters — then took Mr. Beeson's watch to
the watch maker's — Then we went to the
Bib. Nat., & without ceremony or trouble I
got, upon presentation of my note from Sect'y
of Am. legation, a card admitting me to the
privileges of a student in the Print Dep't — for
one month — Then we went into the
Print Dep't & to my great surprise found
that there was nothing in the way of a cat-
alogue — I must know, specifically, just
what I wanted to see before going to the Dep't —
They must make a demand for that to int-
no making things easy & plain for the imperfectly
educated student — then we went out, took cab
back to our lodgings & had lunch — after noon,
I took the boys to the Eiffel Tower & spent the p.m.
there — The day was singularly clear & quiet for the
season — I had my opera glass & carefully studied
the wonderful Panorama — with the naked eye I
could see the towers of Chartres Cathedral 75 K. away
There was no unpleasant cause, to me, of being upon a
dizzy height & I enjoyed the experience very much.
It was nearly 6 when we came down & got to our
lodging — Annie had been out shopping etc.
A late dinner — a dull short eve'g & to bed.
Heavy rain during the night.
