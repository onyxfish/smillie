## Left Page

### Sun. May 28, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A perfectly delicious day — pure, clear and cool. It was 11 o'clk. before A. & I got to the Pitti Palace where E. joined us later — we enjoyed our second visit to the pictures very much & did not leave until nearly 10 o'clk — then home — dinner — after that at least & later E. A. — the boys & Mr. Thurburn eldest boy & I went to the Boboli gardens adjoining the Pitti Palace. Later we were joined there by Mrs. Bason & her daughter — The gardens are the loveliest in their way, that I have ever seen — The atmosphere was perfect & we all revelled in it. I enjoyed so much the paths thro' the dark delicious growing woods — the views down the valley, down the mountains from the Belvidere, but most of all — the superb avenue down the hill to Vasca del Isolotto at its foot — The trees, the sunshine and the color formed a wonderful picture — It was 6 o'clk — when we very reluctantly left by the gate on the Via Romanos and returned home so that the children might have their tea — our every meal — a high tea, was at 7 o'clk. Suey I showed a lot of my etching pls. (by special request) to some of our fellow boarders, in the salon — It was near 10 before I got through with my show.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 29, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — clouded afternoon — It was 10 o'clk. when E. A. & I went to the Badia, an old church where we saw a fine wooden ceiling — some monuments, frescos & cloisters, none of which impressed us profoundly — Then we crossed the street to the Bargello, a national museum in an old Palace — the open court of which we thought exceedingly fine — there was a gallery devoted to a Donatello [illegible] but it was very disappointing to me (Lucca della Robbia — not Donatello) — There was much in the museum that was interesting. Requiring more time for its proper enjoyment than we could give — Soon after 12 we went to the Opera del Duomo or Cathedral museum where we saw more Lucca della Robbia (but not what I sought for) & Donatello in Cantoria or organ-loft — I clever altar by by anybody. Prejudice — the authorities do not agree about such things — also a lot of architectural plans for finishing facade of Cathedral. Then we went to the foot of Giotto's Tower & for a few minutes amused ourselves feeding the great flock of very tame pigeons — then home to lunch — after that I spent some time showing my etching pls. to Mrs. Thurber — at 3.15 — E. A. — the boys & I rode to the Signora — thence took Tram — carried us to near the Porta in Femo where a tram motor was attached & we went away around through Viale Michelangelo & Michelucci past Porta Romano to the monastery & Certosa di Val d'Ema, where Chartreuse cordial is made — There was much that was interesting but as I had expected something quite different I was much disappointed — There was a crowd of 8 right seers under guidance of a very slow monk & we had to run to catch our train before we had quite finished the rounds — I bought a little bottle of cordial & poor Mr. — got home at 6.30 — Dinner — news paper & Diary —
