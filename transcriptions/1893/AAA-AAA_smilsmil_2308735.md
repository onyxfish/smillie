## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 16, 1893

**Weather:** Pension International - Rue Rossini - Room 37  **Temp:** [illegible]

My birth-day, but that is a matter of no consequence except to make a sorrowful note that now, with good reason, I feel like sixty.

A very pleasant day, altho' not altogether cloudless. It was nearly before E. E. & I started out — first to Bankers — where she got letters & I got money — then to Herald office & learned that all the trains from the north were blocked in the snow. Then we went into some of the hotels & looked at rooms & enquired about prices — then back to our hotel to dejeuner. I was much pleased passing thro' the flower, fruit & vegetable market — after lunch, E. & I started out again & taking a cab drove to various excursions — first to those on the Promenade — I was discouraged — we found lower prices but cleanliness was written too plainly over everything — at last we determined to seek more in-land & among other places went to the Pension Internationale, quite an extensive establishment in the Rue Rossini, just off the Rue Alphonse Karr — there there was a general air of comfort & the prices very moderate — one good sized room, a double bed & a nice room off — two single beds, 28 f. per day for the four of us — so, before 4 o'clk, we left to the hotel — packed up our duds & by 5 o'clk were in our new quarters — The manager at the Hotel des Princes, with whom I had been entirely frank, behaved very nicely & did not press us — The H. des P. is advantageously situated for view, in a warm corner, protected by a high cliff at the north — but it is far away from the centre of town — it is not first class in its appointments but is clean & moderately good — We established ourselves as quickly as possible, getting a fire going & alarm lighted — at 5.30 I took the boys down for their every meal & at 6.30 E. A. & I went to table d' hote, which we found fairly good — Well I dozed in an easy chair in front of a warm fire.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 17, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful & almost cloudless day — while we were taking our early "café" — (about 8 o'clk) in our room — the sun shine coming in at the window — a man & a woman, with mandoline & guitar, came & sung in the garden beneath the balcony, where bright colored oranges hung upon the trees & palms spread their plating tops — and as the boys tossed them coffee & laughed in the open windows I felt that at last the winter of our discontent had been made not a glorious summer, at least, a pleasant one — Later, A — the boys & I, sauntered to the garden & Promenade — then to the flower & nut & go market where we saw bread & many figs & [illegible] — then thro' a number of the narrow crooked streets of the old town where I found stuff for a number of sketches — then to the P. O. — home in time for dinner — In the afternoon as A. did not feel like going with us, James, Ralph & I went to the garden & listened for awhile to the band — then along the Quai du Midi to the castle hill — we found it quite a climb to the top by way of the stairs — the views were very lovely over the city & the sea — we wandered all over it & down the north side. Returning home thro' the Place Garibaldi — saw the new statue — over the bridge & thence home. The boys, as usual, had their meal at 5.30 — we had our dinner after 6 — every. E. & A. sat with us for awhile and I dozed — To bed early.
