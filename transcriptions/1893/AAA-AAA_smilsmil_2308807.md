## Left Page

### Fri. June 9, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, warm day until about 3 P.M, when thunderstorms came slowly up from the west & threatened all the rest of the afternoon & early evef — only some light showers falling, barely enough to wet the pavements but quite enough to prevent our going out — Annie & the boys did get to the gardens but were obliged to hurry back to escape the rain. Later I walked over the same ground — Before 10 A.M. — (A. E. & the boys having gone off on a shopping expedition) I took a steam boat & went to the Academy of fine arts where I spent the rest of the morning in religiously studying a few of the most notable paintings there — After the Florentine galleries the collection seems very meagre. After leaving the Academy I walked thro' a number of the streets, then crossed the Grand Canal & back to hotel just in time for lunch at 1 — after that, the afternoon was given over to loafing and watching the weather — my head got very heavy & I was listless — Evef I spent the twilight & until after 9 o'clk on balcony watching the gloom deepen over the water — the ghostly mass of S. Giorgio Maggiore — the twinkling lights on the gondolas with their long, trailing reflections — the crowds on the Riva & listening to the church bells in their seasons of clanging and the occasional strains of the band playing upon the Piazza S. Marco.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 10, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Misty, close & overcast m'f, clearing before noon — storms came up at about 6 P.M. — & there was rain two hours or more. A. & E — spent the m'f at the Academy of fine arts while I sat on our balcony & sketched with the pen, the view looking to the mouth of the Grand Canal. I worked closely until lunch at 1 o'clk — after that we all went in a gondola, by E's invitation, to the Banker's & at about 3 I ret'd to hotel & again worked on pen sketch until 4.30 while A — went to Public gardens with the boys — Before 5 I joined them there, but on my way back stopped to make a sketch of some remarkable painted sails on a boat lying by a landing place. Dinner at 6.30 — Evef wrote part of a letter to Mary —
