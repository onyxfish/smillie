## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 25, 1893

**Weather:** Brilliant, cloudless, windy & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cloudless, windy & cold — we had a meagre help served to us in our bed room & then to Cottage Grove Station elevated R.R. to the fair grounds — by hurrying we got to Etching Club Exhibit — Art Gallery a few minutes after 10. There, by appointment, we met Mr. & Mrs. Koehler & spent the rest of the mng with them — we went through a large part of the Am. Art Exhibit & at about 1 we all went over to the N.Y. house & had a rather poor lunch there, served with the greatest deliberation — it was after 2 when we went out & under Mr. K's direction we went to his exhibit in the government Bldg. After this we left them & A. & I hurried on to the liberal arts bldg — & out to the pier where we took steamboat at 4.15 to the city — strong wind & much pitching. 5 o'clk when we landed in the city — we had a long walk thro' thronged streets, folk Van Buren to 36 Clark St. where we bought section on S. season for next Friday afternoon — then we walked down to Wabash Ave., arrived at the 16 & 17 story buildings & took an Indiana Ave. car to 34th S — then another avenue to Cottage Grove Ave — thence walked to Lake Ave & 37th S. where at No. 3736 we called up on Mrs. Hobbs, Ella Howe's sister, getting there a little before 7. They were at dinner & after a short delay we saw them — we were very busy & after two long walk & made our visit a very short one. — We caught a train at 7.20 on the El. Central suburban & in less than 18 min. got to 62nd S — then by electric cars to Cottage Grove Station & thence to the Delaware House & had a very satisfactory supper. — Then to our room at about 8.30 — a glorious moonlight night — we found a letter from Kate, a very gratifying news from the Montrose home. I wrote about a letter to Charl & got to bed after 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 26, 1893

**Weather:** Splendid day, brilliant, cloudless & much milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Splendid day, brilliant, cloudless & much milder than yesterday — in spite of our efforts it was 10 o'clk. before we got to the pier — we went directly through Machinery Hall — then thro' Agriculture Hall & then onto the Carnival. Santa Maria — then to the Convent of La Rabida — then back to the liberal arts to the Educational Dept — through & out to the Wooded Island, walking the length of it & seeing the Jap. houses — here A. & I separated, she went to the Woman's Bldg & lunched — I went to the Gov't Bldg. & in the Smithsonian offices met Mr. Koehler by appointment at 12.30 — I hoped to meet [illegible] dept main of the Berlin Print Room etc. — some of the Boston museum — neither of those gentlemen materialized — Mr. K. introduced me to Mr. Thos. S Millie, of Washington, with whom I had a pleasant chat — at last, giving up waiting, Mr. K. & I went to the Polish restaurant & had lunch at the Art Bldg — we separated — I went in at 2.30 & met A. — we spent some time among the French pictures & then we went out & saw several government houses — then we went to a chair stand & hired two chairs & to our surprise — Young Chandler of Montrose wheeled A's chair. We went the length of the Midway Plaisance & stopped for half an hour in the Chinese Theatre — it was a little after 6 when we were deposited at the Wonders Bldg — there we met Miss [illegible] & Mrs. Clapp & then we went to the restaurant & had our every meal — then we met two of the Frick Bldg & walked the length of the Wooded Island & spent some time in the Electricity Bldg — then we walked down the Court d'Honeur to the Peristyle, ecstatic over the moonlight & effects — we took a boat off over the lake then walked up the So. side of the Court out at 64th S. gate & home by Trolley cars getting in at about 9.30. After 11 when I got to bed.

Mr. K. introduced me to Frederick Douglass, with whom we had a pleasant chat as we stood & talked — was beset by people who thrust themselves upon him, sometimes in a way excessively disagreeable.
