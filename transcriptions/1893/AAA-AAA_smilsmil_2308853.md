## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 7, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Montrose.*

Very warm & hazy — about 5 p.m. we ran into a very heavy thunderstorm & thro' it before we got to Montrose — chilly eve'g with a very heavy mist — I got up in the m'g with normal pulse & temperature to my great joy — at 6.30 Greta came to my bed room to announce that the water back in the Range had burst and that the kitchen was afloat; consequently we had to put up with a cold b'kfst — we were ready for the app. man when he came for our 6 trunks not before 11 — Annie, whose cold is worse than ever today, spent the m'g out — Walter Cook — Charles — Grace Mclound called — at noon we left the house leaving many moony in charge — & we got off very comfortably on the 1 o'clk train — as we approached Scranton we met the very heavy storm noted above — at 5 — at 6 — I took supper — we lunched on the train — Got to Afford at 7.15 — in time to Montrose at 8 — much surprised to find no one in waiting for us at the Station — got into a bus & rode up to the house & found it locked up & darker than the night — we then went to Chas. Sayre's & soon found from Mary & Kate, now of why all our plans had miscamed — Mr. Kent had left M. 2 or 3 hours before a letter to her naming the time of our arrival arrived — as neither A. or I had written to Mary, Kate & Herrick this information did not reach them — Herrick went to Susquehanna with the band & Mary & Kate remained in their quarters without a thought that we were bowling about in the dark — But everything was soon cleared up — with a lantern we all returned to the house which we found in most admirable order, thanks to Mr. Kent's kind thoughtfulness — fires all ready to light — provisions in the pantry & beds made up — Chas. Sayre came in later with Bessie Moran — After a time we were left alone & then got to bed much to our joy.

*\* departure from N.Y.*

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 8, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy in the early m'g, but soon cleared to be a heavenly day. Annie and I managed to get ourselves dressed in time to get to Chas. Sayre's at 8.20 to take breakfast as invited last night — very pleasant — After it sat in parlor with Mary, Kate & the girls for awhile chatting — introduced to W. Thos. Benedict — then I set to house — saw Herrick — commenced unpacking — went about & looked over part of the grounds — Mary, Kate & girls came up with it — & spent rest of m'g going over her things. Dinner about 1.30 — after that I took a long saunter over the grounds — found things in pretty good order, but every where evidences that a director was wanted — Mr. & Mrs. Benedict & daughter came to the house with Mary before 6 — & then I went down town to do some shopping & back to tea at 7.
