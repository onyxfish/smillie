## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 7, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still lovely weather — don't know whether it is the new medicine or not, but I have felt much better to-day. Spent most of the day indoors working with color upon a sketch made at the Fish Market a few days ago — a short walk before dejeuner — worked on W.C. sketch until about 4 o'clk — then went out with A —, met the boys & they went with us — on the Quai du Midi had a scare — Ralph slipped out of sight while we were standing up watching something — but seeing as he came on up the Quai — we did not know which way he had gone — so A. ran to the hotel thinking that possibly he might have made his way there, while Jim & I went on up the Quai — at last, almost at the far east end, we met him in charge of an English lady who, seeing him crying, had taken to him — it was a great relief to us all — Dinner — Evg. reading "Century" for Prof.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 8, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m'g. but somewhat overcast in the afternoon. In the m'g. finished Fish Mkt. sketch & had a short walk with A. & the boys before dejeuner — After that I went with my W.C. traps to a bit beyond the Fish Mkt. & blocked out a portion of a sketch but the sun going behind clouds I gave up work about 3.30 & took myself back to the Hotel — Then I went to the Jetty Casino & met A. & the boys & we all went in & heard part of a poor concert & saw the whole of a very commonplace Pantomime Ballet. I had been nursing a growing discomfort under my apron all the afternoon — it continued to increase — & by the time I got to the dinner table I was very ill at ease. By the time the fish came on I was obliged to give up to a very severe attack of pains in the stomach & went to my room where I hung about without much relief for an hour or two. Again I am very much discouraged — not knowing what trouble, more serious, may be brewing — who to go to for advice or how I can get the diet that I fear will be a necessity before I can recover.
