## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 23, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the m'f, — cloudy afternoon
ev'f, bits of veiled moonlight. No
men at work until the afternoon
when Hill did some digging. — I
got considerable work done in the
studio — on Mr. Koehler's little mez-
zotint plate. — At 10 o'clk. Ralph
& I went to the P.O. & there I got an
exceedingly comforting & satisfactory
letter from Annie giving full par-
ticulars concerning the accident
Jim's eye. There was also a letter from
Mary, written on Sat. giving a detailed
acc't. — It was a wonderful escape
Ev'f, I worked for some time
upon plans for framework for one
of the tanks.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 24, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy, mild m'f, but the weather con-
tinued clearing until the ev'f was
cloudless, with a full moon. — All
hands at work in the pasture. J.D. Oakley
his team — Stone, Putnam, Hill & Williams
They got out an immense lot of very large
stones & hauled them into place for the
foundations for tank at south end of new
barn, almost entirely filling (the stones are
so large) what I had intended for a
small vegetable cellar. — With Stone
I set out the three small pear trees
that little sent to me. — I went to
the P.O. about 11 a.m. hoping to hear
from Annie, but was disappointed —
got a card by the afternoon mail
saying that all continued to go well
with Jim. — I was out of doors
with the men all day — did no work
in the studio, — where Mr. Koehler's
plate lies nearly finished. — Evening
felt very much used up. Wrote a
letter to Annie — at 9.30 took it to P.O. — then
to bed.
