## Left Page

### Fri. March 17, 1893

**Weather:** Overcast m't  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m't - some bright sunshine in the afternoon, with clouds. Up early & wrote ½ m hour before bkp - on letter to Harriet - Bkp. at 8.30 - after that went with Annie to the Vatican & spent the first of the m't there with Michael Angelo in the Sixtine Chapie & finding Raphael in Stanza [illegible] - I was much pleased to find that I could really enjoy the ceiling of the Sixtine Chapel - it is full of charming & harmonious color to me - definite & subtle tones that were were very delicate in their relations - a quality of grey that I did not at all expect to find. I am not sure as to how much of this is to be credited to the painter & how much to Time & change. I do not care for the work piece at all. The Last Judgment — that has none of the color qualities of the ceiling & has, in many parts, evidently changed for the worse - it seems to be entirely without a scheme in color or com-position - the other frescoes upon the side walls failed to awaken me - The effect of the Chapel, as a whole, was disagreeable to me — gloomy & chaotic, with a sense of bareness owing to the absence of all mouldings — To me, the effect of pointed mouldings, tapestry s etc is very disagreeable. Raphael's frescoes at the Stanzas have failed to touch me - He is the one master whose work is entirely inapplicable to me & this I have earnestly sought for an understanding of it. Back to Hotel at 1 - Luncheon - prepared my own in my bedroom. P.M. - Annie went off at sight seeing with Elizabeth & I went to a terrace on the Pincio & worked upon a pen & ink sketch until after 6 — then walked for an hour — dinner at 6.30 — Evg. Reading newspaper & studying Baedeker.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 18, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day - mild growing breezy in the afternoon — As soon after our bkp. as we could get away, Annie, the boys & I, by bus, went over to St. Peters - strolled again thro' the building - listened to a part of the m't service. I enjoyed the effect of the organ notes & the chanting of the choir in the chapel as I wandered in the more distant parts of the edifice — We all climbed to the dome stopping at the two interior galleries where had close views of the mosaics & on the first one tested its most inspiring greeny effect. There up, between the two & outer dome — Was disappointed to find that the passage way is enclosed & plastered so that it is impossible to see the construction — out at upon the gallery at front of cupola or lantern — delicious atmospheric effect — some clouds over the mountains, clothing them with opalescent shadows — beautiful snow tops — intense interest studying the city as it lay below us — vast plains to the sea — with a glass could distinguish its line — within the two boys continued our climb into the ball — a hard bit of work & the last as a perpendicular ladder has to be climbed, the top passive through a hole less than 2 feet in dia — but I got R— & all eight of us & Jim had a good time, banging on the copper sides of the ball — on descending Jim romped some time on the Roof — it was about 12.30 when we reached the pavement — cab — hotel — lunch at 1 — Afternoon I worked for a couple of hours on pen sketch out of doors — a terrace & the Pines — then I went to the grounds of the Villa Borghese & walked there until 6 — delighted with the grand Pines & the avenue of live oaktrees, finding studies everywhere — Had a short rest before dinner — Evg. - I worked some time on the pen sketch upon which I worked in the afternoon —

Have felt a decided improvement in my condition today — I think that my trouble has been & is, dense catarrh of stomach & bowels and that now healing has commenced — a considerable quantity of mucus & what appears like flakes of dead [illegible]thelium has passed from me at [illegible] in the m't.
