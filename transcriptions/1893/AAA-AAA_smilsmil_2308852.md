## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 5, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot, skim-milk sort of a day — Up at about 7 & before bkfs. went to the grocer's & bought some peaches & plums — also, a news paper. Some time with newspaper after bkfs. — & unpacking preparatory to repacking — Alice Ireland came in — About 11.30 I went to 2°. Nat. Bk. to see some English foreign bit Mr. Case gave me the address of a man near by who did that sort of thing. Then I went to Hunters, the optician, & ordered a pair of double focus glasses upon the Moryes formula. I have not grown my first pair. Then home & had dinner at 1.30 — In the afternoon I gave up to a nap. Well I went to see Dr. Gale but he was out of town — Ed. Cook called — then Charl — I wrote a short note to Ge°. H.S. — & got to bed before 11.

Louise & young [illegible] called — also Charles Collins — & others. Mr. Valentine —

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 6, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another hot day & again did no marketing before bkfs. Annie & I both badly coming down with influenza. I set to work seriously to repack my trunk — Annie also at work — 7 trunks to be emptied — selections made & it repacked — great confusion. I got very tired. Dinner after one — continued packing until near 6 — then out for a short walk — left order with Jackson's [illegible]. Tea about 7. Well Mr. Valentine came in — Then Ge. & Quincy, Slater, Charl. — I was very much used up — considerable fever — headache, &c — & at last I excused myself & went off to bed.

In the afternoon, while down in 23° [illegible], seeing the N.A. A.D. show for a "loan [illegible]" I stopped to see it, but at the door was informed that N.A.'s had no rights & were admitted only up on payment of ½ a dollar each. I did not go in.
