## Left Page

### Thur. July 13, 1893

**Weather:** Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain again during the night + a dull
m'f until 10.30 then A - the boys + I
went out - on our way to the Bankers, in the
fob of an omnibus, we were caught by a
heavy shower - rushed into Printemps
where A. bought a hat - the shower being
past we then went on to Drexel, Harjes
& Co. where I got my new letter of Co.
Then back to our lodgings in a cab.
Lunch - + after that I went to Bib-
nat. + until 4 o'clk. studied their very
poor collection of Ch. Many oils - then
I walked to Notre Dame Cath. + in
it - + into the M[illegible] - then to Hotel
de Ville + wandered about until after
5 when threats of another storm sent
me home by steamboat - got in just
ahead of a dash - Dinner at 7.30
Ev'f, having seen Champ neuf's name
among the Registered visitors - 19 Quai
Voltaire, I sent him a note -

## Right Page

### Fri. July 14, 1893

**Weather:** Rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the early m'f - clearing before 11 -
Very pleasant afternoon + ev'f. After bkfs. I went
to call on Jno. D. Wrand, 164 W. Parnasse, He spent
most of the time in reading latest chapters
of life + times of his father. - I left him at
12 o'clk? to our lodging - after that, a nap.
Then A - the boys + I went to Miss Joshay's
lodgings + with her we went to the Arc d'Triole
+ climbed to the top of it from whence we had
an admirable view - saw Mrs. Carnot and his
escort of Cavalry return from the Review and
then if we did not see any considerable mili-
tary display, we saw, at any rate, a wonderful
procession of cabs - 6 & 8 abreast + a mile or two
long - the view over the city was very charming -
it was after 6 when we got home - dinner
late - to our usual family was added a young
lieut. in the English army in India and nephew of
old Mr. Desmarct - about 9 o'clk. A - Miss Joshay
(who had come to us) + I went to the Trocadero and
saw the fine illumination there - took cab + rode
to the Champs Elysees. Fairy-like illumination -
the trees hanging full of globe-shaped [illegible]
paper lanterns, looking like a strange fruit - the
cafes all aglow with light + cabs + people swarming
everywhere - we drove on down the Rue Rivoli
which was largely ill— by continuous rows of gas
jets extending along the fronts of the buildings ab-
ove the gardens of the Tuileries for nearly half a
mile - the Place de la Concorde was ill - many blue [illegible]
also - the little garden of trees about the Tour St. Jacque
was all alight - myriads of lanterns + colored lights
at the foot of the tower, which was itself ill— But the
finest effects were in the square before the Hotel
de Ville. here were festoons of lights all about,
banners without end + numberless people
who shouted + sang + seemed to be having a
very good time - we left our cab here and
wandered among the crowd for some time -
then took it again + drove thro' the Boulevards
back to the Place de la Concorde - + up the Champs
Elysees - every where great crowds of people
+ cabs - the seats + tables of the cafes encroach-
ing upon the roadways - bands playing at
certain places + now + then dancing, which
became more general later - we got home
a little after 11, really too early to have seen
much of characteristic Parisian frolic.
