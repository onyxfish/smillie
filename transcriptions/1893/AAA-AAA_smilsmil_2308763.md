## Left Page

### Mon. March 13, 1893

**Weather:** A fine day — rather cooler  **Temp:** [illegible]

To me it has been a day of depression & pain commencing very soon after I got up in the mrg. & culminating at about 10 o'clk. Before before, afterwhich I took things very easily until after 10 — Annie went to Eliz & I took my [illegible] & went up on the Pincian Hill & with the camera-lucida made an outline pencil sketch of a plumb tree in full blossom through the masses of branches & blossoms a view of the dome of St. Peters. I got back to the hotel at 12 so uncomfortable that I could not do any work. I sat in easy chair until 1 o'clk. when Annie came in. I went down to lunch tables but could not eat anything. Before 2 o'clk. I determined to go to the Doctor & consult him again — Annie went to Spadoni's lecture on the Palestine Hill — I went to the Dr's and he assured me that in spite of my severe pain I was better, examining my tongue & saying it was much improved — This was probably professionally the correct thing to do, but it was not satisfactory to me. I am now reading anxious about my condition — he evidently thinks that I am "bilhaired". I got to the hotel & being very empty I ate something. About 5 I went out & had a saunter on the Pincian Hill without pleasure — At 6.30 I ate a very light dinner. Much of the Eve. I spent in the bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 14, 1893

**Weather:** Fine mrg. — clouded afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

— was routed out of bed before 6 A.M. by demands that I could not afford to neglect — I also had much discomfort & some pain — so that I did not get up until — had nursing help in bed — Eliz. came [illegible] — A — the boys went off to see the crowds as today is the King's birthday — It was after 11 when I got up & then I went up to the terrace at the Pincio & sunned myself there — I prepared my own lunch & all. It in our own room at 1 o'clk. For some time after lunch I worked on Water-Color sketch of Almond blossoms — Then went out for a walk with A — we went into some photo. stores & bought a lot of Roman views — Back to hotel at 5.30 & had a rest before dinner — at 6.30 — At about 8.30 Eliz. came in a cab & the boys who had been allowed to stay up, A — & I got in & had a curious ride about to see the illuminations which amounted to very little — The Corso had an approach to any illumination & an unusual number of people were there but it could hardly be described as festive —
