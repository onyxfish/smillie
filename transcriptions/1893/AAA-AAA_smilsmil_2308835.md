## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 2, 1893

**Weather:** Really a pleasant day — more sunshine than shadow & some warmth.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to the museum at 10.30 & again to work upon the search for the growth of aqua-tint — a discouraging task, so much that is of no value or interest has to be turned over at the cost of much time & patience, but something may be missed that should be seen — Lunch at 1.30 & then amused myself out of doors for a little while — then in Print Room until 4 — gave up feeling that I was accumulating only a heap of rubbish — then walked to Hyde Park Cor. and took bus to Fulham Rd. & went to see Jack Short — but he is out of town — at b. Then home & had a rest until dinner at 7 — Eve'g going over my notes written during the day & reading newspaper.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 3, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy & cold.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to museum at 10.30 & pursued my aqua-tint studies looking over a great lot of the common stuff issued by English publishers late in the last century & early in this, finding nothing to give me either pleasure or profit — Lunched about 1.30 & amused myself for a while with the Egyptian mummies before returning to my work — About 3 o'clk. Smilie came in & for about an hour she wrote some translations for me concerning S[t]. Non. — Then we went out — A — returned home — I went to Newman's & left my W.C. boy to be mended — then to Rim & tried to get a "Life of Paul Sandby" — then to an afternoon in Bond S[t]. & then home at 6 — letters from home — a rest before dinner at 7 — Eve'g wrote large part of a letter to Kate.
