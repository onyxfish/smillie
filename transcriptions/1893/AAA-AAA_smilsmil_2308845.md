## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 22, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day with some slight showers.
Got to Museum at 10.30 & spent some time
again over the Von Siegen & Prince Rupert prints
& when at last I concluded that I had learned
all that I could of the manner of doing them by
looking at them. I gave it up & commenced
on Sam'l. Cousins — upon these I worked until
after 1.30 — then I lunched in the building.
Then I went to Soho Sq. & saw Mr. Mills about
getting some dry Sepia & he astonished me by
telling me how expensive it is & I gave up the
idea. Then I rode home & with Annie &
the boys went down to So. Kensington Museum
— Dep't. of ship models etc — There we left the boys
while we went to the Imperial Institute & saw
the Royal wedding presents — a sort of general
fair where merchants & tradesmen had sent
specimens of their wares to the Duke of York & Prin-
cess May — Carriages, Pianos, baby-linen, umbrellas,
shoes, sticks, dishes, pictures — all sorts of things —
we rushed thro' the rest of the show, then back
at 6 for the boys, got them — & all walked home
together — Dinner at 7 — Eve'g I spent some
time over the Home Rule discussion now going
on.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 23, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dreary, drizzly m'g — cleared for a time
in the afternoon charmingly, then rained
again — I determined to give the day
at home to gathering my things together
& doing preliminary packing — I was
able to get most of my many & varied
little things together, sorted & bundled —
about 5 o'clk I went with Ralph to
the Round Pond & there we found Jim
visiting his boat — about 6 we all set
together — Dinner was not served until
7.30 — Eve'g I wrote notes to Mr. Sidney
James — Mr. F. Goueding & to J. Pettingall
the photographer who "took us" on the top
of the Spring Coach.
