### Sat. Feb. 11, 1893

*Hotel Belle Vue. Rooms 39. 40. 41.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — very bright & very warm — my room
was flooded with sunshine as soon as the sun rose.
I had my early [illegible] before the others being more
than ready — After it I had some pain but not
of the demoralizing kind from which I have
suffered several times lately — Mr & Mrs Gifford
came over about 9 o'clk. & later went with Bea &
Annie & Pruitt. Jonides quarters while I re-
mained amusing myself on the porch for the
rest of the m[orning]. The boys amused themselves
by playing about — A. & E. ret— after 12, reporting
that they had discovered satisfactory quarters
on the East Bay at the Hotel Belle Vue. We
had dinner — settled our bills, very well
satisfied with the National & its manager & his
wife who came out to our carriage to bid us
adieu — we drove along the water front — when
very near our hotel had to turn back on acct. of
the approaching Carnival Procession — It was a
fine pretty sight, Really! The color, movement in
the Suai Boulevard — the sunshine & the sea —
we had to return some distance before we
could find a place where we could turn out
of the road — which had "confetti" was strewn —
several round lumps of clay — about the size of
peas — they hurt the face severely & soiled the
clothes. The procession was much less pre-
tentious than the one at Nice but it was more
genuine & picturesque. The surroundings also
were more in sympathy with the showing to me
it was very amusing — albeit the confetti throwing
rather Rolled me after getting some stinging shots
in the face. In about half an hour we were
able to resume our journey & soon got to the Belle-
vue where we were graciously rec— I am very
much pleased with the change — the suite of
rooms is charming — one flight up — a salon & three
rooms — 3 enaded — [illegible] [illegible] & the sun
at [illegible] — it looks a little S. of E. Considerably
visible — A — the boys & I took a short walk along
the road by the sea — back before 5 — soon after
The boys & I had our supper — it was a good
one — A. A. & E — dined at Table d'hote at 6.45 —
very four Swiss trio — or [illegible] [illegible] as I have written
After diner Trio — Jan[illegible] 20[illegible] — played in the large
corridor for our amusement. Later in the
Salon — Sig— came in from time to chat &
enjoy the fire — It was about 11 when we
got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 12, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delicious, heavenly m[orning]. mild, balmy and
cloudless — with the sunshine flooding our suite
of rooms — our large salon as a breakfast room —
windows wide open — a wide view of the
Mediterranean whose summer sea surf
lapped, with soothing sound, the shingles
at the foot of our terraced garden of palms,
olives & oranges — It seemed to me as though
we had at last reached Paradise & that
this hotel was the very centre of that best
place. I spent all that remained
of the m[orning]. sunning myself up on the
terrace &, as it seemed to me, drinking
in health & streng[th] with the sweet, pure
air. Déjeuner at 1 — I tried to write
to somebody & part at least of one of the
many letters that I now owe, but nothing
came of the effort. Later in the afternoon
A — the boys & I walked out to the Italian
frontier, less than a mile east of the hotel,
where a gorge is spanned by a bridge of
a single arch. The wind blew very strong
the white, lime dust was so exceedingly
unpleasant that we gave up our walk
at that point & ret— I dined again
with the boys while A. & E. went to table
d'hote — while Annie read aloud for a
little while & I went to sleep.
