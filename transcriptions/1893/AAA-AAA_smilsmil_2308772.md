## Left Page

### Fri. March 31, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got up after an uncomfortable night feeling very wretched — considerable fever that increased so that Annie went for Dr. Scattie after lunch. He came at about 4 o'clk. The cold in my head & throat had come back with more than double force — Still, he thought no drugs were required — only keep very still in bed, & be careful of my diet — To some extent I was comforted for, to tell the truth, I don't like to have fevers fooling about me in this country.

Temperature.
A.M. 99²/₅. 12M. 100²/₅. 7.P.M.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 1, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm, clear, quiet day, but although my head & throat felt better my fever continued. — I did not leave my room & most of the time was on my bed — Annie went out with the children in the morning afternoon. — As, at noon, my temp. was as high as at any time yesterday, A. went to the hospital & saw Dr. Scattie to know if I did not require some treatment, but he said no, keep a record, & if it continues to-morrow let me know. It has been dismal for me.

Temperature.
98⁴/₅. 7.AM. 100²/₅. 12M. 99⁴/₅. 7.P.M. 99²/₅. 10. P.M.
