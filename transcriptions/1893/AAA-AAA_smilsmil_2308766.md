## Left Page

### Sun. March 19, 1893

**Weather:** Brilliant, cloudless day, but cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cloudless day, but cold and with strong wind — In the mts I walked down the Apetta stopping at churches until I got to the Pantheon three across is the Pa. Venezia — then having used up my time, back to hotel — Elly joined us at lunch at 12.30 & at 1.30 we took carriage & started for the Appian Way — We went out thro' the Porta S. Sebastiano & stopped first at the Vigna Farnesini & saw the Remains of the largest ancient Columbarium — we went down into two — ness are others — one is used for a dwelling, another for a wine cellar — we found the place exceedingly interesting. Further on we stopped at the Catacombs of St Calixtus & of course under direction of a guide, explored a portion of them, finding some exceedingly interesting chapels & tombs & inscriptions — what with buildings above ground & borings under grounds these old fellows must have been left out — As we rode on of course we took a good look at the great circular tower, the tomb of Cecilia Metella — then we passed numerous other tombs, more or less ruined, showing what a cheerful drive it must have been at its best — the remains of the old pavement, if no better when first laid, must have miles made any drive a penitential pilgrimage. After a long drive we reached the large tomb Casal Rotondo & thought it a good place to turn about for our return — the wind blew furiously — the mountains — it was very cold — the sky was cloudless but over the mts. were some clouds — the views of the mts. were fine but the middle ground & foreground were uninteresting — the road itself was very bad & the dust was horrible — As a ride the expedition was not a success — we turned upon the New Appian after returning some distance & entered the city by the Porta di S. Giovanna — getting to our hotel at 5 o'clk. Had a rest — dinner at 6.30. Every a very little reading & feeling stupid after the cold, the wind & the dust of the afternoon — Music at the Michel but we did not either of us feel like accepting Elly's invitation —

## Right Page

### Mon. March 20, 1893

**Weather:** Brilliant, breezy & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, breezy & cold. Ely came after us for one, & I went to the Vatican, leaving our boys with the Kiep Elussian who had come with them — We drove direct to the museum entrance — It is indeed a wonderfully collection housed in a wonderful way. I cannot describe my feelings as, at last, I stood before the Apollo Belvedere & rather abashed by the Apollo & yet already so far away as to be so familiar & yet already so far away as to be mythical rather than ideal — Here we saw the veritable originals & I gazed upon them with an admiration that was very near akin to worship — we devoted ourselves thoroughly to the work until 12.30 & then, feeling that we had had but one brief glimpse into a mine of untold riches we returned to our prosaic abiding — after a little rest after lunch, again I started out taking fine with me — Drink had already been twice & did not care to go — we went to the Palatine Hill & I worked carefully, book in hand, exploring the street fully, book in hand, exploring the stupendous labyrinth of ruins — To me it was intensely interesting — Septimius Severus is now a personal acquaintance of mine, because I have stood in the shadow of his arch — Caligula I am on terms of intimacy with because I have explored the cellars of his house — I know Romulus. I have seen the cave in the rock where the wolf suckled him & I have seen the Bronze Wolf in the Capitoline museum — some of the terrace views over the Forum, Colosseum & the city are wonderfully fine — we went in & out, up & down, until 5 — then out & took tram car to hotel — had a rest — then dinner at 6.30 & every evening as most of the evenings are now spent in studying Baedeker trying to make sure of what we have been seeing during the day & what we expect to see on the morrow.
