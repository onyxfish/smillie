## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 28, 1893

**Weather:** Snow in the m'f but the weather soon cleared.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow in the m'f but the weather soon cleared. Kate so under the weather that she could not go out this ev'f. with Charl's Theatre party, so I took her ticket to him that he might dispose of it. He told me to give it away — I gave it to Walter's boy "Gogo". Then I got to work upon my water color, "Campanile, Belluno" — Annie arrived from Germantown in time for lunch — I worked many drawing until 4 — Then I went to 94 Fifth Ave., by appointment, & saw Mr. Harry Jones, publisher of the Quarterly Illustration — He has asked me to write a short paper on Etching for his publication — I agreed to. Then walked home — Dinner — Went Annie & I went to Abbey's new Theatre, Boxes $3.0.5 — & saw Irving & his Co. in Olivia — "The others (except Kate) went to the Broadway & saw Robin Hood. We found the theatre crowded & Annie & I wept over the presentation of Olivia — & while I wept I protested that the play & the acting had many faults & that there was nothing to cry about. The play was very well cast. Irving is a dear old stick of a Vicar — & Folean Teny's a most marvellous example of an aged kitten — The play was out at 11.20 & an hour later we were in bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 29, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Katie Adams arr'd from Phila.*

Disagreeable weather for awhile in the m'f — then streaks of sunshine & mild — I did not feel like working any Water-Color, owing to last-night's dissipation so I gave myself up to cutting mounts & work of that kind. In the afternoon I regained some of my loss by taking a nap. Katie Adams arr'd from Germantown — We all dressed for the entertainment of the ev'f before dinner — I got off letter to Herrick — at 8 I went to Walter's where the youngsters gave us the play of "Poor Pillicoddy" — Carleton Cook and [illegible] Edna Cook was Mr. P.P. — Gogo (Walter) Cook was Cap'n O'Scuttle — Liz Oakley was his wife & Sally was the shop girl. The youngsters did very well & everybody was much amused — The play was over at 9.30 — then came dancing — I would have been glad to beat a retreat but had to wait to take Jim home — orders were not issued until almost 11.30 Then I took him home &, having left my party in charge of [illegible] Valentine, I went to bed myself rolling in just before midnight — Annie did not come along until an hour later.
