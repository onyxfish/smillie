## Left Page

### Wed. July 5, 1893

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m[orning], then clear — very thunderous hot — storms about — showers late in the afternoon that went into a steady rain that continued most of the night — 9kls— at 9 — it was after 10.30 [illegible] & I started out — much of what Remained of the m[orning] was spent in running about from R.R. station to freight station & then to Custom Ho. & getting our trunks that had been sent on from Venice — we had a chance to see something of the town — Back to hotel at 12.30 & had lunch. It was 3 when we again started out, this time without the boys, & until 6 we were doing the Hof Kirche & buying photos, etc. The interior of the old church with its with to all absorbing May? tomb with its quaint guard of gigantic bronze monarchs, was very interesting. Back to hotel at 6 — I took my dinner with the boys at 6.15 — Mrs. & Mrs Beeson are? from Cortina at 4.30 this P.M. Every a [illegible] concert, in the hall way of the Hotel, by one Linden who proved himself to be a quite a dabster —

## Right Page

### Thur. July 6, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Constance — Insel Hotel — Rooms 58–59.*

Awakened at 4 o'clk by nightingales in the garden, went to Annie's room & awakened her that she might hear them — fell asleep again & got up for the day at about 6.30 — we got our breakfast very comfortably & settled our bill (we liked the Innsbruck very much) & went over to the station soon after 7. Taking the train at 9.45 — The light on the clouds above & the clouds clinging, yet drifting, on the mt. tops was wonderfully beautiful — I love the dense fir forests there met. Charmed by an old castle in the sunlight of a foothill near Telz — also by the old Cistercian monastery near Stams — near Ste. Silj? a height, Petersburg & Castle of Count Rodenegg — Then a narrow & precipitous defile — at a junction of two wild streams the old castle of Wiessberg — Beyond Landeck some wild scenery — Raines, cuttings & tunnels — Then the Arlberg tunnel, nearly 6½ m. long — Then we got into dining car & had a very good dinner — soup, omlette, roast beef, beans, potatoes, real bread, sweet & fruit for 1 m. 80k or about 72¢. From our dining room windows we enjoyed a delightful view of the valley in the neighborhood of Wintergasse? — many viaducts, cuttings & tunnels. At last we enter the valley of the Rhine — a flat, alluvial plain spreading to great width before we reach Bregenz — delicious effects of sunshine & shadow over mtsd. clouds clinging to their tops — it was about 3.30 when we got to Bregenz & changed from cars to the steamboat for Constance — just as we got aboard a heavy shower passed over us, followed by two or three others before we got to C — no spite? of them the sail was charming & restful — it was 5.30 when we got to C — went through a farce of Customs examination then at once to Insel Hotel, originally a monastery on a small island in the lake, an establishment delightful & unique in its way — The cloisters, with garden & vines and modern frescoes is a delight — the Dining Room is in the old chapel, some of the old frescoed walls are still carefully preserved under hangings — the Refectory is now a Café Restaurant — beautiful gardens & fine trees surround the buildings — I we had a good meal & after looking about a little went to our rooms having a broad piazza in front of them — Being tired I went to bed early, fell asleep lulled by the very good music of a band in a garden within pleasant hearing distance.
