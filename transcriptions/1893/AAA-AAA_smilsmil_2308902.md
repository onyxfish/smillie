## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 16, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly nasty day — heavy rain much of the time. I kept indoors & worked upon a large sketch 12½×19" — The Campanile at Belluno. I spent most of the time making a careful outline of the tower which I found difficult to enlarge from my meagre sketch. — Evef. I wrote a letter to Herrick explaining, with the aid of some free hand sketches, what I want done in the lower hall at Montrose.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 17, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine, breezy, cold mtf., clouding up and becoming less agreeable as the day advanced Bkft. late — About 11 Annie, the two boys & I walked to the Central Park & to the Belvidere south of the Reservoir, past the obelisk and mt. at 5th Ave. & 82nd St. Annie & Ralph rode home while Jim & I walked up the Ave. a few blocks further, then down to the Plaza Hotel, where I made a very short call on the Colmans. Then home at 2, just in time for dinner. I had a nap in the afternoon — Geo. & Emily called before 6. Tea at 6.30. Evef. Charl called — I wrote a letter to Henry Sprague concerning the Central Trust Co. of St. Louis mortgages — It was midnight before I got to bed.
