## Left Page

### Sat. July 15, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of many threats — a few light showers & a very little sunshine — In the morning
I went to the Bibliothèque Nat'l & looked
over the Rembrandts — joined the choice
of them were in frames hanging about
where I could not get at them. Home
to lunch at 12.30 & in the afternoon
back again to the Rembrandts
& continued until the place closed
at 4 o'clk — from that time until
after 6 I wandered about amused
by the remnants of yesterday's holi-
day, especially by the little shows
permitted in the streets — one, a group
of acrobats & atheletics, with quite an
outfit, before the Opera house —
Dinner — in the evening Champney
called & spent the rest of the evening
with us — in great good spirits —
He made an appointment to meet
us at Versailles to-morrow afternoon.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 16, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day which in the afternoon became
miserable — very wet & chilly — In addition
I had the discomfort of a headache. The
morning, after breakfast, was spent over newspapers,
a short walk & a visit to the Muséum Guimau
which is very near us — a remarkable coll-
ection of oriental things of all descriptions. India
China Japan & other less known countries.
Lunch at 12.45 — About 1.30 Miss Joshay
came to go with us to Versailles where the
14th of July is to be celebrated by playing fountains,
illuminations & fireworks — just as
we were about to start, Mr. Kipling came
& agreed to join us — when we went out
it was raining & looked so unpromising
that I returned to the house — Mr. K.
took A., the boys & Miss Joshay to the
jardin d'acclimatation where they
all had a very good time.
