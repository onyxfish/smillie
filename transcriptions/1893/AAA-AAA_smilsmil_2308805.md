## Left Page

### Mon. June 5, 1893

**Weather:** Bright pleasant m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright pleasant m'f — heavy rain in
the afternoon — clear ev'f. Not feeling
well in the m'f I did not go out but
worked with pen on some men. in
small sketch book — Lunch — then
altho' I saw the brewing storm, I
went to the Cloisters of S. Croche to
finish my sketch. I may have had
a half hour of sunlight, then the rain
came. For a time it poured down
very heavily & the Thunder was immen-
but I worked on undisturbed and
at last, about 5 — got it done after a
fashion, then, the rain having stopped
I walked home. Dinner. Ev'f for
awhile in salon — To bed early.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 6, 1893

**Weather:** Bright m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Bologne. Hotel Brun (Ancien Palais malerasia) Rooms 43–144.*

Bright m'f. — heavy rain between 2 & 5 o'clk —
clear ev'f. Spent the m'f. packing trunks —
I got through soon after 11 & then rested awhile —
A — the boys & Z — off for some last sight seeing — I
got the baggage all off to the station before 1 o'clk
then we had lunch & after that a very affectionate
parting with Misses Constantius & [illegible] — Mrs. [illegible] —
our niece, Miss Nettleton, the T. boys — in fact, all
kinds — off for station at 2 — very black clouds and
thunder. As usual I got perfectly exasperated
at the tedium, provoking & altogether unnecessary
obstructions put in the way of the traveller in getting
his ticket — his baggage attended to — & [illegible] in
getting his seat — but at last, it was over &
we got away, the rain coming down soon after
we soon left the plain & at once on the hills —
then on heavy grades, with two engines we climb-
ed nearly 3000 feet above sea level — tunnel
after tunnel cutting thro' the crests of the mts.
passing between, beautiful views down deep &
wild valleys — The descent was rapid & less
interesting than the ascent — we got to Bologne
at 5.40 — on time — drove at once to the Hotel
Brun — got our rooms, washed up, ordered our
dinner & then went out for a little walk —
we went directly to the leaning towers &
then back & had a very good dinner — after
that I prowled out again & was very much
interested in the picturesque peculiarities
of the city, as especially its arcaded streets — I
went thro' some of the finest streets & squares
& looked at the outside of some of their largest
churches — Then in at 9 & relieved Annie
who joined Eliz. in the Reading Room.

Today, Jim's birthday — Misses C. & F. very
kindly provided a handsome cake with
10 candles for our lunch tables and
we had a brief & hilarious feast.
