## Left Page

### Sun. June 25, 1893

**Weather:** Overcast & alight drizzle at 8 o'clk  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast & alight drizzle at 8 o'clk — before 10 the weather cleared — breezy. Pleasant &
very cool day — glorious moonlight night.
B[illegible] — a little earlier than us all — about
9 with Annie & the boys in Piazza S. Marco, by
appointment — Photo[illegible] came & took
photos of the boys feeding pigeons in the Square.
Men A. H. went into the church & listened to
a portion of the music of High Mass — Men we
went to a store & purchased a "Head all"
to pack for our mt. trip — Men to hotel, where
I got my sketching traps took boat & went
to a place back of S. Rocco & Il Frari where I
made a very hasty & slight sketch — wind
& chilly air made it unpleasant — Soon
after 12 I packed up & walked to S. Pantalon
& took a peep at the inside. Then into S.M.
del Carmine which I found full of people, so
that I did not have a chance to see the youth
work "the Circumcision" — Ruskin's "Glorious Tin
Foretto" — Then on to S. Sebastiani and where, after
much trouble I got the woman with the keys and
I had a quiet time looking at what is really
a noble tomb of P. Veronese, adorned as it is with
so much of his work — I was also interested in
in the S. Nicholas said to have been painted by
Titian in his 86th year — To me it ceased to be
a good example of his work — I did not dis-
cover any evidences of decrepitude. — From
thence I walked back to the hotel getting in
at 1.15 — lunch — after that a rest — A. went
with the boys to the Public Gardens — I had
a short walk before dinner — After that I took
turn about with A. & put the boys to bed while
she & [illegible] went off on a [illegible] — moonlight
gondola & ice cream at glorious — After I
met them on the Square & with them en-
joyed the glorious moonlight night.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 26, 1893

**Weather:** Brilliant & beautiful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant & beautiful day — After b[reak]f[ast].
went with A — to Cook's office & bought
tickets for Belluno & got some Austrian
paper money for Italian gold — also, made
some enquiries concerning sending our
baggage on to Misbrook — Men A., Z., Mrs.
Beson & Miss Annie B. (who arr. here this
mng. at 5 o'clk.) went off in a gondola &
I see to hotel & for a time worked with
[illegible] on my last sketch — Men. Preparing
to pack trunk — lunch at 1 o'clk.
After that went to a forwarding agent to
have our trunks forwarded but was not
able to complete arrangement — then
back to hotel & to work packing — about
4 o'clk. Miss Eliza Torrey made a pleasant
call — after 5 A. & I went to agent and
completed arrangement about baggage.
Then we went looking for a bronze
door knocker that A. had seen & thought
would make a nice present for the
[illegible] for their Southampto[n] house —
but when we found it we did not
like it so well. Back to hotel at
6.45 — dinner — A., Z., & the
B's went off in a gondola — I put
the boys to bed & wrote a short note
to J. Hopkinson Smith & then, at
9.45 went to the Piazza & met the
party — . We promenaded about for
some time — the moonlight was
glorious — & got back to hotel a
little before 11.
