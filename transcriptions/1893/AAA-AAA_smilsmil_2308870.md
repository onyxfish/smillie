## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 11, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The most glorious day yet. Lovely color, cloudless sky, luminous atmosphere, still air & very warm. I could not do any of my in-door work but spent the day pruning shrubs & cutting out the tops of two trees by the side of the driveway — a fine ash & a strong growing basswood. By 5 o'clk I was very tired & I lay down for an hour & had a good rest. Hemick followed me with a pot of hot tar & smeared the cut ends. Hill finished digging for the water tanks & Stone dug holes for the expected fruit trees.

Eveƒ. I wrote part of a letter to George.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 12, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Quite as warm & beautiful a day as yesterday — I feel rather lame from my unusual work & disinclined to exert myself but I kept out of doors all day & did a fair amt. of pruning & general direction of the other workers. Hemick continued his work upon the decayed sills of the barn — The whole of the east one will have to come out.

Eveƒ. finished my letter to Geo.
