## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 16, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

I think to-day is the hottest one yet —
Meniugy, heavy heat is sickening. I
got to the museum at 10·15 & to work at
once upon Liber Studiorum which I find
exceedingly interesting — Lunch in the
museum Bldg as usual at about 1 — &
then to my work again until 4 — then I
went to Newman's — I also say. but Mr. Mills
was out — then, on top of a bus I rode out
to Uxbridge Rd went to Gooding's — had a
pleasant good bye visit with him — got
ink & paper & then back, home, soon
after 6 — Dinner after 7 — a short eve
uncomfortably hot, divided between
newspaper & writing.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 17, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fearfully hot — got up in the morning
feeling sick & continued to feel so out
of sorts that I determined to stay quietly
in doors & not brave the suffocating heat
in the mts. I finished a letter to Mr. Koshler
& mailed it to him — In the afternoon I
wrote to Herrick & in the eve I wrote
to Mary, having rec'd a letter from her in
the afternoon.
