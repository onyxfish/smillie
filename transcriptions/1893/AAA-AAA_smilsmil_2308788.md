## Left Page

### Tues. May 2, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very beautiful, warm day - but feeling
weak & discouraged - Padre Antonio put
in an appearance at 9.30 & by 10 I got
to work & made a sketch of his head alone
turned more away promises than in the
sketch of yesterday - Then I started upon
W.C. sketch of the well, or cistern, by the
wall of the cloisters - I had him take
a position & sat a very slight sketch of
him - also, a pose in the cloisters - Then
I dismissed him & worked faithfully
until after 12 o'clk. Then, feeling re-
tired, I went out & had a half hours
walk on the terrace before lunch.
After that I worked until 3 o'clk. Trimming
up my W.C. Sketch of the well - Then with A & the
boys went out in a boat with a couple of very nice
fellows who rowed us into a grotto near by a
very fine cave with fine color on a sun shining
mf. While in there they sang two of their old
songs, with refrain, gesticulation & clapping
of fingers - it was very interesting - then we rowed
out to some distance from the shore to get
the panorama, as one of them said - again they
sang - stamped their feet (they do so a standing)
- gesticulated while they rowed - we came
in before 6 - Evef. Festa of the Cross - the
Calveri grotto & a long line of steps to the Road
was illuminated by small candles with fine
effect - before 8 a band of minstrels - mandolin
two guitars & a jug which was blown into with
the effect of a bass-tuba note; took up their
position on a terrace below & to the illuminated
crosses played & sang all of the Papaean and
comic music of the day - The whole effect
was absolutely unique - the grotto, the illumi-
nated calvary - the high, overhanging cliff - the
cloisters - the deep twilight - then the rising
moon that grew in brightness until it shim-
mered over the sea & flooded everything with
its light - it was perfect - The situation for
operatic machinery & performance - but
better than that, it was reality - I paced
up & down enjoying it until nearly 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 3, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Ravello. Pension Palumbo.
Rooms 5 & 6.*

Delightful day - Spent the m'f. closely at
work sketching. Finished pen sketch on the
terrace & worked for awhile on slight pencil
sketch in cloisters - Lunch at 1 o'clk.
Miss Joshay arr'd - from Salerno just in
time to sit down with us. Then we
packed our traps - When I came to settle
our bill S'g'r Vozgi voluntarily made a de-
duction of 10 f. a day, or 90 f. for the 9 days.
It was nearly 4 o'clk. when we started
on our ride up to Ravello, 1200 f. above
the sea - a delightful & exciting exper-
ience - in an hour & a half we reached
Ravello, the ruin of an old town of great
importance in the Middle ages - now a poor
little village but full of interesting bits for the
artist. We went direct to the Pension Palum-
bo where rooms had been engaged for us -
Then we wandered in the oddly shaped
little garden - Then I took a walk through
the town - At 6.30 I had an evef. meal with
the boys. Annie & Miss Joshay dined at table
d'hote - Evef. in parlor chatting with
an English lady.
