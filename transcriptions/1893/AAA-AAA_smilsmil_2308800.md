## Left Page

### Fri. May 26, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day in spite of clouds in the n[orth] + a
few drops of rain in the afternoon — It was
about 9.30 when A. E. + J started out. Drove
to Church + monastery of S. Marco — in the latter
we gave ourselves up to the spirit of the past
+ dreamed away a good part of the morning with
Fra Angelico + his bro. Piero + were deeply
stirred by the memorials of Savonarola — We
also paid deference to S. Antonius + to old Cos-
imo — Pater Patriae — we walked very deliberately
through the corridors, the cells, the library where
Savonarola bid his brethren good bye before
delivering himself up to be hung + then burned —
(we saw a book of his autograph sermons written
in a hand quite as small as this) we went thro'
Refectory, Chapter House + outer + inner cloisters
going to see our most loving attention — When
we came out we crossed to the Accademia
delle Belle Arti + fairly rushed through that
managed, in spite of the Cimabues + Botticelli's
to get home in time for lunch — after that, a
rest — Then A. J[ames] + J went out, Ralph playing
at home with the Thurston boys — We rode to Jerons
then to English Pharmacy — then to Watchmakers +
then to S. Michele in Orto where we much admired
the High Altar Canopy by Orcagna, a beautiful
work in marble now darker in color than very old
ivory — then to Ponte Vecchio where we dismissed
cabby — walked over + to Pitti Palace — Thro' the
Casa Guidi, Ely. B. Brownings residence in Florence
— Then to the Ch. of S. Spirito — there we met Mr. +
Miss Beeson — then we walked through old streets
back to Ponte V — sent Jim home to his supper +
we went to the Uffizi — Piazza Signoria, looking
carefully at things of interest — then passed the
Badia + Bargello into the Corso Borgo d. Albizi
where we looked at the Palaces — then to the
Duomo — walked part way around that + through
it — then, at 6.40 cab home. Dinner —
Eve'g — Newspaper + writing diary —

## Right Page

### Sat. May 27, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day with light shower after 6
p.m. + a heavy one bet. 8 + 9 o'clk — Before 10
o'clk I walked down to the Ponte Vecchio into
my sketching traces + established myself at a
point about midway across the bridge for about
two hours I worked diligently upon a pencil sketch
looking north along the bridge — It is such a
thoroughfare that I was considerably troubled by
the curiosity of youngsters who would gather about
me — after 12, having got as much as I could do
comfortably (or uncomfortably) out of doors, I sat
to at Pension + after lunch I put in another
couple of hours upon my sketch — after 5 o'clk
I went out for a walk. I skirted a great part
of the city, thro' the Viale Principe Umberto past the
Fortezza da Basso, out upon the Via Lungo Mugnone
to the Ponte Rosso — Arco Monjée — Porta Fallo,
thro' via Sangallo + via Cavour to the Duomo +
thence home, getting in just before dinner at
7 — Eve'g there being no one in the salon
but E. + A — there being a very good light
there I finished my Ponte Vecchio sketch
