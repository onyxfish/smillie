## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 21, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Annie went to N.Y.*

A dull m'g, with a little rain before 9 o'clk. but the weather soon cleared & by noon it was a bright, mild & lovely day — cloudless moonlight up about 6 — Annie went to N.Y. by the 7.45 train to look after Jim.
Oakley & his team & Rutan put in an appearance & with Stone, Hill and Williams things were very lively in the lot quarrying out bottom stones for foundations for water tanks. I spent much time over seeing the operations — Before noon I went downtown — Cooley's men came up & examined defective tank in studio — they say that only a copper lined one will prove satisfactory — I worked for some time on mezzotint ground for W. Koehler — making my arm & shoulder ache like a tooth ache. About 5.30 I Rec'd from Annie a telegram — "Found Jim doing well — Piece extracted without operation — many wrote you to-day." — which is encouraging. Ralph & I had tea about 6.30 — I put him to bed at 8 & then had a very quiet evening all alone by myself.
