## Left Page

### Mon. May 8, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Weather overcast with threats of rain
a very few drops fell but at sunset
the sky was clear & the promise of good
weather had disappeared. I awak
about 4 o'clk A.M. & did not get to
sleep again — up before 7 feeling tired
out & having a bad headache — after
[illegible] — I lay down again & spent the
rest of the m'g on my bed. Lunch
at 1 o'clk. It was nearly 3 when A — Miss
7\. & the boys rode to Marien Crawfords
Villa while I walked nearly to the
Tops of Capo di monte — then back & thro
the Town getting to Villa Mason after
5 — Rested until 7 — then dinner —
Evef in Drawing Room — nobody there
but ourselves.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 9, 1893

*Capri — Hotel Quisissana — Rooms 27–28*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day — very mild & quiet sky, at times
slightly overcast — then beautifully clear — we left
Sorrento, from a gate at the foot of the cliff under the
gardens of the Villa Mason, in a small boat. At about
10.30 in that waited for the Naples & Capri steamer
that was seen coming — we soon boarded her and at
few minutes before 11 the steamer got away and
less than an hour we were opposite the Blue
Grotto of Capri where a fleet of small skiffs were
waiting to take us in — the work was done with great
alacrity — there may have been 40 passengers on
board — in less than half an hour they had all dis-
embarked — had seen the grotto & went back on the
steamer — there may have been 15 or even 20 skiffs
in the grotto at one time, to the great detriment
of course, of the effect — but in spite of it we found
it very interesting — beautiful — tho' not nearly so
blue as it is represented in pictures — it was not
until just before we went out that we could fully
see the roof & the delicate color our eyes had
been so striated by the glare of sunshine outside —
the steamer then returned to the Marina & landing
at Capri where we were taken to the pier in small
boats — we decided to go up to the town, a ride of
nearly half an hour — we got to the hotel a few min-
utes — I got our rooms & as soon as possible to our
lunch — after that we rested until 3 — then had 4
donkeys & we all rode up to the Rock of Tiberius — immensely interesting & impressive ruins of an
ancient palace — lovely views — terrific precipices —
74 feet down to the water — old light house —
the tarantella was danced for us by four — to me
ugly women — interesting but not exciting — then
at 5, we started on our return — diverging by a
path to a cliff from which we had a full view
of the great Faralioni Rocks on S. side of island —
got to hotel before 6 & rested until 6.30 — then
we had our dinner after which we went out
onto the Piazza (or Public Terrace) where we
had a lovely view over the sea to the island of
Ischia & the sunset sky — to Monte S. [illegible] & its
cliffs etc — interesting old buildings — I was
quite captivated & saw a lot of things that
I wanted to bag — Back before 8 — the boys
got to bed & I followed in an hour.
