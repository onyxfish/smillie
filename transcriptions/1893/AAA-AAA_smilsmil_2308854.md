## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 9, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Had a Cyclone headache all day. Have it had such an experience for years. During my fever experience at Naples last April I had some very severe pain in face & head but of an entirely different character. This seems to be a nervous cyclone — it cleared soon after sundown & I was able to get up & take a little nourishment & back to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 10, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another surprise — got up between 5 & 6 feeling very wretched. I took a little food, thinking that I was hungry. Then I had a great stomach ache that kept me curled up & drenched in perspiration for more than a couple of hours. Then a fever came on & at 1 o'clk. I stood at 103°. Dr. Gardiner came at 4 o'clk — & then I stood at 104⁴⁄₅°. He gave me Phenacetine — I don't know the amt. at 6 P.m. [illegible] had fallen to 102½° & at 10 o'clk. to 101° & I got some sleep thro' the night. This bonfire of a fever is surprising to me — more so than the unnatural headache that took possession of me yesterday.
