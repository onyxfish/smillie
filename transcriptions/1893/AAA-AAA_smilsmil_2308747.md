### Thur. Feb. 9, 1893

*Les Batailles de Fleurs.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - mild & quiet. I got up feeling very seedy altho' I passed a tolerably comfortable night. I worked on a view near the market upon which I had done a little out of door work, until 11, then I went out & sat in the warm sun shine until déjeuner time - then to hotel & immediately after our meal we all went to our places in Tribune "K" in the Promenade Anglaise - after sitting for nearly an hour much shut in, with only a view before us & trying of the allies's place I throwing flowers I left the family & went out into the crowd which in places, was a tremendous crush - in front of the beautiful Mediterranean club building the boustand was occupied by men & boys - a youngster offered me his seat for 4 sols & I promptly [illegible] him off. The seat was easily reached - about 7 or 8 feet above the sidewalk & a foot or more wide - an admirable seat giving an uninterrupted view over the crowds & procession up & down the ave. with the gay figures in front of me - in its assemblees. It was a very pretty & vivacious picture - moving crowds - passing cages & banners - gay equipages & hauled me could - bands of music & [illegible] gallets - but after all - it was very [illegible] to me - but a [illegible] clouds [illegible] that would have been pretty & entertaining if complete under favorable & necessary conditions - but as the finale of days of hard work & costly preparation - it was all together inadequate. ### Fri. Feb. 10, 1893

*Mentone Grand Hotel National Rooms 96, 99.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild, pleasant day - sudden, strong wind came up in the afternoon. I got up with severe pain which lasted for some time & left me in a wretched plight for the rest of the day - Bunnie went for Dr. Lyrus & he came before noon & gave me a careful examination & came to the conclusion, as he said, that my trouble was not more serious than a severe attack of nervous dyspepsia - several complications like brain & spinal trouble kidneys - etc. etc were looked after - on the whole he was encouraging - which was much needed for it seems to me the look out ahead is very dark - I did a little packing before déjeuner - I my [illegible] was to start for Mentone by train leaving at about 2.30 but I had not strength enough to get ready in that time - in fact, had it not been that our rooms must let, we must vacate before 4 o'clk. I do not think I could have got away today at all. As it was, in a forlorn way I tumbled things into my trunk & we left the house soon after 3 & left Nice on the 3.52 train getting to Mentone at 4.45°. The ride could hardly be called interesting — our drive from Monte Carlo had made us familiar with a much more beautiful aspect of the same country. We had many tunnels and rock walls on one side with sea & blazing sunshine on the other. Long way to [illegible] along, altho' it was 1st cl. Rapide - with our belongings we get into a voiture & drove at once to the Cosmopolitan to see W. & Mrs. Gifford - they got us a bus to follow. We saw them - no rooms — we waited ½ an hour or so - then started to find her - after several misses we called at his hotel at & were conducted to our rooms for the night. It seems to be 1st class, I enjoyed numerically as beautifully cooked under-loin steak. Our rooms are on the upper floor - mine - No 99 on S.E. corner with very fine outlook. To night there is a Carnival procession & ball - from my windows I saw fireworks & part of the very modest procession. I had a fine but it was not one of the warming kind owing to the backset five places unreasoned wood - but I managed to keep comfortable until 10 o'clk & then to bed in much better shape than I had affected it to be possible.
— I showed them my etching pts & they showed me some of their W.E. sketches - I thought Mr. B - the cleverest artist of the two.
