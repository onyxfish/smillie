## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 23, 1893

**Weather:** Superb day, cloudless, brilliant & bracing  **Temp:** [illegible]

after a very good nights rest up before 8 & went for our [illegible] to a place nearby marked Need. Me[?]
Grocers Excursionists State - It was fairly good for
half a dollar - for 10 [cents] each. Rode in a wagon to
64[?][cents]: entrance; it was 10.20 when I got [to] NY
etching club exhibit where I hoped to meet Mr.
[illegible] - he did not turn up - so A. & I worked very
faithfully over the exhibits in the gallery on 2nd. 3[rd]
until noon - Then I went up to the office for a few
minutes & saw Mr. Kurtz - in the gallery met Miss
Hallowell - after I A. & I went over to the NY. Bldg
where I introduced myself to Mr. Louis M. Howlund
Govt. manager, NY. State Exhibit & from him got a
pass to the Restaurant where we had a good
table d'hote lunch for 50 [cents] - Then we went into
the Mass. house & a number of other bldgs. there-
about & wandered down the lake front and
[sat?] up in front of the Spain bldg. wrote a big
P.C. card to Kate - Then we continued our peregrinations around to the Cal. House - then
back to nearest point on lagoon & took electric
launch to landing at 30. end of liberal arts Bldg.
It was now after 4 o'clk - the effects of sunlight & shadow over the buildings was simply
ravishing. It was enough just to wander
about & enjoy the dreamy architectural pictures so suggestive, in some ways, of Claudes and
Turners, after the sun went down & the moon
began to assert itself we commenced to look
for our supper - a difficult thing to find - every
place where eatables were served was thronged
to overflowing - at last we went to the Casino
& stood & waited until seats were vacated —
we were a good hour in getting a very light
supper - A. met an old school fellow, Mrs. Corley
with husband & two daughters. Then we walked
to the end of the long pier & had superb views of
moonlight over the lake - & the illuminated [illegible]
bldgs. & the fire works which, in connection
with everything else were simply gorgeous —
wonderful effects of bombs & rain-falls of brilliant
& gem-like color — Then to the basin where illuminations & aquatic sports were in progress —
about 8.30 we left the grounds & [found?] our
way into a trolley car & soon got safely home

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 24, 1893

**Weather:** A raw & disagreeable day with light drizzle of rain much of the time  **Temp:** [illegible]

I was routed out of bed about day break in answer to a
sudden call - asleep after that & did not get
up until about 8.30 - Young Brown brought up
his father & introduced him - Miss B. brought
in a very nice breakfast for us of coffee - bacon,
oat meal & bread & butter that, with fruit, we
ate in our bed rooms - About 10.30 A. went to
the Unity to see Sue & lunch with her - I
went to the Delaware, near by, & spent an
hour & a half with the Tweedys - then I got
to our room & I had a nap that lasted until
near 2 o'clk - when young W. Brown came to
bid me good bye as he was about leaving for
Cornell. - Then I went to the Delaware and
lunched with Mr. Chas. Benedict. It was
near 3 when I got. & found that Annie
had got back - I wrote quite a long letter
to Kate & after that I had a short nap - at
6.30 Annie & I went to the Delaware and
met Mary & Jenny T. just going in to have
some supper. Belle & Mr. B. were having
dinner - so we went in - The T's - A. & I, &
after a long delay had a satisfactory tea -
Belle & Mr. B. joining us later - after tea
we went into Public Parlor & chatted until
9.30 - then we went to our Room & very soon
to bed -
