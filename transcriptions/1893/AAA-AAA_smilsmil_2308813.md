## Left Page

### Wed. June 21, 1893

**Weather:** Bright  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m't, weather much cooler — strong
breeze in the afternoon — Thunderstorms & heavy
Rain about 4 p.m. — clearing before 6 — cloudy
& cool every. Up at 6.30 & wrote for an hour
before b'k'p' to Kate. After b'k'p' went with
E — A & the boys in a gondola to S. Giorgio
maggiore — didn't think much of any of the
Tintorettos & but admired the wood carving in
the stalls in the choir — Then we continued
our travels to the Redentore, another church
where we saw some more poor Tintorettos
& some paintings attributed to Bellini —
poor things, not worth going to see. Then
we crossed & steering a small canal
I was left at S. Trovaso, where I had al-
ready made a slight sketch — I now made
a more careful pen sketch, taking about
an hour, then I went to the Accademia
& there met Annie & Eliz — I spent an hour
in looking at a few of the paintings that
impress me most — Then to hotel — Lunch
at 1 — Resting from 2 to 3 — Then worked
on pen sketches of the map until 5 —
Then with A — went & made a call on
the Sisters Torrey — Miss Eliza, out, Miss
Margaret R'd us very pleasantly —
& back to hotel at 6. H I walked about
until 6.30 — then back — dinner —
after that for an hour on the Piazza
San Marco — back at 9.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 22, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Air still cooler this m't, with some clouds.
Up soon after 6 & again wrote for an hour or
a little to Kate — After b'k'p'. I went out with A —
to "do" some churches in the neighborhoods. On
the way to S. Giovanni in Bragora we met, on
the Riva, a funeral procession — lots of great cau-
dles in heavy metal candlesticks borne by the dirt-
est & coarsest of men in robes of white & black,
equally dirty — & drank & followed — coffee on
the shoulders of six dirty scarlet robed men —
mourners, chanting priests, lots more of cau-
dles, served apparently by street boys & the
following of a tag & football, such as might
follow a band of music — They crossed thro' the
narrow street & into the little church ahead
of us, but we were able to see the old paintings
which I cannot say that I found very interesting.
Then we went on to S. Giorgio degli Schiavoni —
a very little, two story church that, with its old
paintings & its quaint architecture was ex-
ceedingly interesting to us — Here Aubrey has
been sketching for some days & I don't wonder
at his choice of subject — then we went to S.
Giorgio dei Greci — there two we were interest-
ed in a very different style of architecture & old,
Byzantine paintings — Then back to hotel at 11 —
I went out with E & the boys. I worked in-
doors on Trovaso a subject that I commenced
yesterday m'p — a little mooning in S. Marco
— then lunch at 1 — again I worked indoors
& took the boys to the Lido — Before 5 I went
out — steamboat to Torna Landing & then for
about an hour & a half I wandered thro' the
streets & along the canals without definite aim
& got into the very old church S. Giacomo dall'
orio — very quaint — some views of the outside very
picturesque. I am persuaded that I have not
made any photos or views of it — Then north to
the steamboat landing & by boat back to
hotel & dinner — got very chilly in the boat
& did not recover from it all the evening
after trying to warm up by walking. I told
old himself in washing & lay down, only getting
up to go to bed — A & E — went off alone to the
Piazza & amused themselves with the music
& ices & canes — There was a band almost
under our windows, so I too had music
to the shivers.
