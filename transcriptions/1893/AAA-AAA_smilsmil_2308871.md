## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 13, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day, wind from 2. + N.E.
growing stronger until night, when
it blew a gale — Rain commenced
before 5 o'clk. & fell heavily in showers.
Muk & Ham got to work this m'g
moving the pile of stones & dirt, thrown
out by Hill in digging for foundation at
east end of barn — It was carried
to a hollow on the east lawn & spread
there, a darkie helped F. + Hill — the
job taking nearly all day — Henrick was
at work cleaning the decayed o'lle from
under the barn — Stone transplanted
a lot of Berberries, continuing hedge al-
ready started — a young apple tree grown
from a grafting bud — other jobs — I
spent a good part of the day out of doors
with my pruning shears — also in studio
taking preliminary steps to mount the
sketches I made abroad — the boards
having arr'd from Butler yesterday —.
Ev'g — newspapers — & listening
to the howling wind

The wind blew terrifically all night
& kept us from sleeping — I do not re-
member to have heard ever before
anything like it on land. —

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 14, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The wind continued spasmodically
violent all day & again at night —
Before noon, after some scurrying showers,
it was brilliantly clear, with wind
from the south. There were several
rapidly passing showers. I got up
feeling rather stale after my night's
experience — far worse that at night-
at sea in a storm. I got to work
in studio mounting some of the studies
& pen & ink that I made while abroad
& went down town before noon — Dinner
after 1 o'clk — & again worked on my
pasting work until near 5 — Nen again
down town — Very heavy wind, but
not cold — in ev'g Ketting had a
time on the piazza with Raskin —
Tea before 7 — Ev'g — newspaper
& to bed early.
