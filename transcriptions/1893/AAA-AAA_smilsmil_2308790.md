## Left Page

### Sat. May 6, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain & wind during the night — rain in the mts. until about 2 — Then it cleared but it was cold & breezy all day — This mng. we had set to go to Sorrento but, of course, were obliged to give it up — I spent a large part of the mng. in-doors finishing pen sketches. Had a short walk before lunch at 1 — about 3. A. Miss J., the boys & I walked to the Town of la-Scala & went into the church there & thence, by a foot path up the mt. to the little village of Pontu minuto from which place we had a fine view down the ravine to Atrani & a chance to study the geography of the country about Amalfi — The tramp back was rather cold & cheerless — It was 6 o'clk — before we got in — Thee supper with the boys at 6.30 & sat at the table until the regular dinner was over — Surely I made a rough pen sketch over a slight pencil sketch that I had made of the Cathedral tower.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 7, 1893

*Hotel de la Grande Bretagne — Villa May [illegible] — Sorrento — Rooms 13–14 —*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day — a perfect gem — Up at [illegible] — slept alone at 7 — I made a slight sketch after a photo — of fountains in the town with figures — Then I packed & settled bill after a little discussion — about 10 o'clk — we started down on our side to Amalfi — it was a succession of delights — left luggage at lower Cappuccini, Amalfi — ordered boat to meet us at 2 o'clk — telegraphed for landeau to meet us at Praiano — then to upper Cappuccini towers very possibly met by J. Voggi & sister, Donna Donjae — got there before 11.30 & loitered about until 12.30 when we had lunch — We had ample time & got down to our boat & away not before 2 — the breeze was strong & dead ahead but we got to Praiano in 1½ hours — found carriage there waiting for us — a band of tired young girls with folders came down the steps to meet us & bothered us until we got away — The road grew more & more wild — the cliffs more perpendicular — the vegetation & scenery as we went in — Positano seemed to be half ruined — it was in some ways very picturesque — the sun being behind the cliffs & the wind very strong & now became cold which lessened my comfort — It was not much better when we crossed the divide & got upon the sunny side of the slope that led down to Sorrento — The drive after we got down the hill, through long lanes with high walls on either hand, was very tedious to me as I was getting tired & impatient — at last we got to the old shortly before 6 and got rooms without trouble — Jim & I took a short walk to limber out — Dinner at 7 — Put Raepos to bed at 8 — at Ravello this morning he bruised his R. big toe by letting an iron seat fall upon it — dressed it with arnica & think no serious trouble will follow — at 8.30, with Miss J. went to Piazza to see illuminations — fireworks — festa of San Andrea — Too late for the fireworks —
