## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 17, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, windy day — very charming. I spent the m'f. out of doors, poking about — It was nearly 3 o'clk. when we got thro' dinner — then, for variety, I took a nap — after that I wrote a letter to Mr. Woodin in answer to some questions of his concerning cholera in Europe & the most desirable place there to spend the winter — Mr. Benedict & her daughter came and looked at my pen sketches. Then I had a brisk walk on piazza — Ev'f. I wrote out some directions for Hemick & in other ways made preparations for our start for Chicago to-morrow.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 18, 1893

*235 Delaware Ave. Buffalo.*
*Home of Hon. Carleton Sprague.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull rainy m'f. — continuing most of the day. The m'f. spent in getting ready for our departure — I went downtown & Amy returned packed my part of trunk. Bus drove us at 12 — train left at 12.15 got to Alford just in time to catch a milk train for Binghampton, but the delay in getting mileage ticket obliged us to wait for main train at 2 — got to B — before 3 — an immense crowd at station — Buffs. due at 3.08, on time — crowded — cars added — after a time we managed to get a couple of drawing-room seats — met Bella, Mary & Jenny Tweedy on the train & in spite of having seats in different cars we had a very good visit with them — After hard work & patient waiting we got something to eat just before 8 — Got to B— on time at 8.15 — almost the first person we met on alighting was Mary Sprague Cook. She had a carriage & we drove at once to her father's house where we had more supper & then much chatting until 10 o'clk when we went to bed.

Heavy wind & rain during the night.
