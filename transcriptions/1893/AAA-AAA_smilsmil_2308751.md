## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 17, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant until near 11 - Rain began - Soon
falling heavily with some thunder & lightning
fell over in less than two hours. Pleasant
afternoon - Mr. Gifford came in for a
few minutes before 10 o'clk - Then I went
out to the old Town & made an outline
sketch pencil - of one of the odd, tunneled
passage ways up the hill, under the houses.
Then it began to rain & Jim & I had to
hurry home. I worked with color on my
sketch until dejeuner at 12 - & again
after 1 until 3 - Cal. Lewis called and
sat with him three photo prints taken
from negatives made on our travels up to
Raquebrine. After 3 I went out alone
& had a long tramp up the valley of the
Carci - I found the road quite pleasant -
the mountain forms were picturesque &
some of the details - tree forms, buildings,
etc - were very good. I did not get
back until 5.30 & was pleased to find
that I had accomplished my walk
with less discomfort & sense of fatigue
than I have felt after any exercise for
more than three weeks past. Dinner
with the boys at 6.30 - Every some reading
& to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 18, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Changed our Rooms to 41. 42.*

A very lovely day - Immediately after
oblè [illegible] gave up our lovely suite & rooms
the people who engaged them sometime
before we took possession are to come this
afternoon - We retain 41 & take 42. a large
room beyond, communicating. A very good-
sized room where two beds - a front list of
furniture & our belongings are huddled
into it. Our salon has been a constant
comfort & delight to us & we give it up very
regretfully. I spent the nite on the
terrace working upon my pen & ink sketch
& seemed to pay for my industry with a mild
spasm of pain (stomach) at lunch time - At
2 o'clk - We all of us, including E - Rode over
to the Cosmopolitan to get the Giffords - We
found Mr. G - not feeling well enough to
take a Ride - So, A, E, & before Rode around
Cap Martini & Mr. G - Jim & I walked - we
went to the Boulevard & took Tram car to
its western terminus & from there we had
a very pleasant walk making the circuit
of the Promontory - We had many charming
views but, at the tops we had light sunshine
the tops of the hills back of the town were
cloud covered, by which we lost their fine
outlines - my legs seem to have recovered
their strength & I was back without trouble
but was much discomforted by flatulence in
the bowels &, at times, intense heart burn - My
condition puzzles me the pains & discomforts
varying constantly. Getting back to the bus,
we Rode back, Mr. G. leaving us at the Jardin
Public - we Rode on further to a photo store
where I spent some time making a selection
of photo views - It was just about 6, when we got
to the hotel - Dinner at 6.30 - [illegible] I worked for
a time on terrace pen sketch - listening to a mile
orchestra playing in the corridor beneath me.
