## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 3, 1893

*To Monte Carlo & back*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day — Up at 6.30 — Skp. at 8 & at 9
we all got away in a Landeau — The morning
air was crisp & pleasant — drove out at N. E.
side of the city & very soon commenced an
ascent — a 3d horse attached to our team & he
was not released until we had gone 5 or 6
miles — beyond the point on the mts where we
overlooked Villefranche. The ascent was con-
stant — the road excellent & the views delightful
the greatest elevation about 1800 feet — views on
the sea & wound to snow capped mts much
interested in Eza — an ancient village upon
the top of a rocky peak — Stopped at St. [?] ubbia
& took a squint at ruins of extremely old tower
& the view of Monte Carlo & Monaco at our feet —
Roccabrunie — another town perched upon rock
crags — Got to Monte Carlo at 1 — went at once
to Restaurant of Hotel de Paris telling our cocher
to meet us there at 3.30 — Got a good table and
had a good & expensive lunch — At about 2
we went out on terrace of Club House — a won-
derfully beautiful place. In this beautiful day
leaving the boys there, A. Slee. & I went in to the
gambling rooms — great crowds — great amount
of gold & silver — a sad & impressive contrast
to Coney out of doors — at one of the tables we met to
our great surprise, Mr. & Mrs. Jas. Gifford — they had
been to Nice to-day to call upon us but were on
their way back to Mentone. Stopping here for a
few minutes. Together we all wandered in the
gardens until 3.45 — then they went to their
train & we took our landeau & started on our
drive back to Nice by way of the sea shore road —
in its way almost as beautiful as the mountain
road — As the sun went down it grew a little
cool — later almost cold. We reached the
International at 6. having driven in 2½ hours —
bringing to a successful close a most en-
joyable day's outing. I think it benefited
me also! I was very tired —        Dinner
soon after we got in — Every body before a pleasant
fine reading guide books —

Carriage — 40 f. driver 5 f.  fees & beggars — 5 f.
lunch for the party 32 f. 50 c.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 4, 1893

*To Villefranche*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, cooler than yesterday & cloudy most
of the time — After 10 o'clk — I walked west from
town up on to a spur of a hill but did not find
anything interesting. Got back to the hotel
in time for dejeuner — After that, we all walked
to the R. R. station & took 2.35 train (a m. late)
getting to Villefranche in about 10 m — Then we
had a pleasant & picturesque little walk along
the cliff to the town & down to the boat landing.
Hired a boatman to take us to the "Hoch", one of the
largest vessels — we were escorted over much of it
but were denied entrance to the turrets or engine
room — about 600 men on board — swarming all over —
engaged at all sorts of domestic work, washing,
ironing — mending clothes — cutting hair — cooking —
getting in sides of beef & cutting them up, etc. etc.
There was much painting going on, that made
it rather difficult to get about some times & in
some places. In some respects I was disap-
pointed — The armor clad portions did not re-
veal themselves — I did not realize, as I had
expected, tremendous weight & power — There
was, owing to the number of men about, more
huddling of things than I was prepared to see.
On leaving the Hoch we rowed to the formidable
the Admiral's flag ship & then went over that
having as agreeable, polite & agreeable sailor
as our guide. We found much the same thing
on both vessels — 740 men on the 7 — Two immense
grim armed ships — After going ashore we walked
thro' a part of the town, climbing a steep sideline —
I saw much that was very picturesque — one place
in quaintest that we saw, with dark old arches,
stone steps etc had a sign "American Bar" —   We
took the 4.45 train back to Nice — Dinner at 6 —
nearly at 9 o'clk — fireworks on the Rive Massena
to celebrate the naval confratins of the work of
arching over the outlet of the River Paillon — a
great square & gardens is to be made over the
arches & river. The display of rockets & bombs
was very fine —
