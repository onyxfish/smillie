## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 5, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely weather again — mild, with luminous atmosphere. Bkf. about 9 — spent some time directing work out of doors — Then I finished a bit of an architectural sketch of proposed change of cornice finish on brick building now being put up on Public Ave. by Daw. Sayre. Then I went down town with it — did't see S — left it with Gile — Then I attended to a number of small matters — had my hair cut — Dinner at 1.30 — after that some time with Cooley planning for pipe to connect cisterns at he [torn] with the house — ordered 400 feet of lead pipe. I worked in studio on [pen] sketch of a bit in the town of Capri until near 6 — Then down town — had a further conference with Cooley concerning getting water into the house. Tea at about 7 —
Evef. with newspaper — about 2 o'clk. Rec'd. telegram from Annie saying — "expect me Friday evef"

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 6, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Annie ret? — to Montrose.*

Dull day. Threatening rain, a little dull after 6 p.m. — by 10 o'clk. it came on steadily & quietly — I worked in studio for awhile on some of my pen & ink sketches made abroad — went down town before noon — saw D. Sayre — he appro-ves my sketch for cornice of his building. Dinner at 1.30 — after that, as I did not sleep well last night, I took a nap — again at pen work in studio as long as I could see — Down town again at 6 — Tea at 6.30 — at 8 went to R.R. station & met Annie as she ret'd. from Mf. — Surely, we talked over her experiences while away — She is most unhappy over the loss of her watch.
