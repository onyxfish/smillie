## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 24, 1893

**Weather:** Brilliant mt.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant mt. clouding by noon &
with a spattering of rain — pleasant
again later in the afternoon. I went
out for a walk in the mt. with Ralph
but were hurried home by the rain
& the threatening weather — In the
afternoon I again went out — to 59th
St., around by 8th Ave. entrance to
the Park & for the just time, in the twilight, I saw the Italian statue of Columbus — on my way home stopped at
Yale's & consulted with him for awhile —
Home to tea late — Swetf. Charlie
came in — Stockings & chairs to
receive Treasures from Santa Claus
were arranged in the parlor & we
got to bed late.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 25, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful & very mild — Before bkfst. went
to butcher's & grocer's — after bkfs. a great
time, all hands opening presents. Before 11 I went to the Central Park and
had a walk over on the West side of it —
Home before 1 — lunch — Every body
at work converting the house into a Music
Hall & lunch Counter. From a little after
2 until 4.30 I carved cold turkey —
then being very tired I retired to the
attic & had a nap & rest — Up about 5 &
got down in time to see Annie's company
of juveniles go through a little performance
that she & Alice Ireland have arranged
for them — A presentation of the months &
little Jack Cook leading off as 1894 — a
medley of Tableaux vivants, Pantomimes,
Recitation, Rounds & carols. It was over
in a short hour — & at 6.30 all went down
to the library & dining room where there
were seats for 39. Edw. & Mary Cook & all of their
children — Walter & Louise & all of theirs — Mr.
& Mrs. Sprague & Miss Williams — Alec & Emily —
Mr. Valentine — Jos. & Nellie & 3 boys & Charlie —
Mary & her two girls, Kate — Fred & Alice J. —
Sumba, Mary & Grace J. — the children at
the large table in dining room — the others
at small tables in the library — Everything seemed to go off very nicely & Annie
was much complimented up on the
success of her entertainment.
