## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 23, 1893

*San Remo — Hotel Victoria — Rooms 28–29*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, beautiful day — Spent the m[orning]
in-doors, packing — Mr Gifford came and
made a good bye call — Annie went
out & attended to some small matters
in the town — Lunch at 12 — All bills &
fees paid — Soon after lunch or at 1.15, we
got away with all our baggage & drove
to the Mentone Garavan Station where
we waited for the 1.40 train — we soon
crossed the border & got to Ventimiglia at
1.53 & that being the Italian Custom Ho-
use we stopped — The first thing we did
was to set our watches on 45 m. to Roman
time. Then we submitted to the Custom
search — a very mild form of annoyance —
only one trunk was opened — we then wait-
ed until 3.45 Roman time & then resumed
the journey getting to San Remo about
4.15. There was nothing of special interest
to be seen by the way — many tunnels —
wide sea on one hand — at Bordaghiera
a great number of Palms — on getting
to San Remo we that our hand luggage
got into a cab & drove to a number of the
west end hotels without finding such
Rooms on such terms as we wished; then
we drove away to the east end, to Victoria
Hotel & were not well pleased there, but
as it was after 5 — getting cool & both had
had several threatenings, we decided
to stay over the night at least — Soon had a
fire lighted & were comfortable — at 6.30
we dined together a la carte — Even in
the moonlight was very bright I went out
for a walk — air quite chilly — I found nothing
interesting to me & soon returned.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 24, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day — raining heavily all the morn-
ing — rain in the afternoon but pouring down
again at night. There was nothing to do but
spend the m[orning] indoors as we had not our
trunks with us thus change of clothes or shoes.
I wrote a long letter to Charl — Annie sent a
telegram to Boardighem & rec'd answer that nul.
D. Tiffany & Florence Tuesday were at the Hotel
Bellavist[a]! Then A. wrote a note saying that we
would call on them on Sunday — Lunch at 12.30
In the afternoon, as it did not rain, I went
off with the boys & had quite a long walk to the
west end of town — The R.R. runs along the shore
between it & the Promenade, much to the detri-
ment of the appearance of the place. — There
are a number of large hotels but on this first
week & under cloudy skies it (San Remo) did
not seem to be so gay or so gaily picturesque
as many of the other shore places. Annie
[illegible] went off on a shopping expedition for shoes —
she has left off hotel & gone to a less expensive
Pension — At 6.30 A — the boys & I took our even[ing]
meal in the Restaurant — Eveg I finished
my long letter to Charl.
