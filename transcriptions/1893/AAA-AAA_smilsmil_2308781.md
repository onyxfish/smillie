## Left Page

### Tues. April 18, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day with a bit of a thunder shower over Vesuvius in the afternoon, some of the rain reaching us. Late p.m. clear & very quiet. To-day I took a new departure — no more observations of pulse or temperature — I got up at about 10 o'clk. & dressed myself. While the sunshine came in at the windows I kept them open & at times I stood in the delightful warmth & freshness of out-of-doors. At 1 o had a mutton chops lunch. During the afternoon I rested for some time on the bed & was otherwise very careful of myself. Had my dinner at 6.30 & got to bed an hour later. Annie read aloud from Don Orsino until 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 19, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & pleasant. Took my [illegible] in bed but after that got up & dressed myself while the boys were out walking with the woman. Annie & I had a ride of an hour, from 11 to 12 — down the Corso to the quai & along the Public Gardens. The air was delightful but a little too fresh at times for the very sensitive mucous membranes of my head & throat. After lunch, bet. 2 & 3 o'clk. I walked to the hospital & saw Dr. Scotti who told me that he thought it safe for me to go to Amalfi on Friday. I find myself exceedingly weak & unsteady in the head as well as the legs. Rested in the afternoon. Dinner at 6.30. Eve'g. A — finished reading aloud Don Orsino — It was a disappointment — I don't think the story told to explain the mysteries of the lives of Speka & Maria comes equal to the requirements. It is not progressive in its power — it is a weak climax lacking reasonableness.
