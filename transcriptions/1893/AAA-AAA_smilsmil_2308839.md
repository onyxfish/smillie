## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 10, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

another very dry, warm day — veiled sky part of the time & pleasant westerly breeze — Got to Museum at 10½ & right to work, Mr. Cust gave me a good deal of his time this morning & rendered me very great assistance — I could make very little progress without him. It was 1.30 when I got my lunch & then a short walk outside of the museum. About 3 o'clk Annie came & for an hour continued her de Prince Translation — then we went out together & after some shopping she bot. a Traveling bag — then she went home & I went to an Optician's in New Bond St. about a magnifying glass for my work — & then I walked home — sent suit of clothes to Tailor for cleaning & repairing. Dinner after 7 — Annie gave us a Horton Ice Cream Treat. Evey wrote a note to Sidney James, maker of plate-rockers — mezzatint — wrote out some aquatint notes & read N.Y. Tribune.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 11, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still warm & sultry — It was 10.30 when I got to the Museum determined, if possible, to make this my last day over aquatints. But there were a number of others requiring the attention of the attendants so, things moved slowly & 4 o'clk came when I was engaged upon Wm. Danielle, the last man. Then, on top of a bus I rode to Uxbridge Road & walked to Mr. J. Goulding's — had a very pleasant interview with him — he is going to have put up for me some Scotch paper & three kinds of choice black to take home with me to experiment with — then I walked home getting in after 6 & feeling, owing to the weather, pretty well used up. Evey, a very short one — divided between my notes & the newspaper.
