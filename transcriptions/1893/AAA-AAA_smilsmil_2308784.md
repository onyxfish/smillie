### Mon. April 24, 1893

*Amalfi. Albergo dei Cappuccini. Rooms 22, 23.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day, without cloud — warm with a
gentle breeze. Up early & soon completed our
packing — had a hearty sleep — at 8 — at 8.30 had
nothing to do but wait. Left hotel soon after 9
& had alone time at R.R. Station to wait — Got
away at last at about 10.30 — The ride by train was
very interesting from both sides — on the one hand
Vesuvius constantly changing in form as we went
around his base — on the other, the bay & mountains
unfolding as we went N. — "A companion had for a
[illegible] companion a Rock Island, N. Kansas man
with a Kodak — could speak no language but
his own — must much of that — hard of hearing and
apparently confident & illiterate — As a type
we got to La Cava at 12.30 & drove at once to the
Hotel & [illegible] where we had an excellent
lunch. Hired [illegible] — 14 lire — at 2.30
started on our drive for Amalfi. I should note
that in the dining room hung a large crayon
portrait of Wm. Cullen Bryant — who was here —
it was a [illegible] at request of the proprietor — and
sent a small photo portrait from which
this enlarged one was made. We drove
directly to Vietri on the coast of the sea — The
road followed the shore very closely — The
air was delicious & the outlook was very
comfortable — for the first hour or more I en-
joyed it immensely. Then I became tired and
oppressed by the heat & glare of the sunshine —
It was nearly 5 when we got to this hotel — we
were hardly pleased when we found that we
had to climb an endless lot of steps & inclined
planes from the road to the hotel which is other-
wise inaccessible — Our Rooms are exceedingly
pleasant, each one having a loggia facing S. &
where we can sit & sun ourselves until noon —
I was so completely exhausted that I could
hardly keep up. I had a good steak dinner
with the boys at 6.30 — A nnis — it had ed hote
at 7 — I got to bed before 9.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 25, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A marvely day with cloudless, dreamy blue
sky. I awoke very early after a restless & un-
refreshing night. but it was 9 o'clk. before we
got our bkft. After that I had a talk with the
proprietor of the hotel as to terms — his house
being running over full, he is very stiff in his
prices — £.50 per day. Then we went out on the
garden terrace taking it in very lazy way —
looking at the many inviting way side seats
sheltered as we climbed the steep little paths
on the terrace into the woods above — At length
I got to a stone thatch shelter at some height
above the hotel — The outlook was ravishing, the
color, the air, the sounds —
I sat there dreaming
for nearly an hour — it was almost as good
as hours sitting in a hammock on a Mon-
trose piazza — Then I went alone some other
terraces until I got one of the loveliest of views
away to the east — The nets were swimming —
without any substantial bases — the sea was all
that enthusiastic & poetic people have written
about it — a middle ground on the left was
cliff & clustering buildings — beach, boats and
all came away down to be lost behind a fore-
ground group of olive trees — I almost sighed
myself away in delight — lunch after 1 o'clk.
The proprietor plans no cheaper rooms — 40
lire — but he had appealed us & we declined to
continue — Then I wandered about the place
into the cloisters, chapel & other places, finding
a number of bits which I am anxious to get
into my portfolio — Then I lay down for a rest
& got no sleep — The boys went down to the beach
to play — were soon set upon by three other boys who
demanded money — they pressed so hard that
he got out his jack knife & threatened them —
they fell back & a few volleys of stones were ex-
changed & then [illegible] — had an orderly retreat —
Dinner with the boys — & at tables d'hote — Then a
saunter by moonlight on garden terrace —
everything was press-ingly lovely — A's birthday lies
a week ahead — our "b" was [illegible] anniversary a
week beyond that — plenty of honey — plenty of moon-
with deep serenity we enjoyed its sober, affective, cue.
Later A — commenced Crawford's "Sant' Ilario" after
I had gone to bed & kindly reading me to sleep.
