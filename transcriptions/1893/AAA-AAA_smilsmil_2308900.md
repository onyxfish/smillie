## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 12, 1893

**Weather:** Very pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — on my way to school with Ralph I stopped at Char's to see him — too late — after returning to the house I went down to the N.A. & saw Farrington about Academy fire Mrs. Them in to Ass'd. Artists to see about some curtain samples that had gone astray — there to Wallb's. & saw Char who sent for — Smith of "The Royal" with whom I arranged to insure the N.A. — Then to 9th St. & to Hilton & Hughes & found the curtain samples astray from the Associated Artists $23.8 — Then to N.A. & then home to lunch — after that (by the way, Emma D — lunched with us) I spent some time clearing up in the front room where I work, as Annie is going to have a lot of young folks here to do some doll dressing — Then I retreated. & had a nap — at 6.30 we had high tea — Elis. & Talley Oakley Cook — Catharine & Elva. & Wray Cook — Bishop & — Babcock. After tea they had a little dance — at 8 I went to Edw. Cook's for Jim & Ralph — Mr. & Mrs. H. Babcock called for their daughter soon after 8 — Mr. B. took her home while Mr. B. visited with Annie — I took Eugene Silvator (whose name should have been within the items above) to her school, 55 W. 47th St. on my return Mr. & Mrs. B. at the house until 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 13, 1893

**Weather:** A very cold & windy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very cold & windy day — When I got through with my out-of-door chores I got to work upon a new Water Color drawing — a bit in the Town of Them — went for R- at 12.15 — lunch — & then worked as long as I could see — then down to the N.A. of D — & spent some time with Farrington, looking over Academy affairs preparatory to the Quarterly Meeting this eve'g. A run home & dinner — Then to Academy — Quorum present — Two items of business — a statement concerning the pecuniary failure of the Loan Exhib. (my first appearance before the Academicians as Treas.) The second item concerned the hiring or engagement by the Academy of an N.A. as Salesman. To my surprise (& gratification) a resolution was passed with very little opposition declaring that in the future no N.A. should be hired by the Academy to perform any of its artistic duties — from my point of view the self respect of the N.A.'s should have made such a resolution unnecessary. Home at 11 o'clk.
