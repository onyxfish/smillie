## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 27, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another charming day — weather still growing warmer — It was 9.30 before we finished our bfst —
we went out to Cottage Grove Ave — up to returned
to Midway Pleasance down which we strolled
stopping as our curiosity directed at one place
or another. We went first into Sitting Bull's Camp
& saw "Rain in the Face", the Indian who killed General
Custer — a large, pleasant faced, slow moving fellow
who was laboriously writing his autograph and
shaking hands with possibly hundreds of women —
Then we went into the old Cairo street & were very
much amused — there was no end of fun over the
riders on the camels who went thro' great trials
on the rising up & settling down of the beasts — We
spent some time in the Javanese Settlement &
among the South Sea Islanders who rubbed sticks
of wood & made fire pres. It was after 11 when we
got into the fair proper & then we went direct
to the Woman's Bldg — saw the mural decorations
there — also the library & then to the Children's
Bldg & saw the many babies left on check — Then
to the Swedish Restaurant & had a passable meal
served in very dirty fashion — Then at 1.30 we
went to the Art Bldg & commenced our work
there very deliberately, going first over the
sculpture there — then to the English section of oil
& water color paintings & through that — then to
the Japanese — then a portion of the German
— three, both being very tired — we determined
at 4.30, to give up for the day & go home and
rest. We got meals soon after 5 & had read ourselves upon our bed & I had our noons nap —
Before 7 we went to the Chawares House and
had supper — & back to our rooms by 8 o'clk.
I wrote a short letter to Clare & some reading
& to bed in good season.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 28, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant weather still continues — much cooler
today — rec'd letter from Kate this mfg while at our
bfast — A & I wrote notes, relating us somewhat, so
that it was again almost 10 when we got to the fair
— we went at once to the Art Bldg — & worked there
very faithfully until about 1 o'clk — Then we went to
the Restaurant on the woman's side — had sub-
stantial lunch — getting out about 2 — walked down to the
Liberal Arts Bldg & made a tour of the instruments
to study the mural paintings, of which I like
those by Mac Ewen the best — & those by Weir, the
least — Then we went to the Music Hall at 3
o'clk & heard a popular song concert by Agnes
Munson, Soprano & [illegible], Monroe, Baritone, with
Miss Kate Richards as accompanist — The voices
seemed to be good but the resonance of the hall
was so great as to injure the musical effect very
much — out at 4 — walked back to Woman's
Bldg to get A's shawl & then on to Art Building
where again we worked faithfully until 6 o'clk
when we were turned out — glad to be allowed
in at 7 — went across to New York State House
— people being turned out there but my seat
to the Restaurant carried me in — I was for
dinner in the large hall — but I met [illegible]'s
daughter, Mrs. [illegible], & sat talking with her until
I lost my chance for a seat — then to my great
disgust A & I were obliged to go up to the dining
table our dinner in the old place — out at
8. went to Art Bldg & finished it — out
a little after 9 — walked to 4th St. entrance
home by trailer car — packed trunk and
then to bed — glorious moonlight night.
