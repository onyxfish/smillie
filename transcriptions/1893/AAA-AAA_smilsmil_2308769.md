## Left Page

### Sat. March 25, 1893

*Naples. Parker's Hotel. Rooms 15-16.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - up at 6 feeling very
much better - Spent the mf packing
and finishing up all preparations for our
journey - [illegible] - spent part of the mf with
us. We had a good lunch at 11.45 and
left the hotel at 12.15 - our train left on time
at 1.15 - & strange to say, am on time at Naples
at 6.35 - for most of the distance we shared
our comp. with an Englishman & wife - we
were interested, as we crossed the Campagna, in aqueduct remains - the distant views
of St. Peters - & further on, in Palestrina -
the entered a very mountainous region & were
in close valleys most of the way. We had some
fine views of snow tops - also of very picturesque old
towns - altogether the ride was interesting & the
the season is not yet enough advanced to present
its most agreeable aspect & for picturesque
light, we were on the wrong side of the train.
We had a brief view of Vesuvius about an hour
before we reached Naples. Driving by bus
directly from the station it took us nearly
an hour to get to the hotel which is at the
other end of an extremely crooked town with
a number of intervening hills. We got our
dinner as soon as we could but there was
much confusion as the hotel is crowded
& the management seems to be rather
loose. It was almost 9 when we sat
thro' & then the boys were put to bed and
before long we followed them.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 26, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant & warm mf - a shower
of some duration in the afternoon - continuing
cloudy after that. It was about 9 o'ck before
we got down to our blfo. It was after 10 when
we all went out rather aimlessly - met Mr.
Robt Hoyt, his son Rob. & Miss H - they too are at
Parkers - we took cab & rode to Cathedral - a
long drive - streets interesting - picturesque - very
badly paved - dirty - poor looking people - stable
horses & vehicles - swarms - characteristics strongly
marked - a short visit to Cathedral - uninteresting.
Then rode back by way of Caffe - too long and
not a drive for me, so, before reaching hotel,
I got out, with Jim, & walked - found my map
very puzzling - streets cross each other at different angles making it very difficult to turn a
corner that looks perfectly plain on the map.
again met the Hoyts - Young H. walked to hotel
with us. Lunch at 1. a good deal of blundering & attention - I sighed for the cleanliness
of the Russie. As the afternoon was too wet to
go out I had Jim in bed & room & finished letter to
[illegible] after which, my wife having returned
upon me again, I lay down until dinner at
6.30 - more blundering & misunderstandings
of waiters etc - met Miss Glassner in the
corridor - just returned from a trip to the top
of Vesuvius - very cold & wet - our shower came
to them, on the summit, a snowstorm - I sat
in Reading Room for a while & looked over news
papers - then to our room & wrote.
