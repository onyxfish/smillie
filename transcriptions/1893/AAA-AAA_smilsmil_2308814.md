## Left Page

### Fri. June 23, 1893

**Weather:** Clouded, close & muggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded, close & muggy, in the afternoon a heavy shower of rain — I spent the m't indoors — finished my letter to Kate and worked on my pen sketches — Lunch at 1 — after that a rest until 3 — Then worked on figures in Rialto sketch until 4.30 Men went out with Annie for a prova — we went first into S. M. Formosa & had another look at the very fine S. Barbara by Palma Vecchio — then out to S. Giovanni & Paolo & took another look about there — then to S. M. dei Miracoli & there was much interested in noting the beauty that age adds to variegated marbles. The church has been restored & some of the ancient incrustation remains — compared with it the new is cold, raw & harsh — these fine marbles we were told are brought from Africa, Greece, Verona, etc — Then we walked to the Rialto & across to the market — after that Annie returned by steamboat — S. walked & got to the Riva just as she did — Dinner — after that I went to the Piagga S. Marco & bought a few photos, later I rel[ied] to the Piazza with Annie & Elly & bought some Adriatic glass — then we had ice-cream & beer & then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 24, 1893

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast m't with a light drizzle of rain at about 9 o'clk — but before it cleared charmingly — then I went to the Rialto & completed my sketch there — Annie, Elly & the boys came to me at about 12 — at 12.30 I sent my sketching things back with them in gondola & I walked lunch soon after 1 — from 2 to 4 I worked on my own't sketch finishing up details — Then also a thunderstorm seemed to be imminent we all went out in a gondola to explore the a number of the small canals — it was not much of a success — our gondolier was a disagreeable fellow & we didn't get along very well in our search for the picturesque — we returned in a couple of hours — Dinner at 6.30 — Every lovely, clear moonlight, air quite cool & pleasant breeze blowing — Soon after 9 — A. & I went out in a gondola on the Grand Canal — there was music in the air — it was everywhere — in fact, a perfect pandemonium — three brass bands on the Riva — two boat companies of singers on the canals & lots of independents — gondoliers in boats and others all within hearing as we floated along — gondola opposite S. Marco — it was absurd and not altogether pleasant — but the moonlight was good & with a choice selection of music by way of accompaniment, it would have been very delightful — we returned at 10 o'clk — some time in balcony — Swarms of people on the Riva.
