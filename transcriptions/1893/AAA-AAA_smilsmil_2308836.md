## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 4, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy m'f. with bright sunshine before noon — heavy thundershowers until almost 5 & then a beautiful ev'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Was belated in getting to the museum in the m'f, stopped in Hyde Park to see a drill parade of the Coldstream Guards — Worked over dePrince until 1.30 — then lunch & a little rest looking at drawings by Masters — Then worked over later French aqua-tinters until 4 — then to the Reading Room & got a life of Paul Sandby that gives his account of his aqua-tint process — Home soon after 6 — Rest until dinner at 7 — Ev'f finished letter to Kate — Annie also wrote to her.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 5, 1893

**Weather:** cloudy — a little sunshine & only one shower that fell heavily for a few minutes over Kensington Gardens about 4 o'clk — giving Annie & the boys a wetting, they being on the Serpentine in a boat — The shower did not extend to the museum.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I walked to the museum in the m'f — Annie rode & got there ahead of me — she spent the m'f. translating and writing dePrince's document describing his process of aqua-tint — I was still engaged looking up French & German workers — &. Returned home at 12¹⁵. Lunched in the museum at 1 & then took a short walk outside — At 4 when I had to leave the Print Room I went down to the Reading Room & got a life of Paul Sandby & from it copied his formulas for aquatint working — Home at 6 — Rested until dinner at 7 — Ev'f A. Read alond an amusing short story by Rudyard Kipling.
