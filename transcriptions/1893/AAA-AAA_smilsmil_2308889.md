## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 20, 1893

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — went with R. to school & attended to marketing & then got to work on my pen sketches & worked diligently until 12.30 — then I went for R — on my return, Alfred Jones waiting for me — lunched with us — just as we were about to start out Jul. Durand came in & we were detained awhile by his business concerning the work he has under way — about 2.30 the three of us went down town together, Jones & I getting off at 23° S. & going to 2° Nat. Bank & Safe Dep. Co. where formal transfer was made from Jones to me — we went over contents of box in Safe Dep. Co — Then to N.A. where [illegible] gave us combination of safe and I went over the papers in the safe, with him — It was near 5 when we got through — Home — Dinner at 6.30 — at 7.45 at N.A. — Council meeting — which lasted until after 10 — the only important business was the appointment of A. C. Howland to be Academy Salesman at the fall [illegible]. I spoke & voted against it. About 11 Robbins, Geo. & I walked up town together.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 21, 1893

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant [illegible] — then overcast and at night, steady rain — With R. to school. Then to my work on pen sketches until 1.30 — After lunch went with Annie looking for hangings & furniture coverings — to Faulkener's — Arnold's & C — Vantine's — Jap. Trading Co — all the afternoon & very little accomplished. Home at 5.30 — just as it began to rain — men at work putting up the heavy curtains — Dinner at 6.30 — Evey "doing things" — late when we got to bed.
