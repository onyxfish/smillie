## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 19, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day but more or less cloudy —
my old enemy that liked me again
before I got up in the night & made me quite
uncomfortable for a time — BkFt — soon after
I — It was about 11 when A — the boys and
I started out for a walk — going up, back
of the Hospital to the Boulevard Panorama
climbing high above us along the steep hill
face to Gara & & the Italian Frontier — much
of the way was through Olive & Lemon groves
— we had charming views over the sea and
were delighted by groups of birds & beauty —
flowers, cultivated & wild — we went far
far as the frontier point & Jones & returned
by the lower road — along the water front
getting to the hotel just as the dejeuner
gone was sounding at 1 o'clk. I had walked
a little too far & again pain came upon
me making me unhappy for some time —
I gave myself up to it after lunch and
slept for some time — after that I set
myself to writing as I am now very much
behind hand — Today I got a long &
very interesting letter from George.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 20, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudy day with threats of rain — after
BkFt. I worked in doors for a little while for
a sketch, then walked into town with A —
& the boys. I went to the Banque Populaire —
drew some money & got it in Italian paper
with a discount of 3% — then with A. to a
photo-stone but was so much used up by a
very severe spasm of pain in my stomach &
bowels that we had to get a cab & return to
hotel where I curled myself up on a lounge
& did not stir for three or more hours — After
she had had lunch A — went to Dr. Smiley
Kendall's & asked him to call to see me —
about 5 I had recovered sufficiently to
get up & eat a little crème de all — It was
barely in the evening before Dr. Kendall put
in an appearance — a brusque, business-
like man who did very little talking but
seemed to know what he was about —
He had me strip & stretched on my bed
then he manipulated [excruciated] (whew!)
the region of my stomach & bowels — after
a number of questions he said that he found
no evidences of organic trouble — he thought
that I was much out of tone & that the spasms
of pain were of a neuralgic-Rheumatic char-
acter — which is poor comfort for me as it
has been a pet dread of mine that my old
age might be miserably worn out by the agonies
of neuralgia to the distress of every one near
me — He gave me two prescriptions —
Brom-arsenic & dactopapine for tonic and
salipsyrin — a compound of salyclic acid and
antispyrin — in case of return of spasms of
pain. I read until after 10 feeling very,
very slim —
