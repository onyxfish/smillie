## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 3, 1893

**Weather:** Very fine day, clear sky, smooth sea pleasantly fresh air  **Temp:** [illegible]

The band waked us with the usual Sunday m[orning] chorals — the day that followed was a long one feather tedious loafing — the time dragged heavily — I am obliged to confess that, altogether, this has been the least satisfactory of my half dozen transatlantic voyages. Probably owing to the fact that I am under the influence of a very catarrhal condition of stomach & bowels — without being sick I have no appetite — While at dinner near 7 o'clk — Pilot boat 22 put a pilot aboard of us & we felt that at last we were at home. Well I loitered about, spending sometime chatting with Mr. Dooster & then Mr. DeLaRoche [illegible — ~3 lines]

Run at noon 405 m.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 4, 1893

*(header note: Home again —)*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up before 6 & had my bath, then back to bed — had a nap before I got up at 7.30 — Lovely day — cloudless, breezy & warm — Sighted land soon after 8 bfp — & after that, kept the long Island shore in view — got our cabin packing done & the fees paid, amounting in all to more than $16 — Lunch at 12 — Passed Sandy Hook at about 1 o'clk — and in 15 or 20 m. dropped anchor at Quarantine — a Red Star steamer was just ahead of us and kept us waiting so that it was fully two hours before we got under way again & an hour more before we reached the Hoboken dock. In soon after 4 o'clk — on the way up the bay our steward handed us letters from Kate & Mary Sprague Cook — where we at to where we could see the crowd on the dock we recognized among them Charé & Eliz. Horne — It was about 4.30 when we got ashore — Charé had a carriage in waiting in which Annie, Eliz. & the bags were sent at once up home but it was more than an hour before I could get my trunks & 5 small pkfs. together for the Custom Ho. officer — He proved anxious to please & put me through in a prompt & satisfactory manner — Charé had a wagon on the dock to take our trunks & when at last they were got away, we crossed the ferry — took Chris's cars (where we met Mason Bey & wife) & then found our cars & at home just about 6 — Mary Moony was there & shortly afterwards we were joined by Hattie & Katie — Then we had some tea and got refreshed — Later in the even Charé came in. gelt Cook is off on a short vacation — the other members of our two families are out of town — I did some note adding but got to bed very tired at about 10 o'clk, sharing a bed with Ralph in the attic.
