## Left Page

### Fri. March 3, 1893

*Hotel de Russie at [Les] Isles Britannique. Room no. 50.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day. Bkfst at 8.30 — after that I went to R.R. Station & arranged to have our trunks there sent at once to the Hotel de Russie — then back, got our small traps down — into a cab & drove to our new hotel. Found our Room to be a very large one in the corner of the fourth floor with three windows, a fire place & lots of room even after our double bed, two small beds & our four trunks were all in place. Being so high above the street we have a fine outlook over the Plaza del Popolo with lots of sunshine & air. In front of us we have the dome & portico of the church S. Maria in monte Santo, but they do not rise high enough to interfere with us. After getting our things into place we went off & climbed to the Terraca on the Pincian Hill that rises immediately behind our Hotel. There we suntered & suited ourselves until 12.30 — then to lunch. After that the whole party went to the Piazza do Spagna & A. hired her Italian in bargaining with the letter there & finally hired at two horse vehicle & away we went for the Villa Pamphilia. The view of Rome & the Mts beyond from the terrace in front of the church S. Pietro in Montorio was a wonder of color — our ride thro' the Villa grounds was a joy — the avenues of live oaks (?) near the Villa, churches & monastery of S. Onofrio & went in & saw the tomb of Tasso & on his way (?) passed what remains of the oak under which he used to sit — we got back to his city passing in front of S. Peters & to our hotel about 5.30. I took dinner with the boys at 6.30 — West — about 9 — A., J. & I took cab & drove to the Colosseum. Superb moonlight — we went in & wandered about — in black shadows & cold, white moonlight — under the arches & out in the arena — it was exceedingly impressive — I think I saw Christian martyrs — Gladiators — wild beasts & hero, beast of beasts — we got back to hotel soon after 10 — & then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 4, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold, clear, windy day — March is march the world over — Bkfst before 9 — sometime spent in getting our things out of our trunks, then A. went off her way & I took J. bus & Rose to S. Peter's & there spent what remained of the morning in quietly strolling about, most of the time inside of the building. It is less impressive as seen from the space immediately in front owing to being so near that the dome is lost to view. I was surprised to find the inside looking as new — as bright in ornament & detail & as exact in line as though completed but yesterday. For that reason the interior is somewhat disappointing — but of course I could not but be profoundly impressed by its mistress & majesty. I returned to the Hotel in time for lunch at 1 — it was after 3 when Jim & I started out for the Colosseum. We spent some time looking about the immense place & at the excavations not far distant, but the cold & the wind made it unpleasant & we [got] to the Hotel at 5 — A. & J. dined at table d'hote at 7 but I took a simple dinner with the children at 6.30 — Eve'g. I did a little sketching — a little reading & a little writing before going to bed at 11 —
Rec'd a long letter from S. R. Koehler.
