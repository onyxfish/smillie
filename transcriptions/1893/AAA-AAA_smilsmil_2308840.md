## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 12, 1893

**Weather:** Dull m[illegible], but still muggy & close  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfs'- Annie & I went by under ground R.R. to Bishopsgate S[t]- from thence she went on to the People's Palace - Mile End Road - I went to the Am. Consul's - St. Helen's Place, & spent an hour or more there trying to get a certificate that would pass my studies thro' the U.S. Custom Ho without trouble & was provoked beyond measure to find that they had no form suitable to the case of an Am. artist returning home with his studies - they insisted upon describing me as an importer. It was some time before, by erasures & additions I could get the documents into such shape that I was willing to sign them & then I had to pay $6.50 fees - Then I went to Morgan & Co's - drew $75 - saw Mr. Gordon & bid him goodbye - then, by bus to the museum, got lunch there & spent the rest of the time in studying the aquatint works of the late Wm. Daniell, R.A. - at 4 o'clk - went to the Reading Room & did a little copying there & at 5.20 started for home, walking all the way - found a letter from Geo., written from Bar Harbor, telling of his terrible finances times in the U.S. now and of his unprofitable season - He was very plucky in spirit but it was very depressing to me - Dinner at 7 - Suet passed in a desultory sort of reading, writing & chatting -

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 13, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Chingford & Epping Forest -*

Still very close & warm - just before 10 we took underground R.R. to Bishopsgate & then by Gt. Eastern R.R. at 10.33 to Chingford - at the station, where we were at 11.15 we found the coach "Telegraph" waiting - in a few minutes we drove to the Royal Forest Ho. at 11.30 made our start with a full load. A - the boys & I occupying the top most seat & to my mind, the best - some time the sun was exceedingly hot but clouds came & made shade & a refreshing breeze blew - at first the forest was nothing to brag of - waste land, gorse & brake & stunted trees, but afterwards confectedly our round we passed thro' some exceedingly beautiful country - our coach was an admirable one with a fine team of four dark bays who went for 2½ hours, running time, up hill & down dale, without stop at a swinging trot. It was 12.30 when we pulled up at Waltham & had a wait of 30 m - service was just over in the naved part of the old abbey that is now used as a ch. - a very fine bit of Norman architecture - we went into it & saw it, very much interested - at 1 again we started & drove over some charming country & under some grand trees - at 2 we pulled up at the hotel & then had lunch - a very ordinary affair for which we were charged $3.00 - an imposition - then we went out under the oak trees for a time & took the 3.33 train back to London, getting in about 4 - And the boys at once home but I went down to the Tower to have a good look at the New Tower Bridge, now nearly finished - I spent nearly an hour going about the terrace & the sort of park outside of the Tower & then home at about 6 - Dinner before 7 - Suef I wrote for some time on a letter to S.R. Koehler.
