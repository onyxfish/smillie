## Left Page

### Wed. June 7, 1893

*Venice — Hotel Beau Rivage — Rooms 16 & 17*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant mf. overcast, windy & cool in the afternoon & at night. Before 9 o'clk. we were all ready for a start & after going into a restaurant under our bedroom windows that we might see a nightingale & hear it note — of which it was good enough to whistle two or three times, joy less specimens — we took cab & drove to the Piazza Kettumo we visit — then to the Ch. of S. Domenico but even less interested in that than in two strong old tombs in the Piazza outside — Then we went around to the Pal. Bairedaqua-Vicenzi whose court & garden we admired exceedingly — then to S. Stefano — an exceedingly interesting cluster of seven churches and chapels upon the site of an old Pagan temple, the antiquity of some of them, the quantities and variety of styles with many extremely picturesque bits pleased us very much. Then we drove past the leaning towers, thro' the Giardino Montagnola to the R.R. station — got good contentment & fortune at 10.35 we did good bye to the home of the sausage, very sorry that we could not extend our stay. For a long time the ride was over a perfectly level plain — at 12 & 1 an steam on our left going to Venice on time) at 1.18. Into the Grand Canal, we went directly to the Hotel Beau Rivage, pleasantly situated on the Riva degli Schiavoni — we had soon made temps with the Proprietor at 3 o'clk. had our lunch. Soon after that we went out for a stroll — the Ducal Palace, Piazza & Ch. of S. Marco being very near — we wandered about on the Sq. & then into the church where we loitered for half an hour or more taking in all that we could of the archaic & barbaric beauty of the place — A. & E. went to the P.O. & returned to the hotel with the boys — they had their evening meal some time before our dinner at 7 — After that I went out to the Piazza S. Marco alone & for awhile listened to the music of a fine band, watched the people & amused myself looking into the shop windows. — Then to Hotel & as A. & I were both tired we went to bed early —

## Right Page

### Thur. June 8, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day — almost cloudless — breezy and cool, altho' very warm in the sunshine. We breakfasted before 9 — but it was about 10.30 when we all went out in a gondola & spent the rest of the mf. gliding upon the bosom of Grand Canal. We went into the glass manufacturers establishment of Caudiani & saw some of the work done. The mf. was very delightful — & yet, something of a disappointment — While portions of the city, a palace or a church here & there, are wonderfully beautiful in color & architecture, tural form — more beautiful than anything I have seen elsewhere — there is a larger portion than I had expected that is uninteresting or even ugly. Lunch at 12.30 after which we took a rest & then Annie, the boys & I went out for a stroll — we wandered through narrow & crowded streets to the Rialto & there we met Miss Eliza Toney & her sister — Then & the boys set out to the hotel while I went on alone upon a trip of exploration to fish & litter markets & to have another look at the Casa d'Oro — Got back to hotel a little late for dinner. Evening — after the boys were asleep A., E. & I went to the Piazza S. Marco & joined the thousands there in listening to music & the bounds & in eating an ice. — Back soon after 10 & to bed.
