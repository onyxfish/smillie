## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 31, 1893

**Weather:** Superb day — brilliant, almost cloudless, cold.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got up at 8 feeling very stiff but managing, by taking time & care, to get around — Before noon I bundled myself up for a little while & went out to Putau & Breese, who are laying the foundation wall for tank — I also took a look at Herrick's work, but the air was so sharp that I did not dare stay long out in it — Herrick got the roof off ready for rafters & spent most of the day getting an iron rod that is to take the strain at a braced cor. of the piazza upon which the bath-room is being built. I spent some time in the studio finishing up, putting away & packing there. I am much depressed at the slow way things are moving and at my physical condition — I am full of pains & discomforts from which it seems impossible for me to get relief. Eve'g the girls went out — I put Ralph to bed, & then had a lonely time. Got my trunk into large Room & dodged for an hour or more over the packing [illegible]

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 1, 1893

**Weather:** Fine day — fine sunset — Back feeling much better today, so that I was able to get about a good deal.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Most of my time was spent looking after Herrick & another carpenter who were at work putting up rafters over bath-room — changes had to be made & work delayed before corners & angles will come properly together — finally I had to determine to stay over another day — I rec'd a letter from Annie saying that she would be ready for us on Thurs. Also, a letter from J. C. Nicoll, Cor. Sec'y of N. A. D., saying that the Council at its last meeting had unanimously appointed me Treas. — I replied to N. informally saying that I must think before answering. Putau & Breese finished wall & I paid them off — Eve'g, packing box of foreign sketches, mornied — valise with Ralph's things & part of my trunk.
