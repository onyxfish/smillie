## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 1, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Aininteresting day. Opened brilliantly then squally with showers & a heavy head sea into which we bored — we rolled but little but we pitched tremendously throwing up, ahead of us, great billows of water which flew in drenching spray from stem to stern. The wettest boat I was ever on — In the afternoon the sky was entirely overcast & at night things were very dismal. About 7 A.M. we passed a very fine iceberg with one single peak. The Capt. estimated it to be about 700 ft. long & 140 feet high — Two hours or more later we passed another, yet larger, having two peaks, one not unlike the Matterhorn — the sun shining brightly upon it. We passed about 4 m. south of them — In the afternoon we passed a square fishing schooner, riding at anchor, a curious object in that vast waste of water. Jim's dearth continues — it is quite impossible to keep anyone quiet or warm — It is unpleasant below deck & they complain so that their food has been taken up to them to-day.

Run at noon 385 m.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 2, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & beautiful m'f. invigorating air & little motion — cooler afternoon running into cloudy weather — later, some rain — but f., quite warm, with showers. The anniversary of Sedan. At 8 A.M. German Nat. colors on all the masts & the band played Nat. airs — It was a day of the usual loafing & no incident — we passed a steamer of our own line, said to be an emigrant boat bound for Baltimore — Had an interesting chat with Cap't. Rinck concerning the sinking of the Oregon & the saving of her 800 pass'r. & crew by the Fulda of which vessel Cap't. Rinck then had command — but f. we had a holiday dinner — Saloon decked with flags — Band playing patriotic airs & the Germans singing lustily — at the close of the dinner Cap't Rinck made a little speech in German, closing with a toast to the German Emperor — drunk standing — Every later I got into chats with Mr. delaRoche & after that with Mr. Doster of Bethlehem Pa. — That kept me up until after 11,

Run at noon 358 m.
