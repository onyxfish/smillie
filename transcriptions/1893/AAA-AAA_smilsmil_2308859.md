## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 19, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very bright, warm day - bkfs: about 9 -
lounged about, taking a short walk - wrote
Postal Card to Kate & after 11 Mr. S - Mary, A -
& I went out in London & rode for nearly 2
hours, going thro' some beautiful streets -
out to Lincoln Park - to be take front & back
to business portion of the city - called Mr.
Kent at his store - Lunch bet. 1 & 2 - after
that I had a short nap, then I went out
for a stroll about ½ an hour or more -
Before 7 Mr. & Mrs. Kent came to dinner
& we had a very pleasant evelf, Henry
Sprague coming in later in the evelf.
Got to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 20, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lake Shore & Mich. Southern RR
leaving Buffalo at 8.45 p.m.
Sleeper - Sec. 9

A brilliant & lovely day - pure & fresh -
had a few minutes walk on Delaware Ave
before bkfp: at 8.30 - After bkfp: at Mr. S's
request I accompanied him - we
went first to his office & from there, by a series
of rides in street cars we made quite a tour
of the city getting back to his house soon
after 12 - I then rested on lounge until
after 1 when Mary, Annie, Mr. Sprague and
I went to Mr. Kent's to lunch where we
had an exceedingly pleasant time - Mr. K.
has a very handsome new house over which
we were shown after lunch - we left
them, after affectionate farewells about 4 o'clk
then I went off & wandered about, along
the water front & by the canal, the elevator
& coal pockets - much interested getting
back to Mr. S's soon after 6 - Dinner before
7 - soon after 8 carriage came & we drove
to station - much confusion - great crowds
& several trains over different roads leaving
the same station at the same time - all,
apparently, for Chicago - one of the most dis-
agreeable & least convenient stations, for
the amt. of business done, that I was ever in -
Miss Kent & her friend, Miss Worthington -
joined us before train started & had the section
opposite to ours - we soon had our beds made
up & were stowed away for the night.
