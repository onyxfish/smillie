## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 21, 1893

*"The Unity" - Woodlawn Terrace - Room 312. Near 65° & 5°*
**Weather:** Chicago. **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm & windy day winding up with very heavy thunder storms about 9 P.M. After a tolerably comfortable night I got up rel. at 7. At about 7.30 we reached Elk Hart where we stopped 25 m. & had a very good breakfast — all of our party on hand with good appetites. Then an uninteresting run until a little after 10 when we got to the Englewood station about 7 m. from Chicago centre & the nearest station to the gates of the Fair. By trolley car we ran along 63°. 5— then put off at Woodlawn ave — nobody could tell us anything about The Unity Bldg — we wandered for some time, not & tired, before we found it we soon got into our room — very nice & satisfactory we was held up a bit at this Castle — about [illegible] — we went over to the Michigan Columbian Club & had a [illegible] sort of lunch — then A. & J. Rode to Mr. & Stuart H. Brown's on Drexel Ave & saw him & his Rooms & decided to take one — then we went to the Fair getting there about 3 o'clk. I got a telegram off to Kate — then we started to follow Mary Cook's advice & by electric launch make the Round of the lagoon but a bit of shower frightened us into Midway Bld — instead we made a [illegible] of the grounds on ultra mural R.R. — a mistake & then we made circuit by electric launch — but clouds in the west hid the sun — still, the grey effect & the early moon, was very beautiful & [illegible]! We took tea at the Casino — then out — saw illumination of Court of Honor & flotilla of boats — it was a wonderfully fine spectacular effect & quite indescribable. In no state of all the ravings I have heard over these buildings it was as fine far beyond my expectations — about 8 o'clk — a wind storm broke upon us followed almost immediately by deluges of rain — with tens of thousands of others we all had to get shelter — a shower followed shower — at each lull we pause we would make a break to be driven in a moment to another shelter — the thunder & lightning were terrific — ambulances went tearing about — the streets soon became canals & at last we gave up trying to keep dry and floundered on getting in about 10 o'clk — Fortunately, with all the wind & rain, it was very warm & we did not suffer much discomfort — altogether I think the storm was a grand climax to the illumination.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 22, 1893

*At Mr. Stuart H. Brown's. 6422 Drexel [illegible]*
**Weather:** [illegible] **Temp:** 63° + 64° S.M.

Brilliant & beautiful day — overcast at night a few, very light spatters of rain — Up before 7 apparently none the worse for last night's ducking — we had a very satisfactory b'k'f — & then we spent some time packing paintings in musk trays preparatory to our move to Mr. Brown's — While sitting waiting for Annie I was much surprised to see Sue Cook come in — she was no less surprised at seeing me — she is with two friends in the Unity. It was about 10 when A. & J. went to Mr. Brown's where at once installed us in our rooms — very pleasant — & we have the freedom of the house — we wrote letters until after 12, it was 1 o'clk. when we started out on our way to the Fair — we stopped at the Hotel Howard café Restaurant & had a fairly good lunch — then to the Fair, walking all the way. I was near J. where we went in — we went almost directly to the Liberal Arts Bldg — taking a city, as we went through, at 1 min. [illegible], morning and electricity. By elevator we went to the roof & had a wonderful panorama — the air was bland — the atmosphere & color were heavenly — we spent a long time loafing about the vast promenade studying the buildings in some relative positions, the detail coming &, I had almost written, the sea, for it almost over nearly half of the horizon, so limitless is the ocean. Annie met friends up there — When we descended we made our way, with stops & by easy stages, to the Art Bldg — Annie waited below while I went to the offices where I spent nearly an hour with Miss [illegible] & [illegible] & Mrs. Chas. Kurtz — then A. & I went to the lagoon by electric launch we went to basin & So entrance to Liberal Arts — clouds in the west hid the sun but the moon was up & the eastern sky was effect — there were some wonderfully beautiful lights — Finally, after going through the Good Bldg with fisheries & others in that part of the grounds we went into the Polish Restaurant & had a good dinner then by ultra-mural we went to the Colonnade & there to the basin where we saw the illumination of the flotilla of boats with lights & emblematic groups — about 8.30 we were conscious of a mistiness in the air & we started for home getting there without difficulty by electric car — after we got safely in there was a shower.
