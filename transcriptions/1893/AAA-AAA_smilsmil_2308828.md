## Left Page

### Fri. July 21, 1893

*London — At Mr. Tautods's.*
*Wea. 13 Gloucester Place, Hyde Park. Ther.*

Rain during the night & m't until 6 — then clearing into a lovely day —
Up at about 6.30 & at everything ready for departure
Up — at 7.30 — at 8 the 'bus was at the door — we
had a satisfactory settling of accts. with Mad. [illegible] —
an affectionate parting & at 10 m. past 8 we left the
house, got to 8. Lazare Station at 8.30 — got through
all preliminaries promptly & had good seats —
off in time, at 9 o'clk. Enjoyed very much the
clear & fresh m't air — the lovely & sunshine &
the charming views along the river which we
followed until we got to Rouen. Some time after
11 — then we passed thro' a country less interesting until we reached Dieppe at 12.15
just as we finished eating our cold chicken
lunch — Train on quai alongside of boat — a
fine new paddle-wheel steamer "Paris" —
12.45 when we cast off — the "Norwood" [illegible]
a rough passage, but to the contrary, it proved
to be a very delightful one — few passengers —
I rolled myself in rug & felt pleasant
nap — got to New Haven at 4.45 — after about
30 m. delay we got thro' the customs Ho. &
everything seemed ready for a start but for
some unknown reason we waited another
half hour & did not get away until 5.45 —
lovely every — warm sunshine & long
shadows — the ride to London — pleasant
one — got to Victoria Station at about
7.15 — got our trunks up on a hack & reached
our lodgings before 8 o'clk — then, as soon
as possible we had our dinner — It was
9 o'clk — before the boys got to bed — and
an hour later Annie & I followed pretty
thoroughly tired —

## Right Page

### Sat. July 22, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day, altho' much cooler
than the weather we have been having in
Paris — I don't like the damp chilliness of
the English air — we got up soon after 7 —
earlier than we intended, & had a [illegible] about
7.30 — After that A— took Jim in to see St. Earle
who pronounces his trouble dyspeptic — I
started out to make a day of it — went first
to the British Museum & was very pleasantly
met by Prof. Calvin & members of his staff —
spent less than an hour there — then walked
thro' Shaftesbury Ave. — to Piccadilly — & thence into
old & new Broadst — went into the Fine Arts
Socy & saw Mr. Brown & [illegible] call — of water-
color drawings (of Japan) by Alfred Parsons —
Then went out & got a cup of coffee & a Roll —
Then to Royal Academy & there I worked
over the exhibition faithfully until near
5 o'clk — It is a very large exhibition, with
heaps of rubbish — but on the whole, it
might be worse — measured by any
standard, it is higher than the [illegible]
of the Champs de Mars — Home by 'bus
& had a chance to get a rest before the
boys came in — they had been with
their mother to theRound Pond in the Park
sailing the big boats that they bought at
Whitely's this m't — After 7, when
we had our dinner — well, divided
between the "Times" & my diary.
