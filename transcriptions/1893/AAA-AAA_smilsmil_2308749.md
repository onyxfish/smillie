## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 13, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day. A times slightly overcast up before sunrise to take a good look at Corsica on our S.S.E. horizon — I saw it very clearly — Bkf. at 8.30 — Before 10 Mr. Gifford came & after a time I went off with Enillt. He took me through a number of the most peculiar & picturesque passage ways that I have yet seen, within old town, descending that cling to the steep, rocky hillsides, like mud nests of swallows to the side of a barn. I saw a number of places that I wished to sit right down with color boy & paper & make studies — we went to the cemetery on the slope of the hill & had a delightful ride to the east & west & back among the tombs — There I left Enillt & returned to dejeuner at least — after 2 o'clk. I again started out alone — I went again through the old town & picked out 3 or 4 places which I hope to get sketches — then into the nether part of town where I fell in with the Carnival procession — today it moves from the west — in all other respects it was quite the same as the show on Sat. — It is remarkable how these children continue to be amused by the same tom foolery. The same wicked pelting by shot-like confette was patiently submitted to as before — I then walked on to the western outskirts & north & around getting to our hotel at about 4.30 — Pretty tired — I lay down & got a little nap — Dinner with the children at 6.5 —
A & Z. dined at table d'hote. — Every A., 2, o I went out at about 9 to see the burning of King Carnival — but got to the spot a few minutes too late & saw only the smoldering remnants — we walked thro' the illuminated streets where the effect of my road of Chinese lanterns was very pretty. Great crowds of singing & dancing people in dominoes.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 14, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day altho' the weather was somewhat streaky. Filmy clouds, then clear. At about 10 a.m., Coe, W. Rowe Lewis (English), a friend of the Jeffords — called — He came alone — Mr. G. — having so severe a headache that he could not accompany him — He was very pleasant & chatty — we made an arrangement, with proviso, to visit Rochemontune tomorrow afternoon — He left before 11 & then A, Z, & I walked up to the dwelling back to the hotel to dejeuner at 12. After that, a rest, then called a landeau & we all drove in to Ventimiglia. Finding it not particularly gratifying as a drive — some views over the sea were impressive & some views looking back to Mentone & the surrounding mountains were very fine — but the light & color were not very good & the white dust was disagreeable — When we got to Ventimiglia we found a Carnival procession in possession of the town, so after looking in for a while we returned to our hotel having been away about three hours. I dined at 6.30 with the boys. Every general reading — old newspapers — pondering the literature.

R.C. invitation to contribute some of my art work to the 4th Annual extn. of Mentone Art — sent my regrets.
