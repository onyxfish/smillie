## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 27, 1893

*Genoa - Hotel de Londri. Rooms 41 - 42.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 6 o'clk. brilliant, cloudless m'g. after
a night of heavy rain, wind &c &c - Got our
packing finished - b'k'f't at 7 o'clk - and were
somewhat hurried by the' bus to catch our train
at 8.14 which proved, as a matter of course, to
be 30 or 40 m. late. - Inured ourselves until the
arrival of the train - The ride was one of
little interest, much of the way evening to &
through tunnels passing more than 70 before
we got to Genoa - we made up none of our
lost time + on arriving at G - I found that an
Spezia train had gone on without us + that
if we wished to get over the 51 miles in less
than 3½ or 4 hours we would have to wait
over 24 hours - after considerable trouble an
officious Ruddy wrote a commission for us to
stop over night at Genoa + we picked up our
bags + walked over to the Hotel de Londri -
a 3rd class hotel - much worn + dirty but, after
all, fairly comfortable - we had a nice tea
\+ coffee + then went out + rode for a couple
of hours enjoying very much a renewal
of the ints of our visit eight months ago - we
went pretty thoroughly over the town + got
back to the hotel at 4.30 - Then at 10 I went
out + had a little walk to the palaces on
the Via Garibaldi - back - a short rest +
then we all dined together, à la carte,
in a small room - A plain, simple dinner
early + met in the salon where we met
some fellow travelers, Americans, English
\+ Scotch - To bed early.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 28, 1893

*Pisa - Royal Victoria Hotel. Rooms 22 - 23.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain during the night, continuing
through the day with a few cessations until
about 4.30 P. M. + recommencing again in
the eve'g. Up at 7 after a fair nights rest +
had b'k'f't at 8 - at 8.30 we walked across to the
R.R. station - fortunately it did not rain at the
moment + before we got started in our om-
nibus "train for Spezia" - we wish that these
R.R. people could be as enterprising in run-
ning their trains as they were in building the road.
It is a wonderful piece of work, so much of it
being tunneled (?so) under the mountains +
rock. The scenery changed soon after leaving Ge -
the coast became wild, + precipitous + under
lustrous clouds, very dark + forbidding - we
saw some very characteristic views of peaks
crowned by old buildings + deep valleys, some
of them green with olive trees + some of them
mourn fully barren. The views over the sea
were, artistically, far finer + more suggestive
than when seen under a cloudless sky. Our
train was able to make its schedule time all
the way - during the first hour we saw 11 miles -
at 2.30 we got to Spezia - 57 m. in 3½ h. - There we
stopped + walked to the Continental Hotel - the dirtiest
most disgusting place that we have yet got into -
we got a fairly eatable dinner but it was sickening
in its service - as it had again commenced to rain
we rode back to station - train came along a little
before 3 - Trouble to find places but, at last, we all
packed into a compartment where we found a
Nice family; father, mother + 4 children - I had a
chatted time until we got to Pisa, at 4.30 - Rode
at once to R.V. Hotel, on the Lung Arno river, prompt-
ly installed - very soon A - the boys + I sallied out
- it had stopped raining - in fact, was quite clear, +
walked to the Duomo, Baptistry + Leaning Tower +
saw them under the light of a setting sun with
the moon, not quite full, in the eastern sky, over
the Duomo - Back to hotel at 10 - Dinner at 6.30.
Eva ff. J. just part in public room - later, in our
old rooms where we had a pleasant + fine -
The view of the Duomo group of buildings
was a disappointment to me - first, owing to
color, next, because, in reality they did not
appear to me any more dignified or agreeable
in form than their pictured representations.
