## Left Page

### Wed. April 12, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another dismally pleasant day with no improvement in my temperature record — Annie wrote a note to Mary to which I added a couple of pages. I had the satisfaction of a very good Turkey dinner in the Eveg. Annie read aloud from "Don Orsino."

## Right Page

### Thur. April 13, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, mild & beautiful day — very much the same tantalizing conditions in my case. Hope deferred — a gain perhaps of 1/5° on the record of the day before. Jim turned in regularly sick, feverish & coughing, and was put into his mother's bed alongside of mine & there he stayed all day. Dr. Scott came before 11 & made a pleasant, chatty call. He says that he is entirely satisfied with my progress(?) & thinks that Jim, kept in bed for a day or two, will recover without medicine. Ralph went off alone with without complaint with the new maid in the mg & in the afternoon he and his mother had a long trip to Pozzuoli where they saw some sights. I wrote part of a letter to Mary. I had, & enjoyed, a very good chop at lunch time. Eveg. Annie read aloud from "Don Orsino."
