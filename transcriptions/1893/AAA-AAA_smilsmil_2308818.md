## Left Page

### Sat. July 1, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightful day. It was very late — nearly 11 — before I left the hotel for a tramp to the heights on the opposite, or S.W. side of the valley. The air, at the very warm, was deliciously fragrant & refreshing — the color over the mountains was bewitching — I enjoyed every step of the way immensely — I got up to the foot of the rock up on which the Belvedere stands. I took a couple of slight sketches & saw peasants have a couple of swarms of bees. Crossing the stream, in my way to the hostel, I found Annie & the boys — She, in the shade of a tree, writing — the boys dabbling in the water with boats — Dinner soon after — In the afternoon I went to the little chapel where I commenced a color sketch yesterday & finished it about 5 o'clk — then back to hotel & went out for a walk on the north side of the valley — Glorious effects — sunlight on mts. tops & shadows in the valley — Annie & the boys went off on a ride — everybody got back late so that our evening meal was delayed until after 7.30 — my lost overcoat was brought to me in the evening by a carriage driver from Bellinzona. To him I paid 3 francs very thankfully. Every, what little there was left of it, I spent with different parties in conversations more or less vapid.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 2, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still beautiful weather — To-day is a Festa or "Sagra" here — the chief interest being a lottery or "Tombola" for the benefit of the Pompiers of the town. We had early bells and bands & church services & all sorts of noises & confusion & after this — there was a parade of the fire brigade & two old fashioned hand pumps & some ladders — & then there was some good congregational singing. The mt. got away with nothing accomplished — the boys played in the water of the stream & Ralphy managed to tumble in. Dinner at 1 — & after that repairs then the Tombola — The crowd was a clumsy & not picturesque one — the women clad in dark, heavy woolen dresses & wearing little, black flat felt hats — Tombola was drawn by a young peasant woman — after that games united — after 6 — climbing a greased pole — riding a big wooden prison that turned upon an axle — sliding a lance two chairs on a board fixed to the bottom of a trough full of water, the man being upon a platform on top that was quickly run under the out — Breaking earthen pots, hanging, filled with plaster or flower, containing prizes, while blind-folded — running races, etc. etc. — The crowd enjoyed its in a wholesome, decorous way — there was some beer drinking, but not much and very little noise. We had our evening meal at about 6.30 — after that for an hour or more chatting with the Greys & Leila Martin — then a short walk — thunderstorms very near us, 3 & 2 — To bed at 10.30.
