## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 27, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain between 7 and 10 A.m. No rain until 5 p.m. - from that time it rained at times, very heavily. Hill & Williams came in the afternoon & filled in part of trench for pipe - Herrick did some work on frame for south end of new bath room & I had a chance to do considerable work on the Koehler plate - but was not able to finish it. I went down town at 11 o'clk & got a letter from Annie. She is not coming back here this season but is devoting herself to house cleaning in 38' St & says that I had better not go to the city before the middle of next week. I went down town again about 5 p.m. & mailed a postal card for Annie. Eve commenced a letter to Mr. S.R. Koehler.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 28, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming m[or]f. quite mild & sunny, overcast at noon, with westerly winds growing cooler - a gusty rain storm at 5 o'clk. Bright moonlight at 9 - Oakley & team at work hauling stone, Stone helping. Rutan did not put in an appearance & sent no word. Hill & Williams completed filling the trench & started on branch trench to east end of old barn. Herrick got up frame for bathroom. And after much tribulation I mailed Koehler plate & a letter to Kimmel & Voigt. I went down town in the m[or]n. & got a letter from Annie, & again at 5, when I posted the mezzo[?] plate. An attack of lumbago(?) came on about 5 o'clk, accompanied by what I suppose to be rheumatic pains in chest under shoulder blades, making me feel pretty wretched.
