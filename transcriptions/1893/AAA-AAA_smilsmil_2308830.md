## Left Page

### Tues. July 25, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy day with some sunshine & a fresh breeze  **Temp:** [illegible]

About 9.30 I left the house & walked to Hyde Park Cor. then by 'bus to the Academy — worked diligent looking at the pictures & taking notes until 1.30 — Then lunched in the lunch room, & resuming work continued until I got through with the oil paintings — Then — at 3.30, I felt that I could do no more — Again I walked to Hyde Park Cor. & across the Park, home, feeling rested & refreshed by it — Finding no one at home I had a little nap — Dinner at 7 — Evef I wrote awhile upon my Academy notes.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 26, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some sunshine very early in the mfo, then rain until after 11 — then clear for a time — a thunderstorm (1 clap) about 3 o'clk. & then cleared charmingly — Left the house at 9.30 — walked all the way to the Museum — in a shop window in Gt. Russell St. I saw a [illegible] india, without letters. A father's Rocky Mt. plate after Bierstadt — B's presentation to Mr. Tom. Taylor. I bought it, frame & all for a sovereign & presented it to the British Museum. I spent the day in the Print Room very much interested in early aqua-tints. I got my lunch in the Museum Refreshment Room — out for a little airing — & then to work until 5 — Went in to Newman's Soho Sq. & saw old Mr. Mills for a few min. Walked all the way home — dinner at 7 — Evef reading —

At noon I went to Jas. Rimmell & Son's Oxford & Dean St. & bought for A. Jones a copy of the Autobiography of [illegible] Raimbach the engraver. — $1.75
