## Left Page

### Tues. July 11, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant, cool day in spite of several smart, short showers — Up soon after 6 + before bkfs - wrote part of a letter to Kate + after bkfs. I finished it. Annie went out on a shopping tour taking Ralph with her + leaving Jim who again has been gone for some days to Strêtût — I walked up to Trocadero gallery of Architecture + from thence, at 12.30, to our lodgings in a light shower + had lunch. After that, I went to the Biblioteque Nationale + tried to see some of the work of the Master of 1480 without success — Then enquired for certain Durers + a whole lot of soiled scrolls + books were placed on a table before me — no plan of arrangement that I could discover except that the wood screws were by themselves — I was much surprised at the poor + imperfect impressions + want of care + promptly concluded that I had better go to the British Museum for study — I enquired for acquatints by la Place + after a little searching the attendant told me there was none of his work in the collection — I gave up before 4 and went to the Louvre. There until 5 — then to our lodgings — Dinner at 7 — another stupid every [illegible].

## Right Page

### Wed. July 12, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool day much given to showers — I was caught in no less than four — It was about 10 when I started on my walk to the Biblioteque Nationale — 11 o'clk. when I got there — about an hour looking over some French engs. late 15th Cent I. — Then walked to the Louvre + took train to lodgings, getting in just in time for lunch — Heavy showers after that so that it was near 4 when Annie, the boys + I went to the Trocadero Museum to see the Architecture casts — Then we went to the Ave. Kleber to Miss Joshay's lodgings — not in, but on our return to No. 17, found her waiting for us — Then A. went with me to a tailors + I left my light overcoat with him, to be cleaned + lined — Dinner at 7.30 — self I wrote to Wm C. Cox and to Frank Herrick.
