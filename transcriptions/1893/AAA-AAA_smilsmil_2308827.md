### Wed. July 19, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Versailles.*

A charming day — immediately after
bkfst. A. went to the dentist taking Jim
& Ralph with her — I spent most of the
a.m. arranging my things & packing my
trunk — We had an early lunch — 12 o'clk
— & got away as soon as we could by bus
to the St. Lazare Station, we went for no par-
ticular train having been told that they
go every 20 m. — Instead, they run once an
hour & we just missed the 1.20 train and
had to wait until 2.20 — it was 3.15 when
we got to Versailles — cab to the Palace &
there we put ourselves in charge of a
regular guide who showed us a bit of
the most important & interesting Salons —
then I saw Vernet's & Yvon's paintings and
was much surprised to find them so
exceedingly poor — then we took a cab by
the hour & drove to the Grand Trianon &
went thro' it — then to the Petite Trianon
& not go thro' that palace, but into the
courts & to the little village where the
lords & ladies of past times used to play
the Rural — a jolly sport they must have
had in such delightful places — then
we drove through the Bois & set [illegible] on the
So. side of the river to Mr. Parnasse Station
leaving [illegible] 6.05 & getting to the Ave. Trocadero
about 7 — in ample time for dinner —

## Right Page

### Thur. July 20, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the evening.

Very warm day with strong breeze blowing.
After bkfst. A. went with Miss Joshay to the
gallery of the Luxembourg while I took the boys
& went to Cook's & bought tickets to London —
made arrangements for a bus in the m'f —
telegraphed to Mr. Lautset & went to Drexel
Harjes & Co's & drew some money — then back
to our lodging & packed until lunch at
12.45 — After that I took up sketching things
& by boat went up the river to the Isle St. Louis
& made a slight sketch of the Pont Marie —
back before 5 & completed packing —
it was nearly 8 before we had dinner —
quite a gay party — besides the Castados
there were four others whose names I did
not catch — one of them being a young American —
They did not leave until after 10. — Then
we had very affectionate leave takings.
