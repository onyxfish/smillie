## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 20, 1893

**Weather:** Brilliant, windy & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Stomach trouble much increased to-day. I did not take R. to school in the m't but I went for him at 12.30. I worked all the m't on "Old Tower" & finished it. Genevieve Wright & Emma S. lunched with us at 1 o'clk. At 3 o'clk. I went to Knickerbocker Trust Co. & saw Jr. P. Townsend (cor. Fifth Ave. & 27th S.) to see upon what terms the Trust Co. would make a loan to the N.A. He says the N.A's must formally ratify & approve such loan — mee to N.A., Farrington not in — then I called upon Calverly for half an hour — then to Butler's — then home — Dinner. Ev'g I wrote to T. W. Wood about my visit to Townsend.

Mr. W. G. Parks called in the m't & bought two pl's. of my etching after Alma-Tadema's "[illegible] Homer."

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 21, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day with a beastly headache — about 7 p.m. had some farina gruel after which I got up — in parlor until after 11 & then again to bed.
