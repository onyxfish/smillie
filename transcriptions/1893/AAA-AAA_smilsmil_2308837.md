## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 6, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pale, yellow fog in the morning, clearer atmosphere, but cloudy in
the afternoon with threats of rain —
In the m'f Annie went to St. Margaret's
& heard Cannon farmer — not enthusiastic
on her return — I spent some time in
writing to Mr. Koehler & about noon I
went with the boys & had an hours
walk in the park — 1.40 when we had
lunch — after 5 we went again into
the park & had another good walk —
Dinner at about 7 — Eve'f. Annie
Read aloud for a little while.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 7, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bank Holiday —

overcast m'f but clearing very pleasant
about noon — The museum being closed I
went to the print room at about 10.30
& worked there until after 1.30 — then I
had lunch & a little walk outside after
it — then I resumed my work — about
3.30 Annie came & at 4 we walked
down to Charing Cross & made a call
upon the Purnells & found that Mr. P—
had left but yesterday to join her hus-
band somewhere in France — then we
ride to Gloucester R— on the top of a' bus —
Dinner after 7 — Eve'f. I wrote out
some of my notes on aquatinting &
got to bed after 10.

The Bank Holiday was a very quiet
affair as we saw it — London seemed
to be quite deserted — no doubt the
country round about swarmed. The
turnstiles at Sydenham Crystal
Palace showed more than 42,000
entrances.
