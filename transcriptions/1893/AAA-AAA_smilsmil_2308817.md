## Left Page

### Thur. June 29, 1893

**Weather:** Bright mts.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright mts. smart short thunder shower bet. 1 & 2 o'clk - & another one, after much threatening at about 6 - our party got up in the mts. apparently none the worse for their journey of yesterday - we had bkfs- at about 9 - it was after 10, when Annie, Della, Martin, the boys & I went out for a walk - we took it very easily, crossing the stream & going up the hill S.W. from our hotel, where we had a pleasant view up & down the valley - then back by another path & over another ridge, where the boys found a fine place to play in the water. We had dinner at 10 o'clk - very German - thick gravies, nutmeg, cinnamon, mace & vanilla in everything! Afternoon I went off alone for a walk - fine clouds after the mid-day shower & threats of another that kept me watching the sky. Again I went across the stream & to the west, up among some larches. I did not reach the point I aimed at owing to the gathering storm, rel. by another path over another bridge & had to walk very briskly, getting in just as the rain commenced. I but it was soon over - the heaviest of it going east of us - Every meal, a sort of high tea rather than dinner, at 7 - Sleep I did not feel very bright & lounged & napped upon a very comfortable sofa-divan in our room.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 30, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day - Bkfs. at 9 - it was after 10 when Al E., Mt. Beason, the boys & I started out, we went over my walk of yesterday afternoon - I took some slight pencil memo's on getting to where I left my path yesterday, the others went on, returning to the hotel while Jim & I pushed on to the point I then aimed for, a strade right up upon an open space & we got there & had very fine views. But the region does not interest me - The air is delicious - pure & fragrant - just the place for living & exercising out of doors - but I see nothing that incites me to work. We got back to the hotel a little before dinner at 1 - a very good meal, but again spoiled some by having spices in almost every dish from soup to dessert. After dinner I got out my water color traps & went to a bit that I noticed this mts. - an old circular tower & chapel - I worked for two or three hours, a plague color, & did little else than disgust myself very thoroughly - I was not in the beat of shape & everything went wrong - I went back to hotel before 6 & then went out for a short walk - I did not go to table at 7 o'clk - but had a cup of tea & a roll - evening I did a little writing & some reading.
