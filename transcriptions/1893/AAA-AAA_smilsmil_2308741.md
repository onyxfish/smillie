## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 28, 1893

**Weather:** mixed, cloudy day with little sunshine  **Temp:** [illegible]

About 3 o'clk. this n'lf. I was awakened by great pain in bowels — Had a monkey and a parrot time — it was nearly an hour before could get back to bed —. The result, I suppose, unwise punishment of myself with drugs — up again before daylight — pain less severe — & finally, up for all day at 8.30 — very groggy — very sore — about 11 o'clk. I managed to crawl down to Promenade but returned very soon — Had déjeuner in our bed soon — out again for fable walk in the afternoon — was able to sit thro' table d'hate & was be-believe eat — Sat for a little while in the Salon after dinner talking with Sig. & Miss Glauckner — then reading in our own room until I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 29, 1893

**Weather:** Cloudy & later in the day, wet — but mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still — Feeling better in some ways, but weak & came under the belt overy groggy in the legs — About 11 went out with Jim & Ralph, shopping for frammin — not finding it, bought crème de riz — got home just in time to escape a wetting — amused myself by cooking my déjeuner over spirit lamps. Crème de riz — milk & two raw eggs — well beaten together & boiled — did not go down to table d'hate déjeuner with the others. Later in the afternoon, walked on the Promenade Anglaise with Jim, rain falling softly. Spent much time reading — Table d'hote dinner after 6 — something of an infliction, but I managed to get through without eating much of anything. Sig. spent part of the eve'g with us in our bed room — I also gave some time to my work.
