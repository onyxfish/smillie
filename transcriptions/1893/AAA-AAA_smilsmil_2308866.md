## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 3, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Annie & Jim went to N.Y.*

And yet another brilliantly beautiful
day. Up at 6 - [illegible] before 7 - Trains for
[illegible] left at 7.45 we got over there in
good time & Annie & Jim were started
on their journey. Then Ralph & I went
downtown & attended to a number of
little matters - back to house & looked
after Hill who is digging for foundations for
no water tanks & Herrick who is dawdling
over the job of putting up wire for vines on
the [illegible] room balcony. Dinner after 1 -
Afternoon, most of the time out of doors
about the place - Ralph & I being
playfellows together until after 4, when
Wesley got out of school - Tea at
6.30 - Put R - to bed at 8 - a little
reading & to bed myself soon after
9.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 4, 1893

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild, quiet, dull day, turning to rain
before noon - very steady fall during the
afternoon - Ralph & I took a turn down
town in the [illegible]. I spent some
time with Herrick superintending
the putting up of netting for vines,
will dug during the [illegible]. In the
afternoon I spent most of the time
with pen & ink on a Cabin sketch
[illegible]. I wrote to Mr. Kent acknowled-
gement of draft for $135.00 - also
a note to W. J. Hobson of New Haven
who has written to me about Frankfort
black.
