## Left Page

### Thur. July 11, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. After my m[illegible] duties I helped Herrick putting up duck wall covering in Jim's room. Then I did some letter writing until feeling very heavy. At noon, K. T., Jim & I went out on our wheels \+ rode on the fail road out to the top of the long hill on the Silver lake Road, then back \+ down to P.O. \+ home. I was very proud of my performance as I got up several considerable grades without distress. After dinner, again with Herrick. After 5 o'clk. I went down town \+ spent a little time with Broadhead planning to repair a spark fender. Tea before? — Eve'f., did not go down town, Mary \+ her two girls went to Church.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 12, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day. It was 10.30 before I was ready to go down town — then, on my wheel I went to Broadhead's shop and until 12 I was with him fixing the corners of a broken spark fender, bracing with iron \+ winding copper wire over the braces. Then, having my wheel, I took a half hours spin before returning home. Dinner at 1. After that, until near 4 I was writing letters to Falconer, W\[illegible\] to. S. Lee \+ Geo. Cook, concerning the transfer of title of the W.K. Wall farm, in Kingman Co. Kansas, to me. One of the Western farm loans this' which Annie lost several thousand dollars. Then again on my wheel went to Broadheads \+ we finished the Repair of the spark fender. Then off for a ride — thro' town out by Depot St. to M.R.R. Station \+ down the road back of that for some distance — then back \+ out past Roger Searle's. Home at 5.30 pretty well tired out. Before tea at 6.45 I had a rub-down, change \+ [illegible]. Every — very quiet, Reading.
