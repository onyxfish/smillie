## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 14, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliantly beautiful day \- All hands get away upon their pic nic at 9.30 in high hopes of a jolly day. I remained alone hoping to finish my dormer window drawings. I worked faithfully during the mtf. \+ had dinner solus at 1 o'clk. After that I got to work cleaning my bicycle \+ into a mess with chain \+ hind wheel \- It was nearly 4 when again I got to work on the drawings \- Then W.G. Parke came in \+ sat with me until after 6 \- Then we started together for down town \- It was about 7 when we set? to the house \+ at 7.30 as the pic\-nickers had not set? we took our tea \- at about 8 Bessie P. \+ Miss Clewell came \- but they left as our family drove up at 8.30 \- all tired \+ evidently not in jovial frame of mind \- no one had any thing to say in praise of Tripps Lake. Supper was eaten \+ there to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 15, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

hot smuggy \- a few drops of rain fell at about 1 o'clk \- hazy \- All hands seemed a little raw edged after yesterday's pleasure exertion. After my regular duties I got to work upon dormer window drawings \+ stuck to them pretty closely all day getting practically through. I took a short bicycle ride before dinner at 1 o'clk. We were to go out at 4 for another party ride but the P.m. was too hot \+ the roads too dusty \- I worked until after 6 \- then a little time on my wheel \+ a few heads of lattuce transplanted before supper. At 8 went down on my wheel \- Evelyn. Mr. Roland Mumford \+ Chas. Saupre on piazza with the girls for awhile.
