Whereas, The west shore of the Hudson River, from the boundary line between the States of New-York and New-Jersey southward, by reason of its altitude, commands strategically the northern portion of the city of New-York, as well as the city of Yonkers, and the villages of Hastings, Dobbs Ferry, Irvington and Tarrytown, in the State of New-York; and the cities of Hoboken and Jersey-City in the State of New-Jersey, and also commands the navigation of the Hudson River, and the key of the line most exposed to invasion by a foreign power;

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 14, 1895

**Weather:** Brilliant \+ beautiful winter day  **Temp:** [illegible]

At 1.30 I started for the house of W. Seth Low, Pres't of Columbia College — No. 30 2, 64th S — Cor. of Mad. Ave — got there before 1 \+ Mr. Low at once took me to his library where from an hour \+ half we talked of the possibility of raising the standard of art \+ education in the U.S. school to a higher level \+ bringing the school of the A — within the lines of Columbia University. He seemed to be pleased \+ interested \+ promised to give the matter his careful consideration \+ in time, when he has evolved a plan, he will communicate with me. To me the conference was exceedingly interesting \+ filled me with hope. Personally I found him a charming man. Home before 1/2. Then downtown to quit the Wife Ass. Thinking that I had a back premium to pay — pleased to find myself mistaken — Saw Mr. Van Peet for a moment — Home to lunch at 1.30 — It was 3 when I got to studio — very tired from loss of sleep. I had an uneasy nap in my big chair — Mrs. Jacobs came in — Home at 5. Eliz. Home came in for awhile. Mary \+ [illegible] came in while we were at lunch —
News came this noon that W. [illegible] [illegible] died at Buffalo this m'f — Mary \+ Jennie went on at once — At 5.30 I went with Jim \+ helped to Rogers \+ Peet's \+ got each a pair of shoes — Home soon after 6. Dinner. After that wrote a letter to sister Mary — took it to A.C. Station — Then to Geo. Cook's with a paper to sign, relating to Annie's will — not in — left it under his door. Then home \+ puzzled over some of Jim's arithmetical problems — no wonder the poor boy is worried.
