## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 29, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

High winds \+ dark clouds until 3 o'clk p.m. when Rain commenced \+ fell quite steadily thro' the rest of the day \+ evef. From about 10 to 1.30 I worked hard at clearing the Roof \+ piazzas of shingles, broken slate \+ wreckage generally, cleaning out "valleys" \+ troughs in preparation for the threatened Rain which I wished to go without impediment into the cisterns. Dinner shortly after 2 —. After that I wrote part of a letter to Mima Ireland. Nothing accomplished in the evef. Rain continued falling steadily thro' most of the night.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 30, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, clear mf., cloudier later, with a spatter of Rain late in the afternoon. The hands at work again this mf. but it was dismally cold work. It was nearly 11 before I got at my sketching — then I drew in work over the face \+ beard for a second biting. After dinner a short rest looking after the men — Hibbard \+ a helper were on hand waiting for Somis who had promised to come with some flagging stones. Then I completed the work on plate and bit it in — so dark that I dared not go further with it, so put it away for to-morrow — then, down town — \+ to H. S. Beach's \+ saw my new standing press — Evef., making preparations for Jim's journey to N.Y. to-morrow \+ for the expected frost to-night.
