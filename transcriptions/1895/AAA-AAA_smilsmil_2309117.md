## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 7, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day, very cold - a little snow flying thro' the day, settling into an old fashioned snow storm at night. As usual, it was near 11 before I got to studio, fooled away what remained of the a'f. working with lead pencil on the old Lexington Study made in 1860. Home to lunch. - Ralph + I were alone - Did some writing, etc. + took some of the Tiffany Glass that had been broken out of the front door. It was late when I went back to studio - then to N.A. + spent a little time with Mr. Jordan arranging Etching Club 2/6th matters. Home at 6 + spent some time attending to the fires. It was near 7 when we had dinner. Eve'f. Charl's came in for awhile bet. 9 + 10 o'clk. Geo. was called out

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 8, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Exceedingly cold with high wind and very fine snow. Ther. down very near to zero. I would not let either of the boys go to school + I stayed in-doors myself until late in the afternoon. Emily got up with a very bad headache + much upset, but she recovered herself before noon. Katie Adams came in during the a'f. She is visiting with Alice Ireland. She consents to come here to stay for a few days. I busied myself arranging files of bills, letters + papers - Lunch soon after 1 - I wrote letters etc. until about 4 - Louise Cook came in + sat for some time with Emily. I bundled up + went out + walked the neighboring streets for nearly an hour, finding the wind + snow in my face, exceedingly uncomfortable. Geo. cook did not get in to dinner until 7.15 - then dinner - It was 9 before I got the boys to bed.
