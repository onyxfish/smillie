## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 1, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day with snow in the ev'f. My m'f duties engaged me until about 11, then I went directly to Mr. W. H. Lee's & had a few minutes conversation with him concerning business connected with Annie's will. He sent me up to the surrogate's office with one of his young men & there I was engaged upon preliminary forms until after 1 o'clk. Then home & had my lunch after 2 o'clk. The rest of the p.m. was spent writing notes & getting things in order. Keeping myself busy & struggling to make myself conform to the new conditions & things in a natural & healthy manner. I went out before dinner & had a walk. Gretta has so much improved that she has been up all day, spending much of the time in the nursery in a big chair before the fire. In the ev'g she gave Ralph a bath & put him to bed. I cannot altogether overcome a resentful feeling ag't her for recovering. Later in the ev'g Charl came in. I went to Edw'd C's to get address of the old lawyer who drew Annie's will — but find that he is dead. After a dismally uncomfortable ev'f I got to bed before 11 but was up during the night administering Ipecac to Jim who was coughing in a very croupy sort of way.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 2, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy falling snow most of the day. I remained indoors all the m'f. getting many of my things out of the nursery up to the attic store-room. At noon I went up to Gr. Central Station with a letter from Mary to Kate. Then to Groce's & Butche's. Lunch. After that, notes, bills, etc. until near 5 o'clk. Then to N.a. with a number of checks that I had signed. Then to studio & was there clearing up until near 6 — then home. Dinner at 6.30. Ev'g Cha's came in, & later, Ge? & Nellie.

To-day I have transacted business feeling tolerably well. I have had a more hopeful & healthy feeling, having been to a great extent able to divert my thoughts & feelings away from morbid and into normal channels. For the first time I can really hope that I shall be able to rise equal to the occasion — that in spite of inherent weakness I may find strength to do what I know to be my duty. I have a mortal dread of proving myself to be a coward & unworthy the love that Annie has given me.
