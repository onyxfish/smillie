## Left Page

### Wed. June 19, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still cloudless, hot & dry. In the m'g went down town & after attending to various matters I set out to interview some of the prominent townsmen on the subject of sewers. I saw Judge McCullum, his bro. Aley. — A.B. Smith & Rev. A.L. Benton — they all assured me of their approval of my plan of having an expert come here to look over the ground & give an opinion. Dinner at 1 — Katie's Bicycle & mine are in the m'g — late in the afternoon with Jim, we went out for a ride. Of course we were much discouraged — the roads are vile — & we had no pleasure — I came in wet, changed, had a sponge bath & then, tea — Mr. Cap't. Cromwell spent the eve'g on our piazza. I went to P.O. etc. at 8. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 20, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot, hot, hot. The drought is now beginning to work damage. I went out on my wheel for a few minutes before 8 & 8 — but made very unsatisfactory progress, the roads are so hopelessly bad & always on an up grade. I wrote a note or two — went down town twice & spent some time taking measurements & notes in the attic, preparatory to finishing the bedrooms there. In the afternoon, being overcome with sleep, I took a long nap — a useless waste of time & a detriment to my night's rest.
