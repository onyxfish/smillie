## Left Page

### Sat. June 1, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another terrifically hot day - Ther. down in the streets where people had to walk, stood anywhere from 95° to above 100°. After bkfp. I went with Jim to drug store \+ got a preparation with which to spray his nose. Then I bought a hat - then to Century \+ left a note for H. R. Butler - then to Allen's in 49th S. \+ had a little work done on my teeth - then to 2° Nat. Bk. \+ made June deposits - then to Barrey \+ Walls \+ I saw Adams \+ Kellogg about buying Govt Bonds nearly mature with N.A. money - then to see Lee - T. W. Wood just ahead of me - Nicole came in soon after - Needed for two 23° S. houses was signed \+ then Mr. Lea \+ Nicole went to N.A. to attach the seal - I went to Charles office \+ read papers until 12.30 - then again to Lee's where, after more formalities D. Rac. from Met. Life Co's lawyer \+ a certified check for $345,000. It was now 1.30 - Wood went his way - Nicole \+ I went up town by Elevated R.R. - I getting out at 23° S. - went to N.A. \+ put check in safe there - then home soon after 2 - lunch - then a rest - a severe thunder storm passed south of the city at about 4 - but little rain fell uptown - at 5 I went to butcher's \+ grocer's - then to Century - met Butler \+ talked with him until 6.30 - then home to dinner at 7 oey - It was too hot to go to regular monthly meeting of Century club - so I sweltered at home in undress - Charles came in —

## Right Page

### Sun. June 2, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another frightfully hot day - I did not go outside of the house - Bkfp. at 9 - after that reading newspaper for awhile - then got to work assorting \+ arranging many papers concerning N.A. business - State affairs - \+ the new studio to be put up in 36th S. - that have been without much order, in \+ about the writing desk at home. - I kept at it until near dinner time, then a short rest - Geo., Nellie, \+ their three boys came to dinner at 2 - Charles came in later - they stayed until near 5. - I wrote some notes in the afternoon \+ wrote up some old \+ incomplete N.A. reports, so that Farrington may enter them on the minutes. - I completed some work of the same kind in the evng. \+ got to bed soon after 10.
