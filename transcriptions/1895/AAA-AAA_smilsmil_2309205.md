## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 6, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'g., clouded p.m. with the edges of a couple of showers after 5 o'clk. I went down town in the m'g \+ saw Ja. P. Taylor \+ W'm. Cox, Sec'y of Borough Council. No action was taken last night, altho' I had asked for a vote, on the question of meeting expert advice upon the sewerage question. The reports made to me of the discussion in council showed so much pig-headed ignorance that I have determined to give up the fight. Montrose may solve the sewerage problems now as she pleases. I have other more interesting work to do. Got a letter from Maynard concerning Studios in 38th S. which I answered. After dinner, I transplanted lettuce until 4. Then a shower came up. Mrs. Mulford \+ her son Roland, came bringing her Shetland pony "Lorna Doon" which S have hired for the boys. Before 6 we went down to Barneys with the pony to arrange for a harness. Home in a shower. Very Mrs. Paul Sayre \+ Mr. Frank Bentley called.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 7, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A number of showers fell during last night. Pleasant day — afternoon cloudy. The day was a busy one for me between my routine duties. The work of looking after two carpenters who have reached a complex stage in the finishing of Jim's bed room. The fitting out of Lorna Doon with harness \+ saddle \+ the mending of the donkey cart. \+ the getting Sewerage of Borough in shape to appear in this weeks Republican — I went down town in the m'g. At 4.30 horses were at the door. Mary, Katie \+ Wallie \+ Katy \+ Bessie T. \+ the two boys went with me on a ride — no dust — no blazing sunshine \+ pleasant air made it very agreeable — but I was almost used up by a headache that had bothered me all day. I took no supper \+ went to bed at 9.
Gretta ret. from her vacation.
