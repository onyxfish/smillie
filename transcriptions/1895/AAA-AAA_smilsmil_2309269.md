## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 16, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another bright & pleasant day. To studio soon after 9. Got my acid room ready for work & bit in a second series of lines over background of the portrait — was unfortunate enough to tear the ground of darkest portions, biting off the surface. I took the rest of them & for me to right the trouble by running over it with graver. To H.R. Butler's at 1. to lunch. we had a hurried time as he had an engagement down town at 2.30. Mr. B. is very much interested in this N.R. scheme. I ret'd. to studio before 3 & worked as long as I could see. It was after 4 when Butler came in & stayed for half an hour. I was quite used up by an unusual sort of a headache which continued thro' the eve'g. so that I did not go to Cornical meeting.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 17, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day. I attended to my usual duties & then went to H.R. Butler's by appointment — near 11 when I got there. We went very carefully over p'ps of 1st Com. Report and worked until 1 o'clk. Then Mrs. Butler served a very nice lunch. After it I walked down to Putnam's stopping at Dielman's studio — not in. Left the M.S. corrected at Putnam's — Then to the Schaus Gallery to see Caton Woodville's painting "1815". Could not enthuse over it. Then to studio getting there pretty late Eve'g. wrote a note to H.R.B. giving him some later points.
