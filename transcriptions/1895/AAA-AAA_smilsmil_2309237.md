## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 11, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. fell to 31° last night — Very fine day. I scurried about + got considerable work of one kind + another done before 10 o'clk. Then I went down town + on my return to studio I took two prints from my Genl. Lee Portrait Etching: it looks quite as well as I expected but not so well as I had hoped. I shall now leave it to prepare for our move to N.Y. Dinner — Then a clearing up of the mess following printing. After 3 I went out on my wheel with R- \+ his pony. The beast behaved very badly but R. was not thrown altho' very timid — back in less than an hour. Gill \+ Tyler were fussing about on odd jobs — \+ Warner \+ an assistant were painting — Along + very interesting letter was rec'd from Charl., addressed to Kate — at Coblentz when he wrote. I wrote to Mary Cook — Geo. Cook \+ to Hugh Douglass — Went to P.O. at 8½ p.m. The rest was wasted in various ways.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 12, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced falling about 6 a.m. \+ continued, at times quite heavily, until noon — then it cleared partially for a while, thickening up again before night with more rain. My time was spent mainly in clearing up, gathering things together preparatory to leaving, \+ writing some notes etc — I went down town in the m'g at about 11, \+ again at 8 p.m. when, altho' it was raining, Ralph went with me.
