## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 31, 1895

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast mtf.; rain at noon which increased until at night it fell heavily. At 9 o'clk the carpenter came for me \+ we went to the studio \+ I explained to him how I wished to have my closets finished, etc. etc. He is to send a man to-morrow — also heater man came, to see about changing location of one of the radiators — he is to come to do the work to-morrow — also Lord \+ Taylor's man to measure the windows for shades. After I got through with them I went to Union Transfer Co. with shipping receipt for stuff freighted from Montrose. Home to lunch at 1. \+ back at 2 — passed about — of some of the boxes \+ cases that I packed when I left the cor. of Fourth Ave. \+ 23d S. in spring of '92. \+ plastered some of the closet accommodations required in closets for such mistakes in the afternoon Farrington came with checks [illegible] to see. Dinner at 6.30 — just before dinner freight from Montrose came \+ I unpacked a rug — after dinner I opened barrels of apples, pickled beans \+ canned tomatoes etc. — Jim went to a Hallow'e'en party given by one of his school [illegible] \+ was out until 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 1, 1895

**Weather:** Brilliant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day — The carpenter came for me almost as soon as I had finished my bfst. I went to studio with him and the mtf. was spent fitting cases into a closet. I went to a plumbers supply establishment in W. 37th St. where P.F. Ryan was given a very good character. A good Report was also Rec. thro' an agency by kindness of Katz, the butcher. Home to lunch at 1 — at studio drawing plans \+ straightening things until near 5. Then to Lord \+ Taylor's about shades — Home before 6.30 — Evett. went to Mary Cook's about butter \+ dancing — Men home and hung pictures \+ curtains in library until 11 —
