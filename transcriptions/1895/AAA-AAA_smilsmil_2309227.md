## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 21, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Exceedingly hot — very little breeze. 78° when I went to bed at night and with every window, & the blinds, open all night, the Ther. stood at 74° in the m'f. I put in a good day's work on my Gent. Lee etching. I drew in every thing except the face, back-ground — coat, hair and beard — after getting a tip-top transfer from my gelatine tracing — I am disappointed in my work. I had hoped to do something somewhat different from my former formal work, but, in spite of me, it is the same old stuff. After tea I went down to P.O. etc — on my return wrote part of a letter & then had a quiet time on piazza until 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 22, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still horribly hot — Bkfp. before 9 — then I devoted myself to letters when it had been gathered and cared for, I took some to Florence & more to the Christiansens where I spent the rest of the m'f chatting with Mr. & Mrs. C — It was 1 o'clk when I got home. Dinner at 2 — lounged for some time trying to find a cool spot & get a little nap. Then wrote to Mr. [illegible] Lee & Jim fixing Jim's fishing rod and Ralph's boat — then a little bicycle ride on the lawn before tea — ev'f. wrote to Mr. Walton.
