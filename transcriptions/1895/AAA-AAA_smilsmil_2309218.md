## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 3, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Much warmer - a wonderfully beautiful day & brilliant moonlight night wind from S. & S.E. all day. At last I have commenced some studio work - I was looking over some of my foreign pen sketches yesterday & one, the ascent to the Pincian Hill from the Piazza Popolo" took my attention, presenting some new suggestions in the way of composition & treatment - So, to-day I commenced a sketch in charcoal upon a piece of tinted Jalaune paper. We had a full dinner table, The Misses Leverett & Miss Harris came in from Silverlake, friends of the three girls, took dinner & tea with us - In the eve'g the young folks all went to a "candy pull" at the Sayre's with the intention of driving over to Silver Lake later in the eve'g with the three young ladies and getting back here (that is The T-girls & Mabel Cooly) some time before 11.15 - I got a note from deGrand Benedict in the eveg. sending a new note to take the place of one in 2d. Nat. Bk. That eff[or]ces to-morrw - I sent it at once to the bank but am afraid it is too late.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 4, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

I was not able to get up in the mtg. I was so fevered & restless during the night that at about 2 A.M. I took 5 grs Phenacetrne. This knocked the fever down temporarily & I got a little sleep. but in the mg fever was up again - After dinner I sent for Dr. Gardiner who came & said that he thought my trouble was a digestive disturbance. A day of wretched discomfort, for which night brought no relief.
