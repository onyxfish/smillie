## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 25, 1895

**Weather:** overcast m[illegible], disagreeably raw, but clearing to pleasant & milder weather in the afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m[illegible], disagreeably raw, but clearing to pleasant & milder weather in the afternoon. I went to Grocer's market at about 11, & then to Walter's & sat sometime with him. He went thro' with a slight surgical operation yesterday & will have to keep his bed for a few days now. Lunch at 1. After that I went to Rogers, Peet & Co. with one of R's new shoes that already needs repairing — then to 2°. Nat. Bk. Then to Kurtz's — then to studio. I found it too hot there with no means of cooling the place except to open the windows. So I went home — with materials to begin my plans for studio in 2.36' S. A rest before dinner. W[illegible]. at 8 — Council meeting no. 1 D. A hot & disagreeable debate over signing the formal contract for sale to Mr. Ins. Co. Wood & Moran objecting like kicking mules because I have not yet effected a settlement with the holder of the $68,000 mtg that must be arranged before June 1 — The two transactions are entirely separate but W. & M. refused so to consider them, but at last I got a majority vote ordering that signatures & seal be attached. It was after 11 before I got away.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 26, 1895

**Weather:** Raw & overcast in the m[illegible] — pleasanter in the afternoon & milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw & overcast in the m[illegible] — pleasanter in the afternoon & milder. Immediately after bffis., after my usual duties I went down to 20 Nassau S. & saw Mr. W[illegible]. Lee concerning the Phillips m[illegible] sell[illegible] of D. & he relieved my mind by telling me that he had had conversations with W. P. & W. Ledyard (W. P.'s lawyer) in which he was distinctly assured that Mr. P. would be satisfied if the $68,000 could be as safely reinvested & any loss of interest, owing to lower prevailing rates, was made good. Then to studio & for a little while worked on sketch plan for my new studio to be built in 3.64' S. Then to Nat. & spent a little time looking at Water-Color Exbt. & then home to lunch. After that, feeling very heavy, I had a nap of nearly an hour. Then to studio & very nearly completed my sketch plans of the laying out of my new studio. Dinner at 6.30. Ev[illegible]. at NAA at 8 o'clk. Meeting of Site Com. in Council Rooms. All present but J. Q. A. Ward. We agreed upon a form of note to Tilden Trust asking for a place in the negotiations now going on looking to alliances with other corporations, harmonious \+ sympathetic. Then we heard Mr. Howard Russell Butler in presentation of his plan for a federation of art Societies — a grandly enlarged Fine Arts Soc[illegible] — to absorb the one in 57 St. & to spread out over adjacent territory in 5[illegible] S[illegible]. It is an exceedingly interesting proposition — a very well written paper \+ presented in an able manner. It was very well Rec[illegible]. Home about 11.
