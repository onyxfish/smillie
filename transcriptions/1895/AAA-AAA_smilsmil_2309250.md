## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 6, 1895

**Weather:** Faggy, but mild & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio before but neither the carpenter on the heater man turned up before noon. Ryan, the plumber came & took my order to set the gas meter. Then I went to Union Trans. Co, 21 [illegible] & left order with them to get my new standing press from D. L. & W. R. R. After lunch again to studio. The carpenter got my wooden acid tray or sink into its place & finished his work. The heater man did not finish. Mr. Hauck, the builder came in for a few moments. Home before 6. Even I took some things to the studio.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 7, 1895

**Weather:** Overcast — a sort of thin, mild fog  **Temp:** [illegible]

with a little coppery sunshine at times during the day. I got to studio about 9 — Gas men & heater man there. The latter got my Radiator moved & water on at about 3 p. m. Ryan & his man worked at making gas connections so that my meter may be set in the clear, all day. I spent some time in varnishing with asphaltum my sink in the acid room. In the afternoon I went to Sinner & Amende's to get Benzole, etc. & made more varnish on my return. Later P. J. Byrnes, the carpenter, came in & we had along Talk concerning the renting of the store. He wishes for desk room & would like to "chip in" with Ryan. Every. at about 9 I went to the Century hoping to see Nicole, but did not. I saw Legman, Dr. H. Vandyke, Walter Jones & Chas. Collins — Home at 10.30 — The girls went to Louise's to a little musical entertainment given for Her Bans
