## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 9, 1895

**Weather:** Cold, cloudy \+ windy with some light snow flurries.  **Temp:** [illegible]

No mechanics were at work about the house to-day altho' the work is not yet complete. I had trouble with the machinery immediately beneath the bed \+ consequently kept very quiet. I put in a long day's work on the Lee plate \+ at 4.30 lay down for a rest, but did not get much. Eve'g I wrote a letter to Farrington. Young Harry Beach called on the girls.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 10, 1895

**Weather:** Brilliant day.  **Temp:** fell last night to 28°

Very cold in the m'f., growing warmer towards eve'f. No carpenters about, but Warner \+ one of his men came \+ did some painting in the afternoon. Stone worked nearly all day. I put in another pretty good day's work on the Lee plate — altho' I still felt pretty seedy. Cha. Sayre came just as we finished our dinner \+ took Katherine on a long drive (East Brooklyn) \+ did not return until 8 o'clk. I went out for a little while at about 3 with R— pony \+ wheel. Eve'g loafing about with stupid headache.
