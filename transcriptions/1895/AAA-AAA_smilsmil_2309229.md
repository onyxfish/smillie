## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 25, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — at times cloudy — we had a dash of rain — thunderstorm passed north of us — At last the slater arrived \+ got to work preparing the slate. Bill \+ Peter worked, took down finish \+ large south gable \+ started to put up shingles in to place. — Ruan this helper got part of the 4" iron pipe to run up by me \+ the chimneys. Then the diggers having got the ditch down to the old drain construction, they transferred their labors to the ditch. — W. G. Parks came in \+ made a m\[illegible\] call. I went on \+ finished the biting of my dee head in spite of the confusion — After dinner I cleaned off the ground \+ varnish \+ felt quite encouraged. — The rest of the day was given to watching the men until 5.30 — then I went out for half an hour on my wheel — After tea I went down to P.O. at — Evening — usual duties \+ news paper \+ to bed at 10 —

Jim had an attack of indigestion at about 2 this A.M. — too many wild cherries etc. etc. — He did not dress until the middle of the mf and thus the day seemed rather limp.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 26, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant mf. — a smart shower with high wind \+ thunder bet. 2 \& 3 o'clk. \+ then clouds \+ wind. — My mf was almost wholly given up to the men about the house — the slater got started at his work upon the roof — eight mechanics working as busy as bees. — we had dinner earlier to let the boys go fishing with Mr. Bostwick at 1. — Before the rain stopped I went to the lake to look after them — found them by the ice house — all more or less uselessly wet \+ intensely interested in catching some very little fish along shore — after waiting around for 1/4 of an hour and Jim \+ Tom having gone off in the boat with B — started for home with Raefon — The shower made a great mess at home, wetting the men who had to get tar paper over the roof not decided \[illegible\] to shingles — also, a lot of fine dry shingles that were being put upon the front gable. After an hour \+ two the men got to work again. — Last — I wrote to Mr. Jno. Herbert Morse — to Harrington \+ Walter Cook \+ at 10 took my letters to the P.O.
