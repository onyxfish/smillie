## Left Page

### Fri. March 1, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

mary ret. to Po'k.

A very mild, muggy day — I did not go to studio in the m'f. but remained at home writing letters. It was about 11.30 when I went to 2° Nat. Bk from there to Kurtz's, where I learned that the 1871 negative of Annie had been destroyed — then to Savings Bk. of Merchants Clerks, 4th Ave. \+ 15th S. \+ had Jim's acct. there, standing in Annie's name as trustee changed to my name. Then home to lunch after that, more writing — I am very much behind with correspondence of all sorts. mary ret. to Po'k. by 3.30 train — At 4.30 I went to the Casino on invitation of Aronson, the manager, where I met a small party of artists to whom was given a Private View of Living Bronze Antiques — two men of splendid physique smeared with some golden bronze preparation and posing in the attitudes of well known antique statues — The effects were remarkably good. Then to Century where I sat for a while — Chas. Collins walked home with me.
Dinner at 6.30 — most of the evening goes by the time I get Rebekah to bed — after that some quiet writing.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 2, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day — rain in the m'f. changing to snow before noon — an attempt at clearing at 5 p.m. — I wrote for awhile after bfst. to grocers \+ butchers at 11 — on my way back went in to Walter's \+ stayed there talking with him until near 1 — then home to lunch — again writing letters \+ attending to estate business until about 4 — Then Kate arr° from Po'k — at 5 I went out \+ walked to Central Park \+ back. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. Charl came in for a little while bet. 8 \+ 9 — then left — I could not make up my mind to go to monthly meeting at Century, so I sat at home \+ read "The Prisoner of Zenda" until after 10 — New to bed.
