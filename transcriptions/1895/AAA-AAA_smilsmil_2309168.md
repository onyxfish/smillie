## Left Page

### Fri. May 24, 1895

**Weather:** Bright, very warm day with pleasant breeze  **Temp:**

Jim & I were down at 7.30 & had a short turn on our Bicycles before bkfs. at 8. — In the course of the day I made several efforts to get at my work of writing out my views of the Butler scheme for presentation at Site Com. meeting on Sat. night — but, for some reason, to my great disgust, my head wouldn't work worth a cent. — At last I tried to take a nap, but couldn't succeed even at that — Lunch at 1. — I tried to write on my paper again but my head is in an muddle — at last I went out with Jim at about 3 o'clk & had a very good Bicycle Ride. — His cyclometer said about 4 miles — I came in tired & very wet from perspiration — so, after a rest I had a sponge bath & a change of under clothes — Jim & I took a short walk on Whitney ave. before dinner at 6.30 — Recd. some callers — but I did not see them — they left at 9 & we chatted until 10 —
Nealie in bed all day with one of her horrible headaches.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 25, 1895

**Weather:** Hazy & warm  **Temp:**

I spent the morning quietly finishing the paper that I expect to read to the Site Com. This evg. Lunch at 1 — I took Trolley car just before 2 & rode to Station & to N.Y. on 2.30 train, getting to 42d. St. near 5 — 20 m. behind time. — After leaving my things at the house, I went to Century hoping to see either Butler or Nicoll — & saw neither — Home soon after. Dinner at 6.30 — soon after 8 went to Century & had to wait until 9 before 5 of the Com. could be got together. J.Q.A. Ward did not put in an appearance at all. — I think he could not make up his mind which way to vote — so, shirked. — I had a revised opinion on my idea — at length — very guarded but, after all, really favorable to the plan of Butler's. We discussed for a long time making no headway — Wood is a perfect blockhead — Brown does not comprehend the plan & is swayed by sentiment & prejudice — A vote was taken a little before midnight — 3 in favor of recommending the plan to the N.A., 2 agst. it. — Then we adjourned, subject to call — I went home thro' a light rain & got to bed very tired at 12.30.
