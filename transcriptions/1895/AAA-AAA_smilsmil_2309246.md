## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 29, 1895

moved into new Studio
156 E. 36' S-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - cloudy a part of the time
To Na. p.s. - at 10 - met Walter + we walked
down together. Union Transfer Co's van came
up at same time - Got hoisting table out
it + then to 36". S- - for a time I thought it
w'd be impossible to get that table up the
winding stair having such low head room
but after considerable damage to ceiling
\+ woodwork it was accomplished. Then
to house + got my studio furniture from
It was just about 12 o'clk when we got
to studio with our load + 1 o'clk when it
was deposited up stairs, after a little necessary
general damage. Home + had lunch
at 1.30. After that Jos. P. Ryan, plumber
came - evidently anxious to hire the store
under my studio - He had a place in the
old building - He is willing to pay the
rent asked, but would sublet part if the
I would not give him a decided answer.
Then I again went to studio + arranged
things a little - Dinner at 6.30 after
having worked nearly an hour adjusting
my bicycle into shape - At 8 went to Walter's
for a few minutes - After my return home
I strayed into the store room that is in
process of being cleaned - Among the shelves
I found a box containing desk + a number
of letters + cent in central interest that had been
shoved by Minnie - Among them several
from me - my proposals + several immediately
following - a very sad reawakening.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 30, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another pleasant day - at 8.40 went to
Walter's + together we went to studio and
looked over matters there - after he left
I went to grocer's + butcher's - Mary came
to studio + spent an hour talking with me
about the situation in the house. Gretta
is "in her year" + demands more wages. M-
pacified her for the time by promising her
another dollar a month - Next, there
will be a row with Katie the cook -
Then went to Douglass - to Manhattan Storage
to Masincosen + Underray Hill banks - At the
banks I enquired concerning the financial
standing of Jos. P. Ryan - Then to Kyle + Son -
Home to Church, which was much belated
after 1.30 - Then to 2d Nat. Bk. + drew
some money - Then to Lord + Taylors, + gave
orders to have a man sent to studio to measure
for shades, etc - Then to studio where
I found Manhattan Storage van + men writing
from 3.30 until nearly 5 - At work moving
the remainder of my studio possessions
into my new studio - Then home and
had a bit before dinner - At 8 I went
to The Century + there met Nicoll, + later,
Dielman - We talked N.A. matters
\+ particularly, Com. on Site business
until 10 - then I went home.
