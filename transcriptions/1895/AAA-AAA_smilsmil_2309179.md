## Left Page

### Sat. June 15, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day \- breezy + refreshing
altho' warm. Bkfp at 7.30 + after
that a day of general work in getting
things into order \- Two women were at
work all day + a third for half a day
in general cleaning + scrubbing \-
I went down town in the m'g +
again in the aftn every \- considerable
time in the m'g spent in discussing
with some of the towns folks the matter
of Town Sewering \- I am disgusted
to find that in spite of my efforts
nothing has been done yet. Dinner
at 7.30 \- Tea before 7 \- much of
the ev't downtown \- To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 16, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warmer + stiller \- but yet very lovely.
Every loveliness has a point of anguish
in its reawakening of memories of dear
Annie. The songs of the birds are full
of her \- the brightness of the sunshine + the
coolness of the shade bring back to me her
intense vitality + love of life \- in the house
every corner mutely tells of her absence. Her
share of the bed room is all mine now +
my heart is like to burst at times as I
look at her empty place.

The day was spent in putting things
in place in studio + bed room \-
\+ in going about the grounds and
seeing what has been done \- what
has been left undone \- + what
must be done \-

Transplanting lettuce in the
ev'g.      To bed early.
