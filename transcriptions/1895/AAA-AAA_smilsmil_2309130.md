## Left Page

### Sun. March 3, 1895

**Weather:** Pleasant day \- mild & quiet \- late 4 hfs.

It was after 11 when, with Ralph and Louise, I started out for a walk \- we went to the Central Park \- took a look at the animals \- then to 72d. St. \+ back by way of 5th Ave., stopping at the Century to see the paintings there \- Home to dinner at 2 \- after that, Charl came in \+ I spent a couple of hours with him on business matters \- Then Mr. \+ Mrs. Saml. Colman came in, \+ then Grace Ireland \- Then I walked over to Geo. Cook's to get some papers signed by him \- not in \- home to tea at 6.30 \- Charl came in again for a few minutes. Then I read a portion of "the Prisoner of Zenda" \- got to be so much interested that I took no note of time until 11.30 \- then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 4, 1895

**Weather:** Dull \+ disagreeable day, opening pleasantly \+ closing with rain.

It was 11 before I got to studio. I was engaged at some writing when Nicoll came in \+ we talked N.A. matters for nearly an hour \- then home to lunch \- after that, busy for some time \- then rested for an hour \- then to N.A. to studio \+ to Am. Art Galleries to see the Abbey pictures \- There met Mr. Grear who told me that a niece of A.H. Green wished like to see me concerning an etched portrait of her uncle \- Caught in the rain on my way home to dinner. Well went to Council meeting \- nothing interesting \- home at about 11 \- Blashfield walked up with me. Geo was not out, owing to a bad cold.
