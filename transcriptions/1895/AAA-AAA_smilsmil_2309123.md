## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 17, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — brilliant, quiet + mild. Got started in the mf. a little earlier than usual — bkfs. at 9 — but it was not until 12 that Ralph + I went out — we met Charl just outside of the Union league Club + went in there + spent an hour with the early American portraits and the collection of Modern Posters — a fad of to-day that is amusing if not taken too seriously. At 1 o'clk. R. + I walked up the Avenue to the Park + then home getting in a little before dinner at 2. again, in the afternoon, after 4, I took R. up to 5th Yale's + left him with Roy to play while I went on to 59th S. + around to 5th Ave. + 55, where I called upon Mrs. M. S. Beach + her two daughters Ella + Viola — after 20 m. there I got R. + set home just in time for tea at 6.30. Svely, Geo. + Charl came in —

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 18, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of streaks — sunshine + grey — mild + raw weather. It was after 11 before I got to studio, having been to Bank + Safe Deposit. Gave the rest of the mf. to planning some small matters for Montrose. To n.a. eyem a few minutes, then home to lunch. Back to studio + resumed work of the mf. At 5 went down to Wunderlich Gall. + looked at the drawings there by J. Alden Weir — They are simply idiotic met Jeffaw there. Charl came in, my unexpectedly, + dined with us, leaving before 8. Evelf. I went to Council meeting, getting home shortly before 11.
