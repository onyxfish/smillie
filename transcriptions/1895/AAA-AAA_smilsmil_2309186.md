## Left Page

### Sat. June 29, 1895

**Weather:** Dull & muggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull & muggy - I devoted my day to working out of doors, most of the time over my lettuce beds, + to helping Herrick sizing the duck that is to cover the walls of Kate's + Jim's bed rooms in the attic.
It was nearly 5 o'clk. p.m. when I went out in the road on my wheel. Rough as ever, but I think I am gaining in knack & strength in getting up grades. I went out on the Snake Creek, or Binghampton Road, not more than a mile + a half. I came in after 6 pretty well pumped + wet from head to heels. I rested awhile + then took a sponge bath + had my tea after the others. Mr. Calp. Cromwell + daughter called — I went down town + did not get back until 9. — Then chatted with uncl. W. — until 10 + to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 30, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day mostly given over to thunder storms, + which a very good specimen passed over us at noon — my a.m. was spent in the garden. At p. with Jim, umbrellas + overshoes, I went to Presb. Ch. for uncl. W. + Aunt A. Mary, Kate + Bessie — but they got Wm. Cay + one of his teams + rode home. Dinner after 2. The afternoon was muggy with a few rays + sunshine. Thunderstorms about us + at about 6 a gentle rain set in that continued far into the night. I wrote part of a letter to S. R. Koehler. A quiet family even + to bed early.
