## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 19, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day — growing warmer — part cloudy — part sunshine of hazy, Indian summer quality — A big slice was taken out of my m't by Miss Eva Lee of Brooklyn who wishes to be an artist. Before dinner I took a short ride on my wheel — Afternoon, I continued my work of preparations to commence the Gen'l Lee portrait. The copper plate arr'd to-day — After 4 o'clk I seeded some bare spots on lawn north of the house — Then I rode on my wheel out to the top of the hill on the Silver Lake \+ Friendsville Road \+ looked about there studying a "subject" that has been on my mind for a year or more. Home before 6 — a knot \+ rub down before tea — Jim \+ Tom went on their wheels to Harris Pond to do some fishing — They saw some monstrous fish but did not catch them — After tea — to Post Office — an interesting letter from Charles to Mamy — Mr. Cromwell \+ her daughter spent a part of the even with us.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 20, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot, uncomfortable day — the Ther. getting above 80° with very little breeze. I put in a good long days studio work on Lee portrait etching — In the m'g I laid a ground \+ made a tracing on gelatine — Afternoon, I scraped the tracing — wet down paper, \+ had a job in getting my printing press in order, it is so very long since any work was done upon it. At last I got everything ready to take my transfer of tracing about which I feel quite nervous. I went down town before noon \+ saw Shaner, the wagon maker about mending the pony cart that Tom broke — Then I hunted, without success, for a man to do some digging for me. Every so very warm that I did not go away from the house — wrote part of a letter to uncle Willie — Then dreamed on the piazza until the family ret'd from bidding Mr. \+ Miss Cromwell good bye — they leave to-morrow m'g.
