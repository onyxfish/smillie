## Left Page

### Thur. May 30, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*[written above header: Ret. to N.Y. with Jim + Ralph.]*

A very warm day, but with pleasant breeza. I went out + had a short ride on my bicycle before bkft. At 10 Mr. Bennett came, Roger had a wheel so they two, Mollie A., Jim + I started out for a ride. I left them away out on Whitney Ave. + Ret. to the Park Roads where I had had such a good time yesterday. I Rode until after 11, then home had my sponge bath — a rest — + then I packed my valise — The Sup. man came at 4 + took valise + wheels. Kate T. has been prevailed upon to stay over until Monday — after lunch — at 2 o'clk — J.R. + I — bid our good-byes + left for the Station taking 2.30 main. After a very hot ride got to N.Y. Arrive at 4.30. + home as soon as we could get on wheels. Dinner before [illegible] every. I went to Lees Cooks. Walter there — Edw. + Mary go to Buffalo to-morrow every. — Home at 9.30 — Chare there. It was 11.30 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 31, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Frightfully hot day. awoke at about 4 o'clk + did not get to sleep again. After bkft. + my regular duties I went down town to Mr. Lee's + to Mr. Adams' — the latter at cor. of Bdway + Wall. Talked with him about the best way to dispose of the $340000 that I expect to get to-morrow. He introduced his partner, Mr. Kellagg — but no positive plan was suggested — my talk with Mr. Lee was prolonged until nearly 1 — then home, stopping to get some ice cream for lunch + a gr. of Strychininie for the Crow who was soon relieved of his painful existance. After lunch I went to 2d. Nat. Bk. + had an interesting talk with case, the cashier — He told me some stories about Farrington that adds to my certainty that he needs close watching — I do not suspect him of dishonesty but his methods of managing his bank business I do not like. Then I went to T.W. Woods' 51 W. 10. to arrange for the meeting at Lee's to-morrow — Men Telle + to Nicole to come down. Then to Johnson's Cycling School in 34th S? — then home —
Dinner before [illegible] — Every. Mr. [illegible] Lee called upon business for awhile — then Chare came in — the heat in the every was almost unbearable —
Mary went to Po. R. at 3 P.m.
