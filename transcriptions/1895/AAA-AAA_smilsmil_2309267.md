## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 12, 1895

**Weather:** Very cold windy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold windy day — cold appears to be somewhat better. To studio at 9.30 — Coburn, the man who has been at work in studio cellar for more than a week put in his bill over $94.— I paid him $90. Then I went down town — 2 Nassau St. \+ paid $28.— \+ with $9.60 scrip got a $1000 Sun. Arb. R.R. bond. back to studio — home to lunch at 1 — To studio at 2 — worked on dee hier more than two hours. Maynard came in \+ gave me an acct. of what was done at the Academy meeting last night. Home to dinner before 6. Eve'g wrote a letter to H.R. Butler —

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 13, 1895

**Weather:** Very cold, overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold, overcast \+ disagreeable. To studio at 9.30 — Very soon went to 2. Nat Bank \+ attended to some business in the Safe Deposit — Then to Putnam's and arranged concerning Printing N.A. [illegible] Com. Report. — Then to studio before noon \+ until 1 worked on dee plate. To lunch at 1 — back at 2 \+ on dee plate — Dr. Gerster came in \+ spent an hour with me. Dinner before 7. Eve'g wrote to E. Wood Perry — also to Margaret Parker White, the death of whose husband, on the 11th, at Redlands Cal. is in to-days paper.
