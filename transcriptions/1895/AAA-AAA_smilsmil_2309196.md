## Left Page

### Fri. July 19, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very hot, cloudless day, tempered by a breeze. About 10 o'clk. I encountered Herrick in the garden picking early beans \+ I told him that he was no longer entitled to garden privileges — that as he had discharged himself from my employ he could occupy the house only by my pleasure. Of course he was much incensed \+ talked about oppression of the poor by the rich. I sent Spence to his house with a check paying him to 2 o'clk. Tues. \+ deducting rent until last Sat. night. Warner came in the m'f \+ I spent most of the time with him superintending the painting in the attic. I wrote to J. S. Case \+ Geo. S. Cook. out to lake \+ back before dinner — again with Warner. To P. O. on wheel at 5 o'clk — then out to lake \+ a little way beyond. Got in at about 6. very hot — a sponge bath \+ change \+ late to tea. Did not go to P. O. out on piazza until near 10. an hour or more Charley Dayre there with the girls.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 20, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Tremendously hot day closing with a refreshing thunder shower at 5.30 o'clk. After my usual m'f duties I got to work upon drawings for Katie's bedroom attic. I am stuck over the involved lines resulting from the slope of the roof \+ so far, have been unable to extricate myself. After dinner it was so hot as to make work a great trial and when the storm came it was too dark, consequently little progress was made. Ev'f. went down town to P. O. \+ on my return, until 10, read newspaper.
