## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 17, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An Indian-summer day — warm + air brilliant with hazy color — I did some work in studio — still n crayon study of approach to Cincian Hill — I also wrote a letter to Farrington of N.A. in which I took occasion to reiterate my instructions to him concerning his work. After dinner I read awhile — at a little after 4 I went out on my wheel accompanying Bessie — we took the little 4 mile ride out back of "Little Depot" — then I continued on down-town to P.O. etc. Then home + had a change + rest before tea. Eve'g, down-town again soon after 8 —

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 18, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced falling at about 4 A.m. + continued until 7 — at times, very heavy — Thunder + lightning accompaniment — The rest of the w'f. overcast + threatening — a heavy thunderstorm passing to the North at noon — very warm, muggy, sunny breezy P.m. Thunderstorms about at night. Morning, I wrote a letter to Emma Bonnie, setting forth at length what I offer + what I require in the matter of a gardener, hoping that Ackert, uncle Willie's old gardener, would be willing to come here to take Herrick's place. Afternoon, I worked upon a sketch head of Gent. Rob't. E. Lee — Also, set out some lettuce plants. Went down-town at about 5 + back at 6.30 — Eve'g, quite a gay time — commencing with Bessie's birthday cake — Later, Kate Parker Riley — her eldest daughter — her niece Mr. Holmes + her son + daughter — Mary Sayre came in for a few minutes — The matter of art education forgives was discussed.
