## Left Page

### Mon. March 25, 1895

meeting at Andrew Carnegie's.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More mixed weather - Fair in the mf - Rain by 11 o'clk. \+ clear in the early P.m. I was almost completely used up by a headache but managed to keep about. Jim \+ Ralph both under the weather with severe colds, Sent J. to Dr. Y - bfore school - R. has some fever - Y. came to the house to see him. Katey Adams called in the mf. Immediately after bkfp. I went to N.A. upon business - after my return home the mf was divided between writing Katey A. \+ Dr. Y. Lunch at 1. Then I gave up \+ spent the P.m. in bed until 4.30 Then I went to Andrew Carnegie's - 5 W. 51 S - where the Site Com. met him at his request. The full Com. was there - \+ besides, H.W. Butler \+ Mr. Edw. Adams, Banker - The meeting was a very important one - engineered by Butler - Mr. Carnegie has taken hold of B's scheme of federation of art societies in a business way while maintaining unimpaired autonomy in all purely art matters - The discussion on the part of Carnegie was spirited \+ very positive - Mr. Adams spoke in favor of the scheme as a financeer - Letters favorable, were read from J.G. Marquand \+ E.S. Smith - \[B- The Com. asked questions, but were rather cold - in spite of Carnegie's efforts, did not in any way commit themselves\] - At last, as 6.30, Carnegie said that if the Com. would approve the scheme he would from a syndicate to buy \+ hold the lots for the Academy, he taking the risk of a final approval of the scheme by the N.A. \+ the Fine Arts Socs. Man we left. 7 o'clk. when I got home to dinner - Ever Council meeting — there until 10.30 -

## Right Page

### Tues. March 26, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

March-like day - high wind \+ lots of dirt. Took R. to school which has resumed in its quarters in the basement of Mr. Jones's house in Mad. Ave. To N.A., transacted business - saw Ward \+ Turner about being at meeting on Thurs. eve\+ Site Com. - Then to City Hall with Annie's will for another attestation required by the Ries Register - getting into a muddle there I went to her \+ sea's [illegible] the will there - Then home to lunch - after that to Keppe, to see some etchings there - Then to Weildon's in W. 15\+ S - \+ I spent some time there talking of Site Com. - meeting \+ art educational matters Then to H. Mowbray Siddons in 11\+ 82 \+ to Wm. Chase's 57 W. 10\+ S - \+ to J. Alden Weir's 11\+ 2 - none of them in - then to Walters' Cor. 18\+ S - \+ Bderky \+ I spent some time over the plans for studio in 36\+ S - Edw. Couk came in \+ Income tax was discussed for some time - then at 6 - we walked up town together - Immediately after dinner Charl came in with his reed wheel, much to the delight of the boys - I wrote half a dozen notes to various N.A.'s inviting them to the meeting of the Site Com. - on Thurs. eve\+ to discuss art School matters - Charl having got his dinner Returned later \+ stayed until near 11 -
