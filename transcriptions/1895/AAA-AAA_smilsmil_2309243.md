## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 23, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katie (the cook) \+ Gretta left for N.Y.

Beautiful day, altho' cold \+ rather windy. Up a little earlier than usual as Katie \+ Gretta started for N.Y. on the 7.50 train. They are to get the house in some readiness for us, we to follow in two or three days. I had a very busy day at work upon all sorts of jobs. Before noon I went to Beach's foundry \+ got a preparation to put upon iron to protect it vs. Rust — then to R.R. station \+ got tickets for us. Geo. Woodruff came from Cooley's \+ crated our three bicycles. I worked in etching some, smearing my Press \+ all other iron work liable to Rust, with the preparation got at Beaches — about 5 went down town with R — back at 6. Mrs. Rhinevalt now presides in the kitchen \+ under Mary's direction she does very well. Felt stupid at night \+ got to bed early.
Mrs. Mulford sent for "Loma" to-day.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 24, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day. Up early \+ made fires \+ started in on a good day's work. I had hoped that we would be able to get away to-morrow, but soon found that that would be impossible unless I made a greater effort than I was willing to. The weather is so temptingly pleasant that it is no hardship to stay over. Mary \+ the girls appear to be entirely satisfied \+ Mrs. Rhinevalt is able to make us comfortable. I have been able to clear up things more thoroughly by delaying our start for another day. Eve tf, beautiful moonlight altho' cold. Ch. Sayre \+ Hy Beach came \+ begged persistently to be allowed to take Katie \+ Bessie out riding, the geese. Bessie has a very bad cold \+ has been almost in bed from it, but she added her begging. Mary allowed Katie to go for a short time.
