## Left Page

### Thur. June 27, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very satisfactorily wet day \- the rain falling very gently at first \+ then coming down in steady showers \- I gathered my vegetables in the rain after bkft. cut 30 heads of lettuce \+ then took them to my neighbors \- went down town \+ spent most of what remained of the day in trying to develop a practical opposition to the erection of Telephone poles on Public Ave. They should be placed in the back alleys \- Dinner about 1 \- again went down town but found that the only opposition to the fore nuisance was empty talk. A quiet eveg. in doors \+ to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 28, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day \- clearing very warm \- I spent my spare time in superintending \+ helping Henrick stretch \+ size the canvas that is to be used in the finishing of the attic bed rooms. I Rec'd. a letter from S. R. Kohler who is now in London evidently very low in spirits because his physical condition has not improved \+ he cannot do the work that he hoped to do \- I commenced a reply at once but did not finish it. At 5 I went out on my wheel \- found the roads too muddy. So I went to the Fair Gr. Track \+ Rode four miles \- then home \+ had a bath \+ change \- Mary, Kate, the girls, Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. went out riding from 4 o'clk. to 6.30 \+ enjoyed themselves very much \- After tea I went down town \- Eveg. \- Mr. Mrs. Shasfer called.
