## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 26, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The nasty, sleety snow-storm of last night continued until afternoon when it cleared seriously. Dr. Yale came early & finding Annie's symptoms yet more grave he went at once to the U League Club & got Dr. Charl. while at his H.pt. He impressed him into service & he went first, & got a Cylinder of Oxygen which was administered to Annie at short intervals - Then he went to S. Duke's Hospital for a night nurse to relieve Miss Strickland After considerable driving about he got Miss Mattson & brot' her to the house. Yale spent much of the nt & the house and coming again in the eveg. he stayed all night. It was done, however, in such a way that I did not take immediate alarm. He stretched himself upon the parlor sofa saying that he would wait to see the effect of a certain drug administered & then he addressed me to go to bed which I did. Jim came home from New Rochelle after 6. He went at once to Walter Cook's where arrangements had been made for him to stay all night.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 27, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

my Annie died.

A bright beautiful day to this part of our world but to me the darkest day of my life. I was awakened at 5 AM, by hearing the Dr. leave the house after having stayed all night. Altho' this alarmed me I did not get up until 7 - then I went to Annie's Room & found that a very serious change for the worse had taken place - a dropsical condition of the lung. Yale soon came & did not leave us until all was over. Dear Annie was evidently much exhausted, her breathing exceedingly short & labored with the rattling sound so dreadful to be remembered. She was perfectly conscious & bright & brave as only she could be. She gave me many expressions of her love & never gave way to fear. Harley and Jim before the end. She said that she did not despair, altho' I did. She kissed me, putting her arms about my neck & said that she hoped to live to kiss me many times more. Afterwards Miss Strickland told me that during the nt at a time when I was out of the room, Annie told her that she fanned that she was going to be defeated. Half an hour before the end, as she lay in a half comatose condition, I asked her if she knew me. She opened her eyes, as tho' awakening from sleep, & looking at me with a smile she said "of course I do —" Her smile broadening, she added "Do you know me?" Her last articulate words were "Where am I going — where — where?" At 4 o'clk. she did not respond to my cry & at 4.20 her respiration ceased without a struggle & the sweetest, purest life I have ever known went out.

Yale was a bro. & a sister to me. The Cooks & the Irelands were all in the house, & I had abundant sympathy, but to me it was all as nothing. Bro. Charles has been exceedingly kind & George came in in the eveg.
