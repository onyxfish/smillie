## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 16, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot day with very little breeze to mitigate it. Ther. got up to 86°. Just before sunset superb cumulus clouds in the east were lighted up most brilliantly making a spectacle of much grandeur — we all left the tea table to gaze at it \+ watch the rapid changing of form \+ color. I spent most of the day at work upon drawings for dormer windows \+ at last got them finished — much to my relief. After tea R— \+ I went down town. All hands went to bed very early \+ I read newspaper until 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 17, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy w't. with much humidity \+ high wind — Soon after b'f'st I suggested that as there might be a change in the weather soon we had better take a bicycle ride that I had planned some time ago. Only Katey \+ Mascie A — assented \+ at about 10 we started — went out on Alvord road to near Tiffany's — then to right, over the hill to Babcock's \+ then in by little Depot a ride of just 7 miles — when in the old Depot R— about 2 miles from town, we overtook Jim \+ Ralph \+ the pony \+ cart — Then we had a run in together, the boys \+ the pony entering into the race with great zest. We got home at 12 having enjoyed our ride very much — a good rest before dinner at 1. The afternoon cleared brightly \+ was very hot in spite of the wind — I wrote letters \+ went down town about 5. Thunderstorms on the western horizon. Evg. on piazza then reading newspaper. Heavy shower during the night.
