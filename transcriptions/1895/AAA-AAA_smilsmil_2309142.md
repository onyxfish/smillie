## Left Page

### Wed. March 27, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, windy, + consequently, dirty day. After my regular domestic duties + the m.f. I wrote for a little while, then to Tiffany Glass Co. with the two broken front door panels + spent the m.f. then Sausdoris - also I saw Colman + his bro.-in-law Mr. Nottingham - I looked over many sketches of color schemes + made a number of very crude pencil sketches about which I shall consult with Joni. at another time. Then to N.A. for a moment - then home to lunch - After that Belle + Mary Tweedy called - + Jimmy Kimball. At 4. with Jim + Ralph I went to Dr. Alden's in 49. St. + we all three had our teeth examined - made an appointment with him for Sat. p.m. at 2.30 - there we took a walk - Dinner at 6.15 - about 7 I took the boys to Mr. Wickhams on Lex. Ave, that they might see the Barnum, Bailey Circus Procession from her windows. They returned at about 8 o'clk. Evely I went to the N.A. + was much pleased on hearing Mr. Blashfield's lecture before the students upon the Renaissance of Italian Decorative art, with screen enlargements of photos - out at 9.30 walked up with bro. Geo.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 28, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - The day went much as most of the days go now in a desultory sort of work, domestic matters - estate business + N.A. affairs - always a little behind hand because I am so slow.

Frank Waller came at about noon + I had a half hour or more with him that was very painful to me. I am getting into morbid condition, mentally, making me at times more sensitive + unhappy than ever - I must fight it. During the afternoon I tried to get some rest to prepare me for the evelf when the N.A. Com. at N.A. listened to a general discussion up on the question: What sort of Schools shall the new Academy have S. McWard. C.Y. Turner, Frank Jones, Maynard + Alden Weir + Carole Beckwith + Wards with Thompson were present - on the whole, the discussion was interesting but the divergence of opinion among the men who had been educated in the same schools was astonishingly great - It was impossible to formulate anything coherent, altho the discussion was kept up actively until midnight - I was thoroughly worn out.
