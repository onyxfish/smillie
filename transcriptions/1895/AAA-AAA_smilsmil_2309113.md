## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 31, 1895

**Weather:** A bright day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright day. I determined to
return to the world of life as promptly
as possible. My great dread is that I may
not be able to rally — that I may break up
(down) in a cowardly manner \+ before un-
worthy of the love that Annie had for me and
of the unfinished work that she has left for
me. — I went to the Bank \+ Safe Deposit and
was much pained when Selleck of the safe
deposit refused to admit me into the safe —
permit me to open our box. With painful
formalities the officers of the J.D. opened the
box, took out Annie's will \+ sent it to the Sur-
rogate's office. Case, the Cashier of the Bank be-
haved very nicely \+ made very accommodating
arrangements with me. Then I went to the
studio \+ set to work trying to get my etching
exhibit ready, a space in the walls having
been left for me. I worked until 5 o'clk
then home to lunch — back to studio and
before 5 o'clk. I was able to get them ready
\+ take them to the N.A. My studio experience
to-day has been exceedingly depressing —
while alone, it was a constant \+ exhausting
struggle to control the character \+ direction
of my thoughts, the tendency being to a mor-
bid form of grief. I do not wish or expect
to repress grief, but I wish it to be healthy
\+ natural. With what admiration now do
I regard Annie's wonderfully normal and
healthy spirit — It lives before me now as a
stimulus \+ example.

Charles was much about, also the Cooks
\+ Irelands — Mary Ireland spent the day
at the house receiving the many callers.
I got a great number of notes of sympathy.
