## Left Page

### Fri. March 15, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day - raw, penetrating cold in the m'f. drizzle commencing before 11 which soon turned into a slatey snow which fell continuously, but lightly, until sometime in the night. Immediately after bkfs. I went to Fifth Ave Safe Deposit Co. by appointment. met Geo. Cook there. A transfer of the safe that Annie & I held was then made to us, Geo. Taking Annie's place as her Executor. After that I got home & during the rest of the day I busied myself with writing. Among other things I wrote a letter to J. C. Pafsencer, of The Accounting Trust Co. of Topeka, Kansas about his Reports concerning Annie's Kansas farm mtge & I sent him a check for $60. amt of his bill, I also, on same subject, got part through with a letter to Haun & Sprague, asking for his advice as to what I had better do in the matter. It is a bad business upon which I am unwilling to spend another dollar — about 6 P. M. Dr. Gale came in for a few minutes to enquire after Ralph who seems to be better to-day having responded to his medicine given yesterday. Satisfactonly.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 16, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast during a part of the m'f — then it cleared out was cold & windy — After bkfs. & my usual m'f duties I got to work at my writing again & made some progress but I am very slow. I got off my letter to Mr. Sprague & the four Pafsencer Reports concerning the Kansas mtge. Mrs. Johnson (né Lossing) called & got her four little framed etchings that she had in the last Club ex'b'n. Lunch at 1 — after which Kate & Katherine went to a Tannhauser Matinee — I was very much under the weather all the afternoon & eve'g owing to a sudden outbreak of indigestion — I kept very quiet but was far from happy.
