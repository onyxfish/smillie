## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 11, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beastly hot, dry, clear day \- Ther. ups to nearly 80° until after 10 p.m. On the whole I have felt better \+ stronger to-day. Most of my energies were given to my much behind hand correspondence. I rec'd a telegram from Geo. Cook stating that proceeds of sale of N.Y. C. \+ H.R. R.R. stock had been deposited to my c'o. in 2nd Nat. Buk. Then I sent check for $3000, with $11.50 int. to W. \& T. Lamb, builders of the 36th St. Studio. I also tackled the Pahseuor Kansas Farm puzzle \- wrote to Mr. P. \+ got part way thro' with letter to lawyer Lee. The elder Miss Riley \+ Miss Purdy of Troy dined with us to-day.

Eve'g, I wrote more.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 12, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some hopeful signs of a change in weather. \- Low barometer \- overcast skies \- but wind in wrong direction. Soon after b'f'st. Gill came with his horse \+ wagon \+ he took me to his house \+ showed me a lot of roofing slate that he has there \+ then we drove about town \+ looked at several roofs that he has slated \- we went to Mr. Christiansen's \- saw them \+ left lettuce with them \- then to Rev. H. L. Benton's where G. left me. Went with Mr. B. into the church \+ consulted as to the best way to reduce the metallic glitter of some stars on the wall back of pulpit \- he drove up to my studio with me \- I got colors \+ brushes \+ we rec'd to church where I worked until 12, showing Warren how to do it \- then W. B. took me home \- After dinner I wrote letters until 5 \- then I walked down town \+ back without sense of discomfort \- Eve'g in studio \- a quiet time reading \+ writing \-
