## Right Page

### [Feb. 14, 1895]

N.Y. Trib. — E. CARLETON SPRAGUE. Feb. 15 '95

Buffalo, Feb. 14.—E. Carleton Sprague, one of the best-known and most prominent lawyers of this city, died here at noon to-day from uraemic blood poisoning, the result of kidney disease.

Mr. Sprague was born at Bath, N. H., November 26, 1822. The family removed to Buffalo in 1825. The deceased man was a graduate from Harvard College in 1843, studied law at Buffalo with Fillmore & Haven, and was admitted to practice in 1846. In 1876-1877 he was State Senator, filling a vacancy caused by the resignation of Sherman S. Rogers. Mr. Sprague had been attorney for the Erie Railway since 1854. He had been president of the Young Men's Association of Buffalo and of the Buffalo Club, and had held high elective positions with many social and charitable trusts. As an orator he ranked among the best in the State.

Mr. Sprague was married June 25, 1849, to Miss Elizabeth H. Williams, daughter of John R. Williams, of Buffalo. His family consists of four children, two sons and two daughters. In politics he has been a Republican since 1856, though independent. He was a member of the Unitarian Church.
