## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 22, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day, bright + cool. Bkfst. after 9 — At 11.30 Jim, Ralph + I Rode up [illegible] Ave. to 110' S. walked to 5 "Ave. + there entered Central Park. we walked up the hill to the old Powder [illegible] thro' Macgowan's Pass to the Reservoir on 5 "Ave. at 96' S. + down the Res. to 76' St. then home by Mad. Ave. cars. Dinner at 2. Cranston Cook came in after dinner + I saw him for awhile. Then I had a long nap + did not get up until tea time at 6.30 — After tea read [illegible] for a little while to the boys. Then to Studio for a short time — on my way home met H.R. Butler who was coming to meet me. we Ret'd. to the house where we Talked N.A. matters until 10.30 — Then I wrote a note to Sue Cook + took it to Station + mailed it.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 23, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not nice + pleasant. After getting thro' with routine work at home. To Studio + saw the start made in alley for drain pipe. Then downtown to Royal Ins. Co. — + saw Moses Smith about [illegible] on Studio bldg. + furniture. I am not much dissatisfied with terms exacted. back to studio by 11.30 + worked on [illegible] until 1 o'clk. Lunch — After that. to bank — then to Best's in 23d. S. + met Mary + the boys — bought hats — boys then went to French lessons — Mary + I went to Schwartz in 23/4 "St. and bought Steam engines + boats for the boys. Then home + had a good nap before dinner. After — I went to Council meeting of N.A. where, at 9.30, I commenced the reading of Report on [illegible]. It was a hard time for us as I had to fight alone but I carried all points that were essential. An effort was made to suffocate the Report in councils. Some members arguing that after the Resolutions [illegible] voted + the last meeting of N.A. + 11" us. — it was useless to send the Report out as, by those Resolutions it was made of no power — It was after midnight when Geo + I walked up town together.
