## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 9, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeably sunny, warm day. I woke with such a pain at the base of my skull \+ thence down my neck as well as up, over my head, that I could not get up, but spent the [illegible] with a hot water bag at the nape of my neck \- Besides, my bowels had troubled me during the night \+ generally I feel as tho' I had had a new set back. But after dinner I could stand it no longer \+ I got up. For awhile I was in Jim's room where Raedeker \+ Tyler are bringing things to a close. Late in the afternoon Thunderstorms passed north \+ south of us, but we got nothing \- through the night several storms threatened us \+ a few spatters of rain fell but we got no wetting \- I went to bed with the boys at about 8.30.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 10, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very clear, bright day, growing much warmer \- very warm, still night. I have felt much better to-day \- bowels quiet except for occasional qualms of pain \- I had my [illegible] in bed but was up soon after. Rec'd a number of interesting letters \- one from W. Chas. Barmore who approves my sketch of Gen't. Lee's head \+ says "go ahead". I wrote a letter to Chas in the [illegible]. Miss Kitty Riley took dinner with us \- I podged about during the p.m. \+ wrote letters to T. W. Wood \- J. C. Nicoll \+ Ch. Barmore. Ev'g. I did some writing in the studio.
