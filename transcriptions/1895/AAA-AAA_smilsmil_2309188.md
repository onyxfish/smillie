## Left Page

### Wed. July 3, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day. Wandering about as usual but accomplishing little. In the garden part of mf. - then with Herrick taking part in his work of stretchng \+ sizing duck. Went down town in the mf \+ again in the evef. - I also found time for a little exercise on my wheel. - Jim rode out to Heart Lake alone, getting back at 7.15 - Evef. quiet, in doors -

## Right Page

### Thur. July 4, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, with threats of rain which did not come nearest until about 6 p. m. There were some sprinklings at 3 - \+ again at 8 - I spent much of the mf. in the garden \+ looking after the boys who had a good stock of fire crackers that kept them busy most of the day. Jim \+ Katie T. had a bicycle ride in the mf. Dinner at 12 so that the domestics could go to a Roman Catholic Pic nic on the Fair Grounds early, but there was much confusion because Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. did not come in until 12.30\+ Jim \+ Katie did not get in until near 1. Afternoon, I wrote to T. W. Wood - Evef. from 8.30 to 9.30 we had our private show of fire works - \+ out of the house. The boys being greatly delighted. A little before 11 Fannie came in from a day's picnic at Salt Spring - then to bed.
