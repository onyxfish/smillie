## Left Page

### Tues. April 2, 1895

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m'f, but not wet — lighter in the afternoon, almost clearing — and rain at night. I passed a very wretched day, managing to do some necessary writing. I had 1° of fever in the m'f; normal at noon — .o 10 again at 6 p.m. J. C. Nicoll came at 12.30 by invitation + lunched with me. We made use of the opportunity to talk over N.A. affairs

## Right Page

### Wed. April 3, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

I passed such an uncomfortable night, from fever + pain, that I did not get up in the m'f but lay abed until almost noon. Then, as I got up an exceedingly severe rheumatic pain developed itself under the lower point of my right shoulder blade, going thro' to the ribs in front. The pain was so great that I could not take a full breath + every movement that I attempted to make was attended with more or less agony. I sent for Yale. Mary + Louise Cook, Emma + Mary J. spent some time in the middle of the day going over Annie's things. Yale came before 4 p.m. He put me to bed at once with instructions to stay there. I was fairly comfortable so long as I lay in one position with hot-water bag under my shoulder. Fever + more pain came on at night.
