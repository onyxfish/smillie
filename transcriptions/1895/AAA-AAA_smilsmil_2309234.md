## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 5, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another charming day. The slater got at his work at 5.30 in the m'g. He lives in Binghamston & is determined to go home to night. He got thro' only just in time to catch the 6.20 P. m. Train. The Roof by his measurement requires 3820 say, fet to cover it. After my m'g duties I set a small palette & laid in, very roughly, the foundation for a sketch. "The Road into the Valley" \- the material for which I got on the Silver Lake Road just about a mile out of town \- Ralph & Sanfrd Mulford were not on their ponies m'g & afternoon \- about 12 I went out on my wheel & met them out by the Sayre farm. I had a busy time until 6 o'clk about with the men \- After tea Ralph & I went to P. O. A little Reading after he got to bed & then I joined him.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 6, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very beautiful day. Blb'. \+g. After my usual m'g duties Ralph & I went out for a little stroll, he on his pony & I on my wheel \- Lorna behaved herself with unexpected proprority \- I acted as pace-maker, & she trotted along side of my wheel like a dog. R\- seems now to enjoy the riding very much & says he rather likes her to "act up" so long as she does not throw him off \- We rode less than four miles \- On my return I wrote a long letter to Jim. Dinner at 2. After that I wrote a note to S.S. Wright concerning the sale of Trucks \- & then, until sundown I was cleaning the new Slate Roof, valleys & eaves troughs on Studio building. I took no tea \- felt very heavy & stupid in the ev'g \- after getting R\- to bed I went to P. O. with letters
