## Left Page

### Thur. June 13, 1895

*To Montrose.*

**Weather:** Very disagreeable m't., overcast \- muggy, close, stuffy, hot \+ chilly.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up early, b'fp. at 7.30 \- Before 9 o'clk. our eight Trunks \- 2 bicycles \- 3 cups. p'fp. \+ one heavy case, freight, were on a Jackson's W.p. wagon \- J 9.30 \- I went to Dis. Tel. \+ fo°. bill \- then to Putnam's, 23°. St., \+ changed book \- then to 2°. Nat. Bk. \+ left pass book there \- Then to Dr. Allen's in 47° St. \+ had a little more work on my teeth \- then home before 1. Sun shone out very hot making the weather more uncomfortable than ever \- All hands Ready \- a quiet look over the house. \+ before 2 we started in three detatchments for foot of Christopher St. \- we got away on 1 o'clk. train without incident \- A heavy thunderstorm came up after we passed Boonton \- two others followed later \- the last one was still falling as we reached Meford \- \+ drizzling to life at when we got to Montrose \- Herrick was waiting for us \+ we rode at once to the house \- where a small fire on the hearth was very pleasant. We had something to drink \- milk \+ tea \- \+ then, all to bed before 10 \-

## Right Page

### Fri. June 14, 1895

**Weather:** Foggy m't., clearing hot \- but with a pleasant breeze \-  **Temp:** B'fp. about 8 \-

Thru, a day of pottering about \- all over the place \+ looking after all sorts of things \- I went down town in the m't. \- at the Republicans Office I had a "Sewerage" talk with Rev. Mr. Warriner \- Bro. Mr. Benton \- Editor Taylor \+ A.B. Burns \- Down again in the afternoon \- our W.p. \+ freight came this to-day \- Dinner at 1 \- Tea at 7 \- Bed t. very tired \+ to bed early.
