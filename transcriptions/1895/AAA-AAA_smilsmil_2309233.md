## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 3, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another superb day & night — cloudless, quiet & mild. I was very busy all day following the men at their work. I was at Beach's Foundry much of the m[orning] where I had strong irons made to hold up the 4" ventilating pipe on S. gable front. Studio roof was opened & the ridge that had sagged was straightened up. After dinner R. & I went to Beach's & got irons — to P.O. & got mail. at [illegible] — the rest of the p. m. I was with the painter who primed the shingles on So. gable — & with Giles — Tea at 6.30 — at 8 — R. & I went to P.O. & etc — After R. got to bed I did a little reading, but was too tired.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 4, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate went to N.Y.

Weather still continues wonderfully fine — clear & mild. All up very early this m'g. B&B. at 6.45 — Then to station with Kate who started for N.Y. Bessie & Ralph rode on the engine to Alford & back — on my return to studio I spent some time finishing the letter that I commenced to Sirima Ireland last Sunday. Then down-town — worked with burnisher on the etching before dinner. After dinner with the men for some time. Then cleaned my bi-cycle & at about 3.30 Bessie & I started out. Katie & Ralph with pony & cart some distance ahead of us — we overtook them about a mile out of town & almost at once the pony stumbled, fell, rolled over & broke one of the shafts short off near the cart. K. & R. walked back. Bessie & I went on to McWyalusing Road — turned at School House & [illegible] over the old Searle Hill — walked up 3/4 of a mile. Home at 5 — a rest & read newspaper — then a rub down & tea — At 8 R. & I went to P.O. — Sway a little reading & early to bed.
