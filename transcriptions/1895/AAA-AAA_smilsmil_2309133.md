## Left Page

### Sat. March 9, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very dark & gloomy m'f., gradually growing brighter - some snow flakes and light rain - the afternoon was cloudless windy & cold. After bkf. I wrote awhile - I have a great deal of correspondence now - most of it growing out of Estate business or with Annie's friends. It was 10.30 when I went out - To Y.M.C.a & saw W. Crue & made satisfactory arrangements for transfer of my studio liase - then to 57th S. to Architectural League 5 p.m. - & was there until near 1 o'clk. Then home to lunch. After that, writing until nearly 4 - then with R— I went to Newman's - 29th S. near 7th Ave, & got some turnbuckles for small closet doors - went in to Avery's & saw the Smedley drawings - very clever. Dinner at 6.30 - a loafing eve'f as I did not feel altogether comfortable.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 10, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day, bright & mild most the time. Bkf. at about 9 - A very uncomfortable headache & the Rheumatic hipe bothered me all day. I read newspaper until after 11 - then I went out & sauntered about for an hour or more. Then in & did some writing - went to saw Cook's for R— just before dinner at 2. It was after 3 when I started for a walk with the boys - we were to go up to the Rev. Mr. Williams in 59th S. but just as we were about starting I found that I did not have his address. Then we wandered out & after I had gone for about half an hour I told the boys that they would have to go on without me - so they went on to Central Park while I went to the Century &, meeting Chas. Collins there, & had an hours chat with him. Home at 6 - Charl there - tea at 6.30 - after it read to the boys until after 8 - Ge 2 came in later.
