## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 8, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, but at night a very disagreeable wet snowstorm set it. I did not get down to \+hp\. until about 10 — then, for awhile, with newspaper — Then with J. \+ R. to studio — There for a little while, then we went up to Central Park \+ on our way home stopped at Century \+ saw the pictures. Walter \+ Louise there — Dinner at 2 — At 3.30 I went to bed \+ had a nap — wakened at 5. Ge? \+ Emily Cook called — Tea at 6.30 Well I wrote some notes — Char at the house — Fanny used up with a severe cold.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 9, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very raw, overcast \+ disagreeable day — While at \+p\. rec'd from W.W.B. see his re-written "Opinion" concerning the Site com. \+ Butler plan — was so much pleased with changes made that I went at once to H.R. Butler's. 135 W. 47 S. — he was not yet up, being under the weather — severe cold — but I waited for him — He too, much pleased then I went to Putnam's \+ left copy there — Then told a. a row in the life class — I had the Monitor up \+ talked "Dutch Uncle" to him — a case of "hazing". Then to Walter's \+ got a large sheet of paper \+ then to studio at 12 — Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — worked for more than a couple of hours on large diagram of site as proposed in Report of Site com. At 5.30 went over to Lawyer Cales \+ saw him about the recording \+ paving of Sniffen Court — Then home — Dinner at 6.30 — also cat — Bessie still in bed — Mary worrying about her — Kate growing with her cold — Katey out of sorts — and Sam fighting with an impending something — influenza — grippe — laryngitis — bronchitis or something else — at 8 went to Council meeting — got thro' early \+ home at 10 \+ then to bed.
