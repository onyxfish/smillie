## Left Page

### Tues. July 9, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day with strong breeze that gradually grew cooler until at night it was positively cold. The family spent the most under the beech tree \+ I, while not with Herrick directing the tacking of the canvons on Jims attic bed room wall, amused myself on my bicycle over the hills \+ hollows \+ on bumpy lawns. Dinner — and again — the p.m. passed in a desultory sort \+ work \+ amusement I rode my wheel down to Broadhead the black smith \+ an hour or more was passed in tinkering [illegible] the adjustment of my [illegible] — Charles and Jim joined me there. Tea at usual time. Evg at 7 to P.O. as usual. A pleasant fire in Reception Room. Jos. Williams called \+ amused Mary \+ Kate. Katie \+ Bessie went with Chat. Sayre to an Entertainment at the Rink.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 10, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncle W. Aunt A. \+ Chas left —

A brilliant cold day — high winds — Up at about 6.30 — bkfst. at 7 — at 7.30 Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. left the house. most of us went to the R.R. Station with them. Then down-town. W. Mulford (long Elisha) going with Katie \+ Bessie. It was some time before I got home — after my garden work was with Herrick who is tacking the sized duck to the walls of Jim's room — Dinner at 12. \+ at 12.30 again the family went to R.R. Station, this time with Chard who left us on the 12.50 train. I wrote until I felt my sleepy after dinner. At about 4.30 Jim \+ I went out on our wheels — \+ I felt very proud of my work, going from the house out to the pump house on the lake, back into town by lower Road \+ Depot street. At the Methodist Ch. — I was unable to reach the top of the hill by about 2 yds. got off and after a short rest we went on down Cherry St. around by 2nd. Hallmark's \+ to P.O. — from there walked up the hill — a very good piece of work for me \+ done without distress. Changed underclothes \+ had a rub down \+ rest — Tea at 6.10 — Read awhile to R. — then Jim \+ R. went down town with me to P.O. etc. I got to bed early —

Very cool night, Ther. fell to 47°.
