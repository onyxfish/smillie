## Left Page

### Fri. July 5, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudy day, threatening showers; threats that materialized very slightly two or three times. After bkfs. I went out with the boys \+ we weeded for an hour on the new croquet ground, graded \+ seeded last fall. The grass is much interfered with by weeds. I went down town — telegraphed to Case \+ 2d. Nat. Bk. not to protest Benedicto's note — Na \+ D acct. on my return, some writing before dinner at 1 \- after that Herrick \+ I weeded on croquet gr. until driven in by a shower. \+ finished letter to T. W. Wood \+ mailed it. At 5 I went on my wheel all over the place \+ had nearly an hour of exercise. K. T. joined me for awhile. Tea before 7. Then I went for mail. Eve'g Mr. \+ Mrs. Danl. Sayre called \+. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 6, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Charl arr'd. from N.Y.*

Very pleasant day — the usual routine. Work in garden for an hour or so — then with Herrick at work preparing Duck for attic walls. That is finished to-day. Then I wrote a number of notes until near dinner time. After that I began to lay out plans for dormer windows. It was near 5 o'clk. when Jim \+ I went to the Fair Grounds \+ spent about an hour on our wheels. On my return had a bath \+ rub-down. Tea at 7. At 7.45 Charl arr'd. most of us were at the station to meet him. Then I went down town \+ got back to the house before 9. The rest of the eve'g, it being balmy, was spent on the piazza. To bed at 11.
