## Left Page

### Thur. May 16, 1895

**Weather:** Pleasant & cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant & cool - got up a few minu. after 6 - thinking it was 7 - Jim, Ralph + I got down for our bkfts. at 7 before we found our mistake - took R- to 47° S: to school + at 9.30 went to N.F. Butler's, 135 W. 47° S - saw him + left with him W.H. Lee's paper to me concerning his (B.) plan - arranging to meet him at the Century this eve'f. Then to Walter's. Bd'wy + 18° S - + decided about gas + plumbing details for new studio bld'f. Then to Bridgeman in 191 S: + ordered a barrel on wheels + pump for montrose - to be used in spraying fruit trees - thence up 3° Ave. to Grocer + thro to D° Allen, 155 W. 47° S - the fitted a gold cap upon one of my teeth - at 12.30 got R - + home at 1 - Then lunch - after that, my usual nap - before I got up W.J. Parke came + sat with me for some time - then I wrote a note on this + at 6 went to 34° S: + took my second lesson on Bicycle - getting along encouragingly - Home at 6.45 - Chave there but the son left - Roger Adams stayed to dinner - About 8.30 I went to Century - met Butler then also, Nicole + Deeman - we four went to a com. Room + discussed B's plan + Lee's papers to me commenting on it - it was decided that B - should see Mr. I - + the two should discuss the plan together, so that Mrs. - should be able, upon a better understanding of the plan, to give the N.A. more intelligent advice - I got home at 10.45.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 17, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

D: Lewis operated on Jim's nose.

Delicious m'f. but the later afternoon was overcast + raw. Gretta took R - to 47° S: while I waited for Mr. C.C. Morton of 17-19 B'dway - He came at 9.45 + we then went up to 92° S: near 2° Ave to Slivets Brewery. He had led Nicole once to suppose that he had some influence with + met + had already treated with him on the subject of 2½ plat of ground 59° S: + 8° Ave. - as a site for N.A. of D. + the opportunity for a display of munificence to the Academy + T. But when we got to Sliver, there was no response - his price was $750,000 + all he cared to know was, how much cash could we say down - We soon took ourselves - I parted with Morton at the Cor. of 3° Ave. + walked on to 5° Ave. Entered the Park at 10° S - + Rode on a Park wagon to 5° Ave. + 59° S - Lovely Ride. Then I walked thro° Park to 8° Ave + 56° + thence to D: Allen's 155 W 47° - getting there at 11.45 - He took cups of lower part of mouth + then I went for R - at 167 - + we went home to lunch - after that, the family scattered - Mollie + Katie + took R - away to see Roger's Steam Roller and Mary + Kate went to a cooking class - Gretta + I got the parlor bed room in order for an operating room - Table - basins - towels, etc - at 3 D: Davis came, soon after, Yale - Jim was on hand, in spite of disturbing stories that Tom had managed to tell him, he showed most admirable nerve - I went into his part like a soldier - he was soon under the influence of chloroform - in less than 5 m. - the adenoid glands + growths in the nasal cavity were extirpated - It was a very bloody operation, much blood being blown + scattered all about - Jim Reddened very even - after a time he vomited the blood he had swallowed - but long after he was asleep - I sat with him until 6 - Then Mollie + Katie came - I went to Cycle school but could not get my teacher, Lobbing - Home at 6.30 - Dinner before - Roger A. and Chave in the eve'f. At night Jim slept in the bed with me + R - slept in J's bed - Jim slept soundly thro' the night.
