## Left Page

### Fri. May 10, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 87° good. Kaport

An intolerably hot day + night. I took R. to school — then rode to 19th S. — + bot. grass seed + Bridgeman's for Montrose. Went to N.A., Farrington not there — I sat in the galleries for awhile — then to Clarke's + ordered a Pasteur filter. + to O'Neills — Then to Grolier Club + spent some time looking at the Durand Coll. of Engravings (that is, B.B. of plate eng. by A.B. Durand.) Then to butcher's + grocer. Then home for 15 m. then to 47th S. — got R. home to lunch — Jim got home early + with Tom + Edna went somewhere on Long Island to stay till to-morrow eve'g for a month. — Then I had a nap — then wrote to Herrick, etc. — At 6 went to Ja. H. Morees — Saw Mr. M. — to learn if I can take Jim away from school, before the close of term without injury to his rating. He is so much run down, physically, that I want to get him to Montrose as soon as possible —
Roger Adams surprised us by a call — He stayed to dinner — I got a letter from Nettie in the m'g saying her Catharine was down with measles, or something of that kind, so we won't go to New Haven to-morrow. Even'g at 9 I went with K.J. to N.A. f D. Presentation of Prizes etc. to students — there for an hour. Crank at the house on our return — He left before 11.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 11, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very hot, somewhat cooler towards evening. After my m'g duties I went to Bank + drew some money — then home + wrote to D. Carroll Dunham concerning Montrose Sewerage + to Herrick — Lunch — After t Nicole came in + we talked for some time about N.A. matters — Then a nap — At 4 went to Dr. Lewis's in 45th S. + made arrangements with him to perform an operation on Jim's nose next Friday P.M. Then, after 5 — To Howard Russell Butler's, 135 W. 47th S. where we talked N.A. &c till 6.30 — Then home to a late dinner — Jim just home from Rockaway where he had a splendid time, of course. He brought home a young crow that he had taken from a nest to which he had climbed. At 9 o'clk. I went to Century Club where I again met H.R. Butler — Then spent an hour or more very pleasantly with a number of acquaintances — home at 11.
