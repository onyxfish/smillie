## Left Page

### Mon. July 15, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A skim milk day - with considerable wind which increased at night with threats of rain. Nearly all of my day was given to the finish of the attic. Between 12 & 1 I went to P.A on my wheel - a short rides was quite sufficient for me. Dinner - a short rest - more attic work. After hs I took J. \+ R. down town, that they might see the nostrum vendors & their performances such as I saw Sat. night - but they had left town. On my way home stopped at Daut. Sayres -. Executive Com. meeting Village Improvement Socty. I stayed until near 10, then feeling very much out of sorts I excused myself & went home \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 16, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

mr. Frank Herrick discharged himself.

Rain commenced falling at about 7. A.M. some heavy showers before 10 with appearance of plenty more to come - disappointment - very steaming hot from noon until about 4. Then thunder clouds banked the western sky and again there was promise of plenty of rain - but the storms passed around \+ we got only light showers. Warner, the painter, came in the m'g \+ set to work up on the spark fenders with asphaltum while I went down town \+ ordered lead, oil, colors \+ other supplies. Upon my return I worked with female in putting \+ [illegible] on the walls of Katie's Room while Warner painted the [illegible] in Jim's Room. After 12 I went down town on my wheel. Muddy roads. The great heat made the work very hard. Dinner at 1 - It was 2 o'clk. before Herrick ret. to his work which annoyed me very much as he was behind hand with it and Warner would be waiting for him soon. We had been downtown for tacks, altho' I had plenty - I told him that I could go to Boyd. Cooley's \+ back in less than an hour at which he got intensely angry \+ discharged himself at once - taking all his tools from house \+ shops over to his house. I went down town \+ arranged with Chas Sayrs to send one Speice, a colored man, to me. He came \+ I made a provisional agreement with him - then for all \+ he agreed to do my carpentry work at once. I think I will be able to pull thro' without Mr. Herrick. Tea before 7 - at 8 went to P.O. on my return stopped at Daut. Sayres \+ had a short visit with Priscilla \+ Mrs. - home soon after - K. \+ B. were at a "Candy Pull" at the Sayres.
