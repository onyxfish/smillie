## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 22, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More brilliant \+ warmer — a cloudless day — After bkfst. the boys harnessed the pony \+ took Aunt Jessie to the fair ground mcaumpment — I went along to see that no accident happened — I stretched paper \+ commenced work in preparation for making a crayon sketch of Genl. Rabt. E. Lee's Portrait for the Barmore etching — Before noon I took a short bicycle ride — After dinner I commenced to work with crayon on the Lee head \+ getting interested, I stuck to it until 6 o'c Then I took another ride on my wheel. Then supper \+ an evng. with newspaper \+ early to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 23, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sudden change in weather — a pitilessly hot \+ cloudless day — The carpenters at work on dormer windows \+ I gave some time to them. I got to work upon crayon sketch of Genl. Lee's head \+ stuck to it until noon — Then I took a ride on my wheel out to St. Dunton's \+ then to top of long hill on Friendsville R? — very hot \+ dusty — After dinner, some more work on Lee portrait — there I wrote to W. \+ T. Lamb — to Walter \+ to the janitor $160 2.36' 5 — who has put in an application to care for my studio bldg. Went to P.O. at 3.30 \+ there to Knale's \+ had my hair cut. Evng. on Piazza — reading to the boys \+, after they went to bed, then reading news paper.
