## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 30, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day, not without promises of the much prayed for rain — At times the sky was overcast & the wind blew from the South & East — I worked most of the mtf. Tacking up the last pieces on canvas in the dormer window walls of Jim's Room & putting on a last coat of sizing — Then down town — Dinner at [illegible]. Gel Walker (Italian) & assistant, drilled a hole in the big Rock unearthed near the vist by Fair Gr. Gate & after dinner he fired it — a fine display, followed by a shower of stones extending as far as our house — At 3 o'clk. we all went out for a short ride & to take aunt Jessie & Minnie to see the improvements about town. Back at 4.30 — Stone at work among strawberry plants — The carpenters working at the stairway and platform to piazza at N. W. cor. of house. About 5 Ralph & I went out on our wheels for a short ride — Tea at 6.30 — Svet f. I wrote a short note to T. W. Wood to accompany some checks sent to him for his signature — took the letter to P.O. after 9 —

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 31, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull mtf. Rain commencing before 8 — Some heavy showers during the day — clearing in the late afternoon — cool wind from N.W. in the mtf. I made full size, detail drawings for Jim's window seat — I did not finish them until 7 o'clk. Then, after dinner, as it was raining heavily I put a couple of barrow loads of fertilizer, just bought, on the croquet ground, covering nearly the half of it, when it ceased raining, with signs of clearing. Warner came in the mtf. & painted the canvas in the dormer window. I wrote to Ge? Cook & sent him a Power of Attorney to sell the 50 shares of H.R.R.R. Stock, from proceeds of which to pay W. & T. Lamb. K. T. & Jim am. from Silverdake about 6 p.m. Evef. with news magazines.
