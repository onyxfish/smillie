## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 13, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Strong wind & driving storm from the N.W. Some heavy rain in the m'g growing less in the afternoon. Brilliant sunset. wind continuing. In the m'g. I wrote a long letter to Jim & after dinner I spent an hour or two with Ralph rigging his boat. After 5. he & I started out upon a walk & got in about 6.15 Tea at 6.30

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 14, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Blustering wind from N.W. cloudy & cold — clear & quiet at night — As soon as I could get thro' with my routine work I commenced painting a sketch based upon facts on the Silver lake road about a mile out of town — I plan a much larger painting on this theme — "Down into the valley" — Time, Autumn even'g. Sombre effect — a deep valley filled with shadow — far blue hills beyond — a road from foreground leading down into the valley — an old man going down, alone. Very much in harmony with my situation & feelings — I worked at it very closely until dinner at 1 — & after a rest at it again until near 5. Met with K.T. & Ralph I went down town — attended to a number of little matters — Home at 6 — Gill & Tyler have been about nearly all day — they put back in its place the lightening rod — Tea about 7. Curry, read awhile to 9 — Later, wrote a letter to G.W. Maynard.
