## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 27, 1895

**Weather:** overcast most of the day \- struggling attempts at sunshine  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- After p.p. went to Mr. John Bigelow's 21 Gramercy Park, \+ saw him \- Read to him the note I had written as from the Com. on Ite \+ Plan of the N.A. He approved. As I had not got the title of the Tilden Trust exactly right I made the correction \- He talked with me much more freely than heretofore of the alliances now under discussion, involving the Tilden, Astor and Lenox. Then to studio \- Home to lunch after going to Walters with the sketch of ground floor, or plan, of my new studio. After lunch went to 2°. Nat. Bk \+ then to Kurtz to see if he has a negative that he took of Annie in 1871 \- Then to Studio I worked on aqua-tint "Tower on the Rhine at Constance" until 5 o'clk \- Men., along. Round about walk home \- dinner at 6.30 \- Busy. Geo. Cook came \+ united near 11 o'clk \- we were engaged going over estate accounts \- Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 28, 1895

**Weather:** Raw \& misty, but not at all cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- After p.p. I went to Kimmel \+ Voigts, 242 Canal St \- with my two aqua-tint plates to get p.p. \+ to studio before noon. After putting p.p. in press I went to N.A. \+ spent part of an hour looking at Water-Colors \- Men home to lunch \- In the a.m. went to Bank (Savings of Merch. Clerks. 15th St. \& 4th Ave.) where Annie, as trustee, had a small acct. for Jim \- This I wished to have transferred to me, but could not be identified \- After lunch I took Ralph to Mrs. Burlington's, whose boy Rob. is enjoying a birth-day \- Then to studio \- with steam cut off \- (but it wont cut off) \+ windows open, the Ther. stood at 78° \- I could not stand it, so I went to "the Studio" west 33° St. \+ saw "the Living Christ" \- a gigantic head, as bad as I'd [illegible] \- Men to Scribners, hoping to see [illegible] margin \- Then to Avery's \+ saw the Dream pictures of Simonsons \- a new sensation in the picture way \- Then to Durand-Ruel galleries \- 5th Ave. \+ 36th St. \+ saw the large pictures that are to go to Paris \- A clock \+ Bull story has it that they have been asked for by some authority in "No. Salon" \+ that one or two rooms are to be set apart for them \- Such a thing is not possible \- Then I went for Ralph \- Home soon after 5 \- a Rest before dinner \+ the e.g. spent writing \- Box from Herrick Reel with a number of my small negatives of Annie \- a quiet evg. at home.
