## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 18, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright for a little while in the m'f, then overcast \+ raw, at night a light fall of snow. After my regular m'f duties I went to Avery gallery to take another look at 4. H. Smithis Venetian \+ Holland pictures. Met young McDeins of Phil. there \+ helped her make up her mind in selecting one to buy. It was 10.30 when I got to studio. Worked on "Port Maria" until 12.30 \- Then to N.A. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back at 2.30 \+ before 4 I had finished my drawing \+ took it to the N.A. Mr. \+ Mrs. Barhytt called to tell me that they had some copper plates left by A. H. Baldwin that I might have for my etching class \- When I got home found Annie in bed fever \+ general discomfort. Dinner, she sat at table but had to give it up. Immediately after dinner I went up to Yale's \+ she came down at 8.30 \- says it is "grifo" probably. Ralph gives up his bed to his mother \+ takes her place alongside of me \- I loafed through what was left of the ev'g.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 19, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull most of the day, growing brighter towards night \- It was late when I got to studio having gone in to the N.A. \+ attended to some correspondence there. \- At Studio I commenced clearing up \- much confusion after my water-color work \- Home at 1.30 back at 3 \- cleaned up studio and got ready to do a little etching for the Etching Club Show with the Water Color Soc'y. \- Home at 6 \- and dinner at usual time \- Annie in bed all day \- Temp. \- normal in the early m'f. but before night it got up to 101/2°. In some respects she seemed to be better. Soon after 8 I went to Century. Met J. Q. A. Ward \+ Dielman \+ had a long conference with them on Sté. com. business, especially to determine who should fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Jas. Hart. J. G. Brown was selected \- Home at 1.30
