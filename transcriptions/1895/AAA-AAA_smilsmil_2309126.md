## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 23, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another bright, winter day \+ another day at home, taking care of my throat which seems to be improving. I spent the day as actively as possible about the house — among other things, I wrote acknowledgments of the resolutions \+ notes of condolence from the N.Y. league of Victorian Women, the Wed. P.M. Club \+ the Diet Kitchen — to Mrs. Sleninger, Sidney Smith, etc. Eve\. Charl stopped in for a few minutes on his way to Century Club. I read the paper on Dr. O.W. Holmes by Mr. Fields in feby Century. Dear old man — What a lesson for me in the sweetness \+ courage with which he submitted to the loss of his dearest ones. How empty \+ lonesome life is to me now.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 24, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, bright \+ beautiful, but my throat still troubles me so much that I did not dare go out \+ so, passed another day, a prisoner in doors. Katherine T. shared the confinement with me, as she too has a sore throat with general symptoms of cold, or influenza. The boys got an airing by going off \+ taking a walk up to Central Park alone. I spent a part of the mtt. making a first draft of my Will — \+ of course found many puzzling questions \+ possibilities to consider. Dinner at 2. Charl came in and later, Ge°. Cook \+ Jn°. Valentine — Walter C. has undergone a surgical operation to-day for hemorrhoids. Eve\. Walter Ge°. came in. \+ then, Charles Collins.
