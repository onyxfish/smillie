## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 25, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day, altho' the wind blew rather hard & cold. - It was late before we had our b'kfst. but it was the beginning of a very busy day. - I find a thousand & one things that require looking after, taking much time. I Pense & Gno Smith were most of the day at work upon the tanks & cisterns & I got everything satisfactorily cleaned, except one of the tanks at the barn, which, as a matter of prudence, I shall leave until to-morro. Everything now is as dry as a bone. went down town at about 11 o'clk. and attended to considerable business before returning at dinner time. In the afternoon I got all my freight & express matter away & made some advance in packing my trunk — After tea I went down town & paid more bills — settled with Gill, after trying to get a deduction —. the amount bing $23.19 — over $350 of it being for the slate roofing — Home at 9 — finished packing trunk at 10.30 — Then I blacklisted my accts. It was almost 12 when I got to bed & I lay there awake, more than an hour & a half.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 26, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Left Montrose for N.Y.*

Another charming, mild & dreamy Oct. day. - Up at 6 & had a very busy time in doing up all the "last things". I went about very carefully to see that everything was left in good shape. - At abt 10.30 I went down town — sold the heifer cow to Azur Lathrop for $25 — & agreed to pay him $10 for keeping Jessie until next Spring — paid Bostwick's bill & Boyd & Conlys & then I owed no man in town anything. Home at 12 & at 12.30 I shut up the house, leaving Mr. Rhinevalt to do a little finishing & we all started for the train which left at 12.50. Kate & Ralph rode on the engine — left my grip sack at N. Station. A great mortification — telegraphed from [illegible] to have it expressed on to me at N.Y. \+ to Nicholson we went on a milk train — the milk train went onto a switch and [illegible] was flagged. The rest of our journey was uneventful. we had a crowded car & many babies, most of them crying — we got in on time at 7.35 & to the house at 8 o'clk. The girls were ready for us & at once we had tea. - I had to sit up waiting for the trunks which did not arrive until near 11 — it was midnight when I got to bed.
