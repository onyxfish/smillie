## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 5, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very hot, dry \+ uncomfortable. Last night was even worse than the night before. Temp. got up to almost 102° \+ pulse throbbed at 104. I got some sleep bet. 2 \+ 4 A.m. \+ then again later. my bowels began to be troublesome. When the Dr. came in the m[illegible] my Temp. had fallen but I was exceedingly uncomfortable.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 6, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast day with some false promises of the much prayed for rain. Towards ev'g, it cleared to be the horrid, nautiful weather that we are now so unhappily accustomed to. I had a better night \+, but for trouble with the bowels, felt much improved. I had my bkfs. in bed \+ later got up \+ dressed. Warner painted a last coat on Jim's wales \+ Peter came to work on the window seat in the afternoon. I don't know that they will ever finish. In desperation I wrote to Mary Cook telling her to send Tom on at once \+ I would fix a bed for him \+ Jim in the wagon house where they could camp until Jim's room is ready for them. Rev. W. Benton called, troubled about some gilt straps back of the pue [illegible]. He wishes them to be eclipsed in a more artistic manner.
