## Left Page

### Sun. July 21, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot day, with a refreshing thunder shower late in the afternoon. I made a tour of inspection over the place which took much of the m'f. When I got back I commenced a letter to Mary Sprague Cook. Dinner at 2 \+ after that a long siesta — too hot for anything — then the thunder-storm made a diversion — \+ then I finished my letter \+ in the ev'g took it to the P.O. Rain fell gently for about an hour in the ev'g — Oct. 9 \+ 10 —

At the request of Mr. Herrick, Petra \+ Katie went over to see her. The object was to use them to intercede in her behalf.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 22, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'f. \+ afternoon with a period of two or three hours between of unpleasantly hot, muggy weather. Warner came in the m'f \+ I spent much time with him color-mixing — he put 2°. coat on the attic bed-rooms. Then I worked on plans for carpenter for same rooms. Near 5 o'clk. I went to lettuce bed \+ until 6.30 at work transplanting lettuce. Ev'g at 8 o'clk. went to P.O. At Sayre's store met Mr. Condit, Archer, Merrell \+ Condit, \+ we walked up the hill together. I stopped at Dau. Sayre's — Executive Com. of Village Imp. Soc'y. — all out on piazza — very stupid \+ uninteresting to me — at about 10 Bess I went thro. the house \+ slipped out the back door \+ then home.

\* This fellow proved to be a fraud
