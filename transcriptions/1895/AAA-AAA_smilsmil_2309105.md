## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 14, 1895

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — It was about 10.30 when I got to studio — sent my four, small W.C. sketches over to the N.A. — then fixed up Crécy drawing \+ took that over \+ at about noon went to Avery's Gallery \+ spent some time looking at F. Hofs. Smith's W.C. drawings Venice \+ Holland. Then home to lunch After that, to Studio \+ worked on "Pont Marie, Paris" — left at 4 \+ went to Dishman's Studio. 1572 Bdwy. cor. 44th S. — not in — went into F.S. Church's studio and visited him \+ wrote a note to D — on my way out met D — \+ Returning with him to his room I stayed \+ talked N.A. matters until 6 o'clk. Then home — dinner — Ivey. Geo. came \+ at 8 we walked down to a council m— a long session — The matter of most interest to me being Jas. Hart's letter of resignation as a member of the com— on Site — a very spirited discussion followed — he not being present — it seems that he is much displeased with me \+ my manner of conducting business — thinks I am growing rather large for some portions of my clothing — It was after 12 before I started for home.

Ralph has high fever all day \+ night — above 104° at one time, but making no complaint — sent for Yale who seemed to be puzzled.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 15, 1895

**Weather:** Bright \+ beautiful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ beautiful day — Feeling the effect of last night's dissipation. After bkfs. wrote a letter to Jas. Hart — Then to N.A., met Nicoll there \+ was engaged for some time with him — It was about 2.30 when I got to studio — fussed awhile with etching pfs— for Club reports Then home to lunch. After that, some writing. Then I lay down to rest \+ fell soundly asleep \+ did not wake until 6 — consequently was 20 m. late in getting to Mr. Howard Russell Butler's — 135 W. 49th St. where I was invited to dine at 6.45. There I met Nicoll — Maynard, Louw — Tho. Moran — We had a plain, good dinner with pleasant chat, mainly reminiscent of camp experiences. Coffee in the gallery after dinner — Then Returning to the dining room — Mr. B. having Retired, B. had a paper prepared by himself, explaining a plan of federation of art organizations in N.Y. — Expanding the Fine Arts Socy. so as to embrace the N.A. \+ S. — the building to cover a number of lots adjoining the Socy. Bldg. in 57th St. He had diagrams, sections \+ elevations to illustrate his written paper \+ presented his scheme in a very attractive manner. It must have been nearly before his Reading \+ the discussion was completed — I then all went to the studio upstairs — it was near 12 when we left the house.

Ralph's fever continued thro' the day, considerably abated — about noon he got up, was dressed \+ seemed to feel very well
