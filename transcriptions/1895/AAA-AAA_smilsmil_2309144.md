## Left Page

### Sun. March 31, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliantly beautiful day. - Offc about 9 - It was after 11 when Jue, Ralph \+ I went to 6th Ave. \+ 42d S. \+ took train to 116th S.- Then to Morningside Park. It was an Easter m'f. Such a m'f, just about a year ago, when Annie with us went over the same ground. The note of a Song Sparrow was too much for me \+ I wished I were dead. - we sauntered \+ walked, getting to Grant's Tomb - then down Riverside Park Drive until we got to about 110 S. then to Boulevard Cars \+ home at 2. in time for dinner - In the afternoon I went in to see Walter \+ talk about some party wall complications arising at No. 2 Sniffen Court. While there I learned with something of a shock, that he \+ Edmond are talking of leaving this neighborhood \+ possibly, some place up town on the west Side - Home to tea at 6.30 - Wey Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 1, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable, wet, dark day. After an uncomfortable night I felt very much used up generally. Have a severe headache, some fever \+ discomfort in throat \+ chest. I was in my bed much of the m'f \+ afternoon. \+ was not able to read or write. Sent a note to V.A., To T. W. Wood informing him that I could not attend Council meeting - \+ rec'd a note from J. J. a[illegible] to the effect that he could not attend a meeting of Com. on Site next Friday ev'g. Charl came in \+ stayed until 10.30
