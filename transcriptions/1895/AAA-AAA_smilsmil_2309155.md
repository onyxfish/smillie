## Left Page

### Sun. April 28, 1895

**Weather:** Overcast w\+  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast w\+. quite cool \+ breezy — after 3 o'clk. sunny \+ milder — Up at a little before 8 \+ about some time, \+ all the strength I had, in giving myself a regular grooming — I was very weak, \+, as usual now, my temp. considerably below normal. nor does I reach normal at any time during the day — It was about noon when Ralph \+ I walked up to the 5th Ave. Park entrance \+ back. Dinner at 2 — I met Yale \+ asked him how long this physical condition of mine was likely to continue — He told me, with great cheerfulness, that a patient who had been in a state very like mine for more than 12 months, but he that he was improving — after dinner a nap — Then I did a little writing — Every one at 6.30 — after that Geo. \+ Chave came in.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 29, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day — most of the time very wet. Ralph started going to school in 47th S. again where Mr. Walton has located. I wrote until after 11, then I started out on my messages — sent money order to Altee Burpee, Phil? for seeds for Mont. Then to D. Lewis' 14 E. 45th S. — a made app't. to take Jim to him this p.m. Then to 167 w. 47th \+ got R—, then to Morse's school, 423 mad. Ave. \+ saw Jim for a moment — also mrs. morse. Then home to lunch — then a very little nap — Katey Adams ret. to her neighbor home at 3.30 — At 4 I went with J. \+ D. Lewis themselves a very careful ex. of his nose, throat \+ ears — he advises an operation upon his nose — Then he examined my nose throat \+ ears — says my ears are now in better condition than Jim's — he made me very uncomfortable by an application of an astringent back from parete, thinking it might benefit my left ear — Then home \+ wrote some notes. Dinner at 6.30 — While at dinner Mr. Writt. \+ see came with some very important papers concerning a new arrangement \+ matters bet. w. my Met. Life \+ N.A. J. D. The Met. agrees to pay $340000. June 1st for 40,51 2, 23, S. \+ an acct. of N.A. — leaving bal. $235000 unpaid int. upon which to stand for suit — deed of N.A. not to pass until [illegible] '96 — "This was of so much importance that, in spite of bad weather, I had to go to N.A. council meeting where I was able to carry the business thro. \+ get home at 10 — found Chave there — To bed at 11.
