## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 10, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Two or three sharp showers last night but little water fell. \- A very hot day, mitigated by a pleasant breeze. \- I accomplished but little during the day. I went down town about the middle of the m'f. & attended to a number of little matters. \- Barney at last got the pony harness done & Spouse took her down & brought her back in front of the cart making a pretty little picture. After dinner I wrote to Dr. Rob't. Lewis & sent him a check of $75 for operating on Jim's nose. \- Then I loomed over dormer window drawings. \- Near 5 o'dk. I helped the boys get up the pony & cart. They went off for a ride & I accompanied them on my wheel. \- By my cyclometer we did less than four miles \- but it was tremendously hot. \- a rest \- a scrub & a change \- then tea. \- Ev'ff. \- all on to azza. \- Ther. 76° at 10 p.m.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 11, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day. \- get a start at b'f'f. \- a little earlier than usual. \- R. \- had trouble with his eye. \- Young Herrick threw a hairy caterpillar in his face yesterday afternoon. \- It struck under his left eye and a painful swelling with white blotches very promptly followed. \- I had to keep it wet with dilute Ammonia, the irritation passing away in the afternoon. M'f. I wrote a letter to Mary Sprague Cook. \- At noon thunder storms passed S. & N. of us. \- Later, others threatened but did not come. At last, at 8 p.m. Rain commenced very gently & continued crescendo until about 10, at which we were all very happy. Ev'f'f. I wrote part of a letter to Mr. George.
