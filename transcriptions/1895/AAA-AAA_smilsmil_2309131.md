## Left Page

### Tues. March 5, 1895

**Weather:** Brilliant day \- cold, windy + dirty  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfs. I went over to Geo. Cook's \- 42d S. \- near 8th Ave. saw Smiley + got papers that Geo. had signed as executor \- then to 2d. Nat. Bnk then to Studio + wrote letter to Mrs. E. Carleton Sprague, who so recently lost her husband. On my return to the house at 1, found Emma + Grace D — + Louise Cook still there, having also cut most of the work arranging + making distribution of Annie's things \- her dresses, clothing and little belongings \- They found her collection of letters, from her friends + niece, congratulating us upon our engagement \- I could not resist reading some of them, altho' it gave me a very bad time \- bringing so forcibly to me the pure + noble joy that made her life such a noble one \- I am bereft indeed + this afternoon I was crushed by a sense of it \- I wrote until after 4 \- then to studio after 4 \- then to Mr. Babcock's in W. 37th S. \- + got Ralph, who had been playing with Philip. Dinner at 6.30 \- Well, spent some time with Chase preparing for the transfer of stock that stands now in Annie's name. There is something horribly repugnant to me in this work — it seems base, heartless, cruel, when I think of her in that Greenwood lot.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 6, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Part of the day was pleasant \- of part of it was not \- some sunshine + some attempts at snow. I went to 2d. Nat. + 5th Ave. Safe Deposit + got all of the securities standing in Annie's name + then went down town to have them transferred to my name. First to Geo. Cook's, 32 Howard S. got his signature to some Power of Atty \- then to Western Union Tel. \- but could get nothing but instructions \- then to City Hall + got more Surrogate's certificates \- then to Lee + Lee + had a consultation \- then to Hill, Laidstone + Western \- + got more instructions \- then to No 54 + saw Mr. Bowman about assignment of his mtg. \- + got more instructions \- then in to Charles' where, getting more instructions he re sent me to Mr. Adams, 2 Wall S. who helped me \- It was now 2.45 + I had a headache \- I went into the Café Savarin and got a quick lunch \- then again to W.U.T. + made some progress, leaving my cert. of stock with a promise that I could get the new one to-morrow \- then to Geo. Cook's \- more Power of Atty. \- also Notary \- then I rode up to 59th S. \- to Geo's Studio — but I was too tired to see anything \- he must have tho't me very indifferent \- Home at 5 + had a restful nap before dinner \- Suey. \- writing on estate + Academy business until 11 \- then to bed \-

At the bank this mf I met T. A. Richards who recently lost his wife \- we talked awhile together, both being much affected.
