## Left Page

### Wed. July 31, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. fell to 44°. last night. Windy \+ chilly \+ cloudy. I was at about most of the m'f. Stone was brought from the quarry \+ I set Spence to work digging the hole for stone foundation under piazza steps. I wrote some N.E. letters. Went down town at 11.30. Saw Circus enter town at noon. A tawdrey system, but not al together without picturesqueness. Dinner at 12.30 so that Katie, Bessie \+ the boys could go to the circus. At 5 I started out alone for a tramp. I went thro' Darkie Town to Wyalusing Valley Road \+ turning on first Right hand Road came back over the Searle hill, taking observations of road \+ grade with a view to bicycle riding. Got back at 7 o'clk \+ had my tea alone. The rest of the ev'g was used up putting the boys to bed \+ looking over newspapers.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 1, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katie \+ Mollie Adams arr'd from Buffalo.

Dull threatening m'f., quite chilly — growing milder \+ less cloudy — with little showers after 6 P. m. The day was spent on routine duties, working on plans for Jim's Room \+ superintending Gill \+ Rodeker who, by sticking to their work, barely managed to get Katie's Room in the attic in such shape that at 6 o'clk she \+ I put up her bedstead \+ got things in such order that she could occupy it. After tea, between showers, Katie \+ Bessie, the boys \+ I, went to the station to meet the Adams girls who arr'd on the 8.45 train. They brought their bicycles \+ we walked to the house bringing the wheels with us. They had a light supper \+ retired early.
