## Left Page

### Sun. May 26, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded day with threats of showers that didn't come. Bkfs. before 9 — I sent invitation to Nicoll to dinner — \+ about 10.30 called on H. R. Butler at his house \+ talked over the N.A. Situation with him for an hour — then home — Nicoll came \+ I \+ we talked N.A. until dinner at 2 — N. left at about 3 — Charl came \+ stayed until I left at 3.30 — I took 4 train \+ got to N.H. in time at 5.45 — \+ to the Adamss' house at about 6.20 — Tea \+ soon after Roger home for a sunday visit — Evef I was so tired that I went to bed at 9.30

## Right Page

### Mon. May 27, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool — threatening mtf., with heavy thundershower that came up just after lunch \+ continued thro' the afternoon — Before bkfs: Jim \+ I took a little run on our wheels — I gave the rim of my forward wheel a tremendous bang on a big stone — after bkfs: I wrote two notes — to W.H. Lee — \+ to Cooley - Montrose. at a little after 11 Katie T. and went out on our wheels \+ we had a very pleasant ride, getting it at about 12. I am much encouraged now as I can mount my wheel in proper form and was able to get up some inclines that were too much for me two or three days ago — I took a sponge off — a rub down — \+ had a change of older clothes \+ there lay on my vid \+ knitted lunch with equanimity — after lunch as the rain came I spent much of the time in reading a little book on cycling — I also managed to work in a little nap — Dinner at 6 — Evef Mr. Bennell called — a nice man who seems to have the prevailing cycle craze quite as severely as the rest of us — To bed at 11 —
Roger left for N.Y. this mtf.
