## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 15, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A gloomy, blustering mt\- Quite a heavy snow fall for a few minutes \- followed by spattings of snow \+ rain \- clouds broke away for a little while in the afternoon to bank up, wet, at night \- I spent the day in studio pulling down curtains \+ getting things together preparatory to packing to move \- Late in the afternoon Relph \+ I went down town \+ transacted considerable business. At night I had all the symptoms of an attack of influenza \+ began a course of the new remedy, [illegible] \+ got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 16, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day \- violent headache with remnants of the cold that took hold of me yesterday \- At 3 o'clk. I was routed out by bowels \- the character of the evacuation suggesting a hope that the discomfort that I have been suffering for a week or more past may have now cleared itself out. I got up about 7.30 p.m. \+ sat in big chair, very unhappy until about 10 o'clk \- then I took some hot whisky \+ went to bed \-
Chas. Sayre \+ Hy Beach, callers in the evg.
I continued the [illegible] during the day according to directions but cannot say whether I was cured of anything or not.
