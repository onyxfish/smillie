## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 18, 1895

Charl arr. from Naples.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Splendid day — Routed out of bed at 5.30
A.M. by Telegraph boy announcing that the [illegible]
Bismark would reach her Hoboken dock at
about 7.30 — had my [illegible] before 8 \+ got to Hoboken
at 8.30, in time to see the Bis mark worked
into her dock — It was 9 before C. could come
ashore — it was 10.30 before he got through
with the Customs officer — It was 11.20 when
we got to his house — Then I went to market
etc — \+ to studio — Kate came down from [illegible]
getting to the house before noon — lunch at
1 — Charl lunching with us — It was after
8 when he went to studio with me — He
soon left \+ then I tried to do some work
on the Lee portrait — Home at 5 \+ half a
rest before dinner — Geo. came \+ we
walked down to N.A. Council meeting
together — we got away from there before
10 \+ home. Charl there — it was mid-
night before I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 19, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Foggy morn, clearing very mild — I got
an early start \+ went to 1\+7 E. 36th St — \+
saw Mr. Coles, one of the owners of Stables in
Griffin Court about the work that the Board
of Health requires to be done there — When I
got to studio I set to work upon Ste. Con.
Report \+ worked at it until noon — Then
I went to 2d Nat. Bank \+ made another
loan to pay the last Studio Bldg. bill. The
bill of Babb, Cook \+ Willard — Home to lunch
at 1 — To studio at 2 — \+ worked without
much spirit on Bee plate — Ryan came
\+ paid me $50 — being Rent of Studio \+ Store
for Dec. Maynard came \+ took me
up to his room to show me how pleasantly
he had rearranged his furnishing —
I walked about until after 6 — Then
home to dinner — Even. working
on my accts — especially those con-
cerning the new studio which has cost
thus far almost $18,500 — Charl came
in after 9 \+ stayed until near 11.
