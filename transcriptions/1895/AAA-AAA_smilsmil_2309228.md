## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 23, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot, uncomfortable day with a strong, dry, south wind blowing — a busy day for me — Gile came with Peter — Several loads of slate were delivered, but the slater did not come. Ruan & his helpeer came \+ commenced taking down the 4" ventility pipes that ran thro' the roof. I find that I shall have to make several changes to make more sure against sewer gas & smells. A digger also came & dug about the soil pipes to make sure that underground traps were in good condition. Two of the big flag stones in front of kitchen were moved — Thro' it all I managed to do some work on my sea portrait & finished the drawing in but did not dare to commence on the biting. The three boys went off in the afternoon to Brewster's Pond, fishing. They went with the pony & cart & did not get back until 7.30 when it was quite dark — of course I was somewhat worried but they came in quite happy over three tiny fish, the fruits of along half day of hard work.

miss Mabel Cooley left us for N.Y. this m'f by 7.50 train. We all had an early b'fs. \+ most of the family went to the station with her.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 24, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A a bright, breezy day with luminous atmosphere — \+ a very busy day for me. An extra man came with the digger \+ the two, with help of Spouse, got out the large flag stones in front of kitchen \+ tore up the old terra-cotta drain which was found to be in a very foul condition — the bad smells that have troubled us during the past summer were fully explained — Ruan & his helpeer were also at work — \+, part of the day, Peter — I went on with my biting of Gen. Lee portrait, using Nitric acid — my first important work with it. I did not quite finish the biting. About noon I went out on my wheel & had a good ride — Late in the afternoon I walked over to the top of the big hill on Meadsville road & made hasty charcoal sketch of a bit that has for some time interested me — looking down into the valley — Home soon after 6 — [illegible] went down to P.O. etc — Then, after getting R— to bed, newspaper until 10 —
