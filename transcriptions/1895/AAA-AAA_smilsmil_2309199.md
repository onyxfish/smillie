## Left Page

### Thur. July 25, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some warm weather in the m'f — a surprise in the shape of a quite little shower between 1 & 2 o'clk. P. M. — a warm, breezy P. M. — & a quiet, smoky eve'f. I spent the m'f. until 11.30 m & about the garden — Wrote to Cottrel — had a half hour ride on my wheel before dinner — After it wrote to de Graud Benedict & Geo. S. Cook. About 5 o'clk. on my wheel went to P. O. & to Gill's shop. Then rode out to the lake for the boys who were there fishing — found them — back at 6 — rested & changed clothes & was late to tea — To P. O. at 8 — then home & got the boys to bed — news paper & to bed myself.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 26, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day, breezy & fresh. After getting thro' my routine duties, spent the rest of the m'f writing letters — on N. A. matters to M. W. Lee — to Farrington &c. Very soon after b'f't Jim started on his wheel for the Parke farm to spend the day with Greer Parke. I chased him, on my wheel, as far as the outskirts of town, with an extra coat, but was unable to overtake him — At 12 I again mounted my wheel & went to P. O. with my letters — Afternoon I wrote several notes & letters & Ralph & I went down town at 5. 30 — Home at 6.30 Jim got in at 6 o'clk. pretty well tired but having had a good time. Short, quiet eve'f — news paper.
