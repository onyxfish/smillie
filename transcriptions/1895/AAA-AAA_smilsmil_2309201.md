## Left Page

### Mon. July 29, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day. While at my mf duties, J.N. Cattrell arr'd, just from Elmira having made the journey to see me + get, if possible, Herrick's position. I could not make any promise to him. I went down town before noon — Had a talk with young Ralph Lette, Attorney for the Bor' as to the powers of the Town Council in the matter of sewering the town. The result was very discouraging. Work in the attic progressed slowly \+ I devoted some time to superintending it. I also worked for awhile upon plans for Jim's room — Went down town at 8 — got mail etc — + on my way back stopped at Chas. Sayre's where an amateur concert was underway for the benefit of the Village Impr. Soct. At 9 I took the boys home to bed, the others staying later — Norman Parke came after 9, asking that Jim might go on a fishing trip to-morrow with Greene.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 30, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain, in showers, during the night continuing during the mf, mist almost noon — delightfully refreshing + much needed. I had a fine in studio as it was quite cool, + worked upon plans on the finish of Jim's attic bedroom. Work went on, with interruptions, in Katie's room. The afternoon was gloriously bright + breezy; in astonishingly contrast to the mf. About 4.30, Mary, her girls, my boys, + I started out for a walk, Katie turning back — we went on \+ around the lake, a walk of perhaps 3 miles. I went down to P.O. \+ market etc. after tea.
