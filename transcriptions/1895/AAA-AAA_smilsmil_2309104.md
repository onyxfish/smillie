## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 12, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day - Bright & mild - a heavy rain late at night - I got to studio at 10.30 - finished up the little opaque water colors that I commenced in Montrose last fall - Home to lunch & back - along call from Minor - of course, for a friend in the Real Estate business. I commenced sketch for Water-color - Pont Marie - Paris. Home before 5 & had a rest before dinner. Eveg. Charl took Kate and Katherine to theatre - I went to Century Annuae meeting - interesting - Huntington, after 16 years of Presidency, retires - Bishop H.C. Potter takes his place. After the meeting I had conversations with a number concerning Academy matters - with F.P. Church - as to Columbia & N.A. It was after midnight when from Nicoll & Dielmann I learned that Jd. Hart is very much displeased with the high handed way I am carrying on the N.A. business - has laid his complaints before J.W. Wood, who sympathizes with him & has put into his (W's) hands his letter of resignation. The first are now seen near at hand. When I started for home it was raining hard - no umbrella - I ran most of the way - 1 o'clk - locked out, waked Kate up & she came down & let me in.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 13, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright mtl. growing rapidly colder, high wind & snow flurries in the afternoon & at night. Got up rather earlier than usual. Sat for a while with paper after bkft. & then went to studio & toasted some small drawings into frames. Home at 12.30 - with Jim & Ralph went to Century & there met Annie, Kate & Katherine - we went thro' the bldg & home at 2 - Geo. & Friely there but they did not stay to dinner - went to studio again about 3 & was there until near 5 - then home - Geo. & Chas. there. They left before we had tea at 6.30. Ralph again out of soto - no special complaint - says he feels "heavy" & has chills in his legs - his eyes weep. Ther. at 8. 103¾°. I wrote to Van Pelt & dee. Charl came at 8 & took Kate to his rooms.
