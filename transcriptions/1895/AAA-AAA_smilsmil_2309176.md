## Left Page

### Sun. June 9, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day — warm, but a freshly breeze — Bkfs. at 9 — sometime with M[illegible] Parker — Then to work gathering the 100 odd things into groups — about 1 o'clk. I went to N.A. & got printing blankets & some other things there & home at 2 — dinner — after that a rest — wrote a note to Nealie Adams telling her that I had sent to her her mother's portrait. Went to G.C. Station & Teleg. to H.R. Butler at Waterbury asking him if he had heard from Mr. St. Adams — got reply at 8 — Eve'g., a number of callers — Geo. & Emily Cook, Geo. & Chas. Smillie & Stewart Jessups — Little work done — to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 10, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very pleasant weather — after bkfst. I started out on a tour, first to G[illegible]. Cnt. Station — to Manhattan Storage — to Higgins, Neumanns, news man's — Grocer's, butcher's — Station "T" — Einer & Amend's, then to Walter's — Bdway & 18th St. — to N.A. & D. and signed some checks — Then to Dr. Allen's in 40th St., back to 42d St. & had my hair cut — & home a little before 1 — lunch. Then a bit of a rest — Then to work at packing — Got big case with rugs etc finished — Then started on my Trunk & made progress with that — Josephine Smillie called — late in the afternoon Roger Adams called & after dinner he came in again, on his way to D.L. & W. train for Buffalo. Chare came in later & at 10 there was beer, schwartz brod & — well — cheese — To bed at 11.
