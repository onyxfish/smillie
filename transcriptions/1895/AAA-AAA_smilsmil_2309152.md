## Left Page

### Mon. April 22, 1895

**Weather:** Pleasant mfl., growing overcast. light rain in the afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still in the same subnormal condition of Temfs. (97 4/5°) + very weak + depressed. I went to butcher's + grocer's as usual + then rode to 23° S. + 3° ave. + walked across to the 2°. Nat. Bk where I made a deposit — then sauntered up 5° ave + stopped in at Durand-Ruel's gallery + saw miss Cassatt's works — too advanced for me. met Fuller, DeWalle + Papermann + Impressionist crunk, there + had a long talk with him — on my way home at 12.30, I stopped for Ralph — lunch + a nap in the afternoon + then wrote part of a letter to Herrick. It is hard work for my poor head. Dinner — Ewer read aloud for R — until he went to bed — then chatting in a sleepy way with the K's until 10 — then to bed in a very dejected state of mind.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 23, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day — no evidences yet of increased strength or energy — continuous weakness is most depressing. Down to bllfs. at 8.20 — after which I spent so much time reading that it was 11 o'clk before I went out — first to Hugh Douglass to see him about renting the store under the proposed studio in 36° S. — then to grocer's + to butcher's — then wandered about the streets until 12.30 when I went for R — + brought him home. Lunch after 1 — very soon after that the two Katies, Ralph + I went to the Cycling Academy in 34° S. K. T. had an hours ride while K. A. + R — had each a 1° lesson for one half hour. At to close I took R. 6 O'neil's shoe store + got him a pair of shoes — then home at 4 o'clk. + being very tired I took a good nap. After that, wrote part of a letter to Herrick. Dinner at 6.30 — after it, games until 8·5 when the boys went to bed. Then we read until about 10 — then to bed.

At bed time my Temfs. got up to a trifle above 98° — which was very satisfactory to me. Pulse 86.
