## Left Page

### Wed. March 13, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nasty day - drizzle & fog in the m'f - dry, with signs of clearing in the middle of the day and heavy rain at night - Before 10 I went to Noms for Co. & told them that it was too wet to move studio truck but that if the sidewalks dried by 2 o'clk - to be on hand with their van. Then I ret'd to the house & spent the rest of the m'f writing letters - lunch promptly at 1 - at studio at 2 - the o men there - got right to work but it was 4.30 before every thing was down stairs & into the van - A fine drizzle came on & wet some of my things - Wordsworth Morrison & others helped me with their presence - Then home, van getting there as soon as I did - The drizzle kept up, very fortunately & things were rushed into the house - getting every thing in by 5.30 - Then Walter came in & we talked of new studio plans until after 6 - Then I had a hasty scrub down & dinner - Eve'g at 8 o'clk - To N.A. & D - Quarterly meeting - had a quorum in spite of the weather & a somewhat interesting discussion over the "Fine Arts Federation" - Dickson presided the Report & carried it successfully through with a very clear head, pleasant manner & good temper. The discussion over the proposed Portrait system to be held up full, was largely shaped by Caroll Beckwith who is a ready speaker & very shrewd - Eastman Johnson was amusingly vehement & foggy & Tom Moran, as usual, was "agin the government" & every thing else - The Site Com. was let off as adj'd meeting adjourned at about 10.30 - I lost no time in getting away - Rode up town in car with Horace Robbins -

## Right Page

### Thur. March 14, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant & beautiful day. Neumann, the carpenter, came before we had finished our b'f'. He spent all of the m'f, & a part of the afternoon, putting up the closets, made by Herrick, in the wash room off my bed room - I had to go to 29th S. near 7th Ave. to get some hardware - I did little else than "boss" N - . Lunch at 1 - most of the afternoon I spent with David, the darkie, in getting my studio's truck stored away as compactly as poss-ible - some in the attic, more in the basement & plenty of it all over the house. Eve'g Kate & Katherine went to Boston Symphony Concert with Charl's tickets. b'f to meet them after the concert - I went to Edward Cook's to get a duplicate copy of Annie's will that I gave to him but found that he had gone to Buffalo only an hour or two before. I spent what remained of the eve'g, reading.

Immediately after lunch I went to Dr. Yale's & consulted with him concerning Ralph whose condition is not satisfactory.
