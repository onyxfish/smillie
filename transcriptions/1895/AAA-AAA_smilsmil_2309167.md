## Left Page

### Wed. May 22, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day. Rather cool in the early m't. \+ breezy all day. After bkfs. I went to Dr. Allen's \+ he made some changes in the new piece — then on my return home. went to butcher etc. — then to Bicycle Act., got my wheel \+ took it home. — Then packed the boy's things \+ mine from trips to New Haven. Lunch at nearly 2 as the chimney sweeps were in the house. At 2.40 I started with K. T., Jim \+ Ralph. with our three Bicycles for Gr. Central Station. \+ went to N.H. on 3 o'clk Train getting there on time, Katy \+ Mollie meeting us. We went to their new home at once \+ after the usual formalities we went off for a walk getting back to dinner at about 6.45 — Eve'g. chatting until after 10. Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 23, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A charming day — Up soon after 6 \+ went out with my bicycle — after many unsuccessful efforts to make a mount in good form I had to take an ignominious start from a curb stone. I got away finely \+ was much exhilerated by my success until I was unfortunate enough to run into a mortar bed. Then I walked home to bkfs. After that there was some general bicycle riding — in which I took no part — but after 10 Jim \+ I started out. I could not mount in proper style but had to depend upon him for a start — I we had what was quite an exciting ride for me as I had never been on a road (on a wheel) before. I got along amazingly on a ride of 2½ miles, as marked on Jim's cyclometer. I lost my seat only three times — As we got home at 11 the D'owns' carriage was just leaving, with Nellie, M. T. \+ Ralph for a ride. Jim immediately followed them on his course \+ they did not return until 1 o'clk. I had a sponge bath — dry clothes \+ a long rest. Read until lunch. — Then I wrote notes to Messrs. des. \+ R. Butler \+ started upon a paper that I am preparing for the S'tc. com. meeting on Sat. every. — About 5 I walked out to Whitney Lake to meet our party who had gone there to row. Not as I got to the lake they passed me, homeward bound, in a trolley car. I cut \+ thro' the Park. getting in in time for dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. chatting until near 10.
