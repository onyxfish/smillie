## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 1, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cool m'f. - followed by a superb day. - After \[illegible\]. Many took me aside to tell me of the experience of Bessie \+ Jim at Silverlake on Tues. when both of them were nearly drowned while in bathing. Bessie was insensible \+ Jim was relieved after minutes. Then Mary took the opportunity to speak further of her own affairs - evidently not altogether satisfied - but in what way, or what she wishes I did not fully understand. - It was very trying to me. I got the pony saddled \+ Ralph rode for awhile about the lawn until the pony got tired of it \+ bucked him off - then I took her in hand \+ she tried to buck with me but I was too heavy for her. - Until dinnertime, on a tour of inspection over the place. After dinner, headache got the better of me, \+ I spent some time on my bed. At 6 I went down town - \[illegible\] before 7 - Eve'g., family chatteraton.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 2, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Aunt Jessie \+ Emma left for N.Y. — Mabel Cooley came —

Brilliant day. cool \+ almost cloudless. Ther. fell last night to 44°. Up early. Aunt J. \+ E. left by early train. - N.T. - Ralph \+ I went to the Station \+ saw them off. I worked on crayon sketch of Gen'l Robt. E. Lee's head \+ finished it. - Then I went out before noon \+ had a ride on my wheel. - Warner \+ one of his men came in the afternoon \+ painted inside \+ out of dormer window. Fink's team hauled earth which was used to fill hollows to N. \+ E. of house. I packed sketches \+ photos. of Lee's head \+ sent them to Mr. Barmore at Hotel Cadillac. Detroit. Mich. I wrote to him \+ to C.S. Farrington. - I went to P.O. \+ Exp. before 6. Mabel Cooley came by narrow gauge R.R. after 4. - To visit N.T. Bessie T. Ret'd. from Silverlake after 6. Tea. - Eve'g. I made a drawing for carpenters \+ read newspapers.
