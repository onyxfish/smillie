## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 20, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright + beautiful day - Annie had a temp. of nearly 100° at 9 A.M. which got as high as 101.15 in the afternoon. She insisted upon getting up \+ sitting at dinner with us. After 12 Ralph + I went out, stopped to see Yale - not in - left a note for him - went on to 55 n 8t \+ saw Joe? for a moment. Gordon has, it is supposed, a very light attack of Scarlet Fever. Dinner at 2 - Elecd. Howe \+ Robt. Cook dined with us \+ Mary Cook came in - at 5 o'clk I walked up to 73d St. \+ back - stopped to see Yale - not in. Tea before 7 - Quiet eveg. wrote part of letter to Hd Sprague concerning Pafsmore's Report on Kansas mtgs

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 21, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A horrible day of sleet, rain and fog. Annie still very wretched - Kate on her back - Katherine little better - Ynetta sent to bed by Dr. Yale - a fine hospital full of patience. Chas. MacQue [illegible] the day for me by a call soon after 8 klo - in the matter of Sites - as I had letters to write it was late before I got to studio - got out my two aquatint plates \+ White Poronia Dry-p aint study \+ worked awhile over them. Men home to lunch - after that I went to K.\+V.'s in Canal St hoping to get some nfs. but Millatts, Bloodgood \+ Manly were there ahead of me, so, at kefs. m. was dark, I gave it up \+ went home. Eveg. went to N.A. council meeting \+ had a disagreeable time intrying to get vacancy on Site Com. caused by Resignation of Jas. McHart, satisfactorily filled. Home at 11.30 \+ had a very unsettful night.
