## Left Page

### Mon. July 1, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, altho' the sun was hid-
den much of the time. I turned
that fact to acct. by working over
my lettuce beds much of the time
\- also in helping Herrick with
stretching \+ sizing duck to cover
attic walls \- \+ sometime given
to writing letters. The routine of
the day was as prosaic \+ monoton-
ous as usual. Evef. very cool.
made a small fire in Cherry Room
where most of the family sat, with
chatting \+ piano playing until bed-
time.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 2, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day \- very cool in the mtf
growing gradually milder until the
evef. Clear, crisp moonlight. The
mtf was divided between lettuce
Herrick, \+ finishing a long letter to
S. R. Koehler. Katie \+ Bessie began
this mtf. a school hour with the
boys \- just enough to be a little
discipline \+ to keep them from for-
getting all that was learned the winter
before. Dinner at 12 \+ at 1.30 all
the family but me got away for a
drive to Silverdake. I had plenty
to do about the place \+ so did not go.
They ret'd at 7 after a very satisfactory
afternoon. A late tea, \+ the usual
quiet evef. Got a good letter
from Emma Ireland this p.m.
