## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 2, 1895

**Weather:** Cold, sleety m'g, changing to rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, sleety m'g, changing to rain which fell in considerable quantities & quite persistently thro' the day. It was a dreary day of hanging about the studio waiting & watching while the carpenter worked upon the closets getting in a system of shelves and floors that I have devised for the packing away of my possessions.
In the ev'g I went to Monthly meeting of the Century where I met a few friends & enjoyed them until about 10.30 — then home —
After I left the house, at about 8 o'clk. P.M. Ralph tumbled down the kitchen stairs cutting his head so that it bled freely — There was a great excitement for a time but it was quieted in time & when I got home he was very quietly sleeping.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 3, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Greenwood.

A delightful day — after 9 o'clk when we had our b'kfs. After that I had some acc'ts to attend to that took my time until noon, then, with Jim & Ralph, I walked up to Central Park & back in time for our dinner at 2 o'clk.
Immediately after that, Jim, Ralph & I, at 3.05 o'clk., started for Greenwood, getting there in an hour & a quarter — we walked directly to our plat — & got there a few minutes before sunset. The sober, autumn color, the quiet, the cloudless sky & the far-stretching sea, making a pale line of light upon the eastern horizon, made a deep impression, very sober & soothing. As yet, I know no abatement in the keen sense of loss — As I stood in that resting place of my dear Annie I thought that the only change was that I had lost the stupefaction of shock & realized if possible, more sharply, the irretrievable nature of our loss — we walked back in the twilight, leaving the entrance at 5.15 & getting home in one hour — . Ev'g I went to 331 E. 17th S. & lived again much of the past with Emma & Grace Ireland — Mary was at Montauk
