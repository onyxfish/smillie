## Left Page

### Thur. May 2, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely, cloudless day with pleasant cool breeze. Took R. to school immediately after bkfs. Stopped at Dielmann's Studio — not in — then home + after writing a note on two, to market — then down town — to Charl's — then to E.D. Cowman's, 54 Wall, to learn about ut. due on mty yesterday — then to M.A.S. + W. 52 Wall, + got may 'ut on $5000 Bonds — then to Cullen's — Chr S. and got ½ doz. of Chas'. plate holders filled with flew plates — then to M-dee's — then rode up to 2°. Nat. Buk — then to h.d. + home to lunch at 1.45 — after that, a nap — in the afternoon I wrote some letters + got some Sweet Pea seeds off to Herrick —
Eve't at home.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 3, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild + pleasant — Took R. to his school in 47th S. — then back + attended to my usual marketing duties etc. I wrote some notes + about 11 went to Dielmann's Studio, Bdway + 44th St. where I spent an hour with him talking over the crisis in N.A. matters.

T.W. Wood + his friends are making a determined fight for the Presidency — There is a great scare among the old men that the younger men have organized a campaign to capture the Academy —
At 12.15 I went for Ralph. Lunch — after that, a nap. Then wrote letter of instruction to Herrick concerning garden matters — Immediately after dinner Jim + Ralph got away to Jos. H. Morris's school where the boys are to have some sort of masquerading fun — Jim in a girl's dress —
at 8.30 Mr. W. J. Whittmore, Associate of Chas. B. Brush — Sanitary engineer, came + we talked for more than an hour about how to proceed in furthering the Sewerage problem movement in Montrose. We agreed upon a course of action and at 10 I went to Mr. Morris's + brot. home the boys + got them to bed as promptly as possible — following it once myself.
