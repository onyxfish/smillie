## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 2, 1895

**Weather:** Very dark & very wet  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very dark & very wet — To studio's soon after 9 — 3 men in the cellar who, under my direction, cut a channel diagonally across from West to East, Thro' the cement bottom of the cellar in which to lay a porous drain tile pipe to conduct the water into the soil pipe, thro' a trap, & so into the sewer. Some of the channel had to be cut thro' Rock. The cement bottom is of very poor a quality — The earth underneath seems to be saturated with water. I gave much of the time to re-writing S. te Con. Report — To lunch at 1 — After that Mr. Holman of Topeka Accounting Co. called to learn what I would sell the Wall farm for — I gave him a written statement that I would sell it for $1450 net cash. At 2.30 to studio — lord & Taylor's man came & put up curtain Rods. Evg. — N.d. Cornell meeting — Short session — home early.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 3, 1895

**Weather:** Clear & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bessie went to Co'k.

Clear & cold — To studio soon after 9 — men in the cellar cutting a channel, part of it thro' Rock, to soil pipe under east end of cellar. Much of my time taken watching & directing the work. I managed to get away after a time — went to Louis Tiffany's & saw him & the new panels of glass for front door — Will not like them as well as the old ones — Then to see Calverley — Then to Dr. Gerster's & got from him photo's of his wife — he wishes me to make an etching based upon them — Then to 2d Nat. bank — then to Mr. C. L. Tiffany's, in Union Sq. — & saw him about a lost check — Then to Mitchell, Vance Co. & ordered gas lights for studio — home to lunch. To studio at 2 — Mr. Defree, owning stable adjoining studio on south, spent some time explaining his concerns connected with stable — then Miss Habbatt called — & then, the afternoon was gone — Evg. the usual reading to the boys — Then Clare came — Then the evg. was gone.
