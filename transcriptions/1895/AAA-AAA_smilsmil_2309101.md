## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 6, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The nastiest sort of a day. opening with sleet that covered everything with a film of ice, followed by a heavy & steady downpour of rain — Very dark — at night, foggy — The family got up very late — After 10 when we had bkfs — I read newspaper for awhile — then wrote out form of instruction & credentials under which Mr. Koehler is to act for the N.A.D, gathering information concerning galleries & schools part while he is abroad. Dinner at 2. Afternoon, went to see Walter Cook & explained to him Ward's idea of an Academy Bldg. at the Artists gate 7th Ave. & 59th St in Central Park — He "caught on" promptly & seemed as much pleased as I was by it. He will make some sketches. Then, in spite of rain, I walked up to 49th St. & saw Yale — then home at 6. Tea at 730. Every, all hands stupid & loafing about.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 7, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mild, muggy & nasty day — W. Vaux came to the house before 9 to tell me that he had arranged for the purchase by me of the Sniffee Court Stable in E. 36th St. for $9,500. but that it would cost $1800 to buy [illegible] (the carpenters) lease that still has 6 years to run. I agreed to terms — Vaux is to come to-morrow with contract for me to sign upon which I am to pay $1000 down — I then went to 2d Nat. Bnk. & arranged for a loan — Then, studio & got things ready to commence "The River" in Water Colors — Home at 1 — after lunch I went to 20 Nassau & saw Mr. Wm. Lee & tried to carry the business of getting a lease of N.A. Bldg from the Met. Mus. Co — a stage or two further on. Then home at 4.15 & rested for a few minutes, then went to Century where I had an appointment to meet W. T. P. Church & Prof. Ware of Columbia College. we talked for nearly an hour, without evolving anything definite, concerning the possibility of some sort of co-operation between the N.A. & Columbia — then I had conversations with several others — Howard R. Butler invited me to dine with him next Tues. [illegible]. Then home to dinner — at 8 went to N.A.D. council meeting — full meeting & interesting — my letter making Mr. Koehler an authorized agent of the N.A. to collect information concerning Art schools etc. in Europe was approved.
It was midnight before I got to bed.
