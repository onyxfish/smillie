## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 22, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day — about 9 A.m. called at Mr. Coles' 147 E. 36th S\. He had promised to attend to the Health Dep\. Complaint ag\. Sniffen Court — A very few moments' conversation satisfied me that he would do nothing \+ as we had rec\. notices from Atty of Health Dep\. that we were to be fined $200 each for neglecting to obey orders. I thought it best to look out for myself — So, at 10 o'clk. I went to new Criminal Court Bldg in Centre S\. \+ for some time was knocked about from one petty officer to another until I got to Mr. Sued, Chief Inspector \+ by him was advised to write a statement to Simmons Clark, Sec'ty of Health Dep\. \+ ask for a new inspection — Then to Lord \+ Taylors about my curtains — then to Tiffany's — then to Calvery's — Then to Dr. Gersters — \+ Bawlin\. \+ Dr. Lilienthal — 9\. walked up to 32d S\. with me — lunch at 1 — to studio at 2 — Ryan moving in to store \+ cellar — a great amount of old truck carried into cellar — pipes etc. the I spent much of the p.m. writing letter to Simmons Clark — Howard Butler came in at about 5 p.m. to talk \+ progress of matters under direction of Carnegie — So far all seems to be going along without hitch\. It was near 6 when we walked up to Park Ave. \+ 38th S\. together — Dinner at 6.30 — Read aloud to the boys until 8.30 — Then some more writing — at 10 went to studio bldg with some things \+ to look after the fires.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 23, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild \+ very pleasant day — Did not get to studio quite as early as usual \+ made no real progress with any of my work. Katie Addams came in during the m\[orning\] \+ told me that she is engaged to be married to Dr. Selkins (I think that is the name) an astronomer connected with the observatory of Yale at New-Haven. Much of the afternoon was spent in working upon my bicycle \+ getting my hair cut \+ head shampoo'd. Eve\[ning\] at 9 o'clk. I went to Century \+ spent a couple of pleasant hours there — on my return home found Chas. Tuite there — he left soon after. A niggle of fine rain had set in.
