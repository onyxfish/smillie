## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 15, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wonderfully brilliant \+ luminous day. The lettuce showed the effects of a sharp frost last night altho' Ther. on Piazza got down only to 33°. I spent my Sunday m'g as usual \+ at noon I got on my wheel \+ had a pleasant 4 mile ride getting in just before 1. Then a rub down \+ read until dinner at 2 — After that the boys amused themselves for a time with Lorna under saddle — then they went off for a scramble \+ I wrote to Mary Cook — to Chas. Barmore — W. F. Lamb \+ Miss Eva. dee. The latter begs the privilege of bringing some of her painting for criticism. Then I had a little more bicycling \+ then to P.O. Gill \+ Peter came to look over their work — then Tea —

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 16, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast with a little rain in the m'g. One shower at about 11 o'clk. being heavy enough to run a little water into the cisterns — Then it broke away \+ Kaley hung out her wash \+ it dried before a drizzling shower came soon after 3 — but no rain of any consequence fell. I kindled fire in furnace — to my sorrow — it grew warmer out of doors \+ the heat in studios was too much. I did some work in completing the crayon outline, preparatory to water-color to the Pincian Hill from Piazza del Popolo — Roma. I also did some work out of doors, setting out lettuce plants — At 6 I went to P.O. etc. Every I wrote some notes \+ went again to P.O. at about 9.30
