## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 24, 1895

**Weather:** Clear, cold, breezy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold, breezy day — after a much disturbed night of little rest I got up at about 7, hurried thro' bkfst. & then up to the Dr's — as A's temp' kept up, with violent coughing & pain in her chest. Dr. Y — came at about 9.30 & remained some time after careful examination he finds considerable liquid in the right lung cavity & he says A. must have a trained nurse but consents to giving Bella Tanner a trial for a day or two — other duties occupied the rest of the m'g — but I was able to get 15 m — nals before luncheon — after that I went to 88 9th Ave — about 96th S — to Mary Mooney's where Bella T. was, but found that she had just gone to a new place for service — Then I went to 111 W. 34th S — & engaged Miss Strickland to come to-morrow m'g — Then to O'Neills to Vantine's & Stern Bro. to fill commissions & home at about 5 — many callers to enquire concerning A — kept me busy, but bet. 6.30 & 7 I got a short nap & then had dinner — Ev'g Dr. came & found that the liquid had made considerable progress & he insisted that a nurse must come at once, & he went for Miss Strickland — Gretta, altho' her fever is much reduced & is as mad as a March hare & Katie, the cook, is little better — Sister Kate continues in bed & K. T. is well enough to be about — The situation is really distressing. It was about 11 o'clk when Dr. Y. returned with Miss Edmans a nurse who is to stay two' the night and give place to Miss Strickland in the m'g.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 25, 1895

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — Miss Strickland came at about 9 o'clk. & immediately took her place. The nurse reported that Annie had passed a quiet night sleeping part of the time. I felt reassured. The reports thro' the day were favorable. Temp' down, pulse & respiration much better. It was near 3 o'clk when Jim came in from school. & at Annie's request I took him out & bought a skating set for him, then home. & Annie had his things brought to her bed & packed under her direction as he was to go, with Tom Cook & to spend to-morrow with Rob. Putnam at New Rochelle. — Before this, not a short time, the nurse's record shows a change for the worse in temp — pulse & respiration and as time passed the records showed a higher temp — & more frequent respirations & pulse. Yale was evidently alarmed when he came before dinner. Fine — he came again, later & determined to go for Dr. Janeway — he brought him back about 11 p.m. Jr made as thorough an examination as possible & after a consultation it was told to me by them that there was pleurisy & effusion & a more extensive pneumonia affecting the larger part of the right lung — now I realize the danger with which I am brought face to face — Annie suffers great discomfort from the burning piti- ewer & the parched condition of her mouth — she is constantly asking for ice — she coughs a good deal which backs her & gives pain in a spot low down in the right lung.
