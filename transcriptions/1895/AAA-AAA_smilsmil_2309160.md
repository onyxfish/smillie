## Left Page

### Wed. May 8, 1895

**Weather:** A very warm day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day \- I set myself to work upon my N.A. Treas's Report immediately after bkfst \- \+ stuck to it for some time \- Took a rest by writing a note to Katie Adams \+ going to market. I wrote until \- then lunch \- then a few min, more of writing \+ to N.A. of D. at 2\. The meeting was called to order soon after \- very full \- 61 members \- Thup ran very smoothly at first \- my Treas'. Report was very well rec'd \+ I had many compliments concerning its clearness of statement \- Then came the elections. The old fellows were too much for the young ones \+ elected Wood overdieldman by 7 votes \- much to my sorrow \- I cannot but think that with better management \+ proper organization Freeman might have been elected \- The results in the subsequent ballotings for officers \+ new members were many of them very unsatisfactory to me \- the old fogy element prevailing in the main. As I was on both tickets I went in with but one vote against me, the one that I myself cast \- I did not stay to the Academy dinner \- but got home to my own at 7 o'clk. Greatly very tired Charl came in for a few moments \- I got to bed before 10!

Rain during the night.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 9, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hotter yet \- Took R \- to school, stopping on my way to see Charl about the advisability of selling a portion of my N. Pacific Cons. 5's @ 40 \- He does not approve but will make enquiries during the day \- from 47' S. Rode to 18' S. hoping to see Walter Cook \- he is in Buffalo \- then to N.a. then to Bicycle shop 44th S. \+ 1ey. Ave \- then home \- then to butchlers etc \- \+ presently, lunch \- Then a nap \- then writing until 5 \- Then to N.A. \+ other intermediate places \+ home to dinner before 7 \- Then to Mary Cook's for a few min. Charl came with his Bicycle \+ his riding togs \- it is advice from Wall St is not to sell N. Pac. bonds \- It was about 11 when I got to bed.
