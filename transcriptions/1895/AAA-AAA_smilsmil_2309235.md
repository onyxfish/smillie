## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 7, 1895

**Weather:** Cloudy m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'f. A very little rain fell before 10 o'clk. but the day proved to be a pleasant one after all. After my usual m'f duties I wrote a letter to Pt. Lewis — E. 45° S. — about Jim & his nose. Then I got to work up on the ful. See portrait etching with burnisher & mar. At 12 I went out with Ralph — he on the pony, I on my wheel — for 3/4 of an hour. She went very steadily & we brought her in quite sweaty. R. enjoying his ride very much. Dinner soon after 1. Kathenie T. under the weather, did not appear. R. went off with Bessie T. & had another pony ride, she on her wheel. Gill, Tyler, Pratt & his helper at work. Spense has cleaned up about the house so thoroughly that there is now little evidence of the horrible mess that for two weeks has made life miserable. I worked on my [illegible] until near 6. Then R. & I went down town — it was late when we ret'd. Tea at 7. The usual quiet eveg. Some reading for R. & after he gets to bed, then newspapers & magazines.

A sudden shower of rain at 11 p.m.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 8, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the night, ceasing at about 6 A.m. A windy, cloudy, day growing colder. The overflow pipe from attic Tank to leader broke down this m'f & Wm. pumped the Tank full & over to the attic floor so that it ran down thro' the house making a great mess. I put in a good days work on the [illegible] dee portrait etching & did not get out for exercise until 6 o'clk. when R. & I went out & had a good brisk walk for 3/4 of an hour. Fanny Mulford Connell called on Mary in the afternoon & I saw her then. Eveg. I wrote to Receiver of Taxes N.Y. city & sent him a check for ($5) 3d° Taxes on Real & Personal Estate
