## Left Page

### Sat. July 27, 1895

**Weather:** Cool, overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool, overcast with a light shower bet.
12 \+ 1 o'clk. a little sunshine later and
then a series of showers beginning before
6. After my out of door work I wrote
part of a letter to Nicoll that I finished
\+ mailed in the afternoon. Gill
\+ Roedeker came late in the after-
noon bringing some lumber worked
ready to put up in Katie's attic bed-
room \+ R. got started upon the work.
I went down town at noon \+ again
at 5 o'clk. but did not go in the [illegible]
\- The usual newspaper intellectual
feast in the even'g.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 28, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sulpert day after considerable rain
last night. I spent much of the
m'g out of doors going about the place.
Before dinner I began a letter to
Dr. Hale. Dinner before 2 \- \+
after dinner I finished the letter
\- That was about all the work I
did. \- At 4.30 I took my bicycle
\+ rode about the place for the best
part of an hour \- Picked a lot of
blackberries \+ had tea at 6.30.
Glorious moonlight night, recalling some of the nights of the past
which Annie \+ I enjoyed so much
together. She has been in my
mind a great deal to-day \+ I have
felt my lonliness very much.
