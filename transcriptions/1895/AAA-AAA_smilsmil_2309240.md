## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 17, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine cloud effects all day — strong n.w. wind — a good example of an Oct. day in the mts. I got up feeling very seedy after yesterdays experience but continued to grow better during the day. I worked pretty closely at sorting things \+ packing \+ also gave some attention to outside work — Late in the afternoon R. \+ I went down town and transacted some business — Evef. was mostly given to newspaper.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 18, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day — quite mild and cloudless — I worked out of doors, pruning, until after 11, where I went out with Bessie on our bicycles for about three quarters of an hour — it was very delightful, but really too warm for hard work. After dinner, Mary, Katie \+ I went over to the Herrick house \+ through it — Mr. Herrick has left it in very nice order — We spent some time gathering pears — then R. \+ I went to the barber's \+ had our hair cut — Ryan put in the two slop hoppers, finishing the plumbing work for this season — Young Beach came \+ took Bessie out riding in the p.m. I resumed pruning work at about 4.30 — Katie got a long \+ interesting letter from Charles — from Munich.
