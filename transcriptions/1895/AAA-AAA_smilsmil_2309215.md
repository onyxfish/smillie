## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 26, 1895

**Weather:** Partly fair — partly cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Partly fair — partly cloudy — altogether, a pleasant day with some prospects of coming rain. Mrs. I went down town on my wheel \+ after attending to some matters I went to Harrington's mills \+ ordered 14 of a ton of lawn fertilizer. Then home \+ looked after the two carpenters who are making slow progress with Jim's dormer windows. Peter got the sash in to-day — Dinner at 12.30 \+ at 1 o'clk. Mary got away for Silverlake, taking Katherine, Jim \+ Ralph — K. \+ J — to stay there for a week. Stewart Jessup drove. I worked at getting out canvas to cover the yet uncovered portions of walls in Jim's room. At 4 o'clk — down town again on my wheel \+ then a short ride \+ a call from Rev. Mr. Benton who hot a bro. of the cloth. Tea at 6.30 — Mary \+ R. Ret'd before 7 — Evarf. I wrote to W. \& T. Lamb — to Chas. Barmore \+ to Mary Sprague Cook — To P.O — before 10 — then home \+ to-bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 27, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, threatening day — but no more came of it than a mizzle that was not enough to wet anything. I set out some lettuce in the vif. Then I worked awhile to forward the work in Jim's room. I dropped the saddle on Jim's wheel so that Ralph can ride it in the vif, \+ again in the afternoon we went out together for a bicycle ride. In the early evef. Mr. Dave Saye \+ Jessie Comstock Edgar came in for a few minutes — newspaper \+ to bed early.
