## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 16, 1895

**Weather:** Beautiful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day — To studio early. Humm there from 10 to 12, putting up a hanging about in which I am to keep my copper plates. I did a good deal of arranging and at last got my etched plate on my desk \+ sat down to gather myself together for work, just three weeks after my return to the city. In the afternoon Lord \+ Taylor man came \+ put up the shades. Home at 5 \+ had a quiet hour before dinner. After dinner read aloud to the boys until 9 — then to Century — met a number of friends with whom I had pleasant chats — \& Haven Putnam — Chas. Collins \+ a number of the artists with some of whom I had important business conferences relating to N.A. \[illegible\] matters. Home at 12. Chas. Collins walked to Park Ave. cor. with me.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 17, 1895

**Weather:** Disagreeable day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — rain in the mts \+ a fine mizzle in the afternoon. Up late. Also before 10. Then I read until near noon. Then walked up to the Park \+ back. Dinner at 2 — About 3.30 D[illegible] ale came in \+, leaving his boy to play with my boys, together we went to the Union League Club to see the Troyon \+ Daubigny paintings. I met many there whom I knew — artists \+ others. Yale \+ I went to my studio, altho' it was almost dark, \+ after a few minutes there we set to the house. He took Roy away \+ later, Al. came \+ got Gordon. Tea at 6.30. After that read aloud to the boys until 8.15. At 9 went to studio — then to Dr. \[illegible\] Cook's in 37th S. Sue Cook, from Cambridge there — Walter came in after I did. When I got home I was much surprised to find that it was near 11 o'cl — I was distressed to find it so late — I am sure that I must have been detroled after 10.
