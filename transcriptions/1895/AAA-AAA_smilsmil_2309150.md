## Left Page

### Thur. April 18, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

my nurse, miss Ida P. Strickland dismissed herself immediately after lunch, having been with me for very nearly a fortnight. I have been about the house to-day, went down to dining room & had my lunch there — late in the afternoon I got to be very tired & at 6. P. m. my temp. went up a trifle, but at 10 o'clk. it was down again. I am very weak & the discomfort in my head is continuous. It is very hard to keep many thing like a cheerful frame of mind — At night I went into the nursery for a change and shared Ralph's bed with him.

The day has been delightful to look at from the windows — Most of the day Mary (Edw.) Cook had Katie & Katharine off with her — gave them lunch at the Barnard Club.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 19, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day, sunny, quiet & mild. I had my bkft. in my bed room after getting up & dressing — then loafed about until the middle of the m't when I went out and walked up & down the sunny side of the street for 15 or 20 m. Very groggy, of course, but it was a great joy to get out into the fresh & really sweet spring air. Had my noon-day meal with the family. About 2 o'clk. Nicoll came in & made a friendly call. We talked Academy matters for a while — Then again I went out & walked a bit for half an hour or more, coming in refreshed. Then rested until dinner time — Fate came in at about 6. Temp. has been sub-normal all day — 97 4/5 — until, even when it rose to normal. After dinner I sat with the boys & little Katherines — Geo. Beutley called — Kate Adams went to Alice Ireland's for the even'. Beutley went away at 9 — and I went to bed soon after feeling very weak, tired & depressed.

Helen Butler called just before lunch & I saw her for about half an hour.
