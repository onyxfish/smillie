## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 8, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day \- Down-town before 10 o'clk
\+ had many little matters to look after.
Sewerage letter in Republican \- Dorna Dorn's
harness \+ the mending of the Donkey cart
etc \- etc \- One carpenter only at work in
Jim's room \- I tried to do some work
on the plans on paper \- but most of
the work done was in planning and
marking on the spot. Dinner at 1.
\+ shortly after 4 the two Katies \+
Mollie \+ Jim \+ I went out on our
wheels \+ had about two hours of fun on
roads near the lake \+ out back of Tulittle
depot. I have got the knack of coasting
\+ enjoy it immensely \- In at 6 \- Rust
\- rub down \- change \- then tea \-
Very quiet, mild evng. On Piazza
until 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 9, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day \- my time much occupied
by looking after two carpenters at work
in Jim's room \+ worrying over the dormer
window problem \- I made some
drawings full size \+ made clear to
myself some of the intricacies of window
seats, sills \+ casings \- inside \+ out-
side \- Underhill \+ his helper, Battles,
came in the mf. \+ before noon had fin-
ished the job of setting a stone found-
ation under the stair platform at the
N. W. cor. of the house. Dinner at 1.
Drawing on plans took most of my
time until after 5 \- Men worked
at transplanting lettuce for near
an hour \- Thunder storms passed
south of us \- but we got a pleasant
sprinkling that while very refreshing
to vegetation did not put any water,
to speak of, in the tanks.
