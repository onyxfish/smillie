## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 19, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fitful day \- brilliant m't. an overcast mid day, threatening an immediate storm from the S.E. \+ a howling gale, growing rapidly colder, from the N.W. at night. I was out of doors all day, most of the time among the trees \& bushes pruning \& cutting. Part of the time Apollos Stone and with me. Danl. Sayre came in before we had finished \+[illegible] \- to ask us all to tea at his house this evct. At 5, being very tired, I lay down \+ got a little rest \- Soon after 6. R\- \+ I went to Sayre's. Mary \+ the girls already there \- also Mr. \+ Mrs. Tyler \+ Mary \+ Chas. Sayre \- We soon had tea \- At 8 I went to P.O. etc \- Then back to S's \- \+ at about 9 I took R\- home to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 20, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fitful day, tho' not quite so much so as yesterday \- There was a lull following the hurricane that blew nearly all last night. It was so cold that I did very little out of door work, most of the time being in the studio writing a number of notes \-

Evy \+ wrote a letter to Jim \+ took it down to P.O. at 4o.
