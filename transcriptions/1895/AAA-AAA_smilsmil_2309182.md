## Left Page

### Fri. June 21, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At last, the blessed rain — it fell very gently in the early m'f., at about 4 o'clk — \+ at intervals thro'. the day the showers getting heavier until after sundown when the rain came down copiously. I spent a couple of hours in the m'f. working at my lettuce beds transplanting. I also put in a pretty fair day's work planning the finish for the attic. In the afternoon, between showers, the piano came \+ was placed in its old corner in the Cherry Room. It is the same one that we had last year \+ when Bessie sat down to it \+ gave a few chords of "Verlassen" it was too much for me. I am forsaken indeed — \+ the memory of a moonlight night in Interlaken when Annie \+ I sat upon our little balcony — the Staadbach \+ the snow covered mts. before us — \+ two young women upon a neighboring balcony murmured to themselves "Verlassen" as a duett, that memory comes back like a glimpse into a lost heaven.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 22, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'f., clearing delightfully in the afternoon. I worked for some time transplanting lettuce — cut some very fine mignonette lettuce — the first of the season — took two heads to Flora Wiltrop. Then I worked for some time on plans for finish of attic — also in the stitching room where Mary, with characteristic thoroughness is superintending the cleaning. Titian brought up the new set of furniture in the afternoon \+ much time was spent putting that up in Mary's room \+ transferring the older set to Jim's room in the attic. I practiced for nearly an hour upon my bicycle over the grass \+ roads on the grounds \+ came in so warm that I had to take a sponge bath \+ change my underclothes. At 8 o'clk. Ralph went down to P. O. with me. Home at 8.30 — put him to bed — Read Trib. \+ got to bed at 10.30 — a very troubled night. Woke before 12 \+ did not get to sleep again for a long time
