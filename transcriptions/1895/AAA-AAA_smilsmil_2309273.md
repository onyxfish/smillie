## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 26, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim went to Cambridge.

Dark day — rain did not come until night
\+ with it very high wind. After bkf. I
went with Jim to Station \+ started him on
10.03 train to Boston. He started on his long
journey very pluckily. Then to Studio \+ worked
during the rest of the m'f on dee portrait plate.
Alice Ireland came in for a few minutes.
Lunch at 1 — back at 2 — H.R. Butler came
in \+ said that Carnegie wanted us to come
to his house this ev'g at 8 — I worked until
a little after 4 or so \+ as I could see no
longer I went home \+ got a rest. A
Telegram came from Sue at 5.30 announcing the safe arrival of Jim — Dinner
at 6.30 — At 8 went to Carnegie's. H.R.B.
there just before me. We had a very pleasant
informal time until 9.30 — C— is very much
interested in the "alliance" scheme, to bring
all art corporations in N.Y. under one Roof
\+ wrote a strong letter of approval to which
H.R.B. is to get the signatures of a number
of the great art \+ money men of N.Y. are left
together \+ I left B. at the door of Mr. Asdam
in Mad. Ave. Then home — got \+ Chat
there — Gales of wind \+ very heavy showers
after 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 27, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant \+ beautiful. To studio at 9.30
\+ spent the m'f cleaning printing press
working like a mechanic with every
cloth etc. going over all the bright
surfaces \+ giving especial attention
to journals \+ bearings — It was hard
work. Lunch at 1 — The afternoon
was given to fixing blind on printing
table, getting out printing blankets
\+ turning down paper for some printing to-morrow — also having carpenter
\+ gas-man fix gas light in dark closet.
Then a walk \+ home in time for dinner.
By late mail got copies of Report of
Ste. Com. \+ Report of minority of Ste.
Cm. After dinner I went to Butler's
taking a minority copy with me, \+ read it
to him \+ his wife — we had some melancholy
run over it — then I went to The Century
met Marquand, Col. Turner, Perry \+ J.Z. A.
ward \+ we discussed N.A. matters
until near midnight — Then home.
