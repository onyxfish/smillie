## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 28, 1895

**Weather:** Bright m[illegible], growing overcast \+ raw. Snow at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m[illegible], growing overcast \+ raw. Snow at night. I slept fairly well the night before from 11 to 2 A.m. I got up feeling very weak \+ with a crushing sense of my frightful loss that makes my life before me one pleasureless gloom \- friends trooped to the house \+ were rec'd. by Mary Ireland, Louisa Cook \+ Elig. Howe \- Emma I. and other members of the family were in more or less during the day \- Oh! what a dreary one it was for me. I saw several of the callers \- those who had been nearest to Annie, but each one was a new agony until my head \+ heart felt as though they would burst \- After lunch I went out with R. \+ left him at Ed M'd Cook's. Then I went to Walton \+ saw Jim who is staying there \+ is not allowed to go out because he has a cold, showing itself in slight soreness of throat \+ cough \- Mea home \+ have an hour \+ refreshing sleep \- when I went downstairs I found that Mary had air. from P'k \- and that Josephine (Will's widow) was with Kate. I went for R. before 6 \- Miss Strickland is to stay with us for a day or two more \- Gretta continues to improve \- Mary Ireland has been conspicuously helpful having been here all day long receiving callers. The weather was dreary to me as I waited for bed time. Chas. Calverly came at 3 p.m. \+ spent the rest of the afternoon taking a cast of Annie's dear, sweet face \- To me, as she now lies, it is full of a noble, serene sweetness profoundly impressive.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 29, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A couple finches \+ more \+ snow fell last night. The m'f was overcast but there was cloudless sunshine in the afternoon. Another day of exhausting emotion that at times completely prostrated me \- at times so great that I felt that I could not rally from it. Already I realize my absolute dependence upon my dear Annie \+ to live without her seems now almost impossible \- The Ireland sisters, Elig. Howe \+ others of the family, remained about the house all day \+ rec'd. callers, cards, letters etc. I did little else than go aimlessly about the house until about 3 P.m. when I took R. to play with Kennedy Conkling at 36 E. 36th S. Then I walked for some time on the sunny side of Park Ave. going in \- about 5, when I saw Mrs. C. for a few minutes. Gretta was so far recovered that Miss Strickland got her out of her bed so that she might see Annie. but she became so hysterical that she was hurried back to her bed without seeing Annie \- Early. Annie was transferred to the casket \+ placed in the parlor \- she had before remained in our bed room \- I did not see her \- The Ireland sisters \+ Elig. Howe arranged her \+ some beautiful flowers were placed about. It was after 11 when I got to bed where I passed a restless night.
