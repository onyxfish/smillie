## Left Page

### Tues. June 25, 1895

Pleasant day. another day given up to botey cares. It seems impossible for me to get to my legitimate work in the studio — I gave my spare time to finishing the plans for the finish of the two south bedrooms in the attic \+ in the afternoon gave them to Herrick with directions to order the necessary amt. of chestnut \+ I wrote to Boyce, N.f., for the duck to cover the walls. After 5 o'clk. Jim, Ralph \+ I went to the Fair Gr. each with rake or hoe, \+ we went over the course on the North half of the gr. \+ removed all the stones, so that it is now a very good road ⅙ of a mile around. We rode until after 6. on coming down a steep grade, my wheel ran away with me \+ before I could get control I was thrown heavily \+ pretty well shaken up — left knee and right shin, barked. [illegible] we all sat on piazza until after 10 o'clk — no light in the house — Kitty Riley, [illegible] Parker Riley's daughter came with Chas. Sayre. \+ spent the eveg with us.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 26, 1895

Uncle Willie \+ Aunt Agnes ar.?

A very disagreeable day to me — strong east wind, high temp. \+ very muggy. The m'fg was unrest. the p.m. was a luminous fog. I went downtown in the m'f. \+ after my duties I gave the rest of my time to transplanting lettuce. Dinner at 1. I was engaged about the place, out of doors, until nearly 5 — then I went out on the road on my wheel, alone, \+ had a pretty hard ride — not a long one, but out by Jones's lake \+ back by Depot St. \+ the M.\+L. station. I found the grades as much as I could do to surmount. I got in very wet \+ took a bath with much refreshment. After tea I went to the station where I met Uncle Willie \+ Aunt Agnes, just in from N.f. — put them into a bus \+ I went downtown \+ attended to chores. Then home \+ chatted with Uncle W. until near 10 — These all to-bed. A thunder-storm at midnight with a good down pour of rain.
