## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 27, 1895

**Weather:** Brilliant m'f, later, sky overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

threats of showers that didn't come. I got to work in studio early — got out my printing implements — ground ink and by 11 o'clk had taken two inks of Genl. Lee's etching — After dinner I laid a ground on the plate + worked all over the background with roulette + bite — I gave considerable time to the men, looking after the slater, the carpenters + the plumbers. After 5 I had a short ride on my wheel, enough to limber me out and warm me up. Svet[?], the girls (K. + B.) Tom, Jim, Ralph + I went to P.O. R. + I then went to Coakley's + to the butcher's + then home —
Then, some reading —

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 28, 1895

**Weather:** Brilliant day with exquisite color  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- a regular "weather breeder" — I wasted most of the m'f in menial + unnecessary work — gathering + forebairing lettuce — The work about the house went on — slater, carpenters, plumbers + diggers. Plumbers got their work nearly done — have to wait now for material not in stock. Hibb and came + took charge of re-laying the big flag stones in front of kitchen — it was found that one of them had been broken thro' ignorance of Martin's clary. Immediately after dinner all the family except myself went to New Milford for a drive — they did not get back until after 6 — After varnishing my dee plate, at about 4 I went down town — then out to lc/s of hill on Friendsville Road + worked there for an hour on slight crayon sketch — Home before 6. Svet[?], at 8 Katie T., Tom, Jim, Ralph + I went down town —
