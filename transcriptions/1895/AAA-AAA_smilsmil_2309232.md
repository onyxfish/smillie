## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 1, 1895

Jim & Tom left for N.Y.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. fell last night to 32°. A very beautiful day — crisp with charming color — All up at about 6 — early Mfst — Jim, Tom, Ralph & R. + I got to M — R. rode on the engine with Spence the engineer for about half an hour. I got home again at a little after 10 + to work on the lace stitching — I put in at least three hours hitting the second stitching. Dinner — in time with the men some time. Somis delivered the two large flag stories and Hibbard worked at Cen Hd trim but did not finish — At 5.30 I went out + got a 2½ mile spin on my wheel — Tea. Eleft Joe Williams called — At 8, R. + I went down town — Superb, cloudless moonlight night — on Return, after going R. to bed — read newspapers.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 2, 1895

Fair Day.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — very mild with hazy, luminous atmosphere — wonderfully brilliant moonlight night — The carpenters did not work because it was Fair Day — but the slater worked — So did Gill & Hibbard — I worked on lace stitching with burnisher — At 12 I was sent for by the other members of the Art Com. I went up for a few minutes — the art exhibit beneath contempt — but for Mrs. Ben Mulford should have refused to serve — I got to dinner at 12.30 + then gave Gretta + Katie holiday Mary, Katist — Ralph + I went to the fair — Then I finished my duties as a judge + with R. wandered about the grounds until after 4 — Then we went down town — I got a clk from Jim — + a note from Maynard stating that he had moved into studio but there was no heat — I replied at once — then home — Tea — Bet. 7 + 8 o'clk I set fire to a big brush heap out in the pasture lot — to the great delight of Ralph — The effect of full moon + fire was very fine — Then wrote to Walter with reference to heat in Maynard's Studio — + to Hugh Douglass with reference to renting stone floor of studio old + to 2° Nat. Bank with reference to renewal of a note of Degrand Benedicts to P.O. with dates — then to bed
