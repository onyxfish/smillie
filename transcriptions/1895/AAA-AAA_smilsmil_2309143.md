## Left Page

### Fri. March 29, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. About 10 went to Tiffany's and spent some time there looking over glass with young Tost for the vestibule doors at home — Then to N.A. \+ had a look at the Spring exbn. Then to Ortgies \+ saw a part of the Wm. Hart collection — Then to Durand-Ruel's \+ saw the Manet paintings \+ a few miserable little things by Coffin. Home to lunch — When Jim came home from School Mr. Ja. to Morse came in for a little while — Then with Jim \+ R— I went to K. \+ V's in Canal St. \+ got some small copperplates left for me there, then we walked to Watts St: N.E. 35 — to the warehouse of the Premier Bicycle Co. where we examined a 2d hand machine, offered as a great bargain — Then we all came home, going up thro' Macdougal St. \+ King St. to see the house where I was born, but it has been pulled down — thro Washington Sq. \+ up 5th Ave. — not far from 4 miles, in all, I think. Dinner at 6.30. Eve'g... Jim went to a school entertainment in 47th St. \+ did not get home until 10 o'clk. — Charley came in \+ we talked Bicycles — and I consented to purchase the 2d hand one at $50 — altho' I have many misgivings as to the wisdom of such an outlay. It is altogether \+ only for the purpose of keeping in touch with the boys — I hope to establish a feeling, to some small degree, of comradeship.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 30, 1895

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasant day — After my morning duties I went to Johnson's Cycling Academy in 34th St. bet. 3d \+ Lex. Aves \+ bought 5 lesson tickets for $2.50. There was such a crowd there that I did not dare make my first attempt — so, went home \+ wrote to Mr. Howell the Nesquehoning R.R. Treas. sending him the "exemplified" will of Annie, required for transfer of her stock — Kate went to P'k by 10.30 A.M. train — I went to station with her. Mary came down, getting in at 3.30.

After lunch I went with Jim \+ Ralph to H.D. Allen's 155 W. 47th St. where we spent two hours — R. had a filling \+ Jim had some work done. Then we had a walk \+ when we got home found Mary there. Dinner at 6.30. At about 8 Emma I. came \+ took Mary with her to Concert at Carnegie Hall. She had a 3. ticket which I took to Walter's but they already had more than they could use of the same kind. I walked to 42d St. with them — then home. Charley came in \+ I sat \+ chatted with him — Mary did not get in until after 11. — Emma, Mary \+ Grace S— were with her \+ left her at the door — It was after 12 when I got home to bed.
