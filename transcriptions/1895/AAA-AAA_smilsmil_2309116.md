## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 5, 1895

**Weather:** Brilliant \+ exceedingly cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio about 10.30 — to work on experimental dry aqua-tint plate — worked until 1, then home to lunch — back to studio + worked until near 5. It is dreadful to have to work alone with one's self in my frame of mind. Evey, Chas. Collens and Dr. Yale spent some time with me.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 6, 1895

**Weather:** Bitter cold day — but bright + windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary + Katie went to Po'k in the m'fg + Ge-. Emily Cook came to dinner in the eve + stayed all night. The understanding is that they will stay until Sunday. I had various duties that kept me until 10.30 fore I got to studio. Until 1 o'clk. worked on a very careful pencil study that I made from nature at Lexington Greene Co. in 1860. Home to lunch — then went down town to Lee + Lee's + got copy of Annie's will. Then to K. + V.'s + got two prints from experimental plate (aqua-tint) Tower on the Rhine at Constance. Then to studio — then home after stopping at Mr. Conklin's in 36th S. for Ralph. After dinner, Emily's sister + her husband (Mr. Sandham) came in. Later Charl. came in for a few minutes — later Ed + Cook + then Walter + Louise. After 9 o'clk. Ed w. read the will to us, Mr. \+ Mr? S— having gone. Then there was a general discussion. To bed at 11.
