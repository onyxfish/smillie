## Left Page

### Tues. April 30, 1895

**Weather:** Wet & muggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wet & muggy — up a little earlier than usual. bkfs. at 8 — Wrote a note or two & out at 10.30 — after usual business walked to NA & thence to 2d. Nat. Bn. Finished my business there then Omnaged up to 47th S. & Bdwy & got R. & home at 1 o clk — very tired & overheated — lunch soon after — Then I wrote a letter to Jud. Rfpn. of Montrose — then took a nap — & after that I did some more writing. P. J. Burns came in to see if I had any arrangement for settling my business with him to-morrow according to contract — I told him that I had heard nothing from Mr. Lee & could not explain it — He said that he was satisfied to wait a day or two & I promised to write at once to Mr. Lee for explanation, which I did — Dinner at 6.30 — after that I tried the experiment of reading Donohoe to the boys — they submitted amiably — I think it was a mistake — Katie T. went with Charle to Mendelssohn Concert — I read Anthony Hope's — "The Indescretion of the Dutchess."

## Right Page

### Wed. May 1, 1895

*Annie's Birthday*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katie T. went to Po'k at 10.30 AM — Kate came at 3 P.M.

An overcast, rather threatening mt., but clearing in the afternoon to a cloudless & beautiful day — I got away soon after bkfs. having rec'd a note from Mr. Lee arranging for a closing up of the Byrnes assignment & lease & 2 S niffin Court at the property — I went there & saw B. & arranged to be on hand a little before noon — then to bank & made my arrangements there — Several calls on the way — I got back to 36th S. at 11.45 after stopping at home where Mr. Kingsbury & Jeet & Lee came for me and together we went to Byrnes who proved to be in very amiable mood — The place was in such confusion & too dusty that we aet. to 110.38 and there completed the business in a manner satisfactory to all parties apparently before 1 o'clk — lunch soon after 1 — after that, the day being so fine, I determined to carry out my original intention of going to Greenwood — Soon after 2 R & I Started off to intercept him on his way home from school — we met him at 43d S. & Mad. Ave — took Elevated train at 2.30 out to Greenwood & one hour — we walked to the lot on Atlantic Hill in half an hour — What a heart breaking thing — to stand by that grave — Annie under a blanket of her much loved Pansies — with her two boys — my heart went up in prayer for Sc. & strength & courage — The sun shine was bright but the wind blew keen — we lingered only 15 or 20 min: then walked back to the entrance — then train to the bridge — the boys wished to walk over & aeted very tired I assented — it was too much for me — I we got home at 6 — I was very much used up — I found Kate awaiting us — Dinner at 6.30 — Charl came in in the eveg. but as I was so tired I excused myself & got to bed before 10.
