## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 2, 1895

**Weather:** Threatening  **Temp:** [illegible]

Threatening m'f., clearing to be a delightful day in the afternoon. I worked quite faithfully on plans for Jim's room, \+ determined to make a change in them enlarging very much the dormer windows in the place. I spent some time directing Peter in his finishing work in Kate's room \+ consulting with Gill. \-

At about 3.30 K. M\+M. Adams, K.T. Jim \+ I started out on our wheels \+ went over the road that I walked over on Wed. P.M. We had all sorts of experiences in the way of tumbling. I thought the road was very good \+ enjoyed the ride. There was so much loitering however that, with our wheels, we took half an hour longer than I did when I walked. The cyclometers made the distance more than six miles \- The usual rubbing down \+ change on getting home. Every. all showed signs of fatigue \+ we got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 3, 1895

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant, in a general way, with cloudy spells threatening showers \- a few drops of rain fell. Warner sent a man who oiled the wood work in Kate's bedroom. Raedeker did some finishing carpenter work in R's room, also some work in [illegible] room. I went down town about taking the boys with me \+ bought for each of them a pair of shoes \- then I went to the bank \+ other places \- To A.H. McCollum's \+ instructed him as to the kind of lease that I was willing to give. Upon return to the house I wrote a note on the subject to Herrick. After dinner I again went to Bank \+ made a deposit. After 4 o'clk we all went to [illegible] track in fairground \+ spent nearly two hours in practice riding \- I tried "coasting" for the first time. Home about 6. and washed \+ changed \- then supper. Every. \- chatting \+ Reading \- to bed early \-
