## Left Page

### July, Sunday 13, 1890

Overcast most of the day & muggy
until the evef. when it became
quite cool. The donkey got out
last night & was caught on the green
this mf. by Way Jessup. I cut two
heads of 'Trianon' Cos or lettuce & at
10 o'clk. took them down to Parke —
Jim & Ralph accompanying me. I
did not get home again until near
noon. After dinner worked at
training vines etc. until about
4 — then wrote to Emma Brown
& S. R. Koehler — Evef. Annie &
Emily went up to darkie meeting
on the fair ground. I wrote a
note to Bridgeman for some seeds
— peas, lettuce, etc. It was 11 o'clk
when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 14, 1890

Overcast until noon when all
signs of rain passed away & it be-
came a hot, hazy July day, mitigated
by a delightful southerly breeze. I
worked in the garden until 11 — then
in to studio & commenced painting
upon a sketch, on the River Marne
at Crecy. It may be one of my aqua-
tint subjects. After dinner I wrote
my last Good bye to Charl who is
to sail to-morrow (D.V.) to meet the
girls somewhere in Europe & return
with them next Oct. Painted un-
til 4.15. then out riding until
6.0. Evef. Annie was used
up with a sick headache, left
the tea table & went to bed. I
wrote letter to Kate — went down
town with it at 9.30. — on my re-
turn read newspapers & to bed
at 11.
