## Left Page

### May, Thursday 22, 1890

Metropolitan Hotel - Room 166
Washington D.C.

Up between 5 & 6 — beastly headache that pursued me all day & at last sent me supperless to bed at 7 o'clk. The trip was fine — saw W. Vernon nestled in among the trees — In spite of chilly air we were interested in the landscape. got to the City before 8 o'clk — by street car at once to hotel — Hlp — also at Nat. museum & met Mr. Koehler — with him until after 12 — then to hotel had a nap before dinner — dinner at 2 — Mr. K came for us soon after & we went first to Cap. J. T. E. Prudhomme, now in his 90th year — Had a very pleasant call — then we went to where we got a public conveyance to Arlington — day overcast & quite cool — Stage started about 4.30 and got there a little after 5 giving us half an hour to look about. A wonderfully beautiful place & deeply impressive — the great natural beauties & the historic interest makes it to me one of the most attractive places that I ever visited — Got back to the hotel before 7 & went right to bed & had a pretty bad night.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 23, 1890

Room 188.

Up at 7.30 after an uncomfortable night owing to headache & street noises. A fickle day of sunshine & shadow & yet, altogether pleasant & cool — A smart shower after 8 p.m. Before Hlp. wrote a note to Herrick & posted it. It was about 9 when we had Hlp. then waited about & tried to have our bed room changed & eventually succeeded — It was almost 11 before we got to the Nat. museum — Mr. Koehler had just gone over to the hotel to see what had become of us — on his return was introduced to Mr. Dougall — It was after 12 when Mr. K — Annie & I walked to the Washington Mon[ument] — ascended to the top & had charming views — down, after 10 m. at the top — went over to the Printing & Eng. Bureau — I enquired for Cap. Meredeth, Chief — he was out & we were very pleasantly rec. by his aid, Mr. Sullivan, with whom I soon transacted my business, that of getting finished [illegible] from two of my early dies of which I had but etching [illegible]. Then we were placed in the charge of a very pleasant young woman with instructions to make us a "special party". Then we hurried to hotel just in time to get into dining room before the door was closed — we had a fair dinner — Then, all went to the Capital — went into Senate gallery — the House adjourned before we got into it. Gazed admiringly at the works of art, then out & hired a good looking [illegible] in which we rode out to the Soldier's home where we found grounds and news hardly less beautiful or interesting than those at Arlington — Back to hotel about 7.30 where Mr. Koehler bid us good bye — we went in, took possession of our new room & had supper about 8 o'clk. Myself I wrote my diary & read newspaper & Annie wrote to Jim.
