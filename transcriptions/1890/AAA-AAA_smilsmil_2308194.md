## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 5, 1890

Cleared to a bright, warm & very
breezy day — overcast afternoon & cool
bright night — To studio at 9.30 &
wasted m'f. working over 18×30
Pastel sketch — about noon Mary Wiley
Ford of Brick Church, Orange — called
with her 17 year old daughter to
make enquiries about industrial
art schools — About 6 clk. I went
to Bank for Annie — Home at 3 o —
Mary Willard there — lunch — after
it, my head ached so much that I lay
down upon the bed for half an hour —
There Rode down to 10th S. with Mary Cook
— to 16th S. Studio block & called up on
Horatio Walker — J.C. Nicoll — J.Q. Brown
& W.H. Beard, where I saw the ills. for
his book "Action in Art" — Home at 6. H.
dinner — Katherine & Edna — again with
us over night — Ev'g Charl. Geo. &
Nellie called — Charl. to talk over
a plan for next summer's journey —
To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 6, 1890

Bright, breezy day — To studio before
10 & worked all the m'f. on 18×30
Pastel — after having scrubbed, with a
scrubbing brush, the sketch that I
made yesterday until only a stain
remained. After 1.30 when I got
home to lunch — back to studio at
2.30 & finished sketch — It is very
fuzzy, high in key & inclined to be
"chalky" — still, I find much in
the process that is interesting to me.
Home at about 6 — Ev'g. Alice
& Fred Ireland & Geo. & Nicky Cook
dined with us — Catherine & Edna
again stayed with us over night —
At 8.30 I went with a note from
Annie to Walter at Ed'ds, where I
met Carl Sprague & his wife —
To bed before 11.
