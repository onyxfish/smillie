## Left Page

### October, Thursday 9, 1890

Delightful, nice day. latter
part of afternoon, overcast presaging
another change of weather. As
I felt very unlike going on with
my proving, altho' paper & ink
were ready, I determined to spend
the day out of doors with the men
directing the work that now must
be done — so, I was out of doors
all day with Cagle & Stone giving
directions in transplanting &
doing fall pruning. Tea
at 6.30 — Before 8 went down
town to Mawhinny with my
overcoat to have it button repair.
Eve'g. Annie read aloud
Shelton's very interesting army
Experience pub. in "Century"
for Oct. To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 10, 1890

Bright early w'f — nice — later
cloudy with a short smart shower
at about — o'clk. & another one in
the early eve'g. It was 11 o'clk,
as usual, before I got to my studio
work. I printed until 1 o'clk
& had dinner at 1.30 — again, in
the afternoon I printed until
near 5 & then washed up. At 6
I went to P.O. Tea — eve'g
I was very uncomfortable with
a headache & rolled in the
invalid chair in dining room
while Annie read aloud an
article in the last Century —
To bed at 10.30.
