## Left Page

### June, Thursday 5, 1890

An exceedingly hot day — Ther. got up to 90°. After bkf. I went to Dine. & Amend & got Rubber cloth then to Park Row to get castors & fastenings for Montrose — then to F.W. Devon's about Sepia — then to A.B.N. Co — then to D.L. & W. office in Park Place — then to J.K. Campbell's — then to studio, then home to lunch at 1:5 — after lunch spent some time unpacking big box packed two or three days ago by a packer, expect to find where the fair price came from that was running out of it — found an awful mess, but [illegible] could not find the source. Rose down to Cyrus Prosch's in Broome's — he fixed my ink Roller — then to Sharp's in Baptist's — got some small odds & ends of copper — then to studio — Home before 6 & rested for awhile — at 6.45 Annie & I went to Geo. & Hinely's & dined with them left before 9 o'clk — Home & packed again the big box out of which I had pulled the stuffing after lunch — To bed at 10.30 —
Heavy thunder storms during the night.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 6, 1890

Warm only, growing gradually overcast — afternoon, very cool east wind & at 6 o'clk a heavy thunder storm — To studio about 10 o'clk. & worked there until after 12 — then to Knodder's — 2°. Nat. BM. & Kurtz's — Home soon after 1 — back as usual & again worked until after 5 o'clk. Among other things made a rough pen sketch for Mr. Metcalf who is preparing for the N.Y. Tribune a short paper on the summer homes of N.Y. artists — Caught in shower on my way home. Found Jim's birthday festivities in progress. Annie had invited eleven of his little cousins to a frugal feast & all seemed to enjoy very much. I rested awhile & at 6.45 A. & I went to Walter's where we dined — Ed & Mary making the party — after dinner Louise favored us with some delightful music on her new Steinway Grand — Home at [illegible] & right to bed.
