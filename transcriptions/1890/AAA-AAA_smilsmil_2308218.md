## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 25, 1890

A nasty, raw, drizzling day — got to studio at 10.30 — & divided my t[ime] between tracing Union Trust Co. block for P.B.N. Co — & working with crayon over a photo print of Shirlaw's "Sleep" that I am to megatint for the Pratt Institute — As the tracing is trying & hateful work to me, the division of work was not an even one. — Home at 1 — back at 2.45. I divided the work between tracing & crayon work on big sketch of "Poppies" for dry-point. More crayon work than tracing. Home at 6 — dinner at 6.30. Every Annie went to her Rehearsals — I wrote letter to Kate — Chas. Collins came in for a few minutes — then Chas. Smillie came in — I got to bed at about 11 —

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 26, 1890

Bright & pleasant, though cool & at times somewhat Marchy — Felt better, generally, than I have for some days past. Got to studio at 9.30 and spent the day drawing plans for a set of cold frame for tomato plants. — we stand much in need of something of the kind at Montrose. Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — finished drawings before 5 — took them to Station 7 & mailed them to Herrick. — then a walk & home at 6.15 — dinner. Evg., after my newspaper I spent some time completing my list of vegetable seeds for Montrose & partly arranged for the flower seeds. To bed about 10.
