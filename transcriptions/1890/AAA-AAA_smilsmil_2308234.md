## Left Page

### April, Saturday 26, 1890

Overcast but dry until near noon, then rain began + kept at it pretty steadily for the rest of the day + eveg.
Got to studio + on after 10 + to work painting on 18×30 — Gifford came in about noon + stayed until 10'clk — then I went home to lunch — back at 2.45 + painted until near 5 — but did not get canvas covered. Washed brushes + home at 6 '5". dinner — a nasty and rainy eveg. So, we did not go to Private View of Socy of Am. Artists, but read until about 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 27, 1890

Damp, overcast, wet, most of the mg. — an attempt at clearing in the afternoon — + a wet eveg.

Spent much of the mg. arranging die proofs — dinner a little before 2 — after that Annie + I went to the Cooper Union where the seventh of a series of free concerts for working people was given. The work is one that Felix Adler is interested in. Frank Damrosch is the musical director and the Met. Opera orchestra does the playing. The Choral Club agreed to sing after their part in the programme. Annie + I sat back of the orchestra where I enjoyed the novelty of listening to an orchestra in their midst. At 5, walked up home. Had a cup of tea at 6.30 — Eveg. finished the arrangement of my die proofs. Charle came in for a little while at 10 o'clk. To bed just before 11.
