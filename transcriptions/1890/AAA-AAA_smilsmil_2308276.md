## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 23, 1890

Pleasant day with south easterly
wind — overcast after 5 p.m —
In garden until 11. then in to
Etching Room & before dinner
took three proofs — disappointed
to find that the plate is consider-
ably over bitten. Nearly 1.30 when
we had dinner — at 3 all hands
started out in Pic-nic wagon
for Salt Spring — But for the good be-
havior of the horses we might have
had a serious accident. The traces
being too long the end of the pole
slipped out of the ring of the neck-
yoke — In a few seconds things looked
decidedly squally — the pole swayed
striking both horses & the wagon ran
upon their heels — I was able to catch
hold it with the brake & then quieted
the horses. To compensate for his bad
behavior Sam acted like the mischief
on his way over. While the party went
up to the falls I remained with the horses
at 5.20 we started on our return
getting home at 6.45 — Every talk
& planning a trip to Belle Port and
reading newspaper — I made a
check & wrote note to Equitable Life
Ass. Socy.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 24, 1890

Weather threatened until the after-
noon when rain commenced — heavy
rain at night. As usual, I spent
most of the m't. out of doors among
the flowers & in the garden among the
vegetables — about 11, made selection
of a fine Pansy flower & commenced
an example of pure graver work, on a
small copper plate, after the Pansy —
Dinner at 1 — Soon after 2 again at
work with graver & continued until
about 5 o'clk. Then, feeling very tired
I took some exercise on piazza —
Tea soon after 6. After that went
down to Post Office — etc — etc — Very
heavy showers with wind. Later
in the evg. Mary & Miss Williams
played Whist against Annie & me.
To bed at 10.30 with a very uncom-
fortable head.
