## Left Page

### March, Friday 7, 1890

A brilliant winter day — Ther. 17° at 8 AM. I got to studio before 10 &
to work on 20×30 "A bit of brook" —
Repainting solidly the water — the
central clumps of bushes & banks
of the stream on the left hand, improving the picture very much. Home
to lunch at 1.20. Annie & Sue at
Unitarian league — Back to studio
before 3 — Did not do much painting
— at 5 went to Mrs. & Rusty Rees in 16°
St. She wants to buy an old Hotted
place — saw a young woman who represented her — [illegible] for "Painter & Etcher"
plates — Home soon after 6 — went to
Ed[ward]'s for particulars — Mary has been
telegraphed for — her father ill — She & Ed[ward]
go on to Buffalo tonight. Dinner at 6.30
self. Fred Ireland came & went with
Annie & Sue to Met. Opera Ho. Meistersinger.
Tried to answer Sarah Hammond's letter
— gave it up — Read until 10 — then to bed

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 8, 1 o'clk, 1890

*Will Kimball died last night at 1 o'clk.*

Bright cold day — a note was left
at the house about 7 o'clk. A.M. telling
us of the death of Will Kimball — a
great shock to us — He has been confined to the house only a week — a
case of general collapse. I got
to studio about 10 & spent a short
working day charcoaling 20×30
up-right (not christened yet) an
up-hill road with calf — ducks and
watering trough — hoping that it will
please Charle & feel the place of "Spring
is coming". Home to lunch as usual
& to dinner at 6 — Sue went to
New Haven at 10 A.M. to spend Sunday with the Adams' — She is to meet
Annie on the train on Monday to go on
to Cambridge together. Evening
Annie & I went to 5th Phil. Concert,
a very pleasant, enjoyable, old-
fashioned programme. Home
soon after 10 — & to bed.
