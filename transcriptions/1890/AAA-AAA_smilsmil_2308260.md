## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 17. 1890.

An overcast day — one of those disagreeable combinations of close or muggy warmth & chilling breeze.
B'kf't about 8 — then some work out of doors — directing Peter — etc. I picked some very large berries & took them down town with me, where they created quite an excitement. Jim & Ralph went with me. It was noon before I ret'd. home. Before I Henick started for New Milford to get Edw'd & Walter who came from N.Y. on 9 A.M. Train. I wrote to E.J. Denning & Co — Geo. & Chat. and worked about among the vines as long as I could see. Went to P.O. at 4.30. The boys arrived in good shape at 5. Tea at 6. Eve'g reading news papers. To bed after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 18. 1890.

Overcast w'f'. The clouds soon burned away & a delightful day followed with a pleasantly cool eve'g. Henick was so sick that he could not come to his work. I spent much of the m'f', as usual, among the vegetables & berries. After 10 I had the donkey harnessed & went down town with Jim & Edw'd giving them a lesson in driving — attended to messages & home in about an hour. Made up an aqua-tint solution on J.J. Pease's receipt. Dinner abt 1. Worked in training vines — at 4 helped Oscar harness the horses & we all rode until 6. Called to see the Parks just arr'd from Scranton & Henick. Home at 6½. Tea — Eve'g reading newspaper & writing to M. Korheen. To bed about 10.30.
