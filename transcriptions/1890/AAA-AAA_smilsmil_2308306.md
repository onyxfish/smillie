## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 23, 1890

Brilliant day- from 4 to 6 p.m.
being cloudy + windy- a dash of
rain before 10 p.m. A very busy
day- out in garden until afternoon-
then to work in etching room
proving soft-ground plate + etching
Box with 75 Weigela plants arrived.
After dinner got all hands to work
at setting them out + I too pitched
in, giving up my proving. I had
had the man go to the woods in
the m'f + get a big load of leaf
mould. This was mixed with
screened earth + taken to the holes
+ sifted about the roots of the plants.
By working until 6, I got 30 plants
set out- Logier working with me.
Eve'g, some newspaper + some
Stanley. - To bed between 10 + 11.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 24, 1890

Ther. fell to 40° last night.

Brilliant day but windy + cool. I
spent the day out of doors directing my
force of seven men. Alspoler + Stone having
joined my little army. The first thing
done in the m'f was the taking down
of the big Balm of Gilead tree on the
Lane near the road — old, broken + much
decayed — all day was devoted to
completing a French + drain, improving
the ground about the roots of some of the
trees, setting out nearly all the Weigela,
+ clearing up the terrible confusion
as much as possible. At noon I went
to my etching room + laid a rebiting
ground upon portions of soft-ground etching.
Dinner — all the afternoon with the men
until 5.30 — Eve'g. had quite a nap
after tea — then newspaper — again
Annie read aloud from Stanley +
I went to bed soon after 10 o'clk.
