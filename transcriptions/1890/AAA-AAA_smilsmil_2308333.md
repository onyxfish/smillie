## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 18, 1890

Charming day — To studio before 10 & to work upon Shirlaw's mezzotint — a good solid m[or]n'g of work. Home to lunch at 1 — got sketch of Annie's hands as I could not follow Shirlaw's rough scratches & the imperfect photo. To studio at 2.45 and worked until 4.45. Then wet down paper for printing to-morrow — walked down to W. S; then up home — Mollie Adams just arr'd from New Haven — Dinner at 6 — at 7.15 took Annie to Ed W. Cook's that she might go to Brooklyn with Mary, Louise, Miss Williams, Walter & Mr. Valentine to hear a sermon by Mr. Cal[illegible] — I ret'd home & wrote to Miss Hallowell, S.R. Koehler, Carlton Chakman & Mr. Selden — Kate & Mollie got to bed early — I did not wait for Annie but got to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 19, 1890

Bright & pleasant day — got to studio about 9.45 & after working some time on Shirlaw's mezzotint I took a couple of proofs — also four [illegible] from a small plate that Colman has etched for the Etching Club cat. It was nearly 1.45 before I got home to lunch — after 3 when I returned — It took most of the remaining daylight to clean up & then Chas. Collins came to submit a proposition from Go. Haven Putnam for the organization & incorporation of the Knickerbocker Press Co. He stayed until Annie came for me at 4.45. She & I then went to Tracey Etching Co., 5th Ave. & 28th S — Too late — then home — dinner — Evg. I went to meeting of Water-Color Socy. Home at 10.30 & to bed.
