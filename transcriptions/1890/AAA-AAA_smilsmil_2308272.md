## Left Page

### July Tuesday 15. 1890

A very warm mts. clouding over before noon — the outskirts of a thunder storm fell quietly upon us after 1 o'clk & light showers fell until 6 o'clk — Quiet, foggy evng. In garden until 10 o'clk — there making drawings for Herrick — posts for Stair by well. Parke & Mrs. P. came soon after 11 — I got a little work done on ink sketch "On the River Marne" — dinner — then more painting but soon after 5 it was so dark that I gave up. Annie came into studio & read awhile aloud. After tea there was a better light & I painted until near 7 — then I went to P.O. Evng I read newspaper & to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 16. 1890

*Ed. & Mary Cook arr.*

A hot day, but with a delightful breeze — Heavy clouds in the [illegible] promising a thunder storm that did not come — a film overcast the sky part of the P.m. Evng. singularly still & mild. I spent the [illegible] as usual out of doors until 11 — then in — laid wax ground on 5"×7" plate — made tracing outline of "Old House near Boulogne" — transferred it to wax ground — made outline with burnisher thro' the ground & then laid an aqua tint ground over the outline, stopped it out ready for biting. Herrick went to New Milford at 3 o'clk with the team & Parke's big wagon & Ret. at 8.15 with Ed. & Mary Cook, Cath. Edna & Tom & Miss Williams. They were delighted with their ride & after a light supper, spent the remainder of the aftn evng on the piazza that was very prettily lighted with Chinese lanterns — after tea — at 7.30 Guie, Jim & I went down to P.O — to bed about 11.
