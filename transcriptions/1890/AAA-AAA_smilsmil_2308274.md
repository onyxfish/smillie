## Left Page

### July, Saturday 19, 1890

A very cool, breezy day — beautiful clouds & sunlight. It heavy dash of a shower — over in a minute — about noon. I got to my work about 10 o'clk, made fine in etching room — heated acid & commenced biting aqua-tint — "old house, etc." I worked until near 5 o'clk, without having finished the biting — Then all hands went out riding — the children & the donkey had disappeared — we drove about town for half an hour before we found them — there they were piled in with us & we drove around the lake — in at 6.30 — I was amused to see Ralph only a few days past his 3rd birthday, sitting alone in the cart, driving the donkey from the barn to the house. After tea Edw'd & I went downtown & engaged a horse to take him over to Jn'r. Bend to-morrow p.m. Eve'g. by a pleasant fine reading — To bed after 10.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 20, 1890

Edw'd left us.

Cloudy & breezy with dashes of rain clearing later to a lovely afternoon & eve'g. I managed to get a little writing done in the m'g. Edw'd left us at 5 to go over to Jn'r. Bend & thence by Erie R.R. to Buffalo. We had dinner at about 2 — At 4 I harnessed the horses & all hands went off for a ride, getting back at 6 o'clk. Eve'g I wrote a letter to Alex. Schilling on Etching Club matters & to bed at 10.30.
