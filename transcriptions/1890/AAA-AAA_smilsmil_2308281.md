## Left Page

### August, Saturday 2, 1890.

A very warm day — Bkft. after 8 —
After that, with Walter, went through
his stables & kitchen garden — then
over to Mr Hoyt's house. The family
off on a yachting cruise — we went
over the house. From 11 to 1 an rode
— powerful hot — Then a very substan-
tial lunch — About 4 we went off
on a sailing expedition — two boats —
the "Effie" & the "Broad Bill" — Many & Ed. in
the B.B. Walter, Louise, Annie & I in the
Effie with Joned as sailing master —
Quite a thick fog obscured the sun
most of the time — back soon after 6 —
Dinner at about 7 — Ev'g. Louise
played for us upon her piano — we
went to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 3, 1890.

A mild, dull day — Bkft. at 9 o'clk
at 11 o'clk. we went down to the bay, all
hands, & from the dock fished for crabs &
by 1 o'clk. we had caught over one hundred.
Then to the house — lunch before 2 o'clk.
After that I had a short nap. About
4 o'clk. we went sailing — Walter &
Louisa in the Broad Bill. Ed., Mary,
Annie & I in the "Effie" — very little
wind & much loafing — Going west,
beyond Bellport, we made "the 'Noah'" —
a house-boat built by Mr Hoyt. He & his
family were aboard, on their return
from Babylon. Home at 6.30 — Dinner
about 7 — Very mild ev'g. we
all sat out on the western piazza
until 10 o'clk. Then to bed. For
a little while Annie & I watched
the moon & its light over the bay &
creeks from the balcony of our bed-
room.
