## Left Page

### March, Friday 21, 1890

A nasty day of rain & slush —
clearing about 4 — Mrs. J. went
to Shirlaw's studio, 3 Washington
Sq — & saw a number of his paintings
but like best for me just the one
of which I have a photo. Then to
Knoedler's at 2°. Nat. Buk & to
studio after 11 — more pasting down
& drawing paper — Home at 1 &
back at 2.30 — wrote to Norblert &
others. Home at 6 — dinner —
Evg. Annie & I went to
Chickering Hall & heard a very
interesting lecture on the literature
of the Ancient Egyptians by Miss
Amelia Edwards — Home soon
after 10 & in bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 22, 1890

A dark, drenching, pouring day —
After 10 when I got to studio where
I spent the mf. in going through
portfolios & drawers arranging
my artistic possessions — tearing
up many old proofs & sketches —
putting things together in orderly &
methodical groups & in other ways
"putting my house in order" — "for the
night cometh when no man can
work". Home to lunch at 1 —
Annie & I went at 2.30 to Chickering
Hall & heard Miss Edwards's last
lecture in this country — she sails
for England next week. Subject
"Ancient Egyptian women". Again
she was very interesting, but continually
the question arose within me — How
do you know what you assert so pos-
itively. After that I went to studio and
worked as I had done in the mf.
Home at 6.30 — Evg. quietly at home —
Did some work arranging my collection
of father's die proofs — To bed about
11 o'clk.
