## Left Page

### June, Sunday 29, 1890

Glorious day — altho' very warm a refreshing breeze tempered the heat. B[illegible] — at about 8 — then in garden & with plants & vines until 11 o'clk — when, with Annie & the 4 boys, I went up in the Warner lot & around, back of the Fair grounds. It took us until nearly 1 o'clk to do it — then home to dinner. In the afternoon I wrote some letters & at a little after 4 we went at riding getting back a little after 6 —
Ev'f. wrote to Charl, enclosing letter rec'd yesterday from Kate. Took letters to P.O. about 10 o'clk. Glorious moonlight night — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 30, 1890

Very hot but not unpleasant. After my usual out of door work in the m[or]f. I got my printing establishment in order & took a couple of india pts. from my little aqua-tint plates. This consumed most of what remained of the m'f. After dinner, as the sky had become overcast, I transplanted some young Poppy plants. We did not go out riding in the afternoon — Ev'f. I wrote my regular letter to Kate & took it down town to P.O. at 10.30. It was 11 when I got to bed.
