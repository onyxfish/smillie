## Left Page

### June, Monday 9, 1890

*Montrose.*

Delightful m'f. The alarm clock went off an hour earlier than I set it; consequently we were aroused at 5 o'clk. A further consequence was that we never before got away with so little hurry or flurry. The truck arr. at 7 & loaded up in 20 m. we had our blkfs. & the last of spoons etc. were packed into a trunk for the men to take away — We left the house at 8, leaving Kate & Mary (who had come down to help) in charge to attend to the little last things & then lock up. We got to foot of Christo. St. at 8.30 — To Hoboken & the cars at 9 & were off at 9.20 —. The ride proved to me to be hot & tiresome — Fanny Mueford Connell surprised us by coming on the train at Manunka chunk. We had dinner at Scranton as usual & got to New Milford at 3. Herrick & team were on hand — also a team from Pell's to carry our trunks & handmaidens — We took Fanny Connell up with us & left her at Ben Muelford's. We got home at 5. The country is charming but the roads are shocking owing to tremendous rains — Mrs. Curry was at the house & we had tea at 6 o'clk. The children were soon in bed & I went downtown at 8 o'clk & attended to a number of small matters — went in to Ben M's for a few minutes. Home at 9 & to bed about 10 — Annie sleeping with the children in the nursery.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 10, 1890

Very pleasant day — awake very early — 4 o'clk. & heard the birds' m'f song — then to sleep again — Up for all day about 7 — The m'f was spent wandering about the place looking especially after the cold frames, the vegetable garden & the young flowering plants — Harnessed Peggy to the cart & took out Jim, Ralph & Dana Watrous for a short ride — The little beast did very well. Dinner after 1 o'clk — After that, felt so heavy that I lay down & took a long nap — Annie by my side did the same — After that it was too late for a ride — a shower before tea time — Early even. went downtown & attended to little house matters — on my return sat & read until 10 o'clk & went to bed at 10.30 uncomfortably chilly.
