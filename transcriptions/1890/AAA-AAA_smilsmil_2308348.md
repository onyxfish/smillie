## Left Page

### December, Saturday 20, 1890

Lovely day — Bright & much mild[er].
To studio at 9.30. Took 2 proofs
experimental, of neg[ative]. M[ade] to work on 5"×7" line etching.
Before noon Coleman came in &
I made an unsuccessful attempt
to take proofs from one of his plate.
Home to lunch at 1.45 — nearly
3 when I got to studio but by
working hard I succeeded in
getting several p[roof]s from etching
"A bit of brook with some sheep". D
Gerster was with me for an hour.
Home at 6. After dinner Cole's
children came & practised Xmas
Carols for half an hour. Datu[m]
gel came in for a little while.
Being very tired I went to bed
before 10.

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 21, 1890

A mild day — overcast — a little rain
between 1 & 2 o'clk. clearing soon
after. BKlp: at 9 — wrote a letter
to Herrick — at 12.30 went to Union
league Club — 9[?]° — Soon came in and
with Charl we went to the new
Century Building in 43rd St. Went all
over the building which is approach-
ing completion — many things disappoint-
ed me, but more than all others, the
picture gallery, which is radically
defective in many respects. It is
imperfectly lighted & the walls are badly
cut up. Charl went home with me
at 2 o'clk. dinner — at 4 I went to
studio to mount a proof or two that I
must deliver to-morrow. Home at 6.
Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g, loafing with
newspaper.
