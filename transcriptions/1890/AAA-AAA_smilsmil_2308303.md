## Left Page

### September, Monday 15, 1890

Another cool w'f., heavy fog with some rain. I built fire in the studio & was just about to begin a days work indoors when boy am'd from Wm S. Lettes with strawberry plants, asparagus, etc. Coyle came up soon after & the rest of the w'f was spent setting out for dozen plants — of four varieties — after dinner Coyle & Hill commenced digging an asparagus trench in the garden but left on acct. of rain soon after. I wrote to Lette to Chas. Pratt & to Saml. Colman. Eve'f I wrote a letter to Man & to [illegible] P.O. at 9.30 — After my return Annie read aloud from Stanley — It was 11 o'clk when we got to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 16, 1890

The w'f opened with a thunder storm & heavy rain — About noon it cleared — bright & pleasant before sunset — at 11 P.M. heavy thunderstorms to the S.W. more heavy rain during the night. I got to work in etching room at about 11 — after several attempts laid a 5×7 soft ground — also a ground for new work on 5×7 etching — after dinner, as Coyle did not come Oscar & I set to work in the garden & set out 45 asparagus plants — It took us until after 6 o'clk. Tea. Eve'f, at a little before 8 went to P.O. There, for a time reading newspaper — later Annie Read from Stanley aloud until about 10.30 — to bed at 11.
