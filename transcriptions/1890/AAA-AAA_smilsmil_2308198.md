## Left Page

### February, Thursday 13, 1890

Delightful day — my head bothering
me still. Got to studio at 10 — took
Wash'n Wilson's pl. of Goldsmith's Daughter
to Butler's & had it put into frame.
Back to studio at 10.30 — worked for
a time over a large frame full
of Etching's pl. to hang back of screen
— broke the glass & gave it up. Dr.
Hare came in & as he went out
Swain Gifford came in. He brought
a plate of his up on which he wished
me to scour down a part of it. Home
to lunch at 1.30 — after it, Annie
went with me to Avery's Gallery & saw
the Colman pictures, — also the David
Johnston pictures in the Ortgies Gallery —
then to studio — at 4.30 went to Schaus's
then to Knodlers & then to Wunderlich's
dinner — Every. Annie & Mary [illegible]
cook addressed envelopes for Walter's
wedding cards. I took many home
at 10.30 — then back & right to bed
feeling very seedy.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 14, 1890

Dull m'f. with a pouring rain
in the afternoon & bright evening.
Got to studio at 10 & spent the
morning writing notes. Home
soon after 1 — after lunch, went
with Annie & Jim over to the Citrus
(Citrus) Fair — 6th Ave & 35th St. — a
large display of Oranges & Lemons from
San Bernardino Co, Cal. On my way
to studio was caught in a perfect
deluge of rain — Annie & Jim had
to take three lines of cars to get home.
At Studio I jiyed up a large frame
of etchings to hang back of screen.
Home at 6. After dinner wrote a
letter to Herrick — Mary Cook came
again to address envelopes with
Annie for Walter & Louise — Clark
came in a little later with a very
painful wrist that afterward the Dr.
said was a Rheumatic trouble. For
some days I have been fighting off a
headache — after dinner I went all
to pieces — gave up & went to bed soon
after 9.
