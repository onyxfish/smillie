## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 8. 1890.

*[above printed header, in handwriting:]* grand dinner — N.A. of D. —

Sunshine in the m'f — soon overcast & later a dismal rain into which were projected some thunder showers — but not enough to enliven it — quite a heavy thunder storm during the night having gone to [illegible] Gallery & got [illegible] to studio about [illegible] & worked on Union autograph on some "[illegible] Barggin" photo — trust Co. outline. To lunch at 1 and back soon after 2 — worked until after 4 on outline — Bid Champney good bye — he & his family sail for Europe in the m'f. Home about 5 — rested for ¾ of an hour then dressed — at 7 went to N.A. of Design — a brilliant assemblage of men — about 125 — the plan of tables and the dinners thereof will be found in one of the pockets of this book. At 7.35 we took our places & until after 9 were attending to victuals & drink — soon after that Mr. Huntington made a historical address — not too long & very interesting — He was followed by Geo. Wm. Curtis whose address was delightful to listen. He made the whole affair a success. After him, Parke Godwin — & then Jos. Choate — then a short poem by R.H. Stoddard — then a few words from S.J. Helkes — a short address by Wm. S. Hodge & a wind up by that embodiment of complacent egotism — Andrew Carnegie — It was about 12 when we broke up — & 1 o'clk when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 9. 1890

Dismal day — much rain & again some mixed thunder — To studio about 11 having something of a head on me — not to be wondered at considering the experience of last night. I did not do much work — neither did George. I made pen sketch — position in holding mezzatint rocker when laying a ground. Wrote note & sent it to S.R. Koehler — the painter's old father, Gardiner, died this m'f. To bank — then home soon after 1 — It was 3.30 when I got back to studio — worked on outline Union Trust — until after 5 — Miss Lamport, of Rochester, called — Home at 6.30 — Alice Ireland took dinner with us — Evelf Geo. Cook & Mrs. Valentine came in for a few minutes — I read newspaper — then completed list of flower seeds & wrote to Mrs. J. Childs for pansy seeds & to bed at 10.30.
