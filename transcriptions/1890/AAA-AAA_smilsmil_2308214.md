## Left Page

### March Monday 17. 1890

A march-like day — cold, windy
& dirty — It was nearly 11 when
I got to studio but late as 'twas
I commenced the painting of
Charlie's picture 20 × 30 — stuck
to it until — then to Bank —
walked up 5th Ave. & stopped
to see Gifford's (R. Swain) pictures
at Avery's & the Butler (Richard)
collection — It was 2 o'clk. when
I got home to lunch & 3.15 when
I got to Studio — I painted until
after 5 — It was 6.30 when I got
home — dinner — Evelf. Annie
the Adams girls & Mr. W.G. Parke
went to Met. opera Ho. Siegfried
without escort. I stayed at home
& wrote — Charl came in at 9 &
stayed until 10.30 — then I went
to bed — Annie came in after
midnight.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 18. 1890

A very pleasant day — growing
milder & less windy — To studio at
9.30 — Again painting on Charl's
picture as long as I could see, but
did not get it all in — Home to
lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 & home
to dinner, very tired, soon after 6 —
Evelf. Annie went to her Singing
club while I wrote letter to Mary —
I posted it after 10 & got to bed
before Annie came home.
