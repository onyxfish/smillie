## Left Page

### February, Thursday 27, 1890

Overcast, raw & disagreeable —
Got to studio at 10.30. and to
work painting on 20×30" Afternoon —
Pond near Montrose, Pa" — Home at
1.30 to lunch — Katie & Mollie Adams
went home in the afternoon. I got
to studio at 2.30 — Frame for 20×30
came soon after — I painted till
4.30 then to N.A. meeting of the
Academicians to whom the Council
made a proposal to have a great
dinner at the opening of the Spring
exhib". The matter was referred
to the Council with full power —
Thence to Coleman H.C. to find Dimock
Searle — failed — Thence to studio —
Home at 6.30 — dinner — Evel'f wrote
note to Century Com. on Admissions
proposing W. Hamilton Gibson for
membership — Chase came in
at 9 o'clk & stayed until 10 —
To bed at 10.30. Rain —

## Right Page

### February, Friday 28, 1890

A nasty, drizzling, niggling wet day —
got to studio after 10 — set palette &
pitched in to second painting on
20×30 "A bit of brook with ducks".
I worked as faithfully as I could
& got all painted over down to the
foreground — I feel quite encouraged
thinking there are better qualities
of light, air & treatment than I have
got heretofore. Home to lunch soon
after 1 — back at 2.30 & painted until
4.30 — washed some brushes, cleaned
up & then went in to Rose & Co's &
ordered another 20×30 frame & one
12×16 for "Hagenbrook in April" — Home
at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Evel'f wrote my
regular letter to Herrick — Mr. [illegible] N,
Morse came in for a few minutes at
about 9 o'clk — To bed about 10.

The large water tank in attic gave
out & this n't the plumbers began
the putting in of a new lead lining.
