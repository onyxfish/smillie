## Left Page

### November, Monday 10, 1890

A pleasant day — fresh northerly breeze & much cloudiness, but not cold.
Got to studio about 10 o'clk. A man from Clark's came to attend to my gas
fixings that have been leaking. He spent most of the m'f. with me.
I took nearly all the m'f. to clean out the drawers of my cut. Table.
Home at 1 — lunch — back at 2.30
& I started on Mezzatint plate after
Shirlaw's Sleep. Felt somewhat
the need of his aid to determine
some of the vague drawings that at
4.45 I went to his studio — He was
out for the moment but his wife entertained me until his return. I
got home at 6.30 after another call
on W. H. W. Edwards, our neighbors, concerning needed repairs to our fence.
Dinner. Ev'g., read until near 9.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 11, 1890

Rainy m'f., an attempt at clearing before night. I got to studio
before 10 — sometimes later Shirlaw
came & spent the rest of the morning
working over photos of his painting
"Sleep" for which I am to make at mezzo
tint plates for Pratt Institute. I, in
the mean time, writing several
notes & cleaning some pigeon-holes in
my desk. Home to lunch at 1.30
& back to studio at about 3. and
did a little work on the Shirlaw
plate. Home at 6, dinner at
6.30. Eliz. Howe dined with us
— at 7.45 Emma Ireland came
true, all four, went to Met. Opera
House to celebrate Emma's birthday & to witness H. M. Stanley's first
appearance after his return from
"Darkest Africa" — an immense
crowd but we could hear very little
of what he said. Eliz. & Emma
got a dist. mess'. boy to take them home.
We got to bed soon after 11.
