## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 4, 1890

A breezy, rather raw day, with plenty of sunshine & shadow.
Got to studio at 9.30 — Mrs. Duff soon after — & we had a field day
scrubbing out the studio. I bossed things until about noon & then
left her — went home & had dinner & back to studio about
3 — there until 5 — Mrs. D. got all done except the big windows.
Eve'g., unpacked big box and smaller ones Rec'd this mf.
from Montrose. About 11 o'clk when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 5, 1890

Delightful day — cloudless & mild.
Got to studio at 9.30. Mrs. Duff followed me soon after to wash the
big window — The weights stuck so that she could not get the sashes up & down
freely — Mrs. D. balked & said she could not do it — I told her I could.
So, I paid her & I cleaned the window
then unpacked Montrose box, hung curtains & made good progress towards getting into order. Soon after
12, went to Bank — then home to dinner — Back to studio before
3 & worked until 5 straightening things — Home at 6 — Tea — Evening
a little before 8. Walter, Louise &
Miss Williams came & took us to
the 5th Ave. Theatre. Bdway & 28th St.
where we saw W. & Mrs. Kendal "in The
Squire" — a shocking bad play, to my
mind — Mrs. K.'s wailing & anguish
was very admirably done but I was
too unfeeling to weep — I felt only that
I was intrusive & should have refrained
from looking so coldly upon a woman's
sorrows & tears — out at about 10.30 — all
walked up home — entertained ourselves
with crackers & grapes. To bed at 11.30.
