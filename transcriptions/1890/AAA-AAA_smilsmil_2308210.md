## Left Page

### March, Sunday 9, 1890

Brilliant, beautiful day — Bkfp. about 9 — after reading papers for
awhile I commenced to write
a letter answering S.C.J. Hammond's
letter of three weeks ago — Afternoon
Annie & I walked up to 66th St. —
& Central Park & then home before
dinner at 2 — After — at about 4 —
A. & I went to the Kimball's. 42 W.
22d. St. — A. saw Fanny & Carrie —
I saw Rodney & his son — Alfred Kimball
& miss Nelson — It was, of course, a very
painful visit — after that, we walked
over to Aunt Annie's — E. 17th St. — Saw her,
Emma & Grace — & Mrs. Coleman —
Home soon after 6 — dressed — Tea —
At 7.30 we went to Mrs. Lewis's in
E. 16th St. a company of 50 or more to hear
the Beethoven quartette play & Miss
Hamilton sing, — a very pleasant
entertainment — we left at 11 —
To bed just before 12.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 10, 1890

*Annie went to Cambridge.*

Milder & somewhat overcast. Before
bkfp. went to G.C.D. & gave order to [illegible]
man to call for Annie's trunk. Then
home — had bkfp. — again to depot
with Annie & got her off on the 10 A.m.
Shore Line train to Boston. She is to join
her at New Haven. After marketing I
went to studio, getting there at 11 o'clk.
About noon I went to Bk of Metropolis
& made a deposit of diet kitchen funds
for Annie — Then to Mrs. Ruenitz Rees — Saw
her, but our business came to naught — Then
to 2d. Nat. Bk. Met Gifford there — we went
to Reichard's & saw the Abbey & Parsons draw-
ings — then walked up 5th Ave. — I got home
to lunch after 2 — It was after 3 when I
got to studio — Planned & made rough
charcoal sketch, upright, on 20×24 canvas —
Home at 6 — dinner at usual time —
After that Jim wrote a letter to his mother —
So did I — about 8 I went to Ed'wt Cooks —
Telegram just rec'd from Buffalo. W. Sprague
continuing to improve. Home & finished
my letter to Annie — Read until 10 —
To bed soon after.
