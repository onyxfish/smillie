## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 14, 1890.

A very warm day with thunder-showers in the eveg. I had help in bed
& then wrote a note to Mr. L.C. Holden
(Parker's Architect) & sent Messenger boy
to put it on my studio's door — Up & dressed
before 10 — during the mg. Belle, Mindy
& Eliz Howe spent some time with me.
Dinner after 1 o'clk — about ¾ I went
to studio & found that the fool of a messenger boy had stuck note to W. Holden under
the door instead of on it & that W. H. had
been there — wrote a note to him & to J.
Harrison Mills & gathered some sketching
materials & home at 5.30 — much disappointed that I could not go to N.A. of D.
Annual Meeting & dinner. Eliz. H.
took tea with us at 7 & spent the eveg.
Geo. & Mindy came in — & so did Charl.
Annie at last decided that we go
to Virginia Beach, near Norfolk, Va.,
starting on Friday at 2 ½ P.M. —
To bed at 11 & from 3 to 6 A.M,
had another horrible time with
a stopped up nose.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 15, 1890.

Dark mg., rain beginning before 11 &
continuing heavily until after 1.
I spent some time after bkfp arranging things preparatory to our start
tomorrow afternoon — then went out to
bank — then bot tickets to Virginia Beach
— now raining heavily — then to 788
Bdway to see Alex. Schilling — not in —
then to studio — then home — Eliz.
Howe dined with us soon after 1.
Later went with Annie to Electrical
Exhn. I took a nap — then wrote a letter
to Capt. Meredith, Treasy. Dep. Washington
about getting some finished plt. of two dies
of which I have only etching plts. Then went
to studio & made letter & etching plt. into pkg
to send by mail. A little after 6 o'clk W. De[w]
Huntington came in asking me to call on
Mr. Piermont Morgan to arrange, possibly,
to make an etching after a painting of Mr.
[illegible] S. Morgan — Paralyzingly impertinent
flunkey at the door, who had written
message on my card to Mrs. M — before he
would consent to deliver it — Mrs. M. was
very charming — disposed of preliminaries
in very few moments — she does not know
what an etching is — she will write to Mrs.
Morgan who is now on his way to England —
Eveg at home — Charl came in —
To bed at 10.
