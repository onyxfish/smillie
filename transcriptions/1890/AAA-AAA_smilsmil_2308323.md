## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 29. 1890.

*[top margin note: Ther. fell to 32°. last night.]*

Just as nasty a day as any of them —
considerable snow on the ground when
we got up. Rain & snow squalls
frequent during the day — at times
it looked as though it might clear —
but it did'nt. I worked in-doors
all day — clearing up & packing when
ever I could. I mounted 4 proofs
to send to S. R. Koehler. Went
down Town at 1 o'clk. & again at
6 — am quite discouraged at
the poor prospect of getting the
out-of-doors work done. We &
Mr. & Mrs. Daut. Sayre called — after
that I worked until 10 o'clk. at
packing the big house box. To
bed before 11.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 30. 1890.

More snow on roofs & tops of stone
walls early in the m'g. For a little
while the sun shone but clouds again
covered the sky & the day was a
succession of drizzling, wet snow squalls
very raw & very disagreeable. About
5 P.M. there was a clearing in the west
that promised better weather for to-morrow.
Until noon I worked at finishing the
packing of House & Studio box & pkg. of
proofs for S. R. Koehler — I wrote a letter
to S. R. K. to accompany p'g. At 12.30
I drove over to Darrow's with Herrick,
Coyle & Stone to select trees to set out in
the lane — was much disappointed to
find them so small — left Coyle & Stone
to dig three & drove back in a bitter squall
to dinner at 2 — at 3.30 Herrick & Oscar
took team & trucks with our heavy boxes
to Montrose R.R. depot & then on to Darrow's
got the three trees & back after 4 — the three
trees were there set. Cox came with
a team & took away the piano —
worked out of doors training vines & pruning.
