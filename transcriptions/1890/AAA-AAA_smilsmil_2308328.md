## Left Page

### November, Saturday 8, 1890

A bright, warm day — got up with
a beastly headache that made me
more or less useless all day — got
to studio at 10 o'clk. & spent most
of the m[orn]g. mounting etchings. [illegible]
Schilling came in & spent some time.
Home soon after 1 — dinner — It
was 3 before I again got to Studio
— Soon after 4 went to Mr. Huntington
with proof of Casilean's plate of "Head
of a Sibyl" after H's painting, & got him
to sign it for me — then to Bridgman's
seed store & spent some time there
talking about seeds & roots. Home
at 6 o'clk — tea — Self. Annie
went in to see Miss van Auringe.
A short call there — home soon
after 9 — a little reading & then
to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 9, 1890

most of the day overcast — wind from
the east — very heavy fog at night.
B[reakfast]. at 9 — after that read papers
until 11.30 — then with Annie & the
boys went down to 17th St. & called
on Aunt Anna — on our way up
town called at Geo's new quarters,
N.E. cor. 4th Ave. & 25th St. & saw Nellie
& the children. Home to dinner
at 2 — after that, lounged — Bet.
3 & 4 Charle came in & about 4.30
he, Jim & I walked down Madison
Ave. to Mr. Hill's where we called
on Aunt H. for a little while — while
Jim & I were on our way home we
met Mr. Mosentahl — we went to Sam.
Coleman's apartments in 25th St. to see
if any of his servants were there from
whom we could learn of C's condition
& when they were expected to come to
town — no one in — W. M. walked with
us to 38th St. & Mad. Ave. Tea at 7 —
Evelf., having a stupid head, I slept
in my chair & got to bed before 10.
