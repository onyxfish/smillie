### January, Wednesday 1, 1890

A nasty day — dark & damp but not cold — some niggling rain in the afternoon — At the breakfast table Roger had a fainting spell & spent the rest of the day on the bed. Dr Yale came in to see the invalids at 1.30 & took a cup of tea with us. Soon after I went to Chase's — Too dark to take photos of the children — that engagement is off — then to Geo's to tell him & got to Studio soon after 10. I fussed among my pf. getting some dry-points into press & mounting a Mezzo-tint pf. for the Boston Museum — I also did a little work on preparatory studies for Large Poppy dry-point — Home to lunch at 1 — In the afternoon took a long walk. dinner at 6 — Eve ff. Mollie, Frank, Geo. Cook & Truely — Dunnie & I went to the Met. Opera House & saw & heard Lohengrin — Vagel, a new tenor in the chief part — the other parts were filled fairly and poorly — The house was crowded and quite enthusiastic — also very hot — our seats were good ones in Dress Circle — we enjoyed the performance in spite of the mediocrity of most of the performers — the heat & the length of the representation — we did not get out until after 12 — it was 1 o'clk. when we got to bed.
