## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 9, 1890

Bright, breezy & almost uncomfortably cool. A remarkable contrast to yesterday — Worked out of doors until 10.30 — then in to Studio & after some further experimenting with aqua-tint ground-laying I set my palette & commenced the painting of a sketch 8"×13" "Old house at Boulogne" — intending to make a 5"×7" aqua-tint plate for it. Dinner at 1 — Painted until 4½ then out for a ride. Selim coughed worse than ever before & gave other evidences of the advance of his disease — On our way home, stopped at Mr. Brewster's — Saw him in his hay field & contracted for three tons of best hay, to be delivered when wanted — Home at 6.30 — tea & then some work in studio — Evely. enjoyed a wood fire in the large room. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 10, 1890

Ther. fell to 45° last night. Charming day — very cool, breezy — fine clouds all day. A fine very acceptable in doors, even during the middle of the day — Got up with a headache that bothered me all day. After breakfast I spent an hour or more in the garden. It was after 10 when I built a fire in the studio & got to work painting an 8"×13" sketch of "old mills, Virginia coast" — After dinner, head ache nearly got the better of me but I persisted at my work & finished sketch — At 4.20 we started out for a ride & met Mrs. D. Norris, her sister & hers — on their way to call upon us — we turned about & came back with them & they made a short call — It was about 5 when again we started for our ride — back at 6½. Selim coughing very badly — a great sorrow to me — I cannot drive a horse in such condition. "Chores" occupied me after tea until near 9 o'clk — then read newspaper & got to bed about 10.30
