## Left Page

### October, Friday 31, 1890

**Ther.** fell to 24° last night.

Brilliant mtf. Fair weather continued until after 11 — then the clouds thickened & we had a number of snow squalls — the heaviest at about 2 o'clk. Coyle & Stone took up the large tree that was set out last Tues. p.m. & changed its place with one of the three set in the lane yesterday. I went down town at 12.15 — back — was an hour & soon after had dinner. The afternoon was divided between work out of doors & getting things ready to pack into my trunk — at about 6 O went down town — then tea — Ralph seems to be quite out of sorts — feverish — possibly digestive troubles. Well, sometime making out checks & arranging accounts — then to work at my trunk — got it packed & to bed at 11 —
Ralph up during the night — a bad time with his bowels.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 1, 1890

**At home — 110 E. 38th S. N.Y.**

Bleak, dismal mtf. Snow falling freely — up at 6 — bkfst at 7 and at 9¹⁵ we drove away from the house preceded by our load of trunks and leaving everything locked up. In spite of falling snow it was not cold & in spite of seas of mud our ride to New-Milford was not unpleasant. At many places we were charmed by sad, novembery bits. Got to N.m. 15 or 20 m. ahead of time — train at 11.36 — had comfortable seats in the Drawing Room car, where, of course, it was too hot. The children behaved very well — we got to the end of our journey on time at 5.30 & reached home soon after 6 where we were welcomed by Mary Mooney & promptly had a welcome supper. Our trunks came along soon after & then came Charl who entertained us until 11 with his experiences last summer with the family abroad — then to bed.
