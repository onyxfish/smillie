## Left Page

### December, Monday 8, 1890

A raw, overcast day — with snow from about noon until 3 o'clk — & again in the evef. I had become so tired of my imprisonment that I determined to go out whether or no. At 10 o'clk. I went to Charl's & found him up & dressed — the first time since Thurs. Then I went to Studio — Ther. down to 50°. I turned on Steam & lighted a fire in the grate — Annie came & we went in to Mrs. Champney's for a few minutes — Then I went to Kurtz's — He was at home with a bad throat — got proof of large photo. of Junius S. Morgan portrait — back to Studio — Carleton Chakman came — I went home at 4 o'clk — in quite a snow storm & did not go out again — wrote on my Etching Club paper for awhile in the afternoon — Dinner at 6.30 — Mary & Kate with us — later they went together to Charl's while Annie & I sat by our fire side — to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 9, 1890

Bright day wet air raw with melting snow — It was 10 ½ when I got to Studio. The remains of the m'g was divided bet. fussing with photo. of Junius Morgan, laying out preparations for etching & getting a proof of Aqua-tink stretched for a Dieb. Kitchen Fair. Home at 1 — Charl was with us at lunch, still suffering from Rheumatism — At 2.30 went to Kurtz — he was at home sick — Then to J. S. Church — he was out — Then to Cha. A. Platt's — he was out — Then to Studio at about 4 — Met St. Gester as I went in — he stayed until 5 — Soon after 5 walked up town with Geo. Dinner at 6.45 Evef. Geo & Nellie came — stayed until 10 — then to bed.

we had quite a conflagration at lunch time when the alcohol lamp on the table overflowed & set fire to several things — For a moment there was some excitement but it was soon over — a table cloth destroyed, two or three napkins & a spot on the carpet singed.
