## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 11. 1890.

Not an unpleasant day — the sunshine at times struggling through the milky [illegible]. To studio before 10 & got to work upon 20"×30" going over it very generally. Home to lunch after 1 — feeling very headachy and unlike work I determined to spend the afternoon calling. I stopped at my studio for an address — caught sight of my picture, commenced to paint upon it before I took off my hat & coat with the result that I painted until after 4 o'clk. Then a rest. Home before 6 — dressed — then dinner — Evelf. Annie went with the Dosé's to her club rehearsal & I walked down to Chickering Hall where I met the Colemans & Emma & Mary Ireland — at the concert of the Mendelssohn Glee Club — I sat alone in the gallery — a great crowd — the concert was, to me, a very delightful one. After it I walked with Emma & Mary to their home — then to 38 S. & got to bed a little before midnight.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 12. 1890.

A bright, brisk day — to studio before 10 but I felt so unfit for painting that I spent the rest in fixing up several things that for some time past have been neglected, arranging sticking [illegible] in frames — selecting [illegible] of Goldsmith's Daughter for Wash. Wilson etc. etc. Annie came in about 1 & we walked up home together — After lunch I went down to J. Millett's 92 Clinton Place & spent an hour with him, sitting by him as he painted — then up to "the Judge" Bldg & called upon E. A. Abbey — he was at work with models but he told me to come in & I sat an hour with him — we then walked up to N.A. together — Home at 6:15. Carleton & Catherine Cook dined with us. At 8 o'clk. I went to N.A. — meeting of the W.C. Society to vote the Evans Prize to the best picture in the eighty — no quorum — Sat & chatted with F. S. Church until 9:15 then home. Found "Eliʒ" Home there. At 10 she rang for a [illegible] messenger who took her to Mr. Hill's. To bed at about 11.
