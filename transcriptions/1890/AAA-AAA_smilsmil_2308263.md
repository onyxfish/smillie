## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 25, 1890.

Delightful day — clear, breezy and cool — after bkfst. I went down town & got from Mr. Lyon's Garden a few Pansy blossoms, Ret'd. to studio and, selecting two, made a slight pencil sketch — then to work with acid on one of the small copper plates upon which I had laid an aquatint ground, to reproduce the sketch — Dinner at 1 — after that, picked some fine Strawberries — put them into a box made by Herrick & expressed them to Charl. Went out riding at 4.30 — took the boys to creek in valley west of us & leaving them there, drove on to Allen's farm, where I looked at some standing grass & bargained for hay — Then back — picked up the boys & home at 6.45 — Tea — were Mrs. Jno. Courtright & Florence [Tattnap?] called — Glorious moonlight night — after 9.30 when I got my "chores" done, newspapers — & to bed at near 11.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 26, 1890.

Brilliant day — very breezy — cool in shade & wind & hot in the sunshine — It was near 11 o'clk. before I got to work on my aquatinting experiments — While the large-grained example — with pansies, was in the acid, I made sketches for two other experiments with fine grain — Dinner at 1 — lots of peas & strawberries to-day — After dinner I attempted to raise a kite for the boys & as they say, "I got left" — I wasted an hour & did not raise the kite — Put in a little time on my plates before starting on our ride at 3.45 — Stopped at the Parkes & took Mrs. P— out with us — Back at 6 — After tea Mr. S. Tarbell & wife called — I was engaged until 9 o'clk. at work upon vines, watering plants & attending to the horses — Then newspapers — To bed about 10.30
