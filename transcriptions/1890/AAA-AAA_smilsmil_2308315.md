### October, Saturday 11, 1890

A bleak, cloudy day, growing colder. The day's work was divided between the usual out-f-door duties, looking after the men & printing three proofs of 6×6" mezzo-tints "Singles Hollyhood" & in other ways, getting proofs ready for enounting to complete [illegible] of the 5"×7" series for [illegible] 9. ## Right Page

### October, Sunday 12, 1890

A bleak day — leaden over head — and yet, not cold. Up late — after 9 before we had our help. The day was spent in podging about doing a few of the many that make me wild when I think that they must all be done before we leave this for N.Y. Dinner at about 2. Late in the afternoon Parke came & I took a walk down to the lower end of town & back. Tea at 6.30. Evelf. wrote to Koehler — Colman & Schilling. To bed before 11. Brilliant, quiet, cloudless night.
