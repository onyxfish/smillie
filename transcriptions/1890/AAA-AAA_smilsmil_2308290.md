## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 20, 1890

Heavy clouds in the mtf., later in the day the weather cleared charmingly. Much of the mtf was spent in the garden. It was after 11 before I got to work in studio — my hollyhocks had freshened up so much that I was able to work with much comfort on my 5"×7" dry point. Con. Parke came with two young men, cousins of his, & Mrs. Dr. Parke. about noon. Dinner — afternoon more work on dry-point — At 4 o'clk, out riding — home at 6.15. Evef. wrote a letter to Aley Schilling. Then Uncle W. & I walked down to the P.O. with it, near 11 when I got to bed.
Gretta went home on a visit

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 21, 1890

Heavy, cloudy mtf., rain before noon — Very heavy showers with thunder & lightning in the afternoon clearing about sunset. In the garden as usual, until after 10 — after 11 when I got to work on my dry-point "Dark Hollyhocks" — Finished the drawing in & before dinner took a proof. After dinner I worked a little with every paper on the dry point & then took more pts. in all, five, 3 India & 2 Japan — took an india pt. of aquatint & got through with my work, pretty well tired out at 5.30. Lozier came in & saw me about the sale of his place, giving me a paper with list of indebtedness against it. Tea at 6.30. Eve'g Uncle W. & I went down to P.O. on return home. Read newspaper — walked piazza & to bed about 10.30.
