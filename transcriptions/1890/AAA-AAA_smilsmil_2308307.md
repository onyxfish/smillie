## Left Page

### September, Thursday 25, 1890

Ther. fell to 33°. last night.

Brilliant day — wind shifting to S.E. with indications of a change. Ice formed in the garden & evidences of frost were to be seen in many places. Aunytink became interested in the work of taking out the roots of the old Balm of Gilead Tree at entrance of lane & "bossed the job" all day, bringing his big stumps extractor & directing the work of 3 or 4 men. By 5 o'clk, the stumps was pulled out. With Coyle I set out the remaining Weigelas all but two — At noon I laid rebiting ground on Soft-ground etching and varnished the plate. At 3.30 we went out Riding & got back at 5.30 —
Every evening Annie Read aloud, Stanley finishing the 2d vol. at 10.30 — & then we went to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 26, 1890

A day of fog & warm rain — immediately after bkfst. I went to etching room & prepared soft-ground etching for biting or rather, for rebiting — then went to Garden — again at my plate and rebit portions of it — Finding that the whole foreground wanted more color, after two or three attempts I succeeded in laying a rebiting ground over the whole bit and then rebit it smartly. After that I burnished over the sky & at 5 o'clk. after a long day of close work, I gave it up. At 5.30 I went to the V.D. taking Jim & Ralph & walking down the hill with Prof. James Principal of the Academy. Then we took a nice "constitutional" & got home at about 6.30 — Eveft wrote to W. S. Little.
