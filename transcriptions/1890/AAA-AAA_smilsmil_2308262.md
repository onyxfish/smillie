## Left Page

### June, Saturday 21. 1890.

A cold, easterly wind & heavy rain all day — Bkfst. after 8 —
Henrick came to work — Coyle came
up before 10 o'clk — & Oscar found
something to do until noon — I
had stove [illegible] put up in Etching room
& a fire started — with much
deliberation I worked at putting
some of my things away — Dinner
at 1 o'clk. & after that, by way of a
change, I took a nap. After 4
o'clk. I went down town — mailed
my safe-deposit keys to Charl — The
youngsters had a hard day of it
in-doors but behaved very well.
Evg: reading news papers &
new Harper's Mag. (for July)

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 22. 1890.

Dull & overcast — Bkfst. about 9 —
almost all day out of doors, weeding
garden beds, gathering our firstness
of green peas or cutting nests of in-
sects off from Elm Trees & burning them
we had dinner between 1 & 2
— in the afternoon I put the final
touches of order upon the Etching
room — . Tea before 7 — after that
out until dusk. Evg: wrote
to Coleman, Platt & Schilling upon
Etching Club matters & to Bridgeman
the seedsman for information about
the blanching of Cos lettuce —
