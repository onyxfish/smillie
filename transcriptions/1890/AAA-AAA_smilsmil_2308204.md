## Left Page

### February, Tuesday 25, 1890

*Walter Cook married to Louise Oakey*

Another nasty, dark, foggy, wet
day — Soon after bkfs. the Wrights
am — & there was much confusion —
Ed came with a Notary to have A.
& others sign a lease of Williamsburgh
dock property to the B&O R.R. co —
Julie & Dr. Adams came before 11 —
Before 12 carriage came and by
always our feminine party, all fully
dressed, were transported to Ned's in
37 & 5. Gretta, Jim & Ralph were also
taken — with but two or three exceptions
only the families were present — a
Mr. Nottingham, an old friend of the
S[prague]s performed the ceremony
at 2.30 — after that a very substan-
tial "lunch" was served — at
3 o'clk the bride & groom left for the
south — to be gone about six weeks —
Annie & the others went to Walter's
house to see the wedding presents —
I went home & had a chance
to get a good rest before dinner
Edith, Sue, Katie & Mollie stayed
with us. Every Annie went
to her Club Rehearsal — I finished
my letter to Kate, giving her an
account of the wedding — To bed
at 11.

## Right Page

### February, Wednesday 26, 1890

Exceedingly warm day — & dry.
Got up with a headache that
made me useless all day — To
studio about 10.30 — Found steam
full on — windows all shut and Mer.
at 84°. I opened all the windows
& then went to the bank & from there
to Richard's to see Winslow Homer's
Fishing Water Colors — & there met
E. A. Abbey & his fiancée Miss Meade
A. is preparing for a blk. & white exhibi-
tion to follow Homer's. Home to lunch at
1 — Edith & Sue left for Phila. soon
after. I got to studio before 3 — most
of the remainder of the p.m. was spent
with Dr. Mulford. Saw. Coleman —
Calverley & Geo. Home at 6 — dinner.
Eveg. wrote a note to Dimock Searle
— read a little in March Harper & to bed
at.
