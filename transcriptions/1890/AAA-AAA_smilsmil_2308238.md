## Left Page

### May, Sunday 4. 1890.

A raw, dull day — Easterly wind & fog.
Some very heavy rains bet. 10 & 11 A.M.
B[illegible] about 9 — A proposed trip out of
town for the day had to be given up.
I spent a little time with newspapers
& children & then had quite a nap.
Dinner at 2 — John Valentine took
dinner with us — After & Mary(ned)
Arthur Williams & Jno. V. (who left
immediately after dinner) came &
Annies, Jim & I went with them to
Geo. & Eusley's new apartments in 42°
St. From there, Annie Jim & I went to
the foot of 42°. St. West. to the ferry, but no
boat was in & as it was late we returned
stopping at the gas house where we were
allowed to look into the Retort house when
retorts were being drawn & filled — then home
A. & J. by horse car, I walked. A cup of tea
at 6.30 — Eveg. Reading & writing
notes until 10.30.

## Right Page

### May Monday 5. 1890.

Cloud & sunshine — very warm.
Got to studio a little after 10, after
having been to Houlihan's stables to
see a Donkey outfit that he has there.
A young jack that I will not take, &
an old cart & harness that I may take.
Started painting on an old canvas
10 × 14" Riverside Park — "Afternoon in May".
From a little sketch that I took some
days ago when I was up there with
Annie & the boys. Home at 1 — back at
2.30 & painted steadily until 5.30
when I got it finished & I was pretty
nearly used up. I think it is a pretty
good sketch for me. G.W. Shelton came
in a little before 6 — explained some
matters in his Etching Club acct. & took
his book & papers away — Home at
6.30 — dinner — Eveg. — loafed —
wrote a note to Miss Lambsert &
to bed soon after 10.
