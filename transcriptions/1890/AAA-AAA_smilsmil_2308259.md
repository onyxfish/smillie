## Left Page

### June, Sunday 15, 1890

A dull, clouded m'g, with some nizzling rain at times, but clearing
as the day went on. Pleasant, but
very close, muggy afternoon & until
ev'g. B'kf't at 8 — all the m'g out
& about the place — Picked our first
mess of strawberries from the cold frame.
Harnessed the donkey & Foak, Jim
& Ralph about the place. — Dinner
at 1 — out of doors until 4 when
we went out riding — the horses
went very well but the roads are
in a shocking condition — back at
6 — Tea at 6.30 — out again until 8.
Went down to P.O. with letters &
on my way back stopped in at the
old [illegible] Ch., to hear the
Salvation Army Service, or a portion
of it, conducted by three men,
very illiterate & noisy — It was
without interest to me & I soon
went on home — To bed about
10.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 16, 1890

A dull, overcast day. Threatening
rain which did not come.
I worked about the place, planting
flower seeds & doing like work. —
Before noon I wrote part of a
letter to Kate — dinner at 1 —
about 4 Annie, Ralph & I
went out for a ride — Ralph
slept soundly most of the
way — back at 6 — Tea —
Even'g I finished letter to
Kate & at 10 o'clk took it
down to P.O. Then back &
to bed.
