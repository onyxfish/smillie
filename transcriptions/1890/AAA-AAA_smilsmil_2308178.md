## Left Page

### January, Saturday 4, 1890.

Beautiful day - luminous & hazy -
After bkf. attended to my domestic
shopping - then to studio, sketching &c.
& rode up to W. Bishop's, getting there
a little after 10. He was profuse in his
apologies for yesterdays disagreeable
contretemps - after which we got to busi-
ness. He kept me until 1st showing me
his collection of fades & giving me a course
of instruction concerning the same. I have
agreed to attempt one plate not fear that
I will not be able to satisfy his require-
ments. Walked down home getting to
lunch at 1:45. It was near 3 before I got to
Studio. For some time engaged with [illegible]
two ladies Mrs. Thompson & Mrs. Goodsmith
came in - nearly 4 o'clk. where they left
very little daylight left - then home.
At 6.30 Annie & I went to Geo. Smillie's
to dinner - to meet two sisters of Mr. Jacobs.
Mr. Jenks & Miss Sheldon. A very nice
little dinner was served over which we
lingered until near 9 o'clk - At 10 we
left & home & to bed by 11 - Sometime
during the day a small blood vessel in
the cornea of my left eye broke — It
caused me some discomfort to say
nothing of the sanguinary appearance.

## Right Page

### January, Sunday 5, 1890.

A nasty, overcast, raw, mild day. Did
not get bkf. until 9.30 - Spent the
m'f. loafing with newspapers & Ralph.
John Valentine came to dinner with
us. He left immediately after.
I nursed my eye with Boracic acid
cocaine & had a very brief nap. Then
walked up Fifth Ave. to 57th St. and
down Madison Ave. stopping to see
Yale, where I stayed until 6 - Then
home to tea - Netta has been off
all day with her brother - Evely
Charl came in for a short time,
full of the Revolution in progress at
the Union League Club & his share in
it - I was able to do a little reading.
To bed about 10.30.
