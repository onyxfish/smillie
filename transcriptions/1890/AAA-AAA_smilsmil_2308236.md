## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 30. 1890.

Bright day with cool east wind.
Was able to get up to bkfst. but that
was all that I was able to accomplish
most of the m'f. I spent in the bed
Annie went to studio for me & got
letters — among them, check from
A.B.N. Co. for $150 — payment for
drawing of Union Trust Co. Bldg —
After lunch I went to 2° Nat. Bnk.
& deposited it to my Cr.. Then to
Theo. Starr's & bought a little lace pin
for A — her birthday to-morrow — on
my way home, in spite of splitting
headache, I went in to see Exb'n
of Soc'y of Am. Artists — Ortgies gallery —
then home & to bed again until
6 o'clk. dinner at 7 — Evely
Geo. & Emily went over to their new
apartments — Annie read aloud
to me a short story from Harper —
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 1. 1890.

Heavy thunder showers afternoon & eve'g.
Exceedingly hot morning — I
got to studio about 10.30 & did not
do much like finishing my 20×24
"A bit of brook with some sheep" —
was also interrupted by calls, among
others, Schilling came with my draft
for Etching Club circular to discuss
some proposed changes. I got some
work done & home to lunch (after
going to bank) soon after 1 — back at
2.30 — finished sheep — more callers
but I got my painting to where Geo.
said it was finished enough — so, I
washed my brushes & home soon
after, or about, 6.30 — dinner our own
family only — Geo. & Emily having left
us & taken possession of their new
apartments. Evely spent some time
in writing circular for Etching Club, then
posted it to Schilling — 10.30 where I
got to bed.

Eliz. Howe lunched with me & the
children. Annie lunched at Mary
([illegible]) Cook's.
