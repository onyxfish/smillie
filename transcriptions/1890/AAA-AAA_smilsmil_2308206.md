## Left Page

### March, Saturday 1, 1890

Possibly, the beginning of the end.

Raw & wet in the m'g — drying before noon — growing colder — some snow &
sleet at night. To studio about 10.30
& painted on "Bit of brook" going
over the foreground & then back
over the whole thing — Home to
lunch about 1.30 — back at 2.30 &
painted until 4.30 — then washed
brushes — cleaned up & had calls —
Chas. Baker came in bringing a
friend — Home at 6.30 — dinner —
Eve'g, at 8.30 went down to Century
to see my 20×30" "Afternoon" — it is too
hot & is flat, wanting atmosphere —
Metcalfe who read to me notes from Dr.
Biggs — giving analyses of two specimens
of my urine — He finds numerous fatty
& "granular" casts — It seemed to me
like the sentence of a doomed man —
but Hale is not yet willing to accept the
two tests as final evidence that organic
disease has begun its work — He says, wait
a month & have another test. I went
up to the meeting & then wandered about
until about 10.40. Chas. Whitehead
the lawyer, says he wants to buy a proof
of The Goldsmith's Daughter — Home at
11 — went to bed much depressed and
spent most of the night winding up
my affairs — when I did sleep — dreamed
that I had been sent to sea, alone, for my
health.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 2, 1890

Very cold, bleak day — R[illegible] about 9 —
got up feeling the want of a night's
rest — Read newspaper for awhile —
About 11.30 Annie & I went to Polier
Club in [illegible]. S. & spent some time look-
ing at a beautiful collection of Wood-
Eng. proofs — Engd. by an Ass'n of Wood-
engravers — Then to N.A. of D. & took
a last look at W. C. Eytler; then to the
Century & saw pictures there. Home to
dinner at 2.05 — After dinner dozed
in my chair in front of dining-room
fire — At 5 went up to Hale's — saw
him — asked advice as to general
treatment — he refuses to take as
grave a view as I do until there is
further evidence — Snow flying in
real winter fashion — Ther. 22°. I
walked down to studio & closed the
window — Home at 6.30 — tea — Evening
read until near 10 — then to bed.
