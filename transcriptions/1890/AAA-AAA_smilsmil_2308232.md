## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 22, 1890.

A pleasant day — got to studio about
10 — prepared to do a little printing
& then went to Brooks Bros. & bought
a heavy ulster for fall driving at Mount —
a spring overcoat & a spring suit —
then back to studio after 11 & took two
p.p. from "Lady of Cairo visiting" — an electro
from original, from which 10,000 imps have
been taken, no result — Home to lunch
after 1 — after that, a rest — dressed
about 4 & went with Annie at
4.30 to the Abbey wedding reception
at the home of the bride's father No. 3 or
5 W. 56th St. Mr. Mead — A great crush —
after half an hour we left. A — went di-
rectly home, I stopped & saw Yale for
a few minutes — Home — dinner about
6.15 — Suey — All hands — Geo. Smil-
lie & Dr. Adams (who came to town
today) Annie & I went to her Choral
Club Concert — very warm — the
concert a very pleasant success —
Home at about 11 & to bed at 11.30 —
Miss Poshay dined with us & went
with us to the concert.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 23, 1890.

Still pleasant weather, but the
wind blows from the east & there
are signs of a change. To studio at
about 10 — Spent the day printing —
took four p.p. from "The Lady of Cairo
Visiting" which were very good, all
things considered — I wish that I could
get the original plate in its virgin state
— I could then get such proofs as I never
could get before. I also took 4 or 5
p.p. from the 6×6. mezzotint plate
"Single Hollyhocks" 8up — last summer —
Home to lunch at 1.30 & back about
2.30 & worked until 5, then cleaned
up & home at 6. Early dinner, because
Mary McGibney, a waitress with us
for three years, was married at 7 o'c. —
Jim went to the ceremony with Gretta —
Eve'g a quietly at home. Yale
came in for a little while & we
consulted about Etching Club circular
that later in the eve'g I sent to Nicoll —
Chase came in bet. 9 & 10 — At last
we managed to get to bed at 10.30.
