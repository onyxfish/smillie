## Left Page

### February, Saturday 1. 1890.

Cold, raw & overcast — It was 10.30 when I got to studio — spent some time clearing up & then, until near 1 o'clk. signing proofs of dry-point "Poppies" No. 1 belonging to Keppel — Fowler, the advertising agent, came in to insist upon a change in arrangement of Water-Color cat, so that two ads. shall face the title page as p. contract, arranged to have a new title page printed — a second one, to face the ads. leaving the other parts of the cat. as they are. To lunch & back at 2.30 — Spent some time arranging & signing the remaining pt. of dry-point "Hollyhocks" for Keppel. Then rested for an hour — home to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. Took Annie to her b't. Edr's — then walked on to Century — Monthly meeting — & had a very pleasant time until 11.30 — then walked up home with Geo. & Charl — To bed soon after 12.

## Right Page

### February, Sunday 2. 1890.

Misty day — dark & wet. Pt'clp. about 9 — Read paper — before 12 lay down upon my bed & slept until near 2 — then dinner — after that with children & reading until tea after 6 — Eve'g. Reading "Century" for [illegible]. after Charlie came in & for some time studied the maps of Europe with Annie trying to lay out a plan of travel for next summer. To bed at 11.
