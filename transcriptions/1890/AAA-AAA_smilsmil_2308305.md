## Left Page

### September, Friday 19, 1890

A bright, warm, hazy day with wind from the S.E. & at every cloudiness.
In garden with Coyle. The asparagus bed finished & the remaining W.S. Little
asparagus plants set out — Hill & Judson worked all day digging manure pit
for new stable. I got to work before 11. Laid soft ground on new plate &
got about half through with the drawing. Miss McCartney came with samples of
silk & plush for portfolio covers for [illegible] & we made selection — After dinner
with the men until 3. Then are went riding & got home at 6 —
Evelf, Annie & I made our party call on Jude & Mrs. McCallum —
home by 9 o'clk. — To bed before 11 — It rained as we went to bed

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 20, 1890

Bleak, cloudy day, growing cooler —
wind from N.W. After [illegible] — went into
studio & right to work upon 5×7 soft
ground drawing & completed it. Varnished
plate & got things ready in etching room
for biting — It was three near 11 — went to
garden, got vegetables & looked after the
men. Hill, Judson, Fish, Coyle & Herrick
at work out of doors. Back to studio at
noon — Mr. Gast called — Got my etching
"a bit of brook, etc" into the acid — Then
at about 1, went with Herrick & Coyle to
the lane & drove stakes on one side for the
expected Weigelas. Coyle & Oscar at work
digging the holes in the afternoon. Dinner
— after that worked in etching room biting
soft ground etching until after 5. but did
not complete it — then [illegible] & took
a short walk for half an hour or more —
Tea — after reading newspapers Annie
read aloud from Stanley until after
10 — To bed before 11.
