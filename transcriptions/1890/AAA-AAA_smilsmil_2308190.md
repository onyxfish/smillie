## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 28, 1890

A beautiful specimen of a clear, bright, breezy & cold winter day —
BHp — at about 8 — I left at 9 & by elevated R.R. went to Rankin
the printers, in Cortlandt St — Got
up to Kurtz's by 10.30 — Some time
there superintending the getting
out of process blocks — then took two
of them & went to studio — after
working awhile, home to lunch
soon after 1 — back to studio at
2.30 & worked upon blocks until
near 4 — then again to Kurtz's
there until 5.30 when, with nine
blocks I went down to Rankin's —
Home at 6.45, dinner. Just before
& the Bosé girls came & A. went with
them to her club rehearsal — I dozed
awhile in front of the fire — then
finished letter to Kate — mailed it
& got to bed at 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 29, 1890

Another fine day, overcast & bright
rain before m'ls. To studio — then
to N.a & then to Kurtz's where I wait-
ed until near 12 for the finishing of
the last two of the catalogue blocks —
Home to lunch at 1 — after 2, Annie &
I went to Ortgies' gallery & saw the
Whittredge paintings — then I went to
Rankin's in Cortlandt St — found that
they would not be ready for me before
7 or 8 o'clk. Went to A.B.U.Co. saw Mr.
Dor & got some mezzotint rockers —
then to 42d St — to barber's & had haircut,
then home & got a little rest —
Dinner at 6.30 — then to Rankin's — got
there before 8 — worked with the press-
men until near 10 — Found that I
could accomplish nothing in the way
of improving the appearance of the
Kurtz blocks — This is the last dis-
appointment & makes a flat failure
of my attempt to get up an artistic
catalogue — Home & to bed in a
very gloomy frame of mind at 11 oclk.
