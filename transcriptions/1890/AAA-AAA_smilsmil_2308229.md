## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 16, 1890

Charming day — bright & pleasantly
cool — Geo. to studio at 10, wrote
notes to Parke, Koehler & Benizan —
then to work on Union Trust Co. Bldg
until 1.30 — then home to lunch.
Back at 3 & in spite of a wretched
headach I worked until near 6.
Then in my big chair & fell asleep
& did not wake until 6.35 —
then home — dinner at 7 — but
Annie, Geo. & Finley went to a
concert at Chickering Hall & I
went to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 17, 1890

Another charming day — got up in
the mng. with remnants of yesterday's headache which gradually
passed off — It was after 10 when I
got to the studio & the rest of the
mng was passed writing letters and
conferring with Geo. concerning the
purchase by him of the Whiting Co.
in Ridgefield — Home to lunch at —
neither Annie or Finley there — back
at 2.30 — worked on Union Trust Co.
bldg drawing until 5 — then walked
down to Leavitt's auction rooms to
see the Avery collection of prints to
be sold — too late — Home at 6.15,
dinner late, as Geo. did not come in.
Evg. Annie & I read aloud to
each other some short sketches by
Maupassant.
