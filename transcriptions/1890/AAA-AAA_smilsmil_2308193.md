## Left Page

### February, Monday 3, 1890

overcast day with some struggling sunlight in the afternoon — Fine moonlight at night. Got to studio about 9.30 — Intended to start work upon some sloppy water colors but on going to drawer for paper, the piece niters proves to be a bit of Pastel pap— I went into Champney's & borrowed a lot of his old Pastels & made a small drawing before lunch time — not being satisfied with my experiment; on my way home to lunch I ordered a stretcher 18×30 covered with Pastel canvas — On my return at 2.30 I pitched in & by 5 o'clk. had nearly finished my sketch — on the whole, my first day at Pastel is encouraging — At 5.30 went to [illegible] with some signed [illegible] K — in a very complimentary mood — but did it offer me a liberal price for any new work. Home at 6.30 — Eve'g Read until 9 — then wrote part of letter to Mary — To bed very soon after 10.

## Right Page

### February, Tuesday 4, 1890

Nasty wet day — To studio before 10 — Mrs. Fox then came & spent the rest of the day cleaning up in the studio — I did a little work upon the Pastel sketch that I made yesterday before I took hold of the house cleaning — Uncle Willie came in for a while in the m'f, on his way down town to see Charl. Home to lunch at 1.20 — back to studio at 2.30 — Worked with Mrs. Fox until near 5 o'clk. Then home. Annie had gone to the Kimballs to take tea & from there, with Fanny, go to her choral club rehearsal — I dined with the children, Catherine & Edna Cook being with us to spend the night. I read my papers until 8.30 — then finished letter to Mary. At 9.30 walked down to 74th St., got Annie — home — to bed soon after 11.
