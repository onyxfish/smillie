## Left Page

### December, Monday 22, 1890

Beautiful day — bright, breezy & not too cold. To studio at 9.45. Got two sets of proofs of my last summer plates ready & at 11 o'clk. took them to Keppel's & there saw Mr. Jordan and left them with him — I presented him with a proof of my "Voices from the Cliff". Back to studio at 12.30 after calling at Husty's for 2° photo from Junius Morgan portrait — Home to lunch at 1.30 — to studio at 2.30 worked awhile on Etching plate "A bit of brook" & for the rest of the time arranged p.p. etc — Home at 6.6 — Eve'g. reading Harpers for [illegible]. To bed early — about 10.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 23, 1890

Mild, pleasant day. Got to studio at about 10 o'clk. & spent some time getting aqua-tint & soft ground p.p. for Mary Willard into frames — then started upon printing. Saml. Colman came in before noon — I took p.p. from some of his plates — Home abt. 2o — back at 2.30 — resumed printing & took several p.p. from 5"×7" Etching. Soon after 5 went to Husty's & got two large photos from the Junius S. Morgan portrait — again to studio. Got Mary Willard framed p.p. & took them to Butler's — Home soon after dinner — Eve'g. Annie went to her Choral Club rehearsal — I sat with news papers until 9 — then Charlie came in.
