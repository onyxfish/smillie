## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 17, 1890

A nasty, raw & windy day — the wind having got around to the N.W.
no sun shone during the day & at times, in the afternoon there was
a driving mizzle of rain. I got into my studio about 10.30 & worked
for awhile on my 5"×7" etching, putting in some lines on new ground.
The afternoon it was so dark that I gave most of the time to
cleaning up about studio & Etching Room & getting ready for my soft-
ground Etching to-morrow. I spent some time out of doors —
Peter & Hill have worked all day on an asparagus trench at the
foot of the garden — About 6, Jim & I went to P.O. Got ready
newspaper. Annie read about from Stanley & we got to bed a little
before 11.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 18, 1890

At last, a beautiful day — brilliant & warm.
In the garden for a time with Corle &
Hill who were at work upon new asparagus
bed. I got to studio about 10.30 & commenced
my 5×7 drawing upon soft-ground for
Kathler. Finished it after dinner and
found that the pencil had not been
used with sufficient vigor — consequently
the ground had not adhered to the paper
as much as I had wished — This drawing,
however, will answer for the unbitten
example. About 3.30 we went out
riding and got back at 6 — Evening
I prepared a new piece of paper for
another 5×7 soft-ground etching. Annie
read aloud from Stanley — I packed
a lot of Butter Beans in salt while I
listened — It was 11 o'clk. when we
got to bed.

Judson worked about 5 hours. Hill
put in a full day.
