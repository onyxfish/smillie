## Left Page

### July, Thursday 3, 1890

*[Written at top: "Tuesday 1st ✱"]*

Up at about 5.30 — help at 6. Ed W.
& Walter left us in the stage at 6.15
to return to N.Y. by D. L&W. R.R. —
Late in the afternoon we rec'd a tele-
gram from Walter saying that they had
reached home safely. The mf.
was overcast with many indications
of rain — but it did not come. The
day grew to be pitilessly hot & sunny.
before 6 P.M. it again clouded over
with strong S.E. wind. I worked out
of doors until 10 — then down town —
back at 12 — more work with the
vines & at 2.30 lay down for a few
minutes after dinner — after that I
worked for two hours on my Pansy
supplemental aqua-tint — then
out riding until 6 — after tea,
having concluded in my mind
that we were in for a drouth, in
spite of weather threatenings, I spent
an hour or more watering flowers —
then attended to the horses, and
set down paper for proving to-morrow
& at last, after 9 o'clk., sat down to rest
being very tired — to bed at 10 —

✱ By a mistake, I turned over
two leaves & have written upon
Thursday's page for Tuesday.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 4, 1890

*[Written at top: "Geo. Emily & Valentine arr."]*

Thick & muggy mf. that gradually
cleared into a very hot day — I spent
all of the mf. out of doors — At 10.30
Annie, Jim, Ralph & I went down
town to see the Races, some of which
were quite amusing. I engaged
a team of Perez, the livery man, to go to
Kees-Milford for Geo. & party — but Rice,
the man engaged by Herrick managed
to slip past the house at 1 o'clk, with
Sam & Celine without our hearing him.
I went down to Perez's & paid him 75¢
for not using his team. Rice Ret'd with
our team at eight, at about 5 o'clk.
with Geo. Emily & Valentine — we
had a little display of fireworks
for Jim at 8 o'clk. A pin wheel ex-
ploded & burned my left hand
very painfully — At 8.30, Geo., Emily, Val.
& I went to the green & saw the fire-
works — back at 9.30 — & to bed
at about 10.30 — my hand giving
me much pain.
