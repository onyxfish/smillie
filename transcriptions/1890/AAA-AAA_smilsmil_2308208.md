## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 5. 1890.

Bright & beautiful. Went to
studio before 10 & to work on 20×30
"Afternoon" — Painted until 1 o'clk
then home. Annie lunched at
the Clinches in their new flats up
town. To studio at 2.30 — Painted
for a little while on 20×30." Bit of
Brook" — about 4 o'clk. Got out
p.f. of Goldsmith's Daughter & after
selecting the best put it in a mount
— wrote a note to Mr. Chas. E. Whitehead
who had asked me to send a
proof to him, & at 6 o'clk.. I left the
note & proof at his house. 64 W. 35th St.
Then home — dinner — Eve'g. Annie
went with the Butters to the Opera
Lohengrin — I sat for a time in
front of the fire & read — then,
being sleepy — soon after 9 I
went to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 6. 1890.

An old fashioned winter snow storm
which continued, almost blizzard like
until afternoon when it cleared — a
brilliantly cold moonlight night — To
studio before 10 — found there a note
from Mr. Whitehead enclosing check
for $30.00 & returning p.f. for inscription.
I wrote several notes & letters. Home
to lunch at 1 — back to studio at 2.45
again wrote — fixed for frame Etching
proof of Cedars for Louise Oakey Cook.
Took proof of Goldsmith's Daughter
& left it with a note at Mr. White-
head. Home soon after 6 — found
Sue Cook just arr. from Phil. Dinner
at 6.30 — Eve'g. — Reading — at 9
went to G.C. Depot with a letter
posted it for Sue — To bed at 10.
