## Left Page

### January, Thursday 2, 1890.

A very mild, muggy, overcast day.
a little sunshine in the a.m. — with
black, thunderous looking rain clouds
after. I got to studio at about 10.30 —
As I left my room closed with steam on
yesterday a.m., I found the Ther. at 84°.
when I got there — Spent the morning
dodging draughts. I fixed up proof
of mezzotint plate, "Double Hollyhocks," with
a self-working p.p., wrote a letter to
Koehler. & took p.p. to Epp. Co. at noon.
Home to lunch at 1. After that for
some time with Annie looking over
acc.ts — then lay down & had a good
nap. After 4 o'clk when I got to
studio — then down to Century Club — got
home to dinner at 6. — Evely
Took Annie to her Club Rehearsal
& then to Century — Wrote to Herrick
concerning purchase of Losier place — then
read Blake's "Marriage of Heaven & Hell"
until 10 — then for Annie. — Home
& to bed.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 3, 1890.

Bright, fresh & breezy day — to studio
at 10 — Got together some etching p.p.
& then went up to W. Hebert Bishop's, 881
Fifth Ave. near 70th St. by appointment —
Saw him for a moment, he excused himself
for ten minutes to finish some Art Museum
Com. business with W. Marquand — After
I had waited for nearly an hour he and
Marquand went off together evidently leaving
me forgotten — I then left, pretty mad about it —
Walked down home, stopping at Sherwood
Studio Bldg. to see Carroll Beckwith — not in —
she on down — went to Architectural League
[illegible] — Ortgies gallery — Artists not admitted
free — then I went in to Young Avery's gallery.
Home to lunch at 1 o'clk — Annie not there
as she had to preside at the meeting of the
woman's Whitman League, where Rosalie Butler
read a paper at All Souls Church. Katie Adams
announced that her party had determined
to return to New Haven by the 3 o'clk train.
To studio at 2.30 — found it furiously hot
& spent some time in getting it cooled
off. I was able to do a very little in the
work of advancing my new drawing for
large dry-point plate of "Poppies" —
also finished up, signed & numbered
the last of the twenty proofs Dry-point —
Poppies. 6×6. & took them down to
Kepple's. Home at 6. Evening
Jno. Valentine came in for a little
while. I wrote my regular letter
to Herrick — posted it at 10.30 — then
to bed —
