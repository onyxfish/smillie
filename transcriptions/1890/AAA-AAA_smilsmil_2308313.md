## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 7, 1890

Another miserable day — much wet, especially in the afternoon
& early even'g. I had a long day at my printing & was able to get
a number of satisfactory proofs from "A bunch of Pansies" & "Single
Dark Hollyhocks". Jim got his bridle & saddle & was able to get
some donkey back rides between the showers. I washed up at
5 o'clk. & had half an hours rest before I went down to P.O. Tea
at 6.30. After reading newspaper I picked & cleaned India paper
for to-morrow's printing. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 8, 1890

Brilliant, cloudless day — brisk but not cold breeze from N.W. It
was nearly 11 before I got to my work printing. Everything went
beautifully — ink, paper, rags, everything in good condition and I took
satisfactory proofs from aqua-tint, soft-ground & dry-point plates.
I worked until a little after 4 o'clk.
Then dinner — after dinner, cleaned up & at 3 went out riding — some
brilliant color & atmospheric effects.
Back at 5. Herrick & [illegible] began work to-day on framing for
new barn. Tea at 6.30.
Even'g, after newspaper, picking over
India again. Mr. & Mrs. W. Parke
came in before 9 o'clk. & spent an
hour with us. To bed just before
11.
