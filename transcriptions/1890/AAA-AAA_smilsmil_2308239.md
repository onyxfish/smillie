## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 6. 1890.

Rain commenced about 10 & continu-
ed steadily until about 3 — I went
to Stivers', carriage builder in 31st St. &
found that I can buy a new donkey
cart for $60 — then to Houlihan's &
found there a Jenny — said to be up-
ld, sound & gentle, for $40 — I bought
her — then to studio — took things
very easily during the day as I felt
very tired — Spent some time talk-
ing with Geo. about his Ridgefield
visit yesterday — I took two proofs
from "A Lady of Cairo" before I went
home to lunch — & three in the
afternoon — all of them very good.
Had a half hour rest before I
went home to dinner — Ettie
Annie went to Business Meeting of the
Choral Club at Mr. Mitchell's in [illegible] St.
I wrote to Herrick about donkey & to
Parke — Charle came in & then
Geo. & Emily — It was after 10 before I
could commence my letter to Kate.
but I got it into Post box by 10.40 &
to bed by 11 — Annie came in about
midnight.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 7. 1890.

The ninth anniversary of our wedding
day — I got up with a headache. On
my way to studio stopped at Stivers'
& again looked at Donkey carts —
then on to Houlihan's — got Lehigh
Valley R.R. shipper's receipt for Donkey
& paid him $41 — then to studio and
until after 11 at work on painting for
Charl — scraping the surface — & then
glazing heavily with pure, strong
colors over the whole thing — At noon
went to Ringler's, cor. Park Place & Church
St. & left plate "A Lady of Cairo Visiting" to be
steel faced & sent to Estes & Lauriat. Then
home at 1st very much used up by
my headache. Annie lunched at
Manip & two or three of her children lunch-
ed with us — After attending to them I went
to bed & gave up to a very severe head
ache — was not able to get up again.
