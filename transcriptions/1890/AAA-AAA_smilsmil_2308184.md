## Left Page

### January, Thursday 16, 1890

Dark & wet in the early m'f. cleared before noon — cloudy —
windy & growing colder in the
afternoon. Got to studio about
9.30 — from there to U.a. f.D. at
10 — Platt came & we worked
upon Cat. work until 1 — then
home to lunch. At 2.15 went
to Kurtz's to learn what progress
had been made — then to U.a.
& then to studio where I worked
at getting sketches ready to
send to Photo-engravers until 5
o'clk. when I could see no longer.
Then I rested for half an hour.
Home at 6. Dinner at 6.30. Cath-
erine & Edna Cook dined & spent
the night with us. Annie & Miss
Foshay also went to a concert in
Chickering Hall. I took them to 5th
ave & put them in a bus — then home
in, front of a pleasant fire read
until near 10. Then to bed —

## Right Page

### January, Friday 17, 1890

Fine, clear winter day — to studio
before 10 — then to U.a. there for
some time, then to Kurtz's — then
to studio & worked until 1 o'clk.
arranging sketches already in &
fitting them into pages — then home
to lunch — back at 2.30 & resumed
work. Mr. Heber Bishop came in
about 3 o'clk. & took some time
discoursing about jade — I am to
go on with a plate. I worked as
long as I could see — after 5 went to
Moss Eng. Co. with a large batch of
keen drawings — Nearly 6.30 when I
got home. Catherine Cook with us
to dinner & overnight — Miss Foshay
went to Phila this m'f. At 8.20 Annie
& I went to Walter's & congratulated
him upon his engagement to Miss
Oakey (Louise Sprague). Then we went
to Edw. Cook's & A. read Rosalie Butler's
paper for Woman's Unitarian League to
Mary, Mr. Sprague & Edw. — home at 10 —
I wrote short note to Herrick — posted
it & to bed at about 11.
