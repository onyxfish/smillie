## Left Page

### July, Sunday 27, 1890

A very pleasant day — and a wonderfully beautiful night.
Sec the m[illegible] out of doors, looking after vegetables & flowers.
Bert Lathrop made a professional call on Selim this m[illegible]
& pronounces his case a hopeless one of heaves — He say we may
mitigate his cough by careful feeding & that he may be serviceable
for some time yet. Dinner at about 2. After that I wrote
a letter to Walter Coat in answer to his questions about Sauce Creek
matters. Then we rode from 4 to 6:15 tea. Suet on piazza until
nearly 9 — then wrote notes to Ge: to Boyle & to the Rpt. S. Joult
Co. Went to P.O. with them at about 10 o'clk. Then home
& to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 28, 1890

A pleasant day but with many indications of change in weather.
As usual I was in garden & about, out of doors, until near noon, then
worked with graver on "Pansies" until dinner soon after 1 o'clk.
Again in the afternoon I worked for about an hour & a half — then,
after 4 o'clk. Mary, Miss W. Annie Ralph & I rode until after 6.
Tea — every Jo. Williams called
I wrote a short letter to Kate & he took it to P.O. for me — To bed at
11 — About 2 o'clk. Ralph roused us — too many Raspberries trouble
in the bowels — He was up two or three times but settled for the
rest of the night before 4 A.M.
