## Left Page

### August, Thursday 14, 1890

Mild day — overcast most of the time with threats of rain. Clear, beautiful night. Went down to the garden before bkfs. & got through with part of my work there — after bkfs. I spent the rest of the m'g with flowers, vines etc. and after dinner but a short time was given to burnishing on aqua-tint. Hearing that Lozier's deed to Dimock Searce had not yet been put upon Record I went down town & saw Miller S. Allen in the Court Room & by him was told that as Lozier had executed a deed to his sister subsequent to the deed given to Searce an execution had been got out against him & the Lozier place would be advertised for sale by the Sheriff. Later, Lozier called on Herrick who, by my instructions told him to make him a deed for $1325.— We rode from 5 to 6 o'clk. Taking Sallie Countright with us. Ev'g. Read news paper to to bed at a little before 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 15, 1890

*Uncle Willie & Aunt Agnes arr—*

A lovely day — again, much of the m'g was spent out of doors in garden. Parke came to me there — & so did Lozier, with whom I made agreement to purchase his place adjoining us for $1325.⁰⁰ I rec'd a note from D. D. Searce promising the place to me — so it seems as though I might get it. I worked on aqua-tint with burnisher & then took a proof — dinner at 1 — a little more studio work & then, at 4, Annie, Ralph & I drove over to S°. Montrose to intercept Uncle W. & Aunt A. — & bring them home in the carriage — we waited 1 hour & 10 m. for the train — then it passed without stopping — Well — I was mad. I drove home in 15 m. & got there almost as soon as Uncle W. — then down town for the paper — Tea at 7 — Ev'g we chatted until 10.
