## Left Page

### December, Sunday 14, 1890

A charming day, bright & mild. B[illegible]
at 9 — there, for a time with news pa[per] —
there revised some sheets of my
"Observations" on Etching. Before 12
went to Union League Club & with
Charl, looked at the pictures, Mr. Ch[u]
Smith's 12 old masters exciting my sur-
prise. Then walked up to [illegible]'s — not
in — then to Central Park — back to
[illegible]'s at 1.30 — consulted him about
Ralph's throat — Home to dinner at 2.
After dinner Charl came & stayed
until dark. Tea at 6.30 — [illegible]
Reading — then again, for awhile
Tried revision of M.S. Ran up on
a snag of double metaphors and
I went to bed, disgusted, before 10.

## Right Page

### December, Monday 15, 1890

Fine day, altho' a portion of it was over-
cast, windy & raw. Got to studio at 9.45
after stopping at Walter Cook's & getting from
him a note of introduction to Mr. Mc. A. De-
[illegible] — I bit acid on 5×7" etching, but
the ground acting badly I cleaned all off —
Then I gave my Etching club paper a general
revision — then took him to Champney and
inflicted him upon him — he bore it very good
humoredly & even seemed to be pleased —
Mrs. Willard & her br. called & brought two
Etching proofs for me. Home to lunch at
1 — back at 2.30 & then went down to the
[illegible] establishment with Etching club
material & saw Mr. Det — & had a pleasant
chat — then to Keppel's with some Pratt Mr.
[illegible] & saw Mr. Jordan — then to Putnam's
& saw Ge. Haven — then to N.A.f.D. & for
half an hour looked at pictures there.
Home after 6 — dinner — Eve[ning] spent
lounging — Charl with us until after
10 — To bed before 11.
