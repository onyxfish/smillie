## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 26, 1890

Pleasant with, very warm sunshine, but every indication that
Rain was near — clouded P. M —
Rain commenced about 7 o'clk.
Gentle & steady Rain during the eve.
After my usual out of door work some
time was spent in changing boards
& blotters for proofs in standing press.
Then wrote note to R.S. Jould Co. about
mistake made in the goods they sent
to me yesterday. Dinner at 1 —
Then some time about the place directing
work. Then commenced tracing for
5"×7" etching "A bird brook with some
sheep." At 4.30 we went out riding.
Home before 6. Jim did not get home
with his donkey until about 7 —.
Eve'g. Reading news paper until
9 — Then Annie read aloud from
Stanley until 10 — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 27, 1890

Drizzling rain in the early m'g
then strong westerly & N.W. winds
& clearing before noon — delightful
afternoon & eve'g. In spite of the wet
around Coyle, Oscar & Chas. Hill worked
at the new strawberry bed getting it
into shape for the plants. After I got
to my work in studio I made two
transfers, from tracings, upon etching
grounds 5"×7" of "A bit of brook with
some sheep" — one to be placed in the
collection at Pratt Jr." as a transfer
the other to have the usual "etching"
lines drawn — to be in same collection,
unbitten; a third to be bitten. Dinner
at 1 — at 3.15 we started on a drive
going over to South Pond & Brooklyn.
The afternoon was delightful & the
ride was much enjoyed by all —
Back at 6.25. Herrick took the horses
down to his barn, as the Veterans
are to have their annual c[illegible] up on
the fair grounds with much firing of
guns & cannon to the great discomfort
of the horses. Eve'g. I had duties
& reading of news paper that kept
me until 9 o'clk. Then Annie
read aloud for an hour from Stanley's
new book, but I was so sleepy that
I heard none of it. To bed soon
after 10 o'clk.
