## Left Page

### June, Thursday 19, 1890

Glorious day - Breezy, bright and
cool - Herrick still sick - until
10 o'clk. attending to garden & vines.
G.W. Parke came up - I rode down
town with him to butcher's & to Barney's
where I got iron bit for Peggy. Then
P. took me back home - harnessed
Peggy & started Annie & 5 boys to
the lake on a fishing exped'n. I
worked until past noon in Etching
Room getting press etc. cleaned.
Then out to look for fishing party —
They came along all right about 12.30
Dinner at 1 — a short nap. Then
worked at getting wire netting up for
vines at gable of Studio piazza until
4.15 - then out for a ride - back at 4.45 —
Tea at 6.30 — Worked at vines until
after sundown - went down town at
8 o'clk - back before 9 - very cool
evening - fire on hearth - Read news-
paper - filled census blanks and
to bed at 10. 45.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 20, 1890

Another delightful day - but with
unmistakable signs of change of weather —
most of the m'f was spent out
of doors weeding & working about
the vines — Herrick came up &
worked a little, complaining of
being still very weak. I wrote a
letter to Charl on the subject of ex-
changing Oregon Trans.' Bonds for Northern
Pacific & cleared out & curried the
drawers in Etching Room - Dinner
about 1 — Soon after 3 o'clk. Annie,
Ralph & I rode down to Dr. Norris's
Annie went in & made a call on Mrs.
N. while I sat in carriage & held Ralph
who was sound asleep — Mrs. N. came
out to the carriage & was very agreeable.
Home at 6.15 — Suet. at 8.15. A.
& I went to an entertainment given
at the Opera House by the Kellogg, Husk-
some fair playing on Piano & violin — some
very poor elocutionary work & some
singular, but not musical "bird war-
bling" m-whistling by Kellogg — Home
also & then to bed.
