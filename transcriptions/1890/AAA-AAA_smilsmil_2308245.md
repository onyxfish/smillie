## Left Page

### May, Sunday 18, 1890

A delightful day — was wakened at 6.30 & had a warm salt water bath —
then back to bed & slept until near 8 — then
up & had breakfast — quite east
wind & some fine cloud forms — Took our
writing materials & sauntered from
place to place & loafed & wrote — two
letters — one to Koehler & one to Parke —
Then had a good nap before dinner
at about 2 — after that Annie & I
wandered out into the woods — over
sand dunes & along the beach until
sunset — a great inundation of excursion
ists — nearly 600 from Norfolk — many babies
& young children — many thought that there
was a 6.30 train but all had to wait
until 8.30 — we had tea after 7 —
then took books & sat down stairs —
Mr. Crittenden, the Babington came to
us, introduced himself & hoped that we
were satisfactorily cared for.
To bed about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 19, 1890

Superb, cloudless day, with strong breeze
from the south — a little saunter on the
beach before bkft. after 8 — The morning
was spent wandering about, trying one
place after another & reading with more
or less steadiness, Amelie Rives' hysterical
"The Quick & the Dead". It has a brash & un-
tutored sort of power & lays bare the en-
ergies that burn, more or less fiercely, in the
hidden depths of all of us. To me this is an
incomplete picture of a very wayward & morbid
character — near 1 o'clk. we came in & lay
down — I had a nap of half an hour — then
dinner — At 3 o'clk. we went out riding
— one horse & a buck board — down the beach
about 3 miles — got a sketch of some old wind
mills — then inland & by could almost roads
back to the hotel at 5.30 — having had a very
pleasant drive — more sauntering — then I
worked upon my sketch & did a little writing.
It was after 7 when we had tea — after[?]
wrote my regular letter to Kate and
got to bed about 10.
