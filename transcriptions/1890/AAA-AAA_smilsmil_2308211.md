## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 11, 1890

Will Kimball's funeral —

A dismal, depressing day — dark, foggy & rainy — At 10 o'clk. I went to the church cor. 10th S. & Fifth Ave. The funeral services were very impressive to me & I was much affected — I sat with Geo. Cook & Chas. Collins — after the service saw & spoke with many friends — At 11 went to Wunderlich's & saw the Whistler etchings — to studio at about noon — wrote letter to Herrick — Home abt 30 — lunch — To studio before 3 — made charcoal sketch upon 18×30 canvas. Then wrote a letter to Koehler — Home soon after 6 — dinner at 6.30 — After that went to Ed'wd Cook's for latest information concerning W. Sprague — Then home & wrote a letter to Annie — Then one to sister Kate — At 10 went out & posted both letters — Home & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 12, 1890

Very mild, summerish sort of day, with wet sidewalks where the sunshine did not fall upon them. The ther. got above 70° in the shade. To studio before 10 — Got my three pictures into their frames & did a little work upon all of them — At 12 went to Chickering Hall & bought tickets for Miss Edward's Egyptian lectures for Friday ev'g. 21st & Sat. P.M. 22d — Then to Lord & Taylor's — then home to lunch at 1.15 — To studio at 2.45 Chas. Parsons came in to explain the cutting, by the Harpers, of the W.C. Cat. ill. blocks — the explanation not a satisfactory one to me. Spent some time in Miss Abbott's — Chambney's & Geo.'s studios — Miss Van Amringe called, late. Home at 6.30 — dinner — wrote letter to Annie then to 154 E. 37th St. for Buffalo Bulletin — added it to my letter — to G.C.D. and posted it then to Century where Prof. Rossiter W. Raymond gave us a lecture on the Grand Canon of Colorado River with lantern slides — lecture, interesting — slides, rather bad — Home at 10.15 — To bed at 11.
