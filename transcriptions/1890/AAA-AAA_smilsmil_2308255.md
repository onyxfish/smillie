## Left Page

### June, Saturday 7. 1890.

A very pleasant, breezy day. After
Bkfst- Annie & I started out to do
some shopping — we went to Denning's
& saw some light curtainings & some
furniture — then to Stern's & saw more
curtains & bought a pair & to Horne's
& saw furniture — then I left A. & went
back to Denning's & bought light cur-
tains for studio's bay window & small table
& two chairs for same place — then to 368
Bdway — Gould's — & bot. poles, rings, etc.
etc — then to 16th St., Mr. Rumuly, Reese &
Golding, Ports & Etcher's portfolio — then to
Wunderlicht's & saw some mezzotint pts-
then to Union Transfer Co — then to studio
& home about 1.30 — lunch — a short rest —
then to studio & worked there until
6.30 getting my big case packed — Charl
came in for awhile — Home at 7
& had dinner — a rest — & then packed
big case at home — got through with
that & to bed at om. after 10 — Gretta
spent the night with her cousins
in Brooklyn & A. took her place
in the nursery.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 8. 1890.

Delightful clear, cool morning.
Bkfst- about 8 o'clk. I then finished
the packing of the big house-box &
started the packing of my trunk.
Geo & Smiley called before 1 o'clk
we had dinner before 2 — Charl
came in at 3 & I still packed
at my trunk — Before 5 Annie
& I went to Mr. Kimball's in 22d
St- & saw her & Carrie & Fanny —
also Rodney's son Walter — & Mr. &
Mrs. Dres. We saw Mrs. K- in her
bed room for a few minutes, all
seeming to be in good health and
spirits — From there we went to
the Ireland's in E. 17th St- & saw
aunt Anna, Emma, Mary & Grace.
— then I went to the studio and
fixed cards upon my paintings
that are for exhibtns & home at
6.30 & had a cup of tea — Charl
came in after that & stayed until
about 9 — I finished packing
a little after 10 — then right
to bed.
