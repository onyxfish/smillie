## Left Page

### March, Saturday 15, 1890

Snow & rain, mingled, nearly all day — about 5 p.m. cleared bright & cool. To studio about 9.30 — and painted in the sheep in yesterday's painting 20×24 — "a bit of brook with sheep" — Dimock Searle came in just before 1 o'clk. To see me about the Losier place in Montrose. Home to lunch — back before 2.30 — In the afternoon, painted for an hour or two on an old painting, "A Cheeming Co. Pasture — freshening the foreground & making a red cow black — Chase came in — Home to dinner at 6 — Rest & spent in arranging my possessions & die [illegible]. Will Lake came in for a few minutes before 6.30 — Chase came in at 9.30 & stayed for an hour. I got to bed at about 11.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 16, 1890

Cold, cloudy & windy — [illegible] at 9 — as Gretta wanted to go to church I was with the children until 11 — soon after that I walked with Jim up to 5th Ave. & 57th St. & then home. Dinner at 2 — Gretta wanted to go to Brooklyn, so I gave her permission and stayed with the children — Lou & Edna Cook came in & played with them until 5 — I took a rest in the mean time — At 5.30, there being no one else in the house, I got Kate to care for the youngsters, in the dining room, while I went to the Union League Club to meet Chase & Geo. There, took up at pictures, until 6.30 — then home, gave them their supper & at 7.30 put them to bed — just as good as two kittens — At 8 I sat down in parlor — wrote to S.C. & Hammond until near 10 — then to G.C. Stratton — met Annie — home — & to bed before 11.
