## Left Page

### August, Thursday 28, 1890

Clouded, windy & rather bleak.
After my garden duties I spent
some time with the men who were
at work on the new strawberry bed —
they have unearthed some very
large rocks — a drill was put into
the largest one & a blast fired at
noon. I got to work on 5×7 etchy
for the Pratt [illegible]. Dinner soon
after 1 — Gretta ret- from her visit
home. I gave some time to the
men, starting them on digging
for the new shed that is to be put
up north of the cow shed — then I
took [illegible] out of press — then to fair
ground for a little while & saw the
"veterans" engage in a sham battle —
then to etching room & cut some mount
for [illegible] — then out with the men — tea at
6.30 — Eve [illegible] awhile with news
paper — then to etching room & until
near 10 o'clk. — mounting [illegible] of aqua-
tint & dry-point to send to Mr. [illegible] —
about 11 when we got to bed — glorious
moonlight night.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 29, 1890

*Uncle D. taut A. left us.*

Delightful mts. about 5 p.m.
western sky overcast — rain at 7. with
thunder — over by 9 o'clk. Got up
before 7 Am. bk [illegible] before 8 & at 9 o'clk.
we all rode over to the depot with
Uncle D. & Aunt A. to see them started
on their way to N.Y. The train was in time
about 9.30 we drove around the lake
& then home. The men were at work
digging cellar for shed back of the cow
house & I had to do some engineer's work
to establish grades in the barn yard.
The work on the new strawberry bed also
went on — I managed to do a little
on my etching — Dinner at 1 — after
that made up [illegible] pkg. of [illegible] to
send to Mr. K. & wrote him a letter.
At 4.20 I went to [illegible] & P.O.; Ralph
going with me. Tea at 6.30 — Eve
reading etc. — wrote an order for
strawberry plants to W. S. [illegible] —
To bed about 10.30.
