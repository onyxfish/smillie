## Left Page

### March, Sunday 23, 1890

A day of clouds, sunshine and wind — Bkfo about 9 — after that, for awhile with my newspaper then, arranging Father's die proofs until after 12 — then out & walked down to Studio — got some things — then up to 5th Ave. & 43d. S. & home at 1.45 — Dinner at 2. After dinner again at my work until 4.45 then again to Studio & home at 6 — a cup of tea at 6.30 — Ev'g. Charl came in for a little while bringing a letter from Kate — then I labored over Bridgeman's Bead Catalogue making up a list for Montrose. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 24, 1890

Bright & beautiful day — To studio before 10 but my head felt so heavy & I so generally out of sorts that I could not really get to work. I got at my tracing of Union Trust Co. Bldg. but made very little progress on it. Went to Bank for Annie before 10 clk — on my way home stopped in at Reichards & again spent some time looking at Abbey's delightful pen drawings — Home at 1.45 Dinner was about 3 when again I got to Studio. — I worked to about as good purpose as I did in the m'g. Coleman came in for a little while — On my way home stopped in at Boussod, Valedon & Co. to see some Water-Colors by Mlle Lemaire "The flist" & a number of English scenes in Water Color by Fraser — Then up to 46 S. & back home — dinner — Ev'g. at 7.30 went up to Dr. Yale's — met him just going out — but he listened to my plaint for half an hour, then on his way to visit a patient, he walked down home with me. I wrote a letter to S.R. Koehler — To bed at 10.
