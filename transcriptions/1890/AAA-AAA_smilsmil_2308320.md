## Left Page

### October, Thursday 23, 1890

Bleak, loaden on [illegible]. at noon sleet
began to fall, followed by heavy rain
that continued for the rest of the day
with high wind. I sketch the
m[illegible] out of doors, until the rain began
to fall, trimming vines & with coyle
transplanting small plants in the
flower beds. The afternoon was
spent in some cleaning & [packing]
away in the studios & making a
Pansy sketch for the completion of
the bit of graver work commenced
sometime last Summer. Went
down town a little before 6.
Ev[ening]. Several little things were
done in the way of preparation
for our departure at the end of next
week. To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 24, 1890

A beastly day — Heavy rain or
mizzle all day long. I worked
all day in etching room. Taking
last proofs from "Dry-point" Single
"Dark Hollyhocks" worked
until 4.30 then washed up
& rested until 6 o'clk. Wrote
a short note to S. R. Koehler
& at 6.15 went to P.O. There
got a letter from W. Koehler.
Tea at 7. Ev[ening], wrote a
long letter to S. R. K. To bed
at 11, very tired.
