## Left Page

### December, Friday 26, 1890

Heavy, old fashioned snowstorm all day — Got to studio after 10 o'clk
& spent some time in getting the place warmed up — kindled a fire
— opened all steam valves & closed open window. Ther. at 46°. Geo. & Chauncey came in for a Xmas visit. Then
I took Mr. [illegible] plates from the board
& cut margins & otherwise fussed about
until near 2 — then home for the day —
what remained of the afternoon was spent between Ralph, sewing & a nap. Dinner at 6.30. So
we all went over to the Met. Theatre
Balcony & 41¢ S. & saw & heard the
Mr. Duff Comic Opera Co. give "Patience"
Annie, Kate & I, sat in front row, balcony.
Charl, Mary, Katie & Bessie sat in orchestra
front. After the 1st Act, it was so cold in
the balcony & few people in the house, we
went down stairs & occupied seats there.
The performance was fairly good excl.
Brunthorne — First [illegible] taken
a buffoon. Home before 11 & to bed
after.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 27, 1890

A pleasant day but cold & overcast much of the time — Got to studio
soon after 10 — the day was spent in
work of an inconsequential character,
trying to pick up & clean up and in
finishing up proofs. Home at 1 o'clk
had dinner — back to studio at 2.30
fussed about there — Charl coming
in we went up to Geo's studio. Then
Dr. Gester came in & getting on to
his hobbies — education of women —
he talked until dark. At 5.30 I
went to Colman's to enquire after him —
he is improving — Saw Mr. C — Home at
6 — dressed & at 7 Annie & I went
to Walter's where we dined. Mr. &
Mrs. Sprague & Mr. & Mrs. Johnston
of "The Century" being the other guests.
We had a very pleasant time
until after 11 — Then home & to bed
at 12.
