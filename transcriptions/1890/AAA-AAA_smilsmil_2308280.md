## Left Page

### July, Thursday 31, 1890.

Very hot day with strong, drying wind. Clouds in the afternoon and
a very few drops of Rain — The
m'f was spent in the usual out
of door duties & trying to rectify
some blunders in putting up the
small house for cold frame sashes
the afternoon. I worked very hard
in getting down the heavy canvas
upon Piazza floor over milk. Room —
it was not finished until near
6.30 — a little before I went down
town to P.O. Before 9 Mr. Parke
& Mrs Hosey drove up & made a
short call. At 9.30 did some trunk
packing preparatory to a start to-morrow
m'f & to bed at 10. A series of very
heavy thunderstorms with wind, from
about midnight to 4 A.M. Ralph
was wakened & said: "Mamma, what
tumbled down — was it a house?"

## Right Page

### August, Friday 1, 1890.

*At Walter's House. Belleport, L. I.*

Up at 5.30 — a heavy cloud hung over every
thing with now & then a little sprinkle
of rain. The stage came at 6.05 Mary's
trunk was ready — ours was not — consequent-
ly we drove to depot in the depot wagon and
came near having a serious time on the big
hill when near the depot. The pole slipped
out of the neck-yoke ring & for a time things
looked decidedly squally — the horses behaved
admirably — we got to depot just as the train
came up. I settled myself in one corner,
very unhappy. I did not wish to leave home
— I could not bear to leave Ralph — it seemed
to me unnatural — at the same time I
felt forced to go — I was forced to go — the tem-
perature was not uncomfortable until we
neared the City — on time (2.47) at Hoboken
& there Edw° & Carleton met us (Mary, Annie & I)
we crossed to N.Y. by Christopher St. ferry — there
sent our trunks across by special exp — and we
took horse cars — stopped at Huyler's & all hands
(except me) had cream soda. Got to foot of 34th St.
before 4 — took 4.20 train & got to Belleport
before 6.30 — there Walter & Louise met us at the
depot & we were driven to the house at once.
Dinner after 7 — a little tired & sleepy —
chatting in the even'g & to bed at 10 o'clk.
