## Left Page

### July, Thursday 17, 1890

Warm, cloudy mt f. Shower soon after 1 o'clk. A very heavy shower with wind & thunder & lightning about 4.30 — clear, very cool evef. I worked out of doors until after 11 — then to etching room & bit in the aqua-tint outline "Old house near Bolougne". Dinner at 1 — After that I took two india pf° from plate just bitten — Excitement when the storm struck us — Rushing to get things in — clew up pareens & mop up the water that forced its way in — so dark that I could not see to work for a time. — Picked berries for tea — After tea went to P.O. on my way home called at Mr. J.R. DeWitts — Saw her, her br° & his wife & Mr. Goulds — After 9 o'clk Parke, Mr. P— & Mr. Chas. Parke called — they left at 10 o'clk & we got to bed soon after —

## Right Page

### July, Friday 18, 1890

Very cool, breezy, delightful day — I got up for a moment before 7 to bid Emily good bye — She left under escort of W m Parke — I got up again for all day about 8 o'clk. — Out of doors until noon — except that after bkf° — I laid two aqua-tint grounds over "Old house" — the second one was a success. Aftnoon I went to etching room & for about an hour varnished on plates. Soon after 3 Ed m & Mary, Miss Williams, Annie, Ralph & I went out in the Seavy wagon — 3 seats — we rode over past the Morris place, across to Elk lakes & home soon after 6 — having had a delightful drive. Even f. a fire on the hearth — a quiet time reading — To bed about 10.30.
