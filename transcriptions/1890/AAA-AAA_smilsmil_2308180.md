## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 8, 1890

Bright breezy mt. Heavily overcast after 11 — more sunshine about 1 or 2 o'clk
more clouds & squalls with a dash or two of rain — cloudless moonlight at
night. After bkfp. Annie read aloud to me Mary Sprague Cook's paper on "Dialect
in Fiction" to be read this afternoon before the Wed. P.M. club — Exceedingly clever,
I think. Then to Studio — Took jade one to Kurtz & had it photographed — there
until near 2 — to Studio — Swain Gifford came in to see Coleman — Mounted
a proof — home to lunch at 1 — back soon after 3 after having been to Kurtz's
to see the negatives taken this mt.
It was so dark that I could not see
to work — went to see Eaton, Secy. of
W.C. & talked with him of catalogue
matters — then to Kurtz — then to Century —
Home at 6 — dinner. Every. Annie
Read to me a paper by Mr. Frank
Brownell on "Single Tax" — to be read
before Wed. P.M. club — Annie is to
take part in the discussion — The
next matter to be settled, I suppose,
will be the Precession of the Equinoxes —
To bed at about 11 after having written
notes to Chas. Platt & Wm. Pate & Co.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 9, 1890

Bright, breezy & cold — After o when I
got to studio, found Coleman there — he
had commenced the repainting of
the sky on a large canvas — Mr. Ronier.
I worked for a time, with crayon, on
background of a new Seeley drawing for
dry-point. Home to lunch at about 1 —
After that I went out with Annie on
a regular shopping expedition — we
went to furniture stores — silver ware
stores — lamp stores & other places —
getting through at about 4.30 we rode
down to Washington Sq. & called
upon Mr. Ehninger — not in — there
Annie rode up town & I walked,
stopping at the Studio. Dinner at 6.30.
Evng. after a little reading wrote
to Smith & McCallum & Surith & Heinck.
To bed at about 11.
