## Left Page

### February, Saturday 15. 1890.

Bright day as I could see by the
sunshine upon my bed room
window but I was not able to
get out of bed being used up by
a diabolical headache & very
general misery — Was able
to get up in the early evng &
go into the nursery while the
bed & bed room were getting an
airing — back to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### February, Sunday 16. 1890.

A bright, windy, rather cold day —
I was in bed until about 1 o'clk.
still suffering from a headache —
When I got up, staggered about as
though I had been sick for a month.
Sat at table at dinner & did the
carving but ate none of it — Spent
what remained of the day loung-
ing on lounges. About 11 o'clk. Charl
came in then Geo. bringing letters
from Mary — later they went to the
Union League together to see the
pictures. After tea Oscar Ireland
& his wife came in & made a call
of half an hour — I looked over
the newspaper & got to bed at
11.
