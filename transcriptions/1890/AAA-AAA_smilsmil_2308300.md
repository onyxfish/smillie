## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 9, 1890

Disagreeable day — mizzling rain at times during the m't — but the men kept at work until noon —
Rain after noon & no work. Heavy rain at night. I worked in my etching room, biting 5×7"
etching — also, made an experimental drawing upon a soft ground and bit away at it for some times with Dutch Mordant & then, losing patience
I tried nitric & water & very soon destroyed my ground. Also, bit an aqua-tint over an old etching — No. 6 — Eliz'th Howe arr'd from N.Y. at about 4 — After tea I walked down town to P.O. Annies & I had expected to go to the Reception given by Judge McCollum to Justice Williams but it rained so hard that we gave it up. We stayed at home chatting. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 10, 1890

Very disagreeable day with much heavy rain — After bkfs't went to the garden & among other things got a very nice mess of green peas — also, gathered a number of yellow tomatoes & took them to Florence Hathrofs — Men to work biting 5×7 " — bit of brook etc & continuing experiments with soft ground etching — Finished biting of 5×7 soon after 4 — Parke came in at about 5 & soon after 6 I walked down to the P.O. with him. After tea, looking over photos & views about Bar Harbor that Eliz. brought with her — and reading news paper — putting up flower seeds etc — etc — To bed after 10.
