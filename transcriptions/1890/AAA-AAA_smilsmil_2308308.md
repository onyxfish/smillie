## Left Page

### September, Saturday 27, 1890

nasty [illegible]. Fog & mizzle — in the afternoon it was not wet — & Judson came & worked with Hemick & the horses clearing up the lawn. Cleared about 6 — brilliant moonlight night. I was very busy all day in my etching room after getting through with my garden duties in the [illegible]. I took proofs from soft-ground etching & worked upon the plate between p[?] — also p[?] from the etching "a bit of brook." I worked as long as I could see without producing any very satisfactory results — the soft ground plate was not bitten enough at first & the rebiting & one biting spoiled it. At 6 went downtown with Jim and Ralph. Eve[nin]g. after reading newspaper — I read aloud from Oct. Harper. Then Annie read aloud — & as usual I fell asleep. To bed at about 11. . . . .

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 28, 1890

**Ther.** fell to freezing last night.

A glorious day — brilliantly clear with strong, cool wind from N. W. Up late — after b[reakfast], went to the garden & when through with my duties there I went to stable & harnessed the horses — just as I had finished, about 11 o'clk., Hemick came in. It was about 11.30 when we got started on our ride — drove over to Brooklynn & South Pond, enjoying every foot of the way, & got back at 2.30 and had a late dinner — after that I gathered some flower seeds & a little before 5 o'clk. I lay down on the bed & had a nap, waking after 6. & sundown. Tea before 7 — while at tea Parke came in for a few minutes. Eve[nin]g. I wrote letters to S.R. Koehler, Sam[ue]l. Colman & Lauckner. About 10 o'clk. I went to P. O. with our Sunday's mail — To bed at about 11.
