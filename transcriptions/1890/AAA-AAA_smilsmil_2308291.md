## Left Page

### August, Friday 22, 1890

Delightful w'f. Before noon there were indications of showers —
about 3 o'clk. thundershowers passed south of us & a little later a heavy,
steady rain set in — after my w'f work in the garden I attended
to some "chores" that have been waiting
for my attention for some time —
after 11 I went down town & saw
Mr. Smith of McCollum & Smith &
put into his hands the matter of
closing purchase of Lozier place.
Home at 12 — dinner at 1 — about
that time fat Curry came to the house
very drunk & scared Katie, the Cook.
He spent a couple of hours or more on
the grass under the big beech, sleeping.
I worked awhile with dry point on
Aqua-tint — Then got things ready
for printing to-morrow — a quiet
evening in-doors — heavy rain all
night.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 23, 1890

Rain in the w'f — gradually
blowing off into showers from the
N. W. Clearing bleak & cold in the
afternoon. Went down town at 10.30
in the w'f & got Zinc sheet for the
5"×7" pfe. Stopped at Wc. C. & Smith's —
then home & got to proving Aqua-tint.
Wc. C. & S. sent up for me to come down
immediately after dinner. I went
down at 2.30 arranged with Agew
Lathrops to endorse my note to pay
for the Lozier place — the note to run
until I return to W'f, when I can get
at securities in Safe Deposit. Lozier
did not put in an appearance so I
left it with W. Smith to settle & rete
to the house & proved slate until 4.
Then all off for a ride done up in our
winter wraps. The horses had not been
out for 3 days & the very mischief was
in them — they seemed from the start to
be on the verge of a runaway — However
we got home all right at 6.15 Eve'g
I had many little chores to do — went
down town about [illegible] cases — It was
after 9 before I was able to sit down —
To bed at 10.30.
