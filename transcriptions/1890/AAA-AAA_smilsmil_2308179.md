## Left Page

### January, Monday 6, 1890

A beastly day — muggy, damp & mild.
I felt completely prostrated — got
to studio after 10 but was unable to
work. Saw Coleman there, painting
a little. After 12 I went to Bank and
Safe Deposit Co. for Annie, to look over her
Wilson & Toms investments. About 1.30
when I got home to lunch — back
to studio at 2.30 — wrote letters to the
Wilson & Toms Inv. Co. — & to H.H. Sprague
of Buffalo — Home before 6 o'clk.
Dinner. Evef. Annie & I went to
Daly's Theatre & saw "As you like it."
I must say I did not like it much
as presented. Ada Rehan is getting
to be very objectionable to me. She
was no such Rosalind as I would
fall in love with. Stage mounting &
all together it was a disappointment to
me. We met there Fred Smillie & his
wife — Home soon after 11 & to bed
before midnight.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 7, 1890

Weather still nasty — not quite so
muggy as yesterday — at night, almost
pleasant. To studio soon after 10, not
much better able to work than yester-
day. I really accomplished nothing.
T.W. Wood came in on Water Color Soc.
Cat. business. Before 1 messenger came
with jade, for etching, from H.R. Bishop.
Home to lunch at 1.30 — back before 3.
Afternoon, some fussing with proofs
— went to Kurtz's — he was out —
Home at 5.30 — dinner at 6.30 —
Evef. Annie went to her Choral Club
rehearsal with the Bosé girls. I
settled myself to write to many —
Geo. & Emiley came in & chatted for
half an hour or so — then again at my
letter which I finished at 10. Wrote
notes to C.W. Hitchcock & Frederic
Schell. Posted letters soon after 10 and
walked for 20 min. or so. At 10.45
Annie & I took the Bosé girls
home — To bed soon after 11.
