## Left Page

### February, Sunday 23, 1890

A very mild, pleasant morning growing overcast & chilly in the afternoon with wind from the east.
B[illegible] about 9 - newspaper - children & Sue after that until about noon when I went out with Jim to druggist's & then walked in the sunshine, over to foot of H[illegible] S. & East River. Geo. S— called while I was out — Dinner at 2 — At 4 (after a call from Clark) I went out with Sue & Annie — left them at 5th Ave. & 48th S. — on my way home stopped in to church cor. Mad. ave. & 38th S. & heard Rev. O. B. Tiffany — then home — tea at 6.30 — at 7.30 Sue & Annie went out with Miley & Geo. I remained at home — read & wrote — among other notes one to Emma Bowen. Rain before 10. Sue & Annie got in soon after 10.30 — [illegible] about 11.

## Right Page

### February, Monday 24, 1890

Nasty day — pouring rain most of the time. I got up feeling quite in sympathy with the weather & did not dare expose myself by going out. I wrote a letter to Koehler & worked upon some plans for small movable glass frames to shield Tomatoes at Montrose — In spite of the weather Annie & Sue were out — see the [illegible] — the excitement over the wedding to-morrow of course increases — late in the afternoon Katie & Mollie Adams arr. from New Haven — In the eve[illegible], Sue, Katie, Mollie & Annie went to Fifth Ave. theatre to see the Kendals — I was left at home to nurse myself. Carleton Cook came in — stayed all night — about 9 Edith & Maison came & after talking the matter over went to the Murray Hill Hotel — I wrote part of my Regular letter to Kate & to bed before 10.
