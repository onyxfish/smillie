## Left Page

### November, Saturday 22, 1890

Very fine day, bright & breezy — Got to
studio a little before 9 & to work on
Shirlaw mezzotint scraping & burnishing
the more delicate gradations of modelling
upon face & white drapery — Home to
lunch at 1.15 — back at 2.30 & then
commenced to take working [illegible]
from the plate, taking all the re-
maining daylight to take three.
I am disappointed in the result —
the delicate greys being muddy —
partly because the mezzotint ground is
not perfect & partly because of my
imperfect scraping & burnishing — At
I went to Colman's with [illegible] from his color
plate — [illegible] — then I went to Keppel's [illegible]
in half an hour — saw C — he is delighted with
my proofs — thinks he would become an etcher
if he could get such proofs — Home at 6.20 —
Dinner — Charles dined with us — at 8
Annie & I went to the Lenox Lyceum. 9th St
Cor. Mad. Ave. & heard W. Fagan of the British
Museum Print Room. He discoursed in a
very rambling, inconclusive & unsatisfying
way (to me) upon Etching — Rembrandt, Meryon,
Jacquemart & Whistler — The lecture was
very elementary & all of it scrappy — illustrated
by poor transparicon reproductions of etchings.
We got out at 9.45 — home — & to bed at
10.30

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 23, 1890

A very pleasant day — Bkfst
at 9 — From 10 until about 1
o'clk. I wrote upon first draught
of a paper that I hope to finish
for the Etching Club Publication
& catalogue. Then out & took
a walk until 2 o'clk — Then
dinner — Jno. Valentine dined
with us — Later Mr. Atherton
& her two children came & then
Geo. & Nellie & two of their chil-
dren — I went down to studio
at about dusk — Tea after 6.
Eve'g I read until after 9
then to Grand Central Station
& bought R.R. tickets & seats for New
Haven on Wed. next.     Home &
to bed at 9.30
