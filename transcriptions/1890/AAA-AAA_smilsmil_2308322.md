## Left Page

### October, Monday 27, 1890

Ther. fell to 31° last night.

By all odds the worst day yet being
an almost constant succession of
Rain or snow squalls without a ray
of sunshine to mitigate it. I
got my fires going in studio and
etching room & spent the day
there putting things away & doing
preliminary to packing. Herrick
at last got the finishing of stair-
way passage to etching room done,
to my great relief. At 6 o'clk. I
went down to P.O. Tea at 6.30.
Most of the evell was spent in
my dark room getting things there
in shape to leave for the winter —
then some time trying to save some
of my pansy seeds & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 28, 1890

Ther. fell to 29° last night.

For a little time in the mf. promise
of clear weather, but again it was
overcast & there were snow squalls.
Considerable snow lay upon the ground
& roofs when we got up in the mf.
I spent most of the day in the
studio & etching room engaged
upon the work of getting things
separated — the things to leave
being properly put away & the
things to take being prepared
for packing. In the afternoon
Coyle & Oscar took up a fine
young maple successfully and
it was moved from where it was
not wanted to a place at the north
of the house, among the beeches.
