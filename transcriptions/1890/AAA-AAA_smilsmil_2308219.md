## Left Page

### March, Thursday 27, 1890

A dull, raw day - with rain beginning about 10.30 p.m. —
Worked all day at studio making changes on Water-Color drawing
"The Thousand Islands, Lake George from Black Mt.", washing out a large
part of the foreground — putting it in in new form & color & working generally
over other parts — all this because I had rec'd a circular from Ferd.
Kaiser of St. Louis stating that he is about to move into a new store, enlarge
his business & make a start with W.C.'s.
Home to lunch as usual & to dinner before 6 — Dinner at 6 — Miss Foshay,
Mr. G.W. Parke & Bessie P. dined with us & at 7.30 we all went to Chickering
Hall — to Diet Kitchens Concert, where Geo. Smiley & Mr. Valentine joined us —
we had a very enjoyable concert
I lost my heart to Mr. Chas. Tyler
Dutton who sang some ballads deliciously — After the concert took Mr. P.
& Bessie to Murray Hill Hotel. Miss F.
rode on to her home on Madison Ave —
Home before 11 & to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 28, 1890

Heavy showers with thunder & lightning
in the m'g about 9 & in the afternoon
after 4 — with some clear weather
between — After 10 when I got to the
studio — worked all day on drawings
for stairway by the easel at Montrose
Home at 1.20 — back at 2.40 — & home
to dinner at 6.15 — Eve'g wrote a
letter to Herrick — Charl came in
after 9 o'clk. with trial pf. from the
negatives that he took from the comp-
sters on Washington's Birthday. To bed
before 11.
