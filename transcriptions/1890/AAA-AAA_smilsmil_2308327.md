## Left Page

### November, Thursday 6, 1890

A lovely, mild, indian summer sort of day — Got up with a bad head-ache — went in to see neighbor Martin at 8.30 A.M. about the yard fence between us. Douglass came with a man to paint & Morgan came to attend to some carpenter jobbing. It was 10.30 before I got to studio — then Emma Browne came in & sat for awhile. I put most of the little things away — spread my rugs & began to look ship-shape — Then Eliz. Horne came in & then Mr. [illegible]. Home at 1 — dinner — after that I took a nap on dining room sofa — got to studio about 4 & mounted two or three proofs — Young Watrous came in. Home soon after 6 — tea — Ively Mr. [illegible] came in & sat with us for awhile. Finished letter to S. R. Koehler & mailed it — To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 7, 1890

A nasty wet, dark with heavy fog — but before noon the fog lifted & the rest of the day was bright & warm. About 9.30 got to K. & V.'s with my aqua-tint plate to have some proofs taken from it. Met there Mr. Adler of Fischel & Adler, very extensive print publishers & had a long talk with him which I hope may lead to some business. Young Voigt tried hard to please me in proving my plate but he could not produce the counterpart to my proof taken to him for a guide — Home to lunch at 1.15. Annie out at her Woman's league meeting. After lunch I wrote to Herrick & sent him checks — At 3 went to studio — Annie walked down with me. For an hour I worked at getting mounts for some of my proof — At 4.30 went to D. B. Butler's — met Eliz. Horne & ordered some frames — then walked with her to 34th St. & 6th Ave — back to Mad. Ave left her & home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — after that Annie & I went to a Mr. Cook's where Jim, with other children, was celebrating a birthday party — Home at 9 & to bed at 10 with a headache that bothered me all night.
