## Left Page

### Thursday 3°
### July, Tuesday 1. 1890.

Turned over two pages —
For Tues. see Thurs. .
Heavy fog in the m'f., gradually lifting & before noon it was blazing hot and exceedingly muggy — Heavy shower at 1 o'clk. Picked beans & then got at the currant bushes where worms have suddenly appeared in vast numbers, & there I spent the rest of the m'f. Dr. Mulford came & kept company with me for some time —
Dinner at 1. After that wrote to M. S.R. Koehler & sent by Exp. two plt? of my Aqua-tint experiments — took plt? & letter to P.O. at 4.45 — then back and worked until after 6 in Etching Rm. at some further aqua-tint experimenting — Tea — after that down to P.O. — back at 8.30 — then for some time on the piazza — night very still & warm —

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 2. 1890.

Very heavy rain in the early morning. From 9 until about noon it did not rain & there were promises of clearing, but after that, heavy rain again — a windy wet night. Spent the day at work upon my two experimental aqua-tint plates — burnished & scraped & took proofs — Dinner at 1 — then again at my work until 5 o'clk. The plates are interesting as examples, but they have very little artistic value. At 5.30 I went down from to P.O. Jim & Ralph went with me — no rain at the time but lots of moist wind — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g., newspaper & a little writing.
