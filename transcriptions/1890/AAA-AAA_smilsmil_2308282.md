## Left Page

### August, Monday 4, 1890

Up for a b'kf't. at 7.30 — but Walter was not ready for it until 8 — Grey morning but later it cleared with strong S.W. wind blowing — Walter went in town early — on return of horse & vehicle, Louisa, Mary, Annie & I went to town & beyond to attend to some small matters — back by about 10 o'clk. We were to go sailing but Edward did not feel well enough — so, I wrote a letter to the boys & a note to Mrs. Williams — & took a short nap before lunch at about 2. Mr. Hoyt called in the m'f. After lunch, Louisa, and the children, with Mrs. Hoyt's two little girls, played Hayden's "Kinder Sinphonie". At 4 I drove Louisa to make a visit at Mr. Fairchil[d's] after which we ret'd home & taking many things drove down to Patchogue where considerable shopping was done. It was nearly 7 when we got home — dinner — even'g. Some reading along — some chatting — some music from Louisa & to bed shortly after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 5, 1890

*with the Irelands at Easthampton.*

Brilliant day with strong south westerly winds — After b'kf't. I wrote a short letter to Mary — At 10 Annie & I left the house & took 10.29 train to Bridgehampton, getting there at 12.02 thence, by stage, 7 miles, to Easthampton where we were delivered at the door of the Ireland's new house at a little after 1. I found Aunt Anna in very good spirits & looking very well. Catherine, Emma & Mary were with her — we soon had dinner — At about 4, Catherine, Emma, Annie & I started for a walk going to the far end of the village — we met Preston Spring & then went in to his boarding place & saw his wife, baby & Margery. On our way back stopped at That. Moran's — saw his wife, daughters, Percy & Leon M. — his big picture, just started, of Ice bergs — it was near 7 when we got home — tea — even'g chatting until 10.30 — then to bed.
