## Left Page

### May, Friday 2, 1890

Very bright, pleasantly cool day —
Got to studio at 10 + after a little time
went down town — first to Harper
Bros. + saw Mr. Schell, hoping to find
an opening for our Studio's hall boy
Willie — but there is no longer a working
Art Dept. in the Harper Establishment —
+ so, no chance for Willie there. Then
to Bank of Commonwealth in Nassau St —
got May Int. on Mil. + S. + W. Mc. Bonds
then to A.B.N. Co + got some die [illegible] from
MacDonough — then to Ringler's + left
several copper plates to have steel
faces removed — Then, up town — to
2d. Nat. Bnk. dept. wt. + placed bonds in
safe deposit + home to lunch at 1.30 —
Annie in Yonkers — Woman's Unitarian
League. At 2.30, back to studio + spent
the afternoon in signing 50 Holly hook
[illegible] [illegible] + putting in [illegible] other
proofs + preparing to print to-morrow —
among other callers, Dr. Gale came in —
Home at 6.30 — dinner — evening
reading — some of it aloud — to bed
about 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 3, 1890

A very pleasant day — Got to studio
soon after 10 + at once to work to print
a few [illegible] from plate "A Lady of Cairo
Visiting" — but something was wrong with
my ink + when I went home to lunch
at 1 o'clk. I had not been able to get
one good proof. Back at 2.30 + again
printed until 5.30 with hardly any
better result. I got but one really
good proof — Home at 6.30 very tired
+ very much disgusted — Dinner at
6.30 — I rested until nearly — then
took Annie to Edwin's + Walter's — All
out — brought A— home + then went to
Century — My painting "A bit of brook
with some sheep" by gas light looked very
whitish as compared with its color
by daylight — again I felt tired + dis-
gusted. At 10.30 I rode up town with
Dr. Gale + got to bed at 11.30.
