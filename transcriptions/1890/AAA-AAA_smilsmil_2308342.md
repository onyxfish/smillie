## Left Page

### December, Saturday 6. 1890.

A dull, disagreeable day, rain commencing before noon & falling heavily before night. I kept closely in-doors all day feeling dispirited & heavy about the head. I wrote to some extent upon my paper for the Etching club, but without spirit. Jimmie [illegible] out all day. A Wednesday club committee meeting in the [illegible] — a Opera Matinée (with Mary & Kate) in the afternoon & Phil. Concert at night. Colman called to see me at night about 5 o'clk p.m. a kind visit to enquire after my health. The evg. I spent alone trying to do a little writing. It was about 11 when I got to [illegible].

## Right Page

### December, Sunday 7. 1890.

Some sunshine for awhile in the mf., but windy & cold — overcast in the afternoon. Rble. — after 9 — Sat for awhile reading newspaper — Then went out & had a short walk on the sunny side of Park Ave. When I came in I tried to write but could not. Dinner at 2. — Afternoon again tried to write — no ideas — no words — only evagulation. Gave it up & had a nap. Tea at 6.30 — Eveg I amused myself reading & got to bed soon after 10.

Have heard that Charl is again confined to his room Taking heroic doses of medicine — I hope his doctor wont kill him.
