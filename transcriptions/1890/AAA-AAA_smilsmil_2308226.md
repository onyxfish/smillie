## Left Page

### April, Thursday 10, 1890

Some sunshine & much shade — a cold, windy day — To studio soon after 10. To work on outline of Union Trust Co. Bldg. Ye went to Ridgefield to see the Whiting H. I worked until 1 — then home to lunch — Annie lunched with Mrs. Jol. Herbet Morse — after lunch I wrote a short note to Herrick — posted it & got to studio about 3 — on outline until after 5 — home at 6 — dinner after 6.30 Alice Dreland again dined with us — about 8.30 I went to Union League Club — a Water-Color Show — Ye there — just ret'd from Ridgefield — & determined to buy Jenny Whiting's house — under Charlie's direction he wrote her a letter to that effect — I went home at 11 — Walter & the bride had spent part of the evening there — To bed at once.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 11, 1890

Bright, windy & chilly — Got to studio at 10 — spent some time looking over old pencil sketches to find a sketch made years ago on Lake George at Camp Manhattan — then to work on Union Trust Co. outline — Home at 1 & back at 2.30. Finished the outline at last — then wrote a long letter of instruction to Herrick with check — & about 5 o'clk. went to the Am. Art. Ass'n Galleries to see the new exhib'n — groups of works by 8 or 10 of our home artists — Chase, Millet, Remington — Dewey, [illegible], Wefins — Champney, Reinhart — Minor — Poor — Home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Even'g went to Annual Meeting of Etching Club — Shelton's Studio — 106 W. 55th St — On my way up saw Barnum's parade — A large quorum got together after some waiting but the result was to me a very satisfactory meeting — I hope that the tide has now turned in the right direction & that next year we will make a good showing. Home at 11.30 & to bed before 12.
