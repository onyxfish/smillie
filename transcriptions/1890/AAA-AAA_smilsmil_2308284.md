## Left Page

### August, Friday 8, 1890

*At home. Montrose.*

Overcast day, much to our relief. Threats of rain — slight sprinkling while we were in N.Y. city — Showers on Pocono Mt. — Beautiful eve'g. Up at about 6 o'clk. Finished packing of trunk before 7. When sup. man called — bkfst. at 7.30 — at 8 we started for station & took 8.28 train into town where we arrived about 10.40. While I looked after my trunk Annie started on a shopping tour — I went to studio — then to Knoedler's — a lamp store — & Lord & Taylor's — at 11.45 went to Purcell's ordered lunch. Annie arr. soon after. At 12.30 started for foot of Christopher St. — took 1 o'clk train for Montrose — supper at Scranton — Montrose depot at 7.30. Herrick & team waiting. I drove home, getting there at 9 o'clk — latter part of the way, darker driving than I liked. Found all well at home. Miss Williams, Grattie & Katie very glad to see us. Chatted awhile with Miss W. & to bed at 10, closing an outing through which we have been singularly fortunate.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 9, 1890

Pleasant day — Clouded m'g, clear p.m. — heavy thunderstorms from 8 to 11 p.m. — I spent the m'g. about the place, especially in the garden and had a rest before dinner. Soon after 1 — the afternoon was given over to loafing — Parke & his father came up & we sat under the hemlock tree for awhile where Herrick is putting up a small building for cold frame sashes. A little after 4 o'clk — all hands went out in 3 seat wagon & rode until 6. Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. at 8.15 a very heavy thundershower, with hail, came upon us — a succession of heavy showers followed — Rain was falling & thunder roaring when we went to bed at 10.
