## Left Page

### May, Friday 16, 1890

*Chesapeake Bay Str. "Virginia". Stateroom "U". Pat. aft.*

Pleasant day with showers. B'kf't at usual time — after that commenced packing which was finished a few min. before 11 when Sups. Man came for trunks. Geo. came in for a few minutes — After 11 I went out for a little while with Ralph & attended to some little matters. Dinner at 12.30 — we kissed Ralph, still engaged in his m'f'nap in his crib & in charge of Jim, & left the house at 1.15 got to foot of Desrosses S't at 1.30. Took 2.10 Train — very comfortable — good seats, pleasant temperature — smart showers before getting to Phila. Had a light meal on the train at 6 — Got to Steamboat at Baltimore at 7 — Did not get such a Stateroom as I wanted — large — big bedstead etc. but too far aft & too near the wheel — We kept out for some time until I felt compelled to go in — then sat in our room & by electric light read until near 10 — then to bed — a good bed but for all that not an unqualified success so far as securing a night of perfect repose was concerned —

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 17, 1890

*Princess Anne Hotel — Room 84. Virginia Beach — Va.*

Up at about 6.30 after a tolerable, but not a restful night — As our Stateroom was not far abaft the wheel, a profound tremor prevented profound sleep. I got up with considerable Rheumatic Pain in the back of my head & neck — A. & I paced the dock at Old Point Comfort while freight was being unloaded — Started for Norfolk about 7 — Breakfast — got to N. at 8 — transferred to Va. Beach R.R., train started at 8.40 & after 18 miles ride through a miserable half-wilderness country we were landed at the door of the hotel at 9.30 — Very pleasantly impressed by appearance of things — new house — large rooms — everything clean — we started out for a walk up the beach — brilliant sunshine — very warm — back at 1.10 tired — we both lay down upon the bed & slept soundly until 1 o'clk — A good dinner at 1.30 — after that wrote to Ralph — Geo. & Charl. — & wrote to Jim — got letters off in 4.30 mails — then wandered in the woods back of the house & down the beach & sunned ourselves — In at 6.20 — near 7 when we had tea, after that loitered about until after 8 — then to our room & under a bright electric light we read until 10 o'clk — then to bed.
