## Left Page

### January, Friday 10, 1890

A raw, disagreeable day, with wet
pavements in the aft. + overcast
all day long — I was Routed out of my
bed before daylight + feel very shaky
through the day — It was 11 before I
got to the Studio — then to Kurty's
back at 12 — soon an agent for Dunbar
& — Providence, R.I., art dealers came
\+ spent some time looking over my
paintings — then Chat. Parsons came
in about N.C. Cat. Ill. for Harper's Weekly.
It was 2 o'clk when I got home to
lunch — back at 3 — then a young man
came from Keppel's with a lot of proofs
for me to sign — at 4 o'clk I went to
C. Harry Eaton's Room 4, N.C.A. C.A. Platt
there — first meeting of Cat. Com — there
until 5.30 — then home — dressed — at
7 Emma J. "Elig." Home — Chat. Collins and
Walter Cook sat down to dinner —
A pleasant eve'g until 10.30 — to
bed about 11.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 11, 1890

A nasty, dark day — sleet + slippery walks
in the aft. Rain in the middle of the
day + fog at night. To studio soon after
10 — then went to Kurtz's + got photos of
Jade — back to studio + painted on 12×16
sketch "Over Coast Offences" until after
1 o'clk — 1.45 when I got home to lunch.
Back before 3 — went to N.A. f D. + saw
C. Harry Eaton on Cat. business — back to
studio, after going to see Kurtz, washed
brushes + cleaned palette — put some
more of Keppel's dry-paint [illegible] into
press + again went to N.A. f D. — where
I saw members of the B. of Control at
request of Eaton who is opposing my
plan of giving most of the illustrated
to Kurtz — I explained that the Cat.
Com. was responsible only to the Soc'y which
was promptly admitted + no interference
will be attempted — Home at a little
before 6 — dinner — Eve'g. Annie
\+ I went to S°. Phil. Concert — It was
also Annual Century meeting, but
after getting home at 10.30 I went
to bed.
