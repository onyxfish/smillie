## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 6, 1890

*Returned to Belleport.*

Bkfp. about 8 o'clk — grey ovt. with portentous clouds to the north — a slight shower last night, just enough to lay the dust. After bkfp. Catherine, Annie & I walked to the dunes back of the house — an inner line — about 11 we went to the beach — heavy surf & few bathers — was introduced to a number of people — Dr. & Mrs. Herrick among the number — A clap of thunder drove every one away in a hurry at about 12 o'clk. Dinner at 12.30 — as the ice-cream came on to the table the stage drove up & I had to go without it — Started for R.R. station at Bridgehampton at 12.50 — pleasant, breezy & sunless ride — train at 1.55 — dirty ride to Belleport — arr. at 3.15 — Mr. Gould with Edward met us at the station — drove at once to the house — found a very interesting lot of letters awaiting us — a long one from Mary — from Geo. & Nellie & from Uncle Willie etc. — arate Relalie until dinner time at 6.30 — after that, on piazza — a very pleasant eve. Mr. & Mrs. Hayt came in for awhile after that, Louise gave us some music — we got to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 7, 1890

Very bright, pleasant day — got down on piazza at 7.30 — bkfp. an hour later. At 10.30 we went to the dock & before 11 got under way on the "Effie", Mr. Gould sailing — very light, baffling wind — at times it looked as though we would get stuck between Smitty's Point, our goal, and home — helpless at lunch time. about 12.15 we reached our destination — ordered a meal of blue fish, potatoes & bread & butter — then went over to the beach for half an hour — back & ate our meal (6 adults & 6 children) with a relish — then again to the beach where we lay upon the sand & watched the breakers & a bather — back to the "Effie" at 3 & sailed home in an hour — Exposure to the sun had given me a bad headache — I lay down & had a nap — when I got up, felt much better — dressed — dinner at 6.30. Every. the early part of it, on western piazza — later, in the parlor where Louisa entertained us with some delightful piano playing — then we settled some domestic & social problems & to bed a little before 11 o'clk.
