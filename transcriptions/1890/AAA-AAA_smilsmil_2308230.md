## Left Page

### April, Friday 18. 1890.

fine day — cool & rather windy.
To studio at 10 & to work on Union
Trust Co's bldg. closely at it all
the m'f. Miss Hallimee came
in for a moment to get my sig-
to a paper. She desires to have ch[?]
of Art Dep. of Chicago Fair in 1893.
Home abt — back at 2.30. Spent
much of the afternoon cutting back
& mounts for Union Trust drawing.
Home before 6. Annie & Emily
came in to studio about 5.30 with
some Jap. ware purchases made at
an auction. Home with them —
dinner — Eveth. Geo., Emily, Annie
& I went to the Star Theatre & saw
Crane in the "Senator" — a bellowing
farce — altho' I suppose that it is
intended for comedy. We laughed,
of course, but much of the play &
the playing was of a very low order.
Out at 11 — home & to bed at 12.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 19. 1890.

Still bright, breezy & cool — delightful
weather. To studio at 10 & worked
faithfully on Union Trust drawing
getting it into general keeping and
harmony. Home abt — back at 2.30
more work, & then I made paste
& pasted the mounts on the drawing
& put it under weight. Charl
came in about 5 o'ck. having ret-
urned his trip to Montreal with Uncle
Willie where he had gone hoping
to make a sale of his (uncle's)
business there. Home about 6,
dressed & at 7.30 Annie & I went
to Mrs. Ehninger's to dinner — on
Washington Square — a delightful,
rich meal with everything appert-
aining in perfect good taste —
Mrs. E — a singular contradiction —
in some ways showing so much strength
of character — in other ways being so
hopelessly weak in her grieving for
her dead husband. We left at 10 —
walked up home & to bed at 11.
