## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 10, 1890

A raw, overcast day & as my throat felt very uncomfortable
again I did not dare go out but stayed in doors & gave some of the
time to my Etching Club pub. paper — Eve'g I had a very
quiet time — Mary & Kate went to the Opera. Lohengrin — & Annie
& I sat by the fire, each revising our compositions, hers being for the
Ard. afternoon club on "clubs" & mine for the Etching Club publication — we got to bed before 11 o'clk.

Charl. lunched with us to-day again — not yet able to go down Town.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 11, 1890

Mild, pleasant day, altho' partially overcast. Soon after 9 went to P. P.
Morgan's with Keutz's photos, from the painted portraits — M. in Boston.
Then to studio. Worked on hands
in Shirlaw's mezzotint — & then took three proofs, one of which I shall submit to Shirlaw — Home to lunch
soon after 1 — back to studio at 2.30 much of the daylight of the afternoon
was given to getting a passe par-tout made of an old mount, for a chestnut gilt frame, into which I put
a proof of aqua-tint to give to Annie for a Dietrichsen Fair. Soon after 5
went to barber's & had my hair cut.
Home soon after 6. Eve'g I was all alone — Annie gone to her Choral Club
& Mary & Kate to Charl's — To bed at 10.30.
