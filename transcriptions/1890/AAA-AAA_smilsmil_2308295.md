## Left Page

### August, Saturday 30. 1890.

Bright m'f, growing cloudy and bleak as the day advanced — The m'f passed as most others have done. Some time in the garden — then out the men who were digging the cellar for the shed & with Coype & Oscar who were at work on the new Strawberry bed — Then to my work in studio and before dinnertime I finished the drawing in of lines on 5"×7" exptn plate for the butt ins. Parke sat beside me for an hour or more. After dinner worked on small plate getting it ready for an experimental dry-point for printing trials. It was nearly 4 o'clk. when Annie, Ralph & I started out for our ride — a Raw, violent wind made part of it not very pleasant. Home at 5.30 — tea at 6.30 — Eve'g with News papers, Harper & Century & loafing in his chair.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 31. 1890.

Disagreeable, Raw m'f. — Some slight niggling rain that might almost have been snow — Clearer at noon — some bright sunshine & color suggestive of Oct. Bright, cold moonlight night. B'kf. about 9 — In garden until after 11 — then read & had a nap before dinner at 1.30 — Parke dined with us — He left us at about 3.30 — then Annie, the boys & I walked out to the lake & back. Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. Annie discussed some questions proposed for discussion at the women's meetings of the Unitarian League during the coming winter — It was after 11 when we got to bed.
