## Left Page

### March, Monday 3, 1890

A real winter day at last; bright sunny & cold — 2 or 3 inches of snow. During the middle of the day much of the snow melted but at night it froze hard again — Up with a headache that bothered me all day — Got to studio after 10 — some time lighting a fire & cleaning up. Then started on 12×16 — Hazelbrook — April when S.P. Koshler came in — then Saml. Colman — K. stayed until 1 — then home to lunch — then to bank — then to Salmagundi club to see the Juengling proof & studies. A long talk with Kirby & Van Cleef — then to Keppel's & saw the Lalanne collection of etchings — then to Rastke & Jankueis — then to studio at about 5 — Soon after, went to N.A. & got some Catalogues W.C. Home at 6. Fanny Kimball made a short call before dinner — Every I sat in front of a very pleasant fire & read over much of my diary of '81 — the year when we were married. — 70
Bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 4, 1890

Bright, beautiful day — but at night a change; threatening bad weather — To studio about 10 & got to work on 12×16 "Hazelbrook — April" — Painted all over it — changing many of the lines & I think, improving it generally. Home to lunch at 1 — Annie lunched at Mrs. Saml. Colman's — meeting Miss Amused, Miss Holt & Mrs. Latham. The lunch to celebrate Colman's birth day. After lunch I went to 37th St. lex. to 3rd Ave. to see the hill upon which Jim & Ralph, with many other youngsters, were coasting — then to studio & painted on 12×16 until near 5 — then cleaned up & feeling very head tired I walked until 6 — then home — dinner at 6.30 — [illegible]. Annie went to her Choral Club rehearsal & I stayed at home & wrote my letter to Mary — mailed it at 10. To be soon after 11.
