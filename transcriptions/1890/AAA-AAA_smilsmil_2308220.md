## Left Page

### March, Saturday 29, 1890

A disagreeable raw, blustering day with snow flurries — I got to studio about 10 & spent the rest finishing plans for iron Stairway by well at Montrose Home to lunch a little after 1 — at 2.30 Annie & Jim walked down Lex. Ave. with me & I left them at Bro. George's, cor. 33°. S° — Later, Annie came to studio & then met Mlle. Clémence Van den Broeck. Tried to work on Tracing of Beef [illegible] most Co — worked perhaps an hour — Charl came in — home at 6.15 — dinner Evelf. Dr. Yale came in bringing a letter from Dr. Hermann Biggs with analysis [illegible] left with him last Tues. night. He now gives my kidneys a good character — It came to me like a reprieve to a man under sentence of death. After Y. left I read aloud a while — then Annie read aloud & I got to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 30, 1890

not quite so raw as yesterday — a covering of snow over everything when I got up — it soon disappeared — Snow flurries at short intervals until 2 or 3 o'clk. After breakfast & newspaper I got to work at arranging Father's die proofs & continued at it until 12. About 12.30 Annie, Jim & I went to Walter's in 37th St. & saw Alice Ireland who is now in charge there. As we left there so heavy a snow squall came up that Annie determined to go right home. Jim & I walked on up to 51st S° & 5th Ave. then home. Dinner at 2 — after that, more work on Father's proofs until 4.30 then I walked up to 72nd S° — through the Park — out at 59th S° & home — Tea at 6.30 — Evelf. Annie writes to J.C. Nicoll & R.W. Hitchcock. To bed before 11.
