## Left Page

### September, Sunday 7, 1890

A very warm day opening with a
heavy fog. Soon after 11 A.m. a smart
but brief shower. Showers passed
south of us in the afternoon. I did
a great deal of loafing during the
day but found time to unpack
the 9 or 10 boxes in the attic that were
sent here when the 41st Street home
was broken up. The contents were
mostly files of Tribune during the war,
London Ill. News, Punch, Art Journal
& other Periodicals & books. Dinner
at 2. — At 4.30 we went out riding
— delicious eve't — back at 6.30.
Tea. Eve'g. wrote part of a letter
to Kate after I had dozed for an
hour in a chair on the piazza.
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 8, 1890

misty & heavily overcast in the m't
with threats of rain, which, after
all, did not amount to anything —
did a good deal of out-of-doors
work of one kind & another and
also studio work. I commenced
the biting of 5×7" etching after my
painting "A bit of brook with some
Sheep" — also, laid a number of
experimental soft grounds and
made two or three experimental
drawings to find the proper pro-
portions of ground & tallow. The best
kind of baker & the best kind of
vehicle — As the horses were at
hard work all day hauling dirt
& stone we did not ride — Eve'g
finished Katie's letter & wrote to
Letta about the strawberry plants that
don't get here. Took letters to P.O.
before 10 — then home & to bed.
