## Left Page

### February, Friday 21. 1890.

Bright, cold, windy day. Did
not go out of the house — very heavy
head, hot, painful eyes — sore throat
& great hoarseness. Spent most of
the day in bed or lounge, able to
do but little reading. In the
afternoon Dr. Gale came in to see
me — he must get me ready for the
wedding. Catherine & Edna Cook
took dinner with us & stayed over
night. Evtf. Geo. Smillie came
& took my place as escort to Annie
to the Berkeley Lyceum to see some
tableaux vivants got up to aid some
charity. I stayed at home and
nursed myself in front of the parlor
fire, finished reading Stanley's book.
Sue came in, (Walter bringing her from
train,) at 10.30 — I sat with her until
after 11 — then to bed, but not before
Annie came in — she sat & talked
with Sue until after midnight —
but at last she came to bed &
then I got to sleep.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 22. 1890.

Still rather cold & windy, but bright
most of the day. Got up feeling some
what better but not well enough
to go out. Gale favored me with
another call. I spent the day
loafing & with the children. I
slept part of the time. Evtf.
we had Walter & the bride elect
to dine with Sue & us. I felt
pretty well bunged up but was
treated leniently, being allowed
to go to the table in my wrapper
forming a marked contrast to Walter
in the impressiveness of a dress
suit. The diners left early
& we got to bed before 11.
