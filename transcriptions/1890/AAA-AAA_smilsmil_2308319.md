## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 21. 1890.

Cloudy & windy until late in the afternoon — growing colder —
Ev'g. brilliant moonlight — very still. Seven men at work raising
frame to new barn — rapid progress.
I spent some time with the men — then to work taking [illegible]
from dry — [illegible] "Pansies" — In the
afternoon I again worked, printing
until nearly 5 — Then cleaned
up & with Ralph, went down town
meeting Annie on her way up. I
got my overcoat (repaired) from Ma-
chimney — Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g
Read newspaper — wrote note to
H. Jay Smith & to Equitable Life
Ass'n. 11 o'clk when we got to [illegible]

Letter from Miss Hallowell offering
$200 for "A bit of Brook with Sheep" — I
accepted by telegraph —
Gretta ret'd. from her visit home,

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 22. 1890.

Thur. fell to 30°. last night.

A wonderfully beautiful day — clear,
quiet & very warm in the sunshine.
I was out of doors all day working at
the vines, Pansy beds & other things
requiring attention. Before ev'g
the southern & western sky was
overcast & there were signs of a
coming change in the weather —
The men got the roof of the barn
up & the shingle boards on & nearly
all of the siding. Ev'g. after
my newspaper I spent some time
picking over India paper, preparation
of more proofing, while Annie read
aloud — Nearly 11 when we got
to bed.
