## Left Page

### July, Friday 11. 1890

Glorious day, bright & fresh. I did not work in studio at all. Spent the m'f out of doors. Parke came up at about 11 o'clk. & I rode down town with him — among other places went to D. Richardson's & consulted him about Ralph. He says that a surgical operation of the nature of circumcision will, sooner or later have to be performed. Home at 12. Dinner at 1 — Jenie spent the afternoon with Greer Parke having — At 3 o'clk — we went riding — went to Brooklyn & So. Pond — home at 6.30 — Tea. Wef, Mrs. Dant, Sayre & Mrs. Edgar (Jessie Comstock) called — I wrote a short note to Charl. & at 9.30 took it & a letter from Inely to P.O. While down town went in to a 10¢ Variety Show in a tent — much strengthened mentally — home soon after 10 & to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 12. 1890

Another delightful day — worked out of doors until 11 — then in to studio & planned for awhile over another sketch, possibly to be used for one of the aqua-tint plates for W. S. R. Koehler — my head bothering me, I did not accomplish much. Dinner at 1 — After that sat under the big maples & made a charcoal sketch while Anne read aloud to me. Parke having borrowed our hay rigging we got in our little crop using our light wagon. Heinck & Oscar worked until 7 o'clk. I worked at various chores until 9 o'clk. Then read newspapers until 10.
