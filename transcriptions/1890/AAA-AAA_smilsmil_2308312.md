## Left Page

### October, Sunday 5, 1890

Dull, leaden sky in the early morning
that gradually cleared into very
pleasant weather. A late breakfast
— it was 10.45 when Jim, Ralph &
I went down to old Mr. Parks' where
we found that he had got back with
Mrs. P. at 5 o'clk. His wife had
just gone to church — which
I thought very enterprising, under
the circumstances. I immediately
ret'd with Ralph & went to his garden
& filled a large basket with vegetables
— corn, string beans — yellow tomatoes —
celery & lettuce — & sent it to P— by
Henick. Out of doors until dinner
at near 2. Later, I looked over
some paper preparatory to printing
to-morrow. At 5.30 Annie, the boys
& I went to the Parks & saw them for
a few minutes — Home — tea at 6.30
Ev'g. napping & reading and
to bed early.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 6, 1890

A nasty day. Rain beginning about 10
a.m. & falling at intervals through the
day and very heavily at night. Hill
& Judson came up & I paid their bills.
They cut down two wild cherry trees near
the roadway. Appleton Stone worked for
awhile & so did Coyle — also Hotchkiss.
I put in a good day's work at my
printing. I experimented with depicting
& took a number of proofs from my
dry-point plates: "A Bunch of Pansies"
"Single Dark Hollyhocks". I did not
get through until near dark. Then
I rested for awhile before going down
town — tea after 9.30 Ev'g, after
reading part of the newspapers I was
for some time at work, picking over
India for to-morrow's printing. To bed
at 10.
