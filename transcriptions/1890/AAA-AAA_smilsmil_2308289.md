## Left Page

### August, Monday 18, 1890

Heavy fog in the early m't.
with threats of an unpleasant
day but it cleared charmingly.
After 10 o'clk, where I got in
from the garden & went to work
on aqua-tint. I burnished &
took two p'ts & burnished again.
Dinner at 1, more burnishing
& another proof — At 4 went
out riding — drove to Salt-
Spring to get some dark, single
Hollyhocks. I want to start
a dry-point 5"×7" to-morrow —
Home at 6.45. Oscar shot the
8th woodchuck that has been killed
on the place this summer — Tea.
Eve'g wrote to Mary, took it
to P.O. before 10 — Uncle W. walked
down with me — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 19, 1890

Heavy cloudy m'ts with wind from
the east. Before noon rain began
to fall — in the afternoon very violent
rain with some thunder — wind chang'd
to the west — Showers continued thro'
the eve'g. In garden until 10.30
then to studio — after 11 before I got
started upon a 5"×7" dry-point
after the dark hollyhocks that I got
yesterday — Stuck to it until dinner
& again in the afternoon — laid in
two large flowers — Eve'g, wrote
a note to S. R. Koehler. Then Uncle
W. read aloud to us for a time —
Heavy showers at night. To bed
at a little after 10.

Tremendous windstorm at
Wilkesbarre this afternoon about
5 o'clk, destroying many houses
& killing men & animals.
