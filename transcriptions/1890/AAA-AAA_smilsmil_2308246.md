## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 20, 1890

Cloud + sunshine in the early m'g with some heavy showers between 9 + 12 —
afternoon, the weather cleared charmingly.
Up at 6.30 — had a salt water bath + a
good rubbing — back to bed at 7 —
had a nap + up for all day soon after
8 — bkft. before 9 — after that, got out
my water-colors + worked until near
1 making a sketch of the old wind
mills that I caught yesterday. Dinner
before 2 — Then finished sketches +
about 4 Annie + I walked away
up the beach + back after 6, having
had a good walk. — Rested — Tea
after 7 — Eve'g mail, after 8, at last
brought us letters — from home — from
Kate + from W. Koehler — I wrote
a note or two + we got to bed
at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 21, 1890

*Steamboat "Lady of the Lake" — state room 27*

Glorious day but quite cool with N.E.
breeze — Bkfst. after 8 + at 9.30 Annie + I
started out with horse + buck board for the
old wind mills — nasty, shying beast; nearly
got away from me after as nearly ditching
me by a saw mill — Before we got to the old
mills on the beach we found one in active
operation, more inland, so we stopped there
+ after nearly two hours I got a sort of a
water color sketch of it — the miller was
a very nice old man + we climbed into
the mill with him. Back to hotel by way
of the beach — a delicious, exhilarating ride
in at 12.30 — paid bill $51.50 whew! Dinner
at 1 — + at 2 took train for Norfolk —
got there about 3 — transferred to Clyde's
wharf — It was about 4.30 before the
steamboat started — got to Fortress
Monroe before 6.30 — there for 15 or 20 m.
then on — had supper — a pleasant
ground swell — a calm sunset and
as it was too fresh to sit outside and
nothing to interest inside, we went
to bed at about 8 o'clk.
