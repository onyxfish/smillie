## Left Page

### October, Monday 13, 1890

Some feeble sunshine in the m't, gradually lost in laden clouds with niggling rain about 4.30. Household duties kept me from my studio until near 11. Tarbell called to see if I would sell Selim & Sam to a Binghamton undertaker. I printed seven pks from etching 5"×7" before dinn, after that with the men & working among berry vines until 4 o'clk when we went out for a ride in spite of threatening weather. It rained before we returned at 5.30 but that did not incommode us & there was much to see that was very charming. Tea at 6.30. Eve'g, newspapers — picked over some printing paper & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 14, 1890

Heavy fog which developed into rain at about 10 a.m., continuing until about noon — then commenced clearing. Two or three showers during the afternoon, one with considerable fine hail — temperature very moderate. I got to work at my printing at about 10.30 & worked until near dinner — a little more printing in the afternoon. Then spent some time remounting upon the original backer a fine, early india proof of Castlear's engraving after Huntington's "Sibyl". Failed in the first effort & thought that I had hopelessly ruined the proof, but I managed to lift it again & after cutting the india margin entirely away, much to the injury of the proof, I mounted it very successfully. Plants came from Little's & asparagus from Bridgeman's. Ab't went to Collis — they down town — tea at 6.30. Eve'g, newspapers until 9.
