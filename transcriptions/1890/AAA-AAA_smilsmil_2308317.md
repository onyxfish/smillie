## Left Page

### October, Friday 17, 1890

Brilliant day with wind blowing a half gale from the N. W. Deuel,
the man of Rush, from whom I have
got the lumber for new barn, called
this m'f & as Heinck was not on
hand to verify his bill I paid him
$50 on acc't. It was nearly noon
before I got started upon printing
& took but 4 p.f. before dinner at
1.30. Most of the afternoon was
given to daubing p.f., getting them
into press & mounting the last
of the two sets for W. Q. Parke. At
5 Annie, the boys & I, went down
Town & had a walk to the lower end.
It was a superb Oct. sunset, with
fierce clouds & powerful effects of
color & light & shade. Home at 6.
Eve'g, Annie read aloud from
a book on Bacteria. To bed
after 10. No word from the
home-coming ocean travellers,

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 18, 1890

A superb, brilliant day — overcast before
sunset — heavy showers before 10 o'clk. p.m.
After my garden work I spent the m'g
at my printing table, taking p.f. from
soft-ground etching. Parke & his wife
& Mr. Dessauer came in for a while.
Dinner was delayed until near
2 o'clk. so that I might finish my
work — It was nearly 3.30 when
we got started upon our drive. We
went to the Parke's & took W. & Mrs. P—
& baby Helen out with us — back at
5.30. Eve'g, I spent some
time arranging flower seeds
while Annie read aloud — To bed
at 11 o'clk.

At 12.30 we rec'd a telegram from
Chare announcing the safe arrival
of his party at Hoboken & by the m'g
mail we rec'd a last letter from
Kate, from Ventnor, Isle of Wight.
