## Left Page

### August, Sunday 24. 1890.

Ther. fell to 44½° last night.

Clouded — milder than yesterday —
beautiful moonlight night. Up
in the n.f. + made fine fires in
the large room + in the U. E. room
the n.f. being very sharp — as
usual, after bkfs. I went to the
garden + was there until after 11
Uncle W., Aunt A., + Annie went to
the Episcopal Church — I kept
about out of doors until dinner
time — + again after dinner —
At 4 o'clk we all went off + had
a ride to give the horses needed
exercise + get a breezing ourselves.
Back at 6.25 — then tea + Eve'g
Uncle W. went down to the Presb.
Ch. alone. I had many little things
to attend to + was then nearly over-
come with sleep — Annie read aloud
from Stanley's "Darkest Africa" — we
got to bed before the clock struck
10.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 25. 1890.

milder — hot in the sunshine, clouded
afternoon + eve'g. At 10 o'clk Azur
Lathrop sent a team for me + I rode
down to Mc. Collum + Smith's office where
I made a note for $1325 + A. L. endorsed
it. I then took it to the bank + got a
cashier's check — then again to W. C. +
Smith's + paid for the Lazier house + lot.
Then to the Recorder's Office + left the deed
to be recorded — Home at 11.30 + took four
[illegible] — fine aqua-tint plate before dinner
after dinner superintended the ploughing
of a bit of ground near N. E. gate for a new
strawberry bed. Then more printing —
at 4.15 we all went out riding — back
at 6.30 — tea — Eve'g., chores until
many — Annie wrote a long letter
to Kate + at 10 I went to P.O. with it —
to bed before 11.
