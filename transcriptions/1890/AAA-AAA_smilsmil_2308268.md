## Left Page

### July, Monday 7, 1890

*Geo. + Valentine Rel? only.*

Very hot day — help after 8 — & at about 9 15 Geo. & Valentine left for New-Milford + N.Y. Herrick took them to N.M. with our team. I worked out of doors until near 11. then to my etching room & began a series of experiments looking to getting an outline in aqua-tint. After a number of trials in different methods I found that by laying a thin white wax ground — drawing my outline broadly through it with a burnisher & then laying an aqua-tint ground over that I could bite the outlined matter with an aqua-tint grain — went down town in the early evg. So warm that we sat out on piazza in the evg. Some of the Chinese lanterns lighted — I had an attack of nausea & pain & went to bed at about 9.30.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 8, 1890

Exceedingly hot day with a strong southerly breeze that grew stronger as the day advanced — Well overcast at the N.W. with much lightning. It was about 10.30 when I went into studio & began to lay out a sketch 8"×13" of Old Windmills on Va. coast" from which to make an aqua-tint 5"×7" for J. R. Koshler. The day was so hot that I worked very feebly. Dinner at 1 — In the afternoon, finding it cooler in the etching-room, I worked there, laying etching grounds & experimenting with aqua-tint on 5"×7" plates — Made charcoal sketch 8"×13" of "Old house at Boulogne" — Tea after 6. Then went down-town, Ralph & Jim accompanying me. To-day is Ralph's 3° Birthday — it was appropriately celebrated at the tea table. In spite of the wind it was so warm that we sat out on the piazza until nearly 10 — Then a storm of wind & rain struck us.

Barrett Bros. piano came to-day.
