## Left Page

### December, Sunday 28, 1890

Bright, cold & windy. Up late.
Skp. at 9.30 — Annie & I read over the proof of my "observations
in the matter of Etching making corrections. At 12.30 Ralph & I
went up to Dr. Gale's — saw him for a few minutes — left pf. of "Observations"
with him — Dinner at 1.30 — Annie took Jim & Ralph down to All Souls
Church to a children's Xmas service & I stayed at home & shovelled snow
in the back yard. Char came in & spent part of afternoon with Mary —
her gives. Tea after 6. Geo. and Nellie came in for a while in
the early eve'g. Before 9 I went down to studio — got pf. of "Smiths Daughter" & [illegible] Mezzatint
& took them home shortly after 10 — sat awhile & read & to bed [illegible].

## Right Page

### December, Monday 29, 1890

[illegible] fall of snow in the morning — clearing before noon into a nice, pleasant day. Soon after 9 I went to Jo. Pier's at Morgan's house with pf. of Etching & mezzatint. He was to decide which of two photos showed be offered — one giving the whole painting, the other but the head — also which method of reproduction he preferred — He decided to have the large head etched. I got to studio before 10 & set to work house cleaning — Dr. Gale came in & spent some time with me editing my Etching "Observations" — before he left Dr. Gester came in bringing a copy of his book for me. Home at 1 — back at 2 — I had a sitting from Geo. commencing a dry-point 5×7 plate likeness. At 1 went to Kurtz's gallery & left order for 12 copies of photo for Mr. Morgan — then to Canal St. — took to W. Voigh a pf. of [illegible] Mezzatint to him — walked up home. Return'd in at 6.30. Ralph, after complaining again, fell asleep before eating his supper. Gale has made an appointment to operate upon his [illegible] is next Monday — I am very unhappy in view of it — Dinner at 6.30. Eve'g. Read for awhile. To bed at 10.30.
