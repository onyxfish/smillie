## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 11. 1890.

more delightful weather with a procession of thunder-
storms after 3 o'clk p.m. Most
of the m[illegible] was spent about
the place — Before dinner
wrote to Charl & part of a note
to Geo — & then again, I took
a nap — dinner about 1 &
after that finished my letters.
Some work about the flower
beds, then the thunderstorms
were so imminent that we
gave up our ride — I went
down town at 4.30 — back in
about an hour — Tea after 6.
A good part of the eveg spent
trimming lamps & getting new
wicks into them — To bed about
10.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 12. 1890.

Up at 7 — overcast m[illegible] — close & muggy —
a breeze blowing that it was pleasant
to be in — Some sunshine between 10
& 3 o'clk — Heavy showers (thunder) bet.
5 & 6 followed by continuous & heavy
rain. B[illegible] — about 8 — most of the m[illegible]
working about the flower & veg[etable] beds.
At 11.30 went down town — back at 12.30
& rested until dinner, soon after 1 —
Annie read aloud for a short time
after dinner — At 3, horses at the door
& A., the children & I rode until
5 — getting just before the rain
came — Freight boxes from N.Y.
am[?] — I was unpacking until tea
time & after until about 8.30 —
Then Annie read aloud to me from
"Scribner's" an article by H.M. Stanley
until I was almost asleep — Then,
about 10, to bed, Ralph with us in
our room & Jim "going it alone".
