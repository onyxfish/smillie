### October, Wednesday 1, 1890

A very lovely, mild day — after my [illegible]
garden duties I went to etching some
& spent some time in dampoing [illegible]
paper [illegible] & putting them in press.
Then I got to work proving & took
6 [illegible] [illegible] & two large paper, india
[illegible] of soft ground etching, spoiling
the last [illegible]. Dinner at 1.30 — before we
left the tables the horses were at the
door & at about 2.30 we started for
a ride over to Salt Spring — back by
a new road over to the old Silver Lake
road. Home at 5.30 — Herrick
went to Rush with Loomis & train & got
a load of boards. Eliz., Annie & [illegible]
dressed ourselves & at 6.30 went to
Mr. Jeff. Griffith's to tea — a party of
35 or so — passed an agreeable
eve[ning] — pleasant moonlight walk
home at 10.30 & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 2, 1890

*To Scranton & back.*

Up at 5.30 — Cloudy day with a few
drops of rain — B[reakfast] at 6.15 & Eliz.
Annie & I left the house a little before
7 & drove at once to D.L.&W. R.R. depot.
Left the horses at hotel — 8.21 train to
Scranton — Parker met us at depot there —
drove first to Fritz's where I ro[de] at saddle
for Jim's donkey — then to P's new house
where we spent some time going all over
it. Then we took a short ride about town
& to depot at 12 — there we had a good
dinner — left Eliz. & P. to go on to Wt. by a
later train & we ret[urned] to Montrose by 12.44
train — Young Mr. Woodin (Anna Jessup)
came up with us. We rode over from
the station by a new road — one further to
the South — a mile or two longer — but a very
pleasant change. Home before 5 — feeling
very tired — Had a nap on lounge before
tea — even[ing]. Loafed with newspaper
until 9 — to bed at 9.30

Heavy rain at 9 p.m. & through
the night.
