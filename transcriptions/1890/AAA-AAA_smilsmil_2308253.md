## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 3, 1890

*At M. S. Beach's. Peekskill.*

Pleasant day, altho' uncertain as to weather — A smart shower about one o'clk — after which it cleared. Annie & I took 9°° train to Peekskill, getting there about 10.30. Ella Beach met us with carriage. We were then introduced to Mr. Ellis who had come on the train with us & who spent the rest of the day with us — drove at once to the house — a charming one & met W. & Mrs. B— Emma & Violet. The Rhododendrons & Azalias were a sight to see — we tramped & rode all over the place — dinner about 1 — In the afternoon it was cool enough to make a wood fire on the hearth (plus out.) I walked about the place — then, enveloped in wraps we sat on the tiled piazza — Tea at 6 — after that I drove about the grounds, first with Annie — then with Emma. Then gathered about the open fire & chatted until 8 — then we were driven to depot & took 8.30 train, getting into the city at 10 o'clk — having Mrs. Ellis with us — we took her to 5th Ave. bus — then home & to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 4, 1890

A very hot day. Ther. got up to 87°. After attending to matters at home I got to studio about 9.30 & spent the mid in getting pictures for summer & fall exhibits ready & in putting things away. Home at 1 & back at 2.30 — Annie dined with the Kimballs. Afternoon I was getting curtains down & things ready to pack until 6 o'clk. Then home — dinner bet. 6.30 & 7 — Emma J. & Edw & many dined with us — all intending to go to Thomas Concert in the eve'g but while at dinner a thunder storm came up & rain continued all the eve'g. We had a pleas. and chatting time in the twilight of the Reception Room until 10 o'clk. A messenger boy escorted Emma home & we went to bed before 11.
