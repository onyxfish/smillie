## Left Page

### September, Thursday 11, 1890

A nasty day — threats of rain in the m't, but none fell. The men got to work at 1 o'clk. & at 3 were driven off by heavy rain which continued most of the rest of the day. Wind persistently N.E. & not cold. It was 11 before I got to Etching Room. Made drawing for another soft ground etching — got it into Dutch Mordant where it lay for two hours & came out a failure, being very unevenly bitten for some cause beyond my divining. Miss McCartney called to see me about a couple of portfolios that Parke wishes her to make to hold Etching [illegible] of mine to be given as wedding presents. After dinner, I was with the men until they were driven off by the rain — a new man commenced work playing wall — Then I wrote a letter to Koehler & at 4.30 took it to the P.O. Then went to Parke's for [illegible] — there for some time with P— Home at 6. Eve'g. Annie read aloud from Stanley in Africa. To bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 12, 1890

Another stormy day — some light rain in the m't. After 3.30, showers almost continuous. Hotchkiss laid wall from 8.30 until about 4. Hilf, Judson & two men who laid wall worked from 1 to 4. I got to my work in studio at 10.30 — made another experimental soft ground drawing & got it ready for the acid. I also commenced a small dry-point after Pansies for experimental printing, to save the 5×7 dry-point — In the afternoon I worked for a time on graver "pansies." After tea I went to P.O. Eve'g after reading newspaper I spent some time in Etching Room preparing to print to-morrow. For awhile Annie read aloud from Stanley. To bed before 11 — The day has been very mild.
