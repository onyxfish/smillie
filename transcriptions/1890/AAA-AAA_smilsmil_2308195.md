## Left Page

### February, Friday 7, 1890

Disagreeable day — dull, raw &
cold — Spent the day working on
a Pastel sketch — a variation from
an oil sketch that I painted last
spring — "Haglebrook". It was about
1.45 when I got home to lunch —
Acssie in Brooklyn at her Unitarian
Woman's League. I got back to studio
before 3 & managed to finish my
sketch before dark — Home at about
6\. Dinner at 6.30 — Catherine & Edna
again with us over night — Even
before 8, Mary Cook & Mrs. Carl Sprague
came for Annie — I took them over to the
met. Opera H° where they had secured
seats for Gatterdannerung — there, in a
snow flurry I walked down to Coleman's
apartments in 25th S. — Mary & Belle Lundy
there — a very pleasant chatty evnf. — At
10 o'clk. C— am from Boston — then I
went to studio — lighted up — my pastel
sketch shows horribly by gas light — swore
off from pastels — Home in a nasty
sleet storm & to bed before 11 — Sleep
followed by heavy rains — Annie & the
others got out of Opera after 12 & were able
to hire a couké that brought the three of
them home in safety & but little wet

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 8, 1890

Beastly day — pouring rain all
day — Got to studio after 10 — Having
made up my mind that I would
do no more Pastel work, after I had
stretched a new canvas 14×21 — I
cleaned up — re-arranged Champney's
Pastel boxes & took them all in to
him & gave him the Pastel canvas
that I had — Home to lunch at 1
— back at 2.30 — Saml. Coleman came
in for a while — I accomplished nothing.
Home at 6 — found things in some dis-
order arising from Kate, the Cook's, illness.
Dr. Killens came to see her & put her
to bed — some old troubles, complicated
by cold. Dinner a little after time.
Evnfg. Rains had ceased — Annie
& I went to Phil. Rehearsal concert
which we found very enjoyable —
After it, home & to bed at 11.
