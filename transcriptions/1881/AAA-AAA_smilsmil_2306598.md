## Left Page

### July, Friday 1, 1881.

Clear, fresh & breezy — Bkfs
at 8.30 — After some messages
up town — Rode to Alexander's
bindery, 10 Barton R. then to
Notris' in greave S? for brass —
then to Oakey's in 14 S. all
about binding for Osgood book
about 12 to studio & finally
finished up there — then home
stopping on my way at Mr.
Jacobs' Had dinner about 2 —
Kitty Ireland called — Q. &
I packed our trunks and
had them ready before 7 —
Slight dash of a thunder
shower. Our trunks taken
away about 7.30 — feel that
we are at last started for
Montrose — another very
quiet evening — to bed at
10 —

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 2, 1881.

*montrose*

Delightful day — Up soon after
6 — Bkfs at 7 — Foot of Chris. S. at
8 — off on 8.20 train — seats in Parlor
car "Delaware" — Heavy train &
crowded — First part of the ride
very comfortable — cool & without
dust — a change for the worse
after passing the Water Gap —
hot & dusty — delayed by a
"hot boy" — ate lunch before
10 o'clk — not enough of it — got to
Scranton at 3, an hour or more late —
had more lunch — got to New-
milford about 5 — horses & buggy
waiting for me — had a pleasant
drive over to Montrose getting
in at 6. The Courtwright's ready
for us — tea at 6.30 — a little
walk after — M. J. T. gone to depot
for A.V.V. Terrible news from
Washington — attempted assassination of Pres. Garfield —
To bed at 9.30 with a severe
headache.
