## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 16. 1881.

Charming day — after bkfst. Rode down to Adolph Saks's — 47. 5. S. to see about an extra Phil. seat — then over to Dewey & Keating's & left my overcoat to be mended — then to Harris's to look at some rugs for sale there in to look at the Falis collection — three to studio, getting there about 11.30 — at 12 — young man from Tiffany's came in for instructions about drawings & plans for Montrose house — then Davis of Portland came in — then home to dinner — most of p.m. at studio spent writing a letter with plans to Cotrell — Home at 6 — tea near 7. at 8 went to N.A. of D. Water Color Society meeting — back home before 10 — unpacked box rec'd from Montrose — books & things — to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### November, Thursday 17. 1881.

Lovely day. mild & clear. The mf was absent at the studio getting ready for work — Mr. & Mrs. Ehninger called for a moment — that is, Mr. E. came as far as Mrs. Polpe's room & there I went to meet her — Dinner at — back before 3 — the whole afternoon given to callers & smaller matters — home before 6. Emma & grace & fanny to tea — after that took all the girls to Singing club — then went to aunt Anna's — from her got a note of introduction to Mrs. Theodore Thomas — there to her house — 108 E. 17 S. — & had a very pleasant call — my object being to get possession, if possible, of the Phil. seat on my right — I find that [illegible] — f 34 & 5. holds it — Mrs. J. gave me her card introducing me to him — at 9.40 to studio — wrote a note to Koehler — at 10.15 to the club. took Annie home & to bed at [illegible]
