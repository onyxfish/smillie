## Left Page

### October, Sunday 23, 1881

Pleasant day. Unable to get out of my bed. Great pain in my head & discomfort all over. Fearing some further trouble I sent for Dr. Vail who came & pronounced it the effect of cold & nervous fretting over the building operations. I was able to take a little breakfast & dinner but could eat nothing later. Did not get up in the evening & passed a restless night.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 24, 1881

Bleak, disagreeable m'f, not able to get out of my bed — greatest discomfort, a pain in my head. About 11 o'clk. the bricklayer, Dillon, brought over for me by Cottrell, came to my bedside for instructions. I got up in time for dinner but did not feel able. In the course of the afternoon I bundled up & with A. went over to the house & saw our chimney started. Then back & prepared some grumm which I had for tea. Eve'g I persisted in sitting up & watched A. & Flora. Cortright & his wife play whist. —
To bed at 10 & had a miserable night.
