## Left Page

### January, Friday 28, 1881

About an inch of snow fell during the night, improving
somewhat the appearance of the exceedingly dirty streets.
Got to studio at 10 o'clk + spent some time in looking
over old canvases upon which I had pictures in different stages
of progress — at last determined to paint a little lake [illegible] subject
"Approach to the Huddle through the woods". To lunch at 1.30
After that outlined subject on canvas so as to begin the painting
to-morrow — Also went to Riding school with [illegible] Tea in
studio — Then called on Father + Mother — Dined there — and
at 8.30 to Annie's — There until 10.30 — Emma D. there. — To 37th
St. + to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 29, 1881

Clear pleasant day — not so cold. Got to Studio at 9.30
+ began to prepare to paint one picture 1" Approach to the
Huddle. Lake George". but so many callers came that I did
not get a chance to begin to paint until after 12 . Mr. Condct
came + commissioned me to buy a Bridgeman "Lady of Cairo visiting"
After lunch at 2 I went to Moore's
Gallery, saw Bridgeman, but as Moore
was out could not come to terms with
him — back to Studio + painted until
near 4 — then again to Moore's, just in time
to secure the picture as Mr. — was selling
it to another — then to foot of Liberty St.
met Annie + by 5.15 Train went to Roselle
tea at Lib Howe's, meeting Miss Shumway of
Chicago — [illegible] — Mill + Fanny K. — Miss Perry and
Miss Nutting also there — After a pleasant
evef'g, left by 9.42 Train — getting
to 37th St. at 11.20 — Emma D. just in
from seeing "Olivette" — after listening to
some of her descriptions, to 37th St. and
to bed at midnight.
