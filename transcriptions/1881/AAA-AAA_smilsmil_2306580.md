## Left Page

### May, Thursday 26, 1881

Charming day — Until 10 when I got to studio — To work on etching — Taking a pl — then working on some part, then taking another proof — Home to dinner at 1.30 — back at 3 — worked until 5.30 — then Connie came for me and we walked over to Madison Sq. & took a look at the [illegible] Statue — like the statues but think the base an unsuccessful attempt at novelty — At 6.30 walked up home — Tea about 7 — John Valentine there. — [illegible]. Walter came with a sheet of drawings & plans for Montrose house — about 11 when we got to bed —

## Right Page

### May, Friday 27, 1881

Another clear day & warmer. In the m[orning]. mailed a letter from Connie to Mary Willard enclosing check for $500 — to be expended by M.W. during the coming summer in giving recreation to needy female school teachers. Got to studio before 10 & to work on etching — took out the figure in the bay window — laid a ground over the plate & fussed and etched over parts — To dinner at 1.30 — back at 3 — worked over etching — met paper for [illegible] tomorrow & home about 6.30 — [illegible], as it was very warm spent lounging on sofa in dimly lighted parlor. To bed at 10.30

Geo. Valentine, Walter, wife & child started this p.m. on a fishing exc. to be gone about a week — Bushkill.
