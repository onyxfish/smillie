## Left Page

### November, Thursday 24, 1881

*Thanksgiving Day. at home — New York.*

Overcast mng. but afternoon
clean & cold — Had bkfst. about
9 o'clk — a jolly table full —
Father & Mother had already
breakfasted — Most of the mng.
was spent with Neil, a photographer,
who took views of the
Reception Room & parlor. At
about 2 o'clk, we sat down to
dinner — Father, Mother — Mary
& [illegible] Geo. & Nellie — Matt & El. Kate
& Charl — Annie & I — Will, Joe
& Minn — the family without
a break — much to be thankful
for. About 4 I went out alone
for a walk — about 4 in. of snow
— being well shod it was very
enjoyable — & soon after sun-
set — had a light tea at 8 —
sat in parlor until 10 —
& at 10, go to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 25, 1881

Superb, clear & cold — Up at 7 —
Bkfst. at 8. Telephoned to R.R. Station
& found that S. Louis 4p. was on time
then ordered a carriage and in
15 m. it was at the door & Geo., Nellie
Annie, Charl & I were in it & off —
fortunate in getting Drawing Room
car seats — Mr. Nevins on the train —
Got in to N.Y. depot at 10.35 — then
to market with Annie — then home
& at about 11.30 got to studio — wrote
letter to Cottrell enclosing check
Home to dinner soon after 1 —
Had a prophetic feeling in my
stomach that was very depressing
— on bed for an hour — then to studio
— more letter writing — cleared my
easel to get to work — home to
tea at 6. A's Singing Club
out as she did not feel very
well I prevailed upon her
not to go out. Some reading
aloud & some writing —
To bed soon after 10.
