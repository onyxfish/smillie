## Left Page

### October, Friday 7, 1881

Much milder & a delightful day. Bkf. at 9 & as soon after that as we could get ready we started off for a drive in an open carriage, behind Mt. Shepherd's black ponies that he sent down for us — we enjoyed the ride immensely, going out to the N.W. from Saratoga, through Hilkins Park. Lunch at 1 — Afternoon in studio with E — interested in some etching & sketching matters — had a nap —
Dinner at 6 — Evening in parlor in front of a beautiful wood fire — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 8, 1881

Delicious mild mf. After Bkf. E. & I got away in a buggy with our sketching traps at about 10.15 — we drove out on Greenbush ave — to N.W. until we came to some old ice houses by a pond & a picturesque old mill — here we unhitched our horse & pitched our tents & pitched into work. I made my first experimental out-door sketch with tube water colors & was fairly pleased with the result. Lunch at 1.30 — a shower suddenly came up — we hitched up our horse & got to E's, a little moist, soon after 2 — laid an etching ground — then worked a little on my sketch & at about 4.30 I stretched myself on the bed — at about 6 was horribly sick — was relieved by a copious movement of the bowels — could not go down to tea at 7 — Mr. & Mrs. Jos. Drexel — Col. & Mrs. Baker & their sons Guy & Willie were there by invitation — about 8.30 I was able to present myself — Mr. & Mrs. D. left soon after — Mrs. B. sang very pleasantly for us until about 11 — when they left to take train for their home at Balleston about midnight — E. & I got to bed after 1, before I got to sleep.
