## Left Page

### September, Monday 5, 1881

another day of pitiless heat —
The m[orning] quite cool with promise
of rain — but later as hot & dry
as ever. The m[orning] spent at studio
drawing plan for floor beams
of first floor — To P.O. at 12.30 —
Dinner at 1 — The afternoon so hot that I really felt sick
& did little else than lie about
under the maples — I finished
reading Hammer Ford's Hand[book] —
— Tea about 6.30 — Then to P.O.
when I got back. Mr. & Mrs. [illegible] Bent
made us a call — later we went
to Aunt H's & bid Mr. & Mrs. Taylor
& Mrs. Barlow good bye — They leave
tomorrow m[orning]. Then again to
the Sayre [illegible] & [illegible]
in the moonlight until after
10 o'clk — Then, at about 11, to
bed —

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 6, 1881

Hot & dry — after[noon] at 7.30 —
spent some time in hammock
under the trees — nearly 10 before
I could summon courage to
go on with my work. Then to
studio & after conferring with the
carpenter's [illegible] at my Hammerton
Handbook Review — finished the
reading & making notes & commenced
writing — To dinner at 1 — about
when I returned — finished
review — Tea at 6 — under the
trees for an hour. Then to P.O. &
other messages — Paid Jane Williams
Back to Sayre's & [spent] the even[ing]
in hammock under the trees —
To bed at 10.30 — after the
hottest day that ever knew
in Montrose.
