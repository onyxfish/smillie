## Left Page

### November, Thursday 10, 1881

Bright, breezy day + cold again all day out of doors superintending work, except for a short time when I was drawing a plan for finish of large South Gable. Dinner at 1 — Jno. C. — Ret? from Wilkesbarre — The p.m. went as the mf did, much of the time being spent with Dillon at his work on the chimney — tea at 6. Nett. Jno C — Ella + I went to Azurd's where we got Florence + went to the Methodist Ch. where Alfred J. Knight's Lyceum combination amused us for awhile — After'd we went to Mrs. Crossman's — there met Lillie + May Jessups + Mr. Bethwick — they all had oysters — of course I didn't.) Then took Florence home + to bed about midnight.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 11, 1881

Still, clear but in the afternoon giving such indications of another coming storm that I gave up in discouragement + determined if it came to start for home to-morrow. The day was spent as usual with the men at their work in despair of finding a place in the progress of the work where it would be safe to leave them + go to N.Y. Dinner at 1 — afternoon out of doors — tea at 6 — Nett sent a note to Mr. Danl. Sayre excusing myself — invited there to meet Rev. Mr. Miller who has resigned his pastorate here. Then to work packing trunk + a box with Annie's books + some other things — Some snow + sleety rain fell soon after I went to bed, before 11 o'clk.
