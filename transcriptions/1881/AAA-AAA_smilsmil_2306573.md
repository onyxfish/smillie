## Left Page

### May, Thursday 12. 1881.

A most uncomfortably hot day —
Blcbt- about 9 — After that, in
Pavilion with the Wallers until
after 11 — then to our Room, where
until 2 o'clk. I worked upon
a drawing for A.B.N. Co — Dinner
at 2.30 — then more work and
Reading — too hot to go outside
until about 5, when we went
out to the wharf — about 6 me
Took a wagon & rode to the Soldiers
home & went through that institu-
tion being much interested. Back at
7.30 by moonlight — then had tea,
Evening until after 9 in Pavilion
listening to the band, watching
people & the oil-like water and
moonlight — then strolled about
awhile & finally to bed about
11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 13. 1881.

Very hot morning — Blcbt-
som after 9 — At 10.30 me rode
over to the Hampton School. —
there unexpectedly met Elbert
Munroe. — Introduced to Genl
Armstrong who directed some of
our movements. Much interested
in the Indian & Negro Scholars & in
Miss Mackaye's class in Algebra.
At 12 went to the dining Room &
heard the Scholars sing grace
before beginning their meal. Then
a ride through miserable Hampton.
Back to Hotel at 1.30 & had dinner.
After that as the breeze was good
went out & had an interesting sail
out to the Rip Raps. After that
as it had grown quite cool me
took a walk along the beach.
Tea at near 8 — most of evening
in Pavilion & walking outside
to bed before 11.
