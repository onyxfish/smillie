## Left Page

### February, Thursday 17, 1881

Charming day — Up early and spent 3/4 of an hour before bkfst
at Renner's superintending the shipment of Etchings for a
London Exhibt — 11 Etchers here sent 69 Etchings — Got to studio
before 10 — & to work painting on 33 × 44 — digging away at
the trees — Out to lunch at 2 & had a short walk, then
again at painting until 5 o'clk — then to 37th St — at 6
to Annie's & at 6.30 took Annie & Emma J. to Fanny K's
to tea — after that to Singing club at 40 W 32d — then to
Studio — wrote to Mr. Condit & to James Boureet, agent in London
to whom I have shipped the Etchings — At 10.30 again to
32d St. & took Annie & Emma home — to 37th St. & to bed at
11.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 18, 1881

Mild & partly overcast — Rain in the eveg. Got to Renner's
soon after 8 & saw two cases nearly packed with Etchings.
Then to bkfst — & got to studio about 9.45 — then to work at painting
on 33 × 44 & spent all the day working over the large cedar
on the left hand side. Lunch at 2 & a walk — my large
frame came to-day & is very becoming to my picture —
at 4 went to Riding School & had a ride — stopped at
Chave's for a few moments, then on to 37th St. & scrubbed — then
to Studio & at 7.44 had tea there alone. At 9 went to
Annie's where a half dozen friends had come to hear a
young man [illegible] play on the flute
(Welstein or some such name). He
was very clever — to 37th St. and
to bed about 11.30. Heavy Rain
