## Left Page

### August, Friday 26, 1881

Very hot & dry — Up at 7 o'clk —
an alarm from Aunt H — Uncle Wm
Taken ill during the night, at first
supposed to be a slight attack of
paralyses — later, it was thought
to be the result of an overdose of
a morphine medicine — all the
myf. interested in the construction
of a chestnut drains box, 1 ½ of Comp.
Thoroughly Farred inside & out —
Dinner at 1 — all the afternoon
out of doors superintending
work — Tea at 6 — everg.
pacing Piazza — very warm
— for a short time at W. J. J's —
He suffered primarily from a
severe indigestion — green corn —
stomach & bowels clogged — Strain
& consequent clot — slight
paralyses — Dr. Richardson is
not doing anything to Relieve
the blockade — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 27, 1881

Hotter & dryer than ever, so that
I had little spirit for work — I
spent most of the day out of doors
watching proceedings — Dinne
at 1 o'clk. Afternoon went as
the myf. did — no work except
standing about out of doors —
at 6 dressed & with a. went
to H+ Tyler's at 6.30 as invited.
Found that we were too early —
then I went to W. J. J's — Still
in the same semi-conscious
condition — then to P.O. — then
again to Tylers — Mr. & Mrs. Comstock —
Daubayne & wife — Dr. Schweiring & wife —
ms. mylest & Priscilla Pore there.
Tea about 7.30. About 8.30 we were
all invited to go to Sam Sayre's
where the young people were
having an entertainment — the
girls "a broom drill" — the boys, a
travestie upon Wm Tell — then some
Charades & dancing — left soon
after 10 — To bed at 11.
