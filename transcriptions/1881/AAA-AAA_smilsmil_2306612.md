## Left Page

### July, Friday 29, 1881

Thunderstorm in the nit coming from N.E. Lovely effects of clouds. Rain falling in veils & curtains all about us on the horizon. The men at work in the cellar interrupted by the rain — I got out my Osgood work & wrote some letters — Downtown as usual & dinner after 1 — In the afternoon again some light rain. My time was used up in looking after the men & making believe work. — Dressed before tea, at 6.30 — after that a & I went out calling — upon Mrs. Foster — Mrs. Mulford — Mr. De Witt & Gould and Dan Sayre — where Jessie Comstock had a young party under way — There until near 10 — then to our quarters & to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 30, 1881

Mild, overcast day — wind from N.E. After bkfst. a & I walked out towards the lake & gathered wild flowers with which we returned to the studio where I set to work upon a few sketches for head piece to Emerson's "The Apology" in the Osgood book — Down Foun'd noon — dinner at 1 — again to studio & finished pen sketch — Just before 6 went down to P.O. — Gentle, steady rain came on — The Parks had a pic-nic tea on the fair ground & urged us to join but we preferred a plain meal in a warm, dry room. The Rain persisted & so did the pic-nic. The girls did not come in until after 9 o'clk — Flora L. — Mrs. C. a & I played whist — a Rubber — a & I lost — To bed at 10.
