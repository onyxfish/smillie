## Left Page

### September, Friday 9, 1881

Cooler & overcast — Trouble
about measurements of floor
beams — After getting that
fixed — to work upon my
drawings — Spent the rest
of the mng at that — Dinner
at 1 — The afternoon spent
drawing & watching the
bedding of the floor & mud
sills — when they were all
down, learned that a free
circulation of air was needed
under them — To tea at 6,
after tea went down & saw Catrell
& explained my plans of laying
a course of brick & leaving air
spaces under the sill — all proved
& arrangements made accordingly.
Back to Sayre's — the rest of
the evng on piazza & to bed
som after 10.

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 10, 1881

Comparatively cool & partially
overcast most of the day —
much of the mng spent over-
looking the raising of the sills
& inserting a layer of bricks under,
Did some work on detail draw-
ings of stairs & bay window — To
P.O. with letter to Elluinger, then
to dinner at 1. Back to studio
at 2 — all the afternoon there
at work on my drawings and
bossing the setting up of timbers.
To P.O. just before 6 — then to
tea — about 8 Annie & I went
out & called on Aunt H. for a
little while — then to Sayre's
& to bed at 10.45.

In the afternoon Simon, the
well digger, raised a outcry that
he had struck water & he wanted
me to go down the well — I did so
& at a depth of about 27 ft. found
that he had struck a very small
stream of water — At this wonderful
dry season this is more than encouraging.
