## Left Page

### July, Friday 15, 1881.

Another delightful day. Warm but with refreshing breeze. Got to studio about 9.30 & with A's aid think I overcame the troubles of the Roof. Payne came abt 1 & mowed for an hour. Soon after 12 A. & I went down town as usual. Dinner soon after 1. To studio at 2.30 — a short swing in my hammock under the big maples, then to work on drawing. Worked until near tea time, then to the house. Tea soon after 6 — about 7.30 A. & I went down town to P.O., then to Methodist Ch., where a quintette of Yale boys now at Mrs. Jessup's gave a very entertaining concert. "Who is Geo. Washington", a very clever parody on the old English Madrigals amused us very much. After that to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 16. 1881.

Charming mff., breezy & slightly overcast, growing denser until about 2 o'clk. when some heavy showers came down quite spitefully. The mff. was spent as usual in the studio working on front elevation — Afternoon A. & I went down town. Dinner at 1.30. I went over to studio alone in the rain & worked until about 5. Then the showers had cleared away. I went to the house & A. & I had a walk before tea. In spite of cooling breezes we found it very warm. Tea late. Miss Payne of Wilkesbarre joined the family — we sat up on piazza until after 9. Then A. & I took a short walk & to bed at 10.
