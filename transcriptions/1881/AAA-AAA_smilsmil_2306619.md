## Left Page

### August, Friday 12, 1881

Very close hot [illegible] — much of the time overcast & looking as though showers might come — a light one did fall early in the [illegible]. The [illegible] passed without my accomplishing much — I got my board & paper out & commenced W. elevation of house. Also made a rough etching on one of the binders dies that I have. Dinner at 1 — again at studios & spent the afternoon there doing a little work. Tea at 6 — guest. Went to Mr. Park's and spent some time on door steps with friends, the Parks being gone to Montrose depot for friends — then to Gus Crandall's and found there Mr. & Mrs. Chd. Read & Ben Mulford, Miss Sheppard & another young lady — there until near 10 — then to our room & to bid — delicious moonlight night.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 13, 1881

Exceedingly hot [illegible] — but in the afternoon a pleasant change to much cooler weather took place — some of the [illegible] was spent with Kate Risley criticising her drawings — some of it drawing on W. elevation of house, the rest in consultation with Catnell drawing checks & superintending [illegible] laying in the cellar. Down to P.O. before dinner at 1 — after that again to studio & much of afternoon was spent in a tree top sawing out dead wood. About 5 Mr. Dodd & wife came — Mr. D. did not get out of her buggy. Tea at 6 — after that I went to P.O. & on my way back stopped in at the Court House until 8.15 — then to our quarters & read until 10 — then to bed.
