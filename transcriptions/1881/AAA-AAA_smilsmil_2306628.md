## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 30. 1881.

*Uncle Wm. Turrell died.*

Another fearfully hot, dry day — Part of the mtf. out of doors staking out trenches for mud sills — & part of mtf. finishing plans for basement — after 12 went to Uncle W's — found him still worse — wrote home — to P.O. with letter — then to Dr. Dimock's — He says Uncle Wm is dying — Telegraphed to Chas Gunther — then to dinner at 1.20 — after that lounged awhile — then to studio — the sun partially obscured — still at work for sills — I worked upon plans. Tea at 6 — after that to Uncle Wm's. He died quietly at about 6 o'clk. Found Aunt H. placid — Uncle A. arr'd from Lexington by way of Great Bend at about 8 o'clk. Evng with A. on piazza — to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 31. 1881.

Hotter than ever & dryer — After bkft. went to Aunt H's — then to my work — of which I did a little — did not go to P.O. at noon — too hot — Dinner at 1 — after that lounged awhile — then to studio & kept very quiet doing a little work on plans — Tea as usual at 6. Evng spent on upper piazza, the air breathless & horribly dry & hot — to bed soon after 10.
