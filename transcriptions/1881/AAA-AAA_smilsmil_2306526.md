## Left Page

### February, Thursday 3, 1881.

Still brilliant & very cold — another day of practically doing nothing — In the m'g wrote a letter of Sarah Fessenden — out to lunch at 1. 45 — & in the afternoon wrote a letter to Wm Cook about multiple gravers, white line in wood engraving, etc — at about 5 annie came in & we went to see Bridgeman's pictures — & 6 I took her home, then to 37' S'. & at 6.40 back and dined there, with the family, Mirwa. Set, Howe & Miss Shumway of Chicago. At 8. to Union League Theatre where the Choral Club under Mr. Cortado gave their first concert for the season — Annie, Mirwa & I drove back getting to 38' S'. at 10.30 — There until a little after 11 — then to 37' S'. & to bed

## Right Page

### February, Friday 4, 1881.

Delightful day, but cold — got to studio before 10 & spent the whole day shamefully doing nothing — I tried to work & tried to write but between disinclination and visitors I got nothing done — I went to lunch at 2. 00 & immediately on my return Misses Howe and Shumway called — also Wm J. Tunnell — just in from Montrose — at 4 I went with Coleman & had a ride on horseback — then my bath & to Studio soon after 7 — tea & at 8.30 at Annie's there until 10.30 — there to 37' S'. & to bed at 11.
