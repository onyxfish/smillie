## Left Page

### July, Sunday 17. 1881.

Cool day with high wind —
9 o'clk. when we had breakfast.
A. & I went to Episcopal Ch. &
sat through just part of service
but a strong draught of wind
was too much for both of us,
so, as we could not correct it
me got up & went out — went to
studio & read & wrote until
dinner time — Dinner at 2.30
— After that we again went to
the Studios & at about 6 o'clk. A.
& I wandered off over Warner's
lot, through the woods to the
road & out upon Brewster's hill —
then to P.O. & back to C's at 7.45
Then A. & I had tea alone — after
that until 10.30 in parlor chatting
— then to bed — a very cool
night.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 18. 1881.

An almost cold, lowering, bluster-
ing day — more like Oct. than July.
B & P. at 8.30 — after that to Studio
where we found that Clifford
had got started upon the digging
of the cellar — Watched the operation
with some interest — Half in. The
teamster having a great row with
an unruly horse. Then stood at
60° in Studio, so I kindled fire.
Then spent the m'f writing letters
— Soon after 12 went to P.O. — got
to dinner soon after 1 — at 2.30
again to studios. There until
after 5 — then down-town again —
Tea soon after 6. After 8 A. & I called
at Mr. Cooke's & spent a pleasant
eve'g with Mr. C. Bessie — Fanny P. —
Mrs. Webb & Joe Williams — Heavy
shower while there — back to
our quarters & to bed at 10.30.
