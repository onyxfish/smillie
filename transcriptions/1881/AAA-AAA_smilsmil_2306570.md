## Left Page

### May, Friday 6, 1881

Raining until afternoon — took Help as usual with
Geo — then to studio — then
out & bought R.R. tickets —
then to Bank & about 11
got to 37 S. where I packed
up my worldly goods — at
1.30 went to Hotel Royal &
had lunch — back — finished
packing — at 3.30 went to
Annie's — there for a few
minutes — then to Tiffany's
& at 5 at studio again. Mr
& Mrs. Bassett of Boston &
others called — I tried to get
a little rest — soon after 6
went to Annie's — A., Edith &
I took tea alone — all of the others
at Ned's — after hanging some
fans, etc, at about 9 Annie & I
went there also — a very pleasant
family party — there until 10
— then home with Annie &
at 10.30 in my old 37 S. quarters
for the last time —

## Right Page

### [MARRIED.]

*SMILLIE—COOK—On Saturday, May 7, at the residence of the bride's mother, by the Rev. Charles G. Ames, James D. Smillie to Anna C. Cook, both of this city.*

### Saturday, May 7, 1881

Charming weather —
Breakfast as usual with Geo at the [illegible]
House — about 9 o'clk — then
to studio — wrote a short letter to Sister
Mary — then in to Colman's — saw Mr?
C., Belle & Mary Tuesday — then to [Sandy?]
— drug store & at about 10.45 went to
Annie's & gave a helping hand when
ever I had a chance — Then dressed
& packed my valise — By 11.30 friends
began to arrive — just 100 were
present when at 12.45 Rev. Chas.
G. Ames & Phil in several simple
semi-quaker manner performed
the ceremony that made Annie & me
man & wife — There congratulations
— at 1.30 — a fine lunch — everything
& everybody seemed easy, informal &
happy — at 3.15 we left amid showers
of rice thrown after us — a very pretty
picture of groups of men & handsomely
dressed women at the windows and
upon the doorsteps — To [illegible] by Penn.
R.R., 4 o'clk train — to Aldine where I
had engaged rooms & had dinner
about 7 — then in our rooms until 9
then out & walked by moonlight for
an hour — then back & to bed at 11 —
Father & Mother came down this
way & returned this afternoon
