## Left Page

### May, Saturday 28, 1881.

much of the time overcast.
A very warm day altho' pleasantly tempered — Got to studio before
10 & all day worked at the press taking impressions & proofs &
at times, working on the plate.
To dinner at 1.30 — back at
3 — worked until near 5
when Annie came to go with
me to see the Parade of the
Coaching Club on Mad. Sq. —
but my work was not quite
done, so Annie waited until
after 6 when we walked up
Fifth Ave. & home — Tea at 7 —
Evng again lounging — at
near 10 we went out & had
a short walk. I mailed notes
that I had written to Kothlert &
Van Cleef — Rec'd invitation to-day
from Sir Wm R. Drake, F.S.A. Hon. Sect
of Painter Etcher Socty of London to
become a Fellow of the Society —
to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 29, 1881.

Very clear & very hot. Bbkfs:
bkt, 9 & 10 — then with Annie &
her mother to St. Collyer's & heard
a sermon from him that did not
please me much. — As we were
passing out of church Mrs. C. had a
fainting spell — I got a compé &
took her home — She soon seemed
to recover. Geo. Smillie came to
dine with us — left at 4 o'clk. —
& did a little writing & at 6
Rode up to Central Park at [illegible]
st & walked up to 82d. 8d. It
was very delightful — then Rode
down home & had tea at 7.45 —
Evng again keeping close in
a dark parlor & to bed at 11.
