## Left Page

### June, Friday 17, 1881

Rain again, after [illegible] —
wrote some letters & got to
studio by 11 o'clk — feeling
very unlike work — Tried to
compose an illustration for
Emerson's Snows (4th etching
for Osgood's book) made little
headway & home to dinner
at 1.30 — again wrote some
letters & got to studio a
little before 4. Miss Hubart
called at 5 — at 5.30 I went
up to see Dr. Yale but he was
not in — Home to tea at
6.45 — Jno. Valentine took tea
with us. Evelf. Annie read
"Mill on the Floss" aloud &
played on the piano — to
bed at 10.40.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 18, 1881

Clear, warm day but a delightful
breeze blowing — Near 10 when I
got to studio — Mrs. Hinks of Milwaukee, Wis., called in behalf
of Industrial Expo. to be held there
next Sept., Mr. Sweet of Syracuse
called — I worked out my design
illustrating Emerson's "Snows" in
charcoal & went to dinner at
1.30 — back at 3 — Reduced
design by camera-lucida and
transferred it to cobtker by
4 o'clk. Then home, & with Annie
went to Central Park — wandered
about until 7.30 — Home a little
before 8 — then tea — Evelf. a
read aloud & played upon the
piano. To bed about 11.
