## Left Page

### March, Monday 7, 1881

Still very charming weather —
clear & spring-like — Got to studio
at 9.30 & in spite of interruptions
worked pretty steadily at paper
on Etching — out to lunch at 1.45
The afternoon very much interrupted
so that I made little progress
with my etching — About five o'cl.
I went to Brooklyn to Mr. Apt.
Ritchie's Residence & there had
dinner at about 6.30 — Guy came
in later — At 8 we went to the house
of [illegible] Cox — Henry S. — then
met the gentlemen of the Rembrandt
Club — At 9 came to order. Rev. Mr.
Chadwick introducing me — I read
my paper on Etching to a very attentive
audience & was flatteringly applauded
at its close — when Mr. Chadwick, after a
few complimentary words, put the motion
for a vote of thanks which was heartily given —
Then Guy, Champney, Ritchie & son went
with me to Private View of Water Colors at
Brooklyn Academy — 10.30 — about to ferry
11.45 when I got to Studio — had a cup of
hot beef tea, for I was hungry & tired —
then to 37. S. & to bed at a little
before 1 —

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 8, 1881

Still lovely, but with an Easterly
wind & promise of a change — Got
to studio at 9.30 & spent the mfg
picking up — listening to a paper
read by Champney, written by his
wife & other such inconsequential
work — out to lunch at near 2
— then wrote bills agt. those who
sent etchings to London & at 4 went
up to Dickel's Riding School, and
took Chas's horse out in the Park —
He gave me considerable hard work
so that I brought him back at 6.40
then to 37. S. — Sembled — tea at Studio
at 7.30 — at 8.30 went to Mad. Ave. —
Family party — as Father & Mother
Return to P.R. to-morrow — at 9.15
at Annie's — She was tired, house
hunting — I was tired — horse back
riding — at 10.15 went to 37. S. —
& to bed at about 9.30 —
