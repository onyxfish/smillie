## Left Page

### March, Friday 11, 1881

Bright, windy day — In the mtf went down town to Sharp's
& got 8×10 copper plate for Bridgeman etching — then to
Chase's & saw him about Jap Paper that arr'd yesterday — he
could not explain why but a 10th part of my order had been sent
to me — back to studio & prepared lunch & at 2 to Phil. Rehearsal
After that with Annie went to look at a house for sale in E. 38th
St., near Park Ave — then home with her & stayed to tea — very
much interested in the matter of houses — At 8.30 went to the
meeting of N.Y. Etching Club, held in Dielman's studio — 40 St & 7th ave.
Farren there, but not D — Leutim
Chas. Miller & D'[illegible] are came — at 10 I left with Y — & walked up
6th ave. with him & then to 37th St. & to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 12, 1881

Overcast & very raw — to Annie's
to help. at 8.15 — to studio at 9.30
tried blind & laid ground for
Bridgeman etching — At 11 went
to Thomson & Evans' art rooms to meet
committee on proposed Bridgeman
Reception — met Huntington, Jay
& Howland there — waited until
11.30 — then to studio & worked
on outline reduction of Bridgeman
to lunch at 1.45 — At 3 went
to Red. Cook's & met Annie at a lunch
party there — then we went out house
hunting from 55th to 34th St. — in a
miserable sleet storm — at 5.30 I
left her at Miss Arnold's — at 6.30
I went to 38th St. for her & then to the
Kimball's — after tea there, all
went to Phil Concert — a very
enjoyable one — after that, home
in a green (34th St.) car — horrible
rain — dense pack — then a walk
up 6th ave. from 34th St. in at 11 o'clk
to 37th St. & to bed at 11.30.
