## Left Page

### June, Thursday 9, 1881

Dull day with light rains —
To work right after b'kfs't and
gave my plate a last biting —
then cleared it and took
three impressions — This work
consumed the m'f. Dinner
at 1.30 — at 2.30 Uncle Willie
sent for us & at 3 we went out
to Hill Side — Rather a disagree-
able day, but, with umbrellas
up we sauntered about the place
until about 6.30 when we had
tea & at 7.30 me returned home
in a cold driving N.E. storm that
continued all night growing
fiercer towards morning —
Father, Mother, Matte, Ed, Kate,
Annie & I were at Hill Side —
After getting home, Annie
read to me while I worked
until 10 — to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 10, 1881

Cold, driving N.E. storm of wind
& rain. Laid ground on plate
& etched over various parts —
also did some deliberate "jet-
biting" — Dinner at 1.30 —
all of the afternoon consumed
in biting & etching — worked
until 6 — then had tea — a
long walk in the forenoon — about
8, as the rain held up for a
while, I went out for a walk.
Left Miss Loomis's shawl at her
home — then to Prof. Ritter's and
there met Wallace Bruce — after
a short call, as W. B. urged me,
I went to his house — he read
his poem on the Hudson to me
— he wants to have it illustrated
& published — Home at about
9.30 — sat with Annie for a
while & to bed at 10.30.
