## Left Page

### August, Sunday 28, 1881

Horribly hot — indisposed —
after last night's entertainment —
Spent most of the m[orning] in
hammock under the maples,
Annie reading aloud to me.
To dinner at 1 — after
that to studio's again and
made me or two letters —
Taking things as easy as possible
— To tea at 6 — after that
to W.J.J's — then mote a letter
home — mailed it & again
to W.J.J's — Dr. Dimock called
for consultation — Richardson
would not come — D. gave a
cathartic & thinks the patient
has suffered from want of prompt
treatment to clear stomach &
bowels of undigested food — To
bed about 11 — Tremendously
hot & dry —

## Right Page

### August, Monday 29, 1881

Still horribly hot — & awfully
dry — all vegetation seems to be
dying — After breakfast went to W.J.J's
then down town & telegraphed to
A.V.V. in Aunt H's name asking
him to come here & bring St. Warner.
Then to P.O. & got letter from Art
Interchange (Arthur Turnure) asking
me to write a Review of Hammerton's
Handbook for Etchers. Then again
to W.J.J's & after 1 a. & J. got to the
studio — Dinner at 1, after midday
to Turnure, Credner & Sickner —
Again to studio — Spent the p.m.
lounging — To Uncle Mrs after
tea at 6 — He seems to be losing —
back to our quarters before 9
& to bed at 10 — no mitigation
of the heat — very light northerly
breeze after 9.
