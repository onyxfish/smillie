## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 10. 1881.

*on board of Str. "Carolina." Stateroom No. 1 —*

Another delightful day — clear, hot in the sun, but with a refreshing breeze constantly blowing — Bkfst. at 9 — then out & went to R.R. office & bought tickets to Fortress Munroe. $6.50 — Parlor Car $1.00 — Stateroom $1.00 Supper on boat $1.00 — Total of $17.00. Then to hotel — at 11 o'clk went out Riding with the same team we had yesterday — went to West Park & enjoyed it immensely — Back at 1 — Dinner at 2 — then wrote some letters and packed up — At 5 we left the hotel & went to Depot at Broad & Pine — at 5.45 train out to Gray's Ferry Junction where we were attached to the great through train & got Parlor Car seats — the ride was exceedingly hot and dirty — Got to Canton Stations just out side of Baltimore at 9.30 — walked to the boat, 100 yards or more, that lay waiting for us — soon got under way — had a light supper — then on deck — a heavenly night — strong but mild, fragrant breeze and lovely moonlight — walked about for some time finally settling in a place out of the wind where we sat until 11 o'clk — then to bed —

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 11. 1881.

*Hygeia Hotel — Room 164. Fortress Munroe — Va*

Superb day — awake before 6 — up about 7 — "boat reached" Old Point Comfort" a few minutes after 8 — the hotel is but a few rods from the landing — after a little cleansing & change we had bkfst — then to work learning the ropes of our new place — met the Walkers — then feeling very sleepy I spent an hour napping — then more — it was after two when we had our dinner — then some lounging on piazza, enjoying the breeze & looking off on the water that comes up to the doorsteps — About 5 we went to the fortress & wandered about there, up on the parapets & down in the casemates until dress parade at 6.45 — over at 7.15 — then to hotel & had tea — every walked on the beach & sat on piazza until nearly — a superb night — then to bed.
