## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 14, 1881

Very mild day with heavy spring-
like showers of rain — went over
to Rennie's & arranged to have
paintings sent to Donnsburn's this
afternoon — Shortly after 10, got to
studio. made notes — made paste
& stretched paper to make some
charcoal sketches for Water Color
work. Home to dinner at 1 — back
at 2.45 — much oppressed by the
weather — discomfort in stomach
coming on, I gave up & for some
time was on the lounge. Mr. Adams
of Utica called to get pictures for
an exhib'n to take place sometime
in Jany. House at 5.30. Tea
at 6.30 — wrote letters to Tickner
— Koehler & Daniels — then worked
on plans for Cotrells until 10.
Then to bed. Steady Rain all
the evening — I made some
notes & worked on Montrose
plans until 10.30 — then
to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 15, 1881

Snow during the night — Cold,
raw, overcast day until
about 3 P.m when it cleared
bright — Got to studio at 9.30
& after finishing plans for
Cotrell & packing them. I
stretched some paper pre-
paratory to beginning on some
Water Color work — went to
[illegible] office with plans for
Montrose — then home to dinner.
Back to studio at about 3
& made charcoal sketch
about 12 × 24 of old ice house
near Saratoga — at 5, went
to Kummel & Voigt's with tubes
to have filled for Ehninger. Walked
from there to Kimball's, where A.
was — tea at 6.30 — then with
Fanny, Emma & Grace —
went to Singing Club. Then
went to studio & wrote a
letter to Dr. Hamilton of London
Pres. Painter, Etchers — at 9.30 went
for A. — home by 10 — to bed
at 11.
