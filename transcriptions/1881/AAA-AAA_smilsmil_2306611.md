## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 27, 1881

Charming day, but still feeling
much out of sorts — did little
else than hang about studio
until 11 when A. & I went
down town & made some pur-
chases for a proposed pic nic
about 1 o'clk. a woman came
& cleaned studio floor. To
dinner soon after 1 — Showers
passing about us but none over
us. I wrote some letters in the
afternoon — down town soon
after 5 — Tea after 6 — Rest of
A. & I went out calling — went
to Fanny Jessup's — The Biddles &
saw them & Mrs. Janvier & then
to the Parks, where we stayed
until near 10 — then to our
quarters & to bed at near 11.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 28, 1881

Still charming weather — To studio
at about 9.30 — Cottrell there. Talked
over house plans for an hour or
more — altho' I had intended
to commence on Osgood Pewsketty
nothing was accomplished — At
12 we went down town as usual
— back at 12.30 — To dinner abt —
after dinner laid grounds in the
brass binder's blocks or dies — then
to studio where most of the P. M.
was spent in preparations for
a pic nic. Mr & Mrs Park. Mr de S—
Miss del Schimmeltz. Mrs. Atherton. Mr.
Foster. Two Park bros, misses Hollenback
& Laine. Mr. Courtright & wife & 3 young
Hollenbach's were the party — They
came before 6.30 — we made a fine
out of doors & had our tea under the
maples upon an improvised table
of boards — Everything seemed to go
off very nicely — nearing when the
company broke up — Storms S. & W. of
us but we were not troubled — Lawn
party in fair ground but we did not
go — To bed at 10.30.
