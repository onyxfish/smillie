## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 8, 1881

fine, drizzling rain and heavy fog most of the m'f.
I spent all the m'f at the house — Down to P.O.
at 12.30 — Got letter from Geo. enclosing checks for
Chicago ($125 — for view near Portsmouth) & St. Louis ($16 —
P. of Lady of Cairo etching) — I
deposited them in Cooke's
Bank — Dinner at 1 — fog
& clearing afternoon, which
was mostly spent at bed.
Annie made up her mind
to go home to-morrow. I tleg?
to her br. Geo. Courtright
went to Wilkesbarre — Tea
at 6 — Spent writing —
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 9, 1881

*Annie left for home.*

Soft & very mild m'f., nearly
overcast. I went over to B's after
b'kf. — A hole cut through the roof
& Dillon building the chimney
up through it — Annie came over
about 11. Then we went to Aunt
H's — I went on down town and
bought tickets for N.Y. — back to
C's before 12 — Annie ready to
start — She had lunch — at 1.15
she got away in Aunt H's carriage
Mrs. Bard riding to depot with
her. Heavy shower of rain which
continued for some time, wind
going around by S. & W. to N.W.
clearing superbly — Splendid
cloud effects from storm to clearing —
Mr. Sherwood & Joe Williams came
to the house just before sundown —
I was on my feet & out of doors all
day superintending the men —
At sunset took a walk — then
P.O. — Tea at 6.30 — Courtright
did not return from Wilkesbarre
so Ella C. & I were alone — I
spent the evening assorting old
letters — To bed about 10.
