## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 24, 1881

Overcast m'f. but cleared charmingly — Near 10 when
I got to studio — finished the biting of etching — then
home to dinner at 1.30 — back & gave a final touch
then cleaned plate — near 5 when again I got home —
First Reception of friends —
Many came between 3 & 6 —
then Tea — Fanny K. "Lib" Howe —
Miss Perry & Mary Willard to
tea — Surely, more Reception
quite a crowd until after
10 — Then some champagne
& at about 11.30 to bed, very
tired.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 25, 1881

Charming day, altho' rather
warm — as usual, near 10 when
I got to studio — Spent some
time getting prints from my
etching — laid a ground over
the whole plate — & home to
dinner at 1.30 — back about
3 after walking down 5th ave.
to see preparations for unveiling
St. Gauden's Farragut in Madison
Sq — Mr. Baker. Photographer
of Buffalo called to get my views
of commemorative ments & some pho-
to process prints. Etched over
parts of plate — Home to tea
at 6.30 — Evelf spent working
over card list, revising & correcting
— a work very much like locking
the stable door after the horse is
stolen — To bed before 11.
