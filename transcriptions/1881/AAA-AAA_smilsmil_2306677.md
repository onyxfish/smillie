## Left Page

### December, Tuesday 6, 1881

Overcast most of the time. After 4 o'clock — went to Dr. Wm
Thompson's about Rheumatic pains that have been troubling
me for a long time — He says they result from weak circulation
& general low condition — Then to Keuners & superintended
the opening of case of A.M. etchings Rec'd from Bourlet, London.
Then to Studios — Found Mr. & Mrs. Lorus busy there buying pictures —
they bought a small one of Geo's and my "Adirondacks" — 33×44 $500 —
Home to dinner at 1.30 — back to studios after 3 — very dark —
most of time arranging accts of etchers who sent to London
2 [illegible] — Home to tea at 6.30 after going to D. Schwadler's in 34th St. —
not in — About 7.45 Annie, Matt Kate & I went to Geo's and
spent the evng. Jos. Nee, Minnie, Walter & Charles there — cakes
& champagne — Home at 11 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Wednesday 7, 1881

Heavy rain all the mng. clearing between 3 & 4 P.M.
Brilliant moonlight night.
Got to studios about 9.30 —
gave "Adirondacks" a thorough washing & then varnished it.
This took up the bulk of the mng. Home to dinner at
1.30 — fell asleep by accident after it & did not get to studios
until 3.30 — Rec'd a letter from P.G. Hammerton asking
if I had any unpublished etching for "Portfolio" — Wrote
some letters & signed 75 prints for Houghton's selections from Ill.
Longfellow — then to Schwadler's — not in — then home to tea, Gel.
Nellie there — all (i.e. Geo. Nellie, Matt, Kate, Annie & I) went to
Standard & saw "Patience" — We had a proscenium box — Enjoyed
the fun exceedingly — Home cakes at 11 & right to bed.
