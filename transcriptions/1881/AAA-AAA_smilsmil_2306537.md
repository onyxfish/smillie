## Left Page

### February, Friday 25, 1881.

About 4 in of snow — continued falling until about mid morning after that it cleared sunny +
bright + by night nearly every vestige of snow was gone after no end of slush + mud. Got to studio about 9.30 + to work on 33×44 — Painted over the sunlighted part of the ground — To lunch at 2. + after that a saunter about Mad. Sq. Then during the rest of P.m. when not interrupted by callers, I painted anew the R.h. group of trees — got struck, so that I did not get my horseback ride as I had intended — Tea in studio at 7 after a walk up to 37t S. + dressing — After t called + saw father + mother (father has a wretched cold) + at 9 to Annie's. There reading up etching until after 10.30 — Then to 37t S. + to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 26, 1881.

Delightful day — At 8.15 Took [illegible] in 38° S. Then walked down to 35t S. with Annie — Got to work on 33×44 Repainting the foreground trees on l.h. side, getting much of the heaviness of color out of them — more light in my shadows. To lunch at 2 — + a short walk — Then again to work until after 4 when Annie came — Near 5 when we got to 10t S. Studio Building where the artists gave a Reception — There went to [illegible] — Then to 38t S. where I had tea — Read etching until 8.30 — [illegible] Conway called — Then to studio where I made until 11 o'clk. material for Paper on etching to be Read before the Rembrandt Club of Brooklyn on Monday evng. March 7. Then to 37t S. + to bed at 12.
