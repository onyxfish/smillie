## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 5, 1881.

*Steamer "Drew". Stateroom O.*

Brilliant cold day — wind from the N. + Ther. below 40° —
a tumble of 40° since yesterday — after Bkfst — picked up + packed
up — about 11 went to studio —
there for near an hour, then
to Bank — saw arrival of French
visitors (Yorktown Centennial) at
5th ave. Hotel — then to Dunlap's,
Knoedle's, Hunter's — then home
to dinner at 1.30 — Mr. Gibbons
there to dinner — later, Emma
+ Mary J — after dinner, to
P.O. station — 3rd ave. + 28th — at
5 o'clk Coupé came — Rode
to foot of Canal St. + soon
after 6, started on the "Drew"
for Albany + Saratoga — tea at
7 — marvellously clear moon
light — horribly cold — to bed
soon after 9 — everything that
we could get piled upon us
to keep us warm.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 6, 1881.

*At Ehninger's — Saratoga Springs.*

Boat reached Albany before 5
o'clk — got up then, soon found
my mistake + to bed again — Up
for all day at 6.15 — Bkfst — at 7 —
off on R.R. train at 7.45 getting
to Saratoga at 9.25 — Brilliantly
cold mtg. when we started, but
saw it overcast when we got to
S. Mr. + Mrs. Ehninger met us at
the station — drove at once to
their house + had second Bkfst —
about 10 o'clk — then chatting
until 11 when we went to our room.
Lunch at 1 o'clk. + after that a
long ride — the wind strong +
cold — back about 4 but had
to take two or three walks to get
thawed out. Dinner at 6 —
Evening in parlor before delightful
crackling wood fire — to bed
soon after 10.
