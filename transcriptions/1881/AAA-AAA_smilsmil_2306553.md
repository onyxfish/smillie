## Left Page

### March, Thursday 31. 1881.

Dull, Raw day, with occasional spitting rain — After bkfst to studio for a short time — then to Academy of Music & bought Matinee Tickets for Ehminger. Then to Krimmel & Vogt's — Spent most of the mtg there on Osgood Etching business — back to studio about 12 — Surprised by a visit from Oscar Wiel of San Francisco — on his way to London to lunch at 1.30 — Then to 5th Av Hotel for Opera Tickets — again to Academy of Music — again to 5th Av Hotel & then to studio shortly after 3 — a little work on Etching — At 6.20 went for Annie then to Fanny K's — after tea, to Singing Club. Then to studio — Farrer, Coleman & Bellows there discussing details of etching book until 10 — then went for Annie & took her home. J, 6 37' S; & to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### April, Friday 1. 1881.

Still dark & dismal — Got to studio about 9.30 & to work on Bridgeman Etching, but very much disturbed — many callers. Worch Lewis called & invited us to meet him, Geo Whitney & W. T. Richards at the Albemarle at 2 o'clk. for lunch — we did — back to studio at 3.15 — then some writing — did no work of importance — at 6.30 went in to Coleman's & took tea. At 8 we went to Annie C's where we had a musical evening — the last in No. 22 W. 38th St — Walter called it a "House Cooling" — To 37th 82 & to bed about 11.30.
