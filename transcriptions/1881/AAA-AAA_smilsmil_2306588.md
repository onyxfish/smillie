## Left Page

### June, Saturday 11. 1881.

Still dripping wet weather. The wind not so strong or the rain so heavy as yesterday. After bkfs. I laid a rebitiung ground upon a part of my plate & varnished it — then walked up town & telegraphed to Haven Putnam a regret that we could not accept an invitation to dine with him on Sunday — then back to my work. Dinner about 1 — all the afternoon printing & working on my plate between pf., until 6 o'clk. Then tea & about 7.30 Annie & I went to Prof. Ritter's where about 25 of his class of Vassar girls were being entertained. Some singing and playing on piano & violin — all m[et] at 8 — we left about 8.30 — had a walk by moonlight until after 9 — the weather cleared charmingly after 3 o'clk. p.m. Home soon after 9 — & to bed soon after 10. \

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 12. 1881.

Clear & very warm — Bkfs. at 8.40 — feeling very owlish — did not go to church, but with Annie spent the rest out of doors, on the Fox place & in Livingston's woods — back before 1 — Dinner at 1.30. After dinner, lounging for some time — then wrote a letter of condolence to Mr. Geo. Whitney, whose only child, Mrs. Outerbridge died 3 or 4 weeks ago — Before 6 went out & had a short walk — Tea at 6 — then on piazza until sundown & after that walking near the house until soon after 8 when Prof. & Mrs. Ritter called — At 10.30 they left — To bed about 11.
