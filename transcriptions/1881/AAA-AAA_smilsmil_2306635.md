## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 13, 1881

Superb day in spite of the [illegible] much interested all day in the work of house framing — the m[ost] spent mostly out of doors — some drawing on plans — wrote a short note to Turner + made estimates for digging out under studio + laying wall — To P. O. before 1 — then to dinner + after that, again to studio + spent all the P. M. out of doors until near 6. Then tea — The eve[ning] spent quietly in doors + to bed at our usual time.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 14, 1881

A very fine day, but with threats of a change in weather. I spent the day out of doors directing the putting up timbers to P. O. + then to dinner at — after that, again at studio — there until near 6 — then to tea — after tea + I went to P. O. — then to call on Mrs. my Park — not in — then to Strond's — not in — then to Mr. Chas. Read's — Allie Strnd Miss Sherrard + Ben Mulford there — after a half hour call we went to Mr. Mulford's + after half an hour there to Sayre's where I read awhile + to bed about 10.
