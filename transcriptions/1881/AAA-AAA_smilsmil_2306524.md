## Left Page

### January, Sunday 30, 1881

Did not get up until late — eight [illegible] after 10 — there to Dr.
Hastings & heard him deliver a discourse explaining his
reasons for retiring from the ministry & accepting a Prof-
ship in Union Theo Seminary —
Father & Kate there — walked down to their place in Mad. ave — there
until near 2 — then to Annie's &
had dinner — After 4 me went to Mad. ave. & called on Father &
Mother — at 5, Ret'd. to 38t S. and
at 6.30 went to Mr. Gibbons' in 44 S.
where we took tea with the family, Mr. & Mrs. Raynor, Miss Fidelia
Bridges & her sister — the evett
passed pleasantly until near
10 when we Ret'd. to 38th S. — &
at a little before 11 I went to 37th S. & to bed.

Overcast day with a sense of snow overhead.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 31, 1881

Sure day — got to studio
before 10 & to work finishing
the painting in of "Approach
to the Huddle". The Result,
as usual, very provoking — some
good & much bad. Out to lunch
about 2 — after that to Artist's fund
[illegible], then to Thos. Moran's when
I spent an hour talking & arranging
the matter of exhibiting etchings
at Painter-Etcher's exhib'n to be held
in London next March. Then back
to studio & at 4 went with Coleman
to Ride — to 39t S. soon after 5
had a good scrub — to studio at
7.30 — a good meal — & at 9
was at Annie's. She Read to
me a very long diary letter, or
series of letters from Rosalie Butler
now in Europe. — at 10.40 to
37th S. & at 11 to bed.
