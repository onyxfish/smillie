## Left Page

### August, Saturday 6, 1881

Still very hot & smoky —
Catrílle came up between 9 &
10 o'clk. & for an hour or more
we were at work measuring
& drawing stakes for the cellar
walls. Then, as my head ached
I swung for a time in hammock.
Dinner at 1 — Between 1 & 2
a heavy thundershower passed
over us — In the afternoon
I wrote to Mr. Ticknor — went
to P.O. after 5 — another thunder
shower came up about 6 —
& with short intervals it
rained all night. After
tea, A & I walked to piazza
for awhile, then with Miss
Hollenback & Courtright we
played whist until after
10 — Then to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 7, 1881

Rain until 5 am. Rather muggy
m[illegible]. Trying to clear — afternoon
overcast, breezy & cool. B[illegible] —
at 9 — went to Pres[byterian] Ch. & heard
Rev. Mr. Munger interesting dis-
course — a perfect defense &
exceeding great reward. After
service, to studio & wrote a note
to Miss Abbatt — a short walk
& dinner at 2 — At 3.30 went to
fairground where Bro. Bomy
the darkie preacher harangued
for about an hour — Then to studio
after a short walk. Wrote again
until 6 — Then to house & got bread
& butter & [illegible] to studio & wrote
until 7.30 — Then ate bread and
A & I went to P.O. with our letters
& after that took a walk. — Then
to the house & on piazza until
10 — Then to bed.
