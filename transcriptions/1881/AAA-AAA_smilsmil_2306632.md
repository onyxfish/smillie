## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 7, 1881

Still pitilessly hot & dry — under the trees until 10,
then to studio's, very high wind — one of the hottest days
known in Montrose — 102° in many shady places. did not get above
98° in studio. Finished up Revised notes to Turnure — To
dinner at 1 — After dinner went to P.O. then to studio Laid
out detail plan of hall ways keeping busily about in spite
of the heat — tea at 6 — The everg was spent quietly
upon porch at S. Sayre's, listening to accounts of forest fires all about.
Sun & moon obscured by the smoke — a ghastly effect.
To bed soon after 10 —

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 8, 1881

Cooler day owing to a breeze from
N.W. & obscuration of the sun —
which could be seen most of the
day looking like a red hot ball.
Spent the mng at studio in
tribulation over 1 in. scale drawings
of the stairs & hall ways — To
dinner at 1 — back again soon
after 2 & worked until near 6 —
then to tea — After that went
to P.O. met Dr. Mulford & spent
some time with him — then to
Sayre's & the rest of everg with
a— on porch — quite cool everg
but no indications of rain —
To bed soon after 10 —
