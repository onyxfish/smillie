## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 6. 1881.

Cold, windy & dirty — Got to studio at 9.30 — more fussing to get etchings ready to send to Boston Museum — also, did a little work on Mt. Hood drawing — Lunch at 1.30 — Packed etchings & got them off by 2.50 — then at 4.30, went to Arnold & Constable's, met Annie & looked at Curtains — then to 110 E. 38 — then to 37 E. 8. & dressed — at 7 dinner at Annie's — at 8, in Charley Gunther's Box (No. 8) at the Academy of Music — Stokeyvin — full, brilliant house. — The opera too long to suit me — near 12 when it was over — chorus noisy & ill dressed — much of the setting, childish — especially swam & boat etc — Cambanini in very good voice, altho he asked indulgence. Gerster very good — but to me the whole entertainment was painfully conventional — walked up to 37 E. 8. — & to bed at 12.30.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 7. 1881.

Very windy but bright and pleasant — Feel somewhat at a disadvantage after my Opera experience last night — Did not get much work done — Sat for awhile at A.B.N. Co. Mt. Hood drawing — Lunch at near 2 — then for awhile went into Moore's Gallery to see the Young American Pictures — At 4.30 went up to Ride on Colunn's horse — Did not enjoy it much — it was very lovely in the Park but the horse was uneasy under me & constantly shying — back at 6 — at 6.20 went with Annie to Emma D's to tea — then to Club — then to studio where I wrote a letter on Academy matters to the Art-interchange — at 10.30 to Singing Club — got Annie & took her home — then to 37 E. 8. — & to bed soon after 11.
