## Left Page

### November, Friday 4, 1881

Some snow during the night weather growing rapidly
colder — before noon, violent
gusts of wind with blinding
snow plumes. The men at
the building were all ob-
liged to give up work except
Dillon & his nephew, Dorsey.
At times, Dillon could hardly
lay brick, his hands were so cold
& snow so filled the building.
The wind blew boards around
& upset things generally. A
superb squall passed over at
about 4.30 — The sunset and
moonrise particularly fine.
I tramped about for nearly
two hours. Tea soon after 6.
Eve'g. Florence came in
& we played "Boston" until
10 o'clk — About 10.45 when
we got to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 5, 1881

Bleak, windy day. Ther. between
40 & 50° in spite of sunlight. After
[illegible] went to bldg. as usual and
then to quarry — then to work on
full size drawing to show Cotrell
how I wish the siding put on. At
12 again went to bldg.. Dinner
at 1 — The afternoon was spent
between the building, where I
was directing the men at their
work & my room at Courtright's
where I worked upon plans.
Down town about 5 o'clk — back
before 6 & had a rest before
tea. Eve'g passed quietly.
Annie read aloud from
"Romola" & we had a little
game of cards — to bed
at 10 o'clk. when quite a
shower fell.
