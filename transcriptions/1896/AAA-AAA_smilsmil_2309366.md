## Right Page

### Thur. June 25, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, raw \+ cloudy all day with a mizzling fog at night. After 6kfo. I wrote some notes, among others one to Spencerth. Coon of The Tribune. Katie took them to P.O. on her return brot me another Sun. Editorial on the subject of Columbia University \+ Ugh. f.D — I a letter from S. H. Coon. objecting to the note I sent to him with the request that he have it put? in The Tribune. He takes ground that my objection to the inference, most natural from head lines to Sunday's article is a chg. of inaccuracy agt him — he is oversensitive \+ is mistaken I think — but I sat down \+ wrote to him a second note \+ acceded to his request that I withdraw my note for foublication. Then I took it to P.O — both notes to him going in one mail — when I got back to house the mail was gone — After dinner I worked in the shop \+ finished covering my aqua-tint box with muslin — After tea I had a spell of a good deal of discomfort — my bowels misbehaved during the day \+ at night my stomach was unaminable.
