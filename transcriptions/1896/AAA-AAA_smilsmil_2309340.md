## Left Page

### Sun. May 3, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day with several shower which did little more than wet the streets. Bkft. at about 10 — a good part of the remainder of the m'f. I spent at Walters. He is in bed with a swollen face. Trouble from a tooth extracted a few days ago. Trey to Studio — thence to Sam. Colman's in 39th S. for a few minutes — then, dinner at home at 2 — afternoon I had a long nap. Tea at 7 — Ev'g. After getting the boys to bed I read for some time Cummings Annals of the N.A. p's — being particularly interested in the financial struggles \+ the bickerings \+ the constantly changing plans \+ purposes of the N.A.'s in the matter of selling old property — the purchase of new site \+ agreement as to plan for bldg. The present N.A. troubles seem to be smooth water compared with the experiences 40 to 30 years ago.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 4, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast day — the weather in my head was of the same description — much of the day I was rendered almost useless by a headache — I got to studio at usual time \+ was unable to accomplish anything — about noon I went to 2d. Nat. Bk. \+ after that went to Bridgeman's in 19th St. \+ bought some Shirley Poppy seeds \+ sent them to Montrose. Then home to lunch — most of the afternoon I spent in my bed \+ got up at about 5 feeling somewhat better — Ev'g. Council meeting — I had expected some trouble but it was quite harmonious — perhaps because Moran was not there. It was agreed to call a meeting of N.A.'s for next Sat. ev'g to receive report of Site Com. I got home at a little after 11.
