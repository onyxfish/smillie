## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 13, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild pleasant day — It was 10 o'clk before I had slept. Men sat for awhile reading news paper — When Jim \+ Ralph came in from Sunday School we started out, they taking their cameras — By 3d Ave elevated R.R. to 125th S\. — Then, across to Boulevard by cable car — then up to Grant's monument where we got a number of photos — Then to the new Columbia University Bl\+P — to St. Luke's Hospital — down 110th S\. — to Elevated station at 104th S\. — \+ so home in time for dinner at 2 o'clk. — After that wrote a letter to [illegible] Pres. of Accounting Trust Co. of Topeka, Kan. about my farm in Kingman Co — Mold Wall mt\. — Then to M\. Cook's \+ saw Mary — There 331 E. 17th S\. \+ saw Emma Irelan about half an hour — then home at 6.30 Evely Charle came in —

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 14, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — got to studio at 9.30 \+ to work at Vesuvius — I got all the outlining with color painted in — MacRae, the Real Estate agent, called about N.A.P.D. Site — at 12 I went to Calverly's studio — met Emma Ireland there — we were, both of us, very much pleased with his little bust of Annie — I am now quite satisfied — The expression is very sweet and the likeness is admirable — Home to lunch at 1 — to studio at 2 — stopped work at the end of daylight — 4.30 — had a short walk — met Nicoll \+ went into the Cemetry for a short time \+ talked N.A.P.D. Site — Home to dinner at 6.30. To Council meeting at 8 — Geo. not there — telegram from him, too unwell — Dislaw \+ Church not there — Site com. meeting — nothing accomplished — home at 11.
