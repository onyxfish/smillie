## Left Page

### Sun. May 17, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm but with a pleasant tempering breeze - I kept quietly indoors all day until the afternoon when Ralph \+ I went out on our bicycles starting at about 4 o'clk. We got up to the Park very comfortably but there we found the drive ways so crowded with carriages that it was difficult for us to get along. We managed to bore through to the upper end of the Park \+ then out on the west side at 106 S - \+ over to the Boulevard where we were not troubled by carriages but found swarms of bicycles - we saw numerous collisions \+ accidents of no great harm - we managed to get home without trouble at about 5.30, having had a ride, by my cyclometer of about 17½ miles - Ralph did very well. Tea at about 7 - a lazy evening. While I did not feel tired by my ride I was indisposed to do any work \+ got to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 18, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncomfortably hot day with a hot wind storm of brief duration between 2 \+ 3 o'clk. After 6½p. I went to Walter's who went with me to Sniffen Court where we spent some time studying over the problem of reconciling the conflicting grades on the part of the court that I have to re-pave - Later I went to the N.A. \+ attended to some business there getting home to lunch at 1 - After lunch I went to the Am. Tract Socy. Bldg. Cor. Nassau \+ Spruce - to [illegible] Works - saw Engineer Stevenson. [illegible] at last got permission to run a gutter across my sidewalk to keep the water from running along my sidewalk in the winter freezing there. Then to Walter's \+ explained to him a plan that I had worked out for grading \+ paving - he approved - on the way I had gone to Bleaker St. Savings Bank to enquire about making a deposit there of a small N.A. School prize fund. No deposits from Ass'ns or Corporations rec'd. Home \+ rested before dinner - Suet last meeting of N.A. Council for the season - nothing of special importance but much Routine work done - Under a Resolution prepared by me it was announced that a general report on its affairs should be prepared \+ sent to all N.A.'s some time during the summer. After 11 I walked up town with Dielmare - It was after 12 before I got to bed.
