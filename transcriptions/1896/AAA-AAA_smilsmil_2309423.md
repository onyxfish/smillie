## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 16, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charl went to Water Gap.

Disagreeable day \- Raw, misty morning much the same conditions continued throughout the day \- occasional gleams of sickly sunshine being sandwiched between rain threatening gloom. Charl left us soon after 10 \- to drive to New-Milford to take the 12 o'clk. Train to the Water Gap \- to go to N.Y. to register for voting, to return to W.G. same day. He may stay about the gap or Dingman's Ferry for some days \- I put in another good days work in studio. Bt work on Mad. Sq. Garden Tower. \+ on Hills by the lake \- made a second small etching \+ bit it for further aqua-tint experiments \- \+ worked up some other aqua-tint grounds. At noon R. \+ I went up to Fair grounds \+ reeled off 3 m. on our wheels \- then home to dinner. At 4 went down town \+ had one funny plates cut sq. \- on my return worked as long as I could see. After Tea went to P. O.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 17, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \& raw \- fewer clouds at night \& some moonlight. Warner \+ assistant painting about the house all day. Jill also pothering about at odd jobs \- A very successful balloon ascension was made from the green back of the school house at 2.30. I had an excellent view of the whole affair, from start to finish. Thro' an opera glass, while standing upon the upper piazza. The balloon may have gone up 1500 feet when the man separated the parachute from its attachment \& began his descent \- by the same movement a valve in the top of the balloon was opened \- the dark smoke poured out \& the great form collapsed and followed the parachute down. There was no wind \& the man alighted in plain view but a few rods from where he started. I worked all day on my aqua-tint experiments hoping to finish them for the present but did not quite succeed. At noon R. \+ I went to Fair ground \& rode 4 miles on our wheels \- at 6. I went to P.O. \+ took a walk getting back to tea 10 min. late \- at 8 I again went to P.O. \+ there met Editor Cruser who kept me half an hour or more explaining to me the beauties and reasonableness of Free Silver. To-day I recd a very satisfactory note from Pres. Seth [illegible] concerning proposed site for N.A.[illegible] near the Columbia University.
