## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 7, 1896

**Weather:** Bright, windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, windy day. I was not able to get up until 11 o'clk. I still had a wretched headache \+ felt miserable all over. After a light lunch I went up to Dr. Yale's to consult him about my condition which, in several ways, is unusual. I saw him \+ got some of the comfort of sympathy — then I rode down to 28th S. \+ got a Pasteur bougie for filter, Gretta having broken one. Then to studio. Barmore met me there with a number of Parchment \+ [illegible] of Gent. Lee [illegible] portrait?. I signed them for him — he paid me, by check $75.00 \+ another $75.00 check dated Monday, Feby 10th — He is to give me two notes for the $300 bal. at 60 \+ 90 days. It was 6 o'clk. when I got home. Dinner at 6.30 — Charl. came in in the eveg.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 8, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day. After my regular m'g duties I went to 2d Nat. Bk. \+ made deposit — then to studio at about 11 \+ until 1 worked on plate for Gent. Green's mezzo. portrait — commencing by laying a light ground with the finest tool (125) over face \+ neck\+cloth. Home to lunch \+ was surprised to find Mr. \+ Mrs. Dant. Sayre there. They stayed \+ lunched with us \+ left at 2.30. Then Ralph \+ I went to studio where he played until Mary came \+ took him away to buy a suit of clothes for him. I worked, dividing my time between the ground-laying on Green plate \+ scraping on "from Dean's Leap, Lake George". At 5.30 walked up to 5th Ave. \+ 57th S. \+ then home. Dinner. At 9 o'clk. went to Century \+ there met Elihu Vedder who expects to use Maynard's studio for a little while — having arranged with him I ret'd home at about 10.
