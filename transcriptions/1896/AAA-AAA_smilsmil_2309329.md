## Left Page

### Sat. April 11, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day. To studio at about the usual time + spent the m'f, except such time as Mr. Jas. Gifford of Hudson, spent with me, in writing a paper that I am preparing to read at the Special meeting of N.A's, called for Wsd. ev'f next, to consider the matter of site. To lunch, as usual, + back — + worked a little more on paper — but more time was spent with old sketches + studies. A short walk — I am too lame with Rheumatic legs to walk far. Then home to dinner — we the entire family, seven of us, went to Chickering Hall + heard Lieut. Peary give an acct. of his Arctic explorations, illustrated by a great number of remarkable photographs taken by himself. The audience was small + provokingly cold — Out before 10. Then home — found Charl there — He told us the particulars — very sad — of the end of de Bisschop [illegible — ~8 words]

## Right Page

### Sun. April 12, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant m'f, + quite mild but the afternoon sky was overcast + the air was very raw. I got down to my 6½ p. date, after 11 I went to studio. Found the Ther. at 80°. + a big fire in the furnace. I opened all of the windows wide — + closed all furnace draughts — Then home and wrote for awhile on paper on N.A. site — Had a short walk bet. 1 + 2 o'clk, met Jane. Dinner at 2. Mr. + Mrs. Gifford did not come as I had hoped they would. After dinner I went with Ralph to the museum of Nat. history + we rambled thro' the halls there until the closing hour at 5 — on our way out met Ed. m. + Mary Cook, Louise + Jack — we walked across the Park to 72d S — Louise R. + I continued to 60th S. + then home by cars at 6. Tea at 6.30. Ev'f read as usual to R. until 8.30. Then wrote a note to Mr. + Mrs. Gifford, excusing myself for not calling on them owing to the lateness of the hour + bodily misery, + I have no doubt they out of sorts enough to excuse in spirit my sympathy.          Charl came.
