## Left Page

### Tues. March 24, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasant winter day \- To studio \+ got to work on "Capri from Naples" 12×19. Repainting the whole thing \- put in a palette knife sky. After lunch continued the painting until near 5 \- As I left the bld \- one of the stable men in the Court was washing his wagons in the alley using a hose, the water running all over my sidewalk \+ freezing. When I objected he was very abusive. I went to Police station in 35th St. \+ made complaint \- Sargent promised to send policeman in the n't. Then home. dinner \- Eve'g adjourned Special meeting, N.A. to discuss sites. A very full \+ interesting meeting. J.Q. Brown working for 10th St. site \+ E.W.Perry working with tremendous determination for a site in 43 St. It was a hard fight \+ for a time I was uncertain as to how it would go \- but at last, every site offered by the Com \- was rejected \+ I was satisfied \- I am now, in a measure, avenged. After adjournment there was much informal discussion \+ I worked hard to advance the interest in Columbia Colleg site \- It was 12 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 25, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day \- went to Police Station in 35th St. before 10 \+ had a policeman return with me to studio to see the ice on my sidewalk. I finally got to work \+ painted in "Capri" \- Home to lunch \- at 3 went to Columbia College \- but Pres. Dows was with a com., then to studio \+ worked about there until near 5 \- then again to the College \+ had a brief interview with Pres. Low, telling him what had been done at the Academy meeting last night \- He said that he was very much interested \- Home before 6 \+ had a rest before dinner \- Eve'g Mary, Kate \+ Charl went to "Thomas'" Concert \- many went on invitation of Emma Ireland \- I had a stupidly quiet eve'g, accomplishing nothing \- got to bed early.
