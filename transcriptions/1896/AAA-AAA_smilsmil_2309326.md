## Left Page

### Sun. April 5, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day \- altho' a very cool wind blew freshly from the N.W. It was after 11 when Jim & I went to studio. There for a few minutes \- then we rode up to about 100th S\- walked into Park. thro' the Ramble & mall & out at Arsenal gate & home by cars, having had a very pleasant walk. Dinner at 2 \- After it Charl came in to tell us that he had had a talk over telephone with the Boston surgeon who has just performed the operation on de Bischoff\- he gave a favorable report \- at 9 p.m. Charl got a telegram \- still favorable \- I wrote to me giving figures upon which to base inheritance & transfer tax \- After 5 Ralph & I had a walk \- tea at 6.30 \- Evell reading to boys until 8.30 \- Geo. & Charl came in \- I read Garster upon Appendicitis until 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 6, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day. Snow & slush in the mt., melting away by noon \- very dark during remainder of day & foggy at night \- To studio where I spent most of the mt., writing a paper that I may send to N.A.'s if I can get it into a shape that suits me. It is an attempt to show that if the Academy should determine to buy a large site above 110th St. temporary bldgs could be put up, paint & offered for rental at such misting figures as to secure tenants for 30 more studios upon terms that, after paying all expenses would return 2½% or 3% upon investment for site, calculations based upon a term of 10 years. To lunch at 1 \- afternoon I was engaged clearing & arranging some drawers in my closet. Evelf. Counci meeting at N.A. Nothing of especial interest \- After adjournment an animated discussion upon site matters continued until after 11 o'clk \- Taken part in by Beckwith \- Perry \- Hart \- Moran \- Shirlaw \- myself \- at 11.15 I left leaving the others still at it.
