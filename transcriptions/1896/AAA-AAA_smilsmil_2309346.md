## Left Page

### Fri. May 15, 1896

**Weather:** Close, muggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Close, muggy day — n\. bed all day with a very severe headache. Struggled up \+ had something to eat at about 7 P.m. — An uncomfortable eve\. in my big chair \+ to bed again at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 16, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Mary \& Kate went to Plainfield.*

Clear \+ warm but mitigated by a delightful breeze. I got up feeling the effects of yesterdays experience which gradually lessened as the day passed. Immediately after bkft. I took R. to see Dr. Mes. R. has been coughing for the past week, the trouble increasing — \[illegible\] — prescribes tonics — then home. Had I felt better I would have gone with Mary \& Kate to lunch with the Tweedy's at Plainfield. They had a very delightful time. I attended to home duties — then to studio — Had a sale from Maynard \& Vedder — both rather glum — effects of Academy dinner not yet entirely gone — I was not in a condition to accomplish much. I did a little clearing up — After lunch I spent some time with the boys getting our bicycles ready \+ shortly after 3 we three started out — Rode up Mad. Ave. to 60th S. — then into the Park \+ made almost the entire circuit of the Park — cutting off abt. at the north end. We stopped twice for resting spells — but Ralph took every hill without difficulty \+ rode with a good deal of cautious judgment. We got home at about 4.30 without mishaps, much to my gratification — cyclometer showed 8½ m. — To Jim the ride was of course nothing — R. declared that he was not in the least tired — nor was I, but my saddle had made me very sore — I had a rest before dinner — some reading to R — a little more, to myself, after he got to bed — \+ then I got to bed at 10.30.
