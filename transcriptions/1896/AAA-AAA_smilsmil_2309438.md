## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 15, 1896

Jim ret'd. from New Rochelle.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day. Bkf't at 9.30 — Reading until 11 — Then to studio for awhile and at noon walked up to George's boarding place, 150 E. 60'. \+after a little visit with him we walked together to his studio 157 E. 59" where he showed me some of his recent work — especially some Yosemite oil sketches that he thinks could be effectively done large, for decoration. Home to dinner at 2. Found Walter \+ Louise at the house making a call. After dinner I read till my eyes were very heavy — Then, a nap. Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. Took rugs to studio. Home after 9 — Reading until 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 16, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild, pleasant day. To studio about 10 — the larger part of the day was devoted to making a pen sketch — a map of Morningside Heights showing the surroundings of the site south of Columbia University that I have reported upon favorably to the N.A. f.D. To lunch as usual. Dr. Gerster came in for a little while at about 4 o'clk — we went out together. I went to O'Brien's printing establishment in 23d. St. \+took there the pen sketched map. Home after 5 \+had a short rest before dinner. Eve'g. to N.A. Council meeting — all present except Wood. We went up to see Fall Exhib'n just turned over by the hanging com. It is a very depressing affair. After returning to Council room \+adjournment there was a very interesting \+general conversation concerning "Fine Art" Academy policy for the future \+ sites. It was almost 11.30 when Geo. \+ I walked up town.
