## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 6, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Exceedingly cold - but I managed to be very comfortable in my studio after spending much time in the cellar stoking the furnace. All the mtf. I worked upon dee plate - The afternoon was overcast & I could not see to work much after 4 o'clk. Evety. Council Meeting - N.A. - adjourned at 10 - Then Robt. Blum got hold of me with along paper he had written with reference to the Site Com. Reports - Evidently he had planned to read it before the council but I got him into the Supt's Room & he had to read it to me alone. I did not get away from him until near 11 - There was nothing practical in his paper.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 7, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not quite so cold - overcast - snow in the afternoon - To studio at 10. & worked on dee - In the afternoon, which was short, I took some working pfs. & wet down to paper for to-morrow. I have now reached the stage of finish where I have to work alongside of a printing press. Evety. quietly at home. Chase came in - I wrote to Maynard.
