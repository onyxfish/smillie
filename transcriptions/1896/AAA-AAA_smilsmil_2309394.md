## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 19, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another brilliant, cool, September-like day. Ther. in studio at 8 Am, 58°, so I kindled fire. The w't was so inviting that I went out on my wheel to enjoy it, Ralph going with me. We had a very pleasant ride for about an hour. On my return Knoll, the Vet. Surg., had come to call on Jessie, our old cow who is ailing. Her Temp. is 2° above normal, pulse slightly accelerated — no appetite, dry nose, etc, etc. indicating that she is suffering from a fit of acute indigestion. A mixture, about a quart, was prepared \+ with the help of Spense I administered it, he holding her head up by the horns while I poked the neck of the upturned bottle down her throat. I worked a little while on gelatine Tracing, Mad. Sq. tower. After dinner I wrote a letter to Jim \+ went to P.O. with it at 3 — waited for mail — then home — a little more work on Tracing \+ a little more on bicycle — Early part of Eve'g, reading to Ralph — to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 20, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still cool \+ clear — Had a spasm of work today — finished gelatine Tracing of Mad. Sq. tower, scraped \+ prepared it for transferring. Got press ready, giving it a general rub over with every cloth, oiling bearings, etc, etc. Knoll came up to see the cow in the m'g \+ left a number of powders to be given to her three times a day — Spence \+ I did as we were bid \+ Jessie took the powders at 12 \+ 6 o'clk. Mr. Wm. Nulford called to tell us that she had been to Lake Cho conut yesterday \+ had seen Jim who seemed to be in high spirits \+ great good health. Calst. \+ Mr. Cromwell took tea with us \+ spent the eve'g until near 10 o'clk.
