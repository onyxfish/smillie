## Left Page

### Fri. March 6, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — milder & quieter. Went to studio before 8 to see locksmith & get him to remedy some injuries done to the yale locks on front & cellar door by an incompetent carpenter. Then back to blk. when again I went to Studio met Walter there. He came to look at the cellar having rec'd bill for the water proofing work. Others followed — The locksmith — the carpenter — the iron worker etc. At last I got to work & painted all day on "Hazel-brook — April" — I went pretty generally over the foreground work & think that I improved it. Barmore came in after 12 — has ret'd from the south & wishes a number of changes made in the Lea portrait to meet the criticisms of the southerners — also to ask for ten days grace on his note for $300 payable in the 10th inst. Home to lunch after 1 — Two young women — classmates of Bessie's — lunched with us — Painted at Hazelbrook until after 4. Men got out & one other all paintings and decided to take up a view of Calon to-morrow. Commenced some time ago. Took a long walk up town — met Julia Girot in the street & had a long talk with her — Saw gel. Bently & his niece, Ketty Shafer, called. Gel. & Nellie S. came & took Katie out — Charle came in later — much bunged up with a cold. [illegible — ~3 words] — [illegible — ~3 words] to be [illegible] [illegible — ~3 words]

## Right Page

### Sat. March 7, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very dark in f. with some rain; clearing in the afternoon very pleasantly. At studio I got out a sketch commenced some time ago, a view of Calon from the suburbs of Naples, and, inspite of the darkness I painted as best I could upon it. The iron men put up the iron dots in the cellar & brought the iron brackets to be put up on the front of the blk to support signs. In the afternoon Gerster came in for a few minutes — I went home & had a rest before dinner. Jim & Ralph went to Bowery Theatre with Katie T. to see "Robin Hood" Surey. Read to the boys after dinner & at 8.45 I went to Fine Arts Society blk in 67th St. last evef of Architectural League Exbn — great thing. Many invited guests remained after the exhibition was closed to the public to enjoy a "smoker". I went to Century Club — monthly meeting — and late in the evef. met T. Hopkinson Smith — who was in very genial mood — He — Lockwood de Forest, no gel. & I formed a little groupe. Smith, in his breezy way, ordered a bottle of Champagne & it was well after 1 o'clk in the mf. when we got up, after being much entertained by descriptions & constructions of sketching experiences. It was near 2 o'clk. where I got to bed.
