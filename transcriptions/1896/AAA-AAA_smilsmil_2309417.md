## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 4, 1896

**Weather:** Cloudy & dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy & dull receipt for a little time about the middle of the day when a misty sunshine developed some very beautiful color. I accomplished but little during the day but was out of doors & about the place a good deal. Did some tree climbing & cutting out of dead wood among the beeches. Bessie so much out of sorts, fever & pain, that Mary sent for the Doctor. He thinks it is severe cold. Late in the afternoon I went to Judge Jessup's farm & saw Warren Tingley, his man, & got promise of loan of sub-soil plow. Evng. I wrote, in studio, letter to Jim & Note to Carroll Beckwith. \- to P.O. at 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 5, 1896

**Weather:** Still cloudy & raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still cloudy & raw without being cold. I spent some time in the shop fixing my small box for experimenting in dry ground aqua-tint. Then looking after the men \- Hibbard, Rutau, Poke's team & man & Go's. Walker, all at work on the apple terrace to get it into shape ready for the transplanting. As it is worked over it grows to be more promising. At noon I went to Studio & worked for a little while on Garster head. After dinner I went down town with Tom's bicycle & left it at Cooley's to be crated. Then I walked out to Harrington's mill & a whim & arranged with him to begin ploughing reclaimed swamps on Wed. m'f. It was after 4 when I got home. The men were using a scraper on side hill \- that was followed by a harrow & at 6 they had it ready for me to dig holes for the trees. After tea R. & I went down town. Evng. at about 9, Hall & assistant came to subject Jessie, the cow, to the tuberculine test. Requires 24 hours. They will be with her all night. I had a fire made in stove in shop & a cot with some bedding moved up there so that they may get through the night comfortably.

Hibbard, Rutau, Walker \- Poke's team & man, all day.
