## Left Page

### Fri. April 3, 1896

**Weather:** Cold, windy day with snow flurries  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, windy day with snow flurries. Got to studio in good time \+ again to work on studies of Easter lily, making a new one which occupied me until 12 o'clk. Then Alice Ireland came in \+ we had a talk concerning Ralph \+ his school. I told Alice that she might tell miss Miller that I would send R. to her school next winter. Home to lunch. Then to 2d. Nat. Bk. \+ drew money. Then down town to Dep't. Pub. works. Closed. Then to Lee \+ Lee — had to wait for Wm. L. some time. When he came we completed \+ executed my Will. — an unnecessarily lengthy \+ complicated instrument, it seems to me. To studio \+ 4.30 — Eve'g. Dinner at 7 — Ed.ws \+ Mary Cook. Emma Ireland \+ Cha's. Collins dined with us — It seemed to me that the eve'g passed pleasantly. All away at 10.30

## Right Page

### Sat. April 4, 1896

**Weather:** High wind \+ very cold (for the season)  **Temp:** [illegible]

High wind \+ very cold (for the season) Got to studio at about 10 — \+ spent the m'g making another lilly study. Ralph spent most of the m'g with me. Lunch — \+ back. I fussed over some other things of no importance in the afternoon — took a short walk — home at 5.30 — had a rest before dinner — \+ after dinner read to the boys. Charl came in — all very much interested in the case of young de Bischoffs, who has had a relapse \+ to-morrow will will undergo an operation. Laparotomy — At'g I went to Century — monthly meeting — had a pleasant time until 11 — Then home.
