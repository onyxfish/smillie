## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 11, 1896

**Weather:** Dull day, light rain in the afternoon — clear & mild at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

It was 10 o'clk when I got to studio. Continued my work of yesterday on Site Report \+ a paper that I think I shall have printed to go along with it. Home at 1 — Ralph came in from school complaining of a headache & evidently out of sorts — He slept a part of the p.m. \+ in the eve'g I gave him a dose of Rhutan \+ Soda. Carpenter at studio for awhile on little jobs. I did some work on my ainting. Dinner at 6.30 Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. did not come in until after 9 o'clk. I wrote notes to Beckwith & Nicole.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 12, 1896

**Weather:** Another dull day & more light rain in the afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio at 10 — Trouble with the batteries that run the bells & front door catcher interfered with my work. I had to get an electrician who repaired the trouble. I made up my mind to concoct a letter out of the different papers & reports that I have read before the N.A.'s concerning the site on 116th S. \+ Boulevard opposite Columbia University Sq. — to have it printed & sent to the N.A.s as a supplement to my report, because in the report which is printed for the Academy I could not plead as freely as I wished. To lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 — worked on my letter until 3.30 — then walked up to W. 47th S. to Dr. Allen's & got piece for lower jaw — then walked to 57th S. & 6th Ave. — hoping to see Carroll Beckwith — waited awhile for him. Then left a note for him & walked home. Dinner at 6.30. Had invited J. C. Nicole to dine with us but got a note of excuse from him — Elg. Oaky Cook dined with us.
