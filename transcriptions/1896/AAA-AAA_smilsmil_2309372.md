## Left Page

### Mon. July 6, 1896

**Weather:** Cloudy, muggy m'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy, muggy m'g., but before noon there was considerable sunshine. Chas. took a number of photos, about the place among others, some bicycle groups, taking us on our wheels as we circled over the lawn. Before 1 o'clk. we went over to the station + saw him off. Then home to dinner. Afternoons I worked for some time on miniatures of Mr. Comstock — much troubled by a distressing headache + general discomfort. Spent some time before tea transplanting lettuce. After tea went with R— to P.O. on getting home my head ached so severely that I went to bed + had a very uncomfortable night.

Heavily clouded afternoon with threats of rain — showers constantly passing south of us — but nothing came to us.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 7, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy + quite cool — many promises of rain — but late in the afternoon it cleared + a brilliant sunset put an end to hopes for rain. I got up feeling unrefreshed + dejected. As I was not in trim for work I spent the m'g in etching room — cleaning out the "dark room" + making solutions preparatory to developing films for the boys. Dinner at 1 — Jim absented himself without permission — dining with Paul Mulford. Most of the afternoon again in etching room. Planted some poppy seeds. Tea at 6.30 — A short bicycle ride with R— + then we went to P.O. Every with newspaper.
