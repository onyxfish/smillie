## Left Page

### Sat. March 28, 1896

**Weather:** Very pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day. A straight ahead day of work at the studio on painting x upon which I have been engaged for two days past — Some interruptions — Chas. A. Rett came with Miss Kendal to make enquiries at to where Etching materials could be bought. Home to lunch \+ back at 2 — Varied my painting work by a little pen exercise It was 6 o'clk before I got home. Swef. The whole family, except Ralph, were out. Jime went with Imma Ireland to the last of the Thomas Concerts. Charles and K. T. went to the same concert, \+ Mary, Kate, Bessie \+ I went to the Sally Akers Song Recital at Mendelssohn Glee Club Hall in 40th St. Sally had a very full house \+ an enthusiastic reception — She was assisted by Rich. Hoffmann the veteran who played with surprising snap \+ energy in duetts with Mr. Foote — Miss Geraldine on violin \+ Mackenzie Gordon, who has a very pleasant baritone voice — but his style of singing lacked sparkles. Sally has a fine voice which is in some ways very flexible \+ cultivated but, while she is a very agreeable singer, I think she has much to accomplish yet before she can take a really artistic \+ high place. It was after 11 when we all got home.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 29, 1896

**Weather:** Dark \+ wet  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark \+ wet. Heavy rain fell until noon. Bkfst at 9. I sat with my newspaper until noon \+ then to studio — \+ then, as it had ceased raining. I took a short walk — air very heavy — close \+ muggy. Dinner at 2 — wrote a couple of notes \+ then, from 4 to 6, had a nap. Tea at 6.30 \+ after that went to studio — Lived the fire there for the night \+ then home \+ read to the boys until 8.30 when they got to bed. The usual uneventful evg. at home. wrote some notes to Montrose \+ mailed them before I went to bed.
