## Left Page

### Wed. April 29, 1896

**Weather:** Clouds + sunshine \- mild & pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

immediately after 6ff. Kathemie, Bessie +
I rode down to foot of Fulton St. to the St.
Paul which sailed at 10 A.m. carrying Mr.
\+ Mrs. Wm Elkin on their wedding journey.
We saw them off in good style + then ret.
It was 11.30 before I got to work because
I had to get Rhome to repair batteries as the
studio bells would no longer ring. I got
to work on "Meadow Stream" + repainted
a considerable portion of centre in accord
with Saml. Coleman's advice — much to its
advantage. Lunch at 1 — Miss Hunt lunched
with the girls + Master Beale with Ralph.
Back at 2 + painted until 4.30 — Then
down to Sinier + Amend — 3d Ave. + 18th S. Then
to Charity Organization — 4th Ave. + 22d S.
Home to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g read to Ralph
after he got to bed. I got Chase's Christy
Bicycle Saddle (that I had got from his room
in the afternoon) + put in on my bicycle +
about 10 I went out for a ride — was out
for about an hour — riding about the streets
\— my cyclometer showed 7 miles —
It was 11.30 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 30, 1896

**Weather:** Charming bright, cool day  **Temp:** [illegible]

At the studio
I did a little last tinkering on the two
paintings that I expect to sent to Century
[illegible] on Sat. Then, as things in studio
had got to a terrible state of dirt and
confusion, I began to clean up. Home at
1 — back it 2 + during the afternoon
continued my house cleaning. At
5 I went to Sinier + Amend's to get
rubber tubing to fit out my acid room
at studio. Home at 6.30 — dinner —
Before 8 I went around to Saml. Coleman's
21 E. 39th St. for a few minutes — Home
at 8.30 — The boys got to bed late +
I got to bed rather earlier than usual.
