## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 23, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant in streaks  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant in streaks \- much of yesterday discomfort continued with me unfitting me for work \- much of the day was given to mounting photographs taken at [illegible] last summer \- Home at 5 p.m. and rested until dinner time. \- After dinner I went to Council meeting \- all present \+ an interesting meeting. Beckwith led a spirited discussion concerning the composition of the Jury of Selection \+ the Hanging Com. \+ the Possibility of something in the way of Reconstruction before the Spring Exhib. After adjournment site com. business was discussed. It was after 11 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 24, 1896

**Weather:** Cloudy for the most part, with a light shower before 2 o'clk  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy for the most part, with a light shower before 2 o'clk. My dyspepsia trouble continues making me feel unfit for any sort of activity, mental or physical. I podged about the studio all day \- got my blinds for Sticking \+ Printing table out \+ patched them where they were torn \- got out the plates of all kinds upon which I worked last summer \+ made preparations for work. After 4 o'clk I went down to 10½ St. Studio bldg \+ had a conference with Wood \+ Brown about the Academy business that is to come before the meeting to-morrow night \- From there I walked up home. Dinner at 6.30 \- Wolf. Geo. came (taking Charl's place, who is sick) bringing Emily Taylor \+ took K. \+ B. to the Garrick Theatre, on tickets that Charl bought \- At 9 I went to Ed. W. Cook's \+ spent the rest of the evelf there.
