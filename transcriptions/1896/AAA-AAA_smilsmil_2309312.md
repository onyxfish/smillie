## Left Page

### Sun. March 8, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A real March day — Sunshine and snow flurries. I got up at about 8 o'clk. apparently none the worse for my dissipation last night. At about 11 o'clk I went to studio — fixed fire \+ opened windows. Then went out for a walk with the boys but the weather was not sufficiently pleasant to invite to a long walk. We went to Century \+ took a look at the pictures \+ met some friends there up to 57th St. \+ home. Maynard came in at 2 \+ dined with us, leaving at 4. Then I lay down \+ by 6.30 had recovered much of the sleep lost last night. I read to the boys in the early evef as usual. Charl came in — much bunged up by his cold.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 9, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw \+ not altogether pleasant. I got up with a headache that increased as the day went on. I was able to work during the m'f painting on "Capri pm Kattes". But after lunch time I gave up \+ went to bed where I stayed until after 9 o'clk. Katie Adams took dinner with the family but I was not able to see her. After 10 o'clk I went downstairs to the parlor — there my headache had so far subsided that I was able to read. I sat in my easy chair until after midnight. After I got to bed I had but little sleep because I had had so much during the afternoon \+ early evef.
