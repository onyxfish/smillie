## Left Page

### Thurs. Feb. 13, 1896

*(Note at top: "This is Thursday. Feb 13th")*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A horrible day \- beginning with snow, followed by rain filling the streets with slush \- \+ riding it night with fog \+ rain. I got to studio about the usual time in the m'f \+ set to work on mezzo. ground n'f'd. Green plate \- Of this my arm soon tired \+ then I rested it by changing off work \+ scraping on "Tom Dean's Leaf. Lake George". I wrote a note to Edith Mc.Carthy, a belated acknowledgment of a memorial (book) that she sent to me, of her late husband \- To lunch \+ back as usual \- About 3 o'clk Walter Cook came, soon followed by Lamb, the builder of the studio bldg who had with him a young man, expert in fighting water \- we went into the cellar \+ spent some time there studying over the trouble \- I desire a cemented trench made close to the wall on three sides of the cellar, where the water comes in, leading into the trap that I had put in some time ago. But the three objected to that plan \+ agreed with the expert that the best way would be to "waterproof" the cellar by some process of cement \+ asphalt on a low brick wall at a cost of about $250 \- I am much disturbed by their decision because it does not commend itself to me as the best plan \+ because I have not confidence enough in myself to oppose them \- Dinner at 6.30, A quiet even'g at home.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 12, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- To-day is, for the first time in this state, a legal holiday, being Lincoln's birth-day. At the studio I divided my time in the m'f. between writing to J. C. Nicoll \+ Rocking mezzo. ground on Peat. Greene plate. To lunch \+ back it usual time. Afternoon, more mezzo-rocking \+ some more writing \- business notes. The work with the Rocker is so monotonous, mechanical \+ tiresome that I cannot do it continuously \- Vedder came to the Maynard studios in company with a very pleasant voiced lady (her face was veiled so that I saw it but imperfectly) \+ he could not open the door \- I had to come to his aid \+ after some bother managed to open it \- I got but a short walk before dinner \- Edna \+ Tom Cook dined with us. Charl came later in the even'g \- My head troubled me \+ I got to bed early \-
