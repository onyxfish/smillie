## Left Page

### Sun. April 19, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hazy day \- not so uncomfortably warm as yesterday \- Bkfst. at 10 \+ loafed all the rest of the mng. Dinner at 2 \- I read a little \+ soon after 4 I lay down \+ had a series of naps until 6 \- so the day was really given over to loafing \- After tea, my bicycle being in shape to use, I went out. The first time that I have mounted it since we left Montrose last fall. \- Being pretty stiff from Rheumatism \+ half blind in the dark, of course I got a fall \- \+ then went on. I had put a new saddle of Jim's on \- it hurt me \+ I soon ret\- Later I again went out with my own saddle, but that hurt me too, so I took but a short ride.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 20, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible — Thunder storm about midnight]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cleaner air \+ altho' warm, a very pleasant day \- To studio at 10. Douglass' man at Studio finishing the new work in closet by giving it a couple of coats of shellac \+ rubbing his presence \+ work made it impossible for me to paint, as I had planned, so, as I had letters ahead of me still unanswered. I wrote them. Home to lunch \- after that, young Rankin, real estate agent, came in to ask my friendly influence in case the N.A. by a strange freak, should approve of lots in 57th S. alongside of the Fine Arts Socy. as a site \- Afternoon, at Studio, I wrote to Apollos Stone giving him detailed directions on Montrose garden. Alice Ireland called \+ paid me for frame \+ photo. that she gave to Catherine Adams \- Old Wallace Scott came in about the Comstock miniatures that he is doing for Kurtz for me \- He is much disgruntled because I did not accept the one he has done \- At 4.30 I went to P.O. Station H. \+ got money order $1.50 to pay Orlando Geele, Road Tax, Keane lot \- Home \+ had a rest before dinner. Eve'g. went to Council meeting where I had the hardest fight yet, opposed by Pat Hart \+ Thos. Moran in an ignorant \+ insulting manner \- but by a firm, unyielding course I secured my place upon the com. which they tried to deprive me of. to report upon a te\- \+ 116th S.\- \+ my plan of temporary buildings.
