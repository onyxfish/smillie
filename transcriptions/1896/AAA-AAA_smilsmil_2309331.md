## Left Page

### Wed. April 15, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very hot day — most of it spent at home finishing my paper for Academy meeting this eve'g. The carpenter was still at work in my studio and went there to see how he was getting on — About 4.30 I went up to Sherwood Bldg \+ saw Beckwith for a few minutes, \+ then Blashfield, where I made quite a visit. I talked with both men about the 116th S\. S\. ete — then walked down to the Century \+ there met Howard Butler \+ had a talk with him — Home at 6.30 — dinner — At 8 o'clk. at Academy — The meeting was not a very good one — Many of the men that I wished to be there were at the "Shaw" dinner — but, altho' I did not have the audience I wished I said that I would have no better opportunity to present my scheme in connection with acts in 116th St. The ire \+ impatience of Tom Moran, directed against me betrayed him into an excess of gross bad manners when he interrupted me. I tried to prevent my finishing the reading of my paper — but the members shouted to me to go on and I did — \+ later, I beat him at every turn by getting a substitute for the Perry resolution that he urged, passed with but two opposing votes — his \+ Perry's — giving me a com. to report upon the 116th S\. ete.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 16, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A prostrating day — three deaths in the streets \+ several prostrations from the heat. I took things very easy after last night's work. The Tribune reporter called but I had nothing to tell him — I did some writing — back notes —
The afternoon was a continuation of the mtg. \+ the eve'g was no improvement — Charles was in for a part of the eve'g — very much crowded by all sorts of duties — his business — his care of old Mr. de Bisschop \+ the body of young de B — the anticipated arrival of the sister Mme. Bernot \+ her husband \+ his Mexican trip upon which he is to start to-morrow
