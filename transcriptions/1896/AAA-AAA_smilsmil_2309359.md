## Left Page

### Wed. June 10, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy showers, with thunder & lightening during the m'g. The work of flagging in Sniffen Court went on with interruptions. I went to Borrmann's with bicycle saddles — then to Nat'l Storage Co. \+ arranged for deposit of Silver ware. To studio — then to 2d Nat. bk \+ arranged my affairs there until Nov. 1st. Paid of the loan made Nov. 19th '95 — then to Putnams — saw Bishop P. \+ invited his son Robert to make him a visit at Montrose — to Palletz art store \+ bought supplies — home to dinner at 1 — Before 3 I went to Columbia Univ. to see Pres. Low — at home with a strained leg — By telephone he asked me to call there — so I went up to Mad. Ave. \+ 64th St \+ had a pleasant conversation concerning N. A. [illegible] — [illegible] \+ the University — I want to see him to know to what extent I might go in my Report to be printed, making statements positive as to the willingness of the N. Y. Hospital trustees to sell. Then I ret'd to studio — delightful afternoon — with the pavers — home at 5 o'clk — Dinner at 6.30 — every Charl came \+ took many of the girls to see \+ hear Pinalare — Geo \+ Nellie said — in for a few minutes — after getting R. to bed I spent sometime in reading over, carefully, Charl's scheme for construction for Sauce Creek Cattle Co.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 11, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day, brilliant, breezy \+ cool — Got to studio about 9.30 — found pavers at work laying the flagging in the court — I put the newly bronzed letter box plates on the front door and the bolt on Maynard's Elevator shaft door. Then home \+ at about 11, with Ralph I went to the bank \+ drew $100 — Then we went to Hoboken where I bought tickets to Montrose for the family. Home at 1 — dinner — it was 2.30 when again I got to see what the pavers were doing in Sniffen Court — found that the iron grating for sewer opening is too heavy — Stone not thick enough to give a strong rabbit joint — had to go to Central Iron Co. in 30th St. \+ order another one of 78" iron — Then to Station 7th \+ changed address — I then to Am Art Ass'n to see Dondinge — just missed him — Then to Keeh's \+ bought three shirts — Then rode up to Borrmann's — 84th Ave and 444th S't. He had fitted a Messenger saddle on my wheel — I tried it up the Ave. for half a mile \+ back \+ liked it well enough to buy it — then to G. C. Station \+ telegraphed to Jim to Return Friday — Then home \+ wrote some notes — Dinner at 6.30 — Every. I went to 33½ E. 17th St \+ called on the Ireland's \+ found them all in — I took with me some of Smilie's recently discovered diaries of European trips \+ camping expeditions from which I read extracts \+ then we Reminissed — Left at 10 o'clk. \+ walked home.
