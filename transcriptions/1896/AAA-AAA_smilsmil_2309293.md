## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 26, 1896

*Annie died This day, one year ago.*

**Weather:** Mild \+ pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild \+ pleasant day \- B\&P. at 9 \- after that, the newspaper until nearly \- then to studio \- were for more than an hour \- Then walked up to Central Park and back \- in time for dinner at 2. After dinner agains went to studio \- closed the windows \- fixed the furnace and started out for another walk. I went up to 72° S. \+ back \- a four mile walk \- getting home in time for tea at 6.30. Evef, Geo. \+ Nellie came in \- also Chat \- They stayed until 10.30 \- So, I did not get any letter writing done.

For some days past I have dreaded the approach of this day. It has depressed me \- altho' each day \- or very hour of day \+ night \- is, in a sense, an anniversary.

I do not feel now the acute agony that I felt that terrible evef, one year ago, but pain of loss \- the misery of utter lonesomeness is as constant \+ severe as ever it was.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 27, 1896

**Weather:** A very pleasant day \- clear \+ rather crisp  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day \- clear \+ rather crisp. To studio at 9.30 \- K. T \- Spent the mf there. Elig. Home called, bringing Mr \& Mrs. Bowles of Boston. He is editor of "Modern Art" a quarterly pub\. of Pranje's there. I worked on mezzo\- ground laying all the mf. Home to lunch at 1 \- before I was through, Barmore came \+ Together we went to studio \- He had the Lee Plate \+ making some suggestions I worked on it all the afternoon \- Then a short walk before dinner at 6.30 \- Evef, Council meeting \- full \- all apparently in very good humor \- little business but much conversation going to prove that N\. A., was a very liberal \+ progressive institution. walked up with Geo \- just before 11.
