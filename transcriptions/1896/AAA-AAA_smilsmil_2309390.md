## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 11, 1896

**Weather:** T  **Temp:** [illegible]

Blistering hot with a breeze that, later in the day, was quite refreshing. Beautiful sky at sunset \+ very quiet eve'g. After my duties in the m'f. I went to Village Hall \+ was there until dinner time stretching a white glazed cloth over the movable wings that I have had made for the stage there. After dinner my headache so far got the better of me that I had to lie down. I had a nap \+ got up at 4 o'clk. feeling better. Went to the Hall \+ worked there at the screens until near 7 — but did not get them all covered. Eve'g. the young people at the Hall rehearsing until near 11 — I sat on piazza until near 9 — after that, read newspaper — went to bed before the return of the young people.

no one at work in swamp lot to-day

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 12, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Play at Village Hall.

Still very hot — in the m'f. rumbling thunder but refreshing breezes in the afternoon \+ eve'g. It was 11 when I got to Village Hall, but, rehearsals interfering, I could cover but one screen before dinner — Afternoon, the same interference prevented me finishing the last screen before 5 o'clk. At about 8 o'clk. all hands went to the Hall. About 8.30 the performance commenced — "A pair of Lunatics" — J. Mc.Causland \+ Bessie Throop. "As Strangers" — Carleton Cook \+ Margaret Buck — "The Other Man" by Wm. Warner \+ Miss Cromwell. There was a full house \+ the whole affair went off with great eclat — Ralph appeared in the last piece as "Messenger 114" having but two or three words to say — At 10.30 many, Kate, Ralph \+ I went home leaving the young people to dance — the floor having been cleared. They followed at a little after 11.

Three Italians at work filling in ditches to-day.
