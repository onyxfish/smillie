## Left Page

### Sun. July 26, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, altho' during much of it the day was overcast as tho' another storm was brewing. It was noon before I went to studio to do some letter writing. Dinner before two — then some more writing. Later I went out to lettuce beds \+ sowed seeds for different varieties \+ transplanted a few heads. Tea at 6.30 — After that prowled about the place until dark, looking after garden matters etc. Then, after getting Ralph to bed I wrote a letter to Mr. Jas. Morse. At 10 went to P. O. with letters, then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 27, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A light shower in the m'f's at about 8 o'clk after which it cleared very hot. At about 2 a thunderstorm came up \+ perhaps half an hour later there was a tremendous wind storm with torrents of rain. A great deal of damage was done to shade trees in town. I worked for awhile in the m'f. in Studio's making crayon studies of Poppies — Dinner at 12.30 so that the girls, Mollie, Jean, Kathenie \+ Bessie could get away at 1.30 for a picnic drive to Silver lake. The storm came in less than an hour after they left — they got into a barn \+ there they stayed until it blew over — then on to Silver Lake — Rowed on the lake \+ home at 8, having had a good time, of course. I worked again on Poppy sketches — Tea at 6.30 — At 8 went down town — After R— got to bed I read to him at long from Aug. St. Nicholas.

Some Italians worked in the ditches — they say they made 6 h. each.
