## Left Page

### Fri. June 26, 1896

**Weather:** Clouded mt.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded mt. \- clearing rapidly \- glorious afternoon \- I got up with severe headache \+ sore, hammered feeling all over and continued to feel very uncomfortable all day. \- Much of the mt. was absent writing \- wrote first draught of a letter to Mr. Church one of the Editors of the Sun upon the subject of alliance between Columbia University \+ the N.A. of D. Some more writing in the afternoon \- got letter to Church. finished. Before, Ralph \+ I went to P.O. \+ other places \+ then walked out to Jones' Lake hoping to meet Jim, who had been there fishing, but he had gone. \- Home at tea time. Jim very proud of three fish caught by him one weighing 1/2 lb \- He \+ Ralph ate them. [illegible] \- as usual \- time wasted \- Reading newspapers \-

## Right Page

### Sat. June 27, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- after bkfs. wrote a note or two, then down town where a good part of the mtf. was spent. I got a note from Mr. Coon with M.S. of a short article that he has prepared for the Tribune on the subject of alliance between Columbia University \+ the N.A. of D. He wishes my approval. Dinner at 1. Immediately after it, J.R. \+ I went down town on our wheels. I went with J. to two dentists to have a loose tooth extracted but both of them were out. \- Then J. \+ R. went off on their wheels \+ I went with Cooley to his house to look at a new \+ very pensive water-closet that he has just had put in \- it makes too much noise to suit me. Then home \- painted the Aqua-tint box \- Tea at 6.30 \- The boys \+ I went to P.O. after 8 \+ did not get home until 9 \- I read awhile after they got to bed.
Mary quite under the weather with one of her vertigo attacks.
