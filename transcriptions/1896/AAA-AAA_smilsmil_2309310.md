## Left Page

### Wed. March 4, 1896

**Weather:** Bright, but very cold with strong N.W. wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, but very cold with strong N.W. wind. Spent the day repairing a painting by J.W. Shruinger, given to me by Mrs. E., after his death. in size 24" x 36" it represents a monk kneeling in church another monk, in shadow, playing upon an organ — much of the paint had scaled from the canvas. At 4.30 I went to Kurtz's about Prisalla Coom's miniature \+ then into the A.A.A. galleries where there is a great collection of oriental rugs, owned by Douchin, of Hartford, to be sold. Dinner at 6.30 — a stupid eveg. \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 5, 1896

**Weather:** Bright & clear — still windy but growing quieter & milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & clear — still windy but growing quieter & milder. To studio at 9.30 but many things kept me from getting to work until nearly 11 — Then, painted until 1 on Shruinger's painting — lunch — Immediately after I went to 2° Nat°. Bk & made deposit. Then to A.A.A. Galleries — 6 E. 23°. where a collection of oriental Rugs made by Douchin of Hartford, Conn. — commenced at 3 o'clk. I hoped to pick up some bargains altho' there were no Rugs that especially took my fancy — I met Miely Cook There. I stayed for nearly an hour & then, after having made two or three efforts I gave it up. Went to Blackner's in 28° S°. & saw some Glasgow paintings — a style of art with which I have little sympathy — then to studio and painted on the Shruinger for about an hour then home at 5.30 & dressed. At 6.30 Chase came with a young Frenchman Mr. Bischofs & we had dinner. He (Mr. B.) was very pleasant & made an agreeable impression — about 8 they left, taking the Throops girls with them to a theatre. I had a hard time writing a letter to S.R. Kothler I could not write as I wished to — it was after 11 before I finished it — then took it to Grand cent. Station & mailed it.
