## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 24, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Storm of wind + rain - wind so severe that Ralph turned back when on his way to school, + came home with his umbrella almost wrecked + himself quite demoralized. I got to studio at 9.30 - finished first stage of an experimental mezzotint ground + then set palette + spent the rest of the m'f painting a little sketch from which I expect to make an experimental mezzotint. Home to lunch at 1 - after it a rest until Jim came in from school. - Two Miles boys came + at a little after 3 - the M. boys, Jim, Ralph + I went to the Bicycle Show. In spite of the stormy weather there was a great crowd there + I had a hard time dragging the three boys about + keeping them together. It was near 5.30 when we left - home, + then to Studio where I washed brushes etc. + home to dinner at 6.30. The usual ev'f at home. Some reading to the boys until their bed-time - then some desultory reading or writing for myself until my bed time.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 25, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant — tried out not sunny. After [illegible] I went to Kimmel + Voigt in [illegible] St. + had young Voigt tackle the problem of taking for me such a proof from the dee plate as I wished - not too clear, or hard, on the one side - or not too heavily toned on the other. He soon got about what I desired. - Mr. Barmore came in + he was so well pleased that he accepted the plate + gave orders to have the parchment p.p. taken as early next week as possible. - To Studio at 12 - tracked down wet p.p. + mussled myself over the p.p. - Lunch - afternoon I got well advanced on an experimental mezzotint ground + became so much interested that I worked as long as I could see.
