## Left Page

### Sun. June 14, 1896

**Weather:** Very cool - overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cool - overcast m.f. threatening rain but it cleared charmingly in the afternoon. I awoke about 4 a.m. feeling very headachy \- took bromo-caff - did not get up until 9 \+ had a late bkfs. Then, until almost 2 I wandered over the place. To me "Ichabod" is written upon everything - The boys, \+ Mary's girls are delighted but to me it is all very depressing \- There is a dreary vacuity that nothing on earth can fill. I got into the garden \+ gathered Asparagus, Rhubarb \+ Radishes \- After dinner I resumed my wanderings \+ kept about until after 5 when feeling very tired \+ with a stern headache I lay down until tea time. A quiet evef alone \+ to bed about 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 15, 1896

**Weather:** Charming day - very cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - very cool - I got up with a headache that bothered me all day, altho' I managed to work persistently \- Freight \+ bicycles came in the m.f. \+ I unpacked getting things distributed \- I went down town \+ did some marketing, etc - etc \-
Dinner at 1 \- after that for some time with Spence getting rusty trunks into the attic \- Then, getting my things for workroom, studio \+ working room separated \- About 5. feeling very tired I lay down \+ had a little nap \- Eve. at 6.30 The boys had a great frolic with foot ball on the lawn \- Eve f. Mr. Cromwell called \+ Mary \+ I went down to Judge Searle's to attend a meeting of the Executive Com. of the Village Imp. Soc. \- No quorum of course \+ only desultory talk \- Before 10 Dav. Sayre \+ Mrs. Williams \- Mr. Sayre \+ I walked up the hill \- we escorted Mr. W. to Mr. Beach's house \+ then the S's left me at my lane entrance.

Rec'd. a telegram (sent last friday) from Ella Liven announcing the death of her father on Thurs.
