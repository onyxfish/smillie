## Left Page

### Thur. May 21, 1896

**Weather:** Wet all day  **Temp:** 66°

Wet all day, but I don't think that much rain fell. Cold, as compared with the torrid weather of the past few weeks. 66°
in my studio where I painted all day.
I worked over the foreground of an old
painting "A Pond". Late P.M. effect — based
upon a study on Jones' Lake — Montrose —
Then I worked for some time on "Caferi,
from Naples", \+ wound up the day with a
couple of hours work upon the Twilight in
the woods, an old painting which I re-
painted during the winter. Moses Smith
called about N.A. bus. Farrington came in
Academy business. No progress made
in the matter of engraving \+ having my part
of Sniffen Court — I had to write to Mollie Adams
deferring proposed visit to New Haven.
Evg., so used up by a peculiar
sort of headache that came on after
dinner that I was quite useless.
Edwd Cook took Jim with him \+
Tom C — to the Electrical Exbn.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 22, 1896

**Weather:** close m.f., overcast, but growing rapidly warmer \+ clearing  **Temp:** [illegible]

Close m.f., overcast, but growing rapidly
warmer \+ clearing. Immediately after
bkfst went to Dr. Hale's \+ consulted with
him concerning Ralph's condition which
is not satisfactory to me. Then to studio
getting there after 10 o'clk. Mr. Tolfree
called \+ together we looked at the paving in
the Sniffen Court. I got to work painting
"Twilight in the wood" until lunch time.
Then home. Then to 2° Nat. Bk. — Then
to Kurtz's about miniature for Priscilla Paine —
Then in to look at Donahue's Rug collection —
Then to N.A. — met Perry there \+ had a talk with
him about N.A. elections. The mystery as
to who put up Alfred Jones to run as 1st asst.
me, \+ who the 21 men are who voted for him,
grows deeper to me. Perry declares that
it had nothing to do with his "down town ticket" \+
was managed by someone unknown to him.
To studio at 3.30 \+ worked until after 5. Then
home \+ had a short rest before dinner. Evg.
at about 8 o'clk. Bessie, Jim \+ Merrill Bishop
went off on their wheels. Soon after Ralph
\+ I started on ours. R. \+ I went up to 58th
St. across to the Boulevard — then by 72° St.
to Riverside Drive \+ up to about 82° St. —
then home, getting in at about 9 o'clk.
Bessie \+ Jim in ahead of us. I gave
R. a sponge bath \+ rub down — \+ got him
to bed — Then I gave myself a rub down
\+ got to bed shortly after 10.
