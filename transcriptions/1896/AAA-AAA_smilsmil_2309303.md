## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 15, 1896

**Weather:** Bright & very pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & very pleasant — to studio's soon after 9. Found the men at work in the cellar where they continued all day until after 6 p.m. making satisfactory progress. Katie T. \+ Ralph spent the m'f at the studio. Barmore sent his boy up with 3 hee [illegible] I signed 30 — one was too poor to sign. I worked pretty steadily on Greene Mezzo. ground. Home to lunch at 1 & back at 2. Mr. Sally Smerson called, bringing a young girl with her. Ella Beach sent an invitation to me to dine with her at 6.30 to which I sent regret. Home to dinner at 6.30. Jim Ret'd from his Morse visit at noon — did not appear at dinner — after 7 I went to Ed'd Cook's & found him there with a little party of Tom's friends celebrating Tom's 13th birthday — of this we had had no fore-warning. Susy, Mary, Kate, & the two girls went to Opera. After Ralph went to bed I went to Century & heard an entertaining paper read by W. C. Brownell on "Rodin & his Sculpture". I enjoyed the discourse but could not enthuse over the examples as shown by lantern — Home before 12.

Green 1 day.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 16, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow in the m'f, — clearing about noon. Wind & growing much colder at night. Bkfst. at 9. Went to studio before 11 — there for an hour or more — then walked until almost dinner time at 2 o'clk. Soon after 4 I lay down \+ had some solid sleep until 6.30 — Tea — Ev'f. The usual reading aloud to the boys until their bedtime — then I went to studio & attended to matters there & back in a few minutes. Charle came in — I spent some time over my accts. for 1895.
