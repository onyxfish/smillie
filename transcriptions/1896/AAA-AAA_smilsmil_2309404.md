## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 8, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Remarkably brilliant day \+ very warm. I put in a good days work on dipp. head, finishing the work on the face \+ using big graver on hat which alone remains to be worked into shade when the plate will be as far as I can carry without a proof. At noon Ralph \+ I rode on our bicycles out to the chestnut trees on the top of the hill, back of "little Depot" \- 3½ miles out \+ back. Dinner. worked from 2.45 to 4.30, then went into pasture lot to see how the clearing preparatory to plowing is getting along. Then transplanted some lettuce \- Tea. \- Evelf. Geo. \+ Emily were entertained for an hour \+ more by Katherine whistling, Bessie accompanying on piano.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 9, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another day like yesterday. Bkfst. at 7. At 7.30 most of the family went to R. R. station with Geo. \+ Emily to start them on their homeward way. Got to work in studio at a little after 10 on dipp. \+ had \+ stuck to it until almost 1 \- Dinner \- Misses Martin \+ Hunt, friends of K. \+ B., dined with us. Miss Hunt took tea \+ spent the evelf. with us. I worked on plate from 3 until nearly 5. Many made known to me some of her troubles in guarding her girls against disreputable young fellows who enjoy the privileges of "good Society". Tea at 6.30 \- a stupidly quiet evelf.
