## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 26, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Lovely day. I went on with etching of Mr. G. \+ in the m'f completed laying in the lines. Afternoon, I revised it \+ varnished it \+ the two other plates preparatory to biting. I am in a state of encouragement \+ hope. I wrote some business letters \+ did some carpenter work, preparing a staging from which I can prune \+ train the vines about the piazza. Went down to pasture to see how the clearing work there is progressing \+ then on to P. O. Ralph and Sanford Mulford out late. Pettis' Creek fishing \- worry for nothing. After tea read to the boys until near 9 — To bed early.

Mr. Gerster 1 d.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 27, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day \- mild, sunny, hazy \- color very charming. I took it easy in the m'f \+ did not have off. until 10. The rest of the m'f. given to wandering about the place — training \+ trimming piazza vines \+ a little writing. Dinner at 2. In the afternoon Tom, Jim, Ralph \+ I went off for a tramp — across lots past D. Dunton's down into the valley on to the Silver Lake R?. \- then down to the brook \- south \- then up the hill past Hawley's \- more cross lots, \+ home at 6.15 — Tea — Read to the boys until 8.30 — wrote a note to Emily Cook \+ sent 3 photos. of the house. To P. O. at 9. 30 — then to bed
