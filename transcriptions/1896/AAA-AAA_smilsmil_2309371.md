## Left Page

### Sat. July 4, 1896

**Weather:** Cloudy, clearing  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy, clearing, slight showers in the n'f
\+ some smart showers at night. with far
more of pleasant weather than foul \+ yet,
with enough of the latter to seriously mar
the sport of the day for many. Operations
on the fair ground began as early as 9.30
Explosions \+ yells had continued, at intervals,
thro' the night. The n'f was about, after
duties, in sauntering about — dinner at 1 —
At about 2.30 we locked up the house \+
all went to the fair ground — great crowd
for this place — The day's entertainments were
planned by \+ for the benefit of one of the Town
Hose Co's. Programme very imperfectly carried
out — The fiasco of a balloon ascension was
ended by a shower at about 5 o'clc.
Tea at about 7 — Charl, the boys \+ I went to the
R.R. station at 8 \+ saw Carpenter about tickets.
Then to P.O. etc. — Another attempted balloon
ascension from pont of jail at about 9 o'clk.
another shower — The boys to bed late
\+ after that we sat up chatting
until after 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 5, 1896

**Weather:** Cloudy, close \+ warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy, close \+ warm — showers south
p.us — we had a good deal of sunshine
\+ late bkfs. Then in garden \+ like
duties until after 11. Dinner at
2 — loafing about with Charl \+ the
boys most of the afternoon — some
time playing about on our bicycles
under the trees \+ on the grass — I
spent some time clearing up things
in dark room preparatory to developing
the pictures taken by the boys with
their cameras. Tea at 7 — at 8 we
went to P.O. with letters.
