## Left Page

### Sun. May 31, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. B'kf't. at q.30 - a brief m'g reading newspapers & doing a little writing. An early dinner - 1.30 - Immediately after, Jim, Ralph & I went to G. C. Station to catch 2.20 train to Fordham but found that it did not stop there - we waited & took the 2.45 train & got to Fordham at about 3'5. Then we walked about a mile due east & entered Bronx Park - we wandered along the west side of the River going north, crossed a bridge to the east side & stopped at McDvilland Ho. where the boys had soda water - then on down, skirting the River all the way, the boys having a fine time. It was all that could be wished for & the way of exhilarating air, sunshine, fragrance, bird notes, etc, etc — but these charms, & the joy of the boys, only served to make me feel more keenly the absence of Annie, who would have enjoyed it all so much in her happy, healthy way. We walked to West Farms & there took Trolly car to Harlem - 20 minutes - then 3d Ave. elevated to 42d St- getting home shortly before 7. Tea - A little reading to the boys - then Geo. S— came in, & later, Nellie — They stayed until 10.30 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 1, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day - to studio at 10 and worked on the two crayon heads of Mrs. Gerster - modifying both of them very much - I worked freely upon the first one because I did not care whether that was destroyed or not - & that recklessness was to the advantage of the work - Another blue stone man, or [illegible], came & I spent some time with him explaining what I want done in the lane. He promises an estimate in the m'f. After lunch I went to the bank made deposits & had my check, payment of transfer, or legacy tax, certified - stopped at Minty's & got dee- of Mr. Comstock - Then to studio & [illegible] more work on crayon heads - home at 4- Grace Orland called to see the boys - it was about 4.45 when Jim, Ralph & I got away on our wheels - we went up to Central Park - up on the east side of it, out at 106' S- on the west & over to Riverside Ave- then home by the Ave- the Boulevard -58' S- & Madison Ave- Ralph had a narrow escape from being run down by a horse car in 42d S- Dinner at 7.15 Before we left the dinner table Walter Cook came in & later Louise joined him — They made a pleasant, chatty call, leaving before 10 - Soon after, I went to bed.
