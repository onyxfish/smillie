## Left Page

### Sat. May 9, 1896

Special N.A. \+ D. meeting in the eve.

**Weather:** Clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear day growing to be very warm. I took my time \+ did not get to studio until after 10. Then I worked for awhile on Treas. Report \+ then completed the compilation of my remarks for to-night. To lunch at 1 \+ back at 2.30 \+ worked for a little while. Then home \+ had a very comfortable nap before dinner — Geo. came in at about 8 — bringing a Water-Color sketch as a birth-day present to Sister Kate. He \+ I went to the N.A. A very interesting meeting tho' not a large one. I felt very well \+ was able to take my part in the performances. I was much encouraged by the indications that there is a growing approval of the 116th S. Site. Vedder gave the meeting a surprise by presenting a plan of the lots to the west of the Fine Arts Socy in 57th S. (my old site) \+ a nebulous suggestion that somebody wanted to give the academy about $200,000 of the N.A. was any there — but wished to have an expression from the N.A. before any thing more definite could be said — In reply a resolution of interest, equally nebulous, was passed. I think that it is some new scheme of H.R. Butler's.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 10, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot day — exceedingly uncomfortable — I did not get up until near 9 \+ had my bkft. alone at 10 — then loafed with newspaper until near 1 — then to studio \+ back before dinner at 2. After dinner I went to N.A. where the Council met to judge the School drawings — It was very trying work but we all took off our coats \+ worked faithfully until we got thro' before 5 o'clk. The great mass of the work in the different classes seemed to me poor \+ unpromising. J.G. Brown walked up with me and stopped at the house for a few minutes to cool off \+ rest. Tea at 6.30. I read to the boys until 8.30 — got them to bed before 9 \+ soon after followed them.
