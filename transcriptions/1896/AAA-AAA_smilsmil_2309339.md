## Left Page

### Fri. May 1, 1896

*Annie's birth-day.*

**Weather:** Bright mng, clouded afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright mng, clouded afternoon — I got to studio about 9.30 \+ arranged with Maynard to take charge of my paintings that are to be exh'd at monthly meeting of the Century to-morrow night. Then I went to N.A. \+ saw Mr. Gardiner the expert accountant who is auditing N.A. accts. We went to 2d. Nat. Bank \+ examined securities there — \+ back to N.A. \+ checked off bonds \+ things in the big safe — I went into Kurtz's to see about miniature of Mr. Comstock — \+ so got myself into a mess in that business — the work so far produced I decline to accept — stone to lunch at 1 — The boys had gone with Bessie to the Mad. Sq. Gardelin Theatre to take their parts in a spectacular performance for a Charity. Before 2 I started for Greenwood, getting to the gate at 3 — walked out to our lot and spent some time at the side of Annie's grave — She would have been 52 years old to-day had she lived. At 4 I took train back \+ got to studio before 5 — Then home \+ rested before dinner — Eve'g at 8.45 went to Century Club \+ there met by appointment Carmel Beckwith \+ Harry Watrous, we three being a com. on site. We talked over the situation \+ decided that we would attempt to get a meeting of N. A's to consider a report from us on Sat. eve'g. g'ing. B \+ I are to work for that at Council meeting next Monday eve'g. Home just before 11.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 2, 1896

*Dedication services — New Site Columbia University.*

**Weather:** Overcast \& chilly  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \& chilly — At 10 o'clk. met P. W. Wood \+ Gardiner (the auditor) at Lincoln Safe Deposit \+ counted the U.S. Bonds in the N.A. box — Then to studio \+ spent some time writing upon site matters for new report — lunch at 1 — After that went to 116th S. \+ Boulevard where imposing \+ interesting ceremonies attended the Dedication of the new Site of Columbia University. Indulgence \+ accident co-operating gave me an excellent place among reserved seats. There was a vast audience under a tent that I think was, in extent, about 250' × 500'. I listened with interest \+ pleasure to Pres. Low \+ to the singing of the Dedication Ode \+ the music of Miller \+ [illegible] — but during Ex. Mayor Hewitt's address I got so chilly that I had to leave — I climbed about over the new Library building. I went out to terrace over Morningside Park \+ there met Blashfield, Beckwith \+ J. Q. Brown — we walked to 108th S. — Then I went on alone to Central Park — thro' it to Mad. Ave. \+ 82d. S. and home — a very short rest for dinner — Every'g went to Century — monthly meeting — my two paintings — a Meadow Stream \+ Alpine are worse than my worst fears — I met many friends \+ had a number of pleasant little chats — Home soon after 12.
