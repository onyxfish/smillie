## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 17, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Intensely cold day. Weather Bureau Reports Ther. at 6. 10° below zero. Cutting wind & clear. By getting water in my studio heating apparatus up to boiling I had my Room very comfortable. The men in the cellar got the concrete floor down & the cement finish down over about one half the cellar & then left it for two or three days to harden before moving contents of cellar & going on with the other half. I worked on my mezzo. ground & finished it to my great satisfaction. It has used up 6 days, or about 30 hours of actual work. At noon I went to 2? Nat. Bk. Then to lunch. Worked until about 5 o'clk. Then home & had a rest before dinner. We/I went to Council meeting. The Ste Com. did not put in an appearance, to my disappointment — but I got home early.

Katie & Bessie T. went to New Haven for a visit of a week at the Adams's.

Green 1 day

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 18, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold, but Reported to be 8° milder than yesterday. The wind was outl very cutting & added much to my discomfort, as I had a double barreled headache — a rheumatic one that covered the outside, a bilious one that filled the inside of my head. I worked on the Green Plate laying [illegible] ground, making it & making a vermilion transfer upon it of the tracing from my oil sketch. Mary (red) Cook came in for a few minutes in the mt. At 2 o'clk. I went to Kurtz's to left with him dag. of Mr. Comstock which I am having copied as a miniature for Priscilla Bent Corse. Thence to seek ing paintings & to Putnam's & bought a book "The Sun". Then to studio & in spite of headache I started scraping in backgrounds of Genl. Greene — but I ins obliged to give up at 4 o'clk & went home to bed. I was able to sit up for while in my chair but suffered a good deal. Mary & Kate went to Mendelssohn Concert. Charl brought them home.

Greene 1 day.
