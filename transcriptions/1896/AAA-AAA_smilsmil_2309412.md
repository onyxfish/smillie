## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 24, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day - cloudless, warm & hazy - I could not work in doors So Ralph & I went down town & did some shopping & some gadding about. It was 11 when I got back. Then I revised awhile on etching Hill Road near the lake - & I printed some photos. Dinner - & after that I toned the photo. prints & worked at pruning & arranging the vine over Studio piazza & then I took a short bicycle ride. Tea at 6.30 - Evg. I wrote to Ryan & to Water Bureau. N.Y. sending check for water tax for current year for 110 $. 38¢ & Studio $154 156 $. 36¢ - Went to P.G. at 10 - Wonderfully beautiful & brilliant moonlight night.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 25, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - quite mild, with a wind from the east that promises a change of weather. To-day I commenced the etching of portrait of Mr. Gerster. To my great joy everything ran along very smoothly - my big darning needle seemed to swiggle about over the plate without hitch or stick of any kind. I got from Kate a Spanish lace veil for folding it to fall like my drawing I etched from it. Between times, to rest myself, I worked at piazza vines, pruning & arranging them. Evg. great Rally of Republicans - Jno. Wanamaker spoke at the Armory - McCausland gave to Mary a card admitting her & the girls to the Cap but room - they went down before 8 - I followed soon after & saw a poor attempt at a torch light procession - when I got to Armory found Mary Kate & Bessie in the crowd unable to get into Cab't Room - it grew wild & left. By a little promptitude & cheek I saw Mary & Bessie, by a Rear door, onto the stage behind the wings. Wanamaker is a poor speaker both in manner & matter - to be sure, he was quite worn out - the crowd was great but aye very discouragingly irresponsive I thought. By 10 we were tired of it & beat a Retreat before W. ceased speaking. Stopped at Chas. Sayres - got h- & home.
