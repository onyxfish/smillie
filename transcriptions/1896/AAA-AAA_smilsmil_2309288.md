## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 16, 1896

**Weather:** Bright & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

This is the diary of Tues. Jany. 14. written upon this page by mistake.

Bright & cold. — Got to K. & R. about 9.30 & had three dee[p?] [illegible] taken. The trouble in getting the kind of [illegible] I want is not yet overcome. The fact is, the plate is over bitten — On my return to studio a Tribune Reporter came & interviewed me at some length on Academy Ste affairs. He plied me with many questions. I hope that I was not betrayed into saying anything unwise. — To lunch in time & back. Writing up Dites for an hour or more. Percival deSuce called — At 5 I went to Century expecting to meet H.W. Robbins, as I requested, but he denied me the courtesy of even a note. I saw there N.R. Butler & had a conversation with him. — Home to dinner. Evety. Read aloud to the boys — wrote to Rothler & attended to some business.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 17, 1896

**Weather:** Overcast m'f., clearing later  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mollie Adams with us.

Overcast m'f., clearing later. I got to Dr. Allen's, dentist, in W. 47th S. at 9. 40 & was in his chair for two hours. Then home & had a short rest before lunch — back again to Allen's at 2 & underwent torture until nearly 3.30 — He cut down a tooth & put a gold cap over it. After this ordeal I felt pretty well used up. I went to studio for a short time & true home & to bed — I had a nasp. but my whole face, & even my head, was so sore that I felt quite out of sorts. — Mollie breakfasted, lunched & dined with us & we all spent a very quiet evg. together.
