## Left Page

### Sun. June 28, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded m'g. clearing charmingly in the afternoon. Bkfst. at 8.30. after it \+ until noon I was out of doors, most of the time attending to garden matters. Then I wrote for awhile, part of a letter to M'. Coon of the Tribune. Dinner at 2. After that I had a nap. After that I finished my letter to M'. C. Tea at 6.30. Mary felt well enough to sit at tea table with us. After tea, Jim, Ralph \+ I walked about town until 8.

There was a pleasant shower or two during the day.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 29, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant m'g \- clouded at noon \- a slight shower \- clear afternoon \+ very cool at night. Busy for some time after bkfst., duties about the place. Then, about the middle of the m'g, I started work on miniature in water color of M'. Geo. Comstock. This is the miniature that I undertook to have done by Kurtz for Priscilla Remity Poore as a wedding present for her nephew Carleton Comstock, son of M'. Geo. C. After no end of vexatious delays the work was a failure \+ now, at last, I have undertaken to do it myself. Dinner at 1. Again in the afternoon I worked on the miniature, getting discouraged. I Rec'd. by Exp. from Priscilla, at Scranton, a large box of very fine Rhododendron flowers. About 5 I went out alone \+ had a 4½ mile ride on my wheel. It was near 9 when the boys got to bed \- out little m'g left after that for some desultory reading.
