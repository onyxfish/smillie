## Left Page

### Tues. April 21, 1896

**Weather:** Threat & sunshine — at times very warm —  **Temp:** [illegible]

Threat & sunshine — at times very warm — then cooler when it w. cloud up as though to rain — quite cool at night. Douglass' finisher worked at my closet all day the m'f finished at noon — I wrote notes on Academy matters etc — Home to lunch — after I went to Tract Soct. Bldg. Spruce & Nassau, Dep. Pub. works to see Engineer Towle — not there, but subsequently I found him in Chambers S. & was fortunate in getting a deputy, Jacobs, we ret. with me to 36° E. where together we consulted as to the possible remedies — He agrees to my plan of a shallow gutter or water-way across the sidewalk — I hope to be able to make it an accomplished fact. From 3.30 to 5 I worked putting pamphlets & books away in closet — Then home & had a rest. At 6.30 Tom Shearman came and surprised me by telling me that the trouble over the sale of the Eastruder Terrace house has broken out again & that possibly I may find myself involved in a law suit. This all delayed dinner until 7. Mary in bed quite sick — Dr. says it is Grip. Katie & Bessie took 11 o'clk bkfp. with de Bisschop & W. & Madam by invitation. We'f at 8.30 went to Memorial concert by Mendelssohn Glee Club in their hall — Joseph Voesenthal — Three of his pieces were given — "Blest Pair of Sirens" "The Music of the Sea" & "Thanatopsis." Addresses by H.E. Krehbiel & Dr. H.C. Potter — It was a beautiful tribute to a loved & noble memory [illegible — ~4 lines]

## Right Page

### Wed. April 22, 1896

**Weather:** clear, crisp & cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, crisp & cool — To studio at 10 — set palette & got to work again on "Meadow Brook" 19×30 & spent a good part of the day fussing over it — At 12 I went to Mad. Sq. Garden & bot. tickets, Barnum's Circus. for boys & self — Friday P.M. — then home, stopping at D. B. Butler's about [illegible]. Lunch — Then fixed up two little silver presents from the boys to be sent by post to Katie Adams, who is to be married to-morrow. Wrote a little note to her — then to 49th S. and called on Pres. Low at Columbia to tell him something of Site matters — then to studio and worked for a little while longer painting — [illegible] my head was very heavy — It was 6.30 when I got home to dinner — Eve'g went to U.A. Special meeting of Water Color Soct. to discuss the Raising of Standard of System. F. Hopkinson Smith made a characteristic speech in support of his resolution, to accept no picture for [illegible] consigned at a less value than $100 — & no deviation from cat. price to be allowed. It was agreed to almost without debate — out before 10 — [illegible] & I walked up together.
Mary in bed all day, perhaps no worse — apparently no better.
