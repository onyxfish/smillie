## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 21, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim returned from camp.

Clonded day \- milder with threats of rain \- a little shower about 6 P.M. \- I put in a good day's work in studio \- I made two very successful Transfers Tracings to etching grounds \- Mad. Sq. lower \- gelatine 4g×12 sketch "The road to the lake". After dinner all the family went over to Silver lake" leaving me solus \- I laid a ground on 9×12 etching of girl's head after a water color sketch by Lippincott. Then I worked until 5 o'clk. with crayon \+ charcoal on a proof from the plate. Then I went out on bicycles \+ had a 3 or 4 mile ride \- Young Mulford drove up with Jim very unexpectedly at about 6 \- we had our tea at 6.30 \- The rest of the family ret? from Silver lake soon after 8 o'clk. To bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 22, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'g \- clouds gathering \- wind again changing to S.E. with threats of rain \- a little ull before bed time \- Took a little turn on Jim's wheel before I began work in studio \+ again just before dinner \- I commenced work on plate after Lippencott's head \- a ground being laid, felling all etched lines I drew a large foul through such as I wished to rebite. Albert McCoy, who worked for us a part of the summer seven years ago, called with his father who lost his wife three weeks ago. At 5 o'clk I went out on my wheel \+ had a four mile ride \- came in so wet that I took sponge bath \+ made change of clothing. Tea at 6.30. Self Read awhile to Ralph \- him, newspaper \- Then, loafed until bed time.
