## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 21, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day overhead but I am so generally out of sorts that I could not work - so I went to Eimer & Amends pt some double distilled Spts. of turpentine to experim with in my painting - Then to 14th S. looking for a sewing chair for Mary - That I did not find - to Wunderlichs & looked at prints - Then to [illegible] Wilson's & saw him - Poor fellow, he is quite bl[illegible] - had a long talk with him on Real Estate wh as I wish to transfer some property in R. I. [illegible] to Real Estate. Then home to lunch - After[noon] at Studio, worked on a slight pen sketch [illegible] 9×12 - from a slight sketch taken in Paris - a bit on the Seine - Had a Rest before dinner - Eve'g. Councils meeting - Mro' with Council business at 9 - Then Nicals, Watsons & [illegible] came & the Accts Com. - had a session - The downtown sites having been pretty thoroughly discussed some sites above 57th St - were called up - I brought forward the 77th St. site - on South side of Manhattan Square - the discussion over it had not been completed when time for adjournment came at about 11 o'clk - By a close vote, 7 to 6, it was decided to adjourn until Jany 4th - After adjournment J. J. Brown made a very discreditable scene, losing his temper at one-half jokeing remark made by Perry. He charged R. with saying something that he really did not say & then called him a damned liar for saying it - It was disgraceful on Brown's Part - Perry kept his temper & nothing more came of it.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 22, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast most of the day - snow at night - on time at Studio and started the painting of sky on Vesuvius - It was a hard tussle - I have a very clear idea of what I wish to do but I have not the practice that would enable me to keep my tints clean on my palette - everything promptly degenerates into mud - after a hard days work I got over the sky + small part of distances only - At 4.30 I went to Putnam's & made selection of several books for Xmas presents - Home at 6.30 — a quiet eve'g at home Charlie came in - K. T. + Bessie arrived from Po'keepsie
