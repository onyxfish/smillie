## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 20, 1896

**Weather:** Disagreeable m'f., cold with mizzling rain  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable m'f., cold with mizzling rain — soon clearing to delightful, cloudless day — very quiet, brilliant moonlight night. After bkft. I was for some time at work as a stoker — made a fire in big stove in lower hall — one in studio \+ attended to the ones in dining \+ etching Room — Then, as it had cleared \+ the sun was shining brightly I got to printing from a number of the film negatives taken in cameras of the boys — Experience brought new light \+ I got such interesting results that I stuck to it until nearly 1.30 — Then I took a short walk to the top of the hill just beyond Dr. Dunton's — Dinner at 2 — About 3.30 Ralph \+ I walked over the hill, by Silver Lake Road to Pettus's brook \+ thence south by the Road to the next Road running east; the wooded Road running over the hill \+ through darkie-town home, getting in soon after 6. Tea at 6.30 — Five \+ f. Reading to Ralph — walking piazza, etc — etc — I have walked quite a good deal to-day but it has been a painful effort from the start. All of my bones \+ muscles ache — I no longer have elasticity or pleasure in movement. And mentally I think that I am in much the same condition that I am in physically.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 21, 1896

**Weather:** Very pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day. Worked all the m'f. on etching "Mad. Sq. Garden Tower" — more to my mind than the work done before — At 12.30 I went down town with Jim's camera \+ took snap shots at the soldiers mon." \+ the Court House. Immediately after dinner the boys, Tom, Jim \+ Ralph, went out to the lake fishing with Bostwick. I worked on my etching until about 4 — Then out \+ I worked on Plumb Trees until 5 — Then on bicycle to lake \+ saw Bostwick \+ the boys in boat. R. having left his coat on shore — weather cold — I then went on to P.O. Then home — had a rub down \+ rest — The boys did not come \+ at 7 I went out to meet them — moonlight — met them returning. Tea at 7.30 — Jos. Williams came in the eveg \+ gossipped with the girls
