## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 29, 1896

*[Note at top: Azur dattols died. Carleton Cook went to Buffalo]*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day — clear & bracing. Ther. fell to 46° last night. I worked with graver on diSpencatt head until noon. Then, with Ralph & the girls, went with Carleton to R.R. Station & saw him started on his way to Buffalo. Then home to dinner. Worked on head until 4.30 — then Transplanting lettuce for nearly an hour, then out on bicycle & had a 3½ miles spin — a short rest — a run down & tea after the others had theirs. Reading to Ralph & newspaper filled the eve'g, & to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 30, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little milder & more hazy than yesterday. B'k'f't at 9 — some loitering & then I spent an hour in the garden, among the fruit trees, & going over the place. Then I wrote a letter to Mr. Jas. H. Morse at Cotuit. Dinner at 2. The afternoon, from 3 to 6, spent trimming trees — After tea wrote notes to Chase \+ to Carleton Cook — To P.O. at 9.
