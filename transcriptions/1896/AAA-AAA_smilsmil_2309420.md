## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 10, 1896

**Weather:** Brilliant day \- very brisk crisp air  **Temp:** Ther. fell to 32°. last night.

No work on my etchings to-day. I spent some time in the mtf pulverizing some gum mastic preparatory to experiments with dry aquatint \- It was about 11.30 when Charl \+ I went out on our wheels \- his first experience upon our country roads \- we went 5 miles \+ he was disgusted \+ quite busted. We got in late for dinner \- After dinner I wrote to Little the nurseryman. N. Warner \+ assistant came at 8.30 \+ began painting \- various spots \+ bits most requiring paint \- About 4.30 \- Mary \- K. T. Charl \+ I went off for a little tramps \- across lots to Dunton's hill \- saw sunset there \- then home \- Ralph had been most of the day with Paul at the Mulford farm gathering Chestnuts \- Evel\+ some sleepy reading to R \- all hands sleepy. To bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 11, 1896

**Weather:** A pleasant day with warm sunshine a gentle movement of air from the N. E. \- a prevision of bad weather to come.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfs. at 9 \- at 11.30 Bessie, Charl \+ I went to fair ground with our wheels \- Rode 4¾ miles \- Back home at 1 o'clk I did some Tree trimming \- Dinner at 2 \- In the afternoon I had a long nap \- Evel\+ wrote to Jim, Maynard \+ Carole Beckwith \- About midnight Charl \+ I went to P.O. with letters.
