## Left Page

### Tues. March 10, 1896

*Kate went to Po'k.*
*Jim's new bicycle.*

Dull day with snow flying occasionally. Just enough to whiten the ground. I got up feeling but little worse for yesterday's experience. Kate went to Po'k. by 9.30 A.M. min Douglass' men came & commenced work in the "Nursery" — washing the ceiling & clearing the old Palper from the walls. To studio & spent m't. painting on "Capri from Naples". Home at 1 — Douglass there with Miller, the carpenter, to take orders from new doors to bed rooms — Then had lunch. Katie T— in very good humor because at last she has been admitted to m Life class of the N.A. of D. After 2 o'clk. Mary & I went to Warren, Fuller & Large's — in 42nd St. & selected a Palper for the nursery wall. They to Douglass', with a piece of it, so that from it he can determine tints for ceiling and wood work. To studio at 3.30 — Painted on Capri. Then got out some other old works and scraped & and palpered & washed them — Home at 6.30 — Dinner — Soon after 8 Chave came with Jim's new wheel — one that I ordered for him some time ago — There was great excitement over it. Jim was quite dazed — it was so unexpected — it was suggested that as Theodora, signifies "a gift from God" — he should name his wheel Casadora or Caspadore. The boys did not get to bed until after 9. Ralph shared in the excitement as now Jim's old wheel becomes his —

## Right Page

### Wed. March 11, 1896

Almost a blizzard — strong winds & considerable snow — the storm kept up all day long & most of the night. In spite of the darkness I painted during the m't on "Capri from Naples" & got to the end of this painting. I did not return to the studio after lunch but remained at home and prepared my Treas's Report for this ev'g and rested. At 8 p.m. I went to N.A. to attend the double barreled meeting — a special meeting to act upon proposed amendments to the Constitution — to be followed by the regular stated meeting. It was 9 o'clk before a quorum could be counted — There was considerable chatter over the amendments offered but no serious opposition — by 11 o'clk — the ten amendments had been passed — there there was a recess for drinks — 10 m — then the "Stated Meeting" was called to order — I read my Report — & then slipped out, leaving Peny enlarging upon his new By-Laws. Mary & Bessie went with Emma & Mary Ireland to the Opera in 14th St., Damrosch, Walkürie, (?) They did not get home until after we did.
