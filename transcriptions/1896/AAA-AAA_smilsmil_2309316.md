## Left Page

### Mon. March 16, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow still falling heavily in the m'g
\+ until about noon, Then it changed to
rain which fell very heavily during the
afternoon \+ eve'g. A very dark \+ dismal
day. Took Ralph to school in the m'g, then
to Miller, the Carpenter, in 40th St., approved de-
sign for doors for bed room floor \+ ordered
them. Then to studio \+ painted for awhile
on an old painting "A brook with Ducks"
x . Man from Central Iron works came
\+ I promised to give him drawing for the
iron work on Studio drum waiter Tues. p. m.
So, I left my painting \+ set to work on
that. Again, after lunch, I worked on it \+
when it was finished I thought of a much
simpler \+ an less expensive plan that would
be quite as satisfactory \+ for which no drawing
would be necessary. Mr. Van Pelt. The Real
Estate broker came for a while to talk with
me concerning N.A. \+ D. Sites. Every
I had to go through beating Rain \+ seas of slush
to Council meeting of N.A. There were two
very surprising peals of thunder, with vivid
lightning between 6 \+ 7 o'clk P. M. The
meeting of the Council was short but inter-
esting because a Report on Site Com. business
for presentation to the meeting of N.A.'s to-morrow
eve'g. had to be agreed to. Wood \+ Brown
have together for a site about 110th St. while
Starkd \+ Penny favor on on Bdway \- 55th \+ 56th St.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 17, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant overhead but floods of slush
underfoot. I painted on Oct. afternoon, with
"Ship". After lunch I went to Carnegie
"Chamber Music Hall" \- 57th St. \+ 7th Ave. At 3 o'clk
I heard a song recital by Corinne Moore
Lawson \- being strongly urged to it by Emma
Ireland. As I went in I met Emma, \+
a little later, the Colemans. I had a seat
in a corner in the balcony where I could sit
retired \+ unobserved. This is the first time
that I have attended a concert or place of
amusement since Annie was taken from me.
It was a very sore trial for me. There was so
much in the Programme that open the wound
that has not yet begun to heal. I hurried
away, alone, after the concert \+ walked down to
Durand \- Ruel's Gallery, 36th St. \+ 5th Ave. \+ saw some
pictures \- then to studio \- fixed fire \- then home
\+ had a short rest before dinner. Every
at 8 went to N.A. Special meeting to consider
report of Com. on Site No. 2 \- full meeting \- lively
night \- between Brown, favoring site in 110th St. \+ I
Penny, \+ a site on B'd way \+ 56th St. \- An adjournment
was agreed to until Tues. eve'g. of next week
It was near midnight when I got home.
