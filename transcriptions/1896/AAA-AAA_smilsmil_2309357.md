## Left Page

### Sat. June 6, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncomfortably warm in spite of a little breeze. Almost the whole day was given to a worrying struggle with my bank acct. which gets more \+ more entangled as I struggle until at about 5 o'clk. I had to give up, "dead beat." The bank's statement is correct — I can prove that — but I cannot locate my errors — all of them. I went home \+ had a rest before tea at 6.30 — At about 8 Charl came to the house with his wheel, Katharine Jim \+ Ralph got out theirs, \+ finally I got out mine — we did a little riding around the streets \+ at 8.30 R. \+ I came in — Ge°. came and at about 10 I started out with him to go to the Century Club but before I got there decided that I would be better in my bed, so, I returned home.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 7, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw muggy m'g with some rain but it ceased before noon \+ at times the sun shone. wind still E. Up late. 9.30 when I had my bkf. Before 11 I went to studio \+ until after 1.30 I packed the many little things that I have to take to Montrose into bkls. Dinner at 2 — At 3.30 with the boys, I rode up to 75th St. \+ went to Dr. Arpad Gerster's, No. 34 E. His 14 year old boy Johnny, did the honors until his mother came down — Dr. not attending a consultation — left word that we must wait \+ stay to tea — we did so \+ had a very pleasant time — a son of Junius Brown's came in \+ the four boys went off to the park together. A young man — did not catch his name, came in — some relative, I believe, of the D's. The Dr. took me all over his new house \+ after tea played on the organ for us. We left at about 8.15 \+ walked home — Charl there — As our stay to tea was unexpected of course Mary had indulged in all sorts of worrying about us.
