## Left Page

### Wed. May 13, 1896

**Weather:** Still warm (but cooler) & clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annual Meeting, N.A. f.S.

Still warm (but cooler) & clear — To Studio it 10 & finished up my N.A. Report business & home before 1 — light lunch & then to N.A. found the N.A.s at lunch & buzzing — Perry's ticket of nominations had been distributed — D.A. Ward for Pres't — Jud. Hart, V.P. Swain Gifford to Take my place as Treas. — Moran to take George's place as Recording Sect. & Perry to hold on as Cor. Sec'ty. — I set my self to work to do a little electioneering thinking that at least Perry had outwed his usefulness. The meeting was held in large class room — west side — first time — much quieter than library & better ventilated — full meeting 54 present — Reports — Then election — Wood got 30 votes — Ward, 21 — The beginning of Perry's waterloo — got was handsomely re-elected, Norton Dunning behind his ticket — Perry was downed, Beckwith being elected to fill his place. He kept a stiff upper lip but it was but too evident that he was astonished & chagrined. — Gifford, before the vote for Treas. arose & stated that he had not been consulted in the formation of the down town ticket & that he declined to run on it, closing with a few flattering words for me — Alfred Jones, an "up town" man had been induced to take his place. The old man could not, afterwards, explain to me why — I went in again by a vote of 30 to 20 — The injustice of this after ago. me by the "down town" malcontents annoys me. My conscientious & efficient work as Treas. counts as nothing with them — The sensation of the day was the announcement of the Result of voting for N.A.s — no one was elected but Ives Sargent — there was consternation. A new plan of nomination & ballest had been devised by Perry — The Constitution was amended last counter under the amount it was tried for the first time — I think the fiasco was largely due to the adoption of the experiment — The session was very long & tedious & continued without recess until 7 when dinner was announced — then we went in to that function which proved to be a very absintless affair — Two of the gentlemen overloaded themselves with whiskey & mixed drinks an unedifying spectacle. Jel & I left at about 10.30.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 14, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day with some heavy showers in the aft. — I got up feeling the reaction natural after the strain of some days culminating last night — I went to studio — The Tribune Reporter came & I submitted to an interview — Home at 1 — After lunch my head was so uncomfortable that I went to bed & got the relief of a sound nap —

Evelf I read "Tom Grogan" to the boys & they were so much interested & read so hard for me to finish it that I did so, reading until 9.50 — later I worked for some time over my own private acc'ts
