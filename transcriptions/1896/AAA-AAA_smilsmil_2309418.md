## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 6, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy fog in the early m'f. that lifted at last to the leaden clouds that have covered the sky for the past five days. I did no studio work but was at doors much of the time pruning trees \+ doing like work. I finished crating Jim's bicycle \+ that, \+ Toni's, was sent by exp'o. Geo. Walker worked 9 h. \+ finished digging tree holes — the other men did not work. Nole reported in the m'f. that after 10 hours the cow jessie showed no reaction, but the test will not be complete before 8 o'clk this even. I worked for a time in the small box for aqua-tint experiments — Rev. Mc Milly, the Presb'n pastor of years ago — called in the afternoon \+ so did Wm. Mulford, son of Elisha — At 6 I went down town — Even. Read to R— some time with newspapers. Wrote to nursery man a little to send on Trees — mailed it at 6 —

Geo. Walker — 9 h.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 7, 1896

*Kate went to N.Y.*
*Charli am'd. from N.Y.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain last night — Heavy clouds and high wind in the m'f — some spotting showers later in the day — Bkf't. at 7.30 to station with Kate — she left on 7.50 train for N.Y. on her way to Farmington on a visit — Harrington's team \+ Pike's team commenced ploughing the drained swamp lot — making very close progress — Geo. Walker dug a pathway from piazza towards the barn — Spencer cut down a large but very unsound beech tree N.E. from the house — I am going to set a chestnut to take its place. I got to work in studio late — laid grounds on Mad. Sq. Tower \+ on Hive top near Lake — with point dragged some of the bits that I wish to rebite — worked as long as I could see — After tea R— N.T. \+ I went to station \+ met Charles — Even. chatting until near 11.

Two teams ploughing in swamps all day
