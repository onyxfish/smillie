## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 14, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day \- raw wind \+ showers \- at night, clearing \+ moonlight \- I put in a full day of experimental dry aqua-tint work \- laying a\ct grounds by various combinations, etc \- etc on small plates. I also wrote a second note to Pres. Sett. Low telling him that I had written to him Sep. 23d \+ had as yet, Rec'd no reply. I wrote a note to S. P. Avery thanking him for Grolier card to exhi. of me\. work of Chs. Burt. I went down town at 6 P.m \+ again, with letters, at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 15, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day \- full of mellow brilliance and entrancing color \- I worked closely in studio continuing my experiments with dry-aquatinting grounds \- a work that is interesting and provoking \- the results sought being dependent upon the co-operation of so many chances \- and consequently, so elusive \- I went down town just before 12 \- Paid a bill \+ went to P.O \- At about 5 I struck work \+ took my wheel to the cinder track in the Fair Ground \+ rode 6 miles there at one stretch \- The sunset was beautiful \+ the twilight, paling into moonlight was very witching \- Had a change \+ rub-down before tea \- \+ a moonlight stroll on piazza after it.
