## Left Page

### Fri. April 17, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably hot day \- Ther. in studio 83° all day. I got there at about 10 \+ until 11.30 wrote letters that have been in arrears for some time \- Then to Bank \+ from Case the Cash'. got a written statement concerning a loan that I expect to use to-morrow before the appraiser of legacy or Transfer Tax \- Then to Brooks Bros. \+ got a suit of clothes \- Then to Kurtz \+ saw miniature of Mrs. Comstock just done by old Scott \- it is a horrid looking thing \+ I could not have it \- another one is to be done \- Then home to lunch \- To studio \+ again spent the afternoon writing, most of the time given to completion of list \+ order for deeds \+ plants to W. Attee Burpee. F. Stiles Chase started this afternoon for Chicago \+ Kansas City \- \+ the latter place he is to meet a party of capitalists engaged in smelting operations upon a very extensive scale \- They are to go on \- in special car, and make an extended tour in Mexico.

Every at home \- trying studiously to keep cool.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 18, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beat the record for heat.

Another distressingly hot day. Spent the mf at studio writing letters \- Lunch at \+ at 2.15 I was at Lee \+ Lee's office, 20 Nassau St. With Mr. Kingsbury I went to the office of the appraiser for Transfer Tax \+ was sworn \+ put through a regular examination concerning Annie's estate and especially about Niloans to go \- \+ the conversions from personal into Sniffee court real estate \- The impression he made upon me was reassuring \+ I hope to get thro' without great loss \- went to Henderson's \+ bought Poppy seeds \- Then home about 5 \+ had a nap before dinner \- Eve'g went to Century \- Mr. F. Weir lectured, with lantern illustrations, upon "A glimpse of Holland in the time of Rembrandt". I think it w'd have been more interesting if it had not been so hot in the gallery, \+ if he had not been quite so extended in his theorizing upon the subject of Rembrandt's insight into real Christianity \+ his position as an illustrator of the Bible \- After the lecture had conferences with a number of N. A's \+ N. A. matters \+ home at 12.15
