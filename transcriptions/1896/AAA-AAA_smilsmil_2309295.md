## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 30, 1896

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'f. — not very agreeable day but brilliant moonlight night. To studio at ½ 30 & spent the m'f in drawing the degree lines, etc. on my new mezzotint table — To lunch — \+ after that went to Kimmise & Voigt's to see how the felt of the Lee portrait looked on parchment — I was very much pleased. The effect is really far more beautiful than it is possible to get on paper. To studio at 4 — commenced to lay a new ground (mezzo.) on a small plate, confident that with my new appliances I was going to make a success — Instead, I was dejected by a very bad "break" at the very outset. Dinner — Sw'f wrote to John Fox, asking him to give me a lesson or two in mezzotint ground laying — also, a note to Priscilla Bently — dozed about until after 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 31, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

not unpleasant altho' by night time the weather was threatening. Started work again upon my 7"×9" mezzotint, "the memory of Raquette" \+ by lunch time had got it ready for a proof — after lunch I printed two proofs and by them found myself deeper in the problem of how to lay a mezzotint ground — like most other explorations the horizon broadens as we climb. I find much more to be learned than at first I dreamed of. Sw'f went to "River View — Stag — Walter ser Soc'y." A full exhibit of pictures confounded upon receipts with which I am not familiar, \+ that are not agreeable to my palette by crowds of young men who have, like mushrooms, sprung up under my very nose \+ who are all unknown to me. I was a cat in a strange garret \+ got home before 11 where I found Char.
