## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 5, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day. Ther. got up to 86°. in [illegible] studio. I again was able to do some work on gelatine Tracing of Mad Sq. Tower. bath in m'f. & afternoon — \+ I also gave some time to looking after the men in the swamps — now, no longer a swamp, as it is quite dry. I went to P.O. at 6.30. Elisha Mulford's son Wm took tea with us \- \+ spent part of ev'g. N. T. staid at home while Bessie \+ guests went to a progressive Euchre party at Mr. Christman's. Jim has info? slowly during the day — he complains of languor — his physical condition discourages me — he is depressed \+ desires stimulants. In the m'f. I went to Village knoll \+ gave Mr. Pickett instructions about putting wings at either end of the stage for the coming play.

Ev'g \+ Mr. \+ Mrs. Cromwell spent a part of it with us on piazza.

Pope's team \+ man \+ Sherman worked hauling stone to Hibbard \+ Pontain — The three Italians worked at filling in the ditches.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 6, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim went to camp with the Mulford's

Exceedingly hot \+ oppressive. Wm Mulford came to the house in the m'f. \+ as Jim seemed to be much better I promised to let him go to the Choconut Camp with him in the afternoon. In the m'f. I went down town \+ among other business that was attended to I had my hair cut. Mulford came very early after dinner. He had a friend from Boston \+ a young boy, about Jim's age, with him \+ Jim went with them to spend a week or two. Afternoon, I laid grounds on two plates, 9"×12" \+ 7"×12". also, worked a little on the complicated outline on gelatine of Mad. Sq. Tower. Ev'g on piazza — Miss Cromwell there.

Pope's team \+ man \+ Sherman — Hibbard \+ Rutou — Three Italians worked.
