## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 9, 1896

**Weather:** Threatening m't, soon clearing to be a bright, pleasant day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After my usual m't duties I went down town, first to one of the divisions of Surrogate's Office + got 5 certificates of letters testamentary — Then to Tax office + settled int. acct 186 cents on assessment arrearage — Then to see the [illegible], at 12 o'clk. to E.m. Cook's Office, 150 Nassau — Directors + Trustees meeting of Ireland Real Estate Co. E.m., Walter, Geo. J. Cook, Ja. Wright + myself. There until near 2 o'clk. discussing affairs of the Co. — Duane S't. property — etc, especially the matter of the Cleveland, O. dock property — The city of C. is preparing to widen to River buy $0,000 to cut away a considerable portion of the dock front reducing its value very considerably without making adequate compensation. Lunch before 3 + then being tired + wishing to be fresh to-night, I took a nap + after it wrote a short paper to go with a resolution that I intend to present to-night. To N.A. at 8 o'clk. Found that Mr. Hart had prepared a resolution much the same as mine but with an added clause giving the Council + its com. power to approp. $41,000 for site + obey — while laying aside $100,000 for maintenance. Its purpose was evident. He does not wish the money now in the hands of the Academy to be so tied up that he cannot get at it to buy his desired site "in 67th S't." I do not wish the money so tied up that an up town site cannot be bought if the com. can be brought to think that way. So I accepted his clause without discussion, altho his sly manner of working is very irritating to me. A full meeting — many came expecting site development and at first — There was disappointment because it was not so — There is a good deal of intense under-feeling —
Home at 11.30

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 10, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to work at studio at about 10. Commenced laying out on 33" × 44" canvas "Vesuvius from Sorrento" changing proportions slightly from those of the small oil sketch painted a few days ago. I made charcoal outline on the canvas. In the afternoon Dr. Gerster made quite a lengthy call. Evely. Mallie Adams, who has been staying with Alice Ireland for some days past, dined with us and at 8 o'clk., she, N. T. + Bessie went to Walter Cook's + spent the eve'g. I worked at getting my assets in order until after 10 o'clk. — Went out on my wheel + had half an hour or more of riding — Then in + to bed.
