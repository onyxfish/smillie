## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 27, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant cold day \- Went to studio at 10 30 \+ fixed fire \- Then I got out my water colors, for I have determined to lay Vesuvius aside for a few days \+ in the mean time I shall fetch into W. C.s, hoping to be able to fill some of my old frames in time for the W. C. Exbn. I put my W.C. box in soak \+ made other preparations for work in the mornov. Then home, after clearing the ice from a frozen \+ clogged leader on the roof of rear extension I went out with the boys \- We walked up to the lake end of the Mall, Central Park, \+ home in time for dinner at 2 \- After dinner I read a newspaper \+ had a nap. At 5 30 a lot of the Ed. \+ Walter Cook youngsters and Eleanor Cook, just on from Cambridge, came in, for a little while \- I went to studio \+ fixed fire \- home at 6 30 \- tea \- Evg. Reading to the boys until near 9 \- Charle came \+ took Kate to his rooms \- Gel came, leaving before 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 28, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day \- very cold \- Almost used up with a headache \- After bkft. wrote some notes. Ninna Dreland came in \+ I sat chatting so long with her that it was after 11 when I got to studio \- I set aside Vesuvius to dry from few day \+ began preparations for a short water-color campaign \- I got out paper \- mounting boards and gilt frames \- After lunch I made paste \+ mounted paper \- among other preparations I had to clean up my standing press, \+ that I found to be real labor \- Before dark I had a lot of paper in shape ready for work. Evg. Reading to the boys until 9. Then I wrote a note to Milly Taylor of Po'k. acknowledging receipt of her Xmas pen wiper \- Bessie went to a party at Ed\. Cook's \- Jim was all day with Merrill Bishop, out of town, coasting \- says that he never had a better time.
