## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 27, 1896

**Weather:** Overcast & chilly m't. but clearing + milder in the afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9. 30 + to work Scrapling on Genl. Greene, face. Stuck to it pretty closely all day + in the afternoon got it into such shape that I thought it would not be safe to work any longer without a let- so I wet down paper, preparatory to printing to-morrow. Evef. quietly at home, with the boys, their lessons + reading until near 9 o'clk.

Greene 1 day — [illegible]

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 28, 1896

**Weather:** Mild + overcast — raw air + sloppy streets.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9. 30 + to work at my printing. Took two pfs. from small Gelate, etched + mezzotinted, from Dear's deafs. take George + then two from Genl. Greene. In both cases the result was surprisingly dark. On the Greene Plate it is evident that I have an immense lot of scraping ahead of me. I was 20 m. late at lunch + correspondingly late in getting back to studio. For a couple of hours I was engaged in "cleaning up" — Home at 5. 30 + dressed + at 6. 30 was at 1 East 55 th St. To dine with Mr. M[illegible]. Beach her daughters + her son Chas. + his wife. Violet was ill with a cold + soon retired. A pleasant quiet until 10, then I went home —

This afternoon Katie T. went to Co'k. on a School mates Spree.

Greene ½ day.
