## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 25, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day \- clear & cool. I worked all day in etching room biting new work on plate of Lippincott head. I made slow progress because I used Dutch mordant & did some work out of doors cutting out dead wood in trees. At 5.30, having throat & lungs filled with Chlorine gas, I went out for a ride on my wheel. Ralph accompanying me, Jim makes, complaining of a tired feeling \- we rode out back of "little defeat" \- past Post farm & back in to town by big hill & Methodist Ch \- to P.O. Then home. Eve'g. Daud. Sayre & wife called immediately after tea. Dance at Village Hall. All hands went leaving me, & the two boys. I read aloud until near 9 \- After they went to bed I read newspaper for about an hour & then to bed. [illegible — ~8 words]

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 26, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim again went to camp.

Fine day. I worked with burnisher & graver on Lippincott plate. Roland Mulford invited Jim to make a visit to the camps. After getting Lee & Lee's bill $400. for settling Fannie's estate, I decided that I could not go to the Adirondacks not having the money necessary. That being the case I allowed Jim to accept Mr. M's invitation to stay with him until the breaking of camps next Tues. Mr. & Mrs. Wile Mulford drove over just after 6h30. Taking Jim with them. The young people went off on a picnic to Salt Spring & got home at 10.30 I read to Ralph & to bed early.
