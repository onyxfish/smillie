## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 20, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mollie Adams Ret. to New Ham. Uncle Willie came from Po'k.

Overcast \+ very raw. my headache still continues - a dull, heavy feeling that extends to my very heels \+ unfits me for anything or everything. Before bffo. I went to Laffan's. cor. of 39 S. \+ Lex. Ave. but he had not yet come down to bffo. After my bffo., at 9.30, I went again \+ saw him \+ gave him a note I wished published in the Sun - he read it \+ said that it should appear to-morrow. Then to studio but was not able to do much work. In the afternoon I traced portrait enlargement of Gen. Green \+ retitued it upon a piece of mill board preparatory to an oil sketch. Home at 5 \+ lay down \+ had some troubled sleep befor dinner. At 8 went to Council Meeting \+ had a very hard time with my head. A new Site Com. was appointed - I was left out in the cold - \+ J. G. Brown was retained as a reward of merit - I suppose, as he did nothing on the late com. but say no - After adjournment I took pains to track for his report of the special meeting to the newspapers. He unblushinly assumed the responsibilities \+ defended his means, printed \+ perverted Report.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 21, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncle W. Ret. to Po'k.

Dull, overcast day - I got up with my headache that had troubled me all night but in the afternoon it retired. I did little work in the m'f., but in the afternoon I got up enough courage to commence the painting of an oil sketch, in monochrome, portrait of Gen. Green 11" x 14" - the size that I am to mezzotint it.

A quiet evening at home
