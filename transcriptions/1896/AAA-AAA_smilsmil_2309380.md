## Left Page

### Wed. July 22, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very muggy, hot \+ uncomfortable day \- cloudy m'f \- Some sunshine during the middle of the day \- Thunderstorms from about 5 to 7 \- then a cessation for about an hour when rain fell quietly until after 9 \- Kate was expected to-day but sent word that she would wait until it was cooler before she journeyed. My duties \+ looking after the "dagos" in the swamps used up much of the m'f, but I did some work in the studio \- \+ wrote some notes \- At 4.30 the Thorr girls, Mollie A. \+ Jean Atwater, went off with a party of young folks for a pic-nic to Salt Spring \+ a moonlight ride home. The thunderstorm was covering the Southern sky when they left \+ thunder was continuous \- four horse team \+ band-wagon \- going Post driving \- I went to P.O. after tea \- with newspaper from 9 to 10.30

Three Italians worked in the swamps \- a fourth one gave out at 10 A.M. They stopped work at 5 p.m. owing to shower.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 23, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sudden change in the weather, cloudy \+ clearing, high wind \+ quite cool, so that at 6\&5 time we had fire on the hearth. The day was delightful but towards eve'g the sky became overcast \+ rather threatening. After getting thro' with my mart is it duties I retired to my studio, made fine paste \+ mounted the two portraits (crayon) of M'r. Gerster \+ the pencil sketch made a few days ago on the road, top of hill, near Jones' lake \- Dinner at 1 \- after that I found things in studio to attend to \+ prepared to make tracings for the etchings that I am prepared now to begin very soon \- At 5.30 went out \+ had a little tramp over D'r. Dunton's hill \- Home to tea at 6.30 \- went to P.O. at 8 \- The family got to bed early to repair exhaustion from last night's dissipation \- They did not get in until after 12 having had a wet time, but insisting that it was a good time. After 9 I had an hour or more of quiet reading \-

Three Italians worked in ditches \- a fourth one worked in the afternoon.
