## Left Page

### Mon. July 20, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain fell quite steadily most of the day — at times quite heavily — Jim was to have ret'd from his visit to Greer Park to-day, but the storm detained him — Ralph was started out before 5 A.M. — his stomach was emptied of its contents — after which he slept again — he got up late but was unable to sit up — had some fever — Abed during part of p.m. I gave him a good dose of Rhubar B \+ Soda I spent the ev'g reading to him \+ he seemed better \+ brighter. I worked until near 11 o'clk. A.M. transplanting lettuce — for some times with R. Reading — wrote some notes — dinner — again some writing — went down town at 5 — Tea — Read to R — In spite of the bad weather a number of young people came up from Saures — Gnetta's niece came to-day as an extra helper.

no work in ditches in swamps.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 21, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim ret'd from Parke's farm.

A very close, muggy day — very trying — cloudy all the m'f. Sunshine \+ steaming heat in the afternoon — very hazy quiet ev'g. After my m't duties I looked after the four Italians who are digging ditches in the swamps — then I worked for some time fitting blocks into cheeks of printing press. Then I went to Bank \+ made deposit. Dinner — About 3 o'clk. Jim ret'd from his visit with Greer Parke to the farm 4½ miles below town. His work on his wheel over the muddy roads, in the great heat, made him look as tho' he had been boiled. I worked on Mad. Ave. Tower until tea at 6.30. Ev'g. I went to Annual Meeting of Village Imp't Soct. Few present — A very tiresome affair — no proper preparation \+ presentation of the affairs of the Soct. The old officers were re-elected — so that, in spite of my objection, I am again Vice-Pres't. Home before 10.

Ralph seems to be quite recovered to-day. [illegible — ~6 words]
Four Italians worked on ditches [illegible].
