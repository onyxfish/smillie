## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 3, 1896

**Weather:** Superb day  **Temp:** [illegible]

N.T., Ralph \+ I went to N.Y.

Up at 6. Mr. Rhinevault came at 6.30 but we had a hard scrabble to get our bkfst. \+ the many little "last things" attended to before 7.30 when the 'bus came \+ took us to the station. Effects over the landscape, delicious — the partially denuded woods were particularly lovely — The R.R. ride to N.Y. was long \+ tedious — mail train \+ stopping at every station \+ us Drawing Room car — got out foot of Christopher S\- at 10 o'clk \+ thence by car — I stopped at 36 S\. \+ found that burglars had wrecked Ryan's fruit door \+ it had been frightened away before they got entrance. The Trunks got home before I did — found Hattie \+ Bessie writing — Charl came in soon after — chatting until dinner — Jim came in from a 40 mile ride on his bicycle — Every. Charl came again \+ at about 10 o'clk. we, Bessie, Ralph \+ I started out to see the sights of election night. It was an immensely exciting time. We went to Madison Sq — packed with people men \+ women — first class citizens in the very best of humor \+ behaving in a most orderly \+ admirable manner in spite of crowding \+ excitement. Remarkable preparations for flashing the election returns from every part of the Union upon great screens far above the heads of people immediately upon receipt — signal lights \+ [illegible] published codes were flashed from high towers \+ from captive balloons suspended over the city. The crush was immense — the enthusiasm was unbounded — bands played patriotic airs \+ the crowds joined in singing them — it was a pandemonium of noise — a dazzling show of light \+ color — a whirl of excitement — The Bryantites, if there were any, did not put in a recognizable appearance. Ralph was keenly alive to it all \+ was unwilling to leave with us at about 11.30 — Jim did not go with us, being so tired after his long bicycle ride.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 4, 1896

**Weather:** Cloudy \+ mild with light rain commencing at about 8 P.M.  **Temp:** Bkpt. at 8 —

It was after 9 when I started out with Ralph — went with him to 21 W. 42\. S\. School of the Misses Miller \+ Small \+ left him there — Then to Davis, E. 42\. \+ ordered Coal for studio — Then to Miller's in 40 S\. \+ ordered storm doors put up as soon as possible — Then to Grocer \+ Butcher — then to studio — Wrapped up painting from "Capri romMafeles" \+ rode to N.A. of D. meeting Alice Ireland in the car — left my painting at U.A. — saw no one but Mr. Pink — then went to Calverly's new studio, 107 E. 27. St. Found him at work setting up a new clay bust of Annie, made by pressing clay into a plaster mould made from the clay bust he modeled a year or more ago — He says that he is now going right on with the work to finish the bust — Then home to lunch at 1 — Ralph did not get in from school until 1.30 — feeling very tired I had a nap in the afternoon \+ felt much rested after it. Every. at 8. Ralph \+ I went to Edw. Cook's — Saw the whole family, Mr. Sprague, Miss Williams \+ a Buffalo lady Field \+ anhour of chatting, then home — had to borrow an umbrella — Found Charl at the house — It was 11 before I got to bed.
