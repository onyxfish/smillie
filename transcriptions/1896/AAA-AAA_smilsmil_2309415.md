## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 30, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

*Jim & Tom went to N.Y.*

A bright & beautiful day, following a hurricane of wind & rain last night and closing with more wind & rain, tho' less violent than the night before. This was "Fair Day" - & the clear weather was a godsend for the Sus. Agri. Ass". - Up at 6 - Jim & Tom got away for N.Y. on the 7.50 train to be in time for the opening of school to-morrow. I started them on the train their home & started the biting of Mad. Sq. Gard. Tower, which I finished late in the afternoon. Dinner at 12.30 - immediately after it Ralph & I went to the fair. I looked about in a general way & saw some of the sights - the Tight Rope man, etc. - & home before 3 - & worked until near 5 - Clouds then came up & gusty showers began to fall. - I walked piazza for more than half an hour - tea - Everts newspaper & to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 1, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy most of the day with some spittings of rain some charming effects of sunlight & shadow in the middle of the day - Raw, windy, mizzling night. - Putnam Hibbard commenced working on side hill in Tyler lot to terrace a bit where I am going to set half a dozen apple trees. Geo. Walker commenced digging under the house passage way thro' which to carry pipes connecting cisterns so that their contents may be pumped into the tanks at the barn. - I bit in head of Mrs. Gerster & am quite pleased with the result. At 6 R. & I went down town but failed to see those we went for - at 8.30 we tried it again. ordered coal - saw Chas. Sayre & got permission to gather leaf mold on his hill, etc. etc. - Later in the evng. worked over a pen drawing for T. Gill put up a trunk for coal in studio cellar.

Gerster head 1 d. -
Putnam & Hibbard - 1.
Geo. Walker 1.
