## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 12, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain commenced falling before day light. Some heavy showers \+ high wind. A break in the weather for a couple of hours from 9 to 11 \- then a steady, quiet rain set in from another quarter continuing with few interruptions all day. I spent the mf. in studio washing in an effect in black \+ white on sketch from which I made etching of Mad. Sq. Garden tower. Afternoon, until about 5 I experimented laying dry aqua-tints on small copper plates without any very gratifying results. Took a walk at 5.30, going to P. O. Evg. \- with Ralph \+ newspapers.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 13, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain again, commencing some time in the early mf. \+ continuing almost without cessation throughout the day \+ after I went to bed at night. At 6 p. m. the big tanks at the barns had been raised over 16 inches, representing a gain in 36 hours of about 40 bbls. of water. I put in a long days work in studio, experimenting with dryground aquatint. I bit a number of the experimental grounds \+ upon one of the small plates upon which I had bitten an aquatint, I laid an etching ground, drew in a little design \+ then bit that. After 5 Ralph \+ I took a walk down town to P. O. \+ around the S. end of town just for exercise. Evg. in studio reading \+ a little writing.
