## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 18, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day - showers & snow flurries - clearing suddenly after sunset to a still cold, brilliant moonlight night. Date blft. Some writing in the mtf before dinner — About 4, feeling sleepy I fixed myself comfortably upon my nd \+ slept until the tea bell rang at 6.30 — Post, some reading aloud to Ralph \+ after he got to bed. I wrote along letter to Carrell Beckwith concerning N.a.f.S. sites matters. It was after 11 when I went to the P.O. with letters \+ midnight when I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 19, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** Ther. fell to 24° last night.

A cold day of clouds \+ sunshine with a brilliant, still, cloudless moonlight night. In the mtf everything out of doors was covered with ice — my lettuce, outside of the cold frames, was ruined. The sleety rain of yesterday clung to it \+ froze hard during the night — nevertheless, we ate some of the larger heads for dinner. I worked in studio on swale \+ [illegible] plate — dry aquatint — thinking that at last I had caught the secret — but when at 5 o'clk. I cleaned the plate I was disheartened on finding that it was practically a failure — The sensation upon such a discovery is not inspiriting — After tea went downtown \+ back at 8.30. Wrote reply to Pres. Sette Low \+ to bed early.

Dreadful death of young Knell, Veterinary Surgeon on R.R. crossing at New Milford this afternoon — He was to have been married to-morrow.
