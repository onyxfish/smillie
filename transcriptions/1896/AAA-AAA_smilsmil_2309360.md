## Left Page

### Fri. June 12, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant, warmer  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim arr. from New-Haven before 3 o'clk.

Up before 7 — at Studio at about 7.30 - a new idea concerning sewer grating in Sniffen Court came to me while in bed - went at once to Central Iron Co. \+ the foreman there told me that my scheme was entirely practicable \+ w. save the expense of the new grating that I had ordered - He promised to have it done by 12 o'clk. Then home to bkp - After that getting everything together for general packing - in the course of the mg went again to studio to see how the work progressed - Then to Borromani's \+ gave final orders about crating wheels \+ paid him - Then to Manhattan Storage and home - Dinner at 1.30 - at 3 again went to Studio - no grating yet - went to Central Co. in 30' S-- \+ had it sent right up - home \+ at last, after 4 got fairly started upon packing - at my trunk until near 6 - then to Jackson's S.p. \+ left order for to-morrow. Then home \+ had a short rest before tea - having been again to studio \+ found that the pavers had finished their job \+ gone home. Tea at 6.30 - commenced packing again. finished Trunk - big case \+ 1 barrel by 11.30 - Then gave up - Geo S. came in \+ stayed for an hour or so.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 13, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

We all got away for Montrose.

Cool m.t. hazy - threatening rain which we ran into in the afternoon - Up at 6.30 bkp. soon after 7 - I commenced packing at once - getting the glass jelly jars, etc. into a barrel. The S.p. men came for trunks etc at 9.30 just as I was heading up the barrel. Mr. Bishop came to say that Merrill had already broken down with fear \+ homesickness \+ she tho't it best for him not to go. I went about the house to pick up loose ends \+ see that everything was right - About 11 I went to Louise Cook's to arrange for her Edw.'s visit to Montrose - Then to studio \+ found that water running down Sniffen Court did not go thro' the grating prepared for it into the sewer, but ran along my wall \+ thence across my sidewalk in front of the building - vexations. - New home \+ sat up lunch - at 12 we started for Hoboken 3 Ave. Cable car - 18th S. car (transfer) to Christopher S. - 5em in half an hour - checked trunks \+ got over to cars ¼ of an hour ahead of time - a very comfortable journey. I light rain when we got to before at about 7 on a quick run to Montrose getting there at 7.30 - We rose at once to the house getting there before 8 - found Mr. \+ Mrs. Sayre (Dau.) there to welcome us - a fine in large stove in lower hall Mr. Rhinevault in the kitchen \+ a fine there - our trunks followed us promptly \+ they were soon distributed \+ partly unpacked - The boys \+ the Throop girls were in a state of excitement and delight at getting to Montrose \+ I could only wish that I could share with them - we all got to bed before 10. I had to go downtown in the rain for candles - I had provided for all other contingencies
