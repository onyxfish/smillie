## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 31, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine rain from about 6 a.m. to 11. Then it cleared beautifully. I was disappointed as I hoped for more water — there is but 5½" now in the tanks. Worked on Dippincatt head until 12. Then at \+ worked on lettuce bed etc. Dinner at 1. Worked on plate until 4.30 when my head hurt me so that I had to give it up. Tarbell called to borrow my heavy roller. I set out a lot of lettuce plants — \+ then rode about the place on my wheel, getting a little exercise in that way. Eve devoted to newspapers.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 1, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim ret. from Friendsville.

Cool \+ pleasant. Ther. fell to 42°. Jim got home soon after 11 — having left Friendsville by stage at 7 o'clk. He was sick last night \+ not in good trim to-day. I worked on Dippincatt head with dry-point until about 11, then I went to Dathrop's \+ saw Ella \+ Florence. Back at 12 \+ worked in shop making a little lamp shelf to put up in cor. of studio by fire place. Dinner — worked on plate until near 4 — then to Azur Dathrop's funeral — did not go to the cemetery but ret. home \+ worked in shop until tea time. Finished the lamp shelf. Helen Holmes took tea with us \+ spent the eve\. Bessie in bed all day with her cold. Evely [illegible] put up lamp shelf in studio, lighted my student lamp \+ had a very comfortable time. Tom Cook was expected to-day but Jim rec'd. a letter from him saying that he would not be here until Sat.
