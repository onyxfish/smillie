## Left Page

### Wed. July 8, 1896

*Ralph's 9th birthday*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brooding kind of day that at last got to rain about 8 o'clk. in the ev'g. — Every thing hereabouts is so very dry that it is to be hoped, that there will now be a thorough wetting — My day was divided between regular duties, mostly out of doors, writing two or three notes \+ a little work on miniature of Mr. Comstock. After dinner again I worked on the portrait until about 4, when feeling rather "stewed" I got on my wheel \+ with Jim went down to P. O. — then by Depot St. to Little R. R. Station \+ on the road back of that for a mile or so — Then home. Then had a good rest \+ rub-down. Tea at 6.30 — after that all hands went out \+ picked currants until the rain drove us in — Ev'g. Reading \+ chatting.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 9, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At last, the blessed Rain — showers fell during the night \+ they continued thro' the day, rain at times falling heavily. It came in the very best manner \+ was a great refreshment. A good part of the day I worked upon a miniature of Mr. Comstock — the [illegible] one that I have tried — for a time, I thought I was going to be able to preserve the likeness, but there is some essential, I cannot tell exactly what, that is so elusive that before I get the face freely worked up the likeness is lost — It has affected me like a nightmare during the past week \+ I am fairly sick of it — I wish to get at some more serious work. Beautiful clearing late in the afternoon \+ fine sunset — I went down for mail twice before tea \+ after. Ev'g. I tried to do a little writing in studio, but it was very hard work \+ I gave it up.
