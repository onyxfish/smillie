## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 20, 1896

*Uncle Willie \+ Aunt Agnes with us overnight*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still cold \+ rather blustering. After a fair night's rest I got up feeling fairly well but weak. I was in no haste to get to the studio \+ it was after 10 when I left the house — I did some scraping on Gen't. Grease plate. Vedder came in just before 1 o'clk. He is getting some of his material into Maynard's room preparatory to doing some work. Then I went into the cellar to see how the men were getting along there \+ was again detained, so that it was 1.30 when I got home to lunch and nearly 3 when I got back to studio — worked a little more than an hour on Grease — then walked up to Butler's in W. 47th St. \+ left some s'cte com. Reports for him — \+ home, stopping at Century, at 6.30 Was surprised to find Uncle Willie and Aunt Agnes there — on their way home from Pitt where they had been to funeral of Uncle Earle — Charl came in for a while — The old folks got to bed early — quiet eve'g.
