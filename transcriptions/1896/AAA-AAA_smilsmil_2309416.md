## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 2, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, raw day. almost under the weather with a headache that troubled me all night but I put in a fair day's work on Gerster plate, with graver making up parts on the face that I did not bite. Ratau & Hibbard worked all day blasting & digging out stone on the apple terrace. Geo. Walker was all day burrowing under the house & Stone & Spense were most of the day gathering leaf mould from Sayre's hill. I went out from my work from time to time to see how the several jobs were progressing & at 5 o'clk. R. & I went down town. I saw Rogers, the butcher, & protested agt. his charges \- p. subs. Republican until next Oct. Swift I went to the Armory to hear a speaker on Silver at a Pabocratic rally. It was a tame affair — a great deal of talk & very little said. I left at 9.30

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 3, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another dull day. I worked about half of the day on the Gerster head. and made many excursions to the different workers on my farm — Hibbard, Rutan, a team & man, were working at the well for the apple terrace. Geo. Walker was at work digging holes for the coming nut trees. It was late in the afternoon, after I had been for some time at work crating Jim's bicycle, that I went down town. Back just in time for tea. Mr. Isaac Riley & her daughter Kitty, took tea & spent the evng. until 9 o'clk. with us — then I escorted them to their lodgings down town. To bed at 11.
