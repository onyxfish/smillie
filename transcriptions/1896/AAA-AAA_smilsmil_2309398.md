## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 27, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day — a couple of heavy showers before daylight this m'f. After bkfs. I spent an hour setting out lettuce plants Then to work on \+ head with graver A short bicycle ride before dinner at 1\. After dinner Mary \+ her girls, Emily \+ Carleton rode over to the Mumford camps at forest Lake, getting back at 7.15. I worked on my plate until near 4 o'clk. Then went off on a tramp with Ralph. In town we stopped for awhile at the ball ground to see part of a game bel'n Great Bend v.s. Montrose tims. Then through Darkie Valley, by Wyalusing \+ South Road home, getting in at 6.30 Plate, R\- \+ I took our tea before the arrival of the others. Ev'g. Reading to R\- as usual then newspaper \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 28, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cool day — After bkfs. I worked for awhile preparing a bed for lettuce. Then I wrote a note to Lee \+ Dee, enclosing a check for $100, on acct. of their bill for $400. Then I got to work on the Hippincott Head plate with graver \+ stuck to it until 1 o'clk — dinner — continued work until 4.30 — Then for an hour transplanting lettuce — Then Ralph \+ I, went out on our wheels \+ took a little 3 miles spin — Then a rub down \+ tea — After that Read to R\- until near 10 — Then Read newspaper until 10, Then to bed.
