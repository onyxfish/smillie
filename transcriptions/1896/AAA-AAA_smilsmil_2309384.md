## Left Page

### Thur. July 30, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still hot & muggy — but thunder storms to the north of us made a breezy, cool evs't. Again the m'f went & little was accomplished — Part of the time with Italians at work in swamp. Cut long ditch at foot of hill intercepting rein of water, promising good results. Wrote some letters. Roland Mulford called & I agreed to let Jim join his camp'ing party at Friendsville for a fortnight, beginning on Monday next, for two weeks at $15.00 per week. Afternoon, I loafed some time on piazza with the girls — With Italians & then to P.O. at 6 — After tea again went to P.O. & to Exp. Office — Evs't with newspapers from [illegible] until near 11 —

The four Italians at work all day.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 31, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mollie Adams & Jean Atwater ret? to New Haven

Great change in the weather — brilliant & quite cool — almost cold. Up at 6 — bfst. at 7. — bis at 7.30 I went with the girls to the station & got them properly started — then home packed Kate's crockery to be ret'd to O'Neill & looked after the Italians at work in the ditches. In spite of the beautiful weather I did not feel well & at 12 I lay down on my bed & had a nap before dinner at 1 — By the way, I made a call on Azur Battis about 10 o'ck. Afternoon I wrote a long letter to George — I also had a short bicycle ride — After 5, with Jim, went down town to get him some camping outfit — near 7 when we had tea. Mrs. Cornwell & her two daughters called in the early evs't. Bessie & I walked to Ch. Sayre's with them — To bed early.

Four Italians in ditches to-day.
