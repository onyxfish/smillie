## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 10, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless day \+ uncomfortably hot; the Ther. getting up to about 80°. I worked all the m'f. on dif't. plates getting it ready for a proof. Before dinner I took a walk over the drained lot to see what Spence has been doing in the way of clearing it up. After dinner it was so hot in studio, for I had the remnant of a fire kindled in the m'f., that I could not do much work. I fussed around getting ready to make camera. lucida reductions of portrait of M'rs. Gerster \+ the sketch on the road near Jones's lake both of which subjects I am preparing to etch. \- Went down town at about 5\- Tea at 6.30 — Evelf. reading to Ralph. \- to myself \- \+ Racing Piazza. To bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 11, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The hottest day so far in this Sep't. hot spell. Ther. got up to 87° in my bedroom; last night it did not fall below 71°. In spite of the discomfort of the heat I put in a very fair day's work — made camera-lucida reductions on tracing paper of head of M'rs. Gerster \+ of the landscape bit on road near Jones's lake \- laid ground on 9×12 \- and got two excellent transfers of the reductions. \- Men I prepared solutions for photo. developments. At 5.45 I went down to P. U, tea at 6.30. Evelf. in dark Room from 8 to 10 developing some photo. films that I exposed a few days ago in Ralph's camera. I am much pleased to find no more difficulty, or little more, than in developing films on glass.
