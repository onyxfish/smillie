## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 9, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Excessively hot with a sweltering haze over everything. Enjoyed my bathing privileges to fullest extent in the mth \+ had help. Before 9 — little accomplished before dinner at 1.45 \+ a little more after it. I managed to write three or four short letters — The young people of course rose superior to the weather \+ I tried to draw inspiration from them — a daughter of D. Chandler's \+ another young girl came to swell the chorus. Tea before 7 — I found Ralph the centre of a group engaged in telling ghost stories — I took a hand to change the current \+ told a ghost story of my own manufacture that didn't have any ghost in it — Rec'd. with applause — The girls tried to rehearse my story upon Carleton \+ the result was, for a time, rather riotous.

Heavy thunderstorms south of us — tremendous \+ continuous thunder for an hour or more \+ almost constant lightning — a very little rain fell on us \+ a refreshing breeze blew up from the west, but indoors remained close shut in spite of the breeze.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 10, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Almost as hot as ever in spite of the storms of last night. As soon as I got Mrs' with my mtf duties I had to go down town to look after some Village tree matters connected with the play to be given on Wed. next — Home at 12 \+ nothing more accomplished before dinner — Immediately after dinner I made up a pkg — for Jim, his rubber coat, towels, jowsharfs \+ stationery and took it down to P.O. to go out by the stage. Then shopped for some time for material to cover the wings on stage in Village Hall — it was 4.30 before I got back to Studio — a rest \+ then down to the lot to see how work there progressed. Tea there downtown \+ got materials to cover screens — The Cromwells came in the eveg \+ there was much jollification \+ chattering.

[illegible — ~3 words] team \+ man \+ sherman worked all day — Hibbard \+ Rutan worked until 4 P.m. — three Italians worked half a day.
