## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 15, 1896

**Weather:** Raw, cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw, cold & windy — To studio before 10 & to work on Vesuvius. I went at it with a lot of color on my big palette & a pot of [illegible] turpentine, oil & siccatif & Harlum before me & with big brushes I rubbed in a suggestion of the color I wished to get. Before dark I had rubbed in sky, [illegible] & water. Chas. Collins came in for a few minutes in the m'f. About 5 o'clk. I walked up to 60' S. & called at Dr. George's boarding place. G. was at his studio in 59' S. I went there & spent half an hour with him — He is quite under the weather, suffering from cold. Home to dinner at 6.30 — Wa'f Charl came in — I had planned to do a number of things but did none of them & went to bed at 10 o'clk. Sleet, & then snow commenced falling before 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 16, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy snow storm with high winds. Snow fell until about 3 p.m. I got to studio before 10 & painted as long as I could see, or until 4 o'clk. covering a good space on the canvas "Vesuvius." Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — Weather so severe that I w'd. not let Ralph go to school. Ev'f. Charl came in for a short time. I wrote a note to Sam'l Colman sending him a check of $25. for the Mosenthal Fellowship fund. That amt. being a gift of Annie's to the Harvard Ex. for Women, Ret'd. to me by Mrs Collins some times ago.
