## Left Page

### Thur. March 26, 1896

**Weather:** An April-like day. overcast m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunshine & mildness at noon — a smart shower at 6.30 p. m. To studio soon after 9 and to work at painting a picture that I had in the spring exbtn of '92 — I was much interfered with — the dumb-waiter man was at work fixing the dumb waiter & I had other interruptions, but I repainted sky & went over it with palette knife. Lunch at 1 — P.m. painted until after 4, then, feeling very tired I cleaned up \+ went to Grolier club \+ had a careful look over the collection of lithographs there. Some of them are interesting, but as a coll. it does'nt amt. to much — met Saml. Leman there — went with Curator up to "Pals Room" — an imitation of an old dutch interior — Then down to Butler's. Saw frames ordered. Water-color sent to Katie Adams for Wedding present. met Walter Cook \+ walked up town with him — Dinner — Welf — Mary, Kate \+ Bessie went to Thomas Concert. After getting the boys to bed I went to Walters to consult him about N.A. f.D. building matters. Home at 10.15 —

## Right Page

### Fri. March 27, 1896

**Weather:** clear, cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

After my usual m'f duties I went down town — to Ge'. R. Read's first, \+ talked with him \+ Vandset concerning N.A. matters — He told me that "it was the plan of the Nat. Life. to begin building operations on the site of the N.A. in June '97. — This was very interesting news to me. Then to see thee to spent the rest of the m'f on my Will — It was 1.45 when I got home to my lunch — \+ nearly 3 when I got to studio. — I painted on canvas that I worked on yesterday until 5 o'clk — then cleaned up \+ went to N.A., where I spent half an hour in the galleries. To-night is the Reception \+ first View — Home to dinner at 6.30 Quiet ev'g at home.
