## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 1, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cool m'f., weather changing as day advanced — light showery rain at about 5 P.M. — heavier rain at night. Got some work done in studio during the m'f, getting plates ready for ground laying — after dinner I tried to lay the grounds using the old bal of Hinshelwood ground that Dr. Gerster sent to me. For some reason it acted so perversely that I could not lay a ground \+ at last gave it up \+ wrote to Maynard asking him to get some of my ground \+ send it onto me. I went to P.O. at 5 — Gill came to hang blind on Jim's dormer window \+ found that he had put in all of the irons upside down. This blunder, pure carelessness, results in a marring \+ botching of the work — most aggravating. Priscilla Poore called before we had finished our tea — at 8 I went to P.O. Home before 9 — Young Warner calling in K \+ B. Newspaper \+ magazine until 10.30.

Four Italians at work — No. 4 left at 5 o'clk. Hibbard \+ Putnam laying stone drains in the ditches.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 2, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day — Hlb'. after 9. Before dinner I wrote to Orlando Bedee \+ part of a letter to Mary Cook now at Athol Springs, near Buffalo — N.Y. At dinner Jim complained of feeling very much out of sorts — I advised him to go to bed \+ he did so \+ slept for a couple of hours — when he awoke he gave evidence of fever. Ther. showed 101° — I sent for Dr. Gardner — he came at 9 — then 103°. Q — says it is indigestion — left remedies to be given every hour until midnight \+ a purge to be given in the m'f. I had a nap after dinner \+ a walk on the road from 6.30 to 7.30 — Weÿ. considerable time given to Jim — finished letter to Mary Cook \+ went to P.O. at 11.30 — To bed after midnight.
