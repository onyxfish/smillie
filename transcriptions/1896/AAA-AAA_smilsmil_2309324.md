## Left Page

### Wed. April 1, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day — got up with a head that was still duller — After getting to the studio \+ finding that I was unfit for work I went down town to 31 Chambers St. Dep't. of Public works, hoping to see Mr. Stephenson Towle, Engineer, to whom I had a card of introduction — to arrange, if possible some plan to carry surface water of Griffen Court across the sidewalk. He was not in — on my way up Broadway, bought a hat. Went to Safe Deposit \+ left documents there. Home to lunch at 1 — to studio at 2 \+ was able to do some work, sketches from Easter lilies — Dinner at 6 45 — belated by being caught in the Barnum Circus Parade — 3d. Ave. \+ 24th S. Elephants Camels, Elevated R.R. Cable Cars — Calcium lights — myriads of torches — bands of music \+ great crowds made a strong, picturesque combination — Every. Rain. Mary took Jim \+ Ralph to a drill of Mr. White's Berkley School boys at the 34th S. \+ 4th Ave. Armory — They came in at 10 — Mrs. R — \+ I got to beds

## Right Page

### Thur. April 2, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another dull day with a little wet, but clearing breezy \+ cold at night. I spent the morn'f at work with pen \+ ink making sketches of Easter lillies — Home to lunch \+ back at 2 \+ worked upon more sketches of same subject — It was 6 o'clk. before I left the studio — Then I walked until 6.30 \+ home to dinner. Evef. I tried to do a little reading — or some writing, but, as usual, the rest slipped by and at bed time I had accomplished nothing. Chare came in \+ stayed until 10.30

To-day, at last, the carpenters, painters \+ paper-hanger got out of the "nursery" which is very much improved in appearance.
