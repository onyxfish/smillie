## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 22, 1896

**Weather:** Very bright \& pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very bright \& pleasant — It was about 10 when I got to the studio. I put in a good days work on Genl. Green's portrait \& finished the painting of the monochrome sketch. Between 5 \& 6.30 P.M. I walked up to 70th St. \& back. After dinner — read to boys — \& after they went to bed wrote some letters.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 23, 1896

**Weather:** Dull day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day. I got to studio about 9.30 \& spent the day in getting mezzotint tools in order \& experimenting in the matter of laying mezzo. grounds. For the first time I tried pole-rocking. At first I was much discouraged, but I soon got the hang of it \& found that I got along far better \& easier than by the old hand method. Barmore came in late in the afternoon and found me at my mezzotint — when he had helped that I was polishing off Genl. Lee. Katie T. worked at [illegible] studio in the [illegible]. Evng. after dinner, Reading to the boys until their bed time — Then Chark. came in — I wrote some notes.
