## Left Page

### Sun. March 22, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

miss Olga Waite Ret? to Po'k.

Overcast with some rain in the morning clearing in the afternoon. Got up late \- Bkfst. about 10 \- after a little time reading newspaper I worked on a scheme of finance by which the Nat. Academy of Design could buy a site up town \- put up buildings, etc, all within the means at its command \- Before 1 o'clk. went to studio for a few minutes \+ then to Walter Cook's \+ got some building estimates. Dinner at 2 \- Then for a time with Ralph \+ some of his lessons \- After 4, lay down for a rest \- At 5.30, went with Miss W. to R.R. Station where she took 6 P.M. train for Po'k. Katie T \- Jim \+ Ralph accompanied us. Then R. \+ I took a short walk before returning home to tea \- Eva \+ some reading to the boys. Charl came in later \-

## Right Page

### Mon. March 23, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day of falling snow \- very dark and quite cold. I got to studio a little later than usual \+ spent some time trying to arrange a financial scheme by which the N.A. could afford to buy an up-town site \+ carry it for 10 years before building upon it at about 12 I went to Columbia College in 49th St to see Pres't. Seth Low \- He had already gone but w'd return at 3 \- Then I went home to studio after lunch \+ worked at various things until about 4 \- Then again to Columbia College. Saw Pres't Low \+ had a very pleasant conversation with him concerning N.A. site \+ plot of ground on 116th St. south of C. College \+ bldg now going up \- Then walked down to Dav't. Huntington's in 20th St \+ saw him \- He is much pleased with the plot on 116th St. as a possible site for N.A. Then home to dinner \+ after that to Council meeting. In spite of the weather every member, except Blum (who is not well) was present \- After adjournment, Site Com. matters were earnestly discussed \- The division being a sharp one between away up town or down town between 42d \+ 59th S. It was midnight before I got to bed.
