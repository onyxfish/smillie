## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 19, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some sunshine — some shadows + a little bit of a shower — Most of my working day was given to getting mount cut, glass cleaned, of proof of father's eng. "Rocky Mts." belonging to Century Assn. into its new frame. In the afternoon I went down to printers + got proof in pages — after looking over it I determined to go at once to [illegible] + ask him to criticize it — found Alice at home + left it with her. Home at 6 — dressed for dinner — Smily Taylor, of P'o K. + Catherine Cook dined with us — later, Geo + Nellie came + yet later I was surprised by a call from Mr. + Mrs. Squint. Coleman who have just arr. from Newport. All left at 9.30 — then I went to Fred J's + sat with him for awhile and we went over the proofs, he making several corrections, I left + went home before 11.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 20, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day generally, but not so pleasant particularly. Rather cold + windy — I spent a part of the mf. with the printer arranging details about in the matter of N.A. Report + letter — then back to studio + finished pasting proof of Rocky Mts. into its new frame. Diggs came about noon + commenced cleaning windows — so that I was late in getting home to lunch + late getting back — The rest of the afternoon was spent writing addresses of N.A.'s on envelopes. Home at 6. Wt+. getting out my letter to N.A.'s in site matters — the work much increased my stupidity in printing, the maps being put in upside down. I tore them all out and pasted them in correctly — I had some help from Katherine + Bessie but was not able to get them all mailed by 11 o'clk. in time for the last pick-ups. Charl was in for a little while.

I spent part of mf. at N.A. p.D. Varnishing day for autumn Exhib.
