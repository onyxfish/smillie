## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 12, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day opened hot \+ hazy, but soon after noon thunder began to rumble, the storms coming up from the east. Heavy fall of rain commenced about 2.30 \+ continued with few intervals until after 8 P.M. — Bkfst. at 7 \+ with the boys went to R.R. station to see them off on 7.50 train for Heart Lake where they went fishing — I had along morning work in studio etching bit on Road near Jones's lake. coud'nt get the hang of it — I don't like my style \+ am trying to get out of ruts. Dinner at 1. At 2.45 went to station in heavy rain — when train came in rain held up for a few minutes — boy came back all right — D.S.S. Mulford's reply at train from N.Y. \+ Fanny Connell with it — all leaves \+ friends met at station — heavy shower again as they drove to cemetery — I went to Boyd \+ Cooley's \+ P.O. then home — too dark to see to etch — Read newspaper. Tea at 6.30 — Pkg came from Cullen with Printing frames, Solio paper, etc — Much of the ev'g I was preparing solutions to do some Photo. Printing.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 13, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ warm — weather rather queer. After a late bkfst. I went to my dark room \+ made note of several chemicals that I needed to make solutions for the photo. Printing processes — At 10.30 I went to A.B. Burns' \+ got them. On my way home I stopped at Ben. Mulford's \+ saw him \+ his wife \+ Fanny Connell. I was there until near noon — Then home \+ until dinner, at 2, I was mixing \+ compounding Toning \+ fixing baths — After dinner until 6 o'clk. I was setting out Lattuce plants in old frames. Did not take any tea \+ having a headache I went to bed with the boys at 9 o'clk.
