## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 3, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant day, altho' very warm  **Temp:**

Jim had a very thorough cleaning out but his temp. continued above normals until the afternoon. Dr. G. came at about 11 \+ still thinks that the trouble is indigestion. Mary \+ her girls, Kate \+ Ralph started for the Mulford's, at Friendsville, at 10 o'clk. I worked about, clearing up studio \+ getting things ready for etching, until Dr. G. came. Went to P.O. at 12 \+ had my dinner, solitary \+ alone, at 1. Then I went to lot where men were working \+ to P.O. At 5 my head ached so that I lay down \+ had a troubled nap until 7 — when the party ret'd from J. having had a delightful time \+ left Ralph there who was infatuated with the camp \+ begged to stay over night. I sat with Jim until 9 — he seems much better. Mrs. Cansland came \+ had a rehearsal of "the two lunatics" with Bessie. After 10 I went to P.O. with letters.

Three Italians worked in ditches. Hibbard \+ Rutan worked among the stone.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 4, 1896

**Weather:** Bessie T.'s three school friends from Pok. arr.  **Temp:**

A very hot day. Ralph ret'd from his night out at the Mulford Camp before noon, very much pleased with his adventure. Dr. Gardner came before 10 o'clk. \+ says that Jim is better \+ needs no cane \+ a gargle that he prescribed. Priscilla Poor \+ Kitty Schafer called about the Comstock dag't. \+ took it away. After dinner I worked awhile on gelatine tracing of Wad. Sq. — garden tower. Some time spent looking after the men at work in swamp lot. After tea Mary \+ Bessie went to Station \+ met Miss Huey, Taylor \+ Misses Grace \+ Margaret Buck. I went to P.O. The girls chatted on piazza until 9 o'clk. It was 11 when I got to bed.

Three Italians finished digging ditches. H. \+ R. Continued work among the stones.
