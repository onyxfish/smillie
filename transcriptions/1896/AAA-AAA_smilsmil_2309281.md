## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 2, 1896

**Weather:** Bright & beautiful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & beautiful day — Ralph still has fever & medicines given him are inoperative — Immediately after blfst. I went to Yale's & consulted with him. Returning home I gave R. an enema — with good results. He was kept in bed all day. Sent out remaining 10 invitations to N.A.s — Then to Tiffany's & saw Dorist — had an interesting talk with him concerning N.A. matters — Then to Bank and arranged some business there — Home to lunch at 1.15 — then to studio & for the rest of the afternoon I was busily at work clearing in & preparation for the N.A.'s whom I hoped would accept my invitation in the eveng. Home at 5.30, very tired & lay down for an hour before dinner. At 7.45 went to studio & lighted up — At 8.15 E.J. Henry came — 20 m. later Francis Newbury arrived & at 8.45 C.Y. Turner put in an appearance — at he was the last man — Three N.A.'s had interest enough to accept my invitation — we three talked together for about an hour — It was 10 o'clk when I got home — Chane There it was near midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 3, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant day, altho' at times cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, altho' at times cloudy \+ raw. I put in perhaps about a half day of work on the Lee portrait plate — but had divers interruptions — See. notes from Perry & how. I wrote to P. at once. Hall lantern in lower studio hall put up. I got home at 5.30 & had a little rest before dinner. At 8 I went to studio — At 8.30 br. Ge. came along & at near Eastman Johnson came — and the two constituted my N.A. party on the second night. Johnson seemed to be in splendid spirits & talked away until 11.30. I felt so discouraged & humiliated that I took little interest in what he said & found it intolerably tiresome. When at last I did get home & to bed I lay for some time looking ahead & planning. I determined to make as good a fight as I can at the special meeting on the 15th — & if the fight goes against me, then, without sulks or complaint I shall, at the annual meeting, give up my office & after obtaining my freedom devote myself to my own work & interests — Then, to sleep. Ralph has seemed to be very well all day.
