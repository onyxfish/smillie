## Left Page

### Thur. May 7, 1896

**Weather:** Clear, breezy & cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Anniversary of our Wedding.

Clear, breezy & cool — Jack Cook was sent around to go with Ralph to school — as I could not trust the midget with R— alone I took the two of them to school & then went to Borrmanu's about my disabled bicycle — It was late when I got to studio & then I gave the rest of the m'f to preparing Academy Report. After lunch I went to N.A. & spent sometime there with Farrington in going over accts, then to printers & to studio after 4 — little more done. A walk before dinner — after that I read Tom Grogan (T. H. Smith) to the boys — Katherine T— & Stewart Jessup went out after dinner & had an Ev'f. Ride in Central Park on a Tandem bicycle — Mary went to Po'k — this m'f. to be gone for two or three days — I went to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### Fri. May 8, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. The m'f spent at studio working upon Treas'· Report & for awhile on Site Com— Report — Chas. Delham came in for awhile — I went home to lunch at 1 & was surprised to find Priscilla Beatty Pore & her daughter there — They stayed to lunch & we had a very pleasant time — N.T. went to Po'k this m'f. To studio at about 3 & before 5 I went to N.A. & saw Farrington about Academy accts & then to C. Brien's, the printers, about printed statement for Annual Meeting, again to studio — then home. Dinner at 6.30 Ev'f. Grace Dorland took a party to Theatre. H.F. Irving & Miss Terry in Merchant of Venice — Jim & Ralph were invited — I let R— go because I wished him to get a good start. Catherine, Edna & Tom Cook came to the house — Sister Kate & Bessie T— completed the party — Grace came to the house & all started from there — I went to H. W. Butler's to call — No one at home — then I went to Century & there met some artist fellows & sat with them until after 10 — Then home.
