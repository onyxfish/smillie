## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 3, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day — sleet and snow. To studio early & spent the m'f. scraping on my mezzotint experimental plate, "Adirondack lake". After lunch I spent the afternoon printing four p'fs & doing a little work between. Home before 6 and had a half hours rest before dinner. Eve'g. Charl took the girls to Palmer's Theatre to see Drew in "The Squire of Dames" I went to Council Meeting — but little business — & after it, a sociable eve'g until 11 o'clk. Then home in time to meet Charl & the girls — It was just 12 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 4, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very nasty day — snow & drizzle. After getting to studio I started the laying of a new experimental mezzotint ground — on an old etched plate" Deer's leap, Lake George" Using a fine tool & 10° angles it took me all day & then I did not get through by seven times. No artist would ever lay a mezzotint ground — it is too purely mechanical & tiresome a piece of work. But when once a good ground is laid, then the artist would have a good time sampling effects. I took a short walk, in the drizzle & slush, before dinner. Eve'g. Charl came in — I got to bed early.
