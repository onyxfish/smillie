## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 8, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant cold day with cutting wind. To studio at 10 — worked for nearly an hour, when, finding it uncomfortably cold, I went to 2°. Nat. Buk — then to Putnams — then to Am. Art Galleries \+ saw the Chase collection — Then to N.A. — and to studio at noon. Worked until ·) on dee plate, then home to lunch — back at 2.15 \+ worked on plate until 4.45 when I could see no longer — Took a walk \+ attended to some small matters — Paid Yale's bill. On my return to the house found Mr. \+ Mrs. Howard R. Butler waiting — B. talked of N.A. matters. Dinner — a very quiet evg.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 9, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snowing most of the day — dull, cold, unpleasant. Kate went to Pok. at 10 A.m. About that time I got to studio \+ at once set to work taking a working pf. from the Lee portrait. Then I worked upon the plate — then another pf — I put in a good days work, taking four pfs. \+ working between. The Real estate men have begun to bother me again concerning N.A. sites — I had to-day one call \+ two letters on such business. Eve'g, gas behaved badly at home, so that we ate our dinner with a kerosene lamp on the table for our light — I wrote several notes before getting to bed early.
