## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 28, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

light rain during the night — cloudy out pleasant. Got to work in etching room biting "The Lake, from the hill"? Some delay owing to confusion of old acids — finally fixed new nitric 3 acid to 5 water — then all went well immediately after dinner went on wheel to Presbt. Ch. \+ started Ambrose Payne painting organ pipes — then rode out to Harrington's Mills — saw H. arranged with him to do some ploughing from there back to my work until near 5 — then out with my pruning shears \+ did some work — Evef. Reading. Wrote notes to Croton Water Bureau with reference to Water bills for Studio reld's in '95 — \+ to Miss Louise Miller about Ralph \+ his school beginning — Then to P.O.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 29, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

heavy misty clouds all day — wind from N.E. Rain began falling soon after 4 o'clk. A wild night of wind \+ rain. — In the m.f. Harrington's man \+ team came \+ ploughed the rough hillside which I propose to terrace \+ where I will set out half a dozen apple trees. I worked on the lake from hill plate, drawing in upon the bitten plate distance, sky, etc — After dinner I bit again \+ cleaned the plate. I have not been very successful in managing my acid — parts are under bitten — Then I went down town — to P.O — to Barney's to Stone's — to Routain's \+ got thro' with a number of chores before I got home at 5.30. Evef. I went to P.O. \+ to Pape's \+ ordered stage \+ [illegible]. for to-morrow m.f. as Jim \+ Tom are to start for N.Y. to-morrow to go to school. I read for awhile \+ got to bed early.
