## Left Page

### Mon. April 13, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably warm day — quite prostrating. I went to studio \+ worked on N.A. site paper for a little while, then the carpenter came with the cases that are to be fitted into closet \+ for some time I was engaged with him. When he was fairly started I left him \+ returning to the house I resumed work upon N.A. paper \+ continued it until lunch — Immediately after that I went down town, first to Dep. of Public Works to see Engineer Stevenson Towle. not in — Then to Hendersons \+ bot. some deeds \+ sent them to Montrose. Then to Geo. Read's \+ saw Van Pelt for a few minutes concerning uptown N.A. sites — Then to Leat hes \+ there went over affadavit \+ brief concerning transfer or legacy Tax — swore to \+ signed affect then to Chambs St. Dep. P. W. \+ saw Mr. Towle for a few minutes He told me that the only way out of the Griffen court difficulty was to build at the end of the court at the line of the Sidewalk a silt basin strap with iron grating at the opening on the street level. — Then to studio \+ after a little time with carpenter there I went home \+ had a rest before dinner — after dinner I went to N.A. council meeting which was very tame \+ commonplace. Adjourned at 9.30 — I went to the galleries \+ spent a short time looking at the pictures born after 10 Geo. \+ I walked up town — To bed early.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 14, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another uncomfortably hot day — Again the carpenter had possession of my studio while putting in the pigeon-holes \+ drawers in closet. \+ again I worked at home, the most of my time being devoted to N.A. site paper which bids fair to be quite a sermon in length, at least. I am writing in favor of a site opposite Columbia Univ \+ on 116th St. \+ am trying to make my arguments as orderly \+ exhaustive as I can. I continued the same work in the afternoon until after 4, then I went to Brooks Bros. with Ralph \+ got for him \+ myself, light top spring overcoats — then to studio \+ home. Eve'g at home.
