## Left Page

### Thur. April 23, 1896

**Weather:** Bright & cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate Adams was married this m'g

Bright & cool - I got up with a headache but it wore off towards eve'g. After marketing etc. I walked down to grammary park & called at Jno. Bigelow's hoping to see him, but found that he had rented his house - lived out of town - & I could not learn his address. Then to Am. Art Ass'n. & saw Wm. H. Gibson's collection of sketches. Then to 2? Nat. But. & then, to studio at about 12. I set palette & did a little painting on 19×30 before lunch. After that took Ralph with me to the Rooms of Misses Miller & Small, 138 2.40th & conferred with them upon the school question - & agreed to send R. to their school next winter. To studio at 3 - & painted a little more. Home at 5 had a rest until 6 - then dressed & at 7 was at H.R. Butler's 135W.47. S— Miss Jones, with Mr. & Mrs. B - & self made a little dinner party of four. Miss J - is a friend of the Colemans & has a house at Newport. She made herself agreeable & left at 10. I remained a half hour longer to have a more private chat with Mr. B's - & B. walked with me to Cor. of 5th Ave. & 42d S. I got home shortly after 11 & to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 24, 1896

**Weather:** Very pleasant m'g  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant m'g. - weather thickening as the day went on - some light rain beginning about 4 P.m. more later. I got to studio about 10 - m'g work much interfered with by plumber who was putting in water connections in my acid room. I did some pitiful work upon my painting of "Capri" I should have written that Harry Watrous called at the house about 9 o'clk. & for nearly an hour we talked N.A. Soc matters. After lunch, with Jim & Ralph, I went to Barnum's Circus at The Mad'n. Square Garden. I found some amusement, but the boys found more. At 4.30 we got out - a light sprinkling rain - They went home & I went to Brooks Bros where I bought trousers & exchanged a Spring overcoat that I ought a few days ago for a longer & darker one. Then to studio & home. Dinner - The Throb girls dined with W. deBisschels & his daughter & her husband at The Hotel Martin Cor.9th S: & University Pl. I went down there at 9.30 & found in addition to those named, Miss Kuleke & Dr. & Mrs. Allen - all were yet at the table. The deB's speech nothing but French so, our party found it was uphill work. We left soon after 10 - the deB's to sail for home to-morrow m'g. We left Miss R— at her home - & then on to our home.
