## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 7, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very hot day — Tempered in the afternoon somewhat by Thunderstorms all about us. There was quite a shower in the nlf about 6 — \+ two or three light ones in the p.m. \+ evef. That amt. to nothing. I spent much time with lettuce in the nlf, cutting many heads \+ sending to neighbors. Callers consumed the rest of the nlf. After dinner it was too hot to work \+ so I had a nap \+ then I worried for awhile over the Mad. Sq. Tower Tracing — also, looked after the men at work. After tea several callers. Later in the evef Mr. Warner came \+ with the girls had a rehearsal of one of the plays to be given nxt Ws? evef.

P's team \+ man — lost ½ hour in the nlf — also Sherman — Hubbard \+ Katain — three Italians at work.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 8, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Carleton Cook arr? from Buffalo

Still very hot. my day was much broken up. The girls got up a home Pic-nic \+ drove over to Friendsville to the Mulford Camp taking Ralph with them they started at 12. There was a balloon ascension from back of School House at 2.30 — I started to see it but it was too quick for me. I started a "poster" to advertise the play of "two Lunatics" to be given at Village Hall nxt Wsd. evef — I also looked after the setting down of two big baskets of string beans — \+ went to the swamp to see how the men were getting along. Tea at 6.30. At 7.30 went to Station — found the girls there — just in from Friendsville — we waited for the train — Carleton came on it — they all rode up to the house I went to P.O. Another balloon ascension — 8.30 — I stopped on the green \+ was interested in the picturesqueness of the scene — the vagueness of twilight — the grounds — the great form of the balloon — the artificial lights — the ascent — the excitement — the fireworks far up in the sky \+ drifting away. The young folks amused themselves until a little after 10, then to bed —
