## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 8, 1896

**Weather:** Bleak + cloudy, clearing at sunset.  **Temp:** [illegible]

The two teams continued the ploughing in the Tyler lot, getting through with the swampy part + up onto the higher ground. Stone worked with Spense + got the beech that was cut down yesterday, sawed up, most of it split + in the cellar. Knoll came + I paid him $16.00 for doctoring the cow. At noon Charl, Ralph + I took our wheels to the fair ground + rode there awhile — Then out + tried the road but it was too rough — I worked in studio about 2 hours on "Lake from Hill" + "Mad. Sq. Tower" opening lines for exhibiting. About 5 went to P.O. then below town + examined the big cess pool being built to take a part of the town's sewage. I think a serious mistake is being made there in adopting the cesspool system. Ev'g. Reading + chatting until after 10.

Two teams ploughing all day —

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 9, 1896

**Weather:** A leaden overcast, clearing to be a cloudless brilliant day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

No studio work — all the m'g. I was about the place with Charl — The two teams finished the ploughing in the swamp lot soon after 11.30. The afternoon I spent among the trees, pruning. Ev'g. Reading to R—, + newspapers.

Two teams ploughing ½ day.
