## Left Page

### Fri. July 10, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. After my usual out of door duties I again tackled the Comstock miniature, having one more shadow put for a foundation. Again I worked quite hopefully through the first stages but as I progressed the old troubles came — the eyes refused to have the right expression & the mouth failed to tell the story. Before noon I tried & went down to the lot where the boys were making a "lake", thence on to P.O. Dinner at 1. Resumed work on miniature & at 4 gave up in despair & wrote to Priscilla that after a week of work & four efforts, to my great shame & mortification I was obliged to give it up. Took letter to P.O. & then to where boys were at their "lake". Tea at 6.30. At 8 to P.O. then writing. A little thundershower between 10 & 11 p.m.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 11, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day — quite warm, but with a fresh breeze & lovely clouds. Before 1 p. I went down to lower end of Tyler lot where it opens on the rail road, & gave directions to two darkies who are to dig it & dam up a pond there for the boys to play. Later I started a drawing about 6"×12". of the tower of Mad. Ave. Garden as seen from 26th St., E. of 4th Ave. I have a number of photos, taken 2 or 3 years ago, with a view to this drawing for etching. I did not progress very rapidly — the work of laying out such a subject is somewhat dreary. In the afternoon there was a balloon ascension which made some excitement. I just failed to see it. I took a short tramp before tea. What a horribly uninteresting country this is for any such enjoyment. I am getting to feel quite hateful about it. The house & its immediate surroundings are all that can be desired — beyond that there is nothing. Eve'g. Every body went off & I was left alone with the boys. I had to mend —
