## Left Page

### Tues. July 14, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Theatrical Performances Village hup: S.

A very lovely day — fresh \+ invigorating. After my m'f duties I went to Village Hall to see \+ hear a Rehearsal of "The Jack Trust". Much time lost because McCausland was belated owing to business engagements — I was there until 12. wrote to Charl. — dinner at 2 — Afternoon did some work on "Mad. Sq. Garden Tower from 26 St. E. \-\- f 4th Ave." After 5 o'clk a short ride a bicycle — Jim off fishing with his new rod \+ outfit — with Bostwick — Tea at 6.30 — About 8 took Mary \+ the boys to Village Hall — then to P.O. back to Hall at 8.30 — Performance opened with Piano, Singing \+ recitations — Then "The Jack Trust", by Misses Cromwell, Taylor, Kitty Riley \+ Katie \+ Bessie T. \+ Jas. McCausland. It went off unexpectedly well — all taking their parts well — \+ some of them, very well — Mc C — was very good — the girls were all ammusing — It was about 10.30 when the performance was over \+ then there was dancing — There was a full house of excellent character. \- The performance of the Trust was an unmistakeable secccess.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 15, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A rainy m'f until after 11 — Then for a time sunshine struggled struggled — A heavy shower at about 2 o'clk — light showers afterwards — Air, heavy \+ close — B\+f's. delayed a little by last night's dissipation — After \+ I set out lettuce plants until the rain drove me in. I did some work on the Mad. Sq. Garden Tower — It is difficult \+ tedious getting the proportions correctly \+ I find no pleasure in it. — After tea I went down to P.O. with the boys — to bed early.
