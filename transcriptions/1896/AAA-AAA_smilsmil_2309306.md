## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 23, 1896

**Weather:** Bright m\[illegible\]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m\[illegible\] \- growing much milder \- a very little rain in the afternoon \- some moonlight at night. Bkfs. before 10 \- went to studio \+ opened the windows for general airing \- about noon went up to Central Park with the boys \- Riding to 106" S \- entering at 5° Ave \- out at 90° S \+ home 20 m. late for dinner. Kate dined with Ge\[illegible\] \+ his family \- After dinner to studio and closed doors \+ windows. Had a rest before 6 o'clk. Tea at 6.30 \- Swe\[illegible\] amused myself by clipping stray bits from Montrose paper \+ pasting them together in such way as to form rediculous paragraphs \- went to G. C. Station at 10 \+ mailed it to the Throop girls at New Haven \- Then to studio to look after fire \- Charl spent part of eve\[illegible\] with us \- He is to go to Boston to-morrow.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 24, 1896

**Weather:** Bright m\[illegible\]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m\[illegible\] \+ so much milder that it seemed almost spring-like \- threats of showers in the afternoon \+ some sprinkling of rain \- After my usual m\[illegible\] duties \+ getting to studio I went to 2° Nat. Bk. \+ made deposit \- Then to Bon\[illegible\] \+ saw Schaus coll. of paintings \- then in to Kurtz's \- called on Calverly \- much disappointed to find that he has made no further progress in modelling bust of Smilie \- Got to studio about 12 \+ worked upon mezzo. plate for\[illegible\] Greene for an hour, then home to lunch Back at 2 \+ continued scraping on Greene plate until about 4.30 \- Then with men in cellar who are putting down the last of the finishing cement \- Then took a short walk. Went into May Williams Co \- saw Mr Jordan and the Nichols stockings \- Home at 6. Swe\[illegible\] at 8 o'clk went to N.A. Council meeting \- no business of interest \- Home before 11.
