## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 5, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The weather a little pleasanter than yesterday — very raw + nasty. The m'f was spent at the studio finishing the experimental ground that I commenced yesterday afternoon. A laborious + uninteresting piece of work. Maynard came in quite unexpectedly at about 10.30 — n from Washington for a day — Katie T— + I went up to his rooms for a little while. Ralph spent the afternoon with me at the studio — I scraped on the plate "Boulders Leafs — Lake George" — Yulett, Real Estate, came in, I gave him a card of introduction to Jas. M. Hart. Home at about 6. Chase in for a few minutes — dinner — Evett. Reading aloud to the boys — working a little on one of Katie's sketches, and writing a couple of notes.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 6, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Tremendous storm of wind + rain. I got up with a very severe headache + was barely able, after my b'kf't, to go, by 6th Ave. cars, down to the Stewart B'd'g cor. of Chambers St. + B'dway, to answer a summons from Com'r. of Jurors to show cause why my name should not be put on the active list. I took with me a letter written by Dr. Yale, which I hope will secure for me exemption. Then I went to Com'r. of Taxes — Personal — Dain Muldine to answer why why I should not be taxed for $30000 personal property — I hope that I made that clear to him. Then I went to studio + was able to work for a little while on mezzo. ground in "Deer's Leafs — Lake George". Scratping — At 1 o'clk. I went home + to bed where the pain in my head increased to almost unbearable violence. My misery was complete. It was not until 10 o'clk. in the n'f that I was able to get up + take off my clothes.
