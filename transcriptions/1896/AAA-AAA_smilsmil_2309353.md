## Left Page

### Fri. May 29, 1896

**Weather:** Glorious day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day - up early - wrote notes to Donchian, The Augman at Hartford \+ to Mollie Adams - I told her that by the light of the m\? I must tell her that I could not keep my promise of the night before - I must get my work done before I go off on any play \+ fill these notes I took to G.C. Station - mailed them \+ telegr. to Mollie - Then home to bkfp. \+ sent Diggs to 24p. - with two rugs for Donchian. After bkf. - Went to Walters - family at bkf. - Stung Sprague there - W. is to send another stone man to me to make an estimate - Then to studio where I was busy writing letters \+ getting the Morse picture ready to send home. After lunch I packed brass furniture \+ storm doors together with letter box platis from studio front door \+ took them to Newman's in 29\+5= near 7c Ave. \+ left them to be refinished as "Suthan bronze". To studio at 3.30 - worked awhile on Gerster portrait - wrote more letters \+ some clearing up \+ home before 6. Rode my bicycle to Boorman's to have him doctor the front wheel - then Rode up 5ey - Ave. to 58\+5= across to Mad. Ave. \+ then down home, getting in in time for dinner - Every fine went with Merrill Bishops to Carnegie Hall - a School Declamation. I read awhile to Ralph. - Jim did not get in until 11 o'clk. I read \+ waited [illegible — ~10 words]

## Right Page

### Decoration Day —

### Sat. May 30, 1896

**Weather:** Early overcast, clearing to be a charming day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Early on\+, overcast, clearing to be a charming day. To studio at usual time \+ being dissatisfied with the crayon study of M\^r. Gerster's head I determined to try again \+, if possible, to make one, less labored in manner, \+ not so much of a pudding face. Home to lunch - back at 2.30 - Jenny Benson there - Took painting to W\^r. J\^as. Herbert Morse. After 3 D\^r. Gerster came in \+ later, M\^rs. Gerster came. M\^r. G. posed for me in the position of the photo. \+ M\^rs. D. sat behind me \+ directed me what to do - Rather a trying situation but on the whole I think the result was good. The muscle lines in the face were defined in a manner that was not flattering to M\^rs. G. - I assured her that it was not a caricature \+ that when I got to work at it alone I would modify away all such over-strong markings - They left at about 5 - to come next w\^d. p.m. I left soon after \+ went on Madison Ave. for awhile to see the crowds of bicyclers - Dinner at 6.30 - Then some reading to the boys. I Rode on my wheel until 10 o'clk. \+ got my cyclometer up to 399½ miles - I had intended to fill out the 400.
