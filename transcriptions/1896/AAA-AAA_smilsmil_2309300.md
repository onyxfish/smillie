## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 9, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal + disagreeable. drizzle, rain, sleet + snow. B\+p. at 9.30 After 11 Vedder came + together we went to the studio. He seemed to like Maynard's rooms + says he will occupy them for a time while he is in N.Y., Maynard being in Washington now at work upon Congressional Library decorations. I remained at studio until 1 o'clk. + then home. Dinner at 2 — Jn°. Valentine came in about 4 o'clk — for a call — at 5 I lay down for awhile + got a little rest. Tea at 6.30. About 9 o'clk. I went to Walter Cook's and arranged with him to have Daoud, the builder of my studio, take charge \+ alterations in cellar to control the water that has broken out there again. Charl spent part of eve'g at [illegible]

I rec'd. a very kind note from Mr. James Herbert Morse asking me to allow Jim to visit with them for a week, commencing to-morrow. Mr. M— is J's teacher.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 10, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Breezy, bright day. The wk at the studio was divided between scraping on "from Dean's ledge, Lake Geo." + Rocking mezzo. tnt ground with fine tool over the face part of the plate for portrait of Genl. Green. Home to lunch at 1 — after that I went to Century + left there a key to outer door of my studio's self. for Vedder. Ralph was with me as there was no one with whom he could play or stay at home. Then we went to Met. Mus. of Art in Cnt. Park. Annual meeting. Twenty members constitute a quorum but there were only twelve present — Reports were read + then we adjourned. Ralph was allowed to amuse himself in the gallery during the meeting. di Cesnola, after our meeting, took me to see some portions of the Mr. Jn°. Crosby Brown collection of Musical instruments. not yet opened to the public — busy. N.A. Council meeting — nothing interesting — adjourned early — found Charl at the house when I got home.

Green' 12 day
