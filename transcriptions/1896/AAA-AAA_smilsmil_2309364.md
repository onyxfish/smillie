## Left Page

### Sat. June 20, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm, enervating day. After a restless night I did not feel like doing any thing, so, much of the morning was spent with Stone \+ in loafing about with a headache. In the afternoon I feel better and worked in the shop a a job in carpentering, making a new aquatint box for laying dry grounds. I took a short ride on my wheel before tea. \- Every, with my lettuce plants for some time. \- Then, until after 10, sitting on piazza, enjoying as much as I could, a wonderfully beautiful moonlight.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 21, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A close day with a refreshing little shower about 2 o'clk. \- delightful afternoon with a promise, unfulfilled of more showers in the swell. B'kf't at 9 \- after that, some time reading \- then weeding \- then I got to writing some letters that should have been written long ago. \- Dinner at 2 \- a nap after dinner \- more reading \- then some writing \+ then a little riding of my wheel about the grounds before tea time. \- After tea all went out to the carriage house. Jim, Ralph \+ I on our bicycles. \- Ralph at last learned to make the pedal mount. Then the three of us went on our wheels out to the top of the hill overlooking the lake \+ back \- then the boys to bed \+ a little more writing.
