## Left Page

### Sun. July 12, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day in spite of pleasant breezes. Had a good start in the m'f & got thro' with considerable work of one kind & another. Dinner at 2 & most of the afternoon spent in reading "Naulahka", by Kipling & Ballestier — very little to the credit of either of them, I think. Then I sauntered about — it was too warm for anything else — until tea time — then on piazza & reading to Ralph until 9 — then writing letters until near 11 — then to P.O. with them.
By the way, the work of the m'f was done in the dark room trying to develop a spool of films, exposures by Jim in his new camera. For some reason that I was not able to determine, every one of them were failures.
