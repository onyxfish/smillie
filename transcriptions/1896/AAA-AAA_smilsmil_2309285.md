## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 10, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant day \- milder.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to work at about 10 on dee plate & such to it all the m'f. Home to lunch at 1 \- Chas. Barmore, the Publisher of the dee portrait, came in soon after 1 \- when I had had my lunch we walked to the studio together \- the saw for the first time a lot of the plate. He was very much pleased with it & spent more than an hour discussing some of his projects. He wishes me to do plates for him \- he was much pleased with one of my projects, of which I told him something \- that of etching Eastman Johnson's portrait, painted by himself \- in old Dutch Burgomaster Costume \- I wrote to Apollo Stone & Wm Spense, at Montrose. After dinner I went to the Chase sale at Am. Art galleries, & bought a plain print of an etching by Kopping after Liebermann. Its market value was about $12 but Kirby made me pay $5.0 pr \- It is to me a very interesting work \- as an etching, & as a print \- I don't know where I could get another copy, so am not unhappy over the price. Stopped in at the N.A. & there saw Wood, Hart, & Brown. We had a talk over a most recent insubordination in the school. Home before 9. wrote letter to Margaret Parker White \- Redlands, Cal \- Chase came in at 10 & stayed for an hour.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 11, 1896

**Weather:** Brilliant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

After getting through with the routine duties of the m'f. I went to Kimmel & Voigt's, getting there at about 10. Got two pf. of dee \- disappointed in them \- not what I want \- lacking a quality that I can get on my own press. Barmore came there & met me. He had with him an old miniature of Genl Green painted in oil by Col. Trumbull. He wishes me to enlarge it & make a mezzotint 11" × 14". It interested me & I agreed to do it. To studio at about 12. To lunch at 1 & back at 2 \- The rest of the afternoon much broken up by gas fitter, lock-smith and carpenters. Four tons of coal were put in the cellar. It was near 5 when I got home & had a rest until dinner at 6.30 \- Every Jim went to his school \- Jerome's Dale \- & helped him "Recieve" \- Park Ave. & 39th S. Before & I went to Century \- Annual meeting \- after the meeting there was much talk among Academicians concerning the business to come before the meeting next wed. eve'g. It was about 1 o'clk. before I left for home.
