## Left Page

### Mon. June 22, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of high wind, growing much cooler — a thick haze of smoke in the afternoon. Ralph was up shortly before 4 A.M. the result of injudicious eating — his stomach was thoroughly cleared out. It was about 10 o'clk. when he \+ I went down town — most of the rest of the m't. was spent at Boyd \+ Cooley's while repairs were made upon R's bicycle. After dinner I indulged in a nap again — \+ then the rest of the afternoon was devoted to carpenter work finishing the aqua-tint box. — Evelf, after tea, R. \+ I went down town \+ attended to some business — after my return I wrote notes \+ checks for Dr. Yale \+ Ed\[illegible\] Clark — the man who paved my section of Griffen Court. Then for a time with newspaper.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 23, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day, clear \+ quite cool. After bkf. I wrote to Mr. Spencer H. Coon enclosing a note that I asked him to have pub\[illegible\] in the Tribune; in which I object to the allusions in Sunday's article to projects for lliance between Columbia University \+ M.M.M. of D. as being officially discussed — I went down town \+ was detained for some time in the office of the Editor of the Democrat arguing with the Editor against the silver craze \+ was a weak affair \+ time was wasted. Most of the afternoon was again spent in the shops finishing an aqua-tint ground box. About 5 R.j. \+ I went out on on wheels doing about 4 miles. I came in quite used up. The work in these hills \+ bad roads is too hard for me. Mrs. Rhinevault all day cleaning my studio's — Evelf. wasted over newspaper
