## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 28, 1896

**Weather:** A mild, quiet, half-clouded day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katie \+ Gretta went to N.Y.

A mild, quiet, half-clouded day. I got up early \+ at 7.30 went to station, bought tickets \+ started Katie \+ Gretta for N.Y. \- Then home to bkfst. Mrs. Van Auken is to have charge of our kitchen until our departure. I spent the m't out of doors working among the trees. Dinner at 1 \- more work among trees with hot bar \- Then I packed partly my big studio box \- At 5 I got a number of bills \+ drew checks for them \- Tea at 6 o'clk \- At 7.30 Charl \+ I went down town. I paid a number of bills \- Joe Williams walked up to the house with Charl \+ me. I read awhile to Ralph \- Then attended to my chores \+ after some newspaper reading went to bed.

While down town I telegraphed to Little enquiring for my belated trees.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 29, 1896

**Weather:** A very lovely day \- warm \+ hazy  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day \- warm \+ hazy. About 10 o'clk Harrington came bringing the trees from the Rochester nursery of W. S. Little \+ Co. Spencer \+ I got to work as soon as possible \- Stone came after dinner \+ we all worked until 5 o'clk \- getting in the 2 Chestnut \- 2 Black walnut, 2 Butternut \+ 2 Hickories. At 5.30 Charl \+ I went to P.O. \+ I attended to some small matters of business \- Tea soon after 6 \- Charl is to return to N.Y. to-morrow \+, by arrangement made to-day, Bessie is to go with him \-
Ev'g packing rugs, etc \- Wm. son of Elisha Mulford, called.
