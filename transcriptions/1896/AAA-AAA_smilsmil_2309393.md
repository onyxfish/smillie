## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 17, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant September-like day — very cool \+ breezy — cloudy afternoon. After bkfst: I wrote a letter to Jim \+ at 10.30 took it to P.O. Some little matters kept me until 11 — then I went to lot to look after the Italians who are finishing the filling in of the ditches. After that, as I am feeling far from well, I took a stroll to the top of Dunton's hill in the meadow. The air was delicious — Dinner at 1 — Again I was unfit for studio work \+ after lying around until past four o'clk I went out on my bicycle — Rode about 4 miles, bad roads \+ hills made me very warm. Had a rub down \+ read newspaper until tea time. At 7.30 went to Roger Searle's \+ had a talk with him as to best treatment for the land I have just drained — To P.O. Then home. Some reading \+ to bed at 10.

The Italians finished filling ditches. Geo. gave the time as 21 h. for the 3 — attempted to have a settlement but found that there was a discrepancy in our time. By my tally it amounts to 69 d. 8 h. He claims 3d. 13[illegible] more — He is to come again with his book.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 18, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Breezy \+ cool with several showers, very light with us but quite heavy to the south. Ther. fell last night to 50°. Geo. Walker came about 10 o'clk. to settle his acct. with me. It took some time — He cannot write English or Italian \+ has to depend upon others to help tally for him. He had been imposed upon \+ his tally did not agree with mine, but finally I succeeded in straightening it out to his satisfaction. I worked for a little while before dinner on gelatine tracing of Wall Sq. garden [illegible]. After dinner, at 2 o'clk. I went to swamp lot \+ met Roger Searle \+ talked with him about the best treatment for this reclaimed swamp. He advises shallow surface ploughing — deep sub-soil ploughing with an application of lime to the up turned sod — It was after 3 when I got to studio — a little more work on gelatine tracing. Then my head felt so badly that I took a little nap — After tea read to Ralph until 8.45 then he to bed \+ I read for more than an hour before I went to bed. Carleton \+ [illegible] went to a dance (subscription) at Village Hall \+ had such a good time that it was 2 A.m. before they got home.
