## Left Page

### Thur. June 18, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day \- A threat of a Thunderstorm in the afternoon but it passed south of us. Between times given to work about the place I wrote a paper for Mr. Spencer H. Coon which he asked me for, \+ which he may use in the Tribune. Late in the afternoon I went out alone on my wheel \+ made four miles of pretty hard work with but little pleasure in it. When I came in I gave myself a rubdown \+ a change \+ then had tea. Eve'g. I got a note from Donchian relating to the two rugs I sent to him at Hartford for cleaning \+ repair \- I wrote a reply \+ took it to P. O. "The boys" were having a great time with a big bonfire in front of the Court House, celebrating the nomination of McKinley as Republican Candidate for next Pres.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 19, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day \+ almost cloudless, growing hazy in the afternoon. Superb moonlight night. A good part of the m'g was spent in making a fair copy of my views concerning a site for the N.A. of D. near Columbia University \- This I mailed to Mr. Coon in time to go on the noon train. On my way back to dinner I went in \+ spent a few minutes with Azurdaturals who is a very sick man having been confined to his room now more than a month. After dinner I had a long, stupifying \+ unsatisfactory nap. Then I went down town \- paid Taxes and some other bills \- Loitered \+ read newspaper until tea time \- The usual quiet, profitless eve'g.
