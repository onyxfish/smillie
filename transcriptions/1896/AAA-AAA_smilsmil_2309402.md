## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 4, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - brilliant & breezy - I spent most of the m'f., first at Beach's machine shop, then at Newton blacksmith's shop & Boyd & Cooley's, getting a screw fitted to a swinging table that I intend to fix in my new corner by fireplace in studio - from 12 to 1 I worked on Lippincatt head - Dinner - At 3 o'clk - went on a drive taking Emily, Mary, Kate, Katherine & the two boys - went about road beyond So. Montrose, then around, returning over Butterfield's hill - down into the amusing valley \+ home over the Searle hill - a delightful ride. Tea at 6.30 - Supt at 7.30 went to Village Hall - another entertainment or benefit of V. Mb't Soct'y. Some singing & recitations - then a series of "Living Pictures" followed by a farce, "Per Telephone" in which Mary Sayre, Jana Lusk, Miss Warner & Messrs McCausland & Chas. Sayre took part - There was a full house, & everybody seemed to be pleased. We did not stay for the dance - Home \+ to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 5, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Geo. Cook arr'd. from N.Y., Tom Cook came from Buffalo.

Bright m'f. - clouding over before 9 o'clk - High East wind - Rain began to fall before 3 o'clk. I spent most of the m'f. setting out lettuce plants - then to work on Lippincatt head. Dinner - worked on plate until 4.30 - then went down town - immediately after tea I went to R.R. station. Train on time - Geo. Cook from N.Y., & Tom Cook from Buffalo, were on it. Put them into 'bus \+ I went down town - Home before 9 - then chatting for an hour \+ to bed.
