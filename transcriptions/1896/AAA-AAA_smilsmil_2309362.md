## Left Page

### Tues. June 16, 1896

**Weather:** Dull m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m't. \- Hazy sunshine during the middle of the day \- Cloudy, chilly & threatening in the eve't. Having agreed to turn over a new leaf we had blp \- at 7.30 \- I spent some time writing notes, one to Ella D incw \- to Clark the stone flagger, etc \- & about 11 went downtown Back before dinner at 1 \- After that, feeling very heavy, I had a nap \- Then I tried to write an "interview" for Coon of the Tribune who sent me an Editorial from yesterdays Sun urging close Relation between the N.A. f D. & Columbia University \+ asked me to write something that he can publish. I did not succeed in writing as I wished, so I set to work cleaning my bicycle & then went out for a Ride returning soon much tired & disgusted at the abominable roads & the hard hills. Tea before 7 \- a quiet eve't with newspapers which have gathered.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 17, 1896

**Weather:** Fine day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. blp. at 7.30 \- I tried during the m't. to write something for Spencer N. Coon concerning the N.A. & Columbia University but again I was obliged to give it up, unable to bring myself to the work. I spent some time with Appelles Stone wandering all over the place & getting his Reports. My head was so uncomfortable that soon after 12 o'clk I lay down & had a little nap before dinner at 1 \- Cooley & Rueam came up & took measurements so as to give an estimate for putting a Water Closet in bath room off my bedroom \- About 4 I got to work cleaning my bicycle & at about 5 Jim, Ralph & I went out for a short Ride \- we found the roads horribly rough compared with Central Park roads \- but we managed to get over a little more than five four miles \- I made repeated changes trying to adjust my new Mesenger saddle but could not ride upon it with entire comfort \- Jim complained of his & we made the exchange apparently to the advantage of both. Home soon after 6, a change \- tea at 6.30 \- twilight, watering flower beds as of old \- later, in the piazza looking away over the vague & dreamy moonlit hills \- alone \- but full of memories of Annie who used to stand upon such evenings \- with arms about me & head on my shoulder, both fros full of mute enjoyment \-
