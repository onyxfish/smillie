### Sat. May 23, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day \- To studio at 10 \+ worked for awhile on "Twilight in the Woods" \- Then took hold of "A meadow Stream" \+ worked for awhile on that \- wrote a letter to Sam Colman Home to lunch at 1 \- Kate arr. from Po'keepsie to be here next Sunday to meet Charl. I rec'd \- a Telegram from him this m'g. from Kansas City say-ing that he was due in N.Y. To-morrow eve't. A little more work at Studio in the afternoon \- at 5 went to Kahler's \+ got a pair of Russet shoes. Then home Dinner at 6.30. Eve'g. at about 9 o'clk I went to Century having an appointment with J.Q. Brown \+ Horace Robbins, members N.A. pd. Finance Com \- We got through with our business \+ then I chatted with a number of them \- I think that now I know the true inwardness of the 21 votes given to Alfred Jones for Treas. ## Right Page

### Sun. May 24, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charl. ret? from his Mexican Trip.

Pleasant \- Bkf. about 9.30 \- when the boys came in from S.S. \- I proposed a ride on our bicycles \- so, The Three fns started out at 11.30 \- up. Mad. Ave. 60th S't \- Central Park East side \- out at 106th S't \- West side to Boulevard up to Grant's tomb, back by Riverside Drive 72d. S't \- Boulevard to 58th S't \- to Mad. Ave. \+ home at 1 o'clk., our cyclometers showing about 13. miles. It was a very pleasant Ride \- a change \- a Rub-down \+ a rest. Dinner at 2 \- After 4 I went to Ja't. H. Morse's out of town \- Then up to 74th S't \- to call on Dr. Gessler but I had made a mistake in the address \- I went to 34 E. 74th S't \- I learned afterwards that I should have gone to 75th S't Then home \- Eve'g. Mr. \+ Mrs. Geo. Cooke called \- before they left Charl came \- just in from his Mexican Tour having been away about 1 month \- He seems to be in excellent health \+ spirits.
