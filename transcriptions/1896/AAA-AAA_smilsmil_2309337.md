## Left Page

### Mon. April 27, 1896

**Weather:** Clear & cool - cloudy, chilly eve'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10.30 feeling very unlike work — out of sorts generally — but I persisted in painting upon "Capri from Naples" helping to get it into a condition fit to send to Century meeting next Sat. Home to lunch at 1 & back at 2 & painted until 5 - Then home & had a rest until dinner at 6.30 — At 8 I went to Council meeting N.A. of D. I was feeling very uncomfortable owing to digestive derangements that were not made more bearable by the action of the Council on site business. Jas. Hart was (in the absence of Wood) in the Chair. Perry pushed through a Resolution approving a letter to be sent, from the Council, to 50 fellows of the N.A. — asking for their opinion as to whether it was best for the N.A. to select a site up-town or down-town — also — a circular to the N.A. asking an opinion upon same subject — all of which seems to me quite childish under the prevailing conditions of things. Home before 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 28, 1896

**Weather:** Overcast, chilly & raw.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After 10 when I got to studio & to work — The plumber finished putting in traps in acid room at studio. The Collins bros, Real Estate brokers, called concerning Watrous' 34th S. site & I was quite frank with them. Sam'l. Coleman called & gave me some valuable hints about the paintings I have under way. In following them I am sure that I very much bettered "Capri" and to-morrow I hope to follow his hints regarding "A Meadow Stream". Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 & worked until 4.30 — Then to N.A. & from Farrington got copies of Perry's two letters that are to be sent out to 50 fellows & to the N.A.s At first I thought of getting out a counter blast — but after a little consideration concluded that I could fight them better in other ways. Then home & had a rest before dinner. Eve'g. Read to Ralph until 9 — finishing Stockton's story "Adventures of Capt. Horn" — I think it the poorest of his stories that I have read — Then wrote to Mrs. Mulford who sent me $30 for Pony cart & harness that she has sold for me. Then out on my bicycle until almost 11.
