## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 1, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very nasty day - an inch or two of snow fell during the night + went away during the day in slush, melted by light rain + drizzle. I went to studio before 10. T— went with me + amused himself with water-colors, being joined later in the m't. by Jim. I worked until 1. scraping on j×9" mezzotint plates. Then home to lunch. In the after-noon I took p\[illegible\] of mezzos- + worked on it between p\[illegible\]. J. Baker came in bringing p\[illegible\] of some of his etched plates — very good amateur work. He came to get points upon biting + other etching processes. Lastly, the weather being very nasty, I did not have courage enough to go to Century meeting. I stayed at home. Read aloud to the boys until they went to bed. — Then read for myself.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 2, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine clear day — cool + windy — Jr. Dor came to the house about 10 o'clk. we went to studio where I showed him my mezzotint experiments work + I managed to get some valuable hints + suggestions of a practical character from him. Then I went off for a walk — going to Central Park — stopped at Hale's on my way back and saw Mr. Hale. Dinner at 2. after that some reading — At 4, lay down + had a nap. After 6 went to studio — closed windows. etc., as before? — Reading to boys until their bed-times. Then made up an acct. of my work on the plate and find that 40 days were more or less fully occupied upon it. It will pay me a little more than $10 per day net. Charl came in + spent the evg. with us.
