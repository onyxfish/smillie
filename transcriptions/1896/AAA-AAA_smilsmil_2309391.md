## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 13, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very comfortable day for temp. mild sunshine in the m'f., overcast afternoon. See hands on deck at the breakfast table — of course, the battles of the night before were fought o'er again — I wrote an acc't of the entertainment \+ took it down to the Ind. Rep'n. Young Stotesbury called \+ the girls in the m'f.

After dinner my head ached so that I had to go to bed for awhile. The young people all went off with Ch. Post on a pic nic to Salt Spring starting at 5 o'clk. They got back before 11 \+ Rain fell soon after. I got to bed early \+ had a very uncomfortable night owing to headache.

Bessie came home from the pic nic with a sprained ankle.

Three Italians in the ditches all day

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 14, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A heavy shower before m'f. Delightful midsummer day — Ther. did not get above 74° in my studio during the day. I went downtown at 10 — on my way back went to lot where the Italians were at work. Before dinner I wrote a note to Charl. After dinner Ch. Post \+ Norman Parks came \+ took the sisters Buck \+ Miss Taylor on a ride to Springville \+ Elk Lake — they ret? before tea time — at 5 I went down town \+ home. Two lot where the Italians were at work. Before tea I planted more lettuce seed.

Eve'g. great times — a bean year party by girls at Village Hall. Norman P. — munger — W'm. Warner \+ Carleton Cook took our girls to the party — Mary \+ Kate followed to look on — I got R. to bed — sat \+ read until a little after 10 — then to Village Hall — It was more than half anlong before I got away with Mary \+ Kate \+ rel? home \+ to bed. It was along about 1 o'clk. when the young folks came home.

Three Italians in the ditches all day — Pope's team \+ Shearman for half a day.
