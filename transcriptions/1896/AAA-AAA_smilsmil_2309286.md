## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 12, 1896

**Weather:** Dull \+ Raw, clearing overhead at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I had bkfst- after 9 \+ sat with newspaper for some time. At 11 went to studio \+ wrote two or three notes - Then walked to Century \+ took a look at the pictures there, meeting several acquaintances. Home at 2 for dinner. At about 4 I went to bed \+ had some sound sleep, making up for the loss last night. Tea at 6.30. Read to the boys for awhile after it. At about 9 I went to Walter's - saw Mr. Sprague Louise's mother - Stayed for an hour. Then to studio \+ attended to fire. Then home - Geo. \+ Charles there. To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 13, 1896

**Weather:** Cloudy day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio about 9.30 and to work on dee plates upon which I worked quite steadily all the m'f. Katie Throop spent me m'f at the studio (her first me) practicing pen drawing. To lunch at usual time \+ back soon after 2. The afternoon was given to dec Com. work until soon after 4 - Then I went to Mr. Huntington's at 2.20's. \+ saw him. The old gentleman (his 80) is in terrible earnest in his opposition to the co-operation scheme. He was almost hysterical he would not let me go out he insisted upon my listening to him. He says he lies awake nights thinking about it - it is descoration - camelice - I got away \+ went to Kahler's Bdway \+ 12's. \+ got a pair of shoes. Then home. Dinner \+ immediately after went to N.A. Jas Hart had called for a meeting of House Com. before Council meeting - an old trouble had broken out again. Harrington had offered his resignation. Hart, Perry \+ I talked it over \+ the thing was patched up. After Routine business at Council meeting. Perry offered a Resolution in effect to make the new Dec Com. a sub cme. of the Council. The object is to kill the Shelter scheme in any \+ every form - I objected but was voted down.
