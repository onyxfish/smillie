## Left Page

### Tues. June 30, 1896

**Weather:** Another clear, cool day. Ther. fell last night a little below 45°.  **Temp:** [illegible]

my out of door work busied me until after 10 — then I got out a 9×12 copper plate \+ my dry point out fit — put up the cluster of Rhododendrons, sent to me yesterday \+ commenced a dry-point. At 12 took a 2½ mile ride on wheel. Dinner at 1 — Afternoon, worked on dry-point — painted aqua-tint boy 2° coat — set out some lettuce plants. Tea at 6.30 \+ again setting out lettuce plants until 8 o'clk. — Welf short again \+ as usual given to newspaper.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 1, 1896

**Weather:** Very warm day  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- my duties kept me about until 10 o'clk. Hibbard \+ Rouban came to see if they could get a job to work — I worked on my Rhododendron dry-point until about 11.30 — then down-town — on my return stopped at Sapres \+ chatted for half an hour with Mr. \+ Mrs. Kilburn — there to Ager Lathrop's \+ sat for a few minutes with Flora. Dinner at usual time — again worked on dry-point — before tea time, \+ after did some transplanting of lettuce — Welf, Ralph having had a long sleep during the afternoon (he complained of severe headache but on waking said he was all right) sat up late. Many then gives went out — so did the girls in the kitchen. Jim went to bed \+ I sat with Ralph reading to him — After, many other girls set — bringing Wm. Parke with them. He made a short visit.
