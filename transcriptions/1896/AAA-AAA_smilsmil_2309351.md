## Left Page

### Mon. May 25, 1896

K.T. \+ B.T. went to New Haven.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cool \+ pleasant. Got to studio later than usual having gone to Millers (carpenter) in 40th St. He took me in to see the small collection of insects etc. being prepared for [illegible]. I got to work on a charcoal sketch from photo — of Dr. Gerster's wife — of which he has commissioned me to make an etching. Home to lunch. Katie \+ Bessie started on 3 p.m. train for New Haven to visit Mollie Adams. Lamb, the builder, came to studio at 2.30 \+ we spent nearly an hour trying to contrive some arrangement of grades \+ drainage to meet all the conditions \+ requirements in Sniffen Court. At last he left promising to return Wed. p.m. with a paver. I finished Gerster sketch — \+ did something in the way of arranging old files of catalogues. Home after 6. Dinner at 6.30. Suely Charl came in with a lot of his Mexican photos. I stayed to look at them \+ sent my little "painting" "The Giant of the Valley — Indian Head \+ Devil's Pulpit — Lower Ausable Lake" by messenger boy to Suney Cook. Charl amused us with acts of his Mexican journey until 11 o'clk

## Right Page

### Tues. May 26, 1896

Kate ret. to Po'k.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Muggy \+ close. To studio at usual time \+ commenced a crayon sketch of Mrs. Gerster's head upon a light tinted paper. Worked until after 11s, then to 2d Nat. Bank. Then to Kirby's \+ expressed my great dissatisfaction at the manner in which I had been treated in the matter of miniature of Mr. Comstock for Priscilla Poors. The order is now cancelled. Then in \+ saw Kirby — had an interesting conversation with him in reference to the possible arrangements the N.Y.A.D. could make with the Am. Art Ass'n for use of galleries for A-yptes in case the A— should sell. So far from that, it would not be wise to build on its new site for 10 years or more. The Columbia University site seemed to make a very favorable impression on him. Home to lunch. To studio \+ worked on crayon study until about 5. Then out \+ was attracted by the remnants of Col. Waring's street cleaners parade — a very remarkable show — a very fine example of organization — order out of chaos — practical, efficient machine out of a political, inefficient machine. Went to Kahler's \+ had Russell shoes changed. Then walked up home to dinner. Every read awhile to the boys. The rest of the every — very stupid.
