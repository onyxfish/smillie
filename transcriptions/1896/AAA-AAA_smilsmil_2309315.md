## Left Page

### Sat. March 14, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright day but quite cold. When I got to the studio I found men from Central Ironworks Ready to put up iron brackets for signs in front of building. I got them started + then left my studio in their possession + went to N.A. The Hanging Com desiring to pass the Spring Hours over to the Council - but they were not ready, + so, the time was lost to me. It was after 12 when I got back to studio + after 1 when I got home to lunch. The iron workers got away before 3 — I spent two or three hours clearing up things — Home at about 5 + had an hours Rest — then dressed \+ at 7 went to Walters where I had been invited to dine + meet Miss Jennison, Mr + Mrs. Frank Brownell, + Mr. + Mrs. 3[illegible] M. Cook. We had a pleasant, quiet time until 10.30 — Then Walter + Brownell went to The Century but I preferred to go home. Chase sent us word that he had been called to Boston by the sudden + serious illness of Mr. de Beschofts.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 15, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dark day, snow falling continuously after about 11 Am. I went to studio before noon + was there for about an hour. Then home. Dinner at 2. The afternoon was spent by me in planning a form for a new will. About 6 o'clk. I went to studio + fixed furnace fine. Then a short walk in the snow. home. Tea before 7. Eve'g I wrote out with some care The new will + had Mary \+ Katherine Throop sign it as witnesses. This I understand to be legal and binding. On the assurance of Mr. Wm. H. de Lee, of Lee + Lee. 20 Nassau St. I consulted him on the subject, professionally when I saw him a few days ago.
