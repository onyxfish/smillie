## Left Page

### Tues. June 2, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightful day, bright, cool \+ exhilarating \- At 10 o'clk. I went downtown \- to Char's with my Oregon Imp. Co's Re-organization Receipts, but he had gone to Passaic \- left the O. J. doc'. with Zabriskie \- Then to W\. Cullen's Rote. materials \+ spent some time then finally buying two "Birds eye" Cameras with adogen \+ spools \+ films \+ Developer Powders, being birth-day presents for Jim \+ Ralph \- Then to Lee \+ Lea, but Mr. L\- was out of town \- Then to Manhattan Trust \+ got one, rup'. Re. org'. pamphlet. Then home just before lunch \- To Studio at 2 \+ until 4 worked at sorting old cat? \+ pamphlets \+ stowing them away in closet that I have prepared for them \- Home at 4, meeting Miss "Pick" Palaemus who stopped me in the street. Numa \+ Grace J \- at the house. It was 5 o'clk. before Jim, Ralph \+ I got away upon our wheels \- We had a good Kem up to the Park but when near the Met. Mus. \+ Mt. R's wheel broke hopelessly down \- He \+ J. started for home while I made the circuit of the Park \+ met them in Mad. Ave. at 50th S\. R\- left his wheel at Bormann's. Dinner after 9 \- The boys much delighted by their birth-day gifts \- ahead of time \- but 8.30 I went to Walter's \+ after a short consultation with him decided to give contract for paving in Sniffin Court to Ed. Clark \- on my Return home wrote to him to that effect.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 3, 1896

*Katie \+ Bessie Return from New-Haven*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still charming weather \- After bkf. I went to Bormann's \+ gave directions for Repairs to Ralph's bicycle \- Then to Dr. Lewes's in 45th S. \+ had a talk with him concerning Jim's nose \- It is singular to me that while he Reports to me that the mucus membrane in J's nose is a natural condition \+ that there is no mucus gathering in the Throat, yet J\- evidently has trouble in breathing \+ is inconvenienced by collections of some kind in head or Throat \+ is also complaining much of pain in his eyes. After leaving Dr. L's I Ret? home \- Then to the butcher's \+ grocers, \+ then to D. D. Butler's with the chestnut mounting that Miller struck for me for a frame for the "Rocky Mt." pf. for Caption". Then to Na J D\- with Farrington for some time \+ got some old A. catalogues to fill vacancies in my file \- got to studio at 12.30 \- home at 1 o'clk. The Thorpe girls home from New Haven \- very enthusiastic over their visit \- Dinner at one o'clk \- a change in our domestic arrangements with a view to giving us longer afternoons \- at 2. Ret. to Studio \+ worked for some time at arranging my pamphlets \- Dr. \+ Mrs. Gester came after 4 o'clk. \+ to my great delight expressed themselves as entirely satisfied with my crayon sketch \- now I have to etch it \- Home to tea at 6.30 \- Read to the boys \- Char. came \+ took many of the girls to see the wizard Kellar \- I spent some time wrestling with my bank acct. just balanced, \+ it does not agree with my figures \- I had a five mile ride on my bicycle \+ home in at 10.30 \+ to bed.
