## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 20, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible] Ther. 30° last night

Nasty weather again — a few rifts let through some sickly sunshine, but there were many heavy showers that continued well into the night. Got telegram from Chad who is at Hugman's Jerry, storm bound \+ out of humor, I think. I fussed all day on a little aquatint experimental plate \+ then gave up my studio work for the summer. Now I have to begin the disagreeable work of tearing up \+ packing up for our flitting to winter quarters — At night Ralph showed signs of influenza \+ I dosed him with Rhinitas. In the afternoon, late, Prof James came with three of the Teachers' Institution visitors — I remember the name only of Mr. Albro. At 5,30 between showers, I went to P.O. \+ after that had a walk. Every I wrote a letter to Kate \+ read news papers.
