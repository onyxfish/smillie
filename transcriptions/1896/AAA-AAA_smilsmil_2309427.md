## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 24, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wicked sort of day. High, cold westerly wind \- dark clouds \+ some light snow flurries \- I had a hard days work between etching room \+ studio getting things assorted \- putting away such as are to remain here \+ packing such as are to be taken to N.Y. About 1 o'clk. Bissell came with butter \- I had dinner \+ immediately after I went to Bostwick's \- met Bissell Then bought 5 barrels \+ engaged about 20 bush. apples from Bissell's orchard \- It was about 4 when I ret'. With Spence I set out a little oak tree rec'd yesterday from Uncle Willie \- After tea I worked a little while in studio but feeling tired I went to bed about 9 o'clk \-
Our cook, Katie Coleman, went to Binghamton to spend Sunday \- I had a call from Mink who opened the subject of a deal \- He w'd like to trade his house \+ lot, that is the rear of his father's lot, projecting into mine, for the "Logier" house \+ lot. I declined with "thanks".

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 25, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day \- after a cold night. Ther. fell to 25° on piazza \- I gave R. his bath in front of a fine fire \+ then enjoyed a like luxury myself \- much of the day was spent in studio writing letters \- At 1 o'clk. I went to Fair-Gr'. Track \+ rode 3 m. Dinner at 2 \- Afternoon \- pruned the last of the maples by Fairgro. walk. Then worked over Annie's fern \+ wild flower bed under the beeches by the wall by road from barn to Fairg'. gate I put several barrow loads of compost of earth \+ leaf mold over them \- I found a couple of white violet blossoms that I sent to Jim in a letter. Sw'tf. I wrote until near 10 o'clk \- Wm. Warner \+ Chas. Sayer were at the house in the eve'g. They took my letters to P.O.
