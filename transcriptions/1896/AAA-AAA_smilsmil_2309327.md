## Left Page

### Tues. April 7, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A worse day than yesterday as it snowed all day long. Clear starlight at night. Got to Studio at 9.30 \+ until near noon wrote on N.A. Site paper — Then, until 1.30 clearing out big drawers in closet — then I bethought my self of lunch \+ hurried home. Back at 2.30 — \+ set to work writing replies to letters, notices, that should have been answered before — Then [illegible] the Tribune came in \+ insisted upon interviewing me on N.A. matters. I replied to his questioning, suppressing as much as I could. After that I resumed my writing \+ kept at it until after 6 — Home to dinner at 6.30. Read to Ralph until 8.30 — Later, Charl came in — bad news from deBischofs — fever very high \+ Dr. anxious — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 8, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ breezy — a very pleasant. After getting to Studio I was in doubt as to what work to go at — my head was very heavy \+ I did not feel like tackling anything important, but after a time I made up my mind to begin the repainting of the sky upon the Oct. brook \+ duck picture that I painted over a fortnight, or less, ago. I painted until 1 — then home to lunch — after that I went to 2d. Nat. Bank — then to Kurtz's about miniature of Mr. Comstock — then to Butlers \+ ordered a frame for Capri painting — then to Studio on looking at sky painted in the ntg I was so little pleased with it that I scraped off part \+ repainted the whole of it. Home at 5.15 \+ had a rest before dinner. Self read to R. as usual — Charl came in at 8.30 — the news from de Bischofps continues to be discouraging — At 9 I went to Social meeting at N.A. Singing by a glee club. A great crowd — much smoke \+ heat — so left at 10.30
