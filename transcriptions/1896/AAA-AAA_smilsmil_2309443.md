## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 25, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day \- a little rain in the m'g. I am still under the weather \- perhaps a good part of it is apprehension that I may not be in fighting trim for the Academy meeting this ev'g. I did little at the studio beyond scrubbing it &printing pres with emery cloth. There is a good deal of rust on the bright parts of it. Ev'g. National Academy meeting \- Special. from 8.30 to 9 \- a meeting called to act on proposed amendments of the Constitution members to be elected at Stated meetings as well as the annual \- Recess of 15 m \- Then re-convened to consider the matter of Site \- Parry surprised us by presenting a series of Resolutions so moderate in tone that there was no ground for objection \- except upon one point \- He proposed that the council be made a Com. on Site with power to purchase \- To the council he added the Second Site Com \- [illegible] Robbins Harry Watrous & himself \- Other nominations were made \- I nominated J. C. Nicoll \- A. Brest Nicoll, Watrous & Parry were elected \- Then the meeting, a very large one, 52 present, voted to make the council with the three added, a com. with power \- Cox Beard was the only N.A. voting No \-
There was surprisingly little discussion or opposition \- a general feeling of relief & good temper.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 26, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thanksgiving

Weather still dull & uncomfortably warm \- Bkf. at 8.30 \- To studio before 10 \& to work painting on an old picture by Ehninger \- a monk in a church at the foot of cluster of columns, kneeling \- in shadow beyond, another monk playing upon an organ. I got the canvas in a roll from Mr. Ehninger, & had it mounted \- it was in very bad shake \- much of it peeled from the canvas \- I patched it up & now have put a second painting over much of it. Ev'g. Thanksgiving dinner at 6 \- Geo. & his family & Charles with us \- a party of 13. They all seemed to enjoy themselves & their dinner \- Geo. & his family left early \- Then Jim, Ralph & the three girls went to an entertainment at Walter Cook's \- charades \- etc. I went there for them a little before 11 & then learned that I & the older members of my family had been affected. I saw for a moment only, Geo. & Emily, Mr. & Mrs. Frank Brownell, Fred. & Alice, Emma & Mary Ireland \- etc \- etc.
