## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 5, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant day — growing milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — growing milder — To studio about to got out canvas "Normandy [illegible] Yard" — about 5 feet long — that I commenced several years ago \+ never completed the first painting in. Time \+ dirt had put a glaze on it \+ I had almost a morning felt in scrubbing \+ washing it as I have a notion now to recommence work upon it. I commenced a hunt for studies that have a bearing upon it which led to the clearing out of the big drawers in one of my closets \+ the re-arranging of their contents which have not been reduced to order since I got into my new studio. I took a short walk \+ then went home to rest awhile before dinner.

I felt much out of sorts, my stomach troubling me so much that I did not go to monthly meeting at the "Century". I was able to do some work compiling a very brief financial history of the N.A. of D.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 6, 1896

**Weather:** Charming day \+ much milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day \+ much milder — I got up feeling much better than I did last night — worked for awhile on my financial history — at about noon set out with Ralph — we rode up 3d Ave. to 89 St. \+ then walked thro' to the Park. He had his camera with him \+ made some snap shots — we walked thro' the Park to below 70th St — then to Mad. Ave. cars \+ got home just in time for dinner — It was about 4 when I went to Century \+ had a look at the pictures \+ some pleasant bits of chats with good fellows — then home — tea at 6.30 — I found [illegible] Nellie \+ Charl at the house. Evelf. Mary \+ the girls went to church \+ I read to the boys until nearly. I then worked on my compilation until my head ached badly — then I went out on my bicycle \+ rode for nearly an hour then home \+ to bed.
