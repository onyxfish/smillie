## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 30, 1896

**Weather:** mild day \- S.S. wind. cloudy m'f with a little rain about the middle of the day \- clearing in the afternoon \- beautiful sunset.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I had a very busy day \- [illegible] in the early m'f. \- Mr. VanAuken sent up word about 7.30 that she was in bed sick \- Speuse was sent for Mrs. Rhinesbaet who, fortunately could come \& care for us during the day \- Breakfast was much belated \- Extra work was put upon Mary \& she gave up going to Washington to\-morrow \- but Chas. \& Bessie went to N.Y. by the noon train \- I worked with Stone \& [illegible] until noon \& got the six apple trees set out \- In the afternoon 3 doz. Bill's Raspberries were set out \- Before 6 I went down town \- called on Dr. Garduer for his bill \- tea at 6. Every Chas. Sayre came \& took H. I. to a political meeting \- most of the [illegible] m. she had been out riding with [illegible] Muelford.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 31, 1896

**Weather:** Superb day \- bright \& fresh  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. VanAuken was able to be at the house again \& take charge of our meals \- Stone \& Speuse worked at setting out remaining Raspberry \& Currant plants \- that work was completed by about 3 o'clk. \- I went down town in the m'f \& paid five Rs. \- Saw E.S. Wright \& told him what I w'd let him have carriage for in exchange for train work \& Manure \#65 \- Then I got to work with Stone \& at it until 5 o'clk \- then dinner \- After 3 o'clk. I packed canned tomatoes in barrels \- at 5, being very tired, I lay down until 6 \- then tea \- Ev'g \- wrote Receipts from garden book for lotions, plasters \& sprays for fruit trees \- to bed early.
