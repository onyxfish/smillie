## Left Page

### Mon. May 11, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Oppressively warm — To Studio about 10
\+ got into a snarl trying to make some
passages in my Treasr's Report so clear
that any k.a. could understand them
Mr. Redwood called about a possible job
that I had transferred to de Thulstrup.
— also a Real Estate agent — also a trib-
reporter — I got a little work done
Home to lunch, loaded with ice cream
In the afternoon, giving way to my in-
clination to nap I had a good two hours
sleep \+ consequently feel something like
an elderly fighting cock at night.
I went to Council meeting early at the
request of Ja.s Hart, chairman of House Com-
to pass upon bids from coal-dealers, but he
passed me altho' I was in the Council Room
— he Council meeting we had a tilt
over the fine Mr. business \+ he backed out.
I had a great deal of trouble, as expected,
in explaining the printed statement \+ Wood
in particular, was very dense. The trouble
was mainly over my arrangement of the
School, Spttt \+ Academy Fund. Acct.
It was 11.30 before I left — To bed
soon after midnight.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 12, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little air stirring but a very debilitatingly
warm day. To Studio at 10 \+ after a
little more work — again trying to make
my plan of arrangement (which I insist is
right) plain to the dull comprehension, I
went down to the A—. Farrington was not
in but I sat \+ wrote \+ waited \+ at 12.30 he
came — I was with him until near 1. Then
home to lunch — At 2 I went to H.R. Butler's
— was not in but Mr. B. was \+ she explained
to me B's new 57th St. scheme which was, in a
very vague way, brought before the meeting Sat. eve
by Vedder — From there I went again to the A. \+
spent an hour with [illegible] — Then home at 4.30
\+ tried to get a little rest but was no more
than settled when Butler came \+ he spent
about an hour in telling me more of the 57th St.
plan which involves a partial promise of gifts
to the amt. of $75,000 if the N.A. will pay $[illegible]
for an extended plot immediately west of the Fine
Arts Socy in 57th St. It is a secret carefully kept
that Cornelius Vanderbilt is the one most deeply
interested in it. Dinner at 6.30 Mary and
Hattie arr.d fine P.o's. at 7 P.m. At 8.30
went down to 10th St. Studio Bldg as desired
by J. Brown to attend a Caucus in Beard's
Studio — but for some reason it had been
called off \+ no notice had got to me — Then
home \+ rode my bicycle 6 miles \+ to bed
after 11.
