## Left Page

### Tues. July 28, 1896

**Weather:** Cloudy m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'f., clearing muggy & hot. A brisk thunder shower about 5 P. m. Fine sunset. Jim set — from Parke's from after 9 o'clk. Norman was with him \+ G.G.P. himself \+ Bessie \+ a young man. Considerable time was lost. It was late before I got to work in studio on some crayon studies of Poppies. Dinner at 1 — after that went down to lower end of lot \+ spent some time directing the Italians at work in the ditches — thus to garden where Stone \+ Spence were straightening the corn that had been blown over by the wind yesterday. I did very little studio work. After tea I went to P.O. Ev'g, while the young folks played games I read newspaper \+ August "Harper".

Four Italians worked 9 h. each in the ditches.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 29, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot, muggy, disagreeable day unless the time was passed in a hammock. The old story was repeated, many little matters running away with all the time \+ no studio work done. I tried to write some of the letters that I have been owing for a long time, but I made little headway. I did a very little at sketching Poppies. Ev'g, with Mollie \+ Jean, \+ Katherine \+ Bessie \+ Jim \+ Ralph I went to Village to an entertainment given for benefit Village mp. Soc'y. A little farce "Mr. Bob" was given — some of the parts were very well taken, Bessie's duel perhaps the best. Frank Chandler easily the worst. The audience was very large, about 150, well pleased. It was near 11 when we got home — between showers — as it rained heavily soon after we got in.

Four Italians worked half a day in the ditches.
