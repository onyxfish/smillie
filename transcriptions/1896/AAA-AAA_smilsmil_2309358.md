## Left Page

### Mon. June 8, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim went to New-Haven.

A smart shower in the m'f about 7 o'clk. - for a time after it the weather was very threatening, then it cleared with a chilly wind from the east. Thunder storm light again at 5. P.m. I went to studio before bkfs. & found a digger at work in the court going down to sewer to make connection from my set basin — Then home to bkfs — Then Jims preparations for his Journey — at 9.40 went to the H. Cen. Station with him, he taking his wheel, \+ started him for New Haven for a few days visit at his Aunt Nellie's — Then to studio. Then to bank \+ got my bank book \+ acct straightened out — again to studio \+ home to dinner at one — To studio at 2.30 — accomplished but little in the way of packing — wrote a couple of notes — Home at 5 — a telegram from Charl saying that he could not ride with me — so, at 5.30 Ralph \+ I started on our wheels \+ made a circuit of Central Park — a trifle more than eight miles. \+ back at 6.30 — It was 7.15 before I was ready for my tea — feeling very well after my ride \+ grub — Remarkable little weariness. Charl came in at 8 while it was raining — a few minutes before, R.D. had run around to the studio to see that the digger \+ plumber had left the hole properly protected. I wrote a little letter to Jim \+ took it to the H. C. Station \+ posted it.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 9, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Muggy m'f \+ a very light patter of rain before q — during the m'fs at times it was thunderous \+ threatening — the afternoon clear \+ very warm, with pleasant breeze. In the ev'f, thunder storms all about us with but little rain. The brick layer commenced work this m'f, \+ by noon had finished the brick set basin, 4' deep, 2'×1'. I spent some time in bossing the job. The hole was refilled with earth in the afternoon. I finished my studio packing at 4 o'clk \+ went home. Wrote to Dr. Gester \+ to Maynard. Then to Boornauin's \+ got two Messinger bicycle saddles to try — after tea tried to put them on my wheel but my saddle post is too large. I went out \+ had a very little ride with Ralph. Charl came in at 8.30 — after getting R- to bed I went to 152 E. 37th St. \+ saw Mrs. Bishop, arranging with her to have her son Merrill go with us to Montrose on Sat. to visit with Julie for three weeks. Then to studio \+ got some things \+ brought them home with me.
