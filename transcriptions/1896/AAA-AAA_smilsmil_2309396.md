## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 23, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain was hoped for, but, in that respect, it has been a day of disappointment. The mf was misty \+ a very little rain fell \- the clouds grew thin out times the sun shone out. In the afternoon the clouds thickened again \+ a slight shower fell between 6 \+ 7 o'clk. I spent part of the mf transplanting lettuce. Then, for more than an hour I was in the dark room developing a lot of jimm exposures, only a few of which repaid for the trouble. Dinner at 2 \- then some loafing \- \+ more transplanting of lettuce. Tea before 7 \- some reading.
Heavy shower during the night.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 24, 1896

Emily Cook arr. from U.f.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day \- Jim, who has a bed in my room temporarily, waked me at 4 o'clk. crying because of pain in his head \- Soon after he had violent spasms of the stomach but ejected nothing but mucus. I sent for Dr. Gardiner but he did not come until 11 \- Jim remained in bed until near tea time \+ slept a good deal. I worked on Gilpin colt head \- Fixed a multiple graver so that I used it as a multiple etching needle \+ worked over all of the background \+ other parts. Made up a lot of new Dutch mordant. Afternoon set out a lot of lettuce plants \- Tea at 6.30 after that, Carleton, the girls, Ralph \+ I went to R.R. station \+ met Emily Cook on arrival of train \- we walked to the house. Mary provided some tea for Emily \- a short visit, general chatting.
Rec'd. Lee \+ Lee's bill for prof. services in settling estate of Anna C. Smillie $400.75 This obliges me to give up the trip to the Adirondacks that I had hoped to take next week with the boys \+ sister Kate. Rec'd. notice from 2d. Nat. Bk. that rate of [illegible] on my loans there had been raised to 6½.
