## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 22, 1896

**Weather:** Disagreeable day \- cloudy, gusty  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ with some showers, growing colder towards night. I got to work early on my etching of Mad. Sq. Qt. Tower \+ finished the drawing in upon the ground by 5 o'clk. Then I took my hedge shears \+ trimmed the Berkeny hedge at the end of the lane. After that I took a brisk walk out to the top of hill, Jones' lake \+ back in time for tea \- after that I gave some time to newspaper, then a walk on to piazza, then, for a little while, puzzling over N.A. Site com\- report \+ a letter to Prest. Low of the Columbia University.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 23, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. fell to 31° last night \- Brilliant cold day \- I had a very uncomfortable spot just under the belt \+ so, was unfit to work, but I was in the studio nearly all day. I revised much of Mad. Sq. Tower, working over various parts of it \+ doing some "stopping out". I also exposed in printing frames a lot of negatives \+ got pieces that I put away hoping to tone them to-morrow \- I did a little carpenter work just before dinner \- and after dinner I wrote my letter to Pres. Setteldon. After 4 o'clk I went out and made my circuit of investigation over the place. With Spence I covered the young lettuce plants to protect them ags't frost to-night. After tea I retired to my corner in studio \+ tried to write but had no head for it \- then read newspaper \+ walked piazza (brilliant moonlight) until early bed-time.
