### Wed. Jan. 1, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, breezy day. Ralph showed signs of indisposition in the mfg., developing a temp. of 100°. later in the day. Dr. Cale was sent for in \+ prescribed for him. I was detained in getting to the studio \- most of the day was devoted to getting up \+ sending out a note of invitation to N.A.s to come to my studio on the evenings of Jany 2°. 3°. 4°." to discuss the Reports of the Site Com. of the N.A. Upon a duplicating machine of Charl's that happened to be at the house, I made 45 copies of the note \+ mailed 35 of them before 6 o'clk. At 5 I went to 331 E. 17th S\- \+ then saw Kathemie Noland who returns to Boston to-morrow. Home to dinner at 6.30. Ge? came in for a little while. \- Gharol, later. Raeton still has temp°. above normal. Quite late before I got to bed.
