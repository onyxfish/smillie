## Left Page

### Sat. July 18, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day \- milder than yesterday. After my regular duties I went down to the swamps where Jo. Walker, the Italian \+ two of his countrymen were working at me ditches \- Then I did some work in Mad Sq. Tower" \- Norman \+ Greer Park came on horseback \+ asked that Jim might go with them to Me[illegible] farm (four miles south) \+ stay over Sunday \- I gave my consent \+ packed some clothes for him \- \+ he started off on his wheel with them \- Then I went out to top of hill on Binghamston Road overlooking Jones' Lake, \+ made slight sketch \- Then home to dinner \- After that again went down to see how the Italians were getting on \- Then again to place where I went this m[illegible] \+ took a more careful sketch with pencil. Home at 6 \- tea at 6.30 \- We[illegible]. Roland Mulford \+ two young men called \- I went down town to P. O. \- The usual quiet even[illegible].

## Right Page

### Sun. July 19, 1896

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- altho' partialley overcast \+ with promise of change \- Jean A \- went to church with Mary \+ the girls, leaving Mollie A \- Ralph \+ me at home \- My girls in the kitchen also went to church \- After my duties I helped M \- \+ R \- shell peas, string beans \+ do other kitchen work \- Then we wandered over the place until nearly 10 clk. Dinner at 2 \- I used up most of what remained of the afternoon in taking a long nap \- Then transplanted lettuce until 8 o'clk \- stopping awhile to take tea \- Eve[illegible] wrote short notes to Kate \+ Charl and took them to P. O.
