## Left Page

### Wed. May 27, 1896

**Weather:** Very pleasant, but warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant, but warm. To studio about 9.30 \+ from the debris in the cellar raked out the crate for Fanny's bicycle. Found it so much broken that I had to go to carpenters to get a man to repair it. I took F's wheel from the house to studio. Carpenter could not get a pedal or crank off — nor could I. The m'f had been wasted in this vain endeavor. Home to lunch after having gone to Bowmanns, the bicycle man to get him to take off the pedals. To studio at 2. Mr. Dams \+ the paver were there. They spent an hour discussing again various plans but nothing was presented that seemed to me better than the plan that I have worked out. The flagger is to send me an estimate to-night. Bowman came \+ spent the best part of an hour trying to take off a crank or a pedal but he could not do it. So, I finished the crating as well as I could leaving the pedals on \+ projecting beyond the crate \+ before 4 o'clk was able to sit down for a few minutes of work. I drew on the crayon head of Mrs. G. Gerster that I started on yesterday. Maynard came in for a few minutes. Just Ret. from Washington \+ to go back to-morrow or Friday. At 5 o'clk I went home \+ got the boys \+ took them to Millers house in 40th S. where an Agassiz chapter has an interesting collection of bugs, bees, butterfleas, beetles etc. — Home soon after 6 — dinner. Every read for a while to the boys.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 28, 1896

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'f. The afternoon clouded \+ very dark with two or three exceedingly heavy showers \+ thunder \+ lightning. My m'f was much broken, having several callers. Mr. Jot. H. Morse came \+ after carefully looking at the several paintings that I have she selected the late afternoon view on The Pond at Montrose. This, with the frame, The Morses take as payment for Jim's schooling up to his time. Maynard went to Washington. I worked for awhile on crayon portrait of Mrs. Gerster. Home to lunch at 1 — after that it was so dark \+ the rain was so heavy that I did not go to studio. A part of the time I spent, looking over a couple of pkg. of letters that I found in a box together with brief diaries of Annie's of her camping trips \+ trips abroad. The letters were congratulations from friends on the births of Jim \+ Ralph. Vaulkeel came in for a little while to see if he could get a photo. of the Montrose studio for a possible summer article. I packed two rugs to send to Donchian at Hartford to be cleaned \+ mended. Then to Adams \+ Co. in 42nd S. Then, as the rain had ceased, I went to studio \+ was there until after 6. Then home — dinner \+ a little reading to the boys. Wrote to Mollie Adams saying that I \+ the boys wd. start for New-Haven to-morrow to stay over Sunday. With all that I have to do I don't think that I am quite justified in so doing.

Meyer, the blue-stone man sent in his estimate of cost of fixing my part of Sniffen Court — $75. Whew!
