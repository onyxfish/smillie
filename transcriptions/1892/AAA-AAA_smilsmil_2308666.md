## Left Page

### September Saturday 10 1892

*London. Holborn Viaduct Hotel, Room 103.*

A dull day — quite wet at times between
9 & 11 o'clk — at noon it was sunny & warm
& at night it was again wet. I wrote
awhile upon a letter to Charl, commenced
last night — then some time was given to
seeing Mr & Mrs Grouning (of Chicago) & a
party of for London — after that again I
wrote until it cleared & then I went out
& walked in Ventnor Park on the beautiful
cliffs until after 12 — It was very delicious
in the soft, sunny, hazy atmosphere and
I said good bye to the Hawthornes, red
with berries — to the hill slopes, velvety
green & to the wide spreading violet blue
sea with real regret. Then I went to
the beach for A. & the boys — a strangely
incongruous mixture of people always of
amusement — a crowd about the "children's
morning service" where 50 or more young ones
sat on the sand & made responses & sang
hymns under the guidance of two shepherds
— another crowd, within easy hearing dis-
tance, laughing at the singing & clownish
antics of "Slofeers Niggers." We went to the
hotel & at 1 o'clk had our dinner — At a
little before 2 we went to R.R. Station & took
2:9 Train for London, going thru' Portsmouth
Chichester, Arundel, Epsom etc — The
glimpses of these places being more
or less interesting — Of course, our train
fell behind time & when at last we
reached Victoria Station we were 45
m. late — 7:10 instead of 6:25 — Then a
struggle for my luggage further delayed
me — then a long drive to the hotel
where we arrived a little before 8
& got the same room that we had when
we were here before — we had tea as
soon as possible — the boys were put
right to bed — I sat awhile in the
Reading Room subbing opinions of the
cholera scare on the continent, here
and in New York — To bed at 9 30.

## Right Page

### September Sunday 11 1892

A dull day but dry — Bkfst. at 9 & at 10 30
we went to mf service at S. Pauls — we
sat through the musical portion lasting
until nearly 12 — & then left because we
could not understand a word spoken
by the preacher — The music was delightful
— we admired especially one boy voice & a
nice soprano — pure, strong & sweet — used
with much sensitiveness — Before lymia
& Marjory came to the hotel on Annie's
invitation — we went to the Blue-coat boys
school nearby to see them assemble in the
yard & then march in to dinner. We followed
them from a gallery were allowed to see
the 600 take their dinner — after that we
fell to the hotel & took our dinner. E.T.M.
dining with us — later they went with A.
to Hampstead Heath to call upon Mr. Caro.
Satchell — while remained with Ralph.
Read, wrote awhile, napped awhile and
then went out with R. & had a walk —
A.&J. Returned at 7 & then we had our
evening meal — S.&E.F. I read awhile
again, wrote upon letter to Charl —
To bed after 10.
