## Left Page

### June, Sunday 26, 1892

The Ship's band discoursed the usual
solemn chorals of the Sabbath while
I was in my bath — The sun shone
in the early m't but was soon
clouded — Stiff breeze from the east —
many of the weak-stomached passengers
who had ventured out were driven in
again — Service in the cabin but I
did not go in — wrote a note to Chas.
Collins & worked awhile on Etching" —
Gave the boys their lunch on deck as
they felt the movement of the vessel.
Spent three hours of the afternoon with
Ralph sleeping soundly in my arms on
deck — on waking at last he seemed
much refreshed by nap quite himself
Gave him his dinner in our stateroom
after which he was entirely restored.
Twice wrote closely full a large
Postal card for Geo. To bed before 11.
Within early event — a fog settled down
giving poor promise for the morrow
but by 10 o'clk — we had run out
into beautiful starlight.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 27, 1892

A day of days — brilliant & glorious to the end. Up
at 6 — no land visible — bath before — then on deck —
The shore of Spain faintly seen thru' the mist — lovely
color — after bkft — coasting along — Shore scenery seen
very interesting — especially old town of Tarifa. The
interest & excitement grew until between 10 & [illegible]
we sighted the Rock of Gibraltar opposite to the
grand double headed height on the African
side known as Ceuta. Anchor was dropped at 11.30
at 11.45 a steam tug with four or five large boats in tow
came along side & the Capt. told us that we might go
ashore but must return at 1.30 so 50 or 60 of us hurried
off & got to the quay — a few minutes after 12. Mr. & Mrs.
[illegible] — Emma, Annia, Jim & I hired one of the
little vehicles & we tore around in maddest manner
through portcullised gates — over moats & draw bridges
out over white macadamized dusty roads to where
we got a grand view of the inland face of the
Rock — through Victoria garden then back & through
some of the crooked, narrow streets where we saw
the oddest medley of people of all sorts of nations and
tongues — Turks, Moors, Italians, Spaniards & English-
men — Some noble type specimens — comical little
donkeys — no end of pictures for my camera — back
to quay at 1.12 — bought strawberries & figs — passengers
all huddling back — tug took us to steamer at 1.30
on board in 15 or 20 m. I took a few mouthfuls of
lunch — again on deck — under way at 2.30 — the day
a perfect dream — a marvel of tender color — we
watched the unfolding of the sea side of Gibraltar
with coasts — at 3.30 so tired that I took a nap
until after 4 — dinner at 5.30 which was much
illuminated by the sunset — lovely view of the
Spanish Sierra's with snow — new moon. Twilight
and glassy, reflecting water — another dance
on the after deck — the moon rippling its line
of light down into the sea — a little later was
low in the east & dropped its line of light into
the sea — it was delightful even to be rescued
passed. It was after 11 when I went to bed.
