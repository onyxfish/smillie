## Left Page

### March Wednesday 16 1892

Snow in the m'f. Less than 2° f
to clearing before noon — bright p.m
a very little trace of the snow left.
Got to studio at 10.30. Burnished m
jade for an hour or more — then to
D. B. Butler's & completed order for from
Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30
— burnished for another hour — then
to 51 W. 10° S. — & saw J. C. Nicoll & with
him went over accts. of N.Y. Etching Club
Then to Wunderlich's & spent some
time looking at old mezzatint [illegible].
Home at 6 — dinner — Edward Tom
Cook were sent to dine with us as
there is a small dinner party at 154
E. 37. — Annie went there at 8. 45 —
Annual meeting of Water Color Soc.
I should have gone as there is prom-
ise of something of a row — I don't know
by whom — or for what — but I thought
it more prudent for me to stay at
home — and so I did & read & wrote
a letter to W. G. Parke — I got to bed
about 10 — Annie came in an hour
later.

Jade 2 h.

## Right Page

### March Thursday 17 1892

Dull, gray day with rain, chilly
am — was awakened before 6 AM.
by stomach pain which increased
until after 7 when it was very se-
vere — Then drank some hot Carlsbad
& applied a hot bag to stomach.
Immediate relief followed & drew
I fell asleep. Got up at 10 & had
[illegible]. Before 12 went to Lincoln
Safe Deposit Co. & saw W. Van Wormer
about getting a room to store studio
contents. Then walked up to Central
Park — on my way back stopped & saw
Yale for a few moments. — He thinks
pain of this m'f may have been the
result of a plug of mucous moving in a
gale duct. Home to lunch at 2. To
studio about 3. — at 4 went to 51 W.
10° S. & saw J. C. Nicoll & completed
the auditing of the Etching club accts.
On my way back stopped at Wunderlich's
& looked at some old mezzatint [illegible]. Home
before 6. dinner — Charl spent most
of the evn'f with us. To bed at 11.

Jade 1 h.
