## Left Page

### August Friday 19 1892

*Eisenach Elizabethen Ruhe. Room 18. Hotel & Pension.*

Another exceedingly hot day. The Ther. getting up very near to 90°. After 6 o'clk we had a talk with Prop° of Golden Lion the told us of some Pensions a little further up the valley — The boys were provided with boats & left to play by the side of a small, shallow pond. We went to the E.-Ruhe, a house pleasantly situated upon a small terrace & among trees. The prop° Told us he had but one room which was not all that we wished so we walked on to two or three other places with less satisfactory results — some rec° to the E.-Ruhe & engaged the room for one week at 17 m. per day for our family — everything included. Then to the Lion & told the prop°. We stayed there for dinner & had a very good one for 2½ m., 7 m. The party. Then we gathered about & sent baggage to our new quarters. At 5 the boys & I went out to the Schützen Fest — a few minutes further up the valley — where the boys rode on the merry go round & shot at targets. Back at 6. It was after 7 when we had our supper on the Terrace — now quite cool — with the interesting old Wartburg rising before us, crowning a beautiful hill, forest-covered — our supper was very long in coming — German waiters are intolerable! Poles — and stupid. It was not 9 o'clk. when A. & I went to bed, closely followed by the children.

## Right Page

### August Saturday 20 1892

A little cooler, but still a cloudless, hot day. After 6½p. spent some time in settling ourselves in our new quarters — then, leaving the boys to sail their boats in the pond, Annie & I walked into & generally through town seeing such sights as we could find — & back to our new quarters before dinner time at 1. It was 3·5 when A. — the boys & I started on our walk up the hill to the Wartburg — all of the way through woods — we found it exceedingly pleasant. In some places there were noble trees — got to the castle in about an hour & then sat on the Sun terrace for half an hour & had beer & lemonade. Then, with a party of 30 or 40 were taken through the castle — a maddening experience — the Renovation has destroyed the interest of the old Minnesingers singing place from me — frescos & modern work striving to replace the antique everywhere. The Luther room was much more interesting to me for this, I was told, & I saw the walls, the roof & the beams as he had looked upon them — etc. etc. — After going through the rooms we climbed a tower & had a delightful view over the surrounding country. Back, by the way we came — & had our evening meal on the terrace at about 7. While Annie was with the boys after putting them to bed I walked up to the Schützen Fest & amused myself going into different shows — found one — a sort of a monster variety show & beer garden, so novel that I returned & got Annie & together we spent an hour. The show consisted of Comic operetta scenes — duets — gymnastic feats — [illegible] & such like. It was a very characteristic and amusing show — we got back at 10.30 & to bed before 11.
