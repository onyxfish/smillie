## Left Page

### March Friday 4 1892

A bright & pleasant day but I was obliged to stay in-doors
in the evtg I felt so discouraged that I sent for Yae. He came in
the afternoon but was not in good humor, so I got but little out of
him. In the evtg Annie went to the studio & saw Gel. & Rose & Co.
& arranged to have my little painting "An old dam" sent to the Century
club for monthly meeting. Annie was away all day — Unitarian
league & such like work — did not get in until after 6.30 — evtg
I worked for awhile in Garden seed lists for Montrose. Then I
read Rudy and Kipling for a while.

## Right Page

### March Saturday 5 1892

Overcast most of the day. At times a little weak sunshine. Got up with a
headache that annoyed me more or less all day. My chest so sore that
I did not dare go out — so, for another day I was a prisoner. Annie gave
me something to think of by proposing that we go abroad this spring to stay
for a year or so. I did little else during the day than lounge about
in the evtg. I read a little and spent some time looking over last
year's diary & making notes of the [illegible]. Some time spent working on W.
Bishop's Jade plate. To bed before 11.
