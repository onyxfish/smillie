## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 27, 1892

Another very pleasant day — wind in
the afternoon getting a touch of the easterly
nip in it — A late [illegible] — Emma Ireland
called to say that at last she & Margery
Spring have decided to join us in our
European trip. Ralph & I went to the
Manhattan Storehouse, then to the Lincoln
to make enquiries concerning storage —
then to grocers & then to studio — I
did some cleaning up & then at a
lot of p[icture]s, trimming edges & getting
them ready to pack or to give away —
It was about 1.30 when we got home
to lunch — It was nearly 3 before
Ralph & I again got to the studio.
I worked at my p[icture]s until near 5 —
then we rode up to 72d St. & walked
thro' the park to 80th St — then home by
5th Ave bus — In at 6 — Dinner before 7.
Eveʼg wrote a note to Mr. Rives —
Treas. of Century, informing him that
I expected to be out of the country
for a year or more — Then I worked
on curtain pole for lower hall until
after 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 28, 1892

not altogether pleasant — somewhat
hazy at times — an east wind blew raw
& chilly all day. It was 10.30 when
I got to the studio. Fussed about, not
knowing exactly what to do — I have
not my strength & my unhealed
wound is always more or less of an
annoyance. I looked carefully
over Jade & Key p[icture]s — signed them
& made some selections — wrote notes
to W. G. Parks & Jas. Burt, the pictures
dealer. Home at 1 — lunch — at 2
went to Bank & attended to Annie's
business — to studio at 3 — Dr. Gester
came in. At 5 went to Butler's with
the Parchment p[icture] "A voice from the
Cliff" that I stretched for framing —
then home. Dinner. Eve'g
wrote a note to Charl concerning
Montrose fine Ms. & mailed it — tried
to write other notes but could not —
To bed at 10.
