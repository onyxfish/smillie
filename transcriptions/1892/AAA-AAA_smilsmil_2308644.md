## Left Page

### July, Thursday 28, 1892

*Hotel des Alpes, Territet — Rooms 149–150.*

Beautiful day, but very warm — Last night there was rain & distant thunder storms — Up at 7 — B&B at 8 & then, until 10.30 packing — Rearranging our two large trunks got fees from Lugano — Had Annie went to dentists with Jim to have two more cavities filled & I wandered about with Ralph & bought some small things & looked into shop windows — back before 12 — A. & J. came at 12 & we went down to our lunch at 12.15 — very poor & expensive — Settled all charges $7.50 for getting our trunks from Lugano — nearly $20 for our hotel bill. We were not allowed to depart until Mr. Weber, the proprietor, had been called to bow us out which he did very politely — Cannot say that I think much of his hotel — It is finely situated but is musty with an air of decayed grandeur — The Beau Rivage seems to be a pleasanter house — Off on the steamer at 1.20 — The air was delightful but also was almost too breezy & cool — Beautiful, the lovely views of Mt. Blanc — The banks of the lake much like those of the Hudson River at first — Wonderful effects of light & color — sky, mountains & water — after we reached Lausanne — The air was calm — the water was almost glassy — the clouds did not come down upon the mts — but made exquisite effects of light, form & color — all again reflected in the water — The gulls & the Dornabe [?] latter'd sail boats were bellied points in the picture — For an hour & more I stood at the bow of the boat in a dream — We get to Territet at 5.30 — The landing is small — the hotel is large — the steam boat almost enters the front door — The R.R. runs through the basement — the electric cars run on the Terrace & the mountain climbing R.R. — The Territet–Glion–Naye R.R. starts from aside door — the establishment is immense & to me, unique — It is about 150 ft above the lake & has beautiful Terraces & gardens down to the lake — under these terraces the railway runs — we got to our rooms (very pleasant ones) at once — they gave the boys their supper at 6.30 & had our dinner — after that we sauntered in the gardens & watched the curious shadows decline over the lake — It was after 9 when the electric lights were turned on & that drove me in & I went to bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 29, 1892

Another fine day, but very warm — did not get up until 8 — B&B — at 9 — & after 10 started for Chillon — walked with A. & the boys — a hot walk — Had a fairly good guide & a small party & went through the Castle in a satisfactory manner — out at about 12 — Rode back to Territet [illegible] as in an Electric car — Lunch at 12.30 — after that A. & I wrote — I wrote to Chard — at 3.45 we all started on the Glion–Naye R.R. for the top of the Roches Naye, 6700 feet above sea level — The mid portion of the way from Territet to Glion, in up a very steep grade by cable & counter weight — This is done in about 10 m — It may be 700 or 800 ft — Here are large hotels & beautiful Terraces — a new road begins tunnel — the road appeared but two or three days ago and not yet complete — This is like the Zermatt Road in that there is a central rail or ramp with cogs — the incline is very steep & persistent — the road seems to be built very solidly — great walls, cuttings & tunnels — but I did not for a moment lose a sense of danger — should a cog in the ramp break or any portion of the engine — no mortal power could save us on such a mountain side or slide — The views as we went up were wonderfully fine, expanding every moment — The color of the day was delicious — Lake of Geneva lay below us "a mammoth floor of mother-of-pearl." The western extremity was lost in haze — the mountains all about were superb — sublime is not too great a word — It was 5.37 when we reached the present end of the road — within a very little of the wave-like crest of Roche Naye — We climbed up a steep green slope to the peak — a little below, a very large building has been commenced — When finished it will be a wonderful mountain house — The panorama from the top is vast — to the west the lake stretched, lost in a luminous haze — S.W., & S — the mts rose — great masses of light & shadow in the late afternoon sunshine — S. & S.E., were some noble snow peaks already rosy — East were great masses of thunder clouds covering mostly the highest peaks — It was a series of pictures never to be forgotten — at 6.15 we left the top & got down again to the car — at 6.33 commenced the descent — we ate & ate rolls & raved until we reached the ground at 8.08 — after another wonderful & delightful experience — went at once to our room & had some milk & [illegible] water sent up — It was too nearly when the boys got to bed & then I wrote my diary for the day.
