## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 14, 1892

In lodgings. 13 Gloucester Place —
Hyde Park — W.

A bright, pleasant day — E. & M. called at
the hotel just as we finished bkfst. It was near
11 when I went to Cooke & Kirby's to get the list
of lodgings promised but they had nothing to
show so I returned to the hotel & got some Bay-
water addresses of agents from the Directory — it
was then almost 12 — A. The boys & I got into a
cab & drove at once to Cartwright & Co. — Leinster
Terrace — There we got a list of half a dozen
places in the immediate neighborhood which
we like very much — We drove from one place to
another finding all sorts of quarters & people.
Some of the houses were really very pleasant
& the streets were broad & lightsome with
many Terraces, Gardens & Squares — At last
at No. 13 Gloucester Place we found Rooms &
a woman that we thought would suit &
we closed a bargain at four guineas a week
for the Rooms & forty guineas for our Board.
We have the drawing Room floor & the bed-
Room floor above it — four Rooms — the best
in the house — They are simply but nicely
furnished & are clean looking & lightsome.
Our madam — Mrs. Tantost seems to be a
straight forward business-like woman — We
then drove to Whiteley's — a sort of Macy place
& had lunch — after dismissing our cab. Then
we returned to the hotel by 'bus & packed our
trunks — Paid our bill — got our possessions m-
to a four-wheeler & at about 5 started for our
new home — It was after 6 when we got our
trunks into our Rooms & after 7 when tea
was served in our little 10 × 14 dining
Room — we enjoyed it very much —
after the youngsters were put to bed A. & I
spent a quiet evening in our drawing Room
& got to bed about 10.30 —

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 15, 1892

A bright charming day. I had a little run
in Kensington Gardens before bkfst. and am
delighted with the fine old trees & the green
sward — After bkfst. A. went for Emma to bring
her back to see our Rooms that she may report
to our families on her return home. She & Marion
leave for Oxford this afternoon on their way to Liver-
pool to sail next Weds. for N.Y. E. Returned
with A. & was much pleased with what she
saw — Then I went with her for a few minutes
into the gardens that she might see the
beauty of the place — For some time in the
[illegible] — during A.'s absence — I had been in
the Park with the boys & had taken them to
the Round Pond where they saw other boys
sailing their miniature boats — Soon after
we had a very good dinner — after that
I'm afraid that I had a little nap in my chair
— then by 'bus I rode away into the city to the Bank
from there walked to 39 Great Tower S. to Johnson
& Sons for our Genoa trunks — To them I paid
nearly £3. for the transportation & keeping of them.
They are to be delivered to morrow — Then I rode
to the Holborn Viaduct Hotel (after a look at the
outside of London Tower) and there I had to wait
half an hour before I could get a pair of old shoes
left there yesterday — Then I got into a 'bus —
but only carried me to the Marble Arch —
from there I walked home & was consequently
rather late for tea — A quiet eve'g
with books & papers — made some
very slight pen sketches to give Kate
an idea of the appearance of our
Rooms.
