## Left Page

### May, Saturday 7, 1892

*Went to Po'keepsie.*

Fine day — growing much cooler —
Up before 7 — bkf't before 8 — and
at 9.0 Annie, the boys & I got
off for Po'k. where we arr'd in a
couple of hours — Took hack & drove
at once to Mary's — 25 Garfield St —
& found them in waiting for us —
I went out for a prowl around
before dinner. After dinner I
went with Katie — Bessie — Miss Sickley
(a neighbor) & the two boys — down to a
base ball match between school boys
from Bishes's & a Newburgh School.
We watched it for awhile — then on
to Vassar Hospital — High wind & chilly —
from there Katie, the boys & I went up to
Uncle Willie's — getting there a little
after 5. Annie, Mary, Kate & Bessie joined
us soon after — we had a very pleasant
tea party — on Ralph's acct we left
at about 8 o'clk. After the boys were
got to bed we sat at Mary's & chatted
until about 10 o'clk. Men to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 8, 1892

Another fine day, but very windy and
quite cold except when in the sunshine
& sheltered from the wind. Bkf't about
8 o'clk. After the m'f was well advanced
Annie, Katie, Jim, Ralph & I went out
for a walk — first to Vassar Hospital
then into Livingston's woods where we
spent the rest of the m'f. — a very sad
place to me — it is so entirely ruined
by encroachments of smelting furnace
in working shops, etc — We walked
by "the old farm" — over "the big hill"
& got to Mary's in time for dinner —
after that, a nap — then again I
went out, taking Ralph — on our way
back called at Uncle Willie's —
Men home to tea — Suef't, for an
hour or more, chatted in the moon-
lit parlor — Gas lighted at 9 — we
went to bed at 10.30.
