## Left Page

### August, Monday 1, 1892

*Lauterbrünnen, Steinbock Hotel -26-27-*

As fickle as an April day — Sunshine & showers —
Up at 6 — beautiful mts. Packed up things — at 7.30
went out & had a charming walk. B[k]f at 8 —
& at 8.40 were on the wharf at end of [Thuner?] hof
garden waiting for boat — but it was a long before
we got away — Entrance to the lake very beautiful —
light over the mts very fine — good view of the
Blüw tofas at tho' at times a misty rain fell for
a few moments — Enjoyed the sail very much.
Oberhofhen, the first stop, are meeting place as you
from the boat — about ½ when we entered the
canal now in course of construction between the
lakes — Soon reached the fare — at once entered
end of canal & got on top of little cars, drawn
by engine & in 7 in— Reached Interlaken —
There we rid ourselves of our small luggage
& sauntered into town — The main avenue
with its immense old Walnut & Sycamore
trees was delightful — from under the shade
& over the meadows we looked up to the Jungfrau, who was playing with some light clouds,
at times perfectly clear to the summit — after
going the length of the Höheweg — gazing at big hotels, the visitors & the shops we went to
the P.O. where A. got some letters — soon after ½
we went to the Hotel Metropole & prepared
for lunch — had a very good one & again sauntered
back to the fare from whence we started for
this place at 2.15 — a ride of 55 m. through a
gorge, over a narrow gauge R.R. with central
the rail part of the way — The ride was inter-
esting but not exciting like, for instance, the
Zermatt gorge — Went at once to the Steinbock
Hotel & engaged rooms for the week at 27 f. the day.
The clouds came down & rain fell — so I sat down
& wrote for a me time to [George?] — Then out and
had a short walk — The children had
their meal at 6.30 — Then A. & I had
dinner at table d'hôte at 7 — made pleasant
by some young Englishmen — After that
in Salon until 9 when, as I feel cold
& tired I went to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 2, 1892

*Traubbach Hotel — Rooms 12-13.*

Another fickle day — heavy rains last night and
in the mts — cold — but sunshine at mid day
& more heavy rain in the afternoon. It was very
cold & cheerless when we took our B[k]f — at 8.30
after a time the rain ceased & I went out for
a walk — I had hardly more than started
where I heard a call & looking around I saw
one of Mrs. Atwater's girls running after me —
I found Helen A. — in a store & we about faced
to the Steinbock & surprised Annie — Helen's
letter to A. had miscarried — She is stopping
at the Traubbach Hotel with her family. I
went on with my walk & returning about
noon was hailed as I passed the Traubbach
Hotel by Annie who was there with the A.s —
I went in — we saw the proprietor. He showed
us very pleasant rooms & we determined
to make a change — We then went to the
Steinbock, had our lunch and broke the
unpleasant news of our intentions to the
head waiter who seemed much saddened
& said he did not know what could be done
about it — I told him he had better get
my bill & then I would see what I could do
about it — He brought me a bill for nearly 50
francs — which, with fees that had to be found,
obliged us to pay about $5.00 for the privilege of
changing our minds and our hotel — We went
at once to our new rooms & were much pleased —
We look out into the valley — the Jungfrau looms
above a wall of rock — lesser snow-covered mts.
close in the valley & the Staubbach falls almost
tumbles into our window — we have a corner
room with balcony & room adjoining — I wrote
for awhile — then had a nap — As my head
but uncomfortable I did not go to table d'hôte
but took tea with the boys at 6. — Every wrote
for sometime on a letter to [Geo?] — To bed at 10.
