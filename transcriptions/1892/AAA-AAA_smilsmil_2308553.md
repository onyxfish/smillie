## Left Page

### January, Sunday 24, 1892

A nasty day — sleety snow all the m'f — afternoon overcast, mixed with rain. Did not have help until
9.30 — after that toasted my toes until 11 then bundled up and
went to Studio took p[rin]ts from press
& put them into frames & took
them to N.A.f D. Spent a few
minutes there looking over the
etching exhibition — a dismal look-
ing lot. Home to dinner at 2 —
At 3.30 went to P. Hopkinson Smith's
to see him about an etching he
wishes me to make of his head. Home
at 4.30 — then took a rest. Tea at
6.30 — Eve'f we were invited by
Mrs. Ehninger to dine informally
with her but I was not fit to go out.
I Read awhile & wrote notes
to Mr. Vickery & one to Dielmann
congratulating him upon a
very charming plate — head of a
young woman, that he has recently
done.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 25, 1892

Bright & pleasant day — got to studio
about 9.30 — then to N.A. & then to N.Y.'s
with my Key plates & had three proofs
taken — got to studio again at about
12\. Famer came in to urge me
to exhibit a lot of the Key plates — I
told him that I had given up hope
of being able to finish the plate in time
— he was very urgent, as the show that
are now hanging is likely to be a poor
one — he advises me to withdraw my
5"×7" "Etching". A deserted back-yard —
does not think it worthy of me. Home
to lunch at 1. Fanny K — there — she
went with A — to the Dress Rehearsal of
the Choral Club in the afternoon. I
got to studio at 2.30 & burnished on
5"×7" aqua-tint "An old dam" — etc.
It was after 6 when I got home —
A came in later — dinner at 6.30 —
Eve'f Jim went to Sallie Cook's birth day
party & got home a little before 10.
I spent the eve'f reading Harper
for Feb'y.
