## Left Page

### October, Sunday 2, 1892

A yellow fog day — air very still — at
times, gentle rain — & at noon so dark
that I could hardly see to read — I
walked in the park for about an hour
from 12 to 1 & really enjoyed it — it was
like a still Oct. Twilight after a fall storm
has blown itself out — There were many suggestions
of very poetic pictures — Dinner at 2 —
the rest of the afternoon was wasted —
the fog lifted somewhat about 5 o'clk. &
I took the boys out for a half hours
run — then had tea — Eve'g I read
over the last of the 11 N.Y. Tribunes sent to
me & then finished letter to George.
We had our first fire in our Drawing
Room grate — not so much because
it was cold as because the air was
saturated with moisture.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 3, 1892

A cold day with two or three unpleasant
showers and a little sunshine. A bright
very fine moonlight — Had a run with the
boys in the Park before b'kf't. Jim is so out of
sorts generally — Troubled with nasal catarrh
& swollen tonsils among other things, that I
do not think it best to take more swimming
lessons at present. I got to my work at 10
& stuck closely to it until 1 — then out &
had a few minutes walk before dinner —
Boiled Rabbit — not bad — non particularly
good — To work before 3 & until after 5 —
Then out & had a good walk in the Park.
A lovely Twilight — I have a number of
"motives" now for Water-Color work, if I could
only get at it — Annie commenced her
lessons in Italian to-day — Eve'g wrote
part of a letter to Yale.

Morgan — 5½ h.
