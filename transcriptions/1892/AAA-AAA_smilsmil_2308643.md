## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 26, 1892

*Geneva. Hotel de la Paix. Rooms 153–154.*

Glorious day - up before 7 - had a walk in the
garden & through the telescope observed two parties
making the ascent of Mt. Blanc - not a cloud in the
sky. B'f't after 8. Then packed my traps for departure
& sauntered about town with Annie and chatted
in the garden with Lunna - lunch at 12.30 and
at 1.30 went to the Stage office - J. Keypac & Co -
an opposition to Pains & [illegible] Mediterranean service -
Had a place on front seat with Ralph & Jim - very
opposed but could see everything but what was
behind - Sun was warm but the wind was cool -
an ideal day - The first part of our ride - through
the mts - was exceedingly interesting - the road
remarkably good - The drive along the River
side past the gorges of Diosasy - into the valley
at St. Gervais was delightfully exciting - The
views of Mt. Blanc behind us very grand - As we
passed St. Gervais there were terrible evidences
of the sudden outpouring of the flood by which
about 200 lives were lost a fortnight ago -
A portion of glacier had dammed a stream that
suddenly broke away, destroying a village & some
large hotels - Where we passed it had spread
earth, stones & all sorts of debris over some hundreds
of acres of the level & cultivated valley bottom - The
approach to Sallanches was through a lovely
highly cultivated valley with picturesque Mts
surrounding & exceedingly fine views of Mt. Blanc
& its range - Soon after that we entered a
narrow valley & after a long ride got to Cluses at
5.15 - got into waiting train - a carriage to ourselves
& at 6.20 m. started - wonderful views up a valley
to the now distant snow mts - as the day waned
we saw the sunlight fade with exquisite dying
glow from the snowy summits that afterwards paled
like ghosts. We got to Geneva at 7.20 & drove
rode in bus to the Grand Hotel de la Paix on
the Right bank - on the Quai du Mont Blanc - we
got a fine point some with charming view over the
water to the distant mts. As the young ones
had had their every meal in the cars they were
got to bed as promptly as possible - then Annie
& I had something to eat and got to bed about
10 o'clk - not a very restful night owing to con-
stant noise.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 27, 1892

After b'f't - A. the boys & I went out for a stroll -
first, to the P.O. where we got a batch of letters -
then the P.O. & read them - I got an old one from Mary -
& from Jo & one from S. R. Koehler. Then we went
to the Swiss dentist - a German who speaks very im-
perfect English - Jim has been having trouble
with his teeth - I left A. there & with Ralph
to the Promenades des Bastions & the Jardin Bot-
anique - then up to Treille or Terraces & while
sitting there A. & J. came along by chance & joined
us - an appointment had been made for 3 o'clk -
It's P.M. - then we wandered about through the
steep & narrow streets of the old town - to Calvin's house
& like places until we got to Cook's office where I
cashed some money. While there W. Jas. Gifford came
in & with him we went to his quarters in the same
block with our hotel - & there we saw Mr. G. - from
here to the Paix & had our lunch - wrote a note
to Chas. Collins - A. went with J. to the dentist &
I had a very short nap - At 3.45 Ralph & I went
again through the old town to the Treille where
alone we met A. & J. - He had had two [illegible] fillings
in two of his teeth & has made another appointment
for to-morrow - Then we went looking for Mont Katie's
pension on the Rue dela Plaine - after considerable
walking found it - then took tram car & rode to
near the Jardin Anglais on the left bank and
there rested ourselves - I went into the little
building & saw plans of Mt. Blanc & surrounding
mts - to hotel before 6 - gave the boys their
supper & meal - at 6.30 A. & I took Regular dinner
after which we all wandered out for a stroll
to the water front & to look at the monument of
the late Duke of Brunswick - met Mr. & Mrs. Gifford
who had been to call on us - The boys were got
to bed soon after 8 - Later A. & I did a little
writing - To bed at 11.

Do not forget the twilight while sitting at bedroom
window, looking out to Mt. Blanc - over the water - the re-
flecting lights - the bridges - the music & singing from the
café's chantant - the buffon with guitar who sang under
our balcony.
