## Left Page

### July, Monday 18, 1892

*Chamonix — Hotel Beau Site — Rooms 6 + 7.*

After a quiet night up at 6 to find it raining steadily — very depressing — Ralph got up in good spirits + everything was favorable but the weather we had our café + waited — at 8 it still rained but there were signs of clearing — we arranged with a driver to be on hand at 9 — It was then clearing + at 7.20 we got away — The air was altogether pleasant + us' soon as we began the ascent the road was dry — it was charming under the great walnuts — & the chestnuts — Cattle, the beeches & larch — from bottom to top the road was a series of zig-zags — and always charming views out over the Valley of the Rhone + the great mts. of the Oberland beyond — on up to the dip of the St. Bernard pass that parted from our Road. At this point the mt. turning to the left, Ralph rode in his mothers arms + was dumpish — It was about 10 hours before we reached the top — wild + desolate, with a cold wind + misty rain — the Col de Forclaz — Then commenced the steep descent — we had a fine view + a good view of the noble Glacier de Trient — In about 20 mt — we reached the Hotel des Alpes, Trient where we stopped for about an hour + had lunch — Soon after 2 — having changed wagon — the driver we started on a lovely road winding by the Triante + under great larches until we reached the Hotel Tête Noir — Here the pass became exceedingly wild — the depths beside the road tremendous, while rocks & trees growing were very like those of the Adirondacks. Presently the road changed to an ascent — we crossed the frontier into France + the character of the country changed as we entered the lower valley of Bérard — here the rain was driven into our faces by a cold wind + I became discouraged + impatient — at last we reached the summit — the Col de [illegible] from which we should have had a grand view — but alas — there was nothing but mist + rain — As we entered the valley of Chamonix we were soon up on a sculptured road + were confronted by the immense Argentière glacier — on the blue mt beyond the Glacier des Bossons made its long white strip of ghostly white down to the very bottom of the valley — but nothing could be seen of the mt. tops — we drove on — only anxious to get to our journey's end — as we approached the village familiar forms were recognized up on the road ahead — Emma + Marjory had waited out to meet us — a few hundred words + we drove on — through the village — past the big hotels — to the last house — the Hotel Beau Site — Here we took two pleasant rooms — as it was but a little after 5 we had a wash + dinner until dinner at 6.30 — a pleasant table full, mostly all English — a few Americans — Ralph's diarrhea is more persistent — I went to bed at 8 o'clk — with the children, to get warm.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 19, 1892

was awakened by Annie between 4 + 5 A.M. to see the sun rise on Mont Blanc — It was a grand sight — not a cloud in the sky — the old moon above the mt — Rosy sun light tipping the wonderful snow peaks — a revelation since last night nothing of any mt. could be seen anywhere — It was very exciting to see all of these wonderful peaks about us, of which we have a fine view from our windows. By 7 o'clk. clouds gathered rapidly + by 9 not a mt. top was to be seen — Before noon rain fell + the afternoon was cold, wet + dreary — For a time in the afternoon I nursed a small wood fire in the Salon — managed to get it to burn + was warm. After dinner I sat for a little time in the Salon where there was a fire, then went to bed with the boys about 8. Good bed + a very comfortable place.
