## Left Page

### October, Saturday 8, 1892

A very little sunshine — a very little rain — much cloudiness & a pleasant moonlight night. Was delayed in getting at my work in the mtf so that it was 11 o'clk. before I commenced — then at 12 I went with Jim while he took his swimming lesson. After that I went to Whiteley's and got a mirror so that I can see my original photo. Portraits (Morgan) reversed as it is on the plate. Then home to dinner — To work again at 2.30 — The mirror a great comfort to me — worked until 4.30 — could see no longer. Out for a walk — across Park to So Kensington — walked around & looked at the new Imperial Institute — then went into the museum for a few minutes — Home at 6.15 — Tea — Evening Reading in my book on Indigestion.

morgan. 3 h.

## Right Page

### October, Sunday 9, 1892

An unusually large share of sunshine to-day. Grey, wet mtf — a little sunshine, then, about noon, a very heavy shower, after which it cleared for the rest of the day. Fine moonlight night. After [illegible] I read, napped & wrote part of a letter to Gale. A short run with the boys before dinner — About 3 o'clk with A. & the boys, I started for a walk — went across Hyde Park — about the corner — through Green & St. James Parks to Westminster Abbey — too late to get in — then we walked up Whitehall to Charing Cross where we took 'bus to Marble Arch — A. & Ralph rode home — Jim & I went into the Park where for a few min I listened to some Socialist rout. The crowd was not large & seemed to be mainly people of a better class attracted by curiosity. Home just before 6 — Tea about 7 — Evening finished letter to Gale & then went out & had a short moonlight walk. After I came in Annie read aloud two or three short Swiss sketches by Miss Symonds.
