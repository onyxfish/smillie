## Left Page

### September, Thursday 22, 1892

Another "hazy" day as Mr. Tantost [illegible] synonymously describes this beastly weather.
I did not go out of the house at all, but
gradually improved, my sneezing &
discomfort in the head passing quite
away — I got to work at 9.30 & did not
stop until almost 1 o'clk — again at
3.30 & worked until 5.30 — Then I had
my indom walk & exercise before tea.
Evely Annie read aloud for a while,
wrote a note to Abbey & part of a
letter to Kate.

Mlle. Théland commenced her
labors with the boys to-day — 11 to 1 —
& walked with them in the Park.
They like her very much.

morgan — 5½ h.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 23, 1892

A pleasanter day than yesterday — In
the afternoon the sun actually shone
out for a little while. My influenza
had also passed off very nearly, so that
I went out & walked in the Park. I
worked from 10 to 12 on Morgan head —
then went to Paddington Baths, Queen's
Road, & saw W. C. Whyte, 5 mile & champion
of the Thames, & arranged with him to give
Jim lessons in swimming — back to dinner
at 1.30 — Afternoon, Annie & Ralph went to
Hampstead to Mr. Satchell's — I worked from
3 to 4 — then took Jim to swimming bath.
He had his first lesson, doing very well.
Then we had a very brisk walk in the
Park — back at 6.15. Annie in — Tea
at 6.30 — Evely finished letter to
Kate & posted it.

morgan — 3 h.
