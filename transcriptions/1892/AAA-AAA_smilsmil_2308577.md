## Left Page

### March Saturday 12, 1892

overcast — Nelie set? to New Haven
taking Katie with her who has been
visiting at Fred Ireland's. Before
noon Ralph & I went down to the
studio — I left an order at Rose's
to have my large water-color
"The Passing Herd" sent to the home
also, to send up someone to hang
George's painting — Lunch at 1 —
afternoon I devoted myself to
the hanging of Water-Colors in the
dinning room — Men came from
Rose's & hung Geo's painting in [illegible]
Dinner soon after 6 — Twelfth Phil.
Concert. Kate & I did not feel able
to go out — we sent our tickets to
Geo. & Emily Cook — Ed[illegible] & Mary
called for Annie & brought her
home from the Phil. Concert — I
stayed at home & worked diligently
at rehanging paintings in parlor.
To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March Sunday 13, 1892

Cold & windy with some sunshine
in the middle of the day — a late
[illegible] as usual — Then sometime
with "Tribune" — after 12 Annie, Ralph
& I went to Mr. Jas. H. Morse's — Madison
Ave. near 44th St. — I thanked W. M. for
his offer to give Jim a year's tuition
from one of my paintings and accepted.
Then home in time for dinner at
2 o'clk. Charl. came in — then
Mrs. Wes Jane — I went with Charl
to his rooms — & then to studio —
[illegible] carrying my deer's head and
horns & fixed it up in the hallway.
Tea — very uncomfortable — headache
— misery — Charl came in & stayed
until after 10 — Then to bed.
