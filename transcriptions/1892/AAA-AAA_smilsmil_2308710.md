## Left Page

### December Wednesday 7, 1892

A pleasant day — walked to the museum & continued my study of early German prints — after a time getting started on Dürer — Was surprised to meet Jno. Durand in the Print Room — He tells me that he is here on some business for the Graphic Arts Dept. of the Smithsonian Ins. It was 1.30 when I went to Soho Sq. to Newman's, & there met W. Mill the old gentleman directing, & with him I spent a very pleasant half hour — then to Holborn Restaurant & had dinner — It was 3.30 before I got back to Print Room — left at 4 and walked home — Had a rest before Tea — Evng Jno. Durand came in & chatted until 10 —

## Right Page

### December Thursday 8, 1892

A cloudless day, but not a bright one. After bkft walked to the museum & spent the mng there wrestling with Albrecht Dürer & Lucas van Leyden. At 1 o'clk. I went out, headachy, & walked to Barnard's Inn & got a copy of Vierge [illegible] Cat. with Pennell's preface. Lunch — back to Museum about 3 — there until 4 — Jno. Durand came in for a little while — After 4, went down Regents St. to Picadilly looking for a shop that Pennell had told me about — didn't find it — It was nearly 6 when I got home. Evng, loafing & a little reading —

To-day rec'd a long letter from Mielatz
