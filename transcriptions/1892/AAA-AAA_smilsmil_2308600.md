## Left Page

### April, Friday 29, 1892

Quick rain nearly all day — did not go out. Wickham's
carpenter came & I spent most of the time bossing him
in putting up the mirror and other furniture in the hall.
I wrote a letter to Mr. Halsey C. Ives. Chief of Art Dep't. World's
Columbian Exposition. Chicago in answer to his concerning
committee on Water-Color and Etching. Walter Cook came
in late in the afternoon & for a time shared my labors in
bossing Murphy the carpenter.
At 7 Annie & I went down to Mrs. Jno. Ehningers — W. 10th St —
by invitation, to dinner. Had a good dinner, of course, & a
pleasant eve'g. Home a little before 10 — to bed about
11.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 30, 1892

Brilliant cloudless day — cool &
breezy. Wickham's man Murphy
came & spent most of the day
finishing the putting in of mirror,
rack etc. I got to studio at 10.30
& to work packing & sorting prints
etc — getting them into various draw-
ers. I destroyed many prints — Dr.
Gester came in after 11 & I showed
him how to lay a rebiting ground.
Home at 1 — After lunch Annie & I
went to Private View — Soc'y of Am.
Artists. At 5th Ave. Art Galleries —
I met Chase at the entrance & he re-
marked that I had not sent any work
to their exhibition this year — to which I
answered "Oh yes, I sent & you sent
it back" — at which he was evidently
surprised & said something about limited
room in the galleries — etc — etc. On
looking over the collection I concluded
that there was far too much room — the
upper would be much better if half of it
was sent home — To studio at 4 —
worked until 5.45 — then home —
Dinner & every. Reading until
10 — then to bed.
