## Left Page

### August Wednesday 17 1892

A fearfully hot day — cloudless and
blazing — Up at 7 & wrote for some time
before bkfs — on a letter to Kate — After
bkfs — A the boys & I started out — went
to P.O. & got a letter from Kate — then took
tram & rode into town — went to S: Sebaldus —
after a careful survey went on to Durer's
House & spent some time there — then
to the Burg — horribly hot — made a slight
sketch & then back to hotel by tram car.
Lunch at 1 — after that we retired
to our room & did not go out again —
it was so hot — cooler in than out of
doors — Annie & I wrote until near
6 o'clk. About 7 we had our eve'g
meal in the garden & I tried an ex-
periment with München Beer. To bed
early to get a good start in the m'g.

The Württemberg er Hof is just outside of
the old city walls — a few steps only from the R.R.
station — the Post Office & a tram R.R. that
by correspondence, will take you into several
parts of town — Dining Room large — high ceiling,
garden for bkfs & supper — our bed room was
very large on one flight — well furnished — beds
good — Plumbing — Sanitary & Service, as in
the Bavarian hotels in our experience, very
slow & unsatisfactory — good diners and
Reasonable bill — We have tried
the hotel under unusual conditions
of great crowds rapidly changing & great
heat — under normal conditions it would
probably be much better.

## Right Page

### August Thursday 18 1892

*Eisenach — Hotel Goldenen Löwen —
Rooms 34 — 39 —*

The hottest day yet — the air was baked & lifeless —
stifling to breathe & making one feel sick —
I remember but one or two like experiences
in America — Up at 5.30 — Completed packing —
Bkfs before 7 — everything satisfactory wound up
& we got to R.R. Station at 7.40 — Funky experience
with blundering Gepäck men who, with my
tickets before them, checked my baggage first
to Eisfeld then to Erfurt because they were
quarreling with the man ahead of me — much
swearing in German, all hands taking part.
I enjoyed it as I had plenty of time — Journey
before 8 — Slow train — abomination of carriages —
hot & stuffy beyond description — for two hours
a broad flat plain — mostly grain crops — few
trees — the whole plain tremulous with heat —
changed cars at Lichtenfels & left Bavaria —
got to Coburg at about 11.30 & got out to break
our journey — The hottest place & clk — went
to the Bahnhof — a few steps from station — new
building — pleasant & commodious. Had a
quick table d'hote dinner at 12 — very good &
very cheap — only 2 m. each — then rested in a
darkened room until 2 — then to station & at
2.15 the exp. came & we resumed our journey —
still very hot but later a breeze came & my
relief at 5.15 when we reached Eisenach
almost pleasant — Drove at once to
the Goldenen Lion & to our great disappoint-
ment found that they had but two attic
rooms — one being very small — both hot and
uncomfortable. However we made shift
to stay all night & after a little preparation
we had our eveg meal in the garden —
more beer & much [illegible] — after that a short
walk up a hill — the boys to bed soon after
8 & I followed very soon after as I was very
tired — but I had a high Dudley time with
that snore & bear until 3 A.M — from that
to 6, Refreshing sleep.
