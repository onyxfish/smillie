## Left Page

### September Friday 16 1892

A very pleasant day. warm & sunny - a
shower before & a bright afternoon -
A walk in the Park before bkfo. After
that I went to a carpenter & gave
orders for a working Table, to a blind
framer — It was 11 when I took a cab
& drove to Ebury St., Herkomer's Studio,
hoping to find him or get trace of him
— now off on a vacation. Then I went to
a print shop & got the address of Mr.
A. Goulding, the plate printer. Then
home at 12.20 — after dinner I took my
plate of the Morgan Head & by bus rode
away out west to Shepherd's Bush Road
& there at No. 53 — Kingstone House — I found
Mr. Goulding & a model Printing establish-
ment. Mr. G & son were very agreeable
& I was given a proof that differs very
materially from the K. & V. proof that I
brought with me — It is much more power-
ful & brilliant. Then I was shown through
the parlors & reception rooms of Mr. G.'s house
where there were some beautiful proofs
upon the walls — Altogether my visit was
very satisfactory to me & I rode home on
the top of a bus in very good humor, get-
ting in after 6. Then tea — Ever wrote
a letter to Kate describing our London
quarters. To bed after 10.30.

Morgan plate — 3 hours times.

## Right Page

### September Saturday 17 1892

A charming day — cloudless — mild sunlight and
pleasantly cool. A short walk in the Park
before bkfo. after that A. & I spent some time
unpacking our winter trunks that were sent
up from Johnston & Sons yesterday. Then,
with the boys, we went to the great Whiteley's
stores & bought a lot of things — I got a couple
of small scales and an arrangement for the
tinting of water — to use in my etching work.
A. got some clothing for the young ones etc. —
Then we went to "The Basin" Kensington
Gardens, the boys having carried their boats.
There I left them with A. & I ret. home.
Dinner about 1.30 — After that I spent
some time getting my work Table ready,
my blind covered & my tools out. There
is nothing now, that I know of, to prevent
me from beginning my work on Monday
mng. After 4 o'clk. A., the boys and
I went out & had a long stroll in the
Park — we went to the Albert memorial
& back by the Basin — Many of the old
trees are very lovely — the effects of the
tempered sunshine — the greys & the
blues — are very delightful to me. We
got back at about sundown & at 6.30
had tea — A quiet evng. Reading
