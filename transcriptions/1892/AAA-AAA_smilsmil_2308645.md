## Left Page

### July, Saturday 30, 1892

*Thun. Thunerhof — Rooms 139–140
Grand Hotel de Mune*

A very hot, cloudless m'f. - air heavy & thunderous - day grew hotter as it advanced - at noon, the relief of clouds - thunder comes near — afternoon, clear but very hot - We'd very heavy thunder storms with hail up at 7 - b'kp' at 8.30 - Packed up & settled bill - not altogether godless in its extravagance - I should like to visit the place during the season in Sep't & Oct. While we were there there were only 35 names posted on the public register of guests — when every thing is working full [illegible] very gay & beautiful - The handsome proprietor, Mr. Chessey, bowed us out very politely with a bon voyage as we left to take the 10.33 train to Lausanne where we arrived a few minutes before 12 — Soon after we went into the Restaurant du Gare & had a regular déjeuner — 2.50 fr. each — very good, all things considered - the battle of Alsatians was particularly refreshing. We were at table ½ of an hour - our train for Berne did not leave until 1.50 - so Annie read & I amused my self watching the workings of the machinery of a Swiss R.R. Station - I could not see anything in advance of one of our average provincial stations — at 4.30 there was in great crowd — two trains going in different directions — great confusion — pushing & scrambling with employees struggling to control the ill-tempered & impatient ones — we got away 5 m. late in a very crowded train — every body having no end of hand luggage blocking passage ways — our route lay through a very fertile country very like some of the highly cultivated parts of N.Y. or Mass. We saw no mts until we approached Freiburg — at or near 6 am we first saw the Bernese Alps — as storms were approaching we did not see them under a light, altho' we saw them clearly — we made close R.R. connections at Bern at 4.30 & had a pleasant ride to Thun where we arrived at 6.15 — Drove to the G'd Hotel de Thune where we were speedily appalled at the Swellishness there of — & had a bad head ache so we did not go to table d'hôte but had a light meal à la carte — after that on the verandah for a while — at 7.30 to our bed rooms where A., J. & R. went at once to bed — Very heavy N.E. American thunderstorm with wind — Rain & hail to the great improvement of the atmosphere — at 8.30 I lighted candle, wrote my diary and a letter to Hamak — It was after 10 when I went to bed — another thunderstorm before m'f.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 31, 1892

A day of changes — delightful at times with light showers, the fringes of thunderstorms all about us. B'kp' at 9 — after that we all wandered out through the principal streets — up to the old Church & castle — the approaches to the latter were extremely picturesque. A. waited while the boys & I explored the tower to the very roof from which there is a beautiful view over the town into the lake & mts beyond — Haste to the church & from the church yard terrace we had other pleasant views. Back to hotel to lunch at 2.30 — not a very satisfactory meal, the pretentions of the hotel being considered — after that Ralph & I had quite a stroll up on the opposite bank of the river — back — wrote & paid a letter to George & had a nap. Thunderstorms still coursing about so that we did not go out for a walk — at 6 A. & the boys went to the Restaurant for their meals. I waited until 7 & then went to table d'hôte — a very solemn & ordinary function — I had another street walk after dinner. A. & I sat in one of the Salons reading until near 10 — then to bed.

Monday m'f. Paid bill — for pretty imposition & over charge this hotel takes the palm — we got in too late for dinner on Sat. & left Mond. at 8 A.M. Two rooms on top floor 2.4 fr. 3 service 6 fr. light 3 fr. 2 glasses milk 3 fr. — poor letter at most 1.50 fr. — mutton chops, on carte at 2 fr. chg'd in bill 2.50 fr. — things of that kind all through the bill — The Hotel Bellevue on the other side of road from Thunerhof was very inviting to me — the grounds were extensive & charming — the building also had an air of comfort about it that was very pleasant — but was not so fine as poor Thunerhof.
