## Left Page

### August, Monday 29, 1892

*Ostend, Hotel Royal de Prusse & de la Grande Bretagne — Rooms 37–38–50.*

Very pleasant day — slight bklp — soon after 8 — then bought tickets through to Dover — got baggage weighed & checked & everything ready for a start. Then Annie & the boys went out shopping & Emma & I went to the Bank — to the P.O. — to various churches, coming at last to the Cathedral where we were in to take our final look & were joined by Annie & boys — loitered about there until 11.30 — then to hotel & began to nap the waiter for our lunch & bill — at last at 12.15 we got a part of what was ordered & ate it — Very satisfactory as far as it went — prepared a lunch for the end & then I had to wait for the bill while my patience had nearly all gone — at last it came & at last we got away at 12.50 — To the station in a few minutes — a very large train & few seats but we got a very good carriage with but two occupants who proved to be, just before the end of our journey, Prof. Dunbar & his nephew Mr. Copeland & friends of Kitty Ireland & Sue Cook's — they left at Bruges to our regret — we got to Ostend at 3 o'clk & drove to Hotel & at once got the children to bed, where we soon followed them — A smart shower fell very soon after we got to the hotel.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 30, 1892

*Canterbury — Royal Fountain Hotel — Room 21.*

Splendid day — awoke at 4.30 & did not get to sleep again — up for a day at 6.30 & went out & had a walk on the Digue before bklp — at 8 — after that A., the boys, Emma & I walked away down to the east end of the Digue to the Kursaal — many people were already taking their mkt bath & innumerable children were playing on the sands. On our way back we stopped at the Cook's premises to see if we could get some money on our letter of Cr., but he could give us only French money & that we did not want — at the J.D. Morgan had no correspondent in either Ostend or Canterbury we were assured that any Bank would give us what we wanted — Then A., the boys & I walked all around, by the fishers boats & through the fish market where we saw fish wines being put at auction the fish freshly brought in — then on to the steamer "Ville de Douvre". The train came late & it was 11.15 before we got away — Bright, breezy day — steamer crowded — we secured as good seats as possible — the wind freshened & sea rose & by noon we had pitched about considerably — water & spray flying over the whole boat & drenching the forward part — a great deal of sickness — and we went below to get lunch — Poor Ruthven, none of us had a very good time — it was about 3 when we got to Dover — the approach not very impressive — I don't like the chalk cliffs & dingy buildings — Custom Ho inspection of small luggage by officers on the boat, then we went up the pier to the Custom Ho. to find our trunks sent on yesterday — got myself in the hands of a guard & got our trunks without difficulty — Emma found her trunk, but Harrison's was found only after some trouble — at last all our troubles & matters were settled & we got a way on the 4.35 train for Canterbury and had a very pleasant ride getting into C. about 6 — Got to the Hotel promptly & ordered a substantial supper which was eaten before 7 — Annie & I got to bed before 9.

The Royal Hotel de Prusse is hardly a house to be recommended as tho' we could not complain — we had comfortable beds — satisfactory bklp — and a not unreasonable bill.

Prof. Dunbar & Mr. Copeland crossed with us & went directly to London.
