## Left Page

### July, Saturday 2, 1892

*Milan — Hotel de l'Europe — Room 6.*

Up at 5. 30 & packed until 7 getting every thing finished up — then had help. and got away from the hotel promptly at 8.15 — to R.R. Station at 8.30 & got through with the weighing of baggage & buying of tickets without trouble then found that our train from Rome was 50 m. late. We waited patiently as possible — the Mitchells — the Howells — the Giffords — the Callisens & other verna passengers came to go by same train — At last at 10 o'clk — the Train came & we all scrabbled for seats — By good fortune I got all of our party — Hunters, Simma — Marjorie & ourselves into one compartment — our ride through the mts. was very interesting — viaducts over ravines — & tunnels adorably succeeding each other — The day was cloudless & the air was like wine — At Novi we changed cars — were nearly carried off to Turin through stupid R.R. management — our party had to divide itself into three parts but were partially reunited before we got to Milan — our Train lost more time — the run across the flat plain to Milan became monotonous as we were hot, tired, dusty & hungry — 3 o'clk — when we arrived — our luggage not on our train — went at once to the Hotel de l' Europe — washed & then all went down to dining room & had at dinner at 4 — a la carte. After that some time was spent at the Tho. Cook & Son Office arranging our route getting tickets for Geneva — then a run into the Cathedral — & after that through the Galleria — in one of the streets we sat down to table & had a light evening meal — very stupid waiter boy — could make him understand only by pantomime — but at last we got all that we asked for & more too — then we resumed our wanderings — Returning to hotel & to bed before 10.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 3, 1892

Another warm, cloudless day — nearly 9 when we had our breakfast, after I had been out — had a walk through the cathedral, & bought some fruit. After bkfst. A. & I went out & after some shopping bought a small trunk — It was about 11 when A. — Simma — Marjorie & I, with the boys, we went to the Cathedral — after a little time there, it was decided to be best that E. — M. — & I go alone to the Brera Gallery — when we got there — found that the place was not open to the public until 12 — as we did not care to wait we took a cab & rode out old monastery where the Leonardo da Vinci fresco of the Last Supper is to be seen. After a good look at that we rode back to the Brera Gallery & strolled through — I used my eyes until 12.45 — then I left — one — & went back to the hotel & had lunch with A. & the boys & after that wrote a note to Grauer, Brown & Co. — Genoa — concerning our two trunks left with them to be sent to London — then lay down & had a good nap — At 4 went with Simma — Marjorie & Mr. Gifford to the Cathedral & climbed to the roof taking just Ralton — The excursion was altogether satisfactory — we had a good guide, who explained intelligently concerning the building — the city and the outlying country — Back to hotel I had a rest before dinner — after that, roofing & waiting until after 8 — then finding that no one was ready to go with me I started alone for the Public Gardens — great crowds, — promenades all by electric lights — music by a very fine band — after 9 went to Cathedral to see if I could get a moonlight effect upon it. But the electric lights spoiled it so — so I returned to hotel shortly after 10 & to bid. Beds, very bad — not so good as at Genoa — electric light charges, reasonably extortionate.
