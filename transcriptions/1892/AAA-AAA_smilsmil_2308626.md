## Left Page

### June Wednesday 22 1892

*miles run, 371.*

A thick morning — later it cleared &
at times the sun shone faintly out.
At sundown, a fog. The day was
to me a tiresome one — my head &
my legs felt heavy & I was without
appetite. In the m'f I got out my
"etching" paper & tried to write upon
it — but I could make no head-
way & after a time gave it up.
I slept for nearly two hours in the
m'f and again in the afternoon.
much bothered by headache. — I
managed to read a little.

## Right Page

### June Thursday 23 1892

*miles run — 345.*

much the same sort of weather.
Thick in the m'f — almost sunning
at noon, thickening again at night.
Ship's Sunday — the band played in
the m'f, as usual, afr. and in the after-
noon they played, forward, for the steer-
age. For a time in the morning I
worked on my "etching" paper — but it
was not agreeable & after an hour &
I was glad to go on deck & take
a breezing. I finished reading
Rob. Grant's "Reflections of a Married
Man" — not very deep but fairly clever
& amusing. Got a good start on "A
Question of Taste" by Maarten Maartens
a work of mildly sub-acied humor and
upon a higher plane than "Reflections".
The dinner was more interminable
than ever. On deck with Annie until
late!! — music everywhere. Zitter —
Mandoline — Guitar — pianos & singing
to say nothing of the band.
We expect to pass Flores to-night —
the most northerly & westerly of the
Azores — and other islands in the
morning.
