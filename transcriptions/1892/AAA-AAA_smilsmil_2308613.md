## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 25, 1892

*moved my studio from fourth ave.*

Very pleasant day — growing warmer. Got to studio before 9. Hard work for awhile sawing blocks & nailing up boxes containing parts of printing press — then on with other work. The Storage People were to be on hand at 9.30 — I waited until after 11 — then went to the Store house & found that they were coming a week from to-day. Made arrangements to have a van at studio at 2 — then back & worked until 1 — then home to lunch & back to studio at 2 — (Schilling called at the house about Etching club but — we rode down together) Van & a half & 4 men waiting — they worked hard until 6 — It was an awful time — everything tumbled about in any way — Tables turned on their heads, heels up — drawers packed full & never taken out — I went to warehouse at 6 & saw Burnham — then home & rested until dinner — at 6.40 — at 8.30 went to studio & got my last traps — then home. — Charl came in for a little while. — Then I attended to my little toe & to bed.

A heavy thunderstorm about 5 A.M.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 26, 1892

*to Montrose.*

A warm pleasant mt. An early start. After bkf. went to G.C. Station & saw Jackson's rp. man — then to a place across the way in 42d St. & bought a traveling bag. Then, at 9 o'clk., to Manhattan Storage Co. There until 11.30 superintending the unloading of the vans loaded yesterday p.m. Packing is reduced to a fine art in this institution. It astonished me to see the the way things were stowed — all of my studio remains were crowded into a little vault that seemed not a tenth part of the size of my studio that seemed so crowded by bulky & immovable furniture. I got home, very tired, at 11.45 — hustled things into my bag & with Annie, the boys & Anetta, started at 12.° for the Hoboken ferry — We had more than time enough & were fortunate enough to secure good seats in a crowded Drawing Room Car. At first it was intolerably close & hot. As we climbed Pocono Mt. the weather changed — it was much cooler & there was a little rain — got to Santon bet. 6 & 7 and had a very good supper — got to Montrose on time. 7.55 — & found Herrick waiting for us. We rode up to the house — a good fire in big stove — It was some time before we could get the beds ready & the young ones stowed for the night — It was about 10 when I got to bed. Very damp & chilly — Rain in the evef. and heavy rain & thunderstorms during the night.
