## Left Page

### October, Friday 14, 1892

A dull day with some rain & one gleam of sunshine. After bkfst I took Ralphie to see Dr. Earle — he continues to be out of sorts & has constantly temperatures between 99° & 100°. Dr. E. says it is inactive liver & gave him calomel. I worked from 11.30 to 12.30 — then out & had a walk — in at 1.30 — dinner — worked from 3 to 4 — Then out & had a long walk — to Kensington museum & through streets & squares to the Albert gate & thence home. Tea — Eve'g A. rec'd a letter from Emma J— who has arrived, with Marjorie Spring, safely home — she is just to tell the news that the 38th St. house is let. I wrote a letter to Kate.

morgan — 2 h.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 15, 1892

*Kiralfy's Venice*

A day of fog — we all slept until 8.30 when Annie came to tell us that bkfst. was on the table. After bkfst. it was so dark that I could not see to work so I determined to go to the British Museum Print Room. went by R.R. to Gower St — Too long a walk from Gower St. Station to the Museum — I looked over a number of early Italian engravings & did some reading concerning them & made notes. Home at 1.20 — Dinner — as soon thereafter as we could get away, Annie, the boys & I went by R.R. to Addison Rd. Station to the great Spectacle of Imre Kiralfy's, Venice the Bride of the Sea. The most extensive and easily entertaining show that I have ever seen — munificent and elaborate — a large sheet of water between the audience & the stage, the latter not less than 350 feet long — 400 dancers — 300 chorus — 100 Band — 1400 performers — great barges & many gondolas — a real pageant in the water — the finale scene was bewildering — all reflected in the shimmering water of the Grand Canal — Performance over at 4.30 — then we went into Modern Venice — booths & canals — the gondolas taking passengers at 6d each — went in to the Salviati glass works and saw some graceful & highly ornamented vases made — out at 5 — Home before 6 — Tea — Eve'g
