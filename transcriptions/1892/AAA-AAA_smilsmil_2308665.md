## Left Page

### September, Thursday 8, 1892

*Ventnor. Royal Marine Hotel.*

Superb day. Packed our traps ready for a move +
had stk- at 8.30 - left the hotel at 9.20 + took g 40
train for Carisford where we arr. soon after 10 -
we left our traps at the station + walked at once to
the castle. I was very agreeably disappointed in
finding the castle a more extensive picturesque
\+ interesting ruin from than I had expected it.
It is so nearly hidden by trees + overgrown by ver-
dure of one kind + another that his deadly in-
taken for a mound overgrown with vines + trees
when seen from a short distance. We took our
time + wandered all over the place + at noon
returned to the Red Lion Inn - learning that we
could have a hot roast at 1.15 I wandered off
again going wherever the way before me seemed
most enticing. I was much interested seeing
the tile + tessellated pavement of an old Roman
house discovered about 30 years ago. At 1.30 we
had a very satisfactory dinner dat 2.40 started
in a private conveyance for Shanklin - our
ride through hedged lanes + over breezy open
country was simply delightful - the day was
glorious + exhilarating - we got to Shanklin
at 4.25 - left the wagon at the head of the Chine
\+ went down through it - it did not strike me as
a very impressive or lovable hole - View out
on the sands - tide out - very mursy - then up
on the cliff - glorious, far-stretching view over the
sea - very pleasant stage to cliff with cottages
a little distance back - crowds of people - a very
characteristic peri-patetic "town" of banjo per-
formers - two men - two women wearing masks -
playing + singing - dressed pantaloon fashion -
band playing in club grounds - delightful
shady lanes - many villas + few genuine old
straw-thatched cottages - soon after 5 started
on for Ventnor - our driver taxed our patience -
a long hill to the tops of the down - a grand
view from the tops - then a long hill down to Bon-
church - then a steeper one - got to hotel
at 6.15 + were pleasantly welcomed + installed
in our old rooms - found a batch of letters
forwarded from London - gave the boys
their supper + at 7 A. + I dined a table d'hote
After that I read Times until Ralph was asleep
then A. + I. Read our letters - several of them
long ones. I had a brisk walk in the beautiful
moonlight - lovely light over the sea - to bed
at 11.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 9, 1892

Pleasant mts. but soon was grey + overcast. We
had stk- at 9 + after that Mr. Francis asked us to
go out sailing with their son a boy about 12 yrs. old -
neither Mr. or Mrs. F. went, but the boy + two of his com-
panions + a maid + a young English couple whose
names I did not get. A. the boys + I completed the
list of passengers - the wind was steady + the sea
was smooth so we had an uneventful time
and got back to our beach just before 1 o'clk.
There we climbed the cliff through Ventnor
Park + got to the hotel after 1.30 + had a late
dinner at 2 - after that, feeling sleepy had
Mary + I indulged in a nap instead of a walk
as I had intended - after that I wasted more
time reading a copy of the semi-weekly Trib.
sent to me by Charle. We had a rain-fall
tea at 6.30 - after the young ones were in bed
Annie read to me a long letter that she had
just written to Kate - then I mailed it.
