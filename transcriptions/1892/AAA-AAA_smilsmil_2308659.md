## Left Page

### August, Saturday 27, 1892

*Cologne. Hotel du Nord. Rooms 77–78–*

Up soon after 6 — Cool, cloudy, pleasant day.
finished packing & we were all ready soon after
7.30 — bkfst at 8 — & at 8.30 we bid good bye to
the Elisabethenkirche — At 9.21 our Schnell Zug
came along & we got comfortable seats in a
through car. The ride was through a pleasant
but uninteresting country — much grain being
harvested & ground ploughed — we ate our
ample lunch of rolls & chocolate at 1 o'clk —
while our German neighbors stuffed themselves,
one 2 year old baby with ham & brown bread,
veal, claret cakes & chocolate — the ham and
brown bread being raised in news papers, much
interested when we got into the manufacturing
district, passing through Hagen, Barmen, [illegible]
feld, etc — Soon after passing through the
latter places, a fine view over the [am? the?] factory
low country. I got to Cologne at just about
5 o'clk — after a tiresome ride of 7½ hours with-
out change. At once to the Hotel du Nord
washed & made a change & then went
to Table d'hote at 6.15 Had a very acceptable
dinner — after that had a surprising meeting
in the great hall — Sunna & Mannie whom
we thought to be in Paris — they came down the
Rhine today & reached the Hotel about
the same time that we did — we then
had a long consultation concerning the
Cholera scare which has assumed a
more serious character — Cholera is now
Epidemic at Hamburg — has appeared at
Brussels & Antwerp & quarantine regulations
threatening possible trouble to us if we
take the Holland trip have been proposed —
The result of our talk is that we have
abandoned that part of our trip and will
go direct to England — E. & M. will
give up Paris & will go with us.
To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 28, 1892

A pleasant, cool, breezy day — Half past 8 when we got
our bkfst. Then Annie spent some time with Minnie &
Margie & I went out to the Cathedral. I wandered
there for some time — back at 11 with the entire party
to the Cathedral and heard service — the singing
in the Congregation was a surprise to me — the chanting
was a little less than singing & I ventured to think
it peculiar and I was very deeply moved by it.
The organ, the place, all made it so impressive
that I could not altogether control my emotions.
At 12 o'clk. we got tickets & went into the choir,
the chapels & the Treasury house where a dear
old priest patted the heads of the boys & described
& explained the wonders of the shrines & relics —
Then we got tickets & went up into the Temple
or inner galleries — then up one of the towers to the
foot of the spire — thin down & outside along the
nave roof under the buttresses — It was 1.30
when we got down — hurried to hotel and
had a Table d'hote dinner — After that the
boys & I were led out over the iron bridge along
the other side of the river & returned by the
bridge of boats — wandered through some of
the streets & into some churches — then back
to hotel & at 4.45 we all went out riding
going to the various interesting churches (of
course we saw the bones of the 11,000 virgins, the
thorn from the Christ crown & the alabaster
vase that was used at the Cana marriage feast)
we drove through the finest modern streets
& got back to the hotel at 6.30 — then had
something to eat — Read more about cholera
& had a long chat with young W. Cornish
about Greek & Hebrew Poetry — chimes,
bells & things in general — To bed at 10.

The Hotel du Nord is a good tourist's Hotel —
good beds — good food — fair service and,
on the whole, fair charges. The closets
cannot be commended — imperfectly
lighted & ventilated.
