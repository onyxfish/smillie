## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 12, 1892

*Brigue. Hotel d'Angleterre. Room 22.*

Up at 5 & had Help - at 6 - pleasant, cloudy
m'f. Got away from hotel on the top of
a bus promptly at 6.30 and drove into
to Gravellona where we took Train for
Domo dossola where we arr'd about 9.30
after an interesting ride through the valley
of the Tosa - went at once to the Hotel Ville
Ancien Poste - & from there direct to the
Diligence Bursa where we had our
awful row getting matters settled con-
cerning our seats & our baggage - The officials
in these little places & the clownest & clumsi-
est of people - Took a little walk & had
a good look at the mts about - fine - then
back to hotel & waited for lunch — at 12.5
we got away on the Diligence - the clumsiest of all
possible contrivances - in fact a most damnable abortion
"unnot Marjorie took the coupe seats - mr mrs [illegible]
interior & Annie, Ralph & J, Maquette - It was my [illegible]
the wind was with us & the dust was suffocating -
But before long a coming thunder shower gave us shade
& as the road veered & turned the wind was more favorable
It was to us a continuation of excitements - I never saw
grander cliffs or wilder gorges - we had light showers
that were no inconvenience until we got well up
the mt when, for a little time we had the top of [illegible]
up - the clouds clinging to mt tops added mystery - for a time
I walked - at 5.15 we reached the little village of Simplon
& had a good meal - away again at 6 - at 7.5 reached the
old Hospice & in about 20 m's more got to the new one - The
clouds now came down upon us, together with twilight
& there was little that we could see except masses of
the Alpine Rose - A. R. & D. were now in the coupe - Ralph
soon fell asleep in my arms - The descent was exciting
one of the five horses kicked himself clear of his harness
& all went on a run - down the steep, winding
road - wet & slippery - Sometimes in dense cloud -
then with yawning rifts showing dark abysses to me
Rushing torrents below & it was more than exciting -
at times I thought it dangerous - I will never be satisfied
with this experience - I want to walk most of
the way, taking a couple of days for it - at 9 - changed
horses, 2d time, at Bérisal & in one hour more, drove
into Brigue - Ralph sleeping all the way - got to
hotel at once & Right to bed - big headache - soon asleep.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 13, 1892

*Riffle berg - Rooms 16-17.*

A fine day of cloud shadows & sunshine - up at 6 o'clk and
got my traps again all in shape by 7 - The youngsters
were in good humor & we decided to push on at once
& had a little stroll - delightful m'f. - interesting old castle -
[illegible] at 8 & at 9.15 we all got away in good shape - The Hotel
d'Angleterre a poor affair - the best in the place we were told.
Much interested in the R.R. pass. cars - a combination of English
& American styles - possible to pass through the length of the
train - cabinet conveniences - electric lighting - warm and
cold air ventilation within control of passengers -
washing house air brakes - all went to visit Station. [illegible]
Where they [illegible] went on & we waited for an hour -
then took a small train, narrow gauge R.R., 2d class
- I saw a 1st class - a very interesting road [illegible] conf.
See miler - great masonry walls - tunnels & bridges - on
steep grades, central rail & cogs - Roaring glacier
torrent & wild gorges - occasional glimpses of snow
peaks beyond as we advanced - Got [illegible] at 1.15 and
at once to Hotel Mc Cerwin & had lunch - poor, to my [illegible]
then got a room & rearranged our luggage for horse back
to the Riffle berg & changed our clothes to warmer - at 3.45
our Procession started - 4 horses - Ralph, like a midget,
with his colored glasses sticking to the end of his nose,
in the leading horse - A - 2 & Jim in the others - luggage
strapped on behind - each horse led by a man - Marjorie
& I walking - Jim insisted upon walking & I got off for
his horse - he walked nearly all the way - The Trail was
a constant delight - path through larch forest & grand views
of the Matterhorn & a banner of white cloud floating from the
top - got to the Riffle berg about 6 - all in very good condition -
and had our dinner at once - by the shelf. The matter-
horn loomed out, entirely clear - majestic in the evening
light - after dinner I was much distressed by an
attack of dyspepsia - went to bed before - awoke at
about 10 o'clk & seeing bright moonlight, got the pillow
& stepped out of bed - to the window from which I had a
fine view of the Matterhorn - The clouds had disappeared
almost entirely - wreaths of ghost-like mist came up
from the abyss of the valley - the cold light of the moon upon the
glittering snow - and the stillness of all suggested a dead
world - it was tremendously impressive - something
not to be forgotten.
