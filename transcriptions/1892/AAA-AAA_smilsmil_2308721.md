## Left Page

### December, Thursday 29, 1892

Thick, foggy mt & clearing pleasantly
in the afternoon — After 6&p — we went
to Whiteley's & bought a large basket
Trunk — put it on a cab & A. drove
home with it — I set to work at once
our plan of packing was revised —
I packed a trunk with things to be
left in storage here & before 2 the
man came for them — then A having
packed her new trunk & relieved
the congestion I was able to put
my overflow in another trunk and
everything was lovely — Dinner nr
2 o'clk — I did not get all of the
packing completed until after 5.
then had a rest before tea — lively
we sat with Rainb road & one!
we settled up very satisfactorily
with Mrs. Tantost — I wrote part
of a letter to Kate & to bed soon
after 10.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 30, 1892

*At Mlle. Arneuoris, 53 Ave. d' Jena —
Paris —*

Up at usual time — foggy — got a comfortable
b&p — everything in readiness when the bus
came at 8.45 — trunks were piled on top & we got
away just before 10 — fog came in very thick — we
got to Victoria Station at 10.30 — had to pay $1p.25 for
excess of luggage — 3 tickets $32.01 — when we got to
Calais, delayed to get a baggage ticket for A also $2.25 — in
all — it cost just about $50 to get from lodgings in
London to lodgings in Paris — we got away in time
at 11 — freezing cold — but we had plenty wraps &
had taken precautions in dressing — far departing
from the train we left London — At Dover, it was
cloudless — and there — got there just a little
before 1 — were hustled on to the fine new steamer
"Empress" & got away at 1.15 — no more motion in
midway I see, or a mile. Those on the Hoboken ferry.
Got to Calais at 2.35 — but it was after 3 before our
train started — the air did not have the cellar-
like chill that filled the sluggish cars, but it
was very cold — ice forming thickly on the
windows — as in the English cars, a lamp was in
the woodwork overhead & we had a cup of
hot water for our feet but it passes my comprehension to understand why a civilized
public should submit to such discomforts
so needlessly — A great highway of travel
between the two great cities of the world does
not have the common comforts that are to be
found on the plains of the far west of the U.S —
we all had a little nap at last & got to the
Paris Station, Gare du Nord at 7.35 — about 25
min. late — it was 8 o'clk — before our luggage was
arranged in the Custom House — for us — Ellis Howe
& Mr. Kipling met us on the platform — we had a
small bus — got through with our search —
opened only one trunk — at 15 m. — sat about 8.15
We all started for Mlle Arneuoris — it took 40
min. to drive there — brilliant, freezing moonlit. Our rooms ready for us, convenient &
cosy — a fire in each room — it was quite 9
before we all sat down to a supper of hot soup,
chicken & mutton — after that all went to our bed-
room where Mr. Richd. Kipling joined us — the boys
went put to bed at 10 — we sat before the fire
toasting our toes & chatting until after 11 —
then sleigh & the Kiplings left us & we got to our
bed.
