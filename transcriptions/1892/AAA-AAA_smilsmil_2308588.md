## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 5, 1892

Overcast with a number of light sprinklings. Very close & warm but an improvement on yesterday. Went out before bkfs. & did the marketing. After bkfs. went up to Yale's — just missed him — His wife gave birth to a daughter at 5 oclk. this m'f. Got to studio about 10 & to work on Jade Plate, strengthening some shadows. Schilling came in & sat with me some time — very much exercised about matters in the Etching Club — does not like the plan of combining with the Socy of Am. Etchers. Wrote a note to Herrick. Home to lunch & after 2 went to Yale's to see him — arrange etc. for Friday at 3 p.m. To studio at 3.30 — worked on Jade Plate one hour — then fussed an hour getting things ready to mount for framing a Parchment & [illegible] of the Voice from the Cliff. Home at 6.20 — dinner — At 8 went with A. to Mr. Green's — they to go to Choral Club Rehearsal — I walked down to N.A. of D. 23d St & 4th Ave — there for a short time — then to Studio — got Proofs of "Goldsmith's Daughter" & took it home — rearranged etchings in library — Charl came in & spent an hour with me — he is to start for Florida to-morrow m'f. At 10 I went for A. but just missed her — on my return found her waiting on the stoop as she could not get in.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 6, 1892

Overcast m'f, growing cooler & clearer until the evg. which was delightful. A short walk with Ralph before bkfs. To studio about 10 — Found a note from Turneur making an appointment for me to meet him at Bishop's house, 871 Fifth Ave. I wrote notes & fussed about until noon — then to meet T. — stopping a moment at home. Saw T. & learned, as I supposed, that he had no power to judge or pass upon my plate. Got a Yokahoma address for Mr. Bishop. Home to lunch at 2.40 — After 3 went out with A. on a shopping expedition — to Daming's & saw curtains — to John Williams — 27, 52 & 11th Ave, to see iron lamps — then back to 9 Elm Bro's. in 23d St & brought one there — then to studio at 5.10 & found Mrs. Frank Waller & her daughter waiting. Frank came soon after — they like the study — I have painted in payment for their easel & took it with them. Home at 6.30 — dinner. After I wrote a letter to Herrick and brought from the attic a lot of pamphlets to make up into a bundle to be given away. To bed at about 10 o'clk.
