## Left Page

### March Wednesday 2 1892

A nasty, nasty day — dull & raw —
High wind & freezing cold — a thin
coat of snow frozen hard over every-
thing. Got to studio at 10·15 Found
a lot of letters — among them a very
complimentary one from Vickery con-
cerning San Luis Ray Mission plate —
Went in to Tiffany's & saw glass for
the front door — there to N.A. — then to
2d. Nat. Bk. — then to Hunter's — then
again to N.A., got one of my etchings
& back to studio — Home to lunch at
1·15 — back at 3 — went in to Tiffany
& saw Mitchell — they want my studio
& I was politely informed that I would
be expected to vacate by June 1st. —
Dr. Gerster came in & spent some
time & a little money with me — as he
bought a proof of my "Old Cedars, Coast
of Maine" — Home at 6 — Dinner.
Evetg. very much bunged up by
a cold — Charl came in & spent
some time with us — To bed after
10 p.

## Right Page

### March Thursday 3 1892

The weather at last cleared to be
bright but I was so completely busted
that I did not dare to go out of the
house. Evetg. my temperature rose
to 100°. During the day my head
& eyes hurt me so much that I
could not use them — much of the
day was spent watching two men
set the Tiffany glass in the front doors.
It is very rich but in some ways
I am a good deal disappointed —
on the whole I think it monotonous
where more variety of color is intro-
duced, over the door, it is not entirely
harmonious to my eye. Evetg.
I was in such discomfort that I
could only loll about on the sofa.

During the day I managed to write
a letter to Mr. Koshler & part of one to
Mr. Vickery.
