## Left Page

### November Monday 7 1892

A cloudless day but with fog so thick
that the sunlight penetrated feebly
for the few hours between 11 & 3. The
weather was also much cooler. I
worked for about an hour on Morgan
plate & then went out for a walk around
the Paddington station — a short time
after I came in I had again to go out,
taking Jim to his swimming lesson —
Ralph went with us. After the lesson
a walk in the Park & home. abt — a
little more work on my plate, then
dinner — another hour I worked after
dinner & then, before 4 I had to give
up — the fog came down so thick that I
could no longer see to work — I went
out & walked for awhile — Annie
had gone to call upon Mrs. Beeson & Miss
dess — and upon Mrs. Walter Blackman.
Rec'd to-day letters from Charl. Kate
& W. g. Parks — Evey I wrote for a little
while a letter to Mary — then Annie
read aloud, from Madame Maartens
"An old maid's love".

## Right Page

### November Tuesday 8 1892

Beastly day — fog growing heavier
until after 2 o'clk. At noon had to
light the gas to see to do anything — I
went out & did not dare cross to the Park.
I could see nothing as I stood upon the
curb stone but could hear the constant
rumble of slowly passing vehicles. Of
course I could not see to work, altho' at
about 10 o'clk. I tried for awhile. At
2.30 Annie, the boys & I went out to the
Bayswater Road for another look at the
fog — then, supposing it to be thicker in the
city I went by underg'd. R.R. to Bishop's gate
& to my surprise found that there was
no fog there — then home again, vexed
at the loss of a day — I should have gone
at once, in the w't to the Museum where
I could have accomplished some-
thing. Evey, again Annie read
aloud from M. M.
