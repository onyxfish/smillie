## Left Page

### May, Sunday 29, 1892

Bright for a little while in the m'g
then heavier clouds came up from
the west & south west & then light
showers. The wind went around
by the south & at night there was
every threatening appearance of
an easterly storm for Decoration
Day to-morrow. A late breakfast.
The day was spent as was yesterday —
lots of little things done in doors &
out of doors. I gathered our first
cutting of asparagus from our garden.
It was after 2 when we had dinner
— a very good one cooked by Gretta.
When the showers permitted I
trimmed plants & vines upon the
grounds. Tea after 6 — Eveʼg
Annie read aloud to me from a
Scottish dialect series of sketches; "A
window in Thrums".

## Right Page

### May, Monday 30, 1892

*Decoration Day. The Kent's arrived.*

Dull, wet m'g — easterly wind —
changing to the S.W., growing warm
& muggy & clearing about noon —
showers — a lovely, clear eve'g.
Bkfs't about 8 — my work interfered
with by the weather — went downtown
about 10 o'clk — to Newton's black-smith
shop & sat by him until noon when
he finished making irons upon which
to stretch wire netting for vines on
piazza — dinner after 1 — great
trouble in trying to get some one to
finish the cleaning about the house
& piazzas — At 2 went with Ralph
down to the Court House to see the
Procession — Home at 3 & was busy
until 6 — Then Tea — Eveʼg at
7.55 was at Station when the train
came in with Mr. & Mrs. Kent & Miss K —
Mrs. K's Mother, Mr. [illegible] —
After getting them started for the
house I telephoned to W.G. Parke
at Scranton — he said "come
down of course" — Then home —
it was quite 10 when the K's
got settled & to bed. They insisted
upon our occupying our own Room,
much to my gratification —
To bed at 10.30.
