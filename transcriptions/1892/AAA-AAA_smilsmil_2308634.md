## Left Page

### July, Friday 8, 1892

A much warmer day — I feel the
effects fit — my trouble continues — old
Dr Callisau gave me a bottle of his chalk
mixture — I loafed about the house — I
tried to do a little writing but without
success — got part of a letter written to
Chad — late in the afternoon went down
to the bathing houses — Marjorie & Jim
went in — ate a very light dinner — the
color nervuis & lake in the out[?] was
so pecially lonely — The rest of the party
went out in a boat — I did not — I
wrote part of a letter home — after the
arrival of the boat party & when the
boys had been put to bed I joined the
party on the terrace — glorious moonlight
& lemonade together — with some mildly
skittish performances to allow Marjorie
to give vent to some of her feelings —
feelings — Inkisses of moonlight in
Italian lake — to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 9, 1892

A very close, muggy day with thunderstorms
in the mts west of us in the afternoon — I
felt better but still bowels troubled me — after
[illegible] — A. E. M. & the boys — & I took boat and
went over to Isola Bella where we spent
the rest of the morning going through the
Palace & the Gardens where we were very
much interested — in the blazing sunshine
the heat was intense but in the groves of
magnolia — orange trees and American Pines
the relief from glaring light & the coolness of
shade was delightful — the old gardener who
had us in charge was made happy by our interest
in his pets & gave us specimens of Camphor, lemon
& other fragrant leaves. In one of the garden
walks, shut off from visitors, we had an elderly
gentleman seated, reading — The gardener told us
it was Signor the Count Borromeo — we got back
to hotel at 12 — lunch at 12.30 — Ralph very fretful
— got him to sleep & nothing else was accomplished
until 4.30 when A. E. M. & Jim went into the
lake for a bath — As we were all walking in
the garden after dinner we were agreeably sur-
prised to see Mr & Mrs Gifford, just in from Venice —
they got a warm greeting — The eve[ning] wonderfully
quiet & full of color — The full moon outshone itself —
Marjorie was invited to go out rowing with two
young Irish women & Annie took the boys to put
them to bed — At 8.30 I took Emma out rowing —
also — the moonlight effects were wonderful —
the delight & beauty was enhanced by the
bells of Pallanza — Stresa — Baveno & from all the
churches about — they rang for about 15 m. and
the tones ranged all the way from vibrations
of deepest bass up to the clanging of the little
fellows whose treble came from near & far —
two great bells in Pallanza rang with great
deliberation & impressiveness —

To bed before 10 —
