## Left Page

### September, Saturday 24, 1892

when walking before bkf. was caught in a characteristic English drizzle — but later, it cleared & the afternoon was charming — I worked on plate from 10 to 12 — then to Bond where I met Annie & boys with their boats. Home to dinner soon after 1 but that function did not take place until near 2. after that A. took boys to Westminster Abbey & Embankment. I worked from 3 to 4.30 — then I went to the Serpentine & had a row for ½ an hour — then walked briskly until 6 — Tea at 6.30. Eve'g assorting letters & last summer's bills —

Morgan — 3.30 hours.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 25, 1892

Pleasant in the middle of the day — dull m't & late afternoon. It was about noon when we all started out for a walk & went over to the west end of Kensington Gardens & then through to the southerly side admiring much the noble
(grand Promenade or Broad Walk)
trees on the Avenue, for the S.W. corner — then back through the private road known as Kensington Palace Gardens, again into the Park & home about 1.30 — Dinner at 2. It was about 4 when I set out for No. 35 Acacia Road to see if I could find Ernest Parton at home. By underground R.R. I went to St. John's Wood Station & then had a 20 m. walk to the house which was "detached" & pleasant in itself but the road & the part of the city was not agreeable to me. I found that E. is in France, at work there — saw his wife who expects to join him soon — they may be away most of the winter — I was much discouraged and after a pleasant call left feeling more lonesome & homesick than ever — I went into the studio — a small, homelike room at the back of the house & saw there his pictures from the French Salon of last year that he proposes to send to Chicago next year in the English Section — on my way back I walked to the Marlborough Rd. Station — much nearer & got home at 6.45 & had tea — Eve'g I wrote part of a letter to Parker —
