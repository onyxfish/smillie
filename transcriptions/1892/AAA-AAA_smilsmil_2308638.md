## Left Page

### July, Saturday 16, 1892

*Martigny. Hotel de l'Aigle Post. Room 1 ψ*

Up at 7 — glorious mf. Ralph awakened
quite himself — temp. normal — to our great
joy — He had his bfkp. served in our Room
we breakfasted at about 8.30 — then, after
consultation we decided to push on and
we packed our traps — at 10.30 Jim & I went
out for a little tramp — we walked to Dugorny
gorge — a wild rock flume through which the
stream from the Gorner Glacier flows — It am
very wild but I enjoyed it more this walk through
the larch wood — the roar of the torrent & the
glimpses of the grand Matterhorn & other sunu
[illegible] ruts — these, together with the intoxicating
air made the little tramp altogether delightful —
back to hotel at 12 — Rested until 12.45 — then
had a good meal — beef steak, potatoes & eggs —
Paid our bill — entirely satisfied — Left by
the 1.55 min 3d class — Satisfied again — saw all
that we saw going up & much more — It is a
grand bit of valley & gorge — Got to Visp at 4.15
& waited half an our for train to Martigny — we
had a whole 1st class car to ourselves — after
this 2d class Swiss is good enough for us — The
guide book says that the Rhone Valley is un-
interesting but I found enough to keep me
engaged until we got to Martigny at 7.15 —
Rode at once to the Hotel de l'Aigle Post — Had
every thing to ourselves — a 2d class hotel where
all were anxious to please — Had a fairly
good meal & then the boys were put to bed.
Tested R.'s temp. & found it normal — He has borne
the day's work admirably — Interviewed the Chief
of Guides & arranged for a vehicle over the Tête
Noire tomorrow — Then out & walked about as
long as I could see anything —
To bed before 10.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 17, 1892

Up at 8 o'clk. after a somewhat restless night.
Rain falling steadily — no possible chance
for an advance to-day so we settled ourselves
to be as patient & comfortable — The house is
fairly clean if not altogether sweet & there
is an evident desire to please us. Ralph
has been disturbed during the day by a
stomach & a slight increase of temperature,
but is in excellent spirits — A. & I spent
part of the mf. writing letters home — we
took a short walk in the rain to the
pharmacy & got some Bismuth for Ra —
At 1.30 had a fairly good dinner with
lots of delightful Alpine strawberries —
wrote again in the afternoon — finished letter —
then had quite a walk — weather clearing —
supper at 7 — and to bed with a
monstrous great pain under my
apron about 8. —

We were treated well at the Hotel
de l'Aigle but it is not an inviting house
the Hotel Clerc or the Monte Rosa, close
to it are fine houses.
