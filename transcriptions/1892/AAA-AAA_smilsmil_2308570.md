## Left Page

### February Saturday 27, 1892

A brilliant + exceedingly cold day. Up + out soon after 7 — Had a brisk walk on the piazza before bkfp. at 7.30 — At about 9.30 I started for the city getting to the museum of fine Arts before 11. met Mr. Stephen Schoff with Mr. Koehler — saw [illegible] — loning for a few min. Then Mr. Worthington came in + he, K. + I went up stairs to the [illegible] + K. went through most of the [illegible] very carefully with us explaining exhibits — we met Mr. Bicknell, a young Boston Etcher, and he joined the party — It was after 1 when K., B. + I went to a Restaurant near the Museum + had a substantial lunch — after it we sat + talked until 3.30 — Then K. + I ret'd to the Museum where we looked at the exhibits until 5. Then I returned to Cambridge getting in half an hour before tea. Mr. K. was very much pleased with my Aqua-tint "An old dam"! Evef. Annie, Miss Howe and Katchen went to a near neighbor's — Mr. Kennedy's — to see + hear Miss Deitrick explain her new system of Physical Culture — Sue + I had a quiet evening reading, each for ourselves — At 10 the others returned + after talking over the evef's entertainment we got to bed.

## Right Page

### February Sunday 28, 1892

A very disagreeable day — overcast — exceedingly cold (Ther. 10° at 8 o'clk AM) with light, dry snow. I was out at about 8.30 + swept the snow from piazza + then waited for nearly half an hour before breakfast — This meal was served at 9.30 — It was 10.45 when I started for town — there I changed cars for Jamaica Plain + rode out to Roxbury getting to W. Koehler's house 33 Beech Glen St. just at 12 — was very cordially rec'd by Mr. + Mrs. K. + daughter. A very early dinner was served of which I could eat very little — After it — K. + I retired to his "den" where we talked over the plan + scope of the book that he is urging me to attempt! It was before 4 when I left the house to return — The cold was of such a disagreeable, raw penetrating character that I walked a good part of the way — I had a rest before tea — Evef., toasting before a fire, much of the time being spent over a copy of to-day's N.Y. Tribune — Annie wrote a short note to Jim and I wrote one in print for Ralph.
