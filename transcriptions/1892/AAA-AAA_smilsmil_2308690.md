## Left Page

### October Friday 28 1892

*[top margin note: Misses Furgeson & Abbott]*

Another beastly day — dark & very wet.
It was after 10 when, by bus I rode to
Piccadilly Circus & then walked to the
Royal Ins. of Painters in Water-Colors — when
the Private View of the O"[illegible] of the Ins.
of Painters in Oil-Colors was in progress.
A dreary trial for me — 636 pictures &
not one that really stirred me. It is
strange to me that in so large a number
I could not have found a few to my
taste — but with hardly an exception to
my way of seeing & thinking they were
marred by disagreeable color — want
of breadth & atmosphere, & clumsy tech-
nique — everything was very painty & more
or less conventional. The Binton is very
conservative & us ensibly clings to the ways
of his fathers. Annie joined me in the
galleries later & at 2.45 we went home
in a cab. Dinner. After that I had a short
nap & at 3.30 Annie & I, by cab, went to
Holley Lodge — Sey mour Place, Fulham Road
Miss Furgeson was out but Miss Abbott and
her younger sister went with us to Dr. Wood-
ward's, not far distant. He has a position
in the Museum of Nat. History. He has sweet
daughters who sketch & paint; having a studio
in the rear of their house. They had a "tea"
& we were invited. There I met Mr. Prowle.
Short & had a chat with him — we
looked over a lot of Water-Color sketches
made at Strabat last summer — Mr.
Dr. & all other members of the family were
very agreeable — we left at about 4.30 and
the Abbotts — got a cab on Fulham Road
& home. Tea. Early. Read in the
Nov. No. of "Century" —

## Right Page

### October Saturday 29 1892

*[top margin note: Boughton / Blackman-]*

Very much warmer — uncomfortably warm by
comparison with preceding days — a nasty
m[orning]. wind & rain — a very pleasant after-
noon. I worked. 2½ during the m[orning]. on
the Morgan plate — Nenout & had a short
walk. Dinner at 1. Bunnie went to a
Sat. popular concert — St. James' Hall and
Clara took the boys to the "Zoo". I worried
awhile over my plate & then went out
to my [illegible]. Went to Camden Hill R[oad] &
called upon Geo. H. Boughton — He was prepar-
ing to go out but he [illegible] — me very cordially
& I had a chance to see his lovely house &
his noble studio — I was there about 20 m-
Then started out to find Walter Blackman —
at 10 Cheyneston Gardens — I walked down to
Kensington High St. Hailed a cab — asked the
driver if he knew where Cheyneston Gardens
was — he said yes — I got in & he drove me around
the corner & there I was — B — not at home —
in studio up near Boughton's — Cam[den] Studio,
I returned up the hill — called at his Studio —
found that he had been gone but a few
minutes — there I walked to Queen's Road
took a bus & rode to Poke's & found
Theatre seats for next Tues. eve[ning]. Then
home at 5.30 — The boys just [returned] from the
Zoo — about 6. Blackman & his wife
called on their way somewhere to tea —
to find out how long we were to be in
town — Tea at about 7 — Early reading
awhile.
