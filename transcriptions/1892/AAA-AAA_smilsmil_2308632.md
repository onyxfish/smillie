## Left Page

### July, Monday 4, 1892

*Lugano. Hotel du Parc. Room 84.*

Another delightful day, cram jam full of delight — Bkfst at 8.15 & at 9.15 after bidding good bye to the Giffords our party got away for R.R. station — got through the baggage bother without much trouble & secured for ourselves one section — away on time at 10.15 — for about an hour country as flat as prairie — mulberry — Indian corn — wheat — New at [illegible] and the mts — very exciting — Como — a considerable city — before 12 — bus to boat — 25 minutes — Row up to the cathedral — uninteresting architecturally — bought strawberries, cherries, apricots, plumbs — Boat left 12.10 — Rev. W. Pepper — Consul at Milan — introduced himself to us as a fellow country man — exquisite atmospheric effects — astonished to find the mts so high — so steep, so bold — some of them beautifully wooded well up their sides — Every poor lunch served on deck at 12.30 — As we wound through the River, the lake a succession of grandly varied views — the old buildings extremely picturesque — many of the new ones, hedious — painted stucco glaring colors — Got to Menaggio at about 2 — two landings three minutes apart — 1 one for R.R. to Lake Lugano — before we knew it the boat was off with our trunks — so we went on without them after arranging to have them follow — over narrow gauge R.R. — climbing a very steep grade — men & woman making hay — fine groups of houses — down a steep grade to Porlezza on Lake Lugano — smaller lake — this steeper — wilder — less cultivated — got to Lugano about 4, went at once to Hotel du Parc — were assigned Room 84 on upper (3rd) floor — fronting on the lake — large, airy — out on park back of the house — beautiful shade trees — Magnolias in bloom — almond trees — etc — Table d'hote at 6 — fairly good dinner but it was spoiled by my having to run out at 6.30 to look after our belated baggage which arrived safely before 7, having passed the Custom House some time in the garden — just before 8 a heavy shower fell — continued for half an hour — then cleared — A. & I sat at our window until after 9 — looking out upon the quiet lake — the reflecting lights & deep shadows from the mts — Then to our good bed —

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 5, 1892

A delightful morning — at first perfectly clear, then clouds & sunshine that added new beauty to the fine mts about us. After a late bkfst we went out all of us, for a stroll — went to the Swiss Italian Bank & drew $50 — then we sauntered about until lunch time, after that aeltho, the clouds were gathering thicker we went to the cable R.R. station at the foot of St. Salvatore — This is a very steep mt south of Lugano, up which there is a cable road to the top — the power is about mid-way — water generating electricity — the cable runs the power to the top — over a wheel & to the bottom — a car is at each end — as the one car ascends, the other descends, the two meeting at the mid-station — Sometimes passengers transfer, those in the car just up from the foot taking the car just down from the top — vice versa — & so the journey is continued up & down — the grade is exceedingly steep, a part of it being 17%. The views to the north were exceedingly beautique as we Rose — clouds covering many of the highest peaks, developing [illegible] already a thunderstorm to the north and west showers also to the east of us & spattering of rain falling on us — as the clouds seemed to be closing about us with no promise of a better view we decided to return by the next train — despite of the weather we were pleased with the trip. When I got to the hotel I set to work repacking to send our remaining two heavy trunks to Geneva. & the boys to use the small trunk & the valise — valise — I soon got into a frame of mind trying to pack into the valise twice as much as it would hold and had a fine time — In the early eve't Ralph got something into his eye of which he complained much. A. & the others best of him a boat — on returning & going to bed R. in much trouble with his eye & but at last got to sleep — awoke in the crying — & at intervals until 12 after which was quiet.

The Hotel du Parc is delightfully situated — a charming garden & walks & a wood on the stub hill side immediately back of the house — Rooms pleasant — beds good — Table, fair — and comes a mean average.
