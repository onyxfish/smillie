## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 9, 1892

*Lindau — Bayrischer Hof — Rooms 77–78*

Up at 5.30 — Raining — weather cleared before
9 o'clk — became warm — cool & breezy on lake
Constance — beautiful river — Bkf. at 6.30 —
left the place (a middling good hotel — moderate
charges) at 7 — Took train at 7.20 — very slow to Zur-
ich — 9.44 — then changed to Info. for Romanshorn —
a great crush of passengers — the Germans are not
well behaved upon such occasions — I think they
are not so good tempered as Americans and
are ruder. The ride, altogether, was not interest-
ing — much of the country was pleasant, fertile
farm land. Got to Romanshorn at 12.15 and
at once went on board the steamboat when
we found a dinner table set upon the deck —
we were hungry — & so, apparently, were all
of the rest of the passengers for all sat promptly
down to table — with the sauce of hunger — the
pleasant air & the din from noise, tolution of
the cars I thought the lunch was very good
as steamboat lunches go — The lake cannot
be called interesting from a picturesque point
of view — The ground rises at south east end and
there is a distant view of some snow patched
mountains — we got to Lindau soon after
2 — & promptly went through the merely
formality of Custom House — not a thing was open-
ed — not a question was asked — sent all of
the trunks on to Munich — the boys' jackets were
left in the car — telegraphed back for them, then
settled ourselves in our Rooms — pleasant
with balcony — looking out over the Lake —
I felt tired & took a nap — At 5.30 — with
& hired the boys I went out & took a walk
about the place — quite interesting — At 6.30
had our evef. meal on a verandah —
with view of lake & mts. (together with some
very small plumbs) for dessert — After
that I made sketch of the town at entrance
of port — To bed early.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 10, 1892

*Munich — Hotel Belle Vue — Rooms 126–127*

Clouds hung low in the mtf. Light rain
commenced before noon & by the time we
reached Munich it was pouring heavily —
we got up after a long sleep & had bkf.
at 9 — after which A. & the boys fished from
the steps in front of the hotel & I took
a final saunter looking for photographs.
Settled hotel bill — nearly 30 marks — more
expensive than the Swiss hotels & not so
good — very convenient to boat & R.R.
— two large rooms on upper floor — one
with balcony overlooking lake — beds
fairly good. Before noon went to the
R.R. station & got good seats — it was
12.20 when we started — The ride
was through a country that under a
leaden sky was uninteresting — for
more than an hour before reaching
M. the country was a vast plain —
got to M. at 5.15 & drove at once to
the Belle Vue Hotel & were given
rooms on the upper floor. Had
our evef. meals at 6 — having had
a lunch of sandwiches in the car.
Met Dr. Dunham in corridor — here
with his wife — on the way to Bayreuth.
Feeling very tired I went to bed
at 8.30 — & after two brief wakings
awoke at 7 the next mff.
