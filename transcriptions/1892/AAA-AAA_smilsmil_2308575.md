## Left Page

### March, Tuesday 8, 1892

A dull day. Rain commenced about 11 & in the afternoon it poured feeling a little better in the m'g after 10 I took Ralph & went to his studio where I spent what remained of the m'g preparing my two 20"×24" paintings for the Spring exhib., to morrow being the last day for sending in pictures. When I started home with Ralph I found it was raining so we rode up. Annie had gone to Bridgeport to attend the funeral of Miss Juliet Ireland, a cousin of her mother's — 84 yrs. of age. She ret'd before 6 bringing Nellie Adams with her. I stayed at home growing more out of sorts as the day advanced until eve'g where I had considerable fever — & felt wretched enough. I wrote to Yale. Kate arr'd from O.K. about 1 o'clk. Charl came in the eve'g Annie went to her Choral Club and soon after 9 I went to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Wednesday 9, 1892

Bright & comparatively mild day with some cloudy passages. Took my [illegible] in bed — then up & dressed & lay down in bed until Yale came. He stayed an hour with me but if he knows what is the matter with me he doesn't tell. About noon Ralph & I went down to the druggists & got some medicine. Annie went to a small lunch at Mr. Villard's after lunch I went to 2nd Nat. Bk. & made a deposit for Annie — then to studio — there for one hour & a half then home — on bed until dinner — eve'g. Charl came in for a few minutes. I read a little — some of Kipling's short stories — & got to bed before 10.
