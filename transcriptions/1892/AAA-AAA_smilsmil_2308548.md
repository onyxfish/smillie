## Left Page

### January, Thursday 14, 1892

Still nasty weather - very mild and
foggy with some rain during the
middle of the day. On my way to the
studio stopped at Chas. Ratts - not
dressed - he came to studio later
& we planned to help the subject of
the Etching Club that is in a bad way
at this stage. Then Dr. Gester came
in with plate & proof of a large portrait.
He spent the rest of the time watching
me at my work. I took two proofs
on tinted [illegible]. [illegible] of Key plate —
Then home to lunch - back at 2.30 &
found Farrer & Mielatz waiting for me.
They stayed for some time discussing the
coming Etching Club subject & making plans
to avoid the threatened fiasco — Then I
got to work & took two more Key proofs.
The result is very depressing — it falls
so far short of what I had hoped that it
is a failure - it is over bitten & over worked.
Mr. Blackburn came & brought a check
for $30 - payment for lessons. It was
6 o'clk before I got home. Dinner — soon
after 7 went to Dr. Yales with proof of
Etching Club Cat. & we consulted over
it until 10 o'clk. Then home & to bed
about 11. Did not go to Union
league where my painting belongs in
to Charl. is on exhibit.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 15, 1892

Heavy snow until noon — colder & clearing
at night. Got to studio at 10 o'clk. &
at work at once proving dry-point 6"×12"
Hollyhocks. Dr. Yale came in an hour
later with three small plates from which
I took some [illegible] for him. It was after
10 o'clk when I went to 2d Nat. Bank,
at 45 when I got home to lunch —
To studio at 2.45 & resumed proving,
took [illegible] from 5"×7" dry-point. "Sweet
Peas". Washed up soon after 4 and then
went to DeVinne's. & saw W. Hopkins &
placed in his hands revised proof
& a dummy of the Etching Club Pub[lication] —
Then to Keppel's & selected some [illegible]
for the Etching Club subject — Then to studio
& cut down paper for tomorrow's work
& home to dinner a little before 7 —
immediately after, Jurine & Jim went
to Edw. Cooke's to a children's private
theatrical affair & I remained at home
& wrote to Herrick, Kimmel & Voigt and
to Anthony. Mailed notes at 9. It was
9.30 when A. ret'd with Jim. To bed
at 10.30 & from 11 to 12 had a bad & a
monkey time with my stomach.
