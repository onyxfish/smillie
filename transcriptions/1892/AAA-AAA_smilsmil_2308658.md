## Left Page

### August, Thursday 25, 1892

Bright, pleasant m'f — cool breeze and signs of coming rain — overcast P.M.
& rain about 7 o'clk — that continued
steadily — Before 10. A.M. Annie, the boys
& I started for a walk, going over the
same ground that the boys & I took yes-
terday afternoon & splendidly & consider-
ably. We got back to hotel at 12.15 —
Dinner soon after 1 — wrote part of a
letter to Geo. H.S — A. & Jim went to the
Golden Lion for a bath — Ralph & I
went around to the Johannesthal for a
walk & by a side path climbed to
Salchkaukuhe — back before 6.30 and
had our supper at 7 — R'y, wrote
for some time by candle light, letter
home.

## Right Page

### August, Friday 26, 1892

A day of successive heavy showers with some
glints of sunshine between — I find that m'd.
J. Pierpont Morgan, three daughters, a maid &
a courier are at this cheap boarding house —
I spent the m'f indoors writing a letter to
Geo. In the afternoon, between showers
(hardly, as we had to wait in a store for
one to pass over) A & the walked to the
R.R. station & made enquiries concern-
ing trains to Cologne — we decided to start
tomorrow m'f at 9.21 — back, stopping at the
P. O — as photographers & bakers & had our
[illegible] meal at 7 — Eve'g. did some pack-
ing & to bed at 10 —

The Elizabethenruhe is conveniently and
pleasantly situated — with good sized hall-
ways & rooms & might be a delightful place
for a short summer stay — but [illegible] dining, if
not dirty — is imperfectly ventilated — [illegible] &
exasperatingly slow service, & to my taste, a
poor table (greasy, thick gravies — mussy, sour
salads, etc etc.) The beds are fair but
the W.C.s warm of the most primitive and
disagreeable character. Taken al-
together it has been a relief to us to get
away.
