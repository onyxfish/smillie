## Left Page

### November, Friday 25, 1892

Another dark day but dry until the evef, when there was drizzle enough to wet the sidewalks. Put in another four hours of Morgan plate, working up the drapery. I had a couple of walks in the mf & at 4.30 went by U.R.R. to Gower's. & then to Euston Square station to get some information about Sunday trains to Bushy. From there I walked home, a long, dirty, & (for a good part of the way) a dark walk. Home at 6 — tea. Evef A. Read aloud the Libretto "Haddon Hall" by Sydney [illegible]

Morgan — 4 h.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 26, 1892

Very dull & disagreeable — very heavy rain about 3 o'clk. P.M. & so dark that I could no longer see to work. I took the boys out for a walk in the mf. & A — took them to So. Ken. museum in the afternoon. As it was too wet to go out & too dark to work after 3.30 I could do nothing else than take a nap — which I did. At 6 o'clk the weather cleared & the moon shone out. Then I determined to walk down to the Arts Club & dine there. I sat at table with W. Archer & was introduced to Mr. Schmitz Wilson, a writer, with whom I had a pleasant little chat & afterwards read a paper of his in one of the magazines on Königsmark, an entertaining historical sketch of a woman of fortune who was for a short while an influence in the court of Saxony nearly 200 years ago. I then had a chat with Henry Blackburn and at 9.30 I walked home, getting in at 10 & finding letters from Kate, Geo. & Charl.

Morgan — 3 h.
