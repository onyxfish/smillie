## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 26, 1892

Frost. heavy white fog — sun like a red ball.
The air exactly like that of an ice house —
The weather cleared about 1 & remained
pleasant during the rest of the day —
Very soon after b[rea]k[fas]t, I went to Joueding's &
got a couple of p[late]s. b[rea]k[fas]t of my [illegible] — The
result very depressing — I grows to be more
evident as I go on that my system of laying
in the lines was, from the beginning, unsuited to
the whole thing very much over bitten for the
style of printing that I get here — I do not know
what I can do to sufficiently reduce the
background & the coat without destroying
the original line — I walked from Joueding's
home & spent some time coming over my
p[late]s before dinner — after that I commenced
a letter to Kate but about 4 o'clk, feeling
very heavy I gave way to it & took a nap.
Evening I finished a long letter to Kate.

morgan. 3 h.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 27, 1892

A beastly day. Very dark & very wet. Got
up with a heavy head — unwatch for the
day. After b[rea]k[fas]t, by underground R.R., went to
the city & at J. S. Morgan's got $100 — then by
bus to the British Museum where, until
4 o'clk. I looked over a portfolio, in the
print room, of Dèspre's Flaucing's etchings
& took notes — then home to dinner —
Rain pouring down — never saw nastier,
muddier, wetter streets anywhere. In
N.Y. generally the rain is heavy enough
to wash the flagstones & pavements — here
they made the vilest compound that
spattered over & clung to everything —
Rec'd letters from Mary & George — For
a time during the afternoon I pored
over the last p[age]s of Morgan head — Then I
gave up & went to bed with my headache.
Up at 6 — tea — early. Annie read
aloud from the Nov. No. of The Century.
