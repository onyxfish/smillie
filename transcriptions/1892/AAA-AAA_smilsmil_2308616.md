## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 31, 1892

*Scranton.*

Heavy fog until after 9 — then cleared very hot. I got up before 7 & worked until near 9 upon wire netting for vines at east end of piazza — then bkfst — after that packing, etc — then worked with Herrick at vine netting until 12 — The K's are enthusiastic in their admiration of the house. At 12.30 we bid them good bye & walked to the station, stopping at the Lathrop's & Payne's to bid them good bye. Got away on the 12.55 train & at Alvord took the 1.30 milk train for Scranton, getting there at 3.30. Parke met us at the station & we rode at once to his house. The rest of the afternoon was spent upon his piazza — Mrs. Stung is & their Mrs. Holley came — at 6.30 we all sat down to dinner & had a very good time — after dinner we returned to piazza & sat there until 10 o'clk. Dr. Cha. Parke & his wife calling — After giving my lame foot needed attention I got to bed after 11 — A very close night — had the bed not been a very comfortable one the night would have been tedious.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 1, 1892

*Returned to N.Y.*

Another exceedingly hot day. Bkfst after 8 — Are the M.F. I loafed on the cool porch of Parke's house, resting & even, at times, napping. At 12 a pleasant lunch was served and at about 12.20 we left the house & took the 12.55 train — Parke & his son Grere went with us as far as Mamaroneck on their way to Phila. We got to N.Y. at 5.20 after an awfully hot and uncomfortable ride — Got up to the house at 6 — Mary Moony & her husband have taken quarters in the house — After a general scrub we had a comfortable little dinner at 6.30 — Geo. & Juiney Cook came in for a little while in the evef & we got to bed at 9.30
