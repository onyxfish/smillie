## Left Page

### November, Saturday 5, 1892

*Fine Arts Club.*

Dark & disagreeable altho' not actually wet. I got to work at my north window on the Morgan head & began my new scratching of dry-point all over it to lower the tone and worked very closely. I had a walk — then dinner — & after that I worked as long as I could see. It was not until 4 o'clk. — Susie went to a "Pop" concert — Sarabata. I took a nap after my work as it had then begun to rain. — Susy I dressed & after 8, in the rain, I walked down to the Arts Club in Hanover Sq. — getting there a little before 9 — very few there — no one that I knew — before 10 Mr. Fagan came in & we chatted together until nearly 11. The rooms are interesting — the building was put up nearly 90 [illegible] ago by Geo. IV for one of his mistresses — there are some fine old Italian mantle pieces — & a large ceiling — Guido's aurora painted by Angelica Kauffmann — Sketches by Chas. Keene & du Maurier. It was interesting to see — to be in the haunts of men of such varying characters & such historical interest. — Walked home — part of the way in the rain — part by a sickly, wet moonlight —
Mr. Beeson & Miss Hess called at 1 o'clk.

Morgan 4.30 h.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 6, 1892

Nasty, wet m[illegible] — clear, pleasant afternoon. quite mild. — After Help. I wrote notes to Abbey, Herkomer, Blackman & Mielatz — to the last, asking him to bring before the N.Y. Etching Club a proposition to make a collection of American Painter etchings for the British Museum Print Room.

About 11 I went out & walked around the Kensington Gardens. It was 2 o'clk when we had our dinner & after 3 when we all started for a walk. Intending to go to Chelsea Embankment, by underground R.R. we went to Sloane Sq. — there I stupidly turned up Sloane S. instead of down & did not discover my mistake until we got to Knights bridge. Then we took 'bus back to Chelsea Bridge — walked along the embankment, past the Hospitals — the new Albert Bridge — Cheyne walk — to the Battersea Bridge — in the twilight with the pleasant evening sky ahead of us it was an agreeable as well as an interesting walk — then we turned up to Kings Road — took 'bus to Sloan Sq & thence by R.R. back home, getting in at 6.

Evening I wrote awhile upon a letter to Mary.
