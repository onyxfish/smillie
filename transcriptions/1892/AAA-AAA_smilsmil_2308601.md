## Left Page

### May, Sunday 1, 1892

A pleasant day in spite of a strong south-easterly wind — Annie's
48th Birthday — Bkfst. at 9 — read newspapers & wrote a long letter to Mr. Kent
of Buffalo concerning Montrose House,
Herrick - Stone - etc — At about 1 o'clk
Annie, Ralph & I walked up Mad -
Ave. to 56th S. — then home in time for
dinner at 2 o'clk — Afternoon Annie
went up to Mr. Villard's while I
stayed at home to receive callers.
Charl — Alice Ireland — John Valentina - etc — At 5 Ralph & I
went out & amused ourselves for
an hour on the bridge north of the
Grand Central station — Tea
at 7 — Eve'g I made out list of
garden seeds for Montrose — wrote
to Herrick & Stone & did some reading.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 2, 1892

A very hot day with thunder shower
between 5 & 6 o'clk. P. M. Ther. got up
to 85°. I got to studio about 10 & spent
the m'g among my etchings & going
over old letters in my writing desk.
Ralph was at the studio with
me. Before 1 we went to 2°. Nat.
Bk & then home to lunch — It
was after 3 before I got to studio.
I made a parcel to send to Blaney,
an etcher in Boston — containing pf.
of "Aqua-tint" & an old "damin" — Then
wrote a note to him — getting very
dark from coming storm I went
home before 5 — At 6.30 Annie &
I went down to 17th S. & had a very
quiet, pleasant dinner with the
Ireland girls — Home soon after
10 — found Charl waiting for
us with a lot of Baedaker Guide
book for Switzerland.
To bed abt 11.
