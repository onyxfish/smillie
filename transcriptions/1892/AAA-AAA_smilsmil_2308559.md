## Left Page

### February Friday 5 1892

Pleasant day, with some threatening
streaks. Got up feeling weak &
uncomfortable — Took Ralph with me
after help — & after mf rounds at butcher
& grocers we rode down to 11 & B. The
printers in Canal S. where we are —
at 11 & I had 4 india [illegible] of Rey plate
Falcen, hoping that they are the last —
then to studio — left [illegible] & plate. & then
home at 1.30 — Annie in Brooklyn
so the boys & I had lunch alone —
feeling out of sorts I lay down & slept
until after 4 — then walked down to
studio & back — dinner after 6. S[illegible]
Annie & I went to Mr. [illegible] Morse's at 8.30
to what Mrs. Morse termed a "Crony Party"
managed entirely upon lines of her
own. Paper hats & various designs were
prepared for the guests — Millie was a
huge blue & yellow continental cocked
hat that I afterwards exchanged with &
exchanged for a small red ones with Mr.
Roecker — W. D. Howells — Stockton — Mrs.
M. M. Dolce & other lights — literary and
artistic — were there — There was a long
series of versified introductions [illegible] ami
mostly by Miss Ruth Thomas — & there then
were charades in which Stockton,
A. B. Frost, Mrs. Webb & others took part —
Frost was very funny as a german
author & some good hits were made —
Home at 12 —

## Right Page

### February Saturday 6 1892

A beautiful clear, cold day — Got
to studio at 10 — then went to Bank —
back at 10.30 — [illegible] called
to see me about etching some family
portraits — on looking at [illegible] of Rey plate
taken yesterday I am much disappointed
to find that they have been taken
with an ink so weak that they look very
wavy, hazy & poor — I wasted some time
tracking down three of them that had
kept damp under a rubber blanket —
then I burnished awhile on the plates —
also on "Bronsdale dam" — Home to lunch.
[illegible], felt so heavy headed that I lay
down & slept soundly until 4.30 when A.
waked me — Then up — dressed & went — with
to afternoon tea at Rev. Mr. Williams' — 117 E.
35 S. Had a very enjoyable time with
[illegible] — then home to dinner — Evening
about 9 o'clk — went to Century — monthly meeting
had a very pleasant time — Eastman Johnson
had a lifesize portrait of himself in costume
that he wore [illegible] Night — an old Dutch
laugher — a very strong bit of work — the head
with its big hat would make such a stunning etching that I begged his permission to try it which he gave provisionally —
Home soon after 12 & to bed.
