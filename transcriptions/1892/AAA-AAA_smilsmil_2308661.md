## Left Page

### August Wednesday 31 1892

Bright, windy day, uncomfortably chilly —
as sharp shower about 6 Am — I got up at
7 & we had bkfs — shortly after 8 — I had a
run around the outside of the cathedral before
that — after bkfs — Emma & I went to the London
& county Bank & to my dismay we found that
we could not get money on our letters of Cr —
had an interview with Mr. Ward, Prof. of our Hood
& was told that my only way was to go to London
tomorrow mf & return in the afternoon — which
I made up my mind to do — meanwhile we all went
the cathedral — mf service 10 o'ck — 1/4 of an hour —
too long in spite of pleasant music — singing by
choir of men & boys — after service we were shown
all over the building which we found to be extreme
ly interesting — when we got out of that there was
time for us to return to hotel — we had our
dinner soon after & then after dinner, I had
a rest & about 4 o'ck. we all started out again
& went more carefully over the cathedral grounds
& ruins, Emma & everyone went on to the little
old & extremely interesting church of St. Martins
while I returned to the hotel with Ralph —
I soon followed & rejoined the party at the
church — The clerk gave us a very interesting
history of what I saw & then I wandered
about outside enjoying everything — back
to hotel at 6.30 & all had our tea before I
fraternized with an unknown American &
his daughter, just in from Ostend — I went
out & walked about the streets & around the
cathedral in the dim light of a quarter
moon — To bed at 10.

Mr. Ward is a good fellow but I don't think
much of his inn — It is not a model of either
cleanliness or comfort — I did not at all
like what I got to eat & everything about the
house was more or less faded, dingy & worn —
As yet I do not understand English
hotel ways as well as I do the American
ways — Maybe, when I learn more, I may
be pleased more to my liking —

## Right Page

### September Thursday 1 1892

*London — Holborn Viaduct Hotel. Room 103.*

Very bright mf. but daw, drizzling afternoon —
up about 7 & had a walk about the & cathedral
before bkfs — very cool & very breezy — Immediately
after bkfs — had an interview with Mr. Ward —
He was willing to cash a draft for £10 — but not
one for £50 — that being the case I promptly
determined to go at once to London when I
could get what I wanted — Mr. Ward was very
obliging & said he would not only trust me for
the two bills — Emma's & ours — but he would loan
us one £ to help us through to London — we got
away on the 10 o'ck train — 25 m. late — the trip
providing country through which we passed
looking very pleasant — we got to Holborne
station about 12.30 & went at once into the
Viaduct Hotel, entered from the platform —
at 1 o'ck. we had our lunch & after that —
E, M & I got up on the top of a bus & rode
to the Bank & from there to Morgan & Co's 22
old Broad where we got money & the letter
then I went to a Sub-Post, got a money order
for £6.10 — to Mr. Ward — wrote him a note &
posted it — Before I started back, E, & M.
having started off on a hunt for lodgings —
nasty drizzle made things unpleasant and
I stopped at S. Paul's & took a look at the
inside — It was 4 when I got to hotel —
E, M — came in to say that they had found
lodgings — Men A. the boys & I went out
to shop for clothes for the boys — got coats
caps & underclothes — back at 6.30 — had
tea — Evening looking over newspapers
& time tables planning a trip to the
Isle of Wight by way of Winchester and
back by Brighton — To bed at 10.
