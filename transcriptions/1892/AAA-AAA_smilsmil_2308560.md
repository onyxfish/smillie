## Left Page

### February Sunday 7, 1892

An exceedingly disagreeable day.
Dull, leaden grey until noon, then
a slight sleety fall, enough to make
wet walks & a nasty, raw atmosphere.
B'kfst at 9.30 — Read newspapers until
after 11 — went to Century to get a
daylight look at the Eastman Johnson
I would like to make a dry-point
reduction from it, out at about
1 o'clk & as sleet was beginning to fall
I went home — worked awhile on
plans for glass for front door —
At 2 had dinner — Annie took
Ralph down to the Ireland's in 17th St.
& I had a long afternoon nap — the
third day of such indulgence —
Tea at 6.30 — Evg wrote to K &
V. & to Herrick — also, some reading
— Charl came in for awhile —
To bed after 11.

## Right Page

### February Monday 8, 1892

Beautiful day — to studio at 10 — then
in to Tiffany's & saw Louis & we considered
the rough plans that I submitted for
the front door glass — then back to studio
& spent some time laying out new plans
on Louis' suggestions — my head troubled
me very much making it hard for me
to work — Home at 1 & back at 2.30. Found
W. B. Wood waiting for me — he wanted
to see my dry-points & after looking
over my proofs he selected three —
then I fussed among my proofs,
getting some in press & home at 5 —
quite used up & much depressed.
Dinner at 6 — Mrs Annie & I
went to the Ortgies Gallery, to take
a last look at Georges paintings —
found a lot of people there many
of whom we knew. Geo & Nellie and
Charl — & Geo. & Huey Cook & others.
Home at 10 & soon to bed.
