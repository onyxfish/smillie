## Left Page

### December Saturday 17 1892

A dull day, but otherwise not bad.
I spent the m'f., experimenting
upon one of Robertson's small celluloid
plates, making a dry-point upon
fan old tower at Lugano.
At 12.30 I went out to S. Mary's Hospital,
not far from our house, where the
Prince of Wales was to perform some
function — many police along the
route — a considerable crowd lined
the street a few blocks away, while
near the Hospital the crowd was quite
dense — mostly people of poorer class.
A mounted policeman with flying
cloak preceded the carriage, not as
an escort but as an avant courier — two
drab liveried men, with broad coarse
bands on left arm, on the box — no footman
behind. I caught a glimpse of him
in the closed carriage — two others in
the carriage with him — very unsatisfactory
of course — Dinner at 2 — wrote & mailed
a note to Mr. J.P. Morgan explaining and
apologising for the heaviness of proof etchings
of his father. Later became interested
in reading a story of R. L. Stevenson's —
Evg. I got at Etching Club — (after
wrote some additions to it & a note
to Alex. Schilling.

## Right Page

### December Sunday 18 1892

A very pleasant day, from a London point
of view — no sun of course. It was about
1.30 when I went out to Hyde Park with
the boys for a walk — we got in at about
[illegible] had dinner at 2. At 3 I went to
G. H. Boughton's to bid him good bye —
was first shown into the Drawing Room
where a number of ladies were with Mr.
Boughton & then up to the studio where
B. had half a dozen gentlemen with
him. He rec'd me very cordially and
expressed a desire to see a lot of [illegible]
had & made an appointment for me to
call on Tues. p.m. next. Then I walked
home & took a nap before tea. Evg.
I wrote a note to pet. Durand embodying
the incident of my father's first visit
to N.Y. when he did a bit of etching for
his father — the opening, almost, of a career.
