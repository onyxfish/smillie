## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 16, 1892

A nasty, nasty day — raining steadily
from morn till dreary eve. I worked
[illegible] on Morgan plate — went
for a short walk in the rain before
[illegible] & then had dinner alone as
Annie & the boys had gone to Hampstead to lunch at the Satchell's. I
worked again after dinner for a little
while & wrote a letter to Kate giving
a description of the Lord Mayor's Show
which I mailed in time to catch
the Queenstown mail — Then I
managed in a little nap. A — &
the boys ret'd before 6. Ev'g
I went to Pennell's upon his invitation hoping to meet M. Felix Buhot
& M. Chien [?] the French artistic
poster man. But Chien had ret'd to
Paris this m'g. & Buhot had been there
before dinner to excuse himself. I
had a very pleasant ev'g with Mr. & Mrs.
P. There was a Mr. Fisher there — Home
about 11.

morgan 3 h.
