## Left Page

### May, Thursday 19, 1892

*To Plainfield —*

Disagreeable day — overcast
mrg. — light showers, commencing
about noon. Cold & raw —
heavy rain & high wind at
night — Annie, Ralph & I left
the house at 10.20 & by 6th Ave. El.
R.R. went to Liberty St — Got to Plain-
field at about 12.30 — Belle Tweedy
waiting for us at the station — we
rode at once to the Tweedy house
where the other members of the family
greeted us warmly — Lunch at
about 1.30 — a young man — (they
called him cousin) — Benedict
lunched with us — In the afternoon
Annie & Florence T. went to Edith
Wright McCarthy's — Belle & I walked
about looking at new buildings
enjoying ourselves in spite of the
rain — Then back to the house
where a vehicle was waiting &
we drove to Station & took 5.16
train in to N.Y. getting home
at 7.15 — a late dinner and
to bed at 9.30 —

## Right Page

### May, Friday 20, 1892

Disagreeable day — cold, cloudy
& raw — My foot hurt me so much
that I went to Dr. Gerster's & saw Dr.
Lilienthal. He put a pack (wet) upon
the offending little toe & made an
appointment for 5 o'clk p.m. I
then went to studio & worked
about packing until near 1 —
Men home — Fanny K. there to
lunch — At 2 a boy came to say
that Charl was sick & wished to
see me — I went to his rooms &
found him suffering from a violent
indigestion — from which he was re-
covering — Then to studio — At 5 I
went to 33 E. 31st St — & Dr. L. [illegible]
up on my little toe — the soft corn
appeared to be entirely removed.
Dinner at 6.30 — At 7.30 went again
to Charles for a few minutes — then
to Lotus Club — First meeting of the
N.Y. advisory com. of art for the Col-
umbian Expo. of Chicago. All sections
met & formally organized — electing
S. J. A. Ward chairman & Bolton Jones
Sec'y. Richd. Hunt was energetic and
amusing. Stanford White & St. Gaudens
took prominent parts — There was
no specific business before the meeting
but there was a good deal of Talk —
I got tired at 10.30 & excused my-
self — Walked up home —
