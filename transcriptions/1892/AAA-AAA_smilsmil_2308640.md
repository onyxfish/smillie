## Left Page

### July, Wednesday 20, 1892

Heavy rain
Still cold & dismal. Up at 7 — went out & had a walk & on my return, at about 8.30 had bkfst. About 11 I tried to do a little writing on my "Etching" paper but feeling heavy I lay down & had a little naps before lunch. After that I discovered that behind a heavy piece of furniture in our largest bed room there was a fire place, at once I arranged things & had a fire made to our great joy — wood 1½ f. per basket. Ralph was much troubled by diarreah — we tried to get a local physician but he had gone to attend the injured at St. Gervais. I appealed to a Cleveland O. man in the hotel, whom I had heard called Dr. & got some advice & 6 doses of laudanum. I worked on "Etching" for a time & later in the afternoon walked up to the Cascade de with a pleasant little old gentleman whom I took to be a German pastor — we talked of America, of education, Socialism & other trifles. Rain most of the way — back before 6. Evtg. Salon very crowded — many guests weather bound — I left early & got to bed about 9.

The Cascade de B was a very small affair, surroundings considered.

## Right Page

### July, Thursday 21, 1892

Still disagreeable weather — but little rain — clouds lifting — Ralph evidently much better. Up at 7 — started a fire & then went out for a walk. Bkfst. at 8.30 — after 10 got to work on "Etching" & finished before noon. Then read it — went to Annie to test its efficacy upon a layman. After lunch, Annie, Marjorie, Jim & I walked up to the Cascade de Dard. Emma was good enough to stay home with Ralph. The walk was delightful but the Cascade did it amounts to much. Got there at 4 — back at 4.40.

At dinner very few at table — most of the guests had taken wing. I sat at head of table — the only man — nine women bore me company. After dinner, M. Conttet, proprietor and old guide, interested & amused us by telling of some of his experiences & adventures upon Mt. Blanc. After that, a couple of young California girls entertained us awhile. It was 8 o'clk when I went to bed.

On our way to the Cascade du Dard we stopped in at the gallery of paintings by M. Loppi. Some very large ones — all of local scenery — mostly snow & glacier — and all pretty bad.
