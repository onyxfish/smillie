## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 28, 1892

*on the Mediterranean Sea - miles made 333*

Another glorious day - one of varying atmos-
pheres presenting the most delicious hues
& colors - All the mts. running along the
Spanish coast, fascinated by the picture-
forms of the mountains, so bare & treeless,
so harmonious & delicate in color - It
was about noon that we crossed the
Gulf of Valencia & came to the Balearic
Islands along which we coasted - they
on the east side of us - all the afternoon -
Some of the Rock masses were very fine -
The moods of the day changed several
times - about the middle of the day a fresh
breeze sprang up & rippled the otherwise
smooth water giving us the blue Mediter-
anean in perfection - afternoon, such time
as I could spare looking about me - I
spent writing a letter to Charl. Dinner
came at a very awkward time, spoil-
ing our sunset - the every was wonder-
fully beautiful - smooth water reflecting
the western sky, clouds & color - long confabs
with various persons - young Callesen & father
- Mr Howells & others - very mild and very
damp. To state room at about 10.30

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 29, 1892

*miles made 338 - 156 m. to Genoa -*

Another glorious day - the mt. very warm
but about 10 a breeze, light & cool, sprang up
& rippled the water into the lovely blue we
love so much - all the mts. we were out
of sight of land and I took the opportunity
to finish my home letter and do a little
on my etching paper - In the early after-
noon the French coast came dimly into
sight but after 3 - there was as succession
of grand views of the snow-topped French
Alps - there were some clouds cling-
ing to the summits but they only added
to the beauty of the mts. It was after 4
when we passed the French line &
came abreast of Italy and near enough
to see Nice, Monaco, Mentone etc. - we
had an estate time watching the snow capped
Maritime Alps & the wonderful panorama of
mts. until it was too dark to see - Dinner got beat
attention - lovely moonlight - very mild - every-
great excitement in waiting for a sight of the
fresh light of the Genoa Light House - It was 10.30
when we got outside of the mole & signalled for a
pilot - nearly an hour before he came aboard
then slowly we made our way through the
tortuous channel into the inner harbor - It was
hours after midnight before I went below and
then packed until near 1 - infernally hot,
just as I was falling asleep - about 2 - Ralph
had a brief vomiting spell - at last an un-
restful sleep until 4.30 - Up at 6 -
