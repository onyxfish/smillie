## Left Page

### September, Friday 2, 1892

*Winchester, "The Black Swan" — Rooms 14–20*

A nasty, cold, wet m'f. — clear breezy afternoon
— more rain at night. B'kf'st — at 9 — After getting
everything ready for a start I spent the rest of
the time until noon writing a short note to
Charl. & a letter to Kate. Then I had my hair
cut. Annie & the boys took lunch — my [illegible]
still stuck in my crops so that I could eat
nothing. — At a quarter before 2 we left the
hotel for Waterloo Station — & thence went by
the 2.25 train to Winchester, getting there
at 4. — Rode at once to the Black Swan and
got our rooms — & then out & went to the
Cathedral. — An entertaining & agreeable
old fellow took us about & recited much
history to us — going away back to times
before the Christian Era with as little
concern as though he were discussing the
weather of yesterday. The Cathedral is a
wonderfully interesting place, but its old
walls so much out of the perpendicular
& leaning in so many different directions
gave me an uncomfortable feeling that
the whole thing might at any moment
tumble about my ears. — After going thro'
the Cathedral very deliberately, we went
around the outside & back to hotel at 6.30.
I had a solid meal of chops & potatoes,
soon after 7 — After that I went out for a
little walk but was driven in by the
wet. The gas was so poor that I could
not see to read or write so I went to bed.

I cannot say much for the Black Swan —
like most of the English provincial hotels
of which I know anything it is dingy and
comfortless. — I cannot make a more
specific complaint.

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 3, 1892

*Royal Marine Hotel, Ventnor, Isle of Wight — Rooms 64–70*

A day of bright sunshine & heavy showers. Cloudless
in the early m'f — showery until after 2 — then a
superb evening & moonlight night. — Before B'kf'st I
took a short walk — after B'kf'st we all again went
to the Cathedral & took a last look outside & in —
then a hurried walk to the school & chores — then
back to hotel & from there to L.R. Station & took
the 10.15 train — changed at Bishop's Stoke & fell behind
time — some very heavy showers while in station
Train — to Stokes Bay — crossed to Ryde in a
small steam boat — brilliant sunshine — showers
behind & ahead of us — charming sail of less than
half an hour — on getting to Ryde found that we
had just missed the train that would have got
us into Ventnor at 12.49 — had to wait on the
pier until the 1.10 train — that after all sorts
of detentions got us to V. about 2.30 — half an hour
late. — Drove at once to the Royal Marine Hotel
— having written ahead for rooms we were at
once shown to them — we like the situation of
the house very much & its arrangement is
picturesque & promising of comfort. — By 3 o'c'k
we had a good meal of chops, potatoes etc
for which A. bought stores for the boys &
we went to the beach where they dug in the
sand to their great joy until 6 o'c'k. I A. &
I wandering about on the [illegible] promenade & on
the cliffs about this end. — Before 7 we had
a light supper & the boys were put to
bed. — About 9 I went out & had a brisk
walk — the moonlight was perfect — the
sea over the sea, delicious — and the air
was as crisp as Oct. — After 3/4 of an hour I
came in to A — who was in the Drawing Room
— we went out together & had 20 min. —
of walk — then in & to bed soon after 10.
