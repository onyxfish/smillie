## Left Page

### December, Thursday 15, 1892

A nasty, wet m'f, clearing in the after
noon. I got two p'fs of the Morgan etch-
ing fixed up — one to be sent to Mr. J. P. M— on
strong boards, then I went out, got a cab
& with the p'f — drove down to the old B— d
S'. office of J. S. Morgan & Co — there I saw Mr.
Morgan & left the p'f. with him. He
called in some of the old clerks, but none
& there also some of the portrait — Mr. Godar
does not like it — says it does not represent
this man — the objection is not to the etching —
'tis to the original from which there is a
photo- in the office — Then, by bus, went to
the B. Museum — out to lunch before 2 &
then back & looking over more old prints
until 4 — Then to Robertson's 99 Longacre
& looked over their etching materials & [illegible]
a new kind of plate — a sort of celluloid- for
dry-point working — can be printed from like
copper — I bought a few for experiment.
Then to Newman's — Soho S'q — It was near
6 when I got home. I fooled away the
even'g.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 16, 1892

Disagreeable — dull & damp, but
not actually raining. I took proof
of Morgan Etching to the Museum to-day
& presented it to the Print D'ept. Spent
some time over a vol. of Am. B'k Note Co's
work, writing the names of draughtsmen &
engravers under the vignettes. Looked
over W. J. Linton's "Masters of Wood Eng't."
I went out to lunch at 2 & on returning
spent some time in the room where the
drawings are exhibited — at 3 ½ left &
went to Hoskin's store — Oxford S'. & there met
[illegible] — we did some shopping, without
success, for a [illegible] price & then we
walked on down Regent S'. to the Strand &
Buckingham S'. & called upon the Fennells —
finding them both at home. We had a
very chatty pleasant time until after 5, 30
then we left & rode to Marble arch by
bus — thence home on foot — Tea — Even'g
& read something of Robt. L. Stevenson's.
