## Left Page

### May Saturday 21, 1892

A nasty, wet, cold day — got to
studio soon after 10. Pretty badly
crippled in my right foot. Spent
the m't, digging in the dirt of
years & packing two cases. Home
at 1 — to studio again at 3 —
Got the engineer to come up
to my room to take my printing
press to pieces — He fussed for
an hour or more over the key
that holds the long arms & at last
gave it up for the day. I worked
on until near 6 — then home —
Dinner at 6.30 — After that I
went to Charl's room to see
how he was getting on — Found
him all right & entertaining
Ed. Parker. I got home before
9 — I gave my right foot careful
treatment as directed by Dr. J. —
Scalded it in ammoniated water
— scrubbed it & smeared it &
then strapped it with adhesive
plaster & at 10.30 went to bed
with it hurting like fury.

## Right Page

### May Sunday 22, 1892

A grey morning — rain commenced
about 3 p.m. & fell steadily until
after we went to bed — BkfS't. at 9.30
It was nearly when I went to the
studio where I continued packing
until near 2 — then home to dinner.
After that Charl came home for
a while — then I wrote to W. Kent
& to Pate. Ev't. at 7 o'clk. Annie
& I went to Walter's where
the youngsters had a supper &
amused themselves until about
9.30 — The older members of the
party were not forgotten & had a
good time eating & drinking.
Home at 10 — then spent a long time
soaking right foot & working on soft
corn — to bed at 11.30.
