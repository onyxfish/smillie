## Left Page

### December Monday 19 1892

As pleasant as possible without sunshine
in the w'f. walked out to Goulding's with
my celluloid dry-point plate — As he was
not at work up on his Press he very good
naturedly told me that I might amuse
my self by standing in his place & pulling
my own proofs — which I did much to my
enjoyment — He directed a man to grind
ink for me & to pull the press — I was very
much interested in the preparation of the
ink & have made an item of it in my note
book — While I got some results from the
celluloid that were good I am fortified
my belief that it is not a method or idea
worth pursuing further. I got my vellum
print & walked back — dinner ab't 1.30 —
it was 3.30 when I walked down Oxford S.
to Mudie's — then down Shafts'bury Ave. to
the Palace Theatre — got tickets for 1s? every
then through Charing & Road to Oxford S.
shopping for shoes — went into a number
of places & tried on many shoes without
finding anything that suited. Home at
6.30 — tea — Ev'g — newspaper & some
writing.

## Right Page

### December Tuesday 20 1892

Mild, dry & dull — Spent the m'f at home
writing letters — to p'd — Durand. Alex. Schilling,
w'm. Chase & Chas. Platt. Before I went
to Barber's & had my hair cut — then walked
about the streets until 1.30 — then in to
dinner — which was not served until late.
At 3 — went to Geo. H. Boughton's by ap-
pointment, taking a p'f of the Morgan head
as he had asked to see it — & then, hoping to
make a better impression by my smaller work,
I took a number of other p'fs. He looked
them over in an agreeable manner and
said complimentary things very much
as tho' he meant them. After that he
insisted that we should sit down & have
a chat together so we pulled our chairs
up to the fire and sat there until near
5 o'clk. talking about men & things — He
is a very agreeable fellow & æsthetic — we
got no time. after I left him I thought
of many more things that I would have
liked to talk to him about — He said
much the same thing before I left & asked
me to come again that he might show me
some of his books & engravings. Walked
home — tea — Ev'g. Looking over a new
Baedeker Guide Book to Central Italy.
