### July, Wednesday 6, 1892

*[Written at top of page: Bevans. Hotel Belle vue. Room 56.]*

Glorious day. Up at about 4 + took a look over the quiet lake. Ralph sleeping quietly. Took an nap, then up for all day at 5.30. To work packing my trunk to go to Geneva + do all such work done. Then dressed + went out at 7 — Arranged to have our two heavy trunks sent by Grande vitesse to Geneva — bought fruit — then back to hotel. Ralph awake — eye lid [illegible] + swollen but he made no complaint — [illegible] at 8:15 — bid the [illegible] goodbye + with Emma + Marjorie, we left on the 8:45 boat — delicious effects of light + color over the nts + water — many clouds — [illegible] a lovely lake to which we bid goodbye very regretfully — a small boat took us over the [illegible] buildings of the lake — the Ponte Trezza extremely picturesque — would very much like to stop there to do some sketching — the buildings so very picturesque — the [illegible] landscape + water so charming — at the landing we found a custom house. + had to go through the formality of examination — the children using that we had the trouble of [illegible] our overcrowded valises + the trial of getting them shut again. I should have noted that on Lugano, at the end of the promontory, I was much taken by a little old town, [illegible] — further on, just before getting to Ponte della Tresa — we passed through a narrow opening + by another delightful old town Lavena — Here nts., water + habitations all combined in delightful picturesqueness. The R.R. ride over to Luino on Maggiore was very interesting — narrow gauge — up a steep hill — + passing the crest a steep decline, most of the way by a wild [illegible] stream — got to Luino soon after 11 + were told that the boat would not be along for 45 mins — so we wandered into the town + bought bread, cakes + fruit — almost missed the boat — were misinformed as to time but in good + were soon on our way down Maggiore — crossed at once to the other side, passing a little island covered by the 15 cent. castle of Cannero — the home of some of the old robbers Baveno — Thunder storms passed to the north of us + later to the south but we were untouched. The black birds still please us with their [illegible]. Also notes — going to our rooms — I was washed + spent a couple of hours in loafing about — The boys had a very good time paddling + playing in the water on the lake shore. Dinner at 6:30 — after that we all went out in a boat — served by a [illegible] less old boatman who could not make his Italian understood able to Annie + Emma. The sky was cloudless — the color heavenly — the one glimpse of the Simplon something not to be forgotten. Returned in half an hour — met Emma, Marjorie — we wandered about the town — we got into a little old church — very dim twilight — moonlight streaming in — service — no light, no music — an attentive + awe inspiring [illegible] women + children — very impressive — then out + followed a country road and back through narrow, winding streets of a [illegible] part — on an open square, came upon a couple of traveling players — man + wife + infant 18 mos. old — [illegible] on wheels — a sort of Harlequin + Columbine. He danced on stilts + tumbled about to the great joy of a crowd of youngsters — she, dressed in tights, close fitting bodice, bare arms, danced, [illegible] old, among 18 or 20 eggs on the ground — + broke none. An amusing show — To hotel at [illegible] — to bed at 10. Awakened about 12 by desperate nausea — lay in a cold sweat until after 4 + then delivered myself of my entire contents — then slept until about 7 — Up + bad [illegible] at 9 — feeling very slim — then a dismal [illegible] + kept me a prisoner in my room all day — to my great disgust. The day being exceedingly lovely — I spent much of it sleeping.
