## Left Page

### July, Sunday 24, 1892

*Ascent of the Brévent*

Glorious day - up at 6.30 - bkfst. at 7.30 at 8.15
Jim & I got away on two mules with our guide
Alfred Comte & a man to bring back the mules -
Annie had to remain behind on Ralph's account,
very much to my regret. The day was glorious beyond
description - air pure & clear - before we started we
could see through the large telescope a party on their
way up Mt. Blanc - our ride through the woods
was unmitigated joy - above the woods the path
was very steep & winding - through the woods an
hour - Rested for 10 m - then an hour & 5 m - in the open
got to the Chalet, le Bel Achat at 10.30 - ordered lunch
at 11 & got a very good one - at 12 we started for the top
of the Brévent, a peak 8284 feet above sea level - The trail
was exceedingly rough & wide, in places great fields of
soft new snow through which our mules floundered - The
last of the ascent very laborious - reached the top in
55 m. a grand view - some light clouds obscured
the mt. tops to the S.W. but Mt. Blanc & all the mts. S.E. -
E. & N.E. of us were absolutely unobscured - the Bernese
Alps, away to the N - had a few clouds upon their highest
peaks. Several people on the top - a drift of snow with two
or three dozen bottles of wine, etc. stuck into it - cooling &
waiting for purchasers. After 20 m. on the top we
commenced our descent on the Plan Praz Side - The mules
were sent down the side they came up - as the P.P. Side
is entirely impracticable for them - this extremely wild,
in places we looked down over precipices sheer 6 or 700 feet.
The Chimney was a bad place but Jim did exceedingly well.
We had to travel through much new snow & got our feet very
wet & cold - but they dried before our journey was ended -
left the top at 2.0 - entered the wood & rested 15 m -
all the way down, wonderful views of the valley - the mts. &
the cliffs back of us - intensely interesting & the right word
to do it - the walk thro' the woods, lovely - when near the end
and, while yet in the wood, met Annie, Ralph, Emma
& Marjorie - got to hotel at 4.15 - just 8 hrs. from the
time of starting - a run down - a rest & a dinner
thoroughly enjoyed - even in salon until 10. then
to bed.

From the Bel Achat we watched the completion of the ascent
of Mt. Blanc - we saw the man, his guide & his porter on the
Summit & heard the cannon fired in the town.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 25, 1892

Another superb day - clear, cool air, delightfully
warm in the sunshine - Annie & Emma with
Jim, walked to the Plan Achat - a point at the
summit of the wood on the trail to the Bel Achat -
& about an hour & a half from the hotel where a fine
view of Mt. Blanc is had - They got back at 2.30.
I took Ralph & with my sketching traps & went
to the place where I made my W.C. sketches
the other day & made a careful outline -
then I loafed in the sunshine while Ralph
played in the water - I went to Cook's office
& got information concerning the diligences to
Cêles - After lunch I rested awhile & then
with Annie, went to office & secured seats in
diligence for to-morrow at 1.40 p.m. Then we
wandered about, bought photos, and gazed
upon magnificent Mt. Blanc - I regret very
much that we have to leave this place where
we are very comfortably situated and where
the children seem to be doing so very well.
Ralph has recovered his appetite & is enjoying
himself exceedingly. Evg. - after dinner,
was injudicious enough to lie down with Ralph
- we asleep and so, wasted the eve. To bed about
10.

On settling bill at Beau Site Hotel - Sylvain Couttet
Proprietor, I am much pleased - The house is
very plain & simple - The beds are excellent
and the board is good and generous - For
a quiet, economical Resting place it is the
best we have yet met. Couttet is a fine
old fellow & very "square" in his dealings -
This, at least, is my experience.
