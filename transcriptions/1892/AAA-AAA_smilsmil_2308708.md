## Left Page

### December Saturday 3 1892

Nasty, wet, wet — clearing in the afternoon — quite mild — After [illegible] — Annie, the boys & I, went to the British Museum. After looking at many things with them I left them & went to the Print Room where I continued looking over the early Italian prints until 1.30 — then I went to the Art Club & had lunch — At 3.15 I went to Boussod, Valadon & Co. & there met A—. Private view of a number of Water color sketches of an exceedingly impressionistic character by an elderly amateur who has never exhibited before, Mr. Brabazon. He has been urged to this by Jn. S. Sargent who writes an introductory note to the catalogue & other young Radicals. Some few of them were pleasant to me but the utter absence of drawing, or even (as I see it) of sense of general form — the imperfect tone relations & color quality affected me unpleasantly — Of course his friends claim for him, in fullest measure, these very qualities (except, possibly, the first) that I think are wanting — There was also a collection of Water color & oil sketches by Hevner — We came home together bet. 4 & 5 o'clk & I had a good rest before tea.
Ev'f wrote a letter to Collins (chap.)

## Right Page

### December Sunday 4 1892

A fine cold, breezy wintry day — ice on all puddles & a dash of snow over the grass. The day was cloudless until noon & again later in the afternoon & at night — At 10.30 Annie, the boys & I went by undergr. R.R. to St. John's Wood Station & from there walked to the top of Primrose Hill, which we found to be dirty — In spite of the bright day we had no view of London — It was severely cold — then we walked through Regent's Park and back & forth until we had seen most of it — then to Baker St. Station — undergr. R.R. — but it was closed until 1 o'clk — so, we walked home, getting there at about 12.45 — It was near 2 where we had dinner — At 3 we all went to the Albert Hall where we heard some organ music by Mr. Tonking — Miss Emily Davis & Mr. Albon Nash were the vocalists — The Hall is immense & a small audience made it appear barren — I did it care for the organ — it lacked color in its combinations & was loud & commonplace — In the air from "Moses in Egypt" the top remains stop gave some notes that were startlingly humane — The voices of the two singers were clear, but very little, as though they came thro the small end of an opera glass — Out at 4.30 & walked home — tea at 6.30 — Ev'f I lounged and A— read aloud to me.
