## Left Page

### March, Friday 18, 1892

The nastiest day yet - quite a heavy fall of snow during the night, continuing until about 9 A.m. after that just enough of a mist or mizzle to keep every thing wet — air bleak & raw to the last degree. After 10 when I got to the studio - worked for about an hour on jade plate — then spent some time over the plates rec'd. from the Boston Museum $46[illegible]. Burnished awhile on small experimental aqua-tint preparatory to taking some p.p. 16.m[illegible] Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 3 — fussed about until after 4 when Bloodgood & Millater — Etching Club Cat. Com. came in — we laid plans for next years cat— taking our time until near 6 — then home — dinner — every some reading.

In the m[illegible] Dr. Willard Parker came to see if I could etch a plate for a menu card for a class dinner — I begged [illegible]

Jade 1 h.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 19, 1892

A slight fall of snow during last night, then a brilliant day - cold & windy but large portion of yesterday's snow was melted. Got to studio after 10 & spent the m[illegible] taking 7 india p.p. from the little experimental aqua-tint plate — landscapes of which I sold a couple of p.p. at the Etching Club up[illegible]. Home soon after 1. back at 2.30 & worked on jade plate until near 4 when I started for cor. 5th Ave. & 15th St. where I called upon Miss A.S. Abbott and after a time was joined by Annie — then we went in to Champoney's studio & spent some time admiring his apartments & his furnishings — A. rode home - I walked — dinner before 6 — At 6.30 Annie Kate & with both boys — & of Edw'd's & 4 of Walter's children went to Charles' rooms where we saw the Barnum parade — Home before 8 — Charl came in about [illegible] — to bed at 11

Jade 1 h.
