## Left Page

### August, Sunday 7, 1892

*Lucerne. Swan Hotel — Room 2.
Hotil du Cygne*

A glorious day - Up at 6.30 - finished packing - bkfs. at
8 & at 9 we bid W. Von Allman - his hotel "the Straubbach"
& the Lauterbrunnen valley a very reluctant good bye —
Mr. Von A. has treated us very well & we recommend him
& his house heartily - The drive to Interlaken was beau-
tiful to distraction - we sailed over the air - the mountains
in distress - the cooks & the water - we were well over the
Jungfrau, so dazzling - growing mightier as well as more
beautiful every moment as she receded from us -
we had, for a moment, a fine view of the Wetterhorn -
we got to Interlaken (east) in a little more than an
hour - To P.O. - then to R.R. Station - there found
A's trunk that the Geneva Porter had sent to
Interlaken instead of to Lucerne as directed -
for a time it seemed as though the finding of it
would avail nothing as the R.R. official de-
fused to do anything saying it was Sunday -
but at last we got it through in spite of his
ill manners - he was boorish & rude - then took
our seats & waited for the train to move - after
11 the Thun boat am? — passengers huddled
onto our train & we went on to Interlaken West
& were there transferred to the Brientz boat —
The Lake of B. - not so picturesque as Thun - rather
upper end - the cliffs & waterfalls very interesting -
there to Train - narrow guage - cog wheel - cars
for Lucern over the Bruning Pass - we ran along up
to Meiringen - an extremely interesting place - then
a bra sharp angle returned & promptly commenced
a steep ascent - lovely day - air aromatic & amaz-
ons on the heated & pine forests - wonderful
views of the valley & mountains - the cars with out-
side gallery on the view side a clever arrangement -
The descent on other side entirely different but
very fine - the Lungern See & the Sarner See - at
a small station on the Alpnacher See an arm
of Lucern Lake. Transferred to a steamboat and
had an agreeable sail - getting to Lucern at
about 4.30 after a grand day of sight-seeing
most successful in all ways - At 5.30 I had
my every meal with the boys on a verandah -
A. dined - Table d'hote — Every one strolled
along the Lake shore, under the horse-chestnuts
& were bewildered by moonlight - electric
& colored lights - crowds of strollers - music in
the hotels - etc — to bed at 10 -
After our arrival A. went to see Miss Foshay &
I went over the bridges with the boys -

## Right Page

### August, Monday 8, 1892

*To Brunnen*

A very pleasant day, altho' warm - we took
a late bkfs. - Miss Foshay came in soon
after & we all wandered out (after, by the
way, I had spent more than an hour at Cook's
office getting tickets to Munich & Nuremberg)
We went into the cathedral then climbed the
hill to the Pension Neu - Schweizer haus where
A. spent some time 20 years ago & where Mary &
Kate stayed two years ago - Then down the hill
again & went to see Thorwaldsen's store - and
into the geological garden nearby - was fooled
also into going in to see a shocking & grotesque
show of the Jungfrau & neighboring mountains -
to Hotel to Lnch at 12.30 - At 1.30 I went alone
as Jennie preferred to rest - on boat up the Lake
to Brunnen - The day was very hot out as the
boat moved the air was very refreshing - I
had some delightful effects of shimmering
water reflections - some fine glimpses of
the Wetterhorn & the Jungfrau group -
we got to Brunnen at 3.30 - landed and
took a return boat at 3.45 getting back to
Lucerne at 5.20 - The sail back was in some
ways pleasanter than going - Sun ahead of
us - hazy, dreamy light - oil-like water -
I do not like the color of it when it is in motion
but when a reflecting surface it is wonder-
fully beautiful - The boat - the Stadt Lucern -
was a very handsome one - the finest I have
yet seen in this part of the world - I had a
pleasant & comfortable seat & that added
to my enjoyment - A. was out riding when I
got - so I went out & got some photo'. At 6.30
we had our every meal together a la carte -
After the boys were in bed we worried over
R.R. time tables and at last had to give
up & consult the Hotel Porter, at Torwhich
we determined to start at 7.20 to - morrow night.
Then I packed up things & after finishing my
long letter to thee - & posting it I got to bed
just before 11 o'clk.
