## Left Page

### January, Thursday 28, 1892

Bright mtf., overcast p.m — very
much milder — to studio soon after
10 & after a little more burnishing
on 5"×7" aqua-tint "An old dam" I
commenced printing — St. Gessler
came in with a proof of his plate
portrait of old Dr — & spent some
time with me — Home to lunch at
1.30 — back at 2.30 & took four pf.
that I tacked onto boards — in
some respects I have failed on
this plate — in others, it comes out
fairly well — I have yet more work
that I wish to do on it with the
burnisher but one of the pf. taken
to day will have to go into the Etching
Club exbt. to-morrow — Home at 5.30
dinner soon after 6. Edna & Tom sat-
dined with us — Eve'g Aunnie & I went
to Mad. Sq. Garden Concert Hall & heard
Paderewski — the most delightful as
well as astonishing pianist that I
ever heard — His rendering of
Chopin was delicious — walked
home & to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 29, 1892

Mild, bright mtf — overcast afternoon
Got to studio after 10 & spent some
time getting pf. of aqua-tint "An old
dam", ready to take to N.A. & put
into frames. Then to academy with my
pf. & put them into their frames and
hung about there until past 1 o'clk.
Then home to lunch — Aunnie at a
formal lunch party at Rosalie Butler's
Afternoon, awhile at studio trying to
get a little order out of chaos, then
to DeKnine's about Cat. business —
walked home. Dinner at 6.30 —
I wrote my regular letter to Herrick
& at 9 o'clk. went to P. of P. private view
Water Color Socy. at N.A. There until
about 11
