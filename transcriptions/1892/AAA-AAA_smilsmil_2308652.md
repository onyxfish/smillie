## Left Page

### August, Saturday 13, 1892

*Würtemberger Hof. Nuremberg — Room 49.*

Very warm, cloudless day — It was after
10 before we were ready to start out — then
all of us wandered through the streets — to the Cath-
edral — market place — Gate towers etc. I went
to an art material store & bought some paper.
Back to Hotel at 12 & I cut up my papers & did
some packing before Table d'hote at 1 — After
that completed packing at 3 — Paid bill made
out for 4 persons — I objected & got a reduction —
Bill otherwise moderate — relatively — medium
class — Rooms good — Plumbing 2nd class — Service
poor (as it is in all the hotels here.) Table d'hote portions
very small & closely counted — We got away
from the hotel at 3.15 to take a 4.20 train to Nur-
emburg — to station in 5 m — Little trouble
with baggage — then made a rush for train —
no impediment — Train standing by platform
already well filled — every body rushing —
hither & thither in great excitement — the offic-
ials in greater distress than anybody else —
gesticulating & vociferating & moaning, pers-
oning frowns — they seemed to be incapable
of giving coherent or intelligible information
to anybody — engine whistles screaming made
a perfect pandemonium. At last we were
seated in a Berlin car in spite of the protestations of an official who would drive us to the front
of the train where the Nuremberg cars were. [illegible]
we maintained our positions & got to N. without
change of seat. Country flat & uninteresting for
two hours — wheat being harvested — & hops —
at about 7 got to a place where 5 m. was given
for refreshments — 2 or 3 men & women rushed for a
sausage 15 pfennig — I got in in good time and
scooped 6 Rolls — in 2 m. the place was bare —
a crowd howling — all on mad rush to train and
locked in — then we waited 30 m — while a small
river of beer, in mugs, was carried to the prisoners —
no American crowd would have allowed this
to be — got to N. after half an hour late — great
joy — went at once to the W. Hof where, to my
joy I found a large pleasant room all ready
for us — the boys were put right to bed — I went
down to the court & found Emma & Mary my
who also here at 5 till — to bed at 10 & thus,
after a fitful day — peace.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 14, 1892

A pleasant day, uncomfortably warm
in the sunshine — It was after 9 before
I went to sleep & owing to inexcusably
bad service it was after 10 before I got it —
It was nearly 11 when we all — E. & M.
with us, went to the P.O. — There H. got
three letters & I got one from Kate — After
reading our letters we started on a walk
through town — we went into the Cathedral
ch. of St. Lawrence very impressive — to other
churches — to market places — to see Foun-
tains & to the house of Hans Sachs — then back
to hotel at 1 — Table d'hote — a very good
one — fine large room — many diners — After
that a wait in our room until after 3 — then
we all went out for a ride — around the
walls of the old city — then to the cemetery
of St. John where we saw the graves of Hans Sachs
& Dürer, marked by the characteristic slate gravestones
and bronze castings — then back to the city & to the
Kaiser Burg where we spent an hour going through
the museum seeing the Iron Maiden — the Rack &
& many other pleasing instruments of torture — to the
well over 300 feet deep with its subterranean Passage
ages & to the Castle where we went through a series
of rooms very meanly furnished — then we
drove through arches & over bridges & about town
to the different houses — churches — fountains
etc — getting back to the hotel at 6.15 — Had
our early meal out in the court under trees —
great crowds in hotel — all bound for Bayreuth —
the café given up to beds on the floor. I
rec'd a note from the Sec'y of Belle Vue Hotel
Munich enclosing a supplemental bill of
items he had neglected to put in — the first bill
amounting to M. 37.75 — a remarkable oversight
that had not noticed — Read awhile at [illegible]
to bed at 10.
