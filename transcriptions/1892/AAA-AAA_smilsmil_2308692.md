## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 1, 1892

*St. James' Theatre. Oscar Wilde*

A thick m[illegible] - so, I determined to spend it at the British Museum Reading Room - walked down - finished reading Herkomers book & writing notes - walked home to dinner at 1.30 - After dinner Annie went to Putney to call upon Mr. McE. Satchell - I worked with crayon for some time upon a proof of his Morgan head there I had a little nap. Eve[ning] Annie & I went to the St. James Theatre & saw a one act melo-drama "Kit Marlowe" that gave us no pleasure - then Oscar Wilde's play "My Lady Windermere's fan". We were amused. It is an improbable, impossible play in the matter of plot & construction - no such situations could occur with sane people, but it is full of rather bright, epigrammatic bits that glitter as they fly - It is heartless & cynical & indirectly immoral. - I would not like to take anyone, any girl, to see such a play - She could not but imbibe the most pernicious sentiments. It was late when we got out - almost 12 when we got home. I took Clara around to her house & got into bed at 12.30

Morgan. 2 h.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 2, 1892

*Frank Short*

The thickest fog yet in the m[orning]. I saw a number of cabs with their lamps lighted at 11 a.m. By 1 o'clk. the fog lifted & the rest of the day was comparatively pleasant. After [illegible] I took my plate & some of my pfs. & walked to Gouldings - it was very dark, but he said not too dark to take a proof - so we had a consultation & Cole took a pf. on a new plan of wiping - after that a second one was taken, Mr. Goulding himself wiping the plate. In some respects the new plan is an improvement, but the state of the plate is worrying me a good deal - M[r]. G. looked over my pfs. & said that he had nothing to teach me in the way of printing - Home soon after 12, & I spent some time over my pfs. before dinner. It was 3.30 when I started to call on Mr. Frank Short, at Wentworth Studios, Manresa R[oa]d. Kensington - I took some of my pfs. with me - went by underground R.R. to Sloane Sq. - then Kings Road by bus to Manresa R[oa]d. Found Mr. Short & had, to me, a very pleasant call. Showed him my work & we talked of processes, methods & such things. On my way home & I walked from Studios to S.C.N. R.R. Station & wanting more walk I got out at Notting hill Station. & then walked home. - Ida - Eve[ning]. Some Reading.

Morgan. 3 h.
