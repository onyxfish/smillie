## Left Page

### August, Friday 5, 1892

*(header note: Wengemalps.)*

Very fine day altho' partially clouded. A little
walk before 8:15 — sky overcast with clouds hanging
low — immediately after 8:15 — the clouds broke & gave
a view of some of the snow tops — Annie urged me to
try the Wängemalps & at 9.45 I started on horse back
with a boy — First part of the ascent very steep, with fine
expanding views of the valley — much finer than on the
münren side — very suggestive of a small Yosemite —
the snow mts very fine & the Schmadribach an ideal
waterfall — after an hour of hard climbing a comparatively
broad stretch of meadowland is reached, over which,
turning & sitting. The trail ascends gradually for another
hour — Part of the way is through a fine spruce forest —
At last, turning again to the east, we came to the Hotel
de l'Alp from at 11.45 where we stopped — At this
time the clouds were breaking away giving superb views
of the Mönch, Jungfrau & Silberhorn — the Eiger persisted
to hid behind a stationary cloud — one also hung over the
solitary Mönch — The effects were stupendous & I was
very much impressed — lunch at 12 — while at table
the proprietor rushed in & shouted "lavines" (avalanches)
we heard the sound & upset our chairs in our haste to
get to the windows — we had a good view of a mass of
ice & snow falling from the Jung from the glacier over a
great precipice into the Trümmelbach gorge — the stream
lasted probably a minute & was like a cascade of
milk accompanied by thunder — lounged about until
1.30 then rode on for half an hour to the little Scheideck
Hotel Bellevue where I spent half an hour roaming
about enjoying the view down into the Grindelwald
valley — Saw another avalanche from the Jung from
glacier — Many yet another, high up under a cloud —
the reverberations were tremendous — and as we rode
our away down the valley from the Wetterhorn — at
2.30 I commenced my return — gazing spell bound
up at the tremendous heights, smiling in dazzling sun-
light — or above the clouds — The descent was very
pitiless — I walked a good ¼ of the way — found
particularly the last ¼ of the descent where it is so
very steep — In the afternoon light it was especially
lovely — the picture from the "Pension" Silberhorn
was perhaps the finest of them all, the Schmadribach
being the crowning feature — Got in at 5 o'clk.
Had a good scroll down & rested yourself an
hour before dinner — very much disappointed
because Emma did not arrive — no any letter —
I walked down to the other hotel & to the P.O. after
dinner hoping to get some trace of her — about
9.30 beautiful effect of moonlight & the Staubbach —
pleasant, with duet accompanied by two
pleasant female voices from balcony above ours.
To bed after 10 — Sung by miss Hadden & friend
Duett — Verlassen (forsaken) by Koschat.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 6, 1892

Up before 5 & saw the sunrise on the Jungfrau — then
to bed again — up for all day at 7.30 — the clouds
came down on the mts & I gave up the idea of
any expedition for the day — At 10 o'clk — we
all took a ride to the upper end of the valley to
Stechelberg — the end of the wagon road — and
from there we walked up a path for 15 or 20
min & got a fine view of the Schmadribach —
It was a grand picture beautifully lighted
the cloud effects over the snow tops adding
much to the beauty of the mts. As we drove
back a few drops of rain fell while the
sun shone — After lunch I wrote for some
time upon a letter to Geo. Shaw, as the after-
noon threatened showers (& very little came)
I took a nap — On my return we all
took a short walk & awaited the 6 o'clk
train from Interlaken hoping for Emma
or anyone but they came not — neither did
we get a letter from them — They must
be lost somewhere — we have determined
to go on to-morrow —
