## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 20, 1892

Dull day with some struggling sunshine.
Got up in the m'g quite bunged up by an
influenza. As the w't was damp I
did not go out into the Park. Got to
work on my etching soon after 10 and
continued until nearly — then went to
the pond for A. & the boys. Ralph's boat
doing some independent sailing to
please itself detained us until 1.30.
Then before we finished our belated
dinner the French ladies began to come
in answer to A's advt in this mornings
Times. I think eight called before 4
o'clk & as many more notes from agents,
schools, etc. came later. I got to work
again at about 3 & worked until 4.30
Then I went to the Park with the boys
& we rowed on the Serpentine for nearly
an hour. Tea at 6.30. Swelf I was
able to do a little reading — but not
much, my running eyes & nose demanded
all of my attention.

morgan - 3 h.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 21, 1892

Considerable rain & a little sickly sunshine —
my influenza so possessed me that I
did not go out of the house. I got to work
at about 9.30 on my Morgan plate with
my paint coming all over the face.
Worked until 1 — then had an in-door
walk for about 20 m. before dinner.
Annie had more calls in response to
her Times advt., & more notes, but she made
an agreement with Mlle Théland to
come for two hours a day — 11 to 1 — and
talk & walk with the boys. After
dinner I wrote a note to send by way
of Queenstown to Minnie & Marjorie who
have sailed from Liverpool on the City
of Berlin to-day. I worked on my plate
from 3 to 5.15 & then had another in-door
walk of half an hour before tea. Swelf
I read a back No. of the "Century" —

morgan - 5 h.
