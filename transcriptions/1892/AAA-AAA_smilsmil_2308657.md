## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 23, 1892

A very hot, sultry m't. delightful and
refreshing showers with considerable thunder
in the afternoon — cool, clear eve't. Bkf.
at 9 — Annie feeling seedy — and I, not much
better — I spent most of the m't on balcony
writing a letter to S. R. Koehler — & invented
a't which was not an entirely acceptable
meal — In the afternoon changed our room
from 18 on 2nd floor to No. 33 on 3rd floor a better
room, having more space & light &, we hope,
purer air as we will be further from the
kitchen & W.C.'s — The rain prevented
my going out so I read Trollope's Miss
MacKenzie — Between showers, about
6 o'clk. took a short walk & went to the
Gardenon hoping to find letters — but
none were there — Even. Read for
awhile & to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 24, 1892

A hot day mitigated slightly by clouds &
a breeze — In the m't got letter from Ge.
Aug. 9th & in the afternoon one from Kather.
Aug. 11th. About 10 o'clk. Annie, the boys and
I set out upon a walk — went through the
Lautengrafen schlucht where we found
it delightfully cool, shady & damp. These
schluchts are interesting & agreeable
walks but there is nothing of material
for pictures in any of the schluchts or forests
that I have yet been in here — The rocks
are not picturesque — the evergreens,
provided as most of them do under cultivation
are about as picturesque as a gigantic
mouth of corn would be. But as we were not
looking for pictures we enjoyed our walk
by much — Passing through the ravine
we returned by a walk along the tops of
the rocks getting back to hotel after 12.30
Dinner at 1.30 — About 5 P.m. I went out
with the boys & had a very pleasant walk
through some fine beech woods & back over
the Breitengescbeid from whence we had
a pleasant view all around — Back to hotel
at 6.30 & had our eve't meal at 7 — Even't
spent very stupidly, most of the time rolling
on my bed.
