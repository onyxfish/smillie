## Left Page

### June, Thursday 16, 1892

Cool, breezy day with wind from the
east — cloudy most of the time.
I got up at 5 o'clk. & soon got to work
writing on paper "Etching technically
considered" for the N.Y. Etching Club. I
have been much disappointed on
finding that this paper has to be entirely
reconstructed & rewritten. I had helped
that little else than copying portions
of a paper written long ago & read before
the Rembrandt Club. B'kfst at 8 o'clk.
After that for some time I worked at
gathering together things to take with
me & got trunk down into parlor.
Then went out & attended to a number of
things — back before 12 & wrote for an hour
on Etching — then lunch. Kate enthusiastic
having spent part of the m'g on the steamer —
She set? to P'ok. by afternoon boat. Uncle Willie
called in the w'g. In spite of feeling very
tired I worked from garret to cellar until
6 — then rested for half an hour before dinner.
Eve'g, Geo. & Minly & Walter & Louisa and
Chant came in — much business talk
leaving instructions — etc. — To bed at
10.30

## Right Page

### June, Friday 17, 1892

Another exceedingly hot & uncomfort-
able day — awake at 5 but did not get up
until 7 — B'kfst at 8 — I wrote notes until
after 9. Acct. Schilling called for a few
minutes. It was 10 when I started
out on my round of messages — went
to Station 7 — to Studio Bldg & saw Calverly
then to Forster's & bid him & Lilienthal
good bye — then to 2d Nat. Bank then
to D. B. Butler's & made a list of the
pictures left with him — Home before
12 & at once to work packing my trunk
— lunch — & again at trunk & valise. At
3.30 exp. men came for trunks — sent
them away — came again at 4.30
& by hustling all were got away at
4.40 — then a thunderstorm coming
up I started for Barony's to get photo? —
place shut up — hurried home getting
in just ahead of the storm. Grace
Ireland came in & stayed until
there was a lull. Dinner. Eve'g
