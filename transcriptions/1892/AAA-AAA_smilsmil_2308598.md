## Left Page

### April, Monday 25, 1892

*Mary, Kate & goes ret? to Po'k.*

Bright m'g — slightly overcast later in the day & quite cool — After bkfs'.
wrote a letter to Mr. K out of Buffalo about taking Montrose house for the summer — It was 11 when I got to Dr. Gerster's — He gave me a dressing & I felt better — then to studio — then to K. & V. & got more Jade [illegible]. Back to studio at 12.30 — & covered wet [illegible] in rubber — saw Ge., just from Ridgefield — Wm. Pate called — Home at 1.30
After lunch the Po'k party prepared to return by 3.30 train — Ge. came in to say his good byes —. Fanny Kimball came — she is also going to Po'k. to attend a family wedding — all left at 3 — & soon after I went to studio & spent the rest of the afternoon working over my Jade proofs — Home at 6 — dinner — Went about 9. Alice Mcleand came in — Then Charl came in & then Fred Mcleand came — Fred & Alice left about 10 — Charl stayed until near 11 — then we got to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 26, 1892

Brilliant, delightful day — Resumed my regular duties & went to grocery & market on my way to studio where I arrived at 10'15. I soon got to work at clearing up the mess into which I have allowed things to get by my carelessness during the past month — but I made little progress. Home at — back to studio at 2.40 — found note from Coffin, Sec'y of Soc'y of Am Artists informing me that my painting "An old dam" had been rejected — whereupon I said a brave new dam & was dejected — It depresses me to find that I cannot understand these young men or what it is they are aiming at — Worked at house cleaning until near 5 — then rode up to 72nd St. & walked in the Park to 79th St. & then rode home in a 'bus. Arrive out late — dinner at 7 — A quiet ev'g Reading & writing — To bed at 10.30
