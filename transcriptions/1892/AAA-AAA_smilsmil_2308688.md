## Left Page

### October, Monday 24, 1892

Another fine day - delightful morning. Worked an hour on Morgan plate stoning
down the overbitten work, especially the black coat & destroying the neat work that
I had so carefully laid in - my only hope for the plate now lies in the destruction
& defacement of all the neatness thereon.
Before 12. went with Jim (& Ralphy) to the Swimming School for his lesson - then in Park until we were all thoroughly warmed up - Dinner at 1.30 - After that Smillie & I took cab & went to the Fine Arts Society - 148 new Bond S., where I saw Mr. Brown - busy getting an effect on the walls - Pompalias covered, so that he could not show me, what I so much desired to see & study with reference to my present trouble with the Morgan head; & some of the fine modern heads by Unger, Kastner, Flameng, Waldteuver & others — He gave me a card to Mr. D. C. Thompson - at Boussod, Vaedon & Co - went there - Mr. T. was out - looked over a promiscuous lot of prints and found two or three that were interesting - then we went into an exhibit of salon pictures - a sorry lot - I would not like to have to live in the same room with more than two paintings that I saw there - then we walked home.
Eve J. A. finished reading Henry VIII.

Morgan - 1 h.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 25, 1892

*Henry Irving. Lyceum.*

A nasty day - wet & cold in the m[orn]ing,
but improving towards night when it cleared - Mrs. I worked for a couple of hours on Morgan head - In the afternoon did some loafing preparatory to going to the Lyceum Theatre in the evening -
Clara came at 7 & stayed with the children -
we got to the Theatre in good time & had excellent seats in Dress Circle - The house is old & rather dingy looking - Surfaces all flat - very few mouldings - & the decorated in [Pompeian?] or very early Italian style - The play was Henry VIII. Irving in title part. Irving as Wolsey & Ellen Terry as Queen Katherine -
Irving of course was handsome, but his bluffness was, to me, noise & swagger - Irving & Terry are, giving old & are losing none of their peculiarities - Irving's mounting - his dragging down of his brows & looking from the corners of his eyes - the swing of his body & arms & the stiffness of his legs, all in marked measure go to make up his ideal of Wolsey - Terry's voice continues to decline, her face wore a mask of paint - I thought that she acted her part better than Irving did it - perhaps that was because it is to me the more interesting part. The stage setting disappointed me. Much of it was really very indifferent painting & most of the architectural scenes were shown such a rickety, slovenly manner as to give an unpleasant impression that at any moment it might topple over - one scene was, I thought, very fine - Katherine's vision just before his death - The color - the illusion - the chiro oluro & color effect was very impressive - Real angels [illegible] in a beam of heaven's light came down and adorned the sleeping figure to follow them - it was an admirably arranged picture very effectively managed - We got out at about 11 - took cab & were whisked home, getting there about 11.20 - I took Clara to her door - then home & to bed at 12.

Morgan - 2 h.
