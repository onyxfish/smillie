## Left Page

### October Saturday 22 1892

A dull, threatening m'f. cold & windy
but it cleared very pleasantly & the day
passed without a shower. We had
planned to spend the day at Syden-
ham Palace — but I thought that Abbey
might call & I was anxious to get on with
my Morgan head — so I backed out and
Annie & the boys went to the Tower. I
put them into the cars of the under ground
R.R. & then took a walk — & then to work.
I am much discouraged by the condition
of my plates — I now realize how rash I
have been in undertaking such a work.
I had not the experience necessary — I was
a fool that rushed in — etc — the fundamental
work is bad — the modeling & texture in
the lines is stiff & hard & in my finishing
I have only made it worse — now the face
looks as though it had been turned in a
lathe. The whole thing is very much over-
bitten — In desperation I may pull it
through but it can never be what I had
at first rashly — no, normally — hoped to make
it. Dinner at 2 — I worked from 2.30
until nearly 5 — then I went out & walked
out to Holland Park — through the cause-
to Kens'n'ton Rd. & back through Campden
Hill Rd to Notting Hill & thence home getting
in at 6.30 — tea — Evely, just lounged
about.

Morgan — 4. 30 h.

## Right Page

### October Sunday 23 1892

Bright & breezy — really a charming
winter day — B'kf'st. Rather late, of course. It
was nearly 11 when we all started for a walk —
went directly across the Park to Hyde Park Corner —
thence to the Mall. Taking a look at the pal-
aces — then into Pall Mall where we took
a look at the Club Houses — up Q. S't. James
S't to Picadilly — then to P's Circus & there took a
'bus for Marble Arch & from there, walked home.
Dinner at 2 — After that fell asleep in my
chair before the fire so that it was 3.30 before
I started to make my call on W't. Colin Hunter.
I rode by 'bus to Holland Park, avoiding
way for lost passengers from Notting Hill through
to Kensington — then to Melbury Road — on the
other on west side of Holland Park — No. 14. The Ho.
of Mr. Colin Hunter. Evidently one of the suc-
cessful painters — a picturesque home, like no
identity built for an artist. Mr. H. took me
into his studio — a noble big room — where he in-
troduced me to Mr. A. Ritchie of Sidney Australia,
evidently another Scot. Mr. H. had two small
paintings — that is about 3 ft. long — that I admire
very much — a couple of larger ones were
so agreeable in color — were hard & tended
to blackness — his largest painting The Rapids
above Niagara Falls — that he intends to send
to Chicago; I do not like — the water lacks
movement — it is more like cast iron — After
what was to me a very pleasant call of about
half an hour — I departed — I must not forget
a number of etching pts that he showed me from
his plates — they were admirable — I walked
in the pleasant twilight along Kensington
Road to the Albert gate; across the Park. Home —
having had much walking during the day,
I enjoyed lying upon the lounge while Annie
read aloud Henry VIII —
