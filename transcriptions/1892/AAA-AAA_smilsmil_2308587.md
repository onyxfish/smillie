## Left Page

### April, Friday 1, 1892

A very pleasant day — got started away from the house early enough but had so many little matters to attend to that it was fully 10 o'clk when I got to studio. To work at once upon Jade plate, scraper + burnisher. It was 1.30 when I got home to lunch. Annies in Brooklyn — Unitarian League — Back to studio at 2.45, taking Ralph with me. I laid a bit of soft ground, drew my autograph & bit it in. It was near 5 when Ralph & I went to K.+V.'s to make arrangements for proofs to-morrow. At 5.40 when we got home. Dinner after 6. Eve.f Annie, Mary (ned) Cook & Miss Williams went out on a spree — to the opera. I stayed at home & read "April" Century until near 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 2, 1892

An April-like day — growing warmer — most of the time overcast with some light sprinkles of rain — After my usual morng duties I went down to K.+V.'s — Canal S. Lathrop ahead of me with his "Mauve" plate. It was after 11 before young V. could take two [illegible] of my Jade plate — It was after 12 when I got to studio & tacked them down — At 1 when I got home to lunch. After that a short nap & to studio before 3.30 & worked on Jade plate for an hour & a half. Dinner at 6.30 & I went to monthly meeting at the Century — met Charl at our door & we walked up together. Did not have a very lively time. So home soon after 11 — leaving Charl at Union League & Walter leaving me at my own door.
