## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 29, 1892

Dull, dry mefs, but drizzle commencing
soon after noon — then some smart showers
last night, cloudless moonlight. — After
bkfs. I went to Gourdine's by appointment
at 10.30 + he spent the time until noon
in taking four pfs from the Morgan plate.
Two of these were of no use, as he was finding
his way — the other two are fine pfs — but
when I got them home, where I could
look at them quietly, I thought them a
trifle over printed in the face. So I
felt very tired in the afternoon, partly
owing to my short hours of rest last night
as it was very dark & raining, I took
a good long rest — Early Reading —
about 9 took a moonlight stroll down
to Marble Arch + back — Tobed soon
after 11.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 30, 1892

A singularly pleasant day for this
part of the world — cloudless sunshine,
pale & feeble, all day long + moonlight at
night. After some delays after bkfs, I
got started + walked down to the Museum —
There I had a pleasant conversation
with Prof. Colvin who seems anxious to get
for the Museum a good call, if A. W. wood
cut pfs + when I told him that I would
write to Mr. R. W. Gilder on the subject he
said that he would like to write a letter
if I would forward it with mine — & he wrote
me. I looked over the Am. call — it is
a very shabby lot. About 3 o'ck I went out —
wandered through Drury Lane — went
into the lobby of the Theatre — to Covent Garden —
through the markets + past the Royal Opera —
& back to museum soon after 2 and —
until 4 studied Rembrandt portraits —
then walked home — went to Barber's +
home at 5.30 — It was nearly 7 when
we had dinner — Annin had been most
of the day at Hampstead, to some sort of affair,
lunching at the Satchell's.
