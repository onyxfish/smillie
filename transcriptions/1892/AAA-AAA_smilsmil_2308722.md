## Left Page

### December Saturday 31 1892

A brilliant, cloudless, cold day — very
like a mid-winter day at home. I
awoke early in the m'g with a beastly
sore throat, extending down into my chest.
It made me so uncomfortable & apprehensive
that I did not go out of doors all day but
kept closely to our room & nursed a soft
coal fire. About 11 o'clk Eliz. H. came
with her two nieces, Lilley & Hattie Kipling.
They stayed until after 12 — at 12.30 we
had our dejeuner — Tolerable — After that
Annie took the boys to Bois to meet
Eliz. & the Kiplings there & to see the
skating on the Bois Pond. Jim put
on a pair of skates & made his first
effort — It was 5.15 when they got in, very
tired, having walked all the way back.
I did some writing & a good deal of
pacing up & down the room — At 6.30
we had dinner — Rather a poor affair —
& then it was made manifest to me that
I am the only man in the house — about
15 women sat at the table — Mlle. Arneum,
one young English woman & seven or more
Americans. While Annie went to put
the boys to bed I sat awhile in the
Salon with them.
