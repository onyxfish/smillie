## Left Page

### June, Saturday 4, 1892

*Ridgefield*

A dull m'f. - with wind tending to the east — much cooler & threatening a storm — m'f. wrote to Koehler & to Blaney — then, before 11 - to Gerster's. He had engagements preventing his going with me to make choice of one of my paintings. I rode with him in his coupé to my door — lunch about 1 — after that Annie was busy packing Jim's valise so that he could go to Baldwin's on L. I. with Edward M. Cook to spend a week or ten days with his boys. Mary Mooney took him to the station to meet E.M.C. In the haste Jim's valise was left behind. Soon after 3, Annie, Ralph & I went to the Murray Hill Hotel hoping to see the Colmans — not in — then on to G.C. — Station & waited until 4.02 train getting to Ridgefield before 6 — Geo. met us at station & we drove home with him — some wandering over the place before tea — Ev'y. enjoyed the wood five so much that I slept in my chair.     To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 5, 1892

Overcast & cool — thunderstorm during the night with a little rain. Bkf. before 9 — Nellie & Annie went off for a drive — I went to the church across the road from Geo's — a sermon on "self examination", so the preacher said. I thought it was not so much begging as demanding contributions. Out at 12. Annie had returned — she, Geo. & I took a short walk getting back about 1 — dinner soon after — my head very bothersome in the afternoon — I wandered about with Geo. spending most of the time killing worms on the apple trees. I came in before 5 o'clk. with headache & slept until 6. Tea soon after that — Eve'g. most of it looking over & reading Gibson's "Sharp Eyes".     To bed at 10.30.
