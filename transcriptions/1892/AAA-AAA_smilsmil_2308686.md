## Left Page

### October, Thursday 20, 1892

*Reform Club.*

Pleasant day. At bkfs. rec'd. a note from Abbey making a new appointment for me to lunch with him at the Reform Club at 1 5. do. Ab't 1 I dressed, then took Jime to his swimming lesson & after that took cab, getting to Reform Club ab't 1°. Very pleasantly greeted by Abbey & at once went to dining room where we had a lunch of sofo, boiled chicken, salad & bottle of champagne. After that to the smoking room where we chatted for some time — introduced to Colin Hunter & after some further general chatting we three went out and visited several galleries & saw some pictures, wandering on until we got to Hanover Square where I was taken into the Arts Club & proposed for some sort of complimentary memberships — seconded by Mr. Hunter & Mr. Wylie & a third gentleman whose name I did not catch. After we came out Mr. Hunter left us & Abbey & I went on to Oxford St. & into a book store — while there G. H. Boughton came in — we three walked from there a short distance where I parted on Oxford St. — took a bus & got home ab't 4.5 feeling that I had been away on a regular storee. Tea — quiet eveng. reading.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 21, 1892

Pleasant wea. — a smart shower or two in the middle of the day. After bkfs. I went to Goulding's & had a third proving of my plate — I am now entering upon the stage of disappointment and depression. I have got the face, as it seems to me, hopelessly "slicked up", tight & conventional — nothing of the painter-like freedom & freshness that I had hoped to get. I have plenty of color — parts are very heavily over-bitten — so I can bite into it with burnisher, scraper & even Scotch stone — I got back home before 12. Tacked down plate and then went out for a walk before dinner. Got to work before 3 & alt'o' at times it was very dark in the afternoon I kept at work until 5. Then again I went out for a walk. Tea before 7. Eveng. a little reading & then a little writing.

*Morgans H. N.*
