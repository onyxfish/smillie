## Left Page

### September, Sunday 18, 1892

Another delightful day — enjoyed my Sunday m'f, scrib, then had had an hour in the Park with the boys before bkfs. After that I wrote a letter to Herrick — then I went out with Ralph & loafed & sunned myself in the Park until 1.30 then home to a simple but very good dinner — At 3 o'clk. we all started out & by underground R.R. went to Finchly Station, Hampstead & then we went out to the Heath — we walked about a good deal — saw some fine trees & crowds of uninteresting people. I was much interested in the appearance of the Suburban residences near the Heath. After wandering about until after 5 we took a bus into town by Tottenham Court Road to Oxford St — there we met a labor procession with bands of music & many banners — another bus & home at 6.15. Dr. Satchell & daughter Edith had been to call on us. Tea — Ev'f. I wrote a note to Mr. Rob't. Gordon thanking him for his note of Friday giving me information concerning Scarlet Fever — also. Read a little. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 19, 1892

Still very pleasant weather — Jim & I had had an hour in the Park before bkfs — after that I did some necessary shopping before I was able to begin my days work — I was 11 before I got my plate before me & with my burnisher made my first attack since the biting. Dinner about 1.30 — it was after 2.30 when again I got to work — then, as my eyes & head troubled me at 4 I gave up for the day & went to my refuge, the Park. Annie was away with the boys doing some shopping for them & later taking them rowing on the Serpentine. I walked across to Ratten Row & almost to Hyde Park corner — then sauntered back by the edge of the water getting in a little before 6. Annie coming in soon after. Tea at 6.30 — Ev'f. wrote to Nicole.

Morgan 3 h.
