## Left Page

### December, Sunday 11, 1892

Bright for a while in the mf — then a little rain, clearing afternoons. It was about 11 when we all got started for the Thames Embankment at Charing [illegible] & the Tempele Church. For a few minutes at the Marble Arch we gave up, owing to the rain, but the clouds breaking away we went on. Got to Charing & soon after 12 and there lost our way as we tried to get into the Tempele courts & gardens — After some loss of time we got through & to the door of Tempeld Church at 12.30 but the beautifully robed official there scowled & said that it was impossible at that hour to open the door, disturbing the service. A shilling was enough to work a change — he drew aside a curtain & showed us that the door was already open — so we slipped in quietly & sitting in the old circular part alongside of the bronze effigies of the old crusaders, we heard the close of the sermon by the Bishop of Southwark — After service, [illegible] some others, went into the Hall of the Middle Tempele, a very beautiful & interesting old hall, about 600 yrs old. Then out & thro' the gardens to the Embankment & along it to Charing & thence, by stage, home at 2 o'clk. Dinner — Afternoon A— went to a church in the neighborhood & heard some fine music. I stayed in with the boys & moulded a lot of the Morgan head to send to N.Y. Evg [illegible] the a note to J. Pierpont Morgan —

Ralph's temperature was normal this mf & during the day he has seemed very well.

## Right Page

### December, Monday 12, 1892

A nasty mf. Rain — but very pleasant p.m. In the mf I went to the Am. Consul, Mr. Need— 12 St. Helen's Place, Bishopsgate & enquired of him how it was best to send my Morgan [illegible] to J.P.M. To avoid possible Custom House vexations & delays. He said send it through B.S. Morgan & Co — So I went in & saw W. Rob— gordon & he promptly agreed to take charge of it. Then I took 'bus to Strand — Stopped at the law courts & went through all the courts I could get into — among them that of Lord Chief Justice Coleridge — who, for the half hour that I was there, seemed to be asleep in his chair. Then I walked up to Drury Lane Theatre & got tickets for the Xmas Pantomime of Little Bo-Peep — for Tues. P.M. After & was then home in time for dinner — In the afternoon I walked out to Goulding's carrying mounted [illegible] of the Morgan head to guide him in taking more [illegible] tomorrow mf. Then back by 'bus in which I met Jno. Durand — Home at 5 o'clk & rested until tea time — Evg I wrote a short letter to Miss Abbatt —
