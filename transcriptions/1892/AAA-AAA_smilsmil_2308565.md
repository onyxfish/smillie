## Left Page

### February Wednesday 17 1892

Another bright day — but windy,
dirty & cold. It was nearly 10.30
when I got to the studio — Spent
some time fussing over "twelfth-
Night Sketch" for F.H. Smith & then to
work on the study × Painted re-
f doors of "the old dam" — Repainted
most of in — I don't know whether
it will prove to be worth the trouble
or not. Home to lunch at 1.45. A —
at Mary's to lunch — Back to studio
& painted until 4.30 — Cleaned up
& took a walk for an airing — Home
at 5.45 — Fred, Alice & Emma
Ireland to dinner — a pleasant
evening until 9.30 when they
left.

## Right Page

### February Thursday 18 1892

A very pleasant day — to studio
at 10 — Set palette & fussed over the
study of "the old dam" that I painted
out of doors & that I think of offering
to Frank Waller as payment for his
large English easel — Emma Ireland
came in for a few minutes — Home to
lunch & back to studio as usual —
I cut india & dampened [illegible]
for printing to-morrow. Annie
came at about 4 & we went to
Mitchell, Vance & Co's & looked at
some gas fixtures — Then we went
to 20 W. 10th St — to call upon Mr. Ehninger
— not at home — Then to No. 41 & called
upon W. & Mrs. Fred. Dielman — met a
number of pleasant people — I walked
home — dinner after 6 — Evely A. & I
went to the New Music Hall — 57th St. & 7th Ave
— private concert of Rubenstein Club — a
chorus of not less than 100 female voices
under direction of Chapman — well
trained in some respects — lacking, I
think, in refinement. An enjoyable con-
cert — did not get home until 11.30
