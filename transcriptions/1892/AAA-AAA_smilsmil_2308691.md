## Left Page

### October, Sunday 30, 1892

A mild day with weak sunshine through a thin fog — at night near rain — late bkfs — after that I wrote awhile on a letter to Ge[orge]. At 12, Jim, Ralph & I went into the Park for a walk — the band of the Oxford blues was playing & we stood for some time enjoying their excellent music — Home about 1.30 bkft was after 2 when we got our dinner — a brace of Pheasants — small birds, not quite so plump nor so good as a first rate Partridge — It was about 4 d'ck. when Annie, the boys & I went to the Park for a walk — back at 5 — tea at 7 — every. I finished letter to George.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 31, 1892

Another beastly day — fog & wet — so dark that I could not see to work — so, after bkfs — I walked down to the museum, got my ticket as student in the Reading Room & commenced work there by reading Herkomer's new book "Etching & Mezzotint Sup I" until 12.30 taking notes — then walked to Marble Arch & thence home by bus — Dinner — & at once returned to museum & read & made notes — Herkomer, from 3 to 4.30 — then I walked to Duithorne's, Vigo & got a copy of W. Frank Short's little book "The making of Etchings". I was recognized as having been in with Abbey & Hunter — Mr. Duithorne politely presented me with the book. Then I walked to Hanover Square to the Arts Club, from the Sect. of which I had to-day rec'd notice of my election as Honorary member for three months — I had a very pleasant interview with the Sect. who cordially bade me welcome to the Club & its privileges. Then I walked home — not less than six miles in mud & wet to-day on foot. Tea — every. Some reading — wrote two short notes to Yale & Schilling & to bed at our usual time, 11 o'clk — Letter Rec'd from Kate —
