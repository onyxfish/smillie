## Left Page

### August, Monday 15, 1892

*Bayreuth — Rooms of Hans Heuberger —
No. 1 Maximilian Strasse*

Beautiful day, but very hot. B'kp't. At 8.99 — then got
everything ready for our start for Bayreuth — Emma went
with me to P.O. where I got P.O. money order for M. 37.75
& sent it to Belle Vue Hotel, Munich, to pay our supplemental bill sent after me — then to R.R. Station & got
tickets to B— Back to Hotel — left the boys with E—
went to Station at 9.45 — Train ready & we took
our choice of seats — very uncomfortable warm — Lady
Blanche Stewart — daughter & friend occupied the
R.R. carriage with us — an interesting, if not beautiful
specimen of our aristocratic cousins — Miles the
country — got to B— at 12.15 & drove at once to 1 Max.
& R.R. Entrance through tobacco & cheese store — Rooms
— a sitting room & bed Room — very pleasant, on third
floor — Mr. & Miss Benedick friends of Emma, welcomed
us — washed & dusted ourselves & all — went to the
Sonne Hotel & had bad table d'hote dinner — then
back to our Room & waited until 3.15 — went out &
in an Ein Spinner rode to the Opera Ho. on a little
hill just out of town. Ahead of time — so we amused
ourselves walking about looking at people & surroundings — Performance promptly at 4 — first act over at 5.40
when we went out & promenaded about until the
trumpets blew at 6.30 — fine view over the town my
pleasant — 2nd act over at 7.30 — all out again — went
to Restaurant & had supper. At 8.30 3rd act began &
341— 2 — on the back Row middle in front of the boxes —
excellent places — I am seeing & seeing [illegible] example — Arch-
itecturally the best in a very mean affair — dull & heavy —
giving a suggestion of the tombs in Cairo & — accosted
Maestros seem to be good — Kundry by Malten, a portly
middle-aged dame who they say will without [illegible]
me, an obdurate heathen in sympathy with Parsifal
who was represented by Van Dyck, an aldermanic
boy — M. sang charmingly — Gurnemanz by Gregg —
Amfortas by Kaschmann & Klingsor by [illegible] — The
music was all interesting to me — some of it beautiful —
some of it impressive — but of course I did not have a full
understanding of it — The scenery on the whole was
not remarkable — some of it was absurd & childish —
The garden in act I was like an enlarged copy of the cover
of a seedsman's cat, absurdly disproportionate in
size to the Singers — the Temple while not very good
was impressive — The closing scene especially
in its ensemble of music & stage machinery very
noble — I had hoped to be thrilled — awed — but
my density was too great — aétio! At the last moment
I came very near it — I was on my feet instead of
in my knees. We walked home with the crowd —
lamps in our Rooms lighted for us — a chat with Mr.
& Miss B— then A. & I Recklessly indulged in a glass
of Munich beer, but did not drink it all — to bed at 1.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 16, 1892

Hot — cloudless day — B'kp't — & coffee & rolls
arrived in our rooms at 8 — after which we got
to R.R. station in a very leisurely way & in
spite of a rush, aided by my experience, I
was able to secure two good seats on the
shade side — but these German (& Bavarian)
cars are an unmitigated abomination in
hot weather — After a dirty ride (having for
companions three Jews, presumably Philadelphia —
foolish, frivolous things) we got to N. in time —
1.45 — & went at once to Hotel & found the boys
& Emma & Mary up at the dinner Table —
E. & M. had to rush to catch their train
1.45 — for Bayreuth, where they are to spend
the rest of the week & hear four operas — our
dinner, owing to the great crowd, was long
drawn out, being near 3 o'clk when we
got through — well being very tired & the
weather too hot for walking, I took a
long nap but got up after 5 feeling none
the better for it — Before 6 we all went
into Town & after wandering about for
some time stopped at the Cafe Metropole
& had our early meal — After 8 when
the boys got to bed & I soon followed.
