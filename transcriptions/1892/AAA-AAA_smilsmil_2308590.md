## Left Page

### April, Saturday 9, 1892

Still pleasant weather. Yale came in before 8 o'clk. soon followed by Dr. Gerster. They expressed their great gratification at my getting along absolutely without nausea — a very unusual thing — and I had no trouble in passing urine — but, nevertheless, I was extremely wretched. Dr. G. came about noon & again before bed time. He is extremely considerate & attentive. My nurse, Frank Kochanek, is a willing, stupid fellow. The day was a very wretched one for me — pain & fever — temperature getting up to 103⁸⁄₁₀ — pulse 102.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 10, 1892

Slight improvement in condition — a little less temperature (101⁸⁄₁₀) and less pain — Dr. Gerster came about noon & gave me my first dressing, removing the worrying bands & simplifying the arrangement by the substitution of a T band.
