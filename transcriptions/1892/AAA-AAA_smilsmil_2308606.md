## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 11, 1892

*Annual meeting & Dinner. N.A.f.D.*

Heavy rain in the m'f. — dry but clouded p.m. — rain again at night.
Got to studio & worked hard all the m'f taking down hanging closets in large closet & making
some advance in the hopeless work of preparations for "moving."
Home to lunch at 1 — at 2 Geo. &
I went to N.A.f.D. Business meeting until after 6 — a dull & monotonous
affair in spite of the fact that the two Sec'ys. resigned & declined a re-nomination — T. A. Richards — after 40 years
of office — H.W. Robbins after 10 years —
J.C. Nicoll was elected to take the place
of Richards & Geo. succeeds Robbins.
Sat down to dinner at about 7 — a tolerably good dinner — but desperately "slow" — the only incident being a pleasantly reminiscent farewell
from Richards — Geo. & I left at
10 — got home at a. ¼ & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 12, 1892

Brilliant m'f. — overcast afternoon.
Spent the m'f at studio taking books
out of big case & putting them into packing boxes to take home. Annie quite used up with a sick headache — so that she was obliged to recall invitations
she had sent out for a family dinner.
Geo. & I came home to lunch at 1 —
After that I rode directly down to
K. & V.'s in Canal S'. & got Jade plate
& four p's on excessively thin Jap. paper.
Then to studio & worked there until
6 o'clk. Then home to dinner. Annie feeling uncomfortable but able to be
up & at the dinner table. Eve'g
I started out with Geo. to go to Eyb[illegible]
& Soc'y of Am. Artists, but feeling too
tired I set home & spent part of the
eve'g in reading a lot of old letters from
S. R. Koehler, destroying most of them.
