## Left Page

### November, Thursday 3, 1892

Mild & very pleasant — cloudless morning light at nights. After 6½p — I changed my
working quarters into our front bed room
where I have more room & a better light.
I have seriously injured my plate by trying to work at such close quarters in so
small a room — I could not properly judge
of relations of light & shade — a few days
ago I stoned down a part of the face, thinking that it was too dark, I now find that
it really was not dark enough. I spent
a part of the n'f working up one of the [plates]
that I got yesterday with crayon — now I
will have to re-work the entire face.
At 11.30 I went with Jim & Ralph to the
Swimming School — but no why to there —
so we went to Whiteley's & bought some chum
mushrooms & I worried over plates & proofs
until dinner — after that I worked until
3.30 then I walked to Blackman's Studio
but he was not in — I wandered about
through the streets — beautiful twilight —
fine sunset & rising full moon — I en-
joyed, feeling very stupid I loafed

Morgan. 3 h.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 4, 1892

Got up with a wretched head ache that
quite used me up for the day — Pleasant
n'f… beastly rain & wind in the after-
noon — After 6½p — I rode down to the
Museum & spent the rest of the n'f in
the Print Room — a pleasant chat with W.
Tagan — looked over a lot of Wm Unger's
plates — Dinner at 1 — Suinie went to a
"Pop" (short for Popular) Concert. Sarasate
I spent part of the afternoon in the bed.
Toward ev'g, in the rain, went to
Ropes who sent word that he had a
very desirable window on the Strand
for the Lord Mayor's Show next Wed.
Ev'g I wrote to Mielatz my resignation as Chairman of Chicago E[tching] Club; also a note to Mr. Benj. Austin
of Oak Cliff, Texas, who has been trying
to get autographs of Father & Will.
