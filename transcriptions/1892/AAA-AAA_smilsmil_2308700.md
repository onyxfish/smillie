## Left Page

### November, Thursday 17, 1892

Overcast, but dry — Towards eve'g almost pleasant — Worked all the m't on Morgan plate & got it into condition for a proof — At 12.45 went out for a short walk. Promptly after dinner I went to Goneding getting there at 2.30 — The men at lunch, could not get a pf. before 3 — so I walked down to Hammersmith Bridge for the fun of the thing — crossed the bridge & back at 3.10. Caisse took one pf. & that showed me what I wanted — Back home soon after 4 — Tacked down my pf. & then sat for some time studying it — I feel much encouraged. Then took a rest before tea — Eve'g much distressed by Heartburn or Acidity of stomach, a trouble that has bothered me very much lately. Rec'd a pleasant note from Mr. Fagan telling me that he has some fine old papers for me at the Museum & that he severs his connection there next Saturday next. Wrote part of a letter to Charl before going to bed!

Morgan — H'l 2 n.

## Right Page

### November, Friday 18, 1892

*St. James' Hall "Elijah"*

A very pleasant day, (for London) I went to the museum to see Mr. Fagan. Found that Prof. Colvin has returned & had a very pleasant interview with him. I had taken a number of my pfs. to show to Mr. F— there I first showed to Prof. C— to whom I gave 3 or 4 pfs. for the museum call. Then Mr. F— looked over them & was very complimentary — I gave him three & he gave me a lot of very old paper. At 1 I went to the Arts club & got lunch & after that back to museum, where I looked over some Rembrandt to compare — very carefully the pfs. of the 100 guilder plate — and a pf. of [illegible] Flemeng's copy d'[illegible] — At 4 o'clk walked home & had a rest before tea time — After that A— & I went to St. James's Hall to a performance of the Oratorio "Elijah" given by the Royal Society of Musicians of Great Britain — Interesting but not a great performance — Santley sang Elijah with excellent effect considering his years, and was very warmly applauded — Miss Thelia Wilson was the very stout contralto — Miss Anna Williams, the Soprano. It was after 11 when we got home & midnight when we got to bed.
