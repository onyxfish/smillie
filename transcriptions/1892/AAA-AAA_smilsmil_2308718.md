## Left Page

### December, Friday 23, 1892

A pleasant day, beautifully clear at
night. I got away soon after b'kfst — first to Art
Club & while waiting for the Sec'ty, wrote a letter
to R. Swain Gifford asking him to contribute
etchings of his to the collection that I hope to
be able to gather for the Brit. Mus. As the Sec't.
delayed his coming I left & in Oxford S. met
Annie & the boys. As they were shopping I left
them & went to Newmans — finished up my business
there & paid my bill — a long operation as I had
to have my chat with old Mr. Mills — Then I
went to the museum & to and my good byes
to Dr. Garnett, Prof. Colvin & others — I spent
some time at Bookstones near the B.M. trying to
find a copy of W. Jacque's little book giving some
acct of the Rt. It & Drawing Coll. of the museum,
but without success — Home to dinner at 1.30.
About 3 A — & I went to Tooth's Gallery, Hay Mkt —
& for some time looked at the pictures & at th
there, then we parted — I went to the Strand
along Fleet S. to Fetter lane & in West Harding
S., a little court off 7 — lane, at Hughes & Kimber
a largeestal [illegible], I got Copper Plates, varnish &
some strange tools — a "Shading Knife" & Shading
Tool — Then to a Photo. material place on the
Strand & got a tray — Then up the Strand down
to Oxford S — & by bus got home at 6.15 — Tea —
Well, looking over my purchases — putting
my water-color traps in order, etc — & with
Annie, looking over the presents prepared
for the boys —

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 24, 1892

A cloudless, cold, pleasant day — After b'kfst
I went up to Dr. Earl's & paid him — Then to
several of the small shops about — Then I
matted Morgan plate & waxed it. Then by
c.c. to Bishopsgate S — & to Morgan's, where I
left the plate. W. Gordon, not in — Then to
[illegible] Johnston & Son — 39 Great Tower S. and
arranged to have them send to the house
next Thurs. for a trunk that we will pack to
leave with them until our return — Then
home at 1 — Dinner — about 2.45, Annie, the
boys & I went to the Satchell's at Hampstead
where some neighbor children were invited
to meet our boys — They had games & a good
time until after 5 — Then we all had tea —
Dr. S — did not make his appearance —
It was near 8 before we got away & fully
8.30 when we got home — I spent a
little time over newspapers & then to bed.
