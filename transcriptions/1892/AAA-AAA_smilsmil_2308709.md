## Left Page

### December Monday 5, 1892

Another clear, cold day — It was after
11 when I got to the museum & started
again upon the early Italian engravers
giving most of the time to Marc antonio
Raimondi — I was very conscientious
about it altho' I am beginning to fear
that this is not the wisest use of my
time — Perhaps it would be better for me
to study the works of Masters nearer to
the century. I also looked over some
of the works of the early German masters.
At 1.30 I went out & after wandering
about for some time went to Fine Arts
Club & lunched. — Then took buss &
rode down Fulham Road & walked
to Frank Short's studio — not in — much
disgusted — walked home — 4.30 — Then
went out & attended to a number
of little matters — mending of trunk
— sketch-knife bag — etc. etc — Tea
at 6.30 — Eveg. studying a new Hand
Book (Murrays) of the Riviera, just
got by Annie.

## Right Page

### December Tuesday 6, 1892

A beastly mfs. — snow & sleet — heavy
rain & no end of mud & dark withal.
In the afternoon it cleared overhead.
I walked down to the museum thro'
a nasty mess of weather & spent the
time until 2 o'clk. looking over the
works of early german engravers with
a good deal of enjoyment. I like them
much more than the early Italian.
Then I went out & walked down Holborn
to Barnard's Inn — back to Holborn Rest. —
where I had dinner — then to museum
where I looked over Schongauer's work
until 4 — then museum closed — Then
to Soho Sq. Newman's (24) where I bought
a water-color box — Home at 5 '5. Rested
for an hour — then tea. Eveg.
wrote a letter to George.
