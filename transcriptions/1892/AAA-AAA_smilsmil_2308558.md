## Left Page

### February Wednesday 3, 1892

A pleasant, cloudy, breezy day,
growing cooler. To studio at 10.20
worked a little on Rey plate & got
ready to take a proof — one before
dinner — a complete failure — ink
not right — then home to lunch
& back — took three more proofs —
the last one being pretty good —
After washing up I went down to
Keppel's to make some enquiries
concerning lantern slides — Had
quite a visit with him & other
members of his establishment.
Then to Radtke & Sauckner's, about
a p.p. from Dielman's Dry-point plate
Bald Head. Then to Bridgeman's
& got Seed Catalogue — then home
at 6.20 — dinner — Eve. Annie
& I went to Waller's — Mr. & Mrs. Sprague
& Ed & Mary there — I left A. and
walked to N.A. of D. & for a few moments was in the library where Mr.
Dant. Huntington was delivering an
address concerning some of the early
Am. Painters — in the Gallery for a short
time — then back to Waller's — then
until 10.30 — then home & to bed.

## Right Page

### February Thursday 4, 1892

A bright pleasant day — After bfst
went to the Ortgies Gallery. Fifth Ave.
bt. 34 & 35 St. & spent some time looking
over the collection of pictures, oil & W.C.,
by Geo. H. Smillie & J.W. Champney —
they had worked until 2 o'clk. this m't
to arrange them — Geo's contribution I
thought looked very well — they are to
be sold next week. Then to studio — and
in to see Louis Tiffany — Spent some time
with him looking at glass & planning for
the front door at home — It was after 11
when I got to studio — Burnished on Rey
plate until 1 — then home to lunch —
After that rode down to Chas. Platt's Studio
& looking over his portfolio, selected an
etching which I bought — then to studio
at 4 — At 5 walked up to Geo. Rockwell's
photo. establishment & made enquiries of
him concerning the making of lantern
slides for a talk on etching before the N.
A. of D. Home at 6 — dinner — Eve.
spent some time laying out a plan
in glass for front-door — to bed at 10
