## Left Page

### January, Sunday 10, 1892

Still raw & disagreeable altho' drier than yesterday — Blfo —
at the usual Sunday time —
9 o'clk. I spent some time
in doing small chores about the
house — dinner at 2 — At 3 I
took Katie to the Century to see
the Twelfth Night pictures — after
that I went down to studio
& then in to see Coleman, who
has been very sick — saw Mr.
C— also Mr. & Mrs. Kirkham. Home
at 6 — tea — Eve'g Mr. Wm. Mugford
(son of Elisha) & Mr. Emmons called
to see the young ladies. At 9
I proposed that all go around
to Charl's Room as I knew that
he was waiting for them — they
went — when I finished letters
to Herrick & others I followed —
There until near 11 — then home

## Right Page

### January, Monday 11, 1892

A little more disagreeable again.
Raw & wet — Got to studio at 10.30
found Gel. Kelley waiting for me &
he used up an hour & a half fun
time — It mattered little however
as I was so much out of sorts as to
be unfit for work — I went to 2°
nat. Bk. & arranged some of A's
business — then home and after
lunch gave up & spent the rest
of the afternoon in the bed. The
Adams girls & Katchen went to
Germantown by 3 o'clk. Trains —
Fanny Kimball lunched with
us & spent the afternoon with Annie
& Kate. Dinner at 6 — Kate spent
the eve'g with Charl — I Read
"Timothy's Quest" by Mrs. Kate
Wiggin — a clever, readable
little story — finished it just as
Kate came in. To bed at 11.30
