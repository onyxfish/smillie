## Left Page

### December Tuesday 27, 1892

*[Written at top of page: Drury Lane Theatre - Xmas Pantomime]*

Another cloudless, frosty day, but with considerable, smoky fog at times — and milder. Immediately after b'kp. I hurried to Goulding's — but he had his family, just out of town. Got home at 11 & packed for an hour! Soon after 12 a lunch was ready for us & at 1 we all got away for Drury Lane & the Pantomime — which began promptly at 1.30 & from that time on, without end or cessation it ran until 10 m. before 6 — It was a wonderful medley of bodily and nonsense — cleverness & absurdity — Cheap pence & very small beer — At one time rising to the highest degrees of spectacular excellence & at other times, no higher than the level of the youngsters who formed a large part of the audience — The lavish display in every direction was a continuous astonishment to me. The no. of people engaged — the costliness of the costumes, the character of some of the stage setting — all seemed without limit. The Hall & mirrors was an amazing display — immense mirrors lined each side of the stage & reflected interminably the numberless electric stars that glittered in the ceiling of the Palace Hall and the beautiful & the grotesque figures that crowded its floor & the immense street cast rising at the back — The set piece tive. Ballet danced by a dozen or more houris in various colored robes was a thing delightful to look upon — The Harliquinade that followed was a very childish thing — two were on the programme but the second one was cut & in it in its place we had a family of acrobatic tumblers who did wonders, especially a small boy who was tossed about as though made of rubber — We got home at 6.30 & soon had a good dinner — The boys recovered normally from the excitement & soon went to sleep. I sat by the fire, very tired, and read over the libretto & made notes in it.

## Right Page

### December Wednesday 28, 1892

A very disagreeable day — foggy & below freezing — an atmosphere like that of a cellar. After b'kp. I went to Victoria R.R. Station & got tickets for Paris — Then to Fine Arts Club — Secretary not in — then to Oxford St. & got art materials & other things — To Mudie's with A's last book — To British museum & saw Prof. Calvin & then home at 1.30 — Dinner — After 3 went out to Shepherd's Bush R? to Mr. H. Goulding's & got a few last hints for myself — & paid his bill. Then home & attended to a few last little matters & after 5 set myself about final packing — tea at 6.30 — Wed. I worked hard until after 10 when I became desperate & told Annie that it was of no use, I could not get all of my things into my trunk — we must give up going to-morrow, to which she promptly assented & we went to bed.
