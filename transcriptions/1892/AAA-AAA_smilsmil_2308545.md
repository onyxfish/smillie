## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 6, 1892

*Twelfth Night — Century.*

An old fashioned snow storm in the m'f. — 3 or 4 in. of snow fell — then some sleety rain. Got to studio a little after 10. Spent some time getting things put away — then set palette & commenced to paint on Chas's pictures & by noon I had a new sky on it. Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — Gifford spent the p.m. with me working on his plate for Etching Club Cat. I painted on Chas's picture. Anthony came with p'f's from three photo gravure plates. Home at 6, dinner. About 8.30 I went to the Century Club & was there until after 1 o'clk. A.M. The greatest lark at the Century in my experience — fun of all sorts — charades, songs, recitations & dances — Young Farragut (mis 45 years old) appeared as Carmencita the Spanish dancer & was the sensation of the even'f. It is impossible here to give any account of the entertainment. I got home about 1.30 & found myself locked out — managed after some hard work to arouse Annie & got to bed soon after 2.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 7, 1892

Brilliant m'f., overcast afternoon. Up at the usual time feeling fairly well. Got to studio at 10 & from there went to DeVinne's — saw W. Hopkins & left Etching M.S. with him — then to Kimmel & Voigt's & from there to the Moss Eng. Co. & left the Etcher's autographs to be photo-eng'd. Then home by elevated R.R. a few min. before 12. Went with Annie, Hatcher, the Adams girls & Ralph to the Century & spent an hour looking at the Twelfth night collection of pictures. Men home to lunch. Afternoon, they all went out & I had a good nap. Even'f we had a little dinner party. Our family & visitors, Geo. & Emily Cook — Jack Downs, Young Mulford (Elisha's son) young Sackett — an English cub whom none of us had seen before — things seemed to run very smoothly. At 10 they left us behind was 11.30 before we got to bed.
