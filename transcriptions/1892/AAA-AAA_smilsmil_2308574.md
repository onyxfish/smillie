## Left Page

### March Sunday 6 1892

Bright & sunny but the breezes were chilly. Bkfst late — then some time with the Sunday News paper — It was after 12 o'clk. when with Jim & Ralph I went out on to Park Ave. There was no sense of healing in the raw March wind that made the sensitive membranes of head, throat & chest tingle as I breathed it in. We went to cor. & 34 & 5 — Robt. Collyer's Ch. — heard the close of his sermon & after service met Annie as she came out. Then we all went to the Century Club & met many peoples that we knew. I was interested to see how my little painting "An old dam" looked — It was commended. Then home and dinner at 2. After that did not go out again but divided my time squeezes between Reading, writing & doing nothing — Annie went with the boys to Grace Ireland's. Evg. Charly came in & Annie He & I talked of a possible trip to Europe, beginning some time next June. To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March Monday 7 1892

Bright & sunny & much milder — [illegible] feeling very much out of sorts — It was 11 o'clk. before I could get up pluck enough to go to the studio — Then packed two pkfs. of the Sauderis Rey Mission together with other portraits of Durand — Aqua-tint. "An old Dam" & "What o'clk is it?" & then took the pkf. to Adam's Exps. for Mr. Vickery. Then to Yale's — He kept me waiting some time before he saw me & then some time while he was seeing me — my condition did not seem to alarm him. It was 2.20 when I got home to lunch. After that wrote to Vickery & to Central Trust Co. — It was nearly 4 when I got to studio — & 5 when I left for home. Evg. Reading Rudyard Kipling
