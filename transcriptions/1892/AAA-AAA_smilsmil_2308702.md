## Left Page

### November Monday 21, 1892

A dull, foggy day with barely light
enough for me to see to work — I was
able to put in 3½ hours on the Morgan
plate & had two or three exercise
walks — At about 4 o'clk. Annie
& I went to the Blackwares — 10
Cheniston Gardens & made our call
in seltron for our dinner — B. came
in while we were there & we had
a very pleasant visit. A. returned
by R.R. & I walked home.
Eve'g. finished letter to Mr. Koehler.

Morgan — 3½ h.

## Right Page

### November Tuesday 22, 1892

A dull day — no fog — but dark &
dry with disagreeable S.E. wind —
walked up to Gouldings & found
a young man ahead of me getting
a plate proved — I had to wait until
11.30 before Cope got a chance to take
hold of my plate — then he pulled
a satisfactory print & I got home be-
fore 1 o'clk. After dinner — nearly
3 o'clk — Annie & I walked across the
Park — she went to call on the Misses
Abbott — Miss Ferguson & Mrs. Wood-
ward & her daughters — I went on to
Mauresa R² & had a very pleasant
call on W. Frank Short — saw some of
his work & talked with him about
aqua-tint & mezzotinting — walked
home, getting in at 5 — Eve'g.
I wrote a long letter to Mcall.

Morgan 2½ h.
