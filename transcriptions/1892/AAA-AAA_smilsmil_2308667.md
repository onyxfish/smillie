## Left Page

### September, Monday 12, 1892

Dull, sunless day — depressing to me. After
bkp. I went to Morgan & Co's — old Broad St —
& after drawing money & getting a letter from
Kate & one for A. from "Ily" — Home I saw Mr.
Rob. Gordon — He rec'd me very cordially
— assured me that Kensington near the
gardens, was the part of town that we should
seek our lodgings in — It was noon when
I got back to hotel — Mr. Satchell called
to see A — after dinner A. & I went to the
museum — I presented my letter of intro-
duction from W. J. Linton to Dr. Garnett —
we wished to make some enquiries concern-
ing Moncure Conway — learned nothing —
then A. & I. to hotel & I took cab & drove out
to Bedford gardens to see Acl. Parsons — He is
in Japan — learned there that Abbey and
wife live out of town — went to Boughton's — He
too out of town — gave it up — drove to Piccadilly
Circus & called also on a co. of House agents &
enquired for lodgings — They promised
to send us a list tomorrow — Then I took
bus & returned to hotel by way of Strand
quite disheartened. — Tea afterly — Eve.
in Reading Room — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 13, 1892

Cloudy & breezy m't. — considerable sunshine
& less breeze in the afternoon. After bkp. we all
went to Emma & Mary one's quarters — No 6 upper
Bedford Place — Russell Sq. — There for some time
— then to house agents, Wood & Kirby — Theobald's
Road — Cor. Great James St. They promised to
let us have a list of lodgings to-morrow m't.
then we walked back to the hotel & I spent
sometime trying to find some Brome-Caffiene —
wrote a little while on letter to Chard & then
had dinner — After that Annie & Jim went
off to spy out the land about Notting Hill —
while I finished my letter to Chard. Then,
write Ralph I went out to Leadgate Hill &
took a bus top seat to Trafalgar Sq. — Spent
an hour & a half in the National Gallery
making a general sort of re-acquaintance
with the pictures there — Before long we rode
back — Annie had returned shortly
before us — She reported a great amount
of riding back & forth in cabs & busses but
nothing accomplished.
