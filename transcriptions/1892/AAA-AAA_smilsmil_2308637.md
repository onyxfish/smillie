## Left Page

### July, Thursday 14, 1892

Up & down — in bed & out of it at almost all hours from midnight until 7 when at last I got up for all day — The clouds hung low & at times rain fell freely — Bkf. about 8 — we sat about, trying in various ways to kill time — I tried to write a letter but feeling sleepy, I went up to my room & had a series of naps until 1 o clk. when we had lunch — after that, the clouds lifted somewhat & we determined to attempt a little tramp — at 2:30 A, E, M, the boys & I started off — unless those an hour & a half — taking things easy we reached the foot of the Riffle horn where there is a grand view of Monte Rosa & attendant mts — the great white Rosa glacier & five other glaciers running into it — Emma & Marjorie determined to push on to the former gap much higher up — Annie & I stayed behind and watched the clouds for more than an hour as they slowly lifted & sank again — opening & closing — now letting the sunshine lie brilliantly upon the glittering snow or after so that all but the utmost points were visible — It was most fascinating — and I gazed up only when my tired eyes turned upon me with a furious headache — I studied the glaciers & their moraines with greatest interest. The flowers were delightful & Ralph could not give attention to anything else. We got back to the house at about 5 — at 5:30 A. gave the boys their supper & at 6:15 we had our dinner — three for a time by the stove & to bed at 9 —

## Right Page

### Hotel Cervin, Room 107. Zermatt.

### July, Friday 15, 1892

Up dressed soon after 7 — cold dismal mist — snow falling — no fire anywhere to warm me — so I went back to bed — Before Ralph got up he complained that his stomach ached like fury — while his mother was dressing him he vomited freely — what seemed like pure water — after this he seemed quite prostrated — it was impossible for us to carry out our plan of going to Zermatt in time to take 1st train and on it hurrying to night — So Emma & Marjorie decided to wait & we would await developments — as the day advanced the weather improved & by 11 we decided to go down as far as the Riffle Alps at any rate. Guides were engaged, one to carry Ralph — the other to carry our luggage — By 12 the weather was clearing charmingly & settled with sunshine — a pretty big tale — But that had to be accepted in such a place — a place only for the strong & stout. There are no unnecessary comforts & the immediate surroundings are not lovable — Started promptly at 12 — Ralph looking meek & miserable carried by a sturdy, fine looking fellow — we got down to the Riffle Alps in about half an hour & arrived with the guides to go on with us after an hours rest — Annie lunched at table d'hote while I sat in the salon & held Ralph — when she came out I had an excellent beef steak and potatoes — at 2 we started down — & got to Hotel Mr. Cervin in exactly one hour quarter hour — at 3:15 — got into our room — in the attic with fine views of the Matterhorn, as promptly as possible — The sky had cleared gloriously — Ralph was evidently much used up & I was very anxious — Temp. 104 — pulse 130 — face flushed — Head hot & feet cold — we got him to bed as soon as we could & gave him a strong dose of Rhubarb & Soda — & got bottles of hot water at his feet — at intervals also, gave him 3 grain doses of aconite — He was well asleep but two hours the Rhubarb went through him while he was yet asleep making a nasty mess of himself in the bed — he was washed & got into a clean nightgown and repeated the same thing about two hours later after which he slept quietly through the night — The evening views of the Matterhorn exceedingly beautiful — almost a cloudless sky — To bed at 9.
