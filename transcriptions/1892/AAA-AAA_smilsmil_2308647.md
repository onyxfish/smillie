## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 3, 1892

Clouds lying low all the mf — did not
lift entirely all day — but towards eve
some charming glimpses of snow peaks
above the valley were given — In the mf
Annie, the boys & I walked down the valley
toward Interlaken on the carriage road
It was very pleasant, the air being bracing
on our return the sun shone out so warm
that it was uncomfortable — In the afternoon, at 3 o'clk — A. J. & I walked to
the Trummel bach falls — 3/4 of an hour
to the house at the foot — a few minutes
further is the gorge — a small brook has
worked its way back into the cliff by ero-
sion, forming some very curious baskets
for itself — near the bottom it shoots out
of a hole as though projected from a
cannon — This is an interesting little
hole — but a very small bore in a
place like this — we walked across
the meadows & bridges to the road on
the other side of the valley & had some
very fine views — getting back to hotel
a little before 6 — Ralph had stayed
with the Atwater s — he has had a slight
bowel disturbance this afternoon —
The boys had their meal at 6 — we
had dinner at 7 — To bed at 9.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 4, 1892

★ Brilliant mf not a cloud on the mt — Tops —
we went to the station of the cable road up to Mürren
at 9.45 — To our intense disgust we found a great
crowd, the collection of the last few days of cloud
storm, a train load of people had just come up
from Interlaken holding through tickets to Mürren was
given precedence — D. walters in Sauterbrunnen had
its chance — the Casse remained closed, no tickets
could be bought — the one car could seat but thirty
persons — ten more could stand upon a platform for
baggage — a trip could not be made in less than
forty minutes — there was a crowd of perhaps a
hundred — I saw that at best we could not get
up until 12 o'clk — too late to reach Mürren for lunch
— so furious at having to lose so exceptional a day
for such a trip I gave it up — A & I joined the
boys & Helen A's girls & we climbed a trail
partway up the mt — I went on along & got some
charming views — back to hotel before lunch —
• Shortly after 2 o'clk we went to Mürren R.R. Station.
The crowd was gone & we had a chance to give to
the station master a piece of our minds concerning the
management of the road for making it practically
impossible for those who stay at Sauterbrunnen to get
up to Mürren any fine mf, without first going to
Interlaken & getting through tickets from there — we
offered no advice or plan of remedy — only expressed
the condition of things — The result was that after he
had advised us concerning the excellence of patience
he ordered a special trip up for us so as to detach
the Interlaken 3.05 train upon which, no doubt,
there would be another crowd of people — we saw
up the cable part — to Grütschalp — in 3.30 m — then
got into an electric trolley car & ran to Mürren in 25
m — Some of the way up on the edge of precipices from
which we could look into frightful depths — at Mürren
was surprised to find two very large hotels where I
am told an English & American colony are established —
The older one is upon the edge of an immense cliff over
which it seems ready to leap — The panorama & snow
topped mts is vast but was much covered by clouds
that had been gathering Saturday since noon — we
wandered about until near 5 when we went to the
R.R. Station — about 3/4 of an hour before schedule time
a crowd already there & another special — in fact
a car was run just as frequently as possible in disregard
of time table — back to hotel at 6.30, gratified
by the much that we have seen, but not yet rid
of from the disappointment of having lost this,
no fault of our own, so gloriously clear a morning.
