## Left Page

### June, Friday 24, 1892

*miles made 367*

Bright — f, but somewhat misty. Also passed one of the Azores "Graciosa" but a few miles south of us — could see the houses — the mountain tops covered by clouds. At noon we passed "Tercira" a larger island, where the Cruiser Alabama got her men & armament — It was very interesting — many passengers took possession of "the bridge" — the sun shone out brilliantly — the water was a lovely color & the island with its cliffs along shore — its volcanic heights inshore — its houses & cultivated fields — was a delight. — wrote awhile — after lunch had a nap — when I went on deck old Mr. Brown of Annapolis asked me to join a whist party — I accepted reluctantly, thinking it was a lady's party — but worse yet, I was taken to the Smoking Room & there we played a game of 25 points. our side won — 25 to 18, I was glad to escape. It was too late to do anything but wait for dinner — Every there was a dance on the after deck — with electric lights and draping flags & the band — The night was lovely — very still & starlight. — The young people had a very good time & danced away like mad from 9 to 11:30 — I turned in at 11.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 25, 1892

*miles made 365*

A very changeable day — m[ost] very warm shimmery — wind changing to cooler & mist thickening. I longed for more islands to look at — my head was heavy with rheumatic pains & the day was very monotonous. — I did some painting in the m[ornin]g on "Etching" — but it was hard work, — and again a little more in the afternoon. Sat through a long dinner — then a walk on deck — a chat with the Mitchells — then A— & I went below where we read for an hour and then turned in. —
