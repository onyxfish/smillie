## Left Page

### October, Monday 10, 1892

A large share of sunshine to day &
a tiny little shower or two. Jim & I
had a run in the Park before bkp:
& Ralph & I had a short outing after
bkp: I got to work at 10.30 & worked
until near 12 — Just as I was starting
with J. & R. for swimming school, Mr
& Mrs. The[o] Satchell called — I excused
myself & left them to Annie. They
invited us to dinner next Thurs. p.m.
after the swimming lesson, we had a
run in the Park & home to dinner after
one — a pkg. of photos &c. from Sarony — a
letter from Kate & a very pleasant one from
Mr. Kent expressing the great pleasure
the family have enjoyed on the Montrose
place. I worked from 2.45 to 4.30 — at and
after 5 where A. & I went out for a walk —
most of it in Hyde Park — in at about 6 —
tea — Every. I gave my Etching Club
paper its last reading — & then sealed it
up & addressed it to Yale.

Morgan 3.15 h.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 11, 1892

Bright, sunny day — very few clouds and
little wind. The first really dry, en-
joyable day for some time. — Before
bkp: the boys & I had a run in the
Park — cold, grass covered with white frost.
After bkp: I took Ralph in to see Dr. Earle
residing a few doors from us — R— has been
out of sorts for some days — with a temperature
one or two degrees above normal. — Dr.
[illegible] was to me rather prepossessing — thinks
the trouble results from an inactive liver
that misbehaves on acc't. of the beastly
London weather. I worked from 10.30 to
12.30 — then out & enjoyed a good, brisk
walk. Home at 1.15 — dinner at 1.45 — to
work again at 2.45 & at it until 4.30 —
then out & had a long walk — through
Kensington Gardens to the Albert Mem.
then to Hyde Park corner — there, through
Park Lane to the Marble Arch, thence through
home getting in at 6.15 — tea — Evening
I wrote a note to Schilling about the
Etching Club M.S. that I sent to Yale.

Morgan 3.45 h.
