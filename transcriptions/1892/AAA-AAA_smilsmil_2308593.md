## Left Page

### April, Friday 15, 1892

A dull m't. with some snow flakes,
a little rain & much running water from the
melting slush. Towards noon 'it grew
lighter & by ev'g it had cleared beautifully.
Started frank upon my massage at 7 o'clk.
Then bk'p. after being dressed. The
sag wear misery of the nausea was gone
through with about 11 o'clk — & then I had
a little rest. Then Dr. Gerster came in
& made a very pleasant call expressing
his gratification at my progress. Had
a good dinner before 2. — Bay from H.
& V. for another signed pf. from San Luis Rey
plate, under Vickery orders. Annie again
read aloud from "Cranford", Chas. Collins
called — & so did Geo. Tea at 7. Ev'g
I read some & Annie read aloud awhile
from "Cranford". With [illegible] at 100 &
temperature at 100⁸⁄₁₀° I didn't feel
at all comfortable. To bed at about
10.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 16, 1892

*Mary & her jues & Kate arr. from P'k.*

Bright. with sunshine & April clouds. I
got up at about 8 after a comfortable night
& had my bk'p. in the Parlor. Tonight
a disagreeable fight with my bowel evening
& after that had to lie down for a rest —
At about 11 I went out with Ralph for a
short walk — sauntered up Park Ave. nearly
to 40th St — then home & found Wm. Parke
waiting for me. He sat chatting until 12.30.
Dinner at 1.30 — after which I took a
rest. About 3.30 Mary & her two girls &
Kate, from P'k, arr'd. The rest of the
afternoon visiting with them. Geo.
came in at about 6. Before 7
we had tea — Ev'g, Annie read
aloud awhile from "Cranford". To bed
at about 10. Temp. Then 99⁶⁄₁₀ — Pulse, 90.
