## Left Page

### May, Friday 27, 1892

Some promise of clearing weather
- but we were disappointed - the
wind blew chilly & raw & in the
afternoon & evening we had cold
showers. Bkfst. about 8 — after that
I wandered over the place until
10 - then started Harrick off looking
for more helps to clean house & I
went down town with Jim & Ralph
I met everybody & had to stop & speak
with every body — so, it was after 12
when I got home — after 1 o'clk we
had a good Roast Beef dinner - then
I wrote to Donnell & Co. for lamp chimnies
& wicks - & to S.R. Koehler — then with
Ralph, I went to P.O. & again spent
much time by the way — Tea soon
after 6 — Quiet, lounged awhile
with Annie — then gave attention
to my foot.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 28, 1892

Some clouds in the m'f. clearing
into a bright, breezy day — glorious
afternoon & lovely night. — Bkfst. about
8 & then spent the day in work of
all sorts about the house & over the
place — Annie & I went down town
before noon & did some shopping.
Home to dinner at 1 o'clk. The
afternoon was spent doing much
the same sort of general work as
was done in the m'f. It was
near 6 when I again went down
town — got back very tired — my foot
hurting me very much — It was 6.45
when Annie & I went to Agur Lathrap's
to tea — a very nice repast — but killing
from my point of view — Annie enjoyed
it. At 8, Agur, Florence, Annie & I
went down town to the Special meeting
of the Village Improvement Society. It
was held in the G.A.R. hall above Bostwick
& Corwin's grocery. — It had been very
imperfectly announced & few came — About
8.30 I called the meeting to order, & after getting
the thing in running order I vacated my seat
in favor of the Vice Prest. Mr. A.B. Smith — In due
time I presented my resignation as Prest.
There were flattering remarks — Agur &
Johnson his felt & made quite an address —
At last Dan Sayre moved that my res-
ignation be not accepted but that I be
granted leave of absence for as long a time
as I wished — Then we adjourned —
