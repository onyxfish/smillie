## Left Page

### April, Saturday 23, 1892

Pleasant w[eather] followed by cloudi-
ness & then clear weather — It was
11 before I got to R. & V's — sat with
young Voigh coaching him in taking
jade p[iece]s until 12.30 — then home —
at 2 went to Yale's & saw him &
got some professional advice — at
3 rode down to Canal St. V. had
taken four fine jade p[iece]s on large paper.
I rolled them very carefully to take
to studio to tack & stretch them. As
I went out I saw a car coming — I
wished to catch it — started to run —
caught my toe & went headlong, being
too weak to catch myself — my roll of
damp p[iece]s rolled ahead of me. A wretch
of a boy — with good intentions, no doubt,
grabbed the role & crushed it — When
I got to studio I found them so marred
that I hardly [could] have the crease in the
paper will stretch out of them — Tacked
them down — Home at 6 — dinner. Eve[ning]
Annie — Mary — Kate, the two girls & I went
to Met. Opera House. 3d Concert of the Phil-
sonic — centennial celebration — [our] boy 69 — a present from Mr. President Hyde.
The concert was a very fine one and
the boy was a perfect joy —
did not get out until 10.40 — after 11.30
when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 24, 1892

Brilliant, lovely day — Enjoyed my
morning scrub & bath at such length that
I had a late bkfst. It was just 11 o'clk
when I got to Dr. Gerster's — met him at the
door — but he gave me an examination &
a touch of caustic — then he went to my
studio with me & spent an hour chatting.
I found that two of my jade p[iece]s were
not much injured — the other two were badly
marred. Home before 1 — took Ralph
to G.C. Station & got correct time — Home
to dinner at 2 — Chare came in — At
4.15 took a walk with Ralph — then
a nap. Tea after 6. Evef. Mary,
Kate & the girls went to church —
just before they sat? Chare came in
& he stayed until after 11 — I did
not wait for him but went to bed
at 10.30.
