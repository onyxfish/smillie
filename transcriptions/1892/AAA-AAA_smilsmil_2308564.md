## Left Page

### February Monday 15 1892

Brilliant & beautiful day — to
studio at 10 — set palette & for
an hour or more painted a sort
of Portrait of F.H. Smith into my
twelfth night century sketch that
I have given to him — There I
got to work upon 20" × 24" "Mny
among the Adirondacks" — the old,
old canvas that has been under
way for the past 20 years. Home
to lunch at 1 — back at 2.30 and
painted until 5 — Repainting
sky & going generally over most of
the picture. — Then to NA & got some
the etching club Cats — went to Photo-
gravure Co — & made complaints to
the manager, Mr. Edwards — Home
at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Evng., a little
Reading — wrote a note to Charle &
took it to U.L. Club — then a short
walk — home & spent an hour
making a compact pkg — of the old
door-knobs, hinges etc taken from
the front door — To bed at about 11.

## Right Page

### February Tuesday 16 1892

A brilliant day growing colder
towards night — to studio at 10
— to work on 11" × 16" "An old dam"
— the smaller one, painted in my
studio at Montrose from a pencil
sketch & before the oil study for same
subject that I painted out of
doors — I think that I improved
it without losing much that was
worth saving. Home to lunch
(after going to bank) & back as
usual — Painted until near 5 —
Then to DeVinne's with 4 copies
of Etching Club Cats. To make
complaints — The difficulty, it seems,
lay with the imperfectly dried photo-
gravure inks. Home at 6.15 dinner.
Evng. Jim went to Juvenile Theatri-
cals at hat Coaks — Annie to her
Largely Club Rehearsal & I staid
at home & wrote to S.R. Koehler.
Soon after 9 I went for Jim — & soon
after 10 I went for Annie — & soon
after 11 I went for my bed.
