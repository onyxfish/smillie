## Left Page

### December Friday 9 1892

A bleak N.W. wind, cloudy sky & occasional
spiteful dashes of fine, spitting rain.
I went to Newmans, 24 Soho Sq. & saw
old Mr. Mills, the manager, & I was with the
old gentleman the whole, blessed mtf. until
1 o'clk. Trying new colors & papers & giving
directions for the equipment for my Water Color
sketching box. Then I hurried home to dinner
at about 2 o'clk. After that I wrote a letter
to James & then along rest preparatory to an
Eve. at Barnard's hm. — a meeting of the Art
Workers Guild — After tea, soon after 7, I went
to the hm. — had no trouble in getting in &
finding Pannell — I was after 8 before the
meeting was called to order — Pannell
read a portion of his introduction to the
new publication "Pablo de Segovia, illus'd
by Daniel Vierge — The original V. drawings
hung about the room — V. was unqualified
& rash in his praise — Then a paper was
read by Mr. Walker, a practical photo-engraver
who had with him, drawings, negatives & zinc
plates — He took the ground that draughtsmen
should try to conform to the requirements of the
process by which better results would be attained.
He was followed by young Planer-Hill, a draughtsman
who exhibited examples of his work together with
the process results & he took the ground that the
draughtsmen should think first of artistic results
& leave it to the process men to follow — they could
do so if they had to — After this, whisky, brandy & soda
& coffee — Then a general discussion that at times
was very lively — Pannell was well baited —
Two men, whose names I could not get, being
admirable speakers, scoring him in a very cleaver,
good tempered way — P. was not so good tempered,
nor was he so able in answering — Judging
from what I saw & heard there, I think that the
H.P. process men are way ahead of the London
men or even the Paris men — It was 11 when
I left the hall — the night was bright & clear and
I walked all the way home — nearly 4 miles,
I think, getting in at about 12. —

## Right Page

### December Saturday 10 1892

A very pleasant day. I went to the B.
m. & spent the mtf. over Lucas van Leyden.
Home at 1.30 — dinner — Afternoon I
stayed at home with Ralph while A.
went out with Jim — R. has been out
of sorts, a little, for two or three days past
(his mtf.) he had a slight chill and
before noon developed temperature to
102. He lay down on the bed with me
& had quite a nap, getting up evidently
brighter — His trouble is an indigestion —
A came home & has been given him —
Letters &c. — from Kate & Charl. C.
has sent me a Diary (as I had directed)
which is (owing to my fault) too small,
so I at once wrote to him giving more
specific directions & asking him to
send another.
