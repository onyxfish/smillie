## Left Page

### August Thursday 11 1892

overcast — [illegible], clearing slowly during the
day — B'kf' about 9 — met Prof. & Mrs. Dunham
in the B'kf' Room — & about 10 made them a
short visit in their Room — after which A.
the boys & I started out — went to P.O. & got
two letters from Emma — to bank — book store
etc. & wandered about until 12 — then
back to hotel & studied maps of Munich
until 1 — then Table d' hote dinner — After
that, at 2, went to International Ext. of
Art at Glass Palace where I stayed until
6 hard at work looking at as much as
I could see of a collection of 2850 works
of art in 64 galleries — The impression
left by this one visit is that the proportion
of bad & incomprehensible painting is
no greater at home than it is here — very
much was to me dreamy — a large
proportion excited my anger or contempt
& very, very little of it quickened my
pleasure or my admiration — Of course
I gave much of my time to the two Am-
gallaries — [illegible] about half the size
of the S.E. gall. of the N.A. of [illegible] — the smaller one
not more than 20 sq. This one was filled up
in boudoir style after plans by Stanford White
& had hung in it 32 small paintings by 19
different painters & 6 etchings by Whistler —
The men were hardly representative men
nor were all of the works good examples of the
men. In the larger gallery was a frightful
example of Dannat. Spanish ones singing — a
very large & bad Alex. Harrison — Sea beach with
females bathing — a lot of characteristic, [illegible]
(but not good) Whistlers — etc. — When I left
I was more tired than when I came down to
Breveut & I took a little walk to get rested.
Ev'g meal at 7 — Ev'g wrote to Kate
& to Chase & got to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### August Friday 12 1892

A very pleasant day — B'k'f' about 9 — and
at 10.30 we all went riding — all about the
city — giving the driver instructions to show
us as much as possible — went first to the
big statue of Bavaria — then about through the
finest streets — past palaces & institutions
— the Basilica — etc — across the River — then
up — across the River again & into the English
garden through which we had a delightful
drive — & back to hotel before 1 o'clk — in time
for our Table d' hote dinner — At 2.30 A. & I
left the boys playing in the room while
we went to the International Extn of
Paintings at the Crystal palace — where
we spent more than two hours in looking
at the pictures — The collection does not
raise in my estimation after a second visit —
Back to hotel soon after 5 — went out
with the boys & wandered in the street —
giving them a chance to look into
shop windows — At their request bought
some cheap toys for them — Ev'g meal at
7 — Ev'g Annie & I were engaged in
studying up "Parsifal" — To bed soon after
10 o'clk.

During our rides this a.m. we went into the Kgl.
Hofwagenhaus geschirr & Sattelkammer, a very
interesting collection of ancient & modern State
coaches, sleighs & vehicles with saddles & horse
harness & equipments of all kinds — Some of these
State affairs were as gorgeous as any of Barnum's
gorgeous show wagons. Some of them were
historically very interesting.
