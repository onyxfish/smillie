## Left Page

### March, Saturday 26, 1892

*Aunt Huedah died at 1 A.M. this m'g at Americus, Ga.*
*Kate rel- to ok at 3. P.M.*

Milky, overcast &, towards eve'g rather disagreeable. Had a short walk before bkfr — Got to studio about 10 — Very soon W. S. R. Kohler came in & spent some time with me. I tried to lay an aqua-tint background on jade plate but failed. I drew in with crayon upon a proof something to guide me. Home to lunch at 1 — at 2.30 A & I went to Chickering Hall to hear W. & Mrs. Geo. Henschel — very full house — The H's sang very charmingly but I could not enjoy it as I might if I had felt better — much to my disgust. After the concert A & I did some shopping for a hall gas light & bought one at Mitchell, Vance & Co's — Then I went to the studio & cleaned jade plate & prepared it for another attempt at an aqua-tint ground — Home at 6 — dinner Eve'g. Charl came in — I wrote a short note to Sarah C. F. H — & at 10.30 Charl & I walked up to the G.C. Station where I mailed it — Home & to bed at 11.
Jade 2 h.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 27, 1892

Steady rain all day — Bkfr. at 9 — sat with newspaper until near 11 — then went to studio & flowed an aqua-tint ground over jade plate & got a fine ground with one serious defect — but in spite of that think I can use it. Then home — Dinner at 2. Charl came in soon after to say that he had just been advised by telegraph that Aunt H's body was on its way north in charge of a niece's son & would reach here to-morrow p.m. Spent some time making measurements & drawing rough plans for a sort of hanging closet, umbrella rack, etc. for hall — to be placed under the stair. Tea soon after 6 — Annie told Gretta of our proposed trip abroad — Charl brought plans of the steamers with the staterooms unengaged & prices marked. Eve'g. wrote to Herrick — also notes concerning Aunt H's death to Montrose "Republican" & "Democrat" — mailed them at 9.30 — Read awhile & to bed before 11.
