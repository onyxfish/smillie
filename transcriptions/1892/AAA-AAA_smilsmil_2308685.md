## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 18, 1892

A pleasant day, mingling clouds &
sunshine with one trifling shower in the
afternoon. I worked for about two hours
on Morgan plate in the m'f + got it
ready for the printer. Then out + had
a walk before dinner. In the after-
noon, while A. has gone to Hampstead
\+ the children were in the Park I gave
way to my feelings + took a nap. Then
a walk before tea. Clara came at 7.30
to stay with the children + we went to
the Criterion Theatre. A little Operetta —
"Bon Miguonette" — 3 characters — mildly am-
using — after that "Cup Dominos" — very clever
\+ very funny — a characteristic French plot
\+ construction + equally characteristic English
setting — all the actors were clever. Joskins
Tubbs, a toad under a harrow, his wife, was
very funny (Alfred Maltby). Mr. Tubbs, Mrs
fanny Robertson, was wonderfully fitted
by nature for her part. The last act was a
bewildering maze of comic + rapidly shift-
ing situations. Out at 11 — home at 11.30
\+ then took Clara around to her home and
got to bed at 12.

*Morfan — 2. h.*

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 19, 1892

A very pleasant w'f. I got up feeling at
sorts, of course. It was after 10 when I
walked across the gardens to S°. Kensington
museum where I strayed until almost 1
looking at pictures + making some notes. I
met one of the officials in the Art Library and
he kindly favored me with some gossip con-
cerning especially Mr. Lewis Fagan of the Print
room, Print mus. I got home to dinner at
1.30 — after that I lay down for an hour.
At 4 I went with A. across the Park to
the Brompton Road where we took bus to
Fulham R°., getting out at Seymour Place +
at Holly Lodge — No. 5 — we called on misses
[illegible] mount Abbatt who have a studio along the
premises where they sculp°. They were + Skipper
[illegible] between Atwester met them + wrote to Annie
asking us to call on them — miss Abbatt is a young
wonder from Ohio — we did not see her — unwell.
Miss J. was very pleasant but we did not go into
the studio — in the hands of mechanics. Home
soon after 6 — found a telegram from Abbey ask-
ing me to dine with him at the Reform Club
tonight at 7. 30 — I was too miserable to attempt
it — it made me howling mad — telegraphed
refused — so I sent him word that I could
not meet him + gave myself up to fretting —
wasn't. Annie read aloud for awhile. 
For a wonder there has been no shower
today.
