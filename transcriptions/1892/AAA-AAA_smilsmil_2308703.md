## Left Page

### November Wednesday 23, 1892

Dull but with a more apparent attempt
at being pleasant — Dry all day. While
dressing something got into my left eye &
hurt furiously while I ate my b'kf — after that
it was less painful but I could still feel
that there was something there. I placed
a wet bandage on my eye & passed the
rest of the m'g on my back — After dinner
being convinced that something was in my
eye, I went in to Dr. Earle's — he examined
but could see nothing — he called back the
[illegible] & whatever was there he brushed
out without knowing it — I got instant relief — then I consulted him about my stomach troubles & got a couple of prescriptions
from him — Then, by underground I went to
Gloucester Rd. & at Gloucester Mansions No
7. — Harrington Gardens — I called upon
Mr. Archer, found him in a delightful
studio — at the top of a flat house — a room
20 × 35 — with lofty ceiling & very large north
window — I saw his Academy pictures under
way — female figures from a height looking over
the sea for their ships that will never come
in — also a good sized Surrey land scape —
His daughter came in & was very agreeable —
I walked home getting in at about 8.
Tea — We sf — we spent some time over
maps of Europe, planning for a flight.

## Right Page

### November Thursday 24, 1892

A nasty day — dark & damp — I managed
however to find light enough to work
on the Morgan plate for four hours — In
the We'g. at 8 o'clk. A. & I went by invitation to Jos. Pennell's to keep Thanksgiving
night — It was odd & entertaining —
we found there a lot of men, Bohemian-
ous, I should say — & but three women
besides Mrs. P. & A. — Most of the men were
young, but two (besides myself) could be
called elderly — P. introduced me to
some, others he pointed out — Painters,
Etchers — Draughtsmen, Architects —
writers for the Press — The London Cor't of the W'g.
Times — Editor of Pele. Mell — Art writer on the
Standard — ditto Observer — We sauntered
about through a suite of three rooms and
chatted about the books in one room — the
Etchings & pencil drawings in P's working
room & drank whiskey & Soda & claret in
the third, or dining room, where was served sandwiches cakes & confections — There
was much animated conversation and lots
of smoke everywhere — Mrs. P. was very
pleasant & A. has taken quite a fancy to
her. P. asked us to dine with him a week
from to-morrow night &, leaving A. with Mrs.
P., he & I will go to an Art Guild meeting
where a paper on "Paper for Etching" [illegible]
is to be read, followed by a discussion.
It was considerably after 11 when we
got away — about mid night when we
got home! — & 12.30 when we got to bed.

Morgan — H. h.
