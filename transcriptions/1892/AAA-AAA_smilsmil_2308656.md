## Left Page

### August Sunday 21 1892

The cloudless weather — pleasantly cool m[orning]
— easy — breezy — very warm in sunshine — After
9 when I went down for [illegible] — after that I went
a short distance up a walk back of the house
sat upon a bench & wrote until near 1 — d
Annie & the boys being with me part of the time
we had a good dinner & after that I had
a nap, so that it was after 4 before I was
ready to go out for a walk — we wandered
about through a park & garden place. The
grounds originally of a Cartusian monastery
— back of the Golden Lion Hotel — After a time
we joined the great procession going out
to the Schutzen fest as the boys were anxious
to see it. The crowd was great & not at all
picturesque — music & noises & all sorts made a
babel — I cannot say that it was either
very amusing or very interesting — we "fell" to
the hotel at 6.30 & gave orders for our supper
at $35 — Bread & butter — cold milk & cold meat —
& we waited 45 minutes for this order to be
filled — The slowness of these German waiters
beats all Yankee comprehension — The boys
got to bed before 8 — I sat down stairs — Read
& chatted with an English gentleman until
9.30

## Right Page

### August Monday 22 1892

Bright & breezy m[orning] — still hot in the sun-
shine — Soon after 10 we all started out for
a ride — at a short distance from our house
we got out & entered the walk through
the Drachenschlucht — at first a narrow
glen — pretty woods, & very dry stream — after
a time the rocks rise on either side and
come so near together as to form a moss covered
wall of rock & the passage way ony tortuous
too narrow as, in places, to make it difficult
for two persons to pass. This rock passage
is said to be about 200 yds. long — our walk
before we rejoined our vehicle that had gone
on by the Chaussee was quite a quarter of
a mile — Then we rode on — climbing quite
a hill to the Hohe Sonne — A forester's house
with a German Restaurant — & beer drinking
accompaniment — The ride through the woods
was interesting as showing the system in part,
of forest cultivation — It was about 12.30
when we returned — dinner at 1. After
that, again a nap — Then I went into the
town with Annie to do some shopping — A
threatening thunder shower pursued us
back, but no shower fell on our parts — I
read for a time a thin & watery composition
by A. "Isolde" & "Miss Mackenzie" — & before 7
had a simple supper on the Terrace —
Early in the eve. I tried to do a little
writing — commenced a letter to Mr. Koehler
but was too stupid to go on with it — So,
went to bed.

During the night from about 1 to 3 Am.
Annie had a crisis — severe colic with
vomiting & movements of bowels — cannot
guess a reason for it.
