## Left Page

### July, Sunday 10, 1892

A very hot day with thunder storm in
the afternoon that cooled the air pleasantly — Cloudy every. Bkfst — about — after that,
a consultation in which the Giffords
joined & it was decided that we would
start for the mountains to-morrow m'g
The porter was directed to telegraph to Domo
dossola for seven seats — we also directed
that our bills be ready for us after dinner
this eve'g. Then Annie, the boys & I went
out through the village to vineyards & old
buildings a short distance away — I
had my sketch book & made some little
sketches — We found a lovely wooded place
with running water & there we spent the
rest of the morning — back to lunch at 12.30
after that, packed valise — Thunder storm
which soon cleared — some startling effects
of color on the water — brilliant green in the
sunshine — dark green in the shadow — At
3.45 Annie, Ralph & I were rowed to the
fisherman's Island where I made a sketch
— then on to Isola Madre, where we were joined
by M'G — [illegible] Margorie — & together we went
over the island & were delighted — The
luxuriant growth of beautiful plants
& trees formed a delightful contrast to the
art & formality of Isola Bella — It was
nearly 6 when at last we left the island &
got to hotel at 6.15 — gave the boys their
supper & then had our dinner — after
that learned that a party of 32 bound
for the mts. were ahead of us & we could
not get outside D[illegible] seats — so then
we telegraphed for seats Tuesday m'g —
I got to bed quite early —

## Right Page

### July, Monday 11, 1892

Another very hot day — as soon after bkfst. as
I could get my traps ready I started out with
Annie — [illegible] & the boys & went up to a little
village south of Baveno — called Romanires where
I made a water color sketch of the church San
Guiseppe — as I thought, with tolerable success. Back to hotel at 12 — lunch at 12.30 —
Posted a letter for home — finished, with color,
my fishers Island sketch of yesterday — then
a rest — about 4.30 Annie went in bathing
with the two boys — after dinner, at 7.30,
we went out for a last row on the lake —
some charming effects — beautiful over the
massive [illegible] form, Sasso di Ferro, on the
side of the lake opposite Baveno. Returned
in an hour & then the misery of settling the
hotel bill — as we had failed to stay the
full week we were denied Pension rates
& had to pay 29 francs — or about $12 p. day
for our five days stay — The building — the
situation & the surroundings are delightful
but I think the view from Stresa must be
better than from Babeno. The beds at the
Bellevue were good — very. The table was
out fair but as there were only a dozen guests
it may not have been so good as when the
house is full — I don't like the service —
In all of the hotels, so far, we have been
rushed as though on a R.R. time table,
all things being arranged for a start to-
morrow m'g — we went to all ab'o —
