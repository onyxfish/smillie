## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 26, 1892

A clear, windy, very cold day. Ther
down to 7°. Got to studio soon after
10 & until 12 worked at getting pf. of
Rey plate ready for frame — went to
N.A., soon after 12 & met Etching Club
Hanging Com. & revised pf. of Cat. Home
after — Katcheu Cook & Louise Cook
lunched with us. After, went down
to Del's with revised pk — There
to Klackeel's — to N.A. & to studio —
Home at 6 — dinner — at 8 A. & J
to Chickering Hall in coupé to the
Choral Club concert — I sat alone
in the gallery — Chase & Miss Howells
a short distance back of me. A very
enjoyable concert — Home in demo-
cratic street car — near 11 — to bed
before 11.30.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 27, 1892

Still very cold — after blf got Tiffany
glass into its place, of ceiling between hall
& alcove — disappointed — light so poor that
much of the color effect is lost. Nearly
11 when I got to studio — Ther. 57°. Too
cold to work — I got started burnishing
on Aqua-Tint, 5"×7 "An old dame" —
Wrote notes to T.H. Smith — Wm. Pate &
others — Home to lunch at 1.40 — after it to
N.A. & D. Then to studio after 3 & worked
on old dame for an hour or so — Went
round paper for some printing to-morrow.
Home before 6 — dinner — Smif wrote
to Mr. J. Dreer of Phila. concerning some
examples of father's eng. work & prices for
his extended copy of Baker's Engravers
of America.
