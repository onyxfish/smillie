## Left Page

### March Thursday 10 1892

A disagreeable day — dull until after 4 o'clk. when some rain fell.
Wet. growing colder with strong wind. Did not go out of the house all day
— feeling very heavy & stupid. Louis Tiffany called in the m'f. to look at
the glass in front door — Yale came in about the middle of the afternoon when
I was trying to hang some water color in the dining room — He seemed to
think that I was unimproving, but I didn't think I felt like it.
Dinner after 6 — Ev'f. George & Nellie came in — then Choate.
It was 11 before we got to bed.

Suiey Cook lunched with us.
Deming's man picked up lace cur-
tains at library window.

## Right Page

### March Friday 11 1892

A very disagreeable day — Ther. 26° in the m'f. with high wind & lot of
sleet. I did not go out of the house, but
felt better than I have for some days.
I wrote to Koehler in the morning and
had a long walk in the parlor — lunch
(again) — Afternoon. I wrote to Herrick
& Abellos Stone giving to both an intimation
that we may go abroad this summer.
Yale came in after 3 o'clk. & made a
long call — about 5 I went down to
the parlor where Annie was having
a sort of limited afternoon tea — a few
intimates invited to meet Margaret
Parker White. Mr. White came up & quite
a number of friends & relatives, male &
female — all away soon after 6 —
At 6.30 Annie & Nellie went to Ed's to
dinner. Kate & I sat down at home
with the children. Ev'f. Kate went
to bed very early & I sat alone — I
wrote a little & read — A. & N. home
at 10.
