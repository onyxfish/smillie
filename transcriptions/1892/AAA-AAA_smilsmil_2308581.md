## Left Page

### March, Sunday 20, 1892

Bright, pleasant & milder — B&f.
after 9 — Then, of course, newspapers
Soon after 11 I went to the Century
& spent some time looking at the
collection of Jervis Mc. Entee's paintings.
I hope that no misguided friend
will ever make an ex[hibition] of my
studio remains after I am gone.
Sabin Library & read awhile some
art books — Then out & walked up 5.
Ave. to near 70 S — Then home getting
in at 1.30 — dinner soon after 2 —
It was about 4 when I took Ralph
& we walked down to 58 W. 33 S[t.]
to George's boarding house. In his
rooms I found him & his family —
Kate was there & Charle came in
as Ralph & I left. Half an hour later.
Tea after 6 — Eve[ning] reading ment
after 9 — Then wrote notes to S.C.T.
& to S.R. Koehler — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 21, 1892

Brilliant day — still quite cool — Got
to printers (K.+V.) before 10 — left Vickery
[there] & two pfs. with young Voigt to get
wanted up on the taking of the finished
pfs. & then I went out & spent the
largest part of an hour hunting for an
establishment that Tiffany told me about
where I might get fine iron work — and for
a store in Bdway. where I could get poles,
rings etc. for curtains — found neither.
Ret[urned] to K.+V's — with young V. until 12
he took several pfs. — I took the best one
with me to studio — Tacked it down — Then
home to lunch at 1.30 — To studio at 2.30
Rearranged plan of working on Jade Plate
table at large window so that I can get
the Jade into a much better light. I was
tired & my head ached — so I did not
feel like commencing work — At 4
went again to K.+V's to see how the
proving was progressing — got another
pf. & returned with it to Studio — Tacked
[it] down & home at 6 — dinner — Eve[ning]
very stupid — read newspapers and
[do]zed in my chair — To bed at [10] —
