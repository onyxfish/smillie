## Left Page

### July, Friday 22, 1892

At last a fine day — cloudy m'g clearing
to be delightful in the afternoon — cloudy again in
the [illegible] — Up at 7 — kindled a fire — went out to a
little village on the side hill south of Chamonix and
saw some bits that I liked — back to [illegible]. Down
after 8. After that went out shopping with A.
Son after 10 I got my traps & went to the little
village visited in the m'g & worked upon a
water-color sketch of some buildings until
12.15 — then back to lunch. Soon after 2 [illegible]
our party got away for the Glacier du Bosson.
A — on a mule with Ralph in her lap — [illegible]
& I, each on a mule — Marjorie & Jim walking
our mules were almost eaten up by flies and
made riding anything but a pleasure. R.
got along very well — after he tired in his mother's
lap he rode in front of me a part of the way
he walked — The day was heavenly & some clouds
still clung to the summits — we went to the
right side of the glacier to ascend — enjoyed it
very nicely — got to the chalet at 3.30 — Spent
a little time visiting the ice grotto — then
we left [illegible] to descend with the mules and
boy while the rest of us went on with the guide
and crossed the glacier in less than 30 m —
after a short rest started down — through
spruce forests — over moraines & glacier streams
across open meadows — stopping constantly to
enjoy the enchanting views up to snow peaks —
down into the valley & across to the wonderfully
blue mts "across the way". Home soon after 5
ahead of A. & R. — A chance to rest a little
while before dinner — a pleasant bit of Jim
in our room before which I sat until about
9 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 23, 1892

A glorious day — Annie called me before 5 to look
at Mont Blanc with delight of the rising sun upon it.
Up at 7 & had a walk before [illegible] — delicious — at [illegible]
decided that I alone should do the Mer de Glace — So
a guide, boy & mule was ordered & at 9.30 I started — up on
my mule of yesterday & with the same guide — we made
very good time & soon commenced the ascent — all the
way through delightful woods — my mule being a
good walker I got to the Mr. [illegible] Hotel at 11.15 — 3¼ [illegible]
hours ahead of time given by guide — I climbed a height
above the hotel & at 11.45 had a good lunch & gave my
guide one — then rested until 1.5 — then started across
the glacier — a grand appearance — much impressed by
the peculiar mt. formations — the aiguilles — all about — by the
snow & by the swelling sea of ice — the approach to the
glacier on either side is steep & difficult — the character
of the soil much like ashes & cinders — up on the glacier
some of the crevasses were immense — especially towards
the end of it — I was astonished at the size of some of
the immense rocks carried by the glacier — It took
us a little more than an hour to get across — then
up on the moraine — over the Mauvais Pas — that
was not so bad after all — & down to the Chapreau
Chalet where we had quite a rest & I enjoyed
the view down the valley — & the study of the near
ice forms at the end of the Glacier du Bois where
it rounds in great pinnacles & every variety of forms.
I heard much crashing & rumbling sound and the
tumbling of many rocks & stones into the crevasses.
It was about 3 when we started from the Chapreau
& in 20 m. we reached the bottom — ten minutes
ahead of time — Annie drove up in time at 3.30
& by 4 we were at the Hotel Beau Site — Then
had a good bathing & rubbing & at 5 lay down
for a rest — took a short walk before dinner —
very feeling rather tired I went to bed at 9.
