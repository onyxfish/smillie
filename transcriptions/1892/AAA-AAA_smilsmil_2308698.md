## Left Page

### November, Sunday 13, 1892

Actual sunshine, very mild, driving an
hour or two of the mf. It was so delici[ous]
that it made there memory of the past
week, culminating in the fog of yesterday
a veritable nightmare. The afternoon was
leaden overhead, but not foggy. I shall
have to record here a confession — To me
the day-light was so precious from many
points of view that my conscience gave
me absolution, and — I worked on my
plate — I promising to make a muddy
of the first foggy day. Before I went
out & walked in the park with the boys
until 2 — Annie went to All Saints in
Margaret St. (said to be a gem of archi-
tecture) & S. Andrews in Wells St. when
the music is said to be fine — I took
another walk between 4 & 5 — & met
some Radical & Labor clubs out parading
very. Annie finished "An Old Maid's love"
reading aloud to me. The impression
left upon me is not agreeable — Too
much cynicism — too many impossible
clever speeches — too much blind blundering
on the part of creatures of abnormal con-
science or passion —

## Right Page

### November, Monday 14, 1892

A bright, sunny, breezy day, very mild
when in the sunshine protected from the
wind — I got to work upon my plate soon
after bkf. & stuck to it until 12.30 when I
took Ralph & we walked in the Park until
1.30 then in to dinner — In the afternoon from
2.30 to 4.30 I worked on my plate & then had
[illegible] for a proof. I then lay down on the
bed & had a rest. Preparation & the dissipa-
tion of the even. At 6 Annie & I began our
dressing & at 7.15 we took cab & rode to 10
Cheniston Gardens where we had been in-
vited to dine by Mr & Mrs Walter Blackman.
The house inside was very pleasant and
pretty — Mrs B's brother-in-law, Mr Van Duzer,
was the only guest beside ourselves — We
sat down at about 7. 40 & had a simple
dinner served in superior & excellent
manner — fillet of sole, broiled — cutlets and
chestnuts — Pheasants — salad — Plum pudding
cheese crackers & fruit — Sherry, claret and
champagne — Then the ladies had coffee
in the drawing room while the gentlemen
had their coffee & cigarettes in the dining
room — Then to drawing room where
the conversation was mainly reminiscent
of days long ago in N.Y. & of the men that
we then knew there. It was 10.30
when we left —
