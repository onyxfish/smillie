## Left Page

### February Thursday 25 1892

Threatening most of the day + overcast wind from N.E. To studio at 10 & at once to work getting p[roof]s from jade plate — considerable amt. of time spent upon necessary preliminaries + some interruptions — B. Gerster came in with some of his proofs — I took two + was much pleased with the state of the plate + now feel hopeful that some day I may get it finished in a creditable manner. Home to lunch at 1 — after that rode to N.A.Y.'s with aqua-tint plate (Pratt ms.) left it with them to print for me 50 pts. that I will try to dispose of through Blackner — Then to [Delmonico's] — about a bill sent to Etching Club — Then to D. B. Butler's + got back board — then to studio + made p[roof] of p[roof]s to take to Boston — then to N.A. + there met Annie + with her went to Archer, Pancost & Co — Stone Bros & to Dennings to look for gas fixtures + lamps — then to G. C. Station — secured seats for to-morrow + arranged with [Express man?] — Home at 6 — dinner — Eveʼg at 8.30 went to N.A. — Special meeting to consider proposed amend[ment] to Con[stitution], making a Retired Academician grade. A full meeting — much smoke — much bad air, discussion & confusion — no prospect of any order or result coming out of this chaos — so, at 9.40 I became a Retired N.A. Home + to bed soon after 10.

Jade — 2 h —

## Right Page

### February Friday 26 1892

*A. & D went to Cambridge.*

[and] dull mist. B[roken]: a little earlier than usual. A little before 10 we went to the Station — Gretta + Ralph going with us to speed us. Train off on time — A fairly comfortable ride — two or three hours of sunshine. A miserable and expensive lunch — got to Boston at 4.35 — then by electric cars to Cambridge, getting to Sue's house at about 5.45 — dinner in about an hour. Miss Howe, a connection of Sue's, a very agreeable, middle aged lady visiting there — Eve'g chatting in parlor until 10 o'clk — then to bed.
