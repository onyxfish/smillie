## Left Page

### October Thursday 6, 1892

Another foggy day, thicker, darker &
more persistent than the fog of last
Sunday — I could not see to work, but
in some way I managed to do it in
spite of fog, from 10 to 12. Then I went
with Jim to swimming bath & he had
his third lesson. He came out in better
condition than at previous lessons.
Reaction set in promptly & he was
soon warm. Dinner at 1.40 — I ate
lightly, suffering from a headache
that soon after sent me to bed until
6 o'clk. There was Rec'd a letter
from Mr. Heber R. Bishop at Paris — very
civil, enclosing draft, payment in full
for my bill for jade plate. Every
about a little time over a late No. of the
N.Y. Weekly Tribune — then Annie
read aloud for awhile.

morgan — 2 h.

## Right Page

### October Friday 7, 1892

A dull m'f. A pleasant day in
spite of a shower or two — & a bright
moonlight night. At 10, by undergrd.
R.R. I went to the city & got from J.S.
Morgan & Co. & order n.d. M. & Co. of N.Y.
in exchange for Mr. Heber Bishop's bill
of £4. Then back — Had my hair cut &
had shampooed. Wrote note to Charl
enclosing money order. Dinner — Mrs.
Satchell dined with us — I worked
on my plate from 3 to 5. Annie
& the boys went to Kensington Mu-
s. Nat. His. Even'g I wrote letter
to Kate —

morgan, 2 h.
