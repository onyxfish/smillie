## Left Page

### November, Sunday 27, 1892

*Called on Prof. Herkomer at Bushey.*

A very pleasant, mild day — and I was considerably out of sorts — all indigestion — After bkfs. I went with A. & the boys into Kensington Gardens — to the Albert memorial & their home — we had dinner promptly at 1 o'clk & a little before 2 we all started out. A. & the boys went as far as Bala[illegible] & there got out & had a ramble in Regents Park. I went on to Gower St. & then to Euston Sq. Station & took 2.30 train to Bushey, getting there at 3.15. I walked through a muddy little village with a few picturesque [illegible] to D. House — Prof. Herkomer's home — a [illegible] mile from the station. It is an old fashioned house, rather small — in an otherwise very well kept garden — the new house, in course of erection, stands back of it — outside, a heterogeneous collection of buildings containing studio, plate printing rooms & work shops — Mr. [illegible] studio. I found his regular Sunday P.M. reception for his pupils in progress — a great crowd — there — young men & women — he told me more than 140 of them. He rec. me very pleasantly & after a little conversation passed me over to one of his older pupils (of whom more than 40 have made a permanent colony at B—) Mr. Fischer, with instructions to show me over the place — I went through the garden, laid out by a former wife — over his great new [illegible] out among the studios — also this a part of the house he is occupying — & few more [illegible] in his studio where his wife was [illegible] & his daughter serving [illegible] — then I bid him goodbye & returned to R.R. Station — lovely twilight — to find that the 4.50 train had stopped at another station — A gentleman there, disappointed like myself — shared with me in hiring a carriage & we rode 6 miles to Harrow & there took 5.54 Metropolitan train — I found him a very pleasant fellow — he gave me a card to Bot. Gardens — Regents Park — I got to Proad S. at 6.30 & at home found A & boys at tea.

## Right Page

### November, Monday 28, 1892

*Savoy Theatre. Grundy & Sullivan*

Sun not visible, but wind from the west & air inspiring — in the m.f. I went to Brit. Mus. large Reading Room & completed my reading and notes from Stahlsanton's Graphic Arts. A.M. I went out down Holborn to Lincoln's Inn fields & wandered about there — then to restaurant of Hotel & had lunch — after that back to Miss — [illegible] at 4 and walked home — there I had a good rest — was with f. A. & I went to the Savoy Theatre which is the brightest, prettiest theatre that we have yet seen in London — but in a strange out of the way hole — saw the new Grundy & Sullivan comic opera "Haddon Hall." It was very carefully, beautifully put upon the stage. The company, I thought, good in a general way — nothing especially good — nothing especially bad — the orchestra was large & chorus full & well trained — The text of the libretto by Sydney Grundy followed closely upon Gilbert's lines without the sparkle & the witty, nervous quality of Gilbert — it was amusing. The music was clearly Sullivan's but to my mind less piquant, dainty & melodic than Patience & others of his early successes. The gather songs bore strong family resemblance to old familiar ones. The Barcarole & 8 note song suggested. The Needle & the Charm in Patience — far less pleasing — The Puritans in their fancy guise brought to mind the Policemen in the Pirates of Penzance & so on strongly. The comedians were Chas Kenningham (Rupert Vernon) & Rutland Barrington (The McCleountie). Neither of them had good voices, but the enunciation of the first made amends for limited vocal range. The C— was of a very broad type — both were evidently very acceptable to the audience — In the last act there is a bit of dance — the Highland Fling — by MC— and a very good looking young girl whose name I could not place upon the programme — it was about the wildest bit of exercise that I ever saw in the way of dancing — the girl was fairly mad — & so did the House — She threw off [illegible] her arms & legs and all flew & so did her arms & legs and all screamed like savages — It was danced three times. It was almost 12 when we got home — & 1 o'clk before I got to bed.
