## Right Page

### January, Friday 1, 1892

Not unpleasant, altho' overcast, got to studio at about 10 o'clk. and to work trying to lay an aqua-tint ground over a portion of sky of San Louis Ray Mission plate that I am doing for W. Vickery of San Francisco. Accomplished it after awhile — Then got out small plates done last summer — dry-points & an aqua-tint — worked on 6"×12" dry point "Hollyhocks". Charl came with his photographing outfit & set up curtains, etc. Geo. came in & we all posed for photos. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 3 & worked on "Sweet Peas", dry-point, as long as I could see — Home at 6 — dinner — Ev'g, wrote to Herrick & other notes. Looked over English papers. To bed before 11.
