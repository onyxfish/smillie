## Left Page

### June, Thursday 30, 1892

*Grand Hotel de Gênes. Room 1.*

Up at 5 — stale & unrefreshed — Bkfp. at
6 and then the bustle & worry of getting
ashore — Steamer lying at Custom House
wharf — After 7 when I got my party and
luggage together in the Custom House — A—
& me Gifford took a hack & drove into the city
to secure hotel rooms — I looked after Mr.
Gifford & luggage — Emma, Marjorie — Mr. [illegible]
& myself — altogether, between 30 & 40 pieces
of luggage — big & little. The search was an
official farce — an annoyance rather than
a difficulty — paid an English-speaking
man 15 francs to help me through — A. & Mr. G.
still — having secured fine rooms at the Hotel
de Gênes — at 9 o'clk. we all finally got away
from the Custom House in a big bus — all of the
luggage piled on top of it. It was 10.30
when at last it was all transferred to our
rooms — immense apartments — there was
then some unpacking and dressing — after which I
rested until lunch at 12.30 — a very delightful meal —
After that — a sweet solid nap of two hours — all
four of us, in which we made up some of last
night's lost sleep — After that took [illegible] Carriage
& drove for nearly two hours about the city, Emma
& Marjorie with us — Dinner at 7 — to us, after our
ship's confinement a delightful feast — then
after 8 when we finished it — then a stroll
in the streets & back to our palatial rooms —
the boys still awake — to bed at 10 — all soon
asleep —

## Right Page

### July, Friday 1, 1892

Very warm, but delightful day — awoke at
4.30 with bad headache — Bromo. Caf — Some
sleep — ups at 8 — 9 when we had bkfp — Then
out for investigations — R.R. bureau — banks
etc — bought photos — back to hotel & took Emma,
Marjorie & Ralph with us through narrow streets
up to Garabaldi — where we took a look at the
old palaces — went through the galleries of the
Palazzo Rosso & enjoyed the novelty of the thing.
Back at 12.45 — lunch — then trunk-packing —
changing things to send two trunks to London
to lie in store there — Jones until Sep't — At 3
& the boys — Emma — Marjorie & I rode to the
Palazzo Doria — went through the apartments there &
spent some time loitering there — then went out and
hired Garibaldi's own drove away out to the Campo
Santo — the exhibition of monumental statuary and
the dramatic sentiment was amusing rather
than impressive — The great building with open
corridors was a sort of Valhalla for a faithful
spouse & dutiful children — now [illegible] we
rode back through the Exposition grounds where
every thing was in a condition of incompleteness —
no promise of being ready for the 1st of July — the
date now set for the opening — then to the
Cathedral Ch. San Lorenzo — an attaché taking
us into the chapels — back of the high altar &
into the cloisters — Back to the hotel at
20 m. before 6 — very tired — Dinner soon after
7 — after that I stretched myself upon my
bed until 9 — then packed until 10 and
to bed — Rooms very spacious — beds
delightful — Cook excellent — Charges as
high as they could make them without exciting
rebellion — Hotel du Parc delightfully situated
and described by the Mitchells as being very
satisfactory.
