## Left Page

### January Saturday, 4 1873.

Beautiful day. Got to Studio at 9.15 & to work on Water Color — washed out a great portion of it, getting quite desperate, my work seems so bad. Stuck to it until after 1, then prepared lunch — Fred Smillie came up to draw from a cast — He worked until 4 o'clk. So did I, getting my drawing into a more promising condition — Then I went to Appleton's — Saw a copy of my Yo. S. Article for the Journal as improved by Bunce — I do not acknowledge it as mine — Called on Mr. Blackburn — not in — home to tea at 6.30 — Evef wrote some, Read some & fussed some with large photos. To bed at 11.35

## Right Page

### January Sunday, 5 1873.

Perhaps the worst day yet. Rain, sleet and wind; ice forming in beautiful, glass-like shapes over everything — Telegraph wires strung with icicles & trees & vines encased. Wif went to Dr. Hastings. He gave as motto for the year, Isaiah 36th ch. 3, 4 v." Thou wilt keep him in perfect peace whose mind is stayed on Thee." Then an admirable discourse on the nature of peace promised — Walked home in the pelting storm without protection as my umbrella broke down — Dinner at 1 o'clk. P.m. in my big chair. Evef cleared sidewalk — heavy work. Tea just before 6 clk — alone — Read Whymper among the Alps & got to bed soon after 11
