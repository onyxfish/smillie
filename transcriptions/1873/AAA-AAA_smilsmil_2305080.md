## Left Page

### July Wednesday, 23 1873.

Still fine weather — Up soon after 5 and with Will had a first rate swim — back to bed — slept until near 8 — hurried to blfs — at 10, went to Mr. K's Room & worked on my Gay Head sketches while Will Read "Chance acquaintance." — Dinner at 12 as a company of Volunteer soldiers were to come from Taunton — Mass. — They came while we were at table — paraded in front of the hotel, stacked arms and about 3 had their dinner — worked again in the K. Room — about 4 Cook this yacht am — C. came ashore for a few minutes, John's letters, then away. Tea at 5 — all the K's & I wandered after tea — Will, Fanny & I later than the others — in the camp grounds — back at 7.30 & found the house & outside, thronged — music by the band & engine — Things grew merrier until after 8 when dancing commenced. Then the fun was led by the crew of the yacht Falconer — just arrived and danced with their lady friends on the platform in front of the house — Indoors Miss Norton of Edgartown was an unceasingly delightful to me — she was so, really, naturally & energetically happy where everyone else turned, once I shall turned three times — later a comic singer made doleful fun through his nose — then I was drawn to Miss Anthony & her friends Miss Antyle and I fished in shallow water with much sport for an hour — Will K — enjoying it with me — At last worn out at 12.30 I went to bed, leaving a roaring crowd all over the house — but I was soon sound asleep —

## Right Page

### July Thursday, 24 1873.

Up soon after 5 and had another good swim — back to bed and slept until after 8 — douse, down to blfs late after blfs. Re dressed, made up formal and worked on sketches — which consumed all of the morning. The volunteer co. got away about 7 o'clk. while I was asleep, after having kept most of the house in turmoil all night — every body looks jaded and worn out. Dinner at 12.30 — Afternoon went again to N.K's Room & worked while Will K. Read "Chance acquaintance." Mrs. Foster & her sister Miss Brigham came in by invitation and I showed my "Gay Head" sketches. Tea at 5. — then, with Will & W.K. went to the grove & bought a few photographs of Gay Head etc. — then met, as arranged, Tallut K. & Mr. Benson and all, after a little shopping, walked to the [illegible] "View House" — through the halls, parlors, piazzas, etc — out along the beach walk to the pavilion — by quite twilight — after 8, back through the town and to our hotel, all gathered about the Soda fountain & considerable Soda — Ginger Ale & Nectar was drank — at last to my Room and packed so as to be all ready for a start home on the morrow morning — It was almost 11 o'clk. when I got to sleep.
