## Left Page

### July, Saturday, 5, 1873.

*St° Montreal, Stateroom 63.*

Up at 8, after a very sound sleep. Bkfst. at 9 — Met W. Burland in the corridor of Hotel & yielded to him & with him attended to a number of messages about tickets etc. etc. — Got Stateroom on board of St° "Montreal" No. 63. Then walked along the wharves, through Bonsecour market & about the streets until near 11 — then to B.A. Prov° Hotel & met Father. Saw Hy Earle, Walton & others — wrote a letter home. — Rec. me from Charl & back to Hotel at 1.30 — Dinner at 2 & at 3.15. Burland took us out in a doc cart — me Rode about by the locks & shipping — through mud & dust — through the business city, among the private residences around the Little Mountain & at 6.30, down to the St° Montreal. It was after 8 o'clk when the Prescott boat came along & then at once, we got off. The night was glorious beyond description as has been this day. Spent most of the even chatting with Gotthreed and Stern — Mr. Dickerson & wife, whom we met at Niagara came an with us — Paced the deck until 11. Then turned in — Had a first rate sleep in a capital State Room.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday, 6, 1873.

*Quebec — St. Louis Hotel, Room 142 —*

Got first sight of Quebec from Stateroom window about 6.30 AM — Glorious day. Rode up to Hotel & had Bkfst. at once. Then with Father sauntered about his old haunts until 11 when he left me on the Ramparts near St. John's Gate, he going to Church. I wandered to the Heights & then clambered along the cliff at the foot of the Citadel walls. Back to Hotel, lunch at 11.30 — After that went down to Sanguenay St. & then over the ferry to Pt. Levis — back & along the wharves foot of the cliff. Went over one of the Allen Line of steamers. — Then alt to Champlain St. & up to Durham Terrace & waited until near 5 o'clk — The afternoon was marvellous for purity. Dinner at 5 — again with Father went to the Terrace & after 6 went out to Amasse St. to see Mrs. Brown — not in town — but saw her sons Thos & Walter Drysdale — They went with us out to the Plains of Abraham & we walked until near 9 o'clk when we ret. to the Hotel. — Again went to the Terrace. Brilliant moonlight & crowds of promenading people. There until 10 — then back and to bed at 10.30
