## Left Page

### July Monday, 7 1873.

Still delightful weather. Up late as both father & I were tired. Left for at 8.30 — after went to Saugenay boat & secured Stateroom for to-morrow. There until after 12 o'clk. Among photographers selecting photos. Some men wandering & back to hotel at 1.15 — at 2 had a dinner & shortly before 3 started out in a calache — Rode up to the Citadel & went around it — then back through the City & out a Palace Gate, over Dorchester Bridge & out through Beauport to Mont-morenci Falls. At there a little after 5. Sketched for an hour — away at 6.30 and reached Hotel just before 8 o'clk. Took a very light tea & went to our Room. Mr. Tho. Drysdale called — then Dr. Marsden. We got to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### July Tuesday, 8 1873.

Steamer Saguenay. Room 10.

Up at 5. M[illegible], fresh & clear. Bkfst at 6.30 & down to boat at 7. Owing to lateness of Montreal boat, it was nearly 9 before we got away. To my great surprise, Met Miss Ripple [illegible], with her friend, Miss James [illegible] party. The Salle very breezy — the Laurentian Mts showed splendidly — so did the falls of Montmorenci. Sometimes it was not unlike sailing on Lake Champlain — the water was quite rough. Got to Murray Bay — about — Had a good dinner at one. Reached Rivière de-loup a little before 6 and were detained landing freight for two hours. I took a good snooze during the p.m. during which time it showered. Every now then Saloon became dark & showery — About 10.30 we got to Tadoussac — stayed but a short time & at 11 entered the River in a very heavy rain storm. Turned in at about midnight.
