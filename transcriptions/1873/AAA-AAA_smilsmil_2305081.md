## Left Page

### July, Friday 25, 1873

*Portland Str. "Franconia" Room "O"*

Up som after 5. and took my last swim at M. Vineyard — back to bed telefor until 8 o'clk — think [illegible] after that, after settling bills & doing final packing. Read Chance Acquaintance in Mr K's Room, aloud. Carrie K. sick in bed. About 10 o'clk steamer in sight. Read on until about 10.30 said my good byes & at 11, drove me to Vineyard Haven landing with Will K. and Sturtevant. Steamer reached whf at 11.30 and in about 15 m. we were [illegible] intolerably hot — no shade & not a breath of air — Dinner at 12 — 12 at table 5 or 9 [illegible] passengers. An uninteresting [illegible] out for the trip — the stewardess the only woman on board — off Gay Head about 1.30 — caught a breeze and also a swell from that time until 8 o'clk — we swung as though in a hammock — the air was brisk & refreshing. I went forward to the [illegible] myself on the forecastle deck, under the lea of a sail & enjoyed the swinging motion until after 5 o'clk. Had tea at [illegible] — a luminous mist of smoke from fires on Long Island settled about us & for a couple of hours we could see no land — a glorious sunset at 7.30 & at 8 passed [illegible] Gull Island & light; entered the Sound & were at once out of the swell — It blew so hard as to make it hard work to walk the deck & sail had to be taken in (We had been under full sail.) — went below & turned in before 9.30 — in spite of all efforts to air my Room through its little bunghole of a window, it was very close & warm & I suffered from it all night.

## Right Page

### July, Saturday 26, 1873

Up som after 4 — Saw the sun rise gloriously — almost a repetition of its setting last night in color — We were just entering the East River & I enjoyed extremely the panorama of light houses, forts and islands crowded with public bldgs — we went through Hell Gate swimming with the City of Boston ahead & just the great steamer "Rhode Island" right upon our heels — we reached our dock, Pier 38, foot of Market St. about 7.30 — 2 Foot — bee-line of cars to foot of Grand St. — then another line across to Church St. filled with sticky, stinking people — the streets were already seething — home by Bdway cars — got in at 9 afternoon — an hour and a half of horrible travel — very hungry & headachy — Had a good cup of strong tea at once; then spent a good part of the m[illegible] on the sofa — Dinner at 1 o'clk. Made some paste & mounted Gay Head sketches & spent the afternoon in arranging my traps — Tea between 6 and 7 — sultry very close & hot — lounging about doing nothing — Father, Mother and Char the only ones at home — got to sleep on the sofa — to bed at 11.30. & passed a flouncing night.
