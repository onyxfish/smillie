## Left Page

### March Monday, 3 1873.

A march like day — got to studio at 9.15 — Worked awhile trying to charcoal a view of [illegible] of the valley — small thing for Appletons — then miting — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 & wrote, interrupted very much by callers until after 4 — Mr. Diven then came in — got down to Appletons at 4.40 with 18 pp. of M.S. — Bunce very impatient — then back — stopping at Gauthier's — Home to tea at 6.30 — Evely very cold & windy — Father has had $1000 pr annum — voluntarily [illegible] him by a Bill Co. — Father Mother [illegible] & I talked family affairs until 9 — then I wrote Water Color letters until 10.30 — To bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### March Tuesday, 4 1873.

Very cold & windy — got to studio at 9.20 — Tried to go on with miting — head stupid & feeling very hopeless. About 1 o'clk. met over to N.A. & D. — met then Sarah Marten & sister — Detained until 2 o'clk — then home to lunch — stopped at bank on my way back — met Elisha Mulford — got to studio at 3.15 Mr. Diven called — worked away at my M.S. until after 6 o'clk — digging out only 3 [illegible] pages. Miss Woodward brought in several ladies — at 6.30 — met over to Hoffmann's & saw Mr. & Mrs. Diven. Then home to tea — Harriet & Millie in from Montreal. at 8 went up to Jno. Townsend's, No. 53 E. 54 St. — then to Ogilvie's — 55 W. 55 [illegible]. Spent a pleasant evng there until 10.15 — Home at 10.30 and to bed at 11.15
