## Left Page

### Sept. Saturday, 13 1873.

Delightful m'g but clouded over between 10 & 11 & in the afternoon we had a cold, wet norther. Worked on "Durham Terrace" and finished it — Dinner at 1.30 Afternoon, worked on large black Saguenay. About 4.30, got off on Tom. — Seeing rain mostly the time, but we had a jolly good ride & Tom trotted better than he has ever done before. — In at 6.45 & after 5 before I went to tea. After [illegible] had a quick talk with Miss Virginia B[illegible] about the gossip, that was at [illegible] annoying "Mrs Cram" — She desired that I should see Mrs Cram and appointed for this eve'g, when we had another talk — Resulting in expressions of mutual confidence and respect. Very tired and sleepy & went to bed at 10 and

## Right Page

### Sept. Sunday, 14 1873.

Slept until 7 o'clk this m'g. Then up attended to my duties and had to wait until 9.15 for my [illegible]. Chatting for an hour — then over home. Prepared for church — not feeling very sleepy, I lay down upon the bed & slept until 12 — Heard when I woke that we walked [illegible] for half an hour near 2, when we had dinner — the day very cold. Ther. 48°. at 9 a.m. — cloudy — windy & showery P.M. — Sat about & did a little writing & a good deal of talking until after 4 o'clk. — Then I saddled Tom and had a ride that set my blood circulating. Tea at 6.30 and more talk. [illegible] myself, wrote letters home and to George — Went down and saw Fan for a little while. Home and to bed at 10.30
