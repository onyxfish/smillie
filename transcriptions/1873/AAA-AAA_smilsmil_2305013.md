## Left Page

### March Sunday, 9 1873.

Up about 8 — washed & dressed & went down to studio at 11 —
wrote until 12.30 — then home to dinner. Mother had a
heavy fall in Madison ave —
Smashed her glasses & cut
her forehead. — Went again
to studio soon after 3 and
wrote until 5 — Spending
the day very decidedly —
but am driven by necessity.
Home to tea at 6 — Suey
went to Bdway Tabernacle —
heard Rev. Dr. Taylor — after
sermon "All Hail the power of
Jesus' Name" was splendidly
sung by the congregation —
Home at 9.30 — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### March Monday, 10 1873.

morning opened with a snow storm
that made things look like the depths
of winter again — but by noon it was
bright sunlight and not a trace was left
of the snow. Got to studio a little
before 9 — Kindled fire & set to work
writing — Interruptions continue — Elisha
Mulford came in at about 10 & stayed
until 11.30, looking over my M.S. —
About 1 o'clk. Rosalie Goodman
& Mrs. Beers came in & stayed
for nearly an hour. — Home to lunch
at 2 — back at 2.45 — Lots of
interruptions again — Mr & Mrs. [illegible]
Stare & sister called — At 4.30 D —
went down in B'way cars with them —
went to Appleton's. Walked up to
studio — Home at 6.30 — At 8, at Dr.
Mulford's — Elisha looked over
my proof with me — home at
9.30 and to bed at 10.30.
