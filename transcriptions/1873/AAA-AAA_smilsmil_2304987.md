## Left Page

### January Wednesday, 8 1873.

Threatening weather with rain commencing before 5 P.M —
Got to studio at 9.30 — Worked all the mrg on water color —
when Angers criticised it so severely & justly that I determined to abandon it for the present, went next to 6th Avn & 22d St., Miller's, & bought an umbrella — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 & spent the P.M. planning out new subject.
Fred. finished drawing, Kate went with him to Plainfield —
Frost Johnston called — Went to Knoedler's then home at 5.30 —
made a large anatomical drawing for Dr. Throop — Tea at 8. Sucy.
Reading newspapers & Whymper's Alps — To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### January Thursday, 9 1873.

Delightful day. Got to studio at 9.15 and worked in charcoal, trying to arrange Horse-subject for water colors. To nictme. At 11.45 went to Kelmis and had hair cut, then to [illegible] C. A. — and called on Wm Hart, Louis Tiffany & Swain Gifford — then — just before 2, called Mr. [illegible] Newman at No. 262. 23d St. Home to lunch at 2 — back to studio at 2.45 — a note from Mary Avery — worked until 5 — home at 6 — Tea — dressed & at 8.30 was at S. P. Avery's — Besides the young ladies & Miss Whitney there was Miss Redfield Miss Stowe. Mr. [illegible] Stowe and E. L. Henry — a very pleasant evng was passed — music & chatting & at 11 I left — we sat down to supper — up soon after midnight and home about [illegible] — To bed at 1.15
Miss Bright & Miss Bruce called at the studio about 4.30
