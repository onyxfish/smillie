## Left Page

### August Tuesday, 12 1873.

Very cool m'f. Ther 62° at 7.30
AM, Rlcf= at that time — went
down Town soon after and spent
the m'f in the shops of Smith
& Burgess, trying to get some
drawing boards made —
Dinner about 1.30 — Down
town again with Dr. Mulford
& about until 5.30 — Tea at
6.15 — Several showers during
the day & thunderstorms in
the neighborhood, although
it continued quite cool —
in the evef, almost cold —
After tea, went into Mr. Ag[illegible]
Jeltrop's — several young
people danced & played games.
Home at 9.15 — no letters —
Read until 10.30 — To bed at
10.45

## Right Page

### August Wednesday, 13 1873.

Cold easterly storm — heavy rain all
day — After bkf't. Rigged myself with
boots & Rubber coat & went down
town, first to Burgess's — thru to Smith's
Back at work on board for me — there
most of the m'f — Then back on the hill
out to Fairground & about until near
1 o'clk — At 1.5, dinner — After
dinner, Read — Felt very heavy &
sauntered about, at last lying
down on my bed & sleeping for
an hour — Wrote a note to Miss
Sterling & a very short one home —
Tea at 6 — At [illegible] went to P.O. and
mailed letters — found a P.O. card
from Charl[illegible] Called at Mulford's —
there until 8.30 — then home
until 10.45 — chatting with Uncle
Wm. To bed at 11 —
