## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 21 1873.

Glorious m[illegible]. Up late, with a headache — Groomed my horse and went to bed again and did not get up until 1.30 — Still under the weather, but dressed & went over to dinner — After that, with all the family, came back & inspected the new studio, then, with the Misses Boggs and Flora Lathrop, went up to the Fair Ground & remained there until 4.30 — Sauntering about until Tea time — after tea, chatting until after 8 — then home — wrote to father and R.S. Moore — after 11 went to the P.O. To bed at 11.30 —

At 8.30 P.M. Kate Searle McCarty was delivered of a daughter,

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 22 1873.

Superb m[illegible]. Up at 6, feeling very well. "Joni" got an extra grooming. The carpenters fussing over the gable over his window. Strill came — went down town after bkft — to see about lumber. Back at 10.30 — wrote a note to Rea Diven with sketch of Kate Searle's baby. Commenced work on page drawing "Quebec from the River" — Down town again at 12.30 — Dinner at 1.30 — After 3 where again I got to work — at it until 4.30 — then with foot, about lumber — Near 5 started out on "Joni" & had a short ride — left leg quite sore with what promises to be little boils, just where I grip the saddle. Tea at 6.30 — Down town at 8 — called at Faris — then down to Searle's — there until 10 — then home & to bed at 10.30 —
