## Left Page

### July, Monday 21, 1873

*At Gay Head Lighthouse*
*Martha's Vineyard —*

Up at 5 o'clk & out & had a swim — back to bed & slept until near 8 — then up in a hurry & blfs — At 6.5 o'clk Fanny Will & I started with horse and light wagon for Gay Head, said to be about 24 miles from Oak Bluff. We drove through Vineyard Haven and West Tisbury until we reached the southerly shore of the Island & thence along to the head where we arrived at 2.45. The Ride, very monotonous but not without interest — so poor & barren — The extreme end a long neck with many ponds that made the road a devious one. Mrs. Pease, wife of the Light keeper promised to have dinner ready for us in an hour — we had lunched on crackers & cake that we brought in Mrs Haven & so went right to the cliffs — clambered down to the surf & along the shore — The appearance of the head, exactly the same, except that it is not quite so brilliant, as the Jayson Canyon in Cal. Back for our dinner at 3.30 — nearly & when we sat down to Ham & Potatoes, moist bread & coffee — I eat lightly, then all again went to the cliffs & wandered along the surf — tide coming in — breaking with force & churning with a strange, rattling noise — the small stones in and out — The sun going down, flooded the sea with a glorious light & the cliffs towered brilliantly white. Red, ochre & dull slate against a sky intensely blue. At 7.15 we ret'd to the light & from the balcony saw the sun go down behind the spar — then up into the Lantern & saw the process of lighting a first glass light & spent an hour — the deepening gloom outside & the slowly revolving light seeming to grow brighter & brighter inside — An hour or so chatting in the parlor — Then out to see the night light and at 10.30, to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Tuesday 22, 1873

Up shortly after 7 — blfs — then up in tower with Pease to consult as to color to paint his wall in watch room. Between 8 & 9 all ret't went across to the cliffs — went to southern end of cliffs & thence back along shore, stopping to make sketches — took straw & other umbrella. I had shade & pleasant camp. By one o'clk we had made two sketches in Water color — at this time there appeared an eruption of humanities — they swarmed along the beach — they made tiny pinnacles on the cliffs too — The Minnehaha, Steam yacht had landed about a hundred people & others had come down by wagon — the day was so gloriously inviting — got to the house — in a state of siege — Mrs. flying about, distracted — no dinner in sight for us — At last, going to the kitchen & in heat, steam & smoke, fought for & got our dinner — excellent sea bass — tea, tournip bread — At 3 Will & I again went out — took seats — cliffs were us & roaring tide coming up at our backs — looked like a beaver — driven by the tide — but at finished sketch — back to house — loth to leave the glorious outlook over the ocean now dazzling under the sun. At 4.30 good byes were affectionately said & started for home — the ocean I never saw look more glorious in its wide stretch of wonder — blue, except where it burned under the sun — the sky was without cloud & the breeze was sharp without a chill — the poor landscape pathetic in its sterility, green luminous and seemed tremulously joyous in the dreamy atmosphere & the monotonous roar of the surf came to us as we stopped to pick wild roses — honeysuckle — As we rode we were amused by the strange, nautical weather vanes — the names of ships up on the buildings — from the following boat crews — sword fish etc — and the names of ships up on the buildings — from West Tisbury, just as the sun was setting we rode across a wild waste of low oak bushes — passed West Haven in the firelight and just as it was dark — about 8.20 — we got to the Highland House — had tea at once — and then all gathered in Mr. K's Room & we went over the story of the past two days — To bed at 11.
