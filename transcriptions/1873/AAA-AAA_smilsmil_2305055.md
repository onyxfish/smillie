## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 3, 1873

Still wonderfully cool and clear. Up at 7. Had my hair shampooed & was generally brushed up. Had an outrageous breakfast & at 9.30 went to Mr. Whitney's where I was very cordially rec'd by Mr. & Miss W. — went to the works & saw W.W. & at 10.30 the carriage called & the ladies took me riding in Fairmount Park — back at 12.30 & Miss Mary took me to Chestnut from there up to Mrs. Harmer's — nat us — left Miss Mary & walked back to Mr. W's — Dinner at 3 chatting & looking over drawings until 5 — Then wrote a letter to Geo. and napped until 7 when W.J. Richards & Wife and Then W. & Mrs. Redfield Then W. & Mrs. Enoch Lewis. May & Davy — Had tea at about 8 and a very pleasant group until 11 o'clk — To bed at 11.15.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 4, 1873

Up at 7. Rain during the night and mts. rather foggy. After breakfast wrote awhile, about 10. went to the works with W.W. — wandered about until 11, heleftme at Rittenhouse Sq. and I went to 2016 Spruce St. & called on D.J.W. Lewis. Was there until 12 o'clk then rode over to 2224 Green St. & called on the Enoch Lewises. At 1.30 at the Whitney works. W.W. had a message with telegram for me — ruined & left him — Mary has a daughter — 4 o'clk, this mtt all well — great relief to me — Dinner at 2 — W.W., Miss M. & I out to Chestnut hill by 3.45 train. Weather delightful & enjoyed the views exceedingly — back to Germantown at 6.30. Then the carriage met us. Called on Mr. W.J. Richards — Home to tea, at 8 o'clk — after a most charming afternoon. Every, two Misses Baldwin, neighbors, called in. To bed soon after 11 —
