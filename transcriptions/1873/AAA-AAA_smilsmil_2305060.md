## Left Page

### June, Friday, 13, 1873.

Beautiful day — fresh & very cool —
Got down town, at a B.N. Co's about 9.30
& to work on Ginsch's die, putting a
circular ruled border about shield
for State arms. Lunched abt 1 o'clk
with the directors. Bt in Ruling —
very shallow line. Home a little
after 5, wrote a letter to Father,
enclosing one from Geo. Tea abt.
went to the studio a little before
7\. Waited until 7.30 to see the
Orr (late minister to Russia) funeral
pass. 7th Regt. & their band, splendid —
then to Emma Ireland's, to go with
her, Fanny & Will K. to Thomas's garden.
Mr. Cook, her son Ned & young Hoyt —
there until 9 o'clk — gave it up —
but chatting & duetts on the piano
until almost midnight — Home
& to bed at 12.15.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday, 14, 1873.

Still cool & delightful, but with in-
dications of a change in the weather.
Got to the studio at 9.40 — Fixed up
my engraving desk for work, and
etched in N.Y. State arms on Ginsch's
die. Had no drawing and nothing
transferred on to the ground on the
die. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back
at 2.45, worked on die until 4.30.
At 5.30 took boat at foot of 24th St.
for "Pleasant Valley", 5 or 6 miles up
the River, on the west bank. Mr. Winans
has a boarding ho. very pleasantly situated
on the River side & there Geo. is staying.
Very delightful sail — dinner about
6.30 — Even't. did a little wander-
ing about — then chatting in the parlor
and to bed at 10.p.o
