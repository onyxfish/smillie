## Left Page

### March Tuesday, 25 1873

A horrible day — cold, sleety &
ice covering everything — Got
to Studio at 9.20 — Finished
water color sketches "Lake Shore"
— Neniko, the Russians surprised
with a call — just arrived from
South America. Ab't 2 o'clk
went to A.B.N.Co. & made a
dead set at P. Steiner, to induce
him to buy Geo's big picture —
no go — Lunched there — back
to Studio at 2.30 — P.M. —
Commenced two W.C. Sketches
"an Adirondack clearing and
View in the Yosemito." Home
at 6.30 — After tea, at D wings
until 9.15 — Then mounted sketches
and to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### March Wednesday, 26 1873

An atrocious day — Ice ¼ of an inch thick
over everything — hardly possible to
walk — a cold mizzle until near
noon when it poured in torrents
until near mb — then it suddenly clear
— dark, windy night — Got to studio
at 9.30 & worked all day on Tiffany
"Mt Diabalo" to get it ready for the
academy exam. At two, prepared
lunch. At 5 washed up. Carried
Miss Garduer's board to her at Gramerc[y]
Sq. & home to tea at 7 — Wrote mail
to Ferguson and to bed at 11.
