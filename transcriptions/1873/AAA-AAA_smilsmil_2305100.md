## Left Page

### Sept. Monday, 1 1873.

Mf. threatening — very heavy shower with distant thunder at noon — clear
p.m. — & glorious moonlight night —
Up at 6 & spent an hour grooming
my horse — [illegible] at 7, 45. After
some messages got to work, sketch
of "Coming down the Rapids". worked
well on [illegible] until dinner time — 1.15
p.m. worked until 5 o'clk, saddled
horse — Rode over to Carmalts. There
they insisted that I should return
home & dress up — did it — Jos.
Mrs called for me at 6.15 — went
to Co — tea at 7. The Misses Butler,
Fanny Drinker & Mrs Gregory — Had
cards until 11 — a Ride home by
moonlight and to bed at midni[ght]

## Right Page

### Sept. Tuesday, 2 1873.

A perfectly glorious day — Up at 6.30
groomed my horse + [illegible] at 7.45
Spent the m'f at work finishing
"sketch" Old church at Tadousac — after
errands that kept me until 10.30 —
Dinner at 1.30. After dinner, again
worked on "Falls of Montmorenci — from the
river" — Soon after 4 o'clk Joe. Mrs
called — I saddled Tom and we went
off to find oat straw for bedding. Went
beyond the Tannery and enquired
at a number of places, but without
success — Had a grand old ride,
though. Got back at 6.45 — Uncle C.
& Sinnie arr'd from N.Y. About 6.30
went down to Searle's — played
whist until 10 — Then out with Joe &
Hetty & walked — Home & to bed at
11.
