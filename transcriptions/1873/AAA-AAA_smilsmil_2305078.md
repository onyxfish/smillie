## Left Page

### July Saturday, 19 1873.

Mild & overcast, several times almost clear. Up at 7 & bkfp. about 8 — loafed about & got to my work at last, but did little, owing to a headache. Dinner at 12.30, at 2 o'clk, Carrie, Fanny, Will & I started with horse & light wagon & drove to Edgartown — funny dull place — dead since the loss of the whaling trade. Drove down to the dock — hailed the Burnsby, there at anchor — all hands came ashore and we visited for some time. In answer to Kent's persuasive appeals, our grey horse lay down in the sand without breaking any thing — me drove on to Katama House — new, and getting ready to open next week — from there, over to the south shore, about half a mile away, where, as the tide was out, there was a very fine surf. The atmosphere effects very fine — soft, hazy & luminous, collected by the broad ocean surface. Drove back over a wide moon-like meadow, the only road was wheel cuts in the sod. At times a fog came down. Then a rift in the clouds & a stream of sunlight. Back to the hotel at 7.30. Then tea — an hour in the K's room — a walk with Will on the bluff. Some reading in Harper and then to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July Sunday, 20 1873.

Up about 6 — out with Will & had a tip top swim — the sun, warm — the water cool, quiet and clear — out in a few minutes & back to bed where I slept until near 8 — then up for the day — bkfp. about 9 — glorious morning. Spent most of it upon the front piazza overlooking the wonderfully blue water — dinner about 1 — at 2.30 went to church with Mr. Kimball, Carrie, Fanny & Mr. Benson — and the service a sort of Public Bible class — out at 4 o'clk. A great smoke from the west — fire in the woods — left the ladies & went across country a mile or two, to it. Fire running through the dry stubble, bushes and undergrowth, at times very fierce. It was quite a sight, the great masses of swirling, blinding smoke. The dull red flames, sometimes 3 or 4 feet high, lapping up everything before it — the men with shovels & long branches of oak fighting, digging to witching out the fire, now & then swallowed in the smoke. I did some work myself & tears ran down my face, the smoke was so thick. On my way back, climbed a sort of observatory & enjoyed a large horizon of intensest blue ocean. Back soon after 6 & had tea — after tea, walked with Fanny, Will, Mr. & Mrs. K. & Mrs. Benson on the bluffs. Had a very splendid sunset & saw, one after another, the half dozen light houses sparkle into the early night. Back between 8 & 9. Chatted awhile on the piazza — then to my room & finished letter to Father. To bed at 10.30.
