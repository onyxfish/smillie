## Left Page

### July, Tuesday, 29, 1873

Very muggy day with many thunderstorms — Bkfst — a little before 8 o'clk — All day at work on Canadian sketches — some finished with pencil, some with water color. Lunch at 1 — Afternoons, more interrupted with, by storms, making it very dark — Settled into a pretty steady rain that continued at intervals during the night — Tea at 6.30 — At 8, Charl + I again went to Thomas's Gardens — Slim attendance, but very good concert — Met there Dick Stout — Home at 11.10 + to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday, 30, 1873

Bright day, but very hot. Bkfst at 8 — All day planning, laying out + working up sketches for the wood cuts for Canadian article — Very much troubled with distress in my head — an indescribable weariness — Took a nap before lunch at 1 o'clk — Afternoon, worked on sketch for page block "Saguenay Riv". Tea at 6.30 — Mother in bed all day — Eve'g at 8 o'clk. Charl + Rose up to Central Park. Sat + admired Miss Stebbins's fountain by Calcium light — Then sauntered down to Uncle A.W.'s + had a game of billiards — Home at 10.45 — To bed at 11.15
