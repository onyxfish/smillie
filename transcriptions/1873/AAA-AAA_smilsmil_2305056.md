## Left Page

### June, Thursday 5, 1873

Superb day again — at 9.30
got away for Germantown in
carriage, with W.P. Whitney &
the Misses Baldwin — Went to
J.W. Richards's & turned over
his sketches for an hour — then
to Edith Wright's & had a very
pleasant call — Home to dinner
at 2 — after dinner a nap —
Between 4 & 5 o'clk called
on the Redfields, on the this
side of Logan Square and
at 6.30 went to Mr. Enoch
Lewis's to tea — met there
Miss Burnham and spent
a very delightful evening —
left at 10 — Glorious moon-
light night — W.L. walked
part way home with me —
To bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### June, Friday 6, 1873

Up before 7 — after bkfst. went to Mr.
Enoch Lewis's — 9.20 — Rode with
him, his sister & W.P. — in the East
Park until after 11 — Everything de-
lightful. At Fairmount left them
by car, rode to Dr. F.W. Lewis's — not
in — wrote a note & then on to the
Constitutional Convention — Spruce
near 6th St. Uncle Wm took me in
onto the floor & introduced me to
Curtin, Biddle, Mr. Vay & others.
There until 1.15 — Rode home — had
a nap & dinner at 3 — At 3.30
Messrs Enoch Lewis & W.J. Richards
came & with W.W. we all went
to Dr. S. Magoon's 1319 Girard Ave.
& saw his collection — almost a
storm — left at 5.30. Went to the work-
tion soon after [illegible], W.S.R., Edith
Wright & the Redfield girls —
Edith W. gave us some very fine
music on the piano — I took
her to Dr. Neale's at near 10 o'clk.
Fine showers during the
evening, To bed at 10.30
