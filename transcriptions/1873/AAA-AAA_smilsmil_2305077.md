## Left Page

### July Thursday, 17 1873.

Up at 6.30. Overcast day, threatening
a storm. B[illegible] at 7.30 — on [illegible]
until 9 — then to work on sketches
made while I was away — About 11
Cook came up from Edgartown
with his Yacht and at about noon
we got under way — Ned Cook. Brother
Kent — White & Cunningham — Fanny
K. Mrs. Benson — Mrs. Foster — Mr. Foster
Will K. & I, forming the party from the
Hotel.) With a light sea & a steady
breeze we had a jolly time — a
hearty lunch was served in the
cabin between 2 & 3 o'clk — After
that the wind increased & the
sea Rose & we did some tall
sailing — I went aloft & sat on the
gaff, enjoying hugely the motion
of the vessel — About 4 it began
to Rain & at 4.30 — we were landed
all having had a splendid time —
— a short nap and tea at 6.30 —
About 8. Fanny. Will & I. walked on
the bluffs, but Rain drove us in —
Evening, playing cards with the
K's & Mrs. Benson — Heavy wind
& Rain — To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### July Friday, 18 1873.

Stormy day — very heavy east wind and
some rain — Up at 7.15 — B[illegible] about 8 —
Worked with Water Colors over some of
my St. Lawrence Sketches until 12
o'clk. Then, as I had a headache, I
lay down until dinner time at
12.30 — After dinner took a long
walk about among the dog houses —
went through one of them — along
the beach — Seashore house — and
back — Cook & Party walked over from
Edgartown 6 miles — Ret'd by [illegible] but
we walked with them to [illegible]
& back just in time for tea — 6.30 —
After tea in the Kimball's Room —
there until after 10 o'clk. and
to bed at 10.45
Ther. varying from 50° to 60°.
