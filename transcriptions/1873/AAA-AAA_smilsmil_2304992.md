## Left Page

### January Saturday, 18 1873

A pre-eminently atrocious day even in this most atrocious season. Rain, sleet + a thin veneer of hard ice over everything. Got to studio at 9.45 finished outlining large drawing + prepared black + white drawing for [illegible]. Lunched in studio at 1.30 — Not liking to commence color on large drawing on the P.M. of last day of the week — and a very dark one at that, I worked on a large pencil drawing — Home to tea at 6.15 — Weather still very bad — Mary, Geo and I went down to Phil in carriage — found Miss Edith Wright in the seat next to me at the concert which was very enjoyable —
Home at 10.45 and to bed at 12.

## Right Page

### January Sunday, 19 1873

Brilliant m'f. Started for church but instead — as my head ached + the sky beginning to clear over + my desire to see the ice covered trees in the sunlight was great, I went up to Central Park. Some of the effects were marvellously beautiful, but the glistening ice mercilessly had worked sad destruction among the trees. Home at 12 — dinner at 1.30 — at 3.30 went to studio — fired up there somewhat + home at 5.45 —
Eve'g, went with Kate to B'way Tab'ce + heard D'r Taylor — Text 21-2-3°. Chapters of 1st Samuel — a bible history sermon with some lessons deduced — Home at 9.20. To bed at 11.
