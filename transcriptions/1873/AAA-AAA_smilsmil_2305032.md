## Left Page

### April, Friday, 18, 1873

Much of the day overcast. Up about 7 and disheartened to find myself not a bit improved. Matt & Mr. Parker arrived in time for breakfast. About 10.30 went to Studio — picked up letters — took a squint at a new model of Augear's & then went over to the N.A. of D. Found my one little picture hung outrageously, of course, was mad. Then to 4, M.f a. — called on Robbins & Britcher. Home at 12.30 — At Phil. Rehearsal at 2.30. Very much impressed by Rubenstein's manner in conducting the orchestra through his Ocean Symphony. After Rehearsal walked to N.A. of D. — then to Studio & home at 6 o'clk. — Evening about until 10 o'clk — then to bed.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday, 19, 1873

An April day of showers. Got to the Studio a few minutes before 9 o'clk. Kindled fire — Mr. Goodall came in soon after 9. and left details for a S.A., but not drawing. Walter Saterlee called — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back, after going to Knoedler's at 3 — Feeling very tired I took a nap. Home at 6 o'clk. Belle & Mary[?] — at tea. Evening went to Phil. Concert. I was more than ever carried away with Rubenstein. His peculiar magnetic power over orchestra & audience while "directing" his Ocean Symphony was marvellous. The audience went quite wild. I consider it one of the impressions of my life. I was escort for the J's, Matt & Kate. Home at 10.40. Got to bed just about 12.
