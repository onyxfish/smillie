## Left Page

### April Tuesday, 8 1873.

Still very much under the weather with my "Epizooty"
or Horse Distemper. After breakfast, made paste and
mounted some drawings
— got to studio at 12 o'clk.
Had my graham crackers
lunch there & carefully
cleaned and pasted in a
drawing of "Horse Racing in
the Yosemite" — at 4. went
over to Y. M. C. A. & called
on Brevoort, Frost Johnston
&c — then on Mrs. Greatbrey
& Silleck. Back to Studio
& home at 7 — well dying
about — to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### April Wednesday, 9 1873.

Miserable, overcast day that
finally ended in a drizzling
afternoon & wet night. — Altho'
still very much under the
weather from "Epizooty", got
to Studio at 9.30. Found a great
pile at my door which proved
to be ten sets of my Yosemite
article, in sheets. I spent
most of the day, poring over
them, folding, cutting & stitching.
Had crackers & tea for lunch, at
2 o'clk. Brevoort came, about
4 o'clk. & bid me good bye.
Wrote notes for pupils, arranging
to go home & stay until I recover.
Left a note at Emma J's & home
at 7 o'clk. At night, so horribly
stuffed up that I did not go to
bed but spent the night on the
sofa.
