## Left Page

### December 8 Thursday, 4 1873.

Still, horrible weather — wet, sloppy + misgling. Very much under this horrible weather. Got to Studio's about 10 o'clk + tried to work — Transferred Montmorenci Falls to large block. Went over to N.A. f[?] + saw Richards about our Water Color clptie arrangements. Home to lunch at 1.30 + back at 2.30 — Did little work my head hurt me so much. Near 5 o'clk — J. O. Eaton, Louis Tiffany + Nicoll came in answer to a call for a Board meeting — Stayed but a short time — Home to tea at 6.30 at 8.30. Called on Miss Mary Dewey at Park Godwins. 19[illegible] 37[illegible] b— In spite of my miserable feelings, had a pleasant call — all parties being so agreeable. Met Mr. Sanford Gifford there — left at 10 o'clk — Home + right to bed —

## Right Page

### December 9 Friday, 5 1873.

Up late after a miserable night. All the mucus membranes of nose and throat so sore that breathing was a constant pain — my head feeling as though as coal of fire was above + back of my eyes. Got to studio at 10.30 — Unable to work, so spent what remained of the [illegible], staining to imitate black walnut the wood recently put in by carpenters. The day dull + threatening. Home to lunch at 1 o'clk. At 2.30 at Phil. Rehearsal — Can hardly say that I enjoyed it. Walked home with Fanny [illegible] — then to Studio's at 5 — and of a night nearly — then home to tea. Went on sofa + remained there all night, not daring to go to bed, dreading a Repetition of last night.
