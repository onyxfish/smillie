## Left Page

### October, Sunday 5, 1873

Delicious day — very mild —
clouds flying and the loveliest
of autumn's effects — Gave Tom
a good currying and [illegible] —
at 8.30 — The rest of mf, cleaning
about & fussing — Went to Presb[yterian]
ch. & heard the Rev. Mr. Miller —
Text Acts 21st latter part 13th [illegible] —
Dinner at 1 — After dinner wrote
a letter to Father — Soon after 4
started out on Tom & had quite
a long ride — maybe 12 miles —
In at 6.30 — Tea at 7 — Even[ing]
wrote a letter to Fanny K. but
didn't send it — at 10.30 went
down to P.O. — Glorious moonlight —
back & to bed at 10.45.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 6, 1873

Raw, blustering storm of wind and
rain — the very opposite of yesterday.
Up late — went out to stable & surprised
to find Tom gone & in his place a
younger & handsome horse — Carnell
had the change made last night, to
surprise me. Bkft. at 8.30 — Rott.
strange left work this mf. Tried
to use the mf to work, but owing
to a return of the old distress
in my head, could do nothing —
Felt very much discouraged —
at 12.30 — Uncle Wm. went to Phila.
Dinner at 1 — Afternoon, did no
better than the mf, and at
last, about 4 o'clk. Saddled
"Patch" & went out for a ride —
Very little satisfaction — the colt
is very untoward — Called at [illegible]
Carnell's — In at 5.15 — Tea at 6 —
Even. at 8.30 went to Fan M's
after calling at Wm. Jessup's to bid his
daughter Lilian goodbye (she starts for
Syria to-morrow — Home to bed at 10.30
