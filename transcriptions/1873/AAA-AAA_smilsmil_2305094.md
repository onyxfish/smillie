## Left Page

### August Wednesday, 20 1873

Pleasant day, after a struggle — got to work between 9 & 10 o'clk — at my Canadian materials, trying to re-compose some of the subjects. Got very heavy about noon, and giving up to it, went across the Road & had a game of croquet. At 1.30 went to Fanny Drinker's. Dinner at 2.30. Mr Gregory, Jones & Jas. Carmalt & the Misses Butler — Sterlett (Episcopal Minister) Sylvanus Mulford — Jas Carmalt & myself — Coffee on piazza at 4 — then card tables under the trees & croquet. I played at croquet until 6 — then up to tea at 6.30 — After tea, tired & lay down for an hour — then bathed & dressed & at 8.45 to Mr. Wm. Jessop's where there was a pleasant & little company of about 30 — after 11 o'clk took Josie B. & Hetty Searle home then home myself & to bed at 12 o'clk.

## Right Page

### August Thursday, 21 1873

Mizzling, drizzling day — R[ose] a little before 8 o'clk — Soon after 9 got to work & spent the m[ornin]g preparing blocks & working over "Under Stingy Rock" — Dinner between 1 & 2 o'clk — Afternoon, between yawns, worked on tracing, transfer & outline of "Under etc" — Had to take a nap about 4 o'clk. Tea at 6 — tried a game of croquet after. Rec'd letters from Father — R.E. Moore, Will Kimball & Charl. — Then went out with aunt & Called on Mrs. Azur Lathrop's friends, the Misses Allerton — Then by Tyler — only saw his mother-in-law. Then at Mr. Dud. L's — Saw all of the family & home soon after 9 — Read "Hard Cash" until 11 — Then to bed.
