## Left Page

### Sept. Wednesday, 3 1873.

Delicious day — got to work some after hlfo- on block "Descending the Rapids" — worked until a little after 12 — then took a short walk before dinner at 1.30 — Dined at home — it was nearly 3 o'clk — before I got to work — at it until 5 — then saddled Tom and took a short ride — Tea at 6.30 — after, made part of a letter to father, must— to Crossman's with Aunt H — at 7 sht & called on the Reed's and Deal — about 7.30 a large wagon load of young people came for me and we went out to the pond and steamed around until 9.30, the moon finally obscured by clouds. Home at 10.30 — finished my letter to father & took it down to the P.O. To bed at 11.15 — Thunderstorm with very heavy rain during the night.

## Right Page

### Sept. Thursday, 4 1873.

Day opened foggy & unfit promising — afterwards, cloudy, but pleasant. Up early & "groomed" Tom — hlfo- at 8 — messages down town until 10.30 — Then to work on "Descending the Rapids until dinner at 1.30 — After 3 when I got to my work and at it until after 5. Then saddled Tom & with Uncle A & Junie in buggy, went to the new depot & saw the train come in at 5. Back and in at 6.30 after a very pleasant ride. Bathed, dressed and tea at 7.30 — After, a short chat with ladies in Mrs. Lathrop's parlor — then home, more chat and to bed at 10.
