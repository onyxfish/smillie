## Left Page

### June, Saturday, 7, 1873

*at Mr. O.B. Tweedy's*
*Plainfield*

Still delightful weather; clear &
very cool. After slept. packed my
bag & read until 10 o'clk. then
to the works. At 10.30 Carriage with
Mr. & Miss W. came & with Mr. W.
we drove to — Bements in Spring
garden. bet. 17 & 18th St. & wandered
through his halls & galleries for
some time. Then, leaving Mr. W.
at the works, we drove to Earle's
to see Curtin's picture. The portrait
of the Emperor of Russia — back at
1\. dinner a little before 2 — at 2.
40 adieu, were said & I was driven
to the Station in West Phila. Started
at 3 o'clk. & after a very fresh, pleasant
ride got to Elizabeth at 5.20 — at
5.35 the Plainfield Train came along.
Met Alf. Kimball, his bro & wife — Got
to P. at 6 & went directly to the [illegible]
Tea about 7 — Eveng & beat on
the Piazza. Glorious moon-
light night. Suffering very
much from effects of my dinner
so that I did not get to sleep
until after midnight. To bed at
10.30.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday, 8, 1873

Superb day — mf. went with Mary
to the Episcopal Ch. & heard the
Regular Pastor preach a Trinity
sermon from Matt. 28th ch. 19th v —
After service I wandered about
until communion was over then
walked home with Mary. Dinner
about 1.30 — Between 3 & 4. p.m.
Mary, Charley, Nell, Cock and I
walked around by the new house
& away across town to Mr. Chapins
where, by a small stream & under
some trees we spent half an hour
with Mr. & Mrs. C. — then on, and
near 6 I called at Uncle George's
for a few minutes & saw them all.
Tea at 6.30. Eveng. Belle and
I went to the Presb. Ch. & heard
a Mr. Carey from Canada. After
service a short walk & all sat
on the Piazza until after 11 —
To bed at 11.45.
