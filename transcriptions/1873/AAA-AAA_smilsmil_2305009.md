## Left Page

### March Saturday, 1 1873.

Pleasant day — Got to studio at 9.15 — Kindled fire & set to work writing. Revising &
making a fair copy — home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30 — wrote until 5 when
Johnston, art man on the Every Week came in — then
until 6 — a wild, unmannered bohemian. — Home to tea at
6.30 + at 8, at studio again + wrote until 10.30 — then
home and to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### March Sunday, 2 1873.

Mild, raw, and rather threatening. — Mrs[?], went to Dr. Hastings' + heard him preach from 2nd
Thess. 3rd ch. 10th v. "He that will not work, neither shall he eat" —
a capital discourse. After service walked down to studio
+ fixed fires. Home to dinner at 1 — a nap after — at 5
went out to walk — took tea at Uncle Ais — Home at 8.30
wrote a little — talk — and to bed at 11.45
