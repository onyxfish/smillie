## Left Page

### August, Tuesday, 26, 1873

Charming day — much better but suffered more or less
during the day from pain —
During the mtf, cleared
up my Room — did some writing
and a little drawing — Dinner
between 1 & 2 — After dinner
Dr. Taylor, wife & son Mr. [illegible] an hour with me looking
over my sketches & work —
At 4 o clk. Joe Williams
came along with horses &
three seated wagon. Vic. &
Mrs. Gregory, Hetty S. & Josie
Beatty — We had a glorious
Ride for two hours — back to tea
at 6.15 — Every went to the
Searles's with Vic & the Misses
Butler — Cal, Jack until 10 —
Home & to bed at 11 —
Rec'd. letter from George —

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday, 27, 1873

Pleasant day — worked all this
morning on sketch of Breakneck
[illegible] "Quebec" — and dinner at
1.30 — After dinner worked until
near 4, when I finished sketch,
dressed, went down town, after
considerable walking, found
[illegible] & complained to him of
the studio lumber — back to tea
at 6 — Early eve, with aunt
H. called at Deacon Bard's. Then
I made a call on Mrs. Wm. Jessup —
"Phantom Party" at Mr. Crossman's
Uncle W. & Aunt H. went there — I
called on Priscilla Paine, did not
see her — too unwell — Then went
to Riley's. Found there Fanny Jessup
Sam Jessup & wife — Isaac
R. gave a funny exhibition
"how not to Read" — Home
and to bed at 11.
