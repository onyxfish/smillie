## Left Page

### October Friday, 17 1873.

Delicious mng after the threats of last night. Up at 6.45 —
with Patch until 7.45. Bkfs.
at 8 — Too cool to work at my
painting out of doors — so the
mng was spent working about
my studio's building, piling
away lumber, etc. Dinner at
2 o'clk. A numberd strangers to
dinner. Afternoon, before 3 o'clk
saddled "Patch" and with water-
color traps, rode over to the
Mulford farm to get sketches of
calves — glorious pm — but the
calves would'nt stand still &
at last, after 4 o'clk, I gave up
beaten, after making arrangements
to return to-morrow. Fan, just
Mrs. Jenks, with Ben M.: over on a
expedition — Rode over to Sayre
farm to get some notes of effects —
Home at 5.30 — Tea at 6 — I & [illegible]
& went down to call on Emma
Richards — not in — went down
town for letters. Stopped in at
Jan's — Home before 9.30 — To bed
at 10.30 — Routed out at 1.15 by alarm
of fire — Ran down to [illegible] — little

## Right Page

### October Saturday, 18 1873.

building opposite Baptist ch.—
Very high wind & quite an excitement
To bed again at 2. Up at 7.
Threats of a storm. Bkfs. soon
after 8. Wouldn't go to Mulford's
farm to paint calves — not with Corrill
to select oak timber. A large
part of the mng, picking apples
with Carrie. Dinner at 1 —
Afternoon, weather improved —
at 3, saddled "Patch" & rode over
to Carmalts — Gen. Rode over in
buggy — visited there for an hour.
Left Patch & Ret'd with George
at 5.30 — Mitchell painted half
of the mng on Studio — at 6 o'clk
went down to Fan M's to tea —
there until 10.30 — Home and
to bed at 11 — Rec'd letter
from Father enclosing P.O.
order for $50.00 —
