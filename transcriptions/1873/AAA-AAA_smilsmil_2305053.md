## Left Page

### May Friday, 30 1873.

Glorious m[or]n, up at 5.30. [illegible] slept well until 2 o'clk this mrn, but is still very unwell. He insists that I shall not wait — so at 7 o'clk went to Chrns [?] L. Ferry — ½ an hour too early — wrote a note to Mr. G[?] Whitney — off for Montrose on 8.10 train. Met Mr. [illegible] Shurtleff, Probate Judge of Springfield. Mass & had a [illegible] time — Got to Montrose Station at 3.30 — very clear & winmd cold from the N.W. Got to town at 5.20, thoroughly chilled — went at once to Uncle W's Nearly 7 when we had tea. Wrote to Father and mailed it — got to bed at 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### May Saturday, 31 1873.

Still wonderful weather — Up about 7 o'clk — Frost last night. [illegible] at about 9 — Spent the mrn until 11 with Uncle Wm. planning about site for studio. Then went down town — Met Fau. Mulford with her until noon — Then about until 1 o'clk — dinner. at 2.30, at Fau's — horses were brought up & we went out and had a delightful drive, down the creek Road, the across & home at 6 over the old friends ville Road. Took tea at Fau's at 7.30 went after Mr. Coterill the builder, to get an estimate with him until after 8 o'clk Then home with Uncle Wm. To bed at 10 o'clk.
