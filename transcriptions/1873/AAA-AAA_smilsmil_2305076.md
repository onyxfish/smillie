## Left Page

### July, Tuesday, 15, 1873

*at Mr. An°. Nickersons. 77 Worcester St.*
*Boston*

Up at 6.30. Left baggage with Baggage man on the wharf, & at 7.30 went to Parkers to where we had a tip top bkfst. — Read our papers & had an easy time, then sauntered to Tremont & along to the Common where, under the shade of a big tree me slept a good part of a very hot nap. — the breeze being delightful, went over Public garden & around — then left Father in a shady place while I went over the burnt district — It was intensely hot — I ran to Father — got a cab — went to boat, got our baggage & then drove to Mr. Nickersons where we were very warmly welcomed at 2.15 — Dinner at once! — then a nap. At 5.30, Mr. & Mrs. N. Father & I went on a driving — had a most charming time. We went out on Beacon & Road to Chestnut Hill Reservoir — to Somerswood — Brookline — Roxbury & Jamaica Plain & home at 8. N°. A Shawmyer and tea at once — Eve'g, chatting with the family, Mr. Minuslein &c until near 11 o'clk — then to bed —

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday, 16, 1873

*Highland House. Martha's Vineyard*
*Room No. 28.*

Up at 6.30 — a delightfully cool mng. Bkfst at 8. — Soon after 9, bid goodbye & with Father & Mr. N., went to the Boston & Albany depot & got Father started for N.Y. on the 10 AM train. Then with Mr. N. walked through portions of the burned district, through Summer, Washington & Tremont Sts. Stopping at Art Club & Public Library in Boylston St — where I had good bye to Mr. N. Then to Prov. & Boston R.R. & for Martha's Vineyard on 11.30 train — Got to New Bedford at 1.15 & then on boat to "Martha's Vineyard" — Reached "Camp Ground Landing" a little before 4 o'clk — met Will K. on the dock — went at once to the house & found that a Room had been engaged for me. Chatted with the K's until tea time — about 6.30. About 7.30 started out with Will K. & went to the town & brought things to bathe in — Then around & about the campground. Surprised that grown people can amuse themselves for a whole season in such dor[k] houses — without the fun of "camping out" on the comfort of home. — Back up before 9 & about until 10.30 — then to bed — disturbed during the night by Cholera morbutic people —

About two hours after my arrival at Martha's V. — Ned Cook arrived with his yacht — "Burnsby".
