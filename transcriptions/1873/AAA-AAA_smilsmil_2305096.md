## Left Page

### August, Sunday, 24, 1873

Very cold & clear. Ther. did not
reach 60° all day. Immediately
after getting up, my bottom fell out
but there was no pain. I was very
anxious to hear Isaac Riley, so
went to the Presbt. Ch, & heard
him preach a very good sermon
from Acts 8th ch, 4th v., but I was
cold & uncomfortable & went home
feeling much pain. No dinner.
Afternoon on back piazza in the
sun until after 4 when I gave
up & went to bed. Uncle W. &
Aunt H. very kind & brought me
tea to my bed. Got through
the night with comparative
comfort.

## Right Page

### August, Monday, 25, 1873

Much milder — overcast, and at times
looking like rain. Routed out at
7.30 — Aunt H. gave me my blsfo[?], then
back to bed & kept there until noon,
up & dressed, went over to Mr. S.'s
& took a light dinner. P.m.
on my bed again — considerable
of neuralgic-like pain in the
stomach. Tea at 6.30. Evef.
some timber for studio am[?].
Uncle Wm. & Aunt H. went out, so
Carrie & I spent the evef. in
the kitchen by the stove. I
wrote a long letter to Meta A.
To bed at 10.30 and slept quietly
through the night.
