## Left Page

### January Thursday, 2 1873.

Nasty day - up late - got to
studio at 10 - There met Wilt Kimball
Charcoaled 20×24" — Tenacy a
Canon from Glacier Rock" —
Prepared lunch abt 1.30. Made
calls on Mrs. Beers & Miss Harmon
about 3. Miss Emma Beach, Miss
Bullard & the Sisters Buckingham
call — Spent a jolly hour —
Later Mr. Redfield of Phila. came
in — then John Durand — Then
a young lady waiting for Augero.
Home to tea at 6 — Pre. Will took
tea with us — at 8 o'clk — at Mr.
Walter Brown's — with him went
to Union League to see pictures —
Then to my studio — also I went
home — Raining — To bed at
11.

## Right Page

### January Friday, 3 1873.

The worst day yet. Very foggy,
dark, warm, mizzling, damp and
in every way atrocious. Got
to the studio about 9.30 and set
to work on Water Color "Sunlight
on a Shadowed Path" — Find
that I left it in a very bad
condition last winter — Commenced
by washing a good part of it
out. King, a painter that I've
met in the valley (Yos.) called
about noon. Prepared lunch
at 1 — Some after 2 walked down
to Ph. Rehearsal. Mary Whitney
there — walked with her to Avery's
then down to Appleton's — back to
studio & home to tea at 6.30.
Matt & Ed. went to Po at 3.45 P.M.
Spent the eve'g looking over
English papers — To bed a little
before 10.
