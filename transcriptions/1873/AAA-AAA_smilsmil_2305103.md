## Left Page

### Sept. Sunday, 7 1873.

Overcast again — After 7 when I got up — gave my horse a good dressing and [illegible] at 9 o'clk. — went to the Baptist Ch. I heard Mr. Cheshire preach on faith vs materialism. After service, went with Uncle Wm + called on his mother. Dinner at 1.30 — after dinner, a nap — About 4.5 saddled Tom — then it began to rain hard, but I had a delightful ride of at least 10 miles, part of the way through dark, dripping beach woods. [illegible] at 7. Eve'g wrote a letter to [illegible] — posted it, then read until after 11 o'clk — Then to bed —

## Right Page

### Sept. Monday, 8 1873.

again, delightful weather — Stable duties until near 8. R[illegible] at 8 — went down town to do some messages, Uncle Wm met me + took me to the builders to see columns making to front piazza — was kept there until after 11 o'clk, fussing — then home + put things to rights — at 12 went to bid the Misses Butler good bye — Time broken up until dinner about 1.5 — Worked on block "Breakwich [illegible]" — until near 5 — Saddled Tom + rode until 6.15 — Sea at 6.45 — Eve'g, spent an hour at Searle's playing Cal. Jack — home at 9.30 To bed at 10.15. Glorious moonlight night.
