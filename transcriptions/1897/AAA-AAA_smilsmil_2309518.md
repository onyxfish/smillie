## Left Page

### Tues. April 20, 1897

Uncle Willis, aunt Agnes \+ Miss Earle lunched with us.

**Weather:** Clear \+ cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear \+ cold — a sharp frost last night. To studio at 10 \+ more writing — to see \+ see — also to Accounting Co. of Topeka — Meant \+ hunted for Commissioner for State of Kansas. Went up to 42d St \+ then to 33d St. \+ Bdway \+ there found a woman who took my acknowledgment of the deed selling the Wall farm to nobody for $1.00 \+ she chg'd me a dollar for it. Then lunch —
Afternoon, more writing \+ some general fixing up preparatory to starting on Etching work —
Evg'y Read to Ralph — a little reading for myself — and a little writing — \+ then to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 21, 1897

**Weather:** fine day — clear \+ crisp  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine day — clear \+ crisp. To studio about 10:30. Miller, the carpenter, called at the house [illegible] after bhp'd. \+ kept me. See the day in studio was spent cleaning up my printing table \+ press, wetting down paper \+ preparing for printing to-morrow. It was thoroughly done \+ it was hard work. Home to lunch, as usual, — dinner, as usual. Evg'y. Read to Ralph \+ Jim until 9:00. Then Charl, who has been to Boston for some days past, came in \+ stayed until after 11 — I went out on my wheel \+ rode from 10 to 11.
