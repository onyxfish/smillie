## Left Page

### Mon. April 12, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — After my mf duties I went to Tax Com. office. — Bdway + Chambers St. + so presented my Petition in the matter of assessment of Personal Tax in state of Anna C. Smillie that the whole of it was remitted, greatly to my relief. Then I walked down to A.S.N.Co. in Trinity Place + saw John Dos about a mezzotint Rocker that he wishes me to buy — then to 42d S. + saw Mr. Bell about our wheels — Then home — [illegible] back book + went to 2d Nat. Bk — stopped at D.B. Butler's to get remnant of velvet — home to lunch. Afternoon aside with J.+F. — to Spauldings — each of us taking our wheels, + spent some time with Mr. Bell's trying to get fitted out with saddles etc — Then home + had a rest to prepare for a campaign at N.A. To-night — dinner — at N.A. Soon after 8 — a protracted Council meeting — the Tariff being discussed with a good deal of bad temper + absolutely nothing gained for anybody — it was the most dreadfully discouraging session that I ever sat through — The council did not adjourn until 11 — Perry + Watrons sat with us — then there was a short session of Site Com. where a little Routine work connected with the architectural competition was done. I got home at about midnight very tired + very unhappy.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 13, 1897

**Weather:** A fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — strong south easterly breeze. Did not get to studio very early — but at last got started on the repainting of Sauber's Key Mission — but before I had got really warmed to my work, A. Van Cleef came bringing with him the larger portion of the file of N.A. P.D. Catalogues — He wanted money + he was willing to sell, so, upon his promise to bring the bal. of the set before the end of the week, I paid him $25 —. Then I spent some time re-arranging my set + home at 1 — dinner — back to studio — worked until 4 — then feeling that I needed some fresh air + exercise very much
