## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 4, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day, bright \+ mild — but I was in no condition to enjoy it being stupified by a beast of a headache. I kept up all day altho' it was very hard work. I spent the w[h]e at the studio mostly hanging pictures \+ putting paper etc into place. Before 12 went to N.A. \+ straightened out my affairs there, then to 2° Nat'l. \+ then home to lunch — a perfunctory function. To studio before 3 o'clk. \+ fooled about until 6. The gas fitter completed the gas connection under stone of my printing table \+ I begin to feel ready for work. After dinner I took boy Kites \+ Reel around to Charl's rooms \+ then loitered about the streets until after 9 — then home to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Farrington case.

Mild \+ very pleasant, altho' at times it looked quite showery. To studio soon after 9 — completed the hanging of pictures \+ some other like work — Then I wrote a note to Mr. Carpenter of Warren, R. I., who has very kindly given me permission to keep the Genl. Greene miniature yet longer. At 11.30 went to N.A. transacted my business there. Then to Bdway \+ 32° S° \+ saw Mr. Harry Beuty, Adv'g agent for N.A. \+ then home to lunch at 1.45. At 3 went to Municipal Bldg. in Centre S° office of Ass't Dist. Att'y Lloyd — met there Kingsbury \+ Gardner — Ass't Dis. Att'y Campbell was also there — we talked the Farrington business over. I don't think that Mr. Lloyd grasps the methods of J. T[o]n — He frankly said he does not like complicated cases. He also said that such a case should not go before any judge as some cases are better suited to one than another — he suggested Judge Gaff as a good one to try this case — He offered to propose the change to the Ass't who tries cases in Judge G.'s court \+ I approved. Then to N.A. there until 5.30 — then to studio \+ home to dinner at 6.30 — Even't at 8 went with the boys meeting of Agassiz Ass'n. manhattan Chap. in "the Den", 141 E. 40° S° — Miller, the Carpenter \+ his family connections appear to be about the whole of it — The Walking Fern was the subject under discussion — Home at 9.30 — found bro. George there.
