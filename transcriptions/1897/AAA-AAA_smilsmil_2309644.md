## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 30, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant, altho' overcast part of the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant, altho' overcast part of the day - I got to studio at usual time - did a little work on Susq. charcoal sketch, \+ something on the frame \+ mount of Etching Road to Lake, but most time was spent writing on Report \+ Papers in connection with the Farrington settlement. To NA after 3. directing Grumnis until 4.30 - Bldg Com. met - all present \+ both Carere \+ Hastings. An important \+ interesting meeting. C. \+ H. made a proposition \+ had with them drawings to illustrate it, that as the school part was ordered to be built it would be possible to put up the 110' 5: facade, that portion of the Bldg to be ultimately to be used for Galleries \& let at first to be made suitable for the schools. This very new \+ interesting proposition led to a discussion of what some of us in the com. consider fundamental objections to the plans as they now are. I urged particularly that the basement or first floor on the north side should not be given over to water closets, cloak rooms, toilet office \+ Restaurant. I suggested the possibility of putting the entrance on Amsterdam Ave. That was not well recd but my proposition to rearrange the first floor in the north side for a permanent Retrospective exbn did get serious consideration. The discussion was very spirited until after 6 when we adjourned, the Com. to meet Sat. P.M. to discuss C \+ H's proposition \+ report to them as soon after as possible. To dinner at 6.45 every read awhile to Raelph - some more NA writing \+ to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 31, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain, with wind, all day. To studio at about 10 \+ was until near 12 engaged writing a sort of historical Report of the Farrington case. Then to NA of D. \+ made out a number of checks to meet the pay roll. Then to Second. Nat'l Bk. \+ then to McCrearys \+ bought an umbrella for myself, having, at last, sadly given up hope of finding the beautiful umbrella that Annie gave me a short time before her death \+ that so mysteriously disappeared about a week or two ago. It was 1.30 when I got home to my lunch. At 2.30 I had to go to Tel. office in 3d Ave. to send a telegram to Homerdee. To studio \+ did some work on Susq. charcoal study - set it \+ stretched it on board. Dinner at 6.30 - Farrington's written confession came while I was at dinner. This fills all of my requirements. Read awhile to Ralph - then wrote a long letter to H.T. Kingsbury giving history of Farrington settlement - to Homerdee \+ to Beckwith It was 11.30 when I got to bed \+ before I got to sleep I heard the midnight din of the old year out \+ the new year in.

Jim went to a boy's party at Ned. Thurston's \+ got home soon after 11.
