## Left Page

### Sun. March 21, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful, mild closing day. I had a hard night, painful head, restless sleep \+ doleful dreams — so, I did not get ready for my elkp. much before 10 — immediately after, I went up for Yale, found him at home \+ he walked back with me to 38th St. He cannot be sure yet whether Grettie has measles or no — Katie T. is blossoming out \+ is very weak \+ sick. Having no stamina to back her — knowing this I am anxious — After Yale's visit I went to studio \+ opened the windows to give the place a thor' airing. Then to Walter's \+ saw him \+ Louise — It was ¼ past 7 when I got home — I at once gave K— a thorough scrubbing bath in afortable his standing in the sunshine \+ then got him back ups on his bed again — where he had his dinner — Jim dined with us at home — he is still one of Alice Ireland's family — K— left his room for the first time \+ went to the dining room, which was still sunny — The bedroom was thrown open as much as possible \+ flooded with fresh air \+ sunshine until near 9 o'clk — The house \+ air outside being of gentle temperature made it a very good [illegible] for R's so called from his imprisonment from 5 to 8.30 I got a little needed rest for I am sick — have slightly abnormal temp. trouble with bowels \+ pain in head — It makes me feel depressed \+ wretched — Read Hiawatha to R— until I was quite tired out — After he got to bed Gel and Chark came \+ stayed until after 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 22, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m't., clouded afternoon — rain beginning to fall at about 5 — Showers later thro' the night — It was about 10 when I got to studio — I wrote quite a long letter to [illegible] Nash, Stone \+ other Montrose \+ then home before noon \+ gave Ralph his second scrub in the bath tub — After lunch I went to 2nd Nat'l. Bank — Then to Schauss' \+ saw Barbizon paintings — Then to Macbeth's \+ saw minor pictures — Then to Cottier's \+ saw Horatio Walker's pictures — \+ then to Durand-Ruel \+ saw the Roman impressions of Pissaro's — then at about 3.30 — having seen enough of pictures I went to my chap. — Having written in the m't. to Nash \+ Montrose giving him instructions as to lettuce planting — \+ to Stone about garden, I continued my bucolic correspondence by writing to Cooley about cisterns \+ to Gill about minding Alice's house — Then I wrote [illegible] for Nellie in preparation for to-night's session at the Arch. Hospital affairs at home [illegible] encouraging — Both Kate T. \+ Gretta are very wretched — K— getting up to 105 [illegible] \+ 104°. Trained nurse, miss Page, came at night to care for Katie — much to my comfort as most of the Feminities about the house are more or less hysterical. Many money is doing good service \+ \+ I went to N.A. Council meeting until 9 — Then Site Com. — all present — a communication from a number of younger architects desiring some sort of volunteer competition to supplement the close comp'n was Rec'd \+ Read leading to a very confused \+ confusing discussion — I advised that we defer the matter back to the N.Y. Federation — asking for further advice. It is thought desirable by all that the younger \+ comparatively unknown men should have a chance. It is desired that this shall be a model competition — the reference to the N.A.I. was made as suggested — Geo. \+ I walked up together. Harry Watson's invited the Com. to dine with him at Hellicon Vague Club a week from Sat. night Bessie went to Opera with Walter Cook \+ party.
