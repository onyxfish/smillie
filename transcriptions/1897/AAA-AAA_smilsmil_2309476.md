## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 26, 1897

**Weather:** Very cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold day \- got to studio after 10 \+ nearly all of the m'g was spent in cutting mount from very heavy pulp board for "A Path etc." \+ pasting it into it \+ then putting it in the screw press. Home to lunch \- Afternoon worked on old drawing "In a Normandy orchard." Drew the form of a calf \+ carefully washed the underlying color off \- then painted it in \- did not quite finish it \- then to N.A. \+ had a siege with Farrington trying to get some puzzles explained \- Ferry is making much capital out of some misstatements of N.A. fund figures that he charges upon me for campaign purposes only \- in making a general statement more of my site reports giving only approximate \+ speculative ants. I underrated the ant. available for site \+ building purposes by about $14,000 altho' my published reports were (Treas. Rep.) exactly correct. It was this discrepancy that he discovered \+ he "went for me." Dinner at 6.30. W.F. Read a little while to the boys \- at 9 went to Century \- had a pleasant little chat with Abbey \- Then saw Ferry \+ I "went for him" \- we managed to get things straightened out without bloodshed \- then I had a few minutes with Rev. Hy. Vandyke \- Then with Nicoll \- Then home at 10.30

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 27, 1897

*The second Anniversary of Annie's death.*

**Weather:** A dull \+ very disagreeable day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull \+ very disagreeable day \- some snow during the day \- a heavier fall at night. It was after 10 when I got to the studio \- I was not in the humor to accomplish much in the way of work to-day \- Most of the m'g was spent in cutting a mount for "Montrani Path near Ravello" \+ getting it into the frame \- Mary Cook came in for a few minutes \- Home to lunch \- afternoon I put a calf into my old W.C. drawing of "A Normandy Orchard" \- Home at 5.30 \- Emma giving to Jim his music lesson. I told her that, unexpectedly, I found that I had an extra cast of Annie's bust \+ I gave it to her \- Dinner at 6.30 \- the girls told the story of Jordon Blair who cut his own leg off \+ Olga White rolled over in a faint \+ then had a few hysterics from which she was good enough to recover after a short time \- Sheldon came in with a ticket for the Gas Exposition at Mad. Sq. Garden. I gave Jim money \+ he went with him \+ got back at 9.30. The girls had company in the parlor \+ I did a little writing in the dining room \- Chase came in \+ kept me talking till after 11.
