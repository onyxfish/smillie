## Left Page

### Fri. March 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nasty, wet day — part of the time raining heavily. To studio at 9.30 \+ oiled out the parts of Vesuvius that had "sunken in" — About 11 men came with the frame joint — also frames for "Cathedral Tower, Ravello" \+ "Vineyards near Ravello". I got Vesuvius into to frame \+ did some work in the way of glazing. Men home to lunch. Back \+ got Water Colors into frames. The Cathedral Tower is as near right as I know how to make it but the Vineyards need work on the foreground — clearing shadows, etc. — I worked at it until Jerry came \+ took the three to the Customs at about 4.30 — then I spent an hour \+ a half cleaning house. Home at 6 — dinner — Went with the boys until about 9 — then Charl came in — about 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 6, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Made final payment on N.A.f.D. Site.

Ralph taken down with measles.

Cleared pleasant, crisp \+ cool — After my duties I went to N.A. Warrington not there — Then to see Lee's, 20 Nassau St. T. W. Wood there, then to Connell's, the broker \+ saw him — Men to Central P. \+ saw Mr. Sherman \+ arranged our affairs so that I could draw checks \+ have them certified amt to $23000. Then back to L. \+ L. \+ got Wood \+ took him to C.T.Co. where we signed the checks — mine went rapidly \+ as Sat. is only a half business day Howard Kingsbury was sent by Mr. Lee to hurry us up so that the deed could be recorded before 12 o'clk. We answered to Sammis's office in Liberty St. \+ got through the last formalities — the purchase of the 10th St. Site at 11.53 A.M. Then we separated — I went to N.A. f.D. \+ had [illegible] at his inst. Co. Check stubs in order, recording the closing transaction. Then home to lunch a little late — (1.45) — went to B. Fall's after [illegible] \+ saw him — He thinks that Jim's ailing is a grippe \+ also that Ralph is preparing to come out with the measles — I felt tired \+ heavy — under the reaction following the closing of the site business so I went to bed \+ had a very sound sleep — was on a lounge in Mary's room — I went to studio \+ then ate. home \+ gave R. a warm bath \+ got him into bed before dinner time — there is no well defined symptom yet but Miss can be little doubt that it is measles — dinner — Away. Bessie went with Charl to Phil. Concert. It was 9 before I got to century — I found Vesuvius in the west P.S. from the galleries — centre in front of entrance — I hung it feet back \+ as it was necessarily lifted above the line, in act. for the fine placed under it the high horizon of the picture looked rather awkward. Some of the ladies weak in color \+ lacked naps in the foreground — nevertheless many pleasant things were said to me about it. I had talks with several members of the Site Com. concerning the architect question that is to come up on monday every [illegible]. Home at 11.30.
