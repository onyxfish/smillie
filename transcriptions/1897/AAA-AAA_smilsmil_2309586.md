## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudless \+ very warm day \- no air stirring \- not much improvement that I can recognize in my condition. At 10 I went to Dr. G's \- saw him \+ got some medecines. I kept about out of doors most of the aft., \+ about noon I commenced writing a letter to Jim. Dinner at 1.30 \- after that I lay down for awhile \+ after a rest I completed my letter to Jim \- Evg, so warm \+ the moonlight so beautiful that the family gathered on the piazza. I went to P.O. with letters about 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 6, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Chas left us for N.Y.

A very warm day, very still, cloudless \+ hazy \- I got up feeling a little better \+ hoped to be able to do some work, but when I tried to get things ready to take pp. from Rect. Green I had to give it up. I felt very weak \+ wretched all over \- I think I may have an attack of grip in a new form \- I wandered about all day when I was not lying in my bed. Loomis came in the mft with his dynamite outfit \+ gave Rutan \+ his mate some object lessons in the use of dynamite \- He blew up several of the large rocks \+ broke them into such shape that the men could handle them \- Then Rutan \+ mate continued the work. Charl left us on the 12.20 Train much disappointed because he had not been able to raise his box Kites \- Dinner at 1 \- nothing worth recording after that.
