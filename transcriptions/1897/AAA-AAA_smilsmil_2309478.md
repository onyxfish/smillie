## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 30, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another brilliant day — not quite so cold. Got to studio soon after 9 \+ started work again on 33" x 44" — "Vesuvius" — Gave to sky \+ distance a good sand papering \+ then began to repaint. Beckwith came in to say that he had an engagement that w\. prevent his coming to my house to-morrow eve\*g. We talked over the N.A. cite situation —. Carman came \+ got paintings for Utica. Home to lunch at 1 — walked on painting until 4 — much discouraged because I cannot get what I desire of color \+ atmosphere — Already I have got sky \+ distance into a state of weak fussiness — At 5 went to Chad. Collins' \+ found that he had been able to get down to the parlor with his sprained foot which is improving — He seemed to be very glad to see me \+ I sat \+ chatted with him for nearly an hour. Dinner at 6.30 — Kitty sent three fine proofs, vignetted, of photos from Calvely's bust. I went with them to Collins \+ that he might select the pose he liked best. Man home \+ read to Ralph until nearly — Chave came in about that time.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 31, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful winter day. Bkfst. about 9.30 — before bkfst. went to studio \+ fixed fire for the day. After bkfst. wrote until nearly o'clk. Then, with Jim, went to Ed\. Cook's \+ got Ralph \+ we walked until nearly 2 — then dinner — At \+o'clk. I went to Ch. Cor. 38th St. \+ Mad. Ave. \+ heard a portion of the Oratorio of Elijah — a chorus of 35 — but it was so indifferently given, to my mind, that I soon left, went home \+ had an hour just before tea. Lizzie Smillie took tea with us — Chave came in later in the eve'g. T.W. Wood — J.G. Brown \+ J.C. Nicoll came \+ Site matters were discussed until 11 —

Annie \+ I heard "Elijah" given at St. James' Hall, London Nov. 8th 92. Santley taking the title part.
