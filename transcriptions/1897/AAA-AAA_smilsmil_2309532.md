## Left Page

### Thur. May 20, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant day \- hazy p.m. Easterly & rather raw wind.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got to N.A. about 10 & had to wait for Gardner & Collins. There was work of one kind & another that kept me until 11 o'clk \- then I went to Safe Deposit & got Sauces Creek Bonds etc. & took them down town. I went to 15 Broadway & saw Mr. White, a Real Estate man \- & then I went to Ges. Read's & saw Van Pelt \- talked with these men concerning improved Real Estate in the upper part of the City with a view to making investments there. I wish to put some part of the small in Real property that will be likely to grow in value. Then I went to dee dee's & saw W. H. & he & pleased the [illegible] mites. in his hands to begin foreclosure proceedings if necessary \- also the deferred Benedict note for collection. Then I went to Charles' office & left with him my ten Sance Creek Bonds & 400 shares of stock \- then home \- getting there at about 1.45 & had dinner. After that, being tired I had a needed rest. Easter went to studio where I did some writing. Eva & Jim went out with Merrill Bishop to a School entertainment & got home at 11.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 21, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. & Mrs. Woodin at Studio.

A day of rain commencing before 11 & falling at times during the afternoon, in heavy showers \- clearing after sunset with strong, cool west wind. At 9.30 I got to studio & soon after Mr. & Mrs. Woodin came, as agreed, & for an hour & half I showed them oil water-color & pen work \- Resulting in the purchase of Water Color "Cathedral Tower, Ravello" "Pont Paris", & pen sketch, under The Rialto, "Venice" for a total of $200. Soon after 11 O ret. to the house & wrote letters of instructions to Montrose until dinner, soon after 1. It was nearly 3 when I got to the N.A. Gardner & Collins were just going to the Fifth Ave. Hotel to meet Farrington who has a "Proposition" to make. H. Hart & Beckwith came at 4 & we had a house com. meeting to consider the selling of Farrington's place \- we offered it to Miles Fox to make over to summer months & she took the matter under advisement. Gardner & C. \- ret. from interview with F. He proposes to get an endowment policy on his life \- $300 for 10 yrs. Then \- if the N.A. will whitewash him he will assign the policy to the N.A. \- get a well paid clerkship and gradually repay his "loan". I don't understand the man's madness & idiocy. I went home very much dejected \- Every read aloud to the boys & went to bed before 10.
