## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 12, 1897

**Weather:** Cool, crisp day, somewhat cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cool, crisp day, somewhat cloudy - B\+B. at 9. newspapers \+ writing until 12, then out with the boys \+ walked to 59' S.- across to 8th Ave \+ down Broadway \+ home - Dinner at 2 - In the afternoon Sam. Calman called \+ made a pleasant visit - Ja°. \+ Charl came in at about the same time - I got a short rest before tea time - Eve'g I read to Raeoph - Charl came \+ took Kate to his room - I had called on Elw°. Cook in the am. concerning a Trustees meeting. Ireland Read \+ Tele. Co. - on the same subject, I called upon Walter at about 9.30 - To studio to see about my fire - then home \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 13, 1897

**Weather:** A good, winter day - clear \+ not too cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

A good, winter day - Clear \+ not too cold. To studio at usual hour \+ spent the morning working over an old Water Color study made in June '74 at Po'k. on the "East Road," on the Livingston Road, near Uncle Willie's place. I think it a good study \+ I worked it up to give to Chas. as payment for his old bicycle that I got from him last Spring. Home at 1.30 where I found two Telegrams for me, one from Gardner saying that he would be at the N.A. tomorrow if he was well enough - the other from Homer Lea asking me to be at his office at 3 o'clk. I went to Lee's office \+ waited for him for nearly half an hour. Then he came in having been detained at McLaughlin's office, cooking up a proposition to be submitted to the officers of the N.A. as a basis for mediation. He handed me the paper - I read it, \+ was angry at once. It was a letter from M-L - little else than a covert threat of prosecution for damages to C.T.S. for false imprisonment, accompanied by a printed form of release filled out by which, for $1.°° in hand, paid, T. undertakes to release the N.A. \+ me from danger of such suit. I told Lee that I would not take the papers nor w. I consider any proposition that did not cover a confession of T's guilt - I went to D.A. Atty's office but he was too busy to see anybody - then to N.A. for a few minutes and home at 5 - A short rest before dinner - Eve'g N.A. Council meeting - I found that Lee had sent the M-L papers to me there with an additional letter Rec'd. from M-L - after I left Lee's office - I rehearsed the whole story to the council \+ told them that I proposed now to go to the Dis. Atty himself \+ tell of his view of the impossibility, or improbability of convicting at thief, this, the agency of the law machine of this city agreed with that of his 1°. Ass't - \+ then I wanted to consult with some first class, wide awake lawyer who was familiar with criminal practice. The Council agreed, informally, to this plan of action - Home soon after 11.
