## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 15, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

And yet another day of sunny haze \+ evolution color\- altho' my indoor packing should be attended to first\- I had to stay out \+ much of my time was given to my trees, especially cutting out the tops of some that interfered with our piazza view to the south\- I had to give some time to N.A. affairs \+ wrote four letters. The swimming feeling in my head has bothered me a good deal to-day but in spites of it I worked in the tree tops \- Gill was in the beech-tops all day cutting out dead wood \+ having the amputations\- Tretain \+ Porter finished their work \+ I paid them off\- Walker fussed all day with the beech stumps in front of barn \+ set off 4 lbs. of dynamite under it with little effect\- I never saw such a singular matting of roots\-

Mary was downstairs all day \+ even took some part in the work of folding curtains preparatory to packing\- at tea time she took a place at the table. Eve'g\- Theatrical entertainment at Village Hall\- Bessie went.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 16, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day\- uncomfortably so at times\- with a strong southwesterly wind\- I got up with a swimming head\- Immediately after bkfs\- I went down to Dr. Gardner's\- He says my liver is sluggish\- The sluggishness is general \+ all overish\- He gave me pills to wake me up\- I worked out of doors all day among the trees. \+ Stone came up \+ brought Gill's windlass \+ tackle. Quite \+ much hard work by Ge \+ Walker, the beech stumps in front of the barn was blown out \+ pulled out\- nearly all the corn was got into the barn\- \+ Gill packed nearly all the canned fruit \+ tomatoes\- After tea I went to P.O. Mr. W. Warner spent eve'g at the house. Mary spent a good part of the day down stairs, improving steadily.
