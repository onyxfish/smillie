## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 8, 1897

**Weather:** Bright & cold, part of the time cloudy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After 10 when I got to studio. Finished my pen sketch "near San Remo" — Home to lunch — At 2 went to Bank then to N.A. f. D. — then to Calverly's \+ saw the plaster cast of bust of Annie. Calverly says that his charge is $100 — I told him that I would pay him 125 — Then to Blackner's in 28th S. about sending etchings to Phila. Expo. Then to Kurtz in 23rd S. — then to Con. Gas. Co. Fourth ave. \+ 26th S. \+ saw Supt. — about testing gas metres. Then home at 4.30 feeling very sleepy owing to the loss of rest last night, so I fixed myself \+ had a good nap — Dinner at 6.30 — at 8.30 I went to Prisdow's — 30 W. 64th S. — Cor. Mad. Ave. — \+ had a short interview with him on N.A.f.D.-site matters — Then walked down to Ed. Cook's in 37th S. \+ saw him for a few minutes with reference to a Trustee's meeting, Oakland Real Estate Co. called for next Monday — Then to studio — Home at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 9, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Morning at studio, worked for awhile on "Winter Moonlight", finishing it, I think, bettering the snow. Then I put it into its right frame \+ the whole thing looks spick span new. I also put the San Remo drawing into its frame. The cleaning of glasses \+ pasting of paper over backs of frames took some time — afternoon I continued at work \+ mounted pen sketch of view near San Remo — Eve'g. went to Century — Annual meeting. A pleasant eve'g with nothing of especial interest. Home about midnight.
