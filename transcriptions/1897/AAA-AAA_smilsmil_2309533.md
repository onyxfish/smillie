## Left Page

### Sat. May 22, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day. I got to N.A. soon after 10. Found Gardiner \+ Collins there checking off the interest moneys \+ disposing further funds by J. I had my hands full of work of one kind \+ another until after 12 — then with two checks to sign I went to T. W. Wood. 5\. W. 10\. S — He was in J. G. Brown's room — I told them of the latest developments \+ things were lively for a time — Home to lunch at 1.30 — I was in such a frame of mind that I told Ralph that I would take a long bicycle ride with him. Jim was spending the day in Bronx Park, fishing with Tom Cook — So R. \+ I got away \+ rode thro' the Park — very lovely \+ gay with thousands of children in May parties — up 7th Ave. St. Nicholas Ave. \+ the Boulevard to 153. S. Here we turned back by Boulevard to 123 S. \+ up onto Riverside Drive by Grants Mt. over the cinder path to 72\. S. — 8th Ave. \+ 59th S. into the Park again — up to the Mall — out at 59th S. \+ 5th Ave. \+ home at 6.15. Edw. \+ Mary Cook there — Tea at 7 — Sweet. Read for awhile to Ralph \+ later went to the Century — there until 11, then home.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 23, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day, cool, with strong easterly wind. Ble. at 4 — a short time with newspaper. Men, for an hour, I wrote a first draft of that portion of my report as Preas. N.A. \+ D. exonerating myself from blame — Then I had some marking of house linen to do — then to studio — in my way home stopped at Walters for a few minutes, then dinner at 2 — Rested for an hour — It was near 5 when, with the boys, I started out — we took elevated R.R. \+ 6th Ave. \+ 42\. S. — Rode to 155 S. \+ there on the Putnam RR went on to Morris Heights, beyond Washington Bridge some distance — we returned to the Bridge by Sedgwick Ave., a charming walk — we crossed the bridge \+ walked down St. Nicholas Ave. \+ Kings Bridge Road until we got to 155th S. where we took elevated train \+ got home at 7.25 — Rather tired after a very interesting \+ pleasant walk. The upper part of this island is full of promise of a wonderfully beautiful city in the not far distant future. Tea — wrote a note to Gardiner — went to Murray Hill Hotel — not in — left the note. Ge. \+ Charl. spent the evef. at the house.
