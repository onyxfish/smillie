## Left Page

### Mon. May 10, 1897

**Weather:** Thunderstorm & heavy shower at 4.30 P.M.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm day - I got to N.A. soon after 10 - neither Gardner or Farrington there - I went to Scharles, 23 S. for R's engine & to 2° Nat. Bk. & again to N.A. Gardner there - he has secured documentary evidence that proves absolutely that F. is still lying & trying to hide his thefts which, it is now found, extend to school fees taken from Students - This opens a new field & further investigations. F. was called up on telephone & promised to be at N.A. bet. 2 & 3 P.M. I went home to lunch at 1, immediately after I went to Beckwith - 57 St. Saw him & he advised that the whole story be kept from the Council to-night if possible - I then went to N.A. Gardner & F. there - more guilt discovered - The whole of F.'s work honey combed with falsehood - again I urged with energy that he make a clean breast of it - to keep me then I would try to keep him - but he persisted in saying that there was no more to tell - I was sure he was lying & I upbraided him vigorously - Then he left & made a scene in the Janitors Room - bidding different ones good bye & saying he was going to hell - He went to the Ashdaler R° & wrote a number of farewell letters to different ones in the Bldg. Later I got one - I went home at 4.30 just escaping the thunderstorm. I got a rest before dinner — At 8 P.M. went to N.A. Council meeting - as F. had left everything in greatest confusion & had made such a public scene there was nothing for me to do but frankly tell the Council the whole story which came like a thunder clap to all except Beckwith - An extra meeting to consider the matter was ordered for to-morrow wk. Then Council adjo. & Perry Watrous took their places & com. on Subject plan came to order — The programme as amended by the architects was discussed & adapted with two slight amendments - The 4 N.A. to act as jury to select successful competitor was elected - Jas. Hart, F. Dielman, J. C. Beckwith \+ C. Y. Blashfield. I got home & to bed at 12.30

## Right Page

### Tues. May 11, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - not quite so warm as yesterday. Got to N.A. about 10.30 & spent the wk. there in attending to Treas'r business & making a start on my Report. Gardner & his assistant at work on accts. Le Grand Benedict the advertising agent came & made apparent a further shortage of probably near $600 - Farrington did not put in an appearance - near noon I sent Puk up to his house after him - he was not there - had not come home last night - his wife knew nothing of him. I went home to lunch at 1 - Immed. - came at 1.30 & took Jim & Aladdin to Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show & I got to N.A. - Auditor got started on school accts & found what appears to be a further shortage of about $400 - swelling the total to more than $2000 - A brother-in-law of Farrington appeared - searching for F. - I told him of the embezzlement & told him if he found F. to tell him to come to me & - I then went on with my work until near 5 when I went home & had a rest until dinner - while at dinner Puk came with a note from F. who had been found - in it he stated his readiness to return & do what he could to help - I sent word to him to return at once - I was at N.A. at 8 - Saw him & set him to work finishing the clerk work that was in arrears - At 8.30 the Council met & Gardner made a long statement - a history of the case - of F.'s methods, etc. etc. - I then made my statement & answered questions. At 10 o'clk. I brought F. into the Council Room - he begged to be spared - a very trying time for me. I briefly stated to him, before the Council, the charge against him & the nature of the proof I held & he admitted claimed to, which he made no reply. Then I asked him if he could make restitution in any form - he said he had nothing but he would comply with any requirements of the Council - Then he was excused. Dielman drew up a Resolution, after a Com. Wood, Beckwith \+ self, had been appointed to complete investigation directing the Com. to consult a criminal lawyer as to the duty of the Council.
