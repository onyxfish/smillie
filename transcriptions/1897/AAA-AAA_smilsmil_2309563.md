## Left Page

### Wed. July 21, 1897

*The family went to Camp Choconut,*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another overcast, muggy mth, clearing but later, with two or three tantalizing spatters of Rain — Thunderstorms all about us. Mary, the two girls, \+ Wr Ralph started at 10.30, Pergo driving his team, for the Mulford's camp out at Friendsville. They went by special invitation — I did not go because I do not enjoy such pleasure exertions \+ I had too many things at home to look after. I gave time in the mth to mending bamboo screens, also to the plumbers. \+ I wrote to Th. Belts \+ to Cora, the Woodman \+ at 12 went to P.O. I had my dinner in solitary state at 1 — after that, as the house was quiet I enjoyed a nap. After that I wrote to Jal Hart, McLaughlin (Farrington's lawyer,) \+ to Mrs Bradley — to P.O. at 5. Party ret from Mulford's at 7 o'clk. \+ then we had tea. The ride, or something else, upset Mary \+ she went to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 22, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the mth — looked as tho' it might be a wet day but it cleared before 10 — brilliant day with refreshing air, altho' warm — Mary was out of sorts \+ did not get up until just before dinner. I cleared my tables in studio \+ Kitchie's room for work — Part of the mth wette Jim in the dark room helping him develop some films that he exposed at Niagara \+ elsewhere. — Most of my afternoon was given to the Hot air pumping engine that has just been set up in the cellar to pump the water from the house cisterns to the Barn tanks from whence it flows back to the kith tubs, water-closet \+ water-back in the house. It seemed to be an effort for the pump but nevertheless it did the work required for a couple of hours or more. The boys went with me to P.O. at 8 — I read aloud until after 9.
