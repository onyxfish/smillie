## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 20, 1897

**Weather:** overcast, cold, windy & raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim was up \+ away on a 7 o'clk. Train to new Rochelle to meet Mr. Walton, Tom Cook \+ Ted Thurston for a day of fishing \- a sport that, on such a day, excites no envy on my part. I went to Studio intending to get to work on painting enlarged copy after Col. Trumbull's miniature of Genl. Nash? Greene, but Nicoll soon came \+ spent some time selecting \+ samples of O. W. water-color papers \- Before he left, Coon of the Tribune came for an interview on NA matters \- it was noon when he left. Then I had to go to N.A. In addition to my duties there Miss Pierson, a teacher of a night school in Houston S. came begging for tickets for her scholars \+ kept me talking about education until near 2 o'clk. when I started for home for lunch \-. To studio before 3 \+ spent some time sorting my printing papers \+ getting them into my printing case or table \- Home at 5 \+ had a rest before dinner at 6.30. At 8 went to Dr. Yale's to see him, hearing that he was sick \- I saw Mrs. Yale \- The Doctor was with us at the time \- he has considerable fever, but not a return of pneumonia \- Then to The Century where I stayed until 11, having a very good time.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 21, 1897

**Weather:** overcast m\+ clearing pleasant & mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

The wet was again overcast \- The usual Sunday m\+ scrub \+ help\. at 9 \- Before I went to Studio, then to N.A. where I spent a good while studying, alone \+ quietly, the several competitive plans, especially those of Carrere \+ Hastings' \+ Babb, Cook \+ Willard \- The C.\+H. designs certainly have great beauty but there are grave defects of plan \+ I sat before them for a long time trying to devise ways to remedy them. Dinner at 2 \- At home all the afternoon \- Jo. came in at about 4 \- I rested from 5 to 6.30 \- tea \- Tom \+ Lorise Cook took tea with us. At 8 I went to Fred \+ Alice Ireland's \- a creative of hers \- Miss Carpenter \- visiting her \- Robt. Cook \+ his wife came in for awhile \- I left before 10 o'clk.
