## Left Page

### Tues. April 6, 1897

**Weather:** A clearing day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clearing day - I feel very shabby \+ weak but was able to get up for bkfs. Miss Salts died very early this morn. after her operation at Roosevelt Hospital - While I lay on the lounge after bkfs. I.W. Wood came to see me about the 7.A. Federation imbroglio - He is fierce \+ is doing all that he can to get the N.A. to take action to get out of the 7. - I oppose him \+ counsel moderation - He is on the war path which makes me feel that it is necessary for some one to be up \+ after him - I was able to get around to the studio for a little while \+ then went down to Mr. Huntington's \+ saw him - I found him much excited over the entanglement of the N.A. - He insisting that A must be kept absolutely free from all other institutions \+ organizations - but he expressed himself as unwilling to counsel precipitate action altho' he thought the A. should get out of the federation as soon as possible - Wood came in while I was there \+ became much excited when he found that it was not prepared to preach immediate dissolution of the 7.A.7. I left them together discussing the situation \+ went home - Dinner at 6.30 - many returned during the afternoon from [illegible] - having arranged her house \+ letting there satisfactorily. A little reading to Ralph. to to bed early but late before I got asleep.

Ralph went to school to-day - first time since he was taken down with the measles.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 7, 1897

**Weather:** Unsettled weather  **Temp:** [illegible]

Unsettled weather - fresh \+ pleasant air but growing threatening of rain towards night. Miss Page, Katie Throb's nurse left this morning. After I got this with my own duties I went to [illegible] in W.42d S. \+ saw Mrs. Betty about 2 hand wheel for Ralph. Then I went to 57th S. \+ 6th Ave. \+ called on Beckwith about the N.A. meeting to-night - to prevent precipitate action - a long call there - then in to Watrous - he has a resolution prepared for to-night under Wood's inspiration to commit the N.A. to an institution - in favor of a specific tax - I advised him agst. it, out he was set in his determination - Home to lunch at 1 - at 2 I went to Dielman's, 41 W.10th to see him on N.A. business - he had gone to his class at Cooper Union - Saw Mrs. D. - for a moment - then to 51 W.10th \+ saw Perry on same subject - then to Arnold \+ Constable's \+ bought some velvet to cover inside of shadow box. painting "Sailor's Bay" - there to Huntington's - found him still strongly opposed to the Confederacy as he calls it - He read to me his resolutions calling for a prompt withdrawal of the N.A. from the 7.A.7. I asked him if he would not be content with a resolution of censure of 7.A.7. under which the N.A. could withdraw later - He said no - so I left. Stopped at Butler's on my way home \+ left velvet - Had a rest before dinner. Evkg. at 8 went to N.A. - a full \+ excited meeting - Huntington the site \+ plan com. ported progress \+ asked that the plan of competition, as far as carried, be ratified. This was done. Morris being the only objector - then Huntington led off with his resolutions - to which Dielman made no objection - Morris was nuisance \+ Wood was intensely partisan in his speech \+ rulings. The fight was a hot one but the vote was decisively against H. - then Dielman introduced his resolution of censure \+ it was carried by a strong vote - then Watrous introduced his specific duty resolution \+ after another hot time that was lost - Watrous said to me afterwards that up to the last moment he had been sure of carrying his point but I hypnotised his men - Home two rain \+ to bed at midnight
