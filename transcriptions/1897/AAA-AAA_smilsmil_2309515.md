## Left Page

### Wed. April 14, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant day \- Rain fell in the early eve'g \- \+ heavily Thro' the night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph was so out of sorts in the m'g that I sent for Yale, \+ was in bed all day \- Apt. disturbance \- imp'. Rapidley \+ was up \+ took dinner with us. I got to studio at about 10 \+ painted all day on "San Luis Rey". I wish to get the sentiment of the last of day, just before sunset \- but the wish brings no power \- The result is prosaic \+ lifeless \- After 4 o'clk I went to Ex'n of Socy of Am. Artists \- The Exbn was some, stimulating and depressing \- The latter, I think, being dominant. There is much work that is brilliant \+ powerful \- The work of young men \- \+ this is depressing to me because it fills me with envious discouragement. I wish to work \+ win with these young men, but that is physically impossible \- I am too heavily loaded with years \+ weakness that results from imperfect education in art \- And so, I am depressed because I realize fully that I am hopelessly out of the race. Dinner \- Every went to Walters for half an hour \- Then Rel. \+ read to R. until 9 \- Then to studio \+ studied over San Luis Rey by gas light \-

## Right Page

### Thur. April 15, 1897

**Weather:** Dull m'g \- some heavy showers during the middle of the day \- clearing in the after-noon \- cool eve'g.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 \+ to work on "San Luis Rey" \- I repainted quite solidly through the middle distance \+ got more atmosphere. Home to lunch Thro' a heavy shower \- Afternoon I painted until 4 when I got the painting to a point where it all "holds together" \- So I shall put it away for a few days. I washed up \+ got things put away \+ was preparing to take a walk when F. G. Brown came in \+ he stayed until 6. He came to get me to write to Century Com. on Admissions a note in praise of Geo. Hearn \- G.H. as a Centurman would be an interesting figure \- I had a ten or fifteen min. walk before dinner.
