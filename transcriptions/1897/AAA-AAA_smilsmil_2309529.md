## Left Page

### Fri. May 14, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncle W. \+ Aunt A \- am? from Phil?

Threatening weather most of the day, but rain did not begin to free until after 10 o'clk. at night. Immediately after bk[?]. I went to Mr. Lee's Rooms in W. 36th S. \+ saw him \+ asked his advice as to what should be done with Farrington \- I was with him for an hour but after taking time to think he said that he did not think that the K.A. would be benefitted by taking action, either civil or criminal \- then to studio \- after a little to N.A. where I spent some time with Mr. [illegible] etc \- somewhat late to lunch \- then to studio \+ about 5 went to N.A. after having been interviewed by a Reporter from the Sun. At N.A. I saw Mrs. Farrington's Bro. who had been for the most part of the afternoon running about trying to find her \- it had a Royal Arcanum Benefit Cert. belonging to F. \- a sort of his wife's life in favor of his wife for $1000. This Mrs. Farrington had also signed to me \- I am sure that such assignment is not valid. It was nearly 9 o'clk. when I got home to dinner. Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. There, just in from Phila. At 8 I started for 211 W. 121st to see Mrs. Farrington \- went by Lex. Ave. to 105th S. \- Tram? to underground Trolley \+ Lenox Ave. \- to 121st S. \- getting there at about 9 \- saw Mrs. F. \- She cried a little at first \- but after that she was very quiet. She assured me that she had no suspicion of his dishonesty, none of the money had reached her \- etc \- etc. Then I asked to see him \- he had not been out all day \- he came down \- I told him that I wanted him to be at the N.A. to-morrow \+ to have his book \+ check book \- 5th Nat'l Bk. with him \- he promised \- he walked with me to Lenox Ave. \- where I took Trolley car to Columbus Ave. \+ W. 9th S. \- thence down \- I got in a little before 11 \+ soon after, rain began to fall heavily.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 15, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant day \- after bk[?] \- I went to Spalding's with Jim \+ after a conference with Mr. Belts I agreed to take a new wheel for Jim, on Trial, his old one to be sold on best possible terms to cr. my acct. Then to Miller's in 40th S. \+ to studio \- It was almost noon when I got to studio \- Beck with there \- also Farrington, Jardine \+ Collins \- F. produced his private acct., bank book \+ check stubs \- 5th Nat'l Bk. but there was nothing out of the way there \- it was nearly 2 o'clk. before I got home to lunch \- The carpenter got to studio before I did \- to fit up the Rack for porcelain Trays in acid Room. After 4 Davis came from N.A. with letters saying that Jas. Hart wished to see me \- I went to A \- had a conference with Jas. H. concerning the work to be done in distributing the [illegible]. Home at 6.30 \- dinner \- Eva \+ F. Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. \+ Mary went to Charl's room \- Geo. \+ Nellie came later \+ waited until their return.
