## Left Page

### Sat. April 24, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightfully mild \+ pleasant spring day. Got to studio at usual time but visitors kept me from my work until nearly 12 o'ck Mary Cook came to inquire about "Vesuvius" at the Academy \- Care Sprague's wife is one of the officers, as I understood, of the Buffalo Fine Arts Institute, or something of that kind, having a little money to spend, \+ she, having taken a fancy to the painting, wishes to get my lowest price. I got a conflee of imps. from plate o the [illegible] is the lake portrait of Mrs. Gester \- before I went home to lunch. On my return, to work printing again \- took two more gerster print \+ two from the dippincott, fancy head of female \- I was unsuccessful in my attempt to get a good pf. from D.N. print of Rhododendrons \- It was 6 o'clk before I was able to get away from the studio. Dinner at 6.30 \- Read to the boys until 9 \- at about 9.30 went out on my wheel for an hour.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 25, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm \- sky growing gradually overcast \+ at night a cool, threatening east wind. Went to studio at 10.30 \- there for a few minutes \- then to Union League Club and saw Charl \- It was about noon when I got home \- Mr. Robt. Smith of Columbus, O. called to see me, the man who painted "A meadow Stream," purchased by him at the recent [illegible] heldin C \- After dinner I read newspaper \+ then had a nap \- pd. Valentine called before 6 \- Tea at 6.30 \- Evl'g. Read to boys until 8.30 \- I wrote to E.H. Blashfield in reply to a pleasant note rec'd. from him yesterday mf. Geo. came in, \+ later, Charl \-
