## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 29, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - brilliant \+ warm - After getting thro' with my out duties, Raleph \+ I went down town - I had a basket with a number of heads of Mignonette Lettuce - I called at Mrs. DeWitt's \+ left two there - at Judge Jessup's \+ left two there - at the Misses Biddle's \+ then to Percy Read's where I left three - Mr. Schafer was there, to her I gave two - then to Lyon's Store where I left the bal. with instructions to give them away. It was 11 before I got to my work in studio before dinner I had Retouched with wash, pen \+ white, a mounted pf. of "The Road to the Lake" - after dinner I got to work at crating Jim's bicycle - much trouble - could it get one of the pedals off - took it to Cooley's where the whole crank had to be taken off - After much tribulation I got it done at 5.30 just as the Sup. man came for it. Even I wrote a letter to Jim \+ one to J. F. Ryan, to whom I sent the crated bicycle, that Jim might uncrate it there \+ leave the crate in the cellar - to P.O. at 10 -

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 30, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm hazy day - I worked upon a pf. of The Road to the Lake until about 11 and then went down Town - Had my hair cut - Dinner - some out of door work - Warner painting odds \+ ends about the place - cut mount for the etching pf. - worked on this mt. Called on A.B. Smith, Town Burgess, at his request, \+ found that he wished to consult me concerning the big cess-pool that was built in South of town to take Town sewage \+ now complained of as a nuisance. I urged that there be no more experimenting \+ consequent waste of money - Invite a competent Sanitary Engineer \+ get his advice. Tea - the usual reading to R - \+ to P.O. at 8.

Many has continued to improve slowly during the day - normal temp. in the mf. \+ 100° at night. Hattie the blk cook is complaining - almost too sick to work.. Dr. Gardner was consulted at night.
