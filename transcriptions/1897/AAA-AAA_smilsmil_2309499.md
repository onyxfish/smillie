## Left Page

### Sat. March 13, 1897

**Weather:** Very brilliant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very brilliant day. To studio before 10
\+ to work on etchings — I made good
progress, but work under a discouraging
realization that I am producing only a
pretty piece of picture making. The
workmanship is fairly good but it does
not rise to the level of art. I know this
\+ causes wherein I fall short but cannot
rise to a higher plane. Whenever I
go where I can see good pictures my
heart sinks. I gave up work at
4.30 \+ at 5 went out for a walk. I
went down 5th Ave. \+ stopped at some
of the galleries \+ saw two or three good
pictures. I went to the Constable building
5th Ave. \+ 18th st. to learn something of Charles
Barmore — The publisher of etchings — portraits —
whose two notes for $100. made July 10th '96 are
still unpaid — for [illegible] portrait. I am
now going to press him. He was not in.
Home to dinner at 6.30 — Charle came in
for an hour in the eveg. I wrote a note
to Barmore. Ralph continues good

### Sun. March 14, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

a couple of inches of wet snow when I got
up — more falling. Rain followed — weather
cleared in the afternoon \+ by night little of the
snow remained. I got up at 8 \+ had [illegible]
at 9.30 — I helped Mary give Ralph a bath as
he lay in bed — a clean night gown — \+ then an
entire change of his clothing — It was a great
[illegible] to him. At 11 I went to studio. Yesterday
was the last day for receiving pictures at Metl. A —
but I hope to obtain grace until Tues. P.M.
to finish my painting — weekly that line. I had
to work to day. I painted until 2 o'clk. then
home to dinner — Jim came in from Alice
\+ [illegible] at about 3 \+ made us a short visit.
I said to studio at 3.30 \+ painted until 7.30.
The painting is now practically done. I
cleaned up \+ then walked up 5th Ave. to 63rd
\+ home, getting in at 6.45. Tea. Eveg.
