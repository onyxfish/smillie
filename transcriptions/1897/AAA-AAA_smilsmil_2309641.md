## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 24, 1897

**Weather:** Clear \+ very cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

worked all the [illegible] in studio upon charcoal sketch 12×19 for a water color. Subject based upon a very slight sketch that I got some years ago on the banks of the Susquehanna, near Binghampton. Home to lunch at 1 — Frannis came to the house with check book of NA \+ I made out checks for artists who have not yet been paid for their paintings sold at [illegible] just closed. Z- told me that word was [illegible] to NA, just as he left, that Gardner was in the bar room of the New Amsterdam Hotel, Fourth Ave. $21.5¢. I went down there hoping to catch him but he had gone — I wished to ask him as to his choice of the form in which his thefts should be charged against him in the NA acc't. At the Amsterdam the bar keeper told me that I might find him at the Union Sq. Hotel, Cor. 15 \+ 5. I went there — was told that he was an occasional guest but not there at that time. Then I gave up the chase \+ went to Ruppel's \+ looked at some etchings — Then to Wunderlighs — There to Doutyes \+ to big toy stores in 23 \+ 5. Looking to find something for Ralph's Xmas — then to Putnam's \+ bought books — then to N.A. \+ to Meyrourts where, on Chas's acc't. I bought an opera glass for it \+ for his study of birds — Home at 6.30 dinner. Ev't the usual excitement in making preparations for Xmas m'f. Chas came in with a bag full of things — \+ all were arranged for the m'y [illegible].

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 25, 1897

**Weather:** A fine winter day  **Temp:** [illegible]

All up on time — a fine fire in the parlor — After bk'f: we went there \+ opened all f't'g's, Ralph beginning. Chas's presents to the girls made a wonderful display of silver \+ cut glass, which pleased them immensely. At 10 I went to studio but finding the Ther. at 55° I retired after opening furnace draughts. Back again at 11 \+ cut mount for an etching [illegible] — also a little more work on charcoal sketch of yesterday. Home to dinner at 1 — After dinner gave myself over to lounging. Read until I felt very sleepy — then had a good nap — then out \+ had a walk until after 6 — then tea — Ev't dressed and went to Mary Cook's where there was a family gathering — the boys \+ Bessie went — Mary was so much under the influence of a severe cold that she went to bed \+ Katherine remained at home with her. We stayed at the Cook's until about 11, then home.
