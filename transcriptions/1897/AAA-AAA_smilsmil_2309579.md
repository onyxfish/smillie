## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 22, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, altho' somewhat muggy in the m't. \- a thundershower passed south of us about the middle of the afternoon + we got a light sprinkling. \- I got up early \- had my bath + rub \- helped Ralph with his bath + was able to go back to bed for a short rest before bkft. During the m't. I wrote to Geo. Cook about an old Kansas m't + to Mary Cook at Athol Springs \- Dinner at 1.30 afternoon. I did some work in the dark room which owing to Jim's defective camera + my aged chemicals amounted to nothing. There I toned some prints taken weeks ago \- also four untoned prints that have been kept in the dark for two years. They appear to work out fairly well. Every after tea Ralph + I went to P.O. with others \- after that I read to him until 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 23, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Day opened with a fog which lifted luckily at about 9 o'clk, showing a threatening sky from which a very little sprinkle fell between 1 + 2 \- At 5 a smart shower \- after 9 P.M. \- shady, heavy rain. \- I put in a good days work on Genl. Green, working for sometime over the back ground, which is bothering me a good deal, + then on the face. \- At 5 o'clk. I gave up as it was getting too dark to see to work. I started for down town + was caught by so heavy a shower that I turned back. \- It passed before long + then I went to P.O. etc. After tea I read to Ralph until 8.30 later I read newspapers until near 10 \- then to bed.
