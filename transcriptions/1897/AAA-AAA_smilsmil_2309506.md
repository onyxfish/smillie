## Left Page

### Sat. March 27, 1897

**Weather:** Windy. Cloud & sunshine & rather cold.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent the day at home being in no condition for work & interested in the changes made in our domestic hospital. Everything in my bed room was turned out upon the extension roof for sun & air. Kate transferred herself & her immediate belongings to the attic & that room (parlor bed-room) was cleaned & aired. After lunch Katie T. was rolled in blankets like a mummy & carried down to the lower room (Par. bed-R.) the transfer being made with entire success. Yale came in the mng & after seeing Gretta gave permission for her to go down stairs for awhile. Also, to Ralph to go out for a walk — So, R. & I walked to 42d S. with him & then home. I went to studio in the mng & again in the afternoon for a few minutes each time — & I had a rest before dinner. Swey. after reading to R. & getting him to bed I went to Century leaving Charl. at the house. Met some pleasant fellows — had a talk with Watrous about his dinner to the Com. M. Soc. — He assures me that there are no politics in it & begs me not to absent myself. Home before 11, & to bed in my own bed for the first time in many days — & nights.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 28, 1897

**Weather:** Brighter than yesterday &, perhaps, milder & less windy — but not much.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at about 8 o'clk. & gave Ralph his bath — then had my own scrub down & got off at about 9.30 — It was 11.30 when R. & I went to studio — I looked after the fire, then by elevated R.R. we went up to 129th S. & inspected the new 3d Ave. bridge, the new Hudson R.R. bridge & the Mad. Ave. bridge. We found the wind cold & strong. After walking down Mad. Ave. aways we took car & rode to 68th S. — then got out & walked to home, getting in at 2 — dinner — Read until after 4 — then I had a nap — Tea at 6.30 — after that an hour or more of reading — Charl. came in, then Geo. & Nellie — They stayed until 10.
