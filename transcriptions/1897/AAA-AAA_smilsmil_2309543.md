## Left Page

### Fri. June 11, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day, altho' a little warm for exercise. I found that as usual I had a number of business letters to write which took time, some of them I had to write at the N.A. because I had to transfer them in the copying press. Then I went to the 2d. Nat'l \+ saw Case relative to getting some of Farrington's deposit slips \+ a bank clerk to identify them at the examination next Tues. I got home before I did not get dinner until near 2 - after that I made a new start on my N.A. Report \+ worked until 4c. Then I was tired \+ I fixed up my wheel for a ride - It was nearly 6 when I got started - I went up town to look over some of the securities for N.A. mortgages - in 134 - 136 \+ other streets as high as 152. It was quarter past 7 when I got home after a ride of nearly 16 miles - I rested until 8 \+ then had my tea - Charl came in but did not stay long \+ he went off on his wheel - I worked on my new Report until about 10.30

## Right Page

### Sat. June 12, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim went to Buffalo. Katie T. to Po'k.

A warm day but a very beautiful one - After bkf. I worked for an hour or two on my N.A. report which I am now rewriting from very rough notes \+ licking it into shape. Then I went to studio, O'Brien's \+ other places to the N.A. where I found Gardner. After some delay I went to 2d. Nat'l. Bk. but missed seeing Case. Home to dinner at 1 - After that I spent some time cleaning my bicycle as I could not settle myself to my report with the preparations about me for the flitting of Katie T. \+ Jim. N.T. went to Po'k at 3.30 Jim left us about 4.30 to go to Ed Wm. Coaks' in 37th St. He is to go with the Coak family to Buffalo by N.Y. Central - 6 P.M. Train - Jim has been invited to visit Mr. Carleton Sprague at Altnal Springs, on the Lake Shore for a fortnight - I went to Edwin's home about 4 o'clk - \+ coming home gave him $20 for Jim - about $. Ralphs went to Spalding's, I tried to get a spring saddle post that Belts has kept me dancing for a month past - but I did not get it. Then I went to Jenkins' \+ ordered a lot of writing paper - Then home - Tea after 7.30 - Then some Reading aloud to Ralph. At 9.15 I went to the Century - Later Charl came in - he took me in to the Raquette Club \+ showed me around - Home at 11.

Many has engaged two young colored women as servants. They come excellently recommended
