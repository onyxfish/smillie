## Left Page

### Fri. May 28, 1897

**Weather:** Bright, cool \+ breezy  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- but my head troubled me all day \- I got to N.A. soon after 10 \+ found Gardner \+ Collins there \- we set to work to see if we could establish a chain of evidence that would satisfy the Judge Croune \+ upon which we could get a warrant \- I went to Jacob Mahler \- 6th Ave. \+ 31st St \- found him \- he showed me the check that he paid last June to the N.A. \+ that is no where credited on the books of the A. \+ said that he would go with me to court \- I left to \- Gardner \+ Collins had been to Brennan \+ had seen him \+ his checks \- then I hurried down town to see \- had a very unsatisfactory interview with him \- he was in a great hurry to go to some court \+ too excited \+ a condition to give me any clear advice \- this left me with Kingsbury who is a boy, so far as experience goes \+ I was very much provoked. \- I got home at about 12 \- I had something to eat then to N.A. \- Gardner had printers proof of his statement \+ I went over it with him \- there was a part of it where two accts, the A \- Building fund Acct \+ the A. Revenue Acct had been badly muddled \- this I had to disentangle \+ with an aching head, I found it pretty hard work \- Beck with came in for a little while \- I left at about 5.30 \+ went to Spalding \- 42d St. \+ saw Belts about my saddle post \- then home \- used up \+ I went to bed without any thing to eat

## Right Page

### Sat. May 29, 1897

**Weather:** Still fine weather \- cool \+ breezy  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- I awoke at 4.30 A.M. after unrefreshing sleep, with a severe headache \- I took some remedies \+ got some relief \- so that I got up at 7.30 \- after 8 P? I felt very loose \+ shaky \- so I wasted about \- I wrote a letter to Mrs. Lawrence thanking her for her kindness in letting me have the Andrifi sketch that I sold to her before Annie's death \- I am to make a copy for her \+ keep the original \- It was about 11 when I got to Academy \- Kingsbury there very impatient to get to the Harlem Court at 2. By 5\- we went immediately to Mahlers in 6th Ave. \+ took him with us to the Court, only to find that it had adjourned at 11.15 \- it being a half holiday \- both went our way \- after arranging to be there at 9 o'clk. to\-morrow mt. I left to the N.A. \- I got from Gardner the proof from printer \+ the statement of N.A. finances for the fiscal year ending May 31\. \- I wrote some notes \+ got home at 7.30 \+ had dinner \- I rested for an hour after that \- It was nearly 4 when Bessie, Jim, Ralph \+ I went out on our wheels, thro' Central Park out upon 5th Ave. at 72d St \+ over the asphalt up to 181st St \- across to 7th Ave. \+ up \- to Nacomb's Dam Bridge across \+ into Sedgwick Ave. to beyond Washington Ave. Bridge a mile or more \- back to the Dam Bridge, over it \+ up the viaduct to St. Nicholas Ave. \- down to Central Park \+ thus at home \- 19½ miles \- the longest ride that I have yet taken, getting in at 6.45 \- I rested for half an hour before I had my tea \- Every Chaye came in \+ said: last night from a trip to Boston I got to bed early.

Ralph gave us a scare on St. Nicholas Ave. on our way home \- he got too close to a four wheeled wagon \+ a nut in the pavement threw his wheel around between the front \+ hind wheel of the wagon \- by throwing himself from his wheel he saved himself but the wagon went over his bicycle bruising \+ bending but not breaking it \- he was able to ride it home.
