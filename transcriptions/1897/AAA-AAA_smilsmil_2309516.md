## Left Page

### Fri. April 16, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - went with the boys after bkfs. to Spaulding's to get their saddles but Belts was not yet ready - It was about 11 when I got to studio having stopped at the house to put my new saddle-post \+ saddle on my wheel - At studio I got to work cleaning up accumulations upon my Etching Fable as now I am ready to begin my work there - Two of the three W. C. drawings that I had sent to com. to pass for Chicago Institute were rejected \+ returned to me - Murphy, chairman - I wrote to him two or three lines \+ "thanks awfully"! My pictures, well hung in the Academy, make me sick - but this has made me much sicker. The drawings were, I think, above the average of hbt\[?\] merit - it is depressing to find that boys who have made no position \+ are unknown are put upon juries with power to "bounce" anything that does not square with their latest vagaries - In the afternoon I wrote rough draft of a letter on this subject to Mr. Jas. Dole of the Chicago Inst. I continued my work of cleaning up until after 4 o'clk. At about 5 I went again to Spaulding's with the boys - Belts not ready yet - Then we had a short bicycle ride \+ a new post is not satisfactory - Dinner at 6.30. Evg. - wasn't in small bits notwhere in particular - Rafali's cold, in his head \+ throat is very troublesome.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 17, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during last night \+ until about 10 o'clk a.m. Immediately after bkfs., two' a heavy shower I walked up to Yale's to consult him about R's condition, he is now so stuffed in head throat \+ coughing a good deal — just missed him in my way back. Stopped at Borrman's about R's wheel, \+ at Miller's - To studio at 10 - found letter from Columbus, O. - with draft for $112.\[?\] payment for my painting "A Meadow Stream," went to 2. Nat'l Bk. made deposit - Then to Am Art Inst\. \+ saw Birge Harrison paintings - Then to H. to D. B. Butler's \+ to studio at 12 - wrote for half an hour on letter to Mr. Jas. H. Dole of Chicago in protest ag't. the make-up of the N.Y. jury of Selection for the Chicago Art Inst. - J. Francis Murphy — Frank Russell Green \+ Chas. Needham — only ones I knew, having any recognized position in the profession — one entirely unknown - Then home to lunch - I did not return to studio but wrote some letters at home \+ Later, getting very sleepy, I gave way to it \+ had a long nap. The result being that after I got R. to bed, at about 10 o'clk I went to the Century \+ stayed there until midnight, having very full conversations with Beckwith \+ Dielman about Academy matters that are likely to be discussed next Monday evg.
