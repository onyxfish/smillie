## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 2, 1897

Mary \+ Katherine ret? from Montrose.

Election Day.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very stormy mg., clearing late in the afternoon. clear night. By arrangement, the carpenter came to studio \+ spent an hour or so with me putting up pole, one end suspended from the ceiling, upon which I hung curtains by the side of large window. Then I worked until 1.30 getting some order \- Home to lunch \- Charl came in \+ I sat for some time chatting with him \- Mary \+ Katherine arr? from Montrose soon after 4 \- Many looking entirely recovered. I had a rest \+ at 6 dressed \+ at 6.30, in answer to an invitation, Bessie, the boys \+ I went to Walters to dinner \- Had a very pleasant time until 9.30 \- Then we separated to go our several ways to learn the result of the election of the first mayor of Greater N.Y. \- Charl came to the house \+ about 10.30 he, Bessie, Jim, Ralph \+ I walked down to Mad. Sq. \+ thence up Bdway to 34th S. to Herald Sq. There was a crowd \+ much noise but it was totally unlike the hilarious tumult \+ crowd on the night of McKinley's election \- There could be no doubt but that Tammany had carried everything \- The sow has returned to her wallowing in the mire \- If N.Y. wants that sort of thing it's plenty good enough for her. Home \+ to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 3, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Breezy \+ cloudy, clearing beautifully \- At studio until about 11 \- Then to A.G. Newman's in W. 29th S. \+ ordered some brass work for house and studio \- then to N.A. \+ spent some time puzzling over my accts. which are straight enough but there is a kust in the string somewhere. To lunch at 1.30 \- Back to studio \- Diggs helped me to do some heavy lifting \- got the printing table into place \- I got frame for a "Normandy Barnyard" \- 5×7½", outside meas: uncovered \+ up on the legs \+ the canvas in the frame. At 5 I again went to N.A., and continued my puzzlement getting insight of the end when I had to go home to dinner \- Ev'g. Read awhile to Ralph, helped Bessie with some of her accounts \+ then, wrote for myself until 10.
