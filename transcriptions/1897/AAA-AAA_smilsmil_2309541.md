## Left Page

### Mon. June 7, 1897

**Weather:** Cold, Raw day, Threatening sky + wind N.E.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Doran the detective did not come in the m'f as he promised. After my m'f duties I went to N.A. Jos. Hart came in while I was there - I looked over my mail + then had to go to Allen's in 474 5t where I had an appointment at 11.30. I had a filling put in one of my front teeth. It was near where I left - Stopped at Spalding's - Saw Bello + got an cup. Hund Saddle to experiment with as my Christy treat me yesterday a good deal just where it is warranted not to hurt me. Dinner at 1.30 miss Kate Withers made the announcement that she was going to leave us on Wed. as she has taken a new place. I was quite thrown off my balance by the temper caused by this selfish + treacherous action - wanted to pack her out of the house at once but many said that it would only increase the inconvenience. About 3 o'clk. Doran came explaining his failure to come in the m'f as he had promised by saying that he thought he would give me a little more time. I gave him the address of 3. J. Bolton, 248 W. 133 St where Lee told me ok Sat. night that he could be found. I mean Farrington. Doran is to go there early to-morrow m'f + catch him if possible. Joe came in later + with him I went to the Studio + selected some D.W. Paper for him and Nellie - we then walked down to N.A. where I finished my days work. Home to tea at 6.30 a quiet eve'f. I wrote one or two notes. Charl came in for an hour or so.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 8, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A miserable, cold, wet day - The rain began about 10 + continued through the day getting worse until eve-ning. I waited after 6 o'clk. expecting to hear something from Doran - I got his my duties, had been to Studio + tried to make some progress on my N.A. Report, with little success. Out at about 11 I went to N.A. - then to 2d Nat'l + deposited $1200 check - It was about 1 o'clk. when I got home pretty wet. Dinner at 1.30. Afternoon I plodded over portions of my Report, in no humor to do anything - The weather was so bad that I did not go out. Tea at 6.30 - Excitement about 8 o'clk - Hay. Ave. cable car, bound up, crashed into a cab at Cor. of 38th St - we heard the crash - Jim + I went to the Cor. The cab man badly cut about the head - no very great injury to the cab visible - in spite of rain, a crowd, policemen - ambulance + things. Before we had fairly recovered Doran came + told me of the arrest of Farrington about the middle of the day - the difficulty he had in getting hold of him - he got him in the street not far from Belton's House. It was perfectly quiet + his man saw Orden. I at once wrote a number of short notes to see Kingsbury, Gardner, Beckwith, Dielman + Hart. I went to Lee's lodgings in 36th S. + to the Murray Hills Hotel to see Gardner, but found neither of them. On my return found George + Charles. I got to bed soon after 11.
