## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 23, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet day. Many seemed to improve to-day her temp. standing without change at 102°. Peter Radeker came \+ spent most of the day mending the window blinds about the house. I finished my letter to Sarah J. Hammond \+ went to P.O. before 11. Then home \+ out now for pf. of "the Road to the lake" after dinner I made paste, finished mount \+ got it into press. I also mounted two imps. of Mad. Garden Tower. Spent some time working the hot air pump. Rec'd a very kind letter from Bishop Putnam praising him very highly for his excellent qualities as a camping companion. — also, a letter from Ireland from Katie Coleman who declares her willingness to re-enter our service, but wishes to have a "talk" beforehand. At 8 o'clk. I went to P.O.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 24, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable wet day, altho' not much rain fell — began to clear at nightfall. Many seems to be improving — temp. lower than it has been — pain in head, less. — complains of great muscular soreness. Letter from Mollie Adams saying that she expected to reach Montrose Sat. p.m. I worked in doors at a variety of things — etching pfs. etc \+ wrote to Mr. Bradley, H.V. Allison, C.E. Woodin \+ Bishop Putnam. went to P.O. at 5 p.m. Every read to Ralph until 9 o'clk.
