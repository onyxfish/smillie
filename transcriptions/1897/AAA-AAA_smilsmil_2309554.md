## Left Page

### Sat. July 3, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably hot day. I commenced packing case with rugs, etc very soon after bkfs. \+ finished at about 10 - Then I arranged a lot of bed coverings, pillows, cushions etc so that I could make an as small of size of packing box needed. Then went to millers \+ ordered it. Then Home \+ R- took his wheel \+ I took K. \+ B's wheels to the studio where Bormann came \+ put them into the crates I had there - I went to 22 Nal. Bank Then to N.A. It was 12.30 when I left there \+ went home. Dinner at 1.30 - Box from Milley was brought at 2 - I commenced packing [illegible] soon after - box too large - had to go to studio \+ get strips of wood to nail across the inside. I also packed a box with one of the plaster casts from Calverley's bust of Smillie - I worked until 6.30 - Then had tea - Evey writing and assorting papers - at 9 went to Chals to get collapsible crates for bicycle - he would not let me carry it but sent it by messenger boy.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 4, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Too warm for comfort - Bkfs. at 8 - got to work soon after that at arranging papers in my writing desk \+ personal effects to go into my trunk. This took me time until about 11 - Then the packing of other matters about the house, my bicycle etc. took till dinner at 2 - After that I had a little rest after which I tackled the packing of my trunk in earnest. During the p.m. I packed in shape to send by exp. one of the fine Kurtz photos after Smillie's bust \+ wrote a note to Helen Butler to send with it. Tea at 6.30 - Evey continued packing \+ practically finished before 9. Then Charl came in. I went to studio \+ marked the crated bicycles. On my return Charl took many \+ the girls around to his room. They stayed until about 10.30 - I completed my packing and got to bed at 11.30
