## Left Page

### Sat. June 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate Ret. to Po'K —

Warm \+ muggy until the p.m. — when it grew cooler \+ drier. I feel so slim after last nights experience that I did not get up to bhp. until 9 o'clk. After that I moved deliberately — got to studio at 10.30 \+ to N.A. at about 11 — Then went to Second Nal. Bk. — Mee to Lord \+ Taylors \+ got a sweater — then to Rogers \+ Peets \+ got a cab \+ then home to dinner. After dinner I had a rest. At about 4\. Charl came \+ we agreed to take a ride on our wheels — It was 4.30 when he left — \+4.40 when he, Jim, Ralph \+ I got away. We went directly up to McComb's Dam bridge across it, up Sedgwick Ave, across it to the Boulevard — down it to Grant's Tomb — over the Bicycle Path \+ so home — just about 18 miles — we got in at 7 — at 7.30 we had a feast in honor of the birthdays of Kates T, (which was yesterday I think) and Jim's, which will be to-morrow — Before it was over, or at about 8.15, Homer dec called — His coming was a surprise to me. He stayed until 10.30. He is greatly incensed afo. Farrington for the lies that he has told about him \+ told me where he is now hidden upon my promise not to divulge the source of my information. He also told me many disturbing stories about the way that Gardner, the accountant has been talking about me, saying that I could \+ should have discovered the defalcation of Farrington's within a week of his first error — \+ also that he had in his possession the paper drawn up by Beckwiths \+ signed by Wood, Hart \+ Beckwith authorizing me to take legal action ago. Farrington — also that he has heard that there were those in the Academy who thought that I should be held responsible for the loss to the N.A. Something very pleasant for me to go to bed on.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 6, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Misty cloudy day, humid with chilly east wind. It cleared after 12 to be quite bright \+ sunny but toward sunset it clouded over again \+ the east wind blew chillier than ever. After b'kft at 9 I went to N.A. \+ had a brief talk with Davis. Then to Police Office 300 Mulberry St. to see Doran the detective who has the Farrington warrant. He was not expected before 12 so I wandered about through Elizabeth \+ other streets until that hour — I could have almost brought myself in some part of nickles. Then again to Station \+ had to wait until 12.5 — Then had a talk with D — I told him that I thought I knew where F. was \+ from him I learned the regular course of events that would follow the service of the warrant — he made an engagement to call at the house (40 E. 35) to-morrow mng. Then I went to Dlemain's — 41 W. 10 — he was out of town — then to Beckwith's — 57 4 S — \+ 6 Ave. \+ saw him — told him of the mess that things were in \+ upon some points asked advice. Then home to dinner — Rested an hour — at 4.30 with Jim \+ Ralph went out on our wheels — up the Boulevard \+ on to King's Bridge, Returning on the north side of the river Tho' Sedgwick Ave. to McComb's Dam Bridge \+ then home. Tho' 7 — 5" \+ made avg's. getting in at 7.30 — out 3 hours \+ ridden 21, 1/10 miles — my longest so far — I felt pretty tired \+ very saddle sore — I rested until 8 — then had my tea — Charl came in — I went to bed at 10.
