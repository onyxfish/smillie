## Left Page

### Wed. March 3, 1897

**Weather:** Disagreeable damp \+ muggy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable damp \+ muggy day — immediately after bkfo. I went to Dr. Yale's the removed from my right eye a bit of coal or something of that kind, as large as a very small pin head — it has been in my eye 36 hours \+ at times was very painful. I got to studio before 10 \+ to work on Vesuvius — most fit over the foreground. Home to lunch at 1 \+ back. Did not do much work in the afternoon. Yale came in in the afternoon bringing a little early sketch by Wyant \+ I agreed to mount it for him. Home soon after 5. Dinner at 6.30 — Katie \+ Bessie dined with Geo. Cook \+ wife — at 8 I went to N.A. Special Council meeting called at my request to authorize me to make assignment of Book \+ note to Central Trust Co — as collateral for a temporary loan. 7.S. Church the only absentee. My business was soon dispatched — Council adjourned a trin, until after 10 we were engaged informally over the nominations for N.A.'s officers to be voted on at annual meeting — Home before 10.30 to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 4, 1897

Josephine P. Smillie dined with us.

Very fine day. Worked for a few minutes after getting to studio on Vesuvius — then gave it up as finished — went after The Century proofs on sat. where I have determined to send it. Yale came in \+ left small oil sketch by Wyant. I cut it from stretcher — cleaned it somewhat \+ mounted it on heavy trunk board. Then I took hold of "Cathedral Tower, Ravello." In mounting gllt mat not some paste \+ secured it upon a bit of the sky not cost me considerable trouble to remove it, but I succeeded in making a very good job of it. Home to lunch at 1 — after lunch went to Butler's to see how my frames were getting on — then to Bank — Then in hurry \+ about, proofs from Amies out — then to 5or 6 galleries \+ saw some very ordinary paintings — smuts, there to Ohio Socy Rooms \+ saw a collection \+ sm. Paintings — then to Bonssond, Valadon \+ Co. \+ Dew Alden Weirs, strange productions — met Saul. Calugan there \+ lawyer Whitehead — then to studio for a few minutes \+ home. Raefor has been under the weather all day — a slight fever and dizziness — probably indigestion. Socy f. Josephine Mrs. Willis's widow, dined with us by invitation. Before we left the table, jot jessup came in — at 8.30 he escorted Josephine to the cars, after the boys got to bed I went to Walter Cook's had a long talk with him concerning architectural plans for N.A. f.d. and the Lavers \+ Hastings imbroglio — I was there until 10.45 — then to studio — fixed fine. Home after 11.
