## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 18, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. To studio at 9.30 \+ to work on my water-color drawing, commenced some time ago "Evening". It is in transparent wash on a piece of smooth old drawing paper. The color does not stain into the paper but lies on the surface, making it almost impossible to make one clear, flat wash on top of another, the under one always washing up \+ blending. On the other hand, while much artistic dexterity is at a discount, by slapping about in strong color washes with a brush charged with clear water some delightful qualities are produced. I hope to bring out a work that may show some excellence artistic rather than technical. At 4.30 went to N.A. to attend a meeting of N.A. Council \+ the Fine Arts Socy. B'd of Controls to consider the selection of a commissioner to the Paris Exposition of 1900. It was a pretty full meeting. Kenyon Cox thought the matter should go before the Fine Arts Federation. This was not opposed \+ on Beckwith's motion a Com. of 6 was appointed — three from each body — McEwarge Ch'n. I was one of the number — we are to meet next Tues. P.M. I then attended to some N.A. work \+ home to dinner. Evely at 8.30 went to Canteen \+ met Dickman, Maynard \+ Frank Jones — first informal meeting of the Building Com. We met in the library \+ talked for an hour or more, in a general way, of what we considered the gravest defects of the Carrière \+ Hastings plans \+ the various ways in which they may be improved. We parted to meet next Tues. P.M. at the N.A. an hour ahead of the Paris Com. Committee. I got home by 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 19, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim donned his long Trowsers, first time.

Fine winter day, clear, cold \+ rather windy. Okkp: at 9.30 — until 11 with newspapers — then to studio for a short time to look after Jim — home \+ wrote until 1, then out for a short walk. Dinner at 2. Had a nap on dining room sofa. Tea at 6.30 — Jim, with his long trowsers, took Mary \+ her girls to church in the evely. George \+ Charl came in. I read to Ralph who continues pretty well used up by his cold.
