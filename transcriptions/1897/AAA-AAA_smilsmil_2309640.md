## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 22, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another disagreeable day - damp raw \+ cold. Trying all day to snow. To studio - all the m'f making paste-[illegible] writing business letters - cutting mounts for an etching pf. for Kate - pasting it \+ getting it into press. Home to lunch at 1 - back at 2. More mounts \+ planning a change of frame so that with little expense I may have another frame for another new drawing for the N.A. if I can get it done. After 3 I went to Butler's - Then to N.A. \+ from there to G. P. Putnam's where I bought a fountain pen for Jim \+ some books. Home at 6.30 - Svety at home. - Charl came \+ took the two Katherines with him to his room.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 23, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold \+ rather dismal most of the day - clear \+ much colder at night. To studio at usual time. Framed Kate's etching proof for M.W. Luckey \+ later she came in to see it. I worked on a charcoal sketch 12×19 - for a water color - subject, "Lover's walk, on the Susquehanna, near Binghampton", based upon a very slight sketch made three some years ago. Home at 1 - lunch - back at 2. Wrote minutes of sub. com. meeting at the N.A. f.d. Last Tues. p.m. Went to N.A. at 4.30 Full Com. met - a general discussion but no action. Mr Harrison had cabled to Paris asking advice \+ Mr La Farge had written to Washington with like purpose. It was decided to wait until responses were rec'd. from Paris \+ Washington. Home at 6.45 - dinner - a quiet ev'ef by my own fireside.
