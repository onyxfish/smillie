## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 17, 1897

**Weather:** mild, pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild, pleasant day \- Feeling out of sorts in an indescribable way \- \+ unfit for work \- Got to studio soon after 10. Soon after, went to Locke, the upholsterer in ley. Ave. Then to Calverly's \- Then to Butler's \+ told him to send up for my damaged frame, then to Safe Deposit \+ put my N.P. Bonds in safe. Then to Kurtz's \- Then to Grolier Club \+ spent an hour studying the Dürer prints \- Then to studio for a little while \+ home \- Then to Telephone office \+ talked for a while with W. Lee on N.A. matters. Then home to lunch. Mabel Cooley lunched with us. To studio at 2.30 was unable to work \- At 5, to Century \+ met by appointment Robbins \+ Brown, as N.A. Finance Com. \+ discussed proper arrangement of N.A. finances. Home to dinner at 6.30. Sofy read portion of Hiawatha to Ralph who has improved very much to-day. He was in bed, until about 6 \- Then up \+ dressed, seeming very bright. About 9 Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 18, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m\[illegible\], overcast p.m. \- To studio at 9.30. Man got started at work in cellar sheathing the heater \+ hot water pipes with asbestos \+ felting, to save heat. Got to work on Vesuvius \- Painting in foreground trees. Mary Cook \+ Mrs. Sprague came in for a few minutes \- asked me to dine with them to-night but as I have an appointment with W\[illegible\] Lee at 6 o'clk. I had to decline \- Jack Downs called in the afternoon \- At 5.30 I went to W. Lee's rooms, 25 W. 26th St. \+ saw him about getting rid of Parsonal Tax on Anna C. Smillie's estate. Home at 6.15 \- Dinner \- Sofy read Hiawatha to the boys until 8.30 \- I wrote a letter \+ read papers \- Ralph seems to have quite recovered from the shock of his injury day before yesterday.

Man commenced work in studio cellar setting hot water pipes from furnace \+ putting asbestos jacket on furnace to save waste heat
