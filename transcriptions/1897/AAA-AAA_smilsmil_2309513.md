## Left Page

### Sat. April 10, 1897

**Weather:** Very pleasant m'f. clouds \+ cold wind in the afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

After b'f's. I went with Jim \+ Ralph to Spauldings to see about getting bicycle saddles. Mr. Belts is to give us every facility for experimenting - I left the boys \+ went down town to Wall S. - to Central Trust Co. got Midland R\+ [N.J.] coupons - Then to Mr. Lee's - got instructions from him - I think that after all I could have managed - in fact, have managed this business myself - saved the expense of needless legal advice. After leaving Lee I sauntered down Broadway to the Bowling green \+ then to the Battery - Then by 3d Ave. Elevated, home to lunch at 1. To studio in the afternoon \+ spent some time putting my View near San Remo W.C. into its frame \+ pasting it up. I also did a little writing - Van Cleef called \+ we had a long dicker over a complete pile of N.A. \+ D. cats. that he bought some time ago at the Seely Sale. He paid $6 - I think, for the file. The first named price was $100 - I make no offer but plainly intimated that he must come down to $25 before I would even think of it - after a long time he left without having reached a conclusion. Dinner at 6.30 - at about 8. Jim, Ralph \+ I went out on our bicycles for trial - Jim trying a new saddle on his wheel which is found really too large a wheel for him. R. trying his new 2nd hand crescent wheel \+ I, my new lantern - we went down to 23d St. \+ mad Ave. \+ about the streets for nearly an hour - Read Koehler's Dürer Cat. of Prints Pub'd. by Grolier Club.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 11, 1897

**Weather:** Delightful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

B'f's. at 7 - Jim had his b'f's. before that so that he could go by 9 o'clk. Train to New Rochelle with Tom Cook \+ Ned Thurston to spend the day, by invitation, at Mr. Walton's. I went to studio, opened windows \+ fixed fire. Then Rel'd. to the house \+ got my wheel in order. Ralph Rel'd. from S. 4. at 11 \+ by 11.30 we got away on a bicycle ride - we went by 4th Ave. to 60th St. \+ the Park. - Mrs. the Park to 72d St. then out to 5th Ave. \+ up to 110th St. over the new Asphalt pavement, along 110th St. \+ into Park again at 7th Ave. \+ then home, getting in at 1o after a delightful ride of about 9 miles. I rested for a few min., then a rub down, scrub \+ change of flannels \+ linen - Dinner at 2 - K. T. dined with us - About 4.30 I felt heavy with sleep - so I yielded \+ had a nap. Tea at 6.30 - Read awhile to Ralph - Jim got home after 6 having had a very good time - Jas. \+ Nellie came in the eve.
