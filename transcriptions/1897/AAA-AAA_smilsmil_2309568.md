## Left Page

### Sat. July 31, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day. fresh, brilliant, breezy. A good part of the m'f was devoted to the finishing \+ putting up of the bamboo piazza screens that have been for so long lying on the floor awaiting repairs. A letter came from Gardner giving deposits to Cr. of N.a. in 2d. Nat. Bnk. during the month of May. I worked for some time getting the N.A. check book written up in accord with it. After dinner Ryan came \+ cleaned out thoroughly the fire box of the engine (hot air) \+ at about 4. he got it to work pumping. I kept it going until about 9. 30. With the help of Spence I laid out the general dimensions of of Tennis Court. After tea Mr. Daul. Sneyne \+ Mr. Gould called. at 8 I went down to P.O. with the boys.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 1, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded day but not unpleasant. All of the best of the morning was spent worrying over Gardner's statement, rec'd. yesterday, \+ upon which I expected to arrange my N.a.f.D. check book in a ship-shape manner. There are some discrepancies between G's statement \+ mem'd. that I have kept that I cannot explain. Dinner at 1.30. Wandered about with the boys \+ Eve H. Wrote quite a long letter to Gardner — also one to Mr. Bradley. Took them to P.O. \+ to bed at 11.
