## Left Page

### Tues. June 1, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A chilly April day with a couple of brief showers, some sunshine \+ considerable wind. Went up to Yale's immediately after bkfst. to ask him about a sore spot in the middle of the palm of my right hand \- He says it is a clot of blood from a wounded vessel \- Home, and attended to my mfg duties \+ got to N.A. at 11.30 found a maid that took some time to attend to then to work on a table of N.A. mtgs, giving clearly all information concerning the properties upon which the N.A. has $217,000. Backwith came in near 1 o'clk. \+ three Gardner \- we had an interesting conversation concerning G's bill \- G. had promised that it should not exceed $150 \- now he wants $250. A shower came up about 1.30 \+ got a little wet going home to dinner. After dinner I went to the bank with deposits for self \+ N.A. \- Then to N.A. \+ was there until after 4 \- Then to studio \- Home at 6 \+ had a rest before tea at 6.30 \- Every. Chark came just before \- Spent some time in finding \+ correcting error in check book \- having had my book bal. at bank to-day.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 2, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day \- brilliant \+ refreshingly cool. After bkfp. went with Ralph to Spalding \+ left his wheel there for repairs \- Then home to studio, butchers, grocers \+ to N.A. at 10.30. Till 12 I worked on accts. there \+ wrote draft for part of the Report that I expect to print with Auditors Statement \- At noon went to 2? Nal. \+ made a deposit for N.A. \- Then to 155 W. 47 St. Saw Dr. Allen \+ made appointment with him for next Monday at 11.30 \- Then home \- Dinner at 1.45 \- It was 3.15 when I got to N.A. worked at drawing checks to pay outstanding bills until 4.30. Hart came at 4.30 when we had an executive session of the House Com. \- we approved Gardner's bill of $250 \- \+ ratified an agreement with Hine to keep the books of the A. for the year for $250 \- A Reporter from the Journal came having learned that a warrant was out for the arrest of Farrington \- We have been trying to keep this quiet \+ out now, the fat is in the fire. I wrote a note to the Sergeant at 5? St. Station (for Davis to deliver) stating that I wished to have the serving of the warrant transferred from the up-town court to the Central office \- Home at 6.30. Emma D. at the house \- She stayed to tea \- Gel came in for a while between 8 \+ 9 o'clk. At 9.30 I went out on my wheel, made the circuit of the Park \+ home at 10.50 \- 9 7/10 miles \-
