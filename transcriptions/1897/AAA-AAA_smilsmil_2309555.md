## Left Page

### Mon. July 5, 1897

The family went to Montrose.

A horribly hot day - Bkfs. at about 7.30 I had very little work to do. I went down to 342 5° Ave., branch U.S. Exp. \+ gave order to send for bicycles to-morrow - Then to Studio's where the men were at work pasting the burlaks on the wall - At 10 o'clk Jackson's Exp. came \+ took away trunks \+ two cases. Chase came in at about 11 in his crash bicycling suit \+ with his wheel prepared to make a holiday of it. It was about 12 when we left the house with our two attendant Clark S. - Stopped at drug store \+ had drinks all around. Trunks at [illegible] all right - The heat in the car at first was almost intolerable \+ the wind that swept thro' was like a blast from an oven. Ralph \+ I had a lunch at about 2 o'clk, and managed to adjust to the rest of our journey, getting to Montrose in time at 4.35 While the rest of the party went to the house direct R. \+ I went down town \+ bought some very fine strawberries \+ some fireworks - Mrs. Rhinevault was at the house \+ had things clean \+ in readiness for us. William Skeuse was also on hand -
The night, even in Montrose, was a very hot one - Ther. in our rooms were at about 85° but fell before my to 73°. Ralph set off his fireworks after 9 \+ then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 6, 1897

Still very hot weather - After 5 p.m. thunder storms that had been rumbling about for some time backed around to N.E. \+ came over to us from that direction giving us a very refreshing shower, followed by a fine sunset \+ a very quiet evening. Bkfs. at 8.30 - I spent the mof going over the place making note of the work that has been done - The drained swamp with its crop of potatoes, fodder beets, sowed corn \+ buckwheat looks very promising. As usual, little intelligence has been shown in some of the vegetable \+ flower gardening. The lettuce that I had helped for is not there but in its place a growth that I don't recognize - The show of fruit is not encouraging - no cherries - no pears - a few apples \+ load of plumbs -
Dinner at 1.30 - After that I lounged for some time - unpacked my trunk - wrote a short letter to Jim \+ took it to P.O. at 5.45 - got back to the house just before the shower - Tea at 6.30 - walked the piazza for some time - Then sat thro' the twilight in Studio's bay window -
