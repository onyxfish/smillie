## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 3, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charl arr. from N.Y.

Superb day. Had a fairly good night + got up feeling somewhat better. Putrain his mate + the helper worked all day at the barn yard wall. Pake's team + man worked until 3.30. I worked on Gen'l. Green with little spirit + making little progress, from time to time going out to watch the men at their work. Every the girls went to R.R. Station + met Charl who has come up for a two or three days vacation — much chat of course.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 4, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, quiet day — very pleasant, but too quiet for Charl who wished to raise his kites. Blue Hill Box kites. My physical condition does not improve — temp. 3/10° below normal — bronchiae + stomach troubles — went to Dr. Gardner at 12 — not in — again at 6 — not in — He called at the house about 9 P.m. + prescribed for me. During the day I did some work on the Gen'l. Green plate but of course, I could not work with any spirit. I spent some time overseeing the men at work on the barn yard terrace
