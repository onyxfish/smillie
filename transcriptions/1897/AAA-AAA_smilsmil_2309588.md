## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 9, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day — unable to get up in the m'f. — a great deal of pain in my head — Dr. Gardner was sent for \+ came — he gave me a drug that relieved the pain in my head in the course of half an hour \+ ordered a cathartic — I was able to get up at about 3 o'clk \+ had something to eat — for the rest of the afternoon I hung about feeling very, very slim. Another wonderfully beautiful moonlight night. I am very strongly impressed by memories of Shrine as, standing upon our vine covered Piazza, I look off over valley \+ hill, mysterious in the vague moonlight \+ listen to a stillness made audible in the murmuring of the leaves \+ the voices of myriads of insects. As I stood, gazing upon this scene, every sense drinking in its many real \+ suggestive beauties. The town clock rung out 11 — It was the voice of Time in solemn warning and seemed to me then profoundly impressive.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 10, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably hot day. I got up feeling very slim but managed to fodge about all day doing something. Most of the m'f I was with [illegible] picking the plums now ripe \+ decaying; "The Washington", a white, very sweet plumb. A large number were "specked" \+ to save these, Kate \+ I skinned them \+ in the afternoon they were \#canned.
During the afternoon I read, loafed \+ watched the men at work on barnyard terrace. Ev'f. Roland \+ W\. m. Nuelford took tea with us — The house was lighted and there was some decoration with flowers — This, with the lovely moonlight \+ the warm, quiet air that made it desirable to have every door \+ window open, made many charming effects \+ pictures \+ made me feel most keenly that but one presence was wanting to make the joy of possession complete.
