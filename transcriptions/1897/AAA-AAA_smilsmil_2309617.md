## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 6, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day altho' at times almost uncomfortably breezy \- To studio about 9.30 still finding something to take my time in arranging \+ re arranging things there. at 11 I went to N.A. \+ then to Second Nat. Bnk. I have uncomfortable suspicions involving Gardner, the accountant \+ book-keeper. He has lost the bank pass book \+ I am uneasy about it. Home at 1.30 \+ again to studio. Spencer H. Coon called \+ wishes to get material for an article concerning me \+ my work as an etcher to appear soon in one of the cheap, illustrated magazines. His visit took up much of what remained of the afternoon. Dinner at 6.30 \- The boys had a great frolic after it \+ did not get to bed until after 9 \- Then I went to The Century \+ had a very pleasant time chatting with a number of more or less pleasant people. Home a little before 12.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 7, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- After my usual scrub had my Hf\- at 9 \- Read newspaper \+ then went to studio where I spent some time sorting prints, paper, etc. Had a walk bet. 1 \+ 2 o'clk \- then home to dinner. After 3 I again went to studio \+ dampened a proof of The Goldsmith's Daughter \+ mounted it on a stiff mounting board as a preparation for photo. \+ process-block for Spencer H. Coon's magazine article. About 5 went to Century but found none of my friends there \- a short walk before tea time. After tea read awhile to Ralph \- Then finished a letter to S. R. Koehler.
