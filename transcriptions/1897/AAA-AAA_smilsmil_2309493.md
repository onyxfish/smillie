## Left Page

### Mon. March 1, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m'f, but soon changed to cloudy \- with raw east wind, then snow \- making an exceedingly disagreeable day. To studio just before 10 \- the room being cold \+ having to go to the bank (2° Nat'l) I went there \+ got back to studio soon after 11 \- from then until lunch time I was engaged cutting mount for Vinyards near R\- glueing it on wooden frame \+ getting it into press \- then to lunch \+ back \- Afternoon, painted on Vesuvius \- on group of trees l.h. foreground \- also, painted in a girl coming down the path where at first I had a goat. Got home late so that I had no time to get a rest before dinner \- after dinner I was late in getting away for Council meeting so that it was 9 o'clk before I got there. It was a straggling sort of meeting. Wood was not there, nor was Beckwith. Church \- Dielman, \+ Shirlaw were away part of the time \- I had a very interesting conversation with Blum on the subject of an ideal Academy school. After 11 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 2, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another disagreeable day altho' the sunshine struggled thro' for a little time during the m'f. Worked on Vesuvius, repainting the distance \+ going over parts of sky \+ sea \- I think that now I have worked nearly the last remnant of color \+ vitality out of the picture \- it looks to me very flat \+ thin \- Home to lunch \+ back as usual. Painted until nearly 5 \- cleaned up \+ just before 6 started for a short walk \- met Ife°. Hearn on 5th Ave. \+ he took me up to the Salés Club to see a lot of "Barbizon" paintings \- some good \- some very poor \- Home to dinner late \- Eve'g, read to the boys until near 9 \- Charl came in \- I wrote some notes.
