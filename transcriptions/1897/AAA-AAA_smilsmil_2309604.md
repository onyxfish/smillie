## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 11, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mollie Adams \+ Ralph went to N.Y.

Foggy m'f, followed by a windy, but not cold, day threatening rain that did not come. Up at 6 \+ after bkfs. at 7 went to the R.R. Station with Mollie who takes Ralph with her to N.Y. R\- is to stay with Mary Cook in 37 S. \+ go to school to Miss Louise Miller. After the train started I ret'd to the house \+ worked for awhile gathering \+ packing things in studio. Then a letter came to me from Le Grand Benedict who, with an air of injured innocence, claims that he should have a continuance of the N.A. business getting ads. for Cat. I wrote a very plain letter to him \+ then sent his letter to me \+ a copy of my letter to Jas. Hart \- by this time my head was like to split with head ache \- Dinner was to me a farm \- I kept up most of the afternoon but going out of doors \+ looking after the men \- G\- walked, who was grading barn yard \+ [Tutton] \+ [Nate] \- [Fales's] train \+ [male] \+ Whalen, who were at work upon ditch \+ grading down in the lot by the pond \- I went to bed early \+ had an uncomfortable night.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 12, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

High winds all night \- Rain fell heavily from about 6 until after 9 A.M \- then clouds lifted \+ wind backed around south \+ west \- the temp. very mild \- after a shower from the west at 1.30 the weather cleared charmingly. I still felt considerable discomfort in my head but managed to get considerable work done \+ in Etching Room \+ Studio. I gave A Peuse a practical lesson in running the hot air engine \+ pump \- Afternoon continued my work in studio \+ wrote a letter to Jim \+ Ralph. Ev'f at 8 went to P.O. with it \- got a letter from Beckwith. Ev'f with newspapers \+ to bed early \-

Mary continues to improve steadily \- She was dressed to day \+ took a walk into my bedroom.
