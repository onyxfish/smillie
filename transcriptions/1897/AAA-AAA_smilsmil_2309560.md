## Left Page

### Thur. July 15, 1897

**Weather:** Charming day. bright & fresh.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Soon after bkfs. Greer Parke came for Jim, he on horseback — Jim on his wheel, Then started for the Parke from where Jim spent the day. I harnessed Trixy \+ with Ralph went out for our first drive. The beast behaved very well \+ Ralph got along with the driving part nicely — we were out for about an hour. I worked about among my young fruit trees for some time \+ worried for awhile over the balancing of my Bank acct. in the Montrose Bk. In the afternoon Katherine \+ Elizabeth went out with Trixy — I wrote to Bishop Putyeain expressing my gratification at his offer to take Jim with him to the Canada woods for a month of camping. To the P.O. before 6. Evng. Mulford \+ Chas. Sayre spent the evng. with the girls — Wrote a note to Mrs. Edw. D. Thurston, 113, E. 29th St. inviting her son Ned to visit Jim. and mailed it.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 16, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant day altho' somewhat muggy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

clouded part of the time. I got the boys off with their pony \+ cart soon after bkfs. Then I spent some time in getting the curtains up in Studio bay-window — \+ worked at the mending of bamboo screens for piazza. Fill came \+ took measures preparatory to making musquito bar screens to go over tanks in barns. The boys eat? before noon \+ I was with them in the "dark Room" until dinner. The films were old \+ we did not accomplish any thing. Afternoon I wrote to Lee \+ Lee — To Moses Smith — \+ to Thos. Williams asking him to pay up his interest — all M.O. f\[illegible\]. business. To P.O. at 5.30 — home at 6 — sowed lettuce seed \+ after supper saddled Trixy and Jim \+ Ralph rode her about the place for awhile. Evnf. wrote to Mrs. Frances Bradley at the N. A.
