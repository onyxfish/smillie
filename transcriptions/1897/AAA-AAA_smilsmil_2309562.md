## Left Page

### Mon. July 19, 1897

**Weather:** Cloudy m'f. east wind — but no rain —  **Temp:** [illegible]

a two minute shower fell early in the after
noon \+ after that the weather cleared — A
large part of the m'f. spent going over
the place — Then I wrote to Farrington, a
response to his letter of 14th inst. I sent
it under cover to his lawyer, McDaughlin
to whom I wrote asking him for latest in
formation concerning C.S.F. \+ his case —
Dinner at 1 — Cooley's men came and
commenced work setting up the Ericsson
pumps in cellar — I wrote a letter to
Catherine Adams Elfkin inviting her to
bring her husband here to recuperate.
Took it to P.O. at 5.30 — After tea I put up
Tennis net on the old Croquet grounds
without marking the courts — It gave the
opportunity to the youngsters to play in
an informal way for half an hour. At 8 —
later Comstock called — short visit —
I read to Ralph — Then newspapers until
after 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 20, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mean day — making believe rain \+ not
doing it — Clouded m'f. — Then some veiled
sunshine muggy — Thunder — Spatters — no
real rain — \+ so it went on — I wrote to Helen
Butler in the m'f. Rosalie Butler died on
Sat. last — Some funny time was given to the
men who are at work on the Hot air Pumps
\+ its connections — After dinner I wrote to Mr.
Daw. North, Ass't. Manager of the O.W. Paper Co. London.
also to Mary Cook \+ took the letters to the
P.O. at about 5. Before tea I developed
some of Jim's films — After tea I went to
P.O. then to Annual meeting of Village
Imp't. Society — The usual fiasco — no one
(that is, no quorum) there — a little desultory
talk \+ we adj'd. to next Monday even'g.
Home at 9 —
