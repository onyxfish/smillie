## Left Page

### Tues. May 4, 1897

**Weather:** unsettled  **Temp:** [illegible]

Weather unsettled \- some bright sunshine \+ much shadow, but no rain. Miller came with Roof men before 9. \+ began the work of putting in a new leader at the back of the house \- Then I went to his place in 40' S: \+ he took me down into his cellar \+ showed me The Agassiz Rooms where I can have a place for the boys to amuse themselves in Photo, Dark Room \- htc \- etc. After I got to studio I continued my work of preparing asphaltum varnish \+ getting the many small things ready to begin my etching campaign \- I wrote several notes before I went home to lunch at 1. After that I went to studio \+ worked at fixing things in my acid Room \- until about 4 when Jim \+ R. came \+ I went with them to Dark Room \+ they had their first experience in developing photo negatives \- they had a good time \- It was after 6 when we got home \- dinner \- Every Charles came with his wheel \- about 9.30 we went out \+ made the circuit of Central Park, getting home in a little more than an hour \- Had a rub down before getting to bed late.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary \+ Katie P. went to New Haven.

Delightful day \- some time with the men \- roofers, carpenters \+ plumbers, at work about the house \- another day of resultless pooling in the studio \- some letters written \+ some house work done \- but no progress in the direction of art work. Home to lunch \- After that, trouble about Sup. man \- I had to go to 42d Cent. Station \- Mr. Benj. Jessup called \- not at that time \- I got up \- \+ again had to go to station to check the trunk \- Mary \+ Katie got away on the 3 o'clk. train, to go to New Haven to visit McAdams's \- I got to studio soon after 3 \- Ralph came at 4.30 \- I went with him to Scharles, in 23d S: to have his engine [illegible] mended \- then to N.A. \- Press? \+ also bring audited \- also to meet Hart, Beckwith and Dielman to arrange the ballots for nominations for N.A. \+ Ass \- to be voted on at the annual meeting. It was 6.45 when Ralph \+ I got home to dinner \- at 8 I went to Murray Hill Hotel looking for Jardiner, the auditor, but failed to find him \- Then to Charles \- He read to me his Dance Creek Report \+ Horde's proposition to buy out the Bondholders \+ 45¢ in the dollar. I accepted. Then we went to Park Ave Hall \+ until after 10 o'clk \- chinned with old Mr. Benj. P. Jessup \+ his wife \- He is over 84 years old.
