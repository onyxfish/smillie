## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 12, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day — brilliantly clear \+ refreshingly cool. My usual duties \+ my letters (business) kept me engaged until 10 o'clk — then to work on Green, scraping away at the background — hardly more interesting than cleaning the margin. At noon I went to the bank \+ made a deposit ($[illegible]) \+ paid some bills — Afternoon worked on Green until near 5 — then I (\+ Jim) went to the Fair Gr? course with on-wheels \+ I ran 7 miles bringing my cyclometer up to 1000 miles. My Christy saddle in which I was beginning to take some comfort has now so packed in the pads, \+ I have so little gluteus maximus that it has begun to hurt me where it is most desirable that it should not. Providence has delivered the cinders on the path in little heaps so that the riding was a series of dodges — The boys \+ I went down to P.O. at 8 o'clk. Katherine \+ Bessie went off on a moonlight ride with Chas. Sayre, Mr? Norris \+ others — I read my newspapers.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 13, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another glorious day — a little warmer than yesterday, but ravishingly beautiful. It was 12 o'clk before I got to work on Gen'l Green. I had so many other duties \+ the hour that demanded immediate attention. I went up to the track on the Fair Ground \+ worked for awhile with Gs? Walker showing him how to spread the cinders — I had to write to Lee \+ Lee on N.A. business — etc — etc. I got to work on Green's face — now the interesting part has come. I begin to realize that I have been foolishly over-confident in undertaking this work — At the commencement I thought that I could lay a mezzotint ground. Now I almost know how little I do know about it. At 4.30 I went to the shop \+ worked until 6 in making a little affair to hold miniature of green \+ mirror to reverse it — also a mould in which to make the plaster casts as moulds for butterflies prepared by the boys — After tea at 8 o'clk. with Jim \+ Ralph went to P.O. — after that read aloud to them for awhile. Later I finished writing a letter to Nicoll.
