## Left Page

### Fri. July 9, 1897

**Weather:** Clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, hot day after a delightfully cool m'g. Ther. a little below 70° but it got up to 87° indoors before the day was over. I went down town before 10, principally about the pony, wagon, harness etc. — did not see Perego — saw Gardner, Sherer, & Barney — 11.30 when I got home. Mr. Rhinevoet cleaning out my studio. Bicycles am° at last. Dinner at 1.30 — loafed for awhile after that. Rec'd. note from Mary Cook saying that Jim will start for Montrose next Monday. I wrote a letter to Jim — also one to Mr. Woodin & took them to P.O. at 5.30. I mixed a concoction of tobacco juice \+ whale oil soaps & sprayed the young apple trees that are now infested with aphides. Watering plants after — was until 8 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 10, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day in spite of breeze. At night a strong cool easterly breeze with some cloud indications of wet weather. I went down town in the m'g \+ saw Perego \+ closed the bargain for "Hicksy", a little white mare weighing a little over 600 lbs. \+ said to be 8 or 9 y°. old, sound \+ filed \+ trained to harness \+ saddle. She was sent up \+ put in the barn. I potaged about all day working at things in doors \+ out. Things about the house are not quite settled but all of the out\-door work is in a more or less unsatisfactory condition. The kitchen garden shows the want of brains. At 8 o'clk. R— \+ I went down town. Glorious moonlight night. I sat on piazza until after 10 — then to bed.
