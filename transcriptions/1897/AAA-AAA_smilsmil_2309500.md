## Left Page

### Mon. March 15, 1897

**Weather:** Clear, windy + cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, windy + cold — to studio before 10 + managed to find enough to do on Vesuvius to keep me at work all day, except in the m't. when Yale came in to tell me what he thought of Ralph's condition — I spent an hour finishing the mounting of his sketch by Wyant. I painted generally over foreground of Vesuvius, Faking out much of the slackness, both of rock + foliage. I also added the steamer + its trail of smoke — I was late to lunch + late getting back — It was late when I laid down my palette + said to myself that the chapter in the matter of Vesuvius was finished — R. was dressed + safe about his room for several hours, appearing to be particularly well + happy — Every — at 8 went to N. A. of D. Council meeting — followed by Com. on Ste + Plan. It was a long + uninteresting meeting. The object was reselection of the competing architects — but almost all hands seemed unwilling to come on deck. Dielman + myself seemed to be the only ones who were prepared. There was a good deal of fencing + it was after 11 before 5 names were selected — These to be considered again next monday. It was after 12 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 16, 1897

**Weather:** Clear, windy + cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, windy + cold. It was after 10 when I got to studio — Commenced second painting on "Portion of St. Luis Ray — old Spanish Mission — Cal." — This was painted in some years ago — a sketch from it I made as oft-ground etching \+ aqua-tint plate for Vickery of San Francisco. I am now trying to finish it as a painting — so much time Miller, the Carpenter, came to look at the roof that has been leaking badly. He advises a war upon the Tel. Co. whose wives run over the roofs + whose employees are up there frequently — Painted until near 5 — had a rest in my chair + then home to dinner — I had a scare from Ralph who had a faint turn just as I was going down to dinner — was soon recovered — I spent the every with him — Reading until his bed time. Then I wrote to dear Lee on N.A. business to ms. Barhydt about her etching prize project — Mailed them at 10 + had a short walk — crisp moonlight.
