## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 4, 1897

**Weather:** A nasty  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, nasty day — nizzle, wet, + dark. To studio at 9.80 + all the n.f. I was at work mounting paper upon heavy back boards for Water-Color paintings. I prepared one piece of O.W. paper for work. Then I put out some of my Italian pen sketches — unfinished ones — + worked awhile on them. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back + for the rest of the p.m. worked on unfinished pen sketch — The dome of St. Peter's seen thro' the branches of an Almond Tree, in bloom, on the Russian Hill, [illegible] in March. Home at 5 + rested until dinner. At night I rained so hard that I did not have pluck enough to go to the Century meeting but stayed at home + in the early even[ing] read to [illegible]. Later Charles came in.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 5, 1897

**Weather:** A delightful  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day — breezy + cool + bright. Up at about 7 — had my serub — + then back to bed for half an hour before I got up and breakfasted at 9 — awhile with my newspaper — then to studio for a few minutes — then home + took the boys to The Century where we spent some time looking at the pictures — then up 5th Ave. to the Park — Thro' + to 72d S. + down 5th Ave. stopping at "the Netherlands" for a few moments — saw Coleman — then home in time for dinner at 2. Afternoon, Eliz. Howe called — then Mallie Adams. About 5 I went to Dr. Yale's + called on him. He is improving slowly. I talked with him of my dyspeptic troubles that are now so persistently tormenting me. I do not know that I learned anything. Tea at 6.30. Jim took tea with Alice Ireland. Mary + the girls went to church — I was left alone with R. + read to him until near 9.
