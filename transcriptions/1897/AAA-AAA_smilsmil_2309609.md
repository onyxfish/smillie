## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 21, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate \+ Bessie went to N.Y.

A quiet, dull gray day until noon — Thro' the afternoon there was a nizzling wetness — a sort of day, at this season of the year, not without charm to me. Up before 6 \+ for a time was busy building fires, there being no servants in the house. Mr. VanAntken came about 7 as soon had help. I went to station with Kate \+ Bessie who went on the 8.10 train, riding on the engine — Then down town \+ for an hour I was paying bills. Then home \+ for the rest of the mf was pruning wisteria in lane — fruit \+ other trees. At noon went to Station \+ got my mileage book. Dinner at 1 — The afternoon was spent in packing a box, paraphernalia, having all sorts of odds \+ ends in it — Before 3 went to the bank \+ made a deposit — fixing lamps \+ their wicks in the early evening. To P.O. at 8 — Read news \+ papers until 10

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 22, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal day — unbroken clouds — nizzling rain nearly all day. Up before 7 and got the fires going. Mrs. VanAntk came \+ prepared our meals very satisfactorily. After b'kf't I commenced packing trunk \+ continued until 11.30. Then I went downtown \+ attended to various matters. Dinner at 1. Pike's team took away cases, bales, etc. — Mrs. Speuse helped me take down the bamboo screens \+ I packed away the hammocks etc \+ cleared up studio — Then I ret'd to my trunk \+ did some more packing. Tea at 6 — at 8 I went downtown \+ paid the last of my bills. Mary has worked very steadily \+ quietly all day, looking \+ acting much better than she has at any time since her sickness. Every thing in the house is now in unusually good order. At 9 I packed trunk again finishing at 10.15
