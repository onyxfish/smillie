## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 23, 1897

*Left Montrose for N.Y.*

**Weather:** Brilliant mf.  **Temp:** [illegible]

a change at noon when we reached the summit of Mt. Pocono - grey \+ cold for an hour or two - through middle New Jersey to N.Y., bright again \+ warm -

Up at about 5.30 - opened the house \+ got [in] going in dining room \+ had bkfst. quite comfortably about 7 - Many got up and breakfasted with me. Got everything done up without flurry \+ got off on 8.10 train. An uneventful journey to N.Y. getting there at 3.30 - as I got to the house met Legrand Benedict on the door steps. He was somewhat excited \+ insisted upon laying before me a carefully prepared statement of his business with me N.A. for the past 6 years - When he left I went to N.A. Studio - Then to Edw.d Cook's - Ralph not there, but saw Margaret Howard White \+ her little girl - Then home - Jim \+ Ralph there - we had a good dinner \+ a quiet evelf. Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 24, 1897

**Weather:** Overcast, with raw wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfp. - at 9 - quite like home, Bessie having made good progress in getting parts of the house in order - credit also being due to Mary Mooney \+ Gretta who have been at work for several days. I lounged about after bkfp. - uncontrollably lazy \+ did nothing altho' there was lots for me to do - about noon I went to studio but soon left there as it was uncomfortably cold \+ discouragingly dirty - I wandered about the streets for awhile looking at new buildings. Dinner at 2 - more loafing after dinner - Charl came in - tea at 6.30 - Evelf George came in.
