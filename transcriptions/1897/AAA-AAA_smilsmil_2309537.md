## Left Page

### Sun. May 30, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

get on a warrant for Farmington.

Brilliant day — clouded over about 4 p.m. showers, with thunder & lightning from 5 to 8 — I got up early & had my bkfst. alone at 8\. At 9 I went to Police Court in E. 57 S. & then met Kingsbury, Mahler & Gardner — K — had a note to Judge [illegible] from Judge Robt. Cornell & to that note we owed our success — we had to wait for nearly an hour & a half until the [illegible] disorderlies had been disposed of. Then our turn came & we were placed in the hands of Nugent, the clerk of the court, a most promising looking young Irishman to me, but who showed himself singularly quick & intelligent in framing the charge upon which the warrant was granted — He conducted it more clearly & with fewer words than any man who has yet touched the case, has done. Then we got our warrant & with T. officer Doyle, who was ready to go at once for Farmington if he could be accompanied by any one who could identify him. — I was asked to do so & it was suggested that Davis could attend to that disagreeable duty to-morrow, my at 8 — I then went to N.A. & saw Davis — then to studio and home at 12.30 — Dinner at 2 — After dinner I toned some prints from photo — films by Jim — Pa at 6.30 — Susy. Charl & Jno. came in —

## Right Page

### Mon. May 31, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Decoration Day —
Kate came from Po'k.

Heavy rain & thunderstorms during the night — rain in the m'f until after 10 — very close & muggy — Then it began to clear — delightful afternoon. I was at home until about 11 — then I went to N.A. & was there for some time attending to business — then home & cleaned bicycle until dinner at 1.30. Charl came in & passed the p.m. — which was completed after dinner. Davis, janitor at N.A. came at 3 & told me that he & officer Doyle went to 121 St. at 8 this m'f — D — & one Mr. Farmington, who told him that her husband had been ill in Harlem Hospital & was there with friends in Bedford Park. — D. told her that he was wanted at No. [illegible] Harts & made an engagement for him there at a certain hour. — Davis & Doyle went to the Harlem Hospital & found that J. had been taken there by a policeman at 2 A.M., & think he probably drank & had been kept there until Friday P.M. — when he left, alone. He did not take the bait & go to Hart's studio, so D. & D. had their errant there for nothing. — They have concocted another trap for to-night — if that fails nothing will be done to-morrow as it is the annual parade of police —

Afternoon I went out to hunt up & inspect the various properties up town upon which the N.A. holds mtgs — I went from 70 & 6 & Riverside Drive to 118 & 45 Ave & got home at 7 — The run (two) some of the new parts of the city on the West Side was very interesting — & again I am impressed by the magnificent possibilities & the natural beauties & advantages of this city — I was not particularly well pleased with the appearance or the location of all of the property which the N.A. holds as security for its loans —
Tea — Kate came down from Po'k this m'f. Charl came in in the ev'g.
