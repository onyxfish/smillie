## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 16, 1897

**Weather:** Clear m't, but clouded over after noon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up feeling a little seedy after last nights experiences. To studio before 10 \- & in answer to two communications rec'd. from Homerdee. I wrote to him proposing to treat directly with Farrington & without the intervention of McLaughlin \- I sent the letter to him by messenger & rec'd. reply that he w'd. do his best to bring it about. I also Rec'd. a note from D's Att'y. I Scott saying that the 7. case w'd. have to be passed over to his successor \- Then to N.A. There sat 2: Nat'l until 1 o'clk \- then home to lunch & back to N.A. at 3 & there for three hours going over the bank pass book \+ my check book entries with the help of Framms in a systematic manner \- I think the mystery is cleared, but in an unpleasant way \- I find that this is no fault of mine some of the deposits of cash made by Miss Fox did not get on my record & the check book & that for a time covered the theft by Gardner on Oct 5th of $223 \- that he took from Miss Fox to deposit in the bank \- This discovery adds immensely to my mortification & worries me all night \- Home with a bad headache which distressed me all night \- Ralph also, is pretty well used up by a cold & headache.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 17, 1897

**Weather:** Some bright & charming weather in the m't. but later it was overcast & dull.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Practically, all of today has been given to the N.A. I went to the studio at 9.30 & wrote N.A. letters until 11 \- then to N.A. \+ Q's. Nat'l. Bk. I am now just finishing the investigation that has been going on in a desultory way for sometime & yesterday & to-day I have had Framms help me & by going very carefully over the bank book \+ the check book I have been able to identify every deposit \- this was no small task as the entries were sometimes much involved. While I was in Montrose Miss Fox w'd. write that she had deposited, say $480. and I w'd. put it down in check book as one deposit when in fact it might have been half a dozen \- but by getting deposit slips from the bank I straightened it all & found that there were five deposits that I did not have in check book \+ that Gardner had stolen bodily the $223 \- that he took from Miss Fox on Oct 5th to deposit. This, with the $15. that he took from Petty cash in exchange for one of his worthless checks makes him Dr. to me N.A. for $238 \- a depraved & contemptible thief \- I wrote to him giving him a last chance. I went to studio & then home at 5 & had a short rest before dinner \- Every [illegible] came in \- I did some writing \- among others, wrote to Mrs. Sypher (nee Cornwell) of Washington Ralph still suffering from cold \- so that I did not think it best for him to go to school.
