## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 28, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. After an nurses [illegible] night I get up feeling very unfit for the mynic work of the day before me, but the fresh out of door air gave me strength + after a short visit to the studio I went to the Criminal Court Bldg getting to the Dis. Atty's office soon after 10. The department in great confusion, the present occupants moving out to give place to the new administration that is to come in on the 1st fany. Mitchell not in town, so I was told. I waited until near 4 before Homer dee appeared. I argued the new situation with him + asked him to bring McS. to me or take me to him, as I thought the time had come to do away with the middle man. but dee was evidently anxious to hold on to his position. At last I wrote such a letter as I wished J. to write + made changes in the letter that McS. had said he was willing to sign. These dee took to McS. every soon brought McS. to me. Then with them I went to McS' office where the several letters were written (dee written) + the whole matter put into a shape that was mutually satisfactory. Farrington was in an inner office but I did not meet him. It was 1:30 when he last the business was finished. Then dee + I went to a Chop house in Franklin S. + had a lunch. It was about 3 o'clk when I got to N.A. There I made arrangements with Grannis to prepare for the visit of the Representative from the Audit Co. I was so dead tired that I wanted to go home + get a few minutes rest upon I said to meet the Audit man at 4:30, the time appointed. I got home at 3:45 o'clk + my out until 4:30, getting a wink of sleep. I got to N.A. at about 4:50 but the man was gone. He had come at 4. was impatient. I carried over the books + went. I stayed + worked on check book figures until 6. Then home. Dinner. A very. Charles came + wished me to go to his room to hear some Beethoven music on his orchestrelle — but I being too tired, begged off. The boys went to their S.S. festival. I got to bed before 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 29, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — but a nasty sleet storm at night. I got to studio at 9:30 + spent the whole moy. writing a history of the proceedings against Farrington from the complaint in police court until to-day — to present to the Council meeting this P.M. When I got home to lunch I had a note + a telegram from dee, requesting me to be at the Dis. Atty's office at 2 P.M. to meet Mitchell. Hurried down, getting there soon after 2. but there was no Mitchell. nobody knew where he was. Me + dee said that he had made the appointment with them. After an hour of impatient waiting I got a tip that he might be in Porter's Chop House (where I lunched yesterday.) I went there + found him having a good time with three others. He promised to follow me in a few minutes, which he did. In his office he apologized for his forgetfulness. The necessary papers to secure a dismissal of the indictments ago' + were soon prepared and signed out 3:40 I started for the N.A. Got there at 4. Wood + Hart already there. Beckwith not. Com. having power from Council. Wood had his little Report to preface my full one — it was so arranged. The members of the Council then came in — at 5 o'clk. all present — I read report + accompanying papers. there was a small but important amendment to the paper that I am to give to Farrington's lawyer, wherein Mr. F's letter of confession — arranged between Delamar + Beckwith. Otherwise everything went well as I had arranged it. Home at 6:15. Dinner at 6:45 Emma J. with us. This P.M. she took Jim + Ralph to a Spectacular affair at Carnegie lyceum. The Red Piper. Then she dined with us + went to Chas' room to hear his orchestrelle. I was detained until 9 by a call from A.P. Collins \+ A. Gardner's ass'. His story is that it is only within a few days that he has learned of the baseness of G. He replied that I could tell him something of the whereabouts of the scamps. Went to Charles' — he made a good showing of his instrument. At 11 he took Emma, Kate, Bessie \+ me to 710 E. 38th S. in a carriage + then carried Emma on to her home. To bed at 11 30
