## Left Page

### Sat. July 17, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day in spite of several unsuccessful efforts to rain. It was rather muggy + at times there were threats + sprinkles. The boys had Trixy out after [illegible]. + for some time experimented in horse back riding. I spent some time puzzling over N.A. [illegible] accts. Trying to get some things that were neglected in the confusion that followed Farrington's exposure straightened out. The trouble being that the data I have here is incomplete — I have written to Mr. Bradley to supply me with what is missing. — A large part of this afternoon was spent working over my bicycle that has not yet been cleaned + set up. It was after 6 when Jim, Ralph + I went out on our wheels + had a short ride before supper, thereby making that function late. At 8 went down to P.O. for mail. — Every with news papers.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 18, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded day with easterly wind — a late [illegible]. After that started Ralph off on Trixy's back + took a little ride on my wheel. After the asphalt + Park roads + grades of N.J. I find the riding here execrable. I went about over the place + did some reading before dinner at 1.30. After that I did a little writing — got the boys off in their cart + sprayed a number of the young apple trees that are infested with aphids. Every I read to the boys until 9 — Then I wrote to Charl + to Spencerh. Coon \+ at 10 took mail to P.O.
