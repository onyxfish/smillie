## Left Page

### Sun. July 25, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Strange weather - cool, cloudy, damp mtf clearing later, with numerous showers \+ some muttering thunder all about us, but none that came to us. B\&S. at 8 - all of my available mtf. was spent in the shop fitting up a box with compartments in which Jim \+ Ralph can rear their colonies of worms with such great danger of their recommitting the establishment with pests of various kinds. Dinner at one - Part of the afternoon I was in the dark room with Ralph developing some of his old films - Then I wrote a letter to Katie Elkin. Then Jim went out on Dixy \+ Ralph \+ I went to the Fair Ground with our wheels \+ rode there for an hour.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 26, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Due day - Easterly wind, strong \+ cool, Spitting rain many times during the day - Spent some time in the shop, making Plaster of Paris forms for the boys to use in mounting butterflies. Wrote a sharp note to J.A.S. Gardner asking him to send to me at once a statement in detail of all N.A. \[illegible\] deposits in 2nd Nat'l Bk. made in the month of May '97 - To P.O. before 12 - Then saw J often \+ his chickens. Afternoon I wrote to Williams urging him to pay his N.A. \[illegible\] int. before Aug. 1. I commenced work on margin of Gvt. Green mezzotint portrait - cleaning it with Water \[illegible\] Stone - went to P.O. before 5. Evg, stupid, with newspaper - howling wind.
