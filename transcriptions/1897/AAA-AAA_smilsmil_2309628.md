## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 28, 1897

**Weather:** overcast at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant winter day — overcast at night \+ milder — [illegible]. at Q — at 11, to studio for a few minutes, then, with the boys went to N.A. where, meet \+ I studied the new plans \+ took notes — we went hurriedly thro' the exhibit \+ met several of the M.A. then walked until near 2 \+ home to dinner — Had a refreshing sleep before tea at 6.30 — Evelf, read to the boys until 9 o'clk. Charl came' in — I worked upon Estate accts. until after 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 29, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dark \+ nasty day — wet \+ disagreeable — To studio at about 10 — most of the rest of the m'f spent fixing up the frame covered with tissue paper to run up \+ down with pulleys \+ cord. After lunch I went to N.A. \+ was there until after 4.30 trying to get on my check book the bal. shown on bank pass-book — I am forced to believe that Gardner has been guilty of some shady work. I wrote to him asking him to meet me at the N.A. to-morrow at 3 p.m. Home at 5 \+ had a rest until 6.30 then I felt too much out of sorts to eat any dinner — Before 8 started for the N.A. Geo. met me on Parke Ave. \+ we walked down together. Council meeting until 11 — then Geo., Watrous \+ I walked up together. At the house I remembered that I had left my bicycle chain in a pan of strong lye over a gas burner since 12 o'clk. I went at once to studio — gas burning — water evaporated — pan \+ chain covered with a mass of grey gritty powder.
Bright, moonlight night.
