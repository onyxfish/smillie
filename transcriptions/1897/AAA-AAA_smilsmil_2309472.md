## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 18, 1897

**Weather:** cold + bright, with a sudden snow flurry  **Temp:** [illegible]

cold + bright, with a sudden snow flurry that darkened the sky for about an hour before noon + wet the sidewalks. I got to studios before 10 + worked all day on Water. Color - "Vineyards near Ravello". At noon I went to Bank + stopped at Kurtz's, but did not see him — then home to lunch. — Worked on drawing until after 4. — Had a rest before dinner. Ev'g. Council meeting + after 9 — Site Com. Perry delivered himself of a speech intended to be conciliatory + to prove that all difficulties wd. be removed if we would but think as he did. — Beckwith + Nicole responded. Jol. Hart, Chairman, tried his hand, briefly, + then asked the com. to vote by ballot, signed, for site. Result, 7 for 57's; 7 for 110's. Then Watrons proposed that the choice between the two be referred, by circular letter, to the N. A's — They, in turn, to send their votes to the Sec'y. — Opposed by Beckwith as agreed beforehand in our canvass, who urged an open meeting which was finally agreed to — a call was arranged for me, Wed. Ev'g. Feb'y 4th, where, after argument there will be signed ballots for one or the other of the two sites, placed in sealed envelopes to be opened on the following Ev'g. at a meeting of the Site Com.
The signed ballot in sealed envelope, to keep secret the result from the meeting of N. A., was Bolton Jones' idea —. Should the result be declared in open meeting, he whose thing wd. be made public at once + the purchase of the property made more difficult —
To bed at 1 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 19, 1897

**Weather:** Bright + cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright + cold. To studios before 10 + to work on Water. Color "Vineyards near Ravello" — Home to lunch + back at usual time — worked until near 5. — Miss Olga Waite came in the afternoon to spend a few days — a visit to the Thwolps gives.
Ev'g. at home. Jno. Valentine called. — Chase came in later + the children had a very juvenile lark —
