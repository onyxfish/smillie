## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 17, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A stormy mfg. which quickly passed into wind \+ clouds \- as it was not cold it was very pleasant. I worked all the mfg. Taking proofs from Thou plates \- dry-point, Rhododendrons \- etchings Mad. Sq. Garden Blaf- from 4th Ave. \- \+ the view from hile near Jones' lake. After dinner I worked until 4 o'clk \- Men W. Mulford came because there was a high wind \+ he wished to see the Blue Hills Box kites go up. Sherman, Butler \+ Mr. Callinin soon joined \- Two of the kites with a flag were successfully put up \- the third was broken. It was quite exciting \- the wind was strong \+ the kites pulled like fun. We did not get them down until after 6 o'clk \- Ka \- at 8 \- went to P.O. Read awhile to Ralph \- newspapers until after 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 18, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day. After my regular morning duties I prepared starch \+ mounted upon mfg. board four or five of the etching pfs. that I took yesterday mfg. This I have done with a view to working upon them in various ways \- with pen \- washes \- white \- etc. etc. Then I wrote a letter to Charl. Then for a while worked with chisel and mallet on one of the beech trees near the house, cutting away decaying wood. \- At 12.30 I went to the Morris house \+ with him went into the cellar to examine the underpinning \+ beams \- found them badly rotted. Dinner. After that took a number of camera snaps shots at Ralph, his pony \+ dog; worked about out of doors \- clipping hedge, pruning, etc. About 5 went down town on my wheel \+ had a short ride \- home, rested \+ read newspaper until tea-time \- After tea read aloud to Ralph
