## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 19, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day, mild, hazy + quiet — at times as Tho' Thickening up for rain — I finished packing large box in studio before noon, + then, knowing just what was yet unprovided for I was able to decide upon the size of a packing box + at 12.30 went to Gill's + ordered one. After dinner the whole afternoon was practically given to crating Ralph's bicycle + mine. At 8 o'clk went to P.O. + among other letters got one from Lee + Lee enclosing a check covering the arrearages of Thos + Geo. Jenkins to me. A I returned home at once + until near 10 was busily engaged writing letters connected with the business. Then to P.O. Walked part way up the hill with Wm Warner + Bessie, who had been to a lecture at the Armory.

Frink came in + Talked for some time to induce me to buy his little old house + the end of the lot that projects into my place. He will sell for $800. It isn't worth it.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 20, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Our darkie ladies left for Harrisburg.

Damp, thick m'f, some light rain before 11 — gradually clearing after that — clear, cool night. I was up soon after 6 — early bkfs — + then to Station + started Mr. Johnson + Mrs. Jones on their way to Harrisburg. Mrs. Van Auken came to attend to the cooking. I Ret'd to house — packing box made by Gill delivered + I spent the m'f in packing frame + canvas, drawing board + curtains in it. The afternoon was given to general work preparatory to packing trunk, getting the boys things together — also, considerable outside work. At 8 went down town + paid several bills. To bed soon after 9.
