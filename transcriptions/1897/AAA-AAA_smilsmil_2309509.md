## Left Page

### Fri. April 2, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another lovely day — with a head swimming with something very like vertigo, immediately after b[re]f. I went to Yale's — R. went up with me. There was considerable running about after that \+ preparation of the house for fumigation by burning sulphur. When I got to studio it was after 11. I got out papers connection with the W.K. Wall Ranch in Kansas, that is among my possessions \+ after going over them carefully I wrote a draft of a letter to Mr. Topeka, Accounting Co. — who have the property in charge \+ wish me to execute a deed, without name of purchaser or price, \+ send it on to them. Then home to lunch. Back at 2 — wrote out my letter \+ mailed it. I was able to write some other notes \+ at 5.30 I went to Century — met Clinedust there \+ we walked together part way home. Dinner. — Evey \+ Reading as usual to R. Jim came in for a little while — he is now at Ed. Cook's as Alice has a friend with her. Charl came in — after 9 I went out \+ had a short ride on my wheel.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 3, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Watrous dinner at Union League —

Stle pleasant but with a raw \+ forboding east wind. Got to studio at about 10 \+ when I had got fairly started on my days work of printing letters Miss Abbatt came \+ favored me with a long call — \+ others called, so that when I went home to lunch I had accomplished but little work — back at 2 \+ soon Barmore came in \+ we talked over our business for nearly an hour before we came to an agreement that was satisfactory to me. I am to draw a contract \+ he will come on Monday, he says, to sign it — and thus I got partway through my letter to Nicoll and Dr. Gerster came \+ took what remained of the afternoon. Home at 6 — Ralph's trial wheel had come from Spaulding's — I find that it is too small \+ too low in gear. Jim came home to stay the house having been purified by a Salvanisite dose of five \+ brimstone. I dressed out at 7 o'clk was at Union League Club in answer to invitation from Watrous to come on Sitel \+ Plan to dine with him there were eleven — Wood, Brown, G.H. Smillie, Dielmann, Bolton Jones, Beckwith, T.S. Church, Perry, Shirlaw, Watrous \+ self. Nicale was in Naples — Jas. Hart at Bay Ridge \+ Blum was sick. Dinner was served in the decore Room — table beautifully decorated with flowers \+ was a very lively, jolly dinner party — withold [illegible] brilliant wit \+ free \+ doubtful color. It was near 11 when we broke up \+ the body adjourned to the Century — monthly meeting — I was there until near midnight — then home.
