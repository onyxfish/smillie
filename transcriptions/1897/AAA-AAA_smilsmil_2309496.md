## Left Page

### Sun. March 7, 1897

**Weather:** Bright, cold \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold \+ windy \- Ralph is blooming with the measles this m'f. Yale came soon after 10 \- After he left I went to Alice Ireland's \+ arranged with her to keep Jim during quarantine \- Then I went to Walter's \+ had a long talk with him \- Then to Studio \- Then to Century for a few minutes \+ home shortly before dinner \- Spent the afternoon with R \- who is singularly uncomplaining \+ patient \- To studio late in the afternoon \+ fixed fire \- Again to Century \- met Watrons, \+ Hanging Com., got him to up receiving forward 3 or 4 inches, greatly to its benefit \- Tea time when I got home \- Every Chance came in \- I got cot down from attic \+ placed it alongside of my bed, where Ralph is \+ slept there \- R \- passed a quiet night.

Mary Cook came to me in the afternoon nearly in a passion, \+ scolded with quivering lips \+ tearful eye because I had arranged with Alice Ireland, instead of with her, for the keeping of Jim during quarantine. She insisted that it was her right \- Jim was nearer to her than he was to Alice \+ "he fitted into her family" as he did not elsewhere \- I was warned not to do so "again" \- I was surprised, amused, a little bit annoyed, \+ properly penitent.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 8, 1897

**Weather:** A dull day with easterly wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day with easterly wind \- I was bothered all day with a dull, incapacitating headache \- As I had many things to write I decided to pass the day with Ralph who has bloomed into the full beauty of measles \+ is remarkably amiable \+ uncomplaining \- I went to studio \- found to my disgust that I had left the water turned on to the heater all night \+ that consequently it had been running over the roof all the time, leaving the radiators quite cool \- after grocery, market \+ laundry went to Alice Ireland's \+ saw her for a few minutes \- Jim was so much better in the m'f. that he went to school \+ in the afternoon Mary packed his valise \+ he went to Alice's \- Yale came in the m'f. \+ said that R \- was progressing favorably \- I did a little writing but not much owing to condition of my head \+ was obliged to pass an hour or more on my bed in the afternoon. Every. To NA. at 8 \- Council meeting \- at 9 \- St. \+ Plan Com \- The Report from Fine Arts Federation Rec'., very quietly discussed and acted upon without heat or friction \- Beckwith advocated the selection of one Architect or firm \+ giving the commission to them without competition but he was entirely unsupported \- The Report \+ its suggestions were accepted in all that related to a competition \- An attempt was made to get nominations for competitors but no one was ready \+ that matter was deferred until the next meeting \- It was about 11 when we adj'd \- I went to studio \+ had to kindle fire in the furnace \+ got to bed at 11.
