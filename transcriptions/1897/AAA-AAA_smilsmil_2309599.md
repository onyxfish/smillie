## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 1, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm, sunny, hazy, quiet day. I did a little work out of doors about the place. In the morning I wrote a large part of a long letter to Beckwith \+ at noon an interesting letter was brought to me from him — written in London — he will be due in N.Y. Oct 6 or 7th — Afternoon I laid out my Report as Treas. of N.A. to be presented to Council next Monday eve'g.

Eve'g I worked in Dark Room and develofped the last roll of films getting, I think, some very good pictures — it was 10.30 before I got out.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 2, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Singular weather — every promise of a storm in the m'g. Raw east wind — overcast sky \+ a light sprinkling of rain. clearing in the afternoon to a crisp, cloudless eve'g. The day was practically given to N.A. work. I found that there was more work than I expected in preparing my Report for the Council meeting next Monday eve'g. Then a number of letters \+ checks relating to other business of the A. came in \+ I was closely driven to get the work all off my hands before the closing of the mail at 5.30. At 5.30 Mrs. Ja. Carnuaet, her two sons \+ her daughter called — I rode with them to the P.O. \+ left my mail — Then with them to the Montrose H. where I had a little visit with them \+ home soon after 6. Eve'g with Oct magazines.
