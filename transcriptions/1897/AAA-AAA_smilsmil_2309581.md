## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 26, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A glorious day. brilliant \+ cool. Almost at once after bkfs. I got to work in the etching room preparing to take 3? working jefs from Genl. Green, \+ by 11.30 I had taken two satisfactory ones. I am much troubled in studying \+ comparing them with the original little miniature — There are subtleties of concentration in the little original that I can not expand to 11×14 inches. Before dinner I took some prints from some small experimental plates — aqua tint — done last summer. After dinner I took prints from half a dozen plates upon which I had made flat tints — Then I wrote a letter to J. A. L. Gardner \+ at 4.45 I went out with Ralph hunting butterflies. We didn't catch much \+ got home in time for tea. Ev'g. The same old thing — some reading to Ralph before he went to bed — Then some scraping reading for myself — \+ then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 27, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Roger Adams dr.? from New Haven.

Bright pleasant wlf. — cloudy p.m. I rode down to Dr Gardner's on my wheel to consult him concerning my mouth which has troubled me much for the past few days — a portion of the gum inflamed \+ teeth very sore. — He advised an application of Hydrogen di-oxide. After bkfs. to P. O. \+ drug store — then for some time superintending the digging of my crop of potatoes \+ at last to work on Genl. Green who has got into the discouraging stage. In some places I have scraped away too much \+ now I am reiterating rocking \+ receding light tint with fine rocker of the whole face \+ a coarse texture on the background. Ralph rode on Trixy to Salt Spring, in the wlf \+ worried me by not returning to dinner until near 2 o'clk. I worked on my plate until 4.30 — Then out on my wheel \+ rode about 7 miles, most of it on the fair ground track. A change \+ rest before tea — At 7.30 went with the girls to station to meet Roger as he came in on the train — Then to Village Hall, where an entertainment was given p?benefit of V. Mls?. Socty — Miss Saidee N. [illegible] of N.Y. gave a number of recitations with singing \+ some pantomime acting. She was very clever \+ amusing — It was 10.30 before the entertainment was over.
