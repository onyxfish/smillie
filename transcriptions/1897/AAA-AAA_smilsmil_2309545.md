## Left Page

### Tues. June 15, 1897

**Weather:** Beautiful day altho very warm  **Temp:** [illegible]

Farrington's 2d. appearance in Yorkville court

Immediately after bkpi I went to studio, grocers \+ butchers \+ then to Douglass' where I saw Allen, The Mason. Then I rode up to Yorkville court in 5th 5t \+ after some trouble I had Farrington brought into court \+ then with an officer I went into the examination room \+ had a square talk with him. I told him that I had come to him to give him one more chance — If he would tell me what he had done with the stolen money I would plead for mercy at the trial. In the most solemn manner he insisted that he had not taken one dollar of it. Finding that I could not get any thing out of him, I gave it up \+ left him. I got to N.A. at 10.30 \+ found that Jas. Hart \+ Davis had gone to court. I then went to 2 Nst back \+ home before 12 — I had lunch at 12.30 \+ again to N.A. \+ at 1 — met Kings bury \+ Gardiner there — we gathered our material \+ went to Yorkville court getting there before 2 — It was soon called when, to our surprise, a lawyer appeared \+ asked for an adjournment until June 2 at 2. This was granted altho we protested. It was not yet 3 when we got out, I went to Sherwood to see Beckwith \+ Blashfield — both out — then to 42 5t to Spaldings \+ saw Belt about Spring post; then to barbers \+ had my hair cut \+ home at 4.30. I was tired \+ had a rest. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g I read for some time to Ralph then I wrote to Jas. Hart, bro George \+ Conklin the Montrose Tax Collector. At 10.30 I mailed letters \+ took a frame to studio.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 16, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** -

I had a bad night — pain in stomach — got up with a headache \+ had trouble in getting about — It was nearly 10 when I got to studio. Douglass came soon after \+ I arranged with him to color the wall. Then Maynard came in \+ I talked over N.A. matters with him. Then W.S. Macy came \+ said that he would like to fill Farrington's place in the N.A. Then I had a chance to do some work on the Water Color, Port Maine, Paris, that I sold to C.R. Woodin. Home at 1 \+ ate a light lunch but after it was obliged to go to bed where I spent the rest of the afternoon — At 6.30, not feeling any better, I went to druggists \+ took a dose of Castor Oil in Soda Water — Eve'g I wrote to Dielman \+ sent him a galley-slip copy of my N.A. Report letter.
