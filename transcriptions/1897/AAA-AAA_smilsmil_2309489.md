## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 21, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Almost like a Spring day, so bright \+ mild. Blkß — at 9.30 \+ then spent some time reading my paper. Went to studio for a few minutes to look after things there, then took a walk with Ralßn up. town, getting home soon after 1 \- Dinner at 2. Afternoon, feeling very seedy, a couple of hours or more were spent on my bed. I feel as tho' I am in for grips, bronchitis, or something of that kind. Tea at 6.30 \- Swift, read to the boys more of Hiawatha \- Charl came in later.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 22, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very disagreeable, raw day. My grips seems to be progressing so that to-day I have felt quite unfit for work. I got to studio about 10 \+ spent the mt cutting mounts for "Cathedral Tower, Ravello" — remounting the drawing \+ pasting the mounts together preparatory to having them gilded. Walter came in at about 1 o'clk. to post me as to what had been so far accomplished with reference to the request made by M.N.A. to the Federation of J. A. for advice in the matter of getting architectural service into new buildings. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- Olga Waite lunched with us — from 3 to 5 I was on my bed \- Then I went to Studios \- Maillaud Armstrong called to see Maynard, who was not in, \+ he spent a few minutes with me. Dinner at 6.30 Mr. Morris of New Haven, a friend of the Adams's, dined with us. At 8 I went to Council meeting \- Jas. Hart \+ Church absent. no business of importance \- mostly general discussion \+ story telling \- I left at 10 o'clk.
