## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 22, 1897

*Farrington case.*

**Weather:** Fair day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fair day - After going to studio I went down town to Municipal Bldg in Centre St. \+ had a confidential interview with 1st Ass't. Dist Att'y Mitchell in reference to the Farrington case. I wanted to get at the bottom of the difficulty there seems to be in getting this case to trial. Mitchell amazed me by frankly declaring that it was one of a class, termed "book keeping" cases in which it is very difficult to secure convictions. He explained to me at length the almost impossibility of getting an intelligent jury who w. not regard such form of theft with indifference. He said that if all things worked together favorably, we might get a conviction, but he thought the chances w'd be ags't us. I assured him that it was a simple case \+ that 72 had confessed. He said he knew all that. I asked his advice. He said: get out of it as well as you can without going to trial - make a bold bluff or maybe McLaughline will capitulate. He may do as he has already offered. Put in a plea of guilty - if he can get a suspension of sentence. I told him that I was astounded that he should have to make such a dreadful confession - that in this great city of N.Y., the machinery of justice was unable to secure conviction in certain cases of crime, even when confessed by the criminal. Then I went down to see's office \+ saw him - got as little comfort from him - then to Tax office \+ got bus. Then to N.A. \+ to the Bank. Home to lunch. 2:30 in the afternoon. Spent some time staining frames to be covered with tissue paper to be hung in large windows. Home at 5 \+ had a rest before dinner. Ev'g., N.A. council. The meeting was very long \+ important. The most time was spent over the decision \+ appointment of the Building com. I was nominated by two or three members for Chairman but declined in favor of Dielman. The other members are Frank Jones, Rob't Blum \+ G.W. Maynard. Further time was spent discussing the composition of a Ways \+ Means com. I declined a place on it. I took some time explaining the situation as I found it at the Dist. Att'y Office this mtg. - after discussion it was left entirely to me to manage as I thought best. It was after midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 23, 1897

*Kate came from Po'k.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow fell last night - about an inch of it covering things in the m'g. A mixture of March \+ stone weather thro' the day - bright sunshine - snow flurries \+ high, cold wind. To studio at 9.30 \+ got to work with oil colors upon the enlargement after Trumbull miniature of Gen'l Greene. I find that I cannot carry the mezzotint plates to a finish from the miniature - I must finish, as far as I possibly can, the oil sketch - a monochrome. I worked at it all day making great changes \+ am encouraged to think that if I can ever find time I may be able to finish the plate creditably. At noon I went to N.A. \+ was detained so that I was late to lunch. At 4:30 I went home \+ had two hours of solid sleep before dinner, making up for my loss last night. Ev'g., read awhile to the boys - then Charl came in \+ this being the delaney anniversary, when Father was born, \+ Will - \+ when Mary \+ Martha were married, we talked of old times.
