## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 14, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A down-pour of rain all day. At breakfast I rec'd a note from Dis't. Att'y W. M. K. Olcott saying that he w'd be pleased to see me at 10.30 this m't. I went to studio + then to Criminal Court Bldg in Centre S't + after waiting 20 m. for Mr. O. to come in, had an interview that lasted about 20 m. He was courtious + considerate but not one whit more encouraging than Mitchell. He spoke just as freely in declaring the failure of the jury system + said that M.'s opinion was entitled to all respect as he was the best guesser in the service. He promised to confer carefully with M. as to the case + prospects, + let me know the results within two or three days. I then rode to N.A. + was there until 1.30 arranging the confusion of papers that Gardner + Collins have left. Sorted the papers + put in an appearance. I sorted the papers carefully, put them up in separate packages + marked them. Then home to lunch. By that time a headache had made me nearly useless + I was on my bed until after 4. I Wrote for an hour before dinner. D. Curry Read for awhile aloud to Ralph. At 8.15 a messenger came from Homer dee to report that McLaughlin had informed H. L. over the telephone that I had been to the Dis't. Att'y's this m't to arrange for an acquittal of Farrington!

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 15, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Gen. B. Post dinner.

A fine day. To studio at 9.30. I wrote a letter to Homer dee explaining the terms upon which I was willing to treat with Farrington thro' H. L. or McLaughlin, F's lawyer. Sent it to him by messenger. I worked a little while on Water Color, then to N.A. + 2 kal'k. I spent some time with Frannis over the muddle of the bank acc'ts until it was lunch time. Then home. To studio after lunch until 4 then home + rested for an hour — then dressed for the Post dinner given to celebrate his 60th birthday — went to Geo.'s + with him to Fine Arts Soc'y where the dinner was served in the Vanderbilt gallery. There was a very full attendance — said to be 400. I was about 7.30 when we sat down. I had 2 very good seats at table 10 — no. 16.17. on my right sat 2d Lautenbach, the physician — opposite, DeKay + Villard, + near by, so that they took part in the conversation Ch. Stewart Smith, S. R. Avery + Wm Coffin. The dinner itself was pretty poor. Wines disposed of by 9.30 then the speaking began. Bruce Price was a nervous + awkward toast master. Russell Sturgis was the first speaker, followed by the guest, the eel'd Gen. B. Post — after him came the Rev. Thos. Hastings, with an admirable address that was my heartily read. Then came Mr. Beck. Do. Att'y [illegible] dist. Atm'y + prominent in municipal art development in Phila. He made the hit of the ev'g. He was both witty + sound + made a marked impression. He is rather young + showed defects of manner + style that will no doubt pass away as he mellows! M. Judge Howland followed him with a string of laughable stories. Blashfield spoke + said Walker, an architect of Boston. It was nearly 12 when we got away — I walked down with Bolton + Frank Jones.
