## Left Page

### Sun. May 16, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant, cool, slightly overcast, wind east.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkf. at 9 — wrote a letter while before the boys ret. from S.S. It was nearly 11.30 when Jim, Ralph \+ I got away on our wheels — rode up Jev. Ave — through Park to 72 S. to Fifth Ave, \+ up 5th Ave. to Mt. Morris Park. 124th S. — Then back — Thro' Park, put at 100th S. \+8th Ave., over to Boulevard \+ home at 1 o'clk. \+ sat down — a rest — \+ dinner at 2 — News Papers \+ a nap until near 5 — Then a Mr. Meyers came to offer himself as a substitute for Farrington — Tea at 6.30. Uncell, \+ Aunt A — at Dining until near 10 o'clk. About 9 Charles came in —

## Right Page

### Mon. May 17, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncell. \+ Aunt A. ret. to Po'k —

A brilliant day — It was after 10 when I got to studio — I was rexed over a letter rec'd. from Wm H. Beard, charging me with having electroneened ago. Homerslea in the annual meeting — telling the story to his detriment of his having given Farrington his check to cover his shortage overrudely requesting me to write a letter of apology \+ explanation — I spent a little time in concocting a reply which I mailed to him — C. Harry Eaton came in \+ some time was spent with him in looking over the samples of O.W. paper from which he made selections, \+ in arranging plans for the distribution of the samples among the Water-Color Socy members — Then Maynard came in \+ it was time for lunch — It was after 3 when I got to N.A. — Gardiner had gone to bank with deposits — on his return considerable time was taken in explaining the Benedict advertising muddle — Time was taken also in talking with Allison \+ then Stom, both of whom offer themselves as successors to Farrington — at last after a laborious afternoon I got home at 6 — hoping to get half an hour before dinner — I had hardly got settled in my room before I was called to see a Mr. Tanner who came from Homerslea to explain that Farrington had not invested anything in a certain house in 121st S — Again I went to my room \+ was promptly called by Mr. Jno. Dupuis who offered the name of his father in nomination for successor to Farrington — Then I gave it up \+ waited for dinner — At 8 went to N.A. of course, the interest of the eve'g was centered in the Farrington business \+ the plans for Reorganizing the office of Sup'n. It was at last put into the hands of the House Com. Jas Hart — Beckwith \+ self — Watrous, altho' not yet entitled, took his seat as new member — It was near midnight when I got home.
