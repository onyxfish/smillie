## Left Page

### Sun. July 11, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant breeze all day but exceedingly hot in the sunshine. I found enough to do to keep me busy all day \- hanging curtains in studio \- mending bamboo screens for piazza \+ looking after some of the nut trees that were set out last fall. I had a talk with Many in the course which it was made plain to me that she has made up her mind to go abroad next year for an indefinite stay. I said "all right" I would find some way of taking care of myself \+ the boys \- Late in the afternoon I made up some developing \+ some toning solutions \+ Ralph \+ I toned a lot of prints that we made before we left New-York. Thunderstorms threatened savagely for a while but we got little more than wind \+ a decided fall in temp.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 12, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came home from Buffalo \-

Clouded up. Sprinkle of rain before 11 \- but heavy rain did not fall until about 1. Several heavy showers during P.M. \- becoming quite steady at night. Went downtown in [illegible] \+ back about 11 \- Jobbing around until dinner \- about 1 Cooley's men came \+ moved the air pump into cellar. Many \+ the girls worked on Jim's curtains that were put up late in the afternoon making his room one of the prettiest in the house. In the rain, at 6.05 Ralph \+ I took train for Alford where we met Jim at 6.50 He came from Buffalo \- started at 9.30 \- got to Binghamton at 2 \+ had to wait there until nearly 5 for a train that would stop at Alford. He is very much sunburned, looks remarkably well \+ is in good spirits. We all had tea after 8 o'clk \- a quiet evening followed \- decidedly cool.
