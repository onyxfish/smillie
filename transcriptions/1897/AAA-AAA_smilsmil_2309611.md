## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 25, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day — high wind \+ some heavy showers. After 6 P? I went to studio — to market \+ grocers, then to N.A. \+ saw Mr. Graunis, The new Clerk of The Academy — after some time there I went to lea \+ Lee, Nassau \+ consulted about extension of N.A. lease, etc \+ to Gardner's, 59 Wall S., where I saw Collins. Home to lunch at 1 — after that, again to N.A. Saw Gardner \+ went over the Benedict business with him — B's statement, as far as we examined, was verified by the N. A. books — Then prepared my report for the Council meeting in the evef. — left it 4. 30 — home \+ got a rest before dinner at 6.30 — Evef. to N.A. at 7.30, met Benedict for a few moments before Council meeting. Very nearly a full meeting, but nothing of especial interest transpired — at about 11 walked up with Geo. Locked the front door \+ went to bed — next mf. it let! Bessie informed me that I had locked her out \+ that she had slept in mad. Donovan's bed nightgown. She had been to Charles' rooms \+ I knew nothing of it — when she \+ Chas came to the house later they could not make noises enough to arouse any one.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 26, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, high wind, but not cold. Mrs. Lang came to the house at 8, I gave her studio keys \+ she spent the day there cleaning. I went to studio for awhile — then to Davis, Bros in 42 S — then to Knickerbocker Athletic Club, Mad. Ave. 46 S — to inquire about Gymnasium privileges for Jim. Then to Bdway \+ 82 S — Harry Bentz Advty office \+ there met de Fraud Benedict by appointment at 11, I had an unpleasant hour while the two B's discussed the possibility of reaching a harmonious arrangement of the N.A. \+ B's business — I did not see how it was possible under the circumstances \+ at 12 I left the two together. I went to 3rd Nat. Bk, then to N.A. \+ home to lunch at 1 — After that I went down town to Speyer's in Broad S. about Cent. Pacific Bonds maturing next Jany, then to Cyrus Lawrence's \+ asked his advice — then to Cent. Trust Co — \+ saw McHyde on same subject. Then to see \+ see about N.A. business — then to Masson Engine Co. in Cortlandt S. Then to studio \+ home shortly before 6 — Dinner at 6.30 — after that read newspaper \+ to Ralph's — Chas came \+ took Bessie to his room again to hear his new instrument
