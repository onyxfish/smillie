## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 24, 1897

**Weather:** Bright, cloudless, cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cloudless, cold day \- to studio at 9.30 \- Painted on Genl. Greene head until 12 \- then to N.A. \+ to Sterne Bros where I did a little shopping. Home to lunch at 1 \- to studio at 2 \- Painted on Greene and carried the \# work as far as I know how. Miss Sterling, a young woman interested in etching called to know if I wd. give lessons to her in etching. At 4.30 I went to N.A. to finish up some of my accts. There but did not complete my work \- Home to dinner at 6.30 \- A quiet eve'g, before a pleasant fire that held me in thrall \+ indolence.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 25, 1897

Thanksgiving Day.

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day \- rain at night \- not cold. When I got to studio I found that the Genl. Greene portrait, that I thought last eve'g. was finished, was not satisfactory \- so. I worked on it until 1 o'clk. improving it considerably, I think \- then I took a short walk before dinner at 2. We had a full dinner table \- Geo. \+ all of his family being there. Charl came in while dessert was being served. It took us more than two hours to get through with the meal. After it I did some letter writing \- About 6. with Geo. \+ Nellie went to my studio for half an hour \- then home \- no tea \- Eve'g. I wrote more letters, being much in arrears.
