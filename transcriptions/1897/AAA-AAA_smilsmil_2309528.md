### Wed. May 12, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant mt. threats later — Thunderstorm early evelf. — heavy rain thro' the night —  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annual meeting — N. A. f D —

As soon as I could get thro' with my mt. duties I went to N.A. \+ for some time was engaged in attending to the business I found there — Then I started upon the preparation of a report concerning the accounts of the Academy — was employed closely until a little after 1 o'l lunch had commenced in the Council Room but I preferred to go home where I had a letter to eat \+ a rest for 10 minutes — I got vol. A. \+ went into the meeting while Wood was reading his Ret. of. numerously after that I had to present my Report. I stated that for the first time I was informed that a good Report \+ for the first time there were no printed copies of my report as Treas — The Reason I would explain later. Then I read what I had prepared \+ after that I gave, as clearly as I could, an acct. of Farrington's management — The surprise was intense — every neck was craned forwards — every ear was strained \+ the silence was painful. I my nerves did not fail me I went thro' with the story to to and I am told I said — That this I can at L, the first in the journal history of the A — showed have occurred. In my administration of the Treas. — was a humiliation to me complete. I left it to the N. A's to decide the extent to which it had impaired the possibility of my usefulness to the A —, the motion to accept my report was promptly made \+ seconded. out Chas. Miller objected — There was a wrangle — Perry insisted upon his motion — Watrous offered as an amendment a vote of confidence in the officers of the A — Perry accepted it to it was passed up previously \+ unanimously — Chase \+ others especially Huntington made little speeches exceedingly complimentary — all — some even affectionate — I could only way — Gentlemen I am profoundly grateful — I have been on the rack for the past 3 days \+ nights — This makes amends to me for all that I have suffered — After the meeting there were many gratifying exhibitions of [illegible] personal good feeling — The dinner that followed was a very jolly one \+ thus' it was over before 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 13, 1897

**Weather:** Heavy showers throughout the day —  **Temp:** [illegible]

Before I was dressed in the mt., a Reporter from the Journal called to interview me concerning the outrageous article that appeared in This mt. World. \+ shortly after 6h f — a Reporter from the Telegram came on the same errand. I was so much incensed at the World article that I spent some time writing a reply to it — It was noon when I got to studio — Beck was there — I read my note to him — he advised me not to take any notice of the publication [illegible] and I acted on his advice. Before I got away from the A — I was caught by a Herald Reporter who took me to the 5th Ave. hotel \+ we had a conversation before a stenographer who took notes. Then I hurried back o A — \+ then to studio at 2 o'lk. found Homer Lee \+ most of Miller's carpenters standing in the rain writing forms — H. d. is terribly put up on acct. of his intimacy with Farrington \+ the fact that he let F. have a check to keep him out of his difficulties with N.A. The Carpenter put up the drop table along side of my printing table — It was 3 o'lk when I got home to lunch \+ I was caught by a second Herald Reporter — It was 4:30 when I got to studio \+ then found that C. H. Eaton had been there twice to learn about the J. W. samples of Water Color paper — Jos. T. Weir came in for a few minutes, looking for Maynard — at the house Chas Burmore called about our contract for quick Greene portrait, not yet signed. After minutes before 6 I got to 44 \+ was kept there for some time by Davis the janitor \+ Collins, the accountant, as Davis calls him. Then I went to Mr. Lee's rooms in 36 S. W — he was not in — It was 7 o'lk. when I got home to dinner — Uncle W. \+ Aunt A — expected, on their way home from Phila., but they did not come — Every at home — Charl spent most of the evef. with us.
