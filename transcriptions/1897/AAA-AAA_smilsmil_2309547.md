## Left Page

### Sat. June 19, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Went to Ridgefield

A very warm day. Having decided to accept Georges invitation to spend Sunday with him I went to J.C. bought tickets + telegd. to him. Then to Studio + saw Douglass men started at their work there, sizing the walls and preparing a color for the cone. Then to 2d. Natl Bank + to N.A. + D. — where I was kept by correspondence + getting together from the mass of papers gathered by Gardner — Home to dinner — The last one prepared for us by Katie Coleman who leaves us this afternoon to sail for Ireland next Wk. Our darkies were to come this P.M. but when I decided to go to Ridgefield over Sunday, Mary went to see them + postponed their advent until Monday. I went to Studio + had a look at what had been done on the walls there — very unsatisfactory so far. Then home — packed for Raeffe [illegible] + self and at 3.40 I locked up the house + we went to J.C. Station + took 4th train to Ridgefield getting there at about 6. — Ge2 was at the station + together we walked to his house where presently we had our very meal. After that, in the twilight, Ge. Mary + I took a walk. A very quiet every + to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 20, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A close, humid n'f. Thunderstorms before noon — clearing afternoon, very clear night, almost cold, with bright Aurora. After bkfp. Ge2, Mary, + I. sauntered about for some time, up on the Ridge, until it was thought safest to get in before it rained — a very few drops only fell. I was after 2 when we had dinner + later in the afternoon Ge2, Mary + I again walked — this time to the northern end of the village \+ out as far as the cemetery. A late tea. Eve'g., it grew to be so cool that I was pleased when a small wood fire was lighted + very glad when heavy winter blankets were put on my bed.
