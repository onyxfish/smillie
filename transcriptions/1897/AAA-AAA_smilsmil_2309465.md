## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 2, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A struggling day — Trying to be good but not succeeding very well. — I put in a good days work at studio Commenced washing color on "View near San Remo" 13/2 × 22. Paper without sizing \+ tinted a pale blue I got the surface covered with transparent washes, then used body color on the lights. I am hopeful that I may get some good qualities by the combination Evely, Jim \+ Ralph went to a party given by — Dale, Cor. Park Ave. \+ By \+ S. They did not get home until 10.30 — I went to Century \+ met E. A. Abbey \+ had a pleasant little chat with him I got home at 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 3, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty, raw, foggy, muggy day. — Bkf. at 9.30 — Read newspaper until 10.30 then to studio until nearly 1 — then walked until 2, in spite of the weather, then home to dinner. — From 3 to 5 I was on the dining room sofa — then to studio \+ attended to my fine — Tea at 6.30 — Evely Geo. \+ Charl came in — I wrote to Howland asking if the Bd. of Management wd. extend the privileges of Century Club to E. A. Abbey during his stay in this country, as one distinguished in the Fine Arts. — also a note to Jack Downes \+ one to Eliz. Howe.
