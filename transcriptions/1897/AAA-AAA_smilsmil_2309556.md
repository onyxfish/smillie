## Left Page

### Wed. July 7, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day - very busy about the house all day. much of the mng. in Jim's room putting up the new brass curtain poles there - in the afternoon the two large cases (freight) came \+ I had some heavy work unpacking them - In the evet, the case with Annie's bust came by Exp. but no bicycles. While we were at tea Perigo drove the white pony up, hitched to a donkey cart of D. V. Gardner's. He says the pony is 8 or 9 years old - is perfectly kind \+ sound, altho' he admits that he has recently developed a tendency to stiffness in his near hind leg. He wants $30 for him. I told him that I would try to get a cart \+ harness \+ would try him - I want him for the boys. Showers passed so fus late in the afternoon - very fine cloud effects at sunset \+ a delicious moonlight night.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 8, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hot day with tinkering breezes. The mng was overcast \+ very muggy at times, clearing afternoon, with a deliciously cool eveg \+ wonderful moonlight night. I spent much of the mng down town, accomplishing but little. Most of the afternoon I was in Jim's Ice Room finishing the putting up of the poles there \+ rigging the awning gear. Piano for Bessie came while we were at dinner. Eveg on the Piazza until after 10 .
