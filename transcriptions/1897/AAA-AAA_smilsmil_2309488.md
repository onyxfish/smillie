## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 19, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day. After my outf. duties \+ going to studio to see that every thing there was right I walked down to Butlers \+ gave instructions as to repairs on my big frame \- \+ I ordered a frame for my Water Colors Vineyards near Ravello. Then to N.A. and instructed Farrington concerning arrangement of N.A. accts, especially the $100000 Endowment fund. Then down-town to Central Trust. Mr. Hyde went with me to Sub. Treas \+ where I assigned the $14000 U.S. Bonds \- then we went to Cornell's office (Broker) not in \- back to Central Trust, where I waited until C. came \+ with him I arranged for the sale, before March 6th, of all the U.S. Bonds now held by the N.A. in Charles office for a few minutes. 1.40 before I got home to lunch. 2.40 before I got to studio \- in no shape for work. fussed awhile over cutting mount for "Cathedral Tower, Ravello". Man in cellar all day jetting furnace pipes \+ putting asbestos jacket on furnace to save waste heat. \- 6½ y. after dinner, dressed, intending to go first to Architectural Dept. Dinner \- but was too late \- so drove directly to 69th S. to Reception at [illegible] Hearns \- given to the Art Com. of the Lotus club \- a stag affair. The house open from basement to attic \+ thin every past crowded with pictures, some remarkable \- portions very good \- others remarkable portions very bad \- all jumbled together in amazing fashion \- at about 10 I went to Jas. Morse's to a Reception given to Mr. \+ Mrs. Stockton \- met a number of pleasant people there \- home before 11.30.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 20, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Grey day \+ rather raw \- light fall of snow at night. I felt much out of sorts all day \- grippe, or cold, or something of that kind threatening me. Went to 42d S. \+ ordered 4 tons of nut coal for studio furnace. The change in size is in accordance with advice given by Cryer, who put in the heater \- The man jetting the pipes in cellar finished his work to-day. I called at Mary Cook to ask her \+ Edw. to dinner next W[illegible]. She gave me a talking to on the subject of Mary, her girls, \+ Kate going to Sunake for a year or more \- I met Louise in the butcher's \+ asked her \+ Walter to come to dinner next Wed. Then to studio \+ worked steadily until 1, painting over the distance again, trying to get breadth, atmosphere \+ improved color. To lunch and back \+ painted during a part of the afternoon only. \- Dinner at 6.30 \- I read Hiawatha to the boys \- then some reading for my self \+ to bed early.
