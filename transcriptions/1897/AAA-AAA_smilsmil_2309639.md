## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 20, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold \+ raw - the promise of snow in the air, which came about 3.30 - very dark all day. I got to studio at 9.30 \+ to work on Evening Water Color - At 12 went to N.A. I had written to Gardner to meet me there at that hour - his last chance - But he did not put in an appearance, nor did I expect it. He's a fool, as well as a thief, if he has it shipped - I worked at getting Art checks to pay the artists who have sold pictures in the [illegible] that closed Sat. ev'g. Then to bank with deposits \+ home to lunch at 1.30. Back to Studio \+ worked on W. C. until about 4, Then home \+ had a rest before dinner - Went to Council meeting - I made a full report concerning the bold stealing by Gardner - \+ also the present very unsatisfactory condition of the [illegible] case which seems to have [illegible]. I have no doubt but that McDaughline understands the situation even better than I do, \+ knows that with trouble with Gardner - the Dis. Atty. declining to go on with the case \+ the turning of Tables in the Dis. Atty's office under the new administration, it's chances never were better. I have been told that McD. - is expecting a place in the Dist. Atty's office - in that case he may be it's prosecutor \+ defender - It is a nasty muddle. Stayed at N.A. until 11.30 making out checks for artists who have sold pictures during expn just closed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 21, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still disagreeable weather. To studio at usual time - Worked on Water Color all the m'g with little interruption. I had hoped to get something fairly good out of it; but, as ever, am disappointed - \+his dull \+ muddy - I had little chance to work in the afternoon as I had to be at the N.A. at 3.30 for a meeting of the Building Com. All present but Blum. Maynard Secy. Letters from Perry \+ Nicoll were read - Secy directed to notify the architects that we were ready to consider the plans in detail preparatory to beginning building - At 4.45 it was announced that the sub-com. app'd at the meeting last Sat, to nominate a Fine Arts Com'r to Paris Exp'n 1900 was waiting for the Council Room - Bel'g Com - ady? Being a member of sub-com. I waited - all came - La Farge - Jas. Hart. Alex Harrison, A. Wattson \+ R.W. Van Boskerck. La F - in the chair I was app'd Secy. The discussion soon became very earnest, Van B - leading off with the proposition that a painter sh'd fill that place. He proposed Wm Coffin as a man in every way qualified - I [illegible] - thought it was purely a business place \+ business qualifications alone were needed - The juries here \+ in Paris w'd attend to the Art part. I w'd forward the name of Cauldwell - Discussion was continued until after 6 - Then I proposed we adj'n to Thurs p.m. I was directed to notify the Secy of Fine Art Federation that nominations w'd be sent on Monday next \+ requesting a call for a meeting of the Federation at the earliest possible date - Home at 6.45 - Ev'g. Read awhile to R - Then to Century \+ saw several painters \+ buzzed with them on the subject of the Paris Com'n \+ nomination - Home at 11.
