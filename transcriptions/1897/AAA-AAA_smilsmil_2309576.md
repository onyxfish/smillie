## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 16, 1897

**Weather:** Bright, pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, pleasant mtf. a shower between 1 o 2 o'clk. when, for a short time rain fell heavily. High wind during the afternoon \+ evtf. In the mtf. sometimes spent fixing things for Jim, sharpening his big knife, mending broken fish trace casting plaster of Paris butterfly tablets etc. Then I got to work scraping on Gen. Green's uniform. At 12.30 wandered over my farm until dinner times. afternoon worked on place until 5.30 \- then, a short bicycle ride \- \+ a belated tea. Evtf. read while to Ralph \+ then superintended Jim while he mounted, for the first time, a butterfly (one of his own raising) in a Denton tablet. Joe Williams called on the girls in the evening.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 17, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A chilly, windy, cloudy day. I got to work on Gen. Green at 11 o'clk, taking a little rest just before dinner \+ in the afternoon I worked until near 5 \- with some interruptions in connection with the gathering together of Jim's things \+ the packing of his trunk by Mary. I rec? a couple of written applications for Farrington's place \+ a check from P.J. Brennan, \- N.A. funds \- I wrote the necessary replies \+ went to P.O. at 6 \- \+ again, with the boys, at 8. Evening when I shut Jim's trunk, preparatory to locking it; I found that it had given way at one corner so that it must be repaired in some way before he can use it to-morrow \-
