## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 27, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — After bkfs. went to butchers with Bessie & gave her an object lesson in marketing for meat. Then to studio — there until 11 — Then to Speyer & Co. in Broad St. & left my ten Cent. Pacific Bonds, due next Jany. for extension under agreement — Then to see Du. saw Mr. Kingsbury & told him to inform Dist. Atty that I was ready to go on with the Prosecution of Farrington — Then to Second Nat. Bk. & home to lunch at 1 — After that my head hurt me so that I was on my bed until 4 — Then to studio & worked there arranging things in the room & unpacking bicycles in the cellar. Kindled fire in the furnace. Dinner at 6.30 — At 8.30 — Jim, Ralph & I went to studio & brought back a number of small things brought from Montrose — Then Charl came & I went with him to his room & heard his new instrument the Æolian Orchestrelle — Home soon after 11.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 28, 1897

**Weather:** Overcast m'g, growing milder & clear at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'g, growing milder & clear at night. Got up with a headache that almost entirely used me up for the day — I did what I could at studio during the m'g. waiting for carpenter & gasfitter. but they did not come — About noon went to N.A. & attended to some business there. Home at 1 but could take no lunch — again to studio — Freight cases came from Montrose & I unpacked both of them. Bessie, the boys & I, were invited to dine at Ed. W. Cook's but I could not go — had a cup of tea at home — At 9, as I was going to bed, Geo. came in for a few minutes.
