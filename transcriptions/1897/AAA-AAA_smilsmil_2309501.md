## Left Page

### Wed. March 17, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant day — quieter & milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — quieter & milder — To studio at 10 & to work on San Luis Rey. \+ at the close of the day had got pretty well over the buildings & down to the foreground — nothing of interest during the day. Ralph was easily induced to spend the morn on his bed — was dressed after lunch \+ seemed very well & bright during the afternoon. Eve'g. at 8 o'clk. went to the N.A. Annual meeting W. C. Socy. I went there to present the matter of the pure O. W. paper manuft? in England by a Co. of Artist shareholders. Mr. North, the Chairman of the Co. has written to me for advice as to the best way to reach the Water Colorists of the U. S. — and the public here. It was discussed \+ I shall advise him to send samples to us for practical experimentation — If the result warrants it, I shall try to get a Society endorsement. Home soon after 11. K. & B. at German Opera

## Right Page

### Thur. March 18, 1897

**Weather:** Dull & disagreeable  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull & disagreeable. Ralph, while insisting that he felt perfectly well, decided to remain in, or on his bed all day — so, he did not dress. I got to studio after 10 & worked in a desultory sort of way on San Luis Rey mission, finishing the second painting. For a good part of the afternoon I was clearing up about the studio — & did a little writing — at 5 I went to D. B. Butler's & ordered a flush lined shadow box for San Luis R. Then to studio until 6.30. Home to dinner. After that, read awhile to R. Charl came in at about 9 — at 10 he went with me to the studio where I fixed five — then home —
