## Left Page

### Fri. June 25, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortable hot day in spite of a breeze. Up soon after 6 \+ before 6k(p). wrote a long letter to Jim \- After 10 I went to Dis. Atty office first saw Mr. Perkins \- he sent me to Mr. who has charge of the calendar \- He discouraged me, telling me that the Courts \+ Jonsons are crowded \+ that it is by no means certain when the 7\- case can be brought into court. Then I went to Homer Lee's office \- not in \- I wrote a note, guarded, its purpose being to move him, if possible, to get bail for \+. In that case I could have the case hung up until next fall \- then to Croton Bureau \+ paid Water Pay \- then to Judd in Chambers S =, \+ bought brass rods etc for Jims bedroom in Montrose \- Home to dinner at 1.30 \-. Then to N.A., Gardner \+ Collins there working out the problem that I have given them to make the misused checks Fully with embezzled cash that must be clearly \+ specifically traced. Then to studio \+ commenced picking up things there \- Home to tea at 6.30. P \- \+ I were going to take a bicycle ride in the even't, but it was too hot. I wrote a long letter to J. Co Necoss.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 26, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day, bright \+ clear with a refreshing north westerly breeze. After 6k(p). I went to studio, then to N.A., \- There for a short time \+ then back to studio where I commenced the work of packing up, preparatory to a start for Montrose. Home to dinner at 1.30 \+ back to studio until 4 \- then home \- had a short rest \+ soon after 5 Ralph \+ I went to Central Park on our bicycles \+ rambled about there \- stopping for awhile to hear the music on the Mall. It was are delightful but made me feel my lonliness with more than usual pain. It was about 7 o'clk when we got home \+ I did not take tea until near 8 \- I read awhile to R \- at about 9.30 I went to Century Club \+ there met an artist group \- Dielmace, Binstal, Maynard, Perry, Jones (park) \+ C.H. Turner \- I sat with them for nearly an hour, Then home.

This p.m. I rec'd a subpoena to appear at Sessions II on Monday, June 28th. to appear as witness agt. Farrington \- This is a great surprise \- The Dist. Atty is not prepared to go on with the case \- no Asst. has yet been appointed to prosecute the case.
