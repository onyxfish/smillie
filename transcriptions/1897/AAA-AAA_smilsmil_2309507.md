## Left Page

### Mon. March 29, 1897

**Weather:** Very fine day \- bright & clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Alice's Ireland came to Report that Jim was out of order \- went there & found him on his bed \- had been sick during the night. I think that he over worked himself yesterday \- was imprudent in eating and in taking a bath too soon after eating. I went to studio. Jim & Raeon came there at about noon \- I got out Water Color \- view near Snufftown \- crashed off as much of the sky as would come off & put in another one. About 12.30 took a short walk with the boys before lunch. After lunch R \- & I went to 2\. Nat. Orts. I deposited $200 that I got from Maynard \- then back to studio & worked on Snufftown until near 5. Then home & had a rest. Evng. to NA fol \- Hanging Com. \- passed from to Corneil \- I saw Pauvins \- well placed \- but it is so weak & thin in color that it made me feel very unhappy \- After Corneil. the Site and plan com. had a session \- a sub. com. was chosen to arrange the specifications or Requirements to be put into the hands of the competing architects. Beckwith, Dielman & J. were chosen \- my selection was a surprise to me. This gets me into harness at an earlier stage than I expected. \- I had looked forward to a place on the Building Com. \- Beckwith announced a plan to used it strongly of making this new Com. a starting point for a larger one \- by additions to carry the work Thro' to the end \- This was before the members of the sub com. had been named \- his plan was approved \- Thus I may be on the jury \- on the Com. \- having the success ful architect in charge of/upon the Building Com. \- a series of grave Responsibilities. Home at 12 \- to sleep at 2 \- awake at 6 A.M. — 4 hours of sleep \- not enough.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 30, 1897

**Weather:** Clear, mild, lovely \- a true spring day  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bft. I went to Walter's \- saw him & read to him Nicoll's letter from Mexico, urging, upon advice of F. E. Church, now there, that the N.A. shall not decide the question as to who shall be its architect, until some enquiry has been made concerning the architects of the City of Mexico \- Jim came to the house on his way to school to report that he was quite himself again. It was after 10, when having finished my many N.Y. duties I got to studio \- there for a little while \- then to NA & spent the rest of the time looking at the new Report. My Vesuvius is well hung upon the S. wall of the large gallery but its appearance is a dreadful disappointment to me \- it is colorless, vacuous, weak & unattractive. After the coming Report I had hoped that my work upon it had brought it up to a fine pitch, but this hope has had a rude overthrow. Home to lunch at 1 \- at 2 went to Cashlear, Sota in Potgies gallery \- sale to night \- not a good, characteristic collection \- to studio at 3.30 wrote a note writing \- then home & had a good nap, making up the time lost last night \- at nearly 7 o'clk. when we had dinner. Hospital affairs progress slowly \- Kate still groaning about the house \- Katie T. "a little better" \- Grecia crawling about. I read to R \- after dinner until he went to bed \- then went to Earls Cooke \- John remaing there \- at 10 went to studio \- home at 10.30.
