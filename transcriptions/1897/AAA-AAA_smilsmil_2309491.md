## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 25, 1897

**Weather:** Overcast & mild  **Temp:**

To studio at 9.30 - Did not feel much like work but painted on lower R.H. corner of Vesuvius, being much bothered to get perspective of color in the middle distance foliage. Home to lunch \+ back as usual. Painted til 4, then cut a mount (partly) for ring ands near Ravello". By the plug getting out of the valve in one of my hot-water radiators I got into an absurdly awkward mess - The hot water was flying all over my printing table \+ press in my efforts to plug it up until I could find the metal plug that had come out of it - I got very generally wet from head to foot \+ when I went home had to change my clothes, shirt, shoes \& all - Dinner at 6.30. Every, Mary went with Charl to Symphony concert. I read until 10 - to bed early.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 26, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f., overcast p.m., light snowflurry at w'f., later, clear \+ cold.  **Temp:**

To studio at about 10 \+ spent me day, with exception of lunch hour at home, painting upon Vesuvius. I finished the second painting of the lower R.H. corner. Washed up \+ then home just before 4.30 - Then I lay down \+ rested for an hour - Then dressed \+ at 6.30 was at Mrs. Barhytt's - 40 2, 70 b. where I had been invited to dine. Mrs. B\. had a scheme that she desired to explain to me. After dinner Mrs. B\. told me that she desired to do something that w'd. be a memorial of her bro. A.H. Baldwin \+ she had thought of establishing a prize (annual) of $200. to be given to the best free hand teacher in a class to be under my direction. She was impatient to have something done at once - I advised deliberation - promised to think of the matter \+ talk with her in a few days. Home before 10 \+ went to studio \+ took a look at Vesuvius by gas light - A change in the lines of the foreground came to my mind that I think will be an improvement. Home at 10.30 \+ worked until 11.30 [illegible] time to come in - has been taking part in an entertainment at e.m.c.a. under direction of Mr. J.B. Morse with whom he dined.
