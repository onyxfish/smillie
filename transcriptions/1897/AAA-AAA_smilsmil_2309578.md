## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 20, 1897

**Weather:** Glorious day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cool day — I got to work in my etching room as soon as my other duties would permit. I was all the m'f. getting my printing outfit into working condition. Before dinner I took proofs from a small mezzotint plate \+ from a little aqua tint experimental plate. Then I had a little bicycle exercise. After dinner I took two p/p from Pent. Greene, the result being more satisfactory than I had hoped. After that there was considerable clearing up to do \+ then I had a little more bicycle work. Dates in the w'f., near 8 o'clk. H.R. Decker came with 28 young roosters which he put in the Hen house \+ for which I paid him $9.00 being stutsidly outwitted in my bargain with him. To P.O. at 8 o'clk. Saw nothing more exciting than a newspaper.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 21, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of great variety in a weather way. Very cool m'f. — many clouds — high wind — bright sun — quiet — warm — clear — etc. etc. It was 11 before at last I got to work on Pent. Green again — began scraping on back ground, to quiet it \+ make it much lighter. I thought that I had "got on" to the trick of scraping, but someway it did n't work satisfactorily. Soon after 12 I got on my wheel \+ rode out on the road beyond the "little debate". Very warm, hard work. Dinner at 1 — most of the afternoon, was given, after a little work on Green, to survey of my farm \+ its products, especially the plumbs which promise to be a good crop. To P.O. at 6 — tea — to P.O. at 8 — after 9 read for an hour "Aug. Century" — then to bed.

Mr. Bernard de Schweinity called in the m'f. with his two daughters, des cretia \+ Margaret. I have not seen him for years — He is here on a short visit.
