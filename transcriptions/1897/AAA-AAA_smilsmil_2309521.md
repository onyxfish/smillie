## Left Page

### Wed. April 28, 1897

Mary & Katie T. went to Po'k.

Rain the m'f., but it cleared before noon \+ aetro' cloudy was not unpleasant. Mary & Katie T. went to Po'k. in 10.30 train I got to studio shortly after 10 \+ spent the m'f. in getting my acid room in working order \- Had Miller there who took the measure for a rack to hold my porcelain trays \- \+ Ryan who measured to have gas brought under the new copper urn that I have just got. Home to lunch at 1 \+ back at 2 \- Ryan's man at work on gas fixture \- I fussed about generally \- among other things, revised two of my old reports, as "m.as", that go to go on the N.a. records. Home at 6. Dinner before 7 \- Susy reading to R\- until 8.30 \- Chas. came in about that time.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 29, 1897

Conference bet. Competing Architects \+ N.a. Com. on Site \+ Plan.

A wonderfully beautiful day. After 10 when I got to studio \- most of the m'f. spent writing letters \- on to French the Director of the Art Inst. of Chicago \- in the afternoon I drew working drawings for a drop-table to be placed beside my printing table and an arrangement to be placed under water urn I find that the gas burner makes it too hot for the wood work. After lunch I went with Ralph to Spauldings I saw Mr. Belts \+ completed the outfit of R's second hand new wheel \- met Jim on our way home \+ had him go back to Mr. Belts to see if he could get his saddle fixed. Home at 5\- Rested until dinner at 6.30\- at 8 went to N.a. Na. Com. on Site \+ Plan had invited the competing architects to meet them in conference for mutual enlightenment \- all the architects make of the Com. were there \- Post, Carrere \+ Hastings \- Hardenburg, Carey, Flagg \+ Walter Cook \- The programme arranged by Beckwith, Dielman \+ myself was the basis of discussion \- It seemed to me quite evident that the architects were all of a mind on one point \- that was that the Academy did not know what it wanted \+ upon them would devolve the duty of teaching them. Nevertheless there was every evidence of a desire to be agreeable \+ if there were any smouldering fires of jealousy, suspicion or distrust they were carefully hidden. Carrere \+ Hastings took early opportunity to discuss the matter of the drawings that were made by them under guidance of Beckwith \+ that were used so effectively before the Academy at the meeting when the up-town site was chosen! They placed them seemingly in the hands of the other competitors, saying that if it was so desired they could show the drawings that they made \- To me their offer seemed to be as complete amende \+ I did not think that it was as [illegible] as it should have been \- That matter was deferred to be settled by the architects among themselves. The discussion was continued until near midnight \- Watrous, &c., Walter \+ I walked up home together.
