## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 4, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day \- a little breeze, but carrying slight refreshment. June \+ Ralph went with the Presb'm S.S. on a pic-nic to Heart Lake. I went to station to see them off at 9.45. Then to P.O. got letters that required immediate attention, so, I did but little work cleaning margin of Yent Green. After dinner I completed the repairs of Piazza bamboo screens \+ got the last one in its place. Then I loafed in the piazza for an hour or more \- not a really cool place but the coolest I could find \- then a little more work on Green's margin. The boys came home at about 5, having had a fine time. I went to P.O. At 8, went to P.O. again \- on return read awhile to boys. Thunderstorms all about us \- especially heavy to the south. Rain commenced falling about 10 o'clk. \+ continued steadily all night.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded nite, clearing to be a delightful cool day. I got also feeling very uncomfortable about the throat, with shivers, etc., but I managed to put in some work on The Green margin \+ almost completed the stoning out of the mezzotint work on it. Our colored ladies went on a pic-nic at 2 o'clk. which obliged the family to have their dinner at 12. I could not eat at that time \+ waited until 2 when I had mine alone. Gill \+ his men came with the screens and put them over the tanks in the barns. They also did some work in the house, easing doors, etc. In the evening I felt so out of sorts that I went to bed when Ralph did.
