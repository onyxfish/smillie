## Left Page

### Mon. June 21, 1897

**Weather:** Clear, delightfully crisp day  **Temp:**

Ret? to N.Y.

Up at 7 \+ did a little wandering about until bkft. just before 8. Then more writing until 9.20 when Gel., Mary, Ralph \+ D went to 9.50 Train \+ after an uneventful ride, M.R.D got to N.Y. at 11.30. Went at once to house, \+ then to studio where I gave Dongass' men some directions concerning the color to be put on the walls. Then to Grocer \+ butcher \+ to N.A. \+ 2° Nat° bank — gathering material to be used as testimony before the grand jury — It was nearly 2 when I got home to lunch — a very frugal one — M. two colored girls put in an appearances as promised at 12.30. After lunch, Alice Ireland \+ Mollie Adams came in and I made an engagement to go on a bicycle ride with Mollie about 5 o'clk. Then to Studio \+ struggled for some time with Dongass' mill. Home after 4. Mollie came \+ we got away at about 5.15 — Mollie, Ralph \+ D. We went two the Astle (M. on Bessie's wheel) — out at 108th S. to Riverside drive, around Grant's tomb — down Boulevard \+ home at 7 — 12¾ miles — delightful ride — Dinner at 7.30 — Every I got subpœna to appear before Grand Jury to-morrow my Kingsbury came in later. \+ later yet Chrol came. It was after 11 when I got to bed

## Right Page

### Tues. June 22, 1897

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:**

Grand Jury indicted Farmington.

At 8 went to Fred Nolands \+ gave to Mallie Adams two photos, from Calverly's bust of Annie — then home to bkft. then to studio \+ gave the Kalsominer some hurried directions concerning the coloring of studio wall — then to 2° Nat° Bank, saw Case \+ got a list of the deposit slips of last year — N.A. \+ D. Acct. then to N.A. \+ after a little waiting, got Mr. Bradley \+ took her with me to the Dis. Atty's office in Criminal Court Bldg \+ there met Kingsbury \+ Gardner, after some delay indictment clerk Perkins drew up three indictments as: 1., two for grand larceny \+ one for forgery in the third degree. This work was promptly \+ it seemed to me, very intelligently done. Then we were passed to the waiting room of the Grand Jury where we had a tedious time until near 12 o'clk. when the witnesses were called for — Gardner went in first \+ took some time to explain the methods of F. — Then I was called \+ was questioned only as to the endorsement of checks deposited to Cr. of N.A. — no other witnesses were called — The two indictments for grand larceny were passed but the one for forgery was thrown out — I think because the jury did not exactly understand what constitutes forgery in 3° degree. I carried all the heavy books to the Academy \+ home to dinner at 1.30 — to studio in the afternoon — then home \+ rested until 4.30. About 5 R. \+ I went out on our wheels \+ had a charming ride. Home at 6.45 tea at 7.30 — Every Gharl came in for a while — just Ret° from a bicycle ride \+ had not yet had his dinner — I read aloud for a while \+ R. — then wrote to Hart, Beckwith \+ Dielman — posted notes \+ to bed at 11.
