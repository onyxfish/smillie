## Left Page

### Wed. June 23, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightful day. - To studio after offe. Met miss Stites (?) there. She has been writing me - wants the position of S.C.T. at Met A. \+ is sure that she has all the qualifications. Then young Douglass came in with paper hanger to talk about Burlaps on a part of the walls. I made troubles because I insisted upon Burlaps not less than 60" wide. - Then to N.A. - wrote a letter to someone who has been bothering me to introduce me to the competing architects with an assurance that I have perfect faith in his ability as a Structural Suflinger. He wont like my letter. - Home soon after 12. - Dinner at 1.30 - Again went to Studio to see how the work was progressing - at about 5 R. \+ I went out on our wheels \+ had a delightful ride of 13 miles and in at 6.40. - Tea at 7.30 \- Read to R- until near 9. - Charl came in.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 24, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Farrington pleaded not guilty in General Sessions -

At 2 A.M. I was routed out by an internal upheaval that moved everything from turret to foundation stone. Digestion seemed to have stopped \+ a cataclysm promptly followed. Almost simultaneously there was a downward rush \+ an upheaval - it was an agony for a few minutes followed by a cold sweat \+ a nervois ague or shiver - what remained of the night was passed in restless sleep. But I was able to get up at 8 o'clk. \+ take a light breakfast - After I went to studio \+ directed the man who is running the lines on the core. Then I Rd to the house \+ wrote letters until about 1 o'clk. - Douglass' men commenced painting the blinds at the back of the house. - About 3 o'clk I went to B.B. Butler's taking Rialto pen sketch to have a mount put on it for Mr. Woodin. - Then to Tiffany's \+ saw Mr. Platt about Burlaps, prepared for wall. - Then to Richter Co. 121 E. 23d S. on same subject - Then to U.A. - Then to Art Students league in 57. to see Mr. Whitehead the book keeper there, but he was out. - Then to the Sherwood \+ saw Beckwith. - There to 42d S. - Spaldings but did not see Belts. - Then to 42d S. \+ saw Ayre \+ saw young Douglass. - Then to Studio \+ home at 6.30. - Raw after 7. - Eve R. Read to R. - Then I wrote to Hart, Beckwith \+ Gardner concerning F. who plead in Genl. Sessions to-day, not guilty - his lawyer says that he will fight us \+ whips us out of our boots, as he has a good defence. - That defence is I believe some story damaging to the character \+ credibility of Gardner. - Charl came in for a few minutes.
