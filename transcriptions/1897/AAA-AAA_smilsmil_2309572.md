## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 8, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded but not unpleasant day - rather muggy with promise of rain. a very light shower fell at 11 P.m. After breakfast I took up a little book given to me on Annie's birth-day. "A Venetian June" by Anna Fuller. I had read some of the earlier chapters before - the story is not much but the "local color" is strong - so strong that I was carried back to enjoying in Venice with such force as to "break me up" quite completely - so that I did not recover from it all day. Afternoon I wrote to Bishop Putnam Evg. I wrote a long letter to J. A. S. - Gardiner + at 11 went to P.O.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 9, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - somewhat under the weather with the result that I did more than usual on the Penn. Green plate - I finished all of the rough work in cleaning the margin + burnished + finished a line of metal enclosing the work - I also mounted the first pf. taken from the plate + got ready to commence real work on the portrait to-morrow. I wrote to Lee Ilee \+ before noon took it to the P.O. - met Wm Lusk + had a talk with him about St.M. R.R. matters - He gave me some inner history that was very interesting to me. I worked until about 5.30 - then I went to fair ground + rode my bicycle until 6.30 - home + had my tea late + alone - Evg. Wm Mulford called on the girls -
