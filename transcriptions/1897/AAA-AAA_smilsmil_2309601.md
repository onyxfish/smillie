## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

And yet another lovely day, followed by a matchless night. Fontaine \+ a team with his bro. came in the m'f \+ worked on the barn yard wall. In the afternoon R's mate joined him from 1 to 4. Gill \+ Peter worked in the Beech trees north of the house, cutting out deadwood. These matters rec'd some puny attention. Stone got in the last of the buck wheat \+ re-wo[?] threshed it on the barn floor. I gathered some plumbs \+ Kate canned them. I had several notes \+ letters to write which took so much of my time that I did nothing toward packing. I went down town before 5 o'clk. and forgot most of the things I went down for. Eve'g I wrote a letter to bro. George.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 6, 1897

**Weather:** Fair Day —  **Temp:** [illegible]

Threatening m'f., light rain sprinkle. but soon it cleared \+ all clouds burned away, the day being a very pleasant one. After b'kfs. I went down town \+ attended to various matters \+ waited for the m'f mail - back at 10 - with Gill \+ Radeker, who were at work on the beech trees, until 11 - then I went on to the Fair Ground \+ was there until 12 - an unusual crowd of people - home at 12 - dinner at 12.30 - back to Fair Ground at 1.20 with Kate \+ Katherine \+ saw the bicycle trick rider, who, under the circumstances, did reasonably well. Back to the house at 3. Had some N.A. business letters to write \+ about 5 went to P.O. with them - got a batch of N.a. checks \+ ret'd to studio \+ wrote more N.a. letters until 6.30 - then took them to P.O. Tea. Eve'g, all hands were sleepy \+ to bed before 9.

Mary continues to improve \+ her Dr. has discontinued his visits.
