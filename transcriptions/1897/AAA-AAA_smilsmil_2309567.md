## Left Page

### Thur. July 29, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still wet, but coming from another quarter. The wind changed during the m'f. from east, around south \+ to the west, but showers continued throughout the day \+ ev'f. I worked pretty steadily on Jenl. Green Portrait, or rather, on the margin, which I did not finish. Hurry mail to-day I got a note from Chas. Kurty saying that he would like to have "Vesuvius" for St. Louis Ex'n + I wrote to him with reference to it \+ to J.W. Maynard. To P.O. at 5.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 30, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate arr'd. from Ridgefield —

Cloudy m'f. soon clearing to charming, warm day — Thunder showers during early ev'f. from 7 to 9 o'clk. I put in about a half-days work on the margin of Green plate — I had several N.A. \+ D. business letters to write which took up considerable time. I went to P.O. about noon — again at 5.30 — and at 8 P.M.
After tea with the boys I walked over to the station — Kate came on the train at 7.35 having left Ridgefield this m'f. It rained heavily soon after the train got in. She \+ the boys rode up to the house in 'bus — I waited at P.O. for the mail —
