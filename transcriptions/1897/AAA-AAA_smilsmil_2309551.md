## Left Page

### Sun. June 27, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightful day, bright, breezy \+ cool. B'kfo. at 9 \+ at about 10.30 I went to studio \+ worked there until 1.30 \+ selecting things to pack for Montrose \+ arranging things in the studio to leave. When I got home Gardner was there — he had rec'd his summons yesterday \+ was much perturbed at the suddenness of it, as he cannot get his figures into the shape I desire until sometime Monday night — Cash: Case of the 2 ½ at — having promised to let us have all of Farrington's deposit slips for last year. I told him that I was very sure the case could not be tried to-morrow. Dinner at 2. — It was about 4.45 when P — \+ I started out on our wheels — The air was delightful — We rode away out on [illegible] to Washington Ave. \+ at home at 7.15 after a ride of just 17 miles — we were both rather tired — I did not take my autoper until 8.15 — after that I read a short story to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 28, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In Gen. Sessions — Part II.
Katie \+ Bessie ret. from Po'k.

Beautiful day — After my m'f duties went to Studio — to O'Briens — to H.A. \+ then to Criminal Court Bldg. in Centre St. Saw W. Unger who has charge of the Calendar \+ in answer to my questions he explained that the J. Case was set for to-day upon the assumption that he would plead guilty — New I was introduced to Vamp Sauterbach, the Ass. D'ct. Atty. to whom this case has been assigned — We had a few hurried words together as to the character of the offense \+ the testimony that I could offer — Then we went to the Court Room where I met Kingsboury who introduced me to Mr. Laughlin who told us that he had been retained by J. During this m'f. that we had about 40 minutes conversation with him \+ that he considered him mentally unbalanced — He said he must either plead guilty or submit to the examination of a Com. in Lunacy — I am afraid that I made a mistake in assenting to this — but in a few minutes, Mr. Laughlin, Sauterbach, Kingsboury \+ I, it was agreed that the case should be marked off from this term \+ a com. promptly appointed to examine him — This will make it possible for me to go to Montrose at once with the possibility that I may have to return to N.Y. later in the summer if the case should then come into court. I then went to West B'dway \+ made an unsuccessful attempt to get a spring seat post for bicycle then to Fab. Harts — 11 2.14½ S — then home — Dinner at 1.30 — After, that to A.B. Butlers with "Fort Marie," for C.R. Woodin — there to H.A. — Collins there working up J's cash deposits from B'nk deposit slips — I wrote to Kingsboury — there to O'Briens — there to Spaldings \+ saw Belts about Jim's wheel that he sold for $18— Then home \+ had a rest before tea at 6.30 — Busty Chark, just Rel'd. from Boston, came in for a little while —
