## Left Page

### Wed. June 9, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Farrington before The Yorkville Court.
Bessie went to Polk.

A day of pouring rain \+ driving winds until late in the afternoon. After bflo. I went to W. 36" S. \+ saw Wm. N. L. Lee for a few moments \+ learned nothing from him — then to Murray Hill Hotel \+ saw Gardner — then to studio attended to marketing. \+ Rode up to 57 s. getting there at 10.15. Kingsbury, Mahler \+ Gardner followed. Doran \+ Farrington were already there — It was 2 or 3 m. after 11 when our turn came. It was a pitifully dejected looking mortal — In answer to Judge Pools questions he said that he was not guilty — he had been overworked \+ his mind had given way under the strain — he had not intentionally done any wrong — his mind as to the past was a blank — probably he had made mistakes but he had not appropriated any of the money to his own use — it had all been expended in one way or another for The Academy — He was without money \+ without a friend in the world — He wanted a little time to think \+ asked for a week. The judge gave him until Tues. next — at 2 o'clk. bail $1500 which I said he could not get. I never looked at me — By 11.30 we were all away — I rode down to N.A. \+ after a little delay there went home. Dinner at nearly 2. Miss Katherine Withers took her departure without adieu to any one, about 1 o'cllo — I did a little writing during the afternoon that was left — Had a short walk after 6 — went to Millers \+ gave orders for time to do some carpenter work about the house. Tea at 6.30. Evey went to Walter Cook's — Then to Edw. C's — not in — Chave came in — later, Beckwith came in to learn what had been accomplished at Court this m'f.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 10, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudy day — much milder. I did not get on with my N.A. Report as I expected as I found that I had a number of letters of one kind \+ another to write — I went to studio about 10.30 \+ got the frame from the Paris bridge drawing that I sold to Mr. Woodin and took it to D. B. Butlers to be regilt. I then went to N.A. \+ wrote two or three letters — bought some pastel \+ wayons \+ to studio's about 1 — saw Maynard for awhile — Home — dinner at 2 — Some more writing in the afternoon — Josephine Smillie called — I went to N.A. \+ wrote a letter to Lee \+ Rice — Soon after I went to Ed. Cooks \+ swu him \+ left with him Certificates of stock in Ireland Real Estate Co. in Annie's name, to be transferred to me — Then to studio \+ worked with pastels on the Woodie Paris bridge. Home to tea at 7 — Evey. Read aloud to Nelsn. until Chave came — Soon after that I was our friend by a visit from Mrs. Farrington who sat \+ talked with me \+ wept for an hour or more. I am not quite sure as to the object of her visit — She did not seem to be particularly anxious about what was to be done with I — but she did ask some direct questions to learn how much I knew of the women who had been running after I — but she was exceedingly reserved \+ careful in all of her expressions. The indignation of the injured wife if it was there, was not allowed to show itself. Mr. Cook \+ family came in \+ made a call.
