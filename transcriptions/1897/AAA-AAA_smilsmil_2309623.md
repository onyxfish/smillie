## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 18, 1897

**Weather:** Bright, cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold day \- mbed nearly all day \- had a hard time last night \- discomfort in my stomach when I went to bed increased to great \+ constant pain that kept me awake until after 2 o'clk \- then a little sleep, to be awakened after an hour or so to lie awake for another two hours \- In the m'f. I did not feel able to get up \- Cale came to see me in the afternoon \+ gave me a prescription \- Stomach pain succeeded by a severe headache \- After 5 I went to drug store \+ got medicines \- Went to studio \+ There met Grannis \+ filled for him an application for guarantee bond \- Home at 6\. Every Charl came \+ took Mary \- The girls went to a church reception \+ organ recital \- I lay upon sofa for a while \+ then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 19, 1897

**Weather:** Cold, rather raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, rather raw day \- Got up feeling very shaky \- got to studio at 10 but so wholly unfit for work that I sat for some time in my big chair \- The most important work that I did was cleaning my bicycle chain \- Home to lunch at 1 o'clk \- Margaret Buck, from Pkeepsie \- one of Bessie's friends, lunched with us \- on her way to New Haven \- I went back to studio for awhile \- then to N.A. where I hoped to find a lot of N.A. studying the architect's plans for the new Academy, this being the afternoon when they were specially invited to come \- only ten came, to my great disgust \- I went to Brooks Bros. \+ tried on my dress suit \+ overcoat \- they being satisfactory were sent home in the ev'f. Dinner at 6.30 Sw'f. I wrote to Beckwith concerning public eytu of Architects plans \- Went to Walter Cook's \- Carl Sprague \+ wife there \- Of course, the unsuccessful competition was the subject of conversation \- W\- feels very sore \- \+ I sympathize with him \- for the more I study the plans the less I like those of Carrere \+ Hastings \+ the more I like Walter's
