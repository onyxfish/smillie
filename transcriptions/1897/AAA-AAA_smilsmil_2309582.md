## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 28, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully beautiful day. I worked on Gen't. Green doing a fair amount of work but as it was scraping over the background it was of the most monotonous \+ tiresome character. At about 5 o'clk. I went to Fairground with my wheel \+ "did" 8 miles on the track \- Then home \+ read newspaper while I rested before tea time. Ev'tf. at 8 Ralph \+ I went to P. O. Read awhile to Ralph \- Then newspapers.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 29, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Roger Adams went to Buffalo.

A delightful day. After getting through with my regular duties I set myself to write to Beckwith but after two attempts I was obliged to give it up not being at all in the humor. So I went out \+ wandered about until dinner at 1, being earlier than usual to accomodate our colored lady help who had arranged to go on a drive. Afternoon I wrote to Valentine, Jas. Start \+ others \- feeling rather stewed at about 5 I took my wheel up on the Fairground \+ rode for 8 miles. Ev'tf. wrote to Miss Kate Sage at the Academy on business \+ at 9.30 went to P.O. We sat up until 11 o'clk. when Roger left us, driving over to New Milford to take a late train that is scheduled to get to Buffalo at 7 A.M. to-morrow.
