## Left Page

### Fri. April 30, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very warm but overcast & forenoon. Miller the carpenter came while I was at bkfs. — men with him — They repaired a break at the library window & then to work on foundations to rear fence in back yard. Late in getting to studio I wrote to Topeka Accounting Co. — sending abstract of Wall farm deed — also, other writing & I spent some time making asphaltem varnish & a drawing for a contrivance to support acid bottles in my hot water urn — After lunch I took drawing to Central Iron Co. & gave order to make it — Paul. work at studio until 5, then home — Ew. f. Charl came with his two bicycles & at about 9.30 I went out with him, riding his old wheel, which I find requires much less power to drive it than my heavier wheel. We made the circuit of the Park — 10 miles \+ got home at 10.45 — Jim & Ralph did not get home from the Morse entertainment until 11.50 It was after midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 1, 1897

Annie's Birth-day.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast & threatening all day — a slight sprinkle of rain about 10 a.m. & a dash about 4 P.M. — with heavy showers commencing before 10 P.M. & continuing thro' the night. To studio after 10 — there for a few minutes, then to 2d. Nat'l Bk — Men to N.A., where business of various kinds kept me until noon — Men to Central Iron works & get contrivance for hot water urn \+ to studio — To lunch — Afternoon spent some time over Koehler's Dürer Cat. (Grolier) \+ the specimens of plate printing sent on to me some time ago — Feeling the effects of late hours for two or three nights past & my bicycle ride last night, I had a good, restful nap before dinner — I Rec'd. notes from Johnny Kimball & Eliz. Howe referring to the anniversary — Eliz's note was accompanied by a little book "A Venetian June" by Anna Fuller. It was a disappointment to me that the weather prevented me from going to Greenwood & taking the boys.

Ev'y. I prepared to go to Century monthly meeting — but heavy showers kept me at home — Charl spent the ev'y with us.

Mary & Katherine Rel.? from Pol'k Tues' afternoon.
