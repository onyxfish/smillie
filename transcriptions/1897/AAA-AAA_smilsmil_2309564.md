## Left Page

### Fri. July 23, 1897

**Weather:** Bright early m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright early m'f. — soon clouded over + the day was a series of threats, Thunder showers passed all about us — so a very little rain fell upon us. Most of my time in the m'f was spent with the new pump. I kept it going until 3 p.m. + pumped most of the water from the house cisterns to the barn tanks through the old service pipe from barn tanks to house. Afternoon I wrote to Mrs. Bradley at the N.A. \+ to Ja^s. Hart. I make no progress with my studio work to-day.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 24, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Several heavy showers in the m'f before 9 o'clk — the day was a succession of light showers + muggy sunshine — with a breeze that was pleasant enough if you had nothing to do but swing in a hammock + enjoy it. In the m'f. I cleared out the drawers of my work table in studio — an old table of Annie's — I lingered over many old papers, among others, over a bundle of letters written to Annie in 1881 (the year of our marriage) + 1882 — among them two written by Rosalie Butler, who died a week ago, + two written by me to A. Nov. '81. I got my Gent. Green plate out + on my table + my original pf. photo + etc. up on my easel so that it looks now like a start up on my work. To P.O. before noon. letters from Kate + Charl. — Afternoon I wrote to both of them + mailed letters at 5. To P.O. again at 8. Even'g. Reading Harper's monthly for Aug.
