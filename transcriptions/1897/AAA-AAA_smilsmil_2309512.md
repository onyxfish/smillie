## Left Page

### Thur. April 8, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Parks dined with us

A dull day threatening rain which came in the eveg & thro' the night. I felt very shabby after last nights work & did not get to studio until after 10. I was able to do a little writing — wrote art form of contract for Barmore & a letter to Columbus in answer to an offer of $100 on "Meadow Stream" — Then to 2d Nat'l Bk — on my return stopped at Nat. D. & had a short look around the galleries. Home to lunch at 1. I did not go to studio again until near 6 when I attended to fire etc. Stopped at Edw't Cook's who told me that he had made up his mind to offer to buy a house on 89(?)S. near Riverside Drive. When I got home to dinner found W. J. Parke there. He left very soon after dinner & I went to Water Color Socty meeting called to protest ago't Fine Arts Federation action on Mch 24. The meeting was a full one but without the temper that showed itself last night at the N.A. I offered a form of protest which was accepted almost without discussion. Shuttliff's Resolution, declaring that the Socty as an organization, favored a specific duty was lost by a decisive vote altho' almost every man present favored a specific duty. The meeting adj'd before 10 & I got home before 11. Jim — Tom Cook & Thurston went to McAden Nurses. Jim got home at 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 9, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Emma Bourne stayed with us.
W. J. Parke dined with us.

A nasty wet day. Some very heavy showers in the morning. I was late in getting to the studio & spent the day almost altogether in writing letters that I can not get a chance now to write at night. Mtte no't I wrote to Beckwith giving him some acct. of the W. C. meeting last night. Emma Bourne came to the house, on her way to Po'ke. from Conn. & stayed overnight with us. We had a quiet eveg. Katie T. making her appearance in the parlor for the first time since her sickness. I read awhile to Ralph & got to bed at 11.

At 6 p.m. I went to W. [illegible]ee's house, 5th & 36th S. by appointment & saw him about Personal Tax matters — am to go to his office to-morrow.
