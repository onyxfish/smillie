## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 7, 1897

**Weather:** Rather unpleasant, some sunshine.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfo. at 9.30 — It was after 11 when I got to studio — Found there that Maynard was down sick — Rheumatic cold — Yale had been to see him — I went to 69 w. 48' St. to inform his bro. of Mr.'s condition. Then to Century, meeting Howard Russell Butler who congratulated me on the N.A. success rather caborvingly. At the club but a few minutes then home to dinner. After dinner Coon came for another interview — I feel as stupid as a boiled owl. After he left, went to studio and saw Maynard. C.H. Turner T. St. J. Harper were with him — At 6 went to Emma D's in 17th S. with a plaster duplicate of bust of Annie that I have given her. Many \+ Grace were at home \+ I took tea with them \+ stayed until 8 o'clk. Then home \+ said a few minutes to the boys before they went to bed. After that for some time engaged with my accts. Mary \+ Kate had gone to Clark's to spend the evg.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 8, 1897

**Weather:** A disagreeable day — cold, raw \+ sleety.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at usual time \+ until near noon I was engaged writing notes, letters, etc. etc that have been delayed. I gave some attention to Maynard, who seems to be better, Yale came in to see him. I went to 2d. Nat'l. Bk. \+ made a loan of $500, to be able to pay the Marshal's warrant for arrears of Personal Taxes on Estate of Annie. Then in to Purdy's — Home to lunch — again to studio for a little while \+ then to Met. Mus. of Art. Annual meeting — a matter of form, when ten or a dozen meet \+ hear the annual Reports read. Pres. Marquand was there. Chas. Stewart Smith. Salem Wales, J. P. Avery, J. Crosby Brown, T.W. Wood, Beckwith \+ one or two others — After the meeting I walked most of the way home in spite of the weather — Dinner at 6.30 — Evg. N.A. Council. Little business transacted — the most of it was to enable me to assign the Registered bonds (U.S.) of the A. to enable it to pay for its site. After adjournment an hour was spent discussing the problem of the selection of architect, or architects — \+ the security of plans \+ pro' competition, open or close, or none at all. The discussion was entirely informal \+ no conclusion was reached, but I think that Carrère \+ Hastings will draw the prize. It was after 11 when George \+ I walked up together.
