## Left Page

### Sun. June 13, 1897

**Weather:** Close & muggy with a westerly wind that had a humid chill in it. Cloudy most of the time  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got through with my bath & was down to o'clk. before 9 - after that I got to work upon my N.A. Report & finished it at 12. Ralph soon after 10 went with a note to Mary Moony in Columbus ave. near 98th S. He Rel. at 12 \+ I took him out with me at once to look after some up town property upon which the academy has loans. We went to W. 70th S. \+ the River. Then to West End Ave. \+ 98th S.: Then to 102nd S. \+ Boulevard \+ home to dinner at 2, after dinner I rested - it was 20 m. before 6 when R. \+ I went out on our wheels. We made the circuit of the Park. It was delicious - The weather had changed to be quite ideal - The mugginess was gone - the air while warm had vitality. The foliage never looked cleaner, greener or healthier - The last piece of music on the afternoon programmes was being played as we got to the upper end of the mall. I after the the ride was an exceedingly enjoyable one. It was almost 7 when we got home after a run of just 9 miles - I took a dry rub - changed my clothes - had a short rest \+ tea at 8. Early. I finished off my Report \+ have it ready to take to the Printer to-morrow. Charles came in \+ I read it over to him.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 14, 1897

**Weather:** Delicious day  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up early having thought of a new arrangement for my N.A. Report. I divide it, one portion is a plain business Report - the other portion, relating to Farmington \+ which is simply a defense of myself agt. such suggestions, innuendoes, or even charges as I am quite sure some of the Academicians might set against me, I put in the form of a letter to the N.A.'s. I worked on it for a while before o'clk. and again until after 10 o'clk - Then Douglas came \+ I spent some time with him before I went to the studio - then to O'Brien's - the N.A. \+ to the 2\. Nat. Bk. Then to Carmany's \+ bought a pair of shoes - Home at 1.30 - dinner - Before it was over, an elderly gentleman, a connection of Farmington's - I do not remember the name or the connection, came - the object of his business was to assure me that his mother had no money. There another man called to get my advice as to the best way of reproducing a portrait - He waited until I finished my dinner \+ then went to studio with me. I worked there beginning to get things together preparatory to a start for Montrose - Home at 5 \+ rested until tea at 6.30 - Rec'd a long, distressing drive from Farmington - finding fault because his "private character" has been assailed - but no suggestion as to what he has done with the $1400 he took - a letter also from Nicoll in Cal.. At 8.30 I went out with Ralph for a moonlight ride on our bicycles - The night was heavenly \+ we made the tour of Central Park - It was 10 when we got home.
