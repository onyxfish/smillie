## Left Page

### Sat. May 8, 1897

*Heading note: Farrington confessed.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — After going through my m[ail] duties I went to studio. Ralph met me there + together we went to 23d St. + parted, he going on a shopping tour — I, to the N.A. There I found Gardner + Farrington — F. telling G. that he had secured a check to cover his shortage but refusing to show it + still insisting that the shortage was the result of error somewhere in the book keeping — As work that was necessary to the preparation of my report was going on I decided not to return for it + returned to the studio — then home to lunch at 1 — At 2 went to N.A., meeting of Cornell as jury on school awards. I was with them at 3 o'clk when Gardner came in + told me that he had seen the check that F. had + that it was of no value being dated 9 days ahead. I decided to go in + tackle F. — at once + did so — At first I was alone but soon Gardner came in — I told F. — that I knew everything + that I wanted a frank confession from him, as it would simplify the business before me, + he the end, make it easier for him — He was exceedingly resentful + indignant at first — but I hung on to him — It was a very trying scene, but times exceedingly dramatic as when I urged him for the sake of his daughters to see me + then I would do all that I could to help him retain a position of some kind where he could make a living — he struck the table + cried out — "Say nothing about my children." At last he broke down — fell into a chair + could not get up — he sobbed + shook his head to foot — at last he turned to me — he could not speak aloud — + whispered — "Those checks were cashed." That was his confession + I did not press him further. At 4 J. + R. came + we went to Hoboken + went all over the New German Lloyds steamer Königin Luise down into her stoke hole + shaft tunnel — The boys were greatly interested — Home to dinner at 6.30 — at 9 o'clk. I went to N.A. + saw F. + made an effort to get a check that is missing, but he stoutly insists that he knows nothing about it, so I came home.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 9, 1897

*Heading note: To Greenwood.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day, altho' somewhat windy — Phil. at studio for a few minutes at 10 — then home — out at 11.15, Jim, Ralph + I went out on our wheels, about two! the park to 106th St., out to the Boulevard + up to Grant's Tomb — we went in + then rode down the new cinder path to 108th S. Then to Boulevard — home in just two hours, having ridden almost 14 miles — Had a rub down + change of under clothes — then dinner — At 3.15 Jim, Ralph + I went to Greenwood, getting to the gate in a little more than an hour from 42d St. We sauntered out to the lot where we found everything in good order — The sunshine was beautiful — the air was sweet — all was peaceful — restful — but what agony of heart There was in standing there, where she lay buried in the earth, with her dear boys — It was 6 when we left the station at the gate + in 50 m. we were home — Tea at 7 — Wolf — some reading to the boys — Clark came in later.
