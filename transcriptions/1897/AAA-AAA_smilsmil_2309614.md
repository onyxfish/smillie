## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 31, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, cool day. 9 o'clk. before I get my clfo. The boys were even later as I allowed them to sleep after their dissipation at ned Thurston's last night. I lounged with my newspaper until noon - then to studio for a short time \+ a short walk. Dinner at 2 - after that I wrote at letter to Miss (or Mrs.) Anna M.G. Carpenter of Warren, R. I., owner of the Trumbull miniature of Gen. Greene, from which I have been making a mezzotint portrait. She wishes me to return it \+ I beg for a few days more. Then I had a rest before tea time. Ev'g. Read aloud awhile to the boys. Charl came \+ took Bessie to his room. Later, Geo. \+ Nellie came \+ they followed Bessie. I wrote a long letter to S. R. Korhler.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 1, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day - wet during the day growing wetter - very steady, heavy rain at night. Went to studio in the m'f \+ got the carpenter started at putting up picture mouldings on the walls \+ the gas-fitter at putting in some new fittings at printing table. Then I went to Municipal Bldg in Centre St. \+ there met Mr. Lloyd, the Ass. Dist. Att'y who now has charge of the Farrington case. It is on the calendar for next Monday - with H. T. Kingsbury I made an app't to meet him on Friday at 3 p.m. to go over the evidence of the prosecution - went to N.A. and attended to business there \+ home to lunch at 1.30. To studio again in the afternoon - the gas fitter \+ carpenter were at work - the first soon finished - the carpenter finished soon after 5. I had a rest before dinner. Ev'g. N.A. Council meeting - several absent on acc't. of the weather. Home at 11.30
