## Left Page

### Sun. April 4, 1897

**Weather:** Bright. M.f. cloudie p.m. \+ rainy  **Temp:** [illegible]

rwe[illegible]. Got up before 8 with a fairly
level head. Me daughter of our neighbor
Mr. Edwards. was taken away in an am-
bulance about [illegible] a.m. to Roosevelt Hospital
to be operated upon for a pelvic abscess.
After [illegible] I completed letter to Nicoll.
Then I went to Walters \+ had a talk with
him — then home \+ Ralph \+ I had a walk.
Jim having gone to Cou Courtandt Park with
Tom. Dinner at 2 — after that I had
a nap. At 5 went to Century where
Beckwith, Dielman \+ I had our sub-
com. meeting to arrange programmes
for competition — Home at 7. Tea. Eve
read to the boys — Charl came in —
very tired after a 30 mile ride on his
wheel ag[ainst] a strong wind.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More or less rain during the day \+ the night.
I was not out of my bed but was nearly
wild with a headache that did not begin
to loose its grip upon me until after mid-
night. Kate wrote a note for me to Council
[illegible]. Mary went to Prov. early in the
m[orn]f. to attend to some matters connected
with the letting of her house there.
