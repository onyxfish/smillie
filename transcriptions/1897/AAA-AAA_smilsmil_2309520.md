## Left Page

### Mon. April 26, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant until late afternoon - then a heavy shower came up \+ again fell at intervals until late at night. I spent the m'g at studio fussing over the pf. taken Saturday - working with crayon on Hoppincatt head \+ portrait of Mr. Forster lunch — afternoon, at the dirty work of cleaning out my acid Room where the dust has been collecting all winter. My new hot-water urn, from Millers, was delivered - a very desirable convenience when "biting". Home after 5 \+ had a rest before dinner - Ev'g. went to Council meeting - no business of interest or importance. Home \+ to bed at 11.

At Council, all members rec'd. tickets to Grant's Fund from Mayor Strong thro' Homer Lee.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 27, 1897

*Dedication of Grant's Tomb.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw \+ cold winds from N.W. with many clouds. Immediately after b'kf't. I went to grocers \+ butcher then the family went to Charles Rodin, cor. Madison Ave. \+ 40th St. where we had a very fine view down the Ave. About 80 the President \+ escort went by on their way up to the tomb - It was about 11 when the head of the military Div. came \+ it was continuous - without break - marching past until about 3 - Fortunately for us the wind blew strong from the west, \+ we at the west side of the street - we did not suffer as our opposite neighbors did - They could not keep their windows open or they w'd. be almost blown away. At times, when the sun shone out the spectacle was very fine but after 2 or 3 hours of the show we began to be conscious that in spite of some variations there was an underlying sameness that came very near being tiresome especially after lunch hour had passed \+ we began to feel hungry. At 1.30 I went home - found Gretta in possession of the house, Katie \+ Kitty having gone to see the parade. I had a cup of tea \+ something to eat - Gretta made up a lot of sandwiches with them \+ Des. to Charls - At about 3.30 after the military had passed I left \+ went to 5th Ave. The crush of travel, both ways on the elevated R.R. was immense — moving very slowly - after a long time I got to 125th St. \+ walked west to Grant's tomb and got there before the last of the division that I had already seen had arrived - I wandered about looking at the crowd - the wind was dirt laden, cold \+ pitiless - it had swept most of the seats on the stands bare - consequently there was much to mar the spectacle - I went to my stand and for awhile watched the Civic Division that followed the military - having found the military monotonous the civic was positively dull - so, at 5 o'clk when the Pres. left the Reviewing Stand to go to the River to review the Naval Parade of U.S. \+ foreign war vessels, I left - the procession keeping on \+ on, nearly 6 m'les in line - until nearly 7 o'clk. I had a long tedious journey getting home but I found the study of the crowd interesting - it was nearly 7 when I got home - Charl dined with us \+ then went to the Reception at the Union League Club where had some committeeman duties - we were all tired - had a quiet ev'g \+ to bed early.
