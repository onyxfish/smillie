## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 8, 1897

**Weather:** Disagreeable, wet day  **Temp:** [illegible]

most of the morning given to Spencer or. Coon — arranging a set of ten or a dozen prints for him to have re-produced in half tone for the Metropolitan mag. \+ giving him material for his text.
I mounted the Replica of pen sketch of the Hotel of the Capucins at Amalfi, that I am making for the lady who bought the original, before I went home to lunch. After lunch went to NA \+ got thro' with my work there, — Then, on Jay's invitation, went up \+ took a look thro' the exhibition, now very nearly hung. Then to studio \+ cut a mount for the sketch that I mounted in the mtf. Home \+ had a rest before dinner. Evening Council meeting. Nothing of especial interest — at 10 o'clk. Council went to the galleries \+ accepted the hanging from the Com. Home at 11. Gel walked up with me — Beckwith part of the way.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 9, 1897

**Weather:** A nasty, wet day, clearing late in the afternoon with cold, high winds  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30. Finished the mount on pen sketch of Capucin Hotel at Amalfi \+ got it in press. Got out three "blind" frames, all of which have to be re-covered with tissue paper. This took the rest of the mtf \+ part of the afternoon. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back at 2.30. After finishing "blinds" I went on with some of the hopeless, endless work of arranging my many little things of little value in a sort of order. Home at 6 after a short walk. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'f, continuing the work of the day I tackled the disorder of my closet at home \+ worked at that, making but little progress, until 11 o'clk.
