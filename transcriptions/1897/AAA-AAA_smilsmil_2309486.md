## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 15, 1897

**Weather:** Clondy, raw day. Rain at night.  **Temp:**

I got up, after an uncomfortable night with a severe headache which made me almost useless during the day. I wrote some notes after bkfs. \+ got to studio at about 11 \- Soon after I walked up to 57th S. \+ 6th Ave. \+ saw Beckwith concerning Site Com. matters. He introduced me to his wife \- then was walked down 5th Ave. together, parting at 45th S. I got home to lunch at 1 \- to studio at 2 \+ spent the remainder of the afternoon in my big chair suffering very much with my head. Home at 6.30 \- ate a little dinner \+ at 8.15 got to Council meeting feeling very shabby \-
After Council meeting \- Site Com. had a meeting \- The surprise of the even was a note from Jas. Hart stating that after having several attacks of vertigo, by the advice of his physician, who warned him against all excitements he resigned the chairmanship of the com. \+ suggested that T.W. Wood be elected in his place. The resignation was accepted \- complimentary resolutions were unanimously passed \+ T.W. Wood was elected chairman \- Then the next step to be taken by the com. was discussed \+ the "scope of the wood plan" \- titled "Site \+ plan" was talked of. This merged into a discussion as to architectural plans \+ architects' competitions \- resulting in a resolution to refer the matter to the Arts' federation Socy. requesting the architects' section to formulate some plan of competition acceptable to them that might be adopted by the N.A. Then home thro' a shower, stopping at studio to fix fire.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 16, 1897

**Weather:** Bright \+ pleasant.  **Temp:**

Walter Cook called at the studio soon after I got there \+ spent half an hour with me. I got to work on Vesuvius \+ repainted, for the third time, the Volcano \+ parts of the distant mts. The general tone of color is getting away from me \- it is a sort of Robin's egg blue \- \+ that is not what I want. Home at 1 \+ found that Ralph had just staggered in having been brought home from school by the mistress of the dept. He had been knocked over \+ temporarily unconscious, during recess, in Bryant Park \- He was unconscious for a time \+ then sick \- Miss Miller could have sent him home in a cab \- instead, he was put in charge of the woman \+ struggled home, half blind \+ semi-conscious. He could not speak coherently, \+ was in a cold \+ clammy sweat \- he was at once put to bed \+ hot water bag applied \+ Yale was sent for. He came at 2.30 when R. was beginning to recover. He assured us, saying that there was no evidence of serious injury \- Miss Miller came a little later. I stayed with R. until he fell into a quiet sleep \- then I went to barbers \+ had my hair cut. Then to studio \+ in moving a big chasse I knocked Vesuvius off its big frame, tearing up \- another case \- his frame has just been put in order for use \- at a cost of $22. The fall smashed it so that it will have to be sent again to the gilders \+ the cost of repairs will be greater than before. Having no work suited to the occasion I was mute. Then home \+ lay down beside R. until dinner. Eve'g. Mary \+ Kate went to Mendelssohn Club concert. Charles' ticket \- Charl. came in for a few minutes at 9 \+ 10 o'clk. bringing the new Reorganization books of Northern Pacific. I spent some time over my accts \- R. sleeping very quietly \- \+ got to bed at midnight.
