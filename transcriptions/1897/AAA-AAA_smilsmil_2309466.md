## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 4, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very disagreeable day, dark, foggy \+ muggy \+, at night, rain \- I put in a full day of work on my Water Color "Near San Remo". I don't know yet whether I am doing something tolerably good or hopelessly bad \- Ev'g. I went to Council meeting \- at 9 the session of the Site com. commenced, the three members in addition to the council entering \- It was very evident that all were conscious that we were on the eve of important happenings \- all were very many \+ expectant \- At last the consideration of 116' S'. was reached \- Particulars as to possibility of purchase \+ price were asked I answered that such information was to be had of Pres'. Low, but I could not ask for it unless empowered by the Com. \+ I did not think that the com. should request it unless it intended seriously to consider the site \- There must be a reasonable possibility of its being accepted \- There was much sparring before the vote as it w'. be a test \- at last it came \- to make me a com. of one to call on Pres'. Low \- lo ago \- it \+ [illegible] want in the chair \- He could have voted \+ made it a tie \+ do, the motion w'. have been lost, but he refused to do so \-\- so, I am to wait upon Pres'. Low \- There was much surprise among the down-town men as they had counted upon Church voting with them \-
\- I walked home alone, part of the way it rained heavily \- after midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wet m'g. gradually clearing \- After b'f. I wrote notes to Nicoll, Church \+ Beckwith \- also to Pres'. Low. Then to Studio \- Miller, the carpenter, waiting for me \- He kept me for about half an hour \- before I got fairly to work Dramy Watrous came in \+ he kept me for an hour talking over Site com. differences. I find him more firmly set in his views than I had thought \- At last I got to work on my water color \+ soon became so absorbed that I forgot all about my lunch until 1.30 \- then home \- back soon after 2 \+ worked as long as I could see. Then walked up 5th Ave. to 59'. S'. \+ home to dinner. Ev'g. Read to the boys \- Clare came in \- to bed at 11.
