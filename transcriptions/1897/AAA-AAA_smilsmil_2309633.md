## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 8, 1897

**Weather:** Disagreeable, dark day \- Raw, without being wet.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nearly used up by a head ache, especially so later in the day. I got to studio at about 10 o'clk \+ spent the m'g cutting mounts for some of the etchings \+ few sketches recently made \- not a profitable employment of time, but the best that I was fit for. Home to lunch shortly after 1 \- after lunch I went to bed for a couple of hours and had a little fitful sleep. At 4.30 went to N.A. \+ gathered the material for my report to the meeting in the evening \- but could not write out the report owing to interruptions. So I went home at 6 \- had a light dinner \+ then wrote my report getting it finished at 8.30 Then to meeting \- as it was waiting I read my report at once but after that I had to go out for a while to get relief from the tobacco smoke \- when I re-entered I found that a Building com. had been app'd but the Delman Resolutions \+ instruction had been overlooked \- This I corrected at once \- so that the order is now given by the body of the A. \- to go on with the building, limited by funds on hand \- to begin with the schools \- full power being given to the building com. \- F. Delman \- Jst. D. Swillie Rob't Blum, T.C. Jones ofw. [illegible] That being accomplished I went home soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 9, 1897

**Weather:** Foggy most of the day, clearing mild and pleasant in the afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 \- worked all the m'g on my etching pp's \+ mounts \+ preliminary work for Water Color painting. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- back to studio and worked until about 4 \- then I went down to the Gen'l Post Office to see if I could get the address of J.B. \+ Gardner \- it was refused under a rule. Then I went to Homer Lee's office in the Tribune Bldg \- \+ saw him \- Learned nothing of gardner \- we had a long conversation concerning the Farmington case \- Resulting in his calling up McLaughlin \- F's lawyer \- on the telephone \+ making advances on his part in the character of a self-appointed mediator out, F. \+ the Council of the A. \- I stood close to Lee in the little telephone closet \+ heard much of his conversation \- He seemed to be very ready to accept Lee's advances \+ dwell upon his great sympathy for F's family, but with none for F. who, had he been alone, w'd have been "cut up" long ago \- He said that he'd send down a proposition in writing on Sat. \+ told Lee to go to 1st Dist. Att'y. Ass't \- ap. Judge Mitchell, who was a good friend of his, to fix it up with him. The conversation convinced me that there is a good understanding between the prosecuting officer \+ the criminal's lawyer. Home soon after. Alice Ireland \+ Mollie Adams, to dine with us. Kate arr. from Pok this p.m. Ev'g Charle came \+ took all hands to his room to hear his new organ. I stayed with the boys until 9 \- then Fred J \- came \+ I went with him to Charle's \- go. there \- Home after 11 o'clk.
