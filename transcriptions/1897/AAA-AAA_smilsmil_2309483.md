## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 9, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine winter day, bright + crisp. After getting thro' my m'f duties I rode down to Chambers S: + B'dway to Tax assessors office to get release from the assessment upon $5000 personal property. I failed I think because I did not make it plain that 110 E, 38 S. was mine as Residuary legatee The assessor said that it must stand in my name — I shall try him again to-morrow + I went at once to see + see + shall try him (the assessor) again to-morrow. Then to Central Trust Co. + with Pres't Hyde went to our Treas with documents prepared in Council last night. The official there was not satisfied with our Constitution which is not explicit enough for him — The papers will have to be referred to Washington — Then I went to Geo. Reads — saw him + Van Felt + had a satisfactory chat with them — Then home to lunch at 1.20 — to Studio at 2.30. There for a little while — saw Maynard, who is improving — Then to 10 E S. Studio Bldg. to see Nicoll — out of town — Saw Brown + Wood then, being very tired I went home to get a rest. Dinner at 6.30 — Read to the boys until 8.30 — Before I went to Ed'r Cooks. Mrs Sprague, Miss Williams. Mary Willard + Mrs. Valentine there. I left at 10 — went to Studio — a short call on Maynard — then fixed furnace fire + home.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 10, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katie + Bessie Ret'd from Phila.

Overcast, with some sunlight. After my usual m'f duties + having gone to the Studio to see that everything there was right I went to Tax Office. There I saw the deputy under whom I passed yesterday — case re-opened + I so explained my ownership of 110 E, 38 S. + 156 E, 36 S. + the net from thereon that I was relieved of personal Tax for next year. To Studio at about 11 — set Palette + commenced Repainting "Souvenir" — painting out + more distant mts. To lunch — Painted awhile after my return but feeling played out at about 3.30 I gave up + had an uneasy nap in my big chair. Yale came in — Maynard, while well enough to be up about, is not able to go out so I sent what dinner around to him — Supr. Read to boys until near 9. After that I wrote some notes.
