## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 5, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day - after attending to Regular my duties I went down-Town - to Charl's first then to Lee's - explained to him that I was ready to make appointments for the preliminaries to purchase of Cathedral Site for A. \+ D. We went to Cammann's office, 57 Liberty - neither he nor Morgan was in - engaged to return at 1 - again to Charl's \+ with one of his young men went to J.P. Morgan's to have Nor. Pac. Receipts exchanged for the new bonds, etc - too late for this day - then I went to Café Savarin \+ had a light lunch - then again to Lee's \+ with him to Cammann's office - C. \+ M. in - a satisfactory talk with them - then appointment made for to-morrow at 10.30 when contracts are to be signed - left Lee's again to Charl's - then to 10th St studios - Saw Wood \+ Brown - then to my studio - Saw Maynard \+ home at 5 \+ had a rest before dinner - Soc'y. Read to the boys until 9 - then to Edw'd - Coaks for a few minutes - Charl came to the House for a little while -

After this - I went to Walter Cook's \+ had a frank talk with him about Academy matters \+ the proper way to work to get the best Architect aid in drawing plans.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 6, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Signed contract for N.A. \+ D. to purchase Site on 110th St.

A very disagreeable day - high wind \+ Rain - Frank Adams came in before 9 o'clk - just from New Haven by boat - I left him with Mary and after going to studio - grocers \+ butchers I went to Lincoln Safe Deposit in 42d St.- met Wood - opened N.A. safe - took out $14,000 in govt. bonds \+ then we went to Lee \+ Lee's in Nassau St. - W.T.L. \+ Lee joined us \+ we went to the brokers - Cammann - 57 Liberty where we met J.G. Brown - also Mr. P.D. Cummings who owns the larger part of the 110th St. Site. After awhile I went to Central Trust in Wall St. with bonds to get them turned into certified checks - one for $5,000 - the other for $10,000 - had to wait for some time for Mr. Hyde the Pres. The trouble being that the bonds being Registered in the name of the N.A. they are not negotiable until a number of formalities of transfer have been gone thro' with. Mr. Hyde went to Ass. Treas. \+ with me \+ we got blank forms - then I ret'd to Cammann's \+ got Wood \+ Brown \+ we went to Central Trust - where we executed a joint note to the Trust - on return were C. with a deposit of $15,000 - ngs: which I drew two checks which were cert'd. Then we ret'd to Cammann's \+ completed the signing of the preliminary contract. It was near 5 o'clk when we got thro'. Then we separated - I went to Charl's in Wall St. - wrote a note to Cook \+ the Tribune - then home \+ had lunch at about 2 o'clk - Farrington came - after him reporter from the Times came - then I had a chance to get a rest - Dinner at 6.30 - very I hoped to get to Century, monthly meeting - but, the weather was too bad - Charl had given his Philharmonic tickets to Mary \+ Kate \+ they went to Carnegie Hall - Reporter from Herald came in later.
