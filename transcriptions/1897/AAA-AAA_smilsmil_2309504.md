## Left Page

### Tues. March 23, 1897

**Weather:** Bright m'f., clouded afternoon, wet night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Saw Walter after 8.15 - had a half hour talk with him. Things in the hospital are slightly improved to-day. It was after 10 when I got to studio. Found a letter from J.R. Koehler in answer to mine of Sat. last. I sat right down + wrote about Kelsey - at 11.30 went to barber's + had hair cut + head shampooed. It was about 12.30 when I got home. Yale came in - then lunch - went to studio - part of p.m. spent in cleaning up - a telegram came from Maynard shortly after 4 - later I took \+ to Century Club \+ found him in the midst of great rejoicing. He has just been awarded the Shaw Prize - Socy. of Am. Painters - $1500 - a godsend to him as he told me that he had reached the end of the dock. Home to dinner at 6.30 - was read until 8.30 to Ralph - Chare came in for a short time - I wrote notes to Beckwith \+ T.W. Wood saying that I did not approve of the acceptance of Mr. Watson's invitation to Sel. Com. to dine with him at Union League on Sat. Ap. 3. I went to Studio at 10.30 to attend to matters there, then home \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 24, 1897

**Weather:** Wet, disagreeable m'f. — gusty, showery, clearing cold afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Hospital affairs at home progressing as favorably as could be expected - After 10 when I got to studio - wrote to Lee \+ Lee on N.A. business - to Miley Cook, etc. - much of the m'f taken by visits from Dr. George \+ Maynard. After lunch, again to studio - wrote to Barmore making a proposition that differs from his looking to the payment of his indebtedness to me - a short walk before dinner - then reading to Ralph until near 9.
