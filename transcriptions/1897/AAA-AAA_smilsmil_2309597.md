## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 27, 1897

**Weather:** Strong, breezy & cold, brilliantly clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

At 2 o'clk. A.M. I was awakened by spasms of sickness that made the perspiration pour out of me. That passing, the bowels acted an avalanche \+ in a short time I got Relief — felt rather slim when I got up but strength came gradually. During the m'f. I printed six p'f's of Etching "The Road to the Lake." Mrs. Schaper came \+ saw the p'f's that I had prepared for her \+ expressed herself greatly pleased with them. Dinner. After that all hands were out to the pasture with the box kites. Two of them behaved badly in the strong, gusty wind and eventually were disabled. I was there until 4.30. While flying the kites Will Mulford (Elisha's) came to us \+ took Mollie \+ Katherine off for a drive. With Ralph went to P.O. at 5.30. After tea read aloud to R—

Many continued to improve through the day.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 28, 1897

**Weather:** Glorious day, brilliant \& breezy  **Temp:** Ther. went down to 30° last night.

After getting to my studio work much of the m'f. was spent in preparing the mounts for two of the Etching p'f's that I took yesterday. I wrote some checks \+ part of a letter to Mr. Bradley before dinner. After dinner I set up Tennis net that Mollie \+ Bessie might play. Finished my N.A. writing — wrote to Geo. Cook \+ before 5 went to P.O. Then attended to some out of door work. After tea a quiet ev'f. with the usual reading to Ralph before he went to bed — \+ after that, Harper for Oct. To bed early.

Mary's Temp. normal in the m'f. — a little over 100° at night.
