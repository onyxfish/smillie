## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 18, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant, altho' much of the day was cloudy \+ quite cool. Up at 6.30 - Got Frixy harnessed just before) \+ with Ralph drove down to Cooley \+ got a piece of gal. sheet iron \+ some clinching nails - back to the house \+ mended Jim's broken trunk just in time for [illegible]. men - then I got some breakfast \+ we started for R.R. Station train off at 8.10 - the boys rode with Engineer [illegible] on the engine - N.Y. Train on time - 8.60 - Jim got away keeping a stiff upper lip. He is to go direct to Putnam's, there in 23? S: \+ with him to Rye to spend the night \+ to-morrows start for Canada \+ the woods. Mrs. Hamlin, daughter \+ a friend went on the train. Back to Montrose \+ 9.30 Ralph again on the engine - went down town - called on Mr. Bus. Mulford \+ [illegible] missed to send her some young lettuce plants. Home at 11 \+ spent the rest of the [illegible] on N.A. of D. correspondence. After dinner wrote to Jas. Hart and did something in the way of clearing up the etching room, preparatory to taking working [illegible] from Genl. Green - Went to P.O. before 6 - Again at 8 after tea. An open air band concert in front of Tarbell House. Read to Ralph until after 9. I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 19, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - at times very breezy. Thunder storms passed to south of us, giving us a spatter at about 4 P.M. - others passed north in the evst. with some rain before 10 o'clk. I cleared out the etching room - cleaned up printing table \+ press \+ made ready to do some printing to-morrow. Looked over my stock of paper \+ transferred it from the studio closet to the drawers of the printing table - wet down a few sheets. I went down to P.O. at about 10.30 a.m. Got an interesting letter from [illegible] Kingston giving me information concerning Farrington \+ his com [illegible]. I may be called to N.Y. in a fortnight if the Dist. Atty orders his trial - also a letter from Bishop Putnam giving brief itinerary of proposed camping trip. Miss Harris left us on the 6.05 Train bound for Binghamton, her home. a quiet, rather reserved but self-possessed girl. Tall, fair \+ good looking. At 8 Ralph \+ I went to P.O. hoping to get a word from Jim, but nothing came.
