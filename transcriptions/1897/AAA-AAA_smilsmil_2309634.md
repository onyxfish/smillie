## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 10, 1897

**Weather:** Muggy \+ foggy \- part of the day very dark  **Temp:** [illegible]

evg., much clearer. To studio at usual time \- I tried to work but my head was too dull \+ heavy \- so much of the m'g was spent pasting mounts on etchings \+ pen sketches \+ preparing a panel for Water Col. St. Peters \+ the almond tree. I soon "lost light" in the afternoon \+ went over to R.G. Newlands in W. 29th St. with the little bronze candlestick, that Allie Diven gave one to have it repaired. Then to NA where I spent some time with Grannis trying to unravel the mysterious knot in the back acct., but we could not finish it before dinner time. Home to dinner at 6. Evg. I took the boys to "The Dev" of Agassiz Chapter no. at Mullers 144 E. 40th St. — thinking that they might be interested in a promised talk on Snap-Shots \- It was a regular monthly meeting but very slow \+ dull \- The snap shot did not go off \+ a few very poor photographs were shown \- So we excused ourselves at 9.30 \+ got home \+ to bed \-

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 11, 1897

**Weather:** Mild, muggy \+ foggy \- not yet quite in work-  **Temp:** [illegible]

ing trim but feeling better than yesterday \- To studio at 9.30 \- Spent some time in hunting thro' my NA \+ D. Constitutions to make up a history of Set. 11. for Calverly. Then some writing \- \+ then, after an attempt to work on charcoal sketch of St. Peters \+ the flowering almond tree. I fixed up my printing Table \+ its drawers \+ then got out some old W.C. \+ studies \- after lunch I worked over some of them. Miss Abbatt called. At dusk I took a short walk \- then home \+ had a quiet hour before dinner \- a quiet evg. followed \- Reading to Ralph \+ writing some business letters. I did not feel well enough to go to the Alpha Delta Phi reception to which I had cards.
