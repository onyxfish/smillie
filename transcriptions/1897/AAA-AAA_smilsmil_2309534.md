## Left Page

### Mon. May 24, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Besina Ret. from New-Haven.

Cert m.f. Before 9 went to Mr. W. Lee's house in 36 S. to see him but he had gone. After attending to my m.f. duties \+ going to studio for a few min. I went to N.A. \+ spent the rest of the m.f. There I expected Farrington, but he did not come. I found work enough for me in arranging a financial plan to carry the N.A. through to Nov. next without borrowing. Home to dinner at 1.30 - I went again to the N.A. at 5 \+ was there until near 6 - Gardner there, for the first time, gave the exact total of Farrington's stealings, amounting to the startling sum of $4269.17. I went home \+ tried to get a rest before tea time - Besina arrived from New-Haven bringing news of an alarming seizure that had suddenly come to Nelia. The day, on night, before. I went to N.A. at 8 - The session was almost spirited one. The depredations of Farrington were first considered - I gave the figures submitted by Gardner to me in the afternoon \+ they made a sensation - I was examined very closely on many points \+ explained as well as I was able - The next matter of importance was the intention of the competing architects to have the commissions of 5/10 distinctly stated in the programme. This was hotly discussed - T.W. making a splendid exposure of his immense littleness \+ incapacity to comprehend any idea of any importance - Instructions were given to Backwith who is conducting the negotiations. Then came a surprise to me - Jas. Hart presented a proposition from Homer Lee \+ other friends of Farrington, much such as F. had made to Gardner last Friday - it was practically to accept a life ins. pro. \+ give him in return a coat of white wash \+ let me influence the matter drop. I characterized it as monstrous - that such a proposition should be seriously considered while I was looking \+ waiting for F. Today, H. Homer Lee \+ Jas. Hart \+ T.W. Wood were conferring over this plan - Dielman took the matter up boldly \+ insisted that there was but one way out \+ that was to commence criminal proceedings ag. him promptly. I can see no other way; in view of his persistent lying that will put me right before the N.A. Backwith also favored such action \+ it was generally approved \+ was referred to the House Com. Jas. Hart \+ his [illegible] sorry for F.'s family, but would not oppose. After 12 when we adjourned. Ralph, who has been my room mate \+ bed fellow took possession of his little attic bed Room to night.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 25, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Doubtful m.f. clouded \+ wet looking - After my late hours \+ disturbed sleep last night I got up feeling so seedy that I could not go to Peekskill to spend the day at the Beaches as I had promised to. So, I sent a note to Ella B. Then I went to studio for a moment \+ to N.A. There until 11 - then to T.W. Wood's to get his signature to check - then to 41 W. 10. \+ saw Dielman - had a very interesting half hour with him - we agree that there is but one safe path ahead for us - that is to begin criminal proceedings ag. Farrington. If I had suffered, as an individual, by his dishonesty I would, for the sake of his family, have refused to have him imprisoned \+ punished in such way - but I represent the N.A. \+ am accountable to its members - I cannot clean my self before them of charges of neglect or incompatancy except by such action. I am driven to it agt. my will. To 2d Nat. Bk. - then to N.A. \+ home to dinner at 1.30. Wrote for awhile after that. Then, feeling very heavy, I had a good nap - awake at 4.30. [illegible]! Brainerd Smillie called \+ as I was the only one in I saw her - at 5.30 I rode my wheel to Spalding's - saw Belts \+ ordered a spring saddle post. Then rode down to N.A. to see what had been accomplished — then home at about 6.30 - Nea - very. Read aloud a little piece by [illegible] Bangs in Jane Harker, aloud - Then I had writing that engaged me until I went to bed.
