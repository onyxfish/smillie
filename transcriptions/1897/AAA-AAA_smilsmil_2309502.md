## Left Page

### Fri. March 19, 1897

**Weather:** mild. very dark, wet \- rain at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 6 with my headache as usual. now I was late in getting to breakfast \+ later in getting to the studio — too dark to see to work. I spent the day in writing letters \- most time on one to North concerning the New O. W. drawing paper answering his request that I should advise him as to the best way to introduce it into his market — I also wrote to Abbey on the same subject.
Every I spent with Ralph, reading to him \- if the weather had been bright he was to have had a scrubbing to-day. He seems to be getting along very nicely.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 20, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day. mild, moist \+ dark. a day of developments. Katie T. developed what appears to be measles early in the morning \- Before noon Grettie informed Mary that she is to be married on Easter Sunday \- In the afternoon she took to her bed with measly symptoms. I did not feel very well myself \- I got to studio about 10 \- mounted "View near San Remo" W.C. on a solid backer \+ wrote part of a letter to S. R. Koehler \- Home to lunch \+ back at 2. Chas. Barmore came in answer to a note from me \+ was very submissive \- I told him that I intended to finish the mezzotint plate portrait of Genl. Greene \+ would be willing to see [illegible] if Thos. Hine until his indebtedness to me ($650) was paid — then I would deliver the plate to him \- He was much pleased with the proposition \- Thanked me for my liberality \+ made a definite offer that I told him to put into writing which he promised to do \- Finished my letter to Koehler \- a long one. Then, as my head troubled me I rested before I went home to dinner \- Every I had intended to go to Century, but could not find it in my heart to, \+ leave Ralph \- So, I spent the evening with him \- \+ when he went to bed it was too late for me to go to Century.
