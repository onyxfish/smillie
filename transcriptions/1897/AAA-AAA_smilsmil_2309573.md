## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 10, 1897

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'f., Threatening a wet day. At about 10 Rain began to fall, increasing to heavy showers with Thunder. Rain ceased before dinner time. more or less cloudy during the rest of the day. The m'f was so dark that I could not see to work on my plate but I spent some time in getting my scrapers ground \+ in order. Before the rain I set out some lettuce. Afternoon I worked for some time scraping on the background of Gen't. Green portrait. This is the first work with the scraper that I have done on this plate since I abandoned it last summer. At 8 o'clk went with the boys to P.O. Even'f. with newspaper.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 11, 1897

**Weather:** Clouded  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded day but pleasant — a short, smart shower in the m'f. before noon. I got to work regularly on Gen'l. Green, scraping background. About noon I went down town with Jim. Took him to Dr. Gardner, as he has been complaining of his stomach for two or three days. Shopped with him for shoes, etc., for his camp outfit \+ was late to dinner. Afternoon, worked on Green until 5 o'clk. Then took my bicycle up to Fair ground — did 6 miles \+ home to tea. With the boys went to P.O. \+ other places \+ completed Jim's shopping. After our return I read to Ralph until nearly 10 o'clk.
