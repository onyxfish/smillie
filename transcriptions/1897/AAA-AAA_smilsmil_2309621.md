## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 14, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f. gradually overcast in the  **Temp:** [illegible]

afternoon — light rain at night. Had my Sunday m'f scrod early — \+ back to bed awhile before I got up. Bkfs't — at 9 — newspaper — then to studio for a few minutes — then with the boys in Central Park until 2 — Dinner — Geo. \+ Emily Cook came in after that — Later I had a rest — tea at 6.30 out \+ I went to 331 E. 17th St. \+ spent the ev'g with Emma Ireland — Mary \+ Grace left at 10.30 —

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 15, 1897

**Weather:** A nasty day — Rain fell freely most of  **Temp:** [illegible]

the day out was quite dark. I got to studio at 9.30 having planned to paint upon the enlarged copy after the Fraunbule miniature of Genl Nath'l Greene. but it was so dark that I gave it up and practically spent the day planning and ruling pages for Record Books for the N.A.D. home to lunch — Had a rest before dinner — Self. N.A. Cornceil Meeting. The jury on Architects' competition reported in favor of Carrere \+ Hastings' plan — This was not unexpected to me altho' for the past two or three days I have had a thought, based upon vague rumors, that there was a possibility of Babb, Cook \+ Willard getting it. After the meeting the Cornceil went into the Library \+ saw the designs. There were but two sets of the six worthy serious consideration — Carrere \+ Hastings \+ Babb, Cook \+ Willards in some respects I liked Walter Cook's plan better than I did Carrere's — It was midnight when I got to bed.
