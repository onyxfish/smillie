## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 22, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f., cloudy P.M. — snow & rain after 4 o'clk.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To work at 10. Spent some time on "Vineyards etc" then put it away, finished for the present — Then I got out the other drawing "A Path, or Road, to Ravello" and washed off nearly all of the sky & the road — after lunch & continued work on it until 4. In the M'f working. Felica called & selected two paintings for an Exposition to take place there soon — At 4.30 the conspirators came & at about 5 Messrs Hastings & Carrière and with sketches of plans for New Academy on 110' & of pos'n the Cathedrals. The 7 up-town members of N.A. Ste. Com. all being present, then gathered about the table & the plans were spread before them & explained by Messrs H. & C — It was an extremely interesting occasion & I believe, will become historical, for I cannot believe that the scheme as developed upon our side of the Com. & the grand plans as sketched out by Hastings and Carrière can be thrown out by the Academy when presented as it will be on Wed. 26th St. The plans are frankly founded upon the best European models & to me are inspiring to a high degree. To Carroll Beckwith belongs a great share of the credit. — His clear head has been at the service of the architects & his practical knowledge of the actual needs of the Academy has greatly helped them. The discussion was spirited until after 6 o'clk.

Ev'g. I write to Apdalessione — at 9 went to studio — looked over my work & some old frames — fixed fire etc — then home & to bed.

To-day got notices from the Marshal to go to Tax office & pay $338.78 Personal tax arrears, Estate of Anna C. Smillie.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 23, 1897

**Weather:** Pleasant altho' cloudy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Worked on "Path to Ravello" during the day but was balked by the tree group in the centre which I have washed out & painted in several times already.

At the dinner table, at 6.30, I began to feel very uncomfortable, nausea, so that at last I was obliged to beat a retreat & went to my bath room — These my discomfort increased until I realized that I was going thro' an experience the same as one that I had while Burne & I were visiting at Muingers in Saratoga about 14 yr. ago. I seated myself on the W.C. — I was bathed in a cold sweat — no pains — but deathly sickness — with swimming head & ringing ears blackness & unconsciousness came upon me & I fell over. — How long this state continued I have no means of knowing — when I recovered from it there was a prompt & complete evacuation of the bowels & in about a quarter of an hour I was able to crawl into my bed.
