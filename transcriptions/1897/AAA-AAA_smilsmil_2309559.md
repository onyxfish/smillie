## Left Page

### Tues. July 13, 1897

**Weather:** Clouded m'f. bright afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up with a headache to which I was obliged to make complete surrender immediately after dinner. I went down town before & attended to various little matters & mailed two or three notes that I had written. my headache during the afternoon was very severe. I was able to get up at 9 p.m., had a cup of tea \+ to bed again at 11.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 14, 1897

**Weather:** Cloudy \+ close. clearing.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thunder all about. Light showers at 12 \+ later \- Threatenings at sundown, but nothing followed. Spent some time writing letters \- most of them on N. A. business. Wrote to Mr. Barhydt at Poland Sp. Maine, concerning Etching Class prizes \- also to Jas. M. Hart, sending him a number of checks for his signature. I saddled Tiny \+ took her to Barney's to have a harness fitted to her, but a shower came up that prevented \- I left her at Birego's \+ went home \- wrote letters after dinner \+ again went to Barney's with the Pony, taking the cart here \+ waited until after 6 before the harness was finally fitted, then I drove home in style. Ev'f, Mary \+ the girls went to the Presb. Ch. to hear an Armenian lecture. They did not get home until near 10. Ralph wished to set off his fireworks welcoming Jim home \+ he wished many \+ the girls to be present \- consequently they waited up very late. The fireworks were eventually set off making a very satisfactory display, after which we all went to bed.
