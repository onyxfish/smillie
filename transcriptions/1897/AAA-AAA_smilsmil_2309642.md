## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 26, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow storm that continued until about 3 o'clk. 3 or 4 in. of light snow falling. I got up with a headache that hung on all day making me very miserable. Bkfst. at 10 \- at noon went to studio for a few minutes \- Dinner at 2 \- the rest of the afternoon on my bed. Tea at 6.30. At 9 o'clk went to Century. Had a pleasant time in a circle with Eastman Johnston, Edwin d. Weeks, Nicole \& Maynard, talking about mountain climbing. I had a talk with de Farge about the Paris fine Arts Com'n \+ the federation meeting, which resulted in my writing two notes, one to J. Q. A. Ward, the other of Ed. H. Bell. I got home at 12 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 27, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. To studio for a few minutes \+ then down town \- called first at Lee \+ Lee's \+ saw Kingsbury \+ talked with him about the Alex Brown jmt. ntf. \+ of the Farrington case, Then to Audit Co. in Equitable Bldg \+ made an engagement for 4.30 to-morrow, when some one from the Co. will meet me at the N.A. of D. to look over the books. Then to Homer Lee's, not in \- then to Tax office \+ for some time was engaged in getting a correct bill of taxes of N.A. of D. on lots on 10th St: then home at 1 \- lunch \- \+ am so out of sorts, with headache \+ slight fever that I sent for Dale who came later \+ prescribed for a liver \+ stomach that had Rebelled ago' \- a little too much Xmas \- Carpenter at studio for 2½ hours putting up doors that would n't catch, in Maynard's, \+ my rooms. Home at 5 \- at 6 Emma J \- came to ask if I w'd object to her taking the boys to a play on W'sd. P.M. Dinner at 6.30 \- W. C'l. Council \- an interesting discussion over the Farrington case which has now reached a crisis \- a bundle of documents were Rec'd from Homer Lee which were Read \- \+ I had a subpoena to appear to-morrow m'g at Court as wetness in the Farrington case. The Lee papers explain that this will be an opportunity to drop the case gracefully \- By Resolution offered by Beckwith I was emp. powered to agree to a discharge of J \- upon his own Recognizance \- 11.30 when I got home. After 12 when I got to bed \+ about 3 o'clk before I got to sleep. I'm afraid that I am getting too old to keep such continuous late hours \+ carry responsibilities so many that they worry me.
