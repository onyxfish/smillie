## Left Page

### Wed. May 26, 1897

**Weather:** Brilliant, cool day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Com. authorizes me to prosecute C.S.F.

Brilliant, cool day. Immediately after bkft. I went to Mr. Lee in 36'5. \+ told him that I had decided to prosecute Farrington in Criminal Court — He said that he would arrange with the Dist. Attorney for a meeting to-morrow \+ told me what sort of a statement to have prepared. Then attended to my duties — to Studio, \+ to N.A. at 10.30. Then I was engaged until 1.30 when I went home to dinner — It was after 3 when I got back to N.A. Gardiner's statement was put in hands of printer. After 4, T.W. Wood, Jas. Hart \+ J.C. Beckwith came. A Special Com. to deal with Farrington's case. A rather proposition from Homer Lee, Through Jas. Hart, was presented — A Farrington Trust to be formed — a mysterious, unknown man to be bonded to put up 1%. Premium on a life ins. policy on F. in favor of N.A. of $10,000 — The principal of the theft $44,269.17 with 6% to go to N.A. upon the death of F. — The bal. if any, to go to his family. I expressed myself freely \+ said that I hoped to have their cooperation, but I was decided \+ determined that if the N.A. officers did not back me in a criminal prosecution I would exercise my right as a N.A. \+ make the necessary formal complaint on my personal responsibility. — No objection was made. Jas. Hart disclaimed any interest in the Homer Lee proposition \+ Beckwith drew up an authorization for the Com. to me, to make complaint to the Dist. Atty. Mr. J. T. Willson came in \+ had a conference with the Com. — He desires to take F.'s place — later Dielman came in \+ we looked over the books \+ I explained the system of checks upon petty fillfering or mistakes \+ plan of redistricting — keeping the deeds etc. — after the others had gone, D. \+ I had a conversation concerning the contention of the Competing architects. I think that the N.A. is placing itself in an undignified position, when it haggles with brother professionals \+ tries to beat them down in price below a standard (5%) that is so generally accepted. Home to dinner at 6.30 — a quiet evening at home \+ to bed at 10.50

## Right Page

### Thur. May 27, 1897

**Weather:** A bright, cool day — cloudy for a portion of the afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, cool day — cloudy for a portion of the afternoon. Before 9 went over to 36'S. W. \+ saw Mr. Wm. Lee — anxious to begin proceedings \+ as promptly as possible. On my return to the house found that Mr. Kingsbury, from Lee's office, had been to the house — I went to studio — met Mills on the way \+ promised to him, for the Round Lake Summer prize \+ two Water-color paintings — then to N.A. — and finding that the printed statement was not ready \+ I could do nothing I went to Geo. Cook's office 43 Broadway \+ got him to go with me to a Notary \+ we, as executors of Annie's estate, signed a Release of int. on the Wall farm in Kansas. On my way home stopped at Bridgeman's \+ exchanged Sm. Pleyk seed for pansy seed — then home to dinner. Mr. Kingsbury called for me at 2.30 \+ together we went to Mr. A. Gardiner \+ Collins there — together we got evidence that we thought would be all that was necessary to secure a warrant \+ about 3 we went to the Court — at 2.09. S. We could not get a hearing until near 4 — the hour of closing — Magistrate Crane was very short with us, saying that we had not evidence of the right sort \+ that he would not grant a warrant — then K. \+ I went to E. 36'S — hoping to find Mr. Fowler whose check had been used by F., but he was out of town — Then we went to Norman W. Dodge's in Mad. Ave. bet. 36'+ 37'. S. \+ he was out of town — then we parted — I went home \+ got a rest before tea — Every road to Ralph will be 7.30 — at 9 Mr. Kingsbury again came \+ the work was laid out for to-morrow, hoping that we may then get such evidence as is required — Then wrote several notes of instruction to Montrose
