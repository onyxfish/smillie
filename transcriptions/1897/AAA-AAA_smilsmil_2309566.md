## Left Page

### Tues. July 27, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy Rain all day with cold, strong easterly wind \- More like a Sep. Than a July storm. I made a new model in cherry, very carefully for another mounting for the boys' butterflies \+ made a plaster of Paris cast. I finished my letter to Bishop Putnam \+ mailed it. I commenced work in earnest upon the few. green mezzotint plate cleaning the mezzotint ground off from the margin of it, a wearying \+ uninteresting piece of purely mechanical work which I would gladly pay to have it done for me if I knew any mechanic who could be trusted to do it. About 5 o'clks. I dressed in my Rubber suit \+ went to P.O. On my return took a stroll over the place to see how my crops \+ fruit-laden trees \+ gardens were getting along Thro' the storm \- After tea walked the piazza for nearly half an hour \- read newspaper \+ apt to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 28, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wind \+ Rain most of the day, clearing at about Sunset. I gave a good part of the m'f. to an attempt to discover the trouble with the oil burner of the Hot air Engine. The whole fire box is choked with soot or lamp-black, stopping the draught \+ preventing proper burning of the lamps. I was obliged to give it up. Stone came with his bills \+ I had a settlement with him \- At noon I went to P.O. Got a letter from N.A. enclosing notice from book keeper 2? Nat. Bk. That Med's acct. was over drawn 44. On looking over my figures I soon found where I had made a blunder \- By the same mail I got a letter from Sea Tree enclosing check for $325. int. due the [illegible] \- so I wrote at once to the bank \+ sending the check straightened matters out. Dinner at 1. Ruan came but he could not get the Engine burner to perform properly. I wrote to the Co. in N.Y. asking advice. I did not do any work on the few. green portrait at all. To P.O. at 5 \- salame at 8. Ev'g. a little reading aloud to the boys.
