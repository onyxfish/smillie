## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 9, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day, with strong, cold wind directly from the north. Rutran \+ mate came to me to direct them at their new job — so I went down to the pond with them \+ showed them how I wanted the ditches laid \+ covered so that a good entrance can be made from the road nearby — It was a severe trial owing to the sensitiveness of my head \+ throat. — I got to work in studios on packing things after I had written a short note to Mary Cook telling her that I had made up my mind to send Ralph to her with Mollie on Monday. Kate brought up to me from P.O. a batch of N.a. letters that set me to work — de Grand Benedict has assumed that he still has the adult business of N.a. \+ has been getting contracts for the fall cat. It is a piece of colossal impudence on his part that has stirred Hart \+ the others — It obliged me to write a number of letters that really consumed the rest of the day — After 4 I went to P.O. — Then around by Jail Road to where the men were at work — then home. Chores until tea time — Some Reading to Ralph after tea.

many still improves.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 10, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another brilliant, cloudless day following a cold night. The Ther. fell to 26° last night. At about 6 o'clk. lighted the fire that had been laid in my bed room fire place \+ lay in my bed for an hour or more enjoying the crackle of it \+ the reflections on the ceiling — then up \+ had my rub down — then helped Ralph with his bath in portable bath tub before the fire — altogether a very comfortable performance. After bkf. — to work in studios assorting things especially paper of various kinds that I am going to take back to N.Y. as I am satisfied that it is bad for it to be left here during the winter in an unwarmed house. About noon took a stroll over the place. Dinner — wrote a note to Mr. C.R. Woodine telling him that it will be impossible to pay my promised visit on him this fall — Did some pruning of fruit trees \+ bushes \+ a little more work in Studios. Every after tea I went to P.O.
