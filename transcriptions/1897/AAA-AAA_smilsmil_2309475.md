## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 24, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day \- snow falling lightly most of the time. I got up feeling rather slim after last night's experience. At about 11 went to studio \- there until 1 \- then home. Dinner at 2. Soon after 4 I lay down \+ had a pretty solid nap until tea time at 6,30. Ev'g. the conspirators met again, Wood, Beckwith, Nicole, Geo. \+ I were the circle. Brown \+ Church were absent. Beckwith read a draft of a letter that he has prepared, to Cornelius Vanderbilt, stating that as it is rumored that he has promised a benefaction to the N.A., should it purchase a site in 57 St., the subscribers ask if he would be equally willing should the N.A. purchase a site in 110 S. opposite the Cathedral. This was approved \+ Wood, Geo., Beckwith \+ I, the Pres., two Sec'ys \+ the Treas. are to sign it.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 25, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Extremely cold \- strong N.W. wind \- bright. Got to studio after 10, having written notes \+ left them at Mary Coaks \+ Chas. Collin's and been to Miller's in 44th St. with part of W.C. frame that I had him after for me. To work on "A Path to Ravello". I was interrupted by a call from Mr. Morgan, a broker, who called to talk about getting me option for site Con. in 110 S. lots. At 12.30, when he left, I hurried up to Beckwith's studio, 57th St. and signed a letter that the up-town wing of the Con. has decided to send to Cornelius Vunderbilt with reference to a possible benefaction to the N.A.f.D. Walked home \- lunch at 1.30 \- to studio soon after 2 \- worked on "Path &c." \+ put it set aside as finished \- Miller's boy brought the part of frame \+ I took it down to Butler's to have it gilt \- then home \+ had a rest before dinner. Ev'g. to Council meeting \- no business of importance \- adj. at 9.30 \- no site m. meeting, but Beckwith asked for one project monday ev'g at 9 o'clk \- where we expect to present our Camera \+ Hastings plan \+ I will force Terry to make his attack upon my reports as Treas. instead of leaving it to the Special meeting, Wed. the 3d. as was planned. We went up to galleries \+ saw the Welsbach lights just fell in the N. gallery by at Co. at their expense, as an experiment. We all agreed that the light was cold \+ green \- go, the lights will be removed at once before the W.C. \+ the loans.

Calverly brought home Annie's bust \+ it was set in the parlor temporarily \- we express themselves very much pleased with it.
