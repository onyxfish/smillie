## Left Page

### Thur. June 17, 1897

**Weather:** Threatening, with spatters from time to time but not enough to wet the walks.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Farrington waives examination in the Yorkville court. I got up feeling rather slim after a disturbed nights rest. I got to studio about 10 - Douglass' man there plastering cracks \+ preparing to color the walls. Then to N.A. after 11 Gardner came \+ untils near \+ I was with him going carefully through the books to trace the charges as to F. to again assure myself that it is not possible that they are errors of bookkeeping the result of overwork as F. now asserts — again I feel assured that it was dishonesty, pure \+ simple Beckwith came in \+ we walked up town together I got a hurried lunch - Emma Bourne at the house - I got to court in 57th St at 2. met there Kingsoun a young man representing F's lawyer - after a little delay F. was brought \+ for \+ his lawyer waived examination. F. looked wretched \+ was a picture of distress. I had a long talk with the young lawyer \+ he repeated some remarkable lies that F. has been telling his principal showing how he has been overworked, lost his mind \+ was the first one to discover the errors in ms. At 3 I went to Beckwith's studio \+ was with him for an hour - Then to Spaldings \+ Belts showed me some new wheels \+ named very low prices. It was after 5 before I got home - I had a riot before tea at 7 - Wrote to Ralph for a while then wrote notes to Gel, Jas Hart \+ Jim — posted them \+ to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 18, 1897

**Weather:** A very fine day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I went to the indictment clerk of Dist. Atty. I got to studio before 10 \+ found two of Douglass' men at work - I had to fly around to get everything into the middle of the room so that they could work on the wall all around. Then to N.A. \+ found check from Appleton which required answer \+ deposited his check \+ out Carried heavy bags downtown - so delayed that I did not get to Lee's office until 12, until I engaged with Kingsoun trying to get from the books a clear statement of Farrington's methods which we found it very difficult to do - We sent for Gardner \+ Collins to help us \+ went to lunch (R. \+ I) at the Cafe Savarin - R. insisted upon paying \+ besides it was a good lunch - back to office before 2. Collins there but it was soon evident that he could not help us - He was unable to direct us clearly through any one of F's fraudulent transactions from beginning to to end. So, at 3 o'clk. we gave it up \+ R. \+ I went up to the municipal bldg in 52d St. to the Indictment clerks office where we had an appointment - we found him immersed in some other matters, various persons running in \+ out but we finally got so far that he was able to tell us just what more evidence he wanted \+ to say that it was possible it might be sent to the Grand Jury on Mond. or Tues. where it would be acted upon promptly. It was after 4 when we parted - I took 4th Ave. car up town \+ got home at about 5 - It was 5.45 when R. \+ I started out for a bicycle ride - I found that I had a loose pedal \+ I went to Spalding's \+ had it fixed - I there learned from Belts that he had sold Jim's old wheel for $15.00 \+ I was mad \+ told him that I wanted it back - I would use it myself - I was a fool that I had not thought of that solution before. Then R. \+ I went off \+ had our ride in the Park where it was exceedingly delightful - we made the circuit \+ home at about 7.45 - It was after 8 before I took my tea - Late as it was I read awhile to R. - \+ then spent some time over N.A. matters
