## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 21, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day, but quite cold. Had fire made in studio furnace. The large stone in lower hall was prepared + in the very I made a fire there. Mary has been complaining for some days + about noon she gave up + sent for the Dr. She has chilly spells, muscular soreness + fever. I worked with pen, wash, + white + finished a proof of Mad. Sq. garden from 26th St. E. of 4th Ave. I printed a no. of photos. from the neg. films that I developed on Sunday. After dinner I toned + washed the prints. Then I spent some time clipping the Berberry hedge at the end of the lane. I rec'd. a letter from Jts.S. Gardner informing me that Farmington is out on bail, was released Sat. noon. After tea I kindled fire in big hall + stove.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 22, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. + milder — Mary in bed all day with fever reaching 105°. Pain in head + general discomfort. Dr. Leonard has made himself very obnoxious by his disquieting remarks in the sick room — judged by his language he takes a lugubrious view of the case. I wrote to Jas. Hart, Kingsburn and Gardner — at 11 I went to P.O. Got letters from Alice Ireland + Mary Coat — Later a telegram from Alice saying that Jim had arr'd + she had started him for New Haven — yet later I got a letter from Jim, from Cloutier's Platform saying that they expected to be in N.Y. sometime on Wed. All of the afternoon I + spent pruning trees etc. out of doors — most of the time at work in the top of a beech tree. Ev'y. read awhile to Ralph + to bed early.
