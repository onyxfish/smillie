## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 15, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cool night - fog hung in the mts until after 9 o'clk - then the weather cleared wonderfully bright. I cleared up things in the studio + commenced work on the copy that I am to make for W.W. of the Amalfi pen sketch that I made of the Albergo dei Cappuccini on Annie's birthday 93. I made the tracing + part of the trans for most of the afternoon was given to work among the beech trees north of the house. Almost all of them have had their tops cut out more or less extensively + there is more or less dead wood to be cut away. It was that sort of surgery that I was engaged upon - At 6 I went to P.O. + was much relieved by getting, at last, a letter from Jim - very full + satisfactory - He reports that he is in splendid condition + is having a glorious time - He has caught several large trout - one being 22" long. Went to P.O. again at 8 o'clk - + left orders with bus to call for Uncle W. + Aunt A - to-morrow mrf.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 16, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncle W. + Aunt A. left us -

A very uncomfortably hot day with a little temporary relief when a thunder shower passed over us between 4.30 + 5.30 Up at 6.30 - bkfs. at 7 - Uncle W. + Aunt A. started for N.Y. on the 8.10 train - all, except Kate, went to the station to see them off. Kate is so out of sorts that she has kept her bed + sent for Dr. Gardiner who says her trouble is indigestion. I worked on pen sketch replica of Albergo dei Cappuccini Amalfi - just before noon Sleuse cut down the dying beech tree in front of the barn - He + Stone worked cutting it up afternoon. I wrote to Gardiner + Kingsbury - wet down paper to take ffs. To-morrow + after the shower started the Ericsson pumps + ran it for a couple of hours. After tea I wrote a note to Alice Ireland concerning Jim - At 8 went to P.O. After return read until 10, Then to bed.
