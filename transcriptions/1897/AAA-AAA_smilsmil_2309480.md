## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 3, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

meeting of N. A. on Site matters -

Very raw, disagreeable day. about 9 a.m. Charl came with carriage, took Katie \+ Bessie, with their trunk, \+ all went to Phila, the girls to be gone some days, visiting friends there. I got to studio at usual time \+ spent the evg. wanting out what I wish to say at the meeting to-night - Home to lunch - did not return to studio - had a good nap after lunch to the great advantage of my head - Up at 4.30 - went to N.A. by appointment \+ there met Beckwith. Mr. Tom Hastings - his assistants with plans for the proposed buildings on 110'S. We got screens up \+ had the plans mounted upon large stretchers, hung upon them - Home to dinner a little before 7 - to Academy soon after 8 - a very large meeting - 57. Came to order at 8.30 - Jno. Hart, as Chairman of Site Com, opened the discussion - Perry was called upon to follow, but he refused - Then Beckwith was called upon - The three screens with plans were unveiled \+ 13- delivered an admirable address, clear, concise, complete with great fluency \+ earnestness - It was at once evident that the wind was completely knocked out of the opposition - After B- no one was ready to say anything but at last Perry was dragged out \+ produced a M.S. \+ some comic ugly crude drawings of what he thought cd. be the desirable thing on 57'S' site - The little life that was left in his party he destroyed - It was a stupid, blundering effort on his part - I followed him with my gun - \+ Watrous came after with his pop - Prof. McWeir spoke affectionately - \+ so did Huntington. - There was very little dissenting discussion. Then came the sealed ballots - Mowrom \+ Shuttleff tried to make trouble by declaring that anyone could vote for any site he pleased without reference to the two (57'S \+ 110'S.) presented by the Com. They were voted down by a large majority \+ the balloting went on - Sealed ballots were dropped into a locked box, which was sealed up formally \+ sent into the safe to be opened to-morrow night by Site Com- There was just a little ugly little acrimony \+ Hart, Watrous \+ I am sure that no doubt the vote was in favor of 110'6 \+ they would accept the verdict \+ work for the Academy -
After 12 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 4, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

meeting f Site com. \+ counting of votes

fair day - got thro' with usual mf duties \+ to studio at about 10 - worked for some time on a mount to be gilt \+ used on "Tower of Cathedral, Ravello" - Mr. Coon, from Tribune came \+ I gave him an interview concerning N.A. of D. affairs - Lunch at 1 - Back to studio \+ feeling very heavy I had a nap in my chair - About 4 o'clk. Dr. \+ Mrs. Gester came in \+ made a very pleasant call - Dinner \+ in the eveg at 8. to meeting of Site com. at W.A. All present. Perry of course was perverse every time - Before the ballots were counted he took nearly an hour of time to explain a new plan of his - that is, to buy the lots on 6th Ave, bet. 43 \+ 44 \+ 51 \+ side, where car stables now are \+ put up there a bldg. having galleries with stores underneath - shelots to cost more than the amt. of money available by the Academy - At last he got thro' \+ was promptly voted down - \+ when the votes were counted - a very interesting \+ somewhat dramatic event - The result was a surprise to all of us - 57 votes - 40 for 110'S: 16 for 57'S: \+ 1 "neither" - Then a discussion as to how to secure our plans - Beckwith favored a small carefully selected com. to direct one selected architect or firm - I favored a "closed competition" for not more than five architects at $1000 each. The matter was not settled - There was general discussion until after 11. Watrous walked up home with me.
