## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 11, 1897

**Weather:** Bright \+ warm m'f. with pleasant breeze.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Afternoon, wind changed \- sky overcast with promise of rain \+ decidedly cooler. After 6kf. Ralph insisted that I should go out riding with him and I did so, he driving. We drove about for an hour or so \+ in at 10.30 \- Then I tried to do something on Genl. Green but a severe headache developed \+ was unfitted for such work. Afternoon I was little better \- but I kept out \+ about the place. I made some attempts to raise one of Charl's Box-kites but it w'd dive in spite of tinkerings with the bridle. Eve'g. Mary \+ the girls went to the Biddles. I remained quietly at home.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 12, 1897

**Weather:** Bright m'f. but soon clouded over the wind changing around towards the east.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent all the morning pruning \+ trimming my apples \+ pear trees. It was late before we finished our dinner \+ after that I was drowsy. I did a little more trimming of bushes before tea. Ralph, with his dog Tim, \+ Paul Mulford got after a skunk under the pasture wall. They were busy for about two hours \+ then, in triumph, brought the defunct Mephitis to the house \- \+ they brought more \-. They were ordered to "clear out" \- They did \- but there was something in the air that did not obey orders. Ralph has learned something about skunks. Eve'g. I wrote to Ja[illegible] Herbert Morse,
