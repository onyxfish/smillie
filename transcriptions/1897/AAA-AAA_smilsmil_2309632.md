## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 6, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - after a few minutes at the studio I went to NA \+ there looked over the books \+ found that Gardner had really not written any thing in them since the deposition of Farrington. Then to Criminal Court Bldg \+ saw [illegible] Ass. Dis. Atty [illegible] Mitchell in Part [illegible] of Gen. Sessions - he said that he had done nothing to advance the T. case but promised again to "see" McLaughlin - I said I wished like a promise more definite - then he said he would call McL - he told one of the attendants to go \+ find "Jim" McL - McL - came in presently - he was very cocky \+ told me that he had never pleased "Grisley" he w'd admit that he had made errors - Mitchell suggested a plea for mercy in return for plea of Grisley but McL. said that it wd. be impossible to convict T. That was the end of it - after McL left - Mitchell repeated his stories about Jews on juries - etc - etc - \+ me he That there was little chance of securing a conviction but he w'd put it on the calendar if I said so - there I went to 59 W \+ got my E. P. stenographic bonds - Then to 59 W. St. \+ found that Gardner had disappeared leaving no trace behind - Then to Charlie - Then to [illegible], where I could get no comfort - Then to 2d Nat. Bk. - Then home to lunch - a short afternoon - mounted pen sketch - St. Peters \+ the flowering Almond Tree - Home before 5 \+ rested before dinner - Coucil Meeting - a good deal of time was given to my statement of the present state of the T. case \+ the Gardner complication - I have determined to see Dis. Atty. Alcott to know if he holds the same view as Mitchell - Home at 12.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 7, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable, raw day - some mizzle in the early ev'g. To studio at 9.30 \+ worked until near 12 with india ink \+ chinese white on pen sketch mounted yesterday - Then to O'Brien's \+ to NA - then to 2d Nat. Bk. \+ got the deposit slips to see if I could find trace of $223. that Gardner took to deposit Oct. 5 - I could find none. Home to lunch at 1.45 - to studio at 2.30 - cut a mount for pen sketch - then to NA \+ spent some time with Grannis trying to get a cash bal. agreeing with pass book, but at 5 o'clk. gave it up \+ went home \+ had a short rest before dinner - Charl sent his piano to Bessie \+ To night - it stands covering the library - Read awhile to Ralph - then to Ed \+ Cook's for half an hour - then to studio \+ fixed fire - then home - Mary \+ Katherine went to Mendelssohn Glee Club concert.
