## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 2, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely summer day \- bright \& warm without being too warm. Geo. Walker came \& after a little work about one of the cisterns I went up to the fairground with him \& started him upon the work of putting the Ring-Road on the rocks in condition for a Cycle Path. I also spent much time with the Hot air engine \- I succeeded in pumping the barn tanks to the overflow out in spite of care in setting the oil feed, after a four hours run I found the fire box much clogged with soot \- I worked in the shop for a time making a sort of wooden handle in which I can set a cutting stone to be used in cleaning plate margins. Dinner \- wrote to J.J. Grenville \& Jas. Hart. Before 6 went to fair ground with my wheel \& Rode there until 6.30 \- then home to tea \- At 8 o'clk \- with the boys went to the P.O. while down town learned that a cow was in my corn \- so, altho' it was quite dark we went to the lot and we found that the vagabond cow was our own Jessie \- It was owlish work but we managed to get her back into her pasture.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 3, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day with a refreshingly cool breeze. I devoted myself to correspondence \- most of it N.A. of D. \- until 12 \- Then worked in shop \& made a sort of wooden handle in which I set a cutting stone \& worked upon the margin of green plate, cleaning it. After dinner I gave up to a headache that had bothered me all day \& was on my bed until 4. Then I wrote part of a letter to J.C. Nicoll \& then went to Fairground with my wheel \& rode about 8 miles \- Took no tea \- at 8, with the boys, went to P.O. Some newspaper reading in what remained of the even.
