## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 19, 1897

**Weather:** Bright, pleasant mtg — overcast p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

rain commenced about 9.30 eve'g. I spent a good part of the mtg writing a letter to Sarah Fessenden Hammond — Had a stroll over the place before dinner — much of the afternoon I spent in the dark room developing the photos that I took yesterday The films + developer being new I got some good results — Late in the afternoon I had a short bicycle ride on the road — Eve'g. I wrote notes to Mr. Bishop Putnam — Inviting Putnam + Miss Louise Miller, but as it rained I did not go down to the P.O.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 20, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — Cold, windy, wet. Strong wind from N.W. + sculding showers. I got to work in studio soon after 8½. + spent the day etching pfts. that I mounted on Sat. I worked over two pfts of "The Road to the Lake" + two pfts of The Arabia branch. dry-point. I rec'd a letter from Alice Ireland telling me that as her step-mother was visiting her she would not be able to shelter Jim when he arrived, as he may now at any hour, from his camping expedition — she proposed sending him for a week or ten days to the Adamses in New Haven. I wrote to her at once and telegraphed approval. — went to P.O. at 4. Eve'g I wrote a letter to Jim and addressed it to care of Alice I — went to P.O. with it at 10.30
