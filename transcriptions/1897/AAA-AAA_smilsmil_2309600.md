## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 3, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** 34° last nigh[t]

A glorious day but I could not enjoy it as I wished to, feeling pretty thoroughly bunged up — my throat especially being hot \+ sore. After bkfs. I got a fire going in the studio \+ when I was warm there got at my writing but when the sun came in at the bay window I commenced printing from the most recent negatives of my developing \+ then I got my toning bath in order \+ the morning had gone before I knew it — After dinner I resumed the work \+ printed \+ toned a number of photos. I walked some on Piazza. I made a supplemental report \+ wrote a note to Jas. Hart which was mailed in the evef. Evef. a little reading — a little writing — but without enjoyment. Mary sat up in bed to-day — no fever, in fact, in the afternoon her temp. would not register above 96°. The Dr. says she is getting on nicely.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 4, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another lovely day followed by a wonderful moonlight night. I got up feeling very forlorn but improved gradually during the day. During the m'f. I wrote several N.A. business notes \+ in the afternoon completed a long letter to Carroll Beckwith. There was also some out of door work done in gathering plumbs \+ trimming bushes. I went down to P.O. before 5 o'clk. Stone \+ Speuse threshed part of my buckwheat crop on the barn floor this afternoon. Evef. Ralph read awhile to me \+ later I wrote to J. A. Morse (Jim's teacher) on the subject of crowding Jim with Greek at this time.

Many still improves \+ sat up in bed for some time. This is the sixth m'f. that she has had no fever \+ the fourth day that her temp. has been normal m'f. \+ evef.
