## Left Page

### Thur. May 6, 1897

**Weather:** Warm & pleasant - after 6klp. with Tu-  **Temp:** [illegible]

Roofers \+ plumbers for awhile. to studio \+ wrote directions to Stone at Montrose, etc \+ at 11 went to 2d Nat'l Bank then to 740 W. 23d St. Artist-Artisan mo't — Spring exh'n of Students' work — was not much pleased with the artist part but was interested in some of the Artisan work — went in to N.A. to see how the auditing of accts. was getting on — Farrington not there — the accountant not there \+ his assistant sitting idle — it put me out of patience — went to Ponroy's. Cor. 5th & Clinton Sq. W. to get a pair of tress pads — then home to lunch — I was much bothered in the afternoon by a heavy aching head \+ made little progress. I sent pkg. to Kohler with p.o. that he sent to me to look at some weeks ago — also — I overhauled the contents of the pkg of C.W. Paper rack from London — \+ find that I have 175 sheets to distribute among the Water-color men, for Trial. Every about 8 o'clk. Gardiner the Auditor came to see me \+ made the startling revelation that Farrington is short in his accts $600 or 700 that, in some way not yet clear to me — he has got from the payments made for pictures sold, in the exhibits of last Spring \+ Autumn. This is a very sad story — the wreak of a man having many good parts — but weak in his moral perceptions. He has puzzled me many times — I cannot read him — but, a man with a glass eye, is pretty effectually masked. This too, is a humiliation to me as Pres'd. How am I to acquit myself if a charge of carelessness is made agt. me? Unless he has played a game too sharp to be discovered by ordinary care \+ scrutiny. Charle came with his wheel \+ we rode up the Boulevard to 110 S. \+ then home at 10. 45.

Discovery of Farrington's Shortage in N.A. accts.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 7, 1897

**Weather:** Very cool threatening m'f, but clearing until  **Temp:** [illegible]

the w'f which was beautiful moonlight. most of the n'f was spent in arranging systematically the 172 sample sheets of 19 varieties of C.W. Drawing paper sent to me by Abbey, Parsons \+ others, interested in finding a market here, to distribute among the Water-color men — I wrote letters to Alfred Parsons \+ Dawl, North — went to P.O. \+ station Sq. Ave. \+ 44th — then home to lunch — At 2 went to N.A. \+ there met Mr. Gardner \+ his assistant, Mr. Collins — Farrington had been at his desk during the n'f under direction of Mr. Gardner trying to finish up his work that is very much in arrears — then he went down town to see if he could raise $025 to cover his shortage. He came back about 3 saying that he had \+ does not know that Gardner has told me anything about the trouble — aside from evident dishonesty, his books are in a terrible mess — \+ yet, he met me blandly as though everything was going on smoothly, \+ I regretted that his illness caused him to be absent yesterday \+ showed me a D'd certificate — I don't yet know whether he is a very cunning dissembling scamp or if he is a weak minded overwrought wreck. I am in such frame of mind now that I distrust everybody, not excepting myself. I spent some time arranging the steps \+ his money. to studio at 5 — Balme \+ out to Spaldings with my old wheel \+ left it there for repair — Dinner at 6.30 — Every. Charle came \+ took Bessie to Mendelssohn Glee Club concert.
