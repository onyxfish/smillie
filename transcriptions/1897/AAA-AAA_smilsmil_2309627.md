## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 26, 1897

**Weather:** Horrible day \- rain \- wind \- muggy.

I started out after bkfs\. \+ went to studio then down to Municipal Blg. to Dist. Atty's office \+ saw Sp. Judge David Mitchell, 1st Ass't. Had a second talk with him concerning the Farmington case with the result that I instructed him to push it to trial \+ if possible get from J\- a plea of guilty in return for a suspension of sentence. This he says he thinks is the best that can be effected. Then to see three \+ Jones Kingsbury \+ told him how to work on McLaughlin. Then to Dwyer \+ Co. Broad S\. to get information concerning Cont. Pacific Bonds. Saw Joachim Branch. 1900 \- all right. Holiday \- New to Geo. Cooks \- 48 Bdway \- Met 2, Nat. Bk. \+ found the Oregon receipts where they were not lost. Then home \+ lunch \- To studio after, but it was my dark \+ I was tired, so, little was accomplished \- I had a rest before dinner. [illegible] \- a protracted meeting \- evening at home with the boys, Reading \- after [illegible] got to bed \- I spent some time writing

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 27, 1897

**Weather:** Bright, clear \+ cold.

To studio at 10. For a while, worked upon ruling an acc't. book for my estate accts., values \+ income. Then to NA where is worked for an hour or more on NA. business. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- To studio at 2.30 \+ spent most of the remainder of afternoon in rigging an arrangement \- a frame with cord \+ pullies, to hoist before the large window to break the glare of sunlight reflected from windows on the opposite side of the street. A short walk \+ then a rest before dinner. Evef. Charl came in. I went to Century \+ had some interesting exchange of views concerning the new NA plans \- Home at midnight.
