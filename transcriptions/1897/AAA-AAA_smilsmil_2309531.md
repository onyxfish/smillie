## Left Page

### Tues. May 18, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, but warmer. I got a good start in the m'g. \+ after disposing of routine duties I started for the N.A. stopping at Park Ave. Hotel to call on Mr. \+ Mrs. Clim. Woodin — we had a very chatty time \+ I was with them until near 1 - then on to N.A. - Got necessary business finished \+ went to bank with a deposit. Home at 1 o, under a new arrangement, had a mid-day dinner — It was 2.30 when I went to Studio \+ found Jeny \+ his assistant, stuck on the stair with my Vesuvius picture — I took the bossing of the job \+ it was soon got up — At 3 I went to N.A. \+ worked until 3.30 when Hart \+ Beckwith came \+ we considered applications for the Supt. position — there were a number — but we agreed to give Allison the chance — I continued my work until near 6 — then home tea at 6.30 — At 9.30 I went out on my wheel — making the circuit of Central Park — home at 10.45

## Right Page

### Wed. May 19, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katie T. ret'd. from New-Haven.

Charming day — Got to studio at 9.30 \+ spent an hour clearing the place up as I expected Mr. \+ Mrs. Clemuel Woodin — but they did not come — I had hoped that Vesuvius might find a place to rest — At 11 I went to N.A. \+ after some work there to 2° Nat. Bk. — to Dunlap's — then to Brook's Bro. \+ bought some clothes for my pay — then to Bridgeman's to straighten some mis-understanding about seeds — \+ home to dinner at 1.30 Katie T— there, having ret'd. from New-Haven, leaving Bessie there — Again to N.A. \+ spent the p.m. there getting out the checks in payment for pictures \+ prizes in the schools — It was nearly 6 when I got home — Tea at 6.30 — about 7. with Jim \+ Ralph, went out \+ had a little turn on our wheels by twilight — Before \+ Charl came \+ we had an even't. of Mulrein, the B'k plumber who has commenced suit for work done on Garfield Pl. house under direction of B'k. Board of Health. Charl thinks he has a clear case, but on his presentation of it I have but little hope
