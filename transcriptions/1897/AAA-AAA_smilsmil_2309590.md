## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 13, 1897

**Weather:** Overcast m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'f — very like rain — sunshine struggled out before noon, but about 3 o'clk, a fine shower fell, clearing after 5 o'clk. I worked on Green plate in a very dis'pinted way — unable to make any progress n'to see any encouragement ahead. At last I determined to repaint carefully the oil sketch, the size of the engr'l, that I made after the miniature \+ make the translation as complete as possible — So, I sandpapered it down, set my palette and worked in monotone until after 5 o'llk. At 6 Ralph \+ I went down town \+ transacted business \+ home to tea at 7. I am very anxious to hear from Jim — It is now three weeks since I heard from him. A quiet ev'f. Read awhile to Ralph.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 14, 1897

**Weather:** Superbly brilliant day — brisk \+ cool  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superbly brilliant day — brisk \+ cool. I got to work soon after 8h.f't. At my painting of Gen'l Green \+ kept steadily at it until I had gone over the ent're face. I feel encouraged to believe that by this means I shall be able to work out a good enlargement of the miniature from it get my plate out of the hole that it is now in. After dinner I had some small matters to attend to \+ at 4 o'clk. I went out on my wheel accompanied by Ralph on Trixy. We went down the valley road thro' Darkey town. I found it pretty hard work on the rough road \+ up grade — Rode 5 3/10 miles — Rested until tea-time — after that, Read to Ralph —
