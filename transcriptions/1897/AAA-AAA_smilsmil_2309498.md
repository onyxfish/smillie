## Left Page

### Thur. March 11, 1897

**Weather:** Very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - All day waiting for my Vesuvius to be returned from the Century - but it came not - \+ when I went there for an explanation at 6.30 I found it hanging into place on the wall, with no one able to answer my questions. During the day, at the studio, I worked on some old W.C. drawings trying to improve them - At 5.30 I went to Fine Arts Socy. in 57th St. to get my umbrella, left there yesterday p.m. when I went to Architectural league [illegible] I had my brass check but, as it was after 6 I could not get in - Then walked down to Century - \+ from there home to dinner at 6.40 - Charl came in before our dinner was over \+ shared it with us, leaving early in the evening. I spent the eveng with R- who seems to be getting along very nicely - About 9.30 I again went to Architectural league \+ this time got my umbrella \+ walked home. A beautiful night - In before 10.30 \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 12, 1897

**Weather:** Rain in the m'fg. clearing before night  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the m'fg. clearing before night - I got to studio at 9.30 but it was 11[illegible] before Jerry got along with Vesuvius from the Century. I lost no time in getting to work - putting a strong glaze over most of the foregrounds and roughly painting in a treetop that covered the hiatus between the two sides of the picture - Home to lunch at 1. Yale was with Ralph \+ reported that everything seemed to be going very satisfactorily - The boy continues to be remarkably good \+ patient - He has not fretted at all or made a single complaint. Back to studio at 2 and worked until 5. Invited Maynard to dine with us - Sat down in my big chair \+ fell asleep and did not awake until about 6.40 - hurried home \+ found Maynard there - Left. At 8.30 I went to Mrs. Barhydt's 40 E. 70th St. to talk business with the Kismet that Mrs. B- has told me that she is ready to establish a fund to be known as the Albert H. Baldwin Fund to yield an annual income of $200. She wishes this income to be devoted to furthering the art of free hand etching. I advise that two prizes be offered in the Nat. Academy school - one of $150. the other $25[illegible] the bal. of the $200 - i.e. $[illegible] to be used to purchase books of etchings for reference - This she assents to but she will retain the fund in her own keeping until it has been proved experimentally to be a success - I am to formulate rules to govern the formation of the class - the competition \+ the awards - This enterprise appeals to me very strongly - It makes it possible to develop etchdrs upon a basis purely artistic on no way dependent but upon ignorant \+ popular taste or commercial considerations. I left at 10.30 \+ unmaked home.
