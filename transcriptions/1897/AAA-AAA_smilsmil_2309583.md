## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 30, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncle Willie \+ Aunt Agnes arrived.

Heavy clouds, threats \+ a very little rain clearing charmingly during the m'f. - clouds in the afternoon - beautiful sunset - clear \+ quiet eve'f. I wrote on Farrington business to young Kingsbury with dee dee. Worked on Gen'l. Green, scraping background, until 12.30, then rested until dinner, as my head troubled me. Again on Gen'l. Green until 4.30 - some writing \+ to P.O. at 5.30. After tea Katherine T., Ralph \+ I went to R.R. Station to meet Uncle W. \+ Aunt Agnes. Train in at 8 - 25 min. late. R. \+ I went downtown to P.O. etc. - \+ home at 8.30 - Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. left Folk - on the "Mary Powell" at 6.30 \+ have had a long, tiresome journey which they seem to have stood remarkably well.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 31, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another charming day. Spent most of the m'f writing a long letter to Beckwith - Men, some work on Gen'l. Green. After dinner had a long chat with Uncle Willie before I got to work on my plate. I still fuss with the background trying to rid it of the mottling that is so disagreeable to me. It was after 4.30 when I went on my wheel to P.O. \+ then continued my ride "doing" 7 miles, without any walking, before I got home. A long ride for me over these country roads. A bit before tea - Eve'f to P.O. at 8 - then to Village Hall where a fair was in progress for the M'ps'n Soc'y. Home after 9 - wet down [illegible] for printing to-morrow.
