## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 12, 1897

**Weather:** Clear + very cold.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio soon after 10 \- got out of the press the paper that I had prepared for a water-color painting + charcoaled an outline of a bit that I got near Ravello \- a road, cliff \- a ruined tower \- a deep valley + the Mediterranean beyond. Then I got to work with color + was much disgusted to find that the soft, charcoal paper, so beautiful in color would not take a wash as the paper upon which the San Remo painting was made did. Home to lunch + on my return I continued the work as long as I could see \- It would be wiser if I stretched another paper over it. Dinner at 6.30 \- a loafing sort of an every. Charl came in for awhile \- to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 13, 1897

**Weather:** Bright m'g  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- then overcast + at night a weak attempt at snow. To studio about 10 \+ to work on my Ravello water color painting. I determined to fight it out on the paper upon which I had made the beginning + hope that the result will justify my determination. I Rec'd. from Mr. Frances a communication requesting my check for $11. + odd cts. Repayment to Mr. Witherbee for expenditures in relaying pavement in front of Griffen Court. I refused at once denying the right of the claim. Mr. F. came in the afternoon + wheeled me into paying $5.50. I was afraid to do it as I made expenditures to the advantage of Mrs. W. that would more than offset her claims. Sucy Semi-Centennial celebration at Century Club \- an immense crowd. only a small portion could get into the room where addresses were made \- heat exceeding great \- I managed to hear Parks Godwin + our Allen Butler \- Bishops Patter was very happy in his remarks. It was after 12 when I got home \+ when I got to bed
