## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 6, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day - brilliantly clear and cool in shade. I got letters that required immediate attention. I wrote for some time in the m'f \+ did a very little work on the Gen'l. Green margin. Immediately after dinner, Bostwich the Grocer, came \+ to Jim \+ Ralph at fishing on Jones' lake. I rec? $20. from Mrs. Betts for Jim's old wheel \+ R's old wheel - a shabby culmination of my business transactions with him. I went to P. O. at 4.30 - I Rode on fair Ground back until 6.30 - then home to Supper. At 7.30 took my bicycle to Cooley \+ left it there - to have a spring seat post put in. The boys got back from their fishing at 8.50 very tired \+ hungry \+ with 4 or 5 small fishes. They soon got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 7, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. It was about 9.30 that I went to Boyd \+ Cooley's \+ in half an hour I had the new Spring saddle post bushed \+ fitted to my wheel. Josephine Beuty came in - I had not seen her for many years. I rode up home \+ spent some time writing. I did some more work - mechanical grind on the Green margin. After dinner I was showing the working of my new saddle post. I managed to get a very awkward \+ heavy fall against the Piazza rail, bruising my left thigh severely and painfully. It made me very sore and lame, but I was not disabled for work with my scraper on the plate margin. Josie Beuty called \+ Mary \+ Kate about 5 o'clk. A quiet even'g, reading "A Venetian June" by Anna Fuller.
