## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 1, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm, pleasant day. Pope's team came \+ worked during the m'f hauling stone for Rutau \+ his helper to lay in a terrace wall where I set berry bushes \+ apple trees last fall. Rutau \+ his mate worked all day — another helper worked 7 hours. I printed two proof from Gen'l. Green in the m'f \+ was somewhat encouraged by the result. I rec'd. by Exp. from Charl two Blue Hill Boy kites, for the boys, I suppose. Uncle M[illegible] missing trunk arrived in the afternoon. I scraped on Green's background. Ev'f I read awhile to Ralph — then some desultory chat with Uncle M —
During the night — about 4 A.M. I think, heavy showers with one tremendous clap of thunder.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 2, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Foggy, threatening m'f, but by 10 o'clk. it cleared \+ a lovely day followed. I got up feeling very unwell — general discomfort — sore bronchia — headache, etc. Pope's team came up to work but went away again. Routau, his mate \+ a helper got to work about 10 upon the laying of a wall \+ so. end of stable. I was not able to work but spent considerable time fussing with the Ericsson pump before I could get it to work. After dinner I went to Bank \+ to P.O. I sold my crop of potatoes to Bostwick at 50 cts a bushel \+ feel quite like a farmer. Two more pkgs. came by Exp. directed to Charl \+ Mary got a note from him saying that he is coming to make us a two days visit to-morrow. The box kites are for his amusement. Ev'f Mr. Paul. Sayre, Mr. Comstock \+ sister Grosbeck called — I excused myself \+ went to bed when Ralph did, before 9.
