## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 23, 1897

**Weather:** Wet in the early m'g. but the sun soon shone out  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wet in the early m'g. but the sun soon shone out & the day was very pleasant & with the sunshine I began to feel better. Immediately after b'kf. I rec. a note from Mrs. Barhydt, who had invited me to dine with her this, eve'g., asking to be excused until Friday, which was exactly to my mind — At 9.30 went to Lincoln Safe Deposit, met T.W. Wood & got all the U.S. Bonds from the box — Rec. to the house & filled out assignments on the backs — Then after my regular duties went to Sub Treas & had them properly signed — then went to Central Trust & saw Mr. Sherman & made a satisfactory arrangement with him — the Trust to hold the bonds to sell as the market will take them & to advance such amt. as shall be needed on the 6th of March to complete the purchase of the 110 S. date — . I saw Cornell, the broker, & gave him instructions — then went to del's & saw him — then home to lunch at 1.20 — To studio after 2 & painted for awhile on Vesuvius — but could not get up much vigor — At 5 went to Butler with mat for Ravello Tower, to have it gilt. Home soon after 6 — Every. many & her girls went to a church affair — I Redd for a little while to the boys from "Hiawatha" — Nellie Smillie came in, alone, for a few minutes. I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 24, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Had the cook family dine with us —

Bright, crisp day — To studio at about 10 & to work on Vesuvius — on lower R.h. corner — at a part of the painting that I have been fighting shy of for some time — but I got along fairly well and, on the whole, feel encouraged — I worked (except when I went home to lunch) until near 4 o'clk — then cleaned up — got home at 5, had an hours rest, then dressed & at 7 we sat down to dinner. Ed W. & Mary Cook, Mr. Sprague & Miss Williams, Walter & Louise, Ge. & Finley & our own family. 15 at table — Many Mooney helped serve & the affair seemed to pass off nicely — Walter had to leave to be at Century at 8.30 — Louise, not being well, left at 9 — the others stayed until after 10 — Charl came in for a few minutes, with his bicycle, after 10 — To bed after 11.
