## Left Page

### Thur. March 25, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

I awoke at 4 a.m. with very severe headache. At 5 I got up + took a dose of Hydro.brom.Caff. + Soda Sal. but got little relief. I was able to get up at 8 o'clk o'dress, but did scarcely to get out of the bed room which not yet fumigated or cleared up is very disagreeable to me, but I was soon obliged to get back to it. Yale came to see Gretta in the m'f + he saw me on my bed. I was able to send my Stoddard dinner ticket to George but he was in Ridgefield + did not use it — so it was lost. My headache progressed + became a combination of sick + nervous + several other kinds + at last I was nearly wild. It is a long time since I have suffered so much discomfort. It was after 2 A.M. before I recovered sufficiently to be able to get up, undress + get back to bed properly. My stomach troubled me a good deal thro' the remainder of the night.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 26, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouds + sunshine + high, cold wind. I felt so seedy after yesterdays experience that I did not go out of the house. Altho' I was able to get up in the m'f + go down to help. I hung about all day uncomfortable especially in the region of the stomach, and useless. I wrote a long letter to Beckwith on the subject of Watrous + his dinner. The measles has almost disappeared. Gretta is normal + expresses her readiness to get up, but Katie P. altho' normal in temp., heart action + circulation is in a state of helpless hysteria + is dying, by her own statement, all the time. Her mother sympathizes + I find myself in a dreadful atmosphere. Think of the serene level of Annie + then of this dismal fog of hysteria \- it depresses me exceedingly.
