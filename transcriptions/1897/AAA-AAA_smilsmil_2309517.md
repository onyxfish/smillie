## Left Page

### Sun. April 18, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful spring day - Blue at 9. About 10 went to studio - wrote a new draft of letter to Mr. Jas. H. Dole - Home at 11.30 - out about noon, Jim \+ I went out for a walk, Ralph being at Edw. Cooks. We went up to Central Park \+ wandered about there, getting home a little before 2 - dinner - A little after 5 the whole family went to a R.C. church in 43d. S. near 3d. Ave. to see Gretta Sussione married - The time set for the ceremony was 5.30 - altho' we got there at 5.20 we were just too late but went in time to congratulate the bride and groom in the lobby of the church -
Tea at 6.30 - Eve'g. Read to R- until after 8.30 - Jel came in later \+ stayed until after 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 19, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, growing to be very cold at night. All day my work at studio was writing. I wrote to Stone \+ to Spence, at Montrose, giving instructions for Spring work - I also spent much time over my letter to Mr. Jas. H. Dole, V.P. of Art Inst. of Chicago - a pro- test agt. The stuff of which the jury of Selection \[illegible\] is made \+ suggesting to the Inst., through him, a larger \+ more broadly representative com. Eve'g. Council meeting followed by Com. on Site \+ Plan. It was a late session the time being given to revision of "Requirements" under which the competing architects shall work, to be further considered in conference with the competitors next week.
