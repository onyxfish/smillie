## Left Page

### Wed. March 31, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beautiful spring day. Before I went to Dr. Yale's & spent some time with him getting directions as to pretea & general cleaning ups. It was 10.30 before I got to studio spent the rest of the m'f. writing out a form of architectural requirements to be given to the architects in the coming N.A. f D. competition. A short walk before lunch — To studio again after lunch — much bothered by an aching head — but I managed to do a little writing — to Kothler, S— it 5 I went to Century & met Beckwith, and Dielman — we three a sub. com. on drafting form of Requirements in coming architectural competition. We had a very interesting interchange of views lasting until 6.30 — Beckwith \+ I had pretty ready forms — these were put into Dielman's hands that he might prepare one to form a basis of our report \+ adjs. to meet. Sundae p.m. Home to dinner — Every nit\. Cook \+ Emily came — looked over portfolios of photos many of which belonged to I[illegible]. They were separated from the others \+ the portfolio \+ rack are to go to [illegible].

## Right Page

### Thur. April 1, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day. Immediately after B'fast. I went to Dr. Yale's \+ got instructions from him as to final treatment of the measles patients \+ the disinfection of the house then home \+ had some delay there — It was nearly 11 when I got to studio — there for a few minutes — three to N.A. Varnishing Day. Much ferment among the painters over the action of the Fine Arts federation in passing, at its last meeting, Resolutions protesting against all tax on art. As a majority of the N.A. artists have signed a petition to congress to put a specific duty of $50 on each imported work — this action of the F.A.F. puts the artists "in a hole" as the F. distinctly claims to represent them "too" to representatives from the N.A. \+ other artist ass'ns. There was no definite information to be had \+ there was much unnecessary talking — Calls were made for special meeting of N.A. \+ next Wed. of Watercolor Soc. on Thur. Went to Spaulding Co. in 42d St — Saw Mr. Beck — and ordered a 2nd hand Crescent bicycle to present \+ one on approval. for Ralph — Then home to lunch — To studio at 2 — wrote to Dielman on the subject of the F.A.F. action \+ asking for copy of Constitution \+ resolution — Before 5 went with Maynard to Century — met a group of painters \+ architects there \+ until 6.38 we discussed F.A.F. — Then Dinner — meeting Church on my way home going with him into his Rooms for a few min. After dinner, Read to R — after he went — \+ I cleaned my wheel up \+ a little \+ went out for a short ride — The first since last fall. — Charl \+ Bessie went out on theirs on the Boulevard Charl was run into by another rider \+ his hind wheel \+ his dandy new "bike" was completely demolished, but without injury to him.
