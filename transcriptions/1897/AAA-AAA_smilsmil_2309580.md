## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 24, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain recommenced after 5 A.m. \+ fell steadily, at times heavily. For about four hours. Showery day, clearing beautifully. Got up with a beastly headache which unfitted me for work. — So, I took a holiday. All of the house cisterns were full to overflowing except the leaking one at S. W. Cor. of house — so I started the pump to transfer its contents to barn tanks. I had so much trouble with the heating part of it — it burned so badly \+ smoked so furiously that I got Spencer to help me \+ I took it all apart — cleaning the burner thoroughly \+ making a change in the draught [illeg.], after which it worked very well; but I took all of my milk \+ a good part of the afternoon. Loafed about considerably. Rec'd a very interesting letter from Beckwith who is now at Aix les Bains. After tea to P.O. Ralph was at Paul Mulford's \+ he came home with me. Evef. like all the others — stupid — stupid.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 25, 1897

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A sultry, grey mof. Threatening rain — clearing in the afternoon with showers about. Clear, cool night. I worked on Jewl. Green \+ got plates ready for p[illegible] to-morrow. At noon I rec'd letter from Kingsbury in Lee's office asking, at Mc Laughlin's request, if I would consent to a suspension of sentence if Farrington would plead guilty. — \+ when it would be convenient for me next month to be in N.Y. to attend the trial. I answered that I would not agree to anything that would lessen the hold that the law now has on [illegible]. He has not yet paid sufficient penalty for his crime to make it proper to think of making it easy for him. After tea Ralph \+ I went to P. O. \+ I got a letter from Jim, also one from Mr. J.H. Morse. Mr. Wm Warner called in the evef. I wet down paper for to-morrow.
