## Left Page

### Sat. July 9, 1898

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast mf. - light rain very early - a little thunder before 9 \+ some smart showers about noon. The weather cleared \+ the afternoon was bright, breezy \+ warm. I spent the mf. in looking after the plumber \+ doing some writing, N.A., until 10.30 - then to market etc. \+ to N.A. Granniss not there - then to bank - then to Hahler's \+ bought two pairs of shoes for self - then to Hankinson's \+ paid bill. Then home - had my lunch grandly by myself - some more writing - got some of the starched shirts of the boys - made up boy pkg. \+ took them to Exp. Office. Then home \+ read every Post until dinner - Evely Mr. White went out \+ I wrote until 9 o'clk. Then I got on to my wheel and rode up to Central Park, getting back at about 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 10, 1898

**Weather:** clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day - clear, breezy \+ cool. - Water was let into my basin last night - having running water I enjoyed the luxury of a scrub \+ sponge bath. When I got down to bkfs. I found that Mrs. White had not yet Ret? from her brother's - so I had my meal in solemn state - Ed \+ Cook made me a visit during the mf. I wrote until 1, when, getting uneasy about Mrs. W. I went to her flat in 34th S. to see if she were there - then to Fred. Mcland's to get the address of Winthrop Parker, her nce. Saw Alice - just before dinner, at 2, Mrs. W. came in - surprised at my uneasiness. After 4 I got upon my wheel \+ went to the Park \+ up to Grant's Tomb - The afternoon was charming for a ride - on the Riverside Cycle path it was glorious. Home at 6:10 with an uncomfortable headache - a Rest until at 7 - Evely Charl came in - I wrote a letter to the boys \+ took it to the G.C. Station \+ then to bed.
