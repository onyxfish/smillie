## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 5, 1898

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day. Numna J — gave Jim his piano lesson \+ after that went with me to studio \+ saw my "Showers" — Then I started to work but was soon warned of a coming headache. I then went to N.A. mens engaged there for some time \+ then to 2d. Nat'l Bk. \+ to D. B. Butler's and ordered a frame for Jan day's Rang — Then home \+ was so used up by my headache that I went to bed \+ did not get up until dinner time — Then I ate a very little sparingly — Chas came in in the evey. I went to studio \+ fixed up there \+ at 10 o'ck — went to Century. I found that my sketch had made a little ripple among some of the middle aged men — It was hung without the surname of painter — with a paper attached to the frame by me as a precaution to keep Jerry of it, marked in large letters — Wet. Murphy came to me at once \+ congratulated me — Shirlaw — Gifford \+ others spoke to me of it, \+ altogether I feel quite revived — it was very pleasant to my old ears — I stayed at the club until about midnight — Then home \+ to bed — it was after 3 o'clk — before I got to sleep.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 6, 1898

**Weather:** Delightful day overhead  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day overhead — very bad under foot, owing to the thaws. I had bkft. Late \+ after awhile went to Studio \+ opened the windows to give it thorough ventilation. Then to Century a little before 12. My sketch, in the strong, reflected glare, looked more like a view in a rocky glen than the floating clouds. I was there but a few minutes \+ then went to the Colonial Club, cor. Boulevard \+ 72d S. — with card from J\&Hearn to see my old 1876 painting "The lifting of the clouds." I have not seen it for more than 20 yrs \+ was agreeably surprised to find that it is not so bad as I thought. Hearn says that he is going to present it to the N.A. Retrospective Gallery — I want to get hold of it \+ I think that without destroying its old time quality I can improve it very much. W. T. Evans \+ the N.A. Art Com. came in — ours very agreeable — We walked over to Columbus Ave. together — then home \+ dinner at 2 — Afternoon I had a long nap every. Geo. \+ Chas. at the house — Geo. went with me to Studio — I lighted up so that he might see my Showers — Later I wrote some notes \+ it was midnight almost before I got to bed.
