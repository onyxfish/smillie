## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 6, 1898

**Weather:** Fine, bright winter day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine, bright winter day — was detained at home by plumber \+ carpenter till late — got to N.A. at noon \+ worked for more than an hour at making out checks to artists for pictures sold. Then to bank \+ home to lunch at 1.30 — after that I spent some time with the painter who gave a coat of paint to the bath-room wall — then to a rest for an hour — then to N.A. to finish my work of making out checks but found that Allison had got the book of sales \+ locked it in his desk — so, I could do nothing. Home to dinner at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. news paper \+ had a game of Halma with Ralph. Have not been feeling so well for a day or two past. when I went to bed, Temp. 99¾°.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 7, 1898

**Weather:** Brilliant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day — after a very restless, feverish night I awoke for the day, that is, came out of a chaos of fever-dreams \+ nightmares at about 6 o'clk with a Temp. above 102°. and going up. My bowels forced me up \+ they summoned me half a dozen times during the day. I sent for Yale \+ he came at about 11 o'clk. Temp. then 103°. He thinks I have taken cold \+ been too ambitious in commencing work — My stomach is out of sorts as well as my bowels \+ the fever prostrates me — So, I have been very forlorn all day. Duf — came again in the afternoon. As a precautionary measure he has loaded me up with quinine \+ that I have to add to my miseries. I kept to my bed as closely as I could.
