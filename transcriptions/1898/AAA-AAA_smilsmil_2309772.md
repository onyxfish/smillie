## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 9, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - beautiful display of clouds in the afternoon - clear night with Aurora. B&B's at 8 - a short walk \+ then settled to writing letters - among others, one to Charl - finished letter to Emma. J - Then a short walk before lunch at 1.30 - It was after 3 when I went with Dr. and Mr. Janeway to the top of my Knoll to see the view - After that Ralph \+ I went to the top of Roaring Brook fall - a very interesting walk to me because of the changes - a clearing has been made on one portion of the Ridge rendering it impossible for me to recognize the ground - The view from the top of the fall was remarkably fine. Cloud shadows, Sunshine \+ a luminous atmosphere combining to make a charming picture - we got to Hotel at about 6 \+ had dinner at 7 - After dinner I had Dr. Habbs make a careful examination of Jim's leg to discover, if possible, the cause of the lameness - He stripped \+ examined him \+ concluded that it was probably a strained muscle \+ rheumatism, giving him Salol. - I spent the even in the parlor reading my newspaper \+ enjoying the warmth of the fire.

Got a telegram to day from W.H.L. Lee with offer of $1000 a year for 3 year lease of 43 S. Nicholas Place \+ Replied by telegraph accepting -

No pain to-day -

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 10, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouds hung low until noon - then they lifted - weather very cool. - About 10 o'clk. Ralph \+ I went up The Reserve Road about 2 miles to the Trail to Beaver Dam fall - went to the fall - then by River trail to the Chasm - then on West side Trail to the height from which a very fine view is had of the Giant \+ the River gorge. We got here at a little before noon \+ rested awhile. - Then on, getting back to Hotel at 1.30 - I took a half hour rest \+ had lunch at 2. - Wrote to Lee \+ Lee about business connected with the 43 S. Nicholas Place property - After lunch, feeling tired I rolled myself in a blanket on my bed, read the Tribune \+ had a little nap - then to work drawing checks to meet Mr. Lee's several demands connected with S. Nicholas R. - Dinner at 7 - Evey. Mr. Maghee proposed that I go with him to Mr. Wicks's cottage to attend a meeting of the Executive Com. of the Adirondack Trail Improvement Socy. - I went \+ there met Messrs Wicks, White, Weston \+ Maghee. Scott Brown came in later. - There was a good deal of desultory talk concerning Trails and matters connected - It was nearly 10 when we got away. - Then to bed -

No pain, but I got to be very tired before I got in at 1.30 \+ my stomach felt very weak.
