## Left Page

### Thur. March 31, 1898

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy most of the day, clearing in the evef. To studio + worked all the mtf on Mad. Tower. Home to lunch at 1 - To N.A. of D. at 2 + met Hungerford + Bullock who have got the accls of N.A. all written up \+ posted in ledger to the 1st of March. I went carefully over much of it - it is not, inspite of my repeated directions, just as I wish it but it is now so that I can pick out what I want + I shall re-arrange the accounts to suit myself - I attended to other Academy work while there + went to the Bank. It was after 4 when I returned to studio + a little after 5 when I got to Century in Ways + Means Com. Home at 6.30 - Every Charl came in + we worked over Ralph's bicycle - Jine was off on a ride with Thurston and Bishops. Charl Rec'd cable grave from the girls to the effect that they had had a Rough passage + had all been more or less sick. I went to bed sick with a headache.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 1, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant day. After a night of un-rest + pain made more trying because I knew that I had important engagements for to-day, I got up at about 5 o'clk + drugged myself with Acetanelide + Caffiene + went to bed again only to have pain in the stomach added to my other discomforts - At last, before 8 o'clk - I got up + added a dose of Bismuth + morphia to my internal pharmacy + was able to eat some bkft + get thro' the day. I went to lay Office Bldg \+ Chambers S- + met these J. G. Brown + Mr. Monton Assemblyman who has had chg of the bill at Albany to exempt the new site + Academy from taxation - Mr. T. had made an appt with Mr. Bell, 1st Deputy in Tax office. The case was laid before him + I made affadavit that the N.A. was an educational institution + that no officers of the A. got pay for his services. I got away before 12 + went to Brooklyn Bridge for an airing - then to studio + home for lunch - to Studio at 2. Sketching class there - a hard afternoon teaching them how to lay grounds etc. - Mary Cook came in the midst of it - I met her at the front door + she, with considerable show of temper, made an assault upon me because neither the boys or I had been to her house since I had been left alone - I was too much surprised to reply. She wound up by inviting us to dine with her on Sunday. I got through with my class at about 5 + then went to the Durand-Ruel galleines to see the treats of paintings by "Ten American Painters" - seceders from the Socy of Am. Artists - of course I could n't understand them all -

Dinner - Evef. Margaret Parker White called to lay before me her scheme - which was no shot that she + her 10 year old daughter should come to live with me + the boys, + for their care + companywere to find a home - + one payments by her, to be provided for - as she put it, it was purely a business proposition. She cannot keep her present suite of apartments + wishes to provide a place for herself + girls during the summer.
