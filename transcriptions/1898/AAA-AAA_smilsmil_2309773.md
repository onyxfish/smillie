## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 11, 1898

**Weather:** A very clear, cold day \- Ther. fell to 36°  **Temp:** [illegible]

last night \- It was said that ice formed in some places. Bkft. at 9. Wrote to Francins + sent him checks for Hart's signature. I was not able to exercise as I wished to, my stomach feeling strained + heavy — Not a pain but a great discomfort. Mr. Maghee came + at 10'clk, the boys + I accompanied him to his house + we had a pleasant dinner, Mr. Maghee's two sisters present. I managed with difficulty to get through my part + about 3.30 Ret'd to the hotel — found there a heavy mail — a letter from Lee on N.a business requiring a telegram — I had a talk with Dr. Hobb's concerning my condition. He takes hold just as the other doctors do and advises Nuy V\_, Hydrochloric Acid etc — all of which have been tried + found wanting. I loafed about in front of a big fire + Read newspaper until 6.30 — Then tea. In parlor until 9, then to my Room + soon to bed.

no neuralgic pain but lots of stomach discomfort,

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 12, 1898

**Weather:** Brilliant, cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

To-day Mr. Wick's party to tread down his new trail up Chapel Pond brook + over a shoulder of the Giant. Started at about 11. There must have been twenty or more. I was to go with them but W.G. Neilson + Scott Brown came for me to go with them to go over the bushed trail for the proposed road on north side of my Knoll. I went with them + strayed in the shady, cold, damp woods until I was uncomfortably chilled — we are in trouble trying to get the line of the road through without destroying any good sized + sound trees. I went back to the hotel + feeling rather uncomfortable. I went to my Room + Read + wrote until lunch at 1 o'clk — Rev. Louis F. Benson of 1909 Chestnut St. Phila. took me into his cottage, produced a book of his published poems + read to me one which had its inspiration in an etching by Smillie (cedars by moonlight.) I stayed in my room until about 4. Then I strolled over the trail along the cliffs in the Riverside back of Hotel + Mr. Maghee's — Dinner at 7 — Every in parlor until 9 — then to my Room — wrote a short letter to Katharine Throop to go with one written by Jim.

No pain but I have felt singularly uncomfortable all day — weak + all exertion a trial.
