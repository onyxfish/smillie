## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 6, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Returned to Parkes' at Scranton.

Delightful mf. clouded after 3 p.m. - very warm, with pleasant breeze. Up early after a comfortable night. Some wandering about the place after bkp. It was 10 o'clk. before NW ld. \+ I started out in his Motor carriage - by a road that was new to me we went down the north side of the hill \+ around the east end of it to the south \+ up again by the road that we have been over before - a very pleasant \+ interesting ride - we stopped to examine the spring from which the house supply is pumped \+ at other places for views. On my return, packed R's bags mine \+ had a rest before lunch at 1 - an excellent meal beautifully served. At 2.30 we left, young W. \+ family on hand to bid us good bye. In the mf I had been to his house \+ gone over it. The view from the upper loggia is finer than the view from the elder C's house. We took the 3 o'clk train \+ got to Scranton at 4.50. A part of the way I had considerable pain for the first time to-day. Parke met us at train - I went to drug store \+ got a list of drugs. Carlsbad Salts. Calomel. Bismuth \+ morphia \+ Citrate of Magnesia. Then carriage \+ [illegible] P. - we all rode home. Tea at 6. [illegible] chatting \+ music from Miss Kingman. I had a short season of pain soon after eating \+ commenced a course of calomel 1/10 gr. \+ by 11 o'clk had taken 1 gr. — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 7, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded mf. warm for exercise, but I did not exercise - a delightful breeze blew all day long \+ I spent most of the time on the porch. Up at about 5 \+ took bottle of lt. mag. - back to bed \+ slept until after 7. Up before 8 - bkp. about 9 - all went to church \+ I spent much of the mf. on lounge on the porch enjoying light naps. Dinner at 1 o'clk. The afternoon was spent much as the mf. was on the loggia, instead of sleeping I wrote some notes. Every, all went to church \+ I remained at home \+ wrote part of a letter to Charl. After the family ret. from church Miss Kingman played on the piano \+ was after 11 before I got to bed.
