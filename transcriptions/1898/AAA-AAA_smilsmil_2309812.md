## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 2, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day- horrible walking - worked awhile after bkfs. \+ at a little after 10 o'clk. went to Carrere \+ Hastings \+ saw C - with reference to putting up a portion of the N.A. buildings \+ formulating a plan to present to the Advisory Com. at the meeting to-morrow P.M. - Then took a short walk in the sunshine \+ back home \+ more work - most of it in straightening out my own personal accts. Then a short walk before lunch at 1. After that I worked on N.A. accts. Was surprised by a call from Horace Winans \+ he repaid me the $50 - that I loaned him early last summer. Then, after dark, I started out for another walk - met Jim \+ Eve, took a short walk together — Dinner at 6.30. Eve\+ - some more N.A. accts - then Jim read aloud a short story to Ralph \+ me - then I worked on my own accts until 10 - then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 3, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day, overcast, raw, threating - a little rain in the eve\+. I had letters to write after bkfs - so that I did not get to the N.A. until nearly 11; attended to some work there, then to second Nat\+. \+ home about 12.30. Lunch early. About 3 o'clk. I rested on my bed for an hour - then dressed and went to M. Marquand's, Cor. Mad. Ave. \+ 68\+ S\+. to meet the Advisory Com. composed of strong, level headed business men - appointed as advisors of the Ways \+ Means Com. The meeting was a very important one. The Ways \+ Means Com. was present, also Wood, the Tre\+., Jas Hart V.P. \+ Dielman Cw\+ \+ Building Com. Altogether there were 20 or 25 persons present. Bishop Potter presided. The object of the meeting was to consider \+ advise upon the plan of a temporary building for schools \+ storage, to be erected on the new site, as worked out by Jos. Hart. This will cause an expenditure (estimated) of $17000 - Carrere had plans of proposed permanent bldg. \+ explained the Hart plan first - \+ then, as a substitute, a plan of erecting a portion of the permanent School Bldg. at an expenditure of about $100,000 - thus avoiding an expenditure of $117,000, no part of which could be saved after it had to be removed. Mr. Hart explained his plan \+ its incidental advantages at length. Then Messrs Schieff. Jevr Morton, Seth Low - Wm E. Dodge Chas. Barney \+ others spoke. Pres. Low voiced the general sentiment when he advised building the temporary structure at once \+ at the same time bending every energy to putting up, without delay, the north facade on 110\+ S. - the finest part of the building on the most valuable part of the lot - This was approved on a vote. It means, 20,000 for temporary building, the immediate commencement of main Galleries on 110\+ S. at a cost of $300,000 with only $170,000 in sight at present.
