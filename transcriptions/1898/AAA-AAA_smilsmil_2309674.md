## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 25, 1898

**Weather:** Bright, cool & breezy  **Temp:** [illegible]

mollie Adams ret? to New-Haven This m'f
Miss Sickly arr'd. This P.M.

Immediately after bfst. I went to Dr. Yales - He gave a little more attention than usual to my case - says that he thinks I am suffering from an inactive liver & mal-nutrition. It was after 10 when I left him - Then walked to the Studio - was there for a few minutes then to the N.A. where I found Collins at work upon his "Trial balance sheet" - I worked there until near 1 going over the accts since the sale of the N.A. & carefully picking out every transaction that has to do with the money rec'd. from the met. life ins Co. its arrangement into funds & the present condition of those funds - I find that in many ways my instructions given to Farrington were not followed, altho' he always assured me that they were. Home to lunch at 1.15 - back to N.A. at 2.30 & continued the work of the m'f until 5 o'clk. Mr. Appleton, a delinquent mortgager to N.A., called & promised money - not Tue'd. - I went to St. Charles & bought a set of Rollers for Ralph's skates - three to Wermints & said frames not yet gilded, for my 24×50 "Showers" - then to Studio & home late for dinner - Miss Sickey, a friend of Bessie Throope arr'd from 6'k This P.M. - Mollie Adams returned to New Haven This m'f. Eve'g I read to Jim & Ralph until 9 - I was not well enough to go to the joint meeting at the N.A. to secure, if possible, harmonious action with reference to a Fine Arts Commissioner nomination by artists to the Paris Exposition in 1900.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 26, 1898

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got to Studio at usual time & spent a little time with Maynard who told me how successfully the meeting at the M.A. went off last we'k. - a very full meeting which unanimously endorsed Candwell for nominee for Art Com'r. to the Paris Exp'n. of 1900 - I did not do any work during the w'k as I was not in working trim. Home to lunch at 1 & soon after, went to N.A. where I met the two Examiners, Hungerford & Bullock & I worried out the rest of the afternoon with them & the Academy accts. It was not until after 6 that I got home - Dinner - In the early eve'g. I was surprised & pleased by a call from Dielman who, having heard that I was sick, had called to see how I was. I read awhile to Ralph & Jim & got to bed early.
