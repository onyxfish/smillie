## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 24, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thanksgiving Day.

Shocking bad day — dark \+ rainy during the m'g — Rain changing to snow during the afternoon — Got down to parlor soon after 10 \+ tried to settle down to work but I did more trampsing back \+ forth, like a caged bear, than I did of writing. A great load of work has piled up against me since I fell ill \+ I find that it requires a great deal of courage to attack it deliberately — Lunch \+ the usual time \+ then a little afternoon nap. Ev'g., Charl was so determined that I should share his Thanksgiving dinner \+ the family Housewarming of his new bachelor home 29 E. 38th S — That at 6.30 he sent a carriage \+ his big ulster for me — So, in spite of the snow storm I went — Of course, it was a fine display — the first time that I had been in the house — 13 of us sat down to dinner — He at the head of table, I at other end — Many \+ her girls, Kate, Ge[illegible] Nellie, their three boys \+ ney two — we sat at table until 9.15 I got to be very tired — I went up to the parlor for a few minutes — Then Conpie was called \+ I was taken home — I got to bed by 10 o'clk. but it was after midnight before I got to sleep.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 25, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day — clear sky \+ brisk wind from W. \+ N.W. I got up before 10 \+ gave myself a brisk rub-down with hair mittens \+ then a sponge bath, the first that I have had strength enough to give myself for a long time — Then a rest \+ then to parlor where I spent some time in writing out checks \+ other business of that kind for Frances, while aut. A. Porter sat \+ waited. Then I went out \+ walked on the sunny side of Park Ave., with more comfort than at any time within the past two months — At 1 o'clk. had lunch with my family — Then some writing until Yale came about 3.30 — After he left I had a nap — Dinner with the family at 6.30 — Ev'g. Charl came in but I got to bed early.
