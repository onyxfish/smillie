## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 10, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet day - Rain commenced at about 3 A.M. \+ fell steadily during the m'g. \+ in light sprinklings until wet. W.G.P., Bessie \+ Mabel left in the early m'g to go on some sort of an excursion down the valley, to be gone all day. I devoted myself to my trunk - first mending a broken hinge \+ then repacking it from the bottom. Dinner at 1 - after dinner I walked to the station of D.\+H. Co. at foot of street \+ got information concerning trains to Albany - then home - Spent most of the P.M. in P.'s "Den" looking through his collection of books - later I went out \+ prowled about in the neighborhood taking note of the residences \+ the general style of architecture which is not interesting. Tea at 6.30 - I had a light attack of pain after 9 P. M. Park \+ the girls got home after 9 -

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 11, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Stanwix Hall. Albany
Rooms 37 - 38.

Beautiful day - quite cool in the m'g - Mrs. Park too unwell to leave her room. After bkf. I Rode down town with P- got D.\+H. mileage book etc - etc - It was 11 when we got back to the house - then we drove out to the Country Club House \+ on our way back got the boys who had been to a pond sailing boats - To the house \+ finished packing trunks \+ had lunch at 1 o'clk - Said our good byes at 2 \+ with P. \+ Freer Rode to the T R Station \+ took 2.24 train - an au interesting ride until we got over the N.Y. state line - then the River Valleys were very pleasant to look upon - at 5 o'clk at Hinesveh changed to train from Bingham - much faster - at Oneonta, at 6 o'clk had supper - got to Albany at 8.45 \+ went to Stanwix Hall - There I discovered that I had lost the Fountaine pen that Annie gave me years ago - I went back to the station \+ hunted thro' the car out without success - it made me sick
