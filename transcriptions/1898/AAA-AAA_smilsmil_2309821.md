## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 22, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark, miserable day with heavy rain at night. I went to studio + to work on W. C. - "On Susquehanna" Mr. Frank Fowler came in with his sister. He called to enquire about his picture sold at N.A. + not yet paid for - Home to lunch at 1.30 went to N.A. after that + was detained there by the work that I found to do - Before to went up into the galleries + took a rapid look at the collection of of Portraits of women (+men) now on exhibit there - Met Mr. Chas Barney there + we had a few moments comparing opinions - Then home to dinner + a quiet even'g at home.
I have not seen Charl to-day, but he is so much better that he went down to business, but taking every precaution

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 23, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cleared to be a charming day - some clouds + a little fleying shower in the mff. but the p.m. + even'g was cloudless. June upset things in the mff by being sick, first with severe pain in bowels + diarreale + then, vomiting. After bkf: I went to Yall's + was much surprised to find him in bed - Pfersler was there, watching the progress of his attack of appendicitis, + not yet sure but that he may have to perform an operation - I saw if for a few minutes - Then I went to Dr. Wood - at 8 E. 49. S - found him in + made an appointment to meet him at my house at 2 o'clk. Then I went to studio + worked on W. C. - until 1 ilk - Then home to lunch - to studio at 3 and worked until 4.30 - Then to Pietranisi + bought books for Xmas presents + home at 6.30 - dinner, even'g. Mary, Lergires + Kate. We went to the Opera - I played Halma with June as he put up in his bed - Dr. Wood came at 2 ilk + looked Jim over — a case of acute indigestion - even'g, he was much better -
I got to bed early.
