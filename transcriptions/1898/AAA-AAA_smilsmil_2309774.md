## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 13, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another superb day — not so cool as yesterday — Still feeling out of sorts which was increased, no doubt, by the fact that by drugs I had a complete \+ weakening evacuation of bowels. About 10 Ralph \+ I started for a short walk — Jim was too lame yet to join us. We went over the Trail thro' the Ravine under Sunset Ledge. I thought that the ledge was short, a sort of island, but I went on \+ on \+ on until to my surprise we came out at Chapel Pond. I was then very tired \+ weak \+ when I got out on to the Road I had so much pain that I sat down on a log \+ asked R — to go on to the hotel \+ get a horse. \+ Return for me. In about half an hour the pain passed \+ then I trudged on \+ when I got to the clearing where we had entered the Trail, about 8 or 10 minutes from the hotel, I met Jim \+ Ralph with horse \+ wagon — I got in \+ got to hotel at about 1 o'clk. I Rested on my bed until nearly 2.30 \+ then ate a very light lunch. After that I sat in the sun for awhile then to my Room \+ on my bed until near 6. Dinner at 7 — in parlor for awhile — then to my Room \+ to bed early after a very uncomfortable \+ discouraging day —

Some pain after the exhaustion of a long walk as described above.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 14, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Slightly overcast most of the day, with signs of a change of weather. As the sun set a beam of light fell upon the upper part of the Giant and it glowed with superb sunset color. I made the day one of rest \+ did not go beyond the piazzas of the hotel where I took my exercise. I wrote part of a letter to Mary Cook in the m'f'g. Lunch at 1.30 — wrote to Granniss \+ sent him a check before 3 o'clk — then a long nap — then a walk on the piazza \+ dinner at 7 — Ev'g. Read until 10.

no actual pain to-day but a feeling of weakness \+ an uncomfortable feeling over the bowels as well as in the stomach.
