## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 3, 1898

**Weather:** Clear or very cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

After getting to studio I got out an old canvas 24"×50" that I had prepared more than 30 years ago, to paint upon it a view from under Meledge under the Kaaterskill fall, + I got also, a study of storm clouds at sunset, painted at Stockbridge in 1868 — + then I made a very rapid sketch in color on the big canvas without any preparatory drawing — It was great fun when I got the sketch finished at 1 oCl. it was more suggestive, probably better, that it will be at any subsequent stage. Afternoon I commenced the painting left roughly over about half of the sky. About 5 oCl I went to Carrère + Hastings Cor. 41 St + Mad. Ave. Maynard there — Saw Hastings + arranged for meeting of Bldg Com. There to-morrow P.M. at 5 — Mew Maynard + I walked to Century. I expected to find the Ways + Means Com. there, but for some reason it was not — I shall have to suspect by this that I am not recognized as a member, as, so far, I have had no official communication from the com. Home at 6 — dinner — Walk after + went to N.A. + met D.P. Collins there to consult with him concerning two books which, he tells me, are now practically "written up" — left there at 10.30 —
Charl took the girls to a Theatre —

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 4, 1898

**Weather:** Very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Worked all day on 24"×50" canvas which I shall call "Showers". and am, on the whole, rather pleased. I got over the whole of the sky surface. Mr. Cauldwell came in to see Maynard who was out — After lunch I went to the barber's + had my hair cut. Then to studio + painted until after 4 — cleaned up + went to Carrère + Hastings — 41 St + Mad. Ave. Meeting of the Building Com. Definite orders were given by the Com. to begin the work on the 109' S? portion of the drawings — That portion of the buildings to be erected as soon as possible. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. went to Ed.W. Cook's bid Catherine goodbye — she is to sail for Gibraltar with memmele + aunt W. + Mrs Carl Sprague early to-morrow m'g. Then I went to Walters + saw there Mother Sprague, Carls S. — his wife + others. Then to N.A.D. to see Gee. Ward + take a look at drawings in life class + make selections of students for my Etching class — A "students function" was under way + so I failed to get what I wanted — I found D.P. Collins, who is writing up the Academy books, preparatory to handing them over to Grannis — as drunk as a lord — I had a few words with him + then cut him off — On my way home stopped at studio to see my painting by gas light — my sketch "After the Storm" was taken to the Century this m'g.
