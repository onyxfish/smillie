### Wed. Nov. 2, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant day. I passed last night comfortably \+ awoke feeling fairly well altho' still much inconvenienced by the effects of Quinine \- a light bkfs't. at 8.30 \+ then, before my fresh dose of 15 gr? of Quinin got in its work I managed to do some. I prepared my pfo. of Meas't. Report for the printer, wrote to Jeb etc. etc. \+ at 11.30 I had my bed bath. In the afternoon Yale \+ Gerster came together and appeared greatly pleased at my condition.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 3, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary, Kate \+ the girls arrived

Very pleasant day \- Soon after 1 A.M. Charl sent a note stating that the "Lahn" had arr? \+ he was going at once for "the girls." They arr? soon after 10. Nicoll was with me at the time but he soon left \+ then I saw them. They all looked pretty well dragged but said that the voyage had been comparatively comfortable. I passed the day as usual in bed but had no pains, fever or other unpleasant symptoms. Yale made his usual call \+ pronounced every thing satisfactory. When I write that there were no unpleasant symptoms I accept the discomforts of quinine as inevitable, since I took the drug deliberately. The distress in my head \+ ringing in my ears was very great. Nevertheless, I managed to dispose of a little of the work that is piled up ahead of me.

In the afternoon Mr. Gerster sent to me a great bunch of beautiful Roses.
