## Left Page

### Fri. March 11, 1898

**Weather:** foggy & raw east wind most of the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

foggy & raw east wind most of the day — some sunshine & warmth — to studio for a few minutes — then to 6th Ave. & 34th St. to Buskirl's, about my letter that he is to print — he promises proof by 12 — Then to N.A. & addressed envelopes to the N.A.'s who were at the meeting Wed. Eve'g. To studio before 12 \+ wrote for Ways & Means Com — To lunch at 1. To studio at 2 — Proof came at 3 — At 3.30 I went to 4th Ave. & 26th St. \+ made a hasty sketch of the street looking west, giving the Tower — Then strolled about for some time \+ to studio about 4.30 \+ worked until near 6. 24×50
frame from Williment's came — Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g Ralph helped me get my letter to N.A.'s, relative to Tom Moran's attack ready to mail, \+ I mailed them. Chad came in for a while.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 12, 1898

**Weather:** overcast most of the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast most of the day — sunlight, or, sunshine struggling thro' two or three times — To studio at usual time \+ to work laying out lines of composition for Mad. Sq. Garden Tower. First on paper \+ then, by scale, on canvas 24"×40". Went over chalk outline on canvas with brush \+ brown paint — did not get quite finished before I gave up work for the day at 4 o'clk — feeling very listless \+ "springy". Home \+ had a rest before dinner — Eve'g. with the boys until 9 o'clk. At 9.30 went to Century — had a talk with C.H. Turner \+ Frank Jones concerning my circular note sent out yesterday — which leads me to think now that I acted with unnecessary haste — It wd. have been more effective \+ dignified if I had asked for a com. to take charge of the coming audit, to investigate fully \+ to report at the annual meeting. Home at 11, but it was midnight when I got to bed.
