## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 23, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — Dr Yale came in the morning \+ sat chatting by my bedside for some time I got up \+ dressed in time to go down to dinner of which I ate very sparingly. In the afternoon I wrote to Dielman a letter concerning the building of the new N.A. building — I had several callers \+ was kept pretty constantly at work. I began to feel very tired late in the afternoon \+ my stomach began to pain — I ate a light supper — Geo. came in \+ then Char \+ at 8.30 my pain was so considerable that I had to go to bed taking a hot-water bag with me for comfort.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 24, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another miserable day of fever — after I went to bed last night I had a little sleep \+ then was awakened by a rise of temperature without the slightest evidence of chill preceding it but I had great pain in my head, my shoulders \+ my loins — my Temp. went above 103° at about 4 o'clk. where it began very slowly to fall but it did not get down to normal during the day. Dr Yale came in the m't. and I passed a day of misery in bed, changing to "lounge" in front room while my room \+ bed were being aired.
