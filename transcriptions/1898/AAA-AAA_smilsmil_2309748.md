## Left Page

### Sat. July 23, 1898

**Weather:** Clouded  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded \+ muggy during early part of the day. Jim went for Dr. G. before bkf. R's temp. was less but he was evidently very sick \- Dr. came at about 9 \+ prescribed for him saying that he thought it was nothing more serious than indigestion. Dozier at work trimming hedges, bushes \+ tares all day. I spent a little time at the house \+ then returned to Ralph \+ fixing a table wrote letters until after 12 \- Dinner at 1.30 wrote again in the afternoon. Ralph continued improving \- he got up, dressed, \+ went to table at suppertime. \- In the eve there were recitations by a young girl \+ Jessie Edgar sang some of her songs very charmingly. It was after 1 when I got to bid \+ my bowels routed me out at 3.30 A.M.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 24, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

another muggy, sticky mfg. \- almost a fog which soon burned away into a fine day \- Afternoon clouded, with thunder storms coming up after 5 o'clk. Ralph got up quite well \- About 10 o'clk. we went to the house \- The boys each had a bath \- I wrote to Dr. Jais, to Nellie \+ to Charl. At noon went to Mr. Coyles and got our wash. To dinner at 1.30 \- About 3 we all went back to the house, slung hammock for the boys \+ we took turns reading aloud from St. Nicholas for Aug. At 6 o'clk. we went to tea \- The thunderstorms did not reach us. After tea a little summer boarding house piazza conversation \- then read Sunday Tribune until 10 \+ then to bed.
