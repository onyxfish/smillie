## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 26, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rather disagreeable — dark, from heavy clouds most of the day, \+ cold n.w. winds. To studio at 9.30 — with an uncomfortable head — so, I made up my mind not to attempt painting \+ I spent the m'f. writing in the big, new book for that purpose, The Record of the m'tgs for Permanent investment of the N.A. To lunch at home \+ after that I spent the rest of the afternoon on my bed. I got up about 5 feeling much better — went to studio, got the new Mr. policies left by Moses Smith in the m'f — also the big book, Record of m'tgs. \+ took them to [illegible] \+ home to dinner. Eve'g — again to the Academy — Special meeting — to nominate a Commissioner of Fine Arts to the Paris Expp'n of 1900. A long wait before a quorum was drummed up — then the business went along with but little hitch. I took a leading part in securing for the delegates to the F.A. federation, freedom of action \+ it shall be made clear at the F.A.F. meeting that 4.3°. That Cauldwell cannot be nominated. After this was disposed of, Beckwith brought up his Resolution to open the Spring Exh'n Sunday afternoons — it really met no opposition. Huntington \+ Jewell voted apt. it — all others — aye. I called the Council together in Special meeting, \+ was empowered to sign, for the N.A. Satisfaction piece on payment of the Adolph Meijer m'tf of $18 500 — Midnight when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 27, 1898

Third anniversary of Annie's death.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Entirely overcast — cold \+ windy. Spent the day painting on Ruins of San Luis Rey. Cal. — a painting that I began some time ago — the oil sketch from which I made the aqua-tint for Vickery of San Francisco. I repainted the sky in the m'f. \+ The Tower in the afternoon. At 5 o'clk. I went to Century \+ attended angular meeting of the Ways \+ Means Com. of [illegible]. Beckwith, Nicoll, Brown, Watrous \+ Lockwood or Forest. Home at 6.30 — Dinner — Quiet eve'g with the boys — Charl came in at about 9 o'clk.
