## Left Page

### Sun. June 19, 1898

**Weather:** Muggy, Thunderous  **Temp:** [illegible]

Muggy, Thunderous m'f. with chilly wind \- a weather combination that is very disagreeable to me. Showers in the afternoon \+ sw'f. Late also! I went to studio for a short time \- It was near noon when Ralph \+ I started out \- Rode up ley. Ave \+ by Transfer across to Lenox Ave. Thence up to Harlem River at about 146 S. \- Took a look at the new River House. Then down Lenox Ave. by Columbus Ave cars, transf to 8th Ave. at 116th St \- transf again to 59th S; cars at 8th Ave. \+ from there to Fourth Ave. cars at Mad. Ave \+ so home at 2 o'clk. having had about 12 miles ride, each of us, for 5 cents \- a good deal of park for a shilling. Dinner at 2 \- Charl came in while we were at Table, but left soon as he is to go to Boston this p.m. with his two Australian wool men. The afternoon being dark and showery I loafed on my bed. \- I wrote a note to Carrere \+ Carrere \+ Hastings concerning the building material for the new Academy building. The architects think of nothing but white marble. In yesterdays Eve'g Post Russell Sturgis has a letter objecting in several ways to white marble for exterior of buildings \+ this is very much in harmony with my way of thinking \- . Every Read aloud to R. Then some writing \- The doctor says that Sallie White's trouble is progressing favorably \+ that R. has no risks in playing with her \- so, They spent the afternoon together.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 20, 1898

**Weather:** Superb  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day \- brilliant, cool \+ breezy \- I spent the m'f at home trying to write my report to accompany the figures that I have prepared of N.A. accts, but between the masons at work knocking a hole in the brick wall of the extension for a closet window \+ the plumbers who have got through to the roof, I did not make much progress. Lunch at 1 o'clk. After lunch I learned that the plumber was about to tear out my washbasin \+ go through closets \+ store room \+ go to the roof, against the east wall of the house, with a heavy vent pipe to take the place of one already there. \- It made me sick. \- I called a halt \- \+ Ryan was sent for \- I have a suspicion that he is imposing upon me \- There is too much blarney. By to-morrow I may surrender. At about 4 o'clk Ralph \+ I went to Keefe Co. \+ I changed some shirts \- Then we went to Cammeyers in 5 yfth Ave. where I bought a pair of shoes for him and two pairs for myself \- Then home. Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g. Read awhile to R \- Then went to 3yn S. but neither Clew? or Walter Cook were at home \- . No word from Jim, which makes me feel a bit uneasy \-
