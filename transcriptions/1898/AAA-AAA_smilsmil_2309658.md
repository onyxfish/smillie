## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 24, 1898

**Weather:** cold \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary ret? from Po'k in the afternoon.

Cold \+ windy. To studio at usual time \+ set palette \+ painted on the Baboli Garden scene. Getting the sky in before 2. At that time I was surprised by a visit from C. S. Farrington — he stayed for half an hour \+ left me more puzzled than ever as to his moral composition. There is no apparent recognition on his part of his guilt — he is always the aggrieved \+ suffering me. Soon after 12 I went to N.A. \+ the 2ᵈ Nat. Bk. — Then home to lunch at 1.5. At studio, afternoon, I painted in a portion of landscape on the canvas upon which I started this m't. Even. Council meeting where we had a spirited discussion over the matter of nomination for commissioner of Paris 1900 — fine arts — Then I spent some time with Collins giving directions about the writing up of the Academy books.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 25, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, raw day that wound up in a wet \+ sleety snow storm. Immediately after b'f'st I went to McWilsee's rooms in W. 36 S. to see him on N.A. business but found that he had started for Penn. at 7 o'clk this m't. Then to studio \+ painted on Baboli Belvidere until 12 — then to Brooks Bro's \+ committed the extravagance of buying a suit, suitable to wear to afternoon teas, etc. Home to lunch at 1.20 Afternoon, altho' very dark, I painted m — R. Swain Gifford called at 4 o'clk. \+ made quite a visit. Home at 5.
Preparations had been made to have Fanny Kimball \+ Emma Ireland dine with us — F. K. — to stay overnight — the party to go to Charl's to hear his instrument — Emma came but F. K. did n't — Charl came soon after 8 with carriage \+ took Emma, Mary \+ Kate to his rooms — Ge°. came just as they were going \+ he went also. I stayed at home with the boys \+ got to bed early.
