## Left Page

### Tues. March 29, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More dull weather until late in the afternoon when it began to rain, continuing steadily. To studio at about the usual time \+ to work on Mad. Tower — fussing now. It was my hope that I might paint it in broadly \+ directly but I am not sufficiently familiar with the subject, my pigments or my tools — I am in need of training \+ experience in every branch of my work. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2. Goodman, one of the etching class, came to take a print of the plate he etched on Friday — His success was not very brilliant \+ some of my time was lost. It was nearly 5 when I went to N.A. Raining then — Hungerford \+ Bullock there — as usual, a lot of errors to correct that kept me from the work that I had gone there to do. Home to dinner at 6.30. Ev'f read to Ralph until 8.30 — then Charl came in \+stayed until 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 30, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull all the m'f, clear in the afternoon. The Sabre was reported as entering Plymouth This m'f. If all is well Mary \+ Party will land tomorrow \+ I hope to get a cablegram from them — Worked all day on Mad. Tower — painting more broadly — less fussing. I am more than ever uncertain as to the value of what I am doing. Home to lunch at 1 — It was nearly 5 o'clk when I got to N.A. Worked there until 6.30 making checks for prizes, etc. — Then home to dinner. Ev'f, the usual reading aloud until the boys went to bed — Then Charl came in — chatted until 10 — To bed soon after.
