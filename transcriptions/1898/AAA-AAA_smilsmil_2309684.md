## Left Page

### Thur. March 17, 1898

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:**

Dull w'f. after the rain last night, but the weather cleared beautifully before noon. — I got up with a severe headache. — I got to studio at 9.30 — passed a very uncomfortable m'f., doing no work, \+ at 1 o'clk — went home \+ to bed — head ache very severe until 8.30 when I got up \+ had a little to eat. Ge°. \+ Nessie came in — then Charl — [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### Fri. March 18, 1898

**Weather:** charming  **Temp:**

Charming day — feel rather slim after the experience of yesterday — Got to studio before 10 o'clk. \+ wrote a couple of letters — at 11.30 I walked up town, stopping at H. D. Allen's in 47° S. he was out — went on to Fine Arts Socy — 57° S. Varnishing Day — went in \+ took a hasty look over the exhib'. which was to me interesting but not up to the level of some of its predecessors. It seems to me that there is more than usual of what is conventional \+ commonplace in it — my picture "After the Storm", (another painting in the collection bears the same title) hangs above a moderate sized picture \+ looked to me rather pretty. The change in its appearance was to me food for thought — it showed the power of environment. Home to lunch at 1.30 — To studio at 2.30 — some more writing — Had a call from Mr. Herbert Weeks, a builder, with letter of introduction from Shearman \+ Sterling — Later W. B. Haines of Caneve \+ Hastings called bringing a bill from C \+ H. for $200 for services. — Had a very interesting chat with him — A short walk before dinner — Evening at home with the boys — the girls went to Charl's.
