## Left Page

### Wed. July 27, 1898

**Weather:** Clouded & muggy  **Temp:** clear & hot after 10

Clouded & muggy - clear & hot after 10 - I went to house before 6&fp. & found the colored woman, Dodge, at work - also Mr. Rhinevault. Warner & an assistant finished Kalsomining the kitchen ceiling - Dosier worked on lane, clearing weeds - I got to house soon after 6&fp. - I wrote to Ryan. Mr. & Mrs. Edgar came & until near 9 o'clk we worked at getting out curtains etc. from the big chest in the attic - putting them up & spreading rugs on the floors - To dinner at 1.30 - returned to house - wrote to Charl & to Grannis & then had a good bath & change - Then to P.O. - Read paper until tea time at 6.30 - after tea again to the house - got soiled clothes & went to Mr. Coyle's with them - Then to P.O. etc. Evely finished reading R. Harding Davis "King's Jackal" which failed to interest me. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 28, 1898

**Weather:** Rain from 3 A.M. until 6. Clouded until after 10.  **Temp:** very damp & muggy

Rain from 3 A.M. until 6. Clouded until after 10. very damp & muggy - To the house very soon after 6&fp. - wrote to Charl - Parke & Woodin - Mr. & Mrs. Edgar came & we had an active m'f hauling things out, especially the bedding - At 11.30 I went to P.O. & to Cooley's - Then again to house & worked there until 1.30 - Then to dinner - again to house at 2.30 - Geo. Woodward came & picked the locks of the two closets of which I could find no keys. I had a long afternoons work getting up the bamboo screens & such like work. Got to Sayre's to tea at 6.30 so tired that I did not dare eat - I rested an hour & then ate - Evely I read Tribune - Wm. Parke came in very unexpectedly & made a short call - To bed about 10.30
