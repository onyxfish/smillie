## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 30, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine weather - charming day - glorious night Bkft. at 8 - went to hill top \+ waited for Simonds but he did not come. In the afternoon got a note from him saying that he would be over to-morrow or Thurs. Then I went to the gorge of the River \+ climbed about there hunting up some of my old studies - I got back to the hotel tired \+ very uncomfortable - penalty for eating a baked apple at breakfast. I was not able to eat any lunch until 1.30 \+ then, some trout that Ed. Young had given me in the mfg. \+ that were cooked. at 1 were spoiled - After lunch \+ in time for afternoon mail I finished my letter to Beckwith, -- later I went with Ralph over the same trail that I had gone over in the mfg. from the hotel down the River \+ out at the foot of the hill - the woods by the afternoon light were very fine - got to the hotel tired \+ very warm - I had a sponge bath \+ a good rubbing down \+ then rested for half an hour - Dinner at 7 - Jim out of sorts - diarreah - But we were all able to attend the seance, of a mind reader - Fanone he called himself, in the Casino - It was really interesting as well as amusing -.

No neuralgic pain to-day, but much bloating of bowels \+ sore feeling caused I think by baked apple.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 31, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm day - cloudy at times - a dash of Rain in the afternoon - warm, clouded evfg., thunder storms to the south. After bkft. I went to my hill but no Simonds there - lounged about on the top for awhile then ret? to the hotel \+ finding a letter from Ryan giving an unsatisfactory acc't of the breaking of the marble slab in the bath room I wrote on the subject to bath Miller \+ Douglass, whose men, Ryan says, were working there at the time of the breakage. Then I enjoyed a very pleasant saunter for a mile or so on the Reserve Road to the lakes - it was very charming - back before 1 - lunch at 1.30 - after that wrote to Kate Leonard \+ sent her a check to pay for supplies for the month - Then tried to get a nap but the flies wouldn't permit it. Then I got up \+ after the shower started for a walk down the valley but gas in the lower bowels discomforted me very much altho' on persisting I walked it off. I went down as far as the old wyant place, now occupied by Rev. Jo. Mitchell. on my way back met Katharine Cook \+ a friend, by the roadside - stopped \+ chatted with them until the stage they were waiting for came along. When I got to hotel I met Rev. Mitchell \+ daughters on their way home having made the round, up John's Brook to ore bed brook - up that \+ over the Ridge of Gothic \+ down by Rainbow trail. - They both looked so strong, he so sturdy, she so lithe, that it crumpled me; poor decrepit dyspeptic, all up into a little heap with envy. Dinner at 7 - Evfg. without event.

No neuralgic pain to-day but considerable discomfort, afternoon \+ again in the evfg. from gas distending the lower bowels. The seat of pain or discomfort seems to be leaving the stomach for lower down.
