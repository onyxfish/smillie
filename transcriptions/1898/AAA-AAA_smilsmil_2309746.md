## Left Page

### Tues. July 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

No rain during the night but heavy thunderstorms in the m'f. The first one soon after 8. About the middle of the m'f. the boys \+ I went to the house but could not get entrance because we could not find Speuse, we went down town \+ to his house. It was near noon when we got the key \+ got in. Then I opened up everything \+ sent Jim for Mr. \+ Mrs. Edgar. They soon came with Mr. Dav. Sayre \+ we went thro' the house from cellar to garret. Mr. \+ Mrs. E. were much pleased \+ will write to Carleton Comstock that he can have the house for 200 dollars. His wife has a son a week or ten days old \+ her D'r. advises mountain air. Dinner at 1.30. After that I ret' to the house \+ wrote letters to Charl \+ to Kingsbury. Then I took them to P.O. \+ again returned to the house. Saw Speuse \+ had some talk with him about terms of agreement &c. To lea at 6.30. The boys have had an afternoon at the base ball match. Ev'f. Down town to P.O., then to Dav. Sayres. Then read m'f. Tribune \+ at about 10, to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 20, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very hot m'f. Thunderstorms came up soon after 2 o'clk — high wind \+ heavy rain for about half an hour — clouded for rest of afternoon. The boys went to Prest'n picnic at Hart Lake, going on 10 o'clk train. I was about the house all the m'f., part of the time with the men who removed the broken water closet \+ moved it. I unpacked trunk — very uncomfortable about the stomach — sometimes in pain. I filled bath tub but could not take a bath. Dinner at 1.30 — had to hurry back to the house \+ was just in time to shut the windows ag't. the storm. I spent a very uncomfortable afternoon there, most of the time on the bed. At 4 went down town for mail \+ paper — back to the house — there until 6. Then to C. Sayre's. The boys came back from the pic-nic with the Dav. Sayre party in carriage having been protected from the storm. Ralph considerably out of sorts — eat very little \+ went to bed with considerable fever. A card party at the house. I was so out of sorts that I escaped. Mr. Comstock \+ her daughter am'? At 8 I went to P.O. Stopped at Mr. Ben. Mulford's \+ saw Fanny Connelle for a few minutes. From 9 to 10 I went in to Dav. Sayre's \+ com. Mueford \+ wife there. To bed soon after 10.
