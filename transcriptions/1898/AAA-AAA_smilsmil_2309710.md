## Left Page

### Sun. May 8, 1898

17° Anniversary four wedding

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy down pour of rain nearly all day
\- a short respite during the afternoon and
rain again in the eve'g. After bkfp-
went up to Yale's to have him take another
look at my ear - The difficulty there is
progressing favorably altho' tis still very
tender. On my return home I wrote
a portion of my Report to be read at
the N.A. annual meeting next Wrl.
Dinner at 2 - Maynard dined
with me - He left at 3.30 - then
Ralph \+ I went to studio \+ then to
the N.A. Home at 6, having to ride
to escape the rain - a quiet even.
at home with the boys \+ to bed early
after writing a letter to Bessie Throal[illegible]

## Right Page

### Mon. May 9, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant weather at last. I got to
Studio before 10 \+ spent the rest of the
m'g in arranging accts. \+ writing my
report for the Academy - Home to lunch
after mailing letter \+ copy of Metropolitan
magazine to the folks in Dresden. At about
2 I got to N.A. where the council were to
act as jury in making school awards. There
was present Dielman, Blum, Watrous, Church
Brown Wood \+ self - There was friction when
we got to the Composition class. To me the
work shown was entirely unworthy of any such
awards as the 1st \+ 2d Bellagarieu Prizes \+ I said so -
all other members of the council present agreed.
Maynard - Instructor of the class was present, also Ward.
m- took the matter very much to heart - the N. Ward
argued that whatever we thought of the work we
were obliged to make the awards to the best that was
offered - as a compromise \+ to please Maynard,
a majority of the council voted the awards on that
ground. I declined to vote - Home at 5 \+ had a
short rest before dinner - Eve'g got to N.A.
soon after 8 — a long session - The school com.
with the matter of awards, took much time \+ I
took more in considering various items of the
Treas'r business \+ the Report I am to make on Wed.
Dielman, as Ch'n of Audit Com. read his report
of the Com. giving its view of the Farrington business
\+ my work in a way that was, \+ is, entirely satisfactory to me - This Report is to be read at the
meeting on Wed. - It was 12.30 when I
got to bed. very tired, acting as Recording Sec'y
\+ Treas'. at the same time is not light work
