## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 16, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull wtf. clearing into a sunny day before noon — but the afternoon clouded over again. I got up between 10 & 11 o'clk \+ went down to parlor feeling very weak \+ uncomfortable in the head — About noon I went out \+ walked in the sunshine in Park Ave. for about half an hour — A painful exertion — lunch at 1. After that I went to the druggists at the expense of nearly all the little strength I had left — On coming in my head ached so that I went to bed. About 6 o'clk. Young Mr. Lee came with complaint inforeclosure proceedings agt. T. J. Jenkins wife on Mtg to Na. pt. for $2100. I signed \+ swore to it. It was 10 o'clk. when I got to bed with a racking headache that kept me tossing all night.

At 5 o'clk. This p.m. Louise Cook's eldest daughter Elizabeth Oakey was married to Herbert Lawton Coffin — Jim \+ Ralph went. The Throop girls were cit.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 17, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day, with Rain. I was in bed all day with a terrific headache. It was torture until after 9 o'clk. when I was able to take a little to eat. The pain did not cease until after 1 A.M. I got to sleep again before 3 o'clk. \+ woke for the day at about 7.
