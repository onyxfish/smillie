## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 29, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wretched day after the fever of last night \+ this m'g. I did not attempt to get up. After the m'g my temp' began to rise \+ culminated in the afternoon reaching about 102°. I had no sign of chill. About 6 p.m. I took a glass of Kumiss which at the time of taking was grateful but two hours later a terrible pain came on, not neuralgic — but with a sensation as though I had swallowed a box of mustard \+ a bottle of carbolic acid \- By 9 o'clk. it was so unbearable that I asked Miss T. if she would take the responsibility of emptying my stomach. She was unwilling, but she went for Dr. Yale. He was out \- Later Jane went again \+ he came about 11. In the mean time Miss T. had given me a dose of Bi-carb. of soda \+ I had a hot water bag ag't my stomach \- This relieved me \- When Yale came he gave me 1 gr. calomel \+ soon after I got some sleep \-

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 30, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dr. Yale came rather earlier than usual \+ was in time to superintend the very complete work of the calomel \- After that he stated his determination to begin a course of heroic (forcible) treatment with quinine \- He gave me 15 grs., the largest dose I had ever taken \- I passed the day in a sort of stupor, partly the effects of the quinine \+ partly exhaustion after the experiences of last night.
