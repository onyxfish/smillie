## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 21, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mallie Adams arr. from New Haven.

Still horrible weather — very dark, windy \+ wet. I got to studio at about 10, having first gone to the Garrick Theatre 35 S., near 6 "Ave. \+ bought three tickets for Sat. matinee for boys \+ self, meh, 19¢. In spite of the darkness I managed to paint in the figures in little painting "The Valley of the Arno". I shall now lay it aside to dry. In answer to message from N.A. I went there at 4 P.m. \+ met the two examiners who have been going over the books — They had a number of puzzling questions to ask \+ also showed me the fact that Collins has not fully written up the books, there being several important omissions. Under their expert cross questioning in view of the complexity as well as importance of the accts. I began to feel quite distracted. Home at 5.30 \+ had 20 m. rest before dinner. Mallie Adams came from New Haven this afternoon for a little visit. Eve'g. — to N.A. Council meeting two important matters discussed — a new lease or rather postponement of delivery of N.A. bldg. to Met. life — from June '98 to June '99 — and a bill to secure exemption of taxation of new site. I had papers from Winckle in which both questions were carefully considered. The extension was agreed to — the matter of the bill was left with us to make an appeal to the assessors first, before attempting to get a bill through at Albany — at about 11 walked up with Harry Watsons.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 22, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still dark, damp \+ uncomfortable — Towards evening the walks began to dry. Got to studio at about 9.30 \+ spent some time writing to Lee on N.A. business — lease \+ exemption from taxation. Then I worked on a couple of frames, to get an "old ivory" effect — they having been unsatisfactorily prepared by D. B. Butler. Home to lunch — Mollie Adams lunched at Mary Coak's — Afternoon got pen sketch \+ etching proof into "the two old ivory" frames. My old enemy — heartburn — has come upon me with such severity \+ to quite use me up. Home about 5 — Mollie at dinner — but in the Eve'g. she went to a Concert with Fred Ireland. I read to Ralph until near 9 — Charl came in —

I passed a night of much pain, stomach, and but little sleep.
