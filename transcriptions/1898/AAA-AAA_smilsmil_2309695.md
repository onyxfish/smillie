## Left Page

### Fri. April 8, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day, bright & mild. To studio at 10 & to work on the accts. Nicole came in and took some time discussing Na affairs. Then I made ready for Etching class this afternoon & went home to lunch at 1 - 2o studio at 2 - all of the class on hand & old Billings the model. He posed & all hands got to work - I tried my hand - but made a very bad mess of it. Young Stahr made a very promising sketch & hit it is for him & then took a cold plate impression - he was very enthusiastic. It was 6 o'clk before I got everything cleared up - then a short walk & home to dinner. Every after reading awhile to the boys we got much of our wardrobe together preparatory to packing for our journey to New Haven. Charles came in at about 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 9, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At Dr. L. Adams' 146 Edwards S. New Haven.

Brilliant day. After bfst. went to G? Central Station, bought tickets to New Haven & left an order with Jackson's Sup. To call for our trunk. Then home & after some preliminary work in trunk packing went to Butcher's, Grocers' and studio - then home & packed trunk & at 1 o'clk sup. man came for it - lunch - & at 1.30 I went to Na, taking journal & ledger with me - made out checks for all bills to date & gave Grannies his instructions. Home at 2½. At 2.30 we left the house, each with our bicycle - went to station - our belongings were checked through & we, being among the first on the train had our choice of seats. Got to N. H. at 4.30 & there, at the station, Katee & Molly met us with their wheels - so we all mounted & rode up Orange St. to the Adams's where we were pleasantly rec'd. Frank Adams is at Ridgefield. Dinner at 6.30. A very quiet day & to bed at 10.
