## Left Page

### Mon. March 21, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow in the m'g, continuing at intervals during the m'g. a dismal change from the spring weather of a day or two past. I got to studio at usual time \+ wrote a letter to Mary to get to Dresden at the same time that she does. Other letters \+ like business took all of my time for the rest of the day. Eve'g. Charl dined with us. Geo. came in after dinner. The arrangement was that a carriage was to come at 9.30 \- C. \+ the sailing party were then to go to the Lahn at Hoboken, where C. would leave them. This plan saves a rush \+ excitement in the m'g. At 8 o'clk. Geo. \+ I bed them goodbye \+ we went to N.A. Corneil meeting where after routine work, Waldon, of the Hanging Com. delivered the Spring Exhibit to the Council. There is but little really good work in it \+ a great deal of distressingly bad work. There is also some very disagreeable hanging, I think. It was 11.30 before I walked up home — my now, more than ever empty home. The house has been pretty well stripped \+ looks barny. The boys beds have been moved down to the 2° story, but some of them I found them when I came in.

Hungerford \+ Bullock were at the N.A. in the eve'g. at work upon N.A. books.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 22, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary, Kate, Katherine \+ Elizabeth sail for Europe.
S.S. Lahn.

Dismal day — rain \+ mizzle all day long. So far as I know the Lahn got away this m'g, carrying Kate \+ Mary \+ her two girls. Charl left them on the steamer last night in good shape barring the fact that Kate's night outfit was in her steamer trunk which was locked up in a store room on the dock. The others had hand bags. Of course, it was a very serious matter to Kate. The boys \+ I had our breakfast at a slim looking table. The emptiness of my home in the loss of Annie is now made more dismally apparent. At 10 I went to studio, there for only a few minutes — then to N.A. \+ attended to some matters there. Then to 2° Nat'l Bank \+ Safe Deposit. Then to 54 Wall St. — saw W. E.D. Cornair \+ gave to him the abstract of title to property, Lex. Ave. \+ 40th S't. Then in to see Charl for a moment \+ home to lunch at 1.30. Most of the afternoon was spent on my bed as I felt quite used up — headache — stomach ache — \+ lots of other aches. To studio at 5, at 6.30, Jim Ralph \+ I went to Ed'd Cooks to dinner. Old Mrs. Sprague \+ Mr. Henry Sprague were there — an entirely informal, family dinner. Chatting until 9 o'clk. — then home.
