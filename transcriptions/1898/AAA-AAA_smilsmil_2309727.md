## Left Page

### Sat. June 11, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brisk shower at about 11 A.M. lighter ones about 9 - a thunder storm about 11 P.M \+ a Rattler later - The day was comparatively cool \+ pleasant - After some time with the plumbers, who have torn up floors and pulled out pipes so that I feel sure the house must be sure of sewer gas, I got to N.A. at about 10.30 - found Ja. Hart there, very mad at Grannis because he had taken a day off without consulting him - He had Davis telegraph to him to come down at once. I spent most of the mng posting up in check book the totals of def. accts \+ checks. Got thro at 1 ock \+ was disgusted to find that I didn't get a balance - Home to lunch - more time with the plumber - Jo N.A. at 2 - Hungerford \+ Bullock there - B. went over my figures of the mng \+ found several errors \+ straightened out the bal. Right - They also wound up the other accts. \+ pronounced them all right - We worked together until 6.30. Home to dinner - A quiet eveg \+ to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 12, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very oppressive day, owing to heat \+ humidity. I did as little as possible \+ just lolled about the house all day without sufficient energy to do anything. Mrs. White \+ Sallie went to her bro's. in the afternoon \+ did not return until after 9 - About 8, with the boys, went by open cable car to the Battery. great crowds going both ways. The motion fanned us pleasantly with a very warm air - We walked about the Battery for awhile - vast throngs of all sorts of people seeking air there. Met Ed\[illegible\], Mary \+ Edu\[illegible\]. Cook there - Sdara just convalescing from mumps \+ not yet out of quarantine - Rather dangerous for her neighbors. Then home by car - as we got in Nellie and Gordon came in, she had a late letter from Gr.? She expects to go to West Port Point, mass. next Wed.
