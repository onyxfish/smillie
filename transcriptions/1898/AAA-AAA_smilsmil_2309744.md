## Left Page

### Fri. July 15, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day with little mitigation of any kind. Soon after 10 o'clk. Davis came from the N.A. to say that Jas Hart was there \+ co? like to have a conference with me. I went down \+ we went over the Benedict, Hungerford, \+ other items of business to be settled. He holds out agt. Benedict, but readily approved of Hungerford bill. It was 12 o'clk before we got through with our business \-, then home \- lunch at 1 o'clk. The afternoon was spent in looking after the men in the house \- in carrying things to the attic that are to be locked up there, \+ to the parlor, things to be packed in the trunks \- I marked a number of the boys underclothes. Dinner at 6.30. [illegible] Edw. Cook came in \- He accepted my offer of the back parlor as a bed room \- Later Charl came in, but he did not stay long. After he left I worked until 11 gathering all the periodicals, filing them \+ packing them in closets to be locked up.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 16, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very hot day. Worked most of the m'g at the studio \+ finally cleared up there. Then home \+ continued the work of sorting \+ separating, taking to the parlor what I am to put in my trunks \+ to stowing away what I am to leave \- a lot of mechanics at work \- the room soon full of them \- plasterers, plumbers \+ carpenters \- painters at work in the hall. Lunch at 1 \- Mrs. White took her last lunch with us \- Very soon after lunch I was seized by a colic \+ had to lie down \- These spasms use me up completely for the time. \- I was on my bed until after 4 \+ then dragged about languidly until dinner at 6.30. Ev'g I managed to wiggle through considerable work \+ went to bed soon after 10.

The plumber fixed things so that Ralph was able to use the new bath tub.
