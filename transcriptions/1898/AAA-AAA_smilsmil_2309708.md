## Left Page

### Wed. May 4, 1898

**Weather:** Raw, overcast \+ disagreeable  **Temp:**

\- I got to studio about the usual time \+ lost no time in getting to work on my NA. accts. Soon after 10 Bullock came, \+ an hour latter Hungerford arrived. I laid out the work that I wished to be done first \+ they spent the day trying to get the Report for the last year into perfect accord but there was a discrepancy that they could not locate altho' they hunted for it two or three hours. I still puzzled over my analysis of the accts. of four years back. I went home to lunch. Jim had invited Seany Plummer to lunch with him and then accompany him to Daly's Theatre to see "The Circus Girl." I paid for Ralph's ticket \+ he accompanied them. I worked at studio until near 4 — then feeling very tired in the head I left H.\+B. at work \+ I went home \+ then out on my bicycle, through the Park up to Grants Tomb \+ home by Riverside Drive, Boulevard \+ Fifth Ave In at 6, a rest \+ dinner at 6.45. Read to the boys until 9 — Charl came in for a little while.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 5, 1898

**Weather:** A clouded dull day with rain at night  **Temp:**

Telegram from Ja°. arrived at Queenstown.

I got to studio at usual time \+ busied myself with NA accts — until a little before 1 — then had a short walk before lunch. To Studio at 2. Hungerford \+ Bullock there — they worried all the afternoon over troubles that I could not understand. At 6 o'clk. I said that I must go \+ they left without getting the "proof" that they had been working for. I went home to dinner \+ at 8 o'clk went to NA. to meet the Auditing Com. and explain to them my accts. \+ Farmington's devices to decieve me. I had my very elaborate \+ complete presentation of NA. affairs over which I have been laboring for some days. Dielman, Frank Jones, Perry \+ Jewell were there. Alfred Jones was not. I labored until 11.30 to make my case clear — I do not know whether I succeeded or not — my audience was not demonstrative. Home \+ to bed before midnight.
