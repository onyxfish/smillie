## Left Page

### Mon. March 7, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day. Practically all that I accomplished at the studio was writing a preliminary sketch of my Report on the Farrington case, to be presented to the meeting of N.A.'s on Wed. week next. After lunch I went to 2°. Nat'l B'k. Then to N.A. where I was detained for some time. It was quite 4 o'clk when I got to studio. Wm Miller then came in \+ I gave him notes of introduction to Geo. W. Edwards \+ to Walter Satterlee. Home after 5 \+ had an hours rest before dinner. My stomach troubled me so much that I ate nothing. No N.A. Council at 8.30 — nothing of especial interest. Home at 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 8, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day. To studio at 9.30 \+ as yesterday — spent all day on Report of the Farrington case. At 11.30 went to the barbers \+ had hair cut \+ head shampooed. Then home — lunch at 1 — to studio from 2.30 until 4.30 — Then home. Elg. Howe there. At Carrère \+ Hastings soon after 5 — Building Com. there — an interesting meeting. Reports from 21 borings on new site — bed rock at distances below the surface of from 7 to 17 feet. Architects think that we may get their specifications \+ estimates based upon them by the middle of April — I dont believe it. Home to dinner at 6.30. About 9 o'clk. went to N.A. Hungerford \+ Bullock there — stayed about an hour with them. To my mind they are not making progress in the direction that most interests me. Home soon after 10 \+ to bed.

After the Bldg. Com. left C. \+ H.'s we went to the Century \+ held a com. meeting there.
