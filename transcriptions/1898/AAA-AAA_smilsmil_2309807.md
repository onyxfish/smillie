## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 22, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright day \+ pleasant, altho' the wind blew from the east threateningly. After a night of pain in my head I could not get up in the m'g., nor was I able to take any bfft. Yale came in before 11 when the pain was passing away \+ he made an effort to clear my left ear of some impeding wax, but I was not able to sit up in such light as was needed \+ he gave it up. My head, with headache and stoppage of the ear is in a very uncomfortable condition. I got up at about noon \+ had some clam broth. Joe came in \+ gave us an acct. of his visit to Vassar yesterday. He was not able to get any assurance of any kind as to what would be done to supply the loss of Van Ingen. Between 2 \+ 3 o'clk. Ralph \+ I went out \+ had a little walk on Park Ave. but there was nothing enjoyable in it for me. The Rest of the afternoon I spent in the Parlor. Kate came from Pokepsie at 3.30. About 5.30 Herbert Smith, son of Moses, came to see me about five ms. that will expire next month on the N.A., 110 E.38th St. \+ the studio. It was near 9o'clk. when Frank Adams came in to make a call. He found only Mary and me. He says that he is still seeking a position as tutor. Rain commenced at about 10 p.m.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 23, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — damp m'g. — dry in the afternoon, but cloudy. I got up before 10 \+ got down to the Parlor where I spent the rest of the m'g. Wrote to Orlando Reede \+ to Lefrand Benedict. Then got to work completing the record of the Farrington episode for N.A. f.d. Archives. Lunch at 1 — after that I took a short walk out but the rawness of the air was disagreeable. Later I had a short nap \+ at 6.30 I dined with the family. Eve'g. Watrous came in \+ chinned with me for near an hour. I got to bed before 10.
