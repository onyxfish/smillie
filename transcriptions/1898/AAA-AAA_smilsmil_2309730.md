## Left Page

### Fri. June 17, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day - I spent much of the m'g at home writing to Dielmann \+ Ja's Hart concerning NA Investments. About noon I went out to Douglass's \+ to Millers \+ spent some time with Rejau, the plumber - Lunch at 1 - Sallie White out of sorts, so that she went to the apartments in 34 S'. Miller came after lunch \+ took measurements for windows in closet under the Butler's pantry - At 2.30 got to Camera \+ Hastings \+ saw Mr. C - Came to an agreement with him as to 2d. payment on acct. Then to Calvercy's - \+ posed to him for my bust until almost 5. Then to Keasbmg[illegible] Co - \+ completed my order for shirts \+ other furnishing for myself - Then walked up home Dinner at 6.30 - the[illegible] a little newspaper reading in the parlor - a little reading to Ralph - \+ then to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 18, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim went to Orange.

Pleasant, breezy day - After my usual m'g duties, \+ writing some notes, I went to the N.A. at about 11 \+ spent the rest of the m'g there, trying to construct a Balance Sheet" from the one prepared by Hungerford \+ Bullock which is not satisfactory to me. I had to give it up \+ wrote to h- that it w'd be necessary for me to meet him \+ go over the matter with him - To lunch at 1 - After that we loafed about, I reading aloud, until 2.45. Then Jim started on his wheel \+ Ralph \+ I, taking J's traveling case, went by cars to foot of Christopher St., getting there a little ahead of Jim - I got his ticket \+ checked his luggage \+ then we bid him good bye" - He is going to Orange to stay with Merrie Bishop for three or four days - Then R. \+ I came up-town. I went to the barbers \+ had hair cut \+ head shampooed Then to hatters \+ bought a couple of summer hats. Home - Dinner at 6.30 - Every Charl came in for a very few minutes - He has been to Washington with his French \+ Australian wool men \+ is very nearly tired out - one of them sailed for France to-day \+ two others remain for a week longer -

Sallie White kept her room to-day - doctor came to see her — thinks it nothing more serious than Tonsilitis.
