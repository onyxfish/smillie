## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 7, 1898

**Weather:** Still very mild \- somewhat foggy \+ threatening  **Temp:**

I got up feeling fairly well \+ put in a good days work on Showers, painting over a part of the sky \+ laying in all of the landscapes. I got through at 4.30 \+ went home feeling very tired \- I rested for an hour \+ a half before dinner but when I got up I felt very uncomfortable about the head \+ shaky generally. After dinner I went to Council meeting N.A. \+ did not recover myself at all. There was an interesting discussion concerning school matters, rules, regulations and discipline \- of which there seems to be very little in the N.A. Schools \- I had a short conversation with Beckwith with reference to a recognition of my position in the Ways \+ Means Com. I am ex officio a member, but as yet have had no formal recognition \- He assured me that it was not intentional \+ that I shall not have chance to complain hereafter. Home at 11 \- walked up with Geo. \+ Watrous.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 8, 1898

The Aitee sisters left this afternoon \-

**Weather:** mild \+ still inclined to be foggy, with a raw tang in the air  **Temp:**

I went to studio at about 10 \+ feeling out of sorts \+ disinclined to work at painting I spent some time writing business letters that have accumulated. Before noon Moses Smith came in with fire Ins. policies. At noon went to N.A. \+ worked there for some time \- Home to lunch late \- The Aitee girls left before 2 o'clk. I rested for some time after lunch \+ went to N.A. again after 3 o'clk. and worked on Record of Mtgs until 5 o'clk. when there was a meeting of the building com. all present but Wood. Home at 6.30 \- dinner \- Evelf, Mary, Kate \+ the girls went to Charles' \- the Lanes \+ detrudes there. I was at home with the boys and got to bed early.
