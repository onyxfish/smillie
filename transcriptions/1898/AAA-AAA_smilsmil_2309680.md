## Left Page

### Wed. March 9, 1898

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - At studio in the mtfs + wrote out Report on the Farrington case - at about noon went to N.A. - Home at 1 - to studio at 2 - Pasting letters \+ Farrington documents into the file \+ at 4.30 went home \+ had a rest before dinner - Eve'g. Regular meeting of N.A. I Presented Farrington Report and Tom Moran insisted in a most offensive way in questioning me as to just how he managed to take such a lot of money without my finding it out - I attempted to answer him \+ then Beckwith \+ Perry tried to explain but he declared all answers unsatisfactory \+ persisted in obstructing the business of the meeting until he was shut up. In spite of good feeling \+ sympathy shown for me by many it was a disagreeable ordeal for me - I read the remarkable letter that I recd. from Farrington announcing that he had been appointed a local agent for the N.Y. Life Ins. Co. - with offices in the Mutual Life - Mad. Ave. \+ 23d. S: Hungerford \+ Bullock were at the Academy for awhile in the eve'g to get certain data - [illegible — ~6 words]
Home \+ to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 10, 1898

**Weather:** Mild, Spring-like day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mild, Spring-like day - to studio at usual time - I am so much incensed at Morans behavior last night that I wrote a letter explanatory of my position - my responsibility as Pres., \+ my responsibility as book keeper - \+ something of the manner of T. in stealing by cashing from the cash receipts the checks that he deposited - This note I took to Buskerk's, 6th Ave. \+ 34th S: to have it printed met Beckwith - read it to him \+ he approved. When printed I shall send it to the N.A.'s. Home to lunch at 1.15 to studio at 2 - Did some writing for The Ways \+ Means Com. - Went again to Buskerk's at 4.30 - Menlo Centring - meeting of Ways \+ Means Com. - There until 6 - Walked home with Chas. Collins - Was told that my painting "After the Storm" had passed the jury of Socy. of Am. Painters without dissent - Hurrah for the old man - He is not dead yet. Eve'g. read awhile to Ralph, men got out photos that the girls might get views of Cortina - to bed at 11 - Charles came in for awhile - Stuffed up with a cold.
