## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 11, 1898

**Weather:** overcast & mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast & mild. I did a very little painting at studio in the m'f. Most of the time was given to writing — among other things Report of sub-com. on nomination of Fine Arts Commissioner to Paris Exp'n. 1900. To be presented at the meeting this afternoon. To lunch — back at 3 — before 4 went to NA — a meeting of the Council of the NA & the B'd of Control of the Soc'ty of Am. Artists. I read Report — simply a statement of the work of the Com. — & the result of the Reference of the matter to the Fine Arts Federation who tabled our nominations. Then there was a discussion as to what had better be done — Taxon & Barse taking a leading part — resulting in the passage of a Resolution offered by Barse, requesting the M's. of the several Societies of Painters & Sculptors in the city to issue invitations to their several members to a mass meeting to be held at the NA two weeks from this eve'g. I nominated Taxon, Barse & Maynard a com. to prepare a programme to steer the meeting & before I got through, in spite of my protest, found myself on the com. At 6.30 walked up home with Watson. Dinner — Eve'g after reading awhile to Ralph I went, before 9, to NA & with Ward went into his life-class to select a few of the most promising students for my etching classes. I was not very successful but found 3 or 4 who may be good enough. Left at 9.30 — went to studio — Home at 10.30

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 12, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A continuation of the same weather as we have had for some days past. To studio at about 10. Diano serious work but wrote letters — picked up things about the place etc. To lunch & back at 3. Soon after 4.30 Taxon & Barse came, then Maynard — until 6 o'clk. — we planned for the meeting that is to be called for Friday eve'g Feb. 25th. when an effort will be made to unite upon a name to be recommended for Fine Arts Commissioner to the Paris Exp'n. of 1900. We agreed upon a programme, naming the presiding officer — the nominations — committee to draft letter — & the bearer to Washington. Then home to dinner — After — read to Ralph. To Century at 9.30 — Saw La Farge, Dielman & others concerning the meeting above referred to — then home before 11.
