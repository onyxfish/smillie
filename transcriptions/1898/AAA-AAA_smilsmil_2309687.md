## Left Page

### Wed. March 23, 1898

**Weather:** Dull, raw day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

to studio at 9.30 \+ until near 12 writing — among other things, sent notices to 8 students (N.A. f D.) to meet at my studio on Friday at 2 P.M. to form the Etching class. At noon went to N.A. — there until 1 — then home to lunch — to studio at 2.30, until after 4 — clearing up things there. Then to Bormann's — Bicycle shop — then to Carrere \+ Hastings' \+ saw C. — about marble. etc. etc. — then home \+ dinner at 6.30 — a very quiet affair. Read aloud to Jim \+ Ralph until Charl came in — he stayed until after 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 24, 1898

**Weather:** More disagreeable weather — dull \+  **Temp:** [illegible]

raw all day — now \+ then a little spit of rain — All of my day was spent at studio getting things in shape for my etching class that is to meet there to morrow P.M. except an hour or so in the middle of the day when I went to the N.A. \+ was engaged there. Before 5 P.M. I went to Barriere \+ Hastings with the report of proposals on Putnam Co. N.Y. marble, saw C. H. \+ Brainerd \+ had another look at the drawings in progress. Dinner at 6.30 with the boys until 8 — then to N.A. \+ had a long session with Hungerford \+ Bullock. It was midnight before I got home. The Result was discouraging to me — they have done an immense amount of work with which they seem to be well pleased but after all it is not the plain, easily understood record that I wish — there is too much "book-keeping" — I see no other way than to dig \+ dig for myself — a mountain of work before which my heart sinks —
