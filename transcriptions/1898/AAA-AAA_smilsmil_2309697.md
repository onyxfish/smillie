## Left Page

### Tues. April 12, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, but very windy - Ralph & I got up early & rode into town on our bicycles & I telegraphed to Margaret White: "Your proposition accepted on general terms - will settle details later" — Then home to bkfp. I spent most of the m'g. working on M.a. accts., having brought check & Petty Cash books with me. The boys went to the top of East Rock on their wheels. Lunch at 1 — After that, a rest until 3 - Then Molley, Ralph & I went to The Elkins' - Jim has trouble with his stomach & stayed at home - The E's not in - we took the golf sticks & went to the links & finding free ground we played I made shocking bad work of it, but had some fun & lots of exercise - we were overtaken & passed by some excellent players - At about 6 we went to The E's. They had just returned - Jim was there. Then home, where I had a rub down & a change & dinner at 6.30 - Jim took no dinner & later in the eve'g employed his stomach, to his relief - about 9, feeling tired & heavy, I went to bed with Ralph.

Jim's stomach upset & he had trouble during the night -

## Right Page

### Wed. April 13, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To the top of East Rock on bicycle.

Superb day - up at 6 o'clk. Started on my wheel a little before 7 - strong north wind that discouraged me at first but when I got under the lee of East Rock I found it very pleasant - As the road was for some distance out of the wind I determined to explore a little up the East or "English" Road. I found the grade very even & not very hard & after having gone perhaps 1/4 or 1/3 of the way up the Rock, finding a quiet, sunny spot - I rested 5 m. Then the spirit of exploration drove me on & I did not rest again until I got away on the north side of the Rock, quite 2/3 or more of the way up - Then I took another 5 m. rest & then to the summit without another rest - getting up in just 27 m. from the foot, or 17 m. running time in three heats. I coasted the top 10 m. - There had a fine run down the Rock getting to the house in scant 15 m. I had time before bkfp. to take a rub down & a few minutes of rest -
During the m'g. I wrote some letters & worked over my bicycle, finding that a steel cutting had punctured my rear tire - it was like a small, crooked tack. Dined at 1.30 - After that, with the boys went to a Repair Shop to have my tire mended - I waited - The boys went home on in half an hour set? with Molly & they are had a sauntering ride. Getting home before 5 and Then I rested - I had kept up well during the day - but after I lay down on the lounge a feeling of exhaustion came upon me that was really distressing. I forced myself to get up & go through table manners at dinner but was glad to go off with the boys & I got to bed at 9.15

Jim's upset early this m'g. has kept him quiet. Katey & her W.m. went to N.Y. this p.m. on their way to Washington - H. lunched with us - W. came with carriage & trunks later & they went on to the R.R. Station.
