## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 15, 1898

**Weather:** Cold, raw + overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, raw + overcast \- Rain began at about 5 p.m. Spent the m't. second painting of the Valley of the Arno. Tried to get a sky, light free, + having color \- used palette knife \- don't know whether what I got was worth the getting or not. when I went home to lunch I got a letter from Lee + Lee enclosing check for $1899.5. full payment of the mortage \- so I had to go to Na to 2°. Nat'l. Bank \- other business detained me at the Academy so that I did not return to studio \- F.W. Wood gave an evidence of his faith in me \- I sent to him for his signature, a check for $17000 &, thoughtlessly, did not explain to him anything \- + without a question he returned it to me with his signature. Before 5 o'clk Bank Examiner Kimball's assistant came to Academy + with me took a look over the books + arranged to begin an examination of them on Thurs. At 5.30 I went to studio + cleared things up there for the night + at 6 o'clk. went to Mr. Wh[illegible]'s apartments in 36th St. \- met him there by appointment. We talked over several matters \- foreclosures of mortgages \- new lease from Met. Life \- + exemption from taxation. It was near 7 o'clk. when I got home to dinner. Eve'g. Reading to Ralph + my newspaper.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 16, 1898

**Weather:** Clear + cold with a high wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear + cold with a high wind \- To studio before 10 to work on Valley of the Arno + painted in all the landscape + the architectural foreground except the pedestal + figure on the extreme left. Home to lunch \- after lunch Emma D. came bringing her father's flute for Jim + a book of instruction \- worked without interruption until 4.30 when a writer from Met'e. Mag. came to get material for a short article in the Water Color Soc. \- also a reporter from The World who told me that J.A. L. Gardner had been arrested last night + was up before a police magistrate this m'g. + that Farmington had been to the World office + told a pitiful story of how there had been a conspiracy against him managed by Gardner who had made some errors of + \- in his booking, that had resulted from overwork \- to appear as dishonesty + misappropriation of the A. funds to his own use. He is determined to have the whole thing reviewed with a view to justifying himself + making it evident that he is a wronged + entrapped man. At 6 I went to Garrick Theatre. 35th. near 6th. Ave. \- to get seats for boys + self for next Sat. p.m. \- to see "The Little Minister" \- but found that the run is so great that I cannot get the seats I want before Nick 9th. Dinner. Eve'g. Read to Ralph until 9. Charl came in for a little while \- I fussed about until 11 \- Then to bed.
