## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 16, 1898

**Weather:** Cloudy day & threatening but only a few drops  **Temp:** [illegible]

of rain fell. - I was up early - bkft - after 8 - we waited about for some time to see what the weather was to be, then went over to the Chappel Pond Road & went a little way on it - then back & along the trail to the foot of Giant Leap fall - again back & went to the Cottage of Miss Kate Hillard & called upon her. Her young friend, Miss [illegible] was with her. we had a short & pleasant call & then to Hotel & had luncheon at 1.30 - Got a letter from Nellie - After lunch I did not feel like starting off again & I loafed about & had my hair cut - At dinner time I did not feel well enough to take any thing & the boys dined alone. I was very uncomfortable for a time - defferent from the attacks of pain that have been bathering me lately - I feel better about 9 o'clk. & sat on piazza for a time — then in parlor & read until 10.30

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 17, 1898

**Weather:** A day of cloud & sunshine & two or three brief showers.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkft- after 8 - It was about 10 when with Jim & Ralph I started for Chappel Pond - J. had his fishing tackle - got to the Pond about 11 o'clk - hired a boat for an hour & rowed about the pond - some beautiful [illegible] effects of sunshine & shadow over the cliffs, steep mt. sides & tree forms - At 12 o'clk. started on our return & got to Hotel shortly before 1 - Rested until 1.30 & then had our luncheon. During the afternoon I did a good deal of loafing. I am surprised to find what a number of people there are here who know me & come to me, I being too stupid to recognise them - among others Dr. Janeway came to me - & also Powell who was visiting the Altwater in New Haven when I was there in the spring - In the eve'g there was an exceedingly slow show in the parlor - a stereopticon exhibition of photo lantern slides - a tour of the Adirondacks. Mrs McGee (Hattie Vanthorn) was another who proved my stupidity, for I did not know her - she brought her husband & we had a very pleasant little visit - Miss Wilson, too, had to introduce herself - I am mortified in this way constantly.
Reede brought to me a young man to clear the small growth from my lot. I arranged with him to start the work to-morrow.
