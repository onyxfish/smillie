## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 20, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. fell to 45° last night - Brilliant morning - beautiful day - It was after 10 when I got to my hill \+ found Young chopping \+ clearing up. I staid, directing for some time, then sat in the shade of a tree \+ read Tribune for an hour. Then to hotel \+ wrote a letter to Charl. - we had luncheon promptly at 1 o'clk - After it I wrote notes to Swither \+ Brennan who have not paid mtg int. due on the 1st ins\[t\]. It was 2.45 when J\. R\. \+ I started for the Noon Mark Trail - we found it easy to follow \+ some of the woods were very fine - Before we got a quarter of the way up Jim gave out. Ralph was better \+ more enterprising - he pushed on, anxious to get to the tops - We left J\. on a log \+ climbed until we got to a Rock that gave us a splendid outlook to North \+ East - We went on some way beyond \+ met a party descending - They told us that we were yet half an hour from the tops - As it was 4.45 this discouraged us. To go to the tops would not leave us time to get back to the hotel, rest \+ dress before dinner so we turned back \+ soon met Jim who had followed us slowly - R\. \+ the went up to the Outlook Rock - Then down \+ got to hotel at 5.30 - I had no trouble with my stomach until near the end of the tramp - then it commenced misbehaving \+ I was very uncomfortable when I got to the hotel - I went to Room \+ on my bed \+ was getting rested when bell boy came - Sheriff was down stairs - come to make a call - found a note from Katchen Cook who had walked up from the Estes House to see me - Dinner about 7 - dancing in the Casino - there until 9 - Then the boys went to bed, both out of sorts - I cannot prevent their eating improper food.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 21, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day altho' a part of it was cloudy \+ threatening - Thunder-storms near - a light shower at two. Up late, bkfst at 9 - wandered about awhile after it, but head hurting me. I went to bed \+ had some sleep - Then I wrote part of a letter to Kate \+ a note to Katchen Cook.
Dinner at 1.30 - about 3 I went to Sidney Smith's Cottage \+ saw him \+ talked over water-supply \+ other matters - Then I went to Mr. Neilson's house \+ saw his eldest daughter - Mr \+ Mrs. N\. at church - would be in Windrow after service - I went there \+ visited with them - when they came they rec'd me very pleasantly - I talked of Water-supply \+ survey \+ together we went to some of the corners \+ loud works - Men to hotel -
Supper at 6.30 - We \+ a Quartette of Negroes from Atlanta University sang Spiritual Negro Songs, gave Recitations \+ made addresses concerning the institution, its objects \+ its needs - Jos. Twitchell added remarks - a very interesting entertainment. I had a pleasant interview with Mr. \+ Mrs. Twitchell. Later I had a long talk with Mr. McGea concerning my proposed building operations. He told me that my advent had carried consternation to the Neilson Camp - they had begun to feel that I would never appear \+ that my lot would naturally fall into their hands -
