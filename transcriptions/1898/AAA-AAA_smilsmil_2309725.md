## Left Page

### Tues. June 7, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm day with S. easterly breezes. I had many things demanding my attention at home with painters \+ others \+ at the studio — then I went to Richter's in 23d. St. \+ gave order for burlaps with a printed figure in gold bronze \+ light olive-green for hall way wall. Then, after 11, to N.A. where I worked on accts until 1 — then home to lunch. I busied myself about the house until 2.30 where, with Jim, I went to Deem's — we waited for him half an hour, but he had forgotten his engagement \+ gone off — so, Jim went home \+ I went to the N.A. \+ worked until 5 o'clk — then home \+ Jim \+ I went on our wheels up through the Park \+ home at 6.30 — Dinner at 7 — Every. Read awhile to Ralph. Charl came in \+ proposing a ride in an open street car he runs, while went off to enjoy it — I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 8, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day — my duties about the house \+ at the studio kept me until after 10 — when I got to Calverly's — I spent about half an hour there, posing, — then to N.A. \+ worked there until 1 o'clk — then home to lunch — After that went to Carrere \+ Hastings — saw C — half an hour with him — then to N.A. \+ again at work on books — Got so far that I tried to strike a balance, but it would n't come — So, I must go all over the accts of the past year again, to find where the miscalculation has been. Grannis called me to his window to take a look at Farrington who is at a window in the Y.M.C.A. opposite where is is at work auditing the Ass'n. books — a sad commentary this, on the working of the machinery of justice in our city. At 4.30 I went home \+ at about 5 Jim \+ Ralph rode with me thro' the Park \+ home at 6.35 — Dinner \+ soon after 7 — Every. Read to Ralph \+ to bed at 10.30
