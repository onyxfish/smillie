## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 10, 1898

**Weather:** Very Cold & rather unpleasant day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temp. when I got up 99 1/5°. I had a very unsatisfactory night — slept from 11 to 12 — awake until after 3 — hard at work dreaming from that time until 7.30 — B\[illegible\]. in bed — About 10 Grannis sent up check books — passbooks etc — together with a note from Ogden, bookkeeper in 2° Nat. Bk. Stating that the N.A. accts appeared to be overdrawn over $00 doll, upon which I had to get up \+ devote all of the m\[illegible\] to working out a statement for the bookkeeper, explaining how I divide the accts — a statement that he should have had before, but for my illness — lunch at 1 — after lunch, feeling very sleepy I took a nap and after that I worked on the check books and found that I had not after all worked out a proper form of starting the new accts. — The winding up of the old one I think is right — The change necessary will deface the check books \+ mortify me. Dinner at 6.30. Every Ralph read awhile to me from Dickens and then we had a game of Halma.

Bowels behaved badly in the evg.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 11, 1898

**Weather:** Beautiful winter day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

It was 11 o'clk before I got up — there at 12 I went out on Park Ave. for a walk on the sunny side of the way. met Chas. Collins \+ he walked with me for awhile. I was not out for more than 20 m. or half an hour — worked awhile on M.A. check books before dinner at 2 \+ again after dinner. Geo. \+ Nellie \+ Chas. came in for awhile late in the afternoon — Mary Cook was here before dinner \+ took Ralph with her. Kate \+ Jim went to Charl's to tea — Mary \+ her girls, to church — Ralph \+ I had a quiet time together — He read to me, \+ then we had Halma, \+ he beat me.
