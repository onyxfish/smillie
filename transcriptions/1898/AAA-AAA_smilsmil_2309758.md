## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 12, 1898

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** 32 - 58 [illegible]

Cloudy day, but not unpleasant — Rain at 9 p.m. Up at 5.30 after a night of horrible noise \+ uncut. bkft. at 6 \+ at 6.50 took train for Caldwell. Got there at 9.30 — Some beautiful views of the distant Green Mts. before we got to Saratoga I went on boat as soon as we got to Caldwell \+ in a few minutes we started. It was all very familiar to me — It was hard to pass the old Haws Cottage at Huletts \+ when I saw the old store at the Pier where with Annie, Addie Haws \+ Emma Ireland I had had such merry times, \+ when, on the pier I saw the groups of carelessly happy young people a lump was in my Throat \+ I had trouble with my eyes, many memories came back to me with painful vividness. After passing Huletts we had our dinner. Reached Baldwins at 12.50 — took train \+ got to Ticonderoga at 1.20 — went at once on board the Vermont \+ after a pleasant sail, got to West Port at 3.20 — I suffered considerable discomfort \+ pain while on the boat. At West Port we got into one of Kellogg's Stages \+ drove at once to E-town. The boys \+ I being the only passengers — It was about 5 when we got to The Windsor — after my want of Rest last night \+ my long day of Travel I found myself pretty tired \+ stomach sore. As soon as our Room was arranged I lay down \+ rested until 6.30 — after that I got about until about 9.30 Then I went to bed. About midnight Jim got up in a hurry \+ emptied his stomach in short order.
I had a very vivid dream that Annie kept to me \+ that we had a rapturous meeting

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 13, 1898

**Weather:** Glorious  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day. Bkft. about 8. After that I went with Kellogg to the clerk's office in Court house building to get some information concerning arrears of my taxes on the Keene lot. Roberts, the clerk, was not in Town, but I saw tax rolls for 97 \+ could not find my name there. This troubled me \+ I telegraphed at once to A.W. Shields to Treas. at Keeseville — a reply came later in the day stating that tax am't to 5.32 for which aut. I drew a check \+ sent it to him at once. Then the boys being off with other boys, I took a pleasant little tramp out West from town on one road \+ back by another, perhaps a couple of miles — my dinner was delayed until nearly 2 o'clk. waiting for the boys — The afternoon I took another short tramp — I went along the Boquet River \+ found some old study points — one under overhanging oaks looking down the River, the Cobble coming in at the end of a vista — It was not difficult to find altho. of course, the trees were much grown — It was a saddening experience — This walk I took in the aft., the one on the road west, was in the afternoon — At the end of it I took Jim's camera \+ make a snap or two. My old enemy, the stomach pains put in an appearance at about 5 o'clk. much to my disgust as I had felt so very well during the day — The attack was comparatively mild — Tea at about 7 — dottoring about reading newspaper \+ listening to the dance music until 9 o'clk Then I went to bed with the boys.
