## Left Page

### Wed. May 18, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day — Spent all the m'g. at studio writing out minutes of Council meeting May 9' \+ Annual meeting, May 11' — Home to lunch at 1 — R. Rel'. to studio with me \+ for a while we worked together on his Darwin ant nest — About 3. I proposed to him that we go to the Sculpture Soc'y — Ex'b'n in 57' S'— and we went — after about a couple of hours there I went in to Art Students League \+ took a look at the studios there with a view to learning something that I could turn to the advantage of the new Academy School. Alt. Then R. \+ I walked home — Dinner Ev'g. — some reading aloud — after 9 I wrote some notes \+ then to bed. I had intended to go to the Show at Athletic Club, 57' S'. \+ 6' Ave. Prize pictures, but did not feel equal to it.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain in the m'g. from about 9 to 11 — clear \+ warm afternoon — Mnyder storm at 9 P.M. wrote out notes of minutes of last Council then went to N.A. — there until 1 o'clk. working over check book \+ accts. Home to lunch. It was after 3 when with Jim \+ Ralph I went to Rogers \+ Peet's \+ was there with them until near 5 o'clk. selecting suits of clothes, shirts, hats, etc. Then R— \+ I went to N.A. where I remained awhile with Grannis giving him instructions — then home. After dinner I read until 8.30 "Captains Courageous" to R— then I went to see Ed. \+ Mary Cook as I knew that Margaret White was to dine with them. Sawd was at my house this m'g to ask me if I w'd object to his rooming in the house if Margaret W. should take it. I agreed at once but thought that the terms I had offered to her were going to be too easy — I to pay the servants hire or $32.00 a month \+ let them have the use of the house. The thunder storm coming up cut my conference short \+ I ran for home.
