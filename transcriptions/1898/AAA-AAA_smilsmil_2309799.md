## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 4, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — Before bkfs\. wrote a little note of thanks to Mr. Gerster for her gift of Roses — Then gave myself up to quinine — Yale did not come to day as agreed before hand. Up in the afternoon \+ took some walking exercise from front to back Rooms. I have been eating solid \+ nutritious food which has caused me no discomfort, but the constipation continues as obstinate as ever \+ I get relief only by drugs or enemas.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 5, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull mff. with fog, but it passed into a mild pleasant day, which passed with me much as the week before has done. Yale came in the mff. \+ expressed himself pleased at what he terms my "progress". I wish I could feel progress — I have no neuralgic stomach pain but my bowels are obstinately constipated beyond reduction. Quinine keeps my head in an uproar \+ I cannot mark any increase of strength in my legs. I long to get out of doors \+ make a practical test of improvement in that way. I lay on my bed most of the day getting up about 6 p.m. Watrous then came in \+ made a report on three small pieces of property that are offered as security to the U.A. for loans. Eve'g I went down to the parlor for an hour — Last night Kate had all hands up for a good part of the night as she had a severe attack of wind colic. I was not disturbed.
