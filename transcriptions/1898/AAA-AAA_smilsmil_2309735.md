## Left Page

### Mon. June 27, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A horribly hot day. Ralph's [illegible] temp. at 7 A.M. about 1° above normal. Dr. Putnam came at about 8 \+ looked him over very carefully \+ could not feel quite assured that some eruptive fever was not in lurking — his skin is playing all sorts of tricks in the way of irregular blotches \+ discolorations. I got thro' the day with the least possible amt. of exertion, most of my time being given to Ralph — The Dr. came again in the afternoon — temp. higher — 100/5°. Charl came in the early eveg. \+ while he talked with me a violent colic came on \+ I had to succomb. After nearly an hour of pain I was relieved by a movement of the bowels. I got to bed as soon as I could but had a restless uncomfortable night.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 28, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably hot day, altho' not so hot as yesterday, it was muggy \+ steaming. After a night of much broken rest Ralph waked me at about 6.30. I took his temperature \+ found it normal, which was a great comfort to me. Dr. Putnam came at about 10 \+ said that the last chance for more serious than follicular tonsilities had disappeared. Then for the larger part of an hour I poured my miseries into his professional ear \+ he said he would think about it. I did a little writing on my Report — The Plumbers have the water test on the pipes \+ are hammering away on leaks. In the afternoon there was a light shower but Jim \+ I went to Decomps in 33d St. \+ he went thro' the process of having a mask made in plaster — He behaved very well. Charl came in for a few minutes before dinner — another light shower before 8 [illegible — ~3 words]
