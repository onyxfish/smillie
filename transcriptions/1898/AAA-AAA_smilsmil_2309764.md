## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 24, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy Thunderstorm very early in the m'g. - clearing after Bkp't. - very hot - by 11 o'clk. - clouded over \+ cool wind springing up - afternoon, much rain. After Bkp't I went to Scott Brown's office to see him about getting a surveyor - I have lost much time waiting Orlando Reede's movements - on his advice I wrote two notes - one to Simonds, the other to Delong - to be taken to E. town this p.m. - I went to Noormask lodge to see Mr. Neilson but he had gone to the village - then I went to my Pine Knoll, \+ after looking after your for awhile I Rel'd to hotel - at 12 o'clk - an attack of stomach pain came on \+ altho' I took a Bismuth-Morphine powder, it continued quite severely for an hour - at 1.30 I felt well enough to take my lunch - after a rest I spent some time on M.A. business. Two checks have come in \+ I was enabled to pay off the last of the outstanding bills - towards evening I began to feel much better \+ was able to eat my dinner with some comfort. Everett, a young lady favored us with some excellent piano playing. I sat in parlor \+ read until 7.30 - then to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 25, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain during the night \+ in the early m'g. Thunderstorm at 4 A.M. Cleared charmingly during the m'g. Clouded up in the afternoon \+ showers bet. 5 \+ 6 p.m. Bkp't. a little later than usual. It was about 10 when I went over to Mr. Neilson's house - with him \+ Mrs. N. - on their piazza until near noon - I had a very free talk with them telling them what sort of a house I proposed to build \+ what sort of a neighbor I was going to be - I think they were gratified - Miss Parke was present part of the time - about noon I went with N. to my knoll \+ we tried to find some of the line marks without success - It was after when I got to the hotel \+ found that Simonds, the surveyor had come up from E. town \+ had been waiting for me for a couple of hours or more. I had my lunch \+ then joined Simonds \+ his helper, \+ soon after that Neilson came \+ then we commenced the work of a survey commencing at the little fall on the Road. - Pain commenced soon after - I took a Bismuth morphine powder and worried thro' the attack without much inconvenience. Trouble over the west line - It does not measure by two rods, as much as the deed calls for. The men were running the south line when the showers came on \+ we gave it up - Sat in my room \+ read my newspaper until 6.30 - then dressed \+ went to dinner at 7.15 - very soon after dinner, pain came on \+ I could not take a powder but went to my room \+ bore it until it when it passed away - I am getting to be much worried over these persistent attacks - I am trying to get courage to speak to Dr. Janeway about them to ask if he advises me to go to Bkp't at once \+ put myself under treatment.
Prof. Van Amringe arrived to-day.
