## Left Page

### Sun. July 17, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not so warm as yesterday — a very pleasant day. Bkfst. at about 9. I worked most of the mrng about the house handicapped by more or less of the colicky pain that has troubled me so much recently. I was also somewhat depressed by a series of very vivid dreams of Annie that I had last night. She was again with me but so wholly absorbed in Charitable, literary + social work that she paid no attention to me. I awake between the dreams + then falling asleep continued them. In my last dream she was sitting in the midst of a group of women, as though very tired — she had work of some kind in her hands + did not look at me. Edwd Cook came at 10 + stayed for an hour. I invited him to dinner + he returned at 2 + stayed until 3.30 — then packed trunk until 6.30 — Mrs. White came in for a few minutes — then a light tea. At 8 o'clk the boys + I went to the Park on our wheels + found it very delightful. We made the circuit of the park + a little more. Home at 9.40. Edwd Cook there — I installed him in the bed room vacated by Mrs. White \+ then, soon after 10, to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 18, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Montrose.

Rain in the very early mrng — later, a close, muggy day. Up at 6. finished packing + final putting away of things. After bkfst. to Sups. + other places near home. Then to N.A. etc. etc. Saw Miller, Douglass + Ryan + to each gave final instructions. Then put up our lunch at at 12 o Jim, Ralph + I left the house — to foot of Christopher St. + took 1 o'clk train D.L.+W. R.R. to Montrose where, after a somewhat hot + tiresome journey we got at about 7.30. Jessie Edgar, her husband + family, got on to the train at Scranton + came with us to M. We went at once to the Chas. Sayre house — had tea + chops — the boys went to bed at 9 + I followed at 10.

A heavy thunderstorm passed over M. bet. 8 + 9 o'clk —
