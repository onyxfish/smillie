## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 12, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day so far as I could judge from indoors. My experience of yesterday my repeated - a severe headache with facial neuralgia coming on after midnight \+ distressing me until about day light - Had my bkft. in bed \+ got up bet. 10 \+ 11, feeling, on the whole, better than I had for a good while. -
Went down to the parlor, kindled fire \+ spent the day \+ well there. I did some work, but in all, rested more than I worked. - The boys went to Van Cortlandt Park to get material for their aquaria - Late in the day they Ret., Ralph complaining of chills, head-ache, sore throat \+ nausea. - The chills soon passed \+ at 7 o'clk. his temp. was 102°. I sent him with a note to Dr. \+ the Ret. with him - I gave R- a strong dose of Warburg Tinct. before the Dr. came \+ the Dr. directed a cathartic dose of Rhubarb \+ Soda. A bed was fixed by the side of mine \+ he slept there all night. After 9 o'clk. Dielmann called \+ spent half an hour with me. It was 10.30 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 13, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sunny part of the day - clouded later. At 9 A.M. Ralph's temp. was normal. We had our bkft. in bed \+ got up after 10 - Then Yalle came - I begged him to release me from the Quinine treatment as my stomach has begun to rebel, but as again I had headache \+ neuralgia from 2 to 6 A.M., which to him suggested periodicity, he advised its continuance for awhile longer. - I went to parlor, as yesterday, and spent the day \+ well there but felt very uncomfortable - This may be one of my period days, when, if I have no chill or fever, I may expect to feel uneasy - It is four days since I had a rise of temp. last Lord ever. ! Mary Cook came about noon \+ made a call on me - The family is all excitement over the wedding that is to come off next Lord. - I went down to dinner \+ sat at my place at table - Jel came late in the afternoon \+ then Charle - I went to bed early.
