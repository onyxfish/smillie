## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 1, 1898

**Weather:** At Home. at am? on Etruria.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — Bkfst about 8.30 — after a walk on the road near the hotel. Loitered about and "chinned" with Kellogg until 11 when we started for Westport, a stage load came down from Reede's — Dr. Ferris & M. Colby among them — then all went to W.P. In our stage, besides him, Ralph & self, Miss Marsh & a maid went. Barring some dust we had a pleasant ride to the station — There I found Schilling, on his way to N.Y. & looking after the Adler family interests. Train came along on time and Miss Marsh & my party got seats in the State Room of a drawing Room car — the only ones to be had — We had a very good dinner at 1 o'clk in the dining room car & got through just as we got to White hall — I had some pain but not enough to discomfort me very much. Miss Marsh was a very pleasant traveling companion. Got to Albany on time at 4.15 — left Miss M. in the Station as she goes on to N.Y. by night boat. Changed our seats in parlor car to Riverside seats — started a little behind time — 4.45 — Beautiful sunset over the Catskill mts. Got sandwiches for the boys at Po'Keepsie — & reached N.Y. city on time & without accident at 8.15. went at once to the house & found Kate & Jane waiting for us. We had a comfortable tea — I felt pretty well tired out — about 9 o'clk Charl came in saying that between 7 & 8 he had got George & his traps off the Etruria just in from Liverpool — Charl left at about 10 but it was after 11 before I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 2, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A grey o. f. clearing very sunny & hot. I got up at 8 after a good night & had bkfst at 9 — after that I read paper — then to studio and found that the floor there has not been finished as I instructed Douglas — none of the work that I left to be done seems to have been done in accordance with my directions. After 12 I went to Yale's and waited for him until 1.15 & then I left a note for him — Home to dinner at 2. In the afternoon Yale came in & had a professional consultation with himself & me over my physical condition. At present he thinks it is catarrh of the bowels without further complications & he says that he hopes to be able to remove the trouble — Tea at 6.30 Suety. Ed. W. Cook, Catharine & Tom came in — then Jas. & Nellie came & then Charl — I feel very slim to entertain so much company, but got through after a fashion.
