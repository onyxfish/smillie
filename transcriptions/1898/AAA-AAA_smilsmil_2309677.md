## Left Page

### Thur. March 3, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable m'f. - overcast \+ raw, but clearing very charmingly in the afternoon. Priscilla Foore \+ her daughter came in for a few minutes immediately after bkfst. I got to studio at about 10 \- \+ wrote for awhile on N.A. matters \- At noon I went to Bank then to N.A., then to Fates'. Saw young P. \+ asked him if he thought I was a millionaire \- after a short conversation he offered me the plate for $30 half cash, half on 6 months \- But I told him that I could not afford to buy \- then to mills packing \+ home, Lunch \- To studio \- a letter from Sidney Morau of Met. Magazine \- wrote reply \- at 5 o'clk went to Century \- ways \+ means Com. N.A. session from hour. Thence home \- Dinner \- at 8.30 I went to N.A. \+ met Hungerford \+ Bullock \+ was with them until 10.30 \- They are now going over all of the accts. from '94 separating \+ classifying the funds \+ incomes, under my direction. This is work that heretofore I was content to give Farrington directions \+ when he told me that he had done as he was told I did not verify his statement. \_ I find now that there is much confusion \+ there are many errors in this classifying \- so it is being done now from the start, de novo \-

## Right Page

### Fri. March 4, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m'f. Snow commenced falling in the afternoon forming slush at once \- exceedingly disagreeable. Jerry came in the m'f. \+ took Valley of the Arno \+ San deni's Ray to the Century \- I spent most of the m'f. writing a short paper on the N.A. ways \+ means Com., a statement of the resources \+ the needs of the Academy. About 12.30 I took a short walk, stopping at Avery's to see a collection of Water-Color paintings by Wm. T. Richards \+ his daughter \- then to Durand-Ruels \+ saw the monstrously ugly affectations in etchings \+ pastels by Mary Cassatt \- then home to lunch. To studio soon after 2 \- To N.A. before 5. Bullock \+ Hungerford there \- we worked until 6.30. As the work grows I am anxious because I have no definite agreement with them as to compensation. I tried to reach an understanding with them, but was unable, they insisting that it was to be left to me. I feel that I have been imposed upon to some extent. At Case's suggestion I wrote to Bauk examining Kimbro to know if he would take charge of the investigation. In reply I got a note on Kimbale's office paper, in a heavy, official envelope, signed by Hungerford Asst Examiner \- Now I find that this job is a little private perquisite of the office \- Home to dinner late. Mary, Mary, Kate \+ Bessie went out \- I got to bed early. Feeling better.
