## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 1, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot, muggy, cloudy day with several showers to add to the steamy mugginess — I got up feeling pretty well \+ after bkfs't. went with Ralph to the Knoll to look for the surveyor, but he was not there — Then we walked down the road to the old Smith Beede Road \+ along that until we came to Kate Hillard's where we stopped — the youngest miss Neilson was there \+ miss Miller — Soon after Mr. \+ Mrs White drove up \+ for a little while we had a pleasant little party — Then R. \+ I went on intending to get to the top of Roaring Brook fall but I got off on the Giant trail, lost my bearing \+ at last had to return after some stiff climbing. We got to the hotel before 1 — the depressing atmosphere told on me \+ I lay down on my bed suffering considerable stomach discomfort. It was 2 o'clk before I could eat my lunch \+ for some time after that I was uncomfortable. At last I got up \+ wrote a long letter to Jad. Hart about the A Grand. Benedict business. Then sat for awhile on piazza — a very enervating day — Dinner at 7 — in an unhappy frame of mind — Jim also complaining of pain — After dinner I had to lie down again and did not get up until about 9 when my discomfort had passed away — Then I sat in my room \+ read until near 11 —
While on the piazza at 6 — Simonds \+ his assistant came along, having been on my lot all the p.m. running levels for my Road S — said that he told Beede to let me know —

I make record of my physical ailing as I do because I think I may need to refer to it when I get to NY. \+ put myself under expert medical treatment —

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 2, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot, uncomfortable sort of day — Sun blistering hot in the m'f'n., smart thunder shower before 3 p.m. — others very near \+ threatening later. Bkfs't at 8. It was after 9 when I went to the hill \+ found Simonds \+ his assistant \+ S't Young at work running a line for my road up on the west side of the Knoll — I worked with them until 12.30. when we got the line to the top of the hill, also, some measurements there. Then to hotel — tired. Rested until 1.30 — Then had lunch — It was 2.30 when I went again to the hill — soon driven away by the shower — I took refuge at W. McFee's piazza until it was over — Then, with the men commenced running lines to get the grade of the old Road up past the Widow Beede house to S. H — fun — This kept us at it until near 6 when we finished, finding that in 957' the Road rose 142'. Then to hotel — Rested — had a dry rub down and dinner after 7 — Then, until 9 sat on piazza — Clouded over. To bed early —

No pain to-day, but I was weak \+ uncomfortable — after dinner I was uneasy for an hour.
