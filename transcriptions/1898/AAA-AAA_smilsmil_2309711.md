## Left Page

### Tues. May 10, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - got up feeling pretty well after the fatigue of yesterday + got to studio at usual time. Then I completed my Treas. Report and went home at 12 + had a rest before lunch. After lunch Ralph set. with me to the studio + for an hour I did carpenter work for him making a frame upon which he is to fashion an ants nest upon a Darwins pattern. At 4 I returned to the house + about 4.30 he + I went off on our bicycles + had a charming ride in the Park. We went to N.A. site + I examined the sidewalk there as the N.A. has rec. notification to repave it. Home at 6 - dinner at 6.30 - Read to Ralph until 8.30 - Then went to Ed.as Cook's. Talked with Mary Cook \+ Mary Willard about a tutor for Jim - Home soon after. - Chard there - he stayed until 10 + then I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 11, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Annual Meeting, N.A.

Overcast m'f, with rain about the middle of the day clearing again at night. To studio at usual time - did some general work + writing + then home at noon + rested until lunch time. At 1.45 went to N.A. lunch was not yet over there. The meeting of N.A.'s was not called to order until about 2.30. Things ran smoothly until the end of the reading of my report. Then Miller opened fire on me, interposing objections to the acceptance of my report. He asked if any money had been lost through Gardner. I said that by his cashing a worthless check from the cash box the N.A. had lost $15.00 Then Beard wanted to know if that was all that the N.A. had lost thro' G. + I answered that G. had taken $200 to deposit in the bank but had never got there with it. + that was the whole fact. It was all on record in the books + might have been seen there if no one else Miller had had interest enough to investigate. It was evident that there was a concerted plan to work agt. me. When it came to election the vote was — for Hall + — for me. This was very disheartening to me, to think that after all my work + worry so nearly one half of the members were willing to turn me down for no good cause. My disheartement was complete when Weldon was elected a member of the council - he the man whom I believe to have been most active in leading the movement against me. I did not wait for the end of the meeting but went home to get a little fresh air - back at 7 o'clk to the dinner - sat between Dewing + Frank Jones - J. was chatty + pleasant, but the dinner was very stupid - walked up town with Watrous at 10 o'clk.
