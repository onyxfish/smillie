## Left Page

### Sat. March 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy shower in the m'g, with thunder and lightning — clearing by 10 o'clk — a very mild day following — I got to studio about 10 \+ spent the with writing, most of the time on O.W. Palper business, explaining to Mr. Nortt, Chairman of the Co. what had been done at the Water-Color meeting on Wed. ev'g last \+ with copy of Resolution then passed. Home to lunch at 1 — at 2 went to the Garrick Theatre in 35th St. near 6th Ave. with the boys — [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### Sun. March 20, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An overcast day with some sunshine, very warm. It was after 12 when with Jim \+ Ralph I walked up to 70th St. by Mad. Ave. \+ back by Park — getting in just before 2. Dinner. The afternoon after that was mostly given up to a nap, after which I don't think that I felt any better. Before tea time Irvin Bauck called on the girls. In the ev'g, Geo. \+ Emily Cook, Geo. and Nellie \+ Charl came in. [illegible — entire passage]
