## Left Page

### Thur. July 7, 1898

went up West Rock.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Weather still very delightful. Ther. fell to 52° last night — cloudless day — wind easterly \+ fresh. At 10 o'clk. The boys \+ I started on our wheels for West Rock — the roads were not really good — parts were very bad — \+ the roads were not picturesque but after all I found it interesting. It was about 12 when we got to the top, after losing our way \+ resting several times — we visited the Judge's cave \+ lingered over the distant view then home in about 3¼ of an hour — I had a change of clothes \+ rest before lunch — At about 3.30 the boys \+ I started out again \+ by trolly went to Lake Saltonstall — along side it was about 4.30 when we got to the lake — we had about hours row on the lake \+ Jim tried to do some fishing but caught nothing — Then we returned getting home just in time for dinner at 6.30. After dinner I wrote a note to Kate Leonard telling her to expect me to dinner to-morn \+ I posted it. Eve'g Jack Downs called — tobed at 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 8, 1898

Ralph's birthday. I returned to N.Y.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day but with a tempering breeze that was very pleasant if you did not have to walk in the hot sunshine. I had a short walk before bkft. \+ after, I read the paper for some time \+ then with Kaley \+ Raelon I went to Edwards S. \+ called on the A's. Nellie suffering from Neuralgia — R \+ I rel'd. To the E's before 12 \+ I packed my bag \+ arranged the valise to leave with the boys, Katey kindly offering to keep them for a week. Lunch at 1.30 — At a little after 3 Katey \+ the boys with my bag started on the R.R. station by trolley cars \+ I went on my wheel, getting there easily in 30 m. The boys came soon after. Train came at 3.55 — good byes — off at 4 — good seat \+ not uncomfortably hot — got to N.Y. at 5.40 \+ home before 6 — Mr. Parker White the only one at home — dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g Charl came in — also Mr. Wm Wheeler Mulford. After they left I wrote a letter to the boys \+ took it to station to mail — At 6.30 I had taken to P.O. Sub. Station pkg — of undershirts and mailed it to the boys.
