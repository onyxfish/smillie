## Left Page

### Thur. April 14, 1898

**Weather:** Overcast, with raw east wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, with raw east wind. Among the letters handed to me at the \+kfp. table was one from Mrs. Adam Everly, 1814 Delancey Place, Phila., a friend of Miss Biddle, asking for information about the Montrose House, as she is seeking for a place for the summer. I immediately wrote to her giving full particulars. Mrs. Atwater came in for a few minutes \+ I saw her. I was only fairly well after yesterdays afternoon \+ Jim is still quite out of sorts, having trouble with his stomach \+ bowels. I worked for a good long sitting on N.a. accts. during the P.M. \+ after I took a short \+ quiet ride on bicycles with Mallie \+ Jim. The air was too raw to be pleasant. Dinner at 6.30 Evg. wrote short notes to Nicole \+ Maynard.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 15, 1898

**Weather:** Heavy rain last night — dismal wet day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain last night — dismal wet day. I had several letters to write \+ they took most of the morning. After lunch I did some work on the N.A. accts. \+ then, the rain having ceased, at about 4 o'clk. I walked down town to the P.O. with letters — there to 137 Wall St., a large boarding house, where I called upon Maynard's sisters. I had a very pleasant call upon one \+ then walked home in a nizzling rain. Evg. general chatting until 9.30 — then to bed.
