## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 7, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day opened clear but soon clouded over \+ at about 10.30 a gentle mizzle of rain began that increased until later it was a heavy down pour of rain \- About the middle of the afternoon rain ceased \- at night, clear, cold starlight. After bkfst. I went to Kvall with lines \+ measuring tape \- got my North \+ South \+ East \+ West lines \+ made a number of measurements so that I can make a scale plot showing positions of trees \+ rocks so that I can see what space I have for the placing of my house. \- When the rain drove me off I returned to the hotel \+ wrote to Mr. Lee on U.A. business matters and then commenced a letter to Emma Ireland. Lunch at 1.30 read newspaper \- then wrote for awhile on letter to Emma. \- then walked down the hill \+ around by old Smith Beede road \+ back to hotel \- about two miles \- in at 6 \- dressed for dinner at 7 o'clk. \- Every Read in parlor until 9u a short walk on piazza \+ to bed before 10.

no pain to-day \+ I feel stronger \- I think that I am improving \+ am more hopeful.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 8, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

went to the top of Noon Mark.

A brilliant, cool morning. Bkfst. at 8 \- then, the morning being all that could be desired for a tramp, I decided to make a second attempt at Noon Mark. Jim complained of a lame leg, so, Ralph \+ I started from the hotel at 9.30 \+ went in on the Dix trail, just beyond the gate on the arable road. The climb for half an hour was through a fine forest. The trail growing steeper \+ harder as it went up. \- We took it easy \+ I was much pleased to find that I was doing my work without trouble or distress. \- Two gentlemen overtook us \+ together we went to the top. \- I had forgotten that the cliffs at the summit \+ just below were so fine. \- We reached the top at 12. \- just 2½ hours. \- we were on the top 25 m. The views in all directions were clear \+ remarkably fine \- R. \+ I started down alone \+ got to the hotel in 65 m. or at 1.30 \- I went to my room \+ rested \- changed my underclothes, had a rub-down \+ at 2.30 a light lunch \- after that I lay on my bed \+ read until 4.30 \- Then with Miss Van Amrynge I went to my Kvall \+ from there to the top of Windy brow \+ back to hotel at 6.30 \- just in time \- as my bowels demanded my attention \- I had some pain there but it was not neuralgic \- I feel, rather, that my liver is being waked up \+ after the manner in which I did my mornings work I feel very much encouraged. Ralph is a first rate climber \+ detects \+ follows a blind trail admirably \-
Dinner at 7\. \- Every in parlor until 9 \- then to my room \+ soon to bed pleasantly tired but without any feeling of exhaustion.

no pain to-day.
