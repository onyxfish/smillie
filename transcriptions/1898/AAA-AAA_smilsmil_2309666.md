## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 9, 1898

Kathleonie \+ Bessie went to Washington

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thick, cold fog in the m't. reminding me strongly of a fine winter day in London. It was a white fog, but nothing could be seen 300 distant \- \+ the air had the raw chill of a cold, damp cellar. I went to Yale's immediately after bkfs-, walking up to 42. S. with Ralph. I was with Yale for an hour - consulting concerning Jim's condition \+ my own. Jim has much catarrhal trouble again. Then to studio \+ wrote N.A. letters until noon. Then to N.A. \+ made out some checks \+ home to lunch at 1.30. The girls had started on their Washington trip before 12. I got to studio at about 3 \+ worked for an hour then walked down to Wilments'- frame maker in 13. St. — failed to find just what I wanted - met Homer Lee there - walked home at 6 - Emma I - there - we consulted about a flute for Jim \+ I instructed her to get one just for his amusement. The boys dined at Edward Cooks - so many, Kate \+ I had a quiet dinner. Mary Cook was at the house the afternoon \+ told Mary \+ Kate about Eliz. Cook's engagement to Coffin -

To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 10, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very discomforting sort of day - foggy \+ warm with a very chill wind - the ther. getting above 60° in the shade. I worked at studio at various things until near noon - then to White, Potter \+ Page, office 26 w. 24. S. \+ selected a moulding for frame 24 × 50 - for "Showers". Then to 2°. Nat°. \+ to N.A. \+ home to lunch somewhat late. After it I took an hours rest \+ while in my room a messenger called from Chas. Barmore to say that B. was going to leave N.Y. for three months on acct. of his health. As many would not disturb me I did not see him, but in the evef I wrote to Barmore that I wished to see him. To studio at 3 \+ worked on old paintings until 4.30 then home \+ later to Century Club at 5 o'clk meeting of Ways \+ means Com. After the reading of the minutes wherein it was stated that all members of the com. were present at the previous meeting held at the house of W. Oom I objected - all members were not present \+ I was not there. Beckwith repeated before the Com. that he recognized my membership but had not sent me a notification of the meeting because consideration of me, knowing that I had very many things to attend to. Of course I accepted his explanation without comment - nevertheless I would much prefer to be allowed to decide for myself what work I can attend to \+ when I shall ask to be excused. Home at 6.30 - dinner - evef with the boys \+ writing. To bed before
