## Left Page

### Tues. June 21, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy shower at 4.30 a.m. clouded until noon - Then cleared beautifully - quite cool Immediately after bkft. took Ralph to Dr. Yale's \+ was disappointed to find that Y. had gone to Quisset to stay until the middle of Aug. - Some time was given to the plumbers I don't like the way Ryan is acting - I think that he is making all the work that he can. I did some writing but feel out of sorts \+ at 11 Ralph \+ I went off on our wheels \+ away to Grant's Tomb \+ back at 12.45. Lunch at 1.15 - Then I wrote some N.A. business letters \+ a letter to Katharine Throap - At 5 went with R. - to Shoemakers \+ then to Studio - an interesting letter from Mr. Carrere in relation to Mr. Sturges's letter \+ white marble as a building material. I again went to Police Station to complain of the boys that I have driven so often from my door step - Home soon after 6 - Dinner. We \+ at 8 went with Mr. White to Ld W. Cook's - I got home at 9 \+ wrote to Pres. of Essex Co. for fey bills on Keene Valley plat - to first Natl. Bk. Montrose, sending a check - \+ to Central Trust Co - sending a check.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 22, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, cool m'f. My morning was largely given to Ryan \+ Miller - plumber \+ carpenter. The inspector came \+ seemed to be easily satisfied \+ after a long discussion I arranged to have all of the plumbing under the two wash basins cleared out \+ connection made for back airing with an air pipe that is in place \+ goes to the roof - I was able to do a little work on my N.A. Report - Soon after 12 a short but quite heavy thunderstorm passed over - Lunch at 1 - At 4 I was at N.A. \+ there met Hungerford \+ Bullock \+ we worked diligently until 6.30 arranging a "balance sheet". There were a number of puzzling complications the result of my demands for changes to bring items in the statement into harmony with facts as I knew them to exist. Most of our trouble was over the arrangement of the Snydams Fund, which from the beginning has been a good deal of a puzzle to me - I left them at 6.35 - They said then that they had but little more to do. Dinner at 7 — Sue \+ all hands seemed to be tired - So after a little. Reading we went to our rooms - After 9 I went out on my wheel \+ rode on 5th ave. from Central Park to Washington Sq/. In at 10.15.

There was a second thunderstorm with hail between 5 \+ 6 p.m.
