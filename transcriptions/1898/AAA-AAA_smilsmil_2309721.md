## Left Page

### Mon. May 30, 1898

*Decoration Day*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - To studio before 10 + spent most of the a.m. preparing a mount for a choice proof of "Reading Homer" after Alma-Tadema that I am going to give to Mr. H. G. Marquand. I also did a little picking up - Home to lunch + to Studio at 2 - Van D. Perrine was the only etcher - I bit his etching for him, getting through at about 3.30 - Home about 4 - Charl. Thenere, but he decided that as he had ridden 20 miles he had had enough - So, Jim, Ralph + I went out on our wheels - Through the Park - up to Grant's Tomb + home - a little over 13 miles - Then I rested until 7 o'clk - Then dinner - The afternoon was perfection - the Park was wonderfully beautiful, + the number of children buying folks, gaily dressed + playing all sorts of games, being allowed to go everywhere over the grass, was amazing. And if Charl came in + stayed until 9 o'clk - Then I wrote a letter to Mary + mailed it - To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 31, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - After b'kf't to butcher's + grocer's - Then to studio - Then to N.A., + to 2nd Nat. Bk. - Got Oregon Imp. Co. + 1st, St. 2d, St. - Certs + took them down town to Charl. - To Central Trust - To Royal Mt. Co - to dee + dee - to Waterman's + bot. fountain pen - To Ed'd Coale's 150 Nassau - To old paper dealer in Ann St. - to Brush maker in Pearl St. - + bot. 3 hoe brushes for the boys + then home at 1.45 - quick lunch - then to Studio - Kelly, Sterne + Stather there. I worked with them biting their plates until near 6 - Then home - dinner at 6.30 - Mrs. White ret'd from Boston - Well. I took pf. of small etching "Reading Homer" + left it with a note at Mr. Marquand's - Then to Mr. Barhydt's. 402, 704 - She has left town - Then to Dr. Gerster's. 34 E. 75th - There nearly an hour - Then I walked home, getting in at nearly 10 o'clk - Found Mrs. White's bro, Winthrop Parker + his wife calling on her -
