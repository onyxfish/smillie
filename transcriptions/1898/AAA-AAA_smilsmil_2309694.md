## Left Page

### Wed. April 6, 1898

**Weather:** Clear, cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Numa J. came in after [illegible] \+ I was detained so that I did not get to studio until after 10 — finding it cold there \+ having business at 2°. Nat. B'k. I went there \+ to N.A. \+ did not get back to studio until 11.30 — worried over N.A. books until 12.40 then home to lunch — Jim \+ Ralksh there — lunch — \+ at 1.30 Mary Ireland came \+ we all went to Buffalo Bill's Show — it being her treat — I saw B.B.'s show in London when we were last over there \+ was constantly comparing this with that, to the detriment of this — out at 4.30 — Mary left us \+ the boys \+ I went up town — I to studio \+ worried for another hour over the books — then to Spauldings \+ saw Ball about my wheel — then to studio \+ home to dinner — after dinner read to the boys until near 9 — then I went to Jno. Cook's \+ saw him \+ Mary — Walter there — went over the Margaret White proposition to them \+ they approved of it. Then returned to the house — Charl there — we stayed until after 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 7, 1898

**Weather:** Clear \+ Cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio soon after 10. after a short stay there went to N.A. where I was kept for some time, then to 2°. Nat. B'k. To N.A. again, got Petty Cash book \+ then to studio after 12 — Home to lunch at 1 — back to studio after 2 \+ then set myself regularly to work at N.A. acct. books to evolve a system of keeping the accts. to suit myself \+ independent of those who have preceded me — I shall make three great divisions — Exhibition, Schools \+ Expense — of course there will have to be sub-divisions, but I shall make them as few \+ simple as possible. I worked on the Expense acct. starting in 1894 when the N.A. sold its building — At 5 I went to Century Ways \+ means Com. — Beckwith \+ I sat in the small library room where the com. has always met heretofore \+ had a very pleasant chat while we waited until 6.20 for the others — then we started for home — Down stairs we were told that Nicoll \+ Watrous had been until a few minutes before, waiting very impatiently for us — stupid — After dinner read awhile to the boys — Charl came in for a moment bringing a bicycle saddle — at 9 I went to Margaret White's Cor. Lex. Ave. \+ 27's — but she was out — then home my wheel was sent home to-day from Spauldings I got on it \+ had a 3 or 4 mile ride \+ they to bed at 11.
