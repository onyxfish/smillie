## Left Page

### Sat. April 30, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At last, a brilliant, beautiful day. Immediately after Bkf. Mr. Thos. Sturgis called at the house by appointment \+ together we went to Carvers \+ Hastings. I introduced him to Mr. Carvers \+ we had a pleasant 20 m. or half an hour. Then I went to studio \+ started upon my N.A. grind. — deGrand Benedict came in \+ gave me a note, Renewing his old one to the N.A. \+ made his application for the Cat. adv't. next year. Then I went on with my work until 1.30. Then home to my lunch which I took alone — Jim being in Bronx Park for the day — \+ Ralph lunching with his swele friend. Cutting of mad. Ave. To studio at 2.30 \+ worked until 4 — Then home \+ before 4.30 I was off on my bicycle. I rode up to \+ through the Park which was brilliantly beautiful — I enjoyed it immensely \+ did not feel the work of it at all — Home a few minutes before 6 and had a rest, a rubdown \+ change of clothes \+ dinner at 6.45 — Every Read aloud to the boys until 8.30 — I got to bed at 10.30. Someone sent a box of beautiful Roses to the house this ev'g. No doubt a recognition of Annie's birthday to-morrow — There was no card with them or other way for me to know who sent them — I think they came from Helen Butler.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 1, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very mild, sunny day — Bkf. at 9. It was about 10.30 when the boys \+ I started for Greenwood, This being Annie's birthday. Ocean Hill was very lovely with Spring budding \+ the Eastern horizon, where the Ocean should be, was vague \+ dreamy. The wounded heart, as I stood by that grave with those two dear boys bled afresh and seemed as sensitive as I was three years ago. Time it is said heals all wounds. I dread that healing — As I regard it now it would seem like disloyalty. We returned getting home at 1.40 — Dinner at 2. Tom Cook dined with us — I had an hours rest after dinner — At 5 went to studio \+ wrote a letter or two — Home at 6.30. Bessie Parke there, but she left in a few moments — Tea at about 7 — A little Reading to Ralph — Charl came in at 9 \+ left soon after 10.
