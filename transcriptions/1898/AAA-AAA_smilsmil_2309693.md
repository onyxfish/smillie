## Left Page

### Mon. April 4, 1898

**Weather:** Blustering, cold \+ streaked with sunshine \+ shadow.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio \+ until after 11 I wrote notes \+ letters that were in arrear. Then to N.A. \+ bank. Home to lunch \+ to studio at 2.30 — and again I wrote until near 5 — then home \+ had a rest before dinner — Evg. to Council meeting \+ home before 11 —

This mrg I had a talk with Kate Leonard on the subject of the possible coming of Margaret White — She was in a very gracious mood \+ promised to do all in her power to make things pleasant. That difficulty removed, now I have to face the others, which I do with great self distrust — I really wish that the proposition had not been made to me. I wish to be of service to Mrs. White but there are so many formidable lions in the way that I am full of misgivings.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 5, 1898

**Weather:** Snowing all the mrg. over cold \+ windy — a mild blizzard. About 4" of snow fell.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I went to studio as usual \+ was engaged writing letters \+ studying the N.A. acct. books that I brought to the studio yesterday — Home to lunch \+ at 2 went to Calverly's studio as per previous engagement — Jim \+ Ralph followed me soon after — C. had made an engagement with Decomp, the plaster cast man to meet us in his studio to take a mask of Jim's face — C. would it do it altho' fully competent. We waited until near 3 — then I went to D's in 33rd S. near 7th Ave. \+ found that he was too unwell to go out in the storm — Ret'd. to C — Jim went to his French lesson — R. \+ I, to N.A. then R. went shopping \+ I ret'd. to my studio where I worked over acct. books until after 6 — then home to dinner — Evg. Walter Cook came in for a little while — on the whole, he approves of Margaret White coming here to stay for the summer — Charle also came in —
