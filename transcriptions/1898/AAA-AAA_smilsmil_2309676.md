## Left Page

### Tues. March 1, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully beautiful day. my temp. still keeps about 1° below normal, so I went to Yale's immediately after bkfst. While there Gerster came in & we had a very pleasant visit — Yale advises me to add alot of babies food to my diet. After leaving Y— I went to Correre and Hastings — 41st. \+ Mad. Ave — saw both of them \+ saw also the drawings under way — Then to studio \+ did a little fussing on "The Valley of the Arno" After lunch I went to 2°. Natl. Bank, then to N.A. \+ after that to Wm Pates' where I had a short interview with his son. I enquired as to the extent of sales of p[illegible] from "The Goldsmith's Daughter" \+ being told that there were no sales — I said that I would like to buy the plate to save it from the risk of falling into the hands of those who might at some future time abuse it, \+ my memory or reputation, by issuing poor prints. He said that he would consult with his father \+ then write to me. Then to Studio — Dinner at 6.30 Evef. Mary \+ Bessie went out to a concert — Lincoln — \+ aunt Agnes called \+ I went to N.A. where I met Hungerford \+ Bullock \+ worked with them over N.A. accts until 10.30 — Home at 11 — Locked front door — in 15 minutes I found that Mary \+ Bessie had not got in — then I went down \+ unlocked the door.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 2, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate arr² from Po'k —

Overcast mfg. growing more raw \+ disagreeable until late in the afternoon when a sleety drizzle commenced. I was more or less used up all day with a headache. To studio about 10 \+ spent an hour looking over the soiled \+ injured proofs sent to me by the Met. Mag. manager Sidney Moran together with process prints from them to appear in the issue for May — I wrote a letter to him. About 12 I went out for air \+ walked about the streets half an hour or more — then home. Beckwith came in with a paper concerning the bill to exempt the N.A. fds. from Taxation that he wishes me to get into shape — after lunch I went to studio \+ worked awhile on the paper \+ at about 3.30 I went to N.A. with it \+ had Francis Tyke write it — at 6 Davis took it to B— \+ I went home — Dinner — Evef. at 8.30 two' a horrible storm of sleet, I went to the N.A. \+ there met Hungerford \+ Bullock \+ worked at arranging a clear \+ systematic statement of the several accts — until 10.30 — The complexities of book keeping seem to grow — I can believe now that by an indisputable system of book keeping anything may be proved — I am much discouraged.

A note from Pate — he offers to sell plate of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" for $400 — Phew!
