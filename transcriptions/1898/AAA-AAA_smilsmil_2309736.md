## Left Page

### Wed. June 29, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very warm day but not so humid as yesterday. Ralph got up all right in the m't. seems to be quite recovered. Also I went in to Dr. Putnam's \+ had a long talk with him - interesting but developing nothing new. He advises Phosphate of Soda \+ Sub Gallies of Bismuth. Time divided between the men at work in the house \+ a little writing. Lunch after that I went down town, calling first at Greenwood Cemetery Office where I saw Mr. Perry \+ gave permission to remove iron fence about our family lot. Then to Lee \+ Lee's \+ saw me Lousdie about various items of N.A. business. Then to Cullens, the Photographers, \+ to the office. Up town at 5 \+ went to barbers and had hair cut - Then home - dinner - Eve'g some reading - then some business litters - wrote to Mollie Adams inviting an invitation for boys \+ self. to visit at New Haven on the 4th of July. At 10 o'clk, I went out on my wheel \+ rode in Central Park - glorious moonlight night. Coal \+ pleasant in the Park — gayety \+ music that recalled some of the memories of Southern Europe. Home at 11.15

## Right Page

### Thur. June 30, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day - almost cloudless - some air stirring. A busy day at the house, plumbers, carpenters, masons \+ tile men all in together \+ working over one another. They are now in one anothers' way. The mason is putting up a "scratch coat" - the carpenter is preparing the floor for the tile man who is getting his iron lath on up to the 5' line, to which line the mason is working down. At the same time the plumber is soldering up his connections \+ has had the water pressure on. I worked at studio packing away things so that the carpenter can scrape the floor. At 11.30 I went to 2° Nat'l B'k \+ then to N.A. Home to lunch at 1. After 2 went to studio there until 5 — very hot \+ tired. Dinner at 6.30 — feet very uncomfortable for an hour after it. After 8 — E.J. Appleton came about his a/c. due to N.A. since 1st ins'. I told him that he must make some arrangement to raise the money as I could no get along without any longer — Men Charl. came in for awhile. — Mrs. White with Jim, went to a "Pap" concert at Mad. Sq. Roof Garden.
