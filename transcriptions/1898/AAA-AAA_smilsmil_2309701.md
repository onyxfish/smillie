## Left Page

### Wed. April 20, 1898

**Weather:** Pleasant day & mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day & mild - got up with continuation of headach the only change being that it had shifted to the other side of my head. I got to studio late & managed to do a little work on M.a. acct. Maynard came in with a check for $500 - on acct. of arrears of rent - a most welcome payment to me. I went to 2d Nat. Bk and deposited it. Then home at 12.30 - so used up by my headache that I was obliged to go to bed. At 5 o'clk. by great exertion I got up & wrote a note to Mrs. Bishop to inform her that it was impossible for me to dine with her this evef. - then I went back to my bed & did not get up to undress myself until near 1 o'clk. in the mf.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 21, 1898

**Weather:** More or less cloudy all day  **Temp:** [illegible]

More or less cloudy all day - I feel very much the effects of my three days of headache but was able to put in a full day at the studio working on M.a. acct. Every Charl came in for awhile & after he left I worked for some time on my own acct. clearing up details of studio's investment, which, after the bal. that Maynard paid me yesterday proves to be fairly satisfactory.

I have made no note in this journne of the War excitement that is disturbing everybody so greatly. All of my interest so far is in the form of disquiet at the unthinking frenzy that seems to have taken possession of our rulers at Washington. I am not blind to the frightful state of affairs in Cuba - I realize that there is good reason for intervention of some kind - but I do not believe that every effort that a strong, civilized Power should make to avoid a brutal conflict - a slugging match - has been made - a war at the end of the 19th Century by this country against a hopelessly weak & bankrupt country like Spain seems to me nothing short of disgraceful. The Responsibilities that we shall have to assume in sustaining a government in Cuba, if we drive out Spain, seems to get no attention. Next we shall have to drive out the Cubans & re-people the island with real Yankees.
