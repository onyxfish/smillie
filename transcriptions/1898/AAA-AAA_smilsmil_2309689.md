## Left Page

### Sun. March 27, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Horrible day — the wettest sort of a misgling wet rain. Did not go out of doors until about 4 o'clk. when Charl came \+ took me up to Dr. Allen's in 60" St. He (charl) is sure that D. A — is going to make a physically new man out of me. I submit \+ told A — that it was no seeking of mine. If my discomforts can be mitigated so that I may be comfortable a part of the time, it is all that I expect — the Dr. asked me many questions \+ made examinations — says I am organically sound \+ thinks he can relieve some of the functional troubles — Charl left me there at 6 o'clk I walked home. Tea at 6.30 — Suc'f Charl \+ Fe°. came in — I got to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 28, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull weather — light rain at night — to studio as usual \+ painted on Md. Tower, boggling over the long architectural \+ perspective lines in wall on right hand side. I am not so hopeful now as I was at first that I will be able to make a creditable work of it — Home to lunch — Afternoon worked until 5, then home \+ had a short rest before dinner. Suc'f. went to N.A. Council meeting — my time was divided between it \+ Hungerford \+ Bullock who were in the library until 11 at work upon the acct. books trying to get them as I wish to have them — Either, I am very clumsy in my efforts to tell them what I want or else they are obtuse. I do not think that the problem of the best form in which to keep these books is necessarily a complicated one.
