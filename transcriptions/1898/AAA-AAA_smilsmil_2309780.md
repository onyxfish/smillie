## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 27, 1898

**Weather:** Cloudy + rather cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

got up feeling very well + before bkfs. had a brisk walk on the Tennis platform — In the m+ went over to the Knoll + having decided upon the measurements for dining room bl+. etc. I had Young clear more ground — about 12.30 stomach discomfort began + increased until after 1 when I was very miserable but I took a light lunch — By 3.30 the pain had passed + I again went to the Knoll + drove the last Stakes marking corners of dining room + kitchen, ice house etc — also had some clearing done on the line of my road on the west side of my hill — Then I went up to my Maghee's + made a short call There — Ret? to hotel before 6 — went to my Room + lay down upon my bed — began to feel chilly — by 7 I was chattering I undressed + got into bed + sent for D. Hobbs who came after 8 — By that time I had taken 6 gr. Quinine — The chill had passed + the fever was on — How high it went during the night I cannot say but it seemed to me that I came near burning up besides feeling sick + sore in every fibre of my body but I lived through it +

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 28, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day + superb moonlight night. but I had to stay in bed all day — Dr. Hobbs came in at about 9 o'clk + found my temp. 1/5° above normal — He thinks the attack was the result of cold — cold may have precipitated it but I think it was really an acute attack of indigestion. I had a headache all day + two unhealthy movements of the bowels — the only movements that I have had for two months without the aid of drugs or suppositories — I got up at 6.30 + felt just as I have done after a day of one of my old fashioned violent headaches — I went down to dinner at 7. Every I wrote notes explaining my change of plans for now, if I am able I propose to go home on Sat.

I had a little moonlight walk on the Tennis platform.
