## Left Page

### Sat. June 25, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot day. Ther. in my bed room got up to 88° \+ did not at any time fall below 80° notwithstanding a very heavy thunderstorm that came up at 5 p.m. \+ lasted for an hour or more. Ralph got up, complaining of a sleepless night \+ a severe headache. He had a little fever - I gave him a dose of Citrate of Magnesia in the afternoon he appeared to be much better. I kept very quiet but did a fair days work in writing out my Report to accompany the figures that, for some time past I have been preparing. Everett Chard came in for a while - pretty well fagged after his week in Boston \+ vicinity. Ralph seemed to have a return of his discomfort \+ we all got to bed at about 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 26, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An excessively hot day - \+ a very anxious one for me. Up soon after 7 \+ had as thorough a scrub as my limited conveniences would allow. Bkfst - at 9 - Ralph got up, to all appearance in his usual condition but he ate little bkfst. \+ soon began to show signs of fever - etc - after 10 I went to Dr. Putnam, a neighbor two doors from us \+ he came at about noon - thought it might be a case of tonsilities - better watch it - R's temp. was high all day - 103° \+ 104° \+ his pulse from 110 to 120 - he slept a good deal \+ I watched him constantly. He was exceedingly patient, quiet \+ good. Dinner at 2 - After 4, Mrs White, her boy \+ girl \+ Jim went to Fort Lee ferry at 125 St. \+ by trolley to Englewood \+ back getting in at about 8 - [illegible] - Dr. Putnam came in at about 8 and examined R. very carefully but said we would have to wait until tomorrow for developments - I moved his cot into my room alongside my bed. I wrote a note to Mrs Bishop.
