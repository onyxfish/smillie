## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 27, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Miss Sickley ret. to Po'k. man evef train.

A brilliant day — after a satisfactory scrub \+ massage I had wlp. soon after 9 — for awhile I worked over some of the N.a. accts, that have been puzzling me — then, at about noon, Ralph \+ I went out for a walk, getting back at about 1.30 — Dinner at 2 — I rested for an hour then, at 4, I walked down to the Irelands at 33 ( E, 17° S. Catherine I— has been visiting with them for a week — Edew. Cook \+ Edna were there — a very agreeable time until 6 — just as I was leaving, Elizabeth Cook \+ her fiancée, Mr. Coffin, came in \+ I had my first glimpse at him — Home at 6.30 — tea — very soon after, Miss Sickley started for her train to return to Po'k, Jim \+ Bessie escorted her to station \+ then the two went to church — I played games with R— \+ read to him — Charl came in —

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 28, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. To studio at usual time \+ worked on "The valley of the Arno," making, I think, improvements. In the afternoon Spencer Coon came \+ interviewed me, for the Tribune, on the subject of the opening of N.a. spring exh. on Sundays, as was decided at last quarterly meeting. I got home at about 5 p.m. \+ had a rest before dinner — Evef. went to Council meeting — little business — I got papers with reference to taxation of the N.a. for P. W. Wood to take to Albany with him tomorrow — He, Beckwith \+ J. F. Brown are going up to appear before a com. in favor of a bill that has been reported giving to cities of 1st class in the state the privilege of spending $50000 per an. on art works under direction of an art commiss- m — I was invited to go but do not feel physically equal. Walked up with Gel. at about 10.30
