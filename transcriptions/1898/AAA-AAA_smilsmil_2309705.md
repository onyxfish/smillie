## Left Page

### Thur. April 28, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy wind \+ rain \- driving from N.E. I cannot but think of George at sea \- After an unsuccessful night I got up with a sore head, but altho' it made it hard to do my work I pulled through a pretty good days work on the N.A. accounts. It is very puzzling to me at times. Evely. Jim went to an amateur Circus show at the Manhattan Knickerbocker Athletic Club \+ got in at 10.30 \- I was too tired \+ sleepy to accomplish anything.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 29, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still disagreeable weather \- overcast \+ raw \- Rain in the m't until after 9 \- I got to studio at 9.30 \+ was soon followed by Mr. Tom Sturgis who made an appointment with me to go with him to-morrow m't to Carrere \+ Hastings \- I laid etching grounds on a number of plates for the boys expected this afternoon \- then I got to work on my N.A. accts \+ Nicole came in \- It was after 12 when I went to N.A. \+ worked there until after 1 \- then home to lunch \- to studio at 2 \- only three of the etchers came \- \+ instead of the give model an uninteresting man put in an appearance \- so the three boys made up their minds to give it up for the day \+ I went on with my N.A. accts until about 5.30 when I gave up very tired \- I walked about the streets until dinner time \- Ralph \+ I alone at dinner \- Jim dined at Tom Cook's \- Well, I read awhile to Ralph \- Later Charl came in \+ stayed until 10 \-
