## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 21, 1898

**Weather:** Splendid clear, cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Splendid clear, cold day — Ther. last night went down to 33°. Wrote a letter to Ge? Cook \+ at 10 started off with the boys hoping to be able to get up the new Chapel Brook Trail — we had a brisk walk out to the Pond — I had not felt so able for a long time — we got to the Brook \+ went part way up the trail when my stomach gave me warning that I had gone about far enough if I wished to get back in good shape, so I heeded the warning — as Jim to was beginning to complain \+ we turned back \+ sauntered over the Road — on our way a wagon load of people passed us \+ was quickly stopped near over to the side — "There's Mr. Smillie" — It was Taylor who used to be a boarder, with his father \+ mother, at Mrs. Dud — Lathrop's, in Montrose many years ago. — Then he was a red headed boy — now he introduced me to his wife \+ daughter — Got to hotel at 1 — in good shape — Lunch at 1.30 — After lunch I got seriously to work on plans for house on Pile Knall \+ after 4 o'clk went over there with my plans to locate them \+ take more measurements. Then with Ralph I walked over Windy Brow, then to hotel \+ worked on drawings until 6 — Then a brisk walk on Tennis Court platform — Dinner at 7.15 — Eve'g in parlor — about of Mr. Maghee came to me \+ we chatted about building — cows — \+ kitchen gardens until 10 — Men to bed.

Have felt unusually well to-day — altho' at times gas in stomach \+ bowels distressed me. I commenced taking castor oil in 10 drop doses before each meal yesterday — no movement to-day —

While on our way to Chapel Pond, Jim found a lady's gold watch by the road side — I put up a notice in the hotel office on my return.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 22, 1898

**Weather:** Pleasant m[illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m[illegible]. The weather growing gradually milder \+ overcast until the eve'g when Rain set in. I got up \+ went thro' my usual exercise \+ hair mitten scrubbing \+ had a walk on Tennis platform before bkf. Otis's daughter came over \+ claimed the watch that Jim found yesterday. Bkf — at 8.30 after it I worked for awhile on plans, then went to Mrs. White's \+ she took me over her new dining room \+ kitchen — a separate building — I got some valuable hints — then to Mr. Maghee's — he took me down to his property at the foot of the hill \+ showed me all of his improvements — his stables — hen \+ duck yard, his kitchen garden \+ young orchard \+ then we climbed a hill to a clearing of his — he took me on quite as tromp so that when I got to the hotel at 1.30 I was not only hungry out I was tired — so I rested for half an hour before I lunched — Afternoon I went to the Knoll \+ after very careful consideration decided to change the situation of kitchen \+ dining room considerably to the north of where I first had planned to have it. I spent some time measuring the positions plange [illegible] trees so that I can make a flat on paper of their location so that I may make plans for the building. Back to hotel before 6 — Rested awhile — Dinner at 7. Eve'g played Euchre with Ralph until nearly —

I got along fairly well to-day — In the afternoon I had an hour or more of stomach discomfort but it did not amount to actual pain — Through it I continued my work of making measurements on the Knoll — altho' I doubled the castor oil capsules I had to use as a suppository to get a movement — Some mucus in evidence.
