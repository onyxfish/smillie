## Left Page

### Sun. April 10, 1898

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast wt. with some light shatters of rain, but later the weather cleared pleasantly. Aftr bklp' unpacked Trunk \+ put our several wardrobes in closets \+ bureau drawers \+ then read until about noon when, with the boys, I went downtown \+ got some Hind Spt. Canadian Balsam for gargle for Ralph's Throat. Then we walked about the College buildings \+ back home. Dinner at 1.30. Katie \+ her Wm. with us. After dinner Katy \+ Molly went to the Downs' to play piano with Jack \+ Wm Elkins — remained \+ chatted with me until 4. Then he left \+ the boys \+ I went out on our wheels \+ had an 8 mile ride — part way up East Rock. Tea at 6.30 — a quiet evg. \+ [illegible] at 10 —

## Right Page

### Mon. April 11, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudy day with threats of rain, but none came — Up at 7 \+ before bklp. had a 20 m spin on my wheel — Bkfp at 8.30 — Aftr I wrote notes to Margaret White — to A.tt. Pate \+ to Charl. At 12 Molly, the boys \+ I went up to Katy's with our wheels — there Katy \+ William joined us \+ went to the Golf links \+ making three couples we made a round of 7 holes. We beginners found it very amusing — after I we went to Katy's house \+ had a very pleasant lunch. after that we had photos. of the moon \+ places nearer home \+ then all of us went out on our wheels — passing the links we saw some play by three excellent golfers \+ they threw our accomplishments of the wt. into deep shade — we Rode into East Rock Park \+ part way up the Rock. By Jim's cyclometer we did about 12 miles — I had a rest between 5 \+ 6.30 — Then tea. Frank returned from Ridgefield. Evg. I Read aloud from the Evg. Post — the Presidents message to Congress on Cuba — Rec'd from Margaret White a Telegram — "Apartment owner to-day awaiting my answer, please wire yours". Too late to reply —
