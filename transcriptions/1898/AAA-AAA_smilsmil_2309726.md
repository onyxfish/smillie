## Left Page

### Thur. June 9, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably warm day — the weather bureau promised us showers but they did'nt come. Nearly all of my time was consumed about the house, \+ the studio \+ by "chores", so that it was 11.30 when I got to N.a. where I worked until after 1 \+ then home to lunch — To second Nat'l Bk \+ then to N.a. where I worked fruitlessly until after 4 — then I sauntered to studio \+ home — too hot for exercise — Dinner at 6.30 — Evef read aloud awhile. After the boys got to bed — at about 9.30 I got out my wheel \+ made a tour of the Park getting home in very good shape at 11.0 — It was just 12 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 10, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another pleasant day, growing cooler evef., pleasantly cool. Again some time after Bkf. was given to the work about the house — looking after painters \+ stowing \+ changing furniture etc, so as to be out of their way \+ protected agst injury. To studio for a few minutes — then to N.a. at about 11 — After a wkp work I was much discouraged — figures are such unreasonable things — My accts are all right, but I cannot arrange them so as to get a correct, satisfactory bal. Home to lunch at 1.30 — Douglass' men cleaned \+ painted ceiling of library. I went to 2d Nat'l \+ dep'd check from Chicago — then to N.a. \+ again wrestled for a balance — at times it seemed sure to come — but it did'nt. I cannot understand why Hungerford does'nt put in an appearance to help me. At 4.30 I gave it up — went home — at 5 out on my wheel \+ made a tour of the Park — a cool East wind was blowing which as I got very warm, made me very uncomfortable. Home at 6.30 — dinner at 7.15 — before we were thro' Mary Cook came in to see Mrs. Whiet \+ remained until 9 —
