## Left Page

### Fri. March 25, 1898

**Weather:** Brilliant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day. To studio at 9.30 — Busied myself with many little things until 11.30. Then to N.A. — there until near 1 — then home to lunch afternoon Emma — I — to the boys to a concert of fine music for young people — At 2 I went to studio — Seven of my eight boys came \+ made a satisfactorily enthusiastic class — Goodman, Holleander I think, who has done some etching, made a sketch in copper from my head while the others looked on — Then it was bitten — It was nearly 5 when they left — Then I had a short ride on my wheel \+ got home at 6 — Dinner at 6.45 — At 8, with the boys went to Louise Coat's \+ bid her the girls good-bye. They are to sail for Naples to-morrow m'f. Home before 9 — Chase came in shortly after \+ stayed until 10 — Then I went to bed

## Right Page

### Sat. March 26, 1898

**Weather:** Pleasant day — partially overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — partially overcast — to Studio at 9.30 \+ for a time worried over N.A. accts — then set Palette \+ painted on Mad Sy. Garden Power — covering the rest of the canvas as it has not yet been all painted in. At 12 I went to N.A. while there, wrote a note to Beckwith — Home to lunch at 1.30 — After lunch, read a story to the boys \+ was so belated that it was almost 3 o'clk. when I got to studio — Painted on Mad. Power until near 6 — scrubbing in a lot of figures at the last moment — Dinner — after that with the boys until they went to bed at near 9 o'clk — At 9.30 I went out on my wheel — Rode up 5th Ave. to 58th S. \+ down 5th Ave. to the Marble Arch Wash'n Sq. \+ home — about 6 ½ miles — Home at 10.15 — a rub down \+ to bed.
