## Left Page

### Mon. July 11, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasantly cool with a hazy, overcast sky. Interviews with mason \+ plumber after bkfs; — then market \+ grocer — then to N.A. \+ spent some time there on business. Then to 2? Nat. Buk \+ arranged matters there for the summer — then to Brooks bro. \+ bot. a lot of clothes — then to Buskirk's \+ left with him M.S. of my N.A. Treas? Report — then to Descomps \+ paid bill — then home to lunch at 1.20 — Afternoon, interviewed plumber \+ tile man — I don't like the crazed tiles on the bath room walls — then to studio \+ worked there until 5 o'clk. packing things away — Home and rested until dinner at 6.30 — Mrs. W. tells me that she thinks she will return to her flat — consequently our summer arrangement is off. Charl came in after I had written a letter to the boys \+ mailed it at the G.C. Station I went with him to his room \+ he played some Strauss waltzes to stir me up — Home before 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 12, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Partially overcast — strong N. Easterly breeze. After bkfs. some time was given to the men, the carpenters \+ the plumbers — then to studio — to Calverly's with juno mask — to N.A. where I spent an hour \+ a half on N.A. business. Then to Matt Co. 5th Ave \+ 17th St. about bath-room fixtures — to Constables \+ got socks — to Lord \+ Taylor's \+ got neckties — then home to lunch at 1.30 — After that I spent part of an hour writing to George — It was 3.45 when I got to Calverly's \+ gave him a sitting for my bust — he kept me until after 5. I went home \+ finished letter to George \+ had a light supper at 7.15 Mrs. White being at Manhattan Beach. Later, I wrote to the boys \+ mailed the letter at G.C. Station — Then I took a short ride on my wheel, but was driven in by a light, sprinkling rain.
