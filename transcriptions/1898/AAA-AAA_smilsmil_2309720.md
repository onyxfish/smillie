## Left Page

### Sat. May 28, 1898

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m.f. with threats of wet, but in the afternoon there was some mild \+ misty sunlight. I stayed but a short time at studio - then out on a search for my umbrella, which is among the missing - went to Eimer \+ Amend \+ there to 7th \+ saw Mary J - but found no trace of the umbrella - Then to N.A. Left a note for Grannis - then to Calverly's \+ to Butlers and home at 1 - Lunch - The boys \+ Sallie left immediately after \+ went to the Star Theatre where they enjoyed a cheap opera - 10 cents each "Olivette" - I hung about the house until 4 o'clk. Had a call from Mr. Newston - upon whose property the N.A. holds a mtg - about a party wall contract. Then went to Mrs. H.J. Marquand's - 68 5: \+ Mad. Ave. Just meeting of the Advisory Com. to act with Walp re means for f.u.a. - Mr. Marquand, Bishop Potter \+ Mr. Dan'l. Huntington \+ Thos. Hastings the only members present. Some pleasant talk \+ regret from other members - Bishop Potter impressed me as a very clear headed man of the world \+ the advice that he gave was practical \+ valuable. After the meeting Miss Marquand poured tea for us \+ there was a pleasant social half hour. I walked down 5th Ave. with Nicall \+ Watrons - Home after 6 - Dinner at 6.30 - Soon after 8 I went to the Electrical Show at Mad. Square garden taking the boys, Merille Bishop \+ Sallie White. Many Cook Aldua came in for a few minutes before we started - The exhibition was interesting but there was an immense crowd \+ it was hot \+ uncomfortables - The youngsters were interested \+ we dragged about until after 10 o'clk - Then home.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 29, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mean sort of day in the m.f. - overcast with rain enough to wet the sidewalks twice - sunny \+ pleasant for a time in the afternoon - wind easterly all the time - P\&P. late. I read my newspaper until near noon - Then walked to Mr. Jas. Herbert Morse's - 423 Mad. Ave. - he was not in - I saw Mr. M. for a few minutes \+ appointed to call again this p.m. after 5 - Then to Kellie's. Saw her \+ read aletter she rec? Yesterday from Pa. - Home at 1.30 - dinner at 2 - At 4 I went to 75th St. to Dr. Rester's. not in - left a note for him \+ walked through the Park - very charming - some sunshine - music on the mall \+ flowers - It is a beautiful world - \+ an interesting one, as well as very sad. Went to Mr. Morse's at 5.30 \+ saw him. - I told him how anxious I was about Jim - That his progress should be thorough - that I feared he was being dragged over some portions of the course without thoroughly understanding them. I asked him if he could give me no advice by which I could help the boy - I was afraid that he would fall into a state of indifference \+ discouragement. We talked for half an hour but I got nothing that helped me - Home at 6.30 - tea soon after - Sallie took tea at Id \+ Goaks - Eve if I read awhile to Ralph - Soon after 8 I went to Miss Miller's in 40th S: \+ had a talk with her about Ralph - She says there is nothing the matter with him but laziness - I think she is right \+ I see now somewhat more clearly my duty with reference to both of the boys - Home at 9 - Chase There - He left soon after 10 -
