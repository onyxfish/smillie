## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 20, 1898

**Weather:** Sunshine \+ shadow \- fresh north westerly breeze \- good for a well man.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up at about noon \+ then went out to Studio \+ had a short walk \- I didn't feel very brilliant. Dinner at 2. I sat in my place at Table \+ carved for the family, finding it to be quite a chore. I had another short walk in the afternoon \+ then a rest. Son't Charl came in \+ with him \+ Mary I had a talk as to the possibility of Geo. getting the Art Professorship at Vassar College made vacant by the death of Van Ingen. They approved warmly of an effort \- George came in later \+ I promptly broached the subject \+ to our surprise he thought very well of the idea \- after further talk I advised him to go to Po'k to-morrow \+ he agreed to.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 21, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day, bright, sunny, quiet \+ mild. I had my bkfo. in bed \+ got up about 9.30 feeling very well. I had a number of things to do that kept me busy until after 11 when I went to the N.A. of D. I walked to 32nd St. \+ Rode the rest of the way. I was more than half an hour at the N.A. in arranging a number of things that have been waiting for my attention for some time \+ then I sauntered all the way home getting there without feeling tired. Lunch at 1. In the afternoon I took another short walk out \+ then I lay down for a rest. I wrote a report for N.A. Council \+ sent it by messenger. I wrote also to young Smith about fine ins. on N.A. Charl came in the evng. I got to bed early much out of sorts with thick headaches.
