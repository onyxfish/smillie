## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 3, 1898

**Weather:** overcast a part of the time — then sunny — very warm \+ close  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got up feeling pretty well. B\+p. at 8.30 — after that went to Douglas's \+ to Miller's — then to Alice Ireland \+ saw her. Then home. — Emily Cook called — They are to move from 42d S. to Mad. Ave. to-day — I asked her, rfp., to dine with us \+ stay all night. Carpenter came soon after 11 — rain commenced soon after noon \+ quite used me up between 2 \+ 4 o'clk. Jim's friend Seny Plumber lunched with us — at about 4.30 I sent Ralfsr to the N.A. p.d. to Frannie with Check book \+ letters connected with the Benedict case — also a note from me to the Council resigning my place as Pres. — Geo. came in \+ I spent some time with me — Geo. Cook \+ Emily came at 7 — had dinner \+ went to bed about 8 — I read aloud to the boys until 9.30 — to bed about 10.30 —

Kate Leonard went to Mary Moony's yesterday \+ is there now, sick. Mary M — says she thinks it is an attack of Gripo.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 4, 1898

**Weather:** Slightly overcast in the m\+f. Then sunny, muggy \+ humid — thunderstorm \+ heavy shower about 5 P.M. — altogether a most depressing day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I felt very weak \+ incapable of doing anything, mental or physical \+ though without actual pain had hard work to drag about all day — Geo. Cook \+ Emily left immediately after B\+p. The carpenter continued his work \+ finished my closet so that I was able to put away some of my clothes. Lunch at 1 — After that Grannis came with checks for me to endorse, \+ after him Maynard came in \+ we had a doleful time together. He is not well, has no orders \+ is in the dumps. Dinner at 6.30 — Evening Charl came in at 9 \+ stayed for an hour.

Afternoon I went with Ralfsr to Rogers \+ Peet's \+ did some shopping for him, we got home just before the heavy shower.
