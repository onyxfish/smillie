## Left Page

### Mon. May 2, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mixed day — not pleasant — nor yet violently unpleasant — There was some sunshine about the middle of the day \+ light rain at night. To studio at usual time. Before 11 Hungerford \+ Bullock came \+ for a time they worked without hitch, ticking off the accts. as I have arranged them — Then, about noon they wanted the Petty Cash book \+ I went to N.A. for it — Home to lunch at 1 — To studio at 1.30 — H. \+ B. There \+ they worked until about 4 — then they left \+ I went to N.A. with check \+ Cash books — Home after 5 \+ had a rest before dinner. — At 8 went to Council meeting where in addition to my duties as Treas. I took George's place as Recording Secy. — There was no business of especial interest — Home \+ to bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### Tues. May 3, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nasty day. Wet most of the time. nizzling rather than raining. Got to studio about 9.30 \+ worked until after 11 writing notes to various people — most time given to answer a note from Helen Butler rec'd last ev'g. — I was correct in supposing that the roses that were sent to the house on Sat. ev'g were from her. In her note that followed after them she writes evi-dently under the belief that I had no faith in a hereafter — I wrote to correct her. While I cannot accept without question what are known as orthodox news of immortality I have not ceased to hope \+ believe that there is a spiritual life of some kind that follows this. — About 11.30 went to A.A. — Then to N.A. — Then to the bank \+ home to lunch at 1 — to studio at 2 \+ worked on N.A. accts. — Hungerford \+ Bullock came in bet. 3 \+ 4 o'clk. \+ worked until after 6 with the usual result of getting me all tangled up \+ announcing as crushing results from their postings — It is exceedingly wearying to me — and worrying. — The Com. to examine the accts. are to meet on Thurs. ev'g. \+ from present appearances I will not be ready for them. It was near 7 when I got home to dinner — Read awhile to the boys \+ then worried over my bank book, just balanced, until 10.
