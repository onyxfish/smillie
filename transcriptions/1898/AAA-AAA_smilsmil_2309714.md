## Left Page

### Mon. May 16, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another mean sort of day — leaden over head. East wind + more or less wet. I went to studio at about 10 a.m., there for a short time — then to N.A., where I spent the rest of the m'f on accts. Home at 1. Lunch — after that wrote a letter to Mary — So wet that I would not go out — Rested — Dinner at 6.30 — Every went to Council meeting. N.A. got there early + had a private talk with Hart in which I was pleased to find that he strongly opposed the opposition to me that would not examine the books as I urged them to do — Weldon came in + took his place by courtesy as Councillor elect. I acted as Recording Sec'y Pro-tem and had a pretty busy time until "nearly" o'clk — then, Council having adj'd, I excused myself + went home + to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 17, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious day — good enough to make up for two bad ones. To studio at about 10 + after a little confab with Maynard I went to N.A. + spent the rest of the m'f. making out checks for painters of sold pictures, Prizes in the schools + instructors. Home at 1. Lunch. Ralph came to studio + after I had nearly finished a letter to Geo. I worked with him getting up a nest, a la Darcview, for observation of suite. At 4.30 I took Charles camera, loaded with films, (I had had it out with me in the m'f.) + R. + I went to Wash'n. Sq. and there I took a number of snaps at the Wash'n. Arch. Then we walked home. Dinner in the Eve + I read to the boys from "Captain's Courageous" until 8.40 then I finished letter to Geo. + at 9 o'clk. went to Margaret White's + saw her by appointment about her taking up her abode at 110. She is to call to talk with Kate + Jane to-morrow p.m. I was there for an hour, then home + to bed.
