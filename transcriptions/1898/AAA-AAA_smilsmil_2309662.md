## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 1, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The misses Aitee arr. from Phila

Bright for a little while in the morning then more snow — almost a blizzard at one time. — To studio + painted on Sandris Rey — finishing it for the present — now in shape to put away to dry. — After lunch I went to 2d. Natl Bnk — then to studio. — Home about 5. — the Misses Aitee f Phila., acquaintances of the Brooks girls, arr. in the afternoon to make a visit of a week. — Every They all went to Charl's. I remained with the boys + after they went to bed busied myself with writing.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 2, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, clear + cold. — Went to studio full of determination to paint something with more of freshness + spontaneity in it than my average work. — Have been thinking of a small study that I painted from my window at Marblehead Neck in 1882 "After the Storm" — a murky sky — low-lying, heavy clouds and sullen water. — I took an old picture, exhibited in the NA. years ago, over which I had smeared palette cleanings. — Upon this, without preparatory drawing, I paint. — [illegible] my picture — about 30' long. — I finished at 4 o'clk — called Maynard in — he said he liked it very much. —
Then I had a half hours walk before dinner — a quiet evelf. — Most of the time writing in my room. — Charl came in + amused the girls unit [illegible] —
