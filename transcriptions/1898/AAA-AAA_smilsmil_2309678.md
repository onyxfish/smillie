## Left Page

### Sat. March 5, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright very pleasant day — to studio + enroute to Barmore, w. Warner, Jat. Morris + Spense. — after that walked to Architectural League Show at the Fine Arts Bldg in W. 57 St. — there until near 1 — then home to lunch — at studio — more writing until after 4 — then home + had a rest before dinner — after dinner I walked to 4th Ave. + 26th St. where I took a long look, taking mental notes. at the Mad. Sq. Garden tower. The moon was just in right position — needed color very fine + the foreground full of interest — I would give much if I could feel well to stay for a week + have the time to paint a 24×40 sketch. I think I could make something like a Nocturne. — then I walked to Central W, 43 St. my two little paintings: "The Valley of the Arno" + "Sandinis Rey Mission" look most distressingly bad — especially the first. It was most depressing to me. Home at midnight + to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 6, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day — but unfortunately for me, I was in no condition to enjoy it, being under the malign influence of war in the digestives tract. Just before 11 Sam. T. Shaw called with list of winners of Prizes in N.Y. exhibs since '87. He is going to get up a show of them at Athletic Club + he wishes me to help trace ownership of some of them. I went to N.A. + worked for an hour or more over lists of sales to get the information with but little success — then to Century — my work there looks but little better by day light — met Mace + walked part way home with him — Dinner at 2 — wrote a note to Sam Shaw + left it for him at Grand Central Hotel — then walked up town to Boulev'd 66th St. + from there rode to 110th St. inspected N.A. site + looked for evidences of borings, exploring the bottoms for foundation walls — rode home — tea at 6.30 — read to the boys until 8.30 — at 9.30 went to Charles' — Mary, Kate, the girls + Charles there — it was near 11 before I got away.
