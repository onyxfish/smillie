## Left Page

### Mon. July 25, 1898

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m˚7, clouds burning away by 10 o'clk - pleasant breeze - but hot + humid. Went to house very soon after bkfs - Ralph had a ride on his pony - Jim went off to Elk Lake with three boys for an all day fishing excursion - the boys to camp, he to return with horse + wagon - We got a fire made in kitchen stove - Lazier worked on big swinging doors easing them - I wrote an informal agreement for Carleton Comstock - To Sayres about 12.30 Pain in stomach came on about half an hour before dinner but passed away before I had finished eating. Again to house + found N Warner waiting for me - we decided how he was to fix up the ceiling of Kitchen - Then I wrote to Jas. Hart concerning Bentz's Statement closing the Cat. Ad, business - also a letter to J.C. Beckwith stating my reasons for thinking that it would be best to give LeG. Benedict a chance to pay his note to me h.a. - This letter by agreement was to be sent to Hart + by him forwarded to Beckwith. Ralph came to the house about 5 in midst of heavy thunder shower, thoroughly wet, had to change to his skin - Had been to Sayre's farm haying. Jim got back about 6 - having escaped the shower entirely - had a good string of fish - Every shower again - I did not go out. Tried to read but could not - had pain after supper which passed away in an hour. To bed early

## Right Page

### Tues. July 26, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Steady, quiet rain most of last night - the rain continued until after 9 - cloudy until 2 - then rain again until 4 - clearing - fine moonlight eve'g. To house soon after bkfs. Mr. Dodge, colored, working there at house cleaning - Warner + assistant, working on kitchen ceiling - I worked correcting pf. of N.A. report - much discomfort in stomach - At 11.30 went to P.O. then to Dr. Gardner's - saw him + he changed medicine. To studio again - with letters from Grannis + Mcland (C.S.F. has applied for a Civil service clerkship) + from Parke + Woodin. Dinner at 1.30 - Rain prevented me from going to studio until 4 - Then wrote to Fred Ireland + Charl + to P.O. at 5.30. Saw Fanny Connell + her younger son - just going on 6 o'clk Train. Tea at 6.30 At 8 went to P.O. then to the Misses Biddle + made a short call - To Sayre's before 9 - read until 10.
