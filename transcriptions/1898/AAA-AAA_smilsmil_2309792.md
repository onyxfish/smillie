## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 21, 1898

**Weather:** Dull, disagreeable  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, disagreeable day \+ wet night \- I had a comfortable night \+ awoke feeling better \- I had my b\. p\. in bed \+ read in the Tribune Coon's Preliminary Obituary notice of me \+ my Resignation on the Editorial page. There was much in it that was mortifying to me. Yale came before noon. He seems to be very confident that he knows just what is the matter with me. I got up \+ went down stairs to lunch \- A Herald Reporter came to see me \- I got thro' the afternoon with little discomfort until near 6 o'clk then severe Stomach pain came on lasting for more than an hour \- I ate a chop while in pain \- later I had very bitter eructations from the stomach \- my evening was a dismal one \- to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 22, 1898

**Weather:** Dark \+ rainy m'g  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark \+ rainy m'g, clearing sunny for a time in the afternoon \- An almost imperceptible chill wakened me at 1 A.m. Temp. 99° which increased slowly until 4 A.m. when it reached its highest, 100⅜°. Miss T \- Took a specimen of my blood \- Yale came about 10 \+ told me that he had made an app't with Dr. Janeway to meet him here for consultation at 2.45 \- altho' the attack was so slight I was much weakened by it. Yale \+ Janeway came on time \+ I was subjected to a very thorough hammering, \+ punching \+ squeezing \- \+ at last J \- said that he could not find proof by his examination that any thing was the matter with me. Before they left Dr. Gerster came \+ he went over me \- Dr. Wood reported that he found no miasmatic bug in my blood \- Of course it is comforting to know that they found no evidences of diseased condition of any of the organs, but that does not relieve my pain or add to my strength.
