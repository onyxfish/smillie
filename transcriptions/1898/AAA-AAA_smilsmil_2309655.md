## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 18, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day. Worked all day on Water-Color, Susquehanna. It continues to grow more hopelessly commonplace \+ dull. If I had a little more moral courage I would destroy it at once \+ begin anew. Home to lunch \+ back as usual. Worked on drawing as long as I could see — then to work on my bicycle, getting that cleaned up — while engaged at that Mr. W.f. Parke of Scranton came in \+ after a few minutes I took he to a 34th St. car — then I had a half hours walk before dinner. Every Read awhile to R — Charl came in at about 8.30 \+ took Kate to his room \+ I took myself to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, with light rain at night. All day at work upon Susquehanna, W.C. making very considerable changes in its color. A short walk before lunch \+ again between 4 \+ 5 p.m. Then I rested until dinner time. Evening to meeting of Water-Color Soct. where I took an active part — first, in the consideration of the O.W. Palper that has been so liberally distributed among the members for last trial — They are provokingly alpa-tetic — They scrambled for the Palper — got it — now have nothing to say, 'good, bad or indifferent.' Then I led off in the discussion on the nomination of a candidate for Fine Arts Com'n to Paris Exp'n 1900 — To instruct delegates to F.A. Federation to vote for Jno. Cauldwell — J.C. got all the votes but one for Coffin. After the meeting I spent some time in the office with Collins who is writing up the N.A. books.
