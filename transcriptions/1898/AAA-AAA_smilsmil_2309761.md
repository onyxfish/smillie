## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 18, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More cloud \+ sunshine — The clouds sometimes down on the mt. tops — The day much cooler. After bkf't. at 9.30, I went down the road \+ at the Nielson gate found my man — I at once set him to work Chopping \+ soon open a vista to the view north. I stayed with him for more than an hour \+ after laying out his work for him I went over to my neighbor's, Mr. McGee \+ had a very pleasant call there — after that Mr. McG. took me up to Mr. White's house where I saw Mr. \+Mrs. W— I was shown over their house \+ that of Mrs. [illegible], Their daughter \+ Mr. W— gave me much practical information on the matter of building here — to the hotel to lunch at 1.30 — after that I again went to my hill \+ remained with Ed \+ [illegible] the chopper until 5 — I had several callers — the story has gone abroad that I am surely going to build. It has made some excitement among the cottages. Mr. Hardenburg came \+ spent half an hour with me — In her case my stupidity was peculiarly assenine — I took her for Mrs. McGee's mother — I cannot tell why — It was a bad blunder — Young Nielson came with dog \+ gun — Then I went to gate house of Reservation \+ saw Mr. Scott Brown \+ the map of this region showing my plot — He said that he had been looking for me for the past 10 years — I saw my Sidney Smith \+ her son Phillip — To dinner at 6.30 — The boys had been up the River Trail — I was tired \+ had a good deal of stomach discomfort. I got a batch of letters from Charl. from Germany, Kate \+ Katherine — also from Jas. Hart and grannies — check from Carleton Comstock.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ threatening rain — clearing in the afternoon — a very cool day — I got up feeling very seedy after a restless night \+ began the day dubiously but improved as the day went on. Bkf't. was late. I called on Mr. Sidney Smith — \+ got letters from Charl — one thing \+ another took my time until 11 o'clk — when I went to my hill to look after my wood-chopper — While there, Mr. Field of Newark came \+ introduced himself — he wished to purchase the hill, but I told him that I was preparing to build on it for myself. The boys \+ I took a tramp over Windy Brow — then to the Hotel \+ had luncheon — After that I sent checks to 2d. Nat'l. Bk. \+ wrote business notes in connection therewith — Then, for a time we watched the Golf Contest — St. Hubert's Inn \+ pt. Elizabethtown — Then, at 4 o'clk — Jim, Ralph \+ I walked in to the Reservation \+ up the River Trail for an hour \+ then back at 6 o'clk — Rested until 6.30 — then dressed \+ went to dinner — We had music until 9 — then I went to the library, \+ sat with Miss Powell \+ her father to hotel \+ we chatted until 10 — then to bed — Quite a cold way. Wood fires in the fireplaces.
No rain to-day.
