## Left Page

### Thur. May 26, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Horrible day \- strong N.S. wind \+ heavy driving rain \- 10 o'clk when I got to studio \- Then went to Central Iron works \- to J. B. Butlers \+ ordered boy for Goldsmith's Daughter \- met Whettredge there \+ while we waited for the rain to hold up a little, he told me some very interesting stories of his early life \- \+ how he formed a Partnership with another young fellow to travel thro' the country as Portrait Painters \- how they stopped at Charlestown in Va. \+ commenced work \- his partner drank most of the time \- a married woman almost succeeded in seducing him \- he awake to his danger one night, burned his work \+ belongings \+ early the next mng took flight \- etc \- etc \- Kree to N. a. \- men to 2\. N. y. : Bank \+ home at 1.20 \- lunch \- to Studio at 3 \+ went to worked a cataloguing the contents of one of my closets \- Then home to dinner \- Eveg. I read more Kipling to R \- took my Goldsmith's Daughter pf \- out of its frame \+ got another proof ready to put into it \- After 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 27, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull cold day, wet mng \- walks drying off by noon. To studio at 10 \- For awhile cataloguing contents of one of my closets \- At 11.30 I went to Cor. 4th \+ Macdougal St \+ saw Bessie Parke at her book binding under Miss Nordhoff. I had an extended interview with Miss N \- explaining to her some of the methods for getting best results in using acids in Etching \- Home at 1.30 \- lunch \- to Studio at 2\. 5 of my Etching class with Giovanni Mandaro in Moorish Costume for Model \- They worked faithfully until 5\. Then I went to 331 E. 17th St. to bid Minna Ireland \+ Grace good-bye \- They sail to-morrow for England \- met there Katherine J \- \+ Helen Butler. It was nearly 7 when I got home to dinner \- Mr. White went to Boston to-night to be gone until Tues. next. Sallie remains here. Eveg. I read to Ralph, finishing Captains Courageous. I wrote part of a note to Minie Gunther when interrupted by Charl who stayed until after 10. Butler got my framed proof of Goldsmith's Daughter to send to Amy Smillie Gunther as a wedding present.
