## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 10, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty m'f. Slight fall of snow — then wet & dark, but in the afternoon early the weather cleared charmingly — I got to studio on time & worked all the m'f on Water-color "The ford. Ev'f." but last got done with it — It turns out to be a fair specimen of Spinach — that is all the comfort that I can get out of it. Afternoon I tackled the body-color drawing that I made about a year ago, "Vineyards near Ravello". It is much confused, owing to many objects in it, & lacks atmosphere — I set to work upon it with pastels & greatly improved it, I think; pulling it together & spreading an atmosphere over distance &shy. Home before 5 and rested until dinner — Council meeting in the Ev'f — a long session, continued after adjournment by a discussion between Beckwith on the one side & about all of the rest on the other, as to which part of the new Academy should be built first — The Bld'g Com. is unanimously of the opinion that, as suggested by the Architects, the facade should be built first, a part of it adapted to the housing of the schools — Beckwith opposes this very earnestly — he wants to see the school bldg on 109th S. erected first. I do not think his arguments are strong & do not quite understand why he opposes so strongly — Home & to bed after midnight.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 11, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day — some drizzle at night. To studio on time. Most of the m'f spent fussing in getting frames together & the drawings into them. I continued, I completed the work on the "Vineyards" — I wrote two or three N.A. notes. To lunch — Afternoon, I took drawing "On the Road to Ravello" out of its frame & worked on that as long as I could see. Home at 5. Ev'f. Read awhile to Ralph — then a little writing — a little reading & to bed.
