## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 25, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day, altho' clouded over part of the time. After a tolerably quiet night I recorded normal temps. Dr. Yale came \+ changed medecine with the purpose of clearing my foul tongue — calomel \+ Sub-gallate of Bismuth — Having taken Carter's Liver Pills last night I had two very complete evacuations \+ continued to improve in my feelings as the day wore on until the eveny when I feel more free from pain \+ discomfort than at any time for the past two weeks. I dressed in the afternoon but did not go downstairs. Maynard called in the afternoon \+ Watrous \+ Charl in the evef. My callers did not leave until 10 o'clk. \+ I got to bed about 10.30 — I had not been in bed long before a mouse began cavorting about \+ at last got on to my bed \+ down between the mattress \+ the side, making a great scratching — I lighted my candle twice; hunted for him \+ tried to scare him away until at last I could not compose myself — At 12.30 I rung for miss T — \+as she came to the door the mouse ran across her naked feet — while she was still by me the mouse got to scratching on the mattress \+ she heard it. Together we took the bed clothing all off \+ turned the mattress — She left me \+ again I tried to compose myself to sleep but the little torment soon returned \+ at 2 o'clk I gave it up \+ went down to the spare room where Jim was sleeping in the large bed \+ crept in alongside of him. ## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 26, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable, dark, wet day. I lay in Jim's bed until 4.30 A.M. unable to get to sleep and thoroughly upset. Jim is not a quiet bedfellow \+ at 4.30 I retreated to my own bed. I made a full mess of Horlich's malted milk, very hot \+ drank it \+ got some sleep before 6 — then I woke, my shirts all wet with perspiration — I had them changed \+ again got some dreamy sleep but at 7 awoke again, so wet that I had to have a second change — then I slept until near 9 when I had some beef tea — again I slept until Dr. Yale came after 10 — The horrible experience of the night that had left me pitifully weak, seemed to amuse rather than surprise him. During the day, being in bed \+ having no solid food, while I had stomach discomfort I did not suffer actual pain — Emma Ireland came late in the afternoon \+ made a pleasant \+ comforting call — After she left I got up \+ dressed. I went down to the parlor. Stomach pain soon got the better of me \+ for relief I went to bed at 9.30 — I soon went to sleep \+ passed a comfortable \+ restful night.
