## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 28, 1898

**Weather:** Cold \+ raw \- partially overcast \- light suns fell at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent the day painting on Sanduis Rey Mission \- trying to feel my way out on the middle part where the lower walls lie in shadow\- I cannot get rid of a heavy gloom on that part, which is neither natural or desirable. Well, I read awhile to the boys \+ at 9 o'clk. went to Stag View, Water Color Socy. \- met so many there who made themselves so agreeable that it was after 11.30 when I got home. Ge\[illegible\] Heaven told me that he still owned my painting "the lifting of the Cloud" that I had not money enough to buy it, \- that he would not, so long as he lived, part with it \+ that he intended to leave it to the N.A. of D. \- all of which is rather distressing to me. I have not seen it for more than 20 years, \+ from my recollection of it I fear that it is a very fine thing \-
After lunch I went to Brooks Bros. with my new coat \+ left it there for alterations. Then to Am. Art Ass\[ociation\] galleries \+ tried to see the remarkable Stewart paintings, but there were too many people. I got from Kirby a card to admit me on Sunday.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 29, 1898

**Weather:** Snow in the m\[orning\], \- a little more than an inch \- but about noon the weather cleared to a brilliant, cold \+ cloudless winter day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I painted all the m\[orning\] on "Sanduis Rey" but could not get beyond the sticking place \- the middle where I wish to represent a subdued, diffused light; \- hazy \+ grey, but with plenty of color.
After lunch I went down to office of [illegible] \+ spent a couple of hours with W.H.L.Lee discussing N.A. business \- the payment of the Meyer m\[oney\] \- the foreclosure of the Alex. Brown m\[ortgage\] \- the new lease of the \[illegible\] from Met. Life \- the course to be pursued to assure release of new sites from Taxation \- \+ changes in constitution of N.A. to enlarge the powers of the Pres\[iden\]t. To studio at 4.30 Cleared up \+ home at 5 \- Eve\[ning\] f\[amily\]. Read to Ralph until after 9 \- Then I finished off the m\[orning\] with the Every\[body's\] \+ to bed.

Will do\[ne\] at Studio in the m\[orning\] \+ waited here until Maynard came.
