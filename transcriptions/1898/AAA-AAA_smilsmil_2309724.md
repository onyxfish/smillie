## Left Page

### Sun. June 5, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day - up \+ breakfasted at the usual time. I spent all the m'f in-doors trying to arrange things in my room - about my desk, etc. - but I only got confusion worse mixed. At noon Mr. White took the boys to a Recruiting ship lying at foot of 28th S: East River. getting back just in time for dinner at 2. At 3.30 I went to Calverly's \+ was there for two hours while he modeled on the bust as I posed for him. New home \+ with the two boys rode to 110th 8: then walked through the Park. on to the west side \+ back east to the Car Stables at 86th S: \+ Mad. Ave. Rode home - tea at 7.30. Every Charl came in - At 9 I went to Ed.W. Cook for a little while about the Cleveland dock business - Walter \+ Gogo (or Walter Junr.) there - Home in half an hour -

## Right Page

### Mon. June 6, 1898

*Jim's birthday.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, warm day. After my regular m'f duties I went to Richter Mf'g Co. in 23d S: and had further consultation with regard to the color \+ decoration of Burlaps for the hall. Then to N.A. \+ spent the rest of the m'f there finishing the acct. Home to lunch. Detained after it by looking after the men that have begun tearing down the covering on the hall wall. Got to Calverly's after 3 \+ sat for him for an hour while he modelled on my bust. Then to N.A. \+ worked there until 5. Then walked thro' Mad. Sq. \+ up to Decomps' in 33d S: near 7. Ave. made appointment to be there with Jim at 3 p.m. to-morrow. Then to studio \+ butchers' \+ home at 6.30 Gave Jim a birthday dinner. He invited Ned Thurston, Seuey Plummer \+ Merrill Bishop. The dinner was a good one \+ the boys all seemed to enjoy it. They left soon after 9. Charl came \+ I went with him to his "dark Room" where he introduced me to the ropes that I may know how to take care of myself alone there. Home at 10.30.
