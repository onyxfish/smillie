## Left Page

### Sat. May 14, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A charming day — I did not get up until about 2 o'clk. Yale came in the mtf \+ as temperature was still above normal advised me to keep in bed awhile longer. The boys went off to Staten Island in the mtf \+ spent the day there butterfly hunting, getting back at 6 o'clk, with one little butterfly measuring about 5/8" across outstretched wings — but they said they had had a good time. Maynard called for a few minutes in the afternoon \+ later Bessie Parke called on me. I spent part of the day reading "Quo Vadis."

## Right Page

### Sun. May 15, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day of dark \+ rain until late in the afternoon when the rain ceased. I got through last night without fever \+ some small comfort — I went to bed early \+ finding that, after an hour I could not get to sleep, I lighted my candle \+ read in bed until midnight then I fell asleep \+ rested well until 5\. Before 6 I got up \+ had my scrub \+ then I went to bed again \+ had some sleep until 7.30 when Jane came to me with a bowl of Hafergrannen. I soon got up \+ at 9.30 had a light bkfst. with the boys. Later Yale came in \+ chatted with me for awhile — he finds my temp sub-normal again. I read Quo Vadis until dinner — at 2 — \+ later — \+ then I wrote a note to Mrs. Margaret White — Sue\+, I wrote out minutes of last Council meeting. also letter to Jenkins Bros. \+ to Lee \+ Lee, directing foreclosure proceedings — at 9.30 went out, mailed letters \+ went to Studio \+ got letters there.
