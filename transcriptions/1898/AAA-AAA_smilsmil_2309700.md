## Left Page

### Mon. April 18, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got up with a dull headache that stuck to me all day, using me up nearly at night. I had to hustle around after 6&6. to get to the Safe Deposit Co. 5th Ave \+ 23d S. \+ get Cowman mtg. Then to Mr. Cowmans office at 10 where Geo. Cook met us \+ we complied with all the formalities required in giving "Satisfaction piece" on paymt. of mtg. held by me on 6eq. Ave. Cor. 40th S for $5000 —. Then I went to Lee \+ Lee's \+ saw W. H. due on a business — then to tax office \+ learned that the assessors have raised the valuation of 1102, 38th S. from 15000 to 18000 dollars — then to 2d Nat. Bank and deposited the Cowman check for $5114.5. Then home to lunch — then down to Well S. again to Cont. Trust Co. \+ paid $55.00 on acct. jury loan there. Then in to Charles \+ saw him \+ his new office — then to Lee's again concerning the increase of mtg. on studio bldg. to take the place of the $500 mtg. just paid — then to Safe Deposit \+ got mtg. \+ home at 5 o'clk — Had a rest before dinner \+ Every went to Council meeting — head aching very badly — got through with difficulty \+ walked up with Geo. at 10.30

## Right Page

### Tues. April 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the night — dull mfly \+ rain during the afternoon \+ night — I was engaged about the house until near 11 o'clk. Then I went to market \+ after that to W. Pate \+ Co. where I dickered with Albert H. Pate for the purchase of "The Goldsmith's Daughter" plate that I etched some years ago \+ sold to them for $500 — I think that they did not even get their money back in publishing it. A.H.P. agreed to sell it to me for $150 — $150 cash, $50 \+ 12 days \+ $50 in 45 days. By this I secure the plate agt. possible abuse in the future. I propose to work upon it some more — then have one hundred fine p.p's taken, then cut the plate up — To H.D. for a while — then home to lunch \+ after that to bed — my headaching severely — \+ there I lay until 5 o'clk — then got up feeling somewhat better — Then, by appointment, to Margaret White's 6in. 6e. \+ 27th St. had an hour's talk with her. She has taken apartments in 34th S. but, after she has moved into there she \+ her daughter will come to my house to stay with me next now, hoping that she will bring cheerfulness for the boys in their empty home as well as to have some care for them. Dinner at 6.30 — Every. Clark came in for an hour between 9.30 \+ 10.30
