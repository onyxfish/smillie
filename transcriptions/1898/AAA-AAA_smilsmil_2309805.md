## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 18, 1898

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, disagreeable day, more or less wet, heavy rain at night — Got up at 10.30, very groggy. — went down to parlor \+ by driving myself managed to do a little work — Yale came in in the m'g. to have a chat with me \+ to see how I was getting along. I gave a very poor \+ discouraging acct. of myself but he insists that I am doing as well as can be expected. In the afternoon Katherine \+ Bessie went to New Haven to spend a few days with Mallie Adams \+ to see the great football game between Yale \+ Harvard. After an uncomfortable ev'g. I began to feel unaccountably better at about 10 o'clk. when I went to bed \+ had a very comfortable night. Mrs Jeanette Johnston Sutphen lunched with Mary \+ I saw her for awhile.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain most of the day, letting up in the afternoon — dry evening. I got up soon after 10 — man from M.A. with checks to endorse \+ other business, before I got dressed. Before lunch Bessie Parke came — Before I got through with my lunch Gramnis came with a lot more of N.A. work \+ before he got away Dr. Gerster came \+ spent half an hour with me — He was very encouraging in his assurances as to my physical prospects. I lay on my bed for an hour in the afternoon resting, but did not sleep. Dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g. in parlor — Charl came in \+ we talked of the death of Van Ingen — the Vassar Art Prof. vacancy \+ the possibility of inducing Gee to make application for it — I have felt so much better to-day as to be greatly encouraged.
