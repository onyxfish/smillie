## Left Page

### Fri. July 1, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A terribly hot day. Rec'd letter from Katy Elkin stating that Mollie had gone to the Adirondacks \+ answering for her she invited the boys \+ me to spend the 4th at New Haven. after 6½p. I went to G. C. Station \+ telegraphed to Kaley — great crowd then to Miller's \+ to studio — worked there all the m'f \+ made good progress. Lunch at 1. Packed valise until 3.30 — then the boys \+ I took our wheels \+ went to station \+ after waiting about 40 m. we gave it up as hopeless \+ returned to the house — The crowd \+ crush was beyond description — I then went to N.A. to meet The Audit Com. \+ Hungerford \+ Bullock — Dieleman did not put in an appearance, but Frank Jones — Alfred Jones, Yewell \+ Perry were there. I stayed about half an hour \+ left them — went to Calverly's with Jim's mask which is very satisfactory, but Calverly was out — then home \+ was just in time to meet the Express man for valise. Then Jim \+ I went to the station with our wheels \+ altho' there was still a great crush I got wheels \+ valise checked — then home to dinner. Teddy White dined with us — The Whites went out to spend the Eve'g — The boys \+ I got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 2, 1898

**Weather:** Bright \+ pleasant — cooler than yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at 6 o'clk. Bkfst. at 7 — at 7.40 went to Station \+ were just in time for the opening of the doors to the 8 o'clk Train — met miss Henrietta Beebe who spoke to me — I did not at first recognize her, she has grown so stout. The passengers were many but the train was a long one \+ was not uncomfortably full — we had a pleasant trip to N.H. getting [illegible] on time at 10.05 — found our bicycles without trouble \+ got to Dr. Adams' in Edwards St. at about 10.30 — There we found Dr. A., Katie Elkin \+ Miss Joslyn \+ learned that last night Nellie had two convulsions, resulting from uremic poisoning — she has had as many as [illegible] of these attacks within the year past — usually followed by severe sick headache — she was recovering rapidly \+ resting easily. Katie invited us to go to her house, 457 Prospect St. \+ at about 11.30 we all walked up there — A bread ache that had been growing upon me all the m'f then took possession \+ I did not get up until after 8. P.M. — The boys went to bed at 9 \+ I sat with Dr. S. \+ Katie until 10 — then they went to bed \+ I walked in the moonlight until a little after 11 — then to bed.
