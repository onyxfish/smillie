## Left Page

### Wed. June 15, 1898

**Weather:** Bright + breezy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright + breezy \- a vitality in the air making the weather much pleasanter than it has been for some days past. Wrote to Mrs. Barhydt after bkf't, then, with the plumber. It was nearly 11 o'clk when I got to the N.A. worked with Grannis for nearly an hour trying to find what appears to be an error somewhere in the Expenditure accts \- Then Ralph came + we went to the 2d Nat'l. Bank + then to Rogers & Peet to have a summer coat that I bought for him some time ago altered to fit him \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- at N.A. at 2.30 + for more than an hour G \- \+ I worked over the problem of the mf't. At last, at my wits end + worn out, I said that I would make a clean statement on a sheet of paper of the figures as we had them + then I would give up for the day trusting that to-morrow we could find the cause of our trouble. \- We had been trying to find out how, or why we had $50.99 too much \- Suddenly, as I looked at the figures, I said, "why man! That is the amt. you have in your Petty Cash box" \- \+ so it was, it was exactly right \+ for more than three hours we had been trying to prove it was wrong \- Then I went to Keap's. \+ walked home \- dinner \- Ev'g. my paper \- Then I wrote to Dielman + Lee \-

## Right Page

### Thur. June 16, 1898

**Weather:** Bright, with cool northerly winds  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, with cool northerly winds getting around to Easterly later in the day \-, Spent most of the day at N.A. fixing up into finished form my corrected + verified accounts Grannis is now in trouble to make his School Records agree with mine, as they must \- Home to lunch at 1 + after lunch went to Carver + Hastings, but saw no one \- then to Hankinson's \- to Soc'y for Prev'n of Cruelty to Animals + then to N.A. worked there until 4.30 \- then home \- The boys were out on their wheels so I took a ride alone through the Park + got home at 6 \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Ev'g. I had a discussion with Mrs. White growing out of her reading to me some short poems by Mrs. Stetson. She finds in a sinner only an amusing + interesting variation of humanity + nothing abhorrent \- I tried to defend an opposing position but succeeded only in making myself feel very much of a fraud + a pharisee.
