## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 12, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day — dark & wet — To studio on time & worked all the m'f on "Road to Ravells" — much to its advantage, I think. Home to lunch — back & some time spent in getting the drawings into their frames & ready to send to the Sub'm. — Then I worked for awhile on a little old sketch — "inside the fort at St. Augustine, Florida" — Then I went to N.A. & found business enough there to keep me until after 6. I had a short look through the galleries at the Eastman Photographic Show — Home to dinner — Evely. — Read to Ralph — Then busied myself for a time, filing away old bills — I found a number of my letters written from Europe in '93 — & one or two of Annie's — & I read some of them with a great heart ache as I realized the poverty of my life — True — I have the boys — best of boys — but —

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 13, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Milder — Streaked day of sunshine & shadow — To studio at 9.30 & until 11.30 I worked on an old W. C. sketch in old fort at St. Augustine — Fla. Then I went to Allen's, 47th St. near 7th Ave & made appointment with him to fix me of my title next Monday at 3.30 p.m. Then walked home & had half an hour before lunch to assort some of my old receipted bills — After lunch, to studio, a head ache came on which distressed me all the rest of the day & even. At 4.30 went to Century & had, by appointment with Jno. Cauldwell on the subject of his accepting a nomination of Fine Arts Com. to the Paris Exp'n of 1900 — He is willing & I intend to urge his nomination in com. to-morrow p.m. at 5. com. n Ways & means met, but after half an hour I excused myself on acct. of my head ache & went home — I got little chance to rest as D. P. Collins came & kept me some time arranging with him to write Academy books up to date. 7.15 & Trilby Cook came late to dinner. 7.15 I got Mrs' dinner with some little trouble & feel somewhat better later.
