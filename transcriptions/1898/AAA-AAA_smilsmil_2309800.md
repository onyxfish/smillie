## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 8, 1898

**Weather:** Pleasant n'f., clouded afternoon.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I awake in the n'f after a restless but not exhaustive night. I spent the n'f in my bed \+ had calls from Watrous, W. George, Charles \+ Dr. Yale. I enjoyed my meals \+ felt a little stronger. I got up at about 3.30 \+ went down to parlor, kindled fire \+ spent the rest of the afternoon \+ eve'g there. I had my dinner there. Charl came in. I went to bed soon after 9.

Kate went to Po't in the afternoon.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 9, 1898

**Weather:** Pleasant for a time about the middle of the day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. Kingsbury, from Lee \+ Lee, came before 10 o'clk, but thro' a mis-understanding he had not got from Geo. the satisfaction piece for the Owston m'fg. A messenger had to be sent up to Geo. for it — a delay of an hour. Then I signed \+ swore to the seal. I was in miserable plight — severe \+ constant headache \+ rising temp'r. I got up before 4, but could only get into the front room where I lay on the lounge. At 5 I took my temp. \+ found it to be 101° \+ in spite of Cathartics taken each night I have had no movement of the bowels since my wash out last Mond. m'g. I sent Jim up to Dr. Yale with a note \+ he brought back a combination of calomel, Senna, Rhubarb \+ Alors. I took some milk \+ rennet \+ went to bed at 8.30. Charl came \+ took Mary \+ the girls to his new house.

At 12 I awoke with my shirt wet with perspiration. I called in Mary \+ she got a change for me.
