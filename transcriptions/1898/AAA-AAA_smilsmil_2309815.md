## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 12, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, raw-cold day with a light fall of snow at night. I got up \+ had bkfs. with the family — I sent for Yale because I had had trouble with my bowels during the night — bring up at 2, 5, \+ 7 o'clk, in answer to calls. All of my spare energies during the m'f were given to making out the weekly report of Treas. for Council meeting this eve'f. Not having a copy of last week's report I had to work over more ground than ordinarily is necessary — Lunch at 1.30 — A good part of the afternoon was spent sleeping, the rest of it was given to a call from Dr. Yale. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'f. Charl came \+ stayed until 10.30.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 13, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear \+ cold winter day. After a fairly good night's rest I got up \+ took bkfs. with the family. A good part of the m'f was spent in arranging report to be made to N.A. meeting to-morrow eve'f. Nicoll came in \+ made a short call at about 10 o'clk. At noon I went out \+ walked for nearly half an hour on the sunny (western) side of Park Ave. The wind from the West was strong \+ bitter — Lunch at 1.30 — N.A. letters to write in the afternoon — Maynard came in after 3 o'clk — After 5 I lay down for a rest \+ at 6 Watrons came to talk over some plans for to-morrow eve'f. Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'f Ralph \+ I amused ourselves playing a new game of cards — one of pure chance — to bed after I had read my paper.
Bowels quiet all day.
