## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 11, 1898

**Weather:** Pleasant day, so far as weather went  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- awake at about 2.30 A.m. \+ found myself started on a chill \- got miss Thompson up \- she made up a cup of hot Horlick's Malted milk \+ I was soon on my way to the fever. At 5 A.M. I stood at 103⁷⁄₁₀°. It was the old story of misery all over \- the fever began to abate with daylight \+ very slowly passed away \- reaching normal in the eve. D. Gale came in the m'f. \+ again in the afternoon \- He finds periodicity in these attacks \+ is now quite sure that my trouble is malarial. It was a dismal day for me \- Milley Cook called \- also Maynard. I saw each for a moment. Luckily I was able to sit up in bed \+ talk with Charl when he came in.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 12, 1898

**Weather:** Beautiful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

At 3 A.m. I awoke in a dripping sweat, my shirt \+ pillow being saturated \- called Miss T\_ \+ she gave me a change \+ a cup of hot Malted milk, when I settled to sleep again. I felt better during the day but did not get up until 5 o'clk. Then I dressed \+ went into the front room while my room \+ bed were being aired. Beckwith came about noon \+ made a pleasant call. About 6 clk. Emma \+ Mary Ireland called.
