## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate went to Po'k. in the afternoon \-

Miserable day, dark overy wet\- I fussed all day over Arno valley trying to get the base of statue \+ supporting wall on extreme left into harmony with the rest of the pictures \- I went up to Maynards \+ made slight sketch of back of Venus de Milo \+ then, by adaptation made it fit on the pedestal \- At last, after I could no longer see, properly, to paint, in sheer desperation I rubbed in a lot of fresh color \+ smeared it over with my palette knife \- finishing it in less than 10 minutes \+ leaving it much better than it was after hours of work\. About 5 o'clk, Kingsbury, from Lee \+ Lee came with a document for me to sign \+ swear to connected with the foreclosure of the Annie Brown, 43 \& Nicholas Place mtg. I washed up my brushes \+ got home after 6\- Eve'g, Jim went with Tom Cook \+ some other boys, on T.C.'s birthday frolic \- to a Theatre \- I read to R \- until near 9 \- \+ then I spent some time in going over Montrose accts\- to make them tally with Bank Book just balanced.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 20, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beast of a day \- high wind \+ heavy rain I got up with a headache that stuck by me all day but I kept up notwithstanding\. I went outside of the house but once when I went to the corner to post some letters \- I got a good deal of work done in arranging checks for 1895\-6\-7 as a basis for a simple system of accts. that I must keep\. In the eve'g I wrote up my Petty Cash for 1895\. Charl came in for awhile.
