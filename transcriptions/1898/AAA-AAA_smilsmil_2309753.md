## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 2, 1898

**Weather:** Clouded m'f: clearing to a breezy, very pleasant day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I went to the house soon after bkf't \+ inlet it was getting things about the house in shape to leave \+ gathering the things to be packed to the kitchen where our trunks were. About 11 I went down town \+ paid bills etc. etc. getting back to the house at 12.30 — dinner at 1.30 — a short rest \+ then began packing, getting through about 4.30 — Bid the house good bye \+ went to ch. Sayres — had a light meal at 5.15. Said our adieus at 5.40 \+ went to Station. Train left at 6.05 — a wait at Alford — Got to Scranton at 8.20. Parks waiting for us at station — drove at once to his house — looked over some of his Boydell Shakespeare prints — Mr. P— came in from Glen Summit on a later train. Bessie \+ her friend, Miss Kingman out making a call — Mr. Curtis came in for a few minutes — It was about 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 3, 1898

**Weather:** Clouded most of the day \+ not intolerably hot altho' humid.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkf't, with Mr. \+ Mrs P., Ralph \+ I rode down town — went to P's office — then to a clothier's where I bought a suit of clothes — then back to P's office — drove about — marketing etc. \+ then got P— \+ home at about noon — lounged on verandah until lunch at 1 — after that I had a nap. The afternoon was spent loafing, reading magazines \+ listening to Miss Mabel Kingman play on the piano — Eve'g meal at 6 — Thunderstorms began to play around at that time rain falling lightly at about 7 \+ nature continuing until after 11 — notwithstanding the rain Mr. P., Bessie, Mabel \+ Oprear went to Prayer meeting — Jim \+ Ralph went to bed \+ I read until their return. Then, altho' the rain continued we found it very pleasant on the verandah until 10 o'clk — then in \+ Mabel played forces until 11 — a very spirited \+ intelligent player. Then to bed — routed out by bowels at about 4 A.m.
