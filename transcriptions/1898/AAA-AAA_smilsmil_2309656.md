## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 20, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncle Willie came

Dark \+ wet all day \- In spite of darkness I worked all the m'f on Susquehanna, at times feeling encouraged, but realizing at the end of the day that the work is weak, characterless \+ fussed to death. After lunch I went to Charity organ\[illegible\] \+ saw Mr. Ed\[wa\]s T. Devine, Gen\[t\]. Sec'y \+ had a talk with him concerning Farmington who, I find, was years ago an employee of the C.O.Sec'y \+ was dismissed for dishonesty — Then to N.A. \+ at work there on the books until 4 o'clk. Then to studio \+ worked at cleaning my bicycle until near 6 — Then home — Uncle Willie there \- came from P'k. This m'f — stayed with us over night — Charl came \+ took Kate to a concert — I read to R \+ uncle W— until 9 —

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 21, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant altho' partially overcast. I feel so out of sorts that I did not care to work. My Water. Color "Susquehanna" is enough of a disappointment in itself to make me sick — so I podged about my studio \+ spent a good deal of time taking my bicycle to pieces \+ cleaning it. Home to lunch — again to studio — two more work done than in the m'f. About 4 o'clk. I made up two phr. of O. W. Paper \+ then walked down to 23\. S\[t\] \+ 5th Ave to Willstars\[illegible\] \+ sent one ph\[ot\]o to Fernn at Montclair \+ then onto 15th S\[t\]— but was too late for F. W. Edwards — left me ph\[ot\]o with painter \+ then walked down to Washington Arch, \+ spied around that \+ planned a set of N.Y. street views to be based upon photo. snap shots, to be published as "Snap-shot Etchings". Home at 6.30 having been detained at Kipple's where I went to see the collection of Wingell's Pastels \+ Blk \+ white — Mrs Knauff came in O\[illegible\] — came not — she was so very talkative \+ gracious that we saw no wrugells \+ did not get away until after 5 — Every had a short ride on my wheel — Uncle Willie \+ Kate went to hear Charl's orchestrelle — Jim dined with Ned Thurston — Later I went to Chas — George there.
