## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 14, 1898

**Weather:** Parts of the day clear — at other times heavy clouds.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 \+ made a start on Water Color 12×20 "By the Susquehanna near Binghampton". Washed in the sky \+ had the misfortune at the start to get a buckskin buff stain on the light clouds that would not wash out — This is a fatal injury at the start as it makes it impossible to get what I had hoped for, a clear, silvery grey. Home to lunch \+ back — I wrote minutes of last meeting of the sub-com. Fine Arts Com. to Paris, 1900, in preparation for the Com. meeting This P.M. Exchanged two or three notes with La Farge, Relating to the business at 4.30 went to N.A. Com. met \+ confirmed the nominations of Cauldwell \+ Coffin \+ adj\[?] at 6 — I then arranged in the office for the coming of Collins who is to begin writing up the books to night — Then home to dinner — after that I wrote brief Report to present to the meeting of the Council of the Federation at 8 o'clk — I got to the N.A. at 8.15 — La Farge, Watrons \+ Van-Bookcrek there, the two last named soon left — The meeting was special to consider this Report. Mr. La Farge \+ I in brief remarks presented the matter after the Reading of the documents that led up to the business \+ we answered various questions that were put. Architect Marshall moved that the matter be referred to the various organizations members of the Federation, with a Request for prompt action — Passed without dissent. Then I spent some time with Collins at his work over the books.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 15, 1898

**Weather:** Nasty day — dark \+ wet.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 \+ in spite of the darkness put in a good days work on my Susquehanna drawing — It was a good days work in hours only — The work, as work, was very indifferent. Having made a mis-step at the beginning by getting the lights in the sky too warm, that led to washing out \+ fussing — a departure from the original intention \+ a loss of directness and spontaneity — At one time I almost decided to throw the thing aside — However, I pushed on but was not able to cover the whole of the paper. Home to lunch at 1 — About 2 Van Cleef came along \+ bothered me for a time — but at last he left without asking me to lend him anything — Back to studio \+ worked until after 4 — Then to N.A. Home at 5.45 \+ rested until dinner — A quiet eve\[ning] at home.
