## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 26, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudy day, quite cool, with some very fine cloud effects — a very light shower reached us in the eve'g. but heavy ones passed about us — The work of the surveyors was continued — There was a mistake made evidently when I had the lines of the plot run out in the summer of '79 for between points that I could not dispute, where the deed stated that there were 26 rods there, proved to be but 24, Neilson agreed to give me the two missing rods on the steep hillside on the north if I would stipulate to surrender any portion of it that might be required for a road that it is proposed to cut at some time along the north front of the hill — There were numerous conferences between Neilson, McGee, Scott Brown + Self during the day but matters were satisfactorily settled — I got a great batch of detained letters — I cannot make out from the many directions + post marks where they were stuck — Checks came from the U.G., from Beckwith — I endorsed + sent them to 2d Nat. \+ to the Central Trust — There were four letters from Mary, Kate + the girls — I had a wretched time with pain after lunch but on recovering wrote some letters + then went to the Knoll — I felt very uncomfortable all the afternoon — ate my dinner in pain + went from the table to my bed — much depressed. I am afraid that by persisting in staying here with such frequent recurrence of pain I may get into such a condition as George was in here years ago. I got up about 9 o'clk — read my paper + then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 27, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day, very cool w'g., pleasant to be in the sunshine at any time of day. at night, really cold — brilliant moonlight — Waylaid Dr. Janeway after b'kf't. + asked him if, in the condition I was in, it would be better for me to go to N.Y. for treatment than to stay here + complete the work I have in hand — He said "stay" + he got some Aloin Tablets for me. I soon met Neilson + Simonds + went to the Knoll — again we went over the line of the road that I propose + then I arranged with Simonds to come with his assistant on Monday to get the elevation of my Knoll + find the best grades for road. Then I went to hotel + wrote some N.A. + D. business letters — got off a full one to Grannis with checks — Lunch at 1.30 after Ralph + I had had a very pleasant walk on a wood road over the base of Noon mark. After lunch I wrote to Ja's. Morris Jnr. + to Katharine Cook at the Estes House. Then again to Pine Knoll while there Mr. + Mrs. Woston, who have a cottage here came up + pleasantly introduced themselves + invited me to a "Camp fire" at their cottage next Monday eve'g. I was with Younne until 5.30 directing the chopping out of undesirable trees — Then R. + I had a walk over Windy Brow + then to hotel — Dinner at 7 o'clk. again I enjoyed the bran bread that I get from Kate Seaward last Eve'g. Brilliant moonlight + very cold — tried a little piazza walking — dance in the casino — to bed early to get warm.
