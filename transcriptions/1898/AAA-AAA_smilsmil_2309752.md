## Left Page

### Sun. July 31, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant m'f., a still, hot afternoon \+ a shower from 6.30 to about 8 p.m. After off: went to house. Charl bay re came up with two young ladies from his house \+ I showed them from kitchen to garret. I spent most of the m'f writing letters. Dinner at 1.30 \- then again to house \+ wrote to Charl \+ corrected 2d pf. of N.A. Report for the printer. Talea at 6.30 \- after the rain went to P.O. with letters. The rest of the eve'g with Sunday Tribune.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 1, 1898

**Weather:** Clouded m'f.  **Temp:** about 70°

very humid. The weather cleared \+ a delightful breeze sprung up. a smart shower passed over at about 12 o'clk. I spent most of the m'f writing letters. dinner at 1.30 \- The boys went to the lake fishing \+ I followed them over to see what sort of a scow they were in \- I warned them that more showers would come \+ that they must be on the lookout for them. Then to studio \+ more writing \+ some putting away of things. After 5 quite a severe storm came up \+ I got into a stew over the boys who were caught in it\. After 6 I again went to the lake \+ as the rain was ceasing I met them on their way back \- very wet of course. I too had got wet \+ we had to change our clothing \- This made us half an hour late to tea. After tea went to P.O. \+ to Dr. Gardner's paid his bill \- back at 8.30
