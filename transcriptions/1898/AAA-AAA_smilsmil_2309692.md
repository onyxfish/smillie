## Left Page

### Sat. April 2, 1898

**Weather:** A bright cool m'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright cool m'f. changing soon to cloudy with east wind - clearer again in the afternoon - Had a confidential talk with Emma J, when she came to give Jim his lesson, concerning M. W's proposition but she could not decide for me. Then I took my wheel to Spaulding's to have it thoroughly overhauled - on my way back stopped at Carriere & Hastings \+ saw C— \+ the academy plans - \+ talked over some of the details - Got to studio so late that I accomplished nothing before lunch time - Afternoon, gave up to my headache \+ was on my bed until dinner time - Sue f was not able to get to Century - the Regular Monthly meeting. I loafed about \+ was able to do some reading \+ got to bed at midnight.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 3, 1898

**Weather:** Bright but cold, raw & windy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright but cold, raw & windy - Got up with a sore head \+ without ambition. The morning passed \+ at 1 o'clk. I went up to Dr. Allen's in 60t St \+ told my miseries to him - he had very little to say \+ gave me some better tablets to take immediately after meals - then I went to Ed W. Cook's - The boys were there \+ at 2 o'clk - we had dinner. Mary did her best, but I felt very sore \+ she gave me no chance to say anything - At 4 o'clk. I went over to Walter's \+ chatted with him for an hour - then I went to the Irelands in E. 17th St \+ saw Emma \+ Mary \+ talked with them until after 6 - Then I walked up home. near 7 - Jim at table taking his supper - R— at Cook's - After tea Jim \+ I had a quiet talk together in which I tried to gain his fullest confidence - R— came in at 8 \+ I read awhile to him - then Charles \+ George came in. They stayed until 10.30 .
