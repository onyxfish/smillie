## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 4, 1898

At C.R. Woodin's - Berwick Heights - Pa.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Stifling hot m't. after bkfs - Mr. Sturgis came to the house \+ took the three boys \+ me to the PowerHouse where we saw some very interesting electrical machinery - then all went with him over his works for reclaiming Kuhn - back to the house at 10.30 - Prepared my bag \+ Ralph's for traveling - Lunch at 12.30 \+ at 1.30 we left the house for the R.R. station. Bessie \+ Mabel going at same time on their way to Montrose - Ralph went with me - Jim stayed with Greer - our train left at 1.55 - much that was interesting along the Lackawanna until we reached the Susquehanna at Pittston - a broad valley for awhile - Then a narrow one with wooded hills - Heavy Thunder showers came up at about 3 o'clk - Got to Berwick at about 4 - a good deal of discomfort for half an hour or more in stomach but it passed away just as we got to Berwick - met Mr. Woodin there - He had a team \+ his motor carriage - I got into the carriage with him \+ Ralph went in the motor - There was very little rain falling when we started but in a few minutes it came down furiously - we had to take refuge in a barn - but at last got to the house without serious wetting, warmly welcomed by Mrs W - Chatted until dinner - a very excellent meal daintily served - Every some music on an Eolian organ - then Steller gave an again of his Graphhaphone - Ralph sat up until I went to bed at 10 o'clk - Rain fell steadily until near morning.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 5, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Glorious day - Fresh wind from Then, W. beautiful atmosphere. Bkfs - at 8 o'clk - a very satisfactory one - at about 10, Mr. W. with driver, Ralph \+ I went in his motor down to the village of Berwick - the motor performed some wonderful feats. It is propelled by gasoline - Mr. W - took us thro' the foundry, rolling mills \+ foundry pudding mill - a very interesting expedition. During it I had a bad half hour - but it passed away \+ at last we got started on our journey homeward. The motor climbed the hill without difficulty. A very fine lunch after 12 - again my stomach troubled me \+ after lunch I had to lie down for awhile - It was after three o'clk - when I went down stairs feeling better - then Mr. W - took me out \+ for two hours we wandered over the place - Returning to the house sat out \+ doors \+ watched the children \+ a game of Croquet - a very elegant dinner at 6 - but I had to be very careful - more discomfort - Felt better after awhile - Mr. \+ Mrs. Woodin \+ his wife's niece, Mrs. deisenberg came over \+ spent an hour with us - Then Mr. W - showed me all thro' his cellars - It was after 11 when I got to bed.
