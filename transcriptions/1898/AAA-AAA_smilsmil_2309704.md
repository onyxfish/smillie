## Left Page

### Tues. April 26, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very little improvement in the weather immediately after help. I went to the church at Lex. Ave. \+ 35\'S \- the funeral of Jos Evans \- a remarkable assemblage of people of distinction in many ways \- Art, literature \+ other professions. The church was so cold that I could not stay through the whole ceremony \- went to studio \+ got to work upon N.A. accts \- at 12.30 went to N.A. \+ then home to lunch \- to studio at 2.30 \- Young Stahr waiting for me there, with some newly etched plates \- he took some time \- Later, Hungerford \+ Bullock came in \- more time \- but it was given to an explanation on my part to them of the work on the N.A. accts, as I am now doing it. They say that I am working on correct lines \- then Nicoll came in \- more time \- Finance Com. \- then Maynard came in \- more time \- Building Com. \+ the matters generally \- It was after 6 o'clk. before I was able to get away from the studio after having done not half the afternoons work on the accts. Had a short walk \+ got in at 6.30 \- Emma \- I \- already there \- Walter came in soon after \- It was about 7 when we sat down to dinner \- it was after 8.30 when we got up. I brought up the books of the schools for my boys, after they had left the table \+ there was to me a very interesting discussion as to what I had better do \- I wish to take Jim from Morse \+ Ralph from Miss Miller \- Alice Breland came to the house after lunch \+ I talked with her on the same subject. I now feel strengthened in my purpose. Emma \+ Walter left soon after \- going to a musical entertainment \- Charl came \+ stayed until 10.15 \- then I wrote notes of introduction \+ hunted up London addresses for George \- To bed at 11.30

## Right Page

### Wed. April 27, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

George sailed for Liverpool.
Katy \+ Elkin set? home.

At last, some sunshine \- But it did not last longer than noon \- Bright, breezy, cool m'g \- bleak, cloudy, cold afternoon. Charl came for me at 10.30 \+ together we went to the Germania foot of W. 10th St. Geo. was already on the steamer \- Sheldon \+ Mother Jacobs came along soon after. Charl left at about 11.30 but I waited \+ saw the steamer go out. Geo. seemed to be in good spirits which was a comfort to me \- He has as a fellow passenger Gov. Salvation Army Booth who may look after his spiritual wants. I walked most of the way home, doing some shopping \- lunch at 1.30 \- to studio at 2.30 after bidding Katy \+ William good-bye \- they are to return to New-Haven by an afternoon train \- I wrestled with my work \+ a headache wrestled with me until I could hardly tell what I was doing \- At 5.30 I went to the Century \+ wrote a note to the Com. on Ed. in favor of Fred Ireland. I tried to write one for Barse but could not command myself so, gave it up \+ went home \- I could not eat my dinner \- my headache grew rapidly worse \+ I went to bed very much out of sorts.
