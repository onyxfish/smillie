## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 19, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A furious storm of driving wind and rain which continued until near noon. Then for a while the sun shone out. I kept my bed + had much discomfort in the stomach, not neuralgic but acidity — It was 5 P. M. before I got up — then dressed + went down to dinner — Eve'g wrote to Charl. in answer to a letter rec'd from him — Then Walter Cook came in — just home from Europe — they got in last night.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 20, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, mild day. I felt somewhat better in the m'g. Yale did not come, + at about 12 o'clk. I went out + had a little walk in the sunshine on Park Ave. I was so very weak + uncomfortable that I could not stay long — During the afternoon I had a great deal of stomach discomfort, but in spite of it I wrote quite a long letter on Ild. affairs to Dielman + two or three other notes. In the midst of my pain, about 5 P. M. — a man of the Tribune returned (he had called in the m'g) to say that he had seen J. G. Brown who had told him that I had resigned the Preas'dship of the N.A. I told him that I had + he plied me with a lot of questions — I begged him not to publish anything in the form of an interview with me + said that I would much prefer to have it pass without public notice. My pain passed away + I had a tolerably comfortable ev'g. Chas. Collins came in + stayed until near 10.
