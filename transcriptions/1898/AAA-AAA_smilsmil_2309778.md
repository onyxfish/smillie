## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 23, 1898

**Weather:** Rain all night, ceasing about 7 A.m., a mild day with sunshine + showers, settling into Rain before 7 P.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up feeling very well — bkft. at 8.30 — Worked on a map of my hill top, getting the position of trees so that I could determine the site + size of the separate building for dining room + kitchen. Then I walked around the block getting back at about 12.30 — Lunch at 1.30 — From 1 to 3 I felt uncomfortable, but not in pain exactly — the discomfort was caused, I think, by my increasing the dose of Castor oil (which seems to be inoperable to 30 drops before each meal — wrote to Charl + to Elwo Cook. Then after some more work on my map I went over to the grounds to look over it again after my map work — My head was very heavy + I felt sick — I went over Windy brow — but the tramp did not restore me — Back to hotel after 5 + lay down on my bed — there until 7 — Then Ralph + I went down to dinner — I ate very little — I spent the eveng in my room — Ralph with me — part of the time he was mending some of his clothes + then we had a little "encline,

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 24, 1898

**Weather:** Rain continuously thro' the night + until perhaps 8 or 9 o'clk. getting colder.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I arranged my plan to leave here on Friday next — to spend Sat. + Sunday at Elizabethtown — to go to Albany on Monday, Oct 3., Jim to continue on to N.Y., Ralph + I to spend a day in Albany + then go on to Binghampton + Montrose. I wrote notes to Pine, at Montrose to Ch. Sacyre + to Raleleonard — also to Nellie (P.H.S.) Then the boys + I put on our Rubber coats + we went over the Wood Road in the foat of noonmark. It was hard, dragging work for me — the discomfort in stomach + bowels making me feel like curling up in a ball rather than exerting myself walking — Lunch at 1.30 when the discomfort began to abate — I feel quite well in the afternoon + wrote part of a letter to Bessie Throop — At about 4 I went out alone + walked up the Reserve Road to the Gill Brook Reservoir, nearly 2 miles, and then back — a distance altogether of nearly four miles — + enjoyed every step of the way — in strong contrast to the discomfort of the morning. Got in about 5.30 or later — Rested — o then dressed for dinner at 7 — Eveng in parlor for awhile, then finished letter to Bessie + to bed.
