## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 7, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of great misery for me. I dictated to Jim a note to Dr. Yale \+ he took to him before he went to school. The fever \+ the pain all over me was almost unbearable. Dr. came along \+ I told him that he had better send for a trained nurse \+ in the afternoon he sent Miss Edith K. Thompson, a Canadian girl, young, strong \+ incurably jolly. Alice Ireland came up to my room with her \- Yale came again in the afternoon \+ gave her instructions. It was not until the early eve'g that the intense pain in my head modified to any extent. Y. gave me a thorough clearing out with an enema after dosing me with calomel \+ then he put me on a Quinine diet. I got thro' the night without much discomfort.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 8, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dark rainy day \- a gloomy one for me as I felt so weak \+ uncomfortable all day long. Dr. Yale came in the m'g \+ seems to be interested to get me out of the hole in which I am \- He is seconded by Miss Thompson who takes hold in a jovial, strong way, well calculated to raise drooping spirits \- Jim had two of his school fellows to lunch with him, Seeny \+ Thurston \- In the afternoon he went to N.A. for me asking Grannis to come to the house with check book of N.A. but G. did not come. Eve'g Chase came in.
