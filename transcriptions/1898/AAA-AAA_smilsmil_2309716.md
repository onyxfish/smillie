## Left Page

### Fri. May 20, 1898

**Weather:** Brilliant m'f, passing into a very hot, uncomfortable day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant m'f, passing into a very hot, uncomfortable day — Heavy thunder storms passed south of us in the every giving us a slight wetting but doing little to cool the air. Miller came at 9 o'clk by app't \+ looked over some of the repair work necessary to be done. Then I went to studio \+ spent much of the m'f. in getting ready for my etching class in the afternoon \+ in printing from two or three of their plates in the m'f Home to lunch at 1 — to studio at 2 — four of the students there \+ I had all that I could attend to all the afternoon, biting \+ printing. It was 6.15 when I got washed up \+ left the studio — Dinner. Ralph \+ I alone — Jim at Tom Cook's — Read to R — until 9.30 — Charl came in — just back from Boston \+ with the first letter from Geo.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 21, 1898

margaret White \+ sallie came.

Beautiful day. Up at 6.30 so that Ralph might be ready to go with Mr. W. Bayard Cutting when he came for him. Bkf. at 7.30 \+ promptly at 8.05 Mr. C. arr'd. in carriage with footman, his son Bronson \+ a nurse woman \+ they took R. with them to Mr. C's farm on Long Island where they had a good time all day, getting back at 6. I got to studio at usual time \+ commenced a systematic clearing up. Miller came with one of his men \+ set him to work at scraping the floor, to work for an hour as a basis for calculation. About 11 I went to N.A. \+ worked there until 1.15 Beckwith \+ Watrous came in. Home to lunch. Having a headache I rested for an hour after it — then to studio \+ continued clearing up work in one of the closets — Home soon after 4 — cleaned up my wheel \+ then rode to Park \+ made the circuit. Home at 6.15 a rub down \+ change — Dinner at 6.45 Margaret White \+ her daughter came to commence the experiment of her abode in the house during the summer. Charl came in later in the ev'g. Lights out at 10.
