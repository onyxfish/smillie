## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 28, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliant day after a very cold night — some light clouds in the afternoon — then another cold, still, cloudless moonlight night — It was 8 o'clk when I got up after a quiet restful night, feeling fairly well — some little time after 6 kfp. I proposed to the boys that we go up the east side of the River Trail — \+ we started off — after a mile or so we lost the trail \+ got back on to the Road \+ followed that on towards the lake — When we got about half way or a little more, the Aloin that I took last night began to have effect \+ I suffered a good deal of pain but managed to get to the Hotel, \+ after half an hour or more on my bed was able to get up \+ take a "frugal meal". The afternoon was made one of pain \+ misery by action of bowels — I wrote to Kate, Leonard, to Ryan \+ to Trees (for suppositories) but most of the time I was on my bed, a good deal depressed, remembering how George's experience had begun here many years ago in much the same manner. The boys are not well — Jim is complaining all the time. \+ Ralph has periods — I don't know what to do — It is too early to think of going to N.Y. — \+ yet it may be that I should get myself under special treatment as soon as possible — What shall I do with the boys? I took a light tea at 6.30 — on bed for an hour — Then went down to parlor \+ Read there, \+ listened to music, for an hour — Mr. Snow came \+ introduced himself to me as having met me at Lake George, at Rogers Rock Hotel in '78 — He, \+ family, now occupy a cottage here —

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 29, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day opened in a most inauspicious way. lowering clouds, cold \+ high winds — growing milder with showers — Late in the afternoon the clearing \+ cloud effects were very beautiful — at 8 p.m. very strange effects of moonlight, rapidly changing cloud forms \+ lightning from a thunder storm not far west of us — Every. beautiful, misty moonlight, mild \+ very still. I got up at 8 feeling fairly well, but sore in stomach \+ bowels after yesterdays experience — I started out after 9 kfp. to find Simonds but he did not put in an appearance — Some time with Scott Brown \+ W.G. Nedson — Then to the Knoll \+ spent some time there directing Young who stopped work at noon — Then to Hotel, rested \+ read Sunday Tribune until lunch at 1.30 — afternoon wrote some letters, among others to Beck with relating to the depraved Benedict Nate business — At about 3 I took a walk going out East, around by the old Smith Beede house \+ down the Road to the valley — I studied carefully as I went around the line proposed by W.G.N — for a new Road approaching the hotel by a much better grade. Had a very pleasant \+ enjoyable walk \+ got back at 6 — Dinner at 7 — Every. at 8 went to the Weston's "camp fire". A very picturesque entertainment. Chinese lanterns about through the woods — a great camp fire — an open shanty — rustic seats \+ logs — lots of young people full of fun — strong effects — fire light — moonlight and dense shadows — I stayed until 9 — Then in answer to the dictates of prudence I went back to hotel \+ to bed —
No pain to-day other than soreness in stomach and bowels.
