## Left Page

### Thur. July 21, 1898

**Weather:** Muggy, overcast m'f, with a light shower about 9 a.m. clear, hot p.m. with breezy pleasant w'f.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up feeling better but distress in my stomach took possession of me about 10 o'clk \+ I spent much of the m'f on my bed - I got together the "wash" \+ took it to Mrs. Coyle. The boy took "Frizzy" out for a short drive. At dinner I had so much pain that at times I thought of leaving the table - There was ice-cream for dessert - I ate some of it \+ experienced immediate relief \+ had no return of the pains during the afternoon. At 3.30 I went downtown \+ got paper \+ letters - then home \+ spent some time reading the Tribune - At 5.45 I took collars \+ cuffs to laundry \+ then went in to see Dr. Gardner - told him my woes \+ got some medicines - Home to tea at 8.30 \+ after that went to the house \+ locked up. Then downtown to P.O. - back to Sayres \+ until ten o'clk. I took part in summer boarding house piazza conversation.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 22, 1898

**Weather:** Hot \+ cloudy - Thunderstorms about all the afternoon but no showers reached us -  **Temp:** [illegible]

I had quite a comfortable n'f \+ was free from pain until a little before dinner - Then I had some pain until I had eaten \+ during the afternoon I was very uneasy. After b'f: I was looking after the boys until they got off with the Daw't. Sayre's \+ Logans on a pic-nic to Salt Spring - Then I went up to the house \+ wrote to Charl, Kate Leonard \+ "Styron" "J". There I went to P.O. - then to Logier's \+ to M's Rhinevalt's - Then to W'm Mulford's where I sat awhile \+ to Ch. Sayre's where I stayed until after dinner - Then to the house \+ found Logier at work trimming hedge. I wrote to Mary Cook, C.R. Woodin \+ W'm Parke - feeling very uncomfortable I took a troubled nap until 5 o'clk. Then went to P.O. \+ again to Mr. Rhinevalt's - not in - To Sayre's at 6.30 - Every read newspaper until Jo Ralph went to bed with a high fever - at 9 o'clk it reached 104°.
