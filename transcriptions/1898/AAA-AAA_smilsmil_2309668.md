## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 13, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully beautiful day — cloudless sky of marvellous blue — fresh, cool light winds. Bkfst. at 9.30 — it was near noon before Ralph \+ I started out for our walk. We rode up to 84° S\. \+ walked thro' to The Park \+ wandered about there until time to go home to dinner at 2. By this time my head was aching badly. I ate a light dinner \+ very soon after went to bed where I lay until near 10 o'clk. Then got up and went down to parlor where Chas. was. He left at 11.30. We talked very seriously of George's financial troubles \+ of the possibility of their being believed by the visit to London that he proposes. I had a little to eat \+ went to bed again at about 1 dclk.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 14, 1898

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'g., rain commencing at noon \+ falling quite freely until after 2 — then cleared to fine weather in the eve'g. I worked all day on Sandins Key — glazing \+ painting — it is done now until I see it in the frame. At noon I went to Allen's, in 47' S\. \+ made appointment with him for next Thurs. m'g. at 9.30 — then, as it was raining hard, I went to Ralph's school in 42° S\. \+ waited there for him in his class room until about 1 — then we went home to lunch. Worked at studio until 4 — then to Freny's \+ saw Abbey's "Hamlet" — I can't enjoy it — then into Ortgies, in the floor below — \+ saw a piece or two of Wiggins's cattle — nor could I enjoy them. Art is, I suppose, too high for me — W's may be too low. Home at 5.30 — rested until 6.30 — dinner — Soc'y Council meeting — N.A. — at 9.30 — conference with the Jury \+ Hanging Com. They were requested by the council to solicit desirable works in painting \& sculpture from the artists — every effort to be made to make The [illegible] up to date. As there was a quorum of the jury present, the council dispersed \+ left them in possession — Home at 10.30 — out on my wheel for about half an hour — then in \+ to bed.
