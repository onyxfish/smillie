## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 26, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Exceedingly heavy rain during the night. Thunder after midnight - continued heavy rain in the m'f a very dark - but it cleared brightly about 10.30 - cloudy & windy but not cold. To studio about 10 - worked until 1 o'clk on woodlot road painting on central group of Trees - Steroe came in but I told him that was no use for him to go to N.a. p.m. as no one had yet presented themselves for etching class - lunch at 1.30 - then to N.a. Met Mr Manuel there & together we went over the report for last fiscal year - & everything was explained to me satisfactorily. I wrote to Backwith that the School Com- must do something to secure a class or else the Baldwin fund would lapse - the $10.00 fee seems to be the stumbling block - The poor beggars want not only a free professor but free everything else. It was near 5 when I left the N.a. bldg. walked to 83. 5. & Bdway - Pasteur Peter Co- but could not get what I wanted - then to Adams [illegible] & learned latest time to send to Jim his Thanksgiving box - Home to dinner at 6.30 Ev'f with newspapers - Charl came in for a little while - At 10 I went to studio for a few minutes.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 27, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate arr? from Po'k.

more rain during the night - morning dark & wet clearing slowly during the day - growing windy & colder. To studio at 10 & during the m'f painted on woodlot road & got it as far as I know how to carry it - it is brown in tone & "tight" in execution. Home to lunch at 1.30 - afternoon, painted over a portion of the sky of a study that I painted at Stockbridge in 1868 - Ev'f effect - Thunderhead (cumulus) piled up ag't blue sky. The basis of a large painting that I have now under way. Ralph came at 4 & with him I walked down to a bird store, 4th Ave. below 12th St. to inquire about canaries & linnets. Then we walked home getting in about 5.30 - Kate had come from Po'k - This afternoon I learned only to-day that Carrie Kimball died on Sunday last. Her death had been expected for some time. Dinner at 6.30 Ev'f after my newspapers I worked for some time on my acc'ts Charl took Katharine to see Sara Bernhardt - Bessie is sick in bed - severe cold.
