## Left Page

### Thur. April 12, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charl. ret. from Southern Trip.

Heavy rain last night \+ during the mt. \- mizzle continued thro' p.m. I spent the day Etching \+ biting, new background on Mr. G. \- \+ coat on portrait of self \- It was 6.30 when I got home \- I dressed \+ went to Charls' at 7\. \- Dinner at 7.30 \- Mary \+ her girls, Kate, Ge°., Ralph \+ I were the guests. The dinner was very good \+ C. was entertaining as he told us of men \+ things he had seen during his trip. It was 11 o'clc. before we got home.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 13, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, quiet day \- clearing beautifully late in the afternoon \- After bkp. I went to Meyrowitz's \- W. 42°. S\. \+ got glasses left there on Wed. Then to studio \- mounted some Montrose photos to show M. P. Adams. It was 11.30 before I got to work printing \- took p.p. from Mr. G. \+ portrait of self \- Home at 1.30 \- back at 2.30 \- worked on portions of face of Mr. G. \- \+ at last got a pf. to show to Dr. Gerster to-morrow \- Home at 6.45 \- dinner \- Ive\[illegible\] wrote letter to Jim, \+ mailed it \- \+ note to Ge°. Cook

A lawyer came to studio this p.m. \+ asked me to be a witness in a suit between J. T. White \+ one of the Belmonts \- W. suing B. for $1,500 \- for etched portrait (by Ritchie) etc. etc. for Biographical Ency\- I declined\-
