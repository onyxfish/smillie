## Left Page

### Fri. June 29, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day, bright + breezy. Up early + repacked my trunk before 4 fo'. All of the mff was given to little matters down town. It was nearly 11 when I went to Mr. Kents + said my good byes there - then to R.R. station + took 12 20 train. One of Mr. Kents guests, a lady whose name I did not catch, was a fellow traveler with us to Binghampton - as we had more than an hour to wait for our train we went to a poor little hotel nearby + got a poor little dinner for 25¢ each - then to station + still having time I wrote notes - one to J.C. Harrington tome to Wm. Harrington - whom I have missed up in selling some hay that is in the barn. We got to B. after 3 + the train on the D.H. did not leave until 5.30 - It was very hot - a strong wind blowing, + lots of dirt. So I sat in the station + wrote a nice a long letter to Mary - At last 5 30 o'clk. came + we got away. at about 7 we stopped 15 m. at Oneonta to get our every meal - After 10 we got to Albany + transferred at once to the 11 o'clk for Montreal - we got a section amid ships + prepared for the night. I was about as comfortable as I could be under such circumstances - but it was not a restful night - I dozed but did not sleep.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 30, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cold windy mff passing into many showers in the afternoon - high, cold wind at night. We were due at Westport at 3.35 but were nearly an hour late - we were at Port Henry when I awake at 4 o'clk. From the car windows we had very fine views of the sun rising over the Green Mts. At Westport we found teams waiting. It was very cold + I was glad to get our overcoats out of our trunk. we had an easy, two-seat light wagon + went up to I-town at a good rate - Mr. Tuckerman a fellow traveler - we got to "The Windsor" before 6 o'clk - The porter soon had a good fire to comfort us \+ before J we had a good hot bfft. After waiting about for half an hour - Ralph + I started on afoot. we had gone more than a mile + were beginning to warm up when the vehicle overtook us - Mr. Tuckerman mit on his way to Keene Heights - Altho' the temp. was about 45° + a stiff wind blowing we did not suffer discomfort. we got to the top of the Hurricane pass soon after + had to wait there half an hour for the team from Beede's that was to meet us there - we had a stove with a good fire to warm us up. The team came + we bowled thro' to Beede's, getting there at about 11 - we at once went over to the house where I found the main room filled with the carload of furniture which had come thro' with exceeding promptness - we worked at unpacking furniture until 1 o'clk - were caught in a shower + then had lunch - from 2 to 4.30 R + I slept soundly. These mff acted our trunks \+ dressed for dinner which we had before 7 - Evely. for an hour + a half we toasted before a wood fire - got to bed at 10 o'clk - I got into my winter pajamas + piled lots of covering over me + we slept until after 9 the next mff.
