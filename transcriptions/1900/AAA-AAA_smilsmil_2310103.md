## Left Page

### Mon. July 9, 1900

**Weather:** Rain during last night — a clouded day with some sunshine.  **Temp:** about 70°

Fires at night in hotel. I got over to the house before 9 — Webb was there at work but no Buck. Adams was painting blinds \+ the young man I hired was clearing up the rubbish from about the house. About 10 Ralph came over bringing a large mail — among others a barbarous letter from Charl. that made me very angry \+ sick too. In his dictatorial way he took me to task for permitting such foul \+ unsanitary plumbing to remain in my 38th St. house — 2° story back — the cause of illness of every member of his family \+ threatening if I did not have it corrected he would advise Mary \+ the others to seek a new home. I felt outraged by his impudence but shall not reply for some days — if I reply at all. A plumber from Callander came in the afternoon \+ stopped some of the many leaks. But it was impossible to get Otis, who alone knows how \+ where to cut off the Reserve water. I succeeded in getting a fire in the Range but the coal is too large — as it is not what I ordered. I asked Beede to change it. Mr. \+ Mrs. Lowenstein called at the house. To hotel at 6.30 — very tired, depressed \+ discouraged. Piano \+ Violin music was commenced for the season Sat. eveg. I sat for a while in Parlor with my newspaper. Ralph has been off all day with other boys.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 10, 1900

**Weather:** Showers about 8 o'clk. then cloudy, breezy \+ cool — very pleasant at noon.  **Temp:** 74° cool in the eveg. — Ther. 10° lower.

It was after 9 when I got to the house. Both carpenters there — Adams \+ assistant painter — \+ also the plumber. He worked all the morning before he got things right. The trouble at the ball-cock, which w°. not close, was owing to sand in the pipe — he put in a cut-off outside of the house, stopped leaks \+ tightened joints. Ralph \+ I put in good work in the m'g. unpacking china \+ glass. After lunch I wrote to many from whom I rec'd. a letter this m'g. Then again to house \+ unpacked kitchen things, iron lamps etc — at 6 I was completely tired out — had a short rest on the north piazza enjoying the lengthening shadows over the valley — then to hotel — dressed — \+ had dinner after 7. After dinner I went to my room \+ dozed until after 9. To bed about 10.
