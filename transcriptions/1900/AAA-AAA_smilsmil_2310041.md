## Left Page

### Sat. March 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Down, with grip.

After a wretched night of fever \+ sleeplessness I kept my bed all day \- temp. ranging from 100 3/5° to 102 3/5 \- Yale came before 11 \+ says I have grip. many of the symptoms, pains \+ dis comforts \+ much like those I had when sick for three weeks in Naples 1893. Yale gave me Phenactine, Salicin \+ Caffiene \- a composition that kept me awake all day \+ nearly all night. I did n't like it \+ gave it up.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 4, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright day \- my temp. at 8 A.m. was 102½°. \+ I had considerable pain, especially about the head. Yale came after 11 \+ gave me 1/16 gr. of morphia \- that was enough to relieve the pain \+ not enough to give me the dis comforts that usually go with morphia when I take it \- my voice went entirely \+ I could not speak above a whisper. Temp at 5 p.m. 101 2/5°.
