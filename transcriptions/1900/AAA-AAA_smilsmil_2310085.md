## Left Page

### Sun. June 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a very hot night up at 5 A.M. had a bulley bath \+ shampoo — back to bed — a restful nap \+ up for all day at 8 o'clk. It was about 11 when R. \+ I rode up to Central Park, entering at the 72d. S. gate we loitered through the Ramble — finding a very pleasant breeze \+ comparative coolness in the shade. At 1 o'clk. we went into the Met. Mus. \+ saw the F. E. Church paintings. I was disappointed — they did not come up to my recollection or my expectation. Home to dinner at 2 — \+ had a good nap after it — supper at 6.30 — wrote to Mr. Kent \+ at 8.30 went to Geo. Cook's to confer with him about my acct. that he is writing up — Home soon after 10 — a very cool eve'g. — sudden change of weather.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 4, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Chilly wind from east \+ clouded all day. To studio at 9.30 — then downtown — went to the electro man, Ringler, Park Place, \+ left pls. of 24-lib. plates for him to make photo-gravure reductions — then to Jno. Boyle's in Fulton St. \+ bought duck to cover piazza decking at Keene — then to K.TV. in Canal St. — then to Wakefield Rattan Co. Canal St. — then to Fredericks — 140 Sullivan St. \+ got specimen of French Crayon paper — then home — lunch — Studio at 2 o'clk — got the two ex-lib. plates \+ specimen paper \+ again went to Ringler's \+ left the plates to be steel faced — then to K.TV. with specimen paper \+ the colored ink pfs. that D. Van D. approved. Then to the Heywood Bros. \+ Wakefield Co. foot of Charlton St. — saw W. J. D. Walsh — gave him Charl's card of introduction \+ in return he gave me a note to head salesman (W.M. Baxter) Wakefield Co. in Canal St. — then to studio — then home after 5 \+ had a little nap before dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g I wrestled with Ralph's arithmetic — Geo. \+ Charl came in — To bed after 11 — Mary continues to improve.
