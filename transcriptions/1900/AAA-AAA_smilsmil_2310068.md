## Right Page

### Tues. May 1, 1900

**Weather:** Fine day — got up in the m'f with a stupifying headache which rendered me useless during the m'f. but it passed very gradually in the afternoon \+ evening.  **Temp:** [illegible]

While at b'kf's Higgins' Roofers came for studio keys — I went to studio \+ up on the roof with them \+ saw them started on re-lining the gutter — Then home \+ on my bed until 1 o'clk — Then I had a very light lunch — Mary, Kate, Katherine \+ Bessie had tickets for Theatre but Katherine was too much out of sorts to go so, as I was fit for nothing else, I took her ticket \+ went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw Pinero's Farcical Romance "The Amazons" a very amusing bit of nonsense with a little bit of unobtrusive, intelligible moral to it. Walter Allen as Count de Grival interested me — he is a very good all-round man — After the play went to studio for a little while — Dinner at 6.30 — We f Mr. Meyer \+ Charl came in — About 9 — Mr. Jno. Ireland came \+ was with me until 11 o'clk — going over Geo. Cook's books \+ giving an estimated value — These books have been in Annie's possession \+ mine for many years — now Geo. would like to dispose of them.
