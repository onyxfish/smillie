## Left Page

### Fri. May 18, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate went to Po'k.

Dull m't - showers - at 11 - at 1 again in the eve'g. Uncle W - aunt Agnes \+ Dining came to the house just as we finished bfst. I got to studio at 9.30 \+ worked on Vandyke ex-lib until W. H\. Russell Wray called at about 11 o'clk - he strayed until 1 - very chatty - a man of the world, in a way - I gave him a note of introduction to Mr. Marrien, of Scribners \+ he went to him with his M.S. Home to lunch \+ back at 2 - did not feel in good working trim so I did some loafing, but I managed to get all of the work on the V. D. plates drawn in \+ the plate varnished, ready for the acid. I also got Pf. of Century ex-lib. put up in ply \+ a note to Mr. W\. W\. Bispham - Ch\. of library com. but was too late to take it to his house in 18th St - before dinner - home at 6.30 - Every wrote letter to Jim \+ posted it - then did some packing up of things that have been lying about my bedroom -

## Right Page

### Sat. May 19, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night - very heavy rain through the n't \+ continuous thro' p.m. \+ eve'g. So dark after b'fst that I did not go to studio until 10.30 when there I wrote until 12. - Then there was a brief lull in the storm \+ I took some of my record books to G\. Cook's - 156 Mad. Ave - his writing up my acc't \+ to W\. W\. Bispham at 12 W. 18th St., I took Pf. of Century ex-libris together with photos, etc — Then home to lunch - after lunch I went to Lenox library - cor. 5th Ave. \+ 70th St. - spent some time with [illegible] who has chf. of the newly organized Print Dep't. He showed me how the prints are now packed away. Then I went to galleries \+ renewed acquaintance with old friends. I was much impressed by F. E. Church's "Cotopaxi" — He was a great painter - peculiar - original - and having a fine distinction - serious \+ noble - that I think is not sufficiently appreciated now. I spent some time among the old books \+ M.S.S. at 5 walked home thro' pouring rain — by the side of the Park, under the elms, it was delightful. Dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g. Chas came in for a time - Mary continues to improve very slowly -
