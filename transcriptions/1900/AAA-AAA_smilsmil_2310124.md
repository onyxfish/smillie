## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 20, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - cool \+ breezy - morning spent about the place - Buck \+ Webb worked on Cold Room - Adams not on hand. Otis commenced work on road up to house, hauling stone - Ray working on old road bank and clearing up dead stuff. At 11 o'clk I went to hotel for mail - Dinner at 1 - I had a little nap after it \+ at 3.30 following Mary, Katharine, Miss Holt \+ Jim I tramped out to Mr. Loring's camp on Chappel Pond - Spent some time there with Mr. L. \+ her three nieces - Then tramped back in time for tea at 6.30 - Ev'g at 8 I went to annual meeting of Trail Ass'n. in Scott Brown's office - small meeting - White, Weston, Maghee, Howells, Young D. Janeway \+ self the only ones present — walked back with W. Maghee about 9.30.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 21, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - lovely cool air \+ beautiful color - The mtf. spent as usual about the place looking after the men. Buck \+ Webb at Cold Room - Gibbs, plumber, came \+ commenced metal work in ice-box - Two men \+ team at work on road. To hotel at noon - Katharine \+ Ida Holt joined us there \+ we set? home by way of River Trail - dinner at 1 - Immediately after \+ Chase took Mary, Kate, Katharine, Bessie \+ Ida Holt to Lower Ausable Lake \+ spent the afternoon there. Jim spent the morning fishing on Beede brook \+ brought home eight trout. Mr. \+ Mrs. Oppenheim \+ Schilling called in the afternoon while I was alone \+ Mr. Maghee \+ Mrs. Hardenburg came also - The O's. were very pleasant - Mr. O - is a very attractive woman The lake party ret'd. before they left. Tea at 6.30 - Ev'g. It was decided that as Miss Holt had not been very well during the day her departure to-morrow mtf. would be postponed - so after \+ Chase \+ I went to hotel \+ countermanded the order - we now just went to the Weston Camp fire, finding the fire at home more inviting.
