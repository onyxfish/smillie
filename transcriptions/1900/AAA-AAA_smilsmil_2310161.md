## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 2, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild sunny day. To studio at about 10 \+ to work on Normandy barn yard until a little before 12 — then to 2nd Nat'l Bank \+ from there to Scribner's \+ saw W. Marvin for a few minutes — Home to lunch a little after 1 — looking over bank balance \+ my acct. until lunch — again to studio \+ painted until 4.30 — then out \+ sauntered about going into the picture galleries of Clausen, Knordler \+ Knoedler — Home at 6.30 — dinner — Charl came in late — he has no cook so he took dinner with us — after that Mary, Ralph \+ I went to his house — he played on his pianola \+ we looked over recent illustrated serials — Home at 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sound money parade.

A nasty day — raw \+ wet — clearing after night fall — cloudless moonlight after 9 p.m. The weather a great disappointment to the paraders to-day — nevertheless there was a remarkable turn out — the rain from 8. A.m. until after 4 p.m. was constant, but the weather bureau says that only .26 of an inch fell. The Tribune says that by actual count there were in the parade 87,615 — it was nearly 7 hours in passing a given point. I went to studio about 10 \+ worked all day on Normandy Barn yard — going home to lunch at usual time. \+ painted as long as I could see — in fact, longer than I could see in the afternoon. Dinner at 6.30 — Mary Clark came in for awhile — I left him with Mary to go to the Century monthly meeting. I had a pleasant time \+ met many with whom I had more or less pleasant chats — home a little before midnight.
