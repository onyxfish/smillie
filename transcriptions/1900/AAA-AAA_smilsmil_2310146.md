## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightful day \- bkfs. at 7.30 out about 8 30 went to the house \- found Buck \- Adams, Ray \+ Mr. Ducharme at work \- Buck finished the Studio in the m/f \+ then commenced hanging blinds on Anney \- Adams varnished main room floor \+ stained various places \- Ray cleaned up in general \+ Mrs. D \- finished her washing \+ ironing \- At 1 o'clk. Ralph \+ I went to Mr. Hardenburgh's \+ had a very pleasant dinner \+ we sat upon the porch until 2.30 \+ then returned to the house where I was kept busy until dark \- then to Mr. Brown's \- supper at about 7 \- Eve\] \- newspaper \+ chatting with [illegible] Murtland Scott Brown John Brown \+ Lagrand Hale.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 4, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still mild, beautiful weather \- Bkfs \- at 7.30 \- then to hotel P.O. Telephoned to West Port \+ Atwood told me that there was a bar there \- mine \- probably Russell, Hurin Co. hardware \- ordered a month or more ago. I don't want to leave here until I have seen it \- A note from Mr. Cutter said that Ralph would loose but little if he did not get to school before last week. When I got to the house Buck was hanging blinds in Anney \+ Adams was staining floor in Anney \- Then Winch came \+ I spent the most of the m/f with him emptying traps \+ pikes \+ washing out [illegible] \- I had intended to pack my trunk so as to leave to-morrow m/f. That is now impossible \- To dinner at Brown's at 12 30 \- I asked Mr. B\- if we could stay until Monday \+ he was very anxiously assented \- Then Ralph \+ I decided to have a little vacation \- At 2.30 we started on the Reserve Road and walked to Gothic Trail \- 2 miles \- then across to the river at Beaver Meadow fell \- then down the west bank trail \- It was a delightful walk \- The woods were wonderfully beautiful \+ the distant view, over the gorge to the [illegible] was superb \- as the shadows lengthened and the sunshine crept along the trees leaving the bases in violet \+ azure shadow \+ autumn foliage in the foreground framed in the picture I wished for youth \+ strength that I might try to paint such combination of form \+ color \- We got in before 6 \- I had a good rest, \+ supper at 7\. \- A lady friend of Mrs. B's was there \+ Mr. Ford, a bro. of Mrs. B \- I wrote a letter to Mary \+ to bed before 10\.
