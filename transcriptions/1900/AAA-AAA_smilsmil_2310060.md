## Left Page

### Sat. April 14, 1900

**Weather:** fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine day - after bkfs. went to the Bonnard Foundery, 444 W. 16' S. bet. 9 + 10 Ave's - + saw the French + Potter equestrian Statues of Washington that is to be sent to Paris - a contribution from American woman - The place in which I stood was so small that it was impossible to see it properly but it is undoubtedly a fine work - it seemed to me that the two eyes of W. were not looking at the same object. To Studio a few minutes after 11 - worked on Mr. G head until nearly 1 then home to lunch - back to studio at 12 expecting to have a call from D. Gerster - at 4.20 telegram that he was detained - After study of [illegible] Amp. G - Taken yesterday I decided that all of the shaded side of the face was too light - I tried unsuccessfully to lay a Rebiting ground - then in a sort of despair I set to work upon it with grover - I do not know where it will land me - sometimes I think I shall go wild over it - I wish that D. G - w. let me give up the attempt - At 4.30 took a half hour walk with Ralph - Then home + had a good nap until 6.30 - then dinner - at 8.30 went to Ceretine - from 9 to 10 a very interesting lecture by Dellenbaugh describing the expeditions of last summer known as the Harriman exped. that went from N.Y. to San Francisco + then by steamer up the coast to Bheringe Strait - illustrated by a great number of lantern slide photos. He had a phonograph which Reproduced some of the Esquimaux language - songs - etc - also, of Sitta Indians -

## Right Page

### Sun. April 15, 1900

**Weather:** A lovely day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day - up at 6.30 - had my scrub - back to bed at 7.30 - up at 8.30 - bkfs. at 9. It was about 11.30 before Ralph + I went out for our Sunday mt. walk - to 34th S. car to 8th Ave - car to 110th S. we walked to Morningside Park - R. had his camera + made a number of snap-shots at Park - Cathedral + Columbia - home again by way of 8th Ave. Dinner at 2 - I worked at assorting + classifying books in attic + bedroom until after 6. Evening I read aloud portions of Tucker's history of the Mormons which led to discussion - Well - more time in collecting and putting away books + maps -
