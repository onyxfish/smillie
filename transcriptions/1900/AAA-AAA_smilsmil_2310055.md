## Left Page

### Mon. April 2, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant day, altho' fickle  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got to studio's Nicole came — he stayed until near noon — we talked of N.A. matters — especially the proposed sittm' in Central Park \+ the progress that has been made up to present time in that matter "Pulse laying," log rolling" — etc — At 12 I went to 2 Nat. B'k — home at 1 — lunch — at 2 Kate, Jim, Ralph \+ I went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw the farce "A night off — Out at 5 — I went to studio's \+ worked until 6 — then a short walk — dinner — Eveth. Geo. Cook came \+ we worked together until 11 o'clk — going over the collection of books in the house \+ marking those that belong to Geo. — a considerable number have been left here for years — now he wishes to dispose of them in some way.

Mary went to Po'k at 10.30 to stay until Sat.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 3, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant, with exception of a smart shower about noon time — I got to studio after 10 having been detained after hlo', writing some notes — worked on M.P. G. No. 2. At noon left studio with intention of going to the barber's — was caught by shower \+ instead went home — After lunch, it having cleared pleasant, I went to barber's \+ had hair cut \+ head shawn \+ so'd — to studio at 3.45 — worked on head \+ got it ready for pf. At 5.30 went out, had a walk, home to dinner. Eveth., Geo. Cook came \+ we worked until 11 selecting \+ checking off his books. It was 11.30 when I got to bed.
