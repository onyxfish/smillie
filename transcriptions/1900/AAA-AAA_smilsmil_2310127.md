## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 26, 1900

The tramps departed at 9 a.m.

An exceedingly hot \+ uncomfortable day. Was all day about on the north piazza keeping as quiet as possible. Our tramps visitors — got up early \+ had their baths \+ breakfasts \+ left for the upper ausable \+ a tramp beyond just as we were going to our blkf't. \+ after borrowing $25.00 to see them through. I think that Mallia is under some malign influence. Afternoon I painted over the sky study painted yesterday as there was a fine display of clouds \+ color. I made a better study than the one of yesterday but nothing to be proud of. I had a headache all day \+ as the afternoon wore on I became more \+ more uncomfortable. I went to bed about 9 o'clk. with temp. 100 3/5° \+ 98 pulse. I can only imagine that it is some malarial remnant brought out by exposure about the cold room where there has been digging \+ moisture. I took 6 gr. quinine going to bed \+ 3 more about 4 a.m.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 27, 1900

Still hot \+ muggy. Rain came about the middle of the afternoon \+ fell heavily until far on in the night. No one but Ray at work on the place, for which I was, after all, thankful as I was too much out of sorts to direct any of them. I hung about the place all day feeling very weak \+ having headache. Jim went off in the mt. for a day of fishing, taking lunch with him. He did not get home until 6.30 — wet through \+ through of course, but very happy, as he had 18 nice brook trout caught on the Beeds brook.
