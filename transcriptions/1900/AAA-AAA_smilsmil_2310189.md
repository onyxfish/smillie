## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 30, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mg. opened bright but it clouded over - was cold + raw + at night it rained - While I was dressing Mr. C. W. Bayard Cutting came to see if Raefish w. spend New Years day with Bronson at their Country place on Long Island - he left a note + before I got my bkft. I went to telephone + had a little conversation with Mr. Cutting at Islip. Then bkft - Then newspapers - Then a short walk with Raefish to look at the new electric light + power house - foot of East 38 + 9 St. then home + had a nap for dinner - after dinner I had another nap - Charl came in for a while - supper at 6.30 - Before 8 I went to Edw. Cook's - saw Catherine + her fiancé - Clarence Smith - he impressed me agreeably as frank + manly - gave Catherine tickets to N.A.S.D. Private View + Reception - I sent like cards to Rev. Mr. Balcom Shaw + to T. Frank Brownell - Then home + spent a long evg. on my Cash book - getting it written up to date.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 31, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet + disagreeable day after a night of rain - Wrote a note to Mr. C. W. Bayard Cutting expressing regret that owing to bad weather and Raefish's condition (he is suffering from a severe cold) he w. be unable to accept Bronson's invitation to spend New-Years Day with him - Then to studio + painted on left hand foreground of 33×44 - work did not go along smoothly - Home to lunch at 1.30 - it was about 3 when I got back to studio - painted for an hour or more \+ then cleaned up - having finished the work of the 19th century - so far as my individual limits go - This mg. I rec'd. a note from Mr. Kent announcing that she would buy the Montrose house with furniture for $7000 cash - This is a sorry climax but altho' from many points of view it makes me unhappy I am satisfied that, taken all in all, I have done well to accept - Also, with the end of the century I close the Montrose episode - that chapter is closed + now all that I have left is the memory of many happy days with Auntie there - also, some anxious ones - for there Raefish was born - it was hard to give it up but it was harder to hold it - Eveg. I made a free hand chart of the place explanatory of the ten deeds under which I hold the property I at the request of Mr. Kent I am to send them to Hq. Sprague at Buffalo.
