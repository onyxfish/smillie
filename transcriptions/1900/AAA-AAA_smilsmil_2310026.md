## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 1, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bitter cold day with a wicked north west wind. To studio before 10 - and another day was spent on my portrait. It surprises me to find how I do fuss over things - There is but little more that I can see to do to it. I printed three india p[r]o carefully. It was nearly 2 o'clk. when I got home to lunch \+ nearly 3 when I got back to studio.

After 5 I went to Siegle \+ Cosker's \+ bought some gas heaters for etching studio N.45 \+ S. Home to dinner at 6.30 \- Every some reading \+ some writing up \+ monthly acc[oun]ts. Got a note from Orlando Beede enclosing receipt for my Taxes for 1899 - just paid by him - the same taxes that were paid for me by Scott Brown on the 29th [illegible] Dec - and receipted for by the same Collector.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 2, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear \+ Cold. Spent some time after bkfst. writing to Beede \+ others, making out checks, etc. Then went to W.S. Miller's - then to studio - Miller's carpenters came with lumber to commence work on alterations in Maynard's studio - M. not in, so I had to attend to it - Then, trouble with heater in the cellar \+ that took more time - Then I got started on Tracing on gelatine Mr. G. head \+ then S.R. Koehler came in, he is to sail for the Azores with his daughter to-morrow on leave of absence - His wife died a few weeks ago \+ he is very much broken \+ looks wretchedly - I took him home with me to lunch \+ was after 3 when I got to studio - finished tracing, bevacided it \+ got out plate for etching \+ m. - at 4.30 went to J. Q. A. Ward's studio. I met there W. Whittredge's two daughters - young women of very agreeable appearance \+ manner. Nicole, Dilendin, Beckwith \+ Carrere came and there was a general discussion as to the course best to pursue \+ it was agreed that we should severally sound leading news papermen to get their views \+ probable attitude with reference to the project of getting a site in Central Park on 59th St. \+ head \+ Nave. To me was assigned Tribune \+ [illegible] Post. After Carrere left Ward surprised us by saying that "unless there was to be a new competition of Architects, if this project went thro' - he would drop out of it at once" - He was very positive \+ the effect was unpleasant - He said [illegible] Lee Hastings \+ have a pork Tack with him - to my mind This is a very formidable snag. I got home to dinner a little late - Every - all hands went to Charles's - Geo came in - Ralph \+ I ret[urne]d home before \+ - I wrote until 11.
