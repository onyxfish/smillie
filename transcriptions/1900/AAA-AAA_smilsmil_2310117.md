## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 6, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day, mitigated by strong breezes \+ winding up with thundershowers that continued well into the night. Up before 7 \- Buck \+ Webb came to work \+ commenced taking off the siding on the side of ice house. One of the Otis boys \+ Ducharme came to work on the Road but I set them to work digging for foundation for cold room \- they were at it all day making little progress. I set Buck \+ Webb at putting up shelves \+ hooks in the bed room \+ late in the afternoon, as the hinges came with m\+f. I had them put up some of the outside window blinds. I wrote a letter to Callanau, the Keeseville plumber about the trouble I am having with the hot water arrangements \- also to Ryan of N.Y.. After 4 I went to hotel \+ mailed letters. About 6 o'clk. Mollie A. \+ Miss Davies came along wet \+ muddy, having completed their round of rts. \+ been caught in the upper Ausable in a heavy thunderstorm. They stayed with us over night. The ev'g was close \- all seemed to be more or less tired \+ we went to bed early.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 7, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain last night \+ much more through the day \- \+ again at night. I did not get up until late. Bkfs. after 8 \- no workmen came \- but much of the m\+f was given the selection, cutting and making of the curtains to be hung on the shelves that the carpenters put up yesterday. I did some preparatory work in the taking of measurements \+ laying out of plans for the finishing wood work in Studio \+ main room. I am so lame in neck \+ shoulders. Rheumatism I suppose, that it is hard for me to do anything. After dinner I went to hotel. Later in the afternoon Mr. Maghee called his young daughter with him. Ev'g I wrote to Wanamaker \+ to Mr. M\+ Russell Gray.
