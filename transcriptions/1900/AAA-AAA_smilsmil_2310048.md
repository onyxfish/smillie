## Left Page

### Mon. March 19, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. at 8 AM 97¾° — at 10.30 p.m. 97¾°. A dull disagreeable day — quite mild — showers between 12 [illegible] — \+ again from about 5 p.m. thro. The eve'g. To studio at about 10 \+ spent the m'g there — clearing up — changing p/p between blotters \+ working upon a crayon portrait of Yale that I made last April — Home to lunch at 1.30 Eliz, Howe \+ 'Gene Atwater lunched with us. About 2.30 Ralph \+ I started out — went to Higgins. The Roofer — then to 5th Ave. Safe Deposit \+ learned from Abstract of Title that 110 E. 38th St. was probably built in the Spring of 1855. Then to Alexandres — 6th Ave. \+ 19th St. and bought shoes \+ Rubbers for R— \+ two p's of shoes for self — walked up home — in at 5\. Rested until dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g newspaper \+ writing \+ to bed at about 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 20, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temp. at 8 AM 98° at 10.30 p.m. 98°. A mild day — mostly sunny. At 9.30 went to studio \+ was engaged there until after 12 — man came from Higgins \+ I took him up on roof \+ he patched leaks in gutter. I worked awhile on crayon sketch of Yale \+ spoiled what little of good there was in it. I boiled out old ink \+ kept \+ made up some Asphaltum varnish. After 12 I went to Durand Ruel's to see the works of Ten American Artists — much of which is away beyond my comprehension — Met Julius Ohlme there — went over to his gallery to see Madrazo portraits — Then to Montross Gallery \+ saw Tom Dewing's paintings — Then to Knordler's — saw George's group \+ met Mr. Chauncey \+ Miss Woodruff. Home to lunch at 2.15 Maurice Stone came to see me about 3 — at 4 I went to studio \+ busied myself about various things until near 6 — then home — dinner at 6.30 — Miss Emily Taylor dined \+ spent the eve'g with us informally.
