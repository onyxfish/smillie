## Left Page

### Tues. Dec. 18, 1900

**Weather:** Rather raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dinner to Jno. Cauldwell..

Rather raw — To studio & cleared up a little. Painted some sheets into the little Oct. study on fair ground. Montrose — about noon Emma Ireland came in to see my painting — 10 millers at 1.30 for a few minutes — then home to lunch & again to studio by 2.30 — Mary Cook & her daughter Catharine came in for a few minutes — I went home at 4.30 & had a rest for an hour — then dressed & at 7 at Century a dinner given by about 24 artists to John Cauldwell to laud his work as Fine Arts Com't at the Paris Exp'n & to endorse it — It was done without hitch or flaw so far at the 24 who were present was concerned — Maynard was C's left hand man — put da Farge sat on his right. Many Watrons ran the other end of the Table — French \+ Adams. The sculptors were there — Dielman — Isham — Mowbray — Alden Weir — Alexander — the two Jones's — Fowler — Swain Gifford & Beckwith were there \+ Faxon — Perry, Guy & myself represented the old guard — the dinner was very simple and plain & served reasonably well — The room (the small library) & table being uncomfortably crowded — it was 7.30 when we sat down — Alexander was on my left & Gifford was on my right. After the dinner Caldwell was introduced in fewest possible words by Maynard & he gave a running story of his experiences during the past summer, touching lightly on his stripe with the Parisian American artists before 1 o'clk — the Dannat stripe & other incidents had been pretty thoroughly ventilated & freely criticised — of course & got off some stories & was evidently in very good humor with himself & the rest of us. Beckwith spoke very well — Dielman spoke — Alexander spoke — French was forced to say a few words, which he did very modestly — but to me the little speech by Isham was far more interesting than any of the others — it was so unassuming — so modest in manner — & with a vein of fine humor running thro' it. It was very warmly applauded. It was a very clean ev'g — to me an enjoyable one.

## Right Page

### Wed. Dec. 19, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came home from Pomfret

A very fine day — much milder & quiet. I went to studio after bk'f't. 9 o'c came in & it was 10.30 before I got away for Etching class at N.A. dp't — there I found a class of 9 working on plates after a model (a servant of Davis's — the regular model failing to appear) while they worked I made up a lot of soft ground \+ Asph'm. varnish — etc — about 1 I started for home \+ walked across the park to Mad. Ave. & then rode home — lunch — to studio soon after 2 — Jim's train delayed, got in before 3 — I pottered about many little things — Grace Ireland & her friend, Mr. Harrison, came in to see my picture — later Sterne came bringing p'f of a sunset portrait etching that he has just made — Home at 6 — dinner — Kate & Bessie & Charlie's. Ev'g. with the boys & reading.
