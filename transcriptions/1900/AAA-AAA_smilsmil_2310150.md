## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 11, 1900

**Weather:** Clear \+ windy \- not quite so cold as yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- At 9 o'clk. Cooley came \+ we went to the Morris house \+ from there laid out a line for the trench in which to lay the pipe that is now on the surface of the ground carrying lake water to the barn tanks.
After that I saw the Keels \- settled matters with Mr. K \- by paying the half of William's wages while she has had his services \- rather a hard bargain, I think, \+ under which the expenses of the place for the year have been fully $50 more than I have rec'd. in rent \- At 1 o'clk \- lunch at Mary Sayre's \- Cynthia Richards there \- Afternoon packed up for a start \- again went down town \- stopped at Village Hall where the stage scenery was being put up. I got my bank book from bank, balanced \- It was really awaiting around until train time \- Ret'd. to Sayre house about 5 o'clk \- got a lunch in paper \- to train at 6 \- and at 8 we reached Scranton \- Parke met us at the station \+ we drove to his home \- where we were very cordially rec'd. by the entire family except Norman who is studying at Princeton \- After a pleasant supper we sat chatting until 11 \- Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 12, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f. \- hazy sunlight \- quiet \+ milder than yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- Bkft. after 8: after 9, Mr. \+ Mrs. P., Bessie \+ Bosworth P. \- Ralph \+ I went out riding \- drove out to the Park and around the new reservoir which is remarkably like a natural lake \- it was after 11 \- nearly noon \- when we got back to P.'s \- lunch about 1 \- after that sat in P.'s den with him \+ Bessie \+ chatted until it was time for us to take leave to catch train \- P. drove us to station \+ we took the 3.30 to N.Y. reaching the foot of Christopher St. at 7.30 comfortably \+ without incident \- Rode home at once getting there soon after 8. Found Mary alone \- the girls having dined at Charles' \+ gone to theatre with him \+ Flipo \- We sat \+ waited for our friends until 11 \- they did not come \+ then I went to bed.

This m'f. Parke got word by Telephone that David Sayre was dead.
