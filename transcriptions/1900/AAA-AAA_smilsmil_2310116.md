## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 4, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day after a really cold night - It was cool, brilliant \+ exhilerating - At a little time after bkf. Mollie \+ her friend got away on their long walk - about 10.30 Jim, Ralph \+ I went down the valley about 4 miles on our wheels - we stopped at the deserted Brook Russel cottage and peered into the windows - then back to the village \+ made some purchases - we went up Shurtleff's lane intending to make a call but finding that it was later than I thought, gave it up \+ rode home getting in at about noon - I made an entire change of clothing \+ had a rub down \+ a rest before dinner at 1.30 -
I had another rest after dinner - Miss Wilson \+ Miss Mitcheson called \+ at 5.30 I went over to hotel with them \+ got mail - Tea at 6.30 - Evef. a pleasant fire in Main room - Read news papers etc.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 5, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm, pleasant day after a cold night - with a very gentle thundershower after 7 P.m. - Up about 6 a.m. \+ had my usual Sunday mtf. Scrub \+ some writing before bkf. at 9 \+ a little exploring about the place - after bkf. wrote to Elea Diven - to Mr. Lobenstine - to H.S. Conklin etc - About noon went to the hotel - Dinner at 2 - After that tried to get a nap, but the flies were too much for me - About 4 got ready to go on a walk with Ralph - but it was overcast - the air was still \+ close - I did not feel like walking - instead, I carried some of the planed lumber to a place under the piazza - Young Dr. Janeway \+ his wife came after 5 \+ made a pleasant call - Tea before 7 - Evef. Reading D. Van Dyke's "Fisherman's Luck".

Heavy Thunder shower bet. 11 \+ 12 o'clk P.m.
