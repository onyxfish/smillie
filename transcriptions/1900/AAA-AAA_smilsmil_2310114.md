## Left Page

### Tues. July 31, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day — but hot — clouded over late afternoon — Thunder shower bet. 9 \+ 10 o'clk. p.m. Up at 6 — Webb came \+ in the afternoon I got a note from Buck saying that he was sick — w'd be along as soon as he was able. I had not lumber enough to keep Webb at work all day — I made computation of amt. needed for Refrigerator Room \+ gave it to Webb to leave at Fletcher Beede's mill — Adams bros. worked m'g; in studio \+ afternoon stretching ducking on piazza — By mis measurement I am 2 yds. short — Wrote to Boyle to send that amt. to me. Moody worked at chopping out new line of road from Public Road, up the hill to the House — late in the afternoon he cut his hand so that he had to go home. I went looking for Scott Brown to get stone for underpinning for Refrigerator — could not find him — Then walked up the wood Road that comes out by Openheim's \+ Adler's —
Eve'g divided between loafing, reading \+ writing.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 1, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet m'g, rain free until after 9 — then cleared to be a charming day. I got up before 7 \+ cleared the studio so as to take advantage of the wet day to complete the hanging of burlaps in studio. I was at work upon it when at 10.30 the Adams bros. came — They continued \+ finished that work \+ in the afternoon completed the putting down of a duck covering on the piazza deck — I worked all the m'g in putting up the burlaps that separates the attic from the studio — after dinner, went to hotel and sent P.O. money order to Jno. Boyle for $1.62 for duck. Then to Scott Brown \+ arranged with him \+ young Otis to haul stone for underpinning for cold Room — Then back home \+ at about 4 o'clk. Jim, Ralph \+ I rode down the Valley on our wheels 2½ miles — The boys made some small purchases at the stores — back before 5 . . .
Eve'g — so cool that we had a fire in the main Room. About 9 o'clk — Lizzie, the nurse of Mrs. Sidney Smith came to call on Kate, Leonard \+ Jane — She had no walk \+ had lost her way — when she returned I loaned her a lantern.
