## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 10, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A little rain in the m'f - but it soon cleared to be a pleasant, mild, day - At 9 o'clk went to Dr. Noyes to have him look at my eyes \+ new glasses - Then to studio and made a third portrait pen sketch of self. This time I did better - Took it home at lunch time \+ got some suggestions - After lunch went to Avery's \+ the S'ilo galleries to see pictures - Then to studio \+ worked for about an hour on pen portrait - Then to Tach Bro. \+ ordered a dog. \+ The photo's of self - Then to J. T. White's \+ saw young Bixler \+ gave him my pen sketch with which he seemed to be much pleased - Then to Kelpel's - saw him \+ had a chat - Then home to dinner - Eve'f. Charl \+ Mr. Meyer came \+ took Katharine \+ Bessie to the Opera - After my newspaper I wrote to Manuel \+ sent him off of N. A. Treas. Report asking him to make some change in the arrangement of Bld'g fund act.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 11, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded \+ bleak m'f - Rain commenced about 3 p.m - followed by sleet - at night very heavy \+ continuous rain - To studio at 10 - Spent the m'f drawing plans for a coal bin to be a separate little bld'g at Keene Heights, Went to Central Iron works at noon to leave order for a fire escape to be put up at back of 110. There are so many lives lost now by the burning of dwellings - \+110 is so badly constructed that I have become somewhat nervous - Afternoon - rummaged for some time in closets looking for phelps' paper for oil sketching - made very rough sketch on a big old mounting board of my idea of an Artists Gate \+ a m. A. building within Central Park lines on 59th St. at head of 7th Ave. Then sized it with glue preparatory to rubbing in an effect in color. Eve'f. at home - as usual.
