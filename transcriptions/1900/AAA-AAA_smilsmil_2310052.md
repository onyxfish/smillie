## Left Page

### Tues. March 27, 1900

**Weather:** Brilliant m't.  **Temp:** [illegible]

very pleasant day — To studio at 9.30 + at once to work printing. First, took proofs from dil[s]pincatt head but could not get what I wanted. Something was wrong but I could not tell what — perhaps it was everything — ink — rags, paper + all — Then I tried my little head — then small Gester head (Mr. G.) That was a flat failure + very depressing to me. At 1.30 I went to P.O. station — 44th: 6th Ave, with drawings for Coal bin — sent to Franz Branch at Keene. N.Y. Then to lunch — Jim, Ralph + I alone. The other members of the family at the Opera. I continued my efforts at printing until after 5 taking three p's from "The Road to the Lake" + two proofs of — but none of them were satisfactory. After a day of such hard + unsuccessful work I feel despairingly. I had a short walk before dinner — Every evening newspaper + yawning — Geo. came in for a time.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 28, 1900

**Weather:** Bright, breezy + cool.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 prepared to do something desperate on the dil[s]pincatt head plate — I had thought that I might work a wide toothed open mezzotint ground on the face to get a tone over the whole which is already too much cut up with close textured light line work — but when I got to work I stuck to it all day with the graver + went over most of the face + at night once more feel encouraged to hope that I may be able to make something out of it — Home to lunch at 1.30 + back at 2.30 + worked until after 5 — then I took a short walk + in to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. wrote to Miss Ellen M. Kent 512 Delaware Ave. Buffalo. in answer to a note from her asking if the Montrose house was to rent.
