## Left Page

### Thur. Jan. 18, 1900

**Weather:** Overcast \+ raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \+ raw — a light shower or two late p.m. \+ ever. At studio all the mf. working with graver on "The Road to the Lake". Got it ready for a proof — then home to lunch at 1 o'clk — at 2 all hands went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw "An Enemy to the King" by R. N. Stevens I didn't think much of it from almost any point of view. If it had been good my pleasure would have been marred by the fact that the "leading lady" of the Co. died very suddenly a couple of days ago \+ it was gruesome to me to think that her fellow players could go right on without any sign of sorrow. Out before 5. \+ found that we had just escaped a shower. R. \+ I went home — I took 8th Vol. of Am. Nat. Ency. of Biography — that is found to be imperfect \+ took it to J. T. White \+ Co. in 16th St. — but Bigler, whom I had expected to see, was not in — I left the Vol. with a note for him \+ got home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Ever. I helped R. somewhat with his lessons.

## Right Page

### Fri. Jan. 19, 1900

**Weather:** Dull, foggy mf.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, foggy mf. growing brighter about the middle of the day, but foggy \+ damp afternoon \+ night. I got to studio about 9.30 \+ wet paper \+ otherwise prepared for printing at 10.45 I went up to N.A. — carpenter has got all of his work up in the Etching Room \+ plumber has got his work done excepting the traps to acid sink — I varnished the inside of that to-day — left there after 12 \+ went to studio — ground up some ink — then home to lunch at 1.30 — then to studio \+ took some prints from plate — The Road to the Lake — \+ tried twice to get a print from mezzotint plate — after the storm — It seems to be filled with something — possibly wax 40 or 50 years old — I tried without success to get it cleaned by heat — to-morrow I shall give it a potash bath. Mr. White of Jas. T. White \+ Co. came bringing my pen sketch of my head — the photo engraver says that it cannot be reduced to the small size required \+ print satisfactorily. So I told him I wd. make another. To-day, at about 1 o'clk. Mr. Colton, who married one of the Burrell-Holt girls, died of pneumonia — The disease seems to have run a course much the same as in Annie's case, now almost five years ago. Ever. Ralph prepared for his proposed journey to-morrow with Bronson Cutting. I wrote to Jim \+ took the letter to the G.C. Station at 10.30 —
