## Left Page

### Sat. March 17, 1900

**Weather:** clear, cold  **Temp:** Temp. 8 Am. 97 4/5°. at 11 p.m. 97 3/5°.

\+ very windy — I did not dare go out of doors all day — but I put on hat \+ overcoat and opening wide the windows of my bed room \+ letting in all the air \+ sunshine I could, I walked in my bed room from 2 to 12.30 \+ from 3.30 to 4.30 — which gave me a very good airing — In the m'f I wrote to Mr. G. Derby, whites of Ja°. T. white \+ Co — Returning to him with suggestions, galley pf. of bio.' sketch \+ photo. sug. pf. of new portrait. Afternoon I wrote a long letter to Mr. Baohydt about Etching matters in the A. f. S. school. Eve'g with newspapers \+ some desultory reading.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 18, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** Temp. 8 Am. 97 4/5°. at 10.30 a.m. ditto.

a rather grey day with sprees of sunshine — cold \+ rather windy. I got up soon after 8 \+ had a good rub down \+ sponge bath \+ then went back to bed for a little while. B'f'st at 9.30 — During the m'f I read \+ wrote until after 12 — Then I could stand the confinement no longer \+ I bundled myself up \+ went out — on Park Ave. I met Emma Ireland \+ Helen Butler \+ I turned about \+ walked with them to Mad. Ave. \+ 45th St. enjoying the talk \+ walk very much. New home. Dinner at 2 — I had a nap after it in dining room sofa — Then I wrote a note to H\+ W. Jessup — Geo. \+ Nellie \+ his boys came in. Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g I wrote a letter to W. H. Warner of Montrose. It was about 11 before I got to bed.
