## Left Page

### Fri. April 20, 1900

**Weather:** Bright & very mild day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & very mild day — after an unrestful night I got up with large remnants of yesterdays headache which gradually passed off. To studio about 10 — some time arranging refs that were put between blotters yesterday — then, of course, I found more work to do on the Q-head & that kept me busy until after 1 — then home to lunch — after that I went to 57 ag't and spent the most of two hours looking over the Soc of Am. Artists' Exhib — "on me" it is not as interesting as their exhibs used to be years ago — At 4 I went to Baskwith's studio to meet Mr. DeWolf one of the leading men in the Park management. Dillham, Nicoll, Blashfield, deForest, J. G. Brown were there — DeWolf came rather late — He soon let us know that he did not propose to let us into the Park but we thought we might be permitted to build over the 40 foot sidewalk & that we might put the galleries up on stilts or columns — Was talked on these lines until 6 — I was rather disgusted & feel that there was very little to be hoped for I. Dielmann walked with me as we left & told me of the revival of Carnegie's interest & outlined a plan of refreshing negotiations evidently devised by H. B. Butler. "This promises far more than there is in the Park site but that may be used as a lever under Carnegie — at any rate — I took heart again & felt as though the bow now had two strings to it. Walked home \+ got in just in time for dinner. Eve'g, wrote a letter to Jim & mailed it before 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. April 21, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant mt, overcast later & rain at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant mt., overcast later & rain at night. To studio & spent some time making a camera-lucida reduction of Maynard's design for Century celebrities — but found that I could not, with a pencil point, define the detail as I wished — So, I shall have it reduced by Photography — Ralph came to studio & I gave some time to him in toning some photos that he had printed. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — expecting Dr. Gester but he did not come. I, at last, began reading the M.S. for H. R. Wray's second edition of Etching in the U.S., writing notes as I read — about 4 Herbert Smith, ms. agent came & took some of my time — Home about 5 & rested until dinner time after dinner Ralph & I went to the Am. Theatre — 8th Ave & 42d St. & heard, & saw, "The Mikado." It was very amusing altho' in some parts the fun was overdone — Frank Moulan as Ko-Ko — Lord High Executioner was an acrobat & dancer as well as singer & showed astonishing agility & lightness of foot. His quizzical face & his quick, nervous movements were full of fun & his singing was good. D. Eloise Moreau as Yum-yum was pretty & pleasing with charming voice & manner. Gertrude Quinlan as Pitti-Sing was an irrepressible romp & sometimes went beyond the bounds of good taste but her yellow gown was picturesque & becoming other song & dance just before the close of first act — "for he's going to marry Yum-yum — Yum-yum" was a pretty aytte called for two or three encores Ko-Ko's "I" or a little list" was encored half a dozen times & his gags set the house roaring — It was 11 o'clk before the performance ended & then I felt that I had been on a regular spree of nonsense — R. & I walked home & it was after midnight when I got to bed.
