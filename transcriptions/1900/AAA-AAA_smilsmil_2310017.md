## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 14, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant m'g. but soon clouded over  **Temp:** [illegible]

\+ snow fell briskly before 2 o'clk \- at night the moon shone out. Up about 7 \+ had my scrub \- bkfp. at 9.30 \- after 11 Ralph \+ I went up to Central Park \+ through the new Flower \+ Green Houses at 105" St. Then the snow began to fall. From the green houses we walked over to the Na\+l \- 109" St. \+ [illegible] Ave. Took down pfts. of Prize winner Etchings that had been hanging there. \+ made some measurements preparatory to the work to be put in there on Tues. by Miller (carpenter) \+ Ryan (plumber). Home at 2 o' \- dinner \- Dining with us. After dinner I had a long nap. Then wrote to J.J. O'Brien \+ ton. Tea \- then Read aloud to Ralph until 9. Then wrote to W.H. Warner about Village Hall matters \-

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 15, 1900

**Weather:** Slightly overcast \+ not particularly pleasant.  **Temp:** [illegible]

After bkfp. went to Miller's \+ saw the fixtures that are to be sent to Etching Studio \- Na\+l. This P.M. \- then to studio \+ finished the drawing in \+ extra work on "The Road to the Lake" \- varnished the plate \- also. Mad. Sq. Garden Tower \- To lunch at 1.30 \- back before 2.30 \- Mary Cook came in the m'g \+ invited me to dine on Wed. eve'g with Miss Kent of Buffalo. Afternoon, I bit the work on Tower Plate \+ got part of biting done on other plate \- Home at 5 feeling out of sorts generally \- Eve'g \- the discomfort in my head increased so much that I went to bed unusually early.
