## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 1, 1900

**Weather:** Very cold \- a light fall of snow last night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

wrote some notes after bkf. Then went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ bought eight seats for Wed. eve'g \- the whole family to go \- a cheap show \- 50¢ ahead \- then to studio \+ laid grounds on plates \- Mad. Sq. tower from the east \- The Road to the lake \- \+ an old steel plate with a mezzotint ground on it that I got from Father many years ago \- I don't know where he got it. I found employment for myself until lunch time \- then home \- a quiet lunch with Jim \+ Ralph, the other members of the family lunched at Charl's \- After it went with boys to Dr. Yale's as I wish him to give some attention to the cough that is troubling both of them \- \+ out of town \- we ret'd to Charl's \- at 3.15 I went to J. A. Ward's 49 W. 52d St. \- First meeting of Com\- to consider Municipal Site question \- Beckwith, Brown \- nicale, Ward \+ self present \- Dickman the only absentee \- Until about 5.30 we had an entirely informal discussion \- Brown surprised me by a passionate appeal for a gallery bldg. on the present site \- expend $5000 upon it \+ invest for endowment all remaining funds \- whenever the NA is built he wishes the NA to buy the cellar \- I said his galleries would be known as "Brown's Barn" \- Ward was almost equally passionate, but not so fluent, in describing his idea of a monumental edifice for the NA that would be an ornament to the city \- We adj'd to meet next Sunday at 4 p.m. \- Then home \- dinner \- Eve'g quietly at home.
