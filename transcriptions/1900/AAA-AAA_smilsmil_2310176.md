## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 2, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day - bright, sunny & quiet. After my scrub had my help. at 10 o'clk after that sat for awhile with newspapers then out with Ralph - went to Century & saw the pictures there by daylight - my painting did not look quite so bad as it did last night but it is very discouraging. I wish I could abandon the attempt to paint altogether & absolutely - I know that I would be happier as my reason tells me that there is nothing for me to hope for - I have done my best & am too old to do any better. Then we walked to Central Park & home to dinner at 2 - After that I tried to write but feeling very heavy I gave way to it & had a good nap - Then I wrote a letter to Nicoll who is sick - Tea at 6.30 - then I wrote auditing Report for N.A. of D. & a note to Watrous - also a copy of Report & note to date. Then wrote to Beckwith, Chairman of School Com. asking him to ask the Council to waive the $10 fee for entrance to Etching Class as I cannot get a class so long as that is Required. Then I wrote to Webb, the Carpenter Keene Valley sending him $2.00 to pay a couple of small bills there - about 10 o'clk I went out & mailed my letters & had a walk by moonlight.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

mild & pleasant, but not bright & sunny - to studio at usual time & spent an hour in writing draft of letter to Mr. Kent in reference to selling to her property at Montrose - Then to work, with little heart, on Normandy Barnyard - at 12.45 I went to 2d. Nat. Bk & home to lunch at 1.30 - to studio at 2.30 & painted until after 4 - washed up & then rode to Bdway & 83d. St. & bought new bougies, articles for Pastel files - then home & dinner at 6.30 - Evef. Charl came & Kate Ret'd with him to his house - at 9.30 I went there for her - home at 10.30.

my studio cellar has sprung a leak again - above the water proofing - on the west wall, under the stair - water runs in so freely that it fills the furnace pit I have had no trouble since D. K. Martin water proof'd the cellar in March '96.
