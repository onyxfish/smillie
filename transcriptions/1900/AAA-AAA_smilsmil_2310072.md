## Left Page

### Tues. May 8, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day but oppressively warm - a refreshing thunder shower at about 10 p.m. To studio at 9.30 \+ worked all the only day with graver \+ dry-point on Century book plate — Home to lunch at 1 o'clk - after that to NA of D - To meet my etching class - I was with them but a short time - the rest of the afternoon until 5.30 being with Mr. Marvel - accountant, going over the books to get the material for answers to Chas. H. Miller's questions - he makes the mistake of thinking that "liabilities" on a bal. sheet necessarily means debts — \+ that "totals" means net assets - consequently he wants to know how such an indebtedness as $87294.66 - Profit \+ loss acct. has been incurred \+ how it is that the "total assets" have been decreased in the past four years by $153310.29 — the first amt. is really a cr. not an indebtedness — \+ the second shows the amt. of indebtedness that has been paid off in the last four years walked through the park - very lovely. Dinner at 6.30 — Every wrote to Miss Kent in answer to her note stating that her mother wd. take the Montrose house for the summer.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 9, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast m't., rather mild - growing rapidly colder in the afternoon until at night when it was clear \+ positively cold with strong N.W. wind After bkf't. went to Bormann's \+ bought 3 rolls of photo. films for Jim - made up alpha of various things for him that were exp'd. in the afternoon. To studio \+ wrote to H\. R. way - home at 12 - soon after to NA. where I had lunch - The meeting called to order at 2 - 48 present - The proceedings ran without hitch - I read my reply to C.H. Miller's questions - well rec'd. by the meeting \+ without comment by him. After the meeting I took Colman \+ Gifford up to Etching Studio - they were much pleased - then rush'd home \+ got heavy overcoat \+ back soon after the dinner commenced - a fairly good dinner - After it Dielman made considerable efforts to evolve a symposium of wit \+ wisdom, but The material was not at hand - There was a deal of talk but the tide was, with few exceptions, shallow \+ slow - Mr. Weir led off \+ finished himself - The chilliness of the afternoon had led several to seek warmth from whiskey - so, when the dinner \+ wine cause they were soon fuddled - \+ some were more than that - before 10 o'clk Dolple made a disgusting spectacle by tumbling to the floor helplessly drunk. morals was far gone \+ Bristols legs got so tangled up that he could not stand \+ he was sent home in a cab. Altogether it was depressing to me — I am sure it was far below the level of academy dinners fifteen \+ twenty years ago — I left at 10.30 \+ walked all the way home - a little more than four miles — home at 11.45
