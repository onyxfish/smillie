## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 19, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - a regular weather breeder - cool, quiet, brilliant + cloudless. After bkf. Charl + I went to the hotel + there I spent more than an hour in getting telephonic communication with Prescott + Son, Keeseville - they told me that my order for lumber has been filled + delivered to J. & J. Rogers, Ausable Forks - Called up R. + he told me the lumber had been there two or three days. I saw Otis + arranged that he sh'd. send Jonnie + his team to the Forks This afternoon + bring the lumber over to-morrow. We met Mr. O'Farlym in at the hotel - Charl got to chinning with him + found that they had business interests in common - we all went to O's house - had his camera + took a shot at Giant + Roaring Brook Fall from the Roof of his piazza - we lay upon the shingles, basking in the sunshine talking of things. It was dinner time when we got home. At 2 o'clk Munor Holt came with team + wagon + Mary, Kate, Bessie + I went down the valley calling - first to Mrs. Platmanis - found her only at home in the midst of packing - she had many plans not set - Then on to the house where Rev. Malcolm Shaw + wife boarded - they had gone the day before - then back + stopped at Shutt[illegible] - picked Mr. S - up in the way - we made a short call there - surprised to find what beautiful woods + Rocks are on S's land immediately back of his house. The sun having gone off the valley while we were on our way back I found it so chilly that when within a mile of home I got out + walked the rest of the way. Ev'g. Mrs. + after they left I read aloud to the boys -
The boys were taken down the valley during the afternoon by young Hardenburgh.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 20, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day - wet + windy - the line storm probably - Rain commenced about nine o'clk - + grew heavier through the day - at night it was steady + very heavy. Ray put in a little time for house chores in the m't. Charl had made all his plans to drive over to Lake Placid, taking Mary, Kate + the girls - Of course the weather upset all that.
I put in a good days work painting - I made a sketch in all - a composition based upon a memory of the fine view of the Giant from the trail on the west side of the River. It has been on my mind since I first saw it two years ago as a fine subject for a large painting - I treat it with very little regard for exactness so far as the lines of the Giant rather like facts go - I have been impressed by the bigness of the mt. enhanced by cloud effects. It pleases me to hope that I have got something worth getting - A good part of the ev'g. I read aloud to the boys.

Broadner came + says that he will guarantee me a satisfactory flow of hot water for $60. I am to give him an answer sometime next Spring.
