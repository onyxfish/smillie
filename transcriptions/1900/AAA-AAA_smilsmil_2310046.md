## Left Page

### Thur. March 15, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temp. 8 Am. 97 3/5° — at 10 p.m — it stood the same. A quiet, persistent snowstorm all day continuing until after I went to bed — 6" or 8" of snow. Of course I was kept in all day. I had my bkfst. inbed \+ got down to dining room at 10.30 \+ spent the day there reading, writing. \+ pacing the floor. Ev'f in parlor until 10. Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 16, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Temp. at 8 A.m., almost 98° at 10 p.m. 97 3/5°. Early m'f. clouded but by mid-morning the sun shone brightly \+ everything was running water — after the 6 inches of dry snow yesterday the newspapers report two inches of sleet. Everything was covered with a coat of clear ice this m'f. I got up at 8 o'clk. \+ went down to my bkfst. Of course I did not dare to go outside, so I was a prisoner for another day — It was a sore trial to me \+ I find it weakening \+ depressing. I wrote a long letter to Jine in the morning. In the afternoon I had a nap \+ before dinner I had a refreshing visit from Chas. Collins. Dinner at 6.30. Miss Hannah, nurse teacher of Bessie's, took dinner with us. Ev'f. Mary, Kate \+ the girls went to the theatre \+ Ralph \+ I had a quiet time by ourselves.
