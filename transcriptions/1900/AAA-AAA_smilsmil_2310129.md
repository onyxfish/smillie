## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 30, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delightful day - fresh \+ breezy - altho' very warm in the sunshine. - I got through the night fairey well but had to jump out of bed two or three times with cramps in my feet \+ the calves of my legs - never before had so much trouble of that kind - got up weak \+ shaky \+ with remnants of headache but that condition passed off during the day \+ at night I felt quite like myself again. Webb worked all day finishing Piazza railing, 2d. story, \+ got to be quite proud of himself before night. - Maud worked on road spasmodically. I was not able to accomplish much - wrote part of a letter to Miller. - Afternoon, Gilbo came \+ brought with him his Port Henry boss - I did not catch the name. - He looked over my hot water outfit, \+ there talked, as it seemed to me, intelligently about what we be required under physical laws to accomplish what I have tried to get. I feel quite assured that Callenader may be a good plumber, but he knows very little about hot water - I shall have to take radical measures before I can hope for hot water on tap. Mr. \+ Mrs. Wm. Neilson called - Mrs. Alderson \+ Mrs. Coke - \+ the Misses Smith - Katherine \+ Bessie went off this m'f. with Mr. Howells \+ friends on a days tramp up Chappel Brook etc. Family spent ev'g. on North Porch.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 31, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dubious m'f. - clouds down on Giant - but they lifted before 10 \+ a delightful day followed. - I feel better \+ was able to do some work. Wells came \+ finished upper Piazza balustrade. - Otis Sew. was with the others working on road to-day. - Ray was sick \+ did not work - Adams did some painting in the afternoon. - I went to hotel before noon - found a party in O. Reed's Private office admiring a painting by Shurtliff, purchased at "Banker Sale" by one of the Hotel guests. - Afternoon Raelph \+ I did some exploring over the dodges' rack of Mrs. Reed's. - It was quite like old times, going thro' the woods without trails. - Mr. Felix Adler \+ Miss Goldman made a pleasant call at the house this m'f. Ev'g. with newspaper.
