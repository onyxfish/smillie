## Left Page

### Fri. March 9, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant — no fever so far as I took observation to-day but a sub normal temp. that could not get the Ther. above 97¾°. my head was very uncomfortable but I managed with much pain to write a letter to Jim — mainly given to a reply to his observation in his last letter that he thought it a time for him to decide as to which religious denomination he will join. It was a very severe labor on my part but I stuck to it until I got it finished. — I did not get up out of my bed during the day.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 10, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day out of doors — I passed a very uncomfortable night \+ had much discomfort all day — Sub normal until 9 p.m. when it touched normal. — Before 9 a.m. Mary went to Clark's telephone \+ called up D. Male who came at about 11 \+ tried to talk some comfort into me. — I got up at 10 a.m. \+ dressed \+ absent myself in Mary's lounge while my room was sunning \+ airing I kept up more or less during the day, very much depressed \+ miserable. \+to bed before 10
