## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 18, 1900

**Weather:** Dull  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day with light rain before 10 o'clk then it cleared mield — I had my bath \+ my 6klp. At 9 — Reading until 12 when Ralph \+ I went to the Manhattanville viaduct now in course of construction. we got home at 2 o'clk — just in time for dinner — At 4 I went to Ed. Cooke taking Panoramic photos. of Heene by Charl. There until after 5 — then I walked down to E. 17th St. to Emma Ireland's — the three sisters at home. I stayed to tea \+ had a very pleasant evef. until 8 — then home — Charl. was there — Geo. came in after 9 \+ stayed until 11 — then I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 19, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very mild day — a hazy — Part of the time clouded over — It was 10 o'clk when I got to studio. I took an old Academy b. on which I had painted many years ago. x , \+ rubbed in very roughly an effect — the same in theme as the one I painted on Friday last, this one is upright — that was not. This has water in the foreground, that has a road. I got it all in after a fashion by 12.30 — to lunch at 1 — \+ immediately after it I went to N.A. School — first day of Etching class — Sterue was there, but no one else — a very unpromising pen sketch had been left by a young man but he was not there — later, Miss Stoutenburgh, of W. 94th. came, bringing a good pen drawing — she may join the class — I left soon after three o'clk \+ walked down Cent. Park — Wt. to The Paulico — No. 79 — where I stopped \+ saw Saml. Coleman — He looks very old \+ haggard — Mrs. C — had gone to bed — She is failing rapidly. I was with C — for about half an hour — we talked of the literary work he is now engaged upon — he claims to have discovered certain geometrical laws \+ beauty, as I understood him, known to the ancients ages ago \+ afterwards lost. Then I walked down to 42nd St. — had hair cut \+ head shampoo'd — home to dinner at 6.30 — Every Read newspaper — Then wrote awhile.
