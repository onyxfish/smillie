## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 21, 1900

**Weather:** Delightfully clear, mild & quiet  **Temp:** [illegible]

It was nearly 10 o'clk. before I got through with my bath & down to my bkfst. It was about 11 when Ralph & I went out & sauntered up to the Park stopping by the way to see the new waiting room at the Gr. Central Station — then to the R. C. Cathedral & on to the Park where we wandered until 1.30 — on our way home saw the remains of six members of the 69th Regt. just brought home from Cuba, carried into the Cathedral with military honors where a service was performed — To dinner after 2 — late — after dinner went to Emma Ireland's — 331 E 17. St Marjorie Spring with her little 2 yr. old baby was there — & also — Ed W & Mary Cook & Mr. Valentines — I stayed until 6 p.m. — then walked home Tea at 6.30 — Well. wrote a note to Mrs. Sidney Smith in answer to hers of Oct 4. suggesting that her cousin, Grace Elliot Brennan would be a desirable person to take care of me & the boys should Mary & the others go abroad next summer as is (proposed.)

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 22, 1900

Katharine went to visit Mable Cooley "Rockwor".

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild — rather enervating. To studio about 10 & worked all the m'g. on Photo. gravure reproductions of Century & Van Dyke book plates — To lunch at 1.30 — after that went to R. R's in Canal St. & got pf. from the book plates — to Studio & tracked down the pf. at 5 went to D. Chas. Allen's office & the told me in detail (there wasn't much of it) the result of his examination of Jim's physical condition as he passed thro' town on his way to school. Want of nerve, fore something in digestive troubles — mal-nutrition & catarrhal trouble in the head seemed to be the sum of it all. He said he questioned the boy directly as to his habits & says he thinks there is nothing chargeable in that direction — While there, Charl came in to be examined, with reference to a pain in the region of the heart that troubles him — D. told him to cut down his allowance of cigars. Dinner at 6.30 — We & Charl came in for a while — later I arranged a lot of old letters, bills & papers that have been a pile of confusion on my desk.
