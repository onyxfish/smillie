## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 13, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day following a sombre mtf. I wrote a note to Lee + Lee + at noon went to Scott Brown + signed Petition sent to me by A. A., in the matter of taxes on 110 E. 38th St. — Rev. D. Balcolm Shaw + his wife called in the mtf. Gilbs came + put up eaves-trough. Ray came for a little while — sick — Webb did not come, but Adams came in the afternoon + painted for a while. Kate got so wild with the pain in her face that I got D. Hobbs for her — Then her pain passed — he says, neuralgia had availed of the opportunity + had himself examined — D. H. says no injury beyond a very severe bruise from which he will not entirely recover for some weeks yet. Afternoon, Ralph went to top of Moon Mark with Lawrence Adler + a young lady from Phila. — Friend of Whites. Jim + I went down the valley on our wheels + had a good ride — home about 5.30 — had a change of under clothes + rested until supper — Edith read aloud from Bullen's "Cruise of the Cachelot". Got a letter from [illegible] concerning Jim — he does not seem to share my fears + thinks he is suffering from "bilious dyspepsia" — well — reasons [illegible] he as well off at School as anywhere.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 14, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Tea at the Oppenheim's.

Very fine day, cool in the shade — warm in the sunshine. Webb + Adams at work all day on big studio window — the glass was all taken out for + reset, as it had not been "bedded" at first. The water came through all around each pane. The carpenter shed on north side of house was removed to general satisfaction, + the bench taken to laundry. Ray did not come. I spent most of mtf. with Webb + Adams. + did some loafing in the afternoon. Mrs. Oppenheim — Miss Vedan + Schilling called in the mtf. + invited Mary. The girls + me to take tea with them in the evtf. — Charl + Kate were not in condition to accept — we went over at [illegible] + had a very pleasant time. Lawrence Adler came in + played on the piano for us + two Goldmark sisters came in later in the evtf. Schilling walked home with us shortly after 10.
