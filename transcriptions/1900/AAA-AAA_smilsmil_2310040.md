## Left Page

### Thur. March 1, 1900

**Weather:** Wind & rain all day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Wind & rain all day — got up with a headache that stuck to me all day but in spite of it I managed to get in a good day's work. To studio's soon after 9 — \+ to work printing a number of proofs from the little etching of my own portrait — home to lunch at 1.30 back & worked a[illegible] from "Mad. Sq. Garden Tower" plate. I worked until 5 o'clk. & got some satisfactory [illegible] on india & on thin [illegible]. Then I cleaned up & got damp [illegible] between blotters & home at 6.15 — very tired & hungry & headachy — dinner & after it a short nap, after which I felt better — then I worked on my accts for awhile & to bed.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 2, 1900

**Weather:** clear, cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear, cold & windy — all the m'f at studio changing the steel damps [illegible] taken yesterday to dry blotters & cutting and wetting paper for more printing to-morrow — about 1 o'clk. went to Record Nat'l Bk then home to lunch —
About 3 o'clk. I went to Architectural League 84[illegible] & was there until nearly 5 o'clk — there to J.A. Ward's studio — Dielmann, Beckwith \+ Nicoll came afterwards Beckwith read letter from Carrère of C.&H — practically a reply to Ward's expressed wish that C.&H. would retire from the field as Architects of [illegible] C — explained very courteously in his letter that such retirement would mean a great loss to them professionally & financially — & that precedents that had been carried into Court would give them a claim on the [illegible] of $15,000 or $20,000. This made no impression on [illegible] & he insisted that he or C.&H. must withdraw — The other members of the com. were afraid of him & did not dare oppose him — I was too sick to take any part — Dielmann offered to try to patch up a compromise — but I know he must fail — & home at 6.30 — I was able to eat some dinner — then I wrote my regular letter to Jim & went to bed —
