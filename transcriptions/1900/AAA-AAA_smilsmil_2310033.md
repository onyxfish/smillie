## Left Page

### Thur. Feb. 15, 1900

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. To studio at 9.30 \+ spent the m't fussing over Mr. G. plate, much discouraged — I could not get such a print as I wanted — that puzzled \+ worried me — but, aside from that it was evident that the shaded side of the face is underbitten \+ the more I work over it the worse it is — I think I shall have to scrape it out \+ make a new plate — I went home to my lunch much depressed — just before I started Rathbun The Real Estate of five Brs. man came in, having heard, he said, that the N.A. was going to sell its new site \+ buy again further down town. He bored me for sometime but at last got away \+ I went to lunch — after that I made some change in my dress \+ went to Century where I wrote to Hon. Whitelaw Reid asking for an appointment to meet him — then rode down to Hanover Sq. \+ by Walls. Ferry crossed to Brooklyn \+ went to Mr. Augustus White's Recaption 158 Columbia Heights — a very pleasant affair — was cordially rec'd \+ spent half an hour or more chatting with various ones. Mr. \+ Miss Janeway came in just as I was leaving. I got home at 6.15 — dinner — Evely D. Putnam came in to see Kate who is quite sick (Kate Leonard, the cook) — a revival apparently of an old internal tumor trouble — I read awhile to Ralph who is under the weather again from cold.

## Right Page

### Fri. Feb. 16, 1900

**Weather:** cold, raw \+ overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

cold, raw \+ overcast. To studio at 9.30 \+ most of the m't was wasted in another attempt to make a pen sketch portrait of myself for reproduction for J. T. White \& Co's Biographical Enc'y. It was a foolish waste of time \+ I cut the sketch up when done — I was cut up too — everything that I have attempted for some time past has been failure — this is very depressing to me. Home to lunch at 1.30. Dr. Putnam gave Kate Leonard a careful examination this m't \+ he says that she is suffering from her old time trouble — a fibroid tumor — he says she must give up work in the end — will come soon — Her sister came about 2 o'clk. \+ took her away in a carriage — After lunch I went to Kimmel \+ Voigt's — Cause st. I met Dr. Gester there \+ we started Mr. Voigt on the printing of the Jacabi head — 9.5 I rode up town together — I went to studio intending to begin to scrape out a large part of the face of Mr. G's — instead, I went all over it with the graver adding more strength. Home \+ about 6.30 — dinner — very little near G. Ralph \+ I worked at pasting back Nos. of Century mag. into pfp. of one vol. each — then I wrote a letter to Jim \+ after 10 went to G. C. Station \+ posted it.
