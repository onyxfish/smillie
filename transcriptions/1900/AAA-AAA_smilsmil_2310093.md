## Left Page

### Tues. June 19, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another pleasantly cool day — after bkf. waited for Miller, the carpenter, who came about 9.30 — He was about an hour getting directions \+ measurements for boxes for packing things that are to go to Keene — it was then that I discovered that Ralph's bicycle had been stolen from the lower hall within two or three days. I went to 2d Natl \+ arranged matters there for the summer — \+ got a loan for $900 \+ arranged for another if it should be wanted later — Case the Cash. told me some peculiar stories about questionable transactions by Grannins of the N. A. — Then to Riker's to get some drugs — then home to lunch. After lunch I went to Dielmann's 57 W. 10th St. \+ told him what Case had told me about Grannins. Then to studio — arranging things there until 4.30 — Then home \+ I went with Bessie to Spalding's to see if I could get a bicycle bargain for Ralph — Home before 6 — Dinner at 6.30 — I had taken my wheel to Borrmann's in the mf. to be cleaned up — after B Ralph \+ I went there to look at wheels — we were there for an hour but reached no conclusion.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 20, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Katharine ret? from New Haven

clouded most of the day. but much warmer than yesterday. After bkf. to studio. There for sometime getting together things to take with me for the summer. Then to 23d St. \+ bought colors etc. Then to 2d Natl Bank. Then to Burkhart Bdway \+ 26th St. about Teleph. for Keene — Then home \+ cutet lunch time I worried over check book \+ found where the error of 50¢ was that showed in bal. that I got from the bank yesterday — After lunch feeling very heavy I gave up to it \+ slept for nearly two hours very solidly — Then I went to Borrmann's with Ralph \+ bot. a new "Monarch" bicycle for him for $23.— also a lot of photo. material — Then to studio \+ worked there until near 6 — Then home. — dinner — Eve. brought home some things from the studio — Then R. \+ I went to Charles' — the girls there the playing new music — Home soon after 9 — to bed early.
