## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 17, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Not a very pleasant day — too many cold looking clouds — not enough sunshine — but the day was not really cold — Cobb put in an appearance — I had little for him to do but managed to keep him busy nearly all day — He is not to come back until I send for him — I shall do so as soon as the lumber gets here from Prescott \+ Son — Ray came in the m't. but was not here in the afternoon — also Oven Beede law suit. I was busy about the house after I finished my long letter to Emma Ireland — much of the afternoon was given to clearing up studio — the floor was rendered with putty — \+ fixing burlap curtain for attic opening. Evel up. Reading Sunday Trib.

The nurses MacIntosh called in the m't.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 18, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To top of Indian Head —

Very cool, brilliant day — high wind — After bkft. it was decided to take some sort of an outing — at 10° Mary, Katharine, Bessie, Jim, Ralph \+ I started — leaving Katie in a bad humor \+ taking Jim full of evil spirits — owing in bath cases, to ills of the flesh \+ the impossibility of pleasing everybody — Lawrence Adler met us at the entrance to the Reserve — at 11.30 we had reached the trail to the top of Indian Head — Bessie, Ralph \+ I went on the trail — the others went to the end of the road at the lake — We had a sharp climb for a good part of the way — our about half an hour got to the top — the view is not at all an interesting one, looked at by a picture maker — nevertheless — review as it breaks suddenly on coming out of the woods is quite exciting — the lights \+ shadows over the great pyramidal mass of Calvin were very fine, \+ having one of Charl's cameras I took two or three snap shots — after resting awhile in the sunshine on the rock — we started back — getting to the road at 12.30 \+ home at 2.10 — a little after the lake party — I changed my clothes, rested awhile \+ had dinner about 3 — Mrs. Beede called in the afternoon to see if we give him the care of the house during the winter — Ray was on hand all day — so was Adams — Tea at 6.30. At 8 — shut up house — all hands went to the hotel where a little impromptu play was given in the Casino — "The Giant Trail" \+ Roaring Brook farce. To celebrate the coming birth days of Mrs. Janeway Sr. Mr. Loring \+ Mrs. Hughes — At the last moment it was discovered that this was Bessie Throop's birthday — There was no plot \+ very little play — the words of two or three songs had been written by Miss Anna Howell to set to music by Miss Hutcheson — there were many old jokes \+ allusions to persons \+ things about the Hotel — the most amusing part was a song sung by Miss Alderson at Mr. Howell about mushrooms — the two were very funny —
