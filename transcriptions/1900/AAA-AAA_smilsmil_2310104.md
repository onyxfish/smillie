## Left Page

### Wed. July 11, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during the night — heavy showers thro' the w'f. — Then some bright, clearing weather — hot sunshine \+ strong southerly breeze — Heavy thunderstorm at 8 P.m. rain continuing well into the night. Went to house about 9.30 — found Mr. Ducharme \+ two other "ladies" cleaning windows — had got all the bed room windows done — very slop[illegible] — so I started them again at the beginning \+ showed them just what I wanted them to do — much to the advantage of the windows. The large plate of glass in north window — main room — was cleaned to my disqust I find that it is a second hand plate all scratched up, as though it had been rubbed over with salpalis. Buck \+ Wells at work in kitchen \+ pantry — Adams \+ his bro. painting window blinds — man of all work cleaning out main room — Ralph \+ I completed the unpacking of the last Wanamaker goods — a large case of blankets etc. \+ the box ahead of toilette sets. To-morrow I shall begin ticking off the items as I find them on W's bill. Again, as the day waned I got horribly tired \+ got to the hotel at 6.30 quite used up — I got down to dinner at 7.15 — ate little \+ then went to my room \+ was on my bed until about 10 — then read newspaper until 11 \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 12, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy day — showers in the early m'f — cool, cloudy w'f. I spent much of the day at the house — 2 women washing windows and crockery — 2 carpenters, finished kitchen and butler's pantry — \+ started on deck to dining room door — I wrote to Mary \+ Katharine \+ sent a short note to Charl — After lunch I had a nap on one of the beds in the house \+ was wakened by the arrival of W. \+ Mr. Maghee. Mr. Stardenburg \+ three children. I was tired at dinner time but not so tired as yesterday. We'f. walked on piazza \+ then sat in front of a pleasant fire \+ chatted with a couple of ladies until near 10.
