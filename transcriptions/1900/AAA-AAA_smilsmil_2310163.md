## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 6, 1900

Election day.
Katharine & Elizabeth Ret. from P\. k\.

**Weather:** Beautiful day \- Brilliant & Refreshingly cool.  **Temp:**

To studio before 10 \- on my way went to polling booth. 37 S. & Park Ave. finding the way clear I deposited my vote for the Republican ticket \- Then to studio & to work on Mad. Sq. Garden Tower by moonlight \- started two or three years ago \- after working all day on it and painting over a large part of it I came to the conclusion that I did not know enough about moon light to attempt such a work \- I decided to drop it \- Home to lunch \- Charl lunched with us \- walked for an hour, from 4.30 to 5.10 \- dinner \- About 9. o'clk. Charl came & half an hour later we started out \- Ch. going off with Katharine & Bessie \- Ralph & I going together \- we went to Mad. Sq. & were there until 11.30 there was an immense crowd \- nearly one third being women \- It was a pandemonium of noise \- The majority having fish horns, Rattles or some other means of making a noise \- it was senseless & hideous \- The Journal had a great canvas screen stretched in front of the Dewey Arch & had erected a structure for lanterns & electric lights from which was thrown on the screen election Returns as they came in \- between times cinematograph pictures etc were shown to amuse the crowds across 23. S. The Times had a screen & in another place there was a third one \- The crowd was perfectly orderly & seemed equally divided between parties \- The women being most offensive \- It was soon evident that McKinley was getting all that had been claimed by his party \[illegible\] \- At 11.30 R \- & I walked up Broadway to Herald Sq. 34 S: a great crowd filling street & sidewalks all the way \- & a roar of noise \- More Returns and pictures on Herald Bld. 35th St. & a dense enthusiastic crowd \- we got home at midnight pretty thoroughly tired out.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 7, 1900

**Weather:** Fine Day \- Cool & rather breezy \- sudden showers came up soon after 10 P. m. \- & after midnight, violent thunder storms with hail.  **Temp:**

I got up with a headache, of course, & very lame in neck & shoulders. Although unfit for work I went to studio & did some work there writing up my accts \- At 12 o'clk. I went to Broakes Bro. & there to 2. Nat. Bank where I made a loan of $400. to pay bill of D. A. Adams painter of Elizabethtown for work on Keene house \- Then home to lunch. The afternoon was spent in bed where I had a good sleep \- During the evening my head ached severely but I was able to do some writing on accts. & got to bed soon after 10.
