## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 15, 1900

To Giant's Washbowl.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day, but with indications of change of weather. Webb \+ Adams worked about big north window but the improvements were not entirely completed \- they both worked on other things \- Webb has got to the end of what I have for him to do now \- I wrote notes to Miller \+ to Prescott, hurrying them up \- went to hotel after 11 \- Lawrence Adler came to the house about 1½ \+ Ralph went with him for a days climb on the Gothics \- A's father \+ the Mr. \+ Mrs. Weston went on the same week \- At 2.40 I started with Katharine, Bessie \+ Jim on a tramp up Giant to the Washbowl \- I have not been to the Washbowl before this. The climb thro the woods was extremely interesting to me \- we stopped for few minutes at the top of Roaring Brook falls \+ I took a snap shot \- I carried a camera \+ Charlie. It was about 4.30 when we got to the washbowl \+ I found it suggestively picturesque. Its area I judged to be about 20 acres. The water was dark \+ mirror-like \+ the edges for some distance from shore covered with lily pads \- on the north-east shore some Rock Cliffs rose a couple of hundred feet or more above the water \- The forest growth about the pond was silver birch \- maple \+ spruce \- on the westerly side were the Remains of a camp \- I tried to get some photos but think I failed as the day was too far spent \- A short distance from the pond on the trail south we came upon a superb Cliff from which we looked down upon Chappell Pond, feet below us \- it was then a little after 5 \+ the view was very fine \- we then continued on the trail \+ after a very steep descent we reached the Road at 5.30 \- we got home at 6.30 \- all of the party in very good condition after our say mile tramp \- as we came up to the house Ralph came in from his 17 m. tramp, as spry as a cricket \+ delighted with his days experience \- I changed my underclothes \+ after a rest of 15 or 20 m. I had my supper \- after I read aloud to Ralph from "Cruise of the Cachalot." \+ to bed before 10 \-

Between 11 \+ 12 \- I was routed out by severe cramps in the muscles of my thighs \- tailors muscles \- I had never before had anything of the kind \+ for a few minutes the pain was very great \- after chafing with alcohol it passed away \+ did not return.
