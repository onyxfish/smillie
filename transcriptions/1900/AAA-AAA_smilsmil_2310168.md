## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 16, 1900

**Weather:** Bright & quite wintry  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & quite wintry. when I got to studio I didn't feel like going on with the painting of "An Adirondack Valley" so I took an old Academy board x upon which I had painted years ago & without drawing in a composition I painted in very roughly, a sketch — a Recollection of an eve'g view of the Giant as we set from an afternoon walk last summer. I also gave some time to Keppel's scissors & got the legend drawn thro' the ground with a sharp pen, all ready to bite — Home before 5 — wrote a letter to Jim before dinner. Eve'g Ralph & I went to Murray Hill Theatre & saw Pinero's "The Princess & The Butterfly" — not much of a play — & not much acting — After 11 when we got home — to bed at once.

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 17, 1900

**Weather:** Overcast all day & milder  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast all day & milder — to studio & spent the day painting on "An Adirondack Valley" — find that I'm in deep water — when I got over the sky I felt encouraged — now, when I try to paint the mts. in tune with the sky I cant do it — spent the day in varying stages of disheartenment & got half way over the mts — they should have been all slapped in at once — I hate myself for being so timid & pattering. I worked for some time on Keppel's scissors — got them into the acid & had various mishaps & troubles but eventually they came out fairly — After 5 took them down to K — who expressed himself as much pleased. He presented me with a proof of Whistler's etching — A dam'Eve & Inm. Tharnes. Home to dinner late — Eve'g had a call from a funny little English woman — a unique sort of beggar who begs without begging — she said her name was Martin & she was trying to find T.A. Richards. Her story didn't hang together & she did nt actually ask for any thing — nevertheless I gave her half a dollar, because I felt sure she had no home while I did have one.
