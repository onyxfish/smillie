## Left Page

### Tues. April 24, 1900

**Weather:** Very mild & bright, altho' at times there were showery looking clouds about. Cool at night with a light dash of rain.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio about 9.30 + spent the m'g writing letters + some time reading part of Wray's M. S. "Etching in the U. S." + writing notes for his benefit. Home at 1.30 — lunch — then to N. A. school by appointment & met five young men & Maurice Sterne — the five young men are to be the Etching class & Sterne is to be my assistant. I went over preliminary ground with them & laid a etching ground on a zinc plate — left them at 4.30 & went to Bolton Jones — 42° S. Chairman of School Com. To arrange with him some details about Etching class. Spent half an hour or more with Frank Bolton — then home — Dinner at 6.30 — then to Walter Cook's — found them yet at dinner table — Mrs. Stomque just arr. from Spain — I went to ask W. & Louise if they thought the omission of card inviting me to the house & the card for the train in the Hastings-Benedict wedding next monday was accident or design — it seemed strange that a card for the church only should have been sent — it would be a long journey just for the privilege of going to the church, paying my way at that — but after talking it over they thought it was design — so, I cross that off my list. Rec'd a very short letter from Jim — a long one from him for Kate — Walter Pierce & Minnie came in the even'g — I wrote to Kate + sent Jim's letter to her & took it to G. C. Station & mailed it. Gel came in the even'g.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 25, 1900

**Weather:** Brilliant, fresh day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

All the m'g at studio until near 12 writing — most of the time finishing the reading of H. R. Wray's M. S. "Etching in the U. S." & writing notes thereon — then I went to Linart & almends & got Acid, Tray, etc. for Etching class. Then to Exp. Office & sent M. S. to Wray — then home at 1 — lunch & to studio at 2, expecting D. Gesler — while waiting for him I worked on Mrs. G. head — can always find something to do there. — he came at 4 & was in excellent humor over the pfo. that I showed him — expressing himself as pleased and satisfied — nevertheless he had some little suggestions to make — one, affecting the eyes, I acted upon at once — making the pupils larger — with questionable advantage, I think. A little before 6 I went to Jordans in 33° St. & we walked up 5° Ave. together — he promised to meet me at N. A. school on Friday at 4 p.m. Dinner at 6.30 — Sweet. Edgar Turnle came to the displeasure of the Girls (Wally Carnuell was here last night.) Ralph & I retreated from the parlor — in my Room I Read to him from Popular Science about the coming eclipse of the sun — to bed early.
