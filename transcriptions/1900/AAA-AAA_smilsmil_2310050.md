## Left Page

### Fri. March 23, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded for a time in the m'f - a few drops of rain but the day cleared to be very pleasant. To studio \+ worked on dilpincott head until lunch time then home - After lunch, feeling heavy I had a nap on dining room sofa - so it was soon after 4 when I got to studio - I wrote a long letter to Jim \+ worked a little while on plate - then went to P.O. station \+ mailed letter - home dinner at 6.30 - Eveth. with Kate and Katharine Thrush I went to Am. Theatre - 42d. St. \+ 8th Ave. - \+ we saw \+ heard "Patience". I was amused \+ pained - I was pained because I could not be blind to the fact that, as presented, Patience had deteriorated from the refined fun, The comic gravity \+ Restraint of Gilbert's personal direction to the old time D'oyly Carte Companies, into farce \+ rather coarse play - no one of the Co. was particularly good - all were fairly bad. Bimthonie (Frank mordeau) mimicked Henry Irving in many ways very comically \+ most of the troupe enjoyed a good deal of a romp on the stage - they evidently enjoyed it quite as much as the audience did - Home at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 24, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - brilliant, cool \+ breezy - After bkfs. I went to Am. Theatre to get seats for Pinafore \+ the Mikado but they would not be sold until next week - then to studio and spent remainder of m'f. working on dil-pincatt fancy head. home at 1.30 - lunch. After 3 went to my room \+ had a nap in preparation for an eve'f at the Century - on coming down stairs at 5.50 I found a postal card from Nicoll advising me not to forget meeting of Ways \+ Means Com- at Beckwith's Studio this afternoon - I have had no notice of this important meeting but I knew that I was intended to have such a meeting at some time - I am much vexed at thus losing it - Dinner at 6.30 - along \+ very interesting letter &c. from Charl - Hotel Porciana - on the Florida coast - At 8 o'clk I went to Century Club \+ heard a lecture by Prof. Prof. W. Putnam on Ruins of City of Copan - Honduras. It was not much of a lecture but was a continuous string of pictures on screen of ruins \+ strange sculptures of the cities of that unknown people. The pictures were intensely interesting \+ the remarks that attended them were to some degree explanatory - After the lecture I met Mr. Jennings the lawyer, \+ had a little chat with him about Central Park site for h.a. Pro \+ con - had a chat with Frank Stocklen \+ Chad Collins - then a long talk with Dielman about the meeting of Ways \+ Means Com- at Beckwith's this p.m. Ward was there \+ presented the plan of an Artists' gate to Central Park - in other words, a Central Park site for such a p.o. It was well rec'd - he said nothing of his desire to have a new competition - I am more than ever vexed at losing the meeting thro' the carelessness of someone who did not send me a notice at the proper time - Home at 1 o'clk. A.m.
