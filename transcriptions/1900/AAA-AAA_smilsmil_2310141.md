## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 23, 1900

Still disagreeable weather — wind and light showers. I did not get up until 8 o'clk — my rub down \+ bath — \+ bkfst. after 9. I wrote a note to Mr. Kent at Montrose — some fussing about — a long walk on the piazza \+ dinner at 2 — worked on my studio attic curtain — The Maeghes — Hardenburghs — Mr. Loring \+ W. Howell called, all together, after afternoon service — tea at 6.30 — Evetf. at 8 I went to the hotel \+ found nearly all of my friends who are to leave to-morrow. Sitting in a circle about a big fire — I joined them — presently Miss Mitcheson played for us — It was very delightful — I enjoyed especially a Barcarole by Rubenstein — also a Prelude by Griegg — Home at 9.30

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 24, 1900

Charl, Kate, Bessie \+ Jim left us.

Cleared at last — a fine day — Bkfst. at 8 — Holt's wagon came at that time \+ the trunks, boxes \+ bicycles were piled upon it — It started before 9 — Jim riding beside the driver. a pretty little light wagon took the others at 9 o'clk — they are to go direct to Westport \+ take 1.26 o'clk. train for N.Y. — its last run for the season — Kate is to stop at Po'K. — Bessie \+ Jim are to stop at Charl's house — Jim is to go to Pomfret in Ctd. Oct. 3d. Buck \+ Webb reported for work this mng. They set up their bench on north piazza \+ spent the day getting the lumber finished with plane \+ sand paper. — Cob Adams came in the afternoon \+ oiled the boards as they were polished — Ray graded the road back — Dr. Janeway made a call about 10 o'clk — his family leaves this afternoon — I went to the studio before noon. Dinner at 1 o'clk. I found various things to do — among others, went to see Mr. Maeghes's hired man (with his assist'd) Alfred Winch, \+ arranged to have him look after the house during the winter — Tea at 6.30 \+ Evetf. Reading newspaper.
