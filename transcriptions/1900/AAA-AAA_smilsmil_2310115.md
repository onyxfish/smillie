## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 2, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Peculiar weather - The day opened bright, breezy & cool - about the middle of the m'f a thunderstorm came up with little premonition & light showers followed thro' the day - clouded eve'f. Moody sent a small boy to do his chores, with a message that his cut hand had commenced bleeding again & he had gone home - about 12 m. from here - The Adams bro's worked during the day, painting & staining - Otis hauled stone for under. pinning for cold room - I got the boards for studio attic up but not quite complete. Dinner after 1 - About 2 I went to hotel with letters - Mr. & Mrs. Louns made a call on us - The day was so cool that we had a small fire in main room - Eve'f - again fire - we read until about 9 o'clk - Tobed at 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

2d. visit of Mollie A - & miss Daires.

Very cool, cloudy day - I went to hotel before b'kf't for bread & butter. In spite of the cold I had a comfortable time on getting up - I had a little wood fire which soon warmed the room & the water ran tepid - I wrote a letter to Blaney of Ipswich - Lumber for cold room arr'd from mill. Ducharme & another drilled big rock at entrance of new road & blasted it - Adams & bro. painted on outside of house - Moody came & reported that his cut finger was giving him much trouble - I worked on plans for cold room - Afternoon Mollie \+ miss Daires came over the grant - They dined at Mr. White's & spent the night with us. Otis came after tea - had one of his boys with him - & together we looked over the new road & he estimated that it would cost $50 to put it in passable condition - I told him to go ahead & he is to start at it to-morrow. Eve'f I worked for a time on cold room plans.
