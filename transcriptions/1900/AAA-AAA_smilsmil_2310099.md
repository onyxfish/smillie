## Left Page

### Sun. July 1, 1900

**Weather:** Clouded + cold m.f. gradually clearing + mitigating in temp.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded + cold m.f. gradually clearing + mitigating in temp. until the afternoon when it was delightful — We got up after our 11 hour nap rested + with but little trace of yesterdays weariness. We had a late breakfast + after a wait we went to the house + until after 7 o'clk we worked hard — opening boxes + bales + unpacking furniture — Then to hotel — dressed + had dinner — After that sat by fire in parlor + read to Ralph. Mr. Maghee came in + I had a chat with him. Later, R. + I went to Mr. M's + saw Mr. Maghee — back to tea at 6.30. Evg. loafed about until 9.30 + then to bed feeling uncomfortable.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 2, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day — After bkf. Two carpenters reported from branch for work — I took them to the house + they then told me that they would have to get their tool chests + would not be ready to begin work until after 1 o'clk — I then went on a hunt for us to turn on the water from the Reserve — It began to run about noon — the ball-cock in the attic tank does not shut off close + many washer joints leak. Mr. Beede promises me a cleaning woman to-morrow + Orlando promises me two men to unpack furniture + put it in place. Lunch at 1 — after that wrote a letter to Mary — At 3 again went to the house + found Adams — set him to work putting a priming coat on the turned piazza posts. I wandered about — looking after the carpenters + the water works. It was after 6 when we ret. to the hotel + after 7 when we got our dinner — After 8 R. + I went to our room with the Sunday Tribune + account of the conflagration of N. German Lloyd's Hoboken piers + boats — To bed early.
