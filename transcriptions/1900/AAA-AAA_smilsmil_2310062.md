## Left Page

### Wed. April 18, 1900

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast — Rain commencing in the afternoon \+ falling heavily at times thro' the night — After 6\[illegible\] went to All. Theatre \+ changed my seats from Sat. p.m. to the eve'f. It was near 11 before I got to studio between that time \+ lunch at 1 I found something more to do on Mr. G. head —
Back to studio at 2 \+ soon Dr. Gerster came — he looked at my pf. taken yesterday \+ made a couple \+ slight suggestions \+ then to my great relief expressed himself satisfied — He left at about 3 — Maurice Stein came soon after — so anxious to have me start the etching class at the N.A. that J. promised to write to Grannis asking him to post a notice on School bulletin that there would be a meeting of the class next Tues. p.m. Then I made the changes on Mr. G. plate — wet down paper for to-morr \+ home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — at 8 went to 1. 102d St. "The Melba" \+ saw Mr. Thos. B. Adams — I took 21 photos of the Montrose house \+ grounds \+ spent an hour \+ a half explaining them and plans that J. drew — A. was not there. Mr. A. seemed interested \+ pleased but did not in any way commit herself — That was not expected — home at 11.40.

## Right Page

### Thur. April 19, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Walter Throop dined with us —

Grey m'f. but gradually cleared until it was bright \+ warm. I got up with a mean headache — It clung to me all day \+ floored me at night. To studio at 9.30 \+ to work taking pf. from Mr. G. — It was some time before I could compound an ink that give the results I wished — Then I took four pf. — retinted hub. Jap. \+ two pf. on some of the heavy, tinted "Scotch" paper that I got from Goulding while I was in London — using "Toronoco" Jap. as I would India — I got through before — home to lunch at 1.10 — at 2 late, Rebekah I went to the Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw "The Dancing Girl" a rather slow representation of some fast people — out at 5 \+ went to studio \+ cleaned up — home — at dinner we had Walter S. Throop, a cousin of Katherine \+ Bessie — here on a flying visit from Palmyra — I had never met him before — \+ The girls had not seen him for many years — He made a favorable impression — after dinner I gave up to my headache \+ went to bed — I wanted very much to go to the Authors' Club — it being Artists' night \+ I having an invitation —
