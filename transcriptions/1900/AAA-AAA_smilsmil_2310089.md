## Left Page

### Mon. June 11, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Day quite fresh + pleasant in the morning growing rapidly warmer - very close evg - light rain - thunderstorms not far away. After 6kp. went to Alexander's, changed pair of shoes - then to Vantine's + looked over some of his cheap Jap. jute rugs. Then to Arnold + Constable's + bought summer underwear - then to Flint's, in 23? S.T. + looked at furniture - + home at 12.30. Lunch at 1 - Afternoon, went to Wanamaker's + made selections of fire irons - lamps, crockery etc. - Home at 5 - Just before dinner Emma Ireland made her P.P.C. care - goes to Easthampton in a day or two - Dinner at 6.30 - I felt too warm to do anything it too tired - so I skimmed over "The mighty Atom" by Marie Corelli - a dreadful book - I have not read anything by her carefully, but to my superficial skimming she seems nothing more than a dabbler in the profound with sensational manners. The story of me atom is painfully absellent to me.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 12, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - Mary + Katharine went out in a cab for an airing in the Park. + Kate went to P- the 10.30 train - I went to Heywoods in Canal S.t. + bought a lot of the furniture that I selected the other day - then to Kimmel + Voigts - then to Wauamakers. Home to lunch - again to W's + spent the afternoon there making good progress - Home at 6 - Rested until dinner at 6.45 - most of the evg arranging schedules for more purchases to-morrow.
