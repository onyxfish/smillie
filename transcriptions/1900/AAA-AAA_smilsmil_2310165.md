## Left Page

### Sat. Nov. 10, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. & Mrs. J. H. Maghee dined with us.

A much pleasanter day than yesterday - less windy & not so cold - To studio & painted all the mfg. on "Wet" - Showers. making little progress. Home to lunch at 1 - after that Raeoph & I went to the Met. Museum of Nat. His. & heard Mr. Chapwains opening lecture on birds - It was in the new hall of the museum opened a few days ago - The lecture was not particularly interesting to me. We spent an hour in the galleries after the lecture - then home. - Then I dressed & at 7 o'clk. Mr. & Mrs. Maghee came, but Mrs. Hardenburgh did not as she was unwell. We had a good dinner & I have reason to believe that Mr. & Mrs. M. had a fairly good time They left soon after 10 but I sat & read until near midnight.

## Right Page

### Sun. Nov. 11, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

unpleasant weather - raw & threatening - a little rain about noon. Up early & had my scrub & bath - Help. about 9 & soon after I went to N.A. of D. to meet school com. & teachers & pass upon merit of drawings - Beckwith & Clinedinist of com. & Ward, Maynard & Jones were there - I made arrangements for the starting of Etching class & prepared a circular to be sent out - met Mr. Sus, the new clerk of M.A. for first time. Home at 1 o'clk. dinner at 2 - most of the afternoon was spent sleeping - Supper at 6.30 - Wet & Mary & the girls went out - from 8 to 9.30 Raeoph & I were at Walter Cooks where we saw W. & Louise - Ed. (W's son) & Sallie - Home before 10 - [illegible — ~4 words]
