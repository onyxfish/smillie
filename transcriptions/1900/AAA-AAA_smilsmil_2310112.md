## Left Page

### Fri. July 27, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - cool \+ exhilerating - up before 7 - bkft. at 8 \+ then followed a day of constant work \+ no one worry - I made phog. of Ralph's bicycle chain \+ of my watch \+ went to hotel after 11 \+ mailed them to N.Y. - then to Mrs. White's \+ saw her daughter who showed me her cold Storage room, adjoining the ice house - then to Mr. Maghie's \+ he showed me his cold room \+ gave me much valuable information - then home to dinner at 1 - After dinner, Adams \+ his bro. started the putting down of duck, with white lead, on second story deck - I was with them, directing, much of the time. - Vera Beede came \+ ploughed on the line of the road to the kitchen. - Mr. Miller the Int. man from Lake Saranac called. - The grate \+ firebrick for the range came \+ Ray \+ I tried to put it in place without success - at 5 I went to hotel \+ had a 'phone conversation with Callenain who explained how the grate could be put in - also Talked with Vera Beede about the plumber sent up today - is not satisfactory - home to tea at 6. We all had a fine in main Room - Read newspaper in my own Room \+ to bed at about 10.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 28, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another glorious day. Up about 6.30 - bkft. at 8 - Had Buck \+ Webb at odd jobs \+ Adams \+ his bro. putting down duck on upper piazza at 10.30 Mary, Kate, Bessie, Jim, Ralph \+ I started out for a walk - went down Ghost's walk \+ at the bridge beyond the spring we parted. Bessie, Ralph \+ I went on to Giant's Trail \+ up to top of Roaring Brook Fall. - The others ret. home by way of Nielson's house. We had a pleasant little climb to the top of the fall \+ sat for awhile on the rock at the edge enjoying the warmth of the sun \+ the coolness of the breeze - on the way home I left them \+ went to hotel for mail - home at 12.30 - found Mrs. Nahrman (Sam. Colman's niece) there - had a pleasant chatty call from her. She has a cottage about 3 miles down the valley. Dinner at one. I had a nap - after that I had Ray Mossey go down the hill with me \+ I laid out a new road to pass over Nielson's corner as he has agreed. Tea at 6.30 - Evg. I got up the two bracket lamps in main Room \+ lighted them - the effect was very good - To my Room at 9.30
