## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 20, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- got to studio before 10 \+ much of the m'fg was absent in getting every thing ready to put up Tackle \+ face \+ run my Academy picture out of the long slit door at the west end of studio. Got painting securely nailed into frame, the frame off its legs \+ the sling around the frame with rugs for protection. Home to lunch at 1.30 \+ back \- Silleck came for picture before 3 o'clk. Every thing worked nicely I went on the roof \- hauled the block up \+ hooked it on the crane \+ then Silleck worked the fall from the pavement \- the picture was swung out \+ behaved beautifully landing upon the pavement without any "sickening thud". Jennings came along \+ helped me in with the Tackle. Then a Mr. S. Cade an engraver came \+ spent an hour \+ more questioning me about rebiting grounds etc. and showing me photos of his work \+ his staying power was great but he got away at last. Then I got my easles around into place \+ my new 33×44 canvas up \+ squared it off \- \+ my sketch painted at Keene last summer I based upon the Giant \+ squared that off \- \+ blocked out the composition on the canvas with charcoal. About 5 went to Clausen's \- then to Gines \+ Freund. 3d \+ 8'. S. \+ bought Nitric Acid for Etching class \+ New home \- dinner at 6.30 \- Jim dined at Ed's cook I read \+ curole \-

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 21, 1900

**Weather:** Cloudy \+ Raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ Raw. To studio before 10 \+ began work on 33×44. I haven't christened it yet. I made the sketch for it last summer, based on views of the Giant from high points up the gorge of the Ausable. I may call it "The River's Source" \- "Cradle of the River" or some other name attempt to add to the effect of the picture. I got out a lot of my old Adirondack studies \+ got quite into the spirit \- Then to work with charcoal. Home to lunch after a hurried visit to Clausen's. Back to studio at 2.30 \+ worked until near 5 \- when I could no longer see. Took my old overcoat to Marks to be repaired \+ then, as a snowflurry was on I went home. Dinner at usual time \- Eveft with newspaper \+ later I read aloud for awhile to the boys.
