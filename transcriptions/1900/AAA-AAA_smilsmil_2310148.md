## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 7, 1900

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy day - a low scud flying from the south \+ keeping the tops of the mts. covered most of the time - Some remarkably fine atmospheric effects - veiled sunshine - veiled color \+ mystery - After b'f'st. I went to the house \+ completed packing - then back to Mr. B's - got key to new house of Mr. White's just within Reserve - we went thro' it - I prefer my own - then R. \+ I crossed the bridge by the house \+ went up a wood road thro' some fine woods coming out upon a clearing that belongs to Mr. Magee - a very fine view of Grant \+ up Chapal gore - We got back to Mr. B's about 1 - near 2. when we got dinner - After dinner Mr. Ben Pond drove down the valley to see if he could get any tidings of my box of hardware - R. went with him - no tidings - I went to the house \+ dressed myself in linen shirt - starched collar \+ things to match - Then to Mrs Hardenbergh's then to Mr. B's - \+ walked in Reserve until near suppertime - Ralph invited to take tea with Katherine Hardenbergh - Every. at 7.30 I went for him - went into the Magee cottage \+ visited for awhile - bid them good bye - then to Mrs. H's - \+ bid her good bye \+ took Ralph away with me - a fine breezy walk to the Brown's - full moon \+ lots of clouds.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 8, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up at about 6 o'clk - rain falling steadily - b'f'st. at 7 - Soon after 8 young Holt came with his team \+ we went to the house, Scott Brown going with us to help handle the trunks - we got started for Westport at 8.45 \+ had an uneventful ride, getting to the station at 1° to find that the train had gone at 12.9 - a new arrangement of time table that had gone into effect this mf. The next train south left at 11 o'clk. P.M. getting into Albany at 3.° A.M. I decided to take it \+ went with a man to a very mean \+ dirty hotel \+ had a poor apology for a dinner at about 2 o'clk - The vexation developed a headache that added much to my discomfort. We ret. to The Westport, a house near the station, \+ took a room where we tried to get a nap - Ralph \+ I - It was dismal work - at last at 6 o'clk we had a poor supper - walked piazza for a long time \+ then again tried to get a little sleep - at 10.30 we went to station - the train came on time \+ we got into our bed as soon as possible - I was warm \+ as comfortable as I could be with a headache - I think that I slept some - at 2.20 the porter roused us and we left the sleeper \+ went forward into a day coach - This was at Troy where the sleeper went across the river on its way to N.Y. We got to Albany at 3.10 \+ went at once to The Kenmore.
