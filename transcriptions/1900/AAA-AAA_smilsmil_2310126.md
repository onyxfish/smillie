## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 24, 1900

Molly Adams & her Party surprised us-

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded mf., Rain commencing soon after 1 o'clk - It fell very heavily for an hour or more at night some stars were out. Buck & Webb finished their work on the cold Room about noon - commenced to hang blinds on Annex & found that the casings had not been made for blinds - stops or extra casings will have to be nailed on the outside. Adams primed cold Room & painted dark Room black. Ray came with his saw but the rain drove him from his work. - The Otis Bros. worked on Road during the mf. - I wrote several notes & went to Hotel at about noon. Dinner at 1 - much of the afternoon was with Buck & Webb trying to keep them in jobs - at 5 o'clk. I lay down with a headache - at to many came & told me that Mollie Adams, Miss Davies & two others had just presented themselves, drenched & muddy - having come over the Giant Mts. all the pouring rain - They were wholly unexpected & of course found us entirely unprepared. - I thought it inconsiderate & was vexed & told many that they must be content to take what they could get. - They went to the bath Rooms & took baths & we took our supper - they came in just as we finished & formed a "Second Table". The two new comers were introduced as Mrs. Van Horn Miss Hewitt. Later Mollie & Miss D. went to the Hotel to see about a guide - could not get one before Sunday - Returned & invited themselves to stay over until then - They returned to Hotel & before they came back I went to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 25, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very oppressive day - Sunny, hot, muggy & still - a great incursion of mosquitoes added to the general discomfort, Gibbs finished plumbing work on Cold Room & the Rock was filled with ice - Temp. in inner Room fell to 52°. when it was 83°. outside. Buck & Webb fussed on little jobs. Long seats were put on the balustrade at N.E. cor. of Piazza. - Adams painted about most of the day - the Otis men worked on Road part of day - Ray sawed up into fire wood the trees that he has cut down on the west slope of hill to make room for the growing white birches, beech & maple. I set Palette & tried to paint a sky study - a very dismal failure. - Mollie Adams' party spent most of the day in the Reserve woods & did not dine with us. Kate Leonard received her mind to Mr. Van Horn, the member of the party who went to the Kitchen several times during the day for tea. - I went to P.O. before 3 & on my way home stopped at the Donius cottage & called on Mr. & Mrs. I. - About 6 p.m. Mr. & Mrs. Weston made a pleasant call. - Evg. - 12 crowded about the supper table & there was good enough to go around. Rather later, a pleasant breeze from the South came up & the Ther. fell to 72°. By Chinese lantern light the North Piazza was very pleasant (barring mosquitoes) & there we all spent the evey.

I wrote & mailed a letter to Dielman answering his questions concerning Reutz' bill as well as I could.
