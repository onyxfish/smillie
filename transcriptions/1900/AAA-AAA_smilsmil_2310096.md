## Left Page

### Mon. June 25, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Montrose. A very hot day tempered by a strong breeze. After bkfst. I had some errands that I attended to then went to bank + drew another $50 for Petty cash. + home about 10.30 — There was nothing to do then but to sit about + wait until 12 when Ralph + I started for Christophers' ferry took the 1 o'clk. 2.45p. + without incident got to Montrose at time at 7.45 There was a Dining Room car on the train + at 3 o'clk. we had a fairly good dinner costing less than $1.00 apiece. We went at once to Chas. Sayre's — had a light supper — spent the eveg. with Misses Mary Sayre, Cynthia Richards + Mrs. Jessie Edgar. To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 26, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day with a weak attempt at a thunderstorm in the afternoon. Before bkfp. at 8 o'clk. we walked up thro' our lane + had a look at the house. After bkfp. again to our place + went over all the grounds with Speuse — There is much that makes me wish that I could stay here + direct improvements sadly needed. Later I saw Mr. Kent + daughter + the four lady guests. Dinner at Sayre's at 1 o'clk. Most of the afternoon again spent at the house getting together some things that I shall send to Keene. By invitation R. + J. Took supper with the Kents + left after 8 o'clk. + went down town — wrote a short letter to Mary + mailed it — to the Sayre's + to bed at 10.
