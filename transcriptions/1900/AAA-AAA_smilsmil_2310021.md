## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 22, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day - at 8 o'clk my Temp. was but a little above normal but as my head still ached very much I sent for Yale who came at about 11. Got but little satisfaction from him - says neuralgia is very prevalent \+ advised me to take quinine heroically. I got up at 6 p.m. feeling very ragged \+ sat in my easy chair until 10 — then to bed. Head still aching.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 23, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - after an unrestful night I got up with headache of yesterday still continuing \+ feeling generally very "seedy". I commenced taking a course of calomel. 15 gr. every half hour until I had taken ten doses - the result being satisfactory - I went to studio \+ for a time cleaned away the many things lying about - then I worked with crayon \+ chalk on pl. of Mad. lower. sketch in a smokee sky effect, etc - At 12.30 went to Carrere \+ Hastings - Saw H - \+ the sketch plans that he has almost completed of proposed musing fountain in Central Park - at head of 7th Ave. in 54th St. Then home to lunch \+ wrote note to Beckwith - Feeling used up after lunch I had a long nap - up at 4.30 \+ went to C. de Turner in W. 141st St. \+ saw him about a certain portrait age Htee. Then to Lockwood de Forest's - 7 E. 10th St. - to afternoon reception by Mrs. de F. to her friend Mr. Kimale - After that walked home. dinner - Eve'g. Struggled awhile with some of Raefer's arithmetic \+ at 10 I went to Charles for a half hour to talk of some business with him then home \+ to bed at 11.
