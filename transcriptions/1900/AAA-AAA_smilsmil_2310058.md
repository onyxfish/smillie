## Left Page

### Tues. April 10, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

F. E. Church's funeral.

Bright \+ clear m't, with a nip in the air light snow flurries before noon \- again bright \+ to 10 o'clk. I went to funeral of F. E. Church at residence of Mr. Osborne. 32 Park Ave. where he died \- an impressive ceremony \- The surroundings considered \- met there Mr. \+ Mrs. Jas. Gifford \- Men to studio \+ worked on Mr. G. head until 1.30 \- home to lunch \- Nicoll came \+ told me that Mr. DeWolf the Engineer of Central Park w'd meet Com. at Beckwith's studio at 8 This eve't. I went to studio \+ worked as hard as I could until 5 o'clk \- \+ made a sketch in charcoal \+ chalk on tinted paper \- showing my idea of an artist's gate entrance to Central Park at 7th Ave. \+ 59th St. with 3 \+ to galleries for use of D. They Dippeneatt came \+ spent an hour criticising proof of etching after his Water Color Sketch of Girls head \- Home \- dinner at 6.30 \- \+ at 8 went to Beckwith's studio. \- Took with me our sketch made some time ago \+ charcoal sketch made to-day. Delman, Blashfield, Nicoll \+ Beckwith were there but for some reason DeWolf did not come \- Blashfield brought a number of photos. of fine old buildings very interesting with old plac. to the proposed Nd. artists gate \- These photo were carefully looked over \+ many sketches came up for a share of attention \- We talked of Academy affairs \- a proposed amendment to the constitution. Removing requirement that a candidate for membership must exhibit at meeting preceding election \- also, concerning the proposition to admit architects to membership \- It was a far so when we left.

## Right Page

### Wed. April 11, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright pleasant m't, but overcast, windy \+ raw late in the afternoon. \- To studio soon after 9 \- laid grounds on plates \- Mr. G. \- \+ self \- bet. 10 \+ 11 went to funeral of S. P. Avery's daughter Mary \- then back to studio \+ in 36th St. met Mr. Jas. Gifford who had been to studio \- he ret'd with me \+ we had a pleasant chat lasting for an hour \- Home to lunch at 1 \- at 2 Mary, Kate \+ Katharine, Ralph \+ J. \- at Am. Theatre \- 8th Ave. \+ 42d St. "Pinafore" and "Trial by Jury" \- fairly well given \- Out at 5 \- left my glasses at Meyrowit'z' to be fixed then to 57th St. \- Exhib'n of Soct'y of Am. Artists. there until 6 \- the character of these exhibits has changed greatly \- This one differs very little from an ordinary Academy exhib'n. On my way down 5th Ave. Geo. overtook me and walked to Cor. of 38th J. with me \- Dinner at 6.30 \- at 8. went to 40 E. 70th St. to call on Mrs. Barhydt \- she was out \- home \+ spent the rest of the eve'g with newspapers.
