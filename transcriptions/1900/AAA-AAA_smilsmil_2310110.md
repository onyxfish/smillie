## Left Page

### Mon. July 23, 1900

Paid Orlando Beede's acct.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clear \+ very hot day. The old moon as it rises over the Grant looks into my windows \+ wakens me — then the sun follows with brighter beams \+ I cannot sleep. I got up at about 6 o'clk \+ had a good sponge bath \+ rubbing down — I did some cutting \+ then was about with the men — Old Smith \+ his horse were engaged gathering the pine needles \+ shavings \+ burning them. About noon Black, Beede's bookkeeper, came to me with a message from Beede asking for more money — I told him that I didn't have it — \+ he went away — He came back about 5 — asking me to give him a four-months note — I told him that I would go to the hotel after I had had my tea — then I went to Mr. Maghee's seeking advice — but he was away from home — about 8 o'clk I went to the Hotel. I had very little conversation with Orlando — I told Black that I wd give a note where I had receipts from all who had labored on the house — he produced receipts from all except Brouch — as B— had told me that he had been paid in full last Apr. I accepted them — then I said I would not pay 10% on board of mechanics nor on his bill for teaming — he made the deductions — on my demand \+ analyzed the one chg. for teaming \+ gave me the items — then I said I must have Fletcher Beede's bill of $518 receipted — then Orlando went to telephone \+ told Fletcher to come right up — I waited for more than half an hour — then Fletcher came — I could think of nothing more to ask \+ I gave him my note due Nov 23d for $801[illegible]. Then I walked home in the dark — it was very dark, \+ I had some trouble but met with no adventures.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 24, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another hot close day — again wakened by sun rise \+ had fitful sleep until about 6.30 when I got up \+ had a sun bath \+ a rub down — I was very hungry when I got my bkfst. after 8 o'clk — much time spent in finishing the unpacking \+ putting away of things in my trunk — put up hooks \+ towel racks in the girls' rooms — W. Maghee came \+ made a very sociable visit until near 10 clk. I went to hotel for mail \+ back to dinner at about 1.30 — after dinner I had a nap. at about 5 Katherine, Bessie, Jim, Ralph \+ I walked out on Reserve Rd. for about a mile \+ a half — very close \+ warm altho' the sun was veiled — on my return had a good toweling \+ a change of clothes — then had supper — on piazza until 9.30 — then to my room \+ to bed soon after — Buck \+ Webb still at work on stairway from No. piazza — Adams' nephew finishing the painting outside \+ d— staining wood work in main room.
