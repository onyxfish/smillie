## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 6, 1900

**Weather:** Bright m'f. bleak & windy later  **Temp:** [illegible]

Went to Edw. Cook's at breakfast time to learn if there is to be a dividend from Ireland Real Estate Co. There will be none - so I shall have to borrow more money. I went to studio & was able to do a little work on Normandy Barnyard but my head troubled me so much that I went home to lunch early & gave up the afternoon to loafing - went first to 2°. Nat. bank - then to Am. Art galleries - then to Schaus's Wunderhen Knordler's Oehme's, fifth Ave. galleries, Avery's & Clausen's - Then home so used up by my headache that I gave up, went to my room & took no dinner but put myself on a course of calomel.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 7, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

commenced it with a Seidlitz powder which, together with the calomel & last night was effective in driving away the cloud that has hung over me for some days past. When I got to studio I found that three laborers had got to work digging down alongside of the west wall of my studio & [illegible] by 11 o'clk. they reached the water pipe that enters my cellar & found it broken & leaking freely. As this acc'ts for the water that has been running into the cellar for some days past I stopped further work for waterproofing the outside of the wall & the repairmen mended the broken pipe. I got to work on an old painting of "Capri from near Naples" - smeared strong glazes of color over all of it & then plastered in strong color & smoothed it over with palette knife - I may save it yet. - Home to lunch - Sterue met me on my way back & we talked of the Etching class to meet next Wed. n't at 10. I painted as long as I could see - then home - tired - I lay down on my bed & fell asleep - should have gone to reception of Mr. & Miss Coles & Mr. Sid. J. Smith - 6 & 10° St.        Self wrote to Jim.
