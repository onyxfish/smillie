## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 24, 1900

**Weather:** Clear & cold m'g — clouded afternoon & raw.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio at 10 & commenced to work with scraper & burnisher on Mad. Sq. Garden Tower — Miller the carpenter came in & considerable time was lost. I also experimented with some dry Van Dyk Brown, washing it to get the grit out of it. Home at 1.30 — lunch. Mrs. Stakers sisters came to see if they could get the Etching Prize money — I gave them a note to Gramnis — then I went to Miller's & paid his bill — then to studio and very soon Dr. Gester came along & took what remained of day light. I decided to throw aside the plate, Portrait of Mr. G —, over which I have wasted so much time & I told him to do. He wants me to try again — on a smaller, simpler scale — then he asked me to send home to him my painting "After the storm" & gave me his check for $150. I had a long talk with him about Jim & his ambition to be a surgeon — very interesting to me — At 5 o'clk I went to Displen cott's studio — 14 W. 22d. & talked with him about stage setting for Villege Hall — Montrose. He is to make an estimate for me — Home at 6 — dinner — every some reading aloud to Ralph after his lessons — then some writing — & to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 25, 1900

**Weather:** Snasty day, fog & damp — at night, rains.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio at 10 — made a gelatine tracing from Pach. photo of self — cleaned small plate of copper & laid an etching ground on it. It is my intention to make a simple portrait etching of self & give a print of it to J. T. Whete & Co. to make photo. eng. Reproduction of it for the Biographical Encyclopedia — At 12 I went to Kurtz's & left crayon portrait of Mr. Gester to have photo. Reduction made from it — then in to see the Vaux coll. of Am. paintings. I did not have my proper glasses & saw them at a disadvantage — The impression made on me was one of disappointment. Then to 2d Nat'l Bk & deposited Gester check — then to Walter Cook's office — W. 23d St. & saw design for the Carnegie house — then home to lunch — To studio at 3 — dark P.M. — used up what there was of day light in assorting & making a list of my plate printing paper — wet down paper for proofing Japoi plate to-morrow — Dinner — Wey. — at 8.15 took Katharine & Bessie to Sallie Akers' — 27th St. & there I went to Ed & Cook's & made a call on Miss Kent of Buffalo who is visiting there — Had a conference with C.W.C. — concerning affairs of Delund Real Estate Co. — It is likely that the three properties constituting the bulk of the property of the Co., may be without tenants during the coming year — Edw? is evidently very unhappy about it.
