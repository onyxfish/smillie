## Left Page

### Wed. July 25, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy rain beginning about 3 A.M. with a thunderstorm, \+ continuing with decreasing force until late afternoon when there was a fine clearing. — Everything seemed to be soaking wet — it was an ideal day for stretching the burlaps on main room. Adams \+ his bro. came late, but I set them at it under my direction \+ with my assistance \+ at 6.30 it was done successfully. I was very tired. I rec. a letter from Callanan from which I learned that the kitchen range is fitted for burning wood — not coal — at 3 o'clk I went to hotel \+ telephoned to him about it \+ he promised to send the grate \+ fire brick over to-morrow. Evett. I Read my news paper until after 9. \+ then to bed at about 10.

To-day I rec. a conciliatory letter from Charl. — That incident is closed without bloodshed.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 26, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful effects of clearing weather in the mng clouds trailing up the mts. — sunshine \+ frequent air. — clouded over late in the afternoon. — showers. — I was awake from about 4.30 — got up before 7. worked at tacking burlaps on wall of main room. — bkfst. at 8. Then, looking after men \+ things. — In the mtf. Mr. \+ Mrs. Coffin came \+ made a very pleasant call — asked the girls to come to an informal musical party at their cottage next Monday. I went to the hotel at about noon to attend to some business \+ back to dinner at 1 — a good part of the afternoon was given up to a sound nap. — there again to hotel \+ by telephone communicated with Prescott \+ son, Keeseville, \+ Fletcher Beede — Home to tea. — Evg. It was so chilly \+ damp that I made a big fire in main room. Until after 9 o'clk. we all sat about \+ enjoying effects.
