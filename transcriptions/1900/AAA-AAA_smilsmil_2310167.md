## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 14, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant day - very clear & quite cold at night  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day - very clear & quite cold at night. Before I went to studio I wrote a note to Mr. Kent of Buffalo - stating that I had decided to sell the Montrose property asking if she desired to buy it. To studio before 10 - Worked all day on An Adirondack Valley x Repainted the sky & am encouraged. To lunch as usual - after my days work walked up 5th Ave. to Park & back Dinner - Well after 9 o'clk. I went to Charles - M. & Mrs. J. H. Maghee there to dinner - Katharine T. dined with them - Mary & I went after -

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 15, 1900

**Weather:** Dull & raw - afternoon high winds - Snow flurries & gleams of sunshine  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull & raw - afternoon high winds - Snow flurries & gleams of sunshine - I got up with a headache that bothered me all day. To studio at 10 - worked for an hour or more getting a ground upon a blade of pr. \+ scissors & drawing with a pen "Priez Pour la donatrice" for fr. Keppel & at his request. Home at 12 - lunch at 12.30 - with Ralph I took 1.05 train to Tarry Town - Public Opening & Dedication of Hackley Hall - Special car & carriages - we were driven first to the new grounds & went over a portion a beautiful, park-like place then to the older property Hackley Hall - then all thro' that bldg which we found very interesting - I have quite made up my mind to send Ralph here next year. The dedication exercises were interesting but I was exposed to such a draught that I became thoroughly chilled & at 5, immediately upon the close of the exercises & without waiting for the "tea" that followed R. & I walked to the station - 5.30 train - Special car - got to N.Y. at 6.30 - Home to dinner - Well Chare came in for a few minutes bringing prints from a number of the panorama photos. that he took at Keene Heights last summer.
