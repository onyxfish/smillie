## Left Page

### Sun. June 17, 1900

**Weather:** Clouded, after a night of rain, \+ quite chilly  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Ther. standing in the late afternoon, at 62°. I got up at 5.30 \+ had a bath \+ shampoo. Then to bed \+ slept until 7.30 — then for nearly an hour lay in my bed \+ wrote notes for an introductory chapter to Father's auto-biographical sketch. B\&p.: after 9 — then for sometime with my news paper — out about 11 went to Studio's \+ worked there until 1 o'clk. getting things in shape. Then home \+ with Ralph went out for a short walk — dinner at 2 — about 3 R\+I went to Charl's and looked at some modern Jap. bronzes \+ ivory carvings that Bernard has recently sent to him — I don't think much of them — then to studio. There until 6 — then home. Tea at 6.30. Every Charl came \+ stayed until 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 18, 1900

**Weather:** Very cool day — partially overcast — Very soon after [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

I started out — went first to foot of 32° S. 11th Ave. \+ saw W. Witbeck, the freight agt. \+ chartered a car for West port. to be loaded Friday on't. — Then by 10th Ave R.R. to foot of Charlton St. — at Heywood Bros. saw W. J. D. Walsh \+ arranged with him to send furniture to chartered car. Then to Wanamaker's — bought Tack downs, blankets \+ bed spreads. \+ arranged with Mr. Post of Shipping dep't. to have things sent to car. on Friday on't. Then by Elevated R.R. to City Hall \+ to Ringler's about Photo-grav. Reductions of book plates — promised on Wed. next — Then to Studio \+ home to lunch at 1 — after that once more to Wanamaker's to arrange about packing bed cloths. Then to Fred Friedrichs — 140 Sullivan St. \+ bought Easel for Keene \+ two small panel-back stretchers — Then home before 4 — tired \+ headache — had a nap before dinner at 6.30 — quiet eve'g. wrote some notes etc. —
