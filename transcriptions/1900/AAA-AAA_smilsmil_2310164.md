## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 8, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasant. clouded over in the afternoon \+ a little spatter of rain fell at about 6 p.m. To studio at 10 \+ got out canvas x "Eve of \-Showers" \- a subject laid in about 4 years ago, \+ commenced painting on that \- I stuck to it pretty closely but did not make much head way. \- all day fooling over the cumulus that rises on the left hand end of composition I hope that my study of clouds last summer will help me after I get through the ice \- home to lunch \- afternoon worked until 4.30 \- then went to Brooks Bros about overcoat \- then to Keb[illegible]'s \- K. gave me a pair of scissors to etch a legend, or motto, up on one of the blades for him \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Susy, Mary \+ Bessie went to concert. Boston Symphony I worried over my accts until 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 9, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night. cloudy wet m'f. cleared cold with a hurricane of wind. to studio before 10 \+ worked all day on "Eve of \- Showers" \- I am beyond my depth in this work \- in fact, I'm always beyond my depth except in very shallow water. I have no defined color sense \- I know, in a general way, what I want in color \+ have sense enough to know when colors are out of tune \- so, if I get my key note fairly true, succeeding work is a laborious digging out of harmonies, much as a beginner might feel for the correct notes to make a harmony on a piano \- I have been in a sky all day daubing around to get such harmonies. Home to lunch \- afternoon at 5 o'clk. went to Century by appointment \+ met Mr. J. Ferris Lockwood, Business Director of Public Library, at Astor Place \- He wanted to talk to me about making up for the library a good air. coll. of prints. Home to dinner at 6.30 \- Susy wrote a long letter to Jim \+ mailed it.
