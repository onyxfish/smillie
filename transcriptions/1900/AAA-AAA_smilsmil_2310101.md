## Left Page

### Thur. July 5, 1900

**Weather:** Sulky, clouded day threatening rain.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very soon after bkft. I saw Mr Branch \+ had a short talk with him — Then I was engaged writing for some time before I went to the house where I found Buck \+ West at work. Adams did not get along until after 10 — he is dreadfully slow \+ full of promises — I spent the m'g getting bedsteads set up \+ furniture away in the bed rooms. Branch came after 12 \+ we went over the buildings together \+ consulted about work to be done — It was near 2 o'clk before I got to the hotel to lunch. Afternoon, again at the house until 6 — It was after 7 before we had dinner. Went again \+ saw Branch — took him to my room \+ for nearly two hours we were going over the statements sent to me from Beede. Branch is clear on all details \+ gave me much light by his explanations. He confirmed what I was already sure of, that Beede is floundering financially beyond his debts \+ that his accounts are not kept in a strictly businesslike manner.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 6, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A great downpour with heavy thunder \+ lightning of rain, about 6 a.m. followed by lighter showers — a clearing about mid day \+ another downpour in the afternoon — with misty, luminous clearing. Wrote some letters before I went to the house — there until about 1 o'clk then to hotel to lunch — after lunch I had a long, solid nap — It was near 5 o'clk when I wakened — then to house. Adams the painter, had got a woman who was cleaning up some of the floors — I have not been able before this to find any woman worker. The work goes on very slowly \+ I cannot find a man to do general cleaning up about the house — In spite of this I have written to Mary that I think she had better come on at the end of next week with the rest of the family \+ take the chances.
