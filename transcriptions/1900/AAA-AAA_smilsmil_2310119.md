## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 10, 1900

**Weather:** Warm + muggy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Warm + muggy - heavy thundershowers came from the N.W. bet. 4 + 5 p.m. + continued until after 9 o'clk. Remarkably beautiful moonlight effects from both north + south piazzas. The plumber put in appearance about o'clk time + in the course of the m't made the connections in confusion of hot + cold water pipes - came again after dinner, packed a foot or two of pipe with mineral wool - show Ray how to do it - then left by afternoon stage for his home - Ray ret'd to work + this being his first day since the accident - He dug a part of the trench uncovering the water pipes - after o'clk? I got Otis + Ducharme to come + finish it. I took Buck + Webb from their work on the cold room + set them to making a rough box to encase the two hot water pipes between the two houses - They were rather cross about it but later recovered their tempers. I had as much as I could do to follow the several men + direct their work - Otis + Ducharme worked on the road with team + plough + made progress - Jim took Edith Hoet fishing - they caught nothing - but the first shower caught them + they came home perfectly drenched + sights to be seen. Every there was much enjoyment on the upper piazzas - north + south - of changing moonlight effects. Adams put down on S.W. corner upper decking, the piece of Duck that came this m't 2 yds. to make up short measure. The Rain caught him before he had finished it.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 11, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still hot + muggy - winding up with a series of violent showers with thunder. Carpenters finished the boxing of the water pipes in the m't + resumed work on cold room. I went to hotel + telephoned to Callanau for more mineral wool - + to Sanders for a roll of sheathing paper, then back + the rest of the day was given to general superintendence. Ru'f., a little reading.

A night of sleeplessness + some pain - Jim too is evidently much out of sorts - complains of disordered stomach + weak knees. But he + Edith Hoet went fishing in the river this afternoon + were caught in one or two of the heaviest showers - they came home drenched.
