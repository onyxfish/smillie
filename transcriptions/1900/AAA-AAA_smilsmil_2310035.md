## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 19, 1900

**Weather:** Clear & cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear & cold - after a restless night I did not feel at all like work & when I got to studio found that I was not in shape to do any thing that required thought or effort. I made tracings of sky forms that I expect to etch on "Mad. Sq. Garden" & retraced them on the plate & I worked for some time on Mr. G. place making a bevel on the edges of it - Home to lunch & did not return to studio until after 3 - left soon after 4 & went to Sherwood Studios bldg. - called on W. Todd - I met him & his wife at Mr. A. White's reception the other day - & called now at his request to see a portrait that he has just finished - Then I went to Beckwith's - found him at work with a lady sitter - I got away after a few minutes - walked home - dinner - Chet. at 9 o'clk. I went to Charl's - Mr. Dell Sacre & Mrs. Jessie Comstock Edgar there - also Mary, Hale & Bessie - Charl gave an option of orchestrelles & Pianola - Home just before 11.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 20, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - clear, milder & quieter - Got to studio at about 10 after attending to some business matters at home - Found a number of little things to do for an hour & a half - then went to Carrère & Hastings & had a half hours talk with Mr. C. about the position that J.Q.A. Ward has taken, insisting upon a new architectural competition & the N.A. succeeds in getting a site in Central Park - Ward saw C. some days ago & told him without qualification of the stand he had taken - C. was quite upset & instead of contesting took a modest & conciliating course - I assured C. that I had no sympathy with Ward in his course in this matter & did not believe that any other member of the com. had - I talked with C. of the natural consequences & the claims of C.H. upon the N.A. that would follow if Ward had his way - C. told me of precedents where such differences had been carried into court for settlement. taking such cases as guides claims for compensation & damages to the extent of $15,000 could be brought agt. the N.A. Home at 12.30 & wrote a note to Beckwith telling of my call - lunch at 1 - to studio before 2. - Dr. Gelster came soon after - he is pleased with plate No. 2 & makes some just criticisms that will not be difficult to follow - at 4 I went to J. T. White No. 16th st. & saw young Mr. White - gave him pen sketch portrait of self [illegible] gallery pf. a biographical sketch to extend - then I went to Chy. Turner's - W. 14th st. & saw him for a few minutes about curtain for Montrose Village Hall - then to Turner & Amend's & got some drugs & some rubber tubing & Arnold burners for etching Studio at N.A. - Home soon after 6 - Dinner at 6.30 - Eve. quietly at home as usual & to bed soon after 10.
