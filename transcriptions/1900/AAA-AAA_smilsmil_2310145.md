## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 1, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day - wonderful autumnal color - luminous atmosphere - a model fall day. Buck came + worked on studio but Webb did not. I understand that his daughter is very sick. Ray worked about the place. I had all that I could do in my finishing-up work. - About 12.30 I went to hotel + got my mail - got a letter from Hadving Kahler, written at the request of publishers asking me to write a closing chapter for a "History of Etching" written by J. R. Kahler some years ago + publ. by Cassell + Co - afterwards sold to the Cambridge Encyc. Co. - who are now preparing to republish it. The request surprised + gratified me. I doubt my competency but at once wrote to the publishers that I was willing to try. Otis came + got some instructions about work to be done. - Every Mr. Magher made a pleasant call + I worked on the canvases that had been cleansed + put the washed wicks back in them.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 2, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

at W. Scott Browns -
Mary, Katharine + servants went to N.Y.

another wonderfully fine day - cool without being cold - + luminously bright. After an early bkft. Mary + Katharine + the servants got away - baggage wagon + three seater - young Hast driving. Buck came, finished studio, except the two new doors + worked on doors + other things - Mr. Ducha[mp] came + was hed up the linen + Otis came + I laid out certain work for him to do. I wrote a letter to miss Koehler + did a great deal of general picking up + stowing away. - at noon we went to Scott Browns + had a very good dinner - stopped at hotel on my way back, got mail + finished letter to Jim + posted it - more clearing up work in the afternoon - Ray burning the waste stuff about the house. - Mrs. Hardenburgh came + invited us to dinner to-morrow. It was 6.30 when we got to Mr. Brown's to tea - met with papers - D. Murtland of N.Y. - just from lake Saranac - made an unexpected descent upon the Browns - he wants to stay a month out as they have planned to leave here next month he cannot stay.
