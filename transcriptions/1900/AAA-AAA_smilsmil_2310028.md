## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 5, 1900

**Weather:** Chas, cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Miss Emily Taylor of Po'k arr?

Having a number of notes to write & checks to draw it was after 10 before I got to studio - By 12 o'clk I had completed the drawing in of the lines on face of Mr. G. etching - then I got everything ready for the biting & home to lunch at 1.30 - Miss Emily Taylor there, having arr. from Po'keepsie to visit for a few days with the girls. Back to studio at 2.30 & worked at biting of Mr. G. head until after 4 o'clk - then, as I could not finish the biting before dark I stopped for the day - went home, dressed a bit & at 5 went to 515 Mad. Ave. cor. St. & called on Annie Tiffany Mitchell & her two daughters. It was very pleasant to see them in their cosey apartment & then I went to 59 W. 45th St. & called on Mr. & Mrs. Sam. Colman & spent half an hour with them - home to dinner a little late. Evg. Charl came in for a while.

The mechanics commenced putting up fire escape on back of house to-day.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 6, 1900

**Weather:** clouded m't., clearing later  **Temp:** [illegible]

The girl's lunch party.

I got up with a severe headache which stuck to me all day - but I worked through it all. It was 10 o'clk. when I got to studio & I went on with the biting of Mr. G. head - Home to lunch at 1 o'clk. The house given over to a lunch party given by Katherine & Bessie to about half a dozen girls - Ralph had his lunch in the kitchen & I had mine in my bed room - The iron workers finished putting up the fire escape - a clumsy, botched piece of work - my neighbors in the alley back of my studio are making me trouble by piling all sorts of refuse against my building - ashes, garbage, etc. I went to the police in 35th S. & made complaint - as I have done before. I finished my biting & then worked with graver until nearly 5 o'clk - then I walked up to 57th S. & 5th Ave & then home - Dinner at 6.30 - Evg. my head pained me so much that at 9 o'clk. I went to bed.
