## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 14, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouds prevailed - but there was much pleasant sunshine \+ a delightfully fresh air. Jim had trouble with his bowels \+ after bkft. passed a little blood. I went to hotel \+ saw Dr. Hobbs \+ he said There was no need for alarm - but after dinner, Jim who had complained all the m\+ of being cold suddenly developed fever \+ showed 104½° on my Ther. I was alarmed \+ again went for Dr. H. He came after 4 o'clk. \+ after careful examination said he thought the trouble might be malaria. He left medicine \+ said he would call again to-morrow p.m. \+ prescribed quinine for to-morrow. His fever abated \+ at night he was more comfortable. The carpenters did not come - nor did Adams - Moody put in half a day. Otis with his two sons \+ Ducharme put in a good days work on the Road \+ made it passable for vehicles. I worked on plans for finish of main Room. A very quiet every before a pleasant fire \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 15, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m\+. - clouded p.m. fine sunset effects with premonitions of rain - no carpenters or painters appeared - but the men worked on the Road \+ finished it at noon. Ray worked all the m\+ with Otis \+ in the afternoon he worked on the Road bank grading it. I was about all the m\+ \+ did some work on plans for finish of main Room. At about 3 p.m. Katharine, Eliz\+, Edith, Holt, Raelph \+ I started for a walk - went up Reserve Road to Beaver Meadow Fall Trail \+ across to the fall - woods very wet - falls very full \+ fine - Then down the River on west side Trail - all hands enjoying fully the splendid views of gorge, River \+ Mt., culminating with the view of Grant \+ the gorge at our pit - there was a particularly fine atmospheric effect over the Grant when we got to the outlook. We got home a little before 7 o'clk having had about 4 hours of tramping, all in pretty good shape. I got through in a very encouraging manner — At once had a rub down \+ change \+ then a half hours nap \+ at 8 o'clk. had my supper \+ to bed at 10.

Jim's condition was improved to-day. He had very little fever in the m\+ \+ none later in the day, but he had trouble with his bowels \+ passed some blood. I talked with Dr. Hobbs \+ he thought that the bleeding came from internal piles - Jim was kept in bed all day \+ had very little to eat - Dr. came to see him at about 5 p.m.
