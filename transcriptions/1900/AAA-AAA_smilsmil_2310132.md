## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 5, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delicious m'f., cool, brilliant \+ breezy. \- but in spite of it I was out of sorts with a headache that, while it did not send me to bed, interfered with work that I had planned to do. I spent the m'f. putting up poles for curtains \+ small rods on the doors in large room for silk curtains that Mary \+ Kate fixed. \- After dinner I went to hotel for mail. Breadener came in the afternoon \+ again looked over the hot-water arrangement of plumbing. \- He says he must come again \+ look it over yet more carefully before he will make a definite proposition. \- Mrs. Coffin made a call in the late afternoon. \- Eve'g read news paper \+ wrote to J. P. Ryan \+ Second Nat'l Bank.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 6, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'f. with smart shower about 8 o'clk. \- cleared before 10. \- changeable but pleasant day \- fine showery cloud effects. \- Just a heavy thunder shower came down upon us from the north after 6 o'clk. \- Eve'g fog \+ clouds \- quite mild. \- Before b'kf. I wrote a long letter to Yale about Jim whose physical condition gives me much concern. If he does not improve greatly within the next three weeks I will not dare send him to Pomfret to school. \- How I wish for Birnie's courage \+ counsel. \- Went to hotel after 10 \- \+ then visited until near dinner time \- made alone call on Mr. Coffin who showed me over his cottage. \- After dinner I gave way to a short nap. \- Then I took hold of the problem of making the big studio window water tight. \- I lathered white lead on bottom seams \+ tacked strips of duck into the lead \+ then painted over that. Webb worked on broom closet on piazza \+ on inclined plane over butler's pantry. \- Ray worked on open grounds south of house clearing away the weeds. \- Eve'g \- with newspaper as usual \+ walking on piazza.
