## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 13, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain all last night \+ nearly all to-day - clearing with spiteful showers late in the afternoon. To studio at 9.30 \+ spent the day printing \+ working between times on the two plates - Mr. G. head \+ my own portrait. I took, also, three p\[r\]fs from steel mezzotint" after the Storm" but for some reason that I cannot explain I could get only faint \+ weak impressions as though the plate was filled in with something or had been worn. Home to lunch \+ after that I went to barber's \+ had my hair cut, then to studio \+ continued work and printing on my portrait. Dinner at 6.30 with news paper until 8.30. Then I spent an hour on more giving my head a thorough Far soap shampoo.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 14, 1900

**Weather:** clear, cold \+ windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

After b\[reakfas\]t made up a p\[ac\]k\[ag\]e for Jim - shirt, sweater \+ french dictionary to be expressed to him - \+ then wrote a letter explanatory. To studio at 10. Spent the m\[orn\]ing until 12.45 on Mr. G. head, taking naps, \+ working. Then home to lunch. At 2 all hands went to the Murray Hill Theatre where we had a lower box on the right hand side - a poor enough place but the best to be had. Katharine was not well enough to go - Emily Cook took her place. Play was "The Private Secretary" - On the whole it was well given \+ was very amusing. Years ago I saw it at the old Madison Sq. Theatre where it was given in fine style. The actor who in "All the Comforts of Home" had excited my ire by his vulgar over acting took the part of the Sec'y \+ really carried it off very well. Out at 4.30 - I went to studio \+ worked for more than an hour on Mr. G. head with graver. I am in the dumps over this second plate \+ am afraid that it will not be any more of a success than no. 1. At 6 o'clk I walked up 5th Ave. to the Park \+ back - \+ then dinner at 6.30. We \+ Ralph \+ I were left to ourselves, Mary \+ Kate going to Charles' \+ Bessie to some sort of a party.
