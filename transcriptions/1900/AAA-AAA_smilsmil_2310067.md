## Left Page

### Sat. April 28, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another pleasant day — Up early \+ wrote to Miss Kent of Buffalo offering her the montrose house for the summer as Mrs. Adams has just informed me that he can't afford to pay $600 for it. At the studio I wrote a note or two — having called at Charl's on my way — at about 11 I went to 2° Nat'l Bank — Case agreed to reduce interest rate on such part of my loans there as is above 5%. Then I went into Putnams \+ saw Mr. Mr. Ireland \+ he promised to come to the house next Tues. eve'g to appraise Geo. Cook's books that are there. Then as I was feeling the threatenings of a headache I went home at 12 \+ was on my bed until 7 — then lunch. At 2 Ralph \+ I went to The Garrick Theatre \+ saw W.H. Gillette as Sherlock Holmes. This play has been running for nearly 7 months \+ still has full houses — our seats were bought four weeks ago. The play astonished me very much — except for — all of the actors were sticks — conventional \+ disagreeable to me — The play is without plot \+ in its detective work is unreasonable \+ unreal. I cannot understand its popularity — out at 5.30 I went to Century hoping to meet some one there — no one — Carnegie sailed to-day leaving a note saying that he could not give the new site scheme of Butler any consideration. Dinner at 6.30 — an uncomfortable eve'g \+ to bed early —
Summa Bourne left for Po'k. This p.m. Kate Ret.' from Po'k this m'g.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 29, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

charming day — up at 8 — bkfst. at 9 \+ off with R— at 11.30 — rode up to 120th St. \+ there entered Park \+ strolled about enjoying the air, the color, the birds \+ the bruches. we finally came out on 8th Ave. about 96th St. \+ rode home getting in at 1.30 — dinner at 2. afternoon I did something in the way of assorting \+ dusting my foreign view photos. After 5 I went to Emma Ireland's — She \+ Mary at home — I took tea with them \+ had a very pleasant, chatty time until 9.30 — Then walked home.
