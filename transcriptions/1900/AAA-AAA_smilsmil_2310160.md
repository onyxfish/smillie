## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 31, 1900

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast - about noon, a little wet - Got to studio before 10 + soon to work on large canvas "A Normandy Barnyard" - It was started years ago + a handsome frame was made for it but the canvas was never completely covered - This is the first time that I have tackled it since to start. Prof. Geo. J. Comfort of Syracuse came in to get some exhibits for the opening exhibit of the Syracuse museum of Fine Arts - We used up about an hour of time talking over a wide range of ground - At 11.40 I went to the office of Receiver of Taxes in Chambers St + paid my taxes on house + studio bldg - amounting to $634.18 - back to studio at 1 + painted till 11.30 - then home to lunch - after that, back + painted till nearly 5. Then walked up 5th Ave. - Met bro George + walked with him - Dinner at 6.30 - Evey Clark came in for awhile - I gave some time to my accts

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 1, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another dull day - disagreeable drizzly evef. To studio at 9.30 + to work on Normandy Barnyard - worked until nearly 2 o'clk. on my way home met Miller, the carpenter. it was 2.15 before I got to my lunch - about 3 when I got back to studio + painted until nearly 5 in spite of the darkness. Then I went to Brooks Bros + got a suit of clothes + a heavy overcoat - a total of $72.00. Then home - dinner - evef - newspaper - a call from Chas - + some bills \+ accts.
