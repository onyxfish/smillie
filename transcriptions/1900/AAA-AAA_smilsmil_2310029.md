## Left Page

### Wed. Feb. 7, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast day \- Raw \+ rain smelling \- To studio at 10 \+ worked with a graver on G. head. In spite of my intention to make this a free etching I find that I am sliding back into my old ways and smoothing \+ finishing every thing with the graver. At 12 I went to 2d. Nat. Bk. Home to lunch at 1 \- then to studio and worked until 4 \- then home \+ then to Mr. Hy Villard's \- 145 W. 58th St. where I had a very pleasant interview with him in relation to a site for U.a.f.a. in Central Park on 59·5\. at the head of 7th Ave. He has a controlling influence in the Evening Post. I wish to find as far as possible what the attitude of the press will be to a raid upon Central Park \+ I have started with Mr. Villard of the Post. He approves of the plan \+ said he was ready to help the project in any way that he could. Later I saw Mr. Villard \+ the two were very chatty and pleasant. On my way home I stopped at Beckwith. \+ saw him for a few minutes. Home to dinner late \- Eve'g writing after I had read my newspaper.

## Right Page

### Thur. Feb. 8, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Miserable weather \- pouring rain most of the day \+ night. I got up with a beast of a headache that grew upon me until about noon I had to go home \+ to bed where I stayed until about 9 p.m. I went to the studio about 10 \+ wrote a letter to W. A. Warner \- Montrose \- explaining the plans \+ estimates for stage fitting for Village Hall that I got a few days ago from Lippincott \- I made a call \+ added the correspondence \+ estimates of Bosman \+ Landis of Chicago, sent to me by Warner some time ago \- This I two to substation, Lex. Ave. \+ 44th St. \+ then home \+ to bed.
