## Left Page

### Fri. June 1, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during last night — overcast, hot \+ muggy day — some rain late in the afternoon — After a severe headache that lasted all night I get up feeling very much used up but the ache passed away during the mt. At studio I was all day fussing with [illegible] \+ getting some of them into frames. Home to lunch at 1 — back at 2 — at 6 I took to Century framed [illegible] of Jacobi \+ Mr. G. plates. To dinner at 6.30 — Chark came in for a while in the evth.

Mary continues to improve \+ to-day walked across her room. The cook left this mt. Told Kate Leonard arr? from Springfield this afternoon to take the place temporarily.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 2, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still hot \+ close — thunderstorm about 1 \+ again between 4 \+ 6 p.m. I went to studio at [illegible] of 3 small framed etchings and took them to Century club — gave [illegible] keys to studio — from Century walked down to [illegible] Nat'l Bank \+ drew money — then to Norton's \+ to Richter's — R. had sent to me samples of burlaps — I approved \+ gave final order — then to studio at about 11.30. I took [illegible] of ex. lib. plates out of press — Vandyke ret? my samples of paper \+ printing with instructions \+ I prepared to carry them out. Home to lunch \+ again to studio — made pkg of [illegible] to send to Monstray — wrote to him \+ pkg containing M's original design, to send to Vand — \+ wrote to him — then some general clearing up in studio \+ home soon after 4 — just in time to avoid thunder shower — about 6, went to Branch P.O. with pkgs — then dinner — after dinner, dressed. Chad came in — at 9 went to Century — had a pleasant time as a member of the artists \+ the laymen said very nice things to me about my etchings — which, altho' badly hung — no fault of com. or att. — looked well — The [illegible] were — Jacobi — Mr. G. — Portrait of self \+ the Century \+ Vand — book plates — Home at 11.30.
