## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 25, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant m'f. growing overcast in the afternoon, with indications of rain.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Buck \+ Webb made good progress with their work \+ Adamus stained the wood before it was fitted \+ put in place. Ray worked at grading \+ getting around ready for seeding. Lawrence Adler \+ Ralph went to the top of Calvin. R. left the house about 9.30. I wrote a letter to Prescott objecting to much of the lumber he sent me \+ before 12 o'clk went to hotel. Dinner at 1. Mary \+ I alone. Katharine sick \+ Ralph away. Mrs. H o'clk. called (her first call) before About 11.30 I walked into the Reserve about a mile \+ a half by the road \+ met Ralph \+ Lawrence returning between Flume \+ Reservoir. I walked back with them. Stopped at hotel to get Atwood. D. \+ H. Station at Westport on the telephone, but failed. Then home to tea. Ralph has had a lovely time, \+ a 14 miles walk, but is not the least bit tired. Eve'g reading until near 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 26, 1900

**Weather:** A very close, warm day. Ther. getting above 80.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clouded over, as tho' for rain, about the middle of the day. a very little rain fell. At night thunderstorms were about. I gave much time to Buck \+ Webb, putting up the cornice in main room. I went thro' the puzzle of blind hinges. assorting the old ones that were taken off \+ finding the exact number of new ones required to furnish all the blinds already on hand \+ those not yet ordered. I went to the hotel before noon \+ worked the telephone for some time. Prescott, for partition sash. Atwood for pkg. at Westport. \+ Plattsburgh to get information about trains. Dinner at 1. Afternoon, after 4, I went to Mr. Hardenbergh's. Taking Ralph to afternoon tea. Mary \+ Katharine already there. Mr. Vauthorn \+ his daughter, Mr. Vault, are to leave to-morrow. At 6 I went to Mr. Nielson's to bid her good-bye (she is to leave to-morrow) but all of the family were out. Home. tea at 6.30 Eve'g with newspaper \+ to bed early.
