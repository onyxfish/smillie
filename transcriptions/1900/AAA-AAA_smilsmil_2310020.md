## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 20, 1900

**Weather:** Rain during last night \+ fog \+ rain thro' most of the day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain during last night \+ fog \+ rain thro' most of the day — a very depressing \+ debilitating day — spent the day printing my mezzotint "After the Storm" — refused to yield an impression — it is a steel plate \+ I know nothing of its history — it may have been rocked for rather 50 or more years ago \+ has lain all that time coated with wax — after efforts to clean it by various processes I put it in a tray of boiling hot potash \+ the result pleased promptly \+ I got a good pf. Then I took prints from Mad. Sq. Garden Tower — Miller the carpet-cutter called \+ I took him up to Maynard's room — M. talks of having a gallery put up at the east end of his studio. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back to studio \+ after some dry point work on Tower I took four satisfactory pps. I have been in doubt as to the best way to finish his plate — at one time I had quite determined to put an aqua-tint ground over the whole of it \+ work up a tone effect but now I think I had better leave it for an etching adding a sky \+ cloud effect at base of tower it was almost 5 o'clk. when I got everything cleaned up \+ I left the studio — I was very tired but I went to the house \+ dressed, \+ a bit \+ then went to a reception at Washington Wilson's — 166 W. 58th St — great crowd — canvases \+ footmen out of doors — flowers — gaily dressed women — pictures \+ men servants in doors — I spent the best part of an hour pleasantly with some old friends \+ had a few introductions. Met Geo. \+ Nellie there — Home at 6.30 — dinner — Ralph had ret. from his days outing with Bronson Cutting. Evey divided between news paper \+ Ralph's arithmetic —

*[header note: Ralph went with Bronson C. — to Long Island. Mrs. Washington Wilson's reception]*

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 21, 1900

**Weather:** Cold, windy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, windy day — Got up at 7 o'clk feeling as well as usual \+ had my scrub — giving my head a shampoo — then to bed again — up at 9.30 — after 11 went out with Ralph for a walk — but the weather was so unpleasant that we ret'd in less than an hour — My head ached very uncomfortably growing worse as the day went on so that I was practically useless — I went to bed at 9.30 having some fever which increased until about 2 a.m. when it began to abate — I got some rest between 5 o'clk \+ 7.
