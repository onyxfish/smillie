## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 16, 1900

**Weather:** Showers before 6 Am.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day. In the afternoon showers passed north of us, but we had no wet. Late to bkft. Buck & Webb came + I kept them at work in the house all day so as to give the cold room, as far as it is built, + the lumber, a chance to dry out. They worked in the studio, in my room + on my balcony. + I kept about with them — I went to hotel about 10. Saw Dr. Stabbs — more mineral wool + some felt packing. Ray finished packing box in which the hot water pipes lie. In the afternoon Mrs. Notman came bringing her mother, Mrs. Stoward, Sam. Colmain's sister + we had a talk about Sam. + his wife + old times — I was much surprised to learn that Mrs. Colmain is suffering from a cancer, Mrs. H — says, in her side — + that there is no hope — a quiet evey.

Jim passed his regular malarial period this m'f. without a return of fever — I saw Dr. H — at the hotel about 10.30 am. + he said Jim might get up + dress at noon if he had no fever — He got up with a good appetite — had a little trouble with his bowels in the m'f. Altho' up + about all the afternoon + evey. he said he was weak + dizzy.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 17, 1900

**Weather:** Showers last night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very pleasant day. Very busily engaged all day with direction of various jobs about the house. Otis brought cedar posts that were set at the foot of long flight of stairs from North piazza to annex. Buck framed them. Adams came for the first time this week. After dinner Ralph + I took a ride on our wheels to the village — had a rub + change on my return — Jim is up + about, listless + weak but has had no return of fever or bowel trouble.

At about 7.30 all hands except Kate + Jim, went to hotel to an entertainment given under direction of Mrs. Sidney Sheets for a fresh air Charity. There was some good piano music + singing. Young Lawrence Adler played remarkably well — + altogether, it was an enjoyable evey.
