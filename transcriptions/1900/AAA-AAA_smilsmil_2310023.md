## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 26, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A horrid cold, windy day \- part of the time clouded over \- Got to studio at 10 \+ prepared to take p/p. from the Jacobi plate. This took time \- I had to grind in \+ to make sure that I had the color \+ consistency that I wished I had to take 3 or 4 preliminary prints \- I had to cut the india \+ mark my press for position of plate \+ paper. All of this consumed the m/f. After lunch I took five large paper india p/p \- none of them exactly to my mind \- the main trouble I think was with my printing cloths which were too dry \- I could not get the pulpy fullness that a "fat rag" will give \- the first three had too much Van Dyke Brown in the ink \- however \- I had to be satisfied \- they are fairly good p/p. It was nearly 6 when I got through with the work \+ cleaned up. I was very tired \+ I rested in my big chair for half an hour \- then home to dinner \- Evg: I read aloud to Ralph for awhile \+ then wrote part of a letter to Jim \- I was too tired to finish it \+ had not courage enough to face the blast \+ take it to the Grand Cent. Station to post.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 27, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

fifth anniversary of Annie's death.

Fine winter day \- just such a day as the one when Annie passed away \- clear \- not too cold nor so windy as yesterday. After b/f. I finished letter to Jim \+ took it to G. C. station to post. Then to studio \- laid ground on Jacobi plate, etched in autograph \- also my name in full at the request of St. Meister \- Millie's man came \+ spent most of the m/f putting up a second stone in my Acid Room \- This interfered with my mornings work \- at 12 went to 2? Nat'l Bk \+ then to O'Brien's \+ found that Treas. Report had not yet been delivered to N.A. \- Home to lunch at 1 \- After that, to studio \- bit autograph etc \+ at 3.30 went to Kimmel \+ Voigt's with the plate \- back to studio about 4.30 \- home before 6. After Ralph finished his studies in the evg. he \+ I worked most after 10 o'clk pasting backbals. \+ Harpers Mag. in paper wrappers \+ hugs to keep them in order \+ clean for the binder.
