## Left Page

### Wed. March 21, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** Temp. at 8 A.M. 97 3/5. at 10 P.M. 97 2/5.

Cold \+ windy \- clouds \+ sunshine \- Maurice Steme came to studio before 10 \+ I gave him all the lps of his plates that I had with two or three exceptions \- he wished lps. to put in an RyB[illegible] \- Then I gave some time to making of Asphaltum varnish \- to taking 24"×36" Mad. Sq. Tower by moonlight" from its old stretcher \+ putting it on a new panelback stretcher \+ in laying grounds on two plates. About 12 went to Carres \+ Hastings \- saw H. \+ had a pleasant little chat with him \+ congratulated him upon his approaching marriage to Miss Benedict. To come off in Ap[ril]. 30th \- then they go abroad for three months. Then home \+ spent some time trying to fix battery for electric bells \- but it wouldn't fix forme \- Then got out photos. European subjects that have been in a drawer \+ got to be much soiled by dust \- took them to my room \- lunch at 1.30 \- Ralph lunched at Bronson Cutting's \+ went with him \+ other school fellows \+ heard "Patience" \- Before 3 I went to C. T. H's, \+ saw Carrere \+ had a talk with him ab't the Ward imbroglio \- to my surprise I learned that no thing had been heard by them from Delieuze or Beckwith since the Committee meeting nearly three weeks ago \- Then to Studio \- worked for awhile varnishing two plates \- \+ then added some work to portrait etching of self that will improve the likeness I think. Home soon after 5. Dinner at 6.30 \- Quiet ev'g

## Right Page

### Thur. March 22, 1900

Boston Symphony Concert.

**Weather:** Weather mixed \- cloud \+ sunshine \- After going to P.M. Theatre \+ buying tickets for tomorrow ev'g but no Rain \- M[?] at Studio and bit a little etching added on plate Mad. Sq. Garden \+ Tower \+ rebit a portion of the vail on 2d. plate. Mr. G \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- to Studio at 3 \+ worked until after 5 with a graver on face of an etching that I commenced in 1891 after a water color sketch "Girls Head" \- by Wm Lippincott \- Then home after a short walk \- Dinner \- Evening went with Bessie to Carnegie Hall \- 5th \+ last concert of the season \- Boston Symphony Orchestra. Instead of a Mozart Symphony, down in programme, Schumann's symphony in D. minor was given \- for this I was glad \+ enjoyed it much as it came to me, familiar, \+ laden with dear associations \- We then had Beethoven's Concerto 4. G. Maj. for piano by the new young light, Dohmyani. He's undoubtedly an artist, but piano concertos do not reach my heart owing to defects in my constitution. Then a work beyond my comprehension by another new light, Richard Strauss. A Symphonic Poem "Thus Spake Zarathustra" \- The text, as explained in programme was absolutely incomprehensible to me \- As was the music \- then we had our old friend, the overture to "Oberon" \+ we were out at 10.30.
