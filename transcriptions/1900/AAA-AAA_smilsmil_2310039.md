## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 27, 1900

**Weather:** Clear \+ cold \- less windy than yesterday  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 9.30 \- \+ spent the morning taking prints from "Road to lake". Mad. Sq. garden "tower" \- etc \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \+ back \- took prints from Mrs. G. No. 2. \+ at 4.30 went to J. T. White \+ Co. in 16th St. saw young N. W. \+ gave him extended biographical sketch \- also, pen sketch of my head that he had asked for \- then to Keppel's \+ look at some old etchings \- then to Wunderlich's \+ saw Beckwith sketches. also a shocking print from the old Cousins plate of Master Lambton \- done in colors \- it was a dreadful specimen of a dreadful fad \- I am quite sure that the colored print must have been taken recently \- then to studio \- there until after 6 \- wetting paper \+ preparing for more printing to-morrow. Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g. a little reading to Ralph \- \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 28, 1900

**Weather:** Cloudy \+ humid  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio soon after 9 \+ to work printing \- I tested my ink, ground fresh, on small portrait proof. \+ then took three p'f's. from Mad. Sq. tower \+ two from the Road to the lake, on some old French paper that I got from among some of A. H. Baldwin's effects \- Home to lunch at 1 \- at 2 all hands went to the Murray Hill Theatre \+ saw Lord Chumley \- it was mildly amusing \+ tolerably well done \- out at 5 \- I went to studio \+ was there until 6.30 clearing up \+ preparing paper for printing to-morrow \- then home to dinner \- Evening, headachy, but I spent some time over studio bl\+ acct's. To bed at 10.30.
