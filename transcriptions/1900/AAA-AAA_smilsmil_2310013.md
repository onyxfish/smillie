## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 6, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day — various matters kept me at home until after 10 — then I went to Engineers' Club. 5th Ave. — near 35th St. — \+ saw Mr. Manuel \+ had a talk with him — he will call at studio this p.m. — then to 2? Nat'l. Bk \+ paid loan of $750 made on the 20th of last Nov. Then to studio at 11 \+ to work on pen sketch — portrait of self — for Am. Soc. of Biography — Home to lunch at 1.30. — A younger bre of Fred Stahn came just as I finished lunch \+ I ret'd to studio with him \+ gave him Stahn's plates in my possession. Then I worked on pen portrait as long as I could see — Manuel came after 4 \+ I explained what I wanted in the way of re-arrangement of Fables of figures that he had prepared for Treas'y. Nat. Report — It was some time before we could reach a solution, but we got it at last much to my satisfaction — He left at about 6 — I had a short walk \+ home before dinner at 6.30 — Jim \+ Ralph dined at S. W. Cook's — Edith Holt \+ her bro. Rob't. dined informally with us \+ after dinner all hands, except me, — Mary \+ her girls, Kate — Elkin \+ wife — Holt \+ sister — went to the Opera. I was alone until the boys ret'd — before 9 o'clk — To bed early.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 7, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Elkins ret'd to New Haven.

A beautiful, mild day — up at 7 — had my scrub — back to bed — bkfst. after 9 o'clk. — Soon after 11 I started out with the boys — Rode up to Central Park \+ then walked up 5th Ave. — my object being to make observations with reference to the municipal Site for M. A., — a question to be discussed at a com. meeting This p.m. at Ward's Studio — we made the circuit of the Park — \+ looked at Morningside Park \+ Manhattan Sq. We got home before 2 — Dinner — At 3.30 W. \+ Katharine Elkin left for New Haven \+ I went to Ward's — Beckwith \+ Brown also there. We were all of a mind after some discussion — Brown seems to have given up his idea of building a bare gallery on the 110th St. site. I made a very rough sketch of my idea of a gate at 59th St. \+ 7th Ave., entrance to Park, that is to furnish the reason for building met a there and it seemed to be approved — at any rate, it was agreed that I am to see Hastings to-morrow and interest him if possible \+ get some slight tentative sketches from him — It was about 5.30 when we adjourned — I walked home — tea at 6.30 — Sunday, some reading in news paper \+ to bed early.
