## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 24, 1900

**Weather:** Dark, dark m[illegible] - clearing to muggy sunshine  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark, dark m[illegible] - clearing to muggy sunshine - a very unreasonable day - Went to studio at usual time - Tried to get Geo. Carleton Comstock on telephone - it was not until near 11 o'clk. that I got him - asked him if he wanted to buy Montrose property at $9000 - Said that he would like to talk to his wife about it \+ would call to see me Wed. P.M. - Then I went to D. B. Butler's - Then to Horton's - There to Putnam's \+ spent some time looking at books - Home to lunch at 1.30 - at 2.30 went to Vantine's \+ spent some time in selecting a number of inexpensive little things for Xmas presents - Then to Putnam's \+ got some books \+ went home after 6 o'clk. with my arms full of pkgs. - Silleck took my framed painting "Oct Pasture with Sheep" \+ a note to W. Jas Herbert Morse - This wipes out my indebtedness to him on acct. of Jim's Schooling - Dinner at 6.30 - Some little excitement in arranging for the exposing of Xmas presents to-morrow night that soon subsided -

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 25, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Much pleasanter day than yesterday, being bright \+ cool. Went down to bkfst after 8 - Then to Parlor where the Xmas presents were unpacked - that ceremony over I went to Charls' - \+ then to studio - nearly 11 when I got there - Charl \+ Geo. came in bet. 12 \+ 1 o'clk - to see my cartoon of "the baby River" - to lunch soon after 1 - about 3 o'clk I went to 75th S. \+ called on Mrs. Gester - Saw her \+ her son John \+ had a very pleasant half hour - Then walked thro' Central Park to 8th Ave. \+ 57th S. \+ down Bdwy to 55th. \+ called on J. C. Nicoll - Home at 6 - Geo.'s family \+ Chas. with us at dinner. - After dinner, about 8.30, Bessie, Jim \+ I went to Walter's by invitation - a Progressive Card (Hearts) party - about 25 present, all members of family but Mr. Smieth - Katherine Coat's fiancee - and Mr. \+ Mrs. Frank Brownell. - Catherine Ireland there - The eve't passed pleasantly till 11 - Then home.
