## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 16, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable mt. with a very little rain but clearing before 11 - sunny & bright - My headache pursued me thro' the night - at 3 a.m. I got up & took a dose of Caffiem - at 5 I took Acetanilide & Caffe & at 7 o'clk another dose - I was rather jagged when I got up at 7.30 but was able to go to the N.A. at 10 9-55 after bkft. - I had to wait for an hour for carpenters & plumbers - They came at last - miller bringing all his stuff & Ryan's man bringing his. We at once got to work & I was kept busy laying out the work for them until 12 - Then they went to dinner & I walked up to Columbia University - from the top of the great flight of steps up to the library building I surveyed my pet site at the Boulevard or west end of the block opposite & my opinion is unchanged that on no other available site would there have been so fine architectural possibilities for the N.A. of D. - Then I went into & through the library building & sighed that I could not be a boy with unlimited capabilities for study & gathering knowledge - It was all immensely attractive & stimulating to me -
Home at 1.30 & had lunch - after that I gave up to my headache & spent the afternoon on my bed - Dinner at 6.30 - Wef. after my newspaper I wrote to Scott Brown - Calenaun - Central Iron works & Pomfrat School - at 10 went to G.C. Station to post letters -

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 17, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautiful day - Got to studio about 10 - set the mf. biting 2d. Etching in "the Road to the lake". Home to lunch at 1.30 - back at 2.30 & worked with graver until 4.30 then home & from 5 to 5.30 rested - there dressed & at 6.30 went to Edwd. Cook's & dined with the family, miss Keist of Buffalo - a young friend of Catherine Cook's whose name I did not catch & Jno. Valentine - later Mr. Rogers, one of Jino's teachers at Moses' School, came in & we had a chatty time until near 10. Edwd. Cook gave me some information concerning Ireland Real Estate Co. affairs that is not very encouraging - On reaching home I found W. Edgar Tirrell trying to entertain Mary & her daughters - Mary looking determined & the girls looking rather disconsolate — Blood, or tears, I could not tell which, seemed to stand in Edgar's eyes.
