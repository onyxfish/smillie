## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Suffocating mt. — thunderstorm late in the afternoon — Cool eve'g. Much of mt. with Webb directing the putting up of a way to get into bedrooms over dining room by an incline over ice house \+ butler's pantry to the balcony. It was after 11 when I went to hotel — to store — to blacksmith shop — I worked a little upon plans \+ wrote some notes — but it was hard to do any work the heat was so enervating I had a nap late in the afternoon — the storm came about 6 but was of short duration. Eve'g Reading newspapers until 9 —

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 4, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day — brilliant \+ bracing — superb moonlight night. Webb worked most of the mt. on the inclined plane over ice house and butler's pantry to annex bedrooms — P.M. — on odd jobs — I paid him $105. nearly the full amt. that I owe him. Ray worked clearing \+ burning the brush from out the woods. I went to hotel \+ before 12 — met the elder Dr. Janeway \+ sat for a while with him on his porch. Dinner at 1. It was nearly 4 before I got through bossing Webb — Then Ralph \+ I (Jim was complaining) started to climb the rocky shoulder north of Reeds brook. It was a short but hard pull \+ the view was fine — I had Charl's camera \+ altho' the sun was low I took a shot — we got down just as the sun set behind the mt. Home at 6 — changed underclothes \+ rested until tea time — Eve'g feeling very sleepy I went to bed early. The family enjoyed moonlight on south piazza.
