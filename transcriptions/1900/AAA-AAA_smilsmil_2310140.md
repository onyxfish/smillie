## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 21, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charl & the girls went to Placid.

Some heavy showers about breakfast time. Then it cleared suddenly — very mild — Charl & the girls very undecided — Hoelt came with his team — C. decided to go \+ soon after 10 he, Mary, her girls & Kate started for Lake Placid to return to-morrow — The m'f. continued clear — but heavy showers came up about 1 o'clk., with thunder, and continued for an hour or more — Then cleared again. \+ so continued — I got to work immediately after bkft. painting a sketch of the Grant from my loggia \+ stuck to it tho' many interruptions until 1½ — Then to hotel — met Miss Hutcheson \+ she played for miss miller some the songs she composed for the little farce given last Tues. evef. She recited the words \+ Miss Howell \+ sang as well as played some of them — It was vastly more enjoyable than the performances at the Casino — I think her a very clever \+ interesting girl — The boys went again to the Bonaker auction, got home to dinner late — while at dinner Johnny Otis came with the load of lumber from the Forks — just as a shower was coming up — it was raining heavily before it was all got under cover — Adams worked around most of the day — So did Ray. I worked on my m't. sketch most of the afternoon, in studio — Schilling came about 5 \+ stayed until 6.30. I did not show him what I had been painting because I knew that that sort of work w'd not interest him —
Tea with the boys at 6.30 — Evef. I read aloud to them — The Course of the Cachalot until 9.30 —

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 22, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charl & girls returned from Placid.

A little pleasant weather in the morning but there were showers in the afternoon — There was a succession of them — altogether it was a disagreeable day to be out — but I saw many very fine sky \+ storm effects from my windows — In the m'f., with Ray to help me I got the new lot of lumber up on the north piazza \+ am disappointed to find that in many respects it is poor stuff \+ not at all what I ordered. The boys \+ I had a very quiet \+ enjoyable little dinner — After it I went to hotel — then to library — then to Scott Brown's — saw him \+ Mr. B — They said they would keep me for a day or two after my family leaves, if I wish it — At 5 o'clk the Placid party returned all writing in assurances that they had had a delightful time Supper at 6.30
