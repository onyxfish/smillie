## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 22, 1900

**Weather:** Beautiful day \+ mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

Immediately after bkfs. I went up to D. Yale's as I think that my vaccination of 11 days ago is going to take, has taken or is took. I waited for more than half an hour but he had a patient in his room \+ when I ceased to be patient I "let out" \+ went to my studio where I worked all the m[orning] on Charcoal composition on 33×44. Home at 1 \- lunch \+ then to N.A.f.D. \+ met my Etching class. I worked with them till 4.30, biting three of the best etchings, leaving four untouched \- Sterne had a clever etching that I left him biting. It is yet too early to judge of the "stuff" my class is made of \- two members interest me \+ I am hopeful. Walked all the way home \- about four miles. Got in at 6 \- dinner at 6.30 \- Evey May, hergives \+ Kate went to Charls. I stayed at home with my newspaper \+ accts.

At noon I Rec. a letter from Mrs. Kent saying that she does not want the Morris house \+ the Swants lot \+ offering me $55.00 for the Remainder \- She is too smart for me \- I don't want to bother with any Montrose Remains \- but suppose that the wisest thing to do will be to accept her last offer so promptly that there will be no chance to modify it or make a new one.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 23, 1900

**Weather:** Disagreeable day \- mild but raw \+ foggy \- Bkfs.  **Temp:** [illegible]

at 9.30 \- after that went to D. Yale's \- showed him my arm \+ he said it was a belated "take" \+ then home \- Read newspapers for a while \- then to Charl's \+ consulted with him about Mrs. Kent's latest offer for the Montrose house. I think he has some rather wild ideas of what I may get for the house \- nevertheless his counsel is helpful to me. Dinner at 2 \- later in the afternoon I took my usual Sunday nap \- Then I worked on my cash book until tea time \- after that an hour of Reading \- then Resumed work on acct. book \+ continued until 11.
