## Left Page

### Sat. March 31, 1900

**Weather:** Fine day, growing to be cold \+ windy in the afternoon  **Temp:**

To studio's at 9.30 — got ready for printing — worked for awhile in dif[f]ericalt head — experimenting with mezzotint rocker \+ then using graver — Took pf. from it \+ from Mr. G. head — at 12.30 went to 2°. Nat. Bw — home to lunch — To studio — more printing — ill luck seems to follow the Mr. G. head. I cannot get a satisfactory pf. from it — but I did get a very encouraging pf. from the Lippincott head. At 5 o'clk. — washed up — \+ then walked up 5th. Ave to Central Park \+ home to dinner. The cold \+ wind very disagreeable. Eve'g, with newspaper \+ magazine.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 1, 1900

**Weather:** A charming spring day — bright \+ mild  **Temp:**

Got up late \+ did not get my skfs. till 2. 10 o'clk. Between 11 or 2 the boys \+ I went to Central Park — walked thro' the green houses \+ 106th S. \+ down to 96th S. where we took Mad. Ave. cars \+ got home a few min. before 2. Dinner — read for an hour — then a nap — then sometime skeet over European photos. At 6 walked with Ge[o]. over to 44th S. \+ 6th Ave. Met Tom Passenden in the street \+ had a chat with him. Home to tea at 6.45 — Eve'g. maps of Europe with lines of travel followed by Mary \+ Kate were brought out — \+ photos \+ they were discussed — then I wrote to Charl — \+ to bed.
