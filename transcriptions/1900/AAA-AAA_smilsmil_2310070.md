## Left Page

### Fri. May 4, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliantly beautiful, cool \+ breezy, with rain at night. I spent some time at home writing \+ did not get to studio until after 10 — commenced the Etching of Century Sp. Libns - worked until 1 o'clk - then to 2d. Nat'l. Bank \+ home to lunch at 1.30 — Then to studio much of the afternoon was spent in writing a long letter to Jim - I worked for an hour or more on plate — had a short walk \+ home to dinner. Ev'g Ralph \+ I went to Am. Theatre — \+ saw heard Strauss' operetta, "A Night in Venice" a silly libretto with some very pleasing music \+ a little dancing — Gertrude Quinlan made an unnecessarily dreadful Guy of herself as [illegible] \+ Moulau was amusing as Palpacoda — I was disappointed, — We walked home in a pelting rain getting in about 11.30

## Right Page

### Sat. May 5, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright \+ pleasant — went to Ed.d. Cook's immediately after b'kf't. \+ had a short talk with him concerning Ireland Real Estate matters - to studio \+ worked all day etching Century Exbn's plate - Home to lunch at 1.30 \+ back in an hour - worked until 5 \+ got the work all drawn in - Gel came in for awhile - then home \+ rested until dinner at 6.30 — Ev'g - at 8.10 went to Century — had a long talk with W. Harrison, of Cutler's School - Raekli's teacher, about the boy — he had much to say that was pleasant to hear \+ some things not so pleasant — the most serious faults being slow \+ careless work. Later I talked with Fred Ireland about Jim — He encourages me to hope for the best — Jim, he says, may not be a brilliant scholar, but he has confidence to believe that he has qualities that will bring him out right in the end. Home at 11 o'clk.
