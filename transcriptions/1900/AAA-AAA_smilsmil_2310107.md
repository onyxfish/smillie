## Left Page

### Tues. July 17, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very hot day. Again up early - 6.30 - wrote to Jos. P. Ryan \+ spent some time working over balancing my acct with First Natl. Bk of Montrose. B\&P. at 8.30 - about 10.30 when we went to house after receiving letters by our mail from Mary \+ Jim - Carpenters at work on stairway from No. Piazza leading down to annex. Adams \+ bro finished papering studio. To lunch after 1 - After I got back to the house Adams \& bro. were struggling to get burlaps on Maine Room ceiling - it is over 5 feet wide \+ difficult to handle but I helped to work out the problem \+ they got along fairly well. About 5 o'clk Beede's double team came with a ton \+ a half of pea coal - brought "egg" before - too large - now too small - built a big fire in range to test the coal which seemed to work well. To dinner at 7 - Hotel is now filling up very fast - lots of boys \+ girls. Robt. Nielson \+ family arrived - About most of the even on Piazza - a fine thunder storm between 10 \+ 11 P. M.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 18, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m'f, with several heavy showers - clearing in the afternoon to brilliantly breezy \+ cool weather. Up at 6.30 \+ soon after 7 went to the house to see about the fire in the range which I found had died out without consuming all the coal. The water in the boiler was warm but it wd. not run warm in my bedroom. I fear that a change in that part of the plumbing will be necessary. The burlaps put up on the ceiling yesterday has all sagged down in the damp air - this led me to adopt a new plan - that of dampening the burlaps \+ having it shrink tight after it is tacked in place. To B\&P. soon after 8 - To house again at 10.30 - The work went on as usual all day - to lunch at 1.30 - back about 3 - dinner at 7.30 every. Loafing about Piazzas \+ writing in my room. Called up Kellogg on telephone \+ arranged with him to care for my family on its arrival at his house to-morrow eveg.
