## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 27, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another dull day + threats that came to nought - air quite mild - At home writing until 11 o'clk. Then to studio - got out an old canvas 25 × 40 + until lunch time worked upon it with charcoal planning a composition based upon lines of Keene valley as seen from my studio window. After lunch, headachy + stupid, I took a nap + then to studio + again worked for awhile on charcoal sketch of the mt. - Had a walk before I went home to dinner - We lf. about 8, went with Ralph to the Bryan demonstration at Madison Sq. + went over much the same ground that we did last night, finding things not very different - a great crowd, largely corlieu, out to see sights - no enthusiasm - constant movement - an aimless wandering about - the illumination of Mad. Sq. Garden tower with showers of fire works from the top was very fine - the electric lights strung from naval arch to the columns up + down 5th Ave. was fine - the bombs + colored fire + fire works were much the same as last night - as a show taken all together, it was rather ahead of last night - but the parade was contemptible - a display of hoodlums + roughs to make one ashamed - Ralph + I wandered about for nearly 1 hour perhaps - then home + to bed - We did not get into the garden as no one up to 11 o'clk. was allowed near the bldg. unless he had a ticket.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 28, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull wtf. - a little rain in the afternoon. I did not get up until after 8 - + did not get my disp't until 10 - loafed with newspapers until near 1 - Then Ralph + I went out for a short walk but we were soon driven in by a mizzle of rain - dinner at 2 - After that, feeling something of the effects of last nights spree, I had a good, restful nap. Geo. + Chat came in late in the afternoon - tea at 6.30 - Evelf. writing.
