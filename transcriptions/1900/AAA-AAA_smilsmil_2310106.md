## Left Page

### Sun. July 15, 1900

**Weather:** cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy day — still \+ close — growing cooler at night — Up at about 7 — had a sponge bath — Then out \+ had a short walk on the road to the lake — met W. Storbenstein \+ returned with him. Bkft. soon after 8 — at about 10 went to house — got blankets, sheets \+ things into linen closet — wrote a letter to Mary — To hotel at 1.30 — dinner — wrote a short note to Jim — tone to Cooley \+ son Montrose directing them to repair gutters \+ leaders — a note just recd from W. E. A. Kent states that only a small part of the water from the roofs of house \+ barns on Montrose place gets into the cisterns owing to defects and stoppages — After dinner Ralph was throttled by one of the boys in rough sport \+ hurt — I took him to room with me \+ after reading to him we both fell asleep — After 5 we had a short walk before supper — Evef. loitering with newspaper until 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. July 16, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The day opened cloudy — some thunder \+ quite a heavy shower — then it cleared intensely hot — Ther. 92° on piazza — Up early \+ wrote letter to Mrs. Kent \+ other notes — It was nearly 9 before R. \+ I had bkft. It was 10.30 before I got to house — Ray Moodey, the new young man, at work clearing up. Adams \+ bro. finished putting sheathing paper on wall of main room \+ commenced on studio wall — Buck \+ Webb were at work. no word from Mary or Jim — I was very tired when I got to hotel for dinner but did not feel exhausted as I have at other times. Evef. the usual loafing \+ newspapers, which is getting very tiresome — To bed soon after 10 — our bed room very hot — over 80° — altho' it was pleasantest cool outside.
