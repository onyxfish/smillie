## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 29, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — to studio about 10 o'clk. \+ commenced painting on 25×40 — the subject that I charcoaled on Sat. The canvas is an old one upon which I had already painted — \+ over that a grey tone — I had intended to put in a thin light color sketch using turpentine \+ oil but it didn't work well on the surface \+ I found myself painting a thin semi-opaque work — neither one thing or the other. However I stuck to it — getting just near enough to what I was trying to do to keep me very much interested — After 12 I went to Bank \+ deposited Provant-yke's check — Went to Scribner hoping to see Mr. Marvin — he was out — then home to lunch — again to studio \+ worked until 4.30 — painted in sky \+ cut down to valley floor line almost — then walked over to W. 42d St. to see Frank Jones — not in — then to Century Club — not there — I did not go in — Sauntered about until after 6 — then home — dinner — Eve\+ I was preparing to get to bed early when Charlie came in \+ the stayed until 10.30 —

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 30, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m\+, altho' overcast — a mizzle in the eve\+ — just enough to make the walks damps. To studio at 10 \+ got to work at once on 25"×40". "An Adirondack Valley" I think I shall call it. I went back over a part of yesterdays work \+ then down on to the middle ground \+ foreground. \+ by 4.30 I had the canvas covered. Like every thing else that I do it is a great disappointment to me I had hoped to do something so much better — now I am hoping that in repainting it I shall do better — home to lunch as usual — after I cleaned up in the afternoon \+ when I was ready to go out for a stroll I sat down in my big chair \+ fell asleep — after sleeping for half an hour or more I waked — took a short walk — then home — dinner — Eve\+ with newspapers \+ washing brushes — to bed early.
