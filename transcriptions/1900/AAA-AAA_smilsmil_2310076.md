## Left Page

### Wed. May 16, 1900

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast until late in the afternoon \+ quite breezy \+ cool \- so that I had to put on my heavy suit again. After bkfst. wrote notes that kept me until 10 when I got to studio. Then I finished Tracing D. VanDyke's ex-lib. design by Mowbray \- scraped it \+ laid ground on plate \- Geo. came in \+ sat for a while \- Home to lunch at 1 \- After that I went to Lee \+ dee's \- 20 Nassau St. by their request \- got there at 2.30 \- Dielman \+ de Forest had been there in the mt. \- I could not learn that I was wanted for anything in particular farther than to impress it upon my mind that I must be in court to-morrow at 10.30 as the N.A. f D. Tax case will probably come up there \- So I hurried to my work again getting to studio at about 4 o'clk \- I commenced the etching of the Van D \- ex-lib. \+ worked from hour on more \- then went to Bro. G's in W. 44th St. \+ saw him about being in court to-morrow. Home at 6.30 \- dinner. Every Geo. Cook came in \- he has been putting any individual accts. into book keepers shape \+ he brought the books along for my suggestions \- He accepts Jno. Ireland's dedication of his books \+ I said that I wd. pay him on that basis if he would give me time \- He assented \-

Mary continues to improve slowly \- she suffered very much last night from throat's pain.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 17, 1900

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast \- chilly east wind \- a nizzle about 11 o'clk \- At Court, City Hall Park, at 10.30 \- N.A. of D. vs. Tax Com. \- Dielman \- de Forest \- Geo. \+ I were there \- Before 11 the case was reached \+ the judge wouldn't try it \- had too many other cases. It must be tried before a Referee \- so, we all left \- I went at once to studio, laid grd. on Cautuing plate \- Traced \+ transferred May nearly name \+ mink \- drew them on the ground \+ varnished plate \- Then to work on etching of Van Dyke's ex-lib. \- Home to lunch at 1 \- Kate, the girls, Ralph \+ Philip Babcock went to Murray Hill Theatre \+ I went to studio \- worked on VanD. \- Plate until 4 o'clk \- then walked up to Carnegie Blg. 57th St. \- Howard \+ Butler not in \- Then to 25th Anniversary fgtn. of the Art Students League. There until 5.30 \- walked down to Century \+ stopped there for a few minutes \- Home at 6. Geo. \+ Charlt. were there \- Dinner at 6.30 \- The event went somehow \- nothing was done \-

Mary continues to improve slowly \- she was lifted from her bed into a chair where she sat for a little while \- has less pain but cannot move herself.
