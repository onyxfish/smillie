## Left Page

### Thur. July 19, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A superb day - cloudless, brilliant, breezy \+ cool. \- That is, cool for a July day - the Ther. got up to near 80° at midday. - I got up soon after 6 - wrote a letter to Charl in answer to his of 13th inst. - I hope that this will end the correspondence. - Soon after 9 I went to the house - There until near 8. - Then to hotel to bkft. Again to house \+ spent the m'f there every effort being directed to a cleaning up to get things in readiness for the coming of the family to-morrow. - Black worked on the steps \+ flight of steps from North Piazza \+ the rough walk to the alley. - Webb jobbing around \+ assisting. - To lunch at 1.30 \+ back at 3. - Adams finished putting up Burlaps on ceiling \+ commenced painting outside. - I had Ray Moody clean up all the ruins inside of the house \+ started a new fire in the range. - It was 6.30 when I got to the hotel very tired. - At 7.30 when I got my dinner. - At 8.30 I got many on the telephone at S.-town \+ from her the report that all were well \+ happy. - Then Beede came to me \+ wanted $1000 he being in straits. - I looked over the vouchers he had to show me \+ then I gave him a check for $500.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 20, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Family arr'd. from New-York City.

Begin our abode in the house at Pine Knoll
Clear hot, m't. soon clouding \+ giving threat of change of weather - up before 7. but it was not until about 10 that I got to the house. Every effort was made to get things in shape \+ be ready for the coming of the family. - It was about 11.30 when Ralph \+ I went back to hotel expecting the family to come there - we were waiting for them when Jim came in \+ said that all were at the house having driven directly there. - I went right back to the house \+ found them wandering about all in fairly good condition but Bessie. - They w'd not return to hotel with me to lunch as there was food enough in the kitchen - So R. \+ I went back \+ got our lunch. - After that we packed our trunks - then heavy showers came up. - It was about 4 o'clk. when, between showers I went back to house. - It was almost 6 when the trunks of entire family arr'd from hotel \+ were carried to the 2d story three. - Jim, Ralph \+ I set to hotel \+ dined I then I announced my departure at the office. - The beds had been made, the lamps trimmed \+ all hands seemed content. - We spent the ev'g in the little S.W. Cor. room on 1st floor \+ the travellers went to bed early. - I had a good light in my room \+ read newspaper until after 10 o'clk. Then to bed.
