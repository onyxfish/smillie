## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very pleasant day \- laid ground \+ made gelatine transfer for Mr. G. head \- then commenced the etching. My points do not do any more work as I want them to \- do I require more of them or is my hand losing to Trick? Home to lunch as usual \- went to Central Iron works \+ left drawings for irons for A. & S. Etching Studio \+ to Millers with drawing for a table for No. I worked on my etching until 4.30 then home \- dressed \+ went to afternoon tea \- Mr. & Mrs. Villard, given at Sherry's \- 5th Ave. \+ 45th St. quite a swell affair \- many there whom I knew \- so that to me it was unexpectedly pleasant \- Late when I got home to dinner. Soon after 8 I went to a Star party at the opening of Water Color Exh'n at the Waldorf Astoria Hotel \- small, uninvitable rooms lighted by artificial light only \- very crowded \+ stuffy \- I stayed until about 10, then with Waltons I walked up to Century \- monthly meeting \- Time passed pleasantly until after 12. Then walked home. Chds. Collins \+ Loyall Farragut accompanying me \- To bed at 1 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 4, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull m'g \- the middle of the day delightfully mild \+ sunny \- Rain before night \+ heavy rain \+ wind nearly all night. I got up at 9 o'clk, feeling so seedy after my dissipation of yesterday that I did not feel strong enough to take my regular scrub \+ rub. After 11 Ralph \+ I walked up to Central Park \+ sauntered about there for a while \- I did not feel like physical work. Called at Beckwith's but he was out. Home at 1. Dinner at 2. Had a long nap in the afternoon \- tea at 6.30. Eve'g reading aloud to Ralph \+ writing.
