## Left Page

### Thur. June 21, 1900

**Weather:** Charming day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day - Got Wanamaker's bill - no fine irons on it - wrote note to Mr. Bispham - Then to Wanamakers \+ found irons in storage - had that matter attended to \+ then after some delay got things arranged by telephone with Whitbeck of H.R. freight, Haywood bro's, \+ Mr. Ditmar of W's freight terminal where my car is to be \+ W. is to load it. Then to studio - Then home - Then to Miller's - Then to Borrman's \+ Rode my bicycle home. Cases came from Miller's \+ I commenced the packing before lunch. After lunch carpenter came with crate \+ put burlaps in it - Jennings crated my bicycle - I worked hard all the afternoon until 5 o'clk. Annie Ballard called on Mary \+ I sat with her for awhile - then rested until 6.30 Dinner. Eve'g. I packed my easel \+ marked all of the crates \+ boxes - Charl came in - I finished at 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. June 22, 1900

**Weather:** A very hot day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day - Up at 7 o'clk - Truckmen came soon after \+ before 8 everything had been sent over to the freight car. Bkfst. at 8 - about 9 I went to the Terminal Warehouse. 11th Ave., 27th \+ 28th St. \+ spent nearly an hour there with Mr. Ditmar who is in charge. I found a large "furniture car" on the siding \+ several men packing my goods into it. It was near 11 when I got home. I felt very tired \+ needing rest - so I darkened my room \+ rested until lunch at 1 - After that I rode to the D.L. \+ W. R.R. office on B'wy \+ secured two parlor seats for next Monday - Then to studio - There until near 5 - Men home - wrote letter to Jim. Dinner at 6.30. Eve'g Ralph \+ I went to 42nd St. - found Charl at the house on our return.
