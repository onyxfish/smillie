## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 15, 1900

**Weather:** Bright & pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & pleasant day \- Bkft. at 8. Ralph commenced at Cutler's school for the winter. I went up to Dr. Yale's & was with him for an hour & a half \- as usual, we talked of every thing except what I went to talk about \- that is, Jim \- & my nose. From Y's I went to Dept. of Health \- 6th Ave. & 56th St. to complain of the nuisance that my neighbors in Sniffen Court make at the cor. of my building by piling all sorts of garbage \- ashes etc ag°. it \- met with little attention \- one uninterested official passing on to another equally uninterested \- then I went down town \- to 62 Reade St. and called at office of Cambridge Ency. & Pub. Co. about the Koehler work \- but the manager Mr. Delmar was not in \- then to Tax office \- then to Street Cleaning Dept. Park Row \- and there in complaint Dept. met an official \- a woman \- who treated me courteously & gave civil attention to my complaints ag°. Sniffen Court \- She promised to send an inspector to me to-morrow \- then to Lee & Lee \- saw very fat. Fixes on 38th St. house \- Home to lunch at 1.30 afternoon went to studio \- dreadfully dirty \- accomplished but little \- Home at 6 \- dinner at 6.30 \- Mary, Ralph & I — K. B. & Mrs. Kellogg at Chase's to dinner \- Eve'g with Ivory Post & Ralph's arithmetic.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 16, 1900

**Weather:** Bright & mild until about 4 p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright & mild until about 4 p.m. when a squall came up very suddenly with a gust of wind & considerable rain \- the eve'g much cooler \- clear & windy \- Got up with a very heavy headache that yielded to drugs \- Immediately after bkft. an inspector from Street Cleaning Dept. came with a foreman, in answer to my visit to the Dept. yesterday \- both intelligent men \- we went to the studio & saw the condition of things at the cor. of my bldg. in the alley \- to my surprise the inspector gave instructions to the foreman to have the collection of ashes & garbage go up into the alley \- while we were together a policeman from the Health Dept. came & going from stable to stable in the court directed them all to provide proper receptacles for ashes & garbage \- each one for his own premises \- If this is enforced I will not have such trouble as I have had \- Wrote to Scott Brown \- then to Miller's about hardware for keene \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- After lunch went to Russell & Erwin's chainses St. about expensive locks sent to Keene \- $3.40 each \- with knobs etc. got $6.57 for each door \- the door cost only $1.50 each \- but the salesman declined to make any terms to take them back \- he showed me a 50¢ catch that would have done better than the locks \- had to run for elevated train \- got in just as shower came \- then to 42nd St. & grand Central \- & 4th Ave. car to 38th St. \- I did not get wet \- dinner at 6.30 \- eve'g with newspapers & then assorting bills & writing.
