## Left Page

### Thur. April 26, 1900

**Weather:** Brilliant + breezy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant + breezy — It was near 10 when I got to studio, detained by chatting with Uncle Willie — To work at once trying to get a good pf. from Mr. Q. head for Dr. Q. — much disappointed by results — I made a change in the eyes yesterday to please Dr. Q. + now I regret it very much — I could not get a sound, full pf. for some reason — probably because the biting days were not in good condition — I was much depressed + feel disposed to destroy the plate — it is all good enough but a portion of the shaded side of the face, that has been worked over until it is "rotten" — it is mealy + unsound + textureless + spoils the whole picture. my watch stopped at 12.50 — I did not know when to go to lunch — Ralph came for me at 2 o'clk — + I went home with him + had a late lunch — After that I went to Knotty for photo. Reduction of Maynard + Mowbray des'igns of course, they were not done yet — back to studio \+ fussed about there in a dejected state of mind until after 6 — then home — dinner at 6.30 — Evef. with Uncle Willie going over ancient history by the light of father's "Memoriam" + "My Pilgrimage".

## Right Page

### Fri. April 27, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Uncle W. + Aunt Agnes left for Washington D.C.

Fine day. At studio I got out plans for Keene house + upon tracing paper made sketch for piazza at south end extending around on the east side — It was about noon when I went to Walter Coaks, W. 29th St. showed him my sketch + asked him to have a full size drawing made for a turned column for piazza — he promised to do so. Home at 1.5 + had lunch — Afternoon went to N.A.D. to meet Etching class — Steene there + five young men of the school — under Stone's direction they had made drawings on zinc plates from an old man model — I mixed acid \+ bit four of the five plates — It kept me on the jump until almost 6 o'clk — then walked across upper part of Park + home by road. Car in time for dinner — Evef. I wrote a long letter to him — Charl came in + sat in my room for some time chatting — at 10.30 I went with him to his house for a few minutes — then to G. C. Station + mailed letter at 10.50 —
