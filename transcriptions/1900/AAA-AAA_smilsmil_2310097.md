## Left Page

### Wed. June 27, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant hot \+ breezy day - a very little shower last night. I wrote a letter to Jim before Bkft. After Bkft. I went up to the house \+ had a long interview with Spense \+ settled my acct. to date by paying $40. Then I saw Mrs. Kent \+ before 12 went to foundry \+ saw Sprout - no I didn't - it was just before 6 that I saw him. I went downtown before 12 \+ transacted business with various parties \+ to dinner at Sayres at 1 — had my usual after dinner nap. Then up to my shop \+ got a boy ready to pack dark room outfit. Having learned that Sprout had gone back on his promise to rent a cow to Mr. K — I went to the foundry and interviewed him to such effect that he changed his mind \+ said that Mrs. K — could have the cow. Then to other places \+ to Sayres to tea at 6.30. Then again downtown - wrote a note to Cashw. Case \+ sent Mrs. Kents check for $200 pay't on acct. of rent, to 2nd Natl. for deposit. After 8 I went to Mrs. Kent's \+ told her about the cow - then to Morris's \+ agreed to fix roof - to Sayres about 9 - \+ to bed at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 28, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot morning - but I did a good deal of running around - down town \+ up on the hill. I went to the bank \+ saw Warner - to the R.R. station to make enquiries about D. H.H. trains \+ to Conklin about taxes - \+ then to Sayres for dinner at 1. after that I had my nap. at 3 o'clk I met W. Cooley by appointment \+ went to the Morris house \+ after looking at the roof over extension I gave orders to put a metal covering over it - got back to Sayre's just before a very heavy thunder storm broke - the wind was tremendous \+ did much damage - the hard maple at the S.W. corner of our house was split near the ground \+ nearly a third of it broken off. During the storm I wrote part of a letter to Mary. When it passed I went again to the house \+ finished packing box of photo. outfit. Then downtown at 5.30 - went to bank \+ then with Warner went to village Hall \+ inspected that - left him at 6 - tea at 6.30 - at 7.30 again went to R.R. station to make further enquiries about trains - After I got back to Sayres spent balance of eve'g. with Mary Sayres \+ Miss Kelly, Stolesbury's trained nurse.
