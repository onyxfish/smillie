## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 18, 1900

*Char came.*

**Weather:** Cool day - cloudy m'f - cloudy at  **Temp:** [illegible]

sunset with showers north of us. The m'f was absent in putting things to rights generally. went to hotel at noon - Char arr'd. by private conveyance about 1 o'clk. Soon after we had a frugal dinner, the butcher having failed to put in an appearance. Afternoon with Ray part of the time setting out young evergreens on the line of the old road up the hill. Buck \+ Webb at work on cold room - Adams painting roof of Surrey \+ Icehouse. Otis came \+ I made a bargain with him to continue his work on road up to the front door - $140. Ev'f. I went to the library hoping to be able to get VanDyke's "Fisherman's duck" but it was out - Then to hotel where a prestidigitateur was amusing the guests Ralph was there \+ after the performance we ret'd. home

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 19, 1900

**Weather:** Superb day  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- up at 7 had my scrub \+ bath. out at 8 o'clk \+ wandered over Windybrow and about until 9 - feeling something of the exhilaration of old times - after bkfs't. I sat for awhile \+ wrote It was about 11 when I went with Mary, Kathanie Ida Hoct, Char \+ Ralph thro' Ghost's Walk \+ out by Otis's to foot of Giant's leap - then along stream by trail towards Chappel Pond - we followed the stream until near 1 o'clk - then home after a most delightful \+ inspiriting walk. Dinner at 2 - before we had finished W. Maghee came - stayed until 3.30 - Then Kathanie \+ Ida \+ Char went with him on a stroll to his clearing on the west side of the river - they returned with loads of flowers from Ellis's garden \+ lettuce, cucumbers \+ a 12 lb. cabbage. In the mean time I was taking a nap. Tea at 6.30 - Ev'f. on piazza with Char \+ then in-doors enjoying fire on the hearth.

Jim seems to be getting along all right - good appetite \+ sleeps well. He walked up Beside brook for some distance this m'f.
