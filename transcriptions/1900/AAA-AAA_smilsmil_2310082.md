## Left Page

### Mon. May 28, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The eclipse of the sun This mf, was eclipsed by the clerk of the weather who spread his storm clouds so thickly that New-Yorkers had to be satisfied with imaginings. I had letters to write that kept me until 11 o'clk. Ge° came \+ got studio keys — I went to studio got plan of east wall of main room, Keane \+ took it to Walter \+ got some advise from him. back to studio at 12.30 — home to lunch at 1 — showers — back at 2.30 — the afternoon spent cleaning frames. cutting mounts \+ getting ph. of Mr. G. into frame. \+ Portrait of Jacobi partly framed. Home at 6.30 — letter from Jim complaining of indigestion, acidity etc. made up a box of Tilsans Tabules \+ mailed it to him. Nearly went to Ed" Cook's \+ saw Catharines books that she has bound. They seemed to me to be very creditable specimens. To Chas's at 10 \+ got from him roll of films that I exposed yesterday — home at 10. 45 \+ to bed

## Right Page

### Tues. May 29, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine clear, cool day — Ge° worked at studio. I did not get there until near 11 as I had letters to write — until after 12 I worked cutting mounts and getting ph. of Jacobi \+ Mr. G. into frames. There to 2°. Nat'l Bank — lunch. To studio at 2.30 \+ worked on Century \+ VanDyke plates until after 5. making changes as suggested by others. A short walk \+ home to dinner. Eve'f. Chas came.

After bkft this mf I went to Gales to consult him about Jim's condition. Did not get much satisfaction. He was in a hurry to go \+ seemed to be more disposed to talk about his photographs (of which he gave me two or three copies) than he was to talk about Jim.
