## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 25, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day \- ? covers \- worked all day at studio painting on Road to Wood lot \- with the result of discouraging myself \- I wish that I could be content, to give up trying to do what I can't do \- \+ can never learn to do \- Home to lunch \- back \+ painted as long as I could see \- then cleaned up palette \+ set the painting aside to dry for the next stage \- Then took a walk up \+ ave \- back in time for dinner \- rue \+ \- loafing.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 26, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day. Threatening rain but it didn't come \- no studio work done \- but writing letters \+ notes \- paying accts. etc \- To studio about 11 \- went to Second Nat'l. Bnt. about noon \+ arranged for a loan of $1800 to pay off indebtedness of one kind \+ another \-
Then home to lunch \- late afternoon I went to see Dr. Allen about Jim \- The interview was not an encouraging one for me \- his advice is just such as have been giving the boy \+ his prescriptions are just what the boy has been taking \+ such as I would have prescribed \- there is no new light or practice \- wrote a long letter to Jim \- at 5.30 by accident saw the arrival of R. C. Station of Teddy Roosevelt \- After dinner Bessie, Ralph \+ I went to Madison Sq. \- where the great Republican Rally took place \- We wandered all about until near 11 o'clk \- The crowd was immense but there was little [illegible] excitement \- no end of music, fireworks \+ speechifying \- little attention to latter \- The bomb fireworks were plentiful \+ very fine \- about 10 we were able to get into Mad. Sq. Garden. \- The crush was over but thousands were still there \+ speakers were still beating the air.
