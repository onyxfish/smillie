## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 28, 1900

**Weather:** Wet + disagreeable, clearing finely at night.  **Temp:**

To studio at usual time + worked on 33×44. Worked thro' them with some satisfaction but later, when I got down to the middle ground + the daylight was waning rapidly I got away off from the color I wanted + was obliged to leave it in a very unsatisfactory condition. Maynard got a telegram calling him to a sister in Rhode Island who has lost a daughter. — may be gone a week. — Worked until near 5 — then went to Friedrichs — 5th Ave. 48th S'. + paid for a canvas for Maynard. Home at 6 met Mary Hinshelwood there. — She + Jane are in trouble with the Cockrofts with whom they have been for so many years. Dinner at 6.30 — Evett. Charl came in — Jim dined + Edw'd Cook's + Haver went to a dance. Bessie went with Walter's young folks to Murray Hill Theatre.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 29, 1900

**Weather:** Bright, pleasant day, but pretty cold.  **Temp:**

To studio at usual time + worked on 33×44. I have already painted down to foreground on bare canvas — the foreground I rubbed over with a fratter of transparent colors suggestive of the scheme of color that I intend to paint. I also worked for a while upon an old, single sitting sketch painted in '69 — on the Ausable — It had some good work on it but parts were very bad + I greyed + cooled off those spots. Home to lunch at 1 — then to Na. f d. Etching class — spent the afternoon with them, laying grounds — walked home thro' Central Park. Dinner at 6.30 — Evett. went to Century and heard an address from Park Godwin upon the closing century — It was largely reminiscent + altho' he is 84 I found that I could remember almost as much as he could. I was amused as Eastman Johnson + Geo. Hall told to Beckwith + Chas. Collins of their first visit to Antwerp 49 years ago. Johnson was very funny — + I had an interesting conversation with Gov. Haven Putnam about municipal politics — he is in a position to know but did not speak hopefully of the chances of breaking the power of Tammany
