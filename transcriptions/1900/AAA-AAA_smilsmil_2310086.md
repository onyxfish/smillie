## Left Page

### Tues. June 5, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \+ warmer again. Spent much of the w'f at the studio getting things put away for the summer, \+ other things collected to take away with me. After 12 went to 2°. Nat'l. \+ made deposit. To lunch at 1 \- to studio at 2 \- expecting D'r Gerster who had engaged to be there bet. 2 \+ 3 o'clk. Ralph came \+ waited for me for awhile, to go on a shopping tour with him \- G. did not come \- I was quite used up by an attack of indigestion \- headache \+ stomach discomforts for some time in my big chair. Home about 6. Ev'g Geo. Cook came with my check \+ acct. books, having written up my estate accts from '96 to the present. I gave him $50. Eliz. Howe came in to enquire about Mary \- who still continues to improve. Later, Geo. \+ Charl came in \- Ralph wrote a long letter to Jim whose birthday comes to-morrow \- I added a few words of congratulation \- Took the letter to G.C.S. \+ mailed it.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 6, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very hot again \- mitigated by a little breeze \+ a film over the sky. After bkf. to studio \+ from there to Heywood Bro. \+ Wakefield Co. in Canal St. \- Gave W.J.D. Walsh's letter of introduction to Mr. Baxter \+ he passed me over to a salesman who went with me giving me prices as I made my selections. I made a long list \- preparatory to another call. Then to Kimmel \+ Voigt's \- saw H.f. \+ made arrangements with him to take half a dozen india pf? from W°. G. \- plate to-morrow \- left m. plate with K. \+ V. Then went up Elm St. looking for Marvin's Safe warehouse \- did not find it \- home at 12 \- headache \- rested until lunch \- Studio at 2.15. D'r Gerster did not come but I went on with the work of all sorts of cleaning up preparatory to the summer flitting. Home about 6 \- dinner. Ev'g Ralph \+ I went to Dr. Yale's. I to get a photo. that he had promised me. It was after 10 when we got home.
