## Left Page

### Thurs. Jan. 4th, 1900

**Weather:** This is [illegible]  **Temp:**

Katharine \+Bessies dinner party

Superb day \- cold, but milder than yesterday \+less windy \- worked all day at studio on mezzotint "After the storm". \+altho' much interfered with thro' the day I got the whole surface covered and almost ready for the first proof \- At 12 30 I went to 2° Nat° Bk \+home to lunch at 1.30 \- To studio at 2 30 \+found Mills' van at the door for W.C.'s for Erie Art Club \+Bridgeport library \- bell out of order \- fussed with "the batteries \+then went for Thorne \- batteries played out \- in an hour had them renewed \+bells ringing \- worked on my plate until almost 5 o'clk \- Then home \+had a rest \- Manvel came after I had gone to my room \+Mary w° not disturb me \- Up at 6 30 \+dressed \- down to parlor at 7.30 \- I cannot now remember the names of all the guests \- will give them later \- Kate \+the boys dined at Charles \- I took Mrs. Robinson down to dinner she sat on my right \- then Rob.! Wilkinson \- Then Mrs. Holbrook, then Katharine, Then Mrs. Meedar \- Then Many at the head \- Then Mr. Robinson Then Sallie Akers \- Then Bessie \- Then Mr. Schauffler, then Edith Holt on my left \- The Table looked very pretty \- the dinner was good \+well served \+all seemed to have a very enjoyable time \- it was after 9 when we got up from the table \+after 10 when I left the house \+went to the University Club, Cor. 5° Ave \+54th S \- upon a card of invitation from Mr. Howard Kingsbury whom I met there with his uncle Mr. Thayer. The building is wonderfully fine. The dining room is noble \- so is the library \- It was 11 30 when I left \- walked home \- in at 12 \- but it was nearly when I got to bed

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:**

Still cold, clear \+windy \- The day was spent at Studio scraping on mezzotint "After the Storm"; I gave some attention to the proofs of "meas°". Report, got from O'Brien \+found that Mr. Manvel has made changes in the division of Expense Acct. that I could not understand, so I gave it up \+wrote a note to him asking him to make an appointment with me at his earliest convenience \- I sent to W. W° Clarence Martin, for execution, the papers prepared for record by Lee \+Lee with reference to his 2° Story cornice that projects 12" over the front of my building \- Home at 5 p. m. \+had a little nap before dinner \- Evell. The whole family turned out, with Sneed on Smillie added, eight in number, \+went to the Murray Hill Theatre where we had front seats in the balcony, \+saw "All The Comforts of Home" a farce that was given with much energy \+very little grace \- nevertheless, it was an amusing lark for us.
