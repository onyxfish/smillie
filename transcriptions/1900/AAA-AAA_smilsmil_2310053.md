## Left Page

### Thur. March 29, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day \- to studio at 9.30 \- worked for part of the w\+ on d'elpincott head \+ then, for a time, on Mr\? g\- head \- Home to lunch at 1.30. Then to studio \+ getting asphalteum varnish turpentine \+ brushes I went to N.A.D. schools \- Amsterdam Ave. \+ 109th St. \+ spent an hour or more varnishing the acid sink \+ adjacent parts \- Then walked to Central Park \+ thro' it to Mad. Ave. 190th St. Then car home \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. wrote a note to Geo. Cook asking him if he would write up my estate accts. for me as a piece of business.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 30, 1900

**Weather:** Overcast \+ rather raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast \+ rather raw \- went to Wm. miller's am after 9 \+ saw him about acid tray in etching studio at N.A.D. the bottom of which has split \- then to studio \- Then to Kimmel \+ Voigts \- Canal St. \+ got 15 sheets of india paper \- then to Wanamaker's \+ bought cheese cloth \+ other items for etching studio at N.A. \+ from there walked up to studio \- Some time stretching mosquito netting over drying frames \- home to lunch at 1.30 \- after that to Douglas's \+ got white lead \- Then to studio \+ spent most of remainder of p.m. mounting a oil painting made last spring, upon a new canvas backer, using the white lead as a cement \- wet paper for some printing to-morrow. Dinner at 6.30 \- Evef. very stupid with a half headache \- a sort of anemic condition I suppose \- I have not recovered any of my lost strength \+ have no doubt that I am now constantly below normal temp.
