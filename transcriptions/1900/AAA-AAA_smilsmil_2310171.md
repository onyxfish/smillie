## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 22, 1900

**Weather:** Very fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - to studio at 9.30 \+ to work on "Road to Wood lot" \+ struck to it until 1 o'clk. Then home to lunch back to studio at 2.30 \+ painted till 3.30 - then I cleaned up \+ soon after 4 I went to 10th St. Studio Bldg to see Dielman, at his request. He was out so I went to J. G. Brown's room \+ was with him until 5 - Then to Dielman's \+ was with him until after 6. He asked me to act as auditor of N.A. of D. annual Report - then told me the story of Graunis' defalcation amtg to $1100. \+ subsequent Restitution. This history is known only to D. Nicole, Beckwith, deForest \+ Mauvel. I advised that not another one should know it - the N.A. had lost no money but it we be very damaging if it became known that it had again been robbed - so we agreed. Home to dinner - about 8 went to Charlie's \+ got check for $3629.00 from sale of 70 shares of Smelter Con. a 52. This sum is all profit of Kansas Smelter Transaction \+ goes to pay for the Keene building - Home before 9 - [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 23, 1900

**Weather:** Fickle wtr., bright at first then overcast and cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fickle wtr., bright at first - then overcast and cloudy - P.M. \+ P.vt., beautifully clear. Before bkfs. I commenced letter to Jim \+ after bkfs. finished it - Ap.? came in for a few minutes. At 10 I went to 2d Nat. Bk, dep. cht. for $3629.00 \+ then p. loans it back to that amt. Then to Grolier Club \+ for some time studied the collection of pfs from Galliard req.? There. - very interesting work but I cannot see all that his wild admirers claim for him. Home at 1 - lunch at 1.30 - then as I felt out of sorts \+ needing rest, I took it. At 6.30 Emma Melaud came \+ took dinner with us \+ later we went to the Mad. Sq. Garden Theatre - that is Emma, Ralph \+ I went - Emma taking Ralph as her "treat" while I was allowed to accompany them - We had excellent seats, 2d Row, balcony, on aisle - The play was Shakespeare's "King Henry V" Rich. Mansfield as Henry - It was very elaborately put on stage \+ conscientiously given - Mlle. Ida Brassey made a pleasing Princess Katherine - a little bit of stage business, not given by direction of Shakespeare, followed the love scene in the last act, after the love making scene - He has her in his arms - has just kissed her - after he says quickly - "Here comes your father" - Every light client black out \+ we were in total darkness - the a moment the lights flashed up again \+ the curtain was demurely down as though no such incident had happened - It could hardly be considered Shakespearian but was very dramatic and amusing. The tableau - The battle of Agincourt - was a fine picture with a realistically painted curtain cutting up into the background. The effect of the great numbers of diving figures in the foreground - a central among them being Henry on a rearing wonder horse.
