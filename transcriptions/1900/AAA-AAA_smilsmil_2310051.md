## Left Page

### Sun. March 25, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day - cloudless & quiet & pleasantly cool - up after 7 & had my scrub & back to bed for a short rest - It was after 9 when I had my bkf. About 11.30 with Ralph rode up to Central Park & 90th St. Then walked around large Reservoir & down to Met. museum of Art - went in for half an hour, Then home to dinner at 2. - After that I wrote notes to Beckwith & Nicoll expressing my regret at getting no invitation to or notice of the meeting of Ways & Means Com. - at B's studio yesterday afternoon - about 4 o'clk. I lay down on my bed & slept soundly until 6.30 Tea at about 7 - Pvety a little reading to Ralph - then Yde came.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 26, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came from Pomfret.

Weather not pleasant at any time during the day & beastly late in the afternoon & at night. It was 10 o'clk. when I got to studio and until 1 o'clk I was engaged getting things in readiness to do some printing to-morrow. Then home to lunch - Messe Atwater there. She went with us to the Murray Hill Theatre at 2 o'clk. Many Kate, Katharine (Bessie was under the weather) Ralph & I. - The play was amusing though not particularly good - The acting was none of it really bad - nor was any of it passing good - but some of it was much better than we had a right to expect in return for the price we paid - 25¢ for the best seats in the house. I wonder at the endurance of the actors - Twice a day, a new play every week. Out at 5 o'clk. - I bought tickets for next Monday P.M. - Sleety rain falling - Home & lighted fire in parlor & had a look at The Post before dinner - Jim came in at 6.10 just from Pomfret - for his Easter Holidays - looking very well. - Dinner at 6.30 - Pvety as usual - the boys getting to bed early.
