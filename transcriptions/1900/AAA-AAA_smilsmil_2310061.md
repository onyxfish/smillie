## Left Page

### Mon. April 16, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant m'g \- clouded afternoon threat ening rain \- I was belated in getting to studio \+ did little work in the m'g as Maurice Stone came \+ after him, Chas. Barmore \- he made a long stay \+ urged me to take up the Greene head \+ finish it at once \- promising to raise some money for me from it if I would \- Home to lunch at 1 \- back to studio at 3 \- having gone with Ralph to Chas's "dark Room" 4 Ave. \+ 41st. to get the lay of the land" \- I worked with graver over face of Mr. G \- until near 6 \- then out \+ had a short walk \- met young Janeway \+ walked with him \- at dinner got Jim's letter describing the fire at Pomfret by which his quarters were burned out \- many of his belongings lost \+ he himself escaped thro' a window \+ down a ladder \- after dinner I went with R \+ to C's dark Room \+ we were there until nearly 11 o'clk developing some films that R \- exposed last Sunday \- then home \+ to bed

## Right Page

### Tues. April 17, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet day \- to studio at 9.30 \+ to work taking pf. from Mr. G \- (N. 2) I experimented with some thin Toronoso paper that I got from K.S.V. long ago \- as thin as India but much darker in tone \+ takes a fine imp. I made some thin starch \- Tissed the paper just as I would India \- The result was quite as satisfactory as I expected it to be. While I was at work the Rev. M. Vanbycke came bringing an Syttons design by Siddons Mowbray \- He wishes one to etch it \+ I promised to do it. I printed half a dozen pf. from the G head \+ at one, after working on the plate that I expect to submit to D. G. a water to-morrow \- Home to lunch at 1.30 \- after 2 went to N.A. P.D. school \- met May nard who has been cataloguing a great lot of engravings that belong to the Academy \- we looked over them in a hasty way to see if there were any valuable oi \+ interesting prints among them for insurance \- but the collection was largely antiquated rubbish \- \+ what was at all good was generally much injured by careless keeping \- There was a large number of Paranisi prints \- most of them early imp. \- many in good condition \- while \+ some were not \- there were a few early mezzotints but no good proofs. \+ some Durands, Daiforth's, Burt \+ Jones's \- I went to etching Room \+ gave acid sith another coat of asphaltum varnish \- Then, as the rain had ceased I walked across the upper end of the Park to Mad. Ave. \+90 S. \+ took cars home \- Dinner at 6.30 \- eve'g writing \-
