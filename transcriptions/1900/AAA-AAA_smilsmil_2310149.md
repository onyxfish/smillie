## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 9, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Albany — "The Kenmore" Room 198.

Got to The Kenmore about 3.° A.M. \+ were at once shewn to a Room \+ without delay tumbled into a comfortable bed \+ with as little delay fell asleep. I slept soundly till 7 o'clk \- \+ dozed until 8.15 \- then up to bkpt at 9 \- \+ took train for Binghampton at 10 \- The wef was raw \+ cloudy \- but about noon it cleared beautifully \- at 12.35 we stopped for 10 m. at Oneonta \+ had a sandwich \+ chicken jam. we got to B-ton at 2.35, had baggage transferred to D.L.\+W. \+ then went off to amuse ourselves until 5.55 we wandered thro' lots of streets until tired out \- Then back to hotel luncheon \- waited until near 6 \- then to a-tion station \- waited until near 6 \- then to station \+ had another sandwich \- Train on time \- met on board several Montrosians, \+ learned from one of them that Roger Sears' Daut. Sayre had had a severe stroke of paralysis yesterday morning \- transferred to D. \+ M. Train at Alford \+ got to Montrose on time \- 7.25 went at once to Mary Sayre's \+ found her ready for us \- we spent the eveng until after 9 chatting \- then to bed \- night very cold \+ cloudy \- piled on lots of bed clothes.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 10, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold \+ windy \- bkfst soon after 8 \- then sat by fire \+ got warm \- The cold is very trying to me \- It was about 9.30 when I went to P.O. \+ got a heavy mail \- Smelter check among other things \- mailed it at once to 2d Nat'l Bk \- Had a talk with Cooley \+ son \- at about noon went up to the house \+ saw Mrs. Kent \+ family \- Charlotte K \- seemed to me to be surprisingly well \- To dinner at Mary Sayre's soon after 1 o'clk. Before 3 I went to Morris house to see how the painting of the roof there was progressing \- Then over to my barns \- saw Wm. Spense \+ had a talk with him \- went over the grounds and again to the house \+ spent an hour with the K's \+ their friend Miss Lewis (?) Then down town \- went to Cooley's \+ looked over items of my bill \- to tea at 6 \- Evefg. with newspapers in front of big comforting fire \- \+ writing till 10 o'clk.
