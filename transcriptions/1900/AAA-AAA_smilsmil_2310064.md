## Left Page

### Sun. April 22, 1900

*Heading note (above printed header):* Went to Howard Russell Butler's house.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Grey, but pleasant day — enervating in its quiet mildness \+ clearing before evening. Bkf. at 9 \+ at 10:5 I was at Howard Russell Butler's — 135 W. 47 S\. I tried yesterday to get at him by telephone but he was out of town — as I expect to see Dielmann with Reference to new Carnegie developments I wished to see Butler as a preparation. I found him at home \+ ready to talk — we talked until nearly 12 \+ he told me in free detail just what Carnegie had said to him concerning his interest in the NA \+ what had been done in securing an option on 16 city lots on the east end of the block bet. 58, 59 S\. on 2d Ave — at the entrance to the Park \+ opposite the Park gate site with Reference to which we met Mr. DeWolf in Friday P.M. The old idea of Centralization that was the essence \+ the proof of the 7th S\. co-operative scheme under lies this new one \+ is exceedingly attractive to me. After the discouragement following DeWolf's announcement that the NA could not get entrance to the Park but would have to be content with the sidewalk outside for a building site this new project comes as a solace — again I allow myself the pleasure of hoping. Home before 1 — had a little nap before dinner — after that Geo\. Cook came \+ I gave him stubs of check books \+ my cash book to write up — I am satisfied that I would never find time to do it. Later Ralph \+ I went to studio — I set down [illegible] for printing — then we took a walk — Tea at 6.30 about 9 Geo \+ Charl came in \+ stayed for more than an hour — wrote \+ posted a note to DeKay — Sect. Nat. Arts Club Resigning my membership —

## Right Page

### Mon. April 23, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A showery day with mild interludes — To studio soon after 9. \+ at once commenced printing pf. from Mr. G. plate, some india on white backer — india on low toned backer — Tornoroe paper on white backer \+ Tornoroe on low toned backer — the most satisfactory results being, after all, india on white bk\+. At 12.30 I went to Kurtz's \+ left there Maynard's \+ Mowbray's Ey. Libris drawings to be Reduced by photography — then to bank — then to Putnam's to see Mr. Jno\. Ireland, but he was out to lunch — then home — Uncle Willie, Aunt Agnes \+ Emma Bourne there — after lunch was detained by a shower — then to studio \+ worked all the afternoon at printing — J.C. Nicole came about 4 o'clk — with letter to Carnegie signed by Dielmann, J.Q. Brown \+ Beckwith — It set forth that the idea of building the NA on 10th S\. had been abandoned \+ that the wisdom of his views on centralization of art interests was acknowledged \+ his aid was asked in carrying out a project upon a site selected near the Carnegie Hall in W. 57 S\. I was not satisfied with the wording but it was too late to criticise — it was a risky thing I thought to state without qualification that the plan of building on 10th S\. had been given up — but the stake played for was high — I took the risk \+ signed it — Nicole signed it after me \+ then took it to Carnegie who is to sail for Europe on Sat. next. I wrote a note to Dielmann stating that while we had taken a bold step \+ done a risky thing I thought we had good Reason for it. Dinner at 6.30 — Uncle W. \+ Aunt A. with us — Emma B. with relatives — no letter from Jim — I became uneasy \+ telegraphed to Mr. Olmsted — \+ followed it by a letter.
