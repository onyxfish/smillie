## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 23, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm muggy day with rain at night. I got to studio about 10 \+ fixed up p[illegible] that I had taken yesterday ready for delivery — then worried over my accts for awhile — then, in desperation, set palette \+ painted upon a × canvass, a subject commenced years ago — "Road to a wood lot" — I worked upon it freely changing lines of composition as I painted. Home to lunch at 1.30 — back before 3 — painted until 4.30 — then went to Wanamakers \+ did a little shopping for the house — walked up, stopping at Con. Gas Co. \+ ordering a gas stove for laundry. Dinner at 6.10 — [illegible] after newspaper a little writing \+ [illegible] feeling tired I went to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 24, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant \+ still very mild — 10 o'clk when I got to studio \+ painted on Road to wood lot joggling over a group of trees in foreground. Maurice Sterne came with proof of a portrait plate that he has etched. I gave him considerable time. To lunch at 1.30 \+ back at 3 — painted until 5. Then walked down to Union Sq. — to A. Fussell's \+ paid his bill of $2.40 — then to Mr. Wm Bickham's — 12 W. 18 St. \+ left photo-gravure reduction of [illegible] book plate — then home to dinner — eve[ning] I wrote to Miss Korhler \+ before 11 o'clk. went to G. C. Station \+ mailed letter.
