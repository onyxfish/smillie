## Left Page

### Mon. May 14, 1900

**Weather:** Hotter yet. Official Report 88°.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Good ther° in the street recorded as high as 92°. It was almost suffocating. When I got to studio I found that fool Jennings had lighted a fire in my furnace there. So I had to print — it made things exceedingly uncomfortable for me. I took p[late] of Century book plate \+ for the first found that my perpendiculars leaned to one side — after studying it carefully I saw how I could correct it by new work — So I carefully adjusted my base line — the design is on the plate a little crooked — I filed the bottom of the plate true to base line — it was 1/8" out — Then with my square I trued the other lines of the plate which gave me considerable filing to do — when I got a true square on the plate I laid a ground — varnished the back \+ went home to lunch — after lunch I etched the necessary graver \+ burnisher until after 6 — I worked with at 6.30 — Miss Bush was taken ill. This my \+ had to give up — my old nurse Miss Thomas came before dinner. — Eve[ning] I went to S. P. Avery's \+ saw him for a few minutes — He has just given his collection of prints to the Public Library \+ I promised to give my set of Father's p[late]s. On my way home I stopped at Chase's for a few minutes —

## Right Page

### Tues. May 15, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another extremely hot \+ uncomfortable day. Up at 7 o'clock — letter to Mr. Kent before 6 p[.m.] Other letters to write after 6 p[.m.], so it was after 10 when I got to studio. I filed the bevels on Century [illegible] lib. plate \+ then took five india p[late]s. Cleaned up \+ at 12.30 Rose to 2[nd] Nat[ional]. Buk — then home to lunch at 1 — after lunch rose up to NA school to meet my etching class — not one of the beggars there. Stone had written me that he was sick \+ no? or unable to be there — I waited awhile \+ then rode to studio — cleaned up my table — laid ground on plate for Dr. Van Dyke's ex-lib. \+ Then commenced tracing on gelatine — worked until after 6 — nearly two hours. Then home to dinner. Eve[ning] got two' my newspaper as soon as possible — Thunderstorms about \+ some light rain — a little movement of air \+ a trifle cooler, but not enough to make any change of temp. in doors. Gave my head a thor[ough] shampoo before going to bed.
