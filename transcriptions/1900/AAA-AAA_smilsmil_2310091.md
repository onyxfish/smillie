## Left Page

### Fri. June 15, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — delightfully fresh. Up at about 6.30 \+ wrote a letter to Elea Diwen before bkfp. Had some notes to write which kept me busy until about 10 o'clk — then to Wanamaker's — Kate, the cook, met me there \+ we spent the mtf until near 1 o'clk getting the Kitchen \+ laundry things — a great job. Home to lunch at 1.30 — I made up accts of purchases so far for furniture \+ outfit \+ it ames. to $901 — this does not include about $75 for bed clothes bought some time ago. I will not get off for less than $1000. About 4 went to Kep's \+ exchanged shirts, getting better ones. Home at 5 \+ wrote letter to Jim. dinner at 6.30 — Evet Ralph \+ I went out to Lexington Ave. \+ spent some time watching the men at work preparing for the welding of the Rails. The welding, by pouring molten iron about the ends, goes on from 12.30 to 4 A.M. as was explained to us by Mr. Wendell a gentleman interested in the work whom we were fortunate enough to meet on the ground.

## Right Page

### Sat. June 16, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast all day. Cool breeze — I got started before bkfp. by writing a letter to Scott Brown. After bkfp. I went to Miller's \+ saw him about hardware. Then to Douglas — then to Wanamaker's — I was there most of the mtf going the rounds of many departments \+ the business necessary to insure the prompt shipping of my purchases. from attic to sub cellar — It was very interesting, altho' sometimes perplexing \+ tedious — Mr. dens, of the business dept. agreed to a concession in shipping. At 12 I went to N.Y. Central freight office, 11th Ave. \+ 32d St. \+ saw Mr. Whitbeck — learned that I could charter a furniture car to Westport for $34. Then home to lunch. After that, again to Wanamaker's \+ saw Mr. dens, who, on the figures that I gave him, agreed to a concession of 1/2 (or $18.00) cr. on freight bill — Then I went to Canal St., hoping to see Mr. Baxter of Heywood Bro. place closed — K. \+ V. closed — Then I sauntered thro' little Italy — Baxter St. to Five Points — then thro' the Chinese quarter, Mott \+ Pell Sts. It was interesting but not altogether agreeable — in Baxter S't. I could almost imagine myself in Naples. Then to studio — there for a short time, then home \+ being very tired I rested until dinner — Evening with newspapers — Charl came in for awhile.
