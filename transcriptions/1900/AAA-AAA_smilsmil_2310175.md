## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 30, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright pleasant day. The effects of yesterdays headache had nearly passed off - So that when I went to the studio I felt equal to starting work again on a Normandy barnyard. I painted out some of the tree forms that crossed the sky in the upper R. h. corner, bettering the composition. At 12.30 I went to 2°. Nat'l Bk. \+ then home to lunch at 1.30 - again to studio \+ painted until after 4 - Then Ralph came \+ we went to the bird store in H. Ave. near 13. S. \+ bought a canary for $2.50 a male to mate with the female he has. Then home to dinner — Eve'g at home.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 1, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day - rather raw as though snow was in the neighborhood. I went to studio at 9.30 \+ painted on Normandy barnyard, going over a large part of central group of trees changing the quality of the green. I realize now that all of the work that I have put on this canvas within the past few weeks has been in effect to weaken it - I have taken out crudities \+ in their place have got insipidities. To lunch \+ back as usual - Painted until after 4 - home at 5 \+ had a rest before dinner - Eve'g. Charl came in but w. not go with me to monthly meeting of Century - I went there at 9 curious to see what my Road to the Wood lot looked like. There was an unusual number of pictures but none of them strikingly good - Tom Moran had an immense canvas. 12 or more feet long - the falls of the Shoshone - a great sweep of Rocks \+ clouds \+ water, all going down into a misty abyss. My painting made me feel like an abyss. It looked dreadful to me - commonplace in every way - dark \+ heavy in color - tight in handling \+ uninteresting generally. Everybody however was very kind \+ did nothing about it to me. No one can hope to paint well who works at it as spasmodically as I have done. Home \+ to bed at midnight.
