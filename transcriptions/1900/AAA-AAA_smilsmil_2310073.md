## Left Page

### Thur. May 10, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant, windy + cold - To studio at 9.30 + worked until after 12. on Century book plates - Geo. came + presently, a large frame of his. Having rented his studio for the summer he will do a little painting in my studio we moved easles + pictures about + got things into proper shape for him - To lunch at 1 - + then to N.A. where I met some of my class and worked with acid until near 5. Then home through the Park - Dinner at 6.30 - Mary is so sort of sorts again that she kept her room + sent for the doctor - I had so much of a headache that I gave up before 9 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 11, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull day growing milder towards ev'g. To studio + worked all day - that is, about 6 hours, on Century book plates, with graver + dry point - lunch at 1 - back + worked on plate until 4.30 - then wrote to Herrick + Spence at Montrose - I had written to Miss Kent in the m'f. Geo. came in for a while - as he has rented his studio for the summer he has now a large frame + canvas here - Before I went to Locke's + then to Rider's - 6th Ave. + 23d St. Home to dinner, late, Ev'g I wrote a long letter to Jim \+ posted it at G.C. Station at 10.30 - Mary's trouble seems to have developed into a rheumatic fever + she suffers a good deal of pain.
