## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 9, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Am. Theatre" "Pirates of Penzance."

Dull m'g - but weather cleared later. I felt rather shabby after yesterdays experience so I gave the m'g up to running about attending to various matters - I went to Millers - then to Central Iron works - then I went down town to Street Cleaning Dep't. to make complaint ag't. The occupants of Sniffin Court who pile their ashes \+ refuse at the corner of my building - Dep't. closed - the commissioner being out to-day - then to Lee \+ Lee \+ gave instructions to have the agreement signed by Att'y of Mr. Martin, concerning cornice on her second story front projecting over my front sent to Recorder's office for Record. Then I went to Tax office \+ found that taxes on 110 E. 38 S - had been put up another $1500 last year - val. now $21000 - the val. on my neighbors not in proportion. I copied a no. of them \+ went back to Mr. Lee - He is to make a protest - Home after 1 - lunch - to studio at 2.30 worked on G. head until about 4.30 - then home \+ rested for half an hour - then wrote a short letter to Jim - Dinner at 6.30 - Before 8 all hands, including Miss Taylor, went to Am. Theatre - 8 Av \+ 43 St. where The Castle Square Opera Co - gave The Pirates of Penzance - It was a fairly good representation but I could not forget the way it was given on its first coming out many years ago by the original English Co - D'Oyle Carte's - that had been drilled under the instruction of both Gilbert \+ Sullivan -

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 10, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

overcast most of the day - To studio at 9.30 \+ worked on Mr. G. head until 12 o'clk - then I went down town to Street Cleaning Dep't. to find that on Saturdays it closed at 12 - back to studio at 1. worked for half an hour - then home to lunch - to studio at 3 \+ worked until 5 on G. head - then walked up to Central Park \+ back - Dinner at 6.30 - Miss Taylor, Katharine \+ Bessie dined at Charlie's. At 8.30. Mary, Kate, Ralph \+ I went there - C - has just got a fine parlor grand Piano \+ a Pianola attachment - Tonight he gave us a try of it - He has not had a chance to master the mechanism, but gave us ample evidence that it is a very remarkable \+ interesting contrivance -
