## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 26, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day - Ralph very much under the weather - Tempo. above 103°. & headache - After bkft. I went to Charl's & called Yale up on Telephone to come to Ralph - then to Douglas's - got gal. of turpentine & went to N.A.f.D. & met my Etching class - Prepared for printing - eight plates - Some good work - all things considered - It was nearly 2 o'ck when I got home to lunch - To studio at 2.30 - there for 2 hours but did no work - just fooled around - Home at 5 expecting Carleton Comstock who said he w. call bet. 5 & 6 o'ck. to talk about purchase of Montrose property - At 6.30 telegram from him declining to make any offer - Dinner Spent the eve'g with Ralph who still has considerable fever - he is in my Room - Charl came in for awhile.

Jim almost as much out of sorts as Ralph - indigestion - & I don't wonder at it.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 27, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day - After bkft. wrote to Mrs. Kent of Buffalo declining her offer for a part of the Montrose property - that is, less the Morris house & the swamp lot - & stating that I w. accept our first offer of $7000 for the entire property. Then to studio & commenced painting on my 33×44 "baby river" - Home to lunch at 1.30 - back at 2.30 & painted as long as I could see - till 4.30 - & feel encouraged by my start - It seems to me that I have struck a good key note & the work went along without grievous effort.
At 5 went out & after a short walk went to Clausens & ordered a frame for little Oct. Study 11½×15½. Home at 6 - dinner at 6.30 - a loafing eve'g. & to bed early -

Both boys seem to be improved in condition today - Ralph very much so.

Eve'g - send N.A.f.D. - Private View & Reception cards to Mr & Mrs. Oppenheim & to Mr. & Mrs. Lobenstine.
