## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 11, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain during most of the day - the weather cleared late in the afternoon quite warm \+ at night there was moonlight. Webb \+ Ray did not put in an appearance I spent much of the morning in writing - among other things, to Callanan Bro. declining to pay a part of their bill - time of plumber \+ items connected with his last visit here, Aug. 11., when he came to correct errors in his work done last fall. For some time I worked putting up curtain poles \+ running gear in main room - Went to P. O. at noon - Jim went off alone \+ without letting any one know, for a stroll in the woods - up Giant Trail \+ across to Wash bowl, \+ got back before dinner thoroughly drenched - strange boy. Evef. Jim, Ralph \+ I went to the library \+ read until 9 o'clk - then home \+ I read one of Bullen's sketches aloud to the boys -

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 12, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Continuous showers - a gale of wind blowing most of the day - sometimes so heavily as to make me fear that the big studio window would be blown in - Ray did not come but Webb did \+ I kept him at work on putting up shelves \+ like work in-doors. I wrote letter to Walter Cook \+ sent him drawing of east wall of main room \+ asked him to make a drawing for turned posts in main room - also, I described to him my perplexity over Jim's physical condition. After dinner I went to hotel \+ posted my letter \+ drawing to Walter - then to Scott Browns - then to library \+ got a book for myself - then home. Rate very much under the weather - pain in face \+ head. Charl improving slowly - Evef. read Bullen's "Cruise of the Cachalot".
