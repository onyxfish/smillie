## Left Page

### Tues. July 3, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day — Raw, windy \+ with showers that settled into a wet afternoon. When I got to the house I found two young men that Reeds had sent there unpacking the furniture \+ things \+ carrying everything up stairs. They worked all day \+ accomplished a good deal. The carpenters worked closely \+ Adams, the painter, dawdled about \+ accomplished something — Mr. Maghee came \+ wandered through the house with me. I feel very wretched all day I have taken cold \+ it affected me all over, outside \+ inside. I think that I am cattarrble from one end to the other. We went through lunch \+ dinner, being unusually late at dinner. Ralph \+ I got to bed early \+ I had a fair night, except that medecine routed me out at 1 a.m.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 4, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dubious m't., clearing to be a very warm and delightful day. We got up at about 7.30 — after 8\. up I had a number of notes to write which kept me until near 11 o'clk. Then R- \+ I went over to the house — he amused himself firing of his cannon \+ crackers outside while I checked off the Heywood bro's bill with goods &c². \+ other like work until near 1 — then to lunch. After lunch I wrote until the closing of the mail at 3 — after that Ralph \+ I took a walk up the Reserve road for about a mile. I feel much better than I did yesterday but find myself very weak. Back to hotel before 6. had a little rest — then a toweling \+ a change. \+ dinner about 7 — Rest in room, reading newspapers while Ralph wrote a letter. To bed early.
