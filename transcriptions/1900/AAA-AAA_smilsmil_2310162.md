## Left Page

### Sun. Nov. 4, 1900

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — Bkft. at about 10 — Read news papers — at about noon Ralph \+ I went out \+ walked on 5th Ave. until about 1 o'clk — then home — dinner at 2 — after dinner I wrote for awhile on a letter to Jim — about 4.30 I went out \+ walked for about an hour — then home — Gel. \+ Medbip \+ Charl at the house — Gel. stayed to tea — I finished letter to Jim. Charl came back about 9 o'clk. \+ he \+ Gel. stayed until after 10.

## Right Page

### Mon. Nov. 5, 1900

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day — to studio at 9.30 — worked all day on Normandy barn-yard, getting second painting finished — now it is to be put aside to dry — it is about 14 years since the first painting on it — if as long a time passes before the next painting on it, someone else will have to finish it — So it is I am encouraged — I think it is rather above my poor average. I am hoping that I may find a place in the Pan. Am. Exbn at Buffalo next summer. Home to lunch — Mr. Horace Winans there — I painted until after 4 — cleaned up \+ walked up to Freidrick's new Artists materials store — 5th Ave. bet. 47 \+ 48 Sts. \+ bought some brushes. Dinner at 6.30 Eve'g. Charl came in — I spent some time writing up "Petty Cash" — then went out \+ had a walk in the superb moonlight \+ tried to "study up" some effects.
