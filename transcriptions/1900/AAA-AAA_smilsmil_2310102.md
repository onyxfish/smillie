## Left Page

### Sat. July 7, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An excessively hot day — Ther. getting up to 90° in various places. Our attic bed room at the hotel was stifling but all parts of the house at Pine Knoll were very comfortable as a pleasant breeze was moving there all day. I got over to the house quite early after bkfst. A load of lumber for shelving and partitions came & before noon I started Buck \+ Webb at putting up partition in Butler's pantry. The dining room floor \+ the upper hall \+ stairway in house were varnished \+ Ralph \+ I almost completed the furnishing of the bed rooms so that at the close of the week there was apparent a satisfactory promise — In the evg, Beede brought to me a young man \+ I engaged him to do general clearing up at $1.50 a day — Shortly after that Slater appeared with his bro. in law of whom he wrote to me last winter, but he was too late.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 8, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very heavy showers during the n'f — some sunshine about noon — a heavy shower — & then again clearing. Fine moonlight night. I got up at about 6.30 \+ had a sponge bath while Ralph slept. After bkfst. I wrote a long letter to Mary making arrangements for the coming of the family next Saturday — Dinner at about 2 — I had a nap after that — About 5 I went to Mr. Maghee's — not at home — then to Mrs. Hardenburg's \+ had a talk with her about housekeeping at Keene Heights — Then to Pine Knoll for a turn about. Supper at 7 — most of the evg. walking in the moonlight on one of the upper piazzas — quite alone.
