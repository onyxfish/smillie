## Left Page

### Sat. May 12, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day - after a night of headache I got up with it - It seems to be a rheumatic - passed off about noon. After bkfst. some writing kept me until 10.30 - Then to studio \+ worked on Century book plate until 1 - home to lunch - back at 2.30 \+ worked until 4 - Then I went to Walter's office in 29th! \+ got full size drawing of post for Neave Piazza - Then to Putnams \+ pd. Jno. Ireland $5.00 for putting a valuation on Geo. Cook's books in my possession - Then back to studio \+ worked there until 6.30 - Then home to dinner - Evef Charl came in for a little while. Mary has had a sore day but in the evef the doctor spoke encouragingly of prospects -

## Right Page

### Sun. May 13, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day, starting fairly but reaching 86°. in the afternoon. I got up soon after 6 - had my scrub \+ bath \+ went back to bed a little after 7 \+ slept until 8.30 - Then up \+ had bkfst. at 9. Ralph went to Sunday School - Then Philip Babcock came \+ the two went off to the Parks. I wrote notes \+ otherwise used my time until near noon. - Then to studio - To G.C. Station \+ about till 1.30 - dinner at 2 - most of the afternoon was given to a nap. By the way, I went to Charl's in the mfg \+ got my pfp. of Bishop's jade plate - They have been in C's keeping for some 4 years. Tea at 6.30 Evef I worked until near 11 assorting my European Photos. \+ doing them up in wrapping paper.
Mary's trouble progresses - Dr. Smith's says it is rheumatic fever - he advise to have a trained nurse - Miss Bush was got - M - is incapable of helping herself \+ has to be moved in bed.
