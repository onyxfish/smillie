## Left Page

### Wed. May 30, 1900

**Weather:** Fine day \- cool m'f \- warm p.m. breeze from the east.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10 after writing some notes \- worked until 1 o'clk. Taking experimental p'f's different colored inks \+ paper \- from Century \& VanDyke ex\-libs. Also clean p'f's from which to made Photo\-gravures Reduced size \- Lunch. After that Ralph \+ I went to Charles and the three of us went to 155 "S." by Elevated. C. had his new panorama camera \- R\- had his Kodak \- we walked up the Speedway to Washington Bridge \- C. exposed all the films (6) on his roll \- he has not tried this camera before \- unfortunately the sun was obscured a part of the time \- we were very much interested by what we saw as we walked \- we climbed to 10 "Ave. \+ took trolley for home, getting in at 6.30 \- Dinner \- A quiet ev'g.

## Right Page

### Thur. May 31, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day \- after a restless night I overslept in the m'f \+ did not get to my blfs. until near 9 \- after 10 when I got to studio \- worked until after 4 o'clk taking a dozen fine p'f's from the two ex\-lib. plates \- Home to lunch at 1 o'clk \- Heavy thunder shower passed over lower part of the city about 2 p.m. After 4.30 I wrote a note to Prof VanDyke \+ sent him the specimens of paper \+ the printings in different colored inks that I pulled yesterday m'f. Then I wandered about the streets until 6.30 \- then dinner \- Ev'g. Val \+ Gordon came \+ played awhile with Ralph. Charl also came in \- I loafed when I was not worrying over one of Ralph's examples in arithmetick.
