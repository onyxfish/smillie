## Left Page

### Sun. April 8, 1900

**Weather:** Weal  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very fine day in spite of high wind. Up at 5.30 \+ had my scrub \+ bath. Then back to bed until after 8 \- bkfs. at 9. Sat 10 at Dielman's Studio, 51 W. 10" \+ there met him \+ Lockwood de Forest \+ considered some N.A. of D. Tax business demanding immediate attention \- That was disposed of in a few minutes \+ def \- left \- I remained an hour longer talking of Academy matters that we agree require very careful \+ prompt treatment \- the institution will be in a seriously awkward condition \- we agree that there is little to be hoped for in the expensive plans of C. \+ H. for the site at 110 St. The necessary amt. of money cannot be got \- The Central Park site on 59 St. at 7. Ave \- offers more hope in spite of the serious difficulties that threaten \- The artists, irrespective of organizations, must unite in a request for the site \- it must not come from the NA alone \- Home at 12.30 \- with Jim Ralph went to W.S. Millers \+ arranged to get use of Dark Room in his basement \- Home before 2 \- dinner \- after 3. with Jim went to Miller dark Room \+ got him started developing a lot of films that he took exposed at Pomfret \- Then Home \+ had a nap to make up for lost sleep last night \- Tea at 6.30. \- Eve'g Reading \+ writing.

## Right Page

### Mon. April 9, 1900

**Weather:** Wea.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, but breezy \+ cool \- Got up with a heavy headache which disabled me all day \- It was late before I got to studio. I carefully mounted an old painting by A.T. Agate \- 1840 \- that I intend to present to the N.A. of D. Home to lunch at 1.30 \- \+ after that took an hours nap to make up for some of the sleep that I lost last night \- After 3 went to N.A. of D. There until near 6 \- varnishing with asphaltime the acid sink \- etc. Home to dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g. my head still very stupid.
