## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 18, 1900

'Gene Atwater stayed over night -
Interview with Hon. Whitelaw Reid -

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cold, windy day - snow melting nicely in the sunshine - Bkf: after 9. at 11 I was at Hon. Whitelaw Reid's residence No 1451 Madison Ave. He came in promptly to see me & tho' I had determined to make my call as short as possible, he was so much interested & had so much to say himself that it was an hour before I left him. We sat in the large parlor or drawing room before a wood fire. I at once referred to Paragraphs that had appeared in Trib. & other papers stating that the H.A. was considering the matter of a municipal site - Then, as briefly as I knew how, I gave a history of the vale [illegible] property - the purchase of its new site - & its financial condition - I told him of Mayor Vanluyck's remark to Reid with: "Why don't you apply for a municipal site" - there if the several mu- nicites that might be given, but the one desirable one was the one in Central Park at 59th St. head 17th ave - I showed him maps of Park - He said at once that after years of teaching from Olmsted, stiles & the artists themselves it had become a sort of creed or cult with newspaper men to oppose all encroachments upon Central Park & that if, the forces of the city were united in opposing it the failure of the scheme was certain - I told him I knew it & that my visit to him was a first step in the direction of getting advice & to find where the press would be likely to stand; he said go to the leaders of the press & he gave me a number of names, over the attitude of "Strike out hear me" lay the case before them for consideration on its merits - Keep prominent the idea of providing a noble gate or entrance to the park - and that this is a proposition of artists for the adornment of the park for the advantage of the people. I found him altogether genial — He said he was open to conviction & did not feel himself bound to oppose the scheme — never entirely not committal —
Then I went to studio - then walked for awhile - dinner at 2 - most f. P.m. on bed with bad neuralgic headache - Tea at 6.30 - 'Gene Atwater came to stay overnight with the girls.
