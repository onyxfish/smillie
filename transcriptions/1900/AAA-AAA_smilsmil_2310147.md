## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 5, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably warm day — opened as though rain wd. come in an hour or two but instead it cleared to be a brilliant, misty luminous Oct. day — I got to the house about 9 — Buck & Adams there — Buck finished his work except the cedar post gate I made a scale drawing of that for him to work by — Mr. Scott Brown & his friend came \+ I showed them through the houses. I did not make much progress with my trunk packing there were so many things outside to pick up \+ look after — It was after 1 o'clk when I got my dinner — then back to the house \+ busied myself there until dusk — a beautiful twilight — with moonlight — with tints of violet \+ lake \+ red over the mountains — The walk across to Browns was a delight — Supper was late — 7 o'clk — Evely St. Murtland was in evidence \+ talked pretty steadily until near 10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 6, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thunderstorm about 1 o'clk. this m'f. mist enveloped everything until 9 am. — then it cleared away warm \+ hazy. I went to house about 9 — some time was spent getting things in \+ about the annex in order — then I commenced packing my trunk — went to Brown's about 1 o'clk — The color display as I came over the golf links was as wonderful as anything I ever saw — after dinner I went to the hotel \+ arranged my Orlando Basde acct. with Black — Ralph spent the p.m. at the Hardenburg's — I went to the house \+ completed packing trunk — Before 6 o'clk. went to Mr. Hardenburg's \+ got Ralph — The walk to the hotel in the twilight moonlight was very lovely — supper a little before 7 — Evely Mr. Brown brought out a very interesting work on the woods of America — being thin slices of the different kinds of wood accompanied by descriptions.
