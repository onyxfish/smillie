## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 29, 1900

**Weather:** thick  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thick weather until afternoon - then it rained heavily well on into the night. Buck \+ Webb finished work in the main Room \+ about 4 P. m. commenced in the Studio - they stopped about 5 o'clk. impatient to get away - Ray put in a full day digging \+ carrying out gravel from under north piazza \+ chores about the house - I had as much as I could do. On looking among the pots of Addoms I found a sort of varnish stain-like stuff \+ I went over some of the Studio's work with it the color effect being satisfactory. Before 3 o'clk. in a heavy rain I went to hotel \+ got money \+ ck. from Charl ($100.) with Remnants of two A. &N. mileage books. — Tea at usual time and an evef. with books \+ newspapers.

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 30, 1900

**Weather:** mild  **Temp:** [illegible]

A mild day - a day of clouds, light \+ flecy - with little sunshine - Up at 7.30 \+ had my usual scrub - bkft. after 9 - until dinner time at 2 working about the house getting ready for the flitting that is now soon to take place - after dinner I spent most of the afternoon assorting \+ putting together in proper groups the hinges \+ blind fastenings to be set to R. Prescott \+ Son at Keeseville. About 5 I walked over to Otis's - not at home - left word that I wanted him to come to the house. Then Ret. thro' "Ghost's Walk" - lovely after sunset effects among the white birches - the tall ferns underneath, Russet \+ ochre. At home found Mrs. Hardenburgh, Mr. Loring, \+ little Katherine Hardenburgh - singularly attractive youngster to me. Tea at 6.30 — Evef. with books.
