## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 12, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dark, disagreeable day. When I got to studio I set palette \+ pitched into a color sketch of Artist's Gate, as it should be — I don't know why I should do this. I know that I am not competent to work out any thing new or worthy serious consideration from point of view of trained architect — nevertheless I devoted the day to it \+ produced a — sketch. At 5 I went to O'Brien's with revised p/s of Treas". Report — he promised me p/s of Revision to-morrow evef — Then back to studio \+ cleaned up palette — To dinner at 6.30 — Evef The same old sort. newspaper reading — some reading to Ralph \+ a little note writing — Mr. Cutting has written inviting Ralph to spend next Sat. \+ Sunday at Their Long Island Country home with Bronson Cutting \+ another of his school fellows.

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 13, 1900

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — clear, bright \+ mild. Some writing kept me at the house until 10 — Then to studio. I worked on etching Mad. Sq. Garden Tower — adding some more work on a new ground. That was soon done — Then took up plate "The Road to The Lake" \+ in like manner added work to that. Maynard came \+ gave me a check, on acct, for $500 — This I took to bank \+ deposited it \+ then home to lunch — afternoon continued work on Road to Lake until after 4 — Then home \+ had a nap before dinner — Evef, went to Century. Annual meeting — I had an enjoy-able evef \+ remained until after midnight. Then walked home with Walter Cook who left me at my door. To bed at 1 o'clk.
