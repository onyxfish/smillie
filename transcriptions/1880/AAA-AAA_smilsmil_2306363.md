## Left Page

### April, Monday 5, 1880

a genuine Atonic day — full of
sunshine + shower — two heavy
showers passed with thunder
lightning + hail. Got to studio
at 9.30 + made charcoal sketch
of "A Kennebunkport Veteran" —
then made water color sketch
on wood for Homeopathic Fable. To dinner at 2. The
whole afternoon spent in
fixing up my accts to date —
also arranging Mr. Paxton's affairs —
a light meal at 6.45 then to 37
S. + dressed + at 9 went to Mr. T.
Johnston's 8 Fifth Ave. + met the
Trustees of Met. Museum of Art and
members of the Loan Committee to
exchange congratulations — Sat
down to supper at 10.20 — got up
at 12.20 — a very fine affair. Then
walked up to 37 S. with Mr. Robt.
Gordon + Wm S. Dodge Junr. To
bed at 1 o'clk.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 6, 1880

Did not get out of my
bed until 4 o'clk. P.M. —
Paying for my supper last
night with a beastly nervous
headache. Heavy thunder
shower in the afternoon — after
eating a bowl of gruel, I went
to Anna Cook's — there until
10 — then took a walk down
to 25th St + back — then had
some tea + bread + butter —
then Read Tribune until
12 o'clk — then to bed and
had a little sleep.
