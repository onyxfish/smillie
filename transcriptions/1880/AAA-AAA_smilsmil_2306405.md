## Left Page

### June, Monday 28, 1880

A very hot day — B[illegible] at 8 —
Did not go out to work —
most of the mrg. spent in
assorting my samples of Japs
paper — went to P.O. at noon.
Dinner at 1 — then a nap —
after that wrote a letter to
Will about the plate that Root
has etched after my West Point
drawing for Scribner — & another
long letter to Mr. Koehler — Tea
at 6 — light & refreshing
thunder shower — geo. left
for N.Y. & some business best
known to himself at 6.30 P.m.
Evg. Father & I walked up
town — then I read Tol'anne
& got to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 29, 1880

Cooler weather, but still very
hot — After breakfast wrote
letters most of the mrg.
Then got about my etching
maps & made ready to put
in some new work on No. 1.
"Farm Lane" Dinner before
1 — After dinner finished
the etching & bit in parts
then cleaned off ground —
Tea at 6.30 — after a short
walk went to Prof. Ritter's
& spent the evg. there
until 10 o'clk. — Then home
& to bed before 11. [illegible — entire passage]
