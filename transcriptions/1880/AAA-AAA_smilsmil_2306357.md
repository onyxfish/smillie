## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 24, 1880

A high wind & a dirty day a combination of April and Dec. Got to studio at 9.30 & spent the m'g exhibiting & printing etching Sunnyside. Colman & Gifford came in just as I was going to dine. So I did not get out until 2.30 — after dinner went to S. P. Avery's — then back and laid ground & etched on Sunnyside — At 6 went to 37 S. & dressed — back at 7 & had tea — then wrote some letters — & at about 9 went to Mrs. G. P. Putnam's 144 E. 60th St. a company of about fifty there — about 11 walked with Geo. to 37 St. very high wind & exceedingly cold. To bed about 11.45.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 25, 1880

Still fine weather, not quite so windy & so cold. Got to studio about 9.30 & to work on Sunnyside etching — bit my second etching & in so doing destroyed the edges of my first line — then fussed & bothered during the rest of the day with stone, coal & every [illegible] paper trying to restore things — Dinner at usual. Kohlear & Thos. Moran called — near 7 when I got to A[nn]a Cook's to tea — after going to Arnold's, Geo. & I went to Thos. G. Hunt's 116 E. 39 St. where we pleasantly spent the rest of the evening & had an invitation to dine on Sat. next — At 10, to 37 S. & to bed before 11.
