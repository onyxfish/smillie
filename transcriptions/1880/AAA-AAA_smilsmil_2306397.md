## Left Page

### June, Saturday 12, 1880

*Po'keepsie.*

Clear, hot day — up at 6
& worked at packing my
trunk until about 7.30
when I went to B.k.p.
with Geo. Then to Rennis
& back to 37.5. Spent the
rest of the m'f. fighting a
headache & finishing up
the packing of a trunk &
a box. At 1.30 had lunch
with the Hinshelwood girls.
At 2 went to Exfo. Co. Then
to barbers & at 3 to studio.
Said my good byes & then
to R.R. Depot & took 4 o'clk.
train to Po'k. Reaching
home at about 6.45. Tea
& over f until 10 un-packing
and gabbing. Then to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 13, 1880

A hot day with showery
looking sky. Went to the
Bap tist Ch. with father.
Heard one of Kendricks char-
acteristic addresses. After
that saw Uncle Millie and
Aunts Agnes & Jessie as they
came out of the Congregational
ch. Then to Mam's — then
home to dinner. In the
afternoon I wandered out
to the Fox Cedar meadow &
sat there enjoying the breeze
until driven in by a coming
storm whose northern border
passed over us at 5 o'clk.
Matt & Ed. to Tea. Evening
without incident. Wrote to
Courtwright at Montrose en-
quiring about summer board
& boat. Letter to P. O. To
bed at 10.30.
