## Left Page

### September, Thursday 16, 1880

A delicious day of moods — at times the lake would be perfectly placid under heavy clouds — then the clouds rolled away & every thing sparkled under a fresh breeze, soon to be followed by some other equally charming mood —
About 9.30 Anna & I rowed over to Harbors — I had oils, watercolors & pencils with me — worked with the pencil awhile & then pulled to the Floating Batteries — there until after 12 — then home to dinner —
Some time reading but about 4.45 out with Anna & Emma rowing —
back soon after 6 & afterward went to Kimball's where we had tea & stayed until about 9.30 then out on the lake, rowing with the girls for an hour — then back & to bed by 11.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 17, 1880

Beautiful m'g. About 10 got over to my study of Blk. Mt. from Little Harbor channel — had a very unsatisfactory m'g of work. I cannot get any thing that I strive for. Very high wind and rough water at noon — back abt. 5 just in time to see Arthur Hawes, Walter Wright, & the boys, Geo., Oscar, & Edward off for the Hincon [illegible]. Alice & Adele Lemp also went. Had my dinner alone — Read for awhile, then to shops & painted on 1" Icehouse study — tea about 6.30 — Eve'g. Anna read a couple of little comedies by Julian Sturgis — after 9 — Anna, Emma, Helen & I went out on the lake — beautiful moon light & still water — near 11 when we came in — then to bed — during the night a heavy Rain & wind storm, with thunder, passed over us.
