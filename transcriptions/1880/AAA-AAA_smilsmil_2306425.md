## Left Page

### August, Monday 9, 1880

Very brisk breeze from the south
tempering a warm day — In the
mrg. I pulled over to one of
the Harbor Islands, selected
my study — foreground rocks
with a glimpse of distance.
Set up my traps & charcoaled
the outlines — To dinner soon
after 1 — then for some time I
read, sitting up on the piazza
enjoying the breeze — then
took water color paper and
roughly planned a drawing
with figure, looking up the line
of steps that leads to the boat house.
Tea at 6.30 — About 7.30 or 8, went
Rowing — around the Harbor Island
— charming — then home & chatting
until near 11 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 10, 1880

Very warm day — filmy, overcast mrg.
so that for some time I did not know
what work to set about, but at 10 o'clk
I went over to the island & painted
in sky & distance of study that I
charcoaled yesterday mrg. My
first color study for the season &
I worked outwardly — At 12 I
went into the water & had a bath
& then I went home & had dinner
at 1.30 — At 3 I returned to the
island, outlined up right of two
pines & painted in sky — at 6.30
I pulled to another part of the
island, where, upon a flat rock
commanding a lovely view south
Addie had arranged to have tea.
The Terrys came — 6 ladies — & we
had a merry time, with a bright
fire & some charming singing after.
At we left, at 8.30 one of the Paulist Fathers
came down to inform us that we
had been trespassing — Home at
9 — chatting — to bed at 11.
