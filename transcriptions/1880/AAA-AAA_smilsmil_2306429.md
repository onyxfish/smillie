## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 17. 1880.

Another remarkably fine day — Got up with indications of a headache but took two doses of Caffine & got the better of it. Mr. & Mrs. Dix left at 10 o'cl. for their home. — then I went to my foreground study on Harbor Island & got it all laid in by 12.30 — then to dinner at 1.15 — After that went to my up right pine study on Harbor Island & finished the laying in of that except one tree — then pulled awhile before returning, visiting one of the camps — Tea at 6.30 After that, Addie, Miss B, Messrs Hawes, Biddulph & I rowed to the P.O. & then over to Deer's trap & down the lake to [levating] Battery Islands — marvellously beautiful night — everything conspiring together for good — In & to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 18. 1880.

Weather threatening — after bkfs. went to a point just beyond Mrs. [Amy's] — Mrs. S. met us & invited me to join a pic-nic to Paradise Bay — I begged off & went to my work — after getting my subject outlined the wind blew so & the water flew so that I my work were completely spattered so that I had to give up. As I felt very unlike work I wandered about for some time hunting new subjects but not finding what I wanted I returned to the house — Dinner a little before 1. Mr. Biddulph left immediately after. I rowed to N.E. Harbor Island and painted a little sketch of a rock bit at the end of the island. Back at 6. Tea near 7 — Eve'g. Arthur Hawes & I played whist against Addie & Miss B.
