## Left Page

### January, Friday 30, 1880

A mean sort of day — overcast & threatening — yet to
studio at 9.30 and in the
m[orning]. finished the Village.
Dinner at 2 — bothered with
headache. — Afternoons, note
to Koehler, packed my pen
sketches & got them off by
Exp. Father came in &
had tea with us at [illegible] —
It was near 9 when I went
to N.A. Press View of 13th annual
[exhibit] of Water Color Society —
my head troubled me so
that I left soon after 10 — went
to Studio where Father was
& we started out together, he
to go to Charles — Rain commenc[ed]
to fall I had to return for my
umbrella — To 37th St. about 11
& right to bed.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 31, 1880

Unpromising m[orning] for Buyers
"Day" at Water Color [exhibit] — but
between 10 & 11 the weather
cleared charmingly. Spent
the m[orning]. house cleaning &
had dinner about 2 — the
afternoon was given up to
entertaining friends who
came over from the W.C.[Society] —
a great success there in the
way of sales — my "Hay Ricks"
3 of my small drawings and
3 of my etchings sold.
Father breakfasted with us in
the m[orning]. & went to Plainfield
to spend Sunday. Tea at
[illegible] — at 8.30 went to N.A. by
invitation of T. W. Wood — there
until 10 — then walked up
to 37th St. with J.G. Brown & W.
Pike — to bed at 11.
