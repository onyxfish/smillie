## Left Page

### October, Friday 8, 1880.

Another charming day — Bkfst at 8.
After that, to see Koehler — not in —
waited about until 11 — then saw
him & Linton — at 12 went over
together to Art Museum — near
2 when Geo & I dined at a Res-
taurant — Then more parading
of the street — after 3.30 again
went to Koehler & there met
Mr. C. C. Perkins — a pleasant
chat, then to Art Museum —
met Geo there — out at 5 and
walked through Commonwealth
Ave., Public Gardens & from the
Monument hill on the Common
saw the sunset — then to Parker
House & rested until near 7 —
then tea — met F. H. Smith &
Mr. Laffan — at 8 went to 92
Mt. Vernon St. & spent the
evg until 10 o'clk very pleas-
antly with Kitty Ireland —
then to P. House & to bed before
11.

## Right Page

### October, Saturday 9, 1880.

Still delicious weather — Bkfst bet. 8 & 9.
The whole of the mfg. was spent
running around, much of the time
shopping for a trunk — finally bought
one for $23 — Extravagant, I know.
Dinner at the Parker Ho. soon after
1 — at 3 Geo & I went to Mr. Bassett's
office & with him went to Eastern R.R. &
took 3.15 train to Lynn — there at 3.45
Mr. B. met us in their carriage & we
drove at once out to Nahant where
we saw all its loveliness under the
most favorable conditions of light &
atmosphere — after sunset, climbed to
"High Rock" & had a beautiful, sombre
twilight view — then to Mr. B's house
to tea — I would have had a very
good time but my beastly dinner
upset me. At 9.15 Mr. B. walked
with us to the depot — took 9.30
train in to Boston — in at 10 — to
Parker House & to bed by 10.45
