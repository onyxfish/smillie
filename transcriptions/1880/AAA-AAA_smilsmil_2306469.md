## Left Page

### November, Friday 5, 1880

Heavy rain all the night and fog & drizzle the rest of the time. Near 9 when I have b[reakfast]. Then to studio — much time spent over Japan paper — finally at work also on Anthony's drawing "large moon of India" — At 1:30 out to dinner — then to foot of Christopher St. & took 2:30 train to Maplewood. Jno. Durand met me at depot — went at once to his place. A.B.D — found the old gentleman in a pleasant, talkative mood. Went to his studio, looked over his studies & selected me to etch for Am. Art Rev[iew] — called on Mr. Woodman — took 5:20 train back & got to studio at 7 — met Alice Jenny on the train — was at studio — nearly spent there in mounting sketches & writing notes until 10 o'clk. Then to 37th St. & at 11 to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Saturday 6, 1880

Heavy wet day without rain. Near 9 again before I had b[reakfast]. Then to studio — many callers & little work — Anthony came in from Boston & called about the etching book. — I finished "large moon" drawing — Near 2 when I had dinner — most of the afternoon spent fussing over the mounting of some etching [of] "the way to the River" on very light Japan paper. Tea in studio at 6:30 then to 37th St. & at 8 to A.C.S.'s and with her to Coleman's — no rain until just as we got to the house — Mr. C's birthday. Mr. Ireland, [Sinneart?] Grace, Mr. & Mrs. Howells. Will K. & [get] there — at 10:30 took [h.c.c.] home & at 11 o'clk I was in my own little bed.
