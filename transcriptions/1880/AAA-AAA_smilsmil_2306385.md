## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 19, 1880

Cool, overcast day, but no rain — Got to studio about 9 + to work on "Hail Columbia" — all the m'g at it — to lunch at a little after 2 — In the afternoon was quite used up with a neuralgic-like pain in the stomach — at 5 o'clk. Jas. R. Osgood came to talk up a proposed book of etchings. R.S. Gifford came in by my invitation — I think we can come to a satisfactory understanding. Tea in studio at 8 — Sat + Read "fool's Errand" until 10.30 — at 11 to 39' St. + to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 20, 1880

Hot + [illegible] atmosphere again — Got to studio about 9.30 — Exps. man came with big box from Duvell, marked $9.60 charges — I would not take it — wrote a letter at once to Miss Robbins about it. Also wrote a letter to Ehninger. To lunch before 2 — Don't think that I did much of any thing during the afternoon but at 6.45 went to Cennie's + found the store shut — then to Hotel Royal + had tea — then to 39' St. — met Fred + wife — dressed + soon after 8 called on Mr + Mrs. Robt. Gordon + Miss G — + soon after 9 went to Anna C's — there until 10.30 — then to 39' St. + to bed.
