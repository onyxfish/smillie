## Left Page

### November, Monday 1. 1880.

Beautiful day — in the after
noon squares threatened from
the north — After blkft. I had a
delightful walk — called at
man's & bid her good bye —
then home & spent the rest
of the mts. Packing a box of
Japan paper & most of my
etching traps — Dinner at 1 —
After that packed a trunk
with winter under clothes
blankets etc & sent them off
by Am Exps. — Then had a quiet
rest — Then out & had a walk —
Tea at 6 — Left home at 7.15
went to depot & took 7.45
train to N.Y. Half an hour late
— got in at 10.30 — went to 37
St. & got to bed shortly after
11.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 2. 1880.

Glorious day — could not resist
an invitation to go on a walk.
Blkft at 9 — after that — unpacked
my trunk & at 11, was at Grand
Central Depot — met there Geo
& Anna Cook — Will K. & Fanny —
Mr. Valentine & Chas. Collins —
We went to Yonkers & there crossed
in a row boat to the other side
of the River — climbed to the top
of the Palisades by a good road
& following me still better upon
the tops, walked north two or three
miles then stopped & upon a su-
perb cliff at about 1.30 had an ample
lunch. After that wandered for an hour
or more, then walked back & at 4.30 met
our boat — Ret. to Yonkers & by 5.20 train to
N.Y. getting in at 6.10 — then to Anna's
to dinner & at 8 went to foot of Chris-
topher St. to Str. Scythia — met there Geo
& Mrs. Coleman & we bid Corinne and
Charley Smitts good bye — at 10, to studio
all walked to 37 St. & to bed —
