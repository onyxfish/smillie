## Left Page

### August, Sunday 29, 1880

Overcast day with a great deal of threatening & a little Rain — went out for a row between 10 & 11, then to the T. cottage where a number of the neighbors assembled T. Read a part of the Episcopal Service & (to me) a very flat, commonplace sermon by Dr. Pusy — Home at 12.30. Then dinner — after that a little reading & more napping, then a row before tea which was about 6.30 — Everts Read aloud a chapter or two from Carlisle

## Right Page

### August, Monday 30, 1880

Beautiful day. After bkft. note a letter to Father then out to my work — study of Blk. Mt. & Rock foregrounds on little channel, Harbor islands — no end of petty troubles — worked until 1 oclk — then to dinner Afternoon, again returned to the islands — delicious reflections on the water — made a wild dash to catch something of it — again no end of trouble — breeze sprang up — Reflections gone — sun reflecting from rippled water into my face — almost wild, but got a sketch. by sticking to it — at 6, Mr. Condit came to me & after a short pull, we joined the party & pic-nicked — lovely sunset — after tea we rowed about & got in about 8 — Some High, low, Jack & to bed at 10.30
