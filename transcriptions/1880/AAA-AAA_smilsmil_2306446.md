## Left Page

### September, Monday 20, 1880

Overcast & showery during the m'f. About 9.30 Anna, Helen
& I went to P.O. & after that, up the hill to Foster's & from there
along a brook in the woods — part of the time it rained quite
heavily — Some parts of the walk through the woods was very
beautiful — Back shortly before dinner — After that did some
writing — about the middle of the afternoon there was a great out
cry from the breakwater — Mabel had fallen from the end of the
pier & was in the water some minutes before she was rescued
by Mr. Lemp's man who jumped in & swam for her — of course there
was great excitement — but singularly, Mabel seemed in no way injured.
Tea at 6.30 — until 10 o'clk. we sat up on the [piazza] then for half an
hour I tried to draw shadow profiles without success — To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 21, 1880

Brilliant, cloudless m'f., with high wind — later it clouded over
& at night the clouds were thick & dark & the wind was wild. Mr.
Condit left this m'f. About 10 o'clk.
Anna & I rowed to the Harbor Islands where I got to work upon Blk. Mt.
Study from the Channel — Anna joined the others in a fishing party.
They returned for me at 1 — when I had finished my study — then a
hard pull for home & dinner at 1.30 — After that read papers until
4 then went out for a walk with Anna & got a couple of pencil
studies — back at 5.30 & had a rest before tea. Ev'g., again
made attempts at shadow pictures & succeeded in getting
good ones of Surma & Anna —
To bed at 10.30. A night of very high wind.
