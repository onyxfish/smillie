## Left Page

### September, Friday 24. 1880.

Another delightful day — sent
telegram to Geo at Portland telling
him that I would join him on Monday
& one to Ehninger saying that I could not
join him & wife on Blue Mt. Lake Trip.
Before 11 Addie, Anna, Emma, Helen
the girl Kate, Mr. Jeliffe & I started
for Squire Joe Barrett's at Dresden
some 4 or 6 miles away — got to the
house about 1. — C. J. Holcomb sued
Addie for $4.90 on his boy's wages.
The Trial soon commenced & was to
us all extremely interesting. If a
writer like Howells could describe
what occurred there, the odd
speeches — quaint sayings & cited
like ignorance — & if an illustrator
could draw the scenes, doing justice
to the characters — it seemed the
immense — at 2 we lunched in the
corn house — a jury of 4 was after
much trouble obtained — & about 8 the
Trial was over — verdict: no cause
for action — Lily, assisted by Jeliffe
for deft — Davis for plaintiff — About 9
we started for home — moon up before
we got in at after 11 — then & some
supper — & to bed after 12 —

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 25. 1880.

Lovely day — After bkft. worked
for some time on charcoal sketch
& at about 11 Addie, Anna,
Emma, Helen & I got away in
the Snark for Wilson's Cottage, South
of Sabbath Day Point — from there
walked up the road back of Dee's
Gap to an old woman's house, then
back to boat & rowed home, getting
in to dinner at about 2. Mr. White
arrived — After dinner read Rienza
for awhile, then to work on char-
coal sketch — finished it & put
in in its panel — & at 5.30 went
out rowing with Emma — in at
6.30 — Tea about 7.30. Mr. & Mrs.
Cht. Kimball, Mr. & Mrs. Jeliffe, Mr.
Condit, Mr. White were with us by
invitation — Mr. Terry & Lee called —
a pleasant eve'g on piazza
until after 10 — After 11 when
I got to bed.
