## Left Page

### December, Sunday 5, 1880

*Red Letter Day.*

Horrible day of heavy rain & wind. Up late — had bkft. at 10 & at 11 went to A.C.S's — an epoch in my life — I wrote to Anna from Portland, on the mt of 1 uct. Feb. 28th — & since that she has had my proposition under advisement — This out she surrendered unconditionally & I too surrendered upon the same terms — The mt was spent, I don't quite know how — Dinner about 2 — about 3.30 went to studio & wrote letters home — at 6 again at Anna's & with her & Geo. Cook went to Mr. Ireland's to tea — meeting young Conway & Mr. [illegible] there — About 10 we left & walked to 38th St. — I got to 37th S. about 11, saw Geo. for a few minutes — told him of my good fortune & then to bed at about 11.30.

## Right Page

### December, Monday 6, 1880

A dismal day — close & heavy, but clearing at night. Got to studio aftrng & altho' somewhat upset went bravely to work tracing & outlining "old trees" — Out to lunch at 1.45 — a short walk — then in & worked as long as I could see — then wrote notes to father, Mary & Matt — At 6 went in to Colman's & took dinner with them, telling them of my great good fortune — at 7.30 I got started for Brooklyn — crossed So. Ferry & walked to 368 Union S. Mt. Kenyon's where the Rembrandt Club met to hear a paper on Private Illustration of Books — very interesting — I am asked to read a paper before them in Feb. on Etching — Got to 37th S. at 11.15 & right to bed.
