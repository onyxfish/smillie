## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 29, 1880

Very cold with fine falling snow all day — got to studio at 9.30 & to work on Belk. M[illegible] — am getting into a mess with it — near 2 when I went out to lunch — after that went to Gifford's studio and found him in — T. W. Wood called at the studio twice — Young Osborne also in the afternoon — Soon after 4 went to the Riding School & rode Coleman's horse — too much out of sorts to enjoy the ride — pain in stomach — walked to 37 S. — Rested — Scrubbed & changed underclothes, then to studio & at 7.30 had tea, spent the eve[nin]g fixing cards to send out for New Years — to 37 S. at 10.30 & to bed at 11 — Bitter cold night with strong wind.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 30, 1880

*Uncle Geo. found dead this morning.*

Intensely cold with a wind that almost took away my breath — got to studio before 10 — so cold that I could not work, so I stretched paper, fussed, traced & retraced "View near Portsmouth" — Had lunch about 2 — then back to studio & worked until after 4 — took a short rest — Mrs. Coleman & Miss Abbatt came in — After 5 when I started out, met at door a boy with telegram (in pocket of this book) announcing the tragic death of Uncle George. Then to Annie's & with her to [illegible] tea, & at 8 to Jol T. Johnston's, then to studio, there until 10.15 finishing cards for New Years calls, then to Jil. Jly & with Annie home — late — to 37 S. after 11 and right to bed.
