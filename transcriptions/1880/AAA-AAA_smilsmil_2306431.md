## Left Page

### August, Saturday 21, 1880

Heavy rain very early in the mt. Got up without refreshment after a bad night. I suppose that I overexerted myself yesterday. Heavy clouds hung & the water lay dark & placid until after 10 then it cleared hot & muggy. I spent the morning on piazza except when I went to Kimball Cottage & had Mr. Fisher help me mend the seat of my camp stool. Dinner soon after 1 — afternoon spent no more actively than the mt. The discomfort in my head gradually passing away — Strong wind — about sundown the Jerry girls came & had a swimming time off our dock — Tea at 6.30 — Ruel, Addie, Arthur Harves & I went to the landing & got to bags of meal, then to washerwoman's & got clothes; then, after coming home — at 10, superb moonlight — to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 22, 1880

A perfect day, almost cloudless & with a fresh, invigorating breeze. Bkfs. at 8 — about 10 I took my boat & pulled over to one of the Harbor Islands & finding a pleasant spot spent most of the mt. there reading & writing — Back at 12.45 & found that Cyrus Butter & daughter had called in his little steam launch — Dinner at 1.30 — After that a nap in the hammock by the water, sung to sleep & swung by the breezes among the trees. Then, Read & wrote until sun-down then pulled over to the Islands & watched a beautiful effect of the sunlight creeping up the eastern mts. followed by purple shadows — Tea at 6.30 — Ruel on piazza until near 9 when Addie & I went out in the "Adirondack" for an hour or so — To bed soon after 10.
