## Left Page

### March, Thursday 18, 1880

Pleasant day but my head so bunged up from my cold that I could do no work —
much of the m'f was spent with Colman, Gifford, and Waller making up list for the Met. Museum invitations (no,
by the way, that work was done this, Friday, m'f.) little was
done besides calling on the lounge
& calling on Edw. Moran — Had
dinner at 2 — then to Gifford's
then to [illegible] of Socy of Am. Artists
— much amused — then to Wm.
Beard's & at 5.30 to studio — at
6.45 to Anna Cooke's — after dinner
as usual to Arnold's, there to Mad.
Roch's — 713 Mad. Ave — a little
musical affair — there until near
11 — then to 37th St. & to bed at
11.40.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 19, 1880

Atrocious day — first with rain
then with snow & slosh indescribable —
More used up than yesterday —
much of the m'f was spent
making out a list of artists to
be invited to opening of Met.
museum — Dinner at 1 — then
in a heavy snow storm to the
Phil. rehearsal — which, in
spite of my condition I enjoyed
very much — By 5 back to
studio & again on lounge —
Tea about 4 — [illegible] much
of the time on lounge — did
a little writing. to 37th St.
& to bed soon after 11, having
taken 6 grs. of quinine.
