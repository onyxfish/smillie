## Left Page

### April, Saturday 17. 1880.

Lowering, blustering day — a little rain in the m[orning], then wind changed to west & N, m[aking it] got to studio about 9.45 & after a time to work on zinc plate. "Cedar Meadow" etching in 2°, grounded and biting — Lunch at 2. — short walk, then back — Beside fussing over my zinc I layed grounds on three coppers — at 6 went to 37' S² & after 7 back to studio & had tea [&] suet. Made a long letter to Kate Gilbert about hotels in the Adirondack Region to 37' S. after 11 & to bed a little before 12.

## Right Page

### April, Sunday 18. 1880.

Delicious day — Up at 7 and at 7.45 went to Anna C's and had [illegible] — then to foot of 34 S[t.] met Collins, Emma J., Mrs Emmerson & niece & Valentine by & J. R.R., went to Woodbury getting there at 10.30. Went right away to the woods and strolled about looking for & finding lots of Arbutus — had lunch about 1 — & about 2, having filled our baskets we walked a mile & a half or two miles out on the Oyster Bay Road finding many lovely views — Returned to N.Y. by 4.25 train getting back at 6 — went to 37' S[t.], brushed up a little & at 7 went to Mr. Cook's to dinner. [After?] arranged Arbutus & [illegible] ret'd to 37' S— Read [Trib?] until after 10, then to bed.
