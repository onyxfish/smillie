## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 26. 1880.

Mild, overcast day — growing thicker until about 4 p.m. —
when it began to sprinkle —
Blkfs. at 8 — I feel much fagged
& spent most of the mng on
the lounge in studio — Finally
made up my mind that I
was not fit to go down to the
Tweedy's, as I had promised,
so, when I went out to dinner
at 2 — I telegraphed to Belle.
The afternoon passed almost
as energetically at the morning.
I wrote some notes and at
6.30 had tea in the studio.
At 8, went in to Coleman's &
chatted & played cribbage
until 10 — then to 39th S. —
Starlight night — to bed
at 11.

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 27. 1880.

Very pleasant day — Blkfs. at
8.30 — After that went to Academy
of Music & bought Phil. tickets —
then to studio. Got to work
at last up on drawing "Old Trees"
for A.V.S. Anthony — several callers,
among others, my old pupil, Miss
Spring (now Mrs. Alsdo) of Pittsburgh.
To dinner at 2 — As I wished to
finish drawing, I telegraphed father
that I would not go to Po'k. until
tomorrow — worked as long as I
could see — then out with printer's
ink to get it put into tubes for J.W.
Ehninger. Could not find anyone
to do it — back to studio at 6.30
& had tea at 7. At 8 went
with Gee to the Kimball's — saw
Mrs. K. & Carrie — left at 10.
To 39th S. & to bed about 11.
