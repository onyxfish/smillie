## Left Page

### October, Sunday 10, 1880

Mather still as beautiful as ever.
Bkfst at 8.30 — at 10 walked across
the Common & garden — lovely — to
the Holy Trinity — Enjoyed exceedingly
color, form & music — quite ecstatic —
an excellent discourse from Phillips
Brooks — Paul to the Ephesians — where he
counts bodily suffering as nothing compared
to the development of the glory
that was within him — B. is not a
clear speaker & it was difficult at
times to follow him — then to new
"Old South" & more wandering — then
a rest & dinner at Parker Hse at 2
After that wrote until 4, then
rested awhile & at 5.30 sauntered
up to Mr. S.R. Niles' — Beacon St —
where we were rec'd by him &
son — Mr. N. is in Virginia — we
had tea about 6.30 — chatted
awhile — then up stairs to young
N's studio where we stayed
until after 9 — then to Parker Hse
& to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Monday 11, 1880

*marble head neck.*

Still beautiful weather. After bkfst
went to Chauncy St. to call on Mr. H.F.
Shaw — not in — then to Mr. Bassett's
then bought tickets for [illegible], then to Mr.
Koehler's & out with him; went to
Gupiengreep's, Dewing's & Porter's
studios & the Art Club Rooms to see a
large painting by Courbet — at 1 o'clk
to Tremont Hse. Rest'd. Had lunch then
to Parker Hse. & had a half hour rest, then
to Mr. Bassett's & with him took 2.55
train to Lynn where again Mr. B.
met us with carriage & we had the
most interesting ride yet to Marble-
head & beyond — & about Marble Head
neck — then a glorious coast drive into
Lynn — tea at 6.45 — then directed to take
7.12 train — mistake, had to wait until 8.45
Boston at 9.30 — Drove right to Anthony's, 196
Beacon St. a little dinner party — Lawrence,
Hutton — Mrs. Clements — Frank Vinton — Miss
Hooper (I think) — J.R. Osgood — Mr. & Miss H. —
one or two others — a pleasant hour
there — then walked to P. Hse. accompanied
by Vinton — To bed at 11.30.
