## Left Page

### May, Friday 7, 1880

Pleasant but warm — Got to
studio about 9 & spent
the m'f over my etching
of "Red Cedars" & got some b's-
fit — think that I have
injured rather than bettered
it — Near 3 when I went
out & got some lunch. After
that went down to Scribner's
with drawing of West Point —
Back to studio about 4.30
& took some more proofs —
Tea in the studio & at 8
went to Emma J's & made
a call of about 15 m. Then
to Miss Ensted's where I saw
her & Miss Canning — Geo.
joined me later — To 37°
S— about 11 & right to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 8, 1880

Lovely day — at ten 6k [or?] went to
studio & wrote a letter to Ehninger
& at 10.35 at Grand Central Station
met Mr & Mrs. Coleman, Anna C. &
Fanny K, & went to Irvington.
Spent the rest of the m'f on "The
Point" — at 12.30 went to Tiffany's
piazza & at 1 all went to Carroll
Dunham's house & had lunch.
at 2, Emma J — Elizabeth Home
& Chas. Collins came up — after
more lounging on piazza, all
went to the Williams place &
there saw Mr. W. & daughters —
then back on the hills & rambled
& sketched during the rest of the
afternoon, which was heavenly —
at 6.31 took train & got in to N.Y.
at 7.40 — a mizzling fog. Took
home Anna C. & Ely. "H — then Fanny
K & went in & had some tea —
abg, went to studio — then to
37° S— & to bed at 10.30.
