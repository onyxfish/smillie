## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 11, 1880

Another charming day — fresh breeze from the N.E. At near
10 o'clk. I rowed over to the island & worked until near
1, on oil study of foreground —
then rowed over to dinner.
After that, read newspapers &
had a nap — After 3 when
again I rowed to island
& painted on upright study
of pines until after 6 —
then again returned
home & had tea — Every
clouded up, threatening
rain — wrote letters to
father & George. To bed
about 10.30.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 12, 1880

Charming day again — got up
with threatening of headache —
Miss Boughton gave me Caffine
& I spent the m'f. watching
the men behind the dock.
Dinner about 1.30 — after that
my headache continued to
grow worse, so I took a
nap — Mr Arthur Hawes & his
friend Mr Riddulph arrived
tea at 6.30 — & before 9 o'clk.
I was obliged to give up &
go to bed where I passed
a miserable night owing
to pain.
