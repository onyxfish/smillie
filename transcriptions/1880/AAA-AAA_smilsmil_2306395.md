## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 8. 1880.

Cool, overcast day, getting to be positively damp & rain at night. To studio at 9 and at 10.30 started down — from with a lot of messages to do — went to Mundorlich's, Harpers A.B.N. Co. & other places — lunched at A.B.N. Co. & back to studio a little before 3 — Remainder of p.m. packing etching traps & at 6.15 got & were at Mr. Morse's — 112 W. 38 — took tea & afterwards a party adjourned to Mr. Sewell's — Mad Ave. near 75th S. — where the even was spent looking at old etchings — mainly Rembrandt's Dürer's & Turner's — left there at 10.30 — walked to 37th S. & to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 9. 1880.

Mild, pleasant day, partially overcast. Still podging about the studio, all the m'f being spent in picking up & tying up little odds & ends in bundles. Lunch at 2.30 — then back to studio & after 4 went down to Kimmel & Voigt's (printers) & had several final proofs pulled by the printer there from my etching "Old Cedars". back to studio at 6 — out to tea at 7 — back again and every spent writing letters & picking up things. To 34th S. at 9.30 & to bed soon after 11.
