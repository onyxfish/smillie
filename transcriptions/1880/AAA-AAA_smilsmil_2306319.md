## Left Page

### January, Thursday 8, 1880

Charming day — nearly 0 when I got to studio —
Started at W. C. Sketch
about 10 × 14 — twilight —
based upon slight out-
line sketch that I got
at Poughkeepsie some time
ago. To dinner at 2 —
worked until near 5 but
did not feel that I had
accomplished much.
At 6, Geo + I went to
Anita C's to dinner +
after that took a C. to Miss
Arnold's — then we called
at Miss Douglass 15 W. 39' — just
going out to a German — Then
I went to Mr. Adam's 125
E. 38 45 — a pleasant call until
9\. Then to Union League Club
there until after 11. Then
to 37 S. + to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 9, 1880

Miserable, dirty day — Got
to Studio's at 9.45 — Cyrus
Butler & daughter called —
After various interruptions
started my big drawing
at about 11 — to dinner
at 2 — back + to work about
3 + stuck to it until near
5, getting in my sky in
a way that encourages
me. Heard to-day that
S.E.F.'s husband Mr. Hammond
died a few days since.
At 7.30 went for Emma — then to
5th Ave. Theatre to hear "Pirates of the
Penzance" — Mr. & Mrs. Colman, Louis & Mary
Tiffany, Harry Gray, his mother & sister —
Leila Marteus, Emma + I constituted
the party. The opera was exceedingly
funny & well put upon the stage —
the house was crowded + uncomfortable
with many cold draughts — At
10.30 Emma + I went home with the
Colmans where we had crackers and
Cherry Bounce — then home with
Emma + got to 34 S. Shortly after
midnight + right to bed.
