## Left Page

### December, Friday 31, 1880

Brilliant day — very cold
but not so cold as yesterday.
In to-days Times, full acct. of the
death of Uncle Jee (in pocket
of this book) Got to studio at
9.30 — before I got to work. R.
Swain Gifford came in & made
quite a long call — after that
I got to work up on "New num-
Portsmouth" washing in [illegible]
out to lunch at 2 & had a
short walk — worked until
4 — wrote a letter to aunt
Ollie — had tea in studio, [illegible]
7 — at 8 called on Miss King
& secured rooms for father and
mother — at 9 got to [illegible]
Jee came in, having been to
Mils's, with additional [illegible]
from Plainfield — At 10.30
went to 37 St. & to bed bef[ore]
11.
