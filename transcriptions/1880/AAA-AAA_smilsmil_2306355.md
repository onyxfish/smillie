## Left Page

### March, Saturday 20, 1880

Charming day overhead —
got up after a good nights
rest feeling wonderfully better —
went to studio and spent
part of the mtg finishing up
an open sketch for the
Art Interchange Album for
the Irish Relief Fund. — To
dinner at 2 — went to Putnam
Afternoon made crayon
sketch "On the cliff by the
sea" for same album. —
At 6 went to Fanny K.'s
to tea — at 8 — to Philharmonic
concert — lovery evening — then
heavy rain storm — at 10.30
delicious moonlight — went
home with Anna Cook —
to 37 S: + to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### March, Sunday 21, 1880

Another charming day —
Breakfast at 10.30 — then
went to the Ch. of the Messiah.
Park Ave. & 34th S: — heard Robt.
Collyer preach from Jul. 12th ch.
27th v. After that went to 37th
S: then to studio & at 1.15
to Coleman's to dinner — about
3.30 went with Mrs. C. to Flo.
Grey's — a short call — put
them in a 4th Ave. car — then
I returned to studio and
wrote until 5 — then mt —
walked about until 6 — then
to Kimball's to tea — there
until after 9 — then again to
studio — wrote until near
11 — then to 37th S: + Right to
bed.
