## Left Page

### September, Thursday 2, 1880

Clouded m[?]. Threatening
rain. After b[reakfast] — went to
the islands with water colors
& commenced a sketch of
some rocks, but before I, the
sun shone out & as I had no
umbrella I had to give up my
work. Rowed to [N.W.] side of the
lake — got pencil sketches.
Back to dinner early — After
that, went again to the islands
& as it had clouded over I
again worked on water color
until after 6 — then to tea.
Eveg. in chatting & a short
visit to the Jerry's to bid good
bye to Miss Alice T. & Miss [Pease]
who leave to-morrow. Then back
& cards with Arthur on Piazza
until after 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 3, 1880

Again a cloudy m[or]n[ing]. Threatening
rain. At 9.30 started a rock study
at the beach — sun shone out before
noon & I had much trouble — could
not get subject all in before
dinner at 1.30 — after that worked
indoors for awhile. Three feeling
very unlike work, at about 3.30
I went to the dock hoping to get
a sail with Arthur Harris but he
was too far away. The Gawonskie
made a landing at our dock
with Miss Grenelle & May Condit —
made some pencil sketches of
boats — about 5.30 Addie came
down & took me out rowing for
nearly an hour. Lovely evening —
mild & quiet. Tea at near[ly] —
[illegible]. Whist — Arthur & 'Del
Jerry &c. Miss B. & J. To bed
at 10.30 — a very little shower
& very warm night — unrefreshing
sleep.
