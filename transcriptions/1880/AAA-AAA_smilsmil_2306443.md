## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 14, 1880

Cool, windy m[illegible], as I could not work out of doors I proposed a climb under Deer's Leap — some fears of Rattlesnakes were expressed — these being overcome, Addie, Anna, Emma, Hu & I started off in the Viking & had a very rough row. Addie stayed in the boat to fish while the rest of us climbed — we got to the top of the smaller ledge north of Deer's Leap & there encountered two Rattlesnakes but they got away from me. After a couple of sketches & a hard climb down we reached the boat at 1 o'clk & had a very rough row back. Arthur Hawes came out & met us at Vicar Island. Dinner at 2. Letters from Ge. & Father — much of the p.m. which was dark & cloudy, in my bed reading — a short row before tea at 6.30 — Sue, H., Arthur & Helen app[?]. Anna & me at Whist — To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 15, 1880

Rain during the night & much rain & N.E. wind in the morning. Wrote a letter to Geo. & sent it to Mr. Ayres Davis at Portland. Then got to work in doors on my sketch from Kimball's piazza, repainting it. Had dinner at 1.15 — no rain then & some appearance of blowing over. Again worked on my sketch until after 4. Then Emma & I rowed over to Deer's Leap & tried to raise the entangled anchor that we left yesterday — no use. Tea as usual & eve[nin]g divided between Whist & reading — To bed before 11.
