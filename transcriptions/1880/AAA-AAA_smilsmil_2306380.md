## Left Page

### May, Sunday 9, 1880

Up at about 6.45 — overcast
m'f— but I hurried through
dressing & breakfast & got to
the foot of Liberty S', in time
to [illegible] the 8 45 train out
to Plainfields — met Elizabeth
Home on the boat on her way
out to her home at Rozell,
went at once to the Tweedy's
house — weather burned off
clear & hot — I loafed about
the house all day. Dinner
about 1 — Tea between 6 & 7 —
Ev'g, with Belee, called on
Mr. Hart & sisters (Burton's
daughters) & at 9 went to
Mr. Herman White's — there
until after 10 — then back
to J's & to bed at 10.45

## Right Page

### May, Monday 10, 1880

Horribly hot day — Bkfst.
about 8 o'clk., Soon after 10 —
Belee, Mary & I drove to Miss
Lightner's — she joined us & we
rode until 1 o'clk — [illegible] —
the time the wind was fresh
against us & was delightful —
the tender Spring growth — the
blossoms & the color were lovely
beyond description. Dinner
at 1.30 & took 2.48 train in
to N.Y. Stopped at A.B.N.
Co — then to studio at 4.30 —
wrote letters until 6.
Tea in studio — at 8 went to A.F.
Bellows studio, altho' he is in
Boston, Annual meeting of Etching
club held there — I declined &
Gifford was elected Pres. At 9.45
I went down to Colman's where
they had music — At 11.30 I
went home with Anna C — and
at near 12 to 37 S'. & to bed.
Slight showers during p.m.
