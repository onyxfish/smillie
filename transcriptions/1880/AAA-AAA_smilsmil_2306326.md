## Left Page

### January, Thursday 22, 1880

Dismal, overcast day. The girls prepared a b[illegible] of chops which I carefully ate in bed at about 10 o'clk — did not get up until after 11 — then went to Rennie's & to Dr. Yale's — got to studio about 1.30 & out with Geo. to dinner at 2. Back about 3 & fixed my drawing in its frame while Russell & his son waited — finally it was carried off much to my relief. Rain commenced falling heavily about 5 — went up to Anna Cook's in a drencher at 6.30 — did not go to Miss Arnold's with Anna & Fanny but until near 9 stayed with Mr. C. & Geo. C. — then to 39th S — Chatted an hour with the girls & at 10 got to bed feeling quite comfortable.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 23, 1880

Charming, April-like day, but for all that feel very seedy — Got to studio about 9.30 — filled out my W.C. list & went to N.A., with by there to Morse's & saw Salmagundi Sketch club call, then to Knoedler's & about 12 back to studio just in time to meet Judge Hall of E-Town & W. Storms of Boston from whom I had a pleasant call. At 1, out to dinner & at 2 to Phil. Rehearsal — rather a tame affair — after it, walked up town with Anna Cook — to studio before 5 — went out again for a walk — Tea at [illegible] and at 6 [illegible] to [illegible] and [illegible]
