## Left Page

### September, Saturday 18. 1880.

Heavy rain during much of the day. After breakfast, while walking on the piazza saw a charming effect that so pleased me that I attempted to paint it — Dinner at 1.30 — after that painted on my sketch at the front window until about 5 when I got it finished — as good a thing, I think, as I have done this summer. Then out rowing with Emma — wonderful twilight effects — pearly & placid. In some time after 6 — then tea — every did not feel well & sat by the fire until near 10, then as it had cleared to be a lovely moonlight night, Emma, Anna & I went out rowing — never saw Black Mt. look grander than in the vapory, luminous moonlight — In & to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 19. 1880.

Mild pleasant day, much of the time overcast — After a late help, Emma & Helen in the Adirondack & Anna & I in the Boojum went out rowing — back at 11 & went to Kimball's cottage where Mr. Condit had a sermon by Robertson. After that we sat under a pine by the lake until 1.30 — then dinner — after that on piazza until 4 o'ck when Emma, Anna, Helen & I went to the top of the High Rock & sat there until 6 — then home & had tea between 6 & 7 — some very light rain, which did not deter us from going out on the lake. Addie and Helen in the Adirondack, Anna & I in the Boojum — went first to Cook's dock with a basket of clothes, then rowed until after 10 — I never saw anything of the kind more beautiful — a cloud covered sky making broad effects light & shade that were reflected from a surface that much of the time was absolutely still — To bed about 11.
