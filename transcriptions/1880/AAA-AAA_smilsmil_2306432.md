## Left Page

### August, Monday 23, 1880

The hottest day yet, the constant breeze hardly tempering it — after
10:30. lounged about until 10 o'clk
then pulled up to the landing &
waited for the Horican which came
at near 11 — Geo. & Charle on board —
C. on his way home via Burlington
in charge of the Burnham girls who
are on their way to Mt. Desert. Geo.
stops for awhile here. Cyrus Butler
kept us for awhile & it was after 12
before we got home with Geo's heavy
trunk. Dinner after 1 — After more
sitting upon Piazza, Geo. & I went
to the top of the high Rock & spread
ourselves upon the moss where I
read some of Nummerous's poems aloud.
At sunset I pulled with Geo. to the
Harbor islands to watch every effect.
Back to tea about 7 — About 8:30 & 9
Addie & I rowed out taking Mr. [illegible]
Mr. Jewell & Geo. Some delicious effects
of moonlight & smooth water — in at
10 & to bed soon after.

## Right Page

### August, Tuesday 24, 1880

Still another very hot & breezy day —
up with unpleasant indications of
a headache — got Miss Broughton to
prepare some caffeine for me. After
10:30. kept quiet for some time. Then
with Geo. pulled to the Floating Battery
Islands, stopping at different places.
Came back at 12.30, sailing with an
umbrella before a strong southerly breeze.
Dinner about 1.15, after that fixed
myself comfortably in hammock under
the trees near the water & dozed away
an hour or two. Having Rec'd. a note
from Marion Butler saying that Henrietta
Beebe was with her we made preparations
to go down to Black Mt. Point — At 5
Addie, Arthur Hawes, Geo. & I pulled
down to the most southerly Battery & there
had tea, with a little spatter of rain
from a thunder storm north of us. Then
on to the Minnehaha where we found Marion
Miss Stebbins & Miss Beebe — we passed a
very pleasant eveg. Miss B. singing "Jamie"
"'Tis a dream" & the "Kerry Dance." Miss S —
played some on the piano — soon after 10
we pulled for home, having a good breeze
& a cloudless sky, doing the work in
35 m. (Arthur H. & D.) To bed soon after 11.
