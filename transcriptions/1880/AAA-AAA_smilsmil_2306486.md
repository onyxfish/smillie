## Left Page

### December, Thursday 9, 1880

Charming day, but rather dirty — growing colder — got to studio rather late as I stopped at Judge Skien's 9 E. 34th S. at 8.30 to see his painting by Durand. I was delayed by congratulatory letters that I had to answer. Traced Mariner's well & prepared to transfer it — lunch at 2 — in the afternoon Mrs. [illegible] Dart & Miss Woodruff called. Soon after 6 I went to Annie's a jolly dinner time — owing to the avowed failure the meeting was held at 26 E. 39th S. — after leaving the girls we went to Renner's — then to Union League — at 10 I returned to 26 E. 39th for Annie & took her home — to 37th S. & to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 10, 1880

Clear, very cold, windy & dirty. Blef. late — studio at 9.45, again much of the mtf lost with writing & visitors — Had a jolly call from Belle & Florence Tweedy — Spent part of the mtf. transferring tracing of "Mariner's Well" — Had a light lunch in studio at 1.30 then to Phil. Rehearsal — don't think much of the programme. Afterwt, walked up with Annie then to studio — wrote to Fred Ireland — At 6.15 to Annie's had dinner & at 8.30 with her to Colman's where we met the Irelands, Kimball's & Leila Martin. They spent the ev'g making silk covers for some of C's Japanese curios. It was near 11.30 when we left there & 12.30 when I got to 37th S. & to bed.
