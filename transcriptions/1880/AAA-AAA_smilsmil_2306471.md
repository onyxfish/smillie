## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 9, 1880

Very nice & pleasant. got to studio a little before 10 &
pussed around at lots of things
using up the whole morning
& really doing nothing — to
dinner at about 2 — after
that a walk then again
at studio & got things ready
to commence "Cedar Meadow
Swamp") 33×44 — Several callers
among them, Mrs. Hoe & sister.
At 4 o'clk. I went to foot of
Chris Falpherd's, & took 4.40 boat —
Geo. met me there & we went to
Montclair & to Addie Hawes' where
Fanny K. & Anna C. were waiting — we
had tea about 6.30 & I had a
charming eve'g. Dr. Dunning was
there & he with Addie seemed so
sweetly cheerful — We left by
9.40 train & got to 38th S. at about
11 — I then went to 37th S. and
right to bed.

## Right Page

### November, Wednesday 10, 1880

Mild, overcast day — feeling
rather under the weather. got
to studio at about 9.30, and
after awhile to work with char-
coal on 33×44 — Did not
apply myself very closely
& so the day went without
my having completed my
charcoal sketch — Dr. Mulford
called at 1 o'clk. & at 2, out
to dinner — after that went
to Louis Tiffany's and saw
a memorial glass window that
he has just completed, to be
set up in Newark. Back to
studio — there until 5 — then
strolled out & after a walk
went to Centuny — back at 6.30
& had tea in studio. After
writing letters went to Centuny,
there until 10.30 looking over
papers & magazines — then
to 37th S. & to bed after 11.
