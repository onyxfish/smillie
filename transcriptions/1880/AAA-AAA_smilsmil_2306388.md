## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 25, 1880

*Stoney Brook. Timson's.*

Hotter than ever. R[illegible] at 7.45. To 3° ave. Elevated R.R. at 8.30 — to Harlem in 21 m. crossed to N.H. + H. R.R. station + at 9.15 Took Train to Timson's where I arr'd. at 9.30 — went at once to the Abbott's + much of the m'f was spent on the piazza — took a short stroll with Miss A — then commenced a pen sketch of her for an etching that I have projected. Dinner at 2 — then finished sketch — again on piazza until at 6.05 I Took Train + got to Studio's at 7. Tea at Ashland Ho. At 8.9[illegible] + I called on Mr. & Mrs. Louis Tiffany. Back to studio's at 9 — wrote a little. To 39° S. ab't 11 + to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 26, 1880

Still horribly hot + dry. After [illegible] went to Dawson's + did not get to studio's until 10. Tried to get to work on A.B. N. Co. drawing, a N.O. Seal. Slow work — made a pen sketch in Stella McKnight's Album. Swain Gifford called about printing paper for Osgood's book of Etchings. To dinner at 2 — then to Johnston's to do some shopping — Back to studio's + worked until after 6 on drawing — Tea in studio — [illegible] there too until about 9. then walked up Park Ave. to 40th S. Found the Payne's out on their door step — sat there with them until after 10 — then to 37° S. + to bed before 11.
