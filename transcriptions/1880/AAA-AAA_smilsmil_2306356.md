## Left Page

### March, Monday 22, 1880

Still charming weather. Got to studio at 9.30 & commenced etching "Sunnyside" for Putnam — stuck to it closely, etching & biting all day — out to dinner at 2 — a short walk then back to studio — worked until 6, then out for a short walk. — Tea at 7 — at 8 ½ at Mr. Rob. Gordon's — commr. f Met. Museum. There until nearly — then to 37½ S. & to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 23, 1880

Pleasant day — sunshine & overcast with rain after 9 P.m. — Got to studio about 10, after a call on Miss Leggett. For some time engaged comparing Dutch Mordant — then taking [illegible] of etching "Sunnyside" Dinner at 1.30 — then a walk. The afternoon much troubled by visitors — managed to do some work on my plate, get a rebiting ground & stopping out done — Did not go out for my walk until near 7 — Tea late — met at studio milling until near 10. Then T. 37½ S. & to bed at 11.
