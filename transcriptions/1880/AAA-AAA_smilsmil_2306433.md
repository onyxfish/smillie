## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 25, 1880

Very high wind all day long, blowing from the north & covering the lake with white-caps — the early m'f was overcast with a slight spattering of rain, but it gradually cleared from the north. In the m'f. I walked with Ge° to P.O. & washerwoman's — Back to dinner soon after 1 — In the afternoon finished my letter to Harry Fenn & at about sundown Ge° & I went out for a row, finding the water quite rough. Tea at about 5.30 — most of the ev'g I spent reading aloud from Arnold's "Light of Asia" — to bed before [illegible]

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 26, 1880

Splendid day, cloudless & cool with fresh breeze from the north, nearly 10 o'clk. When Ge° & I went out to Harbor Islands where I worked on Rock foreground study. Back to dinner at 1.30. After dinner took Fraps over to the Kimball cottage & from the piazza painted until sundown — then, with Ge° went out Rowing — delightful evening. Tea at about 7 — Evening spent playing whist, Addie & I against Arthur Hawes & Miss Boughton. To bed about 10.30.
