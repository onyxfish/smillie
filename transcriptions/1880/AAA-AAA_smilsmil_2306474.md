## Left Page

### November, Monday 15. 1880.

Slightly overcast, considerable after 9 when I got to studio —
a good part of the rest I was working over paper upon which to make W.C. drawings.
At last, got a fine piece stretch then to work on tracing —
Near 2 when I went out to dinner — Afternoon again at my tracing — at 4.30 went to Wunderlich's with my etching "of the way to the River". Then to Ward, the Tailor & ordered a coat — back to tea soon after 6.
At 8 went to Bellows Room — a meeting of the Etching Club, but I did not stay — after looking at a fine lot of Farren's etchings I went to Mr. Woodbury's — 5 e. 13 & had a very pleasant call there with mother & daughter until after 10. to 37. S. & to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 16. 1880.

Very pleasant day — got to studio a little before 9 & found Mr. J. R. Osgood waiting for me — we talked over some details concerning our Etching Book. Then S. R. Koehler called and he talked Japan paper — then Bellows came in & later, Farren, all interested in etchings — At last I got to work upon the tracing & outline of Black Mr. To dinner at 2 — a short walk, then again at work & finished outline.
Tea at 6.30 — At 8.30 at Storey's — spent the evef. with young Sam. & his eldest sister — again at studio at 10. Glorious moonlight night — to 37. S. & to bed soon after 11.
