## Left Page

### August, Friday 13, 1880

Fine day with some promise of a change in the weather.
Got up feeling very seedy & much of the m'f was spent
watching the dock builders.
Before noon I rowed to & around Vicar's Island to
see what kind of a view I would find of Deer's Leaps.
Dinner after 1. Afternoon
rowed over to Harborside
to work on my study there
but feeling quite useless, I
lounged upon the rocks
until 6 o'clk, then pulled
home & had tea — Every
very threatening — high wind
& a few drops of rain —
To bed soon after 10 — Kept
awake some time by the
wind.

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 14, 1880

Threatening m'f, with rain
in sight but none fell on
us & before noon again it
was charming weather — the
wind changed four times during
the day & rowed around some
time hunting up sketch stuff.
Dinner at 1.30 — afternoon
tried to make a W.C. sketch.
Tea at 6.30 — After that for an
hour went to the Jerrys with
Mr. Dix, Hawes & friend — then
returned & at 9.30 went out
on the lake with Addie — the
night was heavenly — rowed
around the Harbor Islands
in at 9.15 — To bed at 11.
