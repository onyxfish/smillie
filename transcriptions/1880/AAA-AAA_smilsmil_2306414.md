## Left Page

### July, Sunday 18. 1880.

Superb day, but less cloudy and warmer than yesterday — Up at 7.30 & at 8 with Mr. E. went to the Sulpine Spring & drank its waters — back to [illegible] at 9. I did not go to church but alone went through the words to the Excelsior spring — back to lunch, after a delicious m[illegible]f, at 12.30 — After that on the logga with Mr. E. until near 4, note to father — then sauntered out to Hilton's Woodlawn Park & on our way back was met by Mr. E & go. we wandered a little together then back to dinner at 5 — at 6 we went out riding & did not return until near 9 — then callers Mr. & Mrs. Voorhi[s] & Miss Shepherd until 10 — chatting until 11 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 19. 1880.

Up soon after 7 — delicious morning — went to Congress Spring — the beauty of the Park, the m[illegible]f, the music, the people made it all seem like fairy land. Back to [illegible] at 9. All the m[illegible]f spent in the studio with J. W. E. at work laying grounds getting transfers & etching until 12.30 — then lunch — & all the afternoon until 5 biting & [illegible]ing [illegible] of etching — which turned out well considering the difficulties. Then dinner & at 6, out riding — went to a high ridge, a grain field, off the Schuylerville road & had a remarkably fine general view east & west — on return to town stopped at the Grand Union Hotel & went in to the garden to see the Electric lights — the people — & to hear the music — it was a spectacle — home at 9.15 on porch with Mr. E. until 10.30, then to bed.
