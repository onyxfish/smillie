## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 22, 1880

Very high wind & cloudy — impossible to work out of doors.
About 10, Anna, Emma & I started off on a walk & got
as far as Knowlton's Summer house, from which I took a
sketch — then back about 1.30 — late for dinner — Part of the
p.m. I spent reading "Rienzi"
About 5 Emma, Anna & I, out for a row — wind quite subsided
but cloudy & threatening — back to tea at 6.30 — Mr. Candil
spent the evef with us and we made more shadow pictures
— of Mrs. Rutter — Miss Boughton, Mr. C.'s eldest daughter & myself — after 11 when I got
to bed.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 23, 1880

*Black mountain.*

Splendid day — After bkfst, about
9.30 or 10 o'clk, Anna, Emma, Helen
Chas. Kimball & I got away in the
Snark for Black Mt. Point — pulled
down there in less than an hour
— Miss Stebbins & Mr. Searce joined us
& about 11.30 we started for the summit
of the mt. I had a horse — the ascent
was very pleasant — made in about
2 hours — at near 3 we made a
little fire & had a first rate lunch.
After that we lounged upon the rocks
& feasted to the full upon the glorious view
— the distant mts were like the out-
lying heights of heaven. After 5 we began
to descend & had sunset & twilight on
the way — delicious — at 7 started on
our row back — one hour of falling
night — gathering stars & placid water
— Reached home soon after 8 & had
a good supper — a quiet evef
& to bed before 11 — so ending a
perfect day.
