## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 13, 1876

Mild, undecided sort of weather. Got to studio
at 9.20 & to work on "Upright
Newtown Creek," working on
the foreground. Started
for lunch at 12.30. Met
Ed. Brown in Madison Sq. —
then Jno. Sherwood — walked
up the ave. with him & went
into his house No. 15, 44th St.
Got to lunch at 1.30 — back
to studio at 2.30 & worked
until 4.30 — To 44th St. at 5.30
— dressed & at Delmonico's
at 6.20. Dinner of the Am.
Color Manuf. Ass'n. Ate my
share & had a good time until
10.30. Rev. C.C. Tiffany my right
hand man — He made the
speech of the eve'g, others by
Mayor Wickham — Judge Brad—
J. Seaver Page, etc. — Got
to bed at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 14, 1876

Mild overcast day, at times
rather dubious in character.
Got to studio at 9.30 quite
proud to be on time & have
no headache after last night's
experience. Worked on Upright
Newtown Creek. — At 2 went to
Y.M.C.A. & called on Tiffany
(rob. in) Gifford, Bircher, Bellows
& Post Johnston — To lunch in
41st St. at 1.30 — back at 2.30 —
Mrs. Wright (Mrs. Haewley's sister)
& Miss Durfee called — worked
until 4.30 — To Tea at 6.30.
At 8 called on S.C.F. at Jom—
[illegible] 152 W. 44th St. Had a very
pleasant eve'g until nearly
10.30 — then to 41st St. & to bed
at 11.
