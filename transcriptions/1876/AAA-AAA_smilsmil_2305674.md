## Left Page

### September, Saturday 16, 1876

Very heavy fog that lifted about 10 o'clk. Giving a charming mist, but I had a headache that did not lift. At 11 o'clk. went to Gardners 1to & painted on Study No. 1 until 1.20, then in & went to bed without dinner. Slept until 3, then up & as the sky was overcast went out with pencils and paper & sketched under some Butternut trees — very Raw & chilly — At 5.30 Geo came to me & we walked until 6.30 then in to tea — Evef, very quiet, Genl. gone to Wattkuns & Cap.; May & Jack out. Ella Maud. Geo & I chatting in library until 10 o'clk — fussed about until midnight before I went to bed. & did not get to sleep until after 1 o'clk.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 17, 1876

Heavy, pouring rain all day long. Bkfs. at 8.30 — most of the mng. spent trying to write a letter to Miss Wheeler of Prov. R.I. now in England studying art. Dinner at 2 o'clk. Wrote from 4 until nearly 6 — then walked piazza. Tea at 7 — At night, high wind & continuous rain. Evef. in library. Reading & writing. Rec'd. letter from Anna Coat giving me a commission to paint her a $100 picture for wedding present to Oscar Ireland. Urging me to join a party to start for the Adirondacks on Friday next. To bed soon after 10 o'clk. A violent tornado of wind blew as if it would upset the house.
