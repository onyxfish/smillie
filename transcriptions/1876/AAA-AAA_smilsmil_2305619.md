## Left Page

### May, Saturday 27, 1876

Cloudless & very warm — B'kf't
at 8 o'clk. About 9 went out for
a walk. In at 10.30 & got to
work on oil sketch "Sunlight
on Lake Raquette." ×
Dinner about 12.30 — worked
awhile & then had to give
up to sleep — took a nap and
then again to work, altho'
very stupid about the head.
Finished the sketches roughly
Tea at 6.30 — Emma B. came
in to spend Sunday with
us. Eve'g spent in general
chat until near 10 — then
to bed. [illegible — entire passage]

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 28, 1876

Very warm day. Up late and
spent much time in scrubbing
bathing & breakfast. Then to the
Baptist Ch. Heard W. Kendrick
preach a doctrinal sermon,
dwelling upon the Baptist form
of church government & especially
upon conversion as necessarily
preceding baptism. After service
went in to Matt's — Uncle W.'s family
called — To dinner at 1 o'clk.
Afternoon had a long nap and
then wrote a letter to Miss Sterling
Tea at 6.30. Eve'g went with
Emma B. & Kate to the Cong. Ch.
to hear W. Laurence — but instead
heard a "Service of Song" — Emma
went out to "Hillside" — Kate & I
rid to the Terrace. I wrote letters
to Tracy, Harris, Pardee & Miss
Abbott & got to bed at 11.30
