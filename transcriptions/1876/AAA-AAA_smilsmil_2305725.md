## Left Page

### December, Friday 29. 1876.

"Snow, Snow, Beautiful Snow" — the meanest of all things in a city street — my left eye hurting me, after htcfs.
I went to Dr. Throoks's — says I'm caught cold in it — Got to Studio at 6.15 — Matthews called then Mr. Harrison — architect, who wishes me to slap in a back-ground to one of his architectural drawings — To work on I mant.
about a person from Filmer called to get Biographical notes — I prepared lunch in Studio at 2 — worked till 4.15 — Horrible storm — Snow turned to sleet & Rain with violent wind — At 6.15 went to Kimballs to tea — got my feet very wet — had them nicely dried — back to Studio a little before 9 — wrote until 10 — To [illegible] S. at 10.45 & Right to bed.

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 30. 1876.

Fine day — Got to Studio at 9.15 — Mr. Harrison came soon after, I did a little work for myself before his boy came with the drawing — Palser in horribly dirty condition — Scrubbed & cleaned it off & at about 12 got started on my washes. Did not go out to lunch, but ate a Roll while I worked — at it as long as I could see — Mrs H. came in at 4.30 — also Miss Durfees & Mrs. Cuever & Calverey — Mr. H. waited until 6.30 when his frame came — glass broken — I went to tea at 7 — back at 8.30 & worked by gas-light, as I did before tea, until near 10 — To 41 rs. at 11 & Right to bed — after a chat in Renner's Store.
