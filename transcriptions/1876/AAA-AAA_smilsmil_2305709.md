## Left Page

### November, Monday 27, 1876

Pleasant mng. but before noon, overcast & during
P.M. a howling storm of snow & rain. Got to studio
about 9.30 — Miss Storing came — her last day — I
gave her much of my time. Worked on small drawing
"The Butternuts" — To lunch with Maud & May — at Hoffm[an]
4— then with them to Penn[s?]
to pick out frames — To studio at 3 — Miss S. worked as
long as she could see — then paid me $50.00 & bid me
goodbye. To tea at 6.30. Evef. at 8 went down to
aunt Cook's — they are moving into a house in 38th S— Fanning
& Meek came — Anna read her Adirondack journal — at
10.30 walked home with the Ki.s — then to 41st S— & to
bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 28, 1876

Bright mng. at 9.15 called at the Hoffmanlt[?] & bid the
Divens (Maud, May & Jack)
goodbye — they go home tonight.
There until 10 — then to studio —
tried to get to work. Choosing
subject for large drawing,
"under the Butternuts" but
Fairman (Col. Jas.) Snedecor
& others came in so that I
hardly got any thing done.
To lunch at 1 — back at 2
& during the P.M. got my
subject blocked out.
To tea at 6.30. Evef. at 8 called on the Halves family
at 132 W 4th S. There until
9.30 — then to studio — then dressing until near 11 —
then to 41st S. & to bed.
Brilliant moonlight
night.
