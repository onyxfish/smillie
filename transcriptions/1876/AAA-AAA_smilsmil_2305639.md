## Left Page

### July, Thursday 6, 1876

Charming day — delicious breeze that made the day seem delightfully cool altho the Ther. marked 80°. Avg took a short walk into town. Then back to work but Father was busied with trying to get a transfer of dag. outline of "Gaucho." So that I did not get a chance. Dinner at 12.30 — Then to work on "Hill Side" & finally, at 4.30 pronounced it done. Then went out & walked with Father until 6 — Then Mrs. Williams & Holley called — & at 6.30 W. Bisby came and took me out riding until 8.30 — Then home & got my tea — to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 7, 1876

Hazy, hot & absolutely still — looking as though a change of weather was brewing. Between calls reading news papers & writing notes the morning got away without my commencing work on "The Cathedral Spires, Yosemite." My head so troubles me & I am so demoralized by the weather that I feel very unlike work of any kind. Dinner at 12.30. After that I etched on plate die of "The Gaucho," putting in Horse's head. Then got all ready to start to-morrow on Yosemite drawing. Tea at 6.30 — Then an hour with the hose — at 8.30 went with [illegible] up to the Williams's. There until 10.30 — Then home & to bed at 11.
