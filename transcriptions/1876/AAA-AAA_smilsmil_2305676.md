## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 20, 1876

Cold, cloudy & windy — After
bkfp — made to Anna Coat
& did up some chores — At
11 took pencils & paper &
went out among the cows &
Butternut trees — Got so much
interested that I forgot time
& it was 2 o'clk. before I got
in to dinner. Out again
in the afternoon until 6.
Tea at 6.30 — after which the
Genl started for Angelica,
In bidding us good bye he said
he was very glad we had stayed
so long & he hoped we would
come again next summer —
Surf. I rested on sofa — had
a long walk on piazza & then
Read aloud Huxley's first lecture
on Evolution — just delivered in
N.Y. & Reported in Tribune.
To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 21, 1876

Overcast but very mild and
really pleasant to sit out of
doors — About 10 o'clk went out
under the Butternuts & commenced
an oil sketch to get something
of the character of the tree trunks —
Painted until 1.30 — then to dinner
at 2 — Back again soon after &
painted until 6 — So dark
that I could not see to work
longer — In at 6.30. Very tired
& cold — Tea near 8. We were
to have made a call on Mrs.
Langdon in the Surf but Capt
— did not come in until late.
The time passed in looking
over sketches — cleaning brushes
& Reading aloud Huxley's 2d
lecture on Evolution — To bed
at 11.15 clk
