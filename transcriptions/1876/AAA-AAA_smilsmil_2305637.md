## Right Page

### July, Monday 3, 1876.

*[Header note in different hand:] Centennial Celebration at New York.*

Very warm day — After a walk
up town got to work on Hill Side
drawing. Dinner at 12.30 — again
on drawing & at 4.50 I took train
with Charl & got to N.Y. at 7. went
right to Mani's & had tea — At 7.50
went to the Windsor Hotel — miles
Palmer. We sauntered about until
near 9 when we marched down 5th
ave. & B'way to 17th. with "Reg"
then took car & went to Broome St. &
walked up to B'way & then to 14th St. The
crowd immense feeling entire street
many of the illuminations superb —
4th ave car to 26th — then to N.Y.C. of D. got
and had first rate places — then to
Kurt's Club on his Roof — wonderful
effect of the Square — the masses of
people — procession & illuminations —
back to N.A.D. Saw militia pass —
when the declaration came along —
singing we left our balcony & forced
our way through the crowd & luckily the
procession. Here were Rec. & by this
piece of supreme impudence we were
admitted within the lines of militia
on Union Sq. & to the stand elected for
the Society — got there just about midnight.
The scene beggars all description — a sky
cloudless & glorious with full moon — a
great square packed with 100,000 people
fired with excitement. The mass appearing
to dance with torches & lanterns — the blaze
glowing with illumination — thousands of
lanterns hung in long festoons across the
square — the sky was alive & brilliant
with the most wonderful display of
fireworks that I ever saw — The air
was full of wild clamor — booming guns
ringing bells, screaming whistles and
the gay music of military bands —
then the star spangled banner
