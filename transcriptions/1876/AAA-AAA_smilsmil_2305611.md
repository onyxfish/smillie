## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 9, 1876

Down pouring rain — this is much needed & from the energy of the rain, promises & early and afterwards; went down to Earl's &
made arrangements to have him put up some mouldings in W.C.
gallery. Rode out to grounds with Kurtz. Tried to see Jardner &
Catalogue Dep't. Then to Annex
& at 2.30 started for W. W's dinner
& at 4 back again — found Earl's
men waiting for me — by working
until after 6. got part of money
up — the Rain held up in
the middle of the day but
at night again came pouring
down — tea at 9.30 — Eve'g
chatting & Reading and to
bed at 10.45.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 10, 1876

*Opening of Centennial Exp.*

When I got up at 9 a.m. a wet blanket of Rain & mizzle promised to utterly spoil the arrangements for the day — B'kf. at 8 — something of a headache — a gleam of sunshine promised better things — At 8.45 the carriage was at the door & I was driven alone & in style to the cen. grounds — the was now most charming & the display of flags something wonderful. Got to Main Bldg. at 9.20 & went right through to platform, Section M. 3. Stood there awhile — did not like the position — then tried the Sec. J — Diplomatic Corps — Got within 50 feet of front & all the great guns — The sun was very hot but the sight made amends for all discomforts except the long prayer by Bishop Simpson — the orchestral pieces were rather confused but the choral effects were grand beyond all anticipation — I was completely carried away — Whitney of Boston, the soloist made a remarkable exhibition & was encored. The unfurling of flags, the Hallelujah chorus, the booming of guns & the ringing of bells was all that poetry could. It was after 1 o'clk before I commenced to go about, met Mr. Jayens, Johnston & Mr. Happen & went with them to see pictures for an hour, then through the Main Building — Machinery Hall, Gyr. Bldg. — Woman's Hall & then at 3.30 to Restaurant & got a cup of tea — headache bothering me — Rain came up & continued heavily until 6 — went to Horticultural and Agricultural Hall — then again to Art Bldgs & through Main Bldg. out at 6 o'clk — not a car or conveyance to be had & tens of thousands of tired & muddy people slopping their way into town, fully 4 miles away — it is computed that 250,000 people were out — I walked to Race & Vine S. car depot — ½ a mile or more — then got a car & finally got to Mr. Whitney's at 7.40 — Took tea, & finally at 9. got to bed, quite used up with a headache.
