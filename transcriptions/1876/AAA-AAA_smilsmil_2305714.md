## Left Page

### December, Thursday 7, 1876

Charming mild day — Got to
studio at 9.20 — calls — Nicoll
& others — Spent the morning
mounting paper on sketches
for two drawings — To dinner
at 1 — back at 2.15 — The
p.m. forced away from
me. R. S. Gifford & others
called — also a strange lady
begging a sketch for an
album for a charity fair.
Worked as long as I could
see on charcoal sketch.
Then wrote until af[ter]
to tea at 6.45 — At 8.30 at
the Sturts, He with fair until
near 11 — Then to 41. 8. S. &
to bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### December, Friday 8, 1876

Pleasant with a raw feeling
in the air. Got to studio at
9.30 & right to work on "Cows
Pasturing" — at it until 12.30
went to Emma J's & offered Mrs.
to bring my Phil. ticket — She could
not go — So gave it to Miss Abbatt
gas man came & measured
for my drop light — So that I
did not get to dinner until
1.30 — back at 2.10 & worked
until 4.20 — could see no
longer — have now got "Cows
Pasturing" just about done.
Took a rest on lounge & at
6 went to Masonic Temple, 6th
Ave & 23rd S. met Annie Cook, Mlle
& Fany K. & took tea at a fair there.
At 8 took the girls to their Singing
club then to Sturt. He, took fair
up to Renners & picked out three
engravings & frames for Crandall
Janis — back to Hotel at 9.30 — then
until 10.30 — then to 41. 8. S. & to bed
at 11.
