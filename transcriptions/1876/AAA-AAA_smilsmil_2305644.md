## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 18. 1876.

*Montrose, Pa.*
*at Aunt Sayre's*

Up soon after 5 o'clk. walked up to 6th Ave. cars (West B'dway) with Father, starting him up town, then back to boat & b'kfo. at 6,30, at 7, Took carriage & rode down to foot of Barclay St. then over the ferry smuggled ourselves through into the Parlor Car 1/2 an hour ahead of time. Waited very comfortably & got away about 8,20 — breezy, but exceedingly hot & dirty — Got to Scranton at 1,45 & had a lunch. at Nicholson we ran into a very heavy thunder storm — at Montrose, at 3,15 — the rain fell lightly, the force of the storm being absent — a Barouche was in waiting & we waited for half an hour for the Rain to hold up, then we went on & had delightfully cool ride — Got to Aunt H's at 5,45 — to tea at Sayre's at 6. Mother came through splendidly — Svet, telegraphed to Father — met all the Mulford's. Went in & spent the eveg. until near 10 with them — then up to bed at 10,30. Rain free until after 7.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 19. 1876.

Delicious day — altho' very warm b'kfo — soon after 7 — then went over & spent part of mng. with mother who got through last night first rate — wrote a letter to Father in NY. Called on the Sayre's girls — Flora under the weather — Also, went up to see how things were in "my shop" — the mice have taken all the fringe off my Turkish Rug to line their nests. Dinner at 1 — after that took a nap. Then again to studio. J. & Elisha M. came up there — to tea at 6. Eveg. on piazza with Mother until 8,30 — then down town — at Aunt in to fan's & with her, Priscilla P. & St. M. had whist until after 10 — then home with P. & finally to bed with myself a little before 11.
