## Left Page

### May, Thursday 11, 1876

Up all Right in the morning. After 6 clk. went down town to Earles — Carlisle & Joy, painters — Effe. Co. etc. etc. — & finally, at 12.30, got out to Cen. Grnds. Glorious day & wonderful show of flags — Took lunch at Tichners, outside of the grounds — then in & spent most of the P.M. in useless fussing trying to find the painters to arrange the decoration & also with catalogue man — At 4 o'clk gave up & spent 1½ hours about the grounds enjoying myself — very unwilling to leave now — I want to stay & have a good time. In the Main Hall was surprised to meet Aunt H. & [illegible] W. J. S. & A. V. V. At 5.30 Rode into town with them. At 6.30 I took tea with Mrs. Whitne & Miss Mary — M. W. went to N.Y., then met at [illegible] I did them good bye & took 7.35 train to N.Y. where I arr. about 35 m. behind time. Got to 41st St. all Right and to bed shortly after midnight.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 12, 1876

Heavy rain — very mild and occasionally, thunder. Got to the Albemarle about 9.45 & there met M. Geo. Whitney & S. P. Avery. Chatted with them for an hour & first learned that last [illegible] — I was elected an N.A. M. W. walked over to the new studio with me & made a tour of inspection — met Calverley, Colman & others — I fussed about until after 12, then to 41 8. to dinner — back at 1.30 & worked until after 4, when, feeling very tired, I stretched myself on the lounge & lay there until 6 o'clk. It was 7 when I got to tea. At 8.10 at 51 W. 10th St. Spring meeting of the N.C. Socy — a fair attendance — all much interested to hear from Phila. of Centennial matters — we had a good meeting until near 11 — then walked up with Colman & others — got to bed about 11.30
