## Left Page

### June, Thursday 8, 1876

Pleasant day, with showers north of us in the afternoon. As the mf. was very warm & I felt tired, did not walk after blfo. but got to work Re.[illegible] the first picture I ever exhibited — the one sent to N.bt. f M.A. f D. in 1864 &upon which I was elected Ass. Dinner at 12.30 P.M. worked until 5, then as I felt exceedingly tired I spread myself on a bed until 6 — Tea at 6.30. Eve[.] I got out the hose & played until nearly over grass & Road, then rocked myself for awhile in a chair — [illegible] a little, washed up my brushes & got to bed at 9.45.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 9, 1876

Very warm day — and felt very headachy & weak all day — About 9.30 walked up town & posted a letter that I had written to Ferguson. Back & got to work about 11 o'clk. finishing on 7×13 "Evening on Raquette Lake". Dinner abt o'clk. Worked slowly & at intervals until 6 o'clk. when I got my picture finished. Cleared up & took tea — About 7.30 walked up town & called on Sallie (Jeut) Van Kleek — found her father, mother & sister Amilia there — Chatted until about 9.30 — then home. To bed at 10.30.
