## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 9, 1876

Got through the night tolerably
well. B[illegible] a little before 8.
Very soon after, rode in town
with Maud & went right to the
Dr's Saw him & got medicines.
He thinks I have no need of
fear now if I am very careful
Then we drove around to
a number of places, doing
the marketing & other messages
Back at 10.45 — Wrote awhile
& worked awhile upon Tablets
Dinner about 1 o'clk. A
little more Tablet work & at
4 o'clk. Maud, May, Cap. Geo.
& I went out to ride — We left
Maud with a sick friend & then
me drove on to "The Narrows" —
part of the way, very hot & dusty
Back to tea at 6.30 — Sev[eral]
Mr. Hamlin & Frank Hall came &
played Cal. Jack. I had a horrible
headache & finally at 9.30 gave
up to it & went to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 10, 1876

Was not able to get out of
bed owing to a boring head-
ache in my right eye —
About 5.30 P.M. I got up &
was able to take a bath —
Maud made up some Graham
mush gruel & I filled up —
see but Maud & Jack went
to Judge Gray's to tea. Soon
after & Mr. Grauberry. Two
children & nurse arrived
from N.Y. city. I enjoyed
the quiet & lounge of Maud's
room quite, about 10 — It
was after midnight before
I went to bed & about
2 A.M. when I got asleep.
