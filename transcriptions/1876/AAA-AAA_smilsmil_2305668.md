## Left Page

### September, Monday 4, 1876

Breezy mtf with many flying clouds. Jack arr'd. from his Canadian trip at 5 a.m. About 10 I went to Study No. 2 near the gardener's Ho. and by 12 had painted in the elm — Then a shower came up & I went in & painted over mtfs work until after 1 — Dinner at 1.45. July very tired after dinner Took a short nap — then cleaned up palette & brushes & went out for some outline sketches but it was so cold & the wind blew so hard that I got but one — then up on the hill to Reservoir with [illegible] & Cap: Reed came to house in [illegible] his daughter — took a short horse-back ride with her — Tea at 7 — Eveg in library — Whist for an hour with gent. Cap: & May — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 5, 1876

Breezy & cold — [illegible]: at 7.30 [illegible] R. again came up & again I had a short horse back ride with his daughter at 8.30 — Painted in Sky, 2°. Painting, on Study No. 2 near the gardener's & worked on part of foliage — This was done indoors because it was too windy & cold to work out of doors — At 11.30 went out & wandered about until 12.30 then in & dressed — About 1, the Otruia guests arr'd. Mr. & Mrs. McGraw & daughter Jennie & Mr. & Mrs. Sage — also Mrs. Jarvis Langdon & her son Chas. & his wife — At 2.30 we sat down to dinner — nearly 4 when we left the Table — a little later Mrs. Sage and having come on a later train. The rest of p.m. I spent walking & talking about the place — Tea at about 7 — Eveg. Mr. [illegible] Arnot & Chas. Langdon came in for awhile. Later, cards came to the front. Mr. & Mrs. Sage. Mr. McG. & the gent. played Whist — Miss McG. May, the Cap: & I played euchre — Mr. McG. left for town at 6 p.m. — Mrs. Langdon & daughter left before that — To bed at 11 o [illegible]
