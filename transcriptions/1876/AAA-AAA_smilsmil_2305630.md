## Left Page

### June, Sunday 18. 1876.

Overcast & close in spite of strong S.E. wind. — After bkfst wrote a note to Miss Aitken — went to service at Mansionby, Epis. Ch — a sermon on the broad lesson of Man's temptation & fall. Walked down Mills; & at Cong. Ch. met Emma & aunt J. — Rode out to Hillside. Dinner at 1.30 — Then all went to the River & woods — driven in by Rain. Tea at 6. Rode in town at 9 — Went to Baptist Ch. & heard Rev. W. Gallagher — quite an eloquent discourse on the words "And there was no sea there". Rev. [illegible] treating of the calm & rest of Heaven after the toil & separation of this life. Home at 9, wrote to Bro Mulford. At 10.30 went to P.O. & back & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### June, Monday 19. 1876.

Overcast & muggy — Up late. Mr. Loomis came for me while I was at bkfst — heavy shower — at 10, we went to Mr. Wright's school & saw his works of art — Then home but did not get fairly to work before dinner — Set palette & painted on 6×10 — "A Shaded Spot" until 6 o'clk. Then Tea — After tea cleaned palette & with father & Geo. went to So. Sieberry St. & called on the Wright's — found them very pleasant — Thre at 8.45 called on the Palmers, There until 9.30 — Then home & to bed at 10. Charmingly clear, cool night after the showers of the day.
