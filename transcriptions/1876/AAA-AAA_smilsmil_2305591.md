## Left Page

### March, Thursday 30, 1876

Several violent snow squalls during the day — got to studio at 9.30 & to work on Horse's Head for A.B.N. Co. Repainted & glazed. To dinner at 1.2. back at 2.30 — got out my Water Color box and started work on a 6×10 sketch commenced some time ago "In Conway Meadows" $625 B. way about 4 & worked there until nearly 6. Making out a list of values for Insurance — then walked up to Kimball's & took tea. At 7.45 to Fanny & Anna C. to their singing Club. Then to studio & sat again at K. & took Matt & Kate to club. He are near 20°. Heard Hermann Cantata on Hood's Bridge of Sighs. A very good thing — he leading — a few invited friends many I knew — acted as escort again to belt's — to 41 St. at 11.40 & got to bed a little before midnight.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 31, 1876

Muddy & dirty — Did not get to studio until 10 — then a number of visitors — got started painting on Lion's Head for A.B.N. Co. To dinner at 1.15 — back at 2.15 & managed finally to get the head painted in. To tea at 6.45 and feeling very tired. I rested until after 8 — then dressed went down to studio & from there to Mrs. Mary Mapes Dodge 71 Madison Ave. Invited to meet Miss Sartrain, daughter of the Phila. engraver. There until 11 — Then to 41 St. & to bed about 1.40.

Yesterday P.M. went down to the Dis. Attorney's office & saw Horace Russell — then again this m'f. & finally got things into such shape that I hope to get off from jury duty in N.Y.
