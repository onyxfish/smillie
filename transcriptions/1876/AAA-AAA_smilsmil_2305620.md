## Left Page

### May, Monday 29, 1876

Very warm mg. Took a short walk after bkfst. Then in and wrote a letter to Fanny M. at 10 o'clk. Uncle W. came for me with Mrs. Düring. At about 10.30 with Geo., we went out to Hill Side & got there just in time to escape a heavy thunder storm that passed soon & then I went out to the "big Rock" & to summer house in to dinner at 1. Then again out — another slight shower in the afternoon — wandered pretty much over the place — Tea at 6 & before 7 rode into town — stopped at Matt's. Fanny Kimball there — at 9.30 all walked out to the Terrace — but Matt, Fanny & [illegible] did not go in — To bed at 10.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 30, 1876

Remarkably clear, cool day. Ther. 45°, a difference of 40° in 24 hours. Very stupid head walked down to Spring Side and wandered about there — then in to the "old house" & all through that — back to Terrace at 11.30 — then got palette & commenced painting on 6×10." "On the Saco — Bartlett, N.H." Dinner at 12.30 — painted from 2 until 5 when I finished sketch. Then Matt & Fanny came. Walked with F. from 6.40 until near 7 when we went to Matt's — Kate then had tea at 7.15 — about 8.15 went to Congregational Ch. and heard Geo. Parkin's address. Then again to Matt's until 9.30 when Kate & I went home — To bed at 10 — [illegible — entire passage]
