## Left Page

### January, Friday 28, 1876

Heavy, overcast & foggy — still under the weather — felt uncomfortable about the head & unable to work. Got to studio at 9.45 — with Miss a. & among papers until after 11 — then did a little charcoal work on 20×24 — Soon after 12, Markham, architect called & we talked studio studios sky lights until 1.15 — very unsatisfactorily — then to dinner — back at 2.30 accomplished very little during P.M. — Had many callers. Did some work in Calverley's in mending broken plaster horse — to tea at 6.45 — at 8.30 went to Hippodrome — & for an hour listened to Rehearsal of Moody & Sanchy choir — then to studio — to 41st St. at 10.20, Charl. May & the D. walking up with me —
To bed at 11c

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 29, 1876

A mean sort of day — dumb and inclined to rain — about noon it did rain. Not feeling very bright. I took my time & did not get to the N.A. until 10.30 — Found quite a crowd there; it being Private View day — among others, W. C. Whitney who came in from Phila. just on this acct. We had a long talk over my picture, but he did not say he wanted it — There was some very brisk buying. Mrs. J.J. Astor bought Colman's big picture for $1500. and Jenny Milbank Foote Tiffany's at $1000. At 1.30 I went to Emma J's & lunched — back to N.A., there until 6 — to tea in 41st S. at 6.30 — at 8 again at N.A. "Press Views" — a great crowd & much good feeling — Total sales during the day, over $8000. — Did not get away until midnight —
To bed at 12.40.
