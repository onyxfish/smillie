## Left Page

### August, Sunday 27, 1876

Perfectly clear with strong, cold wind from the north. Had bkfo. late & soon after 9 walked in town with the Genl. to Neck D's — there until Genl's carriage came with May & Gee, then with Mr. A.D. we went to J. K. Beecher's church — heard him lay out a plan for his church work, a sort of curriculum covering 7 years of study & progress. His text was "Grow in grace & in the knowledge of the Lord Jesus Christ." He proposes to make a sort of spiritual college & club of his 450 church membership. He gave a general description of the wording & after service a number among them Mrs. Saugden & Mr. & Mrs. Clements (Mark Twain) went over the establishment to the "Romb Room" or Theatre — the Mfr. many etc etc. — Walked to Aleck's & took carriage home. — Dinner about 2 o'clk — Afternoon wrote a little while — took a walk & had tea at 7 — the family gathering, as usual — all away by 9 o'clk. — In library until a little after 10, then to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Monday 28, 1876

Cool mtf., but atmosphere very smoky. — About 9.15 rode into town with May & Gee. — messages & calls — 11 o'clk. when we ret'd home. — Then to work on A.B.M., drawing until 1 o'clk. Mr. Bullard & daughter took dinner with us at 2 o'clk. Again worked on drawing until 5.30 — then climbed the hill to a fire in the woods — quite exciting — a splendid sunset — charming effect of twilight haze mingled with faint, cold light from quarter moon. — Ret'd at 8 o'clk — quite dark. — Took tea alone but was well waited upon. Mr. Olmstead am'd for a visit — from 9 until 10.30 in library & had pleasant games of Euchre with Maud & May — the Genl. & the Cap'n. — Then to bed.
