## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 28, 1876

Very hot in spite of a pleasant breeze. About
9.30 W. Mrs. Bisbee — daughter
& sister called to see my little
paintings — At 9.40 I went
up to W. Williams. Miss Wheeler
too unwell to go with us, so
W. & Mrs. W. & I went out to
Vassar Commencement — In
spite of my headache, I
enjoyed the day, had lunch
at 2.30 at the college & met
many pleasant people —
After 3. Rode in home — I
spent an hour or more in
my bed. Tea at 6. Then
J. & Mrs. Hasbrouck & some of
the children called — Saw
my pictures — at 8. Gel. & I
went up to W. Williams
Miss W. too unwell to appear
— Suet on Piazza until 10 —
Home & to bed at 10.45.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 29, 1876

Overcast most of the day
& comparatively cool. Nearly
all the mf. engaged packing
paintings just finished and
writing letters — Went in town
& [illegible] pressed box — Commenced
work in Water Colors on View
at Hillside — Dinner at 2.30
Emma Bonine with us —
P.m. worked until after 5
on Hillside — then walked
with father until after 6 —
then tea — at 8 o'clk. with
Gel again went to W. Williams
Miss Wheeler well enough to
sit on Piazza until 9 o'clk. —
me stayed until 10 — then
home — I wrote to Renner
for "Saco" 6×10. That W. Williams
has bought. To bed at 11.20
Matt & Kate Rd.? from NY,
this evng.
