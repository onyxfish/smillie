## Left Page

### June, Monday 12, 1876.

Overcast with now & then a sprinkle of Rain — Father,
Willie & Charl got off for N.Y.
in 8.30 A.M. train. I wrote to
Mr. Whitney — walked in town
& felt horribly heavy & stupid
& set palette but accomplished
nothing in the m[orning]. Dinner
at 12.45 — Afternoon I finished
6×10" "Cedars" that I am going
to offer to Kurtz — I think it the
best of those I have painted
up here, so far. Gave up soon
after 4 & wandered to the River
& down the R.R. to below the Cemetery
& back to tea at 6 — Every
still feeling too stupid to
go out. Wrote to Renner —
Earle & Kurtz — To bed at
about 10.30

## Right Page

### June, Tuesday 13, 1876.

Overcast — one little sprinkle
only — wrote two or three letters
& went in town about 10 o'clk.
Did not get at my painting
until after 11 — Then commenced
repainting on "An Adirondack
glimpse", working over sky
& distance — Dinner at 12.30
gave up work about 5 o'clk.
walked to R.R. dépôt and
met Father who came from
N.Y. on 6.32 train — walked
up home together — Tea soon
after 7 — Shortly after 8. Geo.
& I called on Mrs. Williams
& Mrs. Hall (Sterlings) in South
Liberty St. & had a very
pleasant time until after
10 — Then home & to bed
about 11.
