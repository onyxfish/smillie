## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 3, 1876

Overcast — chilly east wind and threatening change — Got out to the grounds about 10 very much worn out & depressed. Spent some time in M. Hall with Whittredge, Williams, Perkins & Bastain trying to pick out leading pictures for centres — Then to my own W.C. work. Had 4 men detailed to me to hang, but they had but one screw driver & no screws or screw eyes — finally found some old screws & a few eyes & set to work with them. Started on the west wall with the close gold frames — at 1.30, went out to a Restaurant & got dinner — at 2.30 back & at 5.30 got west wall done. It was after 6 when I started for Mr. W's — Tea at 7 — Every in lazy lolling & chat — very tired. To bed at 10.30 & a very good night's rest. Rec'd from [illegible] a pkg of Percy's Phosphates,

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 4, 1876

Charming day — Enjoyed a refreshing bath before 6½clp. Got to Cent. grounds about 10 & was seized by Whittredge to help him select more pictures for mem. Hall — Did what I could to help him, then to my own W.C. work — three men to help me with one screw driver & not a screw eye altho Requisition had been made early yesterday m'f. Did what I could in selecting & placing drawings — at 12.45 went out & walked about and eat my lunch — back & worked until 2.30, then dismissed my men went into m. Hall & met Clarence Cook at entrance. Passed him in then to annex with him — He kept me until after 3 clk. He has an article in this m'p's Tribune on the hanging of Rothermel's Picture in M. Hall. Got to Mr. W's to dinner at 4 o clk — after they were done. Had my meal dolus — Rested awhile, then went out & bought screws & screw eyes in at 6.45. W.J. Richards came in soon after 7 — Tea at 7.30 — General chat & over cen. matters until 10 — To bed at 10.30 & had a good night's rest.
