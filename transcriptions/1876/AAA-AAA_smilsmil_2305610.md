## Left Page

### May, Sunday 7, 1876

Very warm Spring day — Up at 7 and
had a good scrub, bkfst. soon after 8 —
& at 9 started with W. W. for Reading
R.R. 9th & Spring Garden. mistake in
time Table — Train did not start
until 9.45 — Got out to Chestnut Hill
at 10.35 & went right to Church —
(Episcopal.) Heard the Rector, Rev J. A.
Harris preach on the Resurrection —
out at 12.10 — hurried over to his house
— picked some violets — sniffed the
warm air & then to station and
ret'd to the city by 12.30 Train, getting
to the house a little before 2. Dinner
right away — After dinner, a little
chatting — then a nap in my chair
& at 4.30 wrote a long history of our
squabble with Sarrain & posted
it to Gel who is to send it to farther.
About 6 o'clk, a cup of tea — then
general chatting & efforts to keep
cool until 10 o'clk. — to bed at
10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 8, 1876

Very warm day with overcast
streaks & threats of Rain which came
in light spittings — So very warm that I did
not wear my overcoat. Bkfst. at 8 — then
to Market & 5th S. looking for gum tags to
number pictures — then to Chestnut & 7th
where I got them — then to Cen. Gds where
I am? — at 10.15. went first to W. Gardner
Head of Catalogue Dept. & arranged to cor-
rect the cat. of W.C. Dept. Then to Gallery
16 (W.C.) & commenced numbering for
catalogue. Whittredge got me to help
him for a time & otherwise my work
was much interfered by a number
of W. exhibitors who had just arrived &
did not know the ropes. Lunch at
12.30 — worked until after 2 — then to dinner
had a chance to rest. After dinner,
again to Cen. Gds. getting there about
4.30. Finished numbering but did
not get away until after 7 & to tea
at W. W's about 8. Very, as
usual, Reading news paper and
chatting — to bed at 10.30 —
Heavy Rain during the night.
