## Left Page

### April, Thursday 27, 1876

Delightful day — mild & clear —
after Bkfs. worked for some time
with model hanging — made 2 or 3
notes & about 10.30 started for the
Cen. Grounds. — Soon after 12 got our
Committee together — Jno. Dartain, Howard
Roberts & — Williams of Phila. Perkins
of Boston & Whittredge & elf. — a great
deal of Talk — I tried to effect an or-
ganization — was nominated chair-
man but Refused — accepted the post
pro-tem — then Jno. Dartain was nominated
balloted for, & elected — but absolutely
Refused. — Under protest I continued to
act as temporary chairman & presented
a petition to Genl. Goshorn to call Com.
on Selection to pass on pictures now
waiting. This was accepted, signed
by Hanging Committee, at 2 o'clk we
all went to Genl. G. with it. He Res-
erved his decision until later & I agre-
ed to Return & meet Com. at 5 o'clk —
Then to dinner at Mr. Whitney's at
3 o'clk. — This has been a Rep near field
day — I never had such a perplexing &
sharp fight. — After dinner Rec. a note
from Whittredge saying that Genl. G. must
send for Com. So I did not Return to
Cen. G. — but spent the latter [illegible]
Remained at the P.M. arranging
model hanging of W. C. & tea at
6.30 & at 7 left Mr. Whitney's house,
took 7.35 Train & Reached NY.
at 10.10 — Got to 41' St. & to bed
about 11.30 —

## Right Page

### April, Friday 28, 1876

A light fall of Rain very early —
Bkfs. at 8.30 — After going to Mans-
tun to Stude's, thru to 4th Ave. & 25th.
Thur to Huntington's — then again
to 212 5th ave. Get there — Did some
tearing down & to 41st St. & dinner
at 12.30 — again to studio at 1.30
Runner did not come until
near 2.30 & before he began to
get things on the cart it began
to Rain — & continued heavily
until about 6.30. He got over
a few things over to new building.
I worked at hauling things down
until near 7 — there, very tired
& dirty, to 41st 8th & to tea —
Every. Read Evef. Post &
fussed with W.C. hanging
matters until 10 o'clk. Gat-
to bed about 10.30.
