## Left Page

### June, Saturday 10, 1876

Very hot day - thunder storms near but none over us. Wrote a letter to Ferguson, went in town, back & finally got to work painting about noon. Dinner at 12.30. Afternoon finished 6×10" "On the Saco near North Bartlett" and the little sketch "Montrose Hill." Painted until tea time but did not quite get the last sketch finished — Charl came in at 6.30 — Messrs Loquis & Williams called — me went out to the Park to see Eastman's presentation of 500 hats to as many urchins — Mr Williams came down — me formed a group & had a very pleasant chatty time. Took a short walk with Ge. Got to bed at 10 o'[clock]

## Right Page

### June, Sunday 11, 1876

Another very hot day with a light spatter of Rain about 12, 15–30. Myf, went with Ge. to Cong. Ch. & heard Wm. Laurence 39' Psalm 3–4' v, — a very niled effort that produced no fever in speaker or hearer. Dinner at 1 — a nap after that — then Ge. Charl. & I sauntered to a knole that overlooked the River & sat there until 6 o'clc. then in to tea. Lovely every enjoyed front balcony & Church bells even so much — Matt & E? at the house — I went alone to the Opera House & heard Abbatt, the free lance on Evolution & kindred theories as incompatible with Christianity without accepting such theories in fully he nevertheless insisted that there was nothing in them at variance with Bible teaching — In thought, he was tumultuous & in manner, rather incoherent. He got very hot himself & so did his hearers. To bed at 11.15
