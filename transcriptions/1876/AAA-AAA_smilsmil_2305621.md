## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 31, 1876

Charming day — not so cool as
yesterday. Late to b[e]d. At
9.30 went into town to find a
vehicle in which to ride this
P.M. Went from stable to stable
& finally found me at the Nelson Ho.
11 o'clk. when I Ret'd. home.
Wrote notes to Emma D. & Anna
C. Fanny K. & Matt came to
dinner which was served
about 1 o'clk. After dinner
I worked a little on charcoal
sketch & at 4.20 horses & wagon
came to the door & Fanny. Kate.
G[e]o. Father, Willie & I went out
Riding — to top of College Hill.
Then out beyond Hydebank. Return[ing]
stopped at Hillside & drove thro[ugh]
the woods. Got home at 8 o'clk.
after a most delightful drive.
Tea at once. General chatting
until 9.30 — then walked up to Matt
with Fanny — Glorious moonlight.
Back to bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 1, 1876

Still cloudless & warmer.
Up late — after b[e]d — walked
out wandering in the fields until
after 10 o'clk. Then in & began
Painting on 12×14 "An Adirondack
Lake" — Driven about 1 o'clk
Fanny K. Ret'd home in 1.27 P.M.
Train — I painted until about
4.30 when Mr Risby called. He
was taken up to Father's Room
& stayed until after 6 — appear-
ing to be very much interested.
Tea about [6] — Swell. Got
out the hose & worked until
after 9 o'clk. giving Road and
grass & shrubbery a thorough
drenching. Glorious moon-
light — To bed at 10.
