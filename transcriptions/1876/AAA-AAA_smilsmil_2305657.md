## Left Page

### August, Sunday 13, 1876

Still very hot but with such pleasant breeze as very materially to mitigate the heat. I got up with a cracking headache at the base of my skull so that I could move my head with difficulty. I spent the morning in a big wicker chair under the trees near the croquet ground — Dinner at 1.30 — In the library then the lounge in hall up stairs until 4.30 — Then, feeling better, I wrote awhile wandered under the trees most of the Diven family came, Tea about 4 — Every reading & wrote a letter to Father. To bed at 11. [illegible — ~4 lines]

## Right Page

### August, Monday 14, 1876

Still intolerably hot & damp. Got off in Street car right after bkf. with Jack & Gale. Rode down to D. Seely's & saw him — got some medicines. Then to Geo. Diven's office — met the carriage with Maud & Ge. Went to Reed's & there again met Maud & Ge. We bid Fau M. good bye — then again to office & took the Gent. to Station. He goes to N.Y. The Reeds & Fau there — more good byes — At last got home about 11.15 — worked until dinner (1.15) on Tablets — more Tablet work after dinner. At 5 Ella went to Watkins — Tea at 6.30 — Very, very hot — on piazza until 9.30 — In library there for an hour & to bed a little before 11.
