## Left Page

### September, Friday 22, 1876

Cold, cloudy & windy, with every appearance of Rain before
noon — so I got out to my work
as soon as I could, starting from
the house at about 8.45 — went
to Studio under the Butternut
& painted until a little after
12. when I finished — The most
mild yet far cold & windy — No
Rain — Dinner at 1.30. Had
an invitation to go over to the Races
with Capts. Reid & Discum but I resisted temptation — Spent part of P.M. in
arranging to pack & when the cows came
went to the yard with them & watched
until after 6. Then in to tea at 6.30
Evng. Mr & Mrs. Geo. Diwan came up to
bid us good bye — they left before 9 o'clk.
Then Capts. went to bed & with Maud,
Ella & May we had a jolly Rubber —
finished the session at 10.30 — I went to Billiard Room
& packed until 12.30 — finished
& to bed —

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 23, 1876

*Off for Montrose.*

Dismal, Rainy day. B'kfst — all —
everybody somewhat out of sorts —
Maud gave me a bronze card case —
& the Allie [illegible] me a pair
of gold-rimmed eye glasses of her
mothers. Ella went to Watkins
at 9 o'clk. to get an aunt to come &
stay with them over Sunday — We
left the house about 10.20, May going
to depot with us. Maud was suffering
when I left & really seemed to take
our going as a desolate affair — train
to leave at 10.52 — did it leave until
near 12 — lost our "right of way" & so
went feeling along, losing all the
time. Opened our lunch box at 1.30 —
splendid lunch that Maud had done
up for us — hungry young party of four
from Detroit, on way to Centennial
sitting back of us, shared a small
part with us. I got to Tunkhannock
at 3.30 — had to wait there until 5.30
for Phila. trains which came along in
sections — 22 cars, 2 engines, crowded
with Returning Centl. pass. — our little
Montrose train was crowded with
105 pass — no seats for all — five in one
car — Stench & heat miserable — no fire
& no light in other — preferred that! —
dismal ride of nearly 2 hours before
we got to Montrose — then fought for
a seat in a leaky sleigh and were
driven all over town before being
set down at last. Supper! Tea a
little before 9 & to bed at 10 to get
rid of my disgusts.
