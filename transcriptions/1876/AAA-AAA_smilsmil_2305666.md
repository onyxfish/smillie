## Left Page

### August, Thursday 31, 1876

As hot & dry as ever — Did not dare to go out to work in the sun, so started a picture 9×16 — a mate to Geo's "Florida" — intending to give it to the gent. Wrote to Reamer & Co. ordering two frames for G's & mine — Dinn at 1.45 — about 4 Rec'd a letter from Dave & Sons saying that they had put up Fablets but that they were ten short, at which I was tearing mad. Found that I had enough board to make 10 new ones, set to work at it — Wrote to Colman & Nicoll — Sea at 7 — Sweet in the library — Some cards as usual — To bed at 10.30 — The gent went to Albany this m'g. ## Right Page

### September, Friday 1, 1876

The same cloudless, brassy sky overhead — It is like living in a furnace — Bkfst at 8 & immediately after, finished Fablets & at 10 they were taken in town. Commenced then painting on 9×16 & put in the sky before dinner at 1.30. Then it came on to rain with distant thunder & lightning — so dark that I could not see to paint — Rain continued all the afternoon — the long prayed for, refreshing rain — Evelf. Eugene [illegible] came up — Stayed to tea — they left at 8 — we were to have gone down to Geo. Diven's, but did not — more light, soaking rain — Maud & I played euchre against TheCals. & May and were beaten 10 straight games. To bed at 10.45
