## Left Page

### September, Thursday 28. 1876.

Glorious day — Got up to Studio
before 9 o'clk. After awhile, to
work on "Oct." 24½×40½ — +
Spent the m'f. painting the
sky (2°. times). To dinner at
1 o'clk — Several very old
ladies to dinner — Hurried
back to my work. Fan M.
+ then Gee came soon after 3.
I painted until about 5
when I cleared up + went
out + took a walk with
them. At 6.30 went down
to Fan's + at ⅞ tea. Ben
off to Auburn — in about 8 —
Jas. Carmalt came in + had
tea with us. Then Geo. came
in — Whist until 10 o'clk
— To bed at 10.45 —

## Right Page

### September, Friday 29. 1876.

Clouded m'f., clearing off char-
mingly — At 9 o'clk. met H.T. Tyeer
as agreed — He backed out of his offer
to sell me his whole lot, so we
went again over the ground and
finally agreed upon lines which
will vastly improve the shape of
my lot — Price $250 — Commenced
a letter to Geo. Whitney — Tyeer + Geer
came + until 1 o'clk. busy surveying
the lines, then to dinner — After that,
with Gee went over the new lot, then
to Studios — then down town + got a
check from Joe Mis — then feeling
very tired, went to my bed room for
an hour — excitement in town over
the Nay-aue's — Scranton firemen —
again to Studios at 4.30 — most part
of a letter to Mr. Gee Whitney — Tea at
5.15 — then went to Studios + fixed up
for the night — back to Sayre's on the
promise of a quiet evening, sat down
to finish the W. letter by fire in sitting
room — Continued interruptions — the
Scranton Band came up + Serenaded
Dan Sayre — Finished letter about
10.30 — went down to P.O. — To bed
about 11.
