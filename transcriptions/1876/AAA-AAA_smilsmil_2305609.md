## Left Page

### May, Friday 5, 1876

Charming w'f. Got out to ground about 9.30 & having screws & screwways got to work with five men & son got most of the pictures up on two walls. Whittridge was walking about, grand, gloomy & peculiar — Sartain had put him out of Mem. Hall (practically) & things had reached such a crisis that something had to be done — W. Perkins & I went to see Sartain to see if he would let W. hang in one of the galleries of the Annex; he felt need as it would interfere with his plan — So there seemed nothing left for us to do and maintain our self respect but resign. A paper was drawn up to that effect & signed by W— P— & self— Williams of Phil— did n't dare to— Roberts saw it later— he too was unwilling to resign. I ate my lunch at 1 o'clk— & worked in W.C. gallery until 2.30. Then was with Roberts until after 3. & finally got to W. W's to dinner at 4 o'clk. After dinner made until after 7— Posted letter & took tea at 7.30— Swenny spending chatting until 10 o'clk. To bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 6, 1876

Very pleasant day— got out to Centennial grounds at 9.30— Spent half an hour waiting to see Genl. Goshorn, gave his up & went to my work— During the w'f had 5 men, at noon gave 3 of them up keeping the best two— Eat my lunch & went up to office— Saw Goshorn for a few minutes, telling him that I protested against the arrangement in Mem. Hall that it must inevitably disgrace us & that the consequences must be visited upon John Sartain— He was very quick & begged that I would go on with the work as best I could— I went back to hung & by 3 o'clk. finished arrangement of W.C's. At 3.30 left, after seeing A.B.N.Co. Specimen Sheet in place. Got to dinner at 4.35— Mr. W's sister, Mrs. Brown, had am[?] A cup of tea at 8, 'til 8.30, W.W. & I went over to Mr. Jn's Redfield's & made a call there— back at 10— got to bd at 10.30
