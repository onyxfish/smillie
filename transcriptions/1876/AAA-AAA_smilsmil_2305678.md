## Left Page

### September, Sunday 24, 1876

got up in the mid with a
miserable headache but
was able to write a two sheet
letter to Maud. Walked to
P.O. with it, saw Fan M. for
a few minutes, then up to
studio, then, soon after 2
went to bed, & did not get
up until midnight when
I sat on the side of my bed,
eat a cracker & picked the
neck of a chicken.

## Right Page

### September, Monday 25, 1876

Still heavy lowering weather,
mild, but no Rain. B[illegible]. at
7.40 — Then went to Chap Sayres
bargained for a stove & at 11 had
it up & a roaring fire in it —
set to work at once house cleaning
& by noon had the studio proper
ready for work. As I felt very shaky
after yesterday's headache I lay down
upon my bed at noon — at 12.20 dinner,
at 1.30 again at studio & commenced
clearing out closets — Found the nests
of the mice who had torn off the fringe
of my rug & killed 4 of the fellows. They
have made an astonishing litter
of everything, but fortunately my rug is
the greatest damage. Their urine over
everything has made the place redolent
besides badly streaking my new overcoat,
left hanging in the east closet. Finished
at 6 & went to tea — at 7 went to my [illegible]
Tumells & he read to us in [illegible] of the
explosion at Hell gate. at 8.30 Geo.
& I went to Fan's — there until 10.20
then to Sayres — I went up to studio
& fixed fire — to bed a little before
11 o'clk.
