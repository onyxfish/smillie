## Left Page

### October, Monday 16, 1876

Still very cold & blustering. to Studio at 8.30 & started on midlight sketch from my Sout window — 10 3/4 × 16 — Painted until 1, then to dinner. After that, worked as long as I could see & finally got it all in in an unsatisfactory way. The gloomy twilight effect is too subtle to catch. Tea at 6. Swett. 9 o'clock & I called on Hetty Biddle. Run until nearly — then to [illegible] Strand's — Gone to Centennial to Mrs. Sayre's at 9. wrote a short note to Mills — took it to the P.O. & to bed at 11 —

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 17, 1876

Milder — cloudy but at times threatening rain — Most of the morn was spent running around attending to errands & staking out Southerly line on my farm. Dinner at 12.30 as the Comstocks went to Phila by D.L.&W. R.R. this P.M. After dinner W. Sayre went to my place & superintended running a line with plough in which to build my line wall — also fired old stumps & brush. [illegible] at 3. with Uncle Mr. Rode over to Horace Brewster's & bought 400 feet of Red oak to finish closets. Back at 5.30 — tea at 6.30. Mrs. Skinner & Mary Kerab there — at 8, to studio, worked on plans for closets — at 9 to P.O. met Ben Mulford — chatted with him & to bed at 10.30.
