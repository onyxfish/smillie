## Left Page

### September, Thursday 14, 1876

Overcast & Raw — At 9.30 went out with pencils
& paper & worked for an hour when the Rain came
on & drove me in. Worked in the library on pencil
sketches & wrote letters. One to Anna Cook in answer to her
invitation to join an Adirondack party. Dinner at 1.30
more work & writing in library.
Very heavy Rain at times with
thunder & lightening — At 4 o'clk
boy and wife [James?] Gee & [5?] put
our pictures in frames (9×16) and
took them down to library. Gee
presented his to Maud & J, mine
to Ella — I think the present
was fully appreciated. About
5 Gee, Eugene & family. Mrs.
Aleck & Mrs. Falfe came. The
gent also came from Albany —
Tea about 7 — Merry, all very
chatting, romping & playing
cards until 10 o'clk — To

## Right Page

### September, Friday 15, 1876

Cloudy misty, thickening up
and getting quite threatening —
Waited for some time to see
which way the weathercat
would jump & finally got
out with pencils & paper
& made outlines in meadow.
Took a walk to warm up.
Dinner at 1.30. Afternoon
again sketching with pencils
& more spattering showers.
In at 5 feeling cold, tired
& generally uncomfortable.
On the bed until 6.30 — tea
time. Eugene & family to
tea & spent the evn'g until
9 o'clk. Some cards and
good chat. To bed at 10.30
