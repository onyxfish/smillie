## Left Page

### August, Saturday 19, 1876

Overcast mrg & strong east wind,
a little tantalizing rain fell
but not enough to wet anything —
got to work at Tablets & spent
the mrg hard at work over them —
a short walk — then dinner
at 1.30. Again in the after
noon worked until 6. In
Tablets, at last getting
them done. They have
cost me far more time &
trouble than I had any
idea of when I started.
Tea at 6.30 — After 9 o'clk
an hour & a half of Cab.
Jack with the Genl. Cap.
& May — to bed at 11.
In the afternoon, between
3 & 4 drove into town with
the Genl. He met friends & left
me — did up my chores & got
right back.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 20, 1876

Cool, breezy mrg with beautiful
clouds — a row below stairs
the effect of Seeley's pills — so I
could not go to church but
stayed in doors most of the
mrg — did some writing —
Dinner at 1. Between 2 & 3
walked with the Genl. over to
Chapsee Grove in Eldridge
Park & heard a Mr. Howe —
"Trance" speaker gush through
an hour & a quarter — His gab
seems to have been wrought
upon even more powerfully than
my liver — guess he took a different
kind of pill — Back about 5 —
After 7 when we had tea — as
usual Jeck. Gene & families
came to the house — Mr. Gran-
bury and from N.Y. at 6. a.m.
Evening in library chatting until
after 10 — then to bed.
