## Left Page

### April, Tuesday 25, 1876

Cold, Raw and disagreeable day. B[illegible]. at 8 — then rode out to Centennial grounds — got there at a[bout] 20 and spent some time hunting for Sartan & Whittredge — Most of the m[orning] spent in chatter and aimless wanderings. The first show of opposition to the hanging of Rothermel's "Gettysberg" in Memorial Hall cropped out. The antagonism of Whittredge & Sartan very apparent — apparently no chance of doing any work for some time — Gave orders, however, and had W.C. gallery cleared of all the Italian cases that crowded it. Ret[urned]. to W. W[illegible] about 2 o'clk. very tired — got a short nap in my chair and dinner at 3. Spent the rest of [t]he m. trying to arrange model of pictures — Tea at 7 — Evg went with Mr. Whitney to Penn. Academy of Fine Arts. Home at about 10 & to bed at 10.30.

## Right Page

### April, Wednesday 26, 1876

Still very threatening, sputting showers, then warm sunshine. Got to Centennial grounds about 9.45 — considerable time lost again in resultless talk. I am losing patience with Whittredge, he seems to be so impractical & bad tempered. He does not appear to have any clear & orderly ideas of work. I got out all of the W.C.'s & had them put in our W.C. gallery & finally got two men at work cleaning the pasted paper from off the glasses — a car load of pictures am[oun]t — & I attended to getting them out. To dinner at 2.30 — Whittredge was expected & we waited until 3. About 5 got out to Cen. again. There until after 6 — then in to tea at 7.15 — Eveg with Mr. W. went up to the Lewis' in Greene S[treet]. Had a very pleasant eveg there until 10 o'clk — home at 10.30 & right to bed.
