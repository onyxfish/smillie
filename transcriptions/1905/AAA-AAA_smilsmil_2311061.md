## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 5, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An ofpressively warm day \- not so well to-day \- considerable fever but I got up \+ dressed. \- I tried to walk out of doors but could not. Geo. was full of business for me trying to arrange to have furniture sent from Storage to The Touraine. He sent a barber to the house who cut my hair, much to my comfort. I felt so much worse that I sent for Buffalo to consult about going to Hospital. He cheerfully acquiesced in my desire to go to the Presb'n. Sent word to Charl in the evlg \+ he arranged for my reception, over phone, with Jessie, at 11 A.M. to-morrow. Later he came in \+ completed arrangements with me.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 6, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

To Presbyterian Hospital. Room H.

Delightful change in weather \- brilliantly clear \+ fresh, invigorating air. but the latter element did not reach me. I was ready for Charl when he came for me with carriage at 10.30 \- we got to the hospital at 11 \+ Jessie met us on the stair \+ I was led at once to my room, large \+ sunny, on second floor. Jessie had attended to preliminaries as far as possible. My pedigree was taken \+ I was introduced to Dr. Brown who will have chg. of me, Miss Carpenter, who has general chg. of private patients in this corridor. To Miss Brandell who is to be my nurse in the m'f. \- to Miss Stevens who cares for me in the afternoon. Miss Mosely has me at night. \- Then Dr. Roc, day orderly, came \+ helped me into bed \- later Salt Cook, who is now in chg. of the accident ward came in \+ yet later Dr. \- The historian who got from me an extended history of my ills from way back. \- Yet later in the afternoon came Dr. Tuttle with Dr. Brown \- His visit was brief \- he had little to ask, his subordinates having gathered all.
