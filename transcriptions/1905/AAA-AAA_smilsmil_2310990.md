## Left Page

### Tues. May 16, 1905

Henry James, at Barnard Club.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Nasty day, foggy, mizzling \+ wet, rain at night. I got up with severe headache \+ was quite useless during the morning. I went to studio — Got out aqua-tint plate, "Old Tower on Rhine, at Constance" — the water was a failure so I decided to scrape it out \+ I set to work with scraper \+ stone. Walter Nettleton called \+ spent some time going over back Cos. of my N.A.D. files, to make a list of his exhibits. Home to lunch at 1. \+ then to bed — after 3 when I got up feeling better — again to studio \+ worked awhile scraping on Constance plate — Home \+ dressed before dinner — after dinner read newspaper till 8.30 then to Barnard Club — great crush, full dress — to hear Henry James read his essay on Balzac — about 300 women \+ 30 men — It was a full dress affair \+ some of the women made a wonderful display — I had a good seat when I first went in but under feminine pressure could not keep it — later I got some rest upon the arm of a chair that stood in a corner supporting a lady's hat. H.J. James is not etherial — he is portly \+ looks like a successful, English manufacturer. His voice is not bad but his manner of reading is decidedly — it is monotonous, hesitating, without vivacity \+ freely interspersed with ums — errs — etc. His pronunciation of some words was singularly incorrect — He many times repeated distinctly novel — model, etc. As I had to stand, near an open window, street noises interfered with hearing satisfactorily — but I had no difficulty in hearing that the lecture was an unendowed laudation of Balzac with some comparative analysis of Thackeray \+ others, English \+ French, of the last century — after the lecture the women ate him up \+ I walked home, no wiser, alas.

## Right Page

### Wed. May 17, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, damp, foggy, raw — despite the weather Maghee came at bout 9.30 \+ took Bessie off for a day's outing on his automobile, went to Inglewood or thereabouts. I got to studio at about usual time \+ had a good mornings work finishing the cleaning of Constance plate — on my return from lunch I tried to get liquid aquatint ground on the cleaned part of the plate but the plate was not flat enough so I had, after much trouble to lay a dry one. \+ then varnished plate ready for acid to-morrow. Home to dinner at 6.30 — belated, waiting for Maghee who having set at 6 with Bessie, went to his club to "brush up" — he stayed till 9 — I got rid of evelf with newspaper.
