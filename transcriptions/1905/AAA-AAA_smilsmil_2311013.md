## Left Page

### Sat. July 1, 1905

Ralph came home.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night — showers, some of them quite heavy — during the m'f. — dry afternoon, but no clearing. I worked about the house till after 10 — then to bank \+ on my way back stopped at Griffen Co. Wall Papers, Cor. 5th Ave. \+ 30th St. \+ selected a paper for wall of 3d. story front bed room — Then home — more desultory work till 1 o'cl — lunch — a colored woman at work all day scrubbing floors, making things look more presentable. Went to Touraine hoping to be able to make some arrangement by which I could send furniture, or part of it, for apartment, there — could not — Ralph came from his last Exam. at Dobb's Ferry, direct — pretty well played out. It has been a severe ordeal for him — 18 subjects crowded into a very short space of time when he was physically unfit — but he hopes to escape heavy conditions. We went to Charl's for dinner at 9 \+ spent the evening there till 9.30 — Then home \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 2, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wet muggy day altho' rather cool — Ther. about 68°-70°. — heavy showers about middle of day \+ again at night — Pkbp'. at 9 — after 10 to studio — then home — some picking up — then wrote to Kate — lunch at 1.30. more desultory work — it seems impossible for me to go right ahead \+ clear up as I go — At this rate I shall be here all summer \+ will get away only by doing something desperate — Sorted a lot of old papers \+ threw away a lot of stuff — Ralph went to Hackley for some last things \+ got back in the afternoon \+ at 6.30 he \+ I went to Charl's to dinner. Geo. came there later — I left at 9 o'cl. home \+ wrote for an hour or so.
