## Left Page

### Sat. July 15, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A delicious day — the Ther. in my room getting no higher than 76°, with cool \+ refreshing breeze. P[illegible] — at 8 — after that I rode into town with Parke — left him at his office — then back to his house — trunk came — unpacked a portion of it \+ made change in dress — then dozed \+ rested on my bed till 1 — lunch — Jerry Ballantine there, just arr. from Montrose with Helen. After lunch P\. \+ I rode into town, Ralph with us, \+ met Mrs. P. — \+ then we had a very enjoyable ride to the High Parkground \+ around the lake (Reservoir) met Ballantine \+ Helen in his big automobile — Helen changed places with Ralph — She rode home with us, getting in near 4 — then I went to my room \+ had more rest. Dinner before eve[illegible]. Greer came home from State Militia cam — very hungry, dirty \+ tired — Ralph \+ I had a short moonlight walk.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 16, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm day with delightfully cool breeze. P[illegible] about 9 — after 10 I rode down town by car, bought Sunday Trib. — then ret. home \+ before dinner at 1.30 had a nap. The Rev. Mr. May, Parke's pastor dined with us \+ we had, after it, a protracted seance on front loggia — after that lounging \+ another nap before supper — much of the eve[illegible] was spent looking at books (old illustrated) china \+ silver ware. To bed at 11.
