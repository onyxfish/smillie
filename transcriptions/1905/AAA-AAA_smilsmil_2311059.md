## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 1, 1905

left St. Hubert's for Elizabethtown.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another wonderfully fine day — cloudless, hazy + warm. I had a good nights rest + got up feeling hopefully well — It was after bkfs. while out in the sunshine on upper piazza + enjoying the color on the mts, that Bessie told me the dreadful news rec'd from Charl last eve'g about eutpker time, that Kate had died suddenly at Dresden about noon of yesterday — heart failure following a severe indigestion. Mary was much overcome. I rallied as promptly as I could + went thro' the finishing of packing — with Katharine's aid I got trunk + packing case filled + at 1 o'clk. had dinner. At about 2.30 Mary + I got away in Deede's easy surry + had, as near as possible, a pleasant ride over the mountain to E. Town, I reached the Windsor at 5.30 hardly tired — The journey was surprisingly easy to me — Katharine + Elizabeth are left at the Mk to finish the closing up, with Mr. Kane, + follow us later in the week.

We had our supper at about 7 — sat by a big fire till after 8 — + then to bed.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 2, 1905

110 E. 38th St. N.Y.

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Yet another very fine day — my night was not quite a restful one but I got up at abt 7 feeling very weak but as tho' I might get thro' the work of the day — The bladder trouble was in evidence but not to the extent of embarrassing one — I had a very short walk before bkfs — about 8.30 — After that most of my time was spent sunning myself on an eastern piazza Till 10.15 when the St. Hubert stage came with B. Garvin + Marie + Duga — In about half an hour we all got away for Westport to catch the 12.18 train due in N.Y. at 8 — Got to W. port sometime before 12 — Train 35 m. late — some had about an hour to wait — more than enough time for me to get the 7 trunks checked + the 3 boxes + two crated bicycles expressed — The wait was very trying to me — when the train came there was a great rush for parlor car seats — we were able to secure our at last, but only to Albany as the car, on this train, went no farther — then we fought for seats in dining car — very small + miserably equipped — After 2 o'clk. Mary + I were served — exasperatingly. we got to Albany 1 hour + 20 m. late + lost our Western connection over Central at 4.55 — After much consultation the officials decided to hitch us to the 7.05 Exp., following the 20th Century. The servants were turned at about into the station waiting room — Garvin + I went to restaurant + got something to eat — I gave the girls their R.R. tickets + "trunk checks" with advice as to the train they were to take at 7.05. Garvin was more trouble than helps to me as he was constantly promising to do things that he didn't — Being nearly worn out + with considerable hour I tried to rest but at 7 o'clk. started a hunt for the girls — They were nowhere to be found — I went twice thro' our very long train — once thro' a train bound north thro' the station — our train went on without them — They must have got onto the 20th Century Exp. + W'd get into N.Y. nay an hour ahead of us — Our run to N.Y. was to me decidedly trying but at 10.30 it came to an end — I had telegraphed from Albany to Geo. + Charl. Just as we got down upon the platform, Mary stumbled over a post mention + fell heavily, striking her face + injuring her right wrist — she was stunned + taken up — G. + Charl. + Elise met us — we were bundled into a carriage + taken to 110 — Mary was in such a state that C. went for Dr. Putnam — he came, bandaged the arm + Mary was put to bed alongside of Nellie + the? went to Charls for the night. I got to bed at once — a pretty pair of cripples.
