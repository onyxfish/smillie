## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 30, 1905

**Weather:** Cold, windy & dirty  **Temp:** [illegible]

as usual — I was engaged in my rooms till after 10 — then went to Kimmel & Voigt's, 242 Canal St. Their printing business is being wound up — I got a couple of plates of mine that had been for some time in their safe — Then to studio — Packed a number of Wm. Cook's books etc. that I have found in the course of my clearing up, to Exp. To Katchese in Cambridge — then to Touraine, Lock's man came & put up & one curtains — I prepared some junket for myself — did not go out till 4 o'clk — then to studio — got pkg for Katchese & took it to Exp Co. Then to my rooms — Jim & Ralph went to Edw. Cook's to dinner at 6.45 & I went to the Murray Hill Hotel & got mine. A little before 8 I went to Edward's — all still at table — Soon after 8, Ralph & I left & went to Academy of Music — 14th St — & Irving Place & saw Babes in Toyland — an exceedingly [illegible] foolish spectacle with some catchy music, showy dances by shameless girls & some extremely ridiculous acrobatic dancers & general horse play Rob't Burns, the sentimental Ruffian & [illegible] the toymaker's assistant & Maud Campbell, attu[illegible] jil. That such apts are popular, proves a low standard of popular taste sad to think of — Got to our beds at midnight.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 31, 1905

**Weather:** overcast with a smell of snow in the air  **Temp:** [illegible]

but it did not materialize — Up at 8 & had as much of a bath & scrub as I could take with my adhesive plaster bands holding my injured ribs in place — While at my bath Louise (Waiter) Cook called me on 'phone & asked the boys & me to lunch at 1.15 Before I went to bkfp. at 9 o'clk. I had a 'phone talk with D.[illegible] ale — At 12.30 I went out & walked about till 1 o'clk — then to rooms & with the boys went to Walter Cook's — Coffin & Elizabeth & young Walter were there — the lunch was a hearty meal — before we left Mr. Dielman came in — It was near 4 o'clk. when we left — The boys went to Brooklyn to make calls & took a stroll of about two miles & then to my room & had a nap — At 7 Jim Ralph & I went to the Park Ave Hotel & had a 75 c. Table'd'hote dinner — It was a fairly good dinner — especially so when price was considered — It was after 8 when we rel? to our rooms — Quiet chat & chatting & reading.
