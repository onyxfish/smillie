## Left Page

### Sun. June 25, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant summer day — hot, yes, but with a refreshing air stirring — Katie Elkin & I got to Woolsey Hall at about 10 o'clk & found that our seats were not very desirable — Sect. 10. Right balcony & rather far back — just before the exercises began we were allowed to change our seats for better ones but even then we did not get choice ones. Pres. Hadley is not a good speaker, nor are the accoustic effects of the hall good, consequently it was hard work listening to him. I cannot understand why Carrere & Hastings when planning a hall for great functions, to be attended by large crowds, should have chosen one having immense supporting columns that necessarily cut off a large percentage of the audience from seeing the middle of the stage.

I have a good report of what I did not hear given in the New Haven Journal Courier — This I shall put with Jim's correspondence to complete the Yale record for him. We all went to The Elkins home after service — had dinner — the afternoon weather was fine, growing rapidly cooler. At 4.30 we went again to Woolsey State & heard Prof. Jepson's organ Recital on the Grand Organ there — He is an accomplished performer & showed great dexterity but it seemed to me that he was too anxious to show the two extremes — power & delicacy — we had too little of a fair average of sound & the color possible with the great number of stops. — We rode back to 477 Prospect — weather decidedly cooler — Dinner — Chatting every one to bed early.

## Right Page

### Mon. June 26, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The weather was cloudy in the m'f. & almost cold — there was one dash of a shower in the m'f., but the p.m. was glorious — a peal of thunder during Class day exercises in the afternoon made us uneasy for a few minutes but nothing came of it. After b'kft. I walked down to Alumni Hall & attended the Alumni meeting — Dickinson W. Richards, lawyer of N.Y. presided with humor — an 1880 class man — Hadley presented the Annual Report, speaking at considerable length — Kennedy, the boating coach made an amusing speech — like a boy reciting something committed to memory. I wonder if somebody else wrote it for him — others spoke altogether it was to me a very interesting meeting. I got to 477 early for lunch & at about 1.30 started for Vanderbilt Hall where we met Jim leading his party, Mrs. Murphy & two daughters, Mrs. Hardenburg & one daughter — two sisters Kipling, Sue Whiting & Rene Atwater — when Katie & I joined he had eleven to look after — we went to the enclosure (temporary) on the campus and the showed us to our seats, all cushioned, under the shade of a big elm in admirable position to see & near enough to hear the speakers — a photographer took a large picture, of which I got a good copy — the exercises were very interesting to me & we enjoyed them very much — I slipped away immediately at the close & went over to Hendrie Hall where for a few minutes I listened to Wm. Taft discourse about Woldlaw — then I ran to Vanderbilt & in Jim's room found a crowd making lots of noise & having a good deal of fun — the girls were there — Hot sapper was at the piano & the boys said they were singing — I left at about 5, feeling that I must have a little rest — went home — on Louise till 6.30 — dinner — then a hurried dressing & before 8 Katie & I went to Woolsey Hall — Glee Club & Band Concert — S. Tyler made a great hit on the his Negro Songs — On the whole the entertainment disappointed me a little, perhaps because we sat under the balcony & it was cold, the ventilators at our feet making me uncomfortable — Jim sat with us — about 10 we had to leave the Hall till the floor was cleared of chairs — it was about 11 before we could return & the dancing of the Senior Prom commenced — we had good seats in the balcony & watched the dancers till midnight — then we went out to our waiting cab — Returned home & got to bed at about 1 o'clk. after a very strenuous day.

I attended no functions in the m'f. as I have recorded — The Alumni meeting was held Tues. m'f., this was written on Wed. — owing to the rush of things I have tumbled over myself.
