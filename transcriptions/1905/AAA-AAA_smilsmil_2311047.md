## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 7, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A disagreeable day, without a gleam of sunshine \- light drizzles at intervals. In spite of weather Bashaw & Earl Moody did good work on the Tennis court & the ground lying south & west of Road preparing it for seeding \- I spent a considerable part of the m't with them & in the garden \- did not go to P.O. Dinner at 1 \- after that, fire in my room & wrote awhile on letter to Emma J. After 4 o'clk. Mr. Hardenburgh & Maghee came \& later, Miss Josephine Bates of N.Y. a music teacher of N.Y. of mature years \- there was a musical seance that was very interesting \- Miss Rose sang with surprising force & feeling & pleased me greatly \- Bessie played very well & there was animated conversation & critical interchange between Miss B. & Miss R \-. Afternoon tea was over at 6,15 & at 6.45 we had supper \- Eve't. Miss Rose gave us further evidence of her entertaining powers \- singing little songs without accompaniment Repeating amusing verse in English & German \- card tricks & telling fortunes \- To bed after 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 8, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day, followed by a beautiful moonlight. Up soon after 6 & wrote to Pearce & Seligman before bkft. \+ after bkft. wrote to Geo. Sometime in the garden \- Bashaw did not come to-day \- I looked after Moody for awhile & at noon went to Dr. duBois' study & with him got correct time \- he took me to his house & gave me a Grot. Nautical Almanac for 1903 from which I got some interesting information concerning the several kinds of time \- True \- mean \- standard sidereal etc \- Went to Scott Brown's \- on my way to P.O. met Jim & Ralph at my gate \- Jim kindly took my letters to P.O. Dinner at 1. Afternoon about the place after an after dinner dessert of some of Miss Rose's songs. Moody, under my direction transplanted some small spruces to the bank above the wall on the road \- Supper before 7. Eve't. Maghee came & Miss R. gave us a farewell concert \- singing several songs of her own composition \- the words & music of the last one "The Spinster's Plaint" being by her. She is a very clever girl \- About 10 I went strolling about in the open south of the house enjoying the moonlight.
