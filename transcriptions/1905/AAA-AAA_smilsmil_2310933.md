## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 20, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day — sunny — crisp but not cold — To studio \+ worked on sketch of Times Bldg — having in mind to etch it. I did not work with any comfort or enthusiasm — is it age? Yale came in soon after 11 \+ spent the rest of the m'g — Poor fellow — he has my sincerest sympathy — Since the death of his boy he has broken perceptibly — Home to lunch \+ after it went to 42d S with pad \+ made some slight sketches \+ notes of the Times Bldg — \+ upon return to studio reconstructed my sketch, changing proportions \+ relations — Home soon after 5 — Rested till dinner. Evelf. wrote to the boys — Went to G. C. Station before 11 — mailed letters

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 21, 1905

**Weather:** Cloudy \+ threatening  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy \+ threatening — To studio soon after 10 — Ther. 60° — So, instead of going on with the sketch of Times Bldg I saw with overcoat on \+ cut mats for the printing of croaked negatives etc — Panel back stretcher for the Ge. Ireland portrait came — Home to lunch at 1.30 — back soon after 2 — I took the Ireland portrait off from its old stretcher \+ when I came to fit it on the new one found that the stretcher was a little more than 1/8 of an inch too large — That will have to be reduced — Went on with photo. work — mounted some prints — Home soon after 5 — The parlor furniture, with its new covering came home this p. m. — Rested till dinner at 6.30 —
