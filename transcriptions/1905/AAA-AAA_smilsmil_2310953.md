## Left Page

### Wed. March 1, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dined with the Beach's

Fine day - rather cold & windy - after bkfs. went to W. S. Miller's & to Douglass & paid bills - then to barber's & had hair cut - It was 11 o'clk. when I got to Studio until after 12 I worked with photos - Then to 2d. Nat'l. Bank - then home to lunch. It was 3 o'clk. before I got to Studio - again working on photo negatives till after 4 - then home at about 5 - Rested - dressed & at 7 went to 1147 E. 38th St. - Lea & Violet Beach - Mrs. Morrison & her sister Miss Buckingham & Mr Chas. Hy. Hart, Mr. Bugelow. were the guests beside myself. There was plenty of chaff & chat but I was out of my element & felt ill at ease - I can now understand & sympathize with Father's extreme unwillingness to go out among people. - It is much easier to stay at home.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 2, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still clear, cold & windy. To Studio at 10 & commenced final painting on Vesuvius from near Sorrento. 33×44 - I say final because I have promised to deliver it to W. G. Parker before the 20th of this month. Worked till 12.30 - then to bank & got a loan of $500 to meet College & school bills of Jim & Ralph - Home to lunch & back to Studio & worked till 4.30 when I was so tired that I stopped for a rest - I worked as long as I could see on some of my photos. It was just about 6.30 when I got home - dinner - Every I went, with Mary & the girls, to Chas's to meet his friend Bernard from Japan. Home soon after 10.
