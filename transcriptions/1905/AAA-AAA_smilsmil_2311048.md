## Left Page

### Sat. Sept. 9, 1905

*Miss Rose left us.*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A wonderfully beautiful day followed by a glorious moonlight night. It was near 10 o'clk. when Miss Rose left us to ride over to Lake Placid there to take a night train to her home at Niagara Falls — Then I spent an hour in the garden \+ until 12 with Basham \& Moody on the Tennis Court — Mr. White is now having a tennis court laid out in the woods near her house \+ at different times during the past four days I have heard the blasting necessary in removing rocks \+ the roots of trees. At noon I went to P.O. — dinner at 1 o'clk — after that I directed the taking of sod from the border of Tennis Court \+ transferring it to a place at W. E. cor. of house — I made a mistake in having the sod on Tennis Ct. ploughed up — it should have been cut off \+ transplanted to several of the sandy slopes where grass seed won't take —
Supper at 6.30 — Ev\+t. all, save Mary \+ self, went to hotel where the Cottagers have their last Sat. ev\+t. gathering — I read till 9 — then I went out \+ was fascinated by the moonlight — a cloudless sky — luminous atmosphere — the mts. looming grandly — Still air — Sons of river \+ rustle of aspens — I promenaded the road out on the alder, south of the house till I was thoroughly tired — it was truly heavenly —

## Right Page

### Sun. Sept. 10, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another very lovely day, altho' some of the clouds had a suspicious look — my Sunday scrub — bkft \+ garden — \+ other functions and duties took up much of the morning time — I loafed about but took no longer walk than to the P.O. before dinner at 1.30 — Some writing \+ more roaming about in the afternoon — Ev\+t., again a glorious moonlight that we all went out to enjoy under the little aspens, looking to the south to Noonmark, Colvin \+ Sawtooth, grand in the misty luminosity of the moon lit air —
