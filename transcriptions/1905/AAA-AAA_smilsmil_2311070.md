## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 23, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day — The day opened for me at 7.30 - Not put in an appearance - much chastened — I got up on the roof at 11 o'clk \+ was there till after 1 — I wheeled myself about to various positions \+ enjoyed the sunshine \+ air immensely — While there young Elliman came to tell me that he \+ Herron would settle the matter of com'n amicably between them — which was very nice of him. Clarence Smith came at about 9 for a consultation. I had my dinner at 1.30 — Then sat in my chair in my room \+ read — Then I went out in the corridor — into Dr. Brown's room \+ had a chat with him — my Trouble is termed, I believe, Pialetins, or something like that, involving the Pelvis of the Right Kidney \+ along the ureter to the bladder. It was after 4 when I again got into bed \+ had a nap — Jessie came in as usual \+ chat. Collins came after 5 — It was after 9 when Jim came — he stayed till 10 — Miss Mosley was again on duty, being laid off last night on acct. of indisposition.

Dr. Tuthill made no call.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 24, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Contract passed for sale of 110.

Some rain in the mt — occasional gleuts of sunshine until afternoon where the clouding was complete. Miss Mosely came at 6.30 when I was lothe to rouse myself — my head hurt me — I was hoarse \+ felt altogether quite out of sorts — After bkfst. at 8 I felt somewhat refreshed. Clarence Smith came at about 9 with copies of contract, which, on reading, I approved. Dr. Brown did not see me in the mt, there being much confusion owing to some change of program of movement of doctors — nor did I see Dr. Brown \+ Tuthill in the afternoon. After my dinner I got up, dressed \+ for about an hour I promenaded the corridor \+ loafed in the Physician's Parlor — Then back to bed — About 5 o'clk. Clarence Smith \+ young Elliman came \+ the contract of sale was formally completed \+ Elliman gave an instrument insuring me agt. claims that might be made by Southack \& Co. for commissions. In the evef. Eddie Cook \+ Dr. Mason came in \+ made pleasant calls \+ after 9 Jim came \+ stayed till 10 —
