## Left Page

### Mon. March 13, 1905

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - Another day divided between work in fixing up my etching room \+ fussing with my photos. I was not in condition for any thing having had a trying \+ restless night - I got a telegram from Park saying that the picture had arr. \+ asking me to come on to hang it at once. I do not feel able to undertake such a journey but should do so if possible I promised to do it \+ the moral effect upon me will be good if I can do it - Fred. Young \+ Gene Atwater at lunch - unexpectedly. To studio at 2 - Men came \+ took "Rivers Source" to Newark for Public Library $46[illegible] Home before 6 - dinner - Eve'g - very stupid - slept in my chair for awhile - to bed early.

## Right Page

### Tues. March 14, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day, bright \+ reasonably mild. I got up feeling far from well \+ decided not to go to Scranton - I was very reluctant in coming to this conclusion - after bkfst. I wrote a letter to Park in explanation - then, on my way to studio I telegraphed him - In writing I gave directions about hanging the painting - after some general work at Studio I went to dm. Art Ass'n \+ spent some time looking at the Edwin \& Weeks' paintings - a remarkable showing for quality \+ quantity of work - on my way home stopped a gas-stove store [illegible] to have a man sent to the house next Monday to examine the laundry gas stove - date to lunch - To studio at 2.30 - After a time walked to [illegible] - $20 - 8 $2.20 St. galvanizing establishment, \+ left bottle stand from hot water tank to be galvanized - Then to Eimer \+ Amend's \+ got a bottle of Nitric acid - then back to studio - after 5 I went to 53 W. 47 St. \+ saw Miss Roelker - she said that she was entirely satisfied with my sketch for a book plate \+ wished me to etch the design for her - Mr. R- came in \+ we had a chat so pleasant that it was late when I got home to dinner - Eve'g some reading of newspapers \+ a little writing
