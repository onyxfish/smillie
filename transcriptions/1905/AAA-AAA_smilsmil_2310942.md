## Left Page

### Tues. Feb. 7, 1905

**Weather:** Cold day & clear  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold day & clear — sidewalks in a dreadful condition from ice, ashes, sand & salt. I got to studio at about usual time in the m'g. I got my three gelatine tracings finished & ready for transfering — then I got my press ready & then I got three capital transfers — Home to lunch — after that I went to 2d. Nat. Bank — then to studio & commenced the etching f a Chapel near Lugano. Home after 5 — Cap. Raising of the Patricia, took dinner with us at 6.30 — Charl came in later — The Cap. is an interesting character.

## Right Page

### Wed. Feb. 8, 1905

**Weather:** milder day  **Temp:** [illegible]

milder day — the condition of street sidewalks is but little bettered — ice & dirty slush prevails — After 10 when I got to studio — worked all day on etching "Chapel near Lugano" & got it drawn in \+ the face of plate varnished — The realization is forced upon me that I cannot etch any better than I can paint in oil or water-color — I see clearly the want of genius, inspiration, in it all — it never rises above the level of a trained workman's product — it is humiliating but it is truth — Home to lunch as usual — worked till after 4 — a short rest before dinner — Evg. Read & wrote to Nicoll about the Rotary Water-Color Exhib. — my eyes have troubled me much all day — blurred & doubled vision — like what troubled Father so much.
