## Left Page

### Tues. June 13, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

a muggy, misty, uncomfortably hot + breezy day — Before 8 went to 110 to see if the Painters had started work as promised — none there at 8.35 when I left \+ Ret. to Charl's to Bkft. After that went to Lewis \+ Congers, W. 42d St. \+ bought Coffee pot — then to 110 — men had come \+ were at work in attic Room — Got books out of book case there — Then, after writing to Jim \+ to Roland Mulford I had lunch with Geo. To Halmenfeld's — Packed a box with Coffee — pot — tea \+ Olive oil \+ at 3.40 went to Emma Ireland's — prepared to varnish Grandfather Ireland again — worked awhile to get a buckle out of the canvas — decided to leave it for the present \+ get blocks to put upon panel back for purchase for ways to stretch it more fully. — Left there at 5.30 — walked over to Bridgeman's to get Pea seed for the Atk — closed — Then to Butler's — paid bill — to Cushman's — paid bill — home at 6.20 Rested till near 7 — Then to Charl's — he going to Union League Club dinner to Joseph Choate — so I dined alone — we'd written letter to Mary \+ got to bed early.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 14, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudless hot day — Official, 86°. which means much hotter in the street. — Bkft. at 9 — about 10 went to Miller's \+ ordered blocks for Ireland panel back stretcher — to studio — to 110 \+ spent rest of m'g in Taking down from closets top a great lot of unbound Vols. of Punch, Ill. Land., News \+ other works. Lunch with Ge? — wrote to K.P, T — Then to studio \+ gathered \+ arranged unbound Punch's There about 4 went to Bridgman's \+ got sue peas for Keene — Then to Alexander's about shoes, then to Buskirk's \+ got writing paper — Then home \+ had a rest before dinner — To Charl's at 7 — dinner — Ge? \+ Gordon came in for awhile \+ in the ev'g. — Later Minnie (Pierce) came \+ had a half hysterical time with Charl \+ self — she is much wrought up over a passion her boy has developed for a girl 8 years his senior — a most desirable person in every other respect — Walter (her husband) is abroad \+ will not get home for a week or ten days yet.
