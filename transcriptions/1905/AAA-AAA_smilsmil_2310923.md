## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 1, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very mild sunny day — after a comfortless night I got up at about 8 o'clk — feeling very seedy — Bkfs. at 9 — For some time after that time went in looking over the 90 odd negatives that Cullen sent home yesterday P.M. Some of these are very fine but not one is perfectly true upon the film. In all cases where architecture is shown the perpendiculars are all out. This is, of course, a grievous misfortune — I went to studio before noon — then walked in the sunshine on Park Ave. \+ There met Miss Thompson who has acted as trained nurse for me two or three times. She was, for the moment, in charge of a baby carriage — went to Charl's for a few minutes. Home to dinner at 2 — Spent most of the remainder of afternoon sleeping — Eve'g at 8 o'clk — after having written a note to Rev. T. C. Williams, I went to Emma J's \+ spent the eve'g with her \+ Mary \+ Grace.
