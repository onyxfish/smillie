## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 13, 1905

**Weather:** I had a chill in the eve  **Temp:**

Jim started for N.Y.

Up at 6 — a blustering, raw m't. Threatening Rain — Bkf't. for the family at 7 o'clk — Im Stage was to come for Jim at 7.30 — it did not — so I went over to Scott Brown the Telegh't. to Ferd Reed's \+ found that it had started, so I hurried back \+ met it coming out of the Art gate with Jim \+ baggage — bid him good bye \+ then walked down to Ferd Reede's to enquire about the ½ Ton of fertilizer that it seems impossible for me to get started from West Port. Then home — Mrs. Hardenburg's baggage went down to Wpt. at 10 — The wagon is to bring back my fertilizer. I took me two walks to Scott Brown's \+ my trip down to Reedes, one after the other without discomfort or feeling tired. I had a dinner that was not very digestible — creamed chicken etc. \+ huckleberry pie — but felt no ill effects that I recognized as tracable to it — but I felt a growing \+ strange uneasiness as the day wore to eve'g. At 6 o'clk I recognized a coming chill. I hustled into bed but was not quick enough to head it off — very soon I was in spasms of chill \+ chatter \+ could with difficulty control myself enough to speak — Dr. Garvin came in answer to call \+ could not stop the spasmodic contractions — after 7 o'clk. my stomach set off a volcanic commotion \+ scattered red hot lava some time \+ the chill ceased after continuing more than two hours — the longest \+ severest attack I ever had — then came the fever — It went to 103° \+ I don't know how much higher — \+ I had a horrible night.

Brilliant moonlight \+ frost that killed corn, squashes, tomatoes etc.

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 14, 1905

**Weather:** The Parkes came.  **Temp:**

A delightfully clear, rather cold day — The Parkes arr'd from E. Town before 10'clk — Kellogg, \+ the winds or having prevailed upon Parke to hire his team during their visit — I was in bed all day racked with fever \+ pain \+ could not see them — Dr. Garvin came in the m't \+ gave me very careful attention — going all over me from Tops to toe — front \+ back, \+ raised my temp. 2°. but he found nothing that satisfied him. In the afternoon I had two or three movements of the bowels with some singular exhibitions of mucous. During the night there was a constant desire to urinate, very little passing \+ having disagreeable odor — The night was one long fever nightmare, every fibre in me sore \+ the smallest movement made only with painful difficulty — I could not lie ten minutes in one position \+ every change had to be made in sections.
