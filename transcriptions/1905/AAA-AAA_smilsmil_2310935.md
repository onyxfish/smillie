## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 24, 1905

**Weather:** light snow, cold \+ wind all day  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio about 10.30 — The days work was divided between pen work on Times Bldg sketch \+ photos — Home to lunch \+ back as usual — Home at 5.30 — a bit \+ dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. went with Katharine to Mendelssohn Hall, 40'S. To annual Meeting, Geographical Socy \+ heard address from Lt. Peary, the Pres. n the worlds Geographic work \+ progress during the past year — It was too much to tell in one eve'g \+ consequently was very scrappy — There was a good audience inspite of inclement weather.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 25, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A genuine blizzard — wind \+ snow all night \+ wind, snow \+ cold all day — it was really tempestuous \+ I was severely tried in facing the storm in coming home at lunch time \+ again in the eve'g. But the studio was comfortable \+ I divided my days work between those everlasting photos \+ my pen sketch of the Times building — I came home to lunch as usual \+ to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. I wrote a letter to Nate \+ mailed it after 10 p.m. — At that hour the storm had abated considerably.
