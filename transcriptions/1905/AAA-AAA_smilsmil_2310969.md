## Left Page

### Tues. April 4, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fickle day \- overcast m'f \- sunshine at mid-day \- light rain at about 6 p.m. \+ heavy thunder showers at 9 o 11 o' clk \-

To studio as usual \+ worked all the m'f on etching "Port Marie, Paris", bitten yesterday \- home to lunch at 1 \- after it Ralph \+ I went to Century \+ saw paintings \- then to 53 w. 14th St. \+ left proof of Ex-libris plate for Miss Rosekee \- then to Pease \+ Ellimans \- but did not see Mr. P \-, then to the Touraine \+ looked at a furnished apartment there \- not satisfactory \- then to Murray Hill Hotel \- saw rooms there that might have been satisfactory, three room suites \+ bath room, but the price, $100 \+ $75 a week, was prohibitive to me, much to my disappointment \- then we went to Studio \+ I showed R \- how to change black Velox prints to Sepia \- Home at 5.30 \+ rested till dinner at 6.30 \- The rain made a change in our plan of going to some place of amusement \- Ralph went to Charl's \+ spent the ev'g there \- I remained at home \+ fooled away the ev'g over my newspapers \-

## Right Page

### Wed. April 5, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The Hingshelwoods came from [illegible] \-

Very dark with heavy rain most of the day \- I spent the m'f at studio working on Port Marie Etching much of the work being cleaning the open spaces of the plate which is very dirty \- Home to lunch at 1 \- then back to studio, made up Velox developing solution \- then home \+ spent the rest of the afternoon with Ralph printing from some of his Ensofean films. At about 6 p.m. Jace \+ Mary Hinshelwood came along having missed their train on Jersey Central \- they stayed with us over night \- in very good spirits having sold their old mahogany furniture, long stored at [illegible] for good prices \- Ev'g. Charl came in \+ did a little more printing before going to bed.
