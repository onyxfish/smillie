## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 3, 1905

**Weather:** Very clear + very cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph dined with us \-

Very clear + very cold \- I got up with a splitting headache \- ate a very light b'kf't + then had to go back to bed again \- I lay there till 11 \- then up + went to Cor. of 5th Ave. + 42d. St. with my camera + got the shot that I was too late for yesterday \- then to studio + developed the photo \- Home at 1 \- took no lunch + went to bed again \- there till 5 \- then up + was surprised by Ralph who had come to town to attend to some Hockey business \- Kindled fire in parlor + sat there till dinner at 6.30 \- Ralph Ret'd. to Hackley on 8.35 train \- I loafed + did some reading \- head still very sore.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 4, 1905

**Weather:** Clear + cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Clear + cold \- to studio after 10 + worked all the m't on tracing of old road side Chapel near Lugano \- then home to lunch + back at 2 \- I commenced a tracing of my pen sketch, Port Marie, Paris, made in 93 + of which I intend to make an etching, but Will Parke came in + he took so much of my day-light that I did not get it finished \- home soon after 5 + had a short rest after that + before dinner \- Evening all hands (that is, Mary + her girls) went to Charl's, leaving me alone \- I passed the time quietly in front of the parlor fire writing to Jim + contemplating bills that I have not money enough in bank to pay.
