## Left Page

### Mon. Dec. 18, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A singularly mild day — with true autumn haze — that thickened up in the afternoon making it less pleasant. I went to see Dr. Brown in his new quarters with Dr. Forbes at 96 Park Ave. This is Dr. Brown's first step after the close of his hospital course. He has made some business arrangement with Dr. Forbes — I had intended to make a congratulatory call only but it promptly took on a professional character \+ I let it go at that — He asked me to call to-morrow with a specimen of my urine, in which there is no pus now, \+ he would massage the Kidney. Then I walked up \+ down Park Ave. for awhile \+ then to studio — where I worked for awhile still assorting rubbish of all sorts — to-day it was the back Nos of various periodecals, St. Nicholas, Round Table, Charities \+ various magazines. — Some that are full files for several years I propose to Return to the publishers — others, illustrated and more ephemeral may go to hospitals or the Salvation Army — about 2 o'clk. I prepared a light lunch for myself at my apartment \+ then another bout at the studio — At dinner time Jim phoned me that he w'd be kept at Scribners till near 10 — so I took my dinner alone \+ near 9 o'clk. went to Charles — there till 10.30 where he escorted me back to the Tourraine — Jim was already in bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Dec. 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another delightful m'f. — overcast later but still pleasant — at 9.30 I went to Dr. Brown — 96 Park Ave. — He stripped me partially \+ massaged my Kidney, expecting possibly to start the flow of pus again, but none followed. I promenaded awhile on the sunny side of Park Ave — then to studio \+ continued my work there till near 1 o'clk — then to Lock's. To Tourraine about 1.30 — Prepared a lunch for myself. By the time I had eaten it Lock's man came with curtains, poles \+ other things — He worked till 5.30 \+ then did not finish — he will have to come again. Alice Ireland made a pleasant call while this work was going on — At 7 o'clk I had my dinner alone — Jim is now kept at Scribner's thro' the eve'f. I read till about 9 — then to studio \+ got bust of Annie \+ carried it to apartment which is now greatly improved by the curtains and hangings — I had a worry over Jim who did not get in till midnight having gone off with one of his chums after Scribner's closed — I sat up for him.
