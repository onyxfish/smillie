## Left Page

### Tues. March 21, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day of Rain \+ wind. I got to studio rather late - all the day was practically about photographs, putting prints between blotters \+ mounting others. I went home to lunch at 1 \+ then back to studio \+ worked there till almost 6 - Then home - dinner - Eve'g. I worked at Velox printing till after 10 - much bothered by some peculiarly dense negatives from which I cannot get satisfactory prints - Such work worries me into such a state that I must give up doing it at night - it spoils my sleep.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 22, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some sunshine, but most of the day was dull \+ rather cold. To studio before 10. A good part of the aft I was fooling with photos, but I did some work on the Roelker book plates. At 12.30 I went to Douglas Volk's studio to see his large canvas for the Governor's room in the St. Paul's Capitol building of Minnesota. It seems to me rather a crude affair - he says he expects to finish it on the wall where it is to hang. Then home to lunch at 1 - Mary \+ Bessie lunched with Mr. T. G. Shearman in Brooklyn. Ryan came \+ looked at 2d story bath room that I am going to have made up to date preparatory to renting the house. To studio at 2.30 made gelatine tracing of R. book plates - Then had a two mile promenade on Park Ave. Dinner at 6.30 - Eve'g., after newspaper I took my Velox printing machine apart \+ got the water out of the drum - Then I wrote for archives in cat. of European felens of last summer's trip
