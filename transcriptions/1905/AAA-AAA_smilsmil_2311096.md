## Left Page

### Wed. Dec. 20, 1905

**Weather:** Fine m'g - overcast afternoon - rain at night.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I spent the m'g at studio after having gone to St. Brown's to leave a not — I met him coming out of his door \+ together we walked down Park Ave. I worked hard selecting \+ dragging out from several piles the pictures that I wish taken to the appartment — Then I went to Lock's \+ arranged to have his wagon sent to studio at 2.30. Then to Touraine \+ had a light lunch — then to studio \+ by 3.30 had all the pictures all at appartment — \+ in another half hour had them stowed in a closet — Later went to studio again \+ on my way back stopped at Charl's \+ saw then Mary \+ Katharine — I arranged to return to C's \+ dine with him — then to my room \+ had a nap — then to C's at 7 o'clk. \+ had a very good dinner — \+ spent the ev'g with him till 10.30 — Then borrowed an umbrella to keep me dry on my way to the Touraine.

## Right Page

### Thur. Dec. 21, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came from New Haven.

A nasty storm during the night \+ continuing during the m'g till about 10 — I went to studio \+ packed a box with the 38 Vols (G. J. Cook) Atlantic Monthly to send to Mr. Murphy — I cleaned up the open parts of studio's, altogether making a marked change in the appearance of the place — Then, at 1 o'clk. to Touraine \+ there found Ralph who had come from Mft. on the 8 am train \+ been delayed. We had lunch \+ before 4 o'clk. went to studio where some more work was done — R. \+ I had denner at 7 \+ spent the ev'g in our rooms at the Touraine, rigging my lounge as a bed for him in Jim's room — Jim came in at about ten — all hands to bed soon after.
