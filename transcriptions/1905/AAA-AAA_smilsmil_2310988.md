## Left Page

### Fri. May 12, 1905

**Weather:** Pleasant  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant — Jim did not return till before dinner after a strenuous day with Social Conditions Class under guidance of Prof. W.m Daily of Yale. — To studio after 10 + printed two or three pfs. from Chapel near Savanna. Had to do considerable work on the plate dry pointing over white spots made by lumps of gum in the powder. Home to lunch + back — Afternoon I ground several colors in burned oil — blue, red, yellow + brown + tried to do some printing in color — but failed — Before going to studio after lunch I took camera + went to 50th & 5th Park Ave. to get a snap shot of two + the great excavation being made by the Central R.R. — but the watchman would not allow it — they have strict orders not to allow photographs to be taken — I got one shot from the bridge over the tracks at 52d St. Home at 6 — dinner. Evef. I wrote a long letter to Ralph — Fred Cornu dined with us + there was singing of course.

## Right Page

### Sat. May 13, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Heavy rain last night — dull day with chilly east wind — a dull headache, matching the day made me nearly useless. — Jim went with me to "le Marquis", a boarding hotel in 31st St. E. of 5th Ave. & we looked at the sixties that I saw some days ago + had another talk with Doug. Then to Studio + fussed about there trying to reduce some of the disorder till 1 o'clk. Then home to lunch + back to studio at 2.30 — made up some aqua-tint solution of Resin + spread some experimental grounds that seemed to be successful — acid will be the final test — Home at 5.30 + rested till dinner at 6.30 —

Munger, a son of Rev. Munger of New Haven, a classmate of Jim's, dined with us. Evef. I went to the Century + had a very interesting acct. from Jr. Carrera of the growth of the Beaux Arts Architects educational work in N.Y., + of its federation with Columbia University. It seemed to me that the N.A. of D. might follow the same lines with advantage in its union of forces with Columbia. — Then with Nicole, we had a long talk with Beckwith who is exceedingly earnest in his opposition to the Columbia scheme. Much of his reasoning is very disturbing to me + I would gladly, if possible, be relieved of the responsibility of a vote in the matter. — Home before 11.

The one snap shot I took yesterday proved to be a complete failure.
