## Left Page

### Sat. Dec. 16, 1905

**Weather:** Dull, cold & raw  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, cold & raw - afternoon, something wet fell - just enough to wet the pavement. I am still so sore that it is difficult for me to get about or do any thing — After bkft. I wrote a letter to Ralph & posted it — then I went to Locke's to learn how his work for me is progressing — then to Studio — made pkg. of Palmer's small head of "Imogen" & took it to the Touraine — I had lunch at 2 o'clk — The afternoon was spent indoors doing I don't know what. Dinner with Jim at 7 — Eveth, Chark & Elizabeth T. came, he brought a pkg. of my letters to Kate, found in one of her four trunks that have recently arrived from Germany I have been unpacked by the Throops — He had also a lot of Norwegian postal picture cards from Kate's collection.

## Right Page

### Sun. Dec. 17, 1905

**Weather:** A bright, cold day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright, cold day — It was after 9 when I had my bkfst alone — Jim did not get up till near noon — he had no bkfst, but went to George's & had his dinner there. There is but little diminution in stiffness & pain in my arms & chest muscles. It was after 11 before I went out — I walked on Park Ave. to 34th St. & then up to & around Grand Central Station — by Lex. Ave. to Park Ave. & up to 54th St. inspecting the very interesting engineering work underway by the R.R — then back to Touraine, a walk of more than two miles — Instead of a regular lunch I took a dose of Castor oil hoping to relieve some of my discomforts in stomach & bowels. I did not go out during the afternoon — about 6 o'clk. I made some junket & had a roll — Jim dined alone & in the eve't went up town to call on a girl who invited him over the telephone yesterday — he was not sure of her name or address.
