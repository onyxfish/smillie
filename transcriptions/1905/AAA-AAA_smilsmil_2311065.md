## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 13, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day — last night was not as comfortable a one for me as the night before — my sleep was more disturbed with more dis comfort about the urethra \+ in passing urine — an unusual amt. of pus was in what I passed \+ Dr. Brown sent it to the laboratory — I started the day at 6.30 — It was a dismal day for me I am getting hopeless. this waiting day after day for Kidney \+ bladder trouble to "clear up" as Dr. Tuthill says — Geo. came at about 11 to tell me that the man who had called at the house with a view to buying it was my old neighbor of 112 \- Martin — \+ then I made up my mind that I did not want to do business with him — Jessie came in mf. \+ evf. — Beef steak at 1.30. After that came the man from Dontia ch's about sale of $110 to Martin I told him I did not care to have dealings with Martin for reasons — Then he told me he had another man \+ he cd. get a report from him — The afternoon at last dragged thro' — Dr. Tuthill \+ Brown came — Dr. T. said symptoms were encouraging \+ I must be patient — as I understand him the pus that is in my urine comes down from my right Kidney, altho all sensitiveness in that region is gone — I am very anxious \+ sometimes wonder if I will ever get out of this. Raw oysters \+ then, Jim, who is faithful \+ kind. My eyes being very tired from so much reading, my best resource, I turned in for the night early.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 14, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, lots of sunshine \+ fresh air in the room all day — It was started as usual at 6 o'clk — Dr. Mason (the one who wrote my history on entering) took the place of Dr. Brown — Jessie made her pleasant visits as usual \+ Katharine came in the mf. bringing more books \+ letters from Frau Schomberger \+ Dr. Hilgendorff giving very full details of Kate's last hours — She had three attacks of indigestion with excessive pain \+ much distension from gas — The first, early in the evf. — The second, about midnight; the third \+ last about 9 am. The doctor got to her at 10 \+ remained till the end at noon — Nothing is written that even suggests that she had any thought of dying — we broke that she was spared the agony of that — the thought that she was dying, as from those among strangers — The day passed heavily with its usual routine — nurses, orderlies, doctors, food, medicine, bath — I had a conversation of more than usual length with Dr. Tuthill but did not learn just what he thinks my condition is — I gather that it is an inflammation of a portion of my right Kidney \+ that from it pus is poured down to the bladder with occasional clogging — I have tried to get them to say that I may recover entirely, but I get only guarded answers leaving the impression that I may get about but will never again be sound, but subject to returns, more or less severe, Resulting from cold — or to a chronic, sub acute inflammation — I hope that my inference is incorrect — I read till my eyes refused to serve me further — Jim, faithful boy, came in the evf. \+ brought me some peaches — I turned in for the night about 10.
Jessie brought me more flowers, carnations (yesterday; lillis of the valley to-day.
