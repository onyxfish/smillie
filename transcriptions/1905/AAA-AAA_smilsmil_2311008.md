## Left Page

### Wed. June 21, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Raw m'f., overcast, heavy rain in the middle of day — cool but humid night. I spent large part of m'f. in attic clearing out bookcase there + gathering maps + guide books together. I did not get out of the house till rather late in the afternoon — Then took some fragile things to studio — then to bakery, 4th ave. \+ 27th S. Jim went to Newark to lunch with a friend + ret? before dinner. I wrote part of a letter to Mary — + got to bed pretty well tired out.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 22, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim returned to New-Haven.

Another excessively hot + humid morning just about 3 a very heavy thunder storm with floods of rain which passed over in an hour more leaving the air a little cooler but still very humid — Before b'f't. at 8 — I wrote to Celia Nathalie Chadwick + sent a photo of her mother, Celia Halcott, Taken in 1861. Also a note to Katie Elkin — I worked about generally till near 11 — then to studio + worked there till nearly — arranging files of Ill. Lond. News, + Harper + Atlantic Mag'z. Am surprised to find the Harper Mag'z files from 1856 to 1869 are very much broken — several of the years being absent altogether — from this I feel quite sure that one of the old Montrose boxes of books must be lost or somewhere out of sight. The discovery disturbs me very much — Home + lunch at 1 — before I had finished, Selleck with two men + horse + wagon came + I had to work in getting away a load of books to studio where I stacked them in a closet — just as we finished the coming storm was so threatening that I sent S. + men away + hurried home before it broke — I finished letter to Mary + after 4 took it to Station 4 — then to Borrmann's + bought more photo. material — then home. Dinner at 7 — Before 8 Jim + I went to Grand Central Station — he took 8.08 train for New Haven — divided even't between reading + work.
