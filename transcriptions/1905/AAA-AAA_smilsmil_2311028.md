## Left Page

### Mon. July 31, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The worst storm day so far this season. The rain was almost continuous during the night with northerly winds increasing in violence during the day, driving the rain in sheets before it. A lull came at about 7 P.M. I did not go away from the house during the day but the boys, Katharine & Elizabeth rigged up in waterproofs & went out in the afternoon to see the falls & Roaring River. I tried to write a letter to Jeanette & Roger but gave it up after some effort. The day passed with nothing accomplished. Miss Macay called in the afternoon — Eve'g I did a little reading.

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 1, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A cloudy day — quite uncertain in character, but on the whole, pleasant rather than otherwise. As usual I spent some time in the garden after bkft. — then kindled fire in my room & I wrote to Jeanette & Roger & others while Ralph, in the easy chair, studied up on Greek history. Dinner — Afternoon, Jim, Ralph & I had a stroll in the woods, going to Roaring Brook fall & a short way up the Chafskee Pond Road. Home — supper at 6.30 — Maghee came & spent the eve'g on piazza with the girls.
