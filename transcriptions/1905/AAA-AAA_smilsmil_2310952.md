## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 27, 1905

**Weather:** Cloudy at times & then clear - cold & rather windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Got to studio about 10 - some time putting damps photos, between blotters - then sit palette & worked on 12×18 till 12.30 on Park Ave. till 1 - then lunch & to studio again soon after 2 - painted till 3.30 - then home - Mary & the girls having a" Reception" but I got only the sound of it - at it went across the street to Mr. Sidney Smith's & heard Rev? T. C. Williams' critical address, with readings, from Matthew Arnold. It was very interesting to me & seemed to be very clever. After the reading I walked for awhile - then home & had a short rest before dinner at Mary the girls completely worn out by their function - talking incessently for nearly three hours was a strane too strenuous. Charl sent them a request to visit him & see his new lantern lens work - Katharine was able to go & I accompanied her - After showing a few slides his 50 candle power electric light blew out & that finished the show. Home soon after 10 -

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 28, 1905

**Weather:** Snowing when I went to studio at 10  **Temp:** [illegible]

at 10 o'clk. The sun was shining brightly from a cloudless sky - later, again it clouded over - I painted on 12×18 till 12.30 - then to 2° Nat. Bank - walked up 5° Ave. - stopped at Nat. Art Club - saw Cha°. DeKay - he was not over civil till I told him who I was - then he explained that he was short sighted - He gave me a card & I went up & saw the beeswax Parian marble Aphrodite by Praxiteles (?) now the object of much attention because of claims made for it - It seems to me to be an example of conventionalized classic art so extreme as to be beyond the range of my sympathies or understanding - Home to lunch at 1.30 - To studio at 2.30 - painted for an hour or more - then began to write a letter to Kate - At 5 had a short walk - dinner at 6.30 - Eve°. finished my letter to Kate & posted it.

Mary has been in bed all day - seems to be tired out - complains of a swimming head - has had such attacks before.
