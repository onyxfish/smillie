## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 15, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day — more sunshine \+ open window Day began at 6 - a tumbler of hot water - then medicine - Then broth \+ then a peach that Jim brought me - After that, the regular routine day - something to eat or drink every two hours my bath, etc, etc - It was not till the afternoon that my first visitor, Jessie, came Dr. Mason came in the m't, but there was no afternoon call - Geo \+ Nellie came after 5 \+ Charl came in yet later \+ made a pleasant call, leaving at 6.30 - Then I had my dinner - a po or little chop - some potatoes \+ bread. About 9 Jim came \+ stayed for an hour.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 16, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More sunshine \+ more open window - the night past was not one of ideal rest \+ comfort \+ as the day opened I felt heavy, lethargic - Miss Mosely \+ Collins made their appearance soon after 6 \+ I went thro' the early m't exercises \+ had some broth - As the m't wore on I became satisfied that I was labouring under depression because of too much eating \+ so I told Dr. Brown when he came but he advised me not to go to the other extreme but to eat as freely as I could without discomfort. I declined some of the nourishment offered but at dinner had a bit of beefsteak - Dr. Tuttell came earlier than usual \+ urged patience \+ hopefulness. But from everything almost that he says I draw an inference that his Kidney Trouble has come to stay - I may get up \+ about but always handicapped by it. Chas. Collins came in before dark \+ soon after Mary Ireland came - They made a very pleasant call, Collins leaving some time after Mary. Oysters at 6\. th with albumen lemonade. Miss Stebbins ret\[urne\]d to duty to-day. Dr. Edw'd. Cook came in at about 9 \+ I had an inquisitive Talk with him about Kidney disorders - the inference I drew from his answers to my questions giving me some comfort.
