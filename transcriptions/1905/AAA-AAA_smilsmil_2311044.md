## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 1, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Day opened clouded \+ misty - light sprinklings of rain fell - at noon it cleared beautifully - afternoon, clouded again, looking showery. Otis \+ Bashaw worked on wall at gate, finishing it, I then had them begin cutting into the bank at the turn of Road at top of hill making the road way wide enough for two vehicles to pass - This soil was taken down to the gate \+ thrown back of the wall, making a sort of terrace. Earl was kept busy with chores \+ repairs on road - I was about with the men much of the day. Mf. I wrote to Parke - also to Nefgen Sw. and sent to them, as requested, all that remained (more than half) of my gross of Carbon Velox. I went to the Post Office at noon. Eve'g. The boys went to a "Camp Fire" at the Mackay's — I read a little.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 2, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Miss Rosa \+ Brother arr?

Beautiful mf. - clouded p.m. - Rain after 7 p.m. After bkf. I spent some time in garden gathering corn, parsnips, carrots etc. - Men with men - Otis \+ Bashaw worked all the mf on Road, cutting the bank \+ turn \+ carrying the earth to the wall near gate - Moody helping - Dinner after 1 o'clk. after Ralph \+ I had been to P.O. Bashaw worked on Tennis Court till I went to him after dinner - Then, under my direction, he set a couple of fine young spruce trees north of the terrace at N. end of house. Miss Rosa \+ her bro. arr'd. from Placid about the middle of the afternoon. Supper at about 7 - Evef. in general confab. Jim \+ Ralph went over to the Hardenburg's -

Miss Rose (pronounced Rosa) is an acquaintance of the Thinks made on the voyage home last Nov. She is of German parentage - 5'. 10" tall - 23 years old \+ has an unusually fine Contralto voice which she uses with much taste - She has a large Repertoire - mostly german - Her bro. is five years older - civil engineer - connected with Electrical power works at Niagara Falls where the family lives
