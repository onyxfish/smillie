## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 28, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

I was able to get up at 3 p.m. The weather was fine & cold \+ I feel much relieved. I read newspapers etc. thro the m[orning]. After I got up \+ dressed I went to the parlor where I found comfort in a pleasant fire. I went down to dinner. Evey. Charl came in — just arr. from Boston. It was after 11 before I got to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 29, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day. I was up \+ had bkf. about 9.30 — A loafing m[orning]. I worked awhile numbering \+ registering European snap-shots. \+ at about 1 went out \+ had an airing on the sunny side of Park Ave. In spite of sunshine the air was raw. I went in to Charl's for a few minutes, then home to dinner at 2. After that I completed the registration of films. Then I had a nap on my bed. Then wrote short notes to the boys — sent $125. To Jim \+ $20 to Ralph. also, wrote notes to Emma J. \+ to Mr. Hugh Cole. Then, at 8 o'clk. I went to 147 E. 38 St. \+ had a very pleasant call on Ella Beach who, with her sister Violet, is established there for the winter keeping house. Home soon after 10 — Charl there — he had a lot of negatives — 4×5 Reductions, for lantern slides from larger photos. \+ I was detained so that it was near 12 before I got to bed
