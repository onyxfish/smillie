## Left Page

### Mon. June 5, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mary \+ K — went to the Ark.

Very warm \+ muggy — up at 6 — bkfs — at 7 — things seemed to run smoothly till at Grand Cent. Station K told me that she did not have the tickets for parlor car seats — did not know where she had left them — ticket man could not help her. She wl have to pay again for her seats \+ when the missing tickets were found \+ forwarded to agent in Chicago the money wd be refunded — She gave me key to her desk — tickets may be there — I got servants in their seats — \+ Mary \+ K. in theirs — then home — found tickets in K's desk — back to station \+ was told to send them on to K — which I did — telegraphed to Kellow, went to Pease \+ Sleiman — not in — then to Chars \+ then home — Ellen Thompson cleaning up — then to studio — cut some mounts for etching p.p. — then began to clear ups with a view to leaving for the summer — went to Millers \+ to Douglas's \+ to Manhattan Storage — Home to lunch at 1 — Then a nap \+ to studio at 2 30 \+ worked there till 5 — then home \+ closed every thing — to Charl's after 6 — dinner at 7 — eve'g chatting till af hr 10 — then to my room \+ to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 6, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright sunshine in the m'g. but presently the sky was filmed with clouds that thickened — then light, sprinkling showers, then heavy down pour till about 6 and a fall of 25° in temp. I went to 110 at about 10 \+ waited for Nellie \+ then for Hugh Douglas — Both came — I went to Pease \+ Ellemas — neither in — then to bank — then to studio I got some supplies \+ about 1 o'clk. fixed a lunch for myself — \+ during the rest of the afternoon I pottered at the studio getting things cleared ups for the summer — to 110 at 6 — changed clothing — then to Charl's — Dinner at 7 — at 8 Geo. came a remarkable story. pub'd in the Sun \+ the American this m'g about the young wife of Geo. Sheldon Smillie, artist, at Central City. Col., carried over a cliff, 200 feet high, into river, nearly drowned — Geo. was interviewed by reporters desiring to know if he had any connection with the affair — He denied it. He has just rec'd letters showing that many of the statements of the young woman concerning herself \+ her family are untruthful — Charl showed us some new slides — I read awhile before going to bed at 11.
