## Left Page

### Fri. March 31, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralf came home from Hackley.
Charl ret'd from Florida.

A beautiful day — clear, breezy + delightfully fresh — to studio at usual time — on digging into an old pot of printing ink I found more than enough in good condition for use. So, instead of going to Voigt for my pot of ink I cleaned up my press — wet down paper + before noon had taken my first print from Noreker book plate — it is generally overbitter, much to my regret, as it fails to present the scheme of effect that I had hoped to get — I wanted the old house to retire + be upon a plane behind the foreground objects — it does not + the foreground is needlessly heavy + dark — Home to lunch — Ralph came in soon after 2 o'clk — down till the 11th on his Easter holiday. To studio again + printed till near 5 o'clk + doing some work on the plate between proofs — then home very tired + had a rest before dinner — Then came an invitation to all to come to Charl's house — he having just got home from his Florida trip — at 8 o'clk all, except Bessie, went, she being confined to her room —. C— appears to be much improved by his trip — Home at 10.30 —

## Right Page

### Sat. April 1, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beautiful day — Got up with a beast of a headache against which I fought all day — After Vkp. I wrote a short note to Jim + sent him a check — then to studio + resumed printing from R— book plate — I should have done some burnishing on it or work of some kind to reduce color — but in my headachy condition I did not realize what I might, could or should have done — I used up my wet paper, having taken nine or ten p's. altogether — Then washed up + went home — Mr. (Rev.) Fred Young there to lunch — ate nothing — at about 2 o'clk — went to bank then by Mad. ave. cars rode to Met. Museum + saw the work of some of the students of Am. Academy at Rome — Then walked down 5th Ave. to the old Devoe library + saw [illegible] + Morgan's collection of old Swedish mezzatints — They disappointed me — They are of course Rare prints + in that way valuable but they are not fine specimens of the art as practiced in England at that time — then home + to bed till dinner — ate very lightly — my head still troubling me so that I did not feel equal to going to Monthly meeting at Century later in the evening — Charl came + was very enthusiastic in describing what Thompson, who has just ret'd from Europe, has accomplished with Malincroate, of Antwerp, + his Syndicate in interesting them in his Electric Canal towing mule — Charl is heavily interested + sent T—, who is the owner of the patents, over there to explain to workings etc.
