## Left Page

### Sat. April 8, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

'Gene Atwater visiting K.R.J.

Fine day — To studio at usual time there for a short time — Then went to Thinnel \+ Voigt's — Canal St — \+ got pot of ink — Then to Dr. Willard Parker's — 8 W. 9th St. \+ he took me in to his neighbor, Mr. H. J. Raymond, who wished to consult me as To value of a few old Am. prints she has — Talked for half or three quarters of an hour with the old lady (83 years) on a great variety of subjects \+ then went to Second Nat'l Bank — then to Standard Co. in W. 31st.: \+ selected a bath tub — then home to lunch — To studio at 2.30 \+ worked on Dresden plate till quite late Then walked to 57th S. \+ Park Ave. examining the work of excavation underway by Central R.R. — very cold — Home to dinner at 645 Fred. Young \+ 'Gene Atwater dined with us — 'Gene to stay over Sunday — Charl came in during the ev'g.

## Right Page

### Sun. April 9, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

went to Po'keepsie

Charming day — at 10 went to Charl's Together we went to R.R. Station — took 10.20 train \+ got To Po'K. about 12.15 Rode up Town on street car \+ got to Uncle W's, 39 Hooker Ave at 12.30 — all at home except Emma who was at church — came in soon after — I find Uncle W. much changed in almost every way — he now shows his age — 92 years — Aunt A. — less changed but very feeble — Aunt J. — while terribly crippled by her Rheumatism is wonderfully bright \+ active. I was much surprised to learn that since last fall a trouble has developed in one of her breasts that the doctor says is cancer — at times it gives her much pain — we had dinner at about 1 o'clk — Then general talk about food \+ nourishment there being much diversity of opinion on these subjects among the old folks. It was after 3 o'clk. when Charl \+ I bid them good bye (Harry Smillie had dined with us) \+ we started out for a week about town — went to top of "big hill" on Academy St. Then thro' Garfield Place — Main St. — Mill St. — Garden — Mansion — Washington — Lafayette \+ Vassar down to R.R. Station to take 4.50 train — learned then that there was no 4.50 on Sunday — next Train 6.45 — We went to River, loafed about \+ then took Trolley car out to Vassar College — grounds closed on Sunday — Rel[?] to Station at 6.34 — took 6.45 Train — had our dinner aboard \+ got to N.Y. at about 9.
