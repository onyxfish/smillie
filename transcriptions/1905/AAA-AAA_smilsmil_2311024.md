## Left Page

### Sun. July 23, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day but a storm is evidently brewing — Had a good scrub \+ b&p. at 9.30. The m'f. passed in loafing about, most of the time with Ralph — dinner at 1 — Immediately after that R. \+ I went down to the Post Office at Winch's — \+ got mail — we loafed on our way back \+ after 4 o'clk. I had a nap. Supper at 6.30. A note came from Scott Brown, enclosing postal cards from freight agent at West Port (B.L. Burritt) addressed to J. D. Smith, dating back to early June being bill for freight — This is probably the missing China \+ Kitchen utensils bought of Jno. Wanamaker last May — R. \+ I went to Scott Brown's after supper — Eve'f. reading paper \+ to bed late.

This m'f. I had a pleasant little wood fire in my bed room —

## Right Page

### Mon. July 24, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rain last night, cloudy m'f., clearing before noon — heavy thunderstorm about 2 o'clk — Then clearing weather again — Ther. did not get to 70° in my room all day — Before 10 o'clk. went to Scott Brown's \+ called up Burritt at West Port — he told me that box \+ barrel were plainly directed J. D. Smith — met Rob'. Neilson \+ we had a characteristic chat together — he introduced a W. Merritt — Then home \+ wrote to Wanamaker — also to Murphy, room clerk at New Kenmore, Albany — Dinner — after the storm passed I went to Maghee's garden with Moody \+ got 55 Cos plants — then back \+ Moody transplanted them into cold frames — at 5 o'clk I lay down \+ had a nap. Supper at 6.30 — Eve'f. a pleasant little fire on hearth in main room — Mr. Maghee came \+ invited girls \+ Ralph to ride over to East Hill with them (the M's) to-morrow —
