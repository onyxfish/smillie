## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 15, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dismal weather again \- so I am told \- The Parkes, with Ralph, drove out to the lower lake, but it was too cold \+ windy to go out on the lake \- so they ret. in time for dinner \- They went with Mary to Windy brow in the afternoon. Dr. Garvin came \+ found that my temp. still held at about 103°. He thinks that among other joys I have a mild form of Cystitis with some enlargement of the Prostate \- so they purely I am an old man now \- my head still aches wickedly \+ my muscles are very sore \- my stomach takes no interest in nourishment of any kind. To-night temp. lowered \+ I had some rest.

## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 16, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

the Parkes left, going north.

A fairly pleasant day in spite of showers. I was not able to be up at all \- Temp. fell to 100⁴⁄₅°. The Parkes came up \+ bid me good bye as I lay in bed; the only time I have seen them during their visit here \- They left to go to Montreal \+ Quebec \- To-day I had much less pain \+ got through with comparative comfort.
