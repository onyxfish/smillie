## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 31, 1905

**Weather:** A fine day — somewhat cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — somewhat cloudy — with one delay \+ another it was almost noon before I had my bath \+ got dressed \+ then went to the roof where I strayed till I was invited to come down \+ take my dinner — after that more aimless loafing — some writing \+ some reading — Mary \+ Bessie made a call about 3 ish \+ I took them up on the roof for awhile. Then down \+ waited for D. Tuthill — I had a very pleasant call from Sam. Colman — D. T. did not come. At about 8.30 I went to the Nurse's Hallowe'en party in the large hall of the Nightengale nurses home — went down to basement, along a corridor, then by subway under 71st. to the Hall — met Dr. Fisher \+ his wife, Sub, \+ introduced myself — The sight on entering the Hall was amusing — there are 150 nurses here — most of them were in the hall cavorting about in all sorts of costumes \+ wearing masks about 10 o'clk. masks were removed but many were still on when I left. There were two or three remarkably fine looking women among them — one, tall \+ very well formed, wore a fencing costume of black — with white over the breast with red heart — she was stately in figure, handsome in face \+ had fine movement — there were many very small ones, often in pairs, to make confusion, I think, two short cowboys — babies in any number — as an offset to the stately fencer, there was a very slender girl of medium height beautifully in her slight form, well shown by close fitting bodice bare arms, short skirt \+ shapely legs in black stockings — she was as agile as a cat — I watched the ongoings till 10 o'clk— then I returned to my room \+ put myself to bed.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 1, 1905

**Weather:** Foggy m't. clearing to sunshine — strong west south wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

Foggy m't. clearing to sunshine — strong west south wind — Before the m't. routine was over — doctor's visit, bath \+ nap \+ then dressing it was almost noon — then I went up on the roof where I found it too breezy for comfort — Down in time for dinner — after dinner Jessie came in \+ I went up on the roof with her for a few minutes — on coming down I met Dr. Tuthill \+ Brown \+ they went with me to my room \+ I partially stripped \+ Dr. T. made examination \+ then said that I may leave to-morrow — after that I finished the reading of that fool book Peter Stiling — \+ wrote several notes — Supper at 6.30 — Evg. I went over to Jessie's room — found D. Brown there reading aloud some Kippling — then he read a Ghoulish poem by Oscar Wilde — then Eddie Cook came in \+ we had a pleasant social time till after 10 — then I returned to my room \+ got to bed.
