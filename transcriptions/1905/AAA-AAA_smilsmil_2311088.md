## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 30, 1905

**Weather:** clear but disagreeably cold & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Thanksgiving Day \-
Clear but disagreeably cold & windy. We had bkfst. late & at about 11 o'clk Ralph & I went to the studio — He got a number of things from his trunk & I fussed about curtains, hangings blankets & things that I am getting into trunks to be taken to the Touraine apartment to be made use of there. At about 2 o'clk. Jim, Ralph & I had lunch — the boys went off together — I did some writing, then had a nap & at 6 o'clk. we went to Charles — Mary & her girls were there — At 6.30 we sat down to dinner — a very fine affair — I partook very sparingly & we got through before 9 o'clk — then Char. read to us from his diary describing his trip from Hamburg — along the coast of Norway to the North Cape & across to Spitzbergen & then back to Hamburg. Very interesting — we broke up at 10.30 Ralph ret. to New Haven on 11 o'clk. train.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 1, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful winter day — Jim & I had bkfst. at 8 — After that I was busy over my accts. till 11 o'clk — then I went to Mad. Ave. & 25. S. & bfst. newspaper man — then down Fifth Ave. to 2d. Nat'l. Bank & transacted first of the month business there — then to Bayerdorfer's & paid bill — then to Mr. Dahn's — then to studio — paid Ryan's bill — then to Pease & Elliman's but did not see any one — then to Century & had lunch — After that I went to a harness maker's in 6th Ave. & 39th St. & got webbing for a belt — then to rooms — read awhile & about 4 o'clk. feeling tired I had a nap — then newspaper till Jim came in — then the Washerwoman — We had dinner at 7 — After that Jim went off with David Harris — At 9 o'clk I went to Charles — there for an hour or more, then to my rooms & to bed.
