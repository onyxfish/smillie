## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 12, 1905

**Weather:** Cloudy m[illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m[illegible] — then hot & misty but drier than yesterday — Wet. about 7. Several thunder storms, with deluges, settling at last in steady rain. my Rheumatism gave me less trouble than it did yesterday — M[illegible]. I did some work on panels on my bed room mantel piece — wrote a note to Parke — went to Scott Brown & interviewed him — then to P.O. & back to dinner — afternoon some more work on mantel piece — went to Mr. Maghee's & borrowed her pyrographic outfit; Returned & did some experimental work. The storms that came at supper time continued so heavily that we could not return to the house — so, the dining table was cleared \+ Ring pong was played for more than an hour — then, between showers we got from Annex to house — I read awhile in Asia \& Europe.

Tried the experiment of taking a photo. in my bedroom this m[illegible], but it was a failure.
(Tis brought four loads of rotted stone for road to-day.

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 13, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Beautifully clear & cool day — cold night, brilliant moonlight. Bkf. before 9 — after that I framed letter to W.G. Keilson with reference to possible purchase of strip of land along my southern line — I went to Scott Browns — then home — then, with Mr. M's pyrographic outfit I burned one of the panels for my Mantel Piece — then, with Ralph, went to P.O. Got medicines from Freese, advised by Yale — my rheumatism is still better to-day. Dinner at 1.30 — I pyroed another panel \+ then went out with the boys & prowled over Windy brow & thro' the woods. Supper at 7 — Wet. pacing piazza — the sisters McKay came in while the young people were all out — Mary & I received them. I built a fire on hearth.
