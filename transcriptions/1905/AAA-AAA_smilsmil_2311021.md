## Left Page

### Mon. July 17, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An exceedingly hot day - Ther. on loggia in front of house getting to 89°  in my bedroom at 10 p.m. at 84°  Up in time for bkfs. at 8 - after that I went with W. P. \+ Helen down to W. P.'s office, had my hair cut - did some shopping - then Parke introduced me to a friend of his who took me back home in his automobile - I read till dinner after 1 o'clk - Later in the afternoon I went with Helen, in trolley car, to her father's office \+ parted - in carriage Parke \+ I went to D. T. H. \+ D. L. \+ W. offices \+ I got those tables - then we rode home - one of the horses out of sorts - walked - went over bye roads - no protection from sun - I thought I would be sunstruck - back at 5 - a little nap before supper - Eve'g Helen, Greer \+ Ralph went to

Mr. \+ Mrs. P. \+ I spent the eve'g in a quiet chat on the loggia.

## Right Page

### Tues. July 18, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Left Scranton - to So. Montrose.

A very hot day, but much drier - Ther. over 90° - Humidity 45° - pleasant breeze - Up at 6.30 - packed trunk - bkfs. at a little after 7 - Mr. \+ Mrs. P. went to Station - Mr. P. to go to N.Y. city on 8 o'clk train - Helen, Ralph \+ I rode down town at 8.30 - got W. P. \+ he, Ralph \+ I took 9 o'clk train - R. \+ I for Montrose - P. for Halstead - we changed at Alford \+ got to Montrose about 11 - Telephoned at once to Mrs. Ballantine - she replied that she was waiting for us - we went to Cox's livery \+ stable but it was near 12 before we got started \+ about 1 when we got to the Ballantine house - an immense house, partially complete, planned upon a great scale \+ sumptuously furnished - we soon had lunch - after that we wandered \+ loafed about - the breeze being delightful but any exertion a discomfort - after dinner we went to the gardens \+ tree nurseries \+ then sat for an hour on the great south porch in moonlight - Bessie has two boys - one about two years, the other, two months old - mother \+ boys seem to be splendidly well -
