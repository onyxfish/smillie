## Left Page

### Thur. Nov. 2, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

left the Hospital —

A brilliant, inspiriting day. Me unt went as usual. The nurse calls, Dr. Brown's call — my bath by Ros — a nap — then dressing \+ a little before noon, on the roof. There I had a breezing for about half an hour — then down \+ packed my belongings — soon after 1 I had my dinner — \+ after that I said my "good byes" \+ then was escorted to the front door \+ there was given my freedom formally. I went out without elation — I am not cured — judging from local sensations \+ appearance of urine I am no better than when I went to the hospital — in general condition I am much better. but I look forward to possibilities in connection with kidney \+ ureter trouble with forebodings \+ little hope — at times I feel assured that I cannot be well again — I went to 3 Ave. \+ took elevated to 18 S. \+ there bought a glass urinal \+ bottle of cascara — then, car to 38 S — walked past 110, to The Touraine — there I found Jc. \+ Nellie — later, Mary \+ Katharine came \+ after 5\. Charl came \+ stayed till after 6 — it was quite a reception — the rooms look bare, the book shelves being empty \+ the drapings, doors \+ windows, without curtains — no doubt they may be made very cosy \+ comfortable — Chas. Collins, faithful friend, also came in — It was not far from 7 where Jim \+ I went down to the Restaurant — I went in in fear \+ trembling dreading the ordeal of studying a menu card — I got as little comfort as I had expected — there was little that was simple \+ nothing that seemed to me to be moderate in price — on a list of a hundred or more items I did not find a dozen that I could or would eat. our dinner was Roast beef (very good) potatoes, miserable little tasteless french [illegible] \+ ice cream. We got rid of the evening somehow \+ to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Nov. 3, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Some clouds — less sunshine — Rain at night. I got up with Jim \+ we had [illegible] together at about 8 o'clk. The cascara that I took last night made trouble for me. Later in the morning I went out for a short saunter — went down 5 Ave. to 34 S. I find a world of difference between walking on the Presby. Hospital roof — in the full blaze of sunshine, no dust — no noise — pure air — \+ walking in the streets where it is all commotion, noise \+ dirt. The sunshine only on one side of the Ave. \+ not at all in the side streets — I had a light lunch at the Touraine \+ in the afternoon struggled around to the Studio — my stomach is already much out of order \+ my legs are weaker — I saw Jc. for awhile \+ went into my studio where the dirt \+ confusion is appalling — I returned to T— in a light sprinkle — Later, in the eve it rained quite hard — Jim \+ I had dinner at about 6.30 — I ate too much — how hard it is to keep within bounds — \+ I had a bottle of White Rock water with dinner — The result being that I passed a miserable eve.
