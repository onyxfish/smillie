## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 25, 1905

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — To studio at about 10
\+ to work painting on 12×18" — Chapel near
dugane" — I worked till near 12 + then I
felt so much out of sorts that I stopped
Before I went home to lunch I had a
very little walk on Park Ave. Then lunch —
feeling no better I lay down on my bed for
a half hour or so — Then out + walked on
the sunny side of Park Ave. for another
half hour or more — Then, at about 4 o'clk,
I went to studio, painted a little + then
cleaned up — home + had a half hour
rest before dinner — at 8 I went to Cha-
\+ together we went to Century + heard
a extremely interesting lecture; illustrated
by Dr. Wilfred Grenfell — a man who may be
about 40 — described as surgeon, master mariner
author + athlete — graduate of Oxford — studied
surgery + then took up missionary work among
the North Sea fishermen — afterwards extended
his work to Labrador + Greenland. He is Supt.
of the Royal Nat'l Mission to Deepsea fishermen.
He is evidently a man wholly + enthusiastically
devoted to his mission — He spoke rapidly + con-
tinuously for one hour + forty min — but no one was
tired — every one was hearty in applause — He had
a great lot of photo. lantern slides, some of them
admirable — he had many good stories + a dry
humor that was very telling.

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 26, 1905

**Weather:** A very fine day, bright, sunny + comfortably cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day, bright, sunny + comfortably
cold. Bkfst. at 9.30 after a good rub down +
bath — After 11 I went out + prowled about
on the sunny + protected side of streets. I went
to studio for a few minutes + after 1 to Char's.
Drome to dinner at 2 — After that read awhile —
then had a short nap — then out + promenaded
on Park Ave. as long as sunshine reached the
pavement, then home, closed in my bed-room
window at about 5 — lighted gas + commenced
relox printing which I continued till after
9 p.m — Eve'g. Charl came in — after
9.30 I loafed. Till I went to bed at 11.
