## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 25, 1905

**Weather:** Cold, cloudy, very windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, cloudy, very windy day \- at times the wind from the N.W. blew so hard agt my big studio-window end that I was uneasy lest the window should come in or the roof be lifted. When, later in the day The Giant was uncovered with a dusting of snow. I spent much of the m'f in my room writing a long letter to W.G. Neilson \- to Jim \+ to several N.Y. Real Estate agents. It was not Till 12.30 before Mood went to P.O. Dinner about 1 \- After that I tried to get a little warmth into the studio by making a fire in the Franklin. I got nothing but smoke \- Then I got Bashaw \+ Moody \+ they got the big base-burning stove from the attic \+ after a power of trouble got it set up \+ then I got a big coal fire in it \+ made a little start for backing \- felt much out of sorts \+ discouraged towards eve'g. ate a very light supper \- I eat very little now, having trouble with stomach \+ bowels as well as bladder \+ ureter. I spent the eve'g in easy chair before the fire.

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 26, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

went back to bed again

A brilliant, cold m'f. with gusty winds from the N.W. I felt little improvement in condition over last eve'g. I got up at 7 o'clk \+ dragged about a peculiar pain, difficult to locate, growing in my right side \- I made up developing solution \+ developed a couple of glass negatives \- then dragged helplessly about till dinner, of which I could eat none, \+ then I went to bed \+ sent for Garvin, determined to take no further risks \- he came, examined me carefully \+ said he thought that part of the pain might be neuralgic \- but it was plain to be seen that he was thinking of my right Kidney. I had considerable fever \+ he said that I must not get out of bed so long as a trace of it Remained \- Then he ordered calomel \+ a Seidlitz powder in the m'f. I had a wretched, fevered night.
