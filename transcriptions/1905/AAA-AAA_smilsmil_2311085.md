## Left Page

### Fri. Nov. 24, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day — soon after bkfst was surprised by receipt of letter from Clarence Bishop Smith — enclosing check of Strong & Cadwalader for $57016.91 bal. of pay for 110 E. 38th St. — the $16.91 being for fire ins. policies having some time yet to run — then were three checks — one $32016.91 for me — one, $20000 for Jim — one of $5000 for Couel & J Ralph (R. had a 2d mtg on the house) — I went to Scribn & Jim endorsed his check to me — then to Geo. J. Chok's — cor. Bdwy & Brome & the endorsed Ralphs check as co-trustee — then to Second Nat'l. Bank \+ deposited the three to my Cr. Then I paid the bank $2378.79, loans with int. to date, leaving me $55688 — all that I had left out of the $60000 paid for the house. Then to the Touraine \+ had lunch — After that I was preparing to go to Presbyt'n Hospital when Josephine (Wills widow) came \+ she stayed for some time telling a doleful story — it was after 4 o'clk. when I went to hospital — saw Jessie \+ left with her a framed [s?] of my die "The old mill door", after Darley, engd in 1857 — to be given to Miss N. J. Crandell, the nurse who took such good care of me — Back to Touraine after 6 — Jim went off with one of his fellows \+ I dined alone \+ after that went to Charles \+ had a talk with him about money matters

## Right Page

### Sat. Nov. 25, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A clouded m'g but clearing delightfully before 11 o'clk. I commenced the day at 5 o'clk with a dose of Castor oil which demanded attention before 8 — I took my bkfst alone, feeling very seedy — after that I had a nap in my chair \+ feeling refreshed I went out — I rode down to Wall St. \+ went to Standard Trust, cor. Broad \+ Sy, Pl. \+ saw W. W. McLane \+ soon opened two accounts, one for self, $35000 — the other for Jim $20000 — 3% int. till we decide how we shall invest it. Then, by el. RR I rode to 6th Ave & 23d, St., went to bank — then to the Rattan Co. on Bdway — then to Scribners \+ gave Jim his pass \+ check books on Standard Trust — Met Carl Sprague there. Then to Touraine \+ had lunch. At 3 o'clk. feeling very sleepy I gave way to it \+ slept till 5 o'clk. Jim went to some sort of a party at the Beaches in Jersey City — about 7 o'clk. I went to the Century — met Maynard \+ asked him to share my dinner with me — M\+ O. Taylor sat on my right — the dinner was good \+ had a pleasant time — After, wrote a letter to Ralph — then began to feel extremely uncomfortable in region of stomach \+ bowels \+ at 9.30 went to Charl's — there an hour — then to Touraine — discomfort still continuing — got to bed as soon as I could.
