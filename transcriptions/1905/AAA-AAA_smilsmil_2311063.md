## Left Page

### Mon. Oct. 9, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More fine weather - the sunshine in my room \+ the pleasant air coming in at my window only aggravate me in my desire to be well \+ out. To-day I have felt a distinct improvement - my temp. is practically normal - The pain in my right side is gone, the discomfort in passing urine is very slight \+ the appearance of the urine is better. - Jessie made me two or three cheering visits - Otherwise the day passed with the usual monotony. - Jessie brought me the Masqueraders \+ I commenced the reading of it - Dr. Tuthill at his visit in the afternoon said some reassuring things, giving me to understand that they were building me up preparatory to a closer examination \+ possible treatment of the urinary organs. Dr. Edw. Cook came in for a short visit in the evening \+ Jim came as he left. I settled myself for sleep at about 10.

## Right Page

### Tues. Oct. 10, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Remarkable weather, beautifully clear \+ mild - my window open all day - last night there was an unpleasant episode for me - Sometime, perhaps at about midnight, there was a loud outcry, made by a child, in my corridor - I was wakened so suddenly \+ the hurrying nurses made so much confusion that for a time I was quite upset \+ for a while had hard work to keep myself from stampeding. - Miss Mosely \+ Collins came at about 6.30 \+ I commenced my day. - When Dr. Brown came I asked if I might get dressed \+ be taken to the Solarium on the roof, but he said I was better in bed. - Jessie \+ Edw. Cook came in \+ Roe spent some time in my room, napping in the rocking chair. I finished the Rich. Harding Davis stories \+ made much progress with the Masqueraders. Katharine [illegible] throop came at about 6 o'clk. \+ Jim came about 7. \+ Jessie came in later. When Dr. Tuthill \+ Brown came at about 8. T- made a rectal exploration \+ was much pleased to find so little enlargement of the Prostate - The sensitive spot in my side cannot be found. I furnished another specimen of blood for the laboratory \+ the young Dr. came in later to report that it was good. Miss Stevens, who has been caring for me during the afternoons, has rec'd news of the death of her mother - Miss Brockway has taken her place.
