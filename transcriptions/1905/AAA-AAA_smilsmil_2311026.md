## Left Page

### Thur. July 27, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very little sunshine — much cloud. several showers \+ one heavy thunder-storm — Temp., cool — Ther. did not get up to 70°. in my room all day \+ fell to 60°. last night. A day without incident — After bkfst. I was with Moody for a short time — then with the boys in the wood to west of house where they were cutting down dead poplars. Miss Hammersly, a stout and elderly lady called in the m'f \+ pleasantly invited us to a tea next Monday. Dinner about 1 — afternoon wrote to H.M. Smith from whom I had rec'd. a five ins. policy on $25.00 worth of books \+ pictures stored for three months in studio — for this $12.50 was chg'd. $50. per an. or 2 per cent — as I think the risk for the next three months will be nearly nothing I returned the policy — I wrote a note to Bessie Ballantine — a short rest before supper — Eve'g. with new paper —

The thunderstorm soon after 2 o'clk. was accompanied by a very heavy fall of rain that washed the roads considerably — There were several light showers before \+ after it.

## Right Page

### Fri. July 28, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day from m'f. to eve'g — the first one this week — much milder — under the influence of direct sunshine the Ther. in my room got up to 76°. After bkfst. I went about the place as the boys had gone off for a walk to "Giant's washbowl." I went alone thro' W.G. Nielsen's place — up the road past Openhy m's place \+ then over \+ out by Scott Brown's \+ then home at about 12 — Dinner at 1 — a little outbreak \+ a bad mannered remark from S.P.T. because the boys \+ self expressed unwillingness to go down the valley to Miss Hammersly's tea next Wed. Afternoon, more wandering about then wrote a letter to Smiley Cook — \+ a nap before supper — Eve'g. I read while the family entertained Mr. Maghee on the north porch —
