## Left Page

### Sat. April 22, 1905

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day. To studio at usual time — spent the day at work upon plates 5"×7" after an old oil painting that I name "Oct. afternoon" — I have laid an aquatint ground over an imperfectly bitten soft-ground etching — The aqua-tint I bit in to-day \+ after that did some scraping \+ burnishing upon it — Home to lunch \+ back — worked till after 6. Then home — dinner — eve\[nin\]g. After 8, went to Edw\[ar\]d\[s\]. Cooks \+ saw most of the family — Mr. Sprague Waltes \+ Louise — After 9 I went to Century to get some enlightenment concerning the proposed merger of N.A.\[of\] D. schools into the Columbia University system — I talked with Beckwith, Millet \+ others I was surprised to find that there w[as] be opposition to the Art school under control \+ management of N.A.\[of\] D. as a part of Columbia University — Beckwith disposed strongly saying he was since it w\[oul\]d be destructive to the school which w\[oul\]d in time become, in the University atmosphere, dry, dusty \+ lifeless — I think if the academy was in full control of the technical part of the school \+ such result followed it would be the fault of the Academy \+ not that of the merging —

## Right Page

### Sun. April 23, 1905

**Weather:** A brilliant day  **Temp:** rather cool

To Hackley with Jim.

A brilliant day — rather cool — up at 7 \+ had my scrub — bkfs\[t\]. at 9 \+ at 10.45 Jim \+ I took train for Tarrytown — we got to the Hackley school a little after 12 \+ found Ralph waiting at the gate for us. We went up to the school \+ were very pleasantly rec\[eive\]d. by acting Head Master Buck \+ by other masters \+ their wives. Jim was taken thro' the buildings — I was especially interested in the Scientific Bld\[g\]. yet unfinished going thro' every part of it \+ up into the attic. We had dinner about 1.30 \+ after that we wandered over the grounds \+ in time for chapel — 4.30 — Jim \+ I slipped out at 5 \+ walked part way to station before overtaken by trolley car — took 5.33 train to N.Y. \+ home at about 6.30 — Tea — Eve\[nin\]g. at home.
