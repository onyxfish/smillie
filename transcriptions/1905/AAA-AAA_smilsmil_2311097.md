## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 22, 1905

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant day — Jim, Ralph \+ I bkf't. together at 8 o'clk. R. \+ I amused ourselves till about 10 o'clk. Then he went to studio with me — Miss Abbatt made a long call — I made some progress in reducing the confusion and began stowing things away in the upper stories of middle closet — at 1 o'clk. R. \+ I. ret. to Touraine \+ had lunch. After that I read to him portions of Father's auto-biography \+ gave him some early family history — It was near four o'clk when I ret. to studio \+ 5 when I got back to Touraine — Then I had a rest \+ at 7 R. \+ I had dinner — Atg we went to Charli's \+ back to our quarters at 11 \+ to bed — Jim came in at 2.30 am. As I afterwards learned he went with a young fellow, a clerk at Scribner's, to some resort where they had chops, soup, beer \+ ale. This is depressing \+ causes me much concern.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 23, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty m'f., rainy \+ foggy — not unlike many winter days I've seen in London — Jim \+ I bkf't. together — he told very little of his experiences last night — Ralph remained in bed till noon. It was near 11 when I went to the bank \+ drew some money — then to Putnam's \+ got a couple of calendars — stopped at Butler's — said he had frame for 9×16 ready to send to studio. To Touraine at 1 — Ralph up — we had lunch. I had a "plain" consommé that used me up for the rest of the day — Rain ceased in the afternoon but I was not able to go out \+ spent most of the time on my bed. R. had dinner alone — We had a long eve'g session hanging pictures in the apartment.
