## Left Page

### Sun. Aug. 20, 1905

**Weather:** Peculiar weather \- cloudy \+ very quiet  **Temp:** [illegible]

a light sprinkle of rain while we were at bkb.
\- a slow clearing up during the day \- lovely
atmospheric effects in the afternoon \- clear at
night \- I don't know exactly how the m'f
got away from me but it all went before I
knew it \- At noon the boys \+ I went to P.O.
Mail had not come \- we went to top of big
Rock at the iron bridge \+ loafed there till near
1 \- again to P.O. \- still no mail \- Then home, meeting
the Goldmark girls \+ Schilling \- Dinner at 1.30
Then I loafed about the place until 4.30
then to Mr. Maghee's with her Pyrographic out
fit \+ a botany book \- Miss Walker, Mr. Loring \+
the family came in to Sunday afternoon tea \-
I Ret'd home before 6 \- Took no supper \-
Ev'f made Velox prints from the two photos
that I took on Friday \- one comes out passably
good.

## Right Page

### Mon. Aug. 21, 1905

**Weather:** Lovely day  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- Adams did not put in an
appearance \- Otis hauled four loads of road
filler \+ Moody helped in part \- After bkb. I
went out to the hill, windy brow, with my camera
\+ took a couple of shots at the house \- It was
near 12 when I went to P.O. Taking my camera
with me \- I climbed to the top of the big Rock
by the iron bridge \+ set my tripod \+ camera
on what promised to be a fine view beautifully
lighted \- the Rock was steep \+ shelving 50 or 60'
above the river \- as I turned the tripod slipped
\+ the whole thing went over the Rock \+ I heard
a crash below \- when I got down to the bridge
I could see the tripod on a bit of sandy beach at the
foot of the Rock but could not see the camera \-
home \+ had dinner \- Then the boys Ret'd with
me to the Rock \- Jim climbed around to the
tripod \+ found the camera near it, partly submer-
ged \+ hopelessly wrecked \- I let Ralph down the
face of the Rock partway, by a Rope \+ he rescued
the plate holder back \+ the focussing glass \- That
completed the round up \- the boys went on down
the valley on their wheels \- I Ret'd home \+ devel-
oped the three plates I exposed in the m'f \- At 6
I went over to Scott Brown's \- Saw him \- also Mr. \+ Mrs.
Sidney Smith, here on a flying visit \- on my way back
I met Mr. \+ Mrs. Weston who had been at the house to
call \- Supper at about 7 \- Ev'f some Reading \-
