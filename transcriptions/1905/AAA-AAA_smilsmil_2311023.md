## Left Page

### Fri. July 21, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day but much too cool to suit me after my recent baking — Up soon after 5 o'clk — Some time after midnight I had an unusual \+ very painful experience — I had sudden \+ exceedingly severe cramps in feet \+ legs — The pain was intense \+ persistent — I could not stand \+ felt as though both feet were in vises being turned in opposite directions — The agony of it brought a copious sweat all over me — head swam — ears rang \+ I fell over on my bed in a half faint. I was really alarmed — I rubbed my legs \+ tried to walk around — I think the struggle lasted 20 minutes before I was able to get into bed again — I had two minor attacks later — I packed my bag \+ Ralph \+ I went down at 6 o'clk — we were charged $6: for our two beds — exorbitant — The house is old, stuffy \+ unsanitary — Went to R.R. station \+ had bkfs. \+ off on 7 o'clk. Train — got to Westport at 11 — \+ up to The Windsor soon after 12 — We had to wait till 1 o'clk for our dinner — \+ it was 2.30 before we were able to start for St. Huberts — R. \+ I were past more than half way in a good rig \+ there we had to get out \+ wait till the farmer unhitched his team from a load of hay \+ harnessed them to a rickety, dirty \+ uncomfortable old wagon — We waited half an hour for this change — we rattled on \+ met K. \+ E. in Keene Valley with Maghee — we got to the house at 6 \+ at 6.30 had supper — about 10 our trunks arr'd \+ until 11 o'clk I unpacked things — then to bed, fearing more cramps that happily did not come.

## Right Page

### Sat. July 22, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More fine weather — \+ cool — too cool for me — I don't like it. This eve \+ the Ther. on piazza is at 57° \+ in my bedroom it is 64°. For exercise this Temp. is delightful — but I am not strong enough to exercise — Bkfs. at 8 — Soon after, boxes \+ a barrel \+ Ralph \+ I used up a large part of the m\+ unpacking Phots. \+ other materials from the studio \+ a barrel of crockery from Wanamakers — I wandered a little over the place — everywhere evidences of neglect \+ unintelligent work — Dinner at 1 — Continued unpacking of trunk \+ then had a restful nap. At 5 Ralph \+ I went to the Maghee "Shanty", an old building at the foot of the hill, fitted up, to afternoon tea by invitation — Mr. Maghee, Hardenburg, Miss Hammersley \+ niece, Dr. Ward, Mary \+ her girls there — Ret'd home at 6 — supper at 6.30 — Eve \+ loafing about till after 10.
