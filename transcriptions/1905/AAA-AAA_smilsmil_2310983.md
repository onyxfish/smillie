## Left Page

### Tues. May 2, 1905

**Weather:** Changable — clouds \+ sunshine  **Temp:**

W. J. H. Maghee dined with us.

Rather late feeling much out of sorts generally — I have been unusually rheumatic lately — I worked on few sketches already made \+ mounted — They are on tinted paper \+ I intensified effect by adding white. Went to bank after 12 — then home to lunch. Gc° lunched with us. After lunch — to studio — measured off an 18"×24" plate into five smaller ones \+ then took it to Sharp \+ sons, 13 Baxter St. \+ had it cut as marked. Back to studio's at 5, — home at about 6. Maghee came in with Bessie. \+ stayed to dinner — Charl came in for a moment \+ later Bessie \+ Maghee went to C's to see some of his lantern slides —

## Right Page

### Wed. May 3, 1905

**Weather:** University Glee Club concert.  **Temp:**

An enervatingy warm day — thinely overcast much of the day — feeling better than yesterday but still considerably below concert pitch — Got to studio rather later than usual as I spent some time at home drawing checks to pay bills and writing notes. I worked on an old plate — Soft ground etching of a roadside chapel near Bavano, from Water-color sketch made in 1893 — I scraped out a lot of work on sky \+ prepared it for an aquatint ground — Home to lunch — back — worked on plate \+ made aqua-tint preparations till near 5 — Then I went over to foot of 33d. St. west \+ over the ground now torn up by Penn. R. R. for their new R.R. station — It was after 6 when I got home — dinner — then dressed \+ with Bessie went to University Glee Club concert at Waldorf-Astoria — Charl joined us at about 9 — The concert was a good one — Miss Anna Bussert was soprano soloist \+ the Dannreuther String orchestra assisted — for the latter, I did not care — Miss B. was pleasing but her voice (but not her person) is thin — The club sang well \+ the selections were good.
