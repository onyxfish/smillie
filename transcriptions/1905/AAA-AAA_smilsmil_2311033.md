## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 10, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ther. got to 83°. in my bed room -
Hot \+ hazy - with heavy thunderstorms from about 7 to 9 o'clk. P.M. After 4 Kf. I wrote for some time \+ finished letter to Kate - It was too hot for work out of doors - I looked after Otis \+ Moody who are taking their time on the Road work - After 11 I went down to P.O. \+ got mail - \+ walked along the Riverside a way \+ found a good point from which to photog. the Ark. Dinner about 1 - afternoon much loafing - Otis \+ Moody ploughed up the ground that we staked off for a Tennis Court - Then I went to Scott Brown's but he was away - Then over to beyond W. Otis's to see where the stuff for my road is being got - home before 6 - my clothes wet with perspiration - changed completely \+ had a good rub down - supper - Eve'g. Jim \+ Ralph went to Lawrence Adler's for a Camp supper - several young people invited.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 11, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy, misty \+ muggy all day - my m't was divided between the garden, Otis on the Road \+ work on mantle piece in my bed room upon which I have painted the motto that years ago I carved over a fireplace in Montrose: "There is no sorrow in my song, nor winter in my year." About noon I went to P.O. on my way down saw Adames, the painter, at work on Maghee's "shanty" - he promises to get to work for me next week - back to dinner at 1 - afternoon completed the Ribbon with Motto finding it very hard work the pain in my left arm distressing me more than at any time before - it depresses me \+ fills me with mbodings - J.H. Maghee came to supper \+ spent the eve'g. I fixed myself in my easy chair in my bed room \+ tried to forget my misery reading Townsend's "Asia \+ Europe".
