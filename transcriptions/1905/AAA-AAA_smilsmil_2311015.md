## Left Page

### Wed. July 5, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A hot day with pleasantly cool breeze. After bkft. I went to Laundry — then to Manhattan Storage, then by Elevated down Town — To Water Dept. \+ paid Croton Tax — then to Tax office \+ saw that assessments on my real estate had not been changed — then to Dimes \+ Amend — then to Cons. Gas Co, in 14th St. then to 2d Natl. Bank — then to D. B. Butler's — then to Bayerdorfer's \+ home at 1 — lunch — at 2 — at Studio, Butler's boy came \+ took painting \+ portfolios to be sent to Mr. Roger Adams. Home at 3, so used up by my headache that I lay on my bed till 6 — a light meal at 6.30 — Evelf, Ralph \+ I went to Studio carrying some articles — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 6, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, muggy a.m., clearing middle of day to sunny \+ quite warm — clouding over again afternoon — rain during the night. Up before 6 — wrote a letter to Mary before bkft. — After bkft. Atwood, packer from Manhattan Storage, came \+ soon after, ten boxes \+ I had a day with him packing books \+ my unbound files of Punch — Ill. Land, News — Harper \+ Century Monthlies — The regular box size for books being a little too small for the Lond. News I had to order three boxes for them — They came in the afternoon — also two barrels, in one of which he packed about 50 glass preserve jars — I got a short rest while packer was at lunch but, except for that, worked hard from 8.30 to 4.30 — Paperhangers at work in bedroom — 2d Story front — Dinner at 6.30 — Evelf read an article in McClure, with portraits \+ descriptive of Jno. D. Rockefeller — To bed before 10.
