## Left Page

### Wed. May 10, 1905

Annual meeting. N.A.D.

Jim came from New-Haven —

Fine day \- To studio at 10 o worked on etching "Port Naire, Paris", till 12 — Then home for a few minutes on on, up to Fine Arts Bldg in 57·51 where the N.A. held its annual meeting \+ had its dinner in the ev. got there before 1 — lunch soon after — sat by Aldau Weir \+ Kenyon Cox \+ had an interesting time — Soon after 2 the meeting was called to order — Business was hurried along — elections went off smoothly — at last we got to the real business of the session — the consideration of the proposal from Columbia university to make the Academy School a part of the University system — It was not till after 3 o'clk that a printed document was brought in \+ distributed to the members — giving Pres. Butler's letter presenting the proposition \+ the text of agreement as approved by the Council — When the Secretary routine business was over — Dielmann (Pres.) Read the document, with some explanatory interpolations Then Blashfield offered a Resolution empowering the Council to accept \+ Ratify the agreement. Jno Weir made some remarks that seemed to be in opposition, but declares that he had an open mind \+ was seeking enlightenment — Kenyon Cox enlightened him — Then Chas Miller sought to make trouble on Constitutional grounds but Dielmann put a stopper on him — Then Back with sailed in, intensely in earnest — predicting sure destruction to the schools \+ protesting against Railroading the thing through at this session — de forest said — Now or never — at went to vote — 29 in favor — 9 against — I also proved — but regret that it was necessary to rush it thro' at once — at would be almost impossible to get a quorum next week — Then it would have been delayed another year \+ probably lost. After 6 when we adjourned — I got a breath of air in the Park with Nicoll before dinner soon after 7. For the first time in the history of the N.A. the Associates were invited to the annual dinner — a very considerable number attended — dinner served in Vanderbilt gallery — after it Cox made an excellent address favoring a union of N.A. \+ Socy of Am. Artists — \+ after 11 I walked home with Watrous —

## Right Page

### Thur. May 11, 1905

Pleasant day in spite of threats of showers — Up at usual time — bkft. at 8 — Jim came from New-Haven yesterday getting here about 6 p.m. He is one of about 40 Seniors studying Social Problems, who, under charge of their professor will go to municipal prisons, hospitals — Emigrant depots — University Settlements — Mills — Hotels — Chinese theatre, etc. etc. — I went to studio \+ spent the day working upon Chalzel near Bavano — biting in a second dry aqua-tint ground \+ then some burnishing upon it to get it ready for a print to-morrow, for which I writ down some paper — lunch \+ dinner as usual — Eve'g — after Reading my paper went to bed somewhat earlier than usual feeling tired — Jim did not come home at night — I do not know where he stayed — something was said of the folks hilety, after an ev'g at Chinese theatre, of a night at a Mill's Hotel.
