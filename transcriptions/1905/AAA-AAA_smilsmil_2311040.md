## Left Page

### Thur. Aug. 24, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very little sunshine — cloudy day. a light shower at noon — All hands except Mary \+ self got away with Maghu soon after bkft — for a day on the Ausable lakes — I fussed about a number of things, wrote to Mrs. Miller \+ to Parke \+ after 10 went to Scott Browns — Telephoned to Orlando Beede asking for the use of his Disc Harrow for one day, to tear up the sod on ground ploughed for Tennis court. I had nearly an hour of sauntering in the Reserve — Then home got letters \+ went to P.O. — Home \+ about 1 o'clk. Mary \+ I had dinner — Afternoon wrote to Nekera Div. Eastman Kodak Co. about trouble I have with Velox paper — Interviewed Moody who says he is sick — wandered over the hills — Lake party Rel? Soon after 6. Supper about 7 — Read awhile in the eve'g.

## Right Page

### Fri. Aug. 25, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Brilliant day \+ quite cool — For awhile I was in garden that is now almost a wreck owing to untoward weather \+ want of fertilizing in the spring — the most of the wy however was given to composing \+ copying a letter to W. W. Q. Neilson concerning the bit of land that he refuses to sell me — At noon I went to P.O. Dinner after 1 o'clk. Miss Smith, a niece of Mrs. Loring's, came in with the girls and dined with us — With newspaper for some time after dinner — then I went down to where the river comes thro' the gorge and getting up on the old River Trail I followed it up to where it led up to the hotel — It was much overgrown \+ choked by slides, fallen timber \+ other debris — It was quite a hard piece of work \+ when I got home I stripped \+ had a wash \+ rub down — Tea at 7 — Eve'g I had a little fire in my bedroom \+ with great comfort \+ enjoyment toasted my toes while I read a Kipling story.
