## Left Page

### Sat. May 6, 1905

**Weather:** Sky overcast — S.E. wind — mild with a suggestion of coming rain.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio after 10 \+ Resumed my efforts to get a satisfactory aqua-tint ground — got one before noon \+ commenced biting — home to lunch — then to 2? Nat'l. bank — back to studio \+ continued the biting — cleaned plate before 5 — think I have been moderately successful — Home before 6 \+ had a short rest before dinner — Eve'g. I went to Century — monthly meeting \+ had interesting talks with Dielman — Watrous, Cox how \+ others about the business to come up at Annual N.A. \[illegible\] meeting next Wed. The merging of the school into the Columbia University system will be presented but the merging of N.A. \[illegible\] \+ Socy. of Am. Art has not yet taken such shape \+ to make it advisable for the Council to report it for general discussion — Home at 11.

## Right Page

### Sun. May 7, 1905

**Weather:** Cleared to be a very warm day with a high S.W. wind.  **Temp:** [illegible]

B\+P. at about 10 — Before 12 I went to studio \+ wet down paper for printing to-morrow. Then I had a wandering walk of inspection along the Central R.R. excavations on Park Ave — up to 57' S. \+ then back, getting home at 2 — dinner — The remainder of afternoon reading and writing — wrote to Ella Beach that I would go to Peekskill to visit her on Tuesday — Eve'g I went to Walter Cook's but no one was at home — no one came to the door in answer to my ringing — Then I went to Edw'. Cook's — found him \+ Mary \+ Carleton \+ his wife — there till 10 — then home.
