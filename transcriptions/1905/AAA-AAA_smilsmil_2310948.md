## Left Page

### Sun. Feb. 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day — got up feeling fairly well \+ had my Sunday m't scrub \+ b'kfs't. at 9.30 — At about 11 I went out for an airing — walked up to Century to see the R. S. Gifford memorial exh'n — was surprised to find it so full — the gallery walls well covered — a number of people there with whom I had conversation — it was after 1 when I left — stopped at Charl's — home just before dinner at 2 — feeling weak \+ tired — after dinner for some time going over the contents of Annie's Sec'ty drawer where there was a lot of old family photos. \+ daguerreotypes — then I had a rest \+ a little sleep — George stayed \+ I departed from my usual custom \+ had some tea — Geo. left early — I read "Pigs in Clover" — a vile book, written by a degenerate for degenerates — it is shocking to think of such literature being distributed promiscuously — from a purely literary point of view it may be good — from a moral point of view it is bad — debasing, loathsome.

## Right Page

### Mon. Feb. 20, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Snow in the n'f — then nizzle — fog at night — I was unfit for anything all day — At noon I went to the butcher's for some raw oysters, about the most important transaction of the day. I hung about reading a little but accomplishing nothing — At night I went to bed much troubled by my eyes — my right eye much inflamed. Charl spent ev'g with us.
