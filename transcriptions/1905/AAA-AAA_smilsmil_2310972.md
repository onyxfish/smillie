## Left Page

### Mon. April 10, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm, enervating day — with a thunderstorm \+ light rain late in the afternoon \+ at night more rain. To studio as usual \+ got to work at biting-in "Chapel near Lugano" — things going well — lunch at home — Tom Cook called at the house after 4½p — \+ I had quite an extended chat with him — He has been abroad for nine months \+ got home last wed. — Resumed work on my plate in the afternoon \+ continued till near 5 but did not finish the biting — Home at 5 \+ rested till dinner — Ralph came from New-Haven rather late — After dinner he \+ I went to the Kl. Theatre — Balcony \+ 45 "S" — \+ saw The "Prince of Pilsen", a farcical comedy with music \+ dancing — It was amusing in a rather low-down way \+ I was not pleased with myself for being so much amused by the antics of a drunken man — The "book" (or words) was entirely without wit — The music was "catchy", but nothing of it remains with me \+ the dancing \+ scenic effects were not remarkable — most of the girls were decently dressed — nevertheless, Ralph \+ I did a good deal of laughing — The "Prince" was Arthur Donaldson — the box is one, Jess Dandon — Good Shrimpton (an excellent take-off of an Englishman) Percy Ames — The widow was Louisa Willis — After the performance R \+ I went across the street to "The Astor" \+ went about more to see the throngs of pleasure seekers finding enjoyment in eating \+ drinking — then home \+ to bed at 12.15

Mr. Edgar VanWinkle, 115 E. 70th St., purchaser of my painting "A Chapel near Lugano", called on me this afternoon \+ paid me $100.
