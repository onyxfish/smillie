## Left Page

### Mon. May 8, 1905

**Weather:** Bright, cool, breezy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, cool, breezy — to studio at usual time \+ at once commenced to point. Was disappointed to find that Chapel near Barrano is so much underbitter that I shall have to lay a new ground (aqua-tint) over it. I then worked a little while on Oct. afternoon (aqua-tint) \+ then pulled some pfs. from it — home to lunch — back — I printed from Oct. P.M. File about 4 — then cleaned up \+ went to Miss Roelker's — getting there at 5 o'clk. with paste, brush \+ other Requisites \+ until 6 pasted imfps. from her book-plate into books, showing how to do it \+ then superintending her work — then home — dinner — even with newspaper \+ to bed early.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 9, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At Peekskill, with Ella Beach.

Charming day — bright \+ cool — Katharine \+ I took 10:30 train \+ reached Peekskill in an hour. Ella B's man with horse \+ light wagon waiting for us \+ we rode at once to her place, we were driven to her little Jap-cottage to meet her — This is a two story plaything that she has recently had erected near the large home — it is the outcome of a visit she made to Jap one some time ago — she had a Jap. architect assist her in drawing plans — it is an odd \+ entertaining jumble of Jap \+ up to date american — There is no furniture, precisely, in the house — cushions and mats take the place of seats — there are electric lights, plate glass windows, chimneys \+ fireplaces \+, hidden behind screens, bath tubs, bas ins \+ all any plumbing — many of the effects are exceeding yprety but I don't want to live in such a house, exactly. We spent some time there, then to the big house \+ lunned ourselves on the South Terrace — till lunch was served — after that a mule \+ light wagon was brought to the door — Then for an hour or more Ella drove us about the place — wherever she pleased provided only that no tree or wall was in the way — over fields, thro' woods, into the orchard \+ under the flowering trees — we stood up in the wagon \+ with shears cut great quantities of flowering branches, always first considering judicious pruning — there were some Jap. apples — wonderfully abundant, pink \+ double, the most beautiful blossoms of the apple kind I had ever seen — Ella says the fruit is small \+ worthless — after a time we got to the house left the mule \+ wandered over to the old Beecher house \+ grounds — then back to the tea Jap. house \+ there E. served tea, all lounging on small square mattresses on the floor — the mule took us to the gate — there we took the 5.20 Trolley car — got to the R.R. Station in 10 m — then 5.47 train to N.Y. at a little after 7 being about 20 m. late — Mary \+ Bessie were at table — we ate our dinners \+ a quiet even \+ to bed early.
