## Left Page

### Sat. Jan. 14, 1905

**Weather:** weather clearing brilliantly but very cold \+ windy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at about 10 \- I soon became interested in photographic work \+ was intently engaged all the morning making reductions upon glass negatives from large photos that I had brought home from abroad last summer \- I find that I can make 4×5 reductions that will go harmoniously with the views I took \+ so I can fill gaps in the pictorial history of our trip \+ make for the boys two albums that must, in after years, be extremely interesting to them \- Home to lunch \+ back \+ continued my work as long as there was light \- After 5 when I got home \- a rest \- dinner \- Eve'g, I went to Century \- Annual meeting \- After the meeting, as unfortunately I was not in mood to enjoy company I returned home at 11 o clk. \+ went to bed.

## Right Page

### Sun. Jan. 15, 1905

**Weather:** fine winter day \- very cold \+ windy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

The streets filled with clouds of whistling dirt \- Up at 7 \+ till 9 engaged in copying my self \- men blefs — then newspapers \- at about 11 went to studio \+ until 12.30 was at work unpacking unbound works, serials, many illustrated, that have been boxed for more than 20 years \- many of them at Montrose, \+ putting them in big book-case in my studio \- then out \+ walked for some time on Park ave \- After 3 o clk. I went to Charl's \- Mary there \- home at 2 \- dinner \- After that I cut printing mats for some negatives \- about 4 I lay down \+ slept soundly till 6.30 \- from 7 to 11 I worked at Velay printing \- then to bed.

Immediately after dinner I went to Mellison league to see the collection of paintings there \- few \+ none very good \- my "Cumulus after Sunset" looked most distressing.
