## Left Page

### Mon. April 24, 1905

*Dean dined \+ stayed overnight with us.*

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast most of the day, but not unpleasant — to studio at about 10 — worked on aqua-tint plate "Oct. afternoon", burnishing \+ clearing up. After 12 went to Putnam's hoping to see Q. Haven P— to get pointers as to where Jim sh'd go for information needed in writing a thesis on "the liquor problem." Q. H. P. is in Eureka — then I went to Scribner's \+ saw H. L. Smith — then home to lunch — to studio after 2 — carpenter came \+ worked on doors that fail to catch. I took proofs from my plates — worked on it between \+ then laid another aquatint ground over it — I spent sometime making improvements in my big aqua tint box as it was not dust-tight at the opening where the plate is slipped in \+ out. Home at 5.30 \+ wrote a note to Louise Cook who had sent me a "hurry call" to dinner this evening — I excused myself because Jim had invited a college friend, Tom Dean, to dine \+ stay overnight with him. Dean came \+ later in the eve'g, he \+ Jim went to the theatre.

## Right Page

### Tues. April 25, 1905

*Rowed dined with Jim.*

**Weather:** A beautiful day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day — to studio a little earlier than usual. I did some biting on 5×7 "Oct. p.m". \+ in the afternoon took some p'fs, my work was interfered with by presence of a carpenter who was fixing studio doors \+ setting a hanging closet, from Montrose, in one of the large closets — the plaster had to be cut from the wall \+ it proved to be a dirty job. He was all day about it. About 3.30 Jim came \+ together we went to Scribners where we had a pleasant interview with Mr. H. L. Smith who is one of the directors of the firm or Co. He gave Jim good advice \+ some information as to the publishing business \+ time to make application for place of position. He told Jim that if he decided that he wished to try for it under, or S &c', he would do what he could to help him — then we walked home together — Jim seems to be uncertain in mind \+ quite helpless — It is evident that he dreads the restraints of having to go to work. Young Gould came — took dinner with Jim \+ later they went off together for the eve'g. I spent the eve'g quietly \+ rather stupidly —
In the m'g I rec'd a "hurry call" from Louise (Mrs. Walter) Cook to dine with her this eve'g. As Jim was to have his friend to dine with him I sent Louise a note of explanation \+ regret.
