## Left Page

### Sat. Aug. 26, 1905

**Weather:** Beautiful m'g. — singularly bright + cool — Rain in the afternoon  **Temp:**

It was after 10 when Mary, Katharine, Mrs. Kellogg + I started for an exploration of the W. G. Neilson place — we sauntered over to his house, wandered about there + down to the camp where we sat in the warm sunshine, enjoying a faint fragrance from the very old balsam bough bed + chatting for some time — then we went down to the old, unused Road + followed it around under the fine old trees till it brought us to the Neilson house again — then home — I got letters + went to P.O. — back at 1 — dinner — I saw Bashaw at Magee's + engaged him to work on my wall + Road — After 1 o'clk. the sky clouded over rapidly — I hurried over to Scott Brown's + telephoned to Sanders to send beef up for dinner To-morrow. Then home + soon after a violent storm from the N.W. broke upon us — the wind + rain was very heavy for a time — then it settled down to a steady rain till about 6 — when cleared for a brief time — supper — eve general talk while sitting before a fire —

## Right Page

### Sun. Aug. 27, 1905

**Weather:** tricky weather — mist + clouds — then some sunshine + in the afternoon a mild repetition of yesterday's storm — cold, clear + rather raw at night  **Temp:**

Up before 6.30 — had a pleasant fire in my bedroom + enjoyed my bath + rub-down — Bkf't at about 7 — as soon as my room was "made up" I sat there + wrote part of a letter to Emma J. At 12, with Jim + Ralph, I went to P.O. Stopped at Ferd. Beede's + phoned to Orlando Beede who said he w'd send me his Discharrow. Dinner before 2 — loafing about awhile reading. Then a little nap — + at about 4 o'clk I went to Wes. Otis's + arranged to have Rob't O— go to Beede's to-morrow for his harrow. On my way home was caught in heavy rain + took refuge under W. G. Neilson's kitchen. As the rain seemed likely to continue for some time I at last broke for home + got there hot + damp. I changed to my skin + had a good hair-belt rub + felt none the worse for me, rain + my wetting — met Louis Lorb on the Chappel Pond Road. Tea at 7 — eve't some reading + some twaddle about clothes that was very irritating to me — I cannot think of a more pitiful + unworthy object to take a leading place in one's mind — How many times I wish that Annie might have been spared to give to her boys the example of a high type of woman —
