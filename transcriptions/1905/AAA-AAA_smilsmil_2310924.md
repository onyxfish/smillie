## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 2, 1905

**Weather:** Dark, dismal & wet  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dark, dismal & wet — It was late when I got to studio & cold there — my head was still uncomfortable & a general misery, like unto the weather, pervaded. — I fussed about doing things of no importance. A young fellow, giving his name, something beginning with B & ending with ski, took a good part of the m'f., making enquiries about etching. — Home to lunch at 1.45 after that I was on my bed till near 4 — then up & wrote letter to Kate — dinner at 6.30. Eve'g all hands went to Charl's to see lantern slides — As he had nothing new to show me I set' home & spent remainder of eve'g. fussing with my negatives.

Mary & Jane H. went to Mary Burling's sometime during the m'f.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 3, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A particularly bad sort of a day — commencing with a mizzle that froze into a thin sheet of ice over everything as it fell — & followed by an increasing snow storm that howled thro' the night as it flung down nearly a foot of snow. It was nearly 11 before I got to studio — more relax printing — struggling with dense negatives from which the getting of every new print is a new problem. — Home to lunch & back & continued my printing till after 6 — then home to dinner — Eve'g — at Maynard's request I made transcript of his acct., indebtedness for Studio rent, now over $700 in arrears.
