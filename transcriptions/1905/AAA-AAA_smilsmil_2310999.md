## Left Page

### Sat. June 3, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Delightful day \- clear & crisp \- had a fairly restful night & got up feeling weak but free from pain \- After bkfst. went to the Analyst in 42d St. & left with him a sample of urin brewed yesterday \- Circumstances considered the brew should be remarkable. Then to the Touraine & saw the manager \- He cannot yet promise me the apartment on fifth floor but assures me he thinks it quite certain that I can have it \- Promises an answer next week \- Then home \- to studio \- grocer \+ butcher \- then to bank \- Then to Scribners \- Saw Mr. Hfd. Smith. & put in a formal application for a place for Jim in the Scribner Establishment somewhere \- Then to D.&H. office in Cortlandt St & got mileage ticket \- Then home & had lunch. After that I had a nap for an hour \- then wrote a number of checks in payment of bills \- & after 4 o'clk. went to studio. Changed proofs in blotter. Then home \- To be my home for a very short time longer \- the realization of this fact, forced upon me by the stripping of the house & consequent disorder & confusion, is very depressing \- Dinner \- qvef. I went to the Century (monthly meeting) to get rid of myself. There were but few members there \- D. Gall gave me the analyst's Report upon the sample of urin left with him this mf. \- Negative in character \- a fair showing for a man of my age \- Indican in excess \- showing digestive disturbance \-
I got home at about 10.30.

## Right Page

### Sun. June 4, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day \- rather warm & humid \- clouding over in the afternoon \- light rain at night \- Hkfst. at 9. after a good scout \- feeling better \- some wandering about in the streets.
