## Left Page

### Tues. Aug. 8, 1905

**Weather:** misty & cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

misty & cloudy - Some sunshine. Then overcast, light showers about noon - then thunderstorms after which the weather cleared - a bright moonlight with chilly air - Before bkfst - I wrote part of a letter to Kate - The mt went as usual - sometime in garden - sometime in studio fussing with photos. At noon went to P.O. & climbed the hill on my return in a shower - Dinner - again, as yesterday I gave way to my feelings & slept an hour or more - Then out & about the place - Otis brought a couple of loads of road filling in the mt & in the afternoon hauled away grass that had been lying out for some days & other refuse from about the house. Supper - Evg. walking on the piazza & reading -

## Right Page

### Wed. Aug. 9, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A warm, misty, muggy day with strong southerly wind - After bkfst. wrote for a while on letter to Kate & before 10 went with Ralph to Scott Brown's & got information from him on various subjects. Then home again & to Seckington's (The Reserve) garden where R. S. & I picked a lot of fine wax beans for dinner - R. returning to the house with them while I went on to P.O. It was after 1 o'clk. when Katharine H. & her friend came to dinner - after that I was looking after Otis & Moody at work on road - O - brought four loads to day then Jim, Ralph & I staked out ground for a Tennis Court - part of it almost necessarily runs over on to W.G. Neilson's land, within bounds that I think it likely I shall eventually acquire - I got letter from Yale which gives me hope that the pain in my left arm is no more than an apt. of Rheumatism \- have had unpleasant visions of neuritis - Supper - Evg. loafing & a little reading.
