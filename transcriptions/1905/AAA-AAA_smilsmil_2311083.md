## Left Page

### Mon. Nov. 20, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very fine weather, clear \+ reasonably cold. Bkfp. at 8 \- To studio at about 11 \- went to Mrs. Dahn's employment office \+ got from her the address of Marie Olsen, whose trunk is still at studio \+ in my way \- Then to Bayerdorfer about old hall clock \- Then to Butler's with die proof of "The Mill Door" which I am to have framed \+ presented to Miss Crandall, nurse at the Presbytn. Hospital \- Then to studio again \+ then to my rooms \- Had a bowl of junket \+ at 1.30 started for Hospital \- saw Dr. B\- \+ had a pleasant interview but I do not think it is possible for him to help me \- Then I went to Jessie's room \+ had a little visit with her \- Then to my rooms \+ had a nap \- Then to Freesis \+ got some drugs \- Then to studio \+ got some things \+ took them to my rooms. Jim came \+ we had dinner at 7 \- Evef loafing till 10 \- Then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Nov. 21, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

More fine weather \- Got up with headache \+ had bkft. a little later than Jim \- at 10 I went to 110 \+ met man from Gas Consumers Ass'n. The removed the Co's device from the gas pipes \- Then to studio \- met Geo. Green (painter) \+ got him to move out some trunks so that I could open them \+ learn what is in them \- After 12 I went to Douglas's \- saw Hugh \+ he bought on acct. of a friend my rake at 110 \- Then to Lewis \+ Congers, W. 42d 6. \+ bought folding step ladder to use in closets \- Then to Torraine \- Green came \+ I gave him old over coat \- It was 2 o'clk. before I had lunch \- I spent the rest of the afternoon getting remaining books on shelves \- about 5 went to Hahnenfelt's \+ got supplies \- Clarence Smith came about 6 \+ got Jim's signature \+ mine to papers necessary to closing sale of 110 \- Southack wrote me, claiming to have sold 110 \+ enclosed bill for his commission \- when I showed it to Clarence he had already seen it \- Herron had taken it to him. Clarence left at 6.30 \- I was not in any condition to eat, so Jim went down alone \- Lavinia Nash, a colored woman, sent by Alice Ireland, came to talk about our laundry work \- I promised to give her a trial next week \- One of Jim's friends, Harris, came in just before 8 \- They went away to some theatre \- I called about \- to bed after 10 \+ then to bed.
