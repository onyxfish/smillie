## Left Page

### Sun. Dec. 24, 1905

**Weather:** fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day - I had bkfs. about 9:th The boys kept their beds till near noon - Before noon I went to studio. One of the Touraine boys carried the Butler frame to studio for me \+ there. I put the little 9"x 16" picture, painted by Geo. forme, into it - it looked very well - Then I had a short walk on the sunny side of Park Ave. Then to my rooms - There was more work at picture hanging, both boys helping. At 7 o'clk. we three dined together in the Touraine Restaurant - Jim went out somewhere — I went to studio got the 9"x 16" painting that I put in frame this m't. \+ took it to Edw. Cook's. 154 E. [illegible] where Carleton \+ his wife are now living - The painting is a belated wedding present to [illegible] Carleton's wife - to my disgust she opened the door for me - All servants being out - consequently I was caught - The painting was set aside without being uncovered \+ I went in, upon invitation, \+ joined the family at the tail end of their Xmas dinner — it was nearly 10 when I got to Char to join Ralph there — it was after 11 when we went to our Touraine Rooms.

## Right Page

### Mon. Dec. 25, 1905

**Weather:** fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day - I had my bkfs. alone - Jim \+ Ralph got up later - At about noon we three started out for a short walk - we walked up Madison \+ Park Ave's to 56th St. examining the progress of the work of excavation for the Central R.R. \+ then down on the east side to station \+ then to Touraine - Jim was in a hurry to meet Lloyd Holsapple with whom he was to go to an Opera Matinee - So, he lunched alone \+ R. \+ I lunched later - There was a little work done at hanging pictures in our Rooms - my feet are now giving me trouble, being sore or very tender in spots - consequently I did not go out during the afternoon - Jim came in soon after 6 \+ soon after we three went to the Hotel Manhattan \+ held there a very good Table d'hote dinner - About 8 we started for Mary's boarding place - getting there (144 w. 25th S.) just as Mary, Kath., Eliz. \+ Clark did - we went up to her room where a small Xmas tree had been put up \+ one time was spent in the distribution of presents \+ then beer \+ german cakes were discussed - we left after 11 o'clk - Chas., Eliz., Jim, Ralph \+ I rode up town in a 6th Ave car - I got to bed at midnight.
