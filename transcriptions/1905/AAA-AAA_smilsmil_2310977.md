## Left Page

### Thur. April 20, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull day with rain late in the afternoon
\+ at night — Douglas came after bkft. \+ I
showed him the parts of the house where I
wished painting \+ kals omining done — Men
to studio — Resumed my work of yesterday
hunting for stray proofs \+ sketches — at 12
I went to Scribner's — saw Mr. Smith \+ had
a long, interesting \+, to me, satisfactory talk with
him about the publishing business as a field for
Jim. It was 1.30 when I left him — then home to
lunch — then with Geo. Smale, Freeman, \+ Fyau till
after 3 — To studio — searching thro' sketches \+ prints
till I found what I sought — Home at about 6 —
Dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g. I wrote a short letter
to Ralph — the rest of eve'g. Jim \+ I passed
in the parlor, reading.

## Right Page

### Fri. April 21, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

An uncomfortably warm day — the Ther.
getting to 73° at noon — official — the air
close \+ enervating till cooled \+ cleared by a
heavy shower about 4 p.m — rain continued
at intervals thro' the eve'g. To studio at usual
time the m't spent in getting my large
aqua-tint box in working order — the one I
made in Montrose some six or eight years
ago \+ that never has been used till now —
I got a dust-ground on a plate upon which
I had an imperfect soft-ground etching
after my painting "Oct. afternoon" — a study
made on Montrose Fair Ground — Home to
lunch — back to studio soon after 2 — work in-
terrupted by darkness at time of heavy shower —
Home at 5.30 \+ had a rest before dinner. Eve'g
went with Jim to Garrick Theatre \+ saw Bernard
Shaw's comedy "You never can tell" — Amusing
\+ rather witty in dialogue at times — a satire,
not unamiable, upon a mother's 20th century
education of her daughters to rise intellectually
superior to sentiment — I did not think much of
the acting — there was too much Rapidity of utterance
\+ so much that a great deal of what was said was
lost to me — there was much unnecessary rant —
It was all very stagey but I was amused notwith-
standing — Armosd Daly was Mr. Valentine — the dentist
old William, the waiter, was John Findlay — Crampton,
the grouty old husband, was Geo. Farren — Bohum,
the bullying solicitor, was Harry Harwood — Mrs.
Clandon (wife of Crampton) was a big \+ dreadfully
misshapen woman — Miss Jeffreys Lewis — Miss
Clandon, the willowy one who loves Valentine in
spite of herself — Drina de Wolf — \+ Dolly Clandon, a
mistress little minx, was Mabel Taliaferro — The
play was over at 11.
