## Left Page

### Fri. March 3, 1905

**Weather:** fair, cool & windy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim came from New-Haven.

fair, cool & windy. I worked all day on Vesuvius but failed to get what I tried for. Jim came down from New Haven to go with Louise Cook to a swell dance at the St. Regis. He did not dine at home \+ did not get back from the dance till 3 A.m. Eve'g. I wrote to Ralph \+ mailed it at 10 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### Sat. March 4, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

About a couple of inches of snow in the n't — some sunshine about noon. \+ slofs — very cold at night & freezing. I got to studio at usual time \+ put in my last day on Vesuvius. at last I got it into shape to ship it to Parke. There is some good, common-place work on it, but in spite of my best endeavors it is a failure as a work of art. Lunch at home — work at studio till after 4 — then home \+ had a rest before dinner. Jim was off with one of his chums. After 8 I went to Century — The Art Com. has tried to get together a representative collection of paintings — it was better than the average but nothing for the Club to brag about. I was much interested in a bust by Herbert Adams — a naturally tinted marble from the Milan Cathedral quarry, a female head, set into bust \+ shoulders of wood — a very pleasing effect. Home \+ to bed at 12.
