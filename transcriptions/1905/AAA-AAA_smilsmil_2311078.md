## Left Page

### Wed. Nov. 8, 1905

**Weather:** Dull mt + wet but clearing - bright, cool afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

After a fairly restful night I got up but found that my Temp. is still a full degree above normal - had bkfts. with Jim about 8 - then I sat in my room till near 11 - then to Manhattan Storage - then to Douglas's - then to studio + back to Touraine at 1 o'clk - fixed some lunch for myself - then a nap in my chair + at 4 o'clk went to Scribner's + had a talk with Mr. H. L. Smith about Jim - then to Brooks bro's but found nothing that suited me - then to my Rooms + read every paper till Jim came in at 6.30 - dinner soon after 7. At 8.30 I went to Charle's + stayed till 10 - Jim was out with some of his chums.

## Right Page

### Thur. Nov. 9, 1905

**Weather:** Pleasant day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkfp. at 8 - soon after 9 went to studio + then to 110 before 10 o'clk - soon Manhattan Storage van, with men came + the last remnants were taken from the house mirror in hall - one in back bay window - war file of newspapers, bound + in case, gas-log etc - then to studio - there, filled four boxes with books for our Rooms - + a lot of other things - my writing desk, bellows for sofa - andirons etc - These studio things were taken to our apartment, the others, to storage - got through that job soon after 12 - then a rest + after 1 o'clk. had lunch - immediately after that, went to "West." hospital + saw Dr. Brown - my interview with him was cut short by Dr. Kinnicut - then I had a visit with Jessie + back to my Rooms after 3 o'clk + had a nap. Then putting writing desk in order. Dinner at 7 - Eve'g Jim did a lot of work, assorting books \+ putting many of them in the cases
