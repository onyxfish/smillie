## Left Page

### Tues. March 7, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beastly day - slushy snow \+ rain - I had some writing to do after 8 o'clk. \+ did not get to studio till after 10. Then I worked with my photos - mounted some - Home to lunch - back soon after 2 \+ worked on pen sketch design for Ex-Libris Plate for Miss Anna E. R\. L\. ker. At 5 o'clk I trudged thro' the slush \+ sleet to her home 53 W. 47 S.; Geo went with me as far as Century - I submitted my sketch to Miss R. \+ think she was pleased - Home at about 6 - dinner at 6.30 - Ev'g in front of a pleasant fire - Time divided between Reading about the great strike of employees on the elevated R.R. in the subway \+ writing.

## Right Page

### Wed. March 8, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very disagreeable day - Rain, fog \+ slush - Got to studio late - very dark - I worked all day upon photos - most of the time retouching upon negatives requiring treatment of one kind or another - Home to lunch \+ back. Could not get to work after 4 o'clk - Just as I was leaving studio, at about 5 o'clk., Mr. K on behalf of Newark Public Library came \+ arranged with me to send "The River's Source" to an ex'n to be held there. Dinner. Ev'g. Velox printing till 9.30 - then to Chas's. Mary, her girls \+ Geo. there - C. is to go to Florida to-morrow for a stay of three or four weeks - Home at 10.30 - Some more printing - to bed at midnight.
