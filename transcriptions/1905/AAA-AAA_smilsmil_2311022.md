## Left Page

### Wed. July 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

lunched at Mr. Kent's

Still very hot in the sunshine - with still a refreshing breeze - bkfst. at 8 - after that, with some difficulty we succeeded in telephoning to Mr. Kent in Montrose + were invited to lunch - Mr. Ballantine ordered carriage + we were sent in to town + got to the Kents before noon + were very cordially received - Mr. White + her two children are now with Mr. K. + Charlotte - we walked about the grounds near the house before lunch - while at lunch a very heavy thunderstorm passed over + rain continued for some time after so that we had no chance for further walking - we left at about 4 - went to Mollie Sayres. Saw her + Jessie Edgar + several young people - then to the Jessups + saw Louise + her aunt Jenny - there to Cox's stable + started from there at 5 - stopped at Dud. Lathrop's + saw Mr. L. + Mr. Isaac Riley - then on to Ballantines + got there ahead of a storm (thunder) that left some fine sunset effects - dinner at 6 - Evt. H. Dr. Gardner came to lance the nurse's hand - he had some trouble with her - she was in such a nervous state - struck his hand + made him cut himself - Tobed at 10.

## Right Page

### Thur. July 20, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Albany - the New Kenmore - Room No. 214.

Glorious mg. after a cool night - Ther. fell to 55°. I got up at 6.30 - packed bag - + at 7.30 went down + walked awhile on road - then on one of the Southern porches - Ther. 66° - the air was delightful - the sky a wonder of freshness + color + the wooded hills stretching away into dim distance, charming beyond description - I paced there till called in to bkfst. at 8 - carriage came just before 9 - + we got to Montrose R.R. Station at 9.40 - train left soon after - close connection - got to Binghamton soon after 11 - I opened my trunk in station + took out a heavier suit, carried it to the Arlington + changed it for the one I had on - then cab to station with the luggage one, put that in my trunk + had R's trunk + mine trans. to D.N.H. Station - loafed about for awhile - there to Arlington at 1 - had dinner - then again loafing till 2.30 when train started for Albany where we arr. at 6.40 after a comfortable journey - went at once to Kenmore - + soon after had our dinner - at 8 wandered out + up to the State House + through its lower corridors - past the new hotel Ten Eyck - sorry that we did not go there - the Kenmore is too old + fusty a place -
