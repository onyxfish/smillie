## Left Page

### Thur. May 4, 1905

**Weather:** Overcast, enervating m't, but weather changed to windy & cold.  **Temp:** [illegible]

To studio at 10.30 — Spent the remainder of m'f working up mastic powder for aquatint ground & then using it in my big box in laying ground on old Soft ground Plate, a road chapel near Davano. Home at 1 o'clk — Uncle W. & Aunt A. there just down from Po'k — They lunched with us. At 2.30 I went to University Club to see the Siddons Mowbray decoration of the library — charmingly harmonious to my eye — met Geo. there — also a number of the artists. Then with Blashfield to his studio in Carnegie Bld't & was much interested in work that he has underway for a church in Bkl'n & for another western State House — then together we went to H.O. Walkers exh'n of his Minnesota State House mural painting which greatly disappointed me — I do not think it good in any way — It is far too large a work for him to undertake — Then I met Mrs. Walker, Mr. Buck & Mr. Gage of Hackly & Catharine & Emma Ireland — Then back to Carnegie to get my umbrella left in Blashfields — went in to H.R. Butlers — found H'f Osborn Taylor there sitting for portrait. Pleasant little visit. Then Geo & I walked down 5' Ave. together — I went home — dinner at usual time — Geo. \+ Charl came in during the eve'g.

## Right Page

### Fri. May 5, 1905

**Weather:** not altogether pleasant — Strong wind at times & rather chilly — veiled sunshine for a short time, late when I got to studio.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Started biting on aqua-tint of yesterday — did not like the way it was going & cleared it off — Then, after that, all day pussing with efforts to get a satisfactory ground on the plate, without success — I could not get an even distribution of dust over the plate — Home to lunch — after that went to bank — on my way back to studio stopped at The Marquis" an apart-ment hotel in 31st. bet. Mad. & 5th Ave. & saw the desert Manager Mr. Albert Bang — he showed me a couple of apartments — 3 rooms & bath room on south side of building that were very comfortable & homelike in appearance — one suite $150 per month the other, larger rooms, $225. — I was very favorably impressed — Then to studio & continued my unsuccessful efforts to get a good aqua-tint gr'd. Home at 6 — dinner at 6.30 — Nell & I wrote to the boys — Charl came in.
