## Left Page

### Mon. Feb. 13, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — more bad weather — more headache — I got to studio at 10 \+ being unfit for anything else I spent the m\[orning\] mounting photos — Then home to lunch — I had wished in the afternoon to be able to go to Annual meeting, Met. Mus. of Art — Reading by T. C. Williams at Mr. Sidney Smith's \+ Recep\[tion\] at Walter Cook's \+ Mr. Sallie Munroe — but instead I went to bed — \+ lay there till the dinner bell rang at 6.30 — Emma J. came in to dinner — I was able to be up immediately after dinner Emma was hurried into the girl's back-parlor room \+ I sat alone in the parlor till 9 — Then, beginning to feel that I had been alone long enough I went to my own room \+ later, to bed feeling very unhappy.

## Right Page

### Tues. Feb. 14, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudless \+ exceedingly cold — To studio at usual time \+ commenced the drawing in upon the plate of Ponte Marie — Paris — Jane \+ Mary Hinshelwood came in for a few minutes on their way to Po\[r\]k. for a brief visit — I had a short walk before lunch — \+ again after it — worked on P.-M. as long as I could see — near 4.20 but did not quite finish it as I had given time to several other things — I got Water-Color drawing, Near San Remo, ready for the Rotary Exhib. \+ I cleaned a couple of Pestle \+ Mortars by boiling them in lye — This is preparatory to making Mastic dust for Aqua-tint — After 5 I took a short walk but the cold \+ wind was so bitter that I did not enjoy it — Home at 6., dinner — Ev\[en\]g by a big fire \+ wrote a note to Louise Cook (Mr. Walter) explaining why I had not called there or been at her reception yesterday. To bed early.
