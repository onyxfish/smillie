## Left Page

### Fri. Feb. 17, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

In bed all day. My fever (102¾°) began to abate at about 3 A.M. \+ I got a very little restless sleep after that before m'f. The pain in muscles \+ joints made me quite helpless \+ my headache was of a depressing sort — The fever passed away during the day \+ I drowsed much of the time. Jane \+ Patty \+ Walter came to dine as invited by K. \+ E. From 11 to 1 o'clk P.M. \+ A.M. I was able to sit propped up in my bed \+ read — then I turned in for the night.

## Right Page

### Sat. Feb. 18, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day but still windy \+ uncomfortably cold. I was able to get up \+ go down to my blest. a little late but the day passed in uncomfortable loafing about the house with head, back \+ limbs all sore. I went out to Park Ave for about ten minutes at noon but was not strenuous enough for the strong winded that cut my face. Again I went out for another 10 m. bet. 1 \+ 2 o'clk. R\[illegible\] called for a few minutes before lunch — he is not in much better physical condition than I am. Ralph had sent a note to Mary, rec'd. this m'f., saying he expected to take dinner with us this ev'g. \+ w'd. bring a school fellow. So Bessie had got up a fine dinner for him. We waited dinner till 6.45 — no Ralph — then we sat down — at 7.° a telegram came — "Do not wait for us — Smillie" — dated Po'keepsie depot — this puzzled me — after dinner we went to Charle \+ on his phone I called up Hackley \+ learned that R. \+ his Hockey team was at Po'k. playing the Riverview boys \+ were belated by some R.R. train failure — Clark showed us some new lantern slides — as I felt sick \+ tired I went home alone soon after.
