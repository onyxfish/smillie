## Left Page

### Mon. May 22, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bessie started for the Ark. —
Delightful day. B\&p. at 7.30 — went to Ap't Cent. station with Bessie at 8.30 \+ saw her started for the Ark — Went to Miller's thence to studio \+ tried again for a time to get a liquid aqua-tint ground over the water on the Constance tower, but it wouldn't work so, reluctantly, I determined to scrape it clean again — Home to lunch — after that went to bank — \+ then to studio — Scraped all the work off the plate from the shore lines down, preparatory to another effort — Dinner at 6.30 Eve'g. printing from Greenwood negatives.

## Right Page

### Tues. May 23, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

fine day — milder — To studio at 10.30 Spent some time cleaning \+ trimming photos, printed last night — C.A.A. Düring came in \+ took much time telling me he is to be married next week (old fool — he has lived more years than I have) \+ telling the trials he is going thro' now \+ the joys he expects in the future. He has had a great row with Carl, his nephew \+ showed me an anonymous note of warning that was sent to the bride elect after the [illegible]. I went to Pease \+ Elliman's \+ saw Mr. E — \+ formally put the letting of 110 in his hands — he is to send a Referee's arbitrature to look at the house — Home to lunch — at 2 P.G.E.'s young man came — went over the house with me — At 3 I went to La Touraine \+ saw the manager — He showed me some Rooms that pleased me very much — in some ways more than those in de Marquis — I am to see him again after June 1st — To studio at 3.30 mounted a lot of photos — Chas. Collins made me a visit — Home to dinner at 6.30 found that P.\&S. had put up "To Rent" on 110 — I had not agreed to this \+ took it down.
