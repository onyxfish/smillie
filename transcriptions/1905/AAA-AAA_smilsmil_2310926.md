## Left Page

### Fri. Jan. 6, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A horrible day of sleet - slush, wind \+ Rain — the day was too dark \+ bad for work — so I gave it up to photography for a change \+ all day, after 10.30 I was arranging negatives \+ taking trial prints. I was working with Berlin views — very dense negatives — also Milan — \+ I had to take a long time-exposure for each print, from 5 to 15 m. I got home to lunch at 1.30 — streets in a horrible condition — every thing coated with ice — back to studio soon after 2 \+ worked steadily till 6.30 — then home to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g, more work with photos, numbering \+ arranging negatives

## Right Page

### Sat. Jan. 7, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A day of clouds \+ sunshine, more clouds than sunshine \+ some cold, unpleasant wind — I got to studio before 10 — set palette \+ made some slight changes — heightening the light on a middle foreground tree — Then Kramer came \+ got Borden photo-gravure plate to cut down the margin — He stayed for some time — then I cut some masks for crooked negatives — so that they wd. print straight \+ with white margins — Home to lunch at 1.30 — Soon after 2 went to 2d. Nat'l Bank — To studio before 3 — made an arrangement of glass, supported at an angle, upon which I can work upon defective negatives. About 4 I began to print \+ kept at it till after 6 — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g Mary, her girls \+ Jim, went to Chas's — I stayed at home with Ralph who is much under the weather from a cold.
