## Left Page

### Fri. Oct. 27, 1905

**Weather:** Beautiful m'g., bright sunshine but wind in wrong direction - n. easterly - clouded afternoon  **Temp:** [illegible]

After an uneasy night - I woke with severe headache, so severe that I asked that my room might be kept dark \+ I left undisturbed When miss Crandall came I asked for a cup of clear coffee \+ drank it — Dr. Brown came at about 10 \+ gave me a powder. \+ advised me to go on the Roof. at 11, feeling much better I dressed \+ in Katharine T— came \+ Ros having fixed me in my chair we went to the roof - under the lee of the sunparlor it was very pleasant. K. sat with me Till 12 — Ros came for me at about 1 — Then I had a dinner of oat meal gruel, Toast \+ fruit. Then, att 5 o'clk I sat in my chair \+ wrote to Dr. Garvin, Katie Selkin etc. or I wandered up \+ down the chilly corridor, or I read — At 5 I went to bed — at 6.30 I had beef steak etc — the eve'g. dragged along — a slight return of headache It was 9 when Ed. Cook came in \+ later Jim came It was after 10 when he left.

## Right Page

### Sat. Oct. 28, 1905

**Weather:** An ambiguous day — showers promised but they did not come — There was pale sunshine about noon \+ again later in the afternoon when it was hazy, mild \+ still — The day went much like those before it except that I was up \+ dressed for a longer time \+ had more exercise.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dr. Brown called in the m'g \+ seemed satisfied that I am making progress but I cannot find the evidence of it. Ros gave me my bath before 11 — Then, after a rest, I dressed \+ at noon went up to the Roof \+ walked there for some time — down in time for dinner after 1 o'clk. The afternoon was a tiresome loaf — reading \+ writing — Jessie came in two or three times — Ge. came in the m'g to tell of a man who wishes to be furnace man at studio — Clarence Smitte came in at about 6 \+ made a pleasant social call — no business. I kept about Till 8 — Then to bed — Jim came in after 9 — stayed Till 10 — Then I fixed myself for the night — nurses very busy — Two serious cases on this corridor — a young doctor, pneumonia — \+ a young woman — Typhoid. At midnight I had to ring for Collins — \+ again at 3 — The response of bowels to a cathartic Tablet given at 10 a.m. Otherwise I slept fairly well, dreaming that I went to a theatre with Annie.
