## Left Page

### Fri. May 26, 1905

**Weather:** Pleasant, altho' there were threats of change overcast sky \+ east wind  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- at night a few drops of rain fell \- To studio's at usual time \+ spent the m't there writing a letter to Kate \- Home to lunch at 1 \- back at 2.30 \- wrote to Ralph \+ at 4 went to Fine Arts Bldg in W. 57' St \- met Emma I there by appointment \- \+ saw Jw. La Farge new painting for the Minneapolis State Ho. court room \- Socrates with his friends \- it is not a pleasing work to my mind \- The color is muddy \+ inharmonious to my eye \- The perspective is forced \- foreground figures too large \- the draperies are like hammered metal \+ the shadows are without reflected light, yet withal, there is a distinction about it that lifts it, inspires criticism, to a high plane \- we met \+ had pleasant chats with several people \- Mr. La Farge, W. \+ Mrs. Blashfield \- Dielman \+ others \- It was near 6 when we left; walked into Cent. Park at 7th Ave. entrance then east \+ out at 5th Ave. entrance \- Emma took Mad. Ave. car \+ I walked home \- Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g I wrote to Jim \-

## Right Page

### Sat. May 27, 1905

**Weather:** Smuggy, half cloudy day  **Temp:** [illegible]

\- To studio's as usual \+ spent the m't. Trying to make something out of the "Constance, Tower on the Rhine" \- Again, as before, I have failed to get snap \+ force enough in the biting \- The spontaneity of the work is gone \- it is ground in upon me that my working days are past \- By worrying over the water with a Rulette I have made it passable merely. Home to lunch at 1 \- but before I got it Mr. Lawrence \+ her daughter came with a permit to see the house \- \+ I showed it to them \- it took one hour \- but I doubt if they will rent me house \- the rent is too high \- To studio at 2.45 \- took some prints from Tower. working between times \- a short walk \- met Chas. Collins \+ walked with him Dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g wasted with newspapers.
