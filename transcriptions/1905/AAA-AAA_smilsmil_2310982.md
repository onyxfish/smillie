## Left Page

### Sun. April 30, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A charming day — April without showers altho' there were lots of beautiful clouds. It was cool \+ breezy — A good scout \+ then bkfp'. at 9 o'clk \+ before 11 I started out on a discovery tramp — took subway train to end of lenox branch at 145th St. Then west to 9th Ave. \+ took elevated to 155th. Then west again by 155th St. viaduct — past Old Trinity Cemetery down to the River where I was interested in viewing driveway improvements under way — following this road up to inst. for Deaf \+ Dumb — Then retraced steps to 157. street entrance to subway \+ home, in time for dinner at 2 — After that loafed away some time \+ then got ready for some Velox printing — I stuck to it till about 4 P.M. — but something went wrong some where \+ I did not get good Results — Then I went to Charl's where many other girls were — near 11 when I got home.

## Right Page

### Mon. May 1, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, but windy \+ almost cold — low temp. \+ snow reported from upstate \+ west. After bkfp'. I had accts \+ writing that must be attended to \+ it was near 11 before I got to studio. Yale came in shortly after 12 \+ little was done before I went home at 1 — ate no lunch — Stomach out of sorts in some way — Went to bed \+ stayed there till about 6 — then up and wrote a birth-day congratulation to Kate — Then out \+ mailed it — dinner at 6.30. Evg. Charl came in \+ reported interview with Beattie, a Col. mining engineer who has been looking after Sheldon — He greatly deplores the marriage — while he can learn nothing to the discredit of the girl he thinks that S. has injured himself socially more than in a business way — B. describes the girl as of very ordinary type — not good looking refined or educated — But he declares that S. has the makings of a high grade mining engineer \+ he has faith to believe that he will yet prove a success.
