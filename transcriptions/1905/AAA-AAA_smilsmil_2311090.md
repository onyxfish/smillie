## Left Page

### Fri. Dec. 8, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day, mild \+ sunny — a big circle around the moon — with other signs during the day — portend bad weather — I hope I shall not be prevented going to New-Haven to-morrow — I wrote to Katie Elkin \+ Ralph to expect me on the 3 o'clk. 24p. Other matters kept me busy till I went to lunch at Century — a considerable part of the afternoon was spent unprofitably in taking a nap — a growing vice — another evidence of the encroachments of age — Dinner with Jim \+ eve'g passed in uneventful way in my rooms.

## Right Page

### Sat. Dec. 9, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull, threatening weather — \+ [illegible] at usual time with Jim — Before so I went down-town to the Hamburg-Am. Co's office \+ secured State-room 88 on Str. Princessin Victoria Louise for her West Indian Trip, Feb'y 6" — then to Charl's office \+ was there for awhile — then to Findler \+ Wibes \+ bot. diary for 1906 ( will I live that year out? ) then to Touraine — had lunch — \+ at 3 o'clk. started for New-Haven, getting there at 5 — Ralph met me at Station \+ together we went to the Elkin's — my bed room there has been greatly improved \+ enlarged since I was last there — it now has a fire place \+ a gas-log — the bed is delightful — we had dinner after 6 — about 9 Ralph was sleepy \+ he was excused — about 10 I turned in \+ had a very comfortable night's rest.
