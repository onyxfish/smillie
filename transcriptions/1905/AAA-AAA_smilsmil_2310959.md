## Left Page

### Wed. March 15, 1905

**Weather:** Fine day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day — about 11 when I got to studio having written letters to Jane Hinshelwood \+ Raefolk after 6\[?]fo's. I worked about getting ready to begin the etching of miss Roelker's book plate. At 12.30 I went to Buskink's — 6th Ave near 34 St. \+ got some writing paper — then home to lunch. To studio at 2.30. Fooling around till after 4 — then to Lefferts — 2.20 St. \+ got iron stand left there yesterday to be galvanized — back to studio \+ got copper plate cut for Mils R's book plate. Home at 6 \+ rested till 6.30 — dinner Evef. after my newspaper I went over my check stubs \+ struck a bal. in accord with bank figures —

Gene Atwater lunched with us.

## Right Page

### Thur. March 16, 1905

**Weather:** Bright m'f  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright m'f — then overcast with raw wind another bad night — I got up much out of sorts \+ discouraged — I went to studio but could not work — Put in some time in experimenting with new solutions that change black velox prints to Sepia tone. I had a short walk before I went home to lunch — after lunch I went to bed \+ remained there till after 5 — then up \+ went to studio for a short time — Home to dinner at 6.30 — Evef. I wrote for some time a list of photo. views taken last summer —
