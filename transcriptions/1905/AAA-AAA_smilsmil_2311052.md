## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 17, 1905

**Weather:** Out of bed for awhile.  **Temp:** [illegible]

A nasty day - leaden clouds hanging low \+ boistrous wind - I awoke after a comfortable night, without pain \+ fever reduced - 99 7/10° - I got up \+ had a decent Toilette - The family was very late on my blsp' - was brought up just as the Dr. came, about 9.30 - I did not get it till after the Dr. protracted visit - later Ralph lighted a fire in the studio Franklin \+ fixed my reclining ch[air] for me \+ I transferred myself to it \+ remained there till after 3 o'clk, when, bedroom having been well aired I returned to it - The room was darkened so that I might sleep but sleep refused such blandishments \+ at night I was long getting to sleep but it came at last but was only a fair to middlin' specimen.

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 18, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** normal

An unpleasant time - weather lowering \+ high wind - some rain - Dr. Garvin found me rejoicing in a return to normal Temp. but regretting that it did not bring with it a cessation of troubling - I really feel more uncomfortable to-day than I did yesterday with nearly 2° more Temp. The Dr. made a long call \+ we had some interesting conversation - I like the Dr. I had some chicken - \+ egg nogg - \+ peptonized milk \+ altogether think I was overfed, but all urged me to eat to regain lost strength - \+ so I did - Bradshaw did not come to-day \+ work was at a standstill - Self I spent on lounge in large room.
