## Left Page

### Thur. Dec. 14, 1905

**Weather:** fine  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine, sunny day, but colder than yesterday - In spite of the soreness from the bang I got last night I had a fairly comfortable night but was dreadfully sore \+ crippled when I got up - Pain thro' the chest w°. catch me spasmodically at times so as almost to take my breath. I got thro' bkf. \+ worried over accts. till noon. Then to Man.° Storage to complain agt. sending to me bills for collection two or three weeks after they had been paid. Then to Studio - \+ at 1 back to Lorraine lunch at 2 - more worrying over accts. at 4 again went to studio - back at 5. Then a short nap on the new mattress that Dock sent in to-day for my Rattan lounge - Dinner at 7 - more worrying \+ to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### Fri. Dec. 15, 1905

**Weather:** overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast, raw \+ windy \+ very cold - I had a hard time last night between my sore ribs on the right side \+ my lame rheumatic shoulder on the left. I managed to get up \+ have bkft. with Jim - At 9.30 I went to Dr. Tuttle's - I stripped for him \+ he felt carefully over my ribs \+ then said it was possible there was a slight fracture of one - he strapped me so closely with adhesive plaster that I had hard work to breathe - Then returned to Lorraine - Katie Elkin came in soon after \+ made a pleasant call - I had lunch at about 2 - Did not go out again during the day - so stiff, sore \+ bandaged up that I could not do anything - Read Bret Harte till I was tired - did not go down to dinner with Jim - went without - Merrill Bishops came \+ spent most of the eveg. with Jim.
