## Left Page

### Thur. May 18, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A slow, clearing day, with clouds + some sunshine. To studio at 10 + spent the day on "lower on Rhine at Constance" — slow work in getting a dry aqua-tint ground to match water to other portions of plate — then to bite it — when biting I found that the central + darker portion must have a larger + deeper grain — so, more time was spent in trying to get such grain with liquid ground — Experimenting is interesting but discouragingly slow — Home to lunch + back — worked till 5.30 — then a walk — Dinner at 6.30 — Euel f. ## Right Page

### Fri. May 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day inspite of cloudiness — 10.30 when I got to studio — continued experimenting on Constance plate — at last I got a dry grd. on plate + bit that, but it is unsatisfactory in texture — I shall have to try some new device to improve the texture + depth of biting in central + darkest portion of reflections — Home to lunch at 1 — I had a call from a young man from Real Estate office of Frothinghiam + Moore on behalf of a client who wishes to rent, unfurnished, a house in the neighborhood of $10 — He is to call at the house to-morrow m'f. I wet down paper for printing to-morrow + then walked the streets for half an hour before dinner at 6.30. Ge° gave me a letter to read to Mary, from Sheldon's wife to his grandmother, Mrs. Jacobs. It gives an acct. of her family now living, her mother + her eight brothers + sisters — Their location + occupation + something of herself + her early acquaintance with Sheldon — The information, so far as it went, was satisfactory — as a specimen of English it was not above reproach — It was taken to Charl's in the ev'f. Later, Geo came in for awhile — I wrote to Jim + to bed early.
