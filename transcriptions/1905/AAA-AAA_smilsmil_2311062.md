## Left Page

### Sat. Oct. 7, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bright, sunny \+ quite cool — my window open all day while I sheltered myself with an extra blanket — It was an uncomfortable day owing to a dismal headache — Twice blood was taken from the lobe of my ear for laboratory examination. There was the usual round of visits from doctors, nurses \+ attendants — I wrote a short letter to Emma Ireland. Roz gave me a good bath — Late in the afternoon Charl came \+ reported progress. Katharine \+ Elizabeth arr. from St. Huberts this m'g — E. their mother are at Park Ave. Hotel — K. is at his house — Furniture has been moved from Storage to Touraine — Geo. \+ Nellie have been arranging it, helped later by Ralph who has come down from New Haven. — In the eve'g Jessie \+ Lizzie Smillia came in for awhile — \+ Later, Jim \+ Ralph — They seem in very good spirits \+ pleased with the Touraine apartment. They stayed till near 10, after which I turned in for the night.

## Right Page

### Sun. Oct. 8, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still fine weather — quite remarkable — I commenced the day early, miss Mosely \+ Collins coming to me before 7 — I still have discomfort in my head — Jessie came in before going to church \+ made a sunshiny call — Young Edw. Cook came between 12 \+ 1 \+ he made a pleasant call — The Regular calls by doctors nurses \+ orderlies came in their turn — Roz gave me a good bath \+ I had gruel, milk, broth \+ etc. etc. — at short intervals — In the afternoon Jessie \+ Lizzie came in \+ Jim \+ Ralph \+ Then George — all at one time — but after that there were no more visitors \+ the even'g was rather dismal.
