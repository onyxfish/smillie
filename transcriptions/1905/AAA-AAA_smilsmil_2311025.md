## Left Page

### Tues. July 25, 1905

**Weather:** Very cool. Cloudy m't. clearing. some beautiful sunny hours + several showers.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim arrived —

After bkfst. I went out to Moody + was with him sometime in the garden — I spent much of the m't. taking weeds out of the sod on hillside south — Katharine, Elizabeth + Ralph went with Maghees to East Hill + had their dinner there — Mary + I had dinner at 1 o'clk — I poked about the place all the afternoon — The East Hill party ret'd before 6 — supper at 6.30 — Jim arrived by stage from E-Town after 7 o'clk — He has come quite directly from Bar Harbor where he has been having "the time of his life" for some weeks past with his friend Harold How. Eve'g, gossiping.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 26, 1905

**Weather:** Some sunshine — more clouds + several cold showers. Ther. indoors, not above 65°.  **Temp:** [illegible]

P.t.b. at 8 — after that working in the garden + getting Moody to work — later, I made fire in my bedroom + wrote a letter to Mr. Kent. Dinner at 1 — wrote to Mr. Paul. Sayre + to H.M. Smith (about insurance) — then went down to the P. O., posted letters, got a money order for Mary + then walked up the hill, home. Tea at 6.30 — Eve'g. read a little of Mr. Blashfield's "Italian Cities" + wondered if many people read such books, treating in a rhapsodical way of the childish art of early Italian days. I think her style of writing may be described as florid + involved — it does not impress me as being serious or, in any degree, profound — I have read but little of her writing — this was a disappointment to me.
