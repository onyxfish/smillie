## Left Page

### Fri. Sept. 29, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day \- just the kind most desirable for the journey planned for to-day. I had a comfortable night, being disturbed but twice \- bladder symptoms much better. Temp. at 6 A.M. 99 3/10°. I spent the m'f as sluggishly as possible \- gave up the wotrop\- because it upset my digestion, but when the D. came, early in the afternoon, he said I must not drop it altogether. Smaller doses \+ more water \- he seemed greatly pleased at my progress \+ said he felt sure I could diseat packing to-morrow and start out Sunday p.m. I wrote a letter to Ralph in the m'f. ## Right Page

### Sat. Sept. 30, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** normal \- out of bed

News Rec'd of Kate's death in Dresden to-day.

An uncomfortably hot day \- I awoke at 5.30 Temp. 1/5° above normal \- after bkfs'. got up \+ dressed \- my Cathartic reported satisfactorily at about 10\. Then I had big chair, with pillows \+ umbrella, moved to sunny upper east porch there I felt unusually comfortable till near 1 o'clk. I wrote a letter to Yale \+ was consulting engineer with Katharine in packing my trunk. While the family were at dinner I changed my clothes for the ones I am to wear to-morrow to facilitate packing \- then dinner \- then Garvin came, said Temp. was normal \+ was greatly pleased. Bashaw \+ Moody finished transplanting cedar this m'f \+ the afternoon, under my direction packed case in studio \- furiously hot \+ felt the drag of the work upon me pronouncedly \- the packing was finished as far a could be before 5 o'clk \- then I undressed \+ went to bed feeling very uncomfortable \+ felt better to a small degree only as the night wore on. About 10 p.m. \- another clearing out of the bowels.

Word Rec'd. that D. duBois reached his home in Essex very comfortably.
