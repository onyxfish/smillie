## Left Page

### Wed. June 7, 1905

**Weather:** Cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy on f. changing to rain - very heavy in the afternoon with thunder \+ lightening. Bkfs. at 8.30 - It was near 10 before I got to the house - Nellie came to look thro' it \+ prepare to take possession - Ge° will occupy it thro' the summer, camping out, using some furniture that I shall let him have if he finds an apartment that he can take next winter - He is to occupy 110, as a sort of care-taker till a tenant is found to take it. I spent the mf. till 12 writing to Katie Elkin, to Jim \+ others - then to studio - about 1 I had a little lunch in the studio - I spent the afternoon there, packing up - to 110 at about 6 \+ dressed as I learned that Prof. Leon Girard, the Belgian expert electrician, who has some one here to investigate the "Electric mule" for towing on Canals, was to dine with Charl - at 7 I went to Ch's - Prof. G. came 15 m. late - of course we had a very good dinner \+ the Prof. proved to be an entertaining man - very genial - withal caustic - using, with great fluency, a form of hybrid English that, at times, it was impossible for me to understand - It was 12 o'clk before he left \+ I went to bed very tired.

Apart of my morning's work was a call at Pease \+ Elliman's - Saw 2 —. Then I went to several apartment hotels bct. 5° \+ 6° Aves \+ 43° \+ 46° S° - was told that I could find good apartments there, much cheaper than on the east side, but I failed to find them - The Hotel Webster in 45° S°. has charming entrance \+ very pleasant \+ convenient suits - southern exposure - $180 per an. Touraine apt. is $200 - Webster is too far from studio - I decided to take the T. apt. - went there - saw manager - he agreed to Repaper parlor \+ library \+ I engaged the suite - to take possession Oct. 1.

## Right Page

### Thur. June 8, 1905

**Weather:** Cold, cloudy  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cold, cloudy on f., but by noon it cleared \+ the afternoon was brilliant \+ delicious. Bkfs. at 9 - I got to the house at about 10 \+ was there a good part of the mf. - then to studio - a light lunch there - then to a Wall Paper Place, Cor. 5° Ave. \+ 38° S°. and selected a bucram for Touraine wall - upon which an estimate is to be made - then to Alexander's - Sixth Ave. \+ 19° S°. \+ ordered shots. Then to 18° S°. \+ 3° Ave. corn plasters - then studio \+ worked there till after 5 - then to 110 - \+ had a half hours rest on my bed - there to Charl's - dinner at 7 - Prof. Girard came \+ stayed till 11.30 - he had been sight seeing during the day with Charl \+ was greatly interested - To bed before midnight.

Ge° Nellie, Val \+ Gordon took possession of 110 - It was homelike to see them at their dinner - It was this mf., not yesterday as written, that I wrote to Katie Elkin \+ [illegible] Jim.
