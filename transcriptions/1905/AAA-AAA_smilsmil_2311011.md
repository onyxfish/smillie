## Left Page

### Tues. June 27, 1905

**Weather:** A wonderful day — very cool, breezy — cloudy in the mfr. with one light dash of a shower \+ a glorious afternoon —  **Temp:** Bhlp — at 8. \+ very soon after I walked down to Alumni Hall where the Alumni held their Annual meeting — Pres. made the opening address (In my writing I have made the mistake of getting dates confused — my work has been so strenuous that at the end of two days the incidents have tumbled over one another — The Alumni meeting was this morning, not yesterday as I have already recorded it — see yesterday.) I got back to Elkins' \+ had an early lunch \+ after that Katie, Mollie, William \+ I started for the ball game getting to the field at about 2.30 — William stopped by the way \+ got his cousin, Mr. Minkley \+ Jim joined us on the stand — our seats were well placed to the left of the batsman where we had a very good view of the field \+ were under cover — The arrival of the various classes, several in costume, \+ with music, was exciting — The class of '80 in kilts, had a big brown pic for mascot \+ managed to get a lot of fun out of it — The crowd was immense, estimated at 12000 — a brilliant picture — Play commenced at 3 \+ was very closely fought as the College Championship depends upon it — Harvard scored 1 in the first inning — 2 \+ 3 innings, neither side scored \+\+, 5\+ 6\. Yale scored 2, 2, 3 — The wildest excitement, prevailing at times — on one double run it was simply indescribable — on the 7 innings Harvard made one — 8\+ 9\+ none — final Yale 7 — Harvard 2 — Then pandemonium — Yale Capt \+ pitchers — (Bowman, 1905. S. — \+ Jackson, 1906. S.) were carried off the field on the shoulders of grads — all the classes with their bands took possession of the field — a mass of singing, howling, procession-dancing — new — We went back to town without difficulty \+ by invitation to Miss Walkers, next door to Pres. Hadley's house, \+ there saw several of the classes come up, salute the Pres. \+ got a little speech from him — We got back to Dr. Elkins at about 7 \+ had a belated dinner — A quiet even — Looking at some of Mollie's Remarkable photos made in the N. W. \+ Mexico.

## Right Page

### Wed. June 28, 1905

**Weather:** Another superb day — Very cool m\+ — gradually growing warmer but at no time really hot.  **Temp:** Bhlp — at 8. Immediately after that I walked down to Vanderbilt, getting there at 9.30 \+ found Jim dressing altho 9.45 was the time set for grads. to take their places on the campus for the procession. Being told that as parent of a member of the graduating class I had a right to a place among the Alumni, I took my stand with graduates before 1860 — There was a long wait \+ it was after 10.30 before the procession started, going thro' the Vanderbilt arch out thro' the gate on to Chapel Street, down to the Green, across that from north to south, to north, thence to Woolsey Hall where we filed in \+ took seats just behind the grads — good places — The singing of the first four verses of Psalm LXV — a same version as at opening of first College in New Haven in 1718 was impressive — The conferring of prizes, degrees \+ Hon. degrees was formal \+ at times a trifle tedious — After all Fin Feste Burg — The old Luther Hymn was given with power by great organ, orchestra \+ large chorus — It was about 12.30 when the service was over — When we Elkins took me to the Yale Club where we had coffee \+ a sand wich \+ at 2, we marched with the class of 1903. The class he is identified with, into University Hall (Commons) where a simple dinner was served — There was a great crowd, the hall being crowded — after the dinner, Hadley introduced the speakers — Where we sat it was impossible to understand all that Hadley said — H\+W. Taft. '80. was the next speaker — I caught most of what he said — I got nothing of \+t \+ E. Howland — voice \+ enunciation so bad — Bishop Keator labored hard \+ got practically all he said but Edwin A. Alderman, Pres. of the University of Va. being a trained orator, I got, with pleas ure, all that he said — it was a delightful oration — His peroration — an appeal to the young men of the north to greet the young men of the south in a spirit of sympathy, high ambition \+ love of country was thrilling \+ carried the entire audience by storm — he was the star performer \+ it was long before the applause subsided — It broke out again when Hadley announced the gift of one million dollars from Mr. D. Rockefeller — To Elkins before 6 — had a short rest before dinner — Every Jim was with us till 10 — then to bed —
