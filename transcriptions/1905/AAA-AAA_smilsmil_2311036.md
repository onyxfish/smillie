## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 16, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Rained almost continuously thro' last night — dismae wet mt. cleared sudde about 2 o'clk — bright afternoon — cloudless, still cold moonlight — the Roar of the Rivers all about us — I got up \+ had my bkft. with the others feeling very slim — I had a fire in my bed room \+ loafed comfortably there — I wrote a note to Parke — Dinner at 1 — afternoon went with the boys to Scott Brown — not at home — then with them to Hotel Casino where I left the boys bowling \+ I went home. I read for awhile Jacobs "At Sunwich Port" — Supper — more Jacobs in my cosey bed room — \+ then some Piazza walking in the moonlight before going to bed.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 17, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A brilliantly beautiful day — immediately after bkft. Young from down the valley came to tell Jim \+ Ralph that a small party were in a carriage at the hotel waiting — \+w? The boys join them in a tramp up Gothic — \+ the boys promptly did so — Maghee came \+ took the young women on a tramp to Roaring brook fall — I gave some time to the garden — wrote to Pease \+ Seligman \+ to Wanamaker. \+ then went to P.O. Dinner at 1.30 — Maghee taking the place of the boys. Adams came \+ got to work painting on the house — Otis came in the mt. with a load of laundry wood — too long — he took it away — came later with a load of earth for road \+ later yet with load of wood — his days work. I spent some time getting levels on the ground ploughed for Tennis court — worked with Paul Moody making a model water-bar on the Road — worked till after sundown — Supper at about 7 — Eve'g I sat in my bed room \+ read Sunwich Port.
