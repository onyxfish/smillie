## Left Page

### Wed. Oct. 11, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

At last the good weather has come to an end cloudy m\+- easterly wind \+ Rain in the afternoon. After a very restful night I awoke at 6 oclk. Collins came in soon after — I washed up — then Mrs Mosler came \+ at 6.30 I had my first feed for the day — I felt much refreshed in every way. When D. Brown came he approved of my progress \+ said I might have some steak for dinner — after my bath I enjoyed my steak greatly — six times more than it co? be safe for me to eat was sent to me but I was cautious — If I had been more cautious eating fruit in the m\+ I might have had less trouble with my bowels — I had half a dozen raw oysters in the eve\+. Jessie very kindly came in to see me several times \+ brought me more books \+ flowers. Jim came in the eve\+ \+ brought me books from Charl's.

## Right Page

### Thur. Oct. 12, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very cold day, some sunshine — some snow — I awoke at 6 oclk. after a very restful night having wakened but once since 10 p.m. Collins \+ miss Mosely put in an appearance soon after \+ I commenced my day — it was spent much like preceding one — but I felt better — Pleas ant calls from Jessie \+ young Edw\+. Cook — I read my newspaper \+ a novel — while I was eating my beefsteak dinner Harold Howe came with a man from the Real Estate House with which he is connected with a proposition for the purchase of 110 — The man who w\+ like to buy is a speculator who proposes to put $5—$10 or $15000 on the house — then sell it — The man is to see the house to-morrow, after which he may make a definite offer — Dr. Tuthill came in the afternoon \+ gave me some time — explained what was thought to be my trouble \+ the hope that the building up of my strength would result in the passing of my trouble — there is inflammation of the bladder \+ possibly, to no great degree, of the right kidney — Later Geo. came in — then Katharine — then Jim, \+ yet later, Geo. Cook came \+ stayed till 9 — so, with reading \+ all, I had no chance to feel lonesome. During the day I wrote to Mrs H\+ m. Kent, at Montrose in response to a sympathetic letter rec\+ from her.
