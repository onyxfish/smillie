## Left Page

### Mon. Jan. 30, 1905

**Weather:** Overcast  **Temp:** [illegible]

Overcast m'f. coming off later clear \+ cold. I got up feeling fairly well but after 6&p'. when I prepared to go to studio I gave up \+ spent the rest of the m'f on my bed \- After lunch I went to studio but did little there \- home at 4 \- did not feel equal to going over the way to Mr. Sidney Smiths to hear Rev'd. T. C. Williams read some of his translations from Latin poets, but I lighted fire in parlor \+ sat there \+ read till dinner at 6.30. Eve'g. I worked over back accts. of Jim \+ Ralph till 10 \- then to bed.

## Right Page

### Tues. Jan. 31, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day but cutting cold \- I still feel, in some indescribable way, quite unfit for work \- my head is not clear \+ I have more or less discomfort or actual pain all over me \- I can think or worry about work but cannot overcome an indisposition to get at it \- Money matters worry me constantly \- I deny myself necessities but am ever under the drag of bills that I cannot pay except by drawing upon the principal of the estate. All of this I have to bear alone \- I can get no real, practical sympathy \- To studio at usual time \- I fussed about \- everything in great confusion \- I tried to "clear up" \- \+ worked awhile among photos. At 12.30 to bank \- home to lunch \- at 2.30 to studio \- worked there till 4.30 \- then home \+ rest\-ed till dinner at 6.30 \- Eve'g worked on accts. of Jim \+ Ralph \- Charl came in \+ stayed till 10.30
