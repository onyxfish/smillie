## Left Page

### Fri. Aug. 18, 1905

**Weather:** Charming day  **Temp:** [illegible]

Charming day. I was busy in ways of no moment after 4½ᵒ in the garden + on the road. Chas. Moody Rel. This mʼg. + Paul disappeared to my regret — + I had to repeat to Chas. my instructions as to construction of effective water breaks or bars. I wrote a note to Wm. Cullen + went with it to P.O. Rec'd a letter from Wm. G. Nelson — is not willing to sell any portion of his loved Windy-brow — fool Twaddle. The boys went down the valley on their bicycles. Juga went on a pic-nic — so, we had lunch in the middle of the day + dinner at night — on my way up from P.O. stopped at Maghee's garden — he was at work there — we walked up the hill together. After lunch I worked out of doors — on Road + getting levels on Tennis court. Wandered over Windy-brow till sunset — then home. Adams painted about piazza all day. Otis hauled 4 loads of dirt for Road. Dinner at 6.30. Evʼg. finished the reading of the Sunwick Port by Jacobs — mildly amusing.

## Right Page

### Sat. Aug. 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A pleasant day, barring a rising south wind that howled at night. After 4½ᵒ I finished the pyrography on the third panel for my bed-room mantle piece — the boys helping me by blowing the bellows. Then I wrote to Bridgeman for grass seed + to a fertilizing Co. in N.Y. Then Ralph + I revised the levels on the ploughed ground for Tennis court. Then I went to Post Office taking my Camera + getting two snakes at the Ask from below — one being at the Riverside — back + dinner after 1. The boys went down the valley to a river pool for a swim + were gone all the afternoon. I gave some time to Adams who painted about piazzas all day — + to Otis who brought four more loads of rotted rock. I worked for some time developing the photos taken this mʼg. + tinting with oil colors + benzine the two end panels for Mantle. Supper — Evʼg. Reading for a short time — my eyes trouble me so much now that it is painful for me to read more than half an hour at a time. The young peoples all went off to Maghee's + to the hotel.
