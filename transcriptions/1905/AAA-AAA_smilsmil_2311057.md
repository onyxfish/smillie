## Left Page

### Wed. Sept. 27, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

miserable dull day \- Temp at 7 A.M. 99 9/10° \- in the afternoon it went up to 103°\- At 10 o'clk, as requested by Dr. G, I sent Moody over to him with a specimen of my urine + note of temp. + pulse. I was startled when I saw the graduate glass in which 6 oz of urine had stood for a couple of hours \- Up to about the 3 oz mark + white precipitate had settled \- a fleecy mucus as white as milk\- . Sent calomel yesterday + a Seidlitz this m'f, I had a movement\- I kept to my bed quietly but fever increased, fell at 4 o'clk \- it was 103°. Between 5 + 6 Garvin came \- He said that tomorrow m'f he w'd wash out my bladder + that I must give up all hope of starting for town on Friday. Possibly I might go Monday, then R\- [illegible] go with me. He made exploration by way of rectum, of Prostrate + said that it was smaller than nine out of ten men of my age showed + was not in bad shape

## Right Page

### Thur. Sept. 28, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bladder washed out.

Temp. at 7 A.M. 100°\- when Garvin came som after 10 o'clk \- 99 9/10 \- at 5.30 p.m \- owing to the operation, it rose to 102 3/10\- The day was fine \- G\- may be a good doctor but he is a very clumsy one\- he swashed + spattered water + solutions all over the floor\- he dashed a lot of water twice over little table at head of my bed, wetting every thing out + knocking some things off out last he broke the glass tube connecting fountain with catheter + later I found splinters of glass in my bed \- notwithstanding, the operation was successful\- first I emptied my bladder naturally + then by catheter he tested for remains \- then was now completely empty \- good work\- the insertion of the soft catheter was comparatively painless \- then he made connection with the fountain bag, after breaking glass tube, + turned in a Boric acid solution\- then for the first, there was pain, not in the bladder, but along the whole length of the ureter \- it was as tho' I was seared with a hot iron \- the feeling continued for about three minutes \- was carried to the limit of my endurance\- I had to endure it four times \- twice with Boric acid \- once with extremely weak silver nitrate solution + then a final wash out \- I was about used up but felt relieved\- lots of burning water\- I took four 7½g\. doses of urotropin during the day + two ¼°\- doses of Quinine\- the urotropin completely upset my stomach \- Temp\- increased until well into the 4 then it fell\-

old V\. du Bois was carried on a cot to Essex, 30 miles away, To\-day.
