## Left Page

### Tues. Oct. 17, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still sunshine \+ open window — surely this is remarkable weather — The day for me commenced at 6 — with first food at 6.30 \+ then the usual routine. To-day I have read Maurice Hewlitt's New Canterbury Tales, getting a new "atmosphere." It interests me to note the entire difference of plane, color, key, — I don't know how to express it — of one book from another as I take one after another — one of Sean Raine's Welsh Stories — Kate Douglas Wiggin's fun — Hewlitt's old english — the absurd Masqueraders — Dt. Brown made a brief visit in the m'f but there was no afternoon visit from him or Dr. Tuthill — Jessie came in the afternoon — I feel more hopeful to-day. I think my symptoms are better — clearer urine \+ less irritation of the urethra — about 9 o'clk. I had a very pleasant call from Dt. Mason who came in \+ sat with me for awhile —

## Right Page

### Wed. Oct. 18, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Kate's body arr. at Quarantine to-night

Some rain \+ clouds in the m'f but sunshine came out before noon \+ later we had sunshine \+ open window — The day was started at the usual hour. D'Edw. Cook made an early call — about 9 — \+ Dt. Brown made his m'f rounds soon after — the gave me more time in a social way — I spent much of the m'f writing — sent a note to Scott Brown objecting to his charge for road material hauled to my place from the Chapel Pond roadside — I had a very enjoyable dinner of beef steak \+ potatoes — well on in the afternoon I had a breezy call from Emma Ireland — Dt. Tuthill came in unusually late \+ alone, but he gave me a lot of time \+ much encouraging information — Just as Collins began to prepare me for night rest, at about 9.30 Jim came in — the first visit from a member of my family since Sunday — He was unwell last night \+ had to work overtime at Scribner's this eve'f.
The "Rhein" with Kate's body arr. at Quarantine to-night but owing to fog did not come up to the city.
