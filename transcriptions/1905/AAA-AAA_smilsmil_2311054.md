## Left Page

### Thur. Sept. 21, 1905

**Weather:** A delightful day, brilliant &breezy.  **Temp:** [illegible]

was up early but not allowed to go down to bkfs\*. I had a severe headache when I awoke + when I got up found myself very weak. I was also bothered by persistent irration of the altogether I felt so discouraged that I wrote a note to D\. Graven practically asking him to make another professional call on me \- I went to garden + gathered corn + carrots \- I followed Bashaw + Moody who finished the fertilizing + reseeding \- Dinner at 1. I was up on roof + under the roof in attic to quiz to find traces of big leaks. Between 3 + I had a nap \- Then D\. G\- came + the lectured me for being down hearted + discouraged + together we concocted a bitter tonic. Lumber came + Bashaw is to commence work on kitchen closet to-morrow \- Tea at 6.30 Eve\*f, reading newspaper by fire side in large room.

## Right Page

### Fri. Sept. 22, 1905

**Weather:** A lovely day, mild + sunny.  **Temp:** [illegible]

up before bkfs\* + did some writing \- Bkfs\* at 8 \- after that gathered corn + parsnips \- Bashaw at work all day laying new decking on east side of Annex \- Earl jobbed around with me \- I spent most of on\*f writing to real estate agents \- Nekera Division \- to Ryan \- to Raefen \- to Adams \- to Z\- town for drugs etc. Earl bot\* up from P.O. letters from Jim + Raefeh. While yet very weak I have to-day noticed the first decided improvement in my condition \- Dinner at 1 \- after that I had Earl dig a platform/for use in front of the house, a short way down the hill, so as to get my camera above the level of the piazza floor, + I made two exposures \- one I am sure is light struck owing to my carelessness \- Then, under my direction Earl cut down several of the smaller trees to make it more open immediately in front of + about the house + for the betterment of the remaining trees. Then I had a nap \- Then supper \- Eve\*f so warm that no fire was lighted \- clouds covered the sky from north + west + there was a scurry of wind about 9 p.m. Then rain.
