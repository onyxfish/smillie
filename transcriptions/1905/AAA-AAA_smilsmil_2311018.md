## Left Page

### Tues. July 11, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fearfully hot day — air, baked \+ lifeless — thunderstorm \+ showers in the afternoon — no mitigation of heat \+ humidity — I got up with a peculiar \+ severe headache \+ trouble with my bowels that developed into a diarreah so that in the afternoon I had to give up. Selleck's wagon \+ men came after 10 o'clk \+ worked steadily till near 1 moving the last of things from house to studio — The latter is now piled so full that it is difficult to get about in it. I ate a light lunch \+ spent the afternoon on my bed. I managed to write a short note to Mary before 4 P.M. — I ate a very light dinner \+ after that was on my bed till I turned in for the night. The Ther. in my room did not fall below 80° \+ got as high as 87° but it was the humidity that hurt.

## Right Page

### Wed. July 12, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very hot day tempered by a pleasant breeze — a very slight exertion produced profuse perspiration. After a fairly restful night I got up this m'g. without a headache but bowels still behaving badly — I crawled about carefully, depending upon Ralph who worked good naturedly \+ faithfully — He went to Man'tn Storage \+ before 10 a van came \+ the last of furniture etc. was taken out — now there remains nothing but dirt — my personal belongings, a few things to be carried by hand to the studio \+ such things as I shall give or loan to George — I leave enough to make the house habitable for him. I kept on my bed as much as possible — ate very sparingly at 1 — \+ again on my bed — no trouble from bowels — wrote to Will Parks telling him that I hoped to be able to start for Scranton on Friday — also to two Real estate agents who ask for particulars about Renting 110 — Dinner at 6.30 — \+ a long confab with Ralph — To bed about 10.
