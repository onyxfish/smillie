## Left Page

### Tues. Jan. 10, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Jim Rec'd. to New-Haven.

Fine day \- Got to studio a little before 9 \- met the young man from Butler's who came to look at the 33×44 frame \+ to get instruction as to changing to color — I painted on Vesuvius \- Morton \+ Brush \+ Pencil came in to inquire as to the likelihood of his getting Na. [illegible] school advertising for B. \+ P. \- at 2. 30 went to bank \- then home to lunch \- after that, with Mary went to Wanamaker's to select covering for chairs \+ curtains for parlor \- to studio at 3.15 \- painted for an hour, then relox printing till 6.30 \- then home to dinner \- Jim left for New-Haven on 8 o'clk. Train — I spent the even't, making checks in payment of outstanding bills.

Rec'd. a pleasant letter from Frank Jewett Mathers (?) [illegible] Post Editorial Rooms \- in Response to my squib of Sunday.

## Right Page

### Wed. Jan. 11, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dull, raw day, with a mizzle \+ freeze at night \- to studio at about 10 — I gave a little time to working upon the Waldo \+ Jewett portrait that I got again from Numer Ireland to revarnish, my first attempt being unsuccessful — Then I experimented with defective negatives in trying to work out dark spots \+ streaks in skies by pencil work \- Then again at painting on Vesuvius — Home to lunch \+ back soon after 2 \+ painted as long as I could see. Miss Abbott came in late \+ made airsit — Then I did a little relox printing — Ernest Rath came in late bringing some of his plates to leave with me \- I am to take some prints from them \+ I shall try to sell some for him \- he has made all his arrangements to sail for Naples on Saturday \- he expects to meet a friend in Florence \+ will spend the winter there \- I am very much interested in the young man \- he is a good fellow \- is desperately in earnest \- is not brilliant but has talent \+ is fighting against immense odds \- I think of my boys as I look at him \+ am deeply affected. Home at 6 \- dinner \- Suef. Mary \+ the girls went to Charl's. I wrote a letter to Uncle Willie.
