## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 14, 1905

**Weather:** A bright cold day — inspinting  **Temp:** [illegible]

A bright cold day — inspinting — Bkfst. with Jim at usual time — then for some time in my room with newspaper \+ printing — Then to studio for a time but could find little to do as I have not strength enough to move some of the trunks that I wish to open \+ get at contents — Had lunch at Touraine — Then to Pomeroy's \+ had trus readjusted — Then to Wakefield Rattan Co. \+ bought rattan lounge for my bedroom Then called to see Mary at her boarding house 444 W. 25. S. but she was not at home — on my way up town stopped in at Wunderlich's \+ saw some mezzotints — old English. Then to my rooms — feeling so out of sorts that I had no dinner — had a glass of milk sent up \+ made a bowl of Junket — Jim dined with a friend — Bordman, I think — I podged about \+ managed by perseverance to get rid of the eveft \+ to bed after 10.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 15, 1905

**Weather:** Overcast with raw wind  **Temp:** Rain at 8 P.m

Overcast with raw wind — got to studio about 10.30 \+ worked at sorting books there till near 12 — Then by train to City Hall \+ walked up Broadway to Canal St. looking for the Herring, Hall, Marvin Safe Co. I found it from the man I talked with there I must have been fooled in some way when I bought my small safe now at 110 — the safe is marked Herring-Hall-Marvin Co. but the Broadway man says they never had a store where I bought my safe — Then I rode up town \+ went to the Century to lunch — met Walter Cook at the table \+ sat beside him — After 2 where I fell to my rooms \+ then again went to Studio there till after 4. when, feeling very tired I went back to Touraine \+ had a good hours nap — Jim came at 6.30 \+ at 7 we had our dinner. Eve\+ I spent rummaging thro' drawers of Annie's writing desk.
