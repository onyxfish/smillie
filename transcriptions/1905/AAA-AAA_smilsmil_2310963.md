## Left Page

### Thur. March 23, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very fine day. To studio before 10 \+ put in a good days work on R. book pl. Finished bevel on plate, laid ground, got a fine gelatine transfer \+ then got it nearly all drawn in \- out at 12.30 \+ had a Park Ave. walk before lunch at 1 \- after that went to bank \+ deposited chk. $25 from Maynard \- to studio at 3 \- worked till after 5 \- then home \+ had a rest before dinner \- after that I dressed \+ at 9 went to Edw. Cook's quite a swell entertainment \- an interesting lot of guests \- I had a pleasant chat with Rev. Minot Savage about Hackley affairs \- he confirmed my belief that trouble with Mr. W. \- Rather than with Mr. Williams led to W's resignation \- we were invited to hear Dr [illegible] discourse upon Chinese Ideals \- The Dr. is a woman \- an orphan adapted by a missionary family \+ educated as a doctor in this country \- she has married a Portugese \+ from what I could learn is a professional entertainer. She speaks English pleasantly \+ without accent \- she is markedly Chinese in face \+ wore a very becoming Chinese gown \- she got up on her soap-box plat. form at a little before 10 \+ spoke for nearly an hour. Referring occasionally to notes \- she gave a brief summary of Confucian \+ Buddhist teaching, showing that in essentials of ideals, the problems of progress in civilization the differences between the Orient \+ the Occident are in many ways quite similar \- She is a fluent speaker but to my mind, not quite profound or convincing \- her missionary associations have not Christianized her \-
I left at 11 o'clk.

## Right Page

### Fri. March 24, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Dull \+ threatening weather, rain at night. To studio after 10 \- completed drawing in of work on R. book plate \+ varnished it for biting \- Home to lunch at 1 \- Bessie \+ I took 2.06 train for Tarrytown \+ 3 o'clk Trolley car for Hackley, getting there before 3.30. Ralph in bath \- Had quite a chat with Mr. Hackley \+ some of my prejudices ag't. her were modified \- Bessie \+ I paraded about until the entertainment began \- Ralph as an usher, very busy \- it was about 4.30 when we went into the improvised theatre \+ music hall \- the orchestra \- the Banjo club \+ the banjo \+ Mandolin club gave us some rather noisy selections \- the play was Howells Garrotes \- given really with much spirit \- Mr. Roberts was taken by Edw. E. Moberly Jun. \- a boy of 14. whose make up \+ abandon was extraordinary \- Mr. Crashaw (pt. C. Reis) another 14 year old big boy Hans was Bella, the maid \- dressed exactly as the other maids about the school \+ as he went about in his costume after the play he was several times mistaken for one of them \+ asked to perform some menial office \- after the play, at about 6.30 a substantial supper was served \+ then came the dancing \- a culminating joy for the young people. About 8 o'clk. as Jack \+ rain had come we phoned to town for a hack \+ Mr. \+ Mrs. Miller (\+ her sister) \+ Bessie \+ I drove down to station \+ took the 8.48 train \- Mr \+ Mrs. Savage joined our party \- we got to N.Y. at 9.50 between showers, fortunately for us.
