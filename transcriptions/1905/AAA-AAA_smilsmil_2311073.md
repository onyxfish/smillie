## Left Page

### Sun. Oct. 29, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Superb day — rather late in getting started, the night nurses having so much to do. I got my blp. soon after 8 & then had a wait for the m.f call of Dr. Brown — when he came we had a long talk about Wellale Club. I wish to learn about it as I think it desirable for Jim to become a member, if possible. Then I had my bath & a short rest — then dressed & at 12 went up to the roof & walked & loafed about there till after 1 o'clk — It was a delightful privilege. Then dinner — not quite to my liking — then pulled my lounge about into a good position, spread blankets & pillows in it & lounged till Jim came at about 3 — then we went to the roof but did not stay there long — down again & Char came — he stayed till 6 — I had supper at 6.30 — Read awhile & prowled about the corridors & at last, to get into a comfortable place once more, went to bed. Today I have passed less urine & more pus than usual.

## Right Page

### Mon. Oct. 30, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another great day — clear, cloudless & cool — I had a talk with Dr. Brown this m.f which on the whole was comforting — he thinks that it will be proper for me to leave the hospital very soon — he came while Jessie was in the room. Then I had my bath & at about 11.30 I went on the roof — walked there till I was tired — then in to sun parlor — Read awhile — then out & walked again — & so time went till about 1 o'clk — then down & dinner at 1.30 Miss Maxwell invited me to attend the All Hallow e'en entertainment to-morrow evening at the Nurses Hall — Before 3 o'clk I went again on the roof for about an hour — then down & in the parlor met Dr. Tuthill & had a long talk with him — the outcome being encouragement altho' in some ways there seemed little basis for it — about 6 young Elliman came to ask if I had an abstract of title to 110 — I promised to write to Geo. Cook asking him to get it from safe deposit vault — Evely I loafed about — I watched thro' two games of checkers that young doctors played in the parlor — Jim came at 9 & at 10 I put myself to bed.
