## Left Page

### Wed. Aug. 2, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A beautiful day with two or three light showers, just to remind us of days past. After bkft. I worked in the garden for awhile then in \+ wrote to Mr. H.d. Smith about Jim \+ to Herbert M. Smith about insurance \+ some other writing. Soon after 11 Ralph \+ I went to the P. O. we met Jim on his way back from the Valley — he \+ R. ret. home while I walked to Wes. Otis's \+ interviewed him about coal, complaining about dirt \+ small size — then home at about 12.30. Dinner at 1 — after 2 Jim, Ralph \+ I went to Scott Browns to continue the subject of coal — found him at the hotel with young Dr. Janeway \+ his wife clearing things up there \+ getting the public rooms in order so that the cottagers may have a place where they may meet sociably. I think that the hotel might be opened \+ run on the club plan \+ the Lake Placid club is — Brown was so busy that I did not finish my coal business with him. So, J.R. \+ I walked a mile or more on the road into the Reserve — then crossed to River \+ back by trail, getting home at about 5.30 — Tea before J — Eve \+ Mary, Eliz'h. \+ Jim spent the eve at Maghees — Kathrine, Ralph \+ I took care of the fire at home.

## Right Page

### Thur. Aug. 3, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very beautiful day — after bkft. \+ awhile in the garden I worked for some time in studio getting things in shape for photo. work — Then I went to Scott Brown's \+ it was nearly an hour before, at last, I got into communication by telephone with D.T.H. agent at Westport about coal. From him I learned that I could get various sizes at Westport — I met Mr. \+ Mrs. Kendall of N.Y. at the Tennis Court \+ Miss Stammerby at Dr. Theo. Janeway's — Before 12 I went to the P. O. \+ home half an hour before dinner — Shortly after bkft. Jim \+ Ralph went on a tramp to the top of Giant — I worked in studio again getting ready for photo-graphic work \+ at 4 o'ck. started for the Giant trail to meet the boys on their return. I got up to the top of Roaring Brook fall \+ basked there for nearly half an hour in the sunshine — it was very lovely — then, about 5, I started to return home \+ on the trail met Mrs. Mackay \+ her sisters on their way up, when I was near the foot of the trail the boys overtook me \+ together we returned home — I stripped \+ had a good rub down \+ change of clothes — Tea at about 7. The young folks spent the eve at the Maghees \+ got to bed early, feeling tired.
