## Left Page

### Wed. March 29, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Still very mild \+ enervating — wrote a letter to Jim \+ one to Saudrezki after 6½ft. To studio about 10 — worked all day on R- book plate \+ finished it as far as I can see without a proof — home to lunch \+ back — left the studio at 5 o'clk. \+ went to Montross' gallery \+ saw paintings by McTee — some good work, but not much — Then home — dinner Eve'g with paksentite 9 — then Bessie came in with Maghee — they have been to Morristown, N.J., all day — looking over a house that Maghee has hired —

## Right Page

### Thur. March 30, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Very mild with showers in the afternoon — To studio at usual time. Butler sent up burnished flat for 12×18" frame \+ I spent a good part of the m'g. glazing points of emphasis with also a few solid touches — Sent check to Barnard Club paying dues to Nick 1st '06 \+ asking that resignation as a member be put on file to take effect on that date — at 2.30 went to Bank — Then to Am. Art Ass'n \+ saw The King collection of pictures — fine names — paintings not so good — Home to lunch at 1.30 — to studio late — went to Kimmel \+ Voigt's for ink — Henry V— not in — home, between showers before 5 — newspaper — dinner at 6.30 — Eve'g, some reading \+ some writing in my Cat. of European photo-films.
