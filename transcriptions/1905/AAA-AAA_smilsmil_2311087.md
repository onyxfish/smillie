## Left Page

### Tues. Nov. 28, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day — cold \+ windy — rain began falling about noon \+ kept at it without intermission through the rest of the day \+ eve'g. B'kf't. with Jim — After 10 I went to Wanamaker's — met Mr. [illegible] — I was detained there till after 11 — was measured for some shirts — then went to Brooks Bro. \+ tried on suit of clothes that are being made for me — after some detention went on to Tho. Lock's — then to Freese's — then the rain commenced — then to Touraine — Had lunch at 1.30 — after that gave myself up to loafing \+ sleeping — feeling very unhappy — Dinner with Jim at 7 — Wef. he went out \+ I busied myself for some time over accts.

## Right Page

### Wed. Nov. 29, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph came from New-Haven

Storm of wind \+ rain all night — I was in a semi conscious condition owing to distress in stomach \+ bowels \+ got little sleep. The storm continued till afternoon when rain \+ wind ceased but the weather did not clear — I spent the m't in my room writing up Jim's accts. I had a light lunch at 1.30 — Ralph came in at about 2.30 — at about 4 we walked down to 44 W. 25th St. \+ called on Sister Mary. She was alone — at 5 we ret'd. to the Touraine. Jim came in at 6.30 \+ at 7 we had our dinner — After that Jim went to Edw'd. Cook's — I continued my work on Jim's accts. while Ralph amused himself reading.
