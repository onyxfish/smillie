## Left Page

### Sun. Sept. 3, 1905

**Weather:** Rain all last night — all to-day & still raining at 11 P.M. — driven before a strong south wind.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Up before 7 — a cheery fire making it pleasant weather in my room — an enjoyable scrub \+ blbf. at 9 — Rigged in my rubber suit \+ went into the garden \+ gathered corn, squash \+ lettuce — much of wf. in my room writing a letter to Emma J. — Dinner about 2 — afternoon, I mounted for albums a number of my European photos. There was a lot up in the rain for awhile in the late afternoon \+ I walked down my road to see the amt of damage done \+ was agreeably surprised to find that the long rain had done little damage. Evely, we had some singing by Miss Rose, whistling by Katharine \+ piano playing by Elizabeth —

## Right Page

### Mon. Sept. 4, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Mr. Rose left for Lake Placid.

Heavy rain from about 10 A.M. till noon — then a rapid clearing \+ sunshine — a heavy shower at 7 P.M. — Ret. at 8 — Soon after 9 Mr. Rose left — \+ not long after that we were surprised by a call from Mr. Weston, Mr. Marshall \+ his daughter Serena — Soon after they came the rain came down in torrents \+ they were storm bound for about an hour \+ a half — we had an improvised concert by home talent which passed off very agreeably. It was near noon before our callers could get away. Dinner at 1 — At about 3 o'clk. I went with the boys to Russell falls, back of St. Hubert's Mn. — a tremendous volume of water rushing down — a remarkable sight — a number of the cottagers there to see it — met Mr. White, \+ the Reserve \+ went with him to see his bridge over which the water was swashing — Then I went to Chapel Pond Road \+ saw roaring brook falls — \+ then home. Supper — Evely Miss Rose told the fortunes of us all, happy marriages being fortold in every case —

Otis came this mf. \+ worked on Tennis court for perhaps an hour.
