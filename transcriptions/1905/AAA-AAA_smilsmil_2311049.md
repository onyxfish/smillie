## Left Page

### Mon. Sept. 11, 1905

**Weather:** Bright mf that passed into a clouded \+ threatening day.  **Temp:** [illegible]

I got up with a headache \+ fought against it till 4 o'clk. when I was obliged to go to bed \- After bkfs\- I went through my garden duties \- then finished a long letter to Emma J\. \- Otis came with a load of river, or cobble, stones \+ I had Bashaw \+ Moody lay them along the east end of house where the drip from the eaves washes the earth from about the roots of a big pine \- then I went to P. O. \- then dinner, but I ate none of it \- then I sowed grass seed over one of the open ground spaces \- \+ then to bed \-

## Right Page

### Tues. Sept. 12, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

The weather seemed unable to decide what to do till late in the afternoon when it gave up to rain \- I got up feeling very seedy after a hard night \- Mary too had a hard time being up at different times during the night with vomiting\. After bkfs\. I went to Scott Brown's \+ Telephoned to Mr Windsor \- O. Kellogg\. \+ he told me that Parke was at the Sagamore \- Lake Geo\. P\- had telephoned him that he w\. come up this P. M. \+ w\. then come on here to\-morrow mf\. \- Then I went to Mrs Maghee's \+ borrowed her Pyrographic outfit \- then, feeling used up I took a nap \+ made up for some that I lost last night\. Dinner at 1 \- much of the afternoon was spent in the redwood ribbon with matter to go over my fire place\.
