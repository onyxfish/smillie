## Left Page

### Sat. March 25, 1905

*Grace Buck with us over night*

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — dark, wet m't without rain — clearing late in the afternoon. To studio about 10 — Some delay getting my acid department into working trim. At 12.15 went to Nat'l Arts Club to see the 38th Annual Exhib'n of Am. Water Color Soc'y — a small show with nothing unusual about it — there till near 1 — then home to lunch. To studio \+ worked till 6 — then cleaned plate — I bit with Nitric \+ Water — 1/2 \+ 1/2 — feet very timid from want of practice \+ familiarity with Nitric — result — some parts were under bitten — others, over bitten — not so bad as to ruin the plate but bad enough to give me lots of extra work. Home at 6.30 — Grace Buck at dinner — visiting K. \+ E. — Eve'g loafing in parlor.

## Right Page

### Sun. March 26, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A lovely day — Rain at night. Bkf't at 9.30 — at 11 I went to Nat. Academy school — 109th St. \+ Amsterdam Ave — burned out a week ago — Davis took me over the bldg. The extent of injury to the bldg \+ the little, comparatively, to the contents, is surprising — the roof is practically all destroyed — the air space underneath \+ the open ventilators caused the fire to "mushroom" under the entire roof — that, water \+ falling ceilings make it astonishing that so few of the pictures, prints or books were injured — the casts in the large "antique" room were generally smashed but can all be replaced — the [illegible] is free — the start of the fire is a mystery — no electric wires near the point of starting — so, the theory of defective insulation seems untenable — After the Nat. visit, I went about St. Lukes Hospital — Columbia College — to 116th St. Subway Station — Then home at 1.30 — dinner — after that to studio for a short time — a short walk — then a rest — Eve'g went to Emma J's — at 10.30 walked home between showers.
