## Left Page

### Sat. July 29, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A misty, overcast day with portents of a coming storm which started in at night. The day was entirely uneventful — the mfg., after Bkfs., was spent in the garden. Dinner about 1 o'clk. as usual — a large part of the afternoon was spent in experimental work to decide as to the best method of pasting the photos, taken while abroad last summer, in the albums for the boys. Tea \+ then a quiet evening reading.

## Right Page

### Sun. July 30, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Continuous rain all day long, after raining all night long before — it was very steady with but few variations — Between 4\+ 5 o'clk A.M. the rain came down in torrents \+ now, after 11 P.M. it is still raining hard. Bkfs. about 9.30 after a satisfactory rub down in front of a fine fire in my bedroom. I did not go away from the house all day except when Jim \+ I went to the garden \+ gathered vegetables for dinner \+ then Jim went off alone \+ had a tramp up the River Trail — I envied him. Dinner after 2 — \+ supper at 7 — It would be hard to tell how the time was spent — I promenaded the Piazzas for a time — Evelyn Lawrence Adler came \+ we passed a chatty time — Elis[illegible] spent the after part of the day \+ the night at the Maghee's.
