## Left Page

### Sun. Jan. 8, 1905

**Weather:** Cold, cloudy \+ windy \- a little sunshine.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Bkf. at 9. After 11 I went to studio \+ was there for a short time, then walked on Park Ave. for exercise. \- Before I went in to Charl's for a few minutes. Then home \+ wrote a note to Art Editor, Meth. Post. In a long description f M.A. \+ D. exhib. in last eve's Post he refers to me as one of the "aboriginal" school of Ame. Painters \- \+ then, later in a word of "homage to the ancients" he names me again with Huntington, Moran, J.G. Brown \+ E.A. Henry \- he kindly says to the surviving pioneers "that we have served our purpose in our rather naive nature worship" \+ paved the way for dimes \+ other grants \- my note was intended to be a bit of good natured chaff. Dinner at 2 \- The afternoon was disposed of by various devices \+ in the eve'g at 6 o'clk. I went with Ralph to R.R. Station \+ he took 6:05 train for T-town. He seems to have recovered to a considerable extent from the effects of the influenza that has been making him miserable for some days past. At 8 o'clk. went to Emma Ireland's \+ spent the eve'g there.

## Right Page

### Mon. Jan. 9, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fairly good day \- At studio, after a little delay I got to work once more on 33×44 \- Resuming. \+ after worrying awhile over the sky I repainted the water, or sea, solidly. With my limited experience in painting I have undertaken far too much in this picture \- The problems of composition \- atmosphere, color, brush-work \+ technique generally are all beyond me. \- Home to lunch \- Painted as long as I could see \- Then I went to Alexanders \- with shot to be enlarged for bunyan \+ home to dinner \- Quiet eve'g at home \- Jim, Mary \+ her girls went to Charl's.
