## Left Page

### Mon. June 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very uncomfortably hot m't, gradually becoming overcast & cloudy & shading from about 90°. in hottest places to 51°. at night when it rained. I had my bkft. at Charl's — very delightful. — then moved myself to my dreadfully dirty & confused quarters at home where Geo. & Nellie are trying to make me comfortable. At 10.30 as no China packers had yet come from the Man'th Storage & went there on my Return found them waiting at the house for me. They had a bale of excelsior & a lot of paper — from 10.45 to 3.30, minus lunch hour, they packed six barrels with glass & ware of all kinds. I was kept pretty busy cleaning out closets, etc. etc. & did some running back & forth between house & studio. I had expected Jim in the late afternoon but got a Telegram saying that he had stayed over for the "peace oration" — Eve'g. I did some work sorting & selecting books.

## Right Page

### Tues. June 20, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

By comparison with yesterday m't. The weather is actually cold. The Ther. fell to 51°. last night — a drop of nearly 40°. in 24 hours. The day continued cloudy, chilly & damp. Bkft. at 8 — Jim came along in the course of the m't & set to work clearing out his room — I worked around at home till near noon — then to Borrowauns & made purchase of Photo. materials — then to Wanamakers \+ bot. night shirts — then to Alexanders for shoes — then to Palette for colors — to Electric Supplies for batteries & home to lunch at 1.30 — Jno. Gaffny came & worked for awhile carrying books down to first floor. I worked during the afternoon feeling very tired & made little progress. At 6.30 — dinner. After that went to B's & got photo. purchase — then to studio with it — then home & was in till after 10 — Selleck, Walter Cook — Rivers & others.
