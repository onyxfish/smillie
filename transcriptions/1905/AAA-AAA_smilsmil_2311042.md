## Left Page

### Mon. Aug. 28, 1905

**Weather:** Cloudy but not cold  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy but not cold — Rain at night — much troubled last night by Rheumatic pains — + cold — Moody's brother Earl came to-day — seems to be quick + willing — I spent a good part of the m'f writing — letters + business notes — At noon Jim, Ralph + I went to P.O. — Dinner about 1 — a rest after that + Reading Sunday's Tribune — Then out with Earl who was cutting the grass — I was digging weeds out of the sod — Supper along about 7 — Eve'g Maghee, Katharine Hardenburg + Ardra Marshall came over + spent the eve'g till 9.30 — There was music, much varied as to grade — some of it was good + some of it was not.

Message from Winter + Smillie — Chas cables from Stockholm "All Well"

## Right Page

### Tues. Aug. 29, 1905

**Weather:** Cloudy m'f, clearing delightfully  **Temp:** [illegible]

Cloudy m'f, clearing delightfully — more clouds in the afternoon, with a very few drops of Rain B'f about 8 + at 9 Mr. Kellogg got away in a Ford Reede conveyance — Otis came with O, Reedes Disc Harrow at about 9.30 — a short trial proved that it did not do the work expected of it so I sent Otis back to his barn for his plough. he ret. at about 11 + by noon he + Earl Moody had cross ploughed it cutting the overturned sod into smaller pieces — he ret. in the afternoon with a wood sled + the sod was carried to the north end of ploughed space to make it level — I was with them a good part of the day — Mary went to P.O. + I spent some time with spade + trowel digging weeds + "witch grass" out of the sod where I have top-dressed + seeded for the past three years. Feeling out of sorts I had no supper — Eve'g Reading
