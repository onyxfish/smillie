## Left Page

### Thur. Oct. 19, 1905

**Weather:** Rain last night — cloudy day — Rain again after 6 p.m.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sat up in chair —

Rain last night — cloudy day — Rain again after 6 p.m. — The ordinary routine of the day was not broken till about noon when a wheeled chair was rolled into the room \+ Ros hellped me out of bed into it \+ rolled me into blankets — Then my bed had a thorough change \+ airing — I sat in my chair till 1 o'clk, then my dinner was brought to me — After eating it, at about 2 o'clk, I got back to my bed again feeling in no way tired — Soon after that Ashley Herron of Southwick \+ Co. came to say that Martin was to give him a definite answer to-morrow p.m. as to purchase of 110. I said that my terms were $60000 cash. $3000 down on signing contract, \+ 60 days. I doubt if M. will buy. Then I wrote a note to Clarence B. Smith concerning this business \+ asking him to call on me. About 5 Charl came \+ stayed with me an hour or more. During the evef. the rain fell heavily \+ my only visitors after Ros left me at 7 were nurse Miss Mosely \+ orderly Collins.

## Right Page

### Fri. Oct. 20, 1905

**Weather:** Rain during the m't — cloudy afternoon, clearing at evef.  **Temp:** [illegible]

Sold 110 2. 38th S\. to Selman.

Rain during the m't — cloudy afternoon, clearing at evef. My m't course was varied somewhat — Miss Mosely brot. me a tumbler full of hot water \+ Phos. Soda \+ then I had nothing to eat till b'kf't. at 8 — I wrote to Mr. H\+d. Smith asking that Jim be allowed to go to the funeral to-morrow \+ I wrote to Ge°. enclosing a note to Edna Cook. D. 2d. Cook came in \+ Jessie \+ Miss Carpenter came at same time — a merry party — I got up \+ into chair at 12 — had a good dinner \+ sat till 3 o'clk — then to bed again. Miss Stebbins nurse, came with telephone message from Ge°. "young man from Pease \+ Selman wants to know if you will sell house — will give $60000 — he is on his way to Presb't's hospital — will be there in a few minutes." In a few minutes he came — Douglas Suldro Selman — a younger brother — young man of pleasing manner — evidently bent upon the prompt transaction of business — said he had just lunched. who said he believed I w'd. sell the house for $60000 — was that so? I told him that I had asked more \+ had set the sum named as my bottom figure in some pending negotiations — he said "sharply — I'll take it \+ give you a check now — I said "hold on — you make my head swim — I don't like to do business so important in such haste — how did you learn that the house was for sale — he told me that a broker called him up on phone \+ told him he had an option on it \+ asked if he had a client who w'd. buy it. whereupon he, Ellison, came directly to me — Martin was trying to sell the house before any contract was made — So, when E. offered me a check for $3000, I gave him a receipt \+ a promise in writing to sell. I s'pose I have not been tricked into a bad position — I am uneasy about it. E. telephoned to Clarence Smith \+ made an appointment with him to come here to-morrow at 9 A.m. Jim came in the evef. — he seems sick \+ out of sorts — I am very unhappy about him.
