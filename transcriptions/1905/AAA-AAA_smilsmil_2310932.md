## Left Page

### Wed. Jan. 18, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Fine day, much milder - To studio at usual time - Finished the putting away of the many unbound books taken from cases in the cellar. Cleaned Borden drawing + prepared p.fo. of the photo-gravure plate to deliver to Scribner + Co. Home to lunch. Some general work - mostly fussing. till near 4 - then I went to D.D. Butler To Scribners (left plate + p.fo. + drawing - did not see Mr. Smitts but left explanatory letter for him) then to Alexanders about my shoe - then home Dinner at 6.30 Eve'g. went to a meeting of the Water Color Soct. at the Sherwood Studio Bldg. - A crisis in Soct. affairs has been reached now as no place can be found for the annual Exhib'n. - There was much desultory talk but nothing new came out of it - I left before adjournment + walked home.

## Right Page

### Thur. Jan. 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Pleasant - partially clouded - Before I went to studio I went to the Touraine in 39th St. a large apartment hotel near the Union League Club, + inquired for an apartment such as w'd accommodate him, Ralph + myself - I can get, furnished a suite of parlor, three bed rooms + bath room for $2000 per an. Then to studio + worked at various matters - Started a pen sketch of Times Bldg + Elevated R.R. Station 42d. St. — Home to lunch - continued work in a desultory way till 5 o'clk - then home - Dinner at 6. Mr. Maghee dined with us + took Katharine + Bessie to Opera in Eve'g. I worked till 11 Velox printing.
