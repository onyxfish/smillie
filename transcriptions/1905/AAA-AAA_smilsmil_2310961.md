## Left Page

### Sun. March 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A dismal day of rain — Bkfst. at 9.30 — About 11 went to studio + got some negatives there, worked a little while, then home + at about 1 o'clk. commenced Velox printing — Dinner at 2 — Recommenced printing at 4 + continued Till 6.30 — then cleaned up + Rested till near 8 — then to Walter Cook's — no one "in" — then to Edward's — found Edna + H.f. Murphy — spent the evg. with them — Saw Edw.? for a moment — Catharine is ill with light attack of Pneumonia.

## Right Page

### Mon. March 20, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Disagreeable day — Clouded, Raw + cold — Rain at night. To studio after 10 — fussed about for some time doing nothing of importance — Then some work on Roreker Book plate design — Otto Sandresch called about the pf. of his etching sent to St. Louis Exbn. Home to lunch — Then wrote to Watrous + Niellatz about S. print — To Studio at 2.30 — Finished drawing of R. book-plate. I found that several of the velox prints I made yesterday were not fully set in the Hypo. So, I gave them all another Hypo bath + a washing — It was 6 o'clk when I got home after calling at Edward Cook to learn how Catharine is — Dinner — Evg. I went to the Waldorf + there saw W.G. Parke, Mr. P. + their daughter Helen + Mr. + Mrs. Atherton — all, except W.A. to sail to-morrow for Naples — left for home soon after 9.
