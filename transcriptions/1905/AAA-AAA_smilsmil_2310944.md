## Left Page

### Sat. Feb. 11, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A fine day but the weather in my head \+ bones has not yet cleared \- nevertheless, when I got to studio about 10 I tackled the drawing in up on the plate of the sketch of the dome of the Frauen Kirche \+ old buildings from the Terrace, Dresden \- I worked steadily till 12.30 \- Then, a promenade on the sunny side of Park Ave, where it was so cold \+ windy that my poor old, ill used eyes wept copiously \- then to lunch \+ after it another 20 min. stroll \- then to studio \+ completed the drawing in up on the plate of my Dresden Etching \- then varnished it for biting \- Home at 5 \+ had a short rest before dinner \- Eve'g worked at Velox printing struggling with some obstinate subjects till 9.30 \- Then to Charl's where Mary \+ the girls were \- he was showing them a lot of new lantern slides working his new lantern \- Home at 10.30

## Right Page

### Sun. Feb. 12, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Another beast of a day \- a foul \+ unclean thing \- the m'f was overcast \+ threatening \- snow began to fly soon after noon \- as the time passed sleet followed \+ then rain, freezing as it fell \- the streets are in a dreadful condition \- Head \+ eye ache continues \- b'f't about 9.30 \- Before 11 I went out \+ walked around thro' the streets from hour or more \- then to Charl's \+ home before dinner at 2 \- After that, some reading \+ at about 5 o'clk. I got to work at Velox printing \+ stuck to it (no supper) till 10.30 \- a good part of that time it was a struggle with some of the pieces, which, while to all appearance beautiful, yet, because of a peculiar denseness or a want of pictorial balance in light \+ shade, fail to yield satisfactory prints \- Charl came in \+ spent the eve'g \. To bed at 11.30
