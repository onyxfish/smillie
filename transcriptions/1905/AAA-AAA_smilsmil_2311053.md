## Left Page

### Tues. Sept. 19, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

A very lovely day, so far as weather goes - but a very dreary one for me, owing to weakness & discomfort. I woke early & amused myself, before getting up, conting the form of a letter to send to W. G. Nielson - it was late before I had my bkfst - then a visit from Mary - so, it was quite late before I got up & dressed - Then for an hour or more I sat in the sunshine out on the upper piazza. For a time I felt fairly well & enjoyed it. I wrote a letter to Jim - but as time wore on I was conscious of a general decadence till finally I felt unaccountably weak & wretched &, giving up, went back to bed. Temps. normal & could complain of nothing specific - my dinner was served in my room & the afternoon passed between lying on my bed & occasional incursions into the studio - Ralph attempted to make photos with my camera but failed with every one - the focal distances marked on the bellows slide don't agree with the facts by a good deal - Mary & the girls went down the valley visiting - Ralph packed - Supper was brought to me, at the usual time. Eve'g. I spent on lounge in big room.

Bashaw at work all day with [illegible] seed

## Right Page

### Wed. Sept. 20, 1905

**Weather:** [illegible]  **Temp:** [illegible]

Ralph left for New-York.

Some light rain during the night, soft & warm. Heavy rain as the day advanced - now & then in torrents - Suddenly, about noon, there was a change - the wind whipped around to the north & shortly, the weather cleared. Very fine sunset. I got up at 7. feeling weak but better than yesterday - my meals were served in my room in spite of my protests. Ralph spent the m'g hard at work, packing. I wrote to Case - 2d. Nat'l. asking for loan of $750 - & to Manning, of the Touraine, asking if he could furnish him & self with beds for a night or two after our return to Town. Ralph got away on 2.30 stage for E. Town - later he will go to Westport & catch the 11 p.m. train, getting into N.Y. about 7.30 a.m. About 3 I wandered out in the sunshine to see what Brashaw was at work at. W. Weston then came & made a pleasant call on me. - About 4 o'clk. I had a long nap.
