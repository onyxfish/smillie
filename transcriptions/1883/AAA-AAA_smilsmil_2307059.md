## Left Page

### December, Monday 10, 1883

Charming day — To studio at 9.30.
Spent the mf. working over
etching "Marblehead neck came
way" — charcoaling & burnishing.
Dinner at usual time — afternoon
laid rebiting ground on part of
No. 30 — & spent some time mounting
large proofs — At 5 went to Dicki
& had a ride — Home at 6.30 — a
surprise party — Fanny & Will K.
to tea — after tea I went to Walter
for a few minutes to consult about
some Montrose plans — then home.
To bed at 10.30 — Jas. just arose
in his night at 11 & was in a state
of unconquerable activity until
after 1 o'clk. requiring the services
of three adults.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 11, 1883

Pleasant mf. — raw, chilly & threat-
ening snow at 1 o'clk. — cleared again
shortly. To studio at 9.30 — in no
mood for work — fussed over pt —
Father & G.P.? — came in at noon.
Poor father has been to see D. K. [illegible] about
his right ear — his hearing is now almost
gone & naturally it is a great grief to
him. D.K. & give him no benefit.
I went with him & bought an ear-
trumpet which somewhat helps him.
Then we went home to dinner —
Father & I — Ret'd. to P.o'k. at 3.30 — I,
back to studio before 3 — did not do
much of anything — home at 6 —
Charle came in to hear particulars
about Father — Na before 7 — At 7.40
Annie, mother Cook & I went to Chick-
ering's — & heard W. & Mrs. Hentzel — a
delightful concert — Home at 10.30
— right to bed.
