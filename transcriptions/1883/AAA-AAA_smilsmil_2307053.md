## Left Page

### November, Wednesday 28. 1883.

Very pleasant day — 10 o'clk, as usual, before I got to studio.
Then to work on Etchings, 29 & 31 — biting some additional
work — To dinner at 1 — back at 3 — cleaned plates and
fussed over them until after 4 — about 5 went to
Dickeis & he put me on a [illegible] mare called "Bay
Cob" — I quite enjoyed my ride — home at 6.45 — Tea
at 7.15 — Early eveng. Read awhile — after 9 — Aunt, &
Mrs Saupre & Mrs. Comstock called for about half an hour.
To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### Thanksgiving Day

### November, Thursday 29. 1883.

Beautiful day — to studio about 10 & at once to work preparing
to prove Etchings 29 & 31 — ground ink etc & before 12.30 had pulled
3 or 4 pls. Home at 1 — back at 3 & worked as long as I could
see, getting only 8 or 9 pls. of the two plates, & these not altogether
satisfactory, owing to the black on the oil or some defect in me.
Washed up & rested awhile in big chair — Then at 6, out &
around Mad. Sq. — then home —
Tea before 7 — Evtg. wrote a letter to Saml. Coleman &
went to G. C. Depot & mailed it.
To bed about 10.30.
