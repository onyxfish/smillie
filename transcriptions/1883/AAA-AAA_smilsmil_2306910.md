## Left Page

### February, Thursday 15, 1883

Heavy Rain nearly all day — Matt
\+ Kate so discouraged that they went
home to Po'k this m'f. I got to
studio about 10 with a headaching
head but worked on Old Orchard
until 1, then home — dinner at
1.30 — Some time spent with
the paper hanger — back to studio
after 3 — Got desperate + smeared
wildly all over foreground of Old
Orchard. — Home at 6.30 — my head
still thick + heavy — Emma J. to
tea — at 8 went with E. + a. — to
Singing Club — then to Courteny when
I met Gifford + Chawkney + we had
a Water Color Soc'ty nominating com.
meeting — we discussed, but
did not settle any thing — at
10 I went for Annie — home before
11 + to bed.

Mary Sprague cook — gave birth
to-day to her fourth child, a son.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 16, 1883

Still dismal, wretched weather
— drizzling Rain or wetting fog all
day — to studio at 10, after going to
see how Mother + child were. — I
worked for awhile on Old Orchard
then put it aside + fixed up a
study that I made at White Head last
last Sepr. Home to dinner at 1 — back
at 3 + worked on a little study of
Ev'f. cloud effect that I made
in Po'k a couple of years ago. At
5 went to Grady's + ordered three
frames. — Home to tea at 7 — Ev'f.
hung etchings in dining room
which now looks very bright and
charming — then read my papers,
did a little writing + to bed before
11 o'clk.
