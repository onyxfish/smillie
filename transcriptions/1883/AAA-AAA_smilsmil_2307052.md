## Left Page

### November, Monday 26, 1883

Evacuation Day — Centennial — Preparations for grand celebration —
overcast until about 10 — then rain
began to fall & continued nearly all
day — Tremendous crowds in the
streets — tens of thousands of people got
a soaking for which they must
pay a penalty — Got to studio at
10 o'clk — to work with charcoal
on sketch — Road near Montrose —
Home at 4 — back at 5 — worked
over some funny summer sketches
but could not work with a
relish — after losing light, took
a rest in big chair — Home
before 6 — Tea before 7 — Soon
after 8 went to Walter's with
some Montrose plans — discussed them until 9.30 — then
home. To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### November, Tuesday 27, 1883

Clear, pleasant day — in bed very
late — Bkft. at 9.30 — to studio at
10.30 — Some work on charcoal
sketch & some work about etching
ppr. About 12 went to 2° Nat. Bk.
& deposited the $200 I got of Geo, then
to Sanborn's — saw Mr. Marvin and
delivered to him the etching plate
& proof — Then to Deesey & Keating's —
paid for overcoat — on my way back
stopped at Mitchell & Vance's & ordered
gas light & shade for writing desk in
north bed room — Home to dinner
at 1.20 — to studio at 2.45 — worked
on charcoal sketch "Stray Lambs" —
(view on the hill near the lake at M.)
until 4.30, when I could see no longer —
rested until 5. Then to Dickle's —
but did not ride — Took up a coat
& got closet — home — tea before 7 —
Every. Read a little & lounged
a good deal. To bed about 10.30.
