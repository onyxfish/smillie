## Left Page

### September, Sunday 9, 1883

Cold, bleak & windy mth. After a disturbed night, up late and had bkfs. after 9 — At 10.30, feeling the need of air & exercise, I walked over to the hills beyond Mr. Frink's where there is a view of Elk Mt., with a foreground that I like very much — ¾ of an hour walking there — when near home after 12 o'clk. met Annie who had come out to meet me. Had a nap before dinner at 2 — After dinner Mr. & Mrs. Jn.º S. Courtright called — Joe Richards, young Comstock & a companion also came — I wrote my letter home — Tea about 7 — Eveg., wrote a note to Will K. To bed at 10.30

## Right Page

### September, Monday 10, 1883

an Oct. day — warm in the sunshine — cold in the shade — another disturbed night — up late — bkfs. near 9 — went down town at 10.30 — got a letter from S.R.K. asking me to send on plate No. 25 at once — went to Warner's & spent some time over my printing table — then to foundry, about wrought irons for large kitchen doors — then to Dr. Richardson's on acct. of Rosina who is not well — Home at noon — kindled fire in studio furnace and got started on No. 25 — Dinner at 1 — then again at 25, softening parts of sky. wrote a note to S.R.K. & at 4 took it to P.O. At 4.30 out riding with Annie — back soon after 6 — Tea at 7 — Eveg., wet down paper for proving to-morrow & wrote another note to S.R.K. withdrawing my offer of 26 as well as 25 — To P.O. before 10 — To bed at 10.30.
