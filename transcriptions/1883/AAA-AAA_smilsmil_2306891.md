## Left Page

### January, Monday 8, 1883

Part of day clear & cold, later
overcast. at night a film of snow.
To studio at 10 — late again —
feeling despondtic — out of sorts —
but I worked on Causeway draw-
ing. Home to dinner at 1 — back
before 3 & did more work on
my drawing & now think it fin-
ished — In some respects it gives
me satisfaction — it has some qualities
of color that come near being good —
parts of it are intelligent & direct
but other parts are uncertain & some
of it is positively bad. I wish that
I could go right on now without inter-
ruption with more W. C. work — I think
I could improve on what I have done —
Rec'd from Seymour Haden a letter formally
accepting invitation to lecture — also his
preface for our See-[illegible] cat. Home
to tea — Will & Fanny K. — Lib — Have — Mary
Willard & Geo. C. There — Every made
two whist tables — Fanny & I — apts 44 &
Annie — all over at 11 & we went to
bed —

Gave Putnams the order to do the
Haden printing.

## Right Page

### January, Tuesday 9, 1883

Most of the day, weather overcast
& growing colder — very raw. After
blkp. at 9.15 went to Mr. Hoe's — saw
him & Mrs. H. Arranged details
about publishing Haden's letter &
acclo Farce. Then to studio. Spent
some time clearing up — then
wrote a letter to Farrer & copied
Haden's preface for our catalogue.
Gifford came in just about 6 o'clk
on the subject of W. C's for Century.
Home to dinner at 1.30 — after it, a
rest & got to studio at 3.45 — wrote
a letter to Haden — a call from
[illegible] & others. At 6 took Haden
letter to Keppel's in 16th S. Then home
at 6.30 — Tea — Every. in front —
fire — Annie reading aloud H&
James's "the Point of View". Later,
I did a little writing. To bed
before 11.
