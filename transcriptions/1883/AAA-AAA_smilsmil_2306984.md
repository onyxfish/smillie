## Left Page

### July, Sunday 15, 1883

A hot day with breeze from
S. & E. — [illegible] drg — in humidity
commenced to pack my trunk —
took a rest & nap before dinner
at 2 — after that, wrote letters,
among others, one to Sir W. P. Drake
with draft for $6.2.3 — after
6 went to Bates' in 6th Ave. & got
some medicines — then to Dickel's
in 56th S. & got towels et. — Rain
began falling briskly so that I
was quite wet on getting home
at 7 — then tea — Rain continued
until after I went to bed at about
10.30.

Dr. Yale's infant daughter
died this evening. The mother
is very ill.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 16, 1883

Bright, breezy day growing to be
very warm — Madss Hervé left to-day.
After [illegible] — started out with a list
of things to be done — Went to 2d. Nat.
Bank — to Johnston's — to Vantines, to
Jackson's & selected Andirons and
shovels & tongs — to Metropolitan Bank
to foot of Chris. S. & bought 2 mileage
tickets — to Hoboken & secured seats
back & to Eimer & Amend's & bought
acids & things — then home at 1 —
Dinner soon after — Rec'd Yale's note
announcing death of his child, while
at table. At 2.30 started for down
town — went to Harper's & was paid
$35. for sketch of East River Bridge — then
to Beckman S. & got faucets — then to K. &
V's in Canal S. — then to studio — then to
Grady & McKeabers 6th Ave. & 41st S. then home
a little before 6 & rested until near
7 when I had tea — Rest did
little but rest — part of time with
geo., Walter & Valentine. To bed
before 11.
