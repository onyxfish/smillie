## Left Page

### April, Friday 20, 1883

Heavy showers & cold, drawing wind in the mtf. clearing to a cold brilliant evtf. After VEp: went to Gradip to see how Ports & Stchrp were getting on in the mounting — To studio soon after 10 — Had to kindle a fire in the grate & got to work on W.C. of Rough Sport — Calverly modeling on bas-Relief at my side. — Home at 1 — back at 3.15. Mr. Reid, Mr. Jewel of Phila. called — I worked until 6.30 — then home — Tea at 7 — besides Nelie & the girls, Mr. Emerson & Miss Gibbons were there — Part of the evtf. I spent drawing on plans for Montrose Main Hall — At 10 — took Mr. E. & miss G. home & then a little farther walk alone, to enjoy the brilliant moonlight — to bed at about 11.30.

## Right Page

### April, Saturday 21, 1883

Pleasant day — headachy — Got to studio before 10 — Kindled fire in grate — then to work on Rough Sport, W.C. — but did not work with any comfort or spirit. Calverly modeled for a time on my portrait bas-relief — Home at 1 — Nelie & the girls rid to Ridgefield in the afternoon — I got to studio before 3 & worked on W.C. until after 5 — then went for a little while to see the pictures at the Socy. of Am. Artists — Home at 6.30 — Wolf, Annie & I went to Miss Henrietta Beebie's concert at Chickering Hall — very full house & long & varied programme — Too much hash — would have liked it better if we had had more Beebe. Miss Spence gave a Recitation. We got home before 11 — then right to bed.
