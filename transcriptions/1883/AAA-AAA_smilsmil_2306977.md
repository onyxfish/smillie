## Left Page

### July, Sunday 1. 1883.

Delightfully fresh, cool day. Ther. last night went down to 62°
in my window. About 11 went to Studio to see about some p/p.
that I am requested to send to the Vienna exposition. Home
to dinner at 2. — Walter, Maria & children came in in the after-
noon as usual. I wrote a letter to Geo. About 6 I went up to
Dickle's & at 6.30 went to the Park on "Barry" & had a pretty
good ride — the evening was simply delicious — back to Stable at
7.30 — had a wash & towelling then home to tea soon after 8 —
Will Cook & Robert there — After tea I felt quite unwell — on my
bed until near 10 — then up &
wrote a short note to father —
Dragged myself to depot with it —
then back & to bed where I
passed a wretched night.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 2. 1883.

An exceedingly hot day — got up feeling very seedy after a night
of great discomfort from nausea that continued almost without
cessation until morning — Got to Studio at 10 & to work on Etching No.
25 "Marblehead Neck" — Put on the Acid — it seemed to work well —
Also, fixed up some p/p. of etchings
for Vienna exptn. Mrs Abbott called
Home to dinner at 1 — back before
3 — another bite on a new section
of No. 25 — then worked until after
7 getting ready the Vienna p/p. —
Home before 8 & had tea — Will
Cook & his son Robert went home
by 10 p.m. train. Fierce, short
thunderstorm at 6. p.m.
Spent most of evening on lounge —
to bed soon after 10.
