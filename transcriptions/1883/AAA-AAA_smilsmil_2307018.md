## Left Page

### September, Friday 21, 1883

Very warm & pleasant — soon after bkfst. Mr. Blakesley of Montrose & Mr. Boss, an artist, of Binghampton called & spent some time going over the house — Then I packed bf— d No. 25 to Sup. to K. & V., & wrote notes to K. & V., G. Mr. Koehler & to Frank Lewis. Annie Fook pkg — trustees down town at noon & I set to work on part of wall in main hall that is defective — Dinner at usual time after that, made pencil sketch of old Stephens Dorsey — then down to Darkie Town & got sketches of two little negroes & some chickens, all for my 9×15 etching of "my colored neighbors hen-yard." Home about 6 — tea about 7. Evef. Annie read aloud for awhile from "Bourbon Lilies" — I soon fell asleep — To bed at 9.30.

## Right Page

### September, Saturday 22, 1883

Delightful day, altho' the wind from the east presages a change — It was 11 o'clk. before I got to my work on W.C. study, hillside n.w. of house. Worked faithfully until 1 — then home to dinner — It was nearly 4 o'clk. when at last I got away with my oil traps — went to meadow on D.D. & W. depot road, south of the lake & worked until 6 on oil sketch, looking toward the pond outlet — a muddy piece of work, unsatisfactory in itself, but I am sanguine of good results all coming — Home at 6.30 — tea about 7 — after 8 went down town to P.O. etc — learned that Mr. Colpe, south of town, died last night from blood poisoning, the result of defective plumbing and a poisoned well — Home before 9 — To bed at 10.30.
