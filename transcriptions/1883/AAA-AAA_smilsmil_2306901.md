## Left Page

### January, Sunday 28, 1883

Dismal, dark day — steady but not heavy rain — Did not get up until near 10 o'clk. — then breakfast — after that, read papers for awhile, then wrote letters for awhile — Abt 4 o'clk went to studios — home to dinner at 2 — after that, a rest, then wrote more letters — again a rest — tea at 7 — Annie read aloud to me for awhile — then I wrote to Father + Sam. Coleman. About 10 took letters to depot. Home + to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### January, Monday 29, 1883

Nasty day — trying, from time to time to snow — Got to studio after 9 — set to work on etching small plate of Marblehead neck cause — way — but the ground blistered off the plate in several great patches in an unaccountable way — cleaned it off — laid other ground in other plate — Home to dinner at one — back about 3 — Outlining a tracing for etching 7 × 9 — Late in afternoon went to Knoedler's + Wunderlich's + Kobbe's — talking to them extra Haden tickets — Home to tea at 6.30 — wrote until 8.30 — then to Sutton's Rooms in 23d St. — meeting of Art Club to arrange for an exhibit of Drawings by members of the club — not interested — slipped out soon after 10 — home — a frosting of snow. To bed soon after 11.
