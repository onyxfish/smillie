## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 17. 1883.

A very hot day — after bkft. did
a little packing & wrote a note to
D. Yale — waited until 10.15 for Chas
who did not come — then went to
studio — packed glass — Soon after
12 the faucets were brought from
Beekman St. & then I finished
packing rug, blanket etc. — & at
1 went to D. & W. Sltp. office &
then home — Louise Dakcy there
to dinner — After that took things
very easily, packing trunk and
resting — about 6 went to 4th Ave
car stables & made sketches of
cars & horses for A.B.N. Co. Slight
shower — Home to tea at 9 —
Swelf. charming cool breeze —
finished packing before 10
— to bed about 10.30.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 18. 1883.

*Montrose*

Charming day, air quite cool —
up at 6.30 — bkft. at 7.30 — Carriage
& wagon for baggage came at 8 — by
8.10 we all got away very comfortably
at the ferry we met Roswa & Anna,
our two new domestics — they joined
the caravan & we took the 9 o'clk
boat. The boy was soon fixed in his
compartment & gave very little trouble — Annie got along very nicely.
At 2. we got to Scranton & had a
plentiful dinner & about 3.30 we
got to Montrose Station where a
Barouche & Baggage wagon was in
waiting for us. The ride to town
was pleasant but almost too cool.
Got to house about 5 o'clk. & found
that Mrs. Powley had left without making
any preparation for our tea — I had
to go down town & curry around —
it was real discomfort to the tired
ones to find everything so unprepared
but the girls flew around & by 9.30
we got to bed — The boy had snuffles
all night so that A. & I got very
little sleep. Had fires to keep warm.
