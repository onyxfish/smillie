## Left Page

### May, Wednesday 2, 1883

Very pleasant day — nearly ten o'clk when I got to studio — made outline
of figure of Liberty, on gelatine, for father — then to work trying to compose
a sun dial subject for an etching to illustrate Lamb's Essay on Benches
of the Inner Temple — made but poor progress. Father came in for a few
minutes — Calverley modeled —
Home at 1 — dinner soon after —
Father came in after 2 + had his dinner — I wanted + at 3.15 went
with him + Mother to G.C. Depot to take 4 o'clk. Train to Pok. — left
them there + got to studio at 4. I
made no progress with design —
just before 6 went to Pike's + sketched
a sun dial — then up to Grady McK's
shop — too late — then to Dickel's + got
cardigans — met J. Z. A. Ward and
walked to 52d St. with him — Home
to tea at 7.15 — Mother Cook spent
the evg. at Edw'd's — Annie read
aloud H.J. James' article on Du-
Maurier — in last "Century" — To bed
just before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Thursday 3, 1883

Dubious wth., clearing mild + rather
muggy before noon. Walter came before
9 to arrange for a trip to Bellport this
p.m. but it was thought best to delay
it owing to promise of bad weather —
Went to McKeever's shop in 42d St., then
to Bierstadt's — Bdway + 32d St. — with lph. of
Rocky Mts. for his autograph — he was
not in — then to studio after 10 + spent
the rest of a.m. in languid fussing
over composition of sun-dial subject
for an etching — Koehler called — Home
at 1 — back at 3 — made a slight sketch
from Geo. & Nellie — + still fussed over
design. Walter came in at 3.45 to
say that we had only time to catch
a train + we agreed not to try to
catch it — at 5 went to Flint's + met
Annie there, bought furniture for Montm
— then to Willard's + ordered bedding — then
home at 7 + had tea — Evg., Annie
read aloud, from Princeton Review
a doleful article on "Am. Art, 1883"
by Clarence Cook. — It amused us
more than it irritated — To bed at
10.45.
