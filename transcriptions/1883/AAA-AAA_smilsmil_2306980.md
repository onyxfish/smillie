## Left Page

### July, Saturday 7, 1883.

A red hot day — the worst of the lot by statement of Ther. — but a strong westerly breeze tempered the heat materially — Got to studio before 10 & at once to work faking insp. from plates 25 & 26 — I took 3 of each — the result was rather disappointing, as both plates show need of more work that I had thought — Home about 1.30 — dinner — Charle came work some Montross photos before 4 — I hurried away & caught 4.30 train from Long Island City to Long Beach — Got there in an hour — delightfully cool & breezy — Inquired terms at hotel — found that Annie, nurse & I could be accommodated for $122.50 per week — Nothing less than $84.00 — Took tea in Restaurant — Thunder storm at 8. 9.25 train started at 10.10 & broke down on the way — Got in to L. I. City at 12 — Home, after many vexations, at 12.30 & to bed at 1.

## Right Page

### July, Sunday 8. 1883.

A blessed change in the weather. Rain began before 2 & fell heavily the rest of this day — wind shifting to N.E. — About 11 went to studio & picked up & put away things preparatory to getting off for what remains of the summer — Home to dinner at 2 — The afternoon was passed idly as I felt the effects of last nights dissipation — Tea at 7 — everyone on lounge until near 10 — then wrote letter home about 10.30 Took it to G.C.D — and mailed it. Raining very hard — wind blowing like a March storm — Home & to bed at 11.
