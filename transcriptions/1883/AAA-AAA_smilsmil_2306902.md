## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 30, 1883

Beautifully clear & pleasant, except under foot, where it was horrid.
After doing some writing it was 10.45 when I got to studio — Mr. Moran came — I signed lease of studio — then F. Seymour Haden & F. Nelpke came — a long talk on lecture & Painter Etcher's Socy. business — after 12 we went to W. S. 48th at N.A. — from there to Chickering hall, which we carefully inspected — I got home to dinner at 2 — after that a rest — then to studio & after 5 went over to Starr's & there met Annie — bought a writing desk — then to Putricans & home soon after 6 — tea at 7 — Ev'g. wrote to Parrish & directed to artists a lot of Haden tickets — ab't 9.30 took a lot of them out and mailed them — to bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 31, 1883

Tremendously heavy rain — dark, with some thunder and lightening — Afternoon, cleared off very pleasant. After 10.45 wrote more addresses — went to P.O. station & mailed them — 11.30 when I got to studio — I was too much broken up to accomplish anything — Home to dinner at 1 — back at 3 — feeling out of sorts — Mr. Koehler came in & we had a long talk over a proposed book of 20 Am. Etchings to be published by Cassell. Ev'g. Dr. Adams & Nellie am'd. while we were at tea — after 8 Annie & I went to Charleboney's where we met Gilder, Jul. Burrows, Herkomer, Benjamin & others — Pleasant until near 10 — then we went to Holt's — cor. 37th & Lex. Ave. & heard "Willie" Hopker sing — Home after 11 — to bed at 12.

In the m'g., went to printer's & ordered Haden Posters to be put up at Chickering Hall.
