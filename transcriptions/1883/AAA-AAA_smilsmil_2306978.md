## Left Page

### July, Tuesday 3, 1883

Another very hot day — no storm but one passed north of us in the afternoon. Got to studio at 9.30. Dinan called for Etching [illegible] for Vienna [illegible]. & I got them ready for him. Then to work on plate No. 25 — putting in lines & biting. Home at 1 — back at 3 & worked until near 6. Got the lines all drawn in & bitten — cleaned plate — it appears to be a success in spite of tearing up of the first ground. The first, faintly bitten lines appear to be an advantage rather than otherwise. At 6, went to P.O, Station, 3d Ave & 28th St. Then took cars to 76th St., then to Central Park where I wandered about, enjoying it immensely, until near 7.30 — then home — tea — [illegible] — lounging. To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### July, Wednesday 4, 1883

Tremendously hot day — got to studio before 10 & commenced work on Etching No. 26 — "Causeway from Marblehead Neck to mainland." — I etched all the m[illegible] — getting in the foreground stuff — then, at 1, home to dinner. Back at 3 & put plate in the acid for 40 m. Then some more work & again in the acid for less than 20 m. At 6 rode up to the Park — came in at 79th St. gate & sauntered about — out at 72nd St. & home at 7.30. Tea — Evening wrote letters to Farrer — Parrish — & Bellows — At 10, took them out & mailed them — Home — had a sponge bath & to bed before 11.
