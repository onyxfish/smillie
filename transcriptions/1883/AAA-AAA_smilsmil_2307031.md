## Left Page

### October, Wednesday 17, 1883

Another brilliant day — all
the m[e]lf out with the men. The
graders & the carpenters on the
studio bay window — Tarbell made
a call & we talked of buying the
Cooper lot. Then down town —
back before 12 — then with pen-
cil & paper went to road near
the lake & made a hasty sketch.
Home to dinner at 1.30 — after
that again with the men until
4 when Cooly came with his
diamonds & cut glass for transom
over opening to main hall — near
6 when I got glass in transom
up. Before 6.30 Mr. & Mrs. de Scherin-
itz came — tea at 7 — evening in
chatting & whist — they left
at 10.30.

## Right Page

### October, Thursday 18, 1883

A raw, windy March-like day. Up
& had b[reakfast] at 8 — about 10 Mr. & Mrs.
Ehninger arr[ived], having come from Ring-
hampton this m[ornin]g & Saratoga yes-
terday — much of remainder of m[ornin]g
spent with them — altho' I did some
drawing & a little overseeing of the
moving of the privy which has gone
on very unsatisfactorily & with un-
expected slowness. Dinner at
about 1.30 — At 3 carriage came
— Annie could not go — Mr. & Mrs. E.
& I drove until 5 — too windy
— no far distant views — roads
rough — Tea at 6.30. Evlg.
Mr. & Mrs. Wm. Cooper called — very
pleasantly — after they left
Annie played on the piano &
for a time she & Mr. E. amused
themselves with German
songs. To bed at 10.30.
