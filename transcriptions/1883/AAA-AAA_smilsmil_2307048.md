## Left Page

### November, Tuesday 20, 1883

*Martha died.*

Pleasant day — B[illegible] at usual time — after it went up to parlor &
tried to arrange large easy chair borrowed from Walter so that Martha
could sit easily in it — she suffers much difficulty in breathing — when
trying to prop up her head it fell forward & she died with out a struggle.
Kate & Miss Wilcox, the nurse, being present. In 5 minutes after death her
face lost all expression of pain &
looked really beautiful — as soon as
I could I telegraphed home — to Uncle
Willie & to Ed. Parker to come down.
Then I went down town to Charl's [illegible]
on my way back stopped & saw [illegible].
Then home to dinner. Soon after I —
Ed. came at 2.30 & when he learned
the worst was terribly affected — went
to studio between 3 [illegible] — there for an
hour or so — Tea at the usual
time — Charl, & others came
in the eveng. Ed. stayed
with me all night.
