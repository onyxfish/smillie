## Left Page

### December, Friday 28, 1883

Clear, cold, windy day — got to studio at 10 & to work on W.C. mise. nd. neck to coast & worked until 1 o'clk, getting it in shape to leave for the present. Mr. Marvin of Scribner's came with proofs from No. 33 for me to sign & $60. in payment for the etching. Home to dinner at 1.30 — after that, went to Bartholdi Pedestal fund Rebus. Annie joined me there later & by 5 o'clk. we were entirely worn out & went home — Tea at 6.30 — at 8 went to Walther's and spent some time going over plans for Montrose Bay window — home and to bed at 1 —

## Right Page

### December, Saturday 29, 1883

Pleasant day — to studio at 9.30 & to work on W.C. view of Narrows — took [illegible] from B&N Mr. Mail "— Traced — traced & continued it on the paper laid in first washes — Home to dinner at usual time & back at 2.45 — went to Bank at 2.30 & deposited $50 — Worked on drawing as long as I could see — At 5 went to Dickle's & [illegible] until 6.20 — home & had tea at 7 — Quietly on lounge — Annie reading for awhile aloud — Afa[r?] all where [illegible] went to bed —

At 1 a.m. Minna, Dr. [illegible]'s daughter — Walter Pierce's wife, was delivered of a son.
