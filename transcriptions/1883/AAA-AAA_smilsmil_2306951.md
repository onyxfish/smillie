## Left Page

### May, Thursday 10, 1883

Chilly, foggy m'f. Stupid head ache still clings to me & I spent
much of my time sneezing & blowing my nose. To studio ab't 9.30
& kindled fire — Spent the m'f
doing many things about the
studio — nothing in particular
but things that had to be done
some time. Home to dinner
at 1 — back at 3 — until 4.30
in at Tiffany's, selecting a glass
for Montrose & superintending
the cutting of it — about 5
Annie came & with her I went
to Conover's & Jackson's to look at
andirons — also for a few minutes
in to Keppel's to look at etchings —
there A. Rose home & I walked —
Tea about 7. Two of the sisters
Clinch took tea with us, by invitation
to meet Miss Juliette J — I spent
most of the ev'g writing a long
letter to Mary on the subject
of Sister Matt's illness — I wish the
family to learn how imminent I
consider the peril. To bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 11, 1883

Light rain in the m'f., clearing about
the middle of the day — headache too,
that also cleared somewhat as the day
advanced — Got to studio before 10 and
the entire m'f was given to writing
notes & letters demanding immediate attention — W. A. Condit sent me his check for
$150.00, payment for "Rainy day on lake
George" — About noon Gifford came in &
stayed until near 1 — walked up town
with me. To dinner at 1 — To bank
before 3. Thru to Johnston's & bought
underclothes — then to studio and
did a little finishing on Yosemite Fall
painting. Dr Yale called — Home at
a little after 6 — Ev'g, worried
for awhile over Montrose plans —
Edw'd Cook & wife called — To bed
before 11.
