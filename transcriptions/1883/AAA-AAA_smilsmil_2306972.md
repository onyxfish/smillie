## Left Page

### June, Thursday 21, 1883

Very cool & cloudy — Ther. 60° slight shower at noon — Much of m[y] time spent with the man who put down part of vestibule tiles — also with Herrick — bales trade on East Piazza — Between 10 & 11 went down town — number of messages — saw Wm. Coulper — Talked of buying his lot — admits that he thinks of putting a summer hotel on it — Dinner soon after 1 — The main work of p.m. getting mantle piece from kitchen to bed room — found irreconcilable differences in measurements — no end of trouble — knocked [it] to pieces — after 5 went out with C. to get photo views of house. Tramped until near 7 — then in & wrote letter to Annie — Tea at 7.30 — After 8, we went down town — messages — then made a little call on Mr. Parks — Back at 9.30 — Sat about until 11 — then to bed.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 22, 1883

Charming day — B[k]p. at 8 — the m[orning] spent with the man in vestibule & with Austin, painting wall in bed room on main floor — Dinner at 1 — The tile man finished his work early in the afternoon. I felt very tired, having been on my feet & active all day — About 6.30 wrote my letter to Annie — Tea at 7 — Soon after 8 went down town with Charl — after a number of messages called on the de Schweinitz's — found Mr. Westcott there & a stranger whose name I did not get. Home soon after 10 & then to bed.

In the m[ornin]g, Charl took piazza pictures in which I took part. I went down town at 11 — back at 12.
