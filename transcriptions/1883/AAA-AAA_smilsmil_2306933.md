## Left Page

### April, Monday 2, 1883.

Pleasant day — got to studio at 9.30 — the rest spent in clearing away etching & printing material — making paste — mounting drawing of "Crisp October" — etc etc — Home to dinner at 1 — at 3 went down Town — first to Chase's & got draft on London of £10.11 — to pay Painters Etcher's Dues — then to Will's — then to Ritchie's — notion — then to Walter Cook's, where we discussed plans for Montrose main room until after 6 — then home — Tea after 7 — Well being stupid with a headache I lounged in front of the fire until about 11 — then to bed.

Matt & Kate came down from [illegible] this m[orning] & [illegible] at 4 P.m.

## Right Page

### April, Tuesday 3, 1883.

Still very delightful weather, but my head does not respond — a thick, unpromising fog of a headache made the morning heavy to me. Got to studio at 9.45 — Had a number of callers — G. W. Ritchie, among others, with inks from the Scribner etchings I have just finished — disappointed — they look dry & poor — Spent the m[ornin]g sketching false paper [illegible]. Home to dinner at 1.30 — Before 3, to Bank — then to barber's & had hair cut & head shampooed — felt better — to studio at 4 — found a note from W. Marvin of Scribner's, enclosing ck. for $100 & expressing great satisfaction with the etchings — fussed with [illegible] until after 6 — then home — Eve[nin]g. A. & I went to Casino & saw La Mascotte — Madame Theo — a vulgar creature — no good singing — a little good acting — left at 11 o'clk — end of 2d act — our party was Kitty Ireland & Chas. Collins, Emma & Grace & [illegible] J. — & Valentina. — Home & to bed at 11.30.
