## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 8. 1883.

A very hot day with heavy thunder storm at 5 p.m. Got up with a nasty sore throat that extended down into my chest. Got to studio about 9.30 & after a time to work painting on Yosemite fall — about near finished. — Ernest Longfellows called about noon — Home to dinner at 1 — back at 3 — The afternoon spent writing letters to Dr. E.O. Harrington & Messrs Hogarth & Sons, London — and in mixing experimental colors for walls of rooms at Montrose. Worked until near 7 — then home — Tea at 7.30 — Eveʼg. wrote a long letter to Cottrel.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 9. 1883.

Very mild spring day — Studio. A headache made me uncomfortable all day — with added miseries of a cold in head & throat. Got to studio about 9.30 & spent the m'g. making trials in oil to guide me in the painting of interior walls when I get to Montrose. Miss Juliette Ireland of Bridgeport to dinner — making a visit. Back soon after 2 & met Geo. & the studio & then to Annual Meeting of the N.A. where we spent the after-noon — no excitement — I did not feel well enough to stay to dinner after the adjournment at 5. I went again to studio, then home soon after 7 & had tea — Eve'g. Annie read aloud an article descriptive of East River Bridge from Harper's mag. To bed before 11. Tremendous thunderstorm between 3 & 4 A.M.
