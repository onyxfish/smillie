## Left Page

### December, Wednesday 26, 1883

Bright mtg. — overcast p.m. — rain at night. To studio ab[ou]t 1 —
got to work on "Bit of Marble" —
head Neck coast — W. C. 13×19.
Worked with desperate resolution
to make something fresh & fine
— bothered by the arrival of a
half ton of coal — Chas. Collins called —
Home soon after — dinner — back
at 2.30 — worked as long as I could
see — at 5.30 went to Fulton St. —
bought Diary — Home at 6.45 — Trouble
— water in gas pipes — after tea
went to Grady's & ordered frames —
He sent a man over to the house
to let water out of gas pipes —
Ret'd home at 10 — to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### December, Thursday 27, 1883

Dark, slushy day — streets in a
neglectful condition — heavy rain
late in the afternoon. Got to studio
about 10 — To work on drawing —
just before going home to dinner,
washed out a good part of sky. —
Back before 3 & washed in a
new sky — much better. Before
dark had my drawing all in
& tolerably well together — think
it a pretty fair work for me.
After 5, took a nap in big chair —
home soon after 6 — tea — Well
at 8, went with Annie to
Mother Cook's in 39th St — Will
Cook & his eldest daughter there
on a visit — a little family
meeting — home at 10. Wrote
a note to H.F. Richter of Phila and
to bed at 11.30.
