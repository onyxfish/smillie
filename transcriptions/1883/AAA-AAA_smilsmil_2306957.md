## Left Page

### May, Tuesday 22, 1883.

Rains in the mtf. — overcast and rather chilly all day — Got to studio about 9.30 & after varnishing backs of plates I started upon the work of putting some sheep in the foreground of my W.C. painting "Causeway from Marblehead neck to Mainland". I Traced them on paper in the position I wished them, then retraced them on to the picture — Home to dinner soon after 1 — back at 3 — washed out carefully the forms of sheep & further cleaned them with pumice rubber & carefully outlined them with pencil — Charl came in at about 5 — Before 6 I went to Dewey & Mastings and ordered pantaloons — Home to tea at 7 — Evtf. worked on full size drawing of transom light & sash over front door — Montrose to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### May, Wednesday 23, 1883.

Rather raw & overcast all day — Worked on Montrose plans until near 10 — then with Annie went to Margaret Parker White's & bid her good bye — She sails early to-morrow mtf for England — Then to studios and spent the mtf. putting in with water color sheep in Marblehead neck picture — Home at 1 — back at 3 — on my way was sieged with a headachy blindness that made it impossible for me to work — Annie came soon after 4 — we went to Vantine's & got some things — then to Wunderlich's and bought a proof of "Solitude" by Chamuel after Rousseau & "Making Drink" by Breton Riviere — Put Annie into a car — I walked home — Tea at 7 — I wrote a letter to Mother congratulating her upon her 74th Birthday (to-morrow) — Took it to G.C. depot at 10.30 — then home & to bed.
