## Left Page

### August, Saturday 18, 1883

A hot day with threats of a thunderstorm which came with heavy rain bet. 3 & 4 o'clk. P.M. Spent the m'g at work on my etchings, most of the time on 26 — wet paper for printing at noon — Dinner abt — commenced printing about 3 & stuck to it until after 7 — Had some good & some bad luck — consider the plates about done — Exceedingly tired — Tea at about 8. Evg Annie read aloud awhile from Mrs Chamberney's "Bourbon Lilies" To bed at 11 — did not get to sleep until after 3 a.m. — a series of heavy thunderstorms from about 1 to 3 o'clk.

## Right Page

### August, Sunday 19, 1883

A breezy, cloudy hot day — slept until nearly — got up feeling like an owl & had [illegible] at near 10 — the m'g was spent lounging, not feeling able to do anything else — Dinner at 2 — after that more lounging — storms south of us, & which we got slight spatters at about 4 P.M. — wrote letters for awhile — Tea at 7 — Evg —, more letters — about 10, went down to P.O. To bed at 11.
