## Left Page

### October, Tuesday 9. 1883.

Another very pleasant day — The
melf. until 12 used up in
directing the work of carpenters
men grading & men putting
up stove pipes in the attic.
Partus was not at work in the
melf — came in the afternoon —
feeling very much worn out, I lay
down from 12 to 1 — then had dinner
— before 3 o'clk — I was out in a full
north of the house with my W.C.
& by 5 o'clk. had completed a
sketch. Then home & superintended
graders until 6 — then walked for
exercise until near 7 — then in
& had tea — We Ey. wrote notes
to Mr. Geo. Whitney, now visiting
at 126 E. 35th S. N.Y. & to O.H.S.
To P.O. at 9 — lovely moonlight night
— To bed at 10 —

## Right Page

### October, Wednesday 10. 1883.

Delicious mild day. Leaving with a
fog. Ther. on north side of house, up to
72°. After melf. got through with my
chores as soon as possible & soon after
10 took large W.C. study & went to
the place & worked on foreground of
it until 1 — Then home to dinner.
After that, with the men at grading
until 3 — then took plate 6×12 &
went out & made etching No. 31 —
worked faithfully until near 6 —
& got it done — Tea near 7 —
lovely, mild moonlight night —
At 8 went down to market — back
& worked on Transom plan until
10 — Then to bed.
