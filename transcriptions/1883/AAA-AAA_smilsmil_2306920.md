## Left Page

### March, Wednesday 7, 1883

Overcast mtg., clearing later —
bright, cold night — Spent the
mtg. worrying over "Old Orchard"
— also' this the last day for
receiving pictures at the N.A. — I
worked to get it ready to send.
Home to dinner as usual & back
— more work on Orchard & then
had the janitor fake [illegible] to
the N.A. at night — Home at 6.30
— about 8.30 at Century & met
Perry, Wm Beard, McEntee, Bristol
Wm Hart & Wood. to further consider the project of a "Rotary"
Exhibition — Perry submitted
a rough draft of a constitution —
I think well of the project, but
not so well of the men who are
directing the movement — I
left them at 10.20 — home &
to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Thursday 8, 1883

Clear, windy, wintry day — Got
to studio before 10 — to work painting on 12×16 "Near Provinceshield
Point" — worried all the morning
over sky & sea — much to my disgust, as I had hoped to be able
to do a fresh, vigorous piece of
work. Home to dinner soon after
1 — back at 3 — Annie went with
me, & sat for Calverly, who has
re-commenced the bas-relief
likeness of Annie — I worked until
almost 6 — more successfully
than in the mtg., I covered the
canvas. Then home. — A. — Took
tea at [illegible] — I took tea at
home — after that, a rest; at 8.30
washed brushes, then dressed
& at 9.30 at Mr Robt. Gordon's Reception
to artists & friends — a very pleasant time as usual — left at
11.45 — home & to bed at 12.45
