## Left Page

### May, Friday 18, 1883.

Delightful day — after[illegible] went up to Dickee's for the drawing I had left there yesterday & then returned to Dard's, the florists in 44th S. & made sketches from plants directly upon my drawing ill'g Haub's essay "Old Bench ers of the Inner Temple" — giving sun dial there until noon — then to florists, cor. of 5th Ave. & 39th S. & made another sketch, then home & before dinner wrote letter to Cotrill. After dinner some work on montrose plans — Eliz. Howe called — To studio at 3.15 — worked awhile on Sun dial ill'n. At 5, Annie, Geo. S. & I went up to Central Park and 79th S. & enjoyed an hour of fresh air & sunlight. Home to tea at 7 — nearly. Annie read aloud from Atlantic a paper by R. G. White. I assorted some of my letters and papers & got to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 19. 1883.

*at home. Po'keepsie.*

Very warm & very pleasant — to studio at 9.30 — daddled over 3 sun dial subject until 1 o'clk — To bagels at 12.30 & deposited money — then home to dinner — After that, clearing up my "muss" of old letters & papers until 3 o'clk — then to G. C. Depot — thy 3.30 train to Po'k — which place I reached, very hot and very dirty at 5.30 — walked up home & found Dr. Otis with Matt who was suffering much pain in arm & breast. I saw Dr. O. alone & he gave me little hope that Matt could live the summer through — I had a quiet talk for a few minutes with Kate & find her calm with mind prepared for the worst. She is doing wonders in her care for Matt. Spent later part of evening with father & mother. To bed at 10.30.
