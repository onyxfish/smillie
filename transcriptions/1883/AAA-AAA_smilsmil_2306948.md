## Left Page

### May, Friday 4, 1883

Very bright pleasant day, in spite of a somewhat nipping east wind. Got to studio before 10 & to work on the sun dial composition — fussed over it all the m[ornin]g. & Calverley modelled. At 1, went home & had dinner. At 2.30 went to Mary (Ned) Cook's, where Annie was lunching with half a doz. other ladies — I sat at table with them for about 10 m. Then A. & I. went to foot of 34° E[ast], met Walter, Maine & little Edw[ard] & took 3.20 boat & from out. N.R.R. for Belle Port. Two miles w. of Valley Stream Station our engine broke down, causing a delay of one hour — a new engine took us on from V.S. but made up no lost time — After a very cold & raw when we got to B. Rode at once to Maj. Underhill's — where we soon had a hot & ample dinner — The rest of the event was killed until 10 — when we went to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Saturday 5, 1883

Bleak & overcast. Ther. unable to struggle up to 60°. Brkf[ast] at 8 — after gave rode out to Walter's house — found it so windy & cold that the drive this time were dismissed and we wandered at will — an hour or more was spent about the building which is being shingled — everything chaotic. Then Annie, Maine & little Edward went off in the woods & after much searching we found a little arbutus, got back to the hotel a little before 1 — then had dinner — at about 3 we started for Patchogue, some 15 miles or so distant — going with the wind it was a very dirty ride but not so cold. Took Train at about 3.45 & got to N.Y. at 6.10 suffering from a violent headache. Took some tea, but before 8 was obliged to lie down — went to bed for the night at 9 with a splitter of a nervous headache — but got to sleep before 10.
