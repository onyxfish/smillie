## Left Page

### May, Thursday 24, 1883

Splendid day — worked on Montrose
plans until about 10 — Then to studio
& worked until 12 on Rough Spot. W.C.
putting some general washes of color
over it to "bring it together" — Then went
over to Fifth Ave. & saw the 1st Regt.
go down to the Brooklyn Bridge, opening. Home to dinner at — after dinner
Dr. Yale came & after 3, M. & J went to
Edw. Cook's for awhile — then, by R.R.
to City Hall at 4. — went to top of "Tribune"
Bldg. & saw the Bridge — Then I went to
B.N. Co., saw Will & got tickets to pass me
to top of that block to-night. Then home
before 6 — tea at 6.30 — at 7 — to 42d St. & 6th
Ave. with Annie & Mother Cook — by E. R. R.
went down town — a tremendous crush
— had to stand on platform — Got to top of
A.B.N. Co. bldg. Bdway & Liberty — before 8 —
Superb view of the Bridge, fireworks,
illuminations in Brooklyn — the River,
etc — etc — left at 8.50 — home without
trouble before 10 — to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### May, Friday 25, 1883

Very warm, pleasant day — after
help, wrote letter to Cottrell & then to
studio at about 10 o'clk. — made
reduction & tracing of Marblehead
neck causeway for Etching — also
a pencil sketch of Geo's hand for
Sun Dial etching — home to dinner
at — Mother C — at Greenwood — back
before 3 — Reduction & tracing of Sun
dial — Parsons, & Harker Bros. called
— arranged with me to make sketch
looking up East River of cover of Mag.
London Edition — also gave me order
to make Christmas design. At 5 o'clk
went to Dickel's & rode around the
C. Park on "Tom", a wheezing, stumbling
horse, but free & easy — again made
sore by chafeing — on Return, met Ward
& went with him to look at a bust he
is just modelling of Toney (or something
like that) of Washington — Then home &
had a bath & at 7.30, tea — Read my
Post — then worked on Montrose
plans & to bed soon after 10.
