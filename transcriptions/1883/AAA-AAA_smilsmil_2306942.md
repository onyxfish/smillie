## Left Page

### April, Sunday 22, 1883

Persistent pouring rain all day & a cold, raw, north easterly wind. Blcfs- about 9 — read still uncomfortable, aching — sat all the mf. before parlor fire —
Dinner at 2 — Afternoon, read some of Lamb's Essays. At 6.30 went out & took a short walk in the rain — Tea about 7.30 Eveg. more Lamb — wrote a note to Bishop Putnam and my usual letter to father — out after 10 and mailed them.
To bed about 11.

## Right Page

### April, Monday 23, 1883

Persistent rain still continues, & the wind blows cold & raw from the N.E. Got to studio at 9.30 spent some time getting transfers on to etching grounds for geo — & one for myself — 6 × 15 — Marblehead Neck sketch — father surprised me by coming in at about 11 — He & mother came down on Sat. & spent Sunday at H.V.V.e — Aunt St— just Ret'd. from the south. Home to dinner abt — Tuinie then brought mother to the house. — Back to studio at 3 & worked on Rough Sport, W.C. until after 5 — then father came — after 6 where we walked up home together — Tea at 7 — Eveg. all together — I worked on Montrose plans —
To bed about 11.
