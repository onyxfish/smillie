## Left Page

### March, Friday 9, 1883

Bright, clear day. To studio at
9.45 — Spent some time studying
over 12×16" near Crowninshield Paint-
painted in yesterday — then
to work making reduced tracing
of Street in FontArabia for etching
for Dr. Victor's Basque book. —
Annie again sat for Calverley who
worked on bas-relief. Home at
1 — dinner — late to Phil. Rehear-
sal. Wagner memorial program.
Enjoyed it very much. Mr. Colman
there. After Rehearsal went up
to "Rembrandt" to see Herkomer —
he is in Boston — Weir[illegible] & called
on Gifford — then to Dickle's and
rode horseback until near 6 —
then home — a short rest before
tea at 7 — rest at home. Young
James Wright called. Then Walter
& Marie — to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### March, Saturday 10, 1883

Wind & spiteful rain all day — clear at
night. Got to studio at 9.30 & to work
on Mr. Conduit's picture — a study, about 15×24.
made one rainy day, from Addie Hawes'
window on Lake Geo. Worried over it
until I went home to dinner at 1 —
back at 3 & continued the worry
until 5 — Miss Clara Walworth called.
Went to Bellows' Room & got his sig-
nature to letter to Council of Painter Etch-
ers Soct. Also, read to him Osgood's letter
offering us half royalty to sell out Poets &
Etchers — Home, exceedingly tired, at
6.20. Edith Wright on from Phila to go
with us to Phil. Concert. Tea at 6.30 —
then a rest & at 8 — with a — Edith
& Ernest, to Phil. In spite of feeling
so much used up, I enjoyed the
Walküre (Wotan's farewell & the fire-
charm) & Gotterdämmerung (Siegfried's
death) immensely — Mr. & Mrs. Sam
Colman there — Home at 10.30 —
to bed soon after 11.
