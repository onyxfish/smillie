## Left Page

### July, Thursday 5, 1883

Another horribly hot day. Ther. in the neighborhood of 90°. In miss A's room, where I worked, it ranged from 84° to 86°. Got to studio at 9.30 & to work on No. 26 — etching & biting — Mr. J. M. Falconer called. Home to dinner at 1. Annie suffering very much from the heat, loss of appetite & nausea with occasional vomiting. The boy still keeps well. Back to studio at 3 — worked until 6 & got all dummy etching in except the sky — Home at 6.30 — Thunder storms passed near us at 7 — we had slight showers — Wetf. kept quiet; to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### July, Friday 6, 1883

Still horribly hot — to studio at 10 & to work on No. 26 — Etched in the sky & gave it 3 m. in the acid — Charl came in. Home to dinner at 1 — back at 3 — put some more work on sky — stopped out a part of it & gave the rest 2 m. more — then cleaned off plate — Wet down papers to take imps. in the m'g. wrote to Kathleen & to Catnell — At 6.30 got [illegible] mad. ave. cars & rode up to 80° St then home at 7.45 and had tea — Wetf. trying to keep cool — to bed before 11 — Thunder storms south of us.
