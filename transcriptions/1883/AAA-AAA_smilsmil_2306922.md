## Left Page

### March, Sunday 11. 1883.

Clear, windy & cold with occasional slight snow flurries. BKp after 9 — Stupid headache — after reading the paper for a while. I rolled myself in big blanket on my bed & spent the rest of the morning there — got up abt — wrote a couple of notes — out & had a short walk — in & had dinner at 2 — after 4, some more writing — To Osgood & Co — there, with Annie went to Edw'd Cook, & saw Mrs. E. & the new baby — then had a short walk — on our return found Mr. Coleman at our door — short call — Tea at 7 — Edith & Emma came in — I wrote my letter home — Coleman came in — he left at 10 & I took letters to depot — to bed before 11.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 12. 1883.

Bright, breezy day — To studio at 9.30 & got to work on 12×16 "near Crowninshield's Point, Marblehead" — Worried over sky & sea most of the m'f. Annie gave another long sitting to Calverly & we went home abt to dinner, after I had taken Walter's Tracings to Epp. to Cottrell for measurements — Back to studio at 2.30 and worked as long as I could see & finally got my picture to hang fairly together — but sky & sea is heavily blue, about 6 took a walk up 5' ave — then home to tea — Eve'g, alone with Annie as Mother Cook went to Germantown with Edith by 4 P.M. train — we had Reading, music & working on Montrose plans — to bed at 10.30.
