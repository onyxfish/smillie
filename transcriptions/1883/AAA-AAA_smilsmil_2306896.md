## Left Page

### January, Thursday 18. 1883.

Clearing day & nieds — a hard one for me — After bkfst. went to studio & found a number of letters with checks for Haden Fund & spent some time in attending to H's change of mind concerning parts of for place for Etching Catalogue — Got to N.A. at 2 — at about 10.30 — learned that Chase had pushed through his Con. Amend. last night, which put me much out of humor — could do little hanging, being so at variance with Chase. Soon after 12 went to Bank & to Putnam's — then home to dinner at 1 — back to N.A. about 3 — there until 5.30 — home at 6 — tried to get a little rest — fanny, Emma & Grace to tea — at 8, took them to Singing club — met Mr. Betty then to N.A. there until 10 — very tired & very cross — then to Hettimball's & waited there until near 11 for Annie — home at 11.30 & to bed — but so worried over the hanging business & Chase's dominance that I got but little sleep.

## Right Page

### January, Friday 19. 1883.

The nastiest of weather again, dull m't. snow before 11 & at night — Rain that froze as it fell — Got up with a head on me — after bkfst. spent some time getting ready Haden receipts & at 10.15 started for studio — met Mrs. Saul Coleman who is in town with her husband for a few days — Got to N.A. at 10.45 — Chase not there until 5 P.m — no work of importance done — I worked with H. Smith until 12 — I changed position of my large W.C. from S. Room to corridor — Chase patronizingly approved — at 12.15 to Bank again & to Putnam's about Haden printing — Home to dinner at 1 — at 2.30 Annie went to Frank Waller's studio — an entertainment — reading by Miss Cowell — I went to N.A. and worked until 5.30 — then to studio — met Geo. went to Station (P.O.) 3d ave. & 28th S. — Home at 6 — tea at 6.30 — to N.A. at about 8 & worked until 10 — home at 10.30 & soon to bed.
