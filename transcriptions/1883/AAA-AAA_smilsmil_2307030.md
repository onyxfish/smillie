## Left Page

### October, Monday 15, 1883

Clear, cold windy day — Spent much of the [illegible] out of doors with the graders — & even put my hand to the work myself. Went down town in the [illegible] & attended to a number of things — Dinner at the usual time — After that out again until about — then in and tried to take a nap — no use, but got a rest — At 6.30 Annie & I went to Dud. Latrobe's to teas — quite a gathering of towns people — 30 or more — Had tea after 7 — Then chatting until 9.30 — then home — The boy kept us up until 11 — after that, the worst night we have yet had with him — so wakeful & cross.

## Right Page

### October, Tuesday 16, 1883

Brilliant quiet day — Ther. went down to 26° last night. Up late after a much disturbed night. After looking after the men & getting my chores done, I took pencils and paper & at about 10.30 started out for the hills east of us where there is a fine view of Elk Mt. I got two sketches, not satisfactory, owing to the absence of foliage — Home and had dinner at 1.30 — After that a short rest & then out with the men — Colville got started on the studio bay window — I got coal tar & smeared a number of trees where the bark had been stripped off — After 6 when I came in — tea before 7 — After that, down town, went to Hy. Turrell's to see him about attending same — no progress — Home & wrote to Jos. W. Drexel & aunt H. To bed at 10.30.
