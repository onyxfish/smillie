## Left Page

### February, Thursday 1, 1883

Partially clearing & rather
cold. Got to studio's at 10 —
cleared up the room and my
etching corner & wrote a note
or two. At 12 went to Bank —
then to Chickering's & paid for
Hall — then to Neppel's. Gifford
there — then home to dinner at 1 —
a rest after it & at 3 to studio —
Herkomer soon came in & the
rest of the afternoon was given to him.
He pulled impr's on my press & worked
on a small plate he brought. After
5 I went to Knoedler's — then to Wunderlich's — then to Guenthing & met the
Haden Com. Ordered to send out 400
more tickets to subscribers. Yale
walked up with me & stopped in
for a while to see Dr. A— Eve'g — I
did not go out, as invited, but worked
on Haden illustrations — To bed
about 11.

## Right Page

### February, Friday 2, 1883

Partially overcast, cold and raw.
Went to P.O. Station in 3rd ave. and
stamped & posted a number of envelopes with Haden tickets to artists and
others — then to studio — then to N.A. &
left tickets with Galt — then to Putnam's & ordered more Haden printing — Back to studio at 11.30 — made
paste & mounted Champney's W.C.
sketch & an etching — then home to
dinner — back to studio at 3 —
a long call from Dr. Yale. I wrote
draft of letter to council of Painter-
Etchers' Soct'y of London from members
in this country. About 5 J[illegible] M.
Falconer came in, then Van Elten,
Bellows & Farrer. We discussed
the situation — whether to exhibit
in London this season or not — until
6.30 — Decided not to — Home to tea
at 7 — Eve'g — Got a new list of Haden
tickets ready for original subscribers
& took them, at 8.30, to Dis, [illegible] etc. Then home —
Read & wrote until after 10. Then
to bed.
