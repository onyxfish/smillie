## Left Page

### June, Friday 29, 1883

Very muggy, hot day — a slight shower between 3 & 4 p.m. — Up at 8.40 after a restless night — felt bunged up — got to studio at 10 & commenced proving "The Sun Dial" & got along very well — Home at — back at 3 & worked hard until 7.15 getting all of my proofs Falcon in a satisfactory way. Nicoll sends urging me to send to the Vienna Exhn of Graphic Arts — Home at 7.30 — tea — rest &c. on lounge until 9.30, then wrote short note to Charl — we have very good news of Matt's condition. Dr. Smith is very hopeful — Out at 10.30 & mailed note — To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### June, Saturday 30, 1883

Excessively hot day with heavy thunder storms in the mtf. and again in the afternoon — To studio before 10 & to work on etching of Marblehead neck, from small oil sketch (plate No. 25). Worked all the mtf drawing in lines on darkest part of foreground. Home to dinner soon after — back at 2.30, just in time to escape a shower — Put my plate in the acid & in a very few minutes the ground began to tear up — no doubt, it had been injured by too much pressure in transferring outline. I scratched in all the outline — also sky & distance & let all bite for a couple of minutes — then laid a solid ground over all & began the etching de novo — I don't know how it will work. After 7 when I got home — every doing nothing in particular to bed before 11.
