## Left Page

### April, Wednesday 4. 1883.

Another pleasant day — To
studio at 9.30 — The morning
spent over proofs & with callers.
Gave out away two Proof copies
of Poets & Etchers. Home to dinner
and — back at 3 & gave Calverley
a sitting for bas relief of my head.
Then to studio — wrote letters
& mounted two P.P. of Etchings
just completed for Mr Marvin
of Scribner's — At 6.30 walked
down to Century & wrote note to
Perry, saying that I could not
attend his Botany Subty committee
meeting this evef. Then home at
7.30 & had tea — Evef. after
reading paper, wrote letter to
Mr Seymour Haden on behalf of
am members of Painter Etchers Ind.
To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### April, Thursday 5. 1883.

Very warm, clean day — To studio at
9.45, after going to Sloan's for stationery,
met Dr. Nulford in the street. Spent
the mf in general work, trying to
get myself up to Condit's picture
of Lake Geo. but a coming influ-
enza made me feel too uncomf-
ortable to work with fresh-
ness or energy. At 1 went home
stopping at P.O. Station in 3d ave. &
mailing letters to Seymour Haden &
Dr. Hamilton, Treas. of Painter Etchers.
Annie & her mother at Mrs. Kimball's
to lunch — so I dined alone — back to
studio before 3 & worked on Condit's
picture while Calverlay modeled on
bas-relief while I painted. Koehler
called — Home at 6.30 — quite
used up by cold in head and
all over — Evef. Annie read
some aloud — To bed at 10.30
