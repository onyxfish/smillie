## Left Page

### June, Wednesday 13. 1883.

An insufferably hot day, close & stifling — Things as usual with Annie & the boy — Got to studio at about 9.30 & until 1 worked over the two Putnam etchings with dry-point, charcoal & burnisher. Home to dinner — back at 3 — overcast — slight sprinkling. Worked until 5 — got [illegible] wet down to take [illegible] to-morrow. Then to Am. Encaustic Tiling Co. in 23d St. & then to tiling place cor. 6th Ave & 23d St. came to no decision — Home at 6.30 — tea at 7 — Eveg. — light Rain & much cooler but little of the cooler air got into the house — Loafed about & wrote a letter to Mr & Mrs. Bassett to bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### June, Thursday 14. 1883.

A perfect day — clear & deliciously cool. Got to studio before 10 & spent the way until after 1 in working on the two Putnam etchings & taking [illegible]. Home at 1.40 & had dinner — then down town to Walter's to get measurements of fire places, then back to [illegible]'s tile establishment in 23d St. There until 6 o'clk. selecting tiles for fire places in Montrose — also arranged to have a man sent up to set them — then to studio found a note from Charl saying that he could not go with me to M— to-morrow. Remained at studio pursing over etchings & [illegible] until after 7 — then home to tea — with Annie until after 9 — then to Bates' drug store — detained there until after 10 — Home & to bed about 11.
