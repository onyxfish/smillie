## Left Page

### February, Friday 23, 1883

fine clear day. To studio at 9.45 — spent mf. on 6¾ × 15½ —
sketch — Home to dinner abt —
Mother Cook started on a visiting
spree to Bridgeport & other
places, at 3 P.m. I got to
studio at 2.45 — finished sketch
& charcoaled 12 × 16 — another
Marblehead subject, Sketched
on way from Crowninshield Point
to Mblehead. Soon after 4 went
to barber's & had my hair cut —
then to Grady's & ordered two
frames — home at 6.30 — Tea —
Read awhile — Packed Jap.
screen to send to Po'ke — at
9 went to Walter's — Talked
Montrose plans — home before
10. To bed at 11.30.

## Right Page

### February, Saturday 24, 1883

overcast; Raw & disagreeable —
snow before 9 P.m — then sleet &
rain. B'kfst. early — got to studio
at 9.30 — To work on 12 × 16 — View
near Crowninshield's Point. Painted
in sky — scraped it off & painted it a
second time — painted distant sea —
scraped it off & went to dinner at 1.
Back at 3 & painted until 5.30 —
covered the canvas — shocking bad
— very tired — 6.30 when I got
home to tea — Evg. lounging
— Reading & doing a little writing
— to bed at 11.
