## Left Page

### July, Sunday 29, 1883

A regular Sep. day — very breezy & very cool. Ther. on
piazza down to 62° — did not get up to 70°. Pleasant to have
fires indoors — Bkf. before 9 —
much of the mf. spent writing
letters & walking piazza with
Annie. Dinner before 2 —
Afternoon, more writing and
a call from Mr. & Mrs. de Schwein-
about 6 went out & strolled for
an hour — then home & had
tea soon after 7 — Swept, a
little more writing in the
studio by a pleasant fire.
Went down to P.O. with letters
— quite a brilliant Aurora —
Home & to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### July, Monday 30, 1883

Very cool, breezy day — Up at
9.30 — After bkf. went down
town & engaged carriage to
take us to depot — At 10.30,
Mother Cook, Annie & I rode
to Montrose Station — Mother C.
returned to N.Y. by 12.02 train —
we got back at 1.30 glad of
overcoat & wraps to keep us
warm — dinner before 2 — then
I did a little etching on No.
25 — but by 4 o'clk. was so over-
come by sleep that I took a
nap — About 5, Mollie & Hetty
Biddle called — Soon after 6 I
went down town — on my way
back, joined Mr. & Mrs. Wm. Jessup
who made us a pleasant call
near 8, when we had our tea
— a quiet eve'g — to bed
at about 10.30. Ther. went
down below 55°
