## Left Page

### December, Monday 24. 1883.

Dark day with some snow & some rain — To studio at 9.30 & got to work on a little W.C. sketch — a bit of Wbl[?] h[?] neck coast — much interested and delighted to find myself, at last, at such work — At Champ[?] request, Geo. & I went down and pitched into his large W.C. draw[ing]. Home at 1 — dinner — back at 2.45 — very dark — stretched another sheet of drawing pap[er] — worked as long as I could on sketch & stretched a few stencil [?]. Sent [?] paper to Osgood in Boston & ph[oned] No. 7 to Richter in Phila. Home at 6.30 — tea — eveng. wrote a long letter to S.C.T. Hammond — To bed about 1.

## Right Page

### December, Tuesday 25. 1883.

A dark Xmas with more snow.

Up late — got to studio about 10 — worked a little on small W.C. sketch for yesterday — finished outlining on W.C. paper "Stray Faults" — prepared other paper for drawings — found that a leak at the window had wet & marled a French etching hanging there — Spent some time washing it — Home at 12.15 — about 12.40 Annie & I, carrying the boy, went to Ed[ward] Cook's in 39th St. — a xmas festival for the children — a very pretty sight — 8 little ones — 20 grown ones — after the tree & presents, an excellent lunch — To studio again at 3 — outlined another little W.C. subject & mounted yesterdays sketch — Heavy snow — went to Dist. Tel. station to see why our wire does not work — Home at 6 — Tea before 7 — eveng. Reading & writing — To bed at 11.
