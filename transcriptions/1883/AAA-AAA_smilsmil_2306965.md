## Left Page

### June, Thursday 7, 1883.

Another intensely hot day. Thur. getting up to near 90°. Family duties kept me until after 10 before I got started for studio — Annie passed a very comfortable night — Some time in Geo's studio — Bruce Crane there — then I got to work printing & before 1 took 3 imps of "Corner at Home" & 2 imps. of "Sun Dial." — Then home to dinner — threatening thunderstorms heavily piled in the west. Had a rest after dinner — then to Sloan's & got cologne & odorator for Annie — then to studio at about 4 — moved a fakee & a number of my things down to Miss Abbott's room — cooler — completed the mounting of 3 pfs. of "Lady of Cairo visiting" — showers falling all the p.m. but atmosphere horribly muggy — Home to tea at 7 — Eveg. passed very quietly with Annie until after 9 — steady rain. To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### June, Friday 8, 1883.

Still very hot, but a mitigation of yesterday's heat — Dr. Yale came before 10 — Annie continuing to improve nicely. I got to studio about 10.30 — Started Geo. biting in his Koehler etching and then to work on N.Y. city & Suspension Bridge for the Harpers — Home at 1.20 — dinner — back at 3 — and divided what remains of the p.m. between the Bridge design — Geo's etching — writing letters etc. — At near 7, went to Waller's with our present for Miss Canning — Home at 7.30 — Emma to tea — Eveg. as usual, quietly with Annie — wrote aunt a note & took J— to debate. To bed at 11.
