## Left Page

### September, Thursday 13, 1883

Steady, soaking rain — Up late again — mf. writing letters
& making drawings for seats in bay window — dinner after 1.
more superintending of carpen-
ter work — at 4 went down
town & took quite a walk
in a mixture of mizzle and
fog. — back at 5 — at 6.30
again down town to P.O. at —
Tea after 7 — warm night &
heavy fog — Eve f. with paper
on lounge for awhile. To bed
at 10.30.

## Right Page

### September, Friday 14, 1883

Cloudy, pleasant & mild. B[illegible]
soon after 8 — much of mf. spent
with Herrick putting seat in bay
window — worked on etching in
piaz for more than an hour —
Dinner at usual time —
after that, washed out some of
the sized canvas & tacked it
up in main hall — After 6 o'ck.
went to P.O. — letter from Saml.
Colman — also one from Mrs. C.
to Annie — Tea about 7 —
Eve f. with paper — walking
on piazza & a little writing —
wrote to Hiram Sibley & Co. of
Rochester, N.Y., enquiring about
lawn grass seed. To bed
soon after 10.
