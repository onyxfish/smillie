## Left Page

### September, Saturday 29, 1883

Unpleasant, threatening day, but no rain came until night —
whew from about 9 until midnight
there was a succession of thunder
showers with much rain —
Emma was taken to D.L.+W. depot
at 10.30 — to go to N.Y. I made a
drawing of seat for hall window +
started Herrick abt. 1 — Dinn.
son after 1 — between 3 & 4 had
a good nap — then made
drawing for bay window transom
mountain hole — Payne [illegible]
Lawn + Dorsey [illegible] dressed it with
leaf moulds. Tea bet. 6 & 7
o'clk — [illegible], a little more
drawing — got casting in bottom
of Main Room fireplace; it seems
to heat the hearth even more
than the direct fire did —
To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### September, Sunday 30, 1883

Very wet day — thunderstorm bet.
6 & 7 o'clk. A.m. Did not get
up until after 9 o'clk. Then
spent the rest of the day between
attending to fires & writing letters
Dinner after 2.30 — a nap in the
afternoon + general loafing —
after 8 when we finished our
tea — quite rain in early
part of eveg. wrote letters
diligently until 10, then to
P.O. with our family mail.
18 letters — Home + to bed
at 11.
