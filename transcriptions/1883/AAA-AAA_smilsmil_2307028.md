## Left Page

### October, Thursday 11, 1883

Overcast until about noon when the weather cleared & again we had delicious Indian Summer. As soon as I could, after bkfs. — I got to work biting my two etchings, 30 + 31 — this takes some time, because I etch upon them while in the bath & otherwise elaborate them — Peters at work with his team — Scott, not — 4 men raking & moving stone, besides Payne's team & Dorsey in the swamp. Dinner soon after 1 — with graders until after 3 — then again at biting. No 5 cleaned. off 31 — hope that I have something of a success in it. with Theresa until 6 — then to P.O. Tea at 6.30. — [illegible]. Took a good rest — glorious moonlight. To bed at 11.

## Right Page

### October, Friday 12, 1883

Overcast — a little rain in the mf. & threats all day — After looking after the graders awhile I got to work & during the mf. finished the biting of No. 30 — did considerable work etching upon & between bitings — Dinner soon after 1 — then cleaned plates & the rest of the P.M. I spent out of doors with the men — got the grass seed sown over the corner in front of Fred Frink's — At 6 went downtown — tea at 7 — [illegible] loafing with newspaper. To bed about 10 — that is, we intended to, but Jas. Jun. had a circus ready for us that was not over until 11 — he kept [illegible] soon after 12 & for the first time in my experience I got up and walked the floor with him, quieting him in a very few minutes — he made other entertainments at 2 & 4 o'clk & I think again at 6 — but by that time I was past taking note of what was passing in the world about me.
