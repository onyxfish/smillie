## Left Page

### January, Tuesday 16. 1883.

My birthday — half a hundred to-day — with much to be thankful for — it is hard
to be very cheerful & realize that the world
is rushing along at its old, breakneck speed
& that the pace is too much for you at 50 years —
that the young men, rejoicing in their strength
pass you in skies of ambitious efforts to
"keep up with the times" — After 6 H.P. army?
Haden cards & at 10 took them to Dist. Tel.
Co. to distribute — snow falling — then to
N.A. of D. Hanging progressing slowly —
Chase & his new ideas ahead of me — home
to dinner at 1 — after that, went over
to Dist. Tel. Co. to complain of the way they had
sent out cards — then to Kelpple's Winderlicks
& Norden's & left 100 cards at each place —
At 3.45 to N.A., & worked there until
5.45 — then to studio — home at 6 — tea
— more Haden — got to N.A. at 8.30 —
there until 10 — home at 10.40 &
right to bed.

## Right Page

### January, Wednesday 17. 1883.

Nasty day — first snow, then rain —
Got up feeling very rusty — did not
get to N.A. until after 10 — nothing done
— Chase not there — Smith wandering in
& out — I set to work — Chase came after
11 — did it like what I had done — I gave
up — I don't dislike C — I like him —
but we are too widely apart in our
way of seeing & thinking to be able to
work together — home at 2.30 & to bed
& had a little nap before dinner. Matt
& Kate came down from Po'k this m'g. —
Saw Dr. Smith & Rel? to Po'k by 4 P.M.
Train — I spent most of the P.M. on
bed, resting. Tea at 6.30 — at 7 —
wrote notes to Chase & Wood & sent
them to N.A. of D. where the Water Color
meeting is to-night. I want Chase to
hold back his amendment to the
Con. until next meeting — wrote notes
to Cottrell — Clarence Sterling &
A. Seymour Haden — to bed
at about 11.
