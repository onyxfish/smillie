## Left Page

### August, Wednesday 8, 1883

Warmer & dryer, altho' not unpleasantly warm. Bkf. as usual. Then for a short time with carpenters who are planing main room floor & preparing to lay floor of main hall — with Austin & with Payne — then to my work, dry pointing on 26. Dinner as usual — then down Town — to Democrat Offices where Lyon read me his hifalutin account of the house — attended to a number of chores & home before 4 — with Austin until 5.15 then out & rode with Annie until 6.30 — Rec'd a letter from Saml. Coleman — Tea at 7.30 — Evef. wet paper to print tomorrow. Looked over bills & did a little writing. Nearly 11 when I got to bed.

## Right Page

### August, Thursday 9, 1883

Another cool hot day — hazy & dry. Mf. until near 11 engaged with the news, then got to my printing — found that my alcohol lamp won't burn — circles probably too long — heated my soapstone on the stove & got along nicely — worked until dinner. about 1.30 — at it again after that & took pf. from both plates 25 & 26 — think they are in an encouraging state. at 5 Mr. deBchevening & sister-in-law called — a few minutes after 5 a. & I went out for a ride & had a very pleasant one, so cool. that I was glad to put on my overcoat & a. wore a shawl — back at 7.30 — tea at 8 — Evef. fussing with my pf. & printing materials. To bed at 10.30.
