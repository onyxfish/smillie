## Left Page

### August, Friday 24, 1883.

Ther. went down to 53° last night
& stood at 60° at bkfs. time — Brilliant
cloudless day — Went down Town
between 10 & 11 — then back & to
work on drawing for A.B.N. Co.
(with interruptions) until 1 o'clk —
Then dinner — At 2, with Ety's
Howe, rode to Montrose depot —
Father & Mother came on the 3.27
Train from N.Y. After a pleasant
ride we reached home at 5 —
both Father & Mother apparently
none the worse for their journey —
Tea at about 7 — before we finished
it, Mr. [illegible] Turree & Mrs. called.
Kindled a fire in main Room which
was very pleasant as we sat and
chatted — F & M to bed before
10. We followed

## Right Page

### August, Saturday 25, 1883.

Superb day — clear & cool — After
bkfs. at 8, engaged with numerous
little matters that took up the whole
of the mng. so that I did little on
A.B.N. Co's drawing — Went down
Town between 10 & 11 o'clk. Dinner
at 1 — Again occupied by
many little things, among other
things, laying out panels & selecting
wood for main hall. About
5 o'clk. out riding — Annie —
Elizabeth & Father — Had a
very good time — back at
6.35 — Tea about 7.30 — Well
in general chatting until
after 9 when Father & Mother went
to bed — I read aloud a few
chapters from "Bourbon Lilies" —
To bed before 11.
