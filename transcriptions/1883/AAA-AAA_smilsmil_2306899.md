## Left Page

### January, Wednesday 24, 1883

not so cold + beginning to haze over head. Got to studio. Picked up a few letters — then to N.A. on my way stopped to see Geo. asking him to withdraw Nellie's picture, as I did not think it would do her credit — Worked on South Room, Smith + I, Chase did not come until noon, when we had it about done, + passed it over to the printer, at last, a part of it — home to dinner at 1 — back at 2.30 — finished So. Room + then, to corridor — great time getting up 16 foot flats, paper umbrellas, hanging in corridor from sky light — Chase came at about 4 — At 6 went to Geo's + had a scene with Nellie — Frank, fearless, kindly criticism is a beautiful theory, but it does not always work — Home — worn out — tea at 6.30, got to N.A. before 8 + Smith + I tried to finish corridor but Chase obstructed by objections — at 10.30 I gave up, sick + tired — The work unfinished — home at 11 — + to bed about 11.30 — but too tired + worried to sleep well.

## Right Page

### January, Thursday 25, 1883

Overcast + milder — got to N.A. about 10 + set to work finishing hanging in corridor. Chase not there — we got everything up that we had room for, before 12 o'clk. but 30 or more pictures (some of them by members) were sent down stairs, much to my regret. I went home very tired about 12.30 — Annie + her Mother did not come in until about 1.40 then we had our dinner — the most of the P.M. I spent on the bed. + had a good rest Dressed. + had tea before 7 — at 7.30 carriage came. + Annie, her mother, Geo. + I went to Chickering Hall, stopping at studio, I made out checks + sent them to Montrose. Choral Club concert, that went off very successfully. Charl + Miss Provost sat next to me — after concert could not find our carriage — went home in stage — after going to our room, curtain took fire from gas burner — a moment of excitement — burned my left hand slightly — enough to interfere with sleep.
