## Left Page

### February, Saturday 3, 1883.

Dark, nasty day — Rain falling &
a thick film of ice over every thing
— many bones broken by falls.
Some mounting of drawings done
Koehler made a long call on the
business of a book of Am. Etchings to
be published by Cassell & Co. — 20
plates — I objected to a com. of three
being selected from among the etchers
to pass judgment upon the other 17 —
they being "hors concours". Home to dinner
at 1 — about 3, annie & I went to the
"Benedict" 80 W. Washington Sq. & saw
Wyatt Eaton & his portrait of Mr. Barrett —
liked exceedingly E's portrait of his
own wife — To studio at 4.30 — Worked
on mounts for W.C's — Yale came
in — at 6 annie came — took home
Mrs. Dr. Bond's Etching — to 122 dey. Mr.
Home to tea at 7 — Abg. to Century
There until 10.15 — then, feeling
decidedly out of sorts, I went
home — to bed at 11.

## Right Page

### February, Sunday 4, 1883.

Very dull & mied in the mrg.
with occasional light Rains —
cooler P.M. — clear at night —
Up late — Bkft. at 9.30. Being
still out of sorts, I lounged in
front of the fire until near noon.
Then to studio for a little while &
from there, over to Will's — 163 W. 34th.
Saw him, but not Joe — then home
to dinner soon after 2 — lounged
again until near 5 — then wrote
a number of little notes — Posted
them & took a short walk about
7 — then in to tea — wrote letter to
Cassells & posted it before 9 — then
annie read a little while aloud
to me — then I wrote letter to
father. To J.C.D. at 10.30 — back &
to bed at 11.
