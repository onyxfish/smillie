## Left Page

### May, Saturday 12. 1883.

Beautiful day — after B&lp. To work making a pencil sketch of a corner in the parlor — book-cases, pictures, etc with Annie in a chair poring over a book — to illustrate Lamb's essay "Detatch-ed Thoughts on Books & Reading". To be etched for Putnams — Miss Juliette's niece dined with us & before 3 they left. At noon I went up to Dr. Yale's — then for an hour nearly — took to him proofs of my Two Etchings for Dr. Victor's book, also a little garment, sent by A. to his wife — After dinner went to studio, there until about 4 — then to Dickle's & rode Pascha in the school — found him an unconquerably energetic beast, a hard buller with exceedingly subtle action — altho' much disappointed at not getting a ride out of doors I enjoyed very much what I got. Home soon after 6 — had a bath — tea at 7 — Edith Wingler came from Phil. at 8.30 went to Century to see Perry about Art Union matters. He wants me to etch a plate after Dodge's "The Awakening" now in Academy extn — Home a little before 11 & to bed.

## Right Page

### May, Sunday 13. 1883.

Still beautiful weather — B&lp. at 9.30 about 11 went to studio & from there to N.A.&D. where I put some varnish on the "Cathedral Rocks" bought by Miss Comstock. Then spent some time looking at the pictures — Home to dinner at 2 — after that; feeling the effects of my ride yesterday afternoon I took a nap. Later, George came in & made a short call — Then Annie & I went out for a short walk & back to tea at 7 — Eveth. Read & wrote to Father & to Mrs. Julia Ruger Donoho. At 10.30 took my letter to G.C. Depot — met Chas. Collins, just in from Dobbs Ferry — He had some wild flowers that he sent to Annie. Home & to bed at 11.
