## Left Page

### May, Monday 28. 1883.

Bright day — warm, but with refreshing breeze — Got to studio's a little after 9.30 — not well — but I started on Sun-dial etching & stuck to it closely until near 1 — Then Frank Walker came in & I gave him a lesson in the use of the burnisher — Nearly 1.30 when I got home — Found Aunt H. there waiting for me with plans for the Turnee monument trations concerning inscriptions. It was 2 o'clk. before she left & then we had dinner — It was 3.15 when I got to studio's — Spent what remained of the afternoon making a 2°. Reduction of Mble. Ho Neck Causeway & laying grounds. Home to tea at 7 — from 8.30 to 10.30 at work on Montrose plans — To bed abt 11.

## Right Page

### May, Tuesday 29. 1883.

An April-like day — pleasantly cool with disposition to showers that came to nothing — Got to studio's at about 10 & worked closely until 1, biting and working on Sun-dial etching. It seemed to go along very successfully. To dinner, & back at 3 — continued drawing in & biting until near 5 o'clk when I cleaned the plates — Then went in to Geo's — Frank Lent there — we went to Havett's to see the Hulbert collection of pictures and bric-a-brac — much disappointed in the pictures — Home to tea at 7 — Everf. Rather out of sorts again — kept quiet — wrote a letter to Cotrell — Took it out & posted it at about 10 o'clk — then in and to bed.
