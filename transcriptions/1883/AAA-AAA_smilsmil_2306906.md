## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 7, 1883

Dark, rainy morning — got up with
a heavy head which gradually
cleared, as did the weather — Bkfst
late — then worked on Haden correspondence
& accts until near 11 — then to
studio & wrote other letters — Mr. Condit
of Prov. came in & made quite a long
call — Home to dinner at 1.25 — back
at 3 — more calls, among them, A. P.
J. — I managed to charcoal a
sketch of some Canadian waterfall
for Picturesque Canada — took proof
of Bryant's head to Eff's. Co. to send to
A. B. Durand for his autograph — Jo?
Sabin called with some Haden money
ge?, under the weather — saw him
at his home at 3 — charming, Spring-
like afternoon — Home to tea at 6.30 —
Annie & I alone. Spent the even-
ing doing absolutely nothing — at 10, went
to E. M. C's for Mother Cook — home &
to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 8, 1883

Very pleasant day — to studio about
9.30 & to work on 8×10 monochrome oil
sketch of a Canadian waterfall — for
Picturesque Canada — Stuck to it and
put in a good mornings work —
home to dinner at 1 — Matt & Kate
came in at about 2 — & rei? to Po'k
by 4 train — Back to studio at 3 &
finished sketch — Home to tea at
6 — Annie & I went to Chickering
Hall at about 8 — Haden's second
lecture — I took acid trays & some
other things to him — a very good
"house" — who listened with wonder-
ful patience — to what to me was
excessively tedious — He spoke until
10.30 — at the close, many of the au-
dience came to the platform to ask
questions etc. — Home & to bed after
11.
