## Left Page

### November, Saturday 24, 1883

Another dismal day — to studio at 10 — stretched 5 plts. Plt of Scribner
Etching — laid a ground over No. 31 —
had a hurried but pleasant call
from Bram Stoker, Irvings business
manager, who came to pay Geo.
for his pictures & give him a commission
for another — Home at 1 — back at
3 — etched over parts of No. 31 — and
mounted plt. of Scribner Etching —
at 5.30 called on Geo. — found
him much better, in fact, feeling
quite well. — Then alone walk
& home at 6.30 — had tea and
spent the evng. in intellectual
exercises — Reading Howard Pyle's
Robin Hood & Emerson's Patro. —
Exhausted before 10 o'clk — &
to bed soon after —

H. F. Bellows died this morning
after intense suffering — for more
than three years he has fought
against a cancer in left breast
& under the arm.

## Right Page

### November, Sunday 25, 1883

Still overcast — cooler — up late — had
Hfp. at about 9. 30 — after 11 went to
studio — fixed up proof of Etching
"a corner at home" to give to Mother
Cook, this being her 79th Birthday —
Home at 1.30 — dinner at 2 — at 3.30
went to Mother Cook's — Arenie, boy
& nurse soon followed. About
4 I went up to Uncle G's — he
was so feeble that I thought best
not to trouble him — Charlie Quitter
thinks he cannot hose out many
days longer. Home before 6 — tea
before 7 — after that — wrote letter
home — at 9 took it to G.C. Depot
then a short walk — then home
— to bed about 10.30.
