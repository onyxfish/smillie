## Left Page

### March, Sunday 25, 1883

Charming spring day. Bkfs.
soon after 9 — Annie & I went
to hear Arthur Brooks, after a
walk down to Madison Square.
A.B's discourse did not interest
me very much — After service, I
walked up to H.V.P.'s — He ret.
from Cuba & the south on Friday —
He is still very far from well —
Home at 2 — dinner at 2.30 — At
4.30 to Studio — wet down some
paper for proving to-morrow — then
saw Chaudfoney & got Basque costume
sketches — At 6 went over to Will's.
He & Joe out — saw Minn & Walter —
Home to tea at 7 — Eve'g, Annie
read aloud to me from Josiah
Quincy's "Figures of the Past" —
At 9, went to Collyer's Ch. for Morton
Cook — then wrote letter home —
To bed soon after 11.

## Right Page

### March, Monday 26, 1883

Very pleasant day — overcast before
sundown — After bkfs. went to Timmel
& Voigt's in Canal St. & got printers' ink
& some paper — Then to studio at 10.30
& commenced printing Basque of
Cart — Home at 1 — back at 2.45
Mr. Marvin sent up for the plate at
3 o'clk. — As I was in the midst of
proving, I sent him p/c & asked
him to leave the plate with me
until 6 o'clk. — I took my last
pf at 6.15 — having taken 18 of all
sorts — Then washed up & home
at 7.15 — exceedingly tired —
After tea I read my paper &
then Annie read aloud from
the new novel "Democracy" —
To bed soon after 10.
