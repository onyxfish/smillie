## Left Page

### March, Thursday 29. 1883.

Very pleasant until — but overcast before 10 o'clk — Up at 7 — Help at 7.30 — Charl. Geo. — Annie & J — Reid — to N.Y. by 8.25 train, getting in at 10.30 — To studio at 11 — A long call from Dr. Hale. To work on Fontarabia etching — Home at 1 — back at 2.30 — Bishop Putnam called about some etchings to illustrate Lamb's Essays — I cleaned ground ff etching at 5 & took pf — was disappointed to find that I had been too timid in biting. Home at 6.30. Valentine & Geo. Cook to dinner to eat some ducks shot by Walter. Evef. Annie read aloud some of Lamb's Essays — I wrote a letter to Cottrell & to bed about 11.

## Right Page

### March, Friday 30. 1883.

A regular, winter snow storm — about 6 inches fell. Got to studio about 9.30 — Laid rebiting ground on Fontarabia plate & varnished it, then went to N.A. Varnishing Day — there for less than an hour — then again to studio & at work on my rebiting — Home at 1 — back at 2.30. Rebit until 5 — after that worked with dry point & burnisher until near 6.30 — Home at 7 — Evef. Annie again read aloud a little from Lamb — and I loafed. To bed about 10.30.
