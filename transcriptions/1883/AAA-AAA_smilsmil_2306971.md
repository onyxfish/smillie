## Left Page

### June, Tuesday 19, 1883

Delicious day - thunderstorms in the
eve'g, all about us — from which we
got slight showers. B'kf't at 8.30 - The
tile man came & got started about
10 o'clk, & by 5 had the three mantles
laid - I also bossed carpenter work
in various departments. Dinner at
1.30 — Dana Austin came for instruct-
ions — Also, Sayre's man fixed the
bust pipes to the Fawle — at 5 went
to P.O., got home letters — then to W. de
Schewienig & begged butter — him bonfire
lots of things, among them, delicious
strawberries — Home soon after 7 -
exceedingly tired — Tea at 7.30
— then a rest — then wrote to
Annie & to Bishop Putnam & at
10 took letters to P.O. Charl
busily engaged in the cellar
developing the 9 photos he has
taken to-day — To bed at
about 11.

## Right Page

### June, Wednesday 20, 1883

Cool, cloudy & pleasant until about
5 when showers came up from S.W. —
ending in a wet night — Up at 8. B'kf't
at 8.30. Tile man in trouble — mis Fake
in size of fireplace in main room in
Walter's drawings, so that I have to
cut down the tiles 6 in. The rest spent
between the man & carpenters — putting
up balustrade on east piazza — Down
town about 11 — bought meat & berries —
Dinner soon after 1 — Afternoon still
[illegible] up men — growing quite
cool — Ther. 62°. Tea a little after
7 — wrote letter to Annie & at 8.30
in a heavy rain, went to P.O. —
back at 9.15 — Spent some time
looking over Charl's photo, prints —
To bed at 10.45.
