## Left Page

### March, Monday 5, 1883

Beautiful, wintry day - Spent the m'f making pen sketch of Yosemite picture for Academy notes - Mr. E. H. Henry called about studios in Chelsea Bldg in W. 23d. S - Then Gifford & Murtz - Home to dinner at about 1.30. After that, to Bank & to H.P. Smith 862 Bday. & urged him to accept nomination for W.C. Soc'ty - but he gave no decided answer. To studio soon after 4 - fussed awhiles on "Old Orchard" - Home at 6.30 - tea - at 7.30 - at Chickering Hall - Dielt Kitchen concert; English Glee Club and Standard Quartette — very good concert & full house - saw many friends. Home before 11 and soon to bed.

## Right Page

### March, Tuesday 6, 1883

Wretched sort of day - snow all the m'f. Rain all the afternoon - slop at night. Got to studio before 10 & to work on "Old Apple Orchard." Hopelessly trying to finish it — Mr. Harriet Stanley [illegible] came in to say that in his effort to get water-color paintings for his London Exh'n he finds F.H. Smith's name on the committee an obstacle, several artists refusing to contribute if he is to act as a judge. Home to dinner at 1.30 — Mother Cook's back from her visiting spree. Back to studio at 3.30 & worked for an hour — then to Dickle's & took my first horse back ride for the season - met J.Q.A. Ward & we rode together - out at 6.40 - home then soon after 7 - Eve'g quietly reading & writing. To bed before 11.
