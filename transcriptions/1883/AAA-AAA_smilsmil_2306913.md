## Left Page

### February, Wednesday 21, 1883

Snow flurry in the m'f. The day breezy, clear & cold — To
studio at 10 — Wrote letters
& Rec'd. callers most of the m'f.
Perry called & talked of Rotary
[illegible] & Mr. Marvin, of Scribner's
called & I promised to make a
couple of etchings for St. Vincent's
book on the Basque Provinces, soon
to be published. — Home to dinner
at 1 — back before 3 — Made
a small charcoal sketch on
canvas but felt so unlike
work that at 4.30 I went over
to N.A., met there young Ritchie
& then Mr. Rob't. Gordon — went
around the galleries with him —
then to 4th Ave. & 10th St — Health food
— then home at 6.30 — Tea at 7 —
Ev'g. Reading — moved book
case in front bed room —
To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### February, Thursday 22, 1883

Mean, overcast, raw day, with
now & then snow or rain enough to
wet the walks. Got to studio at
9.45 & started a little neck
sketch 6½ × 15½ in. Interrupted
by callers — Nicole came in & talked
of W. C. matters — but I painted as
best I could until 1 — then home
to dinner — back before 3 — more
interruptions — Miss Harmon called —
I could not get sketch finished.
Home at 6 — tea before 7 — with
Annie to her singing club — then
to Century — met Perry, Bierstadt,
Wm. Hart, Com. Beard & T.W. Wood &
had preliminary talk about a
Rotary system. I think younger
men should be interested in it.
If they are not taken in to the
councils I will not work with
them — left at 10.15 — went for a
home — & to bed soon after 11.
