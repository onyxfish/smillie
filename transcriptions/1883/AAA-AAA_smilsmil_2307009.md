## Left Page

### September, Monday 3, 1883.

Winds in the N.W. cold, clear
cloudy mtf. Ther. did not get
above 58° at any time during the
day — clear afternoon — After [illegible]
made fire in studio furnace &
to work on etching 28 — added
work & [illegible] — as usual, had
to spend some time with the
carpenters — Dinner about 1.30
after that worked on 29 until
4.30 when I went out riding
taking Mother, Annie & W. K.
around the lake, returning and
then father in mother's place, we
were out until 6.30 — Tea after 7.
Swett, wrote a letter to Wm G.
Nielson at Keene Valley —
to bed at 10.30.
Dorsey at work to-day
putting studio cellar in order.

## Right Page

### September, Tuesday 4, 1883.

Bright, clear & cool. Ther. went
down to 42° last night. Heavy frosts
in some places. Nearly 8.30 when
we had help. At 10.30 Will K. left
us, returning home — I got to printing
as soon as possible & spent all the
mf on 28 — Dinner soon after
1 — after & superintending carpenters
for awhile. Printed again until
4.30 when carriage came. I
took Father & Mother out for a
40 mi. rides — then home & took
Annie in mother's place — home
at 6.45 — Tea after 7 — Before
we had finished, Dan Sayre,
wife & Mr. Comstock & Joe Williams
called — until 9 o'clock — Then
I read papers & looked over
Hartnal's bills until 10.45 —
then to bed.
