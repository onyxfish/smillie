## Left Page

### September, Wednesday 5, 1883

Slight fall of rain during the night — Cold, cloudy, windy with showers bet. 12 and 1 o'clk. Exceedingly brilliant p.m. Ther. did not get above 59° all day — After bkfst — got plate 28 ready to send to Mr. Laut and then down town with it to S[illegible] office. Tried to settle accts with A. Lathrop. Home at noon — Dinner soon after 1 — After that with carpenters — then worked awhile on margin of No. 25. At 4.40 out riding with Annie & father — Mother not feeling quite well stayed in to rest. Breezy, bright & cool — In at 6.30 — Tea about 7 — Sewing with newspapers & lounging. To bed before 11.

## Right Page

### September, Thursday 6, 1883

Brilliant day — Bkfst about 8 and at 10.30 father, mother & I started for Montrose depot & had a very delightful ride over, getting there in time for the 12.02 train, upon which father & mother started for N.Y. manifestly much improved by their fortnights stay with us. I got home at 1.40 & had dinner. The most of the afternoon was spent in washing & re-tacking the canvas in main hall. Tea at 7 — Delicious twilight with quarter moon — Will Mulford & Alice Foster were married in the Episcopal church at 8 this eveg. Our cook and chambermaid represented us at the ceremony — For a while as Annie sat sewing, I read aloud from "Bourbon Lilies". To bed soon after 10.
