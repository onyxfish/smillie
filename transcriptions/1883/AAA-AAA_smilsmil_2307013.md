## Left Page

### September, Tuesday 11, 1883

mild & partially overcast day —
About 10 a.m. got started at my
proofing of No. 25. Had trouble with
my ink, owing to my having put raw
linseed oil over it in the can. But
I soon remedied the trouble with
the loss of some ink — Got some fairly
successful [illegible] — dinner at 1 —
After that, Warner came up with
my new printing table & I put it
right to use. Printed until near
5 — then cleaned up — Packed my
plate (No. 25) & at 6.15, with Annie
went to [illegible] Office, P.O. etc — letters
from Father, Sleuinger & others — Home
to tea at about 7 — Well, being
tired I dawdled about, read
newspaper & walked piazza
with A. — To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### September, Wednesday 12, 1883

mild & overcast — wind, S — Up
late — blkb; near 9 — Spent the
mttf. cleaning up studio, directing
carpenters & writing letters — one
to Frank T. lent, describing house
& giving brief biographical sketch.
To P.O. just before dinner at
1.30 — After that, went down to
the piney with my plate & etched
on the view from the door until 4
o'clk. At 4.45, with Annie,
went out riding — to Hart Lake
& around to depot Road at the
top of the big hill — home at 6.45.
Tea at 7.30 — Evening in big chair
with Baker — Annie read aloud
a little while — started for bed
soon after 10 — but Master James
took an unusual musical turn
& we did not get settled for
the night until after 11.
