## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 28. 1883.

Cool & overcast — wind from the
east threatening a storm — a few
drops of rain — B[illegible] soon after 8 —
Got drawing for A.B.N. Co. Ready
& took it to S[illegible]. office — then
to Warner's & to Foundry & ordered
heavy hinges for kitchen doors — back
home at 11,30 — dinner at — at
2,10 I started for depot — [illegible]
Kimball came on 3,27 train —
bad weather still holding off — had
a pleasant ride & got home at
5 o'clk — Tea about 7 — Eve[illegible]
chatting until 10 — to bed
soon after.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 29. 1883.

A cold, wet day — Ther. got no high
er than 57°  much of the morning
spent in carrying wood for fires.
Occasional light showers. After
a time got to work — laid ground
on small copper plate ( × ) and
smoked it — made very slight
pencil sketch of a view from
Deer's Leap, Lake George. — Traced
[illegible] — then dinner. Soldiers'
encampment on fair ground —
about 2,30 a mock engagement
of which we saw a part from our
piazza — Transferred tracing on
press. & then commenced etching
— intend plate for "The Studio" —
About 6 went down town — Tea
a little before 7 — Eve[illegible]. the
family passed in reading,
writing, chatting until about
10 o'clk.
