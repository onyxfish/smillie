## Left Page

### August, Tuesday 14, 1883.

Another Belp: day — cloudy +
cool. Ther. 55° last night, + 60°
at Belp: I worked on No. 26 —
for a time with dry-point —
then I laid a ground, solid
in parts + re-biting in others —
also a rebiting ground on a
part of No. 25 — Dinner at
afternoon, did some etching
+ biting but was so out of sorts
that I could not finish it —
Soon after 5 Rode down to Park's
where Annie was + we rode
out until 6.40. Then home —
Master Jim strangely perverse
about taking his nurse, causing
Annie much anxiety — Tea
about 8 — Eveg. lying about.
Brilliant, quiet moonlight —
To bed soon after 10.

## Right Page

### August, Wednesday 15, 1883.

Cloudy + cool — at night, over-
cast + threatening rain — Got
to my biting on 26 after Belp:. +
completed it — Then went down
town with Annie + we did some
shopping together — Near 1 o'clk
when we got home — Dinner
Then more work on my plates
after 5.30 before we got started
on our ride — Then over Dules
around the lake + home in less
than an hour — I rode down town
+ got a little kerosene stove to
use while printing — Tea
about 7.30 — fixed [illegible] paper for
printing to-morrow — Then sat
awhile with Annie + read
aloud from Mrs. Chamboney's
"Bourbon Lilies" — To bed before
11.
