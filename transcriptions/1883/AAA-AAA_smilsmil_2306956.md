## Left Page

### May, Sunday 20, 1883.

Slightly overcast during the early
m'g., but soon cleared off to be a
charming day. B'kf's. at 8 — about
9 went up to Mam's & for some time
sat & talked with her of Matt's case
counselling a preparation for the worst.
Home about 10 & met Luciell & Aunt
A. Had a quiet folk alone with
mother. At noon went to Baptist ch.
& after service walked with Father
around by S°. Hamilton St. & went
over Uncle W's new place — then
home to dinner at 1 — After that
had to give up to headache & was
good for nothing until after 8 p.m.
when I rallied again — Mamy had
alarmed Ed, so that he came to me
& I did what I could to reassure
him — Took Kate out for a short
walk in the beautiful moonlight —
then met Father & walked awhile
with him — Then in & to bed at
10 o'clk.

## Right Page

### May, Monday 21, 1883.

at home, N.Y.

Up at 6.30 — overcast day — B'kf's.
soon after 7. Hack came at 8 — said
my good byes & by 8.25 train to
N.Y. getting in at 10.30 — went at once to
Stephens Smith's — saw him & talked
with him of Matt's case for half an hour.
Then home at 11.30 — unpacked bag &
put away things — Then, met dinner
at 1 o'clk., wrote to Matt & Kate. After
dinner, at 2.30 — went to studio, Annie
accompanying me part way — At the
studio found copper plates from [illegible]
Whiteley — & laid grounds on them.
Spent some time with Ge°. & Nellie till-
ing of my Po'k visit — called on Bellows —
had an interesting conversation with him
of his sufferings & experience — Got a book
from him describing Count Matter's Electro-
Homœopathic discoveries — Home at 6.30 —
tea — E'n'g. read the Matter book
which seems to me pure quackery —
finished my letters to Matt & Kate &
at 9.30 took them to G.C.D— just
escaped a ducking in a heavy Thun-
der storm — To bed soon after 11.
