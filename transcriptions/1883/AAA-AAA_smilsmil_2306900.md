## Left Page

### January, Friday 26, 1883

Overcast with some light snow in the mf. Up late & did not get to studio until after 10 — tried to get things to rights & then to work, but had too many calls Dr. Yale & Mr. [Lobpow?] among others. At 12.30 went to N.a. & did a little final straightening up of pictures, then to Bank & home to dinner at 1.45 — found Matt & Kate there — they ret'd at 4 o'clk — to Po'k — I went to studio about 3 & spent another half day (or rather, quarter of one) in doing nothing of importance. Had a call from Bellows who wanted me to tell him where I had hung his picture — home at 6 — Tea at 7, about 8.30 went to Press View at N.a. — a great crowd, as usual, & lots of tobacco smoke — Every body seemed very well pleased except Satterlee who [poured out his woes, in touching manner upon me]. About 11.30 walked up home with Geo., Walter & Valentine & got to bed after midnight.

## Right Page

### January, Saturday 27, 1883

Overcast & rather dismal. — Rain & threatening rain all day, which came plentifully in the evening — Got to studio before 10 o'clk — By appointment, a girl calling herself Pauline Swift, came to studio to see Champney & me as to her fitness for a model — She won't do — About 11 went to N.a. & met Annie & her mother. — Buyer's Day — met many friends — a. was sufficiently interested to buy 3 little pictures — landscape by Nico Bastest — Head by Lippincott & Chrysanthemums by W.P. Dillon — Had a talk with Mr. Sherwood about a new Studio's Bldg. — Home to dinner at 1.15 — to studio at 3 — Charcoaled on Sketch "Crescent Beach, Magnolia" until 4.30 then to Knodler's & Wunderlich's & at 5 to Century — Yale & Gilder there — no Stor. & Butts — Y. walked up to 38th S. with me in the rain — Tea at 7. Ev'f. at home — made a sketch for decoration of hall — to bed at near 11.
